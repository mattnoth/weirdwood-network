## Origins

Barbrey is the surviving daughter of [Rodrik Ryswell](wiki:Rodrik_Ryswell), [Lord of the Rills](wiki:Lord_of_the_Rills), and is the sister of [Roger](wiki:Roger_Ryswell), [Rickard](wiki:Rickard_Ryswell), and [Roose Ryswell](wiki:Roose_Ryswell). Their sister was [Bethany](wiki:Bethany_Bolton), the late second wife of [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), [Lord of the Dreadfort](wiki:Lord_of_the_Dreadfort).(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-9)

[Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark), the eldest son of [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark), [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell), often rode the [Rills](wiki:Rills) while being fostered at [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton) with old Lord [Dustin](wiki:House_Dustin). Infatuated with the heir of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), young Barbrey had an affair with Brandon, who took her [maidenhead](wiki:Maidenhead). Lord Rodrik hoped to wed his daughter to a [Stark](wiki:House_Stark), but Lord Rickard betrothed his heir to [Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun). Barbrey claims Brandon did not want to wed Catelyn(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5) and that the betrothal was the idea of [Walys](wiki:Walys_Flowers), Rickard's [maester](wiki:Maester) at Winterfell.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) Rodrik then hoped to wed Barbrey to Brandon's younger brother, [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), but that was not to be. Barbrey instead wed [Willam Dustin](wiki:Willam_Dustin), the young [Lord of Barrowton](wiki:Lord_of_Barrowton).(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6)

The abduction of [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) by Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) led to the executions of Brandon and Lord Rickard Stark by King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Now Lord of Winterfell, Eddard returned to the [north](wiki:North) to call his banners for [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). Not yet half a year into marriage, Willam rode off to war against Barbrey's objections. Barbrey gifted him with a fine red steed, the pride of her father's herd, and Willam swore he would return to her mounted on the horse.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-7) However, Lord Dustin was slain in the [combat at the tower of joy](wiki:Combat_at_the_tower_of_joy),(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Rawoiafwillam_dustin.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) and Ned built cairns for the fallen.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) After the war ended, Lord Stark returned Willam's steed to Barbrey. She bears a grudge against Eddard, who brought the bones of his sister, Lyanna, to Winterfell for burial in [its crypt](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell), but not the bones of her husband, Lord Dustin, to Barrowton.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-8)

In accordance with the [Widow's Law](wiki:Widow's_Law), Barbrey remained at Barrowton after her husband's passing and rules the town in her own name. She was fond of her sister Bethany's son, [Domeric Bolton](wiki:Domeric_Bolton), who served at Barrowton for four years as her [page](wiki:Page).(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Rawoiafdomeric_bolton.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-10) Since Domeric's death in [297 AC](wiki:297_AC), Barbrey has nursed suspicions about [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow)'s involvement.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-11)

## Appearances & Description

Barbrey is tall, unbent, and handsome.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) She has wrinkles(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) around her mouth and dark eyes,(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and her hair is equal parts brown and grey.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Lady Dustin wears her hair tied behind her head in a widow's knot.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

While intelligent,(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Barbrey has grown into a bitter woman over the years.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) Her smile can appear ugly(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) and feral.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Lady Dustin is distrustful of [maesters](wiki:Maester) and refers to them as "grey rats".(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) She was fond of her late nephew, [Domeric Bolton](wiki:Domeric_Bolton), and despises [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), whom she blames for Domeric's death.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5) Barbrey also bears a grudge against [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark).(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6)(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

Lady Dustin dresses all in black without [gold](wiki:Gold) or [jewelry](wiki:Jewelry).(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-7) One of her gowns has a high stiff collar and sleeves lined with [vair](wiki:Vair), and she has a [sable](wiki:Sable) cloak.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) Barbrey's personal banner flying over the keep of [Barrow Hall](wiki:Barrow_Hall) quarters the spiked crown and crossed long axes of [House Dustin](wiki:House_Dustin) with the [golden](wiki:Gold) [horse](wiki:Horse) head of her father, Lord [Rodrik Ryswell](wiki:Rodrik_Ryswell).(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-8) [Beron](wiki:Beron_(serjeant)) is one of her [serjeants](wiki:Serjeant).(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Lady Barbrey sent a minimal number of soldiers from [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton) to join the army of [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), who was proclaimed [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) but later killed in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-9)

Barbey is one of the signatories of the letters that [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) sends announcing that [Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin) has been retaken from the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn).(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) She hosts the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) at Barrowton, where the wedding of Ramsay to "[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark)" (actually [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)) is to occur. She cannot abide the presence of Ramsay and his [Bastard's Boys](wiki:Bastard's_Boys)(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) and forbids them from [Barrow Hall](wiki:Barrow_Hall), as she suspects Ramsay had a hand in the death of her nephew, [Domeric Bolton](wiki:Domeric_Bolton). She gives grey colts to Ramsay's squires, [Big Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)) and [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)).(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-12)

When [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), learns that King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) has left the [Wall](wiki:Wall), he decides to move [Ramsay's wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Ramsay_Bolton_and_Arya_Stark) from Barrowton to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-13) Barbrey insists on taking custody of "Arya" until the wedding occurs. [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), who travels in Lady Dustin's retinue to Winterfell, sits beside her at the wedding feast. Although Barbrey is astute and has good political savvy, she fails to see through the behavior of [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly), [Lord of White Harbor](wiki:Lord_of_White_Harbor), during the feast. She is offended by Wyman's apparent capitulation despite his son, Ser [Wendel Manderly](wiki:Wendel_Manderly), dying at the Red Wedding, and she dismisses Lord Manderly to Theon as craven to the bone—just after [Wyman serves his pies](wiki:Frey_Pies/Theories).(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3) Theon suspects that Barbrey is aware the bride is an impostor.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-10)

Among the snowmen that squires build along the battlements of Winterfell, one is a snow lady resembling Barbrey. Lady Dustin has Theon lead her into the [crypts of Winterfell](wiki:Crypts_of_Winterfell), where she explains much of her history and story to him. Barbrey resents that [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell), returned the horse of her husband, [Willam Dustin](wiki:Willam_Dustin), [Lord of Barrowton](wiki:Lord_of_Barrowton), after [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), but not Willam's bones. As vengeance, Barbrey tells Theon that if Eddard's bones, which were heading north before Moat Cailin [fell to the ironborn](wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin), are discovered north of the [Neck](wiki:Neck), she will ensure they are never put to rest in the crypts, and she plans to feed them to her dogs.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-11) Barrowton is staunch for [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) largely because of this grievance.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-14) Before leaving the crypts, Barbrey and Theon discover that swords are missing from the tombs, including that of [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark), her lover from her youth.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-12)

As tensions arise in snow-bound Winterfell, Barbrey and her brother, Ser [Roger Ryswell](wiki:Roger_Ryswell), calm knights from [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) when Ser [Hosteen Frey](wiki:Hosteen_Frey) insults Lord Wyman. When Barbrey requests a more cheerful song than "[The Winter Maid](wiki:The_Winter_Maid)" during a meal, [Abel](wiki:Mance_Rayder) sings "[The Queen Took Off Her Sandal, the King Took Off His Crown](wiki:The_Queen_Took_Off_Her_Sandal,_the_King_Took_Off_His_Crown)" and "[The Bear and the Maiden Fair](wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair)". While she appears to be loyal to House Bolton, Barbrey bears a grudge against [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) due to the fact that several of her own men were killed at the Red Wedding. She points this out to Ser [Aenys Frey](wiki:Aenys_Frey) when several suspicious deaths occur in Winterfell.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) She is absent when the Manderlys and Freys clash after the death of Little Walder and Theon escapes the castle.(wiki:Barbrey_Dustin.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
