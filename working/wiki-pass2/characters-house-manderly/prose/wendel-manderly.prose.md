## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Wendel Manderly](wiki:Category:Images_of_Wendel_Manderly)*

Wendel has a large walrus mustache and is bald.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) He is not quite as fat as his father, Lord [Wyman](wiki:Wyman_Manderly), and older brother, Ser [Wylis](wiki:Wylis_Manderly), but he is still considered immense.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) His face is shaped like the moon.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Wendel is loud and boisterous(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) with a booming voice.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) A protective man,(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) he is a prodigious eater who values his honor highly.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Wendel's clothes are food stained.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) The unwed(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) [knight](wiki:Knight) wields a bow.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Ser Wendel accompanies his brother, [Wylis](wiki:Wylis_Manderly), to the muster when [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) calls his banners. They escort [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) from [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) to Robb's encampment at [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin).(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

Wendel stays with Robb's force on the march south and is named as one of his [personal guard](wiki:Robb_Stark's_personal_guard) during the [battle in the Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood).(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Robb is proclaimed [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Lord Wyman considers marrying Wendel to Lady [Donella Hornwood](wiki:Donella_Hornwood) in order to claim the [Hornwood](wiki:Hornwood) lands following the deaths of her husband, Lord [Halys](wiki:Halys_Hornwood), and son, [Daryn](wiki:Daryn_Hornwood).(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Donella is abducted by [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), however.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Wendel is one of Catelyn's escorts after she leaves Riverrun to meet with [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), and he offers to hunt quail for her during the journey south. Wendel is entertained by the [melee at Bitterbridge](wiki:Melee_at_Bitterbridge).(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) They continue on to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) after Renly learns his castle is [under attack](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)) by his brother, [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

At a [sept](wiki:Sept) near Storm's End, the quiet Ser [Robar Royce](wiki:Robar_Royce) and the loud Wendel discuss the upcoming battle between Renly and Stannis. Renly is assassinated by a [shadow](wiki:Shadow_assassin) in his pavilion, however, and Robb's envoys flee Storm's End.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) Since [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) discards her clothing stained with Renly's blood, Wendel gives some of his spare garb to the woman.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Catelyn's party returns to Riverrun, and Wendel cheers when he hears of Robb's victories in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands).(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

### A Storm of Swords

After Robb breaks his marriage contract with [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) by wedding [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling), Wendel offers to marry a daughter of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) as part of an apology to the Freys. Catelyn does not believe that Walder would be placated by a second son like Wendel, however.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Wendel, one of Robb's [sworn swords](wiki:Sworn_sword),(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) commands the baggage train during the journey from Riverrun to the [Twins](wiki:Twins) for the wedding of Lord [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) to [Roslin Frey](wiki:Roslin_Frey).(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Wendel eagerly eats a leg of lamb during the wedding feast, and [Walda Frey](wiki:Walda_Frey) tells him of her own marriage to Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton).(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) When the Freys and [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) betray Robb with the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), Wendel is killed by a crossbow quarrel to his mouth.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

Wendel's bones are returned to his father by a trio of [Freys](wiki:House_Frey)—[Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey), [Jared](wiki:Jared_Frey) and [Symond](wiki:Symond_Frey)—who also come to ensure [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) bends the knee to King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon). Wendel's remains are interred in the [Sept of the Snows](wiki:Sept_of_the_Snows).(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Jared tells the [Merman's Court](wiki:Merman's_Court) that Wendel saved Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) from the [warg](wiki:Skinchanger) [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), at which Lord [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) scoffs.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) In private, however, Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) confides in Davos that he seeks vengeance against the Freys for his son's death.(wiki:Wendel_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
