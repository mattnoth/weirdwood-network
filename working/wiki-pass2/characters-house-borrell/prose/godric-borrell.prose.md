## Origins

During [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) washed up on [Sweetsister](wiki:Sweetsister) while trying to sail to the [north](wiki:North) to raise his banners. Godric's father, [Lord Borrell](wiki:Lord_Borrell_(father_of_Godric)), could easily have turned Eddard over to King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) for a reward. Godric was in attendance when his father instead freed Ned.(wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

Because the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters) have long tolerated smugglers, Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships) for King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), once sent a fleet to [Sisterton](wiki:Sisterton) without Godric's leave and made him hang a dozen friends. Stannis threatened to hang Godric as well should some ship sink because the [Night Lamp](wiki:Night_Lamp) had gone black.(wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

Godric has daughters but no sons, and his granddaughters include [Gella](wiki:Gella).(wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

## Appearances & Description

Godric is a big, fleshy, man with massive shoulders. His face is blunt and brutal with a massive brow. Godric is bald on the top of his scalp, though coarse grey and white hair grows from his cheeks and chin. The black-eyed lord has thick lips, and his nose is lumpy and red veined. Godric has all of his teeth, though most are yellow and one is blackened. He has "the mark", webbing between the middle three fingers on his big right hand.(wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

Lord Borrell is fond of storms and [sister's stew](wiki:Sister's_stew), but not of [northmen](wiki:North).(wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) According to a semi-canon source, he is pragmatic.(wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Rawoiafgodric_borrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) thinks of him as a [robber](wiki:Robber_knight) lord and a [wrecker](wiki:Wrecker). Though Godric has a black repute, he still respects the ancient [laws of hospitality](wiki:Guest_right).(wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

Lord Godric claims the cargo of *[Sloe-Eyed Maid](wiki:Sloe-Eyed_Maid)* after the ship crashes into rocks in the [Bite](wiki:Bite).(wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

A group of [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) led by [Rhaegar Frey](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey) arrive at [Sweetsister](wiki:Sweetsister) en route to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), bringing Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) the bones of Wyman's younger son, Ser [Wendel](wiki:Wendel_Manderly). Disdainful of Wyman, Lord Godric states he would have hanged the Freys if they had extended him such a "courtesy".(wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)

Godric is having supper when Lord [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) is brought before him. Godric updates Davos on the current events, including the death of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). He tells Davos the story of [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark)'s visit, recounting to Davos about a fisherman and his daughter who brought Ned Stark in secret across the [Bite](wiki:Bite) to the [north](wiki:North) at the beginning of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). If Godric is to be believed, Ned left her with a bag of silver and a [bastard in her belly, which she named Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow/Theories) after Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn).(wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14)

It becomes evident that Godric harbors doubts about Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s ability to rule, as he notes that Davos would be in chains if her uncle, Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), were in power. When Godric tells Davos of his father's parting words to Ned Stark, "If you lose, you were never here," Davos replies, "No more than I was."(wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15) Godric then allows Davos to leave for [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) to attempt to curry favor with Lord Manderly.(wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
