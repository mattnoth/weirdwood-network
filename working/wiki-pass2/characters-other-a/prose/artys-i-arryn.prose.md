## Origins

Artys was born of pure [Andal](wiki:Andal) blood in [the Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) in the shadow of the [Giant's Lance](wiki:Giant's_Lance). On his shield he bore the moon-and-falcon and a pair of falcon's wings decorated his silver warhelm, earning him the nickname of the Falcon Knight. He was esteemed as the finest warrior of his day, skilled with sword, lance and morningstar. Artys was a cunning and resourceful leader, beloved by all who fought beside him.

After King [Robar II Royce](wiki:Robar_II_Royce) united the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) of the Vale, the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon), and the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers) under his rule and struck against the divided Andal houses and petty kings, the Andals unified under the leadership of Ser Artys, who was of an age with King Robar. He led the Andal army in the [Battle of Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_Seven_Stars), taking part in its final stage. Artys led a force of over five hundred [knights](wiki:Knight) through a goat track on the Giant's Lance to take the already weakened First Men in the rear, leaving a decoy, who was slain by Robar, among the main fighting force. It is possible he slew Robar himself, though other candidates have been put forth over the centuries.(wiki:Artys_I_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

After the Andal victory, the First Men houses bent the knee to Ser Artys, who was proclaimed [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale), thus founding the Arryn dynasty. In later centuries, singers would conflate him with the legendary "[Winged Knight](wiki:Winged_Knight)".(wiki:Artys_I_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafhouse_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
