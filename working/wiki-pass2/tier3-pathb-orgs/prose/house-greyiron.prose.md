## Origins

### Driftwood Kings[ ]

The Greyirons of antiquity were [rock](wiki:Rock_king) and [salt kings](wiki:Salt_king) on the island of [Orkmont](wiki:Orkmont). [Urras Ironfoot](wiki:Urras_Greyiron) was chosen as the first [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands) since the [Grey King](wiki:Grey_King). After the death of Urras, his son claimed the throne as [Erich I Greyiron](wiki:Erich_I_Greyiron). He stepped down when the prophet [Galon Whitestaff](wiki:Galon_Whitestaff) threatened him with death for not being chosen by a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot). For centuries after, the high kings were chosen between the lords and captains of the Iron Islands in kingsmoots. According to Archmaester [Haereg](wiki:Haereg), the Greyirons had thirty-eight high kings chosen, twice as many as any other house.(wiki:House_Greyiron.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) In some historical accounts, [Qhored I Hoare](wiki:Qhored_I_Hoare), one of the greatest High Kings, is said to have been a Greyiron.(wiki:House_Greyiron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
When King [Urragon III Greyiron](wiki:Urragon_III_Greyiron) died during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), his eldest son [Torgon](wiki:Torgon_Greyiron) had been away raiding along the [Mander](wiki:Mander). His brothers sent him no word of the kingsmoot, hoping that one of them would be chosen. However, [Urrathon IV Goodbrother](wiki:Urrathon_IV_Goodbrother) was chosen instead and later had all of Torgon's brothers killed. When Torgon returned he denounced the kingsmoot as unlawful and revolted against Urrathon, with the aid of priests, lords and Urrathon's own captains.(wiki:House_Greyiron.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Although Torgon the Latecomer was strong, he still lost the [Cape of Eagles](wiki:Cape_of_Eagles) to [House Mallister](wiki:House_Mallister) of [Seagard](wiki:Seagard).(wiki:House_Greyiron.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

### Hereditary Kings[ ]

Torgon and his son, [Urragon IV Greyiron](wiki:Urragon_IV_Greyiron), ruled as high kings without having been chosen in kingsmoots. Urragon IV wanted the crown to pass to his great-nephew, [Urron Greyiron](wiki:Urron_Greyiron), but the priests forced a kingsmoot. Urron descended with his axemen among the ironborn assembled on [Nagga's Bones](wiki:Nagga's_Bones), murdering all their opponents and proclaiming himself king five thousand years before the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:House_Greyiron.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:House_Greyiron.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
Urron made the shorter title of King of the Iron Islands hereditary, so at his death the kingship passed to his heir instead of being chosen in a kingsmoot. They also wore a black iron crown instead of the individualized driftwood crowns of the high kings. The rulers of each Iron Island were reduced to [lords](wiki:Lord), while lines that refused to bend the knee were extinguished. Urron Redhand and his descendants dealt with half a dozen major rebellions and at least two major [thrall](wiki:Thrall) uprisings. Meanwhile, the lords of the mainland took advantage of the disunity among the ironborn, and one by one, the remaining footholds in the [green lands](wiki:Green_lands) were lost.(wiki:House_Greyiron.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
Nevertheless, the Greyiron dynasty endured for "a thousand years".(wiki:House_Greyiron.cite_ref-years_7-0) King [Rognar II Greyiron](wiki:Rognar_II_Greyiron) was brought down, however, by an alliance of [Andal](wiki:Andals) pirates, sellswords and warlords who came to the Iron Islands with the support of the [Orkwoods](wiki:House_Orkwood), [Drumms](wiki:House_Drumm), [Hoares](wiki:House_Hoare), and [Greyjoys](wiki:House_Greyjoy).(wiki:House_Greyiron.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:House_Greyiron.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) The Greyirons were succeeded by the Hoares(wiki:House_Greyiron.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) after, according to the tale, [Harras Hoare](wiki:Harras_Hoare) won a contest of the [finger dance](wiki:Finger_dance).(wiki:House_Greyiron.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
