## Origins

### Bronze Kings[ ]

Calling themselves the [Bronze Kings](wiki:Bronze_King) and wearing the [Runic Crown](wiki:Runic_Crown), the Royce of [Runestone](wiki:Runestone) were the most powerful [First Men](wiki:First_Men) monarchs of the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn_(valley)) in antiquity. Their rivals included [House Shett](wiki:House_Shett) of [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown), who carried the title [King of the True Men](wiki:King_of_the_True_Men) and likewise claimed to date back ten thousand years to the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age).(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
After King [Yorwyck VI Royce](wiki:Yorwyck_VI_Royce) defeated the Shetts in several battles, King [Osgood III Shett](wiki:Osgood_III_Shett) allied himself with Ser [Gerold Grafton](wiki:Gerold_Grafton_(king)), an [Andal](wiki:Andal) warlord from [Andalos](wiki:Andalos) across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). Although they defeated the Bronze King, the death of Osgood in battle led [House Grafton](wiki:House_Grafton) to claim Gulltown for themselves. Yorwyck later repelled several other Andal attacks.(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

### High King[ ]

The last of the Bronze Kings was Yorwyck's young grandson, [Robar II](wiki:Robar_II_Royce), who came to the throne when [Andal invaders](wiki:Arrival_of_the_Andals) had conquered three-quarters of the Vale. Robar gained the allegiance of surviving First Men houses, including [Belmores](wiki:House_Belmore), [Coldwaters](wiki:House_Coldwater), [Hunters](wiki:House_Hunter), [Redforts](wiki:House_Redfort), and [Upcliffs](wiki:House_Upcliff), and he was proclaimed [High King of the Vale, the Fingers, and the Mountains of the Moon](wiki:High_King_of_the_Vale,_the_Fingers,_and_the_Mountains_of_the_Moon).(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)
Robar led the First Men to several victories, but he was eventually defeated Ser [Artys Arryn](wiki:Artys_Arryn)'s Andals in the [Battle of the Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_the_Seven_Stars). The surviving Royces bent the knee and swore fealty to [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn), the new [Kings of Mountain and Vale](wiki:Kings_of_Mountain_and_Vale).(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

### Targaryen Era[ ]

The Arryn kings submitted to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), at the start of the reign of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), [Ronnel Arryn](wiki:Ronnel_Arryn_(king)), the Lord of the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), was imprisoned by his younger brother [Jonos](wiki:Jonos_Arryn), who declared himself [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale). Lord [Allard Royce](wiki:Allard_Royce) led a host which swept away rebels(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) and penned Jonos, his captives, and his followers up in the Eyrie, which led to the murder of Ronnel when his brother sent him flying through the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door).(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Prince [Maegor Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) ended the rebellion by flying to the Eyrie atop his [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Balerion](wiki:Balerion). After Jonos's death, the Arryn line continued through a cousin, [Hubert Arryn](wiki:Hubert_Arryn), who was married to a lady of House Royce. The pair had six children. Aenys rewarded Allard for his service. The Royces later came out against the tyrannical King Maegor I and supported King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)
An unnamed Lord Royce is present at the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) in [49 AC](wiki:49_AC),(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) this could be Lord Allard.
[Yorbert Royce](wiki:Yorbert_Royce), the [Lord Protector of the Vale](wiki:Lord_Protector_of_the_Vale), attended the [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) of [101 AC](wiki:101_AC) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), since Lady [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn) was a minor at that time.(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) wed [Rhea Royce](wiki:Rhea_Royce) in [97 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). It was an unhappy marriage, with Daemon referring to the Lady of Runestone as his "bronze bitch". King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) refused to set aside his brother's marriage, however. Daemon attempted to claim Runestone after Rhea's death, but the castle passed to her nephew and Lady Jeyne warned Daemon against remaining in the Vale.(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Amidst the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Ser [Willam Royce](wiki:Willam_Royce) was killed and his family's [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword, [Lamentation](wiki:Lamentation_(sword)), was lost during the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit).(wiki:House_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

The king's regent, Ser [Corwyn Corbray](wiki:Corwyn_Corbray) ruled that her chosen heir, Ser [Joffrey Arryn](wiki:Joffrey_Arryn) must be [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie), and Ser Corwyn was killed by a crossbowman at Runestone while confronting Lord [Gunthor Royce](wiki:Gunthor_Royce), as Ser Arnold had sought sanctuary there.
