## Origins

[Quellon Greyjoy](wiki:Quellon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), desired stronger ties with the rest of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), but he died in [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). His son, Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), reversed Quellon's reforms and instead desired independence for the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands). Over five years Balon constructed the Iron Fleet, which consisted of ships closer in size to galleys than the ironborn's smaller longships. Their decks were armed with scorpions and spitfires.(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
At the start of [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), the Iron Fleet [burned the Lannister fleet](wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet) at anchor at [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport).(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Balon's fleet was later defeated by Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) in a [sea battle off Fair Isle](wiki:Sea_battle_off_Fair_Isle),(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) however, which allowed the [Baratheons](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing) to invade the islands and force into submission Balon, would-be [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
According to [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), [Lord Captain of the Iron Fleet](wiki:Lord_Captain_of_the_Iron_Fleet), boys in the Iron Islands dream of having the privilege to one day row for the fleet.(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

When Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) declares himself [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands) and attacks the [north](wiki:North),(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) he sends his brother, Lord Captain [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), with the Iron Fleet to sail up the [Saltspear](wiki:Saltspear) and the [Fever River](wiki:Fever_River). From there the ironborn can march on [Moat Cailin](wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin).(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Balon then styles himself [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

After hearing that King Balon has died and that [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) has called a [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk), Victarion returns from Moat Cailin to the Iron Fleet, which has been in the reeds and willows of the Fever River. Nine-tenths of the Iron Fleet sails to [Nagga's Cradle](wiki:Nagga's_Cradle) in a column reaching back many leagues, losing a ship along the way to rough seas and fickle winds.(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
Victarion is offered as a candidate at the kingsmoot, but his brother [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) is eventually chosen as the new king at the kingsmoot.(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) [Baelor Blacktyde](wiki:Baelor_Blacktyde), [Lord of Blacktyde](wiki:Lord_of_Blacktyde), refuses to recognize Euron as king, but his [Nightflyer](wiki:Nightflyer) is captured by the Iron Fleet while attempting to depart Nagga's Cradle.(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
The fleet leads the other ships of the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) in the [taking of the Shields](wiki:Taking_of_the_Shields). Six ships are lost in the ensuing naval clash, including [Hardhand](wiki:Hardhand) of the Iron Fleet. King Euron commands Victarion to take the Iron Fleet to [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) and return with [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) and her [dragons](wiki:Dragons).(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

The small number of ironborn ships left behind in the Fever River are torched by the [Ryswells](wiki:House_Ryswell) and [Dustins](wiki:House_Dustin).(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) convinces the surviving ironborn raiders [under siege at Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin) to surrender to [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), but Ramsay has them executed afterward.(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
Meanwhile, the 93 remaining ships of the Iron Fleet sets sail for [Essos](wiki:Essos) from the [Shield Islands](wiki:Shield_Islands). During the voyage they capture a merchant ship, the [Noble Lady](wiki:Noble_Lady), taking its cargo and adding the ship to the fleet. In the [Redwyne Straits](wiki:Redwyne_Straits) and along the [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) coast they capture five other ships—three cogs, a galleas and a galley—making the Iron Fleet number ninety-nine. After taking on supplies at the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), Victarion splits the fleet into three squadrons and sails for Slaver's Bay, with the squadrons to reunite at the [Isle of Cedars](wiki:Isle_of_Cedars). At [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) Victarion learns that the Volantene fleet will soon sail against Daenerys. The Lord Captain orders his squadron to set sail immediately after they purchase food and water.(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
By the time the three squadrons meet at the tip of the [Isle of Cedars](wiki:Isle_of_Cedars), however, only forty-five ships have arrived. These include twenty-two of Victarion's squadron, fourteen of [Ralf the Limper](wiki:Ralf_the_Limper)'s bigger and slower squadron, which had sailed by [Lys](wiki:Lys), and only nine from [Red Ralf Stonehouse](wiki:Red_Ralf_Stonehouse)'s swift squadron, which had sailed the [corsair's road](wiki:Corsair's_road) near [Sothoryos](wiki:Sothoryos) and the [Basilisk Isles](wiki:Basilisk_Isles). Several straggle back, but some are lost to storms. The Iron Fleet captures nine additional ships, but these are merchant vessels and fishing boats not built for war.(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) The damaged [Shark](wiki:Shark_(ship)) is left at the Isle of Cedars to alert stragglers that the rest of the fleet has continued toward [Meereen](wiki:Meereen). The fleet numbers fifty-three to this point minus the Shark.(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
The fleet continues its voyage, capturing more cogs and galleys along the way. With [Moqorro](wiki:Moqorro), a [red priest](wiki:Red_priest) of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor), now aiding the fleet, the ships make faster progress and should reach [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) before the fleet of [Volantis](wiki:Volantis). The Iron Fleet currently number sixty-one.(wiki:Iron_Fleet.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)
