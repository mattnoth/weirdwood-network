## Appearances & Description

Gilwood, who is closer to fifty than forty, has a bushy mustache.(wiki:Gilwood_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) A heavy drinker of [wine](wiki:Wine), he has a red nose and red cheeks.(wiki:Gilwood_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) According to Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), Gilwood and his brothers are quarrelsome, each more grasping than the last.(wiki:Gilwood_Hunter.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Gilwood pins his [ermine](wiki:Ermine) cloak with a brooch of the arms of [House Hunter](wiki:House_Hunter).(wiki:Gilwood_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

Gilwood's father, Lord [Eon](wiki:Eon_Hunter), dies suddenly and Gilwood inherits the titles. The suddenness of his father's death leads his two younger brothers, Ser [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_Hunter) and Ser [Harlan](wiki:Harlan_Hunter), to claim that Gilwood had murdered Eon.(wiki:Gilwood_Hunter.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Despite his age, Gilwood is now known as Young Lord Hunter, having succeeded his father as the [Lord of Longbow Hall](wiki:Lord_of_Longbow_Hall).(wiki:Gilwood_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

Gilwood is one of the six [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant) who intend to remove Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) from power in the [Vale](wiki:Vale) and to protect their young lord, [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn). After signing a declaration at [Runestone](wiki:Runestone),(wiki:Gilwood_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) they gather six thousand men to the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon), demanding that Baelish give up his position of [Lord Protector of the Vale](wiki:Lord_Protector_of_the_Vale) and leave the province.(wiki:Gilwood_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Gilwood is the last of the Lords Declarant to arrive at the Gates of the Moon with his levies, contributing to the blockade put in place to starve Baelish out of the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:Gilwood_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

Accompanied by Ser [Lyn Corbray](wiki:Lyn_Corbray), the Lords Declarant are invited to the Eyrie for negotiations by Baelish; noticing Gilwood's fondness for [wine](wiki:Wine), [Alayne Stone](wiki:Sansa_Stark) ensures that his cup of stays full. Petyr has secretly bribed Corbray to draw his sword, [Lady Forlorn](wiki:Lady_Forlorn), at the meeting. Gilwood finds Lyn's actions shameful and asks for forgiveness for Corbray's display, which gives Petyr an edge in the negotiations. Petyr asks for a year to set things right in the Vale, which the lords agree to.(wiki:Gilwood_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)

Afterward, Petyr speculates to Alayne that Gilwood will be killed by his brothers, most likely by his youngest brother, Harlan, who is actually to blame for the death of their father Eon.(wiki:Gilwood_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)
