## Origins

In the wake of the death of Princess [Aerea Targaryen](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen), and the horrors he witnessed while assisting Grand Maester [Benifer](wiki:Benifer) in treating the diseased princess, Barth began the researches and investigations that would lead him to write the Unnatural History.(wiki:Dragons,_Wyrms,_and_Wyverns:_Their_Unnatural_History.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
The [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) condemned the book as "provocative but unsound".(wiki:Dragons,_Wyrms,_and_Wyverns:_Their_Unnatural_History.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) On account of Barth's alleged practice of the higher arts and his studies, King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) ordered the book expunged and destroyed during his reign, although some fragments have survived.(wiki:Dragons,_Wyrms,_and_Wyverns:_Their_Unnatural_History.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Dragons,_Wyrms,_and_Wyverns:_Their_Unnatural_History.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Dragons,_Wyrms,_and_Wyverns:_Their_Unnatural_History.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Dragons,_Wyrms,_and_Wyverns:_Their_Unnatural_History.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

While writing [his dragonlore](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister's_dragonlore), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) recalls that ten years previously he had acquired a fragment of Unnatural History that had eluded Baelor's book burnings. He doubts any of Barth's work had found its way across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea).(wiki:Dragons,_Wyrms,_and_Wyverns:_Their_Unnatural_History.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
While enslaved outside of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), Tyrion watches the [Yunkish](wiki:Yunkai) defensive preparations in the eventuality that [Drogon](wiki:Drogon) should return. They are angling their scorpions and mangonels upward at the sky. Tyrion knows that they are wasting their time, and his thoughts turn to Barth's words about the futility of attempting to slay a dragon via its gullet.(wiki:Dragons,_Wyrms,_and_Wyverns:_Their_Unnatural_History.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)
