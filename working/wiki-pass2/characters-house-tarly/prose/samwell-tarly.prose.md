## Origins

Samwell was the first child of [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly), [Lord of Horn Hill](wiki:Lord_of_Horn_Hill), and his wife, [Melessa Florent](wiki:Melessa_Florent). Randyll, regarded as one of the finest military commanders in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), did everything in his power to raise his son into what he deemed a proper heir, but Samwell frustrated him at every opportunity, resisting every effort made to change his nature. A dozen [masters-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms) failed to toughen him. Attempts such as beatings, starving him, dressing him in his mother's clothes and parading him, forcing him to sleep in chainmail, and being bathed in [aurochs](wiki:Aurochs) blood by two [Qartheen](wiki:Qarth) [warlocks](wiki:Warlocks) failed to raise his valor. He even wept to see a chicken slaughtered.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16) When Lord Tarly had Sam thrown into Horn Hill's pond to teach his son to swim, Ser [Hyle Hunt](wiki:Hyle_Hunt) had to rescue the boy.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Randyll insisted that young Sam attend him while holding court at [Horn Hill](wiki:Horn_Hill), and he brought Sam to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) during a visit to see Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) When he was ten,(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rawoiafsamwell_tarly.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Sam accompanied his father on a trip to the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor), but the [Redwyne](wiki:House_Redwyne) twins [Horas](wiki:Horas_Redwyne) and [Hobber](wiki:Hobber_Redwyne) bullied him and Lord Randyll brought him back home. Sam's mother revealed to him that Randyll had meant for Samwell to remain at the Arbor, to serve as Lord [Paxter Redwyne](wiki:Paxter_Redwyne)'s page and squire. Had Sam pleased Paxter, he would have been [betrothed](wiki:Betrothal) to his daughter, [Desmera](wiki:Desmera_Redwyne).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

After Lady Melessa bore her husband three girls in three consecutive years, Samwell's brother [Dickon](wiki:Dickon_Tarly) was born.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-17) When Sam and his mother sang "[The Song of the Seven](wiki:The_Song_of_the_Seven)" to the baby, Randyll reprimanded his wife and his heir.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Randyll also forbade Sam from sleeping in the same bed as his sisters.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Dickon showed all of the physical vigor that Samwell lacked, and Randyll's attentions turned to raising his more promising younger son to take Sam's place. For a time Sam was left to enjoy his music, food, and other soft pursuits, though Randyll forbade him from traveling to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) to study at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), horrified at the notion of a member of [House Tarly](wiki:House_Tarly) wearing a [maester](wiki:Maester)'s chain. To discourage the boy, Randyll had Sam chained and manacled to a wall for three days.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)

When Sam turned fifteen, his father bluntly told him that he was not worthy of their house's ancestral [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) [greatsword](wiki:Greatsword), [Heartsbane](wiki:Heartsbane), and that he must join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) and renounce his family name so that he would not stand in the way of Dickon's inheritance. Failing that, Randyll promised that Sam would suffer an unfortunate hunting accident on the morrow. Sam chose to take the black.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-18)

## Appearances & Description

Samwell is very fat, with dark hair, pale eyes, and a large [moon](wiki:Moon)-shaped face.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) initially estimates Sam to weigh 20 stone (127 kg/280 lbs).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

When he arrives at the [Wall](wiki:Wall), Sam wears a fur-trimmed surcoat embroidered with the striding huntsman of [House Tarly](wiki:House_Tarly),(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) and his great wood-and-leather shield also has the Tarly arms. Sam brings with him a padded doublet, boiled leather, mail, plate, and helm, but Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne) has them replaced with the black equipment of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) Sam later has an enormous black hooded cloak,(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) cap, surcoat, and chainmail hauberk.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Samwell is a timid craven frightened of many things, including high places,(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) [prostitutes](wiki:Prostitutes),(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and bloodshed.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) He dislikes martial activities, hunting,(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) and the cold,(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11) and he is a poor [rider](wiki:Horse).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Although he is insecure and awkward, Sam is observant,(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) intelligent, and liked by animals.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) He is fond of music, songs, and soft velvets,(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12) and he loves books and scrolls.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) The youth likes old items,(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) [kittens](wiki:Kitten),(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13) and dancing, even though he is clumsy.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14) Sam likes to eat,(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) enjoying [lemon cakes](wiki:Lemon_cake) and blueberry tarts.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Samwell is escorted to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) by men provided by his father, Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly), and the youth sees snow for the first time while in the [barrowlands](wiki:Barrowlands).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-19)

Sam arrives at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) shortly after [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) and is immediately mocked for his size and timid nature. Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne), the master-at-arms, joins in the mocking, nicknaming him "Ser Piggy" and doing nothing to prevent [the other recruits](wiki:Jon_Snow's_Night's_Watch_recruitment_group) from bullying Sam and hurting him during their training. Miserable, Sam finds a friend in Jon, with whom he shares his story. Jon takes pity on Sam and, to Alliser's annoyance, takes it upon himself to persuade (and threaten, when necessary) his fellow recruits to stop their abuse.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-20)

Samwell is not helped by Alliser's training and remains an incompetent warrior. Because of this, he is initially deemed not ready to take his vows and become a man of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) with Jon and his fellow recruits. Jon, fearful of what will happen to Sam once he is no longer able to protect him, persuades Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) to allow him to join the [stewards](wiki:Stewards), convincing him that despite his uselessness as a soldier, Sam's literacy and intelligence may be of great value to the Watch. Aemon agrees, and gives Samwell a position assisting him in the [rookery](wiki:Rookery) and library at [Maester Aemon's tower](wiki:Maester_Aemon's_tower), displacing [Chett](wiki:Chett).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3) Jon is disappointed at himself being named to the stewards instead of the [rangers](wiki:Rangers), but Sam points out that his friend is being groomed for command by serving under [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4)

Despite having been born and raised into the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), Samwell decides to take his Night's Watch vows with Jon at at a [weirwood grove](wiki:Weirwood_grove) in the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-5) He is able to impress Lord Commander Mormont by noting some of the odd details of the corpses of [Othor](wiki:Othor) and [Jafer Flowers](wiki:Jafer_Flowers). Jon saves Jeor's life when Othor rises as a [wight](wiki:Wight) and attacks the Lord Commander.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

Sam consoles Jon when they learn about the [arrest of Eddard Stark](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark), Jon's father, in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). When Alliser calls Jon a traitor's bastard in the common hall, Sam places his body between them to stop Jon from stabbing the master-at-arms.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) Sam informs Jon that his half brother, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), has marched south from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). Lord Commander Mormont gives a repaired [Longclaw](wiki:Longclaw) to Jon as a reward for having saved him from the wight. Sam travels to [Mole's Town](wiki:Mole's_Town) to purchase [garnets](wiki:Garnet) for the new pommel of the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

When Jon deserts in the middle of the night to join Robb in the south, Sam unsuccessfully tries to stop his friend from leaving Castle Black. After Jon leaves, Sam informs other mutual friends—[Grenn](wiki:Grenn), [Pyp](wiki:Pypar), [Toad](wiki:Todder), [Halder](wiki:Halder), and [Matthar](wiki:Matthar)—who ride off in pursuit. These black brothers convince Jon to return to the Wall and an anxious Sam.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Clash of Kings

In preparation for the [great ranging](wiki:Great_ranging) venturing [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall), Aemon sends Sam into the [vaults of Castle Black](wiki:Vaults_of_Castle_Black) to search for maps, and the youth spends hours looking at maps, scrolls, and books.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) Pyp, Halder, and Toad invite Sam to join them when they take Grenn to the [Mole's Town brothel](wiki:Mole's_Town_brothel), but Sam declines out of fear of the [whores](wiki:Whores).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)

The apprehensive Sam is placed in charge of the [ravens](wiki:Raven) as Lord Commander Mormont's aide for the great ranging.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-5) He gains some confidence, teaching the birds a few words and working on maps for Maester Aemon.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) When the expedition stays a night at [Craster's Keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep), Sam draws a map for the Lord Commander based on [Craster](wiki:Craster)'s descriptions of [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder)'s whereabouts. Samwell is befriended by [Gilly](wiki:Gilly), a pregnant [daughter-wife of Craster](wiki:Craster's_wives). Gilly is fearful that if she has a boy, Craster will sacrifice it to the [Others](wiki:Others). Samwell, unsure of what to do, turns to Jon for help, but there is nothing that Jon can do. (wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Ghost discovers a cache of [dragonglass](wiki:Dragonglass) weapons near the [Fist of the First Men](wiki:Fist_of_the_First_Men) and leads Jon to it.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Jon gives Sam a cracked warhorn, a spearhead, and a dozen arrowheads from the bundle.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Jon gives him a dragonglass dagger as well.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rawoiafsamwell_tarly.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

### A Storm of Swords

Sam practices his archery while the [great ranging](wiki:Great_ranging) remains encamped at the [Fist of the First Men](wiki:Fist_of_the_First_Men). A small group of mutineers led by [Chett](wiki:Chett) plot to kill [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont) and desert, with Chett planning to personally slit Sam's throat for having replaced him as Maester [Aemon's](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) steward.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasosprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) [Wights](wiki:Wight) begin attacking the Watch's camp, however, and Sam sends ravens to alert [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) and the [Shadow Tower](wiki:Shadow_Tower). When the Watch loses the [fight at the Fist](wiki:Fight_at_the_Fist), Sam releases the rest of the birds but forgets to include messages with them.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

Sam escapes with a group of about fifty survivors led by [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont). The march is hard on Sam, but [Grenn](wiki:Grenn) encourages him not to give up and [Small Paul](wiki:Small_Paul) begins carrying the youth. The trio lag behind and become separated from the other survivors. Paul is attacked and killed by an [Other](wiki:Other), but the weight of his body disarms it as he dies. Samwell, who still carries the dagger given to him by [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), blindly stabs the Other, which dies instantaneously from the [dragonglass](wiki:Dragonglass) blade.

Sam and Grenn rendezvous with the remaining survivors at [Craster's Keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep). Killing the Other has earned Samwell the nickname "Sam the Slayer", but many members of the Night's Watch use it mockingly as they do not believe Sam's feat. Grenn respects the name, however. Lord Commander Mormont discusses the obsidian weaponry with Sam. [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) gives birth to [a son](wiki:Monster), and Sam angers [Craster](wiki:Craster) by suggesting that the Watch could take Gilly and the child with them.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Violence erupts in the keep, with some Night's Watch men believing that Craster is holding out on the food he provided for the Watch, and others harboring mutinous intentions toward Lord Commander Mormont. After Craster is murdered and Jeor is stabbed during the [mutiny at Craster's Keep](wiki:Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep), Grenn slaps and shouts at Sam in an attempt to rouse the shocked youth. Sam is unresponsive, however, so Grenn and others leave him behind.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Jeor gives Sam his dying wish, that his son, the exiled Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont), join the Watch.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

Two of Craster's older wives encourage Sam to flee with Gilly and her child.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) They make it as far as a village Sam believes to be [Whitetree](wiki:Whitetree). Sam prays before the village's [heart tree](wiki:Heart_tree) and then sings the "[The Song of the Seven](wiki:The_Song_of_the_Seven)" to Gilly and her baby in the longhall. A reanimated Small Paul attacks them in the night. Sam fights it off and stabs it with the dragonglass dagger, but the blade shatters on Paul's iron mail. Sam desperately trikes the wight with a piece of charred wood, which catches the dead man's face on fire, destroying it. Sam and Gilly are then beset by at least a score of wights near the [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood). Hundreds of ravens suddenly attack the wights, however, and a mysterious rider helps the humans escape on his giant [elk](wiki:Elk).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

Sam, Gilly, and her son are led back to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) by the rider, [Coldhands](wiki:Coldhands), who has informed them that there will be one in the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort) that must be sent to him. Entering the Nightfort through the ruined castle's hidden [Black Gate](wiki:Black_Gate), Samwell encounters [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark), along with [Hodor](wiki:Hodor), and [Meera](wiki:Meera_Reed) and [Jojen Reed](wiki:Jojen_Reed). Remembering that Bran is Jon Snow's brother, Sam agrees to keep knowledge of Bran's survival a secret. Sam recites the vows of the Night's Watch at the Black Gate, and he leads Bran's party to Coldhands.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

After leaving the Nightfort on the south side of the Wall, Sam and Gilly follow a track near [Deep Lake](wiki:Deep_Lake) and [Queensgate](wiki:Queensgate). They are joined by Ser [Denys Mallister](wiki:Denys_Mallister) and [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh)'s party, also en route to [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) from the [Shadow Tower](wiki:Shadow_Tower).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Sam weeps when he is reunited with [Dywen](wiki:Dywen), [Giant](wiki:Bedwyck), and [Dolorous Edd](wiki:Eddison_Tollett). [Pypar](wiki:Pypar) cheers when he sees Sam, and Grenn gives him a fierce hug. Sam suggests to Jon that Gilly and her son could be sent to live with his family at [Horn Hill](wiki:Horn_Hill), lying that the baby was fathered by him. Sam reluctantly does not tell Jon about having met Bran.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)

In the aftermath of the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall), [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) and his [red priestess](wiki:Red_priest), [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), ask Sam about his having killed the Other, and the king tells Sam that he wants to eventually be shown the Black Gate below the Nightfort. The blind Maester Aemon has Sam describe Stannis's sword, [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer), and Sam later admits to Aemon that the blade emits no heat.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

Stannis urges the swift [election](wiki:Election) of a new Lord Commander and threatens to name one himself if none can achieve the required two-thirds majority. In the voting, [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt) gains ground on the leading candidates, [Cotter Pyke](wiki:Cotter_Pyke) and Denys Mallister. Believing Janos would be a disastrous Lord Commander and inspired by Aemon, Sam approaches Cotter and Denys independently. He lies to each regarding Stannis's intentions to name the other to the office, thus convincing them both to support Jon Snow as a compromise candidate.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) When a bird emerges from the selection kettle and lands on Jon's shoulder during the next choosing, Sam shouts that it is [Mormont's raven](wiki:Mormont's_raven); the Night's Watch then enthusiastically choose Jon as the new Lord Commander. Grenn and Pyp tell Jon that Sam was behind Jon's selection, but Sam insists he had nothing to do with the raven.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Samwell searches the [vaults of Castle Black](wiki:Vaults_of_Castle_Black) for information about the [Others](wiki:Others).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4) One of Jon Snow's first acts as Lord Commander is to send Sam south to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), where he might study to become Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I))'s successor. Jon also sends [Dareon](wiki:Dareon), whom he appoints as [a recruiter](wiki:Wandering_crow) for the Night's Watch, as well as Aemon and the [infant son](wiki:Aemon_Steelsong) of [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder), so as to deprive [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) of the chance to sacrifice them for their king's blood. He also sends [Gilly](wiki:Gilly), separating her from [her own child](wiki:Monster), so that she might nurse Rayder's son.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5)(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) Maester Aemon falls ill on the voyage, however, and stopping in [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) they are forced to spend their coin on an inn and a healer. Aemon's condition worsens, though he briefly wakes and wishes to hear the stories of [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen)'s [dragons](wiki:Dragon) that are spreading throughout the city, having the revelation that Daenerys may be the true fulfillment of the prophecy of [the prince that was promised](wiki:The_prince_that_was_promised).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Samwell urges Dareon to sing at the local taverns to earn money so that they might buy passage on a ship to Oldtown, but Dareon spends most of what he earns on wine and trade from the city's whores. Sam confronts Dareon in a brothel and accuses him of breaking his vows, and Dareon responds with his intention to abandon them and desert the Night's Watch, resulting in a fight whereupon Sam is pulled off Dareon and thrown into a canal. He is pulled out by [Xhondo](wiki:Xhondo), who heard him speaking of dragons and had seen them in [Qarth](wiki:Qarth).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Xhondo offers them passage on his ship, the *[Cinnamon Wind](wiki:Cinnamon_Wind)*, in exchange for work and for some of the last of their possessions.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Dareon remains behind and is eventually killed by [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark).(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

Maester Aemon dies early in the journey from Braavos to Oldtown, and in mourning for his death Gilly briefly becomes a lover to Samwell. She names Mance's child "[Aemon Steelsong](wiki:Aemon_Steelsong)" after Maester Aemon,(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) and it is planned that upon landing at Oldtown she will be sent to Sam's childhood home of [Horn Hill](wiki:Horn_Hill) with the story that her child is a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) fathered by him.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-6)

Samwell continues to the Citadel, where he goes to the [Seneschal's Court](wiki:Seneschal's_Court) to speak with the [Seneschal](wiki:Seneschal). Sam is forced to wait a long while before he will be admitted. When his patience starts to run thin, he is approached by the acolyte [Alleras](wiki:Alleras), who brings him to Archmaester [Marwyn](wiki:Marwyn) and [Leo Tyrell](wiki:Leo_Tyrell_(son_of_Moryn)) at the [Ravenry](wiki:Ravenry). Upon hearing Sam's story, Marwyn orders Sam to keep Aemon's prophecies and Daenerys's dragons a secret from the other [archmaesters](wiki:Archmaester). Marwyn immediately departs the city with the stated intention of going to Daenerys and becoming her maester. Alleras informs Sam that Marwyn's [glass candle](wiki:Glass_candle) had anticipated Sam's arrival, and [Pate](wiki:Pate_(novice)) gives Sam a cell near Archmaester [Walgrave](wiki:Walgrave)'s chamber.(wiki:Samwell_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
