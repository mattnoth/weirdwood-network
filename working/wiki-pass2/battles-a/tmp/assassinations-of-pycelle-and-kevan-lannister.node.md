## Identity

The Assassinations of Pycelle and Kevan Lannister were the murders of Grand Maester Pycelle and Ser Kevan Lannister, Lord Regent, by Varys and his "little birds" in 300 AC at the Red Keep in King's Landing. (wiki:Assassinations_of_Pycelle_and_Kevan_Lannister)

## Origins

Kevan Lannister had been serving as Lord Regent and was successfully stabilizing the realm after Cersei's imprisonment by the Faith. Varys returned to the Red Keep specifically to prevent this stabilization, as Kevan's competent rule threatened the plans Varys had laid for a Targaryen restoration. (wiki:Assassinations_of_Pycelle_and_Kevan_Lannister)

## Narrative Arc

Varys's child spies, the "little birds," killed Grand Maester Pycelle in his chambers. Varys then lured Kevan Lannister to the Grand Maester's rooms with a false message and shot him with a crossbow. As Kevan lay dying, Varys explained that he bore Kevan no personal ill will but that Kevan was too competent — his steady hand was undoing the chaos that Cersei's misrule had created, and Varys needed the realm unstable for Aegon Targaryen's invasion to succeed. The assassinations are the final scene of A Dance with Dragons. (wiki:Assassinations_of_Pycelle_and_Kevan_Lannister)
