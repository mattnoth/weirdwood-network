## Origins

The legendary [Bloodstone Emperor](wiki:Bloodstone_Emperor), the last [God-Emperor](wiki:God-Emperor) of the equally mythic [Great Empire of the Dawn](wiki:Great_Empire_of_the_Dawn), is said to have practiced necromancy, among other forbidden actions that ushered in the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night).(wiki:Necromancy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)
According to semi-canon sources, [Morgon Banefort](wiki:Morgon_Banefort), the last [Hooded King](wiki:Hooded_King),(wiki:Necromancy.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) was said to have been a powerful necromancer who cursed his vanquishers as he died, claiming he would return from the grave. In response, King [Loreon I Lannister](wiki:Loreon_I_Lannister) had Morgon's body cut into pieces and fed to his [lions](wiki:Lion). Two years later the lions broke free within [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), slaying three of Loreon's sons.(wiki:Necromancy.cite_ref-3)
[Dagon Drumm](wiki:Dagon_Drumm), a legendary [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) raider during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), was known as a necromancer.(wiki:Necromancy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
Necromancy is rumored to be practiced in [Qohor](wiki:Qohor), along with divination and [blood magic](wiki:Blood_magic).(wiki:Necromancy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) [Nefer](wiki:Nefer), the last remaining city of the realm of [N'ghai](wiki:N'ghai) is known as a haunt of necromancers.(wiki:Necromancy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_east_of_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Necromancers are also found in [Asshai](wiki:Asshai), where no practice - however depraved - is forbidden and may be carried out with impunity.(wiki:Necromancy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_asshai.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Necromancers were among the scoundrels who lived in the lawless shadow city of [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) west of the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne), prior to its subjugation by [triarchs](wiki:Triarch_of_Volantis).(wiki:Necromancy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
Some members of [House Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower) are rumored to have dabbled in necromancy.(wiki:Necromancy.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn), a former maester and now a member of the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions), is rumored by the servants at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) to practice black magic and necromancy.(wiki:Necromancy.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) states that the [Others](wiki:Others) use necromancy to resurrect the dead as [wights](wiki:Wights).(wiki:Necromancy.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Qyburn confirms he was expelled from the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), stripped of his chain and his position as a [maester](wiki:Maester), for his experiments in necromancy and subjecting living people to vivisection.(wiki:Necromancy.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
