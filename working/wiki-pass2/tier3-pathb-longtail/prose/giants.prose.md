## Appearances & Description

Giants generally grow between ten(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) to fourteen feet tall.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) They are capable of great feats of strength, such as wrenching the bars of an iron gate apart.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) They are covered by a shaggy pelt of fur, that is thicker below their waists than above. The fur of older giants becomes grey and streaked with white. Their heads are thrust forward from their shoulder blades. They have squashed-in faces with square teeth, and tiny eyes amidst folds of horny flesh. Their eyesight is poor, and they snuffle constantly, smelling as much as they see.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Giants have sloped chests, and their lower torsos are about half again as wide as their upper torsos. Their arms hang lower than a man's, while their legs are shorter than their arms, ending in splayed and horny feet that need no shoes even in the coldest weather.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) According to [Osha](wiki:Osha), female giants look similar to males, both being bearded.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

## Culture

According to the stories of [Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan), a servant at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), giants are outsized men who live in enormous castles, wield large swords, and wear huge boots.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) Nan told the children of Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) that [wildlings](wiki:Free_Folk) consort with giants and [ghouls](wiki:Ghouls), (wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) and she also told the children about a man who escaped the castle of evil giants only to fall victim to the [Others](wiki:Others).(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Racok9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Men believe that giants eat human flesh, and Nan claims that giants mix blood into their porridge and eat bulls whole.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
While hostile to men, giants are a shy folk(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) who use little technology. They wield huge clubs that are often no more than logs, though some tie boulders to the end to make crude mauls or use huge stone axes.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Giants ride [mammoths](wiki:Mammoth) as steeds(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) and are protective of the animals.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Giants speak the [Old Tongue](wiki:Old_Tongue) of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men),(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) referring to [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) as woh dak nag gran, or "little squirrel people".(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Radwd13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Giants do not have kings, but [Mag Mar Tun Doh Weg](wiki:Mag_Mar_Tun_Doh_Weg) is considered their greatest living warrior.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) In contrast to stories of bloodthirsty giants, [Wun Weg Wun Dar Wun](wiki:Wun_Weg_Wun_Dar_Wun) is a vegetarian.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)
Giants in the [north](wiki:North) buried their dead, according to Maester [Kennet](wiki:Kennet_(maester))'s [Passages of the Dead](wiki:Passages_of_the_Dead),(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) and some believe a [King of the Giants](wiki:King_of_the_Giants) is buried in the [Great Barrow](wiki:Great_Barrow) instead of the [First King](wiki:First_King).(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Giants roam freely, and reside beneath tall trees or in caverns.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Scholars believe giants probably lived in the caverns beneath [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
[Thenns](wiki:Thenns) interact more with giants than other [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) tribes,(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) sharing usage of the [Old Tongue](wiki:Old_Tongue). Some free folk believe that particularly large men in the [north](wiki:North) have giant blood, but there have been no recorded incidents of crossbreeding.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Some have theorized that the ancient [mazemakers](wiki:Mazemakers) of [Lorath](wiki:Lorath)(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) or the [stoneborn](wiki:Stoneborn) of [Skagos](wiki:Skagos)(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_stoneborn_of_skagos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) descend from giants. There is a song about giants sung by free folk, "[The Last of the Giants](wiki:The_Last_of_the_Giants)".(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)
[House Umber](wiki:House_Umber) of the [Last Hearth](wiki:Last_Hearth) depicts a roaring giant in shattered chains in their sigil.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) The tallest peak in the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) is known as the [Giant's Lance](wiki:Giant's_Lance).(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rawoiafgiant.27s_lance.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
The [demon](wiki:Demon) mother of [ice giants](wiki:Ice_giants) is the cause of shimmering lights in the [Shivering Sea](wiki:Shivering_Sea) in sailors' stories.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_shivering_sea.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

## Origins

### Essos[ ]

Giants once lived in parts of [Essos](wiki:Essos); bones of giants have been found in caves beneath the [Hills of Norvos](wiki:Hills_of_Norvos)(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) and in the [Bone Mountains](wiki:Bone_Mountains).(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) A circle of standing stones in the [Velvet Hills](wiki:Velvet_Hills) is said to have been raised by giants.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Giants also may have once lived in [Ib](wiki:Ib).(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
The [Jhogwin](wiki:Jhogwin), said to be twice as large as the giants of Westeros, once lived in the [Realm of Jhogwin](wiki:Realm_of_Jhogwin) in the [Krazaaj Zasqa](wiki:Krazaaj_Zasqa). The last of these massive giants were slain by the [Jogos Nhai](wiki:Jogos_Nhai).(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)
The [Mazemakers](wiki:Mazemakers) of [Lorath](wiki:Lorath) are an extinct race, whose large bones have led some to suggest they were born of the unions of human men and female giants.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)
[Belicho](wiki:Belicho), a [triarch of Volantis](wiki:Triarch_of_Volantis), was eaten by giants.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) [Yezzan zo Qaggaz](wiki:Yezzan_zo_Qaggaz), one of the [Wise Masters](wiki:Wise_Masters) of [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai), is said to have once owned a giant in his collection of [slave](wiki:Slave) grotesques.(wiki:Giants.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
