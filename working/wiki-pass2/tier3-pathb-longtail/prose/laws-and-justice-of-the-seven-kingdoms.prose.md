## Origins

At the start of [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) appointed [Triston Massey](wiki:Triston_Massey) as the first [master of laws](wiki:Master_of_laws).(wiki:Laws_and_justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) The victorious King Aegon I decided to keep the laws of each of the six conquered kingdoms as they had been before the Conquest. Instead, while he traveled through his realm on his [royal progresses](wiki:Royal_progress), Aegon brought six [maesters](wiki:Maester) with him to advise him on the local laws and traditions.(wiki:Laws_and_justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
In [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) began work to create the first unified [code of law](wiki:Books_of_Law) for the six provinces he ruled over, codifying, organizing, and reforming all the laws of the kingdom. In this task he was assisted by Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth), Grand Maester [Benifer](wiki:Benifer), Lord [Albin Massey](wiki:Albin_Massey), and Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen). Gathering, organizing, and studying the existing laws alone already took them two years. The reforms in total would take decades.(wiki:Laws_and_justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
During the first 187 years of Targaryen rule, [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) remained an independent realm. When Dorne was finally united with the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), one of the conditions was that "Dornish law would always rule in Dorne."(wiki:Laws_and_justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) As per the agreement between King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) and Prince [Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell), the lords of Dorne were allowed significant rights and privileges that the other great houses of the Targaryen kingdom did not. In addition to the use of their royal title and the autonomy to maintain their own laws, the [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) kept the right to assess and gather the taxes they owed the Iron Throne, with the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) having irregular oversight. Other concessions made were similar, causing dissatisfaction among the other nobles of the realm.(wiki:Laws_and_justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
