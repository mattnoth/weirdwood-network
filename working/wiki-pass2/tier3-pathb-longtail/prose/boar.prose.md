## Culture

The boar appears on the sigils of both Houses [Crakehall](wiki:House_Crakehall) and [Vikary](wiki:House_Vikary).(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-10) Ser [Lyle Crakehall](wiki:Lyle_Crakehall) is known as Strongboar.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
Boar [hunting](wiki:Hunting) is a common activity among the male nobility of Westeros. It is not considered to be a sport for ladies, who generally hawk instead.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Racok19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) A hunting party usually includes men to flush the boar from its lair, dogs to gather the prey, and men on horses equipped with boarspears to kill the [animal](wiki:Animal).(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Racok19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) Hunted boar may be served at a feast, roasted, often with its head displayed and an apple in its mouth.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
Boar baiting is a pastime in Westeros.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
[Skinchangers](wiki:Skinchanger) can inhabit the body of boars.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Radwd0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

## Origins

[Crake the Boarkiller](wiki:Crake_the_Boarkiller) is a legendary figure from the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes) that founded [House Crakehall](wiki:House_Crakehall).(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf76.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
In [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Lord [Alaric Stark](wiki:Alaric_Stark) went hunting elk and wild boar in the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood) with his guest, Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen).(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), [Trystane Truefyre](wiki:Trystane_Truefyre) attempted to appeal to [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) by granting them the right to hunt rabbits, hares, and [deer](wiki:Deer) in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), but he forbade them from hunting elk or boar in the royal forest.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
Boar is served during the feast of the [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls).(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
[Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) was [knighted](wiki:Knight) at the age of sixteen by King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) after participating in a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). During the feast following the tournament, Barristan ate boar prepared with [dragon peppers](wiki:Dragon_pepper).(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) is a great hunter of boar, reportedly having killed a thousand of them.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

During his stay at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) wants to have wild boar for a feast. He goes in the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood) to hunt with people from his retinue, as well as people from Winterfell.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
King Robert hunts in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), chasing a white hart. Disappointed to find that [wolves](wiki:Wolves) have killed his prey before him, Robert later hears talk of a monstrous boar living deeper in the forest.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Set on the idea of killing the animal himself, Robert commands everyone to not intervene, but he is intoxicated due to being served [strongwine](wiki:Strongwine) by his [squire](wiki:Squire), [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister), and is mortally gored in the process.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) On his deathbed, Robert asks his friend, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), to serve the boar during his funeral feast.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)
After Robert's death, one rumor spreading in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) says that he had been killed by a boar while hunting, while another story says that he had died eating a boar, stuffing himself so full that he ruptured.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
When the new king, [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon), is serving justice, one case he hears is that of a singer accused of ridiculing the late king Robert. The song is about Robert fighting a pig, and while some verses appear to be talking about the boar that killed him, others sound as if the pig is supposed to be Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister). Joffrey decides that the singer may keep either his fingers or his tongue.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

In King's Landing, Cersei confesses to her brother [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) how she had made certain Robert's squire Lancel gave him strongwine while hunting the boar, leading to his death. She tells Tyrion that the boar was served at Robert's funeral feast with [mushrooms](wiki:Mushroom_(fungi)) and apples, and tasted like triumph.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)
During the [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast) at Winterfell hosted by Prince [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark), he sends a dish with peppered boar to the boisterous [Umbers](wiki:House_Umber).(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)
While pretending to be the servant Nan at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) brings food to Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), including boar.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Roast boar is served at the wedding feast of Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) and Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) at the [Fingers](wiki:Drearfort).(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Cersei, [regent](wiki:Regent) for King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), has become very fond of eating boar since Robert's death.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

During the reopening of the [fighting pits of Meereen](wiki:Fighting_pits_of_Meereen), [Barsena Blackhair](wiki:Barsena_Blackhair) fights a boar, with a dagger as her only weapon. Despite her quickness and skill, the boar manages to rip her open. Moments later [Drogon](wiki:Drogon) descends into [Daznak's Pit](wiki:Daznak's_Pit), kills the boar, and begins to devour both corpses.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)
Some of the [Brazen Beasts](wiki:Brazen_Beasts) wear boar masks in [Mereen](wiki:Mereen).(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1)
[Borroq](wiki:Borroq), a [skinchanger](wiki:Skinchanger) of the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk), can inhabit the body of a boar(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Radwd0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) as large as a bull with tusks as long as swords.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Because his boar does not get along with [Ghost](wiki:Ghost), the [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf) of [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), Borroq takes up residence in an ancient tomb near [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black)'s lichyard, where the boar roots amongst the graves.(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

### The Winds of Winter[ ]

[Izembaro](wiki:Izembaro)'s troupe of [mummers](wiki:Mummer) performs [The Bloody Hand](wiki:The_Bloody_Hand) at the [Gate](wiki:Gate_(playhouse)) for an envoy from Westeros, Ser [Harys Swyft](wiki:Harys_Swyft). In the play, [Big Brusco](wiki:Big_Brusco) plays the role of a [demonic](wiki:Demons) boar that kills a [fat king](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Boar.cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Bmercy_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Cmercy.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)
