## Culture

The Good Masters belong to the great [slaver](wiki:Slaver) families of [Astapor](wiki:Astapor). They light [silk](wiki:Silk) lanterns on the terraces of their [pyramids](wiki:Pyramid),(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) and they wear [tokars](wiki:Tokar).(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
The Good Masters have a wooden platform with tortured slaves on display at the [Plaza of Punishment](wiki:Plaza_of_Punishment) when new slaves first arrive in the city.(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
The Good Masters sell [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied), the [eunuch](wiki:Eunuch) soldiers of Astapor,(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) and do not allow the slaves to retain their given names.(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) advises [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) to contact the Good Masters in order to obtain an army of [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied). Daenerys is shown the [eunuch](wiki:Eunuch) soldiers by [Kraznys mo Nakloz](wiki:Kraznys_mo_Nakloz) and his [slave](wiki:Slave) interpreter, [Missandei](wiki:Missandei).(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Daenerys meets with eight of the Good Masters atop a pyramid, and she agrees to buy all Unsullied in exchange for her three ships and [Drogon](wiki:Drogon).(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) When the transaction is completed in the [Plaza of Punishment](wiki:Plaza_of_Punishment), however, Daenerys turns all three of her [dragons](wiki:Dragon) against the Good Masters and orders her newly-bought Unsullied to kill the slavers during the [fall of Astapor](wiki:Fall_of_Astapor).(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
Daenerys frees the slaves of Astapor and leaves [a council of former slaves](wiki:Ruling_council_of_Astapor) to rule the city when she departs for [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai).(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Daenerys gives [wine](wiki:Wine) from the cellars of the Good Masters to [Mero](wiki:Mero), captain of the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons). The sellswords are then too drunk to fight in the [battle near Yunkai](wiki:Battle_near_Yunkai).(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
Daenerys continues north and [conquers Meereen](wiki:Siege_of_Meereen). The envoy [Ghael](wiki:Ghael) travels to [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) and informs Daenerys that [Cleon](wiki:Cleon) overthrew the Astapori council for allegedly plotting to restore the Good Masters to power.(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Cleon the Butcher King restores slavery to Astapor, with the former masters now enslaved by former slaves.(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
The Yunkai'i are successful in their [siege of Astapor](wiki:Siege_of_Astapor), during which Cleon is killed by his own soldiers.(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
According to the peace treaty between Meereen and Yunkai, Astapor is to be restored in its old form as a slaver city.(wiki:Good_Masters.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
