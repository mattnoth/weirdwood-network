## Origins

### Foundation

The origins of Myr are disputed. There is some evidence that a now-vanished people inhabited the area during the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age) and the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night). Modern Myr was founded by merchant adventurers from the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) after they conquered a walled [Andal](wiki:Andal) town.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)

### After the Doom

Following the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) took Myr with an army and [Lys](wiki:Lys) with a fleet during the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood). For two generations all three cities were ruled from within the [Black Walls](wiki:Black_Wall) of Volantis, but that ended when the Volantene [tigers](wiki:Triarch_of_Volantis) tried to conquer [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh). Lys and Myr rebelled, and [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) came into the war on the Tyroshi side. The [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King), [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon), marched into the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands) and defeated a Volantene host threatening Myr. Lord [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) flew forth from [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) on [Balerion the Black Dread](wiki:Balerion) and burned a Volantene fleet at Lys.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

In [12 AC](wiki:12_AC) during the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), the [Wyl of Wyl](wiki:Wyl_of_Wyl) sold [Alys Oakheart](wiki:Alys_Oakheart) and her handmaids to a Myrish [slaver](wiki:Slaver).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) Myrish and Volantene builders supervised the construction of the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) during the reign of King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) Ser [Howard Bullock](wiki:Howard_Bullock) died in Myr in [53 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) after falling from his horse.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) Ser [Orryn Baratheon](wiki:Orryn_Baratheon) joined the [Maiden's Men](wiki:Maiden's_Men) in Myr after being forced to leave [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh),(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1) and Ser [Borys Baratheon](wiki:Borys_Baratheon) spent some time in Myr and Volantis before joining the [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Jaehaerys_I)).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) [Roy Connington](wiki:Roy_Connington) was stabbed to death in a Myrish gambling den.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-1)

Myrish [sellsails](wiki:Sellsails) joined [Morion Martell](wiki:Morion_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), in the [Fourth Dornish War](wiki:Fourth_Dornish_War), but King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) crushed them in the [Sea of Dorne](wiki:Sea_of_Dorne).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-2) During the [Myrish Bloodbath](wiki:Myrish_Bloodbath) of [91](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)–[92 AC](wiki:92_AC), rival factions fought within the Free City. The losing faction tried to claim the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), but they were forced to flee by the [Archon of Tyrosh](wiki:Archon_of_Tyrosh) and pirate kings. The Myrish outlaws then invaded eastern [Tarth](wiki:Tarth) in the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands). After a Myrman killed Prince [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), the outlaws were destroyed by Prince [Baelon Targaryen](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), Lord [Cameron Tarth](wiki:Cameron_Tarth), and Lord [Boremund Baratheon](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-3) [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) visited Myr during his voyages.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

### The Triarchy and the Daughters' War

Myr combined its strength with [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) and defeated [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) in the [Battle of the Borderland](wiki:Battle_of_the_Borderland). They united, forming the [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy) in [96 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) The Myrish prince-admiral [Craghas Drahar](wiki:Craghas_Drahar) led the Triarchy's conquest of the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones). The Triarchy increased the toll in the Stepstones for passing ships, and battled for the Stepstones against the alliance of Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) and Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), starting in [106 AC](wiki:106_AC), continuing beyond [115 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-1)

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the [High Council of the Triarchy](wiki:High_Council_of_the_Triarchy) accepted Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister)'s offer of alliance with the [greens](wiki:Greens), pledging to support to King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) against his half-sister, [Rhaenyra](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen). Myrish soldiers attacked [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) during the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet), and Myrmen killed Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) with their crossbows. Since most of the Triarchy's casualties during the battle were from Myrish and Tyroshi ships, the widows of those cities blamed Sharako for protecting his own Lysene ships.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

[Black Trombo](wiki:Black_Trombo), a Myrish sellsword in the service of Lord [Vance](wiki:House_Vance_of_Wayfarer's_Rest) and the [blacks](wiki:Blacks),(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) has been suggested as the killer of Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)) during the [Second Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:Second_Battle_of_Tumbleton).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) Tyland Lannister unsuccessfully sought to hire sellswords in Myr for the greens.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

The [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War) broke out after the murder of Sharako. Although the admiral was killed in a personal dispute over the [Black Swan](wiki:Johanna_Swann), it was rumored that he had been assassinated by Myr. Tyrosh claimed the Stepstones when Lys and Myr began fighting, but [Racallio Ryndoon](wiki:Racallio_Ryndoon) claimed the islands for himself as [King of the Narrow Sea](wiki:King_of_the_Narrow_Sea). An alliance of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), [Lorath](wiki:Lorath), and [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) also joined the war.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) When Tyrosh appear close to defeating Racallio, Lys and Myr made peace and attacked Tyrosh, forcing the [Archon](wiki:Archon_of_Tyrosh) to recall his men from the Stepstones.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) The Daughters' War eventually ended with the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands) divided between Lys, Myr, and Tyrosh, although the Myrmen suffered the greatest losses in the arrangement. Most of the Stepstones were claimed by [Dorne](wiki:Dorne).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) Daughters from ancient houses of Myr attended the [Maiden's Day Ball](wiki:Maiden's_Day_Ball).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-1) Myrish ships frequented [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-1)

## Appearances & Description

### Location and Layout

Walled Myr is on the fertile "heel" of [Essos](wiki:Essos), where an ancient [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) [dragonroad](wiki:Dragonroad) on the mainland meets the [Sea of Myrth](wiki:Sea_of_Myrth). Temples and shrines for many gods can be found along the waterfronts and streets. [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) is worshiped at a [red temple](wiki:Red_temple) in Myr, for instance.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Myrish ships have sailed the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) for hundreds of years.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

### People

Some [maesters](wiki:Maester) believe that Myrmen are descended from the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar), as many of the Myrmen share their olive skin and dark hair, but this theory is disputed.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Myrmen speak [a dialect](wiki:Myrish_language)(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) of [bastard Valyrian](wiki:Bastard_Valyrian),(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue) when spoken with a Myrish accent can sound sultry.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) The mercantile port's inhabitants have no established faith.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Myr is ruled by a conclave of [magisters](wiki:Magister), chosen from amongst the most wealthy and noble men of the city.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) The magisters give lavishly to passing [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) *[khals](wiki:Khal)* to prevent their *[khalasars](wiki:Khalasar)* from sacking the city.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

A [slave](wiki:Slave) city, Myr has three slaves for every freeborn. The slaves in Myr are collared and branded.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_pentos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) There are those in the city who have [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied) in their service.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

The Myrmen fight often with [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) and [Lys](wiki:Lys) over the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands), though they will not risk the lives of their citizens in this, preferring to hire [free companies](wiki:Mercenaries) instead.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) Myrmen often use stilettos(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and crossbows(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rasos16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) and [bravos](wiki:Bravo) can be found in Myr as well.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Ragot10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Myrish [pirates](wiki:Pirate) often have nests in the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

"[The Seasons of My Love](wiki:The_Seasons_of_My_Love)" is a Myrish song.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

### Economy

Myr is considered to be among the most advanced of the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), and is famed for its arts and learning.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_myr.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Their main exports are finished goods, such as carpets and lace.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) The best glass and lenses, and telescopes called [Myrish eyes](wiki:Myrish_eye), come from Myr,(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) and lenscrafters from Myr are considered to be without equal in all the world.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) [Myrish lenses](wiki:Myrish_lens) are known to have been used in [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell)(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) and [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

The artisans of Myr, many born as [slaves](wiki:Slaves), are greatly renowned. ## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

In the Western Market of [Vaes Dothrak](wiki:Vaes_Dothrak), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) smells garlic and roast pepper in the air and is reminded days long gone in the alleys of Myr and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), which brings a fond smile to her face. She also see slaves carrying bolts of intricate Myrish lace.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Ragot36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0)

Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) uses a bronze Myrish [far-eye](wiki:Far-eye) in his tower at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)

### A Clash of Kings

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) provides King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) with a Myrish crossbow which can fire three quarrels at once.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) reveals to Tyrion that he became a [eunuch](wiki:Eunuch) while he was part of a company of [mummers](wiki:Mummers) in Myr.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-1)

[Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) hires [mercenary](wiki:Mercenary) sellswords and sellsails from Myr, including [Morosh the Myrman](wiki:Morosh_the_Myrman).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0)(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0) The Myrish ships are slower than war galleys of the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) and have at most eighty oars.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0) During the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), many of Stannis's Myrmen land on the southern shore of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0)

Daenerys sees the Myrish galley *[Silken Spirit](wiki:Silken_Spirit)* in the [port of Qarth](wiki:Port_of_Qarth).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0)

### A Storm of Swords

When [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) and [Lys](wiki:Lys) are on the verge of war, they both attempt to make Myr their ally.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0)

Despite his protests that he is just a soldier, a Myrish crossbowman is hanged by the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) after they defeat [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions) at a [burning septry](wiki:Battle_at_the_burning_septry).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) [Thoros of Myr](wiki:Thoros_of_Myr), a [red priest](wiki:Red_priest) with the brotherhood, explains to [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) that he had been sent to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in an unsuccessful attempt to convert King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

[Daario Naharis](wiki:Daario_Naharis) of the [Stormcrows](wiki:Stormcrows) wields a Myrish stiletto and a [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) *[arakh](wiki:Arakh)* with matching hilts.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) befriends [Taena of Myr](wiki:Taena_of_Myr), the wife of Lord [Orton Merryweather](wiki:Orton_Merryweather).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

Myr is on the verge of war with Tyrosh and Lys.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0) The Myrmen contract the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) and are then rumored to be supporting Tyrosh against Lys,(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0) but the sellswords break their contract unexpectedly(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-1) before hostilities begin.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0)

Lord [Rodrik Harlaw](wiki:Rodrik_Harlaw) sends to Myr for a lens to help him read.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-0) [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North), tells his brother [Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) that he slew a Myrish wizard who was unable to hatch a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) for him.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

Tyrion discovers that [the manse](wiki:Illyrio's_manse) of [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis) has [wine](wiki:Wine) from Myr in the cellar,(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) and Tyrion and Illyrio drink Myrish [fire wine](wiki:Fire_wine) after they depart [Pentos](wiki:Pentos).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-2) During [Young Griff](wiki:Young_Griff)'s lessons with [Haldon](wiki:Haldon) on the *[Shy Maid](wiki:Shy_Maid)*, Tyrion observes that the youth is fluent in the low dialect of Myr.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) He also notices a wonderful Myrish carpet on the floor of a whore's bedchamber in [Selhorys](wiki:Selhorys), but the drunken Tyrion retches onto it.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-0)

The *[Selaesori Qhoran](wiki:Selaesori_Qhoran)* carries fifty Myrish carpets amongst its cargo.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-0)

[Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) notices a threadbare Myrish carpet on the floor of Lord [Godric Borrell](wiki:Godric_Borrell)’s hall in [Breakwater](wiki:Breakwater).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-0) He also notices a Myrish carpet on the floor of Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly)'s secret room in the [New Castle](wiki:New_Castle) where he meets with Wyman and [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0)

At the [Wall](wiki:Wall), [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), considers having apprentices from Myr teach glass-blowing to the [black brothers](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

Victarion captures a Myrish cog named *[Dove](wiki:Dove)* on her way to [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) by way of [New Ghis](wiki:New_Ghis) with a cargo of carpets, sweet green [wines](wiki:Wine), and Myrish lace. Her captain owned a [Myrish eye](wiki:Myrish_eye), which makes far-off objects appear close, and Victarion claims it for himself. Victarion holds the seasoned crew for future ransom.(wiki:Myr.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-0)
