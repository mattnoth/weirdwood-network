## Origins

### Ancient History

Raised by the ancient [First Men](wiki:First_Men),(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) it is claimed that Moat Cailin has defended against southern invasions for [ten thousand years](wiki:Timeline_of_major_events).(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

According to myth, the [greenseers](wiki:Greenseers) of the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) worked dark [magic](wiki:Magic) at Moat Cailin.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) From the [Children's Tower](wiki:Children's_Tower),(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7) they are said to have used the [hammer of the waters](wiki:Hammer_of_the_waters) on the [Neck](wiki:Neck)(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) to break [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) in two,(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) separating the north from the south in the same manner they shattered the [Arm of Dorne](wiki:Arm_of_Dorne). The children failed and only succeeded in flooding it, however, creating bogs and swamps. Some scholars discount the legend, instead attributing the watery landscape to natural events.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

The [Marsh Kings](wiki:Marsh_King) and their [crannogmen](wiki:Crannogmen) held Moat Cailin—sometimes with the assistance of the [Barrow Kings](wiki:Barrow_Kings), [Red Kings](wiki:Red_Kings), and [Kings of Winter](wiki:Kings_of_Winter)—against all attacks from the south.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The Kings of Winter from [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) eventually defeated the Marsh Kings, adding Moat Cailin to the realm of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) The swampy terrain was enough to prevent Moat Cailin from falling during the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to Westeros. It was a key defense of the north(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) against which [Andal](wiki:Andal) armies threw themselves time after time with no success.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

The wooden keep rotted away "a thousand years past".(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Catelyn_14-0) The three remaining towers are more than capable of defending the passage to the south, however, provided that they are fully manned.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8) Moat Cailin has never been taken from the south, although it is vulnerable from the north and the east.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

### Iron Throne

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), some northern lords urged King [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark) to resist [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) at Moat Cailin.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Rather than fight the Targaryen [dragons](wiki:Dragon), however, Torrhen instead submitted to [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) at the [Trident](wiki:Trident), becoming the King Who Knelt.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

[Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) has visited Moat Cailin,(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-9) presumably seeing it for the first time while traveling from [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) after [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Moat Cailin](wiki:Category:Images_of_Moat_Cailin)*

Moat Cailin was once a great stronghold, with twenty towers, a wooden keep, and a great [basalt](wiki:Basalt) curtain wall as high as that of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell)'s. Today only great blocks of black basalt lay scattered about, half sunk in the ground where the wall once stood, and the keep rotted away.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

The remaining three towers, which are covered with green moss(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and white [ghostskin](wiki:Ghostskin),(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) command the causeway from all sides so that enemies must pass between them. Attackers face constant fire from the other towers should they attempt to attack any one tower, wading through chest deep water and crossing a moat full of [lizard-lions](wiki:Lizard-lion).(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

The [Children's Tower](wiki:Children's_Tower) is tall and slender. It has only half of the crenellations of its crown. Legend has it that the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) called upon [their gods](wiki:Old_gods) here to send the [hammer of the waters](wiki:Hammer_of_the_waters) to smash the [Neck](wiki:Neck).(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

The [Gatehouse Tower](wiki:Gatehouse_Tower), the largest of the remaining towers, is squat and wide.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) It is the only tower which still stands straight, even retaining some of the walls around it,(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) although a tree grows through its northern side.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) The tower's hall of dark stone is spotted with lichen and has a high, drafty ceiling. Within the hall is a massive carved table, also of stone.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

The [Drunkard's Tower](wiki:Drunkard's_Tower) is so named due to its great lean. It stands where the south and west walls once met.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), instructs his wife [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) to have Ser [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart) and [Galbart Glover](wiki:Galbart_Glover) fortify Moat Cailin with a hundred bowmen each.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

On the march south from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) after [Eddard's arrest](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) takes the [Gatehouse Tower](wiki:Gatehouse_Tower) as his seat, [Greatjon Umber](wiki:Jon_Umber) takes the [Children's Tower](wiki:Children's_Tower) for his, and [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark) uses the Drunkard's Tower. Robb's mother, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), and great-uncle, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), join him at Moat Cailin with the levies of [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly), but the northmen run low on supplies.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-10) Robb leaves a small force of mostly archers to hold the Moat,(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-11) while the rest of his army continues south toward the [Twins](wiki:Twins).(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Clash of Kings

[Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), [Lord Captain of the Iron Fleet](wiki:Lord_Captain_of_the_Iron_Fleet), is sent by his brother [Balon](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), to take Moat Cailin. Instead of marching from the south, the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) sail up the [Saltspear](wiki:Saltspear) and the [Fever River](wiki:Fever_River)(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) and [attack Moat Cailin](wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin) from the west.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Rawoiafmoat_cailin.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

After [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) [captures Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell) and declares himself the [Prince of Winterfell](wiki:Prince_of_Winterfell), Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) councils Theon that holding [Jojen](wiki:Jojen_Reed) and [Meera Reed](wiki:Meera_Reed) as hostages will stay the hand of their father, Lord [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed), the liege of the local [crannogmen](wiki:Crannogmen).(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

### A Storm of Swords

At [Hag's Mire](wiki:Hag's_Mire), upon receiving the news of Balon's death at [Pyke](wiki:Pyke) from the captain of the *[Myraham](wiki:Myraham)*, Robb plans to march on Moat Cailin. Robb intends for Howland's crannogmen to lead northmen through the swamps of the [Neck](wiki:Neck), so the ironborn-held Moat Cailin can be attacked from the south and the north.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Upon hearing of Balon's death and the return of his hated brother, [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), Victarion leaves a token force to defend Moat Cailin under the command of [Ralf Kenning](wiki:Ralf_Kenning) and returns to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) with his fleet to decide on the succession.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) The ironborn garrison is weakened by [crannogmen](wiki:Crannogmen) attacks.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

Euron is crowned [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North) during the [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot),(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) and he commands Victarion to travel to [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) with the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) to bring back [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen). Victarion sets sail, leaving behind his unknowing loyal garrison at Moat Cailin.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) expects that [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), the son of the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), will wed [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) after the ironborn lose control of Moat Cailin.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Men from Houses [Ryswell](wiki:House_Ryswell) and [Dustin](wiki:House_Dustin) surprise the remaining ironmen on the Fever River and burn their longships, cutting off the garrison of Moat Cailin from naval support.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

During the [siege of Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin), Ramsay sends his captive, Theon Greyjoy, to offer the weakened ironborn garrison food and safe passage if they surrender unarmed. Theon negotiates their surrender, but Ramsay has all of the ironborn flayed and displayed along the causeway. Ramsay and Theon later meet the returning northern host of Lord Bolton and his [Frey](wiki:House_Frey) allies at Moat Cailin.(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)

With the fall of Moat Cailin, [Tristifer Botley](wiki:Tristifer_Botley) advises [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) to abandon [Deepwood Motte](wiki:Deepwood_Motte). They are soon after [captured](wiki:Fight_by_Deepwood_Motte) by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)(wiki:Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)
