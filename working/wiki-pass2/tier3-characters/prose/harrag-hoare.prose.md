## Origins

According to [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), Harrag was chosen by a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot).(wiki:Harrag_Hoare.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Harrag_Hoare.cite_ref-4) Unlike many kings of his time, Harrag's reign resembled those of the reavers of the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:Harrag_Hoare.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
During the reign of [Theon Stark](wiki:Theon_Stark), the [King of Winter](wiki:King_in_the_North) known as the Hungry Wolf, Harrag led a fleet of longships from [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk), [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk), [Pyke](wiki:Pyke_(island)), and [Orkmont](wiki:Orkmont) against the western coast of the [north](wiki:North). Harrag conquered the [Stony Shore](wiki:Stony_Shore) and burned swathes of the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood), while [Bear Island](wiki:Bear_Island) became a base for reaving for Harrag's son, [Ravos the Raper](wiki:Ravos_Hoare). Theon slew Ravos with his own hand, however, and expelled the ironmen from his shores.(wiki:Harrag_Hoare.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
Although Harrag's invasion was repulsed, the ironborn would return during the reign of Harrag's grandson, King [Erich the Eagle](wiki:Erich_Hoare).(wiki:Harrag_Hoare.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)
