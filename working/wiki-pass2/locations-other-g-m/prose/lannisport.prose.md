## Origins

### Kingdom of the Rock

> *See also: [Images of Lannisport](wiki:Category:Images_of_Lannisport)*

According to a semi-canon source, a lesser branch of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) developed a village a mile from [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) into first a town and then a city,(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-sample_5-1) as the fertile vicinity has a natural harbor with abundant fish. While the [Lannisters of Lannisport](wiki:House_Lannister_of_Lannisport) fortified the city, the Lannisters of Casterly Rock became the [Kings of the Rock](wiki:Kings_of_the_Rock). By the time of the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals), the only city in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) larger than Lannisport was [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown). It is said that King [Loreon V Lannister](wiki:Loreon_V_Lannister), nicknamed Queen Lorea, disguised himself as a whore and wandered the docks of the city.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) [Roland I Arryn](wiki:Roland_I_Arryn) visited Lannisport in his youth before becoming [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_the_eyrie.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Due to the city's proximity to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands), wealthy Lannisport has often been a target for their attacks,(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) being burned at least three times and raided two dozen times over the centuries.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-20) After being mutilated by the [Shrike](wiki:Shrike), [Lelia Lannister](wiki:Lelia_Lannister) was sent from the Iron Islands back to Lannisport, which led to years of warfare between the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) and the [westermen](wiki:Westermen).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Ironborn sailors have also traded at Lannisport during more peaceful times.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

### Targaryen Era

Accounts differ whether [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) visited Lannisport prior to [the Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) The [war fleet of Lannisport](wiki:Lannister_fleet) participated in his [invasion of the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands) in [2 AC](wiki:2_AC).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Queen [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen) found a knight in Lannisport who was willing to foster the [bastard](wiki:Bastard) of a [Rowan](wiki:House_Rowan) girl in her service.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) King Aegon I visited Lannisport during his [royal progresses](wiki:Royal_progress),(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) as did King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) during one of his.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

[Melony Piper](wiki:Melony_Piper) and her brothers swore the loyalty of [House Piper](wiki:House_Piper) to [Aegon the Uncrowned](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) at Lannisport after King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett) led the chapter of [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) in Lannisport during the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), and Ser [Tyler Hill](wiki:Tyler_Hill) led five hundred westermen from the city to support Aegon against Maegor.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2) After Aegon and Melony were killed in the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye), Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) fled to Lannisport and then [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3) Queen [Elinor Costayne](wiki:Elinor_Costayne) gave up all claims to nobility later in life, becoming Mother Elinor at the [motherhouse](wiki:Motherhouse) in Lannisport.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

Lannisport, [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), and [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) were included in the new tax laws of [Edwell Celtigar](wiki:Edwell_Celtigar), [Lord of Claw Isle](wiki:Lord_of_Claw_Isle) and [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) for King [Jaehaerys Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Lannisport grew during Jaehaerys's reign, although not as quickly as King's Landing.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) The king visited Lannisport during his royal progress in [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1)

[Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) visited Oldtown, Lannisport, and [Lordsport](wiki:Lordsport) during his youth.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

### The Dance and Regency

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Lannisport supported the [greens](wiki:Greens) of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), the [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands) known as the Red Kraken, sacked the city in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), capturing three-quarters of Lannisport's fleet and sinking the rest. Six hundred women and girls, including the favorite mistress of Lord [Jason Lannister](wiki:Jason_Lannister) and his natural daughters, were taken as [salt wives](wiki:Salt_wives).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) One of Dalton's salt wives was a Lannister of Lannisport.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Ser [Emory Hill](wiki:Emory_Hill), the Bastard of Lannisport, was killed in the [Battle by the Lakeshore](wiki:Battle_by_the_Lakeshore).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

During the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister) ordered the construction of huge fortified granaries in Lannisport, [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), and [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

Lord Jason's widow, Lady [Johanna Lannister](wiki:Johanna_Lannister), led men from Lannisport and [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) to save [Kayce](wiki:Kayce) from Dalton's [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1) Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) tasked [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon), [Lord of the Tides](wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides), with defeating Dalton and restoring peace to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands). Dalton was killed at [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle) by [Tess](wiki:Tess) while Alyn was sailing to the west, however. When Alyn's *[Lady Baela](wiki:Lady_Baela)* arrived at Lannisport, the city's bells rang to honor him and thousands cheered him from the shore.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) Before returning to the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands), Alyn left a third of his fleet at Lannisport with Ser [Leo Costayne](wiki:Leo_Costayne) to guard against the ironborn.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-2)

Johanna sent Leo to intervene in the [struggle for power in the Iron Islands](wiki:Struggle_for_power_in_the_Iron_Islands) in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC),(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) and westermen brought back spoils of war and highborn hostages to Lannisport.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_red_kraken.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Trade expanded in Lannisport during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1)

### Later History

Ser [Arlan of Pennytree](wiki:Arlan_of_Pennytree) once unhorsed the Grey Lion, [Damon Lannister](wiki:Damon_Lannister_(lord)), in a tourney at Lannisport.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Arlan and his squire, the later Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), also helped escort a [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) merchant from Lannisport to the [Prince's Pass](wiki:Prince's_Pass).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1) Duncan learned to kiss from a Lannisport tavern girl in [208 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-2)

Ser [Quentyn Ball](wiki:Quentyn_Ball) attacked the city during the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), slaying Lord [Lefford](wiki:House_Lefford) outside its gates(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) and forcing Damon into retreat.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) During the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness), Lannisport was among the cities hit hardest.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

Trade in Lannisport increased greatly during the rule of Lord [Gerold Lannister](wiki:Gerold_Lannister).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-1)

Having succeeding Lord [Tytos](wiki:Tytos_Lannister), Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) sent [his father's mistress](wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress) through the streets of Lannisport [naked in disgrace](wiki:Walk_of_atonement).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

For parts of [267 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and [268 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) and his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Tywin, ruled the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) while residing at Lannisport and Casterly Rock. Tywin gained the support of powerful merchants by reducing tariffs and taxes on the shipping of Lannisport, [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), and [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown). Aerys later tripled the port fees of Lannisport against Tywin's advice, however, and when the king reversed course Tywin was blamed by the merchants for the brief increase.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

Lannisport hosted the [tournament in honor of Viserys's birth](wiki:Tournament_in_honor_of_Viserys's_birth) in [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-1) Around [286 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [Tysha](wiki:Tysha) was saved from outlaws by Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) and [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) while the brothers were returning from Lannisport.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) Many townsfolk of Lannisport went to [Maggy the Frog](wiki:Maggy_the_Frog) for portions.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

### Greyjoy's Rebellion

During [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), the [ironmen](wiki:Ironmen), led by [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) and [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), sailed into the harbor at Lannisport and [burned the Lannister fleet](wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet) at anchor.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) was captured after the [sea battle off Fair Isle](wiki:Sea_battle_off_Fair_Isle), taken to Lannisport in chains, and spent the rest of the war imprisoned in [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) Robert's forces sailed from Lannisport and [Seagard](wiki:Seagard) to invade the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

At the end of the war, in celebration of their victory over [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy), King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) ordered a tourney to be held. The city was the site of the [tourney at Lannisport](wiki:Tourney_at_Lannisport) of [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), where Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont), [Lord of Bear Island](wiki:Lord_of_Bear_Island), won the hand of [Lynesse Hightower](wiki:Lynesse_Hightower).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) After returning to [Bear Island](wiki:Bear_Island), Jorah hired a harper from Lannisport in an attempt to impress Lynesse,(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-1) and the couple also returned to the city for festivals and fairs.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-2)

### A Clash of Kings

Ser [Stafford Lannister](wiki:Stafford_Lannister) gathers a new host of [westermen](wiki:Westermen), including green boys from the stews of Lannisport, to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)

[Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) wants his father [Balon](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), to attack undefended Lannisport, as he expects Stafford to be occupied with Theon's friend, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), the new [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) Balon instead attacks the [north](wiki:North) and declares himself [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)

After Stafford is killed by Robb's northmen in the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross), the shattered remnants of his force fall back to Lannisport to regroup.(wiki:Lannisport.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)
