## Origins

### Older History

The town takes its name from [the pool](wiki:Jonquil's_Pool) where the legendary [Florian the Fool](wiki:Florian_the_Fool) first spied [Jonquil](wiki:Jonquil) bathing with her sisters(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

The [Mootons](wiki:House_Mooton) of Maidenpool failed to conquer [Crackclaw Point](wiki:Crackclaw_Point).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

One of the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_King), [Monfryd the Mighty](wiki:Monfryd_I_Durrandon), defeated petty kings from House Mooton to add Maidenpool to his realm.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_durrandon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

The [Fall of Maidenpool](wiki:Fall_of_Maidenpool) and the death of [Florian the Brave](wiki:Florian_V_Mooton) during the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) are still recalled through song.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) King [Benedict I Justman](wiki:Benedict_I_Justman) added the town to his realm during his reign.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) [Torrence Teague](wiki:Torrence_Teague) was crowned [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident) at Maidenpool after six years of war in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

The Storm King [Arlan III Durrandon](wiki:Arlan_III_Durrandon) conquered the riverlands, including Maidenpool, from [House Teague](wiki:House_Teague). Three centuries later, [Harwyn Hardhand](wiki:Harwyn_Hoare) conquered the region for [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare),(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) who then ruled as [Kings of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:Kings_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### Targaryen Era

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and his half-brother, [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon), defeated the [Darklyns](wiki:House_Darklyn) of [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) and the [Mootons](wiki:House_Mooton) of Maidenpool in [Aegon's first test](wiki:Aegon's_first_test). Lord [Jon Mooton](wiki:Jon_Mooton) then supported Aegon against [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

Trade began to shift from Duskendale and Maidenpool to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) as the new capital of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) grew under the rule of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Because of their anger at the incestual marriage between Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) and Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC), the septons of Maidenpool refused to bless a feast hosted by Lord Mooton for the couple.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Maidenpool's trade began to recover as merchants avoided the taxes and fees imposed on King's Landing by Lord [Edwell Celtigar](wiki:Edwell_Celtigar), the [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) for King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) The king eventually replaced the harbormaster of Maidenpool.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Jaehaerys and his sister-wife, Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), were to spend a fortnight in Maidenpool during a [royal progress](wiki:Royal_progress) in [51 AC](wiki:51_AC). Holy women attempted to assassinate Alysanne within [Jonquil's Pool](wiki:Jonquil's_Pool), however.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

The town hosted the [tourney for King Viserys I's accession](wiki:Tourney_for_King_Viserys_I's_accession) ca. [104 AC](wiki:104_AC).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragons_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### The Dance and Regency

House Mooton supported the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) at the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). One hundred knights from Maidenpool under the command of Lord [Walys Mooton](wiki:Walys_Mooton) participated in the [retaking of Rook's Rest](wiki:Retaking_of_Rook's_Rest) from the [greens](wiki:Greens), but Walys was killed by [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre) afterward.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Maidenpool was used as a base by the dragonriders Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) and [Nettles](wiki:Nettles). Lord [Manfryd Mooton](wiki:Manfryd_Mooton), Walys's brother, did not want to obey Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen)'s order to kill Nettles. Maester [Norren](wiki:Norren_(House_Mooton)), who was keeper of the *[Chronicles of Maidenpool](wiki:Chronicles_of_Maidenpool)*, informed the dragonriders of the command. When Nettles departed on [Sheepstealer](wiki:Sheepstealer), the scream of [Caraxes](wiki:Caraxes) shattered the windows of Jonquil's Tower. After Daemon and Nettles left Maidenpool, Manfryd changed his allegiance from Rhaenyra to King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Maidenpool welcomed the host of Ser [Corwyn Corbray](wiki:Corwyn_Corbray) when it arrived from the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Lord Manfryd served in the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III). Trade to Maidenpool was hampered due to the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War). The town suffered from [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC),(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) but prospered during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring). Levies from Maidenpool joined the host of Ser [Robert Rowan](wiki:Robert_Rowan) in support of Ser [Joffrey Arryn](wiki:Joffrey_Arryn).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### Recent History

House Mooton hosted a [tourney at Maidenpool](wiki:Tourney_at_Maidenpool) in [208 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

Ser [Arlan of Pennytree](wiki:Arlan_of_Pennytree) once beat his [squire](wiki:Squire), [Dunk](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), for eating a widow's pie at Maidenpool, but the true culprit had been an inn boy.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

Ser Duncan the Tall told his squire [Egg](wiki:Egg) to make for Maidenpool in order to escape the [Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) conspirators at [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls). Egg did not make it to the town, but instead met with the army marching on the castle, led by Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers). Amongst the assembled riverlords was Lord Mooton of Maidenpool.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) was the victor in the mêlée at Maidenpool during the reign of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

## Appearances & Description

Maidenpool has a busy harbor and pink stone walls.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The town is famed for its sweetwater bathhouse, [Jonquil's Pool](wiki:Jonquil's_Pool).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Maidenpool has a tavern called the [Stinking Goose](wiki:Stinking_Goose) and numerous inns, including one near the Fool's Gate.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) The castle of House Mooton sits on a hill, and includes [Jonquil's Tower](wiki:Jonquil's_Tower).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Fisherfolk northwest of Maidenpool fish the waters in leather coracles, while others collect clams.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

Maidenpool is sacked three times during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), once [by "lions"](wiki:First_sack_of_Maidenpool), once [by "wolves"](wiki:Second_sack_of_Maidenpool), and finally [by outlaws](wiki:Third_sack_of_Maidenpool).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) When Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), Ser [Cleos Frey](wiki:Cleos_Frey), and [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) pass through on their way south, the town walls are deserted, the gates are smashed, and half the homes and shops are burned or plundered. [Jonquil's Pool](wiki:Jonquil's_Pool) is filled with corpses. After they depart Maidenpool, Cleos is killed on the [Duskendale road](wiki:Duskendale_road) by [outlaw](wiki:Outlaw) bowmen, and the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions) then capture Brienne and Jaime.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

After the [battle at Duskendale](wiki:Battle_at_Duskendale), Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) marches on Maidenpool, [takes its castle](wiki:Taking_of_Maidenpool), and locks Lord [William Mooton](wiki:William_Mooton) in a tower cell.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rasos74.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

[Spotted Pate of Maidenpool](wiki:Spotted_Pate_of_Maidenpool) is slain by [Mag Mar Tun Doh Weg](wiki:Mag_Mar_Tun_Doh_Weg) during the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

### A Feast for Crows

[Timeon](wiki:Timeon), [Pyg](wiki:Pyg), and [Shagwell](wiki:Shagwell), formerly of the Brave Companions, travel to Maidenpool but are unable to approach a ship.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

[Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) visits Maidenpool during her search for [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark). She learns that Lord Tarly began rebuilding Maidenpool while hunting down the outlaws that haunted the areas around the town. The [sept](wiki:Sept) has a new slate roof, a stone inn is under construction to replace a wooden inn, and new homes are being built. Cleared lots contain vegetable plots, tents for merchants, and pavilion for Randyll's [knights](wiki:Knight). Brienne spots a galley, a galleas, and a two-masted cog in the harbor, along with numerous fishing boats.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

After killing Timeon's outlaws at the [Whispers](wiki:Whispers), Brienne and her companions return to Maidenpool; Lord Tarly has the heads of the outlaws mounted on the town's walls.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Ships in the harbor include *[Titan's Daughter](wiki:Titan's_Daughter)*, *[Gulltown Girl](wiki:Gulltown_Girl)*, and *[Seastrider](wiki:Seastrider)*. Brienne and [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne) seek shelter for a night in *[Lady of Myr](wiki:Lady_of_Myr)*.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) Septon [Meribald](wiki:Meribald)'s circuit around the riverlands from Maidenpool back to Maidenpool usually takes half a year.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

Jaime tasks Ser [Ronnet Connington](wiki:Ronnet_Connington) with escorting Ser [Wylis Manderly](wiki:Wylis_Manderly) from [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) to Maidenpool.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) After resolving the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), Jaime tasks [Rafford](wiki:Rafford) with bringing Ser [Desmond Grell](wiki:Desmond_Grell) and Ser [Robin Ryger](wiki:Robin_Ryger) to Maidenpool, where they can continue on to the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Lord Tarly departs Maidenpool for [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) after hearing that Queen [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) has been arrested by the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

[Harrion Karstark](wiki:Harrion_Karstark) remains a prisoner at Maidenpool,(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) but Wylis sails home to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

Lord Tarly reports that he hanged a killer and gelded a raper, both criminals from the [Mountain's men](wiki:Mountain's_men), while he was Maidenpool.(wiki:Maidenpool.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)
