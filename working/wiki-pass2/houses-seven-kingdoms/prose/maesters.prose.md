## Origins

Although the origin of the Citadel is disputed, the [Hightowers](wiki:House_Hightower) are considered to have been instrumental in its founding,(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-7) as most accounts credit its foundation to Prince [Peremore the Twisted](wiki:Peremore_Hightower), the second son of King [Uthor of the High Tower](wiki:Uthor_of_the_High_Tower). The curious Peremore invited numerous scholars, including wise men, teachers, priests, healers, singers, wizards, alchemists, and sorcerers, to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown). After Peremore's death, his brother, King [Urrigon](wiki:Urrigon_Hightower), granted land alongside the [Honeywine](wiki:Honeywine) to "Peremore's pets", who developed the tract into the maesters' Citadel.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf774_32-1)

Before the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), maesters and archmaesters often traveled to the Freehold of Valyria, searching for answers to the many questions they had.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf774_32-2)

In recent centuries, the maesters of the Citadel have supplanted the once powerful [Alchemists' Guild](wiki:Alchemists'_Guild) through most of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) In [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) unsuccessfully advised the [Conclave](wiki:Conclave) to accept highborn girls into the Citadel.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rfab9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0)

Archmaester [Marwyn](wiki:Marwyn) believes that the Citadel is behind the deaths of the last Targaryen [dragons](wiki:Dragon), because of a conspiracy against magic and prophecy.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

## Organization

### Novices and Acolytes

Students who start their education at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) are known as novices. Once they have earned a link for their chain, they become acolytes.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Most acolytes treat novices as if they are slow-witted.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Acolytes train at letters at the [Scribe's Hearth](wiki:Scribe's_Hearth),(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) and can be hired by [Oldtowners](wiki:Oldtown) to read their letters or write their wills.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) When an acolyte is prepared to take his vows and become a maester, he is placed in a completely dark room with one of the Citadel's [glass candles](wiki:Glass_candles), made of razor-sharp [obsidian](wiki:Obsidian). He must stay in that room for the entire night in darkness, unless he is able to light the candle. The tradition serves as a lesson about truth and learning.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Students who violate the rules of the Citadel can be ordered confined to their rooms by the maesters.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) They can also receive more physical punishments. For example, for stealing from the kitchens, [rectors](wiki:Rector) might place them in stocks at the [Seneschal's Court](wiki:Seneschal's_Court), where acolytes may throw rotten vegetables at them.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### Maesters

Maesters are scholars and healers who have completed their training at the Citadel. They can be sent by the Citadel to serve at the holdings of [lords](wiki:Lord) and [landed knights](wiki:Landed_knight) throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), from the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor) to the [Wall](wiki:Wall),(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) but not in the lands [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall). If a lord does not have a maester in his service, he is seen as unimportant.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Maesters normally serve only at castles,(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Racok19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) but they can accompany their lord when he travels(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) or be sent on missions by the lord.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

[Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and his sister wives each had a maester during [the Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), and as king Aegon had as many as six serving him, separate from the Grand Maester.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rfab3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) A lord has to pay the Citadel for a maester's service,(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rssm1284.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) and some [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) believe that maesters would be unwilling to help them.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Racok19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Maesters wear a collar, which is supposed to remind him of the realm he serves.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) When he completes his education and takes his vows, a maester puts aside his House name.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Although he is supposed to be loyal to the seat to which he is assigned, regardless of the changes in control of that holding,(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) old allegiances or loyalties might continue linger.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

While the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) have rejected the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), most recognize the usefulness of having maesters in the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Ironborn lords who venture more frequently to the [green lands](wiki:Green_lands) are more likely to keep maesters in their retinues.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Ironmen who support the [Old Way](wiki:Old_Way), such as [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), a [priest of the Drowned God](wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God), are more likely to disapprove of maesters, however.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Some Westerosi nobles, such as [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) and [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly), have also opposed their own sons choosing a life of service by becoming maesters.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### Archmaesters

Archmaesters are maesters who have demonstrated mastery of a particular subject. They receive a mask, ring, and rod in the metal corresponding to the link of the maester's chain which signifies their expertise on the topic.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) Archmaesters teach the students at the Citadel in their subject of expertise, and they judge whether a novice or an acolyte has shown enough knowledge on their subject to receive a link for their chain.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) While maesters are directly involved in a [lord](wiki:Lord)'s household—teaching children, healing wounds, tending to [ravens](wiki:Raven)—archmaesters can dedicate themselves to research and study.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

Archmaesters have the right to sit on the [Conclave](wiki:Conclave), a council which elects the Grand Maester(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) and determines when the seasons change.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) The meetings of the Conclave are conducted behind closed doors at the Citadel.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)

Each archmaester is said to carry a heavy, black iron key that will open most of the doors in the Citadel. They keep them close on their person, or hide them well.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)

### Grand Maesters

The office of Grand Maester was created in [5 AC](wiki:5_AC) by [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), who asked for an archmaester to advise him on governing the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rfab3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Sworn to serve the whole realm,(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) the Grand Maester sits on the king's [small council](wiki:Small_council) and acts as one of the royal advisers.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) As the Citadel's representative at the royal court of the [king](wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men) on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne)(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rawoiafmaesters.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) the Grand Maester is elected by the Conclave, and only the Conclave can unmake him again, although several Grand Maesters have died violently. King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) executed three Grand Maesters during his reign(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2)—[Gawen](wiki:Gawen), [Myros](wiki:Myros), and [Desmond](wiki:Desmond_(Grand_Maester))(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf43.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)—while [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) had Grand Maester [Gerardys](wiki:Gerardys) fed to his dragon [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre).(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-3) Grand Maester [Hareth](wiki:Hareth_(Maester)) was executed as well, on grounds of treason.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-4)

Although the Conclave puts effort into demonstrating their consideration of ability before birth, this is generally a pretense, as family status often affects their final choice for Grand Maester.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-5)

More than forty men have served the Iron Throne as Grand Maester.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Grand Maesters are generally older men. [Kaeth](wiki:Kaeth) was chosen as Grand Maester at the age of eighty, [Ellendor](wiki:Ellendor) at almost ninety, and [Merion](wiki:Merion) at the age of sixty-six. After all three died within a short time span, King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) suggested to the Conclave that a younger man might be better. As such, [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle), aged forty-two, was raised to the office.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

The Grand Maester wears many interwoven maester chains to indicate his high office. Gems are woven into the chains.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1)

### Seneschal

The governance of the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) is held by the [Seneschal](wiki:Seneschal), who is chosen from among the archmaesters. A new Seneschal is appointed annually. Because the archmaesters tend to see the office as a thankless task, as it takes them away from their true work, the Seneschal is selected by lot, with the one who draws the black stone taking on the office.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) When the archmaester who draws the black stone is not capable of fulfilling his duties as Seneschal, another archmaester might volunteer to serve his term instead.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

When the [red comet](wiki:Red_comet) appears, the maesters are among the first to notice.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-1)(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-4)

The [Conclave](wiki:Conclave) gathers to consider the reports and measurements made by maesters all over the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). Based on that information, they declare the summer is over, and send out [white ravens](wiki:White_raven) to herald the changing of the seasons.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-5)(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)

Acting as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) has Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) thrown into the dungeons of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) as punishment for betraying his confidential information to Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister).(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Accepting Pycelle's dismissal, the Conclave gathers to discuss who his successor should be. After pretending to consider two lowborn maesters, the Conclave comes close to electing Maester [Gormon](wiki:Gormon_Tyrell), who had been born a [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) of [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden). Thanks to the presence of his [little birds](wiki:Little_birds) at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) learns of this, and warns Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the Hand of the King. To prevent the Tyrells from gaining more influence at court, Tywin immediately restores Pycelle to his office.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-6)

### A Feast for Crows

At the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), the novice [Pate](wiki:Pate_(novice)) steals a black key which supposedly opens every door in the Citadel. He trades the key with the [Alchemist](wiki:Alchemist) for a [golden dragon](wiki:Currency). However, the Alchemist kills him,(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-23) and assumes Pate's appearance and identity.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

[Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), the [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), sends his sworn brother [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) to train at the Citadel and become a maester. [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), the maester of [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), is send with Samwell, but following a long period of illness dies, shortly before they reach Oldtown.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-1) After hearing Aemon's interpretation of [a prophecy](wiki:Prince_that_was_promised), Archmaester [Marwyn](wiki:Marwyn) decides to travel to [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) to reach [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen).(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)

### A Dance with Dragons

Lady [Barbrey Dustin](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin) expresses her distrust of maesters to [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), and refers to them as "grey rats".(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

Following the fall of Cersei, Grand Maester Pycelle successfully seizes control of King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) and the [small council](wiki:Small_council). He offers Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) the [regency](wiki:Regency). Together, they attempt to restore the damage done by Cersei and to mend the Lannister-Tyrell alliance that supports Tommen's hold on the Iron Throne. When he sees they are going to succeed, Varys, the former [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers), assassinates both Pycelle and Kevan.(wiki:Maesters.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0)
