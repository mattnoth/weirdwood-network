## Origins

Legends say the Grey King slew [Nagga](wiki:Nagga), after which the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God) turned the [sea dragon](wiki:Sea_dragon)'s bones to stone. From these bones was made the [Grey King's Hall](wiki:Grey_King's_Hall), which he heated with Nagga's living fire.(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) He took a [mermaid](wiki:Mermaid)(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) as his wife so his children could live on land or in water. The Grey King also wore a crown of driftwood so all who knelt before him would know his power came from the sea and the Drowned God himself.(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

The Grey King brought fire to the earth by taunting the [Storm God](wiki:Storm_God) into setting a tree on fire with a thunderbolt. He taught men to weave nets and sails. The Grey King allegedly carved the first longship from the [pale wood](wiki:Weirwood) of [Ygg](wiki:Ygg), a demon tree which fed on human flesh.(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) (wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

The Grey King's skin turned as grey as his hair and beard as he ruled over centuries. Eventually he cast aside his driftwood crown and walked into the sea to descend to the watery halls of the Drowned God, taking his place at the right hand of the god.(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) The Storm God snuffed out Nagga's fire after the Grey King's death and the sea stole his throne, with Nagga's bones the only remnants of the Grey King's Hall.(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

The Grey King is said to have had a hundred sons who fought after his death. The sixteen who survived divided the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) amongst themselves.(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)

Each great house from the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) claims to be descended from the Grey King, aside from the [Goodbrothers](wiki:House_Goodbrother).(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)

At a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk) called by [Galon Whitestaff](wiki:Galon_Whitestaff), [Urras Greyiron](wiki:Urras_Greyiron) was [chosen](wiki:Choosing) as the first [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands) and the first monarch to rule all of the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) since the Grey King.(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)

[Vickon Greyjoy](wiki:Vickon_Greyjoy), a descendent of the Grey King, was chosen as the new [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands) after [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also [Images of the Grey King](wiki:Category:Images_of_the_Grey_King).*

The Grey King is so named because his hair, beard, and eyes were grey as the winter sea, and at the end of his life even his skin had turned grey.(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) He supposedly wore robes of woven seaweed and a tall pale crown made from [Nagga](wiki:Nagga)'s teeth,(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) but he is alternatively said to have worn a driftwood crown.(wiki:Grey_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
