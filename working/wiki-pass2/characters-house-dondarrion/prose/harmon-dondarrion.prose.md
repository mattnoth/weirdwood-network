## Origins

In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), a pretender [outlaw](wiki:Outlaw) called the [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Aenys_I)) gathered thousands to his banners, calling on all "true [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen)" to avenge themselves on [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) and the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) for the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War) as they poured into the [Dornish Marches](wiki:Dornish_Marches). Writing to King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), Lord Harmon discounted the Vulture King's followers as undisciplined rabble. Not long after, the Vulture King's men stormed and seized [Blackhaven](wiki:Blackhaven), and the Vulture King personally sliced off Dondarrion's nose before putting the castle to the torch.(wiki:Harmon_Dondarrion.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

As [the war](wiki:Second_Dornish_War) escalated, the Vulture King attempted to march west after a failed siege of [Nightsong](wiki:Nightsong), only for Lady [Ellyn Caron](wiki:Ellyn_Caron) to sally forth behind him to join her strength to a strong force of marchers led by Lord Dondarrion. In the resulting "[Vulture Hunt](wiki:Vulture_Hunt)", Lord [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly_(lord)) cut off the Dornish retreat, and the three marcher lords shattered the Vulture King's host, riding after them into the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) and capturing the Vulture King. With the Vulture King executed by Lord Tarly, a grateful King Aenys rewarded Harmon and the marcher lords with gold, offices, and honors.(wiki:Harmon_Dondarrion.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

The Lord of Blackhaven was present at the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) in [49 AC](wiki:49_AC), but it is unknown if this was Lord Harmon.(wiki:Harmon_Dondarrion.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
