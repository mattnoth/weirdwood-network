## Origins

### The Legend

According to legend, Night's King lived during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), not long after the [Wall](wiki:Wall) was complete. While serving as the thirteenth [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), he fell in love with [a woman](wiki:Corpse_queen) he saw from the top of the Wall. This woman had skin white as the moon and eyes like blue stars, and her skin was cold as ice, but Night's King was fearless and chased and caught and loved her, and when he gave his seed to her he gave his soul as well. Night's King brought this woman back to the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort), and declared her a queen and himself her king. With strange [sorcery](wiki:Sorcery), he bound the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) brothers to his will, and ruled the Nightfort as his own castle for thirteen years with his [corpse queen](wiki:Corpse_queen) by his side.(wiki:Night's_King.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

During the dark years of his reign, horrific atrocities were committed, of which tales are still told in the [north](wiki:North). It was not until [Brandon the Breaker](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(Breaker)), the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), and [Joramun](wiki:Joramun), the [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall), joined forces, that Night's King was brought down and the Night's Watch freed.(wiki:Night's_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) After his fall, when it was discovered that Night's King had been making sacrifices to the [Others](wiki:Others), all records of him were destroyed, and his very name was forbidden and forgotten.(wiki:Night's_King.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

### Recorded History

The legend of the Night's King is frequently told, and in it the Night's King may be a [Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton), a [Woodfoot](wiki:House_Woodfoot), an [Umber](wiki:House_Umber), a [Flint](wiki:House_Flint_of_the_mountains), a [Norrey](wiki:House_Norrey), or even a [Stark](wiki:House_Stark), depending on where the tale is told and who tells it. The legend is recorded in [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Harmune](wiki:Harmune)'s book, *[Watchers on the Wall](wiki:Watchers_on_the_Wall)*, which includes many stories of the Nightfort. In the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), archmaesters generally dismiss the tale as only fiction, though some consider the possibility that an early Lord Commander did indeed try to create a kingdom for himself. Some suggest that the corpse queen was actually a [barrowlander](wiki:Barrowlands), a daughter of the [Barrow King](wiki:Barrow_Kings).(wiki:Night's_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

If the event truly happened, it is possible that it is what led the Night's Watch to never construct walls at their keeps, ensuring the keeps would always be accessible from the south.(wiki:Night's_King.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Night's_King.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
