## Origins

### Early life

Jaehaerys's father, King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), had developed a deep distaste for the [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) practice of incestuous marriages and was determined to end the custom. Thus, in [237 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Jaehaerys was betrothed to [Celia Tully](wiki:Celia_Tully), the daughter of the Lord of Riverrun. However, from a young age, Jaehaerys had been of a more traditional frame of mind, as he was in love with his sister, Princess [Shaera](wiki:Shaera_Targaryen). Shaera, in turn, desired him. King Aegon V and Queen [Betha](wiki:Betha_Blackwood) observed this and were alerted because of it. They did everything they could to separate the two siblings, yet this only served to inflame their passion.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

While Jaehaerys had been betrothed to Celia Tully, Princess Shaera had been betrothed in [237 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) to [Luthor Tyrell](wiki:Luthor_Tyrell), the heir to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden).(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) When Jaehaerys's and Shaera's elder brother, Prince [Duncan](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen), broke his betrothal to the daughter of Lord [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon) in [239 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and married a common born girl known as [Jenny of Oldstones](wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones), Prince Jaehaerys and Princess Shaera took note. Duncan was forced to choose between the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and his position as heir to the throne, and his new bride. King Aegon V, in the end, relented, though Duncan was excluded from the line of succession.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Thus, in [240 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Jaehaerys and Shaera followed in Duncan's footsteps. They eluded their guardians, and secretly wed and consummated the marriage. Aegon despaired over this, but felt he had no choice, as the marriage had been consummated. He was forced to relent to his children's wishes, while at the same time dealing with the anger and wounded pride of both [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) and [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell).(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

Following their example, the younger brother of Duncan and Jaehaerys, Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_V)), would later also break his betrothal, meaning all sons of King Aegon V married for love, which the king had only agreed to because he himself had been allowed to do so.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

In [244 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) Jaehaerys and Shaera had their first child, a son who they named [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen). A daughter, [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen), followed within the next two years.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-11)

While Jaehaerys himself had married for love, he later commanded his son, Prince [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), and daughter, Princess [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen), to marry each other, despite neither desiring this match. A [woods witch](wiki:Ghost_of_High_Heart), brought to court by [Jenny of Oldstones](wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones), prophesied that the [prince that was promised](wiki:Prince_that_was_promised) would be born from their line.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Whilst King Aegon V was frustrated by Jaehaerys's decision, he let his son have his way.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) The firstborn child of Aerys and Rhaella, Prince [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), was born in the year [259 AC](wiki:259_AC), on the same day that the [Tragedy at Summerhall](wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall) took place, a tragedy which killed multiple members of House Targaryen, including King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) and his eldest son, [Duncan](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen).(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafrhaegar_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### Reign

Jaehaerys ascended the throne at the age of thirty-four. Despite his young age, his reign would be short, lasting only three years.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) [Dalbridge](wiki:Dalbridge), who would later join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), served as a squire to Jaehaerys II in his youth.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafdalbridge.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

The most notable event that occurred in his reign was the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings). The conflict had been brewing for some time.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) The [Band of Nine](wiki:Band_of_Nine) seized the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) and Jaehaerys feared they would use the islands as a base of operation to launch a war on the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). Despite being the least martial of Aegon V's sons, he proved capable of defending realm from the [Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) pretender [Maelys the Monstrous](wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre).(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafjaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Wanting to end the Blackfyre threat once and for all, Jaehaerys assembled the armies of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) Jaehaerys wanted to command the attack against Maelys himself, but his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Ormund Baratheon](wiki:Ormund_Baratheon), counseled the king to remain at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with Queen [Shaera](wiki:Shaera_Targaryen). Instead, Lord Ormund led the attack himself. In [260 AC](wiki:260_AC), the Targaryen armies landed on three of the Stepstones and the war lasted for most of that year.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) In the end, it was a young knight, Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), who slew Maelys in single combat.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) With Maelys dead, the rest of the [Band of Nine](wiki:Band_of_Nine) lost interest in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) and fell back to their own domains.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6)

Due to his delicate health, Jaehaerys was considered weak by many, but Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), whom he raised to the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard),(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) thought differently. Jaehaerys's reign only lasted three years, but he reigned well in those short years. He proved a capable king, restoring order to the kingdom, ending the [Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) threat and reconciling many of the Great Houses who had grown unhappy with his father's reign.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7) In [262 AC](wiki:262_AC), after barely three years on the throne, King Jaehaerys II Targaryen died after a short [illness](wiki:Illness), complaining of a sudden shortness of breath, at the age of thirty-seven.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8)

## Appearances & Description

Jaehaerys was sickly looking, pale and frail with large [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes) and shoulder-length hair. He suffered from numerous ailments throughout his life.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Jaehaerys was reckoned a weak king by many.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) An amiable and clever, yet sickly man, Jaehaerys died at the young age of thirty-seven.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-ssm_9-0)

Jaehaerys wore a cape that concealed one arm. His crown was the red gold band with black iron points worn by [Maekar I](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) before him.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-ssm_9-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

[Arstan Whitebeard](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) mentions Jaehaerys to [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen). Dany is struck by the realization that Arstan knew her grandfather and will be able to tell her about [her family](wiki:House_Targaryen), the people she comes from. Arstan also tells her of the latent [madness](wiki:Targaryen_madness) in her family and that Jaehaerys once told him that madness and greatness were two sides of the same coin.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

In [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) reveals to [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) that her grandsire, King Jaehaerys II, arranged the marriage between her parents, Prince [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) and Princess [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen).(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

After Daenerys's disappearance Ser Barristan thinks to himself that it is his failures that haunt him at night, one of which is King Jaehaerys, whom he feels he failed, as he had stood in his white cloak beside the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) as madness consumed Jaehaerys’s son Aerys. Ser Barristan recalls that he stood, and saw, and heard, and yet did nothing.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2)

In the [Great Pyramid's audience chamber](wiki:Audience_chamber_(Great_Pyramid)), when Admiral [Groleo](wiki:Groleo)'s severed head is presented to King [Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq), Ser Barristan glances toward the throne, having served so many kings he cannot help but imagine how they might have reacted to this provocation, he thinks that even Jaehaerys, reckoned weak by many, would have ordered the arrest of [Bloodbeard](wiki:Bloodbeard) and the Yunkish slavers.(wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)
