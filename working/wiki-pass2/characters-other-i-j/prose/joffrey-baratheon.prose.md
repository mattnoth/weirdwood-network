## Origins

Joffrey grew up as a spoiled and indulgent child with a cruel streak within him. His father, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), is deeply disappointed with his son and feels little affection for Joffrey(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0),(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) nor did he like the fact that Joffrey would always cry whenever Robert picked him up.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) It is implied that Joffrey craves Robert's respect and approval, and many of his acts are done in an effort to live up to his father's standards.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

Once after learning a kitchen [cat](wiki:Cat) was pregnant, Joffrey killed the [animal](wiki:Animal) and cut open its belly to see the kittens inside. He showed one of the unborn kittens to his father. Robert was so shocked and angry he hit Joffrey so hard it knocked out two of his baby teeth.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) believes Robert would have beaten the boy if she had allowed it.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) It is also implied Joffrey bullied his younger brother, [Tommen](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Cersei assigned [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) to be her son's [sworn shield](wiki:Sworn_shield).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-4) While Sandor is widely known as the [Hound](wiki:Hound), Joffrey refers to his guardian as "dog".(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

For Joffrey's twelfth name day, King Robert organized a [tourney in King's Landing](wiki:Tourney_on_Prince_Joffrey's_name_day) in [298 AC](wiki:298_AC), where, amongst others, Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), and Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) were present.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) Following the tourney, Joffrey and his siblings traveled with Cersei to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) with their grandfather, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

## Appearances & Description

Joffrey has the [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) look(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) and is tall and strong for a boy his age,(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) with blond curly hair.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Known to be handsome,(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) he has deep green eyes and pouty lips.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) thinks that Joffrey looks like a girl.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

Twelve years old at the beginning of *[A Song of Ice and Fire](wiki:A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire)*,(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) Joffrey was strong-willed as a child.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Although he can be gallant(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) and courteous,(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) he has an uncontrollable temper not unlike his mother, Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), and an unchecked sadistic streak.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Joffrey is stubborn and unpredictable.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) The prince can be vengeful,(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) and Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) finds his grandson's decisions foolish.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

Joffrey has no close friends, and he sees his [sworn sword](wiki:Sworn_sword), [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane), as a father figure.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

Joffrey's surcout is divided, showing both the [Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon) stag of his father, King [Robert I](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), and the Lannister lion of his mother, Queen Cersei.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3) He always wears the finest clothing and accents, as befits the royal heir,(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3) including blue wool,(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) black leather,(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) and a deep blue doublet studded with a double row of golden lion's heads.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) The prince possesses a slim coronet made of [gold](wiki:Gold) and [sapphires](wiki:Sapphire).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2) Joffrey wields a longsword named [Lion's Tooth](wiki:Lion's_Tooth),(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-2) and his dagger has a jeweled pommel and inlaid goldwork on the blade.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

Joffrey is a skilled dancer.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) He rides his blood bay courser with reckless abandon.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Joffrey and the rest of the royal family take the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) after Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn)'s death, as King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) asks Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) to become the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Joffrey begins to court the pretty [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), Eddard's eldest daughter, to whom he has been betrothed.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-4) Joffrey spars with [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) in the yard and insults him, wanting to fight with live steel instead of practice swords.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5) He later joins his father during a hunt.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

After [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) is critically injured after falling from the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep), Joffrey's uncle, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), slaps Joffrey for speaking rudely of the boy's condition.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Tyrion is later blamed(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) when [an assassin](wiki:Catspaw) with a [Valyrian steel dagger](wiki:Littlefinger's_blade) fails in an attempt to murder the comatose Bran, which furthers the growing enmity between Houses [Stark](wiki:House_Stark) and [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

On the way from Winterfell to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Joffrey spends a day riding and drinking [wine](wiki:Wine) with Sansa. Along the [Trident](wiki:Trident), they come upon [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) and her friend [Mycah](wiki:Mycah), a butcher's boy, who are practicing sword fighting. A drunk Joffrey commands Mycah to spar with him. When Mycah does not take up the challenge, Joffrey pricks him with his sword, [Lion's Tooth](wiki:Lion's_Tooth), while ignoring pleas to leave the boy alone. Arya smacks him with her stick, allowing Mycah to escape. An enraged Joffrey slashes at Arya, but is injured when her direwolf, [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria_(direwolf)), protects her.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-5) Arya takes Lion's Tooth from him and hurls it into the river. Humiliated and in pain, Joffrey angrily lashes out at Sansa while Arya and Nymeria flee.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-6)

Joffrey later claims before his father's court at [Darry](wiki:Darry) that he had been attacked by Mycah and Nymeria. Search parties are sent out to find Mycah, Nymeria and Arya. [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) rides down Mycah, while Sansa's direwolf, [Lady](wiki:Lady_(direwolf)), is executed after Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) interferes. This earns Joffrey the hatred of Arya. Robert's younger brother, Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), openly laughs at his nephew for being beaten and disarmed by a girl younger than him.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

While drinking with Eddard Stark at the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney) at King's Landing, King Robert openly despairs of his son and heir, revealing to his old friend that he has often thought of abdicating; the only thing that stops him is the thought of Joffrey sitting on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) with his mother whispering in his ear.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

As the new Hand, Ned Stark discovers that Joffrey, along with his siblings, are actually [bastards](wiki:Bastards) born of [incest](wiki:Incest) between Cersei and her twin, Ser [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) of Robert's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) Robert never questioned the boy's parentage. Cersei protects her children's secret by arranging for Robert's death by increasing the strength of his favorite [wine](wiki:Wine) which he drinks while hunting in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

Following Robert's death, Joffrey summons the council and commands that they make arrangements for his coronation as [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms). When Ned produces Robert's will, which declares Ned regent and mentions "the heir" rather than specifying Joffrey as heir to the Iron Throne, Cersei tears it up and advises Ned to swear fealty to her son. Ned in turn reveals that Joffrey has no claim to the throne and that his uncle, Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), is the true heir, but Eddard is quickly arrested for treason.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-1)

After taking the Iron Throne as Robert's heir, Joffrey starts his first court session by naming his grandfather, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), as the new Hand of the King, appointing his mother Cersei to the [small council](wiki:Small_council), and rewarding [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt) with [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal). Against all traditions, Joffrey dismisses the legendary Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) from his service and names Jaime Lannister as the new [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard). When Barristan storms out in disgust, making a remark about the ease with which Stannis would take the throne from him, Joffrey orders the knight seized and questioned,(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1) though Barristan the Bold escapes.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) The vacancy on the Kingsguard is filled by Joffrey's [sworn shield](wiki:Sworn_shield), [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane), the first person to serve with the White Swords without having earned [knighthood](wiki:Knight).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-2)

Further during the court session, Sansa, to whom Joffrey is still betrothed, kneels and begs for him to spare her father Eddard's life, asking that Joffrey to do this for love of her. Joffrey promises Sansa that he will be merciful.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-3) However, after Eddard publicly confesses his crimes at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) and declares that Joffrey is the true heir to the Iron Throne, Joffrey's mercy turns out to be Eddard's public beheading by Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne) before Sansa's eyes.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) This rash act is against his family's wishes to minimize further bloodshed and restore the king's peace and leads to the intensification of hostilities.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)

Eddard's son Robb captures Jaime in the [Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood)(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) and is declared the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) after the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) Robb intends to kill Joffrey in retaliation for Ned's death,(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-1) plunging Westeros into [devastating civil war](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).

Joffrey has Ser [Meryn Trant](wiki:Meryn_Trant) of the Kingsguard strike Sansa on his behalf, and he forces her to look at severed heads on the walls of the Red Keep, including those of her father Eddard and Septa [Mordane](wiki:Mordane). Joffrey threatens to force Sansa to look on her brother Robb's head when he kills him, as he boasts, in single combat. Sansa briefly considers pushing Joffrey to his death, but relents.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) Joffrey fancies himself a warrior, and he considers leading the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) into the field. Cersei withholds the news of Renly's coronation at [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) from Joffrey lest he insist on marching against him.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2)

### A Clash of Kings

[Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone) and the brother of King [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) and the late King [Robert](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), has his supporters spread the message that Joffrey and his siblings, Prince [Tommen](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) and Princess [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), are [bastard](wiki:Bastard) abominations. Stannis presses his own claim to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1)(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) After Renly's death at [Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), Stannis hopes to present Robert's bastard, [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm), as proof that Joffrey is illegitimate.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark), incognito in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), includes Joffrey in a list of those she wants dead.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) tells [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) that [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) refer to the [red comet](wiki:Red_comet) as King Joffrey's Comet. During a [small tourney](wiki:Tourney_on_King_Joffrey's_name_day) held to honor the boy king's name day, Sansa convinces Joffrey to spare the life of the drunken Ser [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard), who becomes Joffrey's new [fool](wiki:Fool).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) While [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) is Joffrey's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Tywin is on campaign during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), Tywin's son and Joffrey's uncle, acts as Hand in Tywin's stead.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-1)

Joffrey rules with whim and caprice, proving difficult for even his mother to control, and he is fond of having men fight to the death.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-2) Sansa becomes imprisoned to his will, and he frequently has his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), with the exception of the [Hound](wiki:Hound), beat her when she displeases him.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-3) When Joffrey, furious after learning of the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross), orders Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount) to publicly strip Sansa naked and beat her, they are stopped by Tyrion.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) Although Joffrey never tries it again, he is determined to eventually have Sansa in his bed whether he marries her or not.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1)(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)

Joffrey's cruelty and the decreased quality of life at King's Landing due to food shortages and other hardships make him an unpopular king. When a mob gathers at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), the king has them dispersed with a volley of arrows which kills four; the king suggests the starving mob [eat the slain](wiki:Cannibalism).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) Tensions culminate with [bread riots](wiki:Riots_in_King's_Landing) sparked by Joffrey's temper, during which the royal retinue is forced to flee back to the Red Keep.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) Acting as his temporary Hand, only Tyrion stands up to Joffrey's authority, and the king develops a special hatred for his uncle. Tyrion, in return, holds his nephew in contempt, viewing him as a monster.

During the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Joffrey wears gilded mail and enameled crimson plate, with a golden lion on his helm, and he carries a new blade, [Hearteater](wiki:Hearteater).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) The king commands the [Three Whores](wiki:Three_Whores) during the battle.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) When Stannis's men begin to attack the gates of King's Landing, [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) has Ser [Osmund Kettleblack](wiki:Osmund_Kettleblack) bring the king to the safety of the Red Keep. Although this leads many of the [gold cloaks](wiki:Gold_cloaks) to abandon their posts(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0) and the Hound's fear of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) causes him to flee,(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0) Stannis is ultimately defeated by the arrival of Lords Tywin Lannister and [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-1)

Following the battle, Joffrey rewards many of the survivors in the [throne room](wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep)). The king puts aside his betrothal to Sansa and instead promises to wed [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), Mace's daughter and the widow of Renly. He is excused from court after cutting his arm on the Iron Throne. Dontos tells Sansa of a plan for her to escape the capital, giving her a silver hair net with [amethysts](wiki:Amethyst) for her to wear on the night of Joffrey's wedding.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-1)

### A Storm of Swords

Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) becomes Joffrey's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), replacing [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Most of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s supporters who survived the Blackwater submit to Joffrey.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0) At [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) burns leeches which have fed on [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm) in attempts to kill Kings Joffrey, [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), and [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0)

[Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) informs [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell), [Margaery's](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) grandmother, of Joffrey's cruel personality, and the [Tyrells](wiki:House_Tyrell) plan to wed Sansa to Margaery's brother, [Willas Tyrell](wiki:Willas_Tyrell).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Tywin, however, quickly arranges for [Sansa to marry Tyrion](wiki:Wedding_of_Tyrion_Lannister_and_Sansa_Stark). King Joffrey gives Sansa away during the wedding ceremony, though he discretely touches her breast while doing so. When Joffrey announces it is time for the bedding custom, Tyrion outrages the king by threatening to geld him in defense of his new wife.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)

Joffrey is delighted when he hears of [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark)'s death at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding) and wants his head so he can serve it to Sansa at his own wedding, in addition to wanting to show no mercy to the [northern](wiki:North) and [river](wiki:Riverlands) lords who surrendered following Robb's death. Tywin instructs Joffrey to be merciful in victory, but then sends the king from his [solar](wiki:Solar) in the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand) when Joffrey is insulting in response.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

Before his wedding, Joffrey receives a [goldenheart](wiki:Goldenheart) bow from [Jalabhar Xho](wiki:Jalabhar_Xho), riding boots from [Tanda Stokeworth](wiki:Tanda_Stokeworth), a jousting saddle from [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), scorpion brooch from Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), silver spurs from Ser [Addam Marbrand](wiki:Addam_Marbrand), and a tourney pavilion from [Mathis Rowan](wiki:Mathis_Rowan). The king is presented with a model of a war galley by Lord [Paxter Redwyne](wiki:Paxter_Redwyne), who says *[King Joffrey's Valor](wiki:King_Joffrey's_Valor)* is being built for him at the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor). Tyrion gives Joffrey a rare book, *[Lives of Four Kings](wiki:Lives_of_Four_Kings)*, and Mace gifts him a seven-sided [wedding chalice](wiki:Wedding_chalice).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

Tywin gives his grandson a [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword as a wedding present, which Joffrey names [Widow's Wail](wiki:Widow's_Wail). Joffrey slices *Lives of Four Kings* with his new sword, demanding a better present from Tyrion and Sansa.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) Since Joffrey boasts that he is no stranger to the metal, Tyrion thinks that Joffrey is referring to the [Valyrian steel dagger](wiki:Valyrian_steel_dagger) given to the [assassin](wiki:Catspaw) at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), though he is unable to fathom why, attributing it to Joffrey's innate cruelty.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) later deduces that Joffrey did it in an attempt to impress his father after overhearing a drunken [Robert](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) say it would be kinder to put the crippled [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) out of his misery.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)

Joffrey's marriage to Margaery is on the first day of [300 AC](wiki:300_AC) at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) During his wedding feast in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s throne room, Joffrey pours wine from his new chalice atop Tyrion when his uncle refuses to join the [dwarfs](wiki:Dwarf) reenacting the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), and the king forces Tyrion to serve as his cupbearer.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4) When Joffrey begins choking after eating pigeon pie and drinking wine, Ser [Meryn Trant](wiki:Meryn_Trant) tries to save him by jamming a spoon down his throat. While the entire court looks on, Joffrey's face turns black as he asphyxiates while tearing at his throat. Joffrey lifts a hand in Tyrion's direction as he dies. Margaery's mother, [Alerie Hightower](wiki:Alerie_Hightower), says that the king choked on his pie, but Cersei accuses Tyrion and Sansa of having [poisoned](wiki:Poison) her son.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5)

While Tyrion is taken into custody by the Kingsguard, Sansa and Ser [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard) escape King's Landing and reach [a ship](wiki:Merling_King) in [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay). Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) takes Sansa aboard the ship and then has Ser [Lothor Brune](wiki:Lothor_Brune)'s men kill Dontos.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0) Littlefinger reveals to Sansa that he orchestrated Joffrey's murder with Olenna Tyrell.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0) Due to the color of the amethysts in the unknowing Sansa's net(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-1) used to poison Joffrey's wine,(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0) and the actual color of the wine, the wedding has since been referred to by fans as the [Purple Wedding](wiki:Purple_Wedding).

Joffrey is laid in state in the Red Keep's [royal sept](wiki:Royal_sept) in gilded armor, and when Jaime returns to King's Landing he makes love to Cersei in front of the corpse.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0) Joffrey is ultimately laid to rest in the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0) Because Joffrey was poisoned, Jaime tasks Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount) with tasting all of King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon)'s food and drink.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-1) He refuses to recognize Joffrey as his son.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-2)

During Tyrion's trial for Joffrey's death, witnesses downplay the king's behavior and exaggerate Tyrion's actions.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0)(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0) Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) accuses Tyrion of having poisoned Joffrey with the [strangler](wiki:Strangler).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-1) Tyrion demands [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle), and he is represented by Oberyn, who seeks vengeance for the death of his sister, Princess [Elia](wiki:Elia_Martell) of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), who was killed years ago by the crown's representative, Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane), during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing). The Mountain kills the Red Viper in combat, however, and Tyrion is deemed to be guilty by [the gods](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-1)

Before Tyrion can be executed for Joffrey's death, Jaime frees his brother from the dungeon with the aid of [Varys](wiki:Varys). Angry at Jaime having deceived him about his first wife, [Tysha](wiki:Tysha), Tyrion lies and takes responsibility for Joffrey's death. While escaping the Red Keep with Varys, Tyrion kills his father Tywin in the Tower of the Hand.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Joffrey, with the exception of his mother [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister),(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) is not particularly mourned by anyone; even his biological father, Jaime, feels that Joffrey deserved his fate(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-3) and wants Tommen to be different.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-0) Tyrion believes that Joffrey would have become a worse king than the [Mad King](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-1) [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) thinks the only good thing that could be said of Joffrey is that he was tall and strong for his age.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) Because of Joffrey, Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) considers Cersei to be an unfit mother.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-0)

As Queen Regent, Cersei promises a [lordship](wiki:Lord) to whomever brings her Tyrion's head.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-1)

Joffrey's younger brother, King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), weds his widow, [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell).(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-0) Cersei is troubled by Tommen's meekness in comparison to Joffrey.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-2) Because [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) is older than Tommen, [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) tries(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3) but fails to crown Myrcella as queen.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0)

One of [Cersei's dromonds](wiki:Cersei's_dromonds) is named *[Brave Joffrey](wiki:Brave_Joffrey)* in the late king's honor.(wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2)
