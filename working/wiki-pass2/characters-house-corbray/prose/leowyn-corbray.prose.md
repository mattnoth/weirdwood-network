## Origins

In [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), Lady [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn) gave Leowyn and his younger brother, Ser [Corwyn](wiki:Corwyn_Corbray) command of her army of ten thousand men sent forth from [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown). After the death of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC), Lord Leowyn stood at the prow of a [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) cog in the [Gullet](wiki:Gullet) and watched a line of [Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) warships tear down Aegon II's sigil and raise up the red dragon of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) in its place, to mark the ascension of King [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen).(wiki:Leowyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Leowyn and his brother divided the army between them. The Braavosi landed Leowyn at [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) and Corwyn at [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool).(wiki:Leowyn_Corbray.cite_ref-4)(wiki:Leowyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Both brothers were welcomed with feasts and flowers. Soon after Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark)'s [hold over court](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf) had begun, Leowyn left Maidenpool accompanied by Lord [Manfryd Mooton](wiki:Manfryd_Mooton), Lord [Brune](wiki:House_Brune_of_the_Dyre_Den), and Ser [Rennifer Crabb](wiki:Rennifer_Crabb). He met up with his brother on the march to King's Landing. When they arrived at the capital, they joined the ruling council and added their voices to those of Lady Jeyne Arryn and the three [Lads](wiki:Lads). This contributed to power beginning to slip from Lord Stark's hands.(wiki:Leowyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Leowyn, as a supporter of the [black](wiki:Blacks) faction, was named [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm), whilst Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), a [green](wiki:Greens), was named [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). By law, both of them were beholden to the [council of regents](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III). Ser Tyland quickly achieved an unspoken dominance over Leowyn, gathering more power to himself as time passed. In [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), Leowyn died of the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever), alongside his mistress and several of his servants.(wiki:Leowyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)
