## Origins

During the [Andal invasion](wiki:Andal_invasion), [Dywen Shell](wiki:Dywen_Shell) and [Jon Brightstone](wiki:Jon_Brightstone) both claimed the title [King of the Fingers](wiki:King_of_the_Fingers), paying [Andal](wiki:Andal) warlords to cross the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) to support their claim. Within a year, the Andal warlords turned against the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) kings and both monarchs were dead.(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray_(lord).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Ser Corwyn claimed the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers), taking Brightstone's daughter for his bride and Shell's wife for his bedwarmer. Unlike many of his fellow warlords, Corbray never named himself a king, instead preferring the more modest [Lord of the Five Fingers](wiki:Lord_of_the_Five_Fingers).(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray_(lord).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
