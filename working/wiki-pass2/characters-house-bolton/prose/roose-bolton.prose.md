## Origins

Roose practices the abolished tradition of the [first night](wiki:First_night), but he is discreet with his activities and amusements in order to avoid the attention and possible ire of the [Wardens of the North](wiki:Wardens_of_the_North), [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) While hunting along the [Weeping Water](wiki:Weeping_Water), Roose once raped a young [miller's wife](wiki:Miller's_wife) and hanged the miller for not gaining his approval for their marriage. When the woman later presented a baby to Roose, he spared the [bastard](wiki:Bastard) boy [Ramsay](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) because he had the same pale eyes as Roose. Lord Bolton granted the mill to the woman, sent her annual supplies and funds, and had the tongue of the miller's brother cut out to prevent Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark) from learning what had happened.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8)

Roose fought at the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). When Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), seriously wounded, was brought before Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Roose counselled that they should kill him. Robert ignored Roose and spared Barristan's life, sending his own [maester](wiki:Maester) to tend to the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard)'s wounds.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

After the death of his first wife, Roose was married to [Bethany Ryswell](wiki:Bethany_Bolton), with whom he had one son survive the cradle, [Domeric](wiki:Domeric_Bolton). Roose's children who did not survive the cradle have been buried below the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9) Roose had Domeric serve as a page to Bethany's sister, Lady [Barbrey Dustin](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin), in [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton) and as a squire to Lord [Horton Redfort](wiki:Horton_Redfort) at the [Redfort](wiki:Redfort).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-10) Roose had [Reek](wiki:Reek), a servant at the Dreadfort who always smelled terrible, whipped for stealing Bethany's perfume in an attempt to mask his stench.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-11) When the mother of his bastard son appeared at the Dreadfort demanding Roose provide her with a servant to help in raising her son, Roose sent her Reek, an act which he intended to be amusing.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-12) Roose eventually became a widower when his wife Bethany died of a fever.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

In [297 AC](wiki:297_AC),(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-23) Domeric died after visiting his half brother, Ramsay. Maester [Uthor](wiki:Uthor) blamed a bad belly, but Lord Bolton attributes his heir's death to [poisoning](wiki:Poison) by Ramsay. Having no other trueborn heirs, Roose brought Ramsay to the Dreadfort but is not believed to have acknowledged him.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

## Appearances & Description

Roose has an unremarkable body, neither plump, thin, nor muscular.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) He has pasty skin and a pallid chest,(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) which is soft and hairless.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Roose has short, strong fingers.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) He has a plain face, beardless and ordinary,(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) with his only noticeable feature being his strange eyes, paler than stone and darker than milk,(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) like two white moons.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) The color is sometimes called "ghost grey".(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) His eyes are paler than those of his son, [Ramsay](wiki:Ramsay_Bolton).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

Though mild-mannered, Roose is remorseless and implacable.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rawoiafroose_bolton.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) He is considered to be cold and cunning.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Roose's voice is small and soft; he rarely raises his voice, forcing those who listen to do so intently, falling silent.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Roose is convinced that regular [leechings](wiki:Leech) improve a person's health, and as such frequently has himself leeched.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Roose's personal motto is "A peaceful land, a quiet people".(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) According to [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), Lord Bolton's [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son, Roose uses [flaying](wiki:Flaying) to learn secrets.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Roose sips his [alcohol](wiki:Alcohol),(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) preferring [hippocras](wiki:Hippocras),(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) or hot spiced [wine](wiki:Wine),(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) although he is also willing to drink [ale](wiki:Ale)(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6) and regular wine.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

Roose often wears black ringmail and a red-spotted pale pink cloak, trimmed with white fur.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) He also owns a suit of dark grey plate armor over a quilted tunic of blood-red leather. Its rondels are shaped like human heads whose mouths are open in agony. Its helmet has streamers of red silk which flutter in the wind. Usually worn with it is a pink woolen cloak embroidered with droplets of blood.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) Roose wields [sword](wiki:Sword) and dagger.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Roose answers his liege's call when [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) summons his banners to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to aid his father, Lord [Eddard](wiki:Eddard_Stark), and his grandfather, Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully). Roose brusquely demands command from Robb, who is unnerved by the Lord of the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

At [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin), Robb devises a plan to split his army in two, with Robb leading one host to save [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) and the other distracting Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). Instead of the [Greatjon](wiki:Jon_Umber), Robb grants command of the second host to the more cautious Roose.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) At the [Twins](wiki:Twins), Roose cautions Robb against [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey). Robb agrees to a betrothal with one of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey)'s daughters. Roose is given command the [northern](wiki:North) and [Frey](wiki:House_Frey) foot and some of the horse when Robb splits his army at the Twins.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

Roose travels south along the east bank of the [Trident](wiki:Trident) to distract Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). He marches through the night and launches a surprise attack in the early morning, but is defeated by Tywin in the [battle on the Green Fork](wiki:Battle_on_the_Green_Fork).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) However, Roose retreats in good order, reforming the battered remnants of his army on the causeway before [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Roose brings his army from the causeway to the [Twins](wiki:Twins),(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) where [he marries](wiki:Wedding_of_Roose_Bolton_and_Walda_Frey) his third wife, [Walda Frey](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Merrett)),(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) one of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey)'s granddaughters.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1)

[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) hears that Lord Bolton has occupied the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) In an effort to limit Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s options, who has marched from [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) in an attempt to meet Robb in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) commands Roose to join Ser [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart), who is coming south from the Twins, and to retake Harrenhal.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

Hoping to take Harrenhal without weakening his army, Roose makes a deal with [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat), leader of the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions), unsavory sellswords in Tywin's employ. The Companions pretend to take prisoner a group of [northmen](wiki:Northmen) led by [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) and Ser [Aenys Frey](wiki:Aenys_Frey). They are then to seize the castle, taking out Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch)'s small [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) garrison and opening the gates to the rest of Roose's men. The [fall of Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal) is done with the aid of an incognito Arya and the assassin [Jaqen H'ghar](wiki:Jaqen_H'ghar).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)

While at Harrenhal, Roose takes Arya as his cupbearer, mistaking her for a commoner named Nan.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5) Arya is in attendance when Roose holds a council with the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey); Roose lies naked and drained by [leeches](wiki:Leech) during the meeting. Because of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s defeat in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), the Freys do not think that Robb can now defeat Tywin. Roose issues orders to have Ser [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart) [burn Darry](wiki:Siege_of_Darry) and then join with Robett Glover to attack [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale). After the council Roose decides to hunt a [pack of wolves](wiki:Nymeria_(direwolf)) near Harrenhal. Arya also learns from the Freys that they have been dishonored, but does not know why. Because Roose intends to leave her with the Brave Companions at Harrenhal, Arya steals a dagger and a map from Roose's [solar](wiki:Solar) and flees Harrenhal.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

In the meantime, Roose's bastard son, [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), has married and starved Lady [Donella Hornwood](wiki:Donella_Hornwood) and claimed her lands as the Lord of the [Hornwood](wiki:Hornwood). When Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) besieges the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn)-occupied Winterfell, Ramsay has the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort)'s garrison [betray the other northmen](wiki:Battle_outside_the_gates_of_Winterfell) and [burn Winterfell](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) The destruction is attributed to [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy)'s ironborn, however.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

### A Storm of Swords

It is revealed that the Freys have been dishonored because King [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) married [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling) instead of a Frey.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) After the Brave Companions take [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) prisoner, [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat) has Jaime's sword hand cut off before bringing him to Roose at Harrenhal. Roose tells Jaime that Vargo acted independently to curry favor and refuge with Lord [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark), as well as drive a wedge between Roose and [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). The ploy does not work, however, as Roose receives Jaime's assurance that he will not be held responsible for Jaime's injury.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) Before leaving Harrenhal in the hands of Vargo, Roose releases Jaime and asks him to give his regards to Lord Tywin; Jaime responds that Roose should give his regards to Robb Stark.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

Robb summons Roose to aid in retaking [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin) and the [north](wiki:North) from the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn). On the march north to the [Twins](wiki:Twins), Roose takes his time when crossing the Trident and his rearguard is [attacked](wiki:Fighting_at_the_fords_of_the_Trident) by Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane). On the other side, he leaves Stark loyalists to guard the river, taking only Dreadfort and [Karhold](wiki:Karhold) men with him, the latter incensed by [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark)'s execution of Lord Rickard. After arriving at the Twins for Lord [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully)'s wedding with [Roslin Frey](wiki:Roslin_Frey), Roose discusses with Robb the disastrous [battle at Duskendale](wiki:Battle_at_Duskendale), in which [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) and [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) forces shattered the force of Ser [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart), [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover), and [Harrion Karstark](wiki:Harrion_Karstark). Roose attributes the loss of a third of Robb's infantry to Robett's initiative, not admitting that he gave Helman and Robett the order to attack Duskendale.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2) Roose convinces Robb that [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) should be kept captive at the Dreadfort.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-3)

During the reception after Edmure's wedding at the Twins, Roose toasts Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey)'s grandsons, mentioning that [Big Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)) and [Little Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)) are now in the care of Ramsay after the battle at Winterfell. Roose later mumbles an excuse to use the privy and leaves the hall. Edmure's bedding and the subsequent playing of "[The Rains of Castamere](wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere)" is the signal for the Freys to betray and attack their guests. When Roose returns to the hall he is dressed in mail armor. Roose approaches Robb, who has already been wounded by Frey arrows, and kills him while saying, "Jaime Lannister sends his regards."(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)

Outside the castle, the Frey, Bolton, and possibly Karstark soldiers attack their fellow [northmen](wiki:Northmen) and [rivermen](wiki:Rivermen) and annihilate them. It is revealed that Roose had planned the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding) in detail with [Lothar Frey](wiki:Lothar_Frey), including the choice of songs.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) For this service, Tywin gives Roose the title of [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North). Roose also receives a royal decree of legitimization for his bastard son and heir, Ramsay, as well as [a girl](wiki:Jeyne_Poole) masquerading as [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) for Ramsay to marry.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Lord Roose marches north with his own forces from the Twins, augmented by two thousand Freys under the command of Ser [Aenys Frey](wiki:Aenys_Frey) and Ser [Hosteen Frey](wiki:Hosteen_Frey).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Despite being named [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), Roose's support among some northern lords is grudging. The first to declare for him are the [Dustins](wiki:House_Dustin) and [Ryswells](wiki:House_Ryswell), to whom he is related through his second wife, [Bethany](wiki:Bethany_Bolton). The faction of [House Umber](wiki:House_Umber) led by [Hother Whoresbane](wiki:Hother_Umber) declares for him since the [Greatjon](wiki:Jon_Umber) is in Frey captivity, and the [Cerwyns](wiki:House_Cerwyn), [Lockes](wiki:House_Locke), and [Manderlys](wiki:House_Manderly) send men as well. [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) and Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) distrust the Boltons, however.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) [Arnolf Karstark](wiki:Arnolf_Karstark), the castellan of [Karhold](wiki:Karhold) who publicly declares for [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), is secretly in league with Roose and awaits the moment to turn his cloak.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) The Boltons and Karstarks plan to defeat Stannis by drawing him against the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-13)

After [Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin) surrenders to Ramsay thanks to a browbeaten [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), Roose meets his son and introduces him to his fiancee, "Arya Stark" (actually [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) Roose is greatly displeased by his son's actions, stating as much to Theon at [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton), where the northern lords are to gather for Ramsay and Jeyne's wedding. Roose is disgusted by the mistreatment of Theon, whom Ramsay calls Reek. After he hears that Stannis has [retaken Deepwood Motte](wiki:Fight_by_Deepwood_Motte), Roose decides to have the wedding moved from Barrowton to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) in order to bait Stannis.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-14) He is accompanied by his new wife, Walda.

Upon arriving at Winterfell, Roose finds the ruined castle now a refuge of more then two dozen squatters. When Roose tells them he will be merciful if they serve well, they are used for a labor force in rebuilding Winterfell. After the work is completed, Roose has them hanged instead of flayed, which Theon considers merciful.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)

At [Ramsay's wedding feast](wiki:Wedding_of_Ramsay_Bolton_and_Arya_Stark), Roose does not eat any of food or drink unless he sees Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly), who brought the food, first eat of the [same dish](wiki:Frey_Pies/Theories). Lady Dustin tells Theon that Roose hopes to become King in the North, not just Warden, because the Lannisters are weakened after the death of Tywin, maiming of Jaime, and disappearance of [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-3)

Roose, Ramsay and the Bolton army remain at Winterfell awaiting Stannis's host, which is slowed by a blizzard during their [march on Winterfell](wiki:March_on_Winterfell). Several Bolton men are murdered during the wait, and suspicion is cast on several of the bannermen.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) In truth, however, most of the murders are committed by the singer Abel and his women, who are actually [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) and several [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) [spearwives](wiki:Spearwives) on a mission to free "Arya Stark". The murders raise tensions within the castle. Tempers flare when Lord Manderly insults the murdered [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)); a clash ensues that leads to the deaths of several men-at-arms. Having learned that Stannis's army is three days from Winterfell, Roose orders the Freys and Manderlys to leave Winterfell by separate gates and march on Stannis's position.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### The Winds of Winter

Having escaped Winterfell with [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole), [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) informs Stannis that Roose has sent out the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) and [Manderlys](wiki:House_Manderly), as well as Ramsay and his men, but kept much of his own strength behind in Winterfell. According to Theon, Roose is keeping his own men back as a reserve, as he once did in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), to weaken potential rivals. Stannis confirms that his supposed ally, [Arnolf Karstark](wiki:Arnolf_Karstark), has been secretly relaying information to Roose.(wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-41)
