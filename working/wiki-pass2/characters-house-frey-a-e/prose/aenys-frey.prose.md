## Appearances & Description

Aenys is a tall grey man with a bald head, round shoulders, and gnarled hands.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) His watery red eyes, close-set above a pointed nose, are said to be hungry.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) His thin brown beard is shaped like a rat's tail and does not conceal his weak chin.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

Aenys is a seasoned soldier, but he is regarded as a commander, not a swordsman. He is cruel and clever.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Ser Aenys is presumably one of the numerous [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) present at the [Twins](wiki:Twins) when [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) comes to treat with Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey). [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) agrees to marry one of Walder's daughters.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Ser Aenys commands the fifteen hundred men of [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) who are part of Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton)'s army. Roose negotiates with [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat) for the [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) captain to betray Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch), castellan of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal). [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) and Aenys head a group of prisoners who are admitted into the castle by Vargo's [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions). An unknowing and incognito [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) helps to free them ahead of schedule, however. After the [fall of Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal), Vargo relinquishes the castle to Roose, and the Lord of the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort) is then introduced to [Rorge](wiki:Rorge) and [Biter](wiki:Biter) by Aenys.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Aenys has difficulty commanding his kin.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) After [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) is defeated in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Aenys stresses to Lord Bolton that he will not be able to hold Harrenhal if Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) attacks them with all of his forces. Roose reminds Aenys that Tywin has to organize the wedding of his grandson, King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), and so he will not attack them anytime soon. After a [raven](wiki:Raven) arrives from the Twins, Aenys informs young [Elmar Frey](wiki:Elmar_Frey) that [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) has been dishonored.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

### A Storm of Swords

Aenys is among the Freys that meet Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) and [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) when they arrive at Harrenhal as prisoners of the Brave Companions. Angry that [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) has wed [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling) instead of a Frey, Aenys spits upon hearing Robb's name. When Brienne says [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark)'s daughters will be killed if Jaime is not delivered safely to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Aenys is unmoved and untroubled if that should happen.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Aenys leads the Frey contingent north to the [Twins](wiki:Twins) to attend the wedding of Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) and [Roslin Frey](wiki:Roslin_Frey). Roose departs Harrenhal with his [northern](wiki:North) army three days later. Aenys has some difficulty crossing the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford) because of heavy rainfall.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Aenys participates in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Ser Aenys and his half-brother, Ser [Hosteen Frey](wiki:Hosteen_Frey), command the two thousand Frey swords that are sent with Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) to retake the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Aenys and Hosteen are distressed that Aenys's second son, [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey), as well as [Symond](wiki:Symond_Frey) and [Jared Frey](wiki:Jared_Frey), disappeared while en route to [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton) from [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

The Freys attend [the wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Ramsay_Bolton_and_Arya_Stark) of [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) to "[Arya Stark](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)" at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) serves [pork pies](wiki:Frey_Pies/Theories) to the Freys during the feast.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

When his squire is found naked and dead in the lichyard, Aenys suggests the man was drunk and became lost in the blizzard blanketing the castle.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Aenys and Hosteen accuse Lord Manderly of causing the deaths of the missing Freys, but Wyman pleads ignorance.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Aenys first suggests that [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) is to blame for some of the deaths occurring in the castle, and he then again blames Wyman.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

When Hosteen and Wyman come to blows after the death of [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)), Roose orders the Freys and Manderlys to leave Winterfell by separate gates to attack [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### The Winds of Winter

It is revealed that Aenys fell from his horse and broke his neck when he and his men rode into pit traps planted near [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) by [Mors Umber](wiki:Mors_Umber) and his levies. This news pleases Stannis, as it means command of the Frey forces now falls to Ser [Hosteen Frey](wiki:Hosteen_Frey), who is not the brightest.(wiki:Aenys_Frey.cite_ref-16)
