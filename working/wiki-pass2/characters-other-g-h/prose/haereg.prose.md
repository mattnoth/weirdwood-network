## Origins

Haereg suggested that the ancestors of the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) were not [First Men](wiki:First_Men), but instead a race of unknown origin from beyond the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea) who created the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair). He ultimately rejected the idea for lack of evidence, however.(wiki:Haereg.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) Archmaester [Hake](wiki:Hake_(archmaester)) believed the [Kings of the Iron Islands](wiki:Kings_of_the_Iron_Islands) from [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) were ungodly men, but Haereg believed they were resented by the ironborn because of their tolerance for the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Haereg.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

Lord [Rodrik Harlaw](wiki:Rodrik_Harlaw) reads [Haereg's book](wiki:History_of_the_Ironborn) before departing for the [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) to get background information on the subject. Haereg believes it has been four thousand years since the last kingsmoot, whereas Maester [Denestan](wiki:Denestan) believes it is two thousand.(wiki:Haereg.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
