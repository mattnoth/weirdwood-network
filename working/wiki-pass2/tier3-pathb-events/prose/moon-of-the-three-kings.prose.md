## Origins

### Three Kings[ ]

King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) escaped [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during [its fall](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) to Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), his half-sister and rival monarch. Rhaenyra ruled the city for only half a year, before she abandoned King's Landing in the aftermath of [riots](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) during the fifth moon of [130 AC](wiki:130_AC).(wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
Although he did not declaire himself king, the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd), the man who had instigated the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit), commanded a mob of tens of thousands from the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) on [Rhaenys's Hill](wiki:Rhaenys's_Hill). He advised his followers to cast aside their possessions and live more humbly, but his influence began to wane.(wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Ser [Perkin the Flea](wiki:Perkin_the_Flea), a [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight) and leader of the rioters, claimed that his squire, [Trystane Truefyre](wiki:Trystane_Truefyre), was a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son of the late [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), the father of Rhaenyra and Aegon.(wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) After Rhaenyra fled King's Landing, Ser [Garth the Harelip](wiki:Garth_the_Harelip) surrendered the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) to Perkin, who then installed Trystane on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Perkin executed Garth and Ser [Harmon of the Reeds](wiki:Harmon_of_the_Reeds), among other knights, and he forced [Mysaria](wiki:Mysaria) to be whipped naked through the streets, which caused her death. Perkin spared Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) and freed all the prisoners held in the Red Keep's dungeons, including Grand Maester [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle), Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), and Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister). Queen Dowager [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) was likewise freed from her confinement. Perkin was aided by Lord [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong).(wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
The second pretender king was [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair), the four-year-old son of [Essie](wiki:Essie), a [whore](wiki:Whore) who claimed the boy had been fathered by Aegon II. Given Aegon's philandering ways, this was not seen as improbable. Gaemon ruled from the [House of Kisses](wiki:House_of_Kisses) atop [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill) and gathered thousands of followers. He issued a series of edicts,(wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) including a declaration that females should be equal to males in matters of [inheritance](wiki:Customs). [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) claimed these edicts were almost certainly the work of [Sylvenna Sand](wiki:Sylvenna_Sand), a [Dornish](wiki:Dornishmen) whore and the [paramour](wiki:Paramour) of Essie.(wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

### Return of Aegon II[ ]

Meanwhile, Aegon II, hiding on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)), recuperated from injuries suffered at [Rook's Rest](wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest). With Rhaenyra on the mainland, Aegon's [greens](wiki:Greens) took her castle through the [fall of Dragonstone](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone). In the tenth moon of [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), Rhaenyra was killed by Aegon's dragon, [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre), when she returned to Dragonstone. Sunfyre succumbed to his own wounds in the twelfth moon of the year.(wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
After mourning Sunfyre, Aegon II readied plans to recover the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in chaotic King's Landing.(wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) led men from the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) to bring order to the capital on the king's behalf.(wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Ser Perkin was imprisoned, and Trystane Truefyre was executed once Aegon took the city, though not before he received the boon of [knighthood](wiki:Knight). Essie admitted that Gaemon had been the son of an oarsman from [Lys](wiki:Lys), and was hanged. Gaemon Palehair was arrested, but then accepted into Aegon's household.(wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Aegon had the Shepherd's tongue torn out, and then the king had the Shepherd and his most devoted [followers](wiki:Barefoot_lambs) burned to death.(wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
