## Origins

Upon the outbreak of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) flew on [Vermax](wiki:Vermax) to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) and [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to ask the [Manderlys](wiki:House_Manderly) and the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) to support the [blacks](wiki:Blacks). [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark), [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell) and [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), agreed upon the [Pact of Ice and Fire](wiki:Pact_of_Ice_and_Fire) with Jacaerys.(wiki:Winter_Wolves.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) [Northmen](wiki:North) began gathering in support of the prince's mother, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), at White Harbor, Winterfell, and [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton).(wiki:Winter_Wolves.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Ser [Medrick Manderly](wiki:Medrick_Manderly) and his brother, Ser [Torrhen](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly), sailed with White Harbor [knights](wiki:Knight) to join Rhaenyra after the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) to her blacks.(wiki:Winter_Wolves.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Meanwhile, [Roderick Dustin](wiki:Roderick_Dustin), [Lord of Barrowton](wiki:Lord_of_Barrowton), led two thousand grizzled greybeards south. Calling themselves the Winter Wolves, these warriors wore old mail and ragged skins, and they rode shaggy [northern](wiki:North) horses. The northmen wielded axes, mauls, spiked maces, and ancient iron swords.(wiki:Winter_Wolves.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) It was long the custom in the north for older men to leave their homes in winter and thereby preserve supplies for their younger kin, so the Winter Wolves expected they would die while marching for glory, adventure, and plunder.(wiki:Winter_Wolves.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
Roddy the Ruin led the Winter Wolves to the [Twins](wiki:Twins), where they met Lord [Forrest Frey](wiki:Forrest_Frey) and his wife, Lady [Sabitha Frey](wiki:Sabitha_Frey). Roderick marched with Forrest and [Red Robb Rivers](wiki:Red_Robb_Rivers) to the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye), where they joined with other [rivermen](wiki:Rivermen) to oppose a host of [westermen](wiki:Westermen) commanded by Lord [Humfrey Lefford](wiki:Humfrey_Lefford). The Winter Wolves begged the honor of leading the attack in the ensuing [Battle by the Lakeshore](wiki:Battle_by_the_Lakeshore), charging five times at the [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) spearmen. Two-thirds of the northmen were killed or wounded in the fighting, which was a decisive victory for the blacks.(wiki:Winter_Wolves.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
After Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) was killed south of the Gods Eye by Robb's longbowmen, Lord Dustin blew his warhorn to begin the charge of the Winter Wolves and their river knight allies against Criston's army in the ensuing [Butcher's Ball](wiki:Butcher's_Ball), a slaughter of the greens.(wiki:Winter_Wolves.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
Screaming northern war cries, the surviving Winter Wolves rode from a postern gate in the [First Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:First_Battle_of_Tumbleton) after most of Ser [Garibald Grey](wiki:Garibald_Grey)'s men reached the safety of [the town](wiki:Tumbleton). Although outnumbered ten to one, Roderick led the northmen to strike at [Ormund Hightower](wiki:Ormund_Hightower), [Lord of the Hightower](wiki:Lord_of_the_Hightower). Roddy the Ruin managed to slay Ormund and his cousin, Ser [Bryndon Hightower](wiki:Bryndon_Hightower), before succumbing to his wounds. The blacks at Tumbleton were then betrayed by Ser [Hugh Hammer](wiki:Hugh_Hammer) and Ser [Ulf White](wiki:Ulf_White), however, and the last of the Winter Wolves fell in the battle.(wiki:Winter_Wolves.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
Rhaenyra fled [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) after the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit). While she was staying at [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), Lord Stark wrote that he would gather for her a host of ten thousand northmen younger and fiercer than the fallen Winter Wolves.(wiki:Winter_Wolves.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Rhaenyra was [killed at Dragonstone](wiki:Flight_to_Dragonstone) by King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen),(wiki:Winter_Wolves.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) however, so Cregan's new army marched south in support of her son, Prince [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen).(wiki:Winter_Wolves.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
