## Origins

During the civil war known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Lord Owen was loyal to King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and the [greens](wiki:Greens), marching with Lord [Ormund Hightower](wiki:Ormund_Hightower)'s host from the [Reach](wiki:Reach). After the deaths of Lord Ormund and his cousin, Ser [Bryndon Hightower](wiki:Bryndon_Hightower), during the [First Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:First_Battle_of_Tumbleton), the host needed a new commander to lead them. Old Ser [Hobert Hightower](wiki:Hobert_Hightower) thought that as the most senior [Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower) left alive, the command should pass on to him. However, when Hobert took charge of the men, he failed miserably and the [Two Betrayers](wiki:Two_Betrayers), [Ulf White](wiki:Ulf_White) and [Hugh Hammer](wiki:Hugh_Hammer), took the command instead. This strongly displeased Ser Hobert as well as other nobles, Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) chief among them.

One night, Hobert and Unwin organised a secret meeting with other lords and landed knights in the cellar of the [Bloody Caltrops](wiki:Bloody_Caltrops), an inn in [Tumbleton](wiki:Tumbleton). The thirteen assembled nobles (later known as "the [Caltrops](wiki:Caltrops)") debated all night on what to do with the Two Betrayers. Lord Owen was dispatched to wake Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)) and bring him back so that the plotters might inform him of their plans.
