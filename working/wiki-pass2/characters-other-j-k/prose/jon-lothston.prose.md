## Origins

Jon is a [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) exile. His surname Lothston was a house that once loomed large in the histories of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). He may or may not be of [House Lothston](wiki:House_Lothston), as in the free companies a man can call himself whatever he chooses.(wiki:Jon_Lothston.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) His claimed surname was one of the houses who once held the infamous castle of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) and was widely believed to have died out long ago.(wiki:Jon_Lothston.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Appearances & Description

Jon wears his worldly wealth upon his person and a lord's ransom in golden arm rings. Each ring signifies one year's service with the Golden Company.(wiki:Jon_Lothston.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

Jon Lothston and [Marq Mandrake](wiki:Marq_Mandrake) exchange a glance when [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) reveals to the highest ranking members of the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) that Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) is still alive, while the other officers remain silent, causing Connington to realize they had already known about Aegon. Like the other officers of the company, Jon swears his allegiance to Aegon before they [invade Westeros](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company).(wiki:Jon_Lothston.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
