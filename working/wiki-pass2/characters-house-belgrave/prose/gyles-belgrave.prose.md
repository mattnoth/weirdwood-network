## Origins

Ser Gyles served King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). Following the defeat of Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) and his army at the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad), Gyles commanded the Aegon's escort after his meeting with the [small council](wiki:Small_council). The fatigued-looking king drank a cup of [Arbor red](wiki:Wine) and before closing the curtains to keep out the chill, ordered Ser Gyles to take his palanquin to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s [sept](wiki:Sept).

When Aegon did not respond after they arrived at the sept, Gyles threw back the hangings and found Aegon dead with blood dribbling from his lips.(wiki:Gyles_Belgrave.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) When the [victorious riverlords](wiki:Lads) arrived at the capital two days later, Gyles and four other [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) were present as Aegon's body was laid on a bier beneath the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Gyles_Belgrave.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

During the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf), Gyles and three other white cloaks who had been present when Aegon died were accused of treason. Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), accused Gyles of either [poisoning](wiki:Poison) the king's [wine](wiki:Wine) himself or failing in his duty to prevent it from happening.(wiki:Gyles_Belgrave.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

On the day scheduled for the executions, most of those condemned chose to [take the black](wiki:Night's_Watch). Gyles, however, accepted death as he had failed in his duty to protect Aegon. The knight was personally executed by Lord Stark, who took his head off with [Ice](wiki:Ice). The heads of Gyles and Lord [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong), who was accused of leading the conspiracy against Aegon, were put on pikes by the gates to the Red Keep.(wiki:Gyles_Belgrave.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
