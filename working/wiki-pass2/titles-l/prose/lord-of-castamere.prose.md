## Origins

The [Reynes](wiki:House_Reyne) of [Castamere](wiki:Castamere) submitted to [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes) when [Loreon I Lannister](wiki:Loreon_I_Lannister), the first [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock), married a Reyne daughter.(wiki:Lord_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) The title of Lord of Castamere became vacant when Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) destroyed the Reynes and their castle during the [Reyne-Tarbeck revolt](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt).(wiki:Lord_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

The title remains vacant until the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). For his role in the downfall of [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), Ser [Rolph Spicer](wiki:Rolph_Spicer) is named Lord of [Castamere](wiki:Castamere) by the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Lord_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
