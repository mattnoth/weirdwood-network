## Origins

### Hundred Kingdoms

During the [Hundred Kingdoms](wiki:Hundred_Kingdoms)(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) the ancient [Dun Fort](wiki:Dun_Fort) of Duskendale was the seat of petty kings of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) from [House Darklyn](wiki:House_Darklyn).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) They sometimes claimed the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush)(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and [Crackclaw Point](wiki:Crackclaw_Point),(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) but were unable to consolidate control.

At times the town swore allegiance to [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon), the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_King) from [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) For instance, [Monfryd I Durrandon](wiki:Monfryd_I_Durrandon) seized Duskendale and [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) after defeating petty Darklyn and [Mooton](wiki:House_Mooton) kings.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_durrandon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

During the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals), the Darklyn king at Duskendale forced [Togarion Bar Emmon](wiki:Togarion_Bar_Emmon) to abandon the northern Blackwater for [Massey's Hook](wiki:Massey's_Hook).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) [Benedict II Justman](wiki:Benedict_II_Justman), [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident), added Duskendale to his [Andal](wiki:Andal) realm, but [House Justman](wiki:House_Justman) was eventually extinguished by the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

The Storm King [Arlan III Durrandon](wiki:Arlan_III_Durrandon) conquered the entirety of the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) during his reign.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) A few generations before the [Wars of Conquest](wiki:Wars_of_Conquest), [Halleck Hoare](wiki:Halleck_Hoare), [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), extended the domain of [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) east to Duskendale and [Rosby](wiki:Rosby).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### Crownlands

At the start of [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), Lords Darklyn and [Mooton](wiki:House_Mooton) were slain [battling](wiki:Aegon's_first_test) [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), who was atop [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), and [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon). When Lord Darklyn's son surrendered Duskendale, [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) took much of the town's wealth but left it undamaged.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

Duskendale became part of the new [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) sworn to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), the kings controlling the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), and [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) began to surpass the older town during the reign of Aegon I.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) visited Duskendale's boatyards during their [royal progress](wiki:Royal_progress) in [51 AC](wiki:51_AC).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Duskendale continued to grow during Jaehaerys's reign.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Duskendale [hosted a tourney](wiki:Tourney_at_Duskendale_in_honor_of_Lord_Darklyn's_wedding) in [72 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) to celebrate the wedding of young Lord Darklyn to a daughter of Lord [Theomore Manderly](wiki:Theomore_Manderly). Prince [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)) was the victor in the melee and was [knighted](wiki:Knight) for his performance in the lists.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### Dance of the Dragons

[House Darklyn](wiki:House_Darklyn) supported the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) when the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) began. In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen)'s forces easily [sacked Duskendale](wiki:Sack_of_Duskendale), burned the harbor, and beheaded Lord [Gunthor Darklyn](wiki:Gunthor_Darklyn). This marked the start of the [greens](wiki:Greens)' offensive.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Gunthor's widow, Lady [Meredyth Darklyn](wiki:Meredyth_Darklyn), worried that Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) would burn Duskendale with his [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

After the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit), Gunthor's widow, Lady [Meredyth Darklyn](wiki:Meredyth_Darklyn), was convinced by Ser [Harrold Darke](wiki:Harrold_Darke) to briefly let Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) stay within Duskendale. Rhaenyra sold her [crown](wiki:Crown) to buy transport from Duskendale on the *[Violande](wiki:Violande)*.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) After Aegon II regained the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) marched on [Rosby](wiki:Rosby), [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth), and Duskendale, forcing their lords to pay heavy ransoms in King's Landing. Levies from Duskendale joined Borros's host, but the Darklyn knights went over to the [Lads](wiki:Lads) during the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) ships landed Lord [Leowyn Corbray](wiki:Leowyn_Corbray) army from the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) at Duskendale.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

During the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), Duskendale's trade was hampered by the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War) in the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). The town suffered from [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC),(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) but then profited during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

### Defiance of Duskendale

During the [Defiance of Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale) in [277 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) was held captive within Duskendale by Lord [Denys Darklyn](wiki:Denys_Darklyn), who hoped to win a charter and more autonomy for the town.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) After Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) rescued the king, House Darklyn was destroyed by Aerys and Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), and Duskendale and its lands and incomes were give to [House Rykker](wiki:House_Rykker).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) Even with the Darklyns gone, many of their kin remain in Duskendale, with names such as [Darke](wiki:House_Darke), [Darkwood](wiki:House_Darkwood), or [Dargood](wiki:House_Dargood). Ser [Dontos the Red](wiki:Dontos_Hollard), last of [House Hollard](wiki:House_Hollard), left Duskendale while a boy.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

### Recent History

Ser [Balman Byrch](wiki:Balman_Byrch) supposedly rode well in a tourney at Duskendale around [294 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-22)

[Addam of Duskendale](wiki:Addam_of_Duskendale) was a merchant from the town who traveled in eastern [Essos](wiki:Essos).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

## Appearances & Description

Duskendale spreads out around its harbor and has cobbled streets. The gatehouse opens to a market square. The castle of [House Rykker](wiki:House_Rykker) that overlooks the port is the [Dun Fort](wiki:Dun_Fort), a squat square stone castle with big drum towers.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) Duskendale is surrounded by strong walls(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) which shimmer palely in predawn.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Guardsmen wield spears and wear chain mail hauberks.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) The largest inn in Duskendale is the [Seven Swords](wiki:Seven_Swords), across from which lives a skilled painter, the [sister of the captain](wiki:Captain's_sister) of the gatehouse.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Duskendale contains multiple [septs](wiki:Sept).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

South of the town is a rocky headland that shelters the harbor from the storms of the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) and north of it rise chalk cliffs. The [Duskendale road](wiki:Duskendale_road) runs northeast beside the shore between the grey-green sea and low limestone hills, and the ruins of the [Hollard castle](wiki:Hollard_castle) are found where the road forks to [Crackclaw Point](wiki:Crackclaw_Point) and [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool). Fishing villages dot the road for miles.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) The [Rosby road](wiki:Rosby_road) connects [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to Duskendale.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Duskendale is marched upon by [northern](wiki:North) infantry under the command of [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover), Ser [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart), and [Harrion Karstark](wiki:Harrion_Karstark). They are met and defeated by Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) and Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) in a [battle at Duskendale](wiki:Battle_at_Duskendale), however. Robett is eventually exchanged for [Martyn Lannister](wiki:Martyn_Lannister) and sails north from Duskendale.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Rawoiafrobett_glover.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Lord Tarly's casualties are buried as heroes in Duskendale's [septs](wiki:Sept), while northern casualties are buried in a mass grave north of the town.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) With [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) recovering from the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), the port of Duskendale is busier than ever. The [Rosby road](wiki:Rosby_road) is safe until Duskendale, but the woods beyond are dangerous with outlaws and [broken men](wiki:Broken_men).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

[Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) visits the town in her search for [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark). She stays at the [Seven Swords](wiki:Seven_Swords), visits the [Dun Fort](wiki:Dun_Fort), and has her shield repainted from the arms of [House Lothston](wiki:House_Lothston) to those she [had once seen](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) at [Tarth](wiki:Tarth). Brienne encounters [a boy](wiki:Podrick_Payne) in the streets and a [dwarf brother](wiki:Dwarf_brother) in the Seven Swords. North of the town she sees remnants from the recent battle.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) Brienne takes the [Duskendale road](wiki:Duskendale_road) north to [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

[Luco Prestayn](wiki:Luco_Prestayn)'s *[Lady Bright](wiki:Lady_Bright)* sails from [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) for [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown), Duskendale, King's Landing, and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh).(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) *[Seastrider](wiki:Seastrider)* sails from [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) to Tyrosh, [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), Duskendale, and Maidenpool.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) While in Braavos, [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) spots trading galleys out of Duskendale.(wiki:Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)
