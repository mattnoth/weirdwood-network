## Origins

### Foundation

Two centuries before the [Doom](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), [Valyrians](wiki:Valyrians) took possession of [the island](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) and built a castle upon it, which became the westernmost outpost of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) The castle towers were shaped by [Valyrian magic](wiki:Magic) to look like [dragons](wiki:Dragon) to make the castle look fearsome, and they placed a thousand [gargoyles](wiki:Gargoyle) upon the walls.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10)

### Targaryen Lords

Twelve years prior to the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), [Aenar Targaryen](wiki:Aenar_Targaryen), the head of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), relocated his family, their five dragons, and all their wealth to Dragonstone, after his maiden daughter [Daenys](wiki:Daenys_Targaryen) predicted the destruction of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold). In Valyria their rivals saw this as an act of cowardly surrender.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Four of the dragons brought from Valyria eventually died on Dragonstone, leaving only [Balerion](wiki:Balerion). However, two [‎eggs](wiki:Dragon_egg) hatched and [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) and [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes) were born.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

Aenar ruled as the first [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), and was succeeded by his son, [‎Gaemon the Glorious](wiki:Gaemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenar)). Gaemon's children, [‎Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Gaemon)) and [Elaena](wiki:Elaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Gaemon)), ruled together as kin and a couple, and were succeeded by their own son, [Maegon](wiki:Maegon_Targaryen), and later Maegon's younger brother, [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon)). Aerys's three sons, [Aelyx](wiki:Aelyx_Targaryen), [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerys)), and [Daemion](wiki:Daemion_Targaryen) ruled Dragonstone in turn.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Daemion kept a [maester](wiki:Maester) at Dragonstone, as did his descendants.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) Daemion's son [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen_(son_of_Daemion)) inherited the seat. His only son by Lady [Valaena Velaryon](wiki:Valaena_Velaryon), [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), was the last Lord of Dragonstone before [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

### Aegon and His Sons

Lord [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) planned his invasion of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) from the Chamber of the [Painted Table](wiki:Painted_Table).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3) At the start of [the Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), Aegon called bannermen and allies to Dragonstone, including the [Velaryons](wiki:House_Velaryon) of [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark), the [Celtigars](wiki:House_Celtigar) of [Claw Isle](wiki:Claw_Isle), the [Bar Emmons](wiki:House_Bar_Emmon) of [Sharp Point](wiki:Sharp_Point), and the [Masseys](wiki:House_Massey) of [Stonedance](wiki:Stonedance).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) Aegon and his sisters, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) and [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen), prayed in the [castle sept](wiki:Sept_at_Dragonstone) before beginning their invasion.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2) The siblings conquered six of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), with only [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) remaining independent. Ser [Quenton Qoherys](wiki:Quenton_Qoherys), the master-at-arms of Dragonstone, was named [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal) by Aegon.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

Aegon established his new seat, the [Aegonfort](wiki:Aegonfort), where he first made landfall at the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush). The city of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), the new capital, eventually formed around it.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf41.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Aegon divided his reign between King's Landing, Dragonstone, and royal progresses throughout the realm.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2) The king raised his eldest son and heir, Prince [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) at King's Landing, while Queen Visenya raised Aegon's younger son, [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) at Dragonstone. In time, Maegor became known as the "[Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone)".(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) In [35 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the Aegonfort was torn down in preparation for the construction of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), so Aegon the Conqueror returned to his beloved Dragonstone. Two years later Aegon died from a stroke while in the Chamber of the Painted Table.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf41.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) burned the king's body in the castle yard.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)

Maegor remained the Prince of Dragonstone during the early years of Aenys I's reign, but after Maegor was exiled in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC) for taking [Alys Harroway](wiki:Alys_Harroway) as a second wife at Dragonstone, Aenys established "Prince of Dragonstone" as a formal title by appointing it to his own heir, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf42.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

At the start of the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), King Aenys I fled to the safety of Dragonstone, where he collapsed and died after hearing that his children, Prince Aegon and Princess [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), were besieged at [Crakehall](wiki:Crakehall).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf42.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) Aenys's body was burned at Dragonstone by [Quicksilver](wiki:Quicksilver), [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor), and [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3)

Maegor returned from his exile in [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), took Aegon's crown at Dragonstone, and claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), Aenys's widow, and her two younger children, Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and Princess [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), fled from Dragonstone to [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark),(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-4) but they were later kept at Dragonstone by Dowager Queen Visenya.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf43.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Prince Aegon was killed by Maegor in the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5)

Following Visenya's death in [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), Alyssa stole [Dark Sister](wiki:Dark_Sister) and escaped with her youngest children on Vermithor and Silverwing.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-6) After being found dead on the Iron Throne in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), Maegor's body was burned in King's Landing and his ashes were interred on Dragonstone.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-7)

### Jaehaerys the Conciliator

King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) continued the use of the title [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) for the heir apparent.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf44.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) When his advisors decided that Princess [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) should wed [Orryn Baratheon](wiki:Orryn_Baratheon) in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), Jaehaerys fled with his sister to Dragonstone, where the pair were wed by Septon [Oswyck](wiki:Oswyck). Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) traveled to Dragonstone and attempted to break up the marriage, but he acquiesced when faced with the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) and Ser [Merrell Bullock](wiki:Merrell_Bullock)'s garrison. Jaehaerys and Alysanne remained at Dragonstone for the rest of his minority. The young king received visitors in the Chamber of the [Painted Table](wiki:Painted_Table)(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) and trained with the Kingsguard, Merrell and his sons [Alyn](wiki:Alyn_Bullock) and [Howard](wiki:Howard_Bullock), and Ser [Elyas Scales](wiki:Elyas_Scales). The [Wise Women](wiki:Wise_Women) were sent by Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) to aid Alysanne.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

Jaehaerys departed Dragonstone for [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in the ninth month of [50 AC](wiki:50_AC).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) The king allowed his sister, [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), to hold Dragonstone in his name. Called the Queen in the West while at [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle), Rhaena became known as the Queen in the East.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Rhaena's daughter, Princess [Aerea Targaryen](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen), disliked living at Dragonstone, which she considered dull in comparison to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). [Elissa Farman](wiki:Elissa_Farman), Rhaena's close friend, eventually departed Dragonstone with three stolen [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_eggs).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Rhaena's husband, [Androw Farman](wiki:Androw_Farman), jumped to his death after admitting to having [poisoned](wiki:Poison) Maester [Culiper](wiki:Culiper), [Cassella Staunton](wiki:Cassella_Staunton), Septa [Maryam](wiki:Maryam), [Alayne Royce](wiki:Alayne_Royce), [Samantha Stokeworth](wiki:Samantha_Stokeworth), and [Lianna Velaryon](wiki:Lianna_Velaryon). Rhaena abandoned Dragonstone after the disappearance of Aerea on [Balerion](wiki:Balerion).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1) After Aerea's death, Rhaena spent her remaining years at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

In [62 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) Jaehaerys granted the title of [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) to [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)),(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) and the title passed to [Baelon Targaryen](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)) after Aemon's death in [92 AC](wiki:92_AC).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) Princess [Daella Targaryen](wiki:Daella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Jaehaerys_I)) wed Lord [Rodrik Arryn](wiki:Rodrik_Arryn) in Dragonstone's sept in [80 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). The ashes of Daella and her sister, Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Jaehaerys_I)), were interred at Dragonstone after their deaths. During the [First Quarrel](wiki:First_Quarrel), Queen Alysanne lived at Dragonstone in a self-imposed exile. She eventually died there in [100 AC](wiki:100_AC).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)

After Baelon's death and the [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) of [101 AC](wiki:101_AC), Jaehaerys named his grandson [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) as Prince of Dragonstone.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1) The ashes of Jaehaerys were interred beside those of Alysanne when the Old King died in [103 AC](wiki:103_AC).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-2)

### Dance of the Dragons

Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) became the first [Princess of Dragonstone](wiki:Princess_of_Dragonstone) in [105 AC](wiki:105_AC), when she was officially declared to be the heir of her father, King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf45.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) Angry at the decision, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) fled to Dragonstone with his paramour, [Mysaria](wiki:Mysaria), and lived there for half a year. Rhaenyra took Dragonstone as her seat in [113 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-3) Rhaenyra secretly wed Daemon at Dragonstone in [120 AC](wiki:120_AC).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-4)

Rhaenyra was on Dragonstone when her half brother, [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC) after the death of Viserys. Rhaenyra was [crowned at Dragonstone](wiki:Coronation_of_Rhaenyra_Targaryen), and from her own seat, she led the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) against Aegon II's [greens](wiki:Greens) during the ensuing [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Ser [Arryk Cargyll](wiki:Arryk_Cargyll), a supporter of Aegon, infiltrated Dragonstone, but he and his twin, Rhaenrya's [Erryk](wiki:Erryk_Cargyll), died fighting each other.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) [Dragonseeds](wiki:Dragonseed) attempted to tame [dragons](wiki:Dragon) on Dragonstone. The [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy) ignored Dragonstone during the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1)

[Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon) was formally installed as Prince of Dragonstone after the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) to Rhaenyra's forces.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Ser [Robert Quince](wiki:Robert_Quince), castellan of Dragonstone, protected Lady [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen) during the war.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1) Joffrey died attempting to stop the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit). After fleeing the capital, Rhaenyra insisted on returning to Dragonstone in late [130 AC](wiki:130_AC). Ser [Alfred Broome](wiki:Alfred_Broome) had orchestrated the [fall of Dragonstone](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone) to Aegon the Elder, however, and the king fed his half sister to [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre) in the yard. Baela and Rhaenyra's son, Prince [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), were imprisoned in the dungeon. Sunfyre died of his wounds in the outer yard of the castle.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

At the end of 130 AC, Aegon II returned to King's Landing from Dragonstone on [Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) ships.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1) After Aegon the Elder died of [poison](wiki:Poison) in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC), Dragonstone's garrison refused to submit to Aegon the Younger, King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). The servants of the castle rebelled, however, and yielded Dragonstone to [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

After the death of Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), his body was buried aboard *[Sea Snake](wiki:Sea_Snake)* in the waters east of Dragonstone.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) Alyn and Baela wed in Dragonstone's sept in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1) Lady [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)), Baela's twin, resided at Dragonstone after learning to ride [Morning](wiki:Morning). After the death of Rhaena's husband, Ser [Corwyn Corbray](wiki:Corwyn_Corbray), Baela traveled from [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) to Dragonstone to comfort her sister.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

### Later History

King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen)'s eldest son, Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), became [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) upon his father ascension. However, he found Dragonstone such a gloomy abode that he preferred to be styled [Prince of Summerhall](wiki:Prince_of_Summerhall) instead.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf414.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

### Robert's Rebellion

King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) attempted to bring forth [dragons](wiki:Dragon) from [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_eggs) found in the depths of Dragonstone.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) The relationship between Aerys and his heir, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), soured, and when Rhaegar married Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell) in [280 AC](wiki:280_AC), the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) moved from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to Dragonstone, where his daughter [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) was born later that same year.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-1)

When the news of Rhaegar's death at the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) reached King's Landing, King Aerys decided to sent his pregnant sister-wife, Queen [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen), and his only surviving child, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), now the Prince of Dragonstone, to Dragonstone, to keep them safe from the approaching rebel army.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf53.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

Rhaella crowned Viserys after the death of Aerys in the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rawoiafrhaella_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) Nine months after leaving King's Landing, Rhaella gave birth to Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), who was granted the title "Princess of Dragonstone".(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) However, Rhaella died in labor, and the Targaryen [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet), which had been protecting the island, was mostly destroyed during a fierce storm. Huge stone blocks were ripped from Dragonstone's parapets and sent hurtling into the wild waters of the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), and the princess is thus known as Daenerys Stormborn.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-1)

King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), who had claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), ordered his younger brother, [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), to built a new royal fleet for [House Baratheon of King's Landing](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing). With the Targaryen fleet destroyed, the garrison at Dragonstone was prepared to sell the Targaryen children to Robert. However, before they could act on this plan, Ser [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry) and several other loyal retainers smuggled Viserys and Daenerys into exile, sailing to the [Braavosian coast](wiki:Braavosian_coast).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-2) Stannis led the successful [assault on Dragonstone](wiki:Assault_on_Dragonstone).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11)(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-3)(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

### House Baratheon

King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) named his younger brother, [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), as [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone) instead of the wealthier position of [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End), which was given to their young brother, [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon). Stannis resented this and believed it to be an intentional slight,(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-12) which Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) agrees with.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-1) According to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin), however, Robert had not necessarily meant it as such.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rssm1046.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) The castle had traditionally been the seat of the heir to the Iron Throne, the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), during most of the Targaryen dynasty, so the then-childless Robert was granting it to his heir at that time, Stannis.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rssm1046.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-1)

Stannis became the head of [House Baratheon of Dragonstone](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_Dragonstone). When Stannis left for [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to serve on the [small council](wiki:Small_council) as [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships), Ser [Axell Florent](wiki:Axell_Florent) was named castellan of Dragonstone.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) has seen Dragonstone.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

## Appearances & Description

A grim place,(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Dragonstone was built by [Valyrians](wiki:Valyrians) with arcane arts,(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf29.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) fire,(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) and sorcery.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Capable of liquefying and reshaping stone with [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame), the [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) used their [magic](wiki:Magic) to shape Dragonstone to look like multiple [dragons](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf774_15-0)(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rssm5683.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

Dragon architecture can be found throughout the castle, such as small dragons framing gates and dragon claws holding torches.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) A pair of great wings cover the armory and smithy, and dragon tails form archways and staircases.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3) The citadel of Dragonstone is wrought all of [black stone](wiki:Dragonstone_(material)).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Doors can be set in the mouths of stone dragons.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Instead of merlons, [gargoyles](wiki:Gargoyle) and grotesques serve as brooding crenellations along the three curtain walls.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Designs include [basilisks](wiki:Basilisk), [cockatrices](wiki:Cockatrice), [demons](wiki:Demons), [griffins](wiki:Griffin), [hellhounds](wiki:Hellhound), [manticores](wiki:Manticore), minotaurs, [wyverns](wiki:Wyvern), and other creatures.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4) Statues in the shape of dragons can be found all over the castle.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Dragonstone has a castle yard(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) and a library.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) There is a fishing village with a port beneath the curtain walls.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) was fond of the salty air of Dragonstone, which smells of smoke and brimstone.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rfab3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Notable locations at Dragonstone include:

It holds a large table, the [Painted Table](wiki:Painted_Table), carved and painted in the form of a detailed map of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

[Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), returns to his seat from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) shortly after King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) travels north to offer the position of [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) Stannis closes off the [island of Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)),(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-13) making it difficult for [Varys](wiki:Varys) to know for certain what Stannis has been doing,(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) though he hears rumors that he is gathering swords.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)

After learning that the children of Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) were not fathered by Robert, Eddard instructs [Tomard](wiki:Tomard) to personally deliver a letter to Stannis at Dragonstone.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Tom is killed in the throne room of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) before the *[Wind Witch](wiki:Wind_Witch)* sets sail,(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) however, and Lord Stark is imprisoned in the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-1)

Varys reports to Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) that he has heard rumors that Stannis is building ships, hiring sellswords, and is bringing a [shadowbinder](wiki:Shadowbinder) from [Asshai](wiki:Asshai).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

### A Clash of Kings

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), the poor lands of Dragonstone give King Stannis too few supporters to engage the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) in battle.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-14) In the [Great Hall](wiki:Great_Hall_(Dragonstone)), Maester [Cressen](wiki:Cressen) dies after a failed attempt to poison [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), a [red priestess](wiki:Red_priest) serving Stannis, with the [strangler](wiki:Strangler).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-15)

The [queen's men](wiki:Queen's_men) sack the [sept at Dragonstone](wiki:Sept_at_Dragonstone) so Melisandre can burn the statues of [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) outside of the castle's gates.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

### A Storm of Swords

After his defeat at the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Stannis retreats to Dragonstone with some fifteen hundred men and the ships of [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-4) Stannis broods in the [Stone Drum](wiki:Stone_Drum), refusing to see anyone but Melisandre.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) is arrested for his plan to kill Melisandre(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) and locked up in the cells of Dragonstone, where he is later joined by Stannis's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Alester Florent](wiki:Alester_Florent).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) When Davos is brought before Stannis in the Chamber of the [Painted Table](wiki:Painted_Table), he is pardoned and named Hand in Alester's stead.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-5) Melisandre wishes to "wake a dragon from stone", insisting she needs to sacrifice Robert's [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son, [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm), to do so.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-9) Davos wonders whether the stone dragons which can be found all over the castle could be real dragons turned to stone.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-10) He later prays that they will never come to life.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

After Davos urges Stannis to sail for the [Wall](wiki:Wall), presenting him with Maester [Aemon's](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) letter asking for aid for the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch),(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Stannis sets sail for the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) Ser [Rolland Storm](wiki:Rolland_Storm), the Bastard of [Nightsong](wiki:Nightsong), is made castellan for the duration of Stannis's absence.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)

### A Feast for Crows

A giddy Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) believes that Stannis has given up and gone into exile.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) She judges that two thousand [Lannister soldiers](wiki:House_Lannister) should be enough to battle the small garrison which Stannis had left behind on Dragonstone, and so keeps them in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to await the arrival of Lord [Paxter Redwyne](wiki:Paxter_Redwyne)'s ships which will carry them from [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay) to Dragonstone.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0) Only a few fishing boats remain to defy the [Redwyne fleet](wiki:Redwyne_fleet),(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) and Paxter encamps his men before the walls of the castle and begins the [siege of Dragonstone](wiki:Siege_of_Dragonstone), intending to starve the defenders out.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)

After the [taking of the Shields](wiki:Taking_of_the_Shields) by the [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) begs Cersei to send word to Dragonstone, as Paxter's fleet is the only one large enough to protect the [Reach](wiki:Reach) from the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn). Cersei refuses, insisting that Stannis's hold over Dragonstone is a knife at King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon)'s throat, and that the Redwyne fleet will only be released once Dragonstone has fallen. Loras asks Cersei permission to take Dragonstone by storming the walls of the castle, so the Redwyne fleet is no longer needed at the island and can return to the Reach.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-1)

Half a day after his arrival, after Rolland refuses to settle the matter by single combat, Loras orders the assault. The castle gates are broken by a ram, and the defenders fall back to an inner keep after the curtain wall falls. Loras leads the attack there as well. Although the Knight of Flowers is successful in taking Dragonstone, he is severely injured in the battle, and remains on Dragonstone, cared for by maesters.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) Cersei becomes convinced that [Aurane Waters](wiki:Aurane_Waters) wants Dragonstone, but she considers that seat to be too much above Aurane's birth and station.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Loras remains at Dragonstone, still dying of his wounds.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) His men search every inch of the castle, but according to Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) cannot find any wealth that might have been left behind. Lord Regent [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) questions the thoroughness of the search, but thinks Stannis would have taken any wealth before he left.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

In the [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) names [a hill](wiki:Dragonstone_(hill)) "Dragonstone", after the citadel she had been born in.(wiki:Dragonstone.cite_ref-Radwd71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0)
