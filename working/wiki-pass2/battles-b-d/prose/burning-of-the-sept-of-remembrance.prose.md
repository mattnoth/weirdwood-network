## Origins

During the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) was the only survivor of the [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) between him and Ser [Damon Morrigen](wiki:Damon_Morrigen). The [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) at the [Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Sept_of_Remembrance) debated as Maegor lay unconscious for weeks; some wanted to end the rebellion as the gods had supported Maegor, while others wanted to continue.(wiki:Burning_of_the_Sept_of_Remembrance.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

Maegor awoke after being treated by [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower).(wiki:Burning_of_the_Sept_of_Remembrance.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) On the thirtieth day after the trial, hundreds of Warrior's Sons gathered at the Sept for their morning prayers. Maegor mounted his [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), and unleashed [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire) upon the Sept of Remembrance without warning. As the sept burned, some tried to flee, but were cut down by the archers and spearmen that Maegor had ready. It is said that the screams of the burning and dying men were heard throughout [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). The burning was so immense that scholars claim there was a pall over the capital for seven days.(wiki:Burning_of_the_Sept_of_Remembrance.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Afterwards, Maegor had the ruins of the [sept](wiki:Sept) cleared, and ordered the construction of a large domed stable, the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit), where [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen) could house their dragons.(wiki:Burning_of_the_Sept_of_Remembrance.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
