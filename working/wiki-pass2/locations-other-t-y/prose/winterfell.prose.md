## Origins

### Older History

According to legend, Winterfell was built by [Brandon the Builder](wiki:Brandon_the_Builder), who was aided by [giants](wiki:Giants),(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) after the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night) ended eight thousand years ago.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) [Maesters](wiki:Maesters) believe it was built in pieces over different eras, since the ancient Starks did not level the ground and plan it as a single structure.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2) Some scholars have suggested there were once linked [ringforts](wiki:Ringfort) where Winterfell now stands.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-3)

For most of recorded history Winterfell was the seat of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark), the [Kings in the North](wiki:Kings_in_the_North) and later [Wardens of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North) after King [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark) bent the knee to [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and his [dragons](wiki:Dragons). During the wars between the Kings of Winter from House Stark and the [Red Kings](wiki:Red_King) from [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton), Winterfell was burned by Kings [Royce II](wiki:Royce_II_Bolton) and [Royce IV Bolton](wiki:Royce_IV_Bolton) of the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort) before the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

Maester [Kennet](wiki:Kennet_(maester)) determined that the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep), the oldest surviving part of the castle,(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-7) was built some time after the Andals came.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-4) Winterfell's inner walls were raised approximately two thousand years ago, and the outer walls were built during the reign of King [Edrick Snowbeard](wiki:Edrick_Stark).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-5) The heads of some giants are said to have been displayed on Winterfell's walls.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5)

According to [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) legend, [Bael the Bard](wiki:Bael_the_Bard) once entered Winterfell under the guise of a singer named Sygerrik. Leaving behind only a [winter rose](wiki:Winter_rose), Bael supposedly disappeared with the daughter of Lord [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_daughterless)) after tricking the lord. The daughter eventually reappeared with an infant, a future Lord Stark, the story claiming that they had been hiding in the [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) The legend is not recorded in the chronicles of Winterfell, however.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-6)

[Harvest feasts](wiki:Harvest_feast) have been hosted in Winterfell.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### House Targaryen

King [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark) called the banners to Winterfell during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), but the King Who Knelt eventually submitted to [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) at the [Inn of the Kneeling Man](wiki:Inn_of_the_Kneeling_Man) on the [Trident](wiki:Trident).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) King Aegon I visited Winterfell in [33 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) during the last of his [progresses](wiki:Royal_progress) throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) visited Winterfell in [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Lord [Alaric Stark](wiki:Alaric_Stark) took Alysanne hunting in the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood) and allowed her to explore the [castle library](wiki:Library_Tower). Lord Stark showed Jaehaerys the tomb of his brother, Lord [Walton](wiki:Walton_Stark_(son_of_Brandon)), in the crypts.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

### Dance of the Dragons

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) visited Winterfell and gained Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) for the cause of Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and the [blacks](wiki:Blacks). According to [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom), Jacaerys slept with [Sara Snow](wiki:Sara_Snow) and wed her in the [castle's godswood](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell), although the [fool](wiki:Fool)'s story is doubted by [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn). The archmaester is also skeptical of Mushroom's claim that [Vermax](wiki:Vermax) left a clutch of [dragon's eggs](wiki:Dragon's_egg) at Winterfell.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

As the war progressed, Lord Cregan gathered hosts to Winterfell, [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton), and [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) before marching south toward [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

After the war ended in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC), blizzards descended on Winterfell. Lord [Benjicot Blackwood](wiki:Benjicot_Blackwood) travelled to Winterfell for the wedding of his aunt, [Black Aly](wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood), to Lord Stark in the godswood.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

### Eddard Stark

At the start of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) left the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) and crossed the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) and the [Bite](wiki:Bite) to return to the north, where he raised the northern banners.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) His younger brother [Benjen Stark](wiki:Benjen_Stark) remained in the castle for the war's duration as the Stark in Winterfell.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-46)

[Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) once accompanied [Lord Commander Qorgyle](wiki:Lord_Commander_Qorgyle) in a visit from the [Wall](wiki:Wall) to Winterfell to see Lord Eddard.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) When [Alys Karstark](wiki:Alys_Karstark) was six, her father, Lord [Rickard](wiki:Rickard_Karstark), brought her to Winterfell to meet Eddard's heir, [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark). Alys danced with a courteous Robb and a sullen [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) during the feast.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) Lord [Yohn Royce](wiki:Yohn_Royce) visited the Starks when his son, Ser [Waymar Royce](wiki:Waymar_Royce), joined the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), and Bronze Yohn defeated Eddard and Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) in the yard.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) The [Cerwyns](wiki:House_Cerwyn) often visit Winterfell because [Castle Cerwyn](wiki:Castle_Cerwyn) is only half a day away.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

## Appearances & Description

Winterfell is a huge castle complex spanning several acres and encircled by two massive granite walls.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) The [winter town](wiki:Winter_town) is a village found outside of the castle.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

Winterfell was built around an [ancient godswood](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell) and over natural hot springs. The water is piped through walls and chambers to heat them, making Winterfell more comfortable than other castles during the harsh northern winters. A dozen small courtyards have open pools.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Winterfell has multiple yards.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Weapons training and [horseriding](wiki:Horse) take place in a large yard, where quintains can be set up for jousting.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Archery butts are found in the old inner ward, which is near the [broken tower](wiki:Broken_tower).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Winterfell's towers and halls have diamond-shaped window panes.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### Structures

- The [Great Keep](wiki:Great_Keep_(Winterfell)) is the innermost castle and stronghold of the castle complex. It was built over natural hot springs to keep it warm.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) The Great Keep contains bedchambers for [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) as well as the [solar](wiki:Solar) of Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The building is connected to the armory by a covered bridge.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) From a window on the bridge, one can see the entire yard.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Beneath the Great Keep are cellars with narrow windows.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

- The [Great Hall](wiki:Great_Hall_of_Winterfell) is used for receiving guests and the place where the household dines together, including the [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell). It is made of grey stone(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) and has wide doors made of [oak](wiki:Oak) and [iron](wiki:Iron),(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) which opens to the castle yard, and a rear exit leads to a dimly-lit gallery.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Inside, it can hold eight long rows of trestle tables, four to each side of the central aisle,(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) and the hall can seat five hundred people.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) There is a raised platform for noble guests, and the walls are covered with banners.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) The hall contains the high seat of the old [Kings in the North](wiki:Kings_in_the_North). The seat's cold stone has been polished by the many lords who have sat upon it, and its massive arms are decorated with the carved heads of snarling [direwolves](wiki:Direwolves).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

- The [small sept](wiki:Lady_Catelyn's_sept) was built for Lady [Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), a [southron](wiki:Southron), by her husband, Lord Eddard.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

- The [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep), a squat and round drum tower, is the oldest surviving part of the castle but is no longer in use. Around it lies a [lichyard](wiki:Lichyard) where the [Kings of Winter](wiki:Kings_of_Winter) would bury their loyal servants. The keep has [gargoyles](wiki:Gargoyle) atop it.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Maester [Kennet](wiki:Kennet_(maester)) determined it was built after the [Andals](wiki:Andals) arrived.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

- The [broken tower](wiki:Broken_tower), also known as the Burned Tower, was once the tallest watchtower in Winterfell. Over 140 years ago a lightning strike set it afire and the top third collapsed inward, but no one rebuilt it.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) It stands behind the old inner ward. [Crows](wiki:Crow) nest atop the broken tower.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

- The ancient [godswood of Winterfell](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell) has stood untouched for ten thousand years, with three acres of old packed earth and close-together trees creating a dense canopy, which the castle was built around. At the center of the grove stands an ancient [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) with [a face carved into it](wiki:Heart_tree), standing over a pool of black water.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) Across the godswood from the heart tree, beneath the windows of the [Guest House](wiki:Guest_House), an underground hot spring feeds three small pools, with a moss-covered wall looming above them. The godswood is enclosed by walls, and is accessed by a main iron gate, or smaller wooden ones.

- The [Glass Gardens](wiki:Glass_Gardens)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) is a greenhouse heated by the hot springs, which turn it into a place of moist warmth.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) It is used to grow fruits, vegetables, and flowers.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) The garden has green and yellow glass panes(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) locked in frames.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

- The [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell), located near the First Keep, is where members of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) are buried. The underground crypts are long and narrow, with pillars moving two by two along its length. Between pillars stand the sepulchers of the Starks of Winterfell, the likenesses of the dead seated on thrones, with iron swords set before them to keep the restless spirits from wandering, and snarling [direwolves](wiki:Direwolves) at their feet. The crypts are deep under the earth, cavernous and bigger than the complex above ground. They are accessed by a twisting stone stair and a huge ironwood door that lies at a slant to the floor. The stair continues below to older levels where the most ancient [Kings in the North](wiki:Kings_in_the_North) are entombed.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

- The [Bell Tower](wiki:Bell_Tower) is connected to the [rookery](wiki:Rookery) by a bridge. The bridge is covered and runs from the fourth floor of the tower to the second floor of the rookery.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)

- The [maester](wiki:Maester)'s turret is below the rookery.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

- The [Library Tower](wiki:Library_Tower) houses the library at Winterfell. A stonework staircase winds about its exterior.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)

- The [Guards Hall](wiki:Guards_Hall) is in line with the Bell Tower, and further back, the First Keep.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

- The [sickroom](wiki:Sickroom), located in a tower(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) with steps and a landing,(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) is across the bailey from the Library Tower.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

- The armory, which contains the castle's forge,(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) is located near the Guards Hall(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3) and is a short walk from the yard.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) A covered bridge with a window connects the armory to the Great Keep and overlooks the yard.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) An ancient [owl](wiki:Owl) sleeps in a dusty loft found above the old armory.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-4) The godswood has a [sentinel](wiki:Sentinel) tree by the armory wall.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-5)

- Winterfell has undercrofts and cellars.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) The castle also has dungeons,(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1) including tower cells.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

### Walls

Winterfell is a huge castle complex spanning several acres, defended by two massive walls of grey granite with a wide moat between them.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) The outer wall is eighty feet high, while the inner is one hundred feet high.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) There are guard turrets on the outer wall and more than thirty watch turrets on the crenelated inner walls.

The great [main gates of Winterfell](wiki:Main_gates_of_Winterfell)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) have a gatehouse made of two huge crenelated bulwarks which flank the arched gate(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4) and a drawbridge that opens into the market square of the [winter town](wiki:Winter_town).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

A narrow tunnel inside the inner wall stretches halfway around the castle, allowing travel from the south gate all the way to the north gate without interruption.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-6)

- The [Hunter's Gate](wiki:Hunter's_Gate) is close to the kennels and the kitchens. It opens directly onto open fields and the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood), so people can come and go without having to cross through the winter town. It is favored by hunting parties.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

- The [East Gate](wiki:East_Gate)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) or east gate(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) leads to the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4) The Kingsroad Gate(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) may be another name for the same gate.

- The [Battlements Gate](wiki:Battlements_Gate) is a small arched postern in the inner wall. It crosses the moat between the walls but does not have a passageway through the outer wall.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

### Winter Town

The [winter town](wiki:Winter_town), so called because it is mostly deserted during summer, is where smallfolk gather in winter. It is outside the walls of Winterfell. Its market square is filled with wooden stalls for merchants, and its streets are muddy and lined with rows of houses made from logs and undressed stone. Less than one in five are occupied during the summer, but they fill up when winter arrives.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) The kingsroad, which runs east of Winterfell,(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-35) is beyond the winter town.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) There is an inn in the winter town, the [Smoking Log](wiki:Smoking_Log).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) hosts King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and the royal family at Winterfell after the death of Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn). They visit the [crypt](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell) so Robert can pay his respects to [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) Eddard hosts a [feast in honor of King Robert's visit to Winterfell](wiki:Feast_in_honor_of_King_Robert's_visit_to_Winterfell).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) While climbing toward the [broken tower](wiki:Broken_tower), [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) is pushed from the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep) after finding Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) cavorting with his sister, Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-8)

[Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) remains in the castle when Eddard travels south to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to serve as the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). A fire is started in the [Library Tower](wiki:Library_Tower) in order to distract the guards during the [catspaw](wiki:Catspaw)'s attempt on Bran's life in the [sickroom](wiki:Sickroom).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)

When Robb hears that Eddard has been taken prisoner in King's Landing, he raises the [northern](wiki:North) bannerman and marches south to rescue his father. Bran remains as the Stark in Winterfell. The direwolf [Lady](wiki:Lady_(direwolf)) is buried in the [lichyard](wiki:Lichyard) of the First Keep.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) appoints Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) as castellan of Winterfell.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) While at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), Robb is proclaimed [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) by northern and [rivermen](wiki:Riverlands) alike.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Bran, the Stark in Winterfell in Robb's absence, hosts numerous northern lords for the [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast_(299)).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3) Songs performed during the festival include "[Iron Lances](wiki:Iron_Lances)", "[The Burning of the Ships](wiki:The_Burning_of_the_Ships)", and "[The Bear and the Maiden Fair](wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair)".

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Winterfell is captured in a [surprise attack](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell) by the [ironmen](wiki:Ironmen) led by [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1) He rules the castle for several weeks, but he lacks the manpower to hold it indefinitely against the Stark bannermen. [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) refuses to help her brother hold the castle, which is far from the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) assembles nearly two thousand northmen to retake Winterfell from Theon. [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), the castellan of the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort), leads the [Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) soldiers in betraying their fellow northmen, however, and Rodrik's army is destroyed in a [battle outside the gates of Winterfell](wiki:Battle_outside_the_gates_of_Winterfell). Ramsay then double-crosses Theon, takes him prisoner, and has the Dreadfort men [sack and burn Winterfell](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) The ceiling of the [Great Hall](wiki:Great_Hall_of_Winterfell) and one whole side of the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep) collapse during the sack of Winterfell. A shallow steaming lake forms beneath the [Library Tower](wiki:Library_Tower), while the Maester's turret and the bridge connecting the [Bell Tower](wiki:Bell_Tower) to the [rookery](wiki:Rookery) are destroyed. Bran and [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark), undetected in the crypts, make their escape from Winterfell in the aftermath.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-5)

### A Storm of Swords

The surviving women and children of Winterfell are taken to the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort) by [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow),(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-54) who also holds [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) as a hostage.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

Due to the alleged deaths of Bran and Rickon Stark, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) is now considered the heir to Winterfell. For this title Lady [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell) plots to have Sansa married to the heir of [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), [Willas Tyrell](wiki:Willas_Tyrell). When Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) is informed of this he has his son, [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), marry her instead, to both Sansa and Tyrion's reluctance.

After King [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) marries [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling), he tells his mother, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), that he plans to legitimize his brother, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), and name him heir of Winterfell and [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) should he and Jeyne not have a child. Due to Sansa's marriage to Tyrion, Robb fears that Sansa will give Tyrion a son and thus control of Winterfell which Robb wants to prevent. Catelyn pleads that Robb consider naming his other sister, [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark), as heir, but Robb insists that no one has seen Arya since his father [Eddard's](wiki:Eddard_Stark) death and that Arya is likely also dead. Robb signs the decree naming Jon his heir,(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rawoiafrobb_stark.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) but the whereabouts of the will are unknown after Robb's death in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).

As Sansa hides at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) after [the murder](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), she reminisces fondly about Winterfell and makes a castle of snow in its likeness with the aid of [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-6)

At the [Wall](wiki:Wall), in exchange for Jon Snow recognizing [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) as king, Stannis offers to legitimize him, which would make him Lord Jon Stark of Winterfell. However, Jon refuses Stannis and he instead becomes the 998th [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch).

### A Feast for Crows

[Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) informs Lord [Rodrik Harlaw](wiki:Rodrik_Harlaw) that when she and her [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) searched Winterfell for Theon they found only scattered bodies scavenged by wolves.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) reminds [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) that he had hoped to bestow Winterfell on him, and that his offer was thrown in his face. Jon replies that by right Winterfell should go to his sister, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark). Stannis asks Jon if he is eager to see the Imp, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), perched on [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark)'s seat. He promises Jon that as long as he, Stannis, is alive that will not happen.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0)

Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), moves the wedding of his newly-legitimized son, [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), to "[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark)", who is actually [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole), from [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton) to Winterfell.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) When the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) arrive at Winterfell, Roose finds the ruined castle a refuge of more than two dozen squatters. When he tells them he will be merciful if they serve well, they are used as labor to partially restore Winterfell. They rebuild burned gates and raise a new roof over the Great Hall. After the work is completed Roose has them all hanged; true to his word he is merciful and does not flay one.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)

[Ramsay marries "Arya"](wiki:Wedding_of_Ramsay_Bolton_and_Arya_Stark) in the [godswood](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell) and becomes [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell), with [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) giving Jeyne away. Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) furnishes the wedding feast and asks the singer [Abel](wiki:Mance_Rayder) to sing of the [Rat Cook](wiki:Rat_Cook).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2)

During a winter storm that hits Winterfell, squires build snowmen on the battlements. A dozen snowy lords are built to command them, including depictions of Lord Manderly, Lord [Harwood Stout](wiki:Harwood_Stout), Lady [Barbrey Dustin](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin), and [Whoresbane Umber](wiki:Whoresbane_Umber). Theon and Barbrey discover that swords are missing from the [crypts](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-2) Tensions heighten during the blizzard as several [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) and northerners are found dead.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)

After Freys fight with [Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) men, Roose sends the Freys out the [main gate](wiki:Main_gates_of_Winterfell) and the Manderlys out the [east gate](wiki:East_gate). They are to march on Stannis's army, which is said to be three days [march from Winterfell](wiki:March_on_Winterfell). Amidst the commotion, Theon and "Arya" flee the castle by passing through the [Battlements Gate](wiki:Battlements_Gate), climbing a stair to the battlements, and leaping into the snow.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1)

It is believed among Stannis's army that [Arnolf Karstark](wiki:Arnolf_Karstark), castellan of [Karhold](wiki:Karhold), would receive Winterfell should it be taken from the Boltons.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0)

### The Winds of Winter

Roose has sent out the Freys and Manderlys, as well as Ramsay and his men, but he has kept much of his own strength behind in Winterfell. According to Theon, who has been brought to Stannis by [Mors Umber](wiki:Mors_Umber), Roose is keeping his own men back as a reserve, as he once did in the riverlands, to weaken potential rivals.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Winds_61-0)

Mors had his troops built pits outside the main gate. The Freys ride into them, causing [Aenys Frey](wiki:Aenys_Frey) to break his neck.(wiki:Winterfell.cite_ref-Winds_61-1)
