## Origins

After driving [pirates](wiki:Pirates) from the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) in [96 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy) gradually became greedier with their tolls for passage. Along with [money](wiki:Currency), [Lysene](wiki:Lys) captains also began taking women, girls, and boys to turn into [slaves](wiki:Slavery) for their [pillow houses](wiki:Pillow_house). Johanna was enslaved when she was fifteen, but her stingy uncle, Lord [Swann](wiki:House_Swann), refused to ransom her. After being sold to a pillow house, Johanna became a celebrated [courtesan](wiki:Courtesan) known as the Black Swan, the eventual ruler of Lys in all but name.(wiki:Johanna_Swann.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

In [131 AC](wiki:131_AC) the Lysene admiral [Sharako Lohar](wiki:Sharako_Lohar) was slain by a rival in competition for Johanna's affections. Since he had been blamed by [Myrmen](wiki:Myr) and [Tyroshi](wiki:Tyrosh) for the disastrous [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet) in the previous year, Sharako's death was initially viewed by Lyseni as a political murder orchestrated by the Myrish. The [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy) collapsed into the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War).(wiki:Johanna_Swann.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Johanna's fate is unknown.
