## Appearances & Description

Young(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Balon is big across the chest, with arms thick with muscle.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) He is burlier but not as tall nor handsome as his [Sworn Brother](wiki:Sworn_Brother), Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Balon is courtly,(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) modest,(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) obedient,(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) and valiant.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Balon's [greathelm](wiki:Greathelm) is decorated with wide white wings.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) The trappings of his [horse](wiki:Horse) display battling black-and-white [swans](wiki:Swan).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) The [silver](wiki:Silver) brooch that clasps his cloak is decorated with [ivory](wiki:Ivory) and [onyx](wiki:Onyx) swans.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Balon is good with a [lance](wiki:Lance), better with a [morningstar](wiki:Morningstar), and exceptional with a [bow](wiki:Bow).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Ser Balon attends court in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with a retinue of [knights](wiki:Knight) and [sworn swords](wiki:Sworn_sword).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) He participates in the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney), where he defeats [Alyn](wiki:Alyn), but is later beaten by Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) [Anguy](wiki:Anguy) outshoots Balon and [Jalabhar Xho](wiki:Jalabhar_Xho) in the [archery](wiki:Archer) competition.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Balon participates in the hunt for a [white hart](wiki:White_hart) in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood) with King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) However, he returns to the capital before the king is wounded by a [boar](wiki:Boar).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Balon is suggested by Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) as a possible ally for Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), in a power struggle against Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), but Ned chooses to recruit the [gold cloaks](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) instead.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

Balon remains at court after Robert succumbs to his wounds. He warns Ser [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard) about approaching Ned's daughter, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), when King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon) holds court.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Ser Balon takes part in the [tourney on King Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_King_Joffrey's_name_day), unhorsing [Morros Slynt](wiki:Morros_Slynt) in the second tilt of the day.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Sansa hopes that a mysterious note summoning her to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s [godswood](wiki:Godswood) came from Balon, but the ally turns out to be Dontos.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

During a dinner given by Lady [Tanda Stokeworth](wiki:Tanda_Stokeworth), Lord [Gyles Rosby](wiki:Gyles_Rosby) raises his glass "to the health of the king", and Balon replies that three glasses would be needed (for Joffrey, his uncle [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), and [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), the new [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North)). The joke is meant to raise the spirits of the guests, but Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) reports it to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), the acting Hand. Tyrion decides not to follow up on it as he believes Balon made a harmless jest.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

Balon supports Joffrey in King's Landing during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). His older brother, Ser [Donnel](wiki:Donnel_Swann), first serves Renly, but he then joins [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) after Renly's death at [Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)). Their father, Lord [Gulian Swann](wiki:Gulian_Swann), pleads [illness](wiki:Illness) while remaining at [Stonehelm](wiki:Stonehelm).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

Balon is among the party that sees Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) off to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), watching the ceremony in the company of Queen Regent Cersei, and he bears a [Lannister lion](wiki:House_Lannister) banner alongside the [Baratheon stag](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing) carried by Ser [Aron Santagar](wiki:Aron_Santagar). The party is attacked by a raging mob, but Balon survives [the riot](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Ser [Preston Greenfield](wiki:Preston_Greenfield) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) is killed in the riot,(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) and Cersei chooses Balon to replace him. Tyrion approves of the choice.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

Tyrion plans for three Kingsguard knights—Balon, Ser [Mandon Moore](wiki:Mandon_Moore), and [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane)—to lead sorties during the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Wielding his mace, a gore-spattered Balon leads Lannister forces alongside Tyrion and Mandon.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) He makes it back inside the city walls once Stannis's forces overwhelm them temporarily.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

After the battle, Balon and the other remaining Kingsguard spend hours knighting more than six hundred men.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) His brother Donnel bends the knee to Joffrey.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### A Storm of Swords

Ser Balon continues to serve in the Kingsguard at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) and its fortress [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Unlike his new Sworn Brother, Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell), Balon is in attendance when Tyrion marries Sansa.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

When Joffrey first wields [Widow's Wail](wiki:Widow's_Wail), a [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword gifted to him at his wedding, Balon has to step back quickly to avoid the cut. The guests laugh at the look on Balon's face.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) The young king dies at his [wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

When [Steelshanks Walton](wiki:Steelshanks_Walton) and [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) escort Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), back to King's landing, Balon intervenes when Loras confronts Brienne and Jaime.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

Balon is called as the first witness against Tyrion in his trial for the murder of King Joffrey. Balon praises Tyrion's bravery at the Blackwater and does not believe he killed Joffrey. Balon does admit that Tyrion had struck the king after the riot of King's Landing. Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) remarks that Kingsguard of old would have removed Tyrion's hand for striking the royal personage.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

Jaime notes that Balon is well suited to the white cloak. When the Lord Commander holds a meeting of the Kingsguard, Balon is ashamed for not having prevented Joffrey's death. Balon promises to defend his king even if it were to mean fighting his family.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) When Jaime is met by Cersei in [White Sword Tower](wiki:White_Sword_Tower), Balon is away guarding the new king, [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Ser Balon stands vigil with Jaime over the corpse of his father, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) He is later assigned by Jaime as Tommen's primary Kingsguard, and he dutifully guards the boy king every morning.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Balon is abruptly re-assigned by Queen Regent Cersei Lannister to bring [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), the [skull](wiki:Mountain's_skull) of Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane), though Cersei also commands him to carry out another secret assignment.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

At the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood), Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) is killed by [Areo Hotah](wiki:Areo_Hotah) and Princess Myrcella is wounded by Ser [Gerold Dayne](wiki:Gerold_Dayne).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Doran orders the noble houses of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) to delay Balon's arrival. The [Wyls](wiki:House_Wyl) keep him hunting and [hawking](wiki:Hawk) for eight days on one end of the [Boneway](wiki:Boneway), and he is feasted for a fortnight by the [Yronwoods](wiki:House_Yronwood) at [Yronwood](wiki:Yronwood) on the other. Lady [Jordayne](wiki:House_Jordayne) arranges games in his honor at the [Tor](wiki:Tor), with Lady [Nymella Toland](wiki:Nymella_Toland) intent on outdoing her at [Ghost Hill](wiki:Ghost_Hill).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Ser Balon arrives at [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) with three knights, eight squires, twenty [men-at-arms](wiki:Men-at-arms), sundry grooms, and servants.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) When Balon presents the Mountain's skull to Doran Martell at a feast held in his honor, also present are Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell), Areo Hotah, [Ellaria Sand](wiki:Ellaria_Sand), a dozen [lords](wiki:Lord), and three [Sand Snakes](wiki:Sand_Snakes), [Obara](wiki:Obara_Sand), [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria_Sand), and [Tyene](wiki:Tyene_Sand). The confident Areo is prepared to fight the knight if need be. Balon tells Tyene of Gregor's death by [poison](wiki:Poison), which he considers shameful. Balon eats little of the feast and resists the flirtations of Arianne.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)

Balon was previously ordered by Queen Cersei to bring Myrcella back to King's Landing under the guise of visiting her brother, King Tommen. As part of the plan, Doran or a retainer will accompany them to fill the vacant Dornish seat on the [small council](wiki:Small_council) left by Prince Oberyn Martell, who had been killed by Gregor. Prince [Trystane Martell](wiki:Trystane_Martell) is invited as well to see the city. Doran states that traveling by [ship](wiki:Ship) might be easier, but an unnerved Balon replies that a voyage by sea would be too dangerous.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)

In a private chamber, Doran reveals to his family that friends in King's Landing informed him that Queen Cersei plans to have the party ambushed during the journey to the capital. The attack will be led by brigands shouting "halfman", and Trystane is to be killed in the fighting. Balon is to falsely claim that he saw Tyrion Lannister during the ambush, placing the blame for Trystane's death on Tyrion. Balon's aversion to Doran's suggestion of a trip by sea proves Doran's suspicions. Doran states that though Balon does not like his orders, he is compelled to obey. Obara is willing to kill the Kingsguard, but Doran refuses because of [guest right](wiki:Guest_right).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)

To stop Cersei's scheme from coming to fruition, Doran has Myrcella meet Balon at the [Water Gardens](wiki:Water_Gardens) and lie about the Greenblood, placing responsibility for her wounds and the death of Ser Arys Oakheart solely on Ser Gerold Dayne. Obara will then lead Balon to Gerold's seat in western Dorne, [High Hermitage](wiki:High_Hermitage).(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9) Balon sends a letter informing King's Landing of Myrcella's claims,(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) and he hunts for the rogue knight Darkstar.(wiki:Balon_Swann.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)
