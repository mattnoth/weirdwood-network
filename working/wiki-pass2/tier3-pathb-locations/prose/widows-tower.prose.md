## Appearances & Description

A stone bridge connects the Widow's Tower to [Kingspyre Tower](wiki:Kingspyre_Tower).(wiki:Widow's_Tower.cite_ref-Racok38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) A winding stair leads to a dungeon below the Widow's Tower, a long stone vault without windows.(wiki:Widow's_Tower.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Origins

The tower's original name was lost with the death of King [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare) in the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal) during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest). It has since been renamed the Widow's Tower.(wiki:Widow's_Tower.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
The impoverished Lord [Maegor Towers](wiki:Maegor_Towers) lived in the [Tower of Dread](wiki:Tower_of_Dread), leaving the other towers empty. King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) settled his widowed sister, Queen [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), in Harrenhal in [56 AC](wiki:56_AC). Rhaena, who lived in the Widow's Tower, became friends with poor Maegor, and she took care of his household after their lord's passing in [61 AC](wiki:61_AC).(wiki:Widow's_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

While Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) holds the castle, the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions), a sellsword company from the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), are housed within the Widow's Tower.(wiki:Widow's_Tower.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) When the Brave Companions return with captured [northmen](wiki:Northmen) and [rivermen](wiki:Rivermen), Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch) has the prisoners taken to the dungeon beneath the tower. During the [fall of Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal), the captives are freed by [Jaqen H'ghar](wiki:Jaqen_H'ghar), [Rorge](wiki:Rorge), and [Biter](wiki:Biter).(wiki:Widow's_Tower.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
