## Origins

The origins of Yeen are one of the greatest enigmas puzzling [maesters](wiki:Maesters) and other scholars. Like the [Toad Stone](wiki:Toad_Stone) of the [Isle of Toads](wiki:Isle_of_Toads), Yeen is built entirely of oily [black stone](wiki:Black_stone), in blocks so large it would require a dozen [elephants](wiki:Elephants) to move them.(wiki:Yeen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Yeen has remained in desolation for many thousands of years, yet the jungle surrounding it has scarce touched it. Every attempt to rebuild or resettle it has ended in horror.(wiki:Yeen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_sothoryos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
During the year spent in [Sothoryos](wiki:Sothoryos) by some of [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria)'s [ten thousand ships](wiki:Ten_thousand_ships), [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar) settled in Yeen, where they had to contend with constant attacks from "[brindled ghouls](wiki:Sothoryi)" from the jungle. At some point a boat was sent from [Zamettar](wiki:Zamettar) to Yeen, only to find that all people had abruptly vanished from the city. According to tales, Nymeria called it "a city so evil that even the jungle will not enter."(wiki:Yeen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_sothoryos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
