## Origins

### Ancient History[ ]

The so-called [Hairy Men](wiki:Hairy_Men) were possibly the forebears of the [Ibbenese](wiki:Ibbenese).(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) The [Port of Ibben](wiki:Port_of_Ibben) is said to have ruled the Ibbenese since the [dawn of days](wiki:Dawn_of_days).(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8)
[Dragons](wiki:Dragon) likely once lived on Ibben, as [dragonbone](wiki:Dragonbone) has been found on the island [Ib](wiki:Ib).(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
[Andalos](wiki:Andalos), the lands on which the [Andals](wiki:Andals) eventually settled, was originally inhabited by a tribe called the hairy men. The [Pentoshi](wiki:Pentos) believe these hairy men were kin to the hairy men from Ib.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Others believe Andalos was inhabited by hairy men from Ib, or that the hairy men from Andalos eventually settled Ib.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)
[Lorath](wiki:Lorath), before it became a [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) settlement, was for a while inhabited by a dark, small, hairy people akin to the men of Ib.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) After the followers of [Boash](wiki:Boash) had settled in Lorath and the city became a daughter of Valyria, men from numerous lands, including Ib, traveled there.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Likewise, before the Valyrians settled in [Norvos](wiki:Norvos), other people lived in the city. While most consider them to have been [Andals](wiki:Andals), there are some who believe them to have been cousins of the men of Ib.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
Evidence of Ibbenese settlements has been found on the [Axe](wiki:Axe), the [Lorathi](wiki:Lorath) isles, along the shores of the [Bitterweed Bay](wiki:Bitterweed_Bay), the [Bay of Tusks](wiki:Bay_of_Tusks), the [Leviathan Sound](wiki:Leviathan_Sound), and the [Thousand Islands](wiki:Thousand_Islands).(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-9) Ib has often fought with Lorath over the supremacy of the waters of Bitterweed Bay.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_the_shivering_sea.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Although Norvos claims dominion over the Axe, the Ibbenese often violently dispute this claim.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)
Before the Andals came to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife) in the [north](wiki:North) was frequently attacked by raiders and slavers. Some scholars believe these raiders were the forebears of the men of Ib.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

### God-Kings[ ]

At least a hundred [God-Kings](wiki:God-King) are said to have ruled from a massive stone castle in the [Port of Ibben](wiki:Port_of_Ibben).(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-10)
The Ibbenese sent their most notorious criminals to [Ib Sar](wiki:Ib_Sar), the only town on [Far Ib](wiki:Far_Ib). These criminals were often mutilated first, so they might never return to Ib. This practice came to an end after the fall of the final God-King, although Ib Sar has not been able to lose its unsavory reputation.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-11)
The [Kingdom of Sarnor](wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor) was a trading partner of Ibben,(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) although the [Ibbenese](wiki:Ibbenese) attempted on several occasions to seize control of the mouth of the [Sarne](wiki:Sarne), resulting in war with the Sarnori settlements [Saath](wiki:Saath) and [Sarys](wiki:Sarys).(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-12)
The Ibbenese eventually succeeded, under leadership of the God-Kings, in gaining a foothold on the mainland of Essos. They conquered the [Kingdom of the Ifequevron](wiki:Kingdom_of_the_Ifequevron), although whether the Ibbenese extinguished the [Ifequevron](wiki:Ifequevron) or not is a matter of dispute. While some claim the Ibbenese killed the race, others simply believe the so-called "wood walkers" went into hiding or fled. At the height of its power, the land the Ibbenese controlled on the mainland of Essos was as large as Ib itself. The rich dominion of [Ibbish](wiki:Ibbish) allowed it to grow into a thriving port and the second city of Ibben.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-13)
Some of the slaves who escaped the grasps of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) and founded [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) were Ibbenese.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) While still keeping its location and existence a secret from the world, Braavos began to trade with Ib.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

### Doom of Valyria[ ]

After the destruction of most of the [Sarnori](wiki:Sarnori) cities by the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) during the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood), the aftermath of the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), [Saath](wiki:Saath) remained as the only Sarnori settlement. This diminished city mostly survives due to the support given by [Ib](wiki:Ib) and [Lorath](wiki:Lorath).(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)
Near two hundred years ago, Dothraki [khals](wiki:Khal) began to attack the [Ibbenese](wiki:Ibbenese) lands on mainland [Essos](wiki:Essos), leading to numerous battles. Ibben won a famous battle against Khal [Onqo](wiki:Onqo), but were pushed further and further back by the Dothraki as [khalasar](wiki:Khalasar) after khalasar came. Khal [Scoro](wiki:Scoro) took [Ibbish](wiki:Ibbish) for the first time, and although the Ibbenese rebuilt the city, a generation later Khal [Rogo](wiki:Rogo) arrived to put half the city to the torch.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-14)
Eventually, Onqo's grandson, Khal [Dhako](wiki:Dhako), arrived at the city, ready to sack it. Learning of his approach, the inhabitants of Ibbish fled across the sea back to [Ib](wiki:Ib). Considering them cowardly, an infuriated Dhako put the city and much of the surrounding land to the torch, resulting in the loss of most of the lands the Ibbenese had ruled on the northern coast of Essos.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-15)(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) Following the destruction of Ibbish, the Ibbenese constructed a new town, [New Ibbish](wiki:New_Ibbish).(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_new_ibbish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) From this colony, built on a peninsula protected by great wooden walls, the Ibbenese rule over their modest remaining foothold in Essos.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-16)
The last of the [God-Kings](wiki:God-King) was thrown down in the aftermath of the Doom.(wiki:Ibben.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_beyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-17)
