## Origins

Maester [Herryk](wiki:Herryk)'s [History of the Kings-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:History_of_the_Kings-Beyond-the-Wall) has a transcription of a [wildling](wiki:Free_folk) song about [Gorne](wiki:Gorne) and [Gendel](wiki:Gendel) in which the brothers mediated a dispute between a clan of [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) and a family of [giants](wiki:Giants) over control of a cave. After discovering the cave was part of a larger system of caverns which extended beneath the [Wall](wiki:Wall), Gendel and Gorne resorted to trickery and convinced both parties to abandon the cave. [Maesters](wiki:Maesters) are skeptical of the wildling tale, however.(wiki:Gorne's_Way.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)
According to stories, Gorne and Gendel were joint [Kings-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:Kings-Beyond-the-Wall) three thousand years ago. The brother kings led a wildling army along the path beneath the Wall to successfully evade the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). However, they were attacked and Gorne was killed by forces from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) when they reached the far side. Wildling legend claims that Gendel attempted to return north of the Wall via the same path, but he did not know it as well as Gorne and became lost in the caves. The descendants of Gendel's folk are said to still be sobbing beneath the hills in their attempts to find a way out.(wiki:Gorne's_Way.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
