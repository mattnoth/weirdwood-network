## Appearances & Description

Forty-four stone ribs are located on the top of [Nagga's hill](wiki:Nagga's_hill). There are nine steep and wide stone steps which lead to where the hall's doors once stood.(wiki:Grey_King's_Hall.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

## Origins

[Nagga](wiki:Nagga)'s ribs became the beams and pillars and her jaws the throne. According to legend, the Hall had been warmed by Nagga's living fire and on the walls hung [tapestries](wiki:Tapestries) made of silver seaweed. The men sworn to the [Grey King](wiki:Grey_King) ate at a table shaped like a large starfish while seated on thrones made from [mother-of-pearl](wiki:Mother-of-pearl). Upon the Grey King's death, the [Storm God](wiki:Storm_God) snuffed out Nagga's fire and the sea stole the fanged throne.(wiki:Grey_King's_Hall.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
[Maesters](wiki:Maesters) do believe the pillars of the hall are the petrified remains of a sea creature, but there is skepticism if they are large enough to have actually belonged to a [sea dragon](wiki:Sea_dragon).(wiki:Grey_King's_Hall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Origins

The Grey King's Hall is where [kingsmoots](wiki:Kingsmoot) were held to determine which [rock](wiki:Rock_king) or [salt king](wiki:Salt_king) would become [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Grey_King's_Hall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
After [the death](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal) of [Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare), [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), [priests of the Drowned God](wiki:Priests_of_the_Drowned_God) crowned the priest-king [Lodos](wiki:Lodos) beneath [Nagga](wiki:Nagga)'s bones.(wiki:Grey_King's_Hall.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Grey_King's_Hall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_island:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
During [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), [Tarle the Thrice-Drowned](wiki:Tarle_the_Thrice-Drowned) crowned [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) as [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands) beneath Nagga's bones.(wiki:Grey_King's_Hall.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

After the passing of [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North), his brother [Aeron Damphair](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) calls for a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) to be held at the ruins of the Grey King's Hall.(wiki:Grey_King's_Hall.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Grey_King's_Hall.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Their brother, [Euron Crow's Eye](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), is chosen king.(wiki:Grey_King's_Hall.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
