## Appearances & Description

A large square castle, Hammerhorn has spiky [iron](wiki:Iron) battlements and iron gates. The stones used for its construction were quarried from the cliff behind it. Below its main entrance lie the openings to the mines and caves within which toil the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) in service to [House Goodbrother](wiki:House_Goodbrother).(wiki:Hammerhorn.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
The shadowy hall of Hammerhorn is dank, drafty, and smoky.(wiki:Hammerhorn.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

After learning of the death of his eldest brother [Balon](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North), [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) travels with [Gormond Goodbrother](wiki:Gormond_Goodbrother) to Hammerhorn to meet with the youth's father, Lord [Gorold](wiki:Gorold_Goodbrother). Aeron wants to speak with Gorold alone in the hall, but the Lord of Hammerhorn insists that Maester [Murenmure](wiki:Murenmure) remain. Aeron is shocked to learn that his hated elder brother, [Euron Crow's Eye](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), has claimed the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair). Gorold offers to host Aeron overnight at Hammerhorn, but the Damphair departs Hammerhorn for [Pebbleton](wiki:Pebbleton) in the company of [Greydon Goodbrother](wiki:Greydon_Goodbrother).(wiki:Hammerhorn.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)
