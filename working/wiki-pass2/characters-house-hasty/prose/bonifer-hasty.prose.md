## Origins

In his youth, Ser Bonifer Hasty was a promising [tourney](wiki:Tourney) [knight](wiki:Knight).(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) He and Princess [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) were infatuated with one another prior to the princess's official [betrothal](wiki:Betrothal) to her brother, Prince [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Rawoiafbonifer_hasty.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) According to Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), Bonifer once wore the princess's favor in a tourney in which he defeated all challengers to name Rhaella his [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty).(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Their love was ultimately a brief thing and could never have been otherwise, as Bonifer was of far too low birth to be considered as a suitor for a princess of the royal [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). When Rhaella married Aerys, the knight found solace in religion, finding only [the Maiden](wiki:Maiden_(the_Seven)) could replace Rhaella in his heart.(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Not many today know of Bonifer's brief relationship with the late Queen Rhaella in their youths; Jaime Lannister only knows that something had happened to Bonifer, a defeat or a disgrace or a near brush with death, and that he stopped jousting afterwards, deciding it was an empty vanity.(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) At one point Bonifer served under Lord [Owen Merryweather](wiki:Owen_Merryweather), a former [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen). Bonifer founded the [Holy Hundred](wiki:Holy_Hundred), a group of one hundred pious men-at-arms.(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

## Appearances & Description

Bonifer is tall, thin, and known for his piety. Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) thinks Bonifer is a withered, solemn stork of a man, with a stern, sad face. Bonifer is prone to salting his speech with appeals to [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Bonifer joins King [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) at the beginning of the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_bonifer_hasty.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Upon Renly's [death at Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), he joins King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon). Bonifer takes part in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), where he loses fourteen of his [Holy Hundred](wiki:Holy_Hundred).(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) He then submits to King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Bonifer and the [Holy Hundred](wiki:Holy_Hundred) accompany Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) to take [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun). On the way, they stop at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), where Jaime announces that Bonifer will hold Harrenhal in the crown's name during the absence of [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

Jaime considers it probable that [Orton Merryweather](wiki:Orton_Merryweather) had convinced Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) to choose Bonifer as castellan of Harrenhal, as Bonifer had once served Orton's grandsire, Lord [Owen Merryweather](wiki:Owen_Merryweather). Jaime also notes Bonifer hails from the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) and has neither any enemies to feud with nor any allies to reward along the [Trident](wiki:Trident), and he and his Holy Hundred have an excellent reputation for justice, sobriety, and discipline.(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

Jaime takes his supper in Harrenhal's Hunter's Hall with Bonifer. He thinks of Bonifer in jest as "Baelor Butthole", in a mocking reference to the pious King [Baelor the Blessed](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen). Bonifer tells Jaime he will not suffer [Pia](wiki:Pia) or [Gregor Clegane's men](wiki:Mountain's_men) remaining at Harrenhal. Jaime obliges and takes Pia and the Mountain's men with him when he leaves.(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7)

### A Dance with Dragons

During her wedding procession in [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), on her way to marry [Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq), Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) takes [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen)'s mind off things by telling her a bit about her mother, Rhaella. Barristan says Rhaella was once briefly smitten with a young [landed knight](wiki:Landed_knight) from the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands). He tells her the knight put away his lance the day her mother wed her father, [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), and become very pious. The knight's passion for Rhaella had no hope of fulfillment. Barristan chooses his words carefully and does not mention the name of the knight to Daenerys, and she does not ask for the identity of the knight who had fallen so deeply in love with her mother.(wiki:Bonifer_Hasty.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)
