## Origins

Galon called for the unification of the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) as a single kingdom, decreeing that [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) must not make war on their own kind or carry off each other's women or raid each other's shores. He summoned a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) at [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk) to choose a [high king](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands) to rule over the [salt](wiki:Salt_king) and [rock kings](wiki:Rock_king) alike. The kingsmoot chose [Urras Greyiron](wiki:Urras_Greyiron), who was crowned with a driftwood crown by Galon himself.(wiki:Galon_Whitestaff.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

After Urras Ironfoot's death, his son [Erich](wiki:Erich_I_Greyiron) seized the crown and proclaimed himself king rather than be chosen in a kingsmoot. Though half-blind and feeble, Galon arose in fury and summoned all "captains and kings" to unmake Erich the Ugly and condemn him to death. Instead, Erich saved his life by breaking down his father's crown and casting it into the sea. The kingsmoot then chose [Regnar Drumm](wiki:Regnar_Drumm).(wiki:Galon_Whitestaff.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Appearances & Description

Galon was called "Whitestaff" for the tall carved staff he carried to smite the ungodly. His staff was made of [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) or from [Nagga](wiki:Nagga)'s bones, depending on the tale.(wiki:Galon_Whitestaff.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
