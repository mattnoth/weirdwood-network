## Origins

After the birth of Samwell, she gave her lord husband, [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly), three girls in as many years (including [Talla](wiki:Talla_Tarly)), then a second son, Dickon.(wiki:Melessa_Florent.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Randyll once took Samwell with him on a voyage to the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor). After they came back, Melessa revealed to Samwell that his father wanted him to remain on the island and become Lord [Paxter Redwyne](wiki:Paxter_Redwyne)'s page and squire.(wiki:Melessa_Florent.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Randyll used to complain to Melessa about Samwell's cowardice.(wiki:Melessa_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Appearances & Description

Melessa is close with her firstborn son, [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly), affectionately calling him "Sam".(wiki:Melessa_Florent.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Together they used to lull Sam's little brother, [Dickon](wiki:Dickon_Tarly), to sleep.(wiki:Melessa_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

When [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) discusses his plan with [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) to send [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) and [her babe](wiki:Monster) to [Horn Hill](wiki:Horn_Hill) - in the guise of the babe being Sam's bastard offspring - he tells Jon that he knows that his mother would want him. Sam is confident that she will find some place for Gilly and it would not be as hard as serving [Craster](wiki:Craster).(wiki:Melessa_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

When he meets with Samwell Tarly in the [Lance](wiki:Lance_(tower)), Ser [Denys Mallister](wiki:Denys_Mallister) recalls that Sam's mother is a Florent.(wiki:Melessa_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### A Feast for Crows

While on the *[Blackbird](wiki:Blackbird)*, Sam thinks of his mother as he clings to the railing and watches waves breaking on a stony shore:

> If she saw me in my blacks, it might even make her proud. "I am a man now, Mother,” I could tell her, "a steward and a man of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). My brothers call me Sam the Slayer sometimes."(wiki:Melessa_Florent.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
