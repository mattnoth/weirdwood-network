## Identity

The sack of Duskendale was a battle during the Dance of the Dragons in 129 AC, in which green forces led by Ser Criston Cole sacked the port town of Duskendale in the crownlands and beheaded Lord Gunthor Darklyn for treason (wiki:Sack_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold).

## Allegiances

Part of the `FIGHTS_IN` Dance of the Dragons. Fought between the blacks (House Darklyn) and the greens (House Targaryen under Aegon II) (wiki:Sack_of_Duskendale).

## Narrative Arc

After King Aegon II Targaryen replaced Ser Otto Hightower as Hand of the King with Ser Criston Cole, the new Hand advised striking against mainland lords who had declared for Rhaenyra. The green host — 100 knights, 500 men-at-arms, and 1,800 sellswords — first secured the bloodless submission of Rosby and Stokeworth, adding their forces. Duskendale, a walled port town, was caught completely unprepared and fell quickly. The town was sacked, ships in the harbor were set afire, and Lord Gunthor Darklyn was beheaded. Most of his household knights submitted, though a few chose death (wiki:Sack_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold). Criston then moved the loyalist forces onward to Rook's Rest, leading to the Battle at Rook's Rest (wiki:Sack_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold).
