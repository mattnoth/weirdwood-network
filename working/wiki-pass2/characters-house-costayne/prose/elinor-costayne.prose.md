## Origins

Elinor enjoyed a quiet life(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rtrotd62.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) with her three children by her first husband, Ser [Theo Bolling](wiki:Theo_Bolling). However, in [47 AC](wiki:47_AC), Theo was arrested by [knights](wiki:Knight) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), accused of conspiring with Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) to place her son, Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), and was executed. After seven days of mourning, Elinor was summoned to wed King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), who sought to marry three women of proven fertility. Her sons' presence at the wedding ensured she would play her part in the ceremony. According to one tale, Elinor scratched Maegor's back to bloody ribbons as they coupled.(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Elinor's sons were sent away from her after her wedding to Maegor; her eldest was [fostered](wiki:Fostered) at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), her second son at [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), and her youngest was given to a [wet nurse](wiki:Wet_nurse).(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

In [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), When Queen [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower) admitted to [poisoning](wiki:Poison) Queen [Alys Harroway](wiki:Alys_Harroway) during her pregnancy, Tyanna promised the same would happen to the pregnant Elinor. Two months later, Tyanna was proven correct when Elinor gave birth to a stillborn abomination said to have been born eyeless and with small wings.(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

That year, Prince Jaehaerys Targaryen [pressed his claim to the Iron Throne](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen's_uprising). Maegor's response was sluggish and confused, but though he was heavily outnumbered, he refused to surrender. At the end of his war council, Maegor remained behind alone in the [throne room](wiki:Throne_room) to brood. He was found the next morning by Queen Elinor, dead upon the Iron Throne with his wrists cut and one of the swords pointing through his neck. His death remains a mystery; some say he was killed by Queen Elinor, others that he had been killed by a knight of his own Kingsguard, yet others say he was killed by a builder who escaped the [massacre](wiki:Red_Keep) the king had ordered three years prior and desired revenge, and many believe that Maegor had been killed by the throne itself for his unworthiness. Others believe that Maegor killed himself by opening his wrists on the blades of the Iron Throne.(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Elinor was one of two wives who survived the king, the other being Queen [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)).(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

After King Maegor's death, Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)) proposed that King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) marry Queen Elinor to reconcile with Maegor's supporters when a bride was being considered for the king, but nothing came of the proposal.(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) After Jaehaerys's ascent, Elinor departed [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) dressed in the robes of a penitent. She visited her two elder sons at the Eyrie and Highgarden before retiring to her father's seat at the [Three Towers](wiki:Three_Towers) with her youngest son.(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

In [51 AC](wiki:51_AC), King Jaehaerys commanded Elinor to be one of his [Seven Speakers](wiki:Seven_Speakers), three men and four women he sent out to spread his [Doctrine of Exceptionalism](wiki:Doctrine_of_Exceptionalism) to the peoples of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). Elinor dressed in queenly raiment, which became shabbier and more threadbare each day she traveled through the [Reach](wiki:Reach), but she eloquently testified to the evils of her late husband Maegor and the goodness of Jaehaerys and Alysanne.(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Years later, Elinor gave up all claims to nobility, becoming [Mother](wiki:Mother) Elinor at the great [motherhouse](wiki:Motherhouse) in [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport).(wiki:Elinor_Costayne.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)
