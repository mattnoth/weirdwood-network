## Origins

When Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey) held [Standfast](wiki:Standfast), parts of the castle's roof had ancient stone grotesques which were so weather-worn their original designs had become indiscernible.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
During the fierce storm which raged amid the birth of [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), gargoyles were ripped from the walls of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) to crash upon the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) below.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Maester [Cressen](wiki:Cressen) has become fond of the monstrous decorations at Dragonstone.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)
[Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) enjoys climbing gargoyles to reach the roof of the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep) at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). The boy also swings from the gargoyles so he can reach the [broken tower](wiki:Broken_tower).(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

While moving from the gargoyles of the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep) toward the [broken tower](wiki:Broken_tower) within [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) discovers Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) and her brother, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), within the First Keep. The knight pushes Bran out the window,(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) crippling the boy.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Ragot9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) After recovering from his coma,(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Ragot17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Bran has a dream involving climbing a tower and seeing red-eyed gargoyles which may once have been lions.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark) later uses the [far-eye](wiki:Far-eye) of Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) to look at the gargoyles atop the First Keep from the maester's turret.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) pats the stone head of the gargoyle at [Dragonstone's](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) port for good luck.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) claims a [Lysene](wiki:Lys) trade envoy once compared the disfigured Princess [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon) to the gargoyles on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone)'s walls.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
Following the [sack of Winterfell](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell), Bran sees shattered gargoyles across the yard.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) insults her younger brother [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), a [dwarf](wiki:Dwarf), by referring to him as a gargoyle.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) also refers to Tyrion as a twisted little gargoyle.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Rasos47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
While at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) makes a snow castle of Winterfell. Petyr suggests she depict snow-covered gargoyles as white lumps on her version of the First Keep.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

When [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) weds "[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark)" (actually [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)) in the [godswood of Winterfell](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell), [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) thinks that [Hother Umber](wiki:Hother_Umber) resembles a gargoyle in the mists.(wiki:Gargoyle.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
