## Origins

### Origin[ ]

According to legend, the founder of [House Dayne](wiki:House_Dayne) tracked a falling star to an island at the mouth of the [Torentine](wiki:Torentine). He discovered there a stone with magical powers, and the castle of [Starfall](wiki:Starfall) was constructed on that spot.(wiki:Dawn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_kingdoms_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) The greatsword Dawn was forged from the heart of this fallen star,(wiki:Dawn.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) and has possibly been wielded by Daynes for ten thousand years.(wiki:Dawn.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

### Arthur Dayne[ ]

The last known wielder of Dawn was Ser [Arthur Dayne](wiki:Arthur_Dayne), a chivalrous knight in the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) of [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Dawn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) The [Smiling Knight](wiki:Smiling_Knight) of the [Kingswood Brotherhood](wiki:Kingswood_Brotherhood) tried to claim Dawn from Arthur through combat, but the [robber knight](wiki:Robber_knight) was killed by the Sword of the Morning.(wiki:Dawn.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Arthur granted knighthood to [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) after the battle. When Arthur tapped Dawn on the youth's shoulder, the blade went through Jaime's tunic and caused him to bleed.(wiki:Dawn.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
After Arthur died in [combat at the tower of joy](wiki:Combat_at_the_tower_of_joy) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) rode to Starfall to deliver Dawn to Arthur's sister, [Ashara Dayne](wiki:Ashara_Dayne).(wiki:Dawn.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Dawn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_the_andals_arrive.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
