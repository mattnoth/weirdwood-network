## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

When young King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_I_Baratheon)'s mother, Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), asks him a question that he refuses to answer, Cersei threatens to send for Pate and have him beaten until he bleeds. Tommen sullenly relents, and answers his mother.(wiki:Pate_(whipping_boy).cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Later, when Tommen argues with his mother, Cersei again threatens to send for Pate.(wiki:Pate_(whipping_boy).cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Some time later, Tommen again argues with his mother, saying that his wife, Queen [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), had told him he should be more active in the rule of the kingdom, and Cersei idly comments that Margaery says too much and she would like to have her [tongue torn out](wiki:Tongue_mutilation). This infuriates Tommen, who forbids Cersei from hurting Margaery. Cersei then orders Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) to take Tommen to his bedchamber, summon Pate, and force Tommen to [whip](wiki:Whipping) Pate himself until the boy bleeds from both cheeks. Cersei further orders, that if Tommen refuses or says one word of protest, [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) will be summoned and remove Pate's tongue in front of Tommen. Tommen is very subdued afterwards, and Cersei is satisfied that punishing Pate served its purpose.(wiki:Pate_(whipping_boy).cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
