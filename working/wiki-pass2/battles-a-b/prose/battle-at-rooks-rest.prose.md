## Origins

Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole), the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) and the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), devised a more aggressive strategy for the [greens](wiki:Greens) than his predecessor as Hand, Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower). Criston planned a trap for the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) at [Rook's Rest](wiki:Rook's_Rest), while also forcing the submission of the nearby [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) houses that were allied with Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).(wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

Criston Cole marched out of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with 100 [knights](wiki:Knight), 500 [men-at-arms](wiki:Men-at-arms), and three times as many [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword): thus about 2,400 in total. [Lord Rosby](wiki:Lord_Rosby) and [Lord Stokeworth](wiki:Lord_Stokeworth) had recently submitted to Aegon to avoid execution in King's Landing, and Criston forced them to prove their loyalty by adding the strength of [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) and [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth) to the green host. Thus augmented, the army advanced upon [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale). The walled harbor town was caught completely by surprise and unprepared, and quickly fell to Aegon II's forces and [was sacked](wiki:Sack_of_Duskendale). Lord [Gunthor Darklyn](wiki:Gunthor_Darklyn) was beheaded for [treason](wiki:Treason), and most of his [household knights](wiki:Household_knight) swore their swords to Aegon, although a few chose death.(wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

[Lord Staunton](wiki:Lord_Staunton) had been forewarned of the approach of Ser Criston's army. He closed the gates of Rook's Rest, but was unable to prevent Criston from burning his fields or killing his [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) and livestock. When supplies in Rook's Rest began to run low, Lord Staunton asked for assistance from Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), sending a [raven](wiki:Raven) to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

## Narrative Arc

Queen Rhaenyra's sons, [Jacaerys](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) and [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon), were eager to join the fight atop their [dragons](wiki:Dragon), [Vermax](wiki:Vermax) and [Tyraxes](wiki:Tyraxes), but she forbade them to go. Instead, nine days after Staunton's message, [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)), the Queen Who Never Was, and her [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Meleys](wiki:Meleys), the Red Queen, arrived alone above Rook's Rest to aid Lord Staunton. However, Criston was prepared, and had his archers and scorpions fire at Meleys. The dragon was largely unharmed by these attacks, and she responded by burning Criston's soldiers with [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire).(wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Criston Cole then sprung his trap. King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and his brother, Prince [Aemond](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), appeared in the sky atop their own dragons, [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre) and [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), and attacked Rhaenys and Meleys. The three dragons fought a thousand feet above the field. When Meleys closed her jaws around Sunfyre's neck, Vhagar fell upon them from above, causing the three dragons to crash into to the ground. Although Aemond and Vhagar rose unscathed, Meleys was broken and ripped to pieces from the fall, and Sunfyre had one wing half torn from his body. Aegon suffered severe burns and broken bones. The blackened body of Rhaenys was found beside the carcass of her dragon.(wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Aemond, Criston, and the surviving greens took Rook's Rest, and killed Lord Staunton and his garrison of one hundred soldiers.(wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

## Aftermath

The heads of [Lord Staunton](wiki:Lord_Staunton) and Meleys were taken to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). The [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) of the city were greatly awed by the sight of the dragon's head, drawn through the city on a cart. Thousands of people left the city, until Dowager Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) ordered the gates closed and barred.

Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) lost eight hundred soldiers to Meleys's dragonfire. King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) was incapacitated by his burns for a year; Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) became [regent](wiki:Regent) while his brother recovered. Aegon's injured dragon, [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre), was flightless and too heavy to be moved, so was left behind in the fields of Rook's Rest to eat carcasses and livestock.

The loss of [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)) and her dragon, [Meleys](wiki:Meleys), was a severe blow to the [blacks](wiki:Blacks). Rhaenys's husband, Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), was enraged, and blamed her death on Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), stating that if the queen or her sons had gone with their dragons, his wife might be alive.

Lord [Walys Mooton](wiki:Walys_Mooton) later led the [retaking of Rook's Rest](wiki:Retaking_of_Rook's_Rest) for the blacks, but was slain in a failed attempt to kill Sunfyre.(wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
