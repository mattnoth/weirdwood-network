## Identity

The Battle at Pyke was the final battle of Greyjoy's Rebellion in 289 AC, fought between royal forces and House Greyjoy at Pyke castle on the island of Pyke. The title is conjectural. (wiki:Battle_at_Pyke)

## Allegiances

Part of `FIGHTS_IN` Greyjoy's Rebellion. (track_b: Conflict)

## Narrative Arc

After victories at Fair Isle and Lordsport, King Robert I Baratheon's forces assaulted the castle of Pyke. Thoros of Myr, wielding a flaming sword, was the first through the breach in the walls. Jorah Mormont followed close behind, earning his later knighthood. During the assault, the south tower collapsed, killing Maron Greyjoy, Balon's heir. After fierce fighting, Balon Greyjoy surrendered and bent the knee to Robert. His surviving son Theon Greyjoy, then a boy of ten, was taken as a hostage and ward by Lord Eddard Stark to ensure Balon's future loyalty. The battle ended the rebellion and confirmed Robert's authority over the Iron Islands.
