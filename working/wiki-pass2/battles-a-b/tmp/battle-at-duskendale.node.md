## Identity

The Battle at Duskendale took place in 299 AC during the War of the Five Kings. It was a crushing defeat for the northern army, engineered in part by Roose Bolton's treachery. (wiki:Battle_at_Duskendale)

## Allegiances

Part of `FIGHTS_IN` War of the Five Kings. (track_b: Conflict)

## Narrative Arc

After the Battle of the Blackwater, Roose Bolton commanded the northern foot at Harrenhal. He ordered Robett Glover and Helman Tallhart to march on Duskendale, ostensibly to threaten King's Landing. This was part of Bolton's secret betrayal of Robb Stark — he sent forces he wanted destroyed on a doomed advance. Lord Randyll Tarly's host caught the northerners at Duskendale, while Ser Gregor Clegane's forces cut off their retreat. Helman Tallhart was killed in the fighting. Robett Glover and Harrion Karstark were taken prisoner. The northern foot suffered devastating losses while Bolton preserved his own loyal forces at Harrenhal. The battle further weakened Robb Stark's war effort and served Bolton's long-term plan to seize the North.
