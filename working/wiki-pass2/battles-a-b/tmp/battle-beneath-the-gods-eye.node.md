## Identity

The Battle Beneath the Gods Eye was fought in 43 AC near the Gods Eye in the riverlands between King Maegor I Targaryen and his nephew Prince Aegon Targaryen, son of King Aenys I. (wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye)

## Allegiances

Part of `FIGHTS_IN` Aegon the Uncrowned's rebellion. (track_b: Conflict)

## Narrative Arc

Prince Aegon Targaryen, called Aegon the Uncrowned, raised a host of some 15,000 men to challenge his uncle Maegor I for the Iron Throne. His supporters included lords from the riverlands, reach, and westerlands. The two armies met beneath the Gods Eye, but the battle was decided in the air: Maegor rode Balerion the Black Dread, the largest dragon alive, while Aegon rode the much smaller Quicksilver. Balerion ripped a wing from Quicksilver, and both dragon and rider fell to their deaths. With their prince slain, much of Aegon's army broke. Approximately 2,000 rebels were killed versus only around 100 loyalists. Many rebel lords were executed in the aftermath, and Maegor's position was secured.
