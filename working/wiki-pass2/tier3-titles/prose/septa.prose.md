## Culture

Women who wish to become septas train first as novices.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) There are some great centers of religious training, such as the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Various_3-0) Upon taking her vows and being consecrated in the Faith,(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) a septa sets aside her last name, even if she comes from a noble family.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Septas take a vow of chastity.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Unlike the [silent sisters](wiki:Silent_sisters),(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) septas do not swear a vow of silence.
Septas often serve at [motherhouses](wiki:Motherhouse)(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and in cloisters.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) They typically dress in white robes,(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) some with a woven seven-color belt.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Septas sometimes wear hoods,(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) but not always,(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) and their hair can be seen.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Like [septons](wiki:Septon), septas often wear a crystal pendant about their necks.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)
Septas teach girls how to read, write, and do sums,(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) and they also educate about piety, deportment, and the mysteries of the Faith.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) Many serve as governesses in noble households, teaching girls needlework and other womanly arts.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) For example, Septa [Roelle](wiki:Roelle) served [House Tarth](wiki:House_Tarth) of [Evenfall Hall](wiki:Evenfall_Hall),(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) and Septa [Mordane](wiki:Mordane) serves [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Septas are supposed to serve as chaperones and guardians when their female charges keep company with men.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)
Septas can receive confession from believers in the Seven.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Septas who copy and illuminate texts for the Faith are known to alter or remove texts they deem unacceptable.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)
A woman who scolds another person might be facetiously called a septa, despite not actually being one.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

## Origins

[Septons](wiki:Septon) and septas were expelled from the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) by King [Hagon Hoare](wiki:Hagon_Hoare),(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) and again by Lord [Goren Greyjoy](wiki:Goren_Greyjoy).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) affirmed that septas accused of crimes could only be tried in the courts run by the Faith.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)
In [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), when his child with [Alys Harroway](wiki:Alys_Harroway) was stillborn, King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) had her midwife and septas executed.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) In [47 AC](wiki:47_AC), Maegor sent Princess [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon)) to be trained as a septa in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2)
Princess [Maegelle Targaryen](wiki:Maegelle_Targaryen) was promised to the Faith in [73 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and eventually became a septa. She nursed victims of [greyscale](wiki:Greyscale), and died of that disease in [96 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)
In [84 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Princess [Saera Targaryen](wiki:Saera_Targaryen), and her [favorites](wiki:Category:Favorites_of_Saera_Targaryen) [Alys Turnberry](wiki:Alys_Turnberry) and [Perianne Moore](wiki:Perianne_Moore), evaded their septas to carry on affairs with men. When the affair became known to Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) and King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), Perianne said Saera was "smarter than the septas". As a punishment, Saera was promised to the Faith as a novice of the [silent sisters](wiki:Silent_sisters) in Oldtown, under the instruction of her sister, Septa Maegelle. However, after a year and a half, Saera fled to [Lys](wiki:Lys), taking service in a [pleasure garden](wiki:Pleasure_garden).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-3)
The author of [A Caution for Young Girls](wiki:A_Caution_for_Young_Girls) (first published in [90 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)) claims to have ended up a septa at the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3)
During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), septas were raped by the [greens](wiki:Greens) during the [sack of Tumbleton](wiki:Sack_of_Tumbleton) in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
Princess [Naerys Targaryen](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen) loved [the Seven](wiki:The_Seven), and might have been a septa if her father, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), had allowed it. But he did not, and instead married Naerys to her brother, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)
In [159 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the [bastard](wiki:Bastard) daughters of [Megette](wiki:Megette) and Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) were given to the Faith to become septas.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)
Some time after Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_III)) was released from the [Maidenvault](wiki:Maidenvault) in [171 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), she became a septa.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)
In [211 AC](wiki:211_AC), Lady [Helicent Uffering](wiki:Helicent_Uffering) lived at [Coldmoat](wiki:Coldmoat) in the [Reach](wiki:Reach). Helicent was a pious but [simple](wiki:Simple) woman, who could not dress herself without the help of her septas.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)
One of the daughters of Ser [Elys Waynwood](wiki:Elys_Waynwood) and [Alys Arryn](wiki:Alys_Arryn) chose to become a septa after her face was scarred by the [pox](wiki:Pox).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)
Around [270 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) accused his wife, Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen), of being unfaithful to him, and forced her to share her bed with two septas every night to ensure she would remain faithful.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)
The mother of [Tyene Sand](wiki:Tyene_Sand) was a septa when she was seduced by Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) in [275 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) or [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-33)
In [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) asked her septa, [Saranella](wiki:Saranella), for the meaning of the word "[valonqar](wiki:Valonqar)", and Septa Saranella told her it was [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian) for "little brother".(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)
Around [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) rescued Lady [Jeyne Swann](wiki:Jeyne_Swann) and her septa from the [Kingswood Brotherhood](wiki:Kingswood_Brotherhood).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)
A cook at the [Shadow Tower](wiki:Shadow_Tower) had raped dozens of septas before he was caught and was sent to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) for his crimes. He burned a seven-pointed star into his flesh for each one.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

When Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) travels to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to serve as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Septa [Mordane](wiki:Mordane) accompanies him so she can continue educating his daughters, [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Ragot22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)
When Robert dies and his son [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) takes the throne, [Eddard is arrested](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark), and Septa Mordane is executed for alleged [treason](wiki:Treason). Joffrey forces Sansa to view Septa Mordane's decapitated head on a spike on the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s walls, along with Eddard's.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Septa [Nysterica](wiki:Nysterica) accompanies Lady [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) to King's Landing for her wedding to King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon). Sansa Stark meets Septa Nysterica, along with Margaery's other [ladies](wiki:Ladies-in-waiting), when she is invited to a supper at the [Maidenvault](wiki:Maidenvault).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)
At [House Baelish](wiki:House_Baelish)'s [tower](wiki:Drearfort) in the [Vale](wiki:Vale), Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) instructs Sansa Stark on the background for her assumed identity as his bastard daughter, Alayne Stone. Alayne was entrusted to the Faith when her mother died in childbirth, but at her [flowering](wiki:Flowering) decided she did not wish to be a septa, and contacted Petyr.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)
In King's Landing, [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) sends Septa [Donyse](wiki:Donyse) to [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth), to alter some gowns to fit her properly.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Septa [Eglantine](wiki:Eglantine) accompanies Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), helping disguise the girl during the voyage.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)
During the wake for Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), seventy-seven septas sing before the altar of [the Mother](wiki:Mother_(the_Seven)) within the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor).(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) At midnight during Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister)'s vigil, softly-singing septas emerge through the Mother's Doors.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)
The bones of septas murdered during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) are brought by [sparrows](wiki:Sparrows) to the statue of [Baelor the Blessed](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) at the Great Sept.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)
Queen [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) is accused of adultery, and she and her cousins are taken into custody by septas. Margaery's [maidenhead](wiki:Maidenhead) is examined by septas and found broken, and Septa [Nysterica](wiki:Nysterica) is confined to a penitent's cell for failure to do her duty.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) The [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow) then has Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) seized by septas and [silent sisters](wiki:Silent_sisters), for her own accused crimes of adultery and murder.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Aboard the [Shy Maid](wiki:Shy_Maid), Septa [Lemore](wiki:Lemore) educates [Young Griff](wiki:Young_Griff) about the Seven.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) wonders if she is a true septa, due to her lack of modesty, and stretchmarks likely indicating a pregnancy.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)
Septas [Moelle](wiki:Moelle), [Scolera](wiki:Scolera), and [Unella](wiki:Unella) serve as Cersei's gaolers.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) They are part of Cersei's escort during her [walk of atonement](wiki:Walk_of_atonement) for the sin of fornication, ringing bells and chanting, "Shame, shame, shame on the sinner."(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)
After her walk of atonement, [Lord Regent](wiki:Lord_Regent) [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) places Cersei within house arrest in the Red Keep, awaiting her trial for her other accused crimes. Rather than her usual [ladies-in-waiting](wiki:Ladies-in-waiting), Cersei is attended by a septa and three novices selected by the [High Septon](wiki:High_Sparrow). The novices are [highborn](wiki:Highborn) girls between the age of twelve and sixteen, and the High Septon insists that no girl spend more than seven days with Cersei, lest she be corrupted. The novices serve as Cersei's [handmaids](wiki:Handmaid), with one sharing her bed every night to make sure she has no other company. The other two sleep in an adjacent chamber with their supervising septa. The novices report to Kevan that of the hours Cersei is awake, she spends a third of that time with her son, King [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), another third praying, and the rest in her tub, bathing four or five times a day, scrubbing herself with [horsehair](wiki:Horse) brushes and lye soap.(wiki:Septa.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)
