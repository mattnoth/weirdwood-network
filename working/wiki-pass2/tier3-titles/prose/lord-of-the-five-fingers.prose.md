## Origins

The [Andal](wiki:Andal) knight Ser [Corwyn Corbray](wiki:Corwyn_Corbray_(lord)) claimed the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers) after the deaths of [Dywen Shell](wiki:Dywen_Shell) and [Jon Brightstone](wiki:Jon_Brightstone), rival [Kings of the Fingers](wiki:King_of_the_Fingers) of [First Men](wiki:First_Men) origin. Corwyn took Jon's daughter for his bride and Dywin's wife for his bedwarmer. Unlike many of his fellow Andal warlords, Corbray did not style himself as a king, instead preferring the more modest Lord of the Five Fingers.(wiki:Lord of the Five Fingers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
At some point in history, the title was abandoned and [House Corbray](wiki:House_Corbray) began to style themselves Kings of the Fingers.(wiki:Lord of the Five Fingers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
