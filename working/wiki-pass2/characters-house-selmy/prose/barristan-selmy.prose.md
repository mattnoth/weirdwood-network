## Origins

### Early life

Barristan was born the first son of Ser [Lyonel Selmy](wiki:Lyonel_Selmy). He squired for Ser [Manfred Swann](wiki:Manfred_Swann) in his youth.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) At the age of ten, Barristan donned the armor of a [mystery knight](wiki:Mystery_knight) and entered a [tourney](wiki:List_of_tourneys_in_Westeros) at [Blackhaven](wiki:Blackhaven). Prince [Duncan Targaryen](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen) took pity on the small mystery knight while others laughed. Duncan jousted Barristan when no one else would and it was Duncan who gave Barristan his epithet.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Again competing as a mystery knight, Barristan received his [knighthood](wiki:Knight) at the age of sixteen from King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) after unhorsing Prince Duncan and Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), during the [winter tourney at King's Landing](wiki:Winter_tourney_at_King's_Landing).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

An eminently successful tourney knight throughout his career, Barristan also proved his worth on the battlefield. During the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings) Barristan slew [Maelys I Blackfyre](wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre) in single combat, ending the male line of the [Blackfyre Pretenders](wiki:Blackfyre_Pretenders). Barristan cut a bloody path through the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) to reach Maelys.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-4)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) The remaining members of the [Band of Nine](wiki:Band_of_Nine) had little to no interest in conquering [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), and soon returned to their own domains. Barristan's deeds during the war gained him undying fame and his name was well known throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) afterwards.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)

Barristan was named to the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) at the age of twenty-three and swore his vows before Ser [Gerold Hightower](wiki:Gerold_Hightower), while King [Jaehaerys II Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen) bestowed the white cloak on him.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) In joining the Kingsguard, he gave up the claim to his family seat, and the girl he was to wed was married to his cousin instead.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-5)

### Aerys II

Barristan continued to serve in the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) after Jaehaerys died and [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) ascended the throne. In [277 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), during the [Defiance of Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale), King Aerys had been held hostage inside of the town for half a year. Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), was planning to storm [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), but Barristan offered to personally attempt to retrieve the captive Aerys himself. Tywin gave him a day.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-6)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

Disguised as a hooded beggar in the dead of night, Barristan climbed the walls of Duskendale, scaled the walls of the [Dun Fort](wiki:Dun_Fort), and slew a sentry before the man could raise the alarm. Barristan freed Aerys from the dungeon and led him to the upper levels. During the escape attempt, Barristan slew two guards and Ser [Symon Hollard](wiki:Symon_Hollard), avenging the death of his [sworn brother](wiki:Sworn_brother), Ser [Gwayne Gaunt](wiki:Gwayne_Gaunt), who had been killed by Symon. Barristan and Aerys rushed to the stables, with the knight fending off those who tried to intervene. Mounted on stolen horses, the pair charged out the Dun Fort's gate and escaped through the streets of Duskendale.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-7) Barristan took an arrow to the chest during the rescue.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) Houses [Darklyn](wiki:House_Darklyn) and [Hollard](wiki:House_Hollard) were destroyed by Aerys afterward, but the king acquiesced to Barristan's plea to spare the life of the child [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) Many songs have since been sung of Barristan's deeds at Duskendale.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-2)

When the band of outlaws known as the [Kingswood Brotherhood](wiki:Kingswood_Brotherhood) caused problems, the king sent his Kingsguard on campaign in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood). Barristan rescued Lady [Jeyne Swann](wiki:Jeyne_Swann) and her septa, and he killed the bandits' leader, Ser [Simon Toyne](wiki:Simon_Toyne), in single combat.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-8) During a [tourney at Storm's End](wiki:Tourney_at_Storm's_End), Barristan defeated Aerys's heir, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), in the final joust, winning the tourney.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-9)

Barristan had greater difficulty keeping his vows in the later years of Aerys's reign, as the king became madder and crueler.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) Like his other sworn brothers of the Kingsguard, Barristan was present at the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal). He was infatuated by the beautiful Lady [Ashara Dayne](wiki:Ashara_Dayne), though he knew nothing could come of his desire for her. Barristan planned on naming her [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty) if he won the tournament, but the knight was unhorsed by Rhaegar.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) The crown prince then crowned [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), setting off a chain of events that led to [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) Years later, Barristan wonders if he could have averted the war if he had defeated Rhaegar in the tournament. He also fantasizes that, if he had won the tourney and crowned Ashara, perhaps she would have turned to him "instead of Stark." He thinks of the consequences of his unspoken love for Ashara as one of his many failures.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

### Robert's Rebellion

During [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), after the defeat of Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) at the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells), Barristan and Ser [Jonothor Darry](wiki:Jonothor_Darry) of the Kingsguard were sent to rally the remnants of the loyalist army.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

Barristan fought in the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), during which he was wounded by arrow, spear, and sword(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-10) while slaying a dozen friends of Lords [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) was killed by Robert in single combat in the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) After the battle, Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) counseled that Barristan's throat should be cut, but Robert instead called for his own [maester](wiki:Maester) to attend the knight.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)

Because he had been wounded so badly, Barristan was not present in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) when the bodies of Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen)'s children, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) and Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar))—slain during [the Sack](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing)—were presented to Robert by Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). Barristan later felt that if he had been present and Robert had smiled at the bodies, nothing would have stopped him from killing the man.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8)

### Robert I

Barristan considered seeking out Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), but the exile was too young to rule on his own and had already begun showing signs of [Targaryen madness](wiki:Targaryen_madness).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) Because the lives of Barristan and many others had chivalrously been spared during the war by the new king, [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon),(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) Barristan instead took Robert's pardon and was named [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard).﻿﻿(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-2)

Having already had reservations about Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) due to the youth's age and inexperience, Barristan did not approve of Robert allowing Jaime to continue his service after having killed King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) in the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing). Barristan was known to say that the Kingslayer's white cloak should be traded for a [black one](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

[Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), knew Barristan when he was younger.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) During [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), Barristan led the attack on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-11) He has remained a formidable fighter even in his advanced years, winning the championship of a tourney at King's Landing at the age of fifty-seven, and unhorsing [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) in a tilt in the [tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_Prince_Joffrey's_name_day) in [297 AC](wiki:297_AC).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

## Appearances & Description

Barristan is tall and has pale blue eyes which some consider to be sad.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) He is an older man, with white hair and lined features. When he was young, he had blond hair.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Some consider him handsome, at least for his age.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Despite his advanced age, Barristan looks strong and graceful and every bit the skilled [knight](wiki:Knight) he was in his youth.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) He is taller than Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) but less muscular.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

Barristan is considered one of the most skilled and respected knights in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms),(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) considered to be made of the true [steel](wiki:Steel) that marks a [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) knight.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) believes him to be the greatest living knight.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) According to a semi-canon source, he is comparable in skill to the acclaimed Ser [Arthur Dayne](wiki:Arthur_Dayne), the [Sword of the Morning](wiki:Sword_of_the_Morning).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-18)

A dutiful(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) and gallant(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) man, Barristan dislikes tricks(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) and scheming.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Some do not consider him cunning.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Barristan can speak some of the [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian) of the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) and the mongrel [Ghiscari](wiki:Ghiscari) of [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Barristan's writing in the [White Book](wiki:White_Book) is smaller and more elegant than that of Ser [Gerold Hightower](wiki:Gerold_Hightower), his predecessor as [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Like other members of the Kingsguard, Barristan's plate armor, cloak, and shield are all in white.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) His enameled scales have silver chasings and clasps.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) He wields a [longsword](wiki:Longsword)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and a dagger.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

As the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), Barristan travels together with Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) and Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne) to the [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads), to serve as an honor guard to the procession led by King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and his new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), on their way back to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) He later accompanies the king during his travels through the city.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

During the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney), Barristan is defeated by Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) after three tilts, having defeated men thirty and forty years younger in his first two contests.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) When Ser [Hugh of the Vale](wiki:Hugh) is killed in the tournament by Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane), Barristan stands vigil over the young knight himself, as the boy had no one else.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) Barristan and Eddard advise Robert against participating in the melee.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3)

As Lord Commander, Barristan is present at the [small council](wiki:Small_council) meeting when King Robert orders the death of the pregnant [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), daughter to the late King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen). He and Eddard are the only council members who protest the move.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-3)

Barristan accompanies Robert on his hunt for a white hart in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) The king is mortally wounded by a [boar](wiki:Boar) during the hunt,(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) however, and Robert is succeeded by his eldest son, King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

In an unprecedented move, as per Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys)'s suggestion,(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) Barristan is dismissed from the Kingsguard and the small council on grounds of old age, the implied accusation being that he did not keep King Robert safe.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2) The [Lannisters'](wiki:House_Lannister) motivations, however, are to allow Joffrey to give a white cloak to his "dog," [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane), and to allow Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) to place her brother Jaime as the new Lord Commander with a seat on the small council.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-1) Outraged by his removal, Barristan throws down his sword and leaves the throne room.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3)

Joffrey orders the knight seized and questioned for a perceived threat uttered in the heat of his dismissal. Barristan enters the [White Sword Tower](wiki:White_Sword_Tower) to edit his [White Book](wiki:White_Book) entry, dutifully recording his own dismissal.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-12) [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt) and three [gold cloaks](wiki:Gold_cloaks) attempt to seize Barristan in the stables. Though he does not have his sword, Barristan kills two guards and flees the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

While his kin at [Harvest Hall](wiki:Harvest_Hall) would welcome Barristan back home, Barristan does not want to inflict Joffrey's displeasure on them. Instead, once outside of King's landing, Barristan sells his horse and disguises himself as a commoner, cloaked in his old age. He returns to the city in a stream of [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), pretending to be fleeing from the war. Having grown a long white beard, Barristan is present in the crowd when Eddard Stark is executed before the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor). Following Lord Stark's death, Barristan goes to the sept to pray for Eddard's soul and thank the gods that he himself had only been dismissed. Reevaluating his years of service in Robert's Kingsguard, he admits that Robert had been a good [knight](wiki:Knight), but a poor king. Barristan decides that he must find his true king and die in his service.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2)

Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) considers Barristan's dismissal a folly. While Barristan is old, he still commands enough respect to make him a threat in case he chooses to back [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) or [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), and he cites it as one reason why he sends his son [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) as acting Hand to King's Landing to keep Joffrey better in check than Queen Cersei is capable of.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-2)

### A Clash of Kings

Tyrion Lannister compares Ser Barristan's reputation among [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) to that of [Serwyn of the Mirror Shield](wiki:Serwyn_of_the_Mirror_Shield) and Prince [Aemon the Dragonknight](wiki:Aemon_the_Dragonknight), and warns Cersei that Robb Stark or Stannis Baratheon will benefit if Barristan publicly supports him.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-3) Their rival Renly Baratheon hopes that Barristan's announcement that he is going to serve the true king refers to him. Renly keeps a cloak in his [Rainbow Guard](wiki:Rainbow_Guard) ready for Barristan in case the old knight offers him his sword. When he concludes that his hopes have been futile, Renly gives Barristan's spot on his Kingsguard to [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth), believing that the knight may support the Starks.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) However, Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) informs him that Barristan is not with Robb. Renly concludes that Barristan must have decided to support his brother Stannis, and he orders his knights to spare Barristan's life.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)

Following his decision to find his true king, [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), Barristan crosses the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) and travels to [Pentos](wiki:Pentos).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-3) Barristan eventually ends up in contact with Magister [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis), and he learns of Viserys's death in [Vaes Dothrak](wiki:Vaes_Dothrak). Illyrio sends Barristan, disguised as the old squire Arstan Whitebeard, alongside the huge eunuch [Belwas](wiki:Belwas) to [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) to bring Queen [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) back to Pentos.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

Barristan and Strong Belwas sail east on the three ships of Captain [Groleo](wiki:Groleo), which visit [New Ghis](wiki:New_Ghis) before arriving at Qarth.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rtloiafjourneys.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) They follow Daenerys and Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont), who are trying to find passage on the ships docked at the [port of Qarth](wiki:Port_of_Qarth). Jorah notices them and becomes suspicious. When a [Sorrowful Man](wiki:Sorrowful_Men) attempts to assassinate Daenerys by presenting her with a box containing a [manticore](wiki:Manticore), Barristan knocks the manticore from her hands and kills it.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) Barristan introduces himself as Arstan Whitebeard, and together with Belwas tell Daenerys that Illyrio had sent them to serve her, and bring her back to Pentos, as there were four kings in Westeros when they left Pentos, and no justice.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

### A Storm of Swords

Rather than sail to [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), Daenerys chooses to sail on Groleo's ships to [Astapor](wiki:Astapor) to buy [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied). Barristan, still keeping his true identity a secret, discusses with Daenerys her [dragons](wiki:Dragon), her father, King [Aerys II](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), and her brother, Prince [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen). Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) distrusts Barristan and Belwas, pointing out that Barristan speaks out too brazenly for a mere squire.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

Barristan is with Daenerys in the [Plaza of Pride](wiki:Plaza_of_Pride) when they meet with [Kraznys mo Nakloz](wiki:Kraznys_mo_Nakloz), the [Good Master](wiki:Good_Master) selling Unsullied. Barristan disapproves of buying [slaves](wiki:Slaves), and when Daenerys asks how she is to win [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) without an army, he replies that half of Westeros would support her because of Rhaegar. When she asks about her father, however, Barristan has little positive to say. He advises Daenerys that she should send envoys, and that even those that opposed her father might support her.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) He also objects to Daenerys's plan to pay for the Unsullied with one of her dragons, but she [conquers Astapor](wiki:Fall_of_Astapor) by ordering her new Unsullied to attack the slavers,(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) after which she frees them from slavery.

When Daenerys announces her plan to take [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai), Barristan agrees with Jorah that she is Rhaegar's sister, adding that she is also a queen. While waiting for the results of the [battle near Yunkai](wiki:Battle_near_Yunkai), Barristan and Daenerys discuss Rhaegar, the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal), and [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-2)

During the [siege of Meereen](wiki:Siege_of_Meereen), [Oznak zo Pahl](wiki:Oznak_zo_Pahl) rides out of the city on a white charger with a fourteen-foot lance, challenging the besiegers to send forth a champion. Barristan tells Daenerys that this challenge must be met, after which Daenerys decides that [Belwas](wiki:Belwas) is the best choice.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-3)

Daenerys later decides that she will ride out amongst her people with only a limited guard. She is attacked by [Mero](wiki:Mero), the former commander of the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons), but Barristan easily dispatches him. The sellsword falls into the water and the freemen fall on his body.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) Daenerys wishes that Baristan be knighted in gratitude, but both Barristan and Jorah Mormont refuse this at the same time. Jorah, highly suspicious, questions how an old squire with a stick could have been capable of killing Mero, the Titan's Bastard, and asks Barristan who he is, to which Barristan replies "a better [knight](wiki:Knight) than you, ser".(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2)

Barristan begs forgiveness from Daenerys for withholding truths from her. Jorah recognizes Barristan and informs Daenerys that Barristan had betrayed [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) to serve [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). Barristan tells her that he is hers, if she will have him. He also explains he took Robert's pardon and served with men not worthy of a place in the Kingsguard, and that, when [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) dismissed him aside and sent men to kill him, he realized that he had to find his true king. Barristan tells Daenerys he kept his identity a secret because he wanted to prevent the Lannisters from learning he had joined her. When Barristan insinuates that Jorah has been spying on Daenerys for [Varys](wiki:Varys) since her wedding to [Drogo](wiki:Drogo), Jorah affirms the accusation is true.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-3)

Daenerys captures Meereen by having Barristan, Jorah, and Belwas infiltrate the city through the sewers and release slaves. Afterward, Barristan shaves his beard and looks ten years younger. Daenerys reveals she had hoped that they would die in the sewers. Barristan admits he had kept his identity hidden to be certain that she did not have the [taint of madness](wiki:Targaryen_madness) like her father had. Barristan tells the queen that her brother, [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), had more of the taint of her father, the Mad King. Rhaegar did not, and he sees no taint in her. He further tells her that he will do whatever she asks, and if she will not have him he would continue to serve Belwas. Daenerys accepts him in her service, and Barristan receives a sword from her, refusing to take one from anyone else. In contrast, Jorah is exiled from Meereen.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-2)

Daenerys realizes that Barristan had personally known her grandfather, King [Jaehaerys II](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen), and he could tell her more about where she came from. After he has been pardoned, Barristan tells Daenerys that her father's secrets, which he had overheard as a Kingsguard knight, now by rights belong to her. He explains that Aerys had not been mad initially, but that his lapses became worse over time, while her mother, Queen [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen), did her best to shield Viserys from his father's insanity. Daenerys decides she does not want to hear it at that moment, but that he should tell her more some other time. Barristan is among her advisors when she states she will not leave but stay and rule as a queen in Meereen.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-3)

### A Dance with Dragons

Daenerys names Barristan the Lord Commander of her [Queensguard](wiki:Queensguard)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) and gives him new gold-and-silver armor.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) The mail is gilded and finely wrought, with supple links and hard enamel plates. His longsword hangs from a white leather belt with golden buckles, and he also wields a dagger.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-9) Barristan wears a long white cloak about his shoulders and carries a white shield.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) His helm has a narrow eye slit and ornate dragon's wings.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-10) His horse is a dapple grey.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

Barristan attempts to lead his queen down an honorable path towards the safety of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen). Barristan distrusts Daenerys's paramour, [Daario Naharis](wiki:Daario_Naharis), and he unsuccessfully suggests holding a [tourney](wiki:Tourney).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-4) [Groleo](wiki:Groleo) and Barristan inspect the ships brought to Meereen by [Xaro Xhoan Daxos](wiki:Xaro_Xhoan_Daxos). Daenerys rejects Barristan's advice to leave Meereen for [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)

Barristan instructs [Grazhar zo Galare](wiki:Grazhar_zo_Galare) and other [noble boys](wiki:Cupbearers_of_Daenerys_Targaryen) in western chivalry.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0) The Lord Commander also begins training more than sixty freed [slaves](wiki:Slaves), with thirty of them persevering.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8) The most promising of them become Barristan's squires, including [Tumco Lho](wiki:Tumco_Lho), [Larraq](wiki:Larraq), the [Red Lamb](wiki:Red_Lamb), and three [Ghiscari](wiki:Ghiscari) brothers.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-11)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0)

When [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) marches on Meereen, [Skahaz mo Kandaq](wiki:Skahaz_mo_Kandaq) advises Daenerys to remain within Meereen's strong walls while Barristan suggests defeating the Yunkai'i in the field. Selmy objects to allowing into Meereen refugees from [Astapor](wiki:Astapor), because of the danger of the [bloody flux](wiki:Bloody_flux),(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) and the knight also objects when Daenerys meets the refugees outside of the walls.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) The Lord Commander does not approve of Daenerys wedding [Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq), since the man would not be accepted in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-1) Barristan is supportive of [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell) when the prince informs Daenerys of the [secret marriage pact](wiki:Secret_marriage_pact), but Daenerys weds Hizdahr instead of Quentyn to ensure temporary peace within Meereen.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

When [Drogon](wiki:Drogon) begins attacking people in [Daznak's Pit](wiki:Daznak's_Pit), Barristan tries to protect Daenerys by drawing the [dragon](wiki:Dragon)'s attention to himself. The queen ends up fleeing Meereen atop Drogon.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

After Daenerys's departure, Hizdahr replaces the royal advisors, but Barristan meets with Skahaz and [Grey Worm](wiki:Grey_Worm).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9) He is present when [Bloodbeard](wiki:Bloodbeard) presents the court with the head of Groleo, who was slain in response to the death of [Yurkhaz zo Yunzak](wiki:Yurkhaz_zo_Yunzak). Barristan is enraged and wants to kill Bloodbeard, but stops because the act would break his queen's peace. Barristan advises Quentyn and his companions to leave Meereen for their own safety,(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) but the [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) remain in the city.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)

Convinced that Hizdahr had attempted to assassinate Daenerys with [poisoned locusts](wiki:Poisoned_locusts),(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) Barristan decides to work with Grey Worm and Skahaz, despite his loathing of the latter and his own inhibitions about making decisions of state in the absence of his queen.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-10) Barristan leads with honor and refuses Skahaz's suggestion to kill the Ghiscari [cupbearers](wiki:Cupbearers_of_Daenerys_Targaryen), the children of powerful Meereenese nobles. Barristan and the [Brazen Beasts](wiki:Brazen_Beasts) plan to take King Hizdahr hostage, but he fears that if Hizdahr is harmed the Yunkai'i will kill their remaining hostages—Daario, [Jhogo](wiki:Jhogo) and [Hero](wiki:Hero)—just as they killed Groleo. When Barristan goes to capture Hizdahr, he slays the king's protector, the formidable pit fighter [Khrazz](wiki:Khrazz).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-12) Meanwhile, Quentyn attempts to tame one of dragons, but [Rhaegal](wiki:Rhaegal) and [Viserion](wiki:Viserion) break free from their imprisonment and Quentyn is mortally wounded by [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame).(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0)(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

Barristan assumes the title of [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), sending a reluctant [Galazza Galare](wiki:Galazza_Galare) an envoy to the Yunkai'i and preventing Skahaz from executing hostages. Barristan refuses to hold court, instead holding a council session which plans for battle with Yunkai. A beacon system will be used to call the attack should the fragile peace between the sides be broken.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2) Barristan discretely meets with Ser [Gerris Drinkwater](wiki:Gerris_Drinkwater) and Ser [Archibald Yronwood](wiki:Archibald_Yronwood), the late Quentyn's companions. Tasking them with speaking on his behalf with the [Tattered Prince](wiki:Tattered_Prince), Barristan offers to grant [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) to the sellsword captain if he protects the remaining hostages in the Yunkish camp.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-3) During a meeting between Barristan and Galazza after she returns, Skahaz notifies Barristan that the [Yunkish catapults](wiki:Six_sisters) are flinging corpses infected with the [pale mare](wiki:Pale_mare) into the city.(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-4)

### The Winds of Winter

The Yunkish threat in the [second siege of Meereen](wiki:Second_siege_of_Meereen) has been augmented by the presence of the [bloody flux](wiki:Bloody_flux). Ser Barristan notes that under normal circumstances, he would defend Meereen from behind its strong walls. However, because the Yunkish introduced the pale mare into Meereen by catapulting infected corpses over the walls, he resolves to ride out and meet his enemy on the field rather than watch the impending outbreak annihilate Meereen. On the eve of the offensive, he gives a pep talk to his newly anointed knights and soldiers. When his talk is interrupted by the arrival of dawn, Barristan orders the [Red Lamb](wiki:Red_Lamb) to "sound the attack".(wiki:Barristan_Selmy.cite_ref-BarristanI_59-0)
