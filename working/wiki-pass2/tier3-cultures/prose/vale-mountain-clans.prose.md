## Culture

The [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) are home to a population of people who have rejected the authority of the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) and are independent from the rest of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) and its [feudal society](wiki:Feudalism). The clansmen are an egalitarian society, believing that every person's voice, regardless of gender, should be heard during councils.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Clansmen are fiercely loyal to each other, while some clans share close bonds.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Conflicts between the clans are often resolved by payment of blood money and clansmen are quick to settle personal grievances through violence.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) considered the clansmen to be savages.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
Clansmen identify themselves with a first name and the name of their father; examples include [Conn son of Coratt](wiki:Conn) and [Chella daughter of Cheyk](wiki:Chella).(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
The clansmen live short and savage lives as bandits and outlaws.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) They are a poor people with poor quality of weapons and armor,(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) wielding stone axes and wooden clubs.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) They subsist by raiding local villages, small groups of travelers, and will attack even the [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie) if he is not properly protected. They take all weapons, grain, and armor they can find, as well as all women, whether they are wedded or not. Some women participate in raids. However, it has been hundreds of years since the clansmen have threatened the [lowlands](wiki:Lowlander) of [the Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) with anything more serious than raids.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)
The clansmen ride small [horses](wiki:Horse) that are suited to the narrow mountain paths.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

## Origins

During the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), [Andals](wiki:Andals) from [Essos](wiki:Essos) conquered the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) from the [First Men](wiki:First_Men). Some First Men submitted to and intermarried with Andals in the aftermath of the [Battle of Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_Seven_Stars), but others refused to kneel. The First Men who fled from the fertile [Vale proper](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn_(valley)) into the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) are the ancestors of the modern clansmen.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)
There is fierce enmity between the mountain clans and the [knights of the Vale](wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale),(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and the clans do not trust the [lowland lords](wiki:Lowlander).(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6) Clansmen have been known to burn, plunder, and carry off women from the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Even after the [Targaryen Conquest](wiki:Targaryen_Conquest), the mountain clans have remained a persistent problem for [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn), the [Lords of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie). In [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), Lord [Darnold Arryn](wiki:Darnold_Arryn) and his brother, Ser [Rymond](wiki:Rymond_Arryn), were slain when they pursued raiders from the mountain clans into the Mountains of the Moon.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Lord [Arryn](wiki:Lord_Arryn_(father_of_Jeyne)) and his sons were killed by the [Stone Crows](wiki:Stone_Crows) in [97 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), after which his young daughter [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn) became [Lady of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie) .(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
In [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), starved by winter, raiders from the Mountains of the Moon descended upon the Vale in large numbers to raid and plunder. Lady [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn), a [regent](wiki:Regent) of King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), was forced to leave her office in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to see to the Vale's defenses.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Her cousin, Ser [Joffrey Arryn](wiki:Joffrey_Arryn), frequently defended the Vale against the clansmen as the [Knight of the Gate](wiki:Knight_of_the_Gate). When Lady Arryn passed away, Ser [Robert Rowan](wiki:Robert_Rowan) led a royal host to enforce Joffrey as her chosen heir, though his army was attacked nightly by clansmen. His baggage train was frequently attacked by the mountain clans, and eventually Robert and many of his men were killed by falling boulders when the clansmen toppled half a mountainside upon his host. Lord [Benjicot Blackwood](wiki:Benjicot_Blackwood), who took over Robert's host, later remarked about the tenacity of the starving clansmen.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)
In the years after the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), an offshoot of the Painted Dogs became known as the Burned Men, who were said to have worshiped a fire-[witch](wiki:Witch), sending their boys to bring her gifts and risk the flames of her [dragon](wiki:Dragon) to prove their manhood.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) suggests this was [Nettles](wiki:Nettles) and [Sheepstealer](wiki:Sheepstealer).(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)
A daughter of [Alys Arryn](wiki:Alys_Arryn) and Ser [Elys Waynwood](wiki:Elys_Waynwood) was on her way to marry a [Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken) when she was carried off by Burned Men.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

The clans have become more active since the death of [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie).(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)
While returning from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) considers visiting her sister, the widowed Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), but decides the passes through the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) are too dangerous.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) After seizing [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) in the [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads), Catelyn decides to bring him east to the Eyrie rather than north to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). Their party is attacked by clansmen along the [high road](wiki:High_road)(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) before they reach the [Bloody Gate](wiki:Bloody_Gate).(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3)
After Tyrion wins his [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) at the Eyrie through his proxy, [Bronn](wiki:Bronn), Lysa has them leave the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) through the dangerous high road. Tyrion and Bronn encounter clansmen during their journey, however. In return for promising them [steel](wiki:Steel)(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) so they can conquer the Vale,(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Tyrion enlists them to fight for his family, "[Clan Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister)".(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)
About three hundred clansmen join Tyrion as he leaves the foothills; [Gunthor](wiki:Gunthor) remains in the mountains to raise more clansmen.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) When Tyrion reaches the encampment of his father, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), near the [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads), he requests three thousand helms and hauberks among other [armament](wiki:Armament).(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8) The three hundred fight alongside Tyrion in the Lannister vanguard during the [battle on the Green Fork](wiki:Battle_on_the_Green_Fork), with perhaps half of them surviving.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Serving as the acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Tyrion brings the surviving clansmen to King's Landing, where they reside in barracks below the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand).(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) reports to Tyrion that Lysa Arryn has been dealing with raids of mountain clansmen in greater numbers and better armed than ever before; Tyrion, who had armed them, suggests he could help Lysa deal with the clans in exchange for her and and her son, Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn), swearing fealty to King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) As Tyrion is without the benefit of [House Lannister guardsmen](wiki:House_Lannister_guards), he uses the clansmen as his own guards and soldiers. Before the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), he sends them into the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood) to disrupt the army of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

After the Blackwater, [red cloaks](wiki:Red_cloaks) and Kingslanders prevent [Chella](wiki:Chella) and the [Black Ears](wiki:Black_Ears) from returning to the capital. [Timett](wiki:Timett) returns to the mountains with the [Burned Men](wiki:Burned_Men), while [Shagga](wiki:Shagga) remains in the kingswood with the [Stone Crows](wiki:Stone_Crows).(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
in the foothills of the Mountains of the Moon, [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) and [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) learn that the clans in the Vale are bolder as of late and are carrying steel, with Gunthor and Timett leading raids.(wiki:Vale_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rasos65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)
