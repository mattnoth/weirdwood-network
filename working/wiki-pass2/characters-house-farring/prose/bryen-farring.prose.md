## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Bryen is present during [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre)'s burning of the [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) [sept](wiki:Sept)'s statues of the [Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). He equips King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) with a leather cape to keep off the flames while he draws the sword [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer) from the [Mother](wiki:Mother_(the_Seven))'s breast. After the ceremony, Bryen and his fellow squire [Devan Seaworth](wiki:Devan_Seaworth) collect Lightbringer.(wiki:Bryen_Farring.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

### A Storm of Swords

When Stannis Baratheon goes north to the [Wall](wiki:Wall), Bryen, as his squire, accompanies him and is part of his retinue at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black).(wiki:Bryen_Farring.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

While Stannis's other squire Devan Seaworth stays at Castle Black with [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), Bryen travels with King Stannis on his march south to face the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton). At the [crofter's village](wiki:Crofter's_village) in the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood) near [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), Bryen dies when he succumbs to the cold and hunger. His body is burned on a funeral pyre.(wiki:Bryen_Farring.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Four days later, a soldier from [House Peasebury](wiki:House_Peasebury), accused of [cannibalism](wiki:Cannibalism) and sentenced to be burned alive, levies insults at Ser [Godry Farring](wiki:Godry_Farring). The serjeant claims he laughed when Godry's cousin Bryen died, that the boy smelled good on the pyre, and that he should have eaten him too. Godry ignores the insults, but another aimed at [Clayton Suggs](wiki:Clayton_Suggs) succeeds in inciting him to cut the serjeant's throat.(wiki:Bryen_Farring.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
