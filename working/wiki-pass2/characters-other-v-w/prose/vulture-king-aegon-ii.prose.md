## Origins

[Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End), gathered six thousand men in support of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). Instead of marching against Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and her [dragons](wiki:Dragon), however, Borros led his host south into the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) to retaliate for incursions from [Dorne](wiki:Dorne).(wiki:Vulture_King_(Aegon_II).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

Borros was victorious in his campaign against the Vulture King,(wiki:Vulture_King_(Aegon_II).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) during which Ser [Steffon Connington](wiki:Steffon_Connington) also proved his valor.(wiki:Vulture_King_(Aegon_II).cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Whether the rebel was related by blood to other "[Vulture Kings](wiki:Vulture_King)" is unknown.(wiki:Vulture_King_(Aegon_II).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
