## Origins

After his father had brought a [septon](wiki:Septon) to court and made him one of his counselors and built the first [sept](wiki:Sept) at [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), Merle converted to the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) and helped fund the construction of septs, [septries](wiki:Septry), and [motherhouses](wiki:Motherhouse) all over the Reach.(wiki:Merle_I_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Merle also took an Andal maiden to wife as a means of binding the bride's father to the realm, as well as other Andals into his service as [knights](wiki:Knight) and retainers, and bequeathed lands and wives and lordships for the more powerful of the Andal kings and other Andal adventurers descending on the Reach in exchange for pledges of fealty.(wiki:Merle_I_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
He also sought after Andal craftsmen, especially blacksmiths and stonemasons, to settle throughout the Reach, supplying his [bannermen](wiki:Bannermen) with [iron](wiki:Iron) instead of [bronze](wiki:Bronze) and strengthening their castles and holdfasts with Andal masonry.(wiki:Merle_I_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
