**Headline finding: almost none of these 16 distinct pairs are genuine same-person duplicates.** The classifier's heuristic (bare slug + disambiguated-variant slug both appear as parents of the same child → assume duplicate) is unsafe here for a specific, systematic reason: **AWOIAF uses bare "Firstname Lastname" pages in two very different ways** — sometimes as a true redirect/primary-page for one person, but very often as a **disambiguation page listing multiple distinct, unrelated people who happen to share a name** (common in House Targaryen/Stark/Tyrell/Lannister genealogies spanning centuries). The classifier's "qc 0 vs 0" signal cannot distinguish these. Only 1 of 16 pairs is a real redirect/duplicate.

## Special flag: `aegon-targaryen` bare slug is a NODE_SPLIT conflation bucket, not a clean duplicate

Confirmed per the task's special-watch instructions: `aegon-targaryen` (bare) is used in the graph as parent of **both** `aerea-targaryen` (real parent = `aegon-targaryen-son-of-aenys-i`, per `Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).json` infobox "Issue: Aerea Targaryen, Rhaella Targaryen") **and** `maegon-targaryen` (real parent = `aegon-targaryen-son-of-gaemon`, per `Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Gaemon).json` infobox "Issue: Maegon Targaryen, Aerys Targaryen"). These are two entirely different Aegons, centuries apart (son-of-Gaemon lived in the Century of Blood, ~114-80 BC; son-of-Aenys-I lived 26-43 AC). The wiki's `Aegon_Targaryen.json` disambiguation page lists both among ~10 total Aegons.

**Recommendation:** do NOT merge `aegon-targaryen` into either variant. Treat both occurrences as **GENUINE_EXTRA_PARENT**: delete the ambiguous `aegon-targaryen -> {child}` edge per-child (one deletion for `aerea-targaryen`, a separate deletion for `maegon-targaryen`), keeping only the correctly-disambiguated parent edge in each case. The bare `aegon-targaryen` node itself should NOT be treated as a person node at all going forward — it's wiki disambiguation-page noise that should probably never have resolved as an edge target/source; flag for the resolver/alias-map team to stop resolving parentage edges to disambiguation-page slugs in general (this same bug pattern — disambig slug mistakenly treated as a person — is the root cause of nearly every REJECT verdict above, not just the Aegon case).

## Summary counts

- **APPROVE-MERGE: 2 pairs** (elenda-caron→elenda-baratheon; alayne→alayne-baelish) — both confirmed via literal redirect and/or duplicate-edge inspection, not quote-count.
- **REJECT-DIFFERENT-PEOPLE: 13 pairs** — all are wiki disambiguation pages (multiple real people sharing a name) or two distinct single-person pages explicitly cross-referencing each other as different people. None should be merged. Recommend GENUINE_EXTRA_PARENT / WRONG_NAMESAKE delete-the-bare-edge treatment per child instead.
- **NEEDS-MATT: 0** — every case was resolvable from local wiki cache with high confidence.
- **SPECIAL-CASE: 1** (alayne/alayne-baelish, also approved above; flagged separately because its surface disambiguation-page shape initially looked like a reject).
- **NODE_SPLIT conflation flag: 1** (aegon-targaryen bare slug, spanning 2 of the pairs above).

**Systemic root cause:** the wiki-infobox edge ingester (`run_id: infobox-merge-20260613` and similar) is resolving parentage edges to **bare disambiguation-page slugs** when a wiki infobox field names someone only by "Firstname Lastname" without the disambiguator. This produces spurious extra-parent edges across the whole Targaryen/Stark/Tyrell/Lannister genealogy, not just this batch. Recommend a follow-up audit: any `PARENT_OF`/`SPOUSE_OF`/etc. edge whose slug matches a **disambiguation-page-backed node** (rather than a specific single-person page) is a candidate for GENUINE_EXTRA_PARENT deletion, and the resolver should be hardened to prefer the disambiguated variant at edge-creation time going forward.
