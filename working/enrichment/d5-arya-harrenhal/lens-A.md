## Proposed NEW nodes

### 1. `stableboy-killing`
- **slug:** `stableboy-killing`
- **type:** event.death
- **name:** Killing of the Stableboy (Needle's First Kill)
- **summary:** During Arya's flight from the Red Keep after Ned's arrest, she stabbed a stableboy in the belly with Needle, her first human kill. She reflects on the act repeatedly in ACOK, confirming it shaped her toward violence. The victim is unnamed in the text.
- **Anchor quote:** "I killed a boy, a fat boy like you, I stabbed him in the belly and he died, and I'll kill you too if you don't let me alone." (acok-arya-01:47)
- **Note on proof:** The killing itself occurs between chapters (off-page after agot-arya-05, before acok-arya-01). The ACOK text is Arya's own recollection, which constitutes proof of the event. No on-page agot-arya-05 line narrates the act; the reference in acok-arya-01 is the primary text anchor.

---

### 2. `yoren-shears-arya`
- **slug:** `yoren-shears-arya`
- **type:** event.incident
- **name:** Yoren Seizes and Shears Arya at the Sept of Baelor
- **summary:** Immediately after Ned Stark's execution, Yoren seized Arya from the crowd, forced her into an alley, and cut her hair close to the scalp to disguise her as a boy named "Arry." He then inducted her into his Night's Watch column. This is the pivot event between the Red Keep arc and the road/Harrenhal arc.
- **Anchor quote:** "Then a hand shot out of the press and closed round her arm like a wolf trap, so hard that Needle went flying from her hand." (agot-arya-05:173)
- **Disguise anchor:** "When Yoren had dragged her into that alley she'd thought he meant to kill her, but the sour old man had only held her tight, sawing through her mats and tangles with his dagger." (acok-arya-01:13)

---

### 3. `gold-cloaks-demand-gendry`
- **slug:** `gold-cloaks-demand-gendry`
- **type:** event.incident
- **name:** Gold Cloaks Demand Gendry from Yoren's Band
- **summary:** Six gold cloaks, including an officer, rode out from King's Landing with a sealed warrant from Queen Cersei to claim "a certain boy" — revealed in the scene to be Gendry (Robert's bastard), not Arya. Yoren faced them down with his full company at an inn and sent them off, but the encounter telegraphed the danger and pushed Yoren to alter their route.
- **Anchor quote (warrant revealed):** "He waved his tankard at the noseless man—'I have a warrant for a certain boy—'" (acok-arya-02:153) and the officer's clarification: "He's the one we want." (acok-arya-02:207)
- **Anchor quote (queen's seal):** "The queen wants him, old man, not that it's your concern." (acok-arya-02:161)

---

### 4. `death-of-lommy-greenhands`
- **slug:** `death-of-lommy-greenhands`
- **type:** event.death
- **name:** Death of Lommy Greenhands
- **summary:** After the survivors of the holdfast fight were captured by Gregor's forces near Gods Eye, Lommy Greenhands (wounded and unable to walk) offered to yield. A Lannister spearman asked if he could walk; when Lommy said no, the man drove his spear through Lommy's throat without another word. Rafford "Raff the Sweetling" is confirmed as the killer in the text of acok-arya-05.
- **Anchor quote:** "The man lifted his spear casually and drove the point through the boy's soft throat. Lommy never even had time to yield again. He jerked once, and that was all. When the man pulled his spear loose, blood sprayed out in a dark fountain." (acok-arya-05:323)
- **Killer ID anchor (ACOK-06):** "Raff the Sweetling, who'd driven his spear through Lommy's throat, she hated even more." (acok-arya-06:57)

---

### 5. `death-of-weese`
- **slug:** `death-of-weese`
- **type:** event.death
- **name:** Death of Weese (Jaqen's Second Death)
- **summary:** At Arya's command, Jaqen H'ghar caused Weese's death: his own spotted dog tore out Weese's throat and savaged him on the cobbles of Harrenhal. The method (turning the dog on its master) is implied supernatural agency. Weese was understeward of the Wailing Tower, Arya's direct overseer at Harrenhal.
- **Anchor quote:** "Weese was sprawled across the cobbles, his throat a red ruin, eyes gaping sightlessly up at a bank of grey cloud. His ugly spotted dog stood on his chest, lapping at the blood pulsing from his neck, and every so often ripping a mouthful of flesh out of the dead man's face." (acok-arya-08:113)
- **Arya commands it:** "She took a step … with each she felt less a mouse. She worked her way down the bench, filling wine cups … Arya leaned close and whispered, 'Weese,' right in Jaqen's ear." (acok-arya-07:135)

### 6. `jaqen-naming-gambit`
- **slug:** `jaqen-naming-gambit`
- **type:** event.incident
- **name:** Arya Names Jaqen Himself — the Naming Gambit
- **summary:** In the Harrenhal godswood, Arya exhausted her leverage: rather than name a third person to die, she named Jaqen H'ghar himself. He was forced to negotiate — agreeing to free the captive northmen (via the weasel-soup dungeon massacre) in exchange for Arya "unsaying" his name. This is the bargain that triggers `guards-killed` / `fall-of-harrenhal`.
- **Anchor quote:** "She put her lips to his ear. 'It's Jaqen H'ghar.'" (acok-arya-09:177)
- **Jaqen's distraught reaction:** "Even in the burning barn, with walls of flame towering all around and him in chains, he had not seemed so distraught as he did now." (acok-arya-09:179)

### 7. `jaqen-gives-arya-the-iron-coin`
- **slug:** `jaqen-gives-arya-the-iron-coin`
- **type:** event.incident
- **name:** Jaqen Gives Arya the Iron Coin and Changes His Face
- **summary:** After the dungeon massacre and fall of Harrenhal, in the yard by the dead guards, Jaqen passed a hand down his face and his appearance changed entirely (new features, new hair). He gave Arya an iron coin and instructed her to say "valar morghulis" to any man from Braavos if she ever wished to find him. He then departed across the narrow sea.
- **Anchor quote (coin given):** "He lifted her hand and pressed a small coin into her palm. 'Here.'" (acok-arya-09:327)
- **Anchor quote (face change):** "Jaqen passed a hand down his face from forehead to chin, and where it went he changed. His cheeks grew fuller, his eyes closer; his nose hooked, a scar appeared on his right cheek where no scar had been before." (acok-arya-09:311)
- **Instruction anchor:** "'If the day comes when you would find me again, give that coin to any man from Braavos, and say these words to him—valar morghulis.'" (acok-arya-09:339)

### 8. `arya-escapes-harrenhal`
- **slug:** `arya-escapes-harrenhal`
- **type:** event.flight
- **name:** Arya's Escape from Harrenhal
- **summary:** On a rainy night after Bolton announced he would leave Harrenhal to Vargo Hoat, Arya organized an escape: she obtained horses by invoking Bolton's sigil, took his map and dagger, met Gendry and Hot Pie at the Tower of Ghosts postern, and killed the gate guard with a throat-cut. They rode out into the rain. This is the terminus event that closes the Harrenhal cluster.
- **Anchor quote (gate guard killed):** "Arya slid her dagger out and drew it across his throat, as smooth as summer silk. His blood covered her hands in a hot gush and he tried to shout but there was blood in his mouth as well." (acok-arya-10:309)
- **Anchor quote (Arya's words):** "'Valar morghulis,' she whispered as he died." (acok-arya-10:311)
- **Gendry/Hot Pie present:** "By the time Hot Pie and Gendry came up with the horses, the rain was falling hard. 'You killed him!' Hot Pie gasped." (acok-arya-10:313)

---

### 9. `roose-appoints-arya-cupbearer`
- **slug:** `roose-appoints-arya-cupbearer`
- **type:** event.incident
- **name:** Roose Bolton Appoints Arya (as "Nan") His Cupbearer
- **summary:** After the fall of Harrenhal, Shagwell the Fool dragged Arya before Roose Bolton. Bolton questioned her briefly, renamed her "Nan," and appointed her as his personal cupbearer. She served him through his remaining time at Harrenhal. This event established Arya's direct proximity to Bolton's war-council conversations, from which she extracted strategic intelligence.
- **Anchor quote:** "'For so long as I remain at Harrenhal, Nan, you shall be my cupbearer, and serve me at table and in chambers.'" (acok-arya-09:385)

## Dropped / Considered-but-Rejected

**1. `yoren-takes-western-route` as a standalone event node.**
Considered as a named pivot-event. The western-route decision is a setting change, not a reifiable event hub (no participants with roles, no victims, no artifact wielded). The causal logic is captured by the edges `gold-cloaks-demand-gendry MOTIVATES yoren-the-crow` + `yoren-march-to-the-wall ENABLES fight-at-the-holdfast`. A node would be thin prose-description only. Drop the node; keep the causal edges.

**2. Arya `WITNESS_IN` her father's execution (agot-arya-05).**
She climbs the statue of Baelor and watches. The execution-of-ned-stark node presumably exists already. However, the prose is contested: Yoren forces her to look away at the moment of the execution itself ("Don't look!" / "Look at me"). She does NOT successfully witness the execution — Yoren shields her. WITNESS_IN requires the character actually SEES the charged incident. She hears but does not see the cut. Drop.

**3. `arya-stark TEACHES gendry` (water-dancing / survival).**
She shows Gendry survival skills, but the text doesn't support a structured TEACHES edge — they're peers sharing knowledge, not a master-student relationship. Drop.

**4. The crying girl / "Weasel" (the child).**
She has no existing node. Could propose one, but she disappears from the text after being left behind (she presumably escapes capture — Lommy says "She ran off when she heard you coming"). No edge payload to propose without more text grounding. Drop.

**5. Koss, Dobber, Cutjack, Tarber, Reysen, Kurz, Gerren as holdfast participants.**
These men are named participants in the holdfast defense. Koss (archer), Dobber (killed), Kurz (wounded, later dies), Cutjack, Tarber, Reysen, Woth (first to die) are all named. However, none have existing nodes (none in baseline). Proposing AGENT_IN/VICTIM_IN roles for unnamed / no-node characters without synthesis knowing the node-layer state risks over-proposal. Flag: if any of these men have nodes, add `FIGHTS_IN fight-at-the-holdfast`. Most likely node-prose candidates.

**6. `praed-s-death-and-burial` causal wiring.**
Praed dies of sickness on the road, not from a combat action. It is causally isolated from the flight→capture spine. The existing node is sufficient. No causal wire needed.

**7. `tickler-village-interrogations` as a new event node.**
Considered. The Tickler's systematic torture is an extended recurring event, not a single incident. Arya witnesses it at the storehouse and on the march. Could propose a hub with arya WITNESS_IN. However, WITNESS_IN requires seeing the act, which she does: "The questioning was done in full view of the other captives." The text supports it. However, the victims (the village captives) have no nodes — they are anonymous smallfolk. The event would have `tickler AGENT_IN`, `gregor COMMANDS_IN`, `arya WITNESS_IN`, but no VICTIM_IN. Thin hub without victim nodes. Recommend node-prose for the Tickler's node rather than a new event hub. Drop the node; note as harvest.

**8. `incident-at-the-trident MOTIVATES arya-stark`.**
Baseline notes this dead-end (gap 9). The trident incident (Mycah's death) is AGOT-pre. The text of acok-arya chapters shows Arya recalling Mycah and Lady explicitly in her kill list: "the Hound for killing the butcher's boy Mycah" (acok-arya-06:57). This DOES support a MOTIVATES edge. However, MOTIVATES target must be a character — `incident-at-the-trident MOTIVATES arya-stark` is valid. But the Lens instructions say: "keep tight; only if text-direct" for AGOT-pre material. The hate is text-direct. **Propose this edge cautiously:**
