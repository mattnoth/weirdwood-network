# The Blackfish escapes Riverrun

Rather than yield or take the black, [Ser Brynden Tully](brynden-tully) — the Blackfish — slips out
of Riverrun the night the castle changes hands. As [Edmure](edmure-tully) later confesses to
[Jaime](jaime-lannister), the garrison raised the portcullis on the Water Gate three feet underwater,
leaving a submerged gap while the gate still appeared closed; the Blackfish, a strong swimmer, pulled
himself beneath the spikes after dark and floated downriver beneath the boom — *"a black fish in a
black river floating quietly downstream."* In the confusion of the surrender it is the next morning
before Jaime learns Brynden is not among the prisoners. [Ser Addam Marbrand](addam-marbrand) leads the
search that never finds him; Jaime suspects the riverlords would sooner shelter him than catch him.
