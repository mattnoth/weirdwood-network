## Reek identity-tangle — RECOMMENDATION

**Do NOT add a `SAME_AS` edge between `reek`, `theon-greyjoy`, and `ramsay-snow`.** SAME_AS is transitive: `reek SAME_AS theon-greyjoy` + `reek SAME_AS ramsay-snow` would collapse all three into a single identity, which is factually wrong and would corrupt cross-identity queries. The name "Reek" passes across three distinct bearers in different time-periods; each is modeled separately.

**Recommended modeling for the two active gaps:**

1. **Ramsay-as-Reek (ACOK).** Model as `ramsay-snow IMPERSONATES reek` (Tier-1, clean, time-bounded to ACOK ch67 reveal). This is a first-use of IMPERSONATES in the graph. The direction follows the established convention: X IMPERSONATES Y = X (real actor) pretends to be Y (assumed identity). The edge is directly analogous to `mance-rayder IMPERSONATES lord-of-bones` (S145 glamour-swap). Evidence from text: Ramsay himself describes dressing in the dead servant's clothes and smearing himself with filth; he gives his name as "Heke" (a corruption of "Reek") during capture. The reveal comes when he lifts his helm and says "The wretch is dead" (acok-theon-06:247), confirming the servant died in his clothes.

2. **Theon-as-Reek (ADWD).** The forcible renaming is NOT a clean impersonation (Theon is not deceiving a third party by pretending to be the dead servant — he has simply had the name beaten into him as an identity destruction). The correct modeling is: **add alias "Reek" and "Reek (III)" to `theon-greyjoy`'s frontmatter aliases list** + a disambiguation note in the node body. This preserves discoverability: a query for "Reek" via the resolver would return `reek` (the original servant, Reek I) AND `theon-greyjoy` (carrying "Reek (III)"), prompting the user to disambiguate by time/context. This is the right tradeoff — it makes the S149 key use-case (ADWD "Reek" → Theon) discoverable without asserting ontological identity-equality. Adding the alias requires `weirwood-refresh` after the alias-add is committed. Note also that `ramsay-snow` carries no "Reek" alias — Ramsay's use of the name was a bounded impersonation, modeled via the edge above, not via an alias on his node. This keeps the resolver clean: "Reek" → {original servant, Theon-in-ADWD}; Ramsay's Reek role surfaces only through the IMPERSONATES edge.
