## JD1: `jon-allows-free-folk-through-the-wall` —MOTIVATES→ `bowen-marsh`

**Claim:** Bowen Marsh's grievance over Jon's wildling policy is a driver behind his role in Jon's assassination.
**Cited quote:** adwd-jon-13:153 — "We should be fitting them for nooses, not giving them our castles."

### Verification

Line 153 is confirmed verbatim in adwd-jon-13.md. The quote is Marsh's direct response when Jon asks him which wildling chiefs might garrison abandoned castles. The immediate next chapter event (adwd-jon-13:322–325) is the stabbing itself, with Marsh explicitly naming his motive: "For the Watch." The text makes the causal chain explicit — Marsh's deepening opposition to the wildling policy is narrated as systemic and bone-deep (adwd-jon-13:149: "their disapproval went bone deep"), not episodic.

MOTIVATES is the right type here. The existing OPPOSES edge captures the policy stance; MOTIVATES adds the dimension that this standing grievance is what drives Marsh to act in the stabbing scene. The distinction is real and book-grounded. The text does not introduce any competing motive for Marsh — his motive is entirely the wildling policy.

No theory leakage. The stabbing and Marsh's participation are confirmed canon text; no pink-letter-authorship or resurrection framing is needed.

**Verdict: CONFIRM**
- Tier-1 is justified. The evidence quote is strong, the causal chain to the assassination is explicit and in-chapter.
- Citation adwd-jon-13:153 is accurate.

## JD2: `jon-allows-free-folk-through-the-wall` —MOTIVATES→ `othell-yarwyck`

**Claim:** Yarwyck's objection to settling the wildlings is the same grievance driving the co-conspirator.
**Cited quote:** adwd-jon-11:267 — "Lord Commander, there are thousands of—"

### Verification

Line 267 in adwd-jon-11.md is confirmed verbatim, but the quote is immediately cut off by Jon finishing the sentence for him: "—scrawny wildlings, bone weary, hungry, far from home." Yarwyck never completes the thought. As cited, this is an incomplete sentence — it establishes Yarwyck's intent to object, but the quote itself carries no substantive content.

However, the claim about Yarwyck's opposition is well-grounded in surrounding text:

- adwd-jon-11:299 — "I would sooner have them dead in the ground." (Direct, unambiguous)
- adwd-jon-11:293 — "All the Weeper knows is rape and murder." (Opposition to the Weeper's passage)
- adwd-jon-11:341 — "The Weeper's word is worthless."
- adwd-jon-13:143 — "More trouble than they're worth, that lot. Sloppy, careless, lazy … Can't be done, my lord." (In-chapter to the stabbing)
- adwd-jon-13:155 — "Bad and worse and worst makes a beggar's choice. My lord had as well present us with a pack of wolves and ask which we'd like to tear our throats out." (In adwd-jon-13 itself, on the wildling garrison question — paired directly with Marsh's nooses line)
- adwd-jon-13:163 — "If the wildlings at Hardhome need saving, let the wildlings here go save them."
- adwd-jon-13:299 — Yarwyck is seen slipping out of the Shieldhall with Marsh immediately after the Shieldhall speech (the same scene that triggers the assassination).

The opposition is genuine and extensive. The problem is solely the cited quote: adwd-jon-11:267 is too thin as the primary evidence anchor for a Tier-1 edge — a truncated sentence does not "clearly support" the causal MOTIVATES claim at the same confidence level as Marsh's quote.

**The fix:** Re-cite to a stronger line that actually carries Yarwyck's opposition to the free folk policy. The best candidates from the same chapter (adwd-jon-13) are:

- adwd-jon-13:155 — "Bad and worse and worst makes a beggar's choice. My lord had as well present us with a pack of wolves and ask which we'd like to tear our throats out." (Direct, emphatic, thematically matches; same scene as Marsh's nooses line; same chapter as the assassination)
- adwd-jon-13:163 — "If the wildlings at Hardhome need saving, let the wildlings here go save them." (Same chapter, clear opposition)

Recommend adwd-jon-13:155 as the new anchor quote — it is structurally paired with Marsh's nooses line (both spoken in the same meeting, one after the other), is emphatic, and is from adwd-jon-13, the assassination chapter, making the thematic proximity to the killing tighter.

With re-cite to adwd-jon-13:155, Tier-1 is defensible. The MOTIVATES type is appropriate for the same reason as JD1 — Yarwyck's slipping out at adwd-jon-13:299 (just before the stabbing) shows him choosing not to stop it, which is the behavioral manifestation of this motive.

**Verdict: ADJUST**
- New citation: adwd-jon-13:155 — "Bad and worse and worst makes a beggar's choice. My lord had as well present us with a pack of wolves and ask which we'd like to tear our throats out."
- Tier-1 is appropriate with the stronger quote. The old citation (adwd-jon-11:267) is a truncated sentence and too thin for Tier-1; the underlying claim is well-supported, just needs a better anchor.
- Edge type MOTIVATES: keep. OPPOSES (existing) handles the policy stance; MOTIVATES adds the causal "drove him toward the killing" dimension.

adwd-jon-13:295 is unambiguously a better trigger-grounding. It quotes the specific act (the call to arms against Winterfell, breaking the Watch's political neutrality) that precipitates the stabbing rather than the conspirators' response to it.

**Verdict: BETTER — the new citation is correct and represents a meaningful improvement.**
