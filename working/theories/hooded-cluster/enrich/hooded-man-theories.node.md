## Claim

Amid a string of unsolved killings inside Bolton-occupied Winterfell, Theon Greyjoy
encounters a hooded, cloaked stranger alone at night, who names him "Theon Turncloak.
Theon Kinslayer" before laughing at his tortured hands and leaving him unharmed. The
theory holds that this figure is one specific, identifiable person working alongside
the wildling spearwife Rowan and Crowfood Umber in a covert anti-Bolton effort --
candidates include Robett Glover, Harwin, and Hallis Mollen -- though it also weighs
the possibility that he is not a literal person at all, but a hallucinated ghost of
Theon's former self, or a manifestation of the Stranger, the Faith's hooded god of
death. A structurally related sub-theory addresses who is actually committing the
Winterfell murders themselves, since ruling the Hooded Man out as the killer is the
turn that opens up the deeper identity question.

## Evidence For

### The core encounter (premise)

The entire theory rests on a single scene:

> Farther on, he came upon a man striding in the opposite direction, a hooded cloak
> flapping behind him. When they found themselves face-to-face their eyes met
> briefly. The man put a hand on his dagger. "Theon Turncloak. Theon Kinslayer."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-a-ghost-in-winterfell-01.md:127 (edge H1, node theon-greyjoy)

The man's hand goes to his dagger as if to attack, but when Theon shows him his
disfigured, tortured hands, he only laughs and leaves Theon to his suffering. In the
same scene Theon himself wonders whether this is the castle's killer:

> "The gods are not done with me," Theon answered, wondering if this could be the
> killer, the night walker who had stuffed Yellow Dick's cock into his mouth and
> pushed Roger Ryswell's groom off the battlements. Oddly, he was not afraid.
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-a-ghost-in-winterfell-01.md:133 (edge H10, node theon-greyjoy)

### The Rowan / Crowfood connection

Only three characters in the whole series call Theon "kinslayer": the Hooded Man,
the wildling Rowan, and Crowfood Umber (Mors Umber), a northman serving Stannis. The
theory reads the shared vocabulary as evidence the three are connected, not proof
that any one of them literally is the Hooded Man:

> "Not us." Rowan grabbed him by the throat and shoved him back against the barracks
> wall, her face an inch from his. "Say it again and I will rip your lying tongue
> out, kinslayer."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-theon-01.md:119 (edge H2, node rowan)

Rowan is also, unusually for a wildling, respectful of Eddard Stark:

> Rowan gave him a hard look. "You have no right to mouth Lord Eddard's words. Not
> you. Not ever. After what you did—"
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-theon-01.md:107 (edge H3, node rowan, tier-4)

Combined with the established detail that Crowfood Umber's own daughter was taken
by wildlings three decades earlier, the theory speculatively identifies Rowan as
that daughter, raised among Stark loyalists:

> His sons died on the Trident, his wife in childbed. His only daughter was carried
> off by wildlings thirty years ago.
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-jon-04.md:111 (edge H4, node mors-umber, tier-4)

**This chain is stacked inference** (age, timing, and identity are all assumed, not
stated) and is tier-4 accordingly -- no passage states or confirms that Rowan is
Crowfood's daughter.

### Candidate: Robett Glover

Robett Glover, brother of Galbart Glover, lost his family and home to the Greyjoy
invasion and was later betrayed into a Bolton trap at Duskendale. By ADWD he is
genuinely inside Wyman Manderly's secret anti-Bolton circle:

> Robett Glover broke in to add, "Your loyalty does you honor, my lord, but Stannis
> Baratheon remains your king, not our own."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-davos-04.md:129 (edge H5, node robett-glover)

This places Robett within the conspiracy's reach, the precondition for him
plausibly having traveled north with Manderly's people and become the Hooded Man.

### Candidate: Harwin

Harwin, one of Eddard Stark's household guards who helped teach Robb, Jon, and Arya
to ride, later joined the group that became the Brotherhood Without Banners and,
by ADWD, rides with Lady Stoneheart's band -- which is actively searching for Arya
and would plausibly have learned that "Arya" has married Ramsay Bolton. **No
passage placing Harwin specifically at or near Winterfell in this window could be
independently grounded in this pass** -- see Ungrounded material. The candidate is
carried here as premise/backstory only; it earns no edge.

### Candidate: Hallis Mollen ("Hal")

Hal Mollen, captain of the Winterfell guard, was given a real, confirmed mission:

> "Hal Mollen will escort them back to Winterfell, it is his place as captain of
> guards."
— sources/chapters/acok/acok-catelyn-05.md:329 (edge H6, node hallis-mollen, tier-4)

Catelyn assigns Hal to carry Eddard Stark's bones home for burial. Whether he ever
completed the journey through a warzone and reached occupied Winterfell is never
confirmed on the page -- the theory extends a real assignment into an unconfirmed
arrival.

### The symbolic / ghost reading, and the literal-kinslayer reading

Theon's arc in this book is framed as the death of "Theon Greyjoy" and the birth of
"Reek," and Winterfell is repeatedly described as haunted for him. The theory reads
the Hooded Man as one of two possible non-literal figures: a projection of Theon's
former, prouder self (who himself wore a hooded cloak in ACOK), or an emblem of the
Stranger, the Faith's hooded death-god, whom Theon defies by choosing to live. The
"Kinslayer" charge is also read, separately, as possibly literal: in ACOK, Reek
killed and disfigured two boys of an age with Bran and Rickon and passed them off as
the Stark boys' bodies:

> The miller's boys had been of an age with Bran and Rickon, alike in size and
> coloring, and once Reek had flayed the skin from their faces and dipped their
> heads in tar, it was easy to see familiar features in those misshapen lumps of
> rotting flesh.
— sources/chapters/acok/acok-theon-05.md:155 (edge H9, node theon-greyjoy)

Whether those boys were literally Theon's own sons by the miller's wife (making
"Kinslayer" true in a blood sense, not just a foster-kin sense) is not stated
anywhere in the corpus. The theory's thematic anchor for "the gods punish even
unknowing kinslayers" comes from an unrelated in-world parable Ygritte tells Jon:

> "Aye," she said, "but the gods hate kinslayers, even when they kill unknowing."
— sources/chapters/acok/acok-jon-06.md:211 (edge H8, node ygritte, tier-4)

### The Winterfell murderer identity (event-layer premise)

The live graph already carries `the-winterfell-murders` as a tier-1 event node
(S149): the killings of Yellow Dick, a Ryswell groom, a Frey squire, and a Flint
soldier are the confirmed, non-theoretical work of Mance Rayder ("Abel")'s
infiltrating spearwives, with Rowan and Mance both carrying SUSPECTED_OF edges to
that node (tier-2). This theory's second header investigates the same murders but
adds nothing the event layer doesn't already establish for those four deaths --
Theon voices the near-confession himself:

> Do me, the way you did the others. Yellow Dick and the rest. It was you.
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-a-ghost-in-winterfell-01.md (quoted verbatim from the
live `the-winterfell-murders` node's own verified edge; edge H11, CONTRADICTS the
narrow sub-claim that the Hooded Man himself is the killer)

Ruling the spearwives in (and the Hooded Man out) as the killers is the turn the
video uses to pivot from "whodunit" to "who/what is the Hooded Man." **One
Winterfell-murders identity claim is genuinely not yet covered by the event node**:
Little Walder Frey's death, which the spearwives explicitly disclaim and which the
event node leaves "a genuine open mystery." The theory attributes it instead to his
ambitious young cousin, Big Walder:

> "Under that ruined keep, my lord," replied Big Walder. "The one with the old
> gargoyles." The boy's gloves were caked with his cousin's blood. "I told him not
> to go out alone, but he said he had to find a man who owed him silver."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-theon-01.md:55 (edge H12, node walder-frey-son-of-jammos)

Theon separately notes the killing's political payoff was suspiciously well-timed:
Little Walder's death is what drove the Freys and Manderlys to blows, which Big
Walder's own ambition for the Frey succession would plausibly benefit from.

## Evidence Against

Hal Mollen's own established character undercuts him as a plausible silent,
long-running covert operative:

> "Hal has the truth of it," she answered. And a loose tongue as well, it would
> seem.
— sources/chapters/acok/acok-catelyn-03.md:299 (edge H7, node hallis-mollen)

**Robett Glover counter-evidence (not edged, premise only).** The source video
raises that Robett Glover, by ADWD, is positioned to know Bran and Rickon are
alive (per the wider Manderly/Stannis conspiracy he sits inside), which would make
it strange for him to genuinely believe -- and use as a personal insult -- the
"Kinslayer" charge against Theon. No single passage states this knowledge outright;
Robett's presence at Manderly's meeting with Davos (H5) only establishes he is in
the circle, not what he specifically knows about the Stark boys, so this
counter-argument is not grounded as its own edge.

**Harwin recognition problem (not edged, premise only).** Harwin and Theon knew
each other for years growing up at Winterfell together, and the Hooded Man's hood
does not hide his face -- the two come face-to-face in the encounter (H1) -- raising
the question of why Theon wouldn't simply recognize him. The video's own rebuttal
(Theon fails to recognize even his sister Asha elsewhere in the text, and Arya
struggles to recognize a thinner, bearded Harwin) is a real structural parallel but
could not be independently grounded to a specific Harwin passage in this pass --
see Ungrounded material.

## Status Notes

**Show:** Game of Thrones never depicted the Hooded Man, Theon's encounter with him,
or the Winterfell-murders whodunit on screen (the show diverges from Winterfell's
occupation storyline well before this point in the books). No show confirmation
exists either way -- `status: open`.

**ASX verdict:** the source video (36Ntv75CfqE) reaches no single confirmed answer.
It weighs Robett Glover, Harwin, and Hallis Mollen as the strongest named candidates
(with Harwin argued to carry the most emotional weight for a reveal), treats the
ghost/hallucination and Stranger readings as live alternatives with their own
problems, and explicitly allows that the mystery may be intentionally unresolved
("maybe it doesn't matter who's under the hood"). For the separate Winterfell
murderer question, the video is confident: the wildling spearwives account for
Yellow Dick, the Ryswell groom, and the Frey squire, and Big Walder is the likely
culprit for Little Walder, with the Flint soldier's death treated as probably
unrelated. That second verdict matches what the live graph's tier-1
`the-winterfell-murders` event node already independently establishes (S149,
pre-dating this theories pass) for everything except the Little Walder /
Big Walder claim, which this node adds (edge H12).
