## Claim

The theory holds that Euron Greyjoy is not merely a ruthless conqueror but the herald of a
third, Lovecraftian supernatural force beyond the ice-and-fire dualism of the Others and the
dragons. The theory chains together six sub-claims: (1) Euron secretly hired a Faceless Man
to murder Balon Greyjoy and clear his path to the Seastone Chair, paying with a dragon egg
rather than gold; (2) as a boy Euron was visited in a dream by Bloodraven (the three-eyed
crow), who opened his "third eye" to magic but ultimately rejected him as a student — a
parallel to Bran Stark's own awakening; (3) Euron secretly possesses greenseer-derived
powers such as skinchanging and dream-walking; (4) Euron is orchestrating a mass blood-magic
sacrifice — depicted in the unpublished *Winds of Winter* sample chapter "The Forsaken" — to
transform himself into a monstrous, godlike being; (5) Euron intends to seize the true Horn
of Winter, secretly in Sam Tarly's keeping at Oldtown, to bring down the Wall and loose the
White Walkers in a new Long Night; and (6) Euron is a cyclical repeat of the ancient
Bloodstone Emperor, the legendary tyrant whose blood magic and blasphemy caused the original
Long Night. The theory treats these as one cumulative arc rather than independent claims, and
the sub-claims vary sharply in how well they are supported by the published books: the
Oldtown/Horn-of-Winter mechanism and Euron's self-mythologized godhood rest on direct textual
beats, while the Bloodraven apprenticeship, the skinchanging power, and the literal monstrous
transformation rest on the unpublished "Forsaken" chapter and on stacked close-reading
symbolism.

## Evidence For

**Euron's self-declared godhood.** Euron openly styles himself divine and untouchable by any
moral order, boasting to Aeron that he has out-prayed every god in the world:

> "Godless? Why, Aeron, I am the godliest man ever to raise sail! You serve one god, Damphair,
> but I have served ten thousand. From Ib to Asshai, when men see my sails, they pray."
— sources/chapters/affc/affc-the-iron-captain-01.md:145 (edge E1, node euron-greyjoy)

This self-mythologizing is not idle boasting in the text: the video's kinslaying sub-claim
(Euron murdered Balon, plus Harlon and Robin Greyjoy as children) is only partly grounded —
Aeron's funeral rite for Balon opens the same chapter as the kingsmoot ("Balon has fallen,
Balon my brother, who honored the Old Way and paid the iron price," affc-the-drowned-man-01.md:41)
but names only Balon; the text does not confirm on-page that Euron killed Harlon or Robin. Euron
also wins the crown itself through open manipulation rather than force: "Euron had seduced them
with his glib tongue and smiling eye and bound them to his cause with the plunder of half a
hundred distant lands" (affc-the-reaver-01.md:57) — Victarion's own bitter assessment, watching
from the sidelines as Euron claims a victory he did not fight for.

**Dragonbinder — a magic horn from the Valyrian ruins.** At the kingsmoot Euron unveils a horn
he claims can command dragons, presented as recovered from the one place in the world reputed
un-survivable:

> "That horn you heard I found amongst the smoking ruins that were Valyria, where no man has
> dared to walk but me. ... It is a dragon horn, bound with bands of red gold and Valyrian
> steel graven with enchantments. The dragonlords of old sounded such horns, before the Doom
> devoured them. With this horn, ironmen, I can bind dragons to my will."
— sources/chapters/affc/affc-the-drowned-man-01.md:185 (edge E2, node dragonbinder)

Euron's crew includes three enslaved warlocks aboard the *Silence* — "Queer men and terrible,
they were, but the Crow's Eye had made them slaves" (affc-the-reaver-01.md:71) — Victarion's
own report of Euron's magical retinue, offered as the reason Euron seemed to foreknow the
outcome of the battle.

**The ship *Silence* itself.** Euron's flagship is built and crewed to be uncanny — mute crew,
black sails, and a mouthless figurehead:

> A windblown mane of black iron hair streamed from her head, and her eyes were mother-of-pearl,
> but she had no mouth.
— sources/chapters/affc/affc-the-iron-captain-01.md:37 (edge E3, node silence)

The theory reads the ship's design (and its name) as thematically pointed — an embodiment of
imposed silence that the video reads against the series' own title, *A Song of Ice and Fire*
(see Ungrounded material below for the un-groundable half of that argument).

**Moqorro's vision — the single strongest published-book "eldritch" beat.** The red priest
Moqorro, scrying in Volantis, sees Euron transfigured into something inhuman:

> "Only their shadows," Moqorro said. "One most of all. A tall and twisted thing with one
> black eye and ten long arms, sailing on a sea of blood."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-tyrion-08.md:285 (edge E4, node moqorro)

This is the theory's strongest textual anchor for the literal-monster reading: it is a
published-book prophetic vision (not drawn from the unreleased "Forsaken" chapter) that
independently corroborates the kraken/tentacle imagery the "Forsaken" excerpts develop at much
greater length.

**A second prophetic vision reads Oldtown under threat.** Melisandre, scrying separately, sees
towers by the sea overtaken by a dark tide:

> "I saw towers by the sea, submerged beneath a black and bloody tide. That is where the
> heaviest blow will fall."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-melisandre-01.md:183 (edge E5, node melisandre)

The theory reads "towers by the sea" as the Hightower at Oldtown specifically — a reading the
line itself does not confirm; Melisandre never names the location.

**Tentacle/puppet imagery around Victarion.** In a nightfire vision, a priest (Moqorro,
serving as Victarion's chained "advisor" east of the Iron Islands) tells Victarion he is being
controlled by unseen forces:

> "I have seen you in the nightfires, Victarion Greyjoy. You come striding through the flames
> stern and fierce, your great axe dripping blood, blind to the tentacles that grasp you at
> wrist and neck and ankle, the black strings that make you dance."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-iron-suitor-01.md:163 (prose only — S216 fresh-verify
DROPPED the proposed edge: Victarion repudiates this vision moments later in the same
chapter, so it cannot carry a SUPPORTS; the passage stays with his rejection quoted below)

The theory reads the tentacle imagery as a deliberate kraken/eldritch echo layered onto the
more literal reading (Victarion as Euron's unwitting puppet, sent to fetch Daenerys for Euron's
own ends) — Victarion himself rejects the framing outright: "Your nightfires lie. I was not
made for dancing, and I am no man's puppet" (adwd-the-iron-suitor-01.md:165).

**Rodrik Harlaw's "history is a wheel."** Rodrik the Reader, briefing his niece on the Crow's
Eye, frames Euron explicitly as history repeating:

> "history is a wheel, for the nature of man is fundamentally unchanging. What has happened
> before will perforce happen again, he said. I think of that whenever I contemplate the
> Crow's Eye. Euron Greyjoy sounds queerly like Urron Greyiron to these old ears."
— sources/chapters/affc/affc-the-krakens-daughter-01.md:127 (edge E7, node rodrik-harlaw)

The line names a specific historical Greyjoy parallel (Urron Greyiron), not the Bloodstone
Emperor — the theory's leap from "Euron repeats ironborn history" to "Euron repeats the
Bloodstone Emperor's apocalypse specifically" is an extension the text itself does not make.

**The Horn of Winter — a mystery the text keeps alive.** Mance Rayder's horn, claimed to be
Joramun's own Horn of Winter, turns out to be a fake:

> "But we never found the true horn, not for all our digging."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-jon-12.md:177 (edge E8, node horn-of-winter)

This leaves the *true* Horn of Winter legend unresolved and, per the theory, still in play.
The text separately keeps a candidate horn in circulation: Jon gives Sam a cracked, unglamorous
horn as a keepsake ("The warhorn he had given to Sam. On closer examination the horn had
proved cracked ... Jon had been unable to get any sound from it," acok-jon-05.md:101) —
narration that repeatedly reminds readers Sam still carries it as he travels on to Oldtown,
which the theory reads as a deliberate long-game Chekhov's gun rather than incidental detail.

**Euron's raids are already turned toward Oldtown.** By late AFFC, ironborn ships under Euron
are pushing toward the Hightower's city directly:

> And now there were reports that this madman who called himself Euron Crow's Eye was even
> sending longships up Whispering Sound toward Oldtown.
— sources/chapters/affc/affc-cersei-08.md:17 (edge E9, node oldtown)

**Leyton Hightower's own household is preparing for something from "the deeps."** A minor
character reports on Oldtown's defenses and, almost as an aside, raises the possibility of
literal supernatural reinforcement:

> "Lord Leyton's locked atop his tower with the Mad Maid, consulting books of spells. Might be
> he'll raise an army from the deeps. Or not."
— sources/chapters/affc/affc-samwell-05.md:47 (prose only — S216 adjudication DROPPED
the proposed edge: the speaker's own "Or not" marks this as half-joking rumor, premise
color rather than evidence)

The line is explicitly hedged in its own context ("Or not") — offered by the speaker as a
half-joking rumor, not a confirmed plan, which the edge note reflects.

**Daenerys's amethyst eyes.** Euron, laying out his plan to marry Daenerys to Victarion,
specifically flags her eye color:

> "Her hair is silver-gold, and her eyes are amethysts . . . but you need not take my word for
> it, brother."
— sources/chapters/affc/affc-the-reaver-01.md:273 (prose only — S216 fresh-verify
DROPPED the proposed tier-5 edge: a single shared word tying a POV-book line to
TWOIAF-fenced material the cluster itself excludes is word-association, not evidence)

The theory reads this as a deliberate echo of the Bloodstone Emperor's usurped sister, the
"Amethyst Empress" of Great Empire of the Dawn legend (see Ungrounded material — that legend
itself is TWOIAF material, outside the 5-book POV corpus) — a single shared word carrying the
entire weight of the parallel. This is the weakest edge in the set: word-association symbolism
stacked on top of an already-speculative historical parallel.

## Evidence Against

This theory is structurally different from a contested theory like R+L=J: the source video is
almost entirely single-direction theory-building, and the extraction itself flags that
explicit counter-evidence or objections appear in only two beats, neither of which grounds to
an independent character's textual skepticism.

**Euron's own account of the dragon egg is the video's only in-text doubt, and it is Euron's
own unreliable telling.** Asked what became of a dragon egg he once possessed, Euron says only:

> "I threw it in the sea during one of my dark moods."
— sources/chapters/affc/affc-the-reaver-01.md:251 (node euron-greyjoy)

The video treats this claim skeptically (dragon eggs are exceedingly rare and valuable) and
floats the alternative that Euron paid it to the Faceless Men as murder-payment instead — but
this is the video's own inference about Euron's own unverifiable statement, not an
independent character's textual pushback, and it bears on the Balon-murder sub-claim rather
than the eldritch-apocalypse core. Kept as prose only, not an edge.

**The video's own hedge on the "Forsaken" chapter's outcome.** The extraction records the video
explicitly flagging that "it's not clear what exactly will happen" as a result of Euron's
blood ritual — treating the Drowned-God-awakening and egg-hatches-into-a-sea-dragon endings as
open speculation, not confirmed outcomes. This beat is sourced entirely from the unpublished
"Forsaken" sample chapter (source_domain: unknown) and could not be grounded to any passage in
the 5-book corpus; it is not usable as an edge under the corpus-only quote rule, so it stays as
a prose note rather than a CONTRADICTS edge.

No edge in this node is typed CONTRADICTS; the theory's own evidentiary shape (heavily
one-directional, per the video's own construction) did not surface a genuinely independent,
corpus-groundable textual objection distinct from Euron's own claims.

## Ungrounded material (outside the corpus)

- **TWOW** — The single densest evidentiary source for this theory is the unpublished *Winds
  of Winter* sample chapter "The Forsaken," read publicly by GRRM at a convention: Euron
  imprisoning and torturing Aeron with shade of the evening; declaring "These are the last
  days, when the world shall be broken and remade. A new god shall be born from the graves and
  charnel pits" and "I am your god"; binding priests of multiple faiths and a woman named Falia
  to his ships' prows for a mass blood sacrifice meant to summon krakens and transform him into
  "a mass of writhing tentacles." None of this material exists in `sources/chapters/` (the
  5-book corpus this cluster is grounded against) and none of it could be quote-verified;
  it is the theory's most vivid evidence but entirely outside the corpus this node is built on.
- **community** — That Euron paid the Faceless Men with the dragon egg (rather than simply
  discarding it) is fan inference layered onto his own unverified in-text claim; not
  independently confirmed.
- **show** — Fan speculation (credited to the show's Night King stealing Viserion) that Euron
  might steal a dragon himself is a show-derived cross-reference, not a book beat.
- **community** — The Bloodraven-dream-visitation sub-claim (Euron as one of the "thousand
  other dreamers" Bloodraven auditioned and rejected, per Bran's vision of "the bones of a
  thousand other dreamers") is pure community inference: the parallel between Euron's claimed
  boyhood flying dream and Bran's tower-fall/three-eyed-crow dream is textually real (both are
  grounded beats — Euron's "how will we ever know unless we leap from some tall tower?" boast
  at affc-the-reaver-01.md:231, and Bran's dream sequence), but the leap from "structural
  parallel" to "Bloodraven personally visited Euron" has no textual confirmation anywhere in
  the 5 books.
- **community** — Euron's skinchanging and dream-walking powers (entering Aeron's and possibly
  Daenerys's dreams; controlling his mute crew or the "dusky woman" companion given to
  Victarion) are argued entirely from narrative pattern-matching (his lips are drug-stained
  blue like the man in Daenerys's unsettling dream; his crew obeys without speech) rather than
  any passage that states he skinchanges. Skinchanging a human is textually established as a
  taboo, abominable act among wargs generally, which the theory notes is "consistent with"
  something Euron might attempt — but consistency with his character is not evidence he has
  done it.
- **book-interpretive (TWOIAF, not book-POV text)** — The entire Bloodstone Emperor parallel
  (murdering his sister the Amethyst Empress to take her throne; practicing dark arts, torture,
  slavery, and cannibalism; "casting down the true gods"; worshipping a black stone; marrying a
  "tiger-woman") is drawn from *The World of Ice and Fire*, a secondary in-universe historical
  text outside the 5-book POV corpus this cluster is grounded against. It is real published
  material (unlike the "Forsaken" chapter) but sits outside this node's quote-verification
  standard, so none of it is an edge; only the Amethyst/amethyst-eyes wordplay (edge E11) ties
  back to a groundable POV-book line, and even that edge is flagged tier-5.
  Additional black-stone parallels the theory draws — the Hightower's foundation, the
  ironborn's Seastone Chair, Asshai — extend the TWOIAF material further and are likewise
  ungrounded to POV-book text.
- **community** — The Lovecraftian "Deep Ones"/fish-people origin of the Hightower's black-stone
  foundation, and the idea that the Drowned God itself is a sleeping eldritch entity Euron's
  ritual will awaken, are community readings with no cited textual source at all (the extraction
  marks both not-groundable).
- **book-interpretive** — The "silence vs. *A Song of Ice and Fire*" and "A Feast for Crows
  literally about opportunists feasting" thematic/title arguments are literary readings of the
  book's structure and title rather than in-world textual claims; they are not falsifiable
  against plot evidence and are not edges.
- **GRRM-interview / authorial-influence (adjacent, not this theory)** — The seed document for
  this cluster (`working/theories-staging/eldritch-apocalypse-seed.md`) separately documents
  GRRM's well-attested real-world Lovecraft fandom as an *authorial/thematic* fact about the
  series, distinct from this in-world fan theory. That authorial-influence material is not
  reproduced here — it is a craft note about GRRM, not a claim about what Euron will do — and
  is deliberately not double-minted into this node.

## Status Notes

**Show outcome:** none. Game of Thrones' final seasons gave Euron a much-reduced, non-magical
role (no Bloodraven connection, no Forsaken-chapter ritual, no Horn of Winter plot); the show
does not confirm or joss any part of this theory, so `status` stays `open` rather than
`show-confirmed` or `jossed`.

**ASX verdict:** the video presents this largely as a speculative synthesis of fan theories
(crediting theorist Poor Quentyn by name) built on textual hints, repeatedly flagging
individual pieces as "theory," "speculation," or "maybe" — while treating Euron's overall
villain characterization and self-mythologized godhood as strongly text-supported. The video's
own framing is that the published-book evidence is real but partial, and the strongest,
most vivid confirmation sits in unpublished material (the "Forsaken" chapter) that this corpus
cannot verify.
