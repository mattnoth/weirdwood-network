## Claim

At the wedding tourney at Whitewalls (212 AC, *The Mystery Knight*), a hedge knight
calling himself Ser Maynard Plumm rides with Dunk and Egg, watches the Second
Blackfyre Rebellion's plot unfold, and vanishes into the night the moment Lord
Brynden Rivers's army arrives to crush it. The theory holds that Maynard Plumm is not
a separate, minor hedge knight at all, but **Brynden Rivers himself in disguise** —
observing the conspiracy firsthand under an assumed identity before revealing his
hand as Hand of the King.

## Evidence For

**Plumm's own evasiveness about his identity.** When Egg asks whether he is kin to
the (real, established) Lord Viserys Plumm, Maynard's answer is oddly hedged rather
than a plain yes or no:

> "Distantly," confessed Ser Maynard, a tall, thin, stoop-shouldered man with long
> straight flaxen hair, "though I doubt that His Lordship would admit to it.
— sources/chapters/tmk/tmk-dunk-01.md:315 (edge P1, node maynard-plumm)

The theory reads this vagueness about his own kinship and station — never quite
confirmed, never quite denied — as consistent with a man who isn't really Maynard
Plumm at all.

**The "single pale white eye."** The theory's strongest textual hook is a specific
startle-beat, distinct from Plumm's general wiki-sourced physical description (which
separately notes his moonstone brooch "resembled a pale white eye in certain
lighting"): in the dark and rain, immediately after Dunk fights off an attacker,
Plumm seems for a moment to *be* the eye itself —

> Dunk whirled. Through the rain, all he could make out was a hooded shape and a
> single pale white eye. It was only when the man came forward that the shadowed
> face beneath the cowl took on the familiar features of Ser Maynard Plumm, the pale
> eye no more than the moonstone brooch that pinned his cloak at the shoulder.
— sources/chapters/tmk/tmk-dunk-01.md:1579 (edge P2, node maynard-plumm)

Brynden Rivers is missing an eye and is known throughout the D&E novellas by the
riddle "how many eyes does Lord Bloodraven have? A thousand eyes, and one" (already
established on `brynden-rivers.node.md`). The theory reads this passage's brief,
unsettling glimpse of "a single pale white eye" — resolved a beat later into an
ordinary brooch — as a deliberate visual echo, possibly a "glamour" (the same kind of
face-changing magic Bloodraven is rumored, elsewhere in the text, to possess).

**Structural timing (premise, not a new quote-edge).** Already established on
`maynard-plumm.node.md`'s own wiki-sourced Origins section: after helping Dunk and
warning him of the plot, "Duncan, Kyle, and Ser Glendon Ball looked for Maynard, but
he had disappeared during the night" — the same night Brynden's army arrives at dawn
to crush the rebellion he had foreknowledge of. The theory reads Plumm's convenient
disappearance at the exact moment Bloodraven's forces appear as more than
coincidence; this timing fact is not re-quoted here as it is already on the
character node, not a new textual beat.

## Evidence Against

No book passage confirms or denies Maynard Plumm's identity; *The Mystery Knight*
leaves the mystery knight of its title (commonly read as referring to Maynard
himself, or to the plot's ambiguities more broadly) unresolved on the page. Ser Kyle
the Cat and Ser Glendon Ball both treat Maynard as an ordinary, if unusually
well-informed, hedge knight throughout the novella, and nothing in his recorded
dialogue is inconsistent with an actual minor knight who simply keeps his ears open
at a tourney full of plotting lords.

## Ungrounded material (outside the corpus)

- **community** — That Plumm "knows too much" and makes pointed references to
  Bloodraven throughout the novella is the video's general characterization; specific
  additional lines beyond the two edges above were not independently grounded to
  citable passages this pass.
- **community** — The broader claim that Bloodraven habitually uses a shapeshifting
  "glamour" to move through the Seven Kingdoms unrecognized rests on the same rumors
  already fenced as premise on `brynden-rivers.node.md` (packs of gray wolves, a
  one-eyed dog, turning into mist) — themselves explicitly framed in-text as unverified
  rumor ("Most of the tales were only tales, Dunk did not doubt"), not confirmed
  magic. The Maynard Plumm identification leans on this unconfirmed premise.
