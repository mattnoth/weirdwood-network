## Claim

The theory holds that the individually-documented northern plots against House
Bolton — Wyman Manderly's Winterfell restoration scheme, a separate succession plot
rooted in Robb Stark's will naming Jon Snow his heir, and possible sympathetic
coordination among the northmen serving inside Stannis Baratheon's own army — are not
three independent, coincidentally-overlapping schemes but facets of one single,
coordinated "Grand Northern Conspiracy" to overthrow the Boltons and restore a Stark
to Winterfell.

**Relationship to the existing event node.** The live graph already carries
[`grand-northern-conspiracy`](../../../../graph/nodes/events/grand-northern-conspiracy.node.md)
as a tier-1 event.conspiracy node — the on-page, confirmed facts of the Manderly-led
plot alone (the fake execution of Davos, the mission to fetch Rickon, the pledge of
Manderly's fleet to Stannis, the Frey pies). That node's scope is deliberately narrow:
one lord's one plot, as GRRM wrote it. This theory node sits one level up: it is the
broader, unproven fan-synthesis claim that the Manderly plot is not alone — that it
interlocks with the Robb's-will thread and the Stannis's-army thread into a single
coordinated whole. Every fact recorded on the event node is treated here as settled
premise for the Manderly leg of the argument; the theory's actual claim is the
*unification* of that leg with the other two.

## Evidence For

### The Manderly-led plot (White Harbor / Winterfell)

Wyman Manderly's own words to Davos are the theory's clearest first-person
articulation of an active, in-progress plot — and the origin of its watchword:

> "My son Wendel came to the Twins a guest. He ate Lord Walder's bread and salt, and
> hung his sword upon the wall to feast with friends. And they murdered him. Murdered,
> I say, and may the Freys choke upon their fables. I drink with Jared, jape with
> Symond, promise Rhaegar the hand of my own beloved granddaughter … but never think
> that means I have forgotten. The north remembers, Lord Davos. The north remembers,
> and the mummer's farce is almost done. My son is home."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-davos-04.md:125 (edge G1, node grand-northern-conspiracy —
S216 adjudication retargeted this edge to the tier-1 EVENT node recording the confirmed
Manderly plot: the event layer as a whole is the theory's anchor evidence, and the edge
doubles as the theory↔event link)

The concrete mechanism of the plot — Wyman's price for bending the knee to Stannis —
names Rickon directly as its centerpiece:

> "Roose Bolton has Lord Eddard's daughter. To thwart him White Harbor must have Ned's
> son … and the direwolf. The wolf will prove the boy is who we say he is, should the
> Dreadfort attempt to deny him. That is my price, Lord Davos. Smuggle me back my
> liege lord, and I will take Stannis Baratheon as my king."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-davos-04.md:211 (prose only — S216 fresh-verify DROPPED
the proposed edge as event-layer material: the Rickon mission is a confirmed fact of
the Manderly thread, owned by the event node, not evidence of cross-thread coordination)

Robett Glover — present at the same meeting, and himself a candidate node for a
sibling-coordination reading with his brother Galbart, discussed below — is the one
who actually relays Wex's testimony that the sack of Winterfell was Ramsay's doing,
not Theon's, the intelligence that makes the whole plot possible in the first place:

> "We may never know all that happened at Winterfell, when Ser Rodrik Cassel tried to
> take the castle back from Theon Greyjoy's ironmen. The Bastard of Bolton claims that
> Greyjoy murdered Ser Rodrik during a parley. Wex says no. Until he learns more
> letters we will never know half the truth … but he came to us knowing yes and no,
> and those can go a long way once you find the right questions."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-davos-04.md:181 (prose only — S216 fresh-verify DROPPED
the proposed edge as event-layer material: Wex's testimony enables the Manderly thread
but does not itself evidence the unification claim)

Two other northern lords, working independently of Wyman, corroborate the same
underlying picture. Roose Bolton himself — the plot's target, not a member of it —
names three of the same suspects when talking to Reek:

> The Cerwyns and the Tallharts are not to be relied on, my fat friend Lord Wyman
> plots betrayal, and Whoresbane … the Umbers may seem simple, but they are not
> without a certain low cunning.
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-reek-03.md:239 (edge G4, node roose-bolton; mid-speech
fragment, Roose Bolton to Reek)

Barbrey Dustin, present at Winterfell and one of the four lords built into the
besieged castle's snowmen in the theory's original video reading (that specific
scene could not be re-verified against the local corpus this session — see Ungrounded
material), gives Theon her own private roster of who is only nominally loyal:

> Old Whoresbane is only here because the Freys hold the Greatjon captive. And do you
> imagine the Hornwood men have forgotten the Bastard's last marriage, and how his
> lady wife was left to starve, chewing her own fingers?
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-turncloak-01.md:165 (edge G5, node barbrey-dustin;
mid-speech fragment, Barbrey Dustin to Theon)

Dustin later uses Wyman's own watchword in front of the Freys themselves:

> "Even Dustins out of Barrowton." Lady Dustin parted her lips in a thin, feral
> smile. "The north remembers, Frey."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-a-ghost-in-winterfell-01.md:203 (edge G6, node
barbrey-dustin, tier-4)

The theory reads the shared phrase as evidence the watchword functions as a coded
signal passed between conspirators rather than a coincidence of two grieving houses
independently reaching for the same natural sentiment — the weaker, more inferential
half of this edge (hence tier-4; the fact of Dustin's hostility to the Freys is solid,
the "coded signal between plotters" reading is not stated in the text).

### The Robb's-will succession thread (Jon Snow)

Separately from Winterfell, Alys Karstark's flight to the Wall shows a living
northern lord turning to Jon specifically because of his Stark blood, independent of
any of the above:

> I did not know where else to turn but to the last son of Eddard Stark.
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-jon-09.md:321 (edge G10, node alys-karstark; closing
line of a longer speech, Alys Karstark to Jon)

Nothing in the text suggests Alys herself knows of Robb's will or any succession
scheme — the theory's leap is from *general northern perception of Jon as Ned's true
heir* (which this passage solidly establishes) to *therefore a live plot to crown
him* (which it does not). Two Riverrun men choosing the Wall over every other
post-siege option after Robb's will was reportedly witnessed there add a second,
weaker data point:

> Two men did not choose to depart with the others. Ser Desmond Grell, Lord Hoster's
> old master-at-arms, preferred to take the black. So did Ser Robin Ryger, Riverrun's
> captain of guards.
— sources/chapters/affc/affc-jaime-07.md:215 (edge G11, node desmond-grell, tier-4;
narration)

The video itself flags the reading that Edmure Tully deliberately sent these two to
make contact with Jon as fan speculation, not textual fact; the edge is tier-4 for
the same reason — the fact of their choice is textual, the motive attributed to it is
not.

### The Stannis's-army thread (mountain clans)

The mountain clansmen marching with Stannis are established as a distinct northern
bloc within his army:

> Stannis had taken Deepwood Motte, and the mountain clans had joined him. Flint,
> Norrey, Wull, Liddle, all.
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-jon-07.md:191 (edge G7, node house-norrey, tier-4;
narration)

Their leaders visit Jon at the Wall in person:

> First to make the ascent were the clan chiefs Flint and Norrey, clad in fur and
> iron. The Norrey looked like some old fox—wrinkled and slight of build, but
> sly-eyed and spry. Torghen Flint was half a head shorter but must weigh twice as
> much—a stout gruff man with gnarled, red-knuckled hands as big as hams, leaning
> heavily on a blackthorn cane as he limped across the ice.
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-jon-11.md:263 (edge G8, node torghen-flint, tier-4;
narration)

In their presence, Jon volunteers his own Stark lineage unprompted:

> "Tormund Giantsbane knows better than to try me. I may seem a green boy in your
> eyes, Lord Norrey, but I am still a son of Eddard Stark."
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-jon-11.md:373 (edge G9, node jon-snow, tier-4)

The text never states why the clan chiefs made the climb, or that Jon's line is a
deliberate signal to a watching northern faction rather than an offhand rebuttal to
Tormund — both readings are the theory's own interpretive layer on top of a
physically-verified visit and a verified line of dialogue.

## Evidence Against

Wyman Manderly's own account of his situation is the theory's sharpest internal
problem. In the same breath as the "north remembers" line above, he admits he cannot
even trust his own castle's communications:

> If Stannis wonders that my letters say so little, it is because I dare not even
> trust my maester.
— sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-davos-04.md:125 (edge G12, node wyman-manderly,
CONTRADICTS; mid-speech fragment, same paragraph as edge G1's quote)

A lord who cannot safely write a letter within his own city is not well-positioned to
run reliable, secret, long-distance coordination with Riverrun, the Neck, and
Stannis's marching column at once — the practical objection the "Grand" (unified)
version of the theory has to answer and does not.

A second complication is the Karstark subplot, referenced above only as background:
Arnolf Karstark's own conspiracy to betray Stannis to the Boltons is a genuine
northern plot that this session could not re-verify to a specific grounded line (see
Ungrounded material), but its shape is instructive either way — it is exposed by Alys
Karstark's own flight to Jon (edge G10), the same edge cited above as *supporting*
evidence for northern perception of Jon. A conspiracy small enough to keep secret from
Stannis was not small enough to keep secret from its own intended victim. If the
"Grand" theory's three threads really were one coordinated whole spanning White
Harbor, the Neck, Riverrun, and a marching army, it would be a considerably larger
secret than Arnolf's — and by the same logic, a considerably harder one to keep.

## Ungrounded material (outside the corpus)

- **book-interpretive** — The specific claim that Wyman built four northern lords
  (himself, Whoresbane Umber, Harwood Stout, Barbrey Dustin) into snowmen at besieged
  Winterfell, and that Stout and Umber were separately seen talking quietly before
  Dustin's crypt visit, is the video's own reading of the Theon/"Reek" Winterfell
  chapters. This session's grounding pass could not re-locate a clean verbatim line
  for either beat in the available search coverage; it is plausible book content but
  is not independently cited here.
- **book-interpretive** — That Howland Reed is the last living witness to the Tower
  of Joy, and that this is the secret motive tying the Neck/riverlands thread to a
  hypothetical plot to crown Jon Snow with proof of Targaryen parentage, depends on
  Jon's parentage itself — which the books have never confirmed on the page. See
  `r-plus-l-equals-j` for the parentage theory this claim is built on top of.
- **book-interpretive** — That Catelyn's real-life dislike of Jon (as a living
  reminder of Ned's infidelity) would make Lady Stoneheart reluctant to crown him is
  a plausible psychological reading, not a stated textual complication.
- **community** — That the singer Tom o' Sevenstreams, while spying on the
  Lannister-Frey camp for the Brotherhood during the siege of Riverrun, also learned
  of prisoner transports and an upcoming Lannister wedding, feeding a theorized
  "Red Wedding 2.0" staged by riverlords, is fan extrapolation beyond what the source
  chapters state.
- **community** — That the mountain clansmen who visit Jon already know about Robb's
  will and come to judge his fitness as a prospective King in the North, and that they
  could be in contact with the Neck conspiracy through Alysane Mormont or through
  Sybelle Glover's suspiciously long prayers in the godswood (echoing how Sansa
  received secret messages there in *A Clash of Kings*), are explicit fan
  extrapolations in the source video, not textual claims.
- **TWOW** — A published sample chapter from *The Winds of Winter* reportedly shows
  Stannis discovering Arnolf Karstark's betrayal and locking up his men — an example,
  per the source video, of a real northern conspiracy that did *not* stay secret. This
  is outside the five-book corpus and is not confirmed publication; it is not treated
  as evidence for or against this theory.
- **show** — Game of Thrones depicted none of this. There is no on-screen Manderly
  plot, no Robb's-will succession scheme, and no Winterfell restoration conspiracy;
  the show's own Battle of Winterfell and Bolton-overthrow storylines follow entirely
  different mechanics (Sansa summons the Knights of the Vale; there is no Rickon
  smuggling plot, no Frey pies, and Brynden "the Blackfish" Tully is killed at
  Riverrun rather than escaping to keep fighting, foreclosing the show's version of
  the Robb's-will thread's Riverrun leg entirely). The Grand Northern Conspiracy, in
  every version discussed here, is book-only material.

## Status Notes

**Status: open.** Three separate facts keep this out of `show-confirmed` or `jossed`
territory: (1) even the source video's own verdict does not endorse the "Grand"
(unified) framing — see below; (2) none of the three sub-plots has concluded on the
page as of *A Dance with Dragons*; (3) the show never depicted any version of this
material, so there is no adaptation-side confirmation or denial to weigh, unlike
`r-plus-l-equals-j`.

**ASX verdict:** the source video concludes it is "probably not one single 'Grand'
conspiracy" — Westeros lacks fast, private communication, and big secrets don't stay
secret (its own example is the Karstark plot, discussed above under Evidence
Against) — but that there genuinely are several separate, medium-sized, loosely
overlapping plots trending toward Stark restoration: Manderly wants Rickon installed
as Lord of Winterfell under King Stannis; Maege Mormont and Galbart Glover seem to
want Jon Snow crowned; Lady Stoneheart and the mountain clansmen appear to have their
own separate priorities. This node's `confidence: tier-4` reflects that split
verdict directly: the individual sub-plots (especially the Manderly leg, already
tier-1 on the event-node layer) are well-evidenced; the *unification* claim this
theory node actually makes is the weaker, more speculative half, and the video that
is this node's primary source argues against it while affirming its parts.
