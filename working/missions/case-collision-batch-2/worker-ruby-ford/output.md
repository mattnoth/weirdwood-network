## Identity

The Ruby Ford is the chief ford (shallow river crossing) of the Trident, located near the crossroads where the river road and the kingsroad intersect in the Riverlands. It takes its name from the Battle of the Trident in 283 AC, when Prince Rhaegar Targaryen attempted to cross the river there against the rebel army of Robert Baratheon. Robert struck Rhaegar down with his warhammer, a blow so powerful it shattered the rubies adorning Rhaegar's breastplate, scattering them across the riverbed and giving the ford its name.

The ford is the decisive location of one of the most consequential battles in the history of Westeros. Rhaegar's death there broke the royal army, which then fled back to King's Landing, effectively ending the Targaryen dynasty's hold on the throne. Characters in the narrative later reference the ford when recounting Robert's Rebellion; Arya Stark and Mycah visit the ford after learning of it, searching for rubies still remaining in the streambed.

After the Red Wedding, Ser Gregor Clegane crosses the Trident and seizes the ruby ford in the aftermath of the Lannister-orchestrated betrayal. The ford thus continues to serve as a strategically important river crossing in the Riverlands across multiple conflicts.
