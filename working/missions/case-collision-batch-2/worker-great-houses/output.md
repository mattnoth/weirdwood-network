## Identity

The Great Houses are the paramount noble houses of the Seven Kingdoms of Westeros — the ruling dynasties of each of the major regions who hold the title of Lord Paramount of their respective territories. Each Great House rules as the dominant power over the lesser lords and knights of their region. After Aegon's Conquest, each Great House became a vassal to the Iron Throne while retaining regional supremacy. Examples include House Arryn of the Eyrie (principal house of the Vale of Arryn), House Tully of Riverrun (Lords Paramount of the Trident), and House Tyrell of Highgarden (Lords Paramount of the Mander).

The loyalty of the Great Houses is a central concern in the political history of Westeros. During the Dance of the Dragons, Aegon II's council worked to win the Great Houses to his cause. During the reign of Daeron II Targaryen, other Great Houses resented the special privileges granted to House Martell as part of Dorne's peaceful integration into the realm. When the realm-wide conflict called the War of the Five Kings erupted, nearly all Great Houses suffered significant losses — only House Arryn of the Vale and House Martell of Dorne emerged with relative strength intact. In 48 AC, all the Great Houses abandoned Maegor I Targaryen in quick succession, contributing to his death.

The term is also used in contexts beyond Westeros — Jorah Mormont and others invoke the category when discussing the balance of power and allegiance in the wider political landscape of the Seven Kingdoms.
