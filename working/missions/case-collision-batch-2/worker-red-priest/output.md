## Identity

Red priest (and the feminine form red priestess) is the informal title given to clergy of R'hllor, the Lord of Light — a deity worshipped across Essos, primarily in the eastern Free Cities and most prominently in Volantis, where the red temple is the largest religious structure in the city. Red priests and priestesses dress in red and red-orange robes, and priests of the Volantene temple bear flame tattoos across their faces (as slaves of the temple; priests from other cities such as Thoros of Myr or Melisandre do not). They are trained in prayers, spells, and fire-reading — learning to see visions in flames — a process requiring many years of study. They also possess, or claim to possess, the ability to resurrect the dead through R'hllor's fire and to work other magical feats including the creation of shadowbindings and the conjuration of glamours.

Red priests operate throughout Essos; in the context of Westeros their most prominent representative is Melisandre, a red priestess from Asshai attached to Stannis Baratheon's court at Dragonstone. Thoros of Myr, another red priest, was sent to King's Landing to convert the fire-obsessed Aerys II Targaryen (he failed) and subsequently became Robert I's drinking companion; he later rediscovered his faith in the Riverlands, where his fire prayers resurrected Beric Dondarrion multiple times. In Volantis, Benerro, the High Priest, also holds the titles Flame of Truth, Light of Wisdom, First Servant of the Lord of Light, and Slave of R'hllor, and preaches openly in the streets of Volantis calling for Daenerys Targaryen's liberation of the city's slaves.
