## Identity

The hammer of the waters is a form of catastrophic magic attributed to the greenseers of the Children of the Forest, allegedly used during the War of the First Men and the Children of the Forest. According to legend, the Children called upon their gods and worked dark magic to shatter the Arm of Dorne, the land bridge connecting Westeros to Essos, in an effort to halt the migration of the First Men. The result was the creation of the Stepstones and the Broken Arm of Dorne.

The hammer of the waters was also reportedly used, or attempted, at Moat Cailin. Old songs hold that greenseers gathered to Moat Cailin and called upon their gods from the Children's Tower, attempting to smash the Neck and break Westeros in two — separating the north from the south in the same manner they had shattered the Arm of Dorne. This second use of the hammer failed; the Children only succeeded in flooding the Neck, creating its bogs and swamps. Some scholars discount both legends, attributing the geographic features to natural events rather than magic.
