## Identity

Gender and sexuality is a wiki meta-topic used to tag passages describing characters' sexual orientation, gender norms, and related social dynamics within the world of ASOIAF. It functions as a thematic label rather than an in-world entity.

The topic appears in multiple contexts. George R.R. Martin has confirmed that Jon Connington is gay; it is implied he was in love with Prince Rhaegar Targaryen, whom he called "my silver prince." The Dornishmen article records that homosexuality is more accepted in Dorne than elsewhere in Westeros, and that many Dornish lords and some ladies keep paramours. In the matter of Rhaenyra Targaryen's marriage to Laenor Velaryon, King Viserys I was told by Rhaenyra that Laenor would prefer her half-brothers rather than her; Laenor spent most of his time on Driftmark surrounded by handsome squires. The topic is also invoked when discussing how a character's sex was weighed as a mitigating factor in sentencing, as in the case of Priscella Hogg, who received a lesser punishment "on account of her sex."
