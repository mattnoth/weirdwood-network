## Identity

The War of the First Men and the Children of the Forest was a prolonged conflict during the Dawn Age of Westeros, fought between the First Men who migrated from Essos across the land bridge called the Arm of Dorne, and the indigenous Children of the Forest. The conflict was triggered when the First Men began settling the new land, cutting down and burning the carved weirwood trees sacred to the Children and their gods.

The Children of the Forest fought back with greenseers, magic, and wood dancers, but the First Men were larger, stronger, and more technologically advanced, wielding bronze weapons while riding horses. According to old songs, the Children gathered their greenseers at the Isle of Faces and at Moat Cailin, where they invoked dark magic and used the hammer of the waters to shatter the Arm of Dorne, creating the Stepstones and the Broken Arm and flooding the Neck into a swamp. Despite this, the First Men were already too numerous in Westeros for the measure to halt them.

The war eventually ended with the Pact, signed on the Isle of Faces in the middle of the Gods Eye. Under the Pact, the First Men gave the Children dominion over the deep forests and promised to stop felling weirwoods; in return, the First Men received claim to the rest of Westeros. The Pact began four thousand years of peace between the two peoples, a period known as the Age of Heroes. Generational warfare against the Children had occurred across Westeros before the Pact, including in the Stormlands and the regions that would become Dorne.
