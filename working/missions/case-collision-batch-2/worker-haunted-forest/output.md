## Identity

The Haunted Forest is the vast, ancient woodland lying immediately north of the Wall in the region known as Beyond the Wall. It stretches for hundreds of miles, bounded roughly by the Wall to the south and the Frostfangs to the west; to the north it gives way to more open tundra before reaching the Lands of Always Winter. The forest takes its name from the eerie reputation it acquired among the Night's Watch, who associate it with unexplained disappearances, Others, and wights. Weirwood groves grow within it, and it was in one such grove that Jon Snow took his vows for the Night's Watch.

The Haunted Forest is the primary operational environment of Night's Watch rangers, and many key events of the series unfold within it: Ser Waymar Royce's patrol was ambushed there by Others in the prologue of *A Game of Thrones*, producing the first wights shown in the narrative; the bodies of Othor and Jafer Flowers were found there; the great ranging of the Night's Watch used the Fist of the First Men — a promontory within the forest — as their base before the attack by the Others and their wights. Coldhands guided Bran Stark's party through the haunted forest to the cave of the three-eyed crow, where they were ambushed by wights at the entrance before Leaf burned them back. During the Targaryen royal progress of 58 AC, Queen Alysanne visited Castle Black and Lothor Burley escorted her into the forest; Silverwing refused to fly north of the Wall on three attempts.
