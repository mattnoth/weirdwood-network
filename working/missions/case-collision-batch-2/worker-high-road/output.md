## Identity

The High Road is a mountain route through the Mountains of the Moon in the Vale of Arryn that connects the Vale to the rest of Westeros. It is the primary land approach to the Eyrie and the Bloody Gate, passing through steep, rocky terrain susceptible to snow in autumn and winter. The road is notoriously dangerous due to raids by Vale mountain clans who prey on travelers.

At the Bloody Gate, the High Road narrows to a pass where only four riders can ride abreast, with twin watchtowers and a covered stone bridge spanning the road overhead. Beyond the Gate, a steep trail descends into the Vale. The fortification was designed specifically to defend this chokepoint in the road.

Several notable journeys are associated with the High Road. Tyrion Lannister and Bronn were released from the Eyrie onto the road by Lysa Arryn, who expected the mountain clans to kill them. Tom of Sevenstreams was sent up the High Road and was robbed by clansmen. Sandor Clegane attempted to take Arya Stark along the High Road to ransom her to Lysa Arryn, but turned back when winter snows closed the high passes. During the Arryn succession conflict of 135 AC, a royal host led by Ser Robert Rowan was slowed by snow and repeatedly attacked by mountain clans while traversing the High Road, losing a third of its strength before reaching the Bloody Gate.
