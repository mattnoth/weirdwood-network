## Identity

The Master of Laws is a seat on the small council of Westeros responsible for justice, law enforcement, and legal matters of the realm. The office-holder serves effectively as the crown's chief legal officer, overseeing the administration of law at the royal level. The position is distinct from the Master of Coin (treasury) and Master of Whisperers (intelligence), focusing instead on legal and judicial authority.

The office has been held by numerous figures across Westerosi history, including Symond Staunton (under Aerys II Targaryen), Renly Baratheon (under Robert I Baratheon, formerly), Kevan Lannister, Randyll Tarly (under Tommen Baratheon), and several historical holders: Aemon Targaryen (son of Jaehaerys I), Albin Massey, Daemon Targaryen, Jasper Wylde, Lyonel Strong, Prentys Tully, Rodrik Arryn, Thaddeus Rowan, and Triston Massey. Randyll Tarly is noted as joining the small council and restoring the title of Master of Laws, suggesting the position had lapsed or been unfilled. Aerys II Targaryen's loyal council faction included Symond Staunton in this role.

The title is also referred to in terms of the office's judicial function — those who hold it may also bear the style "Justiciar" in related contexts (as Randyll Tarly does in his infobox). The Master of Laws sits on the small council at King's Landing alongside the Hand of the King, Grand Maester, Master of Coin, Master of Ships, and Master of Whisperers.
