## Identity

The Known World is the in-universe geographic term for the totality of lands known to the peoples of Westeros and Essos — roughly the equivalent of the observable, mapped world as understood by the characters. It encompasses at minimum the continent of Westeros (from the Wall south to Dorne), the vast continent of Essos (from the Free Cities in the west to the Jade Sea and Yi Ti in the far east), and the lands south of the Summer Sea (including Sothoryos and the Summer Isles). The eastern reaches are dominated by the Dothraki sea, the Bone Mountains, and the further territories of Asshai-by-the-Shadow. The phrase is used by characters when describing travel across all known civilizations: Euron Greyjoy boasts of having sailed and raided "all over the known world" including Asshai and the ruins of Valyria; Lomas Longstrider traveled the known world to catalog its sixteen wonders in his books *Wonders* and *Wonders Made by Man*; Vaes Dothrak is described as reachable via trade routes from across the known world.

The term functions as a scope-delimiter rather than denoting a specific bounded polity or region: the World of Ice and Fire in-universe is described as containing far more than the known world (the "ulthos" hypothesis and lands east of Yi Ti remain uncertain even to maesters). Characters use "known world" to signal that they are speaking of the widest possible civilizational scope.
