## Identity

The first night is an ancient custom originating among the First Men of the Dawn Age, by which a lord or warlord claimed the right to bed the bride of one of his vassals on her wedding night. The practice was rationalized as a form of blessing: if a powerful warrior's seed produced a child, the husband gained the honor of raising a hero's son as his own. The custom predated both the arrival of the Andals and the Faith of the Seven, and was continued by Andal lords after their conquest of the First Men territories.

The first night was formally abolished in the Seven Kingdoms by King Jaehaerys I Targaryen. Queen Alysanne Targaryen brought reports of the practice to Jaehaerys after hearing accounts from women during her royal progress, and Jaehaerys subsequently outlawed it. His Small Council member Albin Massey had initially argued in defense of the custom during the codification of the laws around 55 AC, citing its antiquity, before ultimately assisting in the legal reforms that eliminated it.

Despite its abolition, the custom persisted in some regions where the old gods held sway. Roose Bolton invoked the outlawed first-night tradition when he raped the miller's wife by the Weeping Water after she married without his knowledge. Roose told Theon Greyjoy that Jaehaerys abolished the custom to appease his "shrewish" queen, but that where the old gods rule, old customs linger. The first-night custom also directly contributed to the death of Gargon Qoherys, Lord of Harrenhal: Gargon's habitual abuse of the practice caused a dishonored girl's father to open a gate to the outlaw Harren the Red, who then killed Gargon.
