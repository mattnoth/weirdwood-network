## Identity

The Widow of the Waterfront is an unnamed woman in Volantis who controls a substantial commercial enterprise on the city's waterfront. She was born a pleasure slave, trained in the way of the seven sighs in Yunkai. Vogarro, a powerful Volantene merchant and seven-time triarch who belonged to the elephants faction, fell in love with her and caused a scandal when he freed and married her. Upon Vogarro's death, he left her all his businesses — piers, storehouses, cargo brokerage, money-changing, and insurance for shipowners — as well as his slave-dealing interests. She became known as the Widow of the Waterfront as she managed these enterprises. As a freedwoman, she was barred from the manse Vogarro had owned inside the Black Wall and was forced to sell it.

The Widow operates out of the Merchant's House in Volantis and is clearly a well-connected figure with knowledge of ships, cargoes, and the movements of influential people. When Ser Jorah Mormont brought Tyrion Lannister to her seeking passage to Meereen, she initially refused to help Jorah because she did not trust him. Penny's surprise attack on Tyrion changed her mind: she told Tyrion to board the Selaesori Qhoran when it sailed for Qarth, predicting — as the red priest Benerro had foreseen in his flames — that the ship would never reach its intended destination. She also mentioned the triarch Trianna to Jorah and Tyrion during the encounter. She gave Penny a room at the inn following the incident, and the Selaesori Qhoran subsequently carried Tyrion, Jorah, Penny, and the red priest Moqorro away from Volantis.
