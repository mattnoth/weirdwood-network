## Identity

"Valar morghulis" is a High Valyrian phrase meaning "all men must die." It is the traditional greeting or salutation associated with the Faceless Men of Braavos and their cult of the Many-Faced God, whose religious doctrine holds that death is the one true gift. The phrase is paired with the response "Valar dohaeris" ("all men must serve"), and the exchange functions as a recognition signal between those who know its significance.

The phrase was introduced to Arya Stark by the man known as Jaqen H'ghar at Harrenhal, when he pressed an iron coin into her hand and instructed her that if she ever wished to find him again, she should give the coin to any man from Braavos and say "valar morghulis." Arya, who did not initially understand the meaning, adopted the phrase to end her nightly prayer — a litany of the names of people she wanted dead. She whispered it after killing a Bolton guard during her escape from Harrenhal. Later, at Saltpans, Arya used the coin and the phrase with the Braavosi ship captain Ternesio Terys, who responded "Valar dohaeris" and immediately gave her a cabin and free passage to Braavos.

In a later context in The Winds of Winter, Arya — operating under the alias Mercy — whispers "Valar morghulis" after killing Rafford. The phrase thus functions both as a ritual salutation connected to the Faceless Men and as a personal mantra that Arya has internalized as she moves toward becoming No One.
