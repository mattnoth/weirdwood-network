## Identity

The Master of Whisperers is a seat on the small council of Westeros responsible for intelligence gathering, espionage, and the management of informants across the realm and beyond. The office-holder serves as the crown's spymaster, maintaining a network of agents — often called "little birds" — whose reports flow directly to the monarch and to the council. The position demands no noble birth and has historically been held by individuals whose value lies in their access to secrets rather than martial or administrative distinction.

The position was held by the eunuch Varys under both King Aerys II Targaryen and, after being pardoned at Robert's coronation, under King Robert I Baratheon. Aerys appointed Varys reasoning that only a foreigner with no competing loyalties in Westeros could be trusted to alert him to threats; Varys used crown gold to form a vast web of informers and was constantly seen whispering in the king's ear. Robert retained Varys and Varys continued to operate his network, relaying intelligence such as reports on Daenerys Targaryen's movements from Ser Jorah Mormont in exile. Under House Targaryen during the Dance of the Dragons, the position was held by Larys Strong; Qyburn held a de facto equivalent role under Cersei Lannister. Brynden Rivers (Bloodraven) also held the title under earlier Targaryen kings.

The Master of Whisperers is a title-office, not an individual — multiple holders across history shared the role while the title remained distinct. The office is a seat of the small council and thus tied to the royal court at King's Landing.
