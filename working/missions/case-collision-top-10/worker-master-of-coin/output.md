## Identity

Master of Coin is a formal office within the small council of the Iron Throne, the body that advises the King of Westeros. The Master of Coin holds responsibility for managing the royal treasury, overseeing customs collection, directing the kingdom's tax policy, and maintaining the Crown's financial accounts. The office carries the alternate title of Lord Treasurer, which appears on several infoboxes alongside "Master of Coin" as an equivalent designation.

The position has been held continuously since the Targaryen conquest. Crispian Celtigar served as Master of Coin to Aegon I Targaryen. Under Maegor I Targaryen, Lord Alton Butterwell held the office and was tasked with continuously raising taxes and imposing new ones to fund Maegor's ruinous wars; when those measures failed, Maegor demanded tribute from the high lords directly. In the early reign of Jaehaerys I Targaryen, Lord Edwell Celtigar served in the role but was considered ineffectual; Jaehaerys replaced him with Rego Draz of Pentos, who took loans from the Iron Bank of Braavos to reduce Crown debt and helped fund major construction in King's Landing. Rego was murdered by a mob in King's Landing during the Shivers plague of 59 AC. The Dance of the Dragons brought further turnover: Lord Lyman Beesbury held the seat under Viserys I and was the sole small council member who refused to support Aegon II's claim; after his removal Ser Tyland Lannister was named Master of Coin for the greens and promptly divided the royal treasury into four parts, dispersing it to prevent capture. Bartimos Celtigar served as Master of Coin on the rival Black council for Queen Rhaenyra.

In the main-series era, Petyr Baelish — known as Littlefinger — served as Master of Coin under Robert I Baratheon, having risen to the position within three years of arriving at King's Landing after demonstrating extraordinary skill at customs revenue. He installed loyalists across all subordinate financial offices (Keepers of the Keys, King's Counter, King's Scales, harbor masters, toll collectors, wine factors) and used a complex web of loans, investments, and brothel revenues to supply the king with gold, tripling then quintupling Crown revenues while simultaneously building enormous royal debt. When Petyr was sent to the Vale to woo Lysa Arryn, Tyrion Lannister was appointed Master of Coin in his absence. After Tywin Lannister's death, Cersei blocked Garth Tyrell from the seat and named Lord Gyles Rosby instead; upon Cersei's arrest, Harys Swyft held the position and, together with Grand Maester Pycelle, effectively governed King's Landing and brought Kevan Lannister back as Lord Regent.
