## Identity

The Free Folk — called "wildlings" by those south of the Wall — are the peoples who live beyond the Wall in the lands of the far north. They reject the feudal structures of the Seven Kingdoms entirely: they do not bend the knee to lords, do not pay taxes or recognize noble titles, and do not swear oaths of fealty. As Mance Rayder explains to Jon Snow, the free folk follow strength and the man, not cloth animals sewn on a tunic or chains of office. Those south of the Wall often use the term "wildling" as a pejorative; the people themselves prefer "free folk," a name that encapsulates their core cultural value of personal liberty.

The free folk are not a single unified people but a loose collection of tribes, clans, and groups. Named groups include the Thenns (with their own hereditary Magnars), the Hornfoots, the Nightrunners, the men of the Frozen Shore (who ride bone chariots), the ice-river clans, and various raider warbands. They speak the Old Tongue. Historically they have been led by Kings-Beyond-the-Wall, figures who manage to unite enough tribes to threaten the Wall; Bael the Bard is one legendary such king, while Mance Rayder is the most recent. Women warriors and leaders — such as Val, Harma Dogshead, and Mother Mole — are attested, as are spearwives. Some among the free folk possess magical abilities, including skinchanging and greensight.

The free folk's defining historical relationship is with the Night's Watch and the Wall. They have periodically attempted to push south in force: Gendel and Gorne used a subterranean passage; Raymun Redbeard was broken at Long Lake; and Mance Rayder assembled the largest host in living memory before being defeated at the battle beneath the Wall by Stannis Baratheon. The free folk's flight south during the events of the main series is driven not purely by aggression but by pressure from the Others and their wights, who hunt the free folk in the far north. After the battle, Stannis offers surviving free folk the choice to bend the knee or return beyond the Wall; Jon Snow later negotiates to settle free folk south of the Wall and garrison some abandoned castles, a policy that provokes fierce resistance from northern lords and Night's Watch brothers alike.
