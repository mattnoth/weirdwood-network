## Identity

The Small Council is the principal advisory and governing body of the Iron Throne, composed of the Hand of the King and the chief officers of the realm — the masters of coin, ships, laws, and whisperers, as well as the Grand Maester. It meets in the Red Keep at King's Landing, and kings of the Targaryen and Baratheon dynasties rely on it to administer the Seven Kingdoms. Membership is by royal appointment: seats are granted, filled, and stripped at the king's or regent's pleasure. The council's composition is therefore deeply political; when Ser Otto Hightower returned as Hand during the reign of Viserys I Targaryen, he spent the following decade stacking the Small Council with loyalists of the green faction, while Cersei Lannister, acting as regent after Tywin's death, filled it with her own lackeys and used it as an instrument of personal power.

Throughout its history the Small Council has been the site of consequential decisions: it debated whether to kill Daenerys Targaryen and her unborn child (AGOT), arranged the Tyrell alliance before the Battle of the Blackwater, condemned Eddard Stark (intending he take the black, before Joffrey overruled them with an execution order), and later, under Kevan Lannister's regency, attempted to stabilize the realm by appointing Mace Tyrell as Hand and adding lords Paxter Redwyne and Randyll Tarly. Membership has carried high risk: when Rhaenyra Targaryen captured King's Landing during the Dance of the Dragons, she executed several members of Aegon II's Small Council, and during the Shivers plague Jaehaerys I lost half his council to the disease. Despite a quorum of standing offices, the council has at various times included unusual appointments — Janos Slynt was elevated from the City Watch to a council seat after helping Joffrey seize power, an appointment that caused resentment among nobles.

Notable long-serving members span both the Targaryen and Baratheon eras: Petyr Baelish rose within three years of arriving in King's Landing to become master of coin and council member; Stannis Baratheon served as master of ships under Robert I before his estrangement from court; and Daemon Targaryen briefly held the posts of master of coin and master of laws under Viserys I before his temperament and rivalry with Hand Otto Hightower led to his removal. Corlys Velaryon served as master of ships under Jaehaerys I and later under Aegon II, and resigned from the council in protest when Princess Rhaenys was passed over in the succession. The council has also acted as a forum for intelligence: Varys reported on Daenerys's dragons; Qyburn brought reports of the slave revolts in Essos; and the council received (and suppressed or debated) information from informants across the realm.
