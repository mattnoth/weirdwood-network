## Identity

The Old Gods of the Forest are the nameless deities of stream, forest, and stone once worshipped by the children of the forest and later adopted by the First Men following the Pact between the two peoples. They are called the "old gods" in contrast to the "new gods" — the Seven brought by the Andals — who supplanted them across most of Westeros. The old gods are never individually named; they are not accompanied by priests, holy texts, songs of worship, or formal rites. Prayers to the old gods are offered in silence.

Worship of the old gods centers on godswoods, groves maintained within castle grounds that contain a heart tree — a weirwood with a face carved into its pale bark. Worshippers believe the old gods watch through these trees. North of the Wall, living weirwoods are abundant; in the south, most were cut down during the wars between the First Men and the children, and then again by the Andals when they supplanted the old faith with the Faith of the Seven. By the main series period, open worship of the old gods survives primarily in the North, where it was never displaced. The Crannogmen of the Neck and the free folk beyond the Wall also maintain the faith. The wildlings who passed south under Stannis Baratheon were formally made to swear to R'hllor, but in secret kept faith in the old gods.

The reach of the old faith has contracted over centuries but has never been entirely extinguished. House Stark of Winterfell is the most prominent adherent house. Blood sacrifice to the old gods was reportedly still practiced near White Harbor as recently as five hundred years before the War of the Five Kings. In the aftermath of the Dance of the Dragons, more than a thousand northmen who settled in the riverlands as wives and guardsmen produced a partial revival of old gods worship in that region. The Blackwood lords of Raventree Hall preserved the faith in the riverlands, and Roderick Blackwood's rebellion against King Humfrey I Teague — who had actively suppressed old gods worship — became a defining episode in the faith's contested survival south of the Neck.
