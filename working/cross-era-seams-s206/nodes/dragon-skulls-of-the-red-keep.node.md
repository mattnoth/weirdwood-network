# Dragon skulls of the Red Keep

The nineteen skulls of the dead [Targaryen](house-targaryen) dragons — the persistent physical
trace of the Fire & Blood era standing inside the main-series [Red Keep](red-keep). For three
centuries they hung upon the walls of the throne room, ranged in size from a matched pair no bigger
than mastiff's skulls (the last two hatchlings born on [Dragonstone](dragonstone)) up to the three
great monsters of song and story: [Balerion](balerion) the Black Dread, [Vhagar](vhagar), and
[Meraxes](meraxes) — the dragons [Aegon the Conqueror](aegon-i-targaryen) and his sisters unleashed
on the Seven Kingdoms. Balerion's skull alone could have swallowed an aurochs whole.

When [Robert Baratheon](robert-baratheon) took the throne he had the skulls pulled down and replaced
with banners and tapestries, and they were stored away in a dank cellar beneath the Red Keep — where
[Tyrion Lannister](tyrion-lannister) sought them out, standing between their gaping jaws, wordless
and awed. As black as onyx and polished smooth, the [dragonbone](dragonbone) drank torchlight rather
than reflecting it; the teeth were long curving knives of black diamond that the flame could not touch.

The skulls are the throne room's memory of the age of dragons — the reason a reader standing in the
main-series Red Keep is standing, unknowing, among Balerion and Vhagar and the dragons of the Dance.
