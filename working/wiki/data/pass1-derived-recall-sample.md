## Qualitative Note: What Kinds of Relationships Fall Into Bucket C?

The 17 Bucket C items across all seven chapters cluster into three categories:

**1. Background relationship context (5 items):**  
Historical or contextual facts about character relationships mentioned in passing but not extracted as standalone events. Examples:
- Gregor's rumored killing of infant Aegon Targaryen and rape of Elia Martell (agot-eddard-07: mentioned in one narrative aside, not an event or a dialogue)
- Sandor having left Gregor's household the day Gregor inherited — explains the brothers' enmity in a way no table captures
- Ser Rodrik Cassel teaching Jon and Robb together at Winterfell (adwd-jon-06)
- Theon killing Ser Rodrik at Winterfell (referenced in Jon's memory)

These are **moderate value** for a knowledge graph. The Gregor/Elia and Gregor/Sandor items specifically are important structural edges (Gregor committed war crime, Sandor fled his brother) that would enrich the graph. They fall through the cracks because they're delivered as narrative aside, not as an event or a relationship statement.

**2. POV-only emotional/psychological state (7 items):**  
Internal decisions, realizations, and emotional stances that are only visible to the reader via POV narration. Examples:
- Daenerys privately places dragon eggs in fire — the act is visible in the prose but no extraction table captures Dany's personal relationship to the eggs as quasi-living things she communes with
- Robb's private resolve to endure any humiliation at the Twins ("stewed crow smothered in maggots")
- Roslin's ominous weeping at the wedding (knowing the massacre is coming)
- Cersei recalling Maggy the Frog prophecy when seeing Olenna — this is the Cersei/Maggy relationship, a formative one, visible only in POV memory
- Jon's feeling that Castle Black is "a ruin" / his emotional state about Arya he won't act on

These are **mixed value**. The Maggy/Cersei relationship is high-value (formative trauma, prophecy that drives all Cersei's political behavior); the Robb/endurance edge and Roslin's foreknowledge are meaningful but transient. Most of the pure emotional-state items are low-value for graph edges — they're character texture, not graph structure.

**3. Action-relationship items with no clear table match (5 items):**
- Anguy offered position by Ned, refused — an employment-offer/refusal interaction with a minor character
- Dany remembers living in terror of Viserys for years — relationship to a now-dead character
- Catelyn remembers her wedding night (Jory Cassell tearing her gown, Desmond Grell's bawdy jokes) — long-dead lord relationships
- Robb's dying words naming Grey Wind (emotional attachment to his direwolf superseding human bonds)
- Catelyn clawing her own face in madness before her throat is cut (psychological state at death)

These are **low to moderate value**. The Dany/Viserys "lived in terror" edge and Robb's bond to Grey Wind as his last words are genuinely meaningful graph edges that the Relationships Observed table misses in these chapters (though Robb/Grey Wind may appear in a different chapter's extraction). The wedding-night memories are flavor text with minor historical characters, low graph value.

**Notable high-value Bucket C miss:** The most significant miss across the sample is the **Gregor Clegane / Elia Martell** and **Gregor Clegane / infant Aegon Targaryen** edges from agot-eddard-07. These are war-crime allegations (killed infant, raped and killed Elia) that are delivered in a single narrative paragraph and captured in no table. For a knowledge graph, these are among the most important edges touching House Targaryen's fall.

## Bottom Line

**Table-mining (A+B) closes about 91% of the gap.** The current pipeline captures 64% of meaningful relationships directly via the Relationships Observed table. Mining the remaining Pass 1 tables — especially Events & Actions, Information Revealed, and Hospitality & Guest Right — recovers another 28%, bringing total recall to approximately 91–92%.

The remaining 9% in Bucket C divides roughly as: ~3% high-value edges (the Gregor/Elia war-crime backstory, the Cersei/Maggy prophecy formative relationship, the Dany/Viserys years-of-terror edge) that a prose-reading pass would add meaningfully to the graph; and ~6% texture items (POV emotional states, passing references to long-dead minor characters, dying-moment psychological narration) that are of low graph value.

The **Hospitality & Guest Right** table deserves special attention: for the two Red Wedding chapters (catelyn-06 and catelyn-07), it captures the entire structural arc of the guest-right violation that is the moral and narrative core of those chapters. Without mining it, the current pipeline would capture the *killing* edges (Robb killed, Dacey killed) but miss the *hospitality* framing that makes those killings a distinct category of betrayal — a meaningful distinction for any graph query about honor, betrayal, or guest-right violations.

**Recommendation:** A table-mining pass against all five Pass 1 tables (not just Relationships Observed) is sufficient for ~92% recall at substantially lower cost than a prose-reading pass. A prose-reading pass is warranted only if the project specifically needs: (1) historically-referenced relationship claims delivered in narrative asides (like the Gregor/Elia edges), (2) formative backstory relationships surfaced through POV memory (like Cersei/Maggy), or (3) direwolf-bond edges not captured elsewhere. These are worth a targeted audit rather than a full prose re-read.
