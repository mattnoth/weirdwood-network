## Origins

The Belmores are of [First Man](wiki:First_Man) origin. During the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), they allied with [Robar II Royce](wiki:Robar_II_Royce) against the invading [Andals](wiki:Andals). Robar's forces were defeated in the [Battle of the Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_the_Seven_Stars), however, and the Belmores submitted to [Artys I Arryn](wiki:Artys_I_Arryn), the new [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale).(wiki:House_Belmore.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

The Belmores support Lord [Yohn Royce](wiki:Yohn_Royce) in pressuring Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) to enter the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:House_Belmore.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Ser [Marwyn Belmore](wiki:Marwyn_Belmore) is Lysa's captain of the guards at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), and he is mocked by [Marillion](wiki:Marillion), who names him Ser Ding-Dong.(wiki:House_Belmore.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) After [the wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Petyr_Baelish_and_Lysa_Arryn) of [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) and Lysa, Petyr replaces Marwyn with Ser [Lothor Brune](wiki:Lothor_Brune) as the captain of the guards.(wiki:House_Belmore.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

### A Feast for Crows

Ser Marwyn is now the captain of the guards at the Gates of the Moon.(wiki:House_Belmore.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) He goes to the Eyrie with Lord [Nestor](wiki:Nestor_Royce) and Ser [Albar Royce](wiki:Albar_Royce) to question Petyr and [Alayne Stone](wiki:Sansa_Stark) about Lysa's death.(wiki:House_Belmore.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

Lord [Benedar Belmore](wiki:Benedar_Belmore) is one of the six [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant). The first to arrive at the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon), Benedar sends word that he is blocking food supplies to the Eyrie. Petyr does not consider Lord Belmore as a threat, since he is corrupt and can be bought.(wiki:House_Belmore.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

After the parley at the Eyrie, Lord Belmore befriends Petyr and attends [the wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Lyonel_Corbray_and_Lady_Corbray) of Lord [Lyonel Corbray](wiki:Lyonel_Corbray) in [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown).(wiki:House_Belmore.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
