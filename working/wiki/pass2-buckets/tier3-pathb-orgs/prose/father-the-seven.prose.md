## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) prays in a nameless village’s sept. The sept is modest and has no statues of the Seven, only rough charcoal drawings to represent them. To the Father [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) prays for justice, the strength to seek it and the wisdom to know it. (wiki:Father_(the_Seven).cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

While standing vigil over his [father's](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) body, [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister)'s thoughts turn unbidden to [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth). He wonders where she is. He thinks, Father give her strength. Almost a prayer … but he wonders was it the god he was invoking, the Father Above whose towering gilded likeness is glimmering in the candlelight across the sept? Or is he praying to the corpse that lies before him? He wonders, Does it matter? They never listened, either one. The [Warrior](wiki:Warrior_(the_Seven)) had been Jaime’s god since he was old enough to hold a sword.
