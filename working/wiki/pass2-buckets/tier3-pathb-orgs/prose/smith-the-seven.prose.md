## Origins

During King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen)’s reign he had a [stonemason](wiki:High_Septon_(stonemason)) named as the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon), a man that could carve stonework so beautifully that Baelor believed him to be the Smith in human form. While a fantastic stonecarver, the new High Septon could not read, write or recite any prayers.(wiki:Smith_(the_Seven).cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
In his childhood, Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) was taught by [septons](wiki:Septon) to pray to the Smith for strength.(wiki:Smith_(the_Seven).cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Though Davos did not grow up to become a devout man, he always makes offerings to the Smith before launching a new [ship](wiki:Ship) at sea.(wiki:Smith_(the_Seven).cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) burns the statues of [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) from [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone)'s sept, including the statue of the Smith.(wiki:Smith_(the_Seven).cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)
[Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) prays in a nameless village’s sept. The sept is modest and has no statues of the Seven, only rough charcoal drawings to represent them. She kneels before the Smith, who fixes things that are broken, and asks that he give her sweet [Bran](wiki:Bran) his protection.(wiki:Smith_(the_Seven).cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Courtiers gather in the [royal sept](wiki:Royal_sept) to pray to the Seven for a positive outcome in the [battle to come](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater). [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) sees people pray at the Smith's altar, and a [septon](wiki:Septon) calls upon the Smith to lend strength to King [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon)'s men.(wiki:Smith_(the_Seven).cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) sees [sparrows](wiki:Sparrows) with iron hammers around their necks in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Smith_(the_Seven).cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)
While traveling the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) Septon [Meribald](wiki:Meribald) tells [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne) that he has never known a boy who did not love the [Warrior](wiki:Warrior_(the_Seven)). He remarks that he is old though, and he loves the Smith. He expounds to [Brienne](wiki:Brienne) and her companions,
Without his labour, what would the Warrior defend? Every town has a smith, and every castle. They make the plows we need to plant our crops, the nails we use to build our ships, iron shoes to save the hooves of our faithful horses, the bright swords of our lords. No one could doubt the value of a smith, and so we name one of [the Seven](wiki:The_Seven) in his honour, but we might as easily have called him the Farmer or the Fisherman, the Carpenter or the Cobbler. What he works at makes no matter. What matters is, he works. The [Father](wiki:Father_(the_Seven)) rules, the [Warrior](wiki:Warrior_(the_Seven)) fights, the Smith labours, and together they perform all that is rightful for a man. Just as the Smith is one aspect of the godhead, the Cobbler is one aspect of the Smith.(wiki:Smith_(the_Seven).cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
