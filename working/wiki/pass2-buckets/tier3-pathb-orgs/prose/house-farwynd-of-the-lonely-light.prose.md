## Origins

A thousand years after the time of King [Brandon the Burner](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(Burner)), [ironmen](wiki:Ironmen) sailing out of [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk) were blown off course into a cluster of islands eight days sail from the closest shore. Their captain built a tower and a beacon there, took the name "Farwynd", and declared the [Lonely Light](wiki:Lonely_Light) his seat. His descendants prospered and continue to live there, though other ironborn believe them mad.(wiki:House_Farwynd_of_the_Lonely_Light.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
Lord Farwynd of the Lonely Light was one of the many nobles in attendance for the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:House_Farwynd_of_the_Lonely_Light.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

After the death of [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North), Lord [Gylbert Farwynd](wiki:Gylbert_Farwynd) puts forth his name at the [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk), supported by his sons as his champions. He promises to lead the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) to lands beyond the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea) where every man would be a king and every wife a queen. His gifts are considered poor, his words mad, and his claim is dismissed quickly.(wiki:House_Farwynd_of_the_Lonely_Light.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)
