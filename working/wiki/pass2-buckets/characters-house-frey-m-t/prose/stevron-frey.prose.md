## Origins

Stevron was too old to ride in the [tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_Prince_Joffrey's_name_day) in [297 AC](wiki:297_AC).(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

## Appearances & Description

A polite man in his sixties, Stevron's appearance shows his age.(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) He has watery grey eyes.(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) According to [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), Stevron resembles a weasel, like his father, Lord [Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey).

Walder has groomed Stevron for [lordship](wiki:Lord) of [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey), though Walder often jokes that Stevron has been waiting for him to die for forty years. Through his father, Stevron has learned to place great importance on the family.(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Stevron, unlike his father, is amiable and reasonable. [Big Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)) considers Stevron tired and too old for battles.(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

After King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) comes to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Stevron and his father, Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), are among the nobles demanded to swear fealty in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Stevron leads the parley with the [northern](wiki:North) army when they arrive beneath the walls of the [Twins](wiki:Twins), and [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) then negotiates inside the castle with Lord Walder. [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) allies with [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) in return for him agreeing to marry a daughter of Walder, and Stevron commands the Frey contingent who heads west of the [Green Fork](wiki:Green_Fork) with Robb.(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

At [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) after the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), a cautious Stevron suggests that the north and the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) should let Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) and Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) weaken themselves in [fighting over the Iron Throne](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). Stevron offers to travel to [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) to ransom the captured Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) back to his father Tywin, but the Frey heir receives outrage in response. Instead, Robb is named [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) by the collected northmen and rivermen.(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Robb's mother, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), suggests that Stevron be sent as an envoy to [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands). Robb instead sends his friend, Balon's son [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy),(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) who ends up betraying Robb and [capturing Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Stevron accompanies Robb in his campaign into the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) and personally leads the Frey soldiers into battle. He is wounded at the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross). The wound is not thought serious, but three days later Stevron is found dead in his tent.(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) He is succeeded as heir to the Twins by his first son, Ser [Ryman Frey](wiki:Ryman_Frey). [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) scolds [Big Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)) and [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)) for not being upset at their uncle's passing.(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

### A Storm of Swords

King Robb marries [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling) at the [Crag](wiki:Crag), breaking his promise to marry a Frey girl. On his return to [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), Robb tells Catelyn that if Stevron was alive he felt he could have made amends with the Freys over the broken marriage contract. However, the other Freys, bar [Olyvar](wiki:Olyvar_Frey), are unreasonable.(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Robb often recalls how Stevron died bravely serving him.(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

During the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), Catelyn offers to exchange Stevron's second son, [Aegon Frey](wiki:Aegon_Frey_(son_of_Stevron)), a [fool](wiki:Fool) nicknamed Jinglebell, for the wounded Robb, saying, *"I will spare your boy's life for Robb's. A son for a son."*(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), who has already lost his first son and heir, Stevron, replies, *"A son for a son,*heh*. But that's a grandson..."*(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) When Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) kills Robb, Catelyn responds by sawing through the throat of Aegon.(wiki:Stevron_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)
