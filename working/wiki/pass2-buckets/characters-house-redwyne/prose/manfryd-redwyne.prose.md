## Origins

Lord Manfryd was named [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships), as well as [lord admiral](wiki:Lord_admiral),(wiki:Manfryd_Redwyne.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) by King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) after the former master of ships, Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)), was raised to [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). This made Manfryd the first man raised to the position of master of ships that was not a member of [House Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon).(wiki:Manfryd_Redwyne.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

Manfryd had at least three sons, all squires when he brought them to court with him in [50 AC](wiki:50_AC): [Robert](wiki:Robert_Redwyne), [Rickard](wiki:Rickard_Redwyne), and [Ryam](wiki:Ryam_Redwyne).(wiki:Manfryd_Redwyne.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

After Daemon resigned the office of Hand, some suggested that rivalry with Lord Manfryd played a part in his decision.(wiki:Manfryd_Redwyne.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Lord Manfryd served as master of ships until his own death in [89 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Manfryd_Redwyne.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) His eldest son, Robert, succeeded him as Lord of the Arbor whereas Daemon's grandson, [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), followed him as master of ships.(wiki:Manfryd_Redwyne.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
