## Appearances & Description

### Appearance[ ]

Dragon eggs are large eggs with tiny scales, which shimmer like polished metal in the sunlight.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) Dragon eggs come in a variety of rich colors. The color of the eggshell appears to indicate the coloring of the dragon within: from the deep green egg, with burnished bronze flecks(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) hatches [Rhaegal](wiki:Rhaegal), a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) with jade-green scales(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) and wings,(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) and bronze eyes;(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) from the pale cream egg streaked with gold and bronze(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) hatches the dragon [Viserion](wiki:Viserion), who has golden eyes(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) and cream-colored and golden-colored scales;(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) from the black egg with scarlet ripples and swirls(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) hatches the dragon [Drogon](wiki:Drogon), whose scales(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) and teeth(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) are black, and whose horns, spinal plates, and eyes are red.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)
The three eggs given to [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) are warm to her touch,(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) although other people do not feel the heat.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)
Dragons have been known to lay eggs in a clutch of up to at least five,(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and a dragon may lay several clutches during its lifetime.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Although the subject of [dragon mating habits](wiki:Dragon) is debatable, some [maesters](wiki:Maesters) believe that if a dragon never laid an egg in its lifetime, it must be male.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Whether an egg has to be fertilized by a male dragon in order to hatch is not known.

### Hatching[ ]

There were dragon hatcheries on the island of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) under the [Dragonmont](wiki:Dragonmont), where many eggs and young hatchling dragons could be found.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
Grand Maester [Benifer](wiki:Benifer) believed that dragon eggs might not hatch if far away from the heat of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), explaining that some instead turn to stone.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
When King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) attempted to hatch a dragon egg, according to [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) he sat on one of the eggs for a day and a night.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
After the extinction of their dragons in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen) made numerous attempts to hatch the dragon eggs left behind. King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), the Dragonsbane, sent away for nine mages from [Essos](wiki:Essos) at the suggestion of his [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen). However, the attempt to use their [magic](wiki:Magic) to kindle a clutch of eggs proved both a debacle and a failure.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) prayed over his eggs, while other Targaryens sought to hatch theirs with sorcery,(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) and still others had [maesters](wiki:Maesters) study them.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) The [tragedy of Summerhall](wiki:Tragedy_of_Summerhall) has been blamed on King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen)'s attempt to hatch dragons.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) During his reign, King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) tried to hatch dragons from the eggs that had found in the depths of Dragonstone, some of which had turned to stone because of their great age, but his attempts yielded nothing.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)
Fossilized dragon eggs are more precious than [rubies](wiki:Ruby)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) and could be used to purchase ships.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

### Targaryen Traditions[ ]

Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) supposedly placed a dragon egg in the cradles of her younger siblings [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) shortly after their birth,(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Her brother, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), placed an egg in the cradle of his newborn son, [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), in [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) During the reign of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), it became truly customary to place a dragon's egg in the cradle of a newborn child.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)
It was considered a sign that a child was a true Targaryen if an egg placed within their cradle hatched.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) If the egg did not hatch, however, it was considered an ill omen.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)The tradition of placing an egg in the cradle of a newborn royal continued even after the [last dragon](wiki:Last_dragon) died and the eggs stopped hatching.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) These dragon eggs were cherished possessions of their owners.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Elaena_25-0)

## Origins

### First century after the Conquest[ ]

Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) placed a dragon egg in the cradles of her siblings [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) after they were born. According to the stories, from those eggs hatched [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor) and [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing).(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) Jaehaerys later placed a dragon egg in the cradle of his son [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)).(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)
By [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre) , the dragon of Rhaena Targaryen, had already produced two clutches of eggs.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) During Rhaena's time at [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle) ([49](wiki:49_AC)–[50 AC](wiki:50_AC)), Dreamfyre laid another clutch of eggs. Dreamfyre's presence on the island was making some of the residents anxious, however, and the new clutch of eggs caused a [begging brother](wiki:Begging_brother) to start preaching that Fair Isle "would soon be overrun by dragons" who would eat both sheep and men, and called for a [dragonslayer](wiki:Dragonslayer) to kill the [dragons](wiki:Dragon) to stop it all. The man was arrested, but nonetheless his words inspired fear wherever they were spread.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) When Rhaena departed Fair Isle, she took the eggs with her as she toured the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands). After some time at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), Lord [Lyman Lannister](wiki:Lyman_Lannister) expressed his interest in the three eggs and asked how they would hatch, and when that could be expected. Lady [Jocasta Lannister](wiki:Jocasta_Lannister) suggested to Rhaena that gifting the Lannisters one or more of the three eggs would be a good way to demonstrate her gratitude. After Rhaena refused, Lord Lyman offered to buy the eggs.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) Rhaena was eventually granted [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) as her own seat.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-2) There, the eggs hatched.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3)
In [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), Lady [Elissa Farman](wiki:Elissa_Farman) stole three dragon eggs from the [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) hatcheries and fled with them to [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), from where she traveled to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos). At Braavos, Elissa sold the eggs to the [Sealord](wiki:Sealord), and used the gold to build herself a [ship](wiki:Sun_Chaser). The news about the theft was alarming to King Jaehaerys; if the eggs were to hatch, there would be a [dragonlord](wiki:Dragonlord) in the world that was not of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). Rewards were offered for rumors on the location of the dragon eggs, but every whisper proved worthless.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-4) By [56 AC](wiki:56_AC), Jaehaerys suspected that the dragon eggs had played a part in the financing of the ship. The banker denied all.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) In [57 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the newly appointed [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth), traveled to Braavos and conferred with the Sealord about the eggs, warning that King Jaehaerys would bring destruction upon Braavos should the eggs hatch. The Sealord denied having purchased the eggs, and claimed that, unhatched, dragon eggs were nothing more than "pretty stones". Barth eventually returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) without the eggs, but with the crown's entire loan from the [Iron Bank](wiki:Iron_Bank) forgiven.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2)

### Reign of Viserys I[ ]

During the reign of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), there were more dragons than ever before. Several of the she-dragons regularly produced clutches of eggs. Many, but not all, of the eggs hatched. During Viserys's reign, it became truly customary to place a dragon's egg in the cradle of a newborn child.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)
When his concubine [Mysaria](wiki:Mysaria) became pregnant in [106 AC](wiki:106_AC), Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) presented her with a dragon egg. King Viserys I commanded him to return the egg and return to lawful his wife, Lady [Rhea Royce](wiki:Rhea_Royce), or else be attainted as a traitor. Mysaria was then sent back to [Lys](wiki:Lys), without the egg.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5) Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen)'s three sons by [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon) were each given a dragon egg while in the cradle by royal decree; those who believed the boys' father truly was Ser [Harwin Strong](wiki:Harwin_Strong) whispered that the eggs would never hatch, but they all did, and all three the boys became [dragonriders](wiki:Dragonrider). Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) rejected the "stupid" eggs and "puny" hatchlings of Dragonstone, preferring the great dragon [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar). Dragon eggs were placed in the cradles of Prince [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_II)), Princess [Jaehaera](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen), and Prince [Maelor Targaryen](wiki:Maelor_Targaryen).(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-6) All the eggs except for the ones of Viserys and Maelor hatched.

### Dance of the Dragons[ ]

Prince Daemon's daughter, Lady [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)), had an egg as well, but the dragon that was born from the egg died within hours. Shortly before the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Rhaena had been given an egg from [Syrax](wiki:Syrax)'s most recent clutch. Rhaena slept with the egg every night, and prayed over it.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) When she left for the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), she brought three eggs with her and prayed nightly for them to hatch.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) One of these eggs hatched during the war, bringing forth the dragon [Morning](wiki:Morning).(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)
According to [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom), the dragon [Vermax](wiki:Vermax) left a clutch of dragon's eggs at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) when his rider [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) came to visit Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark). Grand Maester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) and Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) feel that Mushroom's tale can be discounted, and consider Vermax to have been a male dragon, as there is no record of the dragon ever laying another clutch of eggs.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)
Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) took his egg everywhere he went(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) and brought it with him on the [Gay Abandon](wiki:Gay_Abandon). When he was taken captive, he hid his dragon egg.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)
When Prince Maelor Targaryen fled [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) under the care of Ser [Rickard Thorne](wiki:Rickard_Thorne), the two were discovered and identified at [Bitterbridge](wiki:Bitterbridge) when a stableboy found Maelor's egg in Ser Rickard's saddle bags. After both Prince Maelor and Ser Rickard had been killed, Lady [Caswell](wiki:House_Caswell) sent the dragon egg to Lord [Ormund Hightower](wiki:Ormund_Hightower) at [Longtable](wiki:Longtable), hoping to soothe his wroth.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)
King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) commanded the [maester](wiki:Maester) of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) to send him dragon eggs at King's Landing, so he could hatch another egg. The maester sent him seven eggs, in honor of [the gods](wiki:The_Seven).(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2)
[Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon_(daughter_of_Alyn)) was presented a dragon egg upon her birth at [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) in [135 AC](wiki:135_AC), but it hatched a blind and wingless wyrm, white as a maggot. Within moments of hatching, the monster tore a bloody chunk from the infant Laena's arm, and her father Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) ripped it off her and hacked it to pieces. Troubled upon hearing this news, King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) commanded that no dragon eggs were to be allowed in the Red Keep, and forced his brother Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) to send his unhatched egg away to Dragonstone.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

### Recent History[ ]

The [last Targaryen dragon](wiki:Last_dragon) laid a clutch of five eggs.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) After the death of the last dragon, multiple Targaryen kings attempted to hatch dragon eggs, including [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen),(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) [Baelor I](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen),(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2) [Aegon V](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen),(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2) and [Aerys II](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)
During the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), the thief [Quickfinger](wiki:Quickfinger) was caught with stolen dragon eggs.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)
Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) and his brothers were all gifted dragon eggs while in the cradle. He and his brother [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) would often pretend that their eggs would be the ones to hatch.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)
[A tourney](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) was held at [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls) in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC) to celebrate the wedding of Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell) to [the daughter](wiki:Lady_Frey_(wife_of_Ambrose_Butterwell)) of [Lord Frey](wiki:Lord_Frey). The victor's prize was to be a dragon egg, which had been granted to [Lord Butterwell's grandfather](wiki:Butterwell_(Hand_of_the_King)) by King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen). In truth, the wedding and tourney was an attempt to secretly foment the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in the name of [Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre), who had [dreamed](wiki:Dragon_dream) that a dragon would be born at [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls). The tourney was rigged so that Daemon would win the dragon egg, but it went missing before his last joust, and Ser [Glendon Ball](wiki:Glendon_Ball) was framed for the theft. After the defeat of the attempted rebellion, Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), informed Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) that the egg had been safely taken by an agent of his.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)
On the island of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)), there is supposedly a fabled hoard of dragon eggs.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Radwd72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

At [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen)'s wedding to Khal [Drogo](wiki:Drogo) outside [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), Magister [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis) gives her a gift of three fossilized dragon's eggs.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) tells Daenerys that one of her eggs could buy a ship to take her back to the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities). The sale of all three would make her a wealthy woman for the rest of her life.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) After Drogo's death and the death of [their son](wiki:Rhaego), Daenerys places the eggs on her husband's funeral pyre: [the black](wiki:Drogon) beside his heart, under his arm, [the green](wiki:Rhaegal) beside his head, [the cream-and-gold](wiki:Viserion) down between his legs. After the pyre is lit, Daenerys walks into the fire, and the eggs hatch dragons,(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2) the first since the death of the [last dragon](wiki:Last_dragon).(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

At the [Wall](wiki:Wall), [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) ponders that Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) had visited [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) on her dragon, [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing), and that King [Jaehaerys I](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), had come after her on his own dragon, [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor). Samwell wonders to himself as to whether Silverwing could have left an egg behind, or as to whether King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) had found an egg on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)
Lord [Hallyne](wiki:Hallyne), of the [Alchemists' Guild](wiki:Alchemists'_Guild), asks Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) that his pyromancers be allowed to hatch any dragon's eggs that might turn up upon [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) during the search of the castle. Cersei remarks that if any such eggs had remained, [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) would have sold them to pay for his rebellion. She refrains from saying that the plan is mad, as every attempt made by the Targaryens to hatch a dragon after the death of the last dragon had ended in death, disaster, or disgrace.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)
[Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) claims to have held a dragon's egg in his hand. A [Myrish](wiki:Myr) wizard swore he could hatch it if Euron gave him a year and all the gold that he required. Euron claims that he killed the man when he grew bored with the wizard's excuses, and that had thrown the egg in the sea during one of his dark moods.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

According to Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell), the men of his his son, Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell), searched every inch of Dragonstone, but did not find any sign of the fabled hoard of dragon eggs. Lord Regent [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) privately doubts Mace's protestations.(wiki:Dragon_egg.cite_ref-Radwd72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1)
