## Origins

On his deathbed Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) famously commanded his men to boil the flesh from his skull, dip it in gold, and carry it before them when they crossed the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) to retake [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). Each of his successors as captain-general has followed this example; upon their deaths their golden skulls adorn the standard poles of the Golden Company.(wiki:Golden_skulls.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

When [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) arrives at the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company)'s encampment near [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys) he contemplates the golden skulls. One is larger than the rest – [Maelys the Monstrous](wiki:Maelys_the_Monstrous) and his nameless brother. The other skulls have a sameness to them, though several are cracked and splintered by the blows that slew them, and one has filed pointed teeth.(wiki:Golden_skulls.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
When Jon looks at the skull of his old friend, [Myles Toyne](wiki:Myles_Toyne), he sees that death has robbed Myles of his ears, his nose, and all his warmth. Only his smile remains, transformed into a glittering grin. Jon thinks all the skulls are grinning, even [Bittersteel's](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) on the tall pike in the center.(wiki:Golden_skulls.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
During the [landing of the Golden Company](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company), the sellswords hide their skulls and golden battle standards to sow confusion in the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands).(wiki:Golden_skulls.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
