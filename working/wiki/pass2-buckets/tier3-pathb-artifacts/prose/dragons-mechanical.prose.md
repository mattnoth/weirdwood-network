## Origins

Like many other [Targaryen](wiki:Targaryen) kings after the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Aegon was obsessed with regaining [dragons](wiki:Dragon). However, instead of trying to resurrect the dead dragons of his ancestors, Aegon turned to the [pyromancers](wiki:Pyromancers) and commanded them to "build me dragons". The Guild built seven wood-and-iron monstrous engines, fitted with pumps that shot jets of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire). Though the "dragons" might have been useful in a siege, instead Aegon ordered these devices dragged to the [Boneway](wiki:Boneway) to initiate an invasion of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) in [174 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). The king's plan lacked any tactical sense, as the Boneway was too steep for the lumbering constructs. The man-made dragons did not even get that far, due to instability of wildfire and the difficulty of moving the massive siege engines. All seven were consumed by fire in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood); hundreds of men operating them burned alive inside them and a quarter of the kingswood went up in flames. Aegon, after this humiliation, never spoke of conquering Dorne again.(wiki:Dragons_(mechanical).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
