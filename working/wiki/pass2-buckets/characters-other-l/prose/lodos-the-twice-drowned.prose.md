## Origins

The would-be monarch [Lodos](wiki:Lodos) had walked into the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea) and disappeared, early in the reign of King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

After King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) succeeded to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), a man claiming to be Lodos the Twice-Drowned, returned from the halls of the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God), led a [revolt](wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned's_revolt) in the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) against the new king. The revolt, however, was swiftly put down by [Goren Greyjoy](wiki:Goren_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), who descended with a hundred longships on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk) and [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk) where most of Lodos's followers were concentrated. Thousands of his disciples were put to the sword. Lord Goren sent the pickled head of Lodos to the Iron Throne.(wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
