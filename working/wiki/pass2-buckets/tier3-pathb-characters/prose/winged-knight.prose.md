## Origins

Among the hundreds of stories told about him, the Winged Knight is said to have flown upon a huge [falcon](wiki:Falcon) and had armies of [eagles](wiki:Eagle) at this command. He won the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) by slaying the [Griffin King](wiki:Griffin_King) atop the [Giant's Lance](wiki:Giant's_Lance). He is said to have been friendly with [giants](wiki:Giants) and [merlings](wiki:Merlings). His wife, one of the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest), died giving birth to his son.(wiki:Winged_Knight.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
The existence of the Winged Knight is questioned by Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), who includes the knight with other legendary heroes such as [Lann the Clever](wiki:Lann_the_Clever) and [Bran the Builder](wiki:Bran_the_Builder). Archmaester [Perestan](wiki:Perestan) theorized that the story of a huge falcon derives from [dragonriders](wiki:Dragonrider).(wiki:Winged_Knight.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Yandel suggests that singers combined the figure of the Winged Knight with that of Ser [Artys Arryn](wiki:Artys_I_Arryn), who won the Vale for the [Andals](wiki:Andals) from the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) in the [Battle of the Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_the_Seven_Stars), to curry favor with the [Kings of Mountain and Vale](wiki:Kings_of_Mountain_and_Vale) from [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn).(wiki:Winged_Knight.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) The Arryn legends state the Winged Knight drove the First Men from the Vale.(wiki:Winged_Knight.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Young Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn)'s favorite stories are those of the Winged Knight. He demands three stories of Ser [Artys Arryn](wiki:Artys_Arryn) from [Alayne Stone](wiki:Sansa_Stark) before he agrees to prepare for his departure from the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). Alayne agrees she will only tell the stories after they reach the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon).(wiki:Winged_Knight.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### The Winds of Winter[ ]

After Alayne notices that Robert is always comforted when she reads him tales of his legendary ancestor, Ser Artys Arryn, the Winged Knight, she has the idea that he would feel safer if he were surrounded by his own bodyguard of Winged Knights. Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), [regent](wiki:Regent) for Robert, likes her idea and immediately set plans in motion to form the [Brotherhood of Winged Knights](wiki:Brotherhood_of_Winged_Knights). The winners of a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) at the Gates of the Moon will become the first eight members of the new knightly order, and serve Robert for the next three years. Sixty-four knights from across the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) come to the tourney to compete for the eight positions.(wiki:Winged_Knight.cite_ref-Winds_3-0)
