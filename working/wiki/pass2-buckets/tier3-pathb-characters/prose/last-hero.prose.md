## Origins

Legends of the [north](wiki:North) state the last hero and his companions went in search of the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) during the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night), thousands of years ago. The only survivor of the company after attacks from [giants](wiki:Giants), [wights](wiki:Wights), and [Others](wiki:Others), the last hero eventually reached the children and gained their assistance. The [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) then formed and won the [Battle for the Dawn](wiki:Battle_for_the_Dawn). This ended the generation-long winter and sent the Others into retreat,(wiki:Last_hero.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_long_night.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) possibly to the [Land of Always Winter](wiki:Land_of_Always_Winter). The fate of the last hero is unknown.

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

The tale of the last hero is told by [Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan) to [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark):
Oh my sweet summer child … What do you know of fear? Fear is for the winter, my little lord, when the snows fall a hundred feet deep and the ice wind comes howling out of the north, when the sun hides it face for years at a time, and little children are born and live and die all in darkness while the [direwolves](wiki:Direwolf) grow gaunt and hungry, and the [white walkers](wiki:Others) move through the woods.
The [Others](wiki:Others) … Thousands and thousands of years ago, a winter fell that was [cold and hard and endless beyond all memory of man](wiki:Long_Night). There came a night that lasted a generation, and kings shivered and died in their castles even as the swineherds in their hovels. Women smothered their children rather than see them starve, and cried, and felt their tears freeze on their cheeks.
In that darkness, the Others came for the first time … They were cold things, dead things, that hated iron and fire and the touch of the sun, and every creature with hot blood in its veins. They swept over holdfasts and cities and kingdoms, felled heroes and armies by the score, riding pale dead horses, and leading hosts of the slain. All the swords of men could not stay their advance, and even maidens and suckling babes, found no pity in them. They hunted the maids through the frozen forests, and fed their [dead servants](wiki:Wights) on the flesh of human children.
Old Nan is then interrupted by Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin).(wiki:Last_hero.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

At [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) tells [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) that he found one account of the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night) that spoke of the last hero slaying [Others](wiki:Others) with a blade of [dragonsteel](wiki:Dragonsteel). Supposedly they could not stand against it. Jon wonders if dragonsteel refers to [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel).(wiki:Last_hero.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
