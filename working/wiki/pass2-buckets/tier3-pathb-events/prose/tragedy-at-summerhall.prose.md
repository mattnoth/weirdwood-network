## Aftermath

The tragedy that transpired at Summerhall left very few witnesses alive, and those who survived would not speak of it.(wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) The death of King Aegon brought [Jaehaerys II Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen) to the throne, but he had to put aside his grief and make immediate plans for the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings). The young Ser [Gerold Hightower](wiki:Gerold_Hightower) became the new Lord Commander of the Kingsguard.(wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3)
Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), who was born at Summerhall amongst the flames,(wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) often returned there alone, with not even the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) for company. He would sleep under the stars in the ruined hall, and would be inspired to compose songs for his [harp](wiki:Harp), about "twilights and tears and the death of kings."(wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

On [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), [Alester Florent](wiki:Alester_Florent) asks [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) if nothing was learnt from the tragedy of Summerhall. He states that no good has ever come of dreams of [dragons](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
While encamped outside of [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai), [Arstan Whitebeard](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) tells [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) that her brother Rhaegar was considered melancholic at times, that he had a sense of doom. Dany recalls that her brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) had spoken of Rhaegar's birth only once, she wonders to herself if perhaps the tale had saddened Viserys too much. Dany asks Arstan if it was the shadow of Summerhall that haunted Rhaegar. Arstan tells her yes, but that [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall) was the place that the prince loved best.(wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)
In the [Riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), the [ghost of High Heart](wiki:Ghost_of_High_Heart) asks [Tom of Sevenstreams](wiki:Tom_of_Sevenstreams) to play "my [Jenny's song](wiki:Jenny's_song)", and listens to it while rocking back and forth, murmuring the words and crying. She tells [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) that she gorged on grief at Summerhall.(wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

In [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), Maester Aemon mentions to [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) that he once thought that Rhaegar could be the [prince that was promised](wiki:Prince_that_was_promised) due to the smoke from the fire that devoured Summerhall on the day of Rhaegar's birth and the salt from the tears shed for those who died, a reference to the "born amidst salt and smoke" part of the [prophecy](wiki:Prophecy).(wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

In [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) asks Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) what became of the woods witch who foretold that the prince that was promised would be born from her [mother](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) and [father](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen)'s line. Ser Barristan tells her only "Summerhall", in a voice of doom.(wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) Later, Barristan thinks of the marital choices of the sons of King [Aegon the fifth](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), which led to treason and turmoil, ending at Summerhall in sorcery, fire, and grief.(wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
