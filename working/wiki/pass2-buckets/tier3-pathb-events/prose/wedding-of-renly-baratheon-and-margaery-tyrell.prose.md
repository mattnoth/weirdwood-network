## Origins

[Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End), and his lover, Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell),(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-1) unsuccessfully plot for Renly's eldest brother, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), to put aside Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) and instead wed Loras's sister, [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell).(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Ragot32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
After Robert is mortally wounded by a [boar](wiki:Boar), Renly approaches Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), and suggests they seize control of the royal children before Cersei can strike.(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Eddard refuses the offer, however, and Renly flees [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) along with Loras. Cersei has Eddard [arrested](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark) after Robert's death,(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and King [Joffrey I Bratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) eventually has Lord Stark executed at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor).(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
Renly, Loras, and Loras's kin are among the nobles ordered to swear fealty to Joffrey, Renly's nephew, in King's Landing.(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Instead of returning to the capital, Renly journeys with Loras to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell)'s seat in the [Reach](wiki:Reach), and he is proclaimed king by Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) and his sons.(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) [Brienne Tarth](wiki:Brienne_Tarth), who has fallen in love with her liege lord, rides across the Reach to join Renly after learning he has been crowned.(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

## Aftermath

During their parley amid Stannis's [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), Renly claims that Margaery was a [maid](wiki:Maid) on their wedding day.(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Renly is assassinated by a [shadow](wiki:Shadow_child) during the siege.(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Loras buries Renly's body in a place near [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
As part of an alliance between Houses Tyrell and [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister), the widowed Margaery agrees to marry King Joffrey. Ser [Garlan Tyrell](wiki:Garlan_Tyrell) publicly states his sister remains a maiden, as Renly went to war before the marriage was consummated.(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Margaery claims that Renly had been too drunk on [wine](wiki:Wine) to bed her.(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
Instead of wearing a cloak in the colors of [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon) as Renly's widow, Margaery wears a [maiden's cloak](wiki:Maiden's_cloak) in the colors of House Tyrell when she marries Joffrey. The boy king dies during the [royal wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding),(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) however, making Margaery twice widowed. Queen Regent Cersei does not believe Mace's claim that Margaery is still a maid(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) or Margaery's claim that Renly never touched her.(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
Margaery next marries Joffrey's younger brother, King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) [Taena Merryweather](wiki:Taena_Merryweather) later tells Cersei, Tommen's mother, about the night of Renly and Margaery's wedding. Taena claims that she helped Renly disrobe for the [bedding](wiki:Bedding) custom, and that Loras had carried his sister to the bedchamber. Taena casts doubt on Margaery's claim, but admits there was no bloody sheet the next morning to prove the bride had lost her maidenhead.(wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)
