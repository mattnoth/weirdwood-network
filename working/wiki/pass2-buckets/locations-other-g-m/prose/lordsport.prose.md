## Origins

During the reign of [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) as [Kings of the Iron Islands](wiki:Kings_of_the_Iron_Islands), ships from Lordsport began to trade at [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), and the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

[Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) sailed to Oldtown, Lannisport, and Lordsport when he was a young man.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

During [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), the timber-and-wattle castle of [House Botley](wiki:House_Botley) and the village of Lordsport [were razed](wiki:Battle_at_Lordsport) by the forces of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) before the [battle at Pyke](wiki:Battle_at_Pyke).(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) After the war, Lordsport was rebuilt by its [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), who used the stones from the burned remnants and cut new thatch for their roofs. [Otter Gimpknee's inn](wiki:Otter_Gimpknee's_inn) at the harbor doubled in size. The [sept](wiki:Sept) was not rebuilt, however, as the [ironmen](wiki:Ironmen) prefer the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God) over the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), the religion of the victorious Robert.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) Lord [Sawane Botley](wiki:Sawane_Botley) rebuilt his stronghold in stone.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

## Appearances & Description

Half the size of [Lord Hewett's Town](wiki:Lord_Hewett's_Town),(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Lordsport is located near a wooded point with [pine](wiki:Pine)-clad bluffs. Lordsport provides safe anchor for those who wish to reach the castle of [Pyke](wiki:Pyke), seat of [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy). [Otter Gimpknee's inn](wiki:Otter_Gimpknee's_inn) is located near the landing.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

The town is known for its forges and metalworkers, who produce excellent swords, axes, ringmail, and plate.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) [Sigrin](wiki:Sigrin) is a prominent shipwright in Lordsport.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

The seat of [House Botley](wiki:House_Botley) is a small square stone keep atop a hill overlooking the town; the banners of the Botleys droop from the castle's squat corner towers.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Returning to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) for the first time since [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) sails on the *[Myraham](wiki:Myraham)* from [Seagard](wiki:Seagard) to Lordsport. Theon, the heir of [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), is displeased that the merchants of Lordsport do not recognize him. [Aeron Damphair](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) escorts his nephew from Lordsport to Pyke.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

Lord Balon musters [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) longships to Lordsport in preparation for his invasion of the [north](wiki:North) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) Balon prevents merchant vessels like the *Myraham* from leaving the harbor and informing the [green lands](wiki:Green_lands) of the gathering of ironborn ships.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

Theon acquires a horse, [Smiler](wiki:Smiler), from [Sawane Botley](wiki:Sawane_Botley), [Lord of Lordsport](wiki:Lord_of_Lordsport), and takes on the mute [Wex Pyke](wiki:Wex_Pyke), the bastard of Sawane's half-brother [Sargon](wiki:Sargon_Botley), as his squire.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Theon sees his newly-built longship, later named *[Sea Bitch](wiki:Sea_Bitch)*, on the strand of Lordsport. [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy), calling herself "Esgred" and claiming to be the daughter of [Ambrode](wiki:Ambrode) and wife of [Sigrin](wiki:Sigrin), escorts her unknowing brother from the town to Pyke, where she reveals her true identity.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

### A Storm of Swords

The captain of the *[Myraham](wiki:Myraham)* reports that [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) sailed into Lordsport's harbor the day after the death of his brother Balon, [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North). Euron claimed the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair) at Pyke and drowned Lord Botley in a cask of seawater when he objected.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Feast for Crows

It is reported that Euron had Sawane killed because he recognized Balon's heir to be Theon, not Euron.(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) [Ralf of Lordsport](wiki:Ralf_of_Lordsport) comes from the town, whose new lord is [Germund Botley](wiki:Germund_Botley).(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Asha, a claimant to the Seastone Chair, intends to restore Lordsport to Sawane's son and heir, her childhood friend [Tristifer Botley](wiki:Tristifer_Botley).(wiki:Lordsport.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
