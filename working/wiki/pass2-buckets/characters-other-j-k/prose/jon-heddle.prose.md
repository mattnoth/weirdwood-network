## Origins

Long Jon Heddle took over the [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads) when he retired from adventuring. He also took up [ironworking](wiki:Iron), and forged a sign in the shape of a three-headed [dragon](wiki:Dragon). The black iron sign would clank in the wind, and gave his inn its new name, the Clanking Dragon.(wiki:Jon_Heddle.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

In [196 AC](wiki:196_AC), when Long Jon's son was an old man, the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) broke out. Lord [Darry](wiki:House_Darry), who was a fierce [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) supporter, saw the inn's sign and thought it resembled the [coat of arms](wiki:Coat_of_arms) of [the rebels](wiki:House_Blackfyre) (a three-headed black dragon on a red field), so he cut it down and hacked it to pieces.(wiki:Jon_Heddle.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

The inn at the crossroads remained in the hands of Jon Heddle's descendants for several generations. One of these descendants, [Masha Heddle](wiki:Masha_Heddle), told the tale of Jon Heddle and his family's connection to the inn to [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Meribald](wiki:Meribald), who himself passed it along to [Brienne](wiki:Brienne_Tarth) of [Tarth](wiki:Tarth), Ser [Hyle Hunt](wiki:Hyle_Hunt), and [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne).(wiki:Jon_Heddle.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
