## Origins

Marya is the daughter of a carpenter. She married [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth), who was a smuggler at the time. They had seven children together: [Dale](wiki:Dale_Seaworth), [Allard](wiki:Allard_Seaworth), [Matthos](wiki:Matthos_Seaworth), [Maric](wiki:Maric_Seaworth), [Devan](wiki:Devan_Seaworth) (b. [287 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Seaworth) (b. [289 AC](wiki:289_AC) or [290 AC](wiki:290_AC)), and [Steffon](wiki:Steffon_Seaworth) (b. [293 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)).(wiki:Marya_Seaworth.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

In [283 AC](wiki:283_AC), after the end of [Siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Davos was [knighted](wiki:Knight) by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), and received a keep and lands at [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath). Marya became mistress of the keep, where she now resides with their two youngest children.(wiki:Marya_Seaworth.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Marya_Seaworth.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Allard's ship, *[Lady Marya](wiki:Lady_Marya)*, is probably [named after](wiki:Named_after) his mother.

## Appearances & Description

Marya is a good-natured plump woman, with sagging breasts, and a kindly smile. Her husband Davos feels she is the best woman in the world.(wiki:Marya_Seaworth.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

[Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan) tells [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) the legend of [Azor Ahai](wiki:Azor_Ahai) and [Nissa Nissa](wiki:Nissa_Nissa) and the forging of [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer). When Davos thinks of Nissa Nissa, he pictures his wife Marya, and shudders at the idea of killing her, deciding it was much more than he cared to pay for a [magic](wiki:Magic) sword.(wiki:Marya_Seaworth.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

### A Storm of Swords

Davos, a survivor of the Battle of the Blackwater, remembers his four sons who died during the battle, but finds hope remembering his wife and living sons.(wiki:Marya_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Salladhor Saan is angered that Davos refuses his suggestion to go home to his wife and children in favor of revenge on [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), and says when Davos dies he will bring his bones back to Cape Wrath for his sons to wear in bags around their necks.(wiki:Marya_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

On [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), Davos finds himself thinking about Marya and their two young sons almost every day, and part of him wishes he could take [Devan](wiki:Devan_Seaworth) and go home.(wiki:Marya_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) He thinks if he survives telling [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) he has helped [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm) escape, he will return to [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath) and Marya, grieve their dead sons together, raise his living sons to be good men, and speak no more of kings.(wiki:Marya_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

In [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), Davos thinks how he could have abandoned Stannis's cause to return to his wife Marya and their young sons.(wiki:Marya_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

While imprisoned in the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den), believing he is to be executed, Davos writes letters to his wife and three surviving sons. His letter to Marya says that he loves her, and is sorry for the wrongs he did to her. He tells her that if Stannis Baratheon loses the war, she should take the boys to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos). If Stannis should gain the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Devan will help her find [fosterage](wiki:Fosterage) for the boys with noble lords. He wishes he had wiser advice to give her.(wiki:Marya_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
