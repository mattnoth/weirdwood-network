## Origins

### The Faith Militant uprising

In [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), during the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) [uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), Joffrey was chosen as the new [Grand Captain](wiki:Grand_Captain) of the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) to replace the late Ser [Damon Morrigen](wiki:Damon_Morrigen). He was determined to restore the order to its former glory and rode with a hundred men to seek the blessing of the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I)). By the time he arrived at [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), he had amassed two thousand followers of knights, squires and [freeriders](wiki:Freerider).(wiki:Joffrey_Doggett.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

By [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), the Warrior's Sons were outlawed and no longer had the strength to defy the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in open battle, so the Red Dog sent them out disguised as [hedge knights](wiki:Hedge_knight) to hunt down and kill Targaryen loyalists and "traitors to the Faith". Former Warrior's Son Ser [Morgan Hightower](wiki:Morgan_Hightower), Old Lord [Merryweather](wiki:House_Merryweather), Lord [Peake](wiki:House_Peake)'s son and heir, Ser [Davos Darklyn](wiki:Davos_Darklyn)'s aged father and Blind [Jon Hogg](wiki:Jon_Hogg) were all killed by Doggett's followers.(wiki:Joffrey_Doggett.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

By late [45 AC](wiki:45_AC), Joffrey ruled the hill country north of the [Golden Tooth](wiki:Golden_Tooth) in all but name.(wiki:Joffrey_Doggett.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

[Septon](wiki:Septon) [Moon](wiki:Moon_(septon)) and Joffrey led the [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) against the king in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC).(wiki:Joffrey_Doggett.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

### Early reign of Jaehaerys I

Joffrey survived the war against [Maegor the Cruel](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). Given safe conduct, he journeyed to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) with Lord [Prentys Tully](wiki:Prentys_Tully) and his lady wife, [Lucinda Broome](wiki:Lucinda_Broome), to meet the new young king [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), who had come to Oldtown to receive the [High Septon](wiki:High_Lickspittle)'s blessing. The Red Dog was received courteously by the king and he asked that the [laws](wiki:Law_and_justice) forbidding the Faith Militant be undone. Jaehaerys refused, but asked Doggett instead to serve in his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). When Joffrey drew his sword, [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Benifer](wiki:Benifer), who was present, feared Joffrey might intend to harm Jaehaerys. Instead, he placed the sword at the king's feet and, with tears in his eyes, accepted the offer.(wiki:Joffrey_Doggett.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)(wiki:Joffrey_Doggett.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

### Kingsguard

Joffrey guarded King Jaehaerys during the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding), where it was announced that the remaining five slots of the Kingsguard were to be filled in melees that became known as the [War for the White Cloaks](wiki:War_for_the_White_Cloaks). One of the five victors, a young man named Ser [Pate the Woodcock](wiki:Pate_the_Woodcock), was accused by some of not truly being a knight, but such was the lad's skill that Joffrey personally knighted Pate to end the accusations. Hundreds of onlookers cheered and Pate was given the white cloak of the Kingsguard.(wiki:Joffrey_Doggett.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

When Princess [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) discovered her betrothal plans, she informed Jaehaerys, who acted immediately and ordered Ser [Gyles Morrigen](wiki:Gyles_Morrigen), the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), to discreetly travel to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) under the cover of darkness with the rest of the white cloaks. Jaehaerys and Alysanne later flew to the island. On [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), Doggett and the Kingsguard stood witness as Jaehaerys and Alysanne [married](wiki:Marriage), with [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Oswyck](wiki:Oswyck) performing the ceremony. When Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) and Queen Regent [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) arrived and tried to take the royal children back, Joffrey and his sworn brothers defied the [King's Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) by forming a wall in front of their king and queen, informing Rogar that he would be the first to die if his men attacked.(wiki:Joffrey_Doggett.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

During Jaehaerys's minority on Dragonstone, Joffrey and his sworn brothers sparred with their young king, who spent every morning until midday in the castle yard training with sword, shield and any other weapon. In the book allegedly written by Lady [Coryanne Wylde](wiki:Coryanne_Wylde), *[A Caution for Young Girls](wiki:A_Caution_for_Young_Girls)*, the author claimed to have bedded both Joffrey and his sworn brother Ser Pate the Woodcock, although most maesters dismiss these claims.(wiki:Joffrey_Doggett.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

After reaching his [majority](wiki:Age_of_majority), the king went on a [royal progress](wiki:Royal_progress) of the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) and [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) with the queen. During their stay in [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), Joffrey and Ser Gyles Morrigen guarded the entrance of [Jonquil's Pool](wiki:Jonquil's_Pool) when Queen Alysanne and her ladies entered to bathe in the sacred waters. Both men rushed in when they heard screams. Three sisters of the order who attended the pools had tried to kill Alysanne, but her ladies shielded her so the queen was unharmed. Joffrey and Gyles slew two of the attackers and spared the third for questioning.(wiki:Joffrey_Doggett.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

When the [High Septon](wiki:High_Lickspittle) passed away in [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), Jaehaerys and Alysanne traveled to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) upon their [dragons](wiki:Dragon) to pay their respects and to influence the choice of the next [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon). They took two protectors with them. To balance the weight for the dragons, [Jonquil Darke](wiki:Jonquil_Darke) rode with King Jaehaerys on [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor), while Joffrey rode with Queen Alysanne on [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing).(wiki:Joffrey_Doggett.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

During the harsh winter of [59 AC](wiki:59_AC) and the ensuing plague of the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers), Joffrey rode out with King Jaehaerys and the rest of his escort when the news that the [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), Lord [Rego Draz](wiki:Rego_Draz), had been brutally murdered. King Jaehaerys was enraged at the sight of Lord Rego's mutilated corpse. When he saw the king's face, Joffrey thought for a moment that he was looking at the face of King [Maegor the Cruel](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). The men responsible for the murder were later caught and hanged from the walls of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) with their entrails dangling out.(wiki:Joffrey_Doggett.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
