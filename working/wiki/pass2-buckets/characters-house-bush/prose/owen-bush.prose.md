## Origins

In [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), when Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) escaped from King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen)'s imprisonment, the king ordered his knights to bring her son, Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), to his [torturers](wiki:Torture) to discover where she was hiding.(wiki:Owen_Bush.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

In [45 AC](wiki:45_AC), Queen [Ceryse Hightower](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower) died suddenly. A rumor went around the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) that she had said something to offend Maegor. It was widely believed that the king had ordered Ser Owen to [remove her tongue](wiki:Tongue_mutilation), but Ceryse's struggles caused his knife to slip and slash her throat. However, most historians believe this was a slander concocted by the king's enemies to damage Maegor's reputation.(wiki:Owen_Bush.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

In [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), Maegor suspected Queen [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower) of betrayal. He had Owen and his sworn brother, Ser [Maladon Moore](wiki:Maladon_Moore), bring her to the dungeons, where she confessed.(wiki:Owen_Bush.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

That same year, Maegor the Cruel gradually lost political support, resulting in a [rival threat](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen's_uprising) in his nephew, Prince [Jaehaerys Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen). Two of his Kingsguard defected to Jaehaerys, and Maegor lost a third guard when Owen was found dead outside a [brothel](wiki:Brothel) in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), his member cut off and stuffed in his mouth.(wiki:Owen_Bush.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
