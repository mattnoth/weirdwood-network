## Origins

Gyles has been married at least twice but remains childless.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) The aunt of his second wife is Lady [Tanda Stokeworth](wiki:Tanda_Stokeworth), who is herself third cousin to Gyles.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

[Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), once suggested Lord Rosby as a potential husband for his daughter, Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

## Appearances & Description

Gyles is an old, wealthy man. He is sickly and coughs constantly,(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) including into a square of pink [silk](wiki:Silk),(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) a square of scarlet silk trimmed with golden lace,(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and a square of red silk.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Lord Rosby owns a litter with rich carvings and silk draperies. According to [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), Gyles's [horses](wiki:Horse) are better dressed than most [knights](wiki:Knights).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Besides his castle at [Rosby](wiki:Rosby), Gyles also has spacious apartments in the [Kitchen Keep](wiki:Kitchen_Keep) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Rasos58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lord Gyles remains at court in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) when fighting breaks out in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

### A Clash of Kings

Gyles attends the [tourney on King Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_King_Joffrey's_name_day), and he tries to cheer on Prince [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) when the boy rides against a [quintain](wiki:Quintain).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) During a small supper held by Lady [Tanda Stokeworth](wiki:Tanda_Stokeworth), Lord Rosby leads a toast to King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) When he receives his copy of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s letter concerning the illegitimacy of Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s children with King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Gyles forwards it to the [small council](wiki:Small_council).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Due to the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), King's Landing is supplied mainly by Lord Rosby and Lady Stokeworth, for their lands are far enough away from the fighting to be untouched by the war.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Gyles is present when [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) offers his terms for peace for Ser [Cleos Frey](wiki:Cleos_Frey) to take back to [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

Lord Gyles survives the [riots in King's Landing](wiki:Riots_in_King's_Landing) after the royal procession sees Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) off to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). Upon returning to the Red keep, Gyles mentions that he saw the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(fat_one)) overwhelmed by the mob.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

After the riots, Cersei fears for her son's life and decides to send Prince Tommen in secret to [Rosby](wiki:Rosby), dressing him as a page and putting him under the protection of Gyles. Tyrion finds out about the plan from Ser [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister), however, and has Gyles and his entourage intercepted on the [Rosby road](wiki:Rosby_road) by Ser [Jacelyn Bywater](wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Tyrion later decides to release Gyles to appease Cersei.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

During the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Lord Rosby stays in the [Queen's Ballroom](wiki:Queen's_Ballroom) with Cersei and the other noble ladies. He drinks too much and passes out in a trencher of hollowed bread, which disgusts Cersei.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### A Storm of Swords

Gyles is an attendant at the wedding of [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Lord Rosby accompanies Tyrion when he is sent to meet the delegation from Dorne.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Tyrion displaces Gyles of his spacious apartments at the [Kitchen Keep](wiki:Kitchen_Keep) in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Rasos58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

During the [wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) and [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), a melon lands in Lord Rosby's lap when [dwarfs](wiki:Dwarfs) reenact the war. Joffrey later chokes to death during the feast.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

Gyles is called as a witness for the prosecution in the trial of Tyrion for Joffrey's death. He tells of how he saw Tyrion fill the [wedding chalice](wiki:Wedding_chalice).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

### A Feast for Crows

After the funeral of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), Gyles tells Queen Regent Cersei he has commissioned a statue of Lord Tywin to be placed by the [Lion Gate](wiki:Lion_Gate) in King's Landing. Rosby's coughing has gotten much worse and he is beginning to cough up blood.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

Cersei learns that Tywin had named [Garth Tyrell](wiki:Garth_Tyrell) to become the new [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin). In order to prevent another [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) from joining the [small council](wiki:Small_council), she lies to Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell), telling him she has already named Lord Gyles as the new master of coin to the council instead.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

Lord Rosby attends the wedding of King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) and Lady [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) at the [royal sept](wiki:Royal_sept).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

Gyles intends to replace many officials who had been appointed by Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish),(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) and Cersei has him finance [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn)'s experiments on Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5) Cersei styles Lord Rosby as [lord treasurer](wiki:Lord_treasurer).(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

[Noho Dimittis](wiki:Noho_Dimittis), an envoy from the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos), meets with Gyles six times. Noho is irate when he learns from Cersei that she would suspend all payments on the loans from the Iron Bank until the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) is over.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) The envoy hounds Gyles so much that he takes to his bed coughing up blood.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

Fearing that the Tyrells will force Garth as the new lord treasurer if Gyles dies, Cersei orders [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) to keep Lord Rosby alive.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Despite the [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester)'s treatments,(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) however, Gyles is overcome by his sickness and dies at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). Cersei threatens Pycelle by insinuating that he killed Lord Rosby, manipulating the Grand Maester against her rival, Margaery. Gyles does not leave behind an immediate heir, but he does have a [ward at Rosby](wiki:Ward_of_Gyles_Rosby). Cersei claims that Lord Rosby's wish was to leave his lands and wealth to her son, King Tommen. Cersei names Ser [Harys Swyft](wiki:Harys_Swyft) as the lord treasurer.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

### A Dance with Dragons

Six claims have been put forth for the Rosby inheritance.(wiki:Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
