## Origins

Munkun's True Telling was penned about a generation after the events it chronicled. [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) regarded it by far the most detailed and authoritative of the many accounts that survived in his day. The exhaustive history drew on many different sources, including maesters' chronicles, memoirs, stewards' records, and interviews with one hundred forty-seven eye witnesses to the events. Much of Munkun's work drew upon the confessions of [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle), and eye-witness account, yet one whose author was anxious to portray himself in a favorable light and absolve himself of any misdeeds. Although Orywle himself was thus portrayed too favorably by Munkun by Gyldayn's estimation,(wiki:The_Dance_of_the_Dragons,_A_True_Telling.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) his writings were nonetheless an invaluable resource to Munkun.(wiki:The_Dance_of_the_Dragons,_A_True_Telling.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) It may have contained other inaccuracies, such as the illogical claim that [Ser](wiki:Knight) [Byron Swann](wiki:Byron_Swann) had attempted to slay [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), rather than a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) on the side of the [blacks](wiki:Blacks).(wiki:The_Dance_of_the_Dragons,_A_True_Telling.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Orwyle's account was biased in favor of Rhaenyra, however, just as Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons))'s [history of the Dance](wiki:The_Reign_of_King_Viserys,_First_of_His_Name,_and_the_Dance_of_the_Dragons_That_Came_After) was biased in favor of Aegon II.(wiki:The_Dance_of_the_Dragons,_A_True_Telling.cite_ref-4)
Though [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) believed the titular conflict was more properly termed "the Dying of the Dragons," Munkun's work helped raise the more poetic naming into the pages of history.(wiki:The_Dance_of_the_Dragons,_A_True_Telling.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

When [Haldon](wiki:Haldon) Halfmaester asks [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) if he can tell him the name of the knight who tried the same ploy [Serwyn of the Mirror Shield](wiki:Serwyn_of_the_Mirror_Shield) used on the dragon [Urrax](wiki:Urrax) with the dragon [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Tyrion answers that it was Ser [Byron Swann](wiki:Byron_Swann), who was roasted for his trouble, but he corrects the halfmaester by stating that the dragon had been [Syrax](wiki:Syrax), not Vhagar. Haldon tells him he is mistaken, mentioning The Dance of the Dragons, A True Telling by Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) as his source, only to be interrupted by Tyrion, who tells him that Grand Maester Munkun was wrong.(wiki:The_Dance_of_the_Dragons,_A_True_Telling.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Tyrion explains that Byron's squire saw his master die and wrote of the manner of his death. The squire's account says that the dragon was Syrax, [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen)'s she-dragon, which makes much more sense than Munkun's version as Swann was the son of a [marcher lord](wiki:Marcher_lords), and [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) was for [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). Vhagar was ridden by Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), Aegon’s brother, making it, according to Tyrion, unlogical that Swann would have wanted to slay her.(wiki:The_Dance_of_the_Dragons,_A_True_Telling.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
