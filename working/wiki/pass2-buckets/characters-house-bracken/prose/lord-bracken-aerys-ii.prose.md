## Origins

In [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) or [277 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Lord Bracken and [Lord Blackwood](wiki:Lord_Blackwood_(Aerys_II)) travelled to [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) to lay their feud before their liege lord, [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully). During their stay, Lord Hoster announced the [betrothal](wiki:Betrothal) of his twelve-year-old daughter, [Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Tully), to the heir of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark).(wiki:Lord_Bracken_(Aerys_II).cite_ref-Rawoiafcatelyn_stark.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Lord_Bracken_(Aerys_II).cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Lord_Bracken_(Aerys_II).cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

When a border dispute over a mill arose, the two lords came to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to seek judgment from the Crown. The [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), arbitrated in favor of Lord Blackwood but was overruled by King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) who gave the disputed mill to Lord Bracken.(wiki:Lord_Bracken_(Aerys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Lord Bracken offered to Lord Hoster the hand of his daughter in [marriage](wiki:Marriage) for his younger brother, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully). Brynden refused the match, however.(wiki:Lord_Bracken_(Aerys_II).cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
