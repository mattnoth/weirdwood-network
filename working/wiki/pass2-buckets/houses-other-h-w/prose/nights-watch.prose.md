## Origins

### The Long Night

The Night's Watch is one of the oldest orders in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), as it survived the fall of the kingdoms of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals), and [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest). It was founded over eight thousand years ago, at the end of the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night). Under cover of an endless night that lasted for a generation, the [Others](wiki:Others) invaded from the [Lands of Always Winter](wiki:Lands_of_Always_Winter), laying waste to much of Westeros. The Others were finally defeated by the Night's Watch at the [Battle for the Dawn](wiki:Battle_for_the_Dawn), which is recalled in "[The Night That Ended](wiki:The_Night_That_Ended)".(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) The [Wall](wiki:Wall) was allegedly built by [Bran the Builder](wiki:Bran_the_Builder) in order to protect the Seven Kingdoms,(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) with the brothers of the Night's Watch [choosing](wiki:Choosing) their own [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) since that time.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) During the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes) it was also recorded that the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) gave the Night's Watch a hundred [obsidian](wiki:Obsidian) daggers every year.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-3)

Other than the corrupting of the thirteenth Lord Commander, the so-called [Night's King](wiki:Night's_King), further attacks by the Others never came, however. Instead, the most frequent attacks came from the [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings), sometimes led by their [Kings-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:Kings-Beyond-the-Wall), and their constant attempts at raiding in the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1) Little by little, the Night's Watch forgot that its main mission was not the fight against the wildlings, but against the Others.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)

### Hundred Kingdoms

The Night's Watch built nineteen castles along the hundred leagues of the Wall. At the zenith of its power, the Watch had seventeen of the castles manned,(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Racok43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-1) with at least ten thousand men between them. Castle Black alone once quartered five thousand fighting men with all their horses, servants, and equipment.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-5) The highborn of the north have traditionally considered it an honor to serve on the Wall. Many younger sons of northern houses, low in the line of succession, gladly took the black. Shields of nobles from the [Hundred Kingdoms](wiki:Hundred_Kingdoms) of Westeros were proudly displayed in the [Shieldhall](wiki:Shieldhall) at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-2)

Six kings were sent to the Wall after [Nymeria's War](wiki:Nymeria's_War) and the unification of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) by [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_the_coming_of_the_rhoynar.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)

### House Targaryen

The [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) were invaded by the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen) during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest). Although [Lord Commander Hoare](wiki:Lord_Commander_Hoare) was the brother of [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare), [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), the lord commander maintained the neutrality of the ten thousand black brothers under his command, even when Harren died during the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2) King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) became [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms) and ruled from the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Aegon's sister-wife, Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), modeled the vows of the newly-created [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) on those of the Night's Watch.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

In [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) suppressed the [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) chapter of the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons). When the king offered the two hundred knights the option of renouncing the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) and taking the black, or remaining loyal to their faith and face execution, three-quarters of the knights chose to join the Watch.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) Scores of [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) took the black when [Poxy Jeyne Poore](wiki:Poxy_Jeyne_Poore) was hunted down in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood) two years later.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-1)

When [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) succeeded Maegor on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), four of Maegor's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard)—Ser [Olyver Bracken](wiki:Olyver_Bracken), Ser [Symond Crayne](wiki:Symond_Crayne), Ser [Raymund Mallery](wiki:Raymund_Mallery), and Ser [Jon Tollett](wiki:Jon_Tollett)—joined the Watch to avoid execution. Hundreds of Poor Fellows, including [Rob the Starvling](wiki:Rob_the_Starvling), also accepted the clemency of the new king and took the black.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0) In [49 AC](wiki:49_AC), thirty black brothers accompanied Lord [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(father_of_Walton)) to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) for the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) The following year, Ser Olyver and Ser Raymund led [Rimegate](wiki:Rimegate) and [Sable Hall](wiki:Sable_Hall) in rebellion against the Night's Watch. Most black brother remained loyal, however, and Rimegate was retaken with the aid of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), and Lord [Walton Stark](wiki:Walton_Stark_(son_of_Brandon)) beheaded Olyver with [Ice](wiki:Ice). Lord Stark pursued Raymund [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall), but Walton was slain by [giants](wiki:Giants) during the campaign. [Wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) eventually sent Raymund's head to [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0)

During King Jaehaerys's royal progress to the north in [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) flew on [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing) to visit Castle Black and the Wall. Lord Commander [Lothor Burley](wiki:Lothor_Burley) escorted her into the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest), and [Benton Glover](wiki:Benton_Glover) showed Alysanne the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort), which she felt should be abandoned because of its poor condition. Alysanne paid for the construction of [Deep Lake](wiki:Deep_Lake) with her own jewels, and Lothor renamed Snowgate to [Queensgate](wiki:Queensgate) in her honor.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) Alysanne also visited Brandon's Gift, the land south of the Wall given to the Watch for their sustenance. Impressed by the Night's Watch but concerned about their lack of resources, Alysanne convinced King Jaehaerys and Lord [Alaric Stark](wiki:Alaric_Stark) to double the lands with another twenty-five leagues, the New Gift.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-1) According to records at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), the Starks were reluctant to do so, fearing that the Night's Watch would neglect the Gift by focusing their attention beyond the Wall.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0)

Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) suggested that the [greens](wiki:Greens), the rivals of Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and the [blacks](wiki:Blacks), be allowed to join the Night's Watch amid the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). Rhaenyra and her husband, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), decided that their enemies should not be pardoned, however.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0) In the aftermath of the Dance, twenty-two men were arrested by Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) for the death for King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). During this [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf), nineteen of them,(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0) led by Ser [Perkin the Flea](wiki:Perkin_the_Flea), were sent to the Wall by Cregan, with only Lord Corlys being spared and Ser [Gyles Belgrave](wiki:Gyles_Belgrave) and Lord [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong) instead choosing death.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0) Grand Maester [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle) deserted before setting sail on *[North Star](wiki:North_Star)*, so Ser [Medrick Manderly](wiki:Medrick_Manderly) ordered the guard who had freed him to take his place in the Watch.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0)

In [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), the Night's Watch dealt with several wildling incursions as famine threatened during winter.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-1) The following year, a hundred [rangers](wiki:Rangers) helped hunt down the raiders of [Sylas the Grim](wiki:Sylas_the_Grim) after they overwhelmed Queensgate.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0) A third of the Night's Watch died during the winter of [134 AC](wiki:134_AC), and hundreds of the black brothers were killed fighting the thousands of wildlings who crossed the frozen [Shivering Sea](wiki:Shivering_Sea).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0) In [136 AC](wiki:136_AC) during the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), Ser [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long), Lord [George Graceford](wiki:George_Graceford), and several [gold cloaks](wiki:Gold_cloaks) and [Fingers](wiki:Fingers_(guards)) chose to take the black after the [secret siege](wiki:Secret_siege).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-1)

Guilty of treason after the [Third Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Third_Blackfyre_Rebellion), Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) was commanded by King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen) to take the black. Bittersteel was freed en route to the Wall, however, and he fled to the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0) Bittersteel's rival, Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), was sent to the Wall by King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) after the murder of [Aenys Blackfyre](wiki:Aenys_Blackfyre).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1) Bloodraven, many of his [Raven's Teeth](wiki:Raven's_Teeth), and prisoners from the dungeons formed the two hundred men who accompanied Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), Aegon's elder brother, to the Wall.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-2)

The black brothers arrived late at [Long Lake](wiki:Battle_at_Long_Lake), where [Raymun Redbeard](wiki:Raymun_Redbeard), the [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall), was defeated by the Starks and [Umbers](wiki:House_Umber) in [226 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_wildlings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0)

### House Baratheon

Only the [north](wiki:North) have the memory of the old days, but even they believe the [Others](wiki:Others) are no more than vague figures in stories told to frighten children.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-1) Only a few of the houses south of the [Neck](wiki:Neck) have members in the Night's Watch, and most serve because they fell afoul of political machinations or fought on the wrong side of a war. However, there have been fewer wars since [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-71) Northmen such as the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Racok43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-2)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-2) and [Norreys](wiki:House_Norrey)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0) are more likely to hold the Watch in high regard than [southrons](wiki:Southron);(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-0) [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister), for instance, has not been a strong supporter of the Wall.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) Some southerners are still friends of the Watch, however, such as Lady [Shella Whent](wiki:Shella_Whent).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Racok14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1)

By the reign of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), only three castles remain in use—Castle Black, [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea), and the [Shadow Tower](wiki:Shadow_Tower)—and the Night's Watch's numbers have dwindled to fewer than a thousand men.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-11) The Night's Watch is now largely made up of the misfits of the Seven Kingdoms, such as [bastards](wiki:Bastard), debtors, killers, [outlaws](wiki:Outlaws), [peasants](wiki:Smallfolk), poachers, rapers, and thieves.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-0)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2)

The [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), and the [First Ranger](wiki:First_Ranger), Eddard's brother [Benjen](wiki:Benjen_Stark), have discussed settling new [lords](wiki:Lord) in abandoned holdfasts of the [Gift](wiki:Gift) if the lord commander, [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), approves.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-2)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-0) Skeptical of the Night's Watch, some wise men have suggested that the lords of the north should be granted the lands beyond the Wall and tasked with defending against wildlings.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-3)

According to Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) the Wall is a world apart, and news oft reaches them late.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-0) Stories of the Night's Watch are collected in Archmaester [Harmune](wiki:Harmune_(archmaester))'s *[Watchers on the Wall](wiki:Watchers_on_the_Wall)*.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-4)

## Organization

### Structure

The Night's Watch consists of three orders: [stewards](wiki:Stewards), [builders](wiki:Builders), and [rangers](wiki:Rangers). All are subject to the [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), and each of the three orders is led by its own officer, the [Lord Steward](wiki:Lord_Steward), the [First Builder](wiki:First_Builder), and [First Ranger](wiki:First_Ranger), respectively. These officers are appointed by the lord commander.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

- [Stewards](wiki:Stewards) are responsible for an assortment of critical functions, providing vital day-to-day services. They hunt and farm, tend horses, gather firewood, cook meals, make clothing, maintain weapons, and bring supplies needed by the Night's Watch from the South.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Like other members of the Watch, the stewards must be ready to fight at a moment's notice, and all have received at least basic combat training. [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh) serves as the [Lord Steward](wiki:Lord_Steward) or First Steward.

- [Builders](wiki:Builders) are responsible for tending to and maintaining the Wall and its castles. The order provides masons, carpenters, miners, and woodsmen to this end.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) [Othell Yarwyck](wiki:Othell_Yarwyck) leads them as the [First Builder](wiki:First_Builder).

- [Rangers](wiki:Rangers) are the main fighting force, adept at surviving in the wilderness and tasked with scouting and patrolling the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest) beyond the Wall. They actively defend the Wall and ride out to face the Watch's enemies, including [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) and legendary [Others](wiki:Others).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) The [First Ranger](wiki:First_Ranger) is [Benjen Stark](wiki:Benjen_Stark).

Recruiters for the Night's Watch are called [wandering crows](wiki:Wandering_crow).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The organization also includes [septons](wiki:Septon), [maesters](wiki:Maesters), and, at [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea), sailors. Because brothers come from throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) or even [Essos](wiki:Essos), there is an unspoken pact to not discuss politics or former loyalties.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) When war breaks out in the Seven Kingdoms, the Night's Watch tries to remain neutral by honoring all kings and obeying none.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rawoiafmen_of_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

One blast of a sentry's horn represents returning rangers,(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasosprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) while two are used for wildlings(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) and three for [Others](wiki:Others).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Two long blasts followed by a short one are used as a call to mount horses.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

While wildlings and the Watch are often seen as seen as ancient enemies,(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) the black brothers are friendly with some free folk, such as [Craster](wiki:Craster)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and [Haggon](wiki:Haggon).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwdprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) There is greater commerce between the two peoples than generally known,(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) and some wildlings trade at Eastwatch.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwdprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

### Leadership

The [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) is the final authority and oversees the entire order. A lord commander serves in office until the day he dies, when a replacement is elected by the men of the Watch. Any man of the Night's Watch can be nominated to be the lord commander.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

At [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), Lord Commander [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont) is advised by First Ranger [Benjen Stark](wiki:Benjen_Stark), First Builder [Othell Yarwyck](wiki:Othell_Yarwyck), and Lord Steward [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh). He also entrusts leadership to Ser [Jarman Buckwell](wiki:Jarman_Buckwell), Ser [Mallador Locke](wiki:Mallador_Locke), Ser [Jaremy Rykker](wiki:Jaremy_Rykker), [Thoren Smallwood](wiki:Thoren_Smallwood), and Ser [Ottyn Wythers](wiki:Ottyn_Wythers).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Ser [Denys Mallister](wiki:Denys_Mallister) is the commander at the [Shadow Tower](wiki:Shadow_Tower), while [Cotter Pyke](wiki:Cotter_Pyke) has command at [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea). [Maesters](wiki:Maesters) advising the senior leadership are [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) at Castle Black, [Mullin](wiki:Mullin) at the Shadow Tower, and [Harmune](wiki:Harmune) at Eastwatch.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

An honorable opportunity to command, rise in rank, and enjoy hunting north of the Wall are reasons why men join the Night's Watch voluntarily.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) The majority of the officers and leadership of the Watch are pulled from the upper crust of Westerosi society. A noble or [knighted](wiki:Knight) man is almost guaranteed a position as an officer in the Watch,(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) but there are several powerful and influential brothers that are of common birth as well, such as the senior rangers [Qhorin Halfhand](wiki:Qhorin_Halfhand), [Blane](wiki:Blane), and Cotter Pyke, a [bastard](wiki:Bastard)-born pirate. The Watch, as a meritocracy, is one of the few places in [feudal](wiki:Feudalism) Westeros where a [common man](wiki:Smallfolk) can rise high.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### Recruitment

Once, serving on the Wall was honor and a sign of selfless devotion to duty, with many [knights](wiki:Knight), honorable men, and nobles taking the black voluntarily. The Night's Watch is now often seen only as a way to avoid punishment, suitable less for knights than for the dregs of Westeros. These men are salvaged from dungeons by traveling recruiters known as [wandering crows](wiki:Wandering_crow).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Disgraced nobles, bastards, and the unwanted legitimate offspring of nobles can be encouraged to take the black, making many of today's Watch a surly and dissatisfied lot.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

Those who come voluntarily are free to leave during any time of their training, but no man may leave after he has said vows.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) Any deserters are sentenced to death.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) After taking the vows, the men of the Watch cannot own any land, marry, or father children. Men are also encouraged to sever any ties left with their families, if they are lucky enough to have one.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

### Vows

When recruits are considered ready to take the black, they say their vows either in a [sept](wiki:Sept) or before a [heart tree](wiki:Heart_tree). The vows are as follows:

> Night gathers, and now my watch begins. It shall not end until my death. I shall take no wife, hold no lands, father no children. I shall wear no crowns and win no glory. I shall live and die at my post. I am the sword in the darkness. I am the watcher on the walls. I am the fire that burns against the cold, the light that brings the dawn, the horn that wakes the sleepers, the shield that guards the realms of men. I pledge my life and honor to the Night's Watch, for this night and all the nights to come.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

Once a man takes his vows, his crimes and debts are forgiven.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) Brothers of the Night's Watch serve for life.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) It is customary to finish a black brother's eulogy with the words, "And now his watch is ended."(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) contains a [lichyard](wiki:Lichyard),(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) and [knights](wiki:Knight) can be laid to rest in ancient tombs near the lichyard or burned on a pyre.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) According to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin), the Night's Watch would not give its oaths to a boy that is seriously minor, like a twelve year old;(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rssm1054.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) the only known exception is [Osric Stark](wiki:Osric_Stark), who was elected [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) at the age of ten.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)

### Military Strength

The [numbers](wiki:Military_strength) of the Night's Watch have long been dwindling.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), the Night's Watch numbered ten thousand strong.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) However, by [298 AC](wiki:298_AC), their strength has dwindled to less than a thousand.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) Of these thousand, six hundred are stationed at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), two hundred at the [Shadow Tower](wiki:Shadow_Tower), and fewer at [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3)

In late [233 AC](wiki:233_AC), when Ser [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) was sent to the Wall, two hundred men went with him. Many of them were archers from Rivers's personal guard, the [Raven's Teeth](wiki:Raven's_Teeth).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

There are plans to re-garrison the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort) and many of the other castles. The question of who will be responsible for the remanned castles is a point of tension between Jon and King Stannis Baratheon.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_88-0) Jon beheads [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt) for insubordination.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_89-0)

Many of the brothers are disconcerted by Jon allowing wildlings like [Tormund](wiki:Tormund) to pass through the Wall and garrison the empty castles.(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_90-0)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_91-0)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_92-0)(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_93-0) Jon arranges the marriage of [Alys Karstark](wiki:Alys_Karstark) to [Sigorn](wiki:Sigorn) of the [Thenns](wiki:Thenns).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) The lord commander sends [Cotter Pyke](wiki:Cotter_Pyke) on a dangerous mission to rescue wildlings at [Hardhome](wiki:Hardhome).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_94-0) Jon asks for volunteers to march on [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) after receiving a [threatening letter](wiki:Bastard_Letter) from [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), but the lord commander is then stabbed by brothers in a [mutiny at Castle Black](wiki:Mutiny_at_Castle_Black).(wiki:Night's_Watch.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-3)
