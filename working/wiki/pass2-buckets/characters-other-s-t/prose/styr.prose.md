## Origins

[Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) had to defeat Styr three times before the [Magnar](wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn) agreed to support him as [King-beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-beyond-the-Wall).(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Styr](wiki:Category:Images_of_Styr)*

Styr is a tall, lean man. He is clean shaven and bald, with grey eyes and a straight nose. Styr has lost both of his ears.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

The [Magnar of Thenn](wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn) rules with an iron fist and commands absolute loyalty from his men.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) He is seen more as a [god](wiki:God) than a man by the [Thenns](wiki:Thenns).(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Coming from [a valley](wiki:Thenn_(valley)) in the [Frostfangs](wiki:Frostfangs),(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Styr has never seen the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) He speaks the [Old Tongue](wiki:Old_Tongue) as well as the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue).(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Styr wears [bronze](wiki:Bronze) scale [armor](wiki:Armor) and a helm.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) He wields a [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) spear with an ornate bronze head(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) and slings a two-handed greatsword across his back in a leather sheath.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) The Magnar also carries a long knife.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

When [Rattleshirt](wiki:Lord_of_Bones) brings [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) to [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder), Jon thinks Styr is Mance, mistakenly calling him "Your Grace". Styr is wary of Jon, who claims to have deserted the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

Mance tasks Styr, together with [Jarl](wiki:Jarl), to scale the [Wall](wiki:Wall) and take [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) from the rear, and the [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall) has Jon accompany them.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Styr has a hundred [Thenns](wiki:Thenns), five times as many raiders as Jarl.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Jarl and Styr share the command, although Styr, who commands significantly more men than Jarl, resents this and often acts as the sole commander.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Styr distrusts [Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte)'s support for Jon.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) In a [cave near Greyguard](wiki:Cave_near_Greyguard), Jarl and Styr interrogate Jon about patrols of the Night's Watch.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

Jarl falls to his death while attempting to scale the Wall. Styr leads the surviving wildlings in descending at [Greyguard](wiki:Greyguard).(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Styr orders Jon to kill a captive [northman](wiki:Northman) at [Queenscrown](wiki:Queenscrown), but Jon refuses and escapes the wildlings.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Jon is able to warn the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) of the coming attack.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Styr leads his men in an [attack on Castle Black](wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black). During the fighting, Jon sees the Magnar climbing the barricade constructed by the defenders. The Night's Watch trap Styr and his Thenns between fires on the steps, however. Styr is last seen as a portion of the Wall collapses on the lower third of the stair.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

[Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) informs Jon that the new Magnar of Thenn was taken captive during the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall).(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Styr's son, [Sigorn](wiki:Sigorn), is the new [Magnar of Thenn](wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn).(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Jon, now the new [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), fears that Sigorn blames him for his father's death,(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) and he recollects that Styr put [Mole's Town](wiki:Mole's_Town) to the torch.(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Radwd21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Jon arranges the [wedding of Sigorn and Alys Karstark](wiki:Wedding_of_Sigorn_and_Alys_Karstark).(wiki:Styr.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
