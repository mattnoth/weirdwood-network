## Origins

Samantha was the daughter of Lord [Alyn Stokeworth](wiki:Alyn_Stokeworth) of [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth), who was [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) at the end of King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen)'s reign, and at the beginning of the reign of his successor, King [Aenys I](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen). At court, she became a favorite of Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)).(wiki:Samantha_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Samantha arrived on [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle) at the beginning of [49 AC](wiki:49_AC) for Rhaena's [wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Rhaena_Targaryen_and_Androw_Farman) to [Androw Farman](wiki:Androw_Farman).(wiki:Samantha_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) She stayed with the princess at [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle), where she, Rhaena, [Alayne Royce](wiki:Alayne_Royce), and [Elissa Farman](wiki:Elissa_Farman) became nigh inseparable and often rode Rhaena's [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre) together. Ser [Franklyn Farman](wiki:Franklyn_Farman) dubbed the four friends as, "the [Four-Headed Beast](wiki:Four-Headed_Beast)".(wiki:Samantha_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

When Rhaena left Fair Isle upon the ascension of Lord Franklyn, Samantha came with her. At [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), she was asked by a castle [septa](wiki:Septa) if the marriage between Princess Rhaena and Androw had ever been [consummated](wiki:Bedding). Samantha was later part of Rhaena's court on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Samantha_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Samantha died at the end of [54 AC](wiki:54_AC). At first, it was thought she had died of an [illness](wiki:Illness). Later, it was revealed that she had been [poisoned](wiki:Poison) by Androw Farman, who was jealous of Rhaena's friendships with her ladies and weary of their mockery toward him.(wiki:Samantha_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
