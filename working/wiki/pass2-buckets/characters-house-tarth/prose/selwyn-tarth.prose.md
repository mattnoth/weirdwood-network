## Origins

Selwyn's daughters [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Tarth) and [Arianne](wiki:Arianne_Tarth) died in infancy. His son [Galladon](wiki:Galladon_Tarth) drowned when he was eight years old, leaving Brienne as his only living child.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

After the death of his wife, Selwyn took a new [mistress](wiki:Mistress) every year. One mistress came from across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea); she had a slim waist, [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes), and a lovely voice.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Lord Tarth had a [septa](wiki:Septa), [Roelle](wiki:Roelle), who educated Brienne. He also allowed his [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms), Ser [Goodwin](wiki:Goodwin), to train Brienne at arms.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) He kept a singer every year of Brienne's childhood, which led her to memorize all the songs.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

Selwyn [betrothed](wiki:Betrothed) Brienne to the second son of Lord [Bryen Caron](wiki:Bryen_Caron), but the boy died of a [chill](wiki:Disease) when he was twelve.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Selwyn then tried to wed Brienne to Ser [Ronnet Connington](wiki:Ronnet_Connington), but the [knight](wiki:Knight) rejected her for her appearance.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) He last tried an older man for her, Ser [Humfrey Wagstaff](wiki:Humfrey_Wagstaff), but Brienne broke her betrothal to Wagstaff by defeating him in combat. After that, Lord Selwyn stopped trying to arrange a marriage for Brienne.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

Selwyn hosted [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) at [Evenfall Hall](wiki:Evenfall_Hall) during the [Lord Paramount of the Stormlands](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Stormlands)'s [coming of age](wiki:Coming_of_age) [progress](wiki:Progress).(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Some time later, Selywn sent Brienne to serve Renly at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

## Appearances & Description

Selwyn is said to be a good man.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) He has large hands.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

When Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) is sent to ask after the allegiance of the [stormlords](wiki:Stormlords), Lord Selwyn only agrees to a midnight meeting in a grove. The Evenstar informs Davos of his refusal to join [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Selwyn instead supports Stannis's brother, [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon).(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Selwyn's daughter, [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth), becomes one of Renly's [Rainbow Guard](wiki:Rainbow_Guard).(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) During the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), Renly claims he is supported by Tarth arrows.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

At [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) and Brienne discuss Selwyn.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

### A Storm of Swords

Brienne and Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) are captured by the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions), and Jaime protects Brienne from rape by claiming that Selwyn will ransom her with [sapphires](wiki:Sapphire), drawing on [Tarth](wiki:Tarth)'s epithet of "the Sapphire Isle". After they are brought to [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) sends a [raven](wiki:Raven) to Tarth demanding a ransom. Lord Selwyn responds by offering three hundred [gold](wiki:Gold) [dragons](wiki:Currency) for Brienne's safe return. [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat) rejects the offer, however, believing Selwyn is trying to cheat him by withholding sapphires.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) ridicules Brienne and advises her to return to her father at Tarth.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) The [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)) of the [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle) also recommends that Brienne give up her quest and return home, saying that her father would rather have a living daughter than her broken [shield](wiki:Shield). Brienne tearfully explains her family's history and her life since Tarth, and that she must find [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) or die trying.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)

After being captured by the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners), Brienne claims that Selwyn will ransom her [squire](wiki:Squire), [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne), with sapphires, [silver](wiki:Silver), or whatever they want.(wiki:Selwyn_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)
