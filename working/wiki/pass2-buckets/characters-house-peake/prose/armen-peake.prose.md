## Origins

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), Lord Armen joined his strength to King [Mern IX Gardener](wiki:Mern_IX_Gardener)'s host against [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen). However, Armen and his sons perished in the resulting [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire), along with King Mern IX and the male line of [House Gardener](wiki:House_Gardener).(wiki:Armen_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

With Armen's death, the slow decline of [House Peake](wiki:House_Peake) began, as the Peakes were no longer favored by their new liege lords, [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell).(wiki:Armen_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) House Peake protested against the rise of the Tyrells on account of their own distinguished lineage. However, they found no success, perhaps because Lord Armen had taken up arms against [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) on the Field of Fire.(wiki:Armen_Peake.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
