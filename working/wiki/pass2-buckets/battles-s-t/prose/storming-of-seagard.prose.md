## Origins

Six years after [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_Baratheon) claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) declared himself [King of the Iron Isles](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Isles). His younger brothers, [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) and [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), sneaked into [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) and [burned the Lannister fleet](wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet) at anchor, opening the western half of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) to [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) attack.

## Narrative Arc

[Rodrik Greyjoy](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy), the eldest son of Balon, led the assault on [Seagard](wiki:Seagard), the seat of [House Mallister](wiki:House_Mallister) in the [Riverlands](wiki:Riverlands). Beneath the walls of Seagard the battle was ferocious, but Lord [Jason Mallister](wiki:Jason_Mallister) slew Rodrik and drove his men back into the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea). It was the only time in three hundred years that the bell in the [Booming Tower](wiki:Seagard) was rung.(wiki:Storming_of_Seagard.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Aftermath

The battle at Seagard was a major setback for [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy). Rodrik was Balon's heir, and the failed assault on Seagard prevented the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) from grabbing any land in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:Storming_of_Seagard.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
