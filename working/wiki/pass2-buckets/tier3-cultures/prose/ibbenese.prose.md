## Appearances & Description

The Ibbenese are considered by [maesters](wiki:Maesters) to be different from the other races of mankind. They are a heavy, broad-shouldered, and broad-chested people. The Ibbenese are short in stature, seldom reaching a height more than five and a half feet. Their arms are long, while their legs are short and thick. Their skulls have sloping brows with heavy ridges, small sunken eyes, great square teeth, and massive jaws.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
The Ibbenese have the most body hair of all known peoples in the [known world](wiki:Known_world).(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Beneath their pale skins run dark blue veins, but covering their skin is dark and wiry hair. Ibbenese men have dark, wiry hair covering their arms, legs, chests, and backs, while Ibbenese women commonly have coarse dark hair, even on the upper lip.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) A myth falsely claims that Ibbenese women have six breasts.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) The [Red Book](wiki:Red_Book) states many Ibbenese women sold into [slavery](wiki:Slavery) were deemed too ugly to serve as bed slaves and too savage to use as field hands. As such, many enslaved Ibbenese women ended their days in the [fighting pits](wiki:Fighting_pits) of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai), and [Astapor](wiki:Astapor).(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
Although Ibbenese can reproduce with the people of other lands, the children Ibbenese men father upon the women of other lands are frequently malformed and almost(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-11) always sterile, while Ibbenese women, when impregnated by men from other lands, birth only stillbirths and monstrosities.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) Such couplings are uncommon, however.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) Some scholars have speculated that the very hairy [Skagosi](wiki:Skagos) have a large amount of Ibbenese blood in their heritage.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_stoneborn_of_skagos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) The [Pentoshi](wiki:Pentoshi) and most [maesters](wiki:Maesters) of the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) believe the Ibbenese are akin to the [hairy men](wiki:Hairy_men), though it is unknown if the hairy men settled [Ib](wiki:Ib) from Essos or if they originated on the island and then spread to the mainland.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

## Culture

Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) reports the Ibbenese to be a taciturn but cunning folk, composed of skilled craftsmen, able hunters and trackers, and doughty warriors.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) The Ibbenese speak [their own language](wiki:Ibbenese_language), which has been described as grunting and rasping.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
The Ibbenese are a suspicious people, and Ibbenese of old believed that a man would be cursed if he ventured inland away from the sound of the sea.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Visitors from foreign lands are restricted to the harbor precincts of the [Port of Ibben](wiki:Port_of_Ibben). They are only allowed to venture beyond the city in the company of an Ibbenese host, but such invitations are rarely made.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12) The Ibbenese who live in the woods and mountains of Ibben are even more suspicious of strangers. These foresters, goatherds, and miners prefer to make their homes in caves or houses of grey stone which they dig into the earth. The inland Ibbenese rarely live in towns, instead preferring to dwell in solitary compounds. They only gather for weddings, burials, and worship.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)
Despite their suspiciousness, the Ibbenese who live at the shores are a venturesome people. Fishermen travel the northern seas in search of [cod](wiki:Cod), [herring](wiki:Herring), [whitefish](wiki:Whitefish), and [eel](wiki:Eel). Most famously are the Ibbenese [whalers](wiki:Whale), whose big-bellied [ships](wiki:Ship) are a common sight in ports all over the world.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14) Ibbenese ships are renowned for their strength, as they are built to withstand any storm or any assault of a [leviathan](wiki:Leviathan), even the largest.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15) The Ibbenese are fond of chewing blubber.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rssm1226.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
The ferociously-strong Ibbenese excel over [Westerosi](wiki:Westerosi) at their favorite sport, wrestling.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16) Some Ibbenese are fond of carrying [axes](wiki:Armament) into battle,(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) as well as shaggy brown shields.(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Some Ibbenese have found employ as [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword) in the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions).(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

## Origins

There is evidence of Ibbenese settlements on the [Axe](wiki:Axe), the isles of [Lorath](wiki:Lorath), and along the shores of the [Bitterweed Bay](wiki:Bitterweed_Bay), the [Bay of Tusks](wiki:Bay_of_Tusks), [Leviathan Sound](wiki:Leviathan_Sound), and the [Thousand Islands](wiki:Thousand_Islands). History also tells of several Ibbenese attempts to seize the mouth of the [Sarne](wiki:Sarne), which brought them into war with the [Sarnori](wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor) cities [Saath](wiki:Saath) and [Sarys](wiki:Sarys).(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-17)
At its greatest extent, the Ibbenese foothold on Essos was as large as Ib itself, and far richer. More and more Ibbenese crossed over from the isles to make their fortune on the mainland, cutting down trees, plowing land, damming the rivers, and mining the hills. Some two hundred years ago, however, the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) destroyed the Ibbenese settlements on the mainland, aside from the small colony of [New Ibbish](wiki:New_Ibbish).(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-18)
Previously ruled by [God-Kings](wiki:God-King) at the [Port of Ibben](wiki:Port_of_Ibben), the Ibbenese are now ruled by the [Shadow Council](wiki:Shadow_Council).(wiki:Ibbenese.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-19)
