## Appearances & Description

Brogg has black hair, and is described as being a man of few words.(wiki:Brogg.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

Brogg is among the wildling leaders who accompany [Tormund](wiki:Tormund) to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) in declaring a truce with the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). Like the other leaders, Brogg gives a son as hostage to the Watch to assure his and his people's good behavior.(wiki:Brogg.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Lord Commander [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) later considers giving Brogg the [command](wiki:Commander_(Night's_Watch)) of one of the abandoned castles along the Wall but [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh) rejects the idea. Brogg is present in the [Shieldhall](wiki:Shieldhall) when [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) announces the [letter](wiki:Bastard_Letter) purportedly from [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Bolton) entitled 'Bastard'.(wiki:Brogg.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
