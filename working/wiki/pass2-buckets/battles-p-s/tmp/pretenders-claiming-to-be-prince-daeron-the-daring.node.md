## Identity

During the reign of King Aegon III Targaryen, several pretenders claimed to be the late Prince Daeron Targaryen, the youngest son of King Viserys I Targaryen (wiki:Pretenders_claiming_to_be_Prince_Daeron_the_Daring.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii).

## Narrative Arc

Prince Daeron the Daring died at the Second Battle of Tumbleton in 130 AC, but the manner of his death was disputed and his body was never identified. This left room for ambitious imposters to claim his identity in the years following the Dance of the Dragons. King Aegon III and his brother Prince Viserys Targaryen worked together to deal with the claimants, who were all proven to be imposters (wiki:Pretenders_claiming_to_be_Prince_Daeron_the_Daring.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii). The date is uncertain, occurring between 136 AC and 157 AC (wiki:Pretenders_claiming_to_be_Prince_Daeron_the_Daring).
