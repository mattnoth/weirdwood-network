## Identity

The retaking of Rook's Rest was a battle during the Dance of the Dragons in 130 AC, in which black forces led by Lord Walys Mooton retook the castle from the green garrison but failed to kill the wounded dragon Sunfyre (wiki:Retaking_of_Rook's_Rest.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold).

## Allegiances

Part of the `FIGHTS_IN` Dance of the Dragons. Fought between the blacks (House Mooton, House Brune of Brownhollow, House Brune of the Dyre Den, House Celtigar, House Crabb) and the greens (garrison left by Criston Cole, with the wounded Sunfyre) (wiki:Retaking_of_Rook's_Rest).

## Narrative Arc

After the first Battle at Rook's Rest, where Rhaenys Targaryen and Meleys were slain, the greens left a garrison at Rook's Rest to tend the wounded Sunfyre. Lord Walys Mooton led a hundred knights from Maidenpool to Crackclaw Point, where they joined forces of Brunes, Celtigars, and Crabbs. The blacks surprised and retook the castle, then attempted to kill Sunfyre while the dragon slept. Sunfyre killed threescore men including Lord Walys before the rest fled. A fortnight later Lord Manfryd Mooton found his brother's charred body, but Sunfyre had disappeared (wiki:Retaking_of_Rook's_Rest.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold).
