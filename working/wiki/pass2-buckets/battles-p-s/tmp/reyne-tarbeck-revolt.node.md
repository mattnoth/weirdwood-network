## Identity

The Reyne-Tarbeck revolt was an uprising in the westerlands in 261 AC, in which Houses Reyne and Tarbeck rose against their Lannister overlords and were utterly destroyed by Ser Tywin Lannister (wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons).

## Narrative Arc

Houses Reyne and Tarbeck had grown bold during the weak rule of Lord Tytos Lannister, borrowing heavily and defying Lannister authority. When Tywin returned from the War of the Ninepenny Kings and demanded repayment, Lord Walderan Tarbeck was imprisoned. Lady Ellyn Tarbeck retaliated by seizing three Lannisters as hostages; Tytos capitulated and forgave the Tarbeck debts (wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Raffc44)(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons).

In 261 AC, Tywin provoked both houses into open rebellion and marched against them without his father's permission. Tarbeck Hall fell first — Lord Walderan was killed in battle, Lady Ellyn and her son Tion died when siege engines collapsed the keep, and the castle was burned. Lord Roger Reyne attacked with two thousand men but was routed by Tywin's larger force. The surviving Reynes retreated into the mines of Castamere, where Tywin sealed the entrances and diverted a stream to flood the tunnels. Over three hundred men, women, and children perished underground (wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons).

The destruction of both houses made Tywin feared throughout the Seven Kingdoms and inspired the song "The Rains of Castamere" (wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rasos19).
