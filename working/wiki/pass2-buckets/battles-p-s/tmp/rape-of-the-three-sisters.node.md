## Identity

The Rape of the Three Sisters was an invasion of the Three Sisters by the north approximately two thousand years before the events of ASOIAF. It was the first act in a thousand years of conflict between the Starks and Arryns known as the War Across the Water (wiki:Rape_of_the_Three_Sisters.cite_ref-Radwd9).

## Narrative Arc

Previously a free people ruled by their own kings, the Sistermen were conquered by northmen who had tired of their pirating and pillaging. According to chronicles written by Sistermen and men of the Vale, the northmen committed numerous atrocities: killing children and cooking them in pots, disemboweling men, executing three thousand warriors at the Headman's Mount, and Belthasar Bolton making a "Pink Pavilion" from the flayed skins of a hundred Sistermen. Northern accounts do not mention these actions (wiki:Rape_of_the_Three_Sisters.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_vale). In response, the Sistermen turned to Mathos II Arryn, King of Mountain and Vale, for assistance. The Sistermen bent the knee to the Eyrie to drive out the northmen (wiki:Rape_of_the_Three_Sisters.cite_ref-Radwd9).
