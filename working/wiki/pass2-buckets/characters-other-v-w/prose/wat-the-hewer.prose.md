## Origins

During the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), Wat the Hewer led [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) out of the [Reach](wiki:Reach) on a march towards [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). In the [battle at Stonebridge](wiki:Battle_at_Stonebridge) he killed half a dozen [knights](wiki:Knight), including Lord [Meadows](wiki:House_Meadows), the commander of King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen)'s army, Wat also faced [Myles Smallwood](wiki:Myles_Smallwood) in single combat with both men surviving.(wiki:Wat_the_Hewer.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Wat was taken alive and delivered to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in chains.(wiki:Wat_the_Hewer.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Wat_the_Hewer.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Maegor cut off Wat's limbs with his own axe, then commanded his [maesters](wiki:Maester) to keep Wat alive so he could attend the king's wedding to [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower). When Maegor later took Tyanna as [his third wife](wiki:Polygamy) on [Rhaenys's Hill](wiki:Rhaenys's_Hill), surrounded by the remains of the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) who had died there when Maegor [burned down](wiki:Burning_of_the_Sept_of_Remembrance) the [Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Sept_of_Remembrance), Wat was present as a witness.(wiki:Wat_the_Hewer.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
