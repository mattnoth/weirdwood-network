## Origins

Walgrave is considered by many to have the foremost knowledge in [ravencraft](wiki:Raven),(wiki:Walgrave.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) and he wrote a book about the subject called *[Black Wings, Swift Words](wiki:Black_Wings,_Swift_Words)*.(wiki:Walgrave.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) His chambers are located in the west tower of the [Ravenry](wiki:Ravenry), below the [rookery](wiki:Rookery) of the [white ravens](wiki:White_raven).(wiki:Walgrave.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The birds are Walgrave's pride, and he wants them to eat him when he dies.(wiki:Walgrave.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

Walgrave once taught Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) how to read.(wiki:Walgrave.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_preface.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) However, he is now an old man and keeps the title [archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) only by courtesy, his wits having left him a long time ago. He often soils his smallclothes, and has been found weeping in the Citadel's [Library](wiki:Library_(Citadel)), unable to remember the way back to his chambers. The novice [Pate](wiki:Pate_(novice)) has been assigned to help him, tending the ravens, fetching Walgrave's meals, cleaning his chambers, and dressing him every morning. Walgrave cannot recall names very well (though he can easily tell one raven from another), and often calls Pate by the name of [Cressen](wiki:Cressen). Maester [Gormon](wiki:Gormon_Tyrell) now judges those seeking a black iron link in Walgrave's place.(wiki:Walgrave.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

There is a strongbox underneath Walgrave's bed, containing a bag of [silver](wiki:Silver) [stags](wiki:Currency), a lock of yellow hair tied up in a ribbon, a painted miniature of a woman who resembles Walgrave (even to her mustache), and a lobstered [steel](wiki:Steel) [knight](wiki:Knight)'s gauntlet that supposedly once belonged to a prince. The box also contains Walgrave's [archmaester's key](wiki:Personal_key_of_Walgrave), which can open any door in the Citadel. The strongbox's lock was broken by Walgrave after he lost the key that opened it.(wiki:Walgrave.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

## Appearances & Description

Walgrave's ring, rod, and mask are made of black [iron](wiki:Iron), showing his expertise in [ravencraft](wiki:Raven).(wiki:Walgrave.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

A man calling himself an [alchemist](wiki:Alchemist) offers Walgrave's assistant [Pate](wiki:Pate_(novice)) a [golden](wiki:Gold) coin in exchange for Walgrave's [archmaester's key](wiki:Personal_key_of_Walgrave). Pate considers the offer for two days, as he does not want to be seen as a thief, but on the third day he steals the key and the bag of silver stags. He gives the key to the Alchemist and receives the coin, but after he bites the coin it turns out to be [poisoned](wiki:Poison), and he dies.(wiki:Walgrave.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

Walgrave's name is chosen for the position of [Seneschal of the Citadel](wiki:Seneschal_of_the_Citadel), but due to his infirmity, Archmaester [Theobald](wiki:Theobald) offers to perform the duties for him.(wiki:Walgrave.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

When [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) arrives at the Citadel, he wishes to see the Seneschal, but [Alleras](wiki:Alleras) intercepts him in the Seneschal's Court and brings him to the chambers of Archmaester [Marwyn](wiki:Marwyn), who also lives in the Ravenry. After Marwyn hears Sam's story, he decides to leave for [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), but before he goes, he commands "[Pate](wiki:Alchemist)" to find a sleeping cell for Sam in the Ravenry, where he will assist Pate with the ravens. Pate tells Sam there is a room beneath his, with steps that lead right up to Walgrave's chambers.(wiki:Walgrave.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
