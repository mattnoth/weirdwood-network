## Origins

The society originated in the volcanic [slave](wiki:Slave) mines of [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), prior to the founding of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) and the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria). The tale of its beginnings centers around a figure of unknown origins who was the first Faceless Man. This man heard the prayers of the slaves to their various gods and came to conclude that they all prayed to the same god "with a hundred different faces", the [Many-Faced God](wiki:Many-Faced_God), and that he was "that god's instrument". This led to him giving "the first [gift](wiki:Gift_of_mercy)" to the most desperate slave.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-11) Later, the first Faceless Man discovered another slave praying fervently for his master's death. He granted the prayer in exchange for that slave joining him in serving the Many-Faced God.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

The first Faceless Man later brought the gift to the Valyrian masters as well.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-12) Some scholars believe that the cause of the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria) was too many assassinations of the mages who maintained the spells controlling the [Fourteen Flames](wiki:Fourteen_Flames).(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Prince [Harlan Hoare](wiki:Harlan_Hoare) was rumored to have been killed by a Faceless Man.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

It is speculated that the letter King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) received from [Nymor Martell](wiki:Nymor_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), may have contained a threat to hire the Faceless Men to kill Aegon's young son, [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Some believed that the half-naked woman who fled the tent of Septon [Moon](wiki:Moon_(septon)) outside [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) was one of the Faceless Men, since she disappeared after his throat was slit. Scholars are skeptical, however, since Faceless Men are more subtle in their work.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

According to [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom), Ser [Denys Harte](wiki:Denys_Harte) hired the Faceless Men to assassinate a rival in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Many attribute the deaths of [Lysandro the Magnificent](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare) in [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Drazenko Rogare](wiki:Drazenko_Rogare) in [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) to the Faceless Men, since the powerful brothers died within a day of each other.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

During a [small council](wiki:Small_council) meeting discussing King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s demand that [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) die, [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) suggests hiring the Faceless Men to assassinate the exiled princess. The [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), says that doing so would be too expensive; the price to hire the Faceless Men to kill a merchant is twice that of hiring an army of [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword), and the price to kill a princess would be even higher. [Varys](wiki:Varys), the [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers), will instead offer a [lordship](wiki:Lordship) to a successful assassin.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### A Clash of Kings

[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) meets a [Lorathi](wiki:Lorath) criminal who calls himself [Jaqen H'ghar](wiki:Jaqen_H'ghar), traveling in a cage on the way to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) with two other prisoners from the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells), [Rorge](wiki:Rorge) and [Biter](wiki:Biter).(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Racok5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Arya saves them from burning at the [Gods Eye town](wiki:Gods_Eye_town),(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Racok14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) and in return for these three lives saved, Jaqen later promises her three deaths.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Per her wishes, Jaqen assassinates [Chiswyck](wiki:Chiswyck)(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) and [Weese](wiki:Weese)(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Racok38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) and agrees to help her escape [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) After the [fall of Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal), he changes his face to a completely different appearance and leaves the castle, saying that he has duties elsewhere. Before he goes, he gives Arya a worn [iron](wiki:Iron) coin, and tells her if she wishes to find him again, she should give the coin to any man of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) and tell him "*[valar morghulis](wiki:Valar_morghulis)*".(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

### A Storm of Swords

Arya overhears the [ghost of High Heart](wiki:Ghost_of_High_Heart) telling the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) that she "dreamt of a man without a face, waiting on a bridge that swayed and swung."(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) is later reported to have fallen to his death from a bridge at [Pyke](wiki:Pyke).(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Arya finds a [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) ship at [Saltpans](wiki:Saltpans), the *[Titan's Daughter](wiki:Titan's_Daughter)*, and though she cannot afford passage, she remembers the iron coin that Jaqen had given her. She gives the captain the iron coin and says "*valar morghulis*"; he replies "*[valar dohaeris](wiki:Valar_dohaeris)*", and respectfully grants her a cabin on his ship.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Rasos65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

### A Feast for Crows

The [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) novice [Pate](wiki:Pate_(novice)) dies in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) after biting a coin he received from the [alchemist](wiki:Alchemist), who calls himself "no one", and whose appearance matches the one that [Jaqen H'ghar](wiki:Jaqen_H'ghar) changed to.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-25) Later at the Citadel, [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) is introduced to a novice calling himself Pate.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

Arya travels to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) on the *Titan's Daughter*, whose crew treats her with much respect, giving her gifts and asking her to remember their names.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Once in Braavos she comes to the [House of Black and White](wiki:House_of_Black_and_White), and is accepted as a servant there.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) One priest, whom Arya calls the [kindly man](wiki:Kindly_man) because of his kind face, seems to have some position of authority in the guild, as he is the one who takes charge of Arya's training. Another priestess, who Arya calls the [waif](wiki:Waif), works in the House of Black and White handling poisons. Though she is thirty-six years old, she has a shrunken and childlike appearance brought about by exposure to [poisons](wiki:Poison).(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-13)(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

Arya is told she must rid herself of her possessions. After she does so, she is made a novice, and is taught some of the guild's history, as well as receiving lessons in [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian), the Braavosi tongue, and poisons. She also learns to recognize lies, and to rule her face and expressions.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-14) She is sent to learn Braavosi by immersion, and the skill of becoming "no one", by becoming [a fishmonger](wiki:Brusco)'s assistant called Cat of the Canals, but every month at the new moon returns to the House of Black and White to tell what she has learned and for more lessons in the guild.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) Arya kills [Dareon](wiki:Dareon), a deserter from the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), and after she tells the kindly man of this, she is given a potion in her milk that makes her blind.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

### A Dance with Dragons

Arya learns that she has been blinded because she took the Many-Faced God's powers for herself, by judging Dareon and killing him. She continues to work as a novice in the House of Black and White, learning how to use other senses than sight. Her job includes removing the clothes and other possessions of those who have died in the temple, including identifying the coins by touch. She also is disguised as "Beth", a blind beggar on the streets of Braavos, though she avoids the places where she had been known as Cat. She is beaten by an unknown person in the temple several times, and when she identifies her assailant as the kindly man, her sight is returned to her by means of another potion.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

Arya observes a meeting of eleven Faceless Men in the House of Black and White, where they discuss potential assassination contracts. She is given an assignment to kill an [old man](wiki:Old_man) selling ship insurance. After Arya decides to assassinate her target with poison, she is brought to the holy sanctum on the third level for the first time. Arya receives her first new face, that of a disfigured and ugly girl. After successfully completing her assignment by poisoning a coin that the salesman bites, she is made an acolyte. The kindly man then tells her she will be given another new face, a pretty one this time, and will be sent to [Izembaro](wiki:Izembaro) for her first apprenticeship.(wiki:Faceless_Men.cite_ref-Radwd64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9)
