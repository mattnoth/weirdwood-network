## Appearances & Description

The Drunkard's Tower is so named because of its great lean. It stands in the bog where the south and west walls once met. The tower is covered in green moss.(wiki:Drunkard's_Tower.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

[House Karstark](wiki:House_Karstark) takes the Drunkard's Tower as their seat when the [northern](wiki:North) host of [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) stays at [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin).(wiki:Drunkard's_Tower.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

During the [siege of Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin), [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) sends [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) to offer the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) garrison of Moat Cailin food and safe passage if they surrender to [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) unarmed. Theon passes by the Drunkard's Tower to enter the [Gatehouse Tower](wiki:Gatehouse_Tower), where the majority of the garrison are. He discovers eighteen ironmen still hold the Drunkard's Tower.(wiki:Drunkard's_Tower.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
