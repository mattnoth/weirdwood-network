## Culture

The [marcher lords](wiki:Marcher_lords) of the stormlands have a strong martial tradition.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Stormlands_13-1) The Dornish Marches are known for producing excellent longbows, and many famous bowmen come from the region, such as [Fletcher Dick](wiki:Fletcher_Dick).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)
The [Carons](wiki:House_Caron) of [Nightsong](wiki:Nightsong) style themselves [Lord of the Marches](wiki:Lord_of_the_Marches), but they do not have dominion over their fellow marcher lords.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell), [Lord Paramount of the Reach](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Reach), includes [Defender of the Marches](wiki:Defender_of_the_Marches) among his titles.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
The so-called stony [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) of the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) have the most in common with Westerosi living north of the mountains when compared to salty and sandy Dornish living in southern Dorne. Despite this, they have fought lords from the Reach and the stormlands for thousands of years.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Marchers and Dornishmen each consider the other to be liars,(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) and marchers consider the Dornish accent of the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue) to be incomprehensible.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)
Marcher ballads are known for their length(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) and numerous verses.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rasos13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Many songs are about conflicts between brave marchers and cruel Dornishmen.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2)

## Origins

### Antiquity[ ]

Lords from the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands), the [Reach](wiki:Reach), and [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) have fought each other and amongst themselves for thousands of years.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)
[Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) with histories of fighting the marcher lords include the [Blackmonts](wiki:House_Blackmont) of [Blackmont](wiki:Blackmont), the [Fowlers](wiki:House_Fowler) of [Skyreach](wiki:Skyreach), the [Manwoodys](wiki:House_Manwoody) of [Kingsgrave](wiki:Kingsgrave), the [Wyls](wiki:House_Wyl) of [Wyl](wiki:Wyl_(castle)), and the [Yronwoods](wiki:House_Yronwood) of [Yronwood](wiki:Yronwood).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3)
[House Dondarrion](wiki:House_Dondarrion) was founded when a messenger was ambushed by two [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) while riding on the marches on a stormy night. When the rider thought he was doomed, a bright purple lightning bolt killed both Dornishmen. Because the rider was able to deliver his crucial message, the Storm King raised him as the first Lord Dondarrion.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

### Targaryen Era[ ]

[Dornish](wiki:Dorne) parties raided across the marches during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell) resisted [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) during the [Wars of Conquest](wiki:Wars_of_Conquest), so the Dornish Marches guarded the southern border of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) against attacks from Dorne.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)
The Dornish Marches suffered grievously during the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), and Lord [Fowler](wiki:House_Fowler) burned [Nightsong](wiki:Nightsong).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1) Marchers urged Dornish lords to surrender by claiming the Martells had betrayed them, possibly because [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) was not burned by [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire) unlike other Dornish castles.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)
After ascending the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) visited the Dornish Marches during his journey to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) The outlaw [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Aenys_I)) invaded the Dornish Marches in what became known as the [Second Dornish War](wiki:Second_Dornish_War). Lord [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon) defeated Lord [Walter Wyl](wiki:Walter_Wyl) at [Stonehelm](wiki:Stonehelm), and marcher lords such as Lady [Ellyn Caron](wiki:Ellyn_Caron), Lord [Harmon Dondarrion](wiki:Harmon_Dondarrion), and Lord [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly_(lord)) participated in the [Vulture Hunt](wiki:Vulture_Hunt).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)
Lords [Dondarrion](wiki:House_Dondarrion), [Selmy](wiki:House_Selmy), and [Tarly](wiki:House_Tarly) represented the Dornish Marches at the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) visited [Horn Hill](wiki:Horn_Hill), Nightsong, and [Blackhaven](wiki:Blackhaven) after the elevation of [Alfyn](wiki:Alfyn) to [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) During the [Third Dornish War](wiki:Third_Dornish_War), Lord [Simon Dondarrion](wiki:Simon_Dondarrion) led a small force of marcher knights into the western [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) against a [second Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Jaehaerys_I)), and Jaehaerys and Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) pursued the outlaws from the east. Raiding of the marches declined for some time after the end of the war.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)
[Qoren Martell](wiki:Qoren_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), refused to involve Dorne in the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). Once his daughter [Aliandra](wiki:Aliandra_Martell) became Princess of Dorne, however, she encouraged her bannermen to gain her favor by raiding the marches.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) Lord [Royce Caron](wiki:Royce_Caron) resigned from the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) so he can protect Nightsong from the Dornish threats.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) Ser [Steffon Connington](wiki:Steffon_Connington) was slain by [Wyland Wyl](wiki:Wyland_Wyl) while pursuing raiders on the marches.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)
Marchers who hated the Dornish were among the supporters of King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), although Aegon the Unworthy's plan to invade Dorne was a failure.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)
After Dorne joined the Seven Kingdoms peacefully, King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) raised [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall) in the Dornish Marches. Resentment by marcher lords and knights toward the Dornish influence at Daeron's court was one of the causes of the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)
King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) was slain fighting a rebellious lord on the Dornish Marches(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) during the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

[Anguy](wiki:Anguy), a commoner from the Dornish Marches, wins the archery competition at the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)
Two sibling [freeriders](wiki:Freerider) from the marches enter the service of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Ragot44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

The acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), sends Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) in hopes of repairing relations with [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear). As another concession, he offers some castles in the Marches to Dorne.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) Once Myrcella arrives, [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), has pledged to mobilize Dornish hosts in the [Prince's Pass](wiki:Prince's_Pass) and the [Boneway](wiki:Boneway) to keep the [marcher lords](wiki:Marcher_lords) in their castles rather than aid [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) The Dornish refuse to invade after Stannis [takes Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), but Tyrion hopes the marcher lords will remain in their lands.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Anguy is now a member of the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners), the outlaw band led by the marcher lord [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rasos17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) [Tom of Sevenstreams](wiki:Tom_of_Sevenstreams) tells [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) that the only songs that Anguy knows are long marcher ballads.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rasos13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) When Anguy tells Arya tales of the marches, the girl finds his stories droll.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Prince Doran denies [Obara Sand](wiki:Obara_Sand)'s request to allow her and [Nymeria Sand](wiki:Nymeria_Sand) to take the hosts from the Prince's Pass and the Boneway to attack the marcher lords and then [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)
According to [Alleras](wiki:Alleras), [black](wiki:Raven) and [white ravens](wiki:White_raven) are kept separate at the [Ravenry](wiki:Ravenry) because they quarrel like Marchers and Dornishmen.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

One of the [queen's men](wiki:Queen's_men) informs [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) that although the marches can be cold, it is much colder at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black).(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)
Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) considers himself to be a man of the Dornish Marches.(wiki:Dornish_Marches.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)
