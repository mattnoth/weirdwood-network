## Origins

### Antiquity

The faith of the Drowned God teaches the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) that they came from the watery halls of the Drowned God, and were created in his likeness. The priests preach that the ironborn are related to fish and [merlings](wiki:Merling), and not the other races of mankind. However, some ironborn acknowledge the more widely accepted view that the ironborn descend from the [First Men](wiki:First_Men).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-13) In his manuscript *[Strange Stone](wiki:Strange_Stone)*, Maester [Theron](wiki:Theron) suggests that the religion of the Drowned God originates from the undersea fathers of the [Deep Ones](wiki:Deep_Ones).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

The Drowned God is said to have changed [Nagga](wiki:Nagga)'s bones into stone after the [Grey King](wiki:Grey_King) had slain her during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), so that men might never cease to wonder at the courage of the first of kings.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) The Grey King's crown was made of driftwood, so all would know that his kingship came from the Drowned God.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) The Grey King eventually cast aside his crown and walked into the sea to descend to the watery halls of the Drowned God, taking his place at the right hand of the deity.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) The [Storm God](wiki:Storm_God) snuffed out Nagga's fire after the Grey King's death and the sea stole his throne, with Nagga's bones the only remnants of the [Grey King's Hall](wiki:Grey_King's_Hall).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-19)

The greatest of all the priests of the Drowned God was [Galon Whitestaff](wiki:Galon_Whitestaff), who decreed that ironborn must not make war on other ironborn and who forbade them to carry off each other's women or raid each other's shores. He also made the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) into a single kingdom, by calling a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) at [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk) in which [Urras Greyiron](wiki:Urras_Greyiron) was chosen as [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) The priests of the Drowned God refused to allow the ironborn kings to name their successor a third time following the death of [Urragon IV Greyiron](wiki:Urragon_IV_Greyiron). A kingsmoot was assembled on Old Wyk, but in the slaughter that followed half a hundred priests and prophets were among the victims of [Urron Greyiron](wiki:Urron_Greyiron), the first [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3)

### Andals

The [Andals](wiki:Andals) eventually reached the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) during [their invasion](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals). According to Archmaester [Haereg](wiki:Haereg), the newcomwers attempted to force worship of their own gods, [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), onto the ironborn. The native ironmen would not accept the Seven, but they did allow the worship of the Seven to coexist with their worship of the Drowned God. Although the Andals intermarried with the ironborn, the Drowned God remained strong on the Iron Islands and in time, most Andals on the isles converted.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) [Harras Hoare](wiki:Harras_Hoare) was the first Hoare king to marry an Andal.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

The [priests of the Drowned God](wiki:Priests_of_the_Drowned_God) saw the kings of [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) as ungodly usurpers.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Haereg suggests that the priests held that opinion because the Hoares allowed the Faith of the Andals to come to the Iron Islands for the first time. During the reign of [Wulfgar Widowmaker](wiki:Wulfgar_Hoare), the first sept on the Iron Islands was built on [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk). When his great-grandson [Horgan](wiki:Horgan_Hoare) gave permission to construct a second sept on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk), a bloody rebellion began under the goading of priests. The sept was burned, the septon was pulled to pieces, and the worshippers were drowned in the sea. Horgan Priestkiller began to slaughter priests in retaliation.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)

King [Harmund II Hoare](wiki:Harmund_II_Hoare) was the first ironborn king who raised his sons in the Faith,(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2) although he practiced a self-made version of it. He accepted the Seven as true gods, but also continued to honor the Drowned God. He spoke about "the Eight Gods" and decreed that a statue of the Drowned God should be raised at the doors of every sept. Opposed by adherents of both religions, Harmund eventually decided that the Drowned God was an aspect of the [Stranger](wiki:Stranger_(the_Seven)).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-3) The priest known as the [Shrike](wiki:Shrike) led a rebellion against Harmund II's heir, [Harmund III Hoare](wiki:Harmund_III_Hoare), and the king was overthrown with a fortnight. Harmund's younger brother, [Hagon Hoare](wiki:Hagon_Hoare), was crowned in his stead. Hagon the Heartless denounced the Faith, rescinded Harmund's edicts, and expelled the septons and septas. Every sept in the Iron Islands was quickly aflame.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-4)

### Recent History

[Halleck Hoare](wiki:Halleck_Hoare), [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), spent most of his time in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) and only nominally supported the Drowned God.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-5) Halleck's son, King [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare), was killed in the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal) during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

In the aftermath of Harren's death, the priest [Lodos](wiki:Lodos) claimed to be the living son of the Drowned God. [Vickon Greyjoy](wiki:Vickon_Greyjoy), the new [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), allowed the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) to return to the isles in the aftermath of the Conquest, as King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) supported the Seven. In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), Vickon's son, Lord [Goren](wiki:Goren_Greyjoy), crushed a revolt led by a man who claimed he was [Lodos the Twice-Drowned](wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned), returned from having visited the Drowned God. Goren sent Lodos's head to King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), who in return gave Goren leave to expel the septons and septas from the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands). It would take another century for another sept to open upon the islands.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

After the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), ordered Lord [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy) to return the women of the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) captured by his ironmen during the war. The Red Kraken refused to do so, however, claiming that only the Drowned God could end the bond between a man and his [salt wives](wiki:Salt_wives).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

In [Lordsport](wiki:Lordsport) stood a sept of the Faith at the time of [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), but it was not rebuilt it after its destruction during the war.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

## Organization

### Priests

The [priests of the Drowned God](wiki:Priests_of_the_Drowned_God) are ill clad,(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) wearing mottled robes of green, grey, and blue, the colors of their deity.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) Underneath they wear a sealskin clout.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) The priests use dried seaweed which they braid through their long hair and untrimmed beard, and wear a waterskin on a leather strap.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) They have an unkempt appearance.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) For example, [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) has not cut his hair since having been raised up by the Drowned God and becoming a priest.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) Most priests do not bathe in any water but the sea, and they often go barefoot. They have no permanent home, but instead wander the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands), seldom going far from the sea. The lords and peasants on the islands are obliged to give them food and shelter in the name of their god.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8) Priests will drink sea water from time to time, to strengthen their faith,(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) and some eat only fish.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9)

The priests speak with the voice of their god.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) They wield considerable power over the ironborn, having the power to call a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10) Most priests are illiterate, so the prayers and rituals are orally taught.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-11) The priests primarily make use of those things that can be taken from the sea, such as driftwood for makeshift shelters and sealskin for tents.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

A drowned priest is said to be able to sour wells and make women barren with his gaze.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### Drowned Men

The [drowned men](wiki:Drowned_men)(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6) are the acolytes of [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), who is a priest of the Drowned God. They wear mottled robes and carry driftwood cudgels to show their devotion.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7) It is unknown whether acolytes to other priests are called "drowned men" as well.

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

The [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) believe that the [red comet](wiki:Red_comet) is a flame the Drowned God has brought forth from the sea, which proclaims a "rising tide" for the ironborn. According to [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), it means that the people of the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) are meant to go into the world with fire and sword as the ironborn of old did.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)

During the [harrying of the Stony Shore](wiki:Harrying_of_the_Stony_Shore), Aeron drowns [Benfred Tallhart](wiki:Benfred_Tallhart) as an offering to the Drowned God.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)

### A Feast for Crows

Aeron performs the [kiss of life](wiki:Kiss_of_life) on [drowned men](wiki:Drowned_men) at [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-20) After learning of the death of his eldest brother [Balon](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North), Aeron summons the lords and captains of the Iron Islands to [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk) for a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot). He rejects the claim of his older brother, [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), insisting the godless Euron is not worthy to sit the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-21)

Euron is eventually chosen as king by the ironborn who assemble at [Nagga's Cradle](wiki:Nagga's_Cradle).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) Aeron believes that Euron used wizards and sorcerers to prevent the ironborn from listening to the Drowned God and the sea.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6)

### A Dance with Dragons

[Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) has the captain of *[Ghiscari Dawn](wiki:Ghiscari_Dawn)* thrown overboard as a sacrifice to the Drowned God. After Victarion kills a fisherman, [Longwater Pyke](wiki:Longwater_Pyke) and [Tom Tidewood](wiki:Tom_Tidewood) offer the man's body to the sea as well.(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) Because [Moqorro](wiki:Moqorro) heals his wounded hand, Victarion decides to honor [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) in addition to his Drowned God. The iron captain sacrifices slave girls by burning them alive on a fishing ketch set adrift to burn and then sink into [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Drowned_God.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)
