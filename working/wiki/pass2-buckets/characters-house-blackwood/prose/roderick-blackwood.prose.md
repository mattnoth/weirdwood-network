## Origins

Roderick led the Blackwoods when [Humfrey I Teague](wiki:Humfrey_I_Teague) was [King of the Rivers and the Hills](wiki:King_of_the_Rivers_and_the_Hills). When Humfrey began to repress worship of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods), Roderick rose in rebellion, supported by [House Vance of Atranta](wiki:House_Vance_of_Atranta) and [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun). Nearly defeated by Humfrey and the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant), Roderick sought the aid of the [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King) [Arlan III Durrandon](wiki:Arlan_III_Durrandon), who had married one of Roderick's daughters. Arlan intended to restore the riverlands to House Blackwood, but Roderick was slain in battle.(wiki:Roderick_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

After the Teagues were all killed in the [Battle of Six Kings](wiki:Battle_of_Six_Kings), Roderick's heir was a boy of only eight and Arlan distrusted Roderick's brothers. The river lords rejected Arlan's suggestion of having Roderick's eldest child, [Shiera Blackwood](wiki:Shiera_Blackwood), rule the riverlands with Arlan's son by her side. Arlan then decided to add the riverlands to the realm of the Storm Kings.(wiki:Roderick_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)
