## Origins

In [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), when Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) was dispatched to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) to battle Lord [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), twenty [Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower) war galleys commanded by Ser Leo joined the [Velaryon fleet](wiki:Velaryon_fleet). However, Dalton was slain before the fleet arrived and the his ships returned to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands). With the [Lannister fleet](wiki:Lannister_fleet) having been destroyed during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) was reluctant to see the Velaryon fleet depart in fear of the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) returning. Until the Lannisters and [Farmans](wiki:House_Farman) could rebuild their ships, Alyn agreed to allow a third of his own fleet remain in the westerlands, under the command of Leo.(wiki:Leo_Costayne.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

While Alyn had refused to attack the Iron Islands, Leo proved more amenable to Lady [Johanna Westerling](wiki:Johanna_Westerling), the Dowager Lady of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) and [regent](wiki:Regent) for her young son, Lord [Loreon Lannister](wiki:Loreon_Lannister). Swayed perhaps by Johanna's promise to marry him if he conquered the Iron Islands for her son, Leo invaded the isles with his fleet in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC). In the middle of the ironmen's [struggle for power](wiki:Struggle_for_power), Leo and his army took them unawares and put many to the sword, landing thousands of Lannister soldiers on [Pyke](wiki:Pyke_(island)), [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk), and [Harlaw](wiki:Harlaw).(wiki:Leo_Costayne.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Ultimately, Leo was killed by [Arthur Goodbrother](wiki:Arthur_Goodbrother) in the hills of Great Wyk. Leo's host was largely destroyed and three quarters of his fleet were seized or sunk, but a portion of his fleet returned to [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) with highborn captives aboard. Amongst the notable hostages was [Rodrik Greyjoy](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy_(son_of_Dalton)), a [salt son](wiki:Salt_son) of Lord Dalton that Lady Lannister would [geld](wiki:Eunuch) and make her son's [fool](wiki:Fool).(wiki:Leo_Costayne.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)(wiki:Leo_Costayne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_red_kraken.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)
