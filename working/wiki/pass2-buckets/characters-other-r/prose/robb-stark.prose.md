## Origins

Robb was conceived during the first fortnight of the marriage of Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) and Lady [Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Stark)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-8)—on the wedding night at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), according to Catelyn.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) While Eddard rode south to fight in [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Catelyn remained at [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully)'s castle. Near the end of the war, she gave birth at Riverrun(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-9) with the aid of Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) After war ended Catelyn traveled to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) with Robb.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rawoiafrobb_stark.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Robb was raised at Winterfell as his father's heir, and is the closest of companions with his half-brother [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rawoiafrobb_stark.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Robb and Jon trained together at arms ever since they were big enough to walk, and played at being mighty [knights](wiki:Knight) and heroes.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Once, Robb and Jon built a great mountain of snow above a gate. They were spotted by [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder), then a brother of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), who had accompanied the visiting [Lord Commander Qorgyle](wiki:Lord_Commander_Qorgyle). Mance promised not to tell on them, and the two boys eventually dumped the snow on [Fat Tom](wiki:Tomard), the slowest of Lord Eddard's [household guards](wiki:House_Stark_guards), who chased them around afterward.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

After [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion) in [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), Eddard brought to Winterfell a hostage, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), the last surviving son of Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy). Robb came to befriend Theon and enjoy the elder boy's company.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-3)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) Theon joined Robb and Jon in their training at arms under the tutelage of Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel),(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) and Robb and Theon trained at sword fighting at the staircases of Winterfell.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) The Stark children, Jon, and Theon were also educated by Maester Luwin.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) Catelyn resented whenever Jon defeated Robb in a task put before the two youths.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1)

When [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) was only a few years old, Robb took his siblings into the [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell) to show them the empty tombs that would be theirs one day. Jon, having covered himself with flour to appear as a ghost, tried to scare his half siblings by emerging from a tomb, and a sobbing Bran wrapped his arms around the legs of a laughing Robb.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

When [Alys Karstark](wiki:Alys_Karstark) was six, Lord [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark) brought his daughter to Winterfell in an attempt to betroth her to Eddard's heir.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) Although no betrothal was arranged, Robb courteously told Alys that she danced beautifully.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) Robb has often attended Lord Stark's councils,(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) and he has hunted in the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood) with his father, Jon, and [Jory Cassel](wiki:Jory_Cassel).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1) Eddard enjoys [hawking](wiki:Hawking) with his heir.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

## Appearances & Description

Robb opens the series as a boy of fourteen years.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) His appearance favors his [Tully](wiki:House_Tully) side, with blue eyes and thick red-brown hair.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) When he grows a beard, it is redder than his auburn hair.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Fast(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2) and strong for his age,(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Robb is more muscular than his half brother, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-3)

Robb is his father [Eddard's](wiki:Eddard_Stark) son, with a keen sense of [justice](wiki:Laws_and_justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) and courtesy.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) He shares his father's devotion to honor(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) and is caring toward his younger brother [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Despite his youth, Robb forces himself to act more [lordly](wiki:Lord) and take on responsibility when needed.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Robb is a follower of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) and is frequently accompanied by his [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf), [Grey Wind](wiki:Grey_Wind).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) wants to be as strong as her oldest brother,(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) and [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and Bran admire Robb's bravery.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Robb and Jon Snow are best friends and constant companions.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Robb and [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), Eddard's ward and hostage, are friends, with Robb seeming to admire Theon(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) and Theon thinking of Robb like a younger brother.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Robb attends the execution of [Gared](wiki:Gared) and discovers a litter of six [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf) pups in the snow. He claims one of the direwolves for his own and names him [Grey Wind](wiki:Grey_Wind).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-4) Shortly after the court of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) arrives at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), Robb spars with the crown prince, [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), using wooden swords. Bad blood develops between the two when Joffrey challenges Robb to spar with live [steel](wiki:Steel). Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel), Winterfell's master-at-arms, does not allow it, to the mockery and chagrin of many of the present retainers of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1) Robb escorts Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), to the feast in the [Great Hall of Winterfell](wiki:Great_Hall_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)

When his father [Eddard](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell), is named [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to Robert and journeys south to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Robb remains in Winterfell to rule in his father's stead with the help of his mother, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-2) She becomes traumatized when her younger son [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) is crippled by a fall from the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep), however, so more responsibility falls on Robb.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) After [an assassin](wiki:Catspaw) tries to kill the comatose Bran,(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-1) Catelyn travels to King's Landing to inform Eddard.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

Robb holds court as the Stark in Winterfell, meeting with [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) and [Yoren](wiki:Yoren) in the Great Hall and traveling to distant holdfasts.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) While riding in the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood), Robb and his friend, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), rescue the crippled Bran from [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) and deserters from the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), with Robb killing an axeman with his sword and then subduing [Osha](wiki:Osha). Robb criticizes Theon for shooting an arrow near Bran.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-4)

After word arrives of [Eddard's arrest](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark) in King's Landing for treason against the late king Robert's successor, Joffrey, Robb calls the Stark bannermen to Winterfell. [Maege Mormont](wiki:Maege_Mormont), [Lady of Bear Island](wiki:Lady_of_Bear_Island), tries to interest Robb in a granddaughter, and [Medger Cerwyn](wiki:Medger_Cerwyn), [Lord of Cerwyn](wiki:Lord_of_Cerwyn), introduces him to his own daughter, [Jonelle](wiki:Jonelle_Cerwyn). [Halys Hornwood](wiki:Halys_Hornwood), [Lord of the Hornwood](wiki:Lord_of_the_Hornwood), offers gifts in return for land and rights, while [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), [Lord of the Dreadfort](wiki:Lord_of_the_Dreadfort), and [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) both seek battle command. When [Jon Umber](wiki:Jon_Umber), [Lord of the Last Hearth](wiki:Lord_of_the_Last_Hearth), threatens Robb's authority, the youth has [Grey Wind](wiki:Grey_Wind) attack the Greatjon, biting off two fingers; a laughing Lord Umber becomes Robb's greatest champion. Despite his youth and inexperience, Robb unites the bannermen.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-5)

When Robb marches south to the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), his motives are to support his mother Catelyn's family, [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully), in response to the invasion of House Lannister from the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), and to bring back his father Eddard.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-6) Bran remains at Winterfell with Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-7) and Rodrik Cassel.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

Robb begins growing a beard while marching south.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-1) He resides in the [Gatehouse Tower](wiki:Gatehouse_Tower) while encamped at [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin), where his host is joined by Catelyn, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), and the levies from [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-2) They learn that [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) has been besieged by a host led by Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), whilst another force led by [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), [Lord of Casterly Rock](wiki:Lord_of_Casterly_Rock), approaches from the south. Robb plans to split his forces, sending his foot under the command of Lord Bolton to engage Tywin's army, while himself crossing the [Green Fork](wiki:Green_Fork) with the majority of his horse so he can lift the siege of Riverrun.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-3)

For allowing Robb to cross the river at the [Twins](wiki:Twins) and grant the northman his allegiance, [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), [Lord of the Crossing](wiki:Lord_of_the_Crossing), extracts the price of a marriage contract, ensuring that Robb will marry one of his daughters after the war is finished. [Olyvar Frey](wiki:Olyvar_Frey), one of Walder's sons, becomes Robb's personal squire.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2)

[Jason Mallister](wiki:Jason_Mallister), [Lord of Seagard](wiki:Lord_of_Seagard), joins Robb as he continues south toward Riverrun.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-10) Robb is protected by a [battle guard of highborn warriors](wiki:Robb_Stark's_battle_guard) who become his friends and companions.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-11)

With the aid of his great-uncle, Brynden the Blackfish, Robb achieves complete surprise in ambushing Jaime in the [Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood), annihilating the Lannister horsemen. Before being captured, Jaime slays [Torrhen](wiki:Torrhen_Karstark) and [Eddard Karstark](wiki:Eddard_Karstark) and [Daryn Hornwood](wiki:Daryn_Hornwood) while fighting to get to Robb.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-12) Shortly thereafter Robb lifts the siege of Riverrun through the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps),(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) scattering Jaime's remaining forces. This forces Tywin's army, which had scored a victory against Roose Bolton's foot in the [battle on the Green Fork](wiki:Battle_on_the_Green_Fork), to retreat back to [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

Meanwhile, Eddard Stark is executed for treason at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) at the command of King Joffrey, effectively ending the possibility of peace between Stark and Lannister. ### A Storm of Swords

Having suffered a wound in the [storming of the Crag](wiki:Storming_of_the_Crag), Robb is tended to by [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling), Gawen's daughter. When Robb learns of the supposed deaths of Bran and Rickon, Jeyne comforts Robb, which leads to his taking her [maidenhead](wiki:Maidenhead). In an effort to preserve her honor, Robb weds Jeyne immediately thereafter.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) However, the [Frey](wiki:House_Frey) host that had accompanied Robb into the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) are furious and withdraw their support, with [Black Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman)) threatening to kill Jeyne. [Olyvar Frey](wiki:Olyvar_Frey) wants to remain with Robb as as his squire, but Ser [Ryman Frey](wiki:Ryman_Frey) insists he return to the [Twins](wiki:Twins) with his kin.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) Robb shaves his beard but allows his hair to grow to his shoulders.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

Abandoning the castles he had taken in the west,(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Robb returns to [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) with his new wife in tow, much to his mother [Catelyn's](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) horror as, having pledged to wed a daughter of House Frey, this was not a slight that Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) is likely to overlook.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) Catelyn wishes that Robb had wed the more powerful [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) instead of Jeyne.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)

Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully), acting [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident) whilst his father [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) lays on his deathbed, met [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s army as they attempted to cross the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork) near Riverrun and threw them back. While the [Battle of the Fords](wiki:Battle_of_the_Fords) prevented Tywin's force from approaching Robb's from the rear, Robb's plan had been to entangle Tywin's army in a chase in the west while [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) marched on [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Being thrown back at Riverrun allowed Tywin's force to turn around and arrive at the capital in time for the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), which turned the tide of the battle, and indeed the war, in [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister)'s favor. Robb and Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully) reprimand Edmure in private.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)

Robb learns that in her grief over the supposed murder of two of her sons, Catelyn released [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) from captivity and entrusted him with a mission to return her daughters [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark) to her, if possible.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9) This earns her the enmity of [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark), [Lord of Karhold](wiki:Lord_of_Karhold), as Jaime had slain two of Rickard's sons, [Eddard](wiki:Eddard_Karstark) and [Torrhen](wiki:Torrhen_Karstark), while they defended Robb in the [Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood); Rickard requires vengeance for their deaths. In response to Jaime's release, Lord Karstark sends his horsemen to search the countryside for Jaime and then leads a small group in murdering two prisoners, [Tion Frey](wiki:Tion_Frey) and [Willem Lannister](wiki:Willem_Lannister), in a tower of Riverrun. For this crime, Robb sentences Lord Karstark to death and beheads Rickard himself with a poleaxe in the [godswood of Riverrun](wiki:Godswood_of_Riverrun).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

After learning of the defeat of [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) and Ser [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart) in the [battle at Duskendale](wiki:Battle_at_Duskendale), Robb is dumbfounded as to why his northern infantry even attacked the town.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0) With problems on every side, Robb seems only to take solace in the company of the [Westerlings](wiki:House_Westerling): his loving wife Jeyne, her brother Ser [Raynald](wiki:Raynald_Westerling) his banner-bearer, and their younger brother, Robb's new squire [Rollam](wiki:Rollam_Westerling). With all others, he is the solemn King in the North. A solitary Robb burns an unsent letter and spends his time reviewing maps.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-1)

When Lord Hoster Tully finally passes away, Robb aids six rivermen in pushing his grandfather's funereal boat into the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0) He meets with [Lame Lothar Frey](wiki:Lothar_Frey) and [Walder Rivers](wiki:Walder_Rivers), envoys of Lord Walder Frey, who had been slighted by Robb's marriage to Jeyne Westerling. Robb suggests that his uncle Edmure, now [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun), wed one of Lord Walder's daughters in his stead, a proposal to which Edmure reluctantly agrees.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-2)

Leaving Jeyne behind at Riverrun and appointing Brynden the Blackfish as [Warden of the Southern Marches](wiki:Warden_of_the_Southern_Marches), Robb takes his host north toward the Twins, where Edmure is to marry [Roslin Frey](wiki:Roslin_Frey).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Once the wedding is done, Robb intends to then return to the [north](wiki:North), reclaim his territory, much of which has been conquered by the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn), and avenge Bran and Rickon. Because he believes his brothers dead and Arya lost, and fearing that, by the laws of succession, were he to die without issue, Winterfell and the north would go to Sansa's new husband, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), Robb discusses the matter of his succession with his mother at [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones). To her chagrin, Robb suggests that, if he were to die, his [bastard](wiki:Bastard) half-brother [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) should succeed him.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

At [Hag's Mire](wiki:Hag's_Mire), Robb sends [Maege Mormont](wiki:Maege_Mormont) and [Galbart Glover](wiki:Galbart_Glover), [Master of Deepwood Motte](wiki:Master_of_Deepwood_Motte), north to [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed), [Lord of Greywater Watch](wiki:Lord_of_Greywater_Watch), to procure [crannogmen](wiki:Crannogmen) who can guide Robb through the [Neck](wiki:Neck) and thus allow him to attack the ironborn unawares at [Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin). Robb provides letters containing false orders in order to protect Maege and Galbart if they are captured. The [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions) capture Jaime Lannister in the field, chop off his sword hand, and bring him to Harrenhal. Instead of returning Jaime to captivity in Riverrun, Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) allows Jaime to continue on to King's Landing.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-1) When Roose asks Jaime to give his regards to his father, Lord Tywin, Jaime says he will do so if Roose conveys his own to Robb.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0)

Because [Grey Wind](wiki:Grey_Wind) is aggressive toward Freys when Robb arrives at the Twins, the direwolf is penned up. When Robb tries to apologize to Lord Frey, Walder instead has the young king beg for pardon from his assembled daughters and granddaughters. Once that is done, Robb and Catelyn ask for food and wine, signifying [guest right](wiki:Guest_right).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0) After Lord Bolton arrives with the remaining northern infantry, mostly [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) and [Karstarks](wiki:House_Karstark), he claims to Robb that Robett Glover had attacked Duskendale out of grief for [Deepwood](wiki:Taking_of_Deepwood_Motte). Having been struck by multiple arrows, Robb weakly calls out to his mother and Grey Wind. Catelyn tries to trade her son's life for [Jinglebell](wiki:Aegon_Frey_(son_of_Stevron)), but Walder cares not for his lackwit grandson. Catelyn watches Roose Bolton deliver the final blow to her son, stabbing him through the heart while stating, "Jaime Lannister sends his regards".(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0) Driven mad with despair, Catelyn murders Jinglebell and is killed shortly thereafter by Ser [Raymund Frey](wiki:Raymund_Frey).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-1)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0) Grey Wind, who had been chained outside to placate the Freys, is also slain via crossbow bolts, his head removed and sewn onto Robb's body as a final insult to the King in the North.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0)

Most of the river lords surrender in the aftermath of the Red Wedding, although Riverrun remains held by the Blackfish.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-0) Roose Bolton is named [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North) and his son Ramsay is legitimized.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-0) Ramsay is also to wed "Arya Stark", but Jaime recognizes that the girl to be presented to House Bolton is an impostor.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Under the leadership of the reborn Catelyn Stark, now called Lady Stoneheart, the vengeful [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) kills [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-1)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-0)(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-0) [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) sees Catelyn holding Robb's crown after reclaiming it from [a whore](wiki:Queen_o'_whores).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-1)

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) peacefully resolves the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun) and takes Robb's widow, [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling), into custody. Jeyne has torn her clothes out of mourning, and her mother, [Sybell Spicer](wiki:Sybell_Spicer), affirms for Jaime that the girl is not pregnant with Robb's child. Sybell has also confiscated the crown which Robb had given the girl.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

In the aftermath of the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall), [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) requests homage from the bannermen of the [north](wiki:North). From [Bear Island](wiki:Bear_Island), [Lyanna Mormont](wiki:Lyanna_Mormont) responds with the message that "Bear Island knows no king but the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), whose name is STARK."(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0)

The Frey envoys at [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor)—[Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey), Ser [Jared](wiki:Jared_Frey), and [Symond](wiki:Symond_Frey)—tell the [Merman's Court](wiki:Merman's_Court) that Robb turned into a [wolf](wiki:Wolf) as a [warg](wiki:Warg) and slew the knights [Wendel Manderly](wiki:Wendel_Manderly), [Tytos Frey](wiki:Tytos_Frey), and [Garse Goodbrook](wiki:Garse_Goodbrook).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-0) Meeting in secret, Wendel's father, Lord [Wyman](wiki:Wyman_Manderly), and [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) assure Stannis's envoy, Lord [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth), that they do not believe the Frey lies, and Wyman tasks Davos with finding Robb's youngest brother, [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-0) The three Freys disappear while traveling from White Harbor to [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_80-0)

[Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) trains as a [greenseer](wiki:Greenseer) in the [cave of the three-eyed crow](wiki:Cave_of_the_three-eyed_crow). In one vision of the [godswood of Winterfell](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell), Bran sees a younger version of his father [Eddard](wiki:Eddard_Stark) praying, "let them grow up close as brothers, with only love between them".(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-0)

Jaime Lannister ends the [siege of Raventree](wiki:Siege_of_Raventree), as [Tytos Blackwood](wiki:Tytos_Blackwood), [Lord of Raventree Hall](wiki:Lord_of_Raventree_Hall), was the last river lord to continue flying the Stark banner.(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-0)

Now a mutilated captive of [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) at Winterfell, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) regrets having sided with his own family, [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy), against House Stark, realizing that Robb had been more a brother to him than his actual kin, [Rodrik](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy) and [Maron](wiki:Maron_Greyjoy).(wiki:Robb_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1)
