## Appearances & Description

The Others are tall and gaunt, with flesh pale as milk.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) They have cold blue eyes that have been described as burning like ice,(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) or being as bright as blue stars.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Others have pale blue blood and shiny bones like milkglass.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
According to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin), the Others "are strange, beautiful… think, oh… the Sidhe made of ice, something like that… a different sort of life… inhuman, elegant, dangerous."(wiki:Others.cite_ref-graphic_5-0) Further, although [Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan) describes the Others as "dead things",(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Martin has stated that the Others are not dead.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-graphic_5-1) However, the [Night's King](wiki:Night's_King)'s queen, possibly an Other due to her blue eyes and pale skin, is described as a "[corpse queen](wiki:Corpse_queen)" on account of her white, cold skin.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
The Others wear delicate, reflective, camouflaging armor that shifts in color with every step.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) According to comic book artist Tommy Patterson, Martin told him that "the reflective, camouflaging armor" is able to pick up "the images of the things around it like a clear, still pond."(wiki:Others.cite_ref-graphic_5-2) According to Patterson, "[Martin] spoke a lot about what [the Others] were not, but what they were was harder to put into words."(wiki:Others.cite_ref-graphic_5-3)
Some Others wield thin crystal swords; the pale blades are extremely sharp, capable of moving through ringmail as if it is silk.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) The swords are alive with moonlight and have a faint blue glow to them.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) When the sword touches a [steel](wiki:Steel) blade, only a high, thin sound, similar to an [animal](wiki:Animal) screaming in pain, can be heard instead of the sound of metal on metal.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) When the blades brush the flames of a torch, a screech as sharp as a needle can be heard.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) When asked what substance the swords of the Others are made from, Martin answered "Ice. But not like regular old ice. The Others can do things with ice that we can't imagine and make substances of it."(wiki:Others.cite_ref-9) The blades the Others use seem to be cold; they are able to cover a metal blade in frost(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) and can shatter a steel blade.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
The Others are capable of resurrecting dead men or creatures as [wights](wiki:Wights)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) through [necromancy](wiki:Necromancy).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Only burning the bodies of the deceased can prevent the Others from resurrecting them.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Martin declined to answer whether or not the Others control these resurrected people and animals in the same way a [warg](wiki:Warg) or [skinchanger](wiki:Skinchanger) can control an animal.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-15) There are tales of Others riding the corpses of dead animals such as [bears](wiki:Bear), [direwolves](wiki:Direwolf), [mammoths](wiki:Mammoth), and [horses](wiki:Horse).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) Some tales also speak of Others riding [giant ice spiders](wiki:Giant_ice_spiders).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)
The Others go lightly on the snow and leave no prints to mark their passage.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Radwd13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Their movements can be lightning quick and graceful.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) Extreme cold accompanies them,(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) but it is unknown whether the Others only come when it is so cold, or whether they bring the cold with them.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4) [White mist](wiki:White_cold) also rises when they are near.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) They might appear during snowstorms or mist, and melt away when the skies clear.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5) They hide from the light of the sun and emerge at night;(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) although once again some stories claim that their coming brings the night.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-7)
The [language](wiki:Languages) the Others speak is unknown, but has been described as sounding like "the cracking of ice on a winter lake", and their laughter as being as sharp as icicles.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)
Ancient texts mention obsidian,(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-8) otherwise called [dragonglass](wiki:Dragonglass)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) or "frozen fire",(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) as a weakness of the Others. Texts also record a weakness to "dragonsteel",(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-9) which some think may be [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-10) When asked about the ability of Valyrian steel to kill an Other, Martin replied that "the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) would like to know as well".(wiki:Others.cite_ref-21) The Others are said to hate [iron](wiki:Iron) and fire.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Records of the Watch indicate that fire dismays the Others,(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-11) and free folk ring their camps with fire to make Others keep their distance.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)

## Origins

### The Long Night[ ]

According to legend, the Others came from the [Lands of Always Winter](wiki:Lands_of_Always_Winter) six or eight thousand years ago, and brought with them cold and darkness that lasted a generation: the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night). They resurrected dead men and animals to serve them.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)
In the [Battle for the Dawn](wiki:Battle_for_the_Dawn), the Others were finally defeated by the first members of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) and the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest), an alliance made possible by the [last hero](wiki:Last_hero).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_long_night.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) When asked whether there was a closer relationship between the Others and the children than there thus far has seemed to be, [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin) replied that it was possible, and that the topic would be explored later on in the story.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-29)
In some stories from the [Further East](wiki:Further_East) of [Essos](wiki:Essos), it is believed that the Long Night was ended through the leadership of [Azor Ahai](wiki:Azor_Ahai), who wielded [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

### The Wall[ ]

The [Wall](wiki:Wall) was built to protect the people of the realms in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) from the threats of the north,(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) specifically the Others.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) According to legend, the structure was built by [Brandon the Builder](wiki:Brandon_the_Builder)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4) with the assistance of [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) and [giants](wiki:Giants),(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) and it is protected with ancient [spells and sorcery](wiki:Magic).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) The [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) have manned the Wall's defense for thousands of years.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_long_night.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1)
The [Night's King](wiki:Night's_King), the thirteenth [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), is said to have married a woman with pale skin and blue eyes, matching the description of the Others. Reportedly, this [corpse queen](wiki:Corpse_queen) was a sorceress. The Night's King brought his bride to the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort), where he proclaimed himself king and her his queen, and bound his Sworn Brothers in the Night's Watch to his will. After a thirteen-year reign, he was defeated by [Brandon the Breaker](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(Breaker)), [King of Winter](wiki:King_of_Winter), and [Joramun](wiki:Joramun), [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall), after which it was discovered that he had been sacrificing to the Others.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)

### Later History[ ]

In his [Lies of the Ancients](wiki:Lies_of_the_Ancients), Archmaester [Fomas](wiki:Fomas) speculates that the Others were a tribe of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) who had been living in the far north. Fomas suggests that the Long Night pressured these men, the ancestors of the current [wildlings](wiki:Free_folk), to migrate south. Over the years, they became more and more monstrous in the telling of the tales about them, because the Night's Watch and the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) wanted to seem heroic. However, Lies of the Ancients is little regarded nowadays, as it contains erroneous claims about [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), the [Reach](wiki:Reach), and the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_long_night.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-2)
The Others have not been seen since the Long Night ended, and are now regarded as nothing more than fairy tales to frighten the little children.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) Some people believe they never existed at all.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

During a ranging [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall), Ser [Waymar Royce](wiki:Waymar_Royce) is confronted by a group of at least five Others who speak an icy language. Waymar duels one of the Others, but the [ranger](wiki:Ranger) is blinded when his sword shatters. The laughing group butchers Waymar, who then rises as a [wight](wiki:Wight) to slay [Will](wiki:Will).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9)
Lord Commander [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), in conversation with [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), refers to white walkers being glimpsed by fisherfolk on the shore near [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea). Tyrion discounts them, however, by mentioning mythical [merlings](wiki:Merlings).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)
[Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan) tells [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) about the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night), when white walkers moved through woods.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)
Traveling in the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood), Bran encounters six [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) and deserters from the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), who discuss whether to take the boy hostage and return north to sell him to [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder), the [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall). Out of fear for encountering the white walkers, one of the men decides not to do so.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

At [Craster's Keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep), [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) tells [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) that [Craster](wiki:Craster) gives up his infant sons to the cold gods; Jon determines she is speaking of the Others.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Encamped at the [Fist of the First Men](wiki:Fist_of_the_First_Men), the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) hear three horn blasts signifying Others. The brothers are then attacked by [wights](wiki:Wights) in the [fight at the Fist](wiki:Fight_at_the_Fist).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasosprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)
While the survivors retreat through the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest) back to Craster's Keep, an Other knocks away [Grenn](wiki:Grenn)'s torch and kills [Small Paul](wiki:Small_Paul). [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) stabs it in the throat with a [dragonglass](wiki:Dragonglass) dagger, however, and Sam hears a sound similar to the cracking of ice beneath one's foot. The Other's armor, flesh, and bones melt away as a result, dissolving away until nothing remains.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9) (wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10) Sam is dubbed Sam the Slayer, which he finds mocking.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-4)(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)
During the [mutiny at Craster's Keep](wiki:Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep), one of Craster's wives warns Sam that Craster's sons will soon arrive for [Gilly's newborn boy](wiki:Monster).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5)
At the parley during the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall), Mance Rayder reveals to Jon that he is trying to lead the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) south of the Wall to protect them from the Others and their wights.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Sam finds limited information about the Others while researching in the library of Castle Black. Sam mentions the [last hero](wiki:Last_hero) slaying Others with [dragonsteel](wiki:Dragonsteel), which makes Jon think of [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel).(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-12)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), now the [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), and [Tormund](wiki:Tormund) discuss their common foe, the Others, when Tormund's four thousand wildlings cross the Wall. Jon asks if the Others troubled the wildlings on their march to the Wall, and Tormund informs him that the Others had been with them all the way, though they never attacked the free folk in force. Rings of fire protected the free folk camps at night, though some willdings were lost if their fires went out.(wiki:Others.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-3)
