## Origins

The Winter Fever, the worst epidemic [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) had suffered since the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers), first appeared on the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters) in late [132 AC](wiki:132_AC). The disease killed half the population of [Sisterton](wiki:Sisterton) on [Sweetsister](wiki:Sweetsister). The survivors believed that the disease had been brought to their shores by whaling ships from the [Port of Ibben](wiki:Port_of_Ibben), so they slaughtered every [Ibbenese](wiki:Ibbenese) they could find.(wiki:Winter_Fever.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
The disease crossed the [Bite](wiki:Bite) to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) in the [north](wiki:North), where it killed Lord [Desmond Manderly](wiki:Desmond_Manderly) and his son, Ser [Medrick Manderly](wiki:Medrick_Manderly). As a result, the lordship of White Harbor passed to Desmond's younger son, Ser [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly), who gave up his seat on the [council of regents](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) of King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and returned north to claim his lordship. From Sisterton and White Harbor, the Winter Fever spread southward through the major ports of the east coast, striking first [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown), then [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), and then [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale). Around the same time, it Winter Fever was reported to have reached [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) as well.(wiki:Winter_Fever.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
The Winter Fever was reported for the first time in King's Landing on the third day of [133 AC](wiki:133_AC). First afflicted were the sailors, ferrymen, fishermongers, dockers, stevedores, and wharfside whores who worked on the riverside of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush). From there, the disease spread through the city. The Winter Fever was confirmed by [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun), who alerted Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Tyland ordered the [City Watch](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) to close the city, so as to prevent anyone from leaving or entering until the disease had run its course. The gates of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) were barred as well, but this could not stop the disease from afflicting those at court.(wiki:Winter_Fever.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) As the disease raged through King's Landing, King Aegon personally visited the sick. When there were almost no new cases being reported anymore, Ser Tyland fell ill, and he became one of the last to die from Winter Fever.(wiki:Winter_Fever.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)
By mid-[133 AC](wiki:133_AC), Winter Fever had most receded.(wiki:Winter_Fever.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) The disease had grown somewhat less potent as it traveled south,(wiki:Winter_Fever.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) but resurfaced back in the north in [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton) during the second half of the year, marking the first time the disease had managed to travel inland this far. Taking advantage of the sickness and famine in the north, a [wildling](wiki:Wildling) chieftain called [Sylas the Grim](wiki:Sylas_the_Grim) led three thousand raiders south of the [Wall](wiki:Wall) and plundered the [Gift](wiki:Gift), until he was stopped by a northern force led by Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark).(wiki:Winter_Fever.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
In total, the Winter Fever was deathly to three out of every four people stricken by the disease,(wiki:Winter_Fever.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8) and is known to have killed almost one fifth of the population of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Winter_Fever.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
On the [Feast Day of Our Father Above](wiki:Feast_Day_of_Our_Father_Above) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), a suspected [Poor Fellow](wiki:Poor_Fellow) had his tongue removed as punishment for preaching that the [Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) has sent the Winter Fever to Westeros as a punishment for [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen)'s [incest](wiki:Incest). One of the last three prisoners condemned that day was a [Pentoshi](wiki:Pentos) merchantman, who had been found guilty of bringing the Winter Fever from Sisterton to King's Landing. The merchantman's head was removed by Ser [Victor Risley](wiki:Victor_Risley), the [King's Justice](wiki:King's_Justice).(wiki:Winter_Fever.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
