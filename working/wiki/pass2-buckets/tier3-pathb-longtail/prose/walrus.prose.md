## Culture

Some [men of the Frozen Shore](wiki:Men_of_the_Frozen_Shore) ride chariots of walrus bones(wiki:Walrus.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) and wear walrus tusks on their heads,(wiki:Walrus.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and one of their leaders is known as the [Great Walrus](wiki:Great_Walrus).(wiki:Walrus.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
[House Farwynd of the Lonely Light](wiki:House_Farwynd_of_the_Lonely_Light) and their [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) are rumored to be [skinchangers](wiki:Skinchanger) who can can take the forms of walruses, [spotted whales](wiki:Spotted_whale), and [sea lions](wiki:Sea_lion).(wiki:Walrus.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf75.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Walrus.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
Walrus tusks make up much of the trade of [Lorath](wiki:Lorath).(wiki:Walrus.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf821_3-1)

## Origins

In some legends from [Lorath](wiki:Lorath), the [mazemakers](wiki:Mazemakers) were destroyed by [walrus-men](wiki:Walrus-men) from the [Shivering Sea](wiki:Shivering_Sea).(wiki:Walrus.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf821_3-2)
[Lord Toad](wiki:Lord_Toad) described Lord [Walderan Tarbeck](wiki:Walderan_Tarbeck) as "a wallowing walrus of a man".(wiki:Walrus.cite_ref-9)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

During the [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk), Lord [Gylbert Farwynd](wiki:Gylbert_Farwynd) includes walrus tusks among the treasures he offers to the assembled [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn). The captains turn away from his gifts, leaving less prestigious men to scramble after them.(wiki:Walrus.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)
