## Culture

Worthy warriors can be considered true steel, in comparison to lesser men.(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0)
Steel is one of the metals used by [maesters](wiki:Maesters) for their chain links.(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)
The [Street of Steel](wiki:Street_of_Steel) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) contains numerous shops and forges.(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
When [priests of the Drowned God](wiki:Priests_of_the_Drowned_God) pray for a believer, they ask that the follower be blessed with salt, stone, and steel.(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0)(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) "[Steel Rain](wiki:Steel_Rain)" is an old reaving song sung by [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn).(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0)
The [Steel Road](wiki:Steel_Road) is one of the three mountain passes through the [Bone Mountains](wiki:Bone_Mountains) in [Essos](wiki:Essos).(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0)

## Origins

The [bronze](wiki:Bronze) weapons of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) were stronger than the stone and [obsidian](wiki:Obsidian) weapons of the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest),(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0) but both peoples were no match for the iron and steel of the [Andals](wiki:Andals).(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1)(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0)
The [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) made a metal of unparalleled quality, [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel).(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.E2.80.99s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-0)
King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) used the [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame) of [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) to forge the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) out of the swords and daggers of his enemies.(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-0)(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-0)
Ser [Byron Swann](wiki:Byron_Swann) meant to kill a dragon during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) with a spear and a shield of silvered steel, thus mimicking the legend of [Serwyn of the Mirror Shield](wiki:Serwyn_of_the_Mirror_Shield) who slew the dragon [Urrax](wiki:Urrax).(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-2)
[Hugh Hammer](wiki:Hugh_Hammer) was said to be so strong he could twist steel bars into torcs with his bare hands.(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-3)
Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) used a steel-tipped whip to [control](wiki:Dragonrider) his dragon [Caraxes](wiki:Caraxes).(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

In return for their service, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) promises to arm several [Vale mountain clans](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans) with steel weapons and armor.(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-5)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

In preparation for his defense of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Tyrion has the blacksmiths of the city forge a chain of immense steel links long enough to cross the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush).(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-0) During the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), the [royal fleets](wiki:Royal_fleet) of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) and his rival, [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), are blocked by the chain and largely destroyed by [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire).(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) learns that the clansmen of the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) have become bolder after being armed with steel.(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Rasos65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

When looking in the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch)'s archives, [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) reads about [dragonsteel](wiki:Dragonsteel), which he and [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) think might have been Valyrian steel.(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-0)
Since the babe was born during the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall), [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) gives the name "[Aemon Steelsong](wiki:Aemon_Steelsong)" to the son of [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) and his late wife, [Dalla](wiki:Dalla).(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_80-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

The company steel of the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons) is poorly maintained.(wiki:Steel.cite_ref-Radwd66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-0)
