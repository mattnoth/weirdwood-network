## Culture

[House Caswell](wiki:House_Caswell) includes a centaur in their sigil.(wiki:Centaurs.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) [Orbert Caswell](wiki:Orbert_Caswell), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), was known as the Black Centaur.(wiki:Centaurs.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Origins

A realm of centaurs supposedly existed in the [eastern grasslands](wiki:Dothraki_sea) of central [Essos](wiki:Essos) during the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age).(wiki:Centaurs.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Archmaester [Hagedorn](wiki:Hagedorn) has put forth the theory that these "centaurs" were no more than mounted warriors, as perceived by neighboring tribes who had not yet learned to tame and ride horses. His views have become widely accepted at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), despite the purported "centaur skeletons" that turn up in [grotesqueries](wiki:Grotesquerie) from time to time.(wiki:Centaurs.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Centaurs.cite_ref-4)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Lady [Barbrey Dustin](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin) compares the late [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark) and [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) to centaurs because of their fondness for riding [horses](wiki:Horse).(wiki:Centaurs.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
