## Origins

Urragon was called upon by his aged father [Torgon](wiki:Torgon_Greyiron) to help him rule the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) in court and council, remaining at his father's side for the best part of five years during war and peace. When Torgon died, Urragon assumed kingship without a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) and no [priest of the Drowned God](wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God) denouncing him.(wiki:Urragon_IV_Greyiron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

Urragon's reign was long but undistinguished. His dying wish was for the high kingship to pass to his great-nephew, [Urron Greyiron](wiki:Urron_Greyiron), but the priests demanded that a kingsmoot be held to select the successor. During the [choosing](wiki:Choosing) at [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk), however, Urron Redhand had his axemen slaughter many of the attendees, and [his line](wiki:House_Greyiron) henceforth ruled hereditarily as [Kings of the Iron Islands](wiki:Kings_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Urragon_IV_Greyiron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Urragon_IV_Greyiron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
