## Origins

The pleasure castle of [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall) was built by King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Prince of Summerhall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) His son, [Maekar](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), received the castle and became known as the Prince of Summerhall.(wiki:Prince of Summerhall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) In time, many sons of House Targaryen would hold the seat as Prince of Summerhall.(wiki:Prince of Summerhall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
When Maekar became king, his eldest son [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) inherited both Summerhall and [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), though he preferred to go only by the title "Prince of Summerhall."(wiki:Prince of Summerhall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
The castle was destroyed in [259 AC](wiki:259_AC) during the reign of Maekar's son, [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), an event that became known as the [Tragedy at Summerhall](wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall).(wiki:Prince of Summerhall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
