## Identity

Aegon the Uncrowned's rebellion was a civil war within House Targaryen in 43 AC, in which Prince Aegon, eldest son of King Aenys I Targaryen, rose against his uncle King Maegor I Targaryen to reclaim the Iron Throne. (wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion)

## Origins

When King Aenys I died in 42 AC, Maegor seized the throne despite Aegon being the rightful heir. Prince Aegon, called "the Uncrowned" because he was never crowned king, raised a coalition of lords to challenge Maegor's rule. (wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion)

## Allegiances

Prince Aegon's supporters included houses from the riverlands and other regions: `FIGHTS_IN` Houses Tully, Piper, Vance, Frey, Harroway, Roote, Paege, Charlton, Corbray, Connington, Dustin, Caswell, Merryweather, Parren, Westerling, Tarbeck, and Peake. Maegor held the Iron Throne with the support of the crown's forces. (wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion)

## Narrative Arc

The rebellion culminated in the Battle Beneath the Gods Eye in 43 AC, the decisive engagement of the war. Prince Aegon rode the dragon Quicksilver against Maegor atop Balerion the Black Dread. Aegon and Quicksilver were slain, ending the rebellion. Maegor consolidated his grip on the Iron Throne. The defeat had lasting consequences, as it left the anti-Maegor faction leaderless and strengthened Maegor's tyrannical rule until his own mysterious death in 48 AC. (wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion)
