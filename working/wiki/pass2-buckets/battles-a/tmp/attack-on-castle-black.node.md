## Identity

The Attack on Castle Black was a major engagement in late 299 AC during the conflict beyond the Wall, in which Mance Rayder's free folk army assaulted Castle Black from both sides of the Wall while the Night's Watch defended with a skeleton garrison. (wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black)

## Origins

Mance Rayder, the King-beyond-the-Wall, united the free folk clans and marched south to breach the Wall and escape the growing threat of the Others. The Night's Watch, already depleted by the disastrous Great Ranging and the mutiny at Craster's Keep, was critically undermanned. (wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black)

## Narrative Arc

The battle unfolded in two phases. First, a Thenn raiding party under Styr, the Magnar of Thenn, attacked Castle Black from the south side, having climbed the Wall earlier with a small force including Jon Snow (who had infiltrated the wildlings as a spy). Jon Snow broke from the wildlings and returned to warn the Watch. The southern assault was repelled, with Styr killed in the fighting. The larger free folk host then assaulted the Wall from the north with giants, mammoths, and a turtle-ram at the gate. The Night's Watch held the Wall through several days of fighting, with Jon Snow taking effective command after Donal Noye was killed fighting a giant in the tunnel. The siege was broken when Stannis Baratheon's army arrived from the east, smashing the free folk host in a surprise attack and capturing Mance Rayder. (wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black)
