## Identity

The Assassination of Tywin Lannister was the killing of Lord Tywin Lannister, Hand of the King, by his son Tyrion Lannister in 300 AC at the Red Keep in King's Landing. (wiki:Assassination_of_Tywin_Lannister)

## Origins

Tyrion had been convicted of the murder of King Joffrey Baratheon at his trial by combat, when his champion Oberyn Martell was killed by Gregor Clegane. Sentenced to death, Tyrion was freed from the black cells by his brother Jaime Lannister with the aid of Varys. (wiki:Assassination_of_Tywin_Lannister)

## Narrative Arc

After Jaime freed him, Tyrion climbed to the Tower of the Hand instead of fleeing directly. He discovered Shae, his former lover, in his father's bed. Tyrion strangled Shae with a chain of golden hands, then found Tywin in the privy. He confronted his father about Tysha, his first wife whom Tywin had ordered gang-raped by his garrison. Tyrion shot Tywin twice with a crossbow, killing him. Tyrion then escaped King's Landing through the tunnels beneath the Red Keep with Varys's assistance. Tywin's death destabilized Lannister power and left Cersei without her most capable political ally. (wiki:Assassination_of_Tywin_Lannister)
