## Appearances & Description

Sigorn is said to look like his father [Styr](wiki:Styr), albeit younger and shorter. He is lean, with grey eyes, and has a receding hairline that makes him look older than his years. He wears [bronze](wiki:Bronze) greaves and a leather shirt sewn with bronze scales.(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Sigorn wields a bronze [sword](wiki:Sword).(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Sigorn and his people take pride in the fact that they are [Thenns](wiki:Thenns), who have [lords](wiki:Lord) and [laws](wiki:Laws), unlike the other [free folk](wiki:Free_folk).(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Sigorn haltingly speaks the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue) with a thick accent.(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

After the death of his father, [Styr](wiki:Styr), during the [attack on Castle Black](wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black), Sigorn inherits the title [Magnar of Thenn](wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn)(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) and is taken captive by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Sigorn kneels before Stannis after passing through the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) It is rumored at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) that Stannis intends to give lands and castles to Sigorn and [Rattleshirt](wiki:Rattleshirt).(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Sigorn is reluctant to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) after the death of his father.(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) [Iron Emmett](wiki:Iron_Emmett) advises [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), to grant a castle to the new Magnar of Thenn.(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Jon eventually devises a plan to wed Sigorn to [Alys Karstark](wiki:Alys_Karstark), the current heir of [Harrion Karstark](wiki:Harrion_Karstark), the rightful [Lord of Karhold](wiki:Lord_of_Karhold), which creates the new [House Thenn](wiki:House_Thenn).(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Alys has fled before a forced marriage arranged by her great-uncle, [Arnolf Karstark](wiki:Arnolf_Karstark), to his son, [Cregan](wiki:Cregan_Karstark), in order to claim [Karhold](wiki:Karhold).(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Alys believes that Karhold will yield to her and Sigorn's two hundred [Thenns](wiki:Thenns).(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

During [the wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Sigorn_and_Alys_Karstark), which is performed at Castle Black by [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), Sigorn leaps across a flaming ditch with his new bride.(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Sigorn is initially reluctant to dance during the wedding feast, but he joins Alys after becoming inebriated from mulled [wine](wiki:Wine).(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) Jon notes that despite his fierceness, Sigorn is surprisingly tender with his new wife.(wiki:Sigorn.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)
