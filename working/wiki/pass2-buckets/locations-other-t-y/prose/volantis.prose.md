## Origins

### Foundation

Volantis is the oldest of the Free Cities, founded at some point after the destruction of the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis).(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) It was the first colony of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold), her "first daughter".(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-15) Established to protect the borders of the Freehold, Volantis was little more than a garrison protected by the [Black Walls](wiki:Black_Walls) for a hundred years.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-27) Wealthy Valyrians used this military outpost to take advantage of trade along the Rhoyne,(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1) however, and settlements quickly grew on both sides of the river. Construction of the [Long Bridge](wiki:Long_Bridge) to cross the Rhoyne began under Triarch [Vhalaso the Munificent](wiki:Vhalaso).(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-28)

### Rhoynish Wars

Volantis grew powerful trading with Valyria and the Rhoynar, although the nearby Rhoynish city [Sarhoy](wiki:Sarhoy) declined. This led to the lengthy [Rhoynish Wars](wiki:Rhoynish_Wars).(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-29)

In the [Second Spice War](wiki:Second_Spice_War), the last of these wars, Volantis and three Valyrian [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) destroyed Sarhoy and sowed the ruins with salt to prevent the Rhoynish city from rising again.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) Prince [Garin](wiki:Garin_(prince)) of [Chroyane](wiki:Chroyane) declared war and marched at the head of the biggest army Essos had seen. He won victories at [Selhorys](wiki:Selhorys), [Valysar](wiki:Valysar), and [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys), where he faced a hundred thousand enemies, a hundred [elephants](wiki:Elephants), and three dragonlords. After Volon Therys, the Volantenes behind the Black Walls asked the Freehold for help. The alliance between Volantis and the Freehold shattered Garin's host, and it is said the blood of the Rhoynar turned the great harbor of Volantis red as far as one could see.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1)

### Century of Blood

After the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), the Volantenes considered themselves the heirs to the Freehold and rightful rulers of the world. They sent a fleet to claim the [Valyrian peninsula](wiki:Valyrian_peninsula), but it vanished in the [Smoking Sea](wiki:Smoking_Sea).(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)

[Tommen II Lannister](wiki:Tommen_II_Lannister), [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock), stayed at Volantis on his way to Old Valyria during the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood). According to *[The Glory of Volantis](wiki:The_Glory_of_Volantis)*, Tommen promised half of what he would find to the [triarchs of Volantis](wiki:Triarchs_of_Volantis) in return for their generosity and potential aid from their fleet. Triarch [Marqelo Tagaros](wiki:Marqelo_Tagaros) sent ships after Tommen the following year, but no sign of the king was found.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

Those in Volantis were divided on how to achieve conquest against the other [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities). Those of the Old Blood favored war, whilst the moneylenders and merchants favored trade. These two factions became known as the tigers and the elephants, respectively. The tigers held sway for almost a century, and Volantis called for war upon the other cities, with the tigers leading the city into a great conflict with the other daughters. They were successful at first, taking control of [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Myr](wiki:Myr). When they tried to take [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), however, [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) joined the Tyroshi, and Lys and Myr rebelled. The [Sealord](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos) of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) provided ships to aid Lys, and [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon), the Westerosi [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King), joined the conflict and led a host into the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands). It was Argilac's host who defeated a Volantene host which had been attempting to retake Myr.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)

[Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), Lord of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), accepted an alliance with Pentos and Tyrosh against Volantis, flew to Lys, and set ablaze a Volantene fleet preparing to invade the city. Volantis was further defeated at [Dagger Lake](wiki:Dagger_Lake), where galleys from [Qohor](wiki:Qohor) and [Norvos](wiki:Norvos) destroyed much of the Volantene fleet which had been on the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne). Even [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) entered the conflict, falling upon the weakened Volantis.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5)

In the end, the Volantene faction favoring peace, the elephants, took power from the tigers, and the fighting was at an end.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6)(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

### Recent History

For the last three centuries, at least two of the three triarchs in power have been elephants.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) Triarch [Horonno](wiki:Horonno), who had been returned as triarch for forty years, declared himself as triarch for life. In response, he was seized by rioters soon after, tied between two elephant animals, and torn apart.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-30)(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-4)

Volantis fought against the combined power of Myr, Lys, and Tyrosh, in the [Battle of the Borderland](wiki:Battle_of_the_Borderland). The victory of the three smaller cities over Volantis led to the start of the [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy), in [96 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

Gold and arms were provided to them by the [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) kings.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Volantis](wiki:Category:Images_of_Volantis)*

Volantis is a port city, and its large, deep harbor is ideally placed. Volantis spreads across the mouth of the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne) and across the hills and marshes on both sides of the river.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4) The older districts of the city lie upon the eastern banks, while the newer districts are located on the west. These two halves are connected by the [Long Bridge](wiki:Long_Bridge).(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

### Eastern Volantis

The [Black Walls](wiki:Black_Walls) of eastern Volantis protect a large labyrinth of palaces, courtyards, towers, temples, cloisters, bridges, and cellars. The Black Walls are a great oval of fused black [dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(material)), harder than [steel](wiki:Steel) or [diamond](wiki:Diamond), built two hundred feet high by the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold). It protects the oldest part of the city on the eastern shore. It is wide enough for six four-horse [chariots](wiki:Chariot) to race around its top abreast, as is done each year to celebrate the founding of the city.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-5)

Many inside the Black Walls keep the old gods of [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), but [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) is favored outside the Black Walls, both by [slaves](wiki:Slaves) and [freedmen](wiki:Freedmen).(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) The [Temple of the Lord of Light](wiki:Temple_of_the_Lord_of_Light) is found in a plaza near the Black Walls, and according to Archmaester [Gramyon](wiki:Gramyon), it is about three times the size of the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8)

### Long Bridge

The [Long Bridge](wiki:Long_Bridge) joins the two halves of Volantis across the mouth of the Rhoyne. It is a great span with a road supported by massive piers that the Valyrians built at the height of their glory. Its gateway is an arch of black stone carved with [sphinxes](wiki:Sphinx), [manticores](wiki:Manticore), [dragons](wiki:Dragons), and other beasts. The road is wide enough for two carts to pass abreast. Buildings rise on either side of the roadway. One can buy almost anything in the shops on the Long Bridge. In the center of the bridge are displayed the hands of thieves and the heads of executed criminals.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9)(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

### Western Volantis

[Fishermonger's Square](wiki:Fishermonger's_Square_(Volantis)), a bustling square in western Volantis, is where fishmongers sell their catches and is often congested with traffic.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-6) The [Merchant's House](wiki:Merchant's_House), located on Fishmonger's Square, is the finest inn in Volantis.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-7)(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

After [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) and [Selyse Florent](wiki:Selyse_Florent) burn Lord [Guncer Sunglass](wiki:Guncer_Sunglass) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), his brother and heir, the new Lord Sunglass, flees for Volantis.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1)

The [Wise Masters](wiki:Wise_Masters) of [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) send envoys to the great slaving city of Volantis seeking aid against [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), the new Queen of Meereen who has been [liberating slaves in Slaver's Bay](wiki:Targaryen_campaign_in_Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Prince [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell) and his two companions, Ser [Gerris Drinkwater](wiki:Gerris_Drinkwater) and Ser [Archibald Yronwood](wiki:Archibald_Yronwood), search for a ship in Volantis to take them to Meereen, but have no luck. They sign on with the [Windblown](wiki:Windblown), who are recruiting in the city, in order to get to [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-19)

In [Selhorys](wiki:Selhorys), [Qavo Nogarys](wiki:Qavo_Nogarys) tells [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) that the [red priest](wiki:Red_priest) [Benerro](wiki:Benerro) preaches that Volantis will surely burn if the triarchs take up arms against Daenerys of Meereen. He states the city thirsts for war.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-5)

The [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) cancel their contract with [Myr](wiki:Myr)(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) and make their way towards Volantis. [Lysono Maar](wiki:Lysono_Maar) sneaks into the city in inquire about transport to Meereen. Although the harbor teems with numerous ships, he concludes they would need to hire a fleet of [pirates](wiki:Pirates) to take the sellswords to Slaver's Bay. [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) thinks the [triarchs of Volantis](wiki:Triarchs_of_Volantis) would be willing to assist them with transport to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) instead, however.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) captures Tyrion in [Selhorys](wiki:Selhorys) and takes him to Volantis. On the way to the [Merchant's House](wiki:Merchant's_House), they pass the [Temple of the Lord of Light](wiki:Temple_of_the_Lord_of_Light). They stop to watch Benerro, who preaches that Daenerys is an [ancient hero](wiki:Azor_Ahai) reborn of the [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) faith and encourages many to support her. The next day Jorah takes Tyrion to meet the [widow of the waterfront](wiki:Widow_of_the_waterfront) in the inn's common room to arrange passage to Meereen. While there, [Penny](wiki:Penny) attacks Tyrion.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-17) On the widow's advice Jorah books passage for himself, Tyrion, and Penny on the *[Selaesori Qhoran](wiki:Selaesori_Qhoran)*, which the red priest Moqorro is taking to Meereen.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2)

While stopping in Volantis, [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), [Lord Captain of the Iron Fleet](wiki:Lord_Captain_of_the_Iron_Fleet), observes galleys heading to Slaver's Bay taking on provisions. The whole city seems drunk. Sailors and soldiers and tinkers are observed dancing in the streets with nobles and fat merchants, and in every inn and winesink cups are raised to the new triarchs. All the talk is of the gold and gems and [slaves](wiki:Slave) that will flood into Volantis once the dragon queen Daenerys is dead.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-3)

In Meereen [Skahaz mo Kandaq](wiki:Skahaz_mo_Kandaq) informs Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) that Volantis has launched [its fleet](wiki:Volantene_fleet) against Daenerys, and that [Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq) means to open Meereen's gates for them.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)

While at the [Isle of Cedars](wiki:Isle_of_Cedars), Victarion thinks that the storms encountered by his [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) would have scattered the Volantenes as they had his own ships. Many Volantene ships might have sunk or run aground, but there still could be as many as five hundred ships sailing for Slaver's Bay.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-4)

Volantene ships transport the Golden Company to Westeros, though the [landing of the sellswords](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company) in the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) is disrupted by storms in the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea).(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

> Old Volantis, first daughter of [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), the [dwarf](wiki:Dwarf) mused. Proud Volantis, queen of the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne) and mistress of the [Summer Sea](wiki:Summer_Sea), home to noble lords and lovely ladies of the [most ancient blood](wiki:Old_Blood). Never mind the packs of naked children that roamed the alleys screaming in shrill voices, or the [bravos](wiki:Bravo) standing in the doors of [wineshops](wiki:Wine) fingering their sword hilts, or the [slaves](wiki:Slaves) with their bent backs and [tattooed](wiki:Tattoo) faces who scurried everywhere like cockroaches. Mighty Volantis, grandest and most populous of the [Nine Free Cities](wiki:Nine_Free_Cities). Ancient wars had depopulated much of the city, however, and large areas of Volantis had begun to sink back into the mud on which it stood. Beautiful Volantis, city of fountains and flowers. But half the fountains were dry, half the pools cracked and stagnant. Flowering vines sent up creepers from every crack in the wall or pavement, and young trees had taken root in the walls of abandoned shops and roofless temples.(wiki:Volantis.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-18)
