## Origins

At [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) secures the support of [Arnolf Karstark](wiki:Arnolf_Karstark), the castellan of [Karhold](wiki:Karhold), but finds himself unable to convince others to join their strength to his.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) When Arnolf informs him that only fifty men remain at the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort), the seat of [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton), he considers marching on the Bolton castle. However, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), counsels against it.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Unbeknownst to Stannis, Arnolf's loyalty is a pretense; In reality, he is loyal to Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), and was attempting to lure Stannis to the Dreadfort to trap him.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Lord Bolton arrives in the [north](wiki:North) with a girl he claims to be [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark), the youngest daughter of the late Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). In reality, however, the girl is [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole), the daughter of [Vayon Poole](wiki:Vayon_Poole), the former steward of Winterfell.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) Roose marries the fake Arya to his [bastard son](wiki:Bastardy), the recently legitimized [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) Their presence at Winterfell is meant to force Stannis to march on them,(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) and indeed, after following the advice of Jon Snow, thereby securing the support of the [northern mountain clans](wiki:Northern_mountain_clans), and [retaking Deepwood Motte](wiki:Fight_by_Deepwood_Motte) from the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn)—resulting in the capture of [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy)(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)—Stannis leads his army from [Deepwood Motte](wiki:Deepwood_Motte) through the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood) to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). Although the southron knights and lords oppose the march, the northern lords insist that Roose must be removed from Winterfell and "the Ned's" daughter rescued from Ramsay. The [march on Winterfell](wiki:March_on_Winterfell) is a hundred leagues long, and is estimated to take fifteen days. However, snows begin on the fourth day, and the harsh weather slow the army down significantly. After thirty-three days, they arrive at a [crofters' village](wiki:Crofters'_village), located three days from Winterfell, but find themselves snowed in. After eleven days, Arnolf Karstark arrives with four hundred and fifty men.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

At Winterfell, the forces Roose Bolton has gathered—consisting of Houses [Ryswell](wiki:House_Ryswell), [Dustin](wiki:House_Dustin), [Frey](wiki:House_Frey), [Cerwyn](wiki:House_Cerwyn), [Hornwood](wiki:House_Hornwood), [Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly), and part of [House Umber](wiki:House_Umber)—suffer from divided loyalties, infighting, and a series of mysterious murders, which cause tension in the castle between the Freys, Boltons, Manderlys, and other northern houses. At first, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) is suspected to be the murderer, but that idea is quickly dismissed by Roose, who declares Theon to be too broken and weak to have carried out the deeds.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

At [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), the [red priestess](wiki:Red_priest) [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) sees a grey girl on a dying horse in her flames. Believing the girl to be Arya Stark, Lord Commander Jon Snow's younger half-sister, she enlists the help of [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder), the former [King-beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-beyond-the-Wall), to rescue the girl.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) The girl eventually arrives at Castle Black, but she turns out to be [Alys Karstark](wiki:Alys_Karstark), who informs Jon Snow that her uncle Arnolf is plotting to betray Stannis.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Jon sends ravens to [Deepwood Motte](wiki:Deepwood_Motte) in an attempt to warn Stannis.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) At Winterfell, the tensions among the army finally erupt into a brawl (in which Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) becomes injured) and Manderly and Frey men are killed after [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)) is found dead. In response, Roose sends out the forces of House Frey and House Manderly, to engage Stannis into battle at the crofters' village.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Theon and Jeyne—believed by Roose's host to be Arya—leap from Winterfell shortly before the Freys and Manderlys leave Winterfell, and the pair are found by [Mors Umber](wiki:Mors_Umber) beneath the walls of the castle. They are brought to the village where Stannis and his army remain by [Tycho Nestoris](wiki:Tycho_Nestoris), a [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) [banker](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos) searching for Stannis. Stannis is informed of Arnolf's planned treachery, and arrests him, his son [Arthor](wiki:Arthor_Karstark), and his three grandsons.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Theon_26-0)

Because it has been snowing severely, the men on the battlefields of Winterfell cannot see anyone beneath the walls, giving [Mors Umber](wiki:Mors_Umber) and his green boys the chance to dig pits outside the gates. Next, they blow their horns to lure their enemies out.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Theon_26-1) [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) sends out the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) from the main gates and the [Manderlys](wiki:House_Manderly) from the eastern gates.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Some Frey men fall into the snow-covered pits placed by Mors, and Ser [Aenys Frey](wiki:Aenys_Frey) breaks his neck. Hosteen Frey loses his horse but survives.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Theon_26-2)

## Aftermath

At [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) receives [a letter](wiki:Bastard_Letter), supposedly written by [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), which claims that [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) is dead and his army is broken. Though it is unknown if the letter's contents are true or if it was indeed written by Ramsay, it galvanizes Lord Commander Snow into breaking with tradition of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) and forming an army of volunteers—black brothers and [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) alike—to march on Winterfell and defeat the Boltons. Jon is attacked in the [mutiny at Castle Black](wiki:Mutiny_at_Castle_Black), however.(wiki:Battle_in_the_ice.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)
