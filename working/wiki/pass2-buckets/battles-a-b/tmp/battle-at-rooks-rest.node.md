## Identity

The Battle at Rook's Rest was fought in late 129 AC during the Dance of the Dragons. It was a trap set by Ser Criston Cole to lure a black dragonrider into an ambush. The title is conjectural. (wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest)

## Allegiances

Part of `FIGHTS_IN` Dance of the Dragons. (track_b: Conflict)

## Narrative Arc

Ser Criston Cole, the Hand of the King for Aegon II, marched a green host through the crownlands toward Rook's Rest, seat of Lord Staunton, a black loyalist. Staunton sent for aid and Queen Rhaenys Targaryen flew to his defense on her dragon Meleys, the Red Queen. This was the trap: King Aegon II, mounted on Sunfyre, and Prince Aemond, on Vhagar, lay in ambush. When Rhaenys engaged, the two green dragons fell upon her. In the aerial battle, Meleys was killed and Rhaenys died — whether from dragonfire or the fall is unknown. Aegon II was severely burned and Sunfyre was crippled, unable to fly again for months. Aemond on Vhagar was the only combatant to emerge unscathed. Some 800 green soldiers were killed by Meleys's fire before the dragon fell. It was a costly victory for the greens that took Aegon II out of the war for much of its remainder. (wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest.cite_ref-Rfab)
