## Identity

The Battle at Fairmarket was fought during the campaign of Harwyn Hoare, King of the Iron Islands, to conquer the riverlands from the Storm Kings of House Durrandon. (wiki:Battle_at_Fairmarket)

## Narrative Arc

Harwyn's ironborn defeated first Samwell Rivers and then Lady Agnes Blackwood, aided in the latter by Agnes's rival Lord Lothar Bracken. King Arrec Durrandon marched from the stormlands with a great host, but a score of river lords — Brackens, Charltons, Goodbrooks, Paeges, and Vyprens — allied with Harwyn hoping for independence. Arrec's stormlanders were harassed and denied supplies. Though Arrec had half again as many men, they were ill-supplied and tired. Harwyn's longships let him seize the river crossing at Fairmarket on the Blue Fork. Arrec was headstrong and indecisive; the Durrandons lost half their men and two of Arrec's brothers fell, though Arrec escaped. The rivermen's joy was short-lived — Harwyn claimed the riverlands, becoming the first King of the Isles and the Rivers.
