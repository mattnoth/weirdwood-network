## Origins

### Early Life

Petyr's [great-grandfather](wiki:Baelish_(sellsword)) was a [Braavosi](wiki:Braavosi) [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) in the service of Lord [Corbray](wiki:House_Corbray); when his [son](wiki:Baelish_(hedge_knight)) became a [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight), he took the stone head of the [Titan of Braavos](wiki:Titan_of_Braavos) as his sigil. Petyr's [father](wiki:Baelish_(lord)), in turn, was the smallest of small lords of a few rocky acres on the smallest of the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers),(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-7) Petyr's mother was named [Alayne](wiki:Alayne_Baelish).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5) Petyr's father once took him to a hermit's cave to see a man said to have the gift of [prophecy](wiki:Prophecy). Lord Baelish gave the hermit a skin of [wine](wiki:Wine) after he groped the boy a bit and said he would be a great man.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6)

Petyr's father befriended [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) during his service in the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings), and this friendship led to Petyr being sent to be fostered as a [ward](wiki:Ward) at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rssm1381.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

### Riverrun

Petyr was raised alongside Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully)'s children, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), and [Edmure](wiki:Edmure_Tully). Because of Petyr's family holdings and his short stature, Edmure gave him the nickname "Littlefinger," by which he is still called.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9) Petyr grew close to the Tully children,(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-8) and he played with Edmure(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) and his sisters. Catelyn recalls how she and Lysa once made mud pies, which Petyr ate, resulting in him being sick for a week.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) When he had a problem, Petyr went to Lord Hoster's brother, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), just as Lord Hoster's children were wont to do.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) Petyr and Edmure once built a castle out of blocks in Hoster's solar.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2)

When Catelyn was twelve, Petyr accompanied the Tullys on a journey to [Seagard](wiki:Seagard). They camped at [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones), where Catelyn pretended to be [Jenny of Oldstones](wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones) and Petyr her [Prince of Dragonflies](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonflies).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Catelyn and Lysa became separated from the rest of their party while riding back from Seagard, but Petyr rode back and found the girls in the fog.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

As the children grew older, Petyr fell in love with Catelyn,(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) who was several years his elder. Catelyn did not reciprocate Petyr's feelings, loving him as a brother instead.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-10)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1) Catelyn's younger sister, Lysa, on the other hand, fell in love with Petyr.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) Petyr would play at kissing with both Catelyn and Lysa in the [godswood of Riverrun](wiki:Godswood_of_Riverrun), attempting to stick his tongue into their mouths. Catelyn stopped Petyr, but Lysa both allowed and enjoyed it.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)

When Lords [Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken) and [Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood) were at Riverrun to settle a feud with Lord Tully's help, Catelyn and Petyr danced six dances. However, when he tried to kiss her, she pushed him away and laughed at him. Petyr, wounded by the rejection, got drunk, passed out at the table, and was brought to his bed by Brynden Tully. Lysa snuck in after him, and gave Petyr her [maidenhead](wiki:Maiden) that night. Still drunk, Petyr believed he was having sex with Catelyn, and he called Lysa "Cat" and told her he loved her before falling asleep.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-39)

In [282 AC](wiki:282_AC), when the date of Catelyn's [wedding](wiki:Wedding) was announced,(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4) Petyr, scarcely fifteen years old, challenged the twenty-year-old Brandon Stark to a duel for her hand.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-11) Petyr arrived with only a helm, breastplate and mail, leading to Brandon taking off most of his own armor. Petyr asked Catelyn for her favor to wear, but she refused, giving it to Brandon instead.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-2)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Catelyn begged Brandon not to kill Petyr, to which he agreed. While Brandon offered Petyr multiple chances to yield, Petyr refused to give up. In the end, Petyr was staggering and bleeding from a dozen wounds, when Brandon ended the battle with a backhand cut so brutal Catelyn was convinced it was mortal.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1)

Petyr was given a fortnight to recover at Riverrun, during which time Lysa helped care for him. Catelyn did not go to see him, and Petyr refused to see Edmure, who had served as Brandon's squire during the duel.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-2)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) During this fortnight, Petyr slept with Lysa again.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-5) He would later claim to have taken the maidenhood of both Tully sisters, due to his belief that his first encounter with Lysa had actually been with Catelyn.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-9)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-2)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rssm1172.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

When the fortnight had passed and Petyr was strong enough, Lord Hoster sent Petyr away from Riverrun. Catelyn did not say goodbye to him.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-3) However, Lysa had become pregnant with Petyr's child as a result of their second sexual encounter. After Petyr had been sent away, Lysa revealed her pregnancy to her father, hoping he would relent and allow Lysa and Petyr to marry. Instead, Hoster, who had believed Petyr to be too lowborn,(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7) forced Lysa to drink [moon tea](wiki:Moon_tea) to abort the pregnancy.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

Back at the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers), Petyr sent Catelyn a single letter after the death of her betrothed, Brandon. However, she burned the letter without reading it.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-12) Catelyn and Lysa married Lords [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) and [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), respectively, in a [double wedding at Riverrun](wiki:Weddings_of_Catelyn_and_Lysa_Tully) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion),(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) and Catelyn went north to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) after the conclusion of the war.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-1) Petyr used to dream about Winterfell, always imagining it as a dark and cold place.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-3)

### Gulltown and King's Landing

[Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) convinced [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), her new husband and [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie), to give Petyr the customs at [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8) as his first post(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-4) to please her,(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-9) and the young man had control of the city's port.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-10) According to Lysa, Petyr increased incomes tenfold.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-11) Lysa convinced Jon to bring Petyr to court in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-5) According to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), in [289 AC](wiki:289_AC) Jon gave Petyr a minor sinecure in customs, where Petyr brought in three times as much as other royal collectors.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-10) Lord Arryn, also the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) for [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), rewarded Petyr with other appointments.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-12) Within three years of coming to the capital, Littlefinger had become [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) and a member of the [small council](wiki:Small_council).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-11)

Petyr increased his influence by moving his own people in place, such as the four [Keepers of the Keys](wiki:Keeper_of_the_Keys), the [King's Counter](wiki:King's_Counter), and the [King's Scales](wiki:King's_Scales), as well as harbor masters, toll collectors, and [wine](wiki:Wine) factors.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-12) Meanwhile, he also developed a complex web of loans, transactions, and investments, as to supply the king and the Hand with enough [gold](wiki:Currency),(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-13) and he bought a number of establishments (including several [brothels](wiki:Prostitution)).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) The crown's revenues have increased tenfold compared to Littlefinger's predecessor as master of coin, although the royal debts of King Robert are vast as well.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-14) The last [Chief Gaoler](wiki:Chief_Gaoler), a former cloth merchant, purchased his position from Petyr.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

Petyr bet on Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) during the [tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_Prince_Joffrey's_name_day), but he lost [his blade](wiki:Littlefinger's_blade) of [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) to King Robert when Jaime was unhorsed by Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-3)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-6) Littlefinger has also boasted at court of having taken [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark)'s virginity.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1) [Oswell Kettleblack](wiki:Oswell_Kettleblack) has been in Petyr's service for a long time.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)

Living in the capital with the elderly Lord Arryn for years with Petyr nearby has been difficult for Lysa.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-13)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-7) She and Petyr started planning a life together,(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-6) and according to a semi-canon source they have begun a secret affair.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-8)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rawoiaflysa_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) When Lysa learned that her husband planned to send their son, [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn), away to be fostered at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) by Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), Petyr provided her with the [poison](wiki:Tears_of_Lys) she used to kill Lord Jon. He also convinced Lysa to send a message to her sister Catelyn at Winterfell, in which she falsely accused Queen [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) and [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) of the murder of her husband.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-2)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-7)

## Appearances & Description

Petyr had been slight and short for his age as a boy, and nearing his thirties, he is still a short man of slender build. Petyr has sharp features and a small pointed beard on his chin.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) His dark hair has threads of silver(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) running through it which are also described as a silver streak.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) He has laughing gray-green eyes(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) like a [cat](wiki:Cat),(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) but [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) notes that when Petyr laughs, his eyes do not.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) His breath is always fresh,(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) smelling like mint,(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) which he had enjoyed chewing as a boy.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Petyr has always been cunning and clever. ## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) travels to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to inform her husband, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), about the attempt on the life of their son [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark). Petyr, having learned of her arrival despite her efforts to remain unnoticed, intercepts Catelyn and has her brought to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). Catelyn is wary of Petyr and Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) when she shows them the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) dagger used in the attempt by a [catspaw](wiki:Catspaw). Petyr says that he recognizes the dagger, claiming that it was once his but that he lost it to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) in a wager during the [tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_Prince_Joffrey's_name_day).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-13) In truth, however, [Littlefinger's blade](wiki:Littlefinger's_blade) had been lost to King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-4)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-9) Petyr promises Catelyn he will help her husband investigate the death of Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), Lord Stark's predecessor as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-1)

After a meeting of the [small council](wiki:Small_council), Petyr brings Eddard to meet with his wife, whom Littlefinger has hidden at one of his [brothels](wiki:Prostitution), and Petyr convinces the new Hand, against his inclination, to make no move against the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) until they have proof. Catelyn feels she has reconnected with her childhood friend.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-2) Eddard remains wary of Petyr after Catelyn departs the capital, but he becomes more trusting after Littlefinger advises him to investigate Lord Arryn's household. Petyr, however, warns Ned not to trust anyone, including himself.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)

During the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney), Petyr loses a hundred [golden dragons](wiki:Currency) to Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) when [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) unhorses Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1) Petyr meets [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), the eldest daughter of Eddard and Catelyn, during the festivities, and he brushes the girl's cheek while touching her hair.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

Acting on Petyr's lie about the dagger, Catelyn arrests Tyrion at the [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1) and takes him to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1) Tyrion insists the dagger used by the assassin was not his, and he tells Catelyn that Petyr has often claimed at court to have taken her virginity.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-2)

When King Robert learns that the exiled Princess [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) is pregnant, Petyr agrees with Robert in advocating her assassination. Ned resigns as Hand in protest of Robert's command, but before he can arrange to travel back to Winterfell, Petyr approaches him with the promise of assistance in his investigation into Jon Arryn's death.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2) Littlefinger leads Ned to [a brothel](wiki:Chataya's_brothel) where [a young whore](wiki:Barra's_mother) is raising [one of Robert's bastard children](wiki:Barra). When Ned is accosted by [red cloaks](wiki:House_Lannister_guards) led by Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) while returning from the city shortly thereafter,(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2) Petyr flees to find [gold cloaks](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing), but Lord Stark is wounded and the [Hand's guards](wiki:House_Stark_guards) are killed in the meantime.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

Petyr attends court while Eddard sits the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in the Robert's absence, as the king has gone hunting in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) Petyr's flight from Jaime's men bothers Lord Stark.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2)

Robert is mortally wounded hunting a boar, and the dying king names Eddard as [Lord Regent](wiki:Regent) and [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm). Petyr advises Ned that despite their knowledge that Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s children are illegitimate and that Robert's brother, Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), is the legal heir, the wisest course of action is to ensure Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon)'s succession, a maneuver calculated to avoid all-out war and maximize their own power. Eddard refuses the suggestion and instead enlists Petyr's help in securing the allegiance of the gold cloaks for when he moves against Cersei.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2) Littlefinger actually bribes the gold cloaks to support the queen in the [throne room](wiki:Throne_room), however. As he recovers his dagger during the [arrest of Eddard Stark](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark), Petyr reminds Ned he had told him not to trust him.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1)

Petyr wears black as the small council mourns Robert.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) As the majority of Eddard's followers in King's Landing have been killed following his arrest,(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) Petyr takes [Vayon Poole](wiki:Vayon_Poole)'s daughter [Jeyne](wiki:Jeyne_Poole), Sansa's friend, into his custody.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) Littlefinger mocks Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) when the famed [knight](wiki:Knight) is publicly dismissed from the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

Varys visits Eddard in the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells) and convinces him to confess treason and [take the black](wiki:Take_the_black).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) Petyr attends Ned's confession at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), where King Joffrey unexpectedly orders Lord Stark's execution.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)

### A Clash of Kings

To pay for Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s plans for the defense of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Petyr imposes a tax on all who enter the capital.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Upon arriving at King's Landing to serve as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) while Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) is in the field, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) realizes Petyr is more dangerous than he had previously known.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) [Varys](wiki:Varys) hints to Tyrion that Littlefinger might have been the one who whispered in [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s ear about his brother  [Robert's](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) bastards not resembling his royal children with Cersei.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)

Tyrion investigates Petyr's past, learning how Petyr came to be on the small council, naming Petyr a "master juggler" when referring to his skill at finances, and noting on the fact that Petyr, due to his low birth and the way he presents himself, is not seen as a threat by anyone.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-15) Realizing Petyr has many of his own men placed into multiple positions, Tyrion decides not to make a move against Petyr concerning the lie he told about Tyrion's involvement in the attack on [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark)'s life, until he can find a way to replace those men with his own.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-16)

During a meeting of the small council, following the proclamation of King Stannis Baratheon that Cersei's children were born out of [incest](wiki:Incest), Petyr proposes they fight Stannis's claims by spreading rumors of their own. Littlefinger suggests they spread the claim that Stannis's daughter, [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon), was fathered by [Patchface](wiki:Patchface), Stannis's fool at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0)

Littlefinger boasts to Tyrion of having taken the virginities of both [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) and [Lysa Tully](wiki:Lysa_Arryn). The Hand suggests sending Petyr to Lysa in the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) to gain [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) for King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), and in return Littlefinger might be rewarded with [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) and named liege lord of the [Trident](wiki:Trident).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-17)

After [the assassination](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)) of King [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), Petyr and Tyrion plot to form an alliance with [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) to win over any lords of the [Reach](wiki:Reach) or the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) that did not immediately declare for Stannis. Petyr volunteers to travel to [Bitterbridge](wiki:Bitterbridge) to meet with Lord [Mace](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) and Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell), and arranges with Tyrion to provide him with an escort of three hundred [gold cloaks](wiki:Gold_cloak) and forty [knights](wiki:Knight), for protection along the road and to appear as an important person.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0) At [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), Petyr negotiates successfully with Lord Mace Tyrell and his mother, [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell),(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-14) forging a Lannister-Tyrell alliance through the engagement of King Joffrey and [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), Mace's daughter.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0)

Petyr returns to King's Landing with Tyrell forces alongside those of Tywin Lannister. The allied Lannister and Tyrell armies are able to take Stannis's forces in the rear in the last hours of the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), with Petyr providing the idea to dress Ser [Garlan Tyrell](wiki:Garlan_Tyrell) in King Renly's armor during the battle so as to frighten Stannis's men-at-arms.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) After the battle, Petyr is rewarded with the title [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident) and is named [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal), despite the fact that the castle is still held by Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) for [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North). The titles give Petyr nominal control over the war-torn [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) and makes him a high lord.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-2)

### A Storm of Swords

[Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) discretely meets with Ser [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard), who vaguely tells the girl of a plan to return her home and gives her a silver hair net.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-3) Unbeknownst to Sansa, however, the [fool](wiki:Fool) is secretly in Littlefinger's employ.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) Petyr also acquires key informants in the crown's inner circle in Ser [Osney](wiki:Osney_Kettleblack), Ser [Osfryd](wiki:Osfryd_Kettleblack), and Ser [Osmund Kettleblack](wiki:Osmund_Kettleblack),(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-1) who had been hired to guard Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) after [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) sent [Vylarr](wiki:Vylarr) and his [red cloaks](wiki:Red_cloaks) away.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0) Tyrion believes that the Kettleblack brothers are in his pay,(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-2) when in truth their allegiance is to Petyr.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-15)

During a session of the [small council](wiki:Small_council), Petyr informs Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) of the [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) plot to spirit [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) and marry her to [Willas Tyrell](wiki:Willas_Tyrell), giving Tywin the necessary time to wed her in haste to his son Tyrion instead.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

Petyr's newfound status as a high lord gives him the necessary standing to be considered by Tywin for a marriage to the widowed [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) with the intent of delivering the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), which has remained neutral, to the crown without violence. Petyr agrees with the plan and is sent to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) to woo Lysa, believing that to be an easy task given their history. Tyrion is appointed as [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) upon Petyr's departure. He warns Tywin, Cersei, and Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) that Petyr's scheming and wealth makes him dangerous even to their family, despite the Lannisters' [gold](wiki:Gold).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

Though Petyr has left King's Landing, he does not go immediately to the Vale, and instead secretly remains near the city.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-10) [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) marries [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), the boy king [chokes to death](wiki:Purple_Wedding) at their wedding feast.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0) In the ensuing chaos, Sansa Stark is spirited out of the city by a drunken Dontos Hollard, and [Oswell Kettleblack](wiki:Oswell_Kettleblack) rows them to a ship on [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay) where Petyr awaits. When Ser [Lothor Brune](wiki:Lothor_Brune)'s crossbowmen kill Dontos, Petyr explains to Sansa that she will be wanted in connection with Joffrey's death and that Dontos's death is the only sure way to know he will remain silent. Petyr also brags of his involvement in arranging a set of [jousting dwarfs](wiki:Penny) as entertainment, which he explains were calculated to bring Tyrion Lannister into conflict with Joffrey just prior to the king's death. Confident that Tyrion will be executed, he notes to Sansa that widowhood will become her.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-1)

Petyr and Sansa travel on the *[Merling King](wiki:Merling_King)* to the Vale. Instead of continuing on to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) so that Sansa can be returned to the [north](wiki:North), they disembark near Petyr's "[Drearfort](wiki:Drearfort)", his ancestral home on the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers). Petyr gives Sansa the assumed identity of "Alayne Stone", his [bastard](wiki:Bastard) daughter named after [his own mother](wiki:Alayne_Baelish), and he decides that "Alayne's" mother was a gentlewoman of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) who died in childbirth.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-16) Petyr explains his role in Joffrey's murder, having plotted it with [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell). He reveals the involvement of the hair net laced with black [amethysts](wiki:Amethyst) that Dontos had insisted Sansa wear to the feast. The amethysts were in reality the [strangler](wiki:Strangler), the poison used to kill Joffrey; Sansa had unwittingly smuggled them into the feast.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-17)

Lysa arrives at Petyr's tower and insists upon [marrying him immediately](wiki:Wedding_of_Petyr_Baelish_and_Lysa_Arryn) instead of having a grander wedding at the Eyrie. He reluctantly agrees, and Lysa's ladies convince Sansa to help undress Petyr during the [bedding custom](wiki:Customs). As they have sex, Lysa yells out that she wants Petyr to give her a child.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-18) Littlefinger tasks Ser Lothar Brune with protecting Sansa.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-19) Petyr informs Lysa that "Alayne" is actually her niece Sansa, so Lysa has the girl's hair darkened to help hide her identity. Lysa decides to name Petyr as [Lord Protector of the Vale](wiki:Lord_Protector_of_the_Vale) and to betroth Sansa to her own son, Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-20)

They travel to the Eyrie after the wedding, but Petyr spends much of his time at the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon) trying to asserting his authority. Lysa convinces him to shave off his beard. Many lords of the Vale resent Petyr for his marriage and title.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-8)

While helping Sansa build a snow castle of her family's ancestral home, [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), Petyr kisses the girl in the [godswood of the Eyrie](wiki:Godswood_of_the_Eyrie). When Sansa objects, Petyr notes that Sansa is growing into a beautiful young woman and that she looks much like her mother [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) did at that age. Young Lord Robert arrives and destroys the snow castle.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-9)

Lysa witnessed the kiss, and in a fit of jealous rage attempts to kill Sansa in the [High Hall](wiki:High_Hall) later that day, nearly shoving her out the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door). Petyr arrives in time to intervene, at which point Lysa breaks down crying. She accuses Petyr of not loving her despite all she had done for him. She reveals in Sansa's hearing that it had been Petyr who had convinced her to [poison](wiki:Tears_of_Lys) her previous husband, Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), to prevent him from sending Robert away to be fostered. Lysa also admits that it had been Petyr who had convinced her to write a letter to her sister Catelyn, stating that Jon had been poisoned and that the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) were responsible. Petyr temporarily soothes Lysa, assuring her that he has only ever loved one woman. He then adds "only Cat" and shoves Lysa out the Moon Door to her death. The killing is blamed on the singer [Marillion](wiki:Marillion).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-10)

### A Feast for Crows

Following Lysa's death, Petyr remains Lord Protector of the Vale, with Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) as his ward. When Lord [Nestor Royce](wiki:Nestor_Royce), [High Steward of the Vale](wiki:High_Steward_of_the_Vale) and [Keeper of the Gates of the Moon](wiki:Keeper_of_the_Gates_of_the_Moon), arrives at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), Sansa, disguised as "Alayne", verifies Petyr's claim that [Marillion](wiki:Marillion) murdered Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn). Petyr gains Nestor as an ally by granting him the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon), stating it had been the desire of the late Lysa.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2)

Six of the most powerful lords of the Vale gather at [Runestone](wiki:Runestone), planning on removing Petyr as Lord Protector. In [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) decides that these [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant) can participate in deciding who rules over the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) during Robert's minority, but Petyr is not to be harmed.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) Littlefinger asks Cersei to send him tapestries owned by her late husband, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2)

The levies of the Lords Declarant surround the Gates of the Moon,(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3) and the rebel lords ascend the [Giant's Lance](wiki:Giant's_Lance) for a parley. Petyr refuses their demand to leave the Vale and assume rule over [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) in the war-torn [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), and Nestor Royce defends Petyr during the meeting. Ser [Lyn Corbray](wiki:Lyn_Corbray) behaves belligerently, going so far as to draw his [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword [Lady Forlorn](wiki:Lady_Forlorn), breaching [guest right](wiki:Guest_right). Now holding leverage over the Lords Declarant, Petyr successfully demands he be given a year to set the Vale to rights. After the Lords Declarant depart, Petyr notes to Sansa how fortuitous Lyn's action was, and Sansa correctly guesses that Lyn must be on his payroll. Petyr plans to use his time as Lord Robert's regent to eliminate, win over, or marginalize the lords who are currently against him. He will allow Lyn to use his supposed disgust for him to facilitate his joining, and covertly informing on, any conspiracy against the Lord Protector.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-4)

Petyr arranges a marriage between Lyn's elder brother, Lord [Lyonel Corbray](wiki:Lyonel_Corbray), and a [merchant's daughter](wiki:Lady_Corbray), and he then attends [their wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Lyonel_Corbray_and_Lady_Corbray) in [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown). With winter approaching, the Arryn court departs the Eyrie for the Gates of the Moon. Petyr returns from Gulltown with three [hedge knights](wiki:Hedge_knight) he has hired, Ser [Byron the Beautiful](wiki:Byron_the_Beautiful), Ser [Morgarth the Merry](wiki:Morgarth_the_Merry), and Ser [Shadrich of the Shady Glen](wiki:Shadrich_of_the_Shady_Glen).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) Unbeknownst to the Arryn court, Shadrich has been searching for Sansa because of the [gold](wiki:Gold) offered as a reward for finding her by [Varys](wiki:Varys).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0)

When they are alone, Petyr tells Sansa that Cersei's leadership in King's Landing is bringing the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) to ruin faster than he had anticipated. He informs Sansa that he means to marry her to Ser [Harrold Hardyng](wiki:Harrold_Hardyng), the heir of the young and sickly Lord Robert. Once the deaths of Robert and [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) are confirmed, Sansa's true identity can be revealed at her wedding with Lord Harrold and the [knights of the Vale](wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale) will help her regain [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) in the [north](wiki:North). Littlefinger then asks Sansa for another kiss.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-2)

During the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) reminds [Emmon Frey](wiki:Emmon_Frey) that although he was named [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun), Littlefinger is his liege as the [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0) After resolving the siege, Jaime considers Petyr as a possible [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) for [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3)

### A Dance with Dragons

Following the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) produce a girl whom they claim to be the missing [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark). They send the girl to marry [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton)'s son, [Ramsay](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), helping to cement the alliance between [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) and the crown.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0) [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) later learns that the girl sent by the Lannisters to marry Ramsay Bolton is not Arya, but a disguised [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole),(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0) who had been whipped and trained while being kept in one of Petyr's brothels.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0)(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Rawoiafjeyne_poole.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0)

While mulling over [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) recalls that before the Lannisters married her to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), Petyr had offered to marry Sansa himself, but his offer was rejected on account of his low birth.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0)

With the crown having huge financial problems due to the follies of Cersei, Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) wishes that Petyr was back in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) as master of coin.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0)

### The Winds of Winter

After Sansa makes the suggestions, Petyr arranges for a tourney to be held at the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon) to choose the [Brotherhood of Winged Knights](wiki:Brotherhood_of_Winged_Knights). Littlefinger gives King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s tapestries to Lord [Nestor Royce](wiki:Nestor_Royce), and he advises Lords [Benedar Belmore](wiki:Benedar_Belmore) and [Gerold Grafton](wiki:Gerold_Grafton) to hoard their harvests until the price of grain goes up.(wiki:Petyr_Baelish.cite_ref-73)
