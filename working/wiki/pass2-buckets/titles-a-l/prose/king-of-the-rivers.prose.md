## Origins

During [the Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) slew [Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare), [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), in the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:King_of_the_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) The realm of Harren the Black was then divided between the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands), ruled by [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy), and the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), ruled by [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully). Within the riverlands, Aegon granted rule over [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) to [House Qoherys](wiki:House_Qoherys).(wiki:King_of_the_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Soon after Aegon's son, [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), ascended the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), [Harren the Red](wiki:Harren_the_Red) seized Harrenhal from Lord [Gargon Qoherys](wiki:Gargon_Qoherys), slew him, and declared himself [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal) and King of the Rivers. Red Harren claimed dominion over the riverlands but not the Iron Islands, as he was not [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn).(wiki:King_of_the_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) [Harren the Red's rebellion](wiki:Harren_the_Red's_rebellion) was eventually suppressed and the outlaw king slain.(wiki:King_of_the_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
