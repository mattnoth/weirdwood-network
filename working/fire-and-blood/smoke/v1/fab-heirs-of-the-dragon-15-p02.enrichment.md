## Node Prose

### Otto Hightower
Ser Otto, the King's Hand, conceived a great dislike of Daemon and worked to strip him of office — quote: "It was Ser Otto who convinced Viserys to remove Prince Daemon as master of coin". He wrote to his brother the Lord of Oldtown against Daemon's succession — quote: "He would be a second Maegor the Cruel, or worse." At that time he favored Rhaenyra's claim — quote: "Better the Realm's Delight than Lord Flea Bottom". As Alicent's father he became the loudest of her supporters, and in 109 AC Viserys stripped him of his chain — quote: "in 109 AC Viserys stripped Ser Otto of his chain of office".

### Viserys I Targaryen
King Viserys hated dissension and was easily led — quote: "he relied greatly on the counsel of the men around him". He longed for a son and, after Aemma and the infant Baelon died, named Rhaenyra his heir — quote: "Viserys declared his daughter, Rhaenyra, to be his rightful heir". Over Runciter's counsel he wed Alicent Hightower in 106 AC — quote: "He announced his intention to wed Lady Alicent of House Hightower". He kept an anxious balance between his wife's and daughter's parties — quote: "He strove all his days to keep the peace between his women".

### Daemon Targaryen
The king's brother commanded the City Watch's two thousand men — quote: "with two thousand men under his command, Daemon waxed more powerful than ever". Furious at Rhaenyra's elevation he quit the capital with his paramour Mysaria on Caraxes — quote: "taking his paramour Mysaria with him upon the back of his dragon Caraxes". He warred on the Stepstones with Corlys and slew Craghas Crabfeeder in 108 AC — quote: "he slew him singlehanded and cut off his head with Dark Sister". He crowned himself King of the Stepstones — quote: "Prince Daemon declared himself King of the Stepstones and the Narrow Sea". After a public reconciliation in 111, a scandal over Rhaenyra led Viserys to exile him — quote: "King Viserys sent him into exile, never to return".

### Rhaenyra Targaryen
Named Princess of Dragonstone and her father's heir, hundreds of lords did her obeisance — quote: "hundreds of lords did obeisance to the Realm's Delight". She was smitten with Criston Cole, her sworn shield — quote: "the man she called 'my white knight'". She was the center of two rival accounts of scandal involving her uncle and Cole, was forced to consent to marry Laenor under threat, and bore a son in 114 AC — quote: "in the end she consented to be married". She always styled Alicent's sons her halfbrothers — quote: "took care to refer to Queen Alicent's sons as halfbrothers".

### Criston Cole
Ser Criston, son of a steward of Lord Dondarrion, joined the Kingsguard in 105 AC after winning the accession melee at Maidenpool — quote: "he won the melee held at Maidenpool in honor of King Viserys's accession". A comely knight of three-and-twenty, he became Rhaenyra's sworn protector and constant companion — quote: "name Ser Criston her own personal shield and protector". After her marriage his devotion turned to hatred — quote: "the man who had hitherto been the princess's constant companion and champion became the most bitter of her foes". Named Lord Commander in 112, he went over wholly to the greens.

### Alicent Hightower
The clever, lovely eighteen-year-old daughter of the King's Hand, she had read to the dying King Jaehaerys — quote: "the girl who had read to King Jaehaerys as he lay dying". She wed Viserys in 106 AC and bore him Aegon, Helaena, Aemond, and Daeron — quote: "she bore the king a healthy son, naming him Aegon, after the Conqueror". Her early warmth to Rhaenyra soured into open rivalry — quote: "'who protects the princess from Ser Criston?'". She led the "greens" and pressed her sons' claims.

### Corlys Velaryon
The Sea Snake, Lord of the Tides, was as wealthy and powerful as any man in the realm and resented House Velaryon's repeated slights — quote: "his daughter, Laena, scorned just as his son, Laenor, had been scorned". He allied with Daemon to break the Triarchy's grip on the Stepstones, commanding the fleet — quote: "Lord Velaryon would command the fleet, Prince Daemon the army". He later crowned Daemon King of the Stepstones and secured the match of Laenor to Rhaenyra.

### Laenor Velaryon
Corlys's son, a grandson of Prince Aemon and great-grandson of the Old King, was chosen as Rhaenyra's consort though he had never shown interest in women — quote: "yet had never shown any interest in women". Wed to Rhaenyra in 114 AC at twenty, he preferred High Tide and favorites such as Ser Qarl Correy — quote: "he soon found a new favorite in a household knight named Ser Qarl Correy". He wished to name Rhaenyra's son Joffrey but was overruled by Corlys.

### Lyonel Strong
Lord of Harrenhal, a big balding man reputed a battler but learned, having earned six links at the Citadel — quote: "earning six links of his chain before deciding that a maester's life was not for him". Thrice-widowed, he brought two daughters and two sons to court and joined the small council as master of laws — quote: "Thrice-wed and thrice a widower". In 109 AC he replaced Otto as Hand — quote: "'This Hand will not hector me'".

### Harwin Strong
Ser Harwin, called Breakbones, Lyonel's eldest son and heir to Harrenhal, said to be the strongest man in the realm, was made a captain of the gold cloaks — quote: "made a captain in the gold cloaks". Mushroom names him the man who took Rhaenyra's innocence — quote: "It was he who took Rhaenyra's innocence". After her wedding he became foremost of the blacks at her side — quote: "becoming the foremost of the blacks".

### Larys Strong
Lyonel's younger son, "Larys the Clubfoot," joined the king's confessors — quote: "Larys the Clubfoot, joined the king's confessors".

### Mysaria
Daemon's paramour, whom he took to Dragonstone and got with child before giving her a dragon's egg — quote: "Prince Daemon presented her with a dragon's egg". Commanded away, she was sent back to Lys and miscarried in a storm — quote: "Mysaria lost her child during a storm on the narrow sea".

### Laena Velaryon
Daughter of Corlys and Rhaenys, freshly flowered at twelve, who had claimed Vhagar for her mount — quote: "had claimed for her own no less a mount than mighty Vhagar". Runciter urged Viserys to wed her; she cared more for flying than for suitors — quote: "'shows far more interest in flying than in boys'".

### Craghas Drahar
The Myrish prince admiral who led the Triarchy's conquest of the Stepstones and earned his sobriquet by staking out captured pirates to drown — quote: "he staked out hundreds of captured pirates on the wet sands". Daemon slew him singlehanded in 108 AC.

### Runciter
Grand Maester Runciter first urged the king to remarry and proposed Laena Velaryon — quote: "Grand Maester Runciter was the first to urge His Grace to remarry". He records Daemon resuming his council seat and the brothers' second quarrel; he died in 112 AC.

### Joffrey Lonmouth
Ser Joffrey, "the Knight of Kisses," was Laenor's favorite; Rhaenyra's husband gave him a garter at the wedding — quote: "the groom's favorite, Ser Joffrey Lonmouth, known as the Knight of Kisses". Criston Cole cracked his helm at the wedding tourney and he died six days later — quote: "Ser Joffrey died without recovering consciousness six days later".

### Aemma Arryn
Queen Aemma was brought to bed in Maegor's Holdfast and died bearing the son Viserys had long desired — quote: "died whilst giving birth to the son that Viserys Targaryen had desired for so long".

### Jacaerys Velaryon
Rhaenyra's son, born in the waning days of 114 AC, a strapping lad with brown hair, brown eyes, and a pug nose — quote: "a large, strapping lad, with brown hair, brown eyes, and a pug nose". Given a Velaryon name over Laenor's wish to call him Joffrey — quote: "the child was given a traditional Velaryon name: Jacaerys".

### Mellos
Grand Maester Mellos was sent from the Citadel to replace Runciter and dismissed concern over Laenor's tastes — quote: "'I do not like the taste of fish, but when fish is served, \| eat it.'"

### Johanna Swann
A fifteen-year-old niece of the Lord of Stonehelm, enslaved by the Lyseni when her uncle refused her ransom, who rose to become the celebrated courtesan the Black Swan — quote: "she rose to become the celebrated courtesan known as the Black Swan".
