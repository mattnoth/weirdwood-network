# THK — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** THK
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk (Ser Duncan the Tall)
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** Ashford Meadow (with Ashford Castle and Ashford town)
- **location_secondary:** A low hill on the road (Ser Arlan's grave); an inn beside a stream a day's ride from Ashford; Dunk's camp by the green pool beneath the elm; a tower cell in Ashford Castle
- **approximate_timeline:** Opens with Ser Arlan's burial; Dunk rides to Ashford, sells a horse, buys armor, enters the lists; covers the first and second days of the tourney, the puppet-show assault, Dunk's arrest, the trial of seven, Baelor's death and funeral, and the aftermath where Dunk takes Egg as squire.
- **time_markers:** spring (spring rains, spring grass, spring air); the old man's dying: rains "the morrow," wet gusty winds "the one after," a chill "the next," "By the fourth day the old man was too weak to ride," "now he was gone," "Only a few days past he had been singing"; "A day's ride" from the inn to Ashford; "It was full dark" when Dunk left the inn; "the shadows of the afternoon were growing long" arriving at the meadow; first day of tourney "dawned bright and clear"; second day "overcast"; "The night was half gone" before the trial; "the first wan light"/"dawn"; Ser Arlan "closer to sixty than to fifty"; Dunk "sixteen or seventeen years"; Egg "no more than eight or nine," "eight-year-old"; the fair maid's "thirteenth nameday"; Daeron "has only eighteen years"; the last dragon seen "the year before it died"; Otho Bracken slew Quentyn Blackwood "three years past"; Arlan unhorsed Damon Lannister "sixteen years ago" at King's Landing; Storm's End hastilude "nine years past"; Robyn Rhysling lost his eye "not five years earlier"; "more than a hundred years" since the last trial of seven; Ser Arlan had not ridden a tilt "many years before"; Ser Humfrey Hardyng died "only last night."
- **chapter_summary:** After burying his master Ser Arlan of Pennytree, the hedge knight Dunk takes the old man's arms and rides to the tourney at Ashford Meadow, taking on a bald orphan stableboy named Egg as his squire. Dunk sells a horse to buy new armor and, with Prince Baelor's intervention, is permitted to enter the lists. When Dunk beats Prince Aerion Brightflame for assaulting the puppeteer Tanselle, he is arrested for striking the blood of the dragon and faces a trial of seven. Six knights and ultimately Prince Baelor Breakspear himself take Dunk's side against Aerion, Daeron, Maekar, three Kingsguard, and the turncoat Ser Steffon Fossoway; Dunk forces Aerion to yield, but Baelor dies of a head wound dealt by his brother Maekar's mace. Egg is revealed to be Prince Aegon Targaryen, and his father Maekar gives him to Dunk as squire, the two riding off toward Dorne.
- **unit_part:** whole

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Spring rains that softened the ground; wet gusty winds; a chill; rain threatening as Dunk dug the grave; first tourney day "bright and clear"; second day "overcast, with a gusty wind blowing from the west"; a soft rain falling "light as dew" the night before the trial; the trial morning rain tapering off at first light leaving fog; "Tendrils of fog were writhing along the ground like pale white snakes"; "pale blowing mist"/"river mist" on the field; "bleak grey sky."
- **Season indicators:** Spring throughout — "spring rains," "spring grass," "the spring air," "at least he had lived to see another spring"; lore that "the summers have been shorter since the last dragon died, and the winters longer and crueler."
- **Time of day:** Sunset (Arlan loved to watch the sunset; Dunk buries him on the western slope); full dark leaving the inn; moonlit, cloudless, star-speckled night riding to the meadow; "It was full dark"; a falling star at night; cock crowing at dawn; torchlit evenings on merchants' row; dawn of the trial.
- **Lighting:** Warm yellow light spilling from the inn windows; torchlight along merchant's row ("a hundred torches"); firelight of campfires; an oil lamp and an iron lantern; a brazier of coals; beeswax candle; "colored rings haloed the scant few pavilions where fires still burned."
- **Sounds:** The old song Arlan sang; a robin heard at the dubbing; a horse whickering; cock crowing; distant tourney music ("pipes and drums"); the ring of hammers/carpenters; trumpets and herald's horns; the crash of lances, ring of steel; the crowd's roar "like the crash of distant waves"; the crack of Tanselle's finger breaking; the screams of Ser Humfrey's dying horse "almost human"; a man screaming "high and terrible"; "the faint thrum of the dragonfly's wings."
- **Smells:** The smell of rain in the air; woodsmoke and bacon; sausages sizzling over a smoky fire; roasting goat basted with honey and herbs.
- **Notable sensory details:** A dragonfly skimming the green pool (recurs at the close); the field churned to "grey-brown mud and torn grass" after rain; the heat and packed mud inside Dunk's greathelm during the trial; the "blue-grey and pulpy" matter and bone visible in Baelor's crushed skull; a green falling star streaking the black sky.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief over Ser Arlan's death; loneliness ("he only has me"); anxious hope about competing; awe and intimidation before lords and princes; shame at his low birth and clumsiness with words ("Dunk the lunk, thick as a castle wall"); terror facing the trial and possible mutilation; despair when no knight will join him; guilt and grief after Baelor dies for his sake.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Proving himself a true knight rather than a hedge knight — winning "one victory" to earn a place in noble company; later, surviving the trial of seven; finally, what he owes Baelor and what to do with Egg.
- **Key decisions made:** To bury Ser Arlan and take his arms; to take Egg as squire; to sell Sweetfoot and buy armor; to enter the lists under the name "Ser Duncan the Tall"; to defend Tanselle by attacking Aerion; to choose trial by combat over mutilation; to swear himself Baelor's man; to take Aegon as squire on the road rather than at Summerhall, heading for Dorne.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself the stableboy will scoop up the spurned copper "as soon as I am gone"; insists "one victory is all I need"; rationalizes that taking Egg from the inn "would be no kindness"; convinces himself the smallfolk's goodwill and the painted "death" shield are ill omens, then accepts Pate's reframing; repeatedly braces against the old man's verdict that he is "thick as a castle wall."
