# TSS — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** TSS
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk (Ser Duncan the Tall)
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** Standfast and the Osgrey lands (the Chequy Water, Wat's Wood, the dam) in the Reach
- **location_secondary:** The crossroads with the iron crow cage; the plank bridge over the Chequy Water; Coldmoat Castle (its yard, sept, audience chamber, stables, and maester's tower); the three Osgrey villages; recalled: King's Landing, Dorne, Redgrass Field
- **approximate_timeline:** Opens as Dunk and Egg return from Dosk with wine and find two dead men caged at the crossroads; the stream is found dammed; Bennis cuts a digger; smallfolk are levied and drilled; Dunk rides to Coldmoat to parley and learns Ser Eustace was a Blackfyre rebel and the stream is legally the Webbers'; Wat's Wood is burned that night; Dunk quits and then stays; a confrontation at the stream ends in a trial by battle where Dunk kills Ser Lucas the Longinch and nearly drowns; Ser Eustace and Lady Rohanne reconcile and wed; Dunk and Egg depart, turning north toward the Wall.
- **time_markers:** Summer; "the drought entering its second year"; Dunk and Egg gone to Dosk "six days"; Ser Arlan "two years dead"; the Redgrass Field / Blackfyre Rebellion "fifteen years ago"; the Great Spring Sickness "two years past" / dead "in the spring"; Egg has served Dunk "a good year and a half"; Aemon measured Dunk in Oldtown "half a year ago"; Rohanne must wed by "the second anniversary of her father's passing" / "the next new moon"; soldiers might "in a fortnight" stand against other peasants; the Field of Fire "two hundred years ago"; the Osgreys "Marshalls of the Northmarch for a thousand years"; Rohanne "five-and-twenty"; her first husband died when she was ten and he twelve; her second husband near "five-and-fifty," she thirteen, the infant son lived "three days"; events span several days ("on the morrow," "evenfall," "come the break of day," night of the fire, the morning of the battle, recovery "the day they took their leave"); rain returns at the close after a long drought.
- **chapter_summary:** The hedge knight Ser Duncan the Tall, sworn sword to the landed knight Ser Eustace Osgrey of Standfast, discovers that the Chequy Water has been dammed by Lady Rohanne Webber, the Red Widow of Coldmoat, after his fellow sellsword Ser Bennis cuts the face of one of her diggers. Sent to parley, Dunk mistakes Lady Helicent for the Widow, learns the stream was granted to House Webber by royal decree because Ser Eustace was a rebel for Daemon Blackfyre, and is slapped for invoking the name of Addam Osgrey, the Widow's dead love. That night Wat's Wood burns; Dunk quits Eustace's service over the old man's lies but returns to make a stand at the stream. When Lady Rohanne demands Bennis or a trial, Dunk cuts his own cheek to pay the blood price and fights her castellan, Ser Lucas the Longinch, in a trial by battle, killing him and nearly drowning himself. Reconciled over Addam's grave, Ser Eustace and Lady Rohanne wed, making Eustace lord of Coldmoat; Dunk, healed by the ironborn maester Cerrick, departs with Egg — secretly Prince Aegon Targaryen — riding north toward the Wall.
- **unit_part:** whole

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Blistering summer heat and a years-long drought throughout — "the sky was blue and the sun was blazing hot"; baked roads "hard as brick"; no cloud "anywhere to be seen"; nights "hardly cooler than the days"; a rising wind on the day of battle; dark clouds gathering, then steady rain returning at the close after the drought breaks.
- **Season indicators:** Summer ("the summer sun"); the second year of drought; shriveling melons, dead and dying grain, dry wells, low rivers (Blackwater Rush and the Mander running low), the stream dried to sand and stones.
- **Time of day:** Morning (breaking camp); midday heat; evenfall; moonrise; a sleepless night during the fire; dawn ("come the break of day"); the trial fought under a smoke-darkened daytime sky.
- **Lighting:** Blazing sun; the red glow of the burning wood "engulfing half the western horizon" so it looked as if "the sun was rising in the west"; tallow candles and a fat tallow candle in the cellar; lamplight in the bathing chamber; torchlight in the maester's tower; pale yellow light spilling from Standfast's upper windows.
- **Sounds:** The buzzing of countless flies; crows rising "like a black cloud"; the scrape of whetstone on steel; Bennis's "chicken laugh" and his "bray"; the gurgle of falling water at Coldmoat's moat; hounds barking and singing from the castle sept; the clash of arms; ravens "quorking" in the maester's tower; the steady sound of rain at the end.
- **Smells:** The stench of the two rotting corpses; Bennis smelling "like a bad cheese"; the reek of dead fish in the dry streambed; the carcass of a maggot-ridden tree cat; woodsmoke and ash after the fire; the clove-scented unguent on Dunk's eye-cloth.
- **Notable sensory details:** Two starved dead men crammed face-to-face in an iron crow cage, one gnawed at the other's neck, a tongueless mouth gaping; flies crawling on everything; the green-and-gold of the Chequy Water "but all the gold is gone" when the smoke veils the sun; Wat's Wood reduced to "blackened spears" and "fiery islands in a sea of ash and cinders."

## Events & Actions
1. **Discovery of the caged dead men** — Dunk and Egg find two starved corpses crammed in an iron crow cage at the crossroads; one had gnawed the other, crows had been at both, one is tongueless.
   - Witness: Dunk, Egg
   - Location: The crossroads
   - Outcome: They ride on toward Standfast

2. **Return from Dosk with wine** — Dunk and Egg bring two casks of wine back from Dosk after the krakens raided Little Dosk.
   - Agent: Dunk, Egg
   - Instrument: Maester (the mule, carrying the casks)
   - Outcome: Wine delivered to Standfast

3. **Discovery the stream is dry** — At the plank bridge they find the Chequy Water reduced to sand and stones; Dunk deduces the water "can't have been gone long."
   - Witness: Dunk, Egg, Bennis
   - Location: The plank bridge
   - Outcome: Dunk rides upstream to investigate

4. **Discovery of the dam** — Dunk and Bennis find the Webbers have dammed the Chequy Water and diverted it into Coldmoat's fields by a web of channels.
   - Agent: (built by) Maester Cerrick's design and Coldmoat diggers
   - Location: Half a league south, on the Coldmoat side
   - Outcome: Dunk and Bennis confront the diggers

5. **Bennis cuts the digger Wolmer** — Bennis opens the old digger's cheek "from ear to jaw" with his sword to cow the diggers.
   - Agent: Ser Bennis
   - Patient: Wolmer
   - Instrument: Bennis's longsword
   - Witness: Dunk, Egg, the other diggers
   - Location: The dam
   - Outcome: Wounding; the diggers vow their lady will answer it

6. **Levy and drilling of the smallfolk** — Eustace summons every able-bodied man fifteen to fifty; eight recruits are kept, named for their crops, given fire-hardened spears and woven shields, and drilled by Bennis and Dunk.
   - Agent: Ser Eustace (summons); Bennis and Dunk (train)
   - Patient: The eight recruits
   - Location: Standfast and Wat's Wood
   - Outcome: A poor peasant levy; they break and flee in a mounted charge drill

7. **Eustace tells the tale of the Little Lion** — Ser Eustace recounts how his ancestor Ser Wilbert Osgrey slew King Lancel Lannister with a dagger as he died, saving the Reach.
   - Agent: Ser Eustace
   - Location: Standfast solar
   - Outcome: Establishes Osgrey history and the blood-price idea

8. **Dunk rides to Coldmoat to parley** — Sent by Eustace to offer the blood price and ask for the dam's removal, Dunk takes Egg to Coldmoat.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Instigator: Ser Eustace
   - Location: The west way to Coldmoat
   - Outcome: He is admitted to see Lady Rohanne

9. **Dunk mistakes Lady Helicent for the Red Widow** — Ser Lucas lets Dunk address Lady Helicent as the lady of the castle, provoking laughter, until the true Lady Rohanne reveals herself.
   - Agent: Dunk (the error); Ser Lucas (the setup)
   - Patient: Lady Helicent
   - Witness: The Coldmoat court
   - Location: Coldmoat yard
   - Outcome: Dunk is humiliated; Rohanne shows him courtesy

10. **The parley and the royal grant** — Rohanne refuses the blood price and the dam's removal; Maester Cerrick shows the king's sealed grant proving the stream is the Webbers' and Eustace a pardoned rebel.
    - Agent: Lady Rohanne; Maester Cerrick (produces the parchment)
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Instrument: The royal grant of rights
    - Location: Coldmoat audience chamber
    - Outcome: Dunk learns Eustace fought for the black dragon

11. **Rohanne slaps Dunk** — When Dunk appeals in Addam Osgrey's name, Rohanne strikes him, splitting his lip, and orders him from Coldmoat.
    - Agent: Lady Rohanne
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Location: Coldmoat audience chamber
    - Outcome: Dunk is expelled; she demands Bennis "by the morrow, else fire and sword"

12. **The burning of Wat's Wood** — That night the wood burns, lighting the western sky so it seems the sun rises in the west.
    - Patient: Wat's Wood (Osgrey land)
    - Witness: Dunk, Egg, Eustace, Bennis, Sam Stoops
    - Location: Wat's Wood, seen from Standfast
    - Outcome: The Osgrey forest destroyed; Rohanne later denies setting it

13. **Dunk quits Ser Eustace's service** — Learning Eustace lied about his sons dying "for the king" (they died for Daemon Blackfyre) and about owning the stream, Dunk returns the cloak and renounces his service.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Ser Eustace
    - Location: Standfast solar
    - Outcome: Eustace casts them out, then they reconcile

14. **Dunk dismisses the smallfolk** — Rather than lead the levy to death, Dunk drives the recruits home at sword point against Bennis's objection.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: The eight recruits
    - Instrument: Dunk's drawn sword
    - Location: Standfast yard
    - Outcome: The recruits flee home; the levy disbands

15. **The stand at the stream** — Dunk, Egg, and Eustace ride to the Chequy Water to face Lady Rohanne's three-and-thirty men; Eustace forbids her to cross.
    - Agent: Dunk (declares he will stop them)
    - Location: The Chequy Water
    - Outcome: Trial by battle is invoked

16. **Dunk cuts his own cheek (the blood price)** — Privately telling Rohanne it was he, not Bennis, who cut the digger, Dunk slashes his own face: "A cheek for a cheek."
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Instrument: His dagger
    - Witness: Lady Rohanne
    - Location: Midstream of the Chequy Water
    - Outcome: She is moved but still demands a trial of her innocence over the fire

17. **The trial by battle: Dunk kills Ser Lucas the Longinch** — In single combat in the stream, Thunder's hooves kill the Longinch's courser; the horses fall; Dunk loses his sword, is battered, then drowns Ser Lucas and stabs him under the arm with his dagger.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Ser Lucas Inchfield
    - Instrument: Dunk's dagger; Thunder's hooves (against the courser)
    - Witness: Egg, Lady Rohanne, Eustace, Septon Sefton, the escort
    - Location: The Chequy Water
    - Outcome: Ser Lucas dies; Dunk himself "drowns"

18. **Egg pulls Dunk from the stream; Cerrick revives him** — Egg drags the drowned Dunk out; the ironborn maester Cerrick uses drowned-god techniques to bring him back and treats his many injuries.
    - Agent: Egg (rescue); Maester Cerrick (revival/healing)
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Witness: Egg
    - Location: The stream, then Coldmoat's maester's tower
    - Outcome: Dunk survives, broken-boned and scarred

19. **Reconciliation and the wedding** — Rohanne is granted leave to weep at Addam's grave; she and Eustace talk through the night and wed the next morning, Sefton officiating; Eustace becomes lord of Coldmoat.
    - Agent: Septon Sefton (officiates)
    - Patient: Ser Eustace and Lady Rohanne (the spouses)
    - Location: Addam's grave; then Coldmoat
    - Outcome: Coldmoat and Standfast reconciled; the chequy lion flies beside the spider

20. **Bennis robs Standfast** — While Dunk made his stand, Bennis bound Sam Stoops and his wife, ransacked Standfast, and fled with everything of value including Eustace's silver cup.
    - Agent: Ser Bennis
    - Patient: Ser Eustace (and Sam Stoops, bound)
    - Location: Standfast
    - Outcome: Bennis escapes; Dunk vows to meet him again

21. **Dunk cuts off Rohanne's braid** — Declining the offered mare and a place as captain of her guard, Dunk kisses Rohanne and cuts off her long red braid to remember her by.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Lady Rohanne
    - Instrument: His dagger
    - Location: Coldmoat stables
    - Outcome: Dunk and Egg ride out on Thunder and the new palfrey

22. **Turning north** — At the crossroads cage, Dunk and Egg choose the road north toward the Wall rather than south to Summerhall.
    - Agent: Dunk, Egg
    - Location: The crossroads
    - Outcome: They ride north; "I hear it's tall."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Heat-weary, irritable, and increasingly troubled — sweltering and stubborn; ashamed at his ignorance before Rohanne; wounded and angry to learn Eustace lied; torn between duty and conscience; rueful, smitten, and lonely toward the Red Widow.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Doing right by his oath of service while keeping Egg safe and concealed — and, beneath that, the disputed water, the dam, and the looming fight with Coldmoat.
- **Key decisions made:** To investigate the dry stream; to ride to Coldmoat to parley; to quit Eustace's service over his lies, then to stay and make a stand; to dismiss the doomed smallfolk; to cut his own cheek and fight the trial by battle; to refuse Rohanne's mare and place; to ride north to the Wall.
- **Self-deception flags:** Insists "I'm not like to marry her… I'm Dunk of Flea Bottom"; tells himself he must not think of the Red Widow ("She's not for the likes of you"); clings to "Ser Duncan the Tall" against Bennis's mockery of "Dunk the lunk"; minimizes his own feelings for Tanselle and then Rohanne; reassures himself the dream "never happened that way."
