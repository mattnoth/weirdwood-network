What is the Weirwood Network?

A book-grounded loremaster for A Song of Ice and Fire. Ask a question and it answers in the voice of an old record-keeper — dropping the real lines from the books with their chapter citations, and showing the typed chain of cause and consequence it walked through the knowledge graph to get there. It does not bluff: every claim traces to the graph or the books.

The Weirwood Network is a hand-built knowledge graph of the ASOIAF books: ~8,500 typed nodes (characters, events, places, houses, artifacts) joined by ~23,000 typed edges (CAUSES, TRIGGERS, MOTIVATES, ENABLES, and more).

Every node and edge carries a confidence tier — Tier 1 is a verbatim book line with a chapter:line citation; Tier 2 is sourced from the wiki; higher tiers are interpretive. That gap is load-bearing: the loremaster quotes only what the graph can cite.

When you ask a question, an agent resolves your phrase to a node, reads it, and walks the causal chain or its neighbours — then narrates what it found. The “chain walked” panel beside the answer is rendered straight from that typed graph traversal, not scraped from the prose. That is the proof there is a real graph underneath, not a model freestyling.
