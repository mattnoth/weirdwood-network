What is the Weirwood Network?

A book-grounded researcher for A Song of Ice and Fire. Ask a question and it answers from a hand-built knowledge graph — dropping the real lines from the books with citations you can trace to a chapter and line, and showing every graph lookup it made to get there. It does not bluff: a claim it cannot ground goes unanswered rather than invented.

The Weirwood Network is a hand-built knowledge graph of the ASOIAF books: ~8,700 typed nodes (characters, events, places, houses, artifacts, foods, songs) joined by ~23,300 typed edges (CAUSES, TRIGGERS, MOTIVATES, ENABLES, and more).

Provenance is the point. Every node and edge carries a confidence tier — Tier 1 is a verbatim book line with a navigable chapter:line citation, hand-verified against the source text; Tier 2 is sourced from the wiki; higher tiers are interpretive. More than 1,600 nodes carry curated book quotes, each one extracted verbatim from the chapter it cites.

When you ask a question, an agent works the graph with typed tools — it resolves names, reads nodes, walks causal chains, traces family trees, searches the quote layer, and browses whole categories and curated themes (meals & feasts, hospitality, songs & tales…). The receipts panel beside the answer is rendered straight from those tool returns, never scraped from the prose — that is the proof there is a real graph underneath, not a model freestyling.

A citation gate closes the loop: the answer may only cite lines the tools actually returned this turn. A citation that cannot be verified is flagged rather than shown as canon.
