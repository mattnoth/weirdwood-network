# TSS — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** TSS
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** Standfast (the tower — undercellar, roof, and Ser Eustace's solar)

- **approximate_timeline:** Within this unit: one night (a cellar bath, then a nightmare on the roof), the following morning (a failed shield-wall drill, then a meeting in Ser Eustace's solar), and that evening (a second bath and planning). - **time_markers:** "tonight"; "as the last kettle was coming to a boil"; "a good year and a half" (Egg's service to Dunk); "twenty" days (Dunk's hyperbole for Egg's service); "Come the break of day"; "The morning was already hot"; "upon the morrow" / "On the morrow"; "the next time the septon comes" (Will Barleycorn's wedding); "a fortnight" / "longer than a fortnight" (Osgrey sons' training of the levy); "five-and-twenty years" (Daeron II's reign); "nine-and-thirty" (Baelor's age at death); "a moon's turn"; "for a thousand years before the Conquest" (Osgreys as Marshalls); "one winter day" (Eustace's marriage proposal to Lord Wyman); "In a year, his bones will be scoured clean"; "two years as a page at King's Landing" (Egg); "for hundreds of years" (Targaryen sibling-marriage)
- **chapter_summary:** Dunk bathes in Standfast's undercellar with Egg's help while the two argue about the peasant levy Ser Eustace means to arm against the Widow's men; Dunk reflects on Egg's concealed identity as Aegon Targaryen. Sleeping on the roof, Dunk suffers a nightmare of digging graves in the Dornish sands for his dead horse Chestnut, his dead masters, and the doomed recruits. At dawn a shield-wall drill fails when the recruits break and flee before Dunk's charging warhorse, and Dunk reports to Ser Eustace that the levy will not serve. Eustace proposes paying Lady Webber a "blood price" to take down her dam and recounts the fallen history of House Osgrey and his vow never to enter Coldmoat, after which Dunk agrees to ride to Coldmoat himself to treat with the Red Widow. That evening Dunk takes a second bath while Egg relays rumors of the Red Widow's poisonings and black arts, and Dunk forbids Egg from accompanying him.
- **unit_part:** 3 of 8

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Severe drought; "The next time it rained on them up there would be the first." Savage heat by day.
- **Season indicators:** High summer / prolonged dry spell; "After the savage heat of the day"; "The morning was already hot"; a night sky thick with stars.
- **Time of day:** Night (first bath, then stargazing and the nightmare on the roof); dawn ("the break of day") for the drill; morning for the solar meeting; evening/night for the second bath.
- **Lighting:** The cellar is dim and lamplit ("In the dimness of the lamplit cellar"); Egg's eyes look black in the dimness but deep dark purple in better light. The solar is dim ("in the dimness of his solar"). Night roof lit by thousands of stars.
- **Sounds:** Splashing of bathwater; the empty kettle thumping onto the earthen floor; hens screeching and flapping under the warhorse; woven branches cracking and splintering under iron-shod hooves; Ser Bennis rattling off curses; a fat black fly buzzing.
- **Smells:** Ser Bennis "smelled like a bad cheese" (lice and fleas); Dunk fears turning up before Lady Webber "smelling like Ser Bennis"; Egg jokes of "a tub of Maester's droppings"; Dunk's sweat-stained tunic.
- **Notable sensory details:** Steam rising "in a white curtain" as hot water is poured; scalding bathwater cooler farther down; dirt coming off Dunk's arms "in long gray clouds"; water gone "gray and cold"; the dream's "dry hot earth" and "fine sand"; red froth bubbling on Bennis's lips; the sands filling Dunk's mouth, nose, and eyes.

## Events & Actions
1. **The cellar bath** — Dunk bathes in the stone washtub while Egg draws, heats, and pours the water; they argue over the doomed levy and Egg's suppressed identity.
   - Agent: Ser Duncan (Dunk)
   - Patient: —
   - Instrument: washtub, well bucket, iron kettle, soap, long-handled horsehair scrub brush
   - Location: Standfast undercellar
   - Outcome: Dunk bathed; Egg sent to sleep
2. **Egg presses to "use the boot"** — Egg repeatedly urges deploying his concealed token to summon aid for the recruits; Dunk flatly refuses ("No.") each time.
   - Agent: Egg
   - Patient: Ser Duncan
   - Location: Standfast undercellar
   - Outcome: Refused; "the boot is only meant for dire need"
3. **Dunk recalls Egg's true identity** — Dunk reflects that his squire is Aegon Targaryen, youngest son of Maekar, and that Maekar bade Egg keep his head shaved or dyed and tell no man his true name.
   - Agent: Ser Duncan (internal)
   - Patient: Egg
   - Location: Standfast undercellar
   - Outcome: Identity established for the reader
4. **The nightmare on the roof** — Dunk dreams he is digging a grave in the Dornish sands for his dead horse Chestnut while apparitions of Ser Arlan, Baelor Breakspear, and Prince Valarr reproach him; then Big Rob, Ser Bennis, Treb, Wet Wat, Lem, and Pate appear as gutted corpses, and Bennis tells Dunk to dig "eight for them and one for me and one for old Ser Useless, and one last one for your bald-head boy"; the sands collapse and bury Egg and then Dunk.
   - Agent: Ser Duncan (dreamer)
   - Patient: Egg (buried in the dream); the recruits (dead in the dream)
   - Location: Standfast roof / Dornish sands (dream)
   - Outcome: Dunk wakes troubled
5. **The shield-wall drill fails** — At dawn Ser Bennis lines the eight recruits shoulder to shoulder with shields touching and spears out; Dunk and Egg charge on horseback; Egg's mount Maester balks, but Thunder charges home; Big Rob drops his spear and flees first and the rest follow, and Thunder tramples their discarded woven shields.
   - Agent: Ser Duncan (and Ser Bennis, drillmaster)
   - Patient: The eight recruits
   - Instrument: Thunder (warhorse); the recruits' woven-wood shields and spears
   - Location: Outside Standfast
   - Instigator: Ser Bennis (set the drill); Ser Eustace (whose war it is)
   - Witness: Egg ("Egg fought manfully to hold his laughter in, but finally lost the battle")
   - Outcome: Drill collapses; recruits scatter
6. **Dunk reports the failure to Ser Eustace** — In the solar Dunk tells Eustace the levy "will not serve"; Eustace defends them, recalling that only three of his men (Lem, Pate, Dake) came home from the war.
   - Agent: Ser Duncan
   - Patient: Ser Eustace Osgrey
   - Location: Ser Eustace's solar
   - Outcome: Eustace concedes it "may take longer than a fortnight"
7. **Ser Eustace proposes the blood price and tells the Osgrey history** — Eustace proposes paying Lady Webber a silver stag for the wounded man and three for the insult if she takes down the dam; he recounts the Osgreys as former Marshalls of the Northmarch, the loss of Coldmoat to King Maegor, Addam's rejected suit for Lord Wyman's daughter, and the drowning of Dake, and reveals his vow never to enter Coldmoat again "unless to take possession."
   - Agent: Ser Eustace Osgrey
   - Patient: Ser Duncan
   - Location: Ser Eustace's solar
   - Outcome: The blood-price plan set; Eustace cannot go himself
8. **Dunk agrees to ride to Coldmoat** — Dunk volunteers ("I swore no vows") to carry the offer to the Red Widow; Eustace praises him as "the true steel" and the man he had hoped his daughter Alysanne might wed; Dunk kneels and agrees to go "upon the morrow."
   - Agent: Ser Duncan
   - Patient: Ser Eustace Osgrey
   - Location: Ser Eustace's solar
   - Outcome: Dunk committed to the Coldmoat mission
9. **The second bath and the Red Widow rumors** — That evening Dunk bathes again to be presentable; Egg relays gossip that the Red Widow poisoned all four husbands, bore no living children, and deals in black arts, and jokes about gallantry, songs, and the Kingsguard; Dunk chooses his sandsilk Dornish tunic for the visit.
   - Agent: Ser Duncan / Egg
   - Location: Standfast undercellar
   - Outcome: Preparations made
10. **Dunk forbids Egg to come to Coldmoat** — Egg argues a knight is more impressive with a squire; Dunk, fearing the danger (recalling the two men in crow cages), orders Egg to stay and help Bennis with the smallfolk.
    - Agent: Ser Duncan
    - Patient: Egg
    - Location: Standfast undercellar
    - Outcome: "You're not going, and that's the end of it."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Weary and grief-haunted (the nightmare of dead masters, his dead horse, and the doomed recruits leaves him "soiled and sticky as if he'd never bathed"); affectionate toward Egg ("almost as a little brother"); apprehensive about the Coldmoat mission and daunted more by "the prospect of facing the Red Widow" than by the giant Lucas Longinch; quietly flattered and reddening at Eustace's praise and talk of Alysanne.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The coming confrontation over the dam — how to treat with Lady Webber without begging, how to keep the doomed recruits and Egg from being killed, and rehearsing what to say to a highborn lady.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to let Egg "use the boot"; agrees to ride to Coldmoat to offer the blood price and ask for the dam's removal; will wear his sandsilk Dornish tunic; forbids Egg to accompany him and orders him to stay and help Bennis with the smallfolk.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells the recruits' deaths are "their duty... to die, if need be" and "The same thing, only smaller" while privately thinking "this is madness" and "they will soon be dead lads, if they go up against the knights of Coldmoat." Insists to himself of the dream, "It never happened that way... I never wept," while admitting "I might have wanted to." Downplays his dread of the Red Widow as "I'm going to talk to the Red Widow, that's all."
