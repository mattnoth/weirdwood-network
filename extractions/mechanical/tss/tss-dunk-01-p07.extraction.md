# TSS — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** TSS
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** Standfast (tower — cellar, roof, yard) and the ford where the west way crosses the Chequy Water
- **location_secondary:** Wat's Wood (burned to a wasteland), the dam, Coldmoat (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** From the last night at Standfast through the following day — a fire alarm rouses the tower before dawn (Wat's Wood ablaze), the garrison is dismissed at morning, and Dunk, Egg and Ser Eustace ride down through the burned wood to the stream, where Lady Rohanne's host arrives and the parley plays out.
- **time_markers:** "That last night"; "Morning seemed a long way off"; "He woke suddenly, at the sound of a shout"; "The sun was rising in the west"; "The fire burned until morning"; "No one in Standfast slept that night"; "Dawn came with hazy gray skies"; "an early start"; "If we have not returned by nightfall"; "within the hour"; "half a heartbeat later"; "It was more than half my life ago" (Rohanne re Addam); "two hundred years ago" (Field of Fire); "The last of the green kings perished"; "for a thousand years" (Marshalls of the Northmarch); "a boy of ten" (Egg)
- **chapter_summary:** After a restless last night in Standfast's cellar, Dunk is woken by a fire alarm — Wat's Wood is ablaze, the glow filling the western horizon. In the morning Dunk dismisses Ser Eustace's peasant levy and sends them home, then he, Egg, and the old knight ride down through the burned wood to the ford of the Chequy Water while Ser Bennis holds the tower with the steps hauled up. Lady Rohanne (the Red Widow) arrives across the stream with three-and-thirty men and demands Bennis be surrendered; Ser Eustace refuses her all hospitality and accuses her of the fire, which she denies. Dunk rides alone into the middle of the stream to parley, shows her a hidden gold-and-onyx signet ring hinting at a "dragon" patron, and slashes his own cheek to give her "a cheek for a cheek." Rohanne, unmoved on Bennis, rides off demanding that Ser Eustace offer an apology or a trial.
- **unit_part:** 7 of 8

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Drought conditions ("The drought has turned all our woods to kindling"; dry fields); a wind rising near the end ("For once a wind was rising"); "there was no breath of wind" earlier in the yard.
- **Season indicators:** Drought; fleas and mites in the straw; lantern bugs recalled; smoke-choked air.
- **Time of day:** Deep night (fire alarm) → dawn "with hazy gray skies" → morning departure → within the hour at the ford.
- **Lighting:** Egg lights a tallow candle in the dark cellar; the fire's "red glow engulfed half the western horizon," stars vanishing, the King's Crown half obscured; "sun was rising in the west"; smoke veiling the sun so "all the gold is gone" from the water.
- **Sounds:** Lem and Pate snoring; curses and complaints; the shout that wakes Dunk; Bennis cursing "a stream of such surpassing filth"; the whetstone's scrape; chickens cackling; "the flapping of his cloak"; Rohanne's horses and "the faint metallic sound of clinking armor"; the old knight's armor "rattling softly."
- **Smells:** "Before long they could smell the smoke"; the smell of Dunk's blood makes Thunder snort.
- **Notable sensory details:** "Dank vapors filled the cellar, rising through the trap from the deeper vaults below"; flames "dancing in the distance like girls in scarlet skirts"; the burned wood "a smoking wasteland," "fiery islands in a sea of ash and cinders," burned trunks "like blackened spears," "smoke hung in the air like a hot gray haze"; carcasses of a red deer and a badger; "Nothing lived, except the flies."

## Events & Actions
1. **Dunk's restless last night and dream** — Dunk sleeps in the cellar to be far from Eustace; he dreams of dragons red and black, chequy lions, and of the Red Widow shooting arrows at him in Wat's Wood, ending in a kiss.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: Standfast cellar
   - Outcome: Fitful sleep, then waking
2. **Fire alarm; Wat's Wood is afire** — Sam Stoops raises the alarm ("The sky!"); the garrison climbs to the roof to see a red glow on the western horizon obscuring the stars and the King's Crown.
   - Agent: Sam Stoops (raises alarm)
   - Patient: The Standfast household
   - Location: Standfast roof
   - Witness: Dunk, Ser Eustace, the garrison
   - Outcome: All wake; "Wat's Wood is afire"
3. **War-council on the roof** — Ser Eustace and Bennis plan to answer "Fire for fire" by burning Rohanne's crops and her mill and killing the miller; Egg begs Dunk to stop them, and Dunk declines.
   - Agent: Ser Eustace, Ser Bennis
   - Location: Standfast roof/base
   - Outcome: Dunk stays out of it
4. **Dunk dismisses the peasant levy** — At morning Dunk draws his sword against Bennis and roars at the smallfolk (Melons, Beans, Barleycorns) to go home; they scatter in three directions.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: The peasant levy
   - Instrument: His drawn sword; his voice ("GO!")
   - Location: Standfast yard
   - Outcome: The levy flees home
5. **Ser Eustace confronts Dunk over the dismissal** — Eustace, coming down too late, forbids their leaving and accuses Dunk of being bought by the Widow; Egg claims "The chickens were cackling too loud" to hear the order.
   - Agent: Ser Eustace
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Location: Standfast steps
   - Outcome: Eustace resolves to face the Widow ("Better to die a lion than a rabbit")
6. **Sealing the tower; Bennis left to hold it** — Eustace commands Bennis to stay; Dunk helps knock loose the iron pegs and Bennis hauls up the wooden stair, leaving ten feet of air below the door, Sam Stoops and his wife inside.
   - Agent: Ser Bennis (hauls the stair)
   - Instrument: The gray hempen rope; iron pegs
   - Instigator: Ser Eustace (commands him to stay)
   - Location: Standfast entrance
   - Outcome: Tower sealed; Bennis holds it
7. **Ride down through the burned wood** — Dunk, Egg, and Eustace ride down the hill and through the smoking wasteland of Wat's Wood; Eustace tells of the Field of Fire and the Little Lion.
   - Agent: Dunk, Egg, Ser Eustace
   - Location: Wat's Wood / the west way
   - Outcome: They reach the Chequy Water
8. **Eustace's holy vow at the water** — Ser Eustace halts at the stream and refuses to cross: "I took a holy vow. I will not cross that stream. Not so long as the land beyond is hers."
   - Agent: Ser Eustace
   - Location: The ford of the Chequy Water
   - Outcome: They wait on their bank
9. **Rohanne's host arrives** — Within the hour Lady Rohanne rides up with her banner bearer, the Longinch, Septon Sefton, Cerrick, six knights, esquires, and mounted crossbowmen — three-and-thirty fighting men.
   - Agent: Lady Rohanne (and her host)
   - Location: The far bank of the Chequy Water
   - Outcome: Standoff across the stream
10. **The accusation and denial** — Ser Eustace accuses Rohanne of setting the fire; she and Septon Sefton blame the drought and deny it; she demands Bennis be produced and has the crossbowmen ready their bows.
    - Agent: Ser Eustace (accuses); Lady Rohanne (denies, demands Bennis)
    - Instrument: Crossbows (readied at her word via the Longinch)
    - Instigator: Lady Rohanne (orders the Longinch)
    - Location: Across the stream
    - Outcome: Impasse; bows raised
11. **Dunk offers to stop the crossing and parleys alone** — Dunk warns crossing breaks the king's peace and says he will stop them; Rohanne rides into the middle of the stream to hear him "But only you," and Dunk rides Thunder out to her.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Lady Rohanne
    - Location: The middle of the ford
    - Outcome: Private parley
12. **Dunk shows the signet ring** — Dunk reveals a hidden gold-and-onyx signet ring found in a boot, implying a "dragon" patron who would avenge his and Egg's deaths.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Lady Rohanne
    - Instrument: The gold-and-onyx signet ring
    - Location: Mid-stream
    - Outcome: Rohanne intrigued ("I would sooner be the dragon's friend") but still demands Bennis
13. **Dunk slashes his own cheek** — To give Rohanne "a cheek for a cheek," Dunk draws his dagger and cuts his own face, spattering blood on her; he claims it was he, not Bennis, who cut the digger.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Dunk (self)
    - Instrument: His dagger
    - Location: Mid-stream
    - Witness: Lady Rohanne
    - Outcome: Blood given; Rohanne calls him "quite mad"
14. **Rohanne demands an apology or a trial** — Unmoved on Bennis, Rohanne declares she must assert her innocence "before the eyes of gods and men," demands Ser Eustace apologize or face a trial, and wheels her horse about.
    - Agent: Lady Rohanne
    - Patient: Ser Eustace (via Dunk)
    - Location: The ford
    - Outcome: Parley ends; choice left to Eustace
