# TSS — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** TSS
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** Standfast (Ser Eustace Osgrey's towerhouse and its yard, stable, solar, and undercroft)
- **location_secondary:** The three Osgrey villages and their one-room sept; Wat's Wood (spear-cutting); recalled — Redgrass Field, Goldengrove, the Osgreys of Leafy Lake, Highgarden, the Rock, the Reach, Lannisport, the Prince's Pass, Stoney Sept, Oldtown
- **approximate_timeline:** Continues the same day as the previous unit — Dunk and Bennis reach Standfast at the ascent; that evening Ser Eustace orders the muster; "on the morrow" Dunk and Egg ride the villages with the summons; "the next day" a dozen recruits assemble and are trained through to nightfall. Spans roughly three days within this unit.
- **time_markers:** "A partial collapse a few centuries ago"; "died on the Redgrass Field fifteen years ago, at the end of the Blackfyre Rebellion"; "prizes from battles fought long centuries ago"; "known as the Chequy Water for a thousand years or more"; "Alysanne had been his daughter, who had perished in the spring"; "on hot summer days like this"; "on the morrow you must make the rounds"; "every seventh day"; "Twice a year a real septon came through"; "The next day a dozen would-be warriors"; "Lem was fifty if he was a day"; "even those who'd had one just last winter"; "As the sun was going down"; "The end of the afternoon"; "In a fortnight they might stand their own"; "yesterday" (Egg's grievance against Bennis)
- **chapter_summary:** Dunk and Bennis ride up to Standfast, where Egg takes their horses and Bennis spits on the boy's foot. In the tower's solar Ser Eustace shows Dunk the ruined shield of the Little Lion, Ser Wilbert Osgrey, and tells how Wilbert died killing King Lancel of the Rock to save the Reach. Hearing that Bennis cut one of the Red Widow's diggers, Eustace warns that the woman — a reputed husband-murderer whose overlord Lord Rowan is her kin — will retaliate, and commands Dunk to muster his smallfolk. Dunk and Egg ride the three poor villages delivering the summons, and eight ragged recruits assemble the next day. Dunk and Bennis arm, name, and drill the peasant levy through a day of spear practice, baths, and stew, while Bennis privately doubts they could ever stand against knights.
- **unit_part:** 2 of 8

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Hot summer ("hot summer days like this"); the drought continues (the stream is "Dry as a bone, and not from no drought").
- **Season indicators:** Summer; Alysanne "liked to splash in the shallows on hot summer days"; the recruits are "green as summer grass."
- **Time of day:** Daytime arrival and solar conversation; village rounds by day; the drill runs to "the end of the afternoon" and "As the sun was going down," then supper and sleep after dark.
- **Lighting:** Inside Standfast "so dim that Dunk had to let his eyes adjust"; the solar's thick gray walls hung with faded banners.
- **Sounds:** Bennis bellowing drill commands ("Stick and twist and rip it free"); the shout "For Osgrey! For Standfast!"; hens pecking and clucking; a babe suckling; young Wat squealing when pinched.
- **Smells:** Bennis "fragrant at his heels" / "followed fragrant"; Bennis "a look of disgust"; the misshapen heap of purple moss on the stable; soiled straw stuffed in the sacks.
- **Notable sensory details:** A glob of "glistening red phlegm" strikes Egg between two toes; the solar banners "badly faded and covered with dust, their once bright colors gone to gray and green"; the recruits "dirtier than the last," "All eight had lice"; fresh blisters rising on callused hands.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dunk and Bennis ascend to Standfast and rein up at the stable** — They climb the single-file path; Egg watches from a jut of rock; they dismount at the daub-and-wattle stable.
   - Agent: Dunk, Ser Bennis
   - Location: Standfast (the ascent and yard)
   - Outcome: Arrival home with the wine already stowed
2. **Egg takes the horses; Bennis spits on his foot** — Egg takes Thunder's reins, refuses Bennis's horse ("I'm not your squire") until Dunk threatens him; as Egg reaches for the bridle, Bennis hawks and spits red phlegm onto the boy's foot and vows worse next time.
   - Agent: Ser Bennis
   - Patient: Egg
   - Location: The Standfast yard
   - Witness: Dunk
   - Outcome: Egg humiliated but obeys and tends the horses
3. **Dunk and Bennis climb to Ser Eustace in the solar** — They enter through the high oak-and-iron door and mount to the solar, where Eustace putters among his chests and captured banners.
   - Agent: Dunk, Ser Bennis
   - Location: Standfast solar
4. **Ser Eustace shows the Little Lion's shield and tells its tale** — He presents Ser Wilbert Osgrey's ruined shield and recounts how, as Marshall of the Northmarch, the Little Lion barred King Lancel of the Rock and died killing him to save the Reach.
   - Agent: Ser Eustace
   - Instrument: The Little Lion's shield
   - Location: Standfast solar
5. **The Little Lion kills and is killed by King Lancel (recalled)** — In the historical battle Wilbert, bleeding and his shield in ruins, threw himself at Lancel; the king "cut him near in half" with a Valyrian sword, and the dying Wilbert drove his dagger through the gap in the king's armor, killing him; the westermen then withdrew.
   - Agent: Ser Wilbert Osgrey; King Lancel (mutual)
   - Patient: King Lancel; Ser Wilbert Osgrey
   - Instrument: A dagger (Wilbert); a Valyrian sword (Lancel)
   - Outcome: Both dead; the Reach saved
6. **Bennis reports the dry stream and the dam to Eustace** — Bennis tells Eustace the Chequy Water is "Dry as a bone, and not from no drought," and that the dam is too strong to pull down without ropes, picks, axes, and a dozen men.
   - Agent: Ser Bennis
   - Patient: Ser Eustace
   - Location: Standfast solar
7. **Eustace learns Bennis cut a digger and warns of the Red Widow** — Bennis admits he "taught one fool a lesson" with "a little claret on his cheek"; Eustace calls it ill considered, describing the Red Widow as a murderer of three husbands whose brothers all died in swaddling clothes, and warns "she will come for you."
   - Agent: Ser Eustace (the warning)
   - Location: Standfast solar
8. **Dunk proposes appealing to Lord Rowan; Eustace refuses** — Dunk offers to ride to Goldengrove; Eustace refuses because Rowan is kin to the Red Widow (Rowan's sister wed Lord Wyman's cousin Wendell) and "loves me not."
   - Agent: Dunk (proposal); Ser Eustace (refusal)
   - Location: Standfast solar
9. **Eustace commands the muster** — He orders Dunk to make the rounds of all his villages "on the morrow" and rouse every able-bodied man of fighting age: "the chequy lion still has claws!"
   - Agent: Ser Eustace
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Outcome: A levy is called
10. **Dunk and Egg ride the villages with the summons** — Egg bears the Osgrey banner; Dunk commands every able-bodied man aged fifteen to fifty to assemble at the tower on the morrow.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Location: The three Osgrey villages
11. **A village woman asks if it is war; Egg reveals his shaved head** — A thin woman asks if "the black dragon come again"; Dunk answers it is between "the chequy lion and the spiders," and when the woman asks whether the hairless boy is sick, Egg answers "It's shaved."
    - Agent: The thin village woman; Egg
    - Location: A village
12. **Dunk and Egg discuss Bennis and the smallfolk** — Egg complains Bennis pinches him; Dunk recalls Bennis pinching him in his own squire days and warns Egg to treat the smallfolk with respect.
    - Agent: Dunk, Egg
    - Location: The road between villages
13. **Bennis pinched young Dunk on the Dornish escort (recalled)** — Years past, Bennis and Ser Arlan rode with a party hired by a Dornish merchant from Lannisport to the Prince's Pass; Bennis pinched the boy Dunk hard enough to bruise, and a knight later vanished near Stoney Sept, Bennis rumored to have gutted him.
    - Agent: Ser Bennis
    - Patient: Young Dunk
    - Location: The road from Lannisport to the Prince's Pass (recalled)
14. **A dozen recruits assemble; Dunk culls them to eight** — He sends back one too old, two too young, and "a skinny girl," leaving three Wats, two Wills, a Lem, a Pate, and Big Rob the lackwit.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Location: Standfast
    - Outcome: Eight recruits retained
15. **Bennis arms and trains the recruits with spears** — After noting their poor arms and that longbows are forbidden them, Bennis marches them to Wat's Wood to cut spears, then drills them in thrust, parry, and where to kill.
    - Agent: Ser Bennis
    - Patient: The recruits
    - Location: Wat's Wood / Standfast yard
16. **Bennis pinches young Wat** — When Wat asks about swords and armor, Bennis mocks him and "pinched young Wat beneath his arm until the lad squealed in pain."
    - Agent: Ser Bennis
    - Patient: Young Wat
    - Location: Standfast yard
17. **The recruits are given names** — Egg suggests village names, then crop names; the levy becomes four Barleycorns, two Melons, and two Beans; the rock-chucker becomes "Treb" and the younger Wat "Wet Wat."
    - Agent: Egg (the idea); Ser Bennis (dubbing "Treb" and "Wet Wat")
    - Patient: The recruits
18. **Ser Eustace addresses the recruits** — In mail and plate and chequy-lion surcoat, he reminds them of drowned Dake, declares the Red Widow will not have their water, and raises his sword: "For Osgrey! For Standfast!"
    - Agent: Ser Eustace
    - Patient: The recruits
    - Location: Outside the tower door
    - Outcome: The men take up the shout
19. **Dunk and Bennis drill the levy; Dunk tests them** — They drill the men against straw-stuffed sacks, then Dunk in armor with a wooden sword bests them all; only Treb gets a spear past his shield once, and Big Rob drops his spear and flees.
    - Agent: Dunk, Ser Bennis
    - Patient: The recruits
    - Instrument: A wooden practice sword
    - Location: Standfast yard
    - Outcome: The recruits bruised and blistered; Dunk unmarked but soaked in sweat
20. **The recruits bathe, sup, and sleep; Bennis disparages Eustace** — Dunk forces the men to bathe; they eat stew and boast; Bennis privately tells Dunk the men could never stand against knights and mocks Eustace for not siring bastard soldiers.
    - Agent: Dunk; Ser Bennis
    - Patient: The recruits
    - Location: Standfast undercroft

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Wearied and grimly practical; irritated by Bennis's cruelty to Egg ("That tongue of his will get him hurt one day"); privately dismayed at how thin Eustace's fighting strength is ("Two, and I am one of them"); protective of Egg and his secret.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Turning a handful of louse-ridden peasants into fighters before the Red Widow retaliates; managing the friction between Egg and Bennis; his duty to Ser Eustace.
- **Key decisions made:** Forces Egg to tend Bennis's horse rather than let the quarrel escalate; carries out the muster; culls the unfit recruits to eight; drills and tests the levy himself in armor; makes the men bathe.
- **Self-deception flags:** Recalls the old man's counsel not to name horses (to soften battle-grief) while acknowledging Ser Arlan "never took his own counsel" and "named every horse he ever owned," "So had Dunk"; tells Egg the smallfolk "may seem fools to you... treat them with respect all the same," reproving in the boy an attitude he manages in himself.
