# TSS — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** TSS
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** Coldmoat (arrival, outer ward, and the crowd outside the castle sept)
- **location_secondary:** The west way between Standfast and Coldmoat; Wat's Wood; the Chequy Water crossing; Standfast (departure); Redgrass Field and Weeping Ridge (recounted battle)
- **approximate_timeline:** A single continuous stretch within this unit: dawn departure from Standfast; the morning ride down the west way, during which Ser Eustace recounts the Redgrass Field and then turns back at a tree-cat carcass in Wat's Wood; the crossing of the Chequy Water into the Widow's lands; arrival at Coldmoat "several hours later" (afternoon); and the confrontation in the outer ward culminating in the meeting with Lady Rohanne. Ends with Dunk granted an audience.
- **time_markers:** "the light of the morning sun in his face"; "how can it be so hot so soon?"; "Even at this hour, with the sun barely above the horizon"; "By afternoon the saddles will be hot enough to raise blisters"; "these past years" (the west way little used); "Before the Conquest" / "in my father's day" (Wat's Wood); "several hours later" (spied the towers of Coldmoat); "before the sun has set"; "all morning" (Rohanne's devotions); "two years past" (the Great Spring Sickness); "when I was ten" / "He was twelve" (Rohanne's first marriage); "The last died in the spring"; "Centuries of wind and weather" (the worn gate sigil); "for near an hour" (Daemon and Gwayne Corbray)
- **chapter_summary:** Ser Eustace outfits Dunk with a green-and-gold cloak, assigns Egg to accompany him as squire, and rides part of the way toward Coldmoat, recounting the Battle of the Redgrass Field and his belief that Bloodraven's sorcery, not Baelor Breakspear, won it. Eustace turns back at a tree-cat carcass in Wat's Wood, and Dunk and Egg cross the Chequy Water into Lady Webber's lands, Egg coaching Dunk on gallant compliments. At Coldmoat Dunk is met with scorn by the castellan Ser Lucas Longinch and by the drunken Septon Sefton, then embarrasses himself by mistaking the stout Lady Helicent for the Red Widow before the mocking crowd. The true Red Widow — Lady Rohanne Webber, a freckled young archer of five-and-twenty — reveals herself, teases Dunk through his fumbled gallantries, examines the elm-and-star arms painted on his tunic, and grants him a private audience, sending for Maester Cerrick and for Dunk's squire.
- **unit_part:** 4 of 8

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Severe, oppressive heat and drought; "how can it be so hot so soon?"; hot even at dawn; "By afternoon the saddles will be hot enough to raise blisters"; "the ward is too hot and dusty"; "beneath this beastly sun."
- **Season indicators:** High summer / prolonged dry spell; the moat has fallen six feet at least; the diverted stream is used to water crops in the fields.
- **Time of day:** Dawn departure; morning ride; afternoon arrival at Coldmoat.
- **Lighting:** Bright, harsh sun ("shining green and golden in the sun"); the diverted ditch "shining green and golden"; leaded-glass windows of the sept.
- **Sounds:** Singing from the sept; hounds barking in the kennels; the gurgle of falling water into the moat; the whir of the spinning quintain; the smith shoeing a warhorse; laughter and japes of the crowd; the septon's fart.
- **Smells:** The maggot-crawling carcass of a tree cat ("that stinks worse than Ser Bennis"); the guards joke of smelling Bennis coming.
- **Notable sensory details:** "Even the flies have fled"; a thin finger of sweat creeping down Dunk's chest; the septon "sweating worse than Dunk... as damp as if he'd worn them in his bath"; the diverted water "ran straight as a spear, shining green and golden"; the worn chequy-lion sigil above Coldmoat's gate.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dunk wakes and dresses at Standfast** — With the morning sun in his face, Dunk goes to the well, lights a tallow candle, splashes cold water, and dresses; Thunder waits saddled, and Egg waits with the mule Maester.
   - Agent: Ser Duncan
   - Location: Standfast
   - Outcome: Ready to depart
2. **Ser Eustace outfits the pair and assigns Egg** — Eustace gives Dunk a white wool cloak bordered with green satin and cloth of gold, and announces Egg will accompany Dunk to Coldmoat as his squire, dressed in Addam's old doublet.
   - Agent: Ser Eustace Osgrey
   - Patient: Ser Duncan; Egg
   - Instrument: the cloak; Addam's doublet
   - Location: Standfast
   - Outcome: Dunk cloaked; Egg assigned as squire ("Outwitted by a boy of ten")
3. **The company rides the west way; passes the Osgrey graves** — Egg leads them down the hill; they take the west way past the blackberry thicket where Eustace buried his wife and sons.
   - Agent: Ser Eustace Osgrey (leads the route); Egg (leads down the hill)
   - Location: The west way behind Standfast
   - Outcome: On the road to Coldmoat
4. **Ser Eustace recounts the Battle of the Redgrass Field** — Eustace tells the tale of the Blackfyre Rebellion's climactic battle: Daemon's deeds and hour-long duel with Gwayne Corbray, Bloodraven and the Raven's Teeth taking Weeping Ridge and slaying Daemon and his twin sons with arrows and sorcery, Bittersteel's charge, and Baelor's hammerblow; he insists Bloodraven, not Baelor, won the day, and now rules through King Aerys.
   - Agent: Ser Eustace Osgrey
   - Patient: Ser Duncan (listener)
   - Location: The west way
   - Outcome: Dunk troubled; "How many eyes does Lord Bloodraven have?"
5. **Ser Eustace turns back at the tree cat** — In Wat's Wood the party finds a maggot-ridden tree-cat carcass; Eustace reins up, wonders what killed it, confirms Dunk has the coin, bids him "Come home with my water," and rides back.
   - Agent: Ser Eustace Osgrey
   - Location: Wat's Wood
   - Outcome: Dunk and Egg continue alone
6. **Egg coaches Dunk on gallant compliments** — Egg advises winning Lady Webber with "gallant compliments" — praise her hair, ears, or eyes — and jokes about sacks, moats, and using "my boot"; Dunk is doubtful.
   - Agent: Egg
   - Patient: Ser Duncan
   - Location: Wat's Wood / the road
   - Outcome: Dunk rehearses gallantries
7. **Crossing the Chequy Water into the Widow's power** — Emerging upstream of the dam, Dunk fords the risen stream (belly-deep on Thunder) with Egg on the mule; "Once we cross the stream, we are in the Widow's power."
   - Agent: Ser Duncan; Egg
   - Instrument: Thunder; Maester
   - Location: The Chequy Water ford
   - Outcome: They emerge dripping on the Widow's side
8. **Arrival at Coldmoat; the gate confrontation** — Several hours on, Dunk changes to his Dornish tunic; they cross the drawbridge, where two spearmen bar the way; Egg calls a guard "blind, or only stupid," a spear is set to his throat, and Dunk clouts Egg and orders him to tend the horses.
   - Agent: Egg (the insult); Ser Duncan (the clout)
   - Patient: the bearded spearman; Egg
   - Instrument: the guard's spear (to Egg's throat)
   - Location: Coldmoat gate / portcullis
   - Outcome: Egg silenced and left with the horses; Dunk admitted to see Ser Lucas
9. **Dunk confronts Ser Lucas Longinch and Septon Sefton in the yard** — In the outer ward Ser Lucas scorns Dunk as a beggar and outlaw, names him as one who was "with Bennis at the dam," and threatens to hang him, demanding Bennis be delivered up; the drunken Septon Sefton jests and blesses him; Dunk insists on speaking with her ladyship.
   - Agent: Ser Lucas Inchfield
   - Patient: Ser Duncan
   - Location: Coldmoat outer ward, by the quintain
   - Outcome: Lucas grudgingly leads Dunk toward the lady
10. **Dunk mistakes Lady Helicent for the Red Widow** — As worshipers stream from the sept, Dunk addresses the stout Lady Helicent about the dam; her confused replies and the crowd's jape ("the great oaf means to bed her!") provoke mocking laughter, and Helicent cringes in terror.
    - Agent: Ser Duncan
    - Patient: Lady Helicent
    - Location: Coldmoat outer ward, by the castle sept
    - Witness: Ser Lucas Inchfield (chuckles); the assembled crowd
    - Outcome: Dunk humiliated; Helicent shielded by a septa
11. **Lady Rohanne reveals herself as the Red Widow** — The freckled young archer from the butts intervenes, is named "Lady Rohanne," and is revealed to be the Red Widow; Dunk blurts "But you're too—," fumbles gallantries about her gown and hair, and reddens under her teasing while the crowd laughs.
    - Agent: Lady Rohanne Webber
    - Patient: Ser Duncan
    - Location: Coldmoat outer ward
    - Witness: the crowd; Ser Lucas; the Leygood knight (mocks "Ser Duncan the Dim")
    - Outcome: Dunk's true errand established before her
12. **Rohanne examines Dunk's arms and grants an audience** — Rohanne looks Dunk over, traces the elm-and-star painting on his tunic, notes it is "painted, not sewn," and grants him a private audience — sending Septon Sefton to show him to her chamber, ordering wine, summoning Maester Cerrick, and (over Lucas's offer) countermanding Lucas; she also grants Dunk's request that Egg join them.
    - Agent: Lady Rohanne Webber
    - Patient: Ser Duncan
    - Instrument: (touches his painted tunic)
    - Location: Coldmoat outer ward
    - Outcome: Audience granted; Cerrick summoned; Egg admitted

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Physically miserable in the heat (sweating, envying Egg his hat); wary and apprehensive on crossing into "the Widow's power," aware he is "only one man, with a boy of ten to guard his back"; angry contempt for Ser Lucas ("wanted to drive his fist through Lucas Inchfield's crooked yellow teeth"); acute embarrassment and reddening after mistaking Lady Helicent for the Widow; flustered attraction to Lady Rohanne (likes her nose, hair, and figure) mixed with tongue-tied shame at his failed gallantries.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Carrying out the Coldmoat embassy — recovering the water, dealing with the dam, and figuring out how to treat with (and compliment) the Red Widow without disaster.
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts the cloak and Egg's company; crosses the Chequy Water into the Widow's lands; changes into his Dornish tunic; refuses to be turned away by Lucas and insists on an audience; takes public blame for his mistake ("The fault was mine"); asks that Egg be allowed to join the audience.
- **Self-deception flags:** Assures himself the errand is manageable while privately noting the danger ("Once we cross the stream, we are in the Widow's power"; recalling the poison rumor when his thirst "fled at once"); tells himself of Rohanne "all thought of gallantry had abandoned Dunk" even as he then fumbles for compliments.
