# TSS — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** TSS
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** Standfast (tower) and the road/woods between Coldmoat and Standfast
- **location_secondary:** Wat's Wood, the Chequy Water and its dam, Coldmoat (departed at the opening)
- **approximate_timeline:** A single evening/night — Dunk and Egg ride home from Coldmoat, detour to the dam, cross Wat's Wood after dark, and reach Standfast by moonrise, where Dunk resigns Ser Eustace's service.
- **time_markers:** "Not until the walls and towers of Coldmoat had vanished in the west"; "Night caught them in Wat's Wood"; "the sun was low on the horizon and the first stars were coming out, along with clouds of mites"; "The gloom was deepening"; "The moon had risen by the time they came to Standfast"; "a thousand years" (Osgreys as Marshalls; Manderlys driven off); "He has been at Summerhall for a year, and half of another"; "Alysanne was seven when they took her off … and twenty when she died"; "a boy of twelve" (Daemon knighted); "back before the Conquest" / "since the Conquest" (the silver cup, Blackfyre); "It was all years ago" (the rebellion)
- **chapter_summary:** Riding home from Coldmoat, Egg recounts the royal grant that gives the Chequy Water to Lord Webber and would revert Standfast to the crown should Ser Eustace die without a male heir. The pair detour to the dam, find it guarded by Webber crossbowmen, and retreat through Wat's Wood after dark, debating bastardy, Lord Bloodraven, and Dunk's unknown parentage. At Standfast, Ser Eustace confesses that his sons died fighting for Daemon Blackfyre, the "black dragon," not for King Daeron, and that he bought his pardon by giving his daughter Alysanne to King's Landing as a hostage. When Eustace insults Egg's father and threatens to beat the boy, Dunk resigns his service, lays his cloak in the old knight's lap, and is cast out.
- **unit_part:** 6 of 8

## Events & Actions
1. **Egg recounts the grant of rights** — As Coldmoat vanishes behind them, Egg recites the sealed grant from memory: the Chequy Water and hunting/wood rights in Wat's Wood go to Lord Wyman Webber, and Standfast reverts to the crown if Eustace dies without a male heir.
   - Agent: Egg
   - Patient: Dunk (audience)
   - Location: The road west of Coldmoat
   - Outcome: Information conveyed
2. **Detour to the dam** — Dunk leads them north for another look at the dam, half-meaning to tear it apart.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: Wat's Wood / the Chequy Water
   - Outcome: Detour
3. **The dam is found guarded; Dunk retreats** — Two Webber crossbowmen with spider badges guard the dam, one barefoot in the stream; both ready their bows, and Dunk can only "scowl at them threateningly" before retracing his steps.
   - Agent: The two crossbowmen
   - Patient: Dunk and Egg
   - Instrument: Crossbows
   - Location: The dam
   - Outcome: Dunk forced to withdraw
4. **Night crossing of Wat's Wood** — They splash back across the stream at sundown and travel on through the dark wood, debating bastardy and Bloodraven.
   - Agent: Dunk and Egg
   - Location: Wat's Wood
   - Outcome: Arrival at Standfast by moonrise
5. **Bennis reports at Standfast** — Bennis, keeping watch on the steps, tells Dunk the men are abed or on the roof (Treb and Wet Wat) and that he worked the garrison hard, bloodying "that big lackwit."
   - Agent: Bennis
   - Patient: Dunk (audience)
   - Location: Standfast steps
   - Outcome: Status conveyed
6. **Egg tends the mounts** — At Dunk's order, Egg goes to "Tend to Thunder and Maester" before joining them upstairs.
   - Agent: Egg
   - Instigator: Dunk
   - Location: Standfast stable
7. **Dunk reports the parley to Eustace** — Dunk relays the Red Widow's terms (she keeps the water, wants Bennis, holds a sealed royal parchment), omitting the Lady Helicent episode; he admits she slapped him.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Ser Eustace (audience)
   - Location: Eustace's chamber
   - Outcome: Eustace outraged at the slap
8. **Ser Eustace confesses his black-dragon loyalty** — Eustace admits his sons died for Daemon Blackfyre, "the rightful king," not for Daeron; that he marched to seat Daemon on the Iron Throne; and lists the "ifs" by which the rebellion might have been won.
   - Agent: Ser Eustace
   - Patient: Dunk (audience)
   - Location: Eustace's chamber
   - Witness: Dunk
   - Outcome: The concealed loyalty revealed
9. **Eustace recounts the hostage price** — Eustace tells how he bought his pardon by surrendering his seven-year-old daughter Alysanne, who died a silent sister at twenty and refused to speak to him.
   - Agent: Ser Eustace
   - Patient: Dunk (audience)
   - Location: Eustace's chamber
   - Outcome: The daughter's fate revealed
10. **Egg challenges Eustace; Eustace insults Maekar** — Egg presses why Eustace chose Daemon and quotes his father; Eustace calls the boy's father "a fool," and, when Egg objects, threatens to beat him.
    - Agent: Ser Eustace
    - Patient: Egg
    - Location: Eustace's chamber
    - Outcome: Confrontation escalates
11. **Dunk resigns Ser Eustace's service** — Dunk refuses to let Eustace beat Egg, declares they will leave at first light, and lays his rolled cloak in the old knight's lap.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Ser Eustace
    - Instrument: His cloak (laid in the lap)
    - Location: Eustace's chamber
    - Outcome: Service ended
12. **Eustace casts them out** — Eustace accuses Dunk of leaving for the Red Widow, then throws them out: "Go, then. Go. I want none of you."
    - Agent: Ser Eustace
    - Patient: Dunk and Egg
    - Location: Eustace's chamber
    - Outcome: Expulsion

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Betrayed and disillusioned — anger at being "played for a fool," pity for the old knight, unsettled reflection on his own origins.
- **Primary preoccupation:** That Eustace lied about his sons and the stream; whether the old knight's cause and honor were real; and, prompted by the bastardy talk, his own unknown parentage.
- **Key decisions made:** To resign Ser Eustace's service and leave Standfast at first light, refusing to let Eustace beat Egg.
- **Self-deception flags:** "Dunk thought he knew the answer, but it was not one the boy would want to hear"; he omits the Lady Helicent episode from his report because it "made him look an utter fool."
