# TSS — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** TSS
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** The Osgrey lands — the Chequy Water streambed (crossroads, plank bridge, Wat's Wood, and the dam)
- **location_secondary:** Standfast, Coldmoat, Dosk/Little Dosk (recalled), King's Landing (recalled)
- **approximate_timeline:** A single day's ride, in the second year of a great drought; two years after the death of Ser Arlan; six days after Dunk and Egg set out to fetch wine.
- **time_markers:** "summer sun"; "since breaking camp this morning"; "the day they left Dosk"; "back by evenfall"; "the drought entering its second year"; "Six years ago in King's Landing"; "half a year ago" (Aemon measured Dunk in Oldtown); "Ser Arlan's two years dead"; "We've only been six days"; "an hour upstream from the bridge"; "Half a league farther south"; "Two years ago" (the Red Widow drowned Dake); "at night it grew so cold" (Dorne, recalled)
- **chapter_summary:** Riding home to Standfast with two casks of wine, the hedge knight Dunk and his squire Egg pass two dead men rotting in a crow cage at a crossroads and talk of outlaws and the drought. At the plank bridge they rejoin Ser Bennis of the Brown Shield and find the Chequy Water gone dry. Dunk sends Egg on with the wine and rides upstream with Bennis, where they discover a dam that diverts the stream into Lady Webber's fields. The two knights charge a score of Coldmoat diggers; Bennis slashes an old digger's cheek before the diggers are driven off with warnings that their lady will answer. Riding home through Wat's Wood, Dunk and Bennis argue over whether to tell Ser Eustace the truth about who took the water.
- **unit_part:** 1 of 8

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Blazing hot; blue, hard, cloudless sky; still, suffocating, windless air; a great drought in its second year.
- **Season indicators:** Summer. Brown, parched vegetation; devilgrass and goatheads clinging to the verges; melons shriveling on the vines; grapes turning to raisins on the vines at the Arbor; oaks shedding leaves and pines browning with rings of dead needles; wells and rivers running dry.
- **Time of day:** Morning through afternoon (they broke camp in the morning; hoped to be back "by evenfall").
- **Lighting:** Bright, blazing sun; "the glint of sun on water" at the dam; sunlight on the sourleaf Bennis spat.
- **Sounds:** Hundreds of flies buzzing/crawling; crows rising "like a black cloud"; Bennis's laughter (a "chicken laugh" and a braying "louder than Egg's mule"); the bridge planks creaking heavily; the recalled harper's song at Dosk.
- **Smells:** The rot of the two dead men; dead fish in the streambed; Ser Bennis's stink ("smelled the same as well"); sourleaf.
- **Notable sensory details:** A leaf crumbles "like thousand-year-old parchment" in Dunk's fingers; a thrown stone crashes through the crumbling bank "in a puff of dry brown earth"; the road baked "as hard as brick" with ruts deep enough to break a horse's leg.

## Events & Actions
1. **Two dead men rot in an iron crow cage at the crossroads** — Dunk and Egg stop and examine two half-starved corpses forced into one small cage; one had gnawed the other's neck, crows had been at both, one is missing his tongue.
   - Patient: The two dead men (unnamed)
   - Location: The crossroads
   - Witness: Dunk, Egg
   - Outcome: Left behind unburied
2. **Dunk and Egg speculate on the dead men and outlaws** — They debate whether the men were robbers, poachers, or outlaws; Egg has been "seeing gallant outlaws behind every bush" since hearing "The Day They Hanged Black Robin" at Dosk.
3. **Recollection — Ser Arlan helped hang a ring-stealing outlaw** — Dunk recalls an outlaw who cut off men's fingers for their rings and bit women, whom Ser Arlan helped hang.
   - Agent: Ser Arlan of Pennytree
   - Patient: The ring-stealing outlaw
   - Outcome: Hanging
4. **Recollection — Dunk saw Bloodraven in King's Landing** — Six years past, Dunk saw Lord Bloodraven ride up the Street of Steel with fifty Raven's Teeth; Bloodraven turned his single red eye on Dunk.
   - Agent: Lord Bloodraven
   - Location: Street of Steel, King's Landing
   - Witness: Dunk
5. **Recollection — Ironmen raid Little Dosk and kill Pate** — Dagon Greyjoy's ironmen raided Little Dosk, carrying off wealth and women and burning half; they killed Pinchbottom Pate when he tried to stop them taking his daughter.
   - Agent: The ironmen (of Dagon Greyjoy's raid)
   - Patient: Pinchbottom Pate; his daughter (carried off)
   - Instigator: Dagon Greyjoy
   - Location: Little Dosk
   - Outcome: Pate dead; daughter and wealth taken; town half-burned
6. **Dunk and Egg reach the bridge and find the stream dry** — Rejoining Ser Bennis at the plank bridge, they discover only sand and stones beneath it; the Chequy Water is gone.
   - Location: The old plank bridge
   - Outcome: Discovery of the dry stream
7. **Dunk examines the streambed** — He turns over a stone (dry above, moist beneath), notes cracked banks with muddy middle and fresh-dead fish, and concludes the water vanished only the day before.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: The dry streambed
8. **Recollection — the Red Widow drowned Dake** — Two years past, Lady Webber sent knights to seize Dake for sheep-stealing; she sewed him in a sack of rocks and sank him in her moat, after which Ser Eustace took Bennis into service.
   - Agent: Lady Webber
   - Patient: Dake
   - Location: Coldmoat (the moat)
   - Outcome: Dake dead
9. **Dunk sends Egg home and rides to investigate** — Dunk orders Egg to take the wine to Standfast and tell Ser Eustace where he went; Dunk turns Thunder south and west, upstream; Bennis follows to keep him from getting hanged.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg (sent home)
   - Location: The bridge
10. **Ride upstream through Wat's Wood** — Dunk and Bennis follow the dry course an hour to Wat's Wood, cross to the cleared Coldmoat side past black-nosed sheep.
    - Agent: Dunk, Ser Bennis
    - Location: Wat's Wood / the streambed
11. **They discover the dam** — Half a league farther south they find the dam diverting the Chequy Water into Lady Webber's fields via a spider's web of channels; the dam's trees came from Wat's Wood.
    - Agent: Dunk, Ser Bennis
    - Location: The dam
    - Outcome: Discovery that Coldmoat has stolen the stream
12. **The knights charge the diggers; Bennis interrogates them** — Bennis draws his sword and gallops at a score of diggers; they form a ragged line; questioned, a young digger says "Maester Cerrick made it," and an older man says "the gray pup" directed it but the diggers built it.
    - Agent: Ser Bennis, Dunk
    - Patient: The Coldmoat diggers
    - Location: The dam
13. **Bennis cuts the old digger** — Bennis pricks the old digger's chest to draw a bead of blood, then, when the man defies him, slashes his cheek "from ear to jaw" with his longsword.
    - Agent: Ser Bennis
    - Patient: The old digger
    - Instrument: Ser Bennis's longsword
    - Location: The dam
    - Witness: Dunk
    - Outcome: The old digger wounded (cheek opened, bleeding)
14. **The diggers are driven off with warnings** — Three diggers flee; Jorgen hefts a pick but the old man calls it "a fool's fight"; Dunk orders them back to their lady's castle; the diggers promise to tell Lady Webber and warn "This won't be the end of this."
    - Agent: Dunk (orders them off)
    - Patient: The diggers
    - Location: The dam
    - Outcome: Diggers withdraw, vowing to report to their lady
15. **Ride home through Wat's Wood, arguing over the truth** — Dunk and Bennis ride back through the wood; Bennis argues they should lie to Ser Eustace and blame the drought; Dunk insists "A sworn sword owes his lord the truth" and rides ahead to escape Bennis's stink.
    - Agent: Dunk, Ser Bennis
    - Location: Wat's Wood

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Hot, thirsty, and sweltering; increasingly irritated by Bennis's mockery and stench; indignant when he sees the dam ("They are stealing our stream"); "sorely tempted to put his fist through the brown knight's face"; dismayed at the drought and the dry stream; a shiver at the memory of Bloodraven.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The lost water — where the stream went and what it means for the crops; his duty to get the wine to Standfast and to Ser Eustace; the two dead men keep returning to his mind.
- **Key decisions made:** Turn toward Standfast at the fork; keep his tunic on out of duty despite the heat; investigate the stream rather than let it be; send Egg home with the wine; resolve to tell Ser Eustace the truth about the dam; ride ahead of Bennis to escape his smell.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself Egg's lack of sweat "is his dragon blood" ("Whoever heard of a sweaty dragon?"); reassures himself that "Bennis was on his side in this" even as he judges the cutting wrong; measures himself against the "Dunk the lunk" name, insisting he is Ser Duncan the Tall.
