- **chapter_summary:** Robb holds court at Riverrun and presents peace terms to Ser Cleos Frey to carry to Cersei in King's Landing, demanding the return of his sisters, Ned's bones, Ice, his captured lords, and recognition of northern independence. After court, Catelyn argues with Robb about releasing Jaime Lannister and about sending Theon Greyjoy to treat with Balon Greyjoy. Catelyn visits her dying father Lord Hoster, where she finds Ser Brynden Tully freshly returned from scouting; the Blackfish reports on Lannister devastation of the riverlands and a new army mustering at Casterly Rock, and they discuss the possibility of treating with Renly Baratheon.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ser Cleos Frey brought before Robb's court** — Guards bring the prisoner into the Great Hall; Ser Robin Ryger announces him; Theon Greyjoy commands him to kneel
2. **Robb presents himself as king** — Seated with crown and drawn sword across his knees; Grey Wind at his side providing intimidation
3. **Robb announces peace terms** — Demands: (1) return of Sansa and Arya via sea to White Harbor, end of Sansa's betrothal to Joffrey; (2) return of Ned's bones; (3) return of Ice to Riverrun; (4) release of lords taken at Green Fork in exchange for Robb's captives except Jaime; (5) renunciation of all claims over the North and the riverlands as an independent kingdom
4. **Lord Karstark walks out** — During the reading of terms, Lord Rickard Karstark shoves through guards and exits the hall
5. **Grey Wind used to threaten Ser Cleos** — Robb whistles and Grey Wind snarls; Ser Cleos goes "the color of curdled milk"
6. **Robb dismisses court** — Orders Ser Robin to feed and clothe Ser Cleos; lords and knights bend the knee as Robb exits
7. **Catelyn advises Robb in the gallery** — Criticizes the wolf intimidation as unbecoming; notes Karstark's departure
8. **Edmure argues for marching on Harrenhal** — Claims sitting idle weakens them as lords depart
9. **Catelyn blames Edmure** — Points out it was his insistence that Robb gave river lords leave to depart
10. **Robb refuses to release Jaime** — Despite Catelyn's argument that Cersei will only trade for her brother; Robb says his lords would never abide it
11. **Catelyn and Robb argue about the value of the girls** — Catelyn implies girls are not important enough; Robb is hurt but won't admit it
12. **Robb tells Catelyn he's sending Theon to Pyke** — Wants to treat with Balon Greyjoy for longships; suggests Catelyn go to the Twins or with Theon to Winterfell
13. **Catelyn opposes sending Theon** — Suggests Jason Mallister, Tytos Blackwood, or Stevron Frey instead; warns Balon is untrustworthy and may seek his own crown again
14. **Robb dismisses Catelyn's counsel** — Walks off with Grey Wind; Catelyn feels sidelined
15. **Catelyn walks through the godswood** — Notes autumn declared but not yet felt; reflects on season and mortality
16. **Catelyn visits dying Lord Hoster** — Finds him sleeping in his solar; he is white-haired, frail, diminished
17. **Catelyn meets Ser Brynden at Hoster's bedside** — He has returned from scouting, still in mail and travel-stained cloak
18. **Brynden reports on the riverlands** — Devastation by Gregor Clegane, Amory Lorch, and sellsword from Qohor; Beric Dondarrion raiding Lannister foragers; Bracken wounded, Hendry Bracken slain; Blackwood lands scorched; Darry garrison massacred including the child lord
19. **Brynden reports new army at Casterly Rock** — Commanded by Ser Stafford Lannister; made of sellswords, freeriders, and green boys
20. **Catelyn and Brynden discuss Renly** — Conclude he could force Tywin from Harrenhal but will want homage in return

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief-stricken over Ned (wakes expecting him beside her), anxious for her daughters, frustrated at being sidelined by her son, weary, guilty ("my doing, mine, no other") about sending Ned south, fearful for the future
- **Primary preoccupation:** The safety of Sansa and Arya; the fear that Robb's terms are too poor to secure their return; the impossibility of retrieving them without trading Jaime
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to visit her dying father rather than pursue the argument further; does not agree to go to the Twins or Winterfell; silently accepts Robb's decision to send Theon despite her opposition; begins thinking toward treating with Renly
- **Self-deception flags:** Blames herself entirely for Ned's death ("Go, I told him... my doing, mine, no other") — arguably overstates her role; does not fully acknowledge that Robb may have valid political reasons for refusing to trade Jaime beyond stubbornness
