- **chapter_summary:** Maester Cressen observes the red comet at dawn on Dragonstone and receives Shireen and Patchface, showing them the white raven that heralds summer's end. He learns Ser Davos has returned from a failed diplomatic mission — the storm lords will not rally to Stannis. Cressen climbs to counsel Stannis, who refuses to ally with Robb Stark or treat with Renly; Lady Selyse interrupts, advocating for the Lord of Light and suggesting Renly's death. Dismissed, Cressen resolves to poison Melisandre with the strangler. At the evening feast, he finds Pylos in his seat and himself marginalized. He drops the strangler into a cup of wine and offers it to Melisandre; she drinks, then he drinks the remainder. The poison kills Cressen while Melisandre survives unharmed.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Hazy morning air with smoke of cookfires; night wind "sharp with the smell of the sea"
- **Season indicators:** White raven arrives declaring summer's end; "ten years, two turns, and sixteen days" was the length of summer — the longest in living memory; Shireen is a "summer child" who has never known true cold; smallfolk say a long summer means an even longer winter
- **Time of day:** Opens at dawn ("the comet's tail spread across the dawn"); transitions to "full dark" when Cressen wakes for the feast; the feast occurs at night
- **Lighting:** Pink and purple dawn sky; red comet visible by day and night; candlelight in Cressen's chambers; torches along the walls of Dragonstone at night; torchlight in the Great Hall; cookfires like "a field of stars" in the camp at night
- **Sounds:** Sea crashing beneath the balcony; Patchface's cowbells (multiple distinct descriptions: "clang-a-dang bong-dong ring-a-ling clong clong clong"); archers calling "Notch, draw, loose" and arrows sounding "like a flock of birds taking wing"; clatter of knife and plate; low mutter of table talk; night wind whispering through windows
- **Smells:** Night air "sharp with the smell of the sea"; morning air "hazy with the smoke of cookfires"
- **Notable sensory details:** Black stone "rough beneath his fingers"; the sea crashing below; Cressen's papery thin skin showing "the web of veins and the shape of bones beneath"; his trembling hands; the maester's chain feeling heavy and cold against his skin; Melisandre's skin felt "hot, feverish"; wine "sour on his tongue"; "iron fingers tightened round his neck" (strangler's effect)

## Events & Actions
1. **Cressen observes the comet at dawn** — Standing on his balcony between gargoyles, watching the blood-red comet in the sky and reflecting on its ominous appearance
2. **Pylos announces visitors** — Shireen and Patchface have come to see the white raven
3. **Shireen visits Cressen** — She tells him of bad dreams about dragons; Cressen reassures her that Dragonstone's dragon-shaped towers are carved stone
4. **Shireen reports the red woman's interpretation** — Dalla told Matrice that the red woman said the comet was "dragonsbreath"
5. **Cressen explains the white raven** — Tells Shireen the Conclave has declared summer ended after ten years, two turns, and sixteen days
6. **Patchface sings cryptic songs** — About things "under the sea" and "the shadows come to dance"
7. **Pylos shows the white raven** — The bird speaks ("Lady") and impresses Shireen
8. **Pylos reports Davos's return** — Davos arrived in the black of morning and has been with the king most of the night
9. **Cressen encounters Davos on the stairs** — Davos reports his diplomatic mission failed; storm lords will not rise for Stannis; some declared for Renly; Renly has created Rainbow Guard with Loras Tyrell as Lord Commander
10. **Cressen reaches the Chamber of the Painted Table** — Stannis dismisses Cressen's approach, saying he knew the old man would come
11. **Stannis vents about his grievances** — Against Robert for giving Storm's End to Renly, against Renly for declaring himself king, against the storm lords for not supporting him
12. **Cressen counsels alliance** — Suggests common cause with Renly, then Robb Stark, then Lady Arryn (betrothing Shireen to Robert Arryn); Stannis partially considers the Arryn plan
13. **Lady Selyse enters and interrupts** — Advocates for R'hllor's power; references Melisandre's flame-visions showing Renly dead
14. **Cressen is horrified by implication of fratricide** — Protests; Selyse mocks his earlier counsel
15. **Stannis dismisses Cressen** — Tells him Pylos will counsel henceforth
16. **Cressen returns to his chambers and resolves to act** — Names Melisandre aloud; retrieves the strangler crystals from his workroom
17. **Cressen prepares for the feast** — Studies the poison; recalls its properties; plans to put it in Melisandre's wine; naps
18. **Cressen wakes late to find he was not summoned** — Discovers Pylos did not wake him; calls servants to help him to the feast
19. **Cressen enters the Great Hall** — Finds Pylos in his place; is publicly humiliated
20. **Patchface knocks Cressen down** — Dancing fool collides with maester; they fall together; laughter erupts
21. **Melisandre helps Cressen up** — Places Patchface's fool's helm on his head; the hall laughs
22. **Stannis confirms Cressen's replacement** — Says Cressen is too old and confused; Pylos will counsel henceforth
23. **Davos offers his seat** — Cressen sits beside Davos at the lower end
24. **Cressen makes one final appeal for alliance** — Stannis refuses, claiming all others are usurpers and enemies; Selyse invokes R'hllor
25. **Cressen drops the strangler into Davos's wine cup** — Davos sees but no one else does
26. **Cressen offers the poisoned cup to Melisandre** — She accepts; drinks most of it; offers remainder back
27. **Cressen drinks the poisoned wine and dies** — The strangler kills him; Melisandre is unharmed; she declares "He does have power here, my lord" and "fire cleanses"

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear and foreboding (comet, end of summer); paternal love for Stannis, Shireen; deep loathing of Melisandre; despair at being dismissed; grim resolve to commit murder; acceptance of death
- **Primary preoccupation:** Protecting Stannis from Melisandre's influence; preventing fratricide against Renly; his own obsolescence and physical decline
- **Key decisions made:** Resolves to poison Melisandre with the strangler at the feast; when he cannot reach her cup unseen, he openly offers to share wine with her — knowing he will also die
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself he doesn't believe in omens, yet is deeply disturbed by the comet; tells himself he is acting "for the sake of the realm, and the soul of my lord" — but is also motivated by personal grief at being replaced and loss of influence; believes his plan will work despite his hands trembling and his mind wandering
