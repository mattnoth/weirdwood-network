Arya encounters the three chained prisoners — Jaqen H'ghar, Rorge, and Biter — and strikes Biter with her practice sword when he grabs for her. Six gold cloaks arrive with the queen's warrant for "a certain boy," and Arya reveals herself thinking she is the target, but they actually want the Bull, who reveals his true name as Gendry. Yoren forces the gold cloaks to leave at swordpoint.

## Events & Actions
1. **Northward travel on the kingsroad** — Yoren's party of thirty travels dawn to dusk, passing through woods, orchards, fields, villages, market towns, and holdfasts, camping each night by the light of the Red Sword (comet).
2. **Mass displacement observed** — Increasing numbers of refugees travel south toward King's Landing: old and young, on foot and in carts, many armed with improvised weapons. They eye the wagons but let the column of thirty pass.
3. **Madwoman's warning** — A scarecrow-thin woman with hollow eyes and bloody feet screams from the roadside: "Fools! They'll kill you, fools!"
4. **Merchant's offer refused** — A sleek merchant on a grey mare offers to buy Yoren's wagons at a quarter their worth, warning "It's war, they'll take what they want." Yoren turns away and spits.
5. **Graves along the road** — Arya notices graves appearing along the kingsroad, starting with a small child's grave with a crystal set in the earth. Lommy wants the crystal but the Bull tells him to leave the dead alone. More graves appear daily.
6. **Arya's night of unease** — Arya wakes in darkness to eerie silence beneath the Red Sword and stars. She senses something wrong but cannot name it. She goes back to sleep clutching Needle.
7. **Praed's death and burial** — Come morning, Praed has died (Arya realizes his coughing was what she'd missed). Yoren strips his valuables. His boots, dagger, mail, and helm are distributed. His longsword goes to the Bull. Tarber tosses acorns on the grave so an oak might grow.
8. **Arrival at the inn** — The party stops at an ivy-covered inn in a village. Yoren pays for a hot meal. Some recruits use the bathhouse; Arya does not, to protect her disguise.
9. **Supper and beer** — Everyone eats hot pork pies and baked apples. The innkeeper gives free beer because his brother Lync took the black years ago for stealing pepper from Ser Malcolm's table.
10. **War talk in the common room** — The innkeeper warns there's no going north: fields burned, people walled up, riders coming and going. He says it's more than Tully vs. Lannister — wild men from the Mountains of the Moon, and the Starks are in it too. Arya hears them mention "the young lord," "the dead Hand's son."
11. **Wolf rumors** — Hod claims Robb rides a wolf big as a horse into battle. The man in the green cloak reports massive wolf packs near the Gods Eye led by a bold she-wolf. The old woman's cousin confirms hundreds of mankillers led by "a bitch from the seventh hell." Lord Mooton hunted her with wolfhounds and barely escaped alive.
12. **Arya's outburst** — Arya blurts out "Wolves don't eat babies," defending the she-wolf. Yoren grabs her, calls her "greensick on beer," and shoves her outside to the stables.
13. **Encounter with the three prisoners** — Outside, Jaqen H'ghar calls to Arya and asks for more beer, introducing himself as "once of the Free City of Lorath." He names his companions: Rorge (noseless) and Biter (tongueless, teeth filed to points). Rorge threatens Arya; Biter lunges for her but is stopped by his chains.
14. **Arya strikes Biter** — Arya approaches despite her fear, reciting Syrio's words. When Biter grabs for her, she hits him hard between the eyes with her wooden sword. Biter strains against his chains but they hold. Jaqen observes she has "more courage than sense."
15. **The Bull intervenes** — Gendry finds Arya and leads her away, warning that Yoren said to stay away from the three prisoners. He admits they scare him.
16. **Sparring challenge** — Arya asks the Bull to fight. He draws Praed's longsword; she draws Needle. They banter about whose steel is better.
17. **Gold cloaks arrive** — Six gold cloaks ride up on lathered horses. The officer wears a black enamel breastplate with four golden disks. Arya pulls Gendry behind a tall flowering hedge, believing they've come for her.
18. **The warrant** — The gold cloak officer presents the queen's warrant with golden wax seal, demanding "a certain boy." Yoren argues the boy is Night's Watch now and beyond the queen's reach.
19. **Yoren's men rally** — When the officer draws his sword, Tarber takes up a pitchfork, Cutjack his hammer, Kurz his skinning knife, Koss strings his longbow, Reysen grabs his staff, Dobber emerges naked with his dagger, and Hot Pie grabs a rock.
20. **Arya reveals herself** — Arya pushes through the hedge with Needle, declaring "I'm the one you want," assuming they seek her. The officer dismisses her.
21. **The true target revealed** — The officer points his sword at the Bull: "He's the one we want."
22. **Yoren's stand** — Yoren puts his sword to the officer's throat and forces the gold cloaks to leave, taking the officer's dropped shortsword. He gives it to Hot Pie.
23. **Gendry's name revealed** — The Bull reveals his true name is Gendry. Yoren says the queen wants him and orders Arya and Gendry to ride the two coursers, fleeing at first sight of gold cloaks.
24. **Yoren orders night march** — Yoren warns the gold cloaks will return and orders the party to ride all night.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Alternates between quiet melancholy (thinking of Sansa and her father), sharp curiosity (straining to hear about Robb, wondering about Nymeria), stubborn defiance (defending wolves, confronting Biter, offering herself to the gold cloaks), and confusion (realizing the gold cloaks want Gendry, not her)
- **Primary preoccupation:** Maintaining her disguise as Arry; processing war news and rumors about her family; wondering whether the she-wolf is Nymeria
- **Key decisions made:** Does not bathe (to protect disguise); confronts Biter despite fear (guided by Syrio's teachings); reveals herself to the gold cloaks believing they want her; accepts Yoren's instructions to ride a courser and be ready to flee
- **Self-deception flags:** Assumes the gold cloaks must be after her, projecting her own fear of pursuit; does not consider why the queen might want anyone else. Tells herself Nymeria "probably wouldn't even know me now. Or if she did, she'd hate me" — guilt over driving her away
