## Chapter Metadata

- **location_primary:** King's Landing

- **approximate_timeline:** During the lead-up to Stannis's march on King's Landing; after the riots but before the Battle of the Blackwater
- **time_markers:** Night (the Red Keep is dark when Bronn arrives); after evenfall bells (curfew is in effect); half-moon visible in garden; Varys arrives "in the dead of night"
- **chapter_summary:** Lancel informs Tyrion that Cersei plans to send Tommen to Rosby in disguise. Tyrion co-opts the plan, sending Bronn with orders for Ser Jacelyn Bywater to intercept Tommen's party and hold him at Rosby castle. Tyrion visits Shae at her manse, where he threatens a singer named Symon Silver Tongue who recognized him. Varys arrives disguised as a begging brother to report that Ser Cortnay Penrose is dead and Storm's End has fallen to Stannis. Tyrion and Varys discuss moving Shae into the Red Keep as Lollys's maidservant, secret passages built by Maegor, and Varys shares the story of his castration by a sorcerer in Myr. Varys also reports that Lord Tywin may be trapped between enemies and there is no word from Littlefinger.

## Events & Actions
1. **Lancel informs Tyrion of Cersei's plan** — In the sept, Lancel tells Tyrion that Cersei intends to send Tommen to Rosby disguised as a page under Lord Gyles's escort, with darkened hair, posing as a hedge knight's son.
2. **Lancel asks for a boon** — Lancel requests his own command in the next battle; Tyrion gives a noncommittal answer.
3. **Tyrion lights candles** — At the Warrior's altar, Tyrion lights a candle for Jaime. He lights a second candle to the Stranger, for himself.
4. **Tyrion gives Bronn a sealed decoy letter** — The letter instructs Bywater to scout the roseroad; Tyrion's verbal orders are for Bywater to take fifty swords north along the Rosby road, intercept Tommen's escort, and seize control of Rosby castle.
5. **Tyrion instructs Bronn on treatment of Tommen** — No killing in front of Tommen; Lord Gyles is to be kept alive; Bywater to hold Rosby and be elevated to lord.
6. **Tyrion and Bronn ride out through a postern gate** — They exit the north wall into the dark, curfew-enforced streets. Gold cloaks on Coppersmith's Wynd recognize and wave them through.
7. **Bronn and Tyrion part ways** — Bronn turns south for the Mud Gate; Tyrion initially heads toward Chataya's.
8. **Tyrion abandons his cover route** — Impatient and seeing no followers, Tyrion races through the moonlit streets directly to Shae's manse.
9. **Tyrion arrives at Shae's manse** — The Ibbenese man admits him. He hears a singer performing inside.
10. **Tyrion confronts Symon Silver Tongue** — The singer recognized and addressed Tyrion as "my lord Hand"; Tyrion threatens him about the danger of knowing about Shae and her location.
11. **Tyrion and Shae retire upstairs** — They go to bed together; she teases him about frightening the singer.
12. **Tyrion asks Shae to keep Symon close** — He won't have the singer wandering the city spreading tales.
13. **Tyrion goes to the garden afterward** — He sits by the stone bathing pond under the half-moon, hearing a cricket.
14. **Varys arrives disguised as a begging brother** — Shae identifies him despite the disguise; she explains whores learn to "see the man, not his garb."
15. **Varys reports Ser Cortnay Penrose's death** — Penrose allegedly threw himself from a tower; Storm's End has opened its gates to Stannis.
16. **Tyrion reacts with dismay** — He flings his wine cup against the wall; curses both Penrose and Stannis.
17. **Tyrion tells Shae she is not safe** — He references the riots, the goldsmith's death, Lollys's rape, and Ser Aron's murder as examples of mob violence.
18. **Shae asks to be Tyrion's lady openly** — She speaks of wearing his jewels and sitting by him at feasts; he says it can never be.
19. **Shae suggests killing Cersei** — Tyrion refuses, citing kinslaying as accursed and his inability to face Jaime.
20. **Tyrion proposes moving Shae to the castle kitchens** — Shae reacts badly; the idea of being a scullion reminds her of her father's household.
21. **Tyrion tells Shae about Tysha** — He reveals his first marriage, Jaime's role in hiring her, and Tywin's order for guardsmen to gang-rape her while Tyrion watched.
22. **Tyrion slaps Shae** — After she mocks his fear of his father ("What could he do, spank you?"), he strikes her; immediately regrets it and apologizes.
23. **Shae submits reluctantly** — She says "I am yours to command, m'lord" in a flat voice.
24. **Tyrion and Varys ride back toward the Red Keep** — Tyrion asks Varys to arrange moving Shae; they discuss the kitchen plan's dangers.
25. **Varys proposes placing Shae as Lollys's maidservant** — Lady Tanda's current maidservant is a thief; dismissing her creates an opening. Tyrion sees advantages.
26. **Varys reveals the Red Keep has secret passages** — He could slip Shae into Tyrion's bedchamber unseen through a hidden door, built by Maegor.
27. **Tyrion asks about Cortnay Penrose's death** — Varys implies supernatural means; notes Penrose challenged Stannis to combat that same morning, and connects it to Renly's mysterious death.
28. **Varys tells the story of his castration** — As a boy in Myr, a sorcerer bought him from a traveling folly, drugged him, sliced off his manhood with a hooked blade, burned it on a brazier, and a voice answered from blue flames. Varys was cast out and survived by begging, stealing, and selling himself.
29. **Varys declares his hatred of magic** — He says if Stannis uses dark powers, he means to see him dead.
30. **Tyrion asks about military situation** — No word from Tywin crossing the Red Fork; Oakheart and Rowan forces seen north of the Mander; no word from Littlefinger; Lord Tarly has seized Renly's stores at Bitterbridge, killed Florents; Lord Caswell has shut himself in his castle.
31. **Tyrion laughs bitterly** — He reflects that he alone — "the dwarf, the evil counselor, the twisted little monkey demon" — stands between King's Landing and chaos.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious, frustrated, tender toward Shae but increasingly desperate; dismayed by Storm's End's fall; bitter about his role as protector of a city that hates him; guilty after slapping Shae; regretful about revealing Tysha's story
- **Primary preoccupation:** Managing multiple simultaneous threats — Cersei's plots, Stannis's advance, Shae's safety, his father's military situation — while maintaining secrecy
- **Key decisions made:** Co-opts Cersei's Tommen plan (Bywater to hold Rosby); agrees to Varys's proposal to place Shae as Lollys's maidservant; demands access to secret passages
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion wonders if Shae loves him or only his gold ("Fool of a dwarf, it is only the gold and jewels the whore loves"); he wants forgiveness from Shae for his Tysha story but questions why he told her; he says "I trust you implicitly" to Varys with obvious bitter sarcasm, yet continues relying on him
