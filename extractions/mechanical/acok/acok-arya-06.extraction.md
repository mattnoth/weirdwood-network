- **chapter_summary:** Arya recounts eight days of captivity at a storehouse by the Gods Eye where the Mountain's men tortured and killed prisoners daily through the Tickler's questioning about Lord Beric Dondarrion and hidden valuables. The captives then march to Harrenhal, suffering murders and brutality along the way, while Arya begins compiling her nightly recitation of people she wants dead. Upon arrival at Harrenhal — a massive, melted ruin beside the lake — the captives are bathed, and Arya takes the name "Weasel" to hide her identity. She is assigned as a servant to Weese, the understeward of the Wailing Tower.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya reflects on eight days of captivity at the storehouse** — The Mountain selected one prisoner each day for the Tickler's questioning, conducted in full view of the other captives.
2. **Daily interrogations and killings** — The Tickler asks the same questions every day: about hidden gold, food, Lord Beric Dondarrion's location and forces, and other enemies. No one survives the questioning. Bodies are hung beyond the fires for wolves.
3. **Specific victims recalled** — A girl who shared a soldier's bed three nights is picked on the fourth day. All-for-Joffrey (who babbled about his son in the gold cloaks) is picked on the fifth. A young mother with a pox-scarred face offers information willingly; the Mountain picks her daughter the next morning.
4. **Arya doubts herself** — She concludes she is "no water dancer" and feels like a lamb rather than a direwolf, hating herself and the villagers for their meekness.
5. **Arya's identity as a girl is exposed** — On the march, she can no longer hide her sex and must relieve herself in view of others. Hot Pie gapes but the guards don't care.
6. **Murder of a toddler and his mother** — A boy of three won't stop calling for his father; a soldier smashes his face with a spiked mace. Raff the Sweetling kills the boy's screaming mother.
7. **A captive girl is beheaded** — A girl taken by four or five soldiers nightly hits one with a rock. Ser Gregor beheads her with his greatsword in front of all and orders her body left for the wolves.
8. **Arya observes Needle at Polliver's hip** — She thinks it's good they took it, or she would have tried to stab Ser Gregor and been killed.
9. **Arya catalogs her captors' personalities** — She learns who is cruel, who is lazy, who might give bread, who will hit you.
10. **Arya begins her nightly recitation of names** — "Ser Gregor, Dunsen, Polliver, Chiswyck, Raff the Sweetling. The Tickler and the Hound. Ser Amory, Ser Ilyn, Ser Meryn, King Joffrey, Queen Cersei."
11. **Villagers discuss Lord Beric and the old king** — An old woman spits for the Tullys, Lannisters, and Starks alike. An old man praises King Aerys. Arya asks about King Robert. The old man loses both teeth when a guard shuts them up.
12. **Arrival at the outskirts of Harrenhal** — Towers spotted in the distance; deceptively far due to the castle's immense scale. The stink of the Lannister camp reaches them first.
13. **Entry into Harrenhal** — Hot Pie says there are ghosts; Chiswyck tells him his only choice is to "join the ghosts, or be one." They enter the gates.
14. **Captives stripped and scrubbed in the bathhouse** — Goodwife Amabel and Goodwife Harra supervise. They discuss the captives like livestock.
15. **Arya adopts the name "Weasel"** — She dares not say Arya or Arry, so names herself after the orphan girl from her group. Goodwife Amabel orders Arya's lice-ridden hair cut off.
16. **Arya asks to tend horses instead of kitchens** — She hopes to steal a horse and escape. Goodwife Harra slaps her hard enough to reopen her swollen lip.
17. **Arya assigned to Weese** — Goodwife Amabel decides Arya is "not a clever girl" and should go to Weese rather than the kitchens. Given a grey roughspun shift and ill-fitting shoes.
18. **Weese addresses his new charges** — He boasts about smelling defiance and pride; warns that all he wants to smell is fear.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear is pervasive — "as much a part of her days as stale bread." Intense self-loathing for her passivity and meekness. Deep hatred toward her captors and those who killed people she cared about. Grief for everything she's lost. Powerlessness combined with a determined, cold cataloging of her enemies.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival through observation — learning who is dangerous, who is lazy, who might show small kindness. Building and maintaining her hate list as a form of prayer and psychological survival. Concealing her identity.
- **Key decisions made:** Adopts the alias "Weasel" rather than risk revealing her true name or her boy-alias "Arry." Does not attempt to use Needle even when she sees it at Polliver's hip, recognizing she would be killed. Asks to tend horses (hoping to steal one and escape) but is denied.
- **Self-deception flags:** Arya tells herself she is "no water dancer" and compares herself unfavorably to Syrio Forel, but this is a child judging herself against an adult master swordsman in impossible circumstances. She frames her survival instincts as cowardice.
