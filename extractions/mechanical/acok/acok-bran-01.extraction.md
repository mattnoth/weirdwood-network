# ACOK — Bran I

- **approximate_timeline:** Early in ACOK; the comet is visible, a white raven from Oldtown has recently brought word of autumn, and Robb has already gone to war as King in the North. The Walders have been at Winterfell for some time.
- **time_markers:** Dusk/nightfall (candles and hearth fires being lit, "bruised purple dusk"); "the hour grows late" (Luwin); "come the morn" (Luwin); Shaggydog bit Little Walder some days/weeks past; the Walders arrived from the Twins at an unspecified earlier date; the night the "bloody raven" brought word of Eddard's death referenced as a past event; "he had eaten no more than half a day past" (Summer in wolf dream); "night after day after night" (Shaggydog's circling pattern in dream).
- **chapter_summary:** Bran sits in his bedchamber window at Winterfell listening to Summer and Shaggydog howl, and asks various castle residents what the comet and howling mean, receiving different answers. He argues with Maester Luwin about the Walders, the confined direwolves, and his frustration at being unable to walk or ride freely. In flashback, the chapter recounts the Walders' arrival, the lord of the crossing game that ended when Shaggydog attacked Little Walder, and Rickon's subsequent friendship with the Freys. Luwin gives Bran a sleeping draught, and Osha suggests the gods may be trying to speak to him. Bran falls asleep and enters a wolf dream as Summer, prowling the godswood and sensing Shaggydog's fury at confinement.

- **Weather:** Clear enough to see the comet; no rain or precipitation described.
- **Season indicators:** White raven from Oldtown brings official word of autumn. Septon Chayle calls the comet "the sword that slays the season." Season transitioning from summer to autumn.
- **Time of day:** Dusk transitioning to night. Candles and hearth fires being lit throughout Winterfell. Diamond-shaped window panes glowing. Bruised purple dusk. The chapter ends deep in the night during a wolf dream.
- **Lighting:** Candlelight and hearth fires behind diamond-shaped panes; the comet illuminating the sky; gargoyles as black shapes against dusk; darkness among trees in the godswood (wolf dream) with the comet providing light.
- **Sounds:** Direwolf howling (Summer's long and sad, Shaggydog's savage); guards muttering curses; hounds barking furiously; horses kicking stalls; Bran's own howling; squirrels chittering in the oaks (wolf dream); echoes through yards and halls.
- **Smells:** "The green muddy stink of the hot pools" (wolf dream); "the perfume of rich rotting earth" (wolf dream); scent of squirrels (wolf dream); Shaggydog's scent described as "strong and earthy" (wolf dream). Old Nan claims she can smell the comet — "dragons."
- **Notable sensory details:** Soft crackling of fallen leaves underfoot (wolf dream); texture of thick roots, hard stones, deep layers of humus (wolf dream); the taste of hot blood and cracking bones recalled by Summer; the sleeping draught is thick, chalky, with honey; the hot water bubbling from the ground in the godswood pools; Bran's window seat described as hard stone preferred over featherbed.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran sits at window seat** — Bran prefers the hard stone of the window seat to his bed, watching Winterfell light up at dusk and listening to the direwolves howl.
2. **Bran consults castle residents about the howling and comet** — Ser Rodrik deflects; Farlen says the wolves want freedom; Gage says they smell prey; Maester Luwin says they're howling at the comet thinking it's the moon; Osha says "blood and fire"; Septon Chayle says "the sword that slays the season"; Old Nan says "dragons"; Hodor says "Hodor."
3. **White raven arrives from Oldtown** — Brings official word of autumn, seemingly confirming Septon Chayle's interpretation.
4. **Wolves confined to godswood** — Ser Rodrik confined Summer and Shaggydog to the godswood after Shaggydog bit Little Walder.
5. **Bran howls with the wolves** — Bran cups his hands and howls from his window; Summer and Shaggydog answer; they howl together, "last of their pack."
6. **Hayhead investigates** — Guard Hayhead comes to Bran's door and tells him to stop howling.
7. **Maester Luwin arrives** — Luwin tries to get Bran to sleep; checks his brow; Bran refuses to be carried, citing Mikken's iron bars.
8. **Bran tells Luwin about wolf dreams and tree dreams** — Says he turns into a wolf when he sleeps; asks if wolves dream, if dead men dream, if trees dream; tells Luwin a weirwood calls to him.
9. **Bran argues about the Walders** — Bran says he hates the Walders and commanded them sent away; Luwin reminds him they are Catelyn's wards; Bran blames them for Summer's confinement. Bran complains Alebelly wouldn't let him ride Dancer past the gate.
10. **Luwin warns about the wolfswood** — Says it's full of danger, reminds Bran of his last ride, warns outlaws could sell him to the Lannisters.
11. **Bran fantasizes about being a wolf** — Wishes he could find Arya and Sansa by scent, fight beside Robb like Grey Wind, tear out the Kingslayer's throat.
12. **Flashback: The Walders arrive at Winterfell** — Rickon screamed for mother, father, and Robb; Bran soothed him and welcomed the Freys properly with meat, mead, and a seat by the fire.
13. **Flashback: Lord of the crossing game** — Walders teach the game using a log across a hot pool in the godswood; rules explained in detail involving "Mayhaps." Little Walder is lord of the crossing more often than not.
14. **The Walders explain their names and succession** — Big Walder (son of Jammos, Lord Walder's son by fourth wife) and Little Walder (son of Merrett, grandmother was Lord Walder's third wife "the Crakehall") explain Frey naming and succession. Multiple other Walders named: Black Walder, Red Walder, Bastard Walder, Waltyr.
15. **Walders given Jon Snow's old room** — Ser Rodrik decreed it since Jon is in the Night's Watch; Bran resents it, feels they're stealing Jon's place.
16. **Other children join the game** — Turnip, Bandy, Shyra, Palla, Calon, TomToo all join. Bran watches wistfully; was named judge but forgotten.
17. **Shaggydog attacks Little Walder** — Little Walder hits Rickon with the stick; Shaggydog leaps over the plank and draws blood; Walders shriek, Rickon laughs in the mud, Hodor comes running.
18. **Rickon befriends the Walders** — After the attack, Rickon decides he likes them; they play other games, raid kitchens, race walls, train with wooden swords. Rickon shows them the crypts; Bran is furious ("That was our place, a Stark place!").
19. **Maester Luwin brings sleeping draught** — Returns with Osha and Hayhead; carries a green jar. Osha carries Bran to bed.
20. **Bran drinks the sleeping draught** — Thick, chalky, with honey. Luwin promises dreamless sleep.
21. **Osha speaks to Bran about the gods** — Notes Bran talks to the heart tree; suggests the gods may be trying to talk back. Bran falls asleep.
22. **Wolf dream begins** — Bran enters the godswood as Summer, moving on four legs. Rich sensory detail: smells of hot pools, rotting earth, squirrels. Recalls eating deer half a day past. Senses Shaggydog circling the walls in fury, searching for prey, a way out, his mother, his littermates, his pack.
23. **Summer perceives Winterfell** — Walls as "piles of dead man-rock," gates as "cold iron and splintery wood." Remembers the word "Winterfell." Knows he must answer the call of the true world beyond or die.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Frustrated, lonely, resentful, wistful. Bran feels imprisoned by his disability and confined by the adults' rules. He longs for his lost mobility and the companionship of his absent family. He feels a deep kinship with the direwolves and envies their ability to move freely. Brief moments of joy when howling with the wolves.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The wolf dreams and what they mean; his inability to walk, ride, or act as a true prince; resentment of the Walders who can do things he cannot; longing for his scattered family (Robb, Jon, Arya, Sansa, Catelyn); grief for his dead father.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to stop howling (defiance of Luwin and Hayhead); drinks the sleeping draught despite wanting the wolf dreams; resists being carried (uses iron bars instead).
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran tells himself he welcomed the Walders properly and resents being told otherwise, but his resentment of them is partially rooted in jealousy of their mobility, not just their behavior. He insists "Summer never bit anyone" while Luwin points out Summer killed a man in his chamber.
