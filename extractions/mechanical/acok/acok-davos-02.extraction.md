# ACOK — Davos II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 43
- **pov_character:** Davos Seaworth
- **pov_chapter_number:** Davos II
- **first_available:** ACOK Davos II
- **location_primary:** Storm's End
- **location_secondary:** Shipbreaker Bay, Stannis's camp outside Storm's End
- **approximate_timeline:** After Renly's death; Stannis is besieging Storm's End with his combined forces. Eight days since Davos arrived with the fleet.
- **time_markers:** "eight days since Black Betha had joined the rest of the fleet off Storm's End"; "a fortnight to consider my offer" (Stannis to Penrose); morning (parley in morning sun); "one hour hence" (council of war); "last year they were Robert's men. - **chapter_summary:** Davos rides with Stannis's lords to parley with Ser Cortnay Penrose, castellan of Storm's End, who refuses to surrender the castle or yield Robert's bastard Edric Storm, and challenges Stannis to single combat. Stannis refuses and withdraws. In a private audience, Stannis confides in Davos about Renly's death, Melisandre's visions, and his strategic situation, then orders Davos to smuggle Melisandre beneath the castle by boat. That night Davos rows Melisandre through Shipbreaker Bay into a cave tunnel under Storm's End, where she gives birth to a shadow creature that passes through the portcullis.

## Events & Actions
1. **Parley assembly** — Stannis's party rides out to meet Ser Cortnay Penrose beneath the walls of Storm's End. Davos wears mail uncomfortably and notes the great lords' splendid armor contrasted with Stannis's plain garb.
2. **Demand for surrender** — Stannis demands Ser Cortnay open the gates and surrender Edric Storm. Cortnay asks about terms; Stannis offers pardon, freedom for garrison men, retention of weapons and portable property, but requires horses and pack animals.
3. **Penrose refuses** — Ser Cortnay refuses to yield Edric Storm. Debate erupts over Renly's death; Penrose accuses the gathered lords of being turncloaks who changed allegiance from Robert to Renly to Stannis.
4. **Brienne debate** — Guyard Morrigen claims Brienne killed Renly. Penrose defends Brienne, saying she loved Renly. Florent suggests Catelyn Stark did it. Lord Caron cites Ser Emmon Cuy's dying testimony that it was Brienne.
5. **Penrose shames the Rainbow Guard** — Ser Cortnay mocks Morrigen and Caron for wearing Rainbow Guard cloaks while their king died, questioning their honor.
6. **Challenge to single combat** — Ser Cortnay throws his glove at Stannis and challenges him to single combat or trial by champion. Morrigen and Caron both volunteer.
7. **Stannis refuses** — Stannis refuses the challenge, citing his twenty-thousand-man advantage and threatening to hang everyone as traitors if he must storm the walls.
8. **Penrose withdraws** — Ser Cortnay rides back toward the gate, invoking the castle's name ("Storm's End") as defiance.
9. **Return to camp** — Lords discuss the situation during the ride back. Lord Estermont favors single combat. Ser Jon Fossoway and Lord Caron also support it. Stannis silences them all.
10. **Stannis summons Davos** — Stannis rides apart from the lords with only Melisandre and calls Davos to join him. The other lords note this with displeasure.
11. **Private audience** — At the pavilion, Stannis dismisses Melisandre and speaks privately with Davos. Stannis asks Davos's opinion of Penrose, discusses Florent's suggestion to use old Lord Penrose as hostage leverage, which Davos opposes.
12. **Stannis on loyalty** — Stannis reflects on the inconstancy of his lords, his own standards of justice ("a good act does not wash out the bad"), and asks Davos about smallfolk reaction to Renly's death and Cersei's incest.
13. **Patchface rumor** — Stannis angrily reveals the counter-rumor that Selyse cuckolded him with Patchface and that Shireen is not his daughter.
14. **Renly's death reflection** — Stannis confides he dreams of Renly's dying — a green tent, candles, a woman screaming, blood — and insists he was asleep when it happened. Davos senses something wrong.
15. **Renly's peach** — Stannis recounts Renly offering him a peach at their parley, saying he will go to his grave thinking of it; reveals he loved Renly.
16. **Stannis reveals the mission** — Stannis tells Davos that Ser Cortnay will be dead within the day, as Melisandre has seen it. He orders Davos to land a boat beneath the castle unseen, at night.
17. **Davos's moral struggle** — Davos protests, suggesting Penrose might yield if allowed to keep Edric. Stannis insists he must have the boy, saying Melisandre has seen it in the flames.
18. **Night crossing** — Davos sails a small boat with black sail across Shipbreaker Bay, smuggling Melisandre. He reflects on his previous smuggling run sixteen years ago bringing onions during the siege.
19. **Conversation on the water** — Melisandre and Davos discuss good and evil, shadow and light. She calls herself "a champion of light and life." Davos accuses her of killing Cressen and Renly; she denies killing Renly.
20. **Davos invokes darkness** — Davos argues the darkness protects them from the wall guards; Melisandre insists it is the fire (torches blinding the guards) that hides them.
21. **Storm's End spells** — Melisandre reveals Storm's End has ancient spells woven into its stones, "dark walls that no shadow can pass," which is why she needed to be brought inside.
22. **Cave entry** — Davos rows into the cave mouth beneath Storm's End during high tide, navigating jagged rocks. They reach the portcullis in total darkness.
23. **Shadow birth** — Melisandre removes her cloak, revealing she is naked and hugely pregnant. She gives birth to a shadow creature — black, taller than Davos, tall as the tunnel — which passes through the portcullis bars and races across the water. Davos recognizes the shadow as bearing the likeness of the man who cast it (Stannis).

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deep unease and moral discomfort throughout. During the parley, privately agrees with Penrose that the lords are turncloaks. Growing dread during the private audience as Stannis hints at shadow magic. Profound sadness when he understands what is being asked of him. Fear during the night crossing, though professional confidence in his smuggling skills persists. Horror at witnessing the shadow birth.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The moral cost of what Stannis asks him to do — he was "a knight now, no longer a smuggler, and he had never been an assassin." Struggles with loyalty to Stannis versus his own conscience. Also worried about Devan taking up R'hllor worship and distancing from his father.
- **Key decisions made:** Tells Stannis the truth about his lords' inconstancy despite the risk. Advises bypassing Storm's End for King's Landing. Protests the mission but ultimately obeys. Agrees to smuggle Melisandre under the castle.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself "It is not for me to question the king's commands" but keeps questioning them. Notes his phantom fingertips itching when Stannis discusses Renly's death — sensing something deeply wrong but choosing not to press it. Tells himself Devan wearing the flaming heart is "only to be expected" because he's the king's squire.
