# ACOK — Sansa VIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 66
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Sansa VIII
- **first_available:** ACOK Sansa VIII
- **location_primary:** Throne room, Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Sansa's chambers, godswood
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the Battle of the Blackwater; day of court ceremony rewarding victors
- **time_markers:** Ceremony lasts most of the day; "The light outside the windows was fading by the time the session drew to a close"; "Come dark" when Sansa goes to godswood; new knights held vigil "all through the night" and crossed the city "that morning"; wedding "won't be for a moon's turn yet"
- **chapter_summary:** The court holds a grand ceremony to reward those who fought in the Battle of the Blackwater. Lord Tywin is named Hand of the King, the Tyrells are honored and Joffrey agrees to wed Margaery Tyrell — setting aside his betrothal to Sansa. Hundreds of heroes are rewarded with lands, titles, and knighthoods, while captives are brought to swear fealty or face execution. A defiant knight denouncing Joffrey is killed by Ser Meryn Trant after Joffrey cuts himself on the Iron Throne. Later, in the godswood, Ser Dontos tells Sansa the escape plan is set for the night of Joffrey's wedding and gives her a silver hair net set with black amethysts from Asshai.

## Events & Actions
1. **Court assembles in throne room** — Lords, ladies, and courtiers fill the hall in their finest attire for the ceremony honoring the heroes of the Battle of the Blackwater.
2. **Tywin Lannister rides into the throne room** — He enters on his warhorse in magnificent gilded armor; his destrier drops dung at the base of the Iron Throne.
3. **Tywin named Hand of the King** — Joffrey asks his grandfather to assume governance; Lord Tywin accepts "until Your Grace does come of age." Joff fastens the Hand's chain around his neck.
4. **Tyrells honored** — Mace Tyrell, Ser Loras, and Ser Garlan enter; Joffrey places chains of golden roses on each.
5. **Ser Loras joins the Kingsguard** — Loras requests to serve; Joffrey accepts, calling him "brother."
6. **Mace Tyrell joins the royal council** — Lord Tyrell asks to serve on the council; granted.
7. **Ser Garlan proposes Margaery as bride** — Claims Margaery's marriage to Renly was unconsummated; she has come to love Joffrey from afar.
8. **Joffrey feigns reluctance about betrothal** — Claims he is promised to another and must keep his word.
9. **Cersei argues for setting Sansa aside** — Says it would be improper to wed daughter of a traitor whose brother is in rebellion.
10. **High Septon dissolves the betrothal** — Declares the Starks' crimes have freed Joffrey from any promise; no valid marriage contract exists.
11. **Joffrey sets Sansa aside and agrees to wed Margaery** — The crowd cheers "Margaery, Margaery."
12. **Heroes of the Blackwater rewarded** — Paxter Redwyne, Mathis Rowan, Randyll Tarly, Kevan Lannister, Addam Marbrand, and western lords honored.
13. **Lesser-born heroes rewarded** — Ser Philip Foote made lord (given Caron lands); Lothor Brune raised to knighthood; Josmyn Peckledon promised sword, armor, warhorse, and future knighthood; Willit given arms and his sons taken into Lannister service.
14. **Ship captains honored** — Captains of Wildwind, Prince Aemon, River Arrow and officers from other ships recognized.
15. **Hallyne the Pyromancer raised to lord** — Title only, no lands or castle.
16. **Ser Lancel awarded House Darry's lands** — Does not appear; wound may cost him arm or life.
17. **Petyr Baelish granted Harrenhal and named Lord Paramount of the Trident** — Littlefinger jokes about needing sons and grandsons.
18. **Six hundred new knights dubbed** — Held vigil in Great Sept, walked barefoot through city; only three Kingsguard available to dub them.
19. **Captives brought before the throne** — Great lords and knights from Stannis's army presented.
20. **Captives given choice: fealty or punishment** — Most swear fealty; some remain defiant.
21. **Defiant Florent bastard threatens Joffrey** — Warns "The Lord of Light protects King Stannis." Joffrey orders his execution.
22. **Knight with fiery heart surcoat denounces Joffrey** — Calls him "black worm," "abomination born of incest," declares Stannis the true king.
23. **Joffrey cuts himself on the Iron Throne** — Gestures angrily and arm brushes sharp metal; blood soaks through crimson samite; cries for his mother.
24. **Defiant knight seizes spear and declares "The throne denies him!"** — Claims Iron Throne rejected Joffrey.
25. **Ser Meryn Trant kills the defiant knight** — Drives longsword through his chest.
26. **Cersei rushes to Joffrey; maesters bundle him out** — Lord Tywin remains still; gold cloaks drag away the body.
27. **Lord Tywin takes command** — Seats himself on a step of the Iron Throne; continues the session in a clear strong voice.
28. **Sansa returns to her chambers** — Hugs pillow; squeals with joy at being free of Joffrey.
29. **Sansa goes to the godswood** — Passes Ser Osmund Kettleblack; tries to appear miserable.
30. **Dontos warns Sansa she is still in danger** — Says the queen will never let her go; Joffrey may still take her to bed.
31. **Dontos reveals escape plan** — Set for the night of Joffrey's wedding; the confusion of the feast will cover their escape.
32. **Dontos gives Sansa the silver hair net** — Describes the black amethysts from Asshai; calls it "magic," "justice," "vengeance for your father," and "home."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Nervous hope during the ceremony; intense relief and joy when the betrothal is dissolved ("curiously light-headed," "I am free"); anxiety about her family's safety when considering Littlefinger's lordship; joy in her chambers (squeals, almost kisses serving girl); hope then deflation when Dontos warns her, then renewed hope at the escape plan
- **Primary preoccupation:** Freedom from Joffrey — the dissolution of the betrothal dominates her emotional arc; followed by escape planning
- **Key decisions made:** Maintains the performance of distress as Cersei instructed; goes to meet Dontos in godswood; accepts the hair net
- **Self-deception flags:** "Robb has beaten them every time. He'll beat Lord Baelish too, if he must" — reassures herself despite anxiety; brief belief that being set aside makes her truly free before Dontos corrects her
