- **chapter_summary:** On King Joffrey's name day, Sansa attends a small tourney held in the outer bailey of the Red Keep. The jousting is poor — Ser Meryn Trant defeats Ser Hobber Redwyne, Ser Balon Swann unhorses Morros Slynt, and Ser Dontos Hollard arrives too drunk to ride. When Joffrey orders Dontos drowned in a cask of wine, Sansa intervenes by claiming it is bad luck to kill on a name day, and the Hound supports her. She persuades Joffrey to make Dontos his fool instead. After Tommen rides against a quintain, Tyrion Lannister arrives at the Red Keep with a ragged column of sellswords, freeriders, and clansmen, and proceeds to seek the queen and council.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa watches the comet** — From her tower window, Sansa observes the red comet through high clouds on the morning of Joffrey's name day.
2. **Ser Arys escorts Sansa** — Ser Arys Oakheart arrives to escort Sansa to the tourney grounds, interpreting the comet as a sign of Joffrey's glory.
3. **Sansa arrives at the royal box** — Sansa joins Joffrey, Myrcella, Tommen, and the Hound in the royal box in the outer bailey; Joffrey notes her wearing the moonstone hair net.
4. **Joffrey tells Sansa of Viserys's death** — Joffrey informs Sansa that the Dothraki killed Viserys Targaryen by crowning him with molten gold, and threatens to feed Robb to wolves.
5. **Ser Meryn defeats Ser Hobber** — Ser Meryn Trant and Ser Hobber Redwyne break lances on the first pass; on the second, Ser Meryn strikes Hobber in the chest and unhorses him. Ser Horas runs out to help his twin.
6. **Ser Balon defeats Morros Slynt** — Ser Balon Swann strikes Morros Slynt's shield; Morros falls, his foot catches in the stirrup, and his horse drags him the length of the lists.
7. **Ser Horas defeats the elderly knight** — Ser Horas Redwyne vanquishes an unnamed elderly knight whose mount bears silver griffins.
8. **Ser Dontos arrives drunk** — Ser Dontos Hollard appears without breeches, too drunk to mount his horse; he sits in the dirt and concedes.
9. **Joffrey orders Dontos drowned** — The king commands that Dontos be drowned in a cask of wine.
10. **Sansa intervenes** — Sansa blurts out that it would be ill luck to kill a man on his name day, desperately inventing the superstition.
11. **The Hound supports Sansa** — Sandor Clegane confirms the superstition: "What a man sows on his name day, he reaps throughout the year."
12. **Sansa suggests making Dontos a fool** — Sansa proposes that Dontos be dressed in motley and made a court fool rather than executed. Joffrey agrees and orders it.
13. **Dontos thanks Sansa** — Ser Dontos crawls to his knees and thanks both the king and Sansa.
14. **Joffrey ends the tourney** — Disgusted with the poor showing, Joffrey cancels the remaining bouts.
15. **Tommen rides the quintain** — Prince Tommen, in silver-and-crimson armor, charges the quintain shouting "Casterly Rock!" He strikes the shield but the padded mace knocks him from the saddle. He gets up to try again.
16. **Myrcella runs to Tommen** — Princess Myrcella scrambles from the box to check on her brother.
17. **Sansa tells Joffrey to comfort Tommen** — Sansa suggests Joffrey should help his brother up; Joffrey refuses.
18. **Tyrion arrives at the Red Keep** — A column of riders enters through the gatehouse: Lannister men-at-arms, freeriders, sellswords, and mountain clansmen, with Tyrion Lannister riding among them on a tall red horse, arm in a sling.
19. **Tommen and Myrcella greet Tyrion** — Tommen gallops to his uncle; a hairy clansman lifts Tommen from his saddle. Tyrion claps Tommen on the back and spins Myrcella in a circle.
20. **Tyrion greets Joffrey** — Tyrion kneels before Joffrey; the exchange is tense. Tyrion reminds Joffrey of Robert's death.
21. **Tyrion offers condolences to Sansa** — Tyrion tells Sansa he is sorry for her losses; Sansa is unsure if he is mocking her.
22. **Sansa recites her loyalty script** — Sansa reflexively declares her father a traitor and her loyalty to Joffrey; Tyrion calls her "a deer surrounded by wolves." Sansa whispers "Lions."
23. **Tyrion departs for the council** — Tyrion squeezes Sansa's hand, vows he will not savage her, and leaves to see the queen and council.
24. **Sansa resolves to trust no Lannister** — Sansa reflects that Tyrion speaks more gently than Joffrey but is still a Lannister and no friend. She vows never to trust as she once did.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious, fearful, occasionally brave. Sansa lives in constant dread of Joffrey's rages, knows any word could provoke punishment. She experiences flashes of courage — intervening for Dontos, telling Joffrey to comfort Tommen — that surprise even her. Beneath it all is deep grief for her father and longing for her family.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival at court. Managing Joffrey's moods, performing loyalty, hiding her true feelings. She carefully chooses words, wears the moonstone gift, curtsies — all to avoid beatings. Secondary preoccupation: grief for her father and worry for Robb and Catelyn.
- **Key decisions made:** Intervenes to save Ser Dontos by inventing a name day superstition; pivots to suggesting Dontos be made a fool when Joffrey remains unconvinced; resolves after Tyrion's gentleness to never trust a Lannister again.
- **Self-deception flags:** None — Sansa is unusually clear-eyed in this chapter. She sees through Joffrey's gallantry ("So the king had decided to play the gallant today"), recognizes her own performance ("That reflex she had learned quickly"), and explicitly rejects the possibility of trusting Tyrion despite his kindness.
