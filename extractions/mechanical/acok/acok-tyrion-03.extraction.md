- **chapter_summary:** The small council convenes to discuss Stannis Baratheon's letters accusing Cersei and Jaime of incest and declaring himself king. Tyrion and Littlefinger convince Cersei to ignore the accusations and spread a counter-rumor that Shireen is the product of Selyse's affair with the fool Patchface. Tyrion then commissions a massive chain from King's Landing's smiths, overriding Cersei's weapons orders. He travels by litter to Chataya's brothel, which serves as a cover for a secret tunnel leading to Varys, who is disguised as a common soldier. Varys provides a horse and disguise so Tyrion can secretly visit Shae, and they discuss who revealed the incest secret to Stannis.

## Events & Actions
1. **Council discusses Stannis's letters** — Cersei presents two copies of Stannis's letter accusing her and Jaime of incest; Pycelle explains they came through Castle Stokeworth and Lord Gyles; Littlefinger observes every lord in the Seven Kingdoms likely received one.
2. **Cersei demands suppression** — Cersei wants the letters burned and anyone repeating the accusations to lose their tongue; Pycelle agrees; Tyrion argues this will only lend credence to the claims.
3. **Tyrion identifies the religious language** — Tyrion notices "Done in the Light of the Lord" in the letter; Pycelle explains it as red priest usage from the Free Cities; Littlefinger notes Selyse took up with a red priest years ago.
4. **Tyrion proposes using Stannis's religious conversion against him** — Suggests urging the High Septon to denounce Stannis for abandoning the gods.
5. **Littlefinger proposes the Patchface counter-rumor** — Suggests spreading a tale that Shireen is baseborn, fathered by Patchface the fool; the smallfolk will believe the bizarre story; Cersei enthusiastically agrees.
6. **Tyrion privately assesses Littlefinger** — Calls him "a most accomplished liar" and reflects he is "more dangerous than I knew"; Littlefinger responds evenly.
7. **Tyrion hints about the chain** — Tells Cersei he is having "a little chain" made for Joffrey as a gift; she dismisses it; Tyrion says Joffrey may treasure it above all others.
8. **Tyrion ascends to the Tower of the Hand** — Bronn waits outside the council; Lady Tanda calls from the serpentine steps; Tyrion ignores her and goes up. Moon Brothers guard the door. Podrick has laid out clothing.
9. **Tyrion dresses for the audience** — Podrick helps him into the black velvet doublet with golden lion-head studs, the Hand's chain, and the crimson silk cloak.
10. **Tyrion commissions the great chain from the smiths** — Dumps three massive steel links on the floor and orders a thousand more; appoints Ironbelly to oversee; overrides Cersei's arms orders; threatens Salloreon into compliance.
11. **Tyrion departs by litter** — Rides in a curtained litter with Black Ears escort and Bronn clearing the way; observes the street crowd and tries to identify informers; reflects on his efforts to feed the city (fishing boats, opened kingswood, gold cloaks foraging).
12. **Tyrion arrives at Chataya's brothel** — Almost an hour's journey behind the hill of Rhaenys; tells Bronn it's a brothel visit; instructs Black Ears to amuse themselves at cheaper establishments.
13. **Chataya introduces her establishment** — Shows Tyrion girls through a Myrish screen; suggests her own daughter, the dark-skinned girl.
14. **Chataya describes Summer Islands customs** — Explains that in the Summer Isles, those skilled at giving pleasure are esteemed; highborn youths serve in pillow houses to honor the gods.
15. **Alayaya leads Tyrion to the turret room** — She takes him up two flights of stairs and down a long hall to the turret room with the canopied bed and wardrobe.
16. **Tyrion enters the secret tunnel** — Opens the wardrobe's sliding back panel, descends a ladder in pitch darkness, enters a slanting earthen tunnel below street level.
17. **Tyrion meets Varys in disguise** — Varys waits in the tunnel with a candle, disguised as a common soldier; they emerge through a trap door in a stable about three blocks from the brothel.
18. **Varys explains the arrangement** — The tunnel was built for a previous Hand; Chataya guards its secret; Varys knows of it through his "little birds"; the stableboys see only animals; the roughspun cloak will make Tyrion look like a child on horseback.
19. **Tyrion and Varys discuss the incest revelation** — Varys denies being the one who told Stannis; suggests someone read a book and looked at bastard hair color, as Ned Stark and Jon Arryn did; describes Robert's eight bastards as all black-haired despite their mothers' varied coloring; Cersei's three golden children made the truth plain.
20. **Tyrion rides toward Shae** — Takes the piebald gelding; he and Varys exchange mutual ambivalence about their relationship ("best friend... worst enemy").

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Sardonic amusement at Cersei's fury over true accusations; wary reassessment of Littlefinger as more dangerous than previously thought; satisfaction at the chain commission and his political maneuvering; frustrated by Cersei's counterproductive instincts (threatening smiths, wanting tongues cut); lustful at the sight of Chataya's girls; wistful and self-reproaching when remembering his first love ("What a wretched fool you are, dwarf"); anxious about secrecy regarding Shae; ambivalent toward Varys
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing King's Landing's defenses (the chain), managing the political fallout of Stannis's letter, maintaining the secret of his visits to Shae
- **Key decisions made:** Advocate ignoring Stannis's accusations rather than suppressing them; support the Patchface counter-rumor; override Cersei's weapons orders in favor of chain production; appoint Ironbelly to oversee the forging; use Chataya's brothel as cover for secret meetings via Varys's tunnel
- **Self-deception flags:** None obvious — Tyrion is self-aware about the accusations being true ("Only because you're guilty"), clear-eyed about Littlefinger's danger, and honest with himself about his own motives. His dismissal of Cersei as short-sighted may underestimate her political instincts in some areas.
