# ACOK — Theon IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 51
- **pov_character:** Theon Greyjoy
- **pov_chapter_number:** Theon IV
- **first_available:** ACOK Theon IV
- **location_primary:** Winterfell
- **location_secondary:** Wolfswood, Hunter's Gate, Acorn Water (mill mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Theon's seizure of Winterfell; waiting for Asha's reinforcements from Deepwood Motte
- **time_markers:** Middle of the night (woken from sleep); half-moon above Bell Tower; dawn approaching ("sky was greying over the castle walls"); first pale rays of sun at departure; midday when Theon gives up upstream search; dusk/last light fading when hunt abandoned
- **chapter_summary:** Theon wakes in the night to realize the direwolves have gone silent. He discovers Bran, Rickon, Hodor, Osha, and the two Reeds have escaped through the Hunter's Gate, killing two ironborn guards. Theon organizes a hunting party with dogs and pursues the trail into the wolfswood, but the fugitives used the direwolves as a decoy — sending them into a stream while the humans went another direction. The trail goes cold. At nightfall Reek suggests the Starks are hiding at a mill on the Acorn Water, producing a wolf's-head brooch as evidence, and Theon abandons his promise of mercy.

## Events & Actions
1. **Theon wakes in the night** — Something disturbs him; he realizes the direwolves have stopped howling.
2. **Guards dispatched** — Theon sends Urzen to check on wolves, Wex to check on Bran and Rickon.
3. **Discovery of escape** — Wex returns shaking his head; both boys' chambers empty.
4. **Castle roused** — Theon orders everyone driven into the yard for a headcount.
5. **Missing persons identified** — Six missing: Bran, Rickon, Jojen, Meera, Hodor, Osha.
6. **Hunter's Gate investigated** — Squint found eviscerated in moat (killed by direwolves); Drennan found with throat cut in gatehouse (killed by Osha with cheese knife). Drawbridge lowered.
7. **Theon reconstructs the escape** — Woman (Osha) distracted Drennan, killed him, lowered drawbridge; others freed wolves; Squint killed on wallwalk and pushed through crenel.
8. **Theon confronts castle folk** — Demands information; threatens; no one cooperates. Crowd hostile and silent.
9. **Reek suggests flaying** — Theon publicly refuses, declaring no flaying while he rules Winterfell.
10. **Hunting party assembled** — Eleven men, two boys, a dozen dogs depart at dawn through Hunter's Gate.
11. **Trail followed north-northwest** — Pawprints, Hodor's tread, Reed footprints visible initially; dogs pick up scent.
12. **Theon and Luwin discuss mercy** — Luwin argues for sparing the Reeds (leverage over Lord Howland) and Hodor; Theon agrees but promises death for Osha.
13. **Elk carcass found** — Wolves killed it but humans didn't butcher it; first sign something is wrong.
14. **Trail lost at swollen brook** — Dogs lose scent in water; Wex and Joseth notice no human footprints, only wolf pawprints. The wolves entered the water alone.
15. **Realization of decoy** — Osha split from the wolves earlier; sent direwolves as false trail.
16. **Search parties split** — Gariss and Murch double back; Theon follows upstream. Neither finds the trail.
17. **Day-long fruitless search** — All parties reunite with nothing; Walder Frey voices fear of crannogmen; Luwin mentions their knowledge from the children of the forest.
18. **Reek suggests the mill on Acorn Water** — Claims the Starks are hiding there; produces wolf's-head brooch from his sack as evidence.
19. **Theon selects a small party** — Takes Gelmarr, Aggar, Rednose, and Reek; sends rest back to Winterfell with dogs.
20. **Theon abandons mercy** — Tells Maester Luwin "Mercy was for this morning."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious, frustrated, increasingly desperate; oscillates between bravado and fear of humiliation; tastes bile; stomach described as "nest of snakes"
- **Primary preoccupation:** Recapturing the Stark boys to maintain credibility and hold on Winterfell; fear of looking foolish to father, Asha, and the north
- **Key decisions made:** Organizes immediate pursuit at dawn rather than night; refuses flaying; threatens Palla to coerce Farlen; agrees to spare Reeds and Hodor; abandons mercy at chapter's end; trusts Reek's suggestion about the mill
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes he has used the castle folk "gently" despite executing Mikken, Benfred, and Chayle; frames the rapes as something he punished rather than enabled; tells himself he is being just and protective; rationalizes that mercy is a "trap" rather than confronting his cruelty; fantasizes about marrying Sansa as "tightening his grip"
