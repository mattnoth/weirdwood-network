- **chapter_summary:** Edmure Tully rides out from Riverrun with his forces to defend the Red Fork fords against Lord Tywin's army. Catelyn prays in the sept, receives a letter announcing Ser Cortnay Penrose's death and Storm's End's surrender to Stannis, and witnesses two repelled Lannister attacks on the nearest ford. She interrogates Ser Cleos Frey about his peace terms and Sansa's welfare. Days later, news arrives that Edmure has won a decisive victory at Stone Mill, throwing back Ser Gregor Clegane and forcing Lord Tywin to march southeast.

## Events & Actions
1. **Edmure departs Riverrun** — Edmure Tully leads his men out with trumpets, drums, lances raised and banners streaming; drawbridge descends
2. **Catelyn prays in the sept** — She kneels before the Warrior, lights candles for Edmure and Robb; attends the septon's celebration
3. **Rymund sings of Lord Deremond** — In the brewhouse yard, he performs the ballad of Lord Deremond at the Bloody Meadow
4. **Catelyn and Brienne discuss fighting and motherhood** — Brienne says fighting is better than waiting; Catelyn speaks of children as a different battle; Brienne reveals she never knew her mother
5. **Maester Vyman delivers Lord Meadows' letter** — Storm's End has surrendered to Stannis; Ser Cortnay Penrose is dead; Catelyn orders a raven sent to Robb via Ashemark
6. **Catelyn and Brienne discuss Edric Storm** — They reason that Stannis wants to parade Robert's bastard to undermine Joffrey's legitimacy
7. **Catelyn reflects on Jon Snow's parentage** — She thinks of Ashara Dayne and Ned's secret; concludes it no longer matters
8. **Catelyn reflects on Roose Bolton's letter** — Bolton is marching on Harrenhal; he dismisses his bastard Ramsay's death coldly
9. **First Lannister attack on the ford (daytime)** — Approximately fifty riders under a purple unicorn banner (House Brax) attempt to cross; Lord Jason Mallister's men repel them; at least one dead man swept downstream
10. **Second Lannister attack (after midnight)** — Night assault repelled; fire arrows illuminate the battle; hidden caltrops and bowmen devastate attackers; one burning man swept past Riverrun
11. **Brienne's tactical assessment** — She tells Catelyn this was just Lord Tywin's probing, predicting a massed assault will come
12. **Catelyn interrogates Ser Cleos Frey** — He reports Tyrion's peace terms (exchange Sansa and Arya for the Kingslayer); confirms Sansa appeared wan; was not present with Arya; Cersei was absent from court
13. **Catelyn recalls the catspaw's dagger and Tyrion's denials** — She reasserts Tyrion's guilt despite his past defense of her against clansmen
14. **Late supper in the Great Hall** — Rymund sings of Oxcross; garrison howls along; Brienne speaks of Evenfall Hall's singer and Renly's fool
15. **Rider reports Ser Flement Brax repelled** — Lord Jason's men drove back a second crossing attempt six leagues south; Lord Karyl Vance also held upstream
16. **Stone Mill victory** — Edmure's messenger arrives days later; Ser Gregor Clegane's assault broken; Lord Lefford drowned, Strongboar captured, Ser Addam Marbrand retreated thrice; Clegane staggered back wounded; Lord Tywin marching southeast
17. **Celebration at Riverrun** — Casks opened; smallfolk cheer "Riverrun!" and "Tully!"; Rymund plays harp with drummers and piper
18. **Catelyn studies maps, uneasy** — She traces Lord Tywin's likely route toward the Blackwater Rush headwaters; despite victories, she feels afraid

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious, burdened by duty, envious of those who can fight rather than wait; grief for Ned; guilt over Sansa; growing unease despite victories; isolated ("there is only me... I know nothing")
- **Primary preoccupation:** The safety of her children (Robb, Sansa, Arya); whether the fords will hold; the mystery of what duty requires of her; the shadow she saw in Renly's pavilion still haunts her dreams
- **Key decisions made:** Orders Maester Vyman to send word to Robb about Storm's End; orders wine sent to Ser Cleos to loosen his tongue; allows celebration after Stone Mill
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn reasserts Tyrion's guilt ("He lied... The killer was armed with his own knife") despite acknowledging Tyrion defended her against clansmen and had no part in Ned's murder — she cannot reconcile these facts; she questions whether she "always did her duty" while simultaneously claiming duty as her defining trait
