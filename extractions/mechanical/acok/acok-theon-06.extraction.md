# ACOK — Theon VI

## Chapter Metadata

- **chapter_number:** 67
- **pov_character:** Theon Greyjoy

- **first_available:** ACOK Theon VI
- **location_primary:** Winterfell
- **location_secondary:** Market square outside Winterfell's gates
- **approximate_timeline:** Late in ACOK; autumn; the northern host has assembled to retake Winterfell
- **time_markers:** "another sleepless night"; "before nightfall" (the northmen will arrive); "by sunset" / "evenfall" (deadline for dispersal); "first light" / "every dawn and every dusk" (threat cadence for hostage executions); "the sun was low in the west" (during Bolton attack); "blue dusk, with the evening stars" (crows arrive after battle); "as the night grew darker" (sack of Winterfell); the shadow of the broken tower lengthens through the afternoon as Theon practices archery
- **chapter_summary:** Maester Luwin counsels Theon to yield Winterfell, but Theon refuses. He assembles his garrison and offers them the choice to leave; only seventeen men remain. Theon parleys with Ser Rodrik Cassel outside the gates and uses Beth Cassel as a hostage to forestall attack. Maester Luwin later suggests Theon take the black, and Theon briefly considers it. Before he can act, Bolton forces arrive and attack Ser Rodrik's army from behind, destroying it. The commander reveals himself as Ramsay Bolton, who had been disguised as Reek. Theon opens the gates, but Ramsay strikes him down and sacks Winterfell, killing the ironborn and setting the castle ablaze.

## Events & Actions
1. **Maester Luwin counsels surrender** — Luwin tells Theon he must yield when scouts are spotted outside the walls; Theon refuses and demands more ravens be sent to Pyke and his uncle.
2. **Theon sends Luwin away** — After arguing, Theon orders Luwin to send birds and fetch Black Lorren and Wex; demands his mail scoured and garrison assembled.
3. **Garrison assembly and the line in the dirt** — Theon addresses his pitifully small force in the yard, tells them the northmen are coming, and draws a line in the dirt with his longsword, offering them the chance to leave.
4. **Seventeen stay** — Wex crosses first, then Black Lorren, then Red Rolfe, Kromm, Werlag, Tymor and his brothers, Ulf the Ill, Harrag Sheepstealer, four Harlaws, two Botleys, and Kenned the Whale. The rest, including Urzen, Stygg, and all ten of Asha's men from Deepwood Motte, depart.
5. **Black Lorren warns about castle folk** — Lorren says the castle folk will turn on them once fighting begins; he suggests putting them all out. Theon refuses, asks about the noose.
6. **Black Lorren offers to hold the drawbridge alone** — Lorren says he'll stand on the drawbridge with his axe and let them come; Theon recognizes he wants a death worthy of a song and overrules him.
7. **Theon arms for battle** — Wex helps him don black surcoat, golden mantle, ringmail, and boiled leather.
8. **Theon surveys the enemy** — From the watchtower, he counts at least a thousand men (perhaps twice that), with catapults and scorpions. Using Maester Luwin's Myrish lens, he identifies banners: Cerwyn, Tallhart, Manderly, Flint, Karstark, Hornwood — but no Glovers, Boltons, or Umbers.
9. **Cley Cerwyn requests parley** — The young lord appears with a peace banner; announces Ser Rodrik wishes to parley with "Theon Turncloak."
10. **Theon rides out to parley** — Wearing his crown, he rides Smiler through the gatehouse, over the drawbridge, and out to the market square. He notes a woman at the well and Gage the cook watching.
11. **Parley with Ser Rodrik** — Ser Rodrik demands Winterfell and Theon's life, offering to let those who murdered no children walk free. Theon counter-offers: swear fealty to Balon Greyjoy or be destroyed. Ser Rodrik claims near two thousand men to Theon's fifty (Theon knows it is seventeen).
12. **Beth Cassel displayed** — Theon raises his fist as signal; Black Lorren displays Beth noosed on the walls between the tarred heads. Ser Rodrik calls it craven and despicable.
13. **Theon draws parallel to his own hostage-taking** — He compares Beth's situation to his own captivity as a boy of ten in Winterfell.
14. **Ser Rodrik offers himself as hostage** — He asks Theon to take him instead of Beth; Theon refuses, saying the castellan is worth less to Manderly and Tallhart than the girl.
15. **Theon threatens systematic executions** — If the host remains at sunset, Beth hangs; then another hostage at first light, another at sunset, continuing until they leave.
16. **Theon rides back** — He gallops back to the castle, conscious of archers at his back. He passes between the tarred heads and Beth, noosed and crying.
17. **Theon drinks and broods** — In Ned Stark's bedchamber by the fire, he drinks sour red wine and contemplates the hopelessness of his position.
18. **Theon practices archery** — In the old inner ward, he looses arrows until his shoulders ache and fingers bleed. Women come to the well but are driven away by whatever they see on his face.
19. **Maester Luwin suggests taking the black** — He approaches Theon again and proposes joining the Night's Watch; Ser Rodrik would honor such a request.
20. **Theon considers the proposal** — He imagines rising to chief of rangers or Lord Commander, commanding a ship at Eastwatch, hunting beyond the Wall, taking wildling women.
21. **Bolton forces attack** — Kromm interrupts with news: more men arrived and have fallen on Ser Rodrik's forces. Theon briefly hopes it is Asha.
22. **Battle in the market square** — From the battlements, Theon watches the Dreadfort men, displaying the flayed man banner, surprise and destroy Ser Rodrik's larger force through superior leadership and the element of surprise. The inn burns.
23. **Aftermath** — Dead men and dying horses in the square; a wounded man dies trying to reach the well; crows arrive at dusk.
24. **Ramsay presents the corpses** — Red Helm rides up with mounted men and dumps three corpses: Ser Rodrik (left arm lost at elbow), Leobald Tallhart, and Cley Cerwyn (arrow in the eye). Maester Luwin cries out and falls to his knees sick.
25. **Theon opens the gates** — Believing himself saved, he orders the gates opened. He feels strangely empty.
26. **Ramsay reveals his identity** — Removing his helm, the man Theon knew as Reek reveals himself as Ramsay Bolton/Snow. He explains the clothes-swap deception: he dressed the real Reek in his own finery and sent him to the Dreadfort; the real Reek was killed by an arrow.
27. **Ramsay explains the girl** — He describes a girl who ran; his horse lamed in pursuit; he gave his horse, clothes, ring, and sable cloak to the real Reek and disguised himself in Reek's rags.
28. **Ramsay demands Kyra** — He rejects Palla (the promised kennel girl) as smelling of dogshit and demands Theon's bedwarmer Kyra instead.
29. **Ramsay strikes Theon** — When Theon objects, Ramsay backhands him with a lobstered steel gauntlet, shattering his cheekbone.
30. **Sack of Winterfell** — The Dreadfort men pour in and kill the ironborn. Red Rolfe and Kenned are cut down. Black Lorren fights four men with his sword out. Ulf is killed by crossbow bolt. Maester Luwin is speared between the shoulders by a mounted knight and ridden over. Ramsay orders: "Save me the Freys, and burn the rest. Burn it, burn it all."
31. **Stables set ablaze** — A man lofts a torch onto the thatched stable roof. Smiler kicks free with his mane ablaze, screaming.
32. **Theon's last sight** — Lying on the ground with a mouthful of blood, Theon sees Smiler burning — the last thing described before the chapter ends.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Desperation and isolation dominate — Theon feels forsaken by his father, uncle, sister, and Reek. Raw nerves from sleepless nights. Anger at Maester Luwin. Brief bravado during the parley with Ser Rodrik, but underlying fear. A moment of genuine self-awareness when he describes his own hostage experience ("he had never quite realized that until now"). Misery and hopelessness while brooding by the fire and shooting at the butts. A brief flicker of hope and even enthusiasm when contemplating taking the black. Strange emptiness rather than elation when the Bolton forces appear to rescue him. Horror and incomprehension during the sack.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival — every calculation centers on how to avoid death. Who has forsaken him, what leverage he has, what escape routes remain. Secondary preoccupation: dignity and identity ("I am ironborn," "Prince of Winterfell").
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Refuses to yield despite Luwin's counsel. (2) Gives his men the free choice to leave. (3) Uses Beth Cassel as hostage rather than fighting or fleeing. (4) Rejects Ser Rodrik's offer to trade himself for Beth. (5) Nearly decides to take the black. (6) Opens the gates to Ramsay's forces.
- **Self-deception flags:** "I am saved" — Theon takes Ramsay's arrival as deliverance despite immediately feeling empty and uneasy. "To think how close I came to yielding, and taking the black" — he frames his worst decision as a near-miss of weakness. He does not question why Bolton forces appeared or what their true intentions are. His fantasy of rising to Lord Commander of the Night's Watch is grandiose self-delusion. He tells himself his shivering is from cold, "nothing more" — then immediately contradicts this with "even brave men shiver."
