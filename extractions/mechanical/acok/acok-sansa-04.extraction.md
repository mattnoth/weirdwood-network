- **time_markers:** Night (meeting with Dontos); "two nights ago during the black of the moon" (vanguard arrival); fires burn "nightly"; pale morning light when Sansa wakes; breaking fast with Cersei (morning)
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa meets Ser Dontos in the godswood at night to discuss escape plans; he tells her a friend will hire a ship but the time is not yet right. She climbs to the roof of Maegor's Holdfast and encounters Sandor Clegane, who delivers a brutal speech about killing and the falseness of knighthood. That night she dreams of the riot. She wakes to discover she has had her first flowering, panics, and tries to burn the bloodied bedding. She is brought before Queen Cersei, who discusses womanhood, Joffrey's nature, and advises that love is poison.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa meets Dontos in the godswood at night** — They discuss Stannis's approach, the fires burning across the Blackwater, and escape plans.
2. **Dontos reveals a friend will hire a ship** — He says a "certain man" will arrange transport when the time is right, but the city gates and river are closed.
3. **Dontos reports Stannis burned the godswood at Storm's End** — The red priestess made him do it; Stannis has vowed to burn the Great Sept of Baelor.
4. **Dontos grows weepy and tries to kiss Sansa** — She dodges his lips and kisses his cheek instead, bids him good night.
5. **Sansa crosses the unguarded drawbridge to Maegor's Holdfast** — Gold cloaks moved to city walls; Kingsguard have other duties.
6. **Sansa cannot bear to enter her room and climbs to the roof** — From the roof she sees the city's war preparations: fires, soldiers, catapults, hoardings.
7. **Sandor Clegane startles Sansa on the roof** — He grabs her arm to steady her; references her crippled brother.
8. **The Hound and Sansa converse about the riot** — She thanks him; he rejects her courtesy and mocks true knights.
9. **Clegane delivers his philosophy of violence** — Draws his sword, lays it against her neck, says killing is the sweetest thing; says there are no true knights or gods.
10. **Sansa flees the roof** — Afraid of Clegane yet wishing Dontos had his ferocity; affirms to herself that gods and true knights exist.
11. **Sansa has a nightmare of the riot** — The mob drags her from her horse; she calls for heroes but none come; a knife tears her belly.
12. **Sansa wakes to discover her first flowering** — Finds blood on thighs and sheets; panics.
13. **Sansa attempts to destroy the evidence** — Cuts the stain from the sheet, stuffs it and the featherbed into the fireplace, drenches in lamp oil and lights it.
14. **Maidservants discover her and pull her from the fire** — Three women restrain her; it's all for nothing as bleeding continues.
15. **Sansa is bathed and dressed** — Given a green wool shift; told she must go to the queen.
16. **Cersei receives Sansa and discusses her flowering** — Explains it means Sansa can now wed and bear Joffrey's children.
17. **Cersei shares personal history** — Recounts Joffrey's difficult birth, Robert's absence for hunting, Jaime insisting on being present.
18. **Cersei discusses Joffrey's cruelty toward Sansa** — Blames Arya for shaming him on the Trident; says Sansa is stronger than she seems.
19. **Cersei advises Sansa that love is poison** — Says Robert and Tyrion both have the disease of wanting to be loved.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fearful (twisting in tummy, worse every day); ashamed; desperate to escape; conflicted about Dontos as savior; terrified of Clegane yet admiring his ferocity; horrified and panicked upon discovering her flowering; resigned by end
- **Primary preoccupation:** Escape from King's Landing before Stannis's attack; later, terror that her flowering means marriage to Joffrey
- **Key decisions made:** Tries to destroy evidence of her flowering (burns bedding); ultimately resigned to facing Cersei
- **Self-deception flags:** "I love His Grace with all my heart" (conscious lie to Cersei, which Cersei sees through); after fleeing Clegane: "There are gods... and there are true knights too. All the stories can't be lies" (desperately clinging to belief)
