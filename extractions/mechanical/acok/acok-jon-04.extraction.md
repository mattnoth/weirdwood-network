That night, Ghost leads Jon down the hill to a buried bundle containing dragonglass weapons — knives, spearheads, arrowheads — and an old warhorn, all wrapped in a black cloak of the Night's Watch.

## Events & Actions
1. **Night's Watch arrives at the Fist of the First Men** — Jon rides to the summit with Mormont and officers. Ghost refuses to follow and runs off three times during the climb.
2. **Mormont declares camp** — The Old Bear proclaims the Fist good ground and orders camp established to await Qhorin Halfhand.
3. **Camp construction** — Brothers raise black tents, tether horses, cut firewood, dig latrines, and stake every opening in the ringwall before dark.
4. **Jon descends to find Ghost** — After setting up Mormont's tent, Jon goes below the trees. Ghost appears silently beside him.
5. **Ghost refuses to enter the ringfort** — The direwolf sniffs the gap in the stones and retreats; Jon cannot physically force him inside. Jon lets him go hunt.
6. **Jon surveys from the ringwall** — At dusk, Jon climbs the wall and observes the Milkwater, the forest, the Frostfangs, and the surrounding landscape. He feels foreboding.
7. **Jon and Sam talk** — Sam finds Jon on the wall. They discuss the Fist's history and Mormont's plan to wait. Jon tells Sam to ready a raven.
8. **Jon prepares hot spiced wine** — Jon builds a fire and carefully prepares Mormont's particular recipe of spiced wine for the officers' meeting.
9. **Officers' strategy meeting** — Buckwell, Locke, Wythers, Smallwood, and Mormont debate routes into the Frostfangs. Mormont decides to hold the Fist and strengthen defenses rather than advance.
10. **Mormont restricts rangers** — Rangers confined to east side of the Milkwater until Halfhand arrives. Smallwood objects but complies.
11. **Jon and Mormont discuss Benjen** — Mormont explains that if Benjen is alive, he'll find the ranging party's fires. He also acknowledges Benjen might return as a wight.
12. **Jon joins the campfire** — Jon hears Dywen claim he smells "cold." Jon remembers the smell from the wight attack and loses his appetite, giving his stew to Grenn.
13. **Jon reheats wine; night deepens** — Jon tends his fire. Frost expected. First watch takes stations. Torches flicker along the ringwall.
14. **Wolves howl; Ghost returns** — Distant wolf howling heard. Ghost appears inside the camp (having entered on his own). Ghost is restless, not after food.
15. **Ghost leads Jon down the hill** — Jon lies to the guard about fetching water and follows Ghost down the slope with a torch.
16. **Jon chases Ghost through the forest** — Ghost races through the trees; Jon pursues, losing and finding him repeatedly, circling a quarter way around the Fist.
17. **Ghost digs up a buried bundle** — Behind a fallen tree, Ghost furiously digs in a mound of soft earth.
18. **Jon uncovers dragonglass cache** — Two feet down, Jon finds a rounded bundle wrapped in cloth. Inside: obsidian knives, spearheads, arrowheads, a dagger blade. Beneath these, an old warhorn of auroch's horn banded in bronze.
19. **Jon identifies the wrapping as a Night's Watch cloak** — The cloth is good black wool, double weave, damp but not rotted — recently buried. Jon recognizes it as a brother's black cloak.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Growing foreboding and unease throughout the chapter; starts practical (noting water concerns), shifts to a pervasive dread about the haunted forest; alarmed when Ghost returns restless at night; driven by instinct to follow Ghost despite knowing it's reckless
- **Primary preoccupation:** The wrongness of this place — Ghost's refusal to enter, Dywen's smell of "cold," the feeling that something is concealed in the forest; also his uncle Benjen's fate
- **Key decisions made:** Follows Ghost down the hill at night despite knowing it's dangerous ("What am I doing?"); lies to the guard to get past the perimeter; excavates the buried bundle
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself "Stop acting the boy" while atop the ringwall, trying to dismiss his own instincts; tells himself "They ought to be safe here" despite feeling otherwise
