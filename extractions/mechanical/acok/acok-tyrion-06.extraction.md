# ACOK — Tyrion VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 26
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion VI
- **first_available:** ACOK Tyrion VI
- **location_primary:** King's Landing — the Red Keep
- **location_secondary:** Cersei's bedchamber, the throne room (Iron Throne), a deserted colonnade, the barracks, the Small Hall, the Tower of the Hand, Grand Maester Pycelle's quarters, the black cells
- **approximate_timeline:** After Stannis sails from Dragonstone, while the great chain is being forged on the Street of Steel; Tyrek Lannister married Lady Ermesande three days prior
- **time_markers:** "this night" (opening scene in Cersei's bedchamber); "the next morning as he broke his fast" (throne room scene); "that very afternoon" (Ser Cleos departs); "That night" (feast with Stone Crows); "By midnight" (raid on Pycelle's quarters); "once a fortnight" (Shagga's bathing joke); "three days past" (Tyrek's wedding to Lady Ermesande); "Black" moon / "traitor's moon"; "Every day, every night, hammers rang along the Street of Steel"
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion visits Cersei's bedchamber at night to inform her that Stannis has sailed to besiege Storm's End rather than King's Landing, and secretly drugs her wine with a laxative powder. The next morning, with Cersei incapacitated, Tyrion sits the Iron Throne and delivers counter-terms to Robb Stark's envoy Ser Cleos Frey, publicly announces Myrcella's betrothal to Trystane Martell, and orders all of Cersei's Lannister guardsmen to escort Cleos back to Riverrun — concealing four infiltrators among them to attempt Jaime's rescue. Ser Alliser Thorne reports the dead rising at the Wall, but Tyrion mocks him publicly while still granting him men from the city dungeons. At midnight, Tyrion leads Shagga and Timett to arrest Grand Maester Pycelle, who confesses to betraying Tyrion's letters to Cersei, advising Aerys to open King's Landing's gates to Tywin during Robert's Rebellion, and allowing Jon Arryn to die by sending away his maester.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion visits Cersei's bedchamber at night** — finds Lancel singing for her with a harpist and piper accompanying; Ser Meryn Trant guards the door
2. **Cersei dismisses the musicians and Lancel** — kisses Lancel on the cheek; tells Tyrion he's "harmless when he's alone"
3. **Tyrion reveals Stannis's movements** — Stannis has sailed from Dragonstone to besiege Storm's End, not King's Landing; Renly rides to meet him
4. **Tyrion and Cersei share rare moment of sibling camaraderie** — laugh together; Cersei pulls him off the bed and whirls him around, even hugs him
5. **Tyrion drugs Cersei's wine** — sprinkles fine powder into her cup of sweet Arbor red while his back is turned; she drinks it unsuspecting
6. **Next morning: Cersei is incapacitated** — confined to her privy; Tyrion takes the throne in her place
7. **Tyrion sits the Iron Throne and holds court** — Lannister guardsmen and gold cloaks line the hall; Bronn and Ser Preston flank the throne steps
8. **Counter-terms delivered to Ser Cleos Frey** — Robb must lay down his sword, swear fealty, return to Winterfell, free Jaime, send bannermen's sons as hostages; prisoner exchange proposed (Harrion Karstark and Ser Wylis Manderly for Willem Lannister; Lord Cerwyn and Ser Donnel Locke for Tion Frey); Eddard's bones to be returned; Ice withheld until peace is made; Sansa and Arya kept as hostages
9. **Myrcella-Trystane betrothal announced publicly** — Prince Doran has consented to wed his son Trystane to Princess Myrcella; causes murmurs of delight and consternation
10. **Tyrion orders Vylarr to take all Lannister guardsmen to escort Cleos** — all one hundred red cloaks, stripping Cersei of her personal guard; Pycelle protests on Tywin's behalf
11. **Ser Alliser Thorne addresses the court** — reports two dead rangers rose again at the Wall; one killed Ser Jaremy Rykker, another tried to murder the Lord Commander; brought Jared's severed hand as proof, but it rotted to bones while he waited
12. **Tyrion publicly mocks Alliser** — offers him a hundred spades; tells him to bury the dead; court laughs; but also gives him men from the city dungeons and spreads word of "bread and turnips on the Wall"
13. **Alliser confronts Tyrion at the throne's base** — insists the dead walk; calls Tyrion a fool; Ser Preston blocks him; Bronn escorts him out
14. **Varys praises Tyrion's multi-layered moves** — appeasing Stark, stripping Cersei of guards, giving the Watch men while appearing to mock
15. **Littlefinger confronts Tyrion** — angry about the Myrcella deception; if Myrcella weds Trystane, she cannot wed Robert Arryn as Tyrion promised him; warns Tyrion not to lie to him again
16. **Tyrion enlists Varys** — asks Varys to convince Cersei the guard removal is part of a scheme to free Jaime; reveals four infiltrators (thief, poisoner, mummer, murderer) are hidden among the hundred guardsmen to enter Riverrun and attempt Jaime's rescue
17. **Ser Cleos departs that afternoon** — escorted by Vylarr and one hundred red cloaks; Stark's men join them at the King's Gate
18. **Tyrion summons Timett** — finds him dicing with Burned Men in the barracks; tells him to come to his solar at midnight
19. **Tyrion feasts with Stone Crows and Moon Brothers** — shuns wine; tells Shagga it's a "traitor's moon" (black moon) and to keep his axe sharp
20. **Midnight raid on Pycelle's quarters** — Shagga breaks the door with his boot and axe; Timett follows; they find Pycelle naked in bed with a serving girl
21. **Serving girl sent away** — Shagga wants her; Tyrion overrules; Timett drags her out
22. **Tyrion confronts Pycelle about betraying his letter to Cersei** — reveals the three-way trap: told Varys about fostering Tommen, told Littlefinger about wedding Myrcella to Robert Arryn, told only Pycelle about the Dornish offer; Cersei learned about the Dornish offer, proving Pycelle was the leak
23. **Shagga shaves off Pycelle's beard** — hacks off three-quarters with one slash; continues scraping the rest under Tyrion's direction as intimidation
24. **Pycelle confesses to long service to House Lannister** — claims he advised Aerys to open King's Landing's gates to Tywin during the Sack; had hoped Tywin would become king but Robert was too strong and Stark moved too fast
25. **Pycelle confesses about Jon Arryn** — says Jon Arryn "knew" about Cersei's secret; Pycelle sent away Maester Colemon (who was purging Jon Arryn) and let him die; claims the queen needed Arryn dead though she never said so explicitly; blames the actual poisoning on Hugh, Jon Arryn's squire
26. **Pycelle confesses about Robert** — implies Robert's boar wounds could have been treated, but Pycelle let him die; also reveals Renly was plotting to bring the "Highgarden maid" to court to replace Cersei
27. **Pycelle arrested and thrown in the black cells** — dragged out still protesting loyalty to House Lannister
28. **Tyrion searches Pycelle's quarters** — collects small jars from his shelves; notes he needs someone to tend the ravens until the Citadel sends a replacement

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Triumphant and pleased — enjoying his power as Hand, the news of Stannis vs. Renly, outsmarting Cersei, and unmasking Pycelle. Moments of rare warmth with Cersei during their shared laughter. Flashes of genuine unease when Alliser mentions the dead (remembering the Wall). Disgust at Pycelle's confessions. Underlying loneliness (wishes Shae could see him on the throne).
- **Primary preoccupation:** Consolidating power — removing Cersei's guards, neutralizing Pycelle, managing the small council, preparing defenses (the chain), setting up Jaime's rescue attempt. Juggling multiple simultaneous schemes.
- **Key decisions made:** Drug Cersei's wine to incapacitate her; sit the Iron Throne himself; publicly announce the Myrcella-Trystane betrothal; strip Cersei of all Lannister guardsmen; mock Alliser publicly but give him men; arrest and imprison Pycelle; use Varys to manage Cersei's reaction.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion tells himself the begging brothers "were not important enough to battle over" — rationalizing Cersei's extralegal arrests. He mocks Alliser and the wight report despite genuinely remembering his own unease atop the Wall — performing dismissal he doesn't fully feel. He reflects at the end that Varys and Littlefinger are "more subtle, and thus more dangerous" but continues to rely on Varys as his primary instrument.
