## Chapter Metadata

- **approximate_timeline:** Early in Tyrion's tenure as Hand; shortly after he has taken up residence in the Tower of the Hand
- **time_markers:** Evening (supper completed during the chapter); nighttime (the Red Keep is "dark and still" when Tyrion leaves); "the morning tide" (when Summer's Dream sails); "three days hence" (when the White Hart master plans to flee); "the night after next" (Redwyne escape plan)
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion hosts Janos Slynt for dinner in the Small Hall, plying him with wine while extracting information about the killing of Robert's bastard infant. After confirming Slynt's role in Ned Stark's execution and the brothel murder, Tyrion informs Slynt he is being sent to the Night's Watch aboard the Summer's Dream. Ser Jacelyn Bywater arrives as the new Commander of the City Watch to escort Slynt away. Tyrion then meets with Varys, who provides intelligence on several threats and plots; they discuss the nature of power. Tyrion ends the night with Bronn in his solar, learning of Timett's killing and Bronn's recruiting progress.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion plies Janos Slynt with wine and rich food** — A deliberate manipulation over supper in the Small Hall to extract information and set up Slynt's dismissal.
2. **Tyrion raises the topic of Slynt's replacement as City Watch Commander** — Slynt recommends Allar Deem; Tyrion mentions Ser Jacelyn Bywater, whom Slynt disparages.
3. **Tyrion probes about the brothel killing** — Slynt reveals Allar Deem killed a mother and her infant at a brothel on orders; refuses to name who gave the order.
4. **Tyrion confronts Slynt about Ned Stark's execution** — Using the spear clasp as a prop, Tyrion accuses Slynt of driving the spear into Stark's back (metaphorically — betrayal). Slynt grows defensive.
5. **Tyrion intimidates Slynt** — Invokes House Lannister and his father's authority; makes Slynt fear for his children.
6. **Tyrion pronounces Slynt's fate** — Slynt's younger sons will be fostered as squires; eldest inherits the title "Lord Slynt" with lands (not Harrenhal); Slynt himself sails to the Wall on the Summer's Dream at morning tide.
7. **Slynt blusters and threatens** — Claims friends in Joffrey, Littlefinger, and the queen; threatens Tyrion will be the one sent to the Wall.
8. **Ser Jacelyn Bywater appears at the door with six gold cloaks** — Reveals himself as new Commander of the City Watch; escorts Slynt to a litter bound for the docks.
9. **Tyrion gives Bywater a list of six men to join Slynt on the ship** — Quietly instructs that Allar Deem should be "swept overboard" before reaching Eastwatch.
10. **Varys arrives after Slynt's departure** — Compliments Tyrion on the handling of Slynt.
11. **Tyrion confirms Cersei ordered the brothel killing** — Slynt's refusal to name his patron confirms it; Varys admits he knew all along.
12. **Varys reveals he saved an older bastard boy of Robert's** — Confesses he never thought the infant girl would be at risk.
13. **Tyrion and Varys discuss the nature of power** — Varys poses his riddle of the king, priest, and rich man; concludes "power resides where men believe it resides" and "a very small man can cast a very large shadow."
14. **Varys reports intelligence** — The master of the White Hart plots to defect to Stannis (trial recommended); Ser Horas and Ser Hobber Redwyne plan to escape via the Moonrunner (to be stopped); Timett killed a cheating wineseller's son on the Street of Silver; a plague of holy men preach doom because of the comet; Ser Balon Swann made a jest about "three cups" for the king at Lady Tanda's supper.
15. **Tyrion sits alone contemplating Cersei's reaction** — Considers his forces: City Watch, clansmen, sellswords, and that Vylarr's red cloaks won't act against him. Thinks of Eddard Stark's fate.
16. **Tyrion meets Bronn in his solar** — Discusses Slynt's departure, Timett's killing, and Bronn's recruiting (three new men). Asks Bronn if he would kill an infant; Bronn says he'd ask how much.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Calculating and controlled during the Slynt confrontation; bitter and angry when confirming Cersei's role in the infant's murder; philosophical during the Varys exchange; tired and a little drunk by chapter's end; conflicted about the moral cost of his justice ("why do I have this bitter taste in my mouth?")
- **Primary preoccupation:** Consolidating power in King's Landing; replacing Cersei's and Littlefinger's agents with his own; the moral weight of ordering deaths; his inability to touch Cersei directly
- **Key decisions made:** Dismissed Janos Slynt to the Wall; appointed Ser Jacelyn Bywater as Commander of the City Watch; ordered Allar Deem killed at sea; ordered the Redwyne escape thwarted; ordered a trial for the White Hart's master; decided not to act against Cersei yet
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion frames the killing of Allar Deem as justice but recognizes it as "a mummer's show of justice"; he asks Bronn about killing an infant, testing whether his own men are different from Slynt's — and the answer suggests they're not, leaving him wanting Shae rather than confronting the implication
