- **time_markers:** "Is it morning?" (Sam lost track of time underground); morning sky streaked with thin grey clouds; "We ride at first light" (tomorrow); Ser Alliser should reach King's Landing "by the turn of the moon"; ravens to be picked "come evenfall, after the feeding"; news of Robb's crowning arrived "last night" with Conwy
- **chapter_summary:** Jon finds Sam in Castle Black's underground library vault, where Sam has spent all night searching for maps for Lord Commander Mormont. They emerge to see the Wall and the comet dubbed "Mormont's Torch." At the armory, Jon speaks with Donal Noye about Robb's crowning as King in the North, and Noye compares the three Baratheon brothers to different metals. Jon and Sam bring maps to Mormont in the King's Tower, where Thoren Smallwood argues unsuccessfully for command of the ranging. Mormont tells Jon the story of Maester Aemon's refusal of the Iron Throne, drawing a parallel to Jon's situation with a brother who is now a king, and Jon affirms he will keep his vows.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon searches for Sam in the library vault** — Jon descends into the underground library beneath Castle Black before dawn, following a lamp's glow through narrow aisles of shelves.
2. **Sam found maps for Mormont** — Sam has been in the vault all night and found at least a dozen maps, plus Redwyn's pre-Conquest account of ranging to the Frozen Shore.
3. **Jon and Sam discuss the maps and the ranging** — Jon tries to encourage Sam; Sam expresses fear about going beyond the Wall. Jon reminds him the Night's Watch will have 300 men.
4. **Jon and Sam climb to the surface** — They leave through the wormwalks and emerge to the brisk wind. Ghost wakes and joins them.
5. **They observe the Wall and the comet** — Sam notes the comet ("Mormont's Torch") is now visible by day.
6. **They pass the sept** — Voices raised in song; Jon reflects that his gods (the old gods) will be waiting beyond the Wall.
7. **Jon observes Ser Endrew training recruits** — Six new recruits brought by Conwy the night before; Ser Endrew is gentler than Thorne but still raises bruises.
8. **Jon speaks with Donal Noye** — Noye asks about the recruits, reveals they're from a lord's dungeon near Gulltown. Discussion turns to Robb's crowning as King in the North.
9. **Noye compares the Baratheon brothers** — Robert was true steel, Stannis is iron (strong but brittle), Renly is copper (pretty but not worth much). Noye wishes Jon well and asks him to bring back Benjen.
10. **Jon and Sam deliver maps to Mormont** — They enter the King's Tower; the raven greets Jon with "Snow!"
11. **Thoren Smallwood argues for command** — Claims he should be First Ranger and lead the ranging; Mormont firmly refuses, stating Benjen remains First Ranger until confirmed dead.
12. **Mormont dismisses Sam** — Sends him back to Maester Aemon after Sam stammers; considers sending Ser Arnell to Renly instead of Sam.
13. **Mormont examines Jon's burned hand** — Jon shows his healing scars, confirms he can wield Longclaw; Maester Aemon gave him exercises and a salve.
14. **Mormont tells Maester Aemon's full lineage story** — Recounts how Aemon was offered the Iron Throne and refused, choosing his maester's vows; came to the Wall to avoid being used against his brother Aegon V.
15. **Mormont tests Jon's loyalty** — Draws parallel between Jon and Aemon: both have a brother who is king. Points out the hardships of Jon's life at the Wall versus Robb's glory.
16. **Jon affirms his vows** — "Be troubled, and keep my vows."
