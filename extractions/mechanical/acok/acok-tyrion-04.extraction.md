# ACOK — Tyrion IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 18
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion IV
- **first_available:** ACOK Tyrion IV
- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Grand Maester's chambers beneath the rookery, lower bailey, Tyrion's solar, Tyrion's bedchamber
- **approximate_timeline:** Early in Tyrion's tenure as Hand; Renly is marching up the roseroad but has not yet arrived; Stannis has not yet sailed from Dragonstone
- **time_markers:** Dawn meeting with Pycelle; "the sun was well up now" when Tyrion leaves Pycelle; "not an hour later" Varys arrives after Tyrion goes to his bedchamber; Tyrion hoped Varys would come by evenfall or moonrise; Tyrion hopes to visit Shae tonight; "Give me a fortnight" (Littlefinger's request for departure preparation); "the full moon" mentioned by Cersei as possible Renly arrival
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion executes a three-pronged intelligence trap against Pycelle, Littlefinger, and Varys. At dawn he sends sealed letters to Doran Martell via Pycelle's ravens and secretly pockets a bottle from Pycelle's medicine shelves. He then offers Littlefinger Harrenhal and the title of liege lord of the Trident in exchange for persuading Lysa Arryn to support Joffrey, with Myrcella offered as a ward and future bride for Robert Arryn. Finally, Varys deduces that Tyrion's offer to Doran Martell involves Prince Tommen as a ward, a council seat, and justice for Princess Elia's killers. Each man has been told a different piece of Tyrion's plans; if Cersei discovers any specific detail, Tyrion will know which of the three is her informant.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion meets Pycelle at dawn** — Pycelle apologizes for the early hour; his serving girl provides breakfast of boiled eggs, stewed plums, and porridge
2. **Tyrion presents sealed letters** — Two parchments for Doran Martell, Prince of Dorne, sealed with wax at both ends; he orders the serving girl dismissed before discussing them
3. **Pycelle dispatches ravens** — Tyrion insists the letters be sent immediately rather than after breakfast, citing Renly's march and Stannis's threat
4. **Tyrion steals a bottle from Pycelle's medicine shelves** — While Pycelle is upstairs with the ravens, Tyrion browses the medicine display, notes various poisons and remedies, pulls a small dusty bottle from a high shelf, reads its label, smiles, and hides it up his sleeve
5. **Tyrion secures Pycelle's exclusive loyalty** — Instructs Pycelle to inform only him of any reply from Dorne, not Cersei; Pycelle reluctantly agrees; Tyrion thinks "One"
6. **Tyrion meets Bronn in the lower bailey** — Bronn sits on a well, watching knights train rather than looking at serving girls; identifies Tallad as the best fighter but notes a predictable attack pattern
7. **Bronn reports on supplicants** — Lady Tanda's dinner invitation (declined); Braavosi moneylender seeking loan payment (sent to Littlefinger); lordling from the Trident whose lands were ravaged (given lodging and boots); bakers demanding protection after mob violence (told Joffrey shares their fears); Ser Alliser Thorne from the Wall with a rotted hand in a jar (deliberately left waiting in squalid quarters)
8. **A baker was roasted in his own oven** — The mob claimed he charged too much for bread; gold cloaks reportedly participated in the mob; food merchants demand to see the king
9. **Tyrion encounters Cersei at the main gate** — She rides out with her retinue to inspect new scorpions and spitfires at the gates; she is displeased with Tyrion over the Janos Slynt affair
10. **Cersei and Tyrion argue** — Cersei reports Renly's march and demands Tywin bring his army to King's Landing; demands Jaime's release; calls Tyrion "worse than useless"
11. **Tyrion reflects on Stannis as the greater threat** — Stannis is hard, cold, inexorable; fishermen sent to spy on Dragonstone have not returned; Varys's informers in Stannis's household have gone silent; Lysene war galleys seen offshore; Myrish reports of sellsails taking Dragonstone's service
12. **Podrick Payne announces Littlefinger** — Pod announces nervously, staring at the floor; corrects himself through three names (Lord Littlefinger, Lord Petyr, Lord Baelish)
13. **Littlefinger and Tyrion watch Joffrey shoot hares** — Joffrey misses most shots, nearly hits Ser Preston Greenfield, eventually kills one hare
14. **Littlefinger offers Tyrion the Valyrian steel dagger** — Draws it casually, calls it "a trifle plain"; Tyrion refuses: "Never mine" — both understand the knife's significance
15. **Tyrion reflects on Littlefinger's rise and power** — Jon Arryn gave him a customs post ten years ago; rose to master of coin within three years; revenues ten times his predecessor's but debts grew vast; controls the Keepers of the Keys, King's Counter, King's Scales, officers of three mints, harbormasters, tax farmers, customs sergeants, and more
16. **Tyrion proposes the Lysa Arryn gambit** — Asks Littlefinger to use his Tully connections to sway Lysa; offers to give Lysa "Jon Arryn's true killer," peace in the Vale (stopping the clansmen Tyrion himself armed), and Myrcella as a ward and future bride for Robert Arryn, who would be named Warden of the East
17. **Tyrion offers Littlefinger Harrenhal** — Plus the title of liege lord of the Trident, with the river lords doing him fealty; Littlefinger sees through the parallels to Janos Slynt but is too tempted to refuse
18. **Littlefinger agrees** — Requests a fortnight to conclude his affairs and arrange a ship to Gulltown; Tyrion thinks "Two"
19. **Varys visits Tyrion in his bedchamber** — Arrives within an hour; scolds Tyrion for making Pycelle squirm
20. **Varys deduces Tyrion's offer to Doran Martell** — Council seat; justice for Elia's killers (alive or dead); Prince Tommen as a ward and token of good faith
21. **Tyrion and Varys discuss Elia's killers** — Both know it was Gregor Clegane; Varys notes Elia cried "a certain name" when they came for her; Tyrion deflects blame to Robert Baratheon as rebellion's leader
22. **Varys probes Tyrion's intentions regarding Tommen** — Notes Tommen is heir to Joffrey and "notably tractable"
23. **Tyrion sets the trap** — Tells Varys that "what Cersei does not know will never hurt me" and that discovering his plans would reveal "the man who told her to be my certain enemy"; Tyrion thinks "Three"

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Calculating, controlled, pleased with his own cleverness; undercurrent of anxiety about Stannis and the city's vulnerability; irritation at Cersei's contempt; dark amusement at manipulating Pycelle, Littlefinger, and Varys
- **Primary preoccupation:** Executing the three-pronged trap to identify Cersei's informant; simultaneously making real strategic alliances (Dorne, Vale) and managing the city's crises (food shortage, mob violence, defense)
- **Key decisions made:** Sends sealed letters to Doran Martell offering Tommen, a council seat, and justice for Elia; offers Littlefinger Harrenhal and the Trident in exchange for swaying Lysa Arryn with the Myrcella betrothal; deliberately tells each of his three targets (Pycelle, Littlefinger, Varys) different information to identify Cersei's spy; steals a bottle from Pycelle's medicine shelf; orders Ser Alliser Thorne left waiting in squalid quarters; decides to make time for the river lordling petitioner
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion questions whether he truly dares to move against Littlefinger even if proven a traitor ("But do I dare touch him?"); he rationalizes the lordling petitioner's treatment as strategic rather than compassionate; he may be overconfident in his scheme's effectiveness
