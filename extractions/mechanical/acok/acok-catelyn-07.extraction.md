Catelyn reveals to Brienne the news from Ser Rodrik that Theon Greyjoy has killed Bran and Rickon. She visits her dying father Lord Hoster, then at midnight goes to the dungeons to interrogate Jaime Lannister. Jaime confirms he fathered Cersei's children and pushed Bran from the window, but denies sending the catspaw assassin, suggesting the Valyrian steel dagger belonged to Robert. Jaime tells the gruesome story of how Aerys killed Brandon and Rickard Stark, and the chapter ends with Catelyn calling for Brienne's sword.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn and Brienne eat supper alone in the Great Hall** — Servants dismissed to join celebration; Catelyn stares at wine, eating nothing; deep shadows and guttered torches.
2. **Smallfolk celebrate in the yard** — Twenty casks from Ser Desmond; celebrating Edmure's return and Robb's conquest of the Crag.
3. **Catelyn reveals Bran and Rickon's reported deaths to Brienne** — A bird from Castle Cerwyn from Ser Rodrik reported the boys captured at a mill on the Acorn Water and their heads mounted on Winterfell's walls by Theon Greyjoy.
4. **Catelyn describes her daughters** — Sansa's auburn hair and courtesy; Arya's wildness and Ned's face; believes Arya must be dead.
5. **Catelyn states she has sent wine to Jaime** — Announces her plan to visit the Kingslayer; tells Brienne to come at midnight.
6. **Catelyn visits Lord Hoster in his solar** — He is deep in dreamwine sleep; Maester Vyman reports on his condition; Catelyn refuses a sleeping draught.
7. **Catelyn holds her father's hand and speaks to him** — Tells him of her grief; recalls getting lost with Lysa riding from Seagard when Petyr found them; speaks of the Stark words; wishes Robb would make peace.
8. **Catelyn sits with Hoster through the night** — Candle goes out; moonlight comes; Rymund sings outside; hours pass until midnight.
9. **Brienne arrives at midnight** — Catelyn releases her father's hand.
10. **Catelyn and Brienne confront the gaoler** — He cites Edmure's order requiring written permission; Catelyn overrules him by invoking Lord Hoster's authority.
11. **Catelyn enters Jaime's cell** — Notices the wine untouched; the cell is foul and dark; takes down the oil lamp and turns up flame.
12. **Jaime and Catelyn negotiate terms** — He agrees to answer her questions in exchange for news of his family.
13. **Jaime confirms he fathered Joffrey and Cersei's other children** — Direct admission.
14. **Catelyn tells Jaime that Ser Stafford is dead but Cersei, Tyrion, and Tywin live** — Jaime is unmoved by Stafford's death.
15. **Jaime confesses he pushed Bran from the window** — "I flung him from a window"; "I meant for him to die"; Bran was spying on Jaime and Cersei.
16. **Jaime denies sending the catspaw assassin** — Claims he never hires killers; would have done it himself.
17. **Discussion of the Valyrian steel dagger** — Catelyn describes it; initially says Tyrion won it betting on Jaime, then corrects herself; Jaime recalls Robert showed him the dagger at the feast after Loras unhorsed him; implies Robert owned the dagger.
18. **Exchange of war news** — Catelyn tells Jaime that Stannis marches on King's Landing and Renly is dead at Bitterbridge; Robb took the Crag; Tyrells' allegiance uncertain.
19. **Discussion of Rickard Karstark** — Catelyn mentions Karstark wants Jaime dead; Jaime admits killing two Karstark sons while trying to reach Robb.
20. **Jaime's speech about conflicting vows** — Claims the oaths of knighthood are contradictory; no matter what you do, you forsake one or another.
21. **Jaime tells the story of Brandon and Rickard Stark's deaths** — Brandon strangled by Tyroshi device while trying to reach his sword to free his father; Rickard burned alive in his armor by Aerys's pyromancers; Jaime watched in white cloak.
22. **Jaime reflects on killing Aerys** — "A golden sword"; "loved by one for a kindness I never did, and reviled by so many for my finest act"; references Robert's coronation.
23. **Jaime grows increasingly drunk and crude** — Insults Catelyn regarding Littlefinger; mentions Jon Snow; Catelyn calls for Brienne.
24. **Catelyn demands Brienne's sword** — Chapter ends with Catelyn holding out her hand for the sword inside Jaime's cell.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Overwhelming grief for Bran and Rickon; "creature of grief and dust and bitter longings"; "empty place within me where my heart was once"; rage toward Theon, Jaime, Cersei, Tyrion; despair about the war; fear for Sansa and Arya; cold determination to act
- **Primary preoccupation:** The reported deaths of Bran and Rickon; protecting her surviving children (Sansa and Arya); extracting information from Jaime about Bran's fall and the assassin
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to interrogate Jaime at midnight using the wine ploy; overrules the gaoler's enforcement of Edmure's order; demands Brienne's sword at chapter's end (implied decision to free Jaime in exchange for her daughters)
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn clings to the hope that not speaking of Bran and Rickon's deaths might make them untrue ("will holding it secret in your heart make it any less true?"); she also seems partially aware that Petyr Baelish may have lied about the dagger but struggles to accept it
