## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion confronts Ser Mandon Moore** — Ser Mandon blocks Tyrion from entering the council chamber on Cersei's orders. Tyrion introduces Bronn and Timett as implied threats, and Ser Mandon relents, allowing only Tyrion to enter.
2. **Tyrion presents Tywin's letter to the small council** — Tyrion enters the council chamber where Cersei, Varys, Pycelle, Janos Slynt, and Littlefinger are meeting. He presents Tywin's sealed letter naming him acting Hand of the King.
3. **Cersei reads the letter and objects** — She protests that the king names the Hand with consent of council, and that Joffrey named Tywin. Tyrion notes that Tywin, with his army, cannot be easily overruled.
4. **Tyrion claims the Tower of the Hand** — He declines Littlefinger's offer of rooms in Maegor's Holdfast and announces he will take Lord Stark's former quarters in the Tower of the Hand. He recites the grim fates of recent Hands.
5. **Council members depart** — Varys, Slynt, Pycelle, and Littlefinger leave at Tyrion's request for a private word with Cersei.
6. **Cersei reveals Joffrey ordered Eddard Stark's execution** — He was instructed to pardon Stark and let him take the black, but Joffrey called for his head before half the city, and Janos Slynt and Ser Ilyn carried it out without Cersei's word.
7. **Cersei reveals Arya Stark is missing** — She has given out that she holds both Stark daughters, but Arya fled when Ser Meryn Trant came for her, aided by "her wretched dancing master." Cersei believes she is likely dead.
8. **Cersei reveals Varys suggested Ser Barristan's dismissal** — This gave Jaime command of the Kingsguard and allowed Joffrey to elevate Sandor Clegane. Selmy killed two gold cloaks when they tried to seize him at the Mud Gate.
9. **Cersei reveals Littlefinger arranged Janos Slynt's elevation** — Littlefinger brokered Slynt's gold cloaks' support; Slynt was given Harrenhal and a council seat.
10. **Cersei reveals Sansa Stark informed on her father** — Sansa came to Cersei and told her all of Eddard's plans, motivated by her love for Joffrey.
11. **Tyrion and Cersei discuss Jaime's incestuous relationship** — Tyrion states he has always known. Cersei slaps him three times during this exchange.
12. **Cersei reveals how Robert Baratheon was killed** — Lancel gave Robert fortified strongwine (three times normal potency) when he went after a boar. The boar killed Robert. The boar was cooked with mushrooms and apples and "tasted like triumph."
13. **Cersei conditionally accepts Tyrion** — She demands he share all plans and act only with her consent. Tyrion agrees, privately noting he is lying.
14. **Tyrion asks who murdered Jon Arryn** — Cersei claims not to know. The conversation ends.
15. **Tyrion orders the heads removed from the walls** — While riding out through the Red Keep portcullis, he orders Captain Vylarr to give the heads to the silent sisters. Vylarr says Joffrey wants them to stay. Tyrion threatens him and Vylarr complies.
16. **Tyrion rides through King's Landing and observes the city's crisis** — Food shortages, inflated prices, lawlessness, a corpse in the gutter, rats sold as food, bakers hiring guards. No food coming in from the south or west due to war.
17. **Vylarr reports on the city's defenses** — City Watch tripled; walls being strengthened; scorpions, catapults, arrows, and blades being produced; Alchemists' Guild pledged ten thousand jars of wildfire. Littlefinger imposed an entry tax to fund it.
18. **Tyrion arrives at the Broken Anvil inn** — He dismisses Vylarr and the guard, planning to stay the night.
19. **Varys is discovered with Shae** — The eunuch has found Shae at the inn and is sitting with her and Chella's Black Ears. Tyrion understands this as Varys demonstrating his intelligence network and delivering veiled threats about Shae's vulnerability.
20. **Varys delivers coded warnings** — Through seemingly casual remarks about the Gate of the Gods carvings ("eyes that follow you") and the danger of the streets ("men with cold steel and colder hearts"), Varys communicates that he was watching Tyrion's arrival and that Shae could be targeted.
21. **Varys poses the riddle** — King, priest, and rich man each bid a sellsword to kill the other two. Who lives and who dies? Shae says the rich man lives. Tyrion says it depends on the sellsword.
22. **Tyrion and Shae go to her room** — They have sex. Afterward, Tyrion warns himself that Shae is a whore who loves his coin, not him, and thinks of Tysha.
23. **Tyrion resolves to do justice** — When Shae asks what he will do as Hand, he says he will do "something Cersei will never expect" — justice.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Wary but confident; sardonic and darkly amused throughout; genuinely tender and vulnerable with Shae; self-loathing about his physical appearance and his feelings for a prostitute; cold fury toward Joffrey's cruelty (the heads on the walls); disturbed by the city's deterioration; guarded and calculating with Cersei and Varys
- **Primary preoccupation:** Establishing his authority as Hand despite Cersei's resistance and the precariousness of his position; assessing the political landscape — who can be trusted, who is a threat; keeping Shae safe and hidden (already compromised by Varys)
- **Key decisions made:** Claims the Tower of the Hand over Maegor's Holdfast; orders the heads removed from the walls; lies to Cersei about obeying her; resolves to "do justice" as Hand; decides to keep Shae in a separate house with guards and servants
- **Self-deception flags:** Knows intellectually that Shae loves his coin, not him ("She's a whore, damn you... Remember Tysha?") but cannot fully act on this knowledge — his feelings override his judgment; tells himself his agreement with Cersei is strategic lying, but his desire to do "justice" may be idealistic self-flattery given his actual constraints
