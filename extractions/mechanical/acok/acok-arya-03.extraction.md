## Chapter Metadata

- **pov_character:** Arya Stark (disguised as "Arry")

- **location_primary:** Small road west of the kingsroad, approaching the Gods Eye
- **location_secondary:** Briarwhite (holdfast), a burned unnamed village/holdfast, woods and farmland along the route
- **approximate_timeline:** Days to weeks after departing King's Landing; the party has left the kingsroad and is heading northwest toward the Gods Eye, then north
- **time_markers:** "a dozen times a day" (wagon delays); "come dusk" (Koss and Kurz return from hunting); "one night" (Lommy's speculation about Gendry); "one morning" (Yoren announces the route around Gods Eye); "one day" (Arya catches the rabbit); sweetcorn roasted "that night"; "the next day" (Koss scouts the armed camp); "two days at the least" (detour cost); "a day later" (Dobber spots the fire); "by dawn" (fire burned out); "midday" (arrival at burned village); "evenfall" (one-armed woman dies); "pitch-black" when Arya wakes at night
- **chapter_summary:** Yoren's party leaves the kingsroad for smaller roads heading west toward the Gods Eye, slowed by wagons and winding paths. Food runs out and they live off hunting and foraging. They pay for corn at Briarwhite, detour around an armed camp of injured soldiers, and find increasing signs of war. They arrive at a destroyed, burned village where they rescue a one-armed woman and a toddler; the woman dies that evening. At night Arya encounters a wolf pack in the woods and confides in Yoren about wanting to go home.

## Events & Actions
1. **Travel on small road** — The party leaves the kingsroad for a winding, narrow path; wagons slow progress severely with wheels sticking in ruts and needing doubled teams for muddy slopes.
2. **Ox cart encounter** — In a dense oak stand, they meet three foresters with an ox cart; no way to pass, so they wait while the foresters unhitch, turn, and leave the way they came.
3. **Arya practices stealth** — At night, Arya sneaks past sentries to relieve herself; she climbs an oak and moves tree-to-tree above Lommy without being detected.
4. **Discussion of Gendry's parentage** — The orphan boys treat Gendry as someone special because the queen wants his head. Gendry insists he was just an armorer's apprentice. Lommy whispers that Gendry is "the wolf lord's" bastard; Arya angrily denies it.
5. **Yoren announces route change** — Yoren tells the party they'll go around Gods Eye along the western shore to avoid the kingsroad; he turns the wagons west at the next crossroads.
6. **Terrain changes, food runs out** — Farmland gives way to forest; villages become smaller and farther apart; all provisions from King's Landing are consumed.
7. **Hunting and foraging** — Koss and Kurz hunt deer and quail; younger boys pick blackberries and apples; Arya catches a rabbit, which Yoren stews.
8. **Arya shares rabbit with Gendry** — She receives a whole leg and shares it with Gendry; the prisoners each get a spoonful; Jaqen thanks her politely, Biter licks his fingers, Rorge mocks her.
9. **Confrontation at Briarwhite** — Fieldhands surround them in a cornfield demanding coin for corn ears taken; Yoren pays coppers; fieldhands threaten them; Yoren laments the decline of hospitality toward the Night's Watch.
10. **Roasting sweetcorn** — They roast the corn that night; Arya finds it wonderful but Yoren is too angry to eat.
11. **Koss scouts armed camp** — Koss reports 20-30 wounded men in mail and halfhelms with spears, shields, one lame horse; spotted treecat banner (yellow and black on mud-brown field); one man dying.
12. **Detour around the camp** — Yoren cannot identify the banner's allegiance and decides the wounded men would likely take their mounts regardless of side; they detour miles out of their way, losing two days.
13. **Increasing militarization of the countryside** — When they turn north again, armed men guard fields more frequently: standing by roads, patrolling on horses with axes, one man in a dead tree with a bow who tracks them with a nocked arrow.
14. **Yoren curses the archer** — Yoren says the man will scream for the Watch when the Others come.
15. **Distant fire spotted** — Dobber sees a red glow to the north at evening; Yoren identifies it as fire; they watch it grow through the night until it burns out by dawn; none sleep well.
16. **Arrival at burned village** — At midday they find a destroyed village: fields charred for miles, houses as blackened shells, burnt/butchered animal carcasses covered in carrion crows, burnt bodies impaled on stakes atop the palisade walls.
17. **Investigation of the holdfast** — Yoren, Murch, and Cutjack go in on foot through the broken gate while Arya and the boys guard the wagons; ravens rise from inside.
18. **Rescue of survivors** — They emerge with a little girl (no older than two, crying with a whimpery sound, cannot or will not talk) and a one-armed woman (right arm ends at bloody stump at elbow, eyes see nothing, says only "Please" repeatedly).
19. **Rorge and Biter laugh at the woman** — Rorge laughs through his noseless face, Biter joins; Murch curses them and tells them to shut up.
20. **Hot Pie's confession** — Hot Pie tells Arya he's scared; confesses he never truly kicked a boy to death, just sold his mother's pies.
21. **Arya remembers Old Nan's story** — About a man who escaped evil giants only to be taken by the Others who drank his hot red blood; Arya identifies with his predicament.
22. **One-armed woman dies** — She dies at evenfall; Gendry and Cutjack dig her grave on a hillside beneath a weeping willow.
23. **Fireless camp** — No fire that night; supper is wild radishes, dry beans, and brook water with a funny taste.
24. **Night scene — sentries and sounds** — Arya wakes in pitch darkness; hears Yoren sharpening his dirk, Rorge snoring, Biter hissing in sleep.
25. **Hot Pie challenges Arya** — Hot Pie is on watch and stops Arya from going to the woods, warning of wolves; Arya pretends to be afraid, then sneaks out the other side of camp.
26. **Wolf encounter** — While squatting to relieve herself, Arya sees eyes in the woods — counts twelve, a whole pack; one wolf emerges, bares its teeth, then turns and races back into darkness; the pack disappears.
27. **Arya confides in Yoren** — She tells him about the wolves; he says "your kind was fond o' wolves"; Arya talks about Nymeria, chasing her away with Jory, wishing Nymeria had been in King's Landing to prevent her father's execution.
28. **Yoren's reflection** — He has been bringing men to the Wall for thirty years, losing only three; he wonders if going by ship would have been wiser; tells Arya to sleep.
29. **Wolves howl and distant screams** — As Arya lies under her blanket, she hears wolves howling and a fainter sound that might be screams.
