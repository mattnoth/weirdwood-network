- **time_markers:** "a few heartbeats after she took her leave"; "In the days that followed"; "It was a few days after Alebelly's bath"; "that evening over supper"; "later that night"
- **chapter_summary:** A raven arrives at Winterfell bearing news of Robb's victory at Oxcross and Ser Stevron Frey's death. Jojen Reed reveals his green dream of the sea flooding Winterfell and drowning Alebelly, Mikken, and Septon Chayle. Jojen tells Bran he is a warg and urges him to open his "third eye." Ser Rodrik returns with the prisoner Reek, bringing news that the Bastard of Bolton has been killed and Lady Hornwood starved to death. Jojen then reveals a second dream: Reek skinning the faces of Bran and Rickon, and seeing the boys dead in the crypts.

## Events & Actions
1. **Alebelly summons Bran from the forge** — Bran is working Mikken's bellows; Alebelly carries him to the maester's turret
2. **Maester Luwin delivers news of Robb's victory** — Robb shattered a Lannister army at Oxcross, took castles, and writes from Ashemark; Ser Stafford Lannister was slain
3. **Death of Ser Stevron Frey announced** — Wounded at Oxcross, died three days later in his sleep; the Walders discuss succession callously
4. **Bran asks to be excused** — Disturbed by the Walders' lack of grief
5. **Osha carries Bran and discusses the way north** — Tells him to follow the Ice Dragon constellation, the blue star in the rider's eye; confirms giants exist
6. **Bran asks Osha about the three-eyed crow** — She has never seen one
7. **Jojen and Meera visit Bran** — Jojen reveals his green dream: the sea flooding Winterfell, drowning Alebelly, Mikken, and Septon Chayle
8. **Jojen tells Bran he is a warg** — Calls him "the winged wolf"; says the wolf dreams are real; tells him to open his third eye with his heart
9. **Bran tries to warn Mikken, Septon Chayle, and Alebelly** — Mikken laughs it off; Septon Chayle dismisses it; only Alebelly heeds it, stops bathing
10. **Alebelly forced into a bath** — Six guards throw him into scalding water; he screams they're drowning him
11. **Ser Rodrik returns with Reek** — Prisoner served the Bastard of Bolton, helped murder Lady Hornwood
12. **News of the Bastard of Bolton's death** — Shot down with arrows on Hornwood land while doing "something horrible" without clothes
13. **Lady Hornwood's fate described** — Starved in a tower, found with bloody mouth and chewed-off fingers
14. **Ser Rodrik discusses the political complications** — Lady Hornwood's forced marriage and will create succession problems; Manderly and Dreadfort men killing each other; raiders on the Stony Shore
15. **Bran tells Ser Rodrik about Jojen's vision** — Ser Rodrik decides not to take Alebelly if he rides against raiders
16. **Evening tiles game: Jojen reveals second dream** — Reek skinning the faces of Bran and Rickon; sees them dead in the crypts with the dead kings and stone wolves
17. **Meera offers to kill Reek** — Jojen says gaolers will stop her; warns it won't matter

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Excited at first (news from Robb), then disquieted by the Walders' callousness, frightened by Jojen's revelations, muddled and afraid by chapter's end
- **Primary preoccupation:** The green dreams — both the flood vision and the Reek vision; what it means to be a warg; whether the visions can be prevented
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to trust Jojen and share his own dreams; tries to warn Mikken, Septon Chayle, and Alebelly; considers fleeing to Greywater or "the crow"
- **Self-deception flags:** Brief hope that staying away from the sea will save the doomed men ("If they stay away from the sea"); Bran denies being a warg ("I'm not like that... It's only dreams")
