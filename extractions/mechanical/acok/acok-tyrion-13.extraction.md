- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion watches from atop a merlon at the Mud Gate as wildfire consumes ships on the Blackwater. A runner reports enemy forces have landed at the tourney grounds and are ramming the King's Gate. Tyrion rides to King's Gate and finds Sandor Clegane refusing to lead another sortie, broken by fire and wounds. Tyrion volunteers to lead the sortie himself, shames the reluctant men into following, and rides toward the sally port with his gathered force.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion watches wildfire devastation from the Mud Gate** — Perched atop a merlon, he surveys the Blackwater ablaze with wildfire. Half of Stannis's fleet and most of Joffrey's ships are burning. The main channel is engulfed but some enemy ships escape toward the south bank or land under the city walls.
2. **Joffrey mourns his ships** — From the wallwalk behind the ramparts, Joffrey points out his burning ships by name (Kingslander, Queen Cersei, Loyal Man, Seaflower) with his new sword.
3. **Tyrion tells Joffrey the fleet sacrifice was necessary** — Explains the fleet had to engage to avoid Stannis sensing the trap; wildfire cannot be controlled once loosed.
4. **Tyrion mentally reviews the chain stratagem** — Bronn would have started the oxen and winches when Stannis's flagship passed the Red Keep; the chain now stretches taut across the river, trapping the fleet.
5. **Tyrion assesses the battle situation** — Some enemy ships escaped the wildfire; Stannis likely retains thirty to forty galleys, enough to ferry his whole host across once they recover courage. Eight or more ships have landed men under the city walls.
6. **Tyrion orders a runner to Lord Jacelyn about enemy on the riverfront** — Directs a sortie to hit the enemy while they're disorganized from landing.
7. **Tyrion orders Ser Arneld to adjust the Whores 30 degrees west** — Changes the trebuchet angle to throw farther inland rather than into the water.
8. **Tyrion gives Joffrey permission to use the Whores to fling Antler Men** — The Antler Men are trussed naked in the square with antlers nailed to their heads. Gold cloaks have been wagering on whether the traitors will fly across the Blackwater.
9. **Joffrey departs with Ser Meryn Trant** — Hurries off happily to the trebuchets.
10. **Tyrion instructs Ser Osmund Kettleblack to protect Joffrey** — Grabs him by the wrist and orders him to keep the king safe and in place, whatever happens.
11. **Tyrion reflects on his warnings to Trant and Kettleblack** — Has warned them what would happen if harm came to the king; thinks bitterly about protecting Cersei's "wretched bastard" and hopes Cersei keeps Alayaya safe.
12. **A runner reports enemy at the King's Gate** — Hundreds have landed on the tourney grounds; a battering ram is being brought against the King's Gate.
13. **Tyrion rides to King's Gate with Pod and Ser Mandon** — They gallop down River Row (kept clear by Tyrion's orders) and arrive to hear the ram crashing and hinges groaning.
14. **Tyrion orders troops at King's Gate to form up for a sortie** — Finds wounded, horses, sellswords, and gold cloaks in the gatehouse square.
15. **Sandor Clegane refuses to go out again** — Steps from the shadows, wrenches off his scorched helm. He's been out three times, lost half his men. His face is bloody from a gash above his eye, blood running down his old burn scars.
16. **Clegane draws his longsword in implicit threat** — When Tyrion insists, Clegane draws his longsword; his eyes show white with fear.
17. **Tyrion recognizes Clegane is terrified of fire** — Realizes the Hound is truly broken by the wildfire; "dead on his feet."
18. **Clegane rejects water, demands wine** — Spits out the water given by a gold cloak officer, flings the cup away.
19. **Ser Mandon Moore backs Tyrion's authority** — Declares "The King's Hand commands you." Clegane responds: "Bugger the King's Hand."
20. **Tyrion decides to lead the sortie himself** — "This is madness, but sooner madness than defeat. Defeat is death and shame."
21. **Clegane laughs at Tyrion** — Responds with disbelief: "You?"
22. **Tyrion mounts up and calls men to form** — Only about twenty respond initially; the rest sit with white eyes.
23. **Tyrion shames the reluctant men** — "They say I'm half a man. What does that make the lot of you?" This works — a helmetless knight, then sellswords, then more mount and join. The force doubles.
24. **Tyrion gives his rallying speech** — "You won't hear me shout out Joffrey's name... This is your city Stannis means to sack, and that's your gate he's bringing down. So come with me and kill the son of a bitch!"
25. **Tyrion rides toward the sally port** — Unsheathes his axe, wheels his stallion around, and trots toward the sally port, hoping the men follow but not daring to look back.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** A volatile mix of grim resolve, horror at the carnage, bitter pride, and fear. He feels the terrible beauty of the wildfire but cannot cheer. He is dimly aware this is only a half-victory. Growing desperation as the battle shifts from naval to ground assault. Bitter resentment toward Cersei. Ultimately, a rush of desperate courage when he decides to lead the sortie — "sooner madness than defeat."
- **Primary preoccupation:** The tactical situation — the wildfire has not spread evenly enough, Stannis retains enough ships to cross, enemy forces are landing and breaching gates. He is constantly calculating odds and issuing orders.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Gives Joffrey the trebuchets to fling the Antler Men, getting Joffrey away from the fighting. (2) Orders the Whores adjusted 30 degrees west. (3) Decides to personally lead the sortie at King's Gate when Clegane refuses.
- **Self-deception flags:** None prominent. Tyrion is largely clear-eyed about the situation. He acknowledges the battle is only "a half victory" and "it will not be enough." He does not delude himself about his own men's loyalty or courage.
