## Events & Actions
1. **Scout encounter** — Two days from Riverrun, a Frey scout spots Catelyn's party watering horses by a muddy stream.
2. **News of Blackfish and Oxcross** — The scout informs Catelyn that the Blackfish has gone west with Robb, and that Robb won a great victory at Oxcross, killing Ser Stafford Lannister.
3. **Meeting Martyn Rivers** — Catelyn reaches Rivers's camp in a shattered holdfast; he informs her Lord Tywin has left Harrenhal and marches west; three to four days away.
4. **March to Riverrun** — Rivers breaks camp; they ride together, nearly fifty strong, under direwolf, leaping trout, and twin towers banners.
5. **Tales of Robb's campaign** — Rivers tells of Robb's westerlands raids: Karstark and Glover on the coast, Lady Mormont capturing cattle, the Greatjon seizing gold mines; Grey Wind showed Robb the path around the Golden Tooth; rumor that Robb fed Stafford's heart to Grey Wind.
6. **Brienne seeks leave to go** — That night at camp, Brienne asks Catelyn's permission to leave and return to Storm's End to kill Stannis.
7. **Catelyn and Brienne's conversation** — Catelyn argues Brienne should fight for the living; reveals her own desire to kill Cersei; discusses legitimacy of kings.
8. **Brienne swears oath** — Brienne offers to serve Catelyn instead; kneels, lays Renly's longsword at her feet, swears fealty by old gods and new. Catelyn accepts with reciprocal vows.
9. **Fording the Red Fork** — Next day, they cross at a fortified ford upstream of Riverrun; Mallister soldiers guide them past iron spikes and caltrops.
10. **Catelyn observes Edmure's preparations** — She realizes Edmure means to fight at the fords; passes streams of smallfolk, Blackwood camp, and camps of Piper, Darry, and Paege bannermen.
11. **Hanged Lannisters seen** — Dead men in crimson cloaks hang from Riverrun's battlements; Catelyn fears for Jaime and her daughters.
12. **Edmure rides out to meet Catelyn** — With Ser Desmond Grell, Utherydes Wayn, and Ser Robin Ryger; welcomes her back.
13. **News exchange** — Edmure reports no word from Bitterbridge; Storm's End sends pleas from Ser Cortnay Penrose who will not yield Edric Storm to Stannis.
14. **Ser Robin asks about Renly's death** — Catelyn introduces Brienne; declines to speak of the shadow openly.
15. **Edmure explains the escape attempt** — Four Lannister "envoys" tried to free Jaime on the fourth night; a mummer imitated Edmure's voice; Jaime got a sword and killed two men; all recaptured; Jaime now chained in a dark cell.
16. **Catelyn learns of the peace terms** — Ser Cleos brought the queen's terms; Edmure dismisses them.
17. **Discussion in the godswood** — Catelyn argues against giving battle; Edmure reveals his plan: Bolton with 10,000 at the crossroads, Tallhart to join him, together retake Harrenhal while Edmure holds the fords. Tywin trapped between Riverrun and Harrenhal.
18. **Catelyn visits Lord Hoster** — Her father is delirious; confuses her with Lysa; speaks of Jon Arryn, of a "wretched stripling," begs forgiveness; Maester Vyman gives him milk of the poppy.
19. **Maester Vyman's counsel** — Suggests sending for Brynden and Lysa; Catelyn agrees to write Lysa.
20. **Utherydes Wayn informs of Robb's instruction** — Robb wants Catelyn sent to the Twins to assess Lord Walder's daughters for his bride; Catelyn refuses to leave.
21. **Ned's bones revealed** — Silent sisters and Utherydes show Catelyn Ned's remains: only bones in his surcoat, head reattached with silver wire, a longsword (not Ice) in his hands. Ser Cleos brought them from King's Landing.
22. **Catelyn orders bones sent to Winterfell** — Hal Mollen to escort the silent sisters north with Ned's bones.
23. **Catelyn's vigil** — She asks to be left alone with Ned's remains.

- **Primary preoccupation:** Safety of her children (especially Sansa and Arya held in King's Landing — "if they have slain the Kingslayer, then my daughters are dead as well"); Lord Tywin's approach; her father's decline; concern about Edmure's competence as battle commander.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to go to the Twins as Robb instructed; accepts Brienne's oath of service; promises not to hold Brienne back from Stannis; orders Ned's bones sent to Winterfell with Hal Mollen.
- **Self-deception flags:** "Perhaps she was wrong to oppose him. Perhaps it was a splendid plan, and her misgivings only a woman's fears" — Catelyn second-guesses her valid strategic instincts, attributing them to gendered weakness. Immediately follows with wishing Ned or Brynden were there.
