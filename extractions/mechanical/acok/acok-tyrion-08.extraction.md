## Events & Actions
1. **Council convenes to discuss Renly's assassination** — Varys reports Renly was murdered, his throat cut ear to ear by a blade that passed through steel and bone. He delivers five conflicting rumors about the killer: a Rainbow Guard knight, Stannis with his magic sword, a spurned maid, a camp follower, and Lady Catelyn Stark.
2. **Tyrion assesses the strategic implications** — He concludes Stannis must be behind it regardless of who struck the blow. He had counted on the Baratheon brothers destroying each other in battle and is displeased the fight never happened.
3. **Varys reports the political aftermath** — Most lords at Storm's End have bent the knee to Stannis, led by Lord Alester Florent. Loras Tyrell, Randyll Tarly, and Mathis Rowan have not. A fifth of Renly's knights departed with Loras.
4. **Loras Tyrell's violent reaction** — Varys reports that Loras went mad at the sight of Renly's body and slew three of Renly's guards, including Emmon Cuy and Robar Royce.
5. **Renly's corpse has vanished** — Ser Cortnay Penrose holds Storm's End and demands to see the body before yielding, but Renly's remains have disappeared.
6. **Tyrion proposes the Joffrey-Margaery marriage** — He suggests winning the Tyrell alliance the same way Renly did: with a marriage. Varys grasps the implication first.
7. **Cersei objects to breaking Joffrey's betrothal to Sansa** — She argues Joffrey is too proud for "Renly's leavings." Tyrion, Littlefinger, and Varys all argue in favor of the match.
8. **Tyrion asserts his authority** — He tells Cersei that until Joffrey comes of age in three years, the regent and the Hand decide whom the king marries.
9. **Cersei yields** — "Make your offer then, but gods save you all if Joff does not like this girl."
10. **Cersei tries to send Tyrion as envoy** — She suggests Tyrion go to Bitterbridge, flattering his skill with words. Tyrion deflects, suggesting Cersei herself should go.
11. **Littlefinger volunteers as envoy** — He argues he is of the council but not the king's blood (making a poor hostage), knew Loras at court, and is skilled in negotiation.
12. **Escort negotiated** — Littlefinger bargains from 100 to 300 gold cloaks, plus 20 knights with 20 squires for a proper knightly retinue.
13. **Redwyne hostage decision** — Littlefinger proposes releasing both twins to their father as goodwill. Cersei balks. Tyrion compromises: release Ser Hobber to the Arbor, retain Ser Horas.
14. **Littlefinger demands written authority** — He requests a formal commission signed by Joffrey and all councillors with seals, granting full power to negotiate.
15. **Littlefinger hints at wanting reward** — When Cersei asks what he wants, he replies, "I shall need to give that some consideration. No doubt I'll think of something." He departs.
16. **Tyrion orders documents prepared** — He instructs Varys to send for parchment and quill and says someone must wake Joffrey to sign.
17. **Cersei unexpectedly compliments and kisses Tyrion** — After the meeting, she tells him she was wrong about him and thanks him for his help. She kisses him on the brow.
18. **Tyrion grows suspicious** — Astonished by the kiss, he tells Bronn that Cersei has been "behaving queerly of late" and is "hatching something." He instructs Bronn to find out what.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Politically engaged and calculating during the council meeting; displeased that the Baratheon brothers did not destroy each other as he had hoped; wary throughout, especially of Littlefinger's self-interested volunteering; briefly amused by the idea of Joffrey's disappointment over not getting Renly's head; deeply unsettled and suspicious after Cersei's unexpected kiss.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Managing the political crisis caused by Renly's death — preventing Stannis from absorbing the full Tyrell-Baratheon host; keeping control of King's Landing and his position as Hand.
- **Key decisions made:** Proposes the Joffrey-Margaery marriage alliance; agrees to send Littlefinger as envoy despite not trusting him; compromises on the Redwyne hostage situation (release one twin, retain the other); instructs Bronn to investigate Cersei's behavior.
- **Self-deception flags:** None overt. Tyrion is clear-eyed about the political dynamics and correctly suspicious of both Cersei and Littlefinger. His earlier scheme to send Joffrey to Chataya's reflects a belief that the boy can be managed through indulgence, which may be overly optimistic about Joffrey's nature.
