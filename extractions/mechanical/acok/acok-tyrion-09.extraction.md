# ACOK — Tyrion IX

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 42
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion IX
- **first_available:** ACOK Tyrion IX
- **location_primary:** King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Blackwater Bay, Blackwater Rush, Red Keep, Fishmonger's Square, Muddy Way, the Hook, Aegon's High Hill, Flea Bottom, Tower of the Hand
- **approximate_timeline:** During the War of the Five Kings; Myrcella departs King's Landing for Braavos en route to Dorne; defensive preparations for Stannis's expected assault are underway
- **time_markers:** "Another fortnight" (time Tyrion needs for winch towers); "last year" (when ships sailed with Stannis to Dragonstone); "evenfall bell" (curfew imposed); "Dusk had faded to darkness" (when Bywater delivers casualty report); "this very night, perhaps, or on the morrow" (Tyrion's promise to visit Shae); sunlight during Myrcella's departure; events span from daytime through evening into night
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion oversees Princess Myrcella's departure by ship from King's Landing to Braavos, routed to avoid Dragonstone. During the royal procession back to the Red Keep, a starving woman confronts the column and the crowd erupts into a full-scale riot after dung is thrown at Joffrey. The High Septon, Ser Aron Santagar, and Ser Preston Greenfield are killed; Lady Tanda's daughter Lollys is assaulted; and Tyrek Lannister goes missing. Back in the castle, Tyrion slaps and kicks Joffrey, then manages the crisis—dispatching Bronn and Clegane to fight fires in Flea Bottom. That evening, Ser Jacelyn Bywater warns Tyrion that the smallfolk blame him above all others, and Bronn darkly suggests replacing Joffrey with Tommen.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Thin and ragged clouds scuttling overhead; otherwise clear enough for sunlight to catch the High Septon's crystal crown
- **Season indicators:** None explicitly stated
- **Time of day:** Begins in daytime (sunlight on Myrcella's face during blessing); progresses through afternoon (procession and riot); evenfall (curfew imposed); dusk fading to darkness (Bywater's report); night (meeting with Varys and Bronn)
- **Lighting:** Sunlight during the departure ceremony, producing rainbows through crystal crown; growing darkness in Tyrion's solar by evening—he refuses candles and a fire until Bronn insists on a hearth fire
- **Sounds:** Horns blowing fanfares as ships depart; thin and ragged cheers from the crowd; the woman's shrieking ("Brotherfucker!"); the crowd's roar—"a rolling thunder of rage and fear and hatred"; chanting of "Bread, bread!"; the creak of wood and rope; rush of the river around pilings
- **Smells:** Tyrion thinks he can smell smoke from the fires in Flea Bottom, though he considers it might be "the scent of his nerves fraying"
- **Notable sensory details:** The galley rocking underfoot, making Tyrion's waddle worse; the steep plank down to the quay; cobblestones under horses' hooves; the cold greasy capon that repulses Tyrion; rushes on the floor of his solar

## Events & Actions
1. **Myrcella's farewell** — Myrcella says her farewells on the deck of the Seaswift with courage and dignity; Tommen cries and she comforts him.
2. **Tyrion reviews voyage plan** — Tyrion confirms the route with the captain of King Robert's Hammer: coast to Crackclaw Point, cross the narrow sea to Braavos, avoiding Dragonstone.
3. **High Septon blesses Myrcella** — The High Septon gives a long blessing; sunlight through his crystal crown spills rainbows on Myrcella's face.
4. **Tyrion promises a knighthood** — Tyrion promises the captain of Rob's Hammer a knighthood if Myrcella reaches Braavos safely.
5. **Fleet departs** — Lionstar and Lady Lyanna push off first, then Seaswift catches the current with white sails, followed by Bold Wind and King Robert's Hammer.
6. **Sansa tells a tale of chivalry** — When Joffrey mocks Tommen for crying, Sansa mentions Prince Aemon the Dragonknight weeping and the twin knights Arryk and Erryk dying with tears.
7. **Joffrey threatens Sansa** — Joffrey tells Sansa he'll have Ser Meryn give her a "mortal wound" if she doesn't be quiet.
8. **Royal procession begins** — The party rides through the city: Bywater's lancers in front, then banners, then Joffrey and Sansa flanked by the Hound and Ser Mandon, then Tommen, then Cersei, then courtiers.
9. **Wailing woman confronts the procession** — A starving woman holds up her dead baby in front of the king; Sansa leans over and says something to Joffrey; Joffrey flings a silver stag that bounces off the dead child.
10. **The woman calls Cersei "brotherfucker"** — The mother screams "Whore! Kingslayer's whore! Brotherfucker!" at Cersei, dropping her dead child.
11. **Dung thrown at Joffrey** — Someone throws dung that hits Joffrey's face and hair and splatters Sansa's legs.
12. **Joffrey demands retribution** — Joffrey offers a hundred golden dragons for the dung-thrower and sends the Hound after him.
13. **The riot erupts** — The crowd surges from both sides; stones, dung, and filth are thrown; cries of "Bastard," "Whore," "Freak," "Halfman," "Justice," "Robb, King Robb," "Stannis," "Renly," and above all "Bread, bread!"
14. **Tyrion orders retreat** — Tyrion spurs to Cersei's side and orders the return to the castle; Lancel unsheathes his sword.
15. **Ser Mandon amputates a hand** — A hand grasps Joffrey's leg; Ser Mandon slashes it off at the wrist.
16. **Aron Santagar killed** — Santagar is pulled from his saddle; the Baratheon banner is torn from him and ripped apart.
17. **Lannister banner destroyed** — Ser Balon Swann drops the Lannister lion banner to fight with his longsword; the banner is shredded.
18. **Joffrey rides someone down** — Joffrey's horse tramples an unidentified person in the street.
19. **The party reaches the Red Keep** — They clatter across the cobbled square and through the portcullis under cover of crossbowmen.
20. **Casualties assessed inside** — Ser Preston and Ser Aron not returned; Tyrek Lannister missing; Lollys missing; Sansa missing.
21. **Tyrion slaps and kicks Joffrey** — Tyrion slaps Joffrey hard enough to knock the crown from his head, shoves him down, kicks him, and calls him a "blind bloody fool" and "spoiled witless little boy."
22. **Cersei intervenes** — Cersei kneels over Joffrey; Ser Mandon pulls Tyrion off; Bronn restrains Tyrion.
23. **The Hound returns with Sansa** — Sandor Clegane rides in on Sansa's chestnut mare with Sansa behind him, arms around his chest; he reports killing at least one attacker and confirms Santagar is dead.
24. **Sansa treated for wounds** — Maester Frenken tends to the gash on Sansa's scalp. Clegane calls her "the little bird."
25. **Fire in Flea Bottom** — A lookout reports smoke and fire in the city.
26. **Tyrion dispatches forces** — Sends Bronn with men and water wagons to fight fires, with strict orders to protect the Guildhall of the Alchemists; sends Clegane with him.
27. **Clegane's moment of fear** — Tyrion glimpses fear in Clegane's eyes at the mention of fire, then it vanishes behind a scowl.
28. **Tyrion imposes curfew** — Orders the three remaining Kingsguard to escort heralds declaring anyone on the streets after the evenfall bell will be killed.
29. **Cersei defends Tyrion's authority** — When Ser Meryn balks, Cersei declares "The Hand speaks with the king's own voice, and disobedience is treason."
30. **Tyrion sends Shagga to protect Shae** — Tyrion wakes Shagga and orders him to the manse near the Iron Gate to guard Shae.
31. **Bywater delivers the butcher's bill** — High Septon ripped apart; Ser Preston stabbed and hacked beyond recognition; Ser Aron Santagar's head crushed inside his helm; Lollys gang-raped by dozens of men; Tyrek still missing; High Septon's crystal crown missing; nine gold cloaks killed, forty wounded; mob dead uncounted.
32. **Tyrion demands Tyrek be found** — Orders Bywater to find his cousin Tyrek alive or dead.
33. **Bywater warns of deeper crisis** — Reports: the bloody flux spreading in Pisswater Bend; no food available; open talk of treason in guildhalls and markets; the Watch unreliable with poorly trained recruits from Slynt's expansion.
34. **Bywater reveals Tyrion is most hated** — Tells Tyrion the smallfolk blame him most of all: for sellswords and savages, for exiling Slynt, for imprisoning Pycelle, and because he arrived after Robert's better times.
35. **Tyrion summons Varys and Bronn** — Sends Pod to fetch them for a late-night council.
36. **Bronn suggests replacing Joffrey with Tommen** — Bronn hints that Tommen would be a more tractable king. Tyrion recognizes the implication—Joffrey would have to die—and recoils, but cannot bring himself to act on it.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Moves through unease (about the fleet departure), amusement (at Cersei's Kettleblack scheme), irritation (at the High Septon's lengthy blessing), self-consciousness (walking the plank with his waddle), alarm (as the riot erupts), fury (at Joffrey's stupidity — physically assaults him), grim resolve (managing the aftermath), deep despair and self-pity (alone in the dark solar), and bitter resignation (Bywater's warning that he is hated most of all)
- **Primary preoccupation:** Managing multiple simultaneous crises — Myrcella's safe passage, the riot, fires, Sansa's safety (as leverage for Jaime), Tyrek's disappearance, the unreliable City Watch, and Stannis's impending attack — while being despised by the people he is trying to protect
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Sends the fleet with Myrcella via Braavos to avoid Dragonstone; (2) orders retreat to the castle during the riot; (3) imposes martial curfew — anyone on streets after evenfall bell will be killed; (4) dispatches Bronn and Clegane to contain fires and protect the wildfire stores; (5) sends Shagga to guard Shae; (6) orders Tyrek found alive or dead
- **Self-deception flags:** "Perhaps my lord father was right to despise me all these years" — self-pity that conflates political failure with personal worth. Also maintains confidence that his Kettleblack counter-scheme is working perfectly, assuming Cersei has "bought herself three hollow drums" — may be overestimating his own position. Acknowledges "I rely too much on Varys" but does not act to change this.
