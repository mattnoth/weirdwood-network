# ACOK — Bran IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 29

- **first_available:** ACOK Bran IV
- **location_primary:** Winterfell
- **location_secondary:** Greywater Watch (discussed), the Wall (mentioned), the Citadel of Oldtown (mentioned), Valyria (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** After the harvest feast; Ser Rodrik has departed east to deal with the Hornwood/Bolton situation; Bran's ninth name day has recently passed
- **time_markers:** "within a day or two of the harvest feast" (other lords departed); afternoon (tutoring time with Luwin); dusk (Meera visits Bran at his window seat); nighttime darkness (Bran in bed)
- **chapter_summary:** Meera Reed spars with Summer in the godswood while Bran watches. Jojen reveals his green dream of a winged wolf bound in stone chains and presses Bran about his wolf dreams, causing Summer and Shaggydog to become aggressive and tree the Reed siblings. Bran visits Maester Luwin, who dismisses greensight and magic, showing his Valyrian steel link and arguing magic has left the world. At dusk Meera tells Bran one of Jojen's dreams about supper, but at the actual meal nothing unusual occurs. Bran concludes there is no magic and despairs of ever walking or being a knight.

## Events & Actions
1. **Meera spars with Summer** — She circles with net and frog spear; Summer leaps into the net and knocks her down but becomes ensnared.
2. **Meera frees Summer from the net** — She undoes the tangles deftly and the direwolf bounds free.
3. **Bran wrestles with Summer** — They roll and play in the grass; Bran ends up on top.
4. **Bran discusses Greywater Watch with Meera** — She tells him it moves, ravens can't find it, they have no knights or maester.
5. **Bran considers visiting Greywater** — He thinks Ser Rodrik might allow it but Maester Luwin wouldn't.
6. **Jojen tells Bran he should leave Winterfell** — Says it would be good, and sooner rather than later.
7. **Jojen reveals his green dream** — A winged wolf bound to earth with grey stone chains; a three-eyed crow trying to peck through the chains.
8. **Bran admits the crow came to him** — After his fall, during his coma; the crow said fly or die.
9. **Jojen identifies Bran as the winged wolf** — Says the crow sent them to break Bran's chains; tells Bran to open his third eye.
10. **Jojen presses Bran about wolf dreams** — Asks specifically about the harvest feast night when Bran was Summer in the godswood; says he felt Bran in the wolf.
11. **Summer threatens Jojen** — Growling with no play; stalks forward aggressively.
12. **Shaggydog arrives** — Smells Summer's rage; lean black shape emerges from behind the weirwood.
13. **Meera and Jojen climb the weirwood** — Shaggy snaps at Meera's ankle; both wolves aggressive below.
14. **Bran calls Hodor** — Shouts for help; frightened and ashamed.
15. **Hodor chases off the wolves** — Waves arms, stamps feet, shouts "Hodor"; Shaggydog flees first, then Summer returns to Bran.
16. **Jojen promises they will talk again** — The Reeds depart the godswood.
17. **Bran visits Maester Luwin** — Arrives early for lessons; asks about greensight.
18. **Luwin explains greenseers** — Connected to weirwood faces; First Men believed they could see through the eyes of the trees; had power over beasts, birds, fish.
19. **Luwin shows his Valyrian steel link** — Explains it signifies study of the higher mysteries; admits he tried spells as a boy and failed.
20. **Luwin argues magic has left the world** — Calls it a wisp of smoke after a great fire; says Valyria was the last ember; dragons gone, giants dead, children forgotten.
21. **Meera visits Bran at dusk** — Tells him Jojen's dream about supper: Luwin serving Bran the king's cut (rare and bloody), and the Freys old grey meat; the Freys preferred theirs.
22. **Bran sits at supper** — Pigeon pie for everyone; nothing different for the Walders; Bran is relieved but disappointed.
23. **Bran despairs in bed** — Concludes there is no magic and he will never walk, fly, or be a knight.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Playful and happy at first (wrestling with Summer, enjoying the Reeds' company); increasingly anxious and defensive as Jojen presses about dreams; frightened and ashamed when the wolves attack; seeking reassurance from Luwin; relieved but disappointed after supper proves uneventful; despairing at chapter's end
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether his dreams mean something real (warging/greensight) or are just dreams; the memory of falling and the golden man; his inability to walk
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to fully engage with Jojen's claims about his abilities; goes to Maester Luwin for reassurance; chooses to accept Luwin's dismissal of magic (though with disappointment)
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran tells Jojen "I don't have dreams" when everyone in Winterfell knows he wakes screaming; tells himself "If he didn't talk about it, maybe he would forget" regarding the golden man; accepts Luwin's dismissal despite his own experiences (coma dream, the crow, shared dream with Rickon); claims "it was the wolves, it wasn't me" when Summer's aggression clearly mirrors his own emotions
