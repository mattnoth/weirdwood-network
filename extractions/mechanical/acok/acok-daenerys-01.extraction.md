- **time_markers:** "Three days into the march" (first death); "Two nights later" (infant death); "next day, dawn broke" (Vaes Tolorro discovered); "next morn" (Dany sends bloodriders); "Day followed night followed day" (recovery period at Vaes Tolorro); they ride by night and shelter by day; "a fortnight" (Jorah's happiness with Lynesse); "ten years" (Jorah's first marriage); "half a year" (Jorah's gold ran out in Lys); Dany is fourteen years old
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys leads her diminished khalasar into the red waste, following the comet shierak qiya because all other directions are closed to her. Many die during the march — an old man, an infant girl, horses, and finally her handmaid Doreah. She names her three dragons Drogon, Rhaegal, and Viserion, and discovers they will only eat charred meat. They find a dead white city they name Vaes Tolorro and begin to recover. Ser Jorah tells Dany the full story of his second wife Lynesse Hightower and his exile; Dany realizes Jorah loves her romantically. She sends her three bloodriders to scout in different directions, and Jhogo returns from the southeast with three strangers from Qarth: the warlock Pyat Pree, the merchant prince Xaro Xhoan Daxos, and the shadowbinder Quaithe.

## Events & Actions
1. **Comet guides direction** — Dany decides to follow the comet (shierak qiya) into the red waste, acknowledging it is the only direction open to her since Khal Pono blocks the route to Slaver's Bay, the Dothraki sea is too dangerous, and the Lhazareen lands are hostile.
2. **March begins** — Dany garbs herself in the hrakkar skin, mounts her silver mare with the cream-and-gold dragon on her shoulder, and leads her khalasar into the red waste. They ride by night and shelter by day.
3. **Deaths on the march** — Three days in, a toothless old man dies of exhaustion. Two nights later, an infant girl too young to ride perishes. Deaths continue — weak children, old women, the sick.
4. **Supplies exhausted** — Wine runs out first, then mare's milk, then flatbread and dried meat. Only dead horse flesh remains. Doreah wastes away.
5. **Dany's milk dries up** — Her breast milk fails; her nipples crack and bleed; she becomes lean and hard.
6. **Dragon feeding discovered** — The dragons refuse raw horsemeat. Dany recalls Viserys telling her that only dragons and men eat cooked meat. When handmaids char the meat black, the dragons eat eagerly and begin to grow.
7. **Dragons named** — Dany names the green dragon Rhaegal for Rhaegar, the cream-and-gold Viserion for Viserys, and the black Drogon for Drogo. Aggo says the black one is Balerion come again.
8. **Doreah dies** — Doreah develops fever, blood blisters on lips and hands, hair falls out. She can no longer mount her horse. Dany stays with her, gives her water, holds her hand until she dies shivering.
9. **Dany questions Jorah about being lost** — Jorah says great kingdoms lie to the east — Yi Ti, Qarth, Asshai by the Shadow — but admits the way is harder than he expected. He says they are doomed if they turn back.
10. **Dany kisses Jorah's cheek** — She kisses him lightly; heartened to see him smile.
11. **Brimstone pool** — They find a scalding, brimstone-stinking pool; cool the water and drink it despite the foul taste.
12. **Vaes Tolorro discovered** — At dawn, outriders report a pale city an hour's ride away. Dany and her people approach and find it deserted, gates broken, only wind and flies inside.
13. **Khalasar enters city** — Despite Jhiqui and Irri's warnings about ghosts, Dany leads them inside. They camp before a gutted palace on a windswept plaza.
14. **Resources found** — An old man returns with figs; others find fruit trees behind closed doors; Aggo finds a courtyard with grapes; Jhogo discovers a well with pure cold water. Bones and skulls of the dead also found.
15. **Dany bathes** — Irri and Jhiqui scrub her with clean white sand. Jhiqui notes Dany's hair is coming back.
16. **Rhaegal tries to fly** — In the tent, Rhaegal unfolds green wings and flaps half a foot before thumping down, lashing his tail in fury and screaming.
17. **Jorah tells his story** — He brings Dany a peach and tells the full story of his life: Bear Island, his first wife (a Glover), the Greyjoy Rebellion, winning the tourney at Lannisport, marrying Lynesse Hightower, her unhappiness on Bear Island, his debts and dishonorable acts for gold, fleeing Eddard Stark's justice to Lys, Lynesse becoming concubine to Tregar Ormollen.
18. **Jorah reveals Lynesse looked like Dany** — When asked what Lynesse looked like, Jorah says she looked a bit like Daenerys.
19. **Dany realizes Jorah loves her** — She understands he wants her not as a knight loves his queen but as a man loves a woman. She tries to imagine herself with him but sees only Drogo.
20. **Dany reflects on her barrenness** — She recalls Mirri Maz Duur's curse that she would never bear a living child, and wonders what man would want a barren wife.
21. **Dany resolves to restore Jorah's home** — She thinks she can never be his but can one day give him back Bear Island and his honor.
22. **Dany dreams of riding dragons** — She dreams of Drogo and their wedding night, but riding dragons instead of horses.
23. **Bloodriders dispatched** — Dany sends Aggo southwest, Rakharo due south, and Jhogo southeast following the comet, each with three horses loaded with water and food, to scout what lies beyond the waste.
24. **Recovery at Vaes Tolorro** — The city is named Vaes Tolorro (city of bones). Women harvest fruit. Men mend saddles and groom horses. Children explore. One woman stung by a red scorpion (only death). Horses gain flesh. Dany tends Jorah's wound.
25. **Rakharo returns** — Reports the red waste ends at a bleak shore beside poison water, with nothing in between except sand, rocks, thorny plants, and the immense bones of a dragon.
26. **Dany orders plaza torn up** — She sets a dozen men to pulling up paving stones so grass can be planted; they have wells and water enough.
27. **Aggo returns** — Reports southwest is barren and burnt; two more dead cities found, one warded by skulls on iron spears. He brings an iron bracelet with an uncut fire opal and reports crumbling scrolls left behind.
28. **Dany orders gates repaired** — If enemies destroyed these cities before, they may come again.
29. **Jhogo returns with strangers** — After being gone so long Dany feared him lost, Jhogo returns from the southeast with three strangers riding humped creatures (camels). He has been to Qarth.
30. **Three strangers introduce themselves** — Pyat Pree (warlock), Xaro Xhoan Daxos (merchant prince of the Thirteen), and Quaithe of the Shadow announce they have come seeking dragons. Dany declares they have found them.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined and stoic despite fear and grief; resolved to be strong for her people ("I must show no fear, no weakness, no doubt"); grieving Drogo, Viserys, Rhaego, her parents, Ser Willem Darry, Doreah; feels older than her fourteen years; brief despair at the waste's endlessness ("The comet mocks my hopes"); fierce possessiveness over her dragons; sexual confusion when realizing Jorah's feelings — she cannot imagine anyone but Drogo
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival of her khalasar and her dragons; finding a way out of the red waste; being the strength her people need
- **Key decisions made:** Follows the comet into the red waste; stays with Doreah until she dies rather than abandoning her; names the dragons; enters Vaes Tolorro despite superstitious warnings; decides to settle and recover rather than push on blindly; sends three bloodriders to scout in different directions; orders plaza torn up for planting; orders gates repaired for defense
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself the comet is "the herald of my coming" sent by the gods, while acknowledging internally it is the only way open to her; maintains the facade of strength while frightened inside ("However frightened my heart, when they look upon my face they must see only Drogo's queen")
