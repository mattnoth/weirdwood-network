# ACOK — Davos I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 11
- **pov_character:** Davos Seaworth
- **pov_chapter_number:** Davos I
- **first_available:** ACOK Davos I
- **location_primary:** Dragonstone
- **location_secondary:** The port at Dragonstone, an inn on the stone pier, the Stone Drum, the Chamber of the Painted Table; referenced: King's Landing, Storm's End, Lys, Flea Bottom, Cape Wrath, Casterly Rock, Harrenhal, Highgarden, Gulltown, the Fingers, the Three Sisters, White Harbor, the Broken Arm, Dorne, the Arbor, Braavos, the Free Cities, Pentos, Myr, the Wall, Valyria, Asshai
- **approximate_timeline:** Early in the book; Stannis has recently arrived at Dragonstone and is preparing his claim to the Iron Throne. Maester Cressen has recently died. The burning of the Seven marks the public adoption of R'hllor.
- **time_markers:** Morning (the burning ceremony); "well after dark" (Devan's summons to Stannis); a year ago Davos was in King's Landing for Prince Joffrey's name day tourney; for ten years Ser Axell served as castellan of Dragonstone; "come the black of the moon" (Salladhor Saan's payment due); thirty thousand dragons owed
- **chapter_summary:** Davos watches Stannis burn the statues of the Seven outside Dragonstone's gates as Melisandre performs a ritual in which Stannis pulls a sword from the fire, proclaimed as Lightbringer. Davos meets Salladhor Saan at an inn, learning intelligence about King's Landing's weak defenses, Tyrion's arrival there, and Renly's march from Highgarden. That evening, Stannis summons Davos to the Chamber of the Painted Table, reveals his letter declaring Joffrey, Tommen, and Myrcella to be products of incest, and orders Davos and his sons to distribute copies by ship along the coasts. The chapter ends with Stannis explaining why he has turned to Melisandre and R'hllor, using the parable of his childhood goshawk Proudwing.

## Events & Actions
1. **Burning of the Seven** — Melisandre leads a ceremony burning the wooden statues of the Seven outside Dragonstone's castle gates. She prays in three languages (Asshai speech, High Valyrian, Common Tongue), calling on R'hllor to accept the "false gods."
2. **Davos recalls the desecration of the sept** — Queen's men overturned altars, pulled down statues, and smashed stained glass. Ser Hubard Rambton and his three sons fought to defend the sept, killing four queen's men before being overwhelmed. One Rambton son died. Guncer Sunglass withdrew his support for Stannis and was imprisoned along with Septon Barre and the two surviving Rambton sons.
3. **Davos recalls Cressen's death** — He saw Maester Cressen slip poison into a wine cup to kill Melisandre, but R'hllor (or Melisandre's power) shielded her. Cressen died instead.
4. **Stannis pulls the sword from the fire** — Wearing a padded glove and leather cape, Stannis wades into the fire, grasps a longsword thrust through the Mother's heart, and pulls it free. Guards beat out cinders on his clothing. Queen Selyse and the queen's men shout "A sword of fire!"
5. **Melisandre proclaims Azor Ahai reborn** — She declares the sword is Lightbringer and Stannis is Azor Ahai come again.
6. **Stannis's glove smolders** — He thrusts the sword into the earth to extinguish it and beats out flames on his leg; the sword's flames dwindle quickly.
7. **Stannis and Selyse depart** — The king escorts the queen back into Dragonstone, leaving Lightbringer stuck in the ground. Devan and Byren Farring roll up the sword in the burned leather cloak.
8. **Davos walks to the port with Allard and Dale** — They discuss the burning; Allard objects to burning the Seven; Dale notes his water casks are green pine. The queen's men have claimed all seasoned wood.
9. **Davos enters the inn** — He pats the eroded gargoyle outside for luck and enters the crowded common room.
10. **Davos meets Salladhor Saan** — Saan shares intelligence: Tyrion Lannister has been sent to King's Landing, has replaced the gold cloaks commander with a knight with an iron hand, the city's defenses are weak; Renly has left Highgarden with his queen and army, marching up the Roseroad toward King's Landing.
11. **Salladhor Saan tells the tale of Lightbringer** — He recounts the legend of Azor Ahai forging Lightbringer through three attempts, culminating in driving the sword through Nissa Nissa's heart. He concludes the sword Stannis pulled was not truly Lightbringer.
12. **Davos declares "King Stannis is my god"** — When pressed about his own faith, Davos deflects by declaring Stannis his god.
13. **Saan departs** — He reminds Davos that Stannis owes him another thirty thousand dragons by the black of the moon and jokes about wanting Queen Cersei.
14. **Davos reflects on Thoros of Myr** — He recalls the red priest's flaming sword at Joffrey's name day tourney, which was no true magic; Bronze Yohn Royce defeated him.
15. **Devan summons Davos** — After dark, Devan brings a snow-white palfrey and tells Davos the king commands his presence at the Chamber of the Painted Table.
16. **Davos arrives at the Stone Drum** — Lords are leaving; Celtigar and Velaryon give curt nods; Ser Axell Florent stops to speak, describing a vision of dancing maidens in the flames and declaring it a sign of coming glory.
17. **Stannis presents his letter** — Maester Pylos reads the letter aloud (Davos cannot read). It declares Joffrey, Tommen, and Myrcella abominations of incest and claims the Iron Throne for Stannis. Stannis orders removal of "beloved brother" as a lie and addition of "Ser" before Jaime's name.
18. **Stannis assigns Davos to distribute letters** — 117 copies go by raven. Davos is to sail Black Betha north (Gulltown, the Fingers, the Three Sisters, White Harbor). Dale sails Wraith south (Cape Wrath, the Broken Arm, Dorne, the Arbor). Allard takes Lady Marya to Braavos and the Free Cities.
19. **Davos advises Stannis on the letter** — He suggests replacing "Done in the Light of the Lord" with language about the old gods or the Seven, warning Stannis's people will resent the new god.
20. **Stannis explains his turn to R'hllor** — He stopped believing in gods when the Windproud broke up and drowned his parents. He recounts the parable of Proudwing, a goshawk he nursed that would never soar, and Robert's gyrfalcon Thunderclap. His great-uncle Ser Harbert told him to try a different bird. Stannis declares: "The Seven have never brought me so much as a sparrow. It is time I tried another hawk, Davos. A red hawk."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply uneasy about the burning of the Seven; feels ill watching it; loyal to Stannis but conflicted about R'hllor; cautious and watchful around the other lords; aware of his lowborn status and insecure about his place among highborn lords; grimly resigned to the coming war
- **Primary preoccupation:** His debt and loyalty to Stannis versus his discomfort with Melisandre and the new religion; whether Stannis can win with so few allies; the gap between himself and the highborn lords
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees to sail north distributing letters; decides to speak honestly to Stannis about the letter's religious language; chooses diplomatic caution with Ser Axell Florent; declines to speak R'hllor's words during the ceremony
- **Self-deception flags:** Davos tells himself "King Stannis is my god" to deflect Salladhor Saan's question, but this is a calculated evasion rather than true belief — he is aware of this ("The inn is crowded, and you are not Salladhor Saan, he reminded himself. Be careful how you answer"). He also thinks "We have no hope of victory" but continues to serve loyally, not confronting this conviction directly with Stannis.
