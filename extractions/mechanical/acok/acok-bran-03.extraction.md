# ACOK — Bran III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 22
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran III
- **first_available:** ACOK Bran III
- **location_primary:** Winterfell, Great Hall
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell godswood (wolf dream), Bran's bedchamber tower
- **approximate_timeline:** During the harvest feast at Winterfell, while Robb is fighting in the south
- **time_markers:** Evening feast (torches lit, dancing afterward); "much later" sweets served and dancing begins; Bran goes to bed and blows out bedside candle; darkness; dream occurs at night while man-pack howls at play; moon referenced as rising soon
- **chapter_summary:** Bran rides Dancer into Winterfell's Great Hall to preside over the harvest feast as the Stark in Winterfell. He performs his lordly duties, sending food to guests and making toasts. Meera and Jojen Reed arrive unexpectedly to renew their house's fealty oath. After the feast, Bran retires and recalls his father's words about Ser Arthur Dayne and Howland Reed. He falls asleep and has a wolf dream as Summer, encountering the Reed siblings in the godswood.

- **Weather:** Cold air gusts in when doors open at foot of hall
- **Season indicators:** Harvest feast celebrating the bounty; "chilled autumn ale" served; Bran's memory mentions "Summer still reigned then" during Robert's visit, implying it no longer does; reference to steam rising off hot pools in godswood
- **Time of day:** Evening feast through night; Bran retires to bed after dancing begins; blows out bedside candle
- **Lighting:** Torches in the Great Hall (flame brighter when doors open and cold air gusts in); dim-lit gallery outside the Great Hall; bedside candle; darkness when candle blown out

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran rides Dancer into the Great Hall** — Dancer wears Stark bardings; Bran wears grey and white; low stone steps balked Dancer only momentarily; men cheer "Stark!" and "Winterfell!" as he rides the length of the hall.
2. **Bran is lifted to the high seat** — Osha and Hodor undo straps and buckles, lift him off Dancer, carry him to the high seat of his fathers.
3. **Bran opens the feast** — Bids welcome in Robb's name, asks thanks for victories and harvest, raises father's silver goblet; all cheer "A hundred more!"
4. **Feast is served** — Enormous courses brought; Bran takes lord's portion first, eventually can eat no more and waves courses away.
5. **Bran sends food to guests** — Salmon to Lady Hornwood, boar to Umbers, goose-in-berries to Cley Cerwyn, lobster to Joseth, sweets to Hodor and Old Nan, beets to Little Walder, turnips to Big Walder.
6. **Osha breaks flagon over groping soldier** — One of Leobald Tallhart's men slides hand up her skirts; she breaks the ale flagon on his head to roars of laughter.
7. **Bran has a vivid waking dream** — He envisions the godswood: steam, red weirwood leaves, moon rising, his "brother" singing. Ser Rodrik notices Bran is not eating.
8. **Meera and Jojen Reed arrive** — Alebelly announces them; doors open with gust of cold air; they walk the length of the hall.
9. **The Reeds swear fealty** — Ancient oath pledging "hearth and heart and harvest" to Winterfell; sworn by earth and water, bronze and iron, ice and fire. Bran accepts awkwardly.
10. **Jojen asks about the direwolves** — Rickon says they're in the godswood because "Shaggy was bad"; Meera says her brother would like to see them.
11. **Bran commands food for the Reeds** — Mutton chops, aurochs, beef-and-barley stew.
12. **Dancing begins** — Tables shoved back; Hother Umber blows warhorn; Mors Umber dances first with serving girl; Lord Wyman dances with Beth; Cley Cerwyn dances with Beth; Ser Rodrik asks Lady Hornwood who refuses and leaves; Hodor dances alone.
13. **Bran asks to leave** — Dancing makes him sad because he can never do it; calls for Hodor.
14. **Bran carried out through lord's door** — People stare as he passes in Hodor's basket; they encounter Joseth with a woman in the gallery.
15. **Hodor carries Bran to bedchamber** — Uses iron bars to move to bed; Hodor removes boots and breeches; Bran sends Hodor back to feast.
16. **Bran recalls Ned's words about Ser Arthur Dayne** — "The finest knight I ever saw"; Dawn forged from fallen star; "the Sword of the Morning"; "would have killed me but for Howland Reed."
17. **Bran falls asleep and enters wolf dream** — As Summer, he prowls beneath godswood trees with Shaggydog. They scent strangers and find Meera and Jojen, who show no fear.
18. **Jojen reaches out to touch Summer** — Says "He won't hurt me. This is not the day I die." At his touch, the wolf dream dissolves and Bran falls.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pride at being cheered; sadness at all who are gone; loneliness and alienation (watching "as from a distance," part of nothing); discomfort at being carried in public; frustration at his broken body; warmth toward Old Nan and Hodor; curiosity about the Reeds; sadness at dancing he can never do; wistful longing for knighthood
- **Primary preoccupation:** Performing his role as Stark in Winterfell while feeling inadequate and isolated; missing his family; the weight of absence
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts the Reeds' fealty oath; commands food served to them; decides to leave the feast when dancing begins
- **Self-deception flags:** "I want to be a knight" — desire he knows is impossible but still holds; tries to suppress emotion ("He might have cried then, but he couldn't")
