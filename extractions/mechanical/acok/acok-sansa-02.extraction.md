# ACOK — Sansa II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 19
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark

- **first_available:** ACOK Sansa II
- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Maegor's Holdfast, the godswood, the serpentine steps, the yard, the stables, the river walk, the small kitchen, the pig yard
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Tyrion sent Janos Slynt to the Wall; during a period of increasing unrest in King's Landing with refugees crowding in from the war
- **time_markers:** Night ("this time of night," "in the black of night"); the day had been hot; Sansa's bruise from Ser Meryn has faded from purple to yellow (days to weeks old); the queen changes Sansa's servants every fortnight; distant shouting and commotion occur during the night, described as "fools at the gate" wanting a share of Tyrek's wedding feast
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa discovers an anonymous note under her pillow telling her to come to the godswood if she wants to go home. She agonizes over whether it is genuine or a trap, burns the note, and waits. When nighttime commotion draws away the Kingsguard from Maegor's drawbridge, she arms herself with a knife and slips out to the godswood. There she discovers the note was from Ser Dontos Hollard, the knight-turned-fool she saved from Joffrey. Dontos swears a vow before the heart tree to send her home, and they agree to meet secretly in the godswood. Returning, Sansa encounters a drunken Sandor Clegane on the serpentine steps, who mocks her and escorts her back to Maegor's Holdfast, where Ser Boros Blount now holds the bridge.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa discovers anonymous note** — Finds a folded sheet of parchment under her pillow reading "Come to the godswood tonight, if you want to go home." Unsigned, unsealed, in an unfamiliar hand.
2. **Sansa agonizes over the note** — Rereads it a hundred times; considers taking it to the queen to prove loyalty; speculates about who sent it (Redwyne twins, Ser Balon Swann, Beric Dondarrion); fears it may be a trap from Joffrey or Ser Ilyn Payne.
3. **Bedmaid visits** — The mousy bedmaid offers a bath; Sansa hides the note under her sheet and requests a fire instead. Sansa suspects the maid of spying.
4. **Sansa burns the note** — Thrusts the parchment into the fire and watches it curl and blacken.
5. **Sansa observes the drawbridge** — Sees Ser Preston Greenfield guarding the drawbridge of Maegor's Holdfast; realizes she cannot leave without being questioned.
6. **Sansa goes to bed but cannot sleep** — Unlaces her gown and lies in bed, tormented by the decision.
7. **Nighttime commotion erupts** — Sansa hears shouting, horses, pounding feet, and shouts of command. Men run on the walls carrying spears and torches.
8. **Sansa sees the bridge is undefended** — Ser Preston has left his post; the drawbridge is down.
9. **Sansa arms herself and slips out** — Dresses, throws on a grey cloak, takes her meat knife, and crosses the undefended drawbridge.
10. **Sansa crosses the yard** — Red-cloaked swordsmen run past; men saddle horses; Sansa glimpses Ser Preston with three Kingsguard helping Joffrey into his armor. Joffrey shouts for his sword and crossbow. He does not see her.
11. **Sansa climbs the serpentine steps** — Panting at the top, she presses against a wall in a shadowy colonnade. A ragged black cat with a chewed-off ear spits at her and leaps away.
12. **Sansa reaches the godswood** — The noises have faded. She reflects on Lady, on the old gods vs. the Seven, and prays for a friend and true knight.
13. **Ser Dontos reveals himself** — Steps from the shadows; Sansa recognizes him by his blotchy skin. She is heartbroken that it is not a true knight.
14. **Tense exchange** — Sansa draws her knife on Dontos; he says he only wants to help as she helped him. She calls him a drunken old fool; he says she has helped him remember what knighthood means.
15. **Dontos invokes Florian** — References the tale that "another fool once was the greatest knight of all." Sansa whispers "Florian." Dontos says he would be her Florian, kneeling before her.
16. **Sansa lowers the knife** — Decides to trust him despite misgivings.
17. **Dontos outlines a plan** — Getting her out of the castle will be hardest; ships can take her home once outside. He needs time to find coin and make arrangements. Not tonight.
18. **Dontos swears a solemn vow** — Places his hand on the heart tree and vows before the old gods to send Sansa home. He is visibly shaking.
19. **They agree on future communication** — Must meet only in the godswood, nowhere else. "The stones have ears in the Red Keep." He warns they must both play roles and show no connection in public.
20. **Sansa kisses Dontos on the cheek** — Calls him "My Florian" and whispers "The gods heard my prayer."
21. **Sansa runs back through the castle** — Along the river walk, past the small kitchen, through the pig yard with squealing hogs.
22. **Sansa encounters the Hound** — A man lurches from a hidden doorway on the serpentine steps; iron fingers catch her wrist. Sandor Clegane, drunk, demands to know where she was.
23. **Sansa lies to the Hound** — Claims she was in the godswood praying for her father and the king. He does not believe her.
24. **The Hound's drunken monologue** — Comments that she looks almost a woman; demands a song; mocks "true knights"; is reeling drunk.
25. **The Hound escorts Sansa to Maegor's Holdfast** — Pushes her "oddly gentle"; falls into brooding silence on the way down.
26. **Encounter with Ser Boros Blount** — Now guarding the bridge. The Hound insults him ("Paint stripes on a toad, he does not become a tiger"). Ser Boros questions Sansa's presence.
27. **Sansa repeats her lie** — Says she went to the godswood to pray for the king's safety. The lie sounds better this time, "almost true."
28. **Ser Boros reports the commotion** — The disturbance was caused by smallfolk who heard about Tyrek's wedding feast and demanded to be fed. Joffrey led a sortie to scatter them.
29. **The Hound tells the story of House Clegane** — His grandfather was kennelmaster at the Rock; saved Lord Tytos from a lioness; lost a leg; was given lands, a towerhouse, and his son taken as squire. The three dogs on the Clegane banner are the three hounds that died saving Lord Tytos, in the yellow of autumn grass.
30. **The Hound demands a song** — Sansa offers Florian and Jonquil; the Hound dismisses it as "A fool and his cunt" but says one day he'll have a song from her.
31. **The Hound calls Sansa a bad liar** — "A dog can smell a lie... They're all liars here... and every one better than you."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear and hope in constant tension. Sansa oscillates between desperate desire to escape and paranoid fear that the note is a trap. She feels heartbreak when she discovers Dontos, not a "true knight," is her rescuer — but cautious hope wins out. She is frightened of Joffrey, the Hound, and Ser Boros. She also feels grief for her father, Septa Mordane, Jeyne Poole, and Lady. The chapter ends with her vulnerable and exposed — the Hound has seen through her lies.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether to trust the note and go to the godswood; then whether to trust Dontos; then fear of being caught.
- **Key decisions made:** Burns the note rather than taking it to the queen. Arms herself and goes to the godswood when the opportunity arises. Chooses to trust Dontos despite her doubts. Lies to the Hound and Ser Boros about praying.
- **Self-deception flags:** Sansa believes Arya is safe at Winterfell, "dancing and sewing, playing with Bran and baby Rickon." She romanticizes Dontos as "my Florian" almost immediately after calling him a drunken old fool — she needs the narrative of rescue so badly she maps the Florian-and-Jonquil template onto her situation. She tells herself her bruise was "her own fault" — she has internalized the idea that she must not anger Joffrey, suggesting Stockholm-syndrome-like adaptation to abuse.
