# ACOK — Tyrion V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 21
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion V
- **first_available:** ACOK Tyrion V
- **location_primary:** King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Guildhall of the Alchemists (vaults beneath), Gate of the Gods, Red Keep (Tyrion's solar), Cobbler's Square; referenced: Riverrun, Harrenhal, Casterly Rock, Sunspear, Dorne, the riverlands, Great Sept of Baelor
- **approximate_timeline:** Mid-ACOK; War of the Five Kings ongoing, Renly marching north, Stannis's fleet feared, Robb at Riverrun, Tywin at Harrenhal
- **time_markers:** "Only last year" (200 jars found beneath Great Sept); "Only three nights past" (food mob at Red Keep gates); "this morning" (Wisdom Munciter's count); iron torches "lit this morning" for Tyrion's visit; progression from underground vaults through daytime streets to Tyrion's solar
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion inspects the wildfire stockpile beneath the Guildhall of the Alchemists, learning there are 7,840 jars including 4,000 volatile ones from King Aerys's reign. He orders empty clay pots sent to the city gates for training spitfire crews. At the Gate of the Gods, he receives his cousin Ser Cleos Frey, who has arrived from Riverrun under a peace banner with Robb Stark's unacceptable peace terms. Tyrion plans to string Robb along with counter-proposals while Ser Stafford raises fresh levies at the Rock. Returning through the city, he witnesses a prophet denouncing incest and corruption. At the Red Keep, Cersei confronts him furiously about his plan to betroth Myrcella to Trystane Martell in Dorne, but Tyrion presses the case and learns which of his informer-traps has been sprung.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion descends into the wildfire vaults** — Dressed warmly in quilted breeches, woolen doublet, and shadowskin cloak, Tyrion enters the cold dank vaults beneath the hill of Rhaenys with Hallyne the Pyromancer. Timett retreats back to the cellar from the cold.
2. **Tyrion inspects a wildfire jar** — He handles a round, ruddy clay jar, notes its thin fragile pottery and roughened surface. The wildfire inside is thick from the cold, murky green.
3. **Hallyne explains wildfire properties** — The substance burns fiercely, cannot be quenched with water, seeps into cloth/wood/leather/steel. It grows more volatile with age.
4. **Hallyne reveals the old stockpile** — A lower vault stores fruit-shaped jars from King Aerys's day, sealed with wax and flooded with water. Many masters were murdered during the Sack of King's Landing, leaving acolytes unable to destroy the old stock. Last year, 200 jars were found beneath the Great Sept of Baelor.
5. **Wildfire count disclosed** — Wisdom Munciter counted 7,840 jars this morning, including 4,000 from Aerys's reign. Wisdom Malliard believes they will reach the promised 10,000.
6. **Tyrion requests empty clay pots** — He asks for thousands of empty jars to be sent to the captains at each city gate for training.
7. **Hallyne describes wildfire production safety** — Bare stone cells, sand rooms above with collapsible floors to smother fires, the substance is in pyromancers' veins. He warns about common soldiers' carelessness with spitfires.
8. **Ascent through the Gallery of the Iron Torches** — They exit through a grand hall with twenty-foot columns of green fire dancing around black metal, shimmering off polished black marble. Tyrion notes the torches were only lit for his visit.
9. **Tyrion exits onto the Street of the Sisters** — He bids Hallyne farewell at the broad curving steps near the foot of Visenya's Hill.
10. **Bronn delivers messages** — Bronn reports that Ser Jacelyn wants Tyrion urgently at the Gate of the Gods, and Cersei has summoned him to Maegor's. Lancel delivered Cersei's message.
11. **Tyrion chooses Bywater over Cersei** — He deliberately delays responding to Cersei's summons, preferring her angry and stupid to composed and cunning.
12. **Tyrion meets Ser Cleos Frey** — At the Gate of the Gods gatehouse, Ser Cleos (gaunt, haggard) presents Robb Stark's peace terms under a peace banner.
13. **Ser Cleos reports on the riverlands** — Conditions around the Gods Eye and kingsroad are terrible: river lords burn their own crops, Tywin's foragers torch villages and put smallfolk to the sword. His escort was attacked twice by brigands; lost three men, twice as many wounded.
14. **Tyrion reads and dismisses Stark's terms** — Demands include half the realm, release of captives, hostages, his father's sword, and his sisters. Tyrion declares them unacceptable but sees a golden opportunity to delay.
15. **Tyrion refuses Cleos's request to exchange Stark girls for Tion and Willem** — He says they will propose their own exchange terms.
16. **Cleos reveals Lady Catelyn drives the peace push** — It is Lady Catelyn who wants peace, not Robb; she wants her daughters back.
17. **Tyrion instructs Bywater on wildfire training** — Empty pots with green paint for loading/firing drills; replace anyone who splatters. Then lamp oil with lit jars. Only then real wildfire.
18. **Tyrion privately strategizes** — Plans to string Robb along with counter-proposals while Ser Stafford trains a new host at Casterly Rock. Then Stafford and Tywin smash the Tullys and Starks between them.
19. **Tyrion worries about Renly and Stannis** — Renly creeps north and east with his huge host; Stannis's fleet threatens the Blackwater Rush.
20. **Tyrion passes through Cobbler's Square** — A begging brother preaches to a sizable crowd, pointing at the comet and the Red Keep, denouncing incest in the royal bed, the "twisted little monkey demon," the corrupt High Septon, and calling for cleansing by fire. The crowd mostly hoots in derision.
21. **Tyrion returns to Red Keep** — He feels hopeful; thinks about "the chain" and needing time.
22. **Cersei confronts Tyrion in his solar** — She has entered without permission (Crawn's Moon Brothers had the guard). She is furious about the Myrcella-Dorne marriage plan.
23. **Cersei strikes Tyrion (first slap)** — She knocks the wine cup from his hand when he mockingly suggests marrying Myrcella to Tommen.
24. **Tyrion argues for the Dornish match** — Myrcella would be safer in Dorne; terms include Elia's killer, a council seat, castles on the Marches; Ser Arys Oakheart would go as sworn shield.
25. **Cersei strikes Tyrion (second slap)** — He sees it coming; promises it was the last time she will ever strike him.
26. **Cersei threatens Tyrion** — Compares his paper authority to Eddard Stark's; Tyrion reflects that unlike Ned, he has the City Watch, his clansmen, and Bronn's sellswords.
27. **Cersei weeps** — Tyrion is astonished; has not seen her cry since childhood at Casterly Rock. She pushes him away when he tries to comfort her.
28. **Cersei expresses frustration** — Wishes she had been born a man; criticizes Jaime for being captured, Tywin for hiding at Harrenhal.
29. **Tyrion explains Tywin's strategy** — Uses lion-and-fawn metaphor: Tywin at Harrenhal is the poised lion; Robb is the frozen fawn. Bolton's foot cannot cross the Trident. Stafford gathers levies at the Rock.
30. **Tyrion presents Stark's peace terms** — Gives Cersei the letter, winning her cooperation on the Dornish marriage. Notes his cheek still throbs from her slap.
31. **Tyrion identifies an informer** — He reflects that his informer trap has been sprung: "certain knowledge of an informer too . . . well, that was the plum in his pudding."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Alternates between cautious optimism and anxiety. Pleased by the wildfire stockpile but aware of its dangers. Amused by the prophet's rantings but stung by "twisted little monkey demon." Annoyed by Cersei's confrontation, genuinely surprised by her tears, hurt when she rejects his comfort. Ends the chapter feeling hopeful and strategically satisfied.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Defense of King's Landing on multiple fronts — the wildfire program, military strategy against Renly/Stannis/Robb, managing Cersei, and identifying his informer. The cryptic reference to "the chain" suggests a major defense project underway.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Requests empty clay pots for spitfire crew training — a practical safety measure. (2) Goes to Bywater first, deliberately making Cersei wait and angry. (3) Plans to string Robb along with counter-proposals to buy time for Stafford's army. (4) Presses the Dornish marriage alliance despite Cersei's opposition. (5) Orders Cleos's escort kept outside the city walls to prevent intelligence leaks.
- **Self-deception flags:** Acknowledges he may be deluding himself about his support: "Trusting in Varys, in Ser Jacelyn Bywater, in Bronn. Lord Stark had probably had his delusions as well." Tells Cersei Myrcella will be safe ("I promise you, nothing will happen to Myrcella") while privately framing her as a strategic hostage. His optimism about Tywin's strategy is internally questioned: "And you're quite certain that Father is the lion?" is Cersei's challenge he answers glibly but does not fully resolve internally.
