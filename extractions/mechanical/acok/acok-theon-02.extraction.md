At the feast, Asha humiliates Theon publicly with an axe and dirk demonstration. Afterward, Lord Balon convenes a war council revealing his invasion plan: Theon is assigned eight longships to harry the Stony Shore, Asha receives thirty to take Deepwood Motte, and Victarion commands the main thrust to seize Moat Cailin.

## Events & Actions
1. **Theon inspects his new longship** — He visits the strand at Lordsport to see the ship Sigrin built for him before its blessing the next day.
2. **Theon meets "Esgred"** — A woman approaches and flirts with him; she identifies herself as Esgred, daughter of Ambrode and wife of shipwright Sigrin.
3. **Extended flirtation** — Theon and Esgred engage in witty, sexually charged banter; she touches his breeches, unlaces them, then steps away.
4. **Naming the Sea Bitch** — Esgred calls the ship a "sea bitch" and Theon declares that her name.
5. **Walk through Lordsport** — They pass through the crowded town; Esgred greets Bluetooth, Uller, Qarl, and others; Theon notes townfolk bow their heads to him.
6. **Passing the Myraham** — The captain's daughter calls out to Theon from the cog; Esgred notices and comments.
7. **Retrieving Wex from the inn** — Theon finds his mute squire dicing and winning; pulls him away by the ear.
8. **Ride back to Pyke** — Theon and Esgred ride double on Smiler; he gropes her breast and she removes his hand; he tells her stories of war and Winterfell.
9. **Arrival at Pyke** — Dogs rush past Theon to mob Esgred; a stableman addresses her as "Lady Asha."
10. **Asha's identity revealed** — Theon realizes "Esgred" is his sister Asha Greyjoy; she explains she wanted to see who he was first.
11. **Theon's humiliation** — Theon retreats to Guest Keep, drinks wine, stares at the sea, and broods on his situation.
12. **Feast in the Great Hall** — Nearly four hundred lords and captains; finger dancing; Asha sits in the place of honor at Balon's right hand; Theon sits beside her.
13. **Asha's axe-and-dirk performance** — Asha catches a thrown axe, splits Theon's trencher, and draws a dirk from her bodice, calling them her "lord husband" and "sweet suckling babe," mocking Theon's earlier words. The hall roars with laughter at Theon.
14. **Asha lectures Theon** — She tells him he knows nothing and no one on the islands and questions why men should fight for him.
15. **War council in Sea Tower solar** — Lord Balon announces his invasion plan: Theon gets eight longships to harry the Stony Shore (with Aeron and Dagmer Cleftjaw), Asha gets thirty to take Deepwood Motte, Victarion commands the main thrust up Saltspear and Fever River to seize Moat Cailin.
16. **Theon protests** — He objects to only eight ships and tries to offer suggestions; Balon silences him.
17. **Crossing the rope bridge** — Theon stumbles to his knees on the rain-slick bridge; Asha helps him up. They exchange their final lines: he liked her better as Esgred; she liked him better at age nine.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Shifts dramatically — lustful confidence during flirtation; shock and humiliation at Asha's reveal; brooding anger and self-pity in Guest Keep; resentful fury at the feast; wounded pride during war council; drunken despair at chapter's end
- **Primary preoccupation:** His status and recognition on the Iron Islands; whether he truly belongs after ten years away; his claim vs. his uncles' and Asha's position
- **Key decisions made:** Names his ship Sea Bitch; invites "Esgred" to Pyke; decides to attend feast despite humiliation; bites his tongue during war council (mostly)
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes townfolk bowing to him means they've "finally learned who I am" (they may be bowing to Asha); assumes he can easily marry off Asha and manage his uncles; tells himself "I am not weak" when clearly insecure; dismisses Victarion as dim and Aeron as harmless; doesn't register Asha's hint about strangers and the Seastone Chair as applying to himself
