# ACOK — Tyrion XI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 50
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion XI
- **first_available:** ACOK Tyrion XI
- **location_primary:** King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Blackwater Rush (riverfront/quay), Mud Gate, Red Keep, Tower of the Hand, castle sept, Tyrion's solar
- **approximate_timeline:** Late in the book; final preparations before Stannis's assault on King's Landing. Clansmen are being dispatched, fortifications built, wildfire stockpiled, Kingsguard vacancies filled.
- **time_markers:** Dawn breaking at chapter opening; Timett left "two days before"; Black Ears and Moon Brothers left "yesterday"; bells ringing (midday); the investiture ceremony took "most of the morning"
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion sends Shagga and the Stone Crows across the Blackwater into the kingswood to harry Stannis's forces with guerrilla tactics. He orders Bronn to burn the waterfront structures outside the Mud Gate to deny Stannis cover. After reflecting on the fall of Winterfell to Theon Greyjoy, Tyrion inspects the Mud Gate defenses and the three trebuchets called the Three Whores. He attends the Kingsguard investiture of Ser Balon Swann and Ser Osmund Kettleblack, instructs the new High Septon to spread propaganda against Stannis, learns from Hallyne that wildfire production has surged to nearly thirteen thousand jars (including a cache found under the Dragonpit), receives a report from Lord Jacelyn Bywater on Tommen's safety at Rosby, and is warned by Varys about a conspiracy called the Antler Men who plan to open the Old Gate to Stannis.

## Events & Actions
1. **Stone Crows depart via ferry** — Tyrion sees off Shagga and the Stone Crows as they push off from the quay on the Blackwater at dawn, following the Burned Men (two days prior) and Black Ears and Moon Brothers (yesterday) into the kingswood.
2. **Tyrion briefs Shagga on guerrilla tactics** — Strike camps and baggage trains, ambush scouts, hang bodies from trees, cut down stragglers, conduct night attacks. Shagga says he already knows this from Dolf son of Holger.
3. **Tyrion orders Shagga to heed local guides** — The kingswood is not the Mountains of the Moon; the guides know the terrain.
4. **Tyrion reflects on the uncertainty of his forces** — Bronn's ~800 sellswords are fickle; only a quarter of the 6,000 gold cloaks are reliable; he has only ~300 knights, squires, and men-at-arms.
5. **Rotten fish thrown at Tyrion** — As gold cloaks clear a path through the riverfront crowd, someone throws a slimy rotten fish at Tyrion; children with swollen bellies fight over the pieces.
6. **Tyrion orders waterfront structures burned** — He tells Bronn to assemble 100 men and burn everything between the water's edge and city walls, as the structures would let Stannis bypass scaling ladders. Bronn warns the owners won't like it and some may fight.
7. **Tyrion reflects on the fall of Winterfell** — News came from Varys. Tyrion remembers Winterfell and its godswood. He thinks Robb Stark must turn north now. He recalls Theon Greyjoy as a callow youth.
8. **Tyrion inspects the Mud Gate defenses** — Rides through the gate; sees the Three Whores (trebuchets) in the market square and carpenters building hoardings on the battlements.
9. **Trader captains protest at Tower of the Hand** — A dozen angry captains protest the seizure of their ships. Tyrion promises compensation after the war.
10. **Kingsguard investiture ceremony** — Joffrey invests Ser Balon Swann and Ser Osmund Kettleblack in the castle sept. The new High Septon leads vows and anoints them. The ceremony takes most of the morning.
11. **Tyrion instructs the High Septon** — Tells him to announce that Stannis has vowed to burn the Great Sept of Baelor, and that aiding Stannis betrays the gods. The High Septon agrees to spread this and pray for the king and his Hand.
12. **Hallyne reports wildfire production** — Almost 13,000 jars, including 300+ from a cache found under the Dragonpit. A man broke a seal and drank some, mistaking it for wine. Hallyne mentions that their spells seem to be working better and asks if there might be dragons about.
13. **Hallyne recounts Wisdom Pollitor's teaching** — Magic began to go out of the world when the last dragon died.
14. **Lord Jacelyn Bywater reports on Tommen** — Tommen is safe at Rosby, hale and happy, has adopted a fawn. He asks about his mother, writes unfinished letters to Myrcella, does not miss Joffrey. Bywater has secret instructions for Tommen if the battle is lost.
15. **Varys reveals the Antler Men conspiracy** — A group of rich traders, merchants, and craftsmen who believe Stannis will win; they plan to seize the Old Gate and admit the enemy. Among them is the armorer Salloreon. Tyrion orders arrests.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious but resolute. Feels "naked" without his clansmen. Experiences a "queer twinge" watching Shagga depart. Nostalgic and conflicted about Winterfell's fall — knows he should rejoice but feels ambivalent. Wryly pragmatic about his tenuous military position.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Defense of King's Landing against Stannis. Tyrion is acutely aware of the fragility of his forces — fickle sellswords, unreliable gold cloaks, too few knights. He is managing multiple simultaneous preparations: guerrilla warfare, fortification, wildfire stockpiling, intelligence, propaganda, political maneuvering.
- **Key decisions made:** Sends clansmen to harass Stannis's approach; orders waterfront structures burned to deny Stannis cover; instructs High Septon to spread anti-Stannis propaganda; orders arrest of the Antler Men; considers whether to share Balon Greyjoy's alliance proposal with Cersei or the council.
- **Self-deception flags:** None prominent. Tyrion is unusually clear-eyed in this chapter — he acknowledges his forces' weaknesses, doesn't romanticize the sellswords' loyalty, and admits he should rejoice at Winterfell's fall but can't fully bring himself to do so.
