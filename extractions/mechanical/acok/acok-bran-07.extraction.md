# ACOK — Bran VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 70
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran VII
- **first_available:** ACOK Bran VII
- **location_primary:** Winterfell (crypts, then surface)
- **location_secondary:** Wolfswood (edge), godswood
- **approximate_timeline:** After the sack of Winterfell; the group has been hiding in the crypts for an extended period
- **time_markers:** Three days spent warging into Summer; fires burn all through the night; sun rises grey and smoky the next morning; "the rest of the morning" spent circuiting the castle; Osha has lost count of days/nights underground
- **chapter_summary:** Bran wargs into Summer and witnesses the burning of Winterfell from the wolfswood edge. After three days in the wolf dream, he returns to consciousness in the crypts and tells Osha what he saw. The group emerges from the crypts to find Winterfell sacked — buildings burned, bodies everywhere, both ironmen and men bearing the flayed man badge among the dead. They find the dying Maester Luwin in the godswood, who confirms many northern lords are dead and advises the princes be separated for safety. The group splits: Osha takes Rickon and Shaggydog east via the kingsroad, while the Reeds take Bran and Hodor north via the Hunter's Gate, with Summer accompanying them.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran wargs into Summer** — Observes Winterfell burning from the edge of the wolfswood; smells blood, smoke, steel, horses, fire
2. **Summer sees a "great winged snake" in the sky** — Its roar is described as "a river of flame"; it vanishes quickly (likely a vision of fire/smoke)
3. **Fires burn through the night** — A great roar and crash makes the earth jump; dogs, horses, and men make noise all night
4. **Summer and Shaggydog explore the aftermath at dawn** — Find bodies, crows, wild dogs; no living men; a horse dying on a broken leg
5. **Shaggydog kills the dying horse** — Tears out its throat; fights Summer over the carcass; Summer dominates and then allows Shaggydog to feed
6. **Bran resists returning to his body** — Feels the "dark place" pulling him; struggles against it; prefers being wolf
7. **Bran wakes in the crypts after three days** — Meera calls him back; he is weak, thirsty, disoriented
8. **Bran reports the burning of Winterfell to Osha** — She is skeptical at first, calling it a dream
9. **Osha lights a torch** — Illuminates the crypts; Rickon wakes
10. **Bran confirms it is daytime by briefly warging** — Sees double: the crypts and Winterfell's ruined gates, bodies in moat, broken drawbridge
11. **Group prepares to leave the crypts** — Meera straps basket to Hodor; they climb the narrow spiral stairs
12. **Hodor forces open the blocked ironwood door** — Uses his back and legs; enormous effort; stone rubble shifted above
13. **Group emerges at the First Keep** — One side has collapsed; gargoyles and stone strewn across yard; a body crushed beneath
14. **Summer and Shaggydog appear from behind the broken tower** — Rickon reunites with Shaggydog
15. **Group makes a circuit of the castle** — Discovers full extent of destruction: Great Hall roof collapsed, glass gardens shattered, stables burned, Library Tower leaking hot water, bridge collapsed, maester's turret gone
16. **Bran recognizes Poxy Tym among the dead** — Axe wound to face
17. **Osha identifies dead ironmen and Bolton men among the corpses** — Finds Black Lorren's body, Greyjoy's warhorse with arrows, and a corpse bearing the flayed man badge
18. **Summer leads them to the godswood** — They find Maester Luwin dying beneath the heart tree
19. **Maester Luwin reveals he knew the burned bodies weren't the princes** — Could tell by the muscles in the legs; the clothes fit but the body didn't
20. **Bran explains their ruse** — They doubled back from the wolfswood edge and hid in the crypts; sent wolves ahead to make a trail
21. **Luwin advises separating the princes** — Calls them Robb's heirs; says they must not stay together
22. **Luwin reports the war situation** — Cerwyn boy dead, Ser Rodrik slain, Leobald Tallhart slain, Lady Hornwood slain, Deepwood fallen, Moat Cailin threatened, Torrhen's Square soon to fall, ironmen on the Stony Shore, Bastard of Bolton to the east
23. **Luwin suggests White Harbor or the Umbers as refuges** — But admits uncertainty; says war is everywhere
24. **Luwin gives final counsel to Bran** — "You must be strong"; calls him his father's son
25. **Luwin asks Osha for water and "another boon"** — Implied mercy killing; Osha sends children away
26. **Osha stays behind with Luwin** — Implied she grants his request (mercy kill); she says nothing of him when she returns
27. **Group splits** — Osha takes Rickon and Shaggydog east via the kingsroad; Bran goes with Hodor, Meera, Jojen, and Summer north via the Hunter's Gate
28. **Group stops at kitchens for food** — Burned bread, cold fowl, honey, apples
29. **Rickon's tearful farewell** — Sobs and clings to Hodor's leg; Osha smacks him with spear butt to get him moving
30. **Bran's group squeezes under the warped portcullis at Hunter's Gate** — One by one beneath the spikes
31. **Jojen announces their road is north** — Not to Greywater Watch
32. **Bran looks back at Winterfell one final time** — Reflects that "the stone is strong" and Winterfell is "not dead, just broken. Like me."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief-stricken (tears for Luwin, wants to weep for Dancer); fearful (afraid to leave crypts despite wanting to); resolved (accepts his role); torn between wolf-identity and human-identity
- **Primary preoccupation:** The pull of Summer's wolf-consciousness vs. his human self; the destruction of his home; the need to survive and be strong
- **Key decisions made:** Decides they must leave the crypts to investigate; accepts that the group must split; chooses to go north with the Reeds rather than east with Osha
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran tells himself he prefers being the wolf ("Better Bran the beastling") and rationalizes extended warging as acceptable, even while Jojen warns him; he tells himself carrying a sword means something ("the blade felt good in his hand") then immediately acknowledges "it was only a game"
