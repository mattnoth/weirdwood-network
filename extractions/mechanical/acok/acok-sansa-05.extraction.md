In the Queen's Ballroom, Sansa discovers Ser Ilyn Payne standing with Ice, and Cersei explains he is present to deal with treason. Cersei mocks Sansa's belief in true knights and warns her about what happens when a city is sacked. **Note: the chapter source file appears truncated; extraction covers only the available text.**

## Events & Actions
1. **Singing in the sept** — People have been singing hymns in the Red Keep's sept since the first report of enemy sails that morning.
2. **Joffrey mounts up** — Ser Meryn Trant holds a blood bay horse for Joffrey, who wears gilded mail and enameled crimson plate with golden lion helm.
3. **Tyrion speaks with Sansa** — Mounted on a red stallion in battle gear, Tyrion asks if Cersei invited Sansa to Maegor's. He mentions he should have sent her with Tommen. He says this day may change everything, for Sansa as well as House Lannister.
4. **Joffrey calls Sansa** — Joffrey shouts for Sansa across the yard; she thinks "He calls me as if he were calling a dog."
5. **Joffrey shows Hearteater** — Joffrey draws his new sword Hearteater and makes Sansa kiss the blade. He promises she'll taste his uncle's blood on it when he returns.
6. **Sansa provokes Joffrey about Robb** — Sansa tells Joffrey that Robb always goes where the fighting is thickest, calling Robb "a man grown." This makes Joffrey frown.
7. **Joffrey departs** — Joffrey spurs toward the gate with Ser Meryn, Ser Osmund, gold cloaks following four abreast. Tyrion and Ser Mandon Moore bring up the rear. Guards cheer them off; a sudden stillness follows.
8. **Sansa goes to the sept** — Drawn by the singing, Sansa enters the sept, which is more crowded and brightly lit than she has ever seen it. She lights a candle at each of the Seven's altars and sits between an old washerwoman and a young boy.
9. **Sansa prays for many people** — She sings the hymn to the Mother her own mother taught her. She prays for her family (Bran, Rickon, Robb, Arya, Jon, Catelyn, Eddard), extended family (Hoster Tully, Edmure Tully), friends (Jeyne Poole), the dead (Robert, Septa Mordane, Jory Cassel), the living (Ser Dontos, Maester Luwin), soldiers who will die, and finally for Tyrion and the Hound.
10. **Sansa prays specifically for the Hound** — "He is no true knight but he saved me all the same. Save him if you can, and gentle the rage inside him."
11. **Sansa leaves the sept** — When the septon prays for Joffrey's protection, Sansa silently wishes for his sword to break, his shield to shatter, his courage to fail, and every man to desert him. She pushes out through the doors.
12. **Sounds of battle** — Outside, Sansa hears warhorns, catapults, splashing, burning pitch, scorpion bolts, and dying men's cries beneath the singing.
13. **Encounter at Maegor's drawbridge** — Sansa meets Lady Tanda, Falyse, and Lollys. Lollys is sobbing and clinging to her maid, refusing to cross. Sansa gently encourages her.
14. **Lollys dragged across** — Falyse and Shae (the maid) each take an elbow and half-drag Lollys across the drawbridge. Lady Tanda says Lollys has been "sick." Sansa thinks that Lollys is with child.
15. **Entry into Maegor's Holdfast** — Sellsword guards in Lannister livery man the door; one is sitting on a step rather than standing. They enter the Queen's Ballroom.
16. **Sansa sees Ser Ilyn Payne with Ice** — In the shadows at the back wall, Ser Ilyn holds her father's greatsword Ice. Sansa's breath catches. She asks Osfryd Kettleblack why Ser Ilyn is there.
17. **Osfryd explains** — He grins and says the queen expects she'll have need of Ser Ilyn before night's end. Sansa realizes this means executions.
18. **Cersei enters the Queen's Ballroom** — Announced as "Cersei of House Lannister, Queen Regent and Protector of the Realm." She wears a white linen gown with gold satin lining, diamonds and emeralds.
19. **Cersei comments on Sansa's menstruation** — Cersei observes Sansa looks pale and asks if her "red flower" is still blooming. "The men will bleed out there, and you in here."
20. **Cersei explains Ser Ilyn's presence** — "To deal with treason, and to defend us if need be." She says when axes smash the doors, they may be glad of him.
21. **Cersei warns about sacking** — When Sansa says true knights would never harm women and children, Cersei mocks her, saying loyal sellswords are rare, her guards will flee if the battle is lost, and sarcastically tells Sansa to wait for Symeon Star-Eyes and Prince Aemon the Dragonknight to rescue her.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear (battle beginning), resentment toward Joffrey, genuine pity and spiritual generosity during prayer (prays even for those who mistreat her), cold defiance (wishes Joffrey harm as she leaves the sept), unease at Ser Ilyn Payne's presence with Ice, discomfort under Cersei's mockery
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival during the coming battle; the question of whose prayers will be heard; awareness that the battle's outcome will determine her fate
- **Key decisions made:** Deliberately taunts Joffrey by comparing him to Robb ("a man grown"); silently curses Joffrey when the septon prays for him; chooses to pray for Tyrion and the Hound despite their roles as her captors
- **Self-deception flags:** Says "True knights would never harm women and children" but recognizes even as she speaks that the words ring hollow — she is losing her belief in the songs and stories but still clings to remnants of it
