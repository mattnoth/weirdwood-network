- **chapter_summary:** Sansa is summoned by Sandor Clegane to appear before Joffrey in the lower bailey, where the king publicly humiliates and has her beaten by Ser Boros Blount for Robb's victory at Oxcross. Tyrion Lannister intervenes, stopping the abuse and dressing down Joffrey before the court. Sansa is taken to the Tower of the Hand, treated by Maester Frenken, and given dreamwine. When she wakes, Tyrion visits and tells her the truth about the Battle of Oxcross, hints he does not intend for her to marry Joffrey, and offers her wildling guards, but Sansa declines to avoid compromising her secret escape plan with Ser Dontos.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sandor Clegane summons Sansa** — The Hound warns her the longer she keeps Joffrey waiting, the worse it will go. Sansa dresses hurriedly, fears Joffrey has learned about her secret meetings with Ser Dontos.
2. **Sansa walks to the lower bailey** — She walks on the Hound's left to avoid his burned face. The Hound tells her Robb, not she, is the cause of Joffrey's anger.
3. **Sansa observes the crowd** — Lord Gyles coughing; Ser Horas Redwyne averting his gaze; Hobber Redwyne pretending not to see her; stablehands eyeing her insolently; a yellow cat dying with a crossbow quarrel through its ribs.
4. **Ser Dontos approaches** — Riding his broomstick horse, he whispers "Be brave" and squeezes her arm.
5. **Joffrey confronts Sansa** — Standing with Ser Boros and Ser Meryn, winding his crossbow. He orders Sansa to answer for Robb's treasons.
6. **Lancel Lannister reports the Battle of Oxcross** — He claims Robb used sorcery, an army of wargs, and that the northmen feasted on the flesh of the slain.
7. **Joffrey threatens Sansa with the crossbow** — Points it at her face. He recalls Nymeria biting him. Sansa says Lady never hurt him. Joffrey boasts he killed Eddard Stark and killed a man the previous night by shooting him through the throat at the gate, and wounded a woman throwing rocks.
8. **Joffrey orders the Hound to hit Sansa** — Says Cersei warned that killing Sansa would mean Jaime's death; instead she'll be punished and word sent to Robb.
9. **Ser Dontos intervenes** — Shoves forward offering to beat her himself, whacks her with the melon morningstar while riding his broomstick. The melon breaks apart, juice runs down Sansa's face and gown. People laugh.
10. **Joffrey is not satisfied** — Calls for Ser Boros and Ser Meryn. Ser Meryn flings Dontos aside. Ser Boros seizes Sansa.
11. **Sansa is beaten and stripped** — Joffrey commands "Leave her face." Boros punches her in the belly, grabs her hair, strikes her thighs with the flat of his sword. She screams and cries. Joffrey orders Boros to make her naked. Boros rips the front of her bodice, baring her to the waist.
12. **Tyrion Lannister intervenes** — Arrives with Bronn and Timett. Demands to know the meaning of this. Shames Ser Boros for beating a helpless maid. Threatens to have Bronn and Timett kill Ser Boros if he speaks again.
13. **Sandor gives Sansa his cloak** — At Tyrion's request for someone to cover her. She clutches the white wool against her chest.
14. **Tyrion confronts Joffrey** — Tells him Sansa is to be his queen and asks about her honor. Compares him to Aerys Targaryen. Tells him wanton brutality won't win love. Joffrey quotes Cersei: "Fear is better than love."
15. **Tyrion takes Sansa to the Tower of the Hand** — She is taken by Bronn and Timett, not to Maegor's Holdfast as she expected.
16. **Serving girls tend to Sansa** — Strip off ruined clothes, bathe her, wash melon juice from her hair and face.
17. **Maester Frenken treats her wounds** — Spreads salve on the red welts on her legs, gives her dreamwine with honey.
18. **Sansa wakes in the dark** — Finds herself in Arya's old bedchamber. Pain shoots through her legs. She puts on a robe left beside the bed.
19. **The wildling woman guard prevents Sansa from leaving** — Sansa wants to reach the godswood to find Ser Dontos, but the guard says "The halfman said you're not to leave."
20. **A serving girl brings food** — Cheese, bread, olives, cold water. Sansa refuses but the food is left. She drinks two cups of water and nibbles an olive.
21. **Tyrion visits Sansa** — Tells her the truth about Oxcross: no sorcery, Robb used Grey Wind to panic the horses; Stafford hadn't posted sentries; his host was raw levies. Stafford was killed by Lord Rickard Karstark. Multiple lords and knights killed or captured including Tyrion's nephew Martyn Lannister.
22. **Tyrion hints at ending the betrothal** — Says he does not intend Sansa to wed Joffrey; no marriage will reconcile Stark and Lannister. Asks if she has flowered (she has not).
23. **Tyrion offers Sansa protection** — Offers Stone Crows or Black Ears as guards, specifically Chella or Timett. Sansa declines, lying that the Tower of the Hand is haunted by her father's men's ghosts. She needs to remain accessible to Ser Dontos for her escape.
24. **Tyrion escorts Sansa back to her chambers** — Accepts her reasoning and offers to walk her back.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Dread escalating to terror during the beating; humiliation at being stripped publicly; brief gratitude toward Ser Dontos, the Hound, and Tyrion; secret exultation at Robb's victory ("Robb will kill you all"); pain and discomfort from her wounds; anxiety about whether Tyrion's offer of protection is a trick; desperation to reach Ser Dontos in the godswood
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival — performing the role of loyal subject while concealing her true feelings and protecting her escape plan with Ser Dontos
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Refuses Tyrion's offer to stay in the Tower of the Hand with wildling guards, inventing a lie about ghosts, to preserve access to Ser Dontos; (2) Maintains the lie of loving Joffrey before Tyrion despite his gentle probing
- **Self-deception flags:** None — Sansa is acutely aware she is performing; she knows her declarations of loyalty and love for Joffrey are lies and consciously maintains them as survival strategy
