# ACOK — Theon III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 38
- **pov_character:** Theon Greyjoy
- **pov_chapter_number:** Theon III
- **first_available:** ACOK Theon III
- **location_primary:** Stony Shore (battle site and pebbled beach)
- **location_secondary:** Foamdrinker (Dagmer's longship), Sea Bitch (Theon's longship), destroyed fishing village
- **approximate_timeline:** During the ironborn raids on the Stony Shore; after Theon received his command from Balon Greyjoy and began harrying the northern coast
- **time_markers:** "three years past" since Theon last saw Benfred Tallhart; "a fortnight" Theon spent in Benfred's company at Torrhen's Square; Ned Stark rode to Torrhen's Square to see Ser Helman (three years prior); fishing village attacked in "the chill darkness before the dawn"; Theon was with the Starks for "ten years"
- **chapter_summary:** Theon oversees the aftermath of a successful ambush against Benfred Tallhart's force on the Stony Shore. After Benfred spits on him and hurls insults, Theon yields the captive to his uncle Aeron Damphair, who drowns Benfred in the sea as an offering to the Drowned God. Theon kills one of his own men, Todric, with an arrow for drunkenness and fighting over plunder. He then boards Dagmer Cleftjaw's longship Foamdrinker and proposes a secret plan: Dagmer will march openly on Torrhen's Square as a feint to draw out Ser Rodrik Cassel and the Winterfell garrison, while Theon strikes at the true target — implied to be Winterfell itself.

## Events & Actions
1. **Benfred Tallhart spits on Theon and insults him** — Benfred, held between Stygg and Werlag, calls Theon a turncloak and threatens that Robb Stark will kill him
2. **Aeron Damphair demands Benfred's death** — Insists that spitting on Theon is spitting on the Drowned God; Benfred must be given to the god "the old way"
3. **Benfred is gagged and taken to the shore** — Werlag stuffs a rabbitskin in his mouth; Stygg and Werlag drag him to the beach for drowning; Aeron follows
4. **Benfred is drowned in the sea** — His limp body is later seen being dragged from the surf; drowned as an offering to the Drowned God
5. **Theon surveys the battlefield aftermath** — Climbs a jumble of stone to look down on dead men and dying horses; reflects on the one-sided slaughter
6. **Ironborn loot the dead** — Gevin Harlaw saws off a dead man's finger for a ring; general looting underway
7. **Tymor and brothers gather surviving horses** — While Urzen and Black Lorren put down badly wounded animals
8. **Todric fights with a Botley son over plunder** — Knocks the boy down and flings ale in his eyes; Fishwhiskers leaps up
9. **Theon kills Todric with an arrow** — Aims for the hand holding the drinking horn but Todric lurches; arrow takes him through the belly
10. **Botley and sons finish Todric** — Slit his throat and strip him of cloak, rings, and weapons before he's even dead
11. **Theon asserts command** — Reminds the men of his order against drunkards and squabbling; no one challenges him
12. **Theon recalls the ambush of Benfred's force** — Archers concealed behind gorse spoiled the Tallharts' singing with arrows; Theon led men-at-arms to finish with dagger, axe, and warhammer
13. **Theon recalls the fishing village raid** — Brought ships to shore before dawn; led the attack with a longaxe; men killed, women taken as salt wives or thralls, village burned
14. **Theon boards Foamdrinker to speak with Dagmer** — Climbs the plank in four strides; enters the cramped aft cabin
15. **Theon flatters Dagmer and proposes his plan** — Appeals to Dagmer's pride and desire for glory; argues the harrying is beneath both of them
16. **Theon reveals his plan** — Dagmer will march openly on Torrhen's Square as a feint, building siege engines to frighten Leobald Tallhart into sending for help; Ser Rodrik will ride out from Winterfell
17. **Theon implies his true target** — When Dagmer objects that Torrhen's Square cannot be taken, Theon says "It's not Torrhen's Square I mean to take" — implying Winterfell
18. **Dagmer agrees to the plan** — His question about his role signals he has been won over

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitter, conflicted, and resentful. The victory over Benfred's force leaves a sour taste rather than elation. Theon is angry at himself for caring what Eddard Stark would think, angry at his father for not trusting him, and jealous of Asha. Underneath, ambitious and desperate to prove himself.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Proving himself worthy of being Balon's heir; competing with Asha for glory; winning a "great deed" that will earn recognition. Also preoccupied with the gap between what he feels he should feel (triumph) and what he actually feels (bitterness, distaste).
- **Key decisions made:** Yields Benfred to Aeron for drowning rather than questioning him; kills Todric to enforce discipline and establish authority; devises the plan to feint at Torrhen's Square and take Winterfell; chooses to delegate the coastal harrying to Aeron and pursue his own strategy.
- **Self-deception flags:** "Stark is dead and rotting, and naught to me" — actively trying to convince himself he doesn't care about Eddard Stark's moral judgment. Wonders why the victory "did not taste as sweet" as the Whispering Wood but doesn't examine the answer. Frames his plan as benefiting his father ("hand him his kingdom") when it's driven by personal ambition and rivalry with Asha.
