- **first_available:** ACOK Arya VII

- **location_secondary:** Riverrun (referenced), Winterfell (referenced), King's Landing (referenced), the Green Fork (referenced), the ruby ford (referenced), the Eyrie (referenced), Highgarden (referenced), Rushing Falls (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Arya has been at Harrenhal for some weeks under Weese; the Brave Companions arrive a fortnight after her settling in; events span several more days after that
- **time_markers:** "once a fortnight even a bite of meat"; "It took him only three days to earn the place of honor in her nightly prayers"; "a fortnight later, when the queerest company of men she'd ever seen arrived"; "the very night they arrived"; "the next morning Lord Tywin hanged them"; "for days and days"; "one morning she spied three women"; "one afternoon"; "the rest of that day scrubbing steps"; "by evenfall"; "two nights later"; "nothing happened the next day, nor the day after, but on the third day"
- **chapter_summary:** Arya serves as "Weasel" under the brutal overseer Weese at Harrenhal, scrubbing floors and running errands while eavesdropping on war rumors. The Brave Companions arrive under Vargo Hoat, and later Ser Amory Lorch returns with Rorge, Biter, and Jaqen H'ghar among his men. Lord Cerwyn dies before Arya can contact him for help. Jaqen visits Arya at night and offers her three deaths in repayment for the three lives she saved. After hearing Chiswyck boast about Ser Gregor's rape of an innkeeper's daughter, Arya whispers his name to Jaqen, and three days later Chiswyck falls from a wallwalk and dies.

- **Notable sensory details:** Arya's hands raw and bleeding from scrubbing; arms trembling from exhaustion; straw bedding; smooth warm stone-like quality of Jaqen's hand over her mouth; blood running down her thighs from caning

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya establishes routine at Harrenhal** — Works under Weese as "Weasel," scrubbing, running messages, drawing water, fetching food, serving in Barracks Hall
2. **Hot Pie attempts to slip Arya an apple tart** — Caught by two cooks who confiscate it and beat him with a wooden spoon
3. **Arya adds Weese to her nightly prayer list** — Within three days he earns first place; she recites full list nightly
4. **Arya eavesdrops on castle gossip** — Learns of Pretty Pia's affairs, Joffrey's bastardy letter, war rumors about Stannis and Renly
5. **Brave Companions arrive at Harrenhal** — Led by Vargo Hoat under their black goat banner; exotic multinational company described in detail
6. **Fight between Brave Companions and Lannister men** — Ser Harys Swyft's squire stabbed to death; two Mummers wounded
7. **Lord Tywin hangs three men** — Two Bloody Mummers and one of Lord Lydden's archers who started the trouble by taunting about Beric Dondarrion
8. **Vargo Hoat and Ser Harys Swyft forced reconciliation** — Embrace and kiss and swear love under Lord Tywin's gaze
9. **Brave Companions ride out** — Before leaving, one mentions Roose Bolton at the ruby ford
10. **Four Frey brothers take exercise in Flowstone Yard** — Three Freys of the Crossing and their bastard brother fight with staves and wooden shields
11. **Frey brothers ransomed** — Two other Frey brothers arrive under peace banner with gold; all six leave together
12. **Arya considers approaching Lord Cerwyn** — Plans how to get past door guards; he is bedridden with a wound
13. **Lord Cerwyn dies** — Arya sees silent sisters loading his corpse (sewn in silk cloak with battle-axe sigil) into their wagon
14. **Ser Amory Lorch returns to Harrenhal** — Fewer men than before, many wounded; has been chasing Beric Dondarrion around the lake
15. **Arya spots Rorge, Biter, and Jaqen H'ghar** — Near end of Ser Amory's column; they ride past without recognizing her
16. **Arya scrubs steps all day** — By evenfall hands raw and bleeding, too tired for food
17. **Arya recites her prayer** — Considers adding three names (Rorge, Biter, Jaqen) but too tired to decide
18. **Jaqen H'ghar visits Arya at night** — Wakes her with hand over mouth; reveals he recognized her; offers three deaths as repayment for three lives saved
19. **Arya requests escape to Riverrun** — Jaqen refuses: only three deaths, not other services
20. **Ser Gregor returns to Harrenhal** — Brings herd of goats instead of prisoners; lost four men to Beric's night raids
21. **Weese sends Arya to check on Gregor's men's clothes** — She goes up and finds Chiswyck telling his story
22. **Chiswyck tells story of Gregor's rape** — Graphic account of Gregor raping brewer's daughter Layna, with his men joining in; brewer's son killed by Raff
23. **Arya leaves without completing her errand** — Weese canes her until blood runs down her thighs
24. **Arya whispers "Chiswyck" to Jaqen** — Two nights later while serving wine in Barracks Hall; leans close and whispers the name
25. **Chiswyck dies three days later** — Falls from wallwalk; attributed to drunkenness or Harren's ghost; Arya knows Jaqen did it

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear (of Weese, Gregor, Tywin); grief and frustration (Lord Cerwyn's death); longing for family (Robb, Jon, Bran, Rickon, Mother, even Sansa); growing sense of agency after Jaqen's offer; cold satisfaction at Chiswyck's death
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and anonymity at Harrenhal; maintaining her kill prayer list; deciding how to use Jaqen's offer of three deaths; desire to escape to Riverrun and Robb
- **Key decisions made:** Adds Weese to her prayer list (first position); decides to name Chiswyck to Jaqen after hearing his rape story; initially avoids Jaqen for two days while considering the offer; considers but doesn't trust using Jaqen for escape
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself she shouldn't trust Jaqen ("I should kill them myself") while simultaneously using his gift; tells herself "He could never have helped you anyway" about Lord Cerwyn after his death — rationalizing her failure to reach him
