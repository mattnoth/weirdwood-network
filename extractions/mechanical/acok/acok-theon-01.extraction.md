# ACOK — Theon I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 12
- **pov_character:** Theon Greyjoy
- **pov_chapter_number:** Theon I
- **first_available:** ACOK Theon I
- **location_primary:** Pyke (castle and surrounding islands)
- **location_secondary:** Lordsport, the Myraham (ship), the sea approach to Pyke, Sea Tower, Bloody Keep, Great Keep
- **approximate_timeline:** Early in ACOK; the red comet is still visible; Robb Stark has been crowned King in the North and is marching west toward the Golden Tooth; Theon arrives at the Iron Islands after traveling from Riverrun to Seagard and then by sea aboard the Myraham
- **time_markers:** "Ten years" since Theon left Pyke as a hostage; "nigh on sunset" when reaching Pyke's walls; the red comet visible in the sky; "dead these five years" (Sylas Sourmouth); voyage duration from Seagard implied to be several days; reference to "a thousand years before" (the sons of the River King slaughtered); "five thousand years ago" (Urron Redhand); "three hundred years" since the Booming Tower bell was rung (at Seagard)
- **chapter_summary:** Theon Greyjoy returns to Pyke after ten years as Eddard Stark's ward, carrying a letter from Robb Stark proposing an alliance. He arrives by merchant ship at Lordsport, where no one recognizes him, and is met by his uncle Aeron Greyjoy, now a priest of the Drowned God. Aeron performs a seawater blessing and rides with Theon to Pyke in near-silence, refusing to explain why the longships are hosting. At the castle, Theon finds himself treated as a stranger and is given chambers in the Bloody Keep rather than his childhood room. He dresses in fine mainland clothes and crosses to the Sea Tower to meet his father, Lord Balon, who mocks his appearance, breaks his gold chain, and burns Robb's letter — declaring he will take his own crown with fire and sword, targeting not the west but an undefended "plum."

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Strong winds, salt spray, scuttling clouds; the sea is rough and foaming; damp pervades everything
- **Season indicators:** The red comet visible through thin clouds; no explicit season named but cold and damp conditions throughout
- **Time of day:** Daytime arrival by ship; "nigh on sunset" when reaching the walls of Pyke; evening for the meeting with Balon in the Sea Tower
- **Lighting:** Natural daylight on approach; the comet's "fine red tail" visible; nightly watchfire atop the Sea Tower mentioned; braziers inside the castle; torches referenced in Theon's memories of the siege
- **Sounds:** Stroke of oars, pounding of the master's drum, sails snapping, captain cursing; waves crashing and foaming against rocks; seabirds; fishing boats; shorehands rolling casks; fisherfolk crying the day's catch; children playing; boots on stone echoing in Sea Tower stairwell; "ceaseless rumble of the sea below" when crossing bridges
- **Smells:** Salt sharp in the damp air; musty sealskins on Balon; musty-smelling mattress; mildew on wall hangings; old rushes; winey stench of Sylas Sourmouth's breath (recalled)
- **Notable sensory details:** Salt spray burning Theon's eyes during the seawater blessing; the rope bridge swaying underfoot; cold seawater used for bathing; bone-deep damp in the Bloody Keep chambers; splinter snagging Theon's glove on the Sea Tower door; the sting of Balon's backhand across Theon's cheek

## Events & Actions
1. **Theon views Pyke from the sea** — The Myraham sails past Pyke; Theon observes the castle, the Greyjoy banner, and the red comet from the deck.
2. **Theon and captain's daughter go to his cabin** — Theon leads the captain's daughter below, noting the father's silent disapproval.
3. **Sexual encounter** — Theon has the captain's daughter perform oral sex on him; meanwhile he reflects on ironborn history and the Old Way.
4. **Captain's daughter pleads to stay** — She begs to come ashore as his salt wife; Theon refuses and tells her she is likely with child from his attentions, calling it "a king's bastard."
5. **Myraham rounds a wooded point** — Theon sees the rebuilt Botley castle and Lordsport harbor, observing many longships (fifty or sixty) and noting the burned sept was never rebuilt.
6. **Theon searches for specific ships** — He spots the Great Kraken but not Euron's Silence; sees devices of Wynch, Goodbrother, and Harlaw on other longships.
7. **Myraham docks at Lordsport** — The captain announces his cargo and that he has brought back the Greyjoy heir. Lordsport merchants do not recognize Theon.
8. **Theon goes ashore unrecognized** — He pays the captain a golden dragon, demands a horse from the innkeeper, and is not known.
9. **Aeron Greyjoy arrives** — A priest leading horses approaches; the smallfolk bend the knee and whisper "Damphair"; Theon recognizes his uncle.
10. **Theon retrieves his belongings** — A sailor brings his bow and arrows; the captain's daughter brings his pack and tries to embrace him; Theon turns aside and leaves.
11. **Aeron performs a seawater blessing** — Theon kneels in mud; Aeron pours seawater on his head and intones a blessing: "Let Theon your servant be born again from the sea." Theon recites "What is dead may never die."
12. **Ride from Lordsport to Pyke** — Aeron and Theon ride up past the Botley castle into stony hills; Aeron is taciturn and reveals little.
13. **Aeron gives family news** — Theon's mother is on Harlaw with her sister (her cough troubles her); Asha has gone to Great Wyk with Balon's messages; Dagmer has gone to Old Wyk to roust the Stonehouses and Drumms.
14. **Theon asks about the longships hosting** — Aeron refuses to explain, saying he is commanded not to speak of it.
15. **Aeron questions Theon's inheritance** — When Theon insists he is heir, Aeron says "We shall see" and implies Asha may inherit; calls Theon a fool if he thinks Balon will hand the islands to "a Stark."
16. **Arrival at Pyke at sunset** — The gates are open, portcullis raised; guards watch with "strangers' eyes"; no familiar faces or honor guard.
17. **Helya shows Theon to the Bloody Keep** — Not his childhood room in the Sea Tower; chambers are damp, mildewed, with musty mattress and old rushes. Theon demands hot water, fire, and fresh rushes.
18. **Theon washes and dresses** — Uses tepid seawater; dresses in fine mainland clothes: black leather boots, silvery-grey breeches, black velvet doublet with golden kraken, gold chain, white belt, dirk and longsword, black silk gloves with golden tracery.
19. **Theon crosses to the Sea Tower** — Passes through covered stone walkway, crosses three bridges (each narrower), the last a swaying rope bridge over crashing waves. His heart is in his mouth.
20. **Theon meets Lord Balon** — Finds his father seated by a brazier under musty sealskins. Balon is gaunt and smaller than remembered.
21. **Balon criticizes Theon's appearance** — Asks if Ned Stark dressed him like his "sweet daughter"; demands to know if the gold chain was bought with gold or iron.
22. **Balon breaks the gold chain** — Yanks it off Theon's neck, snapping it, and drops it on the brazier. Says he will not have his son "bedeck himself like a whore."
23. **Theon presents Robb's letter** — Describes the military situation: Robb at the Golden Tooth, Tywin at Harrenhal, Kingslayer captive, Lannisport undefended. Proposes a sea attack on Lannisport and asks for Casterly Rock as his reward.
24. **Balon reads and burns the letter** — Notes that "the boy would give me a crown" and objects that "what is given can be taken away." Burns the letter on the brazier.
25. **Balon slaps Theon** — When Theon asks if he's gone mad, Balon backhands him and declares his full titles: Lord Reaper of Pyke, King of Salt and Rock, Son of the Sea Wind.
26. **Balon reveals his own plans** — Will not attack the west or serve Robb's bidding. Casterly Rock is too strong, Tywin too cunning. Balon hungers for "a different plum" — "not so juicy sweet" but "ripe and undefended." Theon understands.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Eager and triumphant at first (returning home as the bearer of a great plan); increasingly anxious and then angry as he finds himself unrecognized and unwelcomed; wounded pride throughout; aghast when Balon burns the letter; stung by the slap but defiant
- **Primary preoccupation:** Winning his father's approval and claiming his rightful place as heir; the military plan to attack Lannisport is his path to glory and a crown; constant anxiety about whether he belongs to the ironborn world or the mainland world
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses the captain's daughter's plea to stay with him; kneels for Aeron's blessing despite finding it demeaning (decides he needs his uncle's support); dresses in fine mainland clothes to impress his father (backfires); presents Robb's plan as his own creation
- **Self-deception flags:** Claims the red comet is "his" comet; believes he is the man for this season and this hour; presents himself as Robb's trusted counselor while also seeing himself as Robb's superior; tells himself that being dressed in finery will impress his father rather than alienate him; imagines that "his plan, his glory, and in time his crown" will unfold smoothly; does not recognize how thoroughly he has become a mainlander in ironborn eyes
