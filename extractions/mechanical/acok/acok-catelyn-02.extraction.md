Renly privately shows Catelyn his vast host and offers to confirm Robb's titles if he bends the knee. A messenger arrives with news that Stannis Baratheon is besieging Storm's End.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn wakes at dawn** — Dreams of her children whole and Ned alive, wakes aching and weary in her tent amid the rolling grasslands.
2. **Morning camp routine** — Shadd cooks oats and brews nettle tea; Ser Wendel offers to hunt quail but Catelyn refuses; the party prepares to ride.
3. **Catelyn recalls her conversation with Robb at Riverrun** — Robb insists she go as envoy to Renly; threatens to send the Greatjon instead; Catelyn accedes.
4. **Catelyn recalls leaving Lord Hoster** — Her father did not recognize her, called her "Minisa"; she told him to wait for her.
5. **The party rides south toward the upper Mander** — Ser Wendel says Lord Renly should not be far.
6. **Robin Flint spots a far-eyes on a windmill** — The watcher is gone by the time they reach the mill.
7. **Renly's outriders intercept them** — Twenty mailed and mounted men led by Ser Colen of Greenpools; Catelyn identifies herself as envoy from Robb Stark, King in the North.
8. **Catelyn's party escorted to Bitterbridge** — They see the smoke, hear the camp, then see the enormous host with siege engines, thousands of soldiers, and banners of southern houses.
9. **They witness a melee tournament in progress** — Fewer than twenty knights remain of 116 who started; tournament has been going a day or more.
10. **Brienne of Tarth defeats Ser Loras Tyrell** — The blue knight (Brienne) grapples Loras to the ground, pulls a dirk, forces him to yield. Crowd is mixed in reaction.
11. **Brienne asks to join the Rainbow Guard** — Renly grants it; she removes helm revealing she is a woman; Renly cuts her torn cloak and fastens a rainbow in its place.
12. **Ser Colen presents Catelyn to King Renly** — Catelyn corrects him about Robb's title as "King in the North."
13. **Renly and Margaery greet Catelyn** — Margaery offers condolences; Renly vows to send Cersei's head.
14. **Brienne corrects Catelyn about Renly's style** — Says she should kneel; Catelyn deflects diplomatically.
15. **Lord Tarly insults Robb** — Calls it weak to send his mother; Catelyn retorts that Robb is "warring, not playing at tourney."
16. **Renly gives Catelyn his pavilion** — Orders steward to see to her comfort; announces feast tonight.
17. **Catelyn examines Renly's luxurious pavilion** — Sees her reflection in his armor and thinks of a drowned woman.
18. **The feast at Lord Caswell's castle** — Lavish food and entertainment; Catelyn observes Renly with Margaery and Loras; various lords boast and carouse.
19. **Fool's jest about "Kinslayer/Kingslayer"** — A fool in gold-painted tin with a lion's head beats a dwarf; Renly corrects "Kinslayer" to "Kingslayer."
20. **Lord Rowan and Catelyn discuss the youth of Renly's lords** — She calls them "knights of summer" and says "winter is coming."
21. **Brienne responds about songs and summer** — Says they'll be sung of; winter won't come for them in songs.
22. **Renly asks Catelyn to walk with him** — Dismisses Brienne's offer of protection; takes Catelyn to rooftop.
23. **Renly asks about Barristan Selmy** — Explains Selmy was dismissed by Lannisters, had kept a Rainbow Guard cloak for him; Catelyn confirms he hasn't come to Riverrun.
24. **Renly reveals he offered Ned a hundred swords** — On the night of Robert's death, to take Joffrey; Ned refused; Renly fled King's Landing.
25. **Renly shows Catelyn the campfires from the rooftop** — Fires cover the earth "like fallen stars"; claims eighty thousand plus reserves.
26. **Renly offers terms for Robb** — Will confirm all lands, titles, honors; Robb can call himself King in the North if he bends the knee and does homage.
27. **Renly invokes precedent** — A Stark king knelt to Aegon the Dragon three hundred years ago.
28. **A messenger arrives with urgent news** — Rider in winged helm announces Storm's End is besieged by Stannis, who now calls himself king.
