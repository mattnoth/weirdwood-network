- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow and his ranging companions spot a wildling watchfire in the Skirling Pass. Qhorin Halfhand orders Jon and Stonesnake to climb up and eliminate the watchers before they can blow their horn. After a grueling night climb, they surprise three wildlings — one more than expected. Stonesnake kills the horn-bearer and Jon kills a second, but the third is a young woman named Ygritte whom Jon takes captive rather than killing. Ygritte tells Jon the tale of Bael the Bard and claims the Starks carry wildling blood. At dawn, Qhorin arrives and tells Jon he must execute Ygritte, but Jon lets her go instead.

## Events & Actions
1. **Watchfire spotted** — The ranging party sees a wildling watchfire glimmering against the mountainside, half a mile ahead and two thousand feet up, positioned to observe the pass below.
2. **Rangers discuss the threat** — Squire Dalbridge, Ebben, Stonesnake, and Qhorin assess the situation. Qhorin determines there are likely two watchers and the horn must not be blown.
3. **Qhorin assigns the mission** — Qhorin orders Stonesnake and Jon to climb up and eliminate the watchers. Ghost must stay behind because white fur is visible in moonlight. Stonesnake is to throw a burning brand when the job is done.
4. **Jon and Stonesnake begin the climb** — They leave behind garrons, helms, mail, and Ghost. Jon commands Ghost to stay. They take rope, spikes, and the felt-wrapped hammer.
5. **Grueling night ascent** — The pair climbs for hours along a steep, twisting trail; Jon shuffles sideways like a crab in narrow spots. Two hours in, fierce wind pins them to the rock. Jon's burned hand stiffens and aches; he rips open a thumbnail.
6. **Technical climbing section** — The trail ends at a massive shoulder of black granite. Stonesnake free-climbs up, roping Jon behind him. He drives iron spikes into cracks with felt-muffled taps. Jon follows, leaving blood smears on the rock.
7. **Approach to the watchfire** — They reach a crooked fissure two-thirds up, crawl along a shelf for three hundred yards, and reach a position above the wildling camp.
8. **Attack on the wildlings** — They discover three watchers instead of two. Stonesnake leaps down and knocks the horn aside before the watcher can sound it. Jon engages the fire-tender, who thrusts a burning brand at his face. Jon bulls into the man and kills him with Longclaw, but the sword gets stuck in the falling body.
9. **Jon discovers Ygritte** — The sleeper sits up and Jon grabs her by the hair, pressing his dirk to her chin — then realizes the third wildling is a girl. Stonesnake tells him to finish her.
10. **Jon takes Ygritte captive** — Despite Stonesnake's objections, Jon offers Ygritte the chance to yield, and she does. He identifies himself as Jon Snow; she says it's "an evil name" — he corrects it to "a bastard name."
11. **Signal thrown** — Stonesnake thrusts a branch into the fire and flings the burning brand out over the pass as the signal for Qhorin.
12. **Ygritte urges burning the dead** — She warns they should burn the bodies; Jon recalls dead Othor and agrees, but Stonesnake says big fires are too visible. They strip and throw the bodies off the cliff instead.
13. **Shadowcats arrive** — Shadowcats growl nearby, attracted by the blood and bodies. Ygritte says they'll eat every shred and crack the bones for marrow.
14. **Jon interrogates Ygritte** — She reveals the free folk number "hundreds and thousands" but refuses to answer about Mance's plans, what's in the Frostfangs, whether they mean to march on the Wall, or Benjen Stark.
15. **Ygritte tells the tale of Bael the Bard** — She narrates how Bael entered Winterfell as "Sygerrik of Skagos," was given a winter rose as reward, stole Lord Brandon's maiden daughter, hid with her beneath Winterfell among the dead, fathered a son who became the next Lord Stark. The darker ending: thirty years later that son killed Bael at the Frozen Ford, the mother threw herself from a tower, and the son was flayed by one of his lords.
16. **Ygritte claims Stark blood in wildlings** — She tells Jon the Starks have Bael's blood, connecting wildlings and Starks.
17. **Dawn arrival and reunion** — Qhorin, Ebben, and Squire Dalbridge arrive at dawn with the garrons. Ghost races ahead and plays the jaw-game with Jon. Ygritte sees Ghost and her eyes go wide.
18. **Qhorin orders Ygritte's execution** — After hearing there were three watchers, Qhorin tells Jon that they have no food to spare and cannot watch her. Squire Dalbridge warns a shout could doom them all. Ebben draws his dagger. Qhorin tells Jon to "do what needs be done" as a man of the Night's Watch, and leads the others away.
19. **Jon lets Ygritte go** — Alone with Ygritte and Ghost, Jon marks her neck with Longclaw's blade but cannot bring himself to strike. He tells her to go. She goes.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Tense and focused during the climb; conflicted and uncertain during the killing; deeply torn when deciding Ygritte's fate; guilt and self-doubt about sparing her; bone-tired by the fire after the climb
- **Primary preoccupation:** The immediate tactical mission (eliminating the watchers without the horn being blown); later, the moral weight of killing a captive who yielded
- **Key decisions made:** Volunteers for the climb; takes Ygritte captive rather than killing her (against Stonesnake's advice); refuses to execute Ygritte despite Qhorin's order and instead lets her go
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells himself "one step and then another, and I will not fall" as a mantra; he thinks "He was his father's son. Wasn't he? Wasn't he?" — questioning his own identity while trying to summon the resolve to execute Ygritte; the repetition reveals deep uncertainty about whether he can or should follow through
