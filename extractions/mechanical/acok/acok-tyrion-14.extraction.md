- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion leads a mounted sortie from a sally port during the Battle of the Blackwater, forming a wedge with Ser Mandon Moore and his squire Podrick Payne. They disperse enemy soldiers manning a battering ram at the King's Gate, then ride along the riverfront to the Mud Gate, fighting through Stannis's forces. After Ser Balon Swann points out that wrecked ships have formed a bridge allowing hundreds of Stannis's men to cross the Blackwater, Tyrion leads a charge onto the bridge. His horse breaks a leg on the wrecked decks; he fights on foot until the bridge begins breaking apart. Ser Mandon Moore offers Tyrion his hand but instead slashes him across the face with a sword. Tyrion falls into the water but hauls himself back onto a deck, where Ser Mandon prepares to deliver a killing blow before being pushed overboard by an unseen rescuer — likely Podrick Payne.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion exits sally port** — Tyrion's men stream out of the sally port and form a wedge formation with Tyrion at the point, Ser Mandon Moore on his right, and Podrick Payne unexpectedly on his left.
2. **Tyrion tells Pod to go back** — Tyrion protests that Pod is too young, but Pod insists he is Tyrion's squire; Tyrion relents and tells him to stay close.
3. **Wedge advances along the walls** — They ride knee to knee following the city walls, arrows darting from the walls above while stones crash down.
4. **Charge at the King's Gate battering ram** — Tyrion orders lances and speeds to a canter; Ser Mandon drives Joffrey's banner through a man's chest; Tyrion kills a helmless knight in a Florent surcoat with his axe, taking off half his head.
5. **Battering ram abandoned** — The ram crashes into the mud as its handlers flee or turn to fight; Tyrion and his men cut through the defenders.
6. **Tyrion observes the burning river** — The Blackwater is jammed with burning galley hulks; wildfire burns in green plumes on the water.
7. **Tyrion redirects to the Mud Gate** — Tyrion commands they ride for the Mud Gate; Ser Mandon repeats the order; the men cry "King's Landing!" and "Halfman!"
8. **Fighting along the riverfront** — Tyrion leads his troop among men crawling from the river, killing those strong enough to stand; he experiences battle fever.
9. **Tyrion chops Stannis's banner** — He rides past a fiery heart banner planted in the mud and chops the staff in two.
10. **A knight attacks Tyrion's shield** — An unnamed knight hacks at Tyrion's shield with a greatsword repeatedly until someone stabs the knight with a dagger.
11. **Yielding knight with severed hand** — A knight lying in a puddle offers his lobstered gauntlet in surrender; when Tyrion reaches for it, wildfire illuminates that the puddle is blood-red and the gauntlet contains the knight's severed hand; Tyrion reels away.
12. **Tyrion kills an attacker at his horse** — A man-at-arms grabs Tyrion's horse and thrusts a dagger at his face; Tyrion knocks the blade aside and buries his axe in the man's neck.
13. **Ser Balon Swann appears** — Ser Balon, gore-spattered, rides up and points downriver with his mace.
14. **Discovery of the ship-bridge** — Tyrion sees that twenty or more wrecked galleys have locked together forming a bridge across the Blackwater, and hundreds of Stannis's men are crossing it.
15. **Tyrion orders the charge onto the bridge** — Tyrion says "Those are brave men. Let's go kill them" and leads a charge down a stone quay with his own men, Ser Balon's, and Ser Mandon.
16. **Celtigar spearman kills Ser Balon's horse** — A spearman with a red crab badge drives his spear through the horse's chest, spilling Ser Balon from the saddle.
17. **Tyrion's horse leaps onto the bridge** — His stallion leaps from the quay onto a galley deck, landing in ankle-deep water; Tyrion is thrown and loses his axe.
18. **Tyrion kills his injured horse** — The horse has broken a leg; Tyrion draws his dagger and slits its throat, drenching himself in blood.
19. **Fighting on the bridge** — Tyrion fights across crooked, waterlogged decks with a broken spear; Ser Balon Swann and Ser Mandon Moore fight back to back, surrounded by Velaryon spearmen.
20. **Arrow lodges in Tyrion's armor** — An arrow lodges between his shoulder and breastplate but he doesn't feel it during battle fever.
21. **A man falls from above** — A naked man falls from the sky and bursts on the deck, spattering blood through Tyrion's helm slit.
22. **The bridge begins breaking apart** — Stones crash through decks; the bridge shudders and twists; a loud crack sounds; the deck lurches and Tyrion slides into the water.
23. **Tyrion struggles in the water** — The river pours into his helm; he rips it off and crawls along the listing deck in neck-deep water.
24. **Tyrion observes battle confusion** — He sees a battle on what he initially thinks is the wrong bank, then realizes the deck has spun him around; he cannot identify who is fighting.
25. **Tyrion remembers his arrow wound** — He reaches for his aching shoulder and sees the arrow, remembering the injury.
26. **Ser Mandon offers his hand** — Ser Mandon stands on an adjacent deck calling Tyrion's name and extending his hand; Tyrion reaches for it.
27. **Ser Mandon slashes Tyrion's face** — At the last instant Tyrion notices Ser Mandon is extending his left hand; Ser Mandon's sword slashes just beneath Tyrion's eyes, cutting across his face.
28. **Tyrion falls into the river** — The shock of cold water jolts him; he finds a broken oar and climbs back up inch by inch, eyes full of water, mouth full of blood.
29. **Ser Mandon prepares to kill Tyrion** — Tyrion lies exhausted on the deck; Ser Mandon stands over him and places his sword point at Tyrion's throat.
30. **Ser Mandon is pushed overboard** — Ser Mandon lurches left, staggers into the rail; wood splits and he vanishes with a shout and a splash.
31. **Podrick Payne rescues Tyrion** — Someone kneels over Tyrion; he croaks "Jaime?" but a boy's voice tells him to be still; it sounds like Pod.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Transitions dramatically — starts commanding and focused, enters battle fever ("drunk with slaughter"), feels admiration for enemy courage, grows exhausted and confused as the bridge breaks apart, experiences shock and betrayal when Ser Mandon slashes his face, ends in helpless exhaustion and pain
- **Primary preoccupation:** Leading the defense of King's Landing; surviving combat; during battle fever, consumed entirely by the fight ("nothing but the instant"); later, simply staying alive and getting off the disintegrating bridge
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to allow Pod to ride with him despite his youth; decides to ride for the Mud Gate after relieving the King's Gate; decides to charge the bridge of wrecked ships ("Let's go kill them"); reaches for Ser Mandon's hand despite a moment of doubt
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion thinks "Who else would save him, if not his brother?" when rescued — reflexively assumes Jaime is the only one who would protect him, not considering his squire; he is unable or unwilling to fully register Ser Mandon's betrayal in the moment, noting only "something niggled at him" about the left hand
