# ACOK — Jon III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 24
- **pov_character:** Jon Snow
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon III
- **first_available:** ACOK Jon III
- **location_primary:** Craster's Keep (beyond the Wall, in the haunted forest)
- **location_secondary:** Haunted forest (line of march), the compound yard, the woods surrounding Craster's Keep
- **approximate_timeline:** During the Great Ranging; the Night's Watch column has been marching for some time north of the Wall, passing seven empty wildling villages before reaching Craster's Keep. They spend one night and depart the next morning.
- **time_markers:** Six days of rain; midafternoon described as dark as dusk in the forest; dusk approaching as Jon makes camp; waking at dawn the next morning with pale pink light; Mormont intends to ride within the hour of morning; Craster says he hasn't seen Benjen Stark for three years; Craster says he hasn't had good southron wine "for a bear's night"; Gilly says Nella has had six children
- **chapter_summary:** The Night's Watch column arrives at Craster's Keep after passing seven abandoned wildling villages. Lord Commander Mormont negotiates for shelter and exchanges an axe as a guest gift. Jon encounters Gilly, one of Craster's daughter-wives, who begs him to take her with the Watch because Craster gives his sons to "the cold gods" — beings with bright blue eyes. The next morning Jon reports what he's learned to Mormont, who reveals the rangers already knew about Craster's sacrifices. Mormont also shares that Craster has confirmed Mance Rayder is gathering the wildlings in the Frostfangs, and the Watch rides north to find him.

## Events & Actions
1. **Night's Watch column reaches Craster's Keep** — After six days of rain and seven abandoned villages, Buckwell's hunting horn signals that Craster's Keep is found and still occupied.
2. **Mormont orders Jon to spread the word** — Jon rides back along the column to announce arrival and warn officers that men must not trouble Craster's wives.
3. **Jon checks on Sam** — Finds Sam slumped in his saddle tending pack horses and ravens; tells him about Craster's Keep.
4. **Jon delivers message to Ser Ottyn Wythers** — Finds the exhausted knight with the rear guard and passes along Mormont's orders.
5. **Jon encounters Dywen and Grenn** — While riding through the forest, meets the outrider pair; they discuss Craster's wives; Ghost appears silently.
6. **Jon surveys Craster's Keep** — Observes the compound: midden heap, pigsty, empty sheepfold, windowless daub-and-wattle hall on a modest rise with earthen dike defenses.
7. **Jon enters the compound** — Passes through the gate flanked by animal skulls; sees scouts setting up camp, women working, piglets rooting, and dogs fighting.
8. **Jon enters Craster's hall** — Finds Mormont already negotiating with Craster; observes the squalid interior and Craster's appearance.
9. **Craster denies knowledge of Benjen Stark** — Says he hasn't seen Benjen for three years and never missed him. Recalls Ser Waymar Royce, Gared, and Will but claims not to track rangers' movements.
10. **Craster demands payment** — Asks for wine and a new axe in exchange for information and hospitality.
11. **Mormont offers Craster escort to the Wall** — Craster refuses, declaring himself free folk.
12. **Mormont describes the wight attacks** — Tells Craster about finding Othor and Jafer Flowers, their rising at Castle Black, and the killing of Ser Jaremy Rykker.
13. **Craster dismisses the wight story** — Claims to be a godly man protected by the gods; says he'd know how to send wights back to their graves.
14. **Craster hints at knowledge of Mance Rayder** — Reveals he had Mance's messenger's tongue cut out and nailed to the wall; offers to tell Mormont where to find Mance.
15. **Craster grants one night's shelter** — Hall floor for the officers, twenty men fed; the rest camp outside. Threatens hand-loss for anyone touching his wives.
16. **Jon volunteers Sam as mapmaker** — Mormont agrees and sends Jon to fetch Sam and Edd; orders Edd to bring the guest-gift axe.
17. **Craster identifies Jon as a Stark** — Notes Jon's appearance; calls him bastard and dismisses him.
18. **Dolorous Edd delivers the axe** — Comments darkly on the wisdom of arming Craster; describes the axe (short-hafted, gold inlay on black steel).
19. **Ghost kills Craster's rabbits** — Ghost breaks open a wooden hutch and kills two rabbits, terrifying one of Craster's wives (later revealed to be Gilly).
20. **Jon confronts the woman and meets hostility** — Lark the Sisterman and Chett mock Jon; Chett blames Jon for losing his position with Maester Aemon; Jon refuses to fight a brother beyond the Wall.
21. **Jon finds Sam and they cook the rabbit** — Jon builds a fire under a rock outcrop; they roast the scrawny rabbit and eat together.
22. **Sam goes to Craster's hall to map** — Jon sends Sam to draw Craster's map with quill and parchment.
23. **Jon sleeps and wakes to an ice-glazed forest** — Morning brings a crystalline landscape of frozen dew and ice.
24. **Gilly comes to Jon wearing Sam's cloak** — Reveals Sam sent her; asks if Jon is truly brother to a king.
25. **Gilly begs Jon to take her** — Offers to be his wife; explains Craster gives his sons to "the cold gods" — the white shadows with bright blue eyes; says Nella predicts her baby is a boy.
26. **Jon refuses** — Explains the Watch rides north, not south, and he cannot help her. Gilly flees in despair.
27. **Morning routines** — Jon breaks fast with the men (refuses to eat Craster's food); Dywen boasts about Craster's women; Dolorous Edd complains.
28. **Buckwell warns Jon** — Tells Jon to keep a good edge on his bastard sword.
29. **Jon enters the hall to see Mormont** — Mormont's raven lands on Longclaw; Mormont tells Jon to saddle his horse for departure.
30. **Jon finds Sam with Gilly** — Sam admits he told Gilly the Watch would take her on the return trip; Jon is angry.
31. **The Watch departs Craster's Keep** — Column rides north and west along a crooked game trail; crosses a brook in full spate; Ghost swims across.
32. **Jon tells Mormont about Craster's sons** — Reports that Craster has no sheep and no sons.
33. **Mormont reveals he already knew** — Says Smallwood told him long ago; all the rangers know. Explains Craster's sons are offerings to the wildling gods; pragmatically, Craster's Keep is too valuable to the Watch to act against him.
34. **Jon quotes his father** — Says Ned told him some men are not worth having; Mormont replies the Watch cannot set the world to rights.
35. **Mormont shares intelligence** — Mance Rayder is gathering all the wildlings in the Frostfangs, confirmed by both Craster and Ser Denys Mallister's intelligence. Mormont believes Mance means to strike south into the Seven Kingdoms.
36. **Mormont declares the Watch's mission** — "Find him. Fight him. Stop him." Jon thinks: three hundred against the fury of the wild.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Weary and physically miserable from six days of rain and heavy gear; rueful humor about the "haunted forest"; brief joy at the morning's icy beauty, quickly destroyed by Gilly's plight; anger at Sam for sending Gilly to him; shame and guilt despite believing he made the right choice; anxiety about what lies ahead (three hundred against the fury of the wild)
- **Primary preoccupation:** The immediate practical concerns of the ranging (shelter, food, following orders) give way to moral distress over Craster's sacrificing of sons and Gilly's desperate plea; then shifts to dread of the mission ahead as Mormont reveals the scale of the wildling threat
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses Gilly's plea to take her with the Watch; refuses to eat Craster's food ("I will not eat Craster's food"); refuses to fight Chett beyond the Wall ("I'll not fight a brother while we're beyond the Wall"); decides to report Craster's secret to Mormont
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells himself "We're here to fight wildlings, not save them" but immediately feels ashamed; his anger at Sam seems partly displaced from his own guilt
