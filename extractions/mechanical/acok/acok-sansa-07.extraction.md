# ACOK — Sansa VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK

- **location_secondary:** King's Landing (city at large), the Mud Gate, King's Gate, Iron Gate, Gate of the Gods, Flea Bottom, the Blackwater, Red Keep, Great Sept of Baelor
- **approximate_timeline:** During the climax and aftermath of the Battle of the Blackwater; begins during the battle's collapse and ends at dawn the following morning
- **time_markers:** Night (battle raging); torches burning low and flickering out during the ballroom scene; sky darker with only pale green ghosts against stars after Sandor leaves; first faint hint of dawn visible in the east when bells ring; transition from deep night to dawn across the chapter
- **chapter_summary:** As the Battle of the Blackwater turns dire, Ser Lancel and Osney Kettleblack report to Cersei that the battle is lost and the city is collapsing into chaos. Cersei orders Maegor's Holdfast sealed and Joffrey brought inside, then strikes Lancel and abandons the ballroom. Sansa calms the frightened women and children before retreating to her bedchamber, where Sandor Clegane is waiting, drunk and bloodied. He demands a song at knifepoint, and Sansa sings the Mother's Hymn; he leaves his white cloak behind. At dawn, bells ring across King's Landing and Ser Dontos brings news that Tywin Lannister, Mace Tyrell, and Randyll Tarly have routed Stannis's forces, with a figure in Renly's armor leading the vanguard.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ser Lancel reports the battle is lost** — He arrives in the ballroom bleeding, tells Cersei the battle is lost; Tyrion was on the bridge of boats when it broke apart; Ser Mandon is likely gone; the Hound has disappeared; gold cloaks are throwing down spears and running after seeing Joffrey leave.
2. **Osney Kettleblack reports further collapse** — Fighting on both sides of the river; some of Stannis's lords may be fighting each other; Ser Balon has fallen back inside the city; riverside belongs to the enemy; the King's Gate is being rammed; men deserting the walls and killing their officers; mobs at the Iron Gate and Gate of the Gods; Flea Bottom rioting.
3. **Cersei orders Maegor's sealed** — She tells Osfryd to raise the drawbridge and bar the doors; refuses re-entry to women who left to pray.
4. **Argument over Joffrey** — Lancel insists Joffrey must stay at the gatehouse to maintain morale; Cersei demands he be brought inside Maegor's; she tells Lancel to prove he's a Lannister and sends Osfryd to retrieve Joffrey.
5. **Cersei strikes Lancel and abandons the ballroom** — She slams her palm into his wound, causing him to cry out and nearly faint; she sweeps from the room without glancing at Sansa.
6. **Panic spreads in the ballroom** — An old woman wails that they're lost; children cry; guests rush to leave; servants begin abandoning the hall.
7. **Sansa takes charge** — She stands and tells the crowd not to be afraid, pointing out the thick walls, moat, and spikes; she says Joffrey is not hurt and the queen will return (the last being a lie); she commands Moon Boy to make them laugh.
8. **Moon Boy performs** — He does a cartwheel, vaults onto a table, and juggles wine cups, smashing them on his head; earns nervous laughter.
9. **Sansa aids Lancel** — She kneels beside the wounded knight, noting Cersei's blow reopened his wound; he gasps "the Imp was right"; Sansa and a serving man get him to his feet and she commands he be taken to Maester Frenken.
10. **Ser Dontos advises Sansa to flee** — He climbs the dais and whispers that she should lock herself in her bedchamber; says he'll come for her when the battle is done.
11. **Sansa retreats to her bedchamber** — She walks slowly from the ballroom, then runs up the stairs; a guard bumps into her on the stair and drops looted items (a jeweled cup and silver candlesticks).
12. **Sansa observes the burning sky** — She opens her drapes and sees the sky aswirl with green wildfire and orange flame, reflected on the clouds; embers drifting like fireflies; the air smells burnt.
13. **Sansa retreats to bed** — She backs away from the window, whimpers "Lady," wondering if she will meet her direwolf in death.
14. **Sandor Clegane seizes Sansa** — A hand grabs her wrist from the dark; another covers her mouth; he identifies himself as "Little bird," drunk and bloody.
15. **Sandor and Sansa converse** — He asks if she wants to know who's winning; says he's only lost; he wants the "bloody dwarf" burned, not dead; he announces he is leaving through the Iron Gate, heading north.
16. **Sandor demands a song** — He says she promised him a song; Sansa says she can't; he pushes her onto the bed and holds his dagger to her throat, demanding she sing.
17. **Sansa sings the Mother's Hymn** — Unable to remember "Florian and Jonquil," she sings "Gentle Mother, font of mercy" instead; after she finishes, Sandor removes the blade.
18. **Sansa touches Sandor's face** — She cups his cheek and feels blood and wetness that is not blood (tears); he says "Little bird" once more.
19. **Sandor leaves** — Sansa hears cloth ripping and retreating footsteps; she finds his white cloak on the floor, twisted, stained by blood and fire; she huddles beneath it.
20. **Bells ring across King's Landing** — Deep-throated bronze booming, joined by multiple bells from across the city and from the Great Sept of Baelor's seven crystal towers; unlike the slow death knell for King Robert, this is joyful thunder.
21. **Ser Dontos brings news of victory** — He bursts in, drunk and ecstatic; reports that Stannis is dead or fled, his host broken; a relief force came up the roseroad and the riverbank through Stannis's burned fields; Lord Tywin on the right, Randyll Tarly in the center, Mace Tyrell on the left; the vanguard was led by a figure in Renly's green armor with golden antlers, said to have killed Ser Guyard Morrigen and a dozen other knights.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terrified throughout — fears Ser Ilyn will execute her; briefly resolute when she takes charge of the crowd; deeply frightened during the encounter with Sandor; moved to compassion when she touches his face; relieved and bewildered at the news of victory
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival — whether she will be killed by Ser Ilyn Payne on Cersei's orders; secondary concern for the people around her
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to stand and calm the crowd rather than flee after Cersei; commands Moon Boy to perform; orders Lancel taken to Maester Frenken; follows Dontos's advice to retreat to her bedchamber; chooses to sing the Mother's Hymn when she cannot remember Florian and Jonquil; instinctively touches Sandor's face
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself "I am soft and weak and stupid, just as Joffrey says" for helping Lancel rather than wanting him dead — she frames her compassion as weakness, echoing her abuser's language
