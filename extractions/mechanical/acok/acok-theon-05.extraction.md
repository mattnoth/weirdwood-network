# ACOK — Theon V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 57

- **location_primary:** Winterfell
- **location_secondary:** Torrhen's Square (referenced), Deepwood Motte (referenced), Acorn Water (referenced), Stony Shore (referenced), Pyke (referenced), King's Landing (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** After Theon's return from Acorn Water; Asha arrives at Winterfell; army gathering against Theon
- **time_markers:** "first vague light of dawn" (Asha's arrival); nightmares recurring "of late"; "the very night they had returned from Acorn Water" (Gelmarr's death); "the next day" (Aggar's death); "one morning" (Rednose found); "before the moon turns" (expected army); Reek gone "before the sun went down"; "come dawn" (Theon walks the walls); "a brisk autumn wind"
- **chapter_summary:** Theon is woken from nightmares by Reek to learn Asha has arrived at Winterfell. She brings only twenty men and agrees to leave only ten. In a private meeting in Ned Stark's solar, Asha urges Theon to burn Winterfell and retreat to Deepwood Motte, warning him he cannot hold the castle so far inland. Theon refuses, and Asha departs. Reek offers to recruit men in exchange for a horse, coin, and the woman Palla; Theon agrees. After another nightmare—a feast with the dead including figures he recognizes as Lyanna, Brandon, and Rickard Stark—Theon is unable to sleep. At dawn he walks the walls and reveals to the reader that the tarred heads on the gatehouse are actually the miller's boys, not Bran and Rickon.

## Events & Actions
1. **Theon's nightmare** — Dreams of wolves the size of horses with children's heads chasing him through a frozen forest with laughing weirwood faces; wakes flailing for his dagger.
2. **Reek delivers news** — Reek and Wex wake Theon to inform him Asha has arrived at Winterfell.
3. **Theon prepares carefully** — Washes, initially dresses in satin and leather, then changes to wool and ringmail after remembering Asha values weapons over beauty; dons his iron crown.
4. **Recap of recent murders** — Narration reveals Gelmarr, Aggar, and Gynir Rednose were killed within Winterfell after their return from Acorn Water.
5. **Theon executes Farlen** — Theon condemned Farlen for the killings to blame someone; botched the execution (four blows), was sick afterward. Killings stopped after this.
6. **Black Lorren's counsel recalled** — Lorren warned about living among enemies; advised leaving Winterfell. Theon refused.
7. **Theon suspects Asha** — Believes Asha wants him dead to steal his place as their father's heir; this is why she ignored his commands.
8. **Theon confronts Asha in Great Hall** — Finds her in the high seat; she brought only twenty men. She mocks him as "Prince of Fools."
9. **Private discussion in Ned's solar** — Asha reveals Dagmer lost at Torrhen's Square; Theon reports enemies gathering (Tallhart, Manderly, Umbers). Asha urges him to burn Winterfell and retreat to Deepwood Motte.
10. **Theon refuses to leave** — Declares he will hold Winterfell.
11. **Asha's parting warning** — "Then hold it you shall, for the rest of your life." Mocks his crown. Departs through Hunter's Gate with ten men.
12. **Reek's offer** — Reek offers to recruit a hundred or two hundred men from the north if given a horse and coin; asks for Palla as reward. Theon agrees.
13. **Reek departs** — Leaves before sundown with a bag of Stark silver.
14. **Dream of the feast with the dead** — Theon dreams of Robert's visit feast that transforms into dining with the dead: Robert, Eddard, Winterfell men, Lyanna with blue roses, Brandon, Rickard, and finally Robb with Grey Wind bleeding from wounds.
15. **Theon wakes screaming** — Guards burst in; Luwin is summoned and leaves a sleeping draught.
16. **Theon pours out draught** — Distrusts Luwin; fears being poisoned.
17. **Theon brutalizes Kyra** — Summons her, has violent sex, sends her away bruised and sobbing.
18. **Dawn walk on the walls** — Theon reflects he is a Greyjoy, not a Stark; regrets not leaving with Asha.
19. **The reveal about the heads** — Theon gazes at the gatehouse heads and reveals they are the miller's boys, not Bran and Rickon; Reek flayed and tarred them to make them unrecognizable.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Guilt-ridden (recurring nightmares of those he's killed or caused to die); fearful (of assassination within Winterfell, of the gathering army); angry (at Asha's mockery and inadequate help); isolated and paranoid (distrusts Luwin, suspects Asha wants him dead); regretful (wishes he'd gone with Asha; "How did I come to this?")
- **Primary preoccupation:** Holding Winterfell with insufficient men while enemies gather and his own people are murdered around him; the secret that Bran and Rickon are alive
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses Asha's counsel to retreat; sends Reek to recruit men with Stark silver; refuses Luwin's sleeping draught
- **Self-deception flags:** Repeatedly tells himself "I had no choice" about the miller's boys; claims it was "blood for blood" vengeance for Rodrik and Maron (but the reasoning is improvised—"the words tumbled out heedlessly"); tells Asha "They brought their fate on themselves"; believes Asha wants him dead to steal his inheritance (may or may not be true); convinces himself Luwin might poison him
