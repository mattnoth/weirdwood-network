- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn prays in a deserted village sept on the eve of battle, reflecting on her family and the implications of Cersei's children being Jaime's. She returns to Renly's camp and enters his pavilion where Brienne is armoring him for battle while Lords Tarly and Rowan discuss tactics. Catelyn proposes that Renly, Stannis, and Robb call a Great Council, but Renly dismisses the idea. A shadow enters the tent and kills Renly by cutting through his gorget with an immaterial blade. When guards burst in and blame Brienne, Catelyn defends her and the two women escape together into the chaos of the disintegrating army.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at deserted village** — Catelyn and her escorts find an abandoned village after dark; the people have fled with all their possessions
2. **Catelyn enters sept** — Ser Wendel lights a torch and leads her through the low door of the seven-walled sept; he and Ser Robar wait outside
3. **Catelyn studies the charcoal faces** — She observes crude drawings of the Seven on the cracked walls; the Stranger's face is a black oval with stars for eyes, making her uneasy
4. **Prayer to the Mother** — Catelyn kneels and prays for her sons Robb, Bran, and Rickon; a crack through the Mother's left eye makes it look like she is crying
5. **Catelyn's reflections** — The flickering torchlight makes the faces shift; she sees her father, Renly, Stannis, Robb, Robert, Jaime, Jon Snow, Arya, her mother Minisa, Lysa, and Cersei in the charcoal drawings
6. **Reflection on Cersei and the incest** — Catelyn considers that Cersei is a mother too; reasons that if Joffrey is truly Jaime's, Robert would have killed both mother and children; concludes Ned and Jon Arryn knew and the queen killed them both
7. **Realization about Bran** — Catelyn whispers that Bran must have seen or heard something about the incest, which is why they tried to kill him
8. **Prayers to all Seven** — Catelyn prays to the Smith for Bran, the Maid for Arya and Sansa, the Father for justice, the Warrior for Robb, and the Crone for guidance
9. **Ser Robar summons Catelyn** — He tells her they must return before dawn
10. **Ride back to camp** — They ride through sparse woodland; Renly's army is arrayed in darkness, lances pointing upward; Storm's End is a wall of black; Stannis's camp visible by torchlight
11. **Catelyn enters Renly's pavilion** — The green silk tent glows with emerald candlelight; Ser Parmen and Ser Emmon stand sentry; inside, Brienne armors Renly while Lords Tarly and Rowan discuss tactics
12. **Battle debate** — Lord Rowan urges immediate attack; Renly insists on waiting for dawn to avoid the charge of treachery; Tarly notes they'll charge into the rising sun; Renly expresses confidence in Ser Loras
13. **Renly orders Stannis's corpse respected** — He commands that no insult be done to Stannis's body as they are blood
14. **Siege of Storm's End recalled** — Lord Rowan mentions Mace Tyrell's siege; Renly recounts how Ser Gawen Wylde tried to surrender and Stannis ordered him catapulted from the walls; Maester Cressen intervened; Gawen died in his cell; the Onion Knight saved them
15. **Renly asks for Barristan Selmy to be spared** — He believes Selmy must be with Stannis since he went to neither Renly nor Robb; the lords agree
16. **Catelyn proposes a Great Council** — She tells Renly that Bran's near-assassination connects to Jaime and Cersei's incest; she asks leave to go to Stannis; proposes all three kings set aside their crowns for a Great Council to decide rulership
17. **Renly dismisses the proposal** — He laughs and asks if direwolves vote on who leads the pack; declares the time for talk is done
18. **Shadow assassination of Renly** — A gust of wind opens the tent door; the shadow lifts a sword; Renly says "Cold" and his gorget parts like cheesecloth; blood gushes from his throat; he collapses into Brienne's arms
19. **Guards accuse Brienne** — Ser Robar and Ser Emmon burst in with men-at-arms; seeing Brienne drenched in blood, Ser Emmon calls her a wicked, vile creature and attacks with a battle-axe
20. **Brienne fights** — She snatches Renly's sword and catches the axe blow; shears through the axe haft; cuts off a man-at-arms' hand and torch; parries the second man-at-arms; holds off Emmon and the remaining attacker simultaneously
21. **Catelyn convinces Ser Robar** — She seizes his arm and swears on Ned's grave and her honor as a Stark that Stannis killed Renly through sorcery; Ser Robar agrees to hold off pursuers
22. **Catelyn strikes Ser Emmon** — She crashes an iron brazier into the back of his head, sending him to his knees
23. **Escape through the tent** — Brienne slashes through the green silk wall; they step out into darkness and dawn chill; the pavilion is ablaze behind them
24. **Brienne vows vengeance** — She swears to kill Stannis with Renly's own sword
25. **Joining the escort** — They reach Hal Mollen, Ser Wendel, Ser Perwyn Frey, and the rest; Catelyn tells Brienne to leave her armor and horse and take a spare mount
26. **Escape ride** — They ride out as dawn breaks; Renly's army disintegrates; Catelyn realizes all of Renly's power now belongs to Stannis
27. **Catelyn recalls Stannis's threat** — She remembers Stannis declaring himself the rightful king and calling Robb a traitor whose day will come

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief-stricken and exhausted; weighed down by loss of Ned, separation from her children, sense of futility ("Who have I served?"); she has stopped eating, food having "lost its savor in a world without Ned"; deeply shaken by Renly's assassination; shifts to urgency and self-command during the escape; ends with a chill of fear recalling Stannis's threat against Robb
- **Primary preoccupation:** Protecting her children (prayer for Robb, Bran, Rickon, Arya, Sansa); guilt over being absent from them; the political crisis of the war; after the assassination, survival and escape
- **Key decisions made:** Proposes the Great Council to Renly; identifies Stannis as Renly's killer and stakes her honor on Brienne's innocence; commands Brienne to flee with her; orders her escort to cut down anyone who tries to stop them; tells Brienne to abandon her armor and horse
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself there was no time to eat, then admits "the truth was that food had lost its savor"; hopes Robb would agree to set aside his crown while acknowledging "hoping it was true" and that "his lords would not" agree
