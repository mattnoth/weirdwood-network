- **chapter_summary:** Bran presides over audiences at Winterfell during the harvest feast as the Stark in Winterfell. Lord Wyman Manderly proposes new customs officers, a royal mint, and a warfleet for Robb. Lady Donella Hornwood warns of the Bolton bastard massing men at the Dreadfort and becomes the center of a succession crisis as multiple lords seek her hand or her lands. Bran visits the godswood where Osha asks about his wolf dreams. The chapter ends with Bran reacting with terror when Cley Cerwyn mentions Stannis's letter alleging Jaime and Cersei's incest, and suffering a nightmare of the three-eyed crow.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran wakes before dawn** — Lies awake dreading the day of governance rather than jousting
2. **Hodor carries Bran down** — Bran dresses in white wool and silver brooch; Hodor places him in basket
3. **Bran watches the jousting** — Observes the Walders tilting at quintains in the courtyard; White Harbor squires also practicing
4. **Walders mock Hodor** — Little Walder calls Hodor an ugly horse; both Freys taunt him; Bran threatens to set Summer on them
5. **Maester Luwin intervenes** — Breaks up confrontation; scolds both Freys; orders Little Walder to stay away from godswood and wolves
6. **Lord Manderly's audience** — Proposes new customs officers, royal mint for Robb, and a warfleet; mentions Brandon the Burner; Ser Rodrik defers to Robb
7. **Manderly raises Lady Hornwood and marriage** — Proposes himself or his unwed son Wendel as husband for Lady Hornwood
8. **Manderly reveals Tywin's letter** — Lord Tywin offers to release Ser Wylis without ransom if Manderly withdraws from the war; Manderly refuses
9. **Lady Hornwood arrives at nightfall** — Brings only six men-at-arms with moosehead badge on dusty orange livery
10. **Lady Hornwood's audience** — Warns of Bolton bastard massing men at Dreadfort; describes Ramsay and Reek; expresses fear for her lands
11. **Ser Rodrik suggests remarriage** — Lady Hornwood rejects Mors Crowfood and Lord Manderly; hints interest in Ser Rodrik himself
12. **Hornwood succession discussion** — Luwin, Rodrik, and Bran discuss competing claims (Tallharts, Flints, Karstarks, Glovers with the bastard Larence Snow, Bolton)
13. **Bran suggests legitimizing Hornwood's bastard** — Thinking of Jon Snow; Ser Rodrik notes this would not please Lady Hornwood
14. **Bran visits godswood** — Finds Summer; glimpses Shaggydog; Hodor takes him to the pool beneath the heart tree
15. **Osha emerges from the pool** — Naked, having swum to try to touch the bottom; discusses her scars, the Freys, and Bran's wolf dreams
16. **Osha warns about Little Walder** — "Big outside, little inside, and mean down to the bones"
17. **Bran denies having wolf dreams** — Osha sees through the lie: "A prince should lie better than that"
18. **Bran's weirwood dream** — Dreams the weirwood calls to him; the three-eyed crow pecks at his face and calls his name
19. **Umber men arrive at night** — Horns and boisterous shouting; banner shows giant in shattered chains
20. **Mors and Hother Umber petition** — Mors asks to wed Lady Hornwood; Hother asks for ships against wildlings crossing Bay of Seals
21. **Ser Rodrik proposes Manderly-Umber collaboration on ships** — Manderly has shipwrights, Umbers have timber
22. **Glover steward arrives** — Reports only a tenth of harvest being set aside; Luwin scolds about hedge wizards; commanded to set aside a fifth; steward praises Larence Snow
23. **Leobald Tallhart proposes fostering Beren** — Offers younger son Beren (near ten) to Lady Hornwood as foster/potential heir
24. **Bran speaks up about Tallhart proposal** — Thanks Leobald before Ser Rodrik can speak; Leobald shows pity for the crippled prince
25. **Ravens arrive from absent lords** — Bolton bastard won't come; Mormonts and Karstarks gone south; Lord Locke too old; Lady Flint with child; sickness at Widow's Watch; Howland Reed silent as always
26. **Cley Cerwyn arrives** — Fourteen-year-old with two dozen lances; greets Bran cheerfully
27. **Cley reveals Stannis's letter** — Stannis claims Cersei bedded Jaime, making Joffrey a bastard
28. **Bran has a panic reaction** — Feels he cannot breathe; giant hand crushing chest; feels as if falling; clutches Dancer's reins
29. **Bran's nightmare of the three-eyed crow** — Crow pecks out both eyes, drives beak into skull; Bran sees himself clinging to a tower; golden man pulls him up and tosses him into the air saying "The things I do for love"

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Frustrated and yearning (wants to be a knight, not a lord); ashamed when scolded; proud when praised; terrified when incest is mentioned; resigned to duty
- **Primary preoccupation:** The contrast between what he wants (knighthood, jousting, physical activity) and what he must do (governance); also avoiding/denying his wolf dreams
- **Key decisions made:** Defends Hodor against the Freys; suggests legitimizing Lord Hornwood's bastard (thinking of Jon); speaks up to thank Leobald Tallhart; denies wolf dreams to Osha
- **Self-deception flags:** Lies to Osha about not having wolf dreams; does not consciously connect his panic reaction (crushing chest, feeling of falling) to the incest revelation about Jaime/Cersei — his body remembers what his conscious mind has suppressed about his fall
