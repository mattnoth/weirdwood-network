Tyrion threatens that whatever befalls the captive will also befall Tommen, intimidating Cersei into retreat. He returns to the Tower of the Hand to find Shae safe in his bed, brought through secret passages by Varys.

## Events & Actions
1. **Pod dresses Tyrion** — Tyrion is dressed in a plush velvet tunic of Lannister crimson for supper with Cersei. He deliberately leaves his Hand of the King chain on the bedside table.
2. **Varys delivers urgent news** — Varys intercepts Tyrion in the yard with a parchment reporting from the north that both Stark boys are dead. Tyrion recalls wolves howling when "the Stark boy" fell.
3. **Tyrion decides to deliver the news personally** — He wants to see how Cersei reacts to the news.
4. **Tyrion arrives at Cersei's supper** — Cersei is elaborately dressed. Tyrion waits until he is seated and served wine before handing her the letter.
5. **Cersei reacts to the Stark boys' deaths** — She blames Jaime for pushing Bran from the window ("for love"), calls it stupid and dangerous, and insists the killings were Greyjoy's work.
6. **Discussion of Jaime's safety** — Tyrion warns that Lady Catelyn might kill Jaime in retaliation. Cersei panics briefly. They agree Sansa is their leverage.
7. **Elaborate supper is served** — Multiple courses described in detail. Tyrion cautiously eats only what Cersei eats, watching for poison.
8. **Discussion of Littlefinger at Bitterbridge** — Cersei expresses distrust of Littlefinger; Tyrion argues Stannis would not work with him.
9. **Lady Tanda's bribe discussed** — The ham came from Lady Tanda, who wants to return to Stokeworth. Tyrion prefers she stay and bring her garrison.
10. **Discussion of Tyrion's clansmen** — Cersei questions why Tyrion sent his "savages" away. Tyrion explains they are better as irregular fighters in the kingswood than as soldiers on city walls.
11. **The Antler Men conspiracy** — Cersei asks about the conspiracy of men plotting to open the gates for Stannis. Tyrion says Varys claims they've found them all.
12. **Cersei warns about Varys** — She argues Varys manipulates everyone, giving each person just enough to seem indispensable. She says he played the same game with her when she first married Robert.
13. **Reassignment of the Hound** — Varys has told Cersei that Tyrion plans to take the Hound from Joffrey. Tyrion confirms he needs Clegane and Balon Swann to lead sorties on the Blackwater.
14. **Joffrey's role in battle debated** — Tyrion argues Joffrey should be visible to inspire the troops, with gold cloaks around him. Cersei is reluctant. Joffrey is thirteen.
15. **Tyrion reassures about the city** — He says the city won't fall, but internally prays Lord Tywin can march to their relief if it does.
16. **Tyrion offers concessions** — He proposes to release Lord Gyles and return Ser Boros Blount. Cersei rejects Boros but asks about Tommen.
17. **Tyrion refuses to return Tommen** — He insists Tommen is safer under Lord Jacelyn's protection.
18. **Cersei reveals her trump card** — After dessert, she announces she has captured Tyrion's "little whore."
19. **Tyrion tries to feign indifference** — He makes quips and avoids naming Shae, trying to assess how much Cersei knows.
20. **Cersei states her terms** — She accuses Tyrion of scheming to kill Joffrey and rule through Tommen. She threatens that the captive will die painfully if Joffrey or Tommen is harmed.
21. **Alayaya is brought in** — Osney and Osfryd Kettleblack drag in Alayaya — beaten, gagged, bound, with a broken lip and bruises. Tyrion realizes Cersei has the wrong woman.
22. **Tyrion plays along** — Rather than reveal the mistake, he asks Cersei to swear she'll release the woman after the battle.
23. **Tyrion counter-threatens** — He threatens that whatever happens to Alayaya will happen to Tommen, including beatings and rape. He adopts his father's cold, flat voice.
24. **Physical confrontation with Cersei** — Cersei slaps at Tyrion; he catches her wrist, bends it back until she cries out, and shoves her to the floor. Osfryd moves to intervene but stops when Tyrion threatens to break Cersei's arm.
25. **Alayaya freed** — Tyrion orders the Kettleblacks to untie and ungag her. He massages her fingers as circulation returns.
26. **Alayaya kisses Tyrion** — She kisses him on the brow with her broken lips, leaving a smear of blood. Tyrion thinks "a bloody kiss is more than I deserve."
27. **Tyrion delivers his threat to Cersei** — "I will hurt you for this... A day will come when you think yourself safe and happy, and suddenly your joy will turn to ashes in your mouth."
28. **Cersei breaks** — Tyrion internally compares it to his father's teaching about armies breaking in battle. Cersei can only say "Get out!"
29. **Tyrion returns to Tower of the Hand** — His legs ache from the climb. He sends Pod for wine. He finds Shae in his canopied bed, nude but for the golden chain of hands.
30. **Shae describes the secret passage** — Varys brought her hooded through a passage: down a ladder, through an iron gate, past a red-and-black dragon mosaic floor, up another ladder, through a low tunnel.
31. **Tyrion searches for the hidden entrance** — He tries a loose sconce (revolves but reveals nothing), the rear panel of his wardrobe (solid), and a stone by the window seat (immovable). He fails to find the door.
32. **Tyrion cannot perform sexually** — Despite Shae's efforts, he goes soft and cannot be roused. He is troubled by guilt over Alayaya's beating.
33. **Tyrion lies awake** — He lies awake long after Shae falls asleep, reflecting.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious, calculating, guilty, angry, and ultimately despairing. Shifts from strategic curiosity (watching Cersei's reaction to the Stark news) to controlled fury when Alayaya is revealed beaten. Guilt weighs on him — Alayaya was hurt because of him. Returns to his chambers with a headache and stress. Cannot perform sexually. Lies awake haunted.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Managing the crisis with Cersei — protecting Shae's identity, maintaining leverage over Tommen, and navigating the power dynamic. Secondarily, the approaching battle with Stannis.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Decides to tell Cersei the Stark news himself to gauge her reaction. (2) Plays along with Cersei's mistake about Alayaya rather than revealing Shae. (3) Counter-threatens with harm to Tommen. (4) Adopts his father's cold voice and manner to project ruthlessness. (5) Promises to release Lord Gyles. (6) Promises future revenge against Cersei.
- **Self-deception flags:** "Not that he truly thought she'd poison him, but it never hurt to be careful" — downplaying real paranoia. Internal voice calls him "fool" for thinking with his cock, for not anticipating Cersei's move. Tells himself "If she thinks me such a monster, I'll play the part for her" — justifying an extreme threat as theater.
