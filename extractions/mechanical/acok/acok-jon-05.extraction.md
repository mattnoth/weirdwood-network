## Events & Actions
1. **Horn blast wakes the camp** — A single long, low horn note sounds in the night. The camp stirs; men reach for weapons. All wait tensely for a possible second blast (which would signal wildlings), but it does not come.
2. **Mormont emerges** — The Lord Commander exits his tent and confirms one blast. He orders Jon to prepare hot food for the arriving men and fodder for horses, and to bring Qhorin to him immediately.
3. **Jon wakes Edd and Hake** — Jon finds Dolorous Edd at the fire; together they wake Hake, who curses but sets a dozen brothers to cutting roots for soup.
4. **Sam asks about Benjen** — Sam approaches Jon and asks if his uncle has returned. Jon says it is only the Shadow Tower men and reflects on the fading hope for Benjen's return.
5. **Shadow Tower men arrive** — The guards open the ringwall. Qhorin's men come up the slope — many wounded, some riding double. Jon notes they look hard and shaggy, clad in leather, fur, and bits of steel or bronze.
6. **Jon meets Qhorin Halfhand** — Jon recognizes Qhorin instantly from his reputation. Qhorin recognizes Jon as having his father's look and mentions knowing Lord Eddard and Lord Rickard Stark.
7. **Qhorin reports to Mormont** — Inside the tent, Qhorin reports that he intercepted Alfyn Crowkiller's scouting party returning along the Wall. Alfyn is dead; some of his company escaped. The cost was four brothers dead and a dozen wounded.
8. **Intelligence from the captive** — Qhorin reveals that they took prisoners; one died, the other lived long enough to be questioned. The captive reported Mance has gathered enormous strength — wargs, mammoths, Rattleshirt, the Weeping Man, and every other chief. Ebben doubts the captive's claims.
9. **Wall defense discussed** — Mormont and Qhorin discuss the need to warn the Wall and the kings. Qhorin says the best hope is Winterfell and the Starks rallying the north. They review which abandoned castles might be garrisoned.
10. **Mance's plan revealed** — Qhorin reveals the wildlings do not plan to climb or tunnel under the Wall — they plan to break it through sorcery. Mance is searching for some unknown power in the Frostfangs.
11. **Scouting plan proposed** — Qhorin proposes sending fifteen men in three parties of five: one to probe the Milkwater, one the Skirling Pass, one to climb the Giant's Stair. Led by Jarman Buckwell, Thoren Smallwood, and Qhorin himself.
12. **Mormont reluctantly agrees** — Mormont consents, and Qhorin argues that even if they die, their deaths will buy time for the Wall's defense.
13. **Qhorin chooses Jon** — Qhorin selects Jon Snow for his party. Mormont objects that Jon is hardly more than a boy and his steward, but Qhorin invokes the old gods and the Stark name. Jon says he wants to go. Mormont sadly agrees.
14. **Recalled: prior debate among commanders** — Jon recalls the earlier argument between Thoren Smallwood (attack up the Milkwater), Ser Ottyn Wythers (retreat to Castle Black), Ser Mallador Locke (strike for Shadow Tower), and Jarman Buckwell (caution about dangerous wildling leaders). No agreement was reached; Mormont chose to wait.
15. **Recalled: Jon overhears mutinous talk** — Jon recalls overhearing Chett and Lark the Sisterman the previous night, complaining about the ranging and hinting at possible insubordination ("Might be we won't give him one"). Jon decided not to inform on them.
16. **Recalled: dragonglass distribution** — Jon reflects on the dragonglass items he fashioned and distributed — daggers for himself, Grenn, and Mormont; the warhorn to Sam; spearhead and arrowheads to Sam; the rest among friends for luck.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Restless and anxious from the long wait at the Fist; relieved that the Shadow Tower men have finally arrived and a decision can no longer be delayed; eager to act ("If they must battle Mance Rayder, let it be soon"); troubled by overhearing mutinous talk from Chett and Lark but conflicted about informing on brothers; curious and alert during Qhorin's briefing; immediate willingness to volunteer for the scouting mission
- **Primary preoccupation:** The stalled situation at the Fist — the waiting, the uncertainty, the lack of decision; secondarily the mystery of the dragonglass cache and Benjen's fate
- **Key decisions made:** Decides not to inform Mormont about Chett and Lark's mutinous talk, rationalizing it as empty words from cold, frightened men; immediately volunteers to join Qhorin's scouting party
- **Self-deception flags:** Dismisses the mutinous talk as "just empty talk" — "They are cold and afraid; we all are" — rationalizing away a potential threat. Jon tells himself it was nothing, but the text signals it was significant enough to stick with him.
