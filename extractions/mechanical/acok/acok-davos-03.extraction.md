- **chapter_summary:** Davos commands Black Betha in Stannis's fleet as it enters the Blackwater Rush to assault King's Landing. He notes the chain boom towers but the chain is not yet raised. The fleet engages Joffrey's smaller naval force; Davos rams and sinks Lady's Shame, then boards and captures White Hart. Wildfire appears in increasing quantities. The Lannister-prepared hulk full of wildfire is rammed by Swordfish, causing a massive explosion that destroys dozens of ships. Davos is thrown into the water and swept downstream, where he sees the chain has been raised, trapping the burning fleet.

## Events & Actions
1. **Fleet forms battle lines** — Davos commands Black Betha as Stannis's fleet organizes into formation entering Blackwater Bay; sails lowered, oars deployed per Ser Imry's orders.
2. **Fleet enters the Blackwater Rush** — The first line of battle sweeps into the river mouth on oars; Davos observes the chain boom towers but the chain is not raised and the river is open.
3. **Davos spots the chain** — He notices sunlight on steel at the base of the south tower and recognizes it as a chain boom, wondering why it hasn't been closed.
4. **Enemy fleet sighted** — Joffrey's galleys are drawn in a thin line across the river; Davos notes several major ships are missing (Lionstar, Lady Lyanna, King Robert's Hammer).
5. **First line engages** — Ships crash together; boarding actions begin; catapults fire from Fury; trebuchets from behind Mud Gate hurl boulders.
6. **Defensive fire rains on starboard wing** — Pots of burning pitch from the Red Keep and arrows from towers strike ships in the first and second lines; Queen Alysanne and Dragonsbane take fire; Courageous burns.
7. **Troops land on south bank** — Devotion drives to shore and lands archers; Prayer and Piety follow; King's Landing cavalry (led by the Hound) charges the landing forces.
8. **Davos rams Lady's Shame** — Black Betha and Lady Marya simultaneously ram Lady's Shame (locked with Queen Alysanne and Lady of Silk), sinking her and freeing Queen Alysanne from the trap.
9. **Wildfire appears on Queen Alysanne** — Green flames erupt from Queen Alysanne's stern; the fire spreads rapidly across the locked ships.
10. **Bold Laughter destroyed** — A boulder from the trebuchets smashes the Velaryon galley, which explodes like a toy.
11. **Davos boards and captures White Hart** — After ramming a glancing blow against a pleasure barge, Black Betha is nearly rammed by White Hart; Davos turns to face her, boards, and his crew takes the ship after hand-to-hand fighting.
12. **Battle becomes general chaos** — Multiple ships burning, sinking, or captured on both sides; Stannis's army begins crossing on smaller ships.
13. **Swordfish rams the wildfire hulk** — Swordfish, already burning from pitch in her rigging, rams a Lannister hulk leaking green liquid; the hulk bursts open releasing massive wildfire.
14. **Massive wildfire explosion** — The wildfire ignites catastrophically; a swirling demon of green flame fifty feet high destroys dozens of ships on both sides (Black Betha, White Hart, Fury, Pride of Driftmark, and many others).
15. **Davos thrown into the river** — The explosion destroys his ship; he surfaces clinging to debris, swept by current toward the bay.
16. **Chain is raised** — Davos sees the chain boom is now taut across the river mouth; a dozen galleys have crashed into it; all are aflame. The fleet is trapped between the chain and the wildfire.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious and fearful before battle (maiden to naval warfare); professional alertness during engagement; growing dread as wildfire appears; horror at the wildfire trap; desperate panic when thrown into water
- **Primary preoccupation:** The chain boom and its implications — why hasn't it been raised? The missing ships. His sons' safety. The tactical folly of Ser Imry's headlong charge.
- **Key decisions made:** Rams Lady's Shame; boards White Hart personally; orders retreat from White Hart when he sees the wildfire hulk threat (too late)
- **Self-deception flags:** None — Davos is clear-eyed throughout; his tactical concerns prove entirely justified
