# ACOK — Tyrion XV

## Chapter Metadata

- **approximate_timeline:** Aftermath of the Battle of the Blackwater; days or possibly weeks after the battle, given partial wound healing and Lord Tywin's arrival and installation as Hand
- **time_markers:** Multiple cycles of waking and sleeping; "It was dark when he woke again"; wakes in darkness repeatedly; enough time has passed since the battle for wounds to partially heal, for stitches to be placed, for arrow wound to show and then be treated for mortification, and for Lord Tywin to arrive and settle into the Tower of the Hand; Tyrion has been fed milk of the poppy multiple times
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion Lannister drifts in and out of consciousness in the aftermath of the Battle of the Blackwater, suffering from fever, a broken rib, an arrow wound, and a massive facial wound inflicted by Ser Mandon Moore. He experiences delirium-dreams of the battlefield dead, a fantasy victory feast, and a memory of Tysha. When he finally forces Maester Ballabar to remove his bandages, he discovers three-quarters of his nose is gone and a jagged scar runs across his face. He learns that Lord Tywin has arrived in King's Landing, taken over as Hand of the King, and is occupying Tyrion's former chambers in the Tower of the Hand. From Podrick Payne he confirms Ser Mandon Moore drowned, and orders the boy to say nothing about the Kingsguard's attempt on his life.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion wakes among the wounded** — He regains consciousness briefly in a room with a cracked stone ceiling, surrounded by groaning, whimpering, and screaming men, the air full of acrid smoke and smells of blood, shit, and burnt flesh. He has fouled his own bedding. He is too weak to move.
2. **Battlefield delirium-dream** — Tyrion walks through a colorless world outside the city, seeing corpses on the tourney fields, silent sisters stripping the dead, wolves feeding, pyres burning, the river choked with sunken ships. He sees surcoats bearing desaturated heraldry (black hearts, grey lions, dead flowers, pale ghostly stags). He thinks: "They died at my command."
3. **Dream of having no mouth** — In the dream, Tyrion discovers smooth skin covers where his mouth should be. He runs toward the city but the gates are shut against him.
4. **Tyrion wakes in a bed** — He comes to in darkness, finds himself in a bed with carved bedposts, velvet canopy, featherbed, and goose-down pillow. He is sweating with fever.
5. **Memory of Ser Mandon's attack** — The battle returns in fragments: the fight along the river, the knight who offered his gauntlet, the bridge of ships. Then the vivid memory of Ser Mandon Moore — dead empty eyes, reaching hand, green fire reflected on white enamel plate. Tyrion is overcome with fear and wets the bed.
6. **Semi-conscious visitors** — Tyrion dreams (but likely experiences) Cersei and Lord Tywin standing over his bed, Varys sighing, Littlefinger quipping. He dismisses them as dreams since he believes Tywin is far away fighting Robb Stark.
7. **Tyrion reasons the battle was won** — He deduces that if he still lives, they must have won, and is pleased both by the victory and by the return of his reasoning ability.
8. **Pod appears with a candle; bandage discovery** — Podrick Payne enters with a candle, sees Tyrion's eyes open, and runs off. Tyrion discovers his face is covered in a mask of hardened plaster with breathing and feeding holes, wrapped in stiff linen.
9. **Maester administers milk of the poppy** — A maester (later identified as Ballabar) arrives with Pod, inserts a curved copper funnel through the feeding hole, and pours milk of the poppy down Tyrion's throat.
10. **Dream of victory feast** — Tyrion dreams of being hailed as a hero at a feast. Marillion plays his woodharp and sings of the Imp's daring deeds. Lord Tywin smiles with approval. Jaime knights Tyrion with a golden sword. Shae embraces him and calls him "her giant of Lannister."
11. **Tyrion wakes in wrong room** — He wakes alone, realizes something feels wrong. He tears down the bed draperies and sees bare walls, a narrow window, his chest, clothes, and battered armor. He realizes this is not his bedchamber or the Tower of the Hand — someone has moved him.
12. **Dream/memory of Tysha** — Extended, tender dream of the cottage by the sunset sea — Tysha teasing him about lazy servants, kissing, lying in bed listening to waves, her singing "I loved a maid as fair as summer." Her declaration of love. Tyrion wakes bitterly thinking it was all lies: "she was a whore, Jaime's whore, Jaime's gift, my lady of the lie."
13. **Tyrion refuses milk of the poppy** — When Maester Ballabar tries to administer more, Tyrion grabs his chain of many metals and twists it into the man's fat neck until he can barely breathe, croaking "No. More."
14. **Tyrion demands bandages removed** — Through gestures and grunted words, Tyrion forces Ballabar to understand he wants the mask off, despite the maester's protests that the queen would not approve.
15. **Bandage removal** — Ballabar uses a long knife with a sawtooth blade to cut through the bandage beneath Tyrion's chin. He washes the wound with warm water, then applies wine with crushed herbs that causes burning agony across Tyrion's face.
16. **Maester describes Tyrion's injuries** — Ballabar reveals: Tyrion was found in a cellar among the dead and dying, wounds filthy; a broken rib (from mace blow or fall); an arrow in the arm at the shoulder showing signs of mortification, treated with boiling wine and maggots, now healing clean.
17. **Tyrion sees his face** — Using a silvered looking glass in an ornate golden frame, Tyrion sees a long, crooked gash from below his left eye to the right side of his jaw, three-quarters of his nose gone, a chunk of lip missing, clumsy catgut stitches across raw red flesh. He says "Pretty" and flings the glass aside.
18. **Tyrion processes the assassination attempt** — He reasons that Ser Mandon would have taken off the top of his head if he hadn't pulled back. He reflects that Jaime always called Ser Mandon the most dangerous Kingsguard. He concludes Cersei must have paid Ser Mandon to ensure he never returned from battle.
19. **Tyrion learns his location** — Ballabar reveals they are in Maegor's Holdfast, in a chamber over the Queen's Ballroom. Cersei wanted him kept close.
20. **Tyrion learns Tywin is Hand** — Ballabar reveals Lord Tywin has taken residence in the Tower of the Hand (Tyrion's former chambers) and assumed the duties of Hand of the King.
21. **Battle outcome explained** — Ballabar recounts that Lord Tywin, Lord Tyrell, the Knight of Flowers, and Lord Littlefinger rode through the ashes and took Stannis in the rear. Smallfolk credit King Renly's ghost.
22. **Tyrion sends for Pod** — Unable to send for Shae, and uncertain who to trust, Tyrion asks for his squire Podrick Payne.
23. **Pod reports Bronn's knighting** — Pod tells Tyrion that Bronn has been made a knight.
24. **Tyrion orders dreamwine from Frenken** — Tyrion tells Pod to get dreamwine from Maester Frenken (not Ballabar) and to watch him make it — showing distrust of Ballabar.
25. **Pod confirms Ser Mandon is dead** — Pod says Ser Mandon drowned, confirming the kill. Pod begins to stammer about not meaning to kill him.
26. **Tyrion orders silence** — Tyrion commands Pod to say nothing about Ser Mandon, about Tyrion, about any of it.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pain, fear, helplessness, anger, bitterness, resentment, brief flashes of dark humor. Deep longing for comfort — wants Shae, dreams of Tysha, calls out for Jaime, for his mother. Terror at the memory of Ser Mandon's attack. Growing fury at discovering Cersei has had him moved and Tywin has taken his position.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and understanding what happened — who attacked him, why, what the current political situation is. Obsessive focus on Ser Mandon Moore's motives and Cersei's involvement. Secondary concern with regaining control of his situation (refusing poppy, demanding bandage removal, sending for his own people).
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses further milk of the poppy; forces bandage removal despite maester's protests; chooses to send for Pod rather than risk exposing Shae; orders dreamwine from Frenken (a trusted maester) rather than Ballabar; orders Pod to say nothing about Ser Mandon's attempt on his life; requests Bronn as personal guard.
- **Self-deception flags:** The Tysha memory is exceptionally tender and detailed — Tyrion clearly cherishes it — yet he bitterly dismisses it all as performed lies ("all feigned, all for gold, she was a whore, Jaime's whore, Jaime's gift, my lady of the lie"). The emotional intensity of the dream and the harshness of the dismissal sit in tension within the text. Also: "He must not let his father see" — even in delirium, Tyrion performs for Tywin's approval. The victory feast dream (Tywin smiling, Jaime knighting him, everyone hailing him as hero) reveals desires Tyrion would never admit while awake.
