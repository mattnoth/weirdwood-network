- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys enters Qarth with her khalasar, escorted by camelry and greeted by gongs and horns. She is courted by Pyat Pree the warlock, Xaro Xhoan Daxos the merchant prince, and Quaithe the shadowbinder, each offering gifts or warnings. Xaro hosts Dany in a wing of his vast palace. She sends Rakharo to scout the city and Jorah to the docks, where he finds Quhuru Mo, a Summer Islander captain who brings news that Robert Baratheon is dead, Joffrey sits the Iron Throne, the Lannisters rule, Robert's brothers have fled to claim the crown, and Ned Stark has been seized for treason. Dany sees Robert's death as an opportunity; Jorah counsels caution.

## Events & Actions
1. **Daenerys approaches Qarth** — Men beat gongs and blow horns on the walls; a column of camelry in scaled copper armor emerges as her honor guard.
2. **Dany rides through Qarth's three walls** — She observes the carved outer (red sandstone, animals), middle (grey granite, war), and inner (black marble, erotic) walls; gates of copper, iron, and gold open at her approach.
3. **Children scatter flowers** — Small children in golden sandals and bright paint rush out to scatter flowers in Dany's path.
4. **Procession through the city** — Dany passes under a bronze arch of mating snakes, through an arcade of heroes on marble columns, and through a cavernous bazaar with a bird-filled ceiling.
5. **Pyat Pree invites Dany to the House of the Undying** — He promises she will "drink of truth and wisdom" there.
6. **Xaro offers gifts and a crown** — He says the Thirteen will crown her with black jade and fire opals; he and Pyat Pree compete for her favor.
7. **Dany declares her goal** — She says the only palace she desires is the Red Keep; asks for ships and swords rather than baubles.
8. **Xaro warns against warlocks** — He tells Dany that warlocks are hollow husks, their power diminished, and that Pyat Pree's gifts will turn to dust.
9. **Jorah warns against both Pyat Pree and Xaro** — He advises Dany to avoid both men and expresses discomfort with Qarth.
10. **Dany reflects on Jorah** — She thinks of him as her "great bear," feels safe but sad, wishes she could love him better.
11. **Dany settles in Xaro's palace** — An entire wing is given to her with gardens, marble pool, scrying tower, and warlock's maze.
12. **Pyat Pree departs with a gift** — He kisses Dany's bare feet and gives her a jar of ointment said to reveal spirits of the air; he vows to petition the Undying Ones for an audience.
13. **Quaithe gives a warning** — She tells Dany to beware "of all," warning that people will lust for the dragons because "dragons are fire made flesh, and fire is power."
14. **Dany orders guard on the dragons** — She tells her bloodriders to let no one enter the palace wing without her leave and to keep the dragons well guarded.
15. **Dany sends Rakharo to scout Qarth** — She tells him to take men and women to explore the parts of the city Pyat Pree did not show them.
16. **Dany sends Jorah to the docks** — She orders him to find what ships are at anchor and gather news from Westeros.
17. **Jorah protests but agrees** — He says the Usurper will kill her and that his place is at her side, but accepts her reasoning.
18. **Dany bathes in the marble pool** — Her handmaids strip her; she floats in the cool water with golden fish nibbling at her skin.
19. **Dany reflects on conquering Westeros** — She thinks of Drogo's strength, her meager khalasar, the challenge of Robert and his allies; her doubts make the water feel cold.
20. **Jorah returns with Quhuru Mo** — Near evenfall, while Dany feeds her dragons, Irri announces Jorah has returned with a stranger.
21. **Quhuru Mo reports Robert Baratheon is dead** — Torn by a boar while hunting in his kingswood; news heard at Oldtown, Dorne, Lys, and other ports. Some say his queen, his brother, or Lord Stark betrayed him.
22. **Quhuru Mo reports political upheaval** — Joffrey reigns but Lannisters rule; Robert's brothers have fled and mean to claim the crown; Ned Stark seized for treason.
23. **Jorah defends Stark's honor** — He declares Ned Stark unlikely to be a traitor; Dany disagrees, calling Stark a traitor to his true king.
24. **Dany tells Quhuru Mo of Viserys's death** — Killed by Khal Drogo with a crown of molten gold.
25. **Quhuru Mo says his ship sails east** — The Cinnamon Wind will make the trader's circle round the Jade Sea; won't return to Westeros for a year or more.
26. **Quhuru Mo departs** — He says seeing the dragons was reward enough; Dany invites him to King's Landing when she sits the throne.
27. **Jorah cautions against speaking freely** — He warns that Quhuru Mo will spread her plans; Dany says let the world know.
28. **Dany and Jorah debate** — Jorah says Robert's death changes nothing; Dany insists it changes everything, comparing the Seven Kingdoms to Drogo's splintering khalasar.
29. **Jorah counsels realism** — He says she will need a fleet, gold, armies, alliances; warns that even dragons can die, comparing her to Rhaegar.
30. **Dany asserts her identity** — She says she is not the frightened girl from Pentos, not Viserys; declares her blood of the dragon. She kisses Jorah on the cheek and says "dragons die, but so do dragonslayers."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Shifts from guarded wariness (entering Qarth, dealing with Pyat Pree and Xaro) to contentment (bathing in the pool) to self-doubt and disquiet (reflecting on how to conquer) to elation (learning Robert is dead) to fierce determination (final exchange with Jorah). Her mood literally changes the sensation of the water from pleasant to cold.
- **Primary preoccupation:** How to win the Iron Throne — she needs ships, armies, and allies. Robert's death transforms her sense of possibility.
- **Key decisions made:** Dany orders guard on the dragons and her wing of the palace; sends Rakharo to scout Qarth and Jorah to the docks; declares she will let the whole world know her purpose now that Robert is dead; asserts Robert's death "changes everything."
- **Self-deception flags:** Dany tells herself that if the gods mean for her to conquer "they will provide, they will send me a sign" — treating the Bleeding Star and Robert's death as divine validation rather than coincidence. She dismisses Jorah's caution about needing a fleet, gold, armies, and alliances with "All this I know" but has none of those things. She wishes she could love Jorah better but does not examine why she cannot.
