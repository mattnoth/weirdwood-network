# ADWD — The Lost Lord I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 25
- **pov_character:** Jon Connington (as "Griff")
- **pov_chapter_number:** The Lost Lord I
- **first_available:** ADWD The Lost Lord I
- **location_primary:** Volon Therys (the Shy Maid, then Golden Company camp three miles south)
- **location_secondary:** Stoney Sept (dream/memory), the Trident (memory), Selhorys (referenced), Griffin's Roost (longed for), Meereen (discussed), Pentos (referenced), Volantis (referenced), Lys (referenced), the Sorrows (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** After the company's departure from the Disputed Lands and arrival near Volon Therys; after Tyrion's escape at Selhorys; during Triarch Malaquo's deployment to contain the company
- **time_markers:** "The day was hot and sticky, as all the days had been since they left the Sorrows"; "the sun was lowering in the west" when they reach the camp; "The sun was reddening the western sky and painting scarlet shadows" when they leave the war council; "the gold and scarlet rays of the setting sun shone through the open flap" at chapter's end; "Seventeen years had come and gone since the Battle of the Bells"; "a dozen years" since Connington was with the Golden Company; Myles Toyne "four years dead"; "a fortnight past" is not used but "twelve years" of exile referenced
- **chapter_summary:** Jon Connington, posing as "Griff," waits aboard the Shy Maid at Volon Therys while Haldon buys horses. After debating with Lemore about the wisdom of revealing Prince Aegon, he rides with Aegon and Haldon to the Golden Company camp. In a war council, Connington reveals Aegon's identity to the officers and the company debates whether to sail east to join Daenerys or invade Westeros directly. Aegon asserts himself, proposing the western invasion, and the officers rally to him, pledging their swords. Alone afterward, Connington examines his right hand, revealing that greyscale is spreading on his fingers.

## Events & Actions
1. **Griff paces the Shy Maid** — He waits anxiously for Haldon's return, worrying about Tyrion's escape and the current situation with the Golden Company and Triarch Malaquo.
2. **Griff argues with Lemore** — She urges caution about revealing Aegon to sellswords; he insists the time for hiding is past and that the boy must be shown to his men.
3. **Connington recalls the Battle of the Bells** — He dreamt of Stoney Sept again, running from house to house searching for Robert, haunted by the sound of bells.
4. **Haldon returns with three horses** — Yandry spots him on the waterfront; horses were expensive due to Dothraki across the river causing an exodus.
5. **Aegon is prepared and presented** — He emerges in black and red with blue-dyed hair and Illyrio's rubies; Connington says his father would be proud.
6. **Aegon expresses doubt** — He quotes "Yollo" (Tyrion) about trusting no one; Connington advises a middle course, citing King Aerys as a cautionary example.
7. **The party rides south to the Golden Company camp** — Three miles south of Volon Therys along the Rhoyne, through willow groves and poppy fields.
8. **They arrive at camp and are met by Franklyn Flowers** — Flowers is now a captain and knight; warm reunion with Griff; escorts them to the war council.
9. **Connington observes the camp** — Compact, orderly, defensible; wonders how many men recognize him after twelve years; reflects on his false death story crafted by Varys.
10. **Connington surveys the gilded skulls** — Contemplates Myles Toyne's skull, remembers his friend, reflects on Bittersteel's legacy and his own alternate path.
11. **War council begins** — Officers introduced; sellsword captains bear bastard names and extinct Westerosi house names; display rude splendor in jeweled weapons and golden arm rings.
12. **Connington reveals Aegon's identity** — Announces him as Aegon Targaryen, firstborn son of Rhaegar and Elia; the officers already knew — Strickland had told them at the river.
13. **Strickland reveals the Yunkish offer** — Twice what Myr paid, plus slaves; he told the envoy he would think on it rather than refuse outright.
14. **Debate over east vs. west** — Lysono Maar reports no ships available for Slaver's Bay; Meereen blockaded; Rivers argues for speed; Gorys Edoryen suggests feigning acceptance of Yunkish offer; Strickland resists, citing need for Daenerys and her dragons.
15. **Aegon asserts himself** — "Then put your hopes on me. I am the only dragon that you need." Proposes claiming the Iron Throne without Daenerys, letting her come to him in Westeros.
16. **Officers rally to Aegon's plan** — Flowers, Peake, Rivers, Mandrake all support the western invasion; strategic arguments made about Tywin's death, Cersei's weakness, potential Dornish support.
17. **The Golden Company pledges to Aegon** — One by one the officers kneel and lay their swords at Aegon's feet; Strickland is the last to kneel.
18. **Post-council dispersal** — Flowers takes Aegon to tour the camp; Connington orders Haldon back to the Shy Maid to fetch Lemore, Rolly, and Illyrio's chests; thanks for Yandry and Ysilla.
19. **Connington alone in his tent** — Removes wolfskin cloak, mail shirt, and right glove; examines his hand — greyscale has blackened the middle fingernail to the first knuckle and begun on the ring finger. Feels nothing when he pricks the darkened skin. Estimates he has a year, two, five — perhaps ten.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Impatient, restless, sick of hiding and waiting and caution. Haunted by the Battle of the Bells — dreams of it, bells ring in his memory. Grief for Myles Toyne and devotion to Rhaegar. Contempt for Strickland's cowardice. Deep resentment of Varys for the shame of his false death story. Fond of Lemore but dismissive of her caution. Growing awareness of his own mortality from greyscale. Determined and purposeful — "I do not have time enough for caution."
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting Aegon to the Iron Throne before the greyscale kills him. Time is running out. He needs to see Griffin's Roost again, reclaim his name and honor, and silence the bells in his dreams.
- **Key decisions made:** Overrules Lemore and reveals Aegon to the Golden Company. Supports Aegon's pivot to westward invasion. Orders the party to consolidate at the Golden Company camp, dismissing Yandry and Ysilla.
- **Self-deception flags:** Connington blames the Battle of the Bells (and by extension, the fall of the Targaryen dynasty) entirely on his own failure to find and kill Robert at Stoney Sept — "the Battle of the Trident would never have been fought if the griffin had only slain the stag." He may be overestimating the ease of invasion and Dornish support out of desperation driven by his limited time.
