A dying rider on a pale horse arrives bearing news that Astapor is burning; three Astapori refugees confirm the city has fallen to Yunkai and New Ghis with terrible slaughter. Dany recalls her forces, debates strategy with her council, and reluctantly concludes she needs Hizdahr zo Loraq to secure peace within Meereen before she can face the enemy without.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dany counts enemy sails** — From her western ramparts each morning, she counts twenty-five ships blockading Slaver's Bay, noting all trade has stopped and fisherfolk cannot put out to sea.
2. **Argument with Groleo** — Her admiral advocates using dragons against the fleet; Dany demands he build ships instead. Groleo complains about the Saduleon's destruction and Dany's refusal of Daxos's ships. Dany dismisses him.
3. **Conversation with Ser Barristan about supplies** — Barristan reports stores are ample, crops planted, Dothraki have cleared slavers from the hills, and Lhazar's friendship secured. He mentions his boy-knight orphans progressing.
4. **Skahaz reports on Hizdahr** — The Shavepate reports Hizdahr visited the pyramid of Zhak (his eleventh pyramid visit) and that murders have stopped for twenty-six days. He accuses Hizdahr of being the Harpy and requests permission to torture him. Dany refuses.
5. **Skahaz presents ship list** — He gives Dany a scroll listing all Meereenese ships in the blockade with their Great Master captains, proposing to seize their kin as hostages. Dany refuses and burns the scroll.
6. **Barristan compares Dany to Rhaegar** — He tells her Rhaegar would have been proud. Dany recalls Jorah's words about Rhaegar dying despite fighting valiantly.
7. **Empty petitioner's hall** — Dany descends to the purple marble hall and finds no petitioners; Reznak says the city is afraid.
8. **Dying rider arrives** — A rider on a pale horse emerges from morning mists crying "She is burning." He is a skeleton under his tokar with a mortified arrow wound. He dies within the hour at the temple of the Blue Graces.
9. **Blue Graces diagnose bloody flux** — Ezzara reports the rider had bloody stool, not just a wound infection. Galazza Galare calls him a harbinger of "wroth and ruin."
10. **Dany orders forces recalled** — She sends riders to find her bloodriders, Brown Ben and the Second Sons, and the Stormcrows (Daario).
11. **Second Sons return (eight days later)** — Brown Ben Plumm arrives with three Astapori refugees who had horses.
12. **Astapori refugees tell of Astapor's fall** — They describe starvation, cannibalism, plague, King Cutthroat and Queen Whore, Cleon's corpse-sortie, the Green Grace's impalement, mass suicide in pyramid of Ullhor, the bloody flux killing three in four, gates breached, city burned by Yunkai and New Ghis.
13. **Dany promises refugees safety** — She tells them they will be safe in Meereen, though she doubts her own words.
14. **Council debate on strategy** — Brown Ben warns of more refugees coming (hundreds, thousands), some sick. Reznak urges refusing them entry and marrying Hizdahr. Brown Ben proposes using dragons or selling Meereen and fleeing west. Dany refuses both. Grey Worm vouches for freedmen. Skahaz advocates defense behind walls. Barristan advocates meeting the enemy in the field.
15. **Dany orders camp for Astapori refugees** — Overruled by Barristan on admitting them, she orders a camp west of the city by the river, with food provided and healthy separated from sick.
16. **Dany orders Second Sons to scout** — Brown Ben is tasked with scouting enemy positions; also authorized to try buying enemy sellsword captains. Gates to be closed and watch doubled.
17. **Final terrace conversation with Barristan** — Barristan gives blunt assessment of her forces' weaknesses. He counsels offensive battle rather than siege, citing Meereen's overcrowding, internal enemies, and inability to endure a long siege.
18. **Dany resolves to accept Hizdahr** — After praying and receiving no answer, she concludes she cannot fight enemies within and without simultaneously, and states she needs Hizdahr zo Loraq.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Besieged, overwhelmed, guilty about Astapor, lonely (misses Daario, Jorah), conflicted about her duty vs. her desires, prayerful/desperate by the end
- **Primary preoccupation:** How to survive the converging threats — the naval blockade, the fall of Astapor, the approaching Yunkish army, internal enemies, and the bloody flux
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to use dragons; refuses to torture Hizdahr; burns the hostage list; orders forces recalled; refuses to admit sick refugees into the city (camps them outside); orders Second Sons to scout and attempt to buy sellsword captains; resolves to marry Hizdahr zo Loraq
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells refugees "You will be safe in Meereen" while knowing she cannot guarantee it ("She knows I lie"); frames the Hizdahr marriage as necessity while avoiding saying it aloud; tries to rationalize not marching to Astapor while feeling guilty
