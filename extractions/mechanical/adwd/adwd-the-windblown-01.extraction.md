# ADWD — The Windblown I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 26
- **pov_character:** Quentyn Martell
- **pov_chapter_number:** The Windblown I
- **first_available:** ADWD The Windblown I
- **location_primary:** Camp near Astapor / coast road between Astapor and Yunkai
- **location_secondary:** Astapor (recalled), Slaver's Bay shore camp, Tattered Prince's command tent
- **approximate_timeline:** During the march north from Astapor toward Yunkai; two days after initial departure the Dornishmen are summoned to the command tent
- **time_markers:** "strike the tents, load the mules, saddle the horses, we march for Yunkai at the break of day"; "less than an hour to strike their camp"; first night camp by the shore with "a half-moon shone upon the waters"; "first watch" drawn by Frog; "just after sundown" when Gerris meets him; "two days later" when Hugh Hungerford summons them; flashback to Astapor battle begins "kicked awake at dawn"
- **chapter_summary:** Quentyn Martell, disguised as "Frog" in the Windblown sellsword company, marches north from Astapor along the coast road toward Yunkai. He recalls the horrors of Astapor and a recent battle against the Astapori Unsullied led by the corpse of Cleon the Great. As Quentyn discusses desertion plans with Gerris Drinkwater, the Tattered Prince unexpectedly summons the Westerosi members of the company and orders them to defect to Daenerys Targaryen as a ruse, under Pretty Meris's command — inadvertently solving the Dornishmen's problem of how to reach the dragon queen.

## Events & Actions
1. **News spreads through camp** — Word passes that Daenerys is marching south; the Windblown are ordered to march north to meet her.
2. **Camp struck** — The Windblown break camp in less than an hour and ride north along the coast road toward Yunkai.
3. **March begins** — The Tattered Prince addresses the company from horseback in High Valyrian: "Astapor was but a taste, Meereen will be the feast." Sellswords cheer.
4. **Quentyn and Gerris discuss desertion** — Riding together, Frog tells Gerris they need to desert soon; Gerris warns to wait and talk that night.
5. **Description of Yunkai'i commanders** — The chapter describes the Yellow Whale, the Girl General, the Little Pigeon and his Herons, and the Clanker Lords among the Yunkish leadership.
6. **First night's camp by Slaver's Bay** — Frog draws first watch at the horse lines; Gerris meets him after sundown under a half-moon.
7. **Desertion planning** — Quentyn urges immediate action; Gerris counsels patience until north of Yunkai, warning of the Tattered Prince's punishment for deserters and Pretty Meris.
8. **Flashback: Battle of Astapor** — Quentyn recalls being kicked awake at dawn; Astapor's new Unsullied poured from the gates; the Windblown and Company of the Cat charged the flanks while a New Ghis legion engaged frontally; Caggo cut down Dead Cleon (whose corpse had been armored and mounted); the Unsullied threw down their weapons and were slaughtered.
9. **Summons to command tent** — Two days later, Hugh Hungerford orders all three Dornishmen to the Tattered Prince's tent.
10. **The Tattered Prince's mission** — He reveals orders to hunt Astapori refugees, but the real mission for the Westerosi contingent: ride east, swing around Yunkai, and defect to Daenerys's forces as a ruse. Pretty Meris will command.
11. **The Tattered Prince provides cover stories** — He explains each Westerosi member has plausible grievances against him (whippings, maiming, rape, theft, killed companions) to justify their "defection."
12. **Mission accepted** — Lewis Lanster asks when to leave; told "at once." The Dornishmen realize this solves their problem — they can reach Daenerys legitimately within the company's plan.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fearful about meeting Daenerys (the more he hears, the more he fears it); haunted by the horrors of Astapor and the battle; conflicted about the betrayal of abandoning companions; relieved when the Tattered Prince's mission inadvertently solves their problem
- **Primary preoccupation:** How to reach Daenerys Targaryen to fulfill his father's marriage pact; the moral cost of betrayal; whether Daenerys is as mad/murderous as her father
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees to wait on Gerris's counsel rather than deserting immediately; recognizes that the Tattered Prince's mission gives them legitimate cover to reach Daenerys
- **Self-deception flags:** Questions whether he must still marry Daenerys if she's murderous like her father, but does not seriously consider abandoning the mission; tells himself the shared meals and comradeship with the Windblown are "the cost of passage to Meereen" rather than genuine bonds being betrayed
