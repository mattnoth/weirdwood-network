# ADWD — Daenerys VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 44
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys VII
- **first_available:** ADWD Daenerys VII
- **location_primary:** Meereen (Great Pyramid, Temple of the Graces)
- **location_secondary:** Dorne/Sunspear (referenced), Braavos (referenced), Dragonstone (referenced), Volantis (referenced), Casterly Rock (referenced), Yellow City/Yunkai (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** The night before and the day of Daenerys's wedding to Hizdahr zo Loraq; the Yunkai'i siege lines are established outside Meereen
- **time_markers:** Night → dawn → full day of court → sunset; next dawn (wedding day); four hours inside the Temple of the Graces; "two nights remain" before the wedding at chapter's opening; Daario returned "from his latest sortie" recently; the pact was made "when I was just a little girl" in Braavos
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys spends her penultimate night with Daario before her wedding to Hizdahr zo Loraq. The next day she holds court, where Galazza Galare tries to raise the topic of Daario and the queen deflects. Former Windblown sellswords present themselves, including three Dornishmen who reveal themselves as Prince Quentyn Martell, Ser Gerris Drinkwater, and Ser Archibald Yronwood, bearing a secret marriage pact signed by Ser Willem Darry and Prince Oberyn Martell. On her wedding day, Barristan Selmy shares details about her parents' romantic histories, and Daenerys marries Hizdahr at the Temple of the Graces.

## Events & Actions
1. **Daenerys lies awake savoring her last night with Daario** — She stays awake through the night watching the candle gutter, touching him, dreading dawn
2. **Daario wakes and they discuss the wedding** — He proposes marriage; she refuses; he dresses and suggests killing Hizdahr
3. **Daario mentions the Westerosi sellswords and "the Frog"** — He tells her about Dornish newcomers from the Windblown who have a gift for her
4. **Dany agrees to hold court** — Daario convinces her to see the new Westerosi, particularly "Frog" with his gift
5. **Missandei brings breakfast and reveals she heard Dany scream in the night** — Dany deflects, saying it was the wind
6. **Dany observes the Yunkai'i siege preparations from her terrace** — Ships, catapults, scorpions, trebuchets being built behind ditches
7. **Dany has a nightmare about Hizdahr** — His lips are blue and bruised, his manhood cold as ice; she wakes disturbed
8. **Dany sleeps on her terrace and wakes at dawn** — Found beneath a blanket of dew
9. **Dany holds court; Galazza Galare raises the issue of "a certain sellsword captain"** — Dany deflects by referencing Brown Ben Plumm's treachery
10. **Daario presents former Windblown sellswords at court** — Pretty Meris, Hugh Hungerford, Webber, Orson Stone, Lucifer Long, Will of the Woods, Dick Straw, Ginger Jack, and three Dornishmen
11. **The three Dornishmen reveal their true identities** — "Gerrold" is Ser Gerris Drinkwater; "Greenguts" is Ser Archibald Yronwood; "Frog" is Prince Quentyn Martell
12. **Quentyn presents the secret marriage pact** — Signed by Willem Darry and Oberyn Martell, witnessed by the Sealord; Viserys to marry Arianne for Dorne's support
13. **Dany tells Quentyn she is pledged to Hizdahr** — She offers him quarters but no marriage
14. **Dany, Daario, and Barristan discuss the pact privately** — Barristan says it changes everything; Dany says it changes nothing; Daario calls the Dornishmen liars
15. **Dany recalls Quaithe's prophecy** — "The sun's son," "the pale mare," "beware the perfumed seneschal," shadows and whispers
16. **Daario and Dany spend their last night together** — She uses skills Doreah taught her; his wound bleeds
17. **Wedding morning: Daario leaves angrily** — He mocks Hizdahr and Quentyn; suggests making a sortie; storms out when Dany commands him to stay
18. **Dany dresses in her tokar with baby pearls for the wedding** — Cannot ride her silver in a tokar; chooses sedan chair
19. **Quentyn pleads with Dany one last time at the pyramid's base** — She kisses his cheek and tells him to be happy for her
20. **Barristan tells Dany about her mother's youthful infatuation** — A knight from the stormlands who wore her favor and became pious after the wedding
21. **Barristan tells Dany about Aerys's desire for Tywin's wife** — Aerys was taken with a lady of Casterly Rock, Tywin's cousin; made a drunken jape about first night; took liberties at the bedding
22. **Hizdahr's procession meets Dany's** — They proceed together to the Temple of the Graces
23. **Dany notes Ezzara missing from the Graces** — Fears the bloody flux has taken her
24. **Wedding ceremony at the Temple of the Graces** — Hizdahr washes Dany's feet; fifty eunuchs sing; they enter the temple; four hours later emerge as man and wife, bound with gold chains

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Torn between desire (Daario) and duty (Hizdahr/peace); melancholy; resignation; longing; occasional anger (at Galazza); forlorn after Daario leaves; disturbed by nightmare; wistful curiosity about her parents; ultimately resolute about the wedding
- **Primary preoccupation:** The impending wedding and loss of Daario; whether peace is worth the personal sacrifice; the siege and her city's survival
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees to hold court (at Daario's urging); deflects Galazza publicly; refuses Quentyn but offers him quarters and future Dornish alliance; proceeds with wedding despite misgivings; chooses sedan chair over palanquin
- **Self-deception flags:** "Hizdahr will bring me peace. He must." — hopeful assertion with no evidence; tells herself she'd give up her crown for Daario but admits he wouldn't want her without it; "It will never be my city. It will never be my home." — stated but then she weds to save it anyway; wonders if her nightmare was prophetic but dismisses the question
