# ADWD — Tyrion V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 19
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister

- **location_primary:** The Sorrows (ruins of Chroyane, the Rhoyne)
- **location_secondary:** Bridge of Dream, Palace of Love/Palace of Sorrow, Dagger Lake (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Mid-journey down the Rhoyne; after departing Illyrio's manse and sailing through Dagger Lake, now passing through the Sorrows
- **time_markers:** Fog obscures time of day throughout; lanterns lit fore and aft suggesting low visibility conditions; "another hour should see us clear of the Sorrows" (Haldon); no sunrise/sunset visible; chapter ends with drowning — timing indeterminate
- **chapter_summary:** The Shy Maid navigates through the fog-shrouded ruins of Chroyane in the Sorrows on the river Rhoyne. The crew discusses greyscale, the Shrouded Lord, and Garin's Curse while passing submerged ruins. They encounter another poleboat (the Kingfisher) whose crew reports that war is coming from Volantis. After passing the Bridge of Dream, Tyrion reveals to Young Griff that he has deduced Young Griff's true identity as Prince Rhaegar's son, and that Griff is Jon Connington. The Shy Maid then loops back to the Bridge of Dream inexplicably, where stone men attack the boat. Tyrion saves Young Griff by tackling a stone man overboard, and both Tyrion and the stone man plunge into the Rhoyne.

## Events & Actions
1. **The Shy Maid navigates through the fog of the Sorrows** — The crew poles the boat through dense, unnatural fog on the Rhoyne. Tyrion tends the brazier while others work poles and tiller. Lanterns are lit fore and aft.
2. **The crew discusses the dangers of the Sorrows** — Ysilla warns about sorcery, restless spirits, and the whispering dead. Haldon warns about Garin's Curse (greyscale). Tyrion explains greyscale's medical progression and treatments.
3. **Tyrion observes a massive stone hand underwater** — A hand large enough to crush the boat reaches up from the murky depths; only two fingertips break the surface, with a pale face visible below.
4. **The crew debates the Shrouded Lord** — Yandry says some believe the Shrouded Lord is Garin risen; Haldon says there have been a score of Shrouded Lords, the current one a corsair from the Basilisk Islands; Duck shares an alternate legend about a statue kissed alive by a grey woman.
5. **The Shy Maid passes through the ruins of Chroyane** — They navigate past a sunken temple, marble stairs, shattered spires, headless statues, and uprooted trees.
6. **A pale shape flies overhead** — Something with pale leathery wings flaps by overhead through the fog and disappears before Tyrion can identify it.
7. **The Shy Maid encounters the Kingfisher** — Another poleboat hails them through the fog. They exchange cargo manifests and the Kingfisher crew reports "war" from Volantis: "Nyessos and Malaquo go hand in hand, and the elephants show stripes."
8. **Griff reacts to the Volantis news** — He is disturbed that Triarch Nyessos, whom Illyrio has paid extensively, appears to be allying with Malaquo and the tiger faction. Tyrion privately thinks elephants and tigers might unite against dragons.
9. **The Shy Maid passes the Palace of Love/Sorrow** — They observe the massive ruined palace, ten times the size of the Red Keep, covered in grey moss and black vines. Tyrion identifies it.
10. **Tyrion has an extended reverie about Tysha and his family** — He thinks of Jaime's childhood gifts, Tysha's smile, Jaime's lie about buying her, and recalls killing Tywin on the privy. He quotes Tywin's last words: "Wherever whores go."
11. **The Shy Maid approaches the Bridge of Dream** — They see lights ahead; Griff identifies the Bridge of Dream and warns about stone men. He orders Young Griff below with Lemore.
12. **Young Griff refuses to go below** — He argues he can fight as well as Duck and is "half a knight." Griff overrules him. Tyrion defuses the tension by joking about his own size and then tells Young Griff "you are everything."
13. **The Shy Maid passes under the Bridge of Dream the first time** — Stone men moan and mutter above under a flickering lamp but take no notice. Duck pushes off a pier with his pole and they pass through safely.
14. **Tyrion reveals Young Griff's identity** — He tells Young Griff that the blue-dyed hair, the septa, the chainless maester, and the hedge knight training all point to someone being hidden and prepared. He notes Young Griff has "noble features for a dead boy."
15. **Tyrion reveals his own identity** — He tells Young Griff he was born Tyrion of House Lannister, son of Tywin and Joanna, and that his father wrapped Young Griff's "corpse" in a crimson cloak and laid it beside his sister at the foot of the Iron Throne.
16. **Tyrion identifies Griff as Jon Connington** — He names him as once Lord of Griffin's Roost and Hand of the King, and as Prince Rhaegar's dear friend, the ideal person to raise Rhaegar's infant son.
17. **The Shy Maid inexplicably returns to the Bridge of Dream** — Despite traveling downstream, they encounter the same bridge again. The marble stairway and surroundings are identical. Haldon says it is "inconceivable" as rivers only run one way. Yandry says "Mother Rhoyne runs how she will."
18. **Stone men attack the Shy Maid** — Stone men on the bridge begin wailing and pointing. One crashes onto the cabin roof, a second lands beside the tiller shattering planks. Ysilla shrieks.
19. **Duck knocks the first stone man overboard** — He swings his pole into the stone man's chest, knocking him into the river where he sinks without a sound.
20. **Griff fights the second stone man** — With sword and torch, Griff drives the creature backward. His blade sparks on calcified flesh and severs the stone man's arm. Yandry and Duck force the creature overboard with poles.
21. **A third stone man lunges for Young Griff** — Despite a shattered leg with bone protruding, the stone man reaches for the prince. Young Griff freezes.
22. **Tyrion saves Young Griff** — He kicks the boy's legs out from under him, leaps over him, and thrusts a torch into the stone man's face, driving him back toward the deck's edge.
23. **The stone man seizes Tyrion's torch and throws it away** — The torch is quenched in the river. The stone man is revealed as a former Summer Islander, black-skinned where not grey, with jaw and half his cheek turned to stone.
24. **Tyrion tackles the stone man overboard** — He drives his shoulder into the stone man, sending them both into the Rhoyne. The stone man grabs Tyrion and drags him underwater.
25. **Tyrion drowns (or nearly drowns)** — Underwater, stone hands grip his face and arm. He cannot breathe. He thinks he perished long ago in King's Landing. Black water fills his lungs and darkness closes in.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Uneasy and deeply melancholic. Tyrion is unsettled by the Sorrows and the fog, comparing the feeling to "being back in the privy again, watching my father die." He is haunted by Tysha, Jaime's betrayal, and his own acts of killing. He considers himself already dead — "he had perished long ago, back in King's Landing." Displays dark humor and self-deprecation throughout. Contemptuous of Griff ("I hate this fog, I hate this place, and I am less than fond of Griff"). At the chapter's end, he is fatalistic: "No man would mourn the thing that he'd become."
- **Primary preoccupation:** His past — Tysha, Jaime's betrayal, killing Tywin and Shae. "Wherever whores go" echoes in his mind. Secondarily: deducing and confirming Young Griff's identity, and the political implications of the Volantis news.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to reveal Young Griff's identity and his own identity openly. Decides to save Young Griff from the stone man, tackling the attacker overboard at risk to his own life despite his stated indifference to living.
- **Self-deception flags:** Claims indifference to his own survival ("No man would mourn the thing that he'd become," "Least of all me") but actively fights to save Young Griff and engages with the political situation. The act of self-sacrifice contradicts his stated death wish. Tells himself "I was born Tyrion of House Lannister, trueborn son of Tywin and Joanna, both of whom I slew" — framing Joanna's death in childbirth as his doing.
