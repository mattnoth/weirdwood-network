# ADWD — The Turncloak I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 42
- **pov_character:** Theon Greyjoy (as "The Turncloak" / "Reek")
- **pov_chapter_number:** The Turncloak I
- **first_available:** ADWD The Turncloak I
- **location_primary:** Winterfell (ruined, occupied by Bolton forces)
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell Great Hall, Winterfell godswood, Winterfell crypts, battlements of the inner wall, the First Keep, Maester Luwin's turret, Mikken's forge, Lady Catelyn's sept, the Burned Tower
- **approximate_timeline:** During a major snowstorm; Stannis Baratheon's host is approaching Winterfell but slowed by snow; shortly after the Ramsay Bolton / "Arya Stark" wedding
- **time_markers:** Snow begins at sunset, continues through the night; moon rises behind snow curtain; "come morning" for the Great Hall scene; Theon unable to sleep "last night"; a later scene (stew) occurs after his wanderings; the crypt visit occurs after the stew scene; snow still raging when they emerge from the crypts
- **chapter_summary:** A heavy snowstorm blankets Winterfell. At breakfast, Roose Bolton declares the northern gods have turned against Stannis. Theon observes the uneasy political dynamics among the Bolton coalition—Freys uncomfortable in the snow, Manderly eating silently, the northmen restive. One of Abel's washerwomen, Rowan, approaches Theon to ask how he captured Winterfell, but he flees, suspecting a trap. Theon wanders the ruins of the castle, climbs the battlements to survey the snow-covered landscape, and prays at the heart tree in the godswood, hearing mysterious sobbing. Lady Barbrey Dustin summons Theon to guide her to the Stark crypts, where she reveals her history as Brandon Stark's lover, her resentment of Rickard Stark's "southron ambitions," her bitterness toward Eddard Stark for not returning her husband's bones from Dorne, and her intention to prevent Ned's bones from ever reaching Winterfell.

## Events & Actions
1. **Snowstorm begins** — First flakes at sunset; by nightfall heavy snow obscures the moon
2. **Roose Bolton's announcement** — At morning breakfast in the Great Hall, Roose declares the northern gods have unleashed their wrath on Stannis, calling him a stranger; his men roar approval
3. **Theon observes political tensions** — Theon notes the Freys do not join the cheering; Manderly eats silently; Harwood Stout and Whoresbane Umber talk quietly together
4. **Theon queues for porridge** — Overhears a Hornwood man and a Rills rider trading quips about winter snows
5. **Theon reflects on impossibility of escape** — Every gate guarded, Bolton controls access; remembers Kyra and the keys; father dead, uncles have no use for him; nowhere to go
6. **Ramsay enters with Bastard's Boys** — Demands music; Abel plays an altered "Dornishman's Wife" about tasting "a northman's daughter"; Bolton smiles, Ramsay laughs, others join; Yellow Dick snorts wine
7. **Theon reflects on Jeyne Poole's captivity** — "Arya" not seen since wedding night; guards outside her chamber; Theon serves as her bath maid on Ramsay's orders; sees her bruises; tells her to "just be Arya"; she calls him "Theon" and he corrects to "Reek"
8. **Theon dreads Ramsay's future cruelty** — Anticipates Ramsay will tire of Jeyne and return to torturing him
9. **Rowan approaches Theon** — One of Abel's washerwomen sits beside him; asks why he eats alone; invites him to dance; offers other washerwomen (Myrtle, Holly); asks how he captured Winterfell for Abel to make into a song
10. **Theon suspects a trap and flees** — Believes Ramsay sent Rowan; wants to hit her, kiss her, but dares not touch her; leaves the hall limping
11. **Theon observes the snowstorm outside** — Ryswells pelting Barrowton boys with snowballs; squires building armed snowmen along the battlements; a sentry japes about "Lord Winter" then spits upon recognizing Theon
12. **Theon wanders the ruins** — Visits Maester Luwin's shattered turret (ravens above), his own former bedchamber (ankle-deep in snow through broken window), Mikken's forge, Lady Catelyn's sept
13. **Theon passes the Burned Tower** — Sees Rickard Ryswell nuzzling the plump washerwoman; she says something that makes Ryswell laugh
14. **Theon climbs the inner wall battlements** — Surveys Winterfell's defenses (inner wall 100 feet, outer wall thicker but lower, frozen moat); observes the snow burying the landscape, kingsroad vanishing, winter town buried
15. **Theon assesses Stannis's chances** — Concludes storm assault would fail against Winterfell's defenses; notes a siege would also be difficult since Bolton's stores are limited and Stannis's men would starve too
16. **Theon visits the godswood and prays** — Kneels beside the pool beneath the heart tree; begs "Save me" through broken teeth; hears faint soft sobbing; wonders if it is Jeyne or the gods weeping
17. **Theon returns to the Great Hall** — Sees snowmen fashioned as caricatures of lords (Manderly, Harwood Stout, Barbrey Dustin, Whoresbane Umber); eats stew and drinks ale
18. **Bolton scouts report** — Two scouts return through Hunter's Gate; Stannis's advance slowed to a crawl; destriers foundering; garrons of the hill clans faring better but dare not press ahead
19. **Ramsay commands a mocking song** — Abel sings a marching song for Stannis; a washerwoman mimes Stannis slashing at snowflakes with a sword taken from Sour Alyn
20. **Lady Dustin summons Theon** — Sends two sworn swords to bring him; notes he wears the same clothes from the wedding; demands he show her the way to the crypts
21. **Expedition to the crypts** — They exit the hall into the storm; cross snow-covered yard to the First Keep; Lady Dustin's men spend half an hour uncovering the entrance and axing open the frozen door
22. **Descent into the crypts** — They go single file down steep spiral steps; Theon notes the air grows warmer underground
23. **Lady Dustin warns about the bride** — Tells Theon that "Arya's" weeping does more harm than Stannis's army; the northmen fear the Dreadfort but love the Starks; Ramsay should teach his wife to laugh or risk losing support
24. **Missing swords discovered** — Theon and Lady Dustin notice at least three crypt swords missing (an unnamed king's, Rickard Stark's, Brandon Stark's); Theon is disquieted — tradition says the iron keeps the dead spirits locked in their tombs
25. **Lady Dustin reveals her history with Brandon Stark** — Brandon fostered at Barrowton; rode the Rills; took her maidenhead; loved his sword; Lady Dustin's pain when he was betrothed to Catelyn Tully; Rickard Stark's "southron ambitions"
26. **Lady Dustin reveals resentment of Eddard Stark** — Her father hoped to wed her to Ned, but Catelyn got him too; left with Lord Dustin, who died in Robert's Rebellion; Ned returned the horse but not Lord Dustin's body; brought his sister's bones north instead
27. **Lady Dustin's threat about Ned's bones** — Catelyn dispatched Ned's bones north before the Red Wedding; Theon's iron uncle seized Moat Cailin blocking passage; Lady Dustin is watching for the bones — means to feed them to her dogs
28. **Return from the crypts** — Lady Dustin warns Theon not to repeat anything she said; Theon understands: "Hold my tongue or lose it"; she notes "Roose has trained you well" and leaves

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pervasive dread and despair; fractured identity cycling between Theon, Reek, and the Turncloak; fear of Ramsay dominates every interaction; deep self-loathing mixed with faint stirrings of his old self (desire to hit or kiss Rowan, momentary thoughts of escape); profound loneliness (no one welcomes his company); grief and guilt over the Starks, particularly Bran and Rickon; desperate prayer at the heart tree reveals a yearning for salvation he cannot articulate
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival under Ramsay's control; the impossibility of escape (gates sealed, nowhere to go, no allies); fear that Ramsay will return his attention to tormenting him now that Jeyne is losing her novelty; the identity struggle — he keeps correcting himself and others to "Reek" but his real self keeps surfacing
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to engage with Rowan or share how he captured Winterfell (suspects Ramsay sent her as a trap); agrees to lead Lady Dustin to the crypts; agrees to keep Lady Dustin's secrets (under threat)
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells Jeyne "He never cut me without cause" — rationalizing Ramsay's torture as justified punishment; thinks "It is her own fault. She has not pleased him" about Jeyne's abuse, parroting Ramsay's logic; reflexively insists he is Reek while his thoughts continually surface as Theon; claims he had Bran and Rickon killed (they were miller's boys), though the text notes "The gods could not kill Bran, no more than I could" and "Bran might still be alive," suggesting he knows the truth
