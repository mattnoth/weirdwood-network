- **chapter_summary:** Aboard the Selaesori Qhoran, seven days out from Volantis, Tyrion observes the red priest Moqorro conducting evening prayers and notices Penny emerging briefly from her cabin before retreating. Tyrion speaks with Moqorro, who reports seeing "dragons old and young, true and false, bright and dark" and Tyrion himself as "a small man with a big shadow" in his flames. After an argument with Jorah Mormont about befriending Penny, Tyrion encounters her in the galley where she throws wine in his face after an exchange about her brother's death. Following a violent storm, Penny approaches Tyrion on the forecastle and they reconcile, beginning a tentative friendship over subsequent days. Late one night, Tyrion sees a red glow in the northeastern sky and Moqorro reveals they are near Valyria, describing a vision of "a tall and twisted thing with one black eye and ten long arms, sailing on a sea of blood."

## Events & Actions
1. **Penny emerges on deck** — Seven days from Volantis, Penny finally comes up from her cabin during evening prayers, but retreats when she notices Tyrion.
2. **Moqorro conducts nightfire prayers** — The red priest leads evening prayers at the great iron brazier amidships, with his five Fiery Hand guardsmen leading responses in Old Volantis.
3. **Tyrion converses with Moqorro about visions** — After prayers, Tyrion asks Moqorro what he sees in the flames. Moqorro reports seeing "dragons old and young, true and false, bright and dark" and Tyrion as "a small man with a big shadow, snarling in the midst of all."
4. **Tyrion and Jorah argue about Penny** — In their shared cabin, Tyrion reports Penny's appearance to Jorah. Jorah tells Tyrion that Penny is his burden and leaves angrily.
5. **Tyrion reads in the galley** — Tyrion rereads the slave girl's erotic story over supper of buttered beets, cold fish stew, and hard biscuits.
6. **Penny enters the galley; wine thrown** — Penny enters the galley; Tyrion offers food, wine, and company. She accuses him of responsibility for her brother's death and the deaths of other dwarfs. When Tyrion lists his actual kills including the stewed singer, Penny throws wine in his face and storms out.
7. **Storm strikes the Selaesori Qhoran** — A violent storm with red sky, black clouds, lightning, thunder, and rough seas hits the ship. Tyrion stays on the forecastle through the storm; Jorah is found drunk in vomit in their cabin afterward.
8. **Post-storm cyvasse** — Tyrion celebrates surviving the storm by drinking rum and playing three games of cyvasse with the ship's cook, winning one and losing two.
9. **Penny and Tyrion reconcile** — Tyrion finds Penny on the forecastle that night. She apologizes for throwing the wine and acknowledges the storm made her realize she wants to live. They bond over conversation about the singer Tyrion had killed, Penny's family history, and their shared dwarfism.
10. **Penny tells her family history** — She reveals her father was a dwarf sold to a slaver at age three who became a famous mummer and bought his freedom, known as "Hop-Bean" in Oldtown. Her mother was not a dwarf. Both parents are dead.
11. **Penny tells the story of the Tyroshi juggler** — An old dwarf juggler in Tyrosh was murdered and cut into three parts placed in the mouths of the Trios statue; his head was taken, presumably as a bounty for Tyrion's head.
12. **Penny reveals the recruiter** — Someone named Osmund or Oswald recruited them in Pentos for the jousting at Joffrey's wedding; Oppo handled arrangements.
13. **Tyrion and Penny begin bonding over subsequent days** — They take meals together, sometimes with the Fiery Hand guards. Tyrion nicknames the five guards "the fingers." Penny shows Tyrion her pig Pretty. Tyrion tries to teach her cyvasse unsuccessfully. Penny asks Tyrion to tilt with her; he declines.
14. **Tyrion sees Valyria's glow** — Unable to sleep, Tyrion goes on deck late at night and sees a dull red glow in the northeastern sky and a monstrous swollen moon. Moqorro reveals they are near Valyria.
15. **Moqorro's second vision** — Moqorro reports seeing shadows of others seeking Daenerys, "one most of all. A tall and twisted thing with one black eye and ten long arms, sailing on a sea of blood."
16. **Moqorro reveals he commanded the captain** — Moqorro tells Tyrion he has commanded the captain to steer the shortest course despite the captain's preference to stay well away from Valyria's shore.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pity for Penny mixed with guilt he refuses to own; loneliness and tedium aboard the ship; fleeting suicidal ideation on moonless nights; bitterness toward Jorah; elation after surviving the storm; cautious warmth toward Penny as they bond; cold chill of fear when learning they are near Valyria; wariness about Moqorro's visions
- **Primary preoccupation:** Penny's grief and whether to befriend her; the tedium of sea travel; reaching Meereen and Daenerys; recurring thoughts of Tysha ("where do whores go"); dreams of the Sorrows and a stony king with his father's face; the danger of Valyria's proximity
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to try befriending Penny despite her hostility; decides not to tell Moqorro about Griff and the young prince; agrees to accompany Penny to Daenerys's court
- **Self-deception flags:** Claims "No" when Jorah asks if he feels guilty about Penny's brother, but his behavior (seeking her out, offering comfort, insisting they bring her from Volantis) suggests otherwise; tells Penny "Daenerys has a kind heart" while internally fearing Daenerys may execute him; dismisses tales of Valyria's curse as superstition while Gerion's disappearance suggests real danger
