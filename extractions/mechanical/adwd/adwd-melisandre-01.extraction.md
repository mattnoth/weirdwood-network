## Chapter Metadata

- **chapter_number:** 32

- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Stannis has marched south from Castle Black; Jon Snow is serving as Lord Commander of the Night's Watch
- **time_markers:** "Almost dawn" when Devan answers Melisandre about the hour; Melisandre has spent the entire night in her chair by the fire; "Another day is given us"; dawn breaks during the chapter; events of finding the heads occur in early morning; the east "just begun to lighten" with stars of morning still in a pitch-black sky; breakfast time; "before the day is done" (Melisandre's prediction about rangers returning); warhorn sounds shortly after dawn
- **chapter_summary:** Melisandre spends the night gazing into her hearthfire seeking visions of Stannis but sees only Jon Snow, skulls, a wooden face with a thousand red eyes, a wolf-faced boy, and a grey girl on a dying horse. She confers with the wildling she calls "Rattleshirt" (who is actually Mance Rayder under a glamor) about rescuing the grey girl she believes is Arya Stark. A warhorn announces returning rangers, and three severed heads on spears are discovered north of the Wall — Night's Watch rangers with their eyes cut out. Melisandre walks with Jon Snow back through the Wall, warns him about a threat to Eastwatch, then brings him to her chambers where she dispels the glamor to reveal Mance Rayder, presenting him as the instrument to rescue Jon's sister.

## Events & Actions
1. **Melisandre prays and gazes into the hearthfire** — Seeking visions of Stannis, whom she considers Azor Ahai reborn, marching south into peril.
2. **She sees multiple visions** — Eyeless faces weeping blood; towers by the sea crumbling under a dark tide; skulls turning to mist; bodies locked in lust; great winged shadows against a blue sky.
3. **She seeks the grey girl** — Tries to find the grey girl on the dying horse again for Jon Snow; has only seen her once, "a girl as grey as ash" who crumbled and blew away.
4. **A face appears in the hearth** — A wooden face, corpse white, with a thousand red eyes in rising flames. "He sees me." Beside it, a boy with a wolf's face howls.
5. **Melisandre experiences physical agony and ecstasy** — Blood trickles down her thigh, black and smoking; heat traces her skin; she hears voices from days long past calling "Melony" and "Lot Seven."
6. **Vision of the Wall** — Snowflakes and ashes whirling; flaming arrows above a wooden wall; dead things shambling in cold beneath a great grey cliff with fires in a hundred caves; white mist sweeps in and the fires go out; only skulls remain.
7. **She sees Jon Snow in the flames** — His long face appears, man then wolf then man, with skulls all around him. "Enemies all around him, daggers in the dark."
8. **She asks Devan for water** — Her throat is raw and parched; Devan pours from the stone jug.
9. **Melisandre reflects on Devan** — The boy is half in love with her; she asked Stannis to leave him at Castle Black to protect him after his four elder brothers died at the Blackwater.
10. **She reflects on her guards** — Stannis left a dozen men: greybeards and cripples, a blinded man, a lame man, a one-armed serjeant, three geldings castrated for rape, two drunkards, and a craven from a noble family.
11. **Dawn breaks** — She opens her shutters; the east lightens; Castle Black stirs; men head to break fast before Wall duty.
12. **She orders breakfast and sends Devan for the wildling** — Nettle tea, boiled egg, fresh bread with butter; tells Devan to fetch "Rattleshirt."
13. **She washes, changes robes, and checks her powders** — Inventories her hidden powders (fire colors, smoke types); notes her carved chest is three-quarters empty; she is stronger at the Wall than in Asshai.
14. **The wildling (Mance in glamor) arrives** — Wearing sleeveless leather jerkin and worn cloak, no bone armor; the glamor's grey mist slides across him; Melisandre's ruby stirs at the closeness.
15. **They discuss the glamor and bones** — Mance describes the ruby's warmth and burning; Melisandre insists the bones are necessary for the spell's integrity.
16. **They discuss the grey girl** — Melisandre identifies her as Jon Snow's sister; she saw the girl near deep blue water with a thin coat of ice; Mance identifies it as Long Lake; the girl is staying away from villages, riding in streambeds.
17. **Mance begins planning a rescue route** — Asks if the lake is east or west of the girl; Melisandre says west; Mance notes she's not on the kingsroad and there's more cover on the other side.
18. **A warhorn sounds** — One long blast: rangers returning. Melisandre thinks "The day has come."
19. **Melisandre orders Mance to put on bones and stay** — Warns that any wildling seen will inflame the black brothers.
20. **She descends and meets Devan on the stairs** — He carries her forgotten breakfast on a tray with fresh bread still hot from ovens.
21. **She proceeds beyond the Wall** — Through the ice tunnel with Morgan (torch) and Merrel (axe); crowd of crows at the gate makes way for her.
22. **Three severed heads found on ash spears** — Black Jack Bulwer, Hairy Hal, and Garth Greyfeather; beards full of ice, snow-capped, eye sockets empty and bloody. Jon Snow, Bowen Marsh, and twenty spearmen already present; a dozen archers on the Wall.
23. **Bowen Marsh argues against sending rangers** — Jon cuts him off: "This is not the time and place."
24. **Ghost investigates and urinates on one of the spears** — Jon says Ghost would have the wildlings' scent if they were still nearby.
25. **Jon orders heads pulled down and burned** — "Leave nothing but bare bone."
26. **Melisandre and Jon walk together through the Wall** — She slips her arm through his; makes the ice drip where she walks; Jon asks about the other six missing rangers.
27. **Jon shares intelligence from Ser Denys Mallister** — Fires in mountains on far side of the Gorge; wildlings massing; possible attack on the Bridge of Skulls.
28. **Melisandre warns of attack on Eastwatch** — She saw towers by the sea submerged beneath a black and bloody tide; identifies it as Eastwatch though privately uncertain.
29. **They emerge south of the Wall; crowd gathers** — Three-Finger Hobb, Owen the Oaf, Mully, Septon Celladar, Alf of Runnymudd all react to the news. Alf howls in grief.
30. **Melisandre invites Jon to the King's Tower** — He agrees reluctantly; she dismisses her guards.
31. **They walk together and Melisandre reflects** — She notes Jon's mistrust; compares him to Stannis; both were younger sons in shadows, both unbelievers, both worshipping honor and duty.
32. **Melisandre raises the topic of his sister** — Jon insists he has no sister under his vows and cannot help Arya.
33. **They enter her chambers and find the wildling eating** — Mance has donned the bone armor; he is spreading butter on bread with his dagger; the broken giant's skull rests on the window seat.
34. **Jon confronts "Rattleshirt"** — Jon refuses to break bread with him; Mance taunts about Jon's guards being a "bad jape"; mentions Ygritte; proposes the rescue mission needing horses and spearwives from Mole's Town.
35. **Jon angrily refuses** — Says Rattleshirt would rape and murder Arya; calls Melisandre's visions "ashes in your eyes"; threatens to take Rattleshirt's head if he tries to leave.
36. **Melisandre dispels the glamor** — Sends Devan away; touches her ruby and speaks a word that echoes queerly; the wildling's ruby darkens; the false features melt away.
37. **Mance Rayder is revealed** — The widow's peak, brown mustache, sallow flesh dissolve; he becomes bigger, broader, long-legged and lean, clean-shaved with windburnt face and long brown hair with grey in it.
38. **Jon recognizes Mance** — "She burned you." / "She burned the Lord of Bones."
39. **Melisandre explains the glamor** — "Glamor, seeming, illusion"; R'hllor is Lord of Light and his servants weave with light; bones help the strongest glamors; "a dead man's boots, a hank of hair, a bag of fingerbones" can be used.
40. **Melisandre presents Mance as Arya's rescuer** — Notes they hold Mance's son and he owes Jon his life; Jon's arrows saved her from the pain of the burning; she presents Mance as "a gift from the Lord of Light … and me."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined and focused but frustrated; cannot find Stannis in her flames and keeps seeing Jon Snow instead. Experiences physical agony and ecstasy during fire-gazing. Haunted by past memories (Melony, Lot Seven). Strategic and calculating in all her interactions — aware of how power is performed. Feels confident in her own abilities (especially at the Wall) but privately uncertain about the meaning of some visions.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding Stannis in the flames and interpreting R'hllor's visions correctly; gaining Jon Snow's trust to advance R'hllor's cause; rescuing the grey girl to win Jon's cooperation; the threat of the "enemy" (the Great Other) growing stronger beyond the Wall.
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to reveal Mance's true identity to Jon Snow ("He leaves me no choice"); keeps Devan at Castle Black to protect him; identifies Eastwatch as the vision's target (privately uncertain); instructs Mance to plan the grey girl's rescue with spearwives.
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes Stannis is Azor Ahai reborn despite R'hllor consistently showing her Jon Snow; interprets all visions through a Stannis-centric framework; dismisses the free folk as "a lost people, a doomed people" despite needing Mance's help; privately uncertain about the towers-by-the-sea vision but tells Jon definitively "Yes. Eastwatch, my lord"; claims R'hllor provides all the nourishment she needs but acknowledges this "was something best concealed from mortal men."
