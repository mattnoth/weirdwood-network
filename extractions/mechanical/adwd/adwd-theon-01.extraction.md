# ADWD — Theon I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 52
- **pov_character:** Theon Greyjoy
- **pov_chapter_number:** Theon I
- **first_available:** ADWD Theon I
- **location_primary:** Winterfell
- **location_secondary:** None (all action is within Winterfell's walls and battlements)
- **approximate_timeline:** During the siege of Winterfell; Stannis's army is nearby but has not yet attacked; Bolton forces are preparing a sortie
- **time_markers:** Dawn/daybreak at chapter opening ("Day stole upon them"); sky beginning to lighten; drums fading away after a night of sounding; "not three days' ride from here" (Stannis's position); Theon "had not slept in days"; breaking fast (morning meal); the bath is described as happening "by day" which is unusual
- **chapter_summary:** Theon observes a tense breakfast in Winterfell's Great Hall disrupted by Ser Hosteen Frey carrying in the murdered body of Little Walder Frey. The accusation against Wyman Manderly triggers a violent brawl that kills eight men. Roose Bolton exploits the chaos to dispatch both Freys and Manderlys against Stannis. Abel's washerwomen seize the moment to execute their escape plan, smuggling Jeyne Poole (posing as Arya Stark) out of Ramsay's bedchamber. The escape goes awry at the battlements when Holly is killed by crossbowmen and Frenya (carrying the rope) is left behind fighting guards. Trapped, Theon grabs Jeyne and leaps from the walls.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Heavy, continuous snowfall; icy wind; storm conditions; ice crystals in the air; snow described as a "white wilderness" filling the yards
- **Season indicators:** A stableboy insists "it isn't even winter"; Theon recalls Old Nan's tales of storms lasting years that buried castles; "Winter is coming" spoken aloud
- **Time of day:** Dawn at opening; morning through the chapter (breakfast, preparations); the escape happens during daylight hours ("By day? We will be seen")
- **Lighting:** Torchlight in the Great Hall; frozen blood sparkles in torchlight; Ramsay's bedchamber in shadow with dying embers and a single candle; blue-white ice crystals when doors open
- **Sounds:** Drums (fading since night, now gone); warhorns heard thrice, each closer; horses screaming at blood scent; dogs growling; shouts, prayers, curses during the brawl; Luton crying for his mother; trumpet blast from ramparts; warhorn from the north (Stannis) at chapter's end
- **Smells:** Blood (sets horses screaming); kitchen smells of roast meats, leeks and onions, fresh-baked bread; warm mud pool stinking of rotten eggs
- **Notable sensory details:** Cold pervasive throughout; ice crystals sparkling blue-white; frozen blood forming pink frost on Little Walder's corpse; steam from hot water pails melting snowflakes; Theon's missing finger-stumps itching and tingling; slippery ice on battlements steps; the pain of falling on one knee on the stairs

## Events & Actions
1. **Dawn breaks over Winterfell** — Day arrives unseen, like Stannis. The castle has been awake for hours awaiting an attack that never came. Drums have faded; warhorns heard thrice, each closer.
2. **Theon observes the Great Hall** — He sits in the back near horses, watching Abel and the washerwomen eat. Roose Bolton enters with Fat Walda. Various lords present: Whoresbane Umber, Aenys Frey, Roger Ryswell, Wyman Manderly, Lord Locke, Harwood Stout.
3. **Ramsay arrives in foul mood** — Buckling his sword belt; argues with his father on the dais. Fat Walda looks fearful.
4. **Theon expresses doubt about Abel's plan** — Warns they will be caught; Ramsay will hunt them. Abel responds that Stannis is near. Squirrel vouches for "Abel's word."
5. **Theon reflects on the escape's futility** — Knows Jeyne is not Arya (wrong eye color); Abel doesn't know the truth; considers the deception.
6. **Ser Hosteen Frey crashes into the Great Hall** — Carries frozen body of Little Walder Frey, armored in pink frost. Big Walder enters behind him, spattered with blood.
7. **Murder announced** — Hosteen identifies the body as "my brother Merrett's son," butchered and shoved under a snowbank. Big Walder says body found under ruined keep with old gargoyles.
8. **Big Walder provides details** — Little Walder went looking for a man who owed him silver won at dice; White Harbor men taught dice.
9. **Hosteen accuses Wyman Manderly** — Claims Manderly ordered this and all the other murders.
10. **Manderly's provocation** — Asks the boy's age (nine), then says "Had he lived, he would have grown up to be a Frey."
11. **Hosteen attacks Manderly** — Kicks table into Manderly's belly; draws longsword; slashes through three of his four chins.
12. **Brawl erupts** — Manderly men rush Freys; one stabs at Hosteen but gets his arm taken off at the shoulder. Manderly collapses. Dogs fight over sausages.
13. **Bolton men restore order** — Two score Dreadfort spearmen part combatants. Six White Harbor men and two Freys dead; a dozen more wounded; Luton dying.
14. **Ramsay kills Luton** — Drives a spear through the dying man's chest to silence him.
15. **Roose Bolton takes command** — Reveals parchment (from raven): Stannis is three days' ride away, snowbound and starving. Orders Hosteen to assemble at main gates, Manderly at east gate.
16. **Hosteen accepts but threatens Manderly** — "After I deliver you the head of Stannis Baratheon, I mean to finish hacking off Lord Lard's."
17. **White Harbor knights form protective ring around Manderly** — Eldest wears surcoat with three silvery mermaids on violet field.
18. **Ramsay enforces his father's command** — Brandishes bloody spear; threatens to gut dissenters.
19. **Roose calls Abel to sing** — His pale eyes search the hall and find Abel beside Theon; Abel plays a sad soft song.
20. **Rowan declares they must escape now** — "The bath. It must be now." Bolton is sending swords out; they must reach Stannis first.
21. **Washerwomen gather** — Squirrel slips away; Theon tells them to find their sisters and fill the tub.
22. **Theon and Rowan walk to the godswood** — Paths are icy trenches; snow has made ten-foot misshapen snowmen; Theon knows every twist.
23. **Godswood scene** — Heart tree pool iced over; face has icicle mustache; northmen praying. Rowan pulls Theon to secluded spot by warm mud pool.
24. **Rowan and Theon argue** — She forbids him from speaking Eddard's words. He accuses her of killing Little Walder. She denies it. She calls him kinslayer.
25. **Theon's internal confession** — Admits terrible things but says the Stark boys were not killed; the two killed were miller's sons. Reflects on the miller's wife.
26. **Rowan rejects his claim of sufficient punishment** — "Not enough."
27. **Theon considers killing Rowan** — Decides against it; the old gods called him Theon; keeps dagger sheathed.
28. **Remaining washerwomen arrive** — Squirrel returns with Myrtle, Willow Witch-Eye, Frenya, Holly. All dressed as serving girls in drab grey roughspun with brown cloaks. No weapons but knives (and Frenya's rope).
29. **Holly outlines the plan** — Six women in, six come out; dress Jeyne as Squirrel; Squirrel will climb out window to godswood.
30. **Party goes to kitchen for hot water** — Guards recognize Reek; let them pass with taunts. Potboy fills six wooden pails. The washerwomen's sullen silence draws notice; guard asks about the usual girls; Theon lies.
31. **Procession through ice trenches to Great Keep** — Frey and Manderly troops moving in opposite directions toward separate gates. Snow and steam.
32. **Pass Great Keep's ground-floor guards** — Six Dreadfort men; serjeant questions the bath but lets them through.
33. **Reach Ramsay's bedchamber** — Sour Alyn and Grunt guarding; Alyn insults Theon's smell; unlocks the door.
34. **Enter bedchamber** — Dark room; dying fire; empty bed. Jeyne found hiding under wolfskins in darkest corner, trembling.
35. **Jeyne's terror** — Believes it's a trick from Ramsay; says she's been trained; offers to do anything; mentions "the dog"; begs not to have her feet cut off.
36. **Theon persuades Jeyne** — Tells her she had brothers (Robb, Bran, Rickon); Rowan mentions Jon Snow as "Lord Crow" and offers to take her to him. Jeyne finally agrees.
37. **Jeyne revealed naked with bite marks** — Wolfskins fall away; small pale breasts covered with teeth marks.
38. **Dress Jeyne in Squirrel's clothes** — Squirrel searches cedar chest, takes Ramsay's quilted doublet and breeches.
39. **Squirrel takes Jeyne's place in bed** — Pulls blanket up.
40. **Exit past guards** — Sour Alyn makes crude remark; Grunt gropes Willow; they don't look closely at Jeyne.
41. **Theon feels momentary elation** — "They never looked. They never saw."
42. **Fear returns on the stairs** — Worries about encountering Skinner, Damon, Steelshanks, or Ramsay.
43. **Exit the Great Keep** — Guards huddled against cold, backs turned; serjeant gives only a quick glance.
44. **Rowan splits the party** — Drops her pail; tells Holly and Frenya to go with Theon; she, Willow, and Myrtle go back for Abel.
45. **Theon leads Jeyne, Holly, and Frenya toward Battlements Gate** — Through the ice-trench maze; Jeyne says "it's cold."
46. **Arrive at Battlements Gate** — Two guards in wool/fur/leather with eight-foot spears. Guard recognizes Reek.
47. **Theon identifies himself as Theon Greyjoy** — Surprises himself; says he's brought women for the guards.
48. **Holly kills first guard** — Pulls his scarf loose, kisses him, slides blade through his neck. Blood on her lips.
49. **Frenya kills second guard** — Grabs his spear shaft; wrests it away; clubs him with butt; drives point through his belly.
50. **Jeyne screams** — Alerting the castle.
51. **Escape through gate and over frozen moat** — Drawbridge left down for defender access to outer battlements.
52. **Frenya stays behind** — "I will hold the kneelers here." Clutches bloody spear.
53. **Theon carries Jeyne up battlements stairs** — Slips on ice, falls on one knee; Holly pulls him up.
54. **Holly realizes the rope is with Frenya** — "Oh, fuck me bloody. The rope." Hysterical laugh.
55. **Holly killed** — First crossbow quarrel through her gut; second between her breasts. Falls, buried by snow turning red.
56. **Theon and Jeyne trapped** — Men approaching from both sides; crossbowman reloading; warhorn from north (Stannis). No sign of Abel or the others.
57. **Theon jumps with Jeyne** — Grabs her about the waist and leaps from the battlements.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pervasive terror of Ramsay dominates all thought; resignation to likely death ("We are all dead"); momentary giddy elation when passing guards; deep grief and guilt over past betrayals; lingering connection to Theon identity vs. Reek conditioning
- **Primary preoccupation:** Fear of recapture by Ramsay; knowledge that the escape plan is doomed because Jeyne is not really Arya; internal struggle between Reek (obedient, wanting to please Ramsay) and Theon (ironborn, capable of agency)
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses not to reveal to Abel that Jeyne is not Arya; chooses not to kill Rowan when he could; keeps his dagger sheathed because "the old gods had known him, had called him Theon"; identifies himself as "Theon Greyjoy" to the gate guards instead of Reek; ultimately leaps from the battlements with Jeyne rather than be recaptured
- **Self-deception flags:** None — Theon is remarkably honest with himself in this chapter. He acknowledges his crimes, recognizes the plan is mad, understands the truth about Jeyne, and still acts. The Reek identity is a recognized threat he actively resists.
