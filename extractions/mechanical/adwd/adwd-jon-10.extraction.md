- **chapter_summary:** Melisandre performs the wedding ceremony of Alys Karstark and Sigorn, Magnar of Thenn, using a fire-leaping R'hllor rite at Castle Black. Jon visits Cregan Karstark in the ice cells and offers him the choice between the black or execution when Stannis returns. At the wedding feast, Jon receives a letter from Cotter Pyke reporting that eleven ships have departed Eastwatch for Hardhome. Ser Axell Florent presses Jon about marrying Val to one of Stannis's knights, which Jon rebuffs. The feast is interrupted by two horn blasts signaling wildlings — Tormund Giantsbane has arrived.

## Events & Actions
1. **Wedding ceremony begins** — Melisandre performs a R'hllor marriage rite at the ditchfire by the Wall, with Queen Selyse and attendees watching in the cold and snow.
2. **Jon gives away the bride** — Jon presents Alys Karstark, speaking the formal words, and steps back.
3. **Sigorn claims Alys** — The Magnar claims her with fire-vows; Alys responds with her own vow.
4. **The couple leaps the ditch** — Alys and Sigorn leap across the fire-filled ditch together, completing the ceremony.
5. **Sigorn cloaks Alys** — He removes the maiden's cloak and fastens the new bride's cloak with House Thenn's improvised arms.
6. **Jon approaches Queen Selyse** — She speaks of wanting a second wedding with Stannis blessed by R'hllor; Melisandre excuses herself to attend her fires.
7. **Ser Malegorn escorts the queen** — He pointedly refuses Satin's service, implying disdain for the steward.
8. **Patchface speaks a prophecy** — "Under the sea the mermen feast on starfish soup, and all the serving men are crabs."
9. **Melisandre warns Jon about Patchface** — She says the creature is dangerous; she has seen skulls about him and his lips red with blood in her flames.
10. **Jon and Melisandre discuss Stannis** — She reports seeing only "snow" when searching for him; Jon mentions the raven sent to Deepwood Motte about Arnolf's treachery and the Braavosi banker searching for Stannis.
11. **Melisandre recites Azor Ahai prophecy** — "When the red star bleeds and the darkness gathers, Azor Ahai shall be born again amidst smoke and salt"; Jon counters that Stannis was born at Storm's End, not Dragonstone.
12. **Jon asks about Mance** — Melisandre sees only "snow" for him as well; Jon wonders if she lied on purpose.
13. **Melisandre warns Jon of danger** — "Daggers in the dark"; she sees his face every time she looks into flames; urges him to keep Ghost beside him.
14. **Jon visits Cregan Karstark in the ice cells** — Jon informs him Alys is wed to the Magnar; Cregan invokes hospitality laws (rejected) and refuses to yield Karhold.
15. **Jon offers Cregan the black** — Take the black or face execution when Stannis returns; Cregan refuses ("Never, never, never").
16. **Jon attends the wedding feast** — Ser Axell toasts Stannis; food and dancing proceed.
17. **Jon observes the northern chiefs** — Old Flint and The Norrey sit in places of honor; they did not spill their wine, suggesting acceptance.
18. **Dancing at the feast** — Queen's men dance with ladies; Ulmer proves adept; Satin dances gracefully with serving girls; Owen and Patchface dance together.
19. **Jon and Alys converse about the Thenns** — Jon explains Thenn culture; Alys mentions her father told her to charm Robb.
20. **Alys reports Karhold's food situation** — Crops failed; winter will be hard; many old and young will die.
21. **Jon invites Karhold to send old men and boys to the Wall** — "Send your old men to the Wall, let them say our words."
22. **Jon receives a letter from Eastwatch** — Cotter Pyke reports eleven ships sailed for Hardhome; Glendon Hewett holds Eastwatch.
23. **Ser Axell presses Jon about Val** — Wants Val married to a queen's knight (himself or Ser Patrek); Jon rebuffs him sharply.
24. **Two horn blasts sound** — The feast falls silent; two blasts mean wildlings. Jon identifies it as Tormund Giantsbane's arrival.

- **Self-deception flags:** "You know nothing, Jon Snow" repeats twice internally — acknowledging the limits of his knowledge; wonders what his dead father or lost uncle would do, suggesting uncertainty in his own authority; dismisses Melisandre's warning about danger growing close despite repeated specific warnings
