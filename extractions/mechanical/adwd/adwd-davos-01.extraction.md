# ADWD — Davos I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 10
- **pov_character:** Davos Seaworth
- **pov_chapter_number:** Davos I
- **first_available:** ADWD Davos I
- **location_primary:** Breakwater Castle, Sisterton, Sweetsister
- **location_secondary:** White Harbor (discussed), the Wall/Castle Black (referenced), Dragonstone (recalled), Eastwatch-by-the-Sea (recalled), King's Landing (referenced), the Bite (recalled), Sisterton town
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Stannis's fleet sailed south from the Wall; twelve days after the Lionstar put in at Sisterton; after Tywin Lannister's death has reached the Sisters but not yet reached the Wall
- **time_markers:** Night (lightning storm throughout); "twelve days ago" since the Lionstar arrived at Sisterton; the journey from the Wall described in retrospect; storm duration implied over multiple days of sailing; six heartbeats between lightning and thunder (opening), then four, then two (storm approaching)
- **chapter_summary:** Davos Seaworth, shipwrecked and separated from Salladhor Saan's fleet, is captured in Sisterton and brought before Lord Godric Borrell of Sweetsister. Davos reveals he carries credentials as Stannis's Hand and is bound for White Harbor. Lord Godric informs him that Tywin Lannister is dead (killed by Tyrion), that Lysa Arryn is dead, that Littlefinger rules the Vale, and that the Freys are sailing to White Harbor to seal a marriage pact with Lord Wyman Manderly. After Davos argues that Stannis's cause is not lost, Borrell tells him the story of Ned Stark passing through Sisterton at the start of Robert's Rebellion, implying he will let Davos go as his father let Stark go.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Violent lightning storm throughout; cold rain; lashing rain against walls; gales referenced in the backstory of the voyage south
- **Season indicators:** "With winter coming on" referenced regarding White Harbor's ice-free port; the cold and storms consistent with late autumn/early winter
- **Time of day:** Night (dark hall, torches, candles, lightning illuminating windows)
- **Lighting:** Only four of twenty iron sconces hold torches, none lit; two fat tallow candles provide meagre flickering light; lightning flashes illuminate the hall periodically through arrow loops
- **Sounds:** Thunder (distant drum at first, growing closer); rain lashing walls; steady dripping from a leaky roof; the sea rumbling and pounding beneath the floor; the outer door closing "with a sound like distant thunder"
- **Smells:** Sister's stew (creamy, with saffron and pepper); Sisterton recalled as "rank with the odors of pig shit and rotting fish"; Davos recalls the burning-flesh smell of the wind from Alester Florent's execution
- **Notable sensory details:** Davos is soaked, cold, sore; his wrists chafe from wet rope; the sea swirling through dungeons beneath the castle; green waters surging and sending up plumes of spray; the bread is hot from the oven; the stew warms "right down to his bones"

## Events & Actions
1. **Davos captured in Sisterton** — Guards find Davos in the Belly of the Whale trying to buy passage off the island; he has twelve dragons and his ribbon of office.
2. **Davos brought before Lord Godric** — Marched across bridges, through gatehouses, across muddy yard, and into Breakwater's keep.
3. **Identity confirmed** — Lord Godric orders Davos's gloves removed; sees his shortened fingers and identifies him as the onion knight.
4. **Interrogation begins** — Godric questions why Davos is in Sisterton rather than seeking him properly; Davos explains he seeks passage to White Harbor on the king's orders.
5. **Davos recalls the fleet's destruction** — Twenty-nine ships sailed from the Wall; multiple ships lost to storms (Oledo and Old Mother's Son on Skagos rocks, Saathos Saan off Grey Cliffs, Bountiful Harvest lost with a towing galley). Fleet scattered across the narrow sea.
6. **Davos recalls Salladhor Saan's abandonment** — Salla complained bitterly about lack of payment and abandoned Stannis's cause, sailing south toward the Stepstones.
7. **Lord Godric reveals Lysa Arryn is dead** — Murdered by a singer; Lord Littlefinger rules the Vale.
8. **Lord Godric reveals Tywin Lannister is dead** — Killed by Tyrion, who escaped his cell; the queen offers lordship for Tyrion's death.
9. **Lord Godric reveals the Frey-Manderly marriage pact** — Lionstar with Freys aboard put in at Sisterton twelve days ago heading for White Harbor; Rhaegar Frey seeks a new wife there; ravens flying between Manderly and Walder Frey.
10. **Lord Godric shares intelligence about Manderly** — Fat man means to bend knee, not to Stannis; broke his vengeance vow quickly; Freys bringing bones of Manderly's dead son.
11. **Davos asks for help reaching White Harbor** — Argues Stannis's cause citing Tywin's death and Cersei's rule.
12. **Lord Godric tells the story of Ned Stark's visit** — At the dawn of Robert's Rebellion, Ned Stark came through Sisterton fleeing the Mad King; Borrell's father let him go despite the maester's urging to send his head to Aerys.
13. **Implied agreement to let Davos go** — The parallel between "If you lose, you were never here" and "He was never here" suggests Borrell will send Davos on his way.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Exhausted, haggard, grief-stricken (four sons dead), worn thin by betrayal (Salla's abandonment); grateful for food and shelter; determined to fulfill his mission to White Harbor despite desperate circumstances; punched in the belly by news of Frey-Manderly pact
- **Primary preoccupation:** Reaching White Harbor to win Wyman Manderly for Stannis; survival in the immediate moment (will Borrell hand him over or let him go?)
- **Key decisions made:** Chose to remain loyal to Stannis rather than sail south with Salla; lies to Lord Godric about Salla being sent south by Stannis (rehearsed lie); decides to argue Stannis's cause using Tywin's death as leverage
- **Self-deception flags:** "I was fond of the old rogue, but never so great a fool as to trust him" — yet Davos clearly was hurt by the betrayal; internally catalogues arguments for Stannis (rightful king, magic sword) but recognizes none would sway Borrell — awareness of his own rhetorical weakness
