# ADWD — Reek III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 33
- **pov_character:** Theon Greyjoy (as Reek)
- **pov_chapter_number:** Reek III
- **first_available:** ADWD Reek III
- **location_primary:** Barrowton — Harwood Stout's keep
- **location_secondary:** Barrowton — Barrow Hall (Lady Dustin's seat); streets of Barrowton; stables at Stout's keep
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly before Ramsay's wedding to "Arya Stark"; Stannis has just taken Deepwood Motte; the march to Winterfell is announced for three days hence
- **time_markers:** Sixteen days of riding for the hunt; feast at sundown; moon rising over Barrowton's wooden walls when Reek and Roose ride to Barrow Hall; march to Winterfell announced in three days; Roose says Ramsay will wed at Winterfell (future)
- **chapter_summary:** Ramsay Bolton returns empty-handed from a sixteen-day hunt for the missing Freys, bringing a severed head of an old man killed for calling him "Lord Snow." That night a feast is held at Harwood Stout's keep, during which Roose Bolton arrives and clears the hall for a private conversation with Ramsay and Reek. Roose discusses the missing Freys, Manderly's untrustworthiness, Stannis's movements, and announces the wedding will be held at Winterfell with a march in three days. Roose takes Reek from Ramsay over his son's protests, and rides with him to Barrow Hall, telling the story of how he sired Ramsay by raping a miller's wife. At Barrow Hall, Roose presents Reek to Lady Barbrey Dustin as "Theon of House Greyjoy," and Reek collapses in terror, insisting he is Reek.

- **Weather:** Cold autumnal sky; autumn chill; wind sweeping across rolling plains beyond Barrowton; wind rattling bare branches of elms lining the street

- **Lighting:** Moonlight during the ride (Bolton's pale eyes "looked empty in the moonlight"); dying fire/embers in Barrow Hall's hearth

- **Notable sensory details:** Rotting flesh of the severed head, green and crusted with dried blood, raining maggots; the ear tearing off between Reek's maimed fingers; blood-soaked rushes on the hall floor after the dog fight

## Events & Actions
1. **Ramsay returns from hunt** — Ramsay and his party of two dozen riders return through the gates of Harwood Stout's keep after a sixteen-day failed search for the missing Freys. Ben Bones leads the dogs, followed by Skinner, Sour Alyn, Damon Dance-for-Me, the Walders, and Ramsay on his stallion Blood.
2. **Dogs mob Reek** — The pack surrounds Reek, licking and nipping; Helicent bites his left hand hard enough that he fears losing more fingers; Red Jeyne knocks him down.
3. **Ramsay throws severed head** — Ramsay flings a rotting, maggot-ridden head at Reek. Reek fails to catch it. Little Walder and the men laugh. The head is left for the dogs.
4. **Big Walder reveals the head's origin** — In the stables, Big Walder tells Reek the head belonged to an old man killed for calling Ramsay "Lord Snow." The old man had been driving a nanny goat and four kids; they ate the kids and milked the mother.
5. **Big Walder declares the missing Freys dead** — Big Walder tells Reek he believes Lord Wyman had them killed, and that he never expected the search to succeed.
6. **Feast held at Stout's keep** — Ramsay commands a feast with slaughtered ox, roasts, ribs, barley bread, vegetables, and ale. Reek is chained by the doors. Little Walder serves as Ramsay's cupbearer; Big Walder pours for the high table.
7. **Dog fight kills Stout's hound** — Maude and Grey Jeyne tear into one of Lord Stout's hounds over a bone Will Short tossed. The old hound is killed. Reek watches Ramsay instead of the fight.
8. **Roose Bolton arrives** — A dozen men in grey mail and iron halfhelms enter the hall. Ramsay greets his father. Roose orders everyone out of the hall.
9. **Private Bolton conversation** — Roose and Ramsay discuss: the failed Frey search; Manderly's untrustworthiness (brought food but no hostages); Ramsay's threat against Lady Dustin; Roose's warning about the importance of the Dustin/Ryswell alliance; the killing of Bran and Rickon (officially blamed on "Theon Turncloak"); Roose's counsel about discretion ("A peaceful land, a quiet people"); Stannis leaving the Wall and taking Deepwood Motte with growing strength from mountain clans; the decision to hold the wedding at Winterfell and let Stannis march to them, where Arnolf Karstark will be summoned into a trap.
10. **Roose takes Reek from Ramsay** — Roose demands Reek be unchained. Ramsay resists but complies, whispering threats to Reek (tell Roose nothing, spy on him, another finger when returned). Reek weeps and pleads. Ramsay slaps him and surrenders him.
11. **Ride through Barrowton** — Roose and Reek ride from Stout's keep to Barrow Hall. Roose corrects Reek's speech ("m'lord" not "my lord"). Roose tells the story of the first Reek, then the story of how he raped Ramsay's mother, hanged her husband, later gave her the mill and sent yearly provisions. Roose reflects that Ramsay's mother and the first Reek made Ramsay what he is.
12. **Roose discusses his sons** — Roose tells Reek about Domeric Bolton, his accomplished trueborn son whom he believes Ramsay poisoned. He discusses Lady Walda and his expectation that Ramsay will kill any future sons.
13. **Reek offered bath and new clothes** — Roose offers a bath, lambswool, fur-lined cloak. Reek panics and refuses, terrified of removing the clothes Ramsay gave him and of anyone seeing his body.
14. **Roose credits Reek for the north** — Roose tells Reek he owes him "the north" because the Starks were "done and doomed the night that you took Winterfell."
15. **Arrival at Barrow Hall** — They pass beneath the gatehouse; Reek sees the banners of many northern houses and the royal stag-and-lion. Reek's legs shake climbing the wooden steps; he stares at the Great Barrow.
16. **Lady Dustin meets Reek** — Lady Barbrey Dustin stands by the hearth in black. Roose presents Reek as "the rightful Lord of the Iron Islands, Theon of House Greyjoy." Lady Dustin is shocked by his condition and smell. Reek collapses, weeping, insisting he is Reek and not the turncloak.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pervasive fear and anxiety; terror at displeasing Ramsay; dread when Roose reveals his true name; weeping multiple times; panic when offered a bath and clean clothes; deep shame about his body
- **Primary preoccupation:** Surviving Ramsay's cruelty; avoiding punishment; maintaining the Reek identity as psychological armor; terror of having his clothes removed (hiding his mutilated body)
- **Key decisions made:** None — Reek has no agency; he is passed between Ramsay and Roose like property; his only "choice" is to refuse the bath and new clothes
- **Self-deception flags:** Reek has fully absorbed the Reek identity — he genuinely recoils when Roose calls him Theon Greyjoy; he tells himself "I'm not him, I'm not the turncloak, he died at Winterfell"; he does not want to think about "anything that had happened before he knew his name"; he refers to Bran and Rickon's faked deaths obliquely ("we dipped the heads in tar") and immediately suppresses the memory as "too hurtful to remember"
