After Bowen Marsh protests, they ride north and discover nine starving wildlings sheltering in the grove, including a giant. Leathers defuses a tense standoff by speaking the Old Tongue. The six recruits swear their vows before the weirwoods, and Jon offers the wildlings sanctuary at Castle Black. They return with the living wildlings and two corpses Jon intends to use. Back in his solar, Jon reads a letter from Stannis announcing the capture of Deepwood Motte and his march toward Winterfell to confront Roose Bolton.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon surveys the haunted forest from atop the Wall** — After seven days of dark skies, the sun breaks through. Jon sees no sign of life in the forest below.
2. **Jon orders the vow-taking expedition** — Tells Dolorous Edd to have Emmett assemble recruits with an escort of ten rangers armed with dragonglass, ready within the hour.
3. **Jon and Edd descend the Wall** — They ride the winch lift down. Jon muses about building glass gardens at Castle Black, modeled on Winterfell's, using Myrish glass and glassblowers.
4. **Ghost greets Jon at the base of the Wall** — The direwolf is rolling in fresh snow.
5. **Bowen Marsh confronts Jon at the stables** — Marsh protests the expedition, argues recruits can take vows in the sept, warns of the Weeping Man, invokes Black Jack Bulwer and Benjen Stark as cautionary examples. Jon insists on the traditions and promises to return.
6. **Jon leads the party through the tunnel beneath the Wall** — Ghost accompanies them. Beyond the Wall, trees stand tall in snow.
7. **Ghost vanishes into the woods to hunt** — Jon notes "a white wolf in a white wood, silent as a shadow."
8. **The column rides north toward the grove** — Tom Barleycorn scouts ahead; Big Liddle and Luke of Longtown flank the column. Jon discusses Hardin's Tower trouble and castle plans with Iron Emmett.
9. **Jon reveals his plan to open three castles** — Deep Lake, Sable Hall, and the Long Barrow, garrisoned with free folk. Long Barrow to be all women. He assigns Iron Emmett as commander with Dolorous Edd as steward.
10. **Jon and Emmett discuss the Magnar (Sigorn)** — Emmett suggests giving him a castle. Jon says Sigorn blames him for Styr's death and was bred to give orders, not take them. Emmett warns of trouble if the Thenns are ignored.
11. **Jon reflects on problems with wildling integration** — Halleck is truculent; one of his followers cut off a builder's ear. Jon needs to open old forts quickly but Yarwyck's builders are at the Nightfort.
12. **Tom Barleycorn reports wildlings in the grove** — Nine wildlings, no guards, some dead or sleeping, mostly women, one child, one giant, with a fire burning.
13. **Jon orders a tactical approach** — Party dismounts; Rory and Pate stay with horses; the rest form a crescent to close in from three sides.
14. **Jon's party rushes the grove** — They find nine weirwoods in a circle with carved faces, a small dying fire, and huddled wildlings. Only the child reacts initially by wailing.
15. **The giant wakes and threatens violence** — He roars and grabs his maul, slamming it against the ground. Ghost bares his teeth. Jon tries to speak but is interrupted.
16. **Leathers defuses the standoff** — Speaks to the giant in the Old Tongue, tells him they came to pray. The giant drops his maul.
17. **Jon assesses the wildlings** — Two dead, one near death, six remaining: mother and child, two old men, wounded Thenn in battered bronze, frostbitten Hornfoot man. They fled after Stannis broke Mance's host.
18. **The wildlings explain why they didn't go to the Wall** — They heard the crows burned everyone who yielded, including Mance. Jon blames Melisandre internally.
19. **Jon offers the wildlings his protection** — Identifies himself as Lord Commander and son of Eddard Stark. Promises no one will burn.
20. **Six recruits take their Night's Watch vows** — They kneel before the weirwoods and recite the oath. Jon kneels silently and prays for the recruits, for Arya, and for wisdom.
21. **Jon rises them as men of the Night's Watch** — He is first to his feet and pulls Horse up.
22. **The party begins the return journey** — Slow going due to the giant's ponderous pace and the need to carry the injured and dead. No moon, only occasional stars. Jon spots the red wanderer (planet) above.
23. **Jon orders the two corpses brought back** — Iron Emmett suggests chopping and burning them; Jon refuses, says he has a use for them.
24. **They return to Castle Black at almost dawn** — Sentry horn sounds. Big Liddle answers. Dolorous Edd opens the gate and quips about needing butter for the giant. The giant crawls through the tunnel.
25. **Jon assigns the giant to Leathers** — Tells Leathers to feed him, find him warmth, stay with him, prevent provocation.
26. **Jon sends wildlings for care and corpses to ice cells** — Hopes most will recover; Hornfoot man will likely lose both feet.
27. **Jon reads Stannis's letter** — Gold wax seal with stag in flaming heart. Stannis reports: taking of Deepwood Motte; mountain clans (Flint, Norrey, Wull, Liddle) joining him; Alysane Mormont's naval ambush of ironmen; 5,000 strong and growing; Roose Bolton moving to Winterfell to wed Ramsay to "Arya"; Stannis marching to confront him; Arnolf Karstark and Mors Umber joining; "I will save your sister if I can."
28. **Jon reflects on the strategic situation** — Wonders about Crowfood's and Arnolf's strength; half the Umbers under Whoresbane will fight for Bolton; most Umber and Karstark strength went south with Robb. Winterfell is a shell but a strategic advantage. Robert Baratheon would have moved swiftly; Stannis is more deliberate.
29. **Jon reflects on Arya and Mance's mission** — He loosed Mance and six spearwives on the north based on Melisandre's vision of "a grey girl on a dying horse." Dolorous Edd smuggled them from Mole's Town. Jon doubts the wisdom of trusting Mance and Melisandre. Worries Bolton may never have had Arya.
30. **Jon reflects on his childhood hero and current doubts** — As a boy he dreamed of being like Daeron the Young Dragon. Now he is Lord Commander and has only doubts.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined but burdened with doubt. Jon feels the weight of command — he must act decisively while managing political friction from Marsh, wildling integration problems, and the Stannis situation. He experiences moments of satisfaction (reading of Stannis's victory at Deepwood Motte), spiritual solemnity (praying during the vows), affection for his men and for Arya, and deep uncertainty about his decision to send Mance after Arya. The chapter ends with him defined by his doubts.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Managing the wildling integration at Castle Black (housing, garrison assignments, preventing violence); and Arya's fate — whether Bolton truly has her, whether Mance can save her, whether any of it was wise.
- **Key decisions made:** To lead the vow-taking beyond the Wall despite Marsh's objections; to open Deep Lake, Sable Hall, and Long Barrow with free folk garrisons; to assign Iron Emmett to Long Barrow with Edd; to bring the living wildlings and two corpses back to Castle Black; to approach the grove wildlings with restraint rather than violence.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells himself his involvement in the realm's politics is limited ("the Night's Watch was sworn to take no side"), yet he reads Stannis's letter with satisfaction, sent Mance on a mission into the north, and cannot stop himself from thinking strategically about the Winterfell situation. He acknowledges this tension explicitly — "All to save my sister. But the men of the Night's Watch have no sisters." He also echoes Ygritte's "You know nothing" about himself: "I know nothing, Ygritte… and perhaps I never will."
