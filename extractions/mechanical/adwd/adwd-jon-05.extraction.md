## Chapter Metadata

- **chapter_number:** 22

- **location_primary:** Castle Black, Mole's Town
- **location_secondary:** Kingsroad (between Castle Black and Mole's Town), the armory at Castle Black
- **approximate_timeline:** After Stannis has marched south from Castle Black; after Sam and Maester Aemon departed by ship
- **time_markers:** Morning (morning light through shutters); Jon spent half the night searching vaults; last night's supper congealed beside his elbow; the column departs in the morning
- **chapter_summary:** Jon wakes after falling asleep over books in the armory. He joins a wagon column south to Mole's Town to distribute food to the free folk sheltering there. Along the kingsroad, they discover three trees carved with faces by the wildlings. At Mole's Town, food distribution nearly erupts into violence until Jon addresses the free folk, offering arms and food to any who will help defend the Wall. Sixty-three wildlings volunteer, led by Halleck, though the Thenns refuse.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon wakes at his desk** — He fell asleep over books he'd fetched from dusty vaults; his candle has guttered out.
2. **Jon worries about Sam, Aemon, and Gilly** — Cotter Pyke reported wreckage sighted near Skagos coast; Jon fears the worst.
3. **Jon declines to move to King's Tower** — Bowen Marsh urged it; Jon refuses to imply Stannis won't return.
4. **Jon observes Castle Black's listlessness** — The castle feels empty since Stannis marched; structures in disrepair.
5. **Jon visits the training yard** — Iron Emmett trains Hop-Robin; Jon offers encouragement.
6. **Jon declines Ty's escort** — Takes only Longclaw and Ghost.
7. **Wagon column departs Castle Black** — Dolorous Edd has Jon's palfrey ready; Bowen Marsh leads wagons south.
8. **Marsh and Jon argue** — Marsh objects; Jon cuts him off and orders departure.
9. **First carved face discovered** — On a twisted ash tree half a mile south; wildlings carved old gods face.
10. **Second carved face discovered** — On a chestnut tree by an icy stream and plank bridge; a raven sits in the tree.
11. **Jon feeds the raven** — The bird lands on his fist crying "Corn"; Jon refuses it corn.
12. **Third carved face discovered** — On huge oak at Mole's Town perimeter; particularly angry expression.
13. **Wildlings emerge from underground** — Stench accompanies them; they form lines behind wagons wordlessly.
14. **Food distribution begins** — Hairy Hal hands out food; one apple or one onion per person.
15. **Woman argues for extra apple for sick son** — Jon shakes his head; she is refused.
16. **Violence breaks out** — A girl shoves the woman; food scatters; naked steel drawn; bowmen nock arrows.
17. **Rory blows the horn** — Silences the crowd.
18. **Jon addresses the free folk** — Explains food scarcity and the threat of the Others; offers arms and food to those who will fight.
19. **Sigorn threatens Jon** — Says he'd rather kill crows; refuses to join.
20. **Jon counters Sigorn** — Reminds him his father failed and asks who would hold the Wall.
21. **Jon offers terms of service** — Not lifetime vows; obedience required on pain of death; boys and girls twelve and older; all genders welcome.
22. **Volunteers begin stepping forward** — A young girl first, then striplings, a scarred one-eyed man, a tall spearwife, others.
23. **Halleck volunteers** — His joining breaks the dam; his cousins and Harma's people follow.
24. **Sixty-three wildlings join** — Thenns refuse; Sigorn and his followers vanish into tunnels.
25. **Column returns to Castle Black** — Jon tells Marsh he'll train, arm, and split the volunteers among castles; plans to open three more forts.
26. **Marsh objects again** — Worries about women among brothers, loyalty, and Tormund or Weeping Man attacking.

- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to move to King's Tower; proceeds with Mole's Town food mission despite Marsh's objections; offers arms and food to any wildling willing to serve; accepts boys and girls as young as twelve; plans to open three more forts
- **Self-deception flags:** None clearly present; Jon is fairly clear-eyed about the risks and the opposition he faces from Marsh
