- **chapter_summary:** Davos is brought before Lord Wyman Manderly in the Merman's Court to deliver Stannis Baratheon's proposal of alliance. A dozen Freys are present, and Ser Jared Frey tells a false account of the Red Wedding blaming Robb Stark. Davos argues that Tommen is illegitimate and offers vengeance, but Lord Wyman lays out the generous terms already offered by the Lannisters, Boltons, and Freys. Despite young Wylla Manderly's passionate defense of the Stark oath, Lord Wyman publicly denounces Davos as a spy and traitor and orders Ser Marlon to take him to the Wolf's Den for execution.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ser Marlon Manderly escorts Davos** — Ser Marlon comes to Davos's guarded chambers and leads him through the castle to audience with Lord Wyman.
2. **Davos reflects on captivity** — He has been held for approximately eighteen days; Merry Midwife (Casso Mogat's ship) departed after waiting four days, and a fortnight has passed since.
3. **Procession through castle** — Davos is escorted past ancient trophies (banners, shields, swords, ship prow figures) with four guards bearing silver tridents.
4. **Entry to Merman's Court** — Herald announces "Ser Davos of House Seaworth"; Davos observes the elaborately painted hall.
5. **Court composition noted** — Women outnumber men five to one; few males present are very old or very young; septons and holy sisters present; a dozen Freys in blue and silver-grey.
6. **Davos observes Lord Wyman** — Notes his massive size, pallid corpse-like appearance, and apparent exhaustion.
7. **Maester Theomore recites Wyman's titles** — Requests Davos kneel as is customary in Merman's Court.
8. **Davos refuses to kneel** — States he is King's Hand and cannot kneel without diminishing his king; recites his own titles.
9. **Lady Leona mocks Davos** — "An admiral without ships, a hand without fingers, in service to a king without a throne."
10. **Lord Wyman confronts Davos** — Calls him a smuggler and accuses him of past thefts from White Harbor.
11. **Davos shows shortened fingers** — Removes glove, displays left hand as proof he already paid for smuggling.
12. **Davos requests private audience** — Denied; Lord Wyman says he keeps no secrets from kin and friends.
13. **Davos identifies the Freys** — Points out that these Freys were at the Red Wedding where Wendel Manderly died.
14. **Ser Jared Frey tells false Red Wedding account** — Claims Robb Stark transformed into a wolf and killed Jinglebell and others; claims Wendel died protecting Lord Walder.
15. **Lord Wyman weeps** — Says Wendel was always brave and died a hero.
16. **Davos calls Ser Jared a liar** — Names him "Jared of House Frey, I name you liar."
17. **Ser Jared draws sword** — Challenges Davos to defend his words with his body.
18. **Lord Wyman intervenes** — Orders no bloodshed in Merman's Court; Ser Jared sheathes sword but promises a reckoning.
19. **Lady Leona urges dismissal** — Warns that the queen might question their loyalty if they hear Davos.
20. **Davos declares Tommen a usurper** — Asserts he is Hand of Stannis, trueborn king.
21. **Maester Theomore argues succession** — Claims Tommen as Robert's issue; son before brother.
22. **Davos asserts incest claim** — Declares Tommen and Joffrey bastard-born, sired by the Kingslayer.
23. **Freys demand Davos's tongue and head** — Multiple Freys speak against him.
24. **Maester Theomore asks for proof** — Davos thinks of Edric Storm but has no proof to offer; can only cite Stannis's word.
25. **Brown-haired woman dismisses words** — "Words are wind. And men will lie to get their way."
26. **Lady Leona threatens Wolf's Den** — Calls Davos poisonous; White Harbor people are "lawful, loyal."
27. **Ser Marlon questions Stannis's strength** — Asks about northern lords, castles held, troop numbers.
28. **Davos admits limited support** — Only Arnolf Karstark pledged; holds Nightfort, Storm's End, Dragonstone.
29. **Maester Theomore diminishes claims** — Says Storm's End and Dragonstone must soon fall; Nightfort is haunted ruin.
30. **Davos cannot answer troop question** — Knows Stannis has only fifteen hundred men; his silence answers.
31. **Ser Marlon declares Stannis offers only defeat** — "He offers us defeat and death ... mount a horse of air and give battle with a sword of wind."
32. **Lord Wyman presents Lannister terms** — Full pardon, son returned for 3000 dragons ransom, holdings preserved (minus Hornwood), Frey marriage alliance.
33. **Davos offers duty** — "The chance to do your duty."
34. **Lord Wyman responds flatly** — "Duty. I see."
35. **Davos makes vengeance speech** — Invokes his own four dead sons and the Blackwater; offers vengeance for murdered lords, kings, and princes.
36. **Wylla Manderly speaks up** — Supports Stannis if he will avenge Robb; "He was our king!"
37. **Lord Wyman silences Wylla** — Threatens silent sisters.
38. **Wylla refuses Frey marriage** — Declares she won't marry a Frey; "They killed the king."
39. **Lord Wyman threatens again** — Speak vows or join silent sisters.
40. **Wylla invokes ancient promise** — Thousand years before the Conquest, Starks took in Manderlys; oaths sworn in Wolf's Den; "we should always be their men. Stark men!"
41. **Maester Theomore counters** — Says Winterfell has fallen and House Stark is extinguished.
42. **Rhaegar Frey speaks** — Addresses Wylla about loyalty; mentions Arya Stark coming north to marry Ramsay Bolton.
43. **Wylla corrects** — "Ramsay Snow."
44. **Wylla denounces Ramsay** — Says he forced Lady Hornwood to marry him, imprisoned her, made her eat her fingers.
45. **Stocky man confirms Ramsay's reputation** — Says Bolton is cold and cunning but manageable; his bastard is "mad and cruel, a monster."
46. **Rhaegar Frey defames Robb Stark** — Calls him monster, beast, faithless; says he abandoned the north and broke his marriage pact.
47. **Lord Wyman appears to agree** — Calls Robb "a vile dog" with ponderous nod; his expression moments before suggested rage toward Rhaegar.
48. **Rhaegar urges peace with the throne** — War of Five Kings is done; Tommen is their only king.
49. **Lord Wyman says "Wise words, and true"** — Publicly endorses Rhaegar's position.
50. **Wylla protests** — "They weren't." Stamps her foot.
51. **Lady Leona drags Wylla from hall** — Seizes her by the braid; Davos thinks "There went my only friend in this hall."
52. **Older sister apologizes for Wylla** — Calls her willful.
53. **Rhaegar suggests marriage will soften her** — "A firm hand and a quiet word."
54. **Lord Wyman denounces Davos** — Calls him spy, peddler of lies, smuggler come to steal gold and blood.
55. **Lord Wyman orders arrest** — Guards surround Davos with silver tridents.
56. **Davos protests envoy status** — "My lord, I am an envoy."
57. **Lord Wyman orders execution** — Commands Ser Marlon to take Davos to the Wolf's Den, cut off head and hands, and return them before supper with an onion in Davos's teeth.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Desperate, outmatched, determined but overwhelmed; feels "a stab of despair"; rising passion in his vengeance speech; recognition of being friendless in the hall
- **Primary preoccupation:** Winning White Harbor's allegiance for Stannis despite having almost nothing tangible to offer
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to kneel; names Ser Jared a liar publicly; decides to offer "duty" rather than material incentives; makes the emotional vengeance appeal as a last resort
- **Self-deception flags:** Davos knows Stannis has only 1500 men but avoids saying it, relying on silence rather than lying; thinks "His Grace should have sent another man" — doubts his own adequacy while simultaneously performing competently
