# ADWD — Victarion I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 56
- **pov_character:** Victarion Greyjoy
- **pov_chapter_number:** Victarion I
- **first_available:** ADWD Victarion I
- **location_primary:** The sea (waters between Isle of Cedars, Yaros, and Yunkai — en route to Meereen)
- **location_secondary:** Isle of Cedars, Ghozai (ruins visible from sea), Astapori hinterlands, Yaros (straits), waters north of Yunkai
- **approximate_timeline:** During the siege of Meereen; the Iron Fleet is sailing east from Slaver's Bay waters toward Meereen
- **time_markers:** Night (first capture under moonlight, "first dawn light" at end of pursuit); a squall the next day; "the best part of the day" for chase of galleys; multiple nights with Moqorro's fires; "the morrow or the day after" before expected contact with warships; fleet grows from 54 to 61 ships over the course of the chapter
- **chapter_summary:** Victarion Greyjoy leads the Iron Fleet through the waters of Slaver's Bay toward Meereen, capturing multiple ships along the way. The black priest Moqorro proves his value by accurately predicting weather, ship encounters, and the return of lost vessels. Victarion sacrifices seven captive girls by burning them on a ketch to honor both the Drowned God and R'hllor. At chapter's end, Victarion examines the dragon horn Dragonbinder with Moqorro, who reveals its inscriptions and explains that the horn's master — not its blower — commands the dragons.

## Events & Actions
1. **Iron Fleet captures Ghiscari Dawn** — In the narrows between Isle of Cedars and Astapori hinterlands, at night under moonlight. Grief and Iron Victory cut across her course; Sparrowhawk and Fingerdancer sweep behind; Lamentation rakes her oars near the ruins of Ghozai at dawn.
2. **Ghiscari captain interrogated** — Claims war for Meereen won, dragon queen dead, "Hizdak" rules. Moqorro translates from Ghiscari.
3. **Victarion has captain's tongue torn out** — For "lying" about Daenerys being dead.
4. **Ghiscari captain drowned** — Bound and thrown overboard as sacrifice to the Drowned God.
5. **Ship renamed Red God's Wroth** — Fleet numbers 54 again.
6. **Squall hits fleet** — Three ships go missing.
7. **Oarsman scourged** — For saying Moqorro is "calling demons down on us"; whipped until "his back was blood from shoulders to buttocks."
8. **Iron Fleet captures Myrish cog Dove** — In waters north and west of Astapor. Crew kept for ransom as free Myrmen.
9. **Dove renamed Shrike** — Victarion claims the Myrish eye for himself.
10. **Moqorro predicts "two more hares"** — Where coast turns north: "swift ones, with many legs."
11. **Two Ghiscari galleys chased and captured** — Daylong pursuit; Iron Wing, Sparrowhawk, Kraken's Kiss run them down. Brief brutal fights.
12. **Galley crews report bloody flux (pale mare)** — Deaths among Ghiscari legions from disease, not battle. Captains repeat claim Daenerys is dead.
13. **Victarion beheads both galley captains** — With his axe, for the lie about Daenerys.
14. **Galley crews put to death; slaves freed** — Victarion breaks chains himself, tells freed slaves they'll row for the Iron Fleet.
15. **Galleys renamed Ghost and Shade** — "To return and haunt these Yunkishmen."
16. **Three lost ships found at Yaros** — Exactly as Moqorro predicted. Victarion gives him a golden torque.
17. **Victarion decides to sail the straits** — Despite memory of Fair Isle defeat by Stannis. "What would the Crow's Eye do?"
18. **Three more prizes taken near Yaros** — A galleas (taken by Vole and Grief), a trading galley (taken by Kite/Manfryd Merlyn), and a fishing ketch (taken by Seven Skulls and Thrall's Bane).
19. **Fisherman reports black dragon's return** — "She flew away upon her dragon, beyond the Dothraki sea."
20. **Victarion strangles fisherman** — For calling him a fool when he asks about sailing the Dothraki sea. Body thrown overboard.
21. **Fleet moves to deep water** — To avoid detection near Yunkai.
22. **Slaver galley Willing Maiden captured** — North of Yunkai, by Grief, Warrior Wench, and Iron Victory. Holds contain 20 perfumed boys and 80 girls for Lys pleasure houses.
23. **Slavers killed, rowers freed** — Perfumed boys chained and thrown into the sea. Girls divided among captains.
24. **Victarion claims seven girls** — Described by physical features. Ship renamed Slaver's Scream. Fleet numbers 61.
25. **Victarion gives speech to fleet** — Warns of warships ahead; invokes two gods; claims every ironman worth twenty slavers.
26. **Seven girls sacrificed on burning ketch** — Kissed, placed on captured fishing ketch, set afire. "We honor both the gods." Their cries seem to change to "joyous song." A great wind fills the sails.
27. **Victarion examines Dragonbinder with Moqorro** — At night after the sacrifice. Moqorro reads the Valyrian glyphs.
28. **Moqorro reveals horn's secrets** — Three inscriptions: "I am Dragonbinder"; "No mortal man shall sound me and live"; "Blood for fire, fire for blood." The horn's master — not blower — commands the dragons. Must be claimed with blood.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Aggressive confidence bordering on exaltation; religious fervor growing ("two gods are with me now"); sexual appetite linked to martial/religious feeling; underlying insecurity about Euron's manipulation
- **Primary preoccupation:** Reaching Meereen to claim Daenerys as his bride; proving himself greater than his brothers (Balon, Euron, even historical Dagon); mastering the dragon horn
- **Key decisions made:** Renamed Ghiscari Dawn to Red God's Wroth (honoring Moqorro's god); decided to sail through Yaros straits despite Fair Isle memory; sacrificed seven girls to honor both gods; chose to examine and discuss Dragonbinder with Moqorro
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes Daenerys will willingly be his queen ("the most beautiful woman in the world has urgent need of my axe"); frames drowning captives and murdering the fisherman as religious devotion; frames sexual violence as conferring "honorable station" on captive women; hears the girls' dying screams as "joyous song"; asks "what would the Crow's Eye do?" while believing himself independent of Euron; claims to serve two gods without recognizing the theological contradiction; believes freed slaves are honored to row for him
