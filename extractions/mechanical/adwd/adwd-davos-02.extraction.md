# ADWD — Davos II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 16
- **pov_character:** Davos Seaworth
- **pov_chapter_number:** Davos II
- **first_available:** ADWD Davos II
- **location_primary:** White Harbor
- **location_secondary:** Seal Rock, the Lazy Eel, Fishfoot Yard, Castle Stair, New Castle (gates)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Davos's time in Sisterton; arrives at White Harbor on the evening tide
- **time_markers:** "evening tide" at arrival; dusk falls while Davos is in the Lazy Eel; Seal Gate closed for the night; the chapter spans from evening into night
- **chapter_summary:** Davos arrives in White Harbor aboard the cog Merry Midwife, disguised as a common sailor rather than arriving in state with Salladhor Saan's fleet as originally planned. He observes the city's strengthened defenses and a Lannister warship in port. He gathers intelligence in the winesink called the Lazy Eel, learning that Robett Glover has failed to raise men, that Lord Manderly's son Wylis is held hostage, and that Bolton forces are riding south. Despite the hopelessness of his mission, Davos resolves to present himself at the New Castle and climbs Castle Stair, where he sees twenty-three war galleys hidden in the inner harbor.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at White Harbor** — The Merry Midwife enters the outer harbor on the evening tide. Davos observes the city's strengthened defenses from the ship.
2. **Observation of Seal Rock** — Davos notes the formerly abandoned ringfort is now manned with scorpions, spitfires, and crossbowmen; smoke rises from it; the seals are gone.
3. **Observation of city banners** — Davos sees Manderly merman banners everywhere but no Stark direwolf and no Lannister lion, concluding Manderly has not yet declared for Tommen.
4. **Sighting of the Lionstar** — Davos spots a warship with black and gold hull, lion figurehead, and the boy king's banner. He reads the name "Lionstar" and realizes the Freys are already in White Harbor.
5. **Exchange with Casso Mogat** — The captain tells Davos the Merry Midwife will wait three days, no longer.
6. **Davos disembarks unnoticed** — Customs men ignore him; he passes through the fish market and Seal Gate without challenge.
7. **Exploration of Fishfoot Yard** — Davos observes the bustling market square, the refugees in the Old Mint, and buys an apple.
8. **Conversation with apple seller** — Davos learns refugees are fleeing the Bastard of Bolton, that young girls are selling themselves, that boys over five feet are being recruited, and that Manderly's granddaughter (not daughter) is to wed a Frey.
9. **Descent to the Lazy Eel** — Davos goes to the winesink to gather intelligence, orders wine, and waits in a shadowed alcove.
10. **Gathering intelligence at the Eel** — As sailors arrive at dusk, Davos overhears news: Tywin dead, Lysa Arryn murdered, Littlefinger ruling the Vale, Balon Greyjoy dead, Sandor Clegane an outlaw, wars in the Free Cities, slave revolt in the east.
11. **Learning critical military news** — Robett Glover failing to raise men; Ryswells and Dustins burned ironmen's ships on the Fever River; Ramsay Bolton riding south with Hother Umber to attack Moat Cailin with 300 spearmen, 100 archers, plus Hornwood and Cerwyn men.
12. **Learning about Manderly's hostage** — The proprietor reveals Ser Wendel was killed (bones in the Snowy Sept) but Ser Wylis is still a captive, giving the Iron Throne leverage over Lord Wyman.
13. **Dragon discussion** — Sailors debate whether Viserys really died, whether Rhaegar's children survived, and a Braavosi oarsman tells of Daenerys with silver hair, purple eyes, and three dragons in Qarth.
14. **Davos's resolution** — After leaving the Eel, finding the Seal Gate closed, Davos resolves not to leave without completing his mission despite the hopelessness.
15. **Climb up Castle Stair** — Davos ascends to the New Castle, pausing to look back and count twenty-three war galleys hidden in the inner harbor behind the jetty wall.
16. **Presenting himself at the New Castle** — Davos shows the guard his black and gold ribbon with royal seals and requests to see Lord Manderly alone.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious and increasingly despairing as intelligence paints a bleak picture; guilty about dragging White Harbor back into war; nostalgic about his smuggling days and fond memories of the city; resolved at chapter's end despite hopelessness
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether Lord Manderly will side with Stannis or with the Boltons/Freys; the hostage situation with Ser Wylis makes things worse
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to disguise himself as a common sailor rather than announce his status; decides to gather intelligence before presenting himself; resolves to climb to the New Castle despite everything he's learned; presents himself with royal credentials
- **Self-deception flags:** Davos repeatedly tells himself he might still return ("If things go well, I could be back by the morrow") while knowing the situation is nearly hopeless; he daydreams about taking his sons to see dragons "one day when the war is done" — an escapist fantasy he seems aware is unlikely
