- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys is woken before dawn to view the corpse of Stalwart Shield, an Unsullied murdered by the Sons of the Harpy — the first of her soldiers they have killed. She orders increased security and a bounty raised to one thousand honors. After dressing in a Ghiscari tokar and visiting Viserion on the terrace, she holds court all day, hearing petitioners including the Astapori envoy Lord Ghael (urging war on Yunkai), Hizdahr zo Loraq (requesting the fighting pits be reopened, denied for the sixth time), and Grazdan zo Galare (seeking payment from freedwomen weavers). The chapter ends when a man presents Daenerys with the charred, broken bones of a child killed by Drogon.

## Events & Actions
1. **Stalwart Shield's corpse presented** — Grey Worm and four Unsullied bring the body of Stalwart Shield to Daenerys in her hall before dawn. His cheeks are slashed ear to ear, with goat genitals forced down his throat (removed by Grey Worm). He was swarmed by six or more attackers.
2. **Dany closes the dead man's eyes** — She kneels and touches the corpse despite Dothraki taboo, orders him buried with cap, shield, and spears.
3. **Dany issues orders on the Harpy investigation** — She commands men be sent to the Temple of the Graces to check for sword wounds, offers gold for Stalwart Shield's stolen short sword, orders inquiry into who has been gelding goats, and decrees no man of hers walks alone after dark.
4. **Barristan counsels on Unsullied limitations** — He argues the Unsullied are soldiers, not investigators, ill-suited for finding covert killers. He notes his knight trainees will take years.
5. **Dany visits Viserion on the terrace** — At dawn, she finds the white dragon coiled around a pear tree. She scratches his jaw, his scales are hot. He knocks down a pear, unfolds wings, and flies away over the Skahazadhan.
6. **Dany dresses in tokar and crown** — With Irri and Jhiqui's help, she dons the Ghiscari garment on her third attempt and puts on her heavy dragon crown.
7. **Reznak and Skahaz advise on the murders** — Skahaz urges taking hostages from great families (Zhak, Hazkar, Ghazeen, Merreq, Loraq, Pahl) and killing them. Reznak opposes. Dany raises the bounty to one thousand honors.
8. **Lord Ghael presents Cleon's gift and proposal** — The Astapori envoy gives gilded slippers and proposes Astapor and Meereen jointly attack Yunkai. Dany refuses, telling him Astapor should feed its people first.
9. **Hizdahr zo Loraq petitions to reopen fighting pits** — His sixth request. Dany recites his own arguments back at him, then refuses again. She internally considers him as a potential husband.
10. **Grazdan zo Galare seeks compensation from freed weavers** — Dany rules he gets nothing from the girls but must buy them a new loom for forgetting his slave's name.
11. **Various petitioners heard** — A rich widow seeks her jewels back (granted) but not her house (denied); a freedman seeks gelding of a noble who raped his wife when she was a slave (gold granted, gelding denied); a scarred boy seeks execution of his family's murderers (denied under blanket pardon).
12. **Scarred boy rushes at Dany** — He stumbles on his tokar; Strong Belwas catches and restrains him. Dany releases him with a warning.
13. **Dragon claims heard** — Twenty-three claimants for livestock killed by dragons. Dany orders payment but requires future claimants to swear holy oaths at the Temple of the Graces.
14. **Man presents child's bones** — The final petitioner brings charred, broken bones cracked for marrow. He says "the black one" (Drogon) came from the sky. Ser Barristan identifies them as a child's bones, not sheep.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determination layered over grief and dread. "The blood of the dragon does not weep" — she forces composure. Growing despair at the intractability of ruling Meereen. Horror at the final revelation of the child's bones.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Security (the Sons of the Harpy growing bolder); governance (holding the city together economically and politically); identity (wearing the tokar, performing Meereenese queenship while feeling alien)
- **Key decisions made:** Raises bounty to 1,000 honors; decrees no man walks alone after dark; refuses fighting pits again; rules on weaver case; denies blanket pardon exceptions; orders dragon claims paid with oath requirement
- **Self-deception flags:** "I am only a young girl and know little of the ways of war" — used as a diplomatic mask, not genuine. She tells herself the 163 crucifixions were justified but fears she "had not gone far enough" — internal contradiction about violence as governance tool.
