# ADWD — Bran I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 5
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran I
- **first_available:** ADWD Bran I
- **location_primary:** Beyond the Wall — forest and frozen lake
- **location_secondary:** Abandoned wildling village (longhall by the lake), Queenscrown (mentioned), Winterfell (mentioned), the Wall (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** Early in ADWD; the party has been traveling beyond the Wall for an extended period after leaving the Wall
- **time_markers:** Days of travel uncounted due to snowfall; "ten days" since the last of their food from the south ran out; "three days" since Meera last caught a fish; "four days ago" they crossed the same river they crossed this morning; dark comes early this far north; dusk falls during the chapter (shadows stealing between trees); first sliver of crescent moon appears when they find the village; "dawn is close" when Coldhands returns near the end
- **chapter_summary:** Bran and his companions (Hodor, Meera, Jojen, and the ranger Coldhands) trudge through the frozen wilderness beyond the Wall seeking the three-eyed crow. Coldhands detects human pursuers and departs to deal with them, directing the group to a fishing village by a frozen lake. Summer leads them to the abandoned village. That night, Bran wargs into Summer and discovers the direwolf has found dead Night's Watch men — killed by Coldhands and his ravens — and takes over a small wolf pack. Bran wakes to find Coldhands has returned with a slaughtered pig, and confronts him about being dead and killing his own brothers of the Night's Watch. Coldhands reveals the three-eyed crow is "the last greenseer," and Jojen insists they must continue or die.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Heavy, wet snowfall throughout; snow has been falling for so long Bran has lost count of the days; snowdrifts taller than Bran in places; wind gusting and shifting
- **Season indicators:** Days growing shorter ("each day seemed shorter than the last"); nights described as "bitter cruel"; extreme sustained cold consistent with deep winter
- **Time of day:** Transitions from day to dusk ("shadows began to steal between the trees, the long fingers of the dusk") to night (crescent moon appears) to near-dawn ("dawn is close")
- **Lighting:** Dim daylight fading to darkness; moonlight on snow during Summer's wolf-run; firelight inside the longhall at chapter's end; "pools of moonlight" in the forest
- **Sounds:** Stillness/silence dominates the forest; raven wings slapping cold air; ravens quorking; wolves howling in the distance at night; wind howling across the lake; Hodor's "hodors"; "murderer's song" of ravens screaming "yours, yours, yours"; stream under ice; elk cracking ice with hoof
- **Smells:** Summer's enhanced senses: wet leaves, dead grass, rotted squirrel carcass, sour man-sweat, musky elk odor; burnt wood/soot/charcoal (dead fire in village); blood, piss, dead skins, bird shit, feathers, wolf-smell (at the kill site); roasting meat (at chapter's end); Coldhands smells of dead meat, dry blood, faint rot, and cold
- **Notable sensory details:** Extreme cold — Bran's face gone numb, Jojen's lips blue, Meera's cheeks dark red, Hodor's beard solid ice; hoarfrost on Hodor's beard with icicles; one of Hodor's eyes frozen shut; frozen snot below Jojen's nose; cold so severe that breath is visible as mist; the warmth of fire described as "comforting as a mother's kisses"; taste of bitter acorn paste; bile in Bran's throat when he realizes Coldhands is dead

## Events & Actions
1. **Ongoing travel through frozen wilderness** — Bran's company trudges through the forest beyond the Wall, led by Coldhands on the elk, with Summer limping behind them
2. **Bran wargs into Summer and Hodor** — Bran regularly slips into Summer's skin to see/hear/smell better, and sometimes into Hodor (who whimpers and thrashes but less violently than before)
3. **Coldhands detects pursuers** — The elk stops; Coldhands announces men (not wolves) are following them; he identifies them as "foes"
4. **Coldhands departs to deal with pursuers** — He instructs the group to find a frozen lake, turn north, and take refuge in a fishing village; leaves with four ravens
5. **Meera expresses distrust of Coldhands** — She notes they've been going in circles, that Coldhands hides his face, does not eat or drink, and does not feel cold
6. **Bran and Meera discuss Coldhands' nature** — Bran observes the scarf never gets icy and they've never seen his breath; Meera confirms this
7. **Travel along the frozen lake** — The elk leads them across ice and along the shoreline; dusk falls; temperatures drop further
8. **Bran wargs Summer to find the village** — He slips into Summer's skin; the direwolf races across the lake and locates the village by scent (old ashes)
9. **Discovery of the abandoned wildling village** — Under a crescent moon, they find round stone houses and a longhall buried in snow; village was abandoned but not burned
10. **Shelter in the longhall** — They settle inside; Meera fetches ice to melt for water; Bran sends Summer to hunt but warns him to leave the elk alone
11. **Meagre supper of acorn paste** — Bran gags on it; Jojen refuses to eat, says his belly hurts; claims he is dreaming of roast chicken
12. **Bran wargs Summer during the night** — Summer finds a kill site: dead men in black cloaks (Night's Watch), faces torn, eyes gone, killed by someone (Coldhands and ravens); five or more dead men
13. **Summer fights for dominance of a wolf pack** — He defeats the one-eyed pack leader, who submits; the female and tail wolf also submit; Summer becomes pack leader
14. **Summer feeds on the dead Night's Watch men** — He eats the biggest one (faceless, clutching black iron, missing one hand with stump bound in leather, throat slashed); feeds on others; pack eats his leavings
15. **Bran recalls the other direwolves** — While inside Summer, he thinks of Lady (dead), Grey Wind (maybe dead), Shaggydog, Nymeria, and Ghost still somewhere
16. **Bran wakes to fire and food** — Coldhands has returned with a slaughtered pig (sow); Meera is cooking meat; everyone eats
17. **Bran confronts Coldhands** — He accuses the ranger of killing the Night's Watch men (their faces torn, eyes gone); identifies them by their black cloaks; asks who/what Coldhands is
18. **Coldhands explains his black hands** — He describes livor mortis: when the heart stops, blood pools in extremities and turns them black
19. **Bran declares Coldhands is dead** — He connects the evidence: no breath, does not eat, could not pass the Wall; recalls Old Nan's tales about monsters being unable to cross the Wall
20. **Meera demands answers about the three-eyed crow** — Coldhands reveals it is "a friend. Dreamer, wizard... The last greenseer"
21. **The ravens echo "yours"** — After Coldhands calls himself "your monster, Brandon Stark," the raven on his arm and the ravens outside chorus "yours, yours, yours"
22. **Jojen insists they continue** — He says they've come too far to turn back and would never make it to the Wall alive; they go with "Bran's monster" or die

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear (of Coldhands' nature, of the pursuers, of the darkness), hunger (constant, gnawing), cold discomfort, but also a strange comfort in warging Summer; revulsion when he tastes bile realizing Coldhands is dead; longing for his lost wolf-siblings
- **Primary preoccupation:** "Are we there yet?" — wanting to reach the three-eyed crow; growing awareness that Coldhands is not alive; hunger
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to warg Summer to find the village (acts without waiting for Meera's response); chooses to confront Coldhands about killing the Night's Watch men and about being dead
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself Hodor "knows it's me" and "is used to me by now" when warging into him, despite tasting Hodor's fear; the boy whispers "we have another pack" to Summer but the situation suggests increasing distance from his human identity
