# ADWD — Jon IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 18

- **location_secondary:** The wormways (tunnels beneath Castle Black), King's Tower
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Stannis's defeat of the wildlings at the Wall; autumn, before Stannis marches south
- **time_markers:** Shadows of the afternoon growing long when they return to the surface; "three turns ago" (referring to prior inventory counts); "two nights past" (Dywen's supper comment); "limes were gone a year ago"; "thirty years ago" (Crowfood's daughter taken); "fifty years ago in Oldtown" (Whoresbane's incident); "Centuries ago" (Harlon Stark's siege of the Dreadfort — took two years)
- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow inspects Castle Black's food stores with Bowen Marsh, Dolorous Edd, and Wick Whittlestick, learning that supplies are dangerously strained by the influx of king's men, queen's men, and wildlings. He then attends King Stannis in the King's Tower, where Stannis gifts him Rattleshirt (bound by Melisandre's ruby), discusses northern politics regarding the Umbers and Karstarks, reveals his plan to march on the Dreadfort, and again offers to legitimize Jon as Lord of Winterfell. Jon refuses the offer but persuades Stannis to instead march to Deepwood Motte via the mountain clans, trading three hundred wildling fighters for the promise of two to three thousand clansmen.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon inspects underground stores** — Dolorous Edd leads Jon through the wormways to meet Bowen Marsh and Wick Whittlestick for a supply inventory.
2. **Supply count through multiple storerooms** — They move through granaries, root cellars, cheese rooms, salt meat stores, smokehouse, spice locker, and preserved food vaults, with Wick marking counts and Marsh comparing to records from three turns ago.
3. **Inspection of cold storage inside the Wall** — They pass through a rusty iron door and up wooden steps into a tunnel-shaped chamber inside the Wall itself, where frozen carcasses hang from iron hooks.
4. **Marsh delivers grim supply assessment** — The Lord Steward warns that stores meant for three to four years of winter are being depleted by king's men, queen's men, and wildlings; recommends winter rations immediately.
5. **Jon orders rations cut by a quarter** — Jon agrees to reduce each man's food portion by twenty-five percent.
6. **Devan summons Jon to Stannis** — Emerging in the afternoon, Jon finds Stannis's squire Devan in the armory, frightened by Ghost; Ghost bares teeth but Jon commands him away.
7. **Jon enters the King's Tower** — Jon is stripped of weapons and admitted to Stannis's crowded solar where captains study a map of the north.
8. **Stannis gifts Rattleshirt to Jon** — Stannis announces Rattleshirt as a gift; Melisandre explains the ruby binding; Rattleshirt offers to range, counsel, or fight but won't wear the black cloak.
9. **Discussion of Mors "Crowfood" Umber** — Jon describes Crowfood's history; Stannis reveals Mors demands Mance's skull and a pardon for Whoresbane; Mors won't fight Umber against Umber.
10. **Discussion of Bolton allies** — Melisandre describes banners she saw in her fires (Hornwood, Cerwyn, Tallhart, Ryswell, Dustin); Jon explains their situations.
11. **Stannis reveals plan to march on the Dreadfort** — Stannis plans to strike while Ramsay goes south toward Moat Cailin; believes he can take it unawares.
12. **Jon argues against the Dreadfort attack** — Jon explains the dangers: Umber lands to cross, beacon fires alerting the castle, Ramsay cutting off retreat, Bolton forces outnumbering Stannis five to one once reunited.
13. **Stannis dismisses all but Jon and Melisandre** — The king orders everyone out for private conversation.
14. **Stannis offers Jon legitimization as Lord of Winterfell** — Stannis offers to make him Jon Stark, Lord of Winterfell and Warden of the North; Jon refuses, saying his sword is sworn to the Night's Watch.
15. **Stannis reveals plan to give Winterfell to Arnolf Karstark** — Jon objects, noting Karstarks abandoned Robb.
16. **Jon trades wildlings for strategic advice** — Jon offers to tell Stannis where to find two to three thousand men in exchange for keeping all the wildlings himself; Stannis agrees.
17. **Jon reveals the mountain clans** — Jon describes the northern mountain clans west of the kingsroad and advises Stannis to visit them personally, eat their food, and honor them to win their swords.
18. **Jon suggests Deepwood Motte as target** — Jon redirects Stannis from the Dreadfort to Deepwood Motte, a wooden castle in thick forest vulnerable to surprise attack via mountain paths.
19. **Stannis agrees to the plan** — Stannis accepts, noting he beat ironmen at sea before and can beat them on land; Jon reflects he'll have a thousand wildlings with no way to feed them.
