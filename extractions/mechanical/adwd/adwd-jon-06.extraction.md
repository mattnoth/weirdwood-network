- **chapter_summary:** Jon sends nine rangers out in three parties of three to range the haunted forest, including Ser Alliser Thorne. A letter arrives from Ramsay Bolton announcing the fall of Moat Cailin and his upcoming marriage to Arya Stark, which deeply distresses Jon. That night, Melisandre approaches Jon and tells him she has seen his sister in her fires — a girl in grey on a dying horse, fleeing toward the Wall — and offers to help save her if Jon will take her hand.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon orders Ser Alliser Thorne to range** — Thorne bitterly accepts, implying Jon wants him dead; threatens to come back as a wight if killed by Others
2. **Jon watches nine rangers depart** — Three parties of three (led by Dywen, Black Jack Bulwer, and Kedge Whiteye), each with two ravens; Jon watches from atop the Wall
3. **Jon and Dolorous Edd descend the Wall** — Ride the winch cage down; scattered snowflakes falling; Edd jokes about a dream where their roles were reversed
4. **Reports from Cotter Pyke and Ser Denys Mallister** — Increasing free folk activity along northern shores; fires north of the Gorge; both commanders requesting more men; both complaining about the wildling replacements Jon sent
5. **Jon spars with three recruits** — Takes on Arron, Emrick, and Jace simultaneously in the yard; knocks Jace down first, teaches them about fighting; demonstrates combat skill
6. **Rattleshirt challenges Jon** — The wildling taunts Jon from the wall of the yard; Jon orders him armored
7. **Rattleshirt defeats Jon** — The wildling fights with unexpected speed and power; destroys Jon's shield; the fight ends on the ground with Rattleshirt smashing Jon's head and threatening to take his eye; Horse and Iron Emmett pull him off
8. **Jon acknowledges the fight** — Spits blood, says "well fought"; tells Emmett to release Rattleshirt despite the threat; recalls Ser Rodrik's teaching
9. **Clydas delivers Bolton letter** — Sealed with pink wax from the Dreadfort; signed by Ramsay Bolton, Lord of the Hornwood, with marks from Lord Dustin, Lady Cerwyn, four Ryswells, and House Umber
10. **Jon reads the letter** — Moat Cailin taken; ironmen flayed and nailed to posts along kingsroad; Roose Bolton summons lords to Barrowton; Ramsay to marry Arya Stark
11. **Jon's emotional crisis over Arya** — Shocked; remembers her appearance; publicly denies having a sister ("I have no sister. Only brothers. Only you"); privately anguished
12. **Jon reflects at nightfall** — Bruises from fight; hears Melisandre's nightfire; thinks about Arya and his vows; remembers Needle
13. **Jon goes outside at night** — Passes Mully and Kegs at the doors; Ghost accompanies him; feels Castle Black as dark, silent, ruinous
14. **Jon experiences Ghost's heightened senses** — Night comes alive with a thousand smells; hears crust breaking on old snow; senses someone warm behind him
15. **Jon mistakes Melisandre for Ygritte** — Sees red hair in moonlight, heart leaps; realizes it is Melisandre
16. **Melisandre approaches Jon** — Tells him not to despair; reveals she knows about Arya; says she has seen "a girl in grey on a dying horse" fleeing toward the Wall in her fires
17. **Ghost approaches Melisandre willingly** — The direwolf sniffs her, shoves his nose against her fingers, lets her scratch behind his ear, licks her face; Jon is disturbed by Ghost's unusual friendliness
18. **Melisandre offers Jon power** — Speaks of power in him and in Ghost; suggests sexual union to create power; speaks of casting shadows
19. **Melisandre prophesies about the rangers** — Three of the nine crows will return eyeless and dead; empty sockets weeping blood; she has seen their pale dead faces in her flames
20. **Melisandre offers to save Arya** — Tells Jon the cost of disbelief will be three lives; urges him to take her hand when the dead rangers return

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Conflicted, burdened, and anguished. Feels the weight of sending men to likely death. Experiences a surge of guilt and grief upon learning of Arya's betrothal to Ramsay Bolton. By night, feels stiff, dark, and guilt-ridden. Momentarily hopeful/startled when he mistakes Melisandre for Ygritte. Wary and distrustful of Melisandre's offer despite feeling tempted.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Arya's fate — the news of her forced marriage to Ramsay Bolton dominates his thoughts from the letter onward. He remembers her appearance, the sword Needle, and struggles between his vows and his love for her. Secondary: the danger of the rangings, managing Castle Black's manpower shortages.
- **Key decisions made:** Sends Thorne and eight others to range (decision already made, executed here); chooses to spar three-on-one and then fight Rattleshirt; publicly denies having a sister to maintain his vow; refuses to answer the Bolton letter; rejects Melisandre's offer of alliance despite temptation.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself the rangers are "all seasoned men" while knowing this didn't save Benjen; tells himself "there is no way I can help her" about Arya while clearly wanting to; denies having a sister while his internal monologue is consumed with her; tells himself Rattleshirt's apparent strength is "the armor, not the man."
