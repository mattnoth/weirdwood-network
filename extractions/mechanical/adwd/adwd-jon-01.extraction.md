- **approximate_timeline:** Early in ADWD; shortly after Jon's election as Lord Commander and Stannis's arrival at the Wall
- **time_markers:** Dawn/early morning ("Grey light leaked through the shutters, promising another bleak cold day"), pale dawn light in the yard, morning meeting with Stannis, "not long ago" Jon was fetching breakfast for Mormont, Nightfort will take "half a year" to restore, "before the year is out" (Stannis's deadline for garrisoning castles), "Two more wildlings turned up" the previous night, guards placed on guards recently, builders work "dawn to dusk" on new stair
- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow wakes from a wolf dream in which he senses his surviving packmates — Nymeria leading a pack of wolves, and Summer beyond the Wall. After morning reports from Dolorous Edd, Jon proceeds to the King's Tower for a contentious meeting with Stannis Baratheon. They argue over Lyanna Mormont's defiant letter, the need for gold and northern allies, Val's marriage prospects, Mance Rayder's fate, and Jon's refusal to sign a grant ceding Night's Watch castles to Stannis's lords. Jon proposes that Stannis's men garrison the forts under Night's Watch officers; Stannis refuses but gives Jon a year's deadline. Afterward, Lady Melisandre walks with Jon and delivers a cryptic warning about daggers in the dark and enemies who smile to his face.

## Events & Actions
1. **Wolf dream** — Jon (as Ghost) runs beneath the Wall in moonlight, senses packmates: Nymeria's brother (likely the "black brother") tearing at a goat in rain; Nymeria singing to moon with a hundred grey cousins; Summer beyond the Wall. Ghost senses four remain of the original six, and one he can no longer sense.
2. **Raven wakes Jon** — Mormont's raven lands on Jon's chest screaming "Snow" repeatedly until Jon wakes.
3. **Dolorous Edd reports** — No trouble from stockades since guards put on guards; two more wildlings surrendered (mother with daughter and dead babe); mother questioned and sent to pens; dead babe burned.
4. **Jon reflects on queen's men's words** — Recalls hearing "Two kings to wake the dragon. The father first and then the son, so both die kings" while Maester Aemon cleaned his wounds; Aemon warned about power in king's blood.
5. **Jon reflects on wolf dreams and dead brothers** — Ghost knows Grey Wind is dead; Robb died at the Twins; Bran and Rickon "murdered" by Theon; but their wolves may have escaped; Summer saved Jon at Queenscrown.
6. **Jon dresses and opens window** — Washes, dons black woolens and leather jerkin; raven flies out the window; Jon notes the raven is too clever and ate Mormont's face.
7. **Jon considers the grant** — Reads the parchment again in his lower room; fears being remembered as "the lord commander who gave away the Wall."
8. **Jon notes Stannis's restlessness** — King has ridden to Queenscrown, prowled Mole's Town, inspected Queensgate and Oakenshield, walks the Wall nightly with Melisandre, visits stockades picking captives for the red woman.
9. **Jon crosses the yard** — Watches Horse defeat Hop-Robin in sparring; advises Horse about dropping his shield.
10. **Ser Godry Farring challenges Jon** — Calls him "Boy," taunts him about Longclaw, offers to spar; Jon declines; Godry mocks him for fear.
11. **Jon surveys Castle Black's ruined state** — Lord Commander's Tower a shell, Common Hall blackened, Hardin's Tower tottering, Wall under reconstruction with new switchback stair.
12. **Jon advises guards on gloves** — Tells shivering queen's men on King's Tower steps to see Bowen Marsh for fur-lined leather gloves.
13. **Jon encounters Sam on stairs** — Sam delivered a letter from Maester Aemon; Stannis took it "not happily"; Sam cannot speak of contents. Jon asks about Sam's archery practice.
14. **Jon surrenders weapons at solar door** — Guards require all arms removed before entering Stannis's presence.
15. **Stannis confronts Jon about Lyanna Mormont's letter** — Reads it aloud: "Bear Island knows no king but the King in the North, whose name is STARK." Orders Jon to keep it secret.
16. **Discussion of northern homage** — Stannis complains only Arnolf Karstark has responded; Jon explains northern lords fear Tywin Lannister and Boltons, see Stannis as "another doomed pretender."
17. **Discussion of gold and White Harbor** — Stannis needs gold for Salladhor Saan; Jon recommends White Harbor and Lord Manderly; Stannis dismisses him as "Lord Too-Fat-to-Sit-a-Horse."
18. **Discussion of Val's marriage** — Melisandre suggests marrying Val to Manderly; Jon explains Val would slit an unwilling husband's throat and wildlings won't follow a lord just because he married her.
19. **Jon advocates for Mance Rayder** — Says only Mance can bind the wildlings; Stannis refuses, says law demands Mance die for desertion and he'll burn him.
20. **Discussion of Mance's son** — Stannis claims the babe will be King-Beyond-the-Wall after Mance dies; Jon corrects him — wildling kingship isn't hereditary.
21. **Jon refuses to sign the castle grant** — Says "You ask too much"; argues the castles belong to the Night's Watch, built with the blood and bones of his brothers.
22. **Jon proposes compromise** — Asks Stannis for men to garrison the forts under Night's Watch officers; Stannis refuses, says his knights won't serve under poachers and murderers.
23. **Jon invokes Stannis's Hand (Davos)** — "Your own Hand is a smuggler"; Stannis replies "Was a smuggler. I shortened his fingers."
24. **Stannis threatens Jon** — Mentions 999th Lord Commander might be more helpful with Jon's head on a spike; says Jon is Lord Commander "by my sufferance."
25. **Jon asserts his legitimacy** — "I am lord commander because my brothers chose me"; invokes his father calling Stannis just.
26. **Stannis issues ultimatum** — Jon may keep the castles for now, but any empty by year's end will be taken; if one falls to the enemy, Jon's head follows. Dismisses Jon.
27. **Melisandre accompanies Jon** — Walks arm in arm with him down the stairs and into the yard, drawing curious looks.
28. **Melisandre's warning** — Tells Jon she has seen him in the flames; warns of enemies who smile to his face and sharpen knives behind his back; says to keep his wolf close; sees "Ice, and daggers in the dark. Blood frozen red and hard, and naked steel. It was very cold."
29. **Melisandre echoes Ygritte** — "Then you know nothing, Jon Snow."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Wary, burdened, resolute but uncertain. Jon feels the strangeness of command ("still felt strange"), reflects ruefully on Castle Black being "as much a ruin as it is a stronghold." He is haunted by wolf dreams growing stronger, conflicted about burning children, determined to protect the Night's Watch's independence despite threats.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The tension between needing Stannis (who saved the Wall) and refusing to yield Night's Watch autonomy; the threat to Mance's son and Dalla's baby; the growing wolf dreams and what they mean.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to sign the castle grant; proposes Stannis's men serve under NW officers (rejected); decides to send Gilly south; refuses Ser Godry's challenge; declines Melisandre's offered friendship (slips arm free).
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon wonders "if the girl's answer might have been different if the letter had been sealed with a direwolf instead of a crowned stag, and signed by Jon Stark, Lord of Winterfell" — then immediately tells himself "It is too late for such misgivings. You made your choice." He also tells himself Gilly's son is merely larger and more robust to justify the baby swap, framing a deception as practical necessity.
