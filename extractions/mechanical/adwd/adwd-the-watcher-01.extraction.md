# ADWD — The Watcher I

## Chapter Metadata

- **chapter_number:** 39
- **pov_character:** Areo Hotah

- **first_available:** ADWD The Watcher I
- **location_primary:** Old Palace of Sunspear (feast hall)
- **location_secondary:** Prince Doran's solar, Water Gardens (discussed), King's Landing (discussed), High Hermitage (discussed), Lys (discussed)

- **chapter_summary:** Ser Balon Swann of the Kingsguard delivers Gregor Clegane's skull to Prince Doran Martell at a feast in Sunspear's Old Palace. After the feast, Doran reveals to the Sand Snakes and Arianne that Cersei's invitation is a ruse to murder Trystane on the road. He assigns tasks: Obara will lead Ser Balon to hunt Darkstar, Nymeria will escort Myrcella to King's Landing and serve as Dorne's voice at court, and Tyene will infiltrate the Faith. Doran also shares intelligence about a Volantene fleet at Lys carrying elephants, possibly connected to Quentyn and Daenerys.

## Events & Actions
1. **Skull delivery** — Ser Balon Swann presents an ebony chest; Maester Caleotte carries it to the dais; it contains Gregor Clegane's skull.
2. **Skull displayed** — Caleotte cannot reach the black marble pedestal; Obara Sand takes the skull and places it atop the column.
3. **Doran's pronouncement** — Prince Doran declares "The Mountain rides no more" with tears in his eyes.
4. **Discussion of Gregor's death** — Tyene asks about the manner of death; Balon confirms he screamed for days; Nym and Balon debate poison vs. sword as death methods.
5. **Doran's speech on justice** — The prince declares this is the justice Dorne hungered for and that the Lannisters have paid their blood debt.
6. **Toast to Tommen** — Ricasso proposes a toast; Hotah carefully notes who drinks and who does not.
7. **Obara's defiance** — Obara fills her cup then upends it on the floor, then leaves the hall; Arianne follows her.
8. **Seven-course feast** — Elaborate meal served; Ser Balon eats little and struggles with the spicy food.
9. **Arianne flirts with Ser Balon** — She touches his hand, his brooch; discusses swans and peacocks; he is wary and not easily seduced.
10. **Doran addresses Cersei's letter** — Near midnight, Doran discusses the invitation to send Trystane to King's Landing and himself to take Dorne's council seat; suggests going by sea (Balon balks).
11. **Private withdrawal** — Obara rolls Doran to his solar, accompanied by Sand Snakes, Arianne, Ellaria, Hotah, and Caleotte.
12. **Sand Snakes question the skull's authenticity** — Obara asks how they know it's really Gregor; Nym argues Cersei would be a fool to deceive them; Tyene vouches for her father's poison.
13. **Ellaria's plea for peace** — She argues the cycle of vengeance must end; names the dead (Tywin, Robert, Amory Lorch, Gregor, Joffrey); asks where it ends; invokes her four younger daughters.
14. **Nym and Obara argue for war** — Nym wants Casterly Rock destroyed; Obara declares the time is ripe given their enemies' disarray.
15. **Ellaria departs** — Doran sends her away gently; she kisses him and leaves.
16. **Doran demands an oath** — He asks the Sand Snakes to swear on their father's grave to serve and obey him; all three swear.
17. **Doran reveals Cersei's plot** — He tells them Trystane is meant to be killed in the kingswood by "outlaws" shouting "Halfman"; he is invited only to witness.
18. **Doran assigns tasks** — Obara to lead Balon to hunt Darkstar; Nymeria to escort Myrcella to King's Landing as Dorne's voice at court; Tyene to infiltrate the reformed Faith and get close to the new High Septon.
19. **The cover story confirmed** — Arianne states that Darkstar killed Ser Arys and attacked Myrcella; Myrcella will tell this to Ser Balon.
20. **Sand Snakes accept and depart** — Arianne kisses each cousin; they say "Unbowed, unbent, unbroken" together.
21. **Arianne's private conversation with Doran** — She wants to go to King's Landing instead of Nym; Doran refuses, says she is his heir. He reveals intelligence about a Volantene fleet at Lys with elephants, possibly carrying Quentyn.
22. **Hotah puts Doran to bed** — Doran asks if no Dornishman dying in the War of the Five Kings until Oberyn is his shame or his glory.
