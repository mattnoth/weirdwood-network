- **chapter_summary:** Jaime rides to Raventree Hall to end the siege by Jonos Bracken against Tytos Blackwood. He first meets with Bracken, then enters the castle and negotiates surrender terms with Lord Blackwood, taking his son Hoster as hostage. On departure, Jaime informs Bracken of the lands granted and demands one of Bracken's daughters as a hostage for court. The column camps at Pennytree village, where Brienne of Tarth arrives near midnight claiming to have found "the girl" but warning that the Hound will kill her if Jaime does not come alone.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jaime's column arrives at Blackwood Vale** — Jaime rides through the devastated landscape toward Raventree Hall, observing the destruction wrought during the war.
2. **Jaime observes the siege** — Notes Bracken's ~500 men, no siege engines, a starvation siege half a year old, boredom having taken over.
3. **Jaime enters Bracken's tent unannounced** — Interrupts Jonos Bracken having sex with Hildy, a "prize of war."
4. **Jaime and Bracken discuss terms** — Bracken suggests Blackwood's daughter as hostage; produces a map of lands promised by Tywin; Jaime keeps the map.
5. **Jaime rides to Raventree with peace banner** — Peck carries the banner; drawbridge lowers before Jaime needs to sound the Horn of Herrock.
6. **Jaime meets Lord Tytos Blackwood in outer ward** — Blackwood agrees to yield to the king, not to Bracken.
7. **Negotiations in Blackwood's solar** — They agree on confession, fealty, pardon, gold/silver payment; negotiate which lands go to Bracken (Woodhedge, Crossbow Ridge, Buckle, Lord's Mill, Honeytree).
8. **Hostage negotiation** — Jaime initially suggests Bethany (daughter, 8); Lord Tytos offers sons instead; they settle on Hoster.
9. **Blackwood reveals family losses** — Robert died nine days ago of dysentery; Lucas murdered at Red Wedding; Brynden is heir; Hoster is bookish.
10. **Jaime departs Raventree with Hoster** — Warns Lord Tytos about harboring outlaws; Blackwood calls him "Kingslayer."
11. **Jaime informs Bracken of the settlement** — Tells him to go home and plant; lists the lands given; demands one of Bracken's daughters as hostage for Queen Regent's court.
12. **The column travels along the Widow's Wash** — Hoster guides them to a ford; they discuss the history of the Teats and the Blackwood-Bracken feud.
13. **Historical discussion about the feud** — Goes back to the Age of Heroes; Brackens usurped the Blackwood crown; hundred peaces sealed with marriages, all broken.
14. **Jaime quotes Tywin** — "Never wound a foe when you can kill him"; references extinction of the Casterlys, Tarbecks, and Reynes; Hoster asks about the Starks.
15. **Column arrives at Pennytree** — Larger village than expected; war damage visible but many homes rebuilt.
16. **Villagers hide in holdfast** — Refuse to open gates, citing past abuse by king's men who burned, stole, killed, and raped.
17. **Jaime camps in the village** — Orders no stealing; column sups on own provisions; Jaime tries to count pennies on the tree.
18. **Brienne arrives near midnight** — Scouts bring her in; she has a bandage on her face ("a bite"); carries Oathkeeper; claims to have found "the girl" but says the Hound will kill her unless Jaime comes alone.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Resigned weariness; dry humor masking inner conflict; occasional bitterness toward Tyrion and Cersei; momentary guilt about Rhaegar's children; lingering concern for Brienne and Arya; shock at Brienne's aged, wounded appearance
- **Primary preoccupation:** Ending the siege and completing his work in the riverlands so he can return (ostensibly to the king, but whispered: to Cersei); underlying concern about Cersei's fate with the High Septon; conflict over whether to help her
- **Key decisions made:** Burns Cersei's letter (recalled from Riverrun); negotiates moderate terms for Blackwood rather than harsh ones; takes Hoster instead of Bethany as hostage; demands a Bracken daughter as counter-hostage; declines to eat at starving Raventree; refuses to storm the Pennytree holdfast; decides to return by a different route hoping to encounter the Blackfish or Dondarrion
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself he returns "to the king" but acknowledges "another part of him whispered, to Cersei"; immediately contradicts this with "even if he had gone back, he could not hope to save her" — oscillating between duty, desire, and resignation
