# ADWD — Reek I

## Chapter Metadata

- **first_available:** ADWD Reek I
- **location_primary:** The Dreadfort
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell (referenced), the Weeping Water (recalled in memory)
- **approximate_timeline:** Sometime before Ramsay Bolton's march to wed Arya Stark; Reek has been imprisoned for an extended period he estimates at half a year or longer
- **time_markers:** "two days since he had eaten, or maybe three"; "half a year at least" estimated in the dungeons; "years ago, it seemed" since the escape attempt with Kyra; night with full moon rising over the castle's eastern walls; "winter was close at hand"; the Walder boys are "still boys" (both still eight), which Reek uses to calibrate how long he's been imprisoned
- **chapter_summary:** Reek, a broken prisoner in the Dreadfort's dungeons, is eating a rat when two squires — Big Walder and Little Walder Frey — come to fetch him. They remind him of his name and lead him through the moonlit yard to the great hall, where Ramsay Bolton feasts with his men and two visiting lords. The lords recognize Reek as Stark's ward. Ramsay announces he is to wed Arya Stark, whom Lord Roose is bringing to him, and tells Reek he will be cleaned up and given a task — riding to war to help fetch Ramsay's bride.

## Events & Actions
1. **Reek eats a rat in his dungeon cell** — He bites into a live rat, eating its belly first, with warm blood running over his lips; he has not eaten in two or three days
2. **Footsteps and keys heard outside the cell** — Reek freezes in terror, afraid that discovery of the rat will lead to punishment from Ramsay
3. **Two boys open the cell** — Torchlight strikes Reek and he shrieks; the boys are initially uncertain they have the right prisoner
4. **The boys identify themselves and remind Reek of his name** — Big Walder and Little Walder Frey, both eight, both squires in matching lambswool doublets; they tell him his name is Reek
5. **Reek remembers his name** — He weeps and recites "My name is Reek. It rhymes with leek." He acknowledges he was someone else in another life
6. **Reek considers escaping** — He thinks he could overpower two eight-year-olds, take the torch, keys, and dagger, but decides it is a trap and remembers the consequences of his last escape attempt
7. **Reek recalls the escape attempt with Kyra** — Kyra had keys and knew a postern gate; they crossed the Weeping Water at night; at dawn Ramsay hunted them with dogs and a horn; they were captured within the hour; Kyra threw a stone at Ramsay and missed; Ramsay said "You must be punished"
8. **Reek recalls Lady Hornwood** — Ramsay married her, locked her in a tower, and starved her until she ate her own fingers
9. **Reek is led through the yard** — Night, full moon, frozen ground, cold wind, dogs barking in kennels; Reek sees his own white, fleshless hands and is shocked
10. **Reek enters the great hall** — Dim, smoky, torches held by skeletal hands on walls; the garrison eats at long tables; the Bastard's Boys sit near the dais; strangers/guests are present; some wrinkle their noses, others laugh at Reek
11. **Reek is brought before Ramsay at the high table** — Ramsay sits in his father's seat with two unnamed lords; Ramsay introduces Reek as his "sour old friend" and claims Reek has been with him since boyhood
12. **The two lords recognize Reek** — The crookback lord mentions the original serving man Reek being dead; the mail-byrnie lord urges the other to look again; the crookback lord identifies him as "Stark's ward. Smiling, always smiling."
13. **The mail-byrnie lord advocates killing Reek** — "A dog who turns against his master is fit for naught but skinning"; Ramsay reveals Reek has indeed been skinned "here and there"
14. **Ramsay confronts Reek about blood on his mouth** — Reek confesses eating a rat; Ramsay claims all rats in the Dreadfort belong to Lord Roose
15. **Reek recalls trying to bite off his own finger** — He tried to bite off his flayed ring finger to stop the pain; Ramsay punished him by taking a toe; Ramsay's method is to flay fingers/toes and wait until the victim begs for amputation
16. **Ramsay announces his betrothal** — He will wed Arya Stark, Lord Eddard's daughter; Lord Roose is bringing her; Ramsay will be lord of Winterfell
17. **Reek recalls Arya** — Remembers her as brown-haired, long-faced, skinny, always dirty; also remembers Sansa as "the pretty one" and his childhood fantasy of marrying her
18. **Ramsay invites Reek to attend the wedding** — Reek hesitates, suspects a trap, then agrees
19. **Ramsay promises to restore Reek** — Offers to clean him, clothe him, feed him porridge and pease pie with bacon; says he has a task requiring Reek's strength
20. **Ramsay announces they ride to war** — Reek will come along "to help me fetch home my virgin bride"

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pervasive terror and dread; brief moments of animal satisfaction (eating the rat); despair about his condition; flickers of defiance immediately suppressed by fear; weeping when reminded of his name; relief mixed with suspicion at Ramsay's offer
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival through absolute obedience — remembering his name (Reek) and the rules Ramsay has taught him; avoiding any action or word that might cost him another finger or toe; "Serve and obey and remember who you are"
- **Key decisions made:** Decides NOT to attempt escape when considering overpowering the two boys — concludes "It is too easy. It is a trap." Accepts Ramsay's invitation to attend the wedding and serve him.
- **Self-deception flags:** "His lord was merciful and kind. He might have flayed his face off for some of the things Reek had said" — internalizing Ramsay's cruelty as justified mercy; "He does not want to hurt me, he told me so, he only does it when I give him cause" — classic victim rationalization; actively suppresses his true identity and former life ("In another life he had been someone else")
