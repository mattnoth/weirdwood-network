# ADWD — Jon III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 11
- **pov_character:** Jon Snow
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon III
- **first_available:** ADWD Jon III
- **location_primary:** Castle Black
- **location_secondary:** The Wall, the tunnel through the Wall, the King's Tower, the armory (used as mess hall and Jon's quarters), the rookery (Clydas's rooms)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Jon's appointment as Lord Commander; after the defeat of Mance Rayder's host by Stannis
- **time_markers:** The execution takes most of the day ("By the time the last of them had bent the knee, night had fallen"); night has fallen by the time Jon passes through the Wall; Jon takes supper in the evening; he writes letters late into the night until "the room was dim and chilly"
- **chapter_summary:** Mance Rayder is executed by burning in a cage before the Wall, while the Horn of Joramun is also set aflame. Stannis offers the captive wildlings a choice to kneel or flee, and most submit, passing through the Wall after burning weirwood branches. Jon orders mercy arrows shot into the burning Mance. After the ceremony, Jon debates strategy with Bowen Marsh over whether to seal the gates, then visits Clydas to discuss Lightbringer and the Azor Ahai prophecy from the Jade Compendium, noting that Stannis's sword is cold. Jon dispatches friends to other castles and retires alone.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Bitterly cold; Jon can see his breath; the Wall is weeping (melting slightly on its face); wind whips at cloaks; the outside air grows colder as night falls
- **Season indicators:** Deep cold consistent with approaching winter; "Cold, and getting colder"
- **Time of day:** Begins during daylight (execution), transitions through dusk to full night; Jon takes supper after dark and works late into the night
- **Lighting:** Firelight from the execution pit; Lightbringer's supernatural blaze (red, yellow, orange); moonlight on the Wall and armor; candlelight from King's Tower windows; dim orange glow from the gate guardpost; candles in Jon's rooms (difficult to light); taper in Clydas's rooms
- **Sounds:** Mance's screams and incoherent shrieks; moaning from the stockade; children crying; the fire crackling and whooshing; queen's men beating spear butts against shields chanting "One realm, one god, one king! STANNIS!"; creak of chains as the winch cage scrapes against ice; wind above the Wall
- **Smells:** Smoke and drifting ash around the fire pit; burned flesh clinging to Jon's blacks; spices of mulled wine (in Clydas's rooms)
- **Notable sensory details:** Heat from the fire pit "palpable even at a distance" and "blistering" for the wildlings close by; the taste of hot blood filling Jon's mouth (from Ghost's kill); Jon's hands stiff from cold; the warmth of mulled wine spreading through his chest; the Wall weeping cold tears in the tunnel

## Events & Actions
1. **Mance Rayder brought to execution** — Bound with rope and noose, dragged by Ser Godry Farring's horse to the fire pit; loses his footing and is dragged bloody.
2. **Jon recalls his failed plea to Stannis** — He had argued Mance was more useful alive, citing his knowledge of the haunted forest, Tormund, and the Others, plus his restraint with the Horn. Stannis refused.
3. **Melisandre addresses the wildlings** — Proclaims the choice between light and darkness; names the Horn "the Horn of Darkness."
4. **The Horn of Joramun displayed and burned** — Black and banded with old gold, eight feet long with First Men runes; burst into green and yellow flame; tumbled into the fire pit.
5. **Mance caged and hoisted above the fire** — Claws at noose, screams of treachery and witchcraft, denies his kingship and his name.
6. **Mance burns** — Does "a little dance" in the flames; his screams become one long wordless shriek.
7. **Jon orders mercy arrows** — Says "Now"; Ulmer, Sweet Donnel Hill, Garth Greyfeather, and Bearded Ben loose arrows; three strike Mance (chest, gut, throat), one hits the cage bar.
8. **Melisandre proclaims Stannis as Azor Ahai reborn** — Sweeps hand toward him; ruby pulses with light.
9. **Stannis draws Lightbringer** — Sword glows with unprecedented brightness; horses shy; the Wall turns colors; seems to diminish the fire pit; Jon thinks "Is this the power of king's blood?"
10. **Stannis offers terms** — Promises food, land, justice to those who kneel; death to those who refuse; orders gates opened.
11. **Gates opened; wildlings pass through the Wall** — Stakes removed, planks dropped across ditches, stockade opened, Night's Watch ranks part; Edd opens the iron gate. Wildlings shuffle through, given weirwood pieces to burn.
12. **Some wildlings flee** — A young woman with two children runs for the trees; one greybeard attacks with the weirwood branch and is killed; roughly one in ten choose the woods.
13. **Most wildlings submit** — Come on clutching wood scraps to feed to flames; "the new god devoured the corpse of the old."
14. **Leaders kneel** — Sigorn (Magnar of Thenn) first; Rattleshirt; two Hornfoot clan chiefs; old wisewoman of the Milkwater; Alfyn Crowkiller's son; Halleck with Harma's pigs.
15. **Night falls; giants and remaining holdouts** — Four giants refuse to leave their mammoth; some dead/dying and their companions stay; about two score remain. Stannis tells them they're free to go and spread the word.
16. **Final wildlings and giants melt into trees** — Only the dead remain.
17. **Jon orders Marsh to break up stockade and burn corpses** — Lord Steward obeys.
18. **Jon and Marsh debate strategy** — Marsh advocates sealing the gates with stone and ice; Jon argues they cannot be blind without rangers and must make common cause with wildlings against the Others.
19. **Marsh warns Jon about appearance of siding with Stannis** — Says there's talk Jon is "too friendly" with Stannis; warns Stannis's cause is doomed and the Watch must not choose the losing side.
20. **Jon discusses political situation** — Notes Tywin is dead, Tommen is a boy; outcome uncertain.
21. **Jon passes through the Wall tunnel** — Edd accompanies with torch; they discuss the horn burning and onion soup.
22. **Jon declines to eat with friends** — In the cellar, rebukes Pyp for mocking Melisandre's prayers; Grenn invites him to sit; Jon refuses, remembering his father's words about lords and friends.
23. **Jon observes Val on the King's Tower roof** — Reflects she is lonely and lovely and lethal; has thrice attempted escape; stabbed a man-at-arms in the neck.
24. **Ghost appears with blood on his mouth** — Jon tastes hot blood (warging connection); forces himself to remember "I am a man, not a wolf."
25. **Jon visits Clydas for mulled wine** — They discuss Mance's death and Lightbringer; Jon reads from the Jade Compendium about Azor Ahai's sword; notes Stannis's Lightbringer is cold.
26. **Jon returns to his rooms and writes letters** — Dispatches Halder and Toad to Shadow Tower; Grenn and Pyp to Eastwatch; reads Ser Denys Mallister's letter; works late into the night.
27. **Jon retires to bed** — Climbs to narrow bed that was Donal Noye's; reflects "This is my lot… from now until the end of my days."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Conflicted and burdened; feels pity for Mance but dares not show squeamishness; lonely — craves company of Sam, Aemon, Pyp, Grenn but forces himself to maintain distance; guilt about sending Aemon, Sam, and Gilly away (dreams of Sam drowning, Ygritte dying, Gilly weeping blood); cold determination mixed with doubt
- **Primary preoccupation:** The tension between duty and desire — must lead rather than befriend; the strategic question of how to defend the Wall with too few men; skepticism about Stannis's divine claims (Lightbringer is cold); whether the wildlings will keep faith
- **Key decisions made:** Orders mercy arrows for Mance; refuses to sit with friends at supper; dispatches Pyp, Grenn, Halder, and Toad to other castles; declines to seal the gates
- **Self-deception flags:** "Another day," the lord commander *lied* — Jon explicitly identifies his own lie when refusing to eat with friends; thinks "I am a man, not a wolf" to deny the warging experience; tells Marsh "I will think on all you've said" but his instincts are already set
