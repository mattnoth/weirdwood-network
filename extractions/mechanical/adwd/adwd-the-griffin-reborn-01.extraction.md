# ADWD — The Griffin Reborn

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 62
- **pov_character:** Jon Connington
- **pov_chapter_number:** The Griffin Reborn
- **first_available:** ADWD The Griffin Reborn
- **location_primary:** Griffin's Roost
- **location_secondary:** Cape Wrath, Shipbreaker Bay, Stoney Sept (in memory), Crow's Nest (referenced), Rain House (referenced), Estermont/Greenstone (referenced), Storm's End (discussed), King's Landing (referenced), Dorne/Sunspear (discussed), Volon Therys (referenced), the Trident (referenced), Lys (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the Golden Company's landing on Cape Wrath; during the period when Cersei and Margaery are both accused of treason in King's Landing and Mace Tyrell has abandoned the siege of Storm's End
- **time_markers:** Night attack on Griffin's Roost; "that night" (victory feast); dawn the next morning (rain, breakfast, greyscale treatment); "later that day" (war council); "four days later" (Prince Aegon arrives); "ten days" set as deadline before marching on Storm's End; "on the morning of the eleventh day, we ride"; a fortnight referenced as the length of Rhaegar's stay at Griffin's Roost in memory; "too many years" since Connington prayed at his father's grave; "first year of exile" when he spoke with Myles Toyne
- **chapter_summary:** Jon Connington leads the Golden Company in a swift, nearly bloodless capture of Griffin's Roost, his ancestral castle. He reflects on his past failure at the Battle of the Bells, where he failed to find and kill Robert Baratheon. He holds a war council where intelligence from captured parchments reveals political fractures in Westeros — Cersei and Margaery quarreling, the Tyrells abandoning Storm's End, and Dorne waiting. Connington secretly treats his advancing greyscale with wine soaks. He announces his plan to take Storm's End within ten days, overruling Strickland's caution. Prince Aegon arrives and insists on leading the attack himself.

## Events & Actions
1. **Archers deployed** — Black Balaq positions 200 archers around Griffin's Roost with orders to bring down every raven leaving the castle
2. **Ram assault on gatehouse** — Franklyn Flowers leads men from concealment in encroaching woods, reaches within twenty yards before being seen; ram strikes gate
3. **Defenders killed on battlements** — Black Balaq's archers kill two men on the battlements before they can raise alarm
4. **Gate breached** — Gate is closed but not barred; gives way at second blow; Flowers' men halfway up the throat before warhorn sounds
5. **Ravens intercepted** — Two ravens shot down within a hundred yards of the castle; a third shot down personally by Black Balaq
6. **Cold oil dumped** — A guard pours a bucket of oil on attackers, but it is unheated; the bucket itself causes more damage than the oil
7. **Walls taken** — Golden Company troops climb over merlons, race along wallwalks shouting "A griffin! A griffin!" (the Connington battle cry)
8. **Maester killed** — The maester is thrown from his tower; "the way his arms were flapping, he might have been mistaken for another bird"
9. **Castle falls** — Remaining guards throw down weapons; Griffin's Roost is taken; Jon Connington is once more a lord
10. **Connington gives search orders** — Directs Flowers to search keep and kitchens; Malo the maester's tower and armory; Byrne the stables, sept, and barracks; warns of hidden stairs under altar of the Mother and under the northwest tower
11. **Haldon takes the rookery** — Ordered to prepare for sending messages
12. **Connington inspects with Strickland** — They walk through the great hall and view the Griffin Seat
13. **Connington climbs east tower** — Ascends to the roof of the tallest tower; reflects on past visits with his father and with Rhaegar
14. **Memory of Rhaegar's visit** — Prince Rhaegar stayed a fortnight, returning from Dorne; played his harp at the welcoming feast
15. **Connington reflects on his failure** — Recalls the Battle of the Bells; Myles Toyne's rebuke about Tywin Lannister burning the town; vows not to fail the son as he failed the father
16. **Prisoners gathered in yard** — Raymund, Alynne, and Ronald Storm identified among captives; confined to west tower
17. **Old retainers identified** — A few captives recognized from Connington's former time as lord: a grizzled one-eyed serjeant, washerwomen, a groom (former stableboy), the enormously fat cook, the castle armorer
18. **Connington addresses captives** — Announces himself as rightful lord returned from exile; demands and receives oaths of allegiance
19. **Victory feast in the great hall** — Connington presides from the Griffin Seat; captive children seated at high table; Ronald Storm threatens that his father will kill Connington; children sent back to cells
20. **Haldon found reviewing intelligence** — In the maester's tower with parchments and maps; reports on Lannister-Tyrell tensions, the political situation across Westeros
21. **Strategic discussion of landing** — Ten thousand men sailed from Volon Therys; not quite half have arrived at the intended landing site on Cape Wrath
22. **Order to write Prince Doran** — Connington instructs Haldon to write Sunspear, revealing that Elia's son is alive and has come to claim Rhaegar's throne
23. **Haldon proposes marriage alliance** — Suggests offering Aegon's hand to win a great House; Connington refuses, insisting Aegon must remain free for Daenerys
24. **Haldon proposes Connington himself as marriage prize** — Connington refuses with a cold stare; internally notes "Death is creeping up my arm"
25. **Connington sleeps in the lord's chambers** — Dusty canopy of red-and-white velvet
26. **Greyscale treatment** — Wakes at dawn; orders worst wine in the cellar; bars the door; soaks his hand in wine (substitute for vinegar prescribed by Lady Lemore); observes black nails on four fingers, grey past second knuckle on middle finger
27. **War council convened** — Nine attend: Connington, Strickland, Haldon, Black Balaq, Franklyn Flowers, Malo Jayn, Ser Brendel Byrne, Dick Cole, Lymond Pease
28. **Greenstone report** — Haldon reports that Marq Mandrake was put ashore on Estermont with ~500 men and has taken Greenstone
29. **Connington's deception strategy revealed** — No banners shown during first attacks; plans to write Tommen asking for pardon to create confusion; will secretly contact allies in stormlands, Reach, and Dorne
30. **Storm's End announced as target** — Connington overrules Strickland's caution; sets ten-day deadline; argues they need a nigh-impregnable stronghold and a demonstration of strength to win Doran Martell
31. **Byrne suggests alliance with Stannis** — Connington rejects it: Stannis is Robert's brother, a thousand leagues away, with little to offer
32. **Prince Aegon arrives** — Four days later; rides at head of 100 horse with three elephants; accompanied by Lady Lemore (in septa's robes) and Ser Rolly Duckfield (in white Kingsguard cloak)
33. **Connington reflects on Duck's Kingsguard appointment** — Considers Duck "solid and true" but not worthy; had tried to dissuade Aegon; Aegon cited the Kingslayer as an example of renown being no guarantee
34. **Aegon meets Connington in the solar** — Keeps Connington waiting "the better part of an hour"; has already spoken with Strickland and Flowers about Storm's End
35. **Aegon insists on leading the attack** — Agrees the attack should proceed but demands to lead it personally

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Driven by determination and a deep need for redemption. Nostalgic and haunted when reflecting on Rhaegar and his past failures. Contemptuous of Strickland's caution. Privately anxious about his advancing greyscale. A complex mix of resolve and grief pervades his thoughts.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Atoning for his failure at the Battle of the Bells by succeeding for Aegon ("I failed the father, but I will not fail the son"). Secondary preoccupation: hiding his greyscale from everyone.
- **Key decisions made:** Capture Griffin's Roost swiftly; show no banners to maintain confusion; take Storm's End within ten days; write Prince Doran Martell; write a deceptive pardon request to King Tommen; reject Haldon's marriage proposals for both Aegon and himself; summon Prince Aegon to Griffin's Roost.
- **Self-deception flags:** His memory of Rhaegar is suffused with unacknowledged romantic longing ("loved too hard," "Rhaegar's love," the way he remembers the prince's beauty and music). He reframes his failure at Stoney Sept as excess of honor rather than tactical incompetence, even as Myles Toyne's rebuke undermines that narrative. He dismisses Strickland as timid but may be overcompensating in the opposite direction with aggressive haste. His internal vow "I will not fail the son" carries the weight of a man who knows he is dying and is desperate for meaning.
