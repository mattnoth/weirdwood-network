- **time_markers:** "solitary supper the night before"; "an hour before first light"; "the hour of the wolf" (pre-dawn waking); "first light" (departure); "Half the morning passed" (before Slynt reports); "the next morning" (execution); two knights rode south "an hour ago"; Kedge Whiteye has seen "forty-odd namedays, thirty of them on the Wall"; Maester Aemon is "one hundred and two years old"; Aegon was "three-and-thirty" when chosen by the Great Council

He sends Sam, Gilly, and Maester Aemon south to Oldtown so Sam can forge a maester's chain. Jon garrisons Icemark under Bedwyck and assigns Greyguard to Janos Slynt, who refuses the command. After Slynt's public defiance the next morning, Jon executes him with Longclaw, receiving a nod of approval from Stannis.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon reads and agonizes over Maester Aemon's letter to King Tommen** — He cannot bring himself to sign it, seeing it as futile given Lannister hostility.
2. **Jon meets with Gilly** — He orders her to take Dalla's baby south and leave her own child at Castle Black, to protect the infant with king's blood from Melisandre.
3. **Gilly resists but Jon forces compliance** — He makes her hold her hand over a candle flame to demonstrate what burning means; threatens that her own son will die if she refuses.
4. **Gilly agrees and departs weeping** — Jon notes Ghost beneath the anvil outside.
5. **Jon meets with Sam** — They discuss the letter; Sam argues it's worth sending as a "paper shield." Jon reluctantly signs and seals it.
6. **Jon lies to Sam about Gilly's visit** — Claims Val sent her to plead for Mance.
7. **Jon orders Sam to Oldtown** — To forge a maester's chain at the Citadel; Maester Aemon and Gilly will accompany him. Dareon joins at Eastwatch.
8. **Sam protests and Jon overrules him** — Sam fears his father, cutting corpses, and his own inadequacy. Jon commands obedience.
9. **Jon recalls Maester Aemon's counsel** — "Kill the boy and let the man be born," advice Aemon once gave to his brother Aegon V.
10. **Jon makes his daily rounds** — Walks Castle Black, visits the Wall, speaks with Kedge Whiteye and Dywen about the "wrong-way rangers" (Horpe and Massey riding south).
11. **Jon has a nightmare** — Dreams of ripping Gilly's babies apart and swapping their heads.
12. **Pre-dawn departure** — Gilly, Sam, Maester Aemon, and Black Jack Bulwer's escort leave Castle Black. Maester Aemon tells Jon about The Jade Compendium. Gilly asks Jon not to name the baby until past two years.
13. **Jon gives Bedwyck (Giant) command of Icemark** — Twenty men from the Watch plus half as many from Stannis; orders beacon towers along the Wall.
14. **Jon assigns Greyguard to Janos Slynt** — Slynt refuses, calls Jon "bastard" and "traitor's bastard," and storms out.
15. **The next morning, Jon confronts Slynt publicly** — In the common room, gives him one last chance to obey. Slynt refuses again.
16. **Jon orders Slynt hanged, then changes to beheading** — Calls for a block and unsheathes Longclaw.
17. **Jon executes Janos Slynt** — Beheads him personally with Longclaw in the yard. Alliser Thorne reaches for his sword but backs down.
18. **Stannis nods approval** — Their eyes meet briefly, then the king returns inside.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Burdened, resolute, grim. Jon dreads the conversations with Gilly and Sam. He is tired — slept fitfully, troubled by nightmares. He feels the weight of command isolating him from those he cares about. During the execution, he is calm and deliberate but internally noting the danger.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The necessity of hard decisions — protecting Dalla's baby by forcing Gilly to swap children, sending Sam away despite needing him, dealing with Slynt's insubordination. The refrain "Kill the boy" recurs as Jon steels himself for each unpleasant duty.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Signs the letter to Tommen despite misgivings. (2) Forces the baby swap on Gilly. (3) Sends Sam, Aemon, and Gilly south. (4) Garrisons Icemark under Bedwyck. (5) Assigns Greyguard to Slynt. (6) Changes Slynt's punishment from hanging to personal beheading. (7) Decides not to confine Slynt to an ice cell (would only delay plotting).
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon lies to Sam about why Gilly was crying (claims Val sent her about Mance). He rationalizes Slynt's assignment as giving him "a chance" — but also acknowledges wanting him away from Thorne. The internal "Kill the boy" mantra may mask guilt about his harshness toward Gilly.
