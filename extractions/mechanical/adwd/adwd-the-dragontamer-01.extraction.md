# ADWD — The Dragontamer I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 69
- **pov_character:** Quentyn Martell
- **pov_chapter_number:** The Dragontamer I
- **first_available:** ADWD The Dragontamer I
- **location_primary:** Great Pyramid of Meereen
- **location_secondary:** Meereen (city streets/alleys), the dragon pit beneath the pyramid
- **approximate_timeline:** Late in ADWD; after Daenerys has disappeared on Drogon and Quentyn has remained in Meereen; occurs during a single night into predawn
- **time_markers:** "The hour of the bat gave way to the hour of the eel, the hour of the eel to the hour of ghosts"; "By the time the hour of the wolf crept upon them"; "dawn was still half an hour off"; night throughout; rain begins; predawn; "The watch changed when the sun came up"
- **chapter_summary:** Quentyn Martell, unable to sleep, burns his hand over a candle flame as a test of courage. After a tense conversation with Gerris Drinkwater about the futility of his plan, the three Dornishmen disguise themselves as Brazen Beasts using masks and cloaks provided by the Tattered Prince. They enter the Great Pyramid through a side entrance using the day's password ("dog"), admit a butcher's wagon carrying meat, chains, and Windblown sellswords led by Caggo and Pretty Meris. When the password fails at the dragon pit, the guards are killed in a violent skirmish. Inside the pit, Quentyn confronts Rhaegal and Viserion; Viserion kills a crossbowman who shoots at him, and when Quentyn attempts to tame Viserion with a whip, Rhaegal sets him on fire from behind.

## Events & Actions
1. **Quentyn lies sleepless** — He stares at his ceiling through the hours of the bat, eel, and ghosts, his mind fevered with thoughts of fire and blood.
2. **Quentyn tests himself with fire** — He holds his palm over a candle flame until it burns him and he cries out in pain.
3. **Gerris confronts Quentyn** — Gerris hears him and comes to talk, suggesting alternatives (Temple of the Graces, abandoning the plan). Quentyn refuses, insisting this is his duty and destiny.
4. **Quentyn dismisses Gerris** — He tells Gerris to find a ship and go home, then returns to bed. He privately regrets not kissing the Drinkwater twins or visiting his mother.
5. **Predawn breakfast** — The three Dornishmen eat a simple meal of fruit, bread, cheese, and goat milk. Quentyn forbids wine.
6. **Arch retrieves the disguises** — He brings the bundle from the Tattered Prince: three Brazen Beast cloaks, masks (bull, lion, ape), cudgels, shortswords, and a whip.
7. **The Tattered Prince's warning recalled** — The password is "dog," obtained by Pretty Meris through interrogation of Brazen Beasts. The Tattered Prince warns Quentyn with a Pentoshi saying: "Never ask the baker what went into the pie. Just eat."
8. **The three disguise themselves** — Quentyn takes the lion mask, Arch the bull, Gerris the ape. Quentyn takes the whip; Arch brings his warhammer.
9. **Descent through the pyramid** — They go down the servants' steps through brick walls of many colors, encountering no one. Dawn is half an hour off.
10. **They approach the side entrance** — Two Brazen Beasts (fox and rat masks) guard bronze doors barred with heavy iron.
11. **Password exchange at the side entrance** — Quentyn gives the password "dog." After a tense pause, the guards accept it and leave.
12. **The butcher's wagon enters** — The wagon carrying an ox carcass, two dead sheep, concealed chains, and armed Windblown enters through the opened doors. Pretty Meris enters undisguised with Caggo (cobra mask) and five other disguised sellswords, two with crossbows.
13. **Meris reports** — She tells Quentyn the Tattered Prince is near with fifty men and will see them safe away once the dragon is brought out. Caggo commands the sellsword contingent.
14. **Discussion about the wagon and dragons** — Arch questions if the wagon is big enough; Caggo notes the dragons are smaller than Drogon. Quentyn explains the pit has slowed their growth, citing his readings about the Dragonpit of King's Landing.
15. **Approach to the dragon pit** — The group descends through brick arches, down a stone ramp, through dungeons, torture chambers, and past cisterns, the butcher's cart rumbling behind.
16. **Confrontation at the dragon pit doors** — Four Brazen Beasts (basilisk serjeant, three locusts) guard the massive iron doors. Quentyn says "dog" but the serjeant stiffens — the password is wrong or has changed.
17. **Quentyn orders the attack** — He croaks "Take them," and the fight begins.
18. **Arch kills the basilisk serjeant** — He flings his torch at a locust, unslings his warhammer, and drives the spike through the serjeant's temple, crushing through mask, flesh, and bone. The serjeant collapses, shaking.
19. **Gerris saves Quentyn** — A locust's spear nearly goes through Quentyn's throat; Gerris slams into him, knocking him aside. The spearpoint grazes the cheek of his lion mask.
20. **The guards are killed** — Gerris drives his sword through one locust's throat. A second locust is hit by a crossbow bolt. The third surrenders but Caggo beheads him with his Valyrian steel arakh, complaining about the noise.
21. **Quentyn realizes the password failed** — He says the word was supposed to be "dog." Pretty Meris tells him his scheme was called madness and to proceed with the plan.
22. **Arch breaks the chain** — He smashes the lock with his warhammer (five swings), and the chains fall away with a loud clatter.
23. **The dragon pit doors are opened** — Arch pulls the rusted iron doors apart; hinges scream. A wash of heat, ash, brimstone, and burnt meat smell pours out. Stygian darkness beyond.
24. **Quentyn enters the pit** — He prays to the Warrior for courage, takes a torch from Gerris, and steps through. He reminds himself of the dragons' names and Daenerys's example.
25. **Quentyn encounters Rhaegal** — Two bronze eyes rise before him. He sees Rhaegal's dark green scales, massive head, furnace-glow mouth, and long uncoiling neck. The dragon has a broken iron collar and chain.
26. **Rhaegal is fed** — Quentyn calls for food; Arch flings a sheep into the pit. Rhaegal catches it in the air, roasting it with a lance of orange-yellow-green fire before eating the burning carcass.
27. **Quentyn searches for Viserion** — He looks up and discovers Viserion has burrowed a cave in the thick pyramid walls with flame and claw. The pale dragon stirs, uncoils, and reveals himself — white scales, golden coal eyes, rattling wings.
28. **Viserion launches from the ceiling** — He flies down and lands between the Dornishmen and the door, roaring. He inspects the intruders and stares longest at Pretty Meris, apparently seeking a female — seeking Daenerys.
29. **Quentyn attempts to tame Viserion** — He calls the dragon's name, takes out the whip, snaps it with a crack, and commands "Down." He lashes Viserion across the face. The dragon hisses.
30. **Viserion loses interest and attacks** — Viserion turns toward the Windblown and the exit. A crossbowman shoots him; the bolt grazes his neck, drawing glowing gold-and-red dragon's blood. Viserion kills the crossbowman — his teeth close around the man's neck, flame gouting from the tiger mask, eyes bursting, brass melting; he tears and eats flesh.
31. **The Windblown pull back** — Even Pretty Meris cannot stomach this. Viserion feeds on the dead sellsword.
32. **Quentyn's final attempt** — He calls Viserion's name, snaps the whip again, commands "Down" and lashes the dragon's face. Viserion hisses.
33. **Rhaegal attacks from behind** — While Quentyn focuses on Viserion, Rhaegal attacks from behind. Quentyn turns and raises his arm to shield his eyes. His whip lash is burning. His hand is burning. All of him is burning.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terror, dread, and duty in fierce conflict. Quentyn is consumed by fear of burning and dying, but equally terrified of being remembered as a failure. He oscillates between desperate courage and paralyzing fear — staring transfixed at the dying serjeant, feeling sick, feeling like a boy. His final moments are desperate determination overriding survival instinct.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Proving himself worthy — of Daenerys, of Dorne, of his father's trust. He tells himself "I must be the hero" and frames the mission as his "grand adventure" and "destiny." He is haunted by what he has lost (Cletus, Will, Maester Kedry) and what he gave up (Gwyneth Yronwood, the Drinkwater twins, visiting his mother).
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to abandon the plan despite Gerris's arguments; forbids wine at breakfast ("There will be time enough for drink afterward"); orders the attack on the pit guards; enters the dragon pit; attempts to tame Viserion with the whip.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself "Wine will help me sleep" and knows it is a lie. Convinces himself he can master dragons because he has ridden horses since age six. Tells himself Daenerys showed him the dragons because "She wants me to prove myself to her." Frames the suicidal plan as heroic destiny — "I must be the hero. The hero never dies, though." Convinces himself the dragons will become docile after feeding, based on experience with snakes in Dorne.
