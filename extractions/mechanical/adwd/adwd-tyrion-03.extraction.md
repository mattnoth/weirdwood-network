# ADWD — Tyrion III

## Chapter Metadata

- **location_primary:** The Valyrian road (eastern Essos), Ghoyan Drohe
- **location_secondary:** The Shy Maid (poleboat on the Little Rhoyne), overgrown yard with old stone well (rest stop)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Tyrion's previous chapter; multi-day journey from Illyrio's company to the Shy Maid
- **time_markers:** Wakes (implied morning/midday); "Just after sunset" they stop to rest; "Come moonrise" they ride again; "By dawn" Tyrion is exhausted; "the next day" they reach Ghoyan Drohe; "Three days ago" Young Griff knocked Duck into a trough (per Haldon)
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion wakes in Illyrio's halted litter and is introduced to Haldon Halfmaester and Ser Rolly Duckfield, who have come to collect six chests and escort Tyrion to their company. Illyrio departs, swearing to rejoin them in Westeros. Tyrion rides with Duck and Haldon along the Valyrian road, learning Duck's backstory and discussing pirates, the Shrouded Lord, and Lomas Longstrider's Wonders. They reach the ruined Rhoynish city of Ghoyan Drohe on the Little Rhoyne, where they board the poleboat Shy Maid and meet Griff, Young Griff, Septa Lemore, and the rest of the crew. Griff reads Illyrio's letter, questions Tyrion about his crimes, burns the letter, and reluctantly agrees to take Tyrion as far as Volantis.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion wakes in the halted litter** — finds Illyrio outside with two unknown riders speaking a foreign tongue
2. **Tyrion relieves himself by the road** — one rider comments "He pisses well, at least"
3. **Introductions and banter** — the riders are Haldon Halfmaester (healer) and Ser Rolly Duckfield (Duck); Duck draws his bastard sword in jest when Tyrion asks to "fetch the duck"
4. **Chests transferred** — Duck shifts six oaken chests with iron hasps from mules to pack horses; Illyrio mentions candied ginger for "the boy" and news of Griff's company
5. **Haldon reports news** — Dothraki khalasars on the move: Motho near Dagger Lake, Zekko in the Forest of Qohor, Pono with 30,000 near the Selhoru headwaters; Griff wants to depart immediately
6. **Illyrio assigns Tyrion the alias "Yollo"** — Tyrion improvises an alternative: "Hugor Hill"
7. **Haldon tests Tyrion's knowledge** — asks about Serwyn of the Mirror Shield and Urrax; then about Ser Byron Swann and Vhagar. Tyrion corrects him: the dragon was Syrax, not Vhagar, citing the squire's account over Maester Munkun's
8. **Illyrio bids farewell** — promises to rejoin them in Westeros, swearing "by my sweet Serra's hands"; appears diminished and almost sad as they ride away
9. **Tyrion rides with Duck and Haldon** — they discuss pirates of Dagger Lake (Urho the Unwashed, Lady Korra) and the Shrouded Lord/Prince of Sorrows; Duck warns against japing about the Shrouded Lord near the Rhoyne
10. **Duck tells his life story** — born in Bitterbridge to an armorer; Lord Caswell's son Lorent took his longsword; Duck beat Lorent with a hammer, fled to the Golden Company; became smith's apprentice, then squire to Harry Strickland; sent to Griff to train his son, knighted by Griff a year later; named "Duckfield" because he saw ducks in the field where he was dubbed
11. **Rest stop at sunset** — they halt in an overgrown yard beside an old stone well, amid ruins of a large stone manse; supper of salt pork, cold white beans, and ale
12. **Tyrion deduces the chests' contents** — notes Duck lifted them too easily for gold; Haldon confirms they contain armor, court clothes, and gifts for a queen
13. **Night ride on the Valyrian road** — the road glimmers like silver ribbon; Tyrion, Haldon, and Duck discuss Lomas Longstrider's Wonders; the Valyrian roads are the fourth wonder, not the fifth
14. **Tyrion recalls his youth** — Uncle Gerion gave him Longstrider's books, made him recite the wonders at feasts; Lord Tywin denied Tyrion permission to tour the Free Cities at age sixteen, gave him charge of Casterly Rock's drains and cisterns instead
15. **Arrival at Ghoyan Drohe** — the next day; ruined Rhoynish city with choked canals, stagnant pools, broken temples; a few impoverished inhabitants remain; Tyrion frightens a naked girl by making a face
16. **Reaching the Shy Maid** — they find the poleboat moored at a half-submerged stone quay; Young Griff waves from the cabin roof; broad-beamed, shallow-draft vessel with muddy paintwork
17. **Meeting the crew** — Young Griff leaps down; an older Rhoynish couple stands by the tiller; Septa Lemore steps from the cabin; Griff commands silence
18. **Griff reads Illyrio's letter** — privately in his cabin with Tyrion; Griff can read and barely moves his lips; ice-blue eyes
19. **Tyrion's confession** — claims to have killed Tywin (crossbow on a privy), his mother (in childbirth), and Joffrey (poison at his wedding); says he'll add Jaime and Cersei before he's done
20. **Griff challenges Tyrion's loyalty** — asks why Daenerys would welcome a kingslayer and betrayer; Tyrion argues the king he slew sat on her throne and those he betrayed were lions
21. **Griff burns the letter** — touches parchment to candle flame; warns Tyrion he is "the last and least" of the company
22. **Griff agrees to take Tyrion to Volantis** — conditionally, if Tyrion proves obedient and useful
23. **Tyrion probes about dragons** — asks what if the talk of dragons is just a sailor's fancy; Griff warns him sharply: "Guard your tongue or lose it. Kingdoms are at hazard here."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Sardonic, darkly humorous, self-deprecating; underlying melancholy and bitterness, especially when recalling Tywin and his youth; flashes of genuine unease (greyscale, the Shrouded Lord); moment of near-peace on the moonlit Valyrian road; calculating and watchful around Griff
- **Primary preoccupation:** Assessing his new companions — testing their intelligence (Haldon), their temperament (Griff), their trustworthiness; the recurring question "where do whores go"; bitter memories of his father's contempt and denied freedoms
- **Key decisions made:** Rejects "Yollo" alias in favor of "Hugor Hill"; chooses to provoke Griff with a list of exaggerated/false confessions (claiming Joffrey's murder) to test his reaction; agrees to be subordinate ("Valar dohaeris")
- **Self-deception flags:** Claims credit for poisoning Joffrey — a false confession presented as truth; presents himself as more monstrous and dangerous than he is, as a kind of psychological armor; thinks "Yes, Father" when Griff gives orders, suggesting he's mapping his father's dynamic onto a new authority figure
