## Chapter Metadata

- **first_available:** ADWD The Iron Suitor
- **location_primary:** Isle of Cedars (aboard the Iron Victory, at anchor)
- **location_secondary:** The Stepstones (recalled), Volantis (recalled), the Shields (recalled), Lannisport (recalled), Moat Cailin (recalled), Pyke (recalled), Lys (recalled), Greenshield (recalled)
- **approximate_timeline:** After a lengthy voyage from the Shields across the Summer Sea; the fleet has been waiting at the Isle of Cedars for straggling ships; Grief is the latest arrival, the 54th ship
- **time_markers:** "daybreak" (Grief's arrival); "three days previous" (Maiden's Bane arrived); days counted between arrivals; "ten days in the water" (Moqorro's claimed survival); "a day out of Old Volantis" (first storm); "four hundred years ago" (the Doom drowning the island); "come sunset" (Victarion emerges after Moqorro's ritual); "the fleet departs upon the morrow, on the evening tide"
- **chapter_summary:** Victarion Greyjoy counts fifty-four ships arrived at the Isle of Cedars, far fewer than the ninety-three that departed the Shields. His sword-wounded left hand festers dangerously despite Maester Kerwin's treatments. He orders the fleet to depart the next evening for Meereen, intending to reach Daenerys Targaryen before the Volantene fleet. A red priest named Moqorro is fished from the sea by the ship Grief and brought before Victarion; Moqorro claims to have seen Victarion in his nightfires and offers to heal the rotting hand. After hours of ritual involving fire, laughter, and singing in High Valyrian, Victarion emerges with a charred and blackened but apparently healed hand, and orders Kerwin's throat cut based on Moqorro's counsel.

## Events & Actions
1. **Grief arrives at daybreak** — The fifty-fourth ship reaches the Isle of Cedars, sailing alone with black sails against pale pink skies.
2. **Victarion tallies the fleet** — He counts 54 ships total: 22 of his own squadron, 14 of Ralf the Limper's, 9 of Red Ralf Stonehouse's, plus 9 captured prizes. Red Ralf himself is among the missing.
3. **Victarion recalls Ralf the Limper's report** — Ralf described three big storms, red winds from Valyria, and black winds; accused Euron of sending them to die. Victarion slapped him twice and warned him not to speak of curses.
4. **Victarion orders departure** — He summons Wulfe One-Ear and orders: the Vole to report, hunting parties recalled, shore camps broken, fruit gathered, pigs driven aboard, Shark to remain behind for stragglers. Fleet to depart the next evening tide.
5. **Victarion reflects on the Volantene threat** — He recalls Volantis celebrating the coming war against Daenerys; estimates the Volantene fleet at 300-500 ships; knows he must reach Meereen before them.
6. **Victarion reflects on Euron** — He considers Euron's voyage to Qarth, Tall Trees Town, and through the Smoking Sea with only one ship, steeling himself to attempt the same boldness with fifty-four.
7. **Victarion contemplates his mission and Daenerys** — He plans to claim Daenerys for himself rather than fetching her for Euron; wonders if she is truly beautiful or if Euron lied.
8. **Victarion retreats to his cabin** — His head pounds from sun, hand pain, or doubts. The dusky woman tends him with a damp cloth on his brow.
9. **Victarion reflects on the dusky woman** — He recalls Euron slicing out her tongue, decides he should have killed her as a sacrifice but never got around to it; now talks to her because she cannot repeat his words.
10. **Victarion explains his strategy to the dusky woman** — He discusses splitting the fleet, the festering hand, and his plan to sweep in from the sea like Lannisport, take Daenerys, and race home before the Volantenes arrive.
11. **Maester Kerwin examines the wound** — Kerwin kneels, inspects and sniffs the wound; says the cut is not healing, suggests he may need to take the hand. Victarion threatens to kill him and give his arse to the crew if he does.
12. **Kerwin lances the wound** — He slashes the palm; thick yellow pus bursts forth; Victarion demands he cut deeper until blood flows. Blood so dark it looks black in lantern light. Kerwin cleans it with vinegar-boiled cloth.
13. **Victarion reflects on Serry's wound** — He recalls catching Serry's longsword with his bare hand at the Shields; the blade bit through his gauntlet. He wonders if the blade was poisoned.
14. **Victarion suspects Kerwin of poisoning him** — He theorizes the maester (given to him by Euron) may be using maesterly tricks to keep the wound from healing.
15. **Longwater Pyke announces Grief's captain has brought a wizard** — A prisoner fished from the sea.
16. **Moqorro is presented on deck** — The Vole introduces the enormous black-skinned red priest; claims he was ten days on the water clinging to a broken spar.
17. **Moqorro declares himself a slave of R'hllor** — Victarion calls him "a pink priest" due to his faded robes; Wulfe calls him "a demon priest."
18. **The crew calls for Moqorro's death** — Burton Humble and others urge killing him. Longwater Pyke draws his dirk. Victarion bellows them down and physically takes Moqorro to his cabin.
19. **The dusky woman hisses at Moqorro** — She bares her teeth and hisses like a snake upon seeing the red priest. Victarion backhands her and orders wine.
20. **Moqorro describes his vision** — He tells Victarion he has seen him in the nightfires: striding through flames with his great axe, blind to tentacles grasping him at wrist, neck, and ankle — black strings that make him dance.
21. **Victarion shows Moqorro his wounded hand** — The new linen is already discolored with blood and pus.
22. **Moqorro offers to heal the hand** — He requests a blade (silver preferred, iron acceptable), a brazier, and fire; warns of terrible pain.
23. **The healing ritual** — Behind barred doors, wild laughter is heard (deep, dark, and mad), then singing — a strange high wailing song in what Kerwin identifies as High Valyrian. The monkeys flee the ship, screeching.
24. **Victarion emerges at sunset** — Naked from the waist up, left arm bloody to the elbow, he raises a charred and blackened hand with wisps of dark smoke rising from his fingers.
25. **Victarion orders Kerwin killed** — Pointing with his smoking hand, he orders the maester's throat cut and body thrown in the sea, saying Moqorro saw in his fires that this will bring favorable winds to Meereen.

- **Primary preoccupation:** The dwindling fleet and the need to depart before the Volantenes; his festering hand that will not heal; reaching Daenerys before anyone else; his bitter rivalry with Euron
- **Key decisions made:** Orders the fleet to depart the next evening tide despite having only 54 ships; allows Moqorro to live and perform his healing ritual despite crew opposition; orders Maester Kerwin's execution based on Moqorro's counsel
- **Self-deception flags:** Dismisses the dusky woman as just a tongueless servant he keeps for convenience, but she is clearly important to him (he confides his doubts only to her); insists "I am no man's puppet" when Moqorro's vision explicitly shows him being manipulated by "black strings"; tells himself the wound was "a scratch from a little kitten" when it was a serious sword cut; considers himself no fool but consistently fails to see how others use him; refuses to see the crew's laughter as anything but mockery, revealing deep insecurity
