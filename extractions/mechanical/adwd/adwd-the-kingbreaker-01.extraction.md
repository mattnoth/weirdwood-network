- **chapter_summary:** Barristan Selmy and Skahaz mo Kandaq finalize their conspiracy to depose King Hizdahr zo Loraq, disagreeing on tactics — Skahaz wants an immediate military strike while Barristan insists on arresting Hizdahr first and offering the Yunkai'i a chance to withdraw. After a day spent checking sentries and training his squires, Barristan arms himself and descends the pyramid at the hour of the wolf. He confronts Hizdahr with accusations about the poisoned locusts and asks if he is the Harpy. When Hizdahr calls for his guard Khrazz, Barristan fights and kills the pit fighter, then takes Hizdahr prisoner. As he leads the king away, a cupbearer arrives with the news that the dragons have been loosed.

## Events & Actions
1. **Conspiracy meeting in armory** — Barristan and Skahaz meet on the Great Pyramid's second level to finalize their coup plan. The password is "Groleo." Skahaz reports all his men are in place.
2. **Debate over strategy** — Skahaz urges an immediate dawn attack through the gates against the Yunkish siege lines. Barristan insists they arrest Hizdahr first, form a council, demand the Yunkai'i return hostages and withdraw, and only then fight if refused. Barristan calls Skahaz's way "dishonorable"; Skahaz calls Barristan's way "stupid."
3. **Discussion of hostages** — Barristan insists someone must rescue Daario, Hero, and Jhogo from the Yunkish camp. Skahaz dismisses their value and suggests letting Daario die would be better for Daenerys. Barristan acknowledges Daenerys's love for Daario is dangerous but insists on rescuing the hostages.
4. **Dispute over the cupbearers** — Skahaz proposes killing cupbearer-hostages in retaliation if slavers harm Meereen's hostages. Barristan refuses absolutely, invoking the murder of Rhaegar's children Rhaenys and Aegon by Tywin's men. He declares he will not permit the murder of children.
5. **Compromise reached** — Skahaz agrees: no harm to Hizdahr or the cupbearer-hostages. The hour of the wolf is confirmed.
6. **Barristan makes morning rounds** — He checks all sentries throughout the pyramid from top to bottom, which takes most of the morning.
7. **Afternoon sparring with squires** — Barristan trains his orphan squires, personally taking up sword and shield. He identifies his six best: Tumco Lho (best natural swordsman since Jaime Lannister), Larraq the Lash (deadly with whip and trident), Red Lamb (all ferocity, no technique), and three unnamed Ghiscari brothers.
8. **Barristan considers knighting squires** — He contemplates knighting Tumco, Larraq, and Red Lamb in case he dies tonight, but decides against it — a traitor's knighting would harm their reputations.
9. **Speech on chivalry** — As afternoon becomes evening, Barristan tells his squires: "It is chivalry that makes a true knight, not a sword. Without honor, a knight is no more than a common killer. It is better to die with honor than to live without it."
10. **Warning to Missandei** — Barristan finds Missandei reading and tells her to stay in the queen's chambers tonight no matter what happens.
11. **Reflection on the terrace** — Barristan stands on the terrace gardens at the pyramid's apex, reflecting on his life of plots and secrets. He recalls Rhaegar, Harrenhal, and Ashara Dayne. He senses a storm coming.
12. **Memory of Harrenhal** — Barristan recalls the tourney: Lord Whent announced it after a visit from Ser Oswell; Aerys (prompted by Varys) suspected Rhaegar of conspiracy and insisted on attending; Rhaegar crowned Lyanna Stark queen of love and beauty; Barristan wishes he had won the last tilt and crowned Ashara Dayne instead.
13. **Memory of Ashara Dayne** — Barristan recalls Ashara's appearance, her stillborn daughter, her suicide by jumping from a tower, and his secret love for her. He notes Daenerys has the same purple eyes.
14. **Barristan bathes and arms himself** — He bathes until the water grows cold, scrubs his skin raw, then dresses in fresh whites and dons the gilded and enameled armor Daenerys gave him. He leaves his helm behind.
15. **Waiting in darkness** — Barristan sits in his small chamber beside the queen's apartments, thinking of all the kings he served and failed, and wondering which Kingsguard brothers would approve of what he is about to do. Rain begins.
16. **Descent begins** — Barristan descends the servants' stairs alone, white cloak rippling.
17. **Meeting Skahaz and the locusts** — Twelve levels down, Barristan finds Skahaz (still in blood bat mask) and six Brazen Beasts masked as identical locusts. They exchange the password "Groleo."
18. **Reaching the king's level** — At the 16th–17th levels, two Brazen Beasts (rat and bull masks) guard the chained doors. They give the password and are let through into a torchlit servants' corridor of red and black brick.
19. **Encounter with Steelskin** — Outside the king's chambers, Steelskin bars the way with his longaxe but relents when he recognizes Barristan. He admits only Barristan, not the locusts.
20. **Entering the king's chambers** — Barristan enters alone. The chambers are dark, windowless, with black oak beams, Qartheen silk carpets, faded Ghiscari tapestries, and sandalwood lover sculptures. Qezza and Draqaz are present; Miklaz has gone to wake the king.
21. **Hizdahr appears** — Hizdahr emerges yawning in a green satin robe over his naked body. A naked woman is glimpsed in the bedchamber. Hizdahr asks for wine and news of Daenerys.
22. **Barristan confronts Hizdahr** — Barristan asks: "Are you the Harpy?" Hizdahr is shocked and denies it. Barristan accuses him regarding the poisoned locusts. Hizdahr blames Quentyn Martell ("the Dornishman") and says Reznak would confirm. Barristan presses: Hizdahr provided the food, urged Daenerys to eat the locusts, and never tasted them himself.
23. **Hizdahr's defense and accusation** — Hizdahr claims hot spices disagree with him and Daenerys was his wife. He suggests perhaps Daario tried to poison her for putting him aside. He notes Barristan is wearing armor and realizes something is wrong.
24. **Barristan draws his sword** — He asks again if Hizdahr is the Harpy, hand on his longsword, promising a swift clean death for the truth. Hizdahr dismisses him from service and orders him to leave Meereen.
25. **Hizdahr calls for Khrazz** — Hizdahr screams for his guard and stumbles backward toward the bedchamber.
26. **Fight with Khrazz** — Khrazz emerges from behind a tapestry with a Dothraki arakh. He boasts he will eat Barristan's heart. The two fight: Khrazz is blazing fast but his arakh is ill-suited to close quarters against an armored opponent. Barristan blocks calmly, takes cuts on his armor, and wounds Khrazz on the left shoulder, then cheek (ear to mouth), then chest. Khrazz flips the iron brazier, scattering coals. His arakh chips Barristan's enamel but cannot penetrate the steel beneath.
27. **Khrazz killed** — Khrazz's arakh tip catches on a wall hanging. Barristan slashes open his belly, parries the freed arakh, and thrusts through his heart. Khrazz's entrails slide out.
28. **Hizdahr taken prisoner** — Barristan finds Hizdahr hiding behind a tapestry, whimpering. He promises Hizdahr will be kept prisoner until the queen returns and no harm will come if nothing is proved against him. He leads Hizdahr out, feeling "strangely light-headed, almost drunk" and wondering "I was a Kingsguard. What am I now?"
29. **News of the dragons** — Miklaz and Draqaz return with wine flagons and stare at Khrazz's corpse. Miklaz says Reznak mo Reznak commands the king to come to the terrace at once. When Barristan asks why, Miklaz stammers: "D-d-dragons. The dragons have been loosed, ser."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply conflicted; Barristan knows the coup may be treason if Hizdahr is innocent, yet believes inaction will doom Daenerys's dragons. He feels no honor in what he does. During the fight with Khrazz, he feels certainty and purpose for the first time all day: "This is what I was made for." Afterward he feels "strangely light-headed, almost drunk." Pervasive melancholy and nostalgia for past failures (Ashara, Rhaegar, the kings he served and failed).
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether deposing Hizdahr is the right thing to do; protecting the dragons and Daenerys's interests in her absence; the tension between honor and necessity. Secondary: the fate of the hostages (Daario, Hero, Jhogo) and the safety of the cupbearer children.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Insists the coup follow his plan, not Skahaz's aggressive dawn attack. (2) Absolutely refuses to harm the cupbearer-hostages. (3) Decides not to knight Tumco, Larraq, and Red Lamb — better a long life as a squire than a short one as a soiled knight. (4) Leaves his helm behind for practical reasons. (5) Proceeds with the coup despite his doubts. (6) Offers Khrazz the chance to yield before fighting. (7) Promises Hizdahr he will be kept alive pending the queen's return.
- **Self-deception flags:** Barristan repeatedly tells himself "we have no choice" and "it all fit" regarding Hizdahr's guilt, while simultaneously acknowledging "no matter how he turned and twisted this, the old knight could find no honor in it." He struggles to convince himself the coup is justified. He also frames Daenerys's love for Daario as purely harmful ("poison") without examining whether his own feelings about Daario are colored by jealousy or paternalism.
