# ADWD — Jon XII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 59
- **pov_character:** Jon Snow
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon XII
- **first_available:** ADWD Jon XII
- **location_primary:** Castle Black / the Wall
- **location_secondary:** The tunnel through the Wall, the wildling camp north of the Wall, Long Barrow, Eastwatch-by-the-Sea, Shadow Tower, Hardhome (via letter), Oakenshield, Frozen Shore (referenced), Skane (in letter)
- **approximate_timeline:** The day the free folk pass through the Wall under Jon's agreement with Tormund; follows Jon's deal with Tormund in prior chapters
- **time_markers:** "the hour of the wolf" (predawn); "Soon enough the sun would rise"; dawn — "the eastern sky had just begun to lighten"; "A deep red blush had appeared in the east"; "From dawn till dusk"; "Just before midday" (oxcart jam); "By afternoon the sun had gone"; "By late afternoon the snow was falling steadily"; nightfall approaching at chapter's end; the passage takes a full day (dawn to dusk)
- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow opens the gate at Castle Black to allow Tormund Giantsbane's wildlings through the Wall. From dawn to dusk, over three thousand wildlings pass through: first one hundred boy hostages, then warriors, then the general population including the men of the Frozen Shore. The skinchanger Borroq and his monstrous boar are among the last through. Jon receives a desperate letter from Cotter Pyke at Hardhome reporting dead things in the woods and water, lost ships, and a plea for help by land.

- **Lighting:** "Predawn gloom" in Jon's chambers; dawn light "pink and gold and purple" shimmering on ice; bright blue sky in morning; day grows darker as clouds arrive; darkening sky at end

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon's nightmare** — Jon dreams of defending the Wall alone against wildlings with drums and warhorns. He fights in black ice armor with a burning red blade, killing wildlings including Ygritte, then turns on his own brothers — Donal Noye, Deaf Dick Follard, Qhorin Halfhand, and finally beheads Robb while screaming "I am the Lord of Winterfell."
2. **Jon wakes** — Mormont's raven pecks him awake in the predawn gloom (the hour of the wolf). The raven says his full name "Jon Snow" for the first time.
3. **Breakfast and final preparations** — Jon breaks his fast with officers in the cellar. Bowen Marsh assures him all is ready. Jon warns that whoever strikes the first blow will lose his head.
4. **Conversation with Dolorous Edd** — Edd reports on Long Barrow's condition (overrun with spearwives), his mules, and serving under Iron Emmett. Edd departs for his wagons on the east road.
5. **Jon assembles his guard** — Jon mounts a fiery grey courser and gathers eight guardsmen: Ty, Mully, Left Hand Lew, Big Liddle, Rory, Fulk the Flea, Garrett Greenspear, and Leathers.
6. **The gate opens** — At Jon's command, Big Liddle roars the order. Sentries blow one long blast of the warhorn. Gates open at both ends of the tunnel. Satin leads through with an iron lantern.
7. **Jon meets Tormund north of the Wall** — Tormund is mounted with his sons Toregg and Dryn and three score warriors. Jon whistles for Ghost, who spooks Tormund's horse.
8. **Hostage boys pass through** — One hundred boys ages 8-16 pass through as hostages ("blood price"). Two stewards count them, a third collects valuables. Almost all are thin. Some are sons of notable wildlings.
9. **Notable hostage fathers identified** — Tormund points out sons of Soren Shieldbreaker, Gerrick Kingsblood, Harle the Huntsman, Harle the Handsome, Howd Wanderer, Brogg, Devyn Sealskinner, Kyleg of the Wooden Ear, Morna White Mask, the Great Walrus, Alfyn Crowkiller, and Varamyr Sixskins.
10. **Two disguised girls discovered** — Jon spots two girls disguised as boys among the hostages. He invokes the story of Brave Danny Flint and sends them to Long Barrow. Tormund agrees to provide two replacement boys.
11. **Dryn given as final hostage** — Tormund surrenders his son Dryn as the hundredth hostage. Jon promises Dryn will serve as his personal page. Tormund tells Dryn to behave and warns he bites.
12. **Wildling warriors pass through** — Five hundred to a thousand armed warriors advance, bearing painted shields and assorted weapons. Spearwives are among them. Jon remembers Ygritte.
13. **Wildling leaders swear oaths** — Brogg, Soren Shieldbreaker, Gerrick Kingsblood (with three daughters), Howd Wanderer, Devyn Sealskinner, Harle the Huntsman, and Morna White Mask each swear loyalty or present gifts. Jon notes Gerrick's claim of descent from Raymun Redbeard and plans to present his daughters to Queen Selyse.
14. **Wildlings surrender treasures** — Warriors deposit valuables in carts: amber pendants, golden torques, jeweled daggers, brooches, rings, cups, goblets, horns, jade comb, pearls, a silver-scale shirt, a broken sword with sapphires, a mammoth-hair toy, an ivory phallus, a unicorn-head helm.
15. **Men of the Frozen Shore pass through** — Bone chariots (some with runners replacing wheels), fearsome dogs as big as direwolves, women in sealskins with infants, children with dark hard eyes clutching stones, men with antler hats or walrus tusks. Thin reindeer bring up the rear.
16. **Tormund gives Jon mead** — Warns him to be wary of the Frozen Shore folk. Jon drinks deep and calls him "Tormund Giantsbabe."
17. **Oxcart jams in the tunnel** — Just before midday, an oxcart wedges at a turn. The driver's kin and those behind nearly come to blows. Jon and Tormund spend an hour resolving it.
18. **Tormund reveals the Horn of Joramun was a fake** — Tormund says Mance's horn was a thousand-year-old find from a giant's grave, not the true Horn. The true horn was never found. Mance used it as a bluff to make the Night's Watch think he could bring down the Wall.
19. **Jon reflects on the Horn's implications** — Wonders whether Mance or Tormund is lying, and where the true Horn of Winter is.
20. **Tormund describes the Others** — Refuses to speak freely about them on the north side of the Wall. Describes them nibbling at the column's edges during the march south — losing outriders, fires shrinking and dying, people freezing in their sleep. Mentions his son Torwynd's death, breaking off emotionally.
21. **Jon thinks about dragonsteel** — Recalls Sam's research: Longclaw was forged in dragonflame and set with spells; Sam called Valyrian steel "dragonsteel."
22. **Weather changes** — Clouds cover the sky; warmth flees; the Wall stops weeping; snow begins to fall. The wildlings hasten, frightened of shadows in the woods.
23. **Stabbing incident** — One man stabbed for trying to cut in line. Toregg breaks it up, sends both men to the back of the column.
24. **Toregg sent to hurry stragglers** — Tormund orders Toregg to burn tents if necessary; gate must close by nightfall; anyone not through will answer to Tormund.
25. **Final wildlings pass through** — Columns of smoke rise from the trees (Toregg burning the dead — those who died overnight in their tents). Toregg returns with a dozen mounted warriors, Tormund's rear guard.
26. **Borroq the skinchanger arrives** — Last through the gate, with a monstrous boar twice Ghost's size. Ghost snarls; Jon restrains him. Borroq calls Jon "Brother" and warns "They're coming, crow."
27. **Bowen Marsh delivers the count** — 3,119 wildlings passed through. Sixty hostages sent to Eastwatch and Shadow Tower. Edd took six wagons of women to Long Barrow. Tormund to lead his folk to Oakenshield within days.
28. **Castle Black transformed** — Jon returns to a changed castle: lights in dark windows, strange voices, wildlings on icy paths, men throwing snowballs in the yard outside Flint Barracks.
29. **Letter from Hardhome** — Clydas delivers a message from Cotter Pyke via Maester Harmune on the ship Talon: six ships at Hardhome, Blackbird lost with all hands, two Lyseni ships aground on Skane, Talon taking water; wildlings eating their dead; dead things in woods and water; Braavosi only taking women and children; witch women calling them slavers; attempt to take Storm Crow defeated with six crew dead; eight ravens left; pleas for help by land.
30. **Jon's closing reflection** — "Night falls, and now my war begins."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Conflicted and burdened. Jon's dream reveals deep guilt — killing his brothers, friends, and kin while screaming he is Lord of Winterfell. Waking, he questions his fitness for command ("what was doing"). During the day he maintains resolve but feels the weight of responsibility. The Hardhome letter at the end shifts him toward dread and determination.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Successfully executing the wildling passage without bloodshed; managing the fragile peace between Night's Watch and free folk; the threat of the Others pressing behind the wildlings.
- **Key decisions made:** Opens the gate to let wildlings through; sends disguised girls to Long Barrow for safety; takes Dryn as his personal page; resolves to present Gerrick's daughters to Queen Selyse; decides to be the last man through the gate.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon thinks "It should have been the Old Bear... my uncle" — deflecting the weight of command onto absent predecessors, though he proceeds anyway. He also thinks Bran is dead (killed by Theon), which is false but Jon believes it.
