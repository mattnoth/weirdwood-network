- **time_markers:** Auction takes place during daylight (morning heat referenced); they travel through camp; evening falls and they perform at a feast; they serve and clean late into the night
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion and Penny are sold at a slave auction outside Meereen, purchased by the Yunkish lord Yezzan zo Qaggaz for five thousand silvers. Tyrion convinces the overseer Nurse to buy Jorah Mormont as well, claiming the knight is part of their act. They are fitted with iron collars and housed in Yezzan's pavilion alongside other "treasures." That evening they perform their jousting show and serve at a feast where Tyrion defeats Brown Ben Plumm at cyvasse. They are told they will joust in the Great Pit of Daznak to celebrate the peace.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion and Penny are auctioned as Lot 97** — The auctioneer presents them as trained dwarfs with dog and pig included. Bidding starts at three hundred silvers.
2. **Bidding progresses** — A matron, the fat Yunkishman (Yezzan), and a crone (Zahrina) in violet tokar bid incrementally.
3. **Dwarfs perform on the block** — The auctioneer has them demonstrate their act; Tyrion falls off Pretty Pig, drawing laughter and higher bids.
4. **The crone inspects Tyrion** — She complains about his missing nose and mismatched eyes; Tyrion makes a crude joke and receives a whip lash.
5. **A sellsword bids two thousand** — An older brown-skinned man in a purple cloak enters the bidding. Tyrion recognizes him as someone who knows his identity.
6. **A girl on a shield bids twenty-five hundred** — She wears a harpy breastplate inlaid with gold.
7. **Yezzan wins the bidding at five thousand** — The fat man outbids all others. The sellsword (Brown Ben Plumm) shrugs and turns away.
8. **Tyrion bids on himself** — He shouts seven thousand, then ten thousand, then "all the gold of Casterly Rock," earning laughter and whip lashes but drawing the sellsword's attention back.
9. **Tyrion is sold** — The auctioneer declares him sold and whips him again; Tyrion goes down.
10. **A young girl is sold next** — About fifteen or sixteen, stripped naked on the block.
11. **Tyrion surveys Meereen** — He gazes at the city walls and considers escape, but cannot abandon Penny.
12. **Nurse takes charge of them** — Yezzan's overseer arrives with a mule cart and two soldiers; introduces himself and describes Yezzan as kindly.
13. **Jorah Mormont is auctioned as Lot 99** — Naked, chained, beaten, branded; no one bids initially. Bidding starts low.
14. **Tyrion convinces Nurse to buy Jorah** — He claims Jorah is part of their act ("the bear and the maiden fair"). Nurse whispers to Yezzan, who bids three hundred and wins Jorah.
15. **The cart travels through the siege camp** — They pass the trebuchets, witness a slave being whipped, men marching in irons, evidence of disease (flux), and a man skinning a dog.
16. **Three escaped slaves are executed by Tolosi slingers** — Tied to crossbeams; lead balls destroy their limbs. Nurse forces Penny and Jorah to watch.
17. **They arrive at Yezzan's compound** — A lemon-colored silk pavilion with nine peaked roofs and gilded harpies.
18. **Iron collars are fitted** — Gilded iron with Yezzan's name in Valyrian glyphs and tiny bells. Penny cries. Tyrion lies that it's solid gold.
19. **Jorah is chained outside** — He is fastened to a stake near the cookfire.
20. **Tyrion and Penny meet Yezzan's other "treasures"** — A goat-legged boy, a two-headed girl from Mantarys, a bearded woman, and Sweets (a hermaphrodite with purple hair and violet eyes).
21. **Sweets warns them about Nurse** — "He is the only true monster here." Sweets reveals Yezzan went to Sothoros ten years ago and has been rotting since.
22. **The dwarfs bathe and are tended** — Hot bath, ointment on cuts, poultice, haircuts, fresh clothing and slippers provided.
23. **Evening feast begins** — Yezzan hosts Yurkhaz zo Yunzak and other Yunkish lords plus two sellsword captains. Entertainment: juggler, tumblers, goat-boy jig, then the dwarf joust.
24. **Tyrion and Penny perform their jousting show** — Riding pig and dog, falling, whacking each other; well received. Tyrion decides not to unchain Jorah for this performance.
25. **They serve wine and water** — Tyrion pours purple wine, Penny pours water. One Yunkishman suggests making the dwarfs fuck; another asks about Tyrion's nose.
26. **A Yunkish lord challenges Tyrion to cyvasse** — Tyrion wins quickly; the lord overturns the table in fury.
27. **Brown Ben Plumm challenges Tyrion to cyvasse** — Stakes are set: if Plumm wins, he gets Tyrion; Yezzan counters that Plumm would get the purchase price in gold instead. They play five games. Tyrion wins three (first, third, fourth, fifth), Plumm wins one (second, for double stakes).
28. **Feast ends** — Yurkhaz departs supported by slaves; other guests leave. Yezzan has fallen asleep drunk during the third cyvasse game.
29. **Servers eat leavings and clean** — Tyrion scrubs wine stain from carpet on his knees, in pain.
30. **Nurse delivers news** — Yurkhaz persuaded Yezzan to let the dwarfs joust in the Great Pit of Daznak to celebrate the signing of the peace. Tens of thousands will watch.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Dark humor masking desperation; calculating; physically pained (whip lashes, cramping legs); sardonic detachment from his own degradation; brief hope at seeing Brown Ben Plumm (a Westerosi he might manipulate); resigned cynicism about "royal rescues"
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and escape — how to make himself valuable enough to stay alive; assessing whether Brown Ben Plumm can be turned; eyeing Meereen's walls as potential refuge; awareness of the poison mushrooms as a last resort
- **Key decisions made:** Bids on himself to inflate value and attract the sellsword's attention; convinces Nurse to buy Jorah (claiming him as part of the act); decides not to unchain Jorah for the first performance; plays cyvasse to win rather than lose strategically; holds his tongue about his true identity
- **Self-deception flags:** "We're too valuable to mistreat" — reassuring Penny while knowing this isn't reliably true; momentarily believes he could "turn" Brown Ben Plumm as he did Bronn, despite the man's cold eyes
