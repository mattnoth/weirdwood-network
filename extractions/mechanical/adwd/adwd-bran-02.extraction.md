# ADWD — Bran II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 14
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran II
- **first_available:** ADWD Bran II
- **location_primary:** Hill above the cave of the three-eyed crow (beyond the Wall)
- **location_secondary:** The cave system beneath the hill, underground river cavern
- **approximate_timeline:** Late in the journey north beyond the Wall; winter conditions, deep snow
- **time_markers:** Sunset/last light of day at chapter start; night falls during the wight attack; twelve days since the elk died; snow stopped three days ago; seven days sustained on elk meat
- **chapter_summary:** Bran and his companions reach the hill containing the cave of the three-eyed crow. During their ascent, wights burst from the snow and attack. Bran warges into Hodor to fight, and a child of the forest rescues them with fire. They enter the cave and descend deep underground through weirwood-root-filled passages. At the bottom, they find the three-eyed crow — an ancient man enthroned in weirwood roots — who tells Bran he will never walk again but will fly.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Sharp, cold air; wind gusting and filling the air with fine white powder; snow stopped three days prior but none has melted; deep snow (waist-deep in places)
- **Season indicators:** Deep winter; ice on all the trees, frozen snow burying trees from root to crown
- **Time of day:** Begins at sunset; transitions to full night during the wight attack; remainder of chapter underground with no natural light
- **Lighting:** Light of the setting sun through trees like a distant fire glow; torchlight from the child of the forest; ruddy orange firelight in the cave; the glow between the weirwood trees at the cave entrance; wight eyes glowing pale blue
- **Sounds:** Raven screaming/shrieking; wind gusting; Hodor whimpering "Hodor"; rushing water of underground river; dripping water in the passages; bones crunching underfoot
- **Smells:** None explicitly described
- **Notable sensory details:** Blood in Bran's mouth from biting his tongue; heat of fire washing over his face inside the cave; damp seeping through gloves; icicles hanging from Hodor's beard; frozen snot on Hodor's mustache glittering redly; dirt crumbling from tunnel ceiling into eyes and hair; iron-like grip of wight fingers

## Events & Actions
1. **Coldhands warns of danger** — The ranger senses something close and urges them to climb before dark, saying their warmth will draw the wights.
2. **Decision to split** — Meera cannot carry Jojen and fight; she sends Hodor with Bran ahead while she follows with Jojen.
3. **Ascent begins** — Hodor climbs the steep snowy hill with Bran on his back; Coldhands accompanies them; Summer follows.
4. **Summer senses danger** — The direwolf stops, sniffs, snarls, and begins backing away.
5. **Wight ambush** — A wight grabs Hodor's leg from beneath the snow; Hodor falls; multiple wights rise from the snow around them.
6. **Bran separated from Hodor** — The fall throws Bran from his basket onto the hillside.
7. **Summer defends Bran** — The direwolf tears apart the wight attacking Bran, ripping off its arm and tearing out its throat.
8. **Bran crawls toward cave** — He drags himself uphill toward the light, clawing at roots and rocks.
9. **Bran warges into Hodor** — Hearing Hodor's whimper, Bran enters Hodor's body; as Hodor, he fights the wights with the longsword, beheading one.
10. **Meera joins the fight** — She drives her frog spear into a wight's back; Bran-as-Hodor picks up Jojen.
11. **Retreat up the hill** — Meera leads while Bran-as-Hodor carries Jojen upward.
12. **Wights set on fire** — Flaming figures appear; the child of the forest uses her torch and a cloud of ravens pours from the cave.
13. **Bran sees the child** — He initially mistakes her for Arya; she is darting about with a torch.
14. **Snow buries Bran** — A tree shakes off its snow onto Bran, hiding/burying him.
15. **Rescue** — The child pulls Bran from the snow; the group reaches the cave interior with a fire.
16. **Coldhands excluded** — The child says the ranger cannot come; reveals he was killed long ago.
17. **Leaf identifies herself** — She reveals she is a child of the forest; explains their true name means "those who sing the song of earth"; says she was born in the time of the dragon and walked among men for two hundred years.
18. **Descent into the cave** — The child leads them down through twisting passages filled with massive weirwood roots; past bones and skulls; past branching tunnels.
19. **Arrival at the greenseer's chamber** — They cross above an underground river and find the pale lord enthroned in weirwood roots.
20. **Meeting the three-eyed crow** — The ancient figure confirms he was once a crow "black of garb and black of blood"; says he has watched Bran with "a thousand eyes and one"; saw his birth, his father's birth, his fall.
21. **Bran asks to be healed** — He asks if the greenseer will fix his legs.
22. **The greenseer's promise** — He says he cannot fix Bran's legs: "You will never walk again, Bran, but you will fly."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear (wight attack), helplessness (being a cripple, unable to fight in his own body), grief (tears when asking about his legs), wonder (at meeting the child of the forest), brief exultation (when fighting as Hodor), despair (when told he won't walk)
- **Primary preoccupation:** Reaching the cave safely; survival during the wight attack; hope that the three-eyed crow will heal his legs
- **Key decisions made:** Warges into Hodor to fight the wights (acts on instinct/necessity); asks the greenseer to fix his legs
- **Self-deception flags:** "Jojen just needs to eat" — Bran tries to convince himself food will fix Jojen, but acknowledges uncertainty ("he wasn't sure it would"); tells himself the bones in the cave passage are just animal bones before realizing they include giants and children
