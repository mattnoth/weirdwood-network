- **chapter_summary:** After a sleepless final night in her cell at the Great Sept of Baelor, Cersei is shaved completely bare by silent sisters and septas, then led through the sept. In the Hall of Lamps, the Warrior's Sons form her escort under Ser Theodan the True, and Cersei spots her cousin Lancel among them. Stripped naked on the marble steps, Cersei walks barefoot through King's Landing from the Sept down Visenya's Hill and up Aegon's High Hill to the Red Keep, enduring abuse from the crowd while Septa Scolera rings a bell and chants "shame." Cersei falls twice, hallucinates familiar faces including a vision of Maggy the Frog repeating her prophecy, and finally breaks down crawling to the castle gates, where Ser Kevan, Kingsguard knights, and Jocelyn receive her. Qyburn introduces her to her new champion, the enormous Ser Robert Strong, who carries her inside.

## Events & Actions
1. **Cersei's sleepless final night** — Cersei paces her cell unable to sleep, recalling the caged lions at Casterly Rock and how she was always bolder than Jaime as children.
2. **Internal debate about the walk** — Cersei considers refusing the walk and insisting on trial by battle, but realizes she needs a champion and must reach Tommen; her uncle and the High Sparrow were adamant, and Ser Kevan refused to act against the High Sparrow.
3. **The shaving** — At first light, Septa Unella, Septa Moelle, Septa Scolera, four novices, and two silent sisters arrive. A silent sister shaves Cersei's head with shears, then soaps and razors the stubble. Cersei is then ordered to remove her shift, and the silent sister shaves her underarms, legs, and pubic hair.
4. **Given the robe** — A novice provides a soft white septa's robe to cover Cersei as she descends the tower stairs and passes through the sept. Cersei requests sandals but is denied by Septa Moelle.
5. **Procession through the Great Sept** — A bell tolls. Cersei descends rough stone steps and enters the Great Sept, crowded with faithful at dawn service. A sudden silence falls as a thousand eyes follow her down the aisle past where her father lay in state.
6. **Warrior's Sons escort assembles** — In the Hall of Lamps, a dozen Warrior's Sons in rainbow cloaks and silver plate await. Ser Theodan the True introduces himself as captain of the escort. Cersei spots Lancel among them and thinks "my blood and my betrayer."
7. **Exiting the Sept** — Two Warrior's Sons open the towering doors. Cersei steps into open air, blinking at sunlight. She breathes in the familiar stinks of King's Landing.
8. **Cersei recalls Ned Stark's execution** — Standing on the same marble steps, she remembers Joffrey commanding Stark's beheading against the plan, Janos Slynt lifting the severed head, and reflects that Joffrey and all Stark's sons are now dead.
9. **The septas' announcement** — Septa Unella declares Cersei a sinner. Septa Moelle announces the High Septon's command for atonement. Septa Scolera completes the ritual declaration.
10. **Cersei recalls Tytos's mistress** — She remembers stories of her father expelling Tytos's lowborn mistress and forcing her to walk naked through Lannisport; resolves she will not cringe as that woman did.
11. **Cersei disrobes** — She shrugs off the robe in one smooth motion, shivering violently. She fights the urge to cover herself, clenches her fists, nails digging into palms.
12. **Walk of atonement begins** — Cersei descends the marble steps naked. The Poor Fellows shove the crowd aside, the Warrior's Sons flank her, the septas follow with novices behind. Septa Scolera rings her bell chanting "shame."
13. **Crossing the plaza** — Cersei keeps her eyes on Aegon's High Hill and the Red Keep. Women in the crowd shout "whore." A rotted vegetable flies overhead. The baker's boy cries his wares.
14. **Descent of Visenya's Hill** — Marble gives way to cobblestones. The street narrows, crowds press closer. Cersei steps in something slick. Septa Unella catches her arm. A big burly man (resembling Robert) flings a meat skewer that hits her leg. The crowd shouts "whore," "sinner," "brotherfucker," "cunt," "traitor," and for Stannis or Margaery.
15. **Refuse thrown from above** — Half-rotted fruit, pails of beer, eggs that burst with sulfurous stink, and a dead cat that bursts open spattering Cersei with entrails and maggots.
16. **A drunkard's toast** — A man on a balcony calls "Queen Cunt" and lifts his cup in mocking toast, followed by "All hail the royal teats!"
17. **First fall** — Halfway down Visenya's Hill, Cersei's foot slips in what might be nightsoil. Septa Unella pulls her up; her knee is scraped and bloody. A man offers to "kiss it and make it better." Cersei has lost sight of the Red Keep.
18. **Ser Theodan drags her forward** — The captain tells her the crowd is growing unruly, yanks her arm, and pulls her along. Cersei stumbles downward, wishing Jaime were beside her with his golden sword.
19. **Sharp pain and protest** — Cersei's heel comes down on something sharp. She cries out, demands sandals from Septa Unella, and the knight wrenches her arm again.
20. **Bottom of the hill** — The slope gentles and street widens. Cersei sees the Red Keep again. She wrenches free of Ser Theodan, refuses to be dragged, and limps on, leaving bloody footprints.
21. **Continued abuse during the walk** — Men shout about their wives' "sweeter teats"; a whore lifts her skirts from a brothel window; a boy says "she's saggy as my mum"; a man in a butcher's apron exposes himself.
22. **Hallucinations begin** — As Cersei climbs Aegon's High Hill, she begins seeing familiar faces: a bald man with her father's frown; a young girl with Melara Hetherspoon's accusing eyes; Ned Stark with little Sansa and a shaggy grey dog; every squirming child becomes Tyrion jeering; Joffrey with golden curls and lovely lips.
23. **Second fall** — Cersei falls again after the hallucinations.
24. **The vision of Maggy the Frog** — After being pulled up, Cersei sees the hag standing in the crowd with pendulous teats, warty greenish skin, and crusty yellow eyes. The hag hisses: "Queen you shall be, until there comes another, younger and more beautiful, to cast you down and take all you hold most dear."
25. **Cersei breaks** — Tears burn down her cheeks. She covers her nipples and sex with her arms, begins running, then falls and begins crawling uphill on all fours as the crowd laughs and applauds.
26. **Arrival at the gates** — The crowd dissolves and the castle gates appear with spearmen in gilded halfhelms and crimson cloaks. Ser Boros Blount and Ser Meryn Trant stride toward her in Kingsguard plate. Cersei cries out for Tommen.
27. **Ser Kevan refuses to produce Tommen** — He says "no son should have to bear witness to his mother's shame" and orders her covered.
28. **Jocelyn wraps Cersei** — Jocelyn bends over her and wraps her in a soft clean green wool blanket.
29. **Ser Robert Strong carries Cersei** — A massive armored figure lifts Cersei off the ground easily. Eight feet tall or more, wearing white enameled plate over gilded mail with a greathelm streaming seven rainbow plumes.
30. **Qyburn introduces Ser Robert Strong** — Qyburn appears, says "it is so good to have you back," and presents "our newest member of the Kingsguard" as Ser Robert Strong, who has taken "a holy vow of silence" to not speak until all the king's enemies are dead and evil driven from the realm.
31. **Cersei whispers his name** — She says "Ser Robert" as they enter the gates and thinks "Oh, yes."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terror and dread before the walk; fierce Lannister pride ("I am a lioness, I will not cringe"); mounting humiliation and vulnerability as the walk proceeds; anger at the septas and the mob; desperate longing for Jaime; love and need for Tommen as anchor; creeping self-doubt about her beauty and authority ("What have I done?"); hallucinations near the end signal psychological disintegration; total breakdown upon seeing the Maggy the Frog vision; savage hope and satisfaction restored upon seeing Ser Robert Strong — "Oh, yes"
- **Primary preoccupation:** Surviving the walk with dignity intact; reaching Tommen and the Red Keep; securing her champion for trial by battle; maintaining the belief that she is beautiful and superior to the mob
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to proceed with the walk rather than refuse and insist on trial (weighing that she cannot trust the Faith to judge her fairly and must reach Tommen); resolves to show no weakness ("I am Cersei of House Lannister, a lion of the Rock"); consciously models herself as the inverse of Tytos's mistress — she will not beg or cringe
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself "words cannot harm me" and "I am blind and deaf" while clearly being wounded by every insult; insists "I am beautiful, the most beautiful woman in all Westeros" even as she catalogs her own aging body's flaws; tells herself the walk will be "a little walk" — minimizing the ordeal; blames Joffrey's execution of Stark entirely on Joffrey's disobedience while ignoring her own role; believes she is fundamentally different from and superior to the mob ("these creatures," "worms," "betters") even as their words devastate her; recalls that she "would have made Sansa a good marriage" while her motive was purely political hostage-taking
