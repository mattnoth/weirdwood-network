# ADWD — The King's Prize I

Includes flashbacks to the debate at Deepwood Motte before departure and Asha's earlier visit to her mother on Harlaw.
- **time_markers:** Golden dawn at departure; first day covers 22 miles; second day 24 miles; third day 14 miles, dusk scene; fourth day of march snow begins; fifth day of storm — frozen pond incident; ninth day of storm — cold count reports begin; fifteenth day — less than half distance covered; twentieth day — Asha freed from ankle chains; twenty-sixth day — last vegetables consumed; thirty-second day — last grain and fodder consumed; "three days from Winterfell" per Benjicot Branch's reckoning near end; "first light" planned departure from crofters' village; final scene: dawn, sky black to white, army snowbound. "Fifteen days" was the knights' original estimate. "One hundred leagues from Deepwood Motte to Winterfell. Three hundred miles as the raven flies."
- **chapter_summary:** Stannis Baratheon's host departs Deepwood Motte for Winterfell with Asha Greyjoy as a chained prisoner in the baggage train, guarded by Alysane Mormont. The march begins well but a snowstorm starting on the fourth day progressively destroys the column — horses die, men freeze, provisions run out, and the southron forces fracture against the hardier northmen. Asha attempts to negotiate her freedom with Stannis, offering her men and Dagmer Cleftjaw's garrison at Torrhen's Square, but Stannis dismisses her. The queen's men push for human sacrifice to end the storm; Stannis refuses. After more than thirty days of a march planned for fifteen, the army halts at an abandoned crofters' village between two frozen lakes and becomes snowbound.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Golden dawn at departure with morning frost on the grass. Snow begins on the fourth day of march as small flurries, then falls continuously and worsens day after day. Wind becomes a cruel lash driving snow before it. The storm swallows sun, moon, and stars for days. By the end, heavy snowfall buries the camp overnight, sealing tents shut with walls of snow.
- **Season indicators:** Big Bucket Wull declares "This is no winter. Up in the hills we say that autumn kisses you, but winter fucks you hard. This is only autumn's kiss." Characters discuss winter approaching. Asha thinks "Winter is almost upon us, boy. And winter is death" (quoting Wull).
- **Time of day:** Opens at dawn (golden dawn). Third day ends at dusk. Multiple night scenes around campfires and in the king's tent. Final scene opens at dawn with the sky turning from black to white but no brighter. Trumpets normally sound at dawn to rouse the camp — their absence at the end signals something wrong.
- **Lighting:** Rising sun glitters off plate and mail at departure. Nightfire roars outside the king's tent each evening, providing orange glow. Candle flame in the king's tent. Beacon fire burning atop the watchtower at the crofters' village appears as a fuzzy orange glow through the storm. Lightbringer's glow lights up the night. As the march deteriorates, fewer fires burn and those that exist throw more smoke than heat.
- **Sounds:** Trumpets at departure. Asha's chains clacking with every movement. The She-Bear's snoring (louder than any man). Queen's men praying and singing around the nightfire. Silence at the end — no trumpets, no warhorns — signals the army has stopped.
- **Smells:** Not explicitly described.
- **Notable sensory details:** Spearpoints shining in the rising sun. Cold that "drives men mad." Asha's ankle gives stabbing pain with every step. Fetters chafe her wrists. Knee-high and then waist-high snowdrifts. Ice cracking beneath wagons over a frozen pond. Freezing water swallowing men and horses. Harwood Fell's lips turn blue, skin pale as milk, shivering violently. The column becomes "snowmen." Food eaten cold, even raw. Asha's feet go numb within an hour of walking.

## Events & Actions
1. **Army departs Deepwood Motte at dawn** — The host uncoils from behind the log palisades: southron knights in plate and mail, squires and servants, clansmen from the hills, baggage train, rear guard with outriders.
2. **Asha rides as prisoner in the baggage train** — Fettered at wrist and ankle in a covered wagon, guarded by Alysane Mormont (the She-Bear). Stannis intends to display her in chains at Winterfell.
3. **Southron lords boast about Robert** — Lord Fell claims Robert would have reached Winterfell in ten days. Justin Massey warns not to mention Robert to Stannis.
4. **Asha recalls her submission at Deepwood Motte** — She yielded to Stannis, asking only that her surviving men be spared. Nine ironborn survived. Stannis gave them their lives.
5. **Asha assesses Stannis and her own political value** — She considers herself worthless as a hostage since Euron won't pay ransom and her husband Eric Ironmaker lacks coin. Notes Stannis's discomfort with women.
6. **Ser Justin Massey courts Asha** — He visits her wagon repeatedly with food and tidings. Alysane says "He wants you." Asha judges he wants the Iron Islands through her. She tolerates his attention for the company.
7. **Flashback: war council at Deepwood Motte** — Northmen (Morgan Liddle, Brandon Norrey, Big Bucket Wull, the Flints, the She-Bear) insist on marching to Winterfell. Southron knights (Justin Massey, Corliss Penny) oppose. Massey cites Winterfell's double walls and lack of provisions for a siege. Harwood Fell says Arnolf Karstark and Mors Umber will join. Stannis declares: "I am not Robert. But we will march, and we will free Winterfell … or die in the attempt."
8. **First two days of march proceed well** — 22 miles the first day, 24 miles the second, passing beyond Glover lands into the wolfswood. Queen's men pray to R'hllor around the nightfire.
9. **Third day: pace slows** — Forest presses close, roads dwindle to game trails too narrow for large wagons. Familiar landmarks from Asha's earlier journey to persuade Theon. Only 14 miles covered.
10. **Third evening: Asha dines in king's tent** — Ser Justin removes her ankle fetters for the walk. She goes to one knee before Stannis and begs to have her chains removed. Offers her men, ships, wits, and Dagmer Cleftjaw's garrison at Torrhen's Square. Stannis dismisses Torrhen's Square as worthless and rejects her with: "The gods did not make you a man. How can I?"
11. **Justin warns Asha never to mention Robert to Stannis** — After Asha's failed appeal referenced Robert's way with former enemies.
12. **Asha recalls her mother at Ten Towers** — Lady Alannys, ruined by the loss of her sons, asked "Did you bring my baby boy?" Asha doubts Theon will survive whatever happens at Winterfell.
13. **Asha and Alysane bond cautiously** — Alysane reveals she has five sisters (Dacey murdered at the Red Wedding, Lyanna on Bear Island, Lyra and Jory with their mother), a son of two, and a daughter of nine. Claims her children were "fathered by a bear" and jokes about Mormont women being skinchangers. Turns cold: "What we are is what you made us."
14. **Snow begins on the fourth day** — Small flurries at first, then continuous, worsening snowfall for days. Wind becomes a lash. Army becomes a column of snowmen.
15. **Northern clansmen outpace the southerners** — Garrons fare better than destriers in snow. Northmen don bear-paws (snowshoes) and walk atop the snow. Southron horses refuse to wear them. The column fractures: northmen pull ahead, baggage train falls behind.
16. **Fifth day of storm: frozen pond disaster** — Baggage train crosses waist-high snowdrifts concealing a frozen pond. Ice cracks: three teamsters and four horses swallowed by freezing water, plus two rescuers. Harwood Fell is pulled out but his lips turn blue, skin pale as milk. He shivers for hours and dies that night.
17. **Queen's men begin calling for sacrifice** — Ser Corliss Penny says the gods of the north sent the storm. Ser Godry insists they're false gods. Clayton Suggs says R'hllor is with them. Justin Massey notes Melisandre is not. Stannis says nothing but hears.
18. **Richard Horpe speaks for the king** — Declares "The storm must break soon."
19. **Storm worsens; army deteriorates** — Tents collapse under snow. Dry wood scarce despite being in a forest. Fires smoke more than heat. Two stormlands squires stab a man-at-arms in a quarrel over fire proximity. Archers set their tent afire. Destriers die of exhaustion and exposure, are butchered for meat. Five miles becomes a good day, then three, then two.
20. **Ninth day of storm: cold count begins** — Captains report losses nightly. "One man dead, three missing." "Six horses lost." "Two dead men, one a knight. Four horses down."
21. **Queen's men push Stannis harder for sacrifice** — Clayton Suggs: "A sacrifice will prove our faith still burns true." Godry Farring: "Only R'hllor can end it. We must give him an unbeliever." Stannis refuses: "Half my army is made up of unbelievers. I will have no burnings. Pray harder."
22. **Asha prays to the Drowned God** — More fervently than ever the Damphair had.
23. **Fifteenth day: less than half the distance covered** — A trail of broken wagons and frozen corpses stretches behind them.
24. **Twentieth day: Asha freed from ankle chains** — One of her wagon horses dies. No replacement available. Justin Massey removes her ankle fetters and tells her she must walk. Her ankle throbs with every step. She falls asleep at supper from exhaustion.
25. **Twenty-sixth day: last vegetables consumed.**
26. **Thirty-second day: last grain and fodder consumed.**
27. **Richard Horpe reports three days to Winterfell** — Benjicot Branch's estimate. Corliss Penny suggests leaving the weakest behind. Horpe says the weakest are beyond saving.
28. **Lord Peasebury turns against the northmen** — Calls the march madness; "More dying every day, and for what? Some girl?" Morgan Liddle and Big Bucket Wull retort: "Ned's girl." Peasebury accuses Wull of being Bolton's creature. Wull laughs in his face and gives a rousing speech about wanting to die fighting for Ned's girl and bathing in Bolton blood.
29. **The northmen cheer Wull's speech** — "Blood and battle!" — banging cups and drinking horns on the table.
30. **Army reaches the crofters' village** — Scouts find it between two lakes: a few huts, a longhall, a watchtower. Richard Horpe commands a halt after advancing barely half a mile. Horpe tells the king there are fish in the lakes.
31. **Stannis orders fish but plans to march at first light** — "Fish, then. But we march at first light." His appearance is skeletal, a man with one foot in the grave.
32. **Army becomes snowbound** — Dawn brings snow and silence. No trumpets, no warhorns. The lakes, woods, and watchtower have vanished in the blizzard. Only shapes of tents and the fuzzy orange glow of the beacon fire visible. Stannis's host sits unmoving, walled in by ice and snow, starving.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitter but pragmatic acceptance of captivity. Alternates between defiance (imagining throttling Stannis with her chains), political calculation (tolerating Massey's courtship for advantage), grief (remembering her mother's ruin), and dark resignation (welcoming the idea of a final battle to end the misery). Throughout, she maintains a wry, sardonic inner voice.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and freedom — negotiating release from her chains, assessing her worth as a hostage (concluding it is nil), evaluating whether Stannis can actually reach and take Winterfell. Secondary preoccupation: her brother Theon's likely fate.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to submit and kneel to Stannis (recalling Balon's philosophy). Decides to offer Dagmer's garrison at Torrhen's Square as a bargaining chip. Decides not to discourage Justin Massey's courtship despite judging it ultimately futile.
- **Self-deception flags:** Asha tells Stannis she can "deliver" Torrhen's Square and Dagmer's garrison but internally adds "Perhaps" — she overstates her influence to Stannis. She tells herself the cold will numb her ankle pain and is "only part wrong."
