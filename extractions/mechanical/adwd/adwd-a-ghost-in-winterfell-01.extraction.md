# ADWD — A Ghost in Winterfell I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 47
- **pov_character:** Theon Greyjoy
- **pov_chapter_number:** "A Ghost in Winterfell"
- **first_available:** ADWD A Ghost in Winterfell
- **location_primary:** Winterfell
- **location_secondary:** Godswood (Winterfell), Great Hall (Winterfell), Battlements Gate, Hunter's Gate
- **approximate_timeline:** During the siege/occupation of Winterfell by Bolton forces, while Stannis's army approaches through a snowstorm
- **time_markers:** "the black of night" (when man fell from wall); "broke its fast that morning"; "half the day was gone"; "the watch had just changed" with midday meal; "by eventide"; "supper"; "the hour of the wolf"; multiple days pass within the chapter (at least 3-4 days of deaths); drumming heard at night
- **chapter_summary:** A series of mysterious deaths plague the Bolton-held Winterfell as a relentless snowstorm buries the castle. Theon observes the growing tensions between the northern lords, Freys, and Boltons as suspicion and distrust spread. Roose Bolton interrogates Theon about the killings but dismisses him as a suspect. Theon prays to the old gods in the godswood, begging for death as Theon rather than Reek, and the weirwood appears to whisper "Bran." Abel's washerwomen find him and demand he come sing for Abel.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Endless, ceaseless snowfall day and night; drifts climbing the walls and filling crenels; wind cold enough to cause frostbite; snow so heavy Theon cannot see more than three feet ahead; "a cold black sky"
- **Season indicators:** Deep winter; "Let winter do its worst"; snow burying everything; references to spring being far off ("might have stayed buried till spring")
- **Time of day:** Spans multiple days and nights; morning (breaking fast), midday meal, supper/eventide, "the hour of the wolf" (deep night), another night in the godswood
- **Lighting:** Dark nights ("black of night"); torch flaming smokily in the hall; glowing braziers in guard turrets; "dim lights floating in the dark" on the outer wall
- **Sounds:** Music from the hall (Abel singing); warhorn — "a long low moan"; drumming "BOOM doom BOOM doom"; weirwood leaves rustling; horses nickering; men whispering "Stannis"
- **Smells:** Reek of the Great Hall — wet dog, wet wool, sodden horse blankets, horseshit, dog turds, human feces; godswood — moss, mud, and decay; steam from hot pools
- **Notable sensory details:** Snowflakes "brushed his cheeks like cold soft kisses"; warm tears on Theon's cheeks in the godswood; icy tears of melting snow on the battlements; excruciating pain from Theon's ruined teeth when chewing; floors slimy with mud and melting snow

## Events & Actions
1. **Dead man found at base of inner wall** — A man-at-arms (44 years old) of Roger Ryswell's found with broken neck, half-eaten by Ramsay's dog Grey Jeyne. Roger Ryswell calls it a drunken fall.
2. **Freerider speaks favorably of Stannis** — A foolish freerider suggests Stannis's sorceress could melt the snows. Overheard by Yellow Dick, Sour Alyn, and Ben Bones.
3. **Ramsay punishes the freerider** — Bastard's Boys seize him; Damon Dance-for-Me whips him; Skinner and Yellow Dick make wagers; he is thrown from the Battlements Gate wall (eighty feet). He survives the fall into snowdrifts, crawls away with a broken leg, and is shot with an arrow.
4. **Whoresbane Umber japes** — Suggests the freerider will be "sucking Lord Stannis's cock before the sun goes down."
5. **Holly approaches Theon** — One of Abel's washerwomen sits beside him at supper, asks to see the crypts, implies Abel sent her, hints at wanting to know how Theon took the castle.
6. **Theon refuses Holly** — Deduces Abel wants to know how to escape Winterfell; tells her to leave him be.
7. **Theon walks the walls at night** — Considers jumping from where the freerider was thrown but decides against it; fears Ramsay's dogs hunting him.
8. **Ser Aenys Frey's squire found dead** — Naked, dead of exposure in the old castle lichyard, face obscured by hoarfrost. Officially blamed on drunkenness.
9. **Crossbowman sworn to Flints found dead** — In the stables with a broken skull. Ramsay declares it a horse kick; Theon thinks it was a club.
10. **Theon reflects on parallels** — Current murders mirror his own time holding Winterfell, with Bolton playing Theon's old role and the dead men playing roles of Aggar, Gynir Rednose, and Gelmarr.
11. **Quarrel in the Great Hall** — Ser Hosteen Frey demands they ride out against Stannis; Harwood Stout objects; Manderly offers to ride with Hosteen, then they nearly come to blows over the missing Freys. Roger Ryswell and Barbrey Dustin calm them.
12. **Roose Bolton shows unease** — Theon observes a look in Bolton's pale eyes he has never seen before — "an uneasiness, even a hint of fear."
13. **New stable collapses** — Twenty-six horses and two grooms die. Bolton orders remaining horses brought inside.
14. **Yellow Dick found murdered** — Castrated, genitals stuffed in his mouth (breaking three teeth), found outside the kitchens buried in snow. Bolton orders the body burned and silence. Ramsay spreads the news anyway, offers a golden dragon for the killer's name.
15. **Horsemeat feast** — Dead horses butchered and served; for once all eat equally.
16. **Abel sings for the hall** — Performs "Iron Lances," "The Winter Maid," "The Queen Took Off Her Sandal, the King Took Off His Crown," "The Bear and the Maiden Fair," and later "The Maids That Bloom in Spring."
17. **Bastard's Boys taunt Theon** — Damon tells Theon that Ramsay means to cut his lips off; they call him Reek and mock his smell.
18. **Theon walks the battlements at the hour of the wolf** — Wrapped in wool and fur, trying to exhaust himself to sleep.
19. **Stannis's warhorn sounds** — A long low moan from beyond the walls, followed by drumming (BOOM doom BOOM doom). The name "Stannis" passes from man to man.
20. **Theon reflects on his fate** — Either Baratheon or Bolton means death; Jon Snow would behead him.
21. **Bolton interrogates Theon** — In Eddard Stark's solar with Lady Dustin, Roger Ryswell, and Ser Aenys Frey. They examine his maimed hands; Lady Dustin and Ryswell clear him as the killer.
22. **Lady Dustin declares "the north remembers"** — Lists houses that lost men at the Red Wedding: Manderly, Umber, Hornwood, Tallhart, Flint, Cerwyn, Ryswell, Dustin.
23. **Theon prays in the godswood** — Begs the old gods for a sword and to die as Theon, not Reek. A leaf falls like a bloody hand. The weirwood seems to whisper "Bran." Theon sees Bran's face in the tree and confesses about the miller's boys.
24. **Washerwomen confront Theon in the godswood** — Holly, Rowan, and a third unnamed woman find him. They reveal they are the killers ("It was you"). Holly draws a blade. Rowan promises him death as Theon but says he must first sing for Abel.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Despair, resignation, self-loathing; occasional flickers of dark humor; terror of Ramsay but desire for death; brief peace in the snow; anguished guilt at the weirwood
- **Primary preoccupation:** The murders and who is responsible; his own identity crisis (Theon vs. Reek); desire to die as himself rather than as Reek; the impossibility of escape
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses Holly's invitation and Abel's offer initially; decides not to jump from the wall; ultimately acquiesces when the washerwomen find him in the godswood
- **Self-deception flags:** "I was fond of the boy, had never done him any harm" (about Bran) — while simultaneously confessing to killing innocent boys in Bran's place; "I … I asked it of him" (about mutilation, repeating Ramsay's conditioning); insists "I'm not" a kinslayer while knowing what he did
