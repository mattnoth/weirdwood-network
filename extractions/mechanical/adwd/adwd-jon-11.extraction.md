- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow concludes negotiations with Tormund Giantsbane beyond the Wall, agreeing to let four thousand free folk pass through in exchange for their valuables and one hundred boy hostages. Tormund surrenders his gold armbands as first payment and shares grief over his dead sons Dormund and Torwynd. Jon returns to Castle Black with Val, who had found Tormund for him, and they meet Queen Selyse, who demands the wildlings accept Stannis as king and R'hllor as god—terms Jon refuses. Val expresses horror at Princess Shireen's greyscale, calling it "the grey death." Jon then summons his officers atop the Wall at sunset and informs them of the agreement, facing strong opposition from Bowen Marsh, Othell Yarwyck, and others before assigning preparations.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon and Tormund negotiate terms** — A long, heated negotiation in Tormund's tent. Tormund bellows, curses, and flings his drinking horn but ultimately agrees to Jon's terms.
2. **Tormund clasps Jon's hand** — The deal is struck. Tormund warns some of his own people will spit on him for these terms.
3. **Tormund shares grief over his dead sons** — Dormund was killed in the battle for the Wall by a knight in grey steel with moths on his shield. Torwynd died of cold and rose as a wight; Tormund had to kill his own reanimated son.
4. **Tormund mentions Munda's marriage** — His daughter married Longspear Ryk.
5. **Tormund gives Jon his gold armbands** — Ancient First Men armbands engraved with runes, inherited from his father. First payment of the toll. Jon suggests Tormund keep them but Tormund refuses.
6. **Jon, Val, and companions depart Tormund's camp** — Jon whistles for Ghost. Mormont's raven appears. Ghost emerges from the trees with Val beside him.
7. **Jon observes Val's appearance** — She is clad entirely in white with a weirwood face brooch. Jon thinks she looks lovely.
8. **Tormund teases Jon and Val** — Suggests Jon steal Val before Toregg does. Jon declines, citing his vows. Val threatens to geld him.
9. **Jon observes the free folk camp** — Sees gaunt, starving women and children outnumbering fighting men three to one. Notes signs of sickness. Herds gone except mammoths.
10. **Jon worries about those at Hardhome** — Thinks Cotter Pyke should reach them soon with his fleet.
11. **Jon tells Val about her lodging** — Hardin's Tower top floor, with Wun Wun in the entry hall. Val not to be troubled by unwanted visitors.
12. **Jon and Val arrive south of the Wall** — Ulmer of the Kingswood asks on behalf of the brothers: peace or blood? Jon answers peace.
13. **Jon gives orders to Satin** — Requests parchment, quills, maester's black for letters; summons Marsh, Yarwyck, Cellador, Clydas, Flint, Norrey, and Leathers.
14. **Jon and Val visit Queen Selyse in the King's Tower** — Ser Patrek commands the queen's guard; kisses Val's glove. Ghost left outside. Patchface dances and sings. Shireen is present.
15. **Shireen and Val exchange words** — Shireen says she is a princess too and mentions a bastard cousin who sailed away.
16. **Jon informs Selyse of the agreement** — Selyse gives a tiny nod, says Stannis wished to grant sanctuary.
17. **Selyse demands wildlings accept Stannis and R'hllor** — Jon refuses; Val says free folk do not kneel. Selyse threatens Jon will answer to Stannis. Jon asks leave and is dismissed.
18. **Val expresses horror at Shireen's greyscale** — Calls it "the grey death"; says north of the Wall it is always mortal; says the child "is not clean" and wants the baby ("the monster") moved away from Shireen. Jon pushes back.
19. **Jon ascends the Wall via winch cage with Leathers** — Cage sways in the wind; ice crystals shower down. Jon passes sentries Ty and Owen.
20. **Jon gazes north from the Wall** — Thinks of Mance and Arya; wonders if Mance found her. Remembers Arya Underfoot and Needle. Thinks of Sam making him Lord Commander.
21. **Officers ascend the Wall** — Flint, Norrey, Bowen Marsh, Othell Yarwyck, Septon Cellador arrive.
22. **Jon announces the agreement** — Gate opens in three days at dawn. Four thousand wildlings coming through.
23. **Officers voice opposition** — Marsh warns wildlings will outnumber them three to one; calls it treason. Yarwyck objects to the Weeper. Cellador calls them godless savages. Norrey and Flint demand wildlings stay off their lands.
24. **Leathers defends the arrangement** — Notes he was on the other side of the battle and now wears the black; says free folk have the same gods as Winterfell.
25. **Jon reveals the hostage terms** — One hundred boys aged eight to sixteen as pages and squires. Old Flint recognizes the tradition from Winterfell's "wards."
26. **Jon recites his Night's Watch oath** — Argues "the shield that guards the realms of men" includes wildlings: "what are these wildlings, if not men?" Marsh is silenced.
27. **Jon assigns preparations** — Clydas to tend the sick; Yarwyck to ready carts and wagons; Marsh to collect the tolls and get valuables to Eastwatch.
28. **Jon recalls Melisandre's prophecy** — Thinks of her words: "Ice, and daggers in the dark. Blood frozen red and hard, and naked steel." His sword hand flexes. The wind rises.
