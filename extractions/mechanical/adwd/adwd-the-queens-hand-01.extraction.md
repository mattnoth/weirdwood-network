# ADWD — The Queen's Hand I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 71
- **pov_character:** Barristan Selmy
- **pov_chapter_number:** The Queen's Hand I
- **first_available:** ADWD The Queen's Hand I
- **location_primary:** Great Pyramid of Meereen
- **location_secondary:** Meereen (city), pyramid of Hazkar, pyramid of Yherizan, pyramid of Uhlez, Daznak's Pit, Pit of Ghrazz, the Golden Pit
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Daenerys's disappearance on Drogon from Daznak's Pit; Quentyn Martell's failed dragon-taming attempt was three days prior
- **time_markers:** "three days dying"; Quentyn dies "just before first light" at dawn; "a thin red slash marked the eastern horizon where the sun might soon appear"; Sons of the Harpy resumed killings "two days ago" — three murders first night, nine the second, twenty-nine the third; council takes "the rest of the morning and most of the afternoon"; rain stops by evening; "the setting sun" behind clouds; candles lit when Green Grace arrives; night falls
- **chapter_summary:** Quentyn Martell dies at dawn from dragon-fire burns sustained three days earlier, tended by Missandei. Barristan Selmy, reluctantly serving as Queen's Hand in Daenerys's absence, convenes a war council with commanders of Meereen's diverse military forces to plan a potential attack on the besieging Yunkai'i, using a beacon signal. He visits the imprisoned Dornishmen Archibald Yronwood and Gerris Drinkwater, learns that Quentyn promised Pentos to the Tattered Prince, and sends them as envoys to negotiate the release of Meereen's hostages. The Green Grace Galazza Galare returns from the Yunkish camp with their refusal of ransom, and the chapter ends with the Yunkai'i trebuchets launching corpses into the city.

## Events & Actions
1. **Quentyn Martell dies** — The Dornish prince takes his last breath at dawn after three days of agony from dragon-fire burns, in the queen's own bed in the Great Pyramid.
2. **Missandei departs** — Barristan dismisses Missandei, who has been tending Quentyn night and day, telling her to rest.
3. **Barristan examines Quentyn's body** — He peels back the coverlet for a final look; the prince's flesh has sloughed away, skull visible, eyes are pools of pus.
4. **Barristan reaffirms Daenerys lives** — He speaks aloud that Daenerys was riding the dragon, fighting his own doubts as each day makes it harder to believe.
5. **Skahaz arrives with city report** — Reports gates closed, sellswords and Yunkai'i being hunted inside the city, Unsullied manning walls, two hundred highborn demanding audience, corpses still being pulled from Hazkar, Great Masters of Yherizan and Uhlez have abandoned their pyramids.
6. **Sons of the Harpy kill twenty-nine** — Escalation from three (first night) to nine (second) to twenty-nine (third night). All victims are freedmen and shavepates. Graffiti messages left: "Dragons must die," "Harghaz the Hero," "Death to Daenerys."
7. **Blood tax announced** — Twenty-nine hundred gold pieces demanded from each pyramid.
8. **Skahaz demands hostage executions** — Barristan refuses again, as he has a hundred times before.
9. **War council convenes** — Barristan meets with commanders from all military factions around a round table in the pillared hall. He announces Quentyn's death.
10. **Discussion of Dornish prisoners** — Council debates the fate of Yronwood and Drinkwater; Spotted Cat wants them in the pits; Barristan refuses.
11. **Barristan announces diplomatic strategy** — He has sent the Green Grace to negotiate ransom for hostages (each man's weight in gold), designed to drive a wedge between Yunkai'i masters and their sellswords. Credits Missandei for suggesting the ploy.
12. **Barristan reveals battle plan** — A beacon atop the pyramid will signal the attack; all commanders must be ready at all times. He presents maps of enemy positions.
13. **Council debates tactics all day** — Arguments over: marching on Yunkai (Tal Toraq), single combat (Spotted Cat/Belwas), river flanking with ships (Camarron), deployment of Unsullied (Widower wants center thrust, Marselen wants flanks, Symon wants them split among freedmen), use of elephants.
14. **Grey Worm warns about hostages** — After others leave, he privately warns Barristan the Yunkai'i will kill hostages when Meereen attacks. Barristan says he has "a notion."
15. **Barristan visits Dornish prisoners** — Takes knights Tum and the Red Lamb as escort; goes to dungeons.
16. **Gerris Drinkwater rages against Daenerys** — Blames her for Quentyn's death; says she spurned him and "went off to fuck her sellsword."
17. **Barristan confronts the Dornishmen** — Declares Quentyn came for dragons not love; accuses Doran Martell of planning war against the Iron Throne.
18. **Yronwood and Drinkwater recount the dragon-taming attempt** — Describe the broken chains, Quentyn's fear, a crossbowman firing and enraging the dragons, the Windblown fleeing, Quentyn burning.
19. **Pentos revelation** — Barristan correctly guesses Quentyn promised Pentos to the Tattered Prince; Yronwood confirms they signed a written agreement.
20. **Barristan recruits the Dornishmen** — Offers to return them to Dorne with Quentyn's bones if they deliver a message to the Tattered Prince: Meereen will honor the Pentos promise in exchange for the safe return of hostages. They agree.
21. **Cupbearers play spinning-dagger game** — Six cupbearers sit in a circle spinning a dagger, cutting hair from whoever it points at. Barristan recalls playing a similar game at Harvest Hall.
22. **Barristan drinks watered wine on the terrace** — Watches the setting sun and sights Viserion in the distance. Reflects on his doubts about being Hand and reads about Kingsguard who served as Hand in the White Book.
23. **Green Grace arrives** — Galazza Galare returns from the Yunkai'i with four Pink Graces. She reports the Yunkai'i refused ransom — "only the blood of dragons" will free the hostages.
24. **Green Grace advocates for Hizdahr** — Claims the gods of Ghis have told her Hizdahr is innocent of the poisoned locusts. Describes Meereen's collapse: refugees fleeing, pyramids destroyed, people turning to drunkenness and fornication.
25. **Green Grace declares Daenerys dead** — Says "Let her dragons die as well," weeping behind her veils.
26. **Yunkai'i trebuchets fire** — Skahaz bursts in to report all six trebuchets are firing. The Green Grace reveals they are launching not stones but corpses.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Exhausted, full of doubt, burdened by a role he never wanted. Grieving for Quentyn ("just a boy"). Anxious about Daenerys — "every day that passed made it harder to believe" she was alive. Oddly relieved when diplomacy fails and war becomes inevitable: "War he understood."
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether Daenerys is alive and will return; whether he is making the right decisions as Hand; planning the military defense of Meereen; securing the return of hostages through the Tattered Prince.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to execute hostages (Meereenese or Dornish); convenes war council and presents battle plan with beacon signal; sends Yronwood and Drinkwater to the Tattered Prince with an offer of Pentos for the hostages; decides on military action when diplomacy fails.
- **Self-deception flags:** Repeatedly tells himself Daenerys is alive despite mounting evidence ("He had said the same a hundred times before … but every day that passed made it harder to believe"). Tells himself he is not the Hand while functioning as Hand. Privately doubts whether he is doing the right things but projects confidence publicly.
