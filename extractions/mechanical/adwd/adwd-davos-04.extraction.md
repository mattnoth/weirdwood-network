# ADWD — Davos IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 30
- **pov_character:** Davos Seaworth
- **pov_chapter_number:** Davos IV
- **first_available:** ADWD Davos IV
- **location_primary:** Wolf's Den, White Harbor
- **location_secondary:** New Castle (White Harbor), Merman's Court (referenced, heard from adjacent room), Seal Gate (referenced), Winterfell (referenced), Deepwood Motte (referenced), the Dreadfort (referenced), Moat Cailin (referenced), Barrowton (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** After the fall of Moat Cailin and the return of Wylis Manderly to White Harbor; during the welcoming feast for Wylis; Stannis has already taken Deepwood Motte
- **time_markers:** Dawn; morning (porridge not yet delivered); "the morning was creeping past"; Davos has been confined for an extended period (multiple days/weeks implied by habitual descriptions and multiple letters written); Manderly says he has been building warships "for more than a year"; night and morning referenced as routine cycles of imprisonment
- **chapter_summary:** Davos Seaworth wakes in his cell in the Wolf's Den, anxious that his routine has been disrupted. He reviews the farewell letters he has written to his wife and surviving sons. Robett Glover arrives and leads him through secret passages to a hidden room in the New Castle, where Wyman Manderly reveals that Davos is officially dead — another man's head and hands were mounted above the Seal Gate in his place. Manderly explains that his hostility at the Merman's Court was a mummer's farce performed to protect his captive son Wylis, and delivers an impassioned speech about the Red Wedding, the treachery of the Freys and Boltons, and White Harbor's enduring loyalty to the Starks. Manderly pledges his considerable forces to Stannis on one condition: Davos must smuggle back one of Ned Stark's surviving sons, whose location is known to Wex, Theon Greyjoy's mute former squire.

- **Weather:** Not described (Davos is confined indoors)
- **Season indicators:** Ser Bartimus's tale references "a long cruel winter" historically; Davos recalls what he saw at Eastwatch-by-the-Sea and that he "did not care to know winter"
- **Time of day:** Dawn at chapter opening; morning progresses during confinement; time unclear during the meeting with Manderly (feast is underway, so likely evening)
- **Lighting:** "Gloom of the Wolf's Den"; greasy tallow candle in cell; Glover lights a torch in the cellars that blazes "red and hot"; beeswax candles burn on the table in Manderly's meeting room
- **Sounds:** Faint, muffled sounds through cell door at opening; pipes and fiddles playing nearby during the Manderly meeting; murmur of many voices, clatter of cups and platters from the feast
- **Smells:** Sleeping pallet smells of mildew; the damp cells in the lower levels are "foul-smelling"
- **Notable sensory details:** Seawater sloshing beneath feet in the cellar passage; weeping walls crusted white with salt; the warmth and comfort of Manderly's meeting room contrasted with the cold passage; splinters in the warped plank floor of Davos's cell

## Events & Actions
1. **Davos wakes in the Wolf's Den** — senses something is wrong; dawn has come but Garth has not brought the usual morning porridge
2. **Davos reflects on his imprisonment** — recalls Manderly's public sentence of death, Garth's threats with Lady Lu and the Whore, the unexpectedly comfortable conditions, interactions with his three keepers
3. **Ser Bartimus recounts the Wolf's Den's history** — (recalled) tells Davos of King Jon Stark building it, the Greystarks, the various houses that held it, the slavers, and Brandon Ice Eyes' retribution
4. **Davos reads his farewell letters** — reviews letters to his wife Marya and three surviving sons (Devan, Steffon, young Stannis), reflecting on his life and failures
5. **Robett Glover arrives at Davos's cell** — introduces himself, announces that Deepwood Motte has been retaken by Stannis, Moat Cailin has fallen, Bolton has returned north with "Ned Stark's younger daughter," and Bolton summons lords to Barrowton
6. **Davos asks that his letters be delivered if he dies** — Glover gives his word and assures Davos he will not die by Glover or Manderly's hand
7. **Glover leads Davos through the Wolf's Den** — they pass through a darkened hall, down steps, across the godswood with its massive heart tree, through a rusted iron gate, down into barrel-vaulted salt-crusted cellars with seawater on the floor
8. **They pass through the secret passage** — Glover pushes open a blank stone wall; they traverse a long narrow tunnel beneath the Castle Stair up to the New Castle
9. **Davos meets Wyman Manderly in secret** — they emerge into a warm, comfortable room adjacent to the Merman's Court where the welcoming feast is underway
10. **Manderly reveals Davos is officially dead** — another man's tarred head and shortened-finger hands were mounted above the Seal Gate with an onion in his mouth; the substitute was a criminal
11. **Manderly explains the mummer's farce** — his hostility at the Merman's Court was theater to satisfy the Freys while Wylis was captive; Wylla spoke bravely from the heart; Wynafryd knew and played along; Lady Leona spoke genuinely
12. **Manderly describes Tywin Lannister's threats** — Tywin wrote personally demanding fealty, surrender of White Harbor, allegiance to Bolton as Warden, or Wylis would die and White Harbor would suffer like the Reynes of Castamere
13. **Manderly reveals Frey espionage** — Symond Frey has bribed several servants and two knights; a handmaid has infiltrated the fool's bed; Maester Theomore is a Lannister of Lannisport and cannot be trusted
14. **Manderly delivers "the north remembers" speech** — passionately denounces the Red Wedding murder of his son Wendel, the Frey lies about wargs, Bolton's lies about Winterfell; declares "the mummer's farce is almost done"
15. **Glover and Manderly reveal truth about Winterfell** — Ramsay, not Theon, put Winterfell to the sword and killed Ser Rodrik during a parley; Ramsay spared the women to march them to the Dreadfort as hunting prey
16. **Manderly describes Ramsay's cruelty** — hunts women naked with hounds; rapes, flays, and feeds corpses to dogs; forced Lady Hornwood into marriage then starved her
17. **Manderly pledges his forces conditionally** — has been building warships for over a year; commands more heavy horse than any lord north of the Neck; can deliver allegiance of all lands east of the White Knife including Widow's Watch, Oldcastle, Ramsgate, Sheepshead Hills, and the headwaters of the Broken Branch
18. **Manderly names his price** — not a king but a smuggler; needs Davos specifically
19. **Wex is brought in** — the mute ironborn boy carves his name (W-E-X) into a wooden beam with a dagger
20. **Glover reveals Wex was Theon's squire at Winterfell** — was present during the fall
21. **Wex's testimony is communicated** — he hid in the heart tree during Bolton's slaughter; heard voices below; saw six people (including two boys and two wolves); four went one way, two another (a woman and a boy); Wex followed the two
22. **Manderly states his price explicitly** — "Smuggle me back my liege lord, and I will take Stannis Baratheon as my king"; the direwolf will prove the boy's identity
23. **Wex throws the dagger at the map** — it sticks quivering in the sheepskin map of the north, indicating the destination
24. **Davos contemplates the mission** — reaches instinctively for his lost fingerbones; considers the destination is a place where people eat human flesh

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious and fearful at the start (disrupted routine, expectation of execution); resigned to death but resolved to face it with dignity; reflective and sorrowful while reading his letters; wary and confused when Glover arrives; increasingly astonished as Manderly's true intentions unfold; horrified by descriptions of Ramsay's cruelty; apprehensive and dread-filled at the chapter's end regarding his mission destination
- **Primary preoccupation:** At first, whether today is the day he dies; transitions to processing Manderly's revelations and the weight of the mission being asked of him
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts Glover's summons despite uncertainty; implicitly accepts Manderly's mission (does not refuse, though he questions why him); asks that his letters be delivered if he dies
- **Self-deception flags:** Davos tells himself "I did not do so ill" but his letters reveal deep self-doubt and regret; he describes himself as a better smuggler than knight, better knight than Hand, better Hand than husband — a descending hierarchy of competence; he instinctively reaches for the lost fingerbones as a source of luck, suggesting he attributes his achievements to luck rather than merit
