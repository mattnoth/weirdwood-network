# ADWD — Daenerys II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 12
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys II
- **first_available:** ADWD Daenerys II
- **location_primary:** Great Pyramid of Meereen
- **location_secondary:** Purple hall (audience chamber), dragon pit beneath the pyramid, terrace pool
- **approximate_timeline:** During the occupation of Meereen; Sons of the Harpy insurgency ongoing; Daario away on a mission to the Lhazarene
- **time_markers:** "the black of night"; "Dawn will not come for hours yet"; predawn; "As the sky lightened and the stars faded one by one" (dawn); court held after dawn; "at sunset on the night of the third attempt" (Drogon's escape, recalled)
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys is woken in the night to learn nine of her people have been murdered by the Sons of the Harpy. She orders the Unsullied pulled from patrols, creates a new city watch of shavepates and freedmen funded by a blood tax on the pyramids, and takes hostage children from the Great Masters. She comforts the grieving Missandei, receives a cryptic visitation from Quaithe warning of approaching dangers, holds court where she adjudicates a guild dispute and hears Hizdahr zo Loraq's seventh plea to reopen the fighting pits, listens to Ser Barristan's tale of escaping King's Landing, then descends to the pit beneath the pyramid to see her chained dragons Rhaegal and Viserion while reflecting on Drogon's escape and the death of the child Hazzea.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dany woken in the night** — Irri shakes her awake; Missandei is weeping for her brother.
2. **Report of nine murders** — Grey Worm, Skahaz, and Reznak report that the Sons of the Harpy killed six Unsullied (in three pairs) and three freedmen including Rylona Rhee.
3. **Dany orders interrogation** — She commands that the wineseller and his daughters be given to the Shavepate for questioning, first sweetly then sharply.
4. **Dany restructures city security** — She pulls Unsullied from patrol duty (walls, gates, and her person only) and orders Skahaz to create a new city watch of shavepates and freedmen.
5. **Blood tax imposed** — One hundred pieces of gold from every pyramid for each freedman slain by the Sons.
6. **Hostage decree** — Dany orders two children (boy and girl) from each pyramid taken as "pages and cupbearers."
7. **Dany comforts Missandei** — They share her bed; Missandei tells stories of Mossador; Dany offers to send her home to Naath; Missandei calls her "Mother to us all."
8. **Dany's restless night** — She cannot sleep; goes to the terrace pool to bathe by moonlight.
9. **Quaithe appears** — A masked woman under the persimmon tree delivers a cryptic warning: "The glass candles are burning. Soon comes the pale mare, and after her the others. Kraken and dark flame, lion and griffin, the sun's son and the mummer's dragon. Trust none of them. Remember the Undying. Beware the perfumed seneschal."
10. **Quaithe vanishes** — When Missandei arrives with a lantern, Quaithe is gone. Dany searches the terrace but finds no one.
11. **Court: guild dispute** — Dany rules that only guild members may use journeyman/master titles, but guilds must open rolls to qualified freedmen.
12. **Court: Hizdahr's seventh plea** — Hizdahr brings seven former fighting slaves (Khrazz, Barsena Blackhair, Camarron of the Count, Goghor the Giant, the Spotted Cat, Fearless Ithoke, Belaquo Bonebreaker) who argue for reopening the fighting pits. Dany says she will consider it.
13. **Barristan's escape story** — As they climb to her chambers, Barristan tells how he was dismissed by Joffrey, fought past gold cloaks, escaped through the Mud Gate, re-entered through the Gate of the Gods in disguise, witnessed Ned Stark's execution, and resolved to find the true king.
14. **Discussion of Eddard Stark** — Barristan reveals that Stark spoke against Robert's wish to have Dany killed and resigned as Hand rather than countenance the murder of children. Dany equates Starks and Lannisters as "the Usurper's dogs."
15. **Descent to the dragon pit** — Dany insists on seeing Rhaegal and Viserion chained forty feet below. She observes they have grown larger; Rhaegal breathes fire, Viserion struggles against his chains.
16. **Dany reflects on chaining the dragons** — She recalls how Viserion was lured in with oxen and chained while sleeping; Rhaegal was netted and took three days to carry down (six men burned); Drogon escaped north across the Skahazadhan after three failed capture attempts (four men died).
17. **Dany reflects on Hazzea** — She recalls the father's accusation that Drogon killed his four-year-old daughter, her decision to pay a blood price and suppress the story, and her growing doubt about whether she is a mother of dragons or a mother of monsters.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fearful (nine dead in one night); furious ("Her fury was a fire in her belly"); lonely and isolated; torn between compassion and cruelty; romantic longing for Daario; anguished guilt about Hazzea and the dragons; questioning her own sanity after Quaithe's visit
- **Primary preoccupation:** The Sons of the Harpy insurgency; the cost of power (Hazzea, the chained dragons, the murders); whether Daario will return; the question of whether she is becoming her father
- **Key decisions made:** Pull Unsullied from patrols; create new city watch; impose blood tax; take hostage children; question the wineseller sharply; agree to consider reopening the fighting pits
- **Self-deception flags:** Dany tells herself "If I look back, I am doomed" while being unable to stop looking back; she wants to believe Hazzea's father lied or the Sons did it, but recognizes her own doubt; she suppresses the Hazzea story rather than facing it publicly; she questions whether she is going mad like her father but deflects with "I was praying"
