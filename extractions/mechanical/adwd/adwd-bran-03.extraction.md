# ADWD — Bran III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 35
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran III
- **first_available:** ADWD Bran III
- **location_primary:** Cave beneath the hill (beyond the Wall) — the great cavern and chambers of the children of the forest
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell godswood (seen through weirwood visions)
- **approximate_timeline:** Extended period during Bran's training with Lord Brynden beneath the hill; multiple moon cycles pass during the chapter
- **time_markers:** Moon described as crescent (recurring), then fat and full, then a black hole, then fat and full again, then crescent again, then black hole, then crescent again — at least five to six lunar cycles pass; "a pale sun rose and set and rose again"; days described as marching past, each shorter than the one before; nights growing longer; Lord Brynden says mastery may come "in a year, or three, or ten"; Leaf claims to have seen two hundred years; sleeping and waking melt into one another underground; Bran tried counting days by sleeping/waking cycles but gave up
- **chapter_summary:** Bran trains as a greenseer under Lord Brynden (the three-eyed crow) in the caves beneath the hill beyond the Wall. Over the course of many weeks, he learns to skinchange into ravens, secretly uses Hodor's body to explore, and observes Jojen's deepening despair and Meera's growing distress. When Lord Brynden declares it is time for the next step, Bran eats a paste of weirwood seeds and experiences his first greenseer visions through the Winterfell heart tree — seeing his father Eddard in the godswood at different points in time, then a rapidly accelerating sequence of visions stretching back through centuries to a human sacrifice before the weirwood.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Outside the cave — storms with lightning and thunder; rain that fell and froze; tree limbs snapping from ice weight; snowflakes drifting soundlessly; drifts growing deep enough to cover the cave entrance; snowdrifts described in shades of rose and pink from a red sun
- **Season indicators:** "Bone-grinding cold of the lands beyond the Wall"; days growing shorter, nights growing longer; deep winter conditions throughout; "the long summer" referenced as past (dried fruit laid by during it)
- **Time of day:** No sunlight reaches the caves; no moonlight touches the stony halls; stars are strangers; time above runs "in its iron circles, day to night to day"; inside the caves sleeping and waking melt together
- **Lighting:** Torchlight, rushlight, cookfires, candles — all described as temporary ("burned for a little while, then guttered out again, their brief lives at an end"); light enters the great cavern "as a trespasser, unwanted and unwelcome"; darkness is the default state; Leaf carries a torch to guide them
- **Sounds:** Songs of the children of the forest drifting up from below; ravens cawing and pecking; wolves howling in the wood; murder of ravens screaming; sound of running water far below in the abyss; hoarse whispers of Lord Brynden; faint rustling of wood and leaf when Brynden speaks; river described as swift and loud; clatter of wood on wood (in vision — children sparring with branches)
- **Smells:** Air described as full of "strange smells" when Bran is inside the raven; no other smells explicitly described
- **Notable sensory details:** Rough wood pressing against Bran's cheek when in the weirwood throne; the warmth of the caves compared to outside; the bitter-then-sweet taste of weirwood paste (honey, new-fallen snow, pepper and cinnamon, mother's last kiss); Bran feeling the icy touch of air when flying as a raven; the cold black depths of the abyss; soft grey moss of Bran's seat; warm furs covering him

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran sits on weirwood throne** — Bran listens to whispers in the dark as ravens walk up and down his arms; dead men with bright blue eyes shuffle outside the cave but cannot enter
2. **Bran and Meera name the singers** — They give names to the children of the forest: Ash, Leaf, Scales, Black Knife, Snowylocks, and Coals; Leaf says their true names are too long for human tongues
3. **Lord Brynden reveals his birth name** — When Meera asks his true name, Brynden makes a ghastly sound like a chuckle and says "the name she gave me at her breast was Brynden"; Bran mentions his uncle Brynden Blackfish
4. **Leaf explains Brynden's condition** — Most of him has gone into the tree; he has lived beyond his mortal span; "He has a thousand eyes and one"
5. **Jojen explains the secrets of the old gods** — Tells Bran that weirwoods hold the truths the First Men knew; crannogmen live closer to the green and remember what Winterfell has forgotten
6. **Meera says Bran could be a greenseer too; Jojen says no** — Jojen says his task was to get Bran here and his part is done; greendreams are all the gods gave him
7. **Bran secretly skinchanges into Hodor** — Bran warges into Hodor to wander tunnels; notes Hodor no longer fights him but curls up and hides deep within himself; Bran tells himself "No one must ever know"
8. **Lord Brynden teaches about darkness** — "Never fear the darkness, Bran... Darkness will make you strong"; the singers make Bran his own weirwood throne
9. **Bran learns to fly as a raven** — Lord Brynden compares it to mounting a horse that has known riders; Bran fails with two birds, succeeds with the third; flies around the cavern and over the abyss
10. **Bran discovers a dead singer in the raven** — Senses "some girl" inside the raven; Brynden explains it is a dead woman of the singers, a shadow on the soul that will not harm him
11. **Lord Brynden explains ravens and the First Men** — Singers taught First Men to send messages by raven; in those days the birds spoke the words; now men write on parchment instead
12. **Lord Brynden explains skinchanger-greenseer rarity** — "Only one man in a thousand is born a skinchanger, and only one skinchanger in a thousand can be a greenseer"; greenseers among singers are marked by red or green eyes
13. **Jojen explains weirwoods as the old gods** — When singers die they go into the wood; singers believe the weirwoods are the old gods; "When singers die they become part of that godhood"
14. **Summer and pack hunt a wight snow bear** — Summer's pack falls upon a skeletal snow bear and tears it into pieces; they gorge on the rotted, half-frozen meat that moves as they eat it
15. **Leaf warns against exploring the deep caves** — The river is swift and black and flows to a sunless sea; passages go even deeper, with bottomless pits; "Even my people have not explored them all"
16. **Leaf describes the dwindling of the children** — Their sun is setting; giants almost gone; great lions slain; unicorns all but gone; mammoths down to a few hundred; direwolves will outlast them all but their time will come too
17. **Meera and Jojen go to the underground river** — Bran wants to come but cannot; he follows them by skinchanging into Hodor; Jojen cannot climb back up the rope, so Hodor hauls him up
18. **Summer digs up a wight's severed arm** — The arm is black, covered in hoarfrost, fingers still opening and closing; Summer eats the meat and cracks the bones for marrow; "Only then did the arm remember it was dead"
19. **Bran explores caves as Hodor** — Finds chambers of bones, shafts, gigantic bat skeletons, crosses the stone bridge over the abyss; discovers a chamber full of enthroned singers in weirwood roots, most appearing dead but eyes following his torch
20. **Bran reflects on his dread of becoming like Brynden** — Fears spending all his years with a weirwood growing in and through him; thinks "I was going to be a knight" but settles on greenseer being "almost as good"
21. **Jojen grows increasingly despondent** — Wanders alone, climbs to the cave mouth to stare at the forest for hours, shivering despite furs
22. **Meera tells Bran that Jojen wants to go home** — Jojen will not fight his fate; "He says the greendreams do not lie"; Meera wonders why they ever came
23. **Meera cries** — Bran wants to comfort her but cannot reach her; considers using Hodor's body to hold her but feels strange about it; Meera bolts into the tunnels
24. **Lord Brynden announces the next step** — "It is time... For the next step. For you to go beyond skinchanging and learn what it means to be a greenseer"
25. **Bran eats the weirwood seed paste** — Snowylocks brings a weirwood bowl carved with faces containing white paste with red veins; Leaf says "You must eat of this"; tastes bitter then sweet; Lord Brynden says it will "awaken your gifts and wed you to the trees"
26. **Bran has his first weirwood vision — Eddard in the godswood** — Sees his father sitting beside the black pool cleaning Ice; Eddard asks "Who's there?" and turns; Bran pulls away in fear
27. **Lord Brynden explains weirwood visions** — Those were shadows of days past; Bran was looking through the heart tree's eyes; time is different for trees; "a thousand human years are a moment to a weirwood"; the past cannot be changed
28. **Brynden reveals his own ghosts** — "A brother that I loved, a brother that I hated, a woman I desired"; he sees them through the trees but cannot reach them
29. **Bran's second vision — younger Eddard praying** — Eddard is much younger, brown-haired; prays "let them grow up close as brothers, with only love between them... and let my lady wife find it in her heart to forgive..."; Bran calls to him but Eddard cannot see or hear him
30. **Rapid succession of visions going backward in time** — Two children sparring in the godswood (a girl beating a boy); a pregnant woman emerging from the black pool begging for a son to avenge her; a slender girl kissing a knight as tall as Hodor; a dark-eyed youth making arrows from weirwood branches; lords in fur and chain mail (some with faces from the crypts); the tree shrinks with each vision
31. **Final vision — human sacrifice** — A bearded man forces a captive to his knees; a white-haired woman with a bronze sickle slashes the captive's throat; Bran can taste the blood through the weirwood

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Complex mixture — wonder and excitement at learning to fly and seeing through weirwoods; deep loneliness and longing for his family (Robb, Arya, Sansa, Rickon, Jon, his mother, his father); dread at the thought of becoming like Lord Brynden ("One day I will be like him. The thought filled Bran with dread"); grief upon seeing his father in the vision ("Bran could feel rough wood pressing against one cheek"); sadness at Leaf's account of the children's dwindling; frustration at being crippled ("Bran hated being crippled then")
- **Primary preoccupation:** Learning greensight and the nature of his powers; missing home and family; the ethical weight of skinchanging into Hodor; whether his visions of Eddard are real
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to eat the weirwood paste despite unease at its appearance; decides to keep skinchanging into Hodor secret ("No one must ever know"); decides not to use Hodor's body to comfort Meera (though he considers it); tells himself that being a greenseer is "almost as good" as being a knight
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself he is not hurting Hodor ("No one wants to hurt you, Hodor... I just want to be strong again for a while. I'll give it back, the way I always do") despite knowing Hodor hides in terror; tells himself being a greenseer is almost as good as being a knight, but the "almost" reveals his doubt; tells himself he is "almost a man grown" (twice) to justify not showing emotion
