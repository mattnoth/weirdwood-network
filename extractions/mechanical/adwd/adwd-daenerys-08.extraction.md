She takes Prince Quentyn Martell down to see the chained dragons Viserion and Rhaegal, hinting at a possible dragon-riding alliance. After the feast, Hizdahr beds her hoping for a son, and Dany lies awake thinking of Daario before Missandei comforts her to sleep.

## Events & Actions
1. **Yunkish peace feast begins** — Yunkish commanders, sellsword captains, and Meereenese hosts feast in the Great Pyramid's hall with music, dancing, and abundant food and wine.
2. **Dany endures the feast in silence** — She speaks only when spoken to, brooding on the slave market outside while Hizdahr makes speeches and entertains.
3. **Entertainment follows the meal** — Yunkish castrati sing; tumblers form a nine-level human pyramid topped with a naked girl.
4. **Guests move to the lower terrace** — Hizdahr leads guests to view Meereen by night; they wander among lemon trees and night-blooming flowers.
5. **Brown Ben Plumm approaches Dany** — He offers a "wedding gift" (the head of an old foe), discusses his betrayal, tells the story of the dead axeman's gold, and warns her never to trust a sellsword.
6. **Dany surveys the siege from the terrace** — She observes Yunkish camps, Ghiscari legions, free company positions, and the slave market by the bay.
7. **Dany consults Ser Barristan** — She asks about assassinating Brown Ben; Barristan demurs. She instructs him to release Pretty Meris as a gesture to the Tattered Prince and to sound out the Long Lances. They discuss but reject approaching Bloodbeard.
8. **Barristan raises the "Dornish road"** — He advocates for Quentyn's suit, citing House Martell's loyalty and Prince Lewyn. Dany notes Quentyn brought a parchment, not swords or ships.
9. **Dany takes Quentyn to see the dragons** — She leads him down through the pyramid with guards. She tells him "The dragon has three heads" and says her marriage need not end his hopes.
10. **Quentyn witnesses Viserion and Rhaegal** — Viserion has broken free of chains, clings to ceiling; Rhaegal roars fire. Quentyn goes white with fear. Dany notes the pit won't hold the dragons much longer.
11. **Dany hints at dragon-riding alliance** — She explains no rider ever flew two dragons; Quentyn claims Targaryen blood through the first Daenerys. Dany kisses his cheeks and says "And so am I" (a dragon).
12. **Dany warns Quentyn of danger** — She tells him he has enemies: Daario, potentially Hizdahr's men. Quentyn refuses to flee, claiming princely pride.
13. **Dany asks Quentyn about the first Daenerys** — On the way back up, she requests he tell her of the Targaryen princess and the Water Gardens.
14. **Guests depart past midnight** — Dany retires to her apartments with Hizdahr.
15. **Hizdahr beds Dany** — He is drunk and quick; whispers hoping they made a son. Dany thinks of Mirri Maz Duur's prophecy but keeps silent.
16. **Dany lies awake tormented** — She thinks of Daario, wonders if he loves her, regrets taking him as lover.
17. **Missandei comforts Dany** — She heard Dany crying; Dany denies it but asks her to stay and tell stories of Naath until she falls asleep.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply conflicted; feels the peace tastes "like defeat"; anger at powerlessness re: slave market; self-loathing about necessary compromises; loneliness and sexual frustration in bed; grief and regret thinking of those she's lost (Doreah, Quaro, Eroeh, Hazzea); longing for Daario mixed with resolve to keep him away
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether the peace is worth its cost; maintaining control while feeling powerless; the slave market outside her walls as personal affront; managing Daario's absence
- **Key decisions made:** Orders Pretty Meris released as overture to Tattered Prince; decides to sound out Long Lances; accepts Barristan's counsel to avoid Bloodbeard; takes Quentyn to see dragons and hints at three-headed dragon alliance; resolves to keep Daario out of her bed/heart going forward
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself "If I look back, I am lost" (repeated mantra to avoid confronting costs); claims "I was not crying" to Missandei when she clearly was; rationalizes pit deaths ("Better a few should die in the pit than thousands at the gates"); tells herself peace is what she wanted while everything in her rebels against it
