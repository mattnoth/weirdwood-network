# ADWD — Tyrion IV

## Chapter Metadata

- **approximate_timeline:** During the river journey south toward Volantis; the party has been on the Shy Maid for at least several days
- **time_markers:** Full moon at night; first pale light of day at dawn; sunrise; three hours spent playing cyvasse; sun low above the reed-beds in the evening; "We should reach the junction with the Noyne by evening"; "Not for a thousand years" (lawlessness above the Sorrows); "Once every fortnight" (fish meals); ten years ago Tyrion read a fragment of Barth's work; tigers held sway almost a century after the Doom; elephants ruled for three hundred years
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion spends a day aboard the Shy Maid as it drifts down the Upper Rhoyne toward Volantis. He observes the morning routines of Griff, Septa Lemore, Yandry, and Ysilla, then watches Young Griff and Duck spar with blunted swords — Young Griff wins by pushing Duck into the river. Duck retaliates by throwing Tyrion in. Tyrion works on a dragonlore manuscript Griff has commanded him to write, then plays cyvasse against Haldon and wins for the first time. The Shy Maid passes the ruins of Ny Sar at the junction of the Rhoyne and the Noyne, where the party witnesses a colossal horned turtle that Yandry calls "the Old Man of the River."

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion lies awake at night** — Cannot sleep on the cabin roof of the Shy Maid; drinks water instead of wine per Griff's orders; suffers sweats and shakes from withdrawal.
2. **Tyrion descends to the afterdeck** — Finds Griff keeping the night watch alone beside the iron brazier, wrapped in a wolfskin cloak.
3. **Tyrion and Griff exchange words at dawn** — Tyrion says "day soon"; Griff wants to continue downstream but Yandry and Ysilla refuse to risk the boat at night due to snags and sawyers.
4. **Septa Lemore bathes in the river** — Her daily morning custom; Tyrion watches and notes her stretch marks from childbirth; Yandry steals glances.
5. **Yandry and Ysilla begin the day's work** — Yandry checks lines; Ysilla prepares biscuits and bacon on the brazier.
6. **The party eats breakfast** — Tyrion snatches a biscuit; Duck smells bacon and comes up from the hold; Ysilla wields her wooden spoon against grabbing hands; Young Griff arrives yawning.
7. **Tyrion and Duck urinate off the stern** — Tyrion quips about the Rhoyne; Yandry defends Mother Rhoyne's supremacy over Westerosi rivers.
8. **Young Griff and Duck spar with blunted longswords** — Tyrion helps Young Griff dress in armor; no shields used; Young Griff lands more blows, Duck's are harder; Young Griff forces Duck back to the stern, ties up their blades, and shoulders Duck into the river.
9. **Duck throws Tyrion into the river** — After being pulled out, Duck grabs Tyrion and chucks him in; Tyrion paddles until his legs cramp; Young Griff extends a pole to pull him out.
10. **Tyrion performs a cartwheel on deck** — Tells a false story about being sold to mummers; actually learned tumbling from his uncle at age six or seven; recalls his father's angry reaction at seeing him tumble at Casterly Rock.
11. **Tyrion changes into dry motley clothes** — Puts on the half-and-half garments Lemore fashioned from Illyrio's children's clothes; Duck laughs.
12. **Tyrion works on dragonlore manuscript** — Writes about the mating habits of dragons on the cabin roof; references divergent views of Barth, Munkun, and Thomax; reflects on rare texts he wishes he had.
13. **Haldon and Tyrion banter** — Haldon tests Tyrion's cover story; they discuss the upcoming Noyne junction; Haldon invites Tyrion to join Young Griff's lesson.
14. **Young Griff's lessons with Haldon** — Languages (Common Tongue, High Valyrian, Pentoshi, Tyroshi, Myrish, Lysene dialects, trade talk, Volantene, Meereenese/Ghiscari); geometry; history of Volantis (tigers vs. elephants, the wars after the Doom, current triarchs).
15. **Tyrion and Haldon play cyvasse** — They wager secrets; game lasts three hours; Tyrion wins for the first time (implied — Haldon goes to bed "in some discomfort" and Tyrion reports "turtles crawling out his arse").
16. **The Shy Maid passes Ny Sar** — Ruins of Nymeria's city at the junction of the Rhoyne and Noyne; enormous ruins with a colossal palace of pink and green marble; bonesnappers basking on old docks.
17. **Yandry describes the Rhoyne's daughters** — Names the Noyne (Wild Daughter), Qhoyne (Darkling Daughter), Lhorulu (Smiling Daughter), and Selhoru (Shy Daughter); describes what each brings and the cities at their confluences.
18. **A giant horned turtle surfaces** — Enormous, dark green shell with brown mottling, water moss and black river molluscs; bellows a roar louder than any warhorn; Ysilla weeps and says "we are blessed"; Yandry calls it "the Old Man of the River."
19. **Tyrion reflects on the omen** — Thinks "Gods and wonders always appear, to attend the birth of kings."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Restless, sardonic, and self-mocking; oscillates between dark humor and genuine pain. Sexually frustrated (fixated on Lemore). Suffering wine withdrawal (sweats, shakes, sleeplessness, light-headedness). Bitter about his father but finding satisfaction in Tywin's death. Carries deep guilt and longing around Tysha.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Coping with enforced sobriety and the monotony of river travel; probing the identities and secrets of his companions; working on the dragonlore manuscript; trying to win information through cyvasse; haunted by Tysha ("where whores go").
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to play the fool willingly ("If Griff wants to cast me as the fool, I'll play the game"); wages secrets on cyvasse; considers joining pirates as a joke but seriously contemplates his situation; commits to finding Tysha or a copy of Galendro in Volantis.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself "I do not want to know her, I only want to fuck her" about Lemore, while clearly noting her mysteries. Claims he doesn't mind the motley because it would horrify Lord Tywin — using spite as armor against humiliation. Invents elaborate false histories (mummer's troupe, water witch) to deflect genuine vulnerability.
