# ADWD — The Discarded Knight I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 60
- **pov_character:** Barristan Selmy
- **pov_chapter_number:** The Discarded Knight I
- **first_available:** ADWD The Discarded Knight I
- **location_primary:** Great Pyramid of Meereen (throne room / audience hall)
- **location_secondary:** Barristan's bedchamber (described in memory from the previous night); Quentyn's quarters (referenced, two levels below the hall); Blackhaven (in Barristan's memory); the Trident (referenced); Daznak's Pit (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the events at Daznak's Pit; Daenerys is missing, Strong Belwas is still recovering from poisoning
- **time_markers:** "The day was young and fresh" (morning court session); "Last night, he had scarce slept at all"; "the black watches of night" (Barristan prayed during the night); "At three-and-sixty" (Barristan's current age); "Fifty-three years ago" (when Barristan was named "the Bold" at age ten); "In a few more days" (Barristan's internal reference to a planned action against Hizdahr)
- **chapter_summary:** Barristan Selmy attends Hizdahr zo Loraq's court session in the Great Pyramid of Meereen. Petitioners are angry and fearful after the events at Daznak's Pit, and a freedwoman asks if Daenerys is dead. A Yunkish delegation arrives with the sellsword Bloodbeard, who throws the severed head of the hostage Admiral Groleo before the throne, demanding blood for blood over the death of Yurkhaz zo Yunzak. The Yunkish return three hostages but retain the remaining three, demanding the dragons be destroyed. After Hizdahr dismisses court, Barristan intercepts Prince Quentyn Martell and urges him to leave Meereen, warning that Hizdahr may use him as a scapegoat for the poisoned locusts at Daznak's Pit.

## Events & Actions
1. **Court opens** — The herald announces Hizdahr zo Loraq's full titles; Barristan loosens his sword beneath his cloak.
2. **Barristan observes the hall** — Notes the missing faces (Missandei, Belwas, Grey Worm, the bloodriders, Irri, Jhiqui, Daario) and that the Shavepate has been replaced by Marghaz zo Loraq as commander of the Brazen Beasts.
3. **Petitioners voice grievances** — A woman wails about a brother who died at Daznak's Pit; another complains about her damaged palanquin; a fat man shows his burned arm; a freedman shoves a man in a tokar to the floor; six Brazen Beasts drag both from the hall.
4. **Freedwoman asks if Daenerys is dead** — Reznak denies it, says bloodriders have been dispatched across the Skahazadhan to find her, each with ten riders and three swift horses.
5. **Freedman rejects Hizdahr** — Yells "He is no king of mine."
6. **Barristan observes the Dornishmen** — Spots Quentyn, Drinkwater, and Yronwood at the rear of the hall; reflects that Quentyn is "his father's son" — decent but plain, not the type to make a young girl's heart beat faster.
7. **Yronwood laughs audibly** — Draws Hizdahr's attention; the king frowns at Quentyn and whispers to his cousin Marghaz.
8. **Barristan reflects on Lewyn Martell and the Trident** — Recalls his Sworn Brother and resolves to help Lewyn's nephew.
9. **Barristan entertains suspicion about Quentyn** — Wonders if the poisoned locusts were meant for Hizdahr rather than Daenerys, and whether Quentyn (kin to the Red Viper) could be responsible.
10. **Yunkish delegation arrives** — Three Wise Masters with retinues; Bloodbeard accompanies them with a leather sack. No Tattered Prince or Brown Ben Plumm.
11. **Bloodbeard throws Groleo's severed head** — Flung at Reznak, who leaps aside; head rolls to the foot of Hizdahr's throne; Goghor, the Spotted Cat, and Khrazz form a defensive wall before the king.
12. **Reznak identifies the head** — "Admiral Groleo."
13. **Barristan compares kings' reactions** — Imagines how Aerys, Robert, and Jaehaerys would have responded; Hizdahr sits frozen.
14. **Yunkish read their message** — The maroon-tokar envoy reads a scroll: seven entered Meereen, seven hostages given; Yurkhaz died as a guest; blood must pay for blood.
15. **Barristan speaks without leave** — Tells the court Yurkhaz died by accident — stumbled on steps, crushed by his own companions, or his heart burst from terror; notes he was old.
16. **Rabbit-toothed Yunkish lord rebukes Barristan** — Questions who speaks without the king's leave.
17. **Hizdahr questions the Yunkish** — Asks who speaks for Yunkai now; the rabbit says "All of us. The council of masters."
18. **Three hostages returned** — Hizdahr's sister and two cousins; returned as "good faith."
19. **Yunkish demand the dragons be destroyed** — Remaining three hostages will be held until the dragons are destroyed.
20. **Uproar in the hall** — Shouting, cursing, stamping feet; Brazen Beasts restore order by pounding spear butts.
21. **Bloodbeard claims Daenerys is dead** — "Burned and devoured. Weeds grow through her broken skull."
22. **Barristan silently assesses Bloodbeard** — "He came to sack a city, and Hizdahr's peace has cheated him of his plunder."
23. **Hizdahr dismisses court** — Says he must consult his council.
24. **Barristan intercepts Quentyn** — Catches the Dornishmen at the top of the steps after court.
25. **Barristan urges Quentyn to leave Meereen** — Advises him to leave the city and return to Dorne; warns that Hizdahr frowned at him and whispered to Marghaz.
26. **Barristan reveals the poisoning** — Tells Quentyn that food in the royal box at Daznak's Pit was poisoned; Strong Belwas ate it all and nearly died; warns Hizdahr may need a scapegoat.
27. **Barristan argues the marriage pact is void** — Says it was made by two dead men, promised Quentyn's sister's hand to the queen's brother (another dead man), and Daenerys was ignorant of it until Quentyn arrived.
28. **Barristan discusses Daario as a suspect** — Says Daario would kill Hizdahr openly, never with poison, and was not present; says Hizdahr needs the Stormcrows and cannot afford to blame their captain.
29. **Barristan hints at coming action** — Internally notes that "in a few more days, if the gods smiled on them, Hizdahr zo Loraq would no longer rule Meereen" and refers to "the bloodbath that was coming."
30. **Quentyn invokes Barristan's fame** — Calls him "Barristan the Bold"; asks what name he will get if he returns without Daenerys.
31. **Barristan recalls winning the name "the Bold"** — At age ten, he entered the lists at Blackhaven as a mystery knight, borrowing armor from Lord Dondarrion's armory; Prince Duncan (the Prince of Dragonflies) jousted against him and named him "A bold boy."
32. **Barristan suggests "Quentyn the Wise"** — Privately thinks "The Prince Who Came Too Late."
