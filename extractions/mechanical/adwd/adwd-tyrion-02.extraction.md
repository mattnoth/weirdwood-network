# ADWD — Tyrion II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 6
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion II
- **first_available:** ADWD Tyrion II
- **location_primary:** Valyrian road between Pentos and the Little Rhoyne (traveling through the Flatlands/Andalos and the Velvet Hills)
- **location_secondary:** Pentos (departed from), Ghoyan Drohe (destination mentioned), Volantis (ultimate destination discussed)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Tyrion's arrival in Pentos; multi-day overland journey east toward the Rhoyne
- **time_markers:** Departure at sunrise (Tyrion never sees it); morning/afternoon/evening meal sequence on first day; "night had fallen" at end of first day; dawn on second day; multiple stops to change horses (thrice in one day); day and night cycles blurring ("day and night seemed not to matter"); "that night" when Tyrion sings; autumn season explicitly stated; "two years before she returns" regarding galley to Asshai
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion and Illyrio Mopatis depart Pentos secretly in a litter, traveling east along a Valyrian road toward the Rhoyne. During the multi-day journey, Illyrio reveals his plans to support Daenerys Targaryen's return to Westeros, tells the backstory of his partnership with Varys in Pentos, and discloses that the Golden Company is marching to Volantis to join Daenerys's cause. Tyrion is to meet a sellsword called Griff at Volantis. Tyrion drinks heavily throughout, haunted by memories of killing his father, strangling Shae, and losing Tysha, and has a violent dream of battle in which he kills both his father and his brother Jaime.

## Events & Actions
1. **Departure from Pentos** — Tyrion and Illyrio depart Pentos secretly through the Sunrise Gate in a curtained litter, with Unsullied guards and a baggage train of mules
2. **Secrecy precautions discussed** — Illyrio assures Tyrion that the galley has been sent to Asshai, the Unsullied will tell no tales, and his household is loyal
3. **Route debate** — Tyrion argues for sea travel to Volantis; Illyrio insists on the overland route, citing autumn storms and pirates on the Stepstones
4. **Discussion of stone men** — Tyrion mentions stone men on the Rhoyne; Illyrio confirms they are real but dismisses the topic
5. **Lavish eating and drinking** — Tyrion and Illyrio eat and drink extensively throughout the multi-day journey, from spiced sausages to roasted larks to garlic snails
6. **Illyrio describes Daenerys** — Illyrio recounts Daenerys's transformation from frightened child to dragon queen, her conquests of Astapor, Yunkai, and Meereen
7. **Illyrio reveals the plan** — Tyrion and Griff are to meet Daenerys at Volantis with fresh forces and ships; Illyrio will remain in Pentos to smooth the way
8. **Tyrion questions Illyrio's motives** — Tyrion presses Illyrio on why a Pentoshi magister would support a Targaryen queen who smashed the slave trade
9. **Illyrio reveals Viserys's promises** — Viserys promised Illyrio the position of Master of Coin and choice of castles, including Casterly Rock
10. **Illyrio tells of Varys's backstory** — Illyrio recounts meeting young Varys in Pentos; their partnership as thief-catchers; Varys's training of "mice" (child spies); Varys's eventual recruitment by a paranoid king (Aerys II)
11. **Illyrio reveals the Golden Company connection** — The Golden Company has broken its contract with Myr and marches toward Volantis to join Daenerys's cause; "some contracts are writ in ink, and some in blood"
12. **Tyrion examines the Valyrian road** — During a stop, Tyrion inspects the road: fused stone, raised half a foot, broad enough for three wagons, unmarred after four centuries
13. **Passage through Andalos** — Illyrio identifies the region as Andalos, ancestral homeland of the Andals; Tyrion recites from The Seven-Pointed Star
14. **Illyrio shows Serra's locket** — Illyrio reveals a silver locket with a painted likeness of his second wife Serra, found in a Lysene pillow house; tells how she died of the "grey death" plague from a Braavosi galley
15. **Tyrion recalls Tysha** — Tyrion thinks of Tysha while looking at fields where gods once walked
16. **Discussion of the Flatlands** — Illyrio describes the ruined landscape, noting Dothraki raids destroy towns; debates with Tyrion about fighting vs. buying off the Dothraki
17. **Illyrio describes Griff and Young Griff** — Griff is a Westerosi-born sellsword with a son he dotes on; Illyrio trusts him like a brother
18. **Tyrion sings "Hands of Gold"** — Drunk, Tyrion sings the song and is consumed by memories of strangling Shae with a golden chain
19. **Tyrion recalls Tysha and Tywin** — Trying to remember Tysha's face, he sees his father on the privy instead; "wherever whores go"
20. **Travel through the Velvet Hills** — They pass standing stones allegedly raised by giants, a deep lake with legends of underwater robbers, and a female Valyrian sphinx beside the road
21. **Omen of the sphinxes** — Tyrion calls the female sphinx "a dragon queen, a pleasant omen"; Illyrio notes the male sphinx was taken to Vaes Dothrak; Tyrion privately thinks that is a less hopeful omen
22. **Tyrion's battle dream** — Tyrion dreams of fighting alongside Barristan the Bold and Bittersteel with dragons overhead; he has two noseless heads; he kills his father again, then kills Jaime, laughing, until realizing his second head is weeping
23. **Approach to Ghoyan Drohe** — Illyrio tells Tyrion they have not yet left the Flatlands but will pass through the Velvet Hills toward Ghoyan Drohe on the Little Rhoyne

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply haunted and self-destructive; drinks heavily throughout; swinging between bitter wit, melancholy, and dark introspection; recalls killing Tywin (crossbow thrum), strangling Shae, and losing Tysha; childhood nostalgia about wanting dragons alternates with present despair
- **Primary preoccupation:** Suspicion of Illyrio's true motives; memories of his crimes (patricide, killing Shae); lost loves (Tysha, Shae); drinking to numb himself
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees to travel with Illyrio; agrees to meet Griff at Volantis; accepts the mission to join Daenerys (though with private skepticism)
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself drinking fire wine might produce dragon dreams; uses bitter humor to deflect from grief; privately yearns for the "homecoming" of fire and sword while ostensibly serving someone else's cause; suppresses emotion through drink
