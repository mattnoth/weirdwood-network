# ADWD — The Prince of Winterfell I

## Chapter Metadata

- **chapter_number:** 38
- **pov_character:** Theon Greyjoy

- **approximate_timeline:** During the early stages of winter; Stannis's army has left Deepwood Motte and could arrive at Winterfell in a fortnight if weather holds
- **time_markers:** Night (crescent moon, candlelight, torchlight); the wedding ceremony takes place at night in the godswood; a fortnight mentioned as the time Stannis could reach Winterfell; Roose Bolton's army recently arrived at Winterfell and completed rebuilding work before the wedding
- **chapter_summary:** Theon Greyjoy, broken and renamed Reek, gives away the false Arya Stark (actually Jeyne Poole) at her wedding to Ramsay Bolton in Winterfell's godswood. During the wedding feast in the rebuilt Great Hall, Theon sits beside Lady Dustin, who shares her opinions on Lord Manderly's cowardice, Roose Bolton's ambitions, and the manipulations of maesters. Roose Bolton announces that Stannis is marching from Deepwood Motte with northern clans and that Crowfood Umber and the Karstarks approach as well. After the feast, Theon is forced to escort the bride to Ramsay's bedchamber, where Ramsay compels him to participate in the consummation.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Hard white frost grips Winterfell; hoarfrost sparkles in moonlight; black ice on paths; drifts of dirty snow piled against walls; icicles long as lances hang from battlements; wind howling from the north; freezing cold outside
- **Season indicators:** Winter is almost upon them per Roose Bolton's speech; fruits and vegetables in the Glass Gardens are dead, black, and frozen; the whole world described as grey
- **Time of day:** Night throughout; transitions from godswood ceremony to feast to bedchamber
- **Lighting:** Candlelight in the bride's dressing room (four candles, three blow out when door opens); moonlight (crescent moon half-obscured by mist); candles and torches flickering along godswood paths; torchlight in the Great Hall; firelight in the bedchamber; candle on bedside table
- **Sounds:** Music of lute, pipes, and drum calling them to the ceremony; drum tremulous as a maiden's heart, pipes high and sweet; ravens muttering in the trees; a raven quorking after the vows; Abel singing "Two Hearts That Beat as One" and later "Fair Maids of Summer"; Lord Manderly calling for "The Night That Ended" and songs of Danny Flint and the Rat Cook; wind plucking at boards over windows; Damon Dance-for-Me whistling while climbing stairs; a raven screaming in the godswood at chapter's end
- **Smells:** Sour Alyn's breath foul with the smell of rotten teeth
- **Notable sensory details:** Warm mists and steam rising from hot springs in the godswood; ground unfrozen inside the godswood while frost grips everything outside; the hall blessedly warm after the cold yard; Theon's missing toes and fingers throbbing with pain; cold caked ash in the hearth of the dressing room; ice crunching beneath boots; bride shaking/trembling

## Events & Actions
1. **Theon prepares the bride** — Theon drapes a fur-trimmed Stark cloak on Jeyne Poole's shoulders in a cold, unheated room. She is trembling and dressed in white lambswool with freshwater pearls.
2. **Jeyne reveals her fear and identity** — The bride tells Theon she will be a good wife and admits she is not the real Arya. She begs Theon to help her escape, offering to be his wife or whore. Theon refuses and tells her to be Arya.
3. **Lady Dustin's instruction recalled** — Theon remembers Lady Dustin telling him he must give the bride away because all of Arya's kin are dead, lost, or captive, and Theon was Eddard's ward, the nearest thing to living kin.
4. **Theon reflects on being used** — He realizes the Boltons are using him to cloak their deception; if Theon accepts the girl as Arya, no northern lord will question her legitimacy. He expects to be given back to Ramsay after the wedding.
5. **Procession through the godswood** — Theon and the bride walk arm in arm through the warm, misty godswood along a mossy path lit by candles and torches. The godswood is warm with steam from hot springs while the rest of Winterfell is frozen.
6. **Theon's memories of the godswood** — He recalls playing here as a boy: skipping stones, hiding treasures in an oak, stalking squirrels, soaking bruises in hot springs with Robb, Jory, and Jon, his first kiss, and losing his virginity.
7. **Wedding guests observed in torchlight** — Theon sees the faces of Lords Stout, Locke, Whoresbane Umber, Big and Little Walder Frey, and Roose Bolton distorted by mist and shifting torchlight. Ravens fill the trees overhead.
8. **The wedding ceremony** — Before the heart tree, Ramsay claims "Arya" and Theon gives her away. Jeyne hesitates — Theon considers urging her to reveal the truth but knows it would mean death. She says "I take this man." They kneel and pray. Ramsay removes the Stark cloak and fastens a Bolton cloak.
9. **Post-ceremony music** — Abel and two of his women sing "Two Hearts That Beat as One" as the wedding party departs the godswood.
10. **Theon's religious crisis** — He wonders if he should pray to the old gods but reflects that they are not his gods; his god is the Drowned God, but Winterfell is far from the sea.
11. **The whispered voice** — A voice seems to whisper "Theon" from the trees. He cannot tell if it is a god's voice or a ghost's. He is frightened and leaves the godswood.
12. **Theon crosses the ruined yard** — He observes the devastation of Winterfell: roofless towers, the frozen Glass Gardens, tents in the yard, dead men hanging from ropes. He reflects on the squatters Bolton hanged after they rebuilt the castle.
13. **Arrival of Bolton's army** — They raised King Tommen's banner and the flayed man above Winterfell. Theon arrived in Lady Dustin's train with the bride-to-be.
14. **Theon's self-reflection in the yard** — He remembers sparring with Robb and Jon under Ser Rodrik's watch. He recalls the night Bran and Rickon escaped, Ramsay (then posing as Reek) at his side suggesting flaying captives. He remembers his horse Smiler burning as the castle fell.
15. **Theon enters the Great Hall** — Warmth and torchlight; packed crowd; Abel singing "Fair Maids of Summer"; banners of northern houses on the walls with blackened walls behind them; the Stark banner behind the dais hits Theon hard — he thinks the arms of House Poole should hang there instead.
16. **Theon's reception** — Someone calls him "Theon Turncloak"; men turn away; one spits. A woman laughs at his hobbling gait.
17. **The feast** — Theon is seated at the end of the high table by a wall, Lady Dustin to his left, no one to his right. The feast is lavish. Manderly serves the pies personally and devours six portions.
18. **Theon cannot eat** — Lady Dustin observes he does not eat. He explains that chewing is agony due to broken teeth.
19. **Lady Dustin's observations on Manderly** — She calls Manderly craven, notes he shared bread and salt with Freys despite his son dying at the Red Wedding, and compares his heart to Theon's.
20. **Lady Dustin's observations on Roose Bolton** — She describes Roose as having no feelings, passions sucked out by leeches; he plays with men as a game. She speculates that Roose aspires to be King of the North, noting the Lannisters are spent and Theon removed the Starks.
21. **Lady Dustin on the maesters** — She calls maesters "grey rats" who manipulate lords. She tells the story of Maester Walys (born Walys Flowers), a baseborn son of a Hightower woman and rumored archmaester, sent to Winterfell to manipulate Lord Rickard Stark. She credits Walys with engineering the Tully marriage.
22. **Three maesters enter** — Medrick (tall), Rhodry (plump), and Henly (young) enter through the lord's door. Medrick whispers to Bolton.
23. **Roose announces Stannis's march** — Stannis and his knights have left Deepwood Motte with the northern hill clans. Crowfood Umber marches down the kingsroad. The Karstarks approach from the east. They mean to join Stannis and take Winterfell.
24. **Ser Hosteen urges riding forth** — He wants to meet the enemies before they combine strength.
25. **Theon knows about Karstark treachery** — He thinks that Arnolf Karstark awaits a sign from Bolton to turn his cloak.
26. **Roose adjourns to the solar** — He tells lords to follow; the rest stay and enjoy the feast. Manderly is so drunk he needs four men to carry him; he mutters about wanting a song about the Rat Cook.
27. **Theon stumbles drunk** — After Lady Dustin leaves, Theon realizes how much he has drunk. He knocks wine from a serving girl's hands.
28. **Ramsay's men seize Theon** — Sour Alyn grabs him, with Yellow Dick and Damon Dance-for-Me. They tell him Ramsay wants him to bring the bride to the bed.
29. **Escorting the bride** — Theon leads the bride from the hall across the yard to the Great Keep, accompanied by six of the Bastard's Boys. Damon Dance-for-Me whistles; Skinner boasts about getting a piece of the bloody sheet.
30. **In the bedchamber** — Ramsay is seated; he dismisses his men but makes Theon stay. He orders Theon to undress the bride with a knife. Theon considers stabbing Ramsay but remembers Kyra's keys — another trap. He cuts her gown off.
31. **Ramsay's cruelty** — Ramsay taunts Theon about his manhood, references "the first night" / droit du seigneur, forces himself on the bride. When she is not physically ready, he slaps her and orders Theon to prepare her with his mouth, threatening to cut out Theon's tongue.
32. **Theon submits** — A raven screams in the godswood. Theon sheathes the dagger and obeys Ramsay's command.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deep fear, shame, self-loathing, and numbness. Theon oscillates between resignation and fleeting impulses toward resistance. He feels phantom pain in missing fingers and toes. He is terrified of Ramsay and knows any misstep could cost him more body parts. He experiences moments of dark humor and bitter self-awareness. By the chapter's end, he is fully subjugated.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival through obedience. He is consumed by maintaining the performance — both his own (playing the compliant servant) and Jeyne's (playing Arya Stark). He repeatedly thinks of Jeyne's real name ("Jeyne, her name is Jeyne, it rhymes with pain") while forcing himself to call her Arya. His identity is fractured between Theon and Reek.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses Jeyne's plea for escape. Does not reveal the bride's true identity at the ceremony despite a fleeting impulse. Does not use his dagger on Ramsay in the bedchamber, recognizing it as a trap (remembering Kyra). Sheathes the dagger and submits to Ramsay's final command.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells Jeyne that Ramsay has called her pretty ("Yes," he lied). Tells himself Ramsay is setting a trap with the dagger, which may be true but also rationalizes his passivity. He frames his refusal to help Jeyne as impossible rather than a choice ("I have no way to save her"). Thinks "A man would help her" — acknowledging he has been broken beyond manhood.
