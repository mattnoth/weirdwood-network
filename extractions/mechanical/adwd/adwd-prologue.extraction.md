# ADWD — Prologue

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Varamyr Sixskins
- **pov_chapter_number:** Prologue
- **first_available:** ADWD Prologue
- **location_primary:** An abandoned wildling village beyond the Wall
- **location_secondary:** Forest beyond the Wall (wolf hunt opening); Varamyr's one-room hut of mud and straw; references to Milkwater, Hardhome, Shadow Tower, Bridge of Skulls, valley of the Thenns, Eastwatch-by-the-Sea, Castle Black, the Wall
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the Battle at the Wall (Mance Rayder's defeat by Stannis's knights); Varamyr has been dying for days in the village
- **time_markers:** Night (opening wolf hunt); darkness inside the hut (drifting in and out of sleep, unable to tell day from night); Thistle gone "two days? Three?"; night again when he pushes snow away from the hut door (silver moon, thousand stars); "last night's snow" referenced during the hunt; Varamyr was six when Bump died; Varamyr was ten when he named himself; gathering of skinchangers when Lump was ten; Haggon traded at Eastwatch when Varamyr was about ten
- **chapter_summary:** Varamyr Sixskins, a dying skinchanger beyond the Wall, wargs into his wolf pack to hunt fleeing wildlings. Alone in an abandoned village, he reflects on his past — his childhood as "Lump," his training under Haggon, his years of power with six beasts, and the aftermath of Mance Rayder's defeat at the Wall. When his last companion Thistle returns, he attempts the ultimate abomination of seizing her body but fails violently. He dies and enters his wolf One Eye, only to witness the Others' wights — including a reanimated Thistle — rising in the village below.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Heavy snowfall (buries the hut); snow has stopped by the time Varamyr exits but wind is rising; "crystal" blown through the air; pale thin clouds, silver moon, thousand stars visible later
- **Season indicators:** Deep winter beyond the Wall; ice frozen between wolf paws "hard as stone"; trees have "icy teeth"; the chill is "deep down in his bones"
- **Time of day:** Night throughout — both the wolf hunt opening and Varamyr's final scene occur at night; prolonged darkness inside the hut with Varamyr unable to distinguish day from night
- **Lighting:** Moonlit snow; silver moon; stars "watched coldly"; firelight inside the hut (until it goes out); pale blue light flickering in the wights' eye-sockets
- **Sounds:** Wolf howls; wailing of a human child; roaring of the men being hunted; Varamyr's coughing; wind sighing through hills; cawing of a hundred ravens; elk trumpeting
- **Smells:** "Rank with the smell of man"; piney wind; fox, hare, seal, stag scents; old skins "dead and sour"; smoke and blood and rot; mold and damp inside the hut; dead flesh, dry blood, mold and rot and urine (the wights)
- **Notable sensory details:** Taste of blood and fat in Varamyr's mouth from the wolf feeding; heat beneath his fingers when touching his wound; hand frozen to the ground; snowflakes melting on his brow; "shock of cold, as if he had been plunged into the icy waters of a frozen lake" at true death; hoarfrost crackling on wight-Thistle

## Events & Actions
1. **Wolf pack hunts humans** — Varamyr, warging into his wolf, leads One Eye, Stalker, and Sly to hunt three fleeing wildlings (two men, a woman with a baby). The pack kills them all and feeds.
2. **Varamyr reflects in his hut** — Leagues away, Varamyr lies dying in a one-room hut, tasting the blood from his wolf's hunt while his body starves and his wound seeps.
3. **Varamyr recalls Haggon's teachings** — He remembers the three abominations: eating human flesh, mating as wolf with wolf, and seizing another human's body. He recalls killing Haggon and taking his wolf Greyskin.
4. **Varamyr recalls the aftermath of the Battle at the Wall** — Thousands fleeing through the forest after Mance's defeat; companions deserting one by one; various leaders (the Weeper, Mother Mole, a Thenn-advocate) drawing followers in different directions.
5. **Varamyr recalls being stabbed** — A boy stabbed him with a bone knife over a dead woman's squirrel-skin cloak; Thistle sewed the wound.
6. **Varamyr discovers his fire has died** — He cannot relight it without flint or dry kindling; the hut grows colder.
7. **Varamyr recalls losing his beasts** — His eagle burned (killed by fire while soaring above the Wall); his shadowcat fled; his snow bear turned on those around her and was killed by a spear after killing four men.
8. **Varamyr pushes through snow wall blocking his door** — Snow had buried the hut; he breaks through to the outside night.
9. **Varamyr remembers his childhood** — Born premature as "Lump"; his brother Bump died at age two (killed by one of the dogs while Lump was skinchanging); father killed all three dogs with an axe, killing Loptail last while Lump was inside him; father then gave Lump to Haggon.
10. **Varamyr collapses in the snow** — His crutch breaks; he lies bleeding in the snow, thinking of death and his second life.
11. **Thistle returns and wakes Varamyr** — She shakes him, screaming that "hundreds of them" are coming.
12. **Varamyr attempts to seize Thistle's body** — He forces his spirit into her; she fights back violently — clawing at her own eyes, biting off her tongue, spitting it out.
13. **Varamyr fails and dies** — His attempt at body-seizure fails; his spirit passes through the weirwood and into the natural world momentarily (owl, hare, trees, earthworms, ravens, elk, a sleeping direwolf).
14. **Varamyr enters One Eye (second life)** — True death comes as a shock of cold; he finds himself in One Eye, running with Sly and Stalker.
15. **The pack sees the wights** — From a crest, the wolves look down at the village now filled with blue-eyed wights — some in brown, some in black, some naked with white flesh. Wight-Thistle has frozen blood icicles for fingers and pale blue light in her eye pits. She sees the wolves.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Oscillates between desperate survival instinct, shame over cowardice and past sins, hunger, fear of true death, brief exultation when spirit dissolves into nature, and finally animal peace/grief in the wolf
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival — finding a way to cheat death, either through the second life (entering a wolf) or the abomination (seizing Thistle's body)
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to attempt seizing Thistle's body rather than accept death; when that fails, enters One Eye as his second life
- **Self-deception flags:** "A mercy" — tells himself the wolf-pack killing of the fleeing family was merciful; "That was the beast, not me" — attributes his worst acts to the animals; justifies past rapes by noting "he never harmed the women"; rationalizes Haggon's death by calling him "weak"
