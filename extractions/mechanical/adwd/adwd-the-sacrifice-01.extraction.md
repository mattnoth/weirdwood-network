- **location_primary:** Crofter's village between two frozen lakes (three days' ride from Winterfell)
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell (referenced), Deepwood Motte (referenced), Dragonstone (referenced), Iron Islands (referenced), Karhold (referenced), Moat Cailin (referenced), Torrhen's Square (referenced), Tyrosh (referenced), Braavos (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** During Stannis's stalled march from Deepwood Motte to Winterfell; the host has been stuck three days from Winterfell for nineteen days
- **time_markers:** "a dark, cold, hungry day, like the day before and the day before that"; "three days since either of them had caught a fish"; "Nightfall would be on them soon"; "Eight days ago Asha had walked out with Aly Mormont" to the weirwood; "three days from Winterfell for nineteen days"; "Four days ago, one of the king's own squires had succumbed"; Lord Arnolf "had found them eight days past"; "Last night the count was sixty-four" (horses); "The cold count last night reached eighty"; "eight hundred horses when we marched from Deepwood Motte"; "before the moon can turn" (Arnolf's boast); "three days hence" (Foxglove's claim about resuming the march)
- **chapter_summary:** At the crofter's village where Stannis's host is snowbound, four Peasebury men caught eating a dead comrade are burned alive as sacrifices to R'hllor, over the protests of northmen who worship the old gods. In the longhall, lords and knights argue whether to march on Winterfell, stay put, or retreat, with no consensus reached. Ser Justin Massey, who has defended Asha against the queen's men wanting to burn her, is publicly humiliated by Ser Richard Horpe and storms out. Riders arrive through the blizzard: Tristifer Botley and other ironborn ransomed from Deepwood, escorting Tycho Nestoris of the Iron Bank of Braavos, who urgently seeks King Stannis. Nestoris presents Asha with a "gift" from Mors Umber — a frostbitten girl and a skeletal, barely recognizable man who reveals himself as Asha's brother Theon.

## Events & Actions
1. **Pyre construction** — Six queen's men wrestle pinewood poles into holes dug by six others on the village green; stakes are hammered into the frozen ground.
2. **Religious argument** — Artos Flint and Big Bucket Wull protest the burning as offensive to the old gods; Ser Godry Farring and Corliss Penny insist R'hllor will save them.
3. **Stannis arrives at the burning** — Stannis emerges from the watchtower with Ser Richard Horpe and Arnolf Karstark; two condemned men plead for mercy; Stannis listens in silence then tells Godry to begin.
4. **The defiant serjeant is silenced** — The oldest of the four condemned men taunts and curses the queen's men; Clayton Suggs slashes his throat, killing him before the burning.
5. **The burning** — Ser Godry leads a prayer to R'hllor; Ser Corliss Penny lights both pyres; the four men (three living, one dead) burn; the weeping young man screams as flames reach him; the crowd watches.
6. **Aftermath of the burning** — Stannis walks wordlessly back to the watchtower; Arnolf Karstark moves to follow but Ser Richard Horpe redirects him toward the longhall; watchers disperse.
7. **Suggs threatens Asha** — Clayton Suggs confronts Asha, pressing his face close, telling her the crowd will be bigger when she burns; Aly Mormont tells him to release her; Suggs insists Asha "is not for burning" yet.
8. **Justin Massey intervenes** — Massey arrives and asserts the king has other plans for Asha; Suggs counters that her king's blood has power; the She-Bear asks who would be next if Asha's burning doesn't stop the snow.
9. **Supper in the longhall** — Asha and Massey enter the longhall; wolves (northmen) sit on one side of the fire trench, southerners on the other; they eat horsemeat and drink ale.
10. **Debate over marching** — Will Foxglove claims Stannis will resume the march in three days; Massey argues they'll die by the hundreds; Humfrey Clifton wants to press on; Ormund Wylde urges staying; Peasebury notes too few fish; Noseless Ned declares the lakes fished out; Arnolf Karstark rallies northmen to march, invoking the Ned and the Young Wolf.
11. **Horpe confronts Massey** — Richard Horpe enters and advocates marching; questions Massey's courage and loyalty; Massey wilts under the pressure and storms out of the longhall.
12. **Asha follows Massey into the blizzard** — Asha goes after Massey but gets lost almost immediately in the whiteout; stumbles back to the village green where the charred stakes still stand; a raven feeds on the burned corpse.
13. **Suggs accosts Asha again** — Suggs finds her on the green, threatens her again, refuses to rape her only because he'd rather see her burn.
14. **Riders arrive** — Asha and Suggs hear horses; riders emerge from the storm — big men on small horses, armed with swords, axes, warhammers, shields. Suggs draws his sword and orders Asha to warn the king, thinking Bolton is upon them.
15. **Tristifer Botley reveals himself** — The lead rider is Tristifer Botley; he kneels to Asha and reports six ironborn survived (Cromm died); they were ransomed from Deepwood's dungeons.
16. **Tycho Nestoris introduces himself** — The tall man in the strange hat reveals himself as a servant of the Iron Bank of Braavos who paid the ransom and urgently needs to speak with King Stannis.
17. **Theon and the girl are delivered** — Tycho explains they found Mors Umber at Winterfell instead of the king; Umber gave them "a gift" for Asha — a frightened, frostbitten girl and a skeletal old man.
18. **Theon recognized** — The old man looks up and says "Sister. See. This time I knew you." Asha's heart skips a beat as she recognizes her brother Theon, who insists on repeating his own name.
