# ADWD — Daenerys VI

## Chapter Metadata

- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys rides out to the Astapori refugee camp outside Meereen's walls to personally oversee food distribution and tend to the sick and dying, shaming her escort into helping burn corpses and bathe the afflicted. She returns to her pyramid and meets with Reznak mo Reznak and the Green Grace about Meereenese wedding customs, most of which she refuses. Over supper with Hizdahr zo Loraq, she learns Yunkai's peace terms: indemnity, resumed slaving, a rebuilt Astapor, and Hizdahr crowned as king beside her. Daario Naharis returns wounded with news that the Yunkish host is marching north with four free companies, Ghiscari legions, elephants, and allied forces — and that Brown Ben Plumm and the Second Sons have defected to the enemy. Dany orders the gates of Meereen closed and takes Daario to her bed.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dany rides to the Astapori refugee camp** — She insists on going personally despite her advisors' objections, accompanied by Ser Barristan, her three bloodriders, Symon Stripeback, Marselen, and sixty soldiers guarding the food wagons.
2. **The Astapori follow in a ghastly procession** — They call out to Dany as "Mother" in many languages, begging for help, food, and medicine.
3. **Marselen reports the desperate conditions** — Scarcely a horse or mule remains; the refugees have eaten every animal and some have begun eating their dead.
4. **Dany observes the horrors of the camp** — Swollen-bellied children, gaunt dying men, two women fighting over a charred bone, a boy eating a rat, unburied dead covered in flies, skeletal women clutching dying infants.
5. **Dany orders the food wagons to halt and distribute** — Riders spread out to keep order; the Astapori press toward the wagons.
6. **Dany discusses options with Ser Barristan** — He urges preserving food for the garrison and proposes assembling an army; Dany prefers to fight from behind Meereen's walls.
7. **Dany sends Aggo to fetch Grey Worm and fifty Unsullied** — She wants the Unsullied to handle the dead, as they have "no fear of corpses."
8. **Dany dismounts and tends to a sick old man** — She kneels beside him, feels his brow, and calls for seawater and oil for the pyres, shaming her escort into helping.
9. **The escort begins humanitarian work** — Symon Stripeback's men separate the living from the dead and stack corpses; Jhogo, Rakharo, and their Dothraki help the living bathe in the sea.
10. **Grey Worm arrives and kneels to help** — He and the Unsullied join the work.
11. **Funeral pyres are built and lit** — A dozen fires burn before midday; columns of greasy black smoke rise.
12. **Grey Worm asks permission to bathe in the salt sea** — He reveals the Unsullied worship a goddess called the Lady of Spears / Bride of Battle / Mother of Hosts, whose true name is secret.
13. **Dany returns to her pyramid** — She finds Missandei reading a scroll and Irri and Jhiqui arguing over Rakharo.
14. **Dany bathes on her terrace** — Missandei washes her back and mentions hearing the Astapori scratching at the walls at night; Dany and the handmaids dismiss this.
15. **Reznak mo Reznak and Galazza Galare arrive** — They discuss Meereenese wedding traditions: the naked examination of the bride, the foot-washing ritual, the white tokar with baby pearls, the dark red veils, the women's cake.
16. **Dany refuses the examination and foot-washing** — She insists Hizdahr can inspect her after the wedding and that he must wash her feet if she is to wash his.
17. **Dany agrees to marry in the Temple of the Graces** — She accepts the white tokar with pearls but refuses the cake.
18. **Reznak proposes reopening the fighting pits as a wedding gift** — The Green Grace endorses it as pleasing to the gods.
19. **Dany defers the fighting pits to Hizdahr** — She says he can reopen them after the wedding if he wishes; she wants no part of it.
20. **Hizdahr zo Loraq arrives for supper** — He dismisses the wedding rituals as "empty" and "foolish old traditions"; he agrees to wash Dany's feet.
21. **Hizdahr reports Yunkai's peace terms** — Indemnity in gold and gems; Yunkai resumes slaving; Astapor rebuilt as a slave city; Dany not to interfere; Hizdahr to be crowned king beside her.
22. **Dany reacts bitterly** — "Marriage or carnage. A wedding or a war."
23. **Ser Barristan interrupts supper** — He reports that the Stormcrows have returned with word of the enemy; the Yunkishmen are on the march.
24. **Dany summons Daario despite Hizdahr's objection** — She dismisses Hizdahr and convenes her captains.
25. **Daario Naharis reports** — He is wounded; a crossbowman nearly shot him; he killed a mutinous serjeant by ripping out his heart. He reports: Astapor is gone; slavers marching north with four free companies; Yunkai'i host on the coast road with four Ghiscari legions, a hundred armored elephants, Tolosi slingers, Qartheen camelry; two more Ghiscari legions to land beyond the Skahazadhan.
26. **Daario reveals the Second Sons' defection** — Brown Ben Plumm has gone over to the Yunkai'i. The Stormcrows gained deserters from the Windblown and others.
27. **Dany reflects on the prophecy of three treasons** — She wonders if Brown Ben is the second or third treason and thinks of Ser Jorah.
28. **Uproar in the hall** — Reznak wails, the Shavepate mutters, bloodriders swear vengeance, Strong Belwas vows to eat Brown Ben's heart.
29. **Dany orders the gates closed** — "No one enters, no one leaves." She orders food gathered and every fighting man on the walls.
30. **Reznak asks about the Astapori** — Dany, anguished, repeats the order to close the gates.
31. **Dany dismisses everyone except Daario** — She takes him to her bedchamber; Irri and Jhiqui tend his wounds.
32. **Dany and Daario become lovers** — She makes him promise never to betray her; she tells him she wanted him from the first; they kiss and she invites him to bed.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Despair and guilt over the Astapori's suffering; disgust at the camp conditions; defiance against her advisors' caution; bitter resentment toward the wedding customs; dread at Yunkai's peace terms and the approaching army; shock and hurt at Brown Ben Plumm's betrayal; desire and longing for Daario; relief and passion when she finally takes him to bed
- **Primary preoccupation:** The impossible tension between her duty to the Astapori and the realities of siege warfare; the looming wedding to Hizdahr and what she must surrender; whether she can trust anyone — the prophecy of three treasons haunts her
- **Key decisions made:** (1) To personally ride out and tend the sick despite all advice; (2) To agree to Meereenese wedding customs at the Temple of the Graces; (3) To defer the fighting pits to Hizdahr; (4) To close the gates — "no one enters, no one leaves" — effectively condemning the Astapori; (5) To take Daario as her lover
- **Self-deception flags:** Dany acknowledges Daario is "fickle, treacherous" and a sellsword who boasted of a hundred (now a thousand) women — yet chooses to believe his promise never to betray her. She tells herself "If I look back I am lost" as a mantra to avoid confronting the consequences of closing the gates. She pushes aside Daario's earlier violent counsel ("fire and blood") as "not worthy of her" but then sleeps with him.
