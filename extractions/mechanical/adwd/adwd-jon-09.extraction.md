- **chapter_number:** 45

- **time_markers:** Melisandre warned Jon of Selyse's coming "almost a day before" the raven arrived; the negotiation with Tycho takes "the better part of an hour" plus "another hour"; Jon falls asleep at his desk and wakes at "a golden dawn"; the day darkens and snow falls during the transition between negotiation and the common hall; Alys says she was six the last time Jon saw her; Alys says Harry's capture at Maidenpool was "almost a year ago"
- **chapter_summary:** Queen Selyse arrives at Castle Black with her retinue and Tycho Nestoris of the Iron Bank. Jon negotiates a deal with the banker: three Braavosi ships and a loan to feed the Watch through winter in exchange for guides and horses to reach Stannis. After falling asleep at his desk, Jon is woken by Mully with news that a girl has been found — not Arya as he hoped, but Alys Karstark, who has fled Karhold to escape forced marriage to her cousin Cregan. Alys reveals that her great-uncle Arnolf Karstark is secretly allied with Roose Bolton and plans to betray Stannis at Winterfell.

## Events & Actions
1. **Queen Selyse arrives at Castle Black** — Descends with daughter Shireen, Patchface, knights, sworn swords, men-at-arms fifty strong, and Tycho Nestoris of the Iron Bank.
2. **Jon greets the queen** — Takes a knee; she mistakes his youth for a stableboy's rank; he identifies himself as Lord Commander.
3. **Introductions** — Selyse introduces Shireen, Ser Axell Florent, eight knights, and Tycho Nestoris.
4. **Encounter with Wun Wun** — The giant emerges from behind the Lord Commander's Tower; the queen's party reacts with fear; Shireen is fascinated.
5. **Patchface triggers Wun Wun** — The fool dances and catches the giant's interest; when Wun Wun reaches for him, Patchface falls; Wun Wun laughs enormously.
6. **Ser Patrek draws steel** — A knight draws his sword at Wun Wun; Jon blocks his path and orders Leathers to escort the giant to Hardin's.
7. **Jon confronts Ser Patrek about guest right** — Invokes northern custom; Ser Patrek sarcastically asks if Jon would offer hospitality to the Others.
8. **Queen proceeds to King's Tower** — Takes Ser Patrek's arm and sweeps past the Watch without a second glance.
9. **Jon engages Tycho Nestoris** — Asks for a quiet word; they go to Jon's solar rather than the top of the Wall.
10. **Jon and Tycho negotiate** — Discussion of Robert's debts, the Iron Throne's default, and Stannis's potential claim; takes two hours plus mulled wine.
11. **Agreement signed** — Jon trades guides/horses to Deepwood Motte for Tycho's three ships and a loan of gold to feed the Watch through winter. Both men half-drunk and unhappy — Jon considers this a good sign.
12. **Jon assesses fleet strength** — Three Braavosi ships bring Eastwatch fleet to eleven total. Still not enough, but delay means free folk at Hardhome die.
13. **Jon and Tycho walk through snow** — Tycho reports ice on Braavos canals; Jon asks about Maester Aemon's party; Tycho mentions strange ships in the Stepstones and talk of dragons.
14. **Jon and Tycho eat in common hall** — The cellar is crowded; Ser Brus and Ser Malegorn entertain brothers with news; three queen's ladies attract admirers.
15. **Ser Axell confronts Jon about Val** — Demands to see the "wildling princess"; lewd and threatening; implies he'll go to the queen.
16. **Jon deflects and leaves** — Claims Val is "somewhere safe" and exits before Ser Axell can escalate.
17. **Jon returns to solar** — Reads Tycho's agreement three times; reflects on the Iron Bank's reputation and consequences of debt; considers that if Stannis accepts terms, he may win the Iron Throne.
18. **Jon falls asleep at his desk** — Wakes stiff as a board; Mully rouses him at dawn.
19. **News of a girl found** — Ty and Dannel found a girl two leagues south of Mole's Town while chasing wildlings on the kingsroad. She arrived alone on a dying horse, asking for Jon.
20. **Jon crosses the yard hoping for Arya** — Sees Melisandre's light still burning; thinks of what he'd do with Arya (send her east across the sea).
21. **Jon discovers it's not Arya** — The girl resembles Arya but is too old; she is tall, skinny, with brown hair and a long face.
22. **Alys Karstark identifies herself** — Reminds Jon of their meeting at Winterfell when she was six; they danced at a feast.
23. **Alys reveals her situation** — Fled Karhold; her brothers Torr and Edd dead; Harry prisoner at Maidenpool; great-uncle Arnolf is castellan; his son Cregan pursuing her for forced marriage.
24. **Alys reveals Arnolf's treachery** — Arnolf allied with Roose Bolton for gold, a pardon, and Harry's head; plans to put a dagger in Stannis's back at Winterfell.
25. **Alys begs Jon's protection** — Kneels before him, invokes his father's name, calls him her only hope.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Guarded and politically alert during queen's arrival; shrewd during negotiations; anxious about the fleet to Hardhome; intensely hopeful then disappointed when the girl is not Arya; protective toward Alys by chapter's end
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing resources (ships, gold) for the Watch's survival; managing the political complications of Selyse's presence; the fleet to Hardhome; then the revelation about Arnolf's treachery
- **Key decisions made:** Signs loan agreement with Iron Bank (ships + gold for guides/horses); decides to plan Hardhome rescue ("Sail now or not at all"); plans to send "Arya" (before learning it's Alys) across the sea to safety; refuses to produce Val for Ser Axell
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon briefly allows himself to believe the girl is Arya despite Melisandre's vision being "a grey girl on a dying horse" (not specifically Arya); he feels "fifteen years old again" and imagines protecting "little sister" before reality corrects him
