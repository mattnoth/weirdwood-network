# ADWD — The Spurned Suitor I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 61
- **pov_character:** Quentyn Martell
- **pov_chapter_number:** The Spurned Suitor I
- **first_available:** ADWD The Spurned Suitor I
- **location_primary:** Meereen
- **location_secondary:** The Purple Lotus (cellar winesink), Great Pyramid of Meereen, old spice market, pyramid of Rhazdar
- **approximate_timeline:** During the siege of Meereen by Yunkai; after the peace between Meereen and Yunkai; after Daenerys's flight on Drogon and her marriage to Hizdahr; after the death of Yurkhaz zo Yunzak and Yezzan zo Qaggaz
- **time_markers:** "The hour of ghosts was almost upon them" (opening — late night); "The next night" (Denzo's arrival); "an hour shy of sunset" (departure for the meeting); "The sun had sunk below the city wall" (arrival at the Purple Lotus); "At this hour" (the house less than half full); "a moon's turn" (how long Pretty Meris can stretch out a man's dying); "a fortnight" — not used, but travel days implied in discussions of Volantis route
- **chapter_summary:** Quentyn Martell, having failed to win Daenerys Targaryen's hand in marriage, resolves to stay in Meereen despite his companions' urgings to flee. He arranges a meeting with the Tattered Prince, leader of the Windblown sellsword company, whom Quentyn and his friends had previously betrayed by deserting. In a cellar beneath the Purple Lotus, Quentyn proposes that Dorne hire the Windblown at double their Yunkish pay. When the Tattered Prince presses for what service is required, Quentyn reveals he needs help stealing a dragon. The Tattered Prince names his price: Pentos.

## Events & Actions
1. **Gerris returns with report** — Ser Gerris Drinkwater returns to the Great Pyramid at nearly the hour of ghosts and reports he found Beans, Books, and Old Bill Bone in a cellar watching slave fights and drinking yellow wine.
2. **Gerris relays Beans's response** — Beans initially pulled a blade, but accepted a gold dragon and agreed to deliver a message to the Tattered Prince, predicting Pretty Meris would cut out Quentyn's liver.
3. **Debate about fleeing** — Gerris urges fleeing to Volantis by ship, citing Barristan Selmy's advice to run. Archibald refuses ships, turning green-cheeked at the mention.
4. **Quentyn resolves to stay** — Quentyn contemplates returning to Dorne empty-handed but decides to stay in Meereen, releasing his friends from obligation. Both Gerris and Archibald choose to stay with him.
5. **Denzo D'han arrives with terms** — The next night, Denzo D'han comes to Quentyn's door to arrange the meeting: by the spice market, door marked with a purple lotus, knock twice and call for freedom, at sunset.
6. **Dornishmen depart the Great Pyramid** — An hour before sunset, Quentyn, Gerris (both with sword belts), and Archibald (warhammer on back) leave the pyramid.
7. **Walk through Meereen's streets** — They traverse a foetid alley toward the old spice market, smelling piss and hearing a corpse cart. They debate the plan and discuss the dragonslayer Harghaz's death.
8. **Discussion of Daenerys and the dragons** — Quentyn recalls Daenerys telling him "the dragon has three heads" and that he has Targaryen blood. Gerris argues dragons won't care about lineage. Quentyn insists the deaths of Cletus, Will, and Maester Kedry must have meaning.
9. **Corpse observed** — They pass a corpse slumped against a brick wall, attended by green flies — a flux victim. Quentyn notes the pale mare is inside the city walls.
10. **Arrival at the Purple Lotus** — They find the painted purple lotus on a weathered door in the shadow of the pyramid of Rhazdar. Quentyn knocks twice and answers "Freedom" in the mongrel tongue of Slaver's Bay.
11. **Entry into the winesink** — They enter: the space is larger than expected, stretching through adjoining hovels into one long hall, hazy with bluish smoke, less than half full, with a fighting pit at the far end where naked men slash at each other with knives.
12. **Zahrina directs them below** — An old woman in a dark red tokar fringed with golden skulls identifies herself as Zahrina and directs them through a hidden door and down steep, dark wooden steps.
13. **Descent to the undercellar** — Archibald leads, Quentyn in the middle, Gerris as rear guard. Near the bottom, Archibald draws his dagger. The undercellar is a brick vault thrice the size of the winesink above, lined with huge wooden wine vats, lit only by a red lantern and a greasy black candle.
14. **Meeting the Windblown leaders** — Caggo Corpsekiller paces by wine vats with his black arakh. Pretty Meris cradles a crossbow. Denzo bars the door behind them. The Tattered Prince sits nursing wine. Three against three — one too many by Quentyn's count.
15. **Tattered Prince's appearance noted** — Without his signature tattered cloak, wearing a brown wool traveler's cloak over chain mail. Silver-grey hair looks almost golden in candlelight. Deep pouches under his eyes.
16. **Negotiation opens** — The Tattered Prince seats Quentyn, offers food and drink, promises not to kill him until he's heard him out, and addresses him as "Prince Frog."
17. **Tattered Prince recounts Windblown grievances** — He catalogs the Dornishmen's betrayal: they swore swords, took coin, deserted, and their lies put several Windblown men in Meereen's dungeons. He tells the anecdote of punishing a deserter by feeding him his own roasted foot.
18. **Quentyn explains the marriage pact** — He reveals his duty to his father and the secret marriage pact. The Tattered Prince and Meris mock its failure — Daenerys married Hizdahr, flew off on a dragon.
19. **Discussion of Yunkish command** — Quentyn raises the death of Yurkhaz zo Yunzak (trampled fleeing a dragon) and Yezzan zo Qaggaz (died of illness). The Yunkish masters now rotate supreme command daily — today the Drunken Conqueror, yesterday Wobblecheeks, tomorrow the Rabbit.
20. **Quentyn presses the strategic argument** — He argues the peace means no battle, no plunder, and the Yunkishmen won't pay four free companies forever.
21. **Tattered Prince surveys Meereen's instability** — He describes multiple factions in tension: Bloodbeard urging violence, freedmen and slavers at each other's throats, Sons of the Harpy plotting, the pale mare killing indiscriminately, Yunkishmen watching the sea, and a dragon eating Daenerys in the grasslands.
22. **Quentyn offers Dorne's contract** — He proposes Dorne will hire the Windblown at double the Yunkish pay, with partial payment in Volantis from banked gold and the rest upon reaching Sunspear, secured by banking papers and his princely seal.
23. **Tattered Prince and Meris mock the offer** — "Twice as many papers." The Tattered Prince characterizes Quentyn as "a proven liar and oathbreaker" offering promises.
24. **Quentyn reveals his true request** — "I need you to help me steal a dragon." Caggo chuckles, Meris half-smiles, Denzo whistles.
25. **Tattered Prince names his price** — He says double doesn't pay for dragons and that men who pay in promises should promise more. When Quentyn offers to triple, the Tattered Prince states: "What I want is Pentos."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined but isolated; weighed down by guilt over the deaths of Cletus, Will, and Maester Kedry; longing for home (Greenblood, Sunspear, Water Gardens, Yronwood's mountain air); aware of the danger but resolved; slightly contemptuous of the Meereenese ("Harzoo" correction shows his discomfort with companions' dismissiveness); feels the rest of Meereen sees him as "an amusing curiosity"
- **Primary preoccupation:** Fulfilling his mission by obtaining a dragon — specifically, "the road leads through her, not to her"; Daenerys is the means, not the end; believes he must give meaning to his companions' deaths
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to stay in Meereen rather than flee; decides to approach the Tattered Prince despite the danger; decides to reveal his plan to steal a dragon; implicitly accepts the negotiation will continue (chapter ends on the Tattered Prince's demand for Pentos)
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes his Targaryen blood gives him a special connection to dragons ("I am a prince of Dorne, and the blood of dragons is in my veins"); frames the dragon-stealing plan as destiny rather than desperation; tells himself "they are men like any other men" about sellswords to minimize the danger; insists the deaths of his friends must have "meaning" — using their deaths to justify increasingly reckless action
