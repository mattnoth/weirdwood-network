He signs the company book in blood as "Tyrion of House Lannister, Lord of Casterly Rock." He and Penny are sent to the company wagons to find armor, guided by Kem, a former King's Landing soldier. They encounter Jorah Mormont already outfitted in company steel. Tyrion selects a dirk while trying to equip Penny despite her reluctance and grief. Jorah assesses that the Yunkai'i are losing the war, and Tyrion announces his intention to turn the Second Sons' cloaks again.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion signs promissory notes** — Tyrion signs approximately fifty to sixty parchments promising one hundred golden dragons each to Second Sons serjeants, using ink from Old Volantis. He signs as "Tyrion of House Lannister."
2. **Higher-value notes signed** — Halfway through the pile, the amounts change to one thousand golden dragons per note for higher-ranking members.
3. **Named personal notes signed** — Three special notes at the bottom: 10,000 dragons each for Kasporio the Cunning and Inkpots (Tybero Istarion), written on fine vellum and made out by name.
4. **Brown Ben's sheepskin scroll signed** — The final note promises Brown Ben Plumm 100,000 golden dragons, fifty hides of fertile land, a castle, and a lordship, inscribed on sheepskin.
5. **Tyrion signs the company book in blood** — Inkpots brings the leather-bound company book. Tyrion pricks his thumb with Inkpots' dagger, squeezes blood into the inkpot, and signs "Tyrion of House Lannister, Lord of Casterly Rock" just below Jorah Mormont's signature.
6. **Tyrion is formally welcomed to the Second Sons** — Inkpots welcomes him as "Lord Tyrion." Tyrion is assigned to work for Inkpots keeping books, counting coin, and writing contracts and letters.
7. **Brown Ben warns Tyrion to stay hidden** — He tells Tyrion to stay inside, avoid the latrines (too many eyes), not go beyond camp without leave, and avoid the poxy, talkative whores. They will disguise him in squire's steel as Jorah's "butt boy."
8. **Tyrion wakes Penny** — He finds her asleep on a thin straw pallet; she is pale and possibly feverish. He internally worries she may have the pale mare but dares not say it aloud.
9. **Tyrion tells Penny he has joined the Second Sons** — Penny asks to sign too; Tyrion says no. She asks about Pretty Pig and Crunch.
10. **Tyrion lies to Penny about the slave-catchers** — Kasporio reported three Yunkish slave-catchers prowling the camps with a dog's head on a spear; Tyrion withholds this from Penny.
11. **Snatch sends Kem to escort the dwarfs to the wagons** — Snatch assigns Kem to take Tyrion and Penny to the armory wagons and have Hammer outfit them.
12. **Tyrion and Kem converse en route** — Kem reveals he is from King's Landing / Flea Bottom, fought for Stannis at the Blackwater, dropped his spear and ran, was left behind at the ships, and crossed the narrow sea to avoid being sent to the Wall. He misses a friend and his brother Kennet who died on the bridge of ships. They discuss bowl o' brown and Casterly Rock.
13. **Tyrion and Penny search the armory wagons** — They find mostly poor-quality armor ("crap iron"). Jorah Mormont appears already fully outfitted in mismatched company steel.
14. **Tyrion selects a dirk** — He rejects a morningstar (too heavy), warhammer (too long), studded mace (too heavy), half a dozen longswords, and a crossbow (can't use stirrup winch). He takes a dirk with a triangular blade.
15. **Tyrion instructs Penny on combat** — He advises keeping cuts below the knee to bring down larger opponents.
16. **Penny breaks down about her brother** — She recalls dreaming of jousting with Groat, riding Crunch and Pretty Pig, with men throwing roses.
17. **Tyrion slaps Penny** — A soft blow to snap her out of grief; tells her to find armor and that the mummer show is over.
18. **Penny mourns their slavery** — She says it wasn't so bad with Yezzan; they were his special slaves, his treasures. Tyrion corrects her: "Slaves. The word you want is slaves."
19. **Tyrion reflects on slavery** — He considers that there has never been a slave who did not choose to be a slave, and does not except himself from this observation.
20. **Tyrion lies to Penny about Yezzan's other slaves** — He tells her Sweets and the others died of the pale mare, though he does not actually know their fates.
21. **Tyrion promises to sell Penny back into slavery if she wishes** — Offers to find her a kind Yunkish master with a golden collar after the war, if she wants.
22. **Jorah assesses the military situation** — He declares the Yunkai'i have lost the war: Meereen has Unsullied infantry and three dragons once the queen returns. The Yunkish side has poorly trained slave soldiers.
23. **Tyrion announces plan to turn the Second Sons' cloaks** — He tells Jorah and Penny to leave it to him.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Sardonic, calculating, and performatively compliant; privately amused at buying "steel swords with parchment dragons"; moments of genuine melancholy when thinking of Jaime, Shae, and Tysha; impatient and harsh with Penny's passivity; deeply pragmatic about slavery and survival
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing his position within the Second Sons while privately planning to turn their allegiance to Meereen; getting armor and preparing for the coming battle; managing Penny's despair
- **Key decisions made:** Signs all promissory notes without protest (decides mummery of outrage isn't worth it); signs the company book in blood as his true identity; selects a dirk as his weapon; decides to conceal the slave-catcher news from Penny; announces plan to turn the Second Sons' cloaks; slaps Penny to shake her from grief
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion's philosophical musing that "there has never been a slave who did not choose to be a slave" — he does not except himself but this framing minimizes the coercion inherent in slavery. His internal justification for lying to Penny ("if Pretty Penny needed lies to stop her mooning, lie to her he would") frames deception as kindness.
