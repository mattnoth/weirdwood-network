# ADWD — Reek II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 21
- **pov_character:** Reek (Theon Greyjoy)
- **pov_chapter_number:** Reek II
- **first_available:** ADWD Reek II
- **location_primary:** Moat Cailin
- **location_secondary:** Bolton camp (north of Moat Cailin), the causeway through the Neck
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Reek's reappearance; Ramsay's forces are positioned north of Moat Cailin while Roose Bolton's host approaches from the south along the causeway
- **time_markers:** "last night" (Ramsay's instructions to Reek); morning mist and morning sunlight during approach; "Ramsay had only given him a day"; "the better part of the afternoon" to organize the surrender; "That night" (Reek's meal among the dogs); "The next morning" (Ramsay sends riders south); "Three days later" (Roose Bolton's vanguard arrives); "half a thousand years" (how long the Drunkard's Tower has leaned); "a few days back" (when Dagon Codd checked the Children's Tower)
- **chapter_summary:** Ramsay Bolton sends Reek to negotiate the surrender of the ironborn garrison at Moat Cailin. Reek enters the ruins, mercy-kills the dying commander Ralf Kenning, and persuades the starving defenders to yield by presenting Ramsay's false promise of safe conduct. Adrack Humble kills the resistant Dagon Codd with a throwing axe, and sixty-three ironborn surrender. Ramsay rewards Reek by making him a dog with a collar and chain. All sixty-three prisoners are killed and their corpses staked along the causeway. Three days later, Roose Bolton arrives from the south with his host, a decoy wearing his armor, and introduces Ramsay to his new wife Fat Walda and his betrothed "Arya Stark"—whom Reek recognizes as Jeyne Poole.

## Events & Actions
1. **Reek sent from Bolton camp** — Damon Dance-for-Me helps Reek mount a broken-down stot and gives him a peace banner, woolen doublet, and fur cloak. Damon warns Reek to come back with the castle or be hunted.
2. **Reek rides south toward Moat Cailin** — He rides alone along the remnants of the log-and-plank road Robb Stark's vanguard once laid. He passes corpses, a rotting horse with a white snake in its eye socket, and blood-bloom flowers growing from the dead.
3. **Reek suppresses his identity** — He reminds himself he is Reek, not the other man who came this way before. He recalls Ramsay's instructions from the night before: pretend to be a prince, but remember you are Reek.
4. **Arrow fired at Reek** — As the Gatehouse Tower doors open, an arrow from the broken curtain wall tears through his banner. Reek falls from the saddle and scrambles inside on hands and knees.
5. **Gate guard confronts Reek** — A bearded ironborn guard holds a knife to Reek's throat and demands identification. Reek claims to be Lord Balon's son. The guard reveals Ralf Kenning commands but is dying.
6. **Reek sees the state of the garrison** — The guard describes men drinking contaminated water and dying of bad bellies, dead left where they fall, cellars flooded with rats and white water snakes.
7. **Reek taken to Ralf Kenning** — The guard leads Reek up a spiral stair to a dark, foul chamber where Kenning lies shivering beneath furs, his body grotesquely swollen and rotting from a poisoned crannogman arrow.
8. **Reek mercy-kills Ralf Kenning** — Reek takes Kenning's sword and cuts his swollen throat, releasing black blood and yellow pus. Kenning jerks and lies still.
9. **Reek addresses the garrison in the hall** — Twenty-four ironborn sit drinking at a carved stone table. Reek announces Kenning's death and presents Ramsay's terms: surrender and receive safe conduct, or die.
10. **Dagon Codd refuses to yield** — Codd draws his longsword, calls Reek "Turncloak," and threatens to pull out his entrails.
11. **Adrack Humble kills Dagon Codd** — The one-armed man buries a throwing axe in Codd's forehead. Codd crashes face-first onto the table. Humble asks who else wants to die.
12. **Ironborn agree to surrender** — No one challenges Humble. Reek knows he has won.
13. **Reek pulls down the kraken banner** — He takes down the Greyjoy banner with his own hands, fumbling because of missing fingers.
14. **Garrison assembles for departure** — Forty-seven in the Gatehouse Tower and eighteen in the Drunkard's Tower; two near death, five too weak to walk; fifty-eight fit to march. Reek orders them to leave weapons behind.
15. **Reek leads column back to Bolton camp** — Slow going with crude litters for four wounded and one carried on his son's back. All exposed to crannogman arrows on the march.
16. **Adrack Humble talks to Reek** — Humble shares he has a rock wife and three salt wives on Great Wyk, most pregnant when he sailed; says he might name a son after Reek.
17. **Ryswell lordling meets the column** — Roger or Rickard Ryswell rides up; surprised there are so few prisoners, notes the ironborn repelled three attacks.
18. **Ramsay arrives with his entourage** — Hounds bay; Whoresbane, Skinner, Sour Alyn, Damon, and the Walders accompany him. Dogs swarm the strangers.
19. **Reek kneels and surrenders Moat Cailin** — Reek dismounts and presents the castle and its last defenders to Ramsay.
20. **Ramsay promises food and care for prisoners** — He orders wine, ale, food, and medical attention for the ironborn. A Codd tries to kiss Ramsay's ring but a hound named Alison bites his ear.
21. **Ramsay tests Reek** — Ramsay kisses Reek's cheek and offers to "free" him. Reek senses the trap and begs to stay as Ramsay's creature, asking only for wine.
22. **Ramsay makes Reek a dog** — Ramsay laughs, denies Reek is a man, promises meat and teeth, and orders Ben Bones to make a collar. Reek is collared, chained, and put back in rags.
23. **Reek's night among the hounds** — Ben Bones gives him a collar, blanket, and half a chicken. Reek fights the dogs for the meat, drinks dark sour wine until he vomits, and sleeps. He wakes to a dog licking vomit from his beard and hears men screaming in the night.
24. **Ramsay sends riders south** — The next morning, three riders are dispatched down the causeway to tell Roose Bolton the way is clear.
25. **Bolton banner raised; prisoners killed** — The flayed man is hoisted above the Gatehouse Tower. Sixty-three corpses are staked along the rotting-plank road, including one missing half an arm and another with the unbroken parchment shoved between its teeth.
26. **Roose Bolton's vanguard arrives** — Three days later, four hundred mounted Freys in blue and grey ride through, led by Hosteen and Aenys Frey.
27. **Northern host follows** — Tattered banners; mostly afoot; very few compared to the twenty thousand who marched south with Robb. Most returnees are Dreadfort men.
28. **Roose Bolton arrives in enclosed wagon** — A decoy rider wears Roose's distinctive dark grey plate and pink cloak. Roose himself emerges from behind velvet curtains of an enclosed wagon defended by crossbowmen.
29. **Ramsay discovers the decoy** — Ramsay's smile curdles when the rider's helm is removed revealing an unknown face. Roose calls it "just caution."
30. **Roose introduces Fat Walda and "Arya Stark"** — Roose presents Lady Walda (his new wife) and "Lady Arya" (Ramsay's betrothed). Reek recognizes the girl as Jeyne Poole, the steward's daughter and Sansa's friend—not the real Arya Stark.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Dominated by fear—fear of Ramsay, fear of failure, fear of the hunt. Brief flashes of pride (ironborn identity) immediately suppressed. A bleak resignation runs throughout; he views death as preferable to returning empty-handed. At the end, a numb, degraded contentment (half a chicken is "the best meal since Winterfell").
- **Primary preoccupation:** Maintaining his identity as Reek. Constant internal rhyming mantras ("Reek, it rhymes with leek/squeak/cheek/leak/weak/freak"). He must not think of himself as Theon, must not remember the other man who came this way before. Simultaneously, he must temporarily pretend to be a prince to accomplish his mission—a dangerous balancing act.
- **Key decisions made:** Kills Ralf Kenning when it becomes clear the dying man cannot command a surrender. When Ramsay offers to "free" him (a trap), Reek chooses the safe answer—begging to stay as Ramsay's creature and asking only for wine. Both decisions show survival calculation overriding everything else.
- **Self-deception flags:** Reek tells himself "Lord Ramsay treats his captives honorably so long as they keep faith with him" while knowing this is false. He tells himself "He has only taken toes and fingers and that other thing, when he might have had my tongue" as if this is mercy. He suppresses every surfacing Theon-memory as dangerous. He rationalizes his degradation ("the best meal he'd had since Winterfell") as a reward.
