# ADWD — Jon XIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ADWD
- **chapter_number:** 70

- **location_primary:** Castle Black

- **approximate_timeline:** Late in ADWD; deep winter, heavy snowfall; the same day Tormund arrives back from settling his people at Oakenshield
- **time_markers:** Evening watch (assembly at Shieldhall scheduled for then); change of watch (Horse and Rory replace Fulk and Mully); night ("The night is dark, and the paths will be slippery"); storm ongoing; Tormund expected back "this afternoon" and arrives when shoveling is done; two hours of discussion between Jon and Tormund before the Shieldhall meeting
- **chapter_summary:** Jon petitions Queen Selyse to help the starving free folk at Hardhome; she refuses and instead arranges marriages between Gerrick Kingsblood's daughters and her knights, and proposes Val wed Ser Patrek. Melisandre warns Jon that all his ships are lost and urges him to look to the skies. Jon meets with Bowen Marsh and Othell Yarwyck who also counsel letting the wildlings die. A letter arrives from Ramsay Bolton claiming Stannis is dead and demanding his bride, Reek, and others. Jon announces at the Shieldhall that he will ride south to Winterfell while Tormund leads the Hardhome ranging. The wildlings rally to Jon. Outside, Wun Wun kills Ser Patrek. In the chaos, Bowen Marsh, Wick Whittlestick, and others stab Jon repeatedly—"For the Watch."

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon petitions Queen Selyse** — Jon asks for help for the starving free folk at Hardhome; Selyse refuses, saying "Let them die" and suggesting they be "reborn into the light."
2. **Selyse announces marriages** — Gerrick Kingsblood's three daughters are betrothed to Ser Axell Florent, Ser Brus Buckler, and Ser Malegorn.
3. **Selyse proposes Val's marriage** — She declares Val shall wed Ser Patrek of King's Mountain. Jon warns about free folk marriage customs.
4. **Jon is dismissed** — The queen cuts him off and sends him away.
5. **Melisandre intercepts Jon on the stairs** — She warns him the ships are all lost (seen in her fires), tells him to look to the skies, and calls herself his only hope.
6. **Leathers reports Toregg's return** — Tormund has settled people at Oakenshield; expected back with eighty fighting men.
7. **Ghost is agitated** — Mully and Fulk report Ghost tried to bite them; Jon finds Ghost pacing and bristling, refusing to calm.
8. **Jon studies maps** — He examines routes to Hardhome, noting the coastal route from Eastwatch is faster and easier.
9. **Meeting with Marsh and Yarwyck** — Both counsel "let them die"; Marsh rubs his scar; Yarwyck drinks; neither offers constructive help.
10. **Jon reflects on the Night's Watch's lack of good leaders** — He thinks of Aemon, Sam, Qhorin, the Old Bear, Donal Noye.
11. **Snow buries ice cells** — Jon orders them dug out; Wun Wun helps. Prisoners are moved.
12. **Cregan Karstark moved** — Karstark is dragged from ice cell to Lord Commander's Tower undervault, fighting all the way.
13. **Corpses left in buried cells** — Jon decides to leave the two dead men entombed in snow, bound in iron chains.
14. **Tormund arrives** — With about fifty warriors (not the promised eighty). Reacts to news of "Gerrick Kingsblood" being named King of the Wildlings with mockery.
15. **Tormund tells the story of the Red Raven** — Raymun Redbeard's younger brother who fled battle at Long Lake.
16. **Ramsay Bolton's letter arrives** — Clydas brings it, trembling. Sealed in pink wax, addressed to "Bastard." Claims Stannis dead, Mance captured, demands bride, queen, princess, red witch, wildling princess, wildling babe, and Reek.
17. **Jon reads the letter to Tormund** — Tormund cannot read. They discuss its likely truth. Tormund suggests it could be lies; Jon identifies details that confirm some truth.
18. **Jon decides to ride south** — After thinking of his siblings and Mance's fate, he says "I think we had best change the plan." They talk for two hours.
19. **Shieldhall assembly** — Jon announces Tormund will lead the Hardhome ranging. Jon reads Ramsay's letter aloud. The hall goes mad.
20. **Jon calls for volunteers to ride south** — He says the Watch takes no part, but he personally will ride to Winterfell. Wildlings rally massively—Soren, Toregg, Brogg, both Harles, Ygon, Blind Doss, the Great Walrus volunteer.
21. **Marsh and Yarwyck slip out** — They and their men leave the Shieldhall during the tumult.
22. **Wun Wun kills Ser Patrek** — Outside Hardin's Tower, the giant is found swinging Ser Patrek's bloody corpse, tearing him apart. Wun Wun has sword cuts on belly and arm.
23. **Jon attempts to control the situation** — He orders a line formed, tells Leathers to speak Old Tongue to calm Wun Wun.
24. **Wick Whittlestick slashes Jon's throat** — Jon twists away, only grazed. He disarms Wick.
25. **Bowen Marsh stabs Jon in the belly** — Weeping, says "For the Watch."
26. **Jon is stabbed repeatedly** — Third dagger between shoulder blades; fourth knife; Jon falls face-first into the snow, whispering "Ghost." He feels only the cold.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Frustrated and disappointed by universal refusal to help Hardhome; contemptuous of Selyse ("This queen never fails to disappoint"); increasingly isolated from his own officers; shocked by Ghost's aggression; deeply stirred by Ramsay's letter—thinking of his siblings with protective love and rage; resolute in his decision to ride south; distracted in his final moments, thinking of Melisandre and the queen; surprised and confused when stabbed
- **Primary preoccupation:** The impossible logistics of saving Hardhome versus the personal call to ride south after Ramsay's letter; balancing his oath with his desire to protect his family
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to hand the Hardhome ranging to Tormund; decides to ride south to Winterfell personally; decides to call for volunteers rather than order brothers to break vows; decides not to reveal Mance's glamor publicly
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells himself he is not making his brothers break their vows ("No man can ever say I made my brothers break their vows. If this is oathbreaking, the crime is mine and mine alone") while clearly inspiring wildlings to follow him south in what amounts to a military campaign; he dismisses Melisandre's warnings despite evidence she may be right about the letter (she saw the raven coming)
