Jon reveals that Mother Mole has led thousands of free folk to Hardhome and warns that the dead will rise as wights if those people perish.

## Events & Actions
1. **Val departs beyond the Wall** — Jon sends Val north to find Tormund Giantsbane, mounted on a half-blind garron, provisioned with salt-preserved food. She promises to return by the first day of the full moon.
2. **Val asks about Jarl's death** — Val confirms Jon did not kill Jarl; Jon says "The Wall killed Jarl."
3. **Val warns Jon about Melisandre and Monster** — Val tells Jon to keep Dalla's baby away from the red woman, who sees kings and dragons in her fires and knows who the baby is.
4. **Passage through the Wall tunnel** — Dolorous Edd leads with torch; Mully has keys to three iron-barred gates; spearmen salute Jon but stare at Val.
5. **Val rides into the haunted forest** — She vanishes behind soldier pines heading north.
6. **Jon returns to chambers; notes Ghost's absence** — Ghost is away hunting, ranging farther due to depleted game near Castle Black.
7. **Jon eats breakfast; raven steals bacon** — Edd brings food; Jon eats sparingly; Mormont's raven takes the bacon.
8. **Bowen Marsh, Yarwyck, and Cellador visit** — They arrive quickly after Val's departure to raise objections.
9. **Discussion of Nightfort repairs** — Yarwyck reports keep mostly restored, roof on kitchens; Queen Selyse wants to move there from Eastwatch.
10. **Jon offers Wun Wun as worker; Yarwyck refuses** — Yarwyck is uncomfortable with the giant; Jon keeps Wun Wun at Castle Black.
11. **Marsh objects to Leathers as master-at-arms** — Jon defends the choice: Leathers is dangerous with a stone axe and can teach the Old Tongue.
12. **Cellador objects to Satin as Jon's squire** — Jon defends Satin and cites worse men in the Watch (rapist cook, family-burning arsonist).
13. **Marsh raises concern about corpses in ice cells** — Jon says he hopes the corpses rise, intending to study wights; officers are horrified.
14. **Confrontation about Val's release** — Marsh calls it potential treason; Jon reveals Val carries his offer to Tormund.
15. **Jon reveals his plan to bring Tormund's people through the Wall** — Hundreds or thousands of wildlings to be granted passage, food, and shelter.
16. **Jon describes Mother Mole and Hardhome** — A woods witch prophesied ships would carry free folk to safety; thousands followed her to Hardhome.
17. **Jon warns of the wight threat** — If thousands die at Hardhome, they will rise as wights with black hands and pale blue eyes. The officers leave in grim silence.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Tense, burdened, isolated. Frustrated with his officers' resistance. Feels the weight of decisions made alone. Brief warmth toward Val. Anger rising when Marsh dismisses wildlings as subhuman.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether Val will succeed and return with Tormund; managing internal opposition; the existential threat of the Others and wights.
- **Key decisions made:** Sent Val despite risk; keeping Leathers as master-at-arms; keeping Satin as squire; maintaining wight corpses for study; revealing Hardhome situation to his officers.
- **Self-deception flags:** Rationalizes breaking his promise to Stannis about Val ("Val is no princess… a feeble sort of evasion, a sad rag wrapped around his wounded word"); acknowledges his father would not approve; tells himself "I am the sword that guards the realm of men" to justify the breach of honor.
