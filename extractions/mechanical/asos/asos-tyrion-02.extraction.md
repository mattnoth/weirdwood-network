## Chapter Metadata

- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Varys's chambers (under the north wall), castle library, serpentine steps, kennels, Tyrion's bedchamber
- **approximate_timeline:** After the Battle of the Blackwater; before King Joffrey's upcoming wedding to Margaery Tyrell
- **time_markers:** Tyrion waits for Varys during the day; spends the afternoon in the library; bathes; waits for moonrise; moon peeping over the castle wall when he departs for Varys's chambers; a candle burns down and gutters during his time with Shae; half the night gone by the time Bronn arrives
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion sneaks into Varys's chambers and extracts intelligence about political maneuverings: Pycelle's restoration, the Kettleblacks spying for Cersei, and the fruitless investigation into Ser Mandon Moore. He asks Varys to arrange a secret meeting with Shae, which takes place that night in Varys's quarters. Despite intending to send Shae away for her safety, Tyrion fails to do so and instead promises to keep her. Shae reveals a secret passage beneath Varys's stone bed. Tyrion then summons Bronn and orders him to find the singer Symon Silver Tongue before someone else does.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion infiltrates Varys's chambers** — Tyrion sneaks into Varys's quarters while the eunuch is summoned by Tywin, searches for hidden passages and secret documents but finds nothing.
2. **Varys returns and they discuss politics** — Varys reveals Pycelle is being restored to the small council at Tywin's command, pre-empting the Conclave's choice of Maester Gormon (a Tyrell). Ser Boros Blount is also being restored to the Kingsguard.
3. **Discussion of Ser Mandon Moore** — Varys shares that Moore was friendless, brought to King's Landing by Jon Arryn, given his white cloak by Robert. Barristan Selmy said he had no friend but his sword. No link to Cersei is established.
4. **Tyrion asks Varys to arrange a meeting with Shae** — Tyrion wants to see Shae one last time before sending her away. Varys reveals the Red Keep's secret passages don't connect to Tyrion's chambers (Maegor designed them only for egress).
5. **They agree to use Varys's chambers** — Varys will steal Shae from Lollys's service after moonrise; they'll meet in his quarters.
6. **Tyrion passes the afternoon** — He reads Beldecar's History of the Rhoynish Wars in the library, bathes, has Pod trim his whiskers, dresses in court clothes, realizes his folly, and changes to simpler garb.
7. **Tyrion walks through the Red Keep at moonrise** — He passes Ser Balon Swann at the door and Ser Loras Tyrell on the drawbridge. He has a conversation with Loras about joining the Kingsguard at seventeen.
8. **Tyrion stops at the kennels** — He watches a dogfight and jokes that the losing dog resembles Sandor Clegane, earning laughs from the men-at-arms.
9. **Tyrion meets Shae in Varys's chambers** — Varys is disguised as a matronly woman. Shae appears naked. They have sex twice.
10. **Tyrion fails to send Shae away** — He tries to tell her she must leave King's Landing for safety, but she deflects with requests for silks, jewels, and attendance at Joffrey's wedding feast. She relays details from Symon Silver Tongue about the feast.
11. **Shae reveals the secret passage** — She tells Tyrion the hidden stairs are under Varys's stone bed, which rises via a counterweight mechanism.
12. **Shae accepts her status** — She calls herself Tyrion's whore, says she'd be his lady but can't, asks him to keep her and keep her safe. Tyrion promises, hating his own weakness.
13. **Tyrion returns and summons Bronn** — He wakes Pod and sends him to fetch Bronn from Chataya's brothel. Bronn mentions bedding Alayaya and Marei together.
14. **Tyrion orders Bronn to find Symon Silver Tongue** — Concerned the singer knows about Shae and could expose her, Tyrion tells Bronn to find Symon before someone else does.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious, lonely, lustful, self-loathing, and conflicted. Tyrion oscillates between political calculation and desperate longing for Shae. He is deeply insecure about his appearance (scar, missing nose, dwarfism) and jealous when he sees a handsome knight speak to Shae. After failing to send Shae away, he feels foolish and weak.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The danger Shae is in if discovered by Tywin; secondary preoccupation with who ordered Ser Mandon Moore to kill him (suspected Cersei but no proof found).
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to see Shae "one last time" despite the danger; fails to send her away and instead promises to keep her; decides to have Bronn find Symon Silver Tongue as a threat.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself this will be the "last time" with Shae but makes no actual arrangements to send her away. Promises to keep her safe while knowing he cannot. Recognizes his own folly explicitly ("Fool, fool") and compares himself unfavorably to the lesson of Tysha, yet acts against his own judgment. Dresses in court clothes for a secret visit to a eunuch, then catches himself.
