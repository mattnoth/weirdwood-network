# ASOS — Jon VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 49
- **pov_character:** Jon Snow
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon VI
- **first_available:** ASOS Jon VI
- **location_primary:** Castle Black
- **location_secondary:** Kingsroad, Mole's Town, the Wall
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Jon's escape from the wildlings; he rides hard to reach Castle Black before the Magnar of Thenn's forces
- **time_markers:** Stars fading in the eastern sky; moonlight against the ice; first light of dawn (pink and purple Wall); waning moon over Mole's Town; Jarman Buckwell returned "a fortnight past"; Jon wakes in darkness after cauterization; multiple wakings implied
- **chapter_summary:** Jon rides feverishly from south of the Wall to Castle Black, stopping briefly at Mole's Town for a fresh horse. He arrives at dawn to find the castle nearly deserted, with only about forty men remaining under Donal Noye's informal command. Noye brings Jon to Maester Aemon, who cauterizes his arrow wound. Jon learns that Lord Commander Mormont was murdered by mutineers at Craster's Keep, that only a dozen men survived the Fist of the First Men, and that Winterfell has been burned and Bran and Rickon reportedly killed by Theon Greyjoy. Jon warns them that the Magnar of Thenn is approaching from the south with a hundred and twenty men to attack Castle Black's gate.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon rides north on a blown mare** — His wounded leg reopens each time he mounts; fever makes him light-headed; twice he rides in the wrong direction.
2. **Jon reaches the kingsroad** — Pats the mare's neck and talks to her; his leg has gone stiff as wood.
3. **Jon nearly rides through Mole's Town** — So feverish he almost doesn't recognize where he is; dismounts at the brothel's adjoining stable by the light of the waning moon.
4. **Jon acquires a fresh mount** — Wakes two stable boys; gets a black gelding with saddle and bridle, a skin of wine, and half a loaf of brown bread.
5. **Jon warns Mole's Town** — Tells the boys to wake the village: wildlings are south of the Wall, everyone should gather goods and make for Castle Black.
6. **Jon sees the Wall at dawn** — The Wall appears above trees and morning mists, glimmering with moonlight, then glowing pink and purple with the first light of dawn.
7. **Jon arrives at Castle Black** — No sentries challenge him; the castle appears nearly deserted, with weeds in the courtyards and old snow on buildings; soot marks the Lord Commander's Tower.
8. **Jon finds Donal Noye in the armory** — The one-armed smith recognizes him; tells Jon that Jarman Buckwell reported seeing him with the wildling column wearing a sheepskin cloak.
9. **Jon confirms he joined the wildlings on Qhorin Halfhand's orders** — Explains it was the Halfhand's last command.
10. **Noye reports the garrison is spread thin** — Harma Dogshead at Woodswatch-by-the-Pool, Rattleshirt at Long Barrow, the Weeper near Icemark; wildlings feinting all along the Wall.
11. **Jon identifies the attacks as feints** — States Mance wants the Watch to spread thin; the real attack will come through Castle Black's gate from the south.
12. **Jon reports enemy strength** — A hundred and twenty wildlings south of the Wall with bronze armor and some steel, coming from Queenscrown. Only forty men remain at Castle Black — crippled, infirm, and green boys.
13. **Noye reveals the command situation** — Ser Wynton Stout named castellan but senile and forgotten; Noye is the effective commander.
14. **Jon asks about Ghost** — No sign of the direwolf since Jon climbed the Wall.
15. **Noye brings Jon to Maester Aemon** — Clydas opens the door; Jon is laid down in the maester's rooms beneath the rookery.
16. **Maester Aemon examines and treats Jon's wound** — Sniffs the wound; orders boiling wine, hot irons, and a sharp knife; prepares for cauterization.
17. **Jon tells them about Styr and his force** — The Magnar of Thenn has a hundred of his own men and a score of raiders who know the Gift.
18. **Jon reports Mance never found the Horn of Winter** — Ygritte told him they opened a hundred graves of kings and heroes in the Milkwater valley but never found it.
19. **Noye asks who Ygritte is** — Jon struggles to explain her; confesses he broke his vows with her.
20. **Jon's wound is cauterized** — Aemon uses a red-hot knife; Noye holds Jon down, Clydas guides Aemon's hand; Jon screams and pounds his fist against the table; he faints from the pain.
21. **Jon wakes to find Pyp and Grenn** — Pyp is at his bedside; Grenn is also there, having fallen asleep.
22. **Grenn reports survivors of the Fist** — About a dozen made it back: Dywen, Giant, Dolorous Edd, Sweet Donnel Hill, Ulmer, Left Hand Lew, Garth Greyfeather, four or five more, and Grenn himself.
23. **Jon learns of Mormont's murder** — The Lord Commander was killed at Craster's Keep by mutineers: Garth of Oldtown, Ollo Lophand, Dirk, and others.
24. **Grenn tells about Sam killing an Other** — Sam stabbed one with the dragonglass knife Jon made him; earned the name "Sam the Slayer."
25. **Grenn explains leaving Sam at Craster's** — Sam wouldn't move; Giant tried to drag him; mutineers (Dirk, Ollo) were tearing up walls for food, fighting, raping Craster's wives; Dolorous Edd feared the mutineers would kill loyal men; they left Sam with the Old Bear's body.
26. **Jon considers who might be Lord Commander** — Bowen Marsh holds position until a choosing; Jon considers Cotter Pyke and Ser Denys Mallister as likely candidates; notes their mutual enmity and different temperaments.
27. **Pyp updates Jon on defensive measures** — Sentries on the Wall watching south; Noye dispatched men to Weatherback Ridge to watch the kingsroad; Aemon sent ravens to Eastwatch and the Shadow Tower.
28. **Maester Aemon tells Jon about events in the realm** — Balon Greyjoy has crowned himself and sent longships against the north; appeals to kings have been ignored; Winterfell has been destroyed.
29. **Jon learns Bran and Rickon are reportedly dead** — Aemon says Theon Greyjoy killed them after taking Winterfell; Theon then burned the castle when bannermen threatened to retake it.
30. **Grenn reports Theon's fate** — Bolton's son killed all the ironmen and is reportedly flaying Theon Greyjoy.
31. **Jon protests the news** — Claims he saw a grey direwolf at Queenscrown that recognized him; questions whether Bran could live on through the wolf.
32. **Jon is given more medicine and falls asleep** — Dreams of Winterfell's hot pools, a white weirwood with his father's face, and Ygritte dissolving to skull and skeleton in the water.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Physically depleted (feverish, wounded, exhausted); relief and warmth upon seeing Noye and reaching Castle Black; grief and denial upon learning of Mormont's death; deeper grief and disbelief upon learning of Winterfell's destruction and his brothers' reported deaths; guilt and conflict over Ygritte (torn between loving her and knowing it was wrong); fear and urgency about the coming wildling attack
- **Primary preoccupation:** Reaching Castle Black before the Magnar; delivering the warning about the southern wildling force; torn memories of Ygritte (her warmth, her killing the old man); after arriving, consumed by news of Mormont's death and Winterfell's fall
- **Key decisions made:** Stops at Mole's Town to warn them and get a fresh horse; insists on reporting to Noye and Aemon despite his injuries; refuses milk of the poppy initially (wants to deliver intelligence first); confesses breaking his vows with Ygritte
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon compares himself to his father — "He was pledged to Lady Stark, and I am pledged to the Night's Watch" — framing a parallel between his father's torn loyalties and his own with Ygritte; grasps at the grey direwolf sighting at Queenscrown as evidence Bran is alive ("If Bran was dead, could some part of him live on in his wolf?"), refusing to accept the news
