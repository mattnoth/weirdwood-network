# ASOS — Catelyn VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 52
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn VII
- **first_available:** ASOS Catelyn VII
- **location_primary:** The Twins (feast hall, Water Tower side)
- **location_secondary:** The other castle (the second tower, hosting the "bastard feast"); the camps outside; Riverrun (referenced); Moat Cailin (referenced); the Neck (referenced); the Dreadfort (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Evening of Edmure Tully's wedding to Roslin Frey; the feast follows the ceremony; Robb plans to march north to Moat Cailin "by this hour tomorrow"
- **time_markers:** "A few more hours, and the worst will be over"; "By this hour tomorrow Robb would be off to another battle"; the feast takes place over several hours through the evening; rain falling outside throughout; bedding ceremony occurs partway through
- **chapter_summary:** The wedding feast of Edmure Tully and Roslin Frey takes place at the Twins, with poor food, loud music, and heavy drinking. Catelyn endures the feast between Ser Ryman Frey and Roose Bolton, noting absent Freys and Bolton's veiled threat about his ward hostages. After the bedding ceremony removes most guests, the musicians begin playing "The Rains of Castamere." Catelyn discovers chainmail beneath Edwyn Frey's sleeve and realizes the trap too late — crossbowmen in the gallery fire on Robb and the northmen, and armed Frey and Bolton men pour through the doors, massacring the unarmed guests. Catelyn takes Jinglebell hostage and begs Lord Walder to spare Robb, but a man in dark armor and a pale pink cloak stabs Robb through the heart saying "Jaime Lannister sends his regards." Catelyn kills Jinglebell, claws her own face in madness, and her throat is cut.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Rain falling steadily outside throughout the chapter
- **Season indicators:** None explicitly stated
- **Time of day:** Evening; the feast extends over several hours into the night
- **Lighting:** A fire roaring in the hearth; rows of torches burning smokily from iron sconces on the walls
- **Sounds:** Drums pounding constantly throughout (described as driving all other music); pipes wailing, flutes trilling, fiddles screeching, horns blowing, skins skirling; sounds echoing off the rafters; guests shouting; dogs snarling; the Greatjon singing; after the massacre begins — crash of distant battle, wild howling of a wolf (Grey Wind); a single drum continues boom doom boom doom; Jinglebell's bells ringing
- **Smells:** Ser Ryman's sour sweat poorly masked by lemonwater; Roose Bolton's sweeter but unpleasant smell; heat and smoke making Catelyn sick; a foul stench from Jinglebell soiling himself when Catelyn holds a knife to his throat
- **Notable sensory details:** Oppressive heat from the fire, torches, and packed bodies; cramped seating on the dais; Catelyn's head throbbing with the drums; the taste of blood in her mouth after being shot; tears burning like vinegar; the sensation of clawing her own face described as "ten fierce ravens raking her face"; the steel at her throat described as "red and cold"

## Events & Actions
1. **The wedding feast proceeds** — Music pounds through the hall as guests eat, drink, and shout. Catelyn sits between Ser Ryman Frey (drunk, sweating) and Roose Bolton (eating little, sipping hippocras) on the dais.
2. **Poor fare is served** — Thin leek soup, salad, river pike, cold turnips, jellied calves' brains, and stringy beef. Robb eats uncomplaining; Edmure is absorbed with his bride Roslin.
3. **Robb dances with Frey women** — As Walder Frey requested, Robb dances with Alyx, Fair Walda, Roslin, the eighth Lady Frey, the widow Ami, Fat Walda, Serra, Sarra, and young Shirei.
4. **Bolton makes a veiled threat** — Roose Bolton toasts Lord Walder's grandsons, pointedly mentioning that "Walder and Walder" are in the care of his bastard son. Lord Walder reacts with visible discomfort.
5. **Catelyn reflects on Sansa's forced marriage** — She remembers Sansa's marriage to Tyrion and prays for her.
6. **Dogs fight over scraps** — Two dogs roll across the floor fighting; someone douses them with ale; one shakes ale and hair on Lord Walder's grandsons.
7. **Catelyn recalls Grey Wind's exclusion** — Lord Walder refused the direwolf entry, citing the attack on grandson Petyr. Robb yielded to avoid further offense.
8. **The Greatjon drinks Petyr Pimple under the table** — After already outdrinking Ser Whalen, the Greatjon begins singing "The Bear and the Maiden Fair" while the musicians play "Flowers of Spring."
9. **Roose Bolton departs** — Bolton murmurs something and leaves for a privy.
10. **Robb sits beside Catelyn in Bolton's seat** — He tells her the farce will be over in a few hours; notes Black Walder has been mild; asks Ser Ryman about Olyvar. Ser Ryman evasively says Olyvar is "gone from the castles."
11. **Catelyn asks about Alesander the singer** — Ser Ryman identifies him as Symond's son, Alyx's brother, but says he is "away." Ser Ryman then staggers out.
12. **Juicy pink lamb is served** — The most appetizing dish of the evening arrives on huge silver platters.
13. **Lord Walder calls for the bedding** — He claps his spotted hands; his sons pound cups; the musicians stop. Walder asks Robb's permission to bed the newlyweds.
14. **The bedding ceremony begins** — Guests swarm the dais. The Greatjon throws Roslin over his shoulder. Roslin is stiff with terror and crying. Edmure is stripped by women. Both are carried from the hall.
15. **Catelyn stays behind with a few others** — Robb also stays; Petyr Pimple and Ser Whalen are asleep; Merrett drinks; Jinglebell scavenges; Ser Wendel eats lamb; Lord Walder remains on his throne.
16. **Dacey Mormont approaches Edwyn Frey** — She touches his arm. Edwyn wrenches away with "unseemly violence" and loudly refuses to dance. Dacey pales.
17. **Catelyn senses something wrong** — She stands, doubt gripping her heart. Ser Wendel notices her alarm.
18. **"The Rains of Castamere" begins** — The musicians play the Lannister song. No one sings the words.
19. **Catelyn discovers chainmail under Edwyn's sleeve** — She grabs his arm and feels iron rings beneath silk. She slaps him so hard she breaks his lip.
20. **Catelyn realizes the trap** — She thinks of Olyvar, Perwyn, and Alesander all absent, and Roslin's weeping.
21. **Robb is shot** — A crossbow bolt sprouts from his side beneath the shoulder. A second pierces his leg. He falls. Musicians in the gallery have crossbows instead of instruments.
22. **Catelyn is shot in the back** — She is running toward Robb when something hits her in the small of the back; she falls to the floor.
23. **Smalljon Umber shields Robb** — He wrestles a table off its trestles and flings it over the king. Crossbow bolts thud into the wood.
24. **Robin Flint is killed** — Ringed by Freys whose daggers rise and fall.
25. **Ser Wendel Manderly is killed** — Rising to his feet with his leg of lamb, a quarrel goes in his open mouth and out the back of his neck. He crashes forward, sending cups, flagons, and food flying.
26. **Smalljon fights then falls** — He bludgeons Ser Raymund Frey with a leg of mutton, but a crossbow bolt drives him to his knees.
27. **Lucas Blackwood is cut down** — Killed by Ser Hosteen Frey.
28. **A Vance is hamstrung** — Black Walder hamstrings him while he wrestles Ser Harys Haigh.
29. **Crossbows kill more northmen** — Donnel Locke, Owen Norrey, and half a dozen others fall.
30. **Dacey Mormont fights and flees** — Ser Benfrey seizes her arm; she smashes a wine flagon in his face and runs for the door.
31. **Ser Ryman enters in full armor** — He pushes through the door with a dozen Frey men-at-arms bearing longaxes.
32. **Ser Ryman kills Dacey Mormont** — He buries his axe head in her stomach.
33. **Bolton men pour through other doors** — Mailed men in shaggy fur cloaks with steel; Catelyn mistakes them for rescue for half a heartbeat until one cuts the Smalljon's head off with two axe blows.
34. **Catelyn crawls to a dagger** — She finds one on the floor near Jinglebell's hiding place beneath a table.
35. **Robb struggles to his knees** — He pushes out from under the table, with arrows in his side, leg, and chest. Lord Walder raises a hand and the music stops, except one drum.
36. **Lord Walder mocks Robb** — "The King in the North arises. Seems we killed some of your men, Your Grace."
37. **Catelyn takes Jinglebell hostage** — She drags him from under the table by his grey hair and holds the dagger to his throat.
38. **Catelyn pleads for Robb's life** — She offers to forget everything, swears by old gods and new, offers herself and Edmure as hostages. "A son for a son."
39. **Lord Walder refuses** — He notes Jinglebell is a grandson, not a son, and "never was much use."
40. **Robb is killed** — A man in dark armor and a pale pink cloak spotted with blood steps up and says "Jaime Lannister sends his regards." He thrusts his longsword through Robb's heart and twists.
41. **Catelyn kills Jinglebell** — She saws at Aegon's neck until the blade grates on bone. Blood runs hot over her fingers.
42. **Catelyn goes mad** — She claws her own face, tearing off strips of flesh, leaving deep furrows of blood. She laughs and screams.
43. **Catelyn is killed** — Someone grabs her scalp and cuts her throat.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Progresses from weary resignation through the feast (headache, disgust at food, discomfort between Ser Ryman and Bolton), to brief sympathy (for Roslin, for Sansa), to mild relief (the farce nearly over, Robb will march tomorrow), to sudden doubt (Edwyn's violent refusal), to cold horror (chainmail under silk), to desperate maternal fury (taking hostage, bargaining), to grief and madness (clawing her own face, laughing and screaming), to final acceptance of death (thinking of Ned, not caring what they do to her)
- **Primary preoccupation:** Enduring the feast → protecting Robb once the trap is sprung. Throughout, she is counting hours until they can leave. Her maternal instinct dominates once the massacre begins — everything becomes about saving her son.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to investigate Edwyn's behavior (follows him, grabs his arm); takes Jinglebell hostage and attempts to negotiate Robb's release; keeps her word and kills Jinglebell after Robb is murdered
- **Self-deception flags:** "It is nothing, she tried to tell herself, you are seeing grumkins in the woodpile, you are become an old silly woman sick with grief and fear" — she almost dismisses her correct instinct that something is wrong. Earlier she consoles herself: "A few more hours, and the worst will be over" and "He will win his battle. He wins all his battles."
