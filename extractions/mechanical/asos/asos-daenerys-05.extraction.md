# ASOS — Daenerys V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 58
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys V
- **first_available:** ASOS Daenerys V
- **location_primary:** Outside Meereen (siege camp, olive grove)
- **location_secondary:** Meereen (viewed from outside), the shore/coast, the Skahazadhan river
- **approximate_timeline:** After the fall of Yunkai; Dany's host has marched along the coast road from Yunkai to Meereen
- **time_markers:** The hero taunts for "an hour"; afternoon sun blazing off the Great Pyramid; the tide coming in during Dany's ride; events span within a single day
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys's army arrives at Meereen, where a champion named Oznak zo Pahl rides out to challenge the besiegers. Dany sends Strong Belwas, who kills the champion in single combat. At a war council, her captains debate how to take the city — siege towers, starvation, and sewers are discussed, with no clear solution. While riding through the freedmen's camps, Dany is attacked by Mero (the Titan's Bastard), who had been hiding among the freedmen. Arstan Whitebeard kills Mero, then reveals himself as Ser Barristan Selmy. Barristan also reveals that Ser Jorah Mormont has been informing on Dany to Varys.

## Events & Actions
1. **Meereenese champion rides out** — Oznak zo Pahl rides from the gates of Meereen in copper-and-jet armor on a white charger, challenging the besiegers to single combat for an hour while mocking them.
2. **Dany restrains her bloodriders** — Aggo, Jhogo, and Rakharo want to fight but Dany forbids it, calling the man a buzzing fly.
3. **Oznak urinates toward Dany's camp** — He dismounts and urinates toward the olive grove; defenders on the walls follow suit.
4. **Dany sends Strong Belwas** — She chooses Belwas as champion because he is expendable in terms of command and because losing to a former pit slave would shame the Great Masters.
5. **Belwas defeats Oznak** — Belwas dodges three lance charges, hamstrings the horse with his arakh, then kills Oznak in close combat, beheading him and flinging the head toward the gates.
6. **Belwas defecates toward Meereen** — He turns his back on crossbow fire, squats, and defecates toward the city, wiping himself with Oznak's cloak.
7. **Belwas returns to camp victorious** — Celebrated by Dothraki, Unsullied, and others; Jhiqui jokes about braiding his nonexistent hair.
8. **Belwas's wound treated** — Dany insists on having a Yunkish freedman healer tend his chest wound with vinegar, stitches, and linen soaked in fire wine.
9. **War council convenes** — Dany and captains discuss how to take Meereen: no wood for siege equipment, boiling oil defenses, no food for a long siege, disease in camp, city resupplied by water.
10. **Jorah advises leaving Meereen** — He counsels marching west to Pentos and using grown dragons to take Westeros later; Dany refuses.
11. **Brown Ben proposes the sewers** — Suggests the brick sewers as a way in; describes iron grates, rats, filth, and darkness; the idea is left unresolved.
12. **Viserion lands on Brown Ben** — White dragon flies at Brown Ben's head and lands on him; Dany says "he likes you"; Brown Ben claims a drop of dragon blood from an ancestor.
13. **Brown Ben tells the Plumm ancestry tale** — Claims an ancestor married a "dragon princess" in the time of a "King Aegon"; had a member six feet long.
14. **Dany fantasizes about Daario** — She recalls his gifts of plants on the road from Yunkai and considers kissing him; thinks about "the dragon has three heads."
15. **Dany rides along the shore** — With Missandei and Arstan, she rides past the Unsullied camp (drilling, bathing) and sees her three ships at anchor.
16. **Dany rides through the freedmen's camp** — Children run behind; freedmen call her "Mother," beg boons, touch her for luck.
17. **Mero attacks Dany** — The Titan's Bastard, disguised with shaved head, yanks Dany from her saddle and threatens to cut off her breasts.
18. **Arstan kills Mero** — Whitebeard defeats Mero with his staff, breaking his leg and cracking his skull; freedmen swarm and finish him.
19. **Dany returns to pavilion shaken** — She drinks spice wine and confronts Ser Jorah about Mero's escape.
20. **Dany proposes to knight Arstan** — Both Arstan and Jorah refuse simultaneously.
21. **Arstan reveals his true identity** — He confesses he is Ser Barristan Selmy, former Lord Commander of the Kingsguard.
22. **Barristan reveals Jorah's betrayal** — He states there has been an informer by Dany's side selling secrets to Varys since she wed Khal Drogo.
23. **Jorah confesses** — He admits to spying, says Varys promised he might go home; drops his sword.
24. **Dany orders both out** — Furious and tearful, she tells them to go, but cannot finish the sentence; "And then she knew" — implying she has decided to send them into the sewers.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Moves from determination to frustration (siege difficulties), to amusement (Brown Ben's ancestry tale, Daario fantasies), to terror (Mero's attack), to fury and devastation (Jorah's betrayal revealed). Ends tearful and conflicted.
- **Primary preoccupation:** How to take Meereen and feed her people; she cannot abandon the freedmen nor leave the city untaken.
- **Key decisions made:** Sends Belwas as champion (calculated choice to shame the Masters); refuses to leave Meereen despite Jorah's advice; refuses to sacrifice Unsullied against oil defenses; begins contemplating the sewer approach; orders both Jorah and Barristan away ("And then she knew" — implied she sends them into the sewers).
- **Self-deception flags:** Dany tells herself her attraction to Daario is "too great a risk" while actively fantasizing about him. She frames sending Belwas as strategic but acknowledges he is the most expendable. She tells herself "the dragon has three heads" while assessing potential partners, conflating romantic interest with prophecy.
