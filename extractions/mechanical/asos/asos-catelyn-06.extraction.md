# ASOS — Catelyn VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 50
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn VI
- **first_available:** ASOS Catelyn VI
- **location_primary:** The Twins
- **location_secondary:** Green Fork, Water Tower
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly before Edmure Tully's wedding to Roslin Frey; Robb's host arrives at the Twins after a long march in the rain
- **time_markers:** "a moon's turn before these waters fall again" (Catelyn's estimate of the river receding); "I may nap an hour" (Edmure); travel described as "many leagues in the rain"; the wedding is the next day or very soon; Robb plans to march for the Neck "as soon as my uncle has been wedded and bedded"
- **chapter_summary:** Robb, Catelyn, and Edmure arrive at the Twins for Edmure's wedding to Roslin Frey. Grey Wind behaves aggressively toward the Frey riders who greet them and later balks at entering the castle. Lord Walder Frey forces Robb to publicly apologize to his unwed daughters and granddaughters for breaking the marriage pact. Roslin is presented and proves to be pretty and gentle, pleasing Edmure. Catelyn secures bread, salt, and wine to invoke guest right. After settling in, Catelyn learns from Maester Brenett that Roslin should be fertile. Roose Bolton arrives with dire news: Lannisters under Gregor Clegane attacked his forces at the Trident, Winterfell has been burned, and Ramsay Bolton holds a flayed Theon Greyjoy captive at the Dreadfort.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Constant heavy rain throughout the chapter; the Green Fork is flooded to half again its normal width, a "boiling torrent" of murky green water; the rain makes it impossible to distinguish banner colors; moats turned to shallow lakes
- **Season indicators:** No explicit season reference; conditions suggest autumn rains
- **Time of day:** Daytime arrival; chapter spans several hours through the day (arrival, audience, settling in, meetings)
- **Lighting:** Grey overcast skies ("beneath such skies the whole world seemed grey"); fires burning in the rooms; firelight in the hall
- **Sounds:** Green Fork described as "an endless susurrus, like the growl of some great beast"; steady rainfall; Grey Wind's growling and snarling; horses whinnying; Jinglebell's bells jingling; stones rumbling as wagons cross the bridge
- **Smells:** Sweet-smelling carpets in the bridal chamber; raven droppings on Maester Brenett's sleeves (implied smell)
- **Notable sensory details:** Mud splattering from horse hooves; the damp and wet pervading everything; characters are thoroughly wet and cold; the warmth of fires is welcome relief

## Events & Actions
1. **Approach to the Twins** — Robb's party hears and then sees the flooded Green Fork; the river is half again as wide as last year, making fording or swimming impossible.
2. **Robb dons his crown** — Robb puts on the bronze-and-iron crown and summons Catelyn and Edmure to ride beside him; Ser Raynald Westerling carries the Stark banner.
3. **Frey riders emerge** — Four Freys (Ser Ryman, Edwyn, Black Walder, Petyr Pimple) ride out from the western gatehouse in grey wool cloaks.
4. **Grey Wind attacks** — Grey Wind growls, then leaps forward snarling at the Frey riders; Ser Ryman's horse shies, Petyr Pimple is thrown; Black Walder draws his sword.
5. **Catelyn intervenes** — Catelyn spurs her mare between Grey Wind and the Freys' horses, cutting off the direwolf; Grey Wind veers away and responds to Robb's calls.
6. **Black Walder confronts Robb** — Black Walder angrily accuses Robb of setting his wolf upon them.
7. **Robb offers his horse to Petyr** — Robb dismounts and offers Petyr his own horse; Petyr declines and rides behind a brother.
8. **Jeyne Westerling's absence noted** — Ser Ryman and Black Walder express displeasure that Jeyne is not present; Catelyn explains she was weary from travel.
9. **Quarters offered** — Edwyn offers chambers in the Water Tower for Robb, Edmure, and Catelyn; army to camp on the far bank with feast tents and drink.
10. **Grey Wind balks at the portcullis** — Grey Wind howls and refuses to pass through the portcullis; Robb must squat and speak softly before the wolf consents.
11. **Robb leaves Grey Wind outside** — Robb orders Ser Raynald to stay with Grey Wind, keeping both the direwolf and the Westerling out of Lord Walder's sight.
12. **Audience with Lord Walder** — Lord Walder receives them from his black oak throne; mocks Robb's bronze crown; introduces Jinglebell.
13. **Lord Walder parades his maidens** — Lord Walder summons all his unwed daughters and granddaughters before Robb and demands a public apology.
14. **Robb publicly apologizes** — Robb addresses the Frey maidens, admits his wrong, asks forgiveness, and requests friendship between Starks and Freys.
15. **Lord Walder accepts** — Lord Walder declares Robb's apology "very good" and asks him to dance with his daughters at the feast.
16. **Roslin presented to Edmure** — Benfrey brings Roslin into the hall; she kneels before Edmure and hopes she is not a disappointment.
17. **Edmure is pleased** — Edmure's face lights up; he calls Roslin a delight.
18. **Roslin weeps** — Roslin cries and says she weeps "for joy."
19. **Lord Walder dismisses them** — Lord Walder promises music, wine, and that "we'll put some wrongs aright"; offers fires, mulled wine, and baths.
20. **Catelyn secures guest right** — Catelyn asks for food; Lord Walder provides bread, cheese, butter, salt, and wine; raises a toast and formally declares them guests.
21. **All eat and drink** — Robb, Catelyn, Edmure, the Greatjon, Ser Marq Piper, and others consume the food and drink; Catelyn feels safer.
22. **Shown to quarters** — Lame Lothar escorts them; bridal chamber is large and richly appointed; Catelyn's room is comfortable with a fire.
23. **Catelyn advises posting guards** — Catelyn tells Edmure they should post their own Stark and Tully guards.
24. **Edmure and Catelyn discuss Roslin** — Edmure is delighted with Roslin's appearance; Catelyn thinks her too small and delicate for childbirth.
25. **Catelyn changes clothes** — Catelyn puts on a warm wool dress of Tully red and blue; washes and brushes her hair.
26. **Catelyn seeks Freys in the hall** — Catelyn finds Lame Lothar and other Freys (Raymund, Lucias Vypren, Ser Damon, Ser Hosteen, Ser Leslyn Haigh and sons) drinking by the fire.
27. **Catelyn asks after Ser Perwyn** — Lothar says Perwyn is away and will regret missing her; claims the rain prevents his return for the wedding.
28. **Catelyn visits Maester Brenett** — Lothar directs her to the maester's turret; Brenett assures her Roslin is likely fertile, citing her mother Lady Bethany's five surviving children.
29. **Catelyn goes to Robb's quarters** — She finds Robin Flint, Ser Wendel Manderly, the Greatjon, the Smalljon, and Roose Bolton present.
30. **Bolton reports Lannister attack on the Trident** — Bolton delayed leaving Harrenhal; Aenys Frey crossed the ruby ford; by the time Bolton's force arrived, the river was in torrent; he was ferrying men in boats when Gregor Clegane attacked with heavy horse, drove Bolton's rear guard into the river; Ser Wylis Manderly captured; many drowned or fled.
31. **Bolton reports on Winterfell** — Ironmen burned Winterfell and the winter town; Ramsay Bolton led the counterattack after Ser Rodrik and Leobald Tallhart were killed; Ramsay took some captives to the Dreadfort.
32. **Bolton shows Theon's skin** — Bolton produces a strip of flayed skin from Theon Greyjoy's left little finger, sent by Ramsay; offers it to Catelyn as a "token of revenge."
33. **Catelyn refuses the skin** — Catelyn resists the urge to take it and asks Bolton to put it away.
34. **Robb decides to keep Theon alive** — Bolton argues Theon's value as a hostage; Robb agrees to keep him alive at the Dreadfort to demand concessions from the ironborn.
35. **Bolton reports on ford defenses** — Bolton left 600 men at the ford under Ronnel Stout and Ser Kyle Condon; Clegane has not crossed.
36. **Duskendale discussed** — Bolton mentions Glover and Tallhart suffered worse losses at Duskendale; Robb curses Duskendale and vows Robett Glover will answer for it.
37. **Bolton reports his strength** — Five hundred horse and three thousand foot, mainly Dreadfort men and some Karstarks kept close due to doubtful loyalty.
38. **Robb assigns Bolton the rear guard** — Robb plans to march for the Neck after the wedding; Bolton will command the rear guard.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Apprehensive and mistrustful approaching the Twins; relief after guest right is secured ("Now we should be safe"); critical and analytical when evaluating Roslin (too small, narrow-hipped, Rosby stock not robust); depressed about her coming captivity at Seagard; grim upon hearing Bolton's news; part of her wanted to clutch Theon's flayed skin to her heart
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing guest right and ensuring safe passage through the Frey alliance; assessing Roslin as a match for Edmure; absorbing the cascade of bad military news
- **Key decisions made:** Insists on guest right bread and salt; decides to investigate Roslin's fertility with the maester; advises posting Stark and Tully guards; refuses the flayed skin trophy
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn thinks "Now we should be safe" after eating bread and salt, placing complete trust in guest right customs; she does not seriously interrogate Roslin's weeping or Perwyn's absence despite sensing something is off
