- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion and Sansa share a strained supper before she departs for the godswood. Tyrion works through Littlefinger's tangled financial ledgers until summoned to the Tower of the Hand, where Lord Tywin reveals a coded message from Walder Frey announcing the deaths of Robb Stark and the capture of Edmure Tully at the Red Wedding. Joffrey gloats and demands Robb's head be served to Sansa, provoking Tyrion to call him a monster and Lord Tywin to compare him unfavorably to Aerys. After Joffrey and Cersei are dismissed, Tywin reveals his plan to blame the dead Ser Amory Lorch for the murders of Elia and her children to appease Prince Oberyn, and outlines the political settlement: Riverrun for the Freys, Roose Bolton as Warden of the North with "Arya Stark" as bride for his bastard, and Lannister marriages to Frey girls.

## Events & Actions
1. **Strained supper** — Tyrion and Sansa eat alone; she apologizes for overcooked pease, he tries to reassure her but the exchange is awkward; Pod serves an excessive amount of pease onto Tyrion's plate.
2. **Sansa requests to visit godswood** — After supper, Sansa asks Tyrion's leave to go to the godswood for prayer; he consents and offers to accompany her but she firmly declines.
3. **Tyrion works on Littlefinger's ledgers** — He tries to trace golden dragons through Baelish's financial records; notes many ventures seem questionable; regrets that Antler Men thrown from walls during Blackwater had taken loans from the crown.
4. **Summons from Lord Tywin** — Ser Boros Blount arrives; Tyrion is relieved to leave the ledgers; crosses the castle to the Tower of the Hand at night in brisk wind.
5. **Red Wedding revealed** — Tyrion finds Cersei, Ser Kevan, Pycelle, and Joffrey already present with Tywin; Tywin shows a coded message from Walder Frey: "Roslin caught a fine fat trout. Her brothers gave her a pair of wolf pelts for her wedding."
6. **Joffrey gloats** — Joffrey proudly announces Robb is dead; demands Robb's head be served to Sansa at his wedding feast.
7. **Ser Kevan protests** — Kevan points out Sansa is now Joffrey's aunt by marriage.
8. **Tyrion confronts Joffrey** — Tyrion tells Joffrey that Sansa is no longer his to torment, calls him "monster," and warns him that "kings seem to be dying like flies."
9. **Cersei defends Joffrey** — She calls Tyrion the real threat and highlights his words as treasonous.
10. **Tywin rebukes Joffrey** — Tywin compares Joffrey to Aerys, noting Aerys also liked to remind men he was king and rip out tongues; delivers the line "any man who must say 'I am the king' is no true king at all."
11. **Joffrey defies Tywin** — Joffrey accuses Tywin of being scared of Aerys and hiding under Casterly Rock while Robert won all the battles; Cersei tries to intervene.
12. **Joffrey dismissed** — Tywin has Ser Kevan escort Joffrey out; orders Pycelle to prepare dreamwine.
13. **Cersei apologizes for Joffrey** — She blames Robert's influence; reveals Robert once hit Joffrey hard enough to knock out two baby teeth over mischief with a cat; Cersei threatened to kill Robert in his sleep if he did it again.
14. **Tywin dismisses Cersei** — He waves her away after she deflects blame to Robert.
15. **Tyrion calls Joffrey "Aerys the Third"** — After Cersei leaves; Tywin says the boy is thirteen and there is time.
16. **Tyrion asks about the Red Wedding conspiracy** — Tywin confirms he and Walder Frey planned it; says no one was told who didn't need to know; criticizes Tyrion's loose tongue.
17. **Discussion of Oberyn Martell** — Tywin describes Doran as cautious and Oberyn as "half-mad"; confirms Oberyn tried to raise Dorne for Viserys; Jon Arryn went to Sunspear and ended talk of war.
18. **Plan to blame Ser Amory Lorch** — Tywin intends to tell Oberyn that Lorch killed Elia and her children; Lorch is dead (torn apart by bear at Hoat's order), so he cannot deny it.
19. **Tywin reveals details of the Sack** — Ser Amory brought Rhaenys's body; found her hiding under her father's bed; Elia and the babe were in the nursery below; Tywin admits Gregor's rape was not ordered but says he didn't tell Gregor to spare Elia; describes Lorch as bestial.
20. **Tywin refuses to surrender Gregor Clegane** — Despite Dornish demands, Tywin considers Gregor too useful and plans to keep him away from the Dornish.
21. **Political settlement revealed** — Riverrun to Ser Emmon Frey; Lancel and Daven must marry Frey girls; Joy to wed a Frey natural son; Roose Bolton becomes Warden of the North with "Arya Stark" as bride for his bastard.
22. **Tyrion questions Arya's identity** — He believes Arya Stark is dead; Tywin implies Littlefinger found her (or a substitute); gives cryptic response "So was Renly, until the Blackwater."
23. **Tywin pressures Tyrion about an heir** — Tells Tyrion the North will go to his son by Sansa if he breeds one; reminds him he must consummate the marriage; Tyrion bitterly wonders when Sansa will be fertile — before or after he tells her about her family's murder.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Weary, irritated, and increasingly horrified. Tyrion starts the chapter in mild domestic frustration with Sansa, transitions to boredom and frustration with Littlefinger's finances, then experiences shock and moral discomfort at the Red Wedding news. He is disturbed by Joffrey's cruelty and provoked into threatening the king. During the private conversation with Tywin, he oscillates between dark humor, genuine horror at the Sack of King's Landing revelations, and bitter resentment at being pressured to produce an heir with Sansa.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Managing the political chaos — Dornish-Tyrell tensions, Littlefinger's tangled finances, and now the fallout from the Red Wedding. Underlying everything is his resentment of being excluded and undervalued by Tywin.
- **Key decisions made:** Defends Sansa against Joffrey despite personal risk; does not refuse outright Tywin's order to support the Lorch cover story; does not commit to producing an heir.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion thinks he doesn't want to know what Sansa prays for, but his internal narrative shows he already knows. He recognizes Tywin's pragmatic logic about the Red Wedding ("Explain to me why it is more noble to kill ten thousand men in battle than a dozen at dinner") and has no reply, suggesting he cannot fully reject the argument even as he finds it repugnant.
