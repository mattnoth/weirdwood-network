- **time_markers:** The wolf-dream opens at sunset ("The sun hung above the tall pines huge and red," "the last red light was filtering through green boughs and golden"); Bran wakes in the vault at dusk/evening; Meera returns with catch and they eat supper; a wolf howls "drifting through the night"; Summer will not return "till dawn"; Jojen says "Come the morrow, we had best move on"; Bran is nine years old; Jojen is thirteen; Meera is sixteen; the tower is described as abandoned "thousands of years before"; days are "no longer growing longer" (approaching or past longest day)
- **chapter_summary:** Bran wargs into Summer, who hunts across a ridge in the wolfswood and defeats a wild wolf pack's alpha to claim a deer kill. Hodor wakes Bran, pulling him back to the vault beneath Tumbledown Tower where they are hiding. Jojen warns Bran he is losing himself in Summer and needs a teacher greater than Jojen — the three-eyed crow beyond the Wall. Meera returns with fish and frogs; they eat froggy stew and debate whether to seek refuge with northern lords or travel north. Bran considers the Umbers, Karstarks, and Manderlys, but ultimately chooses to seek the three-eyed crow, saying "I want to fly."

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran wargs into Summer** — Bran is in Summer's skin, running up a ridge in the wolfswood at sunset, reveling in the wolf's senses and strength
2. **Summer surveys the land from the ridgetop** — From the crest, Summer sees the vast expanse of trees and hills, a kite circling above, and feels himself "prince of the green, prince of the wolfswood"
3. **Summer observes a wild wolf pack** — He spots grey wolves chasing prey below by a swift green brook; recognizes them as his "little cousins"
4. **Summer reflects on his pack siblings** — Remembers five siblings and "a sixth who stood aside"; senses his angry brother with green eyes nearby but growing distant; notes the others are scattered; mourns the lost sister; counts "four and one more, the white who has no voice"
5. **Summer senses his sister has entered the halls of man-rock** — One sister direwolf left the wilds to walk in halls where other hunters rule, and it is hard to find the way back out
6. **Summer catches the scent of deer blood and hunts** — Wind shifts, bringing scent of deer, fear, and blood; Summer races down the ridge toward the prey
7. **Summer finds the wolf pack feeding on a downed deer** — Eight grey wolves surround a dying deer; the pack hierarchy feeds in order — alpha male first, then female; the lowest-ranked (tail) circles warily
8. **Summer confronts the wolf pack** — Leaps onto a fallen log; the tail flees; other wolves snarl; Summer growls and shows his teeth; three wolves break and flee; one attacks and Summer flings it aside by the leg
9. **Summer fights the alpha male** — Extended combat rolling over roots, stones, leaves, and entrails; the female intervenes for her mate; other wolves dart in to snap; Summer kills one attacker by tearing out its throat
10. **The alpha wolf submits** — The old wolf lies down and exposes throat and belly in submission; Summer licks his wounds and claims the deer
11. **Hodor wakes Bran** — Hodor shakes Bran by the shoulders, chanting "Hodor"; the wolf-dream fades; Bran resists returning but is pulled back, shouting "NO!" then "I'm here"
12. **Bran orients in Tumbledown Tower** — He finds himself in the damp vault beneath the ruined watchtower; recalls that Meera found the way down into the vault
13. **Jojen warns Bran about losing himself** — Tells Bran he was gone too long; emphasizes the need to remember himself or "the wolf will consume you"; reminds Bran he forgot to mark a tree as instructed
14. **Bran expresses frustration** — Wanted to eat the deer; resentful about Jojen's exercises (clawing bark, catching rabbits, pushing rocks); says wolf tasks seem unimportant once he's Summer
15. **Meera returns with the catch** — Brings two silvery trout and six fat green frogs; Bran is tired of frogs
16. **The group prepares and eats froggy stew** — Jojen sends Hodor for wood; Bran and Meera clean the fish and frogs; stew made in Meera's helm with wild onions Hodor found
17. **Jojen argues they must move on** — Says Tumbledown Tower is not where they are meant to be; there was a battle at Winterfell; battles mean wars; an army could take them unawares
18. **Bran proposes Robb will return** — Hopes Robb will come back with his banners and chase the ironmen away
19. **Jojen relays Maester Luwin's dying words** — Ironmen on the Stony Shore; the Bastard of Bolton in the east; Moat Cailin and Deepwood Motte fallen; heir to Cerwyn dead; castellan of Torrhen's Square dead; war everywhere, each man against his neighbor
20. **Meera argues against traveling to the Wall on foot** — Points out the Wall is far away; Bran has no legs but Hodor; suggests finding horses from foresters, crofters, or hunters in the wolfswood
21. **Jojen warns against being discovered** — Says a crippled boy with a direwolf, a simpleminded giant, and two crannogmen would be noticed; word would spread; so long as Bran remains "dead," he is safe; alive, he becomes prey
22. **Hodor practices with his sword** — Sent outside by Bran; hacks at a rotted tree while bellowing "HODOR"; can swing for hours
23. **A wolf howls in the night** — Distant howl heard; Jojen asks if it's Summer; Bran says no — it's not Summer's voice; Summer has wandered far and won't return till dawn
24. **Bran suggests seeking northern lords** — Proposes stealing horses to ride to the Umbers at Last Hearth, or stealing a boat to sail down the White Knife to White Harbor and Lord Manderly, or sailing to Riverrun to bring Robb back
25. **Jojen discusses greenseers and the need for a better teacher** — Says greenseers were wargs who could wear any beast's skin and look through weirwood eyes to "see the truth that lies beneath the world"; admits he cannot teach what he does not understand; Bran could fly far and high with the right teacher
26. **Meera presents the choice to Bran** — Affirms the Reeds have sworn faith "by earth and water, bronze and iron, ice and fire"; says the risk and the gift are Bran's; the choice should be his
27. **Bran weighs his options** — Considers the Umbers (loyal but fierce), Karstarks (Karhold is strong), Manderly (friendly), but realizes they may all be dead; if caught by ironmen or the Bastard of Bolton, he would die; staying hidden means staying alive but remaining a cripple
28. **Bran chooses the three-eyed crow** — Cries, then says "I want to fly. Please. Take me to the crow."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Joy and freedom while warging as Summer (feels strong, swift, fierce — a prince of the green); frustration and resentment when pulled back to his crippled body; irritation at Jojen's lectures; wistfulness about Winterfell; fear about the dangers in the North; sadness about being crippled; ultimately resolves into tearful determination
- **Primary preoccupation:** The tension between losing himself in Summer's skin (where he is powerful and free) and maintaining his identity as Bran Stark (a crippled boy who must choose his path); whether to seek safety or seek power/knowledge
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to travel north beyond the Wall to find the three-eyed crow rather than seek refuge with the Umbers, Karstarks, or Manderlys — choosing growth over safety
- **Self-deception flags:** Promises Jojen he will mark trees and remember tasks while warging, but knows he always forgets once he becomes Summer ("I won't forget this time. But I'll eat my deer first, and fight with those little wolves some more"); tells himself he is only nine and will improve, while Jojen warns the days are getting shorter, not longer
