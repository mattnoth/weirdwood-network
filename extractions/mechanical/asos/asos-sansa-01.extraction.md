# ASOS — Sansa I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 7
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Sansa I
- **first_available:** ASOS Sansa I
- **location_primary:** The Maidenvault, Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Aegon's High Hill, King's Gate, Maegor's Holdfast, serpentine steps, training yard (inner ward), outer ward, castle walls
- **approximate_timeline:** After the Battle of the Blackwater; Margaery Tyrell has recently arrived in King's Landing; before Joffrey and Margaery's wedding
- **time_markers:** "When the appointed night arrived" (the supper takes place on a specific evening); Sansa watched Margaery's arrival procession prior to this; "the night of the battle" referenced as a past event (Blackwater); Joffrey's wedding referenced as a future event; Ser Dontos's escape plan set for "the night of Joffrey's wedding"; "sometimes she lay awake at night" (ongoing); fire crackling in the hearth (evening)
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa receives an invitation to sup with Margaery Tyrell and agonizes over whether it is a trap. Ser Loras Tyrell escorts her to the Maidenvault, where Sansa meets Margaery, Lady Olenna Tyrell, and a dozen Tyrell women. Over supper, Lady Olenna presses Sansa for the truth about Joffrey's character, and Sansa — under cover of Butterbumps's loud singing — confesses that Joffrey is a monster who had her father killed and has her beaten by the Kingsguard. The Tyrells then propose that Sansa visit Highgarden and marry Olenna's grandson Willas, which Sansa eagerly accepts after her initial confusion about which grandson they meant.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa receives and agonizes over Margaery's supper invitation** — She fears it may be a trap or cruel jape orchestrated by Joffrey, but knows she cannot refuse.
2. **Sansa recalls Margaery's arrival procession** — She watched from the castle walls as Margaery and Joffrey rode through cheering crowds up Aegon's High Hill; the Tyrell wheelhouse followed, carved with gilded roses.
3. **Sansa writes a gracious acceptance** — She uses quill and ink to accept the invitation.
4. **Ser Loras escorts Sansa** — He comes to her threshold; Sansa is flustered by his proximity and tells him he looks lovely.
5. **Sansa and Loras discuss the tourney and Robar Royce** — Sansa reminds Loras of the red rose he gave her at the Hand's tourney; Loras does not remember. Sansa mentions Ser Robar; Loras reveals he killed Robar at Storm's End and becomes cold.
6. **They pass through Maegor's and observe the training yard** — Ser Balon Swann holds the door; in the yard, multiple knights spar: a Redwyne twin vs. Ser Tallad, Ser Kennos vs. Osney Kettleblack, Ser Osfryd savagely beating Morros Slynt. Ser Garlan trains against three opponents, knocking one senseless.
7. **Sansa and Loras discuss Ser Garlan's training method** — Loras says Garlan is a better sword but Loras is the better lance; Garlan trains against multiple foes because "in battle it is seldom one against one."
8. **They ascend the serpentine steps in silence** — Sansa regrets mentioning Ser Robar.
9. **Arrival at the Maidenvault** — Olenna's identical twin guards Erryk and Arryk (Left and Right) open the doors; Margaery emerges and greets Sansa warmly.
10. **Sansa meets the Tyrell women** — Margaery introduces cousins Megga, Alla, and Elinor; Lady Janna; Lady Leonette; Septa Nysterica; Lady Graceford; Lady Bulwer; Meredyth Crane; and Lady Merryweather. Lady Alerie is recognized by Sansa.
11. **Sansa meets Lady Olenna** — The old woman asks for a kiss; mentions knowing Sansa's grandfather Lord Rickard, and that Lord Hoster Tully is dying.
12. **Supper is served; Butterbumps performs** — Broth of leeks and mushrooms is served; Butterbumps enters cartwheeling, lays a giant egg with live chicks, juggles oranges.
13. **Lady Olenna interrogates Sansa about Joffrey** — She asks directly whether Joffrey has mistreated Sansa. Olenna dismisses Sansa's polite deflections, calls her a fool for giving empty answers.
14. **Butterbumps sings "The Bear and the Maiden Fair" as cover** — Olenna orders the song sung loudly to drown out their private conversation, noting that "in the Red Keep the very walls have ears."
15. **Sansa confesses that Joffrey is a monster** — Whispering under the music, she reveals Joffrey lied about the butcher's boy, made her father kill her wolf, has the Kingsguard beat her, had her father killed despite promising mercy, and forced her to look at his head. She adds "the queen as well."
16. **Sansa panics and begs them not to cancel the wedding** — She fears Joffrey will blame her if the Tyrells withdraw. Olenna reassures her that "Lord Puff Fish is determined that Margaery shall be queen."
17. **Margaery invites Sansa to Highgarden** — She describes its beauty: autumn flowers, groves, fountains, courtyards, marble colonnades, singers, pleasure boats on the Mander.
18. **Lady Olenna reveals the plan to wed Sansa to Willas Tyrell** — Sansa initially assumes they mean Loras and is ecstatic, but Olenna corrects her: it is Willas, the eldest grandson, who is heir to Highgarden but crippled.
19. **Margaery and Olenna explain Willas's injury** — His horse fell and crushed his leg in his first tourney; Olenna blames Oberyn Martell. Margaery says Willas has a good heart, reads, and draws pictures of stars.
20. **Sansa accepts the Willas match** — She asks when she might meet him; Margaery says after Joffrey's wedding, and Olenna will bring her to Highgarden.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious and mistrustful about the invitation; flustered and infatuated around Ser Loras; mortified when she upsets Loras by mentioning Robar; terrified when Olenna presses her about Joffrey; relieved when told the wedding will proceed; dizzy and confused when the Willas reveal overturns her Loras fantasy; ultimately hopeful about escaping King's Landing
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and mistrust — every interaction is filtered through fear of Joffrey's cruelty and the danger of saying the wrong thing. Secondary preoccupation: romantic fantasies about Ser Loras
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts the supper invitation despite fear; ultimately tells the truth about Joffrey to Olenna and Margaery; accepts the proposed marriage to Willas
- **Self-deception flags:** Sansa built an elaborate romantic fantasy around Loras based on a single rose at a tourney, convinced "it meant something, that it meant everything" — Loras does not remember. She initially assumes the marriage proposal is for Loras despite Olenna specifying "my grandson" (not Kingsguard-eligible). She compares Highgarden to "the beautiful magical court she had once hoped to find at King's Landing" — repeating the same idealization pattern that led to her disillusionment with King's Landing.
