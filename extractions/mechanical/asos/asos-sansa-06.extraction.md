# ASOS — Sansa VI

- **location_primary:** The Fingers (Littlefinger's ancestral tower)
- **location_secondary:** Merling King (ship, off the coast); King's Landing (referenced); Highgarden (referenced); the Eyrie (referenced); Winterfell (referenced); Braavos (referenced); Gulltown (referenced); The Twins (referenced); White Harbor (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** After Joffrey's death at his wedding feast; Sansa has been at sea for some time after escaping King's Landing; the chapter covers roughly nine days (arrival day, eight days waiting for Lysa, then the morning after the wedding)
- **time_markers:** "a long while clawing their way back on course" at sea; "we shan't stay more than a fortnight"; "it was eight long days until Lysa Arryn arrived"; "on five of them it rained"; "a grey windy afternoon" when Lysa arrives; wedding at sunset ("as the sun sank in the west"); torches lit at dusk; next morning ("come the morning")
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa arrives at Littlefinger's small ancestral tower on the Fingers after a rough sea voyage aboard the Merling King. Petyr reveals they are not going to Winterfell or White Harbor and instructs Sansa to assume the identity of his bastard daughter, Alayne Stone. He reveals key details of Joffrey's murder — the poison was in Sansa's hairnet and Lady Olenna Tyrell was the one who took the stone from it — and explains his manipulation of the Kettleblacks and the Tyrells. After eight days, Lysa Arryn arrives and insists on an immediate wedding; the ceremony and feast take place that evening. The singer Marillion sexually accosts Sansa that night, but Lothor Brune intervenes. The next morning, Lysa reveals she knows Sansa's true identity, proposes to darken her hair, interrogates her about her virginity, and declares her intention to marry Sansa to her son Robert Arryn.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa climbs to the forecastle** — Lothor Brune lifts her up the steep, splintery ladder of the Merling King as they approach the Fingers.
2. **Sansa reflects on the voyage** — She has been sick with terror, fever, and seasickness; two men were swept overboard and one fell from the mast.
3. **Petyr reveals their destination** — He tells Sansa the bare, stony shore is his ancestral home, not Winterfell; he explains Winterfell has been burned and sacked and those she knew are dead.
4. **Petyr announces his betrothal to Lysa** — He says they will stay no more than a fortnight and that Lysa is riding to meet them; he and Lysa are to be wed.
5. **They row ashore** — Lothor and Oswell row them to the strand; Petyr's household (Grisel, Kella, Bryen, Umfred, two old men, and Kella's bastard daughter) kneels to receive him.
6. **Tour of the tower** — Petyr shows Sansa his small three-story flint tower with its modest hall; she sees his grandfather's shield bearing the Titan's head on a green field.
7. **Petyr tells the Baelish family history** — His grandfather was born in Braavos and came to the Vale as a sellsword for Lord Corbray; took the Titan's head as his sigil when knighted.
8. **Petyr instructs Sansa to become Alayne Stone** — To avoid Varys's informers, Sansa must pose as Petyr's natural daughter; the name "Alayne" comes from Petyr's mother; cover story involves a Braavosi gentlewoman and being raised by the Faith.
9. **Petyr reveals the murder plot** — Dontos was only used to lead Sansa out and ensure she wore the hairnet; the poison was in the black amethysts; Petyr implies Lady Olenna took a stone from the hairnet during the feast.
10. **Petyr reveals the Kettleblack scheme** — Oswell is unmasked as the father of the three Kettleblack brothers; Petyr was the one who placed them in King's Landing through Bronn, making them "three hidden daggers."
11. **Petyr discusses players and pieces** — He calls Ned Stark "a hopeless player," Cersei "utterly predictable," and advises Sansa that knowing what a man wants tells you how to move him.
12. **Petyr reveals his manipulation of the Tyrells** — He planted the idea of Loras joining the Kingsguard while negotiating Margaery's match; his men spread tales and paid singers to romanticize the white cloak.
13. **Petyr predicts Margaery will marry Tommen** — He says the Tyrells needed a king but not Joffrey; Margaery will keep her crown and maidenhead.
14. **Eight days pass at the Fingers** — Rain on five of them; Sansa befriends an old blind dog; Petyr walks the holdings with Sansa (blowhole, Andal landing site marked with seven-pointed star, smallfolk in huts by a peat bog, empty hermit's cave).
15. **Lysa Arryn arrives** — A grey windy afternoon; modest escort of about twenty riders including three maids, a dozen household knights, a septon, and the singer Marillion.
16. **Lysa demands immediate marriage** — She refuses to wait for a ceremony at the Eyrie; Petyr reluctantly agrees.
17. **Wedding ceremony** — Vows said beneath a sky-blue canopy as the sun sets; Lysa's septon officiates.
18. **Wedding feast and bedding** — Feast on quail, venison, and roast boar with mead; songs played include "The Vow Unspoken," "Seasons of My Love," and "Two Hearts That Beat as One"; Sansa dances with younger knights; bedding ceremony follows.
19. **Lysa consummates the marriage loudly** — Her screams are heard throughout the tower; the singer plays "Milady's Supper" to try to cover the noise but fails; sets the dogs barking.
20. **Marillion sexually accosts Sansa** — He finds her in her sleeping alcove, makes aggressive advances, gropes her breast, and refuses to leave.
21. **Lothor Brune intervenes** — He draws a knife, cuts Marillion, and drives him away; tells Sansa "Lord Petyr said watch out for you."
22. **Sansa has troubled dreams** — She dreams of Joffrey dying but his face becomes Robb's; she dreams of her wedding night with Tyrion but he becomes larger and his face is scarred on only one side, saying "I'll have a song from you."
23. **Sansa wakes beside the old blind dog** — She says "I wish that you were Lady."
24. **Morning: Lysa summons Sansa** — Petyr tells Sansa he has revealed her true identity to Lysa.
25. **Lysa studies Sansa** — Says she looks too much like Catelyn; orders her hair darkened before going to the Eyrie.
26. **Lysa interrogates Sansa** — Asks if she is with child and if she is still a maiden; Sansa confirms she is still a maid.
27. **Lysa speaks bitterly of Jon Arryn** — Describes her unhappy marriage; all her babies died but Robert; three girls and two boys.
28. **Lysa proposes Sansa marry Robert Arryn** — A secret wedding once Tyrion is dead; Robert is eight and sickly; Lysa describes his habits and instructs Sansa to always let him win at games.
29. **Lysa declares she'll make Petyr Lord Protector of the Vale** — She tells Sansa to trust Petyr and do as he says.
