- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow rides with Rattleshirt's wildling band down from the mountains to Mance Rayder's massive camp along the Milkwater river. After meeting the Weeper's outriders, they are brought to Mance's tent, where Jon initially mistakes Styr the Magnar of Thenn for the King-beyond-the-Wall. Mance reveals himself as the grey-haired lute player, tells Jon he saw him twice at Winterfell, and explains his own desertion from the Night's Watch over a cloak patched with red silk. Jon convinces Mance of his defection by referencing the humiliation of being seated with the bastards at Robert's feast.

## Events & Actions
1. **Descent from the ridge** — Jon, Ghost, and Rattleshirt's wildling band descend a ridge toward the Milkwater river valley, observing thousands of fires below.
2. **Ghost rejoins the group** — Ghost comes bounding up when the moon rises; Rattleshirt's dogs snarl but keep their distance after Ghost wounded the largest hound six days ago.
3. **Crossing the frozen stream** — Rattleshirt leads the party across a frozen tributary, shattering the thin ice crust.
4. **Encounter with the Weeper's outriders** — Eight riders led by the Weeper intercept them; Rattleshirt and others explain Jon killed Qhorin Halfhand and is a warg; the Weeper sends them to Mance.
5. **Riding through the wildling camp** — Jon observes the massive, disorganized wildling camp: cookfires, tents, weapons being made, women fletching arrows, sparring youths, dancing, children, livestock.
6. **Rattleshirt's group dismounts** — Rattleshirt orders camp and sends Ygritte and Longspear Ryk to bring Jon to Mance's tent.
7. **Walking through camp to Mance's tent** — Jon observes the camp's lack of defenses and vulnerability; notes discipline beats numbers.
8. **Ghost ordered to stay outside** — Guard at Mance's tent refuses entry to Ghost; Jon commands Ghost to stay.
9. **Jon enters Mance's tent** — Jon observes six people inside: the dark young man (Jarl) and blonde woman (Val) sharing mead, Dalla cooking hens, a grey-haired man (Mance) playing lute, a white-bearded man (Tormund) eating, and a bald earless man (Styr) studying a map.
10. **Jon mistakes Styr for Mance** — Jon addresses Styr as "Your Grace"; Styr and Tormund mock him; Tormund directs Jon to turn around.
11. **Mance reveals himself** — The singer puts aside his lute and identifies himself, also recognizing Jon as "Ned Stark's bastard."
12. **Mance introduces his court** — Mance names Styr (Magnar of Thenn), Tormund Giantsbane (with full titles), Dalla (his pregnant wife), Val (Dalla's sister), and Jarl.
13. **Interrogation about the ranging** — Mance questions Jon about how many Night's Watch men were in the party and how he came to be with the Halfhand; Jon lies that the Lord Commander sent him for seasoning.
14. **Tormund identifies Craster as informant** — Tormund blurts that Craster told the Watch where the wildlings were; Mance confirms he already knew and was testing Jon.
15. **Others leave the tent** — Mance dismisses everyone except Dalla; Tormund takes another chicken and departs.
16. **Jon and Mance share a meal** — Jon eats hen and bread, drinks mead; guest right is invoked by Mance.
17. **Mance tells how he knew Jon** — First sighting: escorting Lord Commander Qorgyle to Winterfell, saw young Jon and Robb dumping snow on Fat Tom. Second sighting: infiltrated Robert's retinue at Winterfell feast disguised as a freerider with a lute.
18. **Mance explains his desertion** — A shadow-cat shredded his cloak on a ranging; a wildling wisewoman's daughter healed him and patched the cloak with scarlet silk from Asshai; at the Shadow Tower, Ser Denys Mallister told him the patched cloak was fit for burning; Mance left for a place where a man could wear any cloak he chose.
19. **Jon gives his reason for "turning cloak"** — Jon invokes the Winterfell feast, reminding Mance where the bastard was seated; Mance accepts this and offers him a new cloak.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear, isolation, determination. Jon feels "utterly alone" in his blacks surrounded by enemies. Cold to the bone physically and emotionally. But he maintains resolve about his mission.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Maintaining his cover as a turncloak while remaining loyal to the Night's Watch; completing Qhorin's final order to infiltrate and learn.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to use the Winterfell feast seating as his cover story for why he turned cloak — channeling genuine resentment into a convincing lie. Accepts Mance's hospitality (guest right) and the offer of a new cloak.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon's resentment about being seated among the bastards appears genuine, not manufactured — the line between his cover story and his real feelings is ambiguous. He tells himself he is "doing the last duty that Qhorin Halfhand had laid on him" but his guilt about killing Qhorin ("Though it was his doing more than mine") suggests unresolved conflict.
