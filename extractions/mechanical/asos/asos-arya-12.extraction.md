- **chapter_summary:** After the Red Wedding, Arya travels with Sandor Clegane through the riverlands, feeling emotionally hollow and dreaming nightly of wolves. They encounter a dying survivor from the Twins whom the Hound gives mercy, then head toward the Mountains of the Moon to ransom Arya to her aunt Lysa Arryn at the Eyrie. A wolf dream in which Nymeria pulls Catelyn's body from the river confirms to Arya that her mother is dead. They shelter in an isolated hill village for a fortnight, building a palisade, but are asked to leave. Returning south, the Hound decides to make for Riverrun, while Arya suggests going to the Wall to find Jon Snow — but the Hound dismisses it, mentioning he has a brother of his own.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya's daily grief** — Arya wakes each morning feeling a hollow emptiness where her heart, brothers, and parents had been; her head still hurts from the lump the Hound gave her
2. **Nightly wolf dreams** — Arya dreams of leading a great wolf pack, bigger and stronger than any of them, with brothers and sisters who would never leave her
3. **Daily routine with the Hound** — Sandor forces Arya awake each morning with cursing, shaking, or once dumping cold water on her; she tends the horses
4. **Two horses established** — They ride Stranger (the Hound's warhorse) and Craven (a sorrel palfrey found riderless the morning after the Twins, named for fleeing)
5. **Avoiding detection** — They ride daily, never sleeping twice in the same place, avoiding towns and settlements; Frey columns with twin-tower banners hunt for northmen
6. **Arya considers escape options** — She thinks about killing the Hound in his sleep, riding away on Craven, returning to Sharna's inn, finding Lord Beric, or going to Acorn Hall, but rejects each option
7. **Finding the dying bowman** — In an earthen hollow beneath a fallen oak, they find a man wearing Ser Marq Piper's badge (pink maiden dancing), with a shattered shoulder from a mace blow by a northman bearing a bloody man badge (Bolton)
8. **The bowman's story** — He and the Bolton man drank together at the Twins, toasting Lord Bolton, Ser Marq, Lord Edmure, Lady Roslin, and the King in the North, then the northman attacked him
9. **Recognition of the Hound** — The bowman identifies Sandor as "Joffrey's dog"; Sandor replies "My own dog now"
10. **Mercy killing** — The Hound gives the bowman water (Arya fills the helm from a nearby pond), then kills him with a dagger thrust through surcoat, ringmail, and quilting into the heart, telling Arya "That's where the heart is, girl. That's how you kill a man"
11. **Robbing the dead** — They take two silver stags, ~30 coppers, a dagger with a pink stone hilt (Arya keeps this), a kettle helm (Arya takes this too), and a quiver of arrows (useless without a bow)
12. **Heading east to the Mountains of the Moon** — The rains have mostly stopped; Arya sees sun, moon, and stars; they are heading eastward
13. **The Hound reveals his plan** — He tells Arya they are going to the Eyrie to ransom her to her aunt Lysa Arryn, following the high road to the Bloody Gate
14. **Arya wants to return to the Twins** — She argues they should go back for her mother, insisting they don't know for certain Catelyn and Robb are dead; the Hound refuses
15. **The Hound's fear of fire** — He declares "Death don't scare me. Only fire"
16. **Catelyn's body — wolf dream** — Arya goes to sleep thinking of her mother and wakes into a wolf dream: as Nymeria, she scents something pale and white drifting in a green river clogged with dead men; she swims out and drags the body to shore — a woman with pale wrinkled flesh, cold blood from a cut throat; Nymeria thinks "Rise... Rise and eat and run with us"
17. **Horsemen approach in wolf dream** — Men on horses arrive with black, yellow, and pink banners and weapons; Nymeria drives off younger wolves and abandons the body, fleeing
18. **Arya acknowledges her mother's death** — She tells the Hound "I know she's dead. I saw her in a dream" in a dull voice; he looks at her a long time and nods
19. **Arrival at the hill village** — In higher hills, they find a tiny isolated village surrounded by sentinels and soldier pines; they are offered food and shelter in exchange for work on a wooden palisade
20. **Village elder's warning** — Frost, snow, shadowcats, cave bears, and mountain clans (Burned Men under Timett One-Eye, Stone Crows under Gunthor son of Gurn, Milk Snakes, Sons of the Mist) make the high road to the Eyrie impassable; the clans have steel, swords, and mail hauberks from the war
21. **Arya's identity crisis** — She thinks she is "no one's daughter now," "no one" — not Arya, not Weasel, not Nan, Arry, Squab, or Lumpyhead — "only some girl who ran with a dog by day, and dreamed of wolves by night"
22. **Village life** — Women try to put Arya in a dress and make her do needlework; the village elder's daughter follows her with a cloth doll called "Ser Soldier"; Arya destroys the doll by ripping out its stuffing and throwing it in a brook
23. **Arya practices with sticks** — She practices swordwork ("needlework") with sticks in the woods, then smashes them against trees when she remembers the Twins
24. **The Hound considers staying** — After a fortnight, drunk on ale, he suggests they stay; mentions they'd never reach the Eyrie and the Freys are still hunting survivors; could get a letter to Lysa
25. **Expelled from the village** — The village elder tells them to leave after the palisade is done; they can't feed extra mouths come winter; "a man like you brings blood with him"; they know he is Joffrey's dog; they say he lost his belly for fighting at the Blackwater
26. **The Hound trades for a sword** — He swaps the longaxe from the Twins for an old, nicked, rusty sword; he sharpens it every night
27. **Arya questions the Hound's resolve** — She wonders why he cares about a sharp sword if he lost his belly for fighting; wonders if that's why he fled the Twins
28. **Return south** — Rains have ebbed, floods receding; the Hound turns south toward the Trident and says they'll make for Riverrun to sell Arya to the Blackfish
29. **Arya proposes the Wall** — She thinks Jon will want her even if no one else does; he'll call her "little sister" and muss her hair; she says they could go to the Wall
30. **The Hound refuses** — He lists obstacles: fighting through Freys, lizard lions in the Neck, ironborn in half the castles, thousands of northmen; says he has a brother too (implying his own motivation)

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Profound grief and emotional hollowness — "a hollow place, an emptiness where her heart had been, where her brothers had lived, and her parents"; anger toward the Hound alternating with resigned dependency; contempt for perceived cowardice (the horse Craven, the villagers, herself); longing for belonging and pack
- **Primary preoccupation:** The loss of her family; fantasies about killing the Hound or escaping; whether her mother might still be alive (resolved by the wolf dream); yearning for belonging — she thinks of Hot Pie, Gendry, Lord Beric, but concludes "They were never my pack"; ultimately thinking of Jon Snow as the last person who would want her
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts her mother's death after the wolf dream; does not flee or kill the Hound despite frequent thoughts of doing so; proposes going to the Wall to find Jon (rejected by the Hound)
- **Self-deception flags:** Initially refuses to accept her mother's death ("They didn't really know that her mother was dead, or Robb either"), only accepting it after the wolf dream confirms it; dismisses the wolf dreams as just dreams rather than recognizing them as warg visions; tells herself she could ride Craven away or kill the Hound but never acts; tells herself the Brotherhood and Hot Pie/Gendry "were never my pack" — defensive dismissal after feeling rejected
