- **chapter_summary:** Jaime Lannister rescues Tyrion from the black cells on the eve of his execution, but reveals that Tysha was truly a crofter's daughter and not the whore their father claimed. Enraged, Tyrion separates from Jaime after falsely confessing to Joffrey's murder and revealing Cersei's infidelities. Meeting Varys in the tunnels, Tyrion defies the planned escape route, climbs to the Tower of the Hand, strangles Shae with the Hand's chain after finding her in his father's bed, then confronts and kills Lord Tywin with a crossbow bolt on the privy.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion prepares for death** — Hearing noises outside his cell, Tyrion stands and prepares to die, wishing for a weapon and considering last words.
2. **Jaime arrives at the cell** — Jaime enters with a torch, revealing himself as Tyrion's rescuer. Tyrion sees Jaime is gaunt, his hair hacked short, and his right hand is missing (a stump).
3. **Brothers exchange dark humor** — They joke about being "Handless and Noseless, the Lannister boys" and about Tyrion burning the river (Battle of the Blackwater).
4. **Jaime outlines the escape plan** — Four turnkeys drugged with sweetsleep by Varys; Varys waits at the stair dressed as a septon; plan is down through sewers to the river; a galley waits in the bay; Varys has agents in the Free Cities to provide funds; Tyrion should take another name.
5. **Jaime kisses Tyrion farewell** — Jaime kneels and kisses Tyrion once on each cheek, lips brushing the scar tissue.
6. **Jaime reveals the truth about Tysha** — Jaime confesses that Tysha was no whore; he never bought her for Tyrion; it was a lie Tywin commanded him to tell. Tysha was truly a crofter's daughter, chance-met on the road.
7. **Tyrion reacts with rage** — Tyrion recalls Tysha was just a girl, no older than Sansa. He remembers that Tywin gave her to his guards and made Tyrion watch and participate.
8. **Tyrion strikes Jaime** — Tyrion backhands Jaime with all his strength, knocking him to the floor.
9. **Tyrion swears vengeance** — Tyrion invokes "A Lannister always pays his debts" against Jaime, Cersei, and Tywin.
10. **Tyrion demands the keys** — Tyrion refuses to travel with Jaime and takes the gaoler's keys to find Varys alone.
11. **Jaime asks about Joffrey's murder** — Jaime asks if Tyrion killed Joffrey, calling it a debt of truth.
12. **Tyrion reveals Cersei's infidelities** — Tyrion tells Jaime that Cersei is "a lying whore" who has been sleeping with Lancel, Osmund Kettleblack, and "probably Moon Boy."
13. **Tyrion falsely confesses to Joffrey's murder** — Tyrion claims "I killed your vile son," deliberately lying.
14. **Jaime walks away without a word** — The brothers separate permanently; Tyrion watches him go and part of him wants to call out but thinks of Tysha and holds his silence.
15. **Tyrion finds Varys** — Varys is lurking in a twisting turnpike stair in a moth-eaten brown robe. He says the queen had him watched and he dared not help during the trial; Jaime was persuasive.
16. **Tyrion asks about Sansa** — Varys reports no trace of Sansa Stark or Ser Dontos Hollard in King's Landing; they were last seen together on the serpentine steps the night she vanished.
17. **Descent through the dungeons** — Varys guides Tyrion by hand through the pitch-black fourth level of dungeons, warning of steps and slopes. Tyrion reflects on arriving as Hand and leaving "like a rat scuttling through the dark, holding hands with a spider."
18. **Arrival at the juncture chamber** — They reach a round chamber with a three-headed dragon mosaic, a dragon-head brazier, five doors, and a ladder leading up. Tyrion recognizes it as the place Shae described — below the Tower of the Hand.
19. **Tyrion decides to climb** — Against Varys's urgent protests, Tyrion insists on climbing the ladder (230 rungs) to reach the bedchamber above. Varys warns it is folly.
20. **Tyrion climbs the ladder** — An agonizing ascent: arms trembling by rung thirty, legs burning by fifty, back in dull agony by eighty. He reaches the top at 230.
21. **Tyrion overhears guards through the walls** — In the narrow tunnel, Tyrion hears Lum and Lester joking about "the Imp's whore" and wagering on whether Tyrion will weep or face the axe bravely. He realizes this is how Varys's "little birds" gather intelligence.
22. **Tyrion enters through the fireplace** — He finds the hidden door mechanism (iron hook between stones), opens it, and edges past the hot ash and burning log in the hearth into the bedchamber.
23. **Tyrion finds Shae in Tywin's bed** — Shae calls out "M'lord?" expecting Tywin. She is naked except for the chain of linked golden hands around her throat. She pleads that the queen made her say things and that Tywin frightens her.
24. **Tyrion strangles Shae** — Using the Hand's chain of golden hands, Tyrion strangles Shae, mentally reciting "For hands of gold are always cold, but a woman's hands are warm."
25. **Tyrion arms himself** — He takes Tywin's dagger from the bedside table, then climbs the chest to reach the crossbow and quiver of quarrels from the wall; he cocks and loads the crossbow.
26. **Tyrion exits into the hallway** — He listens at the door, then slips out with the crossbow against his leg. The hallway is empty.
27. **Tyrion confronts Tywin on the privy** — Tyrion finds Tywin seated on the privy with his bedrobe hiked up. Tywin shows no fear and demands Tyrion put down the crossbow.
28. **Tywin claims he planned to send Tyrion to the Wall** — Tywin says Tyrion is not to be killed, that he intended to send him to the Wall but needed Lord Tyrell's consent.
29. **Tyrion demands to know Tysha's fate** — Tywin barely remembers her; says she was paid and the steward sent her on her way. When Tyrion asks where, Tywin replies "Wherever whores go."
30. **Tyrion shoots Tywin** — The crossbow bolt strikes above the groin, sinking to the fletching. Blood seeps around the shaft. Tywin says "You shot me" in disbelief, then "You are no son of mine."
31. **Tywin dies** — Tyrion says "I believe I'm you writ small" and tells Tywin to die quickly. Tywin dies, his bowels loosening — proving the jape about him false: "Lord Tywin Lannister did not, in the end, shit gold."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Begins in resigned fatalism (expecting death), shifts to shock and dark humor at Jaime's arrival, transforms into devastating grief and white-hot rage upon the Tysha revelation. After separating from Jaime, Tyrion is in a state of cold, purposeful fury — he barely hesitates before killing Shae and is methodical in arming himself and confronting Tywin. Beneath the rage is profound pain and a sense of having been betrayed by everyone he ever loved or trusted.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Tysha — the revelation that she was real consumes him. The word "whore" becomes a trigger. His demand to know Tysha's fate drives him to kill. Secondary: vengeance against his family for a lifetime of contempt and cruelty.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) To separate from Jaime rather than escape together. (2) To lie about killing Joffrey and reveal Cersei's infidelities — deliberate acts of cruelty. (3) To defy Varys and climb to the Tower of the Hand instead of escaping. (4) To kill Shae. (5) To confront and kill Tywin. (6) After killing, to proceed with escape ("I have a ship to catch").
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion's false confession to Joffrey's murder is deliberate cruelty disguised as truth-telling. His internal narration about Shae — "That might have hurt me once, when I still felt pain" — suggests he is suppressing grief. He seems to convince himself he feels nothing when strangling Shae, though the warm hands beating away his tears contradict this. His grim joke about being Tywin "writ small" may contain more self-awareness than he intends — he is in this moment acting exactly as Tywin would.
