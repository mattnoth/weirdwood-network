# ASOS — Epilogue

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 82
- **pov_character:** Merrett Frey
- **pov_chapter_number:** Epilogue

- **location_primary:** Oldstones (ruined castle)
- **location_secondary:** The Twins (referenced), the Trident (referenced), the kingsroad (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Some time after the Red Wedding; snow has fallen in autumn in the riverlands
- **time_markers:** "last night's snow"; approaching sunset ("supposed to be at the ruins by sunset"); "the last light of the setting sun"; "the sun had vanished behind a bank of low clouds"; Lord Walder "soon turn two-and-ninety"; Merrett "would be forty in less than three years"; "a fortnight" (how long Merrett was insensible after his head wound in the past)
- **chapter_summary:** Merrett Frey rides to Oldstones to ransom his nephew Petyr Pimple from outlaws with a hundred golden dragons. He reflects on his miserable life, his position in the Frey succession, and his role at the Red Wedding. Upon arriving, he finds Petyr already hanged. The outlaws take his gold, question him about Sandor Clegane and a child, then prepare to hang him. When Merrett protests they have no witness against him, they reveal Lady Catelyn Stark — resurrected, mute, and horribly disfigured — who nods to condemn him. Merrett is hanged.

## Events & Actions
1. **Merrett rides up the hill to Oldstones** — He follows the winding road, anxious about outlaws and suffering from a building headache.
2. **Merrett reflects on his life and House Frey's succession** — He contemplates Lord Walder's advancing age, the dangerous jockeying among heirs (Ryman, Edwyn, Black Walder, Lothar), and his own worthless position.
3. **Merrett recalls his failed career** — He remembers squiring at Crakehall, being captured by the Kingswood Brotherhood's White Fawn, and the head wound that ended his fighting days.
4. **Merrett recalls his role at the Red Wedding** — His assigned task was to get the Greatjon drunk; the Greatjon still managed to fight when the slaughter began.
5. **Merrett enters the ruins of Oldstones** — He dismounts at the gatehouse ruins and hears faint music from within.
6. **Merrett encounters Tom of Sevenstreams** — The singer sits on King Tristifer's sepulcher playing "Jenny's Song" on a woodharp.
7. **The outlaws surround Merrett** — A dozen or more appear; Lem Lemoncloak demands the gold; Jack-Be-Lucky takes it from the saddlebag.
8. **Merrett asks for Petyr and asks which is Beric Dondarrion** — Jack and Lem joke about taking turns being Lord Beric; Tom says Beric was "needed elsewhere."
9. **The outlaws lead Merrett to the godswood** — Notch holds his horse; Lem leads him through the ruins.
10. **Merrett discovers Petyr Pimple hanging dead from an oak** — He was already killed before Merrett arrived.
11. **The outlaws bind Merrett and put a noose around his neck** — Jack loops hempen rope around his neck; Lem catches the other end thrown over the oak limb.
12. **Tom interrogates Merrett about Sandor Clegane** — Asks if the Hound was at the Red Wedding, possibly with a child (a skinny girl about ten, or a boy the same age). Merrett says he doesn't think so.
13. **Tom tricks Merrett with word-play** — Says he'll "tell them to let you go" — then tells Lem "let him go," and Lem refuses.
14. **Merrett attempts to defend his innocence in the Red Wedding** — Claims he only drank; names the actual plotters (his father, Ryman, Bolton, Lothar, Bastard Walder); says they have no witness.
15. **Lady Stoneheart reveals herself** — The hooded woman lowers her hood; Merrett recognizes the undead Catelyn Stark, throat gash hidden by collar, flesh decomposed, face shredded, eyes hateful.
16. **Lady Stoneheart condemns Merrett** — She nods when asked if he was part of it.
17. **Merrett is hanged** — His feet leave the ground; he is jerked up into the air, kicking and twisting.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious, self-pitying, headache-racked, fearful, despairing; shifts to stark terror when Petyr is found dead and when Lady Stoneheart is revealed; final state is pure animal panic
- **Primary preoccupation:** His chronic headaches, his worthless position in the Frey hierarchy, fear of the outlaws, hope that rescuing Petyr will improve his standing
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to continue up the hill despite temptation to flee; decides not to grab the outlaw taking the gold; tries to defend himself by naming the real Red Wedding plotters
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes bringing back Petyr will change his luck; minimizes his Red Wedding role ("all I did was drink") while having actively worked to incapacitate the Greatjon; tells himself the ransom is "a simple task" even he can't muck up
