- **location_primary:** High Heart; abandoned village (Lord Goodbrook's village)
- **location_secondary:** Riverrun (discussed), the Twins (referenced), Acorn Hall (proposed destination), King's Landing (referenced), Starfall (referenced), Harrenhal (referenced), Stoney Sept (referenced), the Eyrie (referenced), Winterfell (referenced), Summerhall (referenced), Mummer's Ford (referenced), Casterly Rock (referenced), the Wall (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** After previous travels with the Brotherhood Without Banners; the group returns to High Heart, receives prophetic news, then rides to an abandoned village where they learn Riverrun is threatened by Lannisters; Arya is captured by Sandor Clegane
- **time_markers:** "golden in the afternoon sun" (arrival at High Heart); "by sunset they were at the top"; "that night the wind was howling"; night (ghost of High Heart's visit); "come morning" (next day); "half a day's ride to the north" (distance to village); "a thousand years" (ghost's claim about her age); "since before I was born" (Wylla's service to House Dayne per Ned); "a long time ago" (village burning per Anguy and Notch)
- **chapter_summary:** The Brotherhood Without Banners returns to High Heart, where the ghost of High Heart delivers prophecies: the kraken king is dead, Hoster Tully has died, and she sees visions of a wolf howling, clangor with little bells, a maid with purple serpents, and a maid slaying a giant in a snow castle. She directs them to seek Catelyn at the Twins for a wedding. During the next day's ride, Arya learns her companion Ned is Edric Dayne, Lord of Starfall, who claims Jon Snow's mother is a woman named Wylla and that he and Jon are milk brothers; Ned also raises the story of Ashara Dayne and Eddard Stark at Harrenhal. At an abandoned village, Thoros sees Riverrun besieged by Lannisters in the flames, and as the Brotherhood debates their next move, Arya runs outside in distress and is captured by Sandor Clegane.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at High Heart** — The Brotherhood returns to High Heart, recognizable to Arya as a great golden hill. They camp at the top by sunset.
2. **Arya and Ned explore the weirwood stumps** — Arya walks the circle of weirwood stumps with Ned (Lord Beric's squire) and they stand on top of one watching the light fade. Arya senses presences and recalls the hill is haunted.
3. **Thoros gazes into the flames** — A great fire is built; Thoros sits crosslegged, staring into it. Ned tells Arya that Thoros sometimes sees the past, future, or distant events in the flames.
4. **Thoros tells his backstory** — In response to Gendry's skepticism (relaying Tobho Mott's judgment), Thoros reveals he was the youngest of eight children given to the Red Temple, was more troublemaker than priest, had a gift for tongues and occasional visions, was sent to King's Landing to convert King Aerys, and became fond of King Robert.
5. **Lord Beric's cryptic remark on death** — Beric interrupts with "Fire consumes," then tells Thoros "Six times is too many" and turns away.
6. **Ghost of High Heart appears** — At night, a dwarf woman barely three feet tall creeps into camp. Her eyes gleam red in the firelight. She addresses Thoros as "the Ember," Lem as "the Lemon," and Beric as "the Lord of Corpses."
7. **Ghost delivers news and prophecies** — She reports: the kraken king (Balon Greyjoy) is dead and the ironborn turn on each other; Lord Hoster Tully has died; "the goat sits alone and fevered as the great dog descends on him." She shares dreams: a wolf howling in rain unheard; a great clangor of drums, horns, pipes, screams, "but the saddest sound was the little bells"; a maid at a feast with purple serpents in her hair, venom dripping from their fangs; that same maid slaying a savage giant in a castle built of snow.
8. **Ghost confronts Arya** — The ghost turns directly to Arya, calls her "wolf child, blood child," says she smells death on her, sobs and references gorging on grief at Summerhall, and demands Arya leave: "Begone from here, dark heart."
9. **Lord Beric promises to take Arya to Riverrun** — He assures the ghost that Arya will leave with them to be taken to her mother.
10. **Ghost redirects them to the Twins** — She says the Blackfish holds the rivers, and if they want Catelyn, to seek her at the Twins for a wedding. She tells Thoros to look in his fires but "not here" — High Heart belongs to the old gods.
11. **Tom sings "Jenny's song"** — The ghost demands her payment: Tom is woken and sings "Jenny's song." The ghost rocks back and forth, murmuring the words and crying.
12. **Thoros draws Arya aside** — He tells Arya the ghost has her own ways of knowing things, that "the weirwoods whisper in her ear."
13. **Storm breaks; ghost vanishes** — Lightning, thunder, and blinding rain. The ghost disappears as suddenly as she appeared.
14. **Morning illness** — Ned, Lem, and Watty the Miller wake with chills. Watty cannot keep breakfast down. Ned is feverish, shivering, skin clammy.
15. **Ride to the abandoned village** — The group rides north through woods and fields, fording swollen streams. Merrit and Mudge also fall ill coughing.
16. **Ned complains about rain and helm** — He says rain on his helm gives headaches, but without it his hair sticks to his face. Gendry curtly suggests he shave his head.
17. **Arya observes Ned's Dornish appearance** — She notes he does not look like a typical Dornishman: big blue-purple eyes, pale ash-blond hair.
18. **Ned reveals his identity and backstory** — Through conversation, Arya learns Ned has been Beric's page since age seven (when Beric espoused his aunt) and squire since ten. He was at the Battle of the Mummer's Ford, dragged Beric from the river, and stood over him with a sword.
19. **Arya reflects on her own kills** — She remembers the stableboy in King's Landing, the guard at Harrenhal, Ser Amory's men at the holdfast, and wonders about Weese, Chiswyck, and the weasel soup deaths. She feels suddenly sad.
20. **Ned reveals he is Edric Dayne, Lord of Starfall** — After mentioning he saw Arya's father at the Hand's tourney and Sansa receiving Ser Loras's rose, he admits he is Edric Dayne.
21. **Ned reveals Wylla as Jon Snow's mother** — He tells Arya that Wylla nursed him because his mother had no milk, and that Wylla is Jon Snow's mother who has served House Dayne for years. He and Jon are "milk brothers."
22. **Arya resolves to tell Jon** — She thinks she must remember the name Wylla to tell Jon, and considers writing him a letter from Riverrun.
23. **Ned raises the Ashara Dayne story** — He mentions Ser Arthur Dayne and Lady Ashara, who threw herself from the Palestone Sword. His aunt Allyria says Ashara and Eddard Stark fell in love at Harrenhal during "the year of the false spring."
24. **Arya angrily denies the story** — She insists her father loved only her mother. Gendry interjects sarcastically about Jon's parentage. Arya throws a crabapple at Gendry and tells him to go back to Stoney Sept.
25. **Gendry speaks about his own father** — He says he doesn't know his father's name, guesses he was a smelly drunk, and recounts his mother's threats. He says both their fathers are dead, so it doesn't matter.
26. **Arya rides ahead to Anguy** — Upset, she asks Anguy if Dornishmen lie. He jokes that marchers are also called liars.
27. **Arya attempts to ride away** — She leaves the trail, leaps a log, splashes through a stream, but knows escape is futile.
28. **Harwin catches Arya and explains** — He tells her the Ashara Dayne tale is old; when Ned Stark met Ashara, Brandon was alive and betrothed to Catelyn, so there was no dishonor. Ashara's death was likely grief for her brother Arthur. Harwin asks Arya not to mention this to her mother at Riverrun.
29. **Arrival at the abandoned village** — They shelter in a grey stone stable with half a roof. Arya asks if Lannisters destroyed the village. Anguy notes the moss and tree growth indicate it was torched long ago.
30. **Notch reveals the village's history** — It was Lord Goodbrook's village, burned by Hoster Tully during Robert's Rebellion because Goodbrook stayed loyal to the king.
31. **Brotherhood builds fire in the stable** — Jack-Be-Lucky hacks wood, Notch and Merrit gather straw, Thoros strikes the spark, Lem fans with his yellow cloak.
32. **Thoros sees Lannisters besieging Riverrun** — Gazing into the flames, Thoros mutters "Riverrun" and then reports a vision: Riverrun as "an island in a sea of fire," with "leaping lions with long crimson claws." He tells Arya the Lannisters will soon besiege Riverrun.
33. **Brotherhood debates what to do with Arya** — Tom doubts the Blackfish will pay for a girl he doesn't know. Lem insists they take her to Riverrun. Tom warns of being caught by lions. Lord Beric says he does not mean to be taken — with the unspoken word "alive." He proposes going to Acorn Hall for shelter and intelligence.
34. **Arya breaks for the door** — Overwhelmed, she runs out into the rain, evading Harwin's grab.
35. **Arya's despairing run** — She runs through rain and lightning, thinking the outlaws are liars, that she should have traveled alone, that they were never her pack.
36. **Sandor Clegane captures Arya** — Arya stumbles into a sentry position and a mailed hand grabs her arm. Sandor Clegane laughs, calls her "wolf girl," says "You're mine," yanks her off her feet and drags her toward his horse. Arya recalls his earlier question: "Do you know what dogs do to wolves?"

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Cycles through curiosity (watching Thoros, listening to the ghost), fear (the ghost's confrontation, cold fingers on her neck), longing for family (Jon, Robb, Catelyn), fierce defensiveness (of her father's honor), sadness (reflecting on her kills, missing Jon), frustration and anger (at the outlaws' broken promises, at being unable to reach Riverrun), despair (learning Riverrun is besieged), and finally terror (captured by the Hound)
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting to Riverrun and her mother; secondarily, processing the revelation about Wylla as Jon's mother and the Ashara Dayne story
- **Key decisions made:** Resolves to remember the name Wylla to tell Jon; decides to run from the outlaws in distress after learning Riverrun is threatened; considers that she should have traveled alone
- **Self-deception flags:** "They were never my pack" — denying her connection to the Brotherhood despite having traveled with them extensively; "If she had been alone, the outlaws would never have caught her" — unlikely given her age and circumstances; insists "He loved my lady mother" with fierce certainty despite never having discussed it with her father
