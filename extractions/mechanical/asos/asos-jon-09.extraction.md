Jon takes dreamwine and sleeps in the King's Tower, only to be roused by Eastwatch men and brought before Janos Slynt and Alliser Thorne, who charge him with oathbreaking, cowardice, and desertion. Rattleshirt testifies that Jon killed Qhorin Halfhand willingly, Maester Aemon defends Jon, but Slynt sentences him to hang and orders Thorne to take him to an ice cell. Jon attacks Thorne before being restrained.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon wakes at dawn in the warming shed** — Owen rouses him; Jon has barely slept, dreaming of fighting when he closes his eyes
2. **Jon surveys the wildling camp through the Myrish eye** — He sees Dalla tending fire, Val milking a goat, Tormund and his sons near the turtle, and Varamyr Sixskins with his shadowcat; no sign of Mance himself
3. **Jon observes the turtle is nearly complete** — The wildlings have skinned a dead mammoth during the night and are lashing the raw hide over the turtle's roof
4. **Jon sends Grenn to sleep** — Noting Grenn's exhaustion from the sounds of chopping all night
5. **News from Shadow Tower recalled** — A raven reported Bowen Marsh won a costly battle at the Bridge of Skulls against the Weeper; over a hundred brothers killed including Ser Endrew Tarth and Ser Aladale Wynch; Marsh himself sorely wounded
6. **Jon recalls sending Zei to Mole's Town** — She never returned; Mully found the entire village deserted including the brothel
7. **Jon forces himself to eat breakfast** — Bread, bacon, onions, and cheese
8. **The turtle begins its approach** — Horse spots it; Jon orders the warhorn blown (two blasts by Kegs)
9. **Fire arrows fail against the turtle** — Wet hides protect it; flames gutter out on impact
10. **Scorpion bolts and catapult stones fail** — Bolts punch deep but do no damage; stones bounce off the layered hides
11. **The turtle reaches the Wall** — Wildlings begin clearing dead giants from the gate; Jon cannot lean out far enough to see without Horse pulling him back
12. **Jon orders the frozen gravel barrels pushed off** — Four barrels are rolled to the edge and tipped over the Wall
13. **The first barrel crushes the front of the turtle** — Splintering wood, shouts and screams; wildlings spill out and flee
14. **All four barrels are used; the turtle is destroyed** — Satin fires quarrels at retreating wildlings; the defenders celebrate; eight barrels remain
15. **Jon descends to the King's Tower** — Leaves Pyp in command of the Wall; takes dreamwine from Maester Aemon and falls asleep
16. **Jon is awakened by four strangers in black** — Sky is black; they drag him from bed and march him up to the Old Bear's solar
17. **Jon is brought before Janos Slynt and Ser Alliser Thorne** — Slynt sits in the Old Bear's chair; Thorne accuses Jon of being a turncloak; Maester Aemon, Septon Cellador, and Ser Wynton Stout are also present
18. **Jon is charged with oathbreaking, cowardice, and desertion** — Slynt demands explanations for Jon joining the wildlings
19. **Jon explains his mission with Qhorin Halfhand** — States he joined the wildlings under Qhorin's orders to learn about the Horn of Winter and Mance's plans
20. **Jon admits to breaking his vows with Ygritte** — Refuses to dishonor her by equating her with Mole's Town whores; states he cared for her
21. **Rattleshirt is brought in as witness** — Testifies that Jon begged for his life and murdered Qhorin willingly; spits blood on Jon's foot
22. **Maester Aemon defends Jon** — States Jon held the Wall, proved himself valiant, loyal, and resourceful; says Donal Noye was satisfied with Jon's explanations
23. **Septon Cellador notes Jon took his vows before a heart tree** — Suggests this connects him to wildling religion
24. **Slynt sentences Jon to hang** — Declares him a traitor and orders Thorne to take him to an ice cell
25. **Jon attacks Ser Alliser Thorne** — Grabs him by the throat and lifts him off the floor; Eastwatch men pull him off; Thorne says "The boy is a wildling"

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bone-deep exhaustion pervades the entire chapter; Jon forces himself to eat, forces smiles, pushes through pain in his leg. During the trial, his exhaustion gives way to anger — grief over Ygritte is "too fresh" to deny her; rage over Slynt calling his father a traitor drives him past caring about consequences. He briefly considers whether they should all abandon the Wall ("Maybe we should all do the same").
- **Primary preoccupation:** The immediate military defense of the Wall; dwindling supplies (no oil, no pitch, arrows running short, no fletchers); the futility of their position with so few men against Mance's horde. During the trial, defending his honor and his father's name.
- **Key decisions made:** Orders frozen gravel barrels pushed off the Wall to crush the turtle (his invention); leaves Pyp in command while he rests; refuses to dishonor Ygritte by comparing her to whores; refuses to accept Slynt calling his father a traitor; physically attacks Thorne rather than submit.
- **Self-deception flags:** None explicitly observed; Jon is remarkably clear-eyed about their dire military situation and his personal vulnerability at the trial.
