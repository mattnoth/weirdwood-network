# ASOS — Jon XII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 80
- **pov_character:** Jon Snow
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon XII
- **first_available:** ASOS Jon XII
- **location_primary:** Castle Black
- **location_secondary:** The Wall (top and tunnel), the killing ground beyond the Wall, edge of the haunted forest
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Stannis Baratheon's arrival at the Wall; the Lord Commander election has been ongoing for multiple rounds (at least ten prior votes mentioned)
- **time_markers:** "hardly slept last night"; walked top of the Wall "till the sun came up"; "the afternoon was growing old, and the sun was low in the west"; sunset described as sun dips behind the Wall; nightfire kindled at dusk; the choosing occurs in the evening/night; "the night was dark"
- **chapter_summary:** Jon trains with Iron Emmett but loses control after a memory of childhood at Winterfell with Robb triggers his inner conflict over Stannis's offer to make him Lord of Winterfell. He wrestles with the temptation throughout the day, overhears Thorne, Marsh, and Yarwyck plotting to support Janos Slynt for Lord Commander, and wanders beyond the Wall to think. Ghost returns to him at dusk after a long absence, and Jon sees in the white direwolf's red eyes a sign of the old gods that decides him against accepting Stannis's offer. He returns to find the election in chaos, learns Edd Tollett has nominated him, and is elected the 998th Lord Commander of the Night's Watch.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon trains with Iron Emmett** — Jon, sleep-deprived from wrestling with Stannis's offer all night, spars poorly against Iron Emmett in the practice yard.
2. **Emmett strikes Jon's temple** — A savage forehand slash catches Jon on the temple, dazing him.
3. **Jon flashes back to childhood at Winterfell** — He remembers sparring with Robb as boys, and the day Robb told him he could never be Lord of Winterfell because he was bastard-born.
4. **Jon loses control and beats Emmett** — In a fury, Jon overwhelms Iron Emmett, disarming him and splintering his shield. Halder and Horse pull him off.
5. **Jon retreats to the armory** — He sits alone, overwhelmed by emotion over Stannis's offer and the memory of Winterfell.
6. **Jon takes the wormways to the bathhouse** — He descends underground to avoid his friends and soaks in a hot tub.
7. **Jon overhears the conspiracy** — Thorne, Marsh, and Yarwyck discuss supporting Janos Slynt, noting Tywin Lannister's favor for Slynt. They are startled when Jon reveals his presence.
8. **Jon walks through Castle Black** — He passes the ruined Lord Commander's Tower, the spot where Ygritte died, the King's Tower, and the charred stair.
9. **Jon passes through the Wall** — He goes through the tunnel and emerges on the north side.
10. **Jon reflects on the election and Stannis's offer** — He concludes Yarwyck will be swayed to support Slynt, leaving him with Winterfell as his alternative.
11. **Jon contemplates accepting Stannis's offer** — He sits on a stump at the forest's edge, fantasizing about Val, children, restoring Winterfell, fostering Mance's son and Gilly's boy.
12. **Jon feels Ghost's hunger through the warg bond** — His desire shifts from lordship to prey and blood, confusing him until he realizes it's Ghost.
13. **Ghost returns** — The direwolf emerges from the forest; they wrestle joyfully. Jon notes Ghost has been absent since he climbed the Wall.
14. **Jon sees his answer in Ghost** — He recognizes Ghost's red eyes and white fur as signs of the old gods, connecting the direwolf to the weirwood, and decides he cannot accept Stannis's offer.
15. **Jon sees Melisandre and Stannis at the nightfire** — They emerge from the tunnel to lead prayers; Jon and Ghost circle wide around the fire.
16. **Jon sees Val in her tower window** — He thinks "I'm not the man to steal you out of there."
17. **Jon enters the cellar during election chaos** — He finds the Night's Watch in uproar: Slynt bellowing, Emmett standing on a table with a sword, Hobb cursing, brothers shouting.
18. **Pyp whistles and silence falls** — Ghost's appearance and Pyp's whistle quiet the hall.
19. **Thorne insults Jon** — Calls him "the turncloak."
20. **Slynt denounces Jon** — Calls Ghost "the beast that tore the life from Halfhand," names Jon a warg unfit to lead or live.
21. **Maester Aemon reveals Jon's nomination** — Tells Jon his name has been put forth as Lord Commander.
22. **Edd Tollett claims credit for the nomination** — Says it's "a terrible cruel thing to do to a friend."
23. **Cotter Pyke challenges Slynt** — Reminds him he wears a black cloak now.
24. **Ser Denys Mallister affirms the nomination is valid** — Any brother may offer any name.
25. **Thorne calls for a vote** — Says they will vote and choose again all night if needed.
26. **Yarwyck withdraws his candidacy** — Initially plans to endorse Slynt, then changes his mind mid-speech and suggests Jon might be better, citing his time on the Wall, being Ben Stark's nephew, and serving the Old Bear.
27. **The kettle is brought for voting** — Sam and Clydas drag it to the table.
28. **Mormont's raven bursts from the kettle** — Flies around the vault and lands on Jon's shoulder, calling "Snow."
29. **Thorne accuses Sam of training the bird** — Claims it's a trick.
30. **The vote is overwhelmingly for Jon** — Arrowheads (Jon's tokens) flood the count, drowning out stones, shells, and copper pennies.
31. **Jon is congratulated** — Dozens of brothers press around him; Dywen, Emmett, Hobb, Marsh, Pyke, and Mallister all speak to him.
32. **Ser Denys Mallister concedes gracefully** — Tells Jon his time will never come now, but urges him not to make him die regretful; praises Jon's uncle and father.
33. **Jon walks across the castle** — With raven on shoulder and Ghost at heels, followed by Pyp, Grenn, and Sam.
34. **Sam's role revealed** — Pyp and Grenn credit Sam for engineering the election; Sam denies responsibility for the raven.
35. **Jon accepts the result** — Takes one swallow of wine. Reflects: "The Wall was his, the night was dark, and he had a king to face."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Turbulent throughout — exhausted and conflicted in the morning; furious during training (loses control); haunted by childhood memory of Robb and Catelyn's rejection; deeply tempted by Winterfell; guilty about wanting it; joyful at Ghost's return; stunned by the election; settling into quiet resolve by chapter's end.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether to accept Stannis's offer of Winterfell (legitimization, Val, the North) or remain with the Night's Watch. This dominates every scene until Ghost's return resolves it.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses Stannis's offer (internal decision triggered by Ghost's weirwood eyes and connection to the old gods — "He had his answer then"); accepts the role of Lord Commander.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells himself "I have always wanted it" (Winterfell) and calls this guilty — he's been suppressing this desire. He also initially frames the choice as practical (Janos Slynt will have him hanged anyway), when it's actually deeply emotional.
