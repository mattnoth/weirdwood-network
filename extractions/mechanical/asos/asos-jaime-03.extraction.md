- **chapter_summary:** Jaime, Brienne, and Cleos Frey ride through war-devastated Riverlands, passing through the ruined town of Maidenpool. They take the Duskendale road and are ambushed by bowmen; Cleos Frey is killed. Jaime seizes Cleos's sword and fights Brienne in an extended duel, but she defeats him. The Brave Companions, now serving Roose Bolton, capture them both. Jaime attempts to bribe Urswyck to take them to King's Landing and lies about Tarth's wealth to protect Brienne. They are brought before Vargo Hoat, who orders Jaime's hand cut off with a Dothraki arakh.

## Events & Actions
1. **Travel through destroyed Riverlands** — Jaime, Brienne, and Cleos ride through miles of blackened fields, burned bridges, and swollen streams along the kingsroad. No people seen; wolves howl at night.
2. **Arrival at Maidenpool** — They find the town ruined: gates smashed, homes burned, the legendary pool choked with rotting corpses. Lord Mooton's banner still flies but the town is deserted except for feral dogs.
3. **Jaime sings provocatively** — Jaime bursts into "Six Maids in a Pool" to taunt Brienne and test for enemies. Cleos nervously warns about Lord Mooton and hidden enemies.
4. **Taking the Duskendale road** — Brienne chooses the faster inland Duskendale road rather than the slower coastal route. Jaime agrees despite Cleos's preference for the coast.
5. **Jaime reminisces about Cersei** — Jaime recalls childhood intimacy with Cersei at Casterly Rock, their mother Joanna catching them, and her death birthing Tyrion. He fantasizes about openly marrying Cersei.
6. **Jaime resolves to return the Stark girls** — Jaime internally decides to return Sansa and Arya (if found) to their mother, amused by the idea of keeping faith when betrayal is expected.
7. **Bowman ambush** — Unseen archers fire from behind a low stone wall near a wheatfield. Jaime's gelding is hit in the rump. Cleos is struck by arrows and dragged by his bolting palfrey.
8. **Brienne hit by arrows** — Brienne takes an arrow in the back and another in the leg but seems unfazed, draws her sword.
9. **Jaime and Brienne charge the bowmen** — Jaime, unarmed and chained, charges on horseback to show Brienne how it's done. She follows, shouting "Evenfall!" and "Tarth!" The bowmen break and flee into the woods.
10. **Discovery of Cleos Frey's death** — They find Cleos tangled in his stirrup with arrows in arm and chest, but the dragging killed him — his skull is crushed, with broken bone moving under the skin.
11. **Jaime claims Cleos's possessions** — Jaime wants Cleos's horse, clothes, and sword. Brienne is shocked at his callousness toward his dead cousin.
12. **Jaime seizes Cleos's sword** — Despite Brienne's refusal to arm him, Jaime pulls the longsword from the scabbard and immediately attacks Brienne.
13. **Extended sword duel** — Jaime fights Brienne aggressively with chained wrists. He initially presses the attack with superior technique, driving her across the road and into trees.
14. **Brienne gains the upper hand** — Jaime's skills have deteriorated from imprisonment, and the chains exhaust him. Brienne is stronger and begins to wear him down. She cuts his brow, bleeding into his right eye.
15. **Jaime realizes Brienne is stronger** — He compares her strength to Robert Baratheon, Gerold Hightower, Arthur Dayne, Greatjon Umber, Strongboar, and the Cleganes, admitting she is overpowering him.
16. **Brienne defeats Jaime** — Jaime slips on a rock, stabs Brienne's thigh in a lunge, but falls. Brienne kicks away his sword, pins him, takes her dagger back, and forces his head underwater until he yields.
17. **Brave Companions arrive** — Armed men surround them on both sides of the brook. Jaime recognizes the diverse sellsword company: Dornishmen, Lyseni, Dothraki, Ibbenese, Summer Islanders.
18. **Jaime identifies the Brave Companions** — He recognizes them and realizes they are no longer in Lannister service.
19. **Urswyck reveals their defection** — Urswyck announces the Brave Companions now serve Lord Bolton and the King in the North. The Lannisters "lost too many battles."
20. **Beatings** — Rorge punches Jaime repeatedly in the stomach. Four Mummers beat Brienne into submission, knocking out two teeth.
21. **Captives bound and mounted** — Timeon binds Jaime and Brienne back to back atop Brienne's plow horse. Mummers strip Cleos's corpse and divvy his possessions; Rorge takes the surcoat.
22. **Jaime warns Brienne about rape** — Jaime tells Brienne she will be raped that night and advises against resistance. She asks if that is what he would do; he says he would make them kill him.
23. **Jaime attempts to bribe Urswyck** — Jaime offers all the gold in Casterly Rock, a royal pardon, and a knighthood if Urswyck takes them to King's Landing. Urswyck is briefly tempted but refuses, slapping Jaime.
24. **Jaime lies about Tarth** — Jaime tells Urswyck that Tarth is called the Sapphire Isle, implying gemstone wealth, to make Brienne more valuable as ransom and thus protect her from rape. Brienne whispers that the name comes from the blue waters, not gems.
25. **Arrival at Vargo Hoat's camp** — They find Hoat sacking a small sept. A hanged septon's corpse is used as archery practice. A fat Dothraki pries chalcedony eyes from a wooden Mother statue. Carved gods have been dragged outside.
26. **Vargo Hoat claims Jaime** — Hoat declares Jaime his captive. Brienne tries to invoke Catelyn Stark's authority and the King in the North; Hoat orders her silenced. Rorge drags her off the horse and kicks her.
27. **Jaime attempts to bribe Vargo Hoat** — Jaime offers gold and warns Hoat against opposing Tywin. Hoat agrees he'll have half the gold of Casterly Rock — but first he must "send a message."
28. **Jaime's hand is cut off** — Jaime is forced to the ground, his arms yanked forward by his chains. A jester hops on his back. The fat Dothraki approaches with the arakh. Jaime resolves not to scream. The arakh comes down and Jaime screams.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Restless, bored, sardonic early on; blood singing with exhilaration during the duel; then chilled by the realization that Brienne is overpowering him; contemptuous when facing the Brave Companions; calculating when attempting bribes; resigned to the possibility of death but unwilling to die yet; feels sorry for Brienne regarding her impending rape; resentful that everything turns on Aerys; resolves not to scream at the end — but does
- **Primary preoccupation:** Cersei — desire to be with her, memories of childhood together; his lost fighting ability; his honor/reputation as oathbreaker; survival and escape
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to return Sansa and Arya Stark to their mother; decides to seize Cleos's sword and fight Brienne; decides to lie about Tarth being rich in sapphires to protect Brienne from rape; decides not to attempt escape against the Brave Companions despite considering grabbing a sword
- **Self-deception flags:** Jaime fantasizes about openly marrying Cersei and keeping Joffrey on the throne by force, dismissing the political impossibility; he initially believes his chained fighting will give Brienne only a brief contest — badly underestimating her; he resolves "no sellsword would make him scream" immediately before screaming
