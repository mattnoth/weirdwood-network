# ASOS — Jaime VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 45
- **pov_character:** Jaime Lannister

- **first_available:** ASOS Jaime VI
- **location_primary:** Road between Harrenhal and the Gods Eye; Harrenhal (return)
- **location_secondary:** Casterly Rock (dream), King's Landing (referenced), the Citadel (referenced), Tarth (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Jaime's departure from Harrenhal following his maiming; his stump is healing but fever lingers; he hopes to arrive in time for Joffrey's wedding
- **time_markers:** "the morn of their departure"; Ser Aenys Frey marched three days before; they ride through the day; "by evenfall" they camp; dream occurs at night under stars; they wake before dawn; "by the time the sun came up, they were halfway back to Harrenhal"; "it was midday before they reached the castle"
- **chapter_summary:** Jaime departs Harrenhal with Steelshanks Walton's escort of two hundred men and Qyburn, heading south toward King's Landing. Along the way, Qyburn reveals that Lord Selwyn offered only three hundred dragons for Brienne's ransom, and that Vargo Hoat is displeased. That night, Jaime has a vivid fever dream in which ghostly Kingsguard and Rhaegar Targaryen accuse him, while Brienne stands with him bearing a flaming sword. He wakes and immediately threatens Steelshanks into turning back to Harrenhal, where they find Brienne in a bear pit armed only with a blunted tourney sword. Jaime jumps into the pit, and Steelshanks's crossbowmen kill the bear. They take Brienne and depart.

## Events & Actions
1. **Departure preparations** — Roose Bolton informs Jaime that Qyburn will accompany him; Steelshanks Walton commands the 200-man escort
2. **Jaime taunts the Brave Companions** — He rides over to Zollo, Pyg, Timeon, Shagwell, and Rorge in the yard, promising to return and pay his debts
3. **Departure from Harrenhal** — Both parties leave through the eastern gate under cold grey skies; they separate six miles on, Bolton heading northeast, Jaime south along the lake road
4. **Travel along Gods Eye** — Walton avoids the kingsroad, using farmer's tracks and game trails near the lake; Jaime wants the faster kingsroad route
5. **Jaime's Harrenhal tourney memory** — Passing a deserted mill triggers memory of his Kingsguard investiture, Aerys's spite in sending him away, and his realization that he was chosen to rob Tywin of his heir
6. **Qyburn discusses Pia** — Reveals he sent a woman to Jaime's bed the previous night; discusses Vargo Hoat's practice of having women examined
7. **Qyburn reveals Brienne's ransom situation** — Lord Selwyn offered only 300 dragons; Vargo Hoat is convinced he's being cheated; Hoat refuses to haggle
8. **Passage through burned village** — Jaime recalls the inn that once stood there; refuses to stop despite Steelshanks's suggestion
9. **Evening camp** — They camp in woods of oak and elm; Jaime takes dreamwine; falls asleep hoping to dream of Cersei
10. **Jaime's fever dream** — Naked in caverns beneath Casterly Rock, he has both hands; cowled figures force him down; Cersei, Tywin, and Joffrey appear then abandon him; Brienne appears in chains which he frees; both receive flaming swords; ghostly Kingsguard and Rhaegar ride in and accuse him; Jaime's flame dies, only Brienne's remains as ghosts rush in
11. **Jaime wakes screaming** — Finds himself in starry darkness; stump wrapped in leather and linen; feels phantom hand; discovers he rested his head on a white (weirwood) stump
12. **Qyburn's ghost speech** — Jaime asks if he believes in ghosts; Qyburn tells of sensing a woman's presence in an empty room at the Citadel; mentions Marwyn was the only archmaester who agreed with him
13. **Decision to return** — Jaime tells Walton to saddle horses, saying he left something at Harrenhal
14. **Jaime threatens Steelshanks** — Threatens to tell Tywin that Bolton ordered the hand cut off and Walton swung the blade; also promises gold
15. **Ride back to Harrenhal** — They ride hard, arriving midday; castle appears dead, gates closed, only Qohor goat banner flying
16. **Entry to Harrenhal** — They shout until admitted; Brave Companions still treat them as allies
17. **Discovery of bear pit** — Jaime hears roaring and laughter; races through outer ward, under stone bridge, around Wailing Tower, through Flowstone Yard
18. **Brienne fighting the bear** — She wears pink satin gown, armed with blunted tourney sword; bear has raked her left arm; she circles but cannot kill the bear
19. **Jaime confronts Vargo Hoat** — Demands Brienne be pulled out; offers ransom; Hoat refuses, tells him to go get her himself
20. **Jaime jumps into the bear pit** — Vaults the marble rail one-handed; flings sand at bear's face; puts himself between Brienne and bear; throws a maggoty jawbone at the bear
21. **Bear killed by crossbowmen** — Steelshanks's archers fire bolts; multiple quarrels strike the bear; it dies
22. **Standoff with Mummers** — Rorge, Three Toes have swords out; Zollo uncoils whip; Steelshanks threatens Hoat; Hoat backs down
23. **Departure with Brienne** — They take Brienne and ride half a league before Steelshanks expresses his anger at Jaime's recklessness
24. **Brienne asks why** — Jaime answers: "I dreamed of you"

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Conflicted and haunted; anxious to return to Cersei; bitter about the loss of his hand and identity; progressively disturbed by guilt surfacing in his dream; ultimately driven by an impulse he cannot fully articulate to save Brienne
- **Primary preoccupation:** The loss of his sword hand and what it means for his identity; phantom hand sensations; the fever dream's accusatory visions of dead Kingsguard brothers and Rhaegar; growing awareness that Brienne is in mortal danger
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses Pia (stays faithful to Cersei); decides to return to Harrenhal despite being safely away; threatens Steelshanks with lies to force the return; jumps into bear pit unarmed
- **Self-deception flags:** Repeatedly tells himself he shouldn't care about Brienne ("why should I care?"); rationalizes his return as owing a debt rather than acknowledging emotional connection; thinks "I have a woman" about Cersei while his dream places Brienne at his side
