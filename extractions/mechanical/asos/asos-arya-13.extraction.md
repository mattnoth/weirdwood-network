# ASOS — Arya XIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 75
- **pov_character:** Arya Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya XIII
- **first_available:** ASOS Arya XIII
- **location_primary:** An inn near the ruby ford (crossroads inn)
- **location_secondary:** The Trident (riverbank camp), Saltpans, the docks at Saltpans, aboard the Titan's Daughter
- **approximate_timeline:** Late in the book; occurs after the Red Wedding (Robb is dead), after Joffrey's death at his wedding feast, after Gregor Clegane has retaken Harrenhal
- **time_markers:** Fight at the inn occurs during daylight; they ride south by east for hours before reaching the Trident; they camp at dusk; Arya watches the moon through branches at night; Sandor wakes her at pale dawn light; they ride until before noon, stop early; Arya reaches Saltpans "on a bright morning six days later"; she arrives at the town "a little after midday"
- **chapter_summary:** Arya and Sandor Clegane stop at an inn near the ruby ford, where they encounter Polliver, the Tickler, and a squire in Gregor Clegane's service. A fight breaks out: Sandor kills Polliver, Arya kills the Tickler and the squire, and recovers her sword Needle from Polliver's belt. Badly wounded, Sandor grows feverish as they ride toward Saltpans; Arya tends his wounds with boiled wine but ultimately refuses his plea for a mercy killing and rides away, leaving him under a tree. Six days later she reaches Saltpans, sells her horse, and secures passage on the Braavosi galley Titan's Daughter by presenting the iron coin Jaqen H'ghar gave her and speaking the words "valar morghulis."

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at the inn** — Arya and Sandor arrive at an inn near the ruby ford. A woman's bones hang from a gibbet outside. Arya recognizes it as an inn she once visited with Sansa and Septa Mordane.
2. **Arya considers escape** — She thinks about riding off on Craven and taking Stranger, but decides to follow Sandor inside.
3. **Recognition** — Inside, they find Polliver, the Tickler, and a young squire — all Gregor Clegane's men. The recognition is mutual.
4. **News delivered** — Polliver informs them that Gregor was at Harrenhal but the queen sent for him; Joffrey is dead (poisoned at his wedding feast); the Imp and Sansa are suspected; the Blackfish is under siege at Riverrun; Randyll Tarly holds Maidenpool; the Brackens have switched sides. Polliver also reports "the sister" is intended for Bolton's bastard.
5. **Arya learns Joffrey is dead** — She feels it should make her happy but feels empty inside, thinking Robb is dead too.
6. **The Hound learns Sansa escaped** — He calls her "the little bird" and says "bloody good for her."
7. **Fight erupts** — The Tickler throws a knife at Sandor, grazing his ribs. Sandor draws his sword and engages Polliver. The Tickler moves to flank Sandor.
8. **Arya throws wine cup at squire** — Hits him on his white pimple, knocking him down.
9. **Arya tries to throw a dagger at the Tickler** — It hits hilt-first and has no effect.
10. **Sandor wounded** — He takes a gash from temple to cheek on his burned side (losing the stub of his ear), a wound on the back of his neck, and an ugly red gash on his upper thigh.
11. **Squire grabs Arya** — She stabs him in the belly with his own knife, twisting it.
12. **Arya retrieves the Tickler's knife from the wall** — She pulls it free for use.
13. **Sandor kills Polliver** — He kicks a bench into Polliver's shins, ducks under Polliver's slash, and delivers a vicious backhand cut that splits Polliver's face, taking half his head off.
14. **Arya kills the Tickler** — She steps up behind him and stabs him through the back, shouting his own interrogation questions: "Is there gold hidden in the village? Is there silver? Gems? Is there food? Where is Lord Beric?" and repeating "How many?" over and over. Sandor drags her off.
15. **Arya recovers Needle** — She unbuckles Polliver's swordbelt and takes the slim blade — "too long to be a dirk, too short to be a man's sword... but it felt just right in her hand."
16. **Arya gives the squire mercy** — At Sandor's prompting ("You remember where the heart is?"), she slides Needle between his ribs and kills him. The squire's last word is "Mercy."
17. **Flight from the inn** — They avoid the ruby ford and angle south by east through weedy fields, woods, and marshes. Hours later they reach the Trident.
18. **Camp by the Trident** — They shelter among willows and weathered rocks. Sandor has Arya build a fire, boil wine in his helm, and pour it on his wounds. He screams when she treats the wound where his ear was, then passes out.
19. **Arya tends wounds** — She bandages him using strips cut from the squire's cloak, hobbles the horses, and watches the moon.
20. **Arya recites her death list** — She says the names: "Ser Gregor the Mountain, Dunsen, Raff the Sweetling, Ser Ilyn, Ser Meryn, Queen Cersei." She notes it feels queer to leave out Polliver and the Tickler. She adds "The Hound" and "Valar morghulis."
21. **Wolf dream** — Arya dreams she is a wolf chasing a riderless horse up a hill with a pack behind her, before Sandor wakes her at dawn.
22. **Sandor weakens** — He is slow, clumsy, sweating, bleeding through bandages; he can barely stay on Stranger. They halt before noon. He falls when dismounting and crawls to a tree.
23. **Arya considers killing Sandor** — She examines his wounds (the thigh wound smells bad); she thinks about riding off and leaving him to die. She draws Needle and takes a water dancer's stance.
24. **Sandor wakes, begs for mercy** — He confesses killing Mycah ("cut him near in half, and laughed about it after"), confesses standing by while Sansa was beaten, confesses wanting to rape Sansa. He sobs. He begs: "The gift of mercy... avenge your little Michael."
25. **Arya corrects him ("Mycah") and refuses mercy** — She says "You don't deserve the gift of mercy," saddles Craven, and rides away. Sandor says "A real wolf would finish a wounded animal." Arya replies "You shouldn't have hit me with an axe. You should have saved my mother." She never looks back.
26. **Six days' travel** — Arya follows the Trident as it widens toward the sea.
27. **Arrival at Saltpans** — She finds a small town with a holdfast, burned shops, and a harbor with three ships (two river galleys and the Titan's Daughter).
28. **Arya sells Craven** — A horse trader woman behind the sept cheats her, giving a pitifully flat purse and refusing extra for saddle and bridle.
29. **Arya approaches the Titan's Daughter** — The captain says they're heading home, not north; the silver isn't enough; he warns of pirates and ice.
30. **Arya presents the iron coin** — She produces the black iron coin from her smallclothes and says "Valar morghulis." The captain responds "Valar dohaeris," touches his brow with two fingers, and grants her a cabin.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Complex and shifting. Empty rather than happy at Joffrey's death ("Joffrey was dead, but if Robb was dead too, what did it matter?"). Panicked during the fight ("she could taste the beginnings of panic in the back of her throat"). Cold and purposeful when killing the Tickler (channeling his own words). Torn between hatred and something else when considering killing Sandor — she draws Needle but cannot strike. Lonely and desolate ("I have no home... I have no pack. And now I don't even have a horse"). Desperate but resourceful at Saltpans.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and finding a way forward; her death list; the question of whether to kill or spare Sandor; escape by sea
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Decides to follow Sandor into the inn rather than flee on the horses. (2) Intervenes in the fight by throwing cups and stabbing the squire. (3) Kills the Tickler by stabbing him from behind while reciting his questions. (4) Gives the squire the "gift of mercy" with Needle at Sandor's direction. (5) Refuses to give Sandor the mercy of death. (6) Rides away alone, leaving him to die. (7) Sells Craven at Saltpans. (8) Uses the iron coin and "valar morghulis" to secure passage on the Titan's Daughter.
- **Self-deception flags:** Arya tells herself she "couldn't" go back for Sandor's money, but her internal logic may mask reluctance to face him again or acknowledge any bond. She justifies leaving Sandor to die by listing his crimes, but the chapter makes clear the decision is emotionally complicated — she had left his name off her list, and when she draws Needle on him she cannot follow through.
