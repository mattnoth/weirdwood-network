# ASOS — Arya IX

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 48
- **pov_character:** Arya Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya IX
- **first_available:** ASOS Arya IX
- **location_primary:** The Trident (flooded river crossing at Lord Harroway's Town)
- **location_secondary:** Ridge overlooking the river; north bank of the Trident; camp beneath an oak tree; Lord Harroway's Town (flooded)
- **approximate_timeline:** During the journey after Arya's capture by the Hound; they have been riding for multiple days in rain and darkness; the Hound plans to reach the kingsroad the next day and ride to the Twins in time for Edmure's wedding
- **time_markers:** "It could not have been much past noon, but the sky was dark as dusk"; "not seen the sun in more days than she could count"; "before dark" (ferryman's estimate for crossing); "by the time the rain stopped and the clouds broke" (evening, makes camp); "On the morrow we should reach the kingsroad"; first day warnings from Hound referenced in flashback; "that first day" and "the first time they made camp" in flashback
- **chapter_summary:** Arya and the Hound reach a flooded river she believes is the Blackwater Rush. They ride downstream to Lord Harroway's Town, which is mostly submerged, and negotiate ferry passage across. During the dangerous crossing, a massive uprooted tree nearly capsizes the ferry and one boatman is swept away and drowned. On the north bank, the Hound pays the ferryman with a worthless parchment note instead of gold. They make camp and eat a cold supper, during which the Hound reveals he is not taking Arya to King's Landing but to the Twins to ransom her to her mother and brother at Edmure's wedding.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya and the Hound reach the ridge** — They crest a ridge and see the flooded river below; Clegane reins up and curses at the sight.
2. **Arya recalls the Hound's warnings (flashback)** — On their first day, Clegane warned her against hitting, running, screaming, or biting, threatening to bind and gag her.
3. **Arya recalls her failed attack (flashback)** — The first time they camped, she crept toward him with a jagged rock to smash his head, but he caught her; she screamed about Mycah and he threatened her.
4. **Arya recalls being bound at night (flashback)** — After the rock incident, the Hound began rolling her in a horse blanket and tying ropes around her every night.
5. **Arya speculates on their destination** — She believes they are heading to King's Landing and the Blackwater Rush; she wishes the sun would come out to confirm direction.
6. **Arya recalls escape attempts** — She scratched her name on tree trunks when relieving herself to help pursuers track them, but the Hound caught her on the fourth time.
7. **The Hound mentions Harroway town and the ferry** — He says Lord Roote stables "Old King Andahar's two-headed water horse" there.
8. **They ride downstream along the ridge** — They follow the river for hours, splashing across two muddy tributary streams.
9. **They reach Lord Harroway's Town** — The town is flooded and desolate; only upper portions of buildings remain above water; smoke rises from the stone roundtower.
10. **The Hound hails the ferrymen** — A man with a crossbow appears in the roundtower window; two men come out of the boat's deckhouse; the bent-backed man negotiates.
11. **Negotiation over fare** — The ferryman demands three gold dragons; the Hound threatens with his sword; they agree the Hound will pay on the north bank; the ferryman accepts "knight's honor."
12. **Boarding the ferry** — The ferry crew (approximately twelve men) pole and row to shore; Stranger balks at the water but is urged aboard; a boatman nearly gets kicked by Stranger.
13. **The ferryman mistakes Arya for the Hound's son** — Arya furiously denies being his son; Clegane grabs her by the collar and shakes her, ordering her into the cabin.
14. **Arya enters the heated cabin briefly** — She warms herself by the red-glowing iron brazier then slips back out to watch the crossing.
15. **The ferry crosses through flooded Harroway** — They navigate between chimneys and rooftops; the crossbow man watches from the tower.
16. **The crossing grows dangerous** — Out in the main channel the current is powerful; the crew rows hard; wind and rain batter them; Stranger panics.
17. **Arya considers jumping overboard** — She remembers Jon saying she swam like a fish; thinks of Joffrey and creeps to the prow; puts a hand on the rail.
18. **An uprooted tree threatens the ferry** — A massive tree rushes at them like a battering ram; two boatmen catch it with poles; one pole snaps with a loud crack; the second pole deflects the tree just enough.
19. **A boatman is swept overboard and drowns** — The man with the broken pole stumbles over the side; the brown water closes over him; no one can throw a rope in time.
20. **Arya loses desire to escape by swimming** — Witnessing the drowning, she goes meekly inside when Clegane shouts at her.
21. **The ferry lands two miles downstream** — The boat slams into the north bank hard, snapping another pole.
22. **The Hound cheats the ferryman** — The ferryman demands six dragons (three for passage, three for the lost man); Clegane gives him a crumpled parchment — a dead man's note "good for nine thousand dragons or nearabouts" — and tells him ten is his share.
23. **The Hound declares "Knights have no bloody honor"** — When the ferryman protests the paper payment and invokes knight's honor, Clegane says knights have no honor and gallops off.
24. **Arya recites her death list** — She silently lists: Ser Ilyn, Ser Meryn, King Joffrey, Queen Cersei, Dunsen, Poliver, Raff the Sweetling, Ser Gregor, the Tickler, and the Hound (three times).
25. **The rain stops; they make camp** — Arya is shivering and sneezing badly; the Hound calls a halt; he tries to make a fire but the wood is too wet; he kicks it apart cursing "I hate fires."
26. **Cold supper and conversation** — They eat hardbread, moldy cheese, and smoked sausage under an oak tree; Arya eyes the dagger and the Hound warns her off.
27. **The Hound says he never beat Sansa** — He tells Arya he saved Sansa from the mob, cutting through them to bring her back; says Sansa sang for him.
28. **The Hound discusses his brother Gregor** — He says Gregor once killed a man for snoring; laughs that Gregor caught Arya without realizing who she had; says he'll tell Gregor that before cutting his heart out.
29. **The Hound guesses Arya wants to kill Sansa** — He reads something in Arya's face when asking about siblings; Arya denies it and says she'd like to kill him instead.
30. **The Hound reveals their true destination** — He tells Arya the river was the Trident, not the Blackwater; they are heading north to the Twins to ransom her to her mother and Robb; he plans to gain service or gold from the Young Wolf.
31. **The Hound mentions Edmure's wedding** — He tells Arya to keep quiet and obey, and "maybe we'll even be in time for your uncle's bloody wedding."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fearful but defiant; physically miserable (sick with fever, soaked, saddle-sore, shivering); angry at being the Hound's captive; briefly considers suicide by drowning but is dissuaded by witnessing the boatman's death; cycles through rage, helplessness, and grim determination; recites her death list as a coping mechanism
- **Primary preoccupation:** Escape — she constantly weighs options (the rock, stealing Stranger, jumping into the river, the dagger); also fixated on where they are going and why
- **Key decisions made:** Decides not to jump into the river after witnessing the boatman drown; continues to ride cooperatively rather than resist after being physically overwhelmed
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes throughout most of the chapter they are heading to King's Landing via the Blackwater, despite the Hound's evasiveness about their direction; tells herself "maybe he'll wash up someplace downstream" about the drowned boatman, immediately recognizes the thought as hollow
