- **chapter_summary:** Bran, Hodor, Meera, Jojen, and Summer travel north through the mountains toward the Wall, avoiding roads on Jojen's insistence. They shelter in a cave during freezing rain and meet a mountain man (likely a Liddle) who shares food and news — Bolton's bastard is alive and paying for wolfskins and news of "walking dead," flayed men ride the kingsroad, and the Night's Watch has gone silent after marching into the haunted woods. After parting, Bran tries unsuccessfully to warg into an eagle. The group discusses Old Nan and Hodor's real name (Walder), and Meera tells the story of the Knight of the Laughing Tree — a crannogman who visited the Isle of Faces, attended a great tourney at Harrenhal, was rescued from bullying squires by a she-wolf, and may have been the mystery knight who defeated the squires' masters.

## Events & Actions
1. **Mountain travel** — Bran's party travels through mountain valleys north of the wolfswood, navigating by the Ice Dragon constellation on clear nights. No roads exist in this terrain.
2. **Bran reflects on Osha and Rickon** — He imagines them safe in White Harbor with Lord Manderly or at Last Hearth with the Greatjon.
3. **Meera and Bran banter** — Meera sighs about hating the mountains, then says she loves them. Jojen says "if ice can burn, then love and hate can mate."
4. **Jojen insists on avoiding roads** — He argues travelers have eyes and mouths to spread tales of "the crippled boy, his giant, and the wolf that walks beside them."
5. **Difficult travel conditions** — Rain, wind, and a fierce sleet storm batter them; game grows scarce in the mountains; some nights they sleep hungry.
6. **Bran describes the mountain clans** — He names the Umbers, Wulls, Harclays, Knotts, Liddles, Norreys, and Flints. His father's mother's mother was a Flint of the mountains.
7. **The Reeds recall Theo Wull** — Meera asks if a Wull rode with their father during the war; Jojen confirms Theo Wull, called "Buckets."
8. **Bran reveals he knows they're being watched** — He has seen the mountain folk through Summer's eyes but says they won't be bothered.
9. **They shelter in a cave with the Liddle** — Summer sniffs out a cave during freezing rain; inside, a man offers them warmth, oatcakes, blood sausage, and ale. Bran identifies him as a Liddle from his gold and bronze pinecone clasp.
10. **The Liddle shares news** — Bolton's bastard lives ("He was dead, but now he's not") and pays silver for wolfskins and gold for word of "certain other walking dead." Flayed men ride the kingsroad; squids are in the wolfswood.
11. **The Liddle warns about the Wall** — The Old Bear took the Watch into the haunted woods; only ravens returned with hardly any messages. "Dark wings, dark words... but when the birds fly silent, seems to me that's even darker."
12. **The Liddle laments the fall of the Starks** — "It was different when there was a Stark in Winterfell. But the old wolf's dead and young one's gone south to play the game of thrones, and all that's left us is the ghosts."
13. **Jojen prophesies** — "The wolves will come again," he says solemnly. When challenged, he says "I dreamed it."
14. **Night in the cave; Bran wargs Summer** — As the fire burns to embers, Bran releases Summer into the night. He closes his eyes and gives himself to the wolf dream — moonlight, silver woods, owls, pale goats on mountainsides.
15. **The Liddle departs before dawn** — He leaves a sausage and a dozen oatcakes (some with pinenuts, some with blackberries) folded in green and white cloth.
16. **Bran vows to repay the Liddles** — He tells himself that when there are Starks in Winterfell again, he'll repay them a hundredfold.
17. **Easier travel; Bran dozes** — The trail improves; sun breaks through by noon. Bran falls asleep to Hodor's stride and humming.
18. **Bran spots an eagle and tries to warg into it** — Meera points out an eagle with grey wings. Bran tries to leave his body and join it as he does with Summer, thinking the greenseers could do it. He fails; the eagle vanishes.
19. **Discussion of Hodor's real name** — Bran reveals Hodor's real name is Walder, as Old Nan told him. Old Nan was Hodor's grandmother's grandmother or something.
20. **Bran worries about Old Nan** — He asks if the ironmen killed her. They didn't see her body or any women dead at Winterfell. Meera says too many of the dead were ironmen, suggesting it wasn't all Theon's doing.
21. **Meera advises Bran to remember Old Nan's stories** — "So long as you do that, part of her will always be alive in you."
22. **Bran asks the Reeds for a story** — He requests stories about knights. Jojen says there are no knights in the Neck. Meera says the bogs are full of dead ones — Andals, ironmen, Freys who tried to conquer Greywater.
23. **Meera begins the Knight of the Laughing Tree story** — Set in "the year of the false spring," about a curious crannogman lad who grew up learning the magics of his people.
24. **The crannogman visits the Isle of Faces** — He travels down the Green Fork, passes beneath the Twins by night, walks to the Gods Eye carrying his boat on his head, and paddles to the Isle of Faces. He meets the green men (details withheld by Meera).
25. **The crannogman arrives at Harrenhal** — He sees the great tourney with tents, banners, and knights. The king is present with his son the dragon prince. The White Swords welcome a new brother. The storm lord, rose lord, and many others attend. The great lion of the rock stays away after quarreling with the king.
26. **Three squires attack the crannogman** — They are no older than fifteen, bigger than him, snatch his spear, knock him down, kick him, and curse him as a "frogeater."
27. **The she-wolf rescues the crannogman** — She scatters the squires with a tourney sword, howling "That's my father's man you're kicking." She takes him to her lair and tends his wounds.
28. **The crannogman meets the wolf pack** — The she-wolf, the wild wolf who led them, the quiet wolf, and the pup who was youngest of the four.
29. **The Harrenhal feast** — The crannogman eats and drinks with the wolves and their sworn swords (barrowdown men, moose, bears, mermen). The dragon prince sings a sad song. The she-wolf cries and the pup teases her, so she pours wine on his head. A black brother recruits for the Night's Watch. The storm lord wins a wine-cup war against the knight of skulls and kisses. A maid with laughing purple eyes dances with a white sword, a red snake, the lord of griffins, and lastly the quiet wolf — but only after the wild wolf spoke to her on the quiet wolf's behalf.
30. **The crannogman spots his attackers' masters** — The three squires serve a pitchfork knight, a porcupine knight, and a knight with two towers on his surcoat (identified as Freys by Bran). The pup offers the crannogman a horse and armor, but the crannogman gives no answer.
31. **The crannogman prays** — Before sleeping, he kneels on the lakeshore looking toward the Isle of Faces and prays to the old gods of north and Neck.
32. **The mystery knight appears** — On the second day's afternoon, a short knight in ill-fitting mismatched armor appears, bearing a shield with a white weirwood with a laughing red face (heart tree device). The Knight of the Laughing Tree defeats the porcupine knight, pitchfork knight, and knight of the two towers. He demands in a booming voice: "Teach your squires honor, that shall be ransom enough."
33. **The king's hostility** — The storm lord and knight of skulls and kisses swear to unmask the mystery knight. The king declares the face behind the helm is no friend of his and urges men to challenge him.
34. **The mystery knight vanishes** — The next morning only two champions appear. The king sends the dragon prince to seek the knight, but only the painted shield is found hanging abandoned in a tree. The dragon prince wins the tourney.
35. **Bran critiques the story** — He wishes the mystery knight had defeated the bad knights directly and won the tourney to name the wolf maid queen of love and beauty.
36. **Meera's cryptic reply** — "She was [queen of love and beauty], but that's a sadder story."
37. **Jojen probes Bran** — He asks twice if Bran is certain his lord father never told him this story. Bran says no.
38. **Bran dreams of the Isle of Faces** — He thinks if the crannogman could visit, maybe he could too, and the green men might help him walk again or make him a knight, even if only for a day.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Wistful and nostalgic — missing Winterfell, Old Nan, Osha, Rickon; worried about Old Nan's fate; impatient about the slow journey; wondrous during the warging dream and when hearing the story; determined to reach the three-eyed crow; briefly frustrated at failing to warg into the eagle; hopeful at chapter's end (dreaming of the Isle of Faces healing him)
- **Primary preoccupation:** Reaching the three-eyed crow to learn to fly; wanting to be a knight or walk again; missing his home and family
- **Key decisions made:** None significant — Bran follows Jojen's lead on routing. He mentally vows to repay the Liddles when Starks return to Winterfell. He asks for a story to pass the time.
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran tells himself Rickon and Osha are "safe" in White Harbor or Last Hearth — a comforting fantasy with no basis. He asks "Theon wouldn't hurt someone like that. Would he?" — clinging to a belief he already doubts. He imagines a future where he is lord and can repay debts, despite his current situation as a fugitive presumed dead. He thinks the green men might "help him walk again, even turn him into a knight" — yearning overriding realism.
