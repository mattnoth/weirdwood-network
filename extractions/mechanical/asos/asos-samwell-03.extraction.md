# ASOS — Samwell III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 47

- **location_primary:** An unnamed wildling village (Sam hopes it is Whitetree but concludes it is not)
- **location_secondary:** Whitetree (referenced/compared), Castle Black (discussed), Horn Hill (recalled), Craster's Keep (referenced), the Frostfangs (referenced), Eastwatch (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after fleeing Craster's Keep; the party has been traveling south for some time (second horse died three days out from Craster's Keep, travel has continued well beyond that)
- **time_markers:** "Night was coming on" at arrival; dusk deepening during Sam's prayer; nighttime during the wight attack; "break of day" referenced as next morning; the second horse died "three days out from Craster's Keep"; travel has been ongoing for what feels like "half his life"; "another few days" estimated to reach the Wall; "a day or a fortnight" Sam estimates to reach the Wall
- **chapter_summary:** Sam and Gilly arrive at a wildling village Sam hopes is Whitetree but realizes is not. They shelter in the longhall, share their last food (black sausages), and Sam sings "The Song of the Seven" to Gilly's baby. During the night, the wight of Small Paul attacks; Sam's dragonglass dagger shatters on Paul's iron mail and his steel knife fails, but Sam kills the wight by forcing a burning ember into its mouth. Outside, a score of wights surround Gilly, but hundreds of ravens descend from the weirwood to attack them. A mysterious rider dressed in black, mounted on a great elk, rescues Sam and Gilly—though his ungloved hand is black, cold, and hard as stone.

- **Time of day:** Arrival at dusk ("Night was coming on," "The dusk was deepening"); nighttime during the attack; transitions from evening to deep night
- **Lighting:** Dim firelight inside the longhall; fire burned down to smouldering red embers before the attack; starlight outside; moonlight mentioned but obscured by ravens at the end
- **Sounds:** Leaves rattling in the wind; horse whinnying and kicking; raven shrieking; weirwood leaves rustling and "whispering to one another in a tongue he did not know"; trees groaning and creaking; thousands of ravens shrieking; the rider's shout ("Brother!")
- **Smells:** "Old smells" in the hovels; Gilly smells of "milk and garlic and musty old fur"; garlic from the sausages
- **Notable sensory details:** Sam's blisters have burst and turned to callus with new blisters beneath; fingers so cold they hurt when warmed by the fire; the wight's fingers "so cold they seemed to burn" and "burrowed deep into the soft flesh of Sam's throat"; Sam's throat felt frozen, lungs on fire; taste of blood in his mouth; the burning ember he grabs with bare hands; "puffs of frost exploding from his mouth" while running; the cold air "hurt to breathe, but such a fine sweet hurt"

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at wildling village** — Sam and Gilly arrive at a wildling village Sam hopes is Whitetree but cannot confirm.
2. **Sam searches the hovels** — He finds nothing but old straw, old smells, ashes, and rats in one corner.
3. **Sam examines the weirwood** — He concludes the tree is not the same as Whitetree's; it is smaller and the face is different.
4. **Sam prays to the old gods** — He kneels before the weirwood and prays for help, admitting he doesn't know what gods he believes in.
5. **Gilly makes a fire in the longhall** — She starts a fire while Sam searches.
6. **Sam and Gilly discuss the journey** — Sam estimates another few days to the Wall; he admits uncertainty about their exact position east-west.
7. **Sam describes Castle Black to Gilly** — He promises warmth, food, drink, and a fire she can stand by.
8. **Sam sings "The Song of the Seven"** — He sings six of the seven verses (omitting the Stranger) to lull Gilly's baby to sleep.
9. **Sam recalls Lord Randyll's cruelty** — He remembers his father forbidding him from singing to baby Dickon.
10. **Discussion of the Stranger** — Gilly notices only six gods were sung about; Sam explains no one sings of the Stranger.
11. **They eat their last food** — Sam saws thin slices of black sausage for each of them.
12. **Sam tends the horse** — He goes outside, breaks ice on the stream for the horse to drink, decides to bring her inside.
13. **Sam leads the horse into the longhall** — With difficulty, he gets the garron through the door and hobbles her in a corner.
14. **Sam and Gilly sleep** — Sam lies under his cloak with Gilly and the baby; reflects on his past.
15. **Sam has a wish-fulfillment dream** — He dreams of feasting his brothers at Horn Hill with no father present; Gilly waits for him in bed.
16. **Sam wakes to extreme cold** — The fire is embers; the air seems frozen; the horse is panicking.
17. **Small Paul's wight enters the longhall** — The reanimated corpse of Small Paul shambles in; his hands are black, face white, eyes glowing blue; a raven pecks at his cheek.
18. **Sam confronts the wight** — He draws the dragonglass dagger and tries to lead Paul away from Gilly while she escapes with the horse.
19. **Sam stabs Paul with dragonglass** — He plunges the dagger into Paul's back, but it shatters on the iron mail beneath his cloak.
20. **Small Paul strangles Sam** — The wight's cold hands close around Sam's throat; Sam's steel knife also fails against the mail.
21. **Sam kills the wight with fire** — He tackles Paul to the ground, grabs a smouldering ember, and smashes it into Paul's mouth; the wight's face bursts into flame and the blue glow leaves his eyes.
22. **Sam exits to find Gilly surrounded** — A score or more of wights surround Gilly at the weirwood; they have torn the horse apart.
23. **Ravens attack the wights** — Hundreds or thousands of ravens descend from the weirwood's branches and attack the wights, pecking at their eyes and flesh.
24. **A speaking raven lands on Sam's shoulder** — It says "Fair, far, fear" and then "Go, go, go."
25. **The mysterious rider appears** — A figure in mottled blacks and greys, mounted on a great elk ten feet tall, calls out "Brother!" and "Here."
26. **Sam and Gilly mount the elk** — The rider pulls Gilly up, then Sam; Sam grasps the rider's hand and finds it black, cold, and hard as stone—ungloved.
