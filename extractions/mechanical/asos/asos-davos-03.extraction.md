- **approximate_timeline:** Some time after the Battle of the Blackwater; Davos has been imprisoned long enough to recover from serious illness
- **time_markers:** Recovery period from illness (unspecified duration); passage of days marked by gaoler visits ("Porridge had come thrice, and Lamprey twice" = approximately three days before Florent's arrival); "one night" when Melisandre visits; "three days later" when Florent arrives; no sun/moon visible in dungeons; torch changes mark time
- **chapter_summary:** Davos recovers from illness in a warm cell beneath Dragonstone, tended by Maester Pylos and two unnamed gaolers he nicknames Porridge and Lamprey. Melisandre visits him, attempting to win him to R'hllor's service, revealing she saw his murder plot in her flames, and declaring Stannis is Azor Ahai reborn. Three days after her visit, Lord Alester Florent is thrown into Davos's cell, accused of treason for secretly writing to Lord Tywin Lannister proposing peace terms—including Stannis renouncing his claim and wedding Shireen to Tommen—without the king's approval.

## Events & Actions
1. **Davos recovers from illness** — After being brought to his cell sick with fever, coughing, and blood blisters, Maester Pylos treats him with garlic broth, milk of the poppy, and leeching until he regains strength.
2. **Davos adapts to imprisonment** — He marks time by gaoler visits and torch changes, nicknames his gaolers Porridge and Lamprey, and attempts unsuccessfully to get them to speak.
3. **Davos reflects on previous prisoners** — He recalls that Lord Sunglass and Ser Hubard Rambton's sons were imprisoned here before being burned, and considers he may face the same fate.
4. **Melisandre visits Davos's cell** — She appears in scarlet, threatens to extinguish his torch, then attempts to convert him to R'hllor's service, offering sexual intimacy to create another shadow creature.
5. **Davos refuses Melisandre** — He calls her "the mother of darkness" and rejects both her offer and her god, invoking the Seven.
6. **Melisandre reveals she saw Davos's murder plot in her flames** — She tells him no one betrayed him; she foresaw his purpose through fire.
7. **Melisandre declares Stannis is Azor Ahai reborn** — She recites the prophecy about the red star bleeding and Azor Ahai being born amidst smoke and salt, claiming Dragonstone fulfills this.
8. **Melisandre departs** — She leaves the torch burning and exits, leaving Davos to contemplate her words.
9. **Davos attempts to see visions in the flames** — He stares at the torch trying to see what Melisandre sees, but finds nothing.
10. **Lord Alester Florent is dragged to the cell** — Three days after Melisandre's visit, guards with fiery hearts on their breasts drag a protesting Alester down, escorted by Ser Axell Florent.
11. **Alester is thrown into Davos's cell** — Ser Axell orders him placed with "the traitors"; Alester protests he is the King's Hand and no traitor.
12. **Alester reveals the military situation** — He tells Davos that Celtigar bent the knee, Velaryon died, Sunglass was burned, Bar Emmon is useless, and only House Florent's strength remains to Stannis.
13. **Alester confesses his treason** — He wrote to Lord Tywin proposing peace terms: Stannis gives up his claim, retracts claims of Joffrey's bastardy, in exchange for being confirmed as Lord of Dragonstone and Storm's End, with Shireen wed to Tommen.
14. **Davos rebukes Alester** — He tells Florent it is not in Stannis to yield a just claim, and that Stannis would sooner see Shireen dead than wed to a product of incest.
15. **Alester weeps** — After Davos tells him Stannis can "choose to die a king," Alester stares helplessly and begins to weep.

- **Self-deception flags:** None apparent — Davos is notably honest with himself and others throughout; Melisandre even praises his honesty
