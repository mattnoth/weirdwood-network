- **chapter_summary:** Arya travels south with the Brotherhood Without Banners, growing suspicious they are heading the wrong way. She recalls Hot Pie's departure to stay at the inn and bake bread. Harwin tells her about the original mission to stop Gregor Clegane, the ambush at the Mummer's Ford, and how the survivors became the Brotherhood. At a burned village, the outlaws learn the Kingslayer has escaped from Riverrun. The next morning Arya confirms from the sun they are going south, not toward Riverrun, and the outlaws reveal they are taking her to Lord Beric Dondarrion. She attempts to flee on horseback but Harwin catches her, and she submits to being their captive.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya notices moss growing on wrong side of trees** — On the third morning of travel, Arya realizes they are heading south, not toward Riverrun. She tells Gendry, who dismisses her concerns.
2. **Arya recalls Hot Pie's departure** — She remembers Hot Pie staying behind at the inn to bake bread for Sharna. He asked if she'd come back when the war was done; she punched his shoulder when he tried to kiss her hand.
3. **Arya shares stories with Harwin** — She tells Harwin about his father Hullen dying in the Red Keep stables, about Yoren and their escape from King's Landing, and much of what happened since — but omits the stableboy she stabbed and the guard whose throat she cut at Harrenhal.
4. **Arya recites her kill list** — At night she takes out Jaqen's iron coin, says "Valar morghulis," and recites: Ser Gregor, Dunsen, Polliver, Raff the Sweetling, the Tickler, the Hound, Ser Ilyn, Ser Meryn, Queen Cersei, King Joffrey.
5. **Harwin recounts the mission to the Mummer's Ford** — He describes how Lord Eddard sent Beric Dondarrion west with forty men to stop Gregor Clegane, how Gregor ambushed them at the Mummer's Ford from both banks, killing Raymun Darry, Gladden Wylde, and Lord Mallery, and wounding Beric gravely.
6. **Harwin describes the Brotherhood's formation** — After Robert's and Eddard's deaths made them outlaws, Beric refused to yield. They fought for the smallfolk, and for every man lost, two volunteers replaced him — common men, women, children, even dogs.
7. **The group shelters in a burned village** — Jack-Be-Lucky signals with his hunting horn and hidden villagers emerge from cellars. They share food and ale; Anguy's goose makes it almost a feast.
8. **Villagers report the Kingslayer has escaped from Riverrun** — The outlaws react with surprise and concern. Greenbeard says Thoros must be told and the Lord of Light will show Lannister in the flames. Lem says Lord Beric would love to capture Jaime.
9. **Tom sings "The Brothers of the Kingswood"** — Tom Sevenstrings plays his woodharp and sings about the Kingswood Brotherhood, an outlaw band.
10. **Arya dreams of Winterfell** — She dreams of being outside Winterfell, up to her knees in mud, unable to reach the gates as the castle fades like smoke. Gaunt grey wolves stalk through the trees with shining eyes, and she remembers the taste of blood.
11. **Arya confirms they are going south** — The next morning, using the sun's position and her map, she confronts the outlaws. They reveal they are not taking her to Riverrun but to Lord Beric Dondarrion.
12. **Greenbeard compares Arya to a "gold squirrel"** — He tells her a peasant who finds a gold squirrel takes it to his lord; they are bringing her to the lightning lord.
13. **Arya attempts to escape on horseback** — She bolts between Greenbeard and Jack-Be-Lucky, galloping across fields and through woods of elm, yew, and birch, jumping ditches and streams.
14. **Harwin catches Arya** — He overtakes her on horseback and grabs her bridle. He compares her riding to her aunt Lyanna's, noting his father was master of horse.
15. **Harwin explains his loyalty** — He tells Arya that Lord Eddard is dead and he now belongs to the lightning lord and his brothers. The Brotherhood fights for the smallfolk, not for Robb. Arya is their captive.
16. **Arya agrees to ride back peacefully** — She submits but adds "For now" internally, and reflects she was better off as Squab, Nan, Weasel, or Arry.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious and suspicious (sensing they are going the wrong way); nostalgic and sad about Hot Pie's departure; guarded (withholding dark secrets from Harwin); ritualistic and determined (reciting her kill list nightly); fearful and excited (news of the Kingslayer's escape makes her hair prickle); desperate and defiant (attempting escape); hurt and betrayed (when Harwin catches her and declares loyalty to Beric over her family); resigned but defiant ("For now")
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting to Riverrun and her mother; maintaining her identity and autonomy against the outlaws who have made her a captive
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to attempt escape when she learns the outlaws won't take her to Riverrun; after being caught, agrees to ride peacefully but internally resolves this is temporary compliance
- **Self-deception flags:** Arya tells herself she was "better off as Squab" or her other disguise identities, clinging to the idea that anonymity equals safety — yet she earlier used her real identity to try to negotiate ("Take me to Riverrun and you'll be rewarded"). She wants to be a wolf but laments being "just some stupid little lady again."
