Stannis reveals his plan to settle wildlings on the Gift and offers to legitimize Jon as Jon Stark, Lord of Winterfell, on the conditions that Jon pledge fealty, accept R'hllor, burn the weirwoods, and wed Val. Jon asks for time to consider, torn between his childhood dream of Winterfell and his Night's Watch vows.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon trains Satin in swordplay** — Jon circles Satin in the Castle Black yard, coaching him on shield use and putting body weight behind strikes.
2. **Melisandre arrives with queen's men** — The yard goes quiet as Melisandre appears with half a dozen queen's men and summons Jon to speak with the king.
3. **Jon prepares to meet the king** — He stores his armor in the armory, changes into fresh blacks in his cell, dons a heavy hooded cloak, and takes Longclaw.
4. **Jon and Melisandre ride the cage to the Wall top** — Melisandre has dismissed her queen's men. In the cage, Jon notices her scent (smoke and blood, like hot iron), her warmth, and her ruby. She places her hand on his cheek to demonstrate her warmth.
5. **Jon meets Stannis atop the Wall** — Stannis stands alone brooding over the battlefield and the northern forest. Melisandre introduces Jon as "the Bastard of Winterfell." Jon kneels; Stannis tells him to rise.
6. **Stannis questions Jon about his past** — Jon admits to riding with wildlings, killing Qhorin Halfhand on his orders, and breaking vows with Ygritte. Stannis says he believes Jon, citing knowledge of Janos Slynt and Ned Stark's honor.
7. **Stannis acknowledges Jon's contributions** — Stannis knows Jon found the dragonglass, held the gate (Jon corrects: Donal Noye held it), brought the magic horn, and captured Val and the babe.
8. **Discussion of wildling leaders** — Stannis asks Jon about honor among the wildlings, Mance Rayder, Rattleshirt, and Tormund Giantsbane. Jon gives candid assessments.
9. **Jon advocates for Val visiting Mance** — Jon asks that Val be allowed to bring Mance's son to see him. Stannis refuses, noting Mance is a deserter his brothers insist must die.
10. **Stannis reveals why he came to the Wall** — He credits Davos Seaworth for reminding him of duty over rights: "I was trying to win the throne to save the kingdom, when I should have been trying to save the kingdom to win the throne."
11. **Stannis reveals the political situation in the North** — Roose Bolton is Warden of the North (reward from Tywin for betraying Robb); ironmen hold Moat Cailin, Deepwood Motte, Torrhen's Square, and the Stony Shore; Balon Greyjoy is dead and ironmen fight amongst themselves.
12. **Stannis offers to legitimize Jon as Lord of Winterfell** — He needs a son of Eddard Stark to rally the northern lords. Jon is stunned.
13. **Melisandre states the conditions** — Jon must accept R'hllor as his god and burn the weirwoods at Winterfell. A king can remove the taint of bastardy.
14. **Jon wrestles internally with the offer** — He recalls childhood dreams of Winterfell, the succession (Robb, Bran, Rickon, Sansa, Arya), and recognizes accepting means forswearing his vows truly and turning against his father's gods.
15. **Stannis reveals his plan for the wildlings** — He intends to allow wildlings through the Wall if they swear fealty, keep the king's peace, and accept R'hllor — including giants. He will settle them on the Gift. Mance will burn for his stubbornness.
16. **Jon agrees the alliance is sound** — He recalls his father and Benjen discussed resettling the Gift, though not with wildlings.
17. **Stannis proposes marriage between Jon and Val** — To seal the alliance. Jon laughs, warning that wildling women must be stolen, not given.
18. **Jon asks for time to consider** — Stannis grants it but warns to decide quickly and tell no one. The final terms: bend the knee, lay sword at Stannis's feet, and rise as Jon Stark.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Stunned and conflicted. Jon is caught between his lifelong dream of Winterfell and his Night's Watch vows. He feels light-headed when the offer is made, "half afraid the wind would blow him off the Wall." He is saddened by Dalla's death. He feels the word "Lord Snow" suddenly sounds "real" rather than mocking. He loved Robb and all his siblings and never wished harm on them.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The legitimization offer — becoming Jon Stark, Lord of Winterfell. The weight of forswearing his vows truly this time (not as a ruse) and turning against his father's gods.
- **Key decisions made:** Asks for time to consider rather than accepting or refusing immediately. Agrees that wildling alliance against the common enemy is sound.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon says "I scarcely know her" about Val but admits she is "very" comely; his internal reaction to the marriage proposal (laughing, knowing wildling customs) suggests more comfort with the idea than he lets on. He tells himself "I never wanted this" about Winterfell, but the text reveals he did dream of it as a young child and was ashamed of those dreams.
