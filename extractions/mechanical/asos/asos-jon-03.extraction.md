# ASOS — Jon III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 27
- **pov_character:** Jon Snow
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon III
- **first_available:** ASOS Jon III
- **location_primary:** Cave in the hills south of the Wall (between Shadow Tower and Castle Black)
- **location_secondary:** Hilltop above the cave; the Wall (visible to the south); Grotto with waterfall and pool (deep within the cave)
- **approximate_timeline:** Night before the wildling party climbs the Wall; the group has been riding south for days through narrow valleys
- **time_markers:** "The last night" before going over the Wall; nighttime throughout; black and moonless sky but clear stars; torch burning down to nothing by chapter's end; "on the morrow we go over"; references to multiple prior nights with Ygritte ("half a hundred times by now"); "the first time, when she slipped beneath his sleeping skins"; Ygritte states she is nineteen; the boy she bedded was "five years past"
- **chapter_summary:** On the last night before the wildlings climb the Wall, Jon Snow ascends a hill and sends Ghost away toward Castle Black, hoping the direwolf can serve as a warning. The Magnar of Thenn, Styr, summons Jon to interrogate him about Night's Watch patrol patterns, numbers, and manned castles; Jon provides mostly truthful information but inflates the garrison numbers. Jon then finds Ygritte in a hidden grotto deep within the cave, where she tells the legend of Gorne and Gendel. They make love repeatedly, and Ygritte says she never wants to leave the cave.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon goes up the hill to find Ghost** — He tells the Thenns at the cave mouth he is looking for Ghost, and they let him pass. He reflects on the stars and constellations, recalling different names the wildlings give them.
2. **Jon and Ygritte discuss the stars** — Ygritte insists the Thief was bright the night Jon "stole" her. Jon denies meaning to steal her. Ygritte compares his stubbornness to... something Jon finds unmatched except by Arya.
3. **Jon finds Ghost atop the hill** — Ghost is sitting on his hindquarters, red eyes drinking the stars. Jon scratches his neck fur. Ghost licks Jon's face, rasping against the eagle-talon scabs.
4. **Jon sends Ghost away toward Castle Black** — He tells Ghost to follow the Wall east to Castle Black, hoping Ghost's arrival will serve as a warning. He had considered writing a message but has no ink, parchment, or quill.
5. **Ghost bounds away into the night** — The direwolf twists free, ears pricked, and races down the hillside. Jon is uncertain whether Ghost understood or is just chasing prey.
6. **Jon reflects on the Wall and his situation** — He sees the Wall looming to the south and thinks about his oaths, his failure to kill Mance at the Fist, and his entanglement with Ygritte.
7. **Jon recalls his sexual relationship with Ygritte beginning** — He recounts the first time she slipped beneath his sleeping skins and how he justified it as playing a part, but it happened repeatedly.
8. **A Thenn summons Jon to the Magnar** — An unnamed broad Thenn in a bronze helm comes up the hill and tells Jon the Magnar wants him.
9. **Styr interrogates Jon about Night's Watch patrols** — Jon describes patrol composition (four men: two rangers, two builders), mule usage, patrol frequency under Qorgyle and Mormont, and variation tactics. The Magnar notes the tale of Arson Iceaxe.
10. **Jon inflates Night's Watch garrison numbers** — He claims 500 at Castle Black, 200 at Shadow Tower, 300 at Eastwatch, adding 300 extra men to the true count.
11. **Jarl catches Jon's lie** — Jarl tells Styr that Jon is lying or including the men lost at the Fist. Styr warns Jon he will take his tongue if Jon lies.
12. **Jon confirms only three castles are manned** — He states Eastwatch, Castle Black, and Shadow Tower are manned; he cannot speak to changes since he left. Jarl's question about Stonedoor and Greyguard tells Jon their approximate position.
13. **Jon reflects on Styr and the Thenns** — He thinks about how Styr commands absolute obedience unlike other wildling leaders, and how Thenn is surrounded by dangerous peoples.
14. **Jon seeks Ygritte in the cave** — Grigg the Goat tells him she took a torch and went deeper into the cavern.
15. **Jon finds Ygritte in the grotto** — He crawls through a narrow passage and finds her beside a waterfall and dark pool, lit by torchlight.
16. **Ygritte tells the legend of Gorne and Gendel** — She explains that the caves connect and there is a way under the Wall (Gorne's Way). Jon and Ygritte jointly recount the tale: Gorne slew the King in the North but was killed by his son; Gendel escaped but got lost in the caves forever. Ygritte says Gendel's children still wander the caves, hungry.
17. **Ygritte undresses and invites Jon to look at her** — She strips naked in the torchlight and insists Jon do the same.
18. **Jon and Ygritte make love in the grotto** — Jon declares his desire and love for her physical features; they have sex. It is the first time they see each other's bodies fully rather than under furs.
19. **Ygritte and Jon discuss her past** — She reveals she was first bedded by a fire-kissed boy at a feast five years ago; Longspear broke his arm when he tried to steal her and he never came back.
20. **Ygritte and Jon discuss Craster** — Jon points out Craster weds his daughters; Ygritte says Craster's father was a Night's Watch man who stole a woman from Whitetree village and abandoned her; Craster's blood is "black" and he bears a "heavy curse."
21. **Ygritte recalls how Jon "stole" her** — She recounts how he jumped down the mountain, killed Orell, and held a knife to her throat, and how she expected him to take her then.
22. **Jon and Ygritte make love again** — The torch burns out; they continue in the dark. Ygritte asks Jon to perform oral sex again ("the lord's kiss") and offers reciprocation.
23. **Ygritte falls into the pool** — In the pitch-dark, she stumbles into the cold water and screeches; Jon laughs and she pulls him in. They wrestle and make love again.
24. **Ygritte expresses her wish to stay** — She tells Jon she never wants to leave the cave, suggests joining Gendel's children rather than returning to Styr and Jarl.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply conflicted throughout — guilt over breaking his Night's Watch vows wars with his growing feelings for Ygritte. Guilt returns after each sexual encounter but grows "weaker than before." Joy and tenderness during their time in the grotto. Anxiety about the coming climb over the Wall. Loneliness — he feels he has lost both his Stark family and his Night's Watch brothers.
- **Primary preoccupation:** His identity crisis: he is neither truly a Stark (bastard, never had a place at Winterfell), nor a loyal brother of the Night's Watch (broken vows, sleeping with Ygritte), nor a genuine wildling. He wonders "What was he now?" and does not want to examine it.
- **Key decisions made:** Sends Ghost to Castle Black as a warning. Decides to provide mostly true intelligence to Styr but inflates garrison numbers by 300. Surrenders to his desire for Ygritte in the grotto, abandoning resistance ("all his vows and all his honor forgotten").
- **Self-deception flags:** Repeatedly tells himself sleeping with Ygritte is "playing a part" as Qhorin instructed — "I am playing the part the Halfhand told me to play." Tells himself "it need never happen again" after the first time, then it happens three more times that night and many more since. Compares his weakness to Eddard's alleged infidelity to normalize it. By the grotto scene, he's stopped pretending and acknowledges "I know I want you."
