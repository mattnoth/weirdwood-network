# ASOS — Tyrion VIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 61
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion VIII
- **first_available:** ASOS Tyrion VIII
- **location_primary:** Great Sept of Baelor; Red Keep throne room
- **location_secondary:** Red Keep castle yard, Kitchen Keep, Red Keep privy, broad marble plaza outside the sept
- **approximate_timeline:** Day of King Joffrey's wedding to Margaery Tyrell; after the fall of Robb Stark and Stannis Baratheon's defeat at the Blackwater
- **time_markers:** Sun had not yet touched the top of the castle wall when Tyrion dresses; evenfall still an hour away when the throne room fills; full night outside the tall windows during Galyeon's song; the feast begins in the afternoon and runs into the night
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion attends the wedding ceremony of King Joffrey and Margaery Tyrell at the Great Sept of Baelor, reflecting during the ceremony that Joffrey was behind the catspaw assassination attempt on Bran Stark. At the lavish seventy-seven-course feast in the throne room, Tyrion endures dwarf jousters mocking the War of the Five Kings, publicly humiliates Joffrey with a retort, and is then humiliated in turn when Joffrey pours wine over his head and forces him to serve as cupbearer. Shortly after cutting the great pie with Ser Ilyn Payne's greatsword, Joffrey begins choking and dies — apparently poisoned. Cersei accuses Tyrion and Sansa of murdering the king and orders the Kingsguard to arrest them both.

## Events & Actions
1. **Wedding ceremony at the Great Sept of Baelor** — Joffrey and Margaery exchange seven vows, seven blessings, seven promises. The wedding song is sung and the challenge goes unanswered.
2. **Tyrion's internal realization about the catspaw** — During the ceremony, Tyrion concludes Joffrey was behind the assassination attempt on Bran Stark at Winterfell, reasoning that Joffrey took a plain Valyrian steel dagger from Robert's weapons and hired a freerider to do the killing. Tyrion believes Joffrey boasted "I am no stranger to Valyrian steel" as confirmation.
3. **Exchange of cloaks** — Mace Tyrell removes Margaery's maiden cloak. Tommen hands Joffrey the bride's cloak. Joffrey drapes Margaery in crimson-and-gold and fastens it at her throat.
4. **The kiss and declaration** — Joffrey and Margaery pledge love with a kiss. The High Septon declares them one flesh, one heart, one soul.
5. **Procession from the sept** — Ser Loras and Ser Meryn lead in white armor; Tommen scatters rose petals; the royal couple follows, then Cersei with Lord Tyrell, Lady Alerie with Lord Tywin, Lady Olenna with Ser Kevan, Ser Garlan and Lady Leonette, then Tyrion and Sansa.
6. **Crowds cheer outside the sept** — The mob loves Margaery for the bounty of Highgarden. Tyrion notes the irony that Mace Tyrell had closed the roseroad and caused the famine.
7. **Tyrion and Sansa exchange brief words** — Tyrion quips about escaping; Sansa calls the ceremony beautiful but looks sad.
8. **Congratulations on the plaza** — Joffrey and Margaery stand atop the steps surrounded by Kingsguard. Tyrion and Sansa wait in line, kiss Margaery's fingers, and move on.
9. **Litter ride to the Red Keep** — Tyrion suggests visiting Casterly Rock (Golden Gallery, Lion's Mouth, Hall of Heroes) and later Braavos. Sansa responds with empty courtesy both times.
10. **Tyrion relieves himself at a privy** — Describes the relief as near as good as a woman.
11. **Podrick brings wine; Tyrion observes the kitchens** — Tyrion drinks in his window seat, reflecting on the military situation: Gregor at the ruby ford and Harrenhal, Black Walder at Seagard, Tarly at Maidenpool and Duskendale, Daven Lannister and Prester at the Golden Tooth marching on Riverrun, Ryman Frey with two thousand spears from the Twins, Redwyne fleet to sail from the Arbor. The War of the Five Kings is nearly over.
12. **Tyrion dresses for the feast** — Shae arranges Sansa's hair with a silver net and dark purple gemstones. Shae asks to attend the feast; Tyrion refuses. Tyrion dons his new crimson velvet doublet.
13. **Tyrion and Sansa greet guests in the yard** — Sansa compliments Lord Gyles's cough, Elinor Tyrell's gown, and asks Jalabhar Xho about Summer Isles customs. Lancel appears, ghastly and thin, supported by Ser Kevan.
14. **Lady Olenna encounters Tyrion and Sansa** — Olenna compliments Sansa, fusses with her hair net (tucking loose strands, straightening it). Mentions she is leaving for Highgarden the day after next and invites Sansa. Sansa declines. Olenna needles Tyrion about dwarf's pennies and the master of coin position.
15. **Entry into the throne room** — Tyrion and Sansa are seated far to the king's right, beside Ser Garlan and Lady Leonette. A dozen others sit closer to Joffrey.
16. **Joffrey and Margaery ride in on white chargers** — Pages scatter rose petals. Both have changed clothes. Kingsguard escort them to the dais.
17. **Opening toast** — Joffrey fills the golden wedding chalice with Arbor red and toasts Margaery. The hall responds "Margaery! To the queen!"
18. **Feast courses and entertainments begin** — Seventy-seven courses served alongside singers, performers, and entertainments in succession.
19. **Hamish the Harper performs** — Debuts "Lord Renly's Ride" about Renly defying death to save the realm. Also plays "A Rose of Gold," "The Rains of Castamere," "Maiden, Mother, and Crone," and "My Lady Wife."
20. **Collio Quaynis performs** — Sings "The Dance of the Dragons" (in two voices), a ballad of lovers amid the Doom of Valyria (in High Valyrian, most guests cannot understand), "Bessa the Barmaid," and "The Rains of Castamere."
21. **Pyromancers conjure flame beasts** — Four master pyromancers create beasts of living flame that tear at each other.
22. **Galyeon of Cuy performs** — Sings the 77-verse "Song of the Blackwater" glorifying Joffrey and Cersei. Tyrion threatens to hang all singers. Ser Garlan tells Tyrion his deeds at the Blackwater were valiant and credits his chain, wildfire, and wildlings for the victory. Sansa blurts out "She never did that" about something in the song about Cersei.
23. **Incidents during the feast** — Grand Maester Pycelle falls asleep during Summer Isles dancers. One of Lord Rowan's knights stabs a Dornishman; gold cloaks drag both away, one to a cell and one to Maester Ballabar.
24. **Dwarf jousters enter** — Two dwarfs joust on a grey dog and a spotted sow in painted wooden armor, one in gold with a stag device and one in grey and white with a wolf device (mocking the War of the Five Kings). They perform a slapstick routine ending with one mounting the other obscenely.
25. **Joffrey challenges Tyrion to ride the pig** — The king calls on Tyrion to joust as "champion" and ride the pig.
26. **Tyrion's retort** — Standing on the table, Tyrion agrees to ride the pig only if Joffrey rides the dog, then delivers: "You're the only man in the hall that I'm certain of defeating!" A moment of shocked silence, then a gale of laughter. Joffrey looks blindly enraged. Tyrion blows Cersei a kiss.
27. **Ser Garlan warns Tyrion** — "My lord, beware. The king."
28. **Joffrey pours wine over Tyrion's head** — Wine soaks Tyrion's hair, stings his eyes, burns his wound, drenches his new doublet. Joffrey mocks him. Ser Garlan says "That was ill done." Tyrion diplomatically deflects by calling it an honor.
29. **Margaery and Olenna intervene** — Margaery calls Joffrey to return to his seat. Olenna sarcastically hopes for "The Rains of Castamere" again. They mention Ser Addam's toast and singer Alaric of Eysen.
30. **Joffrey forces Tyrion to be cupbearer** — Makes Tyrion pick up the chalice (kicks it away), fill it with wine, and kneel to present it. Joffrey drinks deep and sets the chalice on the table.
31. **The great pie arrives** — Two yards across, wheeled by six cooks; squeaks and thumpings come from inside. Joffrey draws Widow's Wail; Margaery restrains him ("Widow's Wail was not meant for slicing pies").
32. **Ser Ilyn Payne provides his greatsword** — Joffrey calls for Ser Ilyn's sword. Ilyn draws six feet of ornate silver with runes, ruby eyes, and a dragonglass skull. Tyrion notes it is not Valyrian steel. Sansa asks "What sword is that?" and "What has Ser Ilyn done with my father's sword?"
33. **The pie is cut** — Joffrey and Margaery swing the greatsword together. Doves burst out in a swirl of white feathers. A roar of delight; fiddlers and pipers play. Joffrey whirls Margaery around.
34. **Tyrion attempts to leave with Sansa** — Tells her she is deathly pale and suggests getting air and a fresh doublet.
35. **Joffrey forces Tyrion to serve again** — Refuses Tyrion leave; Tyrion climbs onto his chair to reach the chalice. Joffrey drinks and eats pigeon pie with his fist, calls it dry, and drinks again.
36. **Joffrey begins choking** — While demanding Tyrion ride the pig, Joffrey coughs, chokes, his face turns red. Wine spews out. The chalice drops. His face darkens.
37. **Attempts to save Joffrey** — Ser Garlan pounds Joffrey's back. Ser Osmund rips open his collar. Mace Tyrell shouts to turn him over. The High Septon prays. Pycelle calls for potions. Ser Meryn pries open Joffrey's mouth with a spoon. Joffrey claws bloody gouges in his own throat.
38. **Joffrey dies** — His eyes meet Tyrion's ("He has Jaime's eyes"). He lifts a hand toward Tyrion — reaching or pointing. A fearful thin sound stops. Tommen screams and cries.
39. **Tyrion pours out the remaining wine** — Finds the dropped chalice with a half-inch of deep purple wine. Considers it a moment, then pours it on the floor.
40. **Chaos in the hall** — Margaery weeps in Olenna's arms. Guests trample each other fleeing. A lone flutist plays a dirge. Ser Addam's gold cloaks try to restore order.
41. **Cersei cradles Joffrey's body** — Sits in a puddle of wine, gown torn and stained, face white as chalk. A thin black dog sniffs at the corpse. Lord Tywin says "The boy is gone" and tells her to let go. Two Kingsguard pry her fingers loose.
42. **Cersei accuses Tyrion and Sansa** — Declares "My son was poisoned." Commands Ser Loras: "Arrest my brother. He did this, the dwarf. Him and his little wife. They killed my son."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitter, uncomfortable, self-loathing, and increasingly drunk. Shifts between contempt for Joffrey, frustration with Sansa's coldness, self-pity about his appearance and status, and occasional warmth (gratitude toward Ser Garlan, admiration for the Fossoway couple's tenderness). During Joffrey's death, feels "curiously calm" amid the pandemonium. At the end, a mixture of dark humor ("My own wedding is looking much better in hindsight") and emerging dread.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The realization that Joffrey sent the catspaw to kill Bran — and that Joffrey now knows Tyrion knows ("My big mouth will be the death of me"). Secondary preoccupation: physical discomfort (full bladder throughout the ceremony and after), revulsion at the feast's excess, and alienation from Sansa.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to compliment the dwarf jousters and give them silver, then find and punish whoever arranged the entertainment. Decides to try to leave the feast with Sansa (thwarted by Joffrey). After the chalice falls, decides to pour out the remaining wine rather than preserve it as evidence. Decides to approach Cersei despite knowing he should leave.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself he wanted to reach through Sansa's "armor of courtesy" but his attempts at conversation are clumsy. Wonders if he spoke about Casterly Rock to connect with Sansa or to distract himself from his bladder. Internally asks "What a pathetic little man you are. Did you think babbling about the Lion's Mouth would make her smile?" Walks toward Cersei at the end despite knowing it's unwise: "I should leave. Now. Instead he waddled toward her."
