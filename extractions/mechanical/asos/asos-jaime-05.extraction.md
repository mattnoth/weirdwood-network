Later, Jaime and Brienne sup with Roose Bolton, who reveals Robb Stark has married Jeyne Westerling and that Sansa Stark has been wed to Tyrion. Bolton agrees to send Jaime to King's Landing under escort but announces he will keep Brienne at Harrenhal for Vargo Hoat.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jaime enters the bathhouse** — Led by guards, he finds Brienne already bathing and joins her in the same tub after dismissing attendants.
2. **Jaime taunts Brienne** — He mocks her about Renly's death, his lost hand, and her body.
3. **Jaime apologizes** — Acknowledging his bitterness, he offers a truce and admits she protected him well.
4. **Jaime proposes a truce** — Brienne resists, saying truces require trust.
5. **Jaime reveals why he killed Aerys** — In a fever-state, he tells Brienne the full story of Aerys's wildfire plot: the caches beneath King's Landing, Lord Chelsted's resistance and burning, Rossart's appointment as Hand, and Jaime's decision to kill Aerys and the pyromancers to save the city.
6. **Jaime challenges Ned Stark's judgment** — He rages that Stark judged him guilty without listening, shouting "By what right does the wolf judge the lion?"
7. **Jaime faints** — Overcome by fever, poison, and the bath's heat, he collapses; Brienne catches him.
8. **Jaime is tended and dressed** — Qyburn diagnoses poison and malnourishment; Brienne scrubs and dresses Jaime; she is given an ill-fitting pink satin gown.
9. **Jaime and Brienne cross to Kingspyre** — Jaime clings to Brienne's arm; they enter the vast cold hall where Bolton waits.
10. **Supper with Bolton begins** — Bolton offers food and wine; reveals Edmure's bounty of 1000 dragons and Karstark's offer of Alys's hand for Jaime's head.
11. **Bolton reveals Robb's marriage** — Robb wed Jeyne Westerling, breaking faith with the Freys; Brienne is shocked.
12. **Bolton reveals Arya Stark is "found"** — He claims she will be returned to the north; Brienne asks about both girls.
13. **Bolton reveals Sansa's marriage to Tyrion** — Brienne is appalled; Jaime hides his surprise.
14. **Bolton explains Vargo Hoat's motives for maiming Jaime** — To remove Jaime as a threat, send a token to Tywin, diminish Jaime's value to Bolton, and implicate Bolton in the crime.
15. **Jaime agrees to absolve Bolton** — He promises to tell Cersei and Tywin that Bolton treated him well, understanding this is the price of freedom.
16. **Bolton announces Jaime's escort** — Walton "Steelshanks" will take Jaime to King's Landing when Qyburn clears him.
17. **Bolton separates Jaime and Brienne** — He announces Jaime goes free but Brienne stays at Harrenhal for Vargo Hoat, warning her to worry about "sapphires."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitter, weakened, identity-questioning; swings between cruel mockery, genuine remorse, feverish vulnerability, and strategic calculation. Feels diminished — "worth less than a girl now."
- **Primary preoccupation:** His lost hand and what it means for his identity; the burden of being called Kingslayer when he saved King's Landing; negotiating survival with Bolton.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to tell Brienne the truth about killing Aerys (fever-driven, semi-involuntary); agrees to absolve Bolton of blame for the maiming; threatens Bolton to establish he is not powerless.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself his arousal at seeing Brienne naked is only because he's been away from Cersei too long; considers himself primarily pragmatic in dealing with Bolton but is genuinely wounded by being judged; his "left hand is good for nothing" refrain may understate his adaptation.
