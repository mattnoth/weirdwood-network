- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion attends a small council meeting presided over by Lord Tywin. The council discusses the war — Varys reports a victory at Duskendale, Balon Greyjoy's alliance offer is debated and deferred, and Littlefinger proposes to travel to the Vale to marry Lysa Arryn. Tyrion is named Master of Coin, replacing Littlefinger. The council divides the lands and titles of defeated lords, with Highgarden reaping the greatest rewards. In a private Lannister family meeting afterward, Tywin announces plans to marry Cersei to Willas Tyrell and Tyrion to Sansa Stark, revealing that Robb Stark has broken his Frey oath by wedding Jeyne Westerling.

## Events & Actions
1. **Council assembles** — Tywin enters and is greeted by lords; he kisses the High Septon's ring and Cersei's cheek, clasps Pycelle's hand, and takes the king's seat at the head of the table.
2. **Tyrion takes Pycelle's old seat** — Tyrion claims the foot of the table, displacing Pycelle to a seat near Cersei.
3. **Tywin opens with war business** — Cersei suggests starting with wedding planning but Tywin overrides her, beginning with the war.
4. **Battle of Duskendale reported** — Varys reports that Lord Randyll Tarly caught Robett Glover outside Duskendale, trapped him against the sea, and won despite heavy losses on both sides. Ser Helman Tallhart is dead along with a thousand others. Glover retreats toward Harrenhal where Gregor Clegane awaits.
5. **Robb Stark's position discussed** — Tywin reports Stark has retreated to Riverrun, abandoning western castles. Ser Daven Lannister reforms at Lannisport; he and Ser Forley Prester will descend on Riverrun once Stark moves north.
6. **Greyjoy alliance debated** — Kevan presents Balon Greyjoy's offer of alliance in exchange for recognition as king and everything north of the Neck. Redwyne and Tyrell favor it. Tywin defers, saying Greyjoy is already fighting Stark for free and a "better option" may present itself.
7. **Lysa Arryn discussed** — Tyrion volunteers to deal with Lysa Arryn and is insulted by Mace Tyrell, who dismisses him from fighting. Tywin says he has "other tasks" for Tyrion.
8. **Littlefinger proposes to marry Lysa Arryn** — Littlefinger volunteers to travel to the Vale to woo and wed Lysa, promising to deliver the Vale without bloodshed. The council approves.
9. **Tyrion named Master of Coin** — Tywin appoints Tyrion to replace Littlefinger as Master of Coin.
10. **Warden of the East discussed** — Tywin agrees to restore Robert Arryn to Warden of the East if Lysa will marry Littlefinger and return to the king's peace.
11. **Littlefinger to depart on the morrow** — Plans to take passage on the Braavosi galley The Merling King; will miss the royal wedding.
12. **Greyjoy alliance deferred** — Tywin dismisses the Greyjoy proposal, preferring to wait.
13. **Joffrey's wedding feast planned** — The High Septon describes Great Sept of Baelor preparations; Cersei details feast arrangements for a thousand in the throne room and more in the wards.
14. **Dornish delegation announced** — Pycelle reports three hundred Dornishmen riding from Sunspear toward King's Landing, expected before the wedding.
15. **Tyrell-Martell tension surfaces** — Mace Tyrell bristles at Dornish approaching without his leave to cross the Reach. Tywin and Kevan smooth it over, citing the coming marriage alliances.
16. **Justice for Elia Martell promised** — Tywin states Doran comes to claim his council seat and "the justice Robert denied him for the murder of his sister Elia and her children."
17. **Spoils of war distributed** — Lands and castles of traitors who fought for Stannis are divided. Forty-seven lesser lordlings and six hundred nineteen knights died under Stannis; their heirs disinherited. Brightwater Keep and its lands granted to Ser Garlan Tyrell. Lesser tracts to Lord Rowan; lands set aside for Tarly, Oakheart, Hightower. Redwyne gets thirty years' tax remission on Arbor vintages.
18. **High Septon's crown to be replaced** — Ser Addam found crystals; confirmed the old crown was broken up and melted. Tywin orders Cersei to commission goldsmiths for a replacement before the wedding.
19. **Varys's intelligence reports** — A true kraken sighted off the Fingers, attacking an Ibbenese whaler. Fighting on the Stepstones. Tyrosh and Lys likely to war, both courting Myr. Sailors report a three-headed dragon hatched in Qarth.
20. **Tyrek Lannister still missing** — Tywin asks after his brother's son; Varys reports no trace found.
21. **Deserter gold cloaks dealt with** — Some gold cloaks who deserted during the Blackwater have returned to barracks. Cersei wants them executed. Varys suggests sending them to the Night's Watch, citing disturbing reports from the Wall. Tyrion supports a partial Wall solution, invoking his visit to the Wall and the Old Bear's fears. Tywin orders their knees broken as a lesson; dismisses the Wall's concerns, saying Stark and Greyjoy should defend the north they claim.
22. **Council dismissed; private Lannister meeting** — Tywin keeps Tyrion, Cersei, and Kevan after the other lords depart.
23. **Tyrell plot revealed** — Kevan reveals that Littlefinger brought word of a Tyrell plot to spirit Sansa Stark to Highgarden and marry her to Willas Tyrell. Tyrion notes it was Littlefinger, not Varys, who provided this intelligence.
24. **Tywin orders Cersei to remarry** — States she must marry to end Stannis's incest slander and bear more children. He proposes Willas Tyrell as the best candidate. Cersei refuses furiously but ultimately sits back down to hear the choice.
25. **Tywin orders Tyrion to marry Sansa Stark** — Tells Tyrion it is his reward for the Blackwater and the key to the north. Tywin reveals his past failed attempts to arrange marriages for Tyrion: Hoster Tully refused him for Lysa, Dorne called it an insult, Yohn Royce and Leyton Hightower declined, and the Florent girl's father preferred a household knight.
26. **Robb Stark's marriage to Jeyne Westerling revealed** — Tywin shares news not yet given to the council: Robb Stark has married Gawen Westerling's eldest daughter Jeyne, breaking his sworn oath to House Frey. Kevan provides background on the Westerling family and Sybell Spicer's lowborn origins.
27. **Tywin's long-term plan articulated** — Tyrion weds Sansa; Greyjoy gets an "autumn crown" and a northern winter; come spring, Tyrion brings "Eddard Stark's grandson" to claim Winterfell, and northmen will rise for the Stark heir.
28. **Tyrion is denied Casterly Rock** — Tywin explicitly states: "You shall never have Casterly Rock, I promise you. But wed Sansa Stark, and it is just possible that you might win Winterfell."
29. **Chapter ends with Tywin's veiled menace** — Tyrion points out the Westerlings should have seen the lesson of Castamere. Tywin replies: "Mayhaps they have. They are well aware of Castamere, I promise you." He then near-smiles and repeats: "You will marry Sansa Stark, Tyrion. And soon."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitter, resentful, and marginalized. Tyrion feels sidelined by the new council arrangement — "The game changed while I lay rotting in my bed, and no one will tell me the rules." He takes wicked pleasure in Cersei's humiliation at the remarriage order. He is resigned but strategic about his own forced marriage, weighing it as possibly giving him a place in the world apart from Joffrey's court.
- **Primary preoccupation:** His diminished status at court; sensing that decisions are made before council meetings and he is being managed. He watches his father closely for hidden agendas and senses Tywin is withholding information (the "better option," the letters, the Westerling reaction).
- **Key decisions made:** Reluctantly accepts the Master of Coin appointment. Agrees to marry Sansa Stark, though unenthusiastically — swayed by the prospect of Winterfell and recognition that alternatives (Lollys, no match at all) are worse.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion believes Shae genuinely cares for him despite recognizing she calls herself his whore — "for all she swears that she is content to be my whore." He does not voice this vulnerability to anyone. He also toys with the idea that a wife and lands would give him independence but doesn't fully reckon with how his father controls every piece on the board.
