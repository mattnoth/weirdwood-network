# ASOS — Davos VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 64
- **pov_character:** Davos Seaworth
- **pov_chapter_number:** Davos VI
- **first_available:** ASOS Davos VI
- **location_primary:** Dragonstone
- **location_secondary:** Chamber of the Painted Table, postern gate, maester's chambers, gallery above the yard, the Stone Drum
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Joffrey's death at his wedding feast; the third of the three leech-predicted deaths has occurred
- **time_markers:** Sunset (nightfire lit at sunset); "purple evening sky"; prayers last "an hour or more"; half moon sliding in and out amongst thin high clouds; night throughout the main action; "the night before" referenced regarding dwindling worshipers; "when he was a boy" (Davos's childhood); travel time implied by Edric needing to reach the galley anchored "just past the point"
- **chapter_summary:** Davos orchestrates the secret removal of Edric Storm from Dragonstone while Stannis, Melisandre, and the queen's men are at their nightly R'hllor prayer. With the help of loyal men, he smuggles the boy through a postern gate to a boat that will row him to Salladhor Saan's galley Mad Prendos. After Edric departs, Davos waits in the Chamber of the Painted Table for Stannis and Melisandre, who arrive discussing Joffrey's death — the third of Melisandre's three leech-predicted deaths. When Melisandre demands Edric for sacrifice to "wake the stone dragons," Davos reveals the boy is gone. Stannis is furious, but Davos defends his actions as his duty as Hand. As Stannis draws Lightbringer, Davos produces a letter and begins reading it aloud.

## Events & Actions
1. **Nightfire prayer ceremony** — Melisandre leads the nightly R'hllor prayer at sunset in the yard of Dragonstone. Queen Selyse leads the responses; Stannis stands beside her but does not join in the responses, staring into the flames. Davos observes from an arched window in the gallery above.
2. **Davos departs the gallery** — Ser Andrew touches Davos's elbow; they leave the window to begin the operation while the worshipers will be occupied for an hour or more.
3. **Recalled planning scenes** — Davos recalls Melisandre telling him fire is a living thing and difficult to read; he recalls warning his recruited men that Melisandre may see their intent; Lewys suggests killing Melisandre first but Davos refuses, citing Maester Cressen's failure; Ser Triston objects to sneaking.
4. **Edric retrieved from Pylos's chambers** — Davos and Ser Andrew enter; Maester Pylos instructs Edric to go with Davos; Edric dons a hooded cloak; Pylos says the Hand speaks with the king's voice.
5. **Descent through Dragonstone** — Ser Gerald Gower joins at the steps; the group descends through the castle, across a shadowed yard, under a frozen stone dragon's tail.
6. **Edric resists, then complies** — Edric wants to see Shireen to say farewell; Davos says there's no time. Edric demands to see Stannis; Davos tells him Stannis does not want to see him and shows his shortened fingers as a threat. Edric relents.
7. **Postern gate departure** — Lewys and Omer Blackberry wait at the postern gate with two bound and trussed guards. A boat with four oarsmen waits; the galley Mad Prendos is anchored past the point. Davos kneels before Edric and bids him farewell, calling it "the start of your life's great adventure."
8. **Edric departs** — Edric goes with Ser Andrew through the postern gate. The others follow except the Bastard of Nightsong, who stays to bar the gate.
9. **Guards disposed of** — Davos instructs Ser Rolland to drag the bound guards into a cellar and release them once Edric is safely under way.
10. **Davos walks to the Stone Drum** — Davos walks alone through Dragonstone, reflecting on having risen too high, wishing he could sail home to Cape Wrath and Marya. Guards at the Stone Drum door let him pass as the King's Hand.
11. **Davos waits in the Chamber of the Painted Table** — He builds a fire, opens curtains and shutters, looks for Mad Prendos from the north window but sees only a dark sea. He observes stars (the Galley, the Crone's Lantern, the Ice Dragon) and stone dragon shadows on the ramparts.
12. **Stannis and Melisandre arrive** — They enter arguing about Melisandre's vision in the nightfire. She claims to have seen a death and heard a mother's wail; Stannis is skeptical. Davos steps forward and confirms Joffrey is dead.
13. **Discussion of Joffrey's death** — Davos reports Joffrey choked on food at his wedding, possibly poisoned by "his uncle, the Imp." Stannis recalls a childhood incident of Joffrey's cruelty (cutting open a kitchen cat). Stannis places his crown on the table and says "they must send for me now."
14. **Melisandre warns of Tommen** — She says Joffrey has a brother who will be crowned. Stannis calls Tommen "another monster in the making, another leech upon the land."
15. **Melisandre demands Edric** — She asks Stannis to "let me wake the stone dragons" and "give me the boy." Stannis reluctantly considers it as his duty. Melisandre invokes the prophecy: Stannis is "the prince that was promised."
16. **Davos reveals Edric is gone** — Davos announces Edric is aboard a Lyseni galley, safely out to sea. Melisandre shows dismay — she did not foresee it.
17. **Stannis's fury** — Stannis accuses Davos of acting without leave. Melisandre accuses Davos of treason and says she should have left him "to the dark." Davos claims he did his duty.
18. **Davos defends himself** — He invokes his four dead sons, his oath as Hand, and argues that Edric is one of the people he swore to protect. He offers his head if he has offended.
19. **Stannis draws Lightbringer** — Its glow fills the chamber. He tells Davos to say what he will, quickly.
20. **Davos reads the letter** — Davos produces a crinkled parchment from his cloak and begins reading it by Lightbringer's light, declaring that a King's Hand should be able to read and write. Chapter ends mid-reading.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Tense and resolute throughout the escape operation; fearful but determined; wistful and self-doubting during his walk through Dragonstone (wishing he'd been content with less); calm defiance during the confrontation with Stannis; resigned to possible death ("If I ever do" come out)
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting Edric safely away before Melisandre can sacrifice him; then surviving the confrontation with Stannis; underlying worry about Devan's safety near the red woman's influence
- **Key decisions made:** Executed the pre-planned smuggling of Edric Storm; chose to confront Stannis directly rather than flee; chose to produce and read the letter as his final argument
- **Self-deception flags:** None. Davos is notably clear-eyed — he acknowledges he lied to Edric (about Stannis not wanting to see him), acknowledges the danger to himself and his companions, and makes no attempt to justify himself through self-serving rationalizations. He explicitly reflects that he has risen too high and too fast.
