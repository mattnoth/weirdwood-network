# ASOS — Daenerys IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 43
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys IV
- **first_available:** ASOS Daenerys IV
- **location_primary:** Daenerys's camp outside Yunkai
- **location_secondary:** Sandstone ridge overlooking Yunkish army; birchwood forest; fields before Yunkai; Yunkai (city)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the fall of Astapor; Daenerys's army is marching toward Yunkai and camps outside the city
- **time_markers:** Stormcrow captains summoned at midday; Second Sons captain summoned two hours later; Yunkai envoys arrive as the sun goes down; full dark by the time Yunkai'i depart; attack planned for an hour past midnight; near midnight Daario arrives; hours past midnight during the battle; the next day they march three leagues to Yunkai; on the morning of the third day the slaves emerge
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys scouts the Yunkish army of five thousand and meets separately with the captains of the Stormcrows, the commander of the Second Sons, and an envoy from Yunkai, offering each terms they refuse. She plans a surprise night attack, and Daario Naharis arrives at midnight with the severed heads of his fellow Stormcrow captains, pledging the company to her service. The battle is won with minimal losses, and after marching to Yunkai, the freed slaves pour from the city gates on the third day, hailing Daenerys as "Mhysa" — Mother.

## Events & Actions
1. **Scouting the Yunkish army** — Dany and Ser Jorah ride through a birchwood forest to a sandstone ridge and observe the Yunkish host: approximately five thousand troops with the Second Sons on the left flank, the Stormcrows on the right, and Yunkish slave soldiers in the center.
2. **Dany orders diplomatic meetings** — She commands that the Stormcrow captains come at midday, the Second Sons two hours later, and the Yunkish envoys in the evening, all separately.
3. **Inspection of camp defenses** — Returning to camp, Dany observes the Unsullied digging a ditch and sharpening birch stakes; she speaks with Grey Worm about the coming battle.
4. **Grey Worm explains his name** — When asked why he kept his slave name, Grey Worm says it is lucky because it was the name he had when Daenerys freed him.
5. **Meeting with Stormcrow captains** — Prendahl na Ghezn, Sallor the Bald, and Daario Naharis arrive; Prendahl insults Dany and refuses her offer to switch sides; Dany offers gold, plunder, and future rewards; they refuse and leave, though Daario inclines his head in polite farewell.
6. **Meeting with Mero** — The Titan's Bastard arrives alone, drinks wine, makes crude sexual propositions; Dany offers him the same terms; he takes a wagon of wine and promises an answer by morning.
7. **Warning about Mero** — Arstan Whitebeard and Ser Jorah warn Dany that Mero has an evil reputation and is dangerous to his employers; no Free City will hire him anymore.
8. **Yunkai envoy arrives** — Grazdan mo Eraz arrives at sunset with fifty horsemen and one camel rider; offers 50,000 golden marks for Dany to leave; boasts of Yunkai's ancient Ghiscari lineage.
9. **Dany's ultimatum to Yunkai** — She demands Yunkai free all slaves within three days, allowing them to take their masters' possessions as payment for servitude; Unsullied will search the city to confirm.
10. **Dracarys** — Dany commands her dragons; Drogon's flame catches Grazdan's tokar; Whitebeard douses the fire with water; Grazdan threatens Dany and departs.
11. **Night attack planned** — After dark, Dany assembles her bloodriders and captains; plans a surprise attack at an hour past midnight, exploiting the fact that the Stormcrows will be arguing, the Second Sons will be drunk on the gifted wine, and the Yunkai'i believe they have three days.
12. **Attack plan detailed** — Unsullied to strike from right and left; Dothraki horse in wedge through center; Jhogo to deal with enemy scouts.
13. **Daario arrives at midnight** — Caught by Unsullied trying to sneak into camp; brings a sack containing the severed heads of Prendahl na Ghezn and Sallor the Bald; pledges the Stormcrows to Dany.
14. **Dragons consume the heads** — Viserion burns Prendahl's head; all three dragons eat the charred flesh.
15. **Daario swears his sword** — Draws his arakh and swears his sword, life, love, blood, body, and songs to Dany in an extravagant gesture.
16. **Jorah objects, Dany overrules** — Jorah wants Daario held under guard; Dany decides to trust him, citing the heads as proof of sincerity; assigns Stormcrows to attack the Yunkish rear.
17. **Confrontation with Jorah** — Dany tells Jorah she honors, respects, and cherishes him but does not desire him; accuses him of pushing every other man away so she must rely on him alone; Jorah goes pale and departs stiffly.
18. **Dany's solitary reflection** — She feels lonely; remembers Mirri Maz Duur's prophecy that she will never bear a living child; tells the dragons they must be her children; plays with Drogon, noting his tail is growing longer.
19. **Waiting during the battle** — Dany stays in her pavilion with her maids while Whitebeard and Belwas guard; she cannot sleep.
20. **Whitebeard tells stories of Rhaegar** — Rhaegar seldom entered tourneys but excelled at one at Storm's End; won the great tourney at Harrenhal and crowned Lyanna Stark queen of love and beauty over his wife Elia; Rhaegar loved Summerhall and his harp; had a sense of doom from birth; was born in grief at Summerhall.
21. **Robert Baratheon discussed** — Whitebeard says Robert liked bawdy songs and only sang when drunk.
22. **Victory** — Ser Jorah returns dusty and bloodied; the Stormcrows turned their cloaks, the slaves broke, the Second Sons were too drunk to fight; approximately 200 Yunkai'i dead, about a dozen of Dany's forces lost; several thousand captives taken; Mero fled.
23. **March to Yunkai** — The next day they march three leagues to the city; Jorah and Grey Worm deploy troops; Dany waits.
24. **Slaves freed** — On the morning of the third day, Yunkai opens its gates and slaves emerge; Missandei tells them they owe their freedom to Daenerys Stormborn.
25. **"Mhysa"** — A brown-skinned man with a child on his shoulder shouts "Mhysa"; the chant spreads through thousands of freed slaves in multiple languages (Mhysa, Maela, Aelalla, Qathei, Tato), all meaning "Mother"; Dany rides into the crowd, bells ringing, as they reach for her.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Confident and commanding during negotiations; amused by Daario's swagger; irritated by Mero's crudeness; angered by Jorah's jealousy; lonely and sad after the confrontation with Jorah; restless and anxious during the battle; deeply moved by the freed slaves calling her "Mother"
- **Primary preoccupation:** Winning the battle at Yunkai with minimal losses; freeing the slaves; managing the loyalty of sellsword companies; navigating Jorah's romantic feelings
- **Key decisions made:** Meets sellsword captains and envoy separately to divide them; refuses Yunkai's gold and demands slave freedom; plans surprise night attack exploiting the timing of her negotiations; trusts Daario over Jorah's objections; confronts Jorah about his possessiveness; rides into the crowd of freed slaves
- **Self-deception flags:** Uses the phrase "I am only a young girl and do not understand the ways of war" as a deliberate rhetorical tool (she clearly does understand); tells herself "He will forgive me" about Jorah after their confrontation; internal attraction to Daario ("And he has blue eyes") suggests her trust in him may not be purely strategic; recalls "I shall be betrayed twice more, once for gold and once for love" but does not apply it to Daario
