# ASOS — Tyrion V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 39
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion V
- **first_available:** ASOS Tyrion V
- **location_primary:** Kingsroad, south of King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Blackwater Rush, King's Landing (visible ahead), Casterly Rock (recalled), Sunspear (mentioned), Oldtown (mentioned), the Citadel (mentioned), Dorne (mentioned), Free Cities (mentioned), Disputed Lands (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly before King Joffrey's wedding; Dornish party arriving for the festivities
- **time_markers:** Daytime (sun shining bright), "pleasantly warm for autumn," references to the recent battle against Stannis (Blackwater), Oberyn's visit to Casterly Rock described as "many and many a year ago" when Tyrion was a newborn, murders of Elia and her children described as "sixteen years stale," Oberyn's duel with Lord Yronwood when he was "no more than sixteen," Oberyn now "a man past forty," Willas Tyrell letter "not half a year past"
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion rides out with an honor guard to meet the arriving Dornish party on the kingsroad south of King's Landing. He discovers that Prince Doran Martell has not come — Prince Oberyn Martell, the Red Viper, has come in his stead with nine of Dorne's great houses represented. Pod identifies the Dornish banners. Oberyn introduces his companions including his paramour Ellaria Sand. As they ride toward the city, Oberyn tells Tyrion about visiting Casterly Rock when Tyrion was a newborn, including a disturbing story of Cersei twisting infant Tyrion's genitals. Oberyn then drops diplomatic pretense and demands justice for his sister Elia and her children, specifically naming Gregor Clegane and implying the orders came from higher up. Tyrion warns Oberyn of the overwhelming military forces in King's Landing.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion waits on the kingsroad** — Tyrion sits mounted with an honor guard of gold cloaks and various lords south of King's Landing, waiting for the arriving Dornish delegation. His father arranged for a larger escort than Tyrion alone, fearing Doran Martell might take offense.
2. **Dornish column emerges from the wood** — The Dornish riders appear from the green of the living wood in a long dusty column, flying too many banners for Tyrion's comfort.
3. **Pod identifies the Dornish banners** — At Tyrion's command, Pod identifies all nine Dornish house banners with Bronn describing each: Martell, Dalt, Blackmont, Manwoody, Qorgyle, Uller, Allyrion, Gargalen, and Jordayne. Tyrion is impressed.
4. **Pod notices no litter** — Pod observes that there is no litter in the Dornish column, meaning Prince Doran has not come. Tyrion has a bad feeling.
5. **Tyrion rides forward to meet the Dornish** — Unable to bear the waiting, Tyrion orders banners forward and rides out. The Dornish spur their own mounts in response.
6. **Tyrion identifies Oberyn Martell** — Tyrion recognizes the Dornish leader as too young and too fit to be Doran. He realizes it is Prince Oberyn, the Red Viper.
7. **Oberyn announces Doran's absence** — Oberyn explains that his brother's health requires him to remain at Sunspear, and that Doran has sent Oberyn to join Joffrey's council in his stead.
8. **Oberyn introduces his companions** — Oberyn names each member of his retinue, ending with Ellaria Sand, his paramour. Tyrion internally worries about the seating problems Ellaria will cause at the wedding.
9. **Oberyn calls Tyrion "Uncle Imp"** — Oberyn turns to his retinue and mocks Tyrion by calling him "his own Uncle Imp." Bronn laughs; Tyrion forces amusement.
10. **Tyrion introduces his escort** — Tyrion names his own party: Ser Flement Brax, Lord Gyles, Ser Addam Marbrand, Jalabhar Xho, Ser Harys Swyft, Ser Merlon Crakehall, Ser Philip Foote, Ser Bronn, and Podrick Payne. He privately acknowledges they are a less distinguished company than Oberyn's.
11. **Column proceeds toward King's Landing** — At Lady Blackmont's request, the combined column rides for the river and King's Landing, with gold cloaks turning crisply at Ser Addam's command.
12. **Oberyn tells of visiting Casterly Rock** — Riding beside Tyrion, Oberyn recounts visiting the Rock when Tyrion was a newborn, with his mother, her consort, and Elia. His mother was ruling in Dorne and Tywin was Hand to King Aerys.
13. **Tyrion recalls his mother and Gerion** — Tyrion internally reflects on Joanna's death in his birth, the family stories of Tywin and Joanna's love, and his Uncle Gerion's words. Gerion is now lost beyond the seas.
14. **Oberyn describes Casterly Rock hospitality** — Oberyn complains that Tywin ignored them, the cell was dark and windowless, the skies too grey, wines too sweet, women too chaste, food too bland.
15. **Oberyn recounts the rumors about Tyrion's birth** — In Oldtown when Tyrion was born, people spoke of a monster born to the King's Hand — with a tail, beard, teeth, claws, and both male and female parts.
16. **Oberyn tells the nursery story** — Cersei claimed Tyrion as hers, threatened the wet nurse, showed the baby to Oberyn and Elia. Elia cooed over him. Cersei twisted infant Tyrion's genitals, saying "He killed my mother." Jaime intervened: "Leave him be, you're hurting him." Cersei said "Everyone says he's like to die soon."
17. **Tyrion goes cold** — Tyrion is deeply affected by the story but masks it with humor, joking about having had a tail that his father had lopped off.
18. **Oberyn raises the whoring tax** — Oberyn mentions hearing about Tyrion's tax on prostitution from Lord Buckler's steward. Tyrion internally notes it was Tywin's idea but he gets the blame; people call it "the dwarf's penny."
19. **Oberyn and Tyrion discuss sexual preferences** — Oberyn says he and Ellaria are curious about a blonde woman. Tyrion deflects, noting he is married and no longer frequents whores.
20. **Oberyn demands justice for Elia** — Oberyn drops all pretense and states he has come for justice for Elia and her children, not for a mummer's show inquiry. He names Gregor Clegane and says the Mountain will reveal who gave the orders before he dies.
21. **Oberyn declares himself dangerous** — Oberyn says the gods were good to make him the third-born, not the first, because he is bloodthirsty unlike his prudent brother Doran.
22. **Tyrion warns Oberyn of the military balance** — Tyrion points out that Oberyn has only three hundred men, while King's Landing holds five thousand gold cloaks, twenty thousand Lannister swords, and fifty to seventy thousand Tyrell troops.
23. **Oberyn invokes Dornish history** — Oberyn quotes "all flowers bow before the sun" and says he'll trample the roses if they hinder him.
24. **They discuss Willas Tyrell** — Tyrion mentions Oberyn crippled the heir to Highgarden. Oberyn says it was a clean hit, Willas's foot caught in a stirrup, and his horse fell on him. He blames Mace Tyrell for pushing Willas into tourneys too young. Willas and Oberyn now correspond about horses.
25. **They discuss Loras Tyrell** — Tyrion mentions Loras's martial prowess, including unhorsing Jaime and killing Ser Robar Royce and Ser Emmon Cuy. Oberyn is unimpressed, distinguishing tourney unhorsing from killing in battle.
26. **Tyrion suggests Oberyn find a blonde companion** — Tyrion recommends Chataya's on the Street of Silk and suggests Dancy or Marie, advising Oberyn to keep one by his side at all times since he wants to die with a breast in hand.
27. **Tyrion rides ahead to the ferry** — Tyrion canters ahead to the ferry barges on the south bank of the Blackwater, having had enough of Oberyn's wit.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Sardonic resignation shifting to genuine alarm. Tyrion is annoyed at being made a welcoming committee, amused by Pod's heraldry prowess, deeply disturbed by Oberyn's nursery story (goes "cold all over"), and increasingly worried about the political danger Oberyn represents. He masks vulnerability with wit throughout.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Managing the volatile Oberyn Martell and assessing the political danger his presence creates — the Dornishmen among the Tyrells could spark violence. Secondarily, the nursery story forces him to confront Cersei's lifelong hatred.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to ride forward rather than wait; warns Oberyn about military realities rather than making empty promises; decides to ride ahead and disengage from the conversation rather than be further provoked.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells Pod he couldn't have named all the Dornish houses himself — acknowledged as a deliberate lie to encourage the boy. Tyrion tells Oberyn he no longer frequents whores — internally qualifies this with "Unless I want to see them hanged," suggesting his abstinence is forced by Tywin's threat, not genuine choice.
