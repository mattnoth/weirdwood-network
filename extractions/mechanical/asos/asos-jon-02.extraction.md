- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow and Tormund Giantsbane ride to observe hundreds of giants on mammoths joining the wildling host along the Milkwater. Tormund tells tall tales, presses Jon about Ygritte, and the wildling band sings "The Last of the Giants." Orell's eagle attacks Jon, ripping open his face. Rattleshirt brings Jon to the Fist of the First Men, where the aftermath of a devastating attack reveals dead horses, blood-stained snow, and stripped equipment — but no human bodies. Mance Rayder interrogates Jon about the Night's Watch strength, and Jon reveals there were three hundred brothers under the Old Bear's command. Mance orders Jon to go over the Wall with Styr and Jarl, and Ygritte claims she and Jon are lovers — which Jon confirms.

- **Weather:** Heavy snowfall throughout, increasing as the day progresses. Wind blowing snow into faces. Cold enough for blood to freeze on skin and for river shallows to have a crust of thin ice.
- **Season indicators:** Winter; references to past winters in Tormund's stories; "the cold is rising" per Mance; deep snow drifts, especially on the eastern bank of the Milkwater.
- **Time of day:** Daytime at the start, transitioning through the afternoon ("several hours later"), with night falling by the time they reach the Fist. Darkening sky noted.
- **Lighting:** Natural daylight dimming to dusk; no artificial lighting described aside from references to torches ordered for patrols.
- **Sounds:** Mammoths passing; eagle wings beating and shrieking; singing ("The Last of the Giants"); spears banging on leathern shields; hoofbeats; the crunch of frozen crust and frozen blood underfoot; a raven quorking from a soldier pine; the clacking of bones (Rattleshirt's armor).
- **Smells:** Choking stink from the giants (or their mammoths).
- **Notable sensory details:** Taste of mud and blood in Jon's mouth after the eagle attack; throbbing pain in his face; blood running into his right eye; pink snow at the Fist; entrails spilled from dead horses "like frozen snakes"; half his vision blacked by blood; snowflakes melting in Tormund's hair and beard; the feel of frozen blood crust crunching underfoot.

## Events & Actions
1. **Giants on mammoths pass the column** — Jon and Tormund ride to observe hundreds of giants emerging from the snow along the Milkwater, seated on mammoths, carrying clubs made from dead tree limbs and stone mauls. Jon tries to count them but loses track past fifty.
2. **Tormund introduces Mag the Mighty** — An old giant on a large grey-and-white mammoth passes; Tormund and Mag exchange words in the Old Tongue. Mag laughs. Tormund tells Jon giants have no kings.
3. **Jon and Tormund ride back toward the column** — They turn back toward the head of the column. Jon asks Tormund about killing a giant.
4. **Tormund tells the giant story** — Tormund recounts crawling inside a sleeping giant for warmth; she woke and suckled him for three moons. He admits he never killed her.
5. **Jon asks about Tormund's other names** — Jon asks about "Horn-Blower" particularly, internally wondering if Mance found the Horn of Joramun. Tormund deflects into the she-bear story instead.
6. **Tormund tells the she-bear story** — Tormund recounts stumbling through a storm to find a woman, who turned out to be a she-bear. Claims she bore him cubs.
7. **Tormund presses Jon about Ygritte** — Tormund asks why Jon refuses Ygritte, suggests his member was cut off, questions wildling courtship customs vs. southern ones. Jon's internal conflict about his Night's Watch vows intensifies.
8. **Jon reflects on Ygritte** — Jon recalls Ygritte throwing her sleeping skins beside his every night, using Ghost as a barrier. He notices her physical features and finds himself attracted despite his vows.
9. **Jon reflects on the wildling host's composition** — Jon catalogs dangerous wildling leaders (Harma, Styr, Varamyr, the Weeper) and diverse peoples (Frozen Shore men, ice-river clans, cave dwellers, Hornfoot men). Notes Mance's achievement in unifying them.
10. **Jon assesses wildling military vulnerability** — Jon notes only one in a hundred wildlings is mounted, and the host is spread thin. He calculates that the Old Bear's three hundred mounted men could strike decisively if they killed Mance.
11. **Tormund's band sings "The Last of the Giants"** — Ygritte starts the song; Tormund, Longspear Ryk, Toregg, Dormund, Munda, and the whole band join in. Ygritte weeps at the song's end.
12. **Orell's eagle attacks Jon** — The eagle suddenly dives at Jon, burying talons in his face. Jon falls from his horse. Ygritte kneels over him protectively with a bone dagger.
13. **Tormund confronts Rattleshirt** — Rattleshirt arrives demanding Jon. Tormund threatens him. Rattleshirt says Mance wants Jon.
14. **Jon rides to the Fist of the First Men** — Jon, Ygritte, and Rattleshirt's band ride two miles down the column, cross the Milkwater, and approach the Fist from the south slope.
15. **Jon sees the aftermath at the Fist** — Dead horses with entrails spilled, legs twisted, eyes blind. Wildlings strip them of equipment. Pink snow, frozen blood. A garron impaled on entrance stakes trying to get out. One of Chett's dogs dead. No human bodies visible. Jon wonders about Sam.
16. **Mance interrogates Jon** — Mance asks what happened to his face, then demands to know how many Night's Watch brothers there were. Jon reveals three hundred (200 from Castle Black, 100 from the Shadow Tower).
17. **Jon reveals the Old Bear commanded** — After pressure, Jon names Jeor Mormont as the commander. Mance asks who commands Castle Black; Jon says Bowen Marsh.
18. **Mance assesses the situation** — Mance notes the dead walked and walls mean nothing against them. He says the crows may have helped by absorbing the wight attack. He orders Varamyr to send wolves to scout for wights and Rattleshirt to double patrols with torches.
19. **Mance orders Jon over the Wall** — Mance tells Jon to go with Jarl and Styr the next day, over the Wall. Styr objects but Mance insists Jon knows Castle Black.
20. **Ygritte claims she and Jon are lovers** — Ygritte defends Jon against Rattleshirt and Styr, asserting they "dance beneath" his cloak. Mance asks Jon directly; Jon confirms with "Yes."
21. **Jon draws Longclaw against Rattleshirt** — After Mance departs, Rattleshirt threatens Ygritte. Jon draws Longclaw and warns him. Ghost appears atop the ringwall with bristling fur and blood-red eyes; Rattleshirt backs down.
22. **Jon and Ygritte descend the Fist** — While crossing the Milkwater, Jon says he never asked Ygritte to lie. She replies she only "left out part" — she never said when they started sleeping together. She tells Jon to find another place for Ghost tonight, implying they will consummate.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply conflicted. Jon is torn between his Night's Watch vows and growing attraction to Ygritte; between his undercover mission and his horror at betraying his brothers' information. He feels fear during the eagle attack, despair at the interrogation ("This is too hard"), and guilt at confirming the lie about Ygritte. He is growing fond of Tormund despite himself. He is grimly determined about killing Mance if necessary but takes "no joy" in the prospect.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The tension between maintaining his cover among the wildlings and remaining loyal to the Night's Watch. He fixates on Qhorin's final orders as justification for his actions. His secondary preoccupation is Ygritte — resisting her advances while finding himself increasingly attracted.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Reveals the Old Bear's identity and force numbers to Mance under pressure. (2) Confirms Ygritte's claim that they are lovers. (3) Draws Longclaw against Rattleshirt. These are all steps deeper into his turncloak role.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells himself "I will not think about Ygritte" while clearly thinking about her extensively. He reassures himself about killing Mance while acknowledging he "half admired and almost liked" the man. He believes Bran and Rickon are still at Winterfell. He clings to his vows while his actions increasingly contradict them — his final "Yes" to Mance marks a point where he can no longer fully distinguish the performance from reality ("he did not know that he could tell honor from shame anymore, or right from wrong").
