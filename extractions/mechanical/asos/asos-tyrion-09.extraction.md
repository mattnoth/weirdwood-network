# ASOS — Tyrion IX

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 67
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion IX
- **first_available:** ASOS Tyrion IX
- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing (Tyrion's tower cell)
- **location_secondary:** Throne room (trial), Castle Stokeworth (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** Days following Joffrey's death at his wedding feast; the trial spans three days within this chapter
- **time_markers:** Pod does not return by sunset or moonrise; Tyrion wakes at dawn; serving man returns an hour after breakfast; "three days hence" until trial; trial begins "come morning"; second day of trial; third day of trial ("on the morrow"); night visits from Oberyn after third day of testimony
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion, imprisoned and accused of Joffrey's murder, attempts to prepare his defense. Bronn refuses to champion him against Ser Gregor Clegane, having been offered marriage to Lollys Stokeworth. The trial proceeds over three days with witnesses including Kingsguard knights, the Kettleblacks, Grand Maester Pycelle, and Varys all testifying against Tyrion. Ser Kevan offers Tyrion the option of confessing in exchange for taking the black. On the final night, Prince Oberyn Martell visits Tyrion privately and offers to serve as his champion.

- **Sounds:** Steady wash of rain outside; nervous laughter in the throne room during trial
- **Smells:** Varys described as "smelling of rosewater"
- **Notable sensory details:** Tyrion sleeps in window seat and wakes "stiff and sore"; teeth clenched so hard they hurt; the hairs on the back of Tyrion's neck bristle when Oberyn hints at Tywin's mortality

## Events & Actions
1. **Ser Kevan informs Tyrion of trial arrangements** — Tywin, Mace Tyrell, and Oberyn Martell will judge; Cersei will name Gregor Clegane as her champion if trial by battle is demanded
2. **Tyrion sends Pod to fetch Bronn** — Promises gold; Pod departs
3. **Pod fails to return overnight** — Tyrion sleeps in window seat, wakes at dawn
4. **Tyrion writes Sansa's name on parchment** — His only potential witness; reflects that she likely poisoned Joffrey but recognizes she is still his wife
5. **Tyrion gives parchment to Ser Kevan** — Kevan notes trial begins in three days; Varys has offered 100 silver stags for word of Sansa, 100 gold dragons for the girl herself
6. **Bronn arrives with Pod** — Reveals he is betrothed to Lollys Stokeworth; refuses to fight Gregor Clegane as Tyrion's champion
7. **Bronn departs permanently** — Walks out of the door, the castle, and Tyrion's life
8. **Tyrion considers alternative champions** — Shagga (hiding in kingswood), Timett (gone to Mountains of the Moon), himself (impossible) — all rejected
9. **Ser Kevan reports Sansa not found** — Nor Ser Dontos, who vanished the same night
10. **Tyrion's sleepless night** — Counts his ghosts: Tysha, Sansa, Joffrey dying, Cersei's eyes, Bronn's smile, Shae's grin
11. **Trial Day 1 begins** — Ser Addam Marbrand and gold cloaks escort Tyrion to the throne room
12. **Ser Balon Swann testifies** — Believes Tyrion innocent but recounts him striking Joffrey during the riot
13. **Ser Meryn Trant testifies** — Expands on the riot incident; claims Tyrion threatened Joffrey with Aerys's fate
14. **Ser Boros Blount testifies** — Echoes the account; mentions Tyrion's threat to have him killed
15. **Tyrion interrupts and is warned** — Demands Blount tell what Joffrey was doing; rebuked by Lord Tywin
16. **Three Kettleblacks testify** — Osney and Osfryd recount threats to Cersei ("joy to ashes"); Osmund claims Joffrey said Tyrion meant to be king in his place
17. **Tyrion calls Osmund a liar and is restrained** — Gold cloaks drag him back; Tywin threatens chains
18. **Prince Oberyn comments on dwarfs** — Says he'll drown himself in wine the day he fears a dwarf's wrath
19. **Trial Day 1 ends** — Lord Tywin adjourns as hour grows late
20. **Tyrion requests Varys** — Asks Ser Kevan to send the eunuch; Kevan agrees
21. **Trial Day 2 begins** — Maesters Ballabar and Frenken testify that poison killed Joffrey
22. **Grand Maester Pycelle testifies** — Names his stolen poisons (greycap, nightshade, sweetsleep, demon's dance, blindeye, widow's blood, wolfsbane, basilisk venom, tears of Lys); names the strangler as the actual poison used; accuses Tyrion
23. **Tyrion interrupts again** — Questions Pycelle about whether any of the stolen poisons could choke off breath; Pycelle says no, only the strangler could, which was not found
24. **Pycelle accuses Tyrion directly** — Claims Tyrion "used it all" to kill Joffrey
25. **Tyrion loses his temper** — Declares Joffrey cruel and stupid; denies killing him; silenced by Tywin with threat of gag and chains
26. **Procession of noble witnesses** — Lord Redwyne, Lord Celtigar, Ser Flement Brax, serving men, a juggler, Lord Gyles, Ser Hobber Redwyne, Ser Philip Foote, Lady Merryweather, old Estermont, young Peckledon, Galyeon of Cuy, Morros and Jothos Slynt all testify
27. **Ser Kevan offers confession deal** — Take the black in exchange for admitting guilt; invokes Tywin's justice; delivers passionate defense of Tywin's character
28. **Tyrion references Eddard Stark** — Notes the same terms were offered to Ned, who was executed anyway
29. **Ser Kevan defends Tywin** — Describes their father Tytos as weak and mocked; says Tywin restored House Lannister and ruled the realm justly for twenty years under a mad king
30. **Trial Day 3 — Varys testifies** — Presents extensive documents; confirms theft of poisons, threats to Cersei, discussions about Tommen as king; confirms everything except the actual poisoning
31. **Cersei announces one final witness for the morrow** — Tywin permits it
32. **Prince Oberyn visits Tyrion's cell at night** — They drink wine together; Oberyn reveals Cersei has offered him marriage and hints she wants Tyrion's head as payment
33. **Oberyn tells the scorpion story** — Lord Tyrell left to rule Dorne was killed by scorpions in bed; the assassination sparked rebellion; implies he would rather face scorpions than Cersei naked
34. **Oberyn discusses Dornish succession law** — Suggests Myrcella could be crowned in Sunspear; raises the prospect of civil conflict
35. **Tyrion reveals truth about Elia's murder** — Names Ser Amory Lorch as Rhaenys's killer; names Gregor Clegane as Aegon's killer and Elia's rapist
36. **Oberyn offers to be Tyrion's champion** — States he can save Tyrion not as a judge but as his champion in trial by combat

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Desperate, angry, isolated, bitter, sardonic; oscillates between dark humor and genuine despair; moments of terror at his situation counterbalanced by characteristic wit
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding someone to champion him or prove his innocence; processing betrayal by Bronn; enduring the systematic dismantling of his reputation at trial
- **Key decisions made:** Decides not to confess despite Kevan's offer; writes Sansa as his only witness; accepts Bronn's departure without begging; accepts Oberyn's offer (implied by chapter ending)
- **Self-deception flags:** Lies to Oberyn about Tywin's responsibility for ordering the murders of Elia's children ("He spoke the lie without hesitation, and never stopped to ask himself why he should"); believes himself completely alone but underestimates Oberyn's motivation; tells himself Sansa "must have" poisoned Joffrey while also noting she could not have acted alone
