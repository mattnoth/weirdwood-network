- **chapter_summary:** Jaime observes Tommen signing royal documents including attainders against the Tullys, grants to Boltons and Freys, and pardons. He watches the false Arya Stark depart with Steelshanks Walton for the North. He reflects on Gregor Clegane's slow death from Oberyn's poisoned spear. Cersei visits his chambers seeking his help against Lord Tywin's plans but Jaime refuses her sexually and they quarrel bitterly. Finally, Jaime gives Brienne the Valyrian steel sword Oathkeeper (reforged from Ice) and tasks her with finding and protecting Sansa Stark, then writes in the White Book.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tommen signs royal documents** — Bills of attainder against Edmure and Brynden Tully; grant of Riverrun to Ser Emmon Frey and Lady Genna; legitimization decree for Ramsay Bolton; naming Roose Bolton Warden of the North; grant of Castamere to Ser Rolph Spicer; pardons for Westerlings, Bracken, Vance, Goodbrook, Mooton.
2. **Jaime departs the council chamber** — Leaves Ser Kevan to handle the rest; Ser Kevan urges him to reconcile with Tywin; Jaime refuses, angered by a mocking gift.
3. **False Arya departs with Steelshanks Walton** — A skinny girl disguised as Arya Stark rides out with the northmen, headed to wed Ramsay Bolton. Jaime internally notes she is not the real Arya and likely older.
4. **Jaime reflects on Gregor Clegane's condition** — Recalls Pycelle's report to the council about the Mountain's flesh mortifying, wounds oozing pus, veins turning black, leeches dying, and convulsions. Pycelle wanted to detain the Dornishmen to learn what poison Oberyn used.
5. **Jaime recalls Tywin's council orders** — Tywin refused to detain Dornishmen to avoid offending Sunspear; ordered Pycelle to heal Gregor so he can be executed by the King's Justice; discussed Stannis's disappearance from Dragonstone and possible moves on Storm's End or Dorne.
6. **Jaime enters White Sword Tower** — Notes Boros snoring, Balon's door shut. Reflects on his need for rest after last night's training.
7. **Cersei visits Jaime's bedchamber** — She is weeping; tells him Tywin has removed her from the council and plans to send her to Casterly Rock and remarry her. She begs Jaime to leave the Kingsguard to stop Tywin.
8. **Jaime and Cersei argue about their children** — Discuss Tommen's upcoming marriage to Margaery; Cersei insists Tommen is her son; Jaime notes he's never been acknowledged as father.
9. **Jaime and Cersei discuss the past** — Robert's death, the incident at Winterfell (pushing Bran), the dagger sent to kill Bran. Jaime deduces Joffrey sent the dagger, motivated by Robert's comment about mercy-killing cripples.
10. **Cersei begs Jaime physically** — She attempts to seduce him. Jaime pushes her away with his stump, refusing sex in the Lord Commander's chambers.
11. **Cersei and Jaime quarrel bitterly** — She questions his manhood, mocks him as a cripple, reveals she has lied to him a thousand times, tells him Tyrion's head will soon be taken. She storms out.
12. **Jaime reflects on his losses** — Notes he has lost a hand, father, son, sister, lover, and will lose a brother; wonders how House Lannister "won" the war.
13. **Jaime sends for Brienne** — Through Ser Boros, he tells Loras he's ready to see her. Hours pass before they arrive.
14. **Jaime and Loras discuss Renly's death** — Loras concedes it may have happened as Brienne says (that it was Stannis/a shadow). Jaime references Ser Cortnay Penrose's strange death.
15. **Jaime gives Brienne Oathkeeper** — He presents her the Valyrian steel sword reforged from Ice, asks her to name it Oathkeeper. Tells her the false Arya is not real, warns her not to attempt a rescue.
16. **Jaime tasks Brienne with finding Sansa Stark** — He reveals Cersei wants Sansa killed; charges Brienne to find Sansa first and protect her; tells her she will be defending Ned Stark's daughter with Ned Stark's steel.
17. **Brienne accepts and departs** — She vows to find Sansa and keep her safe, for Lady Catelyn's sake and for Jaime's.
18. **Jaime writes in the White Book** — At dusk, he writes his own entry documenting his defeat, captivity, maiming, and safe return by Brienne. Notes that more than three-quarters of his page remains blank for him to fill.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bored and frustrated during the council session; physically sore and demoralized from his failed training; conflicted about Cersei (desire warring with rejection); angry after their quarrel; feeling profound loss ("I've lost a hand, a father, a son, a sister, and a lover"); determined and wistful when giving Brienne her mission; reflective and purposeful when writing in the White Book
- **Primary preoccupation:** His inability to fight left-handed and what it means for his identity; his fractured relationships with Tywin and Cersei; his desire to fulfill his oath regarding the Stark girls despite everything
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to leave the Kingsguard despite Cersei's pleas; refuses Cersei sexually; gives Oathkeeper to Brienne; tasks Brienne with finding and protecting Sansa Stark; reveals his father's secrets (the false Arya) to Brienne; begins writing his own White Book entry
- **Self-deception flags:** Claims "Sansa Stark killed Joffrey" and Tyrion is protecting her out of gallantry — this theory may reflect Jaime's desire to exonerate his brother; thinks the real Arya is "buried in some unmarked grave" which is assumption not fact
