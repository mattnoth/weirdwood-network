# ASOS — Arya II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 14
- **pov_character:** Arya Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya II
- **first_available:** ASOS Arya II
- **location_primary:** Banks of the Trident (dead man's garden near a burned cottage); an inn on the Trident
- **location_secondary:** Harrenhal (referenced), Riverrun (referenced), Gulltown (referenced in song), Winterfell (referenced), Dorne (referenced), Lambswold (referenced), the Dreadfort (referenced via sigil)
- **approximate_timeline:** Early in ASOS; Arya, Gendry, and Hot Pie are traveling along the Trident after escaping Harrenhal, heading toward Riverrun. Their stolen food ran out six days prior.
- **time_markers:** Bread and cheese from Harrenhal gave out "six days ago"; daytime (characters foraging, sleeping in shade); enough daylight remaining to walk two miles/a league to the inn; fall season referenced ("A lot of fat as well, in fall")
- **chapter_summary:** Arya, Gendry, and Hot Pie are foraging in a dead man's garden along the Trident when they encounter three men: Tom Sevenstrings, Lem Lemoncloak, and Anguy the Archer, who claim to be king's men. The three persuade the children to accompany them to a nearby inn kept by Sharna and her husband. At the inn, Tom offers a parchment IOU for the children's horses, and Arya counter-proposes trading the horses for the inn's boat. Before the deal is resolved, a band of armed men arrives, and Arya recognizes Harwin, her father's man from Winterfell, who in turn identifies her as Arya Stark.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Not explicitly described; conditions allow outdoor foraging and travel without hardship
- **Season indicators:** Fall — "A lot of fat as well, in fall" (referring to bears); Arya's group has been traveling through woods and along the river
- **Time of day:** Daytime throughout; Gendry is sleeping in the shade at chapter start, and all action occurs in daylight
- **Lighting:** Natural daylight; firelight/hearth implied inside the inn (smoke curling from chimney)
- **Sounds:** Singing drifting up the river road; woodharp accompaniment; soft rush of the river; horse whickering; arrow thrumming "like a bee" after striking the willow trunk; singing of "The Bear and the Maiden Fair" during the walk; ambient inn sounds
- **Smells:** Cooking rabbit drifting from the inn kitchen, making Arya's mouth water
- **Notable sensory details:** Mud along the riverbank; reed beds in muddy shallows; the burned cottage with part of one wall still standing; cabbages falling with "soft thumps"; ale tasting good after days of drinking from brooks, puddles, and the muddy Trident; bread that is hard to chew, thick and lumpy, burned on the bottom; river water said to taste "of war, with all the dead men drifting downstream"

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya forages in a dead man's garden** — She is grubbing for vegetables near a burned cottage on the Trident when she hears singing approaching.
2. **Arya orders Hot Pie to wake Gendry** — She sends Hot Pie to rouse Gendry quietly while she hides.
3. **Arya takes position under the willow** — She draws her stolen longsword and kneels in the grass and mud beneath the trailing branches of a large willow, praying to the old gods to hide her.
4. **Three men arrive and discuss what's behind the wall** — Tom, Lem, and Anguy detect something behind the ruined cottage wall and debate shooting arrows over it.
5. **Arya reveals herself** — She springs up and shows her sword, warning them not to shoot. They see she is a girl.
6. **Gendry rides out** — He emerges from behind the cottage wall on horseback, in chainmail, with Hot Pie leading Arya's horse behind him.
7. **Introductions and false names** — Hot Pie gives his real name; Arya calls herself "Squab"; Gendry calls himself "the Bull." Tom introduces himself, Lem, and Anguy.
8. **Tom identifies them by the Bolton sigil** — He spots the flayed man on Arya's doublet and guesses they escaped from Lord Bolton's service.
9. **Hot Pie reveals their destination** — He blurts out they're looking for Riverrun, to Arya's dismay.
10. **Tom invites them to the inn** — He offers food and safety at an inn kept by friends, two miles upstream.
11. **The group travels to the inn** — They walk along the river road. Hot Pie sings "The Bear and the Maiden Fair" with Tom.
12. **Anguy shoots a duck** — Crossing a small brook, singing flushes a duck and Anguy shoots it in flight. Lem wades in to retrieve it.
13. **Arrival at the inn** — Arya notices the inn's dock and boat and considers sailing to Riverrun.
14. **They enter the inn and meet Sharna** — The tall, sharp-tongued innkeeper scolds Lem for tracking mud and takes charge.
15. **Husband appears from the cellar** — Sharna's husband comes up; she hands him the duck to hang.
16. **Sharna refuses to cook the duck with lemons** — She declares they'll eat rabbit instead (roast or stewed with ale and onions).
17. **Arya offers to trade vegetables for food** — She gives up the foraged carrots and cabbages.
18. **Ale and bread are served** — The serving boy brings tankards of ale and round loaves of burned, lumpy bread. Hot Pie critiques the bread.
19. **Husband and Tom argue about horses** — It emerges Husband sold three horses to travelers and got a skiff in exchange; Tom's group was supposed to intercept the buyers but missed them. The buyers were three: one a big woman dressed as a man, another in chains with unsettling eyes.
20. **Tom offers the parchment IOU** — He presents a dirty scrap of parchment promising three golden dragons for the children's horses, claiming it will be made good after the war.
21. **Arya counter-proposes trading horses for the boat** — She demands the skiff instead of paper money.
22. **Everyone laughs** — The inn erupts in laughter at Arya's proposal; even Arya begins to smile.
23. **Gendry warns of approaching riders** — He bursts in shouting about a dozen soldiers coming down the river road.
24. **Arya tries to draw her sword** — Lem grabs her wrist and twists until she releases the sword.
25. **Arya breaks Lem's nose** — She swings her ale tankard into his face, splashing ale in his eyes and breaking his nose, then bolts.
26. **Lem recaptures Arya** — His longer legs allow him to catch her; he holds her dangling while bleeding.
27. **Tom stops Gendry with a dagger** — When Gendry moves to help Arya, Tom draws a dagger and blocks him.
28. **The band of soldiers enters the inn** — A ragged but well-armed group: a large Tyroshi, crossbowmen with a wounded man, a one-eyed man, a yellow-haired archer, a spearman in a lion-crested helm, an older man with a limp, a Braavosi sellsword, and others.
29. **Arya recognizes Harwin** — She sees Hullen's son, her father's man from Winterfell, among the soldiers.
30. **Arya cries out and reveals herself** — She calls Harwin by name, weeping, desperately trying to be recognized.
31. **Harwin recognizes Arya** — After initial suspicion due to the flayed man sigil, he identifies her as "Arya Underfoot" and tells Lem to release her. He kneels before her and names her "Arya Stark, of Winterfell" and "the Hand's daughter."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fearful and wary throughout, constantly alert to danger. She fears the Bloody Mummers and Bolton's men. She mistrusts Tom and the outlaws, suspecting them of being robbers despite their hospitality. She feels anger when called "little" or "little girl." When she sees Harwin, she is overwhelmed with recognition and hope, breaking down in tears — "weeping like a baby, just like some stupid little girl."
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and reaching Riverrun. She is constantly evaluating threats and escape routes — hiding under the willow, assessing the archer's danger, noticing the boat at the dock and planning to steal or trade for it. Food is a secondary preoccupation; hunger nearly overrides her caution.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to hide under the willow while sending Gendry and Hot Pie behind the wall; decides to reveal herself rather than let arrows fly; decides to go to the inn despite suspicion; offers to trade horses for the boat; tries to fight when soldiers arrive; reveals herself to Harwin.
- **Self-deception flags:** None clearly present. Arya is remarkably clear-eyed about her situation. She acknowledges she cannot defeat the archer, recognizes the men as outlaws despite their claims, and understands they will take the horses regardless of what she says. Her one moment of self-consciousness — "weeping like a baby, just like some stupid little girl" — shows her resistance to her own vulnerability rather than self-deception.
