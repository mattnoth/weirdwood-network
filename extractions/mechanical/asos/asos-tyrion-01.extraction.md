- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion Lannister wakes in a dark chamber in Maegor's Holdfast, recovering from his battle wounds. Bronn visits, now knighted as Ser Bronn of the Blackwater, and updates Tyrion on the political situation: Ser Jacelyn Bywater is dead, the Hound has fled, the Kettleblacks have shifted their loyalty to Cersei, Tommen has been taken from Rosby, and Alayaya was whipped and cast out. Tyrion painfully makes his way to the Tower of the Hand to confront his father, encountering Ser Addam Marbrand along the way. In Tywin's solar, Tyrion asks for Casterly Rock as his rightful inheritance, and Tywin brutally refuses, denouncing Tyrion and threatening to hang any whore he finds in Tyrion's bed.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion wakes in his dark chamber** — He hears iron hinges creak and calls out, fearing another of Cersei's assassins in Maegor's Holdfast.
2. **Bronn arrives and reveals his knighthood** — Bronn shows off his new appearance and sigil: Ser Bronn of the Blackwater, knighted by Lord Tywin's command. He has a burning chain sigil on a smoke-grey field.
3. **Bronn reports the Hound's disappearance** — Sandor Clegane turned craven and fled during the battle; not dead, only gone. Gold cloaks say Tyrion led a sortie in his place.
4. **Bronn reports Ser Jacelyn Bywater's death** — When Cersei sent the Kettleblacks to fetch Joffrey back, gold cloaks began to flee. Bywater tried to stop them and was shot through the neck with an arrow, then dragged off his horse and killed.
5. **Bronn reports Addam Marbrand given command of the City Watch** — Lord Tywin appointed the westerman to replace Bywater.
6. **Bronn reports the Stone Crows remain in the kingswood** — Shagga has taken a fancy to the place. Timett led Burned Men home. Chella and Black Ears were chased off by Tywin's red cloaks.
7. **Bronn reports Alayaya was whipped and released** — Eight or nine days ago, she was tied to a post, scourged, and shoved out the gate naked and bloody. Tyrion is anguished.
8. **Bronn reports Tommen taken from Rosby** — Cersei sent the Kettleblacks after Tommen once she learned Bywater was dead. No one at Rosby opposed them.
9. **Bronn reports the Kettleblacks' loyalty has shifted** — Osney and Osfryd were knighted; Cersei is paying more than Tyrion can.
10. **Bronn reports on the battle's end** — Stannis defeated by what appeared to be Renly's ghost in green armor; Stannis's Lyseni galleys rescued survivors from the bay shore.
11. **Tyrion, Bronn, and Pod leave Maegor's Holdfast** — Tyrion dresses painfully, drinks dreamwine, and descends the stairs with Pod and a walking stick.
12. **They cross the drawbridge** — Ser Meryn Trant initially objects (queen's orders) but lowers it at the mention of Lord Tywin. Ser Osmund Kettleblack greets Tyrion on the other side.
13. **Bronn carries Tyrion up the serpentine steps** — Tyrion cannot climb them himself and swallows his pride.
14. **They traverse the outer ward** — Crowded with Tyrell, Rowan, and Redwyne tents and pavilions for the wedding. Pod explains they're for King Joffrey's wedding.
15. **Tyrion encounters Ser Addam Marbrand at the Tower of the Hand** — Marbrand reports the City Watch is down to forty-four hundred men; Cersei forbids dismissals; Tywin insists on finding Tyrek Lannister.
16. **Tyrion enters Tywin's solar in the Tower of the Hand** — Tywin dismisses Bronn and Pod. Tyrion notes the chill in the room and recognizes something is wrong.
17. **Tyrion and Tywin discuss the military situation** — Stannis cannot be assailed without Redwyne's fleet; northmen under Tallhart and Glover heading to Duskendale will be caught between Tarly and Gregor Clegane.
18. **Tyrion and Tywin discuss Littlefinger's appointment** — Baelish made Lord of Harrenhal for his service arranging the Tyrell marriage. Tyrion warns Littlefinger does nothing without reason.
19. **Tyrion and Tywin discuss the Dornish alliance** — Myrcella safe at Sunspear; Prince Doran promised a council seat and justice/vengeance. Tywin is uneasy about promising vengeance against Gregor Clegane.
20. **Tyrion asks for Casterly Rock** — He argues Jaime forfeited his claim by joining the Kingsguard eighteen years ago. Tywin refuses with the word "Never."
21. **Tywin denounces Tyrion** — Tywin accuses Tyrion of killing his mother, calls him ill-made, devious, and spiteful, and says he cannot prove Tyrion is not his son. He references Tyrion's whores.
22. **Tywin threatens Tyrion** — He warns Tyrion he is done with whores and that the next one found in his bed will be hanged.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitter, angry, fearful, weak, humiliated. Oscillates between dark humor and genuine anguish. Grows increasingly desperate and defiant through the chapter, culminating in the devastating confrontation with Tywin. Feels shame at his physical weakness. Rage at Alayaya's treatment. Guilt that his carelessness endangered her.
- **Primary preoccupation:** His loss of power — the Tower of the Hand taken, his men dismissed, his allies scattered, his hostage removed, his hirelings bought off. Everything he built as acting Hand has been stripped while he lay wounded. Secondary preoccupation: proving Cersei ordered Ser Mandon to kill him.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to confront Tywin despite weakness; decides to ask for Casterly Rock as his inheritance; retains Bronn as his man.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion admits he always knew Tywin would refuse Casterly Rock ("I knew the answer before I asked... I must always have known") yet asked anyway, suggesting he could not fully accept the reality of his father's rejection until it was made explicit. He also internally minimizes his role in endangering Alayaya ("In his carelessness, he had never thought what the role might cost her") — acknowledging fault but framing it as mere thoughtlessness.
