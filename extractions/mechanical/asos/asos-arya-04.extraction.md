- **chapter_summary:** Arya travels with the Brotherhood Without Banners through the Riverlands, stopping at Lord Lychester's ruined keep, a hidden tree village, the burned village of Sallydance, and High Heart as they search for Lord Beric Dondarrion. At High Heart, a tiny old dwarf woman shares prophetic dreams with the outlaws in exchange for a song. The group arrives at Acorn Hall where Lady Smallwood provides hospitality, bathing Arya and dressing her in girl's clothes. Over supper, the outlaws learn that Thoros and Beric were at Acorn Hall a fortnight past and that the Kingslayer has escaped—with a rumor that Lady Catelyn freed him. Arya and Gendry visit the smithy where Gendry recounts Thoros's history with flaming swords and his apprenticeship under Master Mott.

## Events & Actions
1. **Visit to Lord Lychester's keep** — The group arrives at a half-ruined square keep. The old knight Lord Lychester can only mutter about fighting Ser Maynard on a bridge. His young maester tells them Beric was reportedly hanged by the Lannisters near the Gods Eye half a year ago.
2. **Lem disputes Beric's death** — Lem says Thoros cut Beric down before he died. The maester suggests asking the Lady of the Leaves.
3. **Crossing the bridge** — The next morning they cross a stone bridge behind the keep. Tom muses that Lord Lychester would be as famous as the Dragonknight if he'd kept a singer. Lem reveals Lychester's sons died in Robert's Rebellion on both sides.
4. **Arrival at the tree village** — Three days later, Jack-Be-Lucky blows a horn signal and rope ladders unroll from trees. They climb to a hidden village of rope walkways and moss-covered houses concealed in the branches.
5. **Meeting the Lady of the Leaves** — She reports Beric is dead: the Mountain drove a dagger through his eye. A begging brother brought the news. Lem and Jack dispute this, saying losing an eye won't kill a man.
6. **Jack-Be-Lucky's family history** — Jack shares that his father was hanged by Lord Piper's bailiff, brother Wat was sent to the Wall, and the Lannisters killed his other brothers. Losing an eye is "nothing" by comparison.
7. **Lady of the Leaves is reassured** — She blesses Lem for the good tidings and invokes the Warrior and "the red priest."
8. **Shelter at Sallydance** — They find shelter in the scorched shell of a sept in a burned village. The aged septon describes northmen who looted the sept and hacked apart the statues of the Seven, looking for the Kingslayer.
9. **Arya reacts to mention of northmen** — She chews her lip and feels Gendry watching her, feeling angry and ashamed.
10. **Brotherhood men at Sallydance** — A dozen men live in the sept vault. Their leader wears soot-blackened armor with a crude lightning bolt on his cloak, but even he has no word of Beric. Greenbeard tells Arya "the lightning lord is everywhere and nowhere."
11. **Arya asserts her age** — She tells Greenbeard she's "not a squirrel" and will be one-and-ten soon. He jokes about marrying her; she slaps his hand away.
12. **Evening at Sallydance** — Lem and Gendry play tiles. Tom sings about Big Belly Ben and the High Septon's goose. Anguy lets Arya try his longbow but she cannot draw it; he offers to make her a lighter bow from seasoned wood at Riverrun.
13. **Tom reveals ransom plan** — Tom lets slip they would only go to Riverrun to collect Arya's ransom, warning about Lord Hoster hanging outlaws and his son who "hates music."
14. **Tom's history with Lord Hoster's son** — Lem, Tom, and Anguy banter about a song Tom wrote about a "floppy fish" incident involving a wench and Lord Hoster's son.
15. **Arya asks about ransom** — Harwin confirms they need horses, armor, swords, shields, spears, and seed for planting. He says "Winter is coming, remember?" and assures her she won't be the first highborn captive ransomed.
16. **Arya worries about ransom** — She fears Robb might not pay, wonders if her mother would still want her back "after all the things she'd done."
17. **Arrival at High Heart** — The next day they ride to a hill so lofty Arya feels she can see half the world. Around its brow stand 31 huge pale weirwood stumps. Arya and Gendry count them; some are wide enough to use as a bed.
18. **Tom explains High Heart's history** — Sacred to the children of the forest; some of their magic lingers. "No harm can ever come to those as sleep here." Smallfolk shun it as haunted by ghosts of children killed when Erreg the Kinslayer cut down the grove.
19. **Arya's memories of Winterfell** — She recalls hiding in the crypts and playing games among the stone kings.
20. **Storm at High Heart** — A storm wakes Arya at night. The wind pulls her coverlet away. She goes after it and hears voices.
21. **The ghost of High Heart appears** — Arya discovers Tom, Lem, and Greenbeard talking to a tiny old dwarf woman by the campfire embers. The woman says "The old gods stir and will not let me sleep."
22. **The dwarf woman shares her prophetic dreams** — Three visions: (1) a shadow with a burning heart butchering a golden stag; (2) a man without a face on a swaying bridge with a drowned crow with seaweed on its wings perched on his shoulder; (3) a roaring river and a dead woman who was a fish, drifting with red tears on her cheeks, whose eyes opened. She demands gifts for her dreams.
23. **Lem dismisses the dreams** — He grumbles about useless dreams and says he dreamt of kissing a tavern wench. The dwarf woman retorts "The wench is dead. Only worms may kiss her now."
24. **Tom plays for the dwarf woman** — She demands her song or they must leave. Tom plays something "so soft and sad" that Arya only hears snatches. The tune is "half-familiar." Arya thinks Sansa would know it.
25. **Morning departure from High Heart** — The dwarf woman is gone. Tom explains she's "only an old dwarf woman," not a ghost, but "queer" and "evil-eyed." She "knows things she has no business knowing." She always makes Tom sing the same song. Tom says they now "have the scent."
26. **Harwin explains Brotherhood strategy** — Beric moves constantly, never shares plans, so no one can betray him. Hundreds or thousands are sworn to him, scattered in small bands to strike in many places. When captured and put to the question, they can't reveal his location.
27. **Arya recounts the Tickler's methods** — She describes the standard questions ("Is there gold hidden in the village?") and the changing tortures at the Gods Eye village. She recalls the shrieks and the stench.
28. **Harwin responds to the Tickler account** — He says "No child should be made to suffer that" and reports the Mountain lost half his men at Stone Mill. He says the war began when the Hand sent Beric to bring king's justice to Gregor Clegane.
29. **Arrival at Acorn Hall** — At dusk they ford a brook and reach Acorn Hall with its stone curtain walls and great oaken keep. The master is away fighting for Lord Vance. Lady Smallwood, an old friend and rumored former lover of Tom, welcomes them.
30. **Lady Smallwood scolds the outlaws** — She gives them a tongue-lashing for dragging a young girl through the war, becoming more wroth when Lem reveals Arya is highborn. She worries about the Bolton badge getting Arya hanged.
31. **Arya is bathed and dressed** — Lady Smallwood has Arya bathed in scalding water, scrubbed hard, and doused with scented stuff. She's dressed in brown stockings, a linen shift, and a green gown embroidered with acorns.
32. **"Needlework" exchange** — Lady Smallwood asks what Arya likes to do. Arya says "Needlework," meaning her sword Needle. Lady Smallwood misunderstands and speaks of it as a restful activity. Arya says she lost Needle and her new one "isn't as good."
33. **Lady Smallwood's assessment** — She tells Arya she doesn't know who she is but suspects "someone important." She wishes she could keep Arya safe but has walls and too few men. She says in these times it's better to be insignificant.
34. **Arya's internal identity** — She thinks "I'm not a lady... I'm a wolf."
35. **Supper at Acorn Hall** — Gendry laughs at Arya in her dress until Harwin thwacks him. The meal is mutton and mushrooms, brown bread, pease pudding, and baked apples with yellow cheese.
36. **Lady Smallwood reports on Beric** — Thoros and Beric were at Acorn Hall not a fortnight past, driving sheep. Thoros gave her three sheep, claiming that as a priest he knew how to tend a flock.
37. **Tom and Lady Smallwood's tension** — She rebukes him for his songs and for getting milkmaids pregnant ("all drinking tansy tea"). Tom admits he has passed seven sons "many years ago."
38. **Karstark men hunting the Kingslayer** — Lady Smallwood reports "a pack of wolves" came to her gates hunting Jaime Lannister. They wore black with a white sun badge. She joked she had Ser Jaime in her bed and drove them off with quarrels. She believes they went to Blackbottom Bend.
39. **Arya identifies the northmen** — She recognizes the white sun on black as the sigil of Lord Karstark. She thinks they are Robb's men and considers escaping to join them.
40. **Rumor of Catelyn freeing Jaime** — Lady Smallwood reports the Karstark men claimed Lady Catelyn set the Kingslayer free. Tom snaps a string in shock. Arya thinks "It's not true... It couldn't be true."
41. **Arya sent away** — Harwin says such talk is not for her ears. The outlaws insist she leave. She stalks off angry, tempted to break her promise not to run away.
42. **Gendry and Arya visit the smithy** — Gendry lights a candle and examines tools. He recalls Thoros visiting Tobho Mott's forge, buying cheap swords to dip in wildfire. Master Mott called it "an alchemist's trick." Thoros could outdrink King Robert; they were "pease in a pod."
43. **Gendry's unfinished sword** — Master Mott said it was time for Gendry's first longsword and gave him sweet steel, but Yoren took him for the Night's Watch before he could finish.
44. **Thoros at the siege of Pyke** — Gendry recounts that when Pyke's walls crashed down, Thoros was first through the breach with a flaming sword, setting ironmen afire.
45. **Gendry compliments Arya** — He says she looks "like a proper little girl" and "a nice oak tree" and that she smells nice. Arya shoves him, they wrestle and roughhouse across the smithy floor. She knees him and wrenches free. Her acorn dress sleeve is torn.
46. **Return to the hall** — Tom is singing "My Featherbed." Harwin and Anguy laugh. Lem clouts Gendry and tells him to keep his hands off her. Arya says she started it. Harwin confirms she was "much the same at Winterfell."
47. **Second bath and dress** — Lady Smallwood insists on another bath and puts Arya in a lilac dress with baby pearls, too delicate to ride in.
48. **Morning departure** — Lady Smallwood gives Arya her dead son's riding clothes (breeches, belt, tunic, brown doeskin jerkin with iron studs). Her son died at age seven. Arya apologizes for tearing the acorn dress. Lady Smallwood tells her "You are [pretty]. Be brave."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Oscillates between anger, shame, defiance, and vulnerability. Angry when excluded from adult conversations and when told lies about her mother. Ashamed when northmen are mentioned (connection to her identity). Uncomfortable being dressed as a girl but touched by Lady Smallwood's kindness. Feels the tension between wanting to belong and wanting to escape.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding out when she'll be taken to her family; worrying whether Robb and her mother would want her back; processing the rumor about Catelyn freeing the Kingslayer; maintaining her identity as a wolf, not a lady.
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses not to run away despite temptation (she promised Harwin); considers escaping to the Karstark men but does not act on it; engages in roughhousing with Gendry rather than accepting the role of proper lady.
- **Self-deception flags:** None detected. Arya is remarkably clear-eyed, though she refuses to believe the rumor about her mother freeing Jaime ("It's not true... It couldn't be true").
