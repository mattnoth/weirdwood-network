## Chapter Metadata

- **location_secondary:** The Twins (castle, dark stone bridge connecting west and east banks), feast tents (the camp behind them), the river
- **approximate_timeline:** During Edmure Tully's wedding feast at the Twins; simultaneous with the massacre inside
- **time_markers:** Nighttime (darkness, torchlight, gloom); the feast is underway when the chapter opens; events unfold rapidly over minutes
- **chapter_summary:** Arya and the Hound approach the Twins' gatehouse after the feast has begun. As the portcullis rises and drawbridge lowers, armored Frey riders pour out of the castle while the feast tents collapse and burn behind them. Arya recognizes "The Rains of Castamere" being played from both castles. Three Frey riders attack; the Hound kills all three. He tells Arya her brother is dead and urges her to flee. Arya instead runs for the castle gate, and the Hound strikes her in the back of the head with an axe to stop her.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya and the Hound approach the Twins gatehouse** — They move past the feast tents through wet clay and torn grass, into darkness toward the castle.
2. **Arya observes the castle is open** — Despite the sergeant saying it would be closed, the portcullis is being raised and drawbridge has been lowered. Arya feels anxious relief.
3. **The wayn gets stuck in mud** — The left wheel sinks in soft mud, tilting the wayn. The Hound shoves Arya off and she lands in mud. He retrieves a hidden swordbelt from under the wayn seat.
4. **Armored Frey riders pour from the castle gate** — A column of riders in plate armor with longaxes and torches crosses the drawbridge through the flooded river.
5. **A wolf howls in the distance** — Arya hears it through the camp noise, music, and river. The sound "shivered through Arya like a knife, sharp with rage and grief." She senses it may not be her ears that heard it.
6. **The feast tents collapse and burn** — The middle tent collapses first, then fire arrows ignite the others. The heavy oiled cloth settles on the men inside. Dark armored shapes move before the flames.
7. **Arya recognizes "The Rains of Castamere"** — The same song plays from both castles. She recalls Tom o' Sevens singing it at the brewhouse with the outlaws.
8. **Three Frey riders break from the column to attack** — They ride through mud and reeds toward the wayn.
9. **The Hound engages the riders on Stranger** — He cuts Stranger loose from the wayn, mounts, and charges the three destriers.
10. **The Hound fights three Frey riders** — He turns aside the first longaxe; the second rider lands a glancing blow that rips his blouse and exposes mail; the Hound kills all three — one with the axe through the back of the head, one trapped beneath his dying horse drowning, one killed with a broken sword through the throat.
11. **Arya throws a rock at a Frey rider** — When the third rider charges her, she throws a mud-slimy rock, hitting his temple and breaking his charge. She circles the wayn to evade him before the Hound kills him from behind.
12. **Arya retrieves the Hound's dog helm** — She finds it in a sack of dried apples in the wayn and tosses it to him. He puts it on, transforming into "a steel dog."
13. **The Hound declares Robb is dead** — He tells Arya bluntly that her brother is dead and urges her to look at the massacre.
14. **The camp becomes a battlefield** — Arya sees knights riding down running men, burning tents, a catapult flinging oil or pitch. She hears only a solitary drum beating slow monotonous beats.
15. **The Hound urges Arya to flee** — He offers his hand and says they must get away. Arya insists Robb and her mother are in the castle.
16. **The Hound calls Arya "stupid little bitch" and warns her** — He says she won't come out if she goes in, and that Frey might let her kiss her mother's corpse. He calls her "she-wolf."
17. **Arya runs for the castle gate** — She spins away from the Hound and sprints for the portcullis, which is slowly lowering. The drawbridge is rising.
18. **The Hound chases her on Stranger** — He rides after her through the mud and water.
19. **The Hound strikes Arya with the axe** — His axe takes her in the back of the head, ending the chapter.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Begins anxious but hopeful (the castle is open); shifts to confusion (not understanding the collapsing tents); then to horror and panic as she realizes it's a massacre; grief and rage when hearing the wolf howl; desperation when told her brother and mother are dead; terror when running from the Hound at chapter's end
- **Primary preoccupation:** Reaching her mother and brother inside the Twins; clinging to hope that they can be saved even as evidence mounts that they're dead
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to run for the castle gate despite the Hound's warning; refuses to flee with him
- **Self-deception flags:** "Rain. It's only rain. That's all it is." — denying that she is crying; insists "We're here" and "The gate's even open" despite overwhelming evidence of the trap; tells herself "Maybe we can save her" in the face of the Hound's blunt assessment
