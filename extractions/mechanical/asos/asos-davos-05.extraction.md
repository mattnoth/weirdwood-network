# ASOS — Davos V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 55
- **pov_character:** Davos Seaworth
- **pov_chapter_number:** Davos V
- **first_available:** ASOS Davos V
- **location_primary:** Dragonstone (castle)
- **location_secondary:** Stone Drum (chamber of the Painted Table), castle yard, Sea Dragon Tower (maester's chambers below the rookery)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after news of the Red Wedding reaches Dragonstone; Davos has been serving as Hand for some time and taking daily reading lessons with Maester Pylos
- **time_markers:** "every day" (Davos's reading lessons and Edric asking after Stannis); "last night" (Stannis gazing into the hearth and seeing a vision); "good morrow" (lesson scene implies morning); daily routine of Shireen and Edric playing together in Aegon's Garden; Devan described as "not yet twelve"; reference to how long since the Blackwater battle (imprecise)
- **chapter_summary:** News of Robb Stark's death at the Red Wedding reaches Dragonstone. Melisandre, Queen Selyse, and Ser Axell Florent press Stannis to sacrifice Edric Storm to "wake the stone dragon," claiming king's blood is needed. Davos argues against kinslaying and challenges Melisandre's leech magic, noting only two of three named kings are dead. Stannis agonizes over duty versus the cost of sacrificing his nephew, asking Davos "what is the life of one bastard boy against a kingdom?" to which Davos answers "Everything." Later, during a reading lesson with Maester Pylos, Davos discovers a Night's Watch letter warning that the King-beyond-the-Wall marches south and Lord Mormont is feared slain — and decides not to show it to Stannis or Melisandre.

- **Lighting:** Not explicitly described

## Events & Actions
1. **Salladhor Saan reports the Red Wedding** — Saan tells Stannis that Robb Stark has been murdered at a wedding. The smallfolk call it "the Red Wedding." Lord Frey had Robb's head cut off, his direwolf's head sewn in its place, and a crown nailed around the ears. Catelyn Stark was slain and thrown naked in the river.
2. **Ser Axell and Queen Selyse attribute the deaths to R'hllor** — Axell declares it was "the Lord's wrath" and "the hand of R'hllor." Selyse praises the Lord of Light.
3. **Stannis dismisses divine intervention** — Stannis says it sounds more like Walder Frey's handiwork than any god's.
4. **Stannis plans to send envoys** — Stannis tells Saan he will need his fastest ships to carry envoys to the Iron Islands and White Harbor, offering full pardons to those who repent of treason and swear fealty.
5. **Melisandre warns of more false kings** — She tells Stannis she has seen in the flames that more false kings will rise to take up the crowns of the dead.
6. **The queen's faction urges sacrifice of Edric Storm** — Melisandre asks Stannis to give her "the boy" for R'hllor to fulfill ancient prophecy and wake the stone dragon. Axell kneels begging. Selyse kneels on both knees, calling Edric the foul fruit of Robert and Delena's fornication, and begging Stannis to lift the boy's shadow from her womb so she can bear trueborn sons.
7. **Stannis resists but does not refuse** — He says the boy is his own blood and was not at fault, but does not outright refuse.
8. **Melisandre presses further** — She says the Lord of Light cherishes the innocent and that from king's blood and untainted fire, a dragon shall be born. Stannis does not pull away from her touch (unlike from Selyse's).
9. **Stannis reminisces about childhood visit to court** — He recalls visiting the royal court with Robert as a small child, being impressed by what they thought was the king but was actually Tywin Lannister serving as Hand. He reflects on dragon skulls Robert later took down.
10. **Davos intervenes** — Davos speaks up, invoking the Warrior for courage. He argues that kinslaying is the worst curse in the eyes of gods and men.
11. **Davos challenges Melisandre's leech magic** — He points out that only two of three named kings are dead, and the leeches have killed neither: Robb was murdered by Walder Frey, Balon fell from a bridge. "Even an onion smuggler knows two onions from three."
12. **Stannis laughs and agrees** — Stannis snorts with laughter, telling Melisandre "He has you there, my lady. Two is not three."
13. **Melisandre counters with Joffrey** — She argues that if Joffrey dies surrounded by power, armies, and Kingsguard, it would prove R'hllor's power.
14. **Stannis dismisses the queen's faction** — He tells them to go, repeating "Two is not three."
15. **Davos and Stannis discuss Edric privately** — Davos mentions Shireen plays with Edric daily; Stannis says Edric looks like Robert and worships him. Stannis grows angry at Davos's persistence.
16. **Stannis's agonized monologue** — Stannis speaks of duty, prophecy, Melisandre's visions, and his own vision of a king whose crown of fire consumed him to ash. He asks: "What is the life of one bastard boy against a kingdom?"
17. **Davos answers "Everything"** — Stannis tells Davos to go before he talks himself back into the dungeon.
18. **Davos exits to the castle yard** — He smells salt in the air, feels the east wind, and thinks of sailing home to Marya and his two small children. He observes Dragonstone's dragon-shaped architecture and wonders if the stone dragons were once real.
19. **Salladhor Saan joins Davos in the yard** — Saan jokes about the stone dragons coming to life and collapsing the castle. He mentions forgiving but not forgetting the lost plunder from Claw Isle, and warns Davos about the queen's men and his dangerous rise.
20. **Davos reflects on his secret allies** — He has been recruiting "king's men" (as opposed to queen's men): Ser Gerald Gower, Ser Andrew Estermont, and the Bastard of Nightsong, among others. He chose them carefully as a smuggler picks crews.
21. **Davos climbs Sea Dragon Tower** — He reflects on his inadequacy as Hand, recalling conversations with Pylos about the qualities of good and bad Hands throughout history.
22. **Davos encounters Patchface** — The fool waits outside the maester's door, muttering "Under the sea the old fish eat the young fish."
23. **Davos joins the reading lesson** — He finds Shireen, Devan, and Edric finishing their lesson on King Daeron's Conquest of Dorne.
24. **Edric tells of Robert's three victories at Summerhall** — Robert defeated Lords Grandison, Cafferen, and Fell one by one before they could join forces. He slew Lord Fell in single combat and captured Silveraxe. The defeated lords became his men.
25. **Pylos gently corrects Edric** — Notes Robert suffered defeats too, including Ashford against Lord Tyrell and many tournament tilts.
26. **Children depart; Davos begins his reading lesson** — He refuses the Conquest of Dorne and asks for a letter instead.
27. **Davos reads the Night's Watch letter** — He struggles through a letter sealed with black wax, addressed to the five kings, warning that the King-beyond-the-Wall comes south with a vast host of wildlings, that Lord Mormont sent a raven from the Haunted Forest, that he is under attack, and that subsequent birds arrived with no words. They fear Mormont slain with all his strength.
28. **Pylos reveals the letter was ignored** — He brought it to Lord Alester (then the Hand), who dismissed it, saying Stannis lacked men to waste on wildlings. The queen was told but Stannis never saw it.
29. **Davos decides not to share the letter** — He tells Pylos not to bring it to Stannis or Melisandre, troubled by thoughts of Melisandre's warnings about the cold and the night that never ends, and Stannis's vision of torches in the snow with terror all around.
30. **Davos connects the prophecy to Edric** — He thinks of Azor Ahai tempering Lightbringer by thrusting it through Nissa Nissa's heart, and wonders if Edric must play that role.
31. **Davos asks for a different letter** — He calls this one "too troubling" and asks Pylos for another.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fearful and determined; Davos is terrified of Melisandre's power (remembers the shadow-birth) and alarmed at the prospect of Edric Storm's sacrifice, but resolves to oppose it. He also feels inadequate as Hand — can't read, isn't highborn, doesn't know ruling. He misses his wife Marya and his two small surviving children. He takes pleasure in Devan's advancement and grieves for Dale and his other lost sons.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Protecting Edric Storm from sacrifice; wrestling with the Night's Watch letter and its implications about the threat from the north; his secret cultivation of "king's men" allies as counterweight to the queen's men
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Argues against Edric's sacrifice despite risking the dungeon; (2) Decides NOT to show the Night's Watch letter to Stannis or Melisandre, fearing what Melisandre might do with the information; (3) Continues building his network of king's men allies
- **Self-deception flags:** Davos tells Edric that Stannis fares "well" when the king actually has a haggard, haunted look. He tells himself "He will not do it" about Stannis harming Edric, though the chapter shows Stannis clearly wavering. He also rationalizes withholding the Night's Watch letter.
