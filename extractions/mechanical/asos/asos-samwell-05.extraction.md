# ASOS — Samwell V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 79
- **pov_character:** Samwell Tarly
- **pov_chapter_number:** Samwell V
- **first_available:** ASOS Samwell V
- **location_primary:** Castle Black
- **location_secondary:** The Shieldhall, The Lance, the yard
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after Stannis's victory over the wildlings; the Night's Watch has been voting for Lord Commander for ten days without result
- **time_markers:** "It has only been ten days" (since voting began); morning (breakfast scene with Stannis); daytime (sunlight streaming through window); choosing is set for tonight
- **chapter_summary:** King Stannis summons the Night's Watch officers to demand they choose a Lord Commander immediately, threatening to impose one himself. He privately questions Sam about the dragonglass dagger that killed an Other and announces his intent to mine obsidian on Dragonstone and to take the Nightfort as his seat. After Maester Aemon examines Lightbringer and privately notes to Sam that the sword produces no heat, Aemon subtly encourages Sam to influence the election. Sam visits both Cotter Pyke and Ser Denys Mallister, arguing each should withdraw in favor of a third candidate (Jon Snow, unnamed but clearly described), using a lie that Stannis intends to impose the other if they fail to choose.

## Events & Actions
1. **Night's Watch officers summoned** — Stannis summons all LC contenders (plus Bowen Marsh) to kneel before him; keeps them kneeling for an extraordinarily long time
2. **Stannis demands LC election conclude** — Expresses displeasure at ten days without a two-thirds vote; threatens consequences if not resolved tonight
3. **Janos Slynt fawns and suggests royal guidance** — Slynt flatters Stannis and suggests the king should guide the brothers' choice; this outrages other officers
4. **Stannis denounces Slynt's corruption** — Reveals Slynt sold places and promotions in the City Watch, profited from bribes; Jon Arryn had proof; two witnesses died on their rounds; Littlefinger arranged it to profit the crown
5. **Maester Aemon reminds Stannis of Night's Watch law** — Past crimes wiped clean when a man takes the black
6. **Ser Denys asserts Watch independence** — Insists the Watch takes no part in wars of the Seven Kingdoms; Stannis agrees not to ask them to fight his enemies
7. **Stannis demands castles and the Gift** — Wants all abandoned castles for his garrisons (Watch keeps Eastwatch, Castle Black, Shadow Tower); wants the Gift lands; demands nightfires before gates
8. **Melisandre declares war for the dawn** — Proclaims the true war is against darkness; Stannis is Azor Ahai reborn; references the red comet and Lightbringer
9. **Maester Aemon asks about the prince that was promised** — Responds to Melisandre's speech; asks where the prince that was promised is
10. **Stannis dismisses all but Sam and Aemon** — Keeps Sam to discuss the Other he killed
11. **Stannis questions Sam about the Other and dragonglass** — Confirms Sam killed it with obsidian; Melisandre calls it "frozen fire" in Old Valyrian; distinguishes between Others (vulnerable to dragonglass) and wights (vulnerable to steel and fire)
12. **Stannis reveals obsidian mining plan** — Describes deposits on Dragonstone (black, green, red, purple); has ordered Ser Rolland to begin mining
13. **Sam clarifies dragonglass limitations** — Notes the dagger shattered against a wight
14. **Stannis asks about the Black Gate** — Sam confirms passing beneath the Wall through it; Stannis announces his intent to seat himself at the Nightfort and demands Sam show him the gate
15. **Maester Aemon asks to "see" Lightbringer** — Stannis draws the sword; room fills with shimmering radiance; Sam describes it to Aemon
16. **Aemon notes Lightbringer produces no heat** — After leaving, asks Sam if he felt heat; notes the scabbard is unburned; concludes it is not what it seems
17. **Aemon subtly encourages Sam to act** — When Sam asks if he could do something about the election, Aemon smiles and asks "Could you?"
18. **Sam visits Cotter Pyke in the Shieldhall** — Asks Pyke to withdraw for Mallister; Pyke refuses vehemently; disparages Mallister as not a fighter
19. **Sam proposes a third candidate to Pyke** — Describes Jon Snow (unnamed) as a fighter, bastard-born; Pyke is intrigued; Sam lies that Stannis means to force Ser Denys on them
20. **Sam visits Ser Denys Mallister in the Lance** — Asks Mallister to withdraw for Pyke; Mallister refuses; disparages Pyke as lowborn pirate
21. **Sam proposes a third candidate to Mallister** — Describes Jon Snow (unnamed) as castle-born, trusted by Mormont, Noye, and the Halfhand, "his father was a lord, and his brother a king"; Mallister says "mayhaps"
22. **Sam lies to Mallister** — Claims Stannis told Aemon he would name Cotter Pyke if they don't choose tonight
23. **Sam lies to Pyke** — Claims Stannis told Aemon he would force Ser Denys on them if they don't choose tonight

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious at the audience with Stannis; determined but frightened when campaigning for Jon; trembling after lying to both commanders; bolstered by memories of his journey
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting Jon Snow elected Lord Commander by manipulating Pyke and Mallister into withdrawing
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to intervene in the election after Aemon's subtle encouragement; chooses to lie to both commanders (telling each that Stannis will impose the other); decides to approach Pyke first "while his courage was still hot"
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself "I am braver now" — acknowledges this must be true; his lie to Pyke about Aemon sending him ("that wasn't truly a lie") is self-justifying
