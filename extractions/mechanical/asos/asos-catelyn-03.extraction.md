# ASOS — Catelyn III

- **location_primary:** Riverrun

- **approximate_timeline:** Early-to-mid ASOS; follows the freeing of Jaime Lannister and the Karstark retaliation
- **time_markers:** Woken in "the black of night"; events begin at night by torchlight; "When day broke, grey and chilly"; rain all morning; Maester Vyman visits "that afternoon"; Jeyne Westerling comes "at evenfall"; the rain continues through the chapter
- **chapter_summary:** Lord Rickard Karstark and his men murder the captive squires Tion Frey and Willem Lannister in their cell at Riverrun. Robb confronts Karstark, orders the accomplices hanged, and sentences Karstark to death. The Blackfish reports the entire Karstark mounted force (~300 riders) has deserted in the night. Despite Edmure's pleas for clemency, Robb executes Karstark personally at dawn in the godswood. Catelyn sits with her dying father and later counsels Jeyne Westerling on how to support Robb.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Heavy storm through the night — thunder, lightning, hard black rain. Storm diminishes by dawn to a steady soaking rain. Rain continues all morning and into evening.
- **Season indicators:** No explicit season reference, but chilly and grey.
- **Time of day:** Begins in the black of night (characters roused from sleep); transitions through dawn (execution), morning (rain continues), afternoon (Maester Vyman's visit), and evenfall (Jeyne's visit).
- **Lighting:** Torchlit hall at the opening; dark within the audience chamber, illuminated only by an oil lamp; grey daylight at dawn.
- **Sounds:** Grey Wind howling half a castle away; thunder crashing "so loud it sounded as if the castle were coming down about their ears"; steady wash of rain; steps echoing in the great stone hall; rain on the roof mingled with Lord Hoster's breathing.
- **Smells:** Blood scent implied (Grey Wind smells it through stone walls and wooden doors).
- **Notable sensory details:** The weight of the bronze-and-iron crown leaving marks in Robb's skin above his ears; Catelyn's nails digging into her own palms; the cold and wet of rain soaking through clothing; blood drenching both living and dead during the execution.

## Events & Actions
1. **Corpses presented** — The bodies of Tion Frey and Willem Lannister are carried into the torchlit hall and laid beneath the dais.
2. **Catelyn observes the dead** — She notes details of the murders: the blond boy's slit throat, the brown-haired boy's defensive wounds and multiple stab wounds.
3. **Prisoners brought before Robb** — The Greatjon marches five surviving Karstark prisoners into the hall; three wounded. Eight participated total: two killed during capture, one dying.
4. **Edmure reports his men killed** — Two of Edmure's men (Delp and Elwood) were murdered by the Karstarks to access the tower holding the prisoners.
5. **Karstark confronts Robb and Catelyn** — Lord Rickard justifies the killings as vengeance for his sons, blames Catelyn for freeing the Kingslayer, and calls it justice, not murder.
6. **Robb rebukes Karstark** — Robb argues the squires didn't kill Karstark's sons and this was murder, not vengeance.
7. **Greatjon strikes Karstark** — The Greatjon hits Karstark with a mailed fist for calling Robb "boy," knocking him to his knees.
8. **Karstark taunts Robb** — Calls him "King Who Lost the North" and predicts only a scolding.
9. **Blackfish arrives with dire news** — Reports the entire Karstark mounted force (~300 riders, 600+ mounts) has deserted Riverrun under cover of night and rain.
10. **Robb orders hangings** — Commands the seven accomplices hanged (including the dead ones); one who only watched is to hang last.
11. **Private council in audience chamber** — Robb, Catelyn, Edmure, and Brynden discuss the strategic disaster. Karstark forces scattered and hunting for Jaime. Karstark offered his daughter's hand for Jaime's head.
12. **Strategic discussion** — They discuss the hopeless military position: no word from Ser Rodrik, no response from Walder Frey, silence from the Eyrie. Robb's plan to sail from Gulltown to White Harbor and flank Moat Cailin is blocked by Lysa's refusal.
13. **Edmure argues for clemency** — Suggests keeping Karstark as hostage to preserve Karstark loyalty. Robb refuses.
14. **Robb sentences Karstark to death** — Puts crown back on, declares "Lord Rickard dies," citing that Karstark killed his honor.
15. **Dawn execution in the godswood** — Robb personally beheads Lord Rickard Karstark before the heart tree with a poleaxe. Takes three blows to sever the head. Karstark's last words: "Kill me, and be cursed. You are no king of mine."
16. **Karstark's men already hanged** — Their bodies slump from Riverrun's high walls in the rain.
17. **Blackfish rides out** — Assembles 100 men to pursue the scattered Karstarks, doubts he'll bring many back.
18. **Catelyn sits with Lord Hoster** — Returns to her father's solar. Maester Vyman says Hoster's death is imminent.
19. **Jeyne visits Catelyn** — The young queen asks how to comfort Robb. Catelyn counsels patience, citing her own experience with Ned.
20. **Catelyn advises about an heir** — Tells Jeyne that Robb needs an heir. Jeyne reveals she drinks a fertility posset made by her mother and they try frequently. She plans to name sons Eddard and Brandon.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Guilt (believes she caused the squires' deaths by freeing Jaime); grief (thinks of her dead — Bran, Rickon, Ned, Brandon, her mother); fear for Sansa; exhaustion (no tears left); nausea when Karstark accuses her; quiet despair about the military situation.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The consequences of her actions — the dead squires, the lost Karstark forces, Sansa's danger, the hopelessness of Robb's position.
- **Key decisions made:** None overtly — she briefly muses "it might be best" to keep Karstark hostage but doesn't press the point; she counsels Jeyne with patience and the need for an heir.
- **Self-deception flags:** None clearly evident. She is painfully self-aware that she caused this chain of events ("I did this. These two boys died so my daughters might live").
