- **chapter_summary:** Robb's army marches from Riverrun toward the Twins for Edmure's wedding to Roslin Frey, traveling through steady rain that washes out bridges and forces a detour through Hag's Mire. At the ruins of Oldstones, Robb tells Catelyn he intends to name Jon Snow his heir via royal decree, and they argue bitterly. Lord Jason Mallister arrives with a merchant captain who reports that Balon Greyjoy is dead and Euron Crow's Eye has seized the Seastone Chair. Robb reveals his plan to retake the north: a feigned assault on Moat Cailin from the south while crannogmen guides lead his force through the Neck to attack from the north, with Maege Mormont and Galbart Glover sent to Howland Reed at Greywater Watch. Robb commands Catelyn to remain at Seagard after the wedding and presents a document naming his heir for his lords to witness.

## Events & Actions
1. **Robb bids farewell to Jeyne three times** — In the godswood before the heart tree, beneath the portcullis with a long embrace and kiss, and an hour beyond the Tumblestone when Jeyne gallops up to plead with him to take her along.
2. **Grey Wind prowls and bares teeth at the rain** — The direwolf circles Robb and Jeyne during their farewell, then races ahead when the march resumes.
3. **Lame Lothar Frey chats with Catelyn and Edmure** — He describes Roslin's excitement about the wedding and asks Edmure how he feels; Edmure compares it to the feeling before the Stone Mill.
4. **The march departs Riverrun in drizzle** — Thirty-five hundred riders, organized with Greatjon in the van, Catelyn in the main column, Ser Wendel Manderly guarding the baggage train, Robin Flint commanding the rearguard.
5. **Rain intensifies and persists for days** — Drizzle becomes soft steady rain by midday, then heavy rain that turns roads to mud, swells rivers, and strips trees.
6. **Catelyn speaks with Maege and Dacey Mormont** — They discuss Bear Island's warrior women, the carving on their gate, and Jorah's wife Lynesse Hightower's unhappiness in the north.
7. **Edmure worries about Roslin's appearance** — In his pavilion with friends, he wonders if all Lord Walder's daughters look like him; Ser Marq Piper suggests the old man won't give him a pretty one.
8. **Catelyn rebukes Edmure** — She sharply tells him he'd be wiser to pray for a strong, healthy wife with a good head and loyal heart, citing Cersei Lannister as proof beauty doesn't ensure a good wife.
9. **Edmure avoids Catelyn** — He rides with Marq Piper, Lymond Goodbrook, Patrek Mallister, and the young Vances instead; Catelyn regrets her harshness and remembers her own disappointment when she first met Ned.
10. **The army passes through the Whispering Wood** — Catelyn sees signs of the old battle: overturned helm, splintered lance, horse bones, disturbed cairns, a decomposing face emerging from beneath melting flesh.
11. **Catelyn reflects on Ned's bones** — She wonders whether Hallis Mollen and the silent sisters reached Winterfell with Ned's bones, or were blocked at Moat Cailin.
12. **Scouts report the bridge at Fairmarket washed out** — Five days later; also the bridge near Oldstones is gone.
13. **Galbart Glover nearly drowns at Ramsford** — He and two men tried swimming their mounts across the Blue Fork; two horses drowned, one rider drowned; Glover clung to a rock and was pulled to safety.
14. **Route changed to go around the Blue Fork** — Through Sevenstreams and Hag's Mire; bogs and bad roads; half the wagons abandoned to the muck.
15. **Robb mentions Bolton's march** — Hopes Bolton crossed the Trident before the rains; Bolton on the kingsroad should reach the Twins first, even afoot.
16. **Catelyn asks about Robb's plan after the wedding** — Robb says "North" and gives an enigmatic smile, declining to reveal his full plan.
17. **The army reaches Oldstones** — After eight more days of rain; they camp on the hill within the ruined stronghold overlooking the Blue Fork.
18. **Catelyn finds Robb at Tristifer IV's tomb** — Rain has stopped; Robb stands bareheaded with Grey Wind in the gathering dusk; they discuss the history of the ancient river king.
19. **Robb raises the question of his heir** — He hopes to have left Jeyne with child; worries about dying without issue; refuses to let the north pass to Tyrion Lannister through Sansa.
20. **Robb declares his intent to legitimize Jon Snow** — He argues Jon is more a Stark than distant Vale lordlings; cites precedent of Kingsguard dismissals and royal legitimization decrees.
21. **Catelyn opposes Jon's legitimization** — She argues bastard legitimization caused the Blackfyre rebellions; warns Jon's sons could threaten Robb's future children; invokes Theon's betrayal as a parallel.
22. **Robb and Catelyn quarrel** — Grey Wind leaps atop the tomb baring his teeth; Robb's face goes cold; he calls Catelyn's Theon comparison "cruel and unfair"; Catelyn calls it "folly"; Robb says "I don't have to [ask]. I'm the king" and walks away.
23. **Lord Jason Mallister arrives at Hag's Mire with the Myraham's captain** — The captain reports Balon Greyjoy's death and Euron Crow's Eye's seizure of the Seastone Chair.
24. **Captain describes Balon's death** — Balon was crossing a bridge at Pyke when a storm tore it apart; his body washed up two days later, bloated and broken, crabs ate his eyes.
25. **Captain describes Euron's return** — Euron sailed into Lordsport in the Silence (black sails, red hull, crewed by mutes); had been to Asshai and back; sat the Seastone Chair and drowned Lord Botley in a cask of seawater for objecting.
26. **Robb outlines his plan to retake the north** — Three battles to march up the causeway half a day apart; Greatjon leads the van, Robb commands the center, Roose Bolton the rearguard; Robb's center force will melt into the Neck using crannogmen guides to attack Moat Cailin from the north.
27. **Robb sends Maege Mormont and Galbart Glover to Howland Reed** — Two of Mallister's longships to sail around the Cape of Eagles and up the Neck; they carry false letters as cover in case of capture.
28. **Robb orders Catelyn to stay at Seagard** — He wants his mother and wife in separate locations for safety; Catelyn asks to return to Riverrun instead but is refused; Robb calls it his "royal command."
29. **Robb presents the heir document** — He commands his lords to fix their seals as witnesses to his decision about who will succeed him; the specific name is not stated aloud but is understood to be Jon Snow.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Weary, anxious, lonely, grief-stricken, and increasingly powerless. Catelyn carries grief for Ned, Bran, Rickon, and fears for Sansa, Arya, and Brienne. She feels isolated — first angering Edmure, then quarreling with Robb over Jon's legitimization. She describes herself as seldom feeling lonelier. By the end, she feels "defeated" by Robb's political maneuvering.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Managing the Frey alliance and the dangers ahead; the succession question; her lost children and the fragility of what remains. She repeatedly circles back to everything she has lost — "two dead sons, an empty bed, and a castle full of ghosts."
- **Key decisions made:** Catelyn firmly opposes Jon Snow's legitimization despite Robb's determination, citing the Blackfyre rebellions as precedent. She unsuccessfully requests to return to Riverrun instead of staying at Seagard. She chose (before this chapter) to insist Jeyne remain at Riverrun, and she rebukes Edmure about valuing appearance over character.
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn tells herself "I must be stronger" and tries to project confidence, but her inner thoughts reveal deep despair. She acknowledges to herself that she has "always been too hard with Edmure" yet cannot help it. She briefly wonders "Would that I had known how to wield an axe" — questioning whether her diplomatic/maternal approach has failed to protect anyone. She recognizes Robb's Moat Cailin plan was cleverly used to trap her into compliance but can do nothing about it.
