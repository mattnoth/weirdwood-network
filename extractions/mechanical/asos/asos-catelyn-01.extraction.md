- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn is confined to her father's chambers at Riverrun as punishment for freeing Jaime Lannister. She tends to the dying Lord Hoster, who repeatedly murmurs "Tansy" and begs forgiveness. News arrives that Robb was wounded storming the Crag. Over the course of the night, Catelyn deduces that Lysa was pregnant before her marriage to Jon Arryn and that Lord Hoster arranged the match as a political price for Tully swords. Edmure returns and informs Catelyn that Stannis lost at King's Landing, Highgarden and Dorne have declared for Joffrey, and that he has sent ravens to Lord Bolton offering a thousand dragons for Jaime's recapture — which Catelyn realizes endangers Brienne's mission and her daughters.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ser Desmond and Utherydes confront Catelyn** — They inform her of her confinement for freeing Jaime Lannister. Catelyn accepts responsibility and offers to wear the Kingslayer's fetters.
2. **Catelyn confined to Lord Hoster's chambers** — She is granted her request to be confined in her father's chambers rather than a tower cell, with permission to visit the sept.
3. **Utherydes warns Catelyn about Ser Robin Ryger** — He lingers to inform her that Ser Robin has been sent after Jaime and Brienne.
4. **Catelyn's belongings moved to Lord Hoster's bedchamber** — Lord Hoster himself moved half a turn down the stair to face the triangular balcony overlooking the rivers.
5. **Catelyn watches the rivers for sails** — She monitors for Ser Robin's striped sail, relieved to see none.
6. **Lord Hoster wakes and speaks of "Tansy"** — In delirium he says "Forgive me... the blood... Tansy" and "You'll have others... sweet babes, and trueborn."
7. **Maester Vyman administers milk of the poppy** — Lord Hoster has a pain spasm, coughs up bloody sputum, and is sedated.
8. **Catelyn discusses "Tansy" with Maester Vyman** — Neither knows anyone by that name at Riverrun; Vyman confuses a widow named Violet with the name.
9. **Catelyn watches all day and into the night** — A raven arrives in late afternoon.
10. **Maester Vyman brings supper and news at evenfall** — Utherydes confirms no woman named Tansy served at Riverrun. Vyman reveals Lord Tywin has left the riverlands, and that Robb was wounded storming the Crag.
11. **Lord Hoster speaks again in the night** — "Forgive me... Tansy... blood... the blood... gods be kind."
12. **Catelyn deduces Lysa's past pregnancy** — Waking before dawn, she pieces together Lord Hoster's words with memories of Lysa, concluding Lysa was pregnant before her marriage to Jon Arryn and that Lord Hoster arranged the marriage as political payment.
13. **Catelyn confronts her sleeping father** — She whispers her realization: "You made him take her. Lysa was the price Jon Arryn had to pay for the swords and spears of House Tully."
14. **Catelyn writes a letter to Lysa** — Asks her sister to come to Riverrun or write words of forgiveness so Lord Hoster may die in peace.
15. **Catelyn lights candles in the sept** — One to the Father for Lord Hoster, one to the Crone, one to the Mother for Lysa and their lost children.
16. **Edmure's force arrives at Riverrun** — Several hundred riders bearing the leaping trout banner approach the main gate.
17. **Edmure visits Catelyn** — Two hours after arrival, he comes to her spattered in mud. Reports he turned back Tywin, Gregor Clegane, and Addam Marbrand at the fords.
18. **Edmure delivers devastating news** — Stannis lost at King's Landing (fleet burned, army routed); Highgarden has declared for Joffrey; Dorne as well; all the south is lost to Robb's cause.
19. **Edmure reveals his ravens to Lord Bolton** — He sent three ravens offering a thousand dragons for Jaime's recapture, undermining Catelyn's hostage exchange.
20. **Catelyn realizes the danger** — If Jaime's departure is seen as an escape rather than an exchange, the Lannisters have no reason to return Arya and Sansa. Edmure's bounty makes Brienne's mission far more dangerous.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Weary resignation at the chapter's opening; grief for Ned, Bran, and Rickon throughout; hope mixed with dread watching for sails; disturbed and unsettled by Lord Hoster's "Tansy" mutterings; helpless dread upon deducing her father's secret; desperate hope regarding Brienne's mission; despair when Edmure reveals his ravens have undermined everything
- **Primary preoccupation:** The fate of her daughters (Arya and Sansa) — her reason for freeing Jaime; her dying father's mysterious guilt; the strategic implications of losing the south
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts confinement willingly; writes to Lysa asking her to come or send forgiveness; refuses to regret freeing Jaime despite consequences
- **Self-deception flags:** None apparent — Catelyn is clear-eyed about her actions being treasonous and about the dangers she has created
