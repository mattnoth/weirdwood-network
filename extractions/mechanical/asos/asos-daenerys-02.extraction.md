# ASOS — Daenerys II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 24
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys II
- **first_available:** ASOS Daenerys II
- **location_primary:** Astapor (Plaza of Pride, streets, port)
- **location_secondary:** Balerion (ship), Slaver's Bay
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Daenerys's arrival in Astapor; she has recently sailed from Qarth
- **time_markers:** "baking in the sun since dawn"; Unsullied "standing here for a day and a night"; "Only three days past" (a corsair king visited); "Dusk had begun to settle over the waters of Slaver's Bay"; "evening tide" mentioned as potential departure time; Dany recalls a previous night aboard Balerion (Jorah's kiss, encounter with Irri); "The next day, it all seemed a dream"
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys inspects the Unsullied slave soldiers in Astapor's Plaza of Pride, where slaver Kraznys mo Nakloz demonstrates their obedience and reveals details of their brutal training — including the wine of courage, infant-killing, and puppy-strangling. Dany pretends not to understand High Valyrian while Kraznys insults her through his young translator. Arstan Whitebeard argues against buying slaves on moral grounds. After touring the city and boarding her ship Balerion, Dany slaps Ser Jorah in anger, then retreats to the captain's cabin where she tends to her dragons and refuses Irri's offer to pleasure her. At dusk, Jorah makes the pragmatic case for buying the Unsullied, ending with the argument that Rhaegar fought nobly but lost.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dany inspects Unsullied in Plaza of Pride** — One thousand Unsullied are drawn up in ten ranks of one hundred before the bronze harpy fountain for Dany's inspection; they have been standing for a day and a night without food or water.
2. **Kraznys insults Dany in Valyrian** — Throughout the inspection, Kraznys calls Dany a "whore," "savage," "cow," "ignorant," and "slut" in High Valyrian, unaware she understands; his translator diplomatically softens everything.
3. **Kraznys whips an Unsullied** — Brings his whip across a thickset Lhazareen-looking eunuch's cheek, drawing blood; the Unsullied blinks but does not react, and says he would welcome another blow.
4. **Kraznys cuts off an Unsullied's nipple** — Uses a towering Lysene Unsullied's own shortsword to slice off his nipple to demonstrate they feel no pain; the eunuch does not move or react.
5. **Kraznys explains the wine of courage** — Reveals the Unsullied drink a potion of deadly nightshade, bloodfly larva, black lotus root, and other ingredients with every meal, progressively eliminating pain sensation.
6. **Kraznys demonstrates the naming system** — An Unsullied named "Red Flea" today was "Black Rat" yesterday and "Brown Flea" the day before; name disks drawn randomly each dawn from a cask.
7. **Kraznys reveals the infant-killing requirement** — To earn their spiked cap, each Unsullied must buy a slave infant for a silver mark and kill it before its mother's eyes.
8. **Kraznys reveals the puppy-strangling test** — Each boy receives a puppy on the day he is cut; after a year, he must strangle it or be killed and fed to the surviving dogs.
9. **Arstan argues against buying slaves** — Warns that landing in Westeros with a slave army would turn good men against Dany and harm her House's honor; suggests hiring sellswords or waiting for Illyrio's envoys.
10. **Dany departs the Plaza of Pride** — Takes Arstan's arm; Aggo and Jhogo fall in as escort; climbs into her litter.
11. **Dany forbids Jhogo from cracking his whip** — Tells him "These bricks have heard too much of the sound of whips."
12. **Dany observes Astapor from her litter** — Notes the crumbling red brick architecture, sparse population, fine red dust; sees a naked boy picking his nose, a column of mounted guards with fantastically styled hair, and a coffle of naked slaves being driven by an overseer.
13. **Strong Belwas offers Dany roasted dog** — She declines, thinking of the Unsullied and their puppies.
14. **Dany boards Balerion and learns slavers inspected the cargo** — Jorah reports three slavers with scribes and slaves came, crawled over every foot of the holds, and catalogued everything.
15. **Dany slaps Ser Jorah** — In a burst of anger/emotion about the Unsullied's training and Jorah's role in bringing her to Astapor; strikes him across the face rather than cry.
16. **Dany declares she must buy the Unsullied** — Despite her horror, tells Jorah she cannot leave and must find a way to buy eight thousand.
17. **Dany retreats to captain's cabin with dragons** — Drogon screams; Viserion tries to perch on her shoulder but is too big; she sinks into Groleo's leather chair giggling as Viserion clings to her arm.
18. **Irri reports on dragons' misbehavior** — Viserion clawed splinters from the door; Drogon tried to escape when slavers came and bit Irri's hand when she grabbed his tail; Drogon breathed fire into empty air.
19. **Irri offers to pleasure Dany; Dany refuses** — Dany insists Irri is a freed handmaid, not a bed slave, and asks to be left alone.
20. **Dany recalls the night Irri pleasured her** — Dany was aroused after Jorah's kiss; masturbated in her bunk; Irri woke, saw, and brought her to climax; the next day it seemed a dream.
21. **Dany returns to deck at dusk** — Observes Astapor lit by silk lanterns, reflects on the darkness below in barracks and fighting pits.
22. **Jorah argues for buying the Unsullied** — Cites the Sack of King's Landing as evidence that free men also commit atrocities; argues that Unsullied only kill on command.
23. **Dany and Jorah discuss why Astapor has never been sacked** — The Unsullied garrison deters attackers (Dothraki recall their defeat at Qohor); the city bribes passing khals; no nearby enemies are strong enough.
24. **Dany invokes Rhaegar's example** — Argues Rhaegar led free men, not slaves, and that his knights were told to defend the weak.
25. **Jorah delivers the closing argument** — "Rhaegar fought valiantly, Rhaegar fought nobly, Rhaegar fought honorably. And Rhaegar died."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Dany cycles through controlled composure (maintaining the Valyrian ruse), revulsion (at the Unsullied training methods), anger (at Jorah for bringing her to Astapor and for his earlier kiss), grief (for Drogo), confused desire (awakened by Jorah's kiss but directed at an imagined younger lover), guilt/discomfort (about the encounter with Irri), pragmatic resolve (accepting she must buy the Unsullied despite moral objections), and sadness ("Sad and lost" when Irri asks)
- **Primary preoccupation:** The moral calculus of buying slave soldiers — balancing her revulsion at slavery (having been sold herself) against the practical need for an army to retake Westeros. Secondary preoccupation: suppressing the sexual desire awakened by Jorah's kiss and dealing with the complicated aftermath of Irri's intervention.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Decides to maintain the ruse of not understanding Valyrian. (2) Decides she must buy the Unsullied despite moral horror: "I must find some way to buy them." (3) Refuses Irri's sexual offer and insists Irri is free, not a bed slave. (4) Forbids Jhogo from cracking his whip in the slave city.
- **Self-deception flags:** Dany tells herself the light-headedness and flushing are from the heat, not emotion: "The heat, she tried to tell herself." She also tells herself "It is Drogo I want... not Irri, and not Ser Jorah, only Drogo" while her body and thoughts suggest otherwise. She blames Jorah entirely for the kiss ("He should never have kissed me. He presumed too much") while acknowledging "I permitted it."
