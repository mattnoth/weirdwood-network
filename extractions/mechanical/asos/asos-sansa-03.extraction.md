# ASOS — Sansa III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 29
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Sansa III
- **first_available:** ASOS Sansa III
- **location_primary:** The Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Castle sept, Small Hall, Tower of the Hand (bedchamber)
- **approximate_timeline:** During the War of the Five Kings; after Sansa's secret betrothal discussions with the Tyrells and after the Battle of the Blackwater
- **time_markers:** "On the morning her new gown was to be ready"; the chapter spans from morning through the wedding ceremony, the feast, and into the wedding night; Sansa states she is "Thirteen, when the moon turns"
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa is dressed in a fine new gown, believing she will soon marry Willas Tyrell, but Cersei reveals she is to marry Tyrion Lannister instead. Escorted to the castle sept, Sansa weds Tyrion in a ceremony where Joffrey stands in as her father. The wedding feast in the Small Hall is attended by roughly fifty guests, and Sansa dances with Ser Garlan Tyrell, who speaks kindly to her, but Joffrey threatens during their dance that he will still take her to his bed. When Joffrey calls for the bedding ceremony, Tyrion threatens to geld him and Tywin intervenes to cancel it. In the Tower of the Hand bedchamber, Tyrion refuses to consummate the marriage despite his father's command, vowing not to touch Sansa until she wants him to.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa bathed and prepared** — Serving girls fill her tub with steaming hot water and scrub her; Cersei's bedmaid trims her nails, curls her hair, and applies scent (lemon-flower fragrance) behind her ears, under her chin, and on her nipples.
2. **Sansa dressed in new gown** — Cersei arrives with the seamstress; Sansa is dressed in an ivory samite and cloth-of-silver gown with Myrish lace, grey doeskin slippers, and Joffrey's moonstone jewelry. The seamstress tells her she is beautiful.
3. **Sansa admires herself** — She giggles and spins, thinking Willas will love her when he sees her.
4. **Cersei reveals the true marriage** — Cersei calls for the maiden's cloak (white velvet with silver direwolf), and Sansa realizes with dread she is being married. Cersei tells her she will marry Tyrion, not Willas.
5. **Sansa protests** — Sansa says "No" and "I won't" and "You can't make me." Cersei warns she can come quietly or be carried. Sansa thinks of Dontos's warning that the Tyrells only wanted her claim.
6. **Sansa escorted to the sept** — Ser Meryn Trant and Ser Osmund Kettleblack escort her; Kettleblack tells her "Wolves are supposed to be brave." Sansa resolves to go willingly, remembering Tyrion once saved her from a beating.
7. **Joffrey greets Sansa at the sept** — He announces "I'm your father today." Sansa flares back "You're not. You'll never be." Joffrey threatens her with marriage to a pig boy or Ilyn Payne.
8. **Tyrion requests a moment with Sansa** — He offers her the choice to marry Lancel instead, saying Lancel is nearer her age and fairer. Sansa declines, accepting the marriage as her duty.
9. **Wedding ceremony** — The septon performs the ceremony between the Mother and the Father. Prayers, vows, singing, and candles. Sansa cries throughout.
10. **Changing of the cloaks** — Joffrey removes Sansa's maiden's cloak, brushing and squeezing her breast as he does. Tyrion cannot reach to place the Lannister cloak on Sansa because he is a foot and a half shorter.
11. **Sansa refuses to kneel** — Tyrion tugs her skirt three times; she refuses to kneel for him. The guests laugh. Joffrey commands Dontos onto his hands and knees as a stepping stool.
12. **Tyrion cloaks Sansa from Dontos's back** — He places the Lannister cloak on her while standing on the fool. Sansa, ashamed, then kneels so their heads are level.
13. **Exchange of vows and kiss** — They pledge love and are proclaimed man and wife by the septon. Sansa thinks Tyrion is uglier than the Hound.
14. **Wedding feast begins** — Approximately fifty guests in the Small Hall. The Tyrells are present but distant — Margaery gives a sad look, Olenna ignores Sansa, Elinor, Alla, and Megga pretend not to know her.
15. **Dancing** — Joffrey and Margaery lead the first dance. Many guests dance: Elinor with her squire, Megga with Tommen, Lady Merryweather provocatively, Lord and Lady Tyrell sedately, Ser Kevan with Lady Janna Fossoway, Merry Crane with Jalabhar Xho, Cersei with Lord Redwyne, Lord Rowan, and Tywin.
16. **Ser Garlan asks Sansa to dance** — He tells her about Willas naming him "Garlan the Gallant" to protect him from worse nicknames. He tells Sansa that Tyrion will make a better husband than Loras. He mentions his wife Lady Leonette is concerned for Sansa.
17. **Joffrey threatens Sansa during their dance** — He tells her "you'll still have me," claiming a king can have other women, citing his father and King Aegon. He says Tyrion will bring her to his bed whenever he commands, and gives her a moist kiss.
18. **Joffrey calls for the bedding** — He seizes Sansa's arm and calls for the guests to undress "the she-wolf."
19. **Tyrion threatens Joffrey** — He slams his dagger into the table and threatens to geld the king. Shocked silence falls. Sansa pulls away and her sleeve rips.
20. **Tywin intervenes** — He rises and says they can dispense with the bedding. Tyrion claims it was a bad jape, then says he envied the king's manhood. Ser Osmund laughs.
21. **Tyrion and Sansa retire** — Tyrion grabs Sansa roughly and they leave the Small Hall for the Tower of the Hand bedchamber.
22. **The bedchamber scene** — Tyrion asks for Arbor gold wine. Both drink. He reveals he was married before to "Lady Tysha of House Silverfist."
23. **Sansa reveals her age** — She says she is "Thirteen, when the moon turns." Tyrion says "Gods have mercy."
24. **Sansa undresses** — Tyrion commands her to disrobe. She fumbles with her clothes and stands naked; Tyrion stares with hunger in his green eye and fury in his black.
25. **Tyrion refuses to consummate** — He says "I cannot do this. My father be damned." He vows not to touch her until she wants him to, pledging on his honor as a Lannister. He says they will wait.
26. **Sansa asks "And if I never want you to?"** — Tyrion is stung. He says "that is why the gods made whores for imps like me," closes his fist, and climbs down off the bed.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Begins with excitement and vanity at her beautiful gown (thinking of Willas), shifts to shock and dread when learning of the Tyrion marriage, then resignation and numbness during the ceremony ("it seemed to take all her attention just to put one foot down in front of the other"), mortification at the cloaking humiliation, brief relief and laughter during the dance with Ser Garlan, terror during Joffrey's threats, dread at the bedding prospect, pity for Tyrion in the bedchamber, and finally a kind of cold honesty when she asks "And if I never want you to?"
- **Primary preoccupation:** The forced marriage and the loss of her hoped-for Tyrell match; the contrast between her wedding daydreams and the reality; fear of consummation
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to go willingly to the sept rather than be carried ("I am a Stark, yes, I can be brave"); refuses to kneel for Tyrion at the cloaking (then regrets it and kneels in shame); accepts the marriage as duty rather than choose Lancel; honestly tells Tyrion she may never want him
- **Self-deception flags:** Initially tells herself "He will love me, he will, he must" about Willas — pure wishful thinking. Tells herself "He is not so bad as the rest of them" about Tyrion to steel herself. Tries to follow Septa Mordane's advice to "find his beauty" but cannot. Uses courtesy as armor ("It will please me to please my lord husband") as a shield against genuine engagement. Recalls Dontos's warning about the Tyrells only wanting her claim — recognizes a truth she had been ignoring.
