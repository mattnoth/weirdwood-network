- **location_primary:** Queenscrown (holdfast tower on an island in a lake in the New Gift)

- **approximate_timeline:** Mid-ASOS; the party has left the foothills and entered the Gift, heading north toward the Wall. They estimate they are about fifty leagues south of the Wall.
- **time_markers:** "a beautiful crisp clear autumn day, sunny and almost warm"; dark clouds gathering to the west; dusk settling by the time duck and tale are done; rain begins; night falls; lightning and thunder throughout the night
- **chapter_summary:** Bran, Meera, Jojen, and Hodor arrive at an abandoned village in the New Gift and cross a hidden underwater causeway to a holdfast tower on an island in a lake. They discuss how to pass through the Wall, with Jojen insisting they avoid Castle Black to protect Bran's identity. A lone rider takes shelter in the ruined inn, followed after dark by a large group of armed men on foot. When thunder terrifies Hodor into screaming, Bran instinctively reaches into Hodor's mind and silences him—his first experience of warging into a human. The chapter ends with Bran warging into Summer, who is hiding in the brush near the village and can smell and see the armed men.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at the abandoned village** — Bran, Meera, Jojen, and Hodor arrive at the first village they've seen since leaving the foothills. Meera scouts it first; Summer chases three red deer into the brush.
2. **Bran explains the Gift** — Bran tells the Reeds the history of Brandon's Gift and the New Gift, and why the villages are abandoned (wildling raids, Night's Watch too weak to protect the area).
3. **Exploration of the ruined inn** — They find the inn reduced to a chimney and two walls, with an apple tree growing through the common room and rotting apples on the floor. Meera finds no edible apples.
4. **Decision to cross to the holdfast** — Jojen predicts a coming storm. Bran knows of a hidden causeway from Old Nan's stories, and points out the golden-painted merlons (Queen Alysanne slept there).
5. **Crossing the causeway** — Meera leads, probing with her frog spear. The causeway zigzags underwater; Hodor nearly falls twice, scaring Bran. Water reaches Hodor's waist and the Reeds' chests.
6. **Entry into the holdfast** — The warped oak door won't close. Inside, the iron grates are rusted shut. Hodor can't force them. Bran reaches up from his basket and pulls the murder hole grate free, which crumbles with rust.
7. **Climbing the tower** — Meera and Jojen are boosted through the murder hole; they pull Bran up. Hodor piles rocks to climb through. They explore dark cells and climb to the top via stairs.
8. **View from the roof** — They see foothills to the south, mountains beyond, the rolling plains of the New Gift in all directions. They cannot see the Wall, estimating it's about fifty leagues away.
9. **Discussion about passing through the Wall** — Bran suggests Castle Black; Jojen insists they avoid it, warning that someone could betray Bran to the ironmen or the Bastard of Bolton. Jojen suggests using an abandoned castle instead.
10. **A lone rider spotted** — Jojen spots a rider approaching the village from the west near sunset. They retreat inside the tower.
11. **Taking shelter from the storm** — Rain begins. The rider has taken shelter in the ruined inn and made a fire. Jojen vetoes making their own fire (smoke would be visible).
12. **Cold supper and storytelling** — They eat the remaining half-duck. Bran tells the story of Brandon the Shipwright.
13. **Night and the storm intensify** — Darkness falls. Hodor grows restless. Lightning and thunder begin. Hodor panics, screaming "HODOR!" repeatedly.
14. **Armed men arrive in the village** — Jojen sees many armed men (axes, spears) moving under the trees during a lightning flash. They are on foot.
15. **Bran warges into Hodor** — Hodor's screaming threatens to reveal them. Bran instinctively reaches for Hodor's mind "the way I reach for Summer." Hodor goes silent and sits down. Bran is frightened by what he did.
16. **Meera reassures them about the causeway** — They realize the armed men cannot reach the island without knowing the causeway or having a boat. The causeway would be impossible to navigate in rain and darkness.
17. **Bran warges into Summer** — Bran closes his eyes and slips into Summer. Through the direwolf's senses, he smells the men, hears the clink of armor ("hardskin"), sees a man with a stick (spear?) with a skin pulled over his head. He smells fear.

- **Emotional state:** Anxious and fearful but trying to be brave; nostalgic when remembering Winterfell and family; hopeful about seeing Jon and Benjen; frightened by what he did to Hodor; proud of remembering Maester Luwin's lessons
- **Primary preoccupation:** How to reach and get past the Wall to find the three-eyed crow; desire to see Jon and Benjen at Castle Black; fear of the armed men discovering them
