# ASOS — Arya VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 40
- **pov_character:** Arya Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya VII
- **first_available:** ASOS Arya VII
- **location_primary:** Unnamed septry (with mill, brewhouse, and stables) beside a river
- **location_secondary:** Brewhouse (beside the river); Harrenhal (mentioned); Hollow hill (mentioned); Riverrun (mentioned); King's Landing (mentioned); Winterfell (mentioned); Blackhaven (mentioned); Rushing Falls (mentioned); Oldtown (mentioned); south of the Mander (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** After the trial by combat between Sandor Clegane and Lord Beric at the hollow hill; the Brotherhood is traveling toward Riverrun to ransom Arya to Robb Stark
- **time_markers:** Predawn gloom at chapter opening; sky lightening at dawn; eastern horizon glowing gold and pink; half moon overhead; morning mists; night falls; rain begins and stops during the night; come morning (next day); "no rain that day" on departure; "the last harvest burned, this one is drowning, and winter will soon be on us" (seasonal reference)
- **chapter_summary:** The Brotherhood Without Banners attacks a septry occupied by Brave Companions, killing most and rescuing eight brown brothers. Septon Utt and the surviving Mummers are tried and hanged. Lord Beric reveals to Arya that Thoros has resurrected him six times and that his memories are fading. Arya asks if Thoros could bring back a headless man and is told no. Gendry pledges himself to the Brotherhood and is knighted by Lord Beric. Sandor Clegane reappears demanding his gold, is refused, and departs. Arya recites her kill list before sleeping, hears wolves howling in the night, and departs the next morning after a cold farewell to Gendry.

## Events & Actions
1. **Anguy kills the rooftop sentinel** — At predawn, a man on the septry roof stands and stretches; Anguy's arrow takes him in the chest and he tumbles down in front of the septry door.
2. **Kyle and Notch kill the door guards** — Two guards with a torch are shot; one through the throat, one through the belly. The second man's clothes catch fire from the dropped torch, and his screaming ends stealth.
3. **Thoros signals full attack** — Thoros shouts, and the outlaws attack in earnest. Flaming arrows are shot into the septry's wooden walls and through shuttered windows.
4. **Archers pick off fleeing Mummers** — Two axemen emerge and are killed by arrows. Anguy's bodkin-tipped arrows pierce breastplate "as if it had been made of silk."
5. **Myrish crossbowman exchanges fire** — A crossbowman fires a bolt from a window, ducks to rewind, fires again; three arrows hiss past his head, one rattling off his helm.
6. **Mass breakout from the septry** — Mummers boil out: two Ibbenese with shaggy brown shields, a Dothraki with arakh and bells in his braid, three Volantene sellswords with fierce tattoos, and others climbing from windows.
7. **Kyle is killed** — A man in black mail flings a spear through Kyle's belly while he tries to nock another arrow.
8. **Watty is wounded** — Watty falls with a grunt, his bow slipping from his hand.
9. **Full melee** — Lord Beric's band charges from ditches and trees. Lem rides down Kyle's killer. Thoros hacks a hide shield to pieces while his horse kicks a man in the face.
10. **Lord Beric kills a Dothraki with flaming sword** — A Dothraki charges Lord Beric; their blades "kiss and spin and kiss again"; the Dothraki's hair catches fire and he dies.
11. **Battle ends** — Remaining Brave Companions die or surrender. Two Dothraki flee on horseback; Lord Beric lets them go to carry word back to Harrenhal.
12. **Captives rescued from burning septry** — Jack-Be-Lucky, Harwin, and Merrit brave the flames and emerge with eight brown brothers (one so weak Merrit carries him) and Septon Utt in black chainmail over grey robes.
13. **Thoros identifies Septon Utt** — "You are Utt." Septon Utt claims to be "a man of god."
14. **Trial of the Brave Companions** — Outlaws testify about towns sacked, crops burned, women raped and murdered, men maimed and tortured, and boys Septon Utt carried off. Septon Utt weeps: "I am a weak reed... The boys, the sweet boys... I never mean to hurt them."
15. **Brave Companions hanged** — Septon Utt hanged naked beneath a tall elm. Others follow; some fight, one Myrish crossbowman shouts "I soldier, I soldier"; another offers to lead to gold; a third offers to be an outlaw. Tom plays a dirge; Thoros implores R'hllor to roast their souls.
16. **Septry collapses** — Walls can no longer support the heavy slate roof.
17. **Eldest brown brother tells their story** — Forty-four reduced to eight; livestock slaughtered, vineyard torched by "the lions"; subsequent visitors killed brothers to make Elder Brother reveal gold.
18. **Outlaws shelter in brewhouse** — Simple supper shared from hidden food cache.
19. **Religious tension over R'hllor** — Young novice tells Thoros not to pray to his false god under their roof. Lem argues. Lord Beric commands respect: "Beneath their roof we will honor their rules."
20. **Lord Beric reveals his death history to Arya** — Shows three wounds: Ser Burton Crakehall's mace (caved-in temple), "the manticore" at Rushing Falls (hanging scar around neck), the Mountain's dirk (missing eye). Notes three deaths at House Clegane's hands.
21. **Thoros reveals six resurrections** — Admits he has brought Lord Beric back six times; says each time is harder; warns a seventh might mean the end of both.
22. **Lord Beric describes memory loss** — Cannot remember his castle, his betrothed's hair color, who knighted him, or his favorite foods. "Sometimes I think I was born on the bloody grass in that grove of ash."
23. **Arya asks about resurrecting a headless man** — Thoros says he has no magic, only prayers. Describes "the last kiss": filling his mouth with fire and breathing it into the dead. Says R'hllor raised Beric, not him. Arya understands the answer is no and tears well in her eyes.
24. **Lord Beric promises Arya's ransom** — Says he would forgo ransom for her father's sake but needs the gold. Promises to return her to her mother. Swears on his honor as a knight. Mentions Blackhaven or Lady Smallwood as fallbacks.
25. **Tom sings rain songs** — "The Mother's Tears," "When Willum's Wife Was Wet," "Lord Harte Rode Out on a Rainy Day," and "The Rains of Castamere" (lyrics quoted in full).
26. **Gendry offers to smith for the Brotherhood** — Says he can shoe horses, mend mail, beat dents from plate, make swords. Goes to one knee before Lord Beric.
27. **Lord Beric knights Gendry** — Lays sword on Gendry's shoulder (unlit); Gendry swears to defend those who cannot defend themselves, protect women and children, obey captains and liege lord. Named "Ser Gendry, knight of the hollow hill."
28. **Sandor Clegane arrives at the brewhouse** — Rough rasping laughter from the door; rain running off him; burned arm in sling. Demands his gold back.
29. **Confrontation between Clegane and outlaws** — Clegane mocks and threatens; says sentries might be dead; Anguy, Notch, Jack, Merrit, and Thoros draw weapons. Lord Beric stays calm. Lem calls Clegane's gold "foraging." Gendry confronts Clegane about feeding peasants.
30. **Clegane departs** — Looks at every face as if committing them to memory; walks back into darkness and rain.
31. **Harwin checks sentries** — Finds Puddingfoot asleep but unharmed.
32. **Discussion of Clegane's situation** — Thoros notes Clegane has lost master and kennel; cannot return to Lannisters, Young Wolf won't have him, his brother won't welcome him. Lord Beric refuses to kill him, citing the trial by combat as holy. Orders Anguy to kill Clegane's horse if he follows.
33. **Arya's nighttime ritual** — Clutches Jaqen's coin; recites kill list: Ser Gregor, Dunsen, Polliver, Raff the Sweetling, the Tickler, the Hound, Ser Ilyn, Ser Meryn, King Joffrey, Queen Cersei. Notes faces are fading from memory.
34. **Wolves howl in the night** — Arya wakes to wolves howling, sounding close and numerous — "dozens of them, maybe hundreds" — all around the stables. She hopes they eat the Hound.
35. **Morning departure** — Septon Utt still swings from tree. Brown brothers dig graves. Lord Beric gives silver and thanks. Scouts find neither wolves nor hounds.
36. **Arya says cold farewell to Gendry** — Tells him she doesn't care if he gets hanged; says she'll be at Riverrun with her brother.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fierce bloodlust during battle ("Kill them all... Kill every single one"); satisfaction at Septon Utt's hanging; grief and longing when asking about resurrection (tears well in eyes thinking of her father); abandonment and hurt as Gendry decides to stay; strength and comfort from Jaqen's coin; latent fear of the Hound but defiant anger toward him; deep loneliness cataloguing everyone who left or died
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether Robb will want to ransom her; the possibility of bringing her father back; Gendry leaving her; her kill list and the fading faces of those on it
- **Key decisions made:** Does not try to argue about being kept from battle (recognizes she must obey); accepts she cannot have her father back; resolves to learn archery ("I'm going to learn to shoot a bow"); maintains kill list discipline despite fading memories
- **Self-deception flags:** Lies about her age (says twelve); tells Gendry she doesn't care he's leaving (clearly does — "He means to leave me too"); claims dirty nails and messy hair would concern Robb (deflecting deeper fear of rejection); says she could have killed the Hound (no basis for this)
