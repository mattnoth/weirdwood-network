- **chapter_summary:** Jaime Lannister, chained and in a skiff with Brienne of Tarth and Ser Cleos Frey, travels down the Red Fork after Catelyn Stark secretly freed him from Riverrun's dungeon. Jaime has Cleos shave his head as a disguise, and they pass through war-ravaged countryside, finding a burned inn with hanged women bearing signs reading "They Lay With Lions." After Brienne insists on cutting down the corpses, they spot a pursuing galley commanded by Ser Robin Ryger, Riverrun's captain of guards. During a chase through a narrow cutoff, Brienne climbs a cliff and dislodges a massive boulder that destroys the galley, then dives back into the river and is pulled aboard.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dawn on the river** — Jaime awakens at dawn in the skiff, savoring sunlight after long imprisonment in Riverrun's dungeon. He laughs aloud, prompting Brienne to tell him to be quiet.
2. **Verbal sparring** — Jaime mocks Brienne's appearance, comparing her to a cow; she demands he call her Brienne, not "wench"; he demands she call him Ser Jaime, not "Kingslayer."
3. **Recall of escape** — Jaime remembers fragments: the big wench overcame the gaoler, they climbed an endless stair, he was bundled into the skiff, Catelyn commanded the Water Gate portcullis raised.
4. **Oath recalled** — Jaime recalls Catelyn pressing Brienne's sword to his heart, extracting oaths to never take arms against Stark or Tully, and to compel Tyrion to return her daughters.
5. **Brienne identifies herself** — Brienne reveals her father is Selwyn of Tarth, Lord of Evenfall; she serves Lady Catelyn, not Robb.
6. **Bran's fall recalled** — Jaime internally recalls pushing Bran Stark from the window after Bran caught him with Cersei; remembers Cersei's angry response and their subsequent sexual encounter; wonders if Cersei hired the later assassin.
7. **Sail raised** — Wind shifts; Cleos and Brienne raise the striped red-and-blue Tully sail. Jaime throws out the leeboard. They make better speed.
8. **Head shaving** — Jaime has Ser Cleos shave his head bald with a dagger, as a disguise. Lice are found and crushed. His beard is trimmed. He does not recognize his own reflection.
9. **Signs of war observed** — They pass empty villages, slashed fishing nets, a burnt towerhouse with peasants digging in its fields, and a dead Lannister soldier floating in the river.
10. **Burned inn and hanged women** — They find a burned building on the south bank with women's corpses hanging from a live oak tree, bearing signs reading "They Lay With Lions." Brienne insists on cutting them down.
11. **Discussion of perpetrators** — Jaime identifies the area as Bracken land and suggests Lord Jonos Bracken. Ser Cleos suggests Marq Piper, Beric Dondarrion, or Roose Bolton's men. Cleos reveals Bolton took Harrenhal.
12. **Landing and cutting down corpses** — They beach the skiff. Jaime wades ashore and washes in the river. Brienne climbs the oak and begins cutting corpses down with her dagger.
13. **Pursuit spotted** — Brienne spots a sail from the treetop and shouts warning. They scramble back to the skiff and flee downriver.
14. **Chase on the Red Fork** — For over an hour they play "peek-and-seek" with the pursuing galley through river bends and wooded islets. The galley gains steadily.
15. **Ser Robin Ryger identified** — As the galley closes to fifty yards, Jaime identifies Ser Robin Ryger, Riverrun's captain of guards, at the prow. Ryger demands they surrender.
16. **Arrows fired** — After Jaime's defiant response, Ryger's archers fire. One arrow hits the mast, two pierce the sail, one misses Jaime by a foot.
17. **Cutoff maneuver** — Brienne steers left into a narrow cutoff between a wooded island and the north shore bluffs, seeking cover from the archers.
18. **Brienne climbs the cliff** — Brienne leaps from the skiff and climbs the sheer cliff face. Jaime orders Cleos to steer and uses the oar himself.
19. **Jaime distracts Ryger** — Jaime calls out to Ser Robin, challenging him to single combat while chained, buying time for Brienne to reach the clifftop.
20. **Boulder destroys the galley** — As the archers draw, Brienne cascades pebbles then dislodges a boulder the size of a cow from the clifftop. It snaps the mast, kills/injures crew, and punches through the hull. The galley begins to sink.
21. **Brienne rejoins** — Brienne runs along the clifftop ahead of the skiff, dives off the rock into the river. Jaime considers hitting her with the oar but instead extends it for her to grab, pulling her aboard.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Exhilarated and almost giddy from freedom and sunlight after long imprisonment ("alive, and drunk on sunlight"); sardonic and mocking toward Brienne but grudgingly observing her competence; contemptuous of Cleos; darkly reflective when recalling Bran's fall; cavalier about death ("Jaime Lannister had never been afraid of death"); surprised by Brienne's effectiveness with the boulder
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting free of chains; survival and escape from pursuit; his own changed appearance; Cersei
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to have his head shaved as disguise; decides to distract Ser Robin with a single-combat challenge to buy Brienne time; chooses to pull Brienne back into the skiff rather than leaving her behind or striking her with the oar
- **Self-deception flags:** Jaime rationalizes pushing Bran ("The wretched boy was spying on us") while simultaneously admitting he has "come to rue" it; dismisses the High Septon and the gods as frauds while having sworn sacred oaths he doesn't intend to keep; internally acknowledges Cleos is a lickspittle but uses his fawning as cover rather than correcting him
