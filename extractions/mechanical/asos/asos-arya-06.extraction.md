# ASOS — Arya VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 35
- **pov_character:** Arya Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya VI
- **first_available:** ASOS Arya VI
- **location_primary:** The hollow hill (underground cave)
- **location_secondary:** Stoney Sept (flashback), King's Landing (referenced), Mummer's Ford (referenced), Sherrer (referenced), Casterly Rock (referenced), Winterfell (referenced), Darry (referenced), Blackhaven (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** During Arya's captivity with the Brotherhood Without Banners, shortly after the confrontation at Stoney Sept over the Hound's capture
- **time_markers:** "A year in the wild will melt the flesh off a man" (Thoros describing time spent as an outlaw); the Hound was recently captured ("sleeping off a drunk under a willow tree"); Stoney Sept confrontation is a recent event (days prior, implied)
- **chapter_summary:** Arya and Gendry are brought hooded to the hollow hill, the Brotherhood Without Banners' secret refuge. The Mad Huntsman delivers his captive, Sandor Clegane, who is unhooded and confronted by the outlaws. The Brotherhood accuses the Hound of murders committed by Lannister forces. Arya pushes forward to accuse him of killing Mycah the butcher's boy, and the Hound recognizes her. Lord Beric Dondarrion sentences the Hound to trial by combat, igniting his sword with his own blood. After a fierce duel, the Hound kills Beric but suffers severe burns to his arm. Arya steals a dagger and confronts the Hound, who confesses to killing Mycah. Lem disarms her, and then Lord Beric appears alive behind her.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya unhooded in the hollow hill** — Harwin pulls the hood off Arya's head inside the cave; she blinks at the ruddy firelight after being in darkness
2. **Gendry unhooded** — Lem removes Gendry's hood; Gendry asks what this place is
3. **Brotherhood members emerge** — People step from roots, tunnels, crannies, and shadows to look at the captives; men, women, and children watch warily
4. **Arya spots Thoros** — Greenbeard points out the "wizard"; Arya sees a tall thin man in ratty robes talking to Tom and does not initially recognize him as the fat, bald Thoros she remembers
5. **Mad Huntsman delivers the Hound** — The Huntsman shoves his bound captive into the firelight; Arya and Gendry are forgotten
6. **Flashback: Stoney Sept confrontation** — Arya recalls the tense standoff at Stoney Sept where Lem and Greenbeard claimed the Huntsman's captive; Tom soothed the hounds, Tansy brought bones and mutton, Anguy stood with arrow notched in the brothel window; the Huntsman agreed to bring his prize to Lord Beric
7. **Thoros unmasks the Hound** — Thoros yanks the hood off Sandor Clegane and mockingly welcomes him
8. **Clegane and Thoros recognize each other** — The Hound identifies Thoros from tourneys; Thoros says he overthrew the Hound three times with his flaming sword in melees
9. **Thoros declares his religious transformation** — Says the Lord of Light has woken in his heart; many powers long asleep are waking; he has seen forces in his flames
10. **Beric Dondarrion descends and introduces the Brotherhood** — The scarecrow knight descends from the weirwood roots; recounts their origin: 120 men sent from King's Landing by Ned Stark to bring the king's justice to Gregor Clegane; more than 80 now dead; they fight on for Robert and the realm
11. **Brotherhood accuses the Hound** — Members name dozens of people killed by Lannister forces; Thoros accuses House Clegane of being built on dead children, citing the murders of Aegon and Rhaenys
12. **The Hound defends himself** — Denies personal responsibility; says he was not at Sherrer or Mummer's Ford; questions whether being born Clegane is a crime; delivers speech about knights being swords with horses
13. **Arya accuses the Hound of murdering Mycah** — She pushes past Greenbeard and screams her accusation; the Hound does not recognize her at first
14. **The Hound recognizes Arya** — Calls her "the little sister" and "the brat who tossed Joff's pretty sword in the river"; tells her she's believed dead
15. **Beric sentences the Hound to trial by combat** — Declares no one present can judge truth or falsehood; the Lord of Light will judge; trial by battle
16. **Preparations for combat** — The Huntsman cuts the Hound's bonds; Clegane is given his sword but not armor ("your innocence must be your armor"); Beric has his squire Ned remove his breastplate — revealing horrific scars (lance-wound crater, matching back scar, crushed skull, missing eye, ring around neck); Beric dons his surcoat
17. **Thoros warns against treachery** — Three bowmen (Anguy, Dennet, Kyle) nock arrows to guard against the Hound cutting his way free or seizing a hostage
18. **Beric ignites his sword** — Draws his longsword across his left palm, drawing blood; the sword takes fire; Gendry whispers a prayer
19. **Brotherhood prays to the Lord of Light** — Thoros leads call-and-response prayer: "Lord of Light, look down upon us" / "defend us" / "protect us in the darkness" / "shine your face upon us"; Brotherhood chants "For the night is dark and full of terrors"
20. **Trial by combat begins** — The Hound charges; Beric blocks with flaming sword and shield; fierce exchange of cuts; flames trail like ribbons; Beric surrounded by cage of fire
21. **Beric drives the Hound back** — Fills the air with ropes of fire; drives Clegane toward the firepit; the Hound parries grimacing at the heat; Brotherhood shouts encouragement
22. **Extended combat at the firepit** — Back-and-forth fighting; Clegane nearly backed into the fire; regains ground; forced back again; bulling three steps forward and two back
23. **The Hound's shield catches fire** — Beric's downcut splits the shield; flames spread across the painted yellow surface; three black dogs engulfed; Clegane chops the burning shield apart but his sleeve and left arm catch fire
24. **Beric killed** — Beric moves to finish the Hound; Clegane screams, raises his sword in both hands, and brings it crashing down; the burning sword snaps in two; the Hound's cold steel plows into Beric where shoulder joins neck and cleaves him to the breastbone; blood rushes out; Beric falls dead
25. **The Hound extinguishes himself** — Rips off shield remnants, rolls in dirt to smother the fire on his arm; whimpers trying to rise
26. **The Hound begs for help** — Cradling his burned arm, crying, he pleads for someone to help him; Arya is astonished to see him cry
27. **Thoros tends to the aftermath** — Orders Melly to see to the Hound's burns; has Lem and Jack carry Beric's body; wrenches the Hound's sword from Beric and thrusts it into the earth; Thoros, Ned, and the body disappear into a tunnel
28. **Huntsman wants the Hound caged** — Spits and says they should take him to Stoney Sept for a crow cage; Arya agrees
29. **Harwin declares divine judgment** — Says R'hllor has judged the Hound innocent; Arya cannot even pronounce "R'hllor"
30. **Arya steals Greenbeard's dagger** — Yanks the dagger from Greenbeard's sheath and evades both Greenbeard and Gendry
31. **Arya confronts the burned Hound** — The Hound sees the dagger and tells her to shove it in — "It's cleaner than fire"; a piece of burned flesh falls from his arm and his knees buckle; Tom catches him
32. **The Hound confesses** — Admits he rode Mycah down, cut him in half, and laughed; says he watched them beat Arya's sister and watched them cut her father's head off
33. **Lem disarms Arya** — Grabs her wrist and twists the dagger away; she kicks but cannot get it back; screams at the Hound to go to hell
34. **Lord Beric appears alive** — A voice "scarce stronger than a whisper" says "He has"; Arya turns to find Lord Beric standing behind her, bloody hand clutching Thoros's shoulder

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Rage and desire for vengeance dominate — Arya wants the Hound dead for Mycah's murder. She prayed for his death "hundreds and hundreds of times." She feels exultation when it seems Beric is winning ("He's going to lose... He is, he is, he's going to die"), then despair and disbelief when Beric falls. She experiences fury at her sister Sansa for telling a different version of events. She feels astonishment at seeing the Hound cry ("He's crying like a little baby"). Her skin prickles at the phrase "neither wolves nor lions."
- **Primary preoccupation:** Justice/revenge for Mycah's murder; the Hound must die or be punished
- **Key decisions made:** Pushes past Greenbeard to publicly accuse the Hound; steals Greenbeard's dagger to confront the Hound after R'hllor's judgment clears him; ultimately does not stab him
- **Self-deception flags:** Arya is so consumed by her desire for revenge that she dismisses the trial by combat result — "If there were gods, why didn't Lord Beric win? She knew the Hound was guilty." She treats the divine judgment as simply wrong rather than considering any alternative interpretation. She also remembers Sansa's testimony as a pure lie without acknowledging any complexity.
