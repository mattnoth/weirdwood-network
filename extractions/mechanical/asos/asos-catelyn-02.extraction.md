# ASOS — Catelyn II

## Chapter Metadata

- **chapter_number:** 15
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark

- **location_primary:** Riverrun
- **location_secondary:** The Crag (discussed), the Twins (discussed), Winterfell (discussed), King's Landing (discussed), Bitterbridge (discussed), Moat Cailin (discussed)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Edmure's battle at the Stone Mill and Robb's campaign in the west; after the Battle of the Blackwater
- **time_markers:** "It had been raining for days now"; "Her father was growing weaker and more delirious with every passing day"; "On the day her brother returned, a few hours after their argument"; Robb is sixteen; "close to forty men poured out through the castle gates" on a prior day; the Westerlings "have come a long way in a short time"
- **chapter_summary:** Robb returns to Riverrun from his western campaign and publicly forgives Catelyn for releasing the Kingslayer, though Lord Karstark denounces her as a traitor. Robb introduces Catelyn to his new wife Jeyne Westerling, revealing he broke his marriage pact with the Freys. In a private meeting with Catelyn, Edmure, and the Blackfish, Robb and Brynden reveal that Edmure's defense of the fords at the Stone Mill — though tactically victorious — ruined their strategic plan to trap Tywin in the west, which would have allowed Stannis to take King's Landing. The chapter ends with discussion of how to win back the Freys and retake the north, with Catelyn implying that a person, not gold or honors, will be needed to appease Lord Walder.

## Events & Actions
1. **Grey Wind's arrival detected** — Catelyn hears the kennels erupt in baying, recognizing Robb's return by the effect of Grey Wind's scent on the hounds.
2. **Catelyn reflects on her confinement** — She notes Edmure has avoided her since their first visit, Ser Desmond Grell still denies her freedom, and Lord Hoster grows weaker daily, muttering "Tansy" and begging forgiveness.
3. **Ser Robin Ryger's return** — Robin Ryger and his men return on foot, drenched and exhausted. The Kingslayer contrived to sink their galley and escape. Catelyn is denied permission to speak with Ser Robin.
4. **Frey riders depart Riverrun angrily** — On a prior day, Catelyn observed from the roof as close to forty men poured out through the castle gates, some trampling Robb's white direwolf banner. She thinks she recognized Ser Perwyn Frey and Martyn Rivers.
5. **Catelyn summoned to the Great Hall** — Ser Desmond escorts her to Robb's audience. She bathes, dresses, and combs her hair before going.
6. **Robb publicly praises Edmure's victory** — Robb lauds those who fell at the Stone Mill and notes Lord Tywin "ran off to fight Stannis."
7. **Acclamation** — The Greatjon roars "King in the North!" and river lords answer "King of the Trident!" with thunderous stamping and fist-pounding.
8. **Lord Karstark denounces Catelyn** — Lord Rickard Karstark accuses Catelyn of robbing him of vengeance for his sons Torrhen and Eddard, calling her act treason. The Greatjon dismisses it as "a mother's folly."
9. **Robb forgives Catelyn** — Robb rebukes Karstark for calling Catelyn a traitor, then forgives her, saying he knows what it is to follow the heart into folly — a deliberate setup for his own revelation.
10. **Lord Karstark storms out** — Karstark declares amends cannot warm his sons in their cold graves and leaves the hall.
11. **Well-wishers approach Catelyn** — Lady Mormont offers sympathy, the Greatjon lifts her off her feet, Galbart Glover and Jason Mallister are cooler, Jonos Bracken is almost icy. Edmure says he prays for her girls.
12. **Brynden Tully greets Catelyn** — The Blackfish leaps from the dais and pulls her into his arms, saying "It is good to see you home, Cat."
13. **Robb introduces the Westerlings** — Lady Sybell, Ser Rolph Spicer, Ser Raynald, Jeyne, Elenya, and Rollam are presented to Catelyn one by one.
14. **Robb reveals his marriage to Jeyne Westerling** — Catelyn realizes Robb staged his forgiveness of her to preempt her objection to his own "folly done for love."
15. **Catelyn accepts Jeyne** — She kisses Jeyne on both cheeks and says "I have a new daughter," though stiffly.
16. **Westerlings retire** — Lady Sybell requests chambers; Edmure volunteers to escort them; Rollam reluctantly leaves his post as squire.
17. **Catelyn notes Grey Wind's absence from the hall** — She realizes the direwolf was not at Robb's side during the audience.
18. **Catelyn and Robb discuss the political consequences** — Robb confirms the Freys have left; the marriage brings only fifty swords and a dozen knights. Lord Gawen is a captive at Seagard and the marriage was without his consent.
19. **Robb describes the circumstances of his marriage** — He was wounded storming the Crag, Jeyne nursed him, and they slept together the night he learned of Bran and Rickon's supposed deaths. He married her the next day for honor.
20. **Discussion of Grey Wind** — Robb explains Grey Wind has killed men at the Crag, Ashemark, and Oxcross; Jeyne is anxious around him; Grey Wind bares his teeth at Ser Rolph Spicer. Catelyn urges Robb to send Ser Rolph away. Robb initially resists but agrees.
21. **Catelyn invokes the direwolves' divine origin** — She tells Robb the wolves are more than wolves, possibly sent by the old gods — five pups for five Stark children. Robb corrects her: "Six. There was a wolf for Jon as well."
22. **Robb's disillusionment with the wolves** — He says he stopped believing the wolves were protectors when he learned Theon had murdered Bran and Rickon, since their wolves did them no good.
23. **Private meeting in the audience chamber** — Robb, Catelyn, Edmure, and the Blackfish convene. Robb removes his crown.
24. **Blackfish and Robb rebuke Edmure** — They reveal the Stone Mill defense was a strategic blunder: Robb wanted Tywin to come west to be trapped and exhausted, allowing Stannis to take King's Landing. Edmure's defense delayed Tywin just long enough for him to learn of Stannis's advance and join the Tyrells.
25. **Blackfish explains the planned trap** — All-horsed northern force would have led Tywin's mainly-foot army on a chase, then taken a strong defensive position on the gold road. Tywin would have been trapped a thousand leagues from where he needed to be.
26. **Strategic situation discussed** — Once Joffrey weds, the Lannisters and Tyrells will march together. The Freys may fight Robb if Black Walder has his way.
27. **Catelyn insists on retaking the north** — She tells Robb his first duty is to win back Winterfell and hang Theon, or else put off his crown.
28. **Discussion of retaking the north** — Robb reports Ser Rodrik defeated ironmen near Torrhen's Square and was assembling a host at Castle Cerwyn, but there has been no news for a long while. Edmure notes the ironmen control the sea and hold Moat Cailin, and angry Freys could trap them on the causeway.
29. **Robb determines to win back the Freys** — He is willing to offer Lord Walder anything — apologies, honors, lands, gold.
30. **Catelyn implies a person is needed** — She says "Not something. Someone." The chapter ends.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious and fearful while awaiting Robb's judgment; relieved when forgiven; dismayed and annoyed upon learning of the Westerling marriage, mixed with rueful admiration for Robb's staged scene; deeply concerned about the political consequences; alarmed by Grey Wind's absence from Robb's side and by Grey Wind's hostility toward Ser Rolph; strategically focused during the private meeting; grief for Bran and Rickon; pained awareness that Robb is no longer a boy
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether Robb will forgive her, then immediately replaced by the catastrophic political implications of his Frey-breaking marriage and how to recover the situation
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts Jeyne Westerling as Robb's wife (pragmatic acceptance, not approval); urges Robb to send Ser Rolph Spicer away based on Grey Wind's hostility; counsels Robb that a person, not things, will be needed to appease Lord Walder
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn tells herself "He will forgive me" — which proves true but the forgiveness is itself part of Robb's manipulation. She also does not interrogate her own instinct about who was "missing" from the hall until later realizing it was Grey Wind, but the missing figures may also be the departed Frey retainers.
