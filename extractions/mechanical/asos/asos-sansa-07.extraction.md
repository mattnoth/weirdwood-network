# ASOS — Sansa VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 81
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Sansa VII
- **first_available:** ASOS Sansa VII
- **location_primary:** The Eyrie
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell (recreated in snow, extensively described from memory), Gates of the Moon (mentioned), King's Landing (mentioned), Riverrun (mentioned in Lysa's account), the Fingers (mentioned), Dragonstone (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** Late in ASOS; Petyr and Lysa have been married for some time; Sansa has been at the Eyrie posing as Alayne Stone; winter snows are reaching the Eyrie
- **time_markers:** Before dawn (Sansa wakes, room "cold and black," "Dawn had not yet come"); dawn ("Dawn stole into her garden like a thief," "the grey of the sky grew lighter still"); late afternoon ("It was late that afternoon when Lady Lysa summoned her"); Petyr has been gone four days meeting the Corbrays; Sansa describes herself as thirteen; Robert Arryn described as "eight going on three"
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa wakes before dawn and discovers snow falling on the Eyrie. She goes into the garden and builds a snow model of Winterfell, aided by Petyr Baelish, who has returned from the Vale. After building the Broken Tower together, Petyr kisses Sansa against her will. Robert Arryn arrives and destroys parts of the snow castle, leading to a shaking fit when Sansa accidentally rips his doll. Late that afternoon, Lysa summons Sansa to the High Hall, accuses her of seducing Petyr, reveals extensive history with Petyr stretching back to Riverrun, and drags Sansa to the Moon Door intending to push her out. Petyr intervenes, calms Lysa, then pushes her through the Moon Door to her death, immediately framing Marillion for the murder.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa wakes before dawn** — She wakes with every nerve atingle, having dreamed of home and Winterfell, sharing a bedchamber with Arya. She remembers she is Alayne Stone at the Eyrie.
2. **Snow discovered falling on the Eyrie** — Sansa opens her shutters and sees heavy snowfall blanketing the garden, the first snow she has seen since leaving Winterfell.
3. **Sansa dresses and enters the garden** — She dons warm clothing including a blue lambswool dress and white fox fur cloak, and descends to the garden. She finds it a world of whites, blacks, and greys.
4. **Sansa kneels by the broken statue** — She turns her face to the sky by the broken, half-buried statue of the weeping woman, tasting the snow, remembering Winterfell.
5. **Sansa begins building snowballs** — She remembers a snowball fight with Arya and Bran at Winterfell, where Jory Cassel pulled them apart laughing.
6. **Sansa builds a snow castle that becomes Winterfell** — She constructs walls, towers, keeps, kitchen, armory, stables, godswood (twigs for trees), and lichyard (bark for gravestones). She uses her knowledge of Winterfell's layout.
7. **Sansa refuses to break her fast** — Her maid calls from the window; Sansa shakes her head and continues building.
8. **Dawn breaks; observers appear** — Servants watch briefly; Lady Lysa gazes from her balcony in a blue velvet robe trimmed with fox fur, then disappears; Maester Colemon peers from the rookery.
9. **Petyr Baelish arrives and offers to help** — He has returned from four days meeting the Corbrays. He suggests packing snow around a stick for the bridges. He asks permission to enter her castle.
10. **Sansa and Petyr build together** — He constructs a latticework of twigs for the glass gardens; they build the guardshall, the First Keep (with "white lump" gargoyles), and the Broken Tower. He tells Sansa about dreaming of Winterfell after Cat left with Ned.
11. **Sansa throws snow at Petyr** — After building the Broken Tower together, she grabs snow and flings it in his face. He calls it "unchivalrously done."
12. **Sansa confronts Petyr** — She accuses him of playing false by bringing her to the Eyrie instead of taking her home as he swore. She draws courage from being "within the walls of Winterfell."
13. **Petyr admits he played her false, then kisses Sansa** — He says he lied about the castle being what pleased him most, steps closer, pulls her into his arms, and kisses her. He tastes of mint. She briefly yields before wrenching free.
14. **Sansa rebukes Petyr** — She tells him he should be kissing his wife. She glances at Lysa's balcony (now empty). Petyr calls her beautiful. She says "I might have been your own daughter." He admits she might have been, but calls her more beautiful than Catelyn at her age.
15. **Robert Arryn interrupts** — He arrives, sees the snow castle, and begins smashing it with his cloth doll, chanting "I'm a giant." He knocks off gatehouse towers and breaks a section of wall.
16. **Sansa accidentally rips Robert's doll** — She grabs for his hand but catches the doll instead; it tears, leaving her with the head and Robert with the body. Sawdust stuffing spills in the snow.
17. **Robert has a shaking fit** — He collapses across the castle, limbs flailing, shattering towers and bridges. Petyr seizes his wrists and shouts for the maester.
18. **Maester Colemon treats Robert** — Guards and serving girls help restrain the boy. Colemon holds his head and gives him half a cup of dreamwine. The fit ebbs to a shaking of the hands. He orders leeching.
19. **Robert is carried away** — He accuses Sansa of killing his doll, calls her a bastard, says he hates her, protests the leeching.
20. **Sansa impales the doll's head on a stick** — In anger, she smashes the torn doll's head onto a broken branch and pushes it atop the shattered gatehouse. Littlefinger laughs and says "that's not the first giant to end up with his head on Winterfell's walls."
21. **Sansa returns to her bedchamber** — She sits by the fire, worrying Lysa will punish her for Robert's fit. She considers the possibility of being sent to the Gates of the Moon (which she would welcome). She thinks about refusing to marry Robert.
22. **Marillion summons Sansa to the High Hall** — Late afternoon; he tells her Lady Lysa requires her presence. His eyes undress her. He tells her he is composing "The Roadside Rose" about a beautiful baseborn girl. He bars the High Hall doors behind them.
23. **Sansa enters the High Hall** — She walks down the blue silk carpet between marble pillars. Lysa sits alone on the dais in the weirwood chair, wearing cream velvet and sapphires. Robert's chair is empty.
24. **Lysa accuses Sansa of kissing Petyr** — She says she saw it from the balcony. Sansa insists "He kissed me."
25. **Lysa demands confession and threatens punishment** — She mentions the custom of whipping boys used in the Free Cities. She accuses Sansa of being wanton like her mother.
26. **Lysa reveals history with Petyr at Riverrun** — She describes Catelyn dancing six dances with Petyr, Petyr trying to kiss Catelyn (who pushed him away and laughed), Petyr getting drunk, Edmure getting drunk, and Brynden carrying Petyr to bed.
27. **Lysa reveals she gave Petyr her maidenhead** — She stole to his bed that night to comfort him; she bled; he called her "Cat" before falling asleep. She stayed until dawn.
28. **Lysa reveals Catelyn kissed Petyr in the godswood** — But according to Lysa, Catelyn never meant it, never wanted him.
29. **Lysa reveals her father forced her to drink moon tea** — Tansy, mint, wormwood, honey, and pennyroyal — an abortifacient. She says "they murdered him" (the baby). She insists she never knew, she only drank what her father gave her.
30. **Lysa drags Sansa to the Moon Door** — She seizes Sansa's wrist and pulls her across the hall. She commands Sansa to open the door. Sansa lifts the three bronze bars.
31. **Lysa tries to push Sansa through the Moon Door** — She pushes Sansa toward the open door, propelling her toward the six-hundred-foot drop. Sansa's foot breaks through snow at the edge. Marillion continues to play "Hey-nonny, hey-nonny, hey-nonny-hey." Sansa grabs Lysa's braid; they teeter on the edge. Guards pound on the barred door.
32. **Petyr arrives through the lords' entrance** — He comes in the back way behind the dais. He shouts at Lysa to stop.
33. **Lysa reveals she poisoned Jon Arryn** — She tells Petyr: "You told me to put the tears in Jon's wine, and I did. For Robert, and for us!" She also reveals: "I wrote Catelyn and told her the Lannisters had killed my lord husband, just as you said."
34. **Lysa reveals she got Petyr his first post at court** — She made Jon Arryn bring him to court so they could be near each other.
35. **Petyr calms Lysa and gets her to release Sansa** — He tells Lysa he will never leave her side "for as long as we both shall live." Lysa throws herself into his arms.
36. **Sansa crawls to safety** — She wraps her arms around the nearest pillar, her right shoe missing (fallen through the Moon Door).
37. **Petyr pushes Lysa through the Moon Door** — He says "I've only loved one woman, I promise you." Lysa asks "Only one?" He answers "Only Cat" and gives her a short, sharp shove. She falls without screaming.
38. **Petyr frames Marillion** — He tells Sansa to let the guards in and says "This singer's killed my lady wife."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Begins nostalgic and homesick, dreaming of Winterfell and her siblings; transitions to creative absorption and even joy while building the snow castle; draws courage from being "within the walls of Winterfell" to confront Petyr; shifts to alarm and revulsion when kissed; becomes angry and frustrated when Robert destroys the castle ("A mad rage seized hold of her"); feels resigned dread anticipating Lysa's punishment; experiences terror and hysteria at the Moon Door; ends in shock and physical trembling.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Home — Winterfell is the emotional center of the chapter, from dream to snow castle to its destruction. Secondary: fear of her precarious position at the Eyrie, dependent on dangerous and unstable people.
- **Key decisions made:** Confronts Petyr about his broken promise to take her home; resolves to tell Lysa she will not marry Robert (never carried out); agrees to Petyr's framing of Marillion (implicit — she goes to let the guards in).
- **Self-deception flags:** None prominent. Sansa's perceptions in this chapter appear clear-eyed: she recognizes Petyr's dishonesty, Lysa's instability, Robert's unfitness, and her own powerlessness.
