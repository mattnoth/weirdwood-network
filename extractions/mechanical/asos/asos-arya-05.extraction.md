- **chapter_summary:** Arya arrives at Stoney Sept with the Brotherhood Without Banners. Harwin tells her about her father's victory in the Battle of the Bells. In the market square, she finds northmen dying in crow cages for crimes at Tumbler's Falls; she gives them water before Anguy mercy-kills them with arrows. The group stays at the Peach, a brothel disguised as an inn, where Arya meets Bella (who claims to be King Robert's daughter) and quarrels with Gendry. She has a wolf dream that night, and wakes the next morning to the Mad Huntsman returning with a Lannister captive.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at Stoney Sept** — Harwin tells Arya the story of the Battle of the Bells as they ride toward the town gate.
2. **Entry through sally port** — The captain of the gate recognizes the Brotherhood and opens a sally port; reports on food situation, wolves, Mummers, and the Kingslayer hunt.
3. **News of Lord Hoster** — Lem asks about "Lord Edmure" and the captain confirms Lord Hoster is dead or dying.
4. **Riding through damaged town** — Arya observes every third house is a blackened shell; sees no people initially.
5. **Signs of life** — Anguy points out bowmen on a roof and boys in rubble; a baker shouts down to Lem; townspeople emerge.
6. **Discovery of crow cages** — In the market square, Arya finds a dozen iron cages with dead and dying prisoners hung from posts near the fountain.
7. **Arya questions the prisoners** — She asks whose men they were; a townsman explains they killed eight people at Tumbler's Falls seeking the Kingslayer; one did raping.
8. **Arya gives water to the prisoners** — She fills her cup at the fountain and, with Harwin and Gendry boosting her, pours water over the three living prisoners.
9. **Anguy mercy-kills the prisoners** — He shoots three arrows, killing the fat man, the bearded man, and the old man in quick succession.
10. **Arrival at the Peach** — The group dismounts at stables across from the inn; Tansy greets them boisterously, teasing each member.
11. **Tansy pinches Gendry** — She calls him "a fine young ox" and mentions Alyce will like his arms; Gendry blushes.
12. **Forced bathing** — Two serving wenches carry Arya upstairs, scrub her, dress her in linen and lace despite her protests.
13. **Evening at the Peach** — Arya observes the brothel's nature; men come and go; Tom sings.
14. **Bella approaches Gendry** — Claims to be King Robert's daughter, named for the battle; offers to "ring his bell"; Gendry refuses and leaves.
15. **Arya and Bella converse** — Arya notes Bella's black hair is like Robert's and Gendry's; Bella moves on to Jack-Be-Lucky.
16. **Old drunk approaches Arya** — Calls her "pretty little peach"; his eyes crawl over her.
17. **Gendry protects Arya** — Returns, claims Arya is his sister, intimidates the old man into leaving.
18. **Arya and Gendry quarrel** — Gendry is angry about class differences; tells her to go away; calls her "m'lady."
19. **Tansy's gossip about Lady Catelyn** — Arya overhears Tansy telling Lem and Harwin that Lady Catelyn spent the night in Jaime's cell with "the wench who slew Renly" and cut him loose "for love."
20. **Arya recites her death list** — In bed, she whispers: Queen Cersei, King Joffrey, Ser Ilyn, Ser Meryn, Dunsen, Raff, Polliver, the Tickler, the Hound, Ser Gregor the Mountain.
21. **Wolf dream** — Arya dreams of running with a wolf pack through a wet wood; they bring down a horse and feast; she howls at the moon.
22. **Morning: Mad Huntsman arrives** — Arya wakes to dogs barking; from the window she sees the Huntsman's riders, the dead fat man pulled from his cage and thrown to dogs, and a new Lannister captive being threatened with the cage.
23. **Arya recognizes the captive** — She sees the captive's face when a stone turns his head; it is not the Kingslayer; she thinks "the gods had heard her prayers after all."
