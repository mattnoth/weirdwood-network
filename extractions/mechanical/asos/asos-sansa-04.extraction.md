- **chapter_summary:** Sansa wakes from a dream of Winterfell and prepares for Joffrey and Margaery's wedding day. She attends the queen's breakfast in the Queen's Ballroom, where gifts are presented to Joffrey including a Valyrian steel sword from Lord Tywin that Joffrey names Widow's Wail. Joffrey uses the sword to destroy Tyrion's gift, a rare illuminated book called Lives of Four Kings. Afterward, Sansa and Tyrion ride in a litter toward the Great Sept of Baelor, during which Tyrion questions her about Bran's fall at Winterfell and hints at suspicions about Joffrey's involvement.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa wakes from dream** — She dreamed of Winterfell, running in the godswood with Lady; her father and brothers were there, all warm and safe.
2. **Sansa reflects on her losses** — She steels herself ("I must be brave") and lists those she believes dead: Lady, Robb, Bran, Rickon, Arya, her father, her mother, Septa Mordane.
3. **Sansa observes sunrise** — She opens the shutters and watches cloud formations that look like two castles, which merge into one as the sun rises.
4. **Maids arrive with bath water** — Shae and Brella, her new maids (Tyrion replaced Cersei's spies), bring hot water. They look at the clouds.
5. **Sansa bathes and dresses** — She trembles; Brella notices but Sansa blames the water temperature.
6. **Tyrion arrives with Podrick** — He asks for wine. Sansa notes it is the 300th year since Aegon's Conquest. His clothing is soiled and unkempt.
7. **Tyrion changes clothes** — He and Podrick leave to change; both return presentable. Podrick wears purple, white, and gold (House Payne colors).
8. **Sansa asks about House Payne heraldry** — Pod stammers that their arms are purple and white chequy with gold coins. Tyrion says there's a tale behind those coins.
9. **Sansa considers begging off** — She thinks of feigning illness but tells herself to be brave like Robb.
10. **Breakfast in the Queen's Ballroom** — An elaborate meal with music and entertainment from Ser Dontos and Moon Boy.
11. **Tyrion drinks, barely eats; Sansa nibbles** — Tyrion has several cups of wine. Sansa's stomach flutters every time Joffrey looks at her.
12. **Cersei presents the wife's cloak** — The cloak she wore when wed to Robert, the same Joanna wore when wed to Tywin. Sansa thinks it looks threadbare.
13. **Gift-giving begins** — Traditional Reach custom for the wedding morning. Multiple lords and knights present gifts to Joffrey.
14. **Joffrey receives numerous gifts** — Bow and arrows (Jalabhar Xho), riding boots (Lady Tanda), jousting saddle (Ser Kevan), scorpion brooch (Prince Oberyn), silver spurs (Ser Addam), tourney pavilion (Lord Mathis Rowan), model war galley named King Joffrey's Valor (Lord Paxter Redwyne).
15. **Joffrey boasts about Stannis** — Says he'll make the galley his flagship to sail to Dragonstone and kill Stannis.
16. **Tyrion presents Lives of Four Kings** — Joffrey is uninterested and dismissive.
17. **Joffrey insults Tyrion and Sansa** — He mocks them for not having produced an heir; says he'll show Tyrion "how it's done" once he gets Margaery with child. The court laughs.
18. **Mace Tyrell presents golden chalice** — Seven faces with house sigils in gemstones. Joffrey says he'll replace the direwolf with a squid.
19. **Tywin presents Valyrian steel sword** — The ballroom falls silent; red and black ripples in the steel shimmer in morning light.
20. **Joffrey names the sword Widow's Wail** — He slashes at the air and forces Ser Balon Swann to dodge.
21. **Joffrey destroys the book** — He hacks Lives of Four Kings apart with Widow's Wail, cutting through the heavy leather cover and hacking the thick tome in half a dozen cuts.
22. **Ser Garlan Tyrell reproves Joffrey** — He notes only four copies existed illuminated in Kaeth's own hand; now three.
23. **Joffrey demands a better present** — Tells Tyrion and Sansa they owe him a replacement.
24. **Tyrion makes pointed dagger suggestion** — Suggests "a dagger of the same fine Valyrian steel... with a dragonbone hilt." Joffrey reacts sharply, then accepts but wants gold and rubies instead.
25. **They depart for the wedding** — Tyrion takes Sansa by the hand as they leave.
26. **Oberyn and Ellaria walk with them** — Sansa observes Ellaria closely for the first time. She recalls Shae's gossip about Ellaria worshipping a Lysene love goddess.
27. **Oberyn and Tyrion discuss the book** — Oberyn saw the Citadel's copy; they debate King Viserys and Baelor the Blessed.
28. **Sansa defends Baelor** — She cites his walking the Boneway barefoot and rescuing the Dragonknight. Ellaria corrects the viper story — Baelor was bitten half a hundred times.
29. **Oberyn quips about Joffrey** — "I've seen no snakes in this Red Keep... so how do you account for Joffrey?"
30. **Tyrion and Sansa enter the litter** — Tyrion asks Sansa to close the curtains so the people of King's Landing won't throw dung.
31. **Sansa says she's sorry about the book** — Tyrion responds he should have known better and "should have seen... a good many things."
32. **Tyrion questions Sansa about Bran** — He asks if there was ill feeling between Bran and Joffrey. Sansa recalls Bran and Tommen fighting with wooden swords in play.
33. **Tyrion references Catelyn's accusation** — He says Catelyn accused him falsely; he never harmed Bran and means no harm to Sansa.
34. **Tyrion compares love for siblings** — He says Sansa loved her brothers as he loves Jaime.
35. **Sansa deflects carefully** — She calls her brothers traitors who went to traitors' graves, clearly guarding her words.
36. **Tyrion says he won't tell her about Robb and Catelyn's deaths** — He notes she's never asked; Sansa says she'd rather not know, it would give her bad dreams.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fearful, melancholic, isolated, guarded. Opens with grief and loneliness (dreaming of family, listing the dead). Dreads the day ahead. Stomach flutters with anxiety whenever Joffrey looks at her. Wishes she could crawl back into bed. Steels herself with "I must be brave" (twice). Feels bitter about being made a Lannister.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting through the wedding day; fear of Joffrey; grief for her lost family; wariness of Tyrion's intentions.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to attend the breakfast rather than beg off; decides to give a politically safe answer when Tyrion compares their love for siblings ("My brothers were traitors"); decides she'd rather not know how Robb and Catelyn died.
- **Self-deception flags:** Sansa believes all her siblings are dead — Bran, Rickon, and Arya are alive (but she cannot know this within this chapter). She lies to Brella about why she's trembling ("The water is not hot enough"). She gives performatively loyal answers to Tyrion that she does not believe.
