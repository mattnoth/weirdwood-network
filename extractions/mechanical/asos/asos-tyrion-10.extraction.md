# ASOS — Tyrion X

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 71
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion X
- **first_available:** ASOS Tyrion X
- **location_primary:** King's Landing — Red Keep (throne room, outer ward, tower cell)
- **location_secondary:** Castle Black (recalled), The Wall (recalled), Casterly Rock (recalled in Oberyn's story), Sunspear (mentioned), Starfall (mentioned), The Arbor (mentioned), Oldtown (mentioned), Shield Islands (mentioned), Crakehall (mentioned), Lannisport (mentioned), Norvos (mentioned), Dorne (mentioned), Dragonpit (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** Day of final testimony at Tyrion's trial and the following day for the trial by combat; immediately follows Shae's testimony
- **time_markers:** "When dawn broke" (chapter opening); "By evenfall I may stand condemned"; Tyrion sleeps that night; "He rose at first light" the next morning; "The day was grey and windy"; sun breaks through clouds during the combat; "the cold dawn air" (at combat's end)
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion endures Shae's devastating testimony at his trial, in which she lies about their relationship and accuses him and Sansa of plotting to murder Joffrey. After the court laughs at the intimate detail of "my giant of Lannister," Tyrion erupts, declares he is guilty only of being a dwarf, and demands trial by battle. Prince Oberyn Martell volunteers as his champion against Ser Gregor Clegane. The next day, after Oberyn tells Tyrion the story of his and Elia's betrothal quest and hints at crowning Myrcella, the combat begins. Oberyn dominates the fight with speed and his spear, wounding and felling the Mountain, but when he stands over Gregor demanding a confession about Elia, Clegane grabs him, confesses to the murders, and crushes Oberyn's skull. Tyrion is condemned and taken to the black cells.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion contemplates his options at dawn** — He considers denying everything, accusing Sansa and Ser Dontos, confessing to go to the Wall, or relying on the Red Viper to defeat Gregor. He cannot eat.
2. **Podrick Payne announces the escort** — Ser Addam Marbrand and six gold cloaks wait outside. Tyrion asks Pod if he believes Tyrion is guilty; Pod cannot answer. Tyrion thanks Pod for his loyal service.
3. **Tyrion is escorted to the throne room** — Guards on walls, grooms, scullions, and serving girls watch him cross the yard. Inside, knights and lords whisper and move aside.
4. **Shae is brought in as witness** — Gold cloaks lead her in. Tyrion initially thinks Varys betrayed her location, then remembers he himself placed her where she could be found.
5. **Shae testifies against Tyrion** — She claims Tyrion and Sansa plotted Joffrey's murder after Robb Stark's death, that Tyrion planned to kill Cersei, Tywin, and eventually Tommen to take the crown. She claims she was Tyrion's whore, forced into his service after his wildlings brought her at the Green Fork. She reveals the intimate phrase "my giant of Lannister."
6. **The court erupts in laughter** — Oswald Kettleblack laughs first, followed by Boros, Meryn, Cersei, Ser Loras, and hundreds of lords and ladies. Lord Tywin alone does not laugh.
7. **Tyrion demands attention and offers a conditional confession** — He shouts over the laughter; Tywin raises a hand to silence the hall. Tyrion says to remove "this lying whore" and he will confess.
8. **Shae is removed** — Gold cloaks march her out. Her eyes meet Tyrion's. He wonders what Cersei promised her and predicts she will end up servicing gold cloaks.
9. **Tyrion delivers his defiant "confession"** — He declares himself guilty of being a dwarf, of being born and living. He tells his father "I have been on trial for being a dwarf my entire life."
10. **Tyrion declares his innocence and demands trial by battle** — He says he did not kill Joffrey but wishes he had, and wishes he had enough poison for the whole court. He appeals to the gods.
11. **Cersei names Ser Gregor Clegane as the crown's champion** — She confirms Tyrion's right to trial by combat and announces that Gregor returned to the city "the night before last."
12. **Prince Oberyn volunteers as Tyrion's champion** — He rises and says "The dwarf has quite convinced me." Cersei shows sudden doubt. The hall erupts again.
13. **Lord Tywin sets the combat for the morrow** — He declares "I wash my hands of it," gives Tyrion a cold angry look, and strides out with Ser Kevan.
14. **Tyrion spends the evening in his cell** — He drinks wine, eats lightly, reflects on how his demand has disrupted Tywin's plans: a win for Oberyn inflames Highgarden against Dorne; a win for Gregor might push Doran Martell to crown Myrcella. He sings verses of a song.
15. **Tyrion notes Ser Kevan does not visit** — He assumes Kevan is with Tywin placating the Tyrells and expects never to see his uncle again.
16. **Tyrion sleeps deeply and wakes with appetite** — He eats a large breakfast of fried bread, blood sausage, applecakes, and eggs with onions and Dornish peppers.
17. **Tyrion visits Oberyn arming for combat** — Oberyn drinks red wine while four Dornish lordlings help him into armor. Tyrion warns him about Gregor's size, strength, and fighting style.
18. **Oberyn displays his spear** — An eight-foot turned ash shaft with two feet of steel spearhead, the edges glistening black. Tyrion notices but decides not to ask if it is poisoned.
19. **Oberyn hints at a Dornish alliance** — He suggests Tyrion visit Sunspear with Sansa, refers to "Queen Myrcella," and discusses matters of mutual interest with Doran including "the laws of inheritance and succession."
20. **Oberyn tells the story of the betrothal quest** — He and Elia traveled to Starfall, the Arbor, Oldtown, the Shield Islands, Crakehall, and Casterly Rock seeking betrothals. He mocked suitors, including Baelor Hightower ("Baelor Breakwind"). Their mothers — companions to Princess Rhaella at court — had planned to betroth Jaime to Elia (or Cersei to Oberyn).
21. **Oberyn reveals Tywin's refusal** — Tywin refused the match, saying Cersei was meant for Prince Rhaegar. When asked for Jaime, he offered Tyrion instead — taken as an outrage.
22. **Oberyn invokes Elia and her children** — He says they have waited long for justice and that "this day they shall have it."
23. **The crowd assembles for the combat** — Approximately a thousand people; they line wallwalks, perch on steps, towers, windows, bridges, balconies, and roofs. Some bring chairs or barrels. Children sit on shoulders.
24. **Lord Tywin and Ser Kevan take their platform** — Erected beside the Tower of the Hand, halfway between the champions.
25. **The High Septon prays** — He asks the Father Above for judgment and the Warrior to lend strength to the just cause.
26. **Ser Osmund Kettleblack brings Gregor his shield** — The Clegane dogs are painted over with the seven-pointed star.
27. **The combat begins** — Fifty yards apart. Oberyn advances quickly, Gregor ominously. At ten yards, Oberyn calls out, identifying himself and Elia.
28. **Oberyn fights with speed and reach** — He circles, jabs, and darts back, using his spear's length and Gregor's limited vision. He repeatedly demands: "You raped her. You murdered her. You killed her children." Gregor fights in sullen silence, grunting, growing slower.
29. **Gregor kills a stableboy** — During a charge toward the stable, a spectator stumbles into Oberyn's path. Oberyn dodges; Gregor severs the boy's arm, then splits the top half of his head off.
30. **Oberyn wounds Gregor under the arm** — He uses his polished shield to reflect sunlight into Gregor's eyeslit. When Gregor raises his own shield against the glare, Oberyn drives his spear through the joint under the arm, through mail and boiled leather.
31. **Gregor collapses** — The spear goes through the back of his knee. He falls face-first, rolls onto his back.
32. **Oberyn drives the spear through Gregor's belly** — Screaming "EEEEELLLLLLIIIIIAAAAA!" he vaults over the fallen Mountain. The ash shaft snaps. Four feet of broken spear pin Gregor to the ground. Oberyn claims Gregor's greatsword.
33. **Gregor seizes Oberyn** — When Oberyn stands over him, Gregor grabs him behind the knee, pulls him down, wraps an arm around him.
34. **Gregor confesses and kills Oberyn** — He says "Elia of Dorne. I killed her screaming whelp. Then I raped her. Then I smashed her fucking head in. Like this." He pushes steel fingers into Oberyn's eyes, smashes his teeth, and crushes his skull with his fist.
35. **Tyrion vomits** — He falls to his knees retching up his entire breakfast.
36. **Tyrion is condemned** — He never hears his father speak the words. He laughs hysterically when he remembers "snakes had no hands."
37. **Tyrion is taken to the black cells** — He realizes halfway down the serpentine steps that he is not being returned to his tower cell.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Cascading from anxious dread (dawn, facing trial) to bitter humiliation (Shae's testimony, the court's laughter) to volcanic rage (the "confession" and demand for trial by combat) to strange peace (in his cell, having defied his father) to dark humor and resignation (singing, reflecting) to cautious hope (visiting Oberyn, hearing battle plans) to growing terror during combat to momentary elation (Oberyn wounding Gregor) to horror (Oberyn's death) to hysterical despair (laughing, retching, taken to black cells)
- **Primary preoccupation:** His betrayal by Shae, the cruelty of his father's judgment, and whether Oberyn can kill Gregor. Beneath it all, the knowledge that he is being destroyed not for killing Joffrey but for being a dwarf.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Demands trial by battle rather than confessing for a sentence to the Wall. (2) Publicly declares his hatred for the court rather than making a strategic defense. (3) Accepts Oberyn's hints about a Dornish alliance without commitment but without refusing.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion tells himself he feels "strangely at peace" after demanding trial by combat, but his satisfaction is primarily about disrupting Tywin's plans and Cersei's expectations rather than confidence in his survival. He finds the political chaos his death would cause "almost worth dying" for — rationalizing a likely death sentence as a victory. He also tells himself "I put my life in the Red Viper's hands" — framing a desperate gamble as a deliberate choice.
