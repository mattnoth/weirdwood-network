# ASOS — Samwell II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 34
- **pov_character:** Samwell Tarly
- **pov_chapter_number:** Samwell II
- **first_available:** ASOS Samwell II
- **location_primary:** Craster's Keep
- **location_secondary:** The Fist of the First Men (referenced), Castle Black (referenced), The Wall (referenced), White Harbor (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after the survivors of the Fist have reached Craster's Keep; the storm has passed, melt is occurring; they prepare to depart
- **time_markers:** "nine days past" (since Kedge Whiteye took Bannen's foot); "two nights past" (Craster beating a girl); afternoon (when Sam steps outside); sunset (Bannen's body burned); "first light" (planned departure next day); the storm has passed and days at Craster's have been less cold; icicles melting; "drip-drip-drip of water melting"
- **chapter_summary:** The Night's Watch survivors shelter at Craster's Keep where Bannen lies dying and Gilly gives birth to a boy. Sam endures mockery as "Slayer" and discusses dragonglass with Lord Commander Mormont, who worries about how to arm the Watch against the Others. After Craster announces his son's birth and Sam offers to take the child, Mormont rebukes him. Bannen dies and is burned. At a farewell feast, starving brothers accuse Craster of hoarding food; Dirk slits Craster's throat, triggering a mutiny in which Ollo Lophand stabs Mormont fatally. As Mormont dies in Sam's lap—sending a last message to his son Jorah—Craster's wives urge Sam to take Gilly and flee before "Craster's sons" arrive.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sam feeds dying Bannen** — Sam spoons onion broth into Bannen's mouth; the ranger cannot swallow and repeatedly says "I'm cold"
2. **Craster mocks the Watch** — Craster eats sausage while declaring Bannen is dead already; tells Giant he's given them what he can and wants them gone
3. **Gilly gives birth in the loft** — Craster's wives attend her; she screams through labor; Craster threatens to hit her
4. **Sam recalls Craster beating a wife** — Two nights past, Craster beat a younger girl; brothers muttered but did nothing; "His roof, his rule"
5. **Sam exits the hall** — Overwhelmed by smoke, screams, and moans; steps into the cloudy afternoon; observes the surrounding camp
6. **Camp activities observed** — Ollo and Tim Stone tend horselines; brothers butcher weak horses; sentries patrol; axes in forest; firepits burning
7. **Archery practice** — Sweet Donnel and Ulmer compete at the butt; Ulmer wins; Ulmer tells tales of the Kingswood Brotherhood
8. **Sam mocked and humiliated** — Donnel and Alan invite "Slayer" to shoot; Sam refuses, gets boot stuck in mud, flees to laughter
9. **Sam talks with Grenn** — Grenn splits logs; they discuss the Slayer name, bravery, pretending to be brave; Sam insists it was the dragonglass that killed the Other
10. **Sam reflects on dragonglass supply** — Only 2 daggers, 19 arrows, 1 spear remain; Jon's cache left behind on the Fist; fire arrows didn't stop wights
11. **Mormont returns from ranging** — Rides in with Dywen and Ronnel Harclay; Harclay reports only 22 mounts, half won't reach the Wall
12. **Mormont summons Sam** — Discusses dragonglass; asks why the Watch doesn't have more; Sam mentions children of the forest used it; Mormont says children are dead
13. **Craster announces his son's birth** — Exits the hall declaring "I have a son"
14. **Sam offers to take the baby** — Blurts out "We could take him"; Craster spits on Sam's foot; Mormont furiously rebukes Sam inside
15. **Bannen dies** — Giant pulls a fur over Bannen's face; "He said he was cold"; Dirk blames Craster for starving him
16. **Bannen's funeral pyre** — Body burned at sunset; Tim Stone and Garth of Oldtown carry corpse; brothers divide his belongings; Mormont says words; Sam smells burning flesh like roast pork, vomits behind the hall
17. **Dolorous Edd's dark humor** — Jokes about eating Bannen with applesauce; tells Sam they ride at first light; relays Dywen's quip about riding dead horses
18. **Craster announces farewell feast** — Becomes almost amiable; offers horsemeat, beer, bread
19. **Feast begins** — Wives cook and serve; Sam sits between Grenn and Orphan Oss; eats a half-rotten onion raw; cannot stomach horsemeat
20. **Food confrontation erupts** — Clubfoot Karl demands more bread; brothers speculate about hidden stores (hams, sausages, oats, apples, mutton); Mormont orders silence; Karl defies him
21. **Craster attacks** — Grabs his axe, orders men out; Garth curses him "bastard"; Craster vaults the table with axe
22. **Dirk murders Craster** — Grabs Craster's hair, slits throat ear to ear; Craster crashes across Ser Byam
23. **Full mutiny** — Mormont condemns the murder ("The gods will curse us"); Dirk threatens Craster's wife at knifepoint for food; Garth of Greenaway and Ollo block Mormont
24. **Ollo stabs Mormont** — Mormont grabs for his dagger; Ollo shoves knife into his belly and yanks it out
25. **Chaos** — Garth of Greenaway kills Garth of Oldtown; Rolley falls from loft breaking his neck; Sweet Donnel knocks a woman down; Grenn slaps Sam and flees with Giant and Edd
26. **Mormont's dying words** — Sam holds Mormont's head in his lap; Mormont tells him to go to the Wall, tell them everything — the Fist, wildlings, dragonglass; dying wish: tell Jorah to take the black, forgive him
27. **Mormont dies** — Eyes go open and staring; lips stop moving; raven asks "Corn?"
28. **Craster's wives urge Sam to flee** — Two old women and Gilly; tell Sam the mutineers are gorging in the cellar or with young wives in the loft; Dyah has caught two horses; they warn "Craster's sons" (the Others) are coming
29. **Gilly pleads** — Offers to be Sam's wife; says if he doesn't take the boy "they will"; the old woman says "the white cold's rising... the sons"

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Exhausted, frightened, self-loathing, helpless; transitions through guilt (about Gilly), shame (about "Slayer" mockery), horror (mutiny and deaths), and numbness (holding dying Mormont — "I am not afraid. It was a queer feeling")
- **Primary preoccupation:** Inadequacy and cowardice; wishing Jon were here; worry about Gilly's baby; guilt about failing to be brave; obsession with dragonglass as their only weapon
- **Key decisions made:** Offers to take Gilly's baby (impulsive); at chapter's end, faced with decision to flee with Gilly as the old women urge
- **Self-deception flags:** "My father saw me true. I have no right to be alive when so many brave men are dead" — wallows in self-pity rather than recognizing he did slay an Other; believes the dragonglass killed the Other rather than taking credit for wielding it; at the end tells Gilly "I'm a craven" while simultaneously staying at Mormont's side
