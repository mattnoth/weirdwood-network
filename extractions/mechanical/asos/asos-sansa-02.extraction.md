Sansa reflects on the pleasant companionship of Margaery and her ladies. During a recent hawking trip, Margaery called Sansa "sister" and confirmed the plan for Sansa to marry Willas Tyrell; when Sansa warned Margaery about Joffrey's cruelty, Margaery dismissed the danger, citing Ser Loras's protection on the Kingsguard. Sansa recalls a tense encounter with Ser Dontos in the godswood, where he urged her to abandon the Tyrell match and stick with his escape plan involving the silver hair net on Joffrey's wedding night, warning that the Tyrells want her claim to Winterfell, not her. Alone, Sansa fantasizes about life with Willas at Highgarden while wrestling with doubts about his motives and anxiety about Joffrey.

## Events & Actions
1. **Seamstress measures Sansa for a new wardrobe** — An old seamstress measures Sansa for a complete new wardrobe (gown, smallclothes, hose, kirtles, mantles, cloaks) commanded by Queen Cersei, to be ready before Joffrey's wedding. Six seamstresses and twelve apprentices have been assigned to the work.
2. **Sansa suspects Margaery or Olenna behind the gown** — After the seamstress leaves, Sansa wonders why Cersei would be generous to her and suspects the gown is really Margaery's or the Queen of Thorns' doing.
3. **Sansa reflects on companionship with Margaery's ladies** — Sansa recalls the pleasant company of Margaery's ladies: Lady Leonette teaches her the high harp, Lady Janna shares gossip, Merry Crane tells stories, Lady Bulwer reminds her of Arya. The three Tyrell cousins (Elinor, Alla, Megga) have taken Sansa into their company. They do needlework, play tiles, sing in the sept, and share Margaery's bed for late-night whispers.
4. **Megga gushes about Joffrey's beauty** — Megga praises Joffrey's "beautiful lips" and expresses sympathy for Sansa losing him. Sansa suppresses her true feelings, noting Butterbumps is not present to provide noise cover.
5. **Elinor's betrothal to Alyn discussed** — The ladies discuss Elinor's betrothed, Alyn Ambrose, a squire who wore her favor at the Battle of the Blackwater and killed a Myrish crossbowman and a Mullendore man-at-arms. Sansa reflects that these girls are children who have never seen real violence.
6. **Hawking trip with Margaery** — The day before last, Sansa went hawking with Margaery outside King's Landing for the first time since the battle. They crossed the Blackwater on a flat-bottomed ferry and entered the devastated kingswood. Sansa's merlin brought down three ducks; Margaery's peregrine took a heron in flight.
7. **Margaery calls Sansa "sister"** — When briefly alone, Margaery tells Sansa that Willas has the best birds in the Seven Kingdoms and calls her "sister," squeezing her hand.
8. **Sansa warns Margaery about Joffrey** — Overcome with emotion, Sansa urges Margaery not to marry Joffrey, telling her "He's not like he seems" and "He'll hurt you."
9. **Margaery dismisses the warning** — Margaery says Joffrey is "spoiled and vain" and "as cruel as you say" but that her father forced Joffrey to name Loras to the Kingsguard before agreeing to the match. She compares this to Prince Aemon the Dragonknight protecting Queen Naerys.
10. **Margaery initiates a race back to the river** — Margaery says racing will "drive our guards quite mad" and rides off without waiting for an answer.
11. **Sansa reflects on the Dragonknight parallel's flaws** — Sansa thinks that while Aegon the Unworthy never harmed Naerys, he did execute a different Kingsguard who fell in love with one of his mistresses. She worries Loras being a Tyrell might not be enough protection and imagines a war between Lannister and Tyrell men within King's Landing.
12. **Sansa tells Dontos about the Tyrell marriage plan** — In a recalled godswood encounter, Sansa tells Dontos she is going to Highgarden to marry Willas Tyrell.
13. **Dontos urges Sansa to abandon the Tyrell plan** — Dontos says "these Tyrells are only Lannisters with flowers," insists Sansa must wear the silver hair net on the night of Joffrey's wedding and proceed with the escape. He says he has arranged a ship and a boat.
14. **Sansa refuses Dontos's escape plan** — Sansa says "When I wanted to escape you wouldn't take me, and now I don't need to" and insists Willas will keep her safe.
15. **Dontos warns Sansa about her claim** — Dontos tells Sansa "you are heir to Winterfell" and says the Tyrells want to wed her claim, not her. He grabs her pleadingly and she wrenches free, leaving him beneath the heart tree.
16. **Sansa contemplates her claim to Winterfell** — Lying in bed at night, Sansa considers Dontos's words. With Bran and Rickon believed dead, she is heir after Robb. She reassures herself that Robb will marry and have sons, and that Willas will have Highgarden and wouldn't need Winterfell.
17. **Sansa fantasizes about life with Willas** — She whispers his name into her pillow, imagines sitting in gardens with puppies, floating down the Mander, naming children Eddard, Brandon, and Rickon, raising them to be valiant and to hate Lannisters. But her imagination keeps replacing Willas with Loras.
18. **Sansa has a nightmare about marrying Joffrey** — She dreams she is marrying Joffrey instead of Margaery, and on their wedding night he transforms into the headsman Ilyn Payne.
19. **Sansa decides to light candles for Margaery and Loras** — She resolves to pray to the Mother Above for Margaery's protection and to the Warrior for Loras.
20. **Sansa plans to wear the new gown to the wedding** — She decides the new gown must be for the ceremony at the Great Sept of Baelor, and wants to save it to take to Highgarden so she can look beautiful for Willas.
