Arstan Whitebeard discusses dragon lore and Prince Rhaegar's history with Dany and Ser Jorah, provoking Jorah's suspicion. When the wind finally returns after seven days, the fleet resumes sailing. That night, Dany demonstrates the "dracarys" fire command to Jorah, who then warns her against trusting Belwas, Whitebeard, and Illyrio, and proposes diverting to Astapor to buy Unsullied soldiers. After telling the tale of the Three Thousand of Qohor, Jorah convinces Dany to change course. He then kisses her and proposes himself as husband, invoking the "three heads of the dragon."

## Events & Actions
1. **Dany watches dragons from the forecastle** — Standing on the forecastle of the becalmed Balerion, Dany watches Drogon, Rhaegal, and Viserion chase each other across the sky, noting she is as happy as she can remember being.
2. **Dany reflects on the voyage from Qarth** — She recalls her Dothraki's fear of the sea, a squall six days into the voyage, and her own love of the sea from childhood crossings of the narrow sea fleeing the Usurper's knives.
3. **Dany remembers Viserys** — She recalls his cruelty (twisting her hair when she said she wanted to be a sailor) but also his kindness as a boy who told her tales of the Seven Kingdoms.
4. **Captain Groleo comments on the calm** — The captain wishes the ship Balerion could fly like its namesake dragon, speaking in bastard Valyrian with Pentoshi accents.
5. **Description of the three dragons in flight** — Viserion (cream and gold) and Rhaegal (green and bronze) soar in circles trying to climb above each other; they dive, tumble, splash into the sea, and rise again. Drogon is miles away hunting. All three are described as the size of small dogs, all wings and neck and tail.
6. **Ser Jorah joins Dany on the forecastle** — He asks about Drogon; they discuss how large dragons may grow. Jorah mentions tales of dragons plucking giant krakens from the sea and living a thousand years.
7. **Arstan Whitebeard enters the conversation** — He tells of Balerion the Black Dread being two hundred years old at death, able to swallow an aurochs whole; says "a dragon never stops growing so long as he has food and freedom."
8. **Whitebeard describes the Dragonpit** — He recounts the immense domed castle in King's Landing with iron doors wide enough for thirty knights abreast; notes that pit dragons never reached the size of their ancestors due to confinement.
9. **Jorah and Whitebeard debate** — Jorah dismisses Whitebeard's claim about walls limiting growth; Whitebeard retorts "Men are men. Dragons are dragons."
10. **Whitebeard reveals he served under Aerys** — He walked beneath the dragonskulls in the throne room. Dany asks about her father; Whitebeard says Aerys was "often pleasant" but "could be very harsh to those he thought his enemies."
11. **Discussion of Rhaegar** — Whitebeard names Rhaegar's companions: squires Myles Mooton and Richard Lonmouth (both later knighted by Rhaegar), Jon Connington, and Arthur Dayne as his oldest friend. Dany recalls Viserys calling Arthur Dayne the only knight who was Rhaegar's peer.
12. **Whitebeard discusses Rhaegar's character** — He calls Rhaegar "able, determined, deliberate, dutiful, single-minded" and tells of his bookish youth and sudden transformation into a warrior after finding something in his scrolls.
13. **Jorah and Whitebeard's tension over the tourney at Lannisport** — Whitebeard alludes to Jorah's victory with "a lady's favor knotted round an arm," angering Jorah. Dany intervenes, knowing the reference to Lynesse Hightower.
14. **Strong Belwas appears on deck** — Squat, scarred, and demanding food. Whitebeard excuses himself to attend Belwas.
15. **Jorah warns Dany about Whitebeard** — He says the old man is too old to be a squire and too well-spoken for a servant; Dany defends Whitebeard's frankness as commanded.
16. **Dany reflects on threats** — She catalogs dangers: Robert's bounty (now Joffrey's debt), the poisoned wine attempt, Pyat Pree's Sorrowful Man, hostile Dothraki khalasars, Xaro Xhoan Daxos's greed, and Quaithe's cryptic nature.
17. **The wind returns** — After seven days becalmed, the wind fills the sails; the crew cheers and Captain Groleo rushes aft shouting commands; the fleet resumes sailing.
18. **Night: Dany feeds the dragons in her cabin** — She demonstrates the "dracarys" command for Ser Jorah: Drogon sears a chunk of salt pork mid-air and catches it. Rhaegal tries to steal it. Viserion blasts flame at the word, nearly singeing Jorah.
19. **Dany explains "dracarys"** — High Valyrian for "dragonfire"; she chose it as a command no one would utter by chance.
20. **Jorah expresses concerns about Belwas, Whitebeard, and Illyrio** — He invokes the warlocks' prophecy of three betrayals (blood, gold, love); notes Mirri Maz Duur was the first; warns two remain. He argues Illyrio's ships, captains, and men surround her and she will be in Illyrio's power in Pentos.
21. **Jorah proposes diverting to Astapor** — He suggests buying Unsullied soldiers rather than going directly to Pentos, to arrive with an army rather than four swords.
22. **Jorah tells the tale of the Three Thousand of Qohor** — Four hundred years ago, Khal Temmo's fifty thousand Dothraki attacked Qohor. The Bright Banners and Second Sons fled; three thousand Unsullied held the gates. Eighteen charges broke against their shields and spears. Twelve thousand Dothraki died, including Temmo, his bloodriders, kos, and sons. The survivors cut their braids before the surviving six hundred Unsullied.
23. **Dany agrees to change course to Astapor** — Jorah persuades her that Illyrio's trade goods can pay for Unsullied; she decides to command Groleo to change course.
24. **Jorah kisses Dany** — As she dresses, he pulls her close and kisses her. She notes his smell (sweat, salt, leather), his scratchy beard, his sweet mouth. She reflects only Khal Drogo had kissed her before.
25. **Dany pushes back** — She tells Jorah the kiss was not fitting; she is his queen, not his woman.
26. **Jorah proposes himself as husband** — He invokes "the dragon has three heads": three dragons need three riders, as Aegon had Rhaenys and Visenya. He declares no man will ever be half so true to her.
