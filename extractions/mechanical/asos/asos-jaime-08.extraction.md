- **chapter_summary:** Jaime Lannister, newly installed as Lord Commander of the Kingsguard, reads the White Book in the Round Room of White Sword Tower, comparing Barristan Selmy's illustrious entry to his own sparse page. He convenes all five available Sworn Brothers — Ser Boros Blount, Ser Meryn Trant, Ser Osmund Kettleblack, Ser Balon Swann, and Ser Loras Tyrell — to discuss their failure to protect Joffrey and to assert his authority over each. He assigns Boros as food taster, interrogates Kettleblack's background, reprimands Trant, questions Swann's family loyalties, and engages Loras in a discussion about Renly's death and Brienne of Tarth's innocence, ultimately sending Loras to judge Brienne himself.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jaime moves to the Lord Commander's apartments** — He relocates from his Kingsguard sleeping cell (where he has lived for eighteen years) to the topmost floor of White Sword Tower, which has a view of the sea.
2. **Jaime reads the White Book** — He reviews the entries for Barristan Selmy and himself, finding Selmy's page full of accomplishments and his own "scant and mingy."
3. **Jaime reflects on Tyrion's trial** — He has been attending his brother's trial, standing at the back of the hall, unrecognized by Tyrion and many at court.
4. **Jaime reflects on his alienation** — He feels like a stranger in his own house: Joffrey is dead, Tywin has disowned him, and Cersei has avoided being alone with him since Joffrey's funeral.
5. **Jaime reminisces about the Kingswood Brotherhood** — He recalls serving as squire to Lord Sumner Crakehall, saving Sumner's life from Big Belly Ben, fighting the Smiling Knight, and watching Ser Arthur Dayne slay the Smiling Knight with Dawn.
6. **Kingsguard assembles** — Ser Osmund arrives first, followed by the other four Sworn Brothers. Ser Osney and Ser Osfryd Kettleblack guard the king, along with Ser Garlan Tyrell.
7. **Jaime questions the Kingsguard about Joffrey's death** — He asks all five whether Tyrion poisoned Joffrey. Meryn and Boros point to Tyrion; Balon Swann notes others had access; Osmund (called "Ser Oswald" in text) jokingly blames the High Septon; Loras accuses Sansa Stark.
8. **Jaime assigns Boros as food taster** — He orders Ser Boros to taste everything Tommen eats or drinks, citing Boros's failure to protect Tommen from Tyrion's sellswords.
9. **Boros confronts Jaime** — Ser Boros clasps his sword hilt and calls Jaime a cripple, suggesting Jaime should be the food taster. Jaime challenges him to draw his sword. Boros spits at Jaime's feet and walks out without drawing.
10. **Jaime reflects on his vulnerability** — He privately acknowledges Boros could have killed him, but knows the others must not realize how weak he is.
11. **Jaime interrogates Osmund Kettleblack** — He questions Osmund's background; Osmund claims to have fought with the Gallant Men in the Stepstones and Disputed Lands, knighted by "Ser Robert Stone" who is conveniently dead.
12. **Jaime reprimands Meryn Trant** — He tells Trant to stop obeying orders to beat women and children, and to obey only Cersei, Tywin, and Jaime. If Tommen orders something harmful ("kill his horse"), Trant should consult Jaime.
13. **Jaime questions Balon Swann's loyalty** — He praises Swann's valor but presses him about his brother Donnel's shifting allegiances and his father's neutrality. Swann swears he will not do as Jaime did (betray his king).
14. **Jaime engages Loras Tyrell** — He compares their youthful Kingsguard appointments, provoking Loras by claiming he was better. Loras retorts "And now you're older." Jaime recognizes in Loras a mirror of his own younger self.
15. **Jaime asks Loras about Renly's armor at the Blackwater** — Loras reveals Garlan wore the armor, and it was Littlefinger's suggestion to frighten Stannis's men.
16. **Loras discusses Renly's death** — Loras says he buried Renly himself in a secret place at Storm's End. He swears to defend Tommen but will never betray Renly's memory, calling Renly "the best of them."
17. **Jaime defends Brienne** — He argues Brienne is too loyal and forthright to have killed Renly, citing her protection of him during their journey. He challenges Loras to explain how anyone could fight a shadow.
18. **Loras reveals doubt** — He admits the gorget was cut clean through with a single stroke — impossible even for Brienne or the Mountain without a heavy axe — and questions why she would armor Renly then cut his throat.
19. **Jaime sends Loras to judge Brienne** — He instructs Loras to visit Brienne in her cell, hear her answers, and either accuse her or release her based on his own judgment as a knight.
20. **Loras shares Renly's words about Brienne** — Before leaving, he tells Jaime that Renly said all his other knights wanted castles or honors, but Brienne only wanted to die for him. Loras implies that if Brienne is innocent, then Robar and Emmon must have been involved.
21. **Jaime reflects alone** — He wonders why he doesn't feel compelled to avenge Joffrey ("He was my son, my secret son"). He considers getting a golden hand to replace his stump, thinking Cersei might like it. He notes there are "other debts to pay."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Conflicted and reflective. Feels alienated — "a stranger in my own House." Experiences melancholy reading the White Book, seeing how little his page contains. Nostalgic for the old Kingsguard he served with as a youth. Grimly aware of his physical vulnerability — privately acknowledges Boros could have killed him. Feels a troubling absence of grief or rage over Joffrey's death. Wonders what he has become.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Taking command of a Kingsguard he considers unworthy; the gap between who he was and who he is now (both physically and morally); his secret knowledge that Joffrey was his son and his surprising lack of motivation to avenge him; managing his public image of strength despite being unable to fight.
- **Key decisions made:** Assigns Boros as food taster; orders Meryn to obey only Cersei, Tywin, and Jaime; sends Loras to judge Brienne rather than condemning or freeing her himself; considers getting a golden prosthetic hand; considers consulting Varys about Sansa's escape.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jaime tells Loras "I would have done the same" (killed Ser Robar and Ser Emmon in grief-rage) — the narration explicitly calls this "the lie." He maintains an outward image of confidence and combat readiness while privately knowing he is helpless in a fight. He recognizes himself in Loras ("He's me") but frames his own youthful arrogance as something that happened to him rather than a choice. His thought "What am I, if I do not lift the hand I have left to avenge mine own blood and seed?" raises the question but he immediately pivots to thinking about a golden hand and "other debts to pay" rather than pursuing vengeance.
