# ASOS — Davos IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 37
- **pov_character:** Davos Seaworth
- **pov_chapter_number:** Davos IV
- **first_available:** ASOS Davos IV
- **location_primary:** Dragonstone
- **location_secondary:** Claw Isle (discussed), King's Landing (referenced), Storm's End (referenced), the Blackwater (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** After the Battle of the Blackwater; Davos has been imprisoned in Dragonstone's dungeons for some time
- **time_markers:** Nighttime (no sunlight through arrow slits; starry sky visible from the Chamber of the Painted Table); supper time approaching when the chapter opens; "last night" Lamprey brought beef-and-bacon pie
- **chapter_summary:** Davos is retrieved from his dungeon cell by Ser Axell Florent, who threatens him and demands Davos support his bid to become Hand of the King. Brought before Stannis in the Chamber of the Painted Table, Davos is asked his opinion on Ser Axell's plan to sack Claw Isle in retaliation for Lord Celtigar's defection. Davos denounces the plan as folly and cowardice, arguing the smallfolk are not traitors. Stannis agrees, dismisses Ser Axell, and names Davos Lord of the Rainwood, Admiral of the Narrow Sea, and Hand of the King. Melisandre arrives and performs a ritual with three leeches fed on king's blood, naming the three "usurpers" — Joffrey Baratheon, Balon Greyjoy, and Robb Stark — as Stannis throws each leech into the fire.

## Events & Actions
1. **Lord Alester hears footsteps approaching** — He alerts Davos that someone is coming; Davos identifies it as likely Lamprey with supper, but Alester notes multiple voices.
2. **Lamprey opens the cell** — Lamprey arrives with keys; Ser Axell Florent and four guardsmen follow. They call only for Davos, not Lord Alester.
3. **Lord Alester is left in darkness** — Ser Axell calls his brother a traitor and orders the torch taken, leaving Alester in the dark. Lord Alester begs for the light.
4. **Ser Axell declares R'hllor the only god** — When Lord Alester appeals to "gods," Ser Axell corrects him: "There is only R'hllor, and the Other."
5. **Davos ascends from the dungeon** — Davos climbs the turnpike stair, passing iron gates and arrow slits, eventually reaching the surface and crossing the stone bridge to Stone Drum.
6. **Davos smells the sea air** — On the bridge, Davos fills his lungs and prays to wind and water for strength. A nightfire burns in the yard below with queen's men singing to R'hllor.
7. **Ser Axell threatens Davos on the bridge** — Ser Axell stops on the bridge and demands Davos support his bid for Hand of the King. He offers a ship as reward or threatens a fatal fall, physically shoving Davos against the waist-high bridge wall.
8. **Davos is brought before Stannis** — In the Chamber of the Painted Table, Davos sees Stannis looking dramatically aged and gaunt since their last meeting. Stannis greets Davos warmly.
9. **Stannis discusses the penalty for treason** — Stannis asks Davos what the penalty for treason is; Davos answers "death." Stannis cites historical precedents (Daemon Blackfyre, the brothers Toyne, Rhaenyra Targaryen, etc.).
10. **Davos speaks for Lord Alester** — Despite knowing he should stay silent, Davos says Lord Alester meant no treason.
11. **Stannis condemns Lord Alester** — Stannis says Alester would have sold his rights and given Shireen to a bastard born of incest. He contrasts himself with Robert, who could turn enemies into friends.
12. **Ser Axell proposes the Claw Isle raid** — Ser Axell presents his plan (devised with Salladhor Saan) to sack Claw Isle, burn Lord Celtigar's castle, and put its people to the sword as punishment for Celtigar's defection.
13. **Stannis asks Davos's opinion** — Stannis notes the military feasibility of the plan but asks for Davos's honest assessment.
14. **Davos calls the plan folly and cowardice** — Davos argues that Celtigar's smallfolk are not traitors; their men died fighting for Stannis on the Blackwater. Attacking women, children, and old men is wrong.
15. **Davos challenges Stannis about Aerys** — When Stannis insists every man must remain loyal to his rightful king, Davos asks pointedly whether Stannis remained loyal to King Aerys when Robert rebelled. Ser Axell draws his dagger and cries treason.
16. **Stannis dismisses Ser Axell** — Stannis orders Ser Axell to put up his knife and leave, sending for Melisandre instead.
17. **Stannis reflects on Robert and the Iron Throne** — Alone with Davos, Stannis discusses the painful choice between blood and liege during Robert's Rebellion, describes the Iron Throne as uncomfortable, and wonders why his brothers wanted it.
18. **Stannis explains his duty** — He says the throne is his by law as Robert's heir, and he has a duty to bring justice: to punish Cersei for her crimes, to scour the court clean.
19. **Stannis asks why Davos wanted to murder Melisandre** — Davos admits his motive: four of his sons burned on the Blackwater.
20. **Stannis defends Melisandre** — Stannis says the Blackwater fires were the work of the Imp and the pyromancers, not Melisandre. He reveals it was Melisandre who told him to send for Davos when Ser Axell wanted to burn him.
21. **Stannis mentions Edric Storm's illness** — The boy has a chill, fever, and cough; Maester Pylos has been leeching him. Stannis notes "there is power in a king's blood."
22. **Stannis shows Davos the Painted Table** — He sweeps his hand across his realm, noting Aegon's wisdom in painting no borders. "It is all one."
23. **Stannis agrees the Claw Isle plan was evil** — He says Celtigar must pay in his own person, not through his smallfolk. He promises justice when he comes into his kingdom.
24. **Stannis names Davos Lord of the Rainwood, Admiral of the Narrow Sea, and Hand of the King** — He uses Lightbringer to perform the ceremony. Davos protests his low birth and illiteracy but Stannis insists.
25. **Stannis lists his remaining loyal men** — Ser Gilbert Farring (Storm's End with 200 men), Lord Morrigen, the Bastard of Nightsong, young Chyttering, cousin Andrew, but trusts none as much as Davos.
26. **Melisandre arrives with the leeches** — She enters carrying a covered silver dish and speaks of the great battle to come against a nameless evil power.
27. **Stannis describes his fire vision** — He saw white ashes falling like snow, a ring of torches, men in black on a high hill in a forest, and shapes moving through the snow. He felt a terrible cold.
28. **Melisandre requests Edric Storm** — She asks Stannis to give her "the boy" so she can "wake the stone dragon." Stannis refuses, saying the boy is innocent and his own blood.
29. **Stannis orders the leech ritual instead** — He tells Melisandre to use the leeches and do her work, refusing to hear more about dragons.
30. **The leech ritual** — Melisandre throws powder on the brazier coals, producing pale flames. Stannis takes three fat black leeches from the silver dish and throws each into the fire after naming a "usurper": Joffrey Baratheon, Balon Greyjoy, and Robb Stark.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fearful but resolute; Davos expects he may be burned but faces the situation with courage. He feels sick of heart and tired. He experiences shock and being stunned when named Hand. He feels pity for Lord Alester. He is frightened by Stannis's conviction about his fire vision. He harbors murderous intent toward Melisandre (his hands "were still long enough to wrap about a woman's throat") but this impulse is not acted upon.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and loyalty to Stannis. He navigates between Ser Axell's threats and his duty to give the king honest counsel. His four dead sons weigh on him — they are his stated motive for wanting to kill Melisandre.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to reject Ser Axell's bribe/threat and speak honestly to Stannis about the Claw Isle plan. Makes the bold choice to challenge Stannis about his loyalty to Aerys. Accepts (reluctantly) his appointment as Hand.
- **Self-deception flags:** None apparent. Davos is notably honest with himself throughout — he acknowledges his fear, his weak arguments, his murderous impulse, and his limitations (illiteracy, low birth).
