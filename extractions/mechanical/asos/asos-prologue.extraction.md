- **chapter_summary:** Chett, a Night's Watch steward, leads dogs on a failed bear hunt while reviewing his plan to murder Lord Commander Mormont and desert with thirteen other brothers during the third watch. The wildling army of Mance Rayder, estimated at twenty to thirty thousand, is moving south down the Milkwater toward the Fist. Mormont announces a plan to ride at dawn and ambush the wildling column in the foothills of the Frostfangs. As Chett prepares to kill Samwell Tarly that night, a sudden heavy snowfall ruins his escape plan, and then three blasts of the horn sound — signaling the approach of the Others.

## Events & Actions
1. **Failed bear hunt** — Chett leads dogs along a riverbank near the Milkwater to track a bear. The dogs refuse the scent in the extreme cold. The big black bitch snarls at Chett.
2. **Mutiny plan reviewed** — Chett, Lark, and Small Paul discuss their plan to murder Mormont, Blane, Grubbs, Aethan, Dywen, Bannen, and Samwell Tarly during the third watch, then desert with fourteen brothers total.
3. **Small Paul's concern about the raven** — Small Paul worries about who will feed Mormont's talking raven and whether it will tell on them; Chett gives him the bird.
4. **Kedge Whiteye's scouting report recalled** — Chett recalls Kedge's report from three days prior: Harma the Dogshead has 500 mounted in the van; the main body is 20-30 thousand, mostly women, children, and animals, moving slowly south down the Milkwater.
5. **Debate over strategy recalled** — Thoren Smallwood advocates attacking the wildling column. Ser Ottyn Wythers urges retreat to the Wall. Smallwood persuades Ser Mallador Locke to his side. Mormont waits for his scouts (Buckwell, Halfhand) who are late.
6. **Return to the Fist** — Chett, Lark, and Small Paul abandon the hunt and head back, wading across an icy brook. Lark says he'll flee to the Sisters with his cousins.
7. **Sam's archery practice** — Sam practices with a longbow near the forest edge. Misses twice, hits the charcoal outline on his third shot ("between the ribs"). Dolorous Edd and Grenn encourage him.
8. **Chett intimidates Sam** — Chett mocks Sam's marksmanship, describing a wildling planting an axe between his eyes. Dolorous Edd deflects with a joke about Chett's skull.
9. **Chett reports to Mormont** — Chett reports the failed bear hunt at Mormont's tent. Mormont expresses regret about lacking fresh meat. His raven repeats "Meat."
10. **Perimeter circuit** — Chett walks the perimeter, shares sourleaf, takes the mood of the watch. Wind rises; little Giant comments the wind sounds like a wailing babe. Dywen notes the unnatural silence — no frogs, no owls, no wolves.
11. **Chett assesses his conspirators** — At the cookfire, Chett evaluates Dirk (calm), Sweet Donnel Hill (easy japes, likely solid), Sawwood (restless), and Maslyn (sweating, terrified, potential weak link).
12. **Assembly called** — The camp is summoned to the central fire. Mormont, flanked by Smallwood, Locke, Wythers, and Blane, announces the wildlings' approach and the plan to attack at dawn.
13. **Mormont's battle plan** — Ride north at dawn, loop west, ambush the wildling column in narrow valleys in the Frostfangs foothills, hitting in multiple places. Smallwood elaborates: hit and run, burn wagons, scatter herds, kill Mance Rayder if possible.
14. **Night's Watch oath** — After Maslyn cries "We'll die," Mormont invokes the oath. Ser Mallador Locke draws his longsword, others follow, and nearly three hundred voices recite the oath with swords raised. Chett and his conspirators join in to avoid suspicion.
15. **Chett settles in for the night** — Crawls under furs near the dogs; worries about what could go wrong with the mutiny.
16. **Chett recalls Bessa** — Remembers picking wildflowers for her, not just her death; a shift in his usual memory pattern. His heart pounds; ice cakes his beard.
17. **Snow begins to fall** — Heavy snowfall begins. Chett realizes the snow ruins everything: they can't find food caches, can't follow the game trail east, will leave tracks.
18. **Chett goes to kill Sam anyway** — Despite the ruined plan, Chett takes his dagger and goes to Sam's sleeping spot between a rock and the raven cages. He draws his dagger and approaches.
19. **First horn blast** — A single horn blast sounds. Chett pauses — one blast means brothers returning (possibly Buckwell or Halfhand).
20. **Second horn blast** — A second, louder and longer blast sounds. Two blasts means enemies approaching. Sam wakes, dons chainmail, and asks Chett about the blasts. Chett confirms two blasts means wildlings.
21. **Third horn blast** — A third blast sounds, the longest, seemingly unending. Sam says three blasts haven't sounded for hundreds and thousands of years. Three blasts means Others. Chett half-laughs, half-sobs, and pisses himself.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitter, angry, vindictive, increasingly anxious. Burns with resentment toward Jon Snow and Sam Tarly for displacing him. Fantasizes about violence (killing Sam, imagining Jon dead). Briefly tender when remembering picking flowers for Bessa, then confused by the feeling. At the end: despair when snow falls (tears freezing to cheeks), then primal terror at three horn blasts (pisses himself).
- **Primary preoccupation:** Executing the mutiny plan without being caught. Every interaction is filtered through whether it threatens the plan. Obsessively reviewing what could go wrong — someone breaking, someone forgetting, someone informing.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to abandon the bear hunt; decides to attempt killing Sam even after the snow ruins the escape plan; interrupted by the horn blasts.
- **Self-deception flags:** Chett frames himself as clever ("He was the clever one") and his plot as justified, but the plan has clear weaknesses he minimizes (Maslyn's terror, Small Paul's stupidity, the assumption Ser Ottyn won't pursue). His fantasy of becoming a lord or king at Craster's Keep reveals grandiose delusions. He blames everyone else for his situation (Jon Snow, Sam, Bessa, even the snow) while taking no responsibility for his own violence.
