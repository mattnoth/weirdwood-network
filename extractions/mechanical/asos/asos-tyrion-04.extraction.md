# ASOS — Tyrion IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 33
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion IV
- **first_available:** ASOS Tyrion IV
- **location_primary:** King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** King's Gate area, Mud Gate, Muddy Way, Visenya's Hill (wine sink), Tower of the Hand (Lord Tywin's solar), Red Keep
- **approximate_timeline:** After the Battle of the Blackwater and Tyrion's marriage to Sansa Stark; before Joffrey's wedding feast; Littlefinger departed a fortnight ago
- **time_markers:** "a fortnight past" (Littlefinger's departure); "saddled up this morning" (same-day ride); "the night before his wedding" (Tyrion's recalled meeting with Shae); "some eight years past" (Uncle Gerion's quest for Brightroar); "the day before the wedding" (Mott's delivery promise for the swords)
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion and Bronn survey the war-damaged riverfront outside King's Landing's walls, then Tyrion secretly visits the singer Symon Silver Tongue, who blackmails him with a song about Shae. Tyrion agrees to Symon's demand for a place at Joffrey's wedding tournament of singers, then privately orders Bronn to kill Symon. Summoned to the Tower of the Hand, Tyrion sees two newly forged Valyrian steel swords—one for Joffrey, one for Jaime—presented by the armorer Tobho Mott. Tywin presses Tyrion on rebuilding costs, the empty treasury, and consummating his marriage to Sansa, then reveals Mace Tyrell refused to wed Willas to Cersei. Grand Maester Pycelle arrives with news that Lord Commander Mormont may have been killed beyond the Wall, and Tywin maneuvers to install Janos Slynt as the new Lord Commander.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion and Bronn survey outside King's Gate** — They ride past the ruins where nothing remains but mud, ashes, and burned bone. People are already returning to live in the shadow of the walls and sell fish.
2. **Tyrion orders future enforcement** — He tells Bronn to leave the squatters alone but to pull down any hovels built against the walls.
3. **Tyrion reflects on Ser Kevan's grief** — The rebuilding task was meant for Kevan, but he is consumed by grief over Willem's murder, Martyn's captivity, and Lancel's festering wound, forcing Tywin to turn to Tyrion.
4. **Children throw dung from the Three Whores** — Children have climbed the idle trebuchets in the market square. A clod of manure nearly hits Tyrion, and his mare rears.
5. **Tyrion reflects on his unconsummated marriage** — Sansa remains a maiden and half the castle knows it. Stableboys snigger; either Sansa told a bedmaid (all Cersei's spies) or Varys's little birds are responsible.
6. **Tyrion recalls Shae's reaction to the wedding** — He visited Shae the night before via Varys's chambers. She already knew about the marriage from overhearing a page tell Ser Tallad. She was indifferent, saying Sansa was "only a little girl."
7. **Tyrion distributes coppers to children on Muddy Way** — He tosses a fistful of coppers into the air. Hollow-eyed children scramble; the lucky ones might buy stale bread. Market prices are shockingly high.
8. **Tyrion and Bronn navigate to the wine sink** — They turn from Aegon's High Hill into the maze of streets near Visenya's Hill. Tyrion checks for followers. They reach a crooked alley and a dismal cellar wine sink.
9. **Tyrion meets Symon Silver Tongue** — In the dark back room, Symon greets him, references Shae by name, and offers to have sung at the wedding.
10. **Tyrion offers Symon passage to the Free Cities** — He offers thirty golden dragons and proposes a tour of all nine Free Cities, a year in each.
11. **Symon sings the blackmail song** — Symon performs a song clearly about Tyrion's secret relationship with Shae ("He rode through the streets of the city... she was his secret treasure, she was his shame and his bliss... a chain and a keep are nothing, compared to a woman's kiss"). The refrain: "For hands of gold are always cold, but a woman's hands are warm."
12. **Symon blackmails Tyrion** — He threatens to sing the song for Cersei or Tywin if not given a spot in the tournament of singers at Joffrey's wedding, replacing one of the seven performers.
13. **Tyrion agrees to Symon's terms** — He promises Bronn will call on Symon, leaving the gold in his cloak.
14. **Tyrion orders Symon's assassination** — He tells Bronn to wait three days, then tell Symon that Hamish the Harper broke his arm and Symon must be fitted for court clothes. When Symon comes, Bronn should take his silver tongue and ensure the rest is never found.
15. **Bronn suggests the bowl of brown** — He knows a pot shop in Flea Bottom; Tyrion tells him to make certain Tyrion never eats there.
16. **Podrick summons Tyrion to the Tower of the Hand** — Tyrion returns to his chambers and Pod tells him Lord Tywin wants to see him.
17. **Tobho Mott presents two Valyrian steel swords** — In Tywin's solar, the armorer presents two blades with unusual black-and-red rippling. Tywin decides on rubies for the lion's-eye studs on the scabbards, not garnets. One sword is for Joffrey (wedding gift), one for Jaime.
18. **The armorer explains the coloring mystery** — He tried to infuse Lannister crimson but the Valyrian steel resisted, darkening repeatedly. Some folds would not take the red at all. He cannot duplicate the effect.
19. **Tyrion reflects on House Lannister's lack of Valyrian steel** — Brightroar was lost when King Tommen took it to Valyria. Uncle Gerion went seeking it eight years ago and never returned. Tywin tried three times to buy Valyrian swords from lesser houses but was always refused.
20. **Tywin and Tyrion discuss rebuilding costs** — The riverfront is devastated. Tyrion lists needed repairs: dredging the Blackwater, breaking up sunken ships, repairing quays, rebuilding the fish market, replacing two city gates. The treasury is empty; they still owe alchemists for wildfire and smiths for Tyrion's chain.
21. **Tyrion raises the crown's debt crisis** — Littlefinger's borrowing has left the crown's income barely covering usury payments. Tyrion asks if Tywin will forgive the throne's debt to House Lannister; Tywin refuses.
22. **Tyrion suggests a smaller wedding; Tywin refuses** — Tyrion proposes seven courses and three hundred guests instead of seventy-seven courses and a thousand. Tywin insists the Tyrells would think them niggardly.
23. **Tywin pressures Tyrion about Sansa** — Tywin tells Tyrion to consummate the marriage, warning that an unconsummated marriage can be set aside. Getting Sansa with child would secure the North. Tyrion pushes back that Sansa is too young.
24. **Tywin reveals the Tyrell refusal** — Mace Tyrell refused to wed his heir Willas to Cersei. Olenna Tyrell is blamed for the reversal, reportedly calling Cersei "too old and too used." Tywin orders Tyrion never to speak of this—the offer was never made.
25. **Pycelle brings Castle Black news** — Bowen Marsh writes that Mormont sent word from the haunted forest that he was under attack. More ravens returned without letters. Marsh fears Mormont slain with all his strength and the Wall itself may be attacked. He begs for men from all five kings.
26. **Tywin maneuvers to install Janos Slynt** — Pycelle suggests Slynt as Lord Commander. Tyrion objects, calling him a butcher's son and hollow suit of armor. He suggests the Shadow Tower commander (a Mallister) or the Eastwatch commander (an ironman), but Tywin rejects both. Tywin dictates a letter expressing regret at not sending men but hinting at future support if Slynt is chosen.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Black mood throughout; bitter about his marriage, resentful of being laughed at, disgusted by the necessity of killing Symon, wounded by Tywin's favoritism (swords for Joffrey and Jaime but nothing for him), frustrated by impossible financial demands, angry at Tywin's cruelty about Sansa
- **Primary preoccupation:** The humiliation of his unconsummated marriage; desire for Sansa mixed with guilt; the financial impossibility of rebuilding the city and funding the wedding; the Symon Silver Tongue threat
- **Key decisions made:** Orders Bronn to kill Symon Silver Tongue rather than pay or exile him; agrees to find money for both the wedding and rebuilding despite knowing the treasury is empty
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion tells himself Shae is "all the love you're ever like to have" while simultaneously wanting Sansa to come to him willingly; he rationalizes Symon's murder as a lesson well-learned from his father ("See how fast I learn my lessons?") while the act clearly disturbs him (he wants a hot bath after ordering it)
