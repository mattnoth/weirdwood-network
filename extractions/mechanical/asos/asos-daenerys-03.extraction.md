- **time_markers:** Negotiations take place during the day (cool green light filtering through glass); a "long, dark, windy night" follows; Dany dreams and wakes "in the darkness"; "the next morning" she enters Astapor; sun described as "flashing blinding bright" during the exchange in the Plaza of Punishment
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys negotiates with the eight Good Masters of Astapor to purchase all Unsullied, including those still in training, offering her trade goods, three ships, and ultimately Drogon as payment. She acquires and frees the slave translator Missandei. That night aboard Balerion she receives a cryptic visitation from Quaithe. The next morning at the Plaza of Punishment, Daenerys completes the exchange, then reveals she speaks Valyrian, strikes Kraznys with the whip, commands Drogon to burn him, and orders the Unsullied to slay their masters and free the slaves of Astapor.

## Events & Actions
1. **Daenerys announces she wants to buy all the Unsullied** — She tells the slave girl translator to inform the Good Masters she wants all eight thousand, plus the six centuries, plus the boys still in training.
2. **Good Masters debate among themselves** — They argue about selling half-trained boys; the fat one favors immediate gold; a silver-fringed Grazdan worries about shaming their reputation.
3. **Kraznys announces partial acceptance** — They will sell the eight thousands and six centuries but refuse the untrained boys; tell her to return in a year for more.
4. **Dany offers double price for all including untrained** — Still refused initially, then accepted when she sweetens the deal.
5. **Dany offers her ships** — The great cog Balerion and galleys Vhagar and Meraxes. Slavers offer only two thousand for ships plus trade goods.
6. **Dany offers Drogon** — She proposes a dragon in exchange for all Unsullied and untrained boys. Slavers accept; choose Drogon specifically as the largest and healthiest.
7. **Whitebeard objects and is removed** — He kneels and begs her not to trade a dragon. Dany rebukes him; Ser Jorah removes him to the terrace.
8. **Deal concluded** — "Done" said eight times. Missandei given to Dany as a gift/token.
9. **Dany confronts Whitebeard on the terrace** — She tells him to never question her before strangers, but to always speak freely in private.
10. **Dany frees Missandei** — In the litter, she declares Missandei no longer a slave and offers her a position as handmaid. Missandei chooses to stay.
11. **Dany questions Missandei about the Unsullied** — Learns they are truly fearless, feel no pain (wine of courage), completely obedient. Missandei reveals three of them were her brothers.
12. **Night aboard Balerion** — Dany feeds dragons, cannot eat, cries alone, argues with Groleo about the ships, then calls bloodriders and Jorah for counsel.
13. **Dany and Jorah talk on deck** — She recalls Eroeh and reflects on the duty of kings and queens to protect the helpless. Jorah mentions Robert made himself king; Dany says Robert was no true king.
14. **Dany's dragon dream** — She dreams she is Rhaegar riding to the Trident on a dragon, bathing the rebel host (armored in ice) in dragonfire.
15. **Quaithe's visitation** — Dany wakes to find a woman in her cabin. Quaithe delivers her cryptic message: "To go north, you must journey south. To reach the west, you must go east. To go forward you must go back, and to touch the light you must pass beneath the shadow."
16. **Morning procession into Astapor** — Dany rides her silver through the harbor gates with her full retinue, dragons chained to an open litter, eighty-three Dothraki, and their herd of gaunt horses.
17. **Arrival at the Plaza of Punishment** — Dany sees flayed rebellious slaves on the wooden platform; one raised a hand against his owner. She observes 8,600 fully trained Unsullied and 5,000+ behind them.
18. **The exchange begins** — Trade goods piled before the slavers. Kraznys gives handling advice through Missandei, suggesting Dany sack cities and sell captives back.
19. **Drogon's chain handed over** — Jhiqui unfastens chain; Dany hands the end to Kraznys. He gives her the harpy's fingers whip in return.
20. **Dany reveals she speaks Valyrian** — She addresses the Unsullied directly, claiming them as hers, galloping along ranks shouting "YOU ARE THE DRAGON'S NOW!"
21. **Dany strikes Kraznys with the whip** — She slashes his face with the harpy's fingers, declaring "A dragon is no slave."
22. **Drogon burns Kraznys** — At Dany's command of "Dracarys," Drogon engulfs Kraznys in black flame; his eyes melt, his hair ignites.
23. **All three dragons unleashed** — Irri and Jhiqui unchain Viserion and Rhaegal; three dragons take to the air.
24. **Battle in the plaza** — Astapori mounted lancers panic and flee or are killed; Dany's bloodriders fight; Strong Belwas charges; Aggo shoots indiscriminately at tokars.
25. **Old Grazdan killed** — He calls for the Unsullied to defend their masters; Rakharo puts an arrow through his mouth; his sedan chair bearers drop him; he crawls to the Unsullied and dies. They do not move to help.
26. **Dany commands the Unsullied** — She orders them to slay Good Masters, soldiers, and anyone with a tokar or whip; harm no child under twelve; strike chains off every slave. She flings the whip aside and cries "Freedom! Dracarys!"
27. **The Unsullied obey Dany** — They shout "Dracarys!" back and join the slaughter. The sack of Astapor begins.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined and resolved during negotiation (masking desperation); anguished at offering Drogon ("The Mother of Dragons has sold her strongest child. Even the thought made her ill"); crying alone at night; triumphant and exultant during the sack ("all her fear forgotten"); briefly desperate and afraid before giving the final command ("Was this what my brother would have done?")
- **Primary preoccupation:** Executing her plan to acquire the Unsullied without truly losing a dragon — she has weighed this and found no other way. Secondary: her duty as a ruler to protect the helpless (Eroeh, slaves).
- **Key decisions made:** Offers Drogon to complete the deal (knowing she will reclaim him); rebukes Whitebeard publicly to maintain the ruse; frees Missandei; commands the sack of Astapor with specific rules of engagement (no children under 12, free all slaves).
- **Self-deception flags:** None apparent — Dany is executing a deliberate plan. Her anguish over offering Drogon appears genuine even though she plans to reclaim him, suggesting she recognizes the risk that it might not work.
