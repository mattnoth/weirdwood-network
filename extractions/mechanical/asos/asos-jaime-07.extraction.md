# ASOS — Jaime VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ASOS
- **chapter_number:** 63
- **pov_character:** Jaime Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jaime VII
- **first_available:** ASOS Jaime VII
- **location_primary:** King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Roadside inn (unnamed, en route to King's Landing), Red Keep, Royal sept, Tower of the Hand, Gate of the Gods, Street of Seeds, Cobbler's Square
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Joffrey's death at the Purple Wedding; Jaime arrives at King's Landing from the riverlands
- **time_markers:** Night at the roadside inn; "rode hard the next day"; gathering dusk when they see the city; Cersei praying in the sept; the meeting with Tywin occurs the same evening as arrival
- **chapter_summary:** Jaime learns of Joffrey's death at a roadside inn and rides hard to King's Landing with Steelshanks Walton's northmen, Brienne, and Qyburn. Upon arrival, he confronts his own lack of grief for the son he never publicly claimed. At the Red Keep, Ser Loras Tyrell attempts to attack Brienne for Renly's murder; Jaime intervenes and has Brienne placed in a tower cell for her own protection. Jaime reunites with Cersei in the sept where Joffrey lies in state, and they have sex on the Mother's altar. Cersei asks him to kill Tyrion; Jaime proposes marriage and is rejected. He then meets Lord Tywin, who offers him Casterly Rock, a marriage to Margaery Tyrell, and release from the Kingsguard. Jaime refuses everything, and Tywin disowns him with the words "You are not my son."

## Events & Actions
1. **Jaime hears of Joffrey's death** — At a roadside inn, various travelers share rumors: a costermonger says Tyrion cut Joffrey's throat, the innkeep says poison turned his face black, an archer says Sansa vanished in brimstone and a ghostly direwolf prowled the Red Keep.
2. **Jaime reflects on his lack of grief** — He tries to picture Joffrey but keeps seeing Cersei. He feels curiously calm and wonders if he is the monster they claim, since he would choose his hand over his son if offered the choice.
3. **Hard ride to King's Landing** — They ride hard the next day at Jaime's insistence.
4. **Approach to King's Landing** — Jaime canters up to Steelshanks Walton as the city appears at dusk. Steelshanks complains about the smell.
5. **Jaime speaks with Brienne** — He tells her the journey is done and she has kept her vow. She says it was only half — she still must return Catelyn's daughters. She is described as broken and half-dead since hearing about the Red Wedding.
6. **Jaime offers Brienne options** — He offers to speak to his father about returning her to Tarth, or finding her a place at court. She refuses serving with "oathbreakers and murderers."
7. **Arrival at Gate of the Gods** — Wagons line up to pay tolls. Gold cloaks collect coin by command of the Hand and master of coin. A miller says Lannisters hold the city. The captain of the gate says the Imp was master of coin until arrested for killing the king.
8. **Passage through the city streets** — King's Landing shows little mourning for Joffrey. A begging brother prays on the Street of Seeds. Streets full of usual crowds, gold cloaks, vendors, whores. At Cobbler's Square Jaime notes no one recognizes him. Steelshanks says "they have a new Kingslayer now."
9. **Arrival at the Red Keep** — Gold cloaks with pikes bar the way. Ser Meryn Trant recognizes Jaime and leaps to obey.
10. **Meeting new Kingsguard** — In the outer ward, Jaime meets Loras Tyrell and Balon Swann, new Kingsguard members appointed without his consultation. Loras shines in white scales and silk.
11. **Loras confronts Brienne** — Loras sees Brienne and accuses her of Renly's murder, citing Emmon Cuy's dying testimony. He draws his longsword and demands she draw hers.
12. **Jaime intervenes** — He steps between them twice, asserts his authority as Lord Commander, and threatens Loras until the boy sheathes his sword. Ser Balon backs Jaime. Gold cloaks and Dreadfort men nearly draw steel.
13. **Brienne arrested** — Jaime orders Ser Balon to escort Brienne to a tower cell, defending her honor to Loras while doing so. Her blue eyes are full of hurt. Jaime thinks she should be grateful.
14. **Encounter with Osmund Kettleblack** — The new Kingsguard knight doesn't recognize Jaime, mistakes him for Tyrion ("Escaped, have you? And grown a bit"), then realizes his error.
15. **Reunion with Cersei in the sept** — Cersei is kneeling before the Mother's altar; Joffrey lies beneath the Stranger in gilded armor. She weeps, embraces Jaime, says "You should have come sooner."
16. **Jaime reveals his maimed hand** — He lifts his stump; Cersei's eyes go wide; she blames "the Starks" but Jaime corrects her — it was Vargo Hoat.
17. **Cersei accuses Tyrion** — She tells Jaime that Tyrion warned her he would turn her joy to ashes, that Joffrey pointed at Tyrion as he died, and that Tyrion did it "for a whore." She asks Jaime to kill Tyrion.
18. **Jaime refuses to kill Tyrion** — He says Tyrion is still his brother and he is in no state to kill anyone. Cersei insists he could do it with his left hand since Tyrion is a dwarf locked in a cell.
19. **Sexual encounter on the Mother's altar** — Jaime kisses Cersei, pushes her onto the altar, and they have sex despite her initial protests about risk, the septons, and the gods. Her moon's blood is on her. Afterward, Jaime cleans blood from the pale marble altar.
20. **Jaime proposes marriage** — He says the Targaryens wed brother to sister and they should do the same. He offers to make another son in place of Joffrey.
21. **Cersei rejects the proposal** — She says Tommen's throne derives from Robert and the proposal would cost them everything. She says Jaime has changed, recoils from his stump, and sends him away.
22. **Jaime goes to the Tower of the Hand** — Lannister guards recognize him. He climbs to Tywin's solar.
23. **Meeting with Tywin** — Tywin greets him as if they'd last met at breakfast. He had expected Jaime earlier based on Bolton's communication. He wanted Jaime for the wedding.
24. **Tywin discusses Joffrey's death** — Poison; meant to look like choking; Tywin had the throat slit open and maesters found no obstruction. Tyrion served the poisoned wine before a thousand witnesses.
25. **Tywin discusses the investigation** — He has taken Tyrion's squire into custody, Sansa's maids, and Ser Addam's gold cloaks are searching for Sansa. Varys offered a reward.
26. **Jaime mentions Renly's death and Brienne** — He draws parallels between Renly's strange death and Joffrey's. Tywin dismisses Brienne as Renly's murderer.
27. **Tywin reports on Harrenhal** — Gregor Clegane took the castle; sellswords deserted Hoat; Lady Whent's people opened a postern gate; Hoat was found half-mad with festered ear wound; his hands and feet have been removed.
28. **Tywin reveals his plan** — He wants Jaime to leave the Kingsguard, be heir to Casterly Rock, take Tommen as ward, and possibly marry Margaery Tyrell. He plans a new husband for Cersei, possibly Oberyn Martell.
29. **Jaime refuses everything** — He insists he is Lord Commander of the Kingsguard and will remain so. He cites precedent in the White Book and Kingsguard service for life.
30. **Tywin disowns Jaime** — "You are not my son." He turns his face away and tells Jaime to go do his duty.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Curiously calm about Joffrey's death; filled with urgency to reach Cersei; frustrated by lack of recognition; aroused and desperate upon reuniting with Cersei; angry and defiant when confronted by both Cersei and Tywin; sick of lies, secrets, and being controlled; weary
- **Primary preoccupation:** Returning to Cersei; his feelings (or lack thereof) about Joffrey's death; the question of whether Tyrion actually killed Joffrey; his identity as Lord Commander versus heir to Casterly Rock
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to kill Tyrion for Cersei; proposes marriage to Cersei (rejected); refuses to leave the Kingsguard; refuses Casterly Rock and Margaery; orders Brienne arrested to protect her from Loras rather than to condemn her; asserts authority as Lord Commander
- **Self-deception flags:** Wonders if he is "the monster they claimed" because he feels nothing for Joffrey, then immediately rationalizes it — "He had a second son, after all, and seed enough for many more." He tells himself "I loved Tyrion. I was good to him. Well, but for that one time..." — acknowledging but minimizing something he did to Tyrion. Thinks Cersei "has never come to me... She gives, but I must ask" — a pattern he notices but does not critically examine. Expects Cersei to weep and lean on him; she does initially but then pushes him away. He imagines himself done with lies and secrecy, but Cersei immediately reestablishes the secrecy.
