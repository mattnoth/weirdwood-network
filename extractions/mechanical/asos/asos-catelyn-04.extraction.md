- **chapter_summary:** Lord Hoster Tully's funeral takes place at Riverrun with a traditional Tully river burial. Edmure fails three times to light the funeral boat with fire arrows; Brynden Blackfish succeeds. Robb reveals to Catelyn that Sansa has been married to Tyrion Lannister, and they argue when Catelyn urges him to consider bending the knee. At supper, Lothar Frey reports that Winterfell has been burned by Theon Greyjoy and that Bolton's bastard son brought survivors to the Dreadfort. Lothar then presents Lord Walder's terms for renewing alliance: Robb must apologize in person and Edmure must marry Roslin Frey immediately. After resistance, Edmure accepts.

## Events & Actions
1. **Lord Hoster's funeral preparation** — Lord Hoster Tully is laid in a wooden boat in silver armor with his cloak, shield, hunting horn, and a painted wooden sword. Seven men are chosen to push the boat into the river.
2. **Boat launched** — The seven (Robb, Lords Bracken, Blackwood, Vance, Mallister, Ser Marq Piper, and Lothar Frey) wade into the Tumblestone and push the funeral boat into the current from the water stair.
3. **Edmure's first arrow misses** — Edmure lights a fire arrow and shoots but it falls into the water well astern of the boat.
4. **Edmure's second arrow misses** — The second fire arrow overshoots, landing a dozen yards beyond the boat.
5. **Edmure's third arrow misses** — The third arrow comes within a handspan of the sail but still misses.
6. **Brynden lights the boat** — Ser Brynden takes the bow and his flaming arrow finds its mark; the funeral boat catches fire.
7. **Catelyn and Brynden watch together** — They hold hands watching the fire recede; Edmure stands alone apart from them.
8. **Bannermen offer condolences to Robb** — Lord Jason Mallister, the Greatjon, and Ser Rolph Spicer speak to Robb in turn.
9. **Lothar requests audience** — Lothar Frey asks Robb to grant him audience that evening to discuss business.
10. **Robb reveals Sansa's marriage** — Walking in the godswood, Robb tells Catelyn that Sansa has been married to Tyrion Lannister, news received by raven the previous night.
11. **Catelyn urges Robb to consider peace** — Catelyn argues Robb should consider bending the knee to end the war; Robb refuses angrily.
12. **Catelyn and Robb quarrel** — Robb implies Catelyn has forgotten the Lannisters killed Ned; Catelyn asks leave and departs. Robb draws his sword with no one to fight.
13. **Supper and Lothar's report of Winterfell** — Lothar reveals that Winterfell has been burned by Theon Greyjoy, Ser Rodrik Cassel was killed, and Bolton's bastard son brought survivors to the Dreadfort.
14. **Robb reacts to Winterfell news** — Robb slams fist on table and turns away to hide tears.
15. **Lothar presents alliance terms** — Lord Walder will renew fealty if Robb apologizes in person and Edmure marries Roslin Frey immediately.
16. **Edmure resists the marriage** — Edmure protests he should have a choice of brides and refuses.
17. **Edmure accepts** — After pressure from Catelyn, Robb, and Brynden, Edmure agrees to marry Roslin Frey.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deep grief for her father; dread and anxiety about the war's direction; protectiveness toward Robb; numbness and horror at Sansa's marriage; desperation urging peace
- **Primary preoccupation:** Loss upon loss — father dead, Bran and Rickon believed dead, Sansa married to the enemy, Arya lost, no word from Brienne, Lysa silent; fear of losing Robb
- **Key decisions made:** Catelyn advocates strongly for Edmure to accept the Frey marriage, recognizing it as politically necessary despite distaste; she urges Robb to consider bending the knee (rejected)
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn lied to Edmure about Lord Hoster's last words, telling him Hoster whispered his name when actually the word was "Tansy." She rationalizes the silence about Brienne's mission (the raven could have been shot down). She also entertains hope that freeing Jaime might buy peace, presenting it to Robb as primarily about the girls' safety.
