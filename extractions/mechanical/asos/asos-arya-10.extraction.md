- **chapter_summary:** Arya and the Hound approach the Twins disguised as farmers bringing salt pork for Edmure Tully's wedding feast. They bluff past outriders led by Ser Donnel Haigh and Bolton guards at the camp perimeter. Driving through the encampment, Arya searches desperately for familiar Stark banners or faces but finds only strangers. She overhears toasts to Lord Edmure and Lady Roslin, and to "the Young Wolf and Queen Jeyne," wondering who Queen Jeyne is. The Hound drives toward the castle to deliver Arya to her brother.

## Events & Actions
1. **Outriders encounter** — Three riders (Ser Donnel Haigh and two squires) approach Arya and the Hound on the muddy road an hour from the Green Fork; Clegane halts the wayn and keeps his head down.
2. **Cover story deployed** — The Hound tells Haigh they are bringing salt pork for the wedding feast and that Stranger is a wedding gift from Lady Whent for young Lord Tully.
3. **Haigh inspects Stranger** — The knight is suspicious of the war horse's quality but accepts the story; one squire's horse is nearly bitten by Stranger.
4. **Haigh waves them through** — Mocks Lady Whent's supposed attempt to buy back Harrenhal with a horse, then lets them pass.
5. **Hound identifies Haigh** — After the outriders ride off, Clegane reveals the knight is Ser Donnel Haigh and that he has taken horses and armor from him in past tourneys and once nearly killed him in a mêlée.
6. **Flashback: robbery on the kingsroad** — The Hound took the wayn, horses, garb, casks, and boots from a farmer at swordpoint; the farmer cursed him as a robber; the Hound called himself "a forager" and threatened to take the farmer's legs if he didn't give up his boots.
7. **Evenfall approach** — Arya and the Hound continue toward the Twins at evenfall; Arya's stomach is knotted with dread; she has been fighting a fever and had a bad dream the previous night she cannot remember.
8. **Music heard** — Drums, horns, and pipes audible from the castles; the Hound observes they have missed the wedding but the feast continues.
9. **Camp perimeter reached** — Bolton sergeant and guards at the wagon-hedge perimeter challenge them; the sergeant is harder to bluff than Haigh.
10. **Redirected to feast tents** — The sergeant refuses them entry to the castle ("Castle's closed. The lordlings are not to be disturbed") and sends them to unload at the feast tents, telling them to ask for Sedgekins.
11. **Driving through the camp** — They pass rows of silk pavilions (pink, gold, green, striped, emblazoned with sigils), felt tents, canvas tents, and barracks tents; Arya spots Lord Smallwood's yellow tent with six acorns.
12. **Arya searches for familiar faces** — She looks for direwolf badges, grey-and-white tents, Winterfell faces; sees only strangers — a man who isn't Alebelly, a girl emerging from a tent (pale blue, not grey), a man in a treecat doublet, four archers stringing longbows under a tree, a maester too young and thin to be Luwin.
13. **Toasts overheard** — From the feast tents, Arya hears toasts to "Lord Edmure and Lady Roslin" and "the Young Wolf and Queen Jeyne"; she wonders who Queen Jeyne is.
14. **Arya sees northmen** — She recognizes Karstarks, Umbers, and mountain clansmen in the feast tents by their beards, faces, and cloaks of bearskin and sealskin; she asks to stop but the Hound refuses.
15. **Hound drives toward the castle** — He wants to deliver Arya directly to Robb, not stop at the feast tents; tells Sedgekins to "bugger himself with a hot poker."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Knotted dread in her belly, mixed with desperate hope; fighting a fever; cold, wet, and envious of those warm inside the feast tents; longing to see familiar faces; anxious about Roose Bolton potentially recognizing her; a lingering feeling from a bad dream she cannot remember
- **Primary preoccupation:** Reaching Robb and her mother inside the castle; scanning the camp for Stark banners, direwolf badges, or anyone she recognizes from Winterfell
- **Key decisions made:** Chose NOT to reveal herself to Ser Donnel Haigh's outriders because she does not recognize his pitchfork sigil — she would have risked it for Umber or Glover men; defers to the Hound's plan to reach the castle directly
- **Self-deception flags:** Reassures herself "it was Robb's army, so there was no real danger there" then immediately undercuts it with "Was there?"; tells herself Bolton "probably won't even know me" but remains uneasy; recites "Fear cuts deeper than swords" as self-encouragement
