- **chapter_summary:** Jaime, Brienne, and Ser Cleos Frey stop at the Inn of the Kneeling Man on the Red Fork, which is occupied by a squatter and a boy rather than its original innkeeper. They learn the river route is blocked by sunken boats, outlaws, and Lord Beric Dondarrion's band, so they purchase three horses and provisions to travel overland. Brienne wisely avoids the suggested woodland route (suspecting a trap) and takes the southern road instead. During a nighttime camp, Jaime and Brienne argue about his killing of Aerys, and Jaime internally remembers the full circumstances of joining the Kingsguard for love of Cersei and the actual killing of Aerys. He dreams of burning dead men in green flames.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at the inn** — Jaime spots the Inn of the Kneeling Man from the river; notes no smoke or lights. Brienne steers the skiff to the dock. Ser Cleos ties up.
2. **Jaime recognizes the inn** — Sees the sign of a kneeling king and identifies it as the Inn of the Kneeling Man, where Torrhen Stark submitted to Aegon the Conqueror.
3. **Confrontation with the boy** — Jaime enters the inn and faces a loaded crossbow wielded by a chunky boy of fifteen who demands "Lion, fish, or wolf?"
4. **Brienne pays for entry** — She offers silver coin; they must remove swordbelts to be served.
5. **The cook explains the situation** — A sallow man with a cleaver says he buried the innkeeper and his women out back; wolves or lions killed them; he and his wife found them dead and claimed the place.
6. **Meal at the inn** — They eat horse steaks, fried onions, and oatcakes. The cook sits with them and asks for news.
7. **Intelligence about river dangers** — The cook warns of sunken boats blocking the channel thirty miles downriver, outlaws at the Skipping Stones and Red Deer Island, Lord Beric Dondarrion ("the lightning lord") crossing the river at will with Thoros of Myr, and the ruby ford held by "the Leech Lord's wolves."
8. **Cook advises overland travel** — Recommends leaving the river and cutting overland through woods, avoiding main roads.
9. **Horse negotiations** — Three horses in the stable: a brown plow horse, an ancient white gelding (blind in one eye), and a dapple grey palfrey with a bloodstained silver-inlaid saddle. Brienne pays three gold dragons and gives the skiff.
10. **Ankle chain split** — Brienne uses the smith's hammer and steel chisel to split Jaime's ankle chain; refuses to break the wrist chain.
11. **Directions from the cook** — He tells them about a burned village six miles downriver where the road splits, and warns against Ser Warren's towerhouse but recommends the track through woods (south by east).
12. **Horse assignments** — Brienne takes the plow horse, Ser Cleos the palfrey, Jaime the one-eyed gelding.
13. **Departure advice to the boy** — Jaime tells the boy to take up spear or maul instead of crossbow; boy stares distrustfully.
14. **Night ride along the Red Fork** — They ride east along the moonlit river. Jaime recalls his last time on horseback (Whispering Wood).
15. **Brienne takes the southern road** — At the burned village, she chooses the straight southern road instead of the woodland track the cook recommended, suspecting a trap.
16. **Avoiding the towerhouse** — Ruddy light visible from the stone towerhouse; Brienne leads them off into the fields to bypass it.
17. **Camp in the grove** — After half the night, they stop in a small grove of oak and ash beside a stream. No fire allowed.
18. **Jaime and Brienne argue** — They debate kingslaying, honor, oaths, and the Kingsguard. Brienne denies killing Renly (claims a shadow did it). Jaime mocks her. Brienne calls knighthood "a rare and precious gift."
19. **Jaime's memory of joining the Kingsguard** — He recalls Cersei's plan to have him take the white to be near her; the night on Eel Alley; Tywin's fury and resignation as Hand.
20. **Jaime's memory of killing Aerys** — He recalls entering through the king's door in golden armor, having already killed Rossart. Aerys demanded Tywin's head, ran for the throne, and Jaime slashed his throat. Ser Elys Westerling and Lord Crakehall arrived. Jaime sat on the throne with his sword until Eddard Stark came.
21. **Green flame dream** — Jaime dreams of dead men burning in swirling green flames, fighting them with a golden sword; for every one struck down, two more arise.
22. **Morning departure** — Brienne wakes Jaime with a boot; rain falling; they eat and ride before sunrise.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Irritated by chains; contemptuous of the cook and the boy; sardonic humor masking vulnerability; glad to be alive and on his way back to Cersei; defensive about his past; bitter about being judged; nostalgic and pained about Cersei memories; darkly amused at the parallels between his and Brienne's "kingslaying"
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting free of chains and returning to Cersei; managing the dynamic with Brienne; haunted by memories of killing Aerys and the choices that led him there
- **Key decisions made:** Advocated stopping at the inn; supported Brienne's decision to ride at night rather than stay; did not attempt escape (gelding too slow); told the cook he'd killed crossbowmen (lie/deflection)
- **Self-deception flags:** "Before this is done, I'll wrap these chains around the wench's throat" — violent fantasy he cannot act on; internal rationalization that joining the Kingsguard was purely for love of Cersei, suppressing any ambition component; "I ought to have drowned Rossart instead of gutting him" — retrospective regret suggesting he's still processing the events; considers himself justified in killing Aerys but resents that no one asks the full story
