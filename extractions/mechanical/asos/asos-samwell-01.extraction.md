An Other appears on a dead horse and kills Small Paul, but Sam stabs it with Jon Snow's dragonglass dagger, destroying it. At dawn, Sam and Grenn set off east to rejoin Mormont's column.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sam struggles through deep snow** — Exhausted, freezing, and in pain, Sam forces himself to take step after step during the Night's Watch retreat from the Fist of the First Men. His swordbelt keeps slipping down; he has lost his sword but carries two knives including the dragonglass dagger.
2. **Black Bernarr breaks his ankle** — Three or four days prior, Black Bernarr stepped in a hole and broke his ankle. The Lord Commander gave him a horse.
3. **Sam recalls Maslyn's death** — He remembers Maslyn screaming for mercy, dropping his sword and pleading, before a wight lifted him by the throat and nearly ripped his head off.
4. **Sam collapses and falls** — A root catches his toe; he falls to one knee and bites his tongue, tasting warm blood. Unable to rise, he lies down in the snow and decides to die. He reasons the snow will cover him like a blanket and he did his duty.
5. **Extended flashback: Sam sends the ravens** — When the horns blew (three long blasts = Others), Sam found his prepared messages. He struggled to open the raven cages, was pecked, but managed to send one raven to Castle Black and one to the Shadow Tower with messages about the attack.
6. **Flashback: The battle at the Fist** — Wights assault the ringwall from multiple directions. The Watch fights with fire arrows and torches. A dead bear attacks. Sam writes multiple messages chronicling the battle's progress, from optimistic ("we've thrown them back") to desperate ("the battle's lost. We're all lost").
7. **Flashback: Sam forgets to send his updated messages** — When the horns blow to mount up, Sam releases all remaining ravens in a panic but forgets to attach the messages he'd written during the battle.
8. **Flashback: The undead bear kills Thoren Smallwood** — The rotting, partially burned bear attacks near Mormont. Thoren Smallwood charges and nearly takes its head off, but the bear takes his head in return.
9. **Flashback: The mounted breakout** — Mormont orders a spearhead formation. The Watch rides down the south face, jumping the ringwall, trampling wights, and plunging into the forest. Sam clings to a horse through the breakout.
10. **Flashback: Sam loses his horse** — After crossing a frozen stream, a man in black yanks Sam from his horse and gallops away on it. Sam lies weeping until Dolorous Edd finds him.
11. **Flashback: Mormont reorganizes survivors** — Hours after the Fist, the survivors regroup. Dywen brought five packhorses (three survived). Mormont redistributes loads, gives horses to the wounded, organizes walkers, and sets torches as flanking guards.
12. **Grenn tries to rouse Sam** — Grenn finds Sam lying in the snow and shakes him, insists he get up, and kicks him when he won't move.
13. **Small Paul carries Sam** — Paul picks Sam up and carries him, comparing him to a calf he once carried. Sam protests weakly. Grenn tells Sam to think of his family or sing a song; Grenn starts "The Bear and the Maiden Fair" but Sam stops him (the undead bear is too fresh in his memory).
14. **Small Paul asks for a raven** — Paul says Chett promised him the Old Bear's talking raven. He asks Sam for one of the ravens. Sam tells him they're all gone.
15. **They fall behind and are separated from the column** — Paul's strength gives out; he sets Sam down. Grenn realizes they can't see any other torches. They are alone in the dark forest with only Grenn's single torch.
16. **An Other appears** — A dead horse emerges from behind a sentinel tree, covered in hoarfrost with entrails dragging from its open belly. A rider pale as ice sits on its back — an Other. It dismounts gracefully without breaking the snow's crust. Its armor ripples and shifts.
17. **Small Paul confronts the Other** — Paul unslings his axe, asking why the Other hurt "Mawney's horse." The Other's ice-blue sword slashes Grenn's torch, extinguishing it with a screech.
18. **The Other kills Small Paul** — Moving with lightning speed, the Other slides away from Paul's axe and drives its crystal sword through Paul's mail, leather, wool, bone, and flesh. Paul says "Oh" and tries to grab the Other before falling dead. His weight pulls the sword from the Other's grip.
19. **Sam kills the Other with dragonglass** — Hearing the voices of his father, Alliser Thorne, Dickon, Rast, and Jon urging him on, Sam stumbles forward and blindly shoves the dragonglass dagger into the Other's throat. There is a crack like breaking ice and a piercing screech.
20. **The Other dissolves** — The Other's armor runs down its legs in rivulets. Pale blue blood hisses and steams around the dragonglass. The Other's flesh dissolves into fine white mist. Beneath are bones like milkglass, pale and shiny, which also melt. Only the dragonglass dagger remains, wreathed in steam and intensely cold.
21. **Grenn takes the dagger; they set off at dawn** — Grenn picks up the dagger (drops it once from the cold), closes Small Paul's eyes, and points out dawn light to the east. Sam agrees to try walking, and they head east to find Mormont's column.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Overwhelmed by exhaustion, cold, pain, and terror. Deep self-loathing ("fat and weak and useless, even my wits are freezing now"). Alternates between despair and resignation. Prays for mercy to the Mother, then to whatever might be listening ("old gods or new, or demons too"). Brief suicidal acceptance ("maybe die a little"). Guilt about being a coward. At the kill, hysteria ("He giggled hysterically"). At the chapter's end, grim determination to try.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival — each step is a monumental effort. Haunted by the battle at the Fist. Consumed by physical misery (cold, pain, weight of mail, exhaustion). Fixated on his own cowardice and unworthiness. Internally replaying the battle and his failures.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to lie down and die (reversed by Grenn and Small Paul). Stabs the Other with dragonglass — described as almost involuntary, urged by remembered voices of his father, Thorne, Dickon, Rast, and Jon. Agrees to walk east toward dawn at chapter's end ("I'll try. I'll try hard.").
- **Self-deception flags:** Sam tells himself "The snow will cover me like a thick white blanket. It will be warm under the snow" — rationalizing death. He tells himself he "did his duty" to justify giving up, but the reader learns he forgot to send the updated messages with the ravens. He claims "I was remembering" when Grenn says he was falling asleep — uncertain whether Sam was actually sleeping.
