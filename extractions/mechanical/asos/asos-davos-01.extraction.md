- **chapter_summary:** Davos Seaworth lies dying of fever, thirst, and starvation on a barren rock in Blackwater Bay after the Battle of the Blackwater. He recalls the battle — wildfire consuming the fleet, the great chain across the river, and the loss of his ships and his sons Dale, Allard, Maric, and Matthos. He discovers his lucky fingerbones pouch is gone and prays to the Mother, hearing (or imagining) an accusing voice. He blames Melisandre for the disaster but acknowledges his own guilt. When a Lysene galley from Salladhor Saan's fleet sails near, Davos chooses to live, climbs the rock, and signals for rescue. The crew confirms they serve King Stannis.

## Events & Actions
1. **Davos watches an approaching sail** — From his rock in Blackwater Bay, Davos spots a sail on the horizon and debates whether he wants to be rescued or would rather die.
2. **Davos catalogues his deteriorating condition** — He notes his fever, brown-water bowels, shivering, thirst (pools nearly dry), and starvation (only tiny crabs at low tide). He has developed a persistent cough.
3. **Davos contemplates death** — He thinks of his dead sons, considers walking into the sea, and tells himself the gods beneath the waters have been waiting for him.
4. **Davos recalls the Battle of the Blackwater** — He remembers sailing up the Blackwater Rush under the fiery heart banner, the initial engagement beneath the Red Keep walls, then the wildfire explosion that consumed the fleet. He recalls Matthos at his elbow when Black Betha was hit, being thrown into the river, seeing his ships burning, the iron chain blocking escape.
5. **Davos recalls his underwater escape** — He dove beneath the chain and burning ships, swimming through green murk past drowning armored men, nearly drowning himself before losing consciousness.
6. **Davos washed ashore** — He woke on the stony strand of his rock with a broken mast, burned sail, and swollen corpse beside him; all vanished with the next tide.
7. **Davos discovers his fingerbones are gone** — He reaches for the leather pouch at his throat and finds it missing. He quotes his reason for keeping them: "To remind me of my king's justice."
8. **Davos prays to the Mother** — He weeps and prays for mercy, saying the fire took his luck and his sons.
9. **Davos hears (or imagines) a ghostly voice** — A voice like wind or waves whispers "You called the fire... you burned us... burned us."
10. **Davos blames Melisandre** — He cries out that it was the red woman who burned them, recalling her appearance, her origin in Asshai, how she won over Selyse and Stannis, and caused the burning of the Seven at Dragonstone and the godswood at Storm's End.
11. **Davos acknowledges his own guilt** — He recalls rowing Melisandre to Storm's End to loose her shadow child, watching the Seven burn without protest, and doing nothing when Cressen was killed.
12. **Davos decides to live** — As the sail nearly passes, he begins climbing the rock despite fever and weakness, nearly slipping twice on the damp stone.
13. **Davos signals the ship** — From the top of the rock he crouches and waves, screaming for help. He sees the lean striped hull, bronze figurehead, billowing sail, and a name painted on the hull he cannot read.
14. **The galley sends a boat** — The ship lowers its sail, puts out oars, and launches a small boat with four rowers and a fifth man in the prow.
15. **Davos identifies himself** — When asked who he is, Davos struggles to speak, then calls himself a captain and a knight who was in the battle.
16. **Davos declares for Stannis** — Realizing from the striped hull that the galley is Lysene (Salladhor Saan's), Davos shouts that he serves King Stannis. The crew confirms they do as well.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Despair, grief, guilt, and resignation give way to a sudden desperate will to live. Davos is consumed with sorrow over his dead sons, weeping freely. He feels hollow — "a hollow shell, the crab's died, there's nothing left inside." He is wracked with guilt over his role in events: rowing Melisandre, standing by during the burning of the Seven, doing nothing when Cressen died. At the turning point, he decides "he had to live" because "there was something he had to do."
- **Primary preoccupation:** The death of his sons (Dale, Allard, Maric, Matthos, possibly Devan); whether to live or die; his own guilt vs. blaming Melisandre
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to live rather than let the ship pass; climbs the rock to signal for rescue; declares for Stannis to the Lyseni sailors
- **Self-deception flags:** Davos initially places all blame on Melisandre ("It was her!") before immediately correcting himself — "Her work, and yours, onion knight." He also tells himself the Mother sent the ship, attributing divine purpose to what may be coincidence. His thought "there was something he had to do" is left undefined — it may be revenge on Melisandre or duty to Stannis, but he does not specify.
