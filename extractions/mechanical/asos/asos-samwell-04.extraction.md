- **chapter_summary:** Samwell Tarly arrives at Castle Black after walking south from the Nightfort with Gilly and her babe. He finds the castle transformed — the common hall burned, the great wooden stair destroyed, and over a thousand of Stannis Baratheon's soldiers garrisoned there following his victory over the wildlings. The Night's Watch is in the midst of choosing a new Lord Commander, and after nine days of voting, Janos Slynt is steadily gaining support while frontrunners Ser Denys Mallister and Cotter Pyke lose ground. Bowen Marsh withdraws from the contest and publicly endorses Slynt. Sam discusses with Jon Snow the possibility of sending Gilly and the babe to Horn Hill under the pretense that the child is Sam's bastard, and Jon warns him about the risks of deceiving Lord Randyll. Sam privately realizes that someone must convince either Pyke or Mallister to withdraw and support the other to prevent Slynt from winning.

## Events & Actions
1. **Gilly nurses Mance Rayder's son** — Val watches and comments the boy is hungry, having lived on goats' milk and potions from blind Maester Aemon until now.
2. **Sam observes Jon smiling sadly** — Sam notes it is the first time he has seen Jon smile since his return.
3. **Sam recalls the journey from the Nightfort** — He and Gilly walked from the Nightfort to Deep Lake to Queensgate, following a narrow track along the Wall.
4. **Sam's column encounters Ser Denys Mallister's riders** — A day and a half from Castle Black, riders from the west arrive: Ser Denys Mallister from the Shadow Tower, the wounded Bowen Marsh, and survivors from the fight at the Bridge of Skulls. Sam weeps upon seeing Dywen, Giant, and Dolorous Edd.
5. **Sam learns of Stannis's victory** — Giant tells Sam that Stannis landed knights at Eastwatch, Cotter Pyke led him along ranger's roads, and he smashed the wildlings. Mance Rayder was taken captive, a thousand of his best slain including Harma Dogshead, the rest scattered.
6. **Sam arrives at Castle Black and finds it transformed** — The common hall has burned down, the great wooden stair is a mound of broken ice and scorched timbers. Donal Noye, Rast, Deaf Dick, Red Alyn and many more are dead. The castle is packed with over a thousand of Stannis's soldiers.
7. **Sam identifies banners for Gilly** — He points out the royal Baratheon standard, House Florent, Estermont, Bar Emmon, and Wensington banners. He does not recognize the fiery heart sigil.
8. **Sam reunites with Pyp and Grenn** — Pyp shouts "Run and bar the doors, lads, it's Sam the Slayer come back from the grave." Grenn hugs Sam hard enough to threaten his ribs. They tell him about the queen's men and Melisandre.
9. **Val requests to see Mance** — She wants to show Mance his son before he is killed. Jon says Mance is a deserter whose penalty is death, and only Maester Aemon is permitted to see him. Jon promises to ask about it.
10. **Jon and Sam discuss Gilly** — Jon warns Sam he cannot keep Gilly, referencing their vows. Sam admits Gilly made him braver.
11. **Sam proposes the Horn Hill plan** — Sam suggests sending Gilly and the babe to Horn Hill, having Gilly claim the child is Sam's bastard. His mother would take them in. Lord Randyll might be pleased to believe Sam fathered a bastard, proving his manhood.
12. **Jon advises caution** — Jon notes Sam is a poor liar in person but might manage it in a letter. He warns that Lord Randyll would not take kindly to deception, and the boy's fate depends on Randyll and what sort of boy he turns out to be.
13. **Sam and Jon discuss the Lord Commander election** — Jon is frustrated at being removed from duty by Bowen Marsh. Sam assures him Lord Janos will never be chosen. Jon disagrees, saying it has been happening for days.
14. **Jon reveals his isolation** — He mentions being called "the warg" and a turncloak, that he no longer dreams of Ghost, and that all his dreams are of the crypts of Winterfell where he hears Robb's and his father's voices but has no place set for him.
15. **Sam suppresses the secret about Bran** — Sam desperately wants to tell Jon that Bran is alive and heading north with friends on a giant elk to find a three-eyed crow, but he swore three oaths of secrecy: to Bran, to Jojen Reed, and to Coldhands.
16. **Jon goes to the practice yard** — He has been training raw recruits (Satin, Horse, Hop-Robin, Arron, Emrick) and sparring with anyone willing, in the absence of a master-at-arms.
17. **Sam reviews his vote tallies** — After nine days: Ser Denys 213, Cotter Pyke 187, Slynt 74, Yarwyck 60, Marsh 49, Hobb 5, Edd 1. Ser Denys, Pyke, and Marsh have been falling since day three; Yarwyck since day six. Only Slynt is climbing.
18. **Sam feeds the ravens** — Three new ravens have arrived. They cry "Snow" — Sam taught them that. The ravenry seems dismally empty; few birds sent by Aemon have returned.
19. **Sam reflects on the political situation** — His father joined House Tarly to the cause of the boy on the Iron Throne, but neither King Joffrey nor King Tommen sent help when the Watch called. Sam thinks at least Stannis's men came.
20. **Supper scene** — Pyp tells Dolorous Edd about the straw-soldier arrow-catching contest. Edd tells the story of Watt of Long Lake landing in a pool of water after being knocked off the Bridge of Skulls — already dead from an axe in his head.
21. **Sam observes political maneuvering at supper** — Cotter Pyke sits by the fire with Eastwatch rangers; Ser Denys near the door with Shadow Tower men. Janos Slynt has the best position, halfway between fire and drafts, with Bowen Marsh beside him. Ser Alliser whispers with Othell Yarwyck.
22. **Bowen Marsh withdraws and endorses Slynt** — After Dolorous Edd's speech, Marsh publicly asks that his name be withdrawn and urges the brothers to support Lord Janos, keeping his hand on Slynt's shoulder.
23. **The tenth vote is cast and counted** — Sam, Clydas, and Maester Aemon count: Ser Denys 203, Cotter Pyke 169 (or 168 by Clydas's count), Slynt 137, Yarwyck 72, Hobb 5, Edd 2. Jon Snow did not vote. No one is close to two-thirds.
24. **Sam, Pyp, and Grenn discuss strategy** — Sam realizes Pyke and Mallister together still have almost two-thirds and that one of them must withdraw and support the other. Pyp and Grenn suggest Sam himself should approach them, as a lord's son, the maester's steward, and "Sam the Slayer." Sam says he's too craven.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Relief at being back among his brothers; anxiety about the Lord Commander election and Janos Slynt's rise; guilt and longing regarding Gilly; anguish at keeping Bran's secret from Jon; frustration at his own cowardice
- **Primary preoccupation:** The Lord Commander election — Sam is tracking vote tallies with mathematical precision and increasingly alarmed by Slynt's gains; he recognizes the strategic solution (one of the frontrunners must withdraw) but considers himself too craven to act
- **Key decisions made:** Proposes sending Gilly and the babe to Horn Hill with a lie about paternity (not yet executed); privately recognizes someone must broker a deal between Pyke and Mallister
- **Self-deception flags:** Sam tells himself "Lord Janos will never be chosen Lord Commander" even as the evidence he himself has compiled shows Slynt is the only candidate gaining; he immediately recognizes this as self-comfort rather than truth after Jon challenges him. He also deflects the suggestion that he should speak to Pyke and Mallister by calling himself craven, despite having killed an Other.
