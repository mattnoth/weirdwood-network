- **time_markers:** "once a fortnight" (Hamish the Harper plays for Margaery); "every morn and every night" (Loras visits Margaery); "several years" since Cersei last wore a particular gown; "three hundred years ago" (Maegor's decree banning the Faith Militant; Aegon the Conqueror's landing); "a hundred years" (Baelor statue on the plaza); "seven days and seven nights" (historical High Septon's fast before anointing Aegon); "never let more than three days pass" (Margaery's riding habit); "almost seven" (Russell Merryweather's age); autumn season (trees in gold and red and orange)
- **chapter_summary:** Cersei rides up Visenya's Hill to meet the new High Septon, gathering intelligence from Taena Merryweather about Margaery's court along the way. At the Great Sept of Baelor, she finds hundreds of sparrows encamped with bones of murdered holy men piled around Baelor's statue. The High Septon — a thin, barefoot ascetic — has sold the crystal crown, confined Septon Torbert to a penitent's cell, and set the Most Devout to scrubbing floors. After a negotiation before the Crone's altar, Cersei agrees to restore the Faith Militant (the Warrior's Sons and Poor Fellows, banned since Maegor's reign) in exchange for the Faith forgiving the crown's debt of nine hundred thousand gold dragons and blessing Tommen as king. On the return journey, Cersei encounters Margaery Tyrell and her entourage returning from a ride in the kingswood, and the two queens exchange pleasantries laced with hostility.

## Events & Actions
1. **Cersei rides up Visenya's Hill in her litter** — Ser Osmund Kettleblack clears the way; Lady Merryweather rides inside with her
2. **Taena reports on Margaery's court** — describes singers (Hamish the Harper, Alaric of Eysen, the Blue Bard), knights and admirers, Pycelle's visits, and Loras's frequent attendance on his sister
3. **Taena recounts Renly's wedding night** — she helped disrobe Renly and saw that he was aroused; no bloody sheet was shown after the wedding night
4. **Taena has a "wicked thought"** — implies something about Loras and Margaery's closeness, then stops herself
5. **Cersei and Taena discuss the High Septon and the Faith** — history of High Septons, the new one's lowly origins; Cersei explains naming conventions
6. **Cersei invites Taena to bring her son Russell to court** — suggests he could be a companion for Tommen; Taena hesitates, saying Russell knows only Longtable
7. **Cersei recalls Joffrey's death and the valonqar prophecy** — triggered by Taena's mention of fearing for her son; remembers blood in a candle flame and a croaking voice
8. **The litter is blocked by sparrows** — camping in the street near the top of Visenya's Hill; Ser Meryn reports the obstruction
9. **Cersei proceeds on foot** — accompanied by Ser Osmund and Ser Meryn; Ser Osfryd guards the litter
10. **Cersei crosses the sparrow-filled plaza** — recalls her wedding day when crowds cheered; the sparrows now give her dull, hostile looks
11. **Cersei sees bones piled at Baelor's statue** — skulls with flesh, a crow feeding, flies; she demands an explanation
12. **One-legged sparrow explains the bones** — remains of holy men and women murdered across the realm; asks for protection, not vengeance
13. **Man with star on brow challenges Cersei** — says a king who does not protect his people is no king; calls Tommen a "fat boy king"; another man grabs Ser Meryn's wrist urging him to join the Faith
14. **Cersei is stopped by armed men at the sept doors** — wear mail, boiled leather, and red stars on white surcoats; cross their spears to bar her entry
15. **Greybeard insists Cersei's knights disarm** — "this is the house of the gods"; Cersei leaves Ser Osmund and Ser Meryn outside and enters alone
16. **Cersei finds the Most Devout scrubbing floors** — in the Hall of Lamps; Septon Raynard on his knees with bleeding blistered hands
17. **Cersei meets the new High Septon** — thin, barefoot, scrubbing the floor with the rest; he says "Work is a form of prayer, most pleasing to the Smith"
18. **High Septon reveals he has sold the crystal crown and all valuables** — "the poor need food more than we need gold and crystal"; wool will suffice
19. **High Septon reveals Septon Torbert is confined** — penitent's cell on bread and water for being plump while the realm starves
20. **The High Septon leads Cersei to the sept proper** — they pass through the inner doors; she observes hedge knights at the Warrior's altar and sparrows at prayer at the Mother's altar
21. **Cersei and the High Septon confer before the Crone's altar** — kneeling; he prays briefly, then they negotiate
22. **The High Septon describes the suffering of the Faith** — villages destroyed, septs burned, septons killed, silent sisters raped; accuses the Hound of atrocities at Saltpans
23. **Cersei deflects blame onto Stannis and the northmen** — calls Sandor Clegane a traitor who now fights for Beric Dondarrion
24. **The High Septon cites Jaehaerys the Conciliator's oath** — the crown swore to protect the Faith; demands Cersei explain why the king's knights were absent during atrocities
25. **Cersei presses for Tommen's blessing** — the High Septon says the hour is "not yet ripe"; tells the story of the High Septon who fasted before anointing Aegon
26. **Cersei proposes restoring the Faith Militant** — points to the Warrior's sword on the altar; suggests Maegor's laws could be undone
27. **The High Septon eagerly agrees** — calls it "the answer to three hundred years of prayer"
28. **Cersei asks for the crown's debt to be forgiven in exchange** — the crown owes the Faith nine hundred thousand six hundred and seventy-four gold dragons
29. **The deal is struck** — debt forgiven; Tommen blessed; the Warrior's Sons to escort the High Septon to the king; Poor Fellows reborn to defend the land
30. **Cersei departs triumphant** — believes she has rid King's Landing of sparrows, secured the blessing, and saved nearly a million dragons of debt
31. **Cersei and Taena drink hippocras on the ride home** — toast to the Warrior's Sons and Cersei's brilliance
32. **Cersei encounters Margaery's party at the base of Aegon's High Hill** — returning from riding in the kingswood with flowers, admirer-knights, cousins, and the Blue Bard
33. **Cersei and Margaery exchange barbed pleasantries** — Margaery offers to share the queen's burden; Cersei says she has never seen Margaery as a rival; Margaery invites Cersei to ride with them; Cersei warns that Robert died in the woods
34. **Cersei reflects on the Tyrells with paranoia** — believes they freed Tyrion, that Tyrion and Sansa are in Highgarden, that the gold coin proves Tyrell complicity in Joffrey's murder
35. **Cersei laughs at Margaery's remark about Loras** — Margaery says "my brother keeps me well protected"; Cersei recalls Taena's "wicked thought" and laughs; promises Margaery she will share the jest

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Cersei oscillates between contempt (for the High Septon, the sparrows, Pycelle), triumph (after striking the deal), annoyance and hostility (toward Margaery), paranoia (about Tyrell conspiracies), and flashes of grief and fear (Joffrey's death, the valonqar prophecy). She is exhilarated by what she considers political brilliance.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing Tommen's blessing from the High Septon, neutralizing the sparrow threat to King's Landing, and undermining Margaery Tyrell. Cersei is also building a navy independent of the Tyrell-allied Redwynes.
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees to restore the Faith Militant (Warrior's Sons and Poor Fellows) in exchange for debt forgiveness and Tommen's blessing. Decides to continue on foot through the sparrow crowd rather than wait. Invites Taena to bring her son Russell to court as a companion for Tommen.
- **Self-deception flags:** Cersei believes she has outmaneuvered the High Septon ("Even her lord father could have done no better") — she does not see the danger in arming a religious movement. She compares herself to Tywin throughout. She constructs an elaborate conspiracy theory about the Tyrells freeing Tyrion with no solid evidence beyond a gold coin. She attributes Joffrey's lack of friends to circumstances rather than his personality. She thinks restoring armed religious orders will serve her ends against Stannis without considering the orders could turn against the crown.
