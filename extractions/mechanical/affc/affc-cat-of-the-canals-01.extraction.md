- **chapter_summary:** Cat of the Canals (Arya Stark) follows her daily routine: rising before dawn at Brusco's house, traveling by boat to the fishmarket, then selling shellfish at the Ragman's Harbor. She encounters sailors from the Brazen Monkey out of Gulltown who tell her Lady Lysa is dead, murdered by her singer. She visits the Happy Port brothel where Dareon, a Night's Watch deserter, sings for the whores. After selling her oysters, Cat walks with Dareon into an alley and kills him, taking his boots. She returns to the House of Black and White on the night of the black moon, helps the waif learn about poisons, and reports Dareon's death to the kindly man. She drinks warm milk that tastes bitter, and wakes the next morning blind.

## Events & Actions
1. **Cat wakes before dawn** — She rises first in the little room beneath the eaves she shares with Talea and Brea at Brusco's house, dresses quickly in the darkness.
2. **Morning boat trip to fishmarket** — Brusco, his sons, and the three girls take the boat down small canals to the Long Canal, then south to the fishmarket, arriving as the Titan roars at sunrise.
3. **Brusco purchases shellfish** — He inspects the morning catch, tapping casks and crates with his cane to select oysters, clams, crabs, mussels, cockles, and prawns. The girls and sons carry the heavy loads.
4. **Return and distribution** — Back at the house, shellfish divided into three barrows: Brea goes to Purple Harbor, Talea to Moon Pool area, Cat to Ragman's Harbor.
5. **Cat sells at Ragman's Harbor** — She cries her wares in trade tongue, encounters various sailors (Tyroshi, Lyseni, Ibbenese, Summer Islanders, Westerosi), and is followed by stray cats.
6. **Encounter with ships** — Cat notes two ships departed and five new ones arrived: the Brazen Monkey (Gulltown), an Ibbenese whaler, two Pentoshi cogs, and a Volantene galley. A mate on the galley tells her their captain was killed by Lysene pirate Saan near the Stepstones.
7. **News from the Brazen Monkey** — Westerosi sailors from Gulltown tell Cat that Lady Lysa is dead, "murdered by her own singer." They also say the little lord kept the Vale out of the war. Cat is momentarily affected but suppresses her reaction.
8. **Cat visits Tagganaro and Casso** — Tagganaro offers Cat a partnership since Little Narbo's hand was injured. Three of Narbo's fingers will not bend after the Drunken Daughter stabbed him. Narbo plans to be an oarsman.
9. **Cat arrives at the Happy Port** — She meets mummers from the Ship who report Quence found Allaquo abed with Sloey; both fought with mummer swords and left. They'll only have five drunken oarsmen tonight.
10. **Inside the Happy Port** — Cat finds Merry listening to Dareon play his woodharp while Yna braids Lanna's hair. Cat is angry seeing Dareon, a Night's Watch deserter, living lavishly as a singer.
11. **The Sailor's Wife speaks to Cat** — She comments on Dareon's voice and fair face in the Common Tongue. Cat thinks he is "fair of face and foul of heart." The narrator describes the Sailor's Wife's custom of only bedding men who marry her, and her visits to the Isle of the Gods.
12. **Cat sells her remaining oysters** — She announces her wares, Merry wakes and buys them, served with bread and vinegar.
13. **Cat and Dareon leave the Happy Port together** — Dareon boasts about his singing career, plans to play at the Inn of the Green Eel, and aspires to play in the Purple and the Sealord's Palace.
14. **Cat asks about Sam** — She inquires about "the fat one" and the Lady Ushanora. Dareon says Lord Snow commanded them all to sail on it but Sam wouldn't leave "the old man." Dareon says "it's too late now."
15. **Cat kills Dareon** — As they step into a twisty little alley, Cat says "Just so." By the time she returns to Brusco's house, she has Dareon's boots and her purse.
16. **Cat returns to Brusco's house** — She gives Brusco her purse of coins and Dareon's boots. He notes the boots are too small for him. She reminds him the moon will be black tonight. He says "Valar dohaeris." She thinks "Valar morghulis."
17. **Cat walks to the House of Black and White** — Fog rises as she walks through Braavos. She enters through the weirwood door. Only a few candles burn.
18. **Cat becomes "no one"** — In the vaults she removes all of Cat's clothes and bathes in lemonwater to wash away the smell. She dons clean robes and soft slippers.
19. **Cat eats** — Umma saved fried cod and mashed yellow turnips. She eats quickly.
20. **The waif teaches Cat about poisons** — Cat helps prepare potions, climbing ladders for herbs. The waif explains sweetsleep (sweet-tasting, kills in three pinches), the tears of Lys (tasteless, odorless, mimics bowel sickness), and basilisk blood paste (causes violent madness in warm-blooded animals).
21. **Cat asks about basilisk blood and dogs** — The waif slaps her for chewing her lip, an Arya habit.
22. **The waif tells her origin story** — Born the only child of a noble lord; mother died; father remarried; stepmother tried to poison her when a half-sister was born; the poison did not kill but left her "as you see"; father came to the House of Black and White; the waif was given to serve; the stepmother received "the gift."
23. **Cat and the waif's exchange about truth** — The waif says there is one untruth and one exaggeration. Cat guesses the Many-Faced God took two-thirds, not all, of the father's wealth (the exaggeration). The waif says "I lied about the lie" — there was no lie.
24. **The kindly man arrives and asks for three things** — Cat reports: (1) Little Narbo's fingers won't bend, he means to be an oarsman; (2) Quence and Alaquo fought and left the Ship (but she only thinks they'll come back, doesn't know); (3) Dareon is dead — his throat was slit and he was pushed into a canal, his boots were kept.
25. **The kindly man presses on identity** — He asks who did it. Cat says "Arya of House Stark." He asks who Cat is. She says "No one." He says "You lie" and asks the waif to bring wine for him and warm milk for "Arya."
26. **Cat drinks the warm milk** — It smells a little burnt and has a bitter aftertaste. The kindly man tells her to go to bed.
27. **Wolf dream** — She dreams of being a wolf prowling alone, bounding over rooftops, padding beside canals, stalking shadows through fog. No pack.
28. **Cat wakes blind** — The next morning she cannot see.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Complex mixture of contentment in her Cat identity (loves the rare clear days, the noise and smells of Ragman's Harbor, the sailor friends she's made) and underlying anger/identity conflict (angry seeing Dareon's desertion, suppressed grief about Lysa's death, persistent wolf dreams she cannot stop). Satisfaction after killing Dareon. Brief amusement and connection during the exchange with the waif about truth and lies.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Maintaining her Cat identity while being haunted by Arya's memories and instincts (wolf dreams, lip-chewing, remembering the Red Keep cat, emotional reaction to Lysa's death). Also focused on fulfilling the kindly man's requirement to learn three new things.
- **Key decisions made:** Kills Dareon for deserting the Night's Watch. Reports the killing honestly to the kindly man, attributing it to "Arya of House Stark." Asks about basilisk blood's effect on dogs (revealing a lingering Arya preoccupation — the waif catches it).
- **Self-deception flags:** Repeatedly tells herself she is Cat, not Arya, but cannot stop wolf-dreaming or reacting to Arya-connected stimuli. Says "It's nought to me" about Lysa's death, then immediately wheels away. Tells herself "Cats never weep... no more than wolves do" about the nightmare of her mother. The killing of Dareon is an act of Arya Stark's justice, not Cat's — yet she tries to frame it as just another thing learned.
