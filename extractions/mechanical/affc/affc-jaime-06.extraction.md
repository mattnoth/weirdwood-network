## Events & Actions
1. **Jaime rides to the drawbridge to parley with the Blackfish** — He wears a leather jack and crimson cloak; rides his horse Honor to meet Ser Brynden on the drawbridge.
2. **The Blackfish demands Catelyn's daughters** — He asks Jaime to fulfill his oath; Jaime admits he does not have them.
3. **Jaime offers to exchange the Westerlings for Edmure** — The Blackfish refuses, citing his oath to protect his king's queen.
4. **Jaime offers surrender terms** — Lives spared, garrison may take the black or serve Lord Emmon; Blackfish offered the Wall. All refused.
5. **Jaime proposes single combat** — Blackfish rejects it as meaningless since Jaime's promises are worthless and Jaime cannot fight with one hand.
6. **Jaime offers to fight the Blackfish personally** — Asks to be freed from his oath to Catelyn. Blackfish declines, calling the risk too small.
7. **The Blackfish ends the parley** — Mocks Jaime and rides back into Riverrun; portcullis drops.
8. **Jaime returns to camp and orders a war council** — Tells Ser Daven to assemble commanders and river lords.
9. **War council debates how to take Riverrun** — Lord Emmon urges hanging Edmure; Ser Daven wants storm; Strongboar volunteers; Marbrand proposes night raid; Edwyn proposes killing Blackfish with archers; Norbert Vance offers to negotiate; Lord Piper insults the Freys.
10. **Lord Piper storms out after confrontation with Edwyn** — Piper demands news of his son Marq; Edwyn threatens to return him in pieces; Walder Rivers offers to fight Piper.
11. **Jaime ends the council and announces attack at first light** — Dismisses the lords.
12. **Jaime crosses the Tumblestone to the Frey camp** — Takes Strongboar and Ser Ilyn; they wait for the ferry.
13. **Jaime confronts Ser Ryman on the gallows** — Strikes him with the golden hand; dismisses him from command; gives command to Edwyn.
14. **Jaime orders Edwyn to send word to Lord Walder** — Demands all Frey prisoners be delivered to the crown.
15. **Ser Ilyn cuts Edmure's rope** — Edmure collapses; Strongboar pulls him up by the remaining hemp.
16. **Jaime confiscates the bronze crown** — Orders Ryman to take his whore but leave the crown.
17. **Jaime takes the singer and returns across the ferry** — Edmure asks "Why?"; Jaime calls it a "wedding gift."
18. **Jaime reveals Roslin is pregnant** — Edmure confirms his wife carries his child; Jaime internally thinks "that's your death she has growing in her belly."
19. **Jaime threatens Edmure with destruction of Riverrun** — Describes in detail: scaling the walls, killing the garrison, butchering herds, felling the godswood, pulling down walls, diverting the Tumblestone, and launching the baby with a trebuchet.
20. **Jaime has the singer play "The Rains of Castamere"** — Edmure recoils from the singer; Jaime leaves him to consider.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Controlled frustration during parley; anger barely contained when called a cripple; dark resolve after the parley fails; cold ruthlessness during the threat to Edmure; self-awareness that he is becoming like his father
- **Primary preoccupation:** Taking Riverrun without a bloody assault; the tension between his reputation as an oathbreaker and his desire to keep his word; his maiming and what it has cost him
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to storm the castle at first light; dismisses Ser Ryman from command; seizes Edmure and uses him as leverage; threatens total destruction of Riverrun and Edmure's child; resolves to be first on the battlements (suicidal undertone)
- **Self-deception flags:** Claims to himself "Blame yourself for that, Blackfish. You left me little choice" — deflecting moral responsibility; tells himself the lie about provisions "boldly as he could" while knowing it's a bluff; the trebuchet threat mirrors Tywin's cruelty while Jaime has been trying to distinguish himself from his father
