- **chapter_summary:** Brienne, Podrick, Septon Meribald, and Ser Hyle Hunt travel a road lined with hanged corpses — the raiders of Saltpans, each with salt stuffed in their mouths. They arrive at the crossroads inn, now run by orphan children under the direction of a girl named Willow and a young smith named Gendry, whose striking resemblance to Renly Baratheon causes Brienne to realize he is Robert Baratheon's bastard son. During supper Ser Hyle proposes marriage to Brienne, which she refuses. That night, seven armed outlaws arrive — their leader wearing the Hound's helm. Brienne fights and kills the man in the Hound's helm with Oathkeeper, but Biter, a massive pale man, tackles her and savages her: breaking her arm, cracking bones, and biting chunks from her cheek. As Brienne loses consciousness, something that looks like a sword emerges from Biter's mouth.

## Events & Actions
1. **Corpses discovered along the road** — Brienne's party finds hanged men swinging from trees, each with salt packed in their mouths. The first is on a dead lightning-struck tree; the second has been torn down by scavengers.
2. **Ser Hyle finds a lion-crested halfhelm** — Dog sniffs out a corpse; Hyle retrieves a halfhelm with a headless lion crest and a skull with worms inside; offers it to Pod, who refuses.
3. **Road becomes a gallery of the dead** — Corpses appear every hundred yards, hanging from many species of trees, wearing faded cloaks and various heraldic badges. Brienne identifies them as broken men from many armies.
4. **Hyle identifies the hanged as the Saltpans raiders** — Brienne suspects the hangings are the work of Beric Dondarrion and his outlaws.
5. **Meribald recounts the history of the inn** — The Two Crowns, the Bellringer Inn, the Clanking Dragon (with Long Jon Heddle's three-headed iron dragon sign), the River Inn, and now the crossroads inn. The dragon sign was destroyed by Lord Darry because it resembled the Blackfyre sigil.
6. **Saltpans recalled** — Brienne remembers finding Saltpans destroyed, the castle closed tight, and being refused shelter by a woman on the battlements. They spent the night in the woods.
7. **Arrival at the crossroads inn** — They hear hammering; the inn yard is a sea of mud; four girls and a boy are in the yard; a gibbet with rusted chains stands in the yard.
8. **Encounter with orphan children** — The girls say they have no mothers; Willow identifies herself and offers beds; demands silver as payment.
9. **Brienne sees Gendry and is struck by his resemblance to Renly** — She gasps "My lord?" before realizing it is not Renly but a younger boy with the same black hair and deep blue eyes, though with a squarer jaw, bushier brows, and a smith's muscular build.
10. **Children emerge from hiding** — Willow whistles and ragged orphans appear from under the porch and in windows, some with loaded crossbows.
11. **Rooms taken** — They take three adjoining rooms with featherbeds; Brienne pays extra for firewood; Pod stays in her room.
12. **Brienne plans to leave early without Ser Hyle** — She intends to strike out with Pod alone before dawn; Meribald is continuing to Nutten, Riverbend, and Lord Harroway's Town.
13. **Brienne considers her options at the crossroads** — The high road east to the Vale, the river road west to Riverrun, the kingsroad north past the Twins and through the Neck, or the kingsroad south back to King's Landing.
14. **Ser Hyle proposes marriage to Brienne** — He argues it would benefit both: lands for him, children for her. He reveals he has sired at least one bastard. Brienne rejects him coldly, threatening to make him a eunuch if he enters her room.
15. **Hyle notes bloodstains on the common room floor** — Brienne reminds him this is where Sandor Clegane killed three of his brother's men.
16. **Supper in the common room** — Meribald leads grace; Gendry refuses to pray and leaves without eating, arms crossed; Ben Big Mouth reveals Gendry worships the Lord of Light; Willow hits Ben with a spoon.
17. **Children devour the food** — They fall on the meal ravenously.
18. **Brienne brings food to Gendry at the forge** — She watches him work, noting his resemblance to both Renly and Robert.
19. **Brienne questions Gendry about his parentage** — He reveals his mother is dead, he never knew his father, he was born in King's Landing. He calls himself a knight. He recalls seeing Robert at tourneys, at Baelor's Sept, and near the Mud Gate.
20. **Brienne internally realizes Gendry is Robert Baratheon's bastard** — She confirms to herself that Joffrey and Tommen were not Robert's sons, recalling Stannis's claim.
21. **Seven riders arrive at the inn** — Dog barks; armed men in ragged cloaks ride in; some are wounded; the last is Biter (massive, maggot-white, weeping sores). The lead rider wears the Hound's helm, visible in a lightning flash.
22. **Willow confronts the riders with a crossbow** — The man in the Hound's helm threatens her obscenely.
23. **Brienne steps out with Oathkeeper** — She challenges the outlaws to rape her instead, drawing their attention from Willow and the children.
24. **Brienne provokes the helmed man** — She taunts him by saying Shagwell claimed they cut his manhood off when they took his nose.
25. **Brienne fights and kills the man in the Hound's helm** — She uses Ser Goodwin's training: lets him exhaust himself, turns him so rain is in his eyes, and when he charges she runs him through with Oathkeeper along his spine. She whispers "Sapphires" to him as he dies.
26. **Biter attacks Brienne** — He crashes into her from behind, slamming her to the ground in a puddle, tearing Oathkeeper from her grasp, pulling her hair, throttling her.
27. **Brienne fights Biter desperately** — She punches his face repeatedly but it is like hitting wet dough; he is immovable. She draws her dagger and slashes his belly open.
28. **Biter savages Brienne** — He breaks her forearm with his knee, wrenches away the dagger, slams her head against the ground, and bites into her cheek, tearing off flesh and eating it. He bites again, chewing and swallowing.
29. **Fighting continues elsewhere** — Brienne hears Dog barking, men shouting, and steel clashing — she believes Ser Hyle has joined the fight.
30. **Brienne loses consciousness** — Biter opens his mouth and his tongue extends impossibly far, appearing like a pointed sword. Brienne blacks out.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Sad and disturbed by the hanged corpses despite knowing they were evil men; stunned and grief-struck when she sees Gendry and mistakes him for Renly ("No hammerblow to the heart could have felled her half so hard"); conflicted about her quest — torn between continuing and returning home to Tarth; yearning for her father and for Jaime; angry and unforgiven toward Hyle Hunt for the Highgarden wager; protective toward the orphan children; terrified but resolute when the seven outlaws arrive ("No chance, and no choice"); focused and disciplined during the sword fight, channeling Ser Goodwin's training; desperate and fading during Biter's attack; ultimately dissociating ("floating above herself, watching the horror as if it were happening to some other woman, to some stupid girl who thought she was a knight")
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding Sansa Stark; which road to take from the crossroads; the question of Gendry's parentage; protecting the children
- **Key decisions made:** Plans to leave before dawn without Ser Hyle and Septon Meribald; decides to confront the seven outlaws alone to protect Willow and the children; chooses to fight the man in the Hound's helm by provoking him into a one-on-one duel
- **Self-deception flags:** Wonders if Jaime would comfort her if she wept on his shoulder, then immediately dismisses this as "what men wanted" — conflicted desire masked as cynicism; thinks "I cannot die yet, there is something I still need to do" — clinging to her quest even as she dissociates from the violence being done to her
