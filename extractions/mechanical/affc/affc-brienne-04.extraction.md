- **approximate_timeline:** Mid-book; Brienne is traveling northeast from Maidenpool through Crackclaw Point, a journey of several days, searching for Sansa Stark
- **time_markers:** Multiple days of travel condensed; nights spent at inns early on, then a fishing village, then camping in the open; rain lasting multiple days; "the days are growing shorter"; nightfall, dawn, dusk, midday referenced across different travel days; moonrise at chapter's end; "half as old as Pods" (Dick's reference to the beacon tower falling)
- **chapter_summary:** Brienne, Podrick Payne, and their guide Nimble Dick Crabb travel northeast from Maidenpool through Crackclaw Point toward the ruined castle called the Whispers, where Dick claims a fool paid passage for three. Along the way Dick tells tales of Crackclaw Point's history, and Brienne notices a mysterious rider following them. At the Whispers they discover not Dontos Hollard but three Bloody Mummers — Shagwell, Timeon, and Pyg — who kill Dick Crabb and attack Brienne. Brienne kills Pyg and Timeon with Oathkeeper, then kills Shagwell with a dagger after he yields and attacks her. Ser Hyle Hunt reveals himself as the rider, sent by Lord Randyll Tarly to follow Brienne.

## Events & Actions
1. **Travel along coast road** — Brienne, Podrick, and Nimble Dick travel northeast from Maidenpool along the coast of Crackclaw Point, staying at inns along the way.
2. **Dick refuses trust** — Dick repeatedly suggests sharing a room with Brienne; she refuses and warns Podrick to watch Dick while she sleeps.
3. **Dick caught stealing** — While Brienne steps away, Dick rifles through her saddlebags. Podrick catches him. Brienne reveals the purse is a decoy filled with coppers and flour.
4. **Dick tells tales of Crackclaw Point** — He tells stories of the region's history, local heroes (Ser Clarence Crabb, Old Crackbones), and the First Men bloodline. Brienne counters with the tale of Ser Galladon of Morne.
5. **Dick tells of Targaryen loyalty** — Recounts how Visenya took the Crackclaw lords as her own men; names Crabbs and others who fought with Rhaegar on the Trident and served in the Kingsguard.
6. **Fishing village shelter** — They sleep in a hay barn; Brienne claims the loft and pulls up the ladder, leaving Dick below.
7. **Brienne's memory of Septa Roelle** — Recalls being told that men's compliments were false, meant to win her father's favor; this lesson served her at Harrenhal when Hyle Hunt and friends played their courting game.
8. **Brienne recalls Bitterbridge mêlée** — She remembers seeking out and defeating each of her false suitors, and fighting Loras Tyrell last. Falls asleep dreaming of the fight and Jaime fastening a rainbow cloak on her shoulders.
9. **Travel resumes in rain** — Rain continues; the road deteriorates. Dick's cloak soaks through. Brienne feels momentary pity.
10. **Camp among rocks** — Forced to camp above the tideline with no shelter. Dick tells the tale of squishers. Brienne fails to light a fire with wet driftwood. She gnaws salt beef.
11. **Night reflection** — Brienne wonders about Sansa, thinks about passage for three. She dozes and wakes convinced someone is looming over her.
12. **Brienne's regrets** — Reflects on failing Renly, failing to avenge him, failing Lady Catelyn. Wishes Jaime had come with her.
13. **Arrival at Dyre Den** — Road ends at the cliff beneath Lord Brune's castle. They climb a steep stony path through a cleft with carved steps and fantastically eroded rock shapes.
14. **Podrick spots a rider** — From the heights, Podrick sees a rider two or three miles behind on the road they took. Dick denies involvement.
15. **Observation of Dyre Den** — A face (possibly a woman) peers down from a parapet. Dick says only women and babies remain. Brienne notices Dick's torn-away badge and suspects he is a deserter.
16. **Decision to press on** — Rather than confront the rider, they ride into the limestone hills and pine forests beyond Dyre Den.
17. **Deep forest travel** — Disorienting pine forest; Brienne fears getting lost. Dick tries to sing "The Bear and the Maiden Fair" but the pines swallow the sound. Podrick says "This is a bad place."
18. **Bog encounter** — They ride into a bog that swallows horses to their withers; have to fight back to solid ground and find another way.
19. **Brienne's Ser Goodwin memory** — Recalls her master-at-arms' doubts about her hardness; the story of his friend who flinched from killing; being sent to slaughter animals. She hones her sword and resolves not to flinch.
20. **Arrival at the Whispers** — They emerge from the forest to find the ruined castle on a cliff edge. They hear the "whispering" — waves through caverns below.
21. **Dick shows the beacon tower** — The collapsed tower and smugglers' cove below; steps gone with the cliff collapse. Dick touches Brienne's back; she recoils.
22. **Dick's nervousness** — He worries the fool might hold a grudge about the outdated map he sold. Brienne doubts Dontos would be threatening.
23. **Circuit of the walls** — Main gate rotted and collapsed; portcullis rusted shut. Podrick offers to climb over the collapsed wall but Brienne forbids it (loose stones, poisonous ivy).
24. **Postern gate found** — North side, behind blackberry bramble. Berries picked and bush hacked — someone has been through recently.
25. **Entry into the Whispers** — Brienne draws Oathkeeper. She sends Podrick to fetch it from her bedroll, gives her longsword to Dick. They enter through the postern.
26. **Bailey described** — Overgrown with weeds, pines, and a pale young weirwood. Collapsed stable, dungeon steps, remnants of a fire, collapsed cliff-side wall.
27. **Pyg emerges** — A man caked with dirt slides from the bushes with a broken sword. Brienne recognizes his face — small eyes, flat nostrils.
28. **Timeon emerges** — Slips over the lip of the well wearing an iron halfhelm wrapped in stained red silk, carrying a throwing spear.
29. **Shagwell drops from weirwood** — Drops from the weirwood tree in faded motley. Swings his triple morningstar and destroys Dick Crabb's knee.
30. **Shagwell kills Dick Crabb** — Strikes him in the face with the morningstar; sickening crunch.
31. **Timeon tells of the Bloody Mummers' dispersal** — Urswyck went to Oldtown, Rorge to Saltpans, Timeon/Pyg/Shagwell to Maidenpool but couldn't get a ship. Tells of Vargo Hoat's death at the Mountain's hands — dismembered piece by piece.
32. **Brienne asks about Sansa** — Timeon says the Hound has the Stark girl; she was making for Riverrun and he stole her. Beric Dondarrion is also searching. The Hound killed three of his brother's men at an inn by the crossroads; innkeep and whores confirmed the girl was there.
33. **Fight with Pyg** — Brienne attacks Pyg; Oathkeeper cuts through his thigh, then she stabs him through the throat.
34. **Fight with Timeon** — Timeon throws a spear that cuts Brienne's cheek. She attacks: slashes off his ear and half his cheek, hacks the head off his spear, puts a foot of steel into his belly. Cuts off his hand ("That one was for Jaime"). Finishes him at his request.
35. **Fight with Shagwell** — Podrick hits Shagwell with stones from atop the collapsed wall. Shagwell yields. Brienne orders him to dig Dick Crabb's grave.
36. **Shagwell digs the grave** — Takes the rest of the day; his hands are bloody and blistered by nightfall.
37. **Brienne buries Dick Crabb** — Carries him to the grave, apologizes: "I'm sorry that I never trusted you. I don't know how to do that anymore."
38. **Shagwell attacks** — As Brienne kneels, Shagwell attacks with a jagged rock. Brienne kills him with her dagger, stabbing him repeatedly and demanding he laugh.
39. **Brienne's grief** — She realizes the sobs she hears are her own; throws down the knife and shudders.
40. **Dick's burial completed** — Brienne and Podrick lower Dick into the grave. She tosses two gold dragons in — the promised reward.
41. **Hyle Hunt reveals himself** — He is atop the crumbling wall. Explains Lord Randyll sent him to follow Brienne and bring back Sansa Stark if found.
42. **Next steps discussed** — Brienne says she'll look for the Hound based on Timeon's information. Hunt says the Hound will kill her. They cover Dick's grave together by moonlight.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply mistrustful and wary throughout; moments of pity for Dick Crabb; nostalgic pain recalling Renly, Catelyn, and Jaime; furious resolve before the battle; grief and sobbing after killing Shagwell; regret over never trusting Dick
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding Sansa Stark; assessing whether Dick Crabb is trustworthy or leading them into a trap; vigilance against the unknown rider
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to give Dick a sword before entering the Whispers; charges Pyg rather than retreating; kills Shagwell after he yields and attacks; decides to pursue the Hound based on Timeon's information about Sansa heading for Riverrun
- **Self-deception flags:** Brienne tells herself "there's naught here that we need fear" in the forest when she clearly feels the same dread as Podrick; she tells herself she cannot trust anyone, yet her inability to trust Dick Crabb fills her with guilt when he dies — her mistrust may be excessive
