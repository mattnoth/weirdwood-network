- **chapter_summary:** Cersei holds an emergency midnight audience after news arrives that the ironborn under Euron Greyjoy have attacked the Shield Islands with a thousand ships. Margaery Tyrell urges recalling the Redwyne fleet from the siege of Dragonstone and Storm's End, but Cersei refuses. Loras volunteers to storm Dragonstone by force, and Cersei eagerly grants him command, calculating she wins regardless of whether he succeeds or dies. Afterward, Falyse Stokeworth arrives in distress to report that Bronn killed her husband Ser Balman in a duel after their bungled assassination attempt, and Cersei hands Falyse over to Qyburn for his experiments. The chapter ends with Cersei's sexual encounter with Taena Merryweather, intercut with memories of Robert Baratheon's marital abuse.

## Events & Actions
1. **Emergency midnight audience** — Margaery Tyrell bursts into the throne room with news that the ironborn have attacked the Shield Islands with a thousand ships. Lords Hewett and Chester are slain, Lord Serry's son and heir killed, Lord Grimm taken prisoner, Lord Serry has fled to Highgarden.
2. **Council debate on the ironborn threat** — Ser Harys and Merryweather dismiss the numbers; Aurane Waters warns that only the Arbor fleet can oppose such a force; Pycelle dismisses Euron as a carrion crow who will retreat.
3. **Cersei identifies Euron Greyjoy** — Qyburn names the new iron king as Balon's brother, the Crow's Eye. Margaery reports he has raised four lords of his own to replace the slain Shield lords.
4. **Cersei speculates Stannis is behind the attack** — She theorizes Stannis has allied with Euron as a diversion from Dragonstone and Storm's End. Merryweather flatters her cleverness.
5. **Margaery urges recalling the Redwyne fleet** — She wants word sent to her father at Storm's End to lift the siege and send the fleet to the Reach. Cersei refuses, insisting Dragonstone and Storm's End are more valuable than the Shield Islands.
6. **Loras argues the strategic danger** — He explains that from the Shields, ironmen can threaten Oldtown, the Arbor, and sail up the Mander to the heart of the Reach, even threaten Highgarden.
7. **Cersei dismisses Tyrell concerns** — She suggests they use fishing boats, pleasure barges, and ferries to transport troops, and hire sellsails from Myr and Lys.
8. **Loras volunteers to storm Dragonstone** — He kneels before Cersei and asks to take Dragonstone by direct assault, promising it will fall within a fortnight.
9. **Cersei grants Loras command** — She assigns him the dromond Sweet Cersei and commands him to sail for Dragonstone at once. She kisses both Loras and Margaery. She calculates she wins regardless of whether Loras lives or dies.
10. **Cersei, Qyburn, and Pycelle leave via the king's door** — Pycelle cautions against Loras's boldness; Cersei privately hopes Loras will die at Dragonstone.
11. **Qyburn discusses his champion** — He describes a future champion for the Kingsguard who will be devoted, able to kill enemies and keep secrets, and whom no living man can withstand. Cersei has ordered specially heavy armor forged for this champion.
12. **Cersei discusses the agreement with the High Septon** — She recalls commanding Pycelle to prepare papers for the arrangement, overriding his objections about King Maegor's decrees.
13. **Cersei returns to bed and tells Taena everything** — She describes the throne room events, notes Margaery is now "next to naked" without Loras, and mentions the captain of Margaery's guards as a garrulous old man with a squirrel on his surcoat.
14. **Falyse Stokeworth arrives in distress** — A guardsman reports Lady Stokeworth is below in a bad state. Cersei sends Taena back to bed and goes to meet Falyse in her solar.
15. **Falyse reports Ser Balman's death** — Bronn killed Balman's horse with a lance, crushed his legs, forced a confession, and then put a dagger in his eye. Bronn expelled Falyse from Stokeworth, took her clothes, and threatened violence.
16. **Cersei learns Balman confessed under duress** — She is alarmed that Bronn forced a confession from Balman, potentially revealing Cersei's involvement in the assassination plot.
17. **Cersei hands Falyse to Qyburn** — She decides Falyse cannot be allowed to spread tales. She tells Qyburn to take her for his work in the black cells. Qyburn says "the puppeteers are quite used up."
18. **Cersei reflects on Bronn and Tyrion** — She concludes Bronn was not truly harboring Tyrion; the baby-naming was the sellsword's mockery, not Tyrion's doing. She fantasizes about having Loras kill Bronn.
19. **Cersei's memories of Robert's abuse** — Extended recollection of Robert's drunken sexual assaults, their frequency declining over the years, his pretending to forget, the chipped-tooth incident, and her pretending he was Rhaegar.
20. **Cersei's sexual encounter with Taena** — Cersei initiates sex with Taena, intercut with memories of Robert. She discovers "it was no good" and feels nothing. She recalls secretly destroying Robert's seed. Taena climaxes; Cersei rolls away. "It had never happened."
