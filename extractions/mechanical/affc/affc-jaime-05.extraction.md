# AFFC — Jaime V

## Chapter Metadata

- **chapter_number:** 34
- **pov_character:** Jaime Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jaime V
- **first_available:** AFFC Jaime V
- **location_primary:** Jaime's camp near the Red Fork, then the siege camps at Riverrun
- **location_secondary:** Burned village ruins along the Red Fork; the ford upstream of Riverrun
- **approximate_timeline:** Jaime is traveling toward Riverrun with his column; the chapter spans two days — an evening meeting with Ser Daven, a midnight sword practice, and the next day's arrival at the siege
- **time_markers:** "still blue air of dusk" (Daven's arrival); midnight practice with Ser Ilyn ("midnight dance"); "The last day of their journey"; "late afternoon when they sighted Riverrun"; "at first light on the morrow" (planned parley); "the day before last" (finding hanged men); Emmon Frey arrived at Casterly Rock "in his fourteenth year"; Genna "was seven" when betrothed; Genna has been a Frey "for fifty years"; Emmon "half his hair had been gone before he turned thirty. Now he was sixty"; "The nights grow colder" (seasonal); Jaime recalls being kept at Riverrun for "a fortnight" by Hoster Tully as a squire; Genna says Tywin would not speak to her "for half a year" after she told him Tyrion was his true son
- **chapter_summary:** Ser Daven Lannister, the Warden of the West, arrives at Jaime's camp and briefs him on the stalled siege of Riverrun: the Blackfish has two years of stores, Ser Ryman Frey's daily threat to hang Edmure Tully is ignored, and the river lords are unreliable. Jaime practices swordplay with Ser Ilyn Payne at a burned village and is soundly defeated. The column arrives at Riverrun the next afternoon, and Jaime orders a peace banner sent to the Blackfish for a parley at dawn. His aunt Genna Lannister and her husband Lord Emmon Frey visit; Emmon brandishes his royal decree for Riverrun while Genna offers sharp counsel on the siege, critiques Cersei's governance, and tells Jaime that Tyrion — not he — is truly Tywin's son.

## Events & Actions
1. **Daven's arrival** — Trumpets sound at dusk; Ser Daven Lannister arrives at Jaime's camp with half a dozen knights and twoscore mounted archers and men-at-arms.
2. **Daven reports on the siege** — Daven describes the stalled siege: Ryman Frey's daily charade of threatening to hang Edmure Tully, the Blackfish's defiance and two years of stores, the disorderly Frey camp, and the problems with foraging.
3. **Discussion of Frey marriages and politics** — Jaime and Daven discuss Lancel's decision to join the Faith, Gatehouse Ami, the Frey marriage arrangements, and Desmera Redwyne.
4. **Discussion of Kevan's refusal** — Daven reports that Kevan passed through the siege camp heading west, refused to help, and was cold and brooding.
5. **Report of Edmure's wife's pregnancy** — Daven reveals Roslin Frey is with child; Jaime notes Lord Walder will have no further need of Edmure once a son is born.
6. **Report of Jeyne Westerling inside Riverrun** — Daven explains Lord Gawen Westerling fears for his family held inside the castle.
7. **Siege dispositions described** — Daven lays out the three camps: Frey north of Tumblestone, Emmon and river lords south of Red Fork, Daven's camp between the rivers facing the gates, with a boom downstream.
8. **Report of hanged foragers** — Jaime tells of Addam Marbrand's scouts finding stripped, hanged men with crabapples in their mouths beneath a tree; Strongboar vowed vengeance.
9. **Discussion of Dondarrion and outlaws** — Daven reports Lord Beric is elusive, signal fires surround them, and fires burn in villages for a "new god." Jaime identifies Thoros as with Dondarrion.
10. **Jaime walks the camp** — Jaime dons gold hand and brown cloak; shares hare with crossbowmen; advises a young knight; watches washerwomen jousting in the river; bets a copper star on the blond girl riding Raff the Sweetling and loses.
11. **Midnight practice with Ser Ilyn** — Jaime finds Ser Ilyn honing his greatsword; they ride to a burned village and spar. Jaime briefly has the better of it, then Ser Ilyn drives him into the river, defeats him, and holds his sword point between Jaime's lips.
12. **Arrival at Riverrun** — The next afternoon, Jaime's column sights Riverrun; he observes the three siege camps, the gallows with Edmure, and the castle's banners (Tully and Stark). He notes which river lord banners are present and which are absent.
13. **Jaime recalls his first visit to Riverrun** — As a green squire, sent by Sumner Crakehall; Hoster Tully seated him beside Lysa; he was more interested in Brynden Tully's war stories.
14. **Crossing the ford** — Ser Kennos blows the Horn of Herrock; the column crosses the Red Fork with Kingsguard and royal standards.
15. **Camp ordered** — Jaime directs tent placement, baggage, horse lines, latrines; orders Addam Marbrand to inspect the perimeter.
16. **Peace banner dispatched** — Jaime sends Beardless Jon Bettley with a peace banner and message requesting parley with the Blackfish at first light.
17. **Genna and Emmon visit** — Genna embraces Jaime; she has been pinching his ear since childhood but today kisses his cheeks instead. She expresses condolences for Tywin.
18. **Emmon asserts his lordship** — Emmon brandishes the royal decree and insists the castle not be damaged. Jaime corrects him: Petyr Baelish is Lord Paramount of the Trident, and Riverrun is subject to Harrenhal.
19. **Jaime lies about Cleos's death** — He tells Genna and Emmon that Cleos died heroically fighting outlaws, concealing that the Bloody Mummers stripped his corpse.
20. **Genna and Jaime speak privately** — Genna discusses the political error of granting Riverrun to Emmon instead of Kevan; the threat of Tully heirs; Cersei's arming of the Faith; Cersei's poor council appointments; and the historical danger of the Faith Militant.
21. **Genna tells family stories** — She recalls Tywin smiling at Jaime's birth, his distrust of laughter, and how each brother lived in Tywin's shadow.
22. **Genna's betrothal story** — She tells how Tywin, at age ten, was the only one who dared speak against her betrothal to Emmon Frey, and how she loved him for it.
23. **"Tyrion is Tywin's son, not you"** — Genna tells Jaime that Tyrion is Tywin's true son, saying she told Tywin himself and he would not speak to her for half a year.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Complex and shifting. Darkly humorous about his disability; comfortable among soldiers in the field more than at court; bitter and suspicious about Cersei (dreams of her with Moon Boy; thinks "false as fool's gold"); pity for Edmure on the gallows; contempt for Emmon Frey; frustrated by his diminished fighting ability; fond of his squires Lewys and Garrett; conflicted about his vow to Catelyn versus his oath to the king; shaken by Genna's final declaration about Tyrion.
- **Primary preoccupation:** How to break the siege of Riverrun quickly so he can return to King's Landing; balancing his promise to Catelyn Stark against his duty.
- **Key decisions made:** Plans to treat with the Blackfish personally at dawn, offering generous terms; prefers a negotiated end to avoid taking arms against House Tully; dispatches Beardless Jon Bettley with the peace banner; sets up camp on the rise.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself "the sooner we are done with Riverrun, the sooner I'll be back at Cersei's side" followed immediately by "What Jaime would do then he did not know" — he does not actually want to return to Cersei. Allows himself to think his swordplay is improving before Ser Ilyn proves otherwise. Tells the easy lie about Cleos's death without apparent guilt.
