# AFFC — The Iron Captain I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 19
- **pov_character:** Victarion Greyjoy
- **pov_chapter_number:** The Iron Captain I
- **first_available:** AFFC The Iron Captain I
- **location_primary:** Old Wyk (Nagga's Cradle, the sacred strand)
- **location_secondary:** Moat Cailin (recalled), Fever River (recalled)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Balon Greyjoy's death; after the Damphair's summons to kingsmoot; Victarion has sailed from Fever River with the Iron Fleet
- **time_markers:** Evening tide (fleet arrives); night falls during the feast; "four days and four nights" Asha has been on Old Wyk; Euron exiled "three years"; Balon's first rebellion referenced temporally; "the next day" after receiving Aeron's summons Victarion left Moat Cailin
- **chapter_summary:** Victarion Greyjoy arrives at Old Wyk with the Iron Fleet for the kingsmoot. He orders his ships to seal the bay around Euron's Silence, is blessed by Aeron Damphair, and feasts his supporters. At the feast, Euron Crow's Eye arrives and confronts Aeron and Victarion. After Euron departs, Asha proposes an alliance where Victarion would be king and she his Hand, offering a peace plan for the north. Victarion refuses, and Asha declares she will claim the Seastone Chair herself.

## Events & Actions
1. **Iron Victory arrives at Nagga's Cradle** — Victarion and Nute the Barber sight the sacred shore of Old Wyk and the ribs of Nagga.
2. **Victarion recalls Moat Cailin debates** — His captains urged him to claim the Seastone Chair; he refused until the Damphair's summons came.
3. **Victarion left Moat Cailin** — He gave command to Ralf Kenning and sailed the Iron Fleet from Fever River; lost only one ship.
4. **Fleet enters the bay** — Victarion identifies ships already present: Sea Song, Thunderer, Nightflyer, Silverfin, and the Silence.
5. **Victarion sights the Silence** — Recognition of Euron's presence; his fists clench in rage.
6. **Victarion orders tactical deployment** — Commands Grief and Iron Vengeance to stand between Silence and the sea; rest of fleet to seal the bay.
7. **Victarion goes ashore** — He dons his warhelm, entrusts the mysterious chests to Nute, and rows to the strand.
8. **Aeron Damphair blesses Victarion** — Pours salt water on his brow in the surf; they pray together.
9. **Victarion asks about Euron** — Aeron describes Euron surrounded by godless men and monsters in a tent of cloth-of-gold.
10. **Victarion greeted by supporters** — Men from many houses: Blacktydes, Tawneys, Orkwoods, Stonetrees, Wynches, Goodbrothers, Codds, Volmarks, Sparrs, Humbles, Shepherds, Weavers, Netleys.
11. **Victarion feasts captains** — Half a hundred captains in a sailcloth tent; many promise their voices (Fralegg the Strong, Alvyn Sharp, Hotho Harlaw).
12. **Hotho Harlaw offers his daughter** — Twelve years old, "fair and fertile"; Victarion says he'll look at her once crowned.
13. **Baelor Blacktyde argues for peace** — Calls Balon mad, Aeron madder, Euron maddest; asks if Victarion will end the war; departs unsatisfied.
14. **Entertainment at the feast** — Gorold Goodbrother's sons fight; Will Humble eats his boot; Lenwood Tawney fiddles; Romny Weaver sings; Qarl the Maid and Eldred Codd dance the finger dance; Eldred loses a finger.
15. **Asha arrives** — Seen laughing with Qarl the Maid; kisses Victarion's cheek; calls it her "queensmoot."
16. **Asha and Victarion discuss politics** — She reveals the Drumm and Maron Volmark also plan claims; says she has Harlaws and Tris Botley.
17. **Euron enters the feast tent** — Arrives with twelve followers; silence falls.
18. **Euron confronts Aeron** — Claims to be the godliest man alive, having seen ten thousand gods from Ib to Asshai; Aeron calls them fools and storms out.
19. **Euron taunts Victarion and Asha** — Asks after their mother; Asha accuses him of Balon's murder; Euron denies with his mutes as alibi.
20. **Asha and Euron's followers exchange insults** — Asha mocks Left-Hand Lucas Codd and the Red Oarsman; displays her throwing axe.
21. **Victarion orders Euron out** — Slams fist on table; Euron departs touching his eyepatch; several guests leave after.
22. **Asha proposes alliance with Victarion** — They walk the strand; she offers to be his Hand; proposes peace with the north via Lady Glover (return Deepwood Motte, Torrhen's Square, Moat Cailin for Sea Dragon Point and Stony Shore).
23. **Victarion rejects Asha's plan** — Calls it foolish; says Hotho's daughter will get him the Harlaws; insists he needs no Hand.
24. **Asha warns about Euron's reputation** — Says men at cookfires talk only of the Crow's Eye, his deeds, his burning of Lannisport fleet.
25. **Victarion admits Euron's role in his wife's death** — Euron impregnated his salt wife; Balon made Victarion do the killing; Balon exiled Euron.
26. **Asha departs** — Declares she will claim the Seastone Chair herself.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Old rage toward Euron simmering beneath surface; pride in his fleet and ships; determination tempered by uncertainty about his claim; sexual confusion when briefly seeing Asha differently; grief and shame about his killed wife
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether he can/should claim the Seastone Chair; how to handle Euron's presence; proving his worth against the Crow's Eye
- **Key decisions made:** Orders fleet to seal bay (trapping Silence); rejects Asha's alliance/Hand proposal; rejects her peace plan for the north; commits to claiming the throne with Hotho Harlaw's support
- **Self-deception flags:** Victarion claims "I had no choice" about killing his wife while clearly tormented; insists he was the one who burned Lannisport while Asha notes Euron "hatched the scheme"; believes Lady Glover "plays Asha for a fool" without considering the strategic logic; tells himself the north is winnable despite evidence otherwise
