- **time_markers:** Cersei orders arrests completed "before the sun comes up"; "by the time the sun went down that day" all accused are in custody; Qyburn visits "that night"; "the next day" Cersei visits Margaery; the cell begins to darken by evening; Septa Unella visits every hour through "the longest night"; "day brought no relief" — a full day passes in the cell; "near dawn on the second day" Qyburn arrives
- **chapter_summary:** Cersei stages a public hearing in the throne room where Septa Moelle announces charges against Margaery Tyrell, and Grand Maester Pycelle reveals he provided Margaery with moon tea. Cersei has the accused lovers arrested and visits Margaery in her cell at Baelor's Sept, ostensibly to comfort her but actually to steer her toward a trial by combat she cannot win. Cersei then meets with the High Septon, who reveals that under torture Osney Kettleblack has recanted his confession about Margaery and instead implicated Cersei herself — in both fornication and the murder of the previous High Septon. Cersei is seized by the Faith and imprisoned in a tower cell. Two days later, Qyburn visits with grim news: the council has been reshuffled, Aurane Waters has fled with the fleet, and Cersei dictates a desperate letter to Jaime begging him to champion her.

## Events & Actions
1. **Cersei holds court beneath the Iron Throne** — Septa Moelle arrives with six Warrior's Sons knights to deliver the High Septon's charges against Margaery, Megga, Elinor, and Alla Tyrell.
2. **Charges publicly announced** — Margaery is accused of adultery and high treason; her cousins of lewdness, fornication, and conspiracy. Ser Osney Kettleblack is named as the accuser, having confessed before the altar of the Father.
3. **Lord Merryweather challenges the charges** — Questions what offenses were committed and by whom; later notes that Margaery and Lord Tyrell had sworn oaths attesting to her maidenhood.
4. **Pycelle reveals the moon tea** — Grand Maester Pycelle admits that Margaery asked him to make moon tea "not once, but many times," causing uproar in the throne room.
5. **Court cleared** — Cersei orders the gold cloaks to clear the hall; Ser Osfryd is instructed to note who flees.
6. **Council discussion in the aftermath** — Aurane Waters proposes launching the new dromonds as a show of force and to block Mace Tyrell from crossing the river. Cersei agrees. Harys Swyft fears violence from Lord Tyrell.
7. **Cersei announces she will visit the sept** — She says she will speak with Margaery and the High Septon and suggests a trial may be necessary.
8. **Cersei visits Tommen** — Finds him playing with kittens. She has him sign blank arrest warrants with his seal, then sends him off with Jocelyn Swyft.
9. **Cersei fills in the arrest warrants** — She writes in ten names: Ser Tallad, Jalabhar Xho, Hamish the Harper, Hugh Clifton, Mark Mullendore, Bayard Norcross, Lambert Turnberry, Horas Redwyne, Hobber Redwyne, and Wat the Blue Bard. Gives them to Ser Osfryd.
10. **All accused arrested by sundown** — Hamish the Harper collapses; Ser Tallad wounds three gold cloaks before being taken. Redwyne twins get comfortable chambers; the rest go to the dungeons.
11. **Qyburn reports on Hamish** — Hamish has difficulty breathing and calls for a maester. Cersei withholds the maester until he confesses; Qyburn will "help him remember" details.
12. **Cersei prepares to visit Margaery** — Chooses a humble brown woolen dress. Instructs Taena to speak with the three Tyrell cousins and try to win Alla over.
13. **Journey to the sept** — Cersei takes the litter with ten Lannister guards and Boros Blount. Tells Osmund to stay hidden. En route, Taena raises the possibility of Margaery demanding trial by battle. Cersei explains her trap: only Kingsguard may champion the queen, and the only available knights are Boros Blount and the soon-to-be-ill Meryn Trant.
14. **Cersei discusses Russell Merryweather** — Suggests Taena's son come to court to train with Tommen; Taena defers until things are safer.
15. **Arrival at the Great Sept** — Cersei notes the plaza has been cleaned; a crowd gathers but is not large or unruly. Novice septons guard the doors with quarterstaffs.
16. **Ser Theodan the True escorts Cersei** — A Warrior's Son, formerly Ser Theodan Wells, leads her to the High Sparrow.
17. **Cersei meets the High Septon** — He is praying before the Father's altar; makes her wait. Then gives leave for her to speak with Margaery.
18. **Cersei visits Margaery in her tower cell** — Margaery describes her treatment: stripped, isolated, denied sleep, septas demanding confessions hourly. She is barefoot, in a roughspun shift, hair tangled, feet filthy.
19. **Cersei steers Margaery toward trial by battle** — Mentions that the accused lovers will testify to her innocence at trial, and reminds her that her champion must be a Kingsguard member.
20. **Margaery sees through the trap** — Realizes Cersei has engineered the situation. Calls Cersei a "vile, scheming, evil bitch" and orders her to leave.
21. **Cersei threatens Margaery as she departs** — Mentions Pycelle's moon tea testimony and the Blue Bard, warning Margaery to pray.
22. **Cersei descends underground with four elderly septas** — Goes below the Great Sept into Visenya's Hill, through a torch-lit hallway.
23. **Meeting with the High Septon in the audience chamber** — They agree the Faith should conduct the trial with a sacred court of seven judges (three female: a maiden, a mother, a crone). Both agree any trial by combat champion must be Kingsguard.
24. **Cersei asks for Osney back; the High Septon refuses** — Says "No" — one word that shocks Cersei.
25. **The High Septon shows Cersei Osney Kettleblack** — Osney hangs naked from chains, badly whipped. The High Septon explains they "sought after the truth."
26. **Osney recants** — Under torture, Osney now says he never touched Margaery; instead names Cersei as the queen he bedded and reveals she sent him to murder the previous High Septon with a pillow.
27. **Cersei flees** — She pushes past the High Septon, slams the iron door, fights through the four elderly septas (knocks one down, claws another's face), and runs up the stairs.
28. **Cersei remembers Taena** — Stumbles on the stairs in panic, realizing Taena "knows it all" and could be taken and whipped too.
29. **Cersei is captured in the sept** — Women — septas and silent sisters — seize her at the altar of the Mother. A score of them drag her kicking up the tower steps.
30. **Cersei is stripped and imprisoned** — Three silent sisters hold her down while Septa Scolera strips her bare, even her smallclothes. A roughspun shift is tossed at her. She screams "I am a Lannister," threatens that Jaime will kill them.
31. **Cersei destroys her cell** — Tears the shift into pieces, smashes the ewer and chamber pot against the walls. Pounds on the door and screams at the window. No one comes.
32. **Night of torment** — Septa Unella wakes her every hour demanding confession. Cersei calls it the longest night of her life except Joffrey's wedding night. Cell is freezing; she huddles under the thin brown blanket.
33. **Next day in captivity** — Cersei flings gruel at Septa Moelle; drinks water out of thirst; puts on the mildewed shift; eats bread and fish in the evening; demands wine but gets none.
34. **Mob heard outside** — Three times during the day, distant shouting — calling Margaery's name, not Cersei's.
35. **Qyburn's visit near dawn, second day** — He tells Cersei she will be tried for murder, treason, and fornication. Tommen is safe in Maegor's Holdfast. The Blue Bard is being whipped but holds to his coached testimony. Osfryd removed as commander of the gold cloaks, replaced by Humfrey Waters. Qyburn himself dismissed from the council. Harys Swyft and Pycelle rule; they've invited Kevan Lannister to be regent. Merryweather has fled to Longtable with Taena. Aurane Waters has fled with the entire fleet.
36. **Qyburn offers his champion** — Mentions a champion no man can defeat. Cersei says she cannot use him because her champion must be Kingsguard.
37. **Cersei dictates a letter to Jaime** — "Come at once. Help me. Save me. I need you now as I have never needed you before. I love you. I love you. I love you. Come at once." She insists Jaime will not lose, even without his sword hand.

- **Emotional state:** Triumphant and in control during the hearing; exultant as her scheme unfolds; confident during the visit to Margaery; shock and horror when the High Septon says "No" and shows her the broken Osney; blind panic during her flight; defiant rage when captured; terror and despair during imprisonment; exhaustion and vulnerability during Qyburn's visit; desperate hope when dictating the letter to Jaime
