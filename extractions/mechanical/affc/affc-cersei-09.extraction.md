After a nightmare about Tyrion and Maggy the Frog's prophecy, Cersei breakfasts with Tommen and learns Margaery plans to observe Maiden's Day, then instructs Ser Osney Kettleblack to confess to the High Septon that he bedded Margaery and two of her cousins.

## Events & Actions
1. **Cersei interrogates Grand Maester Pycelle** — She berates him for Lord Gyles Rosby's death, accuses him of incompetence regarding past deaths (Joffrey, Robert, Jon Arryn), and demands to know why he has been visiting Margaery in the Maidenvault.
2. **Pycelle confesses about moon tea** — Under threat of the black cells, Pycelle reveals that Margaery has been requesting moon tea from him.
3. **Cersei seizes on moon tea as proof of adultery** — She concludes Margaery has a lover, which constitutes high treason punishable by death.
4. **Cersei orders Pycelle to claim Rosby's inheritance for Tommen** — She directs Pycelle to assert that Lord Gyles's dying wish was to leave all his lands and wealth to the king, dismissing Rosby's unnamed ward.
5. **Cersei, Taena, and Osmund discuss how to prove Margaery's treason** — They note that Margaery's ladies serve as protection and that no lover has been identified; Cersei speculates some ladies may be complicit.
6. **Cersei plans a supper and summons the Blue Bard** — She arranges for the Merryweathers and the singer to dine with her that evening.
7. **Supper is served** — Ham from a castle sow (no wild boar available), baked apples with cheese. Orton is nervous and drinks heavily.
8. **Cersei names Orton Merryweather as Hand of the King** — Ser Harys Swyft is moved to lord treasurer (replacing the dead Gyles Rosby). Cersei notes Owen Merryweather (Orton's grandsire) once served as Hand to Aerys.
9. **Cersei confronts and arrests the Blue Bard** — She asks to see his lute, then smashes it across his face and accuses him of bedding Margaery. Orton Merryweather has him taken to the dungeons.
10. **Qyburn tortures the Blue Bard through the night** — In the black cells, Qyburn uses a razor and other methods. The Blue Bard initially denies everything, praying and pleading.
11. **The Blue Bard gives a coerced confession** — By dawn he names Ser Tallad the Tall, Lambert Turnberry, Jalabhar Xho, the Redwyne twins, Osney Kettleblack, Hugh Clifton, and Ser Loras as Margaery's lovers, and describes fabricated sexual acts involving Margaery's cousins.
12. **Cersei edits the confession** — She removes Ser Loras (hero of Dragonstone, unbelievable) and the Redwyne twins (needs the Arbor fleet against Euron Crow's Eye) from the list. The Blue Bard adjusts his story accordingly.
13. **Cersei orders the Blue Bard's wounds treated** — She tells Qyburn to clean and dress his wounds and give him milk of the poppy. She promises he can take the black.
14. **Cersei and Taena share a bath** — They discuss which of Margaery's cousins to target; Taena volunteers to turn Alla Tyrell. Cersei considers the political dimensions — they need corroboration beyond the Blue Bard's confession.
15. **Cersei has a nightmare** — She dreams she is chained in the black cells. Tyrion, naked and monkey-like, tears off her nipples with his teeth and says "You shall see them crowned, and you shall see them die."
16. **Cersei reveals Maggy the Frog's prophecy to Taena** — She tells Taena about the valonqar prophecy, Melara Hetherspoon's death, and her fear that Tyrion will kill her. Taena offers comfort, suggesting the maegi was merely a spiteful old woman.
17. **Cersei breakfasts with Tommen** — Tommen talks about his kittens and wanting to learn fighting from Ser Loras. He mentions Margaery must "fast and purify herself" for Maiden's Day.
18. **Cersei realizes the Maiden's Day opportunity** — Margaery intends to observe Maiden's Day at the Great Sept, a rite only virgin maids may attend — an implicit claim of virginity that will make the adultery accusation more damning.
19. **Cersei instructs Ser Osney Kettleblack** — She orders him to go to the High Septon and confess that he bedded Margaery and two of her cousins (Megga and Elinor, not Alla). He is to say Alla wept and begged them to stop.
20. **Cersei sleeps with Osney Kettleblack** — To give his lie "flavor" of truth, and because he insists, Cersei has sex with him. He tears her silk gown and tells her to keep the crown on.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Triumphant and ruthless during interrogations and plotting; physically sickened during the Blue Bard's torture but forces herself to watch; fearful and vulnerable during her nightmare about Tyrion; maternal and protective during breakfast with Tommen; calculating and resigned when she sleeps with Osney. Oscillates between power and fear throughout.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Destroying Margaery Tyrell and her influence over Tommen, while maintaining the Tyrell military alliance. The entire chapter is a single-minded campaign to fabricate evidence of Margaery's adultery.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Interpret moon tea as proof of adultery rather than investigate further; (2) Frame the Blue Bard as Margaery's lover through torture; (3) Edit the coerced confession to remove politically necessary allies (Redwynes, Ser Loras); (4) Name Orton Merryweather as Hand; (5) Claim Rosby's inheritance for the crown; (6) Use Osney Kettleblack to confess to the High Septon; (7) Sleep with Osney to give his lie "flavor."
- **Self-deception flags:** "All I do, I do for Tommen" — repeated like a mantra, used to justify torture and fabrication of evidence. Blames Margaery for "forcing" the Blue Bard's torture: "Her scheming forced this on me. She has soiled me with her treachery." Convinced moon tea proves adultery despite no direct evidence of a lover. Believes she is "getting to the truth" when she is manufacturing it. Invokes her father's example ("Lord Tywin would not have turned away") to steel herself through the torture. Refuses to see parallels between the violence she inflicts and the violence she fears from Tyrion.
