# AFFC — Alayne I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 24
- **pov_character:** Alayne (Sansa Stark)
- **pov_chapter_number:** Alayne I
- **first_available:** AFFC Alayne I
- **location_primary:** The Eyrie
- **location_secondary:** Gates of the Moon, Giant's Lance, Sky, Snow, Stone, Maiden's Tower, Morning Hall, Crescent Chamber, solar
- **approximate_timeline:** After Lysa Arryn's death; the Lords Declarant have gathered at the Gates of the Moon and Young Lord Hunter's levies arrived two days prior; autumn waning toward winter; Alyssa's Tears frozen
- **time_markers:** Rising sun/morning at chapter opening; the climb from the Gates takes most of a day on muleback and several days afoot; lords may talk "late into the night"; feast lasts "till morn"; Alayne goes to Petyr's chambers late at night after the feast
- **chapter_summary:** Alayne wakes in the Eyrie and observes the six Lords Declarant encamped below. Robert Arryn refuses his porridge, claims to hear the dead singer Marillion at night, and suffers a shaking fit. Petyr Baelish instructs Alayne to prepare refreshments and a solar for the parley, coaching her on disguise and dress. The Lords Declarant arrive and demand Robert's custody and Petyr's removal to Harrenhal; Lyn Corbray disrupts the parley by drawing Lady Forlorn, and Petyr exploits the incident to negotiate a one-year agreement to remain as Lord Protector. That night Alayne visits Petyr and realizes Lyn Corbray has been secretly working for him all along.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Cold; wind blowing fiercely at the Eyrie's altitude; cold gusts of wind
- **Season indicators:** "Autumn wanes, and we must gird ourselves for winter" (Lady Waynwood); Alayne thinks "It will be worse when winter has us in its grip"; "Winter will make this place as cold as any tomb"
- **Time of day:** Begins at sunrise; progresses through morning (breakfast), daytime (preparations, lords' climb), into evening (parley, feast), then late night (Alayne visits Petyr)
- **Lighting:** Streaming sunlight through windows at dawn; morning light over the Vale; mountains look like "solid gold" in morning sun; candlelight in the solar (scented beeswax candles); candlelight dancing in Petyr's eyes at night
- **Sounds:** Wind moaning and sighing round the towers; Alyssa's Tears silent (frozen); Robert claims to hear Marillion singing every night; maester's chain clinking softly; the Eyrie described as the quietest castle in the Seven Kingdoms — no horses, no hounds, no knights training, servants keep voices down, footsteps strangely muffled in the pale stone halls
- **Smells:** Solar smells of nutmeg and other costly spices (from candles)
- **Notable sensory details:** Cold stone beneath bare feet on the balcony; Alayne kicked in the belly by Robert's flailing foot during his fit; the wind blows up her legs through her robe; the stillness of the Eyrie noted repeatedly

## Events & Actions
1. **Alayne wakes at sunrise** — Gretchel tends the fire; Alayne goes to her carved white stone balcony in Maiden's Tower and surveys the Vale, the frozen Alyssa's Tears, and the lords' encampment below.
2. **Alayne notes the Lords Declarant's forces** — Six lords with a thousand men each; Young Lord Hunter arrived two days ago; Nestor Royce has only 300 men in the Gates garrison.
3. **Alayne dresses in a Tully-colored gown** — Parti-colored red and blue, from Lysa's wardrobe; re-dyes her hair with Tyroshi wash from the night before.
4. **Breakfast in the Morning Hall** — Robert refuses porridge and demands eggs (unavailable due to the food blockade); Alayne tries to coax him.
5. **Robert claims to hear Marillion singing** — Says he hears it every night despite the singer reportedly being dead; Colemon gave him dreamwine.
6. **Petyr and Maester Colemon arrive** — Petyr informs Robert the lords are climbing; Robert protests and demands they be sent away.
7. **Robert's shaking fit** — Robert flings his porridge bowl (hits Colemon), knocks over milk, falls from his chair, kicks Alayne in the belly; guards in sky-blue cloaks carry him to bed.
8. **Colemon and Petyr discuss Robert's treatment** — Colemon mentions bleeding and dreamwine; references Archmaester Ebrose and mother's milk; Petyr mocks the wet-nurse idea and suggests sweetsleep instead.
9. **Petyr requests a kiss from Alayne** — She gives a quick dry peck on his cheek; he calls it "dutiful" and smiles with his mouth but not his eyes.
10. **Petyr gives Alayne instructions** — Mull red wine with honey and raisins; serve bread and sharp white cheese; change out of Tully colors; avoid sky blue and cream (Arryn colors).
11. **Discussion of Bronze Yohn recognizing Sansa** — Alayne reminds Petyr that Bronze Yohn visited Winterfell and saw Sansa at King's Landing; Petyr dismisses the risk, noting her changed hair and height.
12. **Petyr tells Alayne about Lyn Corbray** — Explains the Corbray brothers' rivalry, Lyn's killing of Prince Lewyn, and how Lyn got Lady Forlorn while Lyonel got the lands.
13. **Alayne prepares the solar** — Myrish carpet, trestle table, eight oak-and-leather chairs (six on one side, two on the other), scented beeswax candles from Lord Waxley.
14. **Alayne chooses her attire carefully** — Rejects purple silk and dark blue velvet; selects modest dark brown lambswool with golden embroidery; plain velvet ribbon rather than Lysa's ostentatious jewels.
15. **Alayne receives the lords in the Crescent Chamber** — Redfort, Waynwood, and Belmore ride the basket up; others climb the chimney; she serves bread, cheese, and mulled wine, identifying each lord by heraldry from Petyr's roll of arms.
16. **Bronze Yohn asks "Do I know you, girl?"** — Alayne freezes; Nestor Royce identifies her as Littlefinger's natural daughter; Lyn Corbray makes a crude remark.
17. **Lords Declarant make lewd comments about Alayne** — Hunter and Corbray make suggestive remarks; Lady Waynwood sharply defends her; Corbray responds with a veiled death threat.
18. **Alayne leads the lords to the solar** — Up marble steps past three murder holes and through a portcullis; Belmore labors; Ser Lothor Brune stands guard; Petyr waits with wine and parchment.
19. **Parley begins — Petyr wrong-foots the lords** — Offers to sign their declaration himself; asks to help root out "false friends and evil counselors."
20. **Lords demand Robert and Petyr's departure** — Want Robert raised at Runestone under Bronze Yohn; want Petyr to go to Harrenhal; cite riverlands chaos as reason.
21. **Petyr deflects with discussion of Harrenhal** — Lists its cursed history: Lady Whent, Amory Lorch (killed by a bear), a sellsword (dismembered by Gregor Clegane), Gregor himself (dying of poisoned spear); jokes about giving it to Lord Frey or Cersei.
22. **Petyr mentions Cersei sending tapestries** — Alayne stiffens at Cersei's name; Petyr hints he may need to "remove her from the game sooner than planned" and speaks of pieces having "wills of their own."
23. **Petyr proposes wards and names Harrold Hardyng** — Mentions Grafton and Lynderly each sending a son; asks Lady Waynwood to send Harrold Hardyng to be Robert's companion.
24. **Lyn Corbray draws Lady Forlorn** — Declares talk makes him ill; draws his smoke-grey Valyrian steel blade; demands Petyr arm himself; threatens Lothor Brune.
25. **Bronze Yohn stops Corbray** — "Are you a Corbray or a Frey? We are guests here"; other lords echo him; Corbray sheathes and departs, calling them "Six Old Women."
26. **Petyr exploits the incident** — Coldly says "You brought him here" and threatens to arrest them all; claims he has more honor than some.
27. **Petyr negotiates a one-year agreement** — Demands the Lords Declarant remove their armies; promises to set the Vale to rights within a year; will step down if he fails; will pardon all, including Lyn Corbray.
28. **Bronze Yohn concedes** — "It is plain that he has won"; warns "Not all of us are fooled"; departs the Eyrie.
29. **Feast held that evening** — Humble fare; Robert appears in cream-and-blue doublet; Bronze Yohn and Lyn Corbray already departed; other lords remain till morning.
30. **Alayne visits Petyr late at night** — Cannot sleep; suspects something; finds Petyr still awake writing a letter.
31. **Petyr reveals his plans** — Predicts Redfort and Waynwood may die of old age; Gilwood Hunter will be murdered by his brothers (likely Harlan, who arranged Lord Eon's death); Belmore can be bought; Templeton will be befriended; Bronze Yohn will be isolated.
32. **Alayne realizes Corbray is Petyr's agent** — Asks how Petyr will reward Corbray's "service"; Petyr confirms, laughing: "With gold and boys and promises."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious and fearful throughout — fears recognition by Bronze Yohn, fears the Lords Declarant's power, fears Cersei, fears for Robert's health and sanity. Momentary awe at the Vale's morning beauty. Dutiful but uncomfortable when Petyr requests a kiss. Proud when she manages the hosting successfully. Growing suspicion about Corbray that crystallizes into certainty by the chapter's end.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Maintaining her identity as Alayne Stone; performing the role of hostess and dutiful bastard daughter; managing Robert; worrying about the political danger the Lords Declarant represent.
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses modest brown dress rather than richer options (correctly reading the social situation); decides not to throw herself at Bronze Yohn's feet for protection (considers it, rejects it: "He never fought for Robb, why should he fight for me?"); visits Petyr late at night to confirm her suspicion about Corbray.
- **Self-deception flags:** Alayne repeatedly thinks of herself as Alayne, but Sansa's memories and identity keep surfacing — she remembers Bronze Yohn at Winterfell, recalls falling in love with Ser Waymar, thinks of "her lady mother" and "her brother" at the Twins, remembers her father's greatsword Ice when she sees Lady Forlorn's dark steel. She tells Robert that Marillion is dead despite not having seen the body herself, accepting Petyr's account. She momentarily forgets her cover age ("four-fourteen" — catching herself).
