# AFFC — Sansa I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 11
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark (operating as Alayne Stone)
- **pov_chapter_number:** Sansa I
- **first_available:** AFFC Sansa I

- **location_secondary:** Gates of the Moon (discussed), Winterfell (recalled), King's Landing (recalled), The Fingers (mentioned), The Twins (mentioned), Riverrun (mentioned), Giant's Lance (the mountain), Wendish Town (from song)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Lysa Arryn's death; Lord Nestor Royce ascends to investigate
- **time_markers:** Nights of Marillion's singing since Lysa's fall; "it had not snowed upon the Eyrie since the day that Lady Lysa fell"; dawn (first light prying at shutters); Lord Nestor's party arrives "in the late afternoon"; the meeting runs until "later, much later, after the flagon of Arbor gold was dry"; Sansa wakes "sometime during the night" when Robert climbs into her bed
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa, posing as Alayne Stone, endures Marillion's constant singing from the sky cells while dreading Lord Nestor Royce's arrival to investigate Lysa Arryn's death. She presents Robert Arryn in the High Hall, where she repeats the lie that Marillion killed Lysa; Marillion himself, blinded and possibly maimed, is brought up and confesses. In a private meeting afterward, Petyr secures Nestor's loyalty by granting him hereditary rights to the Gates of the Moon, then explains his political strategy to Sansa. Petyr insists Sansa must be Alayne at all times, even in her heart, and the chapter ends with young Robert climbing into her bed and asking if she is his mother now.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Wind rising in the late afternoon when Nestor's party arrives; cold thin air; bitter cold nights; no snow since Lysa's fall
- **Season indicators:** The valley is "gold and red" beneath them — autumn colors
- **Time of day:** Transitions from night (Marillion singing) → dawn (first light, "On a Misty Morn") → late afternoon (Nestor's arrival) → evening/night (solar meeting, bed)
- **Lighting:** Fire crackling merrily in the solar; fewer than a dozen of fifty silver sconces lit in the High Hall, creating shadows that "danced upon the floors and pooled in every corner"; firelight in Sansa's hearth at night
- **Sounds:** Marillion's singing echoing off the stony shoulders of the Giant's Lance, floating up winding tower steps, penetrating even through latched shutters; wind moaning outside and scratching at the Moon Door; footsteps echoing off marble in the High Hall; silver cups crashing together in a toast
- **Smells:** Mord's clothes have "a rank, ripe smell"
- **Notable sensory details:** Cold throughout — Sansa shivers in the warm solar; the sky cells have "a wall of empty air"; the High Hall is described as a "pale cold place"; Sansa covers her ears with a goose down pillow to block the singing; she feels the cold thin air

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa recalls childhood memory** — A wandering singer stayed at Winterfell for half a year; she begged Eddard not to let him leave; she prayed for a new singer but none came for a year or more.
2. **Marillion sings through the night from his sky cell** — His voice is strong and sweet, singing of the Dance of the Dragons, Jonquil and her fool, Jenny of Oldstones and the Prince of Dragonflies, betrayals, murders, hanged men, and bloody vengeance.
3. **Sansa begs Petyr to make Marillion stop singing** — Petyr says he gave Marillion his word (to keep his harp and tongue); says Lord Nestor ascends on the morrow.
4. **Petyr coaches Sansa on the plan** — They have reached an agreement with Marillion (through Mord's persuasion); if Marillion deviates, they will say he lies; Lord Nestor will believe them because "our lies will profit him."
5. **Sansa reflects on her dual perception of Petyr** — He is both "Petyr, her protector" and "Littlefinger"; she catalogs times Littlefinger failed to help her (Joffrey's beatings, the mob, forced marriage) but acknowledges he got her out of King's Landing.
6. **Sansa mentally reviews her family's fates** — Winterfell burned, Bran and Rickon believed dead, Robb and Catelyn murdered at the Twins, Tyrion executed (she believes), Edmure captured, Blackfish besieged.
7. **Marillion sings specific songs through the night** — "The Day They Hanged Black Robin," "The Mother's Tears," "The Rains of Castamere," then later "Six Sorrows," "Fallen Leaves," "Alysanne," and at dawn "On a Misty Morn."
8. **Lord Nestor Royce arrives at the Eyrie in late afternoon** — With his son Ser Albar, a dozen knights, and a score of men-at-arms; the valley is gold and red beneath them.
9. **Petyr formally welcomes the visitors** — Wearing black velvet with grey, projecting solemnity; introduces Maester Colemon and Alayne.
10. **Sansa fetches Robert Arryn from his bedchamber** — He has been crying, has not slept, his door was locked (at Sansa's request); she washes his face gently, dresses him in sky-blue cloak and cream tunic with crescent moon brooch.
11. **Robert is presented on his weirwood throne in the High Hall** — Seated with pillows beneath him; the hall is dimly lit, cold, and shadowy.
12. **Robert tells the assembled lords that Marillion killed his mother** — "Marillion hurt my mother. He threw her out the Moon Door."
13. **Ser Marwyn Belmore asks if Robert saw it happen** — Robert says Alayne and his stepfather saw it.
14. **Sansa tells the false account of Lysa's death** — She weeps during the telling; Petyr supports her by putting hands on her shoulders.
15. **Robert has a violent shaking spell** — Arms, legs, teeth chattering; he falls from the throne; Ser Lothor catches him; Robert wets himself; the fit lasts long moments; Petyr orders him taken to bed and bled.
16. **Marillion is brought from the dungeons by Mord** — Bathed and dressed in white and sky-blue; blinded; goes to one knee; confesses to killing Lysa out of jealous love; claims madness seized him when she said she was carrying Petyr's child.
17. **Sansa studies Marillion's hands during confession** — Fat Maddy claimed Mord took three fingers; she cannot confirm through the gloves.
18. **Lord Nestor and the knights call for Marillion's death** — Nestor says "No man lingers long in the sky cells. The blue will call to him."
19. **Visitors are dismissed to the Lower Hall** — Oswell shows them the way.
20. **Private meeting in the solar: Nestor warns Petyr** — Bronze Yohn means to question the singer himself and come in force; Symond Templeton, Lady Waynwood, and others will join him; Bronze Yohn means to remove Petyr as Lord Protector.
21. **Petyr gives Nestor the parchment** — Grants hereditary keeper rights to the Gates of the Moon to House Royce; bears Arryn seal, signed by Petyr; Nestor is moved to tears.
22. **Nestor accepts the grant** — Acknowledges it may be called unseemly; notes the Keeper post was never hereditary; but declares he earned it.
23. **Toast: "To House Royce, Keepers of the Gates of the Moon, now and forever"** — Silver cups crash together.
24. **After Nestor departs, Petyr explains his strategy to Sansa** — The lies and grant were calculated: Nestor wants to believe Lysa valued him; he wants more for his son; making the grant depend on Petyr's signature as Lord Protector ties Nestor's interest to Petyr's survival.
25. **Sansa puzzles out the signature strategy** — If Petyr is removed or killed, Nestor's claim is called into question; Petyr praises her understanding.
26. **Petyr insists Sansa must be Alayne always** — Even in her heart; identifies Oswell and Ser Lothor as knowing her identity; says Kettleblack watches Brune and Brune watches Kettleblack; "Trust no one."
27. **Petyr puts two fingers on Sansa's left breast** — Tells her she must be Alayne "even here, in your heart."
28. **Sansa yields: "I am Alayne, Father"** — Internally thinks "Lies and Arbor gold."
29. **Petyr kisses Sansa's cheek** — Says "With my wits and Cat's beauty, the world will be yours, sweetling."
30. **Sansa goes to bed** — Gretchel has laid a fire and plumped her featherbed; she prays Marillion will not sing with guests in the castle.
31. **Robert climbs into Sansa's bed during the night** — She forgot to tell Lothor to lock him in; she puts her arm around him; he asks "Are you my mother now?"; she says "I suppose I am."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Frightened, exhausted, conflicted, isolated. She shivers even in warm rooms. She has not slept through the night since Lysa's death. She feels she has "no place but here" and "no true friend but Petyr." She is torn between gratitude toward Petyr and awareness that Littlefinger is not her friend. She feels absurdly proud when Petyr praises her political insight, then immediately confused by the pride.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Maintaining the lie about Lysa's death; the dual identity of Sansa/Alayne; constant fear of exposure. Also preoccupied by Marillion's singing, which she cannot escape and which disturbs her despite her efforts to suppress pity.
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees to tell the false tale to Lord Nestor. Accepts (outwardly) Petyr's instruction to be Alayne "even in her heart." Tells Robert she is his mother. Chose to have Robert locked in his room the previous night.
- **Self-deception flags:** "He is so bold. Sansa wished she had his courage" — admiring the man who is manipulating her. "Lies had been all that kept her alive in King's Landing" — normalizing Petyr's lies as survival. "A lie is not so bad if it is kindly meant" — repeated mantra that echoes Petyr's coaching. "He saved Alayne, his daughter, a voice within her whispered. But she was Sansa too..." — she is aware of the identity erosion but cannot resist it. She catalogs Littlefinger's failures but concludes "Littlefinger was only a mask he had to wear" — excusing him.
