- **approximate_timeline:** During Brienne's quest to find Sansa Stark; follows her journey with Septon Meribald, Podrick, and Ser Hyle through the riverlands

- **chapter_summary:** Brienne and her companions cross the mudflats to the Quiet Isle, a septry of penitent brothers under vows of silence. They are hosted by the brothers, who show them the stable where a fierce black stallion called Driftwood is kept. After supper, the Elder Brother reveals privately to Brienne that Sandor Clegane is dead — he found him dying by the Trident and buried him — and that the Hound had Arya Stark with him, not Sansa. The Elder Brother urges Brienne to abandon her quest and go home, but she pours out her story and insists she must continue.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at the shore** — Brienne's group reaches the shore opposite the Quiet Isle; Meribald points out Saltpans across the bay and proposes crossing to the septry for a hot meal before facing Saltpans.
2. **The tide recedes** — The water between the island and shore pulls back, exposing broad brown mudflats dotted with tidal pools glittering in the afternoon sun.
3. **Meribald explains the Quiet Isle** — He tells Podrick that the brothers are penitents under vows of silence; only the Elder Brother and proctors may speak (proctors one day in seven). He corrects Podrick's belief that the silent sisters have no tongues.
4. **Crossing the Path of Faith** — Meribald leads them on a winding, crooked path through the mudflats, probing with his quarterstaff. He walks east toward deep water, then turns south with his back to the septry, zigzagging to avoid quicksand. The crossing covers at least a mile and a half.
5. **Dog catches a crab** — Dog sticks his nose in a tidal pool, gets pinched by a crab, fights it, and trots back with the crab in his jaws.
6. **Arrival on the island** — Three brothers meet them at the broken stones ringing the island's shoreline. Two have wool wound around their faces. Brother Narbert, a proctor, greets Meribald.
7. **Introductions and Narbert's reaction** — Meribald introduces the group. Narbert draws up short upon seeing Brienne is a woman, noting women usually come sick, hurt, or pregnant. Meribald explains Brienne is "hunting for the Hound."
8. **The stable and Driftwood** — They leave their animals in a whitewashed stable. A huge black stallion (Driftwood) at the far end kicks and trumpets. Brother Gillam shows his missing ear — bitten off by the horse. Narbert explains the horse also kicked and broke Brother Rawney's shinbone when they tried to harness him to a plow, and gelding did not improve his temper.
9. **Climbing the hill** — They ascend wooden steps past cowled, silent brothers; one leads milk cows, another works a butter churn; three boys drive sheep. They pass a lichyard.
10. **The gravedigger** — In the lichyard, a brother bigger than Brienne digs a grave; he is visibly lame. He flings soil that splatters the visitors. Narbert chides him. When Dog sniffs him, the gravedigger drops his spade and scratches Dog's ear. Narbert identifies him as a novice.
11. **Brother Clement's death** — Narbert reveals the grave is for Brother Clement, age eight-and-forty, who died of wounds from the Saltpans raid. Raiders cut out Clement's tongue when he wouldn't speak despite his vow of silence.
12. **Arrival at the Hermit's Hole** — They enter a cave-turned-sanctum with driftwood furnishings, woolen carpets, tapestries, and beeswax candles. The Elder Brother greets them warmly, embracing Meribald and patting Dog. His smile fades when told of Brienne's quest.
13. **Cider and conversation** — The Elder Brother serves sweet cider in driftwood cups. He describes how the river and tides push strange things to the isle: silver cups, iron pots, sacks of wool, bolts of silk, helms, swords, and rubies.
14. **Rhaegar's rubies** — Ser Hyle asks if the rubies are Rhaegar's. The Elder Brother says it may be so — six have been found, and they await the seventh.
15. **The river's dead** — Meribald and the Elder Brother discuss corpses washing up — drowned cows, deer, swollen pigs, and human dead. The Elder Brother says their gravedigger knows no rest: rivermen, westermen, northmen, Stark and Lannister, Blackwood and Bracken, Frey and Darry, all buried side by side. Women and children are "the cruelest gifts."
16. **Septon Bennet's death** — The Elder Brother asks Meribald to hear confession, noting raiders killed "old Septon Bennet" and they've had no one to confess to since.
17. **The burning of Saltpans discussed** — Hyle asks if the sept at Saltpans was burned. The Elder Brother says everything burned save the stone castle, which was useless. He treated survivors: a woman raped a dozen times whose breasts were "torn and chewed and eaten." She cursed Ser Quincy Cox more than her attackers. Meribald defends Cox as old with no fighting men left.
18. **Prayer at the sept** — They go to the sept to pray for the souls of Saltpans before supper.
19. **Supper in the common hall** — A communal meal: crusty bread, butter, honey, fish stew with crabs, mussels, and three kinds of fish. Meribald and Hyle drink mead; Brienne and Podrick drink cider. A brother plays the high harp. After the musician eats, Narbert and another proctor read from The Seven-Pointed Star.
20. **Sleeping arrangements** — Podrick and Ser Hyle are sent to the cloisters to share a cell. Brienne insists Podrick go with Hyle: "Beneath their roof, their rules."
21. **Walk to the women's cottages** — The Elder Brother leads Brienne to the east side of the isle with a lantern. He points out where Saltpans' fires would be visible. The cottages are beehive-shaped stone structures, low and windowless.
22. **Elder Brother reveals the truth about Arya** — He tells Brienne that Timeon was truthful but she misunderstood: the Hound had "the other one," Arya Stark, not Sansa. They were at the crossroads inn and heading to Saltpans. Arya may have been among the children slain at Saltpans.
23. **Elder Brother reveals Sandor Clegane is dead** — He found Clegane by the Trident, crying out in pain, begging for mercy. Elder Brother bathed his brow, gave him wine and a poultice, but it was too late. "The Hound died there, in my arms." He buried him under stones and set his helm atop the cairn. Another wayfarer stole the helm — the Saltpans raider is not Clegane.
24. **The Elder Brother's characterization of the Hound** — He describes Sandor as bitter, tormented, a sinner who served without pride, fought without joy, drank to drown pain, was driven by hate, and lived only for the hope of slaying his brother Gregor — hope taken when Oberyn poisoned Gregor.
25. **Driftwood identified as Stranger** — The Elder Brother reveals the stallion is Sandor's warhorse Stranger, renamed Driftwood as he was found beside the river. The horse retains "his former master's nature."
26. **Elder Brother's backstory** — He tells Brienne he was once a knight, a thirdborn son trained for battle. He drank, took women by force, wanted to marry a petty lord's younger daughter but had no land or wealth. He fought at the Battle of the Trident for Rhaegar's side, took arrows through thigh and foot, lost his horse, was struck from behind, and fell into the river. He woke naked on the Quiet Isle, stripped of everything. He spent ten years in silence.
27. **Elder Brother urges Brienne to go home** — He says the Hound is dead, never had Sansa, and the man in the helm will be caught. He names Randyll Tarly hunting from Maidenpool, Walder Frey from the Twins, and a new pious lord at Darry. He appeals to her father's love and grief.
28. **Brienne's outpouring** — She weeps and pours out her story: her father deserves better children; Galladon drowned when she was four and he was eight; Alysanne and Arianne died in the cradle; she is the "freakish one." She recounts betrayals and betrothals, Red Ronnet's rose, Renly dancing with her, the wager for her maidenhead, Renly's wedding, the mêlée at Bitterbridge, the rainbow cloak, the shadow in the king's pavilion, Renly dying in her arms, Lady Catelyn, the Trident voyage, dueling Jaime, the Bloody Mummers, Jaime crying "Sapphires," Jaime in the tub at Harrenhal, biting Vargo Hoat's ear, the bear pit, Jaime leaping down, the ride to King's Landing, Sansa Stark, her vow to Jaime and to Lady Catelyn, Oathkeeper, Duskendale, Maidenpool, Nimble Dick, Crackclaw, the Whispers, the men she killed.
29. **Brienne refuses to quit** — She declares she must find Sansa first, before those who would sell her to the queen. "I promised Jaime. Oathkeeper, he named the sword. I have to try to save her . . . or die in the attempt."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined at the start; shocked by the revelation that Arya (not Sansa) was with the Hound; dully accepting Sandor Clegane's death; deeply moved by the Elder Brother's appeal about her father; overwhelmed with grief and vulnerability during her outpouring — tears in her eyes; ultimately resolute and reaffirmed in her quest despite everything
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding Sansa Stark; absorbing the Elder Brother's revelations about the Hound's death and Arya; wrestling with the Elder Brother's urging to go home vs. her sworn vows
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to abandon her quest despite the Elder Brother's counsel; reaffirms her vow to find Sansa — "I have to try to save her... or die in the attempt"
- **Self-deception flags:** Brienne may be underestimating the implications of the Elder Brother's revelation — if the Hound had Arya, not Sansa, then the trail she has been following never led to Sansa at all, yet she does not reckon with this fully; she calls herself "the freakish one, not fit to be a son or daughter" — self-perception at odds with her demonstrated courage and dedication
