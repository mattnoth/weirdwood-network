# AFFC — Cersei IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 18
- **pov_character:** Cersei Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Cersei IV
- **first_available:** AFFC Cersei IV
- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Baelor's Sept (bells heard), the yard (knights training), King's Landing docks (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the death of the High Septon; Tywin Lannister's funeral has recently occurred; Cersei is ruling as regent and has reshuffled the small council
- **time_markers:** Morning (council waiting), bells ring all day until sunset, "yestermorn" (Osney on his horse), "last night" (Cersei dreamed of Maggy the Frog), "that night" (Cersei summons Taena), "late into the morning" (they stay up drinking), references to ongoing events (sparrows arriving "every day," fleet being built)
- **chapter_summary:** Cersei receives another wrong dwarf head from bounty hunters, then attends a small council meeting where she introduces Qyburn as the new master of whisperers. The council discusses Dorne, the Vale's Lords Declarant, building a new royal fleet funded by deferring debts to the Faith and the Iron Bank of Braavos, the Red Wedding's political fallout, the Golden Company's movements, the military situation against Stannis, White Harbor's loyalty, and Jon Snow's election as Lord Commander. Afterward, Cersei privately instructs Ser Osney Kettleblack to seduce Margaery Tyrell and then go to the Wall to assassinate Jon Snow in exchange for a lordship and Cersei herself. That night, she tasks Taena Merryweather with planting the idea of Osney as Margaery's secret admirer.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Blustery autumn wind when Cersei leaves the council chambers; cold wind rising at night
- **Season indicators:** Autumn explicitly stated ("a blustery autumn wind")
- **Time of day:** Morning through the council session; transitions to afternoon/evening after council; night scene with Taena extends "late into the morning"
- **Lighting:** Not explicitly described; interior scenes in audience chamber, council chambers, and bedchamber
- **Sounds:** Bells of Baelor's Sept ringing a song of mourning throughout the day (pealing louder in the yard); twoscore knights hammering each other with sword and shield in the yard
- **Smells:** Smell of decay from the severed head filling the audience chamber "like some rank rose"; Cersei thinks "It smells like Father" comparing it to Tywin's corpse
- **Notable sensory details:** The severed head is grey-green and crawling with maggots; Gyles Rosby's bloody pink spittle; Pycelle's jeweled chain clinking softly

## Events & Actions
1. **Bounty hunters present wrong head** — Three ragged men bring a severed dwarf head to Cersei in a leather sack, but it has a nose (Tyrion's was cut off). This is the third head brought to her; the last was simply an ugly child.
2. **Cersei dismisses bounty hunters** — She threatens them but lets them go to avoid discouraging future bounty hunters. She gives the head to Ser Meryn Trant and has Ser Osmund escort the men out.
3. **Lady Jocelyn vomits** — Jocelyn retches at the sight/smell of the head; Cersei orders her to clean it up.
4. **Cersei recalls Maggy the Frog** — After Osmund assures her they'll find Tyrion, Cersei remembers dreaming of Maggy the Frog and her prophecies. Melara said a forgotten prophecy couldn't come true.
5. **Qyburn reports on skull preparation** — He has cleaned Gregor Clegane's skull using beetles and prepared an ebony and silver box for it, to send to Prince Doran.
6. **Bells of Baelor's Sept ring** — Mourning bells for the dead High Septon ring across the city all day; Qyburn says they will stop at sunset. The Mountain's screams are referenced as having preceded the bells.
7. **Cersei introduces Qyburn to the council** — She seats him between Merryweather and Rosby; Pycelle objects furiously, citing maester's vows; Cersei counters that the Citadel stripped his chain.
8. **Cersei reflects on her new council** — She has replaced all prior councillors with loyal men: Merryweather as justiciar, Rosby as lord treasurer, Aurane Waters as grand admiral, Ser Harys Swyft as Hand.
9. **Council discusses the sparrows** — Septon Raynard reports as many as two thousand sparrows in the city with more arriving daily. Cersei says the Faith should oppose the red god Stannis worships.
10. **Council discusses High Septon succession** — Candidates are Torbert, Raynard, Ollidor, and Luceon. Cersei decrees the new High Septon must pronounce anathema on Tyrion.
11. **Council discusses the Vale** — Pycelle reads a declaration from Runestone signed by Bronze Yohn Royce, Lady Waynwood, Lords Hunter, Redfort, Belmore, and Symond Templeton. They mean to remove Littlefinger forcibly. Cersei decides not to intervene but instructs Pycelle to warn them no harm must come to Petyr.
12. **Council discusses rebuilding the fleet** — Aurane Waters proposes ten new dromonds. Fewer than a dozen ships survived the Blackwater.
13. **Council discusses potential alliance with ironmen** — Merryweather suggests using the ironmen against Stannis. Pycelle notes they want the north, promised to Bolton. Council tries to remember Balon Greyjoy's sons. Cersei rejects the ironborn alliance.
14. **Cersei defers debts to fund fleet** — She decides to defer repayment to the Holy Faith and the Iron Bank of Braavos until war's end. Pycelle warns about the Iron Bank. Cersei dismisses his concerns.
15. **Council discusses Lord Frey's claims** — Qyburn warns that people in the city blame the crown for the Red Wedding. Cersei suggests waiting for Lord Walder to die, then his heirs can sacrifice inconvenient relatives as culprits.
16. **Council discusses the Golden Company** — Aurane Waters reports the Golden Company has broken its contract with Myr. Rumors say Stannis hired them. Qyburn's intelligence from a Myrish galley says they are heading to Volantis, not Westeros.
17. **Cersei summarizes the military situation** — Tyrell has Storm's End invested; Riverrun besieged by Freys and Daven Lannister; Redwyne's ships moving up the coast past the Straits of Tarth; only fishing boats on Dragonstone.
18. **Council discusses the northern situation** — Bolton's bastard son clearing ironmen from Moat Cailin with Umber, Ryswell and others. White Harbor on the point of joining — Manderly to marry granddaughters to Freys and open his port.
19. **Manderly's condition for submission** — He insists his heir be returned first; the heir was taken captive by Gregor Clegane at Harrenhal and may be dead.
20. **Davos Seaworth captured** — Stannis sent his onion smuggler to White Harbor. Manderly clapped him in a cell. Cersei instructs Manderly to execute him immediately, to bind White Harbor to the crown's side.
21. **Council discusses Jon Snow** — Pycelle reminds them the Watch takes no part. Cersei says Jon Snow has given Stannis food, shelter, lands, and castles while claiming neutrality.
22. **Qyburn proposes assassination plan** — Send a hundred men to the Wall ostensibly to take the black, but actually to remove Jon Snow from command. Cersei is delighted.
23. **Aurane Waters mentions dragons** — Sailors from the east speak of dragons. Cersei dismisses it entirely.
24. **Cersei returns to her apartments** — Finds Taena, Jocelyn, and Dorcas laughing about the Redwyne twins' infatuation with Margaery.
25. **Cersei summons Ser Osney Kettleblack** — Dorcas blushes when sent to fetch him (she fancies him).
26. **Cersei propositions Osney** — She offers him a white cloak, seduces him with touches and promises, reveals they have slept together once before.
27. **Cersei orders Osney to seduce Margaery** — "Pluck the little rose for me." She frames it as: he beds Margaery, gets caught, gets sent to the Wall.
28. **Cersei orders Osney to kill Jon Snow** — At the Wall, with a hundred men, he kills Jon Snow and receives a royal pardon and a lordship; Cersei implies she will take him as consort.
29. **Osney agrees** — After hesitation about the Wall, he agrees to the plan.
30. **Cersei plots the discovery** — She plans for Ser Osmund to be the one to discover Osney with Margaery, protecting the other Kettleblacks' loyalty.
31. **Cersei reflects on her triumph** — She thinks if her father could see her, he would not be so quick to speak of marrying her off. She lists the dead: Tywin, Robert, Jon Arryn, Ned Stark, Renly. Only Tyrion remains.
32. **Cersei sends Taena to Margaery** — She instructs Taena to tell Margaery she has a secret admirer, a knight smitten with her beauty. Taena must make Margaery worm out the name Osney.
33. **Cersei instructs Taena about candles** — She tells Taena to mention Cersei has sent seven beeswax candles to Baelor's Sept for the High Septon, expecting Margaery will send seventy-seven to outdo her.
34. **Taena reveals her past lover** — Drunk on Arbor gold, Taena tells Cersei about a Myrish sea captain, half a pirate, with black hair and a scar from chin to ear.
35. **Cersei lies about Robert** — When Taena asks if Cersei has known such a dangerous man, Cersei says "Robert" while thinking of Jaime.
36. **Cersei dreams** — She dreams of Tyrion's head being brought in a sack, having it bronzed, and keeping it in her chamber pot.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Confident, scheming, contemptuous. Cersei feels she was made for ruling and plotting. She oscillates between irritation (at the wrong dwarf heads, at Pycelle, at her council's timidity) and self-congratulatory pleasure (at the elegance of her Margaery/Jon Snow plot). Underlying anxiety about Tyrion and Maggy the Frog's prophecy surfaces briefly.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding and killing Tyrion; neutralizing Margaery Tyrell; consolidating power as regent; proving herself a capable ruler equal to or better than her father.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Defer debts to the Faith and Iron Bank to fund a new fleet. (2) Do not intervene in the Vale dispute. (3) Order Manderly to execute Davos Seaworth. (4) Send 100 men to the Wall to assassinate Jon Snow. (5) Task Osney Kettleblack with seducing Margaery to frame her for adultery. (6) Use Taena Merryweather to plant the idea of Osney as Margaery's admirer.
- **Self-deception flags:** Cersei believes Qyburn is as capable as Varys ("It was the silver all along, not the Spider"); she believes her council of sycophants is superior to the old one; she dismisses the Iron Bank's threat; she dismisses the dragon rumors entirely; she thinks her multi-step Margaery/Jon Snow plot is elegant when it depends on many unreliable people; she holds Ser Harys Swyft as a "hostage" for Kevan's compliance, calling it a flimsy shield but believing it sufficient; she sees herself as a mastermind when her father could see the flaws.
