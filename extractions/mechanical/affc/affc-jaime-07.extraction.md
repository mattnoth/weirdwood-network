# AFFC — Jaime VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 45
- **pov_character:** Jaime Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jaime VII
- **first_available:** AFFC Jaime VII
- **location_primary:** Riverrun
- **location_secondary:** Tumblestone (river crossing to Frey camp), the road west (riding with escort briefly)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the surrender of Riverrun; spans several days as the Frey host departs, the garrison is released, and the escort west is organized; ends with the first snowfall in the riverlands
- **time_markers:** "had not slept since Riverrun had opened its gates"; "the next morning before Jaime had been informed"; "a bright autumn day"; "On the morrow, he would start west"; Edmure "waited most of the day before hauling down the direwolf"; "That night he and Ser Ilyn fought for three hours"; "The next day Ser Dermot returned"; "went out again the next morning"; "That same afternoon"; "The next day he departed" (Strongboar); "The Tully garrison departed the next morning"; "More days passed"; "That night he dreamt"; "When morning broke the snow was ankle deep"; "a fortnight" (Tom had been with Ryman); first snowfall in the riverlands
- **chapter_summary:** Jaime consolidates control of Riverrun after its surrender, interrogating Edmure Tully about the Blackfish's escape through the Water Gate. He interviews Jeyne Westerling and Lady Sybell about Robb Stark's potential heir and the promised Westerling marriages. He learns from Edwyn Frey and Walder Rivers that Ryman Frey has been hanged by outlaws near Fairmarket. Over several days, he dispatches the escort west with Edmure and the Westerlings, releases the Tully garrison, encounters a singer called Tom of Sevenstreams, and dreams of a woman who appears to be his mother Joanna Lannister. He wakes to find the first snow falling on Riverrun, and receives a desperate letter from Cersei begging him to come to King's Landing — he orders it burned.

## Events & Actions
1. **Emmon Frey rages about the Blackfish's escape** — demands Edmure's head; Genna calms him
2. **Jaime interrogates Edmure Tully** — Edmure reveals the Blackfish escaped by swimming under the partially raised Water Gate portcullis at night
3. **Jaime threatens Edmure with oubliettes** — threatens to imprison Edmure and his wife in tight oubliettes beneath Casterly Rock; Edmure relents and reveals the escape method
4. **Jaime reflects on the search for the Blackfish** — Ser Addam Marbrand searches south, Ser Dermot north; riverlords deemed untrustworthy for the search
5. **Emmon and Genna depart the solar** — Genna tells Emmon to hold Riverrun or put it to the torch
6. **Edmure speaks of his father's solar** — recalls Hoster Tully ruling from this room, Catelyn reading to him, building a castle of blocks with Littlefinger; tells Jaime he despises him
7. **Edmure is returned to his tower** — Jaime calls a guard and has him taken back and fed
8. **Jaime interviews Jeyne Westerling and Lady Sybell** — Lady Sybell reveals the scab on Jeyne's forehead came from fighting over Robb's crown; Sybell confirms Jeyne is not pregnant, stating she "made certain of that" as Tywin ordered
9. **Jaime dismisses Jeyne** — she flees sobbing down the stairs
10. **Jaime negotiates with Lady Sybell** — discusses promised marriages for Jeyne (must wait two years), Raynald's potential betrothal to Joy; insults Lady Sybell and dismisses her
11. **The escort departs with Edmure and the Westerlings** — four hundred men (doubled at last moment); Jaime rides a few miles with them to instruct Ser Forley Prester
12. **Jaime orders archers to watch Jeyne Westerling** — warns she is "twice as dangerous as Edmure" if she escaped
13. **Jaime passes the Westerling column** — Lord Gawen nods, Lady Sybell looks through him, Jeyne rides huddled beneath her cloak in torn clothes
14. **Jaime visits Edwyn Frey and Walder Rivers** — learns Ryman Frey and his entire party were hanged by outlaws two leagues south of Fairmarket
15. **Edwyn accuses Black Walder of complicity** — suspects his brother leaked information to the outlaws; Walder Rivers notes Black Walder is at Seagard
16. **Jaime demands the Red Wedding captives** — tells Edwyn to inform Lord Walder that King Tommen requires all prisoners
17. **Jaime asks about Raynald Westerling** — learns Raynald freed Grey Wind at the Red Wedding, took crossbow bolts, and threw himself into the Green Fork; likely dead
18. **Jaime delivers a veiled threat to the Freys** — "I've heard the same is true of hanged men"
19. **Frey camp breaks up** — the remaining Freys strike camp
20. **Jaime orders siege equipment and gallows burned** — rejects Daven's suggestion to drag them to Raventree; says he'll deal with Lord Tytos himself without a siege tower
21. **Jaime and Ser Ilyn practice swords** — three hours; one of his better nights; "Payne only would have killed him twice"
22. **Jaime and Ser Ilyn drink wine and talk** — Jaime muses about Cersei, Osmund Kettleblack, the historical precedents in the White Book; Ser Ilyn draws a finger across his throat suggesting killing Cersei
23. **Ser Dermot returns reporting wolves** — a massive wolf pack led by a monstrous she-wolf (possibly a direwolf) killed two sentries and horses; Jaime wonders if it's the same beast that mauled Joffrey
24. **Riverlords depart** — Lord Piper asks about his son Marq; Lord Karyl Vance advises Jaime to go to Raventree personally
25. **Strongboar departs to hunt outlaws** — wants the Hound's head or Beric Dondarrion; Beardless Jon Bettley goes with him
26. **Tully garrison released** — stripped of arms and armor, given three days' food, sworn oaths never to take arms against Emmon or House Lannister
27. **Ser Desmond Grell and Ser Robin Ryger choose the black** — refuse to leave; Jaime assigns Gregor Clegane's men under Rafford to escort them to Maidenpool
28. **Emmon delivers a three-hour speech to Riverrun** — waves his parchment; light rain falls; sullen silence from the assembled people
29. **Jaime encounters Tom of Sevenstreams** — the singer asks to stay for winter; formerly with Ryman for a fortnight; wants to replace Whitesmile Wat
30. **Jaime dreams of the Great Sept of Baelor** — sees a woman in grey (silent sister's garb) with green eyes and spun gold hair who speaks of Tywin; Jaime has only one hand in the dream; she weeps and turns away
31. **Jaime wakes to snow** — finds snow drifting through the open window; the yard is covered in white; the first snow in the riverlands
32. **Snowball fight in the yard** — squires and pages play; Jaime reflects he needs two hands to make a snowball
33. **Maester Vyman delivers a letter from King's Landing** — not a white raven from the Citadel as Jaime expected; Cersei's desperate plea to come at once, with Qyburn's terse cover
34. **Jaime orders the letter burned** — reads it in the window seat, refuses to answer, tells Peck to put it in the fire
