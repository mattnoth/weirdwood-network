# AFFC — Brienne VIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 43
- **pov_character:** Brienne
- **pov_chapter_number:** Brienne VIII
- **first_available:** AFFC Brienne VIII
- **location_primary:** Brotherhood Without Banners cave hideout
- **location_secondary:** Wooded countryside (traveling), surface woods near the cave (hanging site)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Brienne's fight with Biter at the crossroads inn; she has been feverish for days in transit
- **time_markers:** Red afternoon light (first waking); "just yesterday your flesh felt as if it were on fire" (Thoros); "she never sleeps herself" (about Stoneheart); morning at the end — "shafts of pale dawn light were slanting through the trees"; Catelyn was "three days dead" when found by the river (Thoros's account); the rain had stopped; multiple cycles of consciousness/unconsciousness implying days of travel
- **chapter_summary:** Brienne, wounded and feverish from her fight with Biter, is transported as a captive by the Brotherhood Without Banners. She drifts in and out of consciousness with vivid fever dreams. She learns from Jeyne Heddle that Gendry killed Biter, and from Thoros of Myr that she is being held for judgment. Brought before Lady Stoneheart — the resurrected Catelyn Stark — Brienne is accused of serving the Lannisters. Stoneheart demands she kill Jaime Lannister or be hanged; Brienne refuses to choose. Stoneheart orders all three captives hanged, and as the nooses tighten, Brienne screams a word.

## Events & Actions
1. **Brienne transported as captive** — Bound, feverish, facedown on a horse; she drifts in and out of consciousness with broken bones and bitten face.
2. **Fever dream: Harrenhal bear pit** — Brienne dreams of fighting Biter in the bear pit; Renly, Nimble Dick, Catelyn Stark, and dead outlaws watch; Biter tears a chunk from her face; she screams for Jaime.
3. **First waking** — Under a chestnut tree; Jeyne Heddle gives her wine and tells her Biter is dead, killed by Gendry with a spear through the back of the neck.
4. **Gendry appears** — Tells Brienne she must stand before "m'lady" for her crimes; explains Stoneheart.
5. **Brienne asks about companions** — Learns Podrick, Hyle Hunt, and Septon Meribald were also taken; Gendry says Brienne killed the dog.
6. **Fever dream: The Whispers** — Clarence Crabb charges her on an aurochs; she has no sword; her head is swept off.
7. **Fever dream: boat on foggy river** — Hooded men paddle her across; willows whisper "Beauty," reeds say "freak."
8. **Second feeding** — Jeyne gives hot onion broth; Brienne chokes on a carrot.
9. **Confrontation with "the Hound"** — A man in the Hound's helm and yellow cloak threatens her; says Stoneheart wants her hanged.
10. **Brienne invokes guest right** — Claims bread and salt from the inn; Jeyne dismisses it.
11. **Hooded travel** — A leather hood with no eyeholes is placed over Brienne's face; she is transported further.
12. **Fever dream: Evenfall** — Brienne, age twelve, waits for her betrothed; bites off her own tongue; the young knight mocks her; his griffin cloak changes to lions (becomes Jaime).
13. **Brienne wakes in cave** — Finds herself in a small cave, stripped of armor, arm splinted, face poulticed.
14. **Conversation with Thoros** — Thoros identifies himself; explains the Brotherhood is broken without Beric; a "grimmer shadow" leads them; says "The Hound is dead and buried."
15. **Thoros explains Lem's failure** — Lem was supposed to guard the crossroads inn but was lured away chasing the "Mad Dog of Saltpans"; the Bloody Mummers doubled back.
16. **Thoros feeds Brienne** — Brings bread, cheese, and cold stew; they discuss justice and mercy.
17. **Thoros reveals Septon Meribald was freed** — Says the septon was harmless and released; Podrick and Hyle await judgment.
18. **Brienne brought before Stoneheart** — Led through twisting passage to large cavern full of outlaws, women, children; Lady Stoneheart sits behind trestle table holding Robb's crown.
19. **Evidence presented** — The northman presents Oathkeeper; Thoros presents the royal letter with Tommen's seal.
20. **Brienne accused** — Outlaws call her "the Kingslayer's whore"; the golden lion pommel and royal letter are taken as proof of Lannister allegiance.
21. **Podrick and Hyle Hunt brought forward** — Both are captives; Podrick identified as "the Imp's squire"; Hyle as Randyll Tarly's household knight.
22. **Stoneheart reveals her identity** — Lowers hood, unwraps scarf, revealing her undead face: throat slashed, flesh rotting, hair white as bone.
23. **Thoros explains Stoneheart's resurrection** — Catelyn was three days dead when found; Harwin begged for the kiss of life; Thoros refused; Lord Beric gave his own life to resurrect her.
24. **Stoneheart's ultimatum** — Take the sword and slay the Kingslayer, or be hanged as a betrayer.
25. **Brienne refuses** — Says "I will not make that choice."
26. **Stoneheart orders hanging** — Croaks "Hang them."
27. **Hanging scene** — Brienne, Hyle, and Podrick are led outside to trees; nooses placed; Hyle shouts he'll kill Jaime; Lem cuffs him silent.
28. **Brienne offers ransom** — Offers sapphires and silver from Tarth for Podrick's life; Lem refuses.
29. **Hanging begins** — Ropes pulled; Podrick kicks and chokes; Brienne is being strangled.
30. **Brienne screams a word** — As Podrick is dying, Brienne opens her mouth and screams a single word (unspecified).

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terror, pain, grief, loyalty torn between Jaime and Catelyn. Deep fear of hanging and of failing those she swore to protect. Love for Renly (in dreams). Desperate concern for Podrick. Shame about her appearance (even undead Catelyn's face was once "strong and handsome").
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival; protecting Podrick and Hyle; reconciling her oath to Catelyn with her faith in Jaime's redemption; finding Sansa (even in delirium she repeats her quest).
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to choose between killing Jaime and being hanged ("I will not make that choice"). Screams a word at the very end (nature of word unspecified but implies she changes her mind or acts).
- **Self-deception flags:** None clear — Brienne is remarkably honest with herself throughout. She acknowledges her ugly appearance, her love for Renly, and her faith in Jaime without self-deception.
