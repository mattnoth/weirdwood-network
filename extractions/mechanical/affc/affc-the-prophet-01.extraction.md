# AFFC — The Prophet (Aeron I)

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 2
- **pov_character:** Aeron Greyjoy
- **pov_chapter_number:** Aeron I (styled "The Prophet")
- **first_available:** AFFC The Prophet
- **location_primary:** Great Wyk (shore, then Hammerhorn, then Pebbleton)
- **location_secondary:** Pyke (referenced), Lordsport (referenced), Ten Towers / Harlaw (referenced), Old Wyk / Nagga's hill (referenced), Hammerhorn, Hardstone Hills
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Balon Greyjoy's death; Aeron saw Balon not a moon's turn past; Euron sailed into Lordsport the day after the king's death
- **time_markers:** "not a moon's turn past" since Aeron last saw Balon; "two years ago" since Balon sent Euron away; "long after dark" when Aeron reaches Hammerhorn; crescent moon visible; "dawn was still an hour off" when Aeron and Greydon depart Hammerhorn; sun lightening the hills during ride with Greydon; Aeron breaks fast the next morning at Pebbleton; "when the moon has drowned and come again" is the timeframe for the kingsmoot
- **chapter_summary:** Aeron Greyjoy is performing drowning rituals on the shore of Great Wyk when riders bring news of King Balon's death. He rides to Hammerhorn to confer with Lord Gorold Goodbrother, where he learns that Euron Crow's Eye has claimed the Seastone Chair. Aeron rejects Euron's claim, declaring no godless man may sit the Seastone Chair. After a night of prayer in the sea at Pebbleton, he calls for a kingsmoot on Old Wyk at Nagga's hill, invoking ancient ironborn tradition to choose a worthy king.

## Events & Actions
1. **Drowning ritual on Great Wyk** — Aeron drowns men in the surf as a religious rite. Three go willingly; the fourth, Emmond, struggles and must be held under by four drowned men.
2. **Emmond revived** — Norjen works Emmond's arms while Rus pumps his chest. Aeron gives the kiss of life repeatedly until Emmond coughs up seawater and revives. Emmond recites the words and receives a driftwood cudgel.
3. **Three horsemen arrive** — The Sparr, Steffarion Sparr, and Gormond Goodbrother arrive on horseback during the drowning ceremony.
4. **News of Balon's death** — Gormond tells Aeron that King Balon is dead, having fallen from a bridge at Pyke during a storm.
5. **Aeron's theological interpretation** — Aeron declares the Storm God cast Balon down and that Balon now feasts in the Drowned God's watery halls.
6. **Aeron takes Gormond's horse** — Aeron demands and receives Gormond's horse to ride to Hammerhorn, ordering his drowned men to meet him at Pebbleton.
7. **Ride to Hammerhorn** — Aeron rides through hills, woods, and stony defiles on a rough inland track. Arrives long after dark.
8. **Aeron remembers his brothers** — During the ride, Aeron reflects on all nine sons of Quellon Greyjoy, their mothers, and their fates.
9. **Meeting at Hammerhorn** — Aeron meets Gorold Goodbrother and Maester Murenmure. Demands the maester leave; Gorold refuses; Aeron moves to depart.
10. **Euron's seizure revealed** — Maester Murenmure reveals Euron Crow's Eye sits the Seastone Chair, having sailed into Lordsport the day after Balon's death.
11. **Succession debate** — Maester Murenmure argues Theon is rightful heir (or Asha if Theon is dead) by law. Aeron dismisses "green land law," declares no godless man or woman may rule the ironborn.
12. **Sawane Botley's drowning reported** — Gorold reveals Euron had Sawane Botley drowned for asserting Theon's claim. Aeron notes no blood was shed.
13. **Aeron counsels silence** — Tells Gorold to send neither homage nor defiance to Pyke; he must pray on it.
14. **Aeron declines hospitality** — Refuses to sleep at Hammerhorn; requests a fresh horse.
15. **Night ride with Greydon** — Departs before dawn with Greydon Goodbrother, who shows him the shortest route through the hills to the sea.
16. **Memory of Urri** — Aeron is startled awake by the phantom sound of a rusted hinge. He remembers Urri's death from the finger dance accident, the green-land maester's failed treatment, and Balon's retribution.
17. **Memory of his former self** — Aeron recalls his dissolute youth: drinking, singing, juggling, pissing contests, the bet with his longship Golden Storm.
18. **Memory of capture** — Golden Storm sunk at Fair Isle during Balon's first rebellion; Aeron captured, imprisoned at Casterly Rock.
19. **Greydon departs** — At sunrise, Greydon leaves to carry news to Goodbrother cousins at Downdelving, Crow Spike Keep, and Corpse Lake.
20. **Aeron preaches across Great Wyk** — In every village and lord's yard, Aeron announces Balon's death and that a king will rise. A dozen followers join him.
21. **Arrival at Pebbleton** — Aeron reaches Pebbleton where twoscore drowned men await. He drowns his newest followers.
22. **Night prayer in the sea** — Aeron walks naked into the icy sea at night, praying for guidance on the succession. He finds his answer "in his bones."
23. **Meeting with the Merlyn** — Lord Meldred Merlyn descends from his towerhouse to consult Aeron. Reports receiving ravens from both Pyke (Euron) and Ten Towers (Asha/the Reader).
24. **Aeron blesses the Merlyn** — Pours seawater on Merlyn's bald head, blesses him.
25. **Kingsmoot declared** — Standing on a boulder in the surf, Aeron calls for a kingsmoot at Old Wyk on Nagga's hill, invoking ancient ironborn precedent. His drowned men take up the cry.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief and shock at Balon's death ("Those four small words had knocked one down"); dread of Euron ("I have seen the storm, and its name is Euron Crow's Eye"); shame over his dissolute youth and Urri's death; religious fervor and growing prophetic certainty; restlessness and agitation before his night prayer; calm and conviction after emerging from the sea
- **Primary preoccupation:** The succession crisis — who should sit the Seastone Chair. Aeron fixates on preventing Euron's rule and dismisses both Asha (woman) and Theon (weakling). He sees Victarion as the only viable godly candidate but fears Victarion's deference to elder-brother tradition.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to accept Euron's claim; counsels Gorold to send silence rather than homage or defiance; after night prayer, resolves to call a kingsmoot at Old Wyk — a radical break with recent tradition to invoke ancient ironborn precedent.
- **Self-deception flags:** Aeron frames his political opposition to Euron in purely theological terms ("no godless man may sit the Seastone Chair") but his dread of Euron appears deeply personal — connected to childhood trauma suggested by the "scream of a rusted iron hinge" and "Euron has come again." He presents the kingsmoot as divinely inspired ("the god has shown me the way") but it is transparently a political maneuver to block Euron's claim by bypassing eldest-brother succession.

### Religions & Faiths
- Drowned God — deity of the ironborn; "drowned for us"; watery halls; blesses with salt, stone, steel; "what is dead may never die, but rises again, harder and stronger"; drowning/revival as baptism; seawater as holy water; priests called "drowned men"
- Storm God — enemy of the Drowned God; associated with storms, woe, grief; his creatures include ravens; "sea and sky had been at war" for a thousand thousand years; cast Balon down
- Faith of the Maesters (implicit) — Aeron's contempt for maesters as "chain-neck thralls" who follow green-land ways

### Cultures & Peoples
- Ironborn — sea-faring culture; "sons of the sea"; the Old Way; salt wives; finger dance; reaving; longships; driftwood crowns; kingsmoots; "we came from the sea"; contempt for "green lands" and their customs
- Green-landers — mainland Westerosi as seen by the ironborn; associated with maesters, soft customs, different laws of succession

### Other
- Finger dance — ironborn game/contest involving throwing axes; dangerous; Urri lost his hand to it; Aeron "never again" danced it after
- Kingsmoot — ancient ironborn custom of choosing a king by assembly; not held in a very long time; Aeron calls for one at Old Wyk
- Salt wives — women taken as prizes in raiding; Balon took his first two at the Stepstones
- The Old Way — ironborn traditionalist philosophy; reaving, paying the iron price, choosing kings by kingsmoot
- "What is dead may never die, but rises again, harder and stronger" — central creed of the Drowned God faith
