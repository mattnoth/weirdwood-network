# AFFC — Jaime I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 9
- **pov_character:** Jaime Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jaime I
- **first_available:** AFFC Jaime I
- **location_primary:** Great Sept of Baelor, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Hall of Lamps, plaza outside the sept, Red Keep (recalled), tunnels beneath the Red Keep (recalled), dungeons/black cells (recalled), Red Keep yard (recalled)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Tywin Lannister's murder by Tyrion; Jaime stands vigil over the corpse from dusk through dawn
- **time_markers:** Dusk (opening); sunset; midnight (septons enter for devotions); "the hour of the wolf" (Cersei's visit); dawn (morning light, rainbows through dome glass); Jaime has not slept since his father was alive; Jaime was fifteen years old at his knighting vigil; Tommen is eight years old; Rennifer Longwaters has served twelve years; Rugen held his appointment from King Aerys; seven days and seven nights vigil tradition
- **chapter_summary:** Jaime Lannister stands vigil over his father Lord Tywin's decomposing corpse in the Great Sept of Baelor, from dusk through dawn. Throughout the night he recalls freeing Tyrion from his cell by threatening Varys, Tyrion's parting accusation that Cersei has been sleeping with Lancel and Osmund Kettleblack, the fruitless search for Varys in the tunnels beneath the Red Keep, questioning the chief undergaoler Rennifer Longwaters, and confronting the Kettleblacks over their murder of two turnkeys on Cersei's orders. At the hour of the wolf Cersei arrives disguised and asks Jaime to serve as Hand of the King after Kevan Lannister's refusal; Jaime declines. At dawn, Tommen is overcome by the stench of the corpse and flees the sept; Jaime comforts him and then advises Cersei to send Mace Tyrell to take Storm's End.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jaime stands vigil at dusk** — Ser Jaime Lannister stands in white armor beside Lord Tywin's bier in the Great Sept of Baelor, holding a golden greatsword in his remaining hand.
2. **Swann and Loras offer relief** — Ser Balon Swann and Ser Loras Tyrell offer to relieve Jaime; he refuses and sends them away.
3. **Jaime recalls threatening Varys** — He remembers waiting in Varys's chambers, ambushing the eunuch, holding a dagger under his chin, and demanding help freeing Tyrion from his cell.
4. **Jaime recalls Tyrion's parting words** — Tyrion told Jaime that Cersei has been sleeping with Lancel, Osmund Kettleblack, and possibly Moon Boy, and claimed to have killed Joffrey.
5. **Jaime reflects on Varys's disappearance** — Varys has not returned to his chambers; a search of the Red Keep found nothing; he may have fled by ship with Tyrion.
6. **Jaime recalls searching the tunnels** — He led a dozen guards with torches into the passages beneath the Red Keep, struggling with ladders and crawl spaces due to his missing hand; found only darkness, dust, rats, and a chamber with an iron dragon brazier and a Targaryen mosaic.
7. **Jaime recalls Rhaegar's farewell** — On a windy day in the Red Keep yard, Rhaegar refused Jaime's request to ride to battle, explaining Aerys wanted Jaime close for protection. Rhaegar promised to call a council and make changes when he returned. He rode to the Trident and died.
8. **A thousand mourners visit the bier** — Lords, ladies, and smallfolk file past Lord Tywin throughout the day.
9. **Pycelle speaks to Jaime** — The Grand Maester praises Tywin as the greatest man he ever knew, compares him to Quenton Hightower who did what was needed during the grey plague in Oldtown. Pycelle is old, feeble, and tottering.
10. **Jaime recalls questioning Longwaters** — Rennifer Longwaters, chief undergaoler, tells Jaime about the missing undergaoler Rugen (who held his appointment from King Aerys, was seldom present, had charge of the black cells). Longwaters claims descent from a princess of the Maidenvault and Lord Oakenfist.
11. **Longwaters reveals dungeon staffing** — The crown pays for twenty turnkeys but has only twelve; six undergaolers budgeted but only three serve; only six prisoners currently held.
12. **Jaime learns the two turnkeys are dead** — Longwaters reveals the turnkeys cannot be questioned because they are dead — killed by the Kingsguard.
13. **Jaime recalls confronting Blount and Kettleblack** — Jaime finds the murdered turnkeys in a bloody dungeon; Ser Boros and Ser Osmund say Cersei commanded the killings. One corpse lies facedown in a blood puddle; the other drew a dagger before being run through with a longsword. Jaime berates them for destroying potential witnesses.
14. **Jaime orders Marbrand to search** — He sends Ser Addam Marbrand to search the Street of Silk for Tyrion, noting the gold cloaks will find more under whores' skirts than under beds.
15. **Jaime thinks of Brienne** — He wonders where Brienne of Tarth is and almost prays for her strength.
16. **Midnight devotions** — Several hundred septons in cloth-of-silver vestments and crystal coronals (the Most Devout) and humbler brethren enter through the Father's Doors; white septas march through the Mother's Doors; silent sisters in soft grey come down the Stranger's Steps; brothers in brown and butternut and dun robes with iron hammers and begging bowls. They circuit the sept worshiping at each of the seven altars.
17. **Jaime recalls his knighting vigil** — At fifteen, he knelt all night in a small sept; Ser Arthur Dayne knighted him at dawn, the pale blade so sharp it cut through his tunic.
18. **Cersei arrives at the hour of the wolf** — Disguised in roughspun, soaked from rain, she reveals Kevan has refused to be Hand and knows about their relationship. She asks Jaime to serve as Tommen's Hand; he refuses.
19. **Jaime and Cersei clash** — Cersei pleads "be my Hand" and offers to rule together; Jaime says Tommen is not his son and refuses. Cersei departs angrily.
20. **Dawn reveals decomposition** — Rainbows from the dome glass bathe Tywin's corpse; his face is greenish, eyes are black pits, cheeks fissured, foul white fluid pools beneath him.
21. **Margaery arrives with roses** — She places golden roses at the bier's foot and holds one under her nose.
22. **Tommen retches and flees** — Cersei and Tommen arrive with Osmund Kettleblack; Tommen recoils at the sight, retches, his crown falls off, and he runs for the doors.
23. **Jaime comforts Tommen** — He catches the boy in the Hall of Lamps, takes him outside. Tommen insists he was not scared, only sick from the smell. Jaime tells him "a man can bear most anything, if he must."
24. **Tommen begins a confession about Joffrey** — He says he "used to go away inside sometimes, when Joffy—" before Cersei interrupts.
25. **Cersei berates Tommen** — She scolds him, saying Joffrey would never have shamed her so.
26. **Jaime advises Cersei on Mace Tyrell** — He suggests inviting Mace to supper, then sending him to take Storm's End — either Mace succeeds or fails and looks a fool. Cersei suggests Mace might die in the siege; Jaime concurs.
27. **Cersei compares Jaime to Tywin** — She tells him "for a moment you sounded quite like Father."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Numb and exhausted — no grief for his father ("Where are my tears? Where is my rage?"). Haunted by Tyrion's parting accusation about Cersei's infidelity, which recurs obsessively. Guilt and self-blame for freeing Tyrion ("I loosed Tyrion"; "Whatever Varys did, I made him do"). Phantom pain in his missing hand. Growing revulsion at the corpse's decomposition. A momentary tenderness for Brienne, almost a prayer. Desire to kill (Varys). Fleeting absurd amusement at the grotesquerie of the vigil. Exhaustion bordering on delirium — he sways, may sleep standing.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Tyrion's accusation that Cersei has been sleeping with Lancel, Osmund Kettleblack, and Moon Boy; the phrase repeats in his thoughts multiple times throughout the night, unbidden. He wants to believe it is a lie but cannot stop visualizing it.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to serve as Hand of the King. Advises Cersei to flatter Mace Tyrell and send him to take Storm's End. Arranges supper between Cersei and the Tyrells. Pursues and comforts Tommen rather than continuing the vigil.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself Tyrion's accusation about Cersei and Kettleblack is "a lie" even while involuntarily visualizing it. Avoids acknowledging his own role in Tywin's death — starts to say "the blood is on his hands as much as mine" but cannot finish the sentence. Thinks "I told Varys no one was to be harmed" while knowing the outcome was predictable. Reflects on his knighting as a boy who died, disconnecting his current self from the knight he became.
