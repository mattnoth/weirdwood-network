# AFFC — Prologue

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Pate
- **pov_chapter_number:** Prologue
- **first_available:** AFFC Prologue
- **location_primary:** Oldtown (the Quill and Tankard inn, then streets of Oldtown)
- **location_secondary:** The Citadel (referenced), the Hightower (seen from distance), Battle Island (seen from distance)
- **approximate_timeline:** During the War of the Five Kings, after the Battle of the Blackwater, after Renly Baratheon's death, after Balon Greyjoy crowned himself. Night transitioning to dawn.
- **time_markers:** Night into dawn; "the mists were lightening to the east"; "Dawn, Pate realized. Dawn has come"; "the eastern sky was turning pink"; first shaft of sunlight breaks through clouds; morning bells peal from the septs; "Each morning at first light the red priests gathered"; three days since the alchemist's first offer; Pate arrived at the Citadel five years ago at age thirteen; Alleras at the Citadel for one year; Walgrave found weeping in the Library half a year ago; Lord Leyton has not descended the Hightower in more than a decade; the Quill and Tankard has stood for six hundred years; the Starry Sept was seat of the High Septon for a thousand years before Aegon landed at King's Landing; glass candles brought from Valyria a thousand years before the Doom.
- **chapter_summary:** At the Quill and Tankard inn in Oldtown, novice Pate and fellow Citadel students discuss reports of living dragons in the east while Alleras the Sphinx demonstrates his archery. Lazy Leo Tyrell arrives and reveals that a glass candle is burning in Archmaester Marwyn's chambers. The group disperses at dawn, and Pate walks through Oldtown until the mysterious alchemist he has been waiting for appears on the river road. Pate exchanges a stolen archmaester's key — one that opens every door in the Citadel — for a golden dragon coin. After the exchange, Pate collapses and dies in an alley.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Heavy mist and damp throughout the night, burning away at dawn. "A sea of mist this morning." "The night is damp, and the cobbles will be slippery." Night's mists burn away as the sun rises.
- **Season indicators:** Withered and wormy apples on the tree (suggesting late season). White ravens kept at the Citadel by Archmaester Walgrave (white ravens are associated with seasonal announcements). No explicit season stated.
- **Time of day:** Night transitioning to dawn. The chapter begins in pre-dawn darkness with torchlight and mist, and ends in early morning with sunlight breaking through clouds.
- **Lighting:** Torchlit terrace of the Quill and Tankard. The Hightower's distant beacon "floated in the damp of night like a hazy orange moon." Dawn light grows: eastern sky turns pink, first shaft of sunlight breaks through clouds. The alchemist appears with the rising sun behind his shoulder, making his face hard to see.
- **Sounds:** Nightingale singing in the apple tree by the water. Ravens quorking and screaming (recalled). White ravens muttering "Pate, Pate, Pate." Arrow striking apple: "a soft chunk echoed back across the river, followed by a splash." Emma's laughter from overhead window mixed with a man's deeper voice. Morning bells from the Sailor's Sept, Lord's Sept, Seven Shrines, and Starry Sept — "They made a mighty music." Red priests singing at first light. Butcher's cart rumbling past with piglets squealing.
- **Smells:** Not explicitly described, though "the clean fresh smell of her" is recalled about Rosey.
- **Notable sensory details:** Fearsomely strong cider going to Pate's head when he stands. Cobblestones slippery from damp. Pate falls to one knee in mud on the river road. The gold dragon coin feels warm against Pate's palm. Pate bites the coin. At the end, Pate feels his heart hammering, legs turning to water, cobblestones rushing up — physical sensations of collapse/death.

## Events & Actions
1. **Apple archery on the terrace** — Mollander throws apples for Alleras to shoot with his goldenheart bow over the Honeywine. Alleras cores the first, splits the second in two, and misses the third (his last shot).
2. **Dragon debate** — The group discusses sailors' reports of living dragons in the east. Mollander believes them; Armen dismisses them as stories. Alleras suggests "the dragon has three heads" and identifies Daenerys Targaryen as alive with dragons.
3. **Pate's inner turmoil** — Pate anxiously waits for the alchemist while thinking about Rosey, his failed maester aspirations, and whether to trade the stolen key. He recalls his failures with Archmaesters Vaellyn and Ebrose.
4. **Lazy Leo's arrival** — Leo Tyrell arrives from the old plank bridge, supposedly confined to the Citadel for three more days. He insults Mollander ("Hopfrog"), Alleras (racist slurs about his mother), and Pate ("Spotted Pate the pig boy").
5. **Glass candle revelation** — Leo announces that a glass candle is burning in Archmaester Marwyn's chambers. This silences the terrace. Leo declares "Old powers waken. Shadows stir."
6. **Group disperses at dawn** — Armen, Alleras, Mollander, and Roone leave across the bridge. Mollander is so drunk he leans on Roone. Alleras gives Pate a lingering look before departing.
7. **Leo and Pate alone** — Leo taunts Pate about Rosey, threatening to "make a woman of her." Pate warns Leo to leave Rosey alone, then leaves.
8. **Pate's dawn walk through Oldtown** — Pate crosses the bridge, walks along the river road as morning bells peal from four different septs and red priests sing. He observes Oldtown emerging from the mist. He considers buying a donkey or sailing to Qarth but turns back toward the Citadel.
9. **Flashback: the alchemist's first offer** — The night Rosey brought them together, the alchemist offered to change iron into gold — a golden dragon coin for the key. He said he would return in three days. The coin had a three-headed dragon on one side and a dead king on the other.
10. **Flashback: Pate steals the key** — Pate pulled Walgrave's strongbox from under his bed, found the black iron key inside a knight's gauntlet, took the key and the silver. A white raven called "Pate, Pate, Pate" after him.
11. **The alchemist appears** — On the river road, the alchemist greets Pate. He says Pate was with his friends and he did not wish to intrude. He asks: "Have you decided what you are?"
12. **The exchange** — Pate follows the alchemist through alleys, the old Thieves Market, and Ragpicker's Wynd to a narrow alley. The alchemist walks the golden dragon across his knuckles. Pate takes the coin and gives over the key.
13. **The alchemist reveals his face** — At Pate's request, the alchemist pulls down his hood. He has a young, ordinary face with full cheeks, shadow of beard, faint scar on right cheek, hooked nose, dense black curly hair. Pate does not recognize him. The alchemist calls himself "A stranger. No one. Truly."
14. **Pate's death** — Walking away, Pate feels the cobblestones moving beneath his feet, his heart hammering, legs turning to water. A voice says sadly, "And never will." Pate collapses. His last thought is of Rosey.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious and restless (waiting for the alchemist), lovesick for Rosey (rhapsodizes about her eyes, dimples, toes, smell), resentful of his low status (pink-necked novice after five years), envious (of Alleras's quick progress, Leo's wealth), afraid (of Leo's threats toward Rosey, of being caught as a thief), guilty (stealing the key, drinking when he came to wait), self-pitying (comparing himself to the folk-tale pig boy), and at the end light-headed and giddy after the exchange — then terrified as he dies.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether the alchemist will appear to complete the exchange. Beneath that, Rosey — his desire for her and his belief that the golden dragon is the only way to have her. His sense of failure as a novice runs throughout.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to stay and wait for the alchemist after the others leave. Decides to follow the alchemist when he appears. Completes the exchange — gives the stolen key for the coin. These decisions are presented as already half-made; Pate follows rather than choosing.
- **Self-deception flags:** "Am I still a thief if I put it all back and no one ever knows?" — rationalizing the possibility of returning the key as if it undoes the intent. Fantasizes about becoming a maester or a wandering healer while aware neither is likely. Tells himself "Rosey was all that he wanted in the world" but the chapter shows he also craves status and respect. He tells the alchemist "I am no thief, I am a novice of the Citadel" at their first meeting, defining himself by the identity he is about to betray.
