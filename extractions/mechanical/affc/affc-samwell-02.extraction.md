## Events & Actions
1. **Departure from Eastwatch** — Blackbird sails from Eastwatch-by-the-Sea with Sam, Gilly, her babe, Maester Aemon, and Dareon aboard. The Wall and Eastwatch shrink and disappear behind them.
2. **Sam's seasickness begins** — Sam becomes greensick immediately upon sailing. Dareon teases him about his large belly.
3. **Gilly's distress at sea** — Gilly trembles, weeps, and flees below deck as the shore vanishes. She refuses to come on deck for the rest of the voyage.
4. **Calm crossing of the Bay of Seals** — Ten days of calm sailing along the coast. Dareon sings for the oarsmen. Maester Aemon sits on deck gazing out despite his blindness.
5. **Aemon's reminiscence** — Aemon recalls his journey to the Wall aboard the Golden Dragon, escorted by Ser Duncan at Egg's insistence. He mentions Brynden Rivers was among his "honor guard" from the emptied dungeons, and was later chosen Lord Commander.
6. **Rain and rough seas begin** — Rain starts; Aemon insists on staying on deck, saying the rain "feels like tears." Sam stays with him for nearly an hour.
7. **Aemon falls asleep in the rain** — Sam finds the old man has fallen asleep in the rain. When shaken awake, Aemon says "Egg? Egg, I dreamed that I was old."
8. **Sam carries Aemon below** — Sam lifts the old man, who weighs "no more than a child," and carries him to the cabin. He and Gilly strip Aemon's wet clothes and warm him with furs and body heat.
9. **Ship lurches violently** — A sudden lurch throws Sam down and sends Gilly crashing into his arms. She clings to him desperately. Sam becomes physically aroused despite his fear.
10. **Storms around Skagos** — Severe storms batter the ship as it rounds Skagos. Three storms or one broken by lulls — Sam cannot tell. All are starved but cannot eat.
11. **Wreckage spotted** — They see a wrecked galley on Skagos rocks with dead crew on shore, being eaten by rooks and crabs. Old Tattersalt mutters "Too bloody close."
12. **Brief calm sailing** — Eight days and seven nights of smooth sailing after passing Skagos.
13. **Crew mutters against Gilly** — Crewmen blame Gilly for the bad weather, citing her incestuous origins. One says they should "get rid of her, and that abomination that she whelped."
14. **Sam guards Gilly** — Sam sharpens his knife and accompanies Gilly whenever she leaves the cabin.
15. **Dareon's failed lullaby** — At Sam's urging, Dareon plays a lullaby, but Gilly sobs uncontrollably. Dareon snaps at her and leaves to drink firewine.
16. **Aemon reveals the baby switch** — Aemon tells Sam that Gilly is grieving because the babe she carries is not her own child. He says the babe is "Dalla's son, and not the child of her body," and that Jon Snow made threats and promises to force the switch.
17. **Sam's anguished realization** — Sam understands that Jon switched the babies to protect the prince (Mance Rayder's son) from Melisandre's fires. He is devastated and thinks "there are no happy endings."
18. **Brief clear night** — A still night with visible stars and the red wanderer the free folk call the Thief. Rowers rest. Sam sees more storms gathering to the south.
19. **Sam predicts worse storms** — Sam points south to gathering darkness and lightning. He tells Dareon "The worst is just beginning, and there are no happy endings." Dareon calls him a craven.
