# AFFC — Jaime III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 28
- **pov_character:** Jaime Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jaime III
- **first_available:** AFFC Jaime III
- **location_primary:** King's Landing (Red Keep), the kingsroad, Hayford Castle, Harrenhal
- **location_secondary:** Cobbler's Square, Flea Bottom, Mud Gate, Traitor's Walk, Sow's Horn, the Wode brothers' lands, Flowstone Yard, Hall of a Hundred Hearths, Hunter's Hall, bear pit at Harrenhal
- **approximate_timeline:** After Jaime's return to King's Landing and appointment as Lord Commander; Cersei is regent; the War of the Five Kings is winding down in the riverlands; siege of Riverrun ongoing
- **time_markers:** Multiple days of travel; first night at Hayford Castle; "as the sun went down" for Hayford camp; a horned moon that night; "come morning" next day; "another bright and blustery day was followed by a cloudy one, then three days of rain"; multiple nights of sparring practice; arrival at Harrenhal after several days
- **chapter_summary:** Cersei orders Jaime to ride to Harrenhal and then Riverrun to finish the siege. They quarrel over who should command the Kingsguard in his absence. Jaime rides north with a mixed force of roughly a thousand men, secretly practicing swordplay with Ser Ilyn Payne each night to retrain his left hand. After camping at Hayford Castle and passing through war-ravaged lands, the column reaches Harrenhal, where Jaime finds the Mountain's brutal garrison, the remains of Vargo Hoat (who was dismembered and fed to captives), and a traumatized Pia. He installs Ser Bonifer Hasty as castellan and strikes Red Ronnet Connington for insulting Brienne at the bear pit.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jaime visits Cersei in her chambers** — She criticizes his beard and appearance, calls him a pale cripple, and orders him to ride to Riverrun via Harrenhal to end the siege and deal with the garrison.
2. **Argument over Kingsguard command** — Jaime insists Ser Loras Tyrell should command in his absence; Cersei demands Ser Osmund Kettleblack. They argue about Balon Swann being sent to Dorne.
3. **Cersei slaps Jaime** — After Jaime notes Osmund leers at her. Jaime does not block it. She dismisses him.
4. **Jaime assembles his force at the Red Keep stables** — Forty knights and as many esquires, half westermen, half former foes. Three squires assigned: a Paege, Little Lew Piper, and Josmyn Peckledon.
5. **Jaime refuses to wear the golden hand for the departure** — "Let them see the stump. Let them see the cripple."
6. **The column rides through King's Landing** — Sparse reception compared to Mace Tyrell's cheering departure. Whores call out, a meat pie man cries his wares. In Cobbler's Square, two sparrows harangue smallfolk about godless men and demon worshipers.
7. **Jaime meets the rest of his force outside the city walls** — Ser Addam's outriders, baggage train, Holy Hundred, Sarsfield's archers, Maester Gulian with ravens, Ser Flement Brax's heavy horse. Fewer than a thousand men total.
8. **Flashback: Jaime visited Ser Ilyn's chambers at Traitor's Walk** — Found filthy conditions. Ilyn agreed to ride with him with a silent nod.
9. **First night's camp at Hayford Castle** — Hundred tents below the hill. Jaime sets sentries himself, recalling his uncle Stafford's death at Oxcross.
10. **Supper at Hayford** — Jaime wears the golden hand; Lady Ermesande is presented. Discussion of missing Tyrek Lannister. Jaime knocks over wine goblet and snaps at Flement Brax.
11. **Jaime reflects on Tyrek's disappearance and suspects Varys** — Alone in his tower room, he reasons that Varys could have arranged the kidnapping during the riot, noting Varys gave no warning and didn't attend Myrcella's departure.
12. **First secret sparring session with Ser Ilyn** — In a small courtyard off the Hayford armory, by moonlight, with blunted tourney swords and shields. Ilyn beats Jaime badly. Jaime vows to practice every night.
13. **Jaime explains his bruises as "love bites"** — Tells Little Lew the Hayford wenches are lusty.
14. **Multiple days of travel and nightly sparring** — Practice in a stable, inn cellar, burned barn, wooded island, open field in rain. Addam Marbrand knows but no one speaks of it.
15. **Wolf attack on outrider's horse** — Marbrand's outrider loses his horse to wolves. Ser Bonifer calls wolves "demons in the skins of wolves, sent to chastise us for our sins." Jaime orders the horsemeat salted.
16. **Stop at Sow's Horn** — Ser Roger Hogg reports being attacked first by Amory Lorch's raiders (manticore shield), then wolves. Lost sheep and goats. Jaime advises him to plant and pray for one last harvest.
17. **Column crosses into riverlands territory** — Maester Gulian identifies the Wode brothers' lands; their keeps destroyed.
18. **Jaime hangs outlaws** — Found sheltering in root cellar; one wore a crimson cloak. Jaime reflects: "Goldenhand the Just."
19. **Arrival at Harrenhal** — Horn of Herrock sounded three times. Gates open. Gregor's men emerge — hard-eyed, hard-mouthed. Shitmouth greets Jaime.
20. **Jaime learns about Polliver and the Tickler's deaths** — Killed by the Hound at the crossroads inn, along with "that Sarsfield boy." Sandor had a boy with him.
21. **Jaime views Vargo Hoat's mutilated head** — Learns that Gregor had Hoat dismembered alive and fed to the captives, who were forced to eat human flesh. Hoat was fed his own flesh. Jaime orders the head thrown in the lake.
22. **Jaime installs Ser Bonifer Hasty as castellan** — Mountain's men offered land by Bonifer; they want gold instead. Jaime tells them to petition Cersei.
23. **Jaime inspects the captives** — Pia found with broken nose and missing teeth; falls sobbing at his feet. Ben Blackthumb and the cook are Lady Whent's only remaining people. Shagwell, Pyg, and Zollo are gone.
24. **Ser Wylis Manderly found among captives** — Ragged, filthy, but alive. Collapses sobbing when told he'll be sent to Maidenpool and shipped to White Harbor.
25. **Jaime orders a hot meal** — "Anything but goat." He takes Pia into his care.
26. **Supper with Ser Bonifer in Hunter's Hall** — Bonifer refuses Gregor's sinful men. Jaime agrees to take them. Bonifer also insists Jaime take Pia. Discussion of outlaws: Beric Dondarrion (capture and hold), the Myrish priest (Bonifer's choice), Brave Companions (send word to Jaime), Sandor Clegane (dangerous).
27. **Jaime encounters Red Ronnet Connington at the bear pit** — Ronnet reveals he was once betrothed to Brienne, visited Tarth, mocked her appearance, and gave her a rose as dismissal.
28. **Jaime strikes Ronnet with his golden hand** — Knocks him down the bear pit steps for calling Brienne a freak. Demands Ronnet call her by her name: "Call her Brienne."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply conflicted — physically drawn to Cersei but repulsed by the knowledge of her infidelity (Tyrion's revelation echoes three times); anger at her cruelty and condescension; satisfaction at being on the road away from court; determination to retrain his sword hand; disgust at Harrenhal's horrors; protective tenderness toward Brienne; self-deprecating awareness of his diminished state; a thread of hope ("Perhaps there is yet hope for the both of us")
- **Primary preoccupation:** Tyrion's revelation about Cersei sleeping with Lancel, Osmund Kettleblack, and Moon Boy haunts him throughout; retraining his left hand for combat; the gap between who he was and who he is now
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts the Riverrun/Harrenhal mission despite reservations; insists Loras command the Kingsguard (loses this argument); refuses to wear the golden hand for the departure; takes Ser Ilyn Payne as secret sparring partner; hangs outlaws in crimson cloaks; installs Bonifer as castellan of Harrenhal; takes Gregor's men and Pia; orders Wylis Manderly sent to Maidenpool; strikes Red Ronnet for insulting Brienne
- **Self-deception flags:** Still physically desires Cersei despite loathing her behavior ("He wanted to rip her gown off and turn her blows to kisses"); tells himself Tyrek is dead but his reasoning about Varys suggests deep uncertainty; rationalizes hanging outlaws as "justice" with aspirational self-naming ("Goldenhand the Just"); tells himself he chose not to block Cersei's slap rather than acknowledging powerlessness
