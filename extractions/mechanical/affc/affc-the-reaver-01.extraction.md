# AFFC — The Reaver I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 29
- **pov_character:** Victarion Greyjoy
- **pov_chapter_number:** The Reaver I
- **first_available:** AFFC The Reaver I
- **location_primary:** Mouth of the Mander (sea battle); Lord Hewett's Town, Oakenshield
- **location_secondary:** Old Wyk (recalled), the Mander, Greenshield, Greyshield, Southshield, Grimston, Iron Islands
- **approximate_timeline:** After the kingsmoot on Old Wyk and the taking of the Shield Islands; Euron Greyjoy already crowned king
- **time_markers:** Battle occurs during daytime; ironborn arrived on "the evening tide" the prior day so the setting sun would hide them; Greyshield, Greenshield, and Southshield fell "before the sun came up"; Oakenshield lasted "half a day longer"; Iron Victory lingers "for hours" off the Mander after the battle; sun goes down during the feast; the conversation with Euron occurs at night; "autumn is upon us" (Rodrik Harlaw); "the next day" Aeron vanished from Old Wyk after his conversation with Victarion
- **chapter_summary:** Victarion Greyjoy leads the Iron Fleet's rear guard in a sea battle at the mouth of the Mander, boarding an enemy ship and dueling Ser Talbert Serry before throwing him overboard. After the battle, Victarion sails to Lord Hewett's Town on Oakenshield, where Euron Crow's Eye holds a riotous feast in the captured castle. Euron names new ironborn lords for the four Shield Islands and proposes sailing to Slaver's Bay to claim dragons, but faces opposition from the captains who prefer raiding the Reach. In a private meeting afterward, Euron tasks Victarion with taking the Iron Fleet to Slaver's Bay to bring back Daenerys Targaryen as his bride, offering the Seastone Chair in return. Victarion agrees, but secretly resolves to take Daenerys for himself.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sea battle at the mouth of the Mander** — The Iron Victory rams an enemy longship flying rose banners (white rose on red escutcheon). Victarion vaults aboard and fights the enemy crew.
2. **Victarion slays multiple defenders** — He hews off one man's arm, cleaves another's shoulder, smashes a third with his shield, takes a spear in the back (blocked by armor), and kills the spearman by splitting his head.
3. **Ironborn follow Victarion aboard** — Wulfe One-Ear, Ragnor Pyke, and Nute the Barber join the fight. Nute kills a man with a thrown axe.
4. **Duel with Ser Talbert Serry** — Victarion challenges the young knight. Serry fights well with his longsword, landing blows on Victarion's helm and thigh. Victarion splits Serry's shield, catches Serry's blade in his iron fist (cutting his palm), wrenches the sword away and throws it in the sea.
5. **Serry thrown overboard** — Victarion grabs Serry by the throat and forces him over the side into the bloodstained water. Tells him to go get his sword.
6. **Battle concluded** — The remaining defenders fall. Some try to flee or cry for quarter. A knot of men fight to the death around the mast; Victarion kills them.
7. **Victory tallied** — Victarion removes his helm. No one saw if Serry survived. Victarion says "may he feast as he fought" and gives the captured ship to Ragnor Pyke.
8. **Post-battle orders** — Victarion orders captives stripped of arms, wounds bound, dying thrown in the sea, throats cut for those begging mercy. Wants a count of prizes, knights, lordlings, and banners.
9. **Victarion reflects on Euron's strategy** — Euron sent Torwold and the Red Oarsman as decoys up the Mander; the Shield Island lords pursued them, leaving the islands undefended. The ironborn attacked at evening tide with the setting sun behind them. Euron's wizards rumored to have ensured favorable winds.
10. **Victarion takes the dusky woman** — Goes below to his cabin; takes the dusky woman twice. Blood from his wound smears her. She washes it with boiled vinegar.
11. **Recalled conversation with Aeron on Old Wyk** — Aeron urged Victarion to oppose Euron, calling the kingsmoot corrupted by sorcery. Aeron planned to raise the common folk against Euron. The next day Aeron vanished.
12. **Victarion recalls Baelor Blacktyde's fate** — Blacktyde tried to sail home refusing Euron; Victarion's Iron Fleet blocked the bay. Blacktyde was cut into seven parts to feed the seven gods he worshipped.
13. **Victarion reflects on the dusky woman's origin** — Given by Euron, taken from a slaver. Her tongue was torn out. She reminds him of the first woman Euron gave him.
14. **Iron Victory lingers as rear guard** — With Grief, Lord Dagon, Iron Wind, and Maiden's Bane. Survivors pulled from the sea. Hardhand sinks. Final count: six ships lost, thirty-eight captured.
15. **Arrival at Lord Hewett's Town** — The town is looted, corpses in the streets, the sept burned. Victarion notices the Silence with its iron figurehead. Women and children on a cog are being sold as slaves by Euron's decree.
16. **Victarion protests slavery** — Objects that thralls and salt wives are the Old Way, not slaves bought and sold. Nute dismisses the distinction.
17. **Meeting Rodrik Harlaw, Drumm, and Gorold Goodbrother** — In the castle yard. The Reader warns the islands aren't worth the enmity of House Tyrell. Drumm mentions Lord Tarly and Heartsbane.
18. **The feast in Lord Hewett's hall** — Riotous celebration. Ironborn decked in plunder. Lord Hewett bound and gagged on the dais with Euron beside him. Falia Flowers in Euron's lap. Lady Hewett and seven other women serve, then are forced to strip naked and continue serving.
19. **Hotho Harlaw tells of Ser Harras at Grimston** — The Knight defeated seven champions beneath Grimston's walls until Lord Grimm's septon surrendered. Hotho declares himself the Reader's heir now.
20. **Euron names the new lords of the Shield Islands** — Ser Harras Harlaw (Greyshield), Andrik the Unsmiling (Southshield), Maron Volmark (Greenshield), Nute the Barber (Oakenshield). Victarion realizes Euron is deliberately separating these men from their former lords.
21. **Euron proposes sailing to Slaver's Bay for dragons** — Promises more plunder on the voyage east; tells the captains to prepare to sail on the morrow.
22. **Rodrik Harlaw challenges the plan** — Lists the hazards: galleys at the Redwyne Straits, the Dornish coast, the Stepstones, lack of supply. Says a third of the fleet would be lost.
23. **Euron claims to have sailed to Valyria** — Counters Rodrik by asserting he has made more dangerous voyages. The Reader quietly asks "Have you?" and Euron warns him off.
24. **Captains call for the Arbor instead** — Ralf the Limper, Quellon Humble, Fralegg the Strong, Alvyn Sharp, and Red Ralf Stonehouse advocate raiding the Reach. Euron calls them cravens and leaves with Falia.
25. **The feast continues into darkness** — Drunken men dance the finger dance. Left-Hand Lucas Codd rapes one of Lord Hewett's daughters on a table.
26. **Euron summons Victarion privately** — Via his mongrel bastard son. In Lord Hewett's bedchamber, Euron is naked except for his sable cloak and eye patch. Falia is naked and asleep.
27. **Euron speaks of flying** — Says he dreamt of flying as a boy; the maester said he couldn't. Poses the question of leaping from a tall tower.
28. **Euron offers shade-of-the-evening** — Thick blue-black wine of the warlocks, captured from a Qartheen galleas along with four warlocks. Euron killed one and fed him to the other three. Victarion tastes it and spits it out.
29. **Euron reveals Cragorn's death** — The man who blew the dragon horn died with lungs charred black as soot when the maester cut him open.
30. **Euron mentions throwing a dragon's egg into the sea** — A Myrish wizard promised to hatch it but failed; Euron killed the wizard.
31. **Euron tasks Victarion with the Slaver's Bay mission** — Wants Victarion to take the Iron Fleet to fetch "the last of her line" — a woman with silver-gold hair and amethyst eyes. Offers the Seastone Chair in exchange.
32. **Victarion agrees** — Resolves to go, but secretly plans to take the dragon queen for himself as revenge for Euron stealing his wife.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Battle-exultant during the fight; bitter and resentful toward Euron throughout; nostalgic for simpler obedience under Balon; conflicted about kinslaying; disgusted by Euron's treatment of Lord Hewett and the humiliation of the women (reminds him of his own wife's suffering); drunk and increasingly reckless by the end of the chapter
- **Primary preoccupation:** Resentment of Euron — who steals his glory, his men, and his autonomy. The tension between obedience (deeply ingrained) and rebellion. The desire to kill Euron but fear of the kinslaying curse.
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees to sail the Iron Fleet to Slaver's Bay at Euron's request, but secretly resolves to take Daenerys Targaryen for himself rather than bring her to Euron
- **Self-deception flags:** Victarion claims the battle victory is his alone while using Euron's strategy. He tells himself he has "no luck with wives" while having beaten his previous wife to death. He convinces himself he can outsmart Euron — "Euron's gifts are poisoned, he reminded himself, but still..." suggests he knows the danger yet proceeds anyway. He frames his future betrayal as justice ("you stole my wife... so I'll have yours") rather than acknowledging he is being manipulated into exactly what Euron wants.
