# AFFC — Arya I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 7
- **pov_character:** Arya Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya I
- **first_available:** AFFC Arya I
- **location_primary:** Braavos (approach and arrival)
- **location_secondary:** Narrow sea, the Titan of Braavos, Arsenal of Braavos, Isle of the Gods, Temple of the Moonsingers, Red Temple of R'hllor, the Holy Refuge (the Warren), House of Black and White
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Arya's departure from Saltpans aboard the Titan's Daughter; the crossing of the narrow sea is complete as the chapter opens
- **time_markers:** Last of the night's stars vanishing (pre-dawn/dawn); the Titan's eyes visible as stars; "half a day" estimated for customs inspection at Chequy Port; Arya is "almost eleven, practically a woman grown"; candle-lit interior of the House of Black and White suggests daytime outside but perpetual dimness within
- **chapter_summary:** Arya arrives in Braavos aboard the galleas Titan's Daughter. She watches the approach through the channel beneath the Titan's legs and into the lagoon past the Arsenal. Captain Ternesio Terys puts her ashore in a boat rowed by his son Yorko, who takes her through the canals past the Isle of the Gods to the House of Black and White. Inside the dark temple, Arya gives water to a dying man, is approached by a waif in black-and-white robes, and meets a hooded priest who presses her through her false names until she reveals herself as Arya Stark. The priest tests her by showing a skull face; Arya kisses it and tries to eat the worm from its eye socket, revealing a kindly old man beneath.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Sea mists; wind tugging at Arya's cloak "insistent as a ghost"; spray from waves crashing against the rocky ridges
- **Season indicators:** None stated explicitly
- **Time of day:** Pre-dawn transitioning to dawn and morning — "the last of the night's stars had vanished" as they approach; daytime by the time Arya enters the House of Black and White
- **Lighting:** Star-like lights of the Titan's eyes; huge fires burning in the Titan's eye-caves; dim red candles inside the House of Black and White; candles so dim Arya cannot see her own feet; red candlelight reflected in the priest's eyes; fire brazier atop the Red Temple
- **Sounds:** Captain shouting orders in a deep voice; sailors scrambling; oars heaving; waves crashing against ridges; the Titan's roar — "a terrible groaning and grinding" that drowns out everything; a thousand seabirds taking flight; drums from within galley hulls "bom bom bom bom bom bom bom bom, like the beat of living hearts"; whispering inside the temple; someone weeping; light footfalls; leather sliding over stone; a door opening and closing; water in the pool; soft plop of the dropped cup
- **Smells:** Salt spray; unfamiliar candle scent in the temple that deeper inside smells of "snow and pine needles and hot stew"
- **Notable sensory details:** Arya tastes salt where spray touched her face; warm and heavy air inside the House of Black and White makes her yawn; the pool water is black as ink; the grave worm melts "like a shadow" in Arya's hand; the rough stone floor of the temple (contrasted with polished marble of the Great Sept of Baelor)

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya spots the Titan's light** — Standing at the prow of the Titan's Daughter, Arya and Denyo see a distant light through sea mists; Denyo calls it "the star of home."
2. **Arya reflects on her journey** — She recalls her failed attempts to reach intended destinations: Winterfell (ended at Harrenhal), Riverrun (captured to the hollow hill), the Twins (the Hound stole her), Saltpans/Eastwatch (ended up crossing to Braavos instead).
3. **Arya recites her kill list** — Ser Gregor, Dunsen, Raff the Sweetling, Ser Ilyn, Ser Meryn, Queen Cersei — six names remaining. Joffrey dead, Polliver slain by the Hound, the Tickler and a pimpled squire killed by Arya herself.
4. **The Titan emerges from the mists** — The ship approaches the massive statue; Arya observes its stone legs, bronze armor, green rope hair, fire eyes, and broken sword.
5. **The Titan roars** — A massive groaning and grinding sound warns the Arsenal of the ship's coming; a thousand seabirds scatter; Denyo laughs and explains.
6. **The Titan's Daughter passes beneath the Titan** — The ship threads the channel between the stone legs; Arya notes arrow slits, murder holes, and faces behind iron bars.
7. **The Arsenal comes into view** — A rocky sea mont bristling with siege weapons; dozens of galleys at quays and in sheds.
8. **Escort galleys meet the ship** — Two galleys skim out; captains shout back and forth; a great horn sounds; drums beat within the galley hulls.
9. **Arya observes Braavos from the lagoon** — A flat city of domes, towers, and bridges; a hundred isles; no walls — "our galleys are our walls."
10. **Captain Ternesio orders Arya ashore** — He explains they make for Chequy Port for customs; instructs her to gather belongings and go with Yorko.
11. **Arya considers staying aboard** — She briefly wishes to remain as a ship's girl, but recognizes the captain wants to be rid of her and accepts.
12. **Yorko rows Arya into Braavos** — They pass through the main canal; Arya observes carved bridges, canal boats, flat-bottomed barges, floating houses, the sweetwater aqueduct, and Sealord statues.
13. **They pass the Isle of the Gods** — Yorko identifies the Temple of the Moonsingers, the Red Temple of R'hllor, and the Holy Refuge (the Warren). Arya remembers Thoros of Myr and Lord Beric.
14. **Yorko leaves Arya at the House of Black and White** — He docks at a covered dock at the base of a rocky knoll; says "Valar dohaeris" and rows away.
15. **Arya climbs the steps** — She takes them two at a time "so no one could ever say she'd been afraid," thinking of Syrio and the godswood at Winterfell.
16. **Arya confronts the weirwood-and-ebony doors** — They are locked; she pounds and demands entry, shows the iron coin, says "Valar morghulis"; the doors open silently with no visible hand.
17. **Arya enters the dark temple** — She draws Needle; the temple is much larger within; she moves among statues of many gods and rows of stone benches, smelling candles that evoke snow, pine needles, and hot stew.
18. **Arya finds the black pool and the dying man** — A young man in a silvery cloak weeps by a black pool, dips his hand in, sucks his fingers. Arya fills a stone cup and offers it. He drinks, says "Valar morghulis," drops the cup, then lurches to an alcove — he has a stab wound below his belt. Other alcoves hold old people who appear to be dead or dying.
19. **The waif touches Arya's arm** — A pale little girl in black-and-white robes appears; Arya warns her: "I killed the boy who grabbed me last."
20. **The hooded priest speaks to Arya** — He identifies the place as the House of Black and White, a place of peace and safety, temple of the Many-Faced God.
21. **The priest presses for Arya's true name** — Arya cycles through identities: Salty → Squab → Nan/Weasel → Arry → Arya of House Stark. The priest acknowledges "You are" but says the House of Black and White is no place for Arya of House Stark.
22. **The priest tests Arya's fear of death** — He lowers his cowl to reveal a yellowed skull with a worm in the eye socket and says "Kiss me, child."
23. **Arya passes the test** — She kisses the skull where its nose should be and tries to eat the worm, which melts in her hand. The skull dissolves into the face of a kindly old man.
24. **The kindly man offers food** — "No one has ever tried to eat my worm before. Are you hungry, child?" Arya thinks: "Yes, but not for food."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** A complex layering of grief, defiance, loneliness, and willed toughness. Arya repeatedly tells herself not to be afraid, insisting she is a wolf. Beneath the bravado is deep sadness — she believes nearly all her family is dead. She oscillates between longing for home and connection ("I should have said farewell" to Denyo) and rejecting the need for it ("I don't need any friends, so long as I have Needle").
- **Primary preoccupation:** Loss of family and identity. The kill list remains a ritual touchstone. Finding Jaqen H'ghar and the purpose he promised. Arriving somewhere with no plan for what comes next.
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts going ashore rather than asking to stay on the ship. Enters the House of Black and White. Reveals her true name to the priest despite having maintained aliases. Kisses the skull face without flinching.
- **Self-deception flags:** "I don't need any friends" — immediately contradicted by her loneliness and her wish she'd said farewell to Denyo. "I never was [afraid]" — her repeated insistence on not being afraid suggests fear she's suppressing. Claims Salty "isn't highborn" while being Arya Stark. Believes Bran and Rickon are dead — the text presents this as Arya's sincere belief. "He had it all backwards" about Ned's lone-wolf saying — Arya applies the proverb to conclude the pack dies, inverting her father's intended meaning.
