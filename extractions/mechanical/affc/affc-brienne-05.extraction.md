- **chapter_summary:** Brienne and Podrick return to Maidenpool with Hyle Hunt, who carries the heads of the three Bloody Mummers. Lord Randyll Tarly dismisses Brienne contemptuously, refusing her hospitality and ordering her never to return. Hyle Hunt, dismissed from Tarly's service, provides intelligence that the Hound was last seen at Saltpans heading west along the Trident. They depart Maidenpool with Septon Meribald, a wandering holy man heading northwest along the Bay of Crabs toward Saltpans. Along the way, Meribald delivers a lengthy speech about the nature of broken men versus outlaws, revealing he fought in the War of the Ninepenny Kings as a boy.

- **Time of day:** Sun going down when searching for lodging; evening tide when ships depart; restless night; next morning at the Stinking Goose; midday meeting; departure the following morning at sunrise; near midday at the village.

## Events & Actions
1. **Hyle Hunt takes the heads** — Hunt hacks off the heads of Shagwell, Pyg, and Timeon and ties them by the hair to his saddle, despite Brienne's objections.
2. **Return to Maidenpool** — The party retraces their steps through Crackclaw Point in cold, wet weather over several days; the heads attract flies and rot.
3. **Presentation to Tarly** — At Mooton's castle yard, Hunt presents the heads to Tarly's serjeant; Hunt honestly credits Brienne and Podrick with the kills.
4. **Tarly dismisses Brienne** — Lord Randyll tells Brienne to stop her quest, go home to Tarth, and never return to Maidenpool while he rules; he refuses her hospitality.
5. **Hyle Hunt challenges Tarly** — Hunt points out it's Mooton's roof, not Tarly's, earning Tarly's ire.
6. **Brienne continues her quest** — Brienne refuses Tarly's command to go home, declaring she means to find Lady Sansa.
7. **Search for lodging** — Brienne and Podrick visit all remaining inns in Maidenpool but find no beds available.
8. **Visit to the docks** — They survey ships in harbor; the Titan's Daughter departs on the evening tide; the Gulltown Girl's master mistakes Brienne for a whore; an Ibbenese whaler's harpooner offers to buy Podrick; Brienne buys Podrick an orange on the Seastrider.
9. **Lodging on the Lady of Myr** — They find shelter on a storm-damaged trading galley at the east end of the harbor.
10. **Restless night** — Brienne wakes three times: once from rain, once from a creak (thinking of Nimble Dick), and once from a nightmare about the men she's killed plus Tarly, Vargo Hoat, and Ronnet Connington.
11. **Morning at the Stinking Goose** — Brienne pays for breakfast; the proprietor remembers Nimble Dick and asks about him.
12. **Hyle Hunt delivers intelligence** — Hunt arrives with information from his cousin Alyn: the Hound was last seen at Saltpans heading west along the Trident; Dondarrion's men seek the Hound, not riding with him; Stoneheart leads a separate band.
13. **Hyle reveals his dismissal** — Hunt tells Brienne that Tarly dismissed him from service; he intends to join her quest hoping for reward.
14. **Departure with Septon Meribald** — The party (Brienne, Podrick, Hyle Hunt, Septon Meribald with donkey and Dog) leaves Maidenpool at sunrise heading northwest.
15. **Travel along Bay of Crabs coast** — They follow a crooked track through low, wet lands of sandy dunes and salt marshes, with Meribald navigating.
16. **Encounter with three marsh women** — Three women (one old, one pregnant, one young) give Meribald a basket of clams; he gives oranges in return and hears their confessions.
17. **Theological discussion** — Meribald discusses the Seven Who Are One, the Smith, and the Cobbler aspect; reveals his past sins with maidens; explains going barefoot as penance.
18. **Discussion of the great wolf pack** — Meribald describes a monstrous she-wolf leading a pack of hundreds along the Trident.
19. **Cold camp in the dunes** — No fire; Podrick sent for driftwood finds only mudflats; Meribald warns about dangerous mud.
20. **Second day of travel** — They reach a stilt-house village near midday; Meribald conducts a service, absolves sins, distributes food.
21. **Warning of broken men** — Villagers report three broken men skulking near an old watchtower west of the village.
22. **Broken men speech** — Meribald delivers an extended monologue distinguishing broken men from outlaws, describing how war breaks common soldiers.
23. **Meribald's revelation** — He reveals he was marched off to the War of the Ninepenny Kings as a boy, no older than Podrick; his brothers and friends died.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Exhausted and humiliated by Tarly's abuse; disturbed by nightmares of the men she's killed; wary and distrustful of Hyle Hunt; quietly devout (silent prayer to the Crone and Warrior); uncomfortable when Meribald discusses deflowering maidens (recalls the wager at Highgarden); moved to silence after the broken men speech.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding Sansa Stark; following the lead on the Hound to Saltpans; distrust of Hyle Hunt's motives.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses Tarly's order to go home; decides to follow the Saltpans lead rather than going north; reluctantly allows Hyle Hunt to travel with them; joins Septon Meribald's route.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself "Words are wind... They cannot hurt you. Let them wash over you" about Tarly's abuse, but she walks away "like one asleep" suggesting she is deeply affected. Tells herself she doesn't need Hunt's help, but accepts his intelligence and companionship.
