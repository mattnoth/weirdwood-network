# AFFC — Jaime II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 17
- **pov_character:** Jaime Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jaime II
- **first_available:** AFFC Jaime II
- **location_primary:** King's Landing; road departing via Gate of the Gods
- **location_secondary:** Red Keep, Maegor's Holdfast (Cersei's solar), White Sword Tower, outer yard
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Tywin Lannister's funeral preparations; after Tommen's wedding; after the burning of the Tower of the Hand
- **time_markers:** The procession departs in daytime; "Evenfall found him sitting alone" marks the transition to the final scene; autumn sunlight referenced; Lancel described as looking older; Aurane Waters stated to be two-and-twenty
- **chapter_summary:** Jaime rides with the funeral procession of Lord Tywin as far as the Gate of the Gods, attempting unsuccessfully to reconcile Ser Kevan with Cersei and exchanging barbs with his cousin Lancel. Returning to the Red Keep, he watches knights at the quintain, then meets with Cersei, who is drinking wine and gleeful about Bronn naming Lollys's newborn son "Tyrion." Jaime and Cersei argue about her choices for council positions — she dismisses Kevan, favors Aurane Waters, Lord Hallyne, and Qyburn — and she furiously ejects him when he presses the point. At evenfall, Jaime sits in White Sword Tower reading the White Book, and discusses the histories of past Kingsguard members with Ser Loras Tyrell, ending on Criston Cole the Kingmaker.

- **Weather:** Wind snapping at banners; Jaime's white cloak snapping in the wind
- **Season indicators:** Autumn sunlight referenced when Cersei took Tommen to visit the camps; season of harvest — men returning home to "bring in one last harvest"
- **Time of day:** Daytime for the procession and return; evenfall for the White Sword Tower scene
- **Lighting:** Autumn sunlight; memory of green wildfire light from the burning of the Tower of the Hand at night; evening light in the common room
- **Sounds:** Slow, measured, funereal drum beating — "Dead, it seemed to say, dead, dead"; crimson pennons fluttering; wind snapping banners and cloaks
- **Smells:** None described
- **Notable sensory details:** Jaime feels his phantom fingers itching when he sees Lancel; his missing sword hand twitching after Kevan's departure; his head pounding to the same beat as the funeral drum

## Events & Actions
1. **Tywin's funeral procession departs King's Landing** — Lord Tywin's bones leave the city through the Gate of the Gods in a tall wagon draped with crimson banners, attended by six silent sisters, an honor guard of fifty knights, the lords of the west in crimson cloaks, a hundred crossbowmen, and three hundred men-at-arms.
2. **Jaime rides up to speak with Ser Kevan** — Jaime attempts to reconcile Kevan with Cersei. Kevan refuses, citing Cersei's rejection of his terms. Kevan states his place is at Darry with his son Lancel and says Cersei has left him nothing else to do.
3. **Kevan mentions Sandor Clegane's raiding** — Reports that Sandor Clegane is raiding along the Trident; Cersei wants his head; he may have joined Dondarrion. Jaime suggests Randyll Tarly deal with the outlaws and urges Kevan toward Riverrun instead.
4. **Jaime offers Kevan the role of Hand** — Jaime argues none of the other lords would make as good a King's Hand. Kevan tells Jaime Cersei knows his terms and makes a pointed remark about Jaime being in Cersei's bedchamber, then gallops away.
5. **Jaime processes Kevan's knowledge** — Jaime realizes Kevan knows about the incestuous relationship between Jaime and Cersei, and about Tommen and Myrcella's true parentage. He fears Cersei might have Kevan killed.
6. **Jaime exchanges barbs with Lancel** — Jaime congratulates Lancel on his upcoming marriage with bawdy remarks. Lancel responds stiffly and piously, praying for Jaime and Cersei. Jaime internally dismisses Tyrion's claim about Cersei and Lancel.
7. **Jaime returns to King's Landing through deserted streets** — The city is emptying: Garlan Tyrell took half the Tyrell forces to Highgarden with his mother and grandmother; Mace Tyrell and Mathis Rowan marched south to invest Storm's End; the westermen have gone home to harvest.
8. **Two thousand Lannister veterans remain outside the walls** — They await Paxter Redwyne's fleet to carry them across Blackwater Bay to Dragonstone, which Stannis left with only a small garrison.
9. **Jaime watches knights at the quintain** — In the outer yard, he observes two dozen knights practicing. He reflects that he can no longer joust. Ser Loras outshines all the others. Jaime acknowledges Loras's skill may explain how Loras once unhorsed him.
10. **Cersei reveals Lollys's baby is named Tyrion** — In Cersei's solar, Pycelle delivers news from Stokeworth: Lollys has had a son, and Bronn named the child Tyrion. Cersei is furious. Lady Merryweather and Tommen are present.
11. **Discussion of "gifts" for the baby** — Tommen suggests a kitten, Lady Merryweather suggests a lion cub (with menacing implication), and Cersei hints at something darker — Jaime thinks she means a new stepfather (i.e., having Bronn killed and replaced).
12. **Jaime recalls the burning of the Tower of the Hand** — He remembers Cersei's ecstatic expression during the burning, which reminded him of Aerys Targaryen's arousal at burnings.
13. **Jaime recalls Aerys's cruelty to Queen Rhaella** — Whenever Aerys burned someone, he would visit Rhaella that night. Jaime and Jon Darry stood guard while Aerys hurt Rhaella. Darry told Jaime they were sworn to protect her "but not from him." Rhaella later departed for Dragonstone looking savaged.
14. **Jaime recalls Aerys's physical decay** — The Mad King's matted beard, silver-gold tangled hair to his waist, cracked yellow fingernails nine inches long, and constant wounds from the Iron Throne's blades.
15. **Jaime and Cersei argue privately** — Jaime criticizes her choices: the Kettleblacks, Qyburn, Lady Merryweather. He warns Taena is spying for Margaery; Cersei reveals she is deliberately using Taena as a double agent. Cersei tells Jaime about Olenna Tyrell cheating tradesmen with old lighter-weight gold coins.
16. **Cersei dismisses Kevan and discusses replacements** — She considers Orton Merryweather or Lord Hallyne for Hand, and Aurane Waters for master of ships. Jaime argues for Paxter Redwyne instead. Cersei refuses anyone connected to the Tyrells.
17. **Cersei furiously ejects Jaime** — When Jaime persists in criticizing her judgment, Cersei accuses him of refusing to help when she begged him, references his broken vows and killing Aerys, calls his stump ugly, and hurls her wine cup at his head.
18. **Jaime reads the White Book at evenfall** — Sitting alone in White Sword Tower, turning pages with his stump.
19. **Jaime and Loras discuss Kingsguard history** — Loras names only the famous heroes; Jaime tests him on forgotten Kingsguard members. He makes the point that most brave men are forgotten, and ends by showing Loras the page on Criston Cole, the Kingmaker.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Conflicted, frustrated, and increasingly alarmed. Jaime feels out of place among the red-cloaked westermen in his white cloak. He is disturbed by Kevan's knowledge of the incest, anxious about Cersei's potential ruthlessness, and angry at Tyrion's words haunting him ("fucking Lancel and Osmund Kettleblack and Moon Boy for all I know" — recurring twice). His memories of Aerys and Rhaella fill him with disquiet. The argument with Cersei leaves him ejected and rebuffed.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Tyrion's parting words about Cersei's infidelities torment him — he tries to dismiss them but cannot. He is also deeply concerned about Cersei's governance choices and her growing paranoia and drinking, comparing her unfavorably to their father ("Lord Tywin with teats, but she was wrong") and disturbingly to Aerys Targaryen.
- **Key decisions made:** He chose to ride with the funeral procession to make his farewells to his father rather than as Cersei's messenger. He decided to try to reconcile Kevan with Cersei (failed). He chose not to press the argument with Cersei further after she ejected him.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jaime tells himself "The Imp was lying. Cersei would sooner have Robert's corpse between her legs than a pious fool like Lancel" — actively dismissing Tyrion's revelation even as it clearly haunts him. He also clings to the belief that Cersei would not harm Kevan ("He could not believe that she would ever do him harm") even as he immediately imagines exactly how she might arrange it through the Hound.
