- **approximate_timeline:** After Prince Oberyn's death, after the Sand Snakes' imprisonment by Prince Doran, before the planned move to the Water Gardens
- **time_markers:** "unseasonably cool, even for autumn"; nighttime throughout; "a fortnight past" a trader was killed; "once evening fell"; "in a fortnight we shall be safely away"; Arianne was four-and-ten when she found Doran's letter; "it had been ten years since" Arys took the white; Myrcella is "not quite one-and-ten"; Trystane is "three-and-ten"; Arianne and Tyene are "of an age"; the Bastard of Godsgrace had Arianne's maidenhead when both were fourteen; Oberyn visited Water Gardens "twice a fortnight," Arianne summoned "twice a year"
- **chapter_summary:** Ser Arys Oakheart, a Kingsguard knight protecting Princess Myrcella in Dorne, walks disguised through Sunspear's hostile shadow city to a secret assignation with Princess Arianne Martell. After their encounter, Arys attempts to end the affair out of guilt and duty, but Arianne gradually turns the conversation toward succession politics, arguing that Myrcella is the rightful heir over Tommen. Arianne reveals that her brother Quentyn has secretly crossed the narrow sea and that the Golden Company has broken its contract with Myr, implying a conspiracy against her inheritance. Arys swears his sword to crown Myrcella and defend Arianne's birthright.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arys walks through the shadow city at night** — Disguised in Dornish robes with his hood up, Arys makes his way through the alleys of Sunspear's shadow city, wary of the hostility toward those from King's Landing.
2. **Arys reflects on anti-outsider violence** — He recalls a trader from King's Landing being butchered in the shadow city a fortnight past, and that Dornish hostility has increased since Prince Oberyn's death, though it calmed somewhat after the Sand Snakes were imprisoned.
3. **Arys passes through the evening shadow city** — He hears music and finger drums; a pillow girl calls down from a balcony; a short man grills snake on a brazier.
4. **Flashback: Arys leaves Myrcella playing cyvasse** — He left Myrcella in her chambers in the Tower of the Sun, playing cyvasse opposite Prince Trystane. He reflects on their contrasting appearances and prays she finds more joy than Cersei found with Robert.
5. **Arys reflects on Myrcella's security** — Two doors to her chambers, each guarded by two Lannister household guards; she has maids, Septa Eglantine, and Trystane's sworn shield Ser Gascoyne.
6. **Flashback: Arys's audience with Prince Doran** — Doran apologizes for the delay in receiving them, confirms Arianne has been making Arys welcome, discusses the danger to Myrcella from the Dornish populace, references the murder of Elia's daughter Rhaenys, and proposes taking Myrcella to the Water Gardens. Doran's hands shake; Arys realizes the prince is terrified.
7. **Doran urges secrecy about the move** — He asked Arys not to write King's Landing about the move to the Water Gardens; Arys agreed despite reservations.
8. **Arys arrives at Arianne's secret room** — He finds the room past a candlemaker's shop, through a gate, up worn steps; the room has Myrish carpets, a tapestry of Nymeria, a featherbed, and scented candles.
9. **Arys and Arianne have sex** — Arianne appears wearing only the ornate snake armlet; they have a passionate sexual encounter; she scratches his back deeply enough to draw blood.
10. **Arys experiences shame and guilt** — After the encounter, he stares at the ceiling crack, reflects on his shame at breaking his Kingsguard vows, and resolves to end the affair.
11. **Arys attempts to leave and end the relationship** — He refuses wine, flinches from Arianne's touch, begins dressing. She protests, teases about other women and Sand Snakes, questions whether he truly wants to leave.
12. **Discussion of historical Kingsguard and their lovers** — Arys mentions Boros Blount and Preston Greenfield's secret liaisons (internally), then speaks of Terrence Toyne and Lucamore the Lusty. Arianne counters with Prince Aemon the Dragonknight's alleged affair with Queen Naerys.
13. **Arianne reveals Prince Lewyn had a paramour** — She states her great-uncle Prince Lewyn Martell of the Kingsguard kept a paramour. Arys is shocked; the White Book has no record of it.
14. **Arianne challenges Arys about the Kingsguard's legacy** — She argues his dishonor comes from "the monsters you have served and the brutes you've called your brothers," not from their love.
15. **Discussion of Joffrey, Tommen, and Myrcella** — Arys admits Joffrey's cruelty (including commanding him to strike the Stark girl); they discuss Robert's legacy; Arys reflects that Myrcella is braver, brighter, and more confident than Tommen.
16. **Arianne argues Myrcella's claim to the throne** — She invokes Dornish succession law, the precedent of Rhaenyra and Criston Cole, and suggests Arys was sent to Dorne so "one white knight might make right what another set awry."
17. **Arianne reveals her fear of her father** — She claims Doran would imprison her as he imprisoned the Sand Snakes; says Loreza (six) and Dorea (eight) are imprisoned with their sisters; describes her closeness with Tyene.
18. **Arianne reveals the letter about Quentyn** — She tells Arys she found a letter from Doran to Quentyn when she was fourteen, in which Doran wrote that Quentyn would "sit where I sit and rule all Dorne."
19. **Arianne reveals Quentyn has crossed the narrow sea** — She claims friends have told her Quentyn is not with Lord Yronwood in the Boneway but has secretly crossed the narrow sea posing as a merchant.
20. **Arianne reveals the Golden Company has broken its contract** — She notes the Golden Company has broken its contract with Myr, unprecedented given their reputation; she links this to Quentyn's mission and Lord Yronwood's Blackfyre connections.
21. **Arianne invokes House Toland's sigil** — A dragon eating its own tail; "the dragon is time" and "all things come round again"; she compares Anders Yronwood to Criston Cole reborn.
22. **Arys swears to crown Myrcella** — He sinks to one knee and pledges his sword, life, and honor to defending Myrcella's rights and Arianne's birthright.
23. **Arianne asks for Myrcella** — After accepting Arys's pledge, she says "first" she needs Myrcella, implying a concrete plan to crown the princess.

- **Emotional state:** Deep conflict between duty and desire; shame and guilt over breaking Kingsguard vows; fear for his honor; sexual desire overwhelming his resolve; growing protective/paternal feelings toward Myrcella; unease in the shadow city; admiration and helplessness before Arianne
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether to end his affair with Arianne vs. being unable to resist her; his Kingsguard oaths and what they mean; keeping Myrcella safe
- **Key decisions made:** Initially resolves to end the affair; ultimately abandons that resolve and swears to crown Myrcella, pledging sword, life, and honor to both Arianne and Myrcella's cause
