# AFFC — Samwell III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 27
- **pov_character:** Samwell Tarly
- **pov_chapter_number:** Samwell III
- **first_available:** AFFC Samwell III
- **location_primary:** Braavos (inn room, streets, Ragman's Harbor, the Happy Port)
- **location_secondary:** Purple Harbor, Moon Pool, Ragman's Harbor, the Mummer's Ship (across from the Happy Port)
- **approximate_timeline:** Mid-book; Sam's party has been stranded in Braavos for some time after missing their ship (the Lady Ushanora departed days earlier)
- **time_markers:** Sunset/gloaming at chapter opening; Titan sounds nightfall; night throughout the chapter; "days since they'd last had a fire"; the Lady Ushanora left "days and days ago"; Maester Aemon is 102 years old; they crossed the narrow sea through autumn gales; fourth or fifth time having the conversation about firewood
- **chapter_summary:** Sam waits in vain for Dareon to return to their inn with food and wine. Maester Aemon, gravely ill, wakes and urges Sam to go to the ships and learn about rumors of dragons. Sam ventures out into Braavos at night, searches multiple establishments for Dareon, is threatened by two bravos but rescued by a girl called Cat. He finds Dareon at the Happy Port, where the singer has "married" the Sailor's Wife and declares himself done with the Night's Watch. Sam punches Dareon, is thrown into a canal and nearly drowns, but is rescued by Xhondo, a Summer Islander who claims to know about dragons.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sam watches sunset from window** — Waits for Dareon who promised to return before gloaming with wine and food.
2. **Gilly speaks about Dareon** — She whispers that Dareon doesn't like it with them because it's sad; he prefers wine and smiles.
3. **The Titan sounds nightfall** — Hollow rumbling across the lagoon wakes the babe and then Maester Aemon.
4. **Aemon wakes confused** — Calls out "Egg? It's dark. Why is it so dark?" His wits are wandering.
5. **Sam identifies himself to Aemon** — Aemon recognizes him, asks if it's morning, remembers they are in Braavos.
6. **Aemon asks about the cold** — Sam explains they have no wood and the innkeep demands coin.
7. **Sam recalls spending their last silver** — Paid a healer from the House of the Red Hands who gave only dreamwine and said death was in Aemon's lungs.
8. **Aemon insists they must go to the ships** — Wants to learn about the dragon rumors Dareon brought back.
9. **Sam gives Aemon canal water** — Eases the old man up; half the water dribbles down his chest.
10. **Aemon speaks of death and his family** — Mentions his brothers, his father who never expected the throne, fears about what lies beyond death.
11. **Aemon speaks of dragons and prophecy** — Describes seeing dragons in dreams, a red star bleeding in the sky, white shadows, leathern wings; says his brothers dreamed of dragons and it killed them.
12. **Aemon tells Sam to go to the ships** — Says "Fire consumes, but cold preserves"; regrets leaving the Wall; asks Sam to learn about the dragons as a last service.
13. **Sam departs the inn** — Buckles on swordbelt, takes his cloak, goes out the street door to avoid the innkeep.
14. **Sam searches multiple establishments** — Checks the Inn of the Green Eel, the Black Bargeman, Moroggo's, the Foghouse, Nabbo's Bridge winesink, the Outcast Inn, the House of Seven Lamps, and the Cattery. Dareon is at none of them.
15. **Sam encounters two bravos** — Terro and his fair-haired friend threaten Sam outside the Cattery, mock his weight and his black clothing.
16. **Cat intervenes** — A barrow girl with a knife drives off the bravos; tells Sam wearing a sword invites challenges.
17. **Cat gives Sam information** — Tells him there's another man in black at the Happy Port singing to the Sailor's Wife; gives him her last clams; says she's "no one" but he can call her Cat.
18. **Cat tells Sam the Lady Ushanora is gone** — Sam already knows; he and Dareon watched it leave.
19. **Sam recalls the Lady Ushanora departing** — The captain refused to wait for Aemon and refused to return their passage money.
20. **Sam runs to the Happy Port** — Runs over slick cobblestones, puffing, cloak flapping, holding his swordbelt.
21. **Yna grabs Sam at the Happy Port** — A one-eyed woman throws her arms around him; Sam pushes her away.
22. **Dareon greets Sam** — Calls him "Sam the Slayer"; introduces his "lady wife."
23. **Sam confronts Dareon** — Tells him he can't marry because he said the words; argues he needs to come help with Aemon and the dragon inquiry.
24. **Dareon refuses and declares himself done with the Night's Watch** — Says he's done with black; tosses his cloak at Sam; says he'll be in velvet soon.
25. **Sam punches Dareon** — Hits him in the mouth, tackles him over a table, pummels him, headbutts him, breaks his lip, smashes his nose.
26. **Sam is thrown into the canal** — Someone drags Sam off Dareon; something hard hits Sam's head; he is thrown out into the fog and lands in the canal.
27. **Sam nearly drowns** — Sinks; swordbelt tangles his ankles; panics; cannot get grip on slimy canal walls.
28. **Xhondo rescues Sam** — A big Summer Islander dives in, coils something around Sam, hauls him out, pounds water from his belly.
29. **Xhondo claims to know about the dragons** — After rescuing Sam, says he saw Sam punch the singer, and declares "Xhondo knows these dragons."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious, frustrated, guilty, angry, desperate, afraid. Moves from worried waiting through righteous anger (punching Dareon) to panic (drowning) to relief.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Caring for Maester Aemon; finding Dareon; guilt over squandering their silver; fear of Braavos at night; anger at Dareon's desertion of duty.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to go out and search for Dareon despite his fear; decides to punch Dareon when Dareon declares himself done with the Watch; decides not to touch his sword when confronted by bravos.
- **Self-deception flags:** "I am still a craven" — Sam repeatedly calls himself a craven despite acting bravely (going out alone at night, standing up to Dareon). He avoids asking Gilly whose child she nurses because he fears the answer. He wants to believe Jon didn't do what Aemon thinks (switching the babies).
