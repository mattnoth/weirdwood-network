# AFFC — Samwell I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 6
- **pov_character:** Samwell Tarly
- **pov_chapter_number:** Samwell I
- **first_available:** AFFC Samwell I
- **location_primary:** Castle Black
- **location_secondary:** Vaults/wormways beneath Castle Black, the armory (Jon's solar), the rookery, the lichyard
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Jon Snow's election as Lord Commander; Stannis Baratheon is in residence at Castle Black; autumn; ravens have been sent to northern lords but few have responded
- **time_markers:** Sam's tallow candle nearly consumed (hours of reading); "Had the bean-and-bacon soup been today or yesterday? Yesterday"; daylight upon emerging from the vaults; departure set for "an hour before first light"; "the hour before dawn was dark and still"; "the eastern sky was more grey than black" at departure; light snow falling at departure
- **chapter_summary:** Sam has been researching the Others in the vaults beneath Castle Black and reports his findings to Jon Snow, now Lord Commander. Jon signs a letter to King Tommen requesting aid for the Night's Watch, then orders Sam to travel to Oldtown with Maester Aemon, Gilly, and her babe so Sam can train at the Citadel to become a maester. Sam protests out of fear of his father and of the chain, but Jon commands obedience. The next morning, the party departs Castle Black for Eastwatch, escorted by Black Jack Bulwer and a dozen rangers.

- **Weather:** Sky the color of white lead ("a snow sky"); a snowflake lands on Sam's nose during conversation with Edd; light snow begins falling during departure with "big soft flakes drifting down lazily from the sky"
- **Season indicators:** Autumn referenced ("autumn is a stormy season")
- **Time of day:** Daylight when Sam emerges from vaults; main events during the day; departure the next morning an hour before sunrise; "the hour before dawn was dark and still"; eastern sky shifting from black to grey at departure
- **Lighting:** Fat tallow candle nearly consumed in the vaults; "a pale shaft of light illuminated the steep stone steps" leading to the surface; daylight above; candle used to heat sealing wax in Jon's solar
- **Sounds:** Saws and hammers echoing off the Wall (stair construction); raven crying "Corn, corn!" and later "Blood" and "Snow, snow, snow"; whip snap at departure
- **Smells:** Dust everywhere in the vaults, filling the air with every turned page
- **Notable sensory details:** Dust making Sam's eyes red, raw, itchy, and watery; pins and needles in Sam's calves after long sitting; hard chair cutting into the back of his thighs; gusts of wind strong enough to stagger Maester Aemon; cold (Pyp's ears red from cold); Sam puffing by the fifth step climbing stairs

## Events & Actions
1. **Sam reads about the Others in the vaults** — He has been reading for hours by candlelight, going through stacks of old books and scrolls, his eyes red and raw from dust.
2. **Sam sees a mouse eating his bread crumbs** — A small grey-furred, black-eyed mouse feasts on crumbs from his empty platter. Sam considers killing it with a heavy book but fails when the book slips from his plump fingers.
3. **Sam emerges from the vaults** — He climbs the steep stone steps into daylight, carrying books and scrolls, puffing by the fifth step.
4. **Sam encounters Dolorous Edd** — Edd tells Sam that Jon wants to see him. Edd mentions Jon is currently with "Craster's wife" (Gilly).
5. **Sam encounters Pyp and Grenn** — They carry longbows and tease Sam about missing archery practice and about Val watching from the King's Tower. Pyp calls him "Slayer."
6. **Sam heads to the armory** — He leaves his friends, clutching books, heading to Jon's solar behind the armory.
7. **Sam encounters Gilly leaving Jon's quarters** — Gilly is upset, nearly rushing past. She says the babes are good but her eyes fill with tears. She mentions Dalla's boy cries more than hers.
8. **Sam drops books and retrieves them** — He drops two books catching Gilly's arm; the Jade Compendium is undamaged but Dragonkin gets muddy pages, including the Balerion illustration.
9. **Sam enters Jon's solar** — Hairy Hal and Mully are guarding the door. Ghost lies beneath the anvil. Mormont's raven pecks Sam's hand, drawing blood.
10. **Jon and Sam discuss the letter to Tommen** — The letter, in Aemon's hand, asks the Iron Throne for aid. Jon is reluctant to send it, fearing Lannisters will see them as Stannis's allies. Sam argues a "paper shield" is better than none.
11. **Jon signs and seals the letter** — He scrawls his signature and presses the Lord Commander's seal in black wax. Orders Sam to give it to Aemon for dispatch to King's Landing.
12. **Jon reveals Gilly's visit was about Mance** — Val sent Gilly to plead for Mance Rayder's life. Jon says he'll speak to Stannis but doubts success.
13. **Jon discusses Melisandre's plans** — King's blood to "wake a dragon." Jon dismisses Mance's blood as having no royal power. He says he is sending Gilly away.
14. **Sam reports research findings on the Others** — Obsidian kills them; their armor resists ordinary blades; their swords shatter steel; fire dismays them; they come with cold and darkness; they ride dead animals; the dead must be burned. He mentions "dragonsteel" from an old account.
15. **Jon and Sam discuss dragonsteel** — Both suspect it means Valyrian steel. Jon notes the impracticality of collecting Valyrian steel blades from the lords.
16. **Sam reports on the historical record** — The oldest lord commander list shows 674 commanders, not the traditional 998. Osric Stark was LC at age ten. The oldest histories were written by septons after the Andals came.
17. **Jon orders Sam to Oldtown** — Sam is to travel with Gilly, her babe, and Maester Aemon. Dareon joins at Eastwatch. The Blackbird takes them to Braavos; from there they arrange passage to Oldtown. Sam is to forge a maester's chain.
18. **Sam protests the order** — He cites his fear of blood, corpses, and chains. He invokes his father Lord Randyll's prohibition against Tarlys wearing chains. He stammers and panics.
19. **Jon commands obedience** — "You have no father. Only brothers. Only us." Jon orders Sam not to call himself a craven and to hide his fears.
20. **Sam goes to Maester Aemon** — He delivers the letter and voices his fears. Aemon shares that his own father objected when he chose the Citadel, but his grandfather King Daeron overruled it.
21. **Sam teaches ravens to say "Snow"** — The ravens in the rookery repeat "Snow, snow, snow" — Sam had taught them the word.
22. **Next morning: Sam prepares to depart** — He saddles the mare he rode from Horn Hill, packs provisions including Hobb's ham.
23. **The party assembles at the lichyard** — Two wayns, Black Jack Bulwer and a dozen rangers. Kedge Whiteye lost a wager about whether Sam would come willingly.
24. **Maester Aemon is loaded into a wayn** — Bundled in bearskin; Sam helps him; wind gusts nearly blow him over. Aemon reminisces about an inn on the Honeywine. Gilly and her babe ride with him.
25. **Aemon gives Jon a message about the Jade Compendium** — Left it in his chambers with a passage marked by Clydas. "Knowledge is a weapon, Jon. Arm yourself well before you ride forth to battle."
26. **Gilly makes Jon promise about Dalla's boy** — Find a wet nurse, don't name him until he's past two (ill luck while still on the breast). She insists she is "a mother, not a lady."
27. **The party departs Castle Black** — The wayns rumble down the rutted road in falling snow. Sam says farewell to Clydas, Edd, and Jon. Jon tells Sam to pull his hood up; snowflakes melting in his hair.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Scholarly absorption gives way to anxiety and dread. Sam is terrified of the sea voyage, of chains, of his father, of blood and corpses. He cycles through resistance, pleading, and despairing resignation. At departure, he is torn — hating Castle Black yet devastated to leave it.
- **Primary preoccupation:** First, the Others and his research. Then, overwhelming fear of being sent to the Citadel and what his father Lord Randyll would say. The word "maester" makes him flinch; the thought of wearing a chain triggers traumatic memories of being chained to a wall for three days.
- **Key decisions made:** Ultimately obeys Jon's command despite extreme reluctance. Plans to claim Gilly's babe as his bastard and send her to Horn Hill with his mother and sisters. Decides to send a letter rather than go to Horn Hill himself.
- **Self-deception flags:** Downplays his eating ("that was only a nibble" about bread and cheese, while the platter is empty). Briefly fantasizes about hiding in the vaults among the books or fleeing beyond the Wall, then recognizes these as "crazed thoughts." Tells himself Bran's death is Jon's belief but cannot speak the truth he knows.
