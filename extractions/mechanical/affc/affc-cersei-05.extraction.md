# AFFC — Cersei V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 25
- **pov_character:** Cersei Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Cersei V
- **first_available:** AFFC Cersei V
- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** White Harbor (reported), Storm's End (reported), Lannisport (memory), Greenstone (memory), Stokeworth (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Mid-AFFC; Tommen reigns, Tywin recently dead, dromonds nearing completion, High Septon not yet chosen, sparrows active in King's Landing
- **time_markers:** "the rest of the morning" with Lord Gyles; "midday meal" with merchants; "that afternoon" the Braavosi envoy arrives; Cersei had put off Noho Dimittis "for a fortnight"; "outside, the sun was setting" when Cersei bathes; "past midnight" when Falyse and Balman finally leave; Cersei checks on Tommen "at that hour" (after midnight); tourney at Lannisport in memory when Cersei was ten; Rhaegar was seventeen and newly knighted at that tourney; Aron Santagar killed (prior event); Tommen described as "almost nine"
- **chapter_summary:** Cersei suppresses Tommen's desire to sit the Iron Throne and attend councils, blaming Margaery's influence. She handles court business — finances with Lord Gyles, dromond construction with Aurane Waters, merchant complaints about sparrows, the Braavosi envoy Noho Dimittis demanding Iron Bank debts be paid, and Pycelle's report that Wyman Manderly has allegedly executed Davos Seaworth. She learns from Osmund Kettleblack that Osney is failing to seduce Margaery, who is never alone. After confronting Loras Tyrell for teaching Tommen to joust, she resolves to appoint a Dornish master-at-arms. Qyburn reports foreign intelligence and a treasonous puppet show. Jaime brings Tommen to argue about jousting and calls Cersei "a beautiful golden fool." She hosts Falyse Stokeworth and Ser Balman Byrch, manipulating them into agreeing to arrange Bronn's death. The chapter closes with Cersei's memories of Rhaegar Targaryen and the tourney at Lannisport where Aerys refused Tywin's proposed betrothal.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tommen demands the Iron Throne** — Tommen tells Cersei he wants to sit on the Iron Throne and attend council meetings; Cersei denies him, threatens his whipping boy Pate, and extracts that Margaery put him up to it
2. **Morning with Lord Gyles** — Cersei spends the morning reviewing ledger books with Lord Gyles, listening to him cough about finances (stars, stags, and dragons — currency)
3. **Aurane Waters reports on dromonds** — Waters reports three dromonds nearing completion and begs for more gold to finish them; Cersei grants his request
4. **Midday meal with merchants** — Cersei dines with merchant guild members who complain about sparrows in the streets and squares
5. **Pycelle reports Davos's execution** — Grand Maester Pycelle reports that Wyman Manderly has beheaded Stannis's onion knight; head and hands mounted at White Harbor with an onion in the mouth; Freys confirm
6. **Cersei orders Manderly's son returned** — Cersei sends a raven to White Harbor to release Manderly's son as reward for loyalty
7. **Audience with Noho Dimittis** — Braavosi envoy demands payment of Iron Bank debts; Cersei deflects to Lord Gyles, then dismisses him, saying the Bank will be paid when the rebellion is put down
8. **Osmund reports on Osney's failure** — Ser Osmund tells Cersei that Osney cannot get Margaery alone; she is always attended by companions, cousins, ladies, guards, and male visitors
9. **Tommen jousts at the quintain** — Cersei crosses the yard and sees Tommen in gilded plate hitting the quintain under Loras's instruction; Margaery and her companions cheer
10. **Margaery embarrasses Cersei** — Cersei claims jousting is in Tommen's blood like his father; Margaery asks pointedly about Robert's jousting victories, catching Cersei out (Cersei was thinking of Jaime)
11. **Cersei confronts Loras** — On the serpentine steps, Cersei forbids Loras from training Tommen, citing that Kingsguard duties don't include playing with children; she insults him about Renly
12. **Cersei decides on Dornish master-at-arms** — She resolves to send to Dorne for a master-at-arms, reasoning that Dornish-Highgarden enmity serves her purposes
13. **Qyburn's intelligence reports** — Qyburn reports: (a) Tyrosh-Lys trade war possibly ending, Myr backing off without Golden Company; (b) slave revolt spreading from Astapor to Meereen, sailors speaking of dragons; (c) Doran imprisoned Daemon Sand; (d) Knight of Spottswood's daughter hastily married to old Lord Estermont; both Sand and the girl close to Arianne Martell
14. **Treasonous puppet show** — Qyburn reports a puppet show where lions devour subjects and a stag, then a dragon hatches and devours the lions; Cersei orders surveillance, fines for men of substance, eye-removal for the poor, and execution for the puppeteers
15. **Qyburn requests more subjects** — Qyburn asks for two of the four puppeteers, especially a woman, for his purposes; Cersei notes Senelle is "quite exhausted" and agrees
16. **Cersei's bath interrupted by Jaime** — Jaime bursts in with Tommen, ordering handmaids out; Tommen demands his white courser for jousting; Cersei forbids it until a proper master-at-arms is named
17. **Tommen defies Cersei** — Tommen invokes his kingship, saying Margaery told him everyone must do what the king says; demands jousting, a kitten, and refuses to eat beets; Jaime laughs throughout
18. **Cersei and Jaime argue** — After Tommen leaves, Jaime calls Cersei "drunk or merely stupid"; reports the city walls are sound but reminds her Tyrells are inside the walls; Cersei thinks of the Highgarden coin found under Rugen's chamber pot
19. **Jaime calls Cersei a fool** — "I love you too, sweet sister. But you're a fool. A beautiful golden fool." Cersei thinks of Greenstone and the night Joffrey was conceived; orders him to leave
20. **Cersei dresses for supper** — Dorcas helps Cersei into a new gown of green satin and black velvet with Myrish lace; Cersei admires herself, reflects on Taena and dead Melara Hetherspoon
21. **Supper with Falyse and Balman** — Cersei manipulates the Stokeworths: discusses Tanda's broken hip (saddle girth burst), feigns fear of Tyrion, implies Bronn is sheltering the Imp and gathering swords; maneuvers Balman into agreeing to arrange Bronn's death during "a mishap whilst hunting"
22. **Cersei promises reward** — Promises Lollys a better husband and invokes "We Lannisters pay our debts"
23. **Cersei checks on Tommen** — Past midnight, finds three black kittens sleeping with Tommen, gifts from Margaery; Ser Meryn reports Margaery gave them; Tommen couldn't choose one
24. **Cersei reflects on Rhaegar** — Black kittens trigger memory of Rhaegar's daughter and black cats; leads to extended reverie about the tourney at Lannisport, Rhaegar in black plate, Cersei at age ten seeing him, Aerys refusing Tywin's betrothal proposal, visiting Maggy the Frog's tent
25. **Cersei's closing thought** — "Lions were not good at forgiving. As Ser Bronn of the Blackwater would shortly learn."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Irritable, defensive, paranoid, and nostalgic. Cersei is frustrated by Margaery's growing influence over Tommen, resentful of those who don't obey her as they obeyed Tywin, and bitter about Jaime's failure to support her. Flashes of satisfaction when exerting power (granting Waters gold, sentencing puppeteers, manipulating Stokeworths). Deep nostalgic pain when remembering Rhaegar. Disgust at Qyburn's work with Senelle but willingness to enable it.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Maintaining control — over Tommen, over Margaery's influence, over the realm's finances and loyalty. Fear of losing power is the thread connecting every scene: she refuses Tommen the throne, forbids Loras from training him, plots Bronn's death, and rejects the Iron Bank's demands.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Refuses Tommen the Iron Throne and councils; (2) Grants Aurane Waters more gold for dromonds; (3) Orders Manderly's son returned; (4) Refuses Iron Bank payment until rebellion ends; (5) Forbids Loras from training Tommen; (6) Resolves to send to Dorne for a master-at-arms; (7) Orders surveillance and punishment for puppet shows; (8) Gives Qyburn two more subjects; (9) Manipulates Stokeworths into killing Bronn; (10) Promises Lollys a better husband
- **Self-deception flags:** Cersei attributes Tommen's defiance entirely to Margaery's manipulation rather than recognizing his growing independence. She tells Tommen jousting is "in your blood" referring to Jaime, not Robert, then is caught by Margaery — "It is not like me to forget myself." She dismisses reports of dragons in Meereen as "harpies" — a dangerous misjudgment. She believes she is a shrewd ruler like Tywin, but Jaime calls her "a beautiful golden fool." She claims to fear Tyrion while cynically performing that fear to manipulate the Stokeworths. She believes Osney desires her rather than Margaery — "It is me he's eager for, fool." She sees all resistance to her authority as stemming from her sex — "It is all because I am a woman."
