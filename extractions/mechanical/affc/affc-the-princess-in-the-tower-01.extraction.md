- **chapter_summary:** Arianne Martell is imprisoned near the top of the Spear Tower in Sunspear after her failed plot to crown Myrcella. She spends weeks in comfortable but silent captivity, grieving for the dead Ser Arys Oakheart and agonizing over who betrayed her plans. She manipulates the young servant Cedra into smuggling a letter to Lord Fowler, but Cedra is caught and removed. After falling into depression and refusing food, Arianne is finally summoned by her father Prince Doran, who reveals the fates of her co-conspirators, the approaching threat of Ser Balon Swann, and—most stunningly—that Arianne was secretly betrothed all along to a man who died from molten gold, and that her brother Quentyn has been sent on a perilous voyage to bring back "our heart's desire." Doran presses an onyx dragon cyvasse piece into her palm and whispers: "Vengeance. Justice. Fire and blood."

## Events & Actions
1. **Arianne transported from the Greenblood to Sunspear** — After capture, Hotah brings Arianne on a long dry ride. She pleads with him during the journey, invoking their shared history, but he refuses to discuss anything beyond his duty to obey Prince Doran.
2. **Arianne delivered to the Spear Tower** — Instead of being brought before Doran in the Tower of the Sun, she is given over to Ricasso (seneschal) and Ser Manfrey Martell (castellan). Ser Manfrey reveals the other conspirators were taken to Planky Town and will be shipped to Ghaston Grey.
3. **Arianne confined near the top of the Spear Tower** — Ser Manfrey marches her up the steps to a comfortable but isolating cell. She wonders if Sand Snakes might be in cells behind other doors.
4. **Arianne explores her cell** — Discovers the room's comforts: Myrish carpets, featherbed, wine, books, cyvasse table, privy with marble seat, east-facing and south-facing windows with views of the sea and Tower of the Sun.
5. **Arianne grieves for Ser Arys Oakheart** — Breaks down crying, remembering how Hotah's axe cleaved through him. She questions why he threw his life away.
6. **Arianne dreams of Arys** — He caresses her and tells her he loves her, but quarrels are in him and his wounds are weeping, turning his white cloak red.
7. **Arianne dresses in revealing clothing** — Deliberately chooses "wisps of silk that covered everything and hid nothing" to discomfit her father when he comes to chastise her.
8. **Arianne's first midday meal** — Servants bring kid roasted with lemon and honey, stuffed grape leaves. She refuses at first, thinking of her friends' meager food on the way to Ghaston Grey, but eventually eats.
9. **Days of silence and isolation** — Servants (Bors, Timoth, Morra, Mellei, Cedra, Belandra) tend to her needs but refuse to speak. Arianne tries repeatedly to extract information about Darkstar, Myrcella, and her father's intentions.
10. **Arianne attempts to escape** — Bolts past Belandra when the door opens, sending a platter of spiced eggs crashing against the wall. Guards catch her within three yards and drag her back.
11. **Arianne calls from the window at night** — Hangs out the window shouting for Tyene, Obara, Nym, Ellaria, spending half the night calling until her throat is raw. No one answers, frightening her.
12. **Arianne agonizes over the betrayer** — Mentally eliminates suspects: Garin, Drey, and Spotted Sylva are too dear; Darkstar's betrayal wouldn't explain his attack on Myrcella; she even wonders if Ser Arys's guilt prompted him to confess.
13. **Arianne manipulates Cedra** — Over several baths, she talks casually, then mentions Garin (Cedra's lover), then paints a vivid picture of Garin throwing himself from a cell window. After four more days and two more baths, Cedra agrees to smuggle a letter.
14. **Arianne writes a secret letter** — Composes it on the bottom of a page torn from The Seven-Pointed Star. Addresses it to Lord Fowler at Skyreach. Offers 100 silver stags for delivery and promises her hand in marriage to her rescuer.
15. **Cedra disappears** — Fails to return the next day or the day after. Morra and Mellei replace her. Arianne concludes Cedra was caught.
16. **Arianne asks to see Ricasso** — The seneschal ignores her summons. She pours a flagon of red wine over Timoth's head in frustration.
17. **Arianne falls into depression** — Stops rising from bed, refuses meals, prays to the Mother for mercy and the Warrior for courage, throws food out the window. Loses track of how many days she has been imprisoned.
18. **Hotah wakes Arianne** — Shakes her shoulder, says "The prince has called for you." He tells her Cedra was sent to the Water Gardens. Arianne requests a bath and light food.
19. **Arianne prepares for the meeting** — Bathes, eats sparingly (cheese, fruit, wine), dresses in a simple gown of ivory linen with vine and grape embroidery, no jewels. Realizes she is frightened of her father for the first time in her life.
20. **Arianne brought to Doran's solar at dusk** — Hotah escorts her not to the Tower of the Sun but to the prince's private solar.
21. **Doran speaks of Volantis** — Opens with an oblique reminiscence: "A strange and subtle folk, the Volantenes." He saw Volantis on his way to Norvos, where he met Mellario. Hotah confirms: "The bears danced and the bells rang."
22. **Doran dismisses Hotah** — Hotah stamps his longaxe and leaves.
23. **Doran asks Arianne about the cyvasse table** — Reveals he placed it deliberately. "Sometimes it is best to study a game before you attempt to play it." Admits cyvasse is not for him: "I only play such games as I can win."
24. **Doran demands to know why Arianne plotted** — "Tell me why." Arianne says "For the honor of our House" and accuses Doran of meekness that shames Dorne.
25. **Doran reveals Darkstar has not been captured** — "Would that we had. You were a fool to make him part of this."
26. **Doran reveals Myrcella's injuries** — Survived; her horse shied so Darkstar's slash missed the skull, but opened her cheek to the bone and sliced off her right ear. Maester Caleotte saved her life.
27. **Arianne and Doran argue about the plot's consequences** — Doran reveals Dorne is the least populous kingdom; the Young Dragon inflated their numbers and Dorne has fostered the myth. "Dorne cannot hope to win a war against the Iron Throne, not alone."
28. **Doran reveals the fates of the conspirators** — Ser Andrey Dalt: three years in Norvos serving Mellario. Garin: two years in Tyrosh. Coin and hostages taken from Garin's orphan kin. Lady Sylva: married to Lord Estermont at Greenstone.
29. **Arianne and Doran discuss Ser Arys** — Doran asks "what did you do to him?" Arianne bluntly admits "I fucked him." She recalls how the seduction took half a year and his shame consumed him.
30. **Doran reveals Ser Balon Swann's approach** — Another Kingsguard creeps toward Sunspear with the Mountain's head. Doran's bannermen have been systematically delaying him: the Wyls (8 days hunting and hawking on the Boneway), Lord Yronwood (a fortnight's feast), Lady Jordayne (games at the Tor), Lady Toland (at Ghost Hill next).
31. **Arianne proposes a cover story** — Suggests telling Ser Balon that Arys died defending Myrcella from Darkstar, which is how Kingsguard are supposed to die.
32. **Doran points out the story requires Myrcella's cooperation** — If Myrcella contradicts the story or dies, war is inevitable. Arianne realizes Doran needs her to coach Myrcella.
33. **Arianne confronts Doran about disinheriting her** — Reveals she read his letter to Quentyn: "One day you will sit where I sit and rule all Dorne." Demands to know when he decided to disinherit her. Cries despite her fury.
34. **Arianne accuses Doran of offering her only degrading matches** — Lists the elderly, infirm, and unsuitable suitors: Gyles Rosby, Ben Beesbury, Lord Grandison, Walder Frey.

She was promised; the pact was sealed in secret.

37. **Doran says Arianne will have Dorne** — "Dorne will be yours. You have my word on that." Quentyn "has a harder road to walk."
38. **Doran reveals details about Quentyn's mission** — Quentyn went with Cletus Yronwood, Maester Kedry, and three of Lord Yronwood's best young knights on "a long and perilous voyage, with an uncertain welcome at its end." He has gone "to bring us back our heart's desire."
39. **Doran reveals the Tyroshi connection** — The "green-haired girl" from Arianne's childhood at the Water Gardens was the Archon of Tyrosh's daughter. Arianne was to have been sent to Tyrosh in her place as a cupbearer, to meet her betrothed in secret, but Mellario threatened to harm herself.
40. **Doran's final revelation** — Presses the onyx dragon cyvasse piece into Arianne's palm and whispers: "Vengeance. Justice. Fire and blood."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Progresses through grief (for Arys), anger (at the betrayer), defiance (toward her father), desperation (manipulating Cedra), deep depression (refusing food, sleeping for days), fear ("for the first time in my life, I am frightened of my father"), and finally a volatile mixture of fury, hurt, and stunned recalibration when Doran reveals his secrets. She laughs, cries, and cycles between wanting to beg forgiveness and wanting to attack Doran.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Three interlocking obsessions: (1) identifying the betrayer — "Someone told" is the chapter's recurring refrain; (2) guilt and grief over Arys Oakheart's death and Myrcella's injury ("I never meant for harm to come to her"); (3) the conviction that Doran plans to disinherit her in favor of Quentyn, which drove the entire conspiracy.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to manipulate Cedra into smuggling a letter; chooses to write to Lord Fowler rather than Harmen Uller (too dangerous) or others (too weak); decides to dress revealingly to discomfit her father, then later switches to a demure gown when actually summoned; proposes the cover story for Ser Arys's death (he died defending Myrcella); confronts Doran about the letter to Quentyn rather than begging forgiveness.
- **Self-deception flags:** Arianne plans to weep and beg and believes "When he saw tears rolling down her face, he would forgive her" — treating emotion as a tool, then discovers she cannot bring herself to kneel. She claims "I never meant for harm to come to her" about Myrcella repeatedly, but her original plan to crown Myrcella carried inherent risks she failed to account for. She tells herself "He cannot mean to kill me" with a hundred repetitions that read as self-reassurance rather than certainty. She attributes Doran's silence entirely to cruelty or feebleness ("he thinks he is being subtle when he is only being feeble"), not considering it might be strategic.
