- **chapter_summary:** Jaime arrives at Castle Darry with his column of a thousand men, finding his cousin Lancel absent from the hall and deep in religious fervor. At the feast, Lady Amerei and Lady Mariya discuss the outlaw threat — a scarred, hooded woman leading the band that killed Merrett Frey, and the Hound's atrocities at Saltpans. Jaime visits Lancel in the sept, where Lancel confesses to having slept with Cersei and helped kill Robert, then announces he will renounce his lordship and join the Warrior's Sons. Jaime ends the night practicing swords with Ser Ilyn Payne in the godswood, confessing his own dark memories aloud.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at Darry** — Jaime's column approaches Castle Darry; fields outside are being tilled again after burning; armed peasants and workers flee inside the walls.
2. **Gates opened** — After the Horn of Herrock sounds, the castle gates eventually open. Jaime orders camp made beneath the western wall, ditched and staked against outlaws.
3. **Entry to castle** — Jaime rides through the gate with Ser Dermot (royal banner) and Ser Hugo Vance (Kingsguard standard). He observes crossbowmen, armed peasants, and sparrows with red seven-pointed stars.
4. **Greeted by Maester Ottomore** — Neither Kevan nor Lancel emerge; maester explains Kevan left after the wedding and Lancel is at prayers. Lady Amerei is preparing a feast.
5. **Shown to chambers** — Jaime recognizes Darry from prior visits with Robert and Cersei. He notes bare walls and recalls Tyrion discovering hidden Targaryen tapestries.
6. **Bath** — Little Lew removes boots and golden hand; Peck and Garrett haul water; Pia brings clean clothes. Jaime notes Pia's attractiveness and recalls her whispered words at Harrenhal and his bath with Brienne. He dismisses Pia and suggests Peck take her to bed.
7. **Feast in the great hall** — Jaime sits on the dais beside Lancel's empty chair. Lady Amerei explains Lancel is fasting for the dead High Septon.
8. **Discussion of outlaw threat** — Lady Amerei tells of her first husband Pate being killed by Gregor Clegane. Lady Mariya describes the hooded, scarred woman leading outlaws who killed Merrett Frey. The outlaws vanished into the Neck.
9. **Discussion of Saltpans** — Ser Arwood describes the massacre at Saltpans attributed to the Hound, including burning and rape. Jaime privately doubts it was Sandor's style.
10. **Strongboar pledges to hunt the Hound** — Offers to return after Riverrun to kill Beric and the Hound. Lady Amerei is thrilled.
11. **Jaime spills his wine** — Knocks his cup over with his golden hand; companions pretend not to notice. He interprets their courtesy as pity.
12. **Jaime seeks Lancel** — Leaves the feast despite Lady Amerei's protests.
13. **Sparrows in the yard** — Jaime observes ~100 sparrows cooking sausages at cookfires; thinks them "useless mouths."
14. **Confrontation with sparrows at sept** — Three sparrows (small bearded man, huge bald man with star, armed woman) block Jaime from entering. Lancel emerges and admits him.
15. **Conversation with Lancel** — Lancel speaks of finding faith, sleeping beneath altars, having visions, fasting. Claims he dreamed Jaime would come and kill him for his sins.
16. **Lancel confesses** — Admits to helping kill Robert (with wine) and sleeping with Cersei, though claims he never "spilled his seed in her womb." Says Tyrion made him betray Cersei.
17. **Jaime's internal reaction** — Wonders how many others Cersei slept with; recalls her boast of lying to him a thousand times at White Sword Tower.
18. **Lancel announces plan to join Warrior's Sons** — Will renounce lordship and wife, return to King's Landing, take vows with the revived military order under the new High Septon.
19. **Jaime departs sept** — Sarcastic thanks to the sparrows outside.
20. **Sword practice with Ser Ilyn** — In the godswood, Jaime confesses to Ser Ilyn about having sex with Cersei on Raymun Darry's bed while Robert slept, and Cersei's desire to have Arya Stark maimed or killed.
21. **Jaime challenges Ser Ilyn** — After realizing Ilyn is laughing at him, Jaime curses him and tells him to try to kill him.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Conflicted and bitter; aroused by Pia but checking himself with vows; contemptuous of Lancel's religious conversion yet disturbed by his confessions; darkly self-lacerating during the godswood scene; pity and irritation at being pitied (spilled wine)
- **Primary preoccupation:** Cersei's betrayals — Lancel's confession confirms "Lancel and Ser Osmund and how many more?"; processing the full scope of her deceptions; the memory of her boast about lying a thousand times
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to detour to Darry rather than going directly to Riverrun ("a darker need had brought him here"); dismisses Pia from bath to honor his vows; advises Peck to bed Pia kindly; chooses not to stay and fight outlaws ("My place is with the king")
- **Self-deception flags:** Claims Riverrun "will keep" and half-hopes the siege ends before he arrives (avoiding fighting Tullys); acknowledges his detour to Darry was driven by "a darker need" but doesn't fully articulate what; rationalizes his past actions at Darry (would have killed Robert, would have maimed Arya) as "the things I do for love"
