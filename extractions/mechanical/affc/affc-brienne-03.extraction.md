# AFFC — Brienne III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 15
- **pov_character:** Brienne
- **pov_chapter_number:** Brienne III
- **first_available:** AFFC Brienne III
- **location_primary:** Maidenpool
- **location_secondary:** The road to Maidenpool, the Stinking Goose, Maidenpool fishmarket, Maidenpool harbor
- **approximate_timeline:** After departing Duskendale, arriving at Maidenpool; Brienne and Podrick have been traveling together for days
- **time_markers:** "half a year" since the gate serjeant had an egg; "yesterday" they encountered one of Lord Randyll's patrols; "sun was half-hidden behind a bank of clouds" on arrival at Maidenpool; "most every night" Dick Crabb visits the Goose; "first light" as the agreed meeting time; "thirty years" since ships came to the Whispers; Brienne was betrothed at seven, the boy died two years later; Podrick's mother abandoned him when he was four; Dickon Tarly described as eight or ten years old; "a thousand years" since the Whispers was inhabited; "cool autumn air"
- **chapter_summary:** Brienne and Podrick travel the road to Maidenpool, encountering a farmer and his wife pulling an oxcart of eggs for a wedding. At Maidenpool's gates, Brienne intervenes when a gate serjeant tries to steal the eggs and seize the farmer's wife, and is recognized by Ser Hyle Hunt. Hyle brings her before Lord Randyll Tarly, who is dispensing harsh justice in the fishmarket. Tarly dismisses her quest contemptuously and reveals that Lysa Arryn is dead, pushed off a mountain by a singer, and that Littlefinger holds the Eyrie. Brienne then finds the Stinking Goose, a cellar tavern, where she negotiates with Dick Crabb, who tells her a frightened fool sought passage for three and was sent to a ruined keep called the Whispers on Crackclaw Point.

## Events & Actions
1. **Brienne searches for familiar landmarks** — She looks for the stone wall where Cleos Frey was killed and where she and Jaime fought, but the landscape has changed and she cannot identify the spots.
2. **Podrick asks what she's looking for** — Brienne tells him to stay quiet due to possible outlaws.
3. **Brienne and Podrick encounter a farmer and wife** — An old man and younger woman pull an oxcart of eggs toward Maidenpool.
4. **The farmer tells of wartime losses** — Wolves stole his ox and raped his daughter; she returned after the battle at Duskendale but the ox never did.
5. **The farmer praises Tarly's rule** — Says Tarly hunted down outlaws and "shortened them with that big sword."
6. **Brienne warns the farmer about the Hound** — Passes along the patrol captain's warning about the Hound crossing the Trident with a hundred outlaws.
7. **The farmer reveals Dickon Tarly's wedding** — Eggs are for wedding cakes for Mooton's daughter and Tarly's son.
8. **Brienne recalls her own betrothal** — Betrothed at seven to Lord Caron's younger son, who died of a chill; reflects on what her life might have been.
9. **Arrival at Maidenpool** — They find the town's gates rebuilt, crossbowmen on pink stone walls, banners of Tommen, Tarly, and Mooton flying.
10. **Gate serjeant confrontation** — The serjeant seizes the farmer's eggs and attempts to take his wife. Brienne draws her sword to intervene.
11. **Ser Hyle Hunt appears** — He steps from the gatehouse and defuses the confrontation, identifying Brienne as "Brienne the Beauty" and mocking her while letting the farm folk through.
12. **Brienne enters Maidenpool** — Observes the town's rebuilding: new houses, stone inn replacing burned wooden one, new slate roof on sept, soldiers as laborers.
13. **Ser Hyle leads Brienne to Lord Tarly** — Instead of the castle, they go to the fishmarket by the harbor.
14. **Tarly dispenses justice** — A thief who stole from a sept loses seven fingers. A baker who mixed sawdust in flour is fined fifty silver stags or lashed. A whore who gave soldiers the pox is washed with lye and imprisoned. A sailor who stabbed an archer is punished; the archer who cheated at dice also loses a finger.
15. **Brienne presents herself to Lord Tarly** — He recognizes her, asks about Renly's death, and she denies killing him.
16. **Tarly rebukes Brienne** — Tells her she should never have donned mail or left her father's hall.
17. **Brienne shows her royal letter** — Reveals she seeks Sansa Stark on the king's business.
18. **Tarly reveals Lysa Arryn's death** — Reports that a singer pushed Lysa off a mountain and Littlefinger now holds the Eyrie; predicts Vale lords won't accept Littlefinger.
19. **Tarly dismisses Brienne** — Tells her she'll earn being raped with her folly; rebukes Hyle Hunt for leaving his gate.
20. **Ser Hyle reports deaths at the Blackwater** — Mentions Ben, Farrow, and Will the Stork died; Mullendore lost half his arm. Says the wager "was only a game."
21. **Brienne recalls the wager at Highgarden** — Extended flashback: Ben Bushy, Ambrose, and Hunt started a betting pool on who could claim her maidenhead; Tarly ended it and blamed Brienne.
22. **Brienne sends Podrick to find stables and an inn** — She goes alone to find the Stinking Goose.
23. **Brienne finds the Stinking Goose** — A cellar beneath a knacker's barn, found after an hour of searching; no sign marks it.
24. **Brienne meets Dick Crabb** — He tells her a frightened fool came seeking passage for three across the sea; the fool went white when Tarly's men entered. Dick sent the fool to the Whispers, a ruined keep on Crackclaw Point, claiming ships came there — but none have in thirty years.
25. **Brienne negotiates** — Six golden dragons if they find her sister, two for just the fool, nothing for nothing.
26. **They agree to meet at the east gate at first light** — Dick Crabb says he needs to see a man about a horse.
