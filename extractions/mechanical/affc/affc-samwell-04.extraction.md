- **chapter_summary:** Sam conducts funeral rites for Maester Aemon aboard the Cinnamon Wind off the coast of Dorne. He and Gilly drink spiced rum in the old man's memory, and Sam recalls Aemon's final days — his belief that Daenerys is the prince that was promised, his deathbed warnings about Melisandre's false sword, and his declining health through the voyage from Braavos. That night, drunk on rum and grief, Sam and Gilly have sex. Sam spends the next day avoiding her in guilt over his broken Night's Watch vows until Kojja Mo corners him with the Summer Islander philosophy that sex honors the dead. Sam goes to Gilly and confesses he cannot take a wife; Gilly answers that there are no heart trees at sea to witness his shame.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sam conducts funeral rites for Maester Aemon** — Wearing his blacks on the afterdeck of the Cinnamon Wind, Sam delivers a eulogy declaring Aemon was a great man, blood of the dragon, who could have been king but gave the crown to his younger brother. He breaks down weeping.
2. **Gilly and Kojja Mo repeat the words** — Gilly murmurs "and now his watch is ended"; Kojja Mo repeats in Common Tongue then in Summer Tongue for the crew.
3. **Crew breaches a cask of spiced rum for Aemon's wake** — Xhondo orders a cask rolled out; crew drinks in memory of "the old blind dragon." Summer Islanders revere the elderly and celebrate their dead.
4. **Sam and Gilly drink on the forecastle** — They drink cups to various people and two for Aemon, watching the sunset. Sam tells Gilly the Citadel's healers might have saved Aemon.
5. **Sam recalls Aemon's revival in Braavos (flashback)** — Xhondo's talk of dragons restored Aemon; he ate well and declared Daenerys is the one born amidst salt and smoke, that the prophecy's translation was wrong about gender, and that dragons are neither male nor female. He insisted on going to her.
6. **Sam recalls Aemon boarding the Cinnamon Wind (flashback)** — Aemon walked up the plank on his own legs. Sam sold the Castle Black books and gave his sword to Xhondo for passage, keeping only the broken horn, his clothes, and Aemon's chain.
7. **Sam recalls Aemon's decline during the voyage (flashback)** — At Pentos, Aemon last came on deck. His wits wandered; by Tyrosh he no longer spoke of going east, only of Oldtown and the Citadel.
8. **Sam recalls Aemon's urgent messages (flashback)** — Aemon told Sam he must convince the archmaesters about the wights, white walkers, and the creeping cold. He said Melisandre misread the signs, Stannis's sword is wrong ("light without heat, an empty glamor"), and Daenerys must be sent a maester.
9. **Sam recalls Aemon's final ravings (flashback)** — Aemon spoke of dreams, a glass candle that could not be lit, eggs that would not hatch, said "the sphinx was the riddle, not the riddler," asked Sam to read from Septon Barth, and cried "the dragon must have three heads."
10. **Sam recalls Aemon's death** — Aemon went to sleep and did not wake up. His body was preserved in a cask of blackbelly rum because Quhuru Mo would not allow a funeral pyre aboard.
11. **Gilly recalls Aemon's last night alive** — The night before he died, Aemon held the babe, rocked him, and hummed a song; the baby pulled his lip and it made Aemon laugh.
12. **Sam and Gilly discuss naming the baby** — Gilly proposes "Maester"; Sam corrects that it is not a name but suggests "Aemon." Gilly chooses "Aemon Battleborn. Aemon Steelsong." She will name him when he is two, not before.
13. **Sam and Gilly go below decks** — Sam helps Gilly down the ladder to the women's quarters; he bangs his head on the lantern.
14. **Gilly kisses Sam and they have sex** — Gilly initiates; Sam protests weakly ("We can't") but she insists. Despite his guilt about his Night's Watch vows, he does not stop. She whispers "I am your wife now."
15. **Sam lies awake in guilt** — He listens to Summer Islanders mating and recalls that they "answer death with life." He considers jumping into the sea so no one will know he broke his vows.
16. **Sam wakes with a hangover** — Xhondo rouses him shouting about the wind. Sam's head is splitting, his blisters torn, and he feels nauseated. He smells Gilly on his clothes and is glad of it.
17. **Sam works all day avoiding Gilly** — He goes below when she comes on deck, goes aft when she goes forward, turns away when she smiles.
18. **Xhondo drags Sam to Kojja Mo** — Near the end of Sam's watch, Xhondo seizes him by the collar and dumps him at Kojja Mo's feet.
19. **Kojja Mo lectures Sam about Summer Islander beliefs** — She tells him there is no shame in loving, that Westerosi gods must be demons if they forbid it, and that the gods gave bodies for pleasure and making children. She tells him Gilly knows she cannot keep him but wants him for a little while.
20. **Sam goes to Gilly** — He tells her he would sooner have her than any princess but he said the words before a heart tree. Gilly brushes his tears away and says "there are no trees here. Only water, Sam. Only water."
