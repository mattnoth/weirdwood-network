# AFFC — The Drowned Man I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 20
- **pov_character:** Aeron Greyjoy
- **pov_chapter_number:** The Drowned Man I
- **first_available:** AFFC The Drowned Man I
- **location_primary:** Nagga's Hill (Old Wyk)
- **location_secondary:** Great Wyk (visible across the bay), the Lonely Light, Lannisport (referenced), Asshai by the Shadow (referenced), Valyria (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Balon Greyjoy's death; the kingsmoot has been called and all claimants have gathered at Old Wyk
- **time_markers:** "a fortnight past" (since Aeron's robes were last washed in salt); Aeron prays all night; dawn arrives ("first light stole into the world"); the kingsmoot takes place during daylight hours; Erik Ironmaker claims to be "eight-and-eighty" years old; the Grey King reigned "for a thousand years and seven"; Lonely Light is "eight days' sail to the northwest"
- **chapter_summary:** Aeron Greyjoy bathes in the sea and prays through the night at Nagga's bones on Old Wyk, preparing to conduct the kingsmoot. At dawn, the ironborn gather and Aeron calls for claimants. Gylbert Farwynd, Erik Ironmaker, and Dunstan Drumm each make claims that fail to win the crowd. Victarion Greyjoy makes a strong claim backed by rich gifts and wins loud support. Asha Greyjoy interrupts with a clever speech offering peace and the practical spoils of the north, splitting the crowd between her and Victarion. Euron Greyjoy then sounds a terrible dragon horn, claims to have found it in the ruins of Valyria, promises to conquer all of Westeros using dragons, and wins the kingsmoot decisively.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Dark clouds running before the wind at dawn; damp sea air throughout
- **Season indicators:** None explicitly stated
- **Time of day:** Begins at night (Aeron bathing in the cold sea, praying in darkness); transitions to dawn ("first light stole into the world," "black sky went grey as slate"); kingsmoot takes place through the morning/day
- **Lighting:** Complete darkness at the chapter's opening; gradual dawn light; color returning to the world at sunrise — "black sky went grey as slate; the black sea turned grey-green; the black mountains of Great Wyk across the bay put on the blue-green hues of soldier pines"
- **Sounds:** Waves pounding constantly ("the ceaseless rumble of the waves," "a roar no man could still"); driftwood cudgels clacking and clattering ("as if a hundred trees were pummeling one another with their limbs"); kettledrums booming ("boom-boom-boom-boom-boom"); warhorns bellowing; seagull screaming repeatedly; the dragon horn's terrible sound ("a shivering hot scream," "a wail of pain and fury that seemed to burn the ears"); crowd chanting various names
- **Smells:** Salt from Aeron's robes; brine from his hair; implied cooking fires (thralls gutting fish)
- **Notable sensory details:** Aeron's arms and legs numb from cold seawater; robes crackling with dried salt, stiff, clinging to wet chest; scrub grass between toes; the dragon horn's sound making bones "seem to thrum within him"; blood and blisters on the hornblower's lips; thin wisp of smoke rising from the horn; cold rough stone of Nagga's ribs

## Events & Actions
1. **Aeron bathes in the cold sea** — Aeron Greyjoy immerses himself until numb, then dons his salt-stiff robes and fills his waterskin with seawater.
2. **Aeron blesses a drowned man** — While crossing the strand in darkness, a drowned man stumbles into him and calls him "Damphair"; Aeron lays a hand on his head and blesses him.
3. **Aeron climbs to Nagga's bones** — He ascends the hill, reflecting on the Grey King's ancient hall and the glory of the ironborn past.
4. **Aeron prays all night** — At the top of Nagga's Hill, he prays to the Drowned God for wisdom for the ironborn to choose the true king, praying without sleep until dawn.
5. **Dawn breaks over Old Wyk** — Color returns to the world; banners become visible; thralls and salt wives prepare food; men wake and call for ale.
6. **Aeron hears the Drowned God's voice** — He believes he hears the god promising to be present and that no godless man will sit the Seastone Chair.
7. **Drowned men find Aeron and sound the summons** — Rus asks if it is time; Aeron nods; the drowned men beat driftwood cudgels, kettledrums boom, warhorns sound.
8. **The ironborn assemble at Nagga's Hill** — All manner of ironborn gather: oarsmen, warriors, fishermen, captains, kings, thralls, salt wives, children, women. Common men form a crescent at the base; captains and kings climb the slopes.
9. **Aeron opens the kingsmoot** — He announces Balon's death and asks who shall be king.
10. **Gylbert Farwynd claims the kingship** — He speaks of a wondrous land beyond the Sunset Sea and proposes building ten thousand ships. His gifts are meager (sealskins, walrus tusks, whalebone). Only some Farwynds support him; his claim fails.
11. **Erik Ironmaker claims the kingship** — Borne uphill in a driftwood chair by his grandsons, he boasts of his deeds, age, and heirs. His gifts of silver, bronze, and steel attract some captains.
12. **Asha challenges Erik** — She calls on him to stand up, knowing he cannot. Erik strains to rise but fails; Euron laughs. Erik's claim collapses and his grandsons carry him away.
13. **Dunstan Drumm claims the kingship** — He argues a king need not be a Greyjoy, draws Red Rain, and speaks at great length about Drumm ancestors. His meager bronze gifts fail to win support.
14. **Aeron prompts Victarion** — Aeron declares that of Quellon's nine sons, one was mightier than all the rest, cueing Victarion.
15. **Victarion claims the kingship** — He climbs the steps, receives Aeron's blessing (salt water poured on his brow), and makes a blunt speech about loyalty and deeds. His champions display the Greyjoy banner. His chests spill silver, gold, and gems. The crowd chants his name loudly.
16. **Asha interrupts Victarion's momentum** — She whistles sharply, snatches a golden collar, and pushes past Victarion's champions. She mocks Victarion (counting on fingers, boots, wives dying), argues that Balon's war won only pebbles, pinecones, and turnips, and offers peace — Sea Dragon Point and the Stony Shore, alliance with the northmen against the Iron Throne.
17. **The crowd splits between Asha and Victarion** — Rodrik the Reader, Baelor Blacktyde, Lord Merlyn, Tristifer Botley, many Harlaws, some Goodbrothers shout for Asha. Others shout for Victarion. Shoving breaks out. A pinecone is thrown at Asha's head; her makeshift crown (the golden collar) falls off.
18. **Euron's hornblower sounds the dragon horn** — A monstrous tattooed man blows a shiny black twisted horn taller than a man, bound with red gold and dark steel with Valyrian glyphs. The sound is terrible and seemingly endless. The glyphs glow with white fire. The hornblower staggers afterward with blood and blisters on his lips; smoke rises from the horn.
19. **Euron makes his claim** — He climbs the steps slowly, claims to have sailed farther than any Greyjoy, to have visited Asshai and walked the ruins of Valyria. He promises to conquer all of Westeros using the dragon horn to bind dragons to his will, citing Aegon the Conqueror as precedent. He claims three dragons exist and he knows where to find them.
20. **Euron wins the kingsmoot** — Left-Hand Lucas Codd, the Red Oarsman, Hotho Harlaw, Gorold Goodbrother, Erik Anvil-Breaker, and a thousand voices shout for Euron.
21. **Aeron despairs** — He reaches for his god and finds only silence; all he can hear is the scream of a rusted iron hinge — his recurring trauma symbol.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Devout fervor and purpose at the chapter's opening ("the god was in him"); growing confidence as weaker claimants fail; certainty that Victarion will win ("He shall be our king"); shock and disbelief when Asha's support grows ("for a woman!"); rising dread as Euron's horn sounds; terror and despair at the chapter's close when Euron wins
- **Primary preoccupation:** Ensuring the kingsmoot produces the right king (Victarion) and keeping Euron from the Seastone Chair; his belief that "no godless man will sit my Seastone Chair"
- **Key decisions made:** Decides the timing of the kingsmoot opening; deliberately prompts Victarion to make his claim with a leading introduction ("Nine sons were born from the loins of Quellon Greyjoy. One was mightier than all the rest"); waits for weaker claimants to speak first so Victarion will seem stronger by contrast
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes the Drowned God speaks to him directly ("I shall be with you here this day"); convinced his god will prevent a godless man from winning; yet at the end reaches for his god and finds "only silence"; the recurring "scream of a rusted iron hinge" suggests deep unprocessed trauma that he has suppressed through religious fervor; briefly swept up by Euron's words himself ("even Aeron was swept away by the boldness of his words")
