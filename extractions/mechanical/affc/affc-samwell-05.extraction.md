# AFFC — Samwell V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 46
- **pov_character:** Samwell Tarly
- **pov_chapter_number:** Samwell V
- **first_available:** AFFC Samwell V
- **location_primary:** Oldtown, the Citadel
- **location_secondary:** Redwyne Straits, Whispering Sound, Three Towers, Isle of Ravens, the Ravenry, Honeywine river, Scribe's Hearth, Seneschal's Court, Weeping Dock, Bloody Isle
- **approximate_timeline:** Final stretch of Sam's sea voyage from Braavos aboard the Cinnamon Wind; arrival at Oldtown
- **time_markers:** Deep blue dusk falling as they enter Whispering Sound; "Come morning" next day; next day described as cold and misty; "Only a fortnight ago" (captain of Huntress re: Tyroshi disguise incident); cold damp morning when they reach Oldtown; fog so thick only the Hightower beacon visible; "the morrow" (Sam tells Gilly); pale sunlight slanting through windows as Sam waits; growing dark outside the window at chapter's end; Alleras says Seneschal changes "every year"; "two turns" since Norren was Seneschal; Lorcas "fifty years an acolyte"
- **chapter_summary:** The Cinnamon Wind navigates the ironmen-infested Redwyne Straits and Whispering Sound, passing evidence of recent naval battles, before reaching Oldtown. Sam learns from the captain of the war galley Huntress that the ironmen are raiding in unprecedented numbers and that the Hightower defenses are stretched thin. Upon arriving, Sam goes ashore to the Citadel to present Jon Snow's letters and report Maester Aemon's death, but is kept waiting by the Seneschal's gatekeeper. The acolyte Alleras intercepts him and brings him to Archmaester Marwyn, who possesses a burning glass candle. After hearing Sam's account of Aemon's belief that Daenerys fulfills the prophecy, Marwyn announces he will sail to Slaver's Bay himself, warns Sam to say nothing of prophecies or dragons to the other archmaesters, and departs immediately aboard the Cinnamon Wind. Sam is left in the care of Alleras and a youth who introduces himself as Pate.

## Events & Actions
1. **Cinnamon Wind navigates Redwyne Straits** — Three longships sighted; two outrun, third driven off by Kojja Mo's goldenheart-bow archers after a single volley at two hundred yards; Sam looses an arrow and thinks it reached the ship
2. **Entering Whispering Sound at dusk** — Gilly and Sam observe Three Towers (seat of House Costayne) on the cliffs; Sam feels sad the voyage is ending
3. **Gilly nurses the babe** — Dalla's babe cries; Gilly feeds him; Sam observes she loves this child as much as the one she left behind
4. **Discovery of battle aftermath** — Come morning, the ship bumps against floating corpses with crows feeding on them; scorched fields, burned villages, shattered ships visible on banks and shallows; wrecked dromonds spotted (one burned, one rammed)
5. **Xhondo identifies recent battle** — Points out half-sunken longship with unknown banner (red eye, black pupil, black iron crown supported by two crows); Sam does not recognize the charge
6. **Inspection by the Huntress** — War galley emerges from fog with two companion galleys; flies Tommen's banner and Oldtown's stepped white tower with crown of flame; captain boards Cinnamon Wind, inspects holds with half a dozen knights while crossbowmen and Kojja Mo's archers eye each other
7. **Captain delivers intelligence on ironmen** — Reports unprecedented scale of ironmen raids; hundreds of ships from Shield Islands and rocks around the Arbor; multiple islands and harbors seized; recounts the failed Tyroshi-disguise infiltration attempt foiled by the Lady of the Tower's oarsmaster; reports Lord Leyton consulting spell books, Baelor building galleys, Gunthor guarding harbor, Garth training recruits, Humfrey gone to Lys for sellsails; bitter about Queen Cersei keeping Lord Paxter's fleet committed
8. **Sam worries about Horn Hill's safety** — Recalls Theon taking Winterfell; considers whether Tarly lands are safe with his father fighting in the riverlands
9. **Sam decides on Horn Hill for Gilly** — After considering keeping Gilly in Oldtown (Citadel forbids novices to keep wives) and sending her with the Cinnamon Wind (risk of ironmen capture), decides to hire a wagon and take her to his mother at Horn Hill himself
10. **Arrival at Oldtown** — Cold, damp, foggy morning; only the Hightower beacon visible through fog; boom across harbor; line of warships including the Honor of Oldtown; Ser Gunthor Hightower boards to inspect
11. **Sam explains plan to Gilly** — Will go to the Citadel first, then arrange horses and wagon for Gilly to Horn Hill; Gilly kisses him for luck
12. **Sam goes ashore** — Parts company with Kojja Mo's archers; secures permission for Gilly to stay aboard; Kojja Mo teases him about his appetite
13. **Sam walks through Oldtown to the Citadel** — Follows river road along Honeywine; feels watched; fears being recognized as Lord Randyll's son; pulls up cloak and quickens pace
14. **Sam arrives at the Citadel** — Passes the green sphinxes at the gates; stops at Scribe's Hearth to buy a map; passes the statue of King Daeron I
15. **Sam observes life at the Citadel** — Watches acolytes help an old man and young mother into a boat at Weeping Dock; sees cook's boys gathering frogs; pink-cheeked novices hurrying to the septry; thinks he should have come here as a youth and run away from his father
16. **Novice locked in stocks** — Outside Seneschal's Court, rectors lock an older novice in stocks for stealing food from kitchens; acolytes wait to pelt him with rotting vegetables
17. **Sam enters Seneschal's Court** — Hall with stone floor and high arched windows; man on raised dais (Lorcas) scratching in a ledger; wears maester's robes but no chain
18. **Sam presents himself and Jon's letters** — Gives his name as Samwell Tarly; reports Maester Aemon's death; asks to see the Seneschal; is told to sit and wait
19. **Sam waits for hours** — Others who came after him are admitted before him; when he protests, Lorcas dismisses him; Sam drowses
20. **Alleras introduces himself** — Advises Sam to slip Lorcas a penny; reveals himself as Alleras, called Sphinx; half-Dornish; an acolyte
21. **Sam recognizes the sphinx reference** — Blurts out "The sphinx is the riddle, not the riddler" (Maester Aemon's words); Alleras does not know what it means
22. **Sam tells Alleras his business** — Mentions restoring abandoned castles and needing maesters for ravens; asks about Archmaester Theobald; learns Norren is no longer Seneschal; learns the office rotates yearly by lot; Walgrave drew the stone but Theobald stepped up
23. **Sam pours out his story to Alleras** — Tells of Stannis, Melisandre, Mance Rayder, wildlings, king's blood, dragons, the wights at the Fist, the Other on his dead horse, the Old Bear's murder, Gilly and their flight, Whitetree, Small Paul, Coldhands, Jon becoming Lord Commander, the Blackbird, Dareon, Braavos, the dragons Xhondo saw in Qarth, the Cinnamon Wind, and Aemon's dying words; withholds only the secrets about Bran Stark and the baby swap
24. **Alleras takes Sam to see the Mage** — Says Theobald won't believe half of it but others might; leads Sam across drawbridge to the Isle of Ravens
25. **Alleras describes the Ravenry** — Oldest building at the Citadel; once a pirate lord's stronghold in the Age of Heroes
26. **Sam observes the Ravenry** — Moss-covered walls; ancient weirwood with carved face grown over by purple moss; ravens everywhere; white and black ravens kept apart (quarrel like Dornishmen and Marchers)
27. **Sam meets Leo Tyrell** — Pale blond youth outside Marwyn's door staring into candle flame; mocks Sam's weight and appearance; recognizes Sam as Lord Randyll's son; says Randyll told everyone Sam was dead; asks if Sam is still a craven
28. **Sam claims to be Sam the Slayer** — Tells Leo he went beyond the Wall and fought in battles; does not know why he said it
29. **Marwyn summons Sam and Alleras inside** — Growls "Get in here, Slayer" from the doorway
30. **Sam enters Marwyn's chamber** — Large round room filled with books, scrolls, maps, tapestries; fire in hearth; the glass candle burning in the center
31. **Sam sees the glass candle** — Three feet tall, obsidian, ridged and twisted; flame does not flicker; distorts colors; Marwyn confirms it is dragonglass; explains Valyrian sorcery and glass candles' powers
32. **Sam retells his story to Marwyn** — Marwyn says he knows much of it already but some small parts may have escaped his notice
33. **Marwyn discusses prophecy** — Quotes Gorghan of Old Ghis comparing prophecy to a treacherous woman; says he would not trust prophecy, but pauses with "Still..."
34. **Alleras advocates for Aemon's wishes** — Says Aemon wanted them to send a maester to Daenerys to counsel, protect, and bring her home
35. **Marwyn reveals Citadel conspiracy** — Says it may be good Aemon died before reaching Oldtown or the "grey sheep" might have killed him; claims the Citadel killed all the dragons "the last time around"; says the Citadel is building a world with no place for sorcery, prophecy, glass candles, or dragons; says Aemon's blood (Targaryen) was why he was sent to the Wall
36. **Marwyn announces his departure** — Will go to Slaver's Bay in Aemon's place aboard the Cinnamon Wind; expects the "grey sheep" will send their own man on a galley; with fair winds he should reach Daenerys first
37. **Marwyn instructs Sam** — Tells Sam to stay and forge his chain quickly; says he'll be needed on the Wall; tells him to flatter the archmaesters and say nothing of prophecies or dragons "unless you fancy poison in your porridge"
38. **Marwyn assigns Pate to house Sam** — Tells the pasty-faced novice to find Sam a dry cell and have him help tend ravens
39. **Marwyn departs immediately** — Snatches stained leather cloak, tells Alleras to look after Sam, boots heard stomping down the steps
40. **Alleras reveals the encounter was planned** — Tells Sam that Marwyn sent him to intercept Sam before he spoke to Theobald; Marwyn knew Sam was coming
41. **Glass candle implied as Marwyn's source of foreknowledge** — Alleras nods at the glass candle when Sam asks how Marwyn knew
42. **Pate offers Sam a sleeping cell** — Empty cell in west tower under Pate's own, with steps to Walgrave's chambers; good view of the Honeywine; will bring woolen coverlets
43. **Pate introduces himself** — "I'm Pate, like the pig boy"

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious, conflicted, and awed. Sad that the voyage is ending. Worried about Gilly's safety and where to leave her. Fearful of being recognized in Oldtown as Lord Randyll's son. Frustrated by Lorcas's gatekeeping at the Seneschal's Court. Shocked by the scale of the ironmen threat and the captain's bitterness toward King's Landing. Stunned by Marwyn's revelations about the Citadel's hostility toward magic and dragons. Bewildered by the speed of Marwyn's departure.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Where to safely leave Gilly and the babe; delivering Jon's letters and Aemon's message to the Citadel; processing Marwyn's alarming claims about the Citadel conspiring against magic
- **Key decisions made:** Decides Horn Hill is the safest place for Gilly — he will hire a wagon and take her himself, checking the garrison before leaving her. - **Self-deception flags:** Tells Leo "No" when asked if he is still a craven — the text explicitly notes "lied Sam" and that Jon had made it a command. He does not know why he claimed to be Sam the Slayer. He wrestles with whether he can find the strength to leave Gilly, acknowledging that staying with her longer makes departure harder, but keeps deferring that emotional reckoning.
