# AFFC — Cersei II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 8
- **pov_character:** Cersei Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Cersei II
- **first_available:** AFFC Cersei II
- **location_primary:** King's Landing — Red Keep, Great Sept of Baelor
- **location_secondary:** Aegon's High Hill, Visenya's Hill, Hall of Lamps, Cersei's chambers in the Red Keep
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Lord Tywin Lannister's death; during the period of his lying in state at the Great Sept of Baelor
- **time_markers:** Cersei rises an hour before dawn to bathe and fix her hair; morning service at the Great Sept for the highborn; afternoon service planned for the commons; evening prayers open to all; Ser Kevan arrives "promptly at sunset" for supper; shadows lengthen across the yard while Cersei drinks wine after Qyburn's visit; rain falls through the morning, stops after the service ends
- **chapter_summary:** Cersei accompanies King Tommen to Lord Tywin's wake at the Great Sept of Baelor, where she receives condolences from the court and clashes with Mace Tyrell by revealing she has already appointed Lord Gyles Rosby as master of coin rather than Tyrell's uncle Garth. She meets with Qyburn, who reports finding a pre-Conquest gold coin of House Gardener hidden in the escaped undergaoler Rugen's cell and discusses the dying Ser Gregor Clegane, whom Cersei gives into Qyburn's care for experimentation. At a private supper, Cersei offers Ser Kevan the Handship, but he demands the regency as well and tells her to return to Casterly Rock; she refuses, throws wine in his face, and he departs with a veiled remark suggesting he knows who Tommen's true father is.

- **Season indicators:** Autumn — "the autumn air smelled sweet and fresh"; Olenna mentions "autumn roses"; Cersei thinks of "an autumn sea"; autumn explicitly named multiple times
- **Time of day:** Pre-dawn (Cersei rises an hour before dawn) through morning (funeral service), afternoon (mentioned second service), to sunset (Kevan's arrival for supper); shadows lengthen across the yard in late afternoon
- **Lighting:** Gloomy within the sept due to grey sky outside; colored globes of leaded glass in the Hall of Lamps; hanging crystals in dome that would drape rainbows if the sun shone; torch mentioned during Qyburn's dungeon exploration
- **Sounds:** Seventy-seven septas singing to the Mother for mercy; someone mutters "privy" and chortles in the crowd; Ser Gregor's screaming (reported, not directly heard — frightens Tommen, wakes Cersei at night); Lord Gyles's constant coughing
- **Smells:** Faint whisper of decay from Lord Tywin's corpse, growing stronger during the service; "sweet and fresh" autumn air after rain stops; Lady Merryweather "smells of sin, like some exotic lotus"; Olenna detects an unpleasant smell in the sept
- **Notable sensory details:** Wet marble at the sept steps; water dripping from Kingsguard cloaks puddling on the floor; muddy yard at the Red Keep; Cersei's aching knees from kneeling; the sable mantle's vulnerability to rain

## Events & Actions
1. **Cersei and Tommen depart the Red Keep** — Cersei leads Tommen through the muddy yard to her litter in cold rain, overriding his wish to ride a horse and throw pennies (as Jaime told him he could).
2. **Procession descends Aegon's High Hill** — Two Kingsguard ride before on white horses; fifty Lannister guardsmen in gold and crimson follow behind; streets are empty due to rain.
3. **Arrival at the Great Sept of Baelor** — The mourners are outnumbered by gold cloaks Ser Addam Marbrand has drawn up across the plaza; Cersei tells herself more will come later.
4. **High Septon greets them on the steps** — Cersei and Tommen kiss his fingers; Cersei recalls this High Septon was Tyrion's appointment, which unsettles her.
5. **Procession through the Hall of Lamps** — Trant and Kettleblack flank the royal party; the High Septon walks slowly with weirwood staff; the Most Devout attend in cloth-of-silver.
6. **Cersei and Tommen kneel beside Lord Tywin's body** — Tywin lies in full battle armor on a stepped marble bier; Jaime stands vigil with a golden greatsword. Cersei notes the inappropriate half-smile on Tywin's face and blames Pycelle for not instructing the silent sisters.
7. **Cersei recalls Tywin's eyes and their power** — She remembers a feast of King Aerys where Lord Rykker jested about Tywin and was stared into submission.
8. **The funeral rites proceed** — Seven septons beseech the Father Above; seventy-seven septas sing to the Mother for mercy; Tommen fidgets; the smell of decay grows.
9. **Cersei observes the mourners** — She notes Kevan slumped with grief, Lancel's terrible appearance, Lord Gyles coughing, Pycelle apparently asleep, and the Tyrells (Mace, Olenna, Alerie, Garlan, Margaery with her entourage). She studies Margaery's ladies, wondering which is most wanton or loose-tongued.
10. **The service ends** — The corpse's smell has grown stronger; two Tyrell cousins wrinkle their noses; someone mutters "privy" and chortles.
11. **Hall of Lamps: mourners offer condolences** — Redwyne twins kiss Cersei's hand, their father kisses her cheeks; Hallyne promises a flaming hand in the sky; Lord Gyles announces a statue at the Lion Gate; Ser Lambert pledges to bring Tyrion's head while wearing an eye patch.
12. **Lady Falyse requests to name Lollys's child Tywin** — Cersei rejects angrily, calling Lollys a "lackwit" who was raped by half of King's Landing. Ser Balman Byrch smooths it over.
13. **Cersei encounters Margaery and Olenna with Tommen** — Cersei reminds Kevan of their later meeting; he agrees and withdraws.
14. **Lancel speaks with Cersei** — He tells her of outlaws in Castle Darry, his unwanted Frey marriage (a widow of Darry blood), and his newfound piety after the High Septon prayed for him during his illness. He nearly confesses his love for Cersei; she cuts him off. She worries about what he has told the High Septon, and warns him that "atonement is best achieved through silent prayer."
15. **Margaery embraces Cersei** — Lady Alerie and cousins kiss fingers. Lady Graceford asks to name her child Tywin or Lanna; Cersei consents.
16. **Lady Merryweather offers assistance** — Taena says she has sent word to friends in Myr, Lys, and Tyrosh to seize Tyrion if he appears. Cersei marks her as ambitious and potentially useful: "This one is ambitious, and her lord is proud but poor."
17. **Mace Tyrell presses his claims** — He hints at becoming Hand, announces Garth the Gross is en route as master of coin (claiming Tywin agreed), and mentions placing Garth's sons in the City Watch.
18. **Cersei blocks Mace** — She reveals she has already appointed Lord Gyles Rosby as master of coin. Mace sputters.
19. **Olenna intervenes** — She leads Mace away, makes barbed comments about smells in the sept and Garth's flatulence, and states she will remain until Margaery weds Tommen. Mace pushes to set a wedding date; Cersei says "Soon."
20. **Cersei privately vows to destroy Olenna** — She considers Olenna twice as clever as Mace, and resolves to see her dead.
21. **Exit from the Great Sept** — Rain has stopped; autumn air is sweet and fresh. Tommen removes his crown; Cersei orders it back on. Tommen says Margaery told him they can go to Highgarden after the wedding; Cersei forbids it.
22. **Cersei shares her litter with Lord Gyles** — She formally offers him the master of coin position; he accepts amid violent coughing. He begins naming customs officers and factors to replace (Littlefinger's appointees). Cersei instructs him to backdate his appointment to "yesterday."
23. **Return to the Red Keep** — Tommen rides a horse back; Cersei retires to her chambers and removes her shoes.
24. **Qyburn reports on Tyrion's escape** — He found a hidden hollow beneath a loose stone under Rugen's chamber pot, containing a single gold coin — pre-Conquest, depicting Garth the Twelfth with the hand sigil of House Gardener. Cersei suspects a Tyrell connection to Tyrion's escape.
25. **Qyburn reports on Ser Gregor** — The Mountain is dying slowly of manticore venom that has been thickened (possibly by sorcery); his veins have turned black, water is clouded with pus, a hole in his side is as large as Qyburn's fist. He is addicted to milk of the poppy. Pycelle's treatment has been fruitless.
26. **Cersei gives Gregor to Qyburn** — She orders Qyburn to move Gregor to the dungeons and do as he wishes, but to bring her his head when he dies (promised to Dorne). Qyburn hints at using "the black arts" to learn from the poison.
27. **Qyburn reveals why he lost his maester's chain** — He opened living bodies to understand death, while other maesters only opened the dead. He claims to understand life and death better than anyone in Oldtown.
28. **Cersei contemplates the Gardener coin** — She drinks by the window, wondering about Tyrell involvement in Tywin's death, and about the smell from Tywin's corpse. She suspects Tyrion of orchestrating everything — possibly through Pycelle, Rugen, and the High Septon.
29. **Kevan arrives for supper at sunset** — They eat a simple supper of beets, bread, and bloody beef with Dornish red wine. Kevan is somber and barely drinks.
30. **Cersei offers Kevan the Handship** — She dismisses Jaime as "gallant but a bit of a fool" and frames the offer as continuing the service Kevan gave Tywin.
31. **Kevan demands regency as well as Handship** — He insists Cersei return to Casterly Rock, citing Tywin's own plans to send her back and find her a new husband. He says Tywin always regarded Jaime as his rightful heir.
32. **Cersei refuses** — She insists she is Queen Regent and her place is with Tommen.
33. **Kevan compares her unfavorably to Tywin** — He says she is "not her father" and judges her "as unfit a mother as you are a ruler" based on what he saw of Joffrey.
34. **Cersei throws wine in Kevan's face** — She is furious.
35. **Kevan's alternative proposal** — He suggests she name him castellan of Casterly Rock and appoint Mathis Rowan or Randyll Tarly as Hand, which would strengthen Cersei while weakening Highgarden from within. Cersei suspects he has been bought by Tyrells.
36. **Kevan departs** — He says he will take his brother home. His parting words: Tommen has his mother "and his father too, I think" — a veiled statement suggesting he knows about Jaime's paternity of Tommen.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Conflicted between grief and ambition; deeply insecure beneath a surface of confidence; contemptuous of nearly everyone around her; paranoid about Tyrion's reach; furious at being challenged by Kevan; competitive with her dead father's legacy
- **Primary preoccupation:** Consolidating power as regent; filling the offices (Hand, master of coin) with people she can control; neutralizing Tyrell influence; hunting Tyrion
- **Key decisions made:** Appoints Lord Gyles Rosby as master of coin (blocking the Tyrells); gives Ser Gregor to Qyburn for experimentation; orders Qyburn to secrecy about the Gardener coin; rejects Kevan's terms for the Handship; cultivates Taena Merryweather as a potential ally
- **Self-deception flags:** Repeatedly compares herself favorably to Tywin ("I am a lion too," "you shall be remembered only as Queen Cersei's sire") while demonstrating impulsiveness he never showed; believes she can manage the Tyrells through blunt rejection rather than diplomacy; dismisses Kevan's counsel as betrayal rather than considering its merits; assumes she will be "greater" than Tywin; believes she controls the political situation while actually alienating potential allies; thinks Lady Merryweather's offer is motivated by desire to serve, while simultaneously noting the woman is ambitious
