# AFFC — Brienne I

## Chapter Metadata

- **location_secondary:** Rosby (village and castle), Old Stone Bridge inn, King's Landing (recalled), Duskendale (destination, not reached)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Brienne's departure from King's Landing; she has lingered in the city after Joffrey's death and is now traveling northeast toward Duskendale
- **time_markers:** "Near dusk" when Brienne finds the hedge knights' campfire; she takes first watch through the night; "dawn" awakens her; "midday" when she encounters the sparrows; "three hours later" they meet the merchant; "Night was gathering" when they reach the inn; her candle burns out in darkness before she departs
- **chapter_summary:** Brienne of Tarth searches for Sansa Stark along the road from Rosby to Duskendale, asking travelers if they have seen a highborn girl of thirteen with blue eyes and auburn hair. She camps with two hedge knights, Ser Creighton Longbough and Ser Illifer the Penniless, who accuse her of being Renly's killer before accepting her sworn denial. On the road the next day, the party encounters a procession of sparrows carrying the bones of murdered holy men to King's Landing, then joins a merchant named Hibald and his escort Ser Shadrich, the Mad Mouse. Ser Shadrich reveals he is also hunting Sansa Stark for Varys's bounty and identifies Brienne's cover story. At the Old Stone Bridge inn, Brienne pays for rooms and supper, then slips away alone in the dead of night to ride ahead toward Duskendale.

## Events & Actions
1. **Brienne questions villagers at Rosby** — She asks a goodwife, blacksmith, septon, swineherd, a girl pulling onions, and spearmen at the castle gates about Sansa Stark. None have seen her. The spearmen make crude remarks.
2. **Brienne spots a skinny boy on a piebald horse** — He vanishes behind the sept before she can approach him. She decides not to chase him.
3. **Brienne departs Rosby toward Duskendale** — She rides northeast past apple orchards and fields of barley.
4. **Brienne questions travelers on the road** — She asks begging brothers, a young septon, silent sisters, oxcart drivers, a swineherd, and an old woman in a horse litter. None have seen Sansa. One man warns that past Duskendale there are outlaws and broken men.
5. **Brienne reflects on Sansa's possible escape routes** — She reasons that the kingsroad is unsafe, the ironborn hold Moat Cailin, the Freys sit at the Twins, and King's Landing harbor is in ruins. Trading ships are continuing to Duskendale.
6. **Brienne doubts Jaime's sincerity** — She briefly wonders if Jaime sent her on a fool's errand to conceal Sansa's murder, then dismisses the thought, recalling Oathkeeper.
7. **Brienne encounters two hedge knights at dusk** — Ser Creighton Longbough and Ser Illifer the Penniless invite her to share their trout by a brook. They mistake her for a man until she removes her greathelm.
8. **Brienne questions the hedge knights about Sansa** — They have not seen her. Ser Creighton does not know what "auburn" means.
9. **Ser Illifer accuses Brienne of bearing a liar's shield** — He recognizes the Lothston arms and accuses her of being the Maid of Tarth who killed Renly.
10. **Brienne swears her innocence by the Seven** — She swears by the Mother, Father, Maiden, Crone, Smith, Warrior, and Stranger that she did not harm Renly. The hedge knights accept her oath.
11. **Brienne takes first watch** — She paces the camp through the night, unable to bring herself to leave the hedge knights undefended. She hears riders on the road and noises in the woods.
12. **Ser Illifer relieves Brienne** — She lies down but cannot sleep easily in the presence of men, recalling Highgarden and the Brave Companions.
13. **Brienne reflects on Sansa's situation** — She recalls Catelyn's description of Sansa, considers Tyrion's cruelty, and thinks about the maid Brella's account of the cold marriage between Sansa and Tyrion.
14. **Dawn and breakfast** — They eat roast squirrel, acorn paste, and pickles. Ser Creighton boasts of the Blackwater. Brienne notes the hedge knights are "decent men."
15. **The party resumes travel** — The hedge knights ride on either side of Brienne. Ser Creighton mentions a farm boy on a piebald horse and six rough men with staves and scythes passed during his watch.
16. **At midday, the party encounters a sparrow procession** — Begging brothers lead threescore ragged people, a spotted sow, sheep, and a wayn of bones. Their barefoot septon leader asks the knights to join them marching to King's Landing.
17. **The hedge knights decline to join the sparrows** — Ser Illifer says they are bound for Duskendale. The big man with the star calls them false knights. The septon calms the group and lets them pass.
18. **Brienne asks the sparrows about Sansa** — The septon offers a blessing but has no information.
19. **Three hours later, the party encounters Hibald and Ser Shadrich** — A merchant with six serving men and a hedge knight escort. The serving men arm themselves with quarterstaffs, Hibald with a crossbow. After introductions, they agree to travel together.
20. **Ser Shadrich reveals he is hunting Sansa Stark** — He identifies Brienne's cover story, names Varys's bounty, identifies Ser Dontos as Sansa's likely companion, and offers to split the reward. Brienne denies knowing Sansa.
21. **The party reaches the Old Stone Bridge inn at nightfall** — Brienne pays for rooms. The innkeep Naggle reminds Ser Creighton of a seven-stag debt.
22. **Supper at the inn** — They eat goat. A local asks about the Kingslayer's maiming. Ser Creighton claims a direwolf chewed off his hand. Brienne corrects them: a Qohorik sellsword did it. She reflects on Jaime's fighting prowess and his maiming.
23. **Brienne retires to her room** — She examines Oathkeeper by candlelight, praying to the Crone for guidance. She remembers Jaime's promise about Ned Stark's steel.
24. **Brienne departs the inn in the dead of night** — She gathers her things, slips downstairs barefoot, saddles her mare, and rides off alone across the old stone bridge into darkness, silently apologizing to the hedge knights.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined but increasingly anxious and doubtful; oscillates between resolve and despair about finding Sansa. Haunted by past failures — failing Renly, failing Lady Catelyn. Self-conscious about her appearance and how others perceive her. Briefly warmed by the decency of the hedge knights. Deeply alarmed when Ser Shadrich reveals he knows her true quest. Lonely and devout — prays to the Crone for guidance.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding Sansa Stark; figuring out where Sansa would have gone; the realization that others are also hunting Sansa.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to camp with the hedge knights despite initial wariness; pays for rooms at the inn as reciprocation; decides to ride ahead alone in the night, abandoning her companions; resolves to press on to Duskendale.
- **Self-deception flags:** Brienne briefly suspects Jaime may have sent her on a cruel fool's errand, then dismisses it by recalling Oathkeeper — but this does not fully address whether the task is possible. She tells herself the shortest road will yield results, but acknowledges "elsewhere is a big place." She uses the cover story of searching for her "sister" despite Ser Shadrich seeing through it immediately.
