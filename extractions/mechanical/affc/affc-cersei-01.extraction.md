Cersei asks Jaime to serve as Hand of the King; he refuses, and they quarrel publicly. Ser Boros Blount returns to report that Tyrion has escaped from the black cells, along with a gaoler named Rugen. Cersei spirals into fear, recalling an old woman's prophecy about her doom.

## Events & Actions
1. **Cersei's nightmare** — Cersei dreams she sits the Iron Throne naked while courtiers laugh at her and her dwarf brother capers below; the throne's barbs and blades tear her flesh as she tries to cover herself.
2. **Cersei is woken** — Senelle wakes her with a touch; armed men in chain mail stand around her bed; Ser Osmund Kettleblack and Ser Boros Blount are present with Lannister guardsmen.
3. **News of Tywin's death** — Ser Osmund informs Cersei that Lord Tywin has been found dead in his privy, killed with a crossbow. A hidden door was found behind the hearth in the Tower of the Hand.
4. **Cersei issues orders** — She commands Ser Osmund to accompany her to the Tower of the Hand and Ser Boros to check that Tyrion is still in his cell.
5. **Cersei crosses to the Tower of the Hand** — She pauses on the drawbridge over the dry moat, gazes at the spikes below, and reflects on her father's death and its political implications.
6. **Cersei enters the Tower of the Hand** — She posts guards at the door with orders to allow no one in or out without her permission. Red Lester offers condolences on the steps.
7. **Cersei enters the bedchamber** — She finds Kevan kneeling beside the bed trying to pray; guardsmen cluster near the hearth where the secret passage gapes open. She sees her father's body, shot through the groin, and notes the smell.
8. **Cersei orders the quarrel removed** — She commands that the bolt be taken from Tywin's body and that he be prepared properly — bathed, dressed in ermine, cloth-of-gold, and crimson silk; bells to ring as they did for Robert.
9. **Cersei learns Pycelle already left** — Puckens tells her Pycelle came, saw the body, and left to summon the silent sisters. Cersei is furious she was not summoned first.
10. **Qyburn presents himself** — The former maester offers his services. Cersei recalls he came with Jaime from Harrenhal and treated Jaime's hand (stump). She accepts his help conditionally.
11. **Discovery of Shae's body** — Qyburn asks about "the girl." Cersei flings aside bloody coverlets and finds Shae naked and strangled with the chain of linked golden hands. Her face has turned black.
12. **Cersei orders Shae's existence concealed** — She tells the three Kettleblacks that no one is ever to know Shae was in the chamber, threatening to take the tongue of anyone who speaks of it.
13. **Cersei rationalizes Shae's presence** — She seizes on Qyburn's suggestion that Tywin was questioning Shae about Sansa Stark's disappearance. She recalls speaking with Shae before Tyrion's trial, demanding information about Sansa.
14. **Shae's body removed through the secret passage** — Ser Osfryd carries the bundled corpse through the hidden door and down the shaft to the dungeons at Cersei's direction.
15. **Jaime emerges from the passages** — He reports finding a chamber where half a dozen tunnels meet behind iron gates, chained and locked; he needs keys. He warns the killer might still be in the walls.
16. **Cersei asks Jaime to be Hand** — She embraces him and whispers that he must take their father's place. Jaime refuses, raising his stump: "A Hand without a hand?"
17. **Cersei and Jaime quarrel publicly** — Cersei says she will rule until Tommen comes of age. Jaime says he pities Tommen and the Seven Kingdoms. Cersei slaps him; Jaime tries to block with his stump and fails. Kevan rebukes them both.
18. **Cersei reflects on past Hands** — She mentally catalogues the failures of Jon Arryn, Eddard Stark, Tyrion, and her father as Hands, concluding the next Hand must know his place and decides on Kevan.
19. **Cersei reflects on the Tyrells** — She distrusts Mace Tyrell and his allies (Redwyne, Rowan); suspects they may be involved; resents the betrothal of Tommen to Margaery; doubts Margaery's virginity; resolves to have Varys investigate.
20. **Cersei realizes Varys is missing** — He should be present but is nowhere to be found. She suspects he was part of the murder, having made common cause with Stannis. She orders Ser Meryn to bring him in.
21. **Tyrion's escape is reported** — Ser Boros returns to say Tyrion's cell is empty. A gaoler named Rugen has gone missing. Two other guards were found asleep.
22. **Cersei orders the sleeping guards executed** — She tells Ser Boros to let them "sleep forever."
23. **Cersei's panic** — She recalls the prophecy of "the old woman" (Maggy the Frog) — "I saw my future in a drop of blood. My doom." She feels Tyrion's fingers closing around her neck. She staggers to a settle, overcome.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Moves through terror (nightmare), shock (learning of Tywin's death), eerie calm ("strangely calm," comparing the loss to losing a tooth as a child), fury (at being summoned last, at Pycelle's uselessness, at Jaime's refusal), cold ambition (planning to seize power), disgust (at Shae's body, at the implication of Tywin's hypocrisy), humiliation (Jaime's public rejection), and finally spiraling panic (Tyrion's escape triggering memories of Maggy the Frog's prophecy)
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing power and protecting Tommen now that Tywin is dead; identifying the killers; managing the political fallout
- **Key decisions made:** She will rule as regent until Tommen comes of age; Kevan will be her Hand; Shae's presence must be concealed; the sleeping guards must be executed; Varys must be found; she will not remarry — Casterly Rock is hers now
- **Self-deception flags:** Insists Tywin "had no use for whores" and was merely "questioning" Shae about Sansa Stark, despite clear evidence to the contrary; tells herself "I am the only true son he ever had"; claims no one frightens her while simultaneously spiraling into panic; denies Tyrion could have done it, then fears he did; attributes her nightmare fears to wine rather than genuine anxiety; believes she can rule effectively while her judgment is driven by paranoia and spite
