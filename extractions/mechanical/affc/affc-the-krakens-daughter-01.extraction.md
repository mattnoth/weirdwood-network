# AFFC — The Kraken's Daughter I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 12
- **pov_character:** Asha Greyjoy
- **pov_chapter_number:** The Kraken's Daughter I
- **first_available:** AFFC The Kraken's Daughter I
- **location_primary:** Ten Towers (castle of Lord Rodrik Harlaw, isle of Harlaw)
- **location_secondary:** Old Wyk (discussed), Pyke (discussed), Deepwood Motte (recalled), Stony Shore (recalled)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Balon Greyjoy's death; Asha has just arrived at Ten Towers after sailing from the north
- **time_markers:** Night ("Dark, so soon?"); moonlit sea and yard; feast already concluded (only bones and greasy platters remain); Asha's crew still unloading Black Wind; Rodrik says "We looked for you some days ago"; Asha mentions a rough crossing and beaching on the Stony Shore; three years since Euron was exiled; Theon taken as hostage when he was ten years old
- **chapter_summary:** Asha Greyjoy arrives at Ten Towers to find disappointingly few supporters gathered. She learns from Lord Rodrik Harlaw that Aeron Damphair has called a kingsmoot on Old Wyk, the first in thousands of years. Rodrik counsels Asha against attending, warning that no woman has ever ruled the ironborn, and offers to name her his heir instead. She refuses, determined to claim the Seastone Chair. In the yard, Tristifer Botley reveals that Euron has been buying loyalty with treasure and gathering men on Pyke, then proposes marriage. Asha rejects him at knifepoint and asserts she is his queen, not his wife.

## Events & Actions
1. **Asha surveys the hall from the gallery** — She counts banners and notes disappointingly few supporters: Botley, Stonetree, Volmark, Myre, and various Harlaw branches. The benches are three-quarters empty.
2. **Asha recalls approaching Ten Towers** — Qarl the Maid counted the longships and observed too few had come. Asha could not agree aloud where her crew might hear.
3. **Asha notes Lord Rodrik's empty seat** — The feast has concluded; only bones, greasy platters, and drinking remain.
4. **Asha speaks with Three-Tooth** — Learns Rodrik is in his library with Botley, and Lady Alannys is abed in the Widow's Tower.
5. **Asha learns Tristifer Botley is the new Lord Botley** — Three-Tooth says Sawane Botley was drowned by the Crow's Eye and this is Lord Tristifer now.
6. **Asha reflects on Lady Gwynesse** — The widow who claims Ten Towers should be hers, grieving her husband who died off Fair Isle during Balon's first rebellion.
7. **Asha reflects on her parents** — Her father Balon seemed hard and strong but died from a fall; her mother Alannys is frail and sickly but has outlived him. She dreads telling Alannys that Theon is dead.
8. **Asha recalls her last visit to her mother** — Alannys was wasted and dim-eyed, asking for Theon, who had been taken as a hostage at age ten.
9. **Asha orders Three-Tooth to care for crew and captives** — Demands hot food for the crew and proper quarters (tower, not dungeon) for Lady Glover and children; the babe is sick. Pinches Three-Tooth's nose to enforce compliance.
10. **Asha walks through Ten Towers** — Remembers childhood: racing through steps and wallwalks, fishing off the Long Stone Quay, days in her uncle's library.
11. **Asha climbs the Book Tower** — The fattest tower, octagonal, hewn stone; she climbs to the fifth story.
12. **Asha finds Lord Rodrik reading** — He is hunched over a table by a window, surrounded by scrolls and books, reading Archmaester Marwyn's Book of Lost Books.
13. **Rodrik tells Asha about the book** — Hotho brought it from Oldtown and wants Rodrik to wed his daughter. Marwyn claims to have found three pages of Signs and Portents, visions by Aenar Targaryen's maiden daughter before the Doom.
14. **Asha and Rodrik discuss family matters** — His failing eyesight (sent to Myr for a lens); Lady Gwynesse's fixation on inheriting Ten Towers; her forgetfulness.
15. **Asha asks if Balon was murdered** — Rodrik says Lady Alannys believes so. He recounts the official story: Balon fell from a rope bridge during a storm. He concedes Euron's return was "timely."
16. **Asha demands to know about her fleet** — Only about forty longships moored below, all from Harlaw isle. No ships from Saltcliffe, Orkwood, or the Wyks. Rodrik sent summons in her name.
17. **Rodrik reveals the kingsmoot** — Aeron Damphair has called a kingsmoot on Old Wyk. Baelor Blacktyde has already sailed there. Other drowned priests (Blind Beron Blacktyde, Tarle the Thrice-Drowned, the Old Grey Gull) are preaching it.
18. **Asha and Rodrik discuss the kingsmoot's implications** — She is astonished but sees opportunity. He warns of Urron of Orkmont's massacre at the last kingsmoot, drawing a parallel between Euron Greyjoy and Urron Greyiron.
19. **Rodrik counsels Asha against going** — Argues no woman has ever ruled the ironborn; she is Balon's daughter, not his son; she has three kraken uncles. Offers to name her heir to Ten Towers.
20. **Rodrik advocates for a different political strategy** — Suggests taking one side (Stannis or Tywin) in the war for the Iron Throne in exchange for land, rather than pursuing the crown.
21. **Asha refuses and insists on the Seastone Chair** — Says she wants her father's seat, not Rodrik's. Names the Harlaw succession risks: Boremund's sons, Sigfryd's grandsons, Hotho's ambitions.
22. **Rodrik says the Knight will succeed him** — Ser Harras Harlaw will be Lord of Harlaw, and could protect Asha.
23. **Asha departs the Book Tower** — Rodrik returns to his reading. Asha believes he will come to Old Wyk despite his protests.
24. **Asha considers her political situation** — She needs Harlaw cousins, Volmarks, and Stonetrees. Her crew of Black Wind will support her; half love her as a daughter, half want to bed her, but all would die for her.
25. **Tristifer Botley approaches Asha in the yard** — Steps from behind the well in a sealskin cloak; she reaches for her dirk until she recognizes him.
26. **Tris reports on Euron's consolidation of power** — Harren died at Moat Cailin from a poisoned arrow; Euron drowned Sawane for defiance and gave half the Botley lands to Iron Holt; Lord Wynch was first to bend the knee; Germund Botley now calls himself Lord Botley and rules in Lordsport as Euron's man.
27. **Tris describes Euron's gathering strength** — Silence returned with holds full of treasure (plate, pearls, emeralds, rubies, sapphires, bags of coin); Euron has been buying friends. Orkwood brought twenty longships, Pinchface Jon Myre a dozen. Lists supporters: Left-Hand Lucas Codd, Harren Half-Hoare, the Red Oarsman, Kemmett Pyke, Rodrik Freeborn, Torwold Browntooth. Also brought monsters and wizards from the east.
28. **Asha dismisses Euron's men** — Calls them men of small account, sons of salt wives, grandsons of thralls. Quotes the Codd words: "Though All Men Do Despise Us."
29. **Tris proposes marriage to Asha** — Claims Lady Alannys has given consent. Says he is Asha's man forever.
30. **Asha recalls their childhood relationship** — Tris was among five boys Lady Alannys brought to Pyke to foster after Ned Stark took Theon. He was the first to unlace her jerkin. She recalls her first flowering during the war. Maester Qalen discovered them and Tris was sent to Blacktyde.
31. **Asha recalls losing her virginity** — To a beautiful blond-haired Lysene sailor at six-and-ten; afterward found a woods witch who taught her to brew moon tea.
32. **Asha rejects Tris's proposal** — Tells him she has touched more men than she can count. He cannot understand. She tells him to find a brothel.
33. **Tris grabs Asha's arm** — She puts her dirk to his throat instantly, drawing blood. Declares herself his queen, not his wife. Leaves him bleeding in the moonlight.
