# AFFC — Brienne II

## Chapter Metadata

- **chapter_number:** 10

- **first_available:** AFFC Brienne II
- **location_primary:** Duskendale
- **location_secondary:** The Dun Fort, The Seven Swords inn, road north of Duskendale, Hollard castle ruins (at the fork), a fishing village on the coast road
- **approximate_timeline:** After Tywin Lannister's death (news has reached the common folk); Brienne is searching for Sansa Stark under orders bearing Tommen's seal; Randyll Tarly is currently at Maidenpool
- **time_markers:** Predawn gloom at the gates; sky begins to lighten as the queue grows; dawn breaks and guardsmen appear; bathing occurs during the day; "that night" she dreams; the next morning the captain's sister delivers the shield; an hour north of Duskendale the road forks; rain falls during afternoon travel
- **chapter_summary:** Brienne enters Duskendale seeking information about Sansa Stark and Dontos Hollard. She has her shield repainted at the captain's sister's shop and takes a room at the Seven Swords inn. The maester at the Dun Fort tells her the history of the Defiance of Duskendale and the destruction of Houses Darklyn and Hollard, but can offer no help finding Dontos. A dwarf holy brother tells her of a fool seeking passage for three at Maidenpool, and mentions a man named Nimble Dick at the Stinking Goose. After leaving Duskendale, Brienne shelters at a ruined Hollard castle at a fork in the road, then discovers and confronts a boy who has been following her since Rosby — Podrick Payne, Tyrion Lannister's squire, who wants to help find Sansa.

## Events & Actions
1. **Brienne arrives at Duskendale gates predawn** — She queues behind turnip carts and farm folk, waiting for the gates to open.
2. **Brienne asks farm couple about Sansa** — She describes Sansa as her "sister," three-and-ten, fair, blue eyes, auburn hair, possibly riding with a drunken knight. She fails to come up with a false name. The couple cannot help.
3. **Brienne surveys the queue** — She notes farm folk, wealthy townsmen on palfreys, and a skinny boy on a piebald rounsey. No sign of the two knights or Ser Shadrich.
4. **Guards stop Brienne at the gate** — The captain notes the black bat of Lothston on her shield and questions her. He recommends his sister for repainting the shield and lets Brienne pass, identifying her as "a wench."
5. **Brienne crosses the market square** — She rides past vendors selling produce and looted arms and armor from the recent battle, including northern sigils and Tarly badges.
6. **Brienne considers but keeps the oaken shield** — She passes pine and linden shields for sale but retains the heavy oaken shield Jaime gave her, preferring its protection.
7. **Brienne stables her horse and walks** — The cobbled streets are too crowded to ride.
8. **Brienne finds the captain's sister** — She admires the painted doors (castle in autumn wood with hidden animals). She commissions a new design for her shield based on arms she remembers from her father's armory.
9. **Brienne takes a room at the Seven Swords inn** — The seven wooden swords represent seven Darklyn sons who served in the Kingsguard.
10. **Brienne bathes at the inn** — The woman with the birthmark brings a tub and water. She tells Brienne the Darklyns are gone; Lord Denys was the last. She claims Darklyn royal blood through the surname Darke.
11. **Brienne recalls the bath at Harrenhal** — She remembers Jaime entering the bath naked, then tries to recall Renly's face instead.
12. **Brienne visits the Dun Fort** — She learns Lord Rykker has gone to Maidenpool with Randyll Tarly. Ser Rufus Leek, the castellan, cannot read her letter and sends her to the maester.
13. **The maester recounts the Defiance of Duskendale** — He tells the full history: Lady Serala's influence on Lord Denys, the capture of King Aerys, Ser Symon Hollard killing Ser Gwayne Gaunt, Barristan Selmy's rescue, the destruction of Houses Darklyn and Hollard, and how Barristan saved young Dontos.
14. **The maester dismisses Brienne's quest** — He says gold cloaks with Lord Tywin's warrant already came; he advises her to look in Oldtown, across the narrow sea, Dorne, or the Wall.
15. **Brienne gets lost in Duskendale's alleys** — She ends up in a dead end with pigs and a well. An old woman gives her directions.
16. **Brienne collides with the skinny boy** — He falls in the mud. She finds his face familiar. He wears chain mail and a longsword. He stammers and flees. Brienne realizes he was the boy on the piebald rounsey at the gates.
17. **Brienne enters the crowded common room** — Four septas sit near the fire; locals eat crab stew.
18. **The dwarf holy brother offers Brienne his seat** — He tells her of his holy house near Maidenpool burned by wolves, then rebuilt, then destroyed by sellswords who killed the brothers and took the pigs. He hid in a hollow log and buried his brothers.
19. **Brienne asks the dwarf about Sansa** — She describes Sansa as her sister, offering gold for help.
20. **The dwarf tells of a fool at Maidenpool** — Clad in rags and dirt over motley, furtive, avoiding Tarly's soldiers, seeking passage for three across the narrow sea at the Stinking Goose.
21. **The dwarf mentions Nimble Dick** — Tarly's soldiers came looking for the fool, and later a man named Nimble Dick boasted he'd "fooled a fool" and had gold, buying ale for everyone.
22. **Brienne considers the Imp's involvement** — Passage for three: Sansa, Dontos, and possibly Tyrion Lannister.
23. **Brienne buys the dwarf his promised meal** — Hot crab stew, bread, and wine. He finishes her stew too and advises her to eat more. He departs for King's Landing on foot.
24. **Brienne recalls her investigation in King's Landing** — Brella, Sansa's former maid, told her Sansa always prayed and went to the godswood, and has "gone back north."
25. **Brienne considers Sansa's possible destinations** — She weighs the Wall (too far, too bleak), Riverrun (through siege lines), and the Eyrie (simplest, Lady Lysa would welcome her).
26. **Brienne overhears common room gossip** — People discuss Lord Tywin's murder by "that vile little dwarf" and who will rule for the boy king — Tywin's brother, Lord Tyrell, or the Kingslayer. The innkeep spits at the mention of the Kingslayer.
27. **Brienne dreams of Renly's death** — The shadow sword slices through a green steel gorget, but the dying king transforms into Jaime Lannister. She has failed him.
28. **Brienne receives her repainted shield** — The next morning, the captain's sister delivers the shield painted with a tree with green leaves and a falling star. Brienne recalls tracing this design in her father's armory.
29. **Brienne departs Duskendale by the north gate** — She rides through the fields where Tarly defeated the northerners.
30. **Brienne stops at the common grave** — A cairn marked "HERE LIE THE WOLVES." She prays silently for the dead, for Catelyn and Robb and their men.
31. **Brienne recalls Catelyn's grief** — Catelyn learned her sons were believed dead and said "I have no sons but Robb." She showed Brienne her scarred hands and described Sansa's auburn hair and Arya.
32. **Brienne swears to find Sansa** — She vows to Catelyn's shade to never stop looking, to give up her life, honor, and dreams if need be.
33. **Brienne rides the coast road** — She asks fisherfolk about Sansa; they have not seen her. Barefoot boys in a village mistake her for a man due to her helm.
34. **Rain begins and Brienne reaches the fork** — The road divides at the Hollard castle ruins. Right fork to Crackclaw Point; left fork to Maidenpool.
35. **Brienne shelters in the ruins** — She identifies the yellow sandstone merlons as three crowns — Hollard arms. She ties her horse inside, removes her helm.
36. **Brienne sees a rider and draws her sword** — She fears it may be Ser Shadrich. She recalls her betrothal to Humfrey Wagstaff and how she defeated him, and reflects on Ser Goodwin's fighting instruction.
37. **Brienne recognizes the skinny boy** — It is the same boy from Duskendale, on the piebald rounsey. He takes the left fork toward Maidenpool.
38. **Brienne pursues and confronts the boy** — She whacks his horse with the flat of her sword, unhorsing him. She holds her blade to his throat.
39. **Podrick Payne identifies himself** — He stammers out his name: Podrick Payne. He is Tyrion's squire. Brella told him Brienne was looking for Sansa. He wants to find "his lady" — Sansa is Tyrion's wife. He says Tyrion "left me."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined but often discouraged; shy and self-conscious about speaking to strangers; deeply loyal to Catelyn's memory; conflicted about Jaime (tries to think of Renly but Jaime intrudes); haunted by recurring dreams of Renly's death; aching loneliness and sense of being an outsider
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding Sansa Stark; trying to determine where Sansa would have gone; following the lead about a fool at Maidenpool
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to go to Maidenpool to find Nimble Dick at the Stinking Goose; decides to keep Jaime's oaken shield rather than buy a lighter one; decides to have the shield repainted with the arms from her father's armory rather than House Tarth's arms (because she's believed to be a murderess); decides to pursue and confront the boy following her
- **Self-deception flags:** Tries to summon Renly's face to replace Jaime's in her thoughts; tells herself she can't carry Tarth's arms because men think her a murderess, avoiding deeper reasons for the shield choice
