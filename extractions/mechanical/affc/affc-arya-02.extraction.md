# AFFC — Arya II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AFFC
- **chapter_number:** 23
- **pov_character:** Arya Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya II
- **first_available:** AFFC Arya II
- **location_primary:** House of Black and White, Braavos
- **location_secondary:** Braavos (canals, streets, stone bridge, Ragman's Harbor area)
- **approximate_timeline:** Covers an extended period of weeks or months — Arya arrives as a servant, becomes a novice, learns Braavosi, and is eventually sent out into the city. "The moon turned and turned again" indicates at least two months pass within the chapter.
- **time_markers:** "Each night before sleep"; "one day"; "every day"; prayers at dawn before breaking fast; "one night"; "the next day"; "the next morning"; "the moon turned and turned again"; "upon the morrow" (Lady Bright's departure); Arya thinks she is still ten but uncertain — "the Braavosi counted days differently"; night when she leaves the temple at chapter's end; rain falling at end
- **chapter_summary:** Arya serves as a menial in the House of Black and White, learning from the kindly man and the waif while keeping her kill list prayer. The kindly man tells her the Faceless Men's origin story — how the first Faceless Man arose among Valyrian mine slaves. He demands she give up all her possessions to become "no one." She discards everything into the canal except Needle, which she hides in a loose step, and becomes a novice. After months of learning Braavosi, practicing facial control, and washing corpses, she is sent into the city under the identity "Cat" to sell shellfish for a fishmonger named Brusco.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya whispers her kill list nightly** — Each night she murmurs "Ser Gregor, Dunsen, Raff the Sweetling, Ser Ilyn, Ser Meryn, Queen Cersei" into her pillow. She would add the Freys if she knew their names.
2. **The kindly man confronts Arya about her prayer** — He overhears her names and questions her. She lies, he detects it. She admits they are people she wants dead. He tells her the House of Black and White is the wrong place if she wants to kill for personal hatred — death belongs to Him of Many Faces.
3. **Arya adjusts to life in the temple** — She lives with the kindly man, the waif, three acolytes, two serving men, and Umma the cook. She is given servant's garb. Only the kindly man speaks Common Tongue.
4. **Daily interrogation: "Who are you?"** — The kindly man asks her every day; she answers "No one" each time, but internally lists all her former identities and remembers she is Arya of Winterfell.
5. **Arya observes the blind acolyte's candle-tending** — The youngest acolyte, though blind, always knows which candles have gone out, navigating by scent and warmth. The kindly man tells Arya to try closing her eyes.
6. **Dawn prayers at the black pool** — They pray at dawn kneeling around the pool; Arya prays her kill list silently to the Many-Faced God.
7. **Arya observes worshipers and their preferred gods** — Old women favor the Weeping Woman; rich men the Lion of Night; poor men the Hooded Wayfarer; soldiers Bakkalon the Pale Child; sailors the Moon-Pale Maiden and the Merling King. The Stranger has his shrine but few visitors.
8. **Arya explores the temple's underground vaults** — She finds rooms of weapons/armor and clothing in the vaults. The kindly man catches her and detects her lie about being lost.
9. **Arya works in the kitchen with Umma** — She chops onions, kneads dough, bones and fillets fish. The brackish waters of Braavos provide abundant seafood.
10. **The kindly man explains the nature of death and the candles** — He says death is a gift, not the worst thing; the Many-Faced God sends a dark angel to walk beside each person. The candles soothe the afraid. He asks Arya what the candles smell like.
11. **Arya lies about smelling nothing** — Internally she thinks of Winterfell: snow, smoke, pine needles, stables, Hodor, Jon and Robb, Sansa, the crypts, bread, the godswood, her wolf's fur. The kindly man detects the lie, calls her "Arya of House Stark," and tells her she may leave anytime.
12. **Arya remembers Syrio Forel** — The kindly man's "just so" triggers the memory; Syrio taught her and died for her.
13. **The kindly man demands Arya give up her possessions** — After the waif sees Arya practicing swordplay with Needle, the kindly man tells her all her treasures belong to Arya of House Stark and there is no place for them or for her pride in this House.
14. **The kindly man offers alternatives** — He offers her service in a merchant's household, life as a courtesan (the Black Pearl, the Daughter of the Dusk), marriage, or passage home on Luco Prestayn's Lady Bright bound for Gulltown, Duskendale, King's Landing, and Tyrosh.
15. **The kindly man warns Arya about the cost of service** — He says women rarely serve the Many-Faced God. She must give up everything: ears, nose, tongue, eyes, hands, feet, hopes, dreams, loves, hates. He tells her he does not think she can do it.
16. **Arya disposes of her possessions in the canal** — At night, she dresses in her Westeros clothes and goes to the dock. She throws the silver fork, floppy hat, gloves, coins (five silver stags, nine copper stars, pennies, halfpennies, groats), boots, dagger, swordbelt, cloak, tunic, breeches, and smallclothes into the canal.
17. **Arya hides Needle** — She cannot bring herself to discard Needle. She associates it with Jon, Robb, Bran, Rickon, her mother, father, Sansa, Winterfell, and everything she was. She hides it behind a loose stone step halfway up the stairs, counting steps so she can find it again.
18. **Arya reflects on recovering Needle** — Polliver stole it when the Mountain's men captured her; she found it again at the inn at the crossroads with the Hound. She believes the old gods wanted her to have it.
19. **The kindly man tells the Faceless Men origin story** — They are older than Braavos, predating the Titan, the Unmasking of Uthero, and the Founding. They began in Valyria among slaves in the mines beneath the Fourteen Flames. The first Faceless Man heard slaves of a hundred nations all praying for release from suffering, realized they prayed to one god with a hundred faces, and became that god's instrument. He killed the most wretched slave — the first gift. He would later bring the gift to the masters too.
20. **The kindly man teaches Arya to detect lies** — He explains physical tells: eyes, mouth, jaw muscles, neck. Some liars blink, stare, look away, lick lips, or cover their mouths. A false smile differs from a true one as dusk from dawn.
21. **Arya becomes a novice** — She agrees to learn. The waif will teach her Braavosi; she will teach the waif Common Tongue. Her servant's garb is replaced with a novice's black-and-white robe.
22. **Arya and the waif exchange languages** — They point at objects, learn words, then sentences. Arya finds it harder than Syrio's physical training, harder even than sewing.
23. **The lying game** — Arya and the waif take turns asking questions and trying to detect lies. The waif always knows; Arya mostly guesses wrong.
24. **The waif claims to be thirty-six** — Arya disbelieves it. The kindly man confirms it is true — poisons have made the waif appear as she does. The waif gave everything to the Many-Faced God. Arya realizes the waif pours poison into the black pool each evening.
25. **Arya serves visiting priests** — She pours for the fat fellow and the squinter, standing still as stone as instructed. She overhears much in Braavosi but understands little. She dreams she is a wolf running with a great pack.
26. **Arya asks about Jaqen H'ghar** — The kindly man claims to know no one by that name.
27. **Arya asks about changing faces** — The kindly man dismisses it as years of prayer, sacrifice, and study. He teaches her to practice making faces — controlling facial muscles — and to train before a Myrish mirror daily.
28. **Arya washes corpses with the acolytes** — She is commanded to help prepare the dead. She remembers Old Nan's story about old men who "go hunting" in long winters.
29. **Extended training montage** — "The moon turned and turned again." Arya serves, washes the dead, makes faces at mirrors, learns Braavosi, tries to remember she is no one.
30. **Arya is sent into the city as "Cat"** — The kindly man says she must speak Braavosi daily to truly learn. She will work for Brusco the fishmonger, selling cockles, clams, and mussels. She constructs a cover identity: Cat, an orphan of King's Landing, whose father was an oarmaster on the galley Nymeria.
31. **Arya leaves the temple** — She goes out into Braavos at night wearing orphan's garb with a long iron knife hidden under her cloak. She gets lost, crosses a stone bridge, sees Ragman's Harbor, chants her kill list, and says "valar morghulis" as rain falls on her face.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined but divided. Arya is committed to staying at the House of Black and White but cannot truly let go of her identity. She feels stricken when told to give up her treasures, especially Needle. She experiences a moment of profound grief and attachment when she tries to discard Needle, brought to tears by memories of Jon. She feels "a hole where her heart should be." At the chapter's end, leaving the temple into the rain, she feels happy — "so happy she could dance."
- **Primary preoccupation:** Maintaining her kill list while trying to become "no one." The tension between Arya's identity (Winterfell, family, Needle, revenge) and the Faceless Men's demand that she become no one drives the entire chapter. She is also focused on learning — Braavosi language, facial control, lie detection.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Decides to stay despite the kindly man's repeated offers to leave. (2) Throws all possessions into the canal except Needle. (3) Hides Needle in a loose step — a deliberate act of defiance/preservation. (4) Agrees to become a novice and learn. (5) Adopts the identity "Cat, an orphan of King's Landing" for her work in the city.
- **Self-deception flags:** She tells herself she can become "no one" while preserving Needle and continuing her nightly kill list prayer. She hides the sword specifically because "one day she might have need of it" — planning a future self that contradicts being "no one." She continues the kill list prayer even as a novice, silently at dawn prayers. The kindly man repeatedly tells her she is lying about being "no one," and she knows it — "a sad little lie."
