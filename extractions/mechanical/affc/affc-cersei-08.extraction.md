Cersei delivers the news to Margaery with barely concealed satisfaction. Over the following days, Cersei holds court—dealing with false Tyrion informers, petitioners including the Faith's delegation, and news that Lord Gyles Rosby is dying. She has a vivid dream of her childhood visit to Maggy the Frog, reliving the prophecy of the valonqar. She consults Qyburn about forestalling the prophecy and begins plotting to bring down Margaery by framing her for adultery, sounding out Osmund Kettleblack about his brother Osney's sword skills.

## Events & Actions
1. **Aurane Waters arrives with news** — The Sweet Cersei comes in on the evening tide; Aurane reports Dragonstone has fallen to royal forces.
2. **Report on Loras Tyrell's wounds** — Aurane describes Loras's reckless assault: he ordered attack half a day after taking command when the castellan refused single combat; he was first through the breach, took quarrels through thigh and shoulder, suffered a mace blow breaking ribs, and was doused with boiling oil. He is not expected to survive.
3. **Cersei sups with Aurane** — She has Dorcas bring bread, cheese, and boiled beef; has him repeat the tale so she can relay it to Margaery.
4. **Cersei tells Margaery about Loras** — She goes to the Maidenvault late at night; Margaery weeps but insists "dying is not dead" and orders Cersei to leave.
5. **Cersei reflects with satisfaction** — Lying awake, she considers that Tywin would recognize her as his true heir; plans Loras's funeral as political theater.
6. **Taena reports on Margaery** — Next morning, Taena says Margaery plans to send her own maester to Loras and still believes he will live.
7. **Tyroshi informer presents wrong dwarf head** — He brings a cedar chest with a bald dwarf's head with brown glass eyes. Cersei rejects it — wrong eyes, too old, bald, teeth removed. She sends the Tyroshi to Qyburn via Ser Meryn. This is the seventh wrong head brought to her.
8. **Three more Tyrion informers prove useless** — Claims of Tyrion in an Oldtown brothel, a Braavosi mummer's show, and as a riverlands hermit. All recant when offered the chance to lead knights there.
9. **Court petitions** — Jalabhar Xho begs for men (denied); Lord Hallyne asks to hatch dragon eggs from Dragonstone (denied); merchants petition about Iron Bank debts (told to pay); Faith delegation led by Septon Raynard arrives with Warrior's Sons escort.
10. **Confrontation with Faith delegation** — Cersei demands to know why the High Sparrow didn't come himself. Raynard says he's battling wickedness. Cersei objects to the Faith's disruption of brothels, arguing whoring keeps the peace and funds the crown.
11. **Lancel spotted among Warrior's Sons** — Cersei notes with displeasure that her cousin has forsaken castle, lands, and wife to join the order.
12. **Pycelle reports Lord Gyles dying** — Gyles is too weak to attend court; coughing up bits of lung with blood. Cersei refuses him leave to die and suspects someone wants him dead.
13. **Supper with Tommen** — Tommen defies Cersei, shouting that he is the king and she is not to hurt Margaery or tear out her tongue. Cersei drags him by the ear and orders Ser Boros to have Tommen whip the boy Pate until bleeding, with threat to have Qyburn remove Pate's tongue if Tommen refuses.
14. **Cersei's dream of Maggy the Frog** — She dreams/relives the childhood visit: three girls sneak to the fortune-teller's tent, Maggy drinks their blood, prophesies Cersei will wed the king (not the prince), be queen until a younger more beautiful queen replaces her, have three children with golden crowns and golden shrouds, and the valonqar will choke the life from her. Melara asks about marrying Jaime and is told she will die tonight. Cersei threw a potion in Maggy's eyes and fled.
15. **Nightmare ending** — The dream shifts to Tyrion's mismatched eyes and hands choking her as Joffrey was choked. She wakes with blanket wound around her neck.
16. **Cersei summons Pycelle for sleeping potion** — She demands something to prevent dreaming. Pycelle is eighty-four; he mentions serving since age forty-two, beginning with King Aegon. Cersei asks about prophecy; Pycelle says some doors are best left closed.
17. **Breakfast with Tommen** — The boy is subdued after the whipping. Cersei watches his kittens and vows no harm will come to him while she lives.
18. **Cersei questions Qyburn about Falyse** — Falyse is alive but mentally destroyed by Qyburn's experiments; she can no longer rule or feed herself. Cersei considers using her against Bronn but it is too late.
19. **Cersei consults Qyburn about prophecy** — She tells him about Maggy the Frog, the prophecy of the younger queen, and Melara's death (fell down a well). Qyburn confirms prophecy can be forestalled and implies Cersei knows how — "If she tries I will have my brother kill her."
20. **Cersei plots against Margaery** — She reasons Jaime cannot be relied on; considers methods (sickness, knife, pillow, heart's bane) but settles on framing Margaery for adultery using her own guards.
21. **Cersei sounds out Osmund about Osney** — She asks Osmund how good a swordsman Osney is and whether he could defeat Ser Boros Blount. Osmund says yes easily. Cersei thinks "Osney has" committed treason (implying she plans to use him).

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Triumphant and satisfied at Loras's wounding and Dragonstone's fall; contemptuous of Margaery and her grief; irritated by petitioners and the Faith's defiance; fearful and haunted by Maggy's prophecy (especially the valonqar); coldly determined to destroy Margaery; maternal possessiveness toward Tommen mixed with cruelty when he defies her; restless and unable to sleep
- **Primary preoccupation:** The Maggy the Frog prophecy — the younger, more beautiful queen (Margaery) and the valonqar (Tyrion); how to destroy Margaery before the prophecy can be fulfilled; maintaining her grip on power
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to frame Margaery for adultery using her own guards rather than kill her directly; begins evaluating Osney Kettleblack as a tool for the plan; orders Tommen to whip Pate as punishment for defiance; requests dreamless sleep potion from Pycelle
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes she is Tywin's true heir and a capable ruler ("If Lord Tywin could see me now, he would know he had his heir"); considers herself generous and kind when delivering devastating news to Margaery; tells herself Falyse "would not want to go on living without her husband" to excuse Qyburn's torture; fails to see that her interpretation of the prophecy may be wrong — fixates on Margaery as the younger queen and Tyrion as the valonqar; sees Tommen's defiance as something to crush rather than a sign of his growth; thinks "I could afford to be generous. Loras Tyrell was dying" while presenting herself as concerned
