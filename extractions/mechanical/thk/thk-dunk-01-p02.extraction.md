# THK — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** THK
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** Ashford Castle and Ashford town
- **location_secondary:** Dunk's camp on Ashford Meadow; the river Cockleswent and its ford; Henly's stable in Ashford town; the tourney grounds and lists on the south bank
- **approximate_timeline:** The morning and midday after the first night at Ashford; Dunk wakes at dawn, rides to the castle, sells his palfrey in town, and returns to the tourney grounds before noon.
- **time_markers:** "In the morning, he woke to the sound of a cock crowing"; "I expect I'll be gone most of the day"; carpenters at work at dawn; "the morning was almost done" when Dunk recrosses the ford; "Ser Arlan served his lord father in Dorne, three years past"; Ser Otho / three years past (referenced); a tavern girl "one night in Lannisport, a year ago"; the brothel offer "three years past"; Lord Ashford's daughter's "thirteenth nameday."
- **chapter_summary:** In the morning Dunk wakes Egg, saddles the palfrey Sweetfoot, and rides to Ashford Castle to enter the lists, giving his name to Plummer the steward as Ser Duncan the Tall; Plummer, doubtful, requires that a known knight vouch for him and names Ser Manfred Dondarrion. While Dunk waits to sell Sweetfoot, a hundred princes and Kingsguard arrive under the Targaryen banner; an arrogant young prince mistakes Dunk for a stableboy and insults him, and two Kingsguard, Ser Roland Crakehall and Ser Donnel of Duskendale, tell him Prince Valarr is one of Lady Ashford's champions. Dunk sells Sweetfoot to the stableman Henly in town for seven hundred fifty silver stags, then returns across the ford to the tourney grounds. There he watches a puppet show and invites the Dornish puppet girl for ale, and observes Ser Steffon Fossoway batter his cousin Raymun in the training yard; when Steffon challenges Dunk to spar, Dunk declines and leaves, resolved to hide his skills and to enter the lists only on the third day.
- **unit_part:** 2 of 7

## Events & Actions
1. **Dunk wakes Egg and they saddle Sweetfoot** — Dunk prods Egg awake, refuses him breakfast until the work is done, and together they brush out the palfrey's sorrel coat and cinch Ser Arlan's best saddle on her.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg
   - Location: Dunk's camp
   - Outcome: Sweetfoot saddled
2. **Dunk leaves Egg to guard the camp** — Dunk tells Egg to keep the camp in order and drive off thieves with a knife, threatening to hunt him down with dogs if he robs him and runs.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg
   - Location: Dunk's camp
   - Outcome: Egg left in charge; Dunk rides off
3. **Dunk rides to Ashford Castle** — Dunk crosses the field, passes the carpenters raising the lists, fords the Cockleswent, and reaches the triangular castle with its orange sun-and-chevron banners.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Instrument: Sweetfoot
   - Location: Ashford Meadow to Ashford Castle
   - Outcome: Arrival at the castle
4. **Dunk enters his name in the lists as Ser Duncan the Tall** — Directed by the guards captain to Plummer, Dunk claims knighthood, gives his name as "Ser Duncan. The Tall," and shows Ser Arlan's shield and sword as proof.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Plummer
   - Instrument: Ser Arlan's shield and longsword
   - Location: The steward's turret room
   - Outcome: Name provisionally taken
5. **Plummer demands a voucher** — Plummer, never calling Dunk "ser," says he must consult Lord Ashford and requires a known knight to vouch for Dunk; Dunk names House Dondarrion's banner, and Plummer identifies Ser Manfred and bids Dunk bring him on the morrow.
   - Agent: Plummer
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Location: The steward's turret room
   - Outcome: Dunk must find Ser Manfred Dondarrion
6. **The princes and Kingsguard arrive** — A party of a hundred knights and mounted archers pours through the gates under the Targaryen banner and three Kingsguard in white; Lord Ashford, his sons, and the fair maid hurry out to greet them.
   - Agent: The arriving princes and Kingsguard
   - Location: Ashford Castle yard
   - Witness: Dunk (watches from the stable door)
   - Outcome: Guests received into the hall
7. **The arrogant princeling insults Dunk** — Mistaking Dunk for a stableboy, the princeling orders him to see to his horse and fetch wine and a wench; when Dunk says he is a knight, the prince says "Knighthood has fallen on sad days" and dismisses him.
   - Agent: The arrogant princeling
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Location: Castle stables
   - Outcome: Dunk slinks back inside, forgotten
8. **The master of horse declines to buy Sweetfoot** — The red-faced master of horse says Lord Ashford has no need of the palfrey and directs Dunk to Henly in the town.
   - Agent: The master of horse
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Location: Castle stables
   - Outcome: Dunk sent to Henly
9. **Two Kingsguard greet Dunk** — Ser Roland Crakehall and Ser Donnel of Duskendale meet Dunk in the yard, explain they will not ride against those they are sworn to protect, and tell him Prince Valarr is one of Lady Ashford's champions with two cousins meaning to challenge.
   - Agent: Ser Roland Crakehall; Ser Donnel of Duskendale
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Location: Ashford Castle yard
   - Outcome: Dunk learns of the princely champions
10. **Dunk sells Sweetfoot to Henly** — After much arguing and cursing, Dunk and Henly settle on 750 silver stags; a second dispute arises over the saddle, and Dunk points out the coin has been shaved, gaining more silver and coppers.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Henly
    - Instrument: Sweetfoot; the gold and silver coin
    - Location: Henly's stable, Ashford town
    - Outcome: Sweetfoot sold; Dunk paid
11. **Dunk leaves coin for Sweetfoot's care** — Dunk strokes Sweetfoot's mane, promises to buy her back if he wins, and hands Henly a few coppers for her oats and an apple.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Sweetfoot
    - Location: Henly's stable
    - Outcome: Coppers left for the horse
12. **Dunk recrosses the ford to the tourney grounds** — Dunk splashes back across the Cockleswent as the morning ends and the grounds come to life with winesellers, a dancing bear, jugglers, and puppeteers.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Location: The ford / tourney grounds
    - Outcome: Return to the meadow
13. **Dunk watches the puppet show and tips the girl** — Dunk watches the puppet knight behead the wooden dragon, laughs, and throws the Dornish girl two coppers, one "for last night."
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: The Dornish puppet girl
    - Location: The tourney grounds
    - Outcome: The girl smiles at him
14. **Dunk invites the puppet girl for ale; she declines** — Dunk awkwardly offers her a horn of ale or a sausage; she thanks him but must do another show and runs off to the fat Dornishwoman.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: The Dornish puppet girl
    - Location: The tourney grounds
    - Outcome: Invitation refused
15. **Dunk watches the quintain and training yards** — Dunk watches the Brute of Bracken and Lord Caron tilt at the quintain, and men training afoot with wooden swords.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Location: The tourney grounds
    - Outcome: Dunk judges his own seat inferior
16. **Ser Steffon Fossoway beats his cousin Raymun** — In the yard, Ser Steffon hacks Raymun's shield to pieces and batters him until he yields, mocking "Here's an apple that's not ripe yet," while himself scarcely winded.
    - Agent: Ser Steffon Fossoway
    - Patient: Raymun Fossoway
    - Instrument: Wooden training sword
    - Location: The training yard
    - Outcome: Raymun bruised, bloody, and yields
17. **Ser Steffon challenges Dunk and mocks him** — Steffon invites Dunk to cross swords; Dunk declines, saying he has matters to attend; Steffon sneers "The hedge knight has matters" and calls for Ser Grance instead.
    - Agent: Ser Steffon Fossoway
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Location: The training yard
    - Outcome: Dunk stalks away red-faced
18. **Raymun apologizes and introduces himself** — Raymun catches up, admits it was wrong to urge the fight, warns that Steffon batters men to leave them vulnerable in the lists, and names himself Raymun Fossoway of the cadet branch.
    - Agent: Raymun Fossoway
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Location: The tourney grounds
    - Outcome: Dunk learns Steffon's tactics; the two part cordially
19. **Dunk resolves to hide his skill and enter late** — Dunk decides not to let anyone see him fight before the tourney, and to enter the lists only on the third day, then heads for Steely Pate's stall.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Location: The tourney grounds
    - Outcome: Dunk sets his tourney strategy

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious and self-conscious throughout (his ears go red repeatedly under Plummer's and Steffon's scorn); intimidated by the princes and unwilling to speak to them; grief and guilt at selling Sweetfoot ("as though he were betraying an old friend"); nervous around the puppet girl; giddy yet uneasy with so much coin; determined to prove himself more than a hedge knight.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting into the lists and raising the coin to pay Steely Pate for his armor — selling Sweetfoot and hoarding the silver against the ransom he may owe.
- **Key decisions made:** To enter his name as Ser Duncan the Tall; to seek Ser Manfred Dondarrion to vouch for him; to sell Sweetfoot to Henly; to decline sparring Ser Steffon and hide his skill; to enter the lists only on the third day.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself "All I need is one victory"; imagines living in a whitewashed town with Egg ("It could happen"); assures himself Sweetfoot's flaws will vanish and she'll be worth twice as much so he can buy her back; recites "It makes no difference" about whose shield to strike "though it made all the difference in the world"; frames leaving Egg and trusting him as paying a debt to Ser Arlan.
