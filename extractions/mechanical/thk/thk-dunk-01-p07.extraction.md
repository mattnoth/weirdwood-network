# THK — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** THK
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** Ashford Meadow (the muddy trial field) on the south side of the River Cockleswent
- **location_secondary:** Ashford Castle yard on the north bank of the River Cockleswent (Baelor's funeral fire); Dunk's camp by the green pool under the elm; Lys, Summerhall, the Free Cities, Dorne, the Red Mountains, Flea Bottom (all referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** The trial of seven itself, from the horn-blast opening charge through Aerion's yielding and withdrawal of his accusation; then the immediate aftermath — Baelor's collapse and death, his cremation and lying-in-state, Valarr's rebuke, and days later Maekar's visit to Dunk's camp; closes the next morning when Egg arrives to serve as squire. Directly continues the prior part (the champions arrayed for combat).
- **time_markers:** "A deep expectant silence"; "A horn sounded" (opening of the charge); Dunk thinks "it was still morning" after the fight; "Ser Humfrey Hardyng had succumbed to his wounds only last night"; the guardsmen appear "late one day"; Maekar walks away, and "The boy came the next morning, just as the sun was coming up"; "A few years in the Free Cities" (Aerion's exile); "Not for a year or two" (squiring terms).
- **chapter_summary:** In the trial of seven on Ashford Meadow, Dunk is wounded through the side by Aerion's lance and twice unhorsed, but drags Aerion into the mud, batters him with his own shield, and forces him to yield and withdraw his accusation. Elsewhere Humfrey Beesbury is slain by Donnel of Duskendale, both Humfreys and several Kingsguard fall, and Prince Baelor — fighting Maekar to shield his son Aerion's foes — takes a mace-blow to the skull; when his helm is removed he dies before Dunk. Baelor Breakspear is burned in the yard of Ashford Castle, his son Valarr keeps vigil and bitterly rebukes Dunk, and Dunk's own wound heals under boiling wine. Maekar later visits Dunk's camp, reveals he has sent Aerion to Lys, and offers Dunk a place as a household knight at Summerhall with Aegon ("Egg") as squire. Dunk agrees to take Egg only on the road, not at Summerhall, and the next morning the boy arrives to serve him; they set out for Dorne.
- **unit_part:** 7 of 7

## Events & Actions
1. **The horn sounds and the two lines of seven charge across the field** — With no tilting barrier, the destriers charge straight at one another; Baelor's black outpaces Thunder.
   - Agent: The fourteen combatants
   - Location: Ashford Meadow
   - Outcome: general engagement

2. **Dunk's lance slides off line and glances off Aerion's dragon shield** — He strikes "between two of its heads," failing to unhorse him.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Prince Aerion
   - Instrument: Dunk's lance
   - Outcome: glancing hit, no unhorsing

3. **Aerion drives his lance through Dunk's side** — Three feet of ash and iron pierce oak, wool, and steel into Dunk's ribs; Thunder falls and rises; Dunk yanks the lance out himself, bleeding.
   - Agent: Prince Aerion
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Instrument: Aerion's war lance
   - Outcome: Dunk gravely wounded but horsed

4. **Donnel of Duskendale slays Ser Humfrey Beesbury in the first charge** — Beesbury lies motionless in bloodstained mud, a broken lance in his groin.
   - Agent: Donnel of Duskendale
   - Patient: Ser Humfrey Beesbury
   - Instrument: lance
   - Location: Ashford Meadow
   - Outcome: death

5. **Baelor drives a Kingsguard from the saddle; a second white knight and Maekar are unhorsed** — Baelor gallops past "lance still intact"; Robyn Rhysling fends off the third Kingsguard.
   - Agent: Prince Baelor
   - Patient: a Kingsguard knight
   - Outcome: Kingsguard unhorsed

6. **Aerion's grey stallion barrels into Thunder, unhorsing Dunk a second time** — Dunk's longsword spins away; then Aerion's whirling morningstar smashes his head and drops him senseless.
   - Agent: Prince Aerion
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Instrument: grey stallion / morningstar
   - Outcome: Dunk unhorsed and stunned

7. **Dunk rolls into Aerion, drags him into the mud, and beats him with his own shield** — Dunk twists the dragon shield's straps free and smashes the helm crest "again and again," breaking off the painted flames; knocks away Aerion's poniard.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Prince Aerion
   - Instrument: Aerion's own shield
   - Outcome: Aerion overpowered

8. **Dunk wrenches up Aerion's visor and forces him to yield** — "YIELD!"; "I yield," the dragon whispered.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Prince Aerion
   - Outcome: Aerion yields

9. **Maekar fights toward his son; Baelor holds him off** — The Laughing Storm joins Baelor against Maekar; Maekar takes three blows for every one he lands.
   - Agent: Prince Maekar
   - Patient: Prince Baelor; Ser Lyonel
   - Outcome: Maekar hard-pressed

10. **Dunk drags Aerion to the viewing stand and makes him recant** — He hauls the mud-caked prince before Lord Ashford and the fair maid; "Tell him!" — Aerion: "I withdraw my accusation."
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Prince Aerion
    - Location: the viewing stand
    - Witness: Lord Ashford; the fair maid

11. **Maekar's mace strikes Baelor's skull during the melee** — Maekar does not recall the blow; his mace crushed the helm at the back-left into the gorget.

    - Patient: Prince Baelor

12. **Baelor's helm is removed and he dies before Dunk** — Steely Pate lifts the crushed helm; "something red and wet" falls out; Baelor touches the back of his head and falls; Dunk catches him, but the prince does not rise.
    - Agent: Steely Pate (removes the helm)
    - Patient: Prince Baelor
    - Instrument: the crushed helm
    - Witness: Dunk; Raymun; Steely Pate

13. **Baelor Breakspear is burned in the yard of Ashford Castle and lies in state** — The Targaryens burn their dead; he lies in a black velvet dragon-tunic and gold chain with a golden helm; Valarr keeps vigil.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terror and panic at the horn ("I have forgotten all... I will lose everything"); pain, delirium, and shame during the fight ("Dunk the lunk, thought he could be a knight"); belief he is dying; relief that Daeron survived; grief and guilt over Baelor; a "queer sympathy" for Maekar; fear when the guardsmen come; and hesitation, then resolve, over the squiring offer.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Surviving and defeating Aerion during the trial; afterward, the sense that "a great prince died so a hedge knight might live" and what to do with the life and the choice ("Dragonflies or dragons?").
- **Key decisions made:** To grapple and beat Aerion into yielding rather than kill or maim him; to pledge himself to Baelor; to accept Egg as squire but only on the road, refusing the safe posting at Summerhall.
- **Self-deception flags:** Repeatedly names himself "Dunk the lunk," "no champion," "not even a hedge knight," "I am nothing"; holds himself as much Baelor's killer as Maekar ("So I killed him too, as much as you").
