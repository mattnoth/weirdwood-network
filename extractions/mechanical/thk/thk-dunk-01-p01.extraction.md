- **chapter_summary:** Dunk buries the hedge knight Ser Arlan of Pennytree, who died of a chill on the road, and inherits his sword, armor, horses, and winged-chalice shield. Resolving to enter the tourney at Ashford Meadow, Dunk stops at a roadside inn where he meets a bald stableboy, then rides on alone after catching the boy mounted on his warhorse in the old man's armor. At Ashford he makes camp under an elm, studies the assembled knights' heraldry, and commissions a suit of armor from the smith Steely Pate on credit, paying only a two-silver deposit. The stableboy follows him to camp; Dunk names himself Ser Duncan the Tall and takes the boy, called Egg, as his squire for the tourney.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dunk digs and prepares Ser Arlan's grave** — Dunk digs a grave on a low hill's western slope, chosen because the old man loved to watch the sunset.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: Western slope of a low hill
   - Outcome: Grave dug
2. **Dunk buries Ser Arlan and speaks over him** — Dunk lays the stripped body in the grave, says a few halting words (having no prayers), and fills the hole.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Ser Arlan (corpse)
   - Location: The hill grave
   - Outcome: Ser Arlan buried
3. **Dunk feeds the horses and inventories the old man's possessions** — Dunk tends the three horses and lays out the purse, mail, halfhelm, sword belt, longsword, dagger, razor, whetstone, greaves, gorget, lance, and shield under an oak.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: The hill / campsite
4. **Dunk takes up Ser Arlan's sword and decides to enter the tourney** — Dunk girds the scabbard with a rope belt, draws the longsword, finds it fits his grip, and resolves to ride for the tourney at Ashford Meadow.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Instrument: Ser Arlan's longsword
   - Outcome: Decision to compete at Ashford
5. **Dunk rides to the inn and meets the stableboy** — Sore and tired, Dunk stops at the inn and hands his horses to a naked bald boy emerging from the stream, promising a copper for good work.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg (the stableboy)
   - Location: The roadside inn
   - Outcome: First meeting
6. **The lordling warns Dunk away** — Waking from a wine-puddle stupor, the young lordling points at Dunk, says "I dreamed of you," and tells him to stay well away, then staggers off to be sick.
   - Agent: The young lordling
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Location: The inn common room
7. **Dunk eats and drinks at the inn** — Dunk eats lamb, duck, pease, and bread with four tankards of ale, paying with a silver stag.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: The inn
8. **Dunk catches Egg mounted on Thunder in the old man's armor** — Emerging after dark, Dunk finds the stableboy on his warhorse wearing Ser Arlan's hauberk and halfhelm; he calls the boy a thief and makes him strip off the armor.
   - Agent: Egg
   - Patient: Dunk (his property)
   - Instrument: Thunder; Ser Arlan's hauberk and halfhelm
   - Location: The stables
   - Outcome: Egg dismounts and returns the gear
9. **Egg asks to be taken as squire; Dunk refuses and rides on alone** — The boy begs to squire for Dunk; Dunk declines, has him fill a sack of oats, and rides off leading the horses, flipping a copper the boy refuses to pick up.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg
   - Location: The inn / stables
   - Outcome: Dunk departs alone; coin left in the dirt
10. **Dunk arrives at Ashford Meadow and reads the heraldry** — At day's end Dunk surveys three score pavilions and identifies the sigils of Caron, Baratheon, Tarly, Dondarrion, Fossoway, Lannister, Estermont, Bracken, Blackwood, and many other houses.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Location: Edge of Ashford Meadow
11. **Dunk makes camp and bathes** — Dunk claims an unclaimed spot under an elm by a brook-pool, tends the horses, and washes himself in the pool as the old man had taught.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Location: Dunk's camp
12. **Dunk recalls Ser Arlan's tale of the last dragon** — Sitting naked to dry, Dunk remembers the story of the last dragon, a stunted green female, and Aegon the Dragonbane.
    - Agent: Dunk (recalling)
13. **Dunk visits the merchant stalls and watches the puppet show** — Dunk buys a sausage and ale, watches a wooden knight fight a dragon worked by a Dornish girl, and inspects the armorers' wares.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Location: Ashford Meadow merchant row
14. **Dunk commissions armor from Steely Pate on credit** — Pate measures Dunk and offers a suit for 800 stags, or 600 with the old metal; Dunk pays two silvers as a deposit and promises the rest on the morrow.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Steely Pate
    - Instrument: Two silver stags (deposit)
    - Location: Pate's stall
    - Outcome: Armor reserved for a day
15. **Dunk returns to camp and finds Egg cooking a fish** — Dunk bursts through the bushes with sword drawn, then halts at the sight of the bald boy, who has ridden in on a lamb-cart, groomed the horses, washed the clothes, made the fire, and caught a fish.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Egg
    - Location: Dunk's camp
    - Outcome: Confrontation defused
16. **Dunk names himself and takes Egg as squire** — Prompted by the boy, Dunk invents the name "Ser Duncan the Tall"; the boy gives his name as "Egg," and Dunk agrees to take him as squire for the tourney if he obeys.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Egg
    - Location: Dunk's camp
    - Outcome: Egg becomes Dunk's squire
17. **They eat and sleep; Dunk sees a falling star** — They eat the fish; Egg falls asleep; Dunk watches a bright green streak fall across the sky and thinks the luck is his alone.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Location: Dunk's camp

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grieving and lonely at the burial ("it was hard to throw dirt down on that tired old face"; "he only has me"); somber and anxious about money and his prospects; buoyed by determination to become a champion; softened toward Egg despite trying to be stern.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Entering and winning the Ashford tourney, and raising the coin to pay Steely Pate for armor ("One victory is all I need").
- **Key decisions made:** To bury Ser Arlan and go on to Ashford; to take up the old man's sword and enter the lists; to ride from the inn alone rather than take Egg; to commission armor on credit; to name himself Ser Duncan the Tall; to take Egg as his squire.
- **Self-deception flags:** Insists a tree is "all the pavilion a true knight needs," then admits "I lack the coin for a pavilion"; tells himself leaving Egg is "no kindness" undone because the boy has a better life at the inn; reassures himself "what was true for [Ser Arlan] need not be true for me"; frames sleeping rough and hard fare as the essence of knighthood.
