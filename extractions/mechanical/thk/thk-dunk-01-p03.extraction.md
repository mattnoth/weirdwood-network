- **chapter_summary:** Dunk shows his squire Egg the new armor he bought after selling his palfrey, then seeks Ser Manfred Dondarrion to vouch for him so he may enter the lists, but Manfred refuses and claims not to know him or Ser Arlan. At Ashford Castle's Great Hall Dunk overhears Princes Baelor and Maekar quarreling over Maekar's missing sons, then Baelor — who remembers Ser Arlan and tests Dunk on the Grey Lion's true name — vouches for him and grants him leave to enter the lists. Baelor rules that Dunk, not being Arlan's trueborn son, may not bear the winged-chalice arms and must find a new device. Passing through the market Dunk declines a red-haired whore, collects Egg from a Florian-and-Jonquil puppet show, and commissions the puppeteer Tanselle to paint his shield with a sunset field, an elm tree, and a shooting star.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dunk shows Egg the new armor** — Dunk displays his double-chain mail and rounded, visorless, crestless greathelm and instructs Egg on good steel and how to scour mail.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg
   - Instrument: The new mail and greathelm
   - Location: Dunk's campsite
   - Outcome: Egg critiques the lack of visor and crest; Dunk defends both
2. **Dunk sold his palfrey for the armor** — Dunk confirms he rode off and sold the palfrey to buy the steel (Egg deduced it).
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Outcome: Armor purchased; no pavilion afforded
3. **Dunk asks Ser Manfred Dondarrion to vouch for him** — Kneeling, Dunk recounts that Ser Arlan served Manfred's father against the Vulture King and shows the winged-chalice shield.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Ser Manfred Dondarrion
   - Instrument: Ser Arlan's shield
   - Location: (Manfred's tent/quarters at the tourney)
   - Outcome: Requests a voucher to enter the lists
4. **Ser Manfred refuses and denies knowing Dunk or Arlan** — Manfred says he cannot remember every one of his father's knights and dismisses Dunk even after the sigil origin tale.
   - Agent: Ser Manfred Dondarrion
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Outcome: Refusal ("Begone with you, ser")
5. **Dunk recounts the Dondarrion sigil origin tale** — Dunk retells how the first Lord Dondarrion was saved by purple lightning that killed two Dornishmen, winning the Storm King's favor and a lordship.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Ser Manfred Dondarrion
   - Outcome: Fails to persuade Manfred
6. **Dunk returns to Ashford Castle and enters the Great Hall** — Directed by a guard, Dunk enters by a side door and walks in on Baelor and Maekar arguing about Maekar's missing sons.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: Great Hall of Ashford Castle
   - Witness: Dunk (overhears the quarrel)
   - Outcome: "he had walked in on something that he ought never have heard"
7. **Maekar challenges Dunk's intrusion** — The silver-bearded prince demands who Dunk is and why he bursts in.
   - Agent: Prince Maekar
   - Patient: Dunk
8. **Dunk states his case to the lords** — Dunk explains that Ser Manfred refuses to vouch and presents Arlan's sword and shield; Lord Ashford objects that arms do not make a knight and he might have stolen them.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Lord Ashford, Plummer, Baelor, Maekar
   - Outcome: His claim is doubted
9. **Baelor recalls Ser Arlan and tests Dunk** — Baelor recites Arlan's tourney record and asks Dunk for the Grey Lion's true name.
   - Agent: Prince Baelor
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Outcome: Dunk answers "Ser Damon Lannister"
10. **Baelor vouches for Dunk and grants leave to enter the lists** — Baelor sees no reason to deny him; Plummer inclines his head in assent.
    - Agent: Prince Baelor
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Instigator: Prince Baelor (the master of the games' decision, but Baelor sees "no reason to deny you")
    - Outcome: Dunk admitted to the lists
11. **Baelor rules Dunk must adopt a new device** — Because only a trueborn son may inherit a knight's arms and Dunk is not Arlan's blood, he must find a sigil of his own.
    - Agent: Prince Baelor
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Instrument: The winged-chalice shield
    - Outcome: Dunk must repaint the shield
12. **Maekar dismisses Dunk** — Maekar cuts off Dunk's thanks and orders him off.
    - Agent: Prince Maekar
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Outcome: Dunk bows and departs
13. **Dunk declines the red-haired whore** — Passing the stalls, Dunk considers the red-haired girl but shakes his head and walks away.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Red-haired girl
    - Outcome: He declines
14. **Dunk collects Egg from the puppet show** — Egg watches the Florian-and-Jonquil play with his hood hiding his baldness; Dunk gives him coppers and lets him enjoy the stalls.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Egg
    - Location: The puppet show among the stalls
15. **Dunk commissions Tanselle to paint his shield** — Dunk approaches Tanselle, gives her the winged-chalice shield, and (with Egg's suggestion of an elm) settles on a sunset field, an elm tree, and a shooting star.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Tanselle
    - Instrument: Ser Arlan's shield
    - Outcome: Tanselle agrees to paint it that night and return it on the morrow
16. **Dunk and Tanselle exchange names** — Dunk names himself Ser Duncan the Tall; Tanselle gives her name and the nickname "Tanselle Too-Tall."
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Tanselle

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious that he cannot be vouched for and will be "lost"; struck speechless and indignant at Manfred's refusal; awed, embarrassed (ears/red), and grateful before Baelor; briefly horrified at his own words about the king dying; hopeful and smitten at the puppet stall.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing a knight or lord to vouch for him so he may enter the lists; then choosing a new sigil for his shield.
- **Key decisions made:** Sold the palfrey to buy armor; recited the Dondarrion sigil tale to persuade Manfred; named himself Ser Duncan the Tall; declined the red-haired whore; commissioned the sunset/elm/shooting-star device from Tanselle.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself he ought to beat the boldness out of Egg but admits "He knew he would not"; concedes "My squire is braver than I am, and more clever"; rationalizes Egg's fear of the castle as shyness and shame ("Egg needs time, that's all").
