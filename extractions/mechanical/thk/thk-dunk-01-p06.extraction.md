# THK — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** THK
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** The tourney meadow at Ashford (the lists / challengers' paddock)
- **location_secondary:** The merchants' wagons on the western verge; Steely Pate's wagon; the viewing stand; Dorne (referenced); Pennytree (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Pre-dawn night into first light on the morning of the trial of seven, immediately continuing the events of the prior part; covers Daeron's warning, Dunk collecting his shield, the mustering of champions, Steffon's betrayal, and Baelor joining before the combat begins.
- **time_markers:** "the soft rain" / "the cool soft rain" (night); Pate hailing Dunk "out of the dark" with a lantern; "The rain had all but stopped as the first wan light suffused the eastern sky"; "the morning chill"; "the misty morning air"; "There has not been a trial of seven for more than a hundred years"; the shield rhyme the old man taught Dunk "a long time ago," which he "had not thought of… in years."
- **chapter_summary:** In the rain Prince Daeron tells Dunk of a true dream of a dead dragon fallen atop him, urges Dunk to slay his brother Aerion, and offers to withdraw his own accusation. Dunk finds the puppeteers gone to Dorne but recovers his repainted, re-rimmed shield from Steely Pate for a copper. At dawn he goes to the lists, where champions have gathered — Humfrey Hardyng, Humfrey Beesbury, Robyn Rhysling, Lyonel Baratheon — recruited largely by his squire Egg (Aegon), while the accusers ride out one man short. Ser Steffon Fossoway then betrays Dunk to fight for Aerion, and Dunk's pleas to the assembled lords for a seventh knight are refused until Prince Baelor, in his son Valarr's armor, takes his side. Baelor lays out the rules of the trial and a plan to arm the defenders with long tourney lances.
- **unit_part:** 6 of 7

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Soft, cool rain that "had all but stopped" by dawn; tendrils of fog writhing along the ground "like pale white snakes"; river mist at the north end of the field; morning chill.
- **Season indicators:** Green summer leaves on the shield's painted elm — Pate: "See how green the leaves are? Summer leaves, for certain."
- **Time of day:** Night/pre-dawn during the Daeron and Pate scenes, shifting to "the first wan light" of dawn and the misty morning.
- **Lighting:** Darkness lit by Steely Pate's iron lantern; the shield examined "even by lantern light"; then wan eastern light of first dawn.
- **Sounds:** A roar from the crowd; a fanfare/fanfare of trumpets; Ser Lyonel sharpening his sword on a whetstone; the septon calling the throng to prayer; Dunk's shout "ARE THERE NO TRUE KNIGHTS AMONG YOU?"; only silence answering.
- **Smells:** —
- **Notable sensory details:** The tourney field a "great morass of grey-brown mud and torn grass" after the barriers came down; the three Kingsguard "like ghosts in their gleaming white enamel armor," shields "blank and clean as a field of new-fallen snow"; orange and red flickering through the slashes in Aerion's horse's caparison; a green silk plume on Daeron's helm.

## Events & Actions
1. **Daeron recounts his true dream to Dunk** — In the rain Daeron tells Dunk he dreamed of Dunk and a dead dragon, "huge, with wings so large they could cover this meadow," fallen atop Dunk yet Dunk alive and the dragon dead.
   - Agent: Prince Daeron
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Location: The rainy meadow, walking toward the merchants' wagons
   - Outcome: Warning delivered; Daeron asks Dunk to slay Aerion specifically
2. **Daeron offers to withdraw his accusation** — Daeron says he will not kill Dunk and will withdraw his accusation, but it "won't serve unless Aerion withdraws his," and fears "I've killed you with my lie."
   - Agent: Prince Daeron
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Outcome: Conditional offer; the accusation still stands because of Aerion
3. **The puppeteers flee to Dorne** — Pate reports the troupe has made for Dorne on the girl's uncle's decision; the uncle "did not think she ought see you die."
   - Agent: The girl's uncle (and the puppeteer troupe)
   - Location: Departing the meadow toward Dorne
   - Outcome: The girl gone; shield left with Pate
4. **Steely Pate returns the rebuilt shield to Dunk** — Pate has re-rimmed the shield (twice as thick, banded) and the girl repainted it; he charges "a copper."
   - Agent: Steely Pate
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Instrument: The repaired shield
   - Location: Pate's wagon
   - Outcome: Dunk armed with a stronger shield for a copper
5. **Smallfolk bless and encourage Dunk** — On the way to the lists a woman, an old man, a begging brother, and a maid offer good wishes, a blessing on his sword, and a kiss.
   - Agent: The smallfolk / commons
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Location: Along the fence by the tourney field
   - Outcome: Dunk realizes "They are for me"
6. **The defenders muster in the paddock** — Raymun waits with the horses; Robyn Rhysling, Humfrey Beesbury, and Humfrey Hardyng (on Aerion's charger) are present; Raymun explains their recruitment; Lyonel Baratheon arrives, making five then six.
   - Agent: The defending knights
   - Location: Challengers' paddock, south end of the lists
   - Outcome: Five, then six defenders assembled (short of seven)
7. **The accusers ride out one man short** — The three Kingsguard, then Maekar, Aerion, and Daeron trot from the river mist; Dunk counts only six.
   - Agent: The accusers (Kingsguard, Maekar, Aerion, Daeron)
   - Location: North end of the meadow
   - Witness: Dunk
   - Outcome: Accusers seen to be six — a man short
8. **Egg and Pate arm Dunk** — Egg tells Dunk to don his armor; Pate lends a hand; they fit hauberk, gorget, greaves, gauntlet, coif, and codpiece, checking each buckle thrice.
   - Agent: Egg (Aegon)
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Instrument: Dunk's plate and mail
   - Outcome: Dunk fully armored
9. **Ser Steffon betrays Dunk and joins the accusers** — Steffon declares he is "the seventh, but for the other side," pleading "a duty to my prince," and departs to Aerion's side coveting "Lord Fossoway."
   - Agent: Ser Steffon Fossoway
   - Patient: Dunk (and Raymun)
   - Location: The challengers' paddock
   - Outcome: Dunk left short a champion; Steffon fights for the accusers
10. **Lyonel Baratheon knights Raymun Fossoway** — Summoned to Lord Ashford, Dunk hesitates to knight Raymun; Lyonel dubs him instead, charging him by the Warrior, Father, Mother, and Maid.
    - Agent: Ser Lyonel Baratheon
    - Patient: Raymun Fossoway
    - Instrument: Lyonel's sword
    - Location: The paddock
    - Outcome: Raymun made a knight ("Ser Raymun") to take Steffon's place
11. **Dunk pleads before the viewing stand for a seventh knight** — Invoking Ser Arlan of Pennytree, Dunk appeals to Dondarrion, Lannister, Caron, Swann, and Otho Bracken; each refuses or ignores him.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: The assembled lords (Dondarrion, Lannister, Caron, Swann, Bracken)
    - Location: Along the fence before the viewing stand
    - Outcome: All refuse; Dunk shouts "ARE THERE NO TRUE KNIGHTS AMONG YOU?" — only silence
12. **Prince Baelor takes Dunk's side** — A black knight rides from the mists; taken for Prince Valarr by Lord Ashford, he lifts his visor and reveals himself as Baelor, in his son's armor, declaring Dunk "protected the weak, as every true knight must."
    - Agent: Prince Baelor
    - Patient: Dunk (beneficiary)
    - Location: Across the field, then to the south end
    - Outcome: Dunk gains a seventh champion
13. **Maekar confronts Baelor** — Maekar spurs forward: "Brother, have you taken leave of your senses? This man attacked my son." Baelor answers that the gods should judge.
    - Agent: Prince Maekar
    - Patient: Prince Baelor
    - Outcome: Baelor unmoved; trots to the defenders' side
14. **Raymun changes his sigil to a green apple** — He repaints the Fossoway apple green "lest I be mistaken for my dishonorable cousin," quipping "better green than wormy."
    - Agent: Ser Raymun Fossoway
    - Instrument: His shield
    - Outcome: Raymun visually distinguished from Steffon
15. **Baelor lays out the plan and the rules of the trial** — He orders the defenders to arm with twelve-foot tourney lances, aim for helm or chest, and states the trial's end conditions; he will handle the Kingsguard, whose oath forbids harming a prince of the blood.
    - Agent: Prince Baelor
    - Patient: The defenders
    - Location: The south end of the lists, during the septon's invocation
    - Outcome: Battle plan set as the septon calls the throng to prayer

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fearful of dying, guilty ("as relieved as he was guilty"), heartsick and despairing at the lords' refusals, then grateful and moved when the smallfolk and finally Baelor take his side.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding a seventh knight before he is declared guilty; the ill-omened look of his repainted shield ("The star was falling, what sort of sigil was that? Would he fall just as fast?").
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts the rebuilt shield; declines to knight Raymun himself and lets Lyonel do it; pleads publicly for a champion; accepts Baelor's leadership and battle plan.
- **Self-deception flags:** Calls himself "thick as a castle wall"; frames his offense as trivial — "Guilty of loosening a tooth, and for that I must die"; reads the falling-star sigil as a death omen despite Pate's reassurance.
