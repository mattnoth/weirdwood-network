# THK — Dunk I

## Chapter Metadata

- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** Ashford Meadow (the tourney grounds / the lists)
- **location_secondary:** the viewing stand, merchants' row, the Fossoway pavilion, the puppeteer's stall
- **approximate_timeline:** The first day of the Ashford tourney, dawn through night; continuous single day within this unit.
- **time_markers:** "The first day of the tourney dawned bright and clear"; morning; "For the rest of the morning and well into the afternoon"; "Late in the day"; "the sun was low in the west"; "the shadows of evening crept across the meadow"; "a hundred torches were lit"; night. Retrospective: Ser Humfrey Hardyng "won a great melee at Maidenpool last year"; Ser Robyn Rhysling "lost his eye to a splinter... not five years earlier"; Dunk served at Highgarden "before you were ever born."
- **chapter_summary:** On the first day of the Ashford tourney, Dunk carries the boy Egg on his shoulders among the smallfolk to watch the jousting, in which the five maid's champions (the two Ashford brothers, Leo Tyrell, Humfrey Hardyng, and Prince Valarr) defend against a stream of challengers. Prince Aerion Brightflame deliberately drives his lance into Ser Humfrey Hardyng's horse, crushing Humfrey's leg. That evening Raymun Fossoway invites Dunk to wine in his cousin Ser Steffon's pavilion, where Dunk learns of Prince Maekar's absent sons; Egg then bursts in reporting that Aerion is assaulting the puppet-girl Tanselle. Dunk beats Aerion, is seized by Aerion's men-at-arms, and is freed only when Egg reveals himself to be Aerion's brother and Prince Maekar's son.
- **unit_part:** 4 of 7

- **Smells:** (none stated)
- **Notable sensory details:** Dunk feeling the earth tremble beneath his feet; the goat basted with honey and herbs over a cookfire; the air in the Fossoway pavilion "pleasantly warm" from the brazier; shards of broken lances flying "like wooden knives"; bright blood gouting from the speared horse.

## Events & Actions
1. **Breakfast before the first day** — Dunk buys foodstuffs; he and Egg eat goose eggs, fried bread, and bacon; Dunk has no appetite.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: their camp
2. **Dunk carries Egg on his shoulders to watch** — Egg complains he can only see arses; Dunk sits him on his shoulders on a rise six yards from the fence.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg
   - Location: the meadow by the lists
3. **The five champions defend against challengers (first summons)** — Ten knights ride; all ten lances break on the first pass; a roar of approval.
   - Location: the lists
   - Outcome: all lances broken; "a splendid omen"
4. **Valarr unhorses Ser Abelar Hightower** — Valarr's lance slides off the watchtower shield into Abelar's chest; Abelar is dashed to the ground and carried off dazed.
   - Agent: Prince Valarr
   - Patient: Ser Abelar Hightower
   - Instrument: black lance
   - Location: the lists
   - Witness: Dunk
   - Outcome: Abelar defeated, carried off
5. **Leo Tyrell unhelms the Grey Lion** — Leo rips the Lord of Casterly Rock's helm off; barefaced, the lord salutes and yields.
   - Agent: Leo Tyrell
   - Patient: Lord of Casterly Rock (the Grey Lion)
   - Instrument: lance
   - Outcome: the Grey Lion yields
6. **Humfrey Hardyng beats Lord Tully** — Humfrey unhorses Tully, then beats him into surrender afoot with the sword.
   - Agent: Ser Humfrey Hardyng
   - Patient: Lord Tully
   - Outcome: Tully surrenders
7. **Tybolt Lannister defeats Androw Ashford** — After several courses Androw loses shield, seat, and match.
   - Agent: Ser Tybolt Lannister
   - Patient: Androw Ashford
   - Outcome: Androw defeated
8. **Lyonel Baratheon defeats Robert Ashford** — After breaking nine lances and fighting afoot sword-against-mace, Robert admits defeat.
   - Agent: Ser Lyonel Baratheon
   - Patient: Robert Ashford
   - Outcome: Robert defeated
9. **Valarr defeats Lord Gawen Swann** — Valarr strikes Gawen's shoulder, unhorsing him; Gawen yields ("I yield, Your Grace. Well fought.").
   - Agent: Prince Valarr

   - Outcome: Gawen yields

    - Agent: Leo Tyrell

11. **Humfrey Hardyng defeats Ser Joseth Mallister** — Joseth is carried off the field unconscious.
    - Agent: Ser Humfrey Hardyng
    - Patient: Ser Joseth Mallister

    - Agent: a man-at-arms

    - Instrument: pole-axe
    - Outcome: horse killed

    - Agent: Lord Ashford
    - Patient: Ser Humfrey Hardyng

    - Agent: Prince Baelor
    - Patient: Ser Humfrey Hardyng

19. **Dunk and Egg watch a puppet show of Nymeria at the merchants' row** — Two ships, a rousing sea battle; Dunk buys ale.
    - Agent: Dunk; Egg
    - Location: merchants' row
20. **Raymun Fossoway meets Dunk and invites him for wine** — Dunk follows to the Fossoway pavilion, leaving Egg at the puppet show.
    - Agent: Raymun Fossoway
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Location: merchants' row → Fossoway pavilion
21. **Aerion assaults Tanselle** — At the wrecked puppeteer's stall, Aerion twists Tanselle's arm and breaks one of her fingers while she pleads; his men burn and stamp the puppets.
    - Agent: Prince Aerion Brightflame
    - Patient: Tanselle
    - Location: the puppeteer's stall, merchants' row

    - Agent: Dunk

    - Agent: Aerion's men-at-arms (incl. Wate, Yorkel)

    - Instigator: Prince Aerion (ordering Wate)

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious before the day (belly "hard as a rock"); humbled listening to Egg's knowledge; sickened by the killing of Humfrey's horse; then enraged and horrified at Aerion's assault on Tanselle; braced for death when captured ("he means to kill me... there was hardly even time to be afraid").
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether he can win at least the first challenge on the morrow, and which champion he could beat; he studies Valarr closely and concludes "I would have a chance against him," while recognizing a hedge knight cannot challenge a prince.
- **Key decisions made:** To accept Raymun's hospitality despite discomfort; to run to Tanselle's aid and beat Aerion, wrenching free of Raymun's restraining hand ("he could not listen").
- **Self-deception flags:** Insists aloud "I saw a knight green as summer grass lose control of his lance" though "it had looked that way to him as well" that Aerion meant to kill the horse; frames his social discomfort among lords as inability to "return" hospitality.
