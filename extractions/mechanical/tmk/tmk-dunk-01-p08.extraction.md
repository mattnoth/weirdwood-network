# TMK — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** TMK
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** Whitewalls (Lord Butterwell's castle) — the castle sept and the great hall
- **location_secondary:** The dungeons, the maester's chambers, the postern gate in the north wall; the Twins, Maidenpool, and King's Landing (mentioned as destinations)
- **approximate_timeline:** Night of the wedding feast; picks up as Dunk reaches the sept to recover Egg, moves through the duel with Black Tom, and ends in the great hall where Dunk challenges the Fiddler and Ser Glendon is fetched from the dungeons
- **time_markers:** "Gods grant I am not too late"; "It was there this morning when I woke" (the dragon's egg); "the night before" (food that became the bowl of brown); "before the night is done"; "T'other night" (Dunk last saw the egg); lightning and thunder of an ongoing storm; night
- **chapter_summary:** Dunk enters the castle sept and finds Egg holding Lord Butterwell at bay; Butterwell confesses that Lord Gormon Peake orchestrated a treason and that the dragon's egg has been stolen. Egg admits he frightened Butterwell with a bluff that his father Prince Maekar is marching on Whitewalls with an army. Black Tom Heddle bursts in to seize Egg as hostage; Dunk duels and kills him, severing his forearm and stabbing him through the throat. Dunk sends Egg to flee with Butterwell toward Maidenpool, then goes to the great hall and publicly demands justice from the Fiddler (Daemon) for Ser Glendon Ball, accusing Peake of planting a painted stone to frame Glendon for the egg theft. Daemon orders Glendon fetched from the dungeons and declares he will settle the matter by trial of arms in the lists; Glendon is brought up savagely tortured.
- **unit_part:** 8 of 9

## Events & Actions
1. **Dunk enters the sept and is barred by guardsmen** — Two Butterwell men-at-arms move to cut Dunk off; Egg orders them to let Dunk pass.
   - Agent: Two men-at-arms of House Butterwell
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Location: The castle sept
   - Outcome: Halt, then stand down at Butterwell's command

2. **Egg reveals himself and holds Butterwell at bay** — Egg steps out of the shadows, shaven head shining; Butterwell is on his knees praying.
   - Agent: Egg
   - Patient: Lord Butterwell
   - Location: The castle sept
   - Outcome: Butterwell cowed and praying

3. **Butterwell confesses the treason** — He names Lord Peake as the author of the plot, admits Tom Heddle urged him on, and reports the dragon's egg was stolen from his bedchamber.
   - Agent: Lord Butterwell
   - Patient: Dunk (and Egg, as listeners)
   - Location: The castle sept
   - Outcome: Dunk learns Peake is behind the conspiracy and the egg is gone

4. **Egg recounts frightening Butterwell with a bluff** — Egg told Butterwell and Frey his father Prince Maekar sent them to spy and was coming with an army, invoking Bloodraven's saying "better to be frightening than frightened."
   - Agent: Egg
   - Patient: Lord Butterwell, Lord Frey
   - Instigator: (Egg, on his own account)
   - Outcome: Butterwell terrified; Frey fled to the Twins

5. **Black Tom Heddle storms the sept to seize Egg** — Heddle enters with a dozen armed men and orders "There's the boy. Take him."; offers "A stag to the first man to draw blood."
   - Agent: Ser Tommard Heddle ("Black Tom")
   - Patient: Egg
   - Location: The castle sept
   - Instigator: Ser Tommard Heddle
   - Outcome: Butterwell's guards halt in confusion; Dunk draws to defend Egg

6. **Dunk duels and kills Black Tom Heddle** — Heddle charges; Dunk takes blows on his shield, then bulls Heddle back, cuts through his thigh, severs his forearm, and stabs him through the throat.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Ser Tommard Heddle ("Black Tom")
   - Instrument: Longsword (and the pine shield)
   - Location: The castle sept
   - Witness: Egg (calling "Get him, ser")
   - Outcome: Death (Black Tom killed)

7. **Two of Heddle's men-at-arms flee** — After Black Tom falls, two flee into the rain; the others hesitate.
   - Agent: Two of Heddle's men-at-arms
   - Location: The castle sept
   - Outcome: Escape into the rain

8. **Butterwell resolves to flee Whitewalls** — He declares the deed "ill done," fears Gormon Peake, and proposes slipping out the postern gate in the north wall.
   - Agent: Lord Butterwell
   - Location: The castle sept / Whitewalls
   - Outcome: Plans escape

9. **Dunk sends Egg away with Butterwell** — Dunk tells Egg to go with Butterwell, give Rain his head, and make for Maidenpool before His Lordship changes sides again.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg
   - Instrument: Rain (Egg's horse)
   - Outcome: Egg to flee toward Maidenpool

10. **Dunk passes the departing guests and Uthor Underleaf** — Uthor presses Dunk about the ransom for his sword and his standing offer; Dunk pushes past.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Ser Uthor Underleaf
    - Location: The great hall
    - Outcome: Dunk continues to the dais

11. **Dunk publicly demands justice from the Fiddler for Glendon Ball** — Dunk calls out "Your Grace" and "Daemon," demanding justice; Costayne and Paege shout for Glendon's death; Ser Kyle speaks up for Glendon as Fireball's bastard.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Ser John the Fiddler (Daemon)
    - Location: The great hall
    - Outcome: The hall erupts; the matter is opened

12. **Dunk accuses Peake of framing Glendon with a painted stone** — Dunk claims the "dragon's egg" found among Glendon's things is a painted stone Peake placed there to remove Glendon from Daemon's path.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Lord Gormon Peake
    - Location: The great hall
    - Outcome: Chaos; Daemon demands the egg be brought forth

13. **Peake orders Dunk chained; Daemon overrules him** — Peake commands guards to chain Dunk in the dungeons; Daemon, "dangerously quiet," refuses and takes control.
    - Agent: Lord Gormon Peake
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Instigator: Lord Gormon Peake
    - Outcome: Overruled by Daemon

14. **Daemon orders Glendon fetched from the dungeons** — Daemon sends Sunderland, Vyrwel, and Smallwood with their men to bring Glendon up unharmed, on "the king's business."
    - Agent: Lord Sunderland, Lord Vyrwel, Lord Smallwood
    - Patient: Ser Glendon Ball
    - Instigator: Ser John the Fiddler (Daemon)
    - Outcome: Glendon fetched

15. **Daemon declares a trial of arms** — Daemon rules he will settle the matter "as my father would," meeting Glendon in the lists to let the gods determine guilt and innocence.
    - Agent: Ser John the Fiddler (Daemon)
    - Patient: Ser Glendon Ball
    - Outcome: Trial by combat/joust declared

16. **Glendon is brought up savagely tortured** — Vyrwel's men dump Glendon naked at Dunk's feet: beaten, burned with hot irons, teeth cracked/missing, right eye weeping blood, three fingernails pulled.
    - Agent: (torturers in the dungeons, unnamed; ordered by Peake)
    - Patient: Ser Glendon Ball
    - Instrument: Hot irons
    - Instigator: Lord Gormon Peake
    - Outcome: Glendon grievously injured but alive

17. **Dunk tends Glendon in the maester's chambers** — Dunk washes the blood from Glendon's face and hands; Ser Kyle brings wine; Glendon vows he could have fought and died for Daemon but "could not lose for him."
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Ser Glendon Ball
    - Location: The maester's chambers
    - Outcome: Glendon prepared for the lists

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pained (wounded arm, reopened wound, dizziness), fearful for Egg, grimly determined; "Oh, seven bloody hells" at Heddle's entrance; despairing then resolute in the hall
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting Egg safely away, then securing justice for the wrongly accused Ser Glendon Ball
- **Key decisions made:** Duel Black Tom to protect Egg; send Egg to flee with Butterwell toward Maidenpool; go into the hall and publicly challenge the Fiddler; accuse Peake of planting a painted stone
- **Self-deception flags:** Fears Egg's "loose tongue" will get them killed; calls himself "Dunk the lunk, thick as a castle wall"; notes he has "escaped death twice today" and knows that "would suffice for most men"
