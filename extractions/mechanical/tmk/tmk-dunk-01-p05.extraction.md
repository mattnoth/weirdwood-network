- **approximate_timeline:** Tournament morning, the day after the wedding feast; unit part 5 of 9. Sequence: the first-round jousts play out under punishing heat (Paege/Frey, Ball/Argrave, Caswell/Kyle); Dunk arms himself, dismisses his opponent, and is knocked senseless by Ser Uthor Underleaf's lance; he wakes four hours later in the cellar under a maester's care; he returns to the field to watch the Fiddler unhorse Franklyn Frey, passes the departing dwarfs, and at the well is urged by Maynard Plumm to flee Whitewalls, which he refuses.
- **time_markers:** "The sun rose hot and hard"; "this morning"; "last night" (the feast); "an hour before they called him forth again"; "Two more before my turn"; "You have not ridden in a tilt since Ashford Meadow"; "Ser Uthor is ten years my senior and half my size"; a "weedy youth of twenty" (Lord Caswell); "Four hours, by the sundial" (Dunk unconscious); tale of a knight who "slept for forty years"; "before the day is out"; "clouds ... gathering to the east"
- **chapter_summary:** On a blistering tournament morning at Whitewalls, Dunk watches the early tilts — Franklyn Frey unhorsing Harbert Paege, Glendon Ball unhorsing Ser Argrave, and Lord Joffrey Caswell toppling Ser Kyle the Cat, who threw the match hoping to be taken back into Caswell's service. Overconfident and hungover, Dunk dismisses his own opponent, the snail-sigiled Ser Uthor Underleaf, only to be struck square between the eyes by Uthor's lance and knocked from Thunder. He wakes four hours later in a wine cellar, tended by a maester, and learns he has lost his horse, arms, and armor to Uthor by the terms of the tilt. Returning dazed to the field, he sees the Fiddler unhorse Franklyn Frey and pass the departing troupe of dwarfs. At the well, Ser Maynard Plumm urges him to take his horse and armor and slip out the postern gate, but Dunk — his mount and gear now belonging to Uthor — refuses to leave a beggar or a thief, invoking the honor Ser Arlan taught him.
- **unit_part:** 5 of 9

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Punishing heat — "The sun rose hot and hard, implacable"; "Waves of heat rose shimmering off the white stones"; no wind, "no breath of wind stirred the banners that drooped"; the sun "beat down on his head like a hammer on an anvil"; a few clouds "gathering to the east, dark as Dunk's mood"
- **Season indicators:** High summer / drought — "baked earth and torn grass," "Clods of cracked dry earth sprayed back from beneath their horses' hooves," "too hot for man or mount"
- **Time of day:** Morning through midday; the sun rising then beating down; "Four hours, by the sundial" pass while Dunk is unconscious
- **Lighting:** Bright, glaring sun off the white stones; the cellar is dim and cool ("At least it is cool here"); "the bright blue sky"
- **Sounds:** The crack and explosion of breaking lances; a cheer "short and thin and hollow"; a trumpet "loud enough to make Dunk wince"; "a noise ... part roar and part moan" when a rider falls; a "gale of laughter" at the herald's mockery; cheering "all around him"; Dunk's voice echoing off the cellar ceiling; his head "beating like a drum," skull "pounding like some monstrous war drum"
- **Smells:** The greathelm lining "stank of old sweat"; the ugliest dwarf "smelled like a privy"; (recalled) baked earth
- **Notable sensory details:** Heat-shimmer off white stone; the drooping green, white and yellow banners; the taste of blood in Dunk's mouth on waking; the "foul potion"; the barrel-vaulted cellar ceiling and casks of wine and ale; a hawk/herald soaring in "wide circles" that Dunk envies

## Events & Actions
1. **The early tilts run under the heat** — Paege and Frey break four pairs of lances before a hollow cheer; the field bakes.
   - Agent: Ser Harbert Paege; Ser Franklyn Frey
   - Location: the lists at Whitewalls
   - Outcome: repeated broken lances, no unhorsing (yet)
2. **Ser Franklyn Frey unhorses Ser Harbert Paege** — after many passes, "the golden horse trotting riderless ... as Ser Harbert Paege rolled feebly on the ground."
   - Agent: Ser Franklyn Frey
   - Patient: Ser Harbert Paege
   - Instrument: tourney lance
   - Location: the lists
   - Outcome: Paege unhorsed
3. **Glendon Ball unhorses Ser Argrave the Defiant** — "Ball's coronal found the center of his foe's breastplate with such force that it burst his saddle cinch. Knight and saddle both went tumbling to the dust."
   - Agent: Glendon Ball
   - Patient: Ser Argrave the Defiant
   - Instrument: tourney lance (coronal)
   - Location: the lists
   - Witness: Dunk ("Dunk was impressed despite himself")
   - Outcome: Argrave unhorsed, saddle burst
4. **Lord Joffrey Caswell unhorses Ser Kyle the Cat** — Kyle throws the match; "the Cat went tumbling when Lord Joffrey's coronal chanced to whack his shoulder."
   - Agent: Lord Joffrey Caswell
   - Patient: Ser Kyle
   - Instrument: tourney lance (coronal)
   - Location: the lists
   - Witness: Dunk
   - Outcome: Kyle deliberately falls; his plan to be recognized fails
5. **Dunk arms for his tilt** — dons the greathelm and takes up the hanged-man shield and lance; Egg twice urges him to withdraw.
   - Agent: Dunk (with Egg attending)
   - Location: the south end of the lists
   - Outcome: Dunk rides out despite the warnings
6. **Ser Uthor Underleaf unhorses Dunk** — at ten yards Uthor lifts his point; "Uthor's iron fist took him square between his eyes, with all the force of man and horse behind it."
   - Agent: Ser Uthor Underleaf
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Instrument: tourney lance (iron-fist coronal)
   - Location: the lists
   - Outcome: Dunk knocked senseless from Thunder; forfeits horse, arms, and armor
7. **The maester tends Dunk for four hours** — examines his eyes, gives a potion; reports Lord Smallwood's nephew's broken wrist and Ser Eden Risley's crushed leg, "but no one has been killed."
   - Agent: the maester
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Location: the cellar
   - Outcome: Dunk revives, learns he was unhorsed and his gear is lost
8. **Ser Uthor wins his second tilt against Ser Addam Frey** — "Against Ser Addam Frey, a cousin to the bride ... Indeed he did"; "Her Ladyship fainted when Ser Addam fell."
   - Agent: Ser Uthor Underleaf
   - Patient: Ser Addam Frey
   - Location: the lists
   - Outcome: Addam Frey unhorsed; Lady Butterwell faints and is helped away
9. **Glendon Ball unhorses Lord Alyn Cockshaw** — "plump Lord Alyn Cockshaw was limping off between two squires, the latest conquest of young Glendon Ball," his helm's three feathers broken.
   - Agent: Glendon Ball
   - Patient: Lord Alyn Cockshaw
   - Location: the field
   - Outcome: Cockshaw unhorsed and limping
10. **The Fiddler unhorses Ser Franklyn Frey** — "Franklyn Frey was down"; "The Fiddler had dismounted, to help his fallen foe back to his feet."
    - Agent: Ser John the Fiddler
    - Patient: Ser Franklyn Frey
    - Instrument: tourney lance
    - Location: the field
    - Outcome: Franklyn unhorsed; Fiddler advances toward the dragon's egg
11. **The dwarfs prepare to leave Whitewalls** — hitching ponies to their wheeled wooden pig and a second wayn near the postern gate; the ugliest glares at Dunk.
    - Agent: the dwarfs
    - Location: the postern gate
    - Outcome: departure underway
12. **Maynard Plumm urges Dunk to flee; Dunk refuses** — Plumm: "Take your horse and armor and slip out the postern gate." Dunk: "Even a hedge knight has his honor" — and his gear now belongs to Uthor anyway.
    - Agent: Ser Maynard Plumm (urging); Dunk (refusing)
    - Location: the well
    - Outcome: Dunk stays
