- **approximate_timeline:** "The next day"; unit part 3 of 9. Morning at the ferry crossing gives way to the wedding feast at Whitewalls that same evening. Sequence: the slow ferry crossing and Egg's talk of dragon eggs and Fireball; arrival and seating below the salt at Whitewalls; the wedding procession and toasts; the feast, the tourney talk, the arrival of John the Fiddler, and the wedding pie.
- **time_markers:** "The next day"; "each taking more than an hour"; "it would be midday before they were allowed to board the ferry"; Whitewalls "having been raised a mere forty years ago by the grandsire of its present lord"; "The girl was fifteen and freshly flowered, her lord husband fifty and freshly widowed"; "his heir a chinless boy of four"; Frey wants a grandson "before the year is out"; "died in the spring" (the Great Spring Sickness); Bulwer "Past sixty"; "the jousting on the morrow"
- **chapter_summary:** The next day Dunk and Egg wait through the slow ferry crossing while Lords Costayne and Shawney quarrel over precedence and the Smallwoods arrive; as they wait, Egg reveals he owns a dragon's egg kept at Summerhall, describes his brothers' eggs and a prophecy that the dragons will return, and recounts the history of Ser Quentyn Ball, called Fireball. Deciding to see the dragon's egg for himself, Dunk brings them to Whitewalls, Lord Butterwell's white-stone castle, where the uninvited hedge knights are seated below the salt for the wedding feast and Egg is turned away to the inner yard. At the bench Dunk meets Ser Uthor Underleaf and learns of the tourney's prizes — the dragon's egg and thirty golden dragons for the loser of the final tilt. Lord Ambrose Butterwell weds his fifteen-year-old Frey bride amid a procession of lords and a round of toasts, during which Ser Glendon Ball spills his cup rather than drink to the Hand, Brynden Rivers. Over the feast the talk turns to tourney competitors; John the Fiddler appears and joins the hedge knights, and a great wedding pie is cut open to loose a hall full of live birds before Dunk excuses himself.
- **unit_part:** 3 of 9

## Events & Actions
1. **The ferry crossing** — Ned's ferry is too small for all who wish to cross, so Lords Costayne and Shawney go first with their tails; each of several trips takes more than an hour, slowed by mudflats and by the two lords' shouting match over precedence (Shawney elder, Costayne better born).
   - Agent: Ned (ferryman, implied); Lords Costayne and Shawney (cross first)
   - Location: The ferry landing and lake
   - Outcome: The hedge knights must wait
2. **Egg offers to use his boot to go first** — Egg says they could go first "if you let me use my boot"; Dunk refuses, because the lords were there first and are lords.
   - Agent: Egg (proposes); Dunk (refuses)
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Outcome: Refused; the party waits their turn
3. **Egg names Costayne and Shawney as former rebels** — Egg says they were "for the Black Dragon" (Shawney, and Costayne's father), describing Costayne's arms and the placement of their banners in Daemon's host; Dunk pinches the boy's lips shut and tells him to hold his tongue.
   - Agent: Egg (speaks); Dunk (silences)
   - Patient: Egg
   - Instrument: Dunk pinching Egg's lips
   - Outcome: Egg held his tongue
4. **The Smallwoods arrive; the party waits again** — Lord and Lady Smallwood arrive at the landing with their tail, so the hedge knights must wait once more; the "fellowship of the hedge" has dissolved (Glendon aloof, Kyle courting Smallwood, Maynard gossiping with the innkeep).
   - Agent: Lord and Lady Smallwood
   - Location: The ferry landing
   - Outcome: Further delay
5. **Dunk warns Egg away from Ser Maynard Plumm** — Something about Plumm troubles Dunk; he warns Egg to stay well away, saying "He could be a robber knight"; the warning only makes Plumm more interesting to Egg.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg
6. **Egg reveals he owns a dragon's egg** — Egg says his own egg is at Summerhall, that it was put in his cradle, describes the last dragon's clutch of five and the Dragonstone eggs, and his brothers' eggs (Aerion's gold-and-silver; his own white and green and swirly); Dunk, warning him to keep silent about it, reflects that he sometimes forgets Aegon is a prince.
   - Agent: Egg (reveals)
   - Patient: Dunk
   - Outcome: Dunk warns Egg not to mention the egg where any might hear
7. **Egg tells of the dragons' return** — Egg says the dragons will someday return, that his brother Daeron dreamed of it and King Aerys read it in a prophecy, and that his egg might be the one to hatch.
   - Agent: Egg
   - Patient: Dunk
8. **Egg recounts Fireball's history** — Egg tells Fireball's tale: master-at-arms at the Red Keep who taught Egg's father, uncles, and the Great Bastards; set aside for the Kingsguard by Daeron's naming of Willam Wylde; helped convince Daemon to claim the crown and rescued him from arrest; killed Lord Lefford at Lannisport, routed the Grey Lion, cut down Lady Penrose's sons (sparing the youngest); and was slain by a common archer before the Redgrass Field.
   - Agent: Egg (narrates)
   - Patient: Dunk
9. **The ferry arrives; Dunk decides on Whitewalls** — Seeing the ferry creep across the lake, Dunk resolves to go to Whitewalls to see the dragon's egg and jokes that if he wins the tourney they will both have dragon's eggs; Egg gives him a doubtful look and says he must "learn to hold my tongue."
   - Agent: Dunk (decides)
   - Location: The ferry landing
   - Outcome: They will go to Whitewalls
10. **Seating below the salt at Whitewalls** — The hedge knights are seated well below the salt; Whitewalls and its hall are described; the three uninvited knights are welcomed as it is ill luck to refuse hospitality on a wedding day.
    - Agent: Butterwell's household (seats them)
    - Patient: Dunk, Maynard, Kyle
    - Location: The great hall
11. **The dispute over Glendon at the door** — Butterwell's steward denies Glendon is Fireball's son ("Fireball never had a son"); Glendon touches his sword hilt and a dozen men-at-arms appear with spears; Kirby Pimm defuses it, clasping the steward and murmuring, laughing; the steward says something that reddens Glendon's face, and the young knight is admitted.
    - Agent: Butterwell's steward (denies); Ser Glendon (reacts); Kirby Pimm (intervenes)
    - Patient: Ser Glendon Ball
    - Instrument: Ser Glendon's sword hilt; the men-at-arms' spears
    - Witness: Dunk (watches from afar)
    - Outcome: Bloodshed averted; Glendon admitted to the hall
12. **Egg turned away to the inner yard** — When Dunk tries to bring Egg into the hall, the understeward says the great hall is for lords and knights and directs squires, grooms, and men-at-arms to tables in the inner yard; Dunk warns Egg to watch his tongue among the grown men and leaves him there.
    - Agent: The understeward (turns him away); Dunk (leaves Egg)
    - Patient: Egg
    - Location: The hall door / inner yard
    - Outcome: Egg dines in the yard
13. **Ser Glendon protests his seat** — Glendon tells the understeward "This cannot be my proper place" and invokes his father; the understeward tells him to take his seat or take his leave, and the boy sits below the salt, sullen.
    - Agent: Ser Glendon (protests); the understeward (rebuffs)
    - Patient: Ser Glendon Ball
    - Outcome: Glendon sits below the salt
14. **Dunk eats and is noticed** — Dunk hollows a black-bread trencher; Maynard remarks that the girls on the dais cannot take their eyes off Dunk and points out the Old Ox (Ser Buford Bulwer); Dunk, used to being stared at, says "Let them look."
    - Agent: Dunk; Ser Maynard (observes)
    - Location: Below the salt

    - Agent: Ser Uthor Underleaf

    - Outcome: Dunk weighs the prizes

    - Agent: Lord Butterwell (escorts the bride)
    - Patient: Lady Butterwell
    - Location: The great hall

    - Agent: Lord Frey (and others)
    - Location: The great hall

    - Agent: Ser Glendon Ball
    - Instrument: His wine cup (spilled)
    - Witness: Dunk, Maynard, Uthor
    - Outcome: Open display of disdain for the Hand

    - Agent: Butterwell (opens the feast); Dunk (pockets food)
    - Location: The great hall

    - Agent: Ser John the Fiddler
    - Patient: Dunk; Ser Glendon
    - Location: Below the salt

    - Agent: Lord and Lady Butterwell (cut it open)
    - Instrument: Their swords
    - Location: The great hall
    - Outcome: Birds fly loose about the hall

    - Agent: Dunk
    - Location: The great hall

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Hot and impatient at the ferry; wary of Ser Maynard Plumm; amused and quietly astonished by Egg's revelation that he owns a dragon's egg; anxious in the crowded hall about being recognized ("If I am recognized . . ."); uncomfortable under the dais girls' stares; tempted by the tourney prizes; grows a thirst and drinks more than he meant to.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Seeing the dragon's egg (and perhaps winning it and the ten-dragon prize); keeping Egg quiet and his own and Egg's identities hidden; getting fed.
- **Key decisions made:** To wait his turn at the ferry rather than let Egg "use his boot"; to silence Egg over the rebel lords and the dragon egg; to go to Whitewalls to see the egg; to consider entering the joust; to pocket food for Egg; to excuse himself from the table.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself "It would have been discourteous not to toast the bride and groom . . . and dangerous not to drink to the king and his Hand" — rationalizing drinking too much before the jousting; jokes "If I win the tourney, we'd both have dragon's eggs" though he admits he is poor with a lance (and Egg gives him a doubtful look).
