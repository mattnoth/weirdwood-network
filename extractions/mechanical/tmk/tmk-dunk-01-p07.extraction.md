# TMK — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** TMK
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Dunk
- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** Whitewalls (Lord Butterwell's castle) — tourney grounds, tents, and inner courtyard
- **location_secondary:** The tilting grounds/lists, the stables, Ser Uthor's pavilion, Ser John the Fiddler's pavilion, Dunk's tent, a closed courtyard with a well
- **approximate_timeline:** Late in the wedding tourney day; picks up as Dunk leaves the Snail's tent and runs through the storm-break to nightfall search for Egg
- **time_markers:** "The day seemed darker"; sun "sinking to the west"; "last night" (Egg shoved by other lads); "this morning" (the dragon's egg vanished); a few drops of rain begin, then rain "in earnest"; distant lightning; thunder rumbling; "the grass was green when the sun come up"
- **chapter_summary:** Dunk leaves the Snail's tent and cannot find his squire Egg; the boy's books remain but the boy is gone. Searching the tourney grounds, Dunk speaks with Ser Glendon Ball (who reveals Lord Peake tried to buy him into throwing his match against the Fiddler), then confronts Ser John the Fiddler, who admits he is no hedge knight and that "John" is not his true name. Lord Gormon Peake explains the wedding is a cover for a Blackfyre conspiracy to crown the Fiddler — Daemon Blackfyre II — if a living dragon hatches from the stolen egg at Whitewalls. Peake publicly frames Ser Glendon Ball for stealing the egg and has him seized; Lord Alyn Cockshaw lures Dunk to a well and tries to murder him out of jealous love for Daemon, but Dunk kills-or-drowns him and is saved and treated by Ser Maynard Plumm, who reveals Egg is "with the gods" at the Sept after trying to use a hidden ring. Dunk goes armed to reclaim his sword and shield from Uthor's squire.
- **unit_part:** 7 of 9

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Clouds in the east growing bigger and blacker; sun sinking to the west casting long shadows; sun swallowed by a cloud; a few drops of rain, then rain falling in earnest; leaden sky "spitting down rain"; ground softening to mud with pools of rainwater; distant lightning in the east; thunder rumbling
- **Season indicators:** "The grass was green when the sun come up" (men-at-arms' remark); green grass on the field
- **Time of day:** Late afternoon into evening; sun sinking west; growing dark
- **Lighting:** Long shadows across the yard; a shadow creeping across Dunk's face as the sun is swallowed; shadows and cowls; a well twenty feet deep with algae-slimed walls
- **Sounds:** Herald's trumpet and booming announcements; a "lusty cheer" for the Old Ox; splintering lances; splashes as knights fall; thunder; rain; teeth cracking, a bone snapping; Alyn thrashing and calling "Murder! Someone help me" from the well
- **Smells:** Perfumed lordling (Alyn Cockshaw); boiling wine; Plumm sniffing Dunk's silk tunic suspiciously
- **Notable sensory details:** Mud from waist to ankle on Glendon; blood and rain washing down cheeks; raindrops dimpling the well water twenty feet below; slime of algae on the well walls; wet smooth paving stones; Dunk's left arm red from shoulder to elbow, tunic clinging

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Mounting dread and anger over Egg's disappearance; wary of the conspirators; grim resolve; guilt ("you bear the blame. You should have kept him well away from here"); physical shock and nausea from his wound
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding Egg and getting him to safety; "The boy is blameless. If someone has a quarrel with me, it should not be him who answers for it."
- **Key decisions made:** Offers Glendon friendship and a path north; refuses the Fiddler's gifts; kills/drowns Cockshaw rather than surrender; resolves to go armed to the Sept; reclaims his sword and shield by threat
- **Self-deception flags:** Repeatedly calls himself dull — "Dunk the lunk, thick as a castle wall" / "Dunk the lank" — while in fact piecing the conspiracy together; initially assumes Egg simply went to watch the jousts; briefly doubts he could be the target rather than Egg
