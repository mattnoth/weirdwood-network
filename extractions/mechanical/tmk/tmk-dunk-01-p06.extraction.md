## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** TMK

- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** The tourney encampment at Whitewalls — Dunk and Egg's tent, then Ser Uthor Underleaf's tent
- **location_secondary:** (mentioned) Summerhall, Flea Bottom, Pennytree, the Redgrass Field, Ashford Meadow, Lannisport, Oldtown, King's Landing, the Pussywillows (brothel), across the narrow sea
- **approximate_timeline:** Unit part 6 of 9. Continuous with the tourney day: after his defeat, Dunk returns to his tent, finds Egg brushing Thunder, and refuses Egg's offers to save him. He takes his forfeit horse and armor to Ser Uthor's tent, where a coin-lesson exposes a Daemon Blackfyre gold piece, Uthor lays out his tourney-fixing scheme and Glendon Ball's true parentage, offers Dunk paid employment as a professional loser, and finally reveals he was paid to kill Dunk — closing with a veiled warning.
- **time_markers:** "at sunrise" (the gold delivered); "not long after the master of the games nailed up the pairings"; "Half a year ago" (Glendon's knighting); "The night before the battle" (Redgrass Field); "it has been many a year since I last slept beneath a hedge"; "Six dragons tendered in advance, four more promised when you died"
- **chapter_summary:** In his tent Dunk finds Egg brushing Thunder and, resigned to forfeiting his horse, arms, and armor to Ser Uthor Underleaf, refuses the boy's offers of the horse Rain and service at his father's household in Summerhall, even proposing they part ways. Inside Ser Uthor's richly appointed tent, Dunk examines a gold coin and identifies it as a Daemon Blackfyre piece — "traitor's gold." Ser Uthor, out of armor more merchant than knight, recounts his profitable habit of throwing tourney finals, bribing the master of the games Cosgrove to fix pairings, and exposes Ser Glendon Ball as the whore-born son of Penny Jenny, knighted by Ser Morgan Dunstable in a bargain for his sister's maidenhead. He offers to lend Dunk his gear back if Dunk will travel with him to be unhorsed for show, then reveals he was paid — six dragons in advance, four more on Dunk's death — to strike Dunk's head, the gold delivered by a hooded serving man whose master he does not name. He ties the danger to Dunk having caused the Trial of Seven at Ashford Meadow and the death of Baelor Breakspear, and to the exiled Bright Prince's friends.
- **unit_part:** 6 of 9

## Events & Actions
1. **Egg brushes Thunder; Dunk returns and they reunite** — Egg's eyes "far away," having taken Dunk's fall hard; Egg throws his arms around Dunk ("I knew no stupid snail could kill you, ser").
   - Agent: Egg
   - Patient: Thunder (grooming); Dunk (embrace)
   - Location: Dunk and Egg's tent
   - Outcome: reunion
2. **Dunk refuses Egg's offers and proposes they part** — Egg offers to lend/borrow coin, gives up his horse Rain, and proposes service at his father's Summerhall household; Dunk refuses ("I want no crumbs from your lord father's table") and says "Might be it's time we parted ways."
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg
   - Location: their tent
   - Outcome: Egg refuses to be handed to a proper master-at-arms ("I don't want a proper master-at-arms ... I want you")
3. **Dunk brings Thunder and his arms to Ser Uthor's tent** — Egg leads Thunder (laden with the arms, armor, and the Gallows Knight shield); Dunk tells him to wait outside and stoops through the flap.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Instrument: Thunder (laden with the forfeit gear)
   - Location: Ser Uthor's tent
   - Outcome: Dunk enters to render the forfeit
4. **Ser Uthor's coin-lesson exposes a Daemon Blackfyre gold piece** — Uthor tosses Will a "good" coin to inspect; Dunk reads DAEMON beneath the king's head and names it; Uthor confirms "the Pretender. Daemon Blackfyre struck his own coinage during his rebellion."
   - Agent: Ser Uthor (setting the test); Dunk (identifying the coin)
   - Patient: Will (the pupil)
   - Instrument: the gold coin
   - Location: Ser Uthor's tent
   - Witness: Dunk
   - Outcome: coin declared "Traitor's gold," to be melted down
5. **Ser Uthor strikes and dismisses Will** — "The Snail clouted him along the side of the head. 'Cretin.' ... He hit the man again. 'Get out of my sight.'"
   - Agent: Ser Uthor
   - Patient: Will
   - Location: Ser Uthor's tent
   - Outcome: Will scrambles from the tent
6. **Uthor questions Dunk's knighthood; Dunk recounts his origins** — Dunk tells how Ser Arlan of Pennytree found him chasing pigs in Flea Bottom, needed a squire after his old one was slain on the Redgrass Field, and taught him arms and riding; and how Dunk buried Arlan facing west.
   - Agent: Dunk (recounting)
   - Patient: Ser Uthor (listener)
   - Location: Ser Uthor's tent
   - Outcome: Uthor advises he "leave out the part about the pigs"
7. **Ransom negotiation over the forfeit** — Uthor refuses to lend the gear back on Dunk's oath or "scratch upon a scrap of paper," demanding "sweet silver ... The coin of the realm."
   - Agent: Ser Uthor; Dunk
   - Location: Ser Uthor's tent
   - Outcome: no ransom agreed
8. **Uthor lays out his tourney-fixing scheme** — He wins semis and throws finals for Butterwell's thirty-dragon second prize plus ransoms and wagers; he bribes Cosgrove, the master of the games, to draw the Old Ox and then Glendon Ball for him.
   - Agent: Ser Uthor
   - Instigator: Ser Uthor (bribing Cosgrove)
   - Location: Ser Uthor's tent
   - Outcome: Dunk realizes the pairings are fixed
9. **Uthor exposes Glendon Ball's parentage and sham knighting** — Born of the camp follower Penny/Redgrass Jenny; Fireball's paternity doubtful; raised with his sister in the Pussywillows; knighted by Ser Morgan Dunstable in the brothel before twenty witnesses in exchange for the sister's maidenhead.
   - Agent: Ser Uthor (recounting)
   - Patient: Dunk (listener)
   - Location: Ser Uthor's tent
   - Outcome: Dunk protests "That's just a tale"; Uthor cites Kirby Pimm as witness
10. **Uthor offers Dunk paid employment as a professional loser** — Lend back steed and armor if Dunk travels with him to be unhorsed in "twenty" more tourneys for a tenth of the winnings; suggests Dunk adopt a fiercer device and grow out hair and beard.
    - Agent: Ser Uthor
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Location: Ser Uthor's tent
    - Outcome: Dunk refuses ("I lost my armor, not my honor")
11. **Uthor reveals he was paid to kill Dunk** — He struck the head deliberately as the mortal target: "I was paid to strike you there" — six dragons in advance, four more promised on Dunk's death.
    - Agent: Ser Uthor
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Instrument: the tourney lance / iron-fist coronal
    - Instigator: an unnamed master (via the hooded serving man)
    - Outcome: Dunk survived; the attempt now disclosed
12. **Uthor names the payment's delivery and the likely cause of the enmity** — A hooded serving man brought the gold at sunrise, unnamed master; Uthor ties Dunk's danger to his having caused the Trial of Seven at Ashford Meadow, Baelor Breakspear's death at Maekar's hand, and the exiled Bright Prince's friends. Closes: "if you dance with dragons, you must expect to burn."
    - Agent: Ser Uthor (revealing)
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Location: Ser Uthor's tent
    - Outcome: Dunk shaken ("a cold hand on his heart")

- **Emotional state:** Humbled and ashamed (owes Egg an apology for scorning the "traitor's tourney" warning); needled to anger by Uthor's arrogance ("Dunk wanted to smash that smile off his face"); then dizzy and frightened at the murder reveal ("felt dizzy again"; "a cold hand on his heart")
