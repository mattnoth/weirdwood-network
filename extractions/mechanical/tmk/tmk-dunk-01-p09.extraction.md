# TMK — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** TMK
- **chapter_number:** 1

- **pov_chapter_number:** Dunk I
- **first_available:** pre-agot

- **location_secondary:** The Hand's pavilion (half a mile from the castle, under an elm tree); referenced: Ashford Meadow, Redgrass Field, King's Landing, Harrenhal, Summerhall, Maidenpool, Raventree, Duskendale
- **approximate_timeline:** Pre-dawn to late afternoon of a single day, immediately following the wedding at Whitewalls; the trial-of-arms tilt at dawn, the arrival and collapse of the rebellion by morning, Dunk's audience with Bloodraven that afternoon
- **time_markers:** "It was almost dawn"; "a gray mist was rising"; "the intervening hours"; "It had been only a few years since Dunk had squired for old Ser Arlan"; "the years fell away, and Dunk was back … at Ashford Meadow once again"; "Somewhere in the east, lightning cracked across a pale pink sky"; "the dawn washed over Whitewalls"; "a few heartbeats later"; "It was late that afternoon"; "asking for you for hours"; "In twenty years, no one will remember it existed"; "By the time the moon turns"; "When the moon turns"
- **chapter_summary:** At dawn in the muddy yard of Whitewalls, Dunk squires the accused Ser Glendon Ball for a trial-of-arms tilt against the Fiddler, now revealed as Daemon Blackfyre the Second; Dunk gives Glendon a tourney lance and Glendon unhorses Daemon into the mud, earning the name "the Brown Dragon." As dawn breaks, an army encircles the castle under the banners of King Aerys I and his Hand, Lord Brynden Rivers (Bloodraven), not Prince Maekar. Daemon's rebellion collapses without a fight; he is taken in golden fetters, his banner burned, and Gormon Peake and Black Tom Heddle are beheaded. That afternoon Bloodraven judges the captives — Butterwell is stripped of most of his fortune and Whitewalls is condemned to be pulled down and salted — then interviews his cousin Egg (Prince Aegon) and Dunk, frees them with gold, and explains that Daemon will be kept alive as a captive rather than martyred.
- **unit_part:** 9 of 9

## Events & Actions
1. **Dunk squires Ser Glendon for the trial of arms** — Dunk cinches Glendon's ill-fitting armor, fastens his helm to his gorget, helps him mount, and hands him his shield.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Ser Glendon Ball
   - Location: Whitewalls castle yard
   - Outcome: Glendon armed and mounted for the tilt
2. **Dunk gives Glendon a tourney lance instead of a war lance** — Recalling Ashford, Dunk replaces the war lance with a twelve-foot tourney lance ("It's what we used at Ashford, at the Trial of Seven").
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Ser Glendon Ball
   - Instrument: Tourney lance
   - Outcome: Glendon takes the tourney lance
3. **The herald proclaims the trial of arms** — Ser Glendon the Bastard is named as accused of theft and murder; Daemon of House Blackfyre, Second of His Name, comes forth to prove the accusations.
   - Agent: Herald
   - Location: Whitewalls lists
   - Outcome: The tilt is formally opened
4. **The tilt: Glendon unhorses Daemon** — The two charge; a crack, shout, and thump; Daemon is thrown facedown into a puddle, riderless, while Glendon wheels with a splintered lance.
   - Agent: Ser Glendon Ball
   - Patient: Daemon Blackfyre II
   - Instrument: Tourney lance
   - Location: Whitewalls lists
   - Witness: Dunk (who closes his eyes at the pass, then opens them to see the result)
   - Outcome: Daemon unhorsed and defeated; named "the Brown Dragon" jest for Glendon
5. **The royal army appears out of the mists** — A trumpet blows, sentries raise the alarum, and a host encircles Whitewalls under the banners of King Aerys I and Lord Brynden Rivers.
   - Agent: The royal host (Bloodraven's army)
   - Patient: The rebels at Whitewalls
   - Location: The field outside Whitewalls
   - Outcome: The castle is surrounded by a "ring of iron"
6. **The rebellion collapses without battle** — Daemon calls to fight through to King's Landing, but his men slink away; a grizzled squire refuses ("I'd sooner live").
   - Agent: The rebel lords and men-at-arms
   - Outcome: The Second Blackfyre Rebellion "ended with a whimper"
7. **Daemon is captured** — Daemon rides forth alone and challenges Bloodraven to single combat; Bloodraven's men pull him off his horse and clasp him in golden fetters; his banner is planted and burned.
   - Agent: Brynden Rivers (via his men)
   - Patient: Daemon Blackfyre II
   - Instrument: Golden fetters
   - Instigator: Brynden Rivers
   - Location: Before the royal host
   - Outcome: Daemon taken prisoner; his banner burned
8. **A Costayne knight kills Vrvwel's man** — When Vrvwel's man boasts of being one of Bloodraven's eyes and of coming rewards, a knight of Lord Costayne slits his throat ("Drink that. It's not Dornish, but it's red").
   - Agent: A knight of House Costayne
   - Patient: Vrvwel's man
   - Instrument: A blade
   - Outcome: Death (the only blood shed that day)
9. **The rebels surrender and are disarmed** — A silent column trudges through the gates to toss weapons into a pile, then is bound and led away to await judgment; Dunk, Kyle the Cat, and Glendon Ball among them; Maynard Plumm has vanished.
   - Agent: The captured rebels
   - Location: Whitewalls gates
   - Outcome: Prisoners bound to await Bloodraven's judgment
10. **Beheading of Gormon Peake and Black Tom Heddle** — Their severed heads are impaled on spears flanking Bloodraven's pavilion, shields displayed beneath; Dunk closes Gormon Peake's dead eyes ("I owed him that much").
    - Agent: Brynden Rivers
    - Patient: Gormon Peake; Black Tom Heddle
    - Location: The Hand's pavilion entrance
    - Outcome: Two rebel leaders executed
11. **Bloodraven judges Lord Butterwell** — Rivers lets Butterwell keep a tenth of his fortune and his wife but declares Whitewalls forfeit, to be razed and salted.
    - Agent: Brynden Rivers
    - Patient: Lord Ambrose Butterwell
    - Location: The Hand's pavilion
    - Outcome: Butterwell stripped and dismissed; Whitewalls condemned
12. **Bloodraven interviews Egg and Dunk** — Rivers questions how his cousin came to a nest of traitors; Egg asserts he had matters in hand and demands Glendon Ball be found and rewarded; Rivers frees them and grants gold at Egg's request.
    - Agent: Brynden Rivers
    - Patient: Egg; Dunk
    - Location: The Hand's pavilion
    - Outcome: Egg and Dunk freed; gold granted (as a loan, per Dunk)
13. **Bloodraven explains Daemon will be spared** — Rivers says killing Daemon would make him a martyr and prompt Bittersteel to crown Haegon; alive, Daemon is an obstacle to a third Blackfyre king and an ornament to the court.
    - Agent: Brynden Rivers
    - Location: The Hand's pavilion
    - Outcome: Daemon to be kept a living captive

- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting Glendon safely through the tilt, then surviving Bloodraven's judgment and understanding what will become of the captives and Daemon
