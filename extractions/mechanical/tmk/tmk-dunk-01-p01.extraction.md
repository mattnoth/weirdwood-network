# TMK — Dunk 1

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** TMK
- **chapter_number:** 1

- **first_available:** pre-agot
- **location_primary:** The road from Stoney Sept toward the lake and ferry
- **location_secondary:** Stoney Sept (town gates); the lakeshore (approaching)
- **approximate_timeline:** Opening scene of the unit; spans the departure from Stoney Sept and, "six days later," the road encounter with Lord Gormon Peake's party
- **time_markers:** "A light summer rain was falling" (opening); "Three days ago" (the hunchbacked septon preaching, per Egg); the septon's head "fresh from the look of it"; "Six days later, the rain was just a memory"; "Their last hot meal had been three days ago"; last ferry crossing "six years ago, or maybe seven"; Daemon Blackfyre's revolt / Redgrass Field "Sixteen years ago"; realm "ruled by the descendants of Aegon the Conqueror" for "two hundred years"; "The Wall has stood eight thousand years"; "a thousand leagues from here to there"
- **chapter_summary:** Dunk and his squire Egg leave Stoney Sept in a summer rain, passing a traitor's head on a spike that they recognize as a hunchbacked septon who had preached treason against Lord Bloodraven three days earlier. Six days later, near the lake and its ferry, they argue over spending coin on an inn and a bed, and Egg hints at using his "boot" to cross the water, which Dunk forbids because Egg's shaved head hides Targaryen features. A richly outfitted party overtakes them; Dunk and Egg hide behind a thornbush and are challenged by the sour Lord Gormon Peake of Starpike, alongside two young lords, Alyn Cockshaw and the splendidly dressed hedge knight Ser John the Fiddler. The Fiddler invites Dunk to Lord Butterwell's wedding at Whitewalls, but Peake dismisses him. Afterward Egg identifies Peake and explains that House Peake lost two castles for backing the Black Dragon and that Peake killed Ser Arlan's nephew Roger of Pennytree at the Redgrass Field; Dunk resolves to go by Whitewalls for the feast.
- **unit_part:** 1 of 9

## Events & Actions
1. **Departure from Stoney Sept in the rain** — Dunk on Thunder and Egg on Rain, leading the mule Maester, take their leave of Stoney Sept in a light summer rain.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: Stoney Sept
2. **Egg reins up at the traitor's head** — At the town gate Egg stops sharply at the sight of a fresh severed head impaled on an iron spike, crows at work on it.
   - Agent: Egg
   - Location: Stoney Sept gate
3. **Dunk recalls stealing a head as a boy** — Dunk tells Egg he once stole a head off a spike in King's Landing; internally he admits Ferret took it and he only caught it, and that he and his friends terrorized girls with it.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg (listener)
4. **They recognize the septon** — Dunk squints and knows the face; Egg identifies it as the hunchbacked septon who preached against Bloodraven three days past.
   - Agent: Egg
   - Location: Stoney Sept gate
5. **Recollection of the septon's sermon** — Dunk remembers the septon's treasonous preaching against Bloodraven in the market square.
   - Agent: The hunchbacked septon (in recollection)
   - Location: Stoney Sept market square (recalled)
6. **Riding out through the gates** — Dunk spurs Thunder and the pair ride through the gates, listening to the rain.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: Stoney Sept gates
7. **Argument over inn, bed, and coin (six days later)** — On the road near the lake, Dunk and Egg debate stopping at the inn; Dunk refuses to spend coin on a bed, insisting they sleep beneath the stars and save pennies for the ferryman.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg
   - Location: The road near the lake
8. **Egg proposes the boot; Dunk forbids it** — Egg suggests using his "boot" to cross the lake; Dunk refuses because using it is dangerous and word would spread, given Egg's concealed Targaryen features.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: Egg
   - Outcome: Boot refused
9. **They hear the party and conceal themselves** — Egg hears horses; Dunk sees the dust of a large, hasty party and leads them off the road to hide behind a thornbush; Dunk arms himself with his shield.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: Beside the road
   - Outcome: Concealment
10. **Two lordlings gallop past** — Alyn Cockshaw (on the bay) and John the Fiddler (on the black stallion) streak by whooping and laughing.
    - Agent: Alyn Cockshaw; Ser John the Fiddler
    - Location: The road
11. **Peake reins up and challenges the thornbush** — Lord Gormon Peake, at the head of the column, orders whoever is in the bush to show themselves; two crossbowmen aim quarrels.
    - Agent: Lord Gormon Peake
    - Patient: Dunk (and Egg)
    - Instrument: Crossbows (aimed by his men)
    - Instigator: Lord Gormon Peake
    - Location: The road
12. **Dunk steps out and parleys** — Dunk emerges, shield on arm, and declares himself a hedge knight seeking service; Peake interrogates him about the hanged-man arms, the scar, and possible robbery.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Lord Gormon Peake
    - Location: The road
13. **The young knights mock Dunk** — The returning Alyn Cockshaw calls the pair brigands and mocks Dunk as a lout and giant; the Fiddler orders Alyn to beg Dunk's pardon, which he does grudgingly.
    - Agent: Lord Alyn Cockshaw
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Instigator: Ser John the Fiddler (orders the apology)
14. **The Fiddler invites Dunk to Whitewalls** — Ser John the Fiddler invites Dunk to ride with them to Whitewalls for Lord Butterwell's wedding and to break lances; he says he would love to try his lance on a man Dunk's size.
    - Agent: Ser John the Fiddler
    - Patient: Dunk
15. **Peake dismisses Dunk** — Peake says "We do not need his sort," suggests Dunk might be a brigand or a spy of Bloodraven, and tells him to go "down in Dorne"; Dunk declines the invitation.
    - Agent: Lord Gormon Peake
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Outcome: Dunk not taken into the company; party departs
16. **Egg identifies Peake and his history** — After the party rides off, Egg names Gormon Peake, Lord of Starpike, and explains House Peake lost two of its three castles for fighting for the Black Dragon.
    - Agent: Egg
    - Patient: Dunk
17. **Dunk recalls Roger of Pennytree's death** — Dunk remembers Ser Arlan telling him that his sister's son Roger of Pennytree had his head smashed in by a mace wielded by a lord with three castles on his shield at the Redgrass Field — Gormon Peake.
    - Agent: Lord Gormon Peake (in the recalled event)
    - Patient: Roger of Pennytree
    - Instrument: A mace
    - Location: Redgrass Field (recalled)
    - Outcome: Roger's death
18. **Decision to go by Whitewalls** — Dunk resolves to stop at Whitewalls for the wedding feast, considers entering the lists or a melee, and pictures unhorsing Peake; Egg objects that it is not the time.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Egg
    - Outcome: Plan to divert to Whitewalls

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Reflective and nostalgic about his boyhood in Flea Bottom; wary and mistrustful of the highborn party; stung by the mockery of his size; nursing a fresh grudge against Peake and a private daydream of unhorsing him
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting a hot meal and saving coin for the ferry; guarding Egg's concealed identity; the long journey north to the Wall
- **Key decisions made:** Refuse to pay for an inn bed; forbid Egg's use of the "boot"; get off the road and hide from the party; step out and parley; decline the Fiddler's invitation in the moment, then resolve to go by Whitewalls for the feast and possibly the lists
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself the Redgrass Field and Peake have "nought to do with me" even as he pictures riding Peake down; frames the Whitewalls detour as being about filling their bellies while imagining winning ransoms and unhorsing Peake
