- **approximate_timeline:** The wedding night, continuing directly from the feast; unit part 4 of 9. Sequence: Dunk leaves the hall to relieve himself and overhears Peake and another lord conspiring; returns to the frolics; the bedding, in which he is made to carry the bride; he sees the dragon's egg and is warned off it; he retreats to the tower roof where the Fiddler reveals his ambitions; Peake threatens him; he returns to his tent and instructs Egg to enter him in the lists under a false name.
- **time_markers:** "Outside, the night wind"; the jousting "on the morrow"; "It might be a year before I have another" (cup of hippocras); the Black Dragon's cause "Sixteen years ago"; the Fiddler "They were twelve and I was only seven... I am two-and-twenty now"; night, "beneath the stars," "moonlight"
- **chapter_summary:** Drunk and seeking a privy in the outer yard of Whitewalls, Dunk overhears Lord Gormon Peake and a deep-voiced lord conspiring over a prince, a dragon's egg they expect to hatch, Bittersteel, Bloodraven, and the gold and swords they mean to gather. Back inside he endures the wedding frolics, hears Ser Maynard Plumm's tale that the bride was deflowered by a scullion at the Twins, and is dragooned into carrying the bride to the marriage bed during the bedding, where he handles Lord Butterwell's dragon's egg and is warned off it by the black-bearded knight from Peake's conversation. Retreating to the tower roof, Dunk is joined by John the Fiddler, who claims to have dreamed Dunk as a Kingsguard knight, declares he will take the throne, and insists a dragon will hatch and that he must have the egg. Peake and two guards remove the Fiddler to bed and Peake threatens Dunk with death should he repeat what he heard, whereupon Dunk vomits on his boots. Returning to his tent, Dunk finds Egg reading a roll of arms with a split lip from fighting squires who called his father, Prince Maekar, a kinslayer; Dunk tells him to hold back his true name and enter him in the lists as the Gallows Knight.
- **unit_part:** 4 of 9

- **Notable sensory details:** The dragon's egg — fine red scales "shining bright as jewels," heavier than expected, with gold flecks and "whorls of midnight black"; the suit of gem-and-gold-inlaid plate by the bedchamber door; the Myrish carpets; the bride nearly naked "save for the stocking on her left leg"

## Events & Actions
1. **Dunk leaves the hall drunk and crosses the outer yard** — Swaying with wine, Dunk goes out to find the inner ward and send Egg to enter him in the lists; he passes the erected lists, the three-tiered viewing stand, and the tents with their lance-racks.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: The outer yard
   - Outcome: Dunk gets turned around in the unfamiliar castle
2. **Dunk gets lost, passing the kennels and the sept** — The hounds bark and howl at him; he doubles back past the sept, where a laughing woman runs by pursued by a falling-down bald knight; needing to relieve himself, he steps behind bushes beneath a flight of pale stone steps.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: The kennels, sept, and stone steps
   - Outcome: Dunk relieves himself behind the bushes
3. **Dunk overhears Peake and the black-bearded knight conspiring** — From above the steps, Dunk hears Lord Peake and a deep-voiced lord discuss Bittersteel ("No bastard can be trusted"), a prince who insists the egg will hatch and once dreamed his brothers dead, Old Milkblood's expectations, Bloodraven, and their plan to gather Butterwell's gold and House Frey's swords so that Harrenhal and then the Brackens will follow. Dunk holds his breath and does not reveal himself.
   - Agent: Lord Gormon Peake; the black-bearded knight (speak)
   - Patient: Dunk (unseen listener)
   - Witness: Dunk (overhears from concealment)
   - Location: Below the stone steps
   - Outcome: Dunk keeps hidden; wonders whom they mean ("Fireball's son?")
4. **The wedding frolics** — Back in the hall Dunk finds a Frey daughter playing the harp badly, jugglers and tumblers, and the hall bellowing "The Bear and the Maiden Fair"; Lord Caswell passes out face-down in wine and Lady Vyrwel weeps for an unknown cause; the wine keeps flowing.
   - Agent: The entertainers and guests
   - Location: The great hall
5. **Maynard tells the tale of the bride's scullion** — Pointing out Frey's chinless son as "the lad who made this marriage," Ser Maynard says the bride was deflowered by a scullion at the Twins and caught with him by her little brother, forcing the match; Dunk doubts it, reasoning Butterwell would not wed and gift a dragon's egg for a soiled girl.
   - Agent: Ser Maynard Plumm
   - Patient: Dunk (listener)
   - Location: The great hall
6. **Dunk dozes and dreams** — He lays his head on his arms and sleeps, dreaming of Tanselle Too-Tall and the Red Widow, until the shouts of "Bed them!" wake him.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Location: The great hall
7. **The bedding begins** — Half the guests are on their feet shouting; Ser Franklyn Frey carries the bride down the aisle while men strip her, and the ladies pull Lord Butterwell from his chair, unlacing his boots and tunic as he flails and laughs, both groom and Frey drunk.
   - Agent: Ser Franklyn Frey; Lady Vyrwel and the ladies
   - Patient: The bride; Lord Butterwell
   - Location: The great hall
8. **Dunk is made to carry the bride** — John the Fiddler drags Dunk to his feet crying "Let the giant carry her!"; Dunk climbs the tower stair with the squirming, near-naked bride in his arms, the men japing and the dwarfs swarming his legs with bladders.
   - Agent: Ser John the Fiddler (instigates); Dunk (carries)
   - Patient: The bride
   - Instigator: Ser John the Fiddler
   - Location: The tower stair
   - Outcome: Dunk reaches the bedchamber; the bride down to one stocking
9. **Dunk deposits the bride and hauls off a groping dwarf** — In the lavish bedchamber Dunk plops the bride on the marriage bed; a dwarf leaps beside her and seizes her breast; Dunk grabs the dwarf by the collar to throw him out.
   - Agent: Dunk
   - Patient: The dwarf; the bride
   - Location: The bedchamber
10. **Dunk handles the dragon's egg** — Seeing the egg on its plinth, Dunk drops the dwarf and lifts the egg — bigger than a hen's egg, red-scaled with gold flecks and black whorls, heavier than expected — turning it in his hands.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: The dragon's egg
    - Location: The bedchamber
11. **The black-bearded knight warns Dunk off the egg** — The big black-bearded, boil-marked knight — whose deep angry voice Dunk recognizes as Peake's companion — orders him to put the egg down and keep his "greasy fingers off His Lordship's treasures"; Dunk complies, sets the egg back carefully, and shoves past him out the door.
    - Agent: The black-bearded knight (warns); Dunk (complies)
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Location: The bedchamber
    - Outcome: Dunk leaves; Dunk realizes the knight is the man from Peake's conversation
12. **Dunk retreats to the tower roof** — To avoid the women bringing Butterwell to his bride, Dunk climbs to the tower roof beneath the stars; feeling sick and guilty, he recalls Tanselle's puppet show and the three men dead at Ashford.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Location: The tower roof
13. **The Fiddler reveals his dreams and ambition** — John the Fiddler joins Dunk and says he dreamed of Dunk as a White Sword of the Kingsguard, that his dreams are true, that he will "take the throne" to make Dunk a Kingsguard, and that a dragon will hatch from the egg; he recounts dreaming his brothers dead when he was seven and they twelve. He asks for Dunk's sword and says he must have the dragon's egg.
    - Agent: Ser John the Fiddler (reveals)
    - Patient: Dunk
    - Location: The tower roof
    - Outcome: Dunk is unconvinced, remembering Daeron's dream and Baelor's death
14. **Peake removes the Fiddler and threatens Dunk** — Peake and two men-at-arms come onto the roof; Peake's guards take the Fiddler down to bed, reminding him he jousts Kirby Pimm on the morrow. Alone with Dunk, Peake asks what the Fiddler promised, then threatens "three feet of cold steel through your belly" if Dunk repeats what he heard.
    - Agent: Lord Gormon Peake (threatens); the guards (remove the Fiddler)
    - Patient: Dunk; Ser John the Fiddler
    - Instigator: Lord Gormon Peake (commands the guards)
    - Location: The tower roof
    - Outcome: The Fiddler is taken to bed; Dunk is threatened to silence
15. **Dunk vomits on Peake's boots** — Trying to clear his wits, Dunk bends double and retches, spattering Peake's boots; Peake curses hedge knights, and Dunk answers "We are wanted nowhere and turn up everywhere"; Peake repeats his warning and departs.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Lord Gormon Peake (boots)
    - Location: The tower roof
16. **Dunk returns to the hall, then his tent** — Only Ser Maynard remains, asking whether the bride had flour on her; Dunk pours and abandons a last cup of wine, then crosses the pavilion field to his tent, where Egg sits reading a roll of arms by candlelight.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Location: The hall; the pavilion field; the tent
17. **Egg's split lip and the traitor's tourney** — Dunk sees Egg's broken lip; Egg admits he fought squires who called Prince Maekar a kinslayer, hitting Ser Mallor's squire after a jibe about Aerys. Egg points out that Lord Sunderland — a Sisterman bearing three pale ladies' heads on undy green and blue — fought for the Black Dragon, and calls it "a traitor's tourney."
    - Agent: Egg (reveals); the squires (insulted Maekar, earlier/recalled)
    - Patient: Egg
    - Location: The tent
    - Outcome: Dunk chides Egg for not holding his tongue
18. **Dunk orders the false name** — Dunk tells Egg to find Cosgrove, the master of the games, and enter him not under his own name but as "the Gallows Knight," lest a lord recall Ser Duncan the Tall from Ashford; he pinches out the candle.
    - Agent: Dunk
    - Patient: Egg
    - Location: The tent
    - Outcome: Dunk will compete as a mystery knight

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Very drunk and dizzy throughout; wary and unsettled by the overheard plot; melancholy over Tanselle and Lady Rohanne; aroused and abashed carrying the bride; guilty at touching the dragon's egg (recalling the deaths at Ashford); intimidated but defiant with Peake; sickened to vomiting; then near-sober and angry over the "traitor's tourney" and Egg's safety.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Keeping his own and Egg's identities hidden; the dragon's egg and the conspiracy around it; Egg's welfare and loose tongue; longing for Tanselle.
- **Key decisions made:** Not to shout after the conspirators or reveal himself; to put the egg back and leave the bedchamber; to go up to the roof rather than face the bedding party; to rebuff the Fiddler's offers and leave; to hold back his true name and enter the lists as "the Gallows Knight."
- **Self-deception flags:** Talks himself out of the plot's significance — "Dunk the drunk, what is it that you think you heard?"; tells Egg "Words are wind... Just let them blow on past you," minimizing the treason talk even as he grows angry and admits it is "a traitor's tourney"; dismisses his own arousal and his longing for Tanselle as things he "must have dreamed."
