- **chapter_summary:** Sansa has been confined in a tower room of Maegor's Holdfast for three days since fighting broke out in the Red Keep. On the second day, bells ring announcing King Robert's death. On the third morning, Ser Boros Blount escorts her to the council chambers where Queen Cersei, Lord Petyr Baelish, Grand Maester Pycelle, and Lord Varys inform her that her father Eddard Stark has been arrested for treason. They pressure Sansa into writing letters to her mother, brother Robb, aunt Lysa Arryn, and grandfather Hoster Tully urging them to swear fealty to Joffrey, dangling the promise of her marriage to Joffrey as incentive. Jeyne Poole is removed from Sansa's room and placed in Littlefinger's custody.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa confined in Maegor's Holdfast** — For three days, Sansa and Jeyne Poole are locked in a tower room. Guards refuse to answer questions or let them leave.
2. **Fighting in the Red Keep (Day 1)** — Real combat heard throughout the castle; screams, curses, sounds of dying men. Jeyne Poole is thrust into Sansa's room that night, reporting bodies on the Tower of the Hand stairs and that the Hound broke down her door with a warhammer.
3. **Silence falls (Day 2)** — Fighting ends. Lannister guardsmen control the walls. The iron portcullis is down and drawbridge raised. Servants bring food and clothes but refuse to speak.
4. **Death bells ring (Day 2 sunset)** — A great bell tolls, answered by bells from the Great Sept of Baelor. Sansa intuits that King Robert is dead.
5. **Sansa escorted to council chambers (Day 3)** — Ser Boros Blount brings Sansa across the drawbridge (now lowered) to the council chambers. She sees a body impaled on spikes in the dry moat.
6. **Council informs Sansa of Eddard's arrest for treason** — Cersei, Varys, Pycelle, and Littlefinger tell Sansa her father attempted to steal Joffrey's throne and wrote a letter to Stannis inviting him to take the crown.
7. **Jeyne Poole transferred to Littlefinger's custody** — Cersei orders Ser Boros to take Jeyne to Littlefinger's apartments; instructs that Jeyne be told Littlefinger will take her to see her father. Cersei specifies "not in the city."
8. **Sansa pressured to write letters** — The council dangles the promise of marriage to Joffrey in exchange for Sansa writing letters to her family urging them to swear fealty.
9. **Sansa writes four letters** — To Catelyn, Robb, Lysa Arryn, and Hoster Tully, urging fealty to Joffrey. Letters are sealed with Eddard's direwolf seal (in Varys's possession).
10. **Sansa returned to her room** — Ser Mandon Moore escorts her back. Jeyne and her belongings are gone. Sansa reads stories by the fire.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terrified, confused, desperate; oscillates between hope (that Joffrey loves her) and dread (about her father's fate); resolved to be a "lady" and maintain courtesies
- **Primary preoccupation:** Preserving her betrothal to Joffrey; secondarily, concern for her father's safety
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees to write letters to her family urging them to swear fealty to Joffrey; earlier decision (recalled) to tell Cersei about Eddard's plan to leave King's Landing
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes Joffrey is "good and kind" and will show mercy; believes Cersei shows genuine warmth; convinces herself that writing the letters will lead to a happy outcome where Joffrey eventually pardons Eddard; dismisses Jeyne Poole as "such a child" while being equally naive herself; thinks being a "lady" and maintaining courtesies will protect her
