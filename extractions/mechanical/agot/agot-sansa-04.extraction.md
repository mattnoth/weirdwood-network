- **chapter_summary:** Sansa has been confined in a tower of Maegor's Holdfast for three days since fighting broke out in the Red Keep. On the third morning, Ser Boros Blount escorts her to the council chambers where Queen Cersei, Lord Petyr Baelish, Grand Maester Pycelle, and Lord Varys inform her that her father Eddard Stark has been arrested for treason. Cersei and the councillors manipulate Sansa into writing letters to her mother, brother Robb, aunt Lysa Arryn, and grandfather Hoster Tully urging them to swear fealty to Joffrey. Sansa is returned to her tower room to find Jeyne Poole gone, sent away to Lord Petyr's custody.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa dresses on the third morning** — She struggles with silver fastenings without servants; Jeyne Poole is too distraught to help.
2. **Sansa tries to comfort Jeyne** — Promises to ask the queen to let Jeyne see her father; Jeyne cries harder.
3. **Flashback: First day of confinement** — Sansa hears fighting throughout the Red Keep; calls for her father, Septa Mordane, the king, and Joffrey; guards give no answer. Late that night, Jeyne Poole is thrust into the room, bruised and shaking, reporting bodies on the stairs of the Tower of the Hand and that the Hound broke down her door with a warhammer.
4. **Second day of confinement** — Sansa sees from her window that the portcullis is down, drawbridge raised, and Lannister guards on the walls. Fighting is over. Servants bring meals but won't speak. Women bring clothes from the Tower of the Hand but flee from Sansa.
5. **Sansa asks to see the queen or Joffrey** — Guards refuse to let them leave or deliver messages.
6. **Bells ring at sunset on the second day** — A great bell rings, answered by bells from the Great Sept of Baelor. Sansa knows King Robert is dead.
7. **Sansa dreams of Joffrey on the throne** — She dreams of sitting beside him in a gown of woven gold with a crown on her head.
8. **Ser Boros Blount escorts Sansa to the queen** — On the third morning, Sansa crosses the lowered drawbridge, sees a body impaled on iron spikes in the dry moat, and averts her eyes.
9. **Council meeting with Cersei** — In the council chambers, Cersei sits with Petyr Baelish, Grand Maester Pycelle, and Lord Varys, all in black mourning clothes. Ser Mandon Moore guards the door.
10. **Cersei orders Jeyne removed** — Cersei directs Ser Boros to take Jeyne to Littlefinger's apartments, telling Jeyne that Littlefinger will take her to see her father. Cersei wants Jeyne gone before Sansa returns.
11. **Sansa told her father is a traitor** — Varys declares Eddard a traitor. Pycelle claims Eddard betrayed his oath. Cersei shows Sansa a torn, bloodstained letter from Eddard to Stannis Baratheon, sealed with the Stark direwolf.
12. **Sansa pleads to marry Joffrey** — Cersei, Varys, and Pycelle argue her "traitor's blood" makes the match dangerous. Littlefinger notes Sansa resembles her mother Catelyn. Sansa desperately denies any disloyalty.
13. **Sansa reveals she went to Cersei before the coup** — Sansa confirms she told the queen about Eddard's plan to send her and Arya away from King's Landing.
14. **Sansa disowns Arya** — Says "She has the traitor's blood, not me" and contrasts herself favorably against her sister.
15. **Cersei proposes a test of loyalty** — Sansa must write letters to her mother, Robb, Lysa Arryn, and Hoster Tully urging them to swear fealty to Joffrey and come to King's Landing.
16. **Sansa agrees to write the letters** — She writes four letters; Varys stamps each with Eddard's direwolf seal in pale white beeswax.
17. **Sansa returned to her tower** — Ser Mandon Moore escorts her back. Jeyne and all her things are gone.
18. **Sansa reads stories by the fire** — She reads tales of Florian and Jonquil, Lady Shella and the Rainbow Knight, and Prince Aemon.
19. **Sansa realizes she forgot to ask about Arya** — As she falls asleep, she realizes she never asked about her sister.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terrified, confused, desperate to please, intermittently hopeful when marriage to Joffrey is dangled; grief suppressed in favor of survival and desire; lonely after Jeyne leaves
- **Primary preoccupation:** Maintaining her betrothal to Joffrey; processing her father's arrest; trying to behave like a proper lady
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to write the four letters urging her family to swear fealty to Joffrey; chooses to cooperate fully with Cersei; plans to plead with Joffrey for her father's mercy once she can see him
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes Joffrey is "good and kind" and will listen to her pleas for mercy; believes Cersei's kindness is genuine ("the sweetest and saddest smile"); tells herself she can become queen and then pardon her father; resolves to be "a lady no matter what" as a coping mechanism; gratefully thinks "no more weeping" when Jeyne is gone, not recognizing the sinister implications; forgets to ask about Arya entirely
