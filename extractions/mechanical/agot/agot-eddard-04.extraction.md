- **chapter_summary:** Eddard Stark arrives at the Red Keep and is immediately summoned to a small council meeting, where he learns Robert has commanded a tournament in his honor costing ninety thousand gold dragons. Littlefinger reveals the Crown is over six million gold pieces in debt. After the meeting, Ned discovers the castle yard in chaos and reflects on his daughters' misery following the incident on the Trident. Littlefinger leads Ned through a secret passage out of the castle to a brothel he owns, where Catelyn Stark is secretly waiting with Ser Rodrik Cassel. Catelyn tells Ned about the assassination attempt on Bran and the Valyrian steel dagger traced to Tyrion Lannister, and Ned instructs her to return to Winterfell and prepare northern defenses.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned arrives at the Red Keep** — Rides through the bronze doors sore, tired, hungry, and irritable; the king's steward informs him of an urgent council meeting.
2. **Ned reluctantly agrees to attend council** — Initially refuses, then relents to avoid offending the council before he has even begun.
3. **Ned is assigned Jon Arryn's chambers** — The king's steward tells him he has been given Lord Arryn's former chambers in the Tower of the Hand.
4. **Ned instructs Vayon Poole** — Orders his steward to see his daughters to their bedchambers and have Jory keep them there; specifically says Arya is not to go exploring.
5. **Ned enters the council chambers** — Finds four councillors waiting: Varys, Renly, Littlefinger, and Grand Maester Pycelle.
6. **Varys greets Ned with concern about Joffrey** — Mentions visiting the sept to light candles for the prince's recovery.
7. **Ned meets Renly and Littlefinger** — Remarks on Renly's resemblance to Robert; exchanges barbed words with Littlefinger about Catelyn and Brandon.
8. **The council discusses Robert's command** — Renly presents a sealed letter ordering a great tournament in Ned's honor with ninety thousand gold dragons in prizes.
9. **Littlefinger reveals the Crown's debt** — The treasury is empty; the Crown owes more than six million gold pieces to the Lannisters, Lord Tyrell, the Iron Bank of Braavos, Tyroshi trading cartels, and the Faith.
10. **Ned is stunned and ends the meeting** — Vows to speak with Robert about the extravagance; abruptly adjourns the council.
11. **Ned reflects on the journey's misery** — The last fortnight was terrible: Sansa blamed Arya, Arya brooded over the butcher's boy, the Starks rode ahead to separate from the Lannisters.
12. **Littlefinger intercepts Ned in the inner bailey** — Tells him he's going the wrong way and leads him through a secret passage.
13. **Ned and Littlefinger traverse secret passages** — Through a tower, down stairs, across a sunken courtyard, along a corridor with Targaryen armor, through an oak-and-iron door.
14. **They climb down the bluff outside the castle** — Using niches cut in the rock face, then ride horses into the city.
15. **They arrive at Littlefinger's brothel** — Ned is furious, slams Littlefinger against a wall, puts a dagger to his chin.
16. **Ser Rodrik intervenes** — Confirms Catelyn truly awaits upstairs.
17. **Ned is reunited with Catelyn** — She embraces him fiercely; she has been receiving reports from Littlefinger.
18. **Catelyn tells Ned about the assassination attempt** — The fire in the library tower, the catspaw, Bran's wolf saving him, the Valyrian steel dagger traced to Tyrion Lannister, Varys's knowledge of their investigation.
19. **Ned and Littlefinger debate what to do** — Littlefinger counsels dropping the dagger in the river; Ned refuses; Littlefinger agrees to help investigate Jon Arryn's death.
20. **Catelyn reveals she told Littlefinger their suspicions about Jon Arryn's death** — Littlefinger has promised to help find the truth.
21. **Catelyn warns about Varys's knowledge** — Ser Rodrik spoke to Ser Aron Santagar in secrecy but Varys learned of it anyway.
22. **Ned instructs Catelyn to return to Winterfell** — Orders her to have Tallhart and Glover raise bowmen to fortify Moat Cailin, Manderly to strengthen White Harbor, and a watch kept over Theon Greyjoy.
23. **Ned and Catelyn say farewell** — Ned plans to continue investigating Jon Arryn's death and go to Robert when he has proof.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Exhausted, irritable, and overwhelmed upon arrival; stunned and aghast at the Crown's debt; angry and tense during the encounter with Littlefinger; deeply conflicted about the conspiracy against Bran; rage at the memory of Lady's death and Mycah; torn between duty and fear about confronting Robert
- **Primary preoccupation:** The assassination attempt on Bran and what it means — who ordered it and whether it implicates the queen or king; guilt over killing Sansa's wolf; the misery of his daughters; dread at the political games of King's Landing
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees to attend the council; adjourns the meeting over the tournament; refuses to drop the dagger in the river; accepts Littlefinger's help reluctantly; sends Catelyn home with military orders to fortify the North; resolves to take proof to Robert when he finds it
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells Catelyn "It will not come to that" about war while praying it is true; privately questions whether Robert is "the man I think he is" but tells Catelyn he'll go to Robert for justice; dismisses Varys's intelligence capabilities as merely "spies" despite Catelyn's insistence it is more
