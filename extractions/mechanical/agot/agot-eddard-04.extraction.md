# AGOT — Eddard IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 21
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard IV
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard IV
- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Small council chambers; Tower of the Hand; inner bailey; Littlefinger's brothel (along the river, King's Landing)
- **approximate_timeline:** Day of Ned's arrival at King's Landing, shortly before King Robert arrives
- **time_markers:** Ned arrives during daytime, tired from riding; council meeting happens shortly after arrival; dusk falls during Littlefinger's walk to the brothel ("ruddy glow of dusk," "gathering dusk"); "the last fortnight of their journey had been a misery"; Brandon Stark has been dead "some fourteen years now"
- **chapter_summary:** Eddard Stark arrives at the Red Keep and is immediately summoned to his first small council meeting. There he meets Varys, Renly Baratheon, Littlefinger, and Grand Maester Pycelle, and learns Robert wants a lavish tournament and that the Crown is over six million gold pieces in debt. After ending the meeting early, Ned is led by Littlefinger through secret passages and down a cliff face to a brothel, where Catelyn Stark awaits with Ser Rodrik Cassel. Catelyn reveals that an assassin tried to kill Bran with a Valyrian steel dagger traced to Tyrion Lannister, and Ned instructs her to return to Winterfell and prepare northern defenses.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned arrives at the Red Keep** — He rides through the bronze doors, sore, tired, hungry, and irritable, and is immediately told the small council has convened urgently.
2. **Ned initially refuses, then agrees to attend** — He snaps at the steward, then relents, not wanting to offend the council before he has begun.
3. **Ned is assigned Jon Arryn's chambers** — The king's steward gives him quarters in the Tower of the Hand and offers to arrange garments.
4. **Ned instructs Vayon Poole** — He tells Poole to see his daughters to their bedchambers and have Jory keep them there; Arya is not to go exploring.
5. **Ned enters the small council chambers** — He finds Varys, Renly, Littlefinger, and Pycelle waiting. The king's seat is empty.
6. **Varys accosts Ned** — He expresses sympathy about the kingsroad troubles and says candles have been lit for Joffrey's recovery; leaves powder stains on Ned's sleeve.
7. **Ned meets Renly** — He notes Renly's striking resemblance to a young Robert.
8. **Ned and Littlefinger exchange barbs** — Littlefinger references Catelyn; Ned responds by referencing Brandon. The verbal sparring establishes mutual antagonism.
9. **Ned greets Pycelle and takes his seat** — He notes the Grand Maester's elaborate chain and sits beside the empty king's chair.
10. **Ned observes he does not belong** — Reflects that he does not belong here with these men; remembers Robert saying "I am surrounded by flatterers and fools."
11. **Varys explains absences** — Stannis has gone to Dragonstone; Barristan Selmy rides with the king.
12. **Renly presents Robert's command** — A sealed letter ordering a grand tournament in Ned's honor.
13. **Tournament costs are discussed** — Forty thousand to the champion, twenty thousand to second, twenty thousand for the melee, ten thousand for archery — ninety thousand gold pieces in prizes alone, plus feast expenses.
14. **Littlefinger reveals the Crown's debt** — Over six million gold pieces owed to the Lannisters (~3 million), Lord Tyrell, the Iron Bank of Braavos, Tyroshi trading cartels, and the Faith.
15. **Ned is stunned by the debt** — He cannot believe Jon Arryn allowed this and vows to speak with Robert against the tournament.
16. **Ned ends the meeting abruptly** — He stands and leaves, realizing too late he was too sharp; remembers he is first among equals, not above them, and softens his tone.
17. **Ned crosses the yard** — He observes the chaos of arriving wagons and riders; the king has not yet arrived; Robert reportedly in the wheelhouse, drunk.
18. **Ned reflects on the journey's misery** — The last fortnight: Sansa blaming Arya, Arya brooding over the butcher's boy, Ned dreaming of "a frozen hell reserved for the Starks of Winterfell."
19. **Littlefinger intercepts Ned** — He tells him he's going the wrong way and offers to lead him.
20. **Littlefinger leads Ned through secret passages** — Through a tower, down stairs, across a sunken courtyard, past a corridor of dusty Targaryen armor.
21. **They exit the castle onto a bluff** — Through a heavy oak-and-iron door with a crossbar; Ned realizes they are outside the castle walls, high above the river.
22. **They descend the cliff face** — Using hidden niches cut in the rock; Littlefinger descends "quick as a monkey," Ned follows more slowly with face pressed to rock.
23. **They ride to the brothel** — Mount two waiting horses, ride along a narrow muddy trail and into the city.
24. **Ned realizes it's a brothel and attacks Littlefinger** — He seizes him, slams him against a wall, and shoves a dagger under his chin.
25. **Ser Rodrik intervenes** — He calls out that Littlefinger speaks the truth; Ned recognizes him despite his disguise of brown roughspun.
26. **Ned enters the brothel and reunites with Catelyn** — They pass through the crowded common room; Ser Rodrik waits below; Littlefinger leads Ned to a third-floor room where Catelyn embraces him.
27. **Catelyn tells Ned everything** — The fire in the library tower, the assassin, the catspaw, Bran's wolf saving him, the dagger, Varys and Littlefinger's involvement in identifying it.
28. **Ned reacts to the dagger** — He handles it, slams it into the table; questions why Tyrion would want Bran dead. Recalls Jon Snow's words about the direwolf pups and feels guilt about killing Lady.
29. **Littlefinger implies conspiracy** — Says the Imp would never have acted alone; warns that accusing the king means "dancing with Ilyn Payne."
30. **Ned reflects on Robert's moral failures** — Remembers Robert's talk of assassinating the Targaryen princess, the crushed skull of Rhaegar's infant son, Robert turning away at Darry, the butcher's boy Mycah cloven in two.
31. **Littlefinger advises dropping the matter** — Suggests throwing the dagger in the river; argues the evidence cuts both ways.
32. **Ned refuses** — Declares himself a Stark of Winterfell whose son lies crippled; will not forget.
33. **Littlefinger and Ned exchange bitter words** — Littlefinger references Brandon moldering in his grave fourteen years; Ned says Littlefinger would be the last man he'd include in any party.
34. **Littlefinger pledges to help for Catelyn's sake** — Says he could never refuse Cat anything; will try to keep Ned alive.
35. **Catelyn reveals she told Littlefinger about Jon Arryn** — He has promised to help find the truth.
36. **They discuss Varys's intelligence** — Catelyn warns Varys has ways beyond simple spying; knew of Ser Rodrik's secret conversation with Ser Aron Santagar. Littlefinger claims leverage over Varys.
37. **Ned instructs Catelyn to return to Winterfell** — Orders her to fortify Moat Cailin (Tallhart and Glover, 100 bowmen each), strengthen White Harbor (Manderly), and watch Theon Greyjoy.
38. **Ned and Catelyn discuss the risk of war** — Ned reassures her the Lannisters wouldn't dare attack the north without the realm's full power.
39. **Ned resolves to find proof about Jon Arryn's death** — Plans to play out the "fool's masquerade" as Hand; when he has the truth, he must go to Robert.
40. **Ned and Catelyn embrace and part** — Littlefinger offers them a bedchamber; Catelyn asks for a moment alone; they kiss.
41. **Catelyn thanks Littlefinger** — Calls him "a brother I'd thought lost"; takes his hands. Littlefinger jokes about his cultivated reputation for wickedness.
42. **Ned accepts Littlefinger's help** — Despite distrust; gives thanks politely. Littlefinger exits with a quip.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Exhausted, irritable, and overwhelmed upon arrival; stunned by the Crown's debt; cold fury during exchanges with Littlefinger; deep rage seeing Sansa's face and remembering the butcher's boy and Lady; guilt about killing Sansa's direwolf and recalling Jon's words about the pups; tenderness and fear upon reuniting with Catelyn; grim determination to investigate Jon Arryn's death
- **Primary preoccupation:** The growing sense that he does not belong in King's Landing or among these men; the dagger and what it means about the Lannisters; whether Robert can be trusted to act justly when confronted with truth
- **Key decisions made:** Ends the council meeting early (asserting authority, then realizing he was too sharp); refuses to abandon the dagger or the investigation despite Littlefinger's counsel; instructs Catelyn to return north and prepare military defenses (Moat Cailin, White Harbor, watch Theon Greyjoy); accepts Littlefinger's help despite deep distrust; resolves to find proof about Jon Arryn and take it to Robert
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells Catelyn "It will not come to that" about war while "praying it was true"; his silent doubt — "pray that he is the man I think he is, and not the man I fear he has become" — suggests he already suspects the worse truth about Robert but cannot confront it; recalls multiple instances of Robert's moral failures (Rhaegar's son, the butcher's boy, Darry, the Targaryen princess) yet still plans to rely on Robert's justice; guilt about killing Lady surfaces through Jon's words about the pups but is pushed aside rather than confronted
