- **chapter_summary:** Ser Alliser Thorne announces eight recruits—including Jon—will be passed to the Lord Commander. The boys celebrate with wine and later a feast of lamb and blueberries. Jon notices Sam Tarly is absent and unhappy, fearing what will happen to Sam once the group moves on. Jon rides out alone at night on the kingsroad, contemplates leaving but turns back. He then visits Maester Aemon to argue that Sam should be accepted as a brother and made a steward rather than forced to keep training as a fighter.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ser Alliser announces eight recruits will pass to the Lord Commander** — Names Toad, Grenn, Pyp, Dareon, Matthar, Jeren, one other, and Jon (called by their nicknames: Toad, Stone Head, Aurochs, Lover, Pimple, Monkey, Ser Loon, and the Bastard). Five new boys are coming up the kingsroad with Gueren.
2. **Recruits celebrate in the yard** — Pyp whoops, Halder smacks Toad with a sword, Pyp leaps on Grenn's shoulders, Dareon fetches wine. Matthar and Jeren throw snowballs from the wall.
3. **Jon offers Sam wine** — Sam declines, says he's happy for them, but is clearly sad. Sam tells Jon he'll be First Ranger like his uncle "was"; Jon corrects him to "is."
4. **Sam slips away** — By the time Jon frees himself from the horseplay, Sam has gone.
5. **Special feast in the common hall** — Three-Finger Hobb cooks rack of lamb with garlic and herbs, turnips, salads, and iced blueberries. Bowen Marsh leads Jon to the bench near the fire.
6. **Boys discuss future assignments** — Pyp wonders if they'll stay together. Toad, Grenn, and Matthar hope for rangers. Halder wants to be a builder. Dareon says Jon is certain to be a ranger.
7. **Dareon almost refers to Benjen as dead** — Jon firmly states Benjen Stark is still First Ranger. Jon pushes away his food and leaves.
8. **Pyp follows Jon outside** — They discuss Sam's situation. Jon realizes Sam will be vulnerable to Rast and Cuger once the group moves on.
9. **Jon rides out from Castle Black at night** — Goes to Hardin's Tower for Ghost, saddles a horse, rides south. Ghost runs ahead to hunt. Horses are skittish around Ghost.
10. **Jon contemplates the kingsroad and the wider world** — Lists places he'll never see. Considers that he hasn't yet sworn his vow and could still leave.
11. **Jon's internal debate about leaving** — Remembers leaving Winterfell, Bran broken, Robb, Arya. An inner voice reminds him of his half-brothers, Lady Stark's rejection, and his unknown mother. He wonders about his mother's identity and why Ned was ashamed.
12. **Jon turns back to Castle Black** — Sees the Wall pale beneath the moon, wheels his horse around.
13. **Ghost returns with a bloody muzzle** — Jon thinks of Sam again on the ride back and decides what to do.
14. **Jon visits Maester Aemon at night** — Chett answers the door and tries to refuse him. Jon insists, wedging his boot in the door. Chett admits him, tells him to start a fire in the library.
15. **Jon argues Sam should be accepted as a brother** — Tells Aemon everything, including setting Ghost on Rast. Argues Sam is hopeless with a sword and will be hurt or killed.
16. **Chett and Aemon respond** — Chett calls Sam a pig and craven, says the Wall is no place for the weak. Aemon asks Jon what he proposes.
17. **Jon makes the chain analogy** — Recalls Maester Luwin's explanation that a maester's chain needs all sorts of metals, as a land needs all sorts of people. Argues the Night's Watch similarly needs all sorts, and Sam should be a steward.
18. **Jon proposes Sam serve Maester Aemon** — Notes Sam can read, write, and do sums; Chett can't read, Clydas has weak eyes. Says Sam would be good with the ravens and animals like him.
19. **Maester Aemon says he will think on it** — Compliments Jon's mind, says Maester Luwin taught him well. Sends Jon away.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Restless, melancholic, torn. Happy about passing but weighed down by guilt over Sam and longing for a life he can never have. Deep restlessness drives him to ride out at night.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Samwell Tarly's safety and future; secondary preoccupation with his own identity (unknown mother, bastard status, belonging nowhere).
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Decides not to leave — turns his horse around on the kingsroad. (2) Decides to go to Maester Aemon to advocate for Sam as a steward.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells himself he could leave freely, but his inner voice immediately reminds him there is no place for him at Winterfell or anywhere else. He also insists Benjen "is" still First Ranger despite all evidence pointing to his loss.
