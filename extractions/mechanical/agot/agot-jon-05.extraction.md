- **chapter_summary:** Ser Alliser Thorne announces that eight recruits, including Jon, are being passed from training to the Lord Commander. The boys celebrate, but Jon notices Sam Tarly is absent and distressed at being left behind. Jon rides out alone at night with Ghost, contemplates the kingsroad and the life he will never have, then returns to Castle Black. He visits Maester Aemon after midnight to argue that Sam should be accepted as a brother and assigned to the stewards rather than forced to continue combat training.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ser Alliser announces eight recruits passing from training** — Names Toad, Grenn, Halder, Dareon, Pyp, Matthar, Jeren, and Jon (using their nicknames and "the Bastard" for Jon). Five new boys are coming up the kingsroad with Gueren.
2. **Recruits celebrate** — Pyp whoops, Halder smacks Toad, Dareon brings wine, Matthar and Jeren throw snowballs. General revelry in the yard.
3. **Jon notices Sam standing alone** — Sam is beneath a bare dead tree, forcing a smile. He says he is happy for them but is visibly upset.
4. **Celebratory feast in common hall** — Three-Finger Hobb cooks rack of lamb with garlic-herb crust, mint, mashed turnips, salads, and iced blueberries with cream. Bowen Marsh says it's from the Lord Commander's table.
5. **Recruits discuss future assignments** — Pyp wonders if they'll stay together; conversation turns to rangers, builders, stewards. Dareon predicts Jon will be a ranger.
6. **Dareon nearly says Benjen is dead** — Jon firmly corrects that Benjen Stark "is still First Ranger."
7. **Jon leaves the feast early** — Barely eats; gives his blueberries to Toad.
8. **Pyp follows Jon outside** — Jon explains Sam wasn't at dinner and fears for him once they leave training.
9. **Jon rides out alone with Ghost** — Follows the creek, then rides to the kingsroad. Ghost hunts (returns with bloody muzzle).
10. **Jon contemplates leaving** — Looks down the kingsroad, thinks of all the places he'll never see. Reminds himself he hasn't sworn yet and could ride away. Thinks of Winterfell, then remembers there's no true place for him there.
11. **Jon turns back to Castle Black** — Sees the Wall in moonlight, wheels his horse around.
12. **Jon visits Maester Aemon after midnight** — Demands entry from Chett, starts a fire in the library, and makes his case for Sam.
13. **Jon argues Sam should be a steward** — Uses the metaphor of a maester's chain (different metals for different skills, a chain needs all sorts) to argue the Watch needs all types of people. Suggests Sam assist Maester Aemon specifically, citing Sam's literacy and way with animals.
14. **Maester Aemon says he will think on it** — Does not commit but compliments Jon's mind. Sends him away.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Restless, melancholy, conflicted. Joy at passing from training is immediately undercut by worry for Sam and unresolved grief about Benjen. Deep longing when looking down the kingsroad.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Sam's safety once Jon and the others leave training; secondary preoccupation with his own identity (bastard, motherless, no true home).
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Decides to ride out alone to think. (2) Decides to return to Castle Black rather than ride south. (3) Decides to visit Maester Aemon and advocate for Sam to become a steward.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon insists Benjen "is" still First Ranger despite mounting evidence of his disappearance; this reads as denial rather than informed belief. He also tells himself he "might leave freely" before his vows, entertaining a fantasy he ultimately rejects.
