## Events & Actions
1. **Ser Alliser announces the eight** — Ser Alliser Thorne names eight recruits (Toad, Grenn, Pyp, Jon, and four others) who will be passed to the Lord Commander, making room for five new boys coming up the kingsroad.
2. **Celebration in the yard** — The named recruits celebrate with cheering, horseplay, and passing a skin of sour red wine. Matthar and Jeren climb a wall to pelt everyone with snowballs.
3. **Jon notices Sam standing alone** — Sam stands beneath a bare dead tree in the corner of the yard. Jon offers him wine; Sam declines and says he is happy for them, but is visibly lying.
4. **Sam insists Benjen is gone; Jon corrects him** — Sam says Jon will be First Ranger "just as your uncle was." Jon corrects: "Is."
5. **Sam departs unnoticed** — By the time Jon frees himself from the horseplay, Sam has left.
6. **Special feast in the common hall** — Three-Finger Hobb cooks a celebratory meal. Bowen Marsh seats Jon near the fire. The recruits feast on lamb, salads, and blueberries with cream.
7. **Recruits discuss their future orders** — Pyp, Toad, Grenn, Matthar, Halder, and Dareon discuss whether they'll be rangers, builders, or stewards. Most hope for rangers; Halder wants builders.
8. **Jon leaves the feast early** — Jon barely eats, gives his blueberries to Toad, and leaves. Pyp follows and Jon explains his worry about Sam being left behind in training with dangerous recruits like Rast and Cuger.
9. **Jon rides out alone at night** — Jon goes to Hardin's Tower for Ghost, saddles his horse, and rides south from Castle Black. Ghost runs ahead to hunt.
10. **Jon contemplates leaving the Watch** — On the kingsroad, Jon feels a vast longing for the world beyond. He reminds himself he hasn't sworn his vow yet and could still ride away to Winterfell.
11. **Jon reflects on having no home** — An inner voice reminds him his brothers are half-brothers, Lady Stark won't welcome him, and even his mother had no place for him. He wonders who his mother was.
12. **Jon turns back** — Jon looks at the Wall in the moonlight and wheels his horse around to return to Castle Black.
13. **Ghost returns from hunting** — Ghost rejoins Jon with a blood-red muzzle as Jon crests a rise near Castle Black.
14. **Jon visits Maester Aemon** — Jon knocks on Maester Aemon's door late at night. Chett tries to turn him away; Jon insists and is admitted.
15. **Jon argues Sam should be a steward** — Jon tells Maester Aemon and Chett Sam's full story, including setting Ghost on Rast. He draws an analogy to a maester's chain — the Watch needs all sorts of people, not just fighters. Sam can read, write, do sums, and tend ravens.
16. **Chett opposes Sam's placement** — Chett argues the Wall is no place for the weak and that soft lordlings can't handle steward's work.
17. **Maester Aemon says he will think on it** — Aemon compliments Jon's reasoning ("Your mind is as deft as your blade") and says he will consider the proposal, then dismisses Jon.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Restless and conflicted; happy about advancing but worried about Sam; deeply lonely and uncertain about his identity and belonging; ultimately resolute
- **Primary preoccupation:** Sam's safety once Jon and the others leave training; secondarily, his own identity crisis (no true home, unknown mother)
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Decides to ride out alone at night to think; (2) Decides to turn back from the kingsroad rather than flee south; (3) Decides to visit Maester Aemon to advocate for Sam becoming a steward
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells himself he could leave freely, but his own reasoning reveals he has nowhere to go; he rationalizes his mother's absence as her being dishonorable rather than entertaining other possibilities
