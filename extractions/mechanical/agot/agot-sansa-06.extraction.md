- **chapter_summary:** Sansa spends days in grief-stricken isolation in Maegor's Holdfast after her father's execution, refusing food and contemplating suicide. Joffrey forces her to attend court, where Ser Meryn Trant strikes her for resisting. She watches Joffrey dispense cruel justice in the throne room. Afterward, Joffrey forces her to view the tarred heads mounted on the battlements, including her father's and Septa Mordane's. When Sansa talks back, Ser Meryn beats her again; she briefly contemplates pushing Joffrey off the battlements but the moment passes when Sandor Clegane intervenes to wipe the blood from her lip.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa isolates herself in grief** — After her father's execution, Sansa retreats to her bed in Maegor's Holdfast for days, refusing food, sleeping and weeping, reliving the execution in dreams and waking memories.
2. **Sansa contemplates suicide** — She considers flinging herself from the window but loses her nerve and retreats to bed.
3. **Grand Maester Pycelle examines Sansa** — He visits her chamber, feels her brow, makes her undress, touches her while her bedmaid holds her down, and gives her a sleeping potion of honeywater and herbs.
4. **Sansa has a nightmare about Ser Ilyn** — She dreams of footsteps climbing to her room, Ser Ilyn coming with Ice to take her head; she wakes murmuring pleas.
5. **Joffrey forces Sansa to rise** — Joffrey enters with the Hound and two Kingsguard knights, commands her to bathe and dress for court; when she resists, he threatens to have the Hound dress her.
6. **Sansa confronts Joffrey about her father** — She says she doesn't want to marry him because he chopped off her father's head; Joffrey claims mercy because he gave Eddard a "clean death."
7. **Sansa says "I hate you"** — She tells Joffrey she hates him after seeing him clearly for the first time.
8. **Ser Meryn strikes Sansa** — On Joffrey's command, Ser Meryn backhands Sansa across the ear with a gloved fist, knocking her down; her ear bleeds.
9. **The Hound advises Sansa** — After Joffrey leaves, Sandor Clegane tells Sansa to give the king what he wants: smiles, pretty words, love and fear.
10. **Sansa bathes and dresses** — She bathes (her first wash since her father's death), chooses the green silk gown from the tourney, and uses powder to hide her bruise.
11. **Ser Meryn escorts Sansa to court** — He returns in full Kingsguard armor at midday; Sansa tells him he is "no true knight."
12. **Joffrey holds court** — From a balcony, Sansa watches Joffrey on the Iron Throne: he has a thief's hand chopped off by Ser Ilyn, orders two disputing knights to duel to the death, sends a grieving woman to the dungeons for loving a traitor.
13. **The tavern singer is sentenced** — A plump singer who made a song mocking Robert (about fighting with a pig/boar, with verses that sound like they could be about the queen) is forced to perform it, then told he can keep either his fingers or his tongue.
14. **Joffrey walks with Sansa** — After court, Joffrey commands Sansa to walk with him; he mentions his upcoming name day feast and asks what gift she'll give him; he calls her stupid, quoting his mother.
15. **Joffrey threatens Sansa about children** — He tells her he'll get her with child and chop off her head if the child is stupid; asks when she can have children.
16. **Joffrey forces Sansa to view the heads** — He leads her up the gatehouse stairs to the battlements where tarred heads are mounted on iron spikes, including Eddard Stark's and Septa Mordane's.
17. **Sansa defies Joffrey** — When Joffrey threatens to give her Robb's head, she retorts that maybe Robb will give her Joffrey's head.
18. **Ser Meryn beats Sansa again** — On Joffrey's command, Ser Meryn holds her jaw and strikes her twice, splitting her lip.
19. **Sansa contemplates pushing Joffrey** — She considers shoving Joffrey off the unprotected inner edge of the wallwalk, thinking it wouldn't matter if she went over with him.
20. **The Hound intervenes** — Sandor Clegane kneels before Sansa, positioning himself between her and Joffrey, and delicately dabs blood from her split lip; the moment to push Joffrey passes.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Profound grief, suicidal despair, terror, hatred, brief flashes of defiant rage, resignation. Moves from numb isolation to forced engagement with her tormentor. By chapter's end, reduced to performing courtesy while suppressing murderous impulse.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Surviving Joffrey's cruelty; reliving her father's execution; the tension between obedience and dignity.
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses the green silk gown from the tourney hoping Joffrey will remember being gallant; decides to comply with Joffrey's demands after the Hound's advice; briefly decides she could push Joffrey from the battlements (but the moment passes).
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself she cannot see the heads even when forced to look ("he can make me look at the heads, but he can't make me see them"); initially tries to find safety in Joffrey's smile (recalled from execution, when "she'd felt safe, but only for a heartbeat"); thinks wearing the tourney gown might make Joffrey treat her gently.
