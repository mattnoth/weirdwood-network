# AGOT — Sansa VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 68
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark

- **first_available:** AGOT Sansa VI
- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Maegor's Holdfast (tower room), Throne Room, Gatehouse battlements/wallwalk
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after Eddard Stark's execution; Sansa has been confined in her room refusing food and washing since his death
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa spends days in grief-stricken isolation in Maegor's Holdfast after her father's execution, contemplating suicide. Joffrey forces her to attend court, where Ser Meryn Trant strikes her when she resists. She watches Joffrey dispense cruel justice in the throne room. Afterward, Joffrey takes her to the battlements and forces her to look at her father's tarred head on a spike alongside Septa Mordane's and others. When Sansa talks back, Ser Meryn beats her again. Sansa briefly contemplates pushing Joffrey off the battlements, but Sandor Clegane intervenes by kneeling to tend her wound, breaking the moment.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa's grief isolation** — Sansa spends days in her tower room in Maegor's Holdfast, refusing food, sleeping, weeping, and not speaking to servants.
2. **Suicide contemplation (window)** — Sansa considers flinging herself from the window to end her suffering but loses her nerve and retreats to bed.
3. **Pycelle's examination** — Grand Maester Pycelle visits, examines Sansa (makes her undress, touches her while bedmaid holds her down), and gives her a sleeping potion.
4. **Joffrey's forced summons** — Joffrey arrives with the Hound and two Kingsguard, commands Sansa to attend court. When she resists, he threatens to have Clegane dress her.
5. **Sansa declares hatred** — Sansa tells Joffrey "I hate you" after he describes his father's execution as merciful.
6. **First beating by Ser Meryn** — On Joffrey's command, Ser Meryn backhands Sansa across the ear with a gloved fist, drawing blood.
7. **Sandor Clegane's advice** — The Hound tells Sansa to give Joffrey what he wants: smiles, pretty words, love, and fear.
8. **Sansa bathes and dresses** — She bathes for the first time since her father's death, chooses her green silk gown from the tourney, and covers her bruise with powder.
9. **Court session** — Joffrey dispenses justice: a thief loses his hand, two knights are ordered to duel to the death over land, a woman pleading for a traitor's body is dragged to the dungeons, and a singer is forced to perform a satirical song about Robert and given a choice between losing his fingers or his tongue.
10. **Sansa recalls Littlefinger's words** — She remembers Petyr Baelish telling her "Life is not a song, sweetling" in the throne room.
11. **Walk with Joffrey** — Joffrey commands Sansa to walk with him; he discusses his name day feast, threatens to kill Robb and give Sansa his head.
12. **Sansa's retort** — Sansa says "Maybe my brother will give me your head."
13. **Second beating by Ser Meryn** — Ser Meryn strikes Sansa twice across the face, splitting her lip.
14. **Viewing the heads** — Joffrey forces Sansa to look at Eddard Stark's tarred head and Septa Mordane's decayed head on the battlements, plus empty spikes reserved for Stannis and Renly.
15. **Suicide contemplation (pushing Joffrey)** — Sansa considers shoving Joffrey off the battlements, thinking it wouldn't matter if she went over with him.
16. **The Hound intervenes** — Sandor Clegane kneels between Sansa and Joffrey, gently dabbing the blood from her split lip, breaking the moment of her murderous impulse.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief-stricken, despairing, traumatized; oscillates between numbness, terror, defiance, and brief murderous rage
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival under Joffrey's cruelty; grief over father's death; longing for Winterfell and home
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to comply with Joffrey's demands after the Hound's advice; decides to perform courtesies as a survival strategy; twice considers suicide/violence but does not act
- **Self-deception flags:** Chooses to "not see" the heads on the wall as a coping mechanism ("he can make me look at the heads but he can't make me see them"); the tarred head "did not really look like Lord Eddard"
