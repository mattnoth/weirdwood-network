- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn waits in the Whispering Wood as Robb's army prepares to ambush Jaime Lannister's cavalry column. She reflects on her life of waiting — for her father Hoster Tully, for Brandon Stark, for Ned. The trap is sprung when Jaime's riders enter the valley, and the Battle of the Whispering Wood is fought through the night. By dawn, Robb's forces have won decisively, capturing Jaime Lannister along with many knights and lords. Three of Robb's battle guard — Torrhen and Eddard Karstark and Daryn Hornwood — are killed by Jaime during his attempt to cut his way to Robb. Catelyn reminds Robb and Theon that the war is not won while Tywin Lannister remains free and Riverrun remains besieged.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn waits in the Whispering Wood** — She sits on horseback on a wooded ridge above a stream valley with Hallis Mollen and her thirty-man guard, waiting for the battle to begin.
2. **Catelyn reflects on waiting** — She remembers waiting for her father Lord Hoster at Riverrun, for Brandon Stark who never returned, and for Ned who left her pregnant with Robb after a fortnight.
3. **Robb prepares his men** — Robb moves among the soldiers, touching shoulders, jesting, helping with horses. Olyvar Frey helps him mount and arm.
4. **Robb rides down the line** — He tells Catelyn his father said you should let men see you before a battle, then rides away with Grey Wind and his battle guard.
5. **Snow shrikes signal the approach** — Bird calls (snow shrikes, northern birds) signal that forces are moving; Catelyn and Hal Mollen recognize them as the signal.
6. **Jaime Lannister's column enters the valley** — Catelyn briefly sees Jaime framed between trees, moonlight silvering his armor and gold hair, crimson cloak turned black. Long columns of knights, sworn swords, and freeriders follow — three quarters of the Lannister horse.
7. **Maege Mormont sounds her warhorn** — A long low blast from the east signals that the last of Jaime's riders have entered the trap.
8. **Grey Wind howls** — The direwolf's howl shakes Catelyn; answering horns blow from all sides — Greatjon from the far ridge, Mallisters and Freys east and west, Karstarks from the north where the valley narrows.
9. **The ambush is sprung** — Bowmen hidden in trees loose arrows; riders raise concealed lances; Robb shouts "Winterfell!" and leads his men downhill.
10. **The Battle of the Whispering Wood** — Catelyn listens from the ridge as battle rages below through the night. She hears Robb calling "To me!", Grey Wind fighting, lances breaking, swords clashing, men dying.
11. **Dawn breaks; Grey Wind howls again** — The sounds of battle dwindle until only Grey Wind's howling remains as red dawn breaks.
12. **Robb returns unhurt** — He rides a different horse (piebald gelding replacing his grey stallion); his shield is battered; his glove and sleeve are black with blood, but it is not his own — possibly Torrhen Karstark's.
13. **Jaime Lannister is captured** — Theon and the Greatjon drag Jaime before Catelyn. Jaime kneels, bleeding from a scalp gash, and sardonically offers his sword (which he has "mislaid").
14. **Catelyn demands her family** — She tells Jaime she wants her father, brother Edmure, daughters, and husband — not his sword. Jaime says he has "mislaid them as well."
15. **Theon urges execution; Robb refuses** — Theon says to take Jaime's head. Robb says Jaime is more useful alive and that his father never condoned killing prisoners.
16. **Jaime is led away in irons** — Catelyn orders him put in irons; Robb orders a strong guard, noting Lord Karstark will want his head.
17. **Robb learns of his battle guard's deaths** — Galbart Glover explains that Jaime fought his way up the valley trying to reach Robb; he killed Torrhen Karstark (took his hand off), Eddard Karstark (lodged his sword in his neck), and Daryn Hornwood (split his skull).
18. **Catelyn refocuses Robb on the war** — She tells him to grieve later, that they've won a battle but not the war — Riverrun is still besieged.
19. **Theon reports the scale of victory** — He compares it to the Field of Fire; claims ten Lannisters fell for every Northman; lists captured lords and knights including Lord Westerling, Lord Banefort, Ser Garth Greenfield, Lord Estren, Ser Tytos Brax, Mallor the Dornishman, and three Lannisters (Tywin's nephews).
20. **Catelyn asks about Tywin** — She asks if Tywin has been taken; Theon admits he has not. Catelyn declares the war far from done. Robb agrees and says they still have Riverrun to relieve.
