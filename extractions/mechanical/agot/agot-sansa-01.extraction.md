# AGOT — Sansa I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 16
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Sansa I
- **first_available:** AGOT Sansa I
- **location_primary:** The Trident (river and north bank), traveling camp / inn along the kingsroad
- **location_secondary:** The Neck (recalled), King's Landing (mentioned as origin of riders), Storm's End (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** The royal progress is traveling south toward King's Landing; the party has recently crossed the Neck and is now at or near the ruby ford on the Trident where Robert killed Rhaegar
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa and Arya argue at the Trident over Arya's refusal to ride in the queen's wheelhouse. A royal honor guard arrives from King's Landing, including Ser Barristan Selmy, Renly Baratheon, and Ser Ilyn Payne; Sansa has a frightening encounter with the silent Payne and an awkward one with Sandor Clegane. Joffrey intervenes gallantly and escorts Sansa on a ride along the river, leaving Lady and the Hound behind. They discover Arya practicing swordfighting with Mycah, the butcher's boy. Joffrey menaces Mycah with his sword, drawing blood; Arya attacks Joffrey with a stick, Nymeria mauls his sword arm, and Arya throws Joffrey's sword Lion's Tooth into the river. When Sansa goes to comfort the wounded Joffrey, he regards her with contempt and dismisses her.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa and Septa Mordane breakfast at the inn** — Sansa feeds Lady bacon under the table; Mordane disapproves. The party has been traveling south; the king's party now numbers more than four hundred including Stark household and freeriders.
2. **Sansa finds Arya at the Trident** — Arya is brushing Nymeria on the riverbank with Mycah; wearing the same riding leathers as the previous two days. Arya refuses the invitation to ride in the queen's wheelhouse.
3. **Arya and Sansa argue** — Arya plans to ride upstream with Mycah to search for Rhaegar's rubies at the ford. Sansa fails to convince her. Sansa leaves alone.
4. **Sansa's internal reflection on Arya and Jon** — Sansa wishes Arya were more like Myrcella; notes Arya resembles Jon Snow in appearance; recalls once asking her mother if Arya might be a changeling.
5. **Honor guard arrives from King's Landing** — Two knights (Ser Barristan Selmy and Renly Baratheon) kneel before Cersei at the wheelhouse; Ser Ilyn Payne stands apart by the horses.
6. **Sansa is terrified by Ser Ilyn Payne** — Lady growls at Payne; Sansa is overcome by terror and steps backward into Sandor Clegane.
7. **Sandor Clegane grabs Sansa's shoulders** — Comments on her shaking; she wrenches away; Lady moves between them; bystanders laugh and mock.
8. **Joffrey intervenes on Sansa's behalf** — Commands swords to be sheathed, dismisses the Hound, helps Sansa to her feet; Sansa is grateful and admiring.
9. **Sansa identifies Ser Barristan and Lord Renly** — Demonstrates courtly knowledge; compliments Ser Barristan as "Barristan the Bold"; deduces Renly by his antlered helm and youth.
10. **Ser Ilyn Payne's muteness revealed** — Joffrey explains that Aerys Targaryen had Payne's tongue ripped out with hot pincers fourteen years prior; Payne stares at Sansa without speaking.
11. **Cersei postpones the wheelhouse trip and assigns Joffrey to escort Sansa** — The council needs to meet until Robert returns; Myrcella's day with Sansa is postponed.
12. **Joffrey and Sansa ride along the Trident** — Lady and the Hound left behind; they explore caves, track a shadowcat, dine at a holdfast on trout and wine (Sansa drinks more than usual). Joffrey sings for her; they approach the ruby ford.
13. **Joffrey and Sansa discover Arya and Mycah practicing swordfighting** — Mycah had been pressing Arya; Joffrey finds this amusing, draws Lion's Tooth.
14. **Joffrey menaces Mycah** — Commands Mycah to pick up his stick; Mycah refuses; Joffrey presses Lion's Tooth to Mycah's cheek, drawing blood. Mycah explains Arya asked him to practice with her.
15. **Arya attacks Joffrey with a stick** — Cracks him across the back of the head; Joffrey staggers and whirls; Mycah flees into the trees. Joffrey disarms Arya's second swing.
16. **Nymeria mauls Joffrey's sword arm** — The direwolf leaps and closes her jaws on Joffrey's sword arm; Joffrey drops Lion's Tooth, is knocked to the ground; Arya calls Nymeria off.
17. **Arya holds Joffrey at sword-point with Lion's Tooth** — Joffrey begs her not to hurt him and says he'll tell his mother; Arya throws the sword into the Trident.
18. **Sansa goes to comfort Joffrey** — Kneels beside him, strokes his hair; Joffrey looks at her with contempt and orders her away.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Eager and romantic at the chapter's start (looking forward to the day with the queen and hoping to see Joffrey); frustrated and embarrassed by Arya; briefly terrified by Ser Ilyn Payne; thrilled and worshipful during her time with Joffrey; helpless and horrified during the confrontation at the river; tearful and tender at the end when she tries to comfort Joffrey; stunned and humiliated by his contemptuous dismissal.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Joffrey — being near him, pleasing him, impressing him; being a proper highborn lady; how Arya embarrasses her and ruins things.
- **Key decisions made:** Goes to the riverbank to convince Arya; stays with Joffrey alone without Lady or the Hound; leaves Lady tied up to go riding; tries to restrain Arya during the confrontation; goes to Joffrey after the fight to offer comfort.
- **Self-deception flags:** Sansa tells herself Joffrey's intervention on her behalf was gallant and knight-like, comparing him to legendary heroes; she suppresses awareness of his cruelty to Mycah and his rage during the confrontation; she attempts to comfort him despite having witnessed his unprovoked violence; her final act of tenderness is met with contempt, which the narrative presents as a revelation of his true regard for her.
