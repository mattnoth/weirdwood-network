Survivors from Sherrer, Wendish Town, and the Mummer's Ford testify about raids by a large mounted force — identified by the witnesses and riverlord knights as Gregor Clegane's men riding without banners. After debate among the councillors and petitioners, Ned sends Lord Beric Dondarrion with a force to bring the king's justice to Gregor Clegane, stripping him of all titles, lands, and rank and sentencing him to death. He denies Ser Loras Tyrell's request to lead the mission, choosing Beric instead.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned sits the Iron Throne** — With Robert away hunting, Ned holds court from the Iron Throne despite his shattered leg causing him great pain.
2. **Villagers from Sherrer brought before the throne** — Tattered, bloody survivors kneel before Ned; three riverlord knights (Ser Raymun Darry, Ser Karyl Vance, Ser Marq Piper) escort them.
3. **Testimony about the raids** — Joss the alehouse keeper, a farmer, a smith, and a young girl describe the destruction of Sherrer: burning, killing, livestock slaughter, and the murder of the smith's apprentice boy. Ser Raymun describes Wendish Town's people being burned alive in their timbered holdfast and shot with arrows as they fled.
4. **Identification of Gregor Clegane** — Joss describes the enormous leader; Ser Marq names him as the Mountain. The raiders rode warhorses, wore mail, carried lances and longswords, bore no banners.
5. **Council debate** — Pycelle defends the Lannisters, suggests petitioners seek Lord Tywin's justice. Littlefinger questions the riverlords about where they were during the raids. Ser Karyl explains they were attending lords in the pass below the Golden Tooth; Ser Raymun was camped near Riverrun.
6. **Ned observes Sansa in the gallery** — He feels anger that his daughter is witnessing this; notes the septa could not have anticipated such proceedings.
7. **Ned observes people slipping out** — He spots men leaving through the far door, suspects they are going to inform the queen.
8. **Ned sends Ser Robar Royce** — To carry word of the proceedings to Robert and the hunting party.
9. **Ned pronounces judgment** — He rises from the throne despite his broken leg, invokes First Men tradition about judges wielding the sword, but acknowledges he cannot ride. He assembles a force of men under Lord Beric Dondarrion.
10. **Ser Loras volunteers** — The Knight of Flowers asks to lead the mission; Ned denies him, distinguishing justice from vengeance.
11. **Ned formally sentences Gregor Clegane** — In Robert's name, he denounces, attaints, and strips Gregor of all rank, titles, lands, incomes, and holdings, and sentences him to death.
12. **Ned descends the throne** — Alyn and Porther help him down the steep iron steps. Loras stares sullenly but stalks away.
13. **Varys counsels Ned privately** — Warns that sending Beric instead of Loras was politically unwise (missed chance to ally with the Tyrells) and that Ser Ilyn Payne appeared displeased at being bypassed as King's Justice.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anger at the raids and at the situation he's been put in; physical pain from his shattered leg making him irritable; a sense of grim duty; suppressed fury at the injustice done to the smallfolk; annoyance at being on the throne ("What Eddard Stark was doing sitting there he would never comprehend"); flash of anger seeing Sansa in the gallery
- **Primary preoccupation:** Delivering justice while avoiding Tywin Lannister's trap — he recognizes the raids are designed to provoke Riverrun into striking first so the Lannisters can claim the Tullys broke the king's peace
- **Key decisions made:** Sends Beric Dondarrion rather than Loras Tyrell; bypasses Ser Ilyn Payne (Lannister bannerman); sentences Gregor to death and strips him of all honors; sends Ser Robar to inform Robert
- **Self-deception flags:** None overt, though Ned may be underestimating the political risks Varys warns him about — he dismisses Loras's disappointment and doesn't fully engage with Varys's warnings about Ser Ilyn and the Tyrells
