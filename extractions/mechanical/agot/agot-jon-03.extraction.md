- **chapter_summary:** Jon trains in the Castle Black courtyard, decisively defeating Grenn in sparring under Ser Alliser Thorne's contemptuous supervision. After training, Grenn and three other recruits confront Jon in the armory, leading to a fight broken up by Donal Noye. The armorer rebukes Jon for bullying less-trained recruits and forces him to recognize his privilege. Jon walks outside, encounters Tyrion Lannister, and they share a meal in the common hall. Ser Alliser summons Jon to Lord Commander Mormont, who gives him a letter from Robb revealing Bran has woken from his coma. Jon celebrates and offers to help train Grenn, then publicly jokes at Ser Alliser's expense, earning the master-at-arms's enmity.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon defeats Grenn in training** — Jon presses the attack against Grenn in the courtyard, landing blows to his leg, helm, chest, and wrist. Ser Alliser calls a halt and mocks Grenn.
2. **Ser Alliser mocks Jon** — Calls him "Lord Snow" and tells him he is weak despite winning. Jon reflects that Thorne hates him.
3. **Jon reflects on his isolation** — Walking alone to the armory, Jon notes he has no friends among the nearly twenty recruits and privately assesses each one's fighting weaknesses.
4. **Jon changes in the armory** — Strips off training gear, dons roughspun blacks, feels cold and weary. Remembers Winterfell's warm walls.
5. **Jon recalls his disillusionment** — Reflects that no one told him the truth about the Watch except Tyrion Lannister. Wonders if his father knew.
6. **Jon recalls Benjen's refusal** — Three days after arrival, Jon pleaded with Benjen to join his ranging. Benjen refused, telling Jon he is still a boy and must earn his place. The next dawn, Jon watched Benjen ride out. One of the rangers sang a bawdy song while saddling his garron.
7. **Jon recalls his dark vision** — Watching Benjen leave, Jon imagined him dead in the snow with red blood, recalling Tyrion's words from the kingsroad.
8. **Jon reflects on missing his siblings** — Thinks of Rickon, Robb, Bran, Sansa, and Arya with specific memories and affection. Misses Arya most.
9. **Confrontation in the armory** — Grenn, Toad, and two rapers from the Fingers confront Jon. Toad insults Jon's mother. Jon attacks Toad, slams his head against the ground. The rapers pull Jon off; Grenn kicks him.
10. **Donal Noye intervenes** — The armorer breaks up the fight with a booming voice. Sends Grenn and Toad to Maester Aemon. Keeps Jon behind.
11. **Noye's lecture** — Noye rebukes Jon for acting like a bully, pointing out that the other recruits never had proper training, real swords, or masters-at-arms. Forces Jon to recognize his advantages as a lord's son. Warns him someone will slit his throat if he continues.
12. **Jon gazes at the Wall** — Leaving the armory near midday, Jon is overwhelmed by the Wall blazing in the sunlight. Extended description of its scale and appearance.
13. **Encounter with Tyrion** — Tyrion appears and philosophizes about walls and curiosity. Jon mentions wanting to range beyond the Wall. Tyrion advises Jon to own the name "Lord Snow" so it can't hurt him.
14. **Walk to the common hall** — Jon and Tyrion walk together in rising wind, past creaking buildings and falling snow.
15. **Jon mentions Ghost** — Tells Tyrion he chains Ghost in the old stables during training and sleeps in Hardin's Tower. The other boys are scared of Ghost.
16. **Discussion of Benjen** — Tyrion notes Benjen is overdue. Jon reveals Benjen said he'd return by Jon's name day, which passed a fortnight ago. The ranging was to search for Ser Waymar Royce, possibly as far as the Shadow Tower.
17. **Midday meal** — Jon and Tyrion eat stew in the common hall. Jon sits far from the other recruits.
18. **Ser Alliser summons Jon** — Thorne tells Jon the Lord Commander wants to see him. Mentions a bird from Winterfell concerning Jon's "half brother." Tyrion intervenes when Thorne frightens Jon.
19. **Jon receives the letter** — Jon races to the Commander's Keep, bursts in on Mormont. The Lord Commander has a raven on his arm, feeding it corn. He gives Jon a letter sealed with a direwolf in white wax, written in Robb's hand.
20. **Bran has woken** — Jon reads through tears that Bran has woken from his coma. Mormont notes Bran will be crippled. Jon is overwhelmed with joy.
21. **Jon celebrates** — Runs back to the common hall, lifts Tyrion in the air and spins him. Shows everyone the letter.
22. **Jon offers to help Grenn** — Approaches Grenn, apologizes for the wrist injury, offers to teach him the defensive move. A marked change in attitude following Noye's counsel.
23. **Jon jokes at Ser Alliser's expense** — When Thorne sneers about teaching an aurochs, Jon quips about Ghost juggling. Laughter spreads through the hall.
24. **Ser Alliser's enmity** — Thorne's face darkens, his sword hand curls into a fist. He warns Jon: "That was a grievous error, Lord Snow."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Begins proud and isolated; moves through loneliness and homesickness (missing siblings); cold anger when his mother is insulted; shame and guilt after Noye's lecture; awe before the Wall; wild fear then overwhelming joy upon receiving news of Bran; ends buoyant and socially transformed
- **Primary preoccupation:** His isolation at Castle Black — no friends, contempt from Thorne, separation from family; anxiety about Benjen's overdue ranging; deep longing for siblings, especially Arya
- **Key decisions made:** After Noye's rebuke, Jon shifts from contempt toward recruits to offering help; he approaches Grenn to apologize and teach him; he stands up to Ser Alliser with humor rather than sullenness
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon initially believes the recruits hate him because he's better; Noye forces him to see he's been acting as a bully. Jon tells himself he wants to be alone and will "make solitude his armor," but his thoughts reveal deep loneliness and longing for connection. He insists "Lord Eddard Stark was not a man to sleep with whores" regarding his mother, while knowing nothing about her.
