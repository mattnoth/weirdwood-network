- **book:** AGOT

- **location_primary:** Castle Black
- **location_secondary:** Hardin's Tower (Jon's sleeping cell), the common hall, the armory, the Commander's Keep; the Wall (described); Eastwatch-by-the-Sea (mentioned); the Shadow Tower (mentioned); Winterfell (recalled); Storm's End (recalled); the Trident (recalled)
- **approximate_timeline:** Several weeks after Jon's arrival at Castle Black; Benjen Stark has been gone long enough that he has missed Jon's name day by a fortnight; Jon is not yet fifteen
- **chapter_summary:** Jon defeats Grenn in training but earns only scorn from Ser Alliser Thorne. Grenn and three others then confront Jon in the armory; a brawl breaks out and is broken up by the armorer Donal Noye, who privately lectures Jon that his attitude is alienating his fellow recruits. Jon encounters Tyrion Lannister outside and they discuss the Wall and the disappearance of Benjen Stark. In the common hall, Ser Alliser delivers a summons to the Lord Commander. Jon learns from Jeor Mormont — via a letter from Robb — that Bran has woken from his coma, though he is crippled. Jon is overwhelmed with joy and, in his elation, publicly mocks Ser Alliser with a quip about Ghost, earning the knight's declared enmity.

## Events & Actions
1. **Training yard bout** — Jon defeats Grenn in a sparring match; Ser Alliser Thorne mocks both of them; Thorne dismisses the entire group for ineptitude.
2. **Armory confrontation** — Grenn, Toad, and two unnamed recruits from the Fingers corner Jon in the armory over the broken wrist; Toad mocks Jon's mother; a brawl breaks out and Jon throttles Toad before the others pull him off and Grenn kicks him.
3. **Donal Noye intervenes** — Noye breaks up the fight, sends the others away, and keeps Jon back for a private lecture: Jon is technically superior but shames recruits who never had proper training, making enemies he does not need.
4. **Jon contemplates the Wall** — After leaving the armory, Jon looks up at the Wall and reflects on its scale, age, and the sense that the world depends on it.
5. **Tyrion encounters Jon** — Tyrion finds Jon staring at the Wall; they walk to the common hall together; Tyrion advises Jon to own the name "Lord Snow" rather than resent it; they discuss Benjen's overdue return.
6. **Alliser summons Jon** — In the common hall, Ser Alliser arrives to bring Jon to the Lord Commander; he will not say why; Tyrion presses him and Thorne reveals a bird arrived from Winterfell with news about Bran.
7. **Jon receives Robb's letter** — Jeor Mormont gives Jon the letter; Jon reads it and learns Bran has woken from his coma; he is told Bran is crippled.
8. **Jon's joyful reaction** — Jon runs back to the common hall, lifts Tyrion off the ground in celebration, and approaches Grenn to apologize and offer to teach him the move that hurt his wrist.
9. **Jon mocks Alliser publicly** — When Thorne sneers at Jon's offer to help Grenn, Jon quips he'd like to see Ghost juggle; the common hall erupts in laughter; Thorne declares Jon has made a "grievous error" and names himself Jon's enemy.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Starts the chapter feeling cold, weary, isolated, and resentful; oscillates between pride at his skill and shame at what Noye reveals about his attitude; experiences fear when summoned (thinking Benjen has died); then sudden euphoric joy at Bran's survival that breaks through all his defenses
- **Primary preoccupation:** Loneliness and alienation at Castle Black; missing Winterfell and his siblings; anxiety over Benjen's overdue return
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to approach Grenn with an apology and offer to train him (immediate shift after learning Bran lives); publicly quips at Thorne in the common hall
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon believes his contempt for his fellow recruits is entirely justified by their inferior skill; Noye's rebuttal forces him to acknowledge he has not considered their backgrounds; Jon also admits he previously suppressed his desire to range rather than examine it
