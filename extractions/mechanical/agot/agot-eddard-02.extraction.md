Robert reveals he has named Jaime Lannister as Warden of the East instead of young Robert Arryn. Ned recounts arriving at King's Landing after the sack to find Jaime seated on the Iron Throne, arguing Jaime cannot be trusted, but Robert dismisses his concerns. ## Events & Actions
1. **Ned is woken before dawn** — Alyn shakes Ned roughly from his dreams in the predawn hour.
2. **Robert summons Ned to ride** — Robert is already mounted on his black destrier, wearing furs, with Ser Boros, Ser Meryn, and a dozen guardsmen waiting.
3. **Ned offers to talk inside his tent** — Robert refuses, saying the camp is full of ears and he wants to ride.
4. **Hard gallop south** — Robert sets a punishing pace; they leave the kingsroad and ride across rolling, misty plains; the guard falls back out of earshot.
5. **They halt at dawn on a ridge** — They have ridden miles south of the main party; Robert is flushed and exhilarated.
6. **Robert complains about the wheelhouse** — He rails against the slow pace and threatens to burn the wheelhouse and make Cersei walk.
7. **Robert fantasizes about vagabond freedom** — He proposes he and Ned just keep riding as two knights on the road; Ned reminds him of their duties.
8. **Robert asks about Ned's bastard's mother** — He tries to remember the name, guessing Becca, Aleena, Merryl; Ned identifies her as Wylla and refuses to discuss further.
9. **Ned points out the barrows of the First Men** — They observe the ancient burial mounds on the plains.
10. **Robert delivers Varys's intelligence** — He gives Ned a paper reporting Daenerys Targaryen's marriage to Khal Drogo, sourced from Ser Jorah Mormont who spies for Varys from Pentos.
11. **Robert proposes assassinating Daenerys** — He wants to send an assassin; Ned argues against murdering a child, comparing it to Tywin's slaughter of Rhaegar's children.
12. **Robert rages about the Targaryens** — He invokes the deaths of Brandon, Rickard, and Lyanna Stark at Targaryen hands; swears to kill every Targaryen.
13. **Ned calms Robert** — He points out Robert cannot reach Daenerys and that Dothraki are no threat without ships.
14. **Robert expresses fear of Targaryen loyalists** — He worries that houses who fought for Targaryens will join Viserys if he crosses with a Dothraki horde.
15. **Debate over Warden of the East** — Robert refuses to name sickly Robert Arryn; Ned suggests Stannis; Robert reveals he has already named Jaime Lannister.
16. **Ned argues against Jaime as Warden** — He points out Jaime will also inherit Warden of the West from Tywin, putting too much military power in Lannister hands; Robert dismisses the concern.
17. **Ned questions Jaime's trustworthiness** — He recounts arriving at King's Landing after the sack: Lannister banners over the walls, Aerys dead on the floor, Jaime seated on the Iron Throne in golden armor with his bloodied sword, wearing a lion-head helm.
18. **Robert laughs off the throne room story** — He finds Jaime sitting on the throne amusing and dismisses Ned's concerns, noting Jaime was only seventeen.
19. **Robert rides off** — He declares he's sick of secrets and squabbles and gallops away over a barrow.
20. **Ned feels helpless** — He reflects that he is no Jon Arryn, cannot curb Robert's wildness, and belongs in Winterfell with Catelyn and Bran. He resignedly follows the king.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Groggy at first, then wryly amused during Robert's fantasy of freedom, then angry when pressed about Wylla, then deeply troubled during the Targaryen assassination debate, then bitter when recalling the sack of King's Landing, finally filled with a "vast sense of helplessness" and resignation
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether he can influence Robert at all; the futility of trying to be Robert's moral compass; guilt over past lies and dishonor
- **Key decisions made:** Resolves to keep his temper when Robert proposes killing Daenerys; decides to tell Robert the full truth about the throne room scene with Jaime; ultimately resigns himself to following Robert despite feeling helpless
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned claims to Robert that "Wylla" is his bastard's mother and says he "dishonored" Catelyn — then the narration states "He had lived his lies for fourteen years, yet they still haunted him at night." This strongly implies the Wylla story is itself a lie or partial truth. The "Promise me, Ned" memory from Lyanna surfaces in close proximity to the discussion of his "bastard," suggesting a connection Ned carefully conceals.
