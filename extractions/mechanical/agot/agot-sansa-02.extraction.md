# AGOT — Sansa II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 30
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Sansa II
- **first_available:** AGOT Sansa II
- **location_primary:** Tourney grounds outside King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Riverside feast pavilion; Red Keep; King's Gate; corridor outside Sansa's bedchamber
- **approximate_timeline:** During the Hand's tourney, first day of jousting and the feast that follows
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa attends the Hand's tourney with Septa Mordane and Jeyne Poole. She watches a full day of jousting, including Ser Gregor Clegane killing a young knight from the Vale. Ser Loras Tyrell gives Sansa a red rose. Petyr Baelish introduces himself, remarking on her resemblance to her mother. At the evening feast, Joffrey is courteous and attentive to Sansa. King Robert drunkenly shouts at Queen Cersei about fighting in the melee. Sandor Clegane escorts Sansa back to the castle and tells her how Gregor burned his face as a child, then threatens to kill her if she tells anyone.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa rides to the tourney** — Travels in a litter with Septa Mordane and Jeyne Poole, curtained in yellow silk
2. **Day of jousting begins** — Knights of the Kingsguard and many lords and lesser knights take the field
3. **Jory unhorsed Horas Redwyne** — Jory wins his first joust
4. **Jory defeated by Lothor Brune** — King awards victory to Brune after three passes with no unhorsing
5. **Harwin unhorsed by Ser Meryn** — In Harwin's first tilt
6. **Alyn defeated by Ser Balon Swann** — Alyn falls in his joust
7. **Jaime defeats Andar Royce, Bryce Caron, and Barristan Selmy** — Rides brilliantly all day
8. **Ser Gregor kills a young knight from the Vale** — Lance strikes under the gorget, drives through his throat; the youth dies instantly near Sansa
9. **Jeyne Poole taken away by Septa Mordane** — Jeyne weeps hysterically; later reported ill and returned to castle
10. **Ser Balon Swann falls to Gregor** — After the young knight's death
11. **Lord Renly unhorsed by the Hound** — Violent fall; golden antler on helm snaps; Renly recovers gracefully and gives broken antler to the Hound
12. **Hound tosses antler to crowd** — Commons fight over it until Renly restores peace
13. **Hedge knight kills Beric Dondarrion's horse** — Declared forfeit and disgraced
14. **Beric Dondarrion unhorsed by Thoros of Myr** — After switching to a new mount
15. **Ser Aron Santagar tilts thrice with Lothor Brune without result** — Ser Aron then falls to Lord Jason Mallister; Brune falls to Robar Royce
16. **Ser Loras defeats Robar Royce** — Splits his shield, drives him from saddle; Robar carried off dazed
17. **Ser Loras gives Sansa a red rose** — Stops before her specifically; gives red rather than white
18. **Petyr Baelish approaches Sansa** — Recognizes her Tully features; mentions her mother was his "queen of beauty"; touches her hair
19. **King decrees final three matches for the next morning** — Semifinalists: Hound, Gregor, Jaime, Ser Loras
20. **Feast begins at riverside** — Six roasting aurochs; elaborate multi-course meal; singers, juggler, Moon Boy performing
21. **Joffrey is courteous to Sansa** — Talks to her all night; feeds her snails; serves her meat; pours wine for her and Septa Mordane
22. **King Robert drunkenly shouts at Cersei** — Declares he will fight in the melee; Cersei storms off silently
23. **Robert shoves Jaime to the ground** — Jaime tries to calm him; Robert mocks him as "Kingslayer"
24. **Renly offers Robert a fresh goblet** — Attempts to defuse the situation
25. **Joffrey abruptly leaves Sansa** — Orders the Hound to escort her back; departs without farewell
26. **Sandor escorts Sansa back to the Red Keep** — Through pavilion field, by cart through King's Gate, up tower stairs
27. **Sandor tells Sansa the story of his burns** — Gregor held his face in burning coals over a stolen wooden toy when Sandor was six or seven
28. **Sandor threatens Sansa** — Tells her he'll kill her if she tells anyone what he revealed

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Oscillates between enchantment and unease. Giddy and breathless at the spectacle of the tourney; transfixed but unsettled by the young knight's death; captivated by Joffrey's courtesy at the feast; terrified then ultimately compassionate during the Hound's confession
- **Primary preoccupation:** The beauty and romance of the tourney; her desire for Joffrey's approval; living out the songs she loves
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to blame Arya and Cersei rather than Joffrey for Lady's death; chooses to remain composed rather than cry at the death; promises Sandor she will keep his secret
- **Self-deception flags:** Actively rationalizes Joffrey's responsibility for Lady's death ("it had not been Joffrey's doing, not truly"); tells herself she "could not hate Joffrey tonight" because "he was too beautiful to hate"; frames her non-reaction to the young knight's death as noble composure rather than examining it
