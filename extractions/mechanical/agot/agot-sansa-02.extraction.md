# AGOT — Sansa II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 30
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Sansa II
- **first_available:** AGOT Sansa II
- **location_primary:** Tourney grounds outside King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Riverside feast area, King's Landing, Red Keep
- **approximate_timeline:** During the Hand's tourney, shortly after Eddard Stark becomes Hand of the King
- **time_markers:** Jousting goes "all day and into the dusk"; moon is "well up" when the king ends the jousting; feast begins at dusk and continues into the night; torchlit return to the castle
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa attends the Hand's tourney with Septa Mordane and Jeyne Poole, watching a full day of jousting that includes Ser Gregor Clegane killing a young knight from the Vale. The final four are the Hound, Gregor, Jaime Lannister, and Ser Loras Tyrell, who gives Sansa a red rose. Littlefinger introduces himself to Sansa, remarking on her resemblance to her mother. At the riverside feast, Joffrey is charming and courteous to Sansa until King Robert drunkenly fights with Queen Cersei and the evening collapses. The Hound escorts Sansa back to the castle and tells her the true story of how his brother burned his face, threatening to kill her if she ever reveals the secret.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa rides to the tourney** — Travels in a litter with yellow silk curtains alongside Septa Mordane and Jeyne Poole; takes places among high lords and ladies as her father promised.
2. **Jousting begins** — The Kingsguard takes the field in white-scaled armor; Jaime in gold with lion's-head helm; Gregor Clegane thunders past; many named knights and lords compete.
3. **Jory, Alyn, and Harwin compete** — Jory unhorses Horas Redwyne and a Frey but loses to Lothor Brune on points; Harwin falls to Ser Meryn; Alyn falls to Ser Balon Swann.
4. **Ser Gregor kills a young knight** — His lance rides up and strikes through the young Vale knight's gorget; the youth dies feet from Sansa, blood darkening his blue cloak and turning the crescent moons red.
5. **Jeyne Poole leaves** — Weeps hysterically; Septa Mordane takes her away. Sansa stays, watching with "strange fascination," noting she cannot cry.
6. **Jousting continues** — Jaime defeats Andar Royce, Bryce Caron, and Barristan Selmy; Balon Swann falls to Gregor; Renly is unhorsed by the Hound and graciously gives him the broken antler tine; the Hound tosses it into the crowd; Renly restores order.
7. **A hedge knight kills Dondarrion's horse** — Declared forfeit; Dondarrion remounts but is knocked off by Thoros of Myr.
8. **Final four determined** — The Hound, Gregor, Jaime, and Ser Loras Tyrell remain.
9. **Ser Loras defeats Robar Royce** — Splits his shield and drives him from the saddle; Robar carried off dazed.
10. **Ser Loras gives Sansa a red rose** — He gave white roses to other maidens but singles Sansa out with a red one, calling her "sweet lady" and saying "no victory is half so beautiful as you."
11. **Littlefinger approaches Sansa** — Recognizes her as Catelyn's daughter by her Tully look; says her mother was his "queen of beauty once"; touches her hair and abruptly walks away.
12. **The feast begins** — King decrees last three matches for the next morning; court moves to riverside feast.
13. **Joffrey is charming to Sansa** — Sits beside her, compliments her, shows her how to eat snails, serves her meat, pours her wine.
14. **King Robert's drunken outburst** — Shouts at Cersei that he will fight in the melee tomorrow; Cersei storms off silently; Robert shoves Jaime to the ground and taunts him as "Kingslayer."
15. **Renly intervenes** — Offers to bring Robert a fresh goblet of wine.
16. **Joffrey abruptly leaves** — Tells Sansa it grows late with a "queer look" as if not seeing her; calls the Hound to escort her.
17. **The Hound escorts Sansa** — Mocks her courtesies; calls her "a pretty little talking bird."
18. **The Hound forces Sansa to look at his burns** — Grabs her jaw, holds the torch close, makes her stare at his ruined face.
19. **The Hound tells Sansa the truth about his burns** — Gregor held his face in burning coals over a stolen toy; their father covered it up; Rhaegar later knighted Gregor.
20. **Sansa comforts the Hound** — Finds his shoulder with her hand and whispers "He was no true knight."
21. **Silent return to the Red Keep** — They ride through the King's Gate and up to Sansa's bedchamber corridor in silence.
22. **The Hound threatens Sansa** — Warns her that if she ever tells anyone what he told her, he will kill her.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Giddy and enchanted by the tourney spectacle; fascinated rather than horrified by the young knight's death; conflicted about Joffrey (relief and delight when he's courteous); drunk on the glamour of the feast; terrified of the Hound during the escort but ultimately feels sadness and empathy for him
- **Primary preoccupation:** The romance and pageantry of knightly life; her relationship with Joffrey; wanting to behave as "a great lady" and "a true lady" should
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses not to hate Joffrey, redirecting blame to Cersei and Arya for Lady's death; chooses to comfort the Hound rather than remain afraid; promises to keep his secret
- **Self-deception flags:** Rationalizes Joffrey's role in Lady's death — "it had not been Joffrey's doing, not truly. The queen had done it; she was the one to hate, her and Arya. Nothing bad would have happened except for Arya." Also tells herself a great lady would not notice the Hound's face — performing courtesy as identity.
