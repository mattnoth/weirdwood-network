# AGOT — Tyrion VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 57
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion VII
- **first_available:** AGOT Tyrion VII
- **location_primary:** The inn at the crossroads (Tywin's camp)
- **location_secondary:** Mountains of the Moon foothills, the Green Fork of the Trident, the crossroads
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Tyrion's escape from the Vale; concurrent with the early stages of the War of the Five Kings
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion descends from the Mountains of the Moon with a band of roughly three hundred clansmen and Bronn, heading toward a Lannister army encamped at the crossroads. He rides ahead with Bronn and representatives of each clan to meet his father. At the inn at the crossroads, Tyrion finds Lord Tywin and Ser Kevan, who brief him on the war's progress: Jaime's victories at the Golden Tooth and Riverrun, the capture of Ser Edmure Tully, and the death of King Robert. Tyrion requests arms and armor for his clansmen. A messenger arrives reporting that the Stark host is moving south, and Lord Tywin orders a march to meet them, manipulating the clansmen into joining the fight.

## Events & Actions
1. **Chella scouts the Lannister army** — Reports approximately twenty thousand men with red banners bearing a golden lion at the crossroads.
2. **Tyrion rides ahead to meet the army** — Takes Bronn, Chella, Shagga, Conn, Ulf, and Timett; leaves the remaining ~300 clansmen behind.
3. **Descent through the foothills** — The party passes unmortared stone towers with watchers; a raven takes wing. They reach a strong point with crossbowmen and a low earthen wall.
4. **Escort to the camp** — The strong point captain recognizes Tyrion and provides an escort. They pass blackened fields, burned holdfasts, and signs of recent fighting.
5. **Arrival at the barricade** — A perimeter of sharpened stakes manned by pikemen and archers. Ser Flement Brax rides out to challenge and recognizes Tyrion.
6. **Tyrion observes the camp** — Notes banners of Presters (red ox), Crakehall (brindled boar), Marbrand (burning tree), Lydden (badger). The clansmen are awed by the army's size.
7. **Tyrion sees the hanged woman at the inn** — Recognizes her (implied to be the innkeep); ravens have eaten her face. He addresses her with dark humor.
8. **Shagga confronts the guard captain** — Throws the captain of the guard across the room, snaps his sword over his knee after the guard drew steel.
9. **Reunion with Tywin and Kevan** — Tyrion meets his father and uncle in the inn's common room. They brief him on the war.
10. **War briefing** — Tyrion learns of Jaime's victories, the fall of multiple Riverlands strongholds, the capture of Edmure Tully, and Robert Baratheon's death.
11. **Tywin offers Tyrion a command** — Suggests dealing with Marq Piper and Karyl Vance, or Beric Dondarrion and Thoros. Tyrion deflects.
12. **Tyrion requests arms for the clansmen** — Asks for three thousand helms, hauberks, swords, pikes, spearheads, maces, battle-axes, gauntlets, gorgets, greaves, breastplates, and wagons.
13. **Messenger arrives** — Reports the Stark host is moving south down the causeway.
14. **Tywin orders the march** — Commands Ser Addam to fall back and harass Stark flanks; orders drummers to beat assembly; sends word to Jaime.
15. **Tywin manipulates the clansmen** — Uses reverse psychology ("men of the winterlands are iron and ice, even my boldest knights fear them") to goad the clansmen into volunteering for battle.
16. **Clansmen agree to ride** — Timett, Conn, Shagga, and Chella all commit. Chella insists Tyrion ride with them as surety for his promises.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Sardonic and guarded; acutely self-conscious around his father; resigned but adaptable
- **Primary preoccupation:** Managing the clansmen while navigating his father's contempt; processing the dramatic political changes (Robert's death, Cersei's ascent)
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to ride ahead to meet Tywin with only a small escort; deflects Tywin's offered commands; requests arms for the clansmen; accepts being bound to ride with the clansmen into battle
- **Self-deception flags:** None apparent; Tyrion's self-awareness is notably clear — he recognizes Tywin's manipulations ("Oh, deftly done"), his own precarious position with the clansmen ("Was he their commander or their captive?"), and Cersei's real power
