# AGOT — Eddard V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 26
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard V
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard V
- **location_primary:** Grand Maester Pycelle's chambers, Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Tower of the Hand (steps, Ned's chambers, solar); castle godswood (recalled); yard below Tower of the Hand
- **approximate_timeline:** After Ned has been serving as Hand for some time; after news of Bran's survival has reached King's Landing; before the tourney of the Hand
- **time_markers:** Daytime (heat described, sweat-stained silks); recalled vigil — "all through the dark hours" until dawn; the serving girl brings iced milk during the visit; the tourney referenced as upcoming; Pycelle mentions "the last year of summer"; references King Maekar's summer breaking "in the seventh year"
- **chapter_summary:** Ned visits Grand Maester Pycelle to question him about Jon Arryn's death. Pycelle describes Jon Arryn's sudden illness, his final words ("the seed is strong"), and insists the death was natural while subtly directing suspicion toward Varys. He reveals Jon Arryn had been reading a book on the lineages of the great houses by Grand Maester Malleon. On his way back, Ned encounters Arya practicing her water dancing balance on the Tower of the Hand steps and they discuss Bran's future. Littlefinger then visits Ned's solar, reveals that spies for Varys and the queen watch the Tower of the Hand, and provides the names of four former servants of Jon Arryn still in King's Landing who might be questioned.

- **Time of day:** Daytime; heat at its peak; recalled scene transitions from night vigil through dawn
- **Lighting:** Not explicitly described; daylight implied from the heat and Littlefinger watching knights practice in the yard
- **Sounds:** Pycelle's jeweled chain chinking as he shifts; a silver bell tinkled to summon servant; a raven cawing in the rookery above
- **Smells:** Pycelle recalls smells of Oldtown nights — "perfume and sweat, melons ripe to bursting, peaches and pomegranates, nightshade and moonbloom"

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned visits Pycelle** — Ned arrives at Grand Maester Pycelle's solar to question him about Jon Arryn's death.
2. **Pycelle offers refreshments** — Pycelle rings a silver bell and has a serving girl bring iced milk sweetened with honey for both of them.
3. **Pycelle reminisces** — Pycelle digresses about the heat, comparing it to King Maekar's summer, and recalls his youth at the Citadel in Oldtown.
4. **Pycelle describes Jon Arryn's decline** — Reports that Jon had seemed burdened for some time; one day asked about a book, seemed troubled; next morning was too sick to rise. Maester Colemon initially treated it as a chill on the stomach.
5. **Pycelle explains sending Colemon away** — Says he feared Colemon's purging treatments (wasting potions and pepper juice) would kill the older man.
6. **Pycelle reveals Jon Arryn's final words** — Jon called out "Robert" during his fever (unclear if meaning the king or his son); Pycelle gave milk of the poppy; Jon whispered "the seed is strong" to the king and Lady Lysa before dying.
7. **Ned raises the possibility of poison** — Pycelle dismisses it, says Jon displayed none of the common signs, then deflects suspicion toward Varys and eunuchs.
8. **Ned asks about the book** — Pycelle identifies it as a tome by Grand Maester Malleon on the lineages of the great houses and agrees to send it to Ned.
9. **Pycelle reveals the queen's absence** — Cersei and the children were traveling to Casterly Rock with Tywin when Jon Arryn died; Pycelle sent the queen word by raven.
10. **Pycelle mentions Bran's recovery** — References Maester Luwin's message about Bran as an example of good news by raven.
11. **Ned encounters Arya on the Tower steps** — Arya is practicing balancing on one leg as Syrio Forel taught her; her bare feet are scuffed by the rough stone.
12. **Arya asks about Bran's future** — Ned tells her Bran can never be a knight but could be a lord, builder, sailor, or High Septon; he privately reflects that Bran will never run beside his wolf, lie with a woman, or hold his own son.
13. **Arya asks if she can do those things** — Ned tells her she will marry a king; Arya says "that's Sansa" and resumes balancing.
14. **Recalled scene: godswood vigil** — After the bird came from Winterfell about Bran, Ned took the girls to the castle godswood to pray. Sansa dreamed of Bran smiling. Dragon's breath flowers surrounded the sleeping girls at dawn.
15. **Ned returns to chambers** — Strips off sweaty silks, washes with cold water from basin.
16. **Littlefinger arrives** — Alyn announces him; Ned receives him in his solar.
17. **Littlefinger comments on Barristan Selmy** — Observes Kingsguard practicing in the yard; calls Selmy tiresome despite his sword skills.
18. **Littlefinger reveals Jon Arryn's remaining servants** — Names four: a pregnant kitchen girl wed to one of Renly's grooms, a stablehand who joined the City Watch, a potboy discharged for theft, and Ser Hugh of the Vale (Jon's squire, knighted after Jon's death).
19. **Littlefinger reveals surveillance** — Points out a boy at the armory steps (Varys's spy) and a guardsman on the ramparts above the stables (the queen's spy) watching the Tower of the Hand; warns the Red Keep is "full of eyes."
20. **Littlefinger advises indirect investigation** — Tells Ned to send a trusted man to question the servants rather than going himself, to avoid surveillance.
21. **Littlefinger's parting warning** — When Ned thanks him, Littlefinger says distrusting him was the wisest thing Ned has done since arriving.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Uncomfortable in the heat; deeply concerned about Jon Arryn's death; suspicious of Pycelle's true loyalties; saddened by Bran's condition (grief "too deep for words"); amused by Arya; distrustful of Littlefinger despite accepting his help; uncomfortable and alarmed about the surveillance
- **Primary preoccupation:** Investigating Jon Arryn's death — specifically whether he was poisoned, what his final words meant, and what the lineage book signifies
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to obtain the Malleon lineage book from Pycelle; decides to question Jon Arryn's former servants; accepts Littlefinger's advice to send a trusted man rather than going himself
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned nearly lets his guard down with Littlefinger ("Perhaps I was wrong to distrust you") — Littlefinger himself warns him this is a mistake
