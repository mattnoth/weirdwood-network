# AGOT — Eddard V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 26
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard V
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard V
- **location_primary:** Grand Maester Pycelle's chambers, Red Keep
- **location_secondary:** Tower of the Hand (stairway and Ned's chambers/solar), Red Keep yard
- **approximate_timeline:** During the heat of summer in King's Landing; after Bran's recovery news has reached King's Landing, before the Hand's tourney
- **time_markers:** Extreme summer heat described; Pycelle references "the last year of summer"; Ned recalls a night vigil in the godswood after the bird came about Bran — dawn broke the next morning; the visit to Pycelle and return to chambers appear to take place during the same day
- **chapter_summary:** Eddard visits Grand Maester Pycelle to investigate Jon Arryn's death. Pycelle describes Jon Arryn's final illness as natural, mentions Jon asked for a book on lineages the day before falling ill, and reveals Jon's last words were "the seed is strong." Pycelle deflects Ned's suggestion of poison toward Varys. On his way back, Ned encounters Arya practicing her water dancing balance on the Tower of the Hand steps and discusses Bran's future with her. In his chambers, Littlefinger arrives with information about four remaining members of Jon Arryn's household, including his former squire Ser Hugh of the Vale, and warns Ned about the many spies watching him in the Red Keep.

- **Weather:** Extreme heat; thick, moist air covers the city "like a damp woolen blanket"
- **Season indicators:** Late summer; Pycelle says "the last year of summer is always the hottest" (a smallfolk saying); Pycelle recalls King Maekar's summer which broke in its seventh year; Ned's northern origin referenced with "summer snows"
- **Time of day:** Daytime (heat, sweat-stained silks); the godswood flashback occurs at night through to dawn
- **Lighting:** Not explicitly described for the main scenes
- **Sounds:** Pycelle's silver bell tinkling; a raven cawing loudly in the rookery above Pycelle's chambers; knights practicing swords in the yard (observed from Ned's solar window)
- **Smells:** Pycelle recalls smells of Oldtown nights — "perfume and sweat, melons ripe to bursting, peaches and pomegranates, nightshade and moonbloom"
- **Notable sensory details:** Ned feels his silk tunic clinging to his chest from sweat; the riverside is unruly with the poor fleeing airless warrens for sleeping places near the water; Ned strips off sweat-stained silks and sluices cold water over his head upon returning to his chambers; Arya's bare feet scuffed by rough stone steps

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned visits Grand Maester Pycelle** — Ned arrives at Pycelle's solar to inquire about Jon Arryn's death.
2. **Pycelle orders refreshments** — Pycelle rings a silver bell and a serving girl brings iced milk sweetened with honey.
3. **Pycelle describes Jon Arryn's decline** — Pycelle recounts Jon seeming melancholy and tired, burdened by his sickly son and anxious wife.
4. **Pycelle recounts Jon Arryn's final illness** — Jon came asking for a book, was healthy, then was twisted in pain the next morning. Maester Colemon diagnosed a chill on the stomach.
5. **Pycelle reveals he dismissed Maester Colemon** — Pycelle sent Colemon away, fearing his purging treatments (wasting potions and pepper juice) would kill Jon Arryn.
6. **Pycelle recounts Jon Arryn's last words** — Jon called out "Robert" several times; whispered "the seed is strong" before the milk of the poppy silenced him.
7. **Ned suggests poison** — Ned raises the possibility that Jon Arryn was poisoned. Pycelle calls it possible but unlikely, and deflects suspicion toward Varys.
8. **Ned asks about the book** — Ned requests to see the book Jon Arryn borrowed — a tome by Grand Maester Malleon on lineages of great houses. Pycelle agrees to send it.
9. **Ned asks about the queen's presence at Jon's death** — Pycelle reveals Cersei and the children were traveling to Casterly Rock with Tywin's retinue for the tourney on Joffrey's name day.
10. **Ned encounters Arya on the Tower steps** — Arya is practicing standing on one leg as Syrio Forel taught her; she asks about Bran's future.
11. **Ned recalls the godswood vigil** — Flashback to the night news of Bran's recovery arrived: Ned took the girls to the castle godswood to pray, kept vigil alone through the night, and the girls woke surrounded by dragon's breath flowers.
12. **Ned discusses Bran's future with Arya** — Ned tells Arya that Bran cannot be a knight but could be a lord, builder, sailor, or even High Septon. Arya asks if she could do those things; Ned says she will marry a king. Arya says "that's Sansa."
13. **Ned returns to his chambers** — Ned strips off sweat-soaked clothing and washes; Alyn announces Littlefinger.
14. **Littlefinger provides leads** — Littlefinger identifies four remaining members of Jon Arryn's household: a pregnant kitchen girl married to one of Renly's grooms, a stablehand who joined the City Watch, a discharged potboy, and Ser Hugh of the Vale (Jon's former squire, recently knighted).
15. **Littlefinger reveals the extent of surveillance** — Littlefinger points out a boy in the yard who reports to Varys and a guardsman on the wall who reports to the queen, warning Ned that the Red Keep is full of eyes.
16. **Littlefinger advises Ned to send a trusted man** — Rather than question Jon's former household himself (which would be noted by spies), Ned should send a trusted servant. Littlefinger warns that trusting anyone completely is unwise.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Suspicious and methodical during the Pycelle interview; tender and sorrowful when thinking about Bran and talking with Arya; uncomfortable and frustrated when confronted with the surveillance apparatus in the Red Keep; deeply distrustful of both Pycelle and Littlefinger
- **Primary preoccupation:** Determining the truth about Jon Arryn's death — was it natural or poison? Who benefits?
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to obtain the lineage book Jon Arryn was reading; decides to question the four remaining members of Jon Arryn's household; will send a trusted man rather than going himself
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned briefly considers trusting Littlefinger after his help ("Perhaps I was wrong to distrust you") — Littlefinger himself immediately corrects this impulse
