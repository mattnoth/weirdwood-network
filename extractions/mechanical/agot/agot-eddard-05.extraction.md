# AGOT — Eddard V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 26

- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard V
- **location_primary:** Grand Maester Pycelle's chambers, Red Keep, King's Landing

- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after news of Bran's survival reaches King's Landing; during a period of intense summer heat; Ned is actively investigating Jon Arryn's death as Hand of the King
- **chapter_summary:** Ned interrogates Grand Maester Pycelle about the circumstances of Jon Arryn's death. Pycelle describes the illness as natural, mentions Jon's last words ("The seed is strong"), reveals that Jon had asked to borrow a book on the lineages of the great houses the day before falling ill, and notes that the queen was absent from King's Landing when Jon died. On the way back to his chambers, Ned encounters Arya practicing her water dancing and they discuss Bran's future. Littlefinger then visits Ned and provides the names of four former Arryn household members still in King's Landing, warns Ned that spies for Varys and the queen are watching him, and advises him to send a trusted man to question the witnesses rather than going himself.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned interrogates Pycelle about Jon Arryn's death** — Pycelle describes Jon's decline from apparent health to death over a few days, attributing it to natural illness. He reveals Jon's last words were "The seed is strong," spoken to the king and Lysa.
2. **Pycelle reveals Jon Arryn borrowed a book on lineages** — Jon came to Pycelle asking for a book by Grand Maester Malleon on the lineages of the great houses, the day before he fell ill.
3. **Pycelle dismisses the possibility of poison** — When Ned raises the idea, Pycelle says Jon showed none of the common signs, but pointedly insinuates Varys when Ned mentions poison as "a woman's weapon."
4. **Pycelle reveals Cersei's absence during Jon's death** — The queen and her children were traveling to Casterly Rock with Tywin when Jon died; Pycelle sent word by raven.
5. **Ned encounters Arya practicing water dancing** — On the Tower of the Hand steps, Arya balances on one leg per Syrio's teachings. They discuss Bran's future — Ned tells her Bran cannot be a knight but could serve in other capacities.
6. **Ned recalls the godswood vigil** — After news of Bran's survival arrived, Ned took the girls to the castle godswood to give thanks. Sansa dreamed of Bran smiling.
7. **Littlefinger visits Ned with intelligence** — He identifies four former Arryn household members still in King's Landing: a pregnant kitchen girl, a stablehand now in the City Watch, a discharged potboy, and Ser Hugh of the Vale (Jon's former squire, now knighted).
8. **Littlefinger reveals Ned is being watched** — Points out a boy at the armory who reports to Varys and a guardsman on the wall who reports to the queen. Warns the Red Keep is full of spies.
9. **Littlefinger advises Ned to send a proxy** — Tells Ned to send a trusted man to question the Arryn witnesses rather than going himself, to avoid surveillance.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grimly determined but uneasy; suspicious of Pycelle's motives; uncomfortable with the espionage culture of King's Landing; deep sadness about Bran's condition; tender with Arya
- **Primary preoccupation:** Investigating Jon Arryn's death — specifically whether it was murder by poison and what the book on lineages means
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to request the lineage book from Pycelle; decides to question the former Arryn servants Littlefinger identified; accepts (grudgingly) Littlefinger's advice to send a proxy
- **Self-deception flags:** Expresses gratitude and partial trust toward Littlefinger ("Perhaps I was wrong to distrust you") — immediately contradicted by Littlefinger himself. Ned's instinct to trust is at odds with the political environment he's operating in.
