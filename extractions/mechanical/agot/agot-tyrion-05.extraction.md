# AGOT — Tyrion V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 39
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion V
- **first_available:** AGOT Tyrion V
- **location_primary:** The Eyrie (sky cells, High Hall)
- **location_secondary:** None
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Tyrion's arrival at the Eyrie; he has been imprisoned in the sky cells for an indeterminate number of days
- **time_markers:** "twenty day, thirty, fifty maybe" (Mord's estimate of how long before Tyrion breaks); "that first day" (when Tyrion crawled to the edge); Tyrion is fetched "late that night" after sending his message; the trial by combat is set for "the morrow"
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion languishes in the Eyrie's sky cells, tormented by the gaoler Mord who withholds food and beats him. He reflects on his arrival at the Eyrie and his disastrous first audience before Lysa Arryn and Robert Arryn, where his sharp tongue earned him imprisonment. Growing desperate, Tyrion bribes Mord with promises of gold to deliver a message to Lysa claiming he wishes to confess. Summoned to the High Hall, Tyrion performs a mock confession of petty sins, then demands a trial when accused of conspiring to murder Jon Arryn and sending a knife to kill Bran Stark. When Lysa agrees to a trial before her son, Tyrion demands trial by combat. After Lysa names Ser Vardis Egen as her champion, Tyrion asks for Jaime but is denied delay, and the sellsword Bronn volunteers to fight for him.

## Events & Actions
1. **Mord taunts Tyrion with food** — Mord offers boiled beans but holds them out of reach near the cell's open edge, then drops the plate over the side when Tyrion refuses to approach.
2. **Mord kicks Tyrion** — After Tyrion curses him, Mord drives a steel-toed boot into Tyrion's ribs.
3. **Tyrion reflects on his imprisonment** — He huddles under a thin blanket, recalls losing the shadowskin cloak to Mord, contemplates the design of the sky cells and their psychological torture, and reads a despairing message written in blood on the wall.
4. **Tyrion recalls his arrival at the Eyrie** — He remembers the humiliating climb (Bronn carried him the last stretch), his disastrous first audience before Robert and Lysa Arryn, his defiant exchange with Lysa, Robert's demand to "see him fly," and Catelyn's intervention saving him from harm.
5. **Tyrion speculates on the political situation** — He wonders about Lord Tywin's response, Jaime's possible military actions, whether anyone knows his location, and what Cersei might do. He reflects on the attempted assassination of Bran Stark and suspects a third party may be using him as a catspaw.
6. **Tyrion bribes Mord** — He offers gold and promises of wealth, enduring multiple beatings with the leather strap in the process, until Mord agrees to deliver a message.
7. **Tyrion dictates a written message** — He writes a letter promising gold to Mord and a message to Lysa claiming he wishes to confess his crimes. Mord fetches paper and ink and delivers it.
8. **Tyrion is summoned to the High Hall** — Late at night, Ser Vardis Egen and Mord come for him. Tyrion reclaims his shadowskin cloak before leaving.
9. **Tyrion performs a mock confession** — Before the assembled lords and knights of the Vale, Tyrion confesses to petty sins (whoring, gambling, cheating, wishing his family dead, saying cruel things) rather than the murders he is accused of. The audience laughs.
10. **Lysa and Catelyn confront Tyrion with the real charges** — Catelyn states he is accused of sending a knife to kill Bran and conspiring to murder Jon Arryn. Tyrion denies all knowledge.
11. **Lysa orders the Moon Door opened** — She announces Tyrion will be tried before her son and may leave by one door or the other. The Moon Door is opened, revealing the night sky and a fatal drop.
12. **Tyrion demands trial by combat** — Rather than face judgment from Robert Arryn, Tyrion invokes the gods and demands trial by combat. The hall erupts in laughter.
13. **Multiple lords volunteer as Lysa's champion** — The knight with the green viper sigil, Lord Hunter, Ser Albar Royce, Ser Lyn Corbray, and a dozen others all offer to fight.
14. **Lysa names Ser Vardis Egen as her champion** — Ser Vardis reluctantly accepts, noting it would be shameful to slaughter a dwarf and call it justice.
15. **Tyrion requests Jaime as his champion** — Lysa denies the delay, insisting the combat occur the next day.
16. **Tyrion pressures Lysa publicly** — He turns to Marillion, asking the singer to record how Lady Arryn denied a dwarf his champion. Lysa relents and allows Tyrion to name any champion present.
17. **Bronn volunteers to fight for Tyrion** — After a tense silence, Bronn speaks from the rear of the hall, offering to stand for the dwarf.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Desperate but resourceful; rage at Mord alternating with calculation; shame over physical humiliation (being carried by Bronn, falling in the High Hall); fear of the sky cell's edge and of death; grim determination to talk his way free; sardonic humor masking genuine terror
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and escape from the Eyrie; finding a way to exploit Lysa's legal obligations and the presence of witnesses
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Decides to bribe Mord to deliver a message rather than wait to rot; (2) chooses to claim he wishes to confess as the bait; (3) performs the mock confession to seize control of the narrative; (4) demands a trial rather than accept imprisonment; (5) demands trial by combat rather than face judgment from Robert Arryn; (6) attempts to name Jaime as champion; (7) uses Marillion to publicly shame Lysa into allowing a champion
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion acknowledges his mouth gets him into trouble but continues to use it recklessly; he hopes Bronn will fight for him based on a glance rather than any agreement; he reflects that this plan might be "a colossal blunder" even as he commits to it
