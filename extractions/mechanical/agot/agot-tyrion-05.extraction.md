# AGOT — Tyrion V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 39
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion V
- **first_available:** AGOT Tyrion V
- **location_primary:** The Eyrie (sky cells; High Hall of the Arryns)
- **location_secondary:** Mountains of the Moon (visible from sky cell), Sky (courtyard below the Eyrie)
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after Tyrion's arrival at the Eyrie; follows his imprisonment in the sky cells
- **time_markers:** "twenty day, thirty, fifty maybe" (Mord's prediction of how long until Tyrion breaks); "that first day" (when Tyrion looked over the edge); late at night (when Ser Vardis comes for Tyrion); "on the morrow" (when the trial by combat is scheduled)
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion languishes in a sky cell at the Eyrie, tormented by his turnkey Mord who withholds food and beats him. He reflects on his disastrous arrival at the High Hall, where Lysa Arryn accused him of murder before the assembled court, and Catelyn Stark intervened to prevent violence. Growing desperate, Tyrion bribes Mord with promises of gold to deliver a message requesting to confess his crimes. Brought before the court at night, Tyrion delivers a mock confession of petty sins, then demands a formal trial, and when Lysa agrees, pivots to demand trial by combat. After Lysa names Ser Vardis Egen as her champion and refuses to wait for Jaime, the sellsword Bronn volunteers to fight for Tyrion.

- **Lighting:** Sky cell has natural light — "plenty of fresh air and sunshine, and the moon and stars by night"; High Hall lit by fifty torches burning in sconces along the walls; torches flutter and some gutter out when the Moon Door is opened; "uncaring stars" visible through the Moon Door
- **Sounds:** Wind screaming day and night; rattle of keys when Mord locks the door; laughter in the High Hall (at Tyrion's expense, multiple occasions); Marillion plucking his woodharp; the snap of Mord's leather strap; wind whistling through the Moon Door
- **Smells:** The shadowskin cloak "had smelled of blood and mold"
- **Notable sensory details:** The floor of the sky cell slopes subtly toward the open edge; cold of the cell; Tyrion's mouth full of blood after Mord's beating; the strap's pain making him grit his teeth; the sensation of his fingers brushing "nothing" at the cell's edge

## Events & Actions
1. **Mord taunts Tyrion with food** — Mord offers boiled beans but holds them out of reach, then drops the plate over the edge of the sky cell. Tyrion curses him and receives a kick in the ribs.
2. **Tyrion reflects on his imprisonment** — He huddles under a thin blanket, miserable in the cold, wind-battered sky cell with its sloping floor and open edge. He recalls looking over the edge on his first day and seeing the six-hundred-foot drop.
3. **Flashback: Tyrion's arrival in the High Hall** — Robert Arryn asks if Tyrion is "the bad man." Lysa accuses Tyrion of murdering Jon Arryn. Tyrion responds with sarcasm despite knowing silence would serve him better. Bronn had carried Tyrion up the final leg of the climb, humiliating him.
4. **Lysa threatens Tyrion** — She warns him to guard his tongue, invoking the loyalty of the Vale's knights. Tyrion counter-threatens with Jaime's retribution.
5. **Robert Arryn demands Tyrion fly** — The boy becomes hysterical; guardsmen in sky-blue cloaks seize Tyrion.
6. **Catelyn intervenes** — She reminds Lysa that Tyrion is her prisoner and must not be harmed.
7. **Lysa sends Tyrion to the sky cells** — Ser Vardis Egen takes Tyrion to a sky cell after he collapses and is laughed at by the court.
8. **Tyrion contemplates his situation** — He considers who might rescue him, wonders about Cersei's response, and reflects on the assassin sent after Bran Stark, suspecting a third party may be using him as a catspaw.
9. **Tyrion bribes Mord** — He offers gold in exchange for delivering a message to Lysa. Mord beats him repeatedly during the negotiation but ultimately agrees when Tyrion puts the promise in writing.
10. **Tyrion is brought before the court at night** — Ser Vardis wakes him; Tyrion recovers his shadowskin cloak from Mord.
11. **Tyrion surveys the High Hall** — He notes the assembled lords and ladies of the Vale, his traveling companions (Ser Rodrik, Ser Willis, Marillion), and Bronn lounging in the rear.
12. **Tyrion delivers a mock confession** — He confesses to whoring, gambling, cheating, wishing his father and sister dead, and being cruel — but not to any of the actual charges. The crowd laughs.
13. **Catelyn formally states the charges** — Sending a hired knife after Bran, conspiring to murder Jon Arryn. Tyrion denies all.
14. **Lysa orders Tyrion returned to the cells** — Threatens a smaller cell with a steeper floor.
15. **Tyrion demands a trial** — He appeals to royal justice, asking whether "honor stops at the Bloody Gate," and demands a trial before gods and men, showing his bruised face.
16. **Lysa opens the Moon Door** — She agrees to a trial before Robert Arryn, threatening execution through the Moon Door.
17. **Tyrion demands trial by combat** — He claims the right of the gods' judgment, surprising the court. Laughter fills the hall.
18. **Multiple knights volunteer to champion Lysa** — The unnamed green-viper knight, Lord Hunter, Ser Albar Royce, Ser Lyn Corbray, and a dozen others.
19. **Lysa names Ser Vardis Egen as champion** — Ser Vardis objects, finding it shameful to fight a dwarf.
20. **Tyrion requests Jaime as his champion** — Lysa refuses to wait for Jaime's arrival.
21. **Tyrion pressures Lysa through Marillion** — He tells the singer to record how Lady Arryn denied him the right to a champion, forcing Lysa to relent.
22. **Bronn volunteers as Tyrion's champion** — From the rear of the hall, the sellsword offers to fight for Tyrion.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Oscillates between rage, fear, dark humor, and desperate cunning. Furious at Mord's cruelty; fearful of the sky cell's edge and of dying forgotten; shame from being carried by Bronn and mocked by the court; determined to talk his way out.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and escape from the sky cells. Strategizing how to manipulate the legal situation to his advantage. Growing physical weakness from cold, hunger, and beatings.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to bribe Mord with promises of gold to deliver a message. Chooses the stratagem of requesting to "confess his crimes." Delivers a mock confession of petty sins. Demands trial by combat when conventional trial threatens him with the Moon Door and Robert's judgment. Requests Jaime as champion, then pressures Lysa through Marillion to allow him any champion.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself "Catelyn Stark wants me alive, he doesn't dare kill me" about Mord — but acknowledges this is desperate reassurance. Acknowledges his own "dangerously big mouth" as his primary weakness. Notes his plan may have been "a colossal blunder" when no one initially steps forward to champion him.
