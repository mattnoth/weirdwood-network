# AGOT — Daenerys V

## Chapter Metadata

- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys eats a raw stallion's heart in a Dothraki ceremony before the dosh khaleen, who prophesy that her son will be "the stallion who mounts the world." She names the child Rhaego after her brother Rhaegar. After ritual bathing in the Womb of the World and public consummation with Drogo, a great feast follows. Viserys arrives drunk and armed with a sword — forbidden in Vaes Dothrak — and threatens Daenerys and her unborn child, demanding the crown he was promised. Khal Drogo kills Viserys by pouring molten gold over his head.

## Events & Actions
1. **Stallion heart ceremony** — Khal Drogo presents a raw wild stallion's heart to Daenerys. His bloodriders kneel beside the butchered stallion with stone knives. Dany eats the entire heart with teeth and nails (no steel permitted) while the dosh khaleen and assembled Dothraki watch.
2. **Prophecy proclaimed** — Dany announces "Khalakka dothrae mr'anha" (a prince rides inside me). The eldest crone of the dosh khaleen closes her single eye, peers into the smoke, and prophesies that Dany's son will be "the stallion who mounts the world" — the khal of khals who will unite all Dothraki.
3. **Child named** — The crone asks what the child shall be called. Dany names him Rhaego, after her brother Rhaegar. The crowd roars the name.
4. **Procession along the godsway** — Drogo leads Dany from the pit along the godsway, followed by the dosh khaleen, visiting khals, bloodriders, handmaids, servants, and slaves with torches. They pass stolen hero statues and gods of conquered peoples.
5. **Drogo asks about the name** — Drogo asks Dany the meaning of "Rhaego" in broken Common Tongue. She explains Rhaegar was a fierce warrior; Drogo approves the name.
6. **Ritual bathing in the Womb of the World** — Dany strips naked and wades into the sacred lake, cleansing herself and the child while the procession watches from the grassy shore.
7. **Public consummation** — After Dany emerges from the lake, Drogo waves away Doreah's robe, lifts Dany, and has sex with her publicly. He whispers "the stallion who mounts the world" and bites her throat.
8. **Ride to the feast hall** — Cohollo helps Dany onto her silver mare. They ride down the godsway to Drogo's hall, where the silk roof has been rolled up.
9. **Feast begins** — The hall is crowded with five thousand men. Women dance on tables. Music plays. Dany searches for Viserys but cannot find him.
10. **Conversation with Ser Jorah** — Dany summons Jorah from his place of respect. He reports Viserys went to the Western Market for wine and to recruit sellswords from caravan guards. He reveals Viserys planned to steal Dany's dragon eggs until Jorah threatened him. Jorah explains the "stallion who mounts the world" prophecy.
11. **Viserys arrives armed and drunk** — Viserys enters the hall wearing a longsword (forbidden in Vaes Dothrak), drunk, demanding his place at the feast and calling Dany a whore. The music dies.
12. **Failed intervention** — Dany sends Jorah to stop Viserys, offering the dragon eggs. Jorah whispers to Viserys and takes his arm, but Viserys wrenches free.
13. **Drogo humiliates Viserys** — Drogo directs Viserys to sit with the lowest of the low at the back of the hall. He calls him "Sorefoot King" and orders a cart brought for "Khal Rhaggat."
14. **Viserys draws sword** — After Jorah and Viserys grapple and Jorah knocks him to the floor, Viserys draws his sword. He threatens Daenerys and her unborn child, pressing the blade against her belly and navel, demanding the crown he was promised. He threatens to cut out the baby.
15. **Dany translates for Drogo** — Jhiqui refuses to translate out of terror. Dany tells Drogo what Viserys said. Drogo understands.
16. **Viserys is seized** — Drogo steps down from the high bench. His three bloodriders seize Viserys: Qotho holds his arms, Haggo shatters his wrist, Cohollo takes the sword.
17. **The golden crown** — Drogo removes his gold medallion belt, has cook slaves empty a stew pot and return it to the fire. He melts the gold, dons horsehair mittens, snatches the pot from the flames, and pours the molten gold over Viserys's head, killing him. No blood is shed.
18. **Dany watches without flinching** — Ser Jorah urges Dany to turn away. She refuses. Viserys pleads with her. After his death, she thinks "He was no dragon. Fire cannot kill a dragon."

- **Emotional state:** Determined and steeled during the heart-eating ceremony; proud after completing it; content during the procession and bathing; shock and dismay upon learning of Viserys's egg theft plan; lingering loyalty and affection toward Viserys despite his failings; dread when Viserys arrives armed; terror when he threatens her child; cold resolution when Drogo moves to act; eerie calm after Viserys's death
