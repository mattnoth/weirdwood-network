- **time_markers:** Arrival takes place during daylight (long shadows from the bronze stallions); Drogo must ascend the Mother of Mountains "this night" and return at dawn; Dany plans supper for the evening; she falls asleep after the confrontation with Viserys
- **chapter_summary:** Khal Drogo's khalasar arrives at Vaes Dothrak through the Horse Gate. Along the godsway, Dany observes plundered statues while Viserys complains about the Dothraki and demands his promised army. Ser Jorah and Dany discuss whether the Dothraki could conquer the Seven Kingdoms, with Jorah analyzing their military strengths and weaknesses. After settling into their quarters near the Eastern Market, Dany prepares gifts of Dothraki-style clothing for Viserys and invites him to supper, but he reacts with fury, hitting Doreah and grabbing Dany's arm. Dany strikes him across the face with a bronze medallion belt, driving him away. She refuses supper and falls asleep cradling one of her dragon eggs, feeling the child move inside her.

## Events & Actions
1. **Khalasar arrives at Vaes Dothrak** — Khal Drogo leads the khalasar through the Horse Gate, two gigantic bronze stallions forming a hundred-foot arch, and down the godsway.
2. **Dany observes the plundered monuments** — Along the godsway, Dany sees stolen gods, stone kings, marble maidens, iron dragons, griffins, manticores, and other statues from sacked cities lining both sides of the road.
3. **Viserys disparages the Dothraki** — Speaking in the Common Tongue to avoid being understood, Viserys calls the Dothraki savages and their treasures "trash of dead cities," demanding to know when Drogo will give him his army.
4. **Dany defends the Dothraki** — Dany tells Viserys they are her people now and he should not call them savages.
5. **Ser Jorah explains Dothraki gift-giving customs** — Jorah tells Dany that Drogo views the marriage as a gift exchange, not a transaction, and that one does not demand anything of a khal.
6. **Military discussion between Dany and Ser Jorah** — Jorah analyzes the Dothraki military capability: superior riders, fearless, outranging Westerosi bows, but unable to take castles. He says Robert Baratheon is rash enough to meet them in open field, but his advisors (Stannis, Tywin, Eddard Stark) would counsel otherwise.
7. **Ser Jorah reveals personal bitterness toward Eddard Stark** — Jorah says Stark took from him all he loved "for the sake of a few lice-ridden poachers and his precious honor."
8. **Khalasar enters Vaes Dothrak proper** — They pass through the Western Market bazaar and down broad ways. Dany observes the strange, sprawling, largely empty city and its diverse architecture.
9. **Drogo halts near the Eastern Market** — The khalasar stops near the Eastern Market, beneath the Mother of Mountains. Dany notes the contrast between Illyrio's slave girl's description of a grand palace and the actual wooden feasting hall.
10. **Weapons surrendered** — All riders, including Khal Drogo, unbelt their arakhs and hand them to waiting slaves, as it is forbidden to carry blades in Vaes Dothrak.
11. **Cohollo delivers Drogo's message** — Cohollo informs Dany that Drogo must ascend the Mother of Mountains that night to sacrifice to the gods for his safe return. Only men are allowed on the mountain.
12. **Dany reflects on bloodriders** — Dany thinks about the bloodrider tradition: they are more than guards, they are the khal's brothers, bound to die with him. She notes that Qotho bruises Doreah and makes Irri cry, and that Haggo often glowers.
13. **Dany reflects on Kingsguard treachery** — She wishes her father had bloodriders and thinks about the Kingslayer murdering Aerys and Barristan Selmy going over to Robert.
14. **Dany bathes** — She takes a scalding hot bath in the earthen dwelling, washing off travel dust.
15. **Dany prepares gifts for Viserys** — She lays out Dothraki-style clothing made to his measurements: white linen tunic and leggings, leather sandals, bronze medallion belt, leather vest painted with dragons, and a green sandsilk cloak with pale grey border.
16. **Viserys arrives dragging Doreah** — He has hit Doreah (her eye is red) and is furious that Doreah told him Dany "commanded" him to come to supper.
17. **Viserys rejects the gifts** — He calls them "Dothraki rags," sneers at the cloak, and refuses to dress like the Dothraki.
18. **Dany tells Viserys he has no right to a braid** — She says he has won no victories yet, enraging him further.
19. **Viserys grabs Dany's arm** — He threatens her, saying her "big belly" won't protect her if she wakes the dragon.
20. **Dany strikes Viserys with the medallion belt** — She swings the heavy bronze chain and catches him full in the face, cutting his cheek open.
21. **Dany threatens Viserys** — She tells him to leave before she summons her khas, and warns him to pray Drogo doesn't hear of this or he'll cut Viserys open and feed him his own entrails.
22. **Viserys retreats** — He leaves holding his torn face, threatening future retribution, leaving the gifts behind.
23. **Dany declines supper** — She tells handmaids to share the food and send some to Ser Jorah.
24. **Dany cradles the dragon egg** — She asks for the green dragon egg and curls up with it between her belly and breasts, feeling strength from it.
25. **The baby moves** — Dany feels the child move as if reaching toward the egg, "brother to brother, blood to blood." She whispers "You are the dragon, the true dragon" and falls asleep dreaming of home.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Complex and shifting — anxious about Viserys's behavior and Drogo's patience; relieved when Viserys rides away; hopeful and tender when preparing gifts; shocked and then fierce when Viserys attacks; sad and tired after the confrontation; peaceful and strengthened when holding the dragon egg
- **Primary preoccupation:** Managing the tension between Viserys's demands and Dothraki customs; her growing identity as khaleesi vs. her role as Viserys's sister; her unborn child's future
- **Key decisions made:** Defends the Dothraki as "my people"; asks Jorah hypothetically about a non-Viserys leader commanding the Dothraki; strikes Viserys with the belt when he grabs her; threatens him with Drogo's wrath and her khas; chooses the dragon egg for comfort over food
- **Self-deception flags:** Says "It is not right to make him wait" about Viserys's army — admits she doesn't know why she's defending him; still thinks of Viserys as "her king" and "her brother" even while preparing to manage him; her question about "someone else" leading the Dothraki suggests she's already thinking beyond Viserys as leader but doesn't consciously acknowledge it
