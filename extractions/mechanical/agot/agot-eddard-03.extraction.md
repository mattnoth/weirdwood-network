- **chapter_summary:** Arya is found after four days missing and brought before King Robert for judgment over her altercation with Prince Joffrey. Arya and Joffrey give conflicting accounts; Sansa claims she cannot remember. When the matter of the direwolf arises, Cersei demands Lady's death in place of the missing Nymeria, and Robert acquiesces. Ned insists on performing the killing himself with Ice. As Ned returns afterward, Sandor Clegane arrives with the body of Mycah the butcher's boy, whom he has killed.

## Events & Actions
1. **Vayon Poole reports Arya found** — Ned's steward tells him Jory found Arya, but Lannister guards at the gate informed the queen, and Arya is being taken directly before the king.
2. **Ned descends in fury** — He sends Poole to fetch Sansa and crosses the castle yard to the audience chamber, maintaining dignity despite rage.
3. **Ned finds the hearing already underway** — Robert sits in Darry's high seat; Cersei and Joffrey stand beside him; the room is crowded with Lannister men and few friendly faces.
4. **Ned reunites with Arya** — She is standing alone with Jory, sobbing and apologizing; she is dirty, hungry, and shaking.
5. **Ned demands explanation** — He asks why he was not informed and why Arya was not brought to him; Cersei answers for Robert.
6. **Robert takes control** — He tells Cersei to be quiet, apologizes to Ned, and orders Arya to tell her story, then Joffrey to tell his.
7. **Arya tells her version** — She recounts the incident, including throwing Lion's Tooth into the Trident.
8. **Renly laughs and is dismissed** — He mocks Joffrey for being disarmed by a nine-year-old girl with a broomstick; Robert orders Barristan to escort him out; Renly leaves voluntarily, still laughing.
9. **Joffrey gives his version** — He is pale; his story contradicts Arya's.
10. **Ned calls Sansa to testify** — Sansa claims she doesn't know and doesn't remember; Arya attacks Sansa physically, calling her a liar; Jory separates them.
11. **Robert dismisses the matter** — He tells Ned to discipline Arya, he will discipline Joffrey; Ned is relieved.
12. **Cersei raises the direwolf issue** — She demands the "beast" be dealt with; Robert says the wolf was not found; Cersei offers a bounty of a hundred golden dragons.
13. **Cersei maneuvers for Lady's death** — She points out they have Lady (Sansa's wolf); Robert shrugs and orders Ser Ilyn Payne to do it.
14. **Ned pleads with Robert** — He appeals to their friendship and Robert's love for Ned's sister; Robert curses Cersei but does not relent.
15. **Ned challenges Robert** — He tells Robert to do it himself; Robert leaves with heavy footsteps and dead eyes.
16. **Ned claims the execution** — He refuses to let Ilyn Payne do it; he asks Jory to take the girls away and bring Ice; he tells Cersei Lady "deserves better than a butcher."
17. **Ned kills Lady** — He sits with her, noting she is the smallest, prettiest, most gentle and trusting of the litter; he performs the killing with Ice.
18. **Ned orders Lady's body sent north** — He tells Jory to have four men take the body to Winterfell for burial; he refuses to let the Lannisters have the skin.
19. **Sandor Clegane returns with Mycah's body** — The Hound rides in with a shape wrapped in a bloody cloak; it is Mycah the butcher's boy, cut almost in half from shoulder to waist; Clegane says "He ran" and laughs.

- **Self-deception flags:** None evident — Ned appears to see the situation clearly; he recognizes Robert's weakness before Cersei even as he appeals to their bond
