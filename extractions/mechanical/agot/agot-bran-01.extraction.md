# AGOT — Bran I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 2
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran I
- **first_available:** AGOT Bran I
- **location_primary:** A holdfast in the hills north of Winterfell; riverbank north of the bridge on the road back
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell (destination, referenced); The Wall (referenced in dialogue)
- **approximate_timeline:** Ninth year of summer; Bran is seven years old. The party rides out at daybreak and returns toward Winterfell by midday.
- **chapter_summary:** Bran accompanies his father Eddard and brothers Robb and Jon to witness the execution of a Night's Watch deserter at a holdfast in the hills. Eddard beheads the man with the greatsword Ice, then explains to Bran why the man who passes sentence must swing the sword. On the ride back, Robb discovers a dead direwolf with five live pups beside her. Jon argues that the five pups match the five trueborn Stark children, deliberately omitting himself from the count. Eddard agrees to let the children keep the pups. As the party crosses a bridge, Jon hears a sixth pup — white-furred with red eyes — separated from the litter, and claims it as his own.

## Events & Actions
1. **Execution party rides out at daybreak** — Twenty riders including Eddard, Bran, Robb, Jon, Theon, Jory, Hullen, Harwin, and Desmond ride to a holdfast in the hills. It is Bran's first time attending the king's justice.
2. **Deserter questioned and sentenced** — The Night's Watch deserter is cut down from the holdfast wall, questioned, and formally sentenced to death by Eddard in Robert Baratheon's name.
3. **Eddard beheads the deserter with Ice** — Theon Greyjoy presents the greatsword Ice. Eddard beheads the man with a single stroke. Blood sprays across the snow. Theon laughs and kicks the severed head away; Jon mutters "Ass" under his breath.
4. **Jon counsels Bran** — Just before the blow falls, Jon whispers to Bran to keep the pony in hand and not look away, warning that their father will know. Bran complies and does not look away.
5. **Bran praised by Jon** — After the execution, Jon tells Bran solemnly that he did well, and puts a hand on Bran's shoulder.
6. **Robb and Jon debate the deserter's courage** — On the ride back, Robb says the deserter died bravely; Jon says the man was dead of fear. Robb challenges Jon to a race and they gallop ahead, leaving Bran behind on his slower pony.
7. **Eddard rides alongside Bran and explains justice** — Eddard explains that the man who passes sentence must swing the sword, and describes what he expects of Bran as a future lord.
8. **Jon signals a discovery from a hilltop** — Jon reappears and calls for Eddard and Bran to come see what Robb has found.
9. **Dead direwolf and five pups discovered on the riverbank** — Robb finds a massive dead direwolf half-buried in bloodstained snow. Jory and Theon react with alarm and draw their swords.
10. **Antler extracted from the direwolf's throat** — Eddard kneels and pulls a shattered stag antler from under the carcass, identifying it as the cause of death. The party falls silent; the men react with unease.
11. **Debate over the pups' fate** — Hullen, Harwin, and Theon argue the pups should be killed mercifully. Bran and Robb resist. Theon draws his sword.
12. **Jon argues for keeping the pups** — Jon counts five pups matching five trueborn Stark children in number and sex, deliberately excluding himself. Eddard recognizes the omission.
13. **Eddard grants permission to keep the pups** — Eddard agrees, with conditions: the children must feed and train the pups themselves; the kennelmaster will not be involved.
14. **Jon discovers the albino sixth pup** — Halfway across the bridge, Jon hears something, rides back, and finds a sixth pup — white-furred with red eyes — separated from the litter. Jon claims it for himself.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Nervous excitement at the chapter's opening; determined to prove maturity at the execution; troubled and reflective on the ride back (he cannot stop thinking about the deserter's eyes); delight and fierce protectiveness upon finding the direwolf pups; admiration toward Jon at the chapter's climax.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Proving he is old enough to witness justice; processing what he witnessed; protecting the pup he has claimed as his own.
- **Key decisions made:** Does not look away during the beheading, following Jon's whispered instruction. Cries out "It's mine!" to claim the pup before Theon can kill it. Echoes Robb's vow to nurse the pup himself.
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran "tried to seem older than seven" and "tried to pretend that he'd seen all this before" — narration makes clear he has done neither. He does not understand why the antler frightens the men.
