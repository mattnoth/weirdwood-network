- **book:** AGOT

- **approximate_timeline:** Weeks to months after Daenerys's wedding; the khalasar has been traveling east toward Vaes Dothrak, crossing Norvos and the Forest of Qohor; Daenerys is now fourteen years old (her fourteenth name day at chapter's end) and pregnant
- **time_markers:** "by the third day" (of riding after the wedding); "Day followed day, and night followed night"; "For half a moon, they rode through the Forest of Qohor"; "some time before the dawn" (Drogo's nightly visits); "the setting sun red on her face"; "by the time she reached the khalasar, it was dusk"; "Long after the moon had risen"; chapter ends with Dany's fourteenth name day
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys and Ser Jorah survey the Dothraki sea from a ridge. Dany recalls her painful early days of riding and her gradual physical and emotional transformation, including a pivotal dragon dream. When Viserys confronts her violently on the plains, her khas rider Jhogo stops him with a whip, and Dany commands that Viserys walk back to the khalasar on foot — a devastating Dothraki humiliation. In conversation with Ser Jorah, Dany acknowledges that Viserys will never reclaim the Seven Kingdoms. That night she takes sexual initiative with Drogo for the first time. The chapter ends with the revelation that Dany is pregnant, on her fourteenth name day.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ridge survey** — Dany and Ser Jorah reach the top of a ridge overlooking the Dothraki sea. Jorah describes the different seasonal appearances of the grass and tells of ghost grass in the Shadow Lands beyond Asshai.
2. **Dany commands the party to wait** — She orders Ser Jorah to tell the rest of the party to stay on the ridge while she rides alone. Jorah notes she is "learning to talk like a queen."
3. **Solo ride down the ridge** — Dany gallops down the steep, rocky descent alone, feeling joy and freedom.
4. **Recalled: early days of riding** — Dany remembers the agonizing first days after her wedding, with saddle sores, blisters, chafing, and pain. She was isolated from Drogo during the day and subjected to painful sex each night.
5. **Recalled: the dragon dream** — At her lowest point, Dany dreamt of a black dragon with scales slick with her blood and eyes of molten magma. She embraced its fire and felt cleansed and renewed. She woke transformed — the pain began to ease.
6. **Recalled: touching the dragon egg** — After the dream, Dany touched the largest (black-and-scarlet) dragon egg and found it strangely warm.
7. **Recalled: physical transformation** — Dany's legs grew stronger, blisters burst, hands callused, thighs toughened. She grew skilled at riding.
8. **Recalled: the journey** — The khalasar crossed the hills of Norvos, forded rivers, camped by a waterfall, skirted dead city ruins, raced down Valyrian roads, rode through the Forest of Qohor for half a moon.
9. **Dany rides alone on the plain** — She dismounts, removes her boots, feels the grass and soil beneath her feet, savoring solitude.
10. **Viserys confronts Dany** — Viserys rides up in fury, screams at her for giving commands, grabs and shakes her, digs his fingers painfully into her breast. Dany shoves him away hard — the first time she has ever defied him.
11. **Jhogo whips Viserys** — Jhogo's whip cracks around Viserys's throat and yanks him down. The Dothraki riders hoot. Jhogo asks if Dany wants him killed. Quaro suggests taking an ear.
12. **Dany orders Viserys to walk** — Dany commands that Viserys's horse be taken and he walk back to the khalasar, the ultimate Dothraki humiliation. Viserys pleads with Ser Jorah to hit Dany and kill the Dothraki, but Jorah sides with Dany.
13. **Conversation with Ser Jorah** — As they ride back, Jorah calls Rhaegar "the last dragon" and Viserys "less than the shadow of a snake." Dany wrestles with doubt about Viserys's claim. Jorah says the common people don't care who rules. Jorah says he prays for home.
14. **Dany's realization** — She declares aloud that Viserys will never take back the Seven Kingdoms, acknowledging she has always known this.
15. **Arrival at camp** — Dany reaches the khalasar at dusk. The camp is by a spring-fed pool.
16. **The dragon eggs glow** — In her tent, red light touches the dragon eggs and Dany sees "a thousand droplets of scarlet flame." She touches the black egg — it is warm, almost hot. She attributes it to the sun.
17. **Bath scene** — Dany bathes in a copper tub (a bride gift). She asks her handmaids about dragons. Irri and Jhiqui say dragons are gone. Doreah tells the Qartheen story of the moon-egg and dragons.
18. **Grooming and preparation** — Jhiqui oils Dany and scrapes her pores; Irri sprinkles her with spiceflower and cinnamon; Doreah brushes her hair.
19. **Supper with Doreah** — Dany eats a simple meal with Doreah and they talk long after moonrise. (Implied: Doreah teaches Dany about lovemaking.)
20. **Dany takes initiative with Drogo** — When Drogo comes to her tent, Dany leads him outside under the open sky. She refuses to be taken from behind, insisting on facing him. She mounts him. Drogo calls out her name.
21. **Pregnancy revealed** — Some time later, on the far side of the Dothraki sea, Jhiqui notices Dany's swelling stomach and says she is with child. Dany says she already knows. It is her fourteenth name day.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Transformation arc across the chapter — from peace and freedom on the ridge, through recalled agony and despair (early days of the journey, contemplating suicide), to renewed strength after the dragon dream; fear when confronting Viserys quickly replaced by a "hollow place inside her where her fear had been"; wonder at her own defiance ("I hit him"); dawning clarity about Viserys's weakness; fierce self-assertion ("I am no child"); contentment and empowerment by chapter's end
- **Primary preoccupation:** Her identity as khaleesi rather than princess; her evolving relationship with Dothraki culture and her own agency; the growing irrelevance of Viserys; the dragon eggs and what they might mean; her feelings about "home" (red doors, lights in windows)
- **Key decisions made:** Commands Viserys to walk back to camp on foot (first assertion of authority over him); acknowledges aloud that Viserys will never reclaim the Seven Kingdoms; takes sexual initiative with Drogo for the first time, insisting on facing him
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself the eggs are warm from the sun, despite sensing otherwise; says "I pray for home too" while believing it, but her vision of home (King's Landing, Dragonstone) is imagined, never experienced — she has no true memory of these places; initially still tries to maintain that Viserys is "the true king" before Jorah's questioning forces her to confront the truth
