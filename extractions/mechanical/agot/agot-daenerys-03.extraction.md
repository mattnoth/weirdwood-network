- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys and Ser Jorah pause atop a ridge overlooking the Dothraki sea. In extended flashback, Dany recalls the painful early days of riding and her nightly rapes by Drogo, a transformative dragon dream, and her gradual physical adaptation and growing confidence. In the present, Viserys confronts Dany violently for commanding him to stay behind, but Jhogo whips him and Dany orders his horse taken away, forcing him to walk back to the khalasar. In a pivotal conversation with Ser Jorah, Dany acknowledges aloud that Viserys will never reclaim the Seven Kingdoms. Returning to camp, she examines her dragon eggs and finds them warm. After bathing and hearing Doreah's origin story of dragons from a cracked moon, Dany has Doreah instruct her in lovemaking. She takes charge with Drogo that night. The chapter ends with the revelation that Dany is pregnant, on her fourteenth name day.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dany and Ser Jorah survey the Dothraki sea** — They halt atop a ridge and look out over the vast grasslands. Jorah describes the seasonal changes and ghost grass of the Shadow Lands.
2. **Dany commands the party to wait** — She tells Ser Jorah to hold everyone on the ridge and rides down alone, asserting her authority as khaleesi.
3. **Extended flashback: early days of the journey** — Dany recalls the agony of learning to ride, saddle sores, blisters, and the pain of Drogo's nightly sexual assaults during the first weeks after the wedding.
4. **The dragon dream** — Dany dreams of a black dragon with scales wet with her blood, eyes of molten magma. She embraces its fire and feels reborn — strong, new, and fierce. There is no pain.
5. **Dany's physical transformation begins** — After the dream, her pain eases. She grows stronger, her hands callus, her thighs toughen. She begins to master riding.
6. **Dany touches the dragon eggs** — She runs her hand over the largest (black-and-scarlet) egg and finds it strangely warm. She notes it matches the dragon in her dream.
7. **The journey across Essos** — The khalasar crosses the hills of Norvos, fords four rivers (three wide and placid, one swift and narrow and treacherous), camps beside a high blue waterfall, skirts a vast dead city with blackened marble columns, races down thousand-year-old Valyrian roads, and spends half a moon in the Forest of Qohor.
8. **Dany begins to find pleasure in her nights** — Her relationship with Drogo gradually shifts; she no longer always cries out in pain.
9. **Dany dismounts on the plain** — She rides into the tall grass alone, dismounts, removes her boots to feel the thick black soil beneath her feet.
10. **Viserys attacks Dany** — He rides up in fury at being commanded, grabs and shakes her, reaches under her vest and digs his fingers painfully into her breast.
11. **Dany shoves Viserys away** — For the first time, she physically defies her brother. She sees his lilac eyes incredulous with disbelief.
12. **Jhogo whips Viserys** — The young Dothraki archer's whip coils around Viserys's throat and yanks him to the ground. Quaro suggests taking an ear. The Dothraki riders hoot at Viserys.
13. **Dany orders Viserys's horse taken** — She commands Ser Jorah to take his horse and make Viserys walk back to the khalasar, the ultimate Dothraki humiliation. "Among the Dothraki, the man who does not ride was no man at all, the lowest of the low, without honor or pride."
14. **Ser Jorah sides with Dany over Viserys** — When Viserys pleads with Jorah to attack the Dothraki and punish Dany (in the Common Tongue so the Dothraki cannot understand), Jorah chooses Dany. "He shall walk, Khaleesi."
15. **Viserys is left in the grass** — He sits in the dirt and refuses to move as the others ride away. He is soon lost in the tall grass.
16. **Jorah declares Rhaegar was the last dragon** — He calls Viserys "less than the shadow of a snake" and bitterly questions his own service to Viserys.
17. **Dany and Jorah discuss the common people** — Jorah says the smallfolk pray for rain, health, and summer, not for Targaryen restoration. Dany recognizes the truth in this, which goes against everything Viserys told her.
18. **Jorah reveals he prays for home** — His voice is "thick with longing."
19. **Dany envisions King's Landing and Dragonstone** — She sees them in her mind's eye burning with a thousand lights, all the doors red.
20. **Dany acknowledges Viserys will never reclaim the Seven Kingdoms** — She says it aloud for the first time: "My brother will never take back the Seven Kingdoms." She realizes she has known this all her life but never let herself say the words.
21. **Dany gallops back to camp** — She rides fiercely, leaving Jorah and the others far behind, arriving at the khalasar at dusk.
22. **Dany examines the dragon eggs again** — In her tent, a finger of dusty red light touches the eggs. She sees a thousand droplets of scarlet flame, then blinks and they are gone. The black egg is warm, almost hot. She tells herself they are only stone and rationalizes the warmth as sunlight.
23. **Dany bathes** — Her handmaids prepare a copper tub bath by the spring-fed pool. Doreah builds a fire outside the tent, Irri and Jhiqui fetch the tub from packhorses and carry water. Irri scrubs her back, Jhiqui sluices sand from her hair, then oils her body. She is sprinkled with spiceflower and cinnamon. Doreah brushes her hair until it shines.
24. **Discussion of dragons** — Irri and Jhiqui say dragons are gone and were "terrible evil beasts. It is known." Doreah tells the origin myth of dragons hatching from a cracked moon. Irri dismisses her: "Moon is no egg. Moon is god, woman wife of sun. It is known."
25. **Dany reflects on magic and dragons** — She recalls that magic died in the west when the Doom fell on Valyria, and thinks about manticores, basilisks, spellsingers, warlocks, aeromancers, shadowbinders, and bloodmages still said to practice in the east.
26. **Dany dines with Doreah** — A simple supper of fruit, cheese, fry bread, and honeyed wine. They talk long after the moon rises (implied: Doreah instructs Dany in lovemaking).
27. **Dany takes charge with Drogo** — She waits for Drogo, leads him outside under the open sky (for the Dothraki believe all important things must be done beneath the open sky), and for the first time rides him face-to-face, using what Doreah taught her. Drogo calls out her name.
28. **Dany is pregnant** — Jhiqui announces Dany is with child by touching the soft swell of her stomach. Dany already knows. It is her fourteenth name day.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Transformative arc across the chapter — from peaceful contentment on the ridge, through recalled despair and suicidal thoughts during the early journey ("She would kill herself rather than go on"), to empowerment after the dragon dream, fierce defiance when shoving Viserys, wonder at her own boldness ("I hit him"), growing clarity about Viserys's inadequacy, and finally confident authority in her tent and with Drogo. Fear briefly surfaces when Viserys is left behind and she worries he will be too proud to return. A "hollow place inside her where her fear had been" after seeing Viserys humiliated.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Her shifting identity — from Viserys's frightened sister to khaleesi in her own right. She is realizing she belongs to the Dothraki world, not Viserys's fantasy of Targaryen restoration. The dragon eggs and the dragon dream are a secondary but growing preoccupation.
- **Key decisions made:** Commands her party to wait (asserting authority); shoves Viserys physically for the first time; orders his horse taken away (Dothraki humiliation); acknowledges aloud that Viserys will never reclaim the throne; takes sexual initiative with Drogo.
- **Self-deception flags:** Rationalizes the warmth of the dragon eggs as sun heat ("The sun warmed them as they rode"); tells herself "they are only stone, even Illyrio said so, the dragons are all dead" despite physical evidence and her own recurring dragon dreams. She tells Jorah "I pray for home too, believing it" — but the narration immediately reveals her "home" is an imagined King's Landing and Dragonstone she has never known, not a real memory.
