- **chapter_summary:** Arya struggles with needlework under Septa Mordane's instruction alongside Sansa, Jeyne Poole, Beth Cassel, and Princess Myrcella. After the septa criticizes her stitches, Arya flees the sewing room with her direwolf Nymeria. She finds Jon Snow watching the younger boys — Bran and Prince Tommen — spar in the practice yard. They observe Joffrey refuse to spar with Robb using tourney swords, demanding live steel, with Sandor Clegane backing him. After Joffrey departs with insults, Jon advises Arya to return to her room, where Septa Mordane and her mother Catelyn are waiting for her.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya struggles with needlework** — Arya's stitches are crooked; she compares her work unfavorably to Sansa's exquisite needlework while Septa Mordane fawns over Princess Myrcella.
2. **Girls discuss Prince Joffrey** — Sansa, Jeyne Poole, and Beth Cassel whisper about Joffrey, who told Sansa she was beautiful at the feast. Beth says Joffrey will marry Sansa and she'll be queen.
3. **Arya quotes Jon about Joffrey** — Arya says Jon thinks Joffrey "looks like a girl." Sansa calls Jon a jealous bastard; Arya loudly insists he is their brother.
4. **Septa Mordane inspects Arya's work** — The septa notices the disturbance, crosses the room, and publicly criticizes Arya's stitches: "This will not do at all."
5. **Arya flees the sewing room** — Humiliated, with tears running down her cheeks, Arya makes a sarcastic excuse ("I have to go shoe a horse"), gives a stiff bow to Myrcella, and runs down the stairs.
6. **Arya reflects on Sansa** — Running through the castle, Arya catalogues Sansa's superiorities (sewing, dancing, singing, beauty, Tully looks) and her own: riding horses and managing a household.
7. **Arya retrieves Nymeria** — Arya finds her direwolf waiting in the guardroom at the base of the tower stairs, unties her, and they head off together.
8. **Arya joins Jon at the window** — Arya and Nymeria find Jon and Ghost at a window in the covered bridge overlooking the practice yard. Nymeria and Ghost greet each other cautiously.
9. **Bran and Tommen spar** — The younger boys fight with padded swords under Ser Rodrik's supervision. Bran defeats Tommen, knocking him down. Spectators including Robb and Theon Greyjoy watch and cheer.
10. **Jon and Arya bond** — Jon messes up Arya's hair; they reflect on their shared Stark looks versus the Tully coloring of the other siblings. Jon explains bastards are not allowed to spar with princes.
11. **Joffrey refuses to spar with Robb** — Ser Rodrik calls Joffrey and Robb for a round. Joffrey calls it a children's game and demands live steel. Ser Rodrik insists on tourney swords with blunted edges.
12. **Sandor Clegane confronts Ser Rodrik** — The burned man challenges Ser Rodrik's authority to deny the prince live steel, asking if he is "training women." He claims he killed a man at twelve.
13. **Robb is provoked and restrained** — Joffrey declines and insults Robb ("Come and see me when you're older, Stark"). Robb curses; Theon Greyjoy physically restrains him from going after the prince.
14. **Jon reacts to the confrontation** — Jon's face goes still "as the pool at the heart of the godswood." He tells Arya to go back to her room before the penance gets worse.
15. **Arya returns to her room** — She finds not just Septa Mordane but also her mother waiting for her.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Frustration, humiliation, resentment, defiance, and affection (for Jon and Nymeria). Arya feels inadequate compared to Sansa, resentful of the unfairness of gendered expectations, and humiliated by the septa's public criticism. She finds comfort with Nymeria ("the wolf pup loved her, even if no one else did") and with Jon.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The unfairness of her situation — Sansa excels at everything expected of girls, while Arya's talents (riding, household management) go unvalued. The refrain "it wasn't fair" / "life was not fair" recurs throughout.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to flee the sewing room rather than endure further humiliation; decides to go watch the boys practice instead of hiding in her room.
- **Self-deception flags:** "The wolf pup loved her, even if no one else did" — Arya catastrophizes her social standing; Jon clearly loves her, her family cares for her, but she feels isolated in the moment.
