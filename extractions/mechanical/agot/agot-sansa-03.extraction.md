- **chapter_summary:** Sansa and Jeyne Poole share supper discussing the day's court events — Ned's decision to send Lord Beric instead of Ser Loras against Gregor Clegane. Sansa recalls Littlefinger's unsettling words about life not being a song. The next morning, Sansa watches Lord Beric's force ride out at dawn. At breakfast, Sansa and Arya quarrel about Joffrey, the Hound, and past events, culminating in Arya throwing a blood orange at Sansa and ruining her ivory silk dress. Ned summons both daughters at midday and tells them he is sending them back to Winterfell by ship for their safety. Sansa's inadvertent comment about Joffrey's son having golden hair triggers a realization in Ned.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa and Jeyne Poole share cold supper** — They discuss the day's court events by lamplight in the Tower of the Hand.
2. **Sansa recalls Ned's refusal to send Ser Loras** — She is bewildered that her father chose Lord Beric over the Knight of Flowers to pursue Gregor Clegane.
3. **Sansa recalls Littlefinger's encounter on the stairs** — Baelish overheard Sansa questioning her father's decision, touched her cheek, and told her "Life is not a song, sweetling."
4. **Sansa and Jeyne discuss Joffrey and the white hart** — Sansa shares her "dream" of Joffrey killing the white hart with a golden arrow.
5. **Jeyne mentions seeing Arya walking on her hands in the stables** — Sansa dismisses it.
6. **Sansa recounts court events** — A black brother (Yoren) begged men for the Wall; two freeriders from the Dornish Marches pledged service.
7. **Sansa and Jeyne search for lemon cakes** — Find none, but eat half a cold strawberry pie on the tower steps.
8. **Sansa watches Lord Beric's force ride out at dawn** — Three banners (king's stag, Stark direwolf, Beric's forked lightning); Alyn carries the Stark banner.
9. **Sansa and Arya quarrel at breakfast** — Dispute over Joffrey, the Hound, Mycah, and Jaime Lannister's crimes.
10. **Arya throws blood orange at Sansa** — Hits her in the forehead; ruins her ivory silk dress.
11. **Sansa screams at Arya** — "They should have killed you instead of Lady!"
12. **Septa Mordane sends both girls to their rooms** — Sansa retreats, strips off ruined dress, throws it in the hearth, cries herself to sleep.
13. **Sansa dreams of Lady** — She dreams of running with her direwolf; wakes at midday and the dream fades.
14. **Ned summons both daughters to his solar** — He is studying a large leather-bound book.
15. **Sansa pleads her case against Arya** — Cites the ruined dress Cersei gave her.
16. **Arya apologizes** — Offers to wash or make a new dress; Sansa dismisses her.
17. **Ned announces he is sending both girls back to Winterfell** — By trading galley, for their safety; three of his men were killed and Robert does nothing.
18. **Sansa pleads to stay** — Declares her love for Joffrey, comparing it to Naerys and Aemon, Jonquil and Florian.
19. **Sansa says she will give Joffrey a son with golden hair** — Ned has a sudden realization ("Gods… out of the mouth of babes").
20. **Ned arranges departure details** — Fast trading galley, Septa Mordane, guards, Syrio Forel if he agrees; tells them to keep it secret.
21. **Sansa weeps as she is marched out** — Insults Arya by comparing her to Hodor; storms into her bedchamber.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bewildered by her father's decisions, romantically idealistic about Joffrey, increasingly distressed and angry during quarrel with Arya, devastated by news of returning to Winterfell, grieving Lady
- **Primary preoccupation:** Her betrothal to Joffrey and the courtly life she fears losing; sees everything through the lens of songs and stories
- **Key decisions made:** None — Sansa is passive throughout; events happen to her
- **Self-deception flags:** Convinced Joffrey had no part in the attack on Ned's men ("that had been his wicked uncle, the Kingslayer"); believes her butcher's boy version of events ("Your butcher's boy attacked the prince"); romanticizes her relationship with Joffrey through the lens of legendary romances; her "dream" about the white hart was actually a wish but she calls it a dream because "everyone knew that dreams were prophetic"; insists Joffrey is nothing like Robert despite clear parallels
