- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Daenerys I — the chapter covers the day of Daenerys's wedding to Khal Drogo and its immediate aftermath (wedding night)
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys Targaryen marries Khal Drogo in a dawn-to-dusk Dothraki ceremony outside Pentos, attended by forty thousand Dothraki warriors plus uncounted women, children, and slaves. She receives wedding gifts including three dragon eggs from the Shadow Lands beyond Asshai, given by Magister Illyrio, as well as handmaids (Irri, Jhiqui, Doreah), books from Ser Jorah Mormont, and a silver-grey filly from Khal Drogo himself. At least a dozen men die in brawls during the ceremony, which Illyrio notes is considered auspicious. After the feast, Drogo rides out with Daenerys and consummates the marriage; Drogo is unexpectedly gentle, and Daenerys ultimately gives her consent.

## Events & Actions
1. **Pre-wedding dinner at Illyrio's manse** — Daenerys, Viserys, Illyrio, and Ser Jorah dine together. Viserys presses Illyrio on when Drogo will fulfill the promise of a crown. Illyrio says after the wedding and the procession to Vaes Dothrak, if omens favor war. Viserys is dismissed with vague assurances.
2. **Daenerys's dream** — The night before the wedding, Daenerys dreams of Viserys beating her, then of a dragon appearing amid columns of flame, its molten eyes meeting hers. She wakes shaking and afraid.
3. **Wedding ceremony begins at dawn** — Daenerys and Drogo are seated on an earthen ramp above forty thousand Dothraki. The ceremony spans from dawn to dusk with drinking, feasting, and fighting.
4. **Dothraki fighting and killing during the feast** — Warriors fight over a dancing woman; one warrior is disemboweled with an arakh. At least a dozen men die over the course of the day.
5. **Bride gifts presented at dusk** — Viserys gives three handmaids (Irri, Jhiqui, Doreah). Ser Jorah gives books of histories and songs. Illyrio gives a cedar chest with fine fabrics and three dragon eggs from the Shadow Lands. The bloodriders Haggo, Cohollo, and Qotho give traditional weapons (whip, arakh, dragonbone bow), which Daenerys formally refuses and redirects to Drogo per custom. Other Dothraki give jewels, clothing, furs, and sundry gifts.
6. **Khal Drogo gives Daenerys the silver-grey filly** — Drogo's bride gift is a young filly called "the pride of the khalasar." He lifts Daenerys onto the saddle.
7. **Daenerys rides the silver filly** — She gallops through the crowd and leaps a firepit. The Dothraki hoot and shout. Drogo smiles for the first time.
8. **Viserys threatens Daenerys** — As the sun sets, Viserys grabs Daenerys's leg and threatens her to please Drogo or "see the dragon wake as it has never woken before."
9. **Drogo and Daenerys ride out for the consummation** — They ride alone into the dark, stopping at a grassy place beside a stream.
10. **Consummation of the marriage** — Drogo is unexpectedly gentle; he undresses Daenerys slowly and touches her carefully over a long period. He asks her consent with "No?" as a question. She takes his hand and guides him, saying "Yes."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pervasive fear and dread throughout most of the chapter — fear of Drogo, fear of Viserys, fear of the Dothraki, and fear of what the wedding night holds. Brief exhilaration while riding the silver filly. At the chapter's close, some tentative sense of agency and warmth.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Surviving the day without shaming Viserys or provoking Drogo; then getting through the consummation.
- **Key decisions made:** She chooses to give her consent to Drogo rather than remain passive — she takes his hand and guides him, actively saying "Yes."
- **Self-deception flags:** Daenerys repeatedly tells herself "I am the blood of the dragon" as a mantra; she believes she must remain fearless but internally is terrified. She privately thinks "there are no more dragons" about Viserys but does not dare say it.
