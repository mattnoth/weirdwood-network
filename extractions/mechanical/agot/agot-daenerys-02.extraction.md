- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys Targaryen marries Khal Drogo in a Dothraki ceremony outside Pentos. The days leading up to the wedding include a dinner where Viserys grows impatient about his promised crown and army, and Dany has a vivid dream of a dragon. The wedding is a daylong feast marked by fighting, coupling, and at least a dozen deaths. Dany receives bride gifts including three handmaids, old books from Ser Jorah, three petrified dragon eggs from Illyrio, traditional weapons from the bloodriders, and a silver-grey horse from Drogo. She rides the horse and feels a moment of freedom. That night, Drogo rides with her to a private place where he consummates the marriage with unexpected gentleness.

## Events & Actions
1. **Pre-wedding dinner at the manse** — Illyrio, Viserys, Ser Jorah, and Dany dine together. Illyrio reports the Pentoshi magisters have doubled the city guard due to the khalasar's presence. Viserys demands to know when he'll receive his crown. Illyrio and Ser Jorah counsel patience; Viserys threatens Jorah.
2. **Dany's dragon dream** — Dany dreams Viserys beats her, she is naked and bloody; then Viserys vanishes and a great dragon appears amid columns of flame. It turns its molten eyes to her and she wakes terrified, covered in sweat.
3. **Wedding ceremony begins at dawn** — Dany is seated beside Drogo on an earthen ramp above the Dothraki. The feast involves gorging on horseflesh and mare's milk, and warriors grease their braids with fat.
4. **Viserys seated below Dany** — He is placed below her and the bloodriders, fuming at being served after the khal and bride. His mood blackens through the day.
5. **Dany feels isolated** — She has no common language with Drogo; Illyrio and Viserys are too far below to talk to. She nurses honeyed wine and tells herself she is blood of the dragon.
6. **First death at the wedding** — A warrior steps into a dance circle, takes a woman; two warriors fight over the same woman with arakhs. One is cut open from backbone to belly. The winner takes a different woman. Slaves carry off the body.
7. **Multiple deaths at the wedding** — Before the day is over, a dozen men have died. Illyrio had told Dany a Dothraki wedding without three deaths is deemed dull.
8. **Bride gifts presented at sunset** — Drogo claps his hands and the feast halts. Gift-giving begins.
9. **Viserys gives three handmaids** — Irri (riding), Jhiqui (Dothraki language), and Doreah (arts of love). Illyrio provided them; Viserys implies he and Illyrio have personal experience with Doreah.
10. **Ser Jorah gives books** — Histories and songs of the Seven Kingdoms, written in the Common Tongue. He apologizes for the modest gift.
11. **Illyrio gives dragon eggs** — Three petrified eggs in a cedar chest with fine fabrics: one deep green with bronze flecks, one pale cream with gold streaks, one black with scarlet ripples. From the Shadow Lands beyond Asshai.
12. **Bloodriders give traditional weapons** — Haggo (leather whip, silver handle), Cohollo (gold-chased arakh), Qotho (dragonbone bow). Dany refuses them with the traditional words, passing them to Drogo.
13. **Other Dothraki gifts** — Slippers, jewels, silver hair rings, medallion belts, painted vests, furs, sandsilks, jars of scent, needles, feathers, bottles of purple glass, a gown of a thousand mouse skins.
14. **Drogo gives the silver horse** — A young filly, grey as the winter sea, mane like silver smoke. The whole khalasar falls silent. Drogo lifts Dany onto the saddle.
15. **Dany rides the silver** — She takes the reins, rides through the crowd with growing confidence, jumps the filly over a firepit. She tells Illyrio to tell Drogo he has given her the wind. Drogo smiles for the first time.
16. **Viserys threatens Dany** — As Drogo saddles his stallion, Viserys grabs Dany's leg and tells her to please Drogo or she will "see the dragon wake as it has never woken before."
17. **Drogo and Dany ride out alone** — They leave the khalasar as stars come out. Drogo rides at a hard trot, speaking no word. Dany whispers "I am the blood of the dragon" to keep her courage up.
18. **Arrival at the stream** — In full dark, they stop at a grassy place beside a small stream. Drogo lifts her down. She begins to cry.
19. **Drogo speaks "No"** — He wipes her tears with a callused thumb. Dany is astonished he speaks any Common Tongue. He touches her hair and murmurs in Dothraki with unexpected warmth.
20. **Drogo unbraids his hair** — He removes the bells; Dany helps. She unbraids his hair, which spreads out like a river of darkness.
21. **Consummation** — Drogo undresses her slowly with deft, tender fingers. He looks at her body, then touches her gently and progressively. He asks "No?" and Dany consents, saying "Yes."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pervasive fear throughout — fear of the Dothraki, of Viserys, of consummation. Brief exhilaration when riding the silver horse. Loneliness and isolation at the feast. Wonder at the dragon eggs. Surprise and tentative comfort at Drogo's gentleness at the stream.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Surviving the wedding day and the coming night; trying to hold herself together through fear
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to ride the silver boldly (including jumping the firepit); chooses to consent to Drogo ("Yes")
- **Self-deception flags:** Repeats "I am the blood of the dragon" as a mantra to maintain courage, while internally noting "There are no more dragons" — aware that the Targaryen identity she clings to may be hollow
