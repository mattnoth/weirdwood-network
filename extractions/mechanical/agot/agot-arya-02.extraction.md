# AGOT — Arya II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 23
- **pov_character:** Arya Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya II
- **first_available:** AGOT Arya II
- **location_primary:** Tower of the Hand, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Small Hall (Tower of the Hand), Arya's bedchamber (Tower of the Hand); Winterfell and the Trident referenced in recollection
- **approximate_timeline:** After Lady's death and Mycah's killing; shortly before the Hand's tourney; Ned has recently been appointed Hand of the King
- **chapter_summary:** Arya eats a tense dinner with her father, Sansa, and household guards in the Small Hall of the Tower of the Hand. Grief over Mycah's death drives her from the table; she retreats to her bedchamber with Needle. Ned discovers the sword and confronts Arya; after she confesses her guilt over Mycah, he comforts her, invokes the Stark family ethic of pack survival, and ultimately returns Needle to her. Three days later, Ned arranges a private swordsmanship instructor for Arya: Syrio Forel, a Braavosi who was formerly First Sword to the Sealord of Braavos, who begins teaching her the Braavosi "water dance" style.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dinner in the Small Hall** — Ned arrives late to dinner; the first course (pumpkin soup) has already been removed. Jory announces a tourney will be held in Ned's honor. Ned is displeased but agrees both daughters can attend after Septa Mordane argues it would look strange if they did not.
2. **Sansa mocks Arya** — Sansa tells Arya she "shan't be wanted" at the tourney. Ned rebukes Sansa and orders the sisters to stop fighting.
3. **Ned leaves the table early** — He announces he has no appetite and walks out.
4. **Arya bolts from the table** — Overcome by grief over Mycah, Arya pushes away her plate, defies Septa Mordane, and runs from the hall.
5. **Arya evades Fat Tom** — She slides between Fat Tom's legs at the tower door and runs up the stairs to her bedchamber.
6. **Arya bars her door and retrieves Needle** — She drops the crossbar, cries, then opens her clothes chest and removes Needle from where she had hidden it beneath her garments.
7. **Septa Mordane pounds on the door** — Arya brandishes Needle and threatens her; Mordane threatens to tell the Hand; Arya shouts her away.
8. **Arya fantasizes escape** — She imagines climbing out the window, finding Nymeria, and returning to Winterfell or going to Jon at the Wall.
9. **Ned comes to Arya's door** — He knocks softly; she opens the door. He enters alone, discovers Needle.
10. **Ned confronts Arya about Needle** — He examines the blade, identifies Mikken's mark, and questions how she came to have it. Arya refuses to betray Jon as the source.
11. **Ned invokes Lyanna and Brandon Stark** — He compares Arya to Lyanna ("wolf blood"), notes both died young, and says Arya even looks like Lyanna.
12. **Arya confesses guilt over Mycah** — She breaks down crying; Ned holds her and absolves her, placing responsibility on the Hound and the queen.
13. **Arya confides her hatreds** — She names the Hound, the queen, the king, Joffrey, and Sansa as people she hates; explains Sansa lied about the Trident incident to please Joffrey.
14. **Ned reveals he knew about Nymeria** — He tells Arya he deduced she drove the wolf off; confirms Jory kept his promise and did not tell him. Arya explains she threw rocks to drive Nymeria away to save her from the queen.
15. **Ned's "lone wolf / pack survives" speech** — He invokes House Stark words and sigil; tells Arya the lone wolf dies but the pack survives; urges her to stop fighting Sansa and Septa Mordane and to grow up.
16. **Ned returns Needle** — He gives the sword back to Arya, hilt first, telling her to keep it.
17. **Arya apologizes to Septa Mordane** — The next morning, she apologizes; Septa is suspicious but Father nods approval.
18. **Syrio Forel introduced as dancing master** — Three days after the Needle confrontation, Vayon Poole sends Arya to the Small Hall; the trestle tables have been dismantled. Syrio Forel is waiting, holding two wooden practice swords. He tells her she is late (calls her "boy").
19. **First sword lesson** — Syrio corrects Arya's two-handed grip to one hand (left hand), corrects her stance to a sideways profile, adjusts her grip to be light and deft, and explains the water dance philosophy. Arya tries to strike him for four hours without success.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deep grief over Mycah's death; guilt and self-blame; alienation from the household guard and from Sansa; loneliness; eventual relief and love after Ned's conversation; cautious hope after receiving sword training
- **Primary preoccupation:** Grief and guilt over Mycah; sense of isolation in King's Landing; longing for Winterfell, her brothers, Jon, and Nymeria
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to betray Jon as the source of Needle even to Ned; vows to grow up and be strong; accepts Syrio Forel's instruction
- **Self-deception flags:** Arya tells herself she likes being alone and not being talked to, but the narration makes clear she is deeply lonely and misses her family
