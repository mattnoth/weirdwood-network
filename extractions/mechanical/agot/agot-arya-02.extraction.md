- **chapter_summary:** Arya eats supper with her father's household guard in the Small Hall, where talk of the upcoming tourney surfaces. She is estranged from the guards she once trusted because none of them acted to prevent Mycah's death. After fleeing the table and barricading herself in her room, Ned finds her with Needle. He recognizes Mikken's work, compares Arya to his sister Lyanna, speaks of "the wolf blood," warns her that King's Landing is dangerous, and returns Needle to her. Three days later, Ned arranges a "dancing master" — Syrio Forel, former First Sword of the Sealord of Braavos — to teach Arya the water dance.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned arrives late to supper** — He comes to the Small Hall after fighting with the council, finding the first course already cleared.
2. **Tourney announced** — Jory reports yard talk of a tourney in Ned's honor as Hand. Ned is displeased.
3. **Sansa asks to attend the tourney** — Septa Mordane supports the request by noting Princess Myrcella will attend. Ned reluctantly agrees for both daughters.
4. **Arya-Sansa conflict** — Arya says she doesn't care about the tourney; Sansa says Arya won't be wanted. Ned sharply rebukes them both and demands they behave as sisters.
5. **Ned leaves supper** — He excuses himself, claiming he has no appetite.
6. **Arya sits isolated at supper** — She reflects on missing her brothers, her alienation from the household guard since Mycah's death, and her guilt.
7. **Arya flees the table** — She refuses to eat, defies Septa Mordane, and bolts from the hall.
8. **Arya barricades herself in her room** — She runs past Fat Tom, up the tower stairs, and bars her door. She cries.
9. **Arya retrieves Needle** — She digs through her clothing chest and draws the sword from its sheath.
10. **Septa Mordane demands entry** — Arya threatens with Needle and slashes at the air. The septa threatens to tell the Hand and leaves.
11. **Arya fantasizes about escape** — She imagines climbing down the tower, finding Nymeria, returning to Winterfell or running to Jon at the Wall.
12. **Ned visits Arya** — He asks to see the sword, recognizes Mikken's maker's mark, identifies it as a "bravo's blade."
13. **Ned compares Arya to Lyanna** — He speaks of "the wolf blood" that his father identified in Lyanna and Brandon, noting it brought them early deaths. He says Arya looks like Lyanna.
14. **Arya confesses about Mycah** — She breaks down, blaming herself for Mycah's death. Ned comforts her, placing the blame on the Hound and "the cruel woman he serves."
15. **Arya confesses about Nymeria** — She admits throwing rocks to drive Nymeria away. Ned reveals he already knew ("even a blind man could see that wolf would never have left you willingly") and says the lie was "not without honor."
16. **Ned warns Arya about danger** — He invokes the Stark words "Winter is coming" and the pack metaphor ("the lone wolf dies, but the pack survives"), urging her to stop fighting with Sansa and grow up.
17. **Ned returns Needle** — He gives the sword back to Arya, telling her it's hers "for true."
18. **Arya apologizes to Septa Mordane** — The next morning at breakfast, she asks for pardon.
19. **Arya meets Syrio Forel** — Three days later, Vayon Poole sends Arya to the Small Hall, where a Braavosi swordsman introduces himself as her "dancing master."
20. **First lesson with Syrio** — He corrects her grip, stance (sideface), and hand (approves her left-handedness). He distinguishes the water dance from the "iron dance of Westeros." Arya practices for four hours.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief, guilt, anger, isolation, fear. Arya is consumed by guilt over Mycah's death and alienated from everyone around her. She cycles through self-blame, rage at the Hound/queen/Joffrey/Sansa, and loneliness. After Ned's visit, she feels loved and resolved. Meeting Syrio brings focus and physical engagement.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Mycah's death and her perceived responsibility for it. Secondary: isolation from her siblings and former friends among the household guard.
- **Key decisions made:** Retrieves Needle and clings to it; vows to grow up and be strong; accepts Syrio's training.
- **Self-deception flags:** Arya tells Ned "I don't hate Sansa, not truly" — the narrator notes "it was only half a lie." She also blames herself entirely for Mycah's death despite Ned's correction.
