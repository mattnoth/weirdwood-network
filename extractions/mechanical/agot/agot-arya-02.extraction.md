# AGOT — Arya II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 23
- **pov_character:** Arya Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya II
- **first_available:** AGOT Arya II
- **location_primary:** Tower of the Hand, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Small Hall (Red Keep)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the Stark household's arrival in King's Landing; the tourney of the Hand has been announced but not yet held
- **time_markers:** Supper scene (evening); Arya cries in her room (night); "the next morning" she apologizes at breakfast; "three days later, at midday" she meets Syrio Forel; training lasts "four hours"; "the next day their real work began"
- **chapter_summary:** Arya attends an uncomfortable supper in the Small Hall where news of an upcoming tourney is discussed. Still grieving Mycah's death and angry at everyone's inaction, she flees the table, barricades herself in her room, and retrieves Needle from her trunk. Her father comes to her, discovers the sword, recognizes Mikken's maker's mark, and they share an emotional conversation about Mycah, Nymeria, Lyanna, the wolf blood, and the need for family unity. Ned returns Needle to Arya. Three days later, Ned has arranged a sword instructor — Syrio Forel, former first sword to the Sealord of Braavos — who begins teaching Arya the water dance.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned arrives late to supper** — Arya can see from his face that he has been fighting with the council again. The first course (pumpkin soup) has already been cleared.
2. **Tourney announced** — Jory reports that knights will come from across the realm for a tourney honoring Ned's appointment as Hand. Ned is unhappy about it.
3. **Sansa begs to attend the tourney** — Septa Mordane supports the request, noting Princess Myrcella will attend and it would "look queer" if the Hand's family were absent. Ned reluctantly agrees for both Sansa and Arya.
4. **Arya dismisses the tourney** — She says she doesn't care about "their stupid tourney" because Joffrey will be there.
5. **Sansa insults Arya** — Tells Arya "You shan't be wanted." Ned angrily rebukes Sansa and demands the sisters stop fighting.
6. **Ned leaves the meal** — He excuses himself, saying he has "small appetite tonight."
7. **Arya broods at the table** — She reflects on how the household guards she once trusted did nothing when Lady was killed and the Hound murdered Mycah. She recalls Jeyne Poole telling her Mycah was cut to pieces and returned to the butcher in a bag.
8. **Arya remembers Winterfell** — She recalls her father's custom of dining with his men, the household she loved, Fat Tom calling her "Arya Underfoot," and the nickname "Arya Horseface" which she liked less.
9. **Arya bolts from the table** — She pushes away her untouched ribs, asks to be excused, is denied by Septa Mordane, shouts "You clean it!" and runs for the door.
10. **Arya evades Fat Tom** — She slides between his legs at the Tower of the Hand door and runs up the winding tower steps.
11. **Arya barricades herself in her room** — She slams the massive oak door and drops the heavy crossbar. She cries.
12. **Fat Tom knocks** — He asks if she's in there; she shouts "No!" and he goes away. "Fat Tom was always easy to fool."
13. **Arya retrieves Needle** — She digs through her clothing chest, tossing handfuls of silk, satin, velvet, and wool on the floor, and draws the slender blade from its sheath at the bottom.
14. **Arya thinks of Mycah** — Her eyes fill with tears. "Her fault, her fault, her fault."
15. **Septa Mordane pounds on the door** — Demands Arya open up. Arya threatens with Needle drawn ("You better not come in here!") and slashes at the air. The septa threatens to tell the Hand and retreats.
16. **Arya fantasizes about escape** — She imagines climbing down the tower like Bran, running away with Needle and good boots and a warm cloak, finding Nymeria in the wild woods below the Trident, and going to Winterfell or to Jon at the Wall.
17. **Ned comes to Arya's room** — He comes alone. He is "more sad than angry." Arya lets him in and is ashamed.
18. **Ned discovers Needle** — He examines the blade, recognizes it as "a bravo's blade" with Mikken's maker's mark. He asks how his nine-year-old daughter is being armed from his own forge.
19. **Arya refuses to name who gave her Needle** — She "would not betray Jon, not even to their father." Ned lets the matter drop.
20. **Ned speaks of the wolf blood** — He compares Arya to Lyanna, saying she has "a wildness" his father called "the wolf blood." Lyanna had it, Brandon had it more, and it brought them both to early graves. He says Arya reminds him of Lyanna and "you even look like her."
21. **Arya is startled by the Lyanna comparison** — She says "Lyanna was beautiful," noting that beauty "was not a thing that was ever said of Arya."
22. **Arya breaks down about Mycah** — She confesses she asked Mycah to practice swords with her and blames herself. Ned embraces her: "Grieve for your friend, but never blame yourself. That murder lies at the Hound's door, him and the cruel woman he serves."
23. **Arya confesses her hatreds** — She hates the Hound, the queen, the king, Joffrey, and Sansa. She says Joffrey lied and Sansa "did remember, she just lied so Joffrey would like her."
24. **Ned reveals he knew about Nymeria** — "We all lie. Or did you truly think I'd believe that Nymeria ran off?" Confirms Jory kept his word not to tell. "Even a blind man could see that wolf would never have left you willingly."
25. **Arya describes driving Nymeria away** — They threw rocks; she told the wolf to go be free; other wolves were howling and woods were full of game; Nymeria kept following until Arya hit her twice and she whined.
26. **Ned validates Arya's choice** — "It was right. And even the lie was … not without honor."
27. **Ned counsels Arya about unity** — He invokes "Winter is coming" and the direwolf sigil. "When the snows fall and the white winds blow, the lone wolf dies, but the pack survives." He warns that King's Landing is "a dark dangerous place" with enemies and tells Arya it is time to grow up.
28. **Ned returns Needle to Arya** — He gives the sword back hilt-first. She is astonished. He jokes he'd find "a morningstar hidden under your pillow within the fortnight" if he took it. "Try not to stab your sister, whatever the provocation."
29. **Arya apologizes to Septa Mordane** — The next morning at breakfast, she asks for the septa's pardon. The septa is suspicious but Ned nods.
30. **Arya meets Syrio Forel** — Three days later at midday, Vayon Poole sends Arya to the Small Hall, now cleared of tables. A slight bald man with a great beak of a nose and a Free Cities accent steps from the shadows holding two wooden swords. "You are late, boy." He introduces himself as her "dancing master."
31. **First sword lesson** — Syrio teaches Arya to hold the sword one-handed (left hand), stand sideface to minimize target, and grip delicately. He approves of her left hand: "All is reversed, it will make your enemies more awkward." He explains this is the water dance, the bravo's dance — "swift and sudden," not the "iron dance of Westeros." He reveals he was first sword to the Sealord of Braavos for nine years.
32. **Arya trains for four hours** — She tries to strike Syrio the entire time without success. "The next day their real work began."
