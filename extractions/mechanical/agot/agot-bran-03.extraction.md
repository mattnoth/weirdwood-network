- **time_markers:** No explicit time markers; the dream has an indeterminate, timeless quality; Bran notes "it seemed as though he had been falling for years"; cold in the room when he wakes; window is open
- **chapter_summary:** Bran experiences a prolonged coma dream in which he falls endlessly through darkness. A three-eyed crow accompanies him, urging him to fly rather than fall. During the dream, Bran sees visions of distant people and places: Winterfell and its inhabitants, his mother on a galley crossing the Bite, his father and sisters in the south surrounded by shadows, lands across the narrow sea, and terrifying things beyond the Wall in the far north. The crow tells him "winter is coming" and that he must live. Bran chooses to fly, and the crow pecks him between the eyes, at which point he wakes in a tower room in Winterfell. He names his direwolf Summer.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** A storm is gathering ahead of Catelyn's galley on the Bite — "a vast dark roaring lashed by lightning." Snow on mountain peaks seen from above. Cold in Bran's tower room when he wakes; the window is open.
- **Season indicators:** "Because winter is coming" — the crow's words. Snow and cold dominate the northern visions. The far north is described as a frozen wasteland.
- **Time of day:** Not specified; the dream occurs in darkness with no sun or stars. Time of day when Bran wakes is not stated.
- **Lighting:** Darkness throughout the dream — "no sun, no stars." A golden shining face appears briefly in the mist. The Wall shines "like blue crystal." Yellow eyes of the direwolf shine "like the sun" when Bran wakes. The curtain of light at the end of the world in the far north.
- **Sounds:** Grey mists "howled" around Bran. The crow caws and shrieks. Death reaches for him "screaming." The serving woman shouts "He's awake" when Bran wakes.
- **Smells:** None described.
- **Notable sensory details:** Cold in the darkness of the dream. Bran feels how fast he is falling. The warmth of the direwolf "enfolded him like a hot bath." Bran's hand trembles "like a leaf." Burning sensation between his eyes where the crow pecked him. A basin of water shatters on the floor. Bran feels weak and dizzy upon waking. He tries to get out of bed but "nothing happened" — he cannot move his legs. He feels nothing when the wolf lands on his legs.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran falls through darkness** — He experiences an extended dream of falling through grey mists and darkness with no sun or stars.
2. **A voice urges him to fly** — A high, thin voice tells Bran to fly instead of fall, and to try.
3. **The crow appears** — A crow spirals down with Bran, asks for corn, and lands on his hand to eat golden kernels from Bran's pocket.
4. **The crow challenges Bran** — It tells him he will die when he hits the ground and that the answer is flying, not crying.
5. **A golden face appears** — A shining golden face emerges from the mist and says "The things I do for love." Bran screams.
6. **The crow intervenes** — It tells Bran to forget the golden face, that he does not need it now. It pecks at him and the face vanishes.
7. **Bran sees visions of the world** — Looking down, he sees the whole realm: Winterfell from above (Maester Luwin, Robb, Hodor, the godswood weirwood), his mother on a galley on the Bite with Ser Rodrik, his father with the king and his sisters in the south surrounded by shadows, lands across the narrow sea to Asshai, and the Wall and beyond to the far north.
8. **The weirwood stares back** — When Bran watches the weirwood in the godswood, it lifts its eyes from the pool and stares back at him "knowingly."
9. **The crow reveals its purpose** — It tells Bran "Now you know why you must live" and "Because winter is coming."
10. **Bran sees the crow's third eye** — The crow has three eyes, and the third eye is "full of a terrible knowledge." Bran sees frozen ice spires below with bones of a thousand other dreamers impaled on their points.
11. **Bran remembers his father's words** — He recalls asking "Can a man still be brave if he's afraid?" and his father answering "That is the only time a man can be brave."
12. **Bran chooses to fly** — The crow urges him to choose: fly or die. Bran spreads his arms and flies upward, soaring above the world.
13. **The crow pecks Bran between the eyes** — It beats its wings in his face, slowing him, and stabs its beak at his forehead between his eyes, causing blinding pain.
14. **Bran wakes** — The grey mists rip away and the crow becomes a serving woman with long black hair. Bran is in a bed in a chilly tower room at Winterfell. The woman drops a basin and runs shouting "He's awake."
15. **Bran discovers he cannot move** — He tries to get out of bed but nothing happens. He feels nothing when the wolf lands on his legs.
16. **Summer arrives and is named** — Bran's direwolf pup, now noticeably bigger, jumps on the bed. Its yellow eyes shine like the sun; its warmth enfolds Bran. He reaches out with a trembling hand.
17. **Robb arrives** — Robb bursts into the room breathless. The direwolf is licking Bran's face. Bran looks up calmly and says "His name is Summer."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terrified throughout the falling dream — desperate, crying, wanting to wake up. Brief elation when he flies ("It was better than climbing. It was better than anything"). Calm upon waking despite discovering he cannot move his legs. Awe during the visions.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival — the crow frames the dream as literally life or death ("fly or die"). Learning to fly is learning to live.
- **Key decisions made:** Bran chooses to fly rather than die. He names his direwolf Summer.
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran initially insists "It's just a dream" and that he'll wake when he hits the ground — the crow corrects him that this is not an ordinary dream.
