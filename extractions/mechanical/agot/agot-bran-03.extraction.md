- **approximate_timeline:** Bran awakens from his coma; chapter follows his injury from the fall
- **chapter_summary:** Bran dreams he is falling endlessly through grey mist, accompanied by a three-eyed crow that urges him to fly rather than fall. The crow shows him a sweeping vision of the world from above — Winterfell, his family scattered across the realm, north of the Wall, and beyond. The crow whispers that Bran must live "because winter is coming," then pecks him between the eyes. Bran wakes in his tower room, discovers he cannot move his legs, and names his direwolf Summer.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran's falling dream** — Bran experiences a prolonged dream of falling through grey mist, unable to fly.
2. **Three-eyed crow appears** — A crow with a third eye spirals down alongside Bran, speaks to him, eats corn from his hand, and urges him to fly.
3. **Golden face appears** — A shining golden face appears in the mist saying "The things I do for love"; the crow drives it away by shouting at Bran to put it aside.
4. **World-spanning vision** — The crow commands Bran to look down; Bran sees a panoramic vision encompassing Winterfell, the Bite, the Trident, across the narrow sea, and deep into the far north.
5. **"Because winter is coming"** — The crow tells Bran he must live; when Bran asks why, the crow answers "Because winter is coming."
6. **The crow reveals its third eye** — Bran sees the crow has three eyes, the third full of "terrible knowledge."
7. **Ned's voice echoes** — Bran hears his own small voice asking "Can a man still be brave if he's afraid?" and hears Eddard's voice reply "That is the only time a man can be brave."
8. **Bran chooses to fly** — The crow urges "Fly or die." Bran spreads his arms and flies upward, escaping the ice spears below.
9. **Crow pecks Bran between the eyes** — After Bran cries out in delight at flying, the crow pecks him sharply between his eyes; he feels sudden blinding pain.
10. **Dream dissolves; serving woman revealed** — The grey mists rip away; the crow is revealed to be a black-haired serving woman from Winterfell, who drops a basin and runs shouting that Bran is awake.
11. **Bran wakes and discovers paralysis** — Bran wakes in his tower room, tries to get out of bed, and nothing happens — he cannot move.
12. **Direwolf lands on Bran's legs** — Bran feels nothing when the wolf lands on his legs; the wolf's yellow eyes meet his.
13. **Bran names his direwolf Summer** — First use of the name; Bran names him when Robb enters the room.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terror throughout the dream (fear of falling, fear of death, fear of what lies beyond the Wall); sudden delight at the moment of flight; weak and dizzy upon waking; calm when Robb enters
- **Primary preoccupation:** Falling and the impossibility of flying; the crow's insistence that he can and must fly; the need to survive
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to fly rather than die — spreads his arms and accepts flight at the crow's ultimatum; names his direwolf Summer
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran insists repeatedly "I can't fly" and "It's just a dream" — the crow challenges both assertions directly
