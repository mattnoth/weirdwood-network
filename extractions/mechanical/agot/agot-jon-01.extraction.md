# AGOT — Jon I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 6
- **pov_character:** Jon Snow
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon I
- **first_available:** AGOT Jon I
- **location_primary:** Winterfell — Great Hall and outer yard
- **location_secondary:** The Wall (mentioned); Casterly Rock (mentioned); Dorne (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** The evening of King Robert's welcoming feast at Winterfell; immediately follows the royal party's arrival
- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow drinks summerwine among the squires at the low end of the Great Hall, having been seated away from the high table at Lady Stark's instruction. He observes the royal procession and converses with his uncle Benjen Stark, asking to be taken to the Wall and to join the Night's Watch. Benjen rebuffs him as too young. Jon is overcome with emotion after Benjen's pointed remark about bastards and flees the hall in tears. In the yard, he encounters Tyrion Lannister, who offers him counsel about embracing his bastard identity, and the two share an unexpected exchange before Tyrion returns to the feast.

## Events & Actions
1. **Royal procession enters the Great Hall** — Eddard Stark escorts Queen Cersei; King Robert escorts Catelyn; the royal and Stark children follow in pairs; Benjen Stark and Theon Greyjoy enter last among the high lords.
2. **Jon observes all procession members** — He is seated at the low end of the hall on a bench among young squires and gets a close look at all entrants.
3. **Jon drinks heavily** — He consumes multiple cups of summerwine with no parental supervision, to the encouragement of the squires around him.
4. **Ghost confronts a mongrel dog** — A black bitch approaches Ghost's chicken; Ghost bares his fangs silently and the dog retreats.
5. **Benjen Stark joins Jon** — Benjen sits at the bench, takes Jon's wine cup, and the two converse about Ghost, Jon's seating, Eddard's mood, and Robert's afternoon visit to the crypts.
6. **Jon asks to join the Night's Watch** — Jon requests Benjen take him to the Wall and intercede with Eddard; Benjen refuses, calling Jon too young and not yet a man.
7. **Jon cites Daeren Targaryen** — Jon argues youthful achievement is possible; Benjen counters by noting Daeren's conquest was short-lived and he died at eighteen.
8. **Jon declares he will never father a bastard** — Responding to Benjen's parting remark about coming back after fathering bastards, Jon spits this vow out "like venom."
9. **Jon flees the hall in tears** — Overcome by emotion after the table falls silent watching him, Jon pushes up from the bench, stumbles into a serving girl, spills spiced wine, and runs out into the yard with Ghost.
10. **Tyrion encounters Jon in the yard** — Tyrion is seated on the ledge above the Great Hall door; he descends via an acrobatic tumble and approaches Jon and Ghost.
11. **Tyrion examines Ghost** — He attempts to pet Ghost; the direwolf bares fangs but holds still at Jon's command.
12. **Tyrion identifies Jon as Ned Stark's bastard** — Tyrion states he can see "more of the north" in Jon than his brothers.
13. **Tyrion delivers counsel on bastardry** — He advises Jon to make his bastard status his armor. He reveals his mother died at his birth and that his father has never been certain of his legitimacy. He delivers his closing line and returns to the feast.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Initially relieved and somewhat pleased to be free of the high-table constraints; enjoyment of camaraderie among the squires. Swells with pride at Benjen's praise of his perceptiveness. Grows impassioned and then wounded during the Night's Watch conversation; breaks down in anger and tears when Benjen's remark about fathering bastards strikes him. Briefly intrigued and disarmed by Tyrion's encounter.
- **Primary preoccupation:** His status as a bastard and how it defines and limits him; his desire to join the Night's Watch as a path to purpose and honor despite his birth.
- **Key decisions made:** Declares emphatically and with deliberate venom that he will never father a bastard. Decides to trust Tyrion enough to engage in conversation and allow Ghost to be touched.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells himself he is "fortunate" to have Ghost at the feast and that his seat among the squires has advantages — a coping mechanism for being excluded from the high table by Catelyn. He claims Ghost "would tear out your throat" if Jon weren't present, then internally admits "it wasn't actually true yet."
