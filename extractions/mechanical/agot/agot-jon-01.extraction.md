## Chapter Metadata

- **first_available:** AGOT Jon I
- **location_primary:** Winterfell (Great Hall)
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell courtyard
- **approximate_timeline:** Evening of the welcoming feast for King Robert's arrival at Winterfell
- **time_markers:** "fourth hour of the welcoming feast"; nighttime (yard is dark when Jon goes outside); the royal party arrived and processed into the hall earlier that evening; the king visited the crypts "this afternoon"
- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow sits apart from his siblings at the welcoming feast for King Robert, drinking with squires at the lower benches because Lady Stark deemed it inappropriate to seat a bastard with the royal family. He observes the royal procession in detail, then feeds his direwolf Ghost under the table. His uncle Benjen Stark joins him, and Jon asks to join the Night's Watch, but Benjen discourages him, saying he is too young to understand what he would sacrifice. Jon storms out in tears after an emotional outburst and encounters Tyrion Lannister sitting above the Great Hall door, who offers him blunt counsel about embracing his identity as a bastard.

## Events & Actions
1. **Royal procession enters the Great Hall** — The royal party and Stark children process into the hall in pairs: Eddard with the queen, Robert with Lady Stark, Rickon alone, Robb with Myrcella, Arya with Tommen, Sansa with Joffrey, Jaime and Tyrion Lannister, and lastly Benjen Stark with Theon Greyjoy.
2. **Toasts and feasting begin** — After all are seated, toasts are made and the welcoming feast commences. Jon begins drinking heavily.
3. **Jon observes the feast from the lower benches** — Jon watches his father's tension, the king's heavy drinking, and the queen's cold demeanor. He drinks summerwine with the squires.
4. **Ghost confronts a mongrel bitch** — A black mongrel three times Ghost's size tries to take his chicken. Ghost bares his fangs silently and the larger dog retreats.
5. **Benjen Stark joins Jon** — Benjen sits with Jon, tastes his summerwine, eats a roasted onion, and they discuss direwolves, the Wall, and Jon's observation skills.
6. **Jon asks to join the Night's Watch** — Jon formally requests that Benjen take him to the Wall, citing his martial skills and the example of Daeron Targaryen.
7. **Benjen refuses Jon's request** — Benjen tells Jon he is too young and doesn't understand what he would sacrifice. He advises Jon to come back after he has lived more.
8. **Jon's emotional outburst** — Jon declares he will never father a bastard; the table falls silent; Jon flees in tears, stumbling into a serving girl and spilling a flagon of spiced wine.
9. **Jon encounters Tyrion in the courtyard** — Tyrion is sitting on the ledge above the Great Hall door. He asks about Ghost and jumps acrobatically down from the ledge.
10. **Ghost reacts to Tyrion** — Ghost backs away uncertainly from Tyrion, then bares fangs in a silent snarl, but obeys Jon's command to sit and allows Tyrion to pet him.
11. **Tyrion identifies Jon as Ned Stark's bastard** — Jon confirms his parentage. Tyrion observes that Jon has "more of the north" in him than his brothers.
12. **Tyrion offers counsel** — Tyrion advises Jon to embrace his identity as a bastard so it can never be used as a weapon against him, drawing a parallel to his own dwarfism. He returns to the feast.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Oscillates between defiant pride (drinking freely, reveling in the company of squires), bitterness about his bastard status (exclusion from the high table), longing for belonging (desire to join the Night's Watch), and raw emotional pain (tears, anger at Benjen's dismissal). Ends the chapter in a more reflective state after Tyrion's counsel.
- **Primary preoccupation:** His identity as a bastard and what place he can earn in the world. His exclusion from the family table crystallizes his sense of not belonging.
- **Key decisions made:** Declares his intention to join the Night's Watch (though Benjen refuses). Vows he will never father a bastard.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells himself he is "fortunate" that no one cared about Ghost being at his end of the hall, framing his exclusion as advantage. He claims Ghost would tear out Tyrion's throat — then admits to himself "it wasn't actually true yet." He curses the smoke for making his eyes sting, though the tears are emotional.
