# AGOT — Daenerys I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 4
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys I
- **first_available:** AGOT Daenerys I
- **location_primary:** Pentos — Illyrio's estate
- **location_secondary:** Khal Drogo's manse (Pentos), streets of Pentos
- **approximate_timeline:** Evening of the feast at which Daenerys is presented to Khal Drogo; early in the book's timeline
- **time_markers:** Setting sun observed from Dany's window; red priests lighting night fires; streets pitch-dark when they depart; moonlight in the courtyard of Drogo's manse; "nigh on half a year" living in Illyrio's house; Dany is thirteen; Viserys was eight when they fled King's Landing; Daenerys born nine moons after the flight to Dragonstone
- **chapter_summary:** Viserys Targaryen presents Daenerys with a gown from Magister Illyrio in preparation for a feast where she will be displayed to Khal Drogo as a potential bride. Daenerys reflects on her childhood as an exile wandering the Free Cities with Viserys after fleeing Dragonstone. She is bathed and dressed by Illyrio's slaves, then travels by palanquin with Viserys and Illyrio to Drogo's manse. At the feast, she sees the khal for the first time, begs Viserys not to make her marry him, and is silenced by his threats. Illyrio brings Drogo over as the chapter ends.

## Events & Actions
1. **Viserys presents the gown** — Viserys shows Daenerys a deep plum silk gown gifted by Illyrio, telling her she must look like a princess tonight.
2. **Viserys threatens Daenerys** — He tells her not to fail him, pinches her nipple cruelly, and warns her not to "wake the dragon."
3. **Daenerys looks out over Pentos** — She watches the sunset over the bay, hears the red priests singing and children playing, and wishes she could be among them.
4. **Daenerys reflects on exile** — She recalls Viserys's stories of Westeros, the fall of the Targaryens, Rhaegar's death, the sack of King's Landing, her birth on Dragonstone during a storm, their mother's death, the rescue by Ser Willem Darry, life in Braavos in the house with the red door, Ser Willem's death, and their wandering through the Free Cities.
5. **Slaves bathe and dress Daenerys** — Two of Illyrio's slaves bathe her in scalding water with fragrant oils, wash and brush her hair, anoint her with spiceflower perfume, and dress her in the gown with jewels and the golden torc.
6. **Daenerys sees herself in the mirror** — She sees a princess but feels a chill when she thinks of the golden collar resembling a slave's collar.
7. **Illyrio greets Daenerys** — He calls her "a vision," blesses her in the name of the Lord of Light, and reassures Viserys that Drogo will be enraptured.
8. **Viserys expresses doubt** — He calls her too skinny and questions whether Drogo likes women this young.
9. **Illyrio affirms her suitability** — He cites her blood, her Valyrian features, her highborn status.
10. **Travel by palanquin** — The three ride through the pitch-dark streets of Pentos to Drogo's manse; Viserys fantasizes about conquering Westeros with ten thousand Dothraki and names potential allies.
11. **Illyrio flatters Viserys** — He claims the people of Westeros lift secret toasts to Viserys and sew dragon banners, attributing this to "my agents." Dany mistrusts his words.
12. **Arrival at Drogo's manse** — They are stopped at the gate by an Unsullied guard; Illyrio speaks Dothraki to gain entry.
13. **Entry into the feast** — A eunuch announces them; they enter a pillared courtyard with Dothraki horselords, sellswords, a red priest, men from Ibben, lords from the Summer Isles. Dany realizes she is the only woman.
14. **Illyrio identifies guests** — He points out Drogo's bloodriders, Khal Moro and his son Rhogoro, the brother of the Archon of Tyrosh, and Ser Jorah Mormont.
15. **Dany learns about Ser Jorah** — Illyrio explains Jorah is an anointed knight exiled for selling poachers to a slaver.
16. **Daenerys sees Khal Drogo** — She observes his size, copper skin, long uncut braid, and dark eyes. Viserys explains the significance of the braid.
17. **Daenerys begs not to marry Drogo** — She tells Viserys she doesn't want to be Drogo's queen and wants to go home.
18. **Viserys threatens and silences her** — He drags her into shadows, tells her she will wed and bed Drogo, says he'd let the whole khalasar have her if needed, and orders her to dry her eyes.
19. **Illyrio brings Drogo over** — The chapter ends with Dany brushing away tears, Viserys telling her to smile and stand straight, and Illyrio escorting Drogo toward them.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear (of Drogo, of Viserys, of the unknown future), wistfulness (for the house with the red door, for a childhood she never had), resignation, sadness. She trembles when Illyrio releases her hand. She wishes she could be a barefoot child playing in the streets.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The impending presentation to Khal Drogo and what it means for her life; longing for home (not Westeros — the red door in Braavos).
- **Key decisions made:** None — Daenerys is entirely passive in this chapter, complying with Viserys's demands. She smiles and stands up straight at the end, obeying.
- **Self-deception flags:** None apparent. Dany is clear-eyed about Illyrio's motives, Viserys's delusions, and her own powerlessness. She mistrusts Illyrio's sweet words while Viserys does not.
