- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion has been reading in Winterfell's library through the night and descends at dawn. In the yard he encounters Sandor Clegane and Prince Joffrey, who speaks callously about Bran; Tyrion slaps Joffrey twice and orders him to go offer condolences to Lord and Lady Stark. Tyrion then joins Jaime, Cersei, and the royal children for breakfast and reports that the maester believes Bran may survive, though he will never walk. He observes an exchanged glance between Jaime and Cersei upon hearing the news. Tyrion announces his intention to travel north with Benjen Stark and Jon Snow to see the Wall before returning south.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion ends his all-night reading session** — At dawn, Tyrion closes his book (a discourse on changing seasons), wakes Chayle, instructs him to reshelve the books carefully, and descends the library tower.
2. **Joffrey and the Hound mock Tyrion's stature** — In the yard, Sandor performs his "voice from nowhere / spirits of the air" mockery at Tyrion's height; Joffrey laughs.
3. **Joffrey speaks callously about Bran** — Joffrey calls Bran's dying slow and says he cannot abide women's wailing; the Hound offers to silence the direwolf.
4. **Tyrion slaps Joffrey twice** — First for refusing to visit the Starks; second when Joffrey threatens to tell his mother. Tyrion orders Joffrey to go to Lord and Lady Stark and offer condolences on his knees.
5. **Joffrey flees the yard** — He leaves holding his cheek; the Hound warns Tyrion the prince will remember this.
6. **Tyrion joins the Lannister family breakfast** — He seats himself uninvited in the Guest House morning room with Jaime, Cersei, Tommen, and Myrcella.
7. **Tyrion reports on Bran's condition** — He tells the family: the maester believes Bran may live; his back and legs are broken; he will never walk; the direwolf outside his window appears connected to his survival (window was closed once, Bran weakened; reopened, heart beat stronger).
8. **Tyrion observes the Jaime-Cersei glance** — When Tyrion reveals Bran may survive, he notices a glance pass between Jaime and Cersei lasting no more than a second before Cersei drops her gaze.
9. **Cersei expresses hostility toward the direwolves** — She says they are unnatural and dangerous and she will not allow them south.
10. **Tyrion announces his plan to travel north** — He tells the family he intends to accompany Benjen Stark and Jon Snow to the Wall.
11. **Cersei takes the children and leaves** — She exits the morning room after Tyrion's crude joke about celibacy and whores.
12. **Tyrion and Jaime discuss Bran privately** — Jaime suggests ending Bran's suffering would be a mercy; Tyrion advises against saying this to Ned. Jaime calls a crippled life grotesque; Tyrion counters by invoking his own experience. Jaime expresses dark suspicion about Tyrion's loyalties after Tyrion says he hopes Bran wakes and can speak.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Wry, sharp, and somewhat detached; amused by the hypocrisies around him; capable of genuine anger (slapping Joffrey) but returns quickly to sardonic calm
- **Primary preoccupation:** Bran's condition and what it means; managing Joffrey's behavior; his plan to travel to the Wall
- **Key decisions made:** To travel north with Benjen Stark and Jon Snow to see the Wall before returning south
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion claims to love his family in the final line, delivered wolfishly and in response to Jaime's suspicion — presented as sardonic deflection rather than sincere declaration
