# AGOT — Daenerys VIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 65
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys VIII
- **first_available:** AGOT Daenerys VIII
- **location_primary:** Dothraki sea (unnamed stretch of brown, sere land along the march)
- **location_secondary:** Khal Drogo's tent (erected beneath a jagged outcrop of black rock)
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after the sack of the Lamb Men town; Drogo's wound has been festering for at least six days since he tore off Mirri Maz Duur's poultice
- **chapter_summary:** Khal Drogo, delirious and weakened by his festering wound, falls from his horse during the march. Dany orders the khalasar to halt and has Drogo carried into his tent, where Ser Jorah confirms the wound is mortal and urges Dany to flee to Asshai. Dany instead summons Mirri Maz Duur, who declares Drogo beyond a healer's skill but offers bloodmagic — sacrificing Drogo's red stallion in a ritual bath while chanting in an unknown tongue. When Qotho tries to stop the ritual, a violent fight erupts: Qotho wounds Ser Jorah and kills Quaro before Jorah cuts him down; Haggo is killed by Rakharo and Jhogo; Cohollo is killed by Aggo after threatening Dany. Dany goes into labor during the chaos and, with the birthing women fled, Ser Jorah carries her into the tent where Mirri Maz Duur's ritual is underway.

## Events & Actions
1. **Drogo falls from his horse** — While riding at the head of the march, Khal Drogo, weakened by his festering wound and days of delirium, falls heavily from his stallion. The bloodflies land on him where he lies.
2. **Dany commands the halt** — Despite Qotho's objection that it is not a woman's place to bid them halt, Dany orders the camp set up, telling them to say Drogo commanded it.
3. **Dany orders Mirri Maz Duur brought** — She commands Qotho to fetch the godswife, threatening that Drogo will hear of his defiance.
4. **Drogo carried into tent** — Irri, Doreah, and others help move Drogo into his tent; Dany posts her khas as guards.
5. **Ser Jorah examines the wound** — He scrapes away the mud plaster and fig leaves, revealing a black, corrupted wound seeping pus and blood. He declares Drogo is "good as dead."
6. **Ser Jorah urges Dany to flee** — He proposes escaping to Asshai and then by ship to Pentos, warning that the khalasar will tear itself apart and Drogo's heir will be killed.
7. **Mirri Maz Duur arrives and examines Drogo** — She confirms the wound has festered beyond healing and says she can only ease his death — unless Dany permits bloodmagic.
8. **Haggo strikes Mirri** — Haggo punches Mirri to the ground and kicks her; Qotho threatens her with torture, rape, and death.
9. **Dany protects Mirri** — Dany forbids them from harming Mirri; Ser Jorah draws his sword to back her. Qotho warns Dany is nothing after Drogo dies.
10. **Dany consents to bloodmagic** — After Mirri explains that "only death may pay for life," Dany agrees. Mirri says it is not Dany's death that is required.
11. **Stallion sacrificed in ritual** — Drogo is placed in the copper tub; his red stallion is brought in and Mirri slits its throat with the bronze ritual knife, chanting "Strength of the mount, go into the rider."
12. **Mirri begins the ritual dance** — Braziers are lit, red powder is added, and Mirri orders everyone out. Through the sandsilk walls, Dany sees shadows moving — some that do not look human, including a great wolf and a man wreathed in flames.
13. **Qotho attacks** — Qotho returns with Haggo, Cohollo, and the hairless men (eunuch healers). He draws his arakh and charges the tent.
14. **Quaro killed by Qotho** — Quaro tries to stop Qotho and is cut down, the arakh biting through leather, skin, muscle, and rib bone.
15. **Ser Jorah kills Qotho** — Despite being wounded (cut to face, hip gash through mail), Jorah severs Qotho's forearm when the arakh catches on bone, then delivers a killing blow to Qotho's face.
16. **Haggo killed by Rakharo and Jhogo** — Jhogo's whip coils around Haggo's throat and pulls him down; Rakharo splits his head with an arakh.
17. **Cohollo killed by Aggo** — Cohollo grabs Dany by the hair and puts a knife to her throat. Aggo shoots him with an arrow through the arm, piercing lungs and heart.
18. **Dany goes into labor** — During the fight, Dany feels sharp pain and wetness on her thighs; contractions intensify throughout the chaos.
19. **Dany is stoned by the crowd** — Someone throws stones at Dany; one tears her shoulder bloody.
20. **Birthing women refuse to come** — They say Dany is accursed and have fled.
21. **Ser Jorah carries Dany into the tent** — With the birthing women gone, and at Aggo's suggestion to take her to Mirri, Jorah carries the laboring Dany into the tent where the ritual is underway despite Dany's silent protests.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Mounting dread that gives way to desperation, grief, and terror; fierce determination to save Drogo at any cost; moments of courage ("blood of the dragon") alternating with helpless fear
- **Primary preoccupation:** Saving Khal Drogo's life; protecting her unborn son
- **Key decisions made:** Orders the khalasar to halt and lies about Drogo commanding it; overrides the bloodriders to summon Mirri; consents to bloodmagic despite Jhogo's pleas; orders Qotho killed when he attacks
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself Drogo will want his bells "when he is well"; insists "No one has died" when Mormont warns her; equates the blood sacrifice to the stallion heart ceremony at Vaes Dothrak ("This is the same. The same.")
