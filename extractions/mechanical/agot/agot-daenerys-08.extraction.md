# AGOT — Daenerys VIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 65
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys VIII
- **first_available:** AGOT Daenerys VIII
- **location_primary:** The Dothraki sea (open grassland/steppe along the march route)
- **location_secondary:** Khal Drogo's tent
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after the sack of the Lamb Men's town; Drogo's wound has been festering for at least six days since he tore off Mirri's poultice
- **time_markers:** "since they had mounted up at dawn"; "not even that much since midday"; "the heat of the afternoon sun"; "the sun was low on the horizon" at chapter's end; "He will be gone by morning" (Mirri's prognosis); "six days ago" since Drogo tore off the poultice; "these past three days" of heavy poppy wine drinking; Dany's labor begins near sunset; "The sun had set" by the chapter's final scene
- **chapter_summary:** Khal Drogo, his wound badly festered despite the herbwomen's mud plaster, falls from his horse during the march. Dany orders camp made and summons Mirri Maz Duur, overriding the bloodriders' objections. Ser Jorah examines the wound and urges Dany to flee to Asshai before Drogo dies, warning that rival kos will seize power and kill her son. Dany refuses to leave and asks Mirri to save Drogo with bloodmagic. Mirri sacrifices Drogo's red stallion in a blood ritual and begins chanting alone in the tent with shadowy shapes. The bloodriders attempt to stop the ritual — Qotho kills Quaro, then Ser Jorah kills Qotho in single combat; Rakharo and Jhogo kill Haggo; Aggo kills Cohollo. Dany goes into labor during the violence, and Ser Jorah carries her into the tent where Mirri's ritual is underway.

## Events & Actions
1. **Drogo rides in silence** — Bloodflies circle him; he does not swat them or respond. His wound is covered in a plaster of fig leaves and blue mud made by herbwomen, replacing Mirri's poultice which he tore off six days ago. He has been drinking poppy wine heavily.
2. **Drogo falls from his horse** — After Dany rides closer and touches his arm, Drogo reels in the saddle and falls heavily. Bloodflies settle on him.
3. **Dany orders camp made** — She commands the bloodriders to make camp, telling Haggo to claim Drogo ordered the halt and to say her time is near if asked. She sends Qotho to fetch Mirri Maz Duur despite his refusal.
4. **Tent is erected** — Slaves set up Drogo's tent beneath a black rock outcrop. Irri and Doreah help walk Drogo inside. Eroeh sets up a brazier. Drogo is stretched on a woven mat, muttering "No" in the Common Tongue.
5. **Dany posts guards and orders secrecy** — She forbids the tent flaps opened and posts her khas outside. Eroeh says "He dies." Dany slaps her. Jhiqui says he fell from his horse.
6. **Dany acknowledges the gravity** — She reflects that a khal who cannot ride cannot rule, and many witnessed the fall.
7. **Dany orders Drogo bathed** — She calls for the copper tub and cool water. She undoes his braid and removes his bells.
8. **Ser Jorah arrives and examines the wound** — After the handmaids leave, he scrapes away the mud plaster with his dagger, revealing black, corrupted flesh oozing blood and pus with a foul sweet smell.
9. **Ser Jorah urges flight** — He declares Drogo "good as dead" and urges Dany to flee to Asshai, then take a ship to Pentos. He warns that Jhaqo, Pono, and other kos will fight for power, Rhaego will be killed, and the bloodriders will take Dany to Vaes Dothrak before joining Drogo in death.
10. **Dany refuses to leave** — She recalls Viserys's story of Rhaegar's murdered children and declares she will not abandon Drogo.
11. **Mirri Maz Duur arrives** — Qotho and Haggo bring her with her chest. She is limping, haggard, with blistered feet. Seeing the wound, Haggo drops the chest and Qotho swears. Mirri says the wound has festered.
12. **Bloodriders attack Mirri** — Qotho calls her maegi. Haggo strikes her and kicks her. Qotho threatens to stake her out for gang rape and abuse. Dany forbids it.
13. **Qotho threatens Dany** — He says she is responsible too and suggests staking her out. Ser Jorah intervenes with hand on sword. Qotho warns she is nothing once Drogo dies. The bloodriders leave.
14. **Dany asserts her identity** — She declares "Before I was khaleesi, I was the blood of the dragon" and summons her khas. Ser Jorah warns Qotho is dangerous. Dany sends Jorah to don armor.
15. **Mirri agrees to attempt bloodmagic** — She says Drogo is beyond a healer's skills but knows a spell learned in Asshai from a bloodmage of the Shadow Lands. She warns it is dark magic with a price: "Only death may pay for life." Dany fears it means her own death, but Mirri says no. Dany agrees.
16. **Drogo is lowered into the bath** — Rakharo and Quaro place him in the tub. Drogo mutters "Must ride."
17. **The stallion is brought in and sacrificed** — Jhogo leads the red stallion into the tent. Mirri chants in an unknown tongue and produces the bronze knife. She cuts the stallion's throat, singing "Strength of the mount, go into the rider."
18. **Dany orders the carcass burned** — The men of her khas drag it out. Blood covers everything in the tent.
19. **Mirri begins the ritual and sends everyone out** — She throws red powder on brazier coals, warns that the dead will dance and no living man must look. Dany kisses Drogo and leaves.
20. **Confrontation outside the tent** — The bloodriders return with "the hairless men" (eunuch healers). Qotho declares "This must not be." Cohollo spits in Dany's face.
21. **Qotho kills Quaro** — Qotho draws his arakh and heads for the tent. Dany tries to stop him. Quaro steps forward and Qotho's arakh catches him under the arm, a fatal wound.
22. **Ser Jorah fights and kills Qotho** — Qotho is faster with the arakh but Ser Jorah's armor absorbs blows. Qotho cuts Jorah's face (half his ear), hip, and through mail. When Qotho's arakh catches on bone, Jorah severs his forearm and then destroys his face.
23. **Rakharo and Jhogo kill Haggo** — Jhogo's whip coils around Haggo's throat, pulling him down. Rakharo swings his arakh through the top of Haggo's head.
24. **Aggo kills Cohollo** — Cohollo grabs Dany by the hair and puts a knife to her throat. Aggo shoots him with an arrow under the arm, piercing lungs and heart.
25. **Stones are thrown at Dany** — Someone in the crowd throws stones, tearing her shoulder.
26. **Dany goes into labor** — She feels sharp pain in her belly and wetness on her thighs during the fight. The pain intensifies.
27. **The khalasar begins to fragment** — Dothraki steal back to their tents; some saddle horses and ride off.
28. **Birthing women refuse to come** — They say Dany is accursed and have fled.
29. **Dany glimpses shadows in the tent** — Through the sandsilk she sees shapes dancing that do not look human — a great wolf and a man wreathed in flames.
30. **Ser Jorah carries Dany into the tent** — Despite Mirri's warning that no living man should enter, Jorah carries the laboring Dany inside.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Escalating dread and fear throughout; grief at Drogo's decline; desperate determination to save him; terror during the violence; agony as labor begins; helplessness at the chapter's end
- **Primary preoccupation:** Saving Drogo's life at any cost; protecting her unborn son Rhaego
- **Key decisions made:** Orders camp made and lies about the reason; overrules bloodriders to summon Mirri; refuses Ser Jorah's counsel to flee; agrees to bloodmagic despite the unknown price; orders her khas to kill Qotho; ultimately carried into the ritual tent
- **Self-deception flags:** "She told herself she would die for him" while being relieved when told the price is not her death; tells Jhogo the stallion sacrifice is "the same" as the heart-eating ceremony in Vaes Dothrak, though she likely knows it is not; "He would want them again when he was well, she told herself" about Drogo's bells — clinging to hope against evidence; "She was more frightened than she dared admit, even to herself"
