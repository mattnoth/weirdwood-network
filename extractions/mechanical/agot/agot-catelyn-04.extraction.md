They take rooms at an inn on Eel Alley rather than going to the castle, and Ser Rodrik departs to find Ser Aron Santagar. While Catelyn sleeps, gold cloaks arrive bearing Littlefinger's seal and escort her to the Red Keep. There she meets Petyr Baelish and Lord Varys, who already knows about the dagger. Littlefinger identifies the Valyrian steel dagger as formerly his own, lost in a bet to Tyrion Lannister during a tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn and Ser Rodrik approach King's Landing** — The Storm Dancer nears the city after a difficult voyage from White Harbor, during which Ser Rodrik was severely seasick and nearly went overboard in a storm off Dragonstone.
2. **Catelyn pays the oarmen** — She gives each of sixty oarmen a silver stag and pays two men a copper each to carry their chests, despite Moreo's attempt to hold the money.
3. **Catelyn recalls Littlefinger's history** — She tells Ser Rodrik about Petyr Baelish: her father's ward at Riverrun, his unrequited feelings, his challenge to Brandon Stark, the scar Brandon gave him, his departure, and his unread letter after Brandon's death.
4. **Catelyn and Ser Rodrik observe King's Landing from the harbor** — The city is described in detail: three hills, the Great Sept of Baelor, the Dragonpit, the Red Keep, the crowded harbor.
5. **They plan their approach** — Ser Rodrik argues Catelyn should stay hidden; he will fetch Ser Aron Santagar, as his shaved whiskers make him unrecognizable. They take rooms at an inn on Eel Alley instead of going to the castle.
6. **Ser Rodrik departs to find Ser Aron** — He leaves wearing ringmail, dagger, longsword, and a dark hooded cloak, promising to return before nightfall.
7. **Catelyn sleeps and is awakened** — She falls asleep and wakes at sunset to pounding on her door by gold cloaks bearing Littlefinger's mockingbird seal.
8. **Gold cloaks escort Catelyn to the Red Keep** — She rides through lamplit streets surrounded by the City Watch, enters through a postern door, and climbs to a tower room.
9. **Catelyn meets Littlefinger** — He dismisses the guards. She confronts him about the summoning. He has changed little from boyhood — still small, slender, quick, with grey-green eyes and now a silver mockingbird clasp and threads of silver in his dark hair.
10. **Littlefinger explains Varys found her** — He tells her Varys's "little birds" detected her arrival. He claims he was the logical person for Varys to inform since Selmy and Renly rode north, Stannis is on Dragonstone, leaving only Pycelle and himself.
11. **Lord Varys arrives** — He enters plump, perfumed, powdered, and hairless. He offers sympathy about Bran and healing help. He already knows about the dagger before Catelyn shows it.
12. **Catelyn suspects Moreo informed on them** — She deduces the Tyroshi captain sold information about their identity and location.
13. **Varys asks to see the dagger** — He knows about it, stunning Catelyn. He explains Ser Rodrik visited Ser Aron Santagar at the armory, they discussed the dagger, then went to the inn together and are now drinking in the common room.
14. **Catelyn produces the dagger** — Varys examines it, cuts his thumb on the Valyrian steel blade.
15. **Littlefinger identifies the dagger** — He claims it was his, lost in a wager when Loras Tyrell unhorsed Ser Jaime at the tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day. He names Tyrion Lannister as the winner and current owner.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Tense and anxious throughout; pain in her hands a constant reminder of the attempt on Bran's life; wary and distrustful of those she encounters in King's Landing; stunned by Varys's knowledge; fearful when the dagger's owner is named
- **Primary preoccupation:** Identifying the owner of the Valyrian steel dagger and reaching trustworthy people at court; protecting her mission's secrecy
- **Key decisions made:** Insisted on hiring the faster galley over a fishing sloop; chose to stay at an inn rather than the castle; paid the oarmen directly despite Moreo's objection; agreed to go with the gold cloaks after seeing Littlefinger's seal; showed the dagger to Varys and Littlefinger
- **Self-deception flags:** Trusts Littlefinger's identification of the dagger's owner without question, despite acknowledging she trusts him "only a little"; her judgment of Littlefinger — "it is one thing to be clever and another to be wise" — may reflect insufficient caution about his current motives
