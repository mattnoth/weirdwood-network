- **chapter_summary:** Sansa attends the first court session of King Joffrey's reign in the throne room. Joffrey and the council issue decrees: summoning lords to swear fealty or be declared traitors, naming Tywin Lannister as Hand, seating Cersei on the small council, elevating Janos Slynt to lord of Harrenhal and a council seat, dismissing Ser Barristan Selmy from the Kingsguard, naming Jaime Lannister as Lord Commander, and appointing Sandor Clegane to the Kingsguard. Ser Barristan strips off his armor in defiance and departs. Sansa then steps forward to plead for mercy for her father Eddard Stark, and Joffrey agrees to grant it on the condition that Eddard confess his crimes and acknowledge Joffrey as king.

## Events & Actions
1. **Throne room stripped** — The hunting tapestries King Robert loved have been taken down and stacked in a heap; the walls are bare.
2. **Sansa enters the throne room** — She enters unguarded for once, having been given "freedom of the castle" by Cersei, though she is normally escorted by honor guards. She notes the gates are watched by gold cloaks and Lannister guards.
3. **Sansa mingles with lords** — She tries to greet lords and knights but is shunned; Lord Gyles feigns coughing, Ser Dontos is warned off by Ser Balon Swann. Only about twenty lords are present where a hundred used to attend Robert.
4. **Council members arrive** — Grand Maester Pycelle is already seated; Varys enters quietly; Littlefinger enters smiling and chats with Ser Balon and Ser Dontos.
5. **Royal entrance** — Joffrey and Cersei enter, led by Ser Barristan Selmy; Ser Arys Oakheart escorts the queen, Ser Boros Blount walks beside Joffrey. Six of seven Kingsguard are present (Jaime absent). Joffrey takes the Iron Throne.
6. **Traitor decree read** — Pycelle reads a long list of names commanded to present themselves and swear fealty or be declared traitors with lands and titles forfeit. Names include Stannis, Renly, Tyrells, Tullys, Arryns, Starks, Martells, and many others. Sansa is shocked to hear her family named, including Arya.
7. **Tywin named Hand** — Pycelle reads decree naming Tywin Lannister as Hand of the King in place of "the traitor Eddard Stark."
8. **Cersei seated on council** — Pycelle reads decree seating Cersei on the small council in place of "the traitor Stannis Baratheon."
9. **Janos Slynt elevated** — Pycelle reads decree raising Slynt to lord, granting him Harrenhal and a council seat. Slynt enters with sons carrying his new shield bearing a bloody spear sigil. Lords mutter angrily but make way.
10. **Ser Barristan dismissed** — Cersei announces Barristan is relieved as Lord Commander of the Kingsguard. Barristan protests that Kingsguard vows are for life. Joffrey accuses him of letting Robert die and being too old. Janos Slynt bluntly tells him he is dismissed. Varys offers him lands near Lannisport.
11. **Barristan's defiance** — Ser Barristan spits upon their pity, strips off his white cloak, helm, and breastplate. He declares he is a knight and shall die a knight. Littlefinger quips "a naked knight." Everyone laughs, including the other Kingsguard.
12. **Barristan draws his sword** — He freezes Ser Boros and Ser Meryn with contempt, declares he could cut through all five remaining Kingsguard, calls them unfit to wear the white under the Kingslayer, and flings his sword at the foot of the Iron Throne. He tells Joffrey to melt it down and suggests Stannis will sit the throne.
13. **Barristan departs** — He walks out through the silent hall; lords part for him; the doors close behind him.
14. **Joffrey reacts** — Joffrey complains Barristan called him "boy" and mentioned Stannis. Varys dismisses it as idle talk. Joffrey demands Barristan be seized; Janos Slynt volunteers his gold cloaks and departs with his sons.
15. **Sandor Clegane appointed to Kingsguard** — Cersei announces Sandor Clegane will join the Kingsguard. The Hound accepts but refuses to take knight's vows. Ser Boros objects but is silenced.
16. **Jaime named Lord Commander** — Cersei announces Jaime Lannister will replace Barristan as Lord Commander of the Kingsguard.
17. **Sansa pleads for Eddard** — Sansa steps forward and kneels on Barristan's discarded cloak. She asks for mercy for her father. Pycelle calls treason a noxious weed. Littlefinger asks if she denies her father's crime. Sansa says she doesn't deny it but asks for mercy, suggesting he was deceived and affected by milk of the poppy.
18. **Joffrey grants conditional mercy** — Joffrey says Sansa's words have moved him and he will show mercy, but only if Eddard confesses his crime and acknowledges Joffrey as king. Sansa believes Eddard will confess.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Nervous, fearful, hopeful, desperate. Sansa is terrified of the court session but summons courage to plead for her father. She oscillates between dread and hope, reassuring herself repeatedly that Joffrey loves her. She feels sympathy for Ser Barristan ("her heart went out to the gallant old man"). She ends elated when Joffrey conditionally agrees to mercy.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Saving her father; maintaining composure in a hostile court where she is shunned by lords who once knew her.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to step forward and publicly plead for mercy for Eddard Stark. Decides not to deny her father's crime but to ask for mercy and provide excuses (milk of the poppy, being deceived).
- **Self-deception flags:** Repeatedly tells herself "Joff loves me and the queen does too, she said so"; "He does love me, he does"; "Please, please, be the king I know you are, good and kind and noble." She interprets Joffrey's conditional agreement as genuine compassion. She convinces herself Eddard will confess ("Oh, I know he will") with her heart soaring.
