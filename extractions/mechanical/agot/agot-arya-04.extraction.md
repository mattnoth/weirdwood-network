- **chapter_summary:** Arya trains with Syrio Forel in the Small Hall when Ser Meryn Trant and five Lannister guardsmen arrive to seize her. Syrio fights them with his wooden sword, defeating all five guardsmen, but faces Ser Meryn in full armor as Arya flees. Arya discovers the Stark household has been attacked — finding dead guards and the dying Hullen in the stables. A stableboy tries to capture her for the queen, and Arya kills him with Needle. She steals candles from the royal sept and escapes through the underground passages beneath the Red Keep, past the dragon skulls, heading into darkness.

## Events & Actions
1. **Syrio Forel trains Arya in the Small Hall** — They spar with wooden swords; Syrio tests Arya by calling left but striking right, hitting her in the breast. He teaches her that "watching is not seeing."
2. **Syrio tells the story of the Sealord's cat** — He explains how he became first sword of Braavos by correctly identifying an ordinary fat tomcat that other bravos mistook for an exotic beast, teaching Arya the lesson of true seeing.
3. **Syrio mentions reaching Winterfell** — He says when they reach Winterfell it will be time to put Needle in Arya's hand. Arya excitedly mentions Jon.
4. **Ser Meryn Trant and five Lannister guardsmen burst into the Small Hall** — Ser Meryn claims Eddard wants to see Arya. Syrio challenges why Lannisters come instead of Stark men. Arya refuses to go.
5. **Syrio fights the five Lannister guardsmen** — Using only his wooden sword, he breaks one man's fingers, kicks another in the face, puts out a third's eye, shatters a fourth's kneecap, and causes one guard to accidentally kill another. All five are down, dead, or dying.
6. **Syrio faces Ser Meryn Trant** — Ser Meryn draws his longsword in full armor. Syrio fights with his wooden stick, landing blows on helm, gauntlet, and gorget, but the fourth slash from Ser Meryn's steel sword splinters the stick in two, shearing through its lead core.
7. **Arya flees through the kitchens** — She bowls over a baker's helper with bread, runs past a butcher, descends a turret stair to an ale cellar, then climbs out a window.
8. **Arya sees the Tower of the Hand under attack** — The door is splintered, a dead guardsman in Stark grey-and-white lies on the steps, and fighting sounds come from within.
9. **Arya recalls the warning about a second Hand dying** — She remembers the man with the yellow beard's words from the underground passage.
10. **Arya finds Hullen dying at the stables** — The master of horse has been stabbed many times; he whispers for Arya to warn her father, then dies.
11. **Arya discovers more dead inside the stables** — A groom, three household guards, and Desmond are dead, along with one Lannister soldier. A wagon packed for departure stands abandoned.
12. **Arya retrieves Needle from her clothing chest** — The chest fell and broke open during the fighting; she digs through scattered clothes and finds Needle at the bottom.
13. **The stableboy confronts Arya** — He recognizes her as "the wolf girl" and tries to seize her for the queen's reward, claiming her father is dead.
14. **Arya kills the stableboy with Needle** — She drives the blade upward through his belly and out between his shoulder blades. He asks her to take it out; when she does, he dies. This is Arya's first kill.
15. **Arya abandons her plan to ride out** — She realizes the castle gates will be closed and guarded.
16. **Arya crosses the yard to the royal sept** — She walks slowly across the open yard despite gold cloaks on the walls, using Syrio's calm. She steals two prayer candles from the empty sept.
17. **Arya navigates back to the underground passages** — She finds the alley where she cornered the one-eared tom, gets lost, crawls through windows, hops walls, and after more than an hour finds the window down to the dragon skull dungeon.
18. **Arya enters the dragon skull dungeon and continues underground** — She lights a candle, passes the dragon skulls (calling them "Dragons"), and heads right (away from the well entrance) into the dark passages, choosing to go deeper rather than back.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Begins confident and eager during training; shifts to fear and panic when Lannisters arrive; grief upon seeing dead household members; horror and guilt after killing the stableboy; grim resolve as she escapes underground. The chapter traces an arc from childhood play to traumatic violence to survival instinct.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival — escaping the Lannisters and finding/warning her father. Secondary: processing Syrio's likely death and her own first kill.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Trusts Syrio's instinct that the Lannisters are not sent by her father; (2) Goes down the turret stair instead of up toward the expected route; (3) Kills the stableboy rather than submitting; (4) Abandons the plan to ride out through the gates; (5) Chooses the underground passage beneath the Red Keep as her escape route; (6) Keeps her candle lit despite fear (fear cuts deeper than swords).
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself "Everything would be better once she was home again, safe behind Winterfell's grey granite walls" — clinging to the idea that Winterfell means safety. Also unsure whether Syrio is dead: "the white knight had probably killed him" — the uncertainty is real, not self-deception, but she pushes past it to keep moving.
