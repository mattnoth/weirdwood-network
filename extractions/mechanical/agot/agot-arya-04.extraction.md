## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 51
- **pov_character:** Arya Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya IV
- **first_available:** AGOT Arya IV
- **location_primary:** The Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** The Small Hall; stables; cellars and tunnels beneath the Red Keep; the royal sept; the dungeon with the dragon skulls
- **approximate_timeline:** During the violent seizure of Stark household members in the Red Keep; Arya's last chapter in King's Landing before fleeing
- **chapter_summary:** Arya is practicing swordplay with Syrio Forel in the Small Hall when Ser Meryn Trant and five Lannister guardsmen arrive to seize her. Syrio fights them with his wooden sword, defeating all five guardsmen while Arya flees. Arya discovers the Stark household has been attacked — she finds Hullen dying at the stables and several dead Stark men including Desmond. A stableboy tries to capture her for the queen, and Arya kills him with Needle. Unable to leave through the castle gates, she escapes through the underground tunnels beneath the Red Keep using the route she discovered while chasing cats.

## Events & Actions
1. **Syrio and Arya's training session** — Syrio drills Arya with called strikes, catches her with a misdirection ("said left and went right"), and teaches her the lesson of "true seeing" through the story of the Sealord's cat.
2. **Arrival of Ser Meryn Trant and Lannister guardsmen** — A Kingsguard knight and five Lannister soldiers in red cloaks enter the Small Hall demanding Arya come with them, claiming her father sent for her.
3. **Syrio challenges the Lannisters** — Syrio questions why Lord Eddard would send Lannister men; Arya refuses to go. Syrio steps between Arya and the guardsmen.
4. **Syrio defeats five Lannister guardsmen** — Using his wooden sword, Syrio breaks one man's fingers, blinds another (thrusts through left eye), shatters one's kneecap, and causes two others to die (one from friendly fire, one from throat strike). Five men are down, dead or dying.
5. **Ser Meryn draws his longsword against Syrio** — After all five guardsmen fall, Ser Meryn engages Syrio personally. Syrio's wooden sword is cut in two. Arya flees, not seeing the outcome.
6. **Arya's flight through the Red Keep** — She runs through kitchens, knocks over a baker's helper, descends a turret stair to the cellar, and climbs out through a narrow window.
7. **Discovery of the attack on the Tower of the Hand** — Arya sees the splintered door, a dead man on the steps in Stark colors, and hears fighting inside.
8. **Finding Hullen dying** — Hullen, stabbed many times, tells Arya to warn her father before losing consciousness.
9. **Discovery of dead Stark men in the stables** — Arya finds a groom she knew, three household guards including Desmond, and one dead Lannister guardsman.
10. **Arya kills the stableboy** — When a stableboy recognizes her and tries to seize her for the queen, Arya stabs him with Needle. He dies when she withdraws the blade.
11. **Arya abandons the plan to ride out** — She realizes the castle gates will be closed and guarded.
12. **Escape through the tunnels** — Arya crosses the yard undetected, takes two candles from the royal sept, finds the tunnel entrance, passes through the dragon skull dungeon, and heads into the darkness beneath the Red Keep.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Progresses from confidence and joy during training → confusion and fear at Lannister arrival → panic during flight → grief and terror at discovering dead household members → cold resolve as she escapes through the tunnels
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and escape; desire to reach her father and warn him; later, getting home to Winterfell
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Refuses to go with Ser Meryn; (2) Flees when Syrio tells her to run; (3) Kills the stableboy with Needle; (4) Abandons plan to ride out through the gates; (5) Chooses to escape through the underground tunnels
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself "Everything would be better once she was home again" — a comforting belief with no basis in current evidence
