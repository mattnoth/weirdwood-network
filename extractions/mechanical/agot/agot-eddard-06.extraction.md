# AGOT — Eddard VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 28
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark

- **location_primary:** King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Tower of the Hand, the Street of Steel, Tobho Mott's shop and forge
- **approximate_timeline:** During the lead-up to the Hand's tourney; shortly after Ned's arrival at court
- **time_markers:** "last night" (referring to crimes in the city); "the night before" (woman's head found in the Great Sept); "a few days past" (Renly showed Ned the locket); "early this morning" (Tomard and Desmond left the castle to take up positions); references to the heat and the upcoming tourney
- **chapter_summary:** Ned presides over a small council session where Janos Slynt reports on the crime surge caused by tourney crowds, and Ned orders fifty new watchmen hired and loans twenty of his own guard. Afterward, Ned reviews the book of lineages in his chambers and discusses with Jory Cassel the results of questioning four people Littlefinger identified who knew Jon Arryn — a stableboy/watchman, Ser Hugh, a serving girl, and a potboy. Learning that Jon Arryn and Stannis Baratheon visited a brothel together, Ned rides to the Street of Steel to visit master armorer Tobho Mott, where he meets Gendry, a black-haired, blue-eyed apprentice boy whom Jon Arryn and Stannis had also visited. Ned recognizes something in the boy's appearance and concludes Gendry is a king's bastard.

## Events & Actions
1. **Council session on tourney disorder** — Janos Slynt reports to the small council on the surge of crime and overcrowding caused by the Hand's tourney: drowning, tavern riot, knife fights, rape, fires, robberies, a drunken horse race, and a severed woman's head found in the Great Sept's rainbow pool.
2. **Ned authorizes reinforcements** — Ned orders fifty new City Watchmen hired (Littlefinger to provide coin) and loans twenty of his own household guard under Alyn's command.
3. **Renly mocks Stannis** — Renly jokes about Stannis's proposal to outlaw brothels and his joyless marriage, provoking laughter from the council.
4. **Ned expresses interest in Stannis's absence** — Ned asks when Stannis intends to return from Dragonstone to resume his council seat.
5. **Ned studies the lineage book** — In his chambers, Ned reads the Lineages and Histories, focusing on House Lannister, searching for whatever Jon Arryn sought in it.
6. **Jory reports investigation results** — Jory recounts what was learned from the four people Littlefinger identified: the stableboy/watchman (Jon Arryn was kind to horses, rode often with Stannis, visited a brothel), Ser Hugh (refused to talk to a mere captain), the serving girl (Jon Arryn was reading heavily, troubled about his son's frailty, gruff with his wife), and the potboy (kitchen gossip including the commissioned armor and that Stannis accompanied Jon Arryn to the armorer).
7. **Ned decides to visit the armorer** — Based on the potboy's information about the commissioned armor and Stannis's involvement, Ned decides to visit the armorer personally.
8. **Ned orders Jory to investigate the brothel** — Ned tells Jory to begin visiting whorehouses to find which one Jon Arryn and Stannis visited.
9. **Ned rides through King's Landing** — Ned rides with Varly and Jacks from the Red Keep through the King's Gate into the city streets, watchful for anyone following; Tomard and Desmond have been positioned along the route.
10. **Lord Beric arrives at the Mud Gate** — Lord Beric Dondarrion rides through the Mud Gate with an escort bearing a black banner with purple lightning, declaring he's come to win the Hand's tourney.
11. **Ned climbs the Street of Steel** — Ned rides up the winding Street of Steel past blacksmiths, freeriders, and ironmongers to Tobho Mott's shop at the top.
12. **Ned meets Tobho Mott** — The master armorer receives Ned with wine and boasts of his craftsmanship, his clientele (Knight of Flowers, Lord Renly), and his ability to work Valyrian steel learned at Qohor.
13. **Tobho reveals Jon Arryn's visit** — When Ned asks about a falcon helm, Tobho admits Jon Arryn came with Lord Stannis but did not patronize his shop — they asked to see "the boy."
14. **Ned meets Gendry** — Tobho brings Ned to the forge to meet Gendry, a muscular apprentice about Robb's age with thick black hair and blue eyes. Gendry shows his bull-head helm and stubbornly refuses to sell it.
15. **Gendry describes Jon Arryn's questions** — Gendry says Jon Arryn asked about his wellbeing, his mother (who had yellow hair, sang to him, worked in an alehouse, and died when he was little), and how he was treated. Stannis ("the bald one") only glared.
16. **Ned recognizes something in Gendry** — Ned studies the shape of Gendry's jaw, his blue-ice eyes, and thinks "Yes, I see it."
17. **Ned learns who paid Gendry's fee** — Tobho reluctantly reveals an unnamed lord paid twice the customary apprentice fee in gold: stout, round-shouldered, brown beard with red in it, wearing a rich purple velvet cloak with silver threads and a shadowing hood.
18. **Ned departs** — Ned tells Tobho to send Gendry to him if the boy ever wants to wield a sword. Outside, Ned wonders what Jon Arryn wanted with a king's bastard and why it was worth his life.
