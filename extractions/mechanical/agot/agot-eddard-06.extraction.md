# AGOT — Eddard VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 28
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard VI
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard VI
- **location_primary:** King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Red Keep (council chambers, Tower of the Hand), Street of Steel, Tobho Mott's shop
- **approximate_timeline:** During the lead-up to the Hand's tourney; the city is swelling with visitors
- **chapter_summary:** Ned presides over a small council meeting where Janos Slynt reports rising crime due to tourney crowds; Ned authorizes fifty new watchmen and twenty of his own guard. Afterward, Ned reviews intelligence Jory gathered from four people Littlefinger identified who knew Jon Arryn. The most intriguing lead—that Jon Arryn and Stannis Baratheon visited a brothel and an armorer together—sends Ned to the Street of Steel to visit master armorer Tobho Mott. There, Ned meets Gendry, a black-haired, blue-eyed apprentice boy whom Jon Arryn and Stannis had previously come to see. Ned recognizes something in Gendry's appearance and concludes Gendry is one of the king's bastards, wondering why Jon Arryn's interest in the boy cost him his life.

## Events & Actions
1. **Council meeting on tourney security** — Janos Slynt reports surging crime in King's Landing due to the Hand's tourney crowds. Ned authorizes hiring fifty new City Watchmen and assigns twenty of his own household guard under Alyn's command.
2. **Ned studies the lineages book** — In his chambers, Ned reads the Lannister section of Malleon's tome, searching for whatever truth Jon Arryn sought in it.
3. **Jory reports on the four witnesses** — Ned reviews what Jory learned from four people Littlefinger identified who knew Jon Arryn: Ser Hugh (unhelpful), a serving girl (Jon Arryn was troubled, reading heavily, gruff with his wife), a potboy/cordwainer (kitchen gossip — quarrels with the king, fostering plans for his son, commissioned armor with Stannis, interest in hunting hounds), and a watchman/stableboy (Jon Arryn visited a brothel with Stannis and three guards).
4. **Ned rides to the Street of Steel** — Ned takes guards through the city to Tobho Mott's shop, with Tomard and Desmond posted along the route to watch for anyone following.
5. **Lord Beric Dondarrion arrives at King's Landing** — Rides through the Mud Gate with his banner and escort, declaring he will win the Hand's tourney.
6. **Ned meets Tobho Mott** — The master armorer boasts of his craft, his famous clients, and his ability to work Valyrian steel. He confirms Jon Arryn and Stannis came to his shop but did not commission armor from him — they asked to see "the boy."
7. **Ned meets Gendry** — Ned sees the apprentice: black hair, blue eyes, strong build, about Robb's age. Gendry tells Ned that Jon Arryn asked about his welfare and his mother. Ned studies Gendry's features and recognizes something.
8. **Ned learns about Gendry's apprentice fee** — Tobho Mott reveals an unnamed lord paid double the usual fee in gold — once for the boy, once for Mott's silence. The man was stout, had a brown-red beard, wore a rich purple velvet cloak with silver threads, and kept his hood up.
9. **Ned concludes Gendry is the king's bastard** — Ned wonders what Jon Arryn wanted with "a king's bastard" and why it cost him his life.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Frustrated, homesick, wary. The tourney irritates him; the investigation into Jon Arryn's death consumes him. He yearns for Winterfell, Catelyn, and his sons.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Discovering why Jon Arryn died — specifically why Jon Arryn and Stannis were investigating Gendry and visiting brothels.
- **Key decisions made:** Authorizes fifty new watchmen and twenty of his own guard for tourney security. Decides not to summon Stannis from Dragonstone yet. Orders Jory to begin visiting brothels to trace Jon Arryn's steps.
- **Self-deception flags:** None apparent. Ned is clear-eyed in his suspicions but puzzled by the evidence.
