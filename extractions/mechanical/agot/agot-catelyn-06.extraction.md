# AGOT — Catelyn VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 35
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn VI
- **first_available:** AGOT Catelyn VI
- **location_primary:** The Vale of Arryn (the high road, the Bloody Gate, the Gates of the Moon, the mountain ascent, the Eyrie)
- **location_secondary:** Riverrun (recalled), King's Landing (referenced), Winterfell (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the journey from the crossroads inn through the Mountains of the Moon; the party has survived clansmen attacks and reaches the Vale
- **time_markers:** Morning light when entering the Vale; sun well to the west when reaching the valley floor; full dark when reaching the Gates of the Moon; night ascent through Stone, Snow, and Sky waycastles; dawn breaking at Sky; sun well above the mountains when reaching the Eyrie
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn's diminished party is met in the mountain pass by Ser Donnel Waynwood and Arryn men-at-arms. They pass through the Bloody Gate, where Catelyn reunites with her uncle Brynden Tully, the Knight of the Gate. As they descend into the Vale, Catelyn tells Brynden everything — Lysa's letter, Bran's fall, the dagger, Littlefinger, and Tyrion's capture. Brynden warns her that Lysa has changed and may not be helpful. They reach the Gates of the Moon, where Lysa demands Catelyn ascend immediately. The bastard girl Mya Stone guides Catelyn up the mountain through three waycastles. At the Eyrie, Lysa greets her warmly but immediately turns hostile, accusing Catelyn of dragging the Vale into her quarrels. Lysa's sickly six-year-old son Robert Arryn appears, still nursing at his mother's breast, and suggests they make Tyrion "fly."

## Events & Actions
1. **Ser Donnel Waynwood meets Catelyn's party in the mountain pass** — He chides them for not sending word ahead and reports the mountain clans have grown bolder since Jon Arryn's death. He mentions Lysa has forbidden knights to leave the Vale.
2. **Catelyn reflects on Tyrion's ambiguous status** — Though nominally her prisoner, Tyrion rides armed with weapons taken from the dead and won gambling, with Bronn as his close companion. She doubts whether he is truly guilty.
3. **Catelyn requests Maester Colemon for Ser Rodrik** — Ser Donnel tells her Lysa has commanded the maester to remain at the Eyrie to care for Lord Robert; only a septon is available.
4. **The party reaches the Bloody Gate** — A knight rides out to challenge them. He is revealed to be Brynden Tully, Catelyn's uncle, the Knight of the Gate.
5. **Brynden formally admits them to the Vale** — Using the ritual formula in the name of Robert Arryn.
6. **Catelyn enters the Vale of Arryn** — She sees the valley floor, the Giant's Lance, Alyssa's Tears, and the distant white flash of the Eyrie.
7. **Ser Rodrik declares he can go no farther** — Catelyn leaves him, Ser Willis Wode, and others at the Bloody Gate to rest. Marillion and Bronn insist on continuing.
8. **Catelyn tells Brynden everything during the descent** — Lysa's letter, Bran's fall, the assassin's dagger, Littlefinger, and the capture of Tyrion.
9. **Brynden warns Catelyn about Lysa** — He describes Lysa as changed, fearful, and unlikely to be helpful. He mentions Robert Arryn's weakness, Nestor Royce as high steward for fourteen years, and suitors gathering for Lysa's hand.
10. **Brynden warns about Riverrun's vulnerability** — He notes that if the Lannisters march, Riverrun lies in their path.
11. **The party crosses the valley floor** — They ride through greenwoods, hamlets, orchards, wheat fields, and streams, arriving at the Gates of the Moon after dark.
12. **Lord Nestor Royce greets them** — Catelyn invokes hospitality. Nestor tells her Lysa demands she ascend immediately.
13. **Mya Stone volunteers to guide Catelyn up** — The bastard girl of seventeen or eighteen is confident and capable.
14. **Catelyn reacts to Mya's bastard name** — She thinks of Jon Snow on the Wall, feeling angry and guilty.
15. **Tyrion is confined to a tower cell** — Lord Nestor orders him sent to a tower cell with meat and mead.
16. **Night ascent begins** — Catelyn and Mya ride mules up the carved mountain steps through dense forest.
17. **Mya tells Catelyn about Mychel Redfort** — Her love, a squire to Ser Lyn Corbray. Catelyn sadly reflects that no Redfort would wed a bastard.
18. **They reach Stone waycastle** — Catelyn eats charred meat and onions; they switch to fresh mules.
19. **They reach Snow waycastle** — Offered bread, cheese, and fire; they decline and continue.
20. **The ascent grows dangerous** — Steep trail, worn steps, fierce wind. Mya dismounts repeatedly to clear fallen rocks.
21. **Catelyn freezes on the narrow saddle crossing** — Overcome by vertigo and fear on a twenty-foot path with precipitous drops on both sides. Mya returns and leads her across blind and trembling.
22. **They reach Sky waycastle** — Dawn is breaking. Catelyn decides to ride up in the basket with the supplies rather than climb the chimney.
23. **Catelyn arrives at the Eyrie** — Ser Vardis Egen and Maester Colemon greet her. The castle seems strangely deserted.
24. **Lysa greets Catelyn warmly, then turns hostile** — After embracing her sister and dismissing attendants, Lysa accuses Catelyn of dragging the Vale into her quarrels with the Lannisters.
25. **Catelyn confronts Lysa** — Reminds her it was Lysa's letter that warned the Lannisters had murdered Jon Arryn. Lysa says the letter was only meant as a warning to stay away.
26. **Robert Arryn appears** — Sickly, thin, clutching a cloth doll. He climbs into Lysa's lap and nurses at her breast despite being six years old.
27. **Lysa recalls Jon Arryn's last words** — "The seed is strong." She interprets this as Jon affirming Robert's health and strength.
28. **Robert Arryn suggests making Tyrion "fly"** — Lysa murmurs "Perhaps we will."
