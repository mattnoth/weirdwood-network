# AGOT — Catelyn VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 35
- **pov_character:** Catelyn
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn VI
- **first_available:** AGOT Catelyn VI
- **location_primary:** The Vale of Arryn — the high road, the Bloody Gate, the Gates of the Moon, the mountain ascent, and the Eyrie
- **location_secondary:** Riverrun (recalled), King's Landing (referenced), Winterfell (referenced), the Wall (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the mountain road journey; Catelyn's party arrives at the Bloody Gate and ascends to the Eyrie within roughly a day and a night
- **time_markers:** Morning light when they pass through the Bloody Gate into the Vale; sun well to the west when they reach the valley floor; full dark when they reach the Gates of the Moon; night ascent through Stone, Snow, and Sky; dawn breaking as they reach Sky; sun well above the mountains when Catelyn reaches the Eyrie
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn's diminished party reaches the Bloody Gate escorted by Ser Donnel Waynwood, where she reunites with her uncle Brynden Tully, the Knight of the Gate. They descend into the Vale, and Catelyn tells Brynden everything about Lysa's letter, Bran's fall, the assassin's dagger, and Tyrion's capture. Brynden warns her that Lysa has changed and may not be helpful. At the Gates of the Moon, Lord Nestor Royce receives them, but Lysa demands Catelyn ascend immediately. The bastard girl Mya Stone guides Catelyn on a harrowing night climb through three waycastles — Stone, Snow, and Sky — to the Eyrie. There, Lysa furiously rebukes Catelyn for bringing Tyrion to the Vale, reveals she never intended to fight the Lannisters, and nurses her sickly six-year-old son Robert Arryn at her breast while he suggests making Tyrion "fly."

- **Season indicators:** Mya mentions her mother said it used to snow at the level of the waycastle Snow, but she has never seen snow that far down in her lifetime; the snow crown begins at Sky, with frost and long spears of ice; Catelyn thinks "Winter is coming" but does not say it aloud
- **Time of day:** Morning (passing the Bloody Gate into the Vale); afternoon/evening (descent and ride across valley floor); full dark (arrival at Gates of the Moon); night (the ascent); dawn (reaching Sky); morning (reaching the Eyrie)

- **Sounds:** Wind howling, shrieking, skirling; scuffling of leather on stone; quiet of the pine forest path where mule shoes made only the softest sound on rock; Lysa's raised voice in the solar

## Events & Actions
1. **Ser Donnel Waynwood escorts Catelyn's party through the mountain pass** — He remarks that the high road is not safe and the clans have grown bolder since Lord Jon died; he reveals Lysa has forbidden her knights from fighting the clansmen or attending the Hand's tourney.
2. **Catelyn reflects on her prisoner** — She observes that Tyrion is armed with a dirk and axe, wears Chiggen's chainmail and Marillion's shadowskin cloak, and has grown close with Bronn; she doubts whether he is truly guilty.
3. **Catelyn requests Maester Colemon for Ser Rodrik** — Ser Donnel tells her Lysa has commanded the maester to remain at the Eyrie at all times; a septon at the gate tends to wounded instead.
4. **Arrival at the Bloody Gate** — The party reaches the fortified pass; a knight rides out and calls the traditional challenge; it is Brynden Tully, Catelyn's uncle.
5. **Catelyn reunites with Brynden Tully** — He recognizes her, calls her "little Cat"; she asks him to remove his helm; they share a warm greeting.
6. **Brynden grants passage through the Bloody Gate** — He invokes the name of Robert Arryn and his titles to bid them enter freely.
7. **The party enters the Vale** — Catelyn sees the Vale of Arryn spread before them in the morning light; she spots the Giant's Lance, Alyssa's Tears, and a distant flash of the Eyrie.
8. **Ser Rodrik declares he can go no farther** — He is too weak and feverish; Catelyn arranges for him, Ser Willis Wode, and others to remain at the Bloody Gate.
9. **Marillion asks to accompany Catelyn to the Eyrie** — She grants permission; Bronn announces he will come as well; Catelyn dislikes this but cannot graciously refuse.
10. **The party descends to the valley floor** — Catelyn rides beside Brynden; Bronn, Tyrion, Marillion, and six of Brynden's men follow.
11. **Catelyn tells Brynden everything** — She recounts Lysa's letter, Bran's fall, the assassin's dagger, Littlefinger, and her capture of Tyrion at the crossroads inn.
12. **Brynden warns Catelyn about Lysa** — He describes Lysa as changed, afraid, and unlikely to be helpful; he reports Lord Robert is sickly and prone to weeping; Nestor Royce has governed as high steward for fourteen years; suitors are gathering.
13. **Brynden expresses concern about Tyrion's lack of chains** — He notes the axe, dirk, and sellsword trailing Tyrion; he doubts Lysa will react well.
14. **Arrival at the Gates of the Moon** — They reach the castle at full dark; Lord Nestor Royce greets them.
15. **Lysa demands Catelyn ascend immediately** — Lord Nestor relays that Lysa wishes to see Catelyn at once; Brynden calls the night ascent madness.
16. **Mya Stone offers to guide the ascent** — The wiry seventeen-year-old girl in riding leathers and silvered ringmail volunteers; Lord Nestor vouches for her.
17. **Catelyn reacts to Mya's bastard surname** — She struggles to keep her composure; the name "Stone" makes her think of Jon Snow on the Wall; she feels angry and guilty.
18. **Tyrion is taken to a tower cell** — Lord Nestor orders meat and mead brought to him; Catelyn charges Lord Nestor to keep a close guard.
19. **Night ascent begins** — Catelyn and Mya ride mules through a dense forest of pine and spruce; Mya claims she has owl-like night vision.
20. **Mya speaks of Mychel Redfort** — She says he is her love and they will wed; Catelyn privately doubts a Redfort would ever wed a bastard, comparing Mya's innocent hopes to Sansa's dreams.
21. **Arrival at Stone waycastle** — They eat charred meat and onions; saddles are moved to fresh mules.
22. **Ascent continues to Snow** — The trail grows steeper and more treacherous; Mya dismounts repeatedly to clear fallen rocks; the wind grows stronger.
23. **Arrival at Snow waycastle** — They decline bread, cheese, and fire; take fresh mules; Catelyn's new mule is white ("Whitey").
24. **Mya mentions the snow line used to be lower** — Catelyn thinks about winter coming and nearly speaks the Stark words aloud.
25. **The narrow crossing between spires** — Catelyn freezes on a twenty-foot-long, three-foot-wide path with precipitous drops; she cannot move; Mya comes back and leads her across step by step with eyes closed.
26. **Arrival at Sky waycastle** — Dawn is breaking; the snow crown begins; frost and ice spears; mouth of a cave leads to stables and barracks inside the mountain.
27. **Catelyn chooses the basket** — She decides to ride up the final six hundred feet in a winch basket rather than climb the chimney-like stone ladder.
28. **Arrival at the Eyrie** — Ser Vardis Egen and Maester Colemon greet her; the castle seems strangely deserted.
29. **Catelyn meets Lysa** — Lysa initially embraces her warmly, then immediately turns hostile once the door closes, accusing Catelyn of dragging them into her quarrels with the Lannisters.
30. **Lysa reveals she never meant to fight** — She says she sent the letter only to warn Catelyn to stay away from the Lannisters, not to provoke conflict.
31. **Robert Arryn enters** — The sickly six-year-old appears clutching a cloth doll; Lysa introduces Catelyn; Robert does not remember her.
32. **Lysa nurses Robert** — She bares her breast and the six-year-old boy suckles; Catelyn is incredulous, comparing him unfavorably to three-year-old Rickon.
33. **Robert suggests making Tyrion "fly"** — Lysa murmurs "Perhaps we will."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Catelyn cycles through exhaustion, numbness ("heart had turned to stone"), warmth at reuniting with Brynden, doubt about Tyrion's guilt, irritation at Bronn's closeness with Tyrion, nostalgia for childhood, physical fear during the mountain crossing (vertigo, cold sweat, trembling), anger and disbelief at Lysa's reaction, and incredulity at Lysa nursing six-year-old Robert.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting Tyrion to the Eyrie to face judgment; securing Lysa's alliance and support against the Lannisters; worry about the coming conflict and Riverrun's vulnerability.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to leave Ser Rodrik at the Bloody Gate to recover; allows Marillion and Bronn to continue to the Eyrie; entrusts herself to Mya Stone for the night ascent; chooses to ride the winch basket rather than climb the final stretch; pushes her doubts about Tyrion's guilt away with resolve.
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn deliberately pushes away her doubts about Tyrion's guilt — "Resolute, she pushed her doubts away" — rather than examining them; she clings to the assumption Lysa will want Tyrion to answer for his crimes, despite Brynden's explicit warnings to the contrary.
