- **chapter_number:** 69

- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Mirri Maz Duur's blood magic ritual; Dany has been unconscious for an extended, indeterminate period
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys experiences intense fever dreams filled with visions of dragons, her dead brother Viserys, Rhaegar, Drogo, and her unborn son burning. She wakes to discover her son Rhaego was stillborn and monstrous, Drogo's khalasar has fractured into a dozen splinter groups, and Drogo himself survives only as an empty shell — alive but unresponsive. After failing to rouse Drogo through the night, Dany accepts he is lost and smothers him with a cushion.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dany's fever dreams** — Extended dream sequence: Dany walks a hallway toward a red door, sees Drogo on the Dothraki sea, sees Jorah fading while calling Rhaegar "the last dragon," sees Viserys screaming with molten gold on his face, sees her son burning from within, passes ghosts of Targaryen kings who urge her faster, feels her skin tear open and wings emerge, flies, and sees Rhaegar whose face is her own.
2. **First waking** — Dany wakes to Jhiqui, tries to crawl toward dragon eggs, is carried back to bed by Ser Jorah. Mirri Maz Duur gives her something to drink and she sleeps again.
3. **Second waking** — Dany wakes in dark tent, asks how long she has been sick. Irri says "long." Mirri gives her wine. Dany asks for a dragon egg before falling asleep again.
4. **Third waking and recovery** — Dany wakes with the pale dragon egg in her arms, feels heat and movement inside it. Her fever is broken. She feels strong, asks for water, fruit, Ser Jorah, and Mirri.
5. **Rhaego's death revealed** — Jhiqui tells Dany her son did not live. Mirri describes the stillborn child as scaled, blind, with bat-like wings, a tail stub, flesh sloughing off bone, full of graveworms, dead for years.
6. **Khalasar's dissolution reported** — Ser Jorah explains Ko Pono left first calling himself khal, then Jhaqo, then others in bands large and small. Only ~100 remain: the old, frightened, weak, sick, and sworn.
7. **Eroeh's fate reported** — Jhogo tells Dany that Mago seized Eroeh, raped her, gave her to Khal Jhaqo, who gave her to his six bloodriders, who then cut her throat.
8. **Dany's oath of vengeance** — Dany swears by multiple gods, the Mother of Mountains, and the Womb of the World that Mago and Jhaqo will die screaming. She declares herself of the blood of Aegon and Maegor.
9. **Drogo's condition revealed** — Dany sees Drogo lying in the sun, blind, unresponsive. He can walk if led, eat if fed, but does not speak, see, or react.
10. **Confrontation with Mirri Maz Duur** — Dany accuses Mirri of cheating her and murdering her child. Mirri is unapologetic, says the stallion who mounts the world will burn no cities now, and challenges Dany's claim of having "saved" her by recounting the horrors she witnessed. Dany orders her bound.
11. **Mirri's prophecy** — Mirri says Drogo will return "when the sun rises in the west and sets in the east, when the seas go dry and mountains blow in the wind like leaves, when your womb quickens again and you bear a living child."
12. **Dany bathes Drogo** — She washes him, combs his hair, tends to him through the evening.
13. **Dany's vigil under the stars** — She leads Drogo outside at night, tries to reach him through memory, intimacy, stories, and tears. He does not respond.
14. **Dany mercy-kills Drogo** — At dawn, she accepts he is lost. She returns to the tent for a cushion, kisses him, and presses the cushion over his face.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief transmuted into determination; she notes all grief has been "burned out" of her. Feels fury toward Mirri. Deep sorrow at Drogo's state but resolves to act rather than mourn. By dawn, acceptance.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The dragon eggs (instinctive pull toward them even while delirious); then Drogo's condition and whether he can be restored; then the reality of her situation and what she has left.
- **Key decisions made:** Swears vengeance on Mago and Jhaqo; orders Mirri bound; attempts to revive Drogo through the night; mercy-kills Drogo at dawn.
- **Self-deception flags:** Mirri directly accuses Dany of lying to herself about the price of the blood magic ("That was a lie you told yourself. You knew the price."). Dany's internal refrain "If I look back I am lost" functions as a deliberate mechanism to suppress doubt and grief. She tells herself "all the grief has been burned out of me" and feels Rhaego "receding as if he had never been."
