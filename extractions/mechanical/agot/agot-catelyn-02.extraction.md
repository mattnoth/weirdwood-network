## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 7
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn II
- **first_available:** AGOT Catelyn II
- **location_primary:** Winterfell — Catelyn's bedchambers (Great Keep)
- **location_secondary:** Riverrun (referenced), Starfall (referenced), the Eyrie (referenced), King's Landing (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Robert's arrival at Winterfell; the same night or immediately following Robert's visit (post-feast, pre-departure preparations)
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn and Ned debate whether he should accept the role of Hand of the King. Maester Luwin arrives late at night with a secret message that had been left anonymously in his observatory — a letter from Catelyn's sister Lysa Arryn, written in a private cipher, accusing the Lannisters and Queen Cersei of murdering Jon Arryn. This intelligence forces Ned's hand; he agrees to go south. He decides Sansa, Arya, and Bran will travel with him, while Robb and Rickon remain at Winterfell with Catelyn. When the question of Jon Snow's disposition arises, Maester Luwin reveals that Benjen Stark has already approached him about Jon's wish to join the Night's Watch, and Ned reluctantly agrees to the arrangement.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned and Catelyn debate the Handship** — After lovemaking, Ned tells Catelyn he will refuse Robert's offer. Catelyn argues he cannot without arousing suspicion and endangering their house.
2. **Maester Luwin arrives with urgent news** — A man-at-arms named Desmond announces Luwin at the door. Ned admits him.
3. **Luwin describes the secret delivery** — A carved wooden box appeared in his observatory while he slept; no one was seen leaving it. It contained a Myrish lens and, hidden in a false bottom, a sealed letter.
4. **The letter is identified as addressed to Catelyn alone** — Sealed with the moon-and-falcon of House Arryn.
5. **Catelyn decodes Lysa's letter** — Written in a private girlhood cipher; accuses the Lannisters (specifically the queen) of murdering Jon Arryn.
6. **Catelyn burns the letter** — She places it among the kindling before revealing its contents verbally.
7. **Ned agrees to go south** — After Catelyn and Luwin press him, and after a private moment of grief at the window, Ned capitulates.
8. **Ned assigns governance of Winterfell to Catelyn** — She is to rule in his absence; Robb is to be included in councils to learn governance.
9. **Ned arranges the children's dispositions** — Sansa and Arya go south with him; Bran goes south to befriend the princes; Robb and Rickon remain at Winterfell.
10. **Catelyn pleads for Bran to stay; Ned overrules her** — Ned argues Bran can build friendship with Joffrey the way Robert and Ned were friends.
11. **Jon Snow's disposition debated** — Catelyn refuses to let Jon remain at Winterfell. Ned cannot take him south (no place for a bastard at court).
12. **Maester Luwin reveals Benjen's approach about Jon** — Benjen spoke to Luwin about Jon's wish to join the Night's Watch.
13. **Ned agrees Jon will join the Night's Watch** — Reluctantly; he is troubled by Jon's youth (fourteen). He says he will speak with Benjen and tell Jon himself before departure.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious and resolute; grieving the coming separations; armoring herself against sentiment to achieve what she believes is necessary for the family's survival; cold fury over Jon Snow's presence at Winterfell
- **Primary preoccupation:** Persuading Ned to accept the Handship; protecting her children's futures; managing her hatred of Jon Snow
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses not to speak against Jon going to the Night's Watch (relief); accepts that Sansa, Arya, and Bran will go south; presses the case for Ned going south even though it means losing him
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn frames her desire to remove Jon Snow from Winterfell in terms of his own wellbeing ("Ned would do the boy no kindness by leaving him here"); she also presents the Night's Watch resolution as purely pragmatic, while inwardly she is relieved that Jon will be unable to father children who could contest Winterfell
