# AGOT — Catelyn II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 7
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn II
- **first_available:** AGOT Catelyn II
- **location_primary:** Winterfell — Catelyn's bedchambers in the Great Keep
- **location_secondary:** Riverrun (recalled), the Eyrie (referenced), Starfall (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after King Robert's arrival at Winterfell; nighttime, the same period as the feast
- **time_markers:** Night; "fifteen long years" since Catelyn and Ned's wedding; "three years since Rickon" was born; Ned was "eight when my father sent me to foster at the Eyrie"; Sansa is "only eleven"; Joffrey is prince; Bran is "only seven"; Jon is "fourteen"; Robb is "fourteen"; "a fortnight before we are ready to depart"; "in a few years" Arya will be of age to marry; "first year of her marriage" (recalled); "a fortnight to marshal her courage" (recalled, re: asking about Ashara Dayne); "a few days ago" Benjen spoke to Luwin about Jon
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn and Ned discuss Robert's offer of the Hand of the King position in their bedchamber. Ned wants to refuse, but Catelyn argues he must accept. Maester Luwin arrives with a secret letter from Lysa Arryn, hidden inside a carved box containing a Myrish lens, claiming Jon Arryn was murdered by the Lannisters — by the queen. This convinces Ned to accept the position. They decide Catelyn will stay at Winterfell with Robb and Rickon, while Ned takes Sansa, Arya, and Bran south. Maester Luwin suggests Jon Snow join the Night's Watch at Benjen Stark's recommendation, which Ned reluctantly accepts.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn observes Ned after lovemaking** — Ned rises from bed and opens the windows; Catelyn watches him and reflects on their marriage, her desire for another child, and his resemblance to the youth she married
2. **Ned declares he will refuse the Handship** — He tells Catelyn he will refuse Robert's offer; his eyes are haunted, his voice thick with doubt
3. **Catelyn argues Ned must accept** — She warns that a king will suspect opposition if refused; invokes the betrothal of Sansa to Joffrey as an honor not to be thrown back
4. **Ned reflects on Brandon** — He says everything was meant for his brother Brandon — Catelyn, Winterfell, the Handship; Catelyn counters that Brandon is dead and Ned must accept his role
5. **Desmond announces Maester Luwin** — A loud knock; Luwin insists on an urgent audience despite Ned's orders not to be disturbed
6. **Maester Luwin delivers the carved box and lens** — He reports finding a carved wooden box with a Myrish lens left anonymously in his observatory, likely from someone in the king's party
7. **Catelyn identifies the hidden message** — She recognizes the significance of a lens ("an instrument to help us see"); Luwin produces a tightly rolled paper from the box's false bottom, addressed to Catelyn alone
8. **Catelyn reads Lysa's letter** — Sealed with the moon-and-falcon of House Arryn in blue wax; written in a childhood cipher shared with Lysa; the letter accuses the Lannisters — specifically the queen — of murdering Jon Arryn
9. **Catelyn burns the letter** — She crosses naked to the hearth, slides the paper among kindling, and lights a fire to destroy it
10. **Ned resists going south** — He says "The south is a nest of adders I would do better to avoid"
11. **Catelyn and Luwin persuade Ned** — Luwin argues the Hand has power to find truth and protect Lysa; Catelyn asks if Ned would leave Robert surrounded by Lannisters
12. **Ned agrees to become Hand** — After gazing out the window, he says farewell to Winterfell; references his father Rickard who "went south once" and never returned; Luwin counters "A different time. A different king."
13. **Ned assigns Catelyn to govern the North** — She must stay at Winterfell with Robb (who must learn to rule) and Rickon; Ned entrusts Luwin to advise her and teach Robb
14. **Ned decides to take Sansa, Arya, and Bran south** — Sansa must wed Joffrey; Arya must learn southron ways; Bran should befriend the young princes as Ned befriended Robert
15. **Catelyn pleads to keep Bran** — She asks for Bran to remain, but Ned argues he can bridge the gap between Robb and Joffrey; Catelyn yields, telling Ned to "keep him off the walls" because Bran loves to climb
16. **Catelyn's internal recollection of Jon Snow's origins** — She recalls Ned bringing Jon home from war, the rumors of Ashara Dayne, Ned's furious command never to ask about Jon's mother
17. **Catelyn demands Jon leave Winterfell** — She refuses to keep Jon in her home; Ned cannot take him south where a bastard would be shunned
18. **Luwin proposes the Night's Watch for Jon** — Benjen Stark approached Luwin days ago about Jon's aspiration to take the black; Ned reluctantly agrees, saying he will speak to Benjen and tell Jon himself before departure

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Shifts through multiple phases — post-coital warmth and hope (wanting another child); growing alarm and political calculation (arguing for the Handship); dread when the letter arrives; determination and pragmatism after reading Lysa's accusation; deep pain at losing four of her children to the south; cold resolve regarding Jon Snow; private resentment about Jon's presence and what he represents
- **Primary preoccupation:** Protecting her family through political maneuvering — she sees accepting the Handship as necessary for survival, not as an honor; the secondary preoccupation is ensuring Jon Snow leaves Winterfell
- **Key decisions made:** Argues successfully that Ned must accept the Handship; agrees (reluctantly) to let Sansa, Arya, and Bran go south; demands Jon leave Winterfell; burns Lysa's letter to destroy evidence; accepts remaining at Winterfell to govern the North
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn frames her demand that Jon leave as doing "the boy no kindness by leaving him here" — rationalizing her own inability to accept him; she tells herself Jon's resemblance to Ned "made it worse," suggesting awareness that her resentment is not entirely rational; she congratulates herself that the Night's Watch solution means Jon will "father no sons who might someday contest with Catelyn's own grandchildren for Winterfell" — revealing dynastic calculation beneath the emotional rejection
