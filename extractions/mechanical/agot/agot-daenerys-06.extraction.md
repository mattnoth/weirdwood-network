# AGOT — Daenerys VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 55
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys VI
- **first_available:** AGOT Daenerys VI
- **location_primary:** Vaes Dothrak
- **location_secondary:** Western Market (Vaes Dothrak), Eastern Market (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** Late in Daenerys's AGOT arc; she is heavily pregnant, Viserys is already dead, the khalasar is preparing to move
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys tries to convince Khal Drogo to invade Westeros, but he dismisses crossing the sea. At the Western Market, a wine merchant attempts to poison Daenerys with a cask of wine; Ser Jorah intervenes after receiving a warning letter from Illyrio. The letter reveals Robert Baratheon has offered rewards for the deaths of Daenerys and Viserys. Alone, Dany places her three dragon eggs in a lit brazier, but nothing happens. When Drogo returns from hunting a white lion and learns of the assassination attempt, he vows to cross the sea, conquer the Seven Kingdoms, and give the Iron Throne to his unborn son Rhaego. The khalasar departs Vaes Dothrak two days later with the captured wineseller chained to Dany's horse.

## Events & Actions
1. **Drogo refuses to cross the sea** — Dany argues for invading Westeros; Drogo insists the stallion who mounts the world needs no iron chairs and plans to ride east toward the Jade Sea after Rhaego is born.
2. **Dany consults Ser Jorah** — She asks him to help persuade Drogo; Jorah counsels patience and notes Drogo has never seen the Seven Kingdoms and considers eastern riches more tempting.
3. **Caravan arrival announced** — A caravan of 400 horses from Pentos via Norvos and Qohor under Merchant Captain Byan Votyris has arrived; Ser Jorah suggests visiting the Western Market for possible letters from Illyrio.
4. **Dany visits the Western Market** — Carried in a litter with her khas and handmaids; she browses stalls, eats sausages, buys a feathered cloak, scented oils, and a fertility charm for Doreah.
5. **Ser Jorah departs to find the merchant captain** — He declines Dany's offer to accompany him; Dany finds his insistence on going alone curious.
6. **Wine merchant offers poisoned wine** — A Lysene-looking merchant identifies Dany as royalty after she speaks Valyrian; offers a cask of Arbor red as a gift after Doreah announces Dany's titles.
7. **Ser Jorah intervenes** — Returning, he demands the cask be opened and the merchant taste the wine first; the merchant refuses, sweats, and makes excuses.
8. **Dany orders the merchant to drink** — She threatens to have the wine poured down his throat; the merchant instead hurls the cask at her.
9. **Assassination attempt foiled** — Ser Jorah knocks Dany aside; Doreah catches her so she lands on her legs, not her belly; the cask smashes on the ground.
10. **Wineseller captured** — He flees but Jhogo trips him with his whip; Byan Votyris's caravan guards seize him; Votyris gifts the wineseller's goods to Dany.
11. **Illyrio's letter revealed** — Ser Jorah shows Dany a letter from Illyrio to Viserys revealing Robert Baratheon offers lands and lordships for the deaths of Dany and/or Viserys.
12. **Dany places dragon eggs in the brazier** — Alone, she puts all three eggs into burning coals; the scales seem to glow and drink the heat, but nothing else happens; she concludes they are only stone.
13. **Drogo returns from hunting** — He killed a hrakkar (great white lion); he was raked on the leg; he promises Dany a cloak from its skin.
14. **Drogo vows to conquer Westeros** — Upon learning of the assassination attempt, he pledges horses to Jhogo and Jorah, vows to cross the sea, destroy the men in iron suits, and give the Iron Throne to Rhaego; he swears before the Mother of Mountains.
15. **Khalasar departs Vaes Dothrak** — Two days later, the khalasar strikes south and west; the wineseller is chained naked to Dany's horse, forced to keep up on foot.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Frustrated at Drogo's refusal early on; wistful and sad when reflecting on the concept of "home"; frightened during and after the assassination attempt; determined and fierce when confronting the wineseller; seized by a strange compulsion when placing eggs in fire; disappointed when eggs remain inert; resolved and emboldened by Drogo's vow
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting Drogo to invade Westeros; protecting her unborn son; her identity as "the last dragon"
- **Key decisions made:** Orders the wine merchant to drink (exposing the plot); places all three dragon eggs in the brazier alone; tells Drogo about the assassination attempt
- **Self-deception flags:** Rationalizes the egg experiment as possible "strange wisdom buried in her blood" rather than madness; quickly adopts the mantra "the dragon does not fear" as a coping mechanism against genuine terror
