- **chapter_summary:** Eddard Stark languishes in the black cells beneath the Red Keep, injured, feverish, and starving in total darkness. Varys visits him disguised as a gaoler, reveals the political situation — Arya has escaped, Sansa pleaded for Ned's life, Tyrion has escaped Catelyn, Robb marches south, and Stannis gathers strength at Dragonstone. Varys urges Ned to confess treason and support Joffrey in exchange for being allowed to take the black, using the implicit threat to Sansa's life as leverage.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned is confined in the black cells** — Thrown into a dark dungeon cell beneath the Red Keep with no window, bed, or slop bucket; his injured leg throbs constantly.
2. **Ned reflects on his downfall** — He damns Littlefinger, Janos Slynt, the gold cloaks, Cersei, Jaime, Pycelle, Varys, Barristan, and Renly, but ultimately blames himself for his foolishness.
3. **Ned hallucinates or imagines Cersei** — Her face floats before him, repeating "When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die."
4. **Ned grieves for his daughters** — He would weep but cannot; his grief and rage freeze inside him as a Stark of Winterfell.
5. **Ned endures extended sensory deprivation** — He loses track of time; cannot distinguish sleeping from waking; dreams of blood and broken promises; his leg becomes feverish and hot.
6. **Ned builds castles of hope** — He reasons that Robert's brothers are raising armies, his household guard will return, and Catelyn will raise the north with the river lords, mountain lords, and lords of the Vale.
7. **Ned dreams/hallucinates a conversation with Robert** — Robert appears as he was in youth, then his face cracks and peels away to reveal Littlefinger grinning beneath; Littlefinger's lies turn to pale grey moths.
8. **A rat-faced gaoler brings water** — Ned drinks desperately; asks about his daughters; receives only grunts and kicks in response.
9. **Ned's sense of smell fades** — The straw no longer smells; his body is deteriorating.
10. **Ned remembers the tourney at Harrenhal** — He recalls being eighteen, Brandon's laughter, Robert's melee prowess, Jaime Lannister taking Kingsguard vows before Aerys, the six White Swords welcoming Jaime.
11. **Ned remembers Rhaegar's triumph** — Rhaegar in black ruby-studded armor defeated Brandon, Bronze Yohn Royce, Ser Arthur Dayne, and Ser Barristan Selmy in jousting.
12. **Ned remembers the moment all smiles died** — Rhaegar rode past his wife Elia Martell and laid the queen of beauty's crown of winter roses, blue as frost, in Lyanna's lap.
13. **Ned dreams of grasping the crown of roses** — Thorns hidden beneath the petals claw his skin; he sees blood trickle down his fingers and wakes trembling.
14. **Ned remembers Lyanna's deathbed** — "Promise me, Ned" whispered from her bed of blood; she had loved the scent of winter roses.
15. **Ned continues to deteriorate** — Stomach cramps begin; he begs for food but is not fed; he reasons Cersei wants him alive because Catelyn holds her brother.
16. **Varys arrives disguised as a gaoler** — Stouter and shorter than the rat-faced man, wearing leather half cape and spiked steel cap; brings a wineskin; Ned recognizes his voice.
17. **Ned touches Varys's face** — Feels coarse false stubble; Varys has transformed into a grizzled turnkey reeking of sweat and sour wine.
18. **Varys shares wine** — Drinks first to prove it's not poisoned; Ned drinks the strong dregs.
19. **Varys reveals Arya has escaped** — She fled Ser Meryn; neither Varys nor the Lannisters have found her; the new king "loves her not."
20. **Varys reveals Sansa's situation** — Still betrothed to Joffrey; Cersei keeps her close; she came to court to plead for Ned's life.
21. **Varys tells Ned he is a dead man** — Bluntly states Ned's position.
22. **Ned argues Catelyn holds Cersei's brother** — Varys counters that she held "the wrong brother" and has let Tyrion escape; he expects Tyrion is dead in the Mountains of the Moon.
23. **Ned confronts Varys about standing by during the slaughter** — Varys replies he was unarmed and surrounded by Lannister swords; explains his philosophy that each man has a role to play, and the master of whisperers must be sly and obsequious.
24. **Ned asks Varys to free him** — Varys says he could but won't, as questions would lead back to him.
25. **Ned asks Varys to carry a message** — Varys offers paper and ink but will read the letter and deliver it or not as serves his own ends.
26. **Varys reveals his goal is peace** — Claims he was the one soul desperate to keep Robert alive; protected him for fifteen years from enemies but not from friends.
27. **Varys asks why Ned warned Cersei** — Ned admits "the madness of mercy."
28. **Varys confirms Cersei's role in Robert's death** — Lancel was given the wineskins by Cersei, told it was Robert's favorite vintage; but Varys says it was Ned's mercy, not wine, that killed the king.
29. **Varys outlines the political landscape** — Jaime fighting river lords; Lysa Arryn in the Eyrie; Martells brooding in Dorne; Robb marching south with a northern host; Stannis gathering swords at Dragonstone; Renly raising armies at Storm's End.
30. **Varys describes Cersei's nightmare** — While Lannisters fight Starks and Tullys, Stannis will land, proclaim himself king, and behead Joffrey and Cersei.
31. **Ned declares Stannis the true heir** — Says the throne is his by rights.
32. **Varys proposes a deal** — Ned should confess treason, command Robb to lay down his sword, proclaim Joffrey true heir, denounce Stannis and Renly; in exchange Cersei would allow Ned to take the black.
33. **Ned thinks of Jon with shame and sorrow** — Wishes he could see and talk to the boy again.
34. **Ned asks if Varys is in league with Littlefinger** — Varys says he'd sooner wed the Black Goat of Qohor; calls Littlefinger the second most devious man in the Seven Kingdoms; says he feeds Littlefinger whispers to make him think Varys is his.
35. **Varys claims to serve the realm** — Swears by his lost manhood.
36. **Ned refuses the deal** — Says his word would be hollow; his life is not so precious.
37. **Varys threatens Sansa's life** — Asks how precious Ned's daughter's life is; invokes the fate of Rhaenys Targaryen and her kitten Balerion; describes the Lannisters breaking down Rhaenys's door.
38. **Varys delivers his ultimatum** — The next visitor will bring bread, cheese, and milk of the poppy — or Sansa's head. The choice is Ned's.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Despair, self-blame, grief, shame, physical agony, intermittent hope, fever-induced delirium; rage and grief that "froze hard inside him" as a Stark; shame and sorrow when thinking of Jon Snow; defiance when refusing to confess, then fear when Sansa is threatened
- **Primary preoccupation:** His daughters' safety (especially Sansa and Arya); his own failure and foolishness; guilt over Robert's death; the memory of Lyanna and her deathbed promise
- **Key decisions made:** Initially refuses Varys's deal to confess and take the black, saying his word would be hollow and his life not worth that price; left at an agonizing impasse when Varys invokes Sansa's safety
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned builds "castles of hope" about rescue (Robert's brothers raising armies, Catelyn rallying the north, his guard returning) — Varys systematically dismantles most of these; Ned initially believes Catelyn's holding of Tyrion protects him, which Varys corrects ("the wrong brother")
