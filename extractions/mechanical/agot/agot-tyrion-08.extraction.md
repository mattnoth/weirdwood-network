- **time_markers:** Twilight/evening (opening meal); dusk settling; predawn darkness (trumpets wake camp); dawn/sunrise (battle assembly); rising sun burning off fog; battle occurs in morning light
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion dines with his father Lord Tywin and the Lannister commanders, learning he has been assigned to the vanguard under Ser Gregor Clegane. He leaves the meal angry, finds his way to his tent, and meets Shae, a camp follower Bronn has procured for him. In the predawn hours, trumpets sound battle assembly as Robb Stark's forces arrive sooner than expected. Tyrion leads his clansmen on the left flank of the vanguard along the river, fights in fierce combat where he kills one man and captures a knight. The Lannisters win the battle, but Tyrion learns afterward that Robb Stark was never with this army — he crossed at the Twins with his cavalry, riding for Riverrun.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion arrives late to his father's evening meal** — He takes his place beside his uncle Ser Kevan at the trestle table on the hill.
2. **Tywin reports Stark movements** — The Stark host has moved south from the Twins with Lord Frey's levies, approximately a day's march north.
3. **Lord Lefford complains about arming the clansmen** — He questions whether Tyrion's "savages" will fight, having reluctantly provided arms and armor for three hundred men Ulf brought from the foothills.
4. **Ser Kevan announces the vanguard assignment** — Tyrion and his clansmen are assigned to the vanguard; Tyrion suspects this is meant to get rid of him.
5. **Tyrion reports indiscipline among the clans** — A Moon Brother stabbed a Stone Crow over a sausage; three Stone Crows retaliated by cutting the killer's throat; Ulf demands blood money that Conn and Shagga refuse to pay.
6. **Tywin reassigns Tyrion under Ser Gregor** — After Tyrion's report of indiscipline, Tywin places him under Ser Gregor's command rather than giving him independent command, offering the baggage train as alternative.
7. **Tyrion angrily accepts the vanguard** — He spits out his food and leaves the table; the lords laugh at him as he walks away.
8. **Tyrion wanders the Lannister camp alone** — He is surrounded by twenty thousand Lannister men but feels completely alone; no one looks at or speaks to him.
9. **Tyrion visits the Stone Crows** — Conn invites him to share their ox; Tyrion declines and moves on to his tent.
10. **Tyrion meets Shae** — Bronn has procured a camp follower; Tyrion establishes terms — she will share his tent, pour wine, laugh at his jests, and take no other men. She agrees.
11. **Tyrion and Shae have sex** — He reflects it has been nearly a year since he lay with a woman, since before setting out for Winterfell.
12. **Tyrion speaks with Bronn outside the tent** — Bronn admits he took Shae from a knight by threat of his dirk; Tyrion asks him to note the knight's name so he won't be beside him in battle.
13. **Tyrion asks Bronn to protect him in battle** — Promises Bronn he can name his reward if Tyrion survives.
14. **Battle assembly trumpets sound in predawn** — Robb Stark's forces have crept down the kingsroad in the night and are less than a mile north, forming battle lines.
15. **Tyrion arms for battle** — He dons mismatched armor from Lord Lefford's wagons; Shae and Pod help buckle him in; his proper armor is at Casterly Rock.
16. **Lannister army deploys** — Ser Kevan commands the center with foot archers, pikemen, and three hundred heavy horse alongside Lords Lefford, Lydden, and Serrett. Ser Addam Marbrand commands the right wing of four thousand cavalry. Lord Tywin takes the reserve of five thousand on the hill.
17. **Tyrion takes position on the far left of the vanguard** — Ser Gregor orders him to hold the river. The vanguard is composed of the worst troops: mounted archers, freeriders, sellswords, fieldhands, half-trained boys, and the clansmen.
18. **The clansmen cheer Tyrion** — They chant "Halfman!" as he leads them.
19. **Battle begins** — Lannister archers rain arrows on advancing northerners; Ser Gregor leads charge into Karstark spearmen; his horse is killed but he fights on.
20. **Shagga breaks through the shield wall** — Stone Crows and other clansmen follow through the gap Gregor created.
21. **Tyrion fights in close combat** — He knocks aside a spear, rides over a man-at-arms, chops at a man with a shield, kills a tall northerner by burying his axe in his head after his horse bites the man's cheek.
22. **Tyrion is unhorsed** — A knight with a morningstar shouting "For Eddard and Winterfell" smashes Tyrion's elbow and knocks him from his horse.
23. **Tyrion defeats the knight** — He headbutts the knight's horse in the belly with his spiked helm; the horse falls on the knight, trapping him; the knight yields and surrenders his sword.
24. **Ser Kevan's center supports the van** — Pikemen push northerners back against the hills.
25. **Lord Tywin commits the reserve** — Five hundred knights sweep along the river; the Stark lines shatter.
26. **Tyrion surveys casualties** — Ulf son of Umar dead (arm gone at elbow) with a dozen Moon Brothers; Conn son of Coratt killed by spear thrust; Shagga alive though riddled with arrows; Chella has four ears; Timett loots bodies. Roughly half of the three hundred clansmen survive.
27. **Tyrion confronts his father** — Accuses Tywin of intending the vanguard to be butchered as a feigned rout to lure Robb Stark into overcommitting.
28. **Tywin confirms the plan** — He placed the least disciplined men on the left expecting them to break, hoping to lure Robb into the gap, then flank him with Kevan's pikes and the reserve.
29. **Ser Addam reports** — Captured commanders include Lord Cerwyn, Ser Wylis Manderly, Harrion Karstark, four Freys. Lord Hornwood is dead. Roose Bolton escaped. Robb Stark was never with this army — he crossed at the Twins with his cavalry, riding for Riverrun.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitter and angry at his father's contempt and the humiliating vanguard assignment; lonely and isolated in the camp despite being surrounded by twenty thousand men; briefly tender and content with Shae; terrified before and during battle (queasy liquid feeling in bowels, hard knot in stomach, cold sweat); grim satisfaction at surviving; sardonic amusement at learning Robb Stark outmaneuvered his father
- **Primary preoccupation:** His father's contempt and the near-certainty that the vanguard assignment is meant to kill him; survival
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts vanguard position rather than suffer the humiliation of the baggage train; establishes exclusive arrangement with Shae; fights rather than flees in battle
- **Self-deception flags:** Suspects Shae's delight is feigned but decides "it did not matter" — "That much truth he did not crave"; reflexively compares himself unfavorably to Jaime, wondering if Jaime contemplates death before battle
