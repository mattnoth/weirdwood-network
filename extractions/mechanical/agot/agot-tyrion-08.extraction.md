# AGOT — Tyrion VIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 63
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion VIII
- **first_available:** AGOT Tyrion VIII
- **location_primary:** Lannister camp along the kingsroad, near a river
- **location_secondary:** Battlefield (the Green Fork)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly before and during the first major battle between Lannister and Stark forces; Robb Stark's host has marched south from the Twins
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion dines with his father Lord Tywin and bannermen, where he learns his clansmen will serve in the vanguard under Ser Gregor Clegane. Humiliated, he returns to his tent and meets Shae, a camp follower Bronn procured for him. Battle horns wake them before dawn — the Stark host has arrived sooner than expected. Tyrion leads his clansmen on the left flank of the van, fights in brutal close combat, takes a knight captive, and survives with a badly wounded elbow. After the battle, Lord Tywin reveals the van was meant to collapse as bait to draw Robb Stark into a trap, but the Stark boy was never on this field — he crossed at the Twins with his cavalry and rides for Riverrun.

## Events & Actions
1. **Evening meal on the hill** — Tyrion arrives late to dine with Lord Tywin, Ser Kevan, Lord Lefford, and other bannermen. Lord Tywin reports that the Stark host has moved south from the Twins with Lord Frey's levies and is about a day's march north.
2. **Vanguard assignment** — Ser Kevan announces Tyrion and his clansmen will serve in the vanguard. Lord Tywin specifies Tyrion will serve under Ser Gregor Clegane, not in command. Tyrion angrily accepts rather than be assigned to the baggage train.
3. **Meeting Shae** — Tyrion returns to his tent and meets Shae, a camp follower Bronn procured from a knight (by threat of his dirk). Tyrion sets terms: she will share his tent, take no other men, and serve as companion. They sleep together.
4. **Late-night conversation with Bronn** — Bronn reveals he took Shae from a knight by threatening him. Tyrion asks Bronn to protect him in the coming battle and promises he can name his reward if Tyrion survives.
5. **Pre-dawn battle alarm** — Trumpets wake the camp. Robb Stark's host has stolen a march and is less than a mile north, forming for battle.
6. **Battle formation** — The Lannister army deploys: Ser Kevan commands the center (archers, pikemen, men-at-arms, three hundred heavy horse), Ser Addam Marbrand commands the right wing (four thousand cavalry), Lord Tywin commands the reserve from the high ground (five thousand). Tyrion and clansmen are placed on the far left of Ser Gregor's van.
7. **The battle of the Green Fork** — The Stark host attacks. Lannister archers devastate the advancing northerners. Ser Gregor's van charges into the Karstark spearmen. Tyrion fights in close combat, kills at least one man (buries axe in his head), has his shield destroyed, is struck by a morningstar, loses his axe, and is unhorsed.
8. **Tyrion captures a knight** — A knight with a morningstar demands Tyrion yield. Tyrion headbutts the knight's horse in the belly; the horse falls on the knight, trapping him. The knight yields and surrenders his sword to Tyrion.
9. **Aftermath and casualties** — Roughly half of Tyrion's three hundred clansmen are dead. Conn son of Coratt and Ulf son of Umar are killed. Shagga survives despite being full of arrows. Chella takes four ears. Timett loots the dead.
10. **Tywin reveals his strategy** — Lord Tywin explains he placed the least disciplined men on the left expecting them to break, hoping to lure Robb Stark into the gap where Ser Kevan's pikes would flank him and the reserve would finish him.
11. **Robb Stark's deception revealed** — Ser Addam Marbrand reports that Robb Stark was not with this army. He crossed at the Twins with the main body of his horse and rides hard for Riverrun. The Lannisters have captured Lord Cerwyn, Ser Wylis Manderly, Harrion Karstark, and four Freys. Lord Hornwood is dead. Roose Bolton has escaped.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitter and humiliated at dinner; isolated and alone walking through the camp; tender and vulnerable with Shae; terrified but resolute before and during battle; grimly satisfied at surviving; angry at his father's deliberate sacrifice of the van
- **Primary preoccupation:** His father's contempt and the feeling of being expendable; fear of death in battle
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts the vanguard assignment rather than suffer the greater humiliation of the baggage train; agrees to keep Shae as companion on his own terms; promises Bronn any reward for protecting him
- **Self-deception flags:** Suspects Shae's delight is feigned but tells himself "it did not matter" — "That much truth he did not crave"
