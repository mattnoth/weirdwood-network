# AGOT — Bran IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 25
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran IV

- **location_primary:** Winterfell
- **location_secondary:** The Wall (mentioned), King's Landing (mentioned), winter town (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** After Eddard's departure for King's Landing; Bran has recently turned eight; Robb is acting lord of Winterfell
- **time_markers:** Bran's eighth name day has come and gone; daytime scene in the yard (sunlight streaming through door); evening feast in the Great Hall; night (Bran's dream and being put to bed); Robb mentions "Mother will be home soon"
- **chapter_summary:** Bran watches Rickon play with the direwolves from his window, bitter about his broken legs. Old Nan tells him the story of the Others and the last hero during the Long Night, interrupted by the arrival of Tyrion Lannister and men of the Night's Watch. Tyrion presents Bran with a saddle design that would allow him to ride despite his paralysis. The direwolves aggressively confront Tyrion before being called off. At the evening feast, Yoren and the Night's Watch brothers report that Benjen Stark is missing beyond the Wall. Robb carries Bran to bed and promises they will ride together.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran watches from window** — Bran observes Rickon running in the yard with Grey Wind, Shaggydog, and Summer from his window seat. He is bitter and tearful about being unable to join them.
2. **Bran rejects Old Nan's stories** — Bran angrily tells Old Nan he hates her stories. She is his constant companion now, left to watch over him, but he resents it.
3. **Old Nan tells the story of the Others** — After Bran admits he likes scary stories, Old Nan tells of the Long Night: a winter that lasted a generation, the coming of the Others with their dead horses and hosts of slain, and the last hero who set out with companions to find the children of the forest. She describes the Others hunting with packs of pale white spiders big as hounds.
4. **Maester Luwin interrupts** — Maester Luwin arrives with Hodor, announcing visitors: Tyrion Lannister and men of the Night's Watch with word from Jon.
5. **Hodor carries Bran to the Great Hall** — Hodor carries Bran down the steps and through a gallery to the hall where Robb is already receiving visitors.
6. **Robb confronts Tyrion** — Robb sits in the lord's high seat with bare sword, welcoming Night's Watch men but pointedly excluding Tyrion. Tyrion and Robb exchange hostile words.
7. **Bran is placed in the high seat** — Hodor sets Bran in the high seat of the Starks at Robb's command.
8. **Tyrion questions Bran about his fall** — Tyrion asks Bran how he fell. Bran insists he never fell. Maester Luwin explains Bran remembers nothing of the fall or the climb.
9. **Tyrion presents the saddle design** — Tyrion gives Bran a rolled paper with a design for a special saddle that would allow a cripple to ride. Maester Luwin examines it and approves. Tyrion says Jon asked it of him.
10. **Direwolves attack Tyrion** — Rickon bursts in with the direwolves. Summer, Grey Wind, and Shaggydog converge on Tyrion, growling and snapping. Grey Wind tears a scrap of cloth from Tyrion's sleeve. Bran commands Summer to his side; Robb calls Grey Wind; Rickon calls Shaggydog.
11. **Robb offers hospitality; Tyrion declines** — After consulting with Maester Luwin, Robb offers Tyrion the hospitality of Winterfell. Tyrion rejects it, preferring the inn in the winter town. Tyrion tells Yoren he will meet him on the road at daybreak.
12. **Robb hosts the Night's Watch at feast** — Robb has rooms prepared and invites the brothers to feast, speaking rehearsed words of welcome.
13. **Bran is carried back to bed** — Hodor carries Bran back to his room. Old Nan has fallen asleep in her chair. Hodor carries her off too.
14. **Bran hugs Summer and dreams of riding** — Bran tells Summer they will go hunting in the woods soon, then falls asleep.
15. **Bran's nightmare** — Bran dreams of climbing a windowless tower into the night sky. Stone gargoyles with glowing red eyes (once perhaps lions) watch him, whisper in stone voices, and pull free from the tower to approach him. He wakes gasping in terror.
16. **Hodor comforts Bran** — Hodor lights a candle, washes Bran, and dresses him.
17. **Evening feast** — Bran and Robb eat with the Night's Watch. They eat suckling pig, pigeon pie, turnips in butter, and honeycombs. Yoren reports Benjen Stark is missing and likely dead.
18. **Robb reacts angrily to Benjen news** — Robb stands, hand on sword, and declares "My uncle is not dead." Yoren is unimpressed.
19. **Bran invokes the children of the forest** — Bran blurts that the children of the forest will help Benjen, connecting Old Nan's story to the present danger. Maester Luwin dismisses this, saying the children are dead and gone. Yoren suggests things may be different beyond the Wall.
20. **Robb carries Bran to bed** — Robb carries Bran upstairs himself. They sit together in the dark. Robb promises to find Bran a horse and speaks hopefully of riding to meet their mother and visiting Jon at the Wall. Bran hears Robb sob. They hold hands in the dark.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deep bitterness and grief over his broken legs; anger at his helplessness; resentment toward Old Nan for being his constant companion; longing for his family who have all left; fear during Old Nan's story and his nightmare; brief hope when Tyrion offers the saddle design; fear that it might be another lie like the crow's promise of flight; sadness and tenderness with Robb at the end
- **Primary preoccupation:** The loss of his former life — running, climbing, riding; feeling abandoned by his family; wanting things to be "the way it had been before"
- **Key decisions made:** Calls Summer off Tyrion, preventing violence; chooses to believe he might ride again ("I can ride now," he whispers to Summer)
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself he is "almost a man grown now, too old to cry" at eight years old; insists "I never" fell, though he has no memory of the event
