- **chapter_summary:** Bran watches Rickon play with the direwolves from his window, bitter about his inability to walk. Old Nan tells him the story of the Others and the last hero during the Long Night. Maester Luwin interrupts to summon Bran to the great hall, where Robb is receiving Tyrion Lannister and four men of the Night's Watch. Tyrion presents Bran with a saddle design that would allow a cripple to ride, saying Jon asked it of him. The three direwolves aggressively menace Tyrion before being called off. At the evening feast, Yoren and the black brothers report that Benjen Stark is missing beyond the Wall. Robb carries Bran to bed and they share an emotional moment, Robb promising they will ride together.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran watches from window** — Bran observes Rickon running in the yard with Grey Wind, Shaggydog, and Summer from his window seat, bitter and tearful about his inability to join them.
2. **Bran rejects Old Nan's stories** — Bran snaps at Old Nan, saying he hates her stories; she persists, offering the story of Brandon the Builder.
3. **Commotion at the gatehouse** — Bran hears commotion and sees Rickon running toward the gatehouse with the wolves, but cannot see what is happening from his tower window.
4. **Old Nan tells the story of the Others** — Old Nan tells of the Long Night, the Others who came with cold and death, their pale dead horses and hosts of the slain, and the last hero who sought the children of the forest with his companions, all of whom died.
5. **Maester Luwin interrupts** — Maester Luwin arrives with Hodor, announcing visitors: Tyrion Lannister and men of the Night's Watch with word from Jon.
6. **Hodor carries Bran to the great hall** — Hodor lifts Bran and carries him down through the gallery to the hall, with Maester Luwin following.
7. **Robb confronts Tyrion** — Robb sits in the high seat with bare sword, welcomes Night's Watch but pointedly excludes Tyrion; Tyrion calls him "boy," Robb declares himself lord.
8. **Tyrion sees Bran alive** — Tyrion remarks on Bran's survival: "You Starks are hard to kill."
9. **Bran placed in the high seat** — Hodor sets Bran in the high seat of the Starks at Robb's command.
10. **Tyrion asks about Bran's fall** — Tyrion asks how Bran fell; Bran insists he never fell; Maester Luwin says Bran remembers nothing.
11. **Tyrion presents saddle design** — Tyrion offers a gift: a specially designed saddle that would allow Bran to ride despite his paralysis, saying Jon asked it of him. He hands Maester Luwin a rolled paper with the design.
12. **Direwolves menace Tyrion** — Rickon bursts in with the direwolves; Summer, Grey Wind, and Shaggydog surround and attack Tyrion, tearing his sleeve. Bran calls Summer off, Robb calls Grey Wind, Rickon calls Shaggydog.
13. **Tyrion departs** — Tyrion declines Robb's belated offer of hospitality and chooses to stay at the winter town inn; tells Yoren they go south at daybreak.
14. **Night's Watch hosted** — Robb offers the Night's Watch brothers rooms, hot water, and a seat at table; his speech is rehearsed and awkward.
15. **Bran returned to bed** — Hodor carries Bran back to his room; Old Nan is asleep; Bran hugs Summer and whispers about riding and hunting.
16. **Bran's nightmare** — Bran dreams of climbing an ancient windowless tower; gargoyles with glowing red eyes that were once lions pull free from the stone and approach him. He wakes gasping.
17. **Hodor tends to Bran** — Hodor wakes Bran from the nightmare, lights a candle, washes sweat from him, dresses him.
18. **Feast in the Great Hall** — Bran and Robb eat with Yoren and the Night's Watch; they eat suckling pig, pigeon pie, turnips in butter, and honeycombs; direwolves take scraps and fight over bones.
19. **News of Benjen** — Yoren reports that Benjen Stark is missing beyond the Wall, sent to find Waymar Royce and long overdue. Robb angrily insists Benjen is not dead.
20. **Bran invokes the children of the forest** — Bran blurts that the children of the forest will help Benjen; Maester Luwin says they have been dead for thousands of years; Yoren says beyond the Wall, one cannot tell what is alive or dead.
21. **Robb carries Bran to bed** — Robb carries Bran up the stairs himself, puts him in bed. They share an emotional moment in the dark; Robb promises to find Bran a horse, talks of riding to meet their mother and visiting Jon at the Wall. Robb sobs. They hold hands in the dark.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitterness, grief, anger, and longing dominate. Bran aches to run and climb, resents being left behind, and feels as if everyone has died or forgotten him. Moments of hope emerge with Tyrion's saddle design ("Will I truly be able to ride?") but are guarded by fear of another lie. Deep comfort in Robb's presence at the end, mixed with sadness. Terror during the nightmare.
- **Primary preoccupation:** His disability and isolation — unable to walk, unable to join Rickon and the wolves, left behind while family went to King's Landing and the Wall. Secondary: the crow's broken promise ("I can't fly. I can't even run").
- **Key decisions made:** Calls Summer off Tyrion, preventing potential bloodshed. Chooses to believe in the saddle design (whispers to Summer about riding and hunting).
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran insists "I never" fell — the text notes he remembers nothing of the fall or climb, but his insistence carries emotional rather than factual weight. He tells himself he is "almost a man grown now, too old to cry" at eight years old.
