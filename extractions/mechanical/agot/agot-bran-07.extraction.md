# AGOT — Bran VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 67
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran VII
- **first_available:** AGOT Bran VII
- **location_primary:** Winterfell
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell crypts, Maester Luwin's turret, Winterfell yard
- **approximate_timeline:** Late in AGOT; Eddard is known to be a captive of the queen in King's Landing; Robb has marched south with the fighting men
- **time_markers:** Morning (comet observed in morning sky); Rickon states "I'm four now"; the dream of the crow happened "last night"; the raven arrives at chapter's end with news implying Eddard's death; reference to measuring shadows and comet position
- **chapter_summary:** Bran watches new recruits training in the yard and discusses his desire to fight with Maester Luwin. He tells Luwin about a recurring dream of a three-eyed crow that led him to the crypts where he saw and spoke with his father, who was sad. Osha carries Bran down to the crypts to prove the dream false, where Bran recounts the history of the Kings of Winter. They discover Rickon and Shaggydog hiding in Eddard's empty tomb — Rickon had the same dream. Back in Luwin's turret, the maester lectures on the children of the forest and the history of Westeros while Osha contradicts him about the children still living beyond the Wall. Summer and Shaggydog howl, and a wounded raven arrives bearing news that Eddard Stark is dead.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran watches recruits training** — From Maester Luwin's turret balcony, Bran observes new, unskilled recruits being drilled by Ser Rodrik in the yard below.
2. **Bran expresses desire to fight** — He suggests Hodor could be his legs while he uses a poleaxe, which Luwin dismisses.
3. **Bran recounts his crow dream** — He tells Luwin about dreaming of the three-eyed crow again, which flew into his bedchamber and led him down to the crypts where he spoke with his father, who was sad about something to do with Jon.
4. **Bran reveals Hodor refused the crypts** — He tried to get Hodor to carry him down to the crypts to verify the dream, but Hodor would not descend beyond the top step.
5. **Luwin agrees to go to the crypts** — To prove the dream is just a dream, Luwin summons Osha to carry Bran down.
6. **Descent into the crypts** — Osha carries Bran, Luwin leads with a torch, Summer follows but stops at the steps and refuses to go further.
7. **Bran recounts Stark history** — As they walk past the statues, Bran identifies and tells the stories of the Kings of Winter and later Starks: Jon Stark, Rickard Stark, Theon Stark, Brandon the Shipwright, Brandon the Burner, Rodrik Stark, Torrhen Stark, Cregan Stark, Lord Rickard, Lyanna, and Brandon (Ned's brother).
8. **Bran tells Lyanna's story** — He recounts Robert's betrothal to Lyanna, Rhaegar carrying her off, the war, and her death.
9. **Shaggydog attacks Maester Luwin** — When Luwin reaches into Eddard's empty tomb to prove no one is there, Shaggydog springs from the darkness and savages his arm.
10. **Summer fights Shaggydog** — Summer charges from the dimness and tackles Shaggydog; the two direwolves roll and snap at each other while shadow wolves fight on the walls.
11. **Rickon is discovered** — Rickon appears in the mouth of his father's tomb; he calls Shaggydog off and reveals he also dreamed of his father in the crypts.
12. **Bran convinces Rickon to come with him** — Bran persuades Rickon to wait together in Luwin's tower with their wolves.
13. **Luwin warns about Shaggydog** — He says Shaggydog is the third man he's savaged and the wolf must be chained or killed; Bran overrules him.
14. **Return to the turret** — Osha carries Bran back to Luwin's tower; Rickon and both wolves follow.
15. **Osha bandages Luwin's wounds** — She cleans and bandages the wolf bites while Luwin lectures about the shared dream being natural.
16. **Luwin shows obsidian arrowheads** — He produces a jar of dragonglass arrowheads used by the children of the forest; Bran and Rickon each keep some.
17. **Luwin tells the history of the children of the forest** — He recounts the Dawn Age, the arrival of the First Men, the wars, and the Pact at the Isle of Faces.
18. **Osha contradicts Luwin** — She insists the children of the forest still live north of the Wall; Luwin dismisses this as folly.
19. **Luwin tells of the Andals** — He describes the Andal invasion, the destruction of weirwoods and children in the south, and how only the North resisted.
20. **Summer and Shaggydog howl** — Both direwolves suddenly howl, then stop.
21. **A wounded raven arrives** — A raven with dried blood on its wings lands on the windowsill; it has been attacked by a hawk or owl in flight.
22. **The letter is read** — Luwin reads the letter and, weeping, tells Bran and Rickon they will need to find a stonecarver who knew their father's likeness — confirming Eddard's death.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Resentful about his disability early on; stubbornly hopeful he could still fight; disturbed by his crow dream; sad as he reaches the end of the crypt statues; fearful in the crypts (hairs bristling, shivering); dread at the direwolves' howling; certainty of despair as he realizes his dream was prophetic; grief at the chapter's end, pulling Rickon close
- **Primary preoccupation:** The three-eyed crow dream and his father's appearance in the crypts; his father's fate; his inability to walk
- **Key decisions made:** Overrules Maester Luwin about Shaggydog — insists the wolf will not be chained, asserting his authority as the Stark in Winterfell; decides they will all wait together in Luwin's tower; convinces Rickon to come with him
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran knows what the dream means ("He had known it since last night … He had known it, but he had not believed. He had wanted Maester Luwin to be right.") — he suppressed his own certainty that his father was dead, hoping Luwin's rationalism would disprove it
