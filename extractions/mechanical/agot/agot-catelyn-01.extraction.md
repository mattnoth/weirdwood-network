- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn finds Ned cleaning Ice in the Winterfell godswood after executing a Night's Watch deserter. They discuss the children naming the direwolf pups and Ned's concerns about the Night's Watch losing strength and Mance Rayder. Catelyn delivers the news of Jon Arryn's death, which grieves Ned deeply. She also informs him that King Robert is riding to Winterfell with a large royal party including Queen Cersei, their children, and the queen's brothers. Ned begins planning to receive the king, intending to send Jory south with an honor guard and to notify his brother Benjen on the Wall.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn enters the godswood** — She goes to find Ned, knowing he always seeks the godswood after taking a man's life. She spreads her cloak and sits beside the pool, her back to the weirwood.
2. **Ned and Catelyn discuss the children** — The children are in the kitchen arguing about names for the direwolf pups. Arya is in love with hers, Sansa is charmed, Rickon is unsure. Ned insists Rickon must learn to face his fears.
3. **Ned reflects on the execution** — The deserter "died well." He was the fourth deserter this year and was "half-mad" with fear. Ned shares that Ben writes the Night's Watch is below a thousand men and losing men on rangings.
4. **Discussion of threats beyond the Wall** — Ned attributes losses to wildlings and mentions possibly calling the banners to deal with Mance Rayder. Catelyn raises the Others; Ned dismisses them, saying Maester Luwin would say they never lived at all. Catelyn counters that no one had seen a direwolf until this morning either.
5. **Catelyn delivers news of Jon Arryn's death** — She tells Ned that Jon Arryn is dead, per a letter from King Robert bearing the royal seal. Maester Pycelle could not save him but gave milk of the poppy. Ned is deeply grieved.
6. **Discussion of Lysa and her son** — Lysa and her son have returned to the Eyrie. Catelyn wishes they had gone to Riverrun instead. Ned notes Brynden Tully is in the Vale as Knight of the Gate. Ned urges Catelyn to go to Lysa.
7. **Catelyn announces Robert's visit** — The king is riding to Winterfell. Ned is pleased — a smile breaks across his face.
8. **Catelyn notes the omen** — She has heard talk in the yards about a direwolf dead in the snow with a broken antler in its throat. She feels dread but smiles for Ned.
9. **Planning for the royal visit** — The party includes roughly 100+ knights, retainers, freeriders, Queen Cersei, the royal children, and the queen's brothers. Ned plans a feast with singers, expects Robert will want to hunt, will send Jory south with an honor guard to meet them on the kingsroad, and will have Maester Luwin send a bird to Benjen. Catelyn warns Ned to guard his tongue about the queen.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Uneasy and apprehensive. Catelyn dislikes the godswood — it is alien to her Faith of the Seven upbringing. She feels dread about what lies beyond the Wall when it comes up. She is deeply unsettled by the dead direwolf with a broken antler (Stark/Baratheon sigils), but forces herself to smile for Ned. She wishes she could share his joy about Robert's visit.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Delivering difficult news (Jon Arryn's death, Robert's impending visit) while managing her own sense of foreboding. The omen of the direwolf and stag weighs on her.
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to deliver the news of Jon Arryn's death before telling Ned about Robert's visit. Forces herself to smile and not burden Ned with her dread about the omen.
- **Self-deception flags:** None explicit. However, she suppresses her dread about the direwolf omen rather than voicing it to Ned, who "put no faith in signs."
