# AGOT — Tyrion III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 22
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion III
- **first_available:** AGOT Tyrion III
- **location_primary:** Castle Black (Lord Commander's Keep, top of the Wall)
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell (mentioned), Eastwatch-by-the-Sea (mentioned), Shadow Tower (mentioned), Mole's Town (visible from the Wall), Lannisport (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** The morning after a feast at Castle Black; Tyrion departs southward the following dawn. This chapter follows his extended stay at the Wall and precedes his return ride south via Winterfell.
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion attends a farewell dinner with Lord Commander Mormont and senior Night's Watch officers, trading barbs with Ser Alliser Thorne and earning admiration from Maester Aemon. After the meal, Mormont privately urges Tyrion to advocate for the Night's Watch before the king and his family, warning of coming darkness and dwindling Watch strength. Tyrion rides the cage lift to the top of the Wall for a final look and encounters Jon Snow on night guard duty. Jon asks Tyrion to carry messages to his siblings at Winterfell and to help Bran; Tyrion agrees. They stand together at the Wall's northern edge and look out into the haunted forest, where wolves howl in the darkness and Jon speaks of going to find his uncle Benjen if he does not return.

## Events & Actions
1. **Farewell dinner at Castle Black** — Tyrion dines with Lord Commander Mormont, Bowen Marsh, Ser Jaremy Rykker, Maester Aemon, Ser Alliser Thorne, and other officers. Crabs have arrived from Eastwatch packed in snow.
2. **Tyrion mocks Ser Alliser Thorne with a crab fork** — After Thorne challenges Tyrion to visit the yard, Tyrion hops on his chair and pokes Thorne with a crab fork, causing the table to erupt in laughter. Thorne leaves the room stiffly.
3. **Maester Aemon calls Tyrion "a giant"** — In a rare quiet remark, the ancient blind maester says Tyrion is "a giant come among us, here at the end of the world." Tyrion is briefly at a loss for words.
4. **Mormont's private plea** — After the other officers leave, Mormont privately urges Tyrion to speak to the king, Tywin, and Jaime about the Watch's desperate situation: fewer than 1,000 men, the Wall 100 leagues long, winter coming, white walkers reportedly sighted, mountain peoples fleeing south.
5. **Tyrion ascends the Wall in the cage lift** — On an impulsive whim for a last look, Tyrion bypasses his chambers and rides the iron cage lift to the top of the Wall after midnight.
6. **Tyrion encounters Jon Snow on night guard** — Jon is walking his mile of Wall armed with spear, sword, and warhorn, Ghost at his side. They recognize each other and walk together.
7. **Jon requests messages for his siblings** — Jon asks Tyrion to carry a joking message to Robb, an explanation of his absence for Rickon (who cannot yet read), and help for Bran. Tyrion agrees to help Bran.
8. **Jon and Tyrion clasp hands** — Jon removes his glove and offers his bare hand, calling Tyrion "friend." Tyrion reciprocates. Jon jokes that Tyrion is "the first bastard I've had to friend."
9. **Jon speaks of Benjen and vows to search for him** — Standing at the Wall's northern parapet overlooking the haunted forest, Jon tells Tyrion he watches for Benjen each night. He promises that if Benjen does not return, he and Ghost will go find him. Wolves howl in the far north.
10. **Tyrion's private assessment of Mormont's plea** — Tyrion inwardly acknowledges he will keep his promise to speak to the king and his family, but expects Robert will ignore him, Tywin will think him mad, and Jaime will only laugh.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Sociable and sharp-tongued at dinner; briefly moved by Aemon's words; privately dismissive of Mormont's doom-saying yet genuinely fulfills his promise; impulsively sentimental in ascending the Wall one last time; unexpectedly and "oddly touched" by Jon's friendship.
- **Primary preoccupation:** His imminent departure south; performing social dominance through wit; quietly calculating what his advocacy for the Night's Watch can realistically accomplish.
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees to carry Jon's messages to his siblings at Winterfell; agrees to "give words" to Bran; gives his word to advocate for the Watch before the king, Tywin, and Jaime (fully intending to keep it, even knowing it will be futile).
- **Self-deception flags:** None explicit. Tyrion is unusually clear-eyed in this chapter — he is honest with himself that his advocacy will fail, and honest with Jon that he can only offer "words" to Bran.
