- **chapter_number:** 50

- **location_primary:** The Red Keep, King's Landing (Tower of the Hand; the throne room)
- **location_secondary:** Storm's End (mentioned), Highgarden (mentioned), Winterfell (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** The morning after Robert's death; Ned had been planning to send his daughters away by ship that evening
- **time_markers:** "The grey light of dawn"; "an hour before dawn" (when Renly departed); "It was an hour later when Grand Maester Pycelle came"; "crowned within the fortnight"; "by midday" (departure deadline for daughters); "this evening" (planned ship departure)
- **chapter_summary:** Eddard Stark wakes at dawn to the sound of Lannister men training in the yard. He breakfasts with his daughters and Septa Mordane, refusing Sansa's wish to see Joffrey and granting Arya a final lesson with Syrio. Grand Maester Pycelle arrives to announce King Robert's death. Ned convenes the small council in his solar and learns Renly has fled the city with Ser Loras Tyrell; he presents Robert's sealed letter naming him Protector of the Realm. A royal steward summons them to the throne room, where Joffrey sits the Iron Throne and demands oaths of fealty. When Ned publicly declares Joffrey has no claim and names Stannis the true heir, Cersei tears up Robert's letter and orders his arrest. The gold cloaks, whom Littlefinger had promised to secure for Ned, betray him — killing Tomard, Varly, and Cayn — and Littlefinger holds Ned's own dagger to his throat.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned wakes at dawn** — Lifts his head from his table where he slept; looks out window to see Lannister guardsmen training in the yard below.
2. **Ned questions Cersei's failure to flee** — Wonders why Cersei has not left King's Landing despite his warnings.
3. **Breakfast with daughters** — Ned eats with Sansa, Arya, and Septa Mordane. Sansa is disconsolate, refuses to eat. Arya eats eagerly.
4. **Arya granted a final lesson** — Arya asks for one last lesson with Syrio before they take ship; Ned agrees but sets a midday deadline.
5. **Sansa denied farewell to Joffrey** — Sansa asks to say goodbye to Prince Joffrey; Ned refuses, saying it would not be wise. Sansa runs weeping from the solar.
6. **Robert's death announced** — Grand Maester Pycelle arrives an hour later and tells Ned that King Robert is dead.
7. **Ned summons the small council** — Orders Pycelle to summon council members to his solar rather than the council chambers, which he considers less secure.
8. **Ser Barristan arrives** — Requests leave to attend "the young king"; Ned tells him his place is here.
9. **Littlefinger reports task complete** — Arrives in last night's clothes with dusty boots; tells Ned the task Ned set him is accomplished.
10. **Varys reports Renly's flight** — Renly left through a postern gate an hour before dawn with Ser Loras Tyrell and fifty retainers, galloping south toward Storm's End or Highgarden.
11. **Robert's letter read** — Ned presents Robert's sealed letter. Ser Barristan confirms the unbroken seal and reads it aloud: Ned is named Protector of the Realm, to rule as regent until the heir comes of age.
12. **Ned withholds the succession truth** — Internally reflects that "the heir" is already of age (referring to Stannis), but keeps silent, planning to wait until his daughters are safe and Stannis has arrived with his forces.
13. **Ned asks council to confirm him as Lord Protector** — Watches the faces of Pycelle, Littlefinger, and Varys for reactions.
14. **Royal steward summons them to throne room** — Fat Tom interrupts; the king's steward insists the small council attend the new king immediately.
15. **Procession to the throne room** — Ned is supported by Littlefinger; escorted by eight Stark men-at-arms. Gold cloaks visible on ramparts and gates; no Lannister crimson in the escort.
16. **Janos Slynt meets them at the throne room** — Armored in black-and-gold plate; his men open the great doors.
17. **Joffrey proclaimed king** — Royal steward announces "Joffrey of the Houses Baratheon and Lannister, the First of his Name."
18. **Ned limps the length of the hall** — Recalls the first time he came this way on horseback after Robert's Rebellion, forcing Jaime Lannister down from the throne.
19. **Throne room positions described** — Five Kingsguard in crescent around throne base; Cersei with Myrcella and Tommen behind Ser Boros and Ser Meryn; Sandor Clegane at foot of stair; twenty Lannister guardsmen behind throne; gold cloaks lining walls with spears, outnumbering Lannisters five to one.
20. **Joffrey demands coronation** — Commands coronation within the fortnight and oaths of fealty from councillors.
21. **Ned presents Robert's letter to Cersei** — Has Varys carry it to her. Cersei reads "Protector of the Realm," mocks it as "a piece of paper," and rips it to pieces.
22. **Cersei offers Ned terms** — Tells him to bend the knee, swear fealty to Joffrey, and he can retire to Winterfell.
23. **Ned declares Joffrey's illegitimacy** — States Joffrey has no claim, names Stannis as Robert's true heir.
24. **Myrcella's confusion** — Asks her mother if Joffrey isn't the king now.
25. **Cersei orders Ned's arrest** — Commands Ser Barristan to seize "this traitor."
26. **Ser Barristan hesitates** — Surrounded immediately by Stark guardsmen with bare steel.
27. **Escalation** — The Hound draws his longsword; Kingsguard and twenty Lannister guardsmen move to support him.
28. **Joffrey screams for killing** — "Kill him! Kill all of them, I command it!"
29. **Ned orders Janos Slynt to arrest Cersei** — Commands the City Watch to take the queen and her children into protective custody.
30. **Gold cloaks betray Ned** — Instead of arresting Cersei, a gold cloak drives a spear through Tomard's back, killing him. Janos Slynt slashes Varly's throat. Cayn fights back briefly but is cut down by the Hound.
31. **Littlefinger's final betrayal** — Slides Ned's dagger from its sheath and holds it under Ned's chin, saying "I did warn you not to trust me, you know."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Exhausted, grief-stricken but suppressing emotion; "something died within him" at Robert's death. Would give all his titles for the freedom to weep. Growing unease as events unfold. Pain from his injured leg is constant.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing the regency and getting his daughters safely out of King's Landing; managing the succession crisis without tipping his hand about Joffrey's illegitimacy until Stannis arrives
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Convenes council in Tower of the Hand rather than council chambers for security. (2) Withholds the truth about Joffrey's parentage from the council, planning to wait until Stannis arrives. (3) When Cersei forces the confrontation, publicly declares Joffrey illegitimate. (4) Orders Janos Slynt to arrest Cersei — relying on Littlefinger's assurance that the gold cloaks are secured.
- **Self-deception flags:** Trusted Littlefinger's assurance that the gold cloaks were secured despite having been warned not to trust him. Believed the gold cloaks' visible presence on ramparts was reassuring. Did not consider that "the task accomplished" might mean accomplished for Cersei, not for him. Placed faith in a paper document (Robert's letter) as sufficient authority.
