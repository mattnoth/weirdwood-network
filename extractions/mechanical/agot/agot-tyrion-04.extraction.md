- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion Lannister travels as Catelyn Stark's captive along the high road through the Mountains of the Moon toward the Eyrie. In flashback, Tyrion recalls his capture at the crossroads inn and Catelyn's deception in announcing they would ride for Winterfell before taking the eastern road instead. The party loses horses to the harsh terrain. During a rest stop, Tyrion argues his innocence regarding the attempt on Bran's life and challenges Littlefinger's account of the dagger. Mountain clansmen attack, and Tyrion fights with a borrowed axe, saving Catelyn Stark's life. After the battle, which kills three of their party, Catelyn allows Tyrion to keep his weapons.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion watches Chiggen butcher his dead horse** — In the predawn chill, Chiggen deftly butchers Tyrion's mare for meat. Bronn and Chiggen discuss eating horse; Tyrion is bitter, noting Jaime gave him the mare.
2. **Tyrion recalls his capture at the crossroads inn (flashback)** — Catelyn Stark revealed his identity, showed her scarred hands, and accused him of sending the dagger to kill Bran. Tyrion restrained Jyck from drawing his sword and submitted to capture, calculating that fighting was suicide.
3. **Tyrion assessed the room during capture** — He noted only about a dozen of fifty people responded to Catelyn's plea; two Freys stirred but sat back down when their captain didn't move. Tyrion offered a reward from his father to the swordsman for carrying word.
4. **Ser Rodrik ordered weapons confiscated** — Bronn disarmed Tyrion's party. Yoren, as a black brother, stepped aside and took no part.
5. **Catelyn announced they would ride for Winterfell** — She recruited volunteers to guard the prisoners. They departed in rain at a hard gallop; Tyrion was hooded and bound.
6. **The hood was removed at dawn on the high road** — Tyrion discovered they were heading east, not north. Catelyn had announced Winterfell loudly and often as deliberate misdirection.
7. **Days of hard travel along the high road** — Tyrion's bonds and hood were eventually removed as escape became impractical. They lost horses to the harsh terrain. Tyrion recognized they were heading for the Vale and Lysa Arryn.
8. **Rest stop argument about the dagger** — Tyrion argued for slowing down, insisted on his innocence regarding Bran's assassination attempt, and challenged Littlefinger's story about the dagger wager. He revealed that Littlefinger boasts of taking Catelyn's maidenhead, provoking fury.
9. **Kurleket threatens Tyrion with his dirk** — After Tyrion's claim about Littlefinger, Kurleket grabbed Tyrion by the hair, bared his throat, and offered to cut out his tongue. Catelyn ordered him to let Tyrion speak.
10. **Tyrion begins to undermine Littlefinger's story** — He asks how Littlefinger said the dagger was won and confirms it was supposedly during the tourney when Jaime was unhorsed by the Knight of Flowers. He sees a flicker of doubt in Catelyn's eyes.
11. **Mountain clansmen attack** — Lharys spots twenty to twenty-five riders (Milk Snakes or Moon Brothers). Catelyn orders preparations; Tyrion demands to be armed, arguing they need every sword.
12. **Tyrion and his men are armed** — Catelyn reluctantly agrees. Bronn gives Tyrion a double-bladed axe; Jyck gets his sword back; Morrec takes a bow and quiver.
13. **The battle** — Clansmen charge at dawn in boiled leather and mismatched armor. Ser Rodrik, Bronn, Chiggen, and Ser Willis ride to meet them. Ser Rodrik fights the leader in the shadowskin cloak, who is killed by an arrow to the throat. Jyck charges bareback and is cut down from behind.
14. **Tyrion fights and kills** — He kills a horse with the axe, then kills the unhorsed rider. He crushes Marillion's fingers under his heel. He fights on the fringes, darting from rock to rock, killing a wounded clansman and taking a halfhelm.
15. **Tyrion saves Catelyn Stark** — Three clansmen trap Catelyn against the mountainside. Tyrion attacks from behind, splitting one man's knee. Catelyn cuts a second man's throat. The third flees.
16. **Aftermath of the battle** — Three of their party dead (Kurleket, Mohor, Jyck) versus nine clansmen killed. The dead clansmen are thin, ragged, poorly equipped. Tyrion washes blood in the stream.
17. **Debate over burial** — Catelyn wants to bury the dead under cairns. Bronn refuses. Ser Rodrik advises against lingering. Catelyn relents.
18. **Tyrion keeps his weapons** — Lharys demands the dirk back, but Catelyn allows Tyrion to keep both dirk and axe, noting they may need him if attacked again. She makes clear she still does not trust him.
19. **The party rides on** — Ser Willis leads, Bronn takes the rear, Catelyn and Ser Rodrik in the middle. Marillion has broken ribs, a broken woodharp, and four broken fingers but has acquired the shadowskin cloak.
20. **Shadowcats heard behind them** — Within half a mile, they hear shadowcats growling and fighting over the corpses they left behind.
21. **Tyrion resumes his argument** — He rides up to Catelyn and states he never bets against his family, undermining Littlefinger's story about the dagger wager.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitter rage at being outwitted by Catelyn; cold fury at the Starks for multiple debts (the capture, the horse, the hard travel); grudging respect for Catelyn's cleverness; satisfaction and surprise at surviving his first battle; calculating and watchful throughout
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and plotting escape/revenge; undermining Catelyn's trust in Littlefinger's account of the dagger; mentally cataloguing his captors for future repayment ("A Lannister always paid his debts")
- **Key decisions made:** Submitted to capture at the inn rather than fighting (calculated survival); demanded to be armed when clansmen attacked; chose to save Catelyn's life despite internal impulse to let her die; resumed his argument about Littlefinger after the battle
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion tells Catelyn his father might not bother to pursue, calling it "only half a lie" — he knows Lord Tywin would act to defend House honor even if not for Tyrion personally; he internally congratulated himself on his subtlety before Catelyn's misdirection proved him wrong
