# AGOT — Tyrion IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 32
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister

- **location_primary:** The high road through the Mountains of the Moon
- **location_secondary:** The inn at the crossroads (recalled in memory)
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after Tyrion's capture at the inn; the party is traveling the eastern high road toward the Vale of Arryn
- **time_markers:** Predawn chill at chapter opening; dawn light seeping through hood cloth when they first stop on the high road; "long days later" after initial capture; dawn during the clansmen attack; sun scarcely moved after the battle ends; half a mile ridden after the battle before hearing shadowcats
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion watches sellswords butcher his horse for food in the predawn cold and reflects bitterly on his capture at the inn by Catelyn Stark. He recalls the scene at the inn — his surrender, the ride through the rain hooded and bound, and the moment he discovered they were on the eastern high road to the Eyrie rather than the kingsroad to Winterfell. On the high road, Tyrion argues with Catelyn about Littlefinger's lie regarding the dagger, but they are interrupted by a mountain clan attack. Tyrion fights with a double-bladed axe, kills two clansmen, and saves Catelyn Stark's life. After the battle, Catelyn allows Tyrion to keep his weapons, and as they ride on, Tyrion resumes his argument that Littlefinger lied about the dagger wager.

## Events & Actions
1. **Horse butchering** — Chiggen butchers Tyrion's dead horse in the predawn chill while Bronn and Tyrion watch. Tyrion recalls the mare was a gift from Jaime for his twenty-third name day.
2. **Tyrion's flashback to capture at the inn** — Tyrion recalls being seized at the inn: Jyck reaching for a sword, the innkeep shrieking, Catelyn displaying her scarred hands and accusing Tyrion, a dozen swords rising to help, Tyrion surrendering peacefully, weapons confiscated by Bronn, Ser Rodrik's order to take their weapons.
3. **Tyrion assesses the inn's crowd** — In memory, Tyrion notes only about a dozen of fifty responded to Catelyn's plea; the Freys sat back down when their captain failed to move; Tyrion subtly announces his father will pay for information.
4. **Departure from inn** — Catelyn orders immediate departure with fresh mounts and provisions; Tyrion is hooded, hands bound, and set on a horse in the rain.
5. **Hard gallop through the night** — They ride at hard gallop through rain; Tyrion is blinded by the hood, nearly falls repeatedly, rope chafes wrists raw, breathing is a struggle through wet cloth.
6. **Revelation of the eastern road** — At dawn, when the hood is removed, Tyrion sees the high road and snowcapped peaks and realizes Catelyn deceived everyone — she announced Winterfell but took the eastern road to the Vale.
7. **Days of hard travel on the high road** — Over subsequent days, Tyrion's bonds and hood are removed; the party loses horses; the land is harsh with shadowcats and mountain clans.
8. **Rest stop and argument about Littlefinger** — The party stops by a stream. Ser Willis and Ser Rodrik urge rest. Tyrion argues they are safe from Lannister pursuit and confronts Catelyn about Littlefinger's lie about the dagger.
9. **Tyrion reveals Littlefinger's boast** — Tyrion tells Catelyn that Littlefinger claims at court to have taken her maidenhead. Catelyn denies it angrily and defends Petyr's youthful love as real and pure. Kurleket holds a dirk to Tyrion's throat.
10. **Tyrion challenges the dagger story** — Tyrion asks how Littlefinger said he came by the dagger; Catelyn says he won it in a wager when Jaime was unhorsed by the Knight of Flowers. Tyrion is about to expose the flaw when interrupted.
11. **Lharys spots riders** — Lharys, posted as lookout on the ridge, screams a warning. He reports twenty to twenty-five riders, likely Milk Snakes or Moon Brothers, who have hidden watchers and know the party's position.
12. **Catelyn arms the prisoners** — Tyrion demands to be armed. Catelyn hesitates but agrees. Ser Rodrik tosses Jyck his sword; Morrec takes a bow; Bronn gives Tyrion a double-bladed axe.
13. **Mountain clan attack** — Lean dark clansmen in boiled leather charge out of the dawn. Ser Rodrik duels the big man in the shadowskin cloak. Bronn and Chiggen fight ferociously. Ser Willis charges with his morningstar shouting "Harrenhal!" Morrec and Lharys loose arrows. An arrow kills the clan leader.
14. **Tyrion's first kills** — Tyrion axes a charging horse in the throat, then buries the axe in the fallen rider's neck. He steps on Marillion's fingers and tells him to play dead. Tyrion fights on the fringes, hacking at horse legs, kills a wounded clansman, and takes a halfhelm from a dead man.
15. **Jyck killed** — Jyck is cut down from behind while fighting another man.
16. **Kurleket killed** — Tyrion stumbles over Kurleket's body; his pig face has been smashed in with a mace. Tyrion takes his dirk.
17. **Tyrion saves Catelyn** — Catelyn is trapped against the mountainside by three clansmen. Tyrion attacks from behind, splitting the first man's knee. The second man comes at Tyrion; Tyrion ducks under his sword and strikes with the axe. Catelyn slits the second man's throat from behind. The third man (mounted) flees.
18. **Battle aftermath** — Nine clansmen dead. Three of Catelyn's party dead: Kurleket, Mohor, and Jyck. Tyrion washes blood from his face in the stream.
19. **Bronn loots Jyck** — Bronn pulls Jyck's good boots off his corpse. Chiggen loots the clansmen's corpses.
20. **Burial argument** — Catelyn wants to bury the dead; Ser Willis says the soil is too stony; Catelyn suggests cairns; Bronn refuses. Ser Rodrik advises they cannot linger. Catelyn reluctantly agrees to ride on.
21. **Tyrion keeps his weapons** — Lharys asks for Tyrion's dirk back, but Catelyn lets him keep it and orders the axe returned too.
22. **Departure and aftermath** — Tyrion takes Jyck's spotted gelding. Ser Willis leads, Bronn at rear, Catelyn in the middle. Marillion has broken ribs, broken woodharp, broken fingers, but wears the shadowskin cloak. They hear shadowcats growling and fighting over corpses behind them.
23. **Tyrion resumes the dagger argument** — Tyrion rides up to Catelyn and says: "I never bet against my family" — the flaw in Littlefinger's story.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitter rage at being outwitted by Catelyn; cold, sore, and miserable from the journey; fearful but controlled during the battle; exhilarated and astonished to survive his first combat; sardonic humor throughout as a coping mechanism
- **Primary preoccupation:** Escaping captivity; discrediting Littlefinger's lie about the dagger; immediate survival on the dangerous high road
- **Key decisions made:** Surrendered peacefully at the inn rather than fight; demanded to be armed before the clan attack; chose to save Catelyn despite briefly thinking "let them have the bitch"; took Kurleket's dirk and a fallen clansman's halfhelm during battle; resumed the Littlefinger argument after the battle
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion initially congratulated himself on his subtlety at the inn (planting the idea of Lannister rewards) — but Catelyn outwitted him completely by going east. He prided himself on his cunning as "the only gift the gods had seen fit to give him" yet was outmaneuvered at every turn.
