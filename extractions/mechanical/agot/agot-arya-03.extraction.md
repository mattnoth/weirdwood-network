- **chapter_summary:** Arya catches the one-eared black tomcat in the Red Keep after a long chase, but is discovered by Myrcella and Tommen, who don't recognize her. She flees and gets lost in the dungeons, where she discovers a room full of dragon skulls. Deeper underground, she overhears two men discussing plots involving the Hand, the wolf and the lion, a pregnant princess, and the need to delay or hasten war. She follows them through tunnels and emerges from a sewer at the river. Returning to the castle, she reports what she heard to her father, who dismisses the men as mummers. A Night's Watch brother named Yoren arrives with urgent news about Catelyn's abduction of Tyrion, which Ned hears privately.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya catches the one-eared black tomcat** — After a long chase through the castle, Arya corners and catches the last cat she hadn't been able to catch, hugging it to her chest and kissing it between the eyes.
2. **Myrcella and Tommen discover Arya** — The royal children, accompanied by a septa and two Lannister guards, find Arya in the alley. They mistake her for a boy.
3. **Arya escapes the royal party** — When the guard Godwyn reaches for her, Arya uses her water-dancing training to dodge, bowls over Tommen, and flees through the castle.
4. **Arya loses herself in the dungeons** — She climbs through a window and descends into a pitch-dark cellar, eventually finding herself in a room full of dragon skulls.
5. **Arya discovers the dragon skull room** — She touches the skulls, feels their teeth, and overcomes her initial fear by remembering Syrio's teachings.
6. **Arya overhears two conspirators** — From the top of a great underground well/shaft, she sees two men ascending with a torch. They discuss the need to delay or hasten conflict between the wolf and the lion, a pregnant princess, the Hand's danger, and various political players.
7. **The stout man seals the well entrance** — He activates a mechanism that drops a huge stone slab from the ceiling, concealing the well behind solid rock.
8. **The conspirators discuss further plans** — They mention needing gold, fifty more "birds," young children who know their letters, and the risks of keeping tongues silent.
9. **Arya follows the conspirators through the tunnels** — She trails them for miles through increasingly rough tunnels, from dressed stone to raw earth with timber supports, ending knee-deep in foul water.
10. **Arya emerges from a sewer at the river** — She exits a sewer mouth, strips, and bathes in the river to clean herself. She washes her clothes in the moonlight.
11. **Arya returns to the Red Keep gatehouse** — She finds the portcullis down and gates barred. Gold cloaks refuse her entry, mistaking her for a beggar boy.
12. **Arya reveals her identity** — She declares herself Arya Stark and demands entry, naming Jory Cassel and Vayon Poole as people who can confirm her identity.
13. **Arya reports to her father** — Harwin and Fat Tom escort her to Ned's solar, where she tells him about the two men and their plot. Ned dismisses them as mummers.
14. **Yoren arrives with urgent news** — A Night's Watch brother named Yoren arrives, having ridden hard from an inn. He brings news about Catelyn's abduction of Tyrion and warns that sellswords and riders have scattered — some to King's Landing, some to Casterly Rock.
15. **Arya is sent to bed** — Desmond escorts Arya to her bedchamber; she asks him about her father's safety and the Lannisters' numbers.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Exhilarated and proud when catching the cat; panicked when discovered by Myrcella and Tommen; afraid in the dark dungeons but masters her fear using Syrio's teachings; urgently worried when overhearing the conspirators; frustrated when her father dismisses her; anxious about her father's safety at chapter's end
- **Primary preoccupation:** First — catching the black tom as Syrio's challenge. Then — escaping the royal children unrecognized. Then — fear and wonder in the dungeons. Then — the conspiracy she overheard and trying to convince her father of the danger. Finally — worry about her father's safety given the Lannisters' superior numbers.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to flee rather than be identified by Myrcella and Tommen; decides to follow the conspirators through the tunnels; decides to reveal her identity to the gate guards; decides to report what she heard to her father
- **Self-deception flags:** None — Arya is earnest and direct throughout, though she doesn't fully understand the political significance of what she overheard
