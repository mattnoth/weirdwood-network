# AGOT — Eddard XII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 46
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard XII
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard XII
- **location_primary:** Tower of the Hand, Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Godswood of the Red Keep
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after the street fight with Jaime Lannister; Robert is away hunting; shortly before Sansa and Arya are to sail on the Wind Witch
- **time_markers:** "an hour after the Grand Maester had left" (Littlefinger's visit); "Late that afternoon" (summoning Tomard); "She came to him at sunset"; "three days hence" (the girls' scheduled departure on the Wind Witch); "A dozen years" (length of Robert and Cersei's marriage); "some ninety years ago" (Tya Lannister wed Gowen Baratheon); "Thirty years before that" (another Lannister-Baratheon marriage); "three hundred years" (Targaryen intermarriage); stars coming out at chapter's end
- **chapter_summary:** Ned recovers from his broken leg in the Tower of the Hand. Grand Maester Pycelle warns him that Tywin Lannister is angry about the force sent after Gregor Clegane. Littlefinger visits and reports that sellswords are gathering at Casterly Rock, that the white hart was found killed by wolves, that Robert hunts a monstrous boar, and that Joffrey and the Hound have returned to King's Landing. Ned reflects on what he has learned about Cersei's children and decides he must act before Robert returns. He has Tomard carry a message summoning Cersei to the godswood, where he confronts her with the truth that all three of her children are Jaime's. Cersei confirms the incest, attempts to seduce Ned into silence, and when he refuses and warns her to flee before Robert returns, she delivers the warning: "When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die."

## Events & Actions
1. **Pycelle treats Ned's leg** — Grand Maester Pycelle checks on Ned's healing broken leg, leaves milk of the poppy, and warns that Tywin Lannister is furious about the party sent after Gregor Clegane.
2. **Ned dismisses Pycelle's concern** — Ned says Lord Beric rides under the king's banner and Robert will enforce his justice. Pycelle departs hurriedly.
3. **Ned reflects on Pycelle's loyalty** — Suspects Pycelle went straight to Cersei, and that Cersei instructed him to deliver Tywin's threat.
4. **Ned drinks honeyed wine and reflects** — Asks himself what Jon Arryn would have done, wonders if Jon acted on his discovery and died for it.
5. **Ned recalls Sansa's innocent observation** — Remembers Sansa saying Joffrey was "not the least bit like that old drunken king," which crystallized the truth for him.
6. **Littlefinger visits** — Reports that freeriders and sellswords are flocking to Casterly Rock, the white hart was found killed by wolves, Robert pursues a monstrous boar, and Joffrey and the Hound have returned to King's Landing.
7. **Littlefinger notices Malleon's book** — Reads the title aloud; Ned reveals Jon Arryn was studying it when taken sick; Littlefinger deflects with a quip and departs.
8. **Ned assesses his allies** — Finds almost no one in King's Landing he trusts: Littlefinger saved his own skin during Jaime's attack, Varys knows too much, Pycelle serves Cersei, Barristan is rigid.
9. **Ned considers the urgency** — Robert will return soon; honor requires Ned to tell him. Sansa and Arya are scheduled to sail on the Wind Witch in three days.
10. **Ned dreams of Rhaegar's murdered children** — Remembers Tywin laying the bodies beneath the Iron Throne wrapped in crimson cloaks. Resolves he cannot let that happen again.
11. **Ned considers Robert's capacity for mercy** — Notes Robert pardoned Pycelle, Varys, and Balon Greyjoy, but recognizes this betrayal is different: "poison in the dark, a knife thrust to the soul."
12. **Ned acknowledges his duty** — Feels obligation to Robert, the realm, Jon Arryn's shade, and Bran, who must have stumbled on the truth.
13. **Ned summons Fat Tom** — Tells him to double the guard on the Tower of the Hand and deliver a sealed message.
14. **Ned is carried to the godswood** — Tomard and Varly help him descend the tower steps and cross the bailey.
15. **Ned waits in the godswood** — Sits beside the heart tree (a faceless brown oak); finds peace in the silence, birdsong, and crickets.
16. **Cersei arrives at sunset** — Comes alone, dressed simply in hunting greens, with a bruise on her face from Robert.
17. **Ned tells Cersei he knows the truth** — Declares he knows what Jon Arryn died for.
18. **Cersei confirms the incest** — Admits Jaime has been her lover since childhood; invokes Targaryen precedent; says they are "one person in two bodies."
19. **Cersei confirms Bran saw them** — Acknowledges Bran witnessed them, justifies it by saying she loves her children.
20. **Ned states all three children are Jaime's** — Cersei confirms, saying "Thank the gods."
21. **Ned reviews Malleon's evidence** — Recalls the Baratheon-Lannister marriages always produced black-haired children; "the gold yielding before the coal."
22. **Cersei reveals she aborted Robert's child** — Says Robert got her with child once, and "my brother found a woman to cleanse me"; has not let Robert inside her for years.
23. **Cersei reveals Robert's wedding night** — On their wedding night, Robert called her "Lyanna" while in bed.
24. **Ned tells Cersei he pities them both** — Thinks of "pale blue roses."
25. **Cersei attempts to seduce Ned** — Touches his leg and face, offers alliance, argues the realm needs a strong Hand and no one wants war.
26. **Ned rejects the offer** — Asks if she made the same offer to Jon Arryn; she slaps him.
27. **Cersei attacks Ned's honor** — Brings up his bastard, asks about the mother: "Some Dornish peasant you raped?" — references Ashara Dayne throwing herself into the sea, asks about "the child you stole."
28. **Ned warns Cersei to flee** — Tells her to take her children and leave before Robert returns; suggests the Free Cities, Summer Isles, or Port of Ibben; warns Robert's wrath will follow.
29. **Cersei warns Ned** — Says he should have taken the realm for himself when he found Jaime on the Iron Throne; delivers: "When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die."
30. **Cersei departs** — Raises her hood and leaves Ned alone in the darkening godswood under a blue-black sky.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pain-ridden but determined; deeply conflicted between duty and mercy; haunted by memories of Rhaegar's murdered children; momentary grief at the mention of Lyanna; disgust at the truth of Cersei's children; distrust of nearly everyone around him; resigned to the necessity of acting
- **Primary preoccupation:** What to do with the knowledge that Cersei's children are not Robert's — how to tell Robert while preventing another massacre of children; urgency of getting Sansa and Arya out of King's Landing
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Decides to confront Cersei directly before telling Robert; (2) Warns Cersei to flee with her children before Robert returns; (3) Orders the guard doubled on the Tower of the Hand; (4) Resolves that he must tell Robert the truth when the king returns
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned is "not near as confident of Robert as he pretended" but projects certainty to Cersei and Pycelle; believes warning Cersei is merciful but does not consider that it gives her time to act against him; trusts that Robert's return will resolve matters; assumes his honor-driven approach will work in a political environment where it manifestly doesn't
