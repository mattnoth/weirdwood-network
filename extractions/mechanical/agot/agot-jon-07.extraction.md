# AGOT — Jon VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 53
- **pov_character:** Jon Snow
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon VII
- **first_available:** AGOT Jon VII
- **location_primary:** The haunted forest beyond the Wall; Castle Black
- **location_secondary:** Lord Commander's Tower, common hall, storeroom at the base of the Wall, stables
- **approximate_timeline:** Mid-book; summer is ending ("spirit summer" drawing to a close), close to half a year since Benjen Stark departed
- **time_markers:** "The morning was unnaturally warm" (discovery of bodies); "well past midday by the time they started back"; afternoon passes "as if in a dream"; "by the time the sun went down" (evening meal); night (wight attack); candle burns out during the night; moonlight floods the solar during the fight; close on half a year since Benjen left; summer has lasted ten years; Jon was a babe in arms when summer began
- **chapter_summary:** Rangers discover the corpses of Othor and Jafer Flowers — two of Benjen Stark's missing men — in the woods beyond the Wall. Sam Tarly observes that the bodies show no signs of normal decomposition: no blood pooling, no rot, no animal scavenging, and their eyes have turned blue. Mormont orders the bodies brought back to Castle Black for Maester Aemon to examine. Upon return, Jon learns that King Robert is dead and Lord Eddard has been imprisoned for treason. After Jon attacks Ser Alliser Thorne for calling him a "traitor's bastard," he is confined to his cell. That night, the corpse of Othor rises as a wight and kills Jon's guard. Jon and Ghost fight the wight in Mormont's solar, and Jon ultimately sets it ablaze with an oil lamp.

## Events & Actions
1. **Discovery of corpses** — Ser Jaremy Rykker identifies the bodies of Othor and Jafer Flowers in the woods beyond the Wall, both men of Benjen Stark's ranging party.
2. **Mormont examines the scene** — The Lord Commander dismounts and questions Ser Jaremy about how the deaths went undetected so close to the Wall.
3. **Dogs refuse the corpses** — Bass the kennelmaster cannot get the hounds to take the scent; they yowl, fight their leashes, and one bitch lunges at Chett and escapes into the woods. Ghost is the only animal unafraid.
4. **Sam's forensic observations** — Sam notes the absence of bleeding from Ghost's amputation wound, the lack of rot or maggots, no animal scavenging, and no blood on the ground despite massive wounds.
5. **Dywen notes the blue eyes** — Dywen observes that Othor never had blue eyes before; Ser Jaremy confirms neither did Jafer Flowers. Silence falls over the party.
6. **Rangers suggest burning the bodies** — Anonymous voices urge burning; Mormont refuses, wanting Maester Aemon to examine them first.
7. **Difficulty transporting corpses** — Horses refuse to carry the bodies; rangers must hack branches into crude slings and carry them on foot.
8. **Mormont orders a search** — Commands Ser Jaremy to search every inch within ten leagues for Ben Stark and any others, dead or alive.
9. **Return to Castle Black** — Party rides back; Jon notes the Wall weeping and the unnaturally warm weather. Ghost vanishes into trees then reappears with bloody muzzle.
10. **Mormont commends Sam and Jon** — The Old Bear tells Sam "You're fat but you're not stupid" and praises both for their observations.
11. **Horn sounds** — One blast of the watchman's horn signals returning rangers.
12. **Bowen Marsh delivers urgent news** — The Lord Steward meets them at the first gate, says a bird has arrived with an important letter.
13. **Jon notices something wrong** — Ser Alliser Thorne stares with a half smile; Donal Noye wishes Jon well; people watch him with knowing eyes.
14. **Bodies stored in ice cell** — The dead men are placed in a storeroom carved from the ice at the Wall's base.
15. **Jon learns the king is dead** — Pyp tells Jon that Robert Baratheon has died, learned from a guard who overheard Clydas reading the letter.
16. **Jon learns of Eddard's arrest** — Mormont tells Jon that Lord Eddard has been imprisoned and charged with treason — plotting with Robert's brothers to deny Joffrey the throne.
17. **Mormont plans to write a letter** — Intends to appeal to Pycelle, Stannis, and Ser Barristan to let Eddard take the black.
18. **Mormont warns Jon not to act stupidly** — Reminds Jon his duty is to the Night's Watch now.
19. **Jon attacks Ser Alliser Thorne** — In the common hall at evening meal, after Thorne calls him "a traitor's bastard," Jon vaults onto a table with a dagger and slashes at Thorne's face. Sam, Pyp, Grenn, and Toad restrain him.
20. **Jon confined to his cell** — His weapons are confiscated; a guard is posted; friends forbidden to visit; Ghost is allowed to stay.
21. **Jon's restless night** — Jon sits against the wall staring at his candle, which eventually burns out. He dozes.
22. **Ghost alerts Jon** — Ghost stands on hind legs scrabbling at the door, baring fangs in a silent snarl; deep gouges in the wood.
23. **Jon discovers dead guard** — Opens his door to find the guard sprawled on the steps with his head twisted completely around.
24. **Sounds from above** — Jon hears a boot scraping on stone and a latch turning from Mormont's chambers.
25. **Jon takes the guard's sword** — Arms himself and climbs the tower steps with Ghost.
26. **Wight attacks** — The reanimated corpse of Othor, cloaked and hooded with glowing blue eyes, moves toward Mormont's sleeping cell. Ghost attacks.
27. **Jon fights the wight** — Jon cuts off the wight's arm; the severed arm continues to move and claws at his leg. Jon recognizes Othor's face. The wight feels no pain, sheds no blood.
28. **Wight overwhelms Jon** — Othor slams Jon down, forces black corpse fingers into his throat, choking him. Ghost pulls the wight off.
29. **Mormont appears** — Emerges from his sleeping cell naked with an oil lamp; the severed arm crawls toward him.
30. **Jon burns the wight** — Jon kicks the arm away, seizes the lamp, flings it into the window drapes. The hangings ignite. Jon grabs burning cloth and whips it onto the wight. The raven screams "Burn!"

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Progression from numbness and unease (discovery of corpses) → relief at returning to the Wall → shock and grief (father's imprisonment) → fury (attacking Thorne) → misery and defiance in confinement → terror and desperate courage (fighting the wight)
- **Primary preoccupation:** His father's fate; whether Eddard will live or die; his identity as a bastard and a brother of the Night's Watch; the tension between duty and family
- **Key decisions made:** Resolves to ask his father about his mother ("Even if she was a whore, I don't care, I want to know"); attacks Ser Alliser with a dagger (impulsive, not planned); takes up the guard's sword and climbs to fight the wight; seizes the lamp and sets the wight on fire
- **Self-deception flags:** "It is only a wood... and they're only dead men" (trying to suppress fear); "The Others are only a story" (dismissing his own intuition about the supernatural threat); "I will not sleep tonight" (then dozes off); dismisses his recurring Winterfell crypt dream as "only a dream" while clearly deeply disturbed by it; tells himself the sound outside is just the guard
