## Chapter Metadata

- **location_primary:** The inn at the crossroads
- **location_secondary:** Kingsroad (north of the Trident), the crossroads junction
- **approximate_timeline:** During the lead-up to the Hand's tourney in King's Landing; Catelyn is returning north from King's Landing
- **time_markers:** "near dark when they reached it" (arrival at inn); "for the past week, the travelers had been thick as flies" (week of heavy road traffic); "we left King's Landing a fortnight ago" (Catelyn's cover story); supper bell rings at dusk; "a wet dusk was falling outside"
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn and Ser Rodrik ride north on the kingsroad in the rain, debating whether to risk stopping at the crossroads inn. After passing Lord Jason Mallister's party unrecognized, Catelyn decides the inn is safe. While eating supper and conversing with a singer named Marillion, Tyrion Lannister arrives unexpectedly with Yoren of the Night's Watch and two servants. Marillion inadvertently draws Tyrion's attention to Catelyn, and Tyrion recognizes her. Catelyn seizes the moment, calling upon the Tully bannermen present — Whent, Bracken, and Frey men — to help her arrest Tyrion for the attempted murder of her son Bran.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn and Ser Rodrik ride north on the kingsroad in the rain** — Ser Rodrik urges her to cover her head; she refuses, enjoying the soft southern rain and reminiscing about childhood at Riverrun.
2. **They debate stopping at the crossroads inn** — Ser Rodrik warns it's risky; Catelyn recalls Masha Heddle and the inn from her youth.
3. **Lord Jason Mallister's column passes them on the road** — Armed men fording a swollen stream, heading south to the Hand's tourney. Ser Rodrik urges Catelyn to pull up her hood.
4. **Catelyn studies Lord Jason and is not recognized** — She observes how he has aged; he gives only a curt nod to apparent strangers. This convinces her the inn is safe.
5. **They arrive at the crossroads inn near dark** — Masha Heddle assigns them two garret rooms under the bell tower; she is fatter and greyer, no longer offering smiles or sweet cakes.
6. **Catelyn reflects on her route options while waiting for supper** — She considers turning west to Riverrun (father bedridden), east to the Eyrie (dangerous mountain road), and decides to continue north to Winterfell.
7. **Catelyn surveys the riverlands from memory** — She catalogs the Tully bannermen and worries about their loyalty if war comes, recalling the Trident and Lord Frey's late arrival.
8. **Catelyn and Ser Rodrik adopt a cover story** — They will pose as father and daughter; Ser Rodrik slips and calls her "my lady," then corrects himself.
9. **They eat supper in the crowded common room** — She notes Bracken men-at-arms, a large Frey party, and various travelers. They sit near the kitchen.
10. **Conversation with Marillion** — The young singer boasts of his talent, discusses the upcoming tourney, claims familiarity with Riverrun and Edmure.
11. **Tyrion Lannister arrives at the inn** — A servant announces him; he arrives with Yoren of the Night's Watch and two servants. He buys a room from a freerider with a gold coin.
12. **Marillion inadvertently draws Tyrion's attention** — He offers to sing for Tyrion, who declines. Tyrion's gaze sweeps the room and finds Catelyn.
13. **Tyrion recognizes and greets Catelyn** — He addresses her as "Lady Stark," exposing her identity to the entire common room.
14. **Catelyn seizes the moment and arrests Tyrion** — She stands, identifies herself as Catelyn Tully to Masha Heddle, then rallies the Whent, Bracken, and Frey men by invoking their allegiance to her father. She publicly accuses Tyrion of conspiring to murder Bran and calls for his arrest in King Robert's name. A dozen swords are drawn.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Nostalgic early in the chapter (childhood memories of Riverrun, Lysa, Littlefinger, her father); anxious about being recognized; deeply conflicted about her route (duty to Winterfell vs. desire to see her father and sister); shocked and afraid when Tyrion arrives; coldly determined when she decides to arrest him. Sees Bran "choking, drowning on his own blood" when looking at Tyrion.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting safely back to Winterfell without being recognized; weighing whether to visit Riverrun or the Eyrie; concern about potential war and the loyalty of Tully bannermen. After Tyrion's arrival, focused entirely on whether to act and how.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides the inn is safe after Mallister fails to recognize her; decides against turning west to Riverrun (father too ill) or east to the Eyrie (road too dangerous); decides to continue north to Winterfell; decides to publicly arrest Tyrion when he exposes her identity, seizing the moment rather than fleeing.
- **Self-deception flags:** States as fact that Tyrion "conspired to murder my son" — this is Catelyn's firm belief, stated as certainty in her public accusation.
