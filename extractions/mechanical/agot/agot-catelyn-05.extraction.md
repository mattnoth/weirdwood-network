# AGOT — Catelyn V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 29
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn V
- **first_available:** AGOT Catelyn V
- **location_primary:** Inn at the crossroads
- **location_secondary:** Kingsroad (north of the crossroads), the crossroads itself
- **approximate_timeline:** During the lead-up to the Hand's tourney; travelers thick on the kingsroad heading south to King's Landing
- **time_markers:** "near dark when they reached it" (the inn); supper bell rings at dusk; "a wet dusk was falling outside"; "We left King's Landing a fortnight ago" (Catelyn's cover story); Lord Frey plans to take a new wife on his ninetieth name day; Hoster Tully bedridden "these past two years"; "the summer had been long and peaceful"; "past week, the travelers had been thick as flies upon the kingsroad"
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn and Ser Rodrik ride north on the kingsroad in the rain, passing Lord Jason Mallister's party unrecognized. They take rooms at the inn at the crossroads, run by Masha Heddle. Catelyn considers her options — west to Riverrun, east to the Eyrie, or north to Winterfell — and decides to continue north. At supper she encounters the singer Marillion and converses with him until Tyrion Lannister arrives unexpectedly with Yoren of the Night's Watch. Tyrion recognizes Catelyn, and she seizes the moment to publicly accuse him of conspiring to murder Bran and calls upon the Tully bannermen present to help her take him prisoner.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn and Ser Rodrik ride north on the kingsroad in the rain** — Ser Rodrik urges Catelyn to cover her head; she enjoys the southern rain, which reminds her of childhood at Riverrun
2. **Catelyn recalls childhood memories** — Playing in the rain at Riverrun with Edmure; making mud pies with Lysa and feeding them to Littlefinger; sweet cakes from Masha Heddle
3. **Ser Rodrik suggests avoiding the inn** — He recommends a small holdfast to maintain anonymity; they hear riders ahead
4. **Lord Jason Mallister's column passes on the road** — Armed men fording a swollen stream; indigo cloaks, silver eagle of Seagard; riding south to the Hand's tourney with knights, his son Patrek, and squires
5. **Catelyn studies Lord Jason without being recognized** — Notes his aged appearance (brown hair salted with white, face chiseled gaunt); he gives only a curt courtesy nod to strangers; Patrek doesn't even look at them
6. **Catelyn decides it is safe to stay at the inn** — Their disguise as common travelers held against a lord who knew her
7. **Arrival at the inn at the crossroads near dark** — Masha Heddle assigns them two garret rooms under the bell tower; takes their coin; orders boots left downstairs; no smiles, no sweet cakes
8. **Catelyn reflects on her route options from the crossroads** — West to Riverrun (father bedridden two years), east to the Eyrie (road dangerous with shadowcats, rock slides, mountain clans), north to Winterfell; decides to continue north
9. **Catelyn surveys the riverlands in memory** — Catalogs Tully bannermen: Blackwoods and Brackens (ever enemies), Lady Whent (last of line in Harrenhal), Lord Frey (outlived seven wives, arrived late to Trident); Darrys, Rygers, Mootons fought for Rhaegar; worries about loyalty in war
10. **Catelyn and Ser Rodrik adopt false identities** — She suggests posing as father and daughter until past the Neck; Ser Rodrik accidentally calls her "my lady," then corrects to "my daughter"
11. **They enter the common room for supper** — Crowded with diverse travelers: dyers with black and purple hands, rivermen reeking of fish, ironsmith thick with muscle, wizened old septon, hard-bitten sellswords, soft plump merchants; Bracken men-at-arms (3) by the fire; large Frey party (20+) in blue steel ringmail and silvery grey capes; Whent man in the corner
12. **Catelyn and Ser Rodrik sit near the singer Marillion** — He greets them with "Seven blessings"; peppers them with questions; Catelyn says they left King's Landing a fortnight ago
13. **Marillion boasts of his singing career** — Claims to be better than Alia of Braavos; lost silver betting on the Kingslayer, plans to bet on Ser Loras next; claims Riverrun keeps a chamber for him and Edmure is "like a brother"; dismisses the North as "blizzards and bearskins"
14. **Tyrion Lannister arrives at the inn** — His servant calls for stabling and a room; accompanied by Yoren of the Night's Watch and two servants; Masha has no rooms
15. **Tyrion secures a room with gold** — Flicks a gold coin to a freerider who yields his room; orders roast fowl, best wine, and double portions for his men; invites Yoren to sup with him
16. **Marillion calls attention to Tyrion** — Bounds to his feet and offers to sing "the lay of your father's great victory at King's Landing"; Tyrion declines dryly: "Nothing would be more likely to ruin my supper"
17. **Tyrion recognizes Catelyn** — His mismatched eyes sweep past Marillion and find Catelyn; she turns her face away but too late; he addresses her as "Lady Stark, what an unexpected pleasure"
18. **Catelyn's identity is revealed to the room** — Masha Heddle and Marillion react; muttering and eyes upon her; Catelyn acknowledges she "was still Catelyn Tully the last time I bedded here"
19. **Catelyn rallies the Tully bannermen present** — Appeals in sequence to the Whent man-at-arms (black bat of Harrenhal), the three Bracken men (red stallion), and the twenty-plus Frey soldiers (twin towers), invoking their oaths to her father
20. **Frey captain reports Lord Walder's plans** — Lord Walder is well, plans to take a new wife on his ninetieth name day, has asked Lord Tully to attend the wedding
21. **Tyrion sniggles at Lord Frey's marriage plans** — This confirms Catelyn's resolve to act
22. **Catelyn publicly accuses Tyrion of conspiring to murder Bran** — Points at Tyrion and calls on those present in the name of King Robert to seize him and return him to Winterfell for the king's justice; Ser Rodrik stands at her side with sword drawn
23. **A dozen swords are drawn** — The bannermen respond to Catelyn's call; Tyrion is taken prisoner
