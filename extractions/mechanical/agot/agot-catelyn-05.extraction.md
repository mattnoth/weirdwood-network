# AGOT — Catelyn V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 29
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn V
- **first_available:** AGOT Catelyn V
- **location_primary:** The inn at the crossroads
- **location_secondary:** The kingsroad (south of the crossroads), the crossroads junction
- **approximate_timeline:** During the lead-up to the Hand's tourney in King's Landing; Catelyn and Ser Rodrik are traveling north from King's Landing
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn and Ser Rodrik ride north on the kingsroad disguised as common travelers. They pass a Mallister party unrecognized and stop at the crossroads inn kept by Masha Heddle. Catelyn considers branching west to Riverrun or east to the Eyrie but resolves to continue north to Winterfell. While eating supper in the common room, Tyrion Lannister arrives and recognizes Catelyn. She responds by invoking the loyalty of Tully bannermen present — Whent, Bracken, and Frey men — and publicly accuses Tyrion of conspiring to murder Bran, calling on the assembled swords to seize him.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn and Ser Rodrik ride north on the kingsroad** — They travel in rain, disguised as common travelers, heading for Winterfell.
2. **Encounter with the Mallister party** — Lord Jason Mallister, his son Patrek, their knights and squires ride south to the Hand's tourney. Lord Jason gives a courtesy nod but does not recognize Catelyn.
3. **Arrival at the crossroads inn** — Catelyn and Ser Rodrik take the last two rooms under the bell tower. Masha Heddle assigns them garrets and warns about the bell and mud.
4. **Catelyn deliberates her route** — She considers three paths: west to Riverrun (rejected because her father is too ill), east to the Eyrie (rejected as too dangerous with only one knight), and north to Winterfell (chosen, where her sons and duty await).
5. **Supper in the common room** — Catelyn and Ser Rodrik eat among a crowd of travelers. She notes armed men wearing Bracken and Frey sigils.
6. **Conversation with Marillion** — The young singer boasts of his talent, mentions betting on the Kingslayer and then Ser Loras at tourneys, and claims familiarity with Riverrun and Edmure.
7. **Tyrion Lannister arrives at the inn** — He enters with Yoren of the Night's Watch and two servants, seeking a room. He buys a freerider's room with a gold coin.
8. **Tyrion recognizes Catelyn** — Marillion's attempt to sing for Tyrion draws the dwarf's attention across the room, where he spots and identifies Catelyn.
9. **Catelyn seizes the initiative** — She publicly identifies herself, rallies the Tully bannermen present (Whent, Bracken, and Frey men), accuses Tyrion of conspiring to murder Bran, and calls on them to seize him in the name of King Robert.
10. **Tyrion is taken prisoner** — A dozen swords are drawn in response to Catelyn's call.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Nostalgic early in the chapter (memories of Riverrun childhood), then anxious and calculating at the inn, shifting to fierce resolve when she decides to seize Tyrion
- **Primary preoccupation:** Returning safely to Winterfell unrecognized; protecting her family; the threat posed by the Lannisters
- **Key decisions made:** (1) To stop at the crossroads inn despite the risk of recognition. (2) To continue north to Winterfell rather than divert to Riverrun or the Eyrie. (3) To adopt the disguise of father and daughter with Ser Rodrik. (4) To publicly confront and seize Tyrion Lannister rather than let him pass.
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn states as fact that Tyrion "conspired to murder my son" — she presents an accusation as established truth to rally support. Her certainty about Tyrion's guilt is absolute in her speech, brooking no doubt.
