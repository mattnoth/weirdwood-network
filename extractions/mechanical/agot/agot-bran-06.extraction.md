Bran watches from the walls, unable to visit the crowded winter town due to Robb's orders. In the godswood, Bran prays to the old gods and encounters Osha, who warns that the true threat lies north beyond the Wall. At a feast for the Karstarks, Bran overhears cruel remarks about his condition and tells Maester Luwin he wants to learn magic. Two days later, Robb rides south to war, leaving Bran as lord of Winterfell.

## Events & Actions
1. **Karstark arrival** — Lord Rickard Karstark arrives at Winterfell with three hundred horsemen and near two thousand foot, the last bannermen to arrive, bringing Robb's total force to approximately twelve thousand men.
2. **Bran watches from the walls** — Bran observes the arrival from a guard turret atop the outer wall, perched on Hodor's shoulders, using Maester Luwin's bronze far-eye.
3. **Bran descends and crosses to the godswood** — Hodor carries Bran down the tower via chisel-cut handholds and across the drawbridge, passing the Karstark lancers entering the castle.
4. **Summer spooks the horses** — Bran whistles for Summer, and the direwolf's scent sends the Karstark horses into a frenzy.
5. **Bran prays in the godswood** — Bran prays to the old gods to keep Robb safe, bring everyone home, and help Rickon understand.
6. **Rickon's wildness recalled** — Rickon has been wild since learning Robb is leaving; he hid in the crypts with a rusted sword, and Shaggydog attacked Gage and Mikken before Robb and Grey Wind subdued the wolf. Shaggydog is chained in the kennels.
7. **Bannermen test Robb (recalled)** — Bolton and Glover demand battle command; Maege Mormont offers her granddaughter; Lord Cerwyn brings his daughter; Lord Hornwood brings gifts seeking concessions. Robb handles each with cool courtesy.
8. **Greatjon Umber confrontation** — The Greatjon threatens to take his forces home over march order; he flings ale, knocks down Hallis Mollen, overturns a table, and draws his greatsword. Grey Wind bites off two of his fingers. Robb warns that baring steel against your liege lord means death. The Greatjon laughs and becomes Robb's staunchest champion.
9. **Robb confesses fear to Bran** — That night, Robb comes to Bran's bedchamber pale and shaken, admitting he was terrified of the Greatjon and unsettled by Roose Bolton's silent stare. He wishes Father were here.
10. **Rumors and Sansa's letter** — Various conflicting rumors arrive about events in the south. A raven brings Sansa's letter (in Father's seal) saying Robert is dead, Father conspired treason, and Robb must swear fealty to Joffrey. The letter says nothing of Arya.
11. **Bran encounters Osha in the godswood** — Osha appears and tells Bran the old gods are answering his prayers through the rustling leaves, but they have no power in the south where the weirwoods were cut down. She warns that the true threat is north — white walkers, wights, cold winds rising.
12. **Osha mentions Mance Rayder** — Osha describes Mance as a former black crow from the Shadow Tower who calls himself King-beyond-the-Wall but has never tasted real winter.
13. **Hodor emerges naked** — Hodor returns from the hot pools naked and dripping; Osha comments he has giant's blood in him.
14. **Discussion of giants** — Osha tells Bran giants still live beyond the Wall; her brother killed one ten feet tall. Maester Luwin says giants are all dead.
15. **Osha urges Bran to warn Robb** — Osha says Robb is marching the wrong way; he should go north, not south. Bran promises to tell Robb.
16. **Karstark feast** — Bran hosts the feast in Robb's place, formally welcoming the Karstarks. Eddard and Torrhen Karstark mutter cruel remarks about Bran being broken.
17. **Bran tells Luwin he wants magic** — Bran says "I want to learn magic. The crow promised that I would fly." Luwin offers maester training instead and dismisses Osha's warnings as folly.
18. **Robb's departure** — Two days after the feast, Robb rides out at red dawn. He tells Bran he is now the lord in Winterfell. Bran asks him to tell Rickon he'll return. Rickon refuses to come down to say farewell. Robb rides out with Grey Wind, Hallis Mollen carrying the Stark banner, flanked by Theon Greyjoy and the Greatjon.
19. **Winterfell emptied** — After Robb departs, Bran looks around at who remains: women, children, old men, and Hodor. Winterfell feels deserted and dead.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Miserable, scared, ashamed, lonely, determined. Bran feels helpless and humiliated by his disability — remembering how he used to climb and fight. He is terrified by the prospect of Robb leaving and everyone not returning, recalling that Starks who go south don't come back. He feels a dull ache knowing people will never cheer for him as they do for Robb. At the chapter's end he feels deserted.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Fear of losing Robb and the rest of his family; shame about his broken body; desire to be useful and not helpless. Also grappling with his new role as lord of Winterfell.
- **Key decisions made:** Promises Osha he will tell Robb about the northern threat (but never gets the chance). Tells Maester Luwin he wants to learn magic. Accepts (miserably) the role of lord in Winterfell.
- **Self-deception flags:** None clearly evident. Bran is remarkably honest with himself about his fear and limitations.
