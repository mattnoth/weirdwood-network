# AGOT — Bran VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 54
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran VI
- **first_available:** AGOT Bran VI
- **location_primary:** Winterfell
- **location_secondary:** Godswood (Winterfell), Great Hall (Winterfell), yard beneath the gatehouse; mentioned: Karhold, the Dreadfort, King's Landing (Red Keep), Riverrun, the Eyrie, Dragonstone, the Neck, the kingsroad, the Citadel (Oldtown), Shadow Tower, Beyond the Wall
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly before Robb marches south with the northern host; the Karstarks are the last bannermen to arrive; Robb departs two days after the feast for Lord Karstark
- **chapter_summary:** The Karstarks arrive at Winterfell as the last of Robb's bannermen, bringing the northern host to roughly twelve thousand men. Bran watches from a tower on Hodor's shoulders, forbidden from leaving the castle. In the godswood, Bran prays for Robb's safety and encounters Osha, who tells him the old gods have no power in the south and urges that Robb should march north, not south, because of rising cold winds, wights, and white walkers. At the feast Bran hosts in Robb's place, the Karstark sons mock him as broken; Maester Luwin suggests he consider becoming a maester, but Bran says he wants to learn magic and that "the crow" promised he would fly. Two days later Robb rides south, leaving Bran as lord of Winterfell.

## Events & Actions
1. **Karstark arrival** — Lord Rickard Karstark arrives at Winterfell with three hundred horsemen and near two thousand foot, the last of Robb's bannermen. This brings the host to approximately twelve thousand men.
2. **Bran watches from the tower** — Bran observes the Karstarks through Maester Luwin's bronze far-eye while perched on Hodor's shoulders atop the outer wall.
3. **Robb forbids Bran from leaving the castle** — Robb cites danger (a knifing at the Smoking Log) and lack of men to guard Bran as reasons.
4. **Rickon's wild episode** — Rickon refused to eat, screamed through a night, punched Old Nan, then hid in the crypts with a rusted sword; Shaggydog attacked Gage and Mikken before Robb and Grey Wind cornered him. Farlen chained Shaggydog in the kennels.
5. **Bannermen test Robb** — Roose Bolton and Robett Glover demand battle command; Maege Mormont offers a granddaughter; Lord Cerwyn brings his daughter; Lord Hornwood brings gifts and asks for concessions.
6. **The Greatjon confrontation** — The Greatjon threatens to leave, draws his sword in the Great Hall; Grey Wind bites off two of his fingers. The Greatjon laughs it off and becomes Robb's staunchest champion.
7. **Robb confesses fear to Bran** — That same night, Robb comes to Bran's chamber, pale and shaken, admitting he was terrified. He worries about the Greatjon, Roose Bolton, and Lord Cerwyn's daughter.
8. **Sansa's letter arrives** — A raven brings a letter in Sansa's hand, sealed with Eddard's seal, reporting Robert's death, Eddard's arrest for treason, and summoning them to swear fealty to Joffrey. No mention of Arya.
9. **Bran prays in the godswood** — Bran prays for Robb's safety, for his family's return, and for Rickon to understand.
10. **Osha's warning** — Osha tells Bran the old gods have no power in the south because the weirwoods were cut down. She warns of white walkers, wights, and cold winds rising beyond the Wall, and insists Robb should march north, not south.
11. **Osha mentions Mance Rayder** — She refers to him as King-beyond-the-Wall, a former black crow from the Shadow Tower, who thinks he can fight the white walkers.
12. **Bran hosts the feast** — In Robb's absence, Bran fills the high seat and formally welcomes the Karstarks. Eddard and Torrhen Karstark mock him as broken.
13. **Bran tells Luwin he wants to learn magic** — Bran says the crow promised he would fly; Luwin says no man can teach magic, and suggests the maester's chain instead. Bran says the children of the forest could teach magic.
14. **Bran relays Osha's warning to Luwin** — Luwin dismisses it as wildling tales and advises Bran not to trouble Robb with it.
15. **Robb's departure** — Two days after the feast, Robb rides south with the army. Hallis Mollen carries the Stark banner; Theon and the Greatjon flank Robb. Bran is named lord of Winterfell in Robb's absence.
16. **Bran left behind** — After the army departs, Winterfell feels deserted. Bran looks at those who remain: women, children, old men, and Hodor.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Miserable, frightened, ashamed, isolated. Bran feels helpless about his inability to protect himself or influence events. Brief moments of comfort in the godswood and with Summer. Bitterness when called "broken."
- **Primary preoccupation:** Fear for Robb and his family's safety; desire not to be helpless and broken; longing to be a knight or learn magic.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to relay Osha's warning to Maester Luwin (though not directly to Robb, as Luwin advises against it). Accepts the role of lord of Winterfell when Robb departs.
- **Self-deception flags:** None detected. Bran is remarkably clear-eyed about his situation and limitations.
