# AGOT — Bran VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 54
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran VI
- **first_available:** AGOT Bran VI
- **location_primary:** Winterfell
- **location_secondary:** Godswood, Great Hall, the yard beneath the gatehouse
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly before Robb marches south with his army; the Karstarks are the last bannermen to arrive, and Robb departs two days after the feast for Lord Karstark
- **time_markers:** "near a fortnight" of comings and goings with portcullises kept up; "Only two days ago" one of Bolton's men knifed one of Cerwyn's; "Only yesterday" Osha tried to speak to Robb; feast takes place the night of the Karstarks' arrival; Robb departs "two days later" after Bran's conversation with Osha; departure occurs at "a red dawn"; Rickon described as "near four" years old; Robb has "not yet seen his sixteenth name day"
- **chapter_summary:** Bran watches the Karstarks arrive at Winterfell, the last of Robb's bannermen, bringing the northern army to roughly twelve thousand men. Confined to the castle by Robb's orders, Bran visits the godswood to pray for his family, where Osha warns him the old gods have no power in the south and that the true threat lies to the north. At the feast that night, Bran hosts the Karstarks in Robb's stead and endures cruel remarks about his condition, then discusses with Maester Luwin his desire to learn magic. Two days later, Robb departs Winterfell for war, leaving Bran as lord in his place.

## Events & Actions
1. **Karstark arrival** — Lord Rickard Karstark arrives at Winterfell with his sons Harrion, Eddard, and Torrhen, bringing three hundred horsemen and near two thousand foot. They are the last bannermen to arrive, bringing the total force to roughly twelve thousand men.
2. **Bran watches from the wall** — Bran observes the arrival from a guard turret atop the outer wall, perched on Hodor's shoulders, using Maester Luwin's bronze far-eye.
3. **Descent from the wall** — Hodor carries Bran down via chisel-cut handholds in the tower's inner wall, with Bran in the wicker basket on his back.
4. **Summer frightens Karstark horses** — Bran whistles for Summer as they pass beneath the gatehouse portcullis. The direwolf's scent sends the Karstark lancers' horses into a frenzy; one stallion rears, screaming.
5. **Bran prays in the godswood** — Bran asks the old gods to keep Robb safe, bring his family home, and help Rickon understand.
6. **Rickon's wildness recalled** — Bran remembers that Rickon has been wild since learning Robb is going to war: refusing to eat, crying and screaming, punching Old Nan. Rickon hid in the crypts with a rusted iron sword from a dead king's hand. Shaggydog attacked Gage and Mikken. It took Robb and Grey Wind to subdue the wolf. Farlen chained Shaggydog in the kennels.
7. **Bannermen test Robb** — Robb faces challenges from his lords: Bolton and Glover both demand battle command; Maege Mormont points out his youth and offers a granddaughter; Lord Cerwyn brings his daughter; Lord Hornwood brings gifts while asking for lands and rights.
8. **The Greatjon confrontation** — The Greatjon threatens to take his forces home over the order of march. Robb tells him he'll be hanged as an oathbreaker. The Greatjon throws ale into the fire, knocks Hallis Mollen down, kicks over a table, and draws his greatsword. Grey Wind bites off two of his fingers. Robb quips about cutting his meat. The Greatjon laughs, and afterwards becomes Robb's staunchest champion.
9. **Robb confides in Bran** — That same night, Robb comes to Bran's bedchamber pale and shaken, confessing he was terrified of the Greatjon and fears Roose Bolton's silent stare. He mentions Cerwyn's daughter and wishes Father were there.
10. **Rumors and the raven's letter** — Various conflicting rumors about events in the south circulate. A raven brings a letter sealed with Father's seal, written in Sansa's hand, saying Father conspired at treason, Robert is dead, and they are summoned to swear fealty to Joffrey. Sansa says nothing of Arya.
11. **Bran reflects on Lady's death** — Bran remembers the return of Lady's bones and the direwolves howling. Lady was buried in the ancient lichyard beneath the First Keep.
12. **Osha appears in the godswood** — Osha encounters Bran while praying to the old gods. She tells him the gods are answering — the rustling leaves are their voice. She says the old gods are sad because they have no power in the south, where the weirwoods were cut down.
13. **Hodor returns naked** — Hodor emerges from the hot pools naked and smiling. Osha comments he has giant's blood in him.
14. **Osha speaks of giants and the threat beyond the Wall** — Osha tells Bran giants exist beyond the Wall, describes their size and habits. She warns of the cold winds rising, wights with blue eyes and cold black hands, and white walkers. She mentions Mance Rayder gathering the Free Folk. She says she tried to warn Robb but was ignored and shoved aside by the Greatjon.
15. **Bran promises to tell Robb** — Osha urges that Robb should march north, not south. Bran promises to relay her warning.
16. **Feast for the Karstarks** — That night Bran hosts in the Great Hall in Robb's place while Robb dines with the lords in the solar. Hallis Mollen announces "Brandon Stark, of Winterfell." The Karstark sons mock Bran in whispers — Eddard says he'd sooner die, Torrhen calls him broken inside and out, too craven to take his own life.
17. **Bran tells Luwin he wants magic** — Bran says he wants to be a knight, then says he wants to learn magic, mentioning the crow promised he would fly. Luwin offers history, healing, herblore, the speech of ravens, building, navigation, and the Citadel — but says no man can teach magic. Bran suggests the children of the forest could.
18. **Bran relays Osha's warning to Luwin** — Luwin dismisses Osha's tales and advises Bran not to trouble Robb with such folly. He says the Lannisters hold Father, not the children of the forest.
19. **Robb departs Winterfell** — Two days later at red dawn, Bran says farewell to Robb in the yard. Robb tells Bran he is lord in Winterfell now and must take his place. He rides out on a grey stallion in grey chainmail, with Grey Wind, Hallis Mollen carrying the Stark banner, Theon Greyjoy and the Greatjon flanking him, and knights in a double column behind.
20. **Rickon refuses to say farewell** — Rickon stays in his chamber, red-eyed and defiant, screaming "NO farewell!" Robb says Mother will be home soon and promises to bring back Father.
21. **Bran left behind** — After Robb vanishes beneath the portcullis and the cheering fades, Winterfell feels deserted and dead. Bran looks at those who remain — women, children, old men, and Hodor — and feels he is Bran the Broken, who cannot even dismount his own horse except to fall.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Miserable, frightened, ashamed, lonely, and increasingly feeling broken and useless. He yearns to be part of the action but is painfully aware of his limitations. He feels cold inside upon learning the contents of Sansa's letter. At the end, he feels a dull ache knowing people will never cheer for him the way they cheer for Robb.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Fear for his family — Father's unknown fate, Robb riding to war, Rickon's wildness, Mother's absence. Secondary preoccupation with his own identity and worth: he does not want to be "Bran the Broken" and desires to learn magic or become a knight.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to visit the godswood to pray rather than seek out Robb; promises Osha he will relay her warning to Robb; accepts the role of lord in Winterfell when Robb departs (though passively — he has no real choice).
- **Self-deception flags:** None clearly evident. Bran is notably honest with himself about his fear, shame, and limitations throughout the chapter.
