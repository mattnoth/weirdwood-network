# AGOT — Jon VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 49
- **pov_character:** Jon Snow
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon VI
- **first_available:** AGOT Jon VI
- **location_primary:** Castle Black
- **location_secondary:** The haunted forest (weirwood grove beyond the Wall)
- **approximate_timeline:** Day of the oath-taking ceremony for new recruits; follows completion of training
- **time_markers:** Morning (breaking fast, morning light through sept window); afternoon (set out late that afternoon); sun setting below the trees when they reach the grove; evenfall (vows taken as sun sets); grey day became black night; dark falling on return
- **chapter_summary:** Jon and his fellow recruits are assigned to the three orders of the Night's Watch. Jon is assigned to the stewards rather than the rangers, which infuriates him until Sam explains that Mormont likely requested Jon as his personal steward to groom him for command. Jon and Sam ride beyond the Wall to a grove of nine weirwoods in the haunted forest to swear their vows before the old gods. After the ceremony, Ghost returns carrying a severed human hand.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon breaks his fast** — Jon is eating applecakes and blood sausage when Sam arrives with exciting news.
2. **Sam announces his assignment** — Sam has been summoned to the sept; he is being passed out of training to assist Maester Aemon with the library and ravens.
3. **Recruits gather in the sept** — Jon, Sam, Pyp, Grenn, Toad, and other recruits assemble. Septon Celladar swings a censer. Pyp is surprised to see Sam.
4. **High officers arrive** — Maester Aemon (leaning on Clydas), Ser Alliser, Lord Commander Mormont, Bowen Marsh, Othell Yarwyck, and Ser Jaremy Rykker enter together.
5. **Mormont delivers ceremony speech** — The Old Bear addresses the recruits about washing away past crimes and loyalties; explains the meaning of the Night's Watch vows; warns that desertion means death.
6. **No recruits choose to leave** — When Mormont offers the chance to depart, no one moves.
7. **Jon declares for the old gods** — Jon stands and announces he keeps the old gods; Mormont notes his uncle did the same and says there is a weirwood grove half a league away.
8. **Sam asks to join Jon** — Sam requests to also take his vows before a heart tree, forsaking the Seven who never answered his prayers.
9. **Order assignments are read** — Mormont reads from a paper: Halder and Albett to builders; Grenn, Pyp, Matthar, and Todder to rangers; Sam, Dareon, and Jon to stewards.
10. **Jon reacts with shock and anger** — Jon is furious at being assigned to the stewards; he sees Ser Alliser smiling and believes this is deliberate humiliation.
11. **Officers and other recruits depart** — Rangers leave with Ser Jaremy, builders with Othell Yarwyck; Jon is left with Sam, Dareon, Maester Aemon, and Bowen Marsh.
12. **Bowen Marsh gives steward assignments** — Sam to the rookery/library (taking Chett's cell); Dareon to Eastwatch to help Cotter Pyke with trade provisions; Jon as Mormont's personal steward in the Lord Commander's tower.
13. **Jon protests his assignment** — Jon angrily asks if they take him for a servant.
14. **Maester Aemon rebukes Jon** — Aemon says they took him for a man of the Night's Watch and perhaps were wrong.
15. **Dareon sneers at Jon** — Outside, Dareon mocks Jon as "Lord Snow" who considers himself above steward work; mentions the girl who pulled him through a window.
16. **Sam explains the true purpose** — Sam argues Mormont wants Jon close to groom him for command, drawing a parallel to how his own father trained Dickon by keeping him at court.
17. **Jon accepts his assignment** — Jon feels ashamed of his behavior and agrees Sam is right; he was "acting the boy."
18. **Party rides through the Wall tunnel** — Late afternoon, the group leads horses through a narrow, twisting tunnel with three sets of iron gates that Bowen Marsh unlocks.
19. **They enter the haunted forest** — On the north side, Jon whistles for Ghost who races ahead; the forest feels ominous and different from the wolfswood.
20. **They reach the weirwood grove** — A clearing with nine weirwoods in a rough circle; bone-pale trunks, bloodred leaves, ruby-hard dried sap in carved eyes; horses left outside.
21. **Jon and Sam swear the Night's Watch vows** — They kneel and recite the full oath together as twilight turns to night.
22. **They are declared men of the Night's Watch** — Bowen Marsh tells them to rise as men.
23. **Dywen senses danger** — The old forester urges they head back, saying something in the night's smell he dislikes.
24. **Ghost returns with a severed hand** — Ghost emerges between two weirwoods carrying something black in his jaws; it is identified as a human hand.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Moves through excitement (for Sam's news) → shock and rage (steward assignment) → shame (after Sam's explanation) → acceptance → awe and solemnity (in the weirwood grove) → disquiet (Ghost's resemblance to weirwoods; the severed hand)
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether his steward assignment is a punishment from Ser Alliser or an opportunity from Mormont; his sense of identity as a fighter versus a servant
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to accept the steward assignment after Sam's counsel; chooses to swear vows before the old gods rather than in the sept
- **Self-deception flags:** Initially frames his anger as righteous ("It's not fair") when it stems from wounded pride and a sense of entitlement; Jon realizes this himself ("I was acting the boy")
