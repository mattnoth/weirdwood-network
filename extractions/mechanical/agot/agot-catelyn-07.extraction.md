## Events & Actions
1. **News from Riverrun arrives** — A raven brings word that Jaime Lannister is massing a host at Casterly Rock. Edmure Tully has taken command of Riverrun's defense and sent riders demanding Tywin's intent. He has commanded Lords Vance and Piper to guard the Golden Tooth pass.
2. **Catelyn resolves to leave the Eyrie** — Catelyn decides to return to Winterfell with Ser Rodrik, planning to request an escort to Gulltown and take ship from there.
3. **Brynden Tully resigns as Knight of the Gate** — After Lysa refuses his request for a thousand men to ride to Riverrun, Brynden resigns his position and declares he will leave for Riverrun by evenfall.
4. **Catelyn recruits Brynden** — She offers Brynden safe passage through Winterfell and promises him a thousand men for Riverrun's defense. He agrees to travel with her.
5. **Catelyn argues with Lysa** — Catelyn tries to stop the trial by combat, arguing the Imp has value alive and warning of consequences. Lysa insists, citing Robert's wishes and Tyrion's own demand for trial by combat.
6. **Maester Colemon's revelation** — Colemon tells Catelyn that Jon Arryn planned to foster Robert at Dragonstone. Catelyn corrects him, saying it was Casterly Rock and arranged after Jon's death. Colemon insists it was Jon's own plan. The conversation is interrupted by the bell.
7. **Trial by combat begins** — Ser Vardis Egen (heavily armored, wielding Jon Arryn's sword, carrying a large oak shield) faces Bronn (lightly armored in ringmail and leather, no shield). The septon performs the ceremony with a crystal sphere.
8. **Bronn's strategy of attrition** — Bronn refuses to stand and fight, instead circling and retreating, forcing the heavily armored Ser Vardis to chase him. He targets the knight's unshielded right side.
9. **Catelyn's memory of the Brandon-Petyr duel** — The combat triggers Catelyn's recollection of Brandon Stark fighting Petyr Baelish at Riverrun. Brandon drove Petyr across the bailey and down the water stair. Petyr refused to yield and was gravely wounded. He murmured "Cat" as he fell.
10. **Bronn wounds Ser Vardis** — Bronn damages the falcon wing on Ser Vardis's helm, then cuts into his elbow joint, causing the knight to bleed heavily and weaken.
11. **Ser Vardis's final charge** — On Lysa's command to "finish him," Ser Vardis charges desperately, smashing his shield into Bronn's face (breaking a tooth). His overhead blow misses as Bronn dodges, and the silver sword strikes the marble statue and snaps.
12. **Bronn kills Ser Vardis** — Bronn pushes the Alyssa statue onto Ser Vardis, pinning him, then drives his sword through the gap between arm and breastplate, through the ribs. Ser Vardis dies.
13. **Tyrion claims his freedom** — Tyrion invokes House Arryn's words ("As High as Honor") to demand release. Lysa is forced to comply.
14. **Lysa sends Tyrion via the high road** — Lysa orders Tyrion and Bronn escorted to the Bloody Gate with horses and supplies to reach the Trident, but via the dangerous high road — effectively another death sentence.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Worried about her father's health and Riverrun's defense; frustrated and weary of arguing with Lysa; grimly observant during the trial; haunted by the memory of the Brandon-Petyr duel; resigned to the outcome
- **Primary preoccupation:** Returning to Winterfell and her sons; protecting her prisoner Tyrion's strategic value; the military threat gathering against Riverrun
- **Key decisions made:** Resolves to leave the Eyrie for Winterfell; recruits Brynden Tully to travel with her and promises him a thousand men for Riverrun; attempts (and fails) to stop the trial by combat
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn almost wishes she had burned Lysa's letter before reading it — suggesting regret about the chain of events she set in motion. She accepts the poison theory uncritically despite acknowledging she cannot identify which Lannister is responsible.
