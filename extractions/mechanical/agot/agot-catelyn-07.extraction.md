- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn learns from Ser Rodrik that the Kingslayer is massing forces at Casterly Rock and that Edmure has taken charge of Riverrun's defense, suggesting Lord Hoster is too ill to command. She resolves to leave the Eyrie and return to Winterfell. Her uncle Brynden Tully announces he has resigned as Knight of the Gate after Lysa refused to send men to Riverrun, and agrees to travel with Catelyn. Catelyn fails to dissuade Lysa from the trial by combat. Bronn defeats and kills Ser Vardis Egen in the duel, winning Tyrion Lannister's freedom. Lysa orders Tyrion and Bronn escorted to the Bloody Gate and released onto the high road, which Catelyn recognizes as another form of death sentence.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn watches dawn from her balcony** — She reflects on the waterfall Alyssa's Tears and the legend of Alyssa Arryn, and wonders how large a waterfall her own tears would make when she dies.
2. **Ser Rodrik reports news from Riverrun** — A raven brought word that the Kingslayer is massing forces at Casterly Rock; Edmure has commanded Lords Vance and Piper to guard the Golden Tooth; Edmure vows to yield no foot of Tully land without watering it with Lannister blood; no mention of Lord Hoster.
3. **Catelyn infers her father is gravely ill** — He would not have given defense of Riverrun to Edmure unless very sick. She says she should have been woken when the bird arrived; Lysa had told Maester Colemon to let her sleep.
4. **Catelyn resolves to leave the Eyrie** — She tells Ser Rodrik they must depart; she plans to ask Lysa for an escort to Gulltown and take ship to Winterfell. Ser Rodrik looks green at the mention of another ship.
5. **Catelyn and Ser Rodrik encounter Brynden Tully outside Lysa's apartments** — Brynden reveals he asked Lysa for a thousand men to ride to Riverrun; she refused and told him his place is as Knight of the Gate. He has resigned the position and will leave for Riverrun by evenfall. He declares "Black fish or no, I am still a Tully."
6. **Brynden agrees to travel with Catelyn** — Catelyn offers him a thousand men from Winterfell and invites him to travel the long way via the North. He accepts: "It's the long way home, but I'm more like to get there."
7. **Catelyn enters Lysa's apartments and the garden terrace** — Lysa is holding court among her suitors and retainers, freshly scrubbed in cream velvet with sapphires and moonstones; Robert Arryn watches a puppet show on an elevated platform; food and wine are served.
8. **Catelyn observes Lysa's suitors** — Lord Hunter and Ser Lyn Corbray stand highest in Lysa's current favor. Catelyn assesses both as unsuitable: Hunter is old, gouty, with three quarrelsome sons; Corbray is vain, reckless, hot-tempered, and rumored to be uninterested in women.
9. **Catelyn confronts Lysa about the trial** — She argues that Tyrion alive has value, dead he is only food for crows, and Jaime won't care about a trial. Lysa's suitors dismiss her. Lysa insists Robert wants to see Tyrion fly and that Tyrion demanded the trial himself.
10. **Lysa accuses Tyrion of murdering Jon Arryn** — She says he poisoned the Hand of the King and left her sweet baby fatherless. She stalks away across the terrace.
11. **Catelyn and Ser Rodrik discuss who killed Jon Arryn** — Catelyn believes the Lannisters did it but cannot say which one. Ser Rodrik notes poison could be the dwarf's or Cersei's work, but says the Kingslayer is not the sort — he's too fond of the sight of blood. Catelyn notes Lysa named Cersei in her letter but now blames Tyrion, perhaps because Tyrion is present.
12. **Catelyn almost wishes she had burned Lysa's letter** — She recognizes how Lysa's letter set events in motion she cannot control.
13. **Catelyn comments on Robert Arryn** — She says the boy is utterly without discipline and will never be strong enough to rule unless taken from his mother.
14. **Maester Colemon reveals Jon Arryn planned to foster Robert at Dragonstone** — Catelyn corrects him, saying it was Casterly Rock, arranged after Jon's death without Lysa's consent. Colemon insists it was Lord Jon who planned it, and Dragonstone, not Casterly Rock. The conversation is cut short by the bell.
15. **Tyrion Lannister is brought out for the trial** — Led by two guardsmen in sky-blue cloaks; the Eyrie's plump septon escorts him to the statue of Alyssa at the garden's center.
16. **The champions appear** — Ser Vardis enters in full plate with Jon Arryn's engraved sword and a heavy shield; Bronn enters in light armor with his ugly grey sword. Bronn spits and waves away the offered shield. Lysa proudly describes Jon Arryn's sword.
17. **The septon performs the trial ceremony** — He lifts a faceted crystal sphere; rainbows dance across Tyrion's face; the septon asks the gods to bear witness and departs hastily.
18. **Tyrion whispers to Bronn** — Before being led away, Tyrion leans over and whispers something in Bronn's ear; Bronn rises laughing and brushes grass from his knee.
19. **The duel begins** — Robert commands the fight. Bronn uses mobility and evasion, circling right then left to Ser Vardis's unshielded side. Ser Vardis advances carefully behind his heavy shield. The lords call Bronn a coward.
20. **Catelyn recalls the duel between Brandon Stark and Petyr Baelish** — At Riverrun's lower bailey. Petyr wore only helm, breastplate, and mail; Brandon removed most of his armor to match. Catelyn gave Brandon her favor (a pale blue handscarf with leaping trout). She pleaded with him to spare Petyr, calling Petyr "a foolish boy" she had loved "like a brother." Brandon promised with "the cool grey eyes of a Stark." Brandon drove Petyr across the bailey and down the water stair, raining steel on him. Petyr refused to yield despite bleeding from a dozen wounds. Brandon ended it with a brutal backhand cut below the ribs, through rings and leather. Petyr murmured "Cat" as he fell. Catelyn thought the wound mortal. A fortnight passed before Petyr could leave; Lysa nursed him with the maester. Edmure called on Petyr but was sent away — Edmure had been Brandon's squire. Lord Hoster sent Petyr away in a closed litter to the Fingers.
21. **Bronn gains the advantage** — He hacks notches in Ser Vardis's armor. He hides behind the statue; Ser Vardis strikes the marble, making a spark. Bronn comes out fast and lands blows — a wing on the helm collapses with a crunch, gashes appear across the knight's plate. He wounds Ser Vardis in the sword arm at the elbow; a bright finger of blood runs along the forearm. Every parry grows slower and lower.
22. **Lysa orders Ser Vardis to finish it** — "Enough, Ser Vardis! Finish him now, my baby is growing tired."
23. **Ser Vardis makes his final charge** — He charges in a bull rush, crashes into Bronn, slams the shield lip into the sellsword's face, smashing his lip. Bronn nearly loses his feet. Ser Vardis throws aside his shield and raises his sword two-handed for a killing blow.
24. **Jon Arryn's sword breaks** — Bronn jerks back; the blade strikes the marble elbow of the Alyssa statue and snaps clean a third of the way up.
25. **Bronn topples the statue onto Ser Vardis** — He puts his shoulder into the statue's back; the weathered likeness of Alyssa Arryn topples with a great crash, pinning Ser Vardis beneath it.
26. **Bronn kills Ser Vardis** — He kicks aside the shattered rondel, drives his sword under the arm and through the ribs with all his weight. Ser Vardis shudders and lies still.
27. **Silence falls** — Bronn yanks off his halfhelm, spits out a broken tooth. His lip is smashed and bloody.
28. **Tyrion claims his freedom** — He refuses to be made to fly and invokes House Arryn's words: "As High as Honor."
29. **Robert shakes and screams** — "You promised I could make him fly." He begins to shake.
30. **Lysa orders Tyrion released** — She commands guards to escort Tyrion and Bronn to the Bloody Gate with horses, supplies sufficient to reach the Trident, and all goods and weapons returned — but sends them onto the high road. Catelyn recognizes this as another form of death sentence.
31. **Tyrion accepts** — He favors Lysa with a mocking bow and says "I believe we know the way."
