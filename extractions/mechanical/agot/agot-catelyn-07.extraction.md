- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn learns from Ser Rodrik that Jaime Lannister is massing forces at Casterly Rock and Edmure Tully has taken command of Riverrun's defense, implying Lord Hoster is gravely ill. Catelyn confronts Lysa about the trial by combat but fails to dissuade her. Brynden Tully resigns as Knight of the Gate after Lysa refuses to send men to Riverrun, and agrees to travel with Catelyn to Winterfell. The trial by combat takes place between Ser Vardis Egen (champion for Lysa) and Bronn (champion for Tyrion); Bronn kills Ser Vardis through superior speed and endurance. Tyrion and Bronn are released per the gods' judgment but sent via the dangerous high road.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn watches dawn from her balcony** — She reflects on Alyssa's Tears and wonders how large a waterfall her own tears would make.
2. **Ser Rodrik delivers news from Edmure's letter** — The Kingslayer masses forces at Casterly Rock; Edmure commands Lord Vance and Lord Piper to guard the Golden Tooth pass; Lord Hoster is not mentioned, implying illness.
3. **Catelyn decides to leave the Eyrie** — She tells Ser Rodrik they must depart; plans to travel to Gulltown and take ship to Winterfell.
4. **Brynden Tully resigns as Knight of the Gate** — After Lysa refuses to send men to Riverrun, Brynden declares he will leave by evenfall.
5. **Catelyn offers Brynden alliance** — She invites him to travel north with her and promises a thousand men for Riverrun; he agrees to take the long way via Winterfell.
6. **Catelyn confronts Lysa about the trial** — She argues that Tyrion is more valuable alive and warns the combat may go badly; Lysa and her suitors dismiss her concerns.
7. **Lysa accuses Tyrion of poisoning Jon Arryn** — She insists Tyrion murdered her husband and left her son fatherless.
8. **Catelyn and Ser Rodrik discuss who really killed Jon Arryn** — Catelyn believes the Lannisters are responsible but cannot say which one; Ser Rodrik muses on poison as a weapon.
9. **Maester Colemon reveals Jon Arryn's fostering plans** — He says Jon Arryn planned to send Robert to Dragonstone, contradicting Catelyn's understanding that the plan was Casterly Rock and arranged after Jon's death.
10. **A bell tolls and the trial by combat begins** — Tyrion is led out by guardsmen; the septon performs the ritual with a crystal sphere; Tyrion whispers to Bronn.
11. **The champions take the field** — Ser Vardis in full plate with Jon Arryn's sword and a heavy shield; Bronn in light armor, refusing a shield.
12. **Catelyn flashes back to the Brandon-Petyr duel** — She remembers watching at Riverrun; Brandon drove Petyr across the bailey and down the water stair; Petyr nearly died but survived; Lysa nursed him; he was sent away to the Fingers.
13. **Bronn employs a strategy of movement and attrition** — He circles left, avoids Ser Vardis's shield, forces the knight to chase him in heavy armor.
14. **Bronn damages Ser Vardis progressively** — He hacks notches in armor, wounds the sword-arm elbow, leaves deep gashes across plate.
15. **Ser Vardis makes a final desperate charge** — He smashes his shield into Bronn's face (splitting his lip, breaking a tooth), then throws aside his shield for a two-handed blow.
16. **Jon Arryn's sword breaks** — Ser Vardis's swing misses Bronn and strikes the marble statue of Alyssa; the silver blade snaps a third of the way up.
17. **Bronn topples the statue onto Ser Vardis** — He shoulders the weathered statue; it falls and pins the knight.
18. **Bronn kills Ser Vardis** — He drives his blade under the arm and through the ribs of the pinned knight; Ser Vardis shudders and dies.
19. **Tyrion demands release** — He invokes House Arryn's words ("As High as Honor") and insists on leaving via the turnip hoist, not the Moon Door.
20. **Lysa releases Tyrion and Bronn** — She orders them escorted to the Bloody Gate with horses and supplies, but sends them via the high road — effectively another death sentence.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Worried about her father's health and the Lannister threat; frustrated and weary with Lysa's recklessness and inconstancy; grim foreboding about the trial's outcome and its consequences; melancholy (reflecting on Alyssa's Tears, wondering about her own tears); nostalgic pain during the duel flashback
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting out of the Eyrie and returning to Winterfell; preventing the waste of Tyrion as a political asset; the growing military crisis with Lannister forces massing
- **Key decisions made:** Resolved to leave the Eyrie and travel to Winterfell; invited Brynden to join them and promised a thousand men for Riverrun; decided to travel via Gulltown and take ship
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn "almost wished she had burned her sister's letter before reading it" — suggesting dawning awareness that Lysa's accusations may be unreliable, but she has not fully confronted what this means for the chain of events she set in motion by seizing Tyrion
