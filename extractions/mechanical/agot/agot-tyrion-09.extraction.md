- **chapter_summary:** A bloodied Crakehall messenger reports to Tywin Lannister and his captains that Robb Stark's forces have broken the siege of Riverrun, capturing Jaime Lannister and destroying the Lannister besieging army. Tywin's bannermen debate how to respond, with some advocating for peace and others for battle. After dismissing his lords, Tywin privately reveals to Tyrion and Kevan that Renly Baratheon has married Margaery Tyrell and claimed the crown, and that Stannis is also making preparations on Dragonstone. Tywin announces plans to march to Harrenhal and unleash raiding parties across the riverlands. He then orders Tyrion to go to King's Landing to serve as Hand of the King (in practice, if not in name) and rein in Joffrey's misrule.

## Events & Actions
1. **Messenger arrives with news** — A bloodied Crakehall courier reports that Robb Stark's forces have broken the siege of Riverrun and captured Jaime Lannister.
2. **Messenger details the battle** — The Blackfish led the van against the north camp; Lord Brax drowned trying to cross the river on a raft in full armor; Greatjon Umber fired the siege towers; Robb Stark led two columns of armored horse with a monstrous wolf at his side; Tytos Blackwood led a sortie from Riverrun; Edmure Tully was freed from chains.
3. **South camp retreat reported** — Ser Forley Prester retreated in good order with ~4,000 men, but a Tyroshi sellsword defected to the enemy with his freeriders.
4. **Council debate** — Ser Harys Swyft advocates suing for peace; Ser Addam Marbrand argues for aggressive response; Lord Lefford suggests prisoner exchanges and ransom; Tyrion dismisses all proposals as unrealistic given Eddard Stark's execution.
5. **Tyrion shatters his wine cup** — Hurls his cup to the floor to demonstrate that peace is impossible after Joffrey had Eddard Stark beheaded.
6. **Tywin dismisses the council** — Orders everyone out except Tyrion and Kevan; gives Tyrion his untouched wine.
7. **Tywin reveals Renly's claim** — Renly Baratheon has married Margaery Tyrell and claimed the crown; Mace Tyrell and his sons have sworn their swords to him.
8. **Strategic discussion** — Tywin, Kevan, and Tyrion discuss their dire position: enemies on all sides, Stannis building strength on Dragonstone, Cersei demanding they march to King's Landing.
9. **Tywin criticizes Joffrey's court** — Condemns the council's advice, the elevation of Janos Slynt to lord of Harrenhal, and the dismissal of Barristan Selmy.
10. **Tywin announces march to Harrenhal** — Plans to move to Harrenhal the next day; orders Ser Addam's outriders to screen movements; orders Ser Gregor, Vargo Hoat, and Ser Amory Lorch to raid the riverlands with 300 horse each.
11. **Tywin assigns Tyrion to King's Landing** — Orders Tyrion to go to court and rule in Tywin's name; to control Joffrey and deal with treacherous councillors. Offers Tyrion's clansmen for the raiding parties, but Tyrion refuses.
12. **Tywin forbids Shae at court** — Tells Tyrion "You will not take the whore to court" before departing.
13. **Tyrion contemplates his assignment** — Sits alone in the common room, then returns to his garret; sees the innkeep's body on the gibbet; tells Shae he intends to take her to King's Landing.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Complex mixture of anger, dark humor, grudging pride, resentment, and defiance. Tyrion is angered by Swyft slandering Jaime, sardonic about the military situation, startled and secretly pleased to be trusted by his father, deeply wounded by Tywin's cruel reference to his past marriage, and ultimately defiant about Shae.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The catastrophic military situation and its implications; Jaime's capture; his father's unexpected trust and its poisoned root (Tywin has given up Jaime for lost).
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to hand his clansmen over to the raiding parties (keeps Shagga and Timett with him); decides to defy Tywin's order and take Shae to King's Landing.
- **Self-deception flags:** None overt — Tyrion is remarkably clear-eyed in his assessments throughout this chapter. His decision to take Shae to court despite his father's explicit prohibition may reflect willful blindness to the risks.
