# AGOT — Tyrion II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 14
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion II
- **first_available:** AGOT Tyrion II
- **location_primary:** The kingsroad, between Winterfell and the Wall
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell (recalled), King's Landing (recalled), Casterly Rock (recalled), the wolfswood, a long-abandoned holdfast
- **approximate_timeline:** Roughly eighteen days after departing Winterfell; the party left on the same day as the king's departure south
- **time_markers:** "a fortnight on the wild track"; left Winterfell on the same day as the king; "by the end of the first week"; "three days ride from Winterfell"; "by the fifth day"; "on the eighteenth night of their journey"; nights "well below freezing now"; night's first watch drawn by Jon Snow
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion Lannister travels north along the kingsroad with Benjen Stark, Jon Snow, Ghost, his own men, and Yoren with two recruits, bound for the Wall. On the eighteenth night, Tyrion reads by a stream about dragon history and reflects on having visited the dragon skulls in King's Landing. Jon Snow approaches and asks why Tyrion reads so much; Tyrion delivers a speech about his mind being his weapon. Their conversation turns harsh as Tyrion confronts Jon with the reality of the Night's Watch, causing Jon distress. Ghost knocks Tyrion down in defense of Jon, but the two reconcile with humor and share wine. The party eats stew around the fire and retires for the night, with Jon taking the first watch.

## Events & Actions
1. **Departure from Winterfell** — Tyrion, Benjen Stark, and Jon Snow ride north from Winterfell on the same day as the king's southern departure, amidst commotion of the royal retinue.
2. **Journey through settled lands** — For the first three days, the road passes through farmland with holdfasts, inns, and stone bridges over rivers; terrain is flint hills to the west and rolling plains to the east.
3. **Entry into the wolfswood** — After three days, the farmland gives way to dense ancient forest; the flint hills rise into mountains; wolf howls fill the nights.
4. **Yoren joins the party** — At the edge of the wolfswood, they stay at a forest holdfast where they are joined by Yoren, a Night's Watch brother, with two young recruits (rapers from the Fingers).
5. **Jon observes his future brothers** — Jon watches Yoren and his recruits with an expression like dismay; Tyrion notices and feels sorry for the boy.
6. **Benjen's coldness toward Tyrion** — Benjen shares his brother's distaste for Lannisters and warns Tyrion he won't enjoy the ride; Tyrion refuses to complain despite physical suffering.
7. **Tyrion takes the bearskin** — Benjen offers his riding fur expecting refusal; Tyrion accepts, reflecting "The Lannisters took what was offered."
8. **Tyrion reads by a stream (night 18)** — Tyrion settles beside a swift stream near an ancient oak, drinks Summer Isles wine, and reads about dragonbone properties.
9. **Tyrion recalls the dragon skulls** — Extended memory of visiting the nineteen dragon skulls stored in the cellar beneath King's Landing's throne room; he was awed by their beauty.
10. **Tyrion recalls the Field of Fire** — He reflects on his ancestor King Loren's defeat by the three Targaryen dragons and Loren's subsequent surrender.
11. **Jon approaches and asks why Tyrion reads** — Tyrion delivers a monologue about being a dwarf and using his mind as his weapon: "My mind is my weapon... a mind needs books as a sword needs a whetstone."
12. **Jon reveals his age** — Jon says he is fourteen when Tyrion guesses twelve.
13. **Tyrion shares his dragon dreams** — Tyrion tells Jon he used to dream of having a dragon and used to start fires at Casterly Rock imagining his father and sister burning.
14. **Tyrion confronts Jon about the Night's Watch** — Tyrion challenges Jon's idealized view, calling the Watch "a midden heap for all the misfits of the realm" and mocking its dangers as "watching for grumkins and snarks."
15. **Jon becomes angry and distressed** — Jon screams "Stop it!" and nearly comes to tears, coiling his fists.
16. **Ghost attacks Tyrion** — The direwolf knocks Tyrion flat on his back without a sound; Tyrion's mouth fills with dirt and blood, his back spasms.
17. **Ghost stands between them** — Ghost shows his teeth silently, preventing Tyrion from getting up; Jon smiles and tells Tyrion to ask nicely.
18. **Tyrion swallows his pride** — Tyrion crushes his anger and asks Jon politely for help; Jon lifts him to his feet and returns his book.
19. **Reconciliation with humor** — Jon jokes Ghost thought Tyrion was a grumkin; Tyrion laughs and asks what Ghost does to snarks.
20. **They share wine** — Tyrion offers the wineskin; Jon drinks and asks if what Tyrion said about the Night's Watch is true; Tyrion nods.
21. **Jon accepts the truth** — Jon says "If that's what it is, that's what it is"; Tyrion praises him: "Most men would rather deny a hard truth than face it."
22. **Return to camp** — They walk back; Tyrion refuses Jon's help over roots; the camp is set against the wall of an abandoned holdfast.
23. **Benjen scolds Jon** — Benjen emerges and chastises Jon for wandering off alone, saying "I thought the Others had gotten you."
24. **Supper** — Yoren skins a squirrel for the stew; Morrec tends the pot; Tyrion tastes and requests more pepper; they eat stew, black bread, and hard cheese; Tyrion shares wine.
25. **The party retires** — Company drifts off to sleep; Jon draws the first watch; Tyrion, last to retire, pauses to look back at Jon standing near the fire, staring into the flames.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Physically suffering (raw thighs, cramping legs, cold) but stubbornly refusing to show weakness; intellectually engaged when reading; guilty after confronting Jon too harshly; humiliated when Ghost knocks him down; ultimately wistful and melancholic
- **Primary preoccupation:** Dragons — their history, their bones, his childhood fascination with them; also Jon Snow's naïveté about the Night's Watch
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to confront Jon with the truth about the Night's Watch rather than let him maintain illusions; swallows his pride when Ghost stands over him; refuses Jon's help walking over roots — "He would make his own way, as he had all his life"
- **Self-deception flags:** None clearly evident — Tyrion is notably self-aware in this chapter, stating "I have a realistic grasp of my own strengths and weaknesses"
