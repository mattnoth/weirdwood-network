- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn returns to Riverrun by boat with Robb, Grey Wind, and Theon Greyjoy after the lifting of the siege. She is greeted by her brother Edmure and Lord Tytos Blackwood. Catelyn visits her dying father Lord Hoster Tully, who is bedridden with a painful illness. Hoster asks after Lysa and expresses continued displeasure about Brynden's refusal to marry. Robb convenes a war council in the Great Hall to debate whether to support Renly Baratheon, Stannis Baratheon, or seek peace. Catelyn argues for peace and a prisoner exchange for her daughters, but the lords reject this. The Greatjon declares Robb "the King in the North," and the northern and river lords kneel and acclaim him.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn crosses the Tumblestone** — Catelyn, Robb, Grey Wind, and Theon arrive at Riverrun by boat, with Brynden, the Greatjon, and Lord Karstark in a second boat behind them.
2. **Passage through the Water Gate** — The iron portcullis is raised; Catelyn notes the rust and wonders about its defensive integrity.
3. **Arrival at the water stair** — Edmure Tully and Tytos Blackwood greet them. Grey Wind startles a guard. Theon lifts Catelyn onto a dry step.
4. **Edmure embraces Catelyn** — He offers condolences for Ned's death and promises vengeance. Catelyn rebuffs the promise sharply.
5. **Catelyn visits Lord Hoster** — Edmure escorts her to Hoster's solar. She learns he is dying. His bed has been moved to the balcony.
6. **Conversation with Hoster** — He describes his painful illness ("crabs in my belly"). He recounts watching the battle from the battlements. He asks after Lysa, who has not come. He asks whether Brynden has married. He falls asleep.
7. **Catelyn speaks with Brynden** — She confirms Hoster is dying. Brynden jokes that Hoster will chide him about the Redwyne girl even on his funeral pyre.
8. **Catelyn finds Theon boasting** — Theon regales the garrison with tales of the Whispering Wood in the Great Hall.
9. **Catelyn finds Robb in the godswood** — Robb kneels before the heart tree with northern lords and Tytos Blackwood. Catelyn waits and recalls childhood memories of the godswood, including kissing Petyr Baelish.
10. **Robb announces the need for council** — He tells Catelyn that Renly Baratheon has claimed the crown. Catelyn is shocked, having expected Stannis.
11. **War council convenes in the Great Hall** — Lords of the north and the Trident debate strategy. Proposals include: marching on Harrenhal, striking Casterly Rock, waiting and denying Tywin provisions, pledging fealty to Renly, and suing for peace/truce.
12. **Robb rejects Renly's claim** — He argues Stannis has the better legal claim as Robert's elder brother, but is troubled by the implications of opposing Joffrey.
13. **Stevron Frey counsels waiting and negotiation** — He offers to go to Harrenhal to arrange terms and ransoms. The lords shout him down.
14. **Catelyn argues for peace** — She urges prisoner exchange (four Lannisters for Sansa and Arya) and an end to war. The lords reject her counsel.
15. **The Greatjon declares Robb King in the North** — He rejects both Renly and Stannis, calls for northern independence, and kneels before Robb with his greatsword.
16. **The lords acclaim Robb as king** — Karstark, Mormont, Blackwood, Bracken, Mallister, and others kneel and shout "The King in the North!" — words not heard in the realm for more than three hundred years.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deep grief over Ned's death (wound "still too fresh for softer words"); rage at the world — at Edmure, Lysa, the Lannisters, the maesters, Ned, her father, and "the monstrous gods"; nostalgic and melancholy when recalling her childhood at Riverrun; despairing when the lords reject peace; fierce maternal protectiveness for Robb and her daughters; pride in Robb ("fiercely proud"); tears she wipes away angrily
- **Primary preoccupation:** The impossibility of bringing Ned back; wanting her daughters returned; fear of losing her father too; worry about Robb being consumed by war
- **Key decisions made:** Argues publicly for peace and prisoner exchange at the war council, breaking with the prevailing martial sentiment
- **Self-deception flags:** A voice whispers that she herself caused the war by seizing Tyrion ("It was your doing, yours"); she pushes away thoughts of Ned ("She could not think about Ned now. She would not. It would not do."); she sees Robb's "true bride" as his sword rather than the Frey girl he is pledged to marry
