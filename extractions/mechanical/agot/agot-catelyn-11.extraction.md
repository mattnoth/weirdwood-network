Catelyn reunites with her brother Edmure and visits her dying father Lord Hoster, who is bedridden with a wasting illness. Hoster asks about Lysa and revives his old quarrel with Brynden over marriage. A war council convenes in the Great Hall to debate their next move — whether to support Renly, Stannis, sue for peace, or march on Harrenhal. Catelyn argues for peace and the return of her daughters, but the lords reject it. The Greatjon declares Robb the King in the North, and the assembled lords — both northern and riverlands — kneel and acclaim him.

## Events & Actions
1. **Crossing the Tumblestone** — Catelyn, Robb (with Grey Wind), and Theon cross the river by boat to enter Riverrun. Brynden, the Greatjon, and Lord Karstark follow in a second boat.
2. **Entry through the Water Gate** — The iron portcullis is raised; Catelyn notes the rust and wonders about its defensibility. They pass under the arch into the castle.
3. **Reunion with Edmure** — Edmure Tully and Tytos Blackwood await at the water stair. Edmure embraces Catelyn, swears vengeance for Ned's death. Catelyn cuts him short.
4. **Grey Wind startles a guard** — Grey Wind bounds out of the boat and one guard drops his pole, stumbles, and sits in the river.
5. **Visit to Lord Hoster** — Edmure escorts Catelyn through the lower bailey and up the spiral stair to Hoster's solar. Hoster is dying — shrunken, white-haired, in constant pain ("crabs in my belly"). He and Catelyn share an emotional reunion.
6. **Hoster asks about Lysa** — He hopes Lysa came; Catelyn tells him she stayed in the Eyrie with Robert Arryn. Hoster is visibly disappointed.
7. **Hoster's quarrel about Brynden's marriage** — Even dying, Hoster raises Brynden's refusal to marry Bethany Redwyne and his rejection of other matches (Bracken's girl, Frey girls). Catelyn reminds him Bethany married Lord Rowan years ago.
8. **Hoster describes watching the battle** — He was carried to the gatehouse battlements to watch the siege tower burn; calls it beautiful.
9. **Catelyn speaks with Brynden** — She confirms Hoster is dying. Brynden chuckles that Hoster will chide him about the Redwyne girl even on his funeral pyre.
10. **Catelyn finds Theon in the Great Hall** — Theon is telling the garrison about the Whispering Wood battle; describes Grey Wind tearing a man's arm off and horses going mad.
11. **Catelyn finds Robb in the godswood** — Robb kneels before the weirwood heart tree with his sword thrust in the earth, surrounded by praying lords (Greatjon, Karstark, Maege Mormont, Galbart Glover, Tytos Blackwood). These are the ones who keep the old gods.
12. **Catelyn's godswood memories** — She remembers her father teaching her to ride, Edmure's broken arm, and she and Lysa playing at kissing with Petyr Baelish. Petyr chewed mint and tried to put his tongue in her mouth.
13. **Robb calls for a war council** — Renly Baratheon has claimed Robert's crown. Catelyn is shocked, expecting Stannis.
14. **War council convenes** — Lords from both north and riverlands attend. Fugitive Trident lords have returned: Karyl Vance (now lord), Marq Piper, a young Darry, Jonos Bracken. Northern lords sit opposite the river lords.
15. **Debate on strategy** — Some want to march on Harrenhal; Piper urges striking Casterly Rock; Mallister counsels patience; Blackwood wants to finish the Lannisters; Bracken urges pledging to Renly.
16. **Robb rejects Renly's claim** — Robb argues Renly is not the rightful king; by law the throne goes Joffrey → Tommen → Stannis. Lady Mormont agrees Stannis has the better claim.
17. **Marq Piper argues for Renly** — Five or six great houses would back him; they'd have all the Lannister heads on pikes within a year.
18. **Stevron Frey urges caution** — Wait, then bend the knee to the victor; offer truce and ransoms. He is shouted down by the other lords.
19. **Catelyn argues for peace** — She wants her daughters back; would trade four Lannister captives for Sansa and Arya; wants Robb safe at Winterfell. The hall goes quiet.
20. **Lords reject peace** — Brynden questions what terms; Karstark asks what his sons died for; Bracken won't kneel to those who sent Gregor Clegane; Blackwood notes they'd be traitors to Renly if they made peace with Joffrey.
21. **The Greatjon declares Robb King in the North** — He rejects both Renly and Stannis, asks why southron kings should rule the north, and declares "There sits the only king I mean to bow my knee to." He kneels and lays his greatsword at Robb's feet.
22. **Lords acclaim Robb as King in the North** — Karstark kneels with his sword. Maege Mormont declares "The King of Winter!" and lays her mace. River lords rise too — Blackwood, Bracken, Mallister — kneeling and shouting words not heard for three hundred years.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief-stricken over Ned's death (wound "too fresh for softer words"); anger at the world — at Edmure, Lysa, Lannisters, maesters, Ned, her father, the gods; fierce pride in Robb; nostalgia tinged with sadness at Riverrun memories; despair during the council when peace is rejected; tears she wipes away angrily
- **Primary preoccupation:** The loss of Ned and the desire to protect what remains of her family — she wants her daughters back, wants Robb safe, wants to go home. She is also absorbed by practical assessments (the rusted portcullis, defensibility) — "thoughts like that were seldom far from her mind these days."
- **Key decisions made:** Argues for peace and prisoner exchange at the war council, but is overruled. Does not resist the outcome.
- **Self-deception flags:** Internal voice whispers that the Lannister attack was her own doing ("If you had not taken it upon yourself to seize the dwarf …") — she acknowledges guilt but pushes it aside. She tells herself she "must be strong" and "could not think about Ned now."
