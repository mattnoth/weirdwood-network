- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn arrives at Riverrun by boat with Robb, finding the siege lifted and her brother Edmure freed. She visits her dying father Lord Hoster Tully, who is bedridden with a wasting illness. A war council convenes in the Great Hall to debate their next move, with lords arguing over whether to march on Harrenhal, strike Casterly Rock, support Renly or Stannis, or seek peace. Catelyn pleads for peace and a prisoner exchange to recover Sansa and Arya, but the lords reject it. The Greatjon declares Robb "The King in the North," and the northern and river lords acclaim him, kneeling and laying their swords at his feet.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at Riverrun** — Catelyn, Robb, Grey Wind, and Theon cross the Tumblestone by boat and enter Riverrun through the Water Gate. Ser Brynden, the Greatjon, and Lord Karstark follow in a second boat.
2. **Reunion with Edmure** — Edmure embraces Catelyn on the water stairs; he is wounded and battle-worn. Lord Tytos Blackwood stands with him, having led the sortie that freed Edmure from the Lannister camp.
3. **Visit to dying Lord Hoster** — Catelyn visits her father in his solar. He is bedridden, wasted, in constant pain from a stomach ailment. He watched the battle from the gatehouse battlements. He asks about Lysa, who has not come. He still resents Brynden for refusing to marry Bethany Redwyne.
4. **Catelyn speaks with Brynden** — She confirms Hoster is dying. Brynden notes Hoster will chide him about the Redwyne girl until the end.
5. **Theon boasts in the Great Hall** — Theon regales the garrison with tales of the Whispering Wood battle, describing Grey Wind's ferocity and the Lannister rout.
6. **Robb prays in the godswood** — Robb kneels before the heart tree with northern and some river lords. Catelyn recalls childhood memories: her father teaching her to ride, Edmure breaking his arm, kissing games with Petyr Baelish.
7. **War council convenes** — Lords debate strategy. Factions advocate: marching on Harrenhal, striking Casterly Rock, waiting and denying Tywin supply lines, pledging to Renly, or seeking truce. News: Renly has claimed the crown; Roose Bolton holds the causeway; Tywin marches to Harrenhal.
8. **Robb argues succession law** — Robb asserts Renly's claim is illegitimate because Stannis is the elder brother, and even Joffrey/Tommen have stronger legal claims as Robert's trueborn sons.
9. **Catelyn pleads for peace** — She argues for a prisoner exchange (four Lannisters for Sansa and Arya) and an end to fighting, saying vengeance cannot bring back the dead. The lords reject her plea.
10. **Declaration of the King in the North** — The Greatjon rejects both Renly and Stannis, draws his greatsword, points it at Robb, and declares him "The King in the North." He kneels and lays his sword at Robb's feet. Karstark follows, then Mormont, then the river lords — Blackwood, Bracken, Mallister — all kneeling and chanting "The King in the North."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deep grief for Ned; anguish at her father's impending death; rage at the world and the gods; despairing when her peace plea fails; fierce pride in Robb tempered by fear for him
- **Primary preoccupation:** Mourning Ned; worrying about her father; wanting Sansa and Arya back; wanting the war to end
- **Key decisions made:** Publicly advocates for peace and prisoner exchange at the war council; this is rejected
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn experiences guilt ("It was your doing, yours … if you had not taken it upon yourself to seize the dwarf") but suppresses it quickly. She tells herself she must be strong, must not think about Ned — actively compartmentalizing grief.
