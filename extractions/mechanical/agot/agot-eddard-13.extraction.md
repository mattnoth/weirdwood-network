# AGOT — Eddard XIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 48
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard XIII
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard XIII
- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing (Maegor's Holdfast — the king's bedchamber; Ned's own chambers)
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell crypts (dream), Dragonstone (referenced), Casterly Rock (referenced), Storm's End (referenced), the kingswood (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Immediately following Robert's return from his hunting trip; late night through pre-dawn hours
- **time_markers:** Middle of the night (room "black as pitch," someone hammering on the door); moonlight described as "low over the walls, ripening toward full"; Robert's hunting party took two days to return him; events span from the nighttime summons through to pre-dawn ("before break of day"); the Wind Witch sails "on the evening tide" (the coming evening)
- **chapter_summary:** Ned is woken in the night and summoned to Robert's bedchamber in Maegor's Holdfast, where the king lies mortally wounded by a boar during his hunt. Robert dictates his will naming Ned as Lord Regent and Protector of the Realm, though Ned substitutes "my heir" for "my son Joffrey." Robert expresses regret over the assassination attempt on Daenerys Targaryen and asks Ned to let her live. After Robert is given milk of the poppy, Ned refuses Lord Renly's urgent proposal to seize the royal children immediately, then summons Littlefinger and reveals the truth about Joffrey's parentage. Ned rejects Littlefinger's suggestion to keep Joffrey on the throne and instead commits to supporting Stannis's claim, arranging to send a letter to Dragonstone and to bribe the City Watch through Littlefinger.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned's dream of the Winterfell crypts** — Ned walks through the crypts, passing the Kings of Winter with their ice eyes and stone direwolves. He reaches his father's tomb where Brandon and Lyanna lie beside Rickard. Lyanna's statue whispers "Promise me, Ned," wearing pale blue roses, her eyes weeping blood.
2. **Ned is woken and summoned** — Tomard, Cayn, and the royal steward wake Ned in the middle of the night. The king commands his presence at once.
3. **Ned dresses and crosses to Maegor's Holdfast** — Cayn helps him dress. They cross the inner bailey under moonlight and enter Maegor's Holdfast, passing three Kingsguard (Ser Boros, Ser Preston, Ser Barristan).
4. **Ned sees the mortally wounded Robert** — Robert lies on the canopied bed, gored from groin to nipple by a boar. Cersei sits at the bedside; Renly paces; Pycelle hovers. Robert's wound is bandaged but already mortified, and the room reeks of blood and death.
5. **Robert explains the injury** — Robert blames too much wine and a missed thrust. Renly confirms Robert commanded the Kingsguard to stand aside. Robert says he killed the boar by driving a knife through its eye.
6. **Robert orders everyone out** — Robert dismisses Cersei, Renly, Pycelle, and the servants so he can speak privately with Ned. He refuses the milk of the poppy.
7. **Robert dictates his will** — Robert commands Ned to write his will naming Ned as Lord Regent and Protector of the Realm until "my son Joffrey" comes of age. Ned writes "my heir" instead of "my son Joffrey," unable to tell the dying Robert the truth about Joffrey's parentage.
8. **Robert expresses regret about Daenerys** — Robert has a change of heart about the assassination order on Daenerys Targaryen, calling her "only a child" and saying Ned was right. He asks Ned to stop the assassination if possible.
9. **Robert asks Ned to help his son** — Robert asks Ned to "make him be better than me," and says he will give Lyanna Ned's love. He asks Ned to take care of his children.
10. **Ned's painful internal response** — Unable to lie about Robert's children, Ned remembers Robert's bastards (Barra, Mya, Gendry) and says "I shall guard your children as if they were my own."
11. **Robert's will is sealed and witnessed** — Renly and Pycelle are called back as witnesses. Robert presses his seal into hot yellow wax. He then accepts the milk of the poppy and falls asleep.
12. **Pycelle's prognosis** — Pycelle tells Ned the wound has mortified and only the gods can heal Robert now. It took two days to bring him back from the hunt.
13. **Renly's wonder at Robert's strength** — Renly marvels that Robert slew the boar even as his entrails were sliding from his belly, comparing himself physically to Robert ("might have been Robert's ghost — young and dark and handsome").
14. **Ned instructs Ser Barristan** — Ned tells Barristan that no one may disturb Robert without Ned's leave. Barristan expresses that he has "failed my sacred trust."
15. **Varys appears and questions the wine** — Varys materializes and asks who gave Robert the wine. Ser Barristan identifies Lancel, the king's squire, as the one who kept fetching fresh wineskins. Varys pointedly notes Lancel is Ser Kevan's son and the queen's cousin.
16. **Ned orders Daenerys's assassination cancelled** — Ned tells Varys to unmake whatever arrangements were made regarding Daenerys. Varys says it may be too late but will try.
17. **Renly proposes seizing the royal children** — On the bridge over the dry moat, Renly offers Ned a hundred swords and urges him to seize Joffrey, Myrcella, and Tommen from Cersei immediately, arguing "the man who holds the king holds the kingdom."
18. **Ned refuses Renly's plan** — Ned refuses to "dishonor Robert's last hours by shedding blood in his halls and dragging frightened children from their beds." He says he will convene the council. Renly warns that delay favors Cersei and departs.
19. **Ned reflects on Cersei's warning** — Back in his chambers, Ned recalls Cersei's words: "When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die." He wonders if refusing Renly was the right choice.
20. **Ned arranges the departure of his daughters** — Ned reassigns the escort of his daughters aboard the Wind Witch from Porther to Tomard, increasing the guard from ten to twenty men.
21. **Ned orders a letter delivered to Stannis at Dragonstone** — Ned instructs Tomard to deliver a letter to Lord Stannis personally at Dragonstone, with Captain Qos flying the Stark banner on approach.
22. **Ned writes the letter to Stannis** — Ned composes a letter informing Stannis of Robert's impending death, signing it as Lord of Winterfell, Hand of the King, and Protector of the Realm. He reflects that Stannis must sail for King's Landing before the Lannisters can march.
23. **Ned thinks of home** — Ned reflects that his regency will be short and thinks longingly of Winterfell — Bran's laughter, hawking with Robb, watching Rickon play, sleeping beside Catelyn.
24. **Littlefinger arrives and already knows about the regency** — Littlefinger arrives and reveals he already knows Robert named Ned Protector, having been tipped off by Varys.
25. **Ned reveals the truth about Joffrey** — Ned tells Littlefinger that Joffrey and Tommen are Jaime Lannister's bastards, born of incest, and that the throne passes to Stannis.
26. **Littlefinger proposes keeping Joffrey on the throne** — Littlefinger argues that crowning Stannis means war and suggests Ned rule as regent, make peace with the Lannisters, wed his children to the royal children, and eventually dispose of Stannis or reveal Joffrey's secret to install Renly.
27. **Ned rejects the proposal as treason** — Ned invokes Jon Arryn's murder, Jory Cassel's death, and the dagger used to attack Bran. He declares Stannis is the rightful heir and nothing can change that.
28. **Ned asks Littlefinger to buy the City Watch** — Ned admits he needs the gold cloaks. Littlefinger agrees to go to Janos Slynt and buy the City Watch for six thousand gold pieces — a third for the Commander, a third for officers, a third for the men.

- **Primary preoccupation:** How to manage the succession crisis — securing Stannis's claim while protecting against the Lannisters; the moral weight of his choices; getting his daughters to safety
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Substitutes "my heir" for "my son Joffrey" in Robert's will; (2) Refuses Renly's plan to seize the royal children by force; (3) Commits to supporting Stannis as rightful heir; (4) Rejects Littlefinger's proposal to keep Joffrey on the throne; (5) Sends his daughters away on the Wind Witch; (6) Writes to Stannis urging him to come to King's Landing; (7) Agrees to buy the City Watch through Littlefinger
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned feels "soiled" by writing "my heir" but rationalizes it as "the lies we tell for love"; he trusts Littlefinger despite recognizing his lack of honor, relying on Littlefinger's professed love for Catelyn; he hopes Cersei will flee rather than fight, despite evidence she won't; he imagines his regency will be short and he'll return to Winterfell; he wonders if refusing Renly was right but does not act on that doubt
