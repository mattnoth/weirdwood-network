- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn has not left Bran's sickroom since his fall. Robb confronts her about neglecting her duties and the other children. A fire breaks out in the library tower, drawing everyone away, and an assassin enters Bran's room to kill him. Catelyn fights the man bare-handed, suffering deep cuts to her fingers, before Bran's direwolf kills the attacker. After sleeping four days recovering, Catelyn reveals to Robb, Ser Rodrik, Maester Luwin, and Theon Greyjoy her suspicion that the Lannisters were behind Bran's fall and the assassination attempt. She decides to travel to King's Landing herself by ship from White Harbor, accompanied only by Ser Rodrik.

## Events & Actions
1. **Maester Luwin brings accounts** — He arrives at Bran's sickroom at night with books of account and a reading lamp, urging Catelyn to review the costs of the royal visit and make household appointments (steward, captain of guards, master of horse).
2. **Catelyn refuses to engage** — She refuses to look at the figures and erupts in anger when the master of horse position is raised, saying she would butcher every horse in Winterfell to open Bran's eyes.
3. **Robb intervenes** — He enters the sickroom, takes the list of appointments from Maester Luwin, and dismisses the maester.
4. **Robb confronts Catelyn** — He tells her she hasn't left the room since Bran was hurt, that Rickon is frightened and crying, and that he needs her help. His voice breaks with emotion.
5. **Direwolves howl** — A wolf begins howling outside; Robb opens the window. Three wolves howl in chorus — Bran's wolf, Shaggydog, and Grey Wind. Catelyn is shaken and screams for them to stop.
6. **Robb helps Catelyn to bed** — He lifts her from the floor and puts her in the narrow bed in the sickroom corner, urging her to sleep.
7. **Fire breaks out in the library tower** — Dogs begin barking; Robb sees fire through the window. The direwolves fall silent.
8. **Robb leaves to fight the fire** — He orders the guards to come with him and rushes downstairs.
9. **Catelyn watches the fire from the window** — She sees flames and smoke from the library tower across the bailey and is relieved Bran is safe.
10. **Assassin enters the sickroom** — A small dirty man with a dagger appears, muttering that no one was supposed to be here.
11. **Catelyn fights the assassin** — He grabs her and puts the dagger to her throat. She grabs the blade with both hands, cutting her fingers deeply. She bites his hand, tearing flesh. He throws her down.
12. **Bran's direwolf kills the assassin** — The wolf slips through the open door, leaps on the man, and tears out half his throat. His blood sprays across Catelyn's face.
13. **The wolf joins Bran** — The wolf licks the blood from Catelyn's hand, then jumps on Bran's bed and lies beside him. Catelyn laughs hysterically.
14. **Catelyn is found and tended** — Robb, Maester Luwin, Ser Rodrik, and guards burst in. She is wrapped in blankets and taken to the Great Keep. Old Nan bathes her. Maester Luwin dresses her wounds and gives her milk of the poppy.
15. **Catelyn sleeps four days** — She wakes feeling resolute, ashamed of her earlier behavior. She asks for bread and honey and to have her bandages changed.
16. **Council in Catelyn's chambers** — Robb, Ser Rodrik, Theon Greyjoy, and Hallis Mollen arrive. They discuss the assassin: unknown man, hiding in the stables, paid ninety silver stags.
17. **Ser Rodrik identifies the dagger** — The blade is Valyrian steel with a dragonbone hilt — too fine for such a man. Someone gave it to him.
18. **Catelyn reveals her suspicions** — She swears them to secrecy and tells them Lysa believes the Lannisters murdered Jon Arryn, and that Jaime Lannister stayed behind in the castle the day Bran fell. She believes Bran was thrown, not that he fell.
19. **Robb deduces the motive** — Someone is afraid Bran might wake and reveal what he knows.
20. **Security for Bran ordered** — Robb orders guards posted: one in the sickroom, one outside the door, two at the bottom of the stairs, and the direwolf to stay with Bran.
21. **Catelyn decides to go to King's Landing** — She determines she must go herself to investigate. She will travel with only Ser Rodrik, following the White Knife to White Harbor and taking a ship, to arrive ahead of Ned's party.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Begins in grief-stricken paralysis and exhaustion — she hasn't slept properly since Bran's fall, her head hurts constantly, she's trembling and sobbing. After the attack and four days of sleep, she undergoes a transformation: she feels "weak and light-headed, yet strangely resolute, as if a great weight had lifted from her." She is ashamed of how she had been before.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Bran's survival dominates the first half. After waking, her focus shifts decisively to identifying the threat to her family and taking action — who tried to kill Bran, why, and what to do about it.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Decides the fire was a deliberate diversion and the assassin was sent to kill Bran. (2) Decides to reveal Lysa's letter and her suspicion about Jaime Lannister to the inner council. (3) Decides she must personally go to King's Landing to investigate. (4) Chooses to travel by river and ship via White Harbor rather than the kingsroad, with only Ser Rodrik as escort.
- **Self-deception flags:** None apparent — Catelyn's shift from paralysis to action is presented as clear-eyed. She accurately recognizes and is ashamed of her prior state ("She had let them all down").
