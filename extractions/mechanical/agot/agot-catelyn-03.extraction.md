- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn has been keeping a sleepless vigil at Bran's bedside for days, neglecting her duties and other children. Robb confronts her about this. A fire breaks out in the library tower, drawing away the guards, and an assassin enters Bran's sickroom with a Valyrian steel dagger. Catelyn fights the assassin bare-handed, suffering deep cuts to her fingers, before Bran's direwolf kills the man. After sleeping four days, Catelyn recovers her resolve, shares her suspicion that Bran was thrown from the tower by Jaime Lannister, and decides to travel secretly to King's Landing with Ser Rodrik via White Harbor to investigate.

## Events & Actions
1. **Maester Luwin visits with accounts** — Eight days after Ned's departure, Luwin comes to Bran's sickroom at night with account books and a reading lamp, wanting to review figures from the royal visit and discuss vacant appointments (steward, captain of the guard, master of horse).
2. **Catelyn refuses to engage** — Catelyn dismisses the accounts and erupts in anger when Luwin mentions the master of horse position, declaring she would butcher every horse in Winterfell to save Bran.
3. **Robb intervenes** — Robb arrives and offers to handle the appointments himself, taking the list of names from Luwin.
4. **Robb confronts Catelyn** — After dismissing Luwin, Robb tells Catelyn she hasn't left the sickroom since Bran's fall, didn't come to the gate to see their father off, and that Rickon (age three) needs her. He admits he can't manage alone.
5. **Direwolves howl** — A wolf begins howling outside the tower; Robb opens the window and identifies three wolves — Bran's wolf, Shaggydog, and Grey Wind — by their voices.
6. **Catelyn breaks down** — Overwhelmed by grief, cold, and the howling, Catelyn collapses, begging them to kill the wolves if necessary to stop the noise. Robb lifts her and puts her on her narrow bed.
7. **Fire in the library tower** — Dogs begin barking, and Robb spots fire in the library tower. The direwolves fall silent.
8. **Robb leaves to fight the fire** — Robb tells Catelyn to stay and runs out with the guards to deal with the fire.
9. **Assassin enters Bran's sickroom** — With the guards gone, a small, dirty man with a dagger enters the room, surprised to find Catelyn there. He says killing Bran is "a mercy."
10. **Catelyn fights the assassin** — Catelyn grabs the dagger blade with both hands to keep it from her throat, suffering deep cuts. She bites the assassin's palm, tearing flesh, and screams.
11. **Bran's direwolf kills the assassin** — The wolf slips through the open door, leaps on the assassin, and tears out half his throat. Blood sprays across Catelyn's face.
12. **The wolf guards Bran** — The wolf licks blood from Catelyn's hand, then jumps onto Bran's bed and lies beside him.
13. **Catelyn is found and treated** — Robb, Maester Luwin, Ser Rodrik, and guards find Catelyn laughing hysterically. She is wrapped in blankets, taken to the Great Keep. Old Nan bathes her; Luwin dresses her wounds and gives her milk of the poppy.
14. **Catelyn sleeps four days** — She wakes feeling resolute, ashamed of her prior behavior, resolved to be strong.
15. **Council in Catelyn's chambers** — Robb, Ser Rodrik, Theon Greyjoy, and Hallis Mollen (new captain of the guard) report on the assassin: unknown man, hiding in the stables, had ninety silver stags.
16. **Catelyn deduces the assassination plot** — She reasons the fire was set as a diversion and that someone fears what Bran might say if he wakes.
17. **Ser Rodrik identifies the dagger** — The blade is Valyrian steel with a dragonbone hilt, too fine for such a man — someone gave it to him.
18. **Catelyn reveals her suspicions** — After swearing everyone to secrecy, she tells them Lysa believes the Lannisters murdered Jon Arryn, and that Jaime Lannister stayed behind from the hunt the day Bran fell. She concludes Bran was thrown, not fallen.
19. **Security measures ordered** — Robb orders guards posted around Bran at all times and allows the direwolf to stay in the room.
20. **Catelyn decides to go to King's Landing** — She announces she must go herself to investigate, rejecting Robb's offer to go (he must stay — "There must always be a Stark in Winterfell"). She plans to travel secretly with Ser Rodrik, following the White Knife to White Harbor to hire a ship, arriving before Ned's slow column.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Progresses through several phases: obsessive grief and exhaustion (sleepless vigil), shame (at being caught shouting), terror and desperation (during the assassination attempt), hysteria (laughing after the wolf saves them), and finally resolute determination (after waking from four days' sleep). She feels ashamed of having let everyone down and resolves to be strong.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Bran's survival dominates the first half; after the attack, her focus shifts to identifying the conspiracy behind Bran's fall and the assassination attempt, and protecting her family.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Allows Robb to handle household appointments. (2) Decides she must go to King's Landing herself to investigate. (3) Chooses to travel secretly with only Ser Rodrik, via the White Knife and White Harbor rather than the kingsroad. (4) Orders the direwolf to stay with Bran.
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn internally notes Ned "had no choice, he had told her, and then he left, choosing" — she sees his departure as a choice disguised as duty, though she does not confront this directly. She also acknowledges her prior behavior was a failure ("She had let them all down") but frames her recovery as Tully strength vs. northern expectations, suggesting she processes her breakdown through house identity rather than purely personal terms.
