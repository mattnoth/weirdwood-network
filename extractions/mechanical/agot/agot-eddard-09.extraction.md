- **chapter_summary:** Ned visits Chataya's brothel with Littlefinger to see another of Robert's bastard children — a baby girl named Barra. While riding back through the rainy streets of King's Landing, Ned asks Littlefinger about Robert's bastards and why Jon Arryn might have investigated them. They are ambushed by Jaime Lannister and Lannister guardsmen demanding to know about Tyrion's capture. Jaime orders Ned's men killed; Jory Cassel, Heward, and Wyl are slain while Ned suffers a broken leg when his horse falls. Littlefinger returns with the City Watch to find Ned cradling Jory's body.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned descends from upstairs in Chataya's brothel** — He finds Littlefinger chatting with a tall, elegant black-skinned woman in the common room. Heward plays forfeits with a wench, Jory watches by a rain-streaked window.
2. **Ned announces departure** — He pulls on his gloves and tells the group it's time to leave. Heward gathers his things; Jory goes to bring the horses.
3. **Littlefinger taunts Ned** — He jokes about Ned doing "the king's business" and mocks the Hand's role. Ned cuts him off, stating he is no longer the King's Hand and will not endure mockery.
4. **The party rides through rainy King's Landing** — Warm rain falls from a starless sky. Littlefinger talks about buying Chataya's brothel as an investment. Ned ignores him. Streets are dark and deserted.
5. **Ned recalls Lyanna's words about Robert** — He remembers Lyanna at Winterfell, on the night of her betrothal, warning that Robert would never keep to one bed. She knew Robert had already fathered a child in the Vale.
6. **Ned reflects on Barra's mother** — He recalls the very young girl with light red hair and freckles, nursing the baby Barra. She named the girl Barra and asked Ned to tell Robert how beautiful she is and that she's been faithful.
7. **Ned recalls touching baby Barra's hair** — The baby has fine, dark hair like "black silk." Ned notes Robert's firstborn had the same fine hair.
8. **Ned promises to tell Robert about Barra** — The girl begs Ned to tell Robert she's waiting and has been with no one else. Ned promises Barra shall not go wanting.
9. **Ned thinks of Jon Snow** — Riding in the rain, Ned sees Jon Snow's face before him, "so like a younger version of his own." He muses on why the gods fill men with lusts if they frown on bastards.
10. **Ned asks Littlefinger about Robert's bastards** — Littlefinger reveals: an acknowledged boy at Storm's End fathered the night Stannis wed (mother was a Florent, niece to Lady Selyse); rumored twins at Casterly Rock (Cersei allegedly had them killed and sold the mother to a slaver).
11. **Ned wonders why Jon Arryn investigated the bastards** — Littlefinger dismisses it as routine Hand's business. Ned insists it must be more than that, or why kill him. Littlefinger mocks the idea.
12. **Ned remembers Rhaegar Targaryen** — He wonders if Rhaegar had frequented brothels; thinks not.
13. **Jaime Lannister ambushes Ned's party** — The street fills with Lannister soldiers: at least twenty men in ringmail, golden lion helms, with longswords and spears, blocking both directions. Jory draws his sword.
14. **Jaime confronts Ned about Tyrion** — Jaime rides forward on a blood bay stallion, wearing a golden breastplate. He demands to know who took Tyrion, saying his father is "quite vexed."
15. **Ned admits Tyrion was taken at his command** — He states Tyrion was taken "to answer for his crimes."
16. **Jaime challenges Ned to draw steel** — He threatens to butcher Ned "like Aerys" and dismisses Littlefinger contemptuously. Littlefinger flees to fetch the City Watch.
17. **Ned warns Jaime that killing him means Tyrion dies** — Ned says Catelyn will slay Tyrion if Ned is killed. Jaime pokes Ned's chest with his gilded sword, doubts "the noble Catelyn Tully" would murder a hostage, but is unwilling to risk his brother's life.
18. **Jaime sheathes his sword but orders Ned's men killed** — He tells Tregar to ensure no harm comes to Lord Stark, then orders "kill his men."
19. **Battle in the street** — Ned rides down a Lannister man. Jory charges and his horse's hoof catches a guardsman in the face. Wyl is pulled from his dying horse. Ned strikes Tregar's helm, shearing the lion crest. Heward is speared in the belly. Jory is dragged down and hacked to death.
20. **Ned's horse falls and his leg breaks** — The horse slips in the mud. Ned sees splintered bone poking through his calf. He loses consciousness.
21. **Ned wakes alone with the dead** — His horse bolts from the scent of blood. He drags himself through mud. People watch from windows but no one helps.
22. **Littlefinger and the City Watch find Ned** — He is cradling Jory Cassel's body in his arms in the street.
23. **Ned is brought to the Red Keep** — The trip is a blur of agony on a litter. The Red Keep's pale pink walls appear blood-colored in the rain at dawn.
24. **Grand Maester Pycelle treats Ned** — Pycelle gives Ned milk of the poppy, orders wine heated to boiling, and calls for clean silk.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Cold determination at the brothel giving way to somber reflection; guilt and sorrow when thinking about Robert's bastards, Jon Snow, and promises to Lyanna; hollow when promising Barra's mother; horror and rage during the ambush; agony from his broken leg
- **Primary preoccupation:** Robert's bastard children and why Jon Arryn was investigating them; the connection between the bastards' appearances and something Ned is piecing together (notes Robert's firstborn had the same dark hair as Barra); memories of Lyanna and promises made
- **Key decisions made:** Admits to Jaime that Tyrion was taken at his command rather than deflecting; uses Catelyn's hostage-holding as leverage rather than fighting; tried to save his men rather than flee
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned "assured her that what Robert did before their betrothal was of no matter, that he was a good man and true who would love her with all his heart" — Lyanna was right and he knew it even then. He also promised Barra's mother he would tell Robert, knowing Robert's pattern of forgetting.
