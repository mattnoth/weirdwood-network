- **chapter_summary:** Three Night's Watch rangers — Will, Ser Waymar Royce, and Gared — track a band of wildling raiders beyond the Wall. Will had scouted ahead and found eight wildlings apparently dead at their camp. When the three return to the site, the bodies have vanished. The Others appear from the woods and engage Ser Waymar in single combat; his castle-forged steel shatters against the Other's crystal blade. The watching Others move in and kill him. Will descends from the tree where he hid, only to find Royce reanimated with a burning blue eye, who strangles him.

## Events & Actions
1. **Gared urges turning back** — As the woods grow dark, Gared argues the wildlings are dead and they should return, citing the long ride back and worsening weather.
2. **Royce questions whether the wildlings are dead** — He challenges the evidence, noting no blood was found and the Wall has been weeping (too warm for men to freeze).
3. **Will describes the wildling camp** — He reports eight dead wildlings (men and women), a lean-to against a rock, no fire, no movement, including a woman lookout in an ironwood tree.
4. **Gared attributes the deaths to cold** — He gives an extended account of how cold kills, showing his ear stumps as evidence, and mentions his brother dying frozen.
5. **Royce orders them forward** — He commands Will to lead them to the campsite, refusing to return to Castle Black a failure on his first ranging.
6. **The three ride toward the campsite** — Will in front on a garron, Royce on his destrier, Gared in the rear muttering; twilight deepens to full dark, a half-moon rises.
7. **They dismount and proceed on foot** — Will dismounts under an ironwood; Royce dismounts and draws his longsword; Gared is ordered to stay and guard the horses. Gared's request for a fire is denied.
8. **Will discovers the bodies have vanished** — Crawling under the sentinel tree at the ridge, Will sees the clearing is empty. All bodies are gone, but the wildling's battle-axe remains.
9. **Royce orders Will up a tree to look for a fire** — Royce refuses to accept the situation and commands Will to climb the sentinel tree to search for signs of the wildlings.
10. **The Others appear** — While Will climbs, pale shapes glide through the wood. An Other emerges and confronts Royce.
11. **Ser Waymar fights the Other** — Royce engages in single combat; blades meet with an unearthly keening sound; Royce's sword accumulates frost; the Other wounds Royce beneath his arm.
12. **The Other speaks** — It says something mocking in an unknown language; voice like cracking ice.
13. **Royce's sword shatters** — After shouting "For Robert!" and swinging with full force, Royce's castle-forged steel shatters on contact with the Other's blade. Shards wound his eyes.
14. **The watching Others kill Royce** — Five or more Others move in together in silence, cutting through his ringmail with their pale blades. Will hears their voices and laughter.
15. **Will descends and recovers the broken sword** — After a long wait in the tree, Will climbs down, finds Royce's body facedown, and picks up the broken sword as proof.
16. **Royce reanimates and kills Will** — Will rises to find Ser Waymar standing over him — clothes tattered, face ruined, a sword shard in one blind white eye, the other eye burning blue. Reanimated Royce strangles Will with icy cold hands.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Mounting dread and fear throughout; shares Gared's unease from the chapter's opening; fear described as "a meal he could not digest"; terrified paralysis during the Others' attack; brief hope when the Other pauses to study Royce's sword
- **Primary preoccupation:** Something is deeply wrong in the forest — "an edge to this darkness"; the feeling of being watched by "something cold and implacable that loved him not"; survival
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses not to warn Royce when the Others appear (self-preservation over duty — "It was his duty. And his death, if he did"); picks up the broken sword as proof; decides to seek Gared, Mormont, or Maester Aemon with the evidence
- **Self-deception flags:** Tries to rationalize the pale shapes as "a bird, a reflection on the snow, some trick of the moonlight" when he first glimpses the Others; "Perhaps he was wrong"
