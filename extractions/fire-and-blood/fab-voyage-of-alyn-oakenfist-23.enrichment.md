## Node Prose
### Alyn Oakenfist
Lord Alyn Oakenfist, also styled Alyn Velaryon and husband of Lady Baela, led the Velaryon fleet on an epic voyage around the "arse of Westeros" toward the Sunset Sea to face the Red Kraken — quote: "speak of Lady Baela’s lord husband Alyn Oakenfist on his epic voyage". At the Stepstones he chose to parley rather than fight, sailing under a flag into Bloodstone to treat with Racallio Ryndoon — quote: "sailed her into Bloodstone beneath a parley flag". At Oldtown he befriended Lyonel Hightower, gaining pledges of warships, and received the High Septon's blessing — quote: "struck up a fast friendship that did much to put all the old enmities". He was famed for having humbled the Sealord of Braavos — quote: "the man who had humbled the proud Sealord of Braavos". Learning a secret from Drazenko Rogare at Sunspear, he sailed for Lys and returned bearing the lost prince Viserys, agreeing personally to a costly ransom — quote: "delivering Viserys from his captivity in Lys".

### Racallio Ryndoon
Racallio Ryndoon was a Tyroshi adventurer who ruled the Stepstones for a few short years, styled "Queen Racallio" by the Archon of Tyrosh — quote: "for a few short years, they made him a king". Little reliable is known of him; he was said to stand six-and-a-half feet with one shoulder higher than the other, spoke a dozen Valyrian dialects, and dyed his hair purple — quote: "supposedly, with one shoulder higher than another". He honored every god and threw the bones before battle to choose which to placate with sacrifice — quote: "He honored the gods: all gods, everywhere". He kept Alyn a fortnight at Bloodstone, alternately friend and captor, and finally allowed the fleet passage for three ships, a signed alliance, and a kiss — quote: "kept him for more than a fortnight in his sprawling wooden fortress". The narration states flatly that he was mad, yet beloved by his men — quote: "Racallio Ryndoon was mad."

### Aegon III Targaryen
King Aegon III Targaryen wed the six-year-old Lady Daenaera on the last day of 133 AC — quote: "wed Lady Daenaera on the last day of the 133rd year". Called the Broken King, he blamed himself for fleeing the Gay Abandon on Stormcloud and abandoning his brother Viserys before the Battle of the Gullet — quote: "he fled the Gay Abandon on dragonback". His gloom lifted with Daenaera's cheer and again, greatly, when Viserys was restored to him, whereupon he wept and embraced the boy — quote: "throwing himself upon this boy in a fierce embrace". He began attending council but withdrew after the Hand belittled his questions — quote: "such weighty matters were beyond the understanding of a child".

### Viserys Targaryen
Prince Viserys, youngest son of Queen Rhaenyra and Prince Daemon, was taken from the Gay Abandon before the Battle of the Gullet and presumed dead for nearly five years — quote: "the youngest son of Queen Rhaenyra and Prince Daemon". He was held captive first by Sharako Lohar, then sold to Bambarro Bazanne, and finally passed to Lysandro Rogare — quote: "passed into the hands of another nobleman, Lysandro Rogare". Lysandro married him to his youngest daughter Larra, whose marriage Viserys had already consummated — quote: "married him to his youngest daughter, the Lady Larra Rogare". He was seven when taken and twelve on his return in 134 AC, and became the undisputed heir apparent — quote: "Prince Viserys had been seven when he was taken from the Gay Abandon".

### Unwin Peake
Lord Unwin Peake, King's Hand, was sour, proud, and by 134 AC a most unhappy man after the king rejected his daughter Myrielle in favor of Daenaera — quote: "the king's rejection of his daughter, Myrielle". He mistrusted Baela and Rhaena and the "insolent and rebellious Oakenfist," believing the twins conspired against him — quote: "both of them, he was convinced, were working against him". Overruled repeatedly by the other regents and furious at the ransom Alyn had pledged, he threatened to resign and the council accepted at once — quote: "the council accepted his resignation with alacrity". He withdrew to Starpike, though his many appointees kept their offices — quote: "removed himself to his seat at Starpike to brood".

### Dalton Greyjoy
Lord Dalton Greyjoy, the Red Kraken and Lord of the Iron Islands, gathered hundreds of longships to meet Alyn and mockingly called his younger foe "that boy" — quote: "he never called him anything but “that boy.”". He held two-and-twenty salt wives and boasted he might take Baela for a salt wife — quote: "‘Tis true, | have two-and-twenty salt wives". On the eve of his greatest battle he was slain in bed by his salt wife Tess — quote: "opened his throat from ear to ear". His death dissolved his fleet and left a disputed succession, the Seastone Chair falling to his salt son Toron, not yet six — quote: "the Seastone Chair passed to the eldest of his salt sons".

### Tess
Tess was one of Dalton Greyjoy's salt wives, of whom nothing certain is known but her name and her deed — quote: "One bore the name of Tess". Accounts of her differ so utterly she must remain a mystery — quote: "Tess must remain forever a mystery in the annals of history". On a rainy night at Faircastle she slit Dalton's throat with his own dagger and threw herself into the sea — quote: "Tess slipped his dagger from its sheath and opened his throat".

### Aliandra Martell
Aliandra Martell, Princess of Dorne and hailed the "new Nymeria," met Alyn's fleet off Sunspear and had just turned eighteen — quote: "The “new Nymeria” had just celebrated her eighteenth nameday". She was much taken with the young admiral, to the fury of her suitors and her siblings Qyle and Coryanne — quote: "much taken with the young, handsome, dashing “Hero of the Stepstones”". Whether Alyn returned her intimate favors is disputed between his two vulgar biographies; according to *Bastard Born* he did, and to *Hard as Oak* he did not — quote: "Bastard Born would have us believe that he provided them". It was at her court that Alyn learned Dorne had joined the Daughters' War against Racallio and met Drazenko Rogare — quote: "Dorne had joined the Daughters’ War".

### Larra Rogare
Larra Rogare, youngest daughter of Lysandro, was married to Prince Viserys and became known as Larra of Lys — quote: "would become known in the histories of Westeros as Larra of Lys". At nineteen she was seven years her husband's senior, a beautiful young woman well into her childbearing years — quote: "was nineteen, seven years his senior".

### Lysandro Rogare
Lysandro Rogare, "the Magnificent," was patriarch of a rich Lyseni banking and trading dynasty tracing its blood to Valyria before the Doom — quote: "whose bloodlines could be traced back to Valyria before the Doom". Once he learned his captive was a prince, he married Viserys to Larra rather than sell his favors — quote: "the magister quickly revised his plans". He struck the ransom terms with Alyn: a hundred thousand dragons, lordships for three sons, and a pledge never to void the marriage — quote: "the Iron Throne would pay a ransom of one hundred thousand golden dragons".

### Bambarro Bazanne
Bambarro Bazanne was a Lyseni magister who bought the captive Viserys from Sharako Lohar for the boy's weight in gold — quote: "he sold the boy to a certain magister of that city named Bambarro Bazanne". He kept the prince well but confined, meaning to ransom or sell him after the Dance — quote: "forbidden to leave the grounds of Bambarro’s manse". He died in the Disputed Lands in 132 AC when his sellsword company turned on him, and his creditors seized his household including the prince — quote: "Bambarro Bazanne died in the Disputed Lands in 132 AC".

### Lyonel Hightower
Lyonel Hightower welcomed Alyn to Oldtown and, warmed by his courtesy to Lady Sam, formed a fast friendship that eased old black-and-green enmities — quote: "Lyonel Hightower himself came forth to meet them". He promised twenty warships for the fleet — quote: "Oldtown would provide twenty warships for the fleet".

### Johanna Lannister
Lady Johanna Lannister came out from Casterly Rock to honor Alyn with a golden seahorse when he reached a Fair Isle already freed — quote: "Lady Johanna herself emerged from Casterly Rock". She urged an attack on the Iron Islands, offering to put every ironman to the sword and sell their families to slavers — quote: "We should put every man of them to the sword".

### Leo Costayne
Ser Leo Costayne, "the Sea Lion," commanded the twenty Hightower galleys that joined Alyn's fleet — quote: "commanded by Ser Leo Costayne, a grizzled seafarer known as the". At Lannisport, Alyn left him with a third of the fleet to guard the west — quote: "Leo Costayne, would remain at Lannisport with a third of the fleet".

### Drazenko Rogare
Drazenko Rogare, a Lyseni envoy at Aliandra's court, drew Alyn aside during the Maiden's Day feast at Sunspear and whispered a secret that changed the course of history — quote: "whispered something in the admiral's ear, a secret".

### Daenaera
Lady Daenaera, a happy six-year-old, became Aegon III's queen and lightened his gloom with her sunny nature — quote: "Queen Daenaera was a happier child". At the docks she hung a sapphire-studded golden chain about the returning admiral's neck — quote: "a heavy golden chain studded with sapphires".

### Gunthor Goodbrother
Gunthor Goodbrother, a "roaring giant" of the Faircastle garrison, slew his fellow commander Alester Wynch in a quarrel over Lysa Farman — quote: "the roaring giant Gunthor Goodbrother".

### Alester Wynch
Alester Wynch, called "the sly," helped hold Faircastle for the ironborn until Gunthor Goodbrother killed him — quote: "under the sly Alester Wynch".

### Russell Stillman
Ser Russell Stillman squired for Alyn, was knighted by him, later lost a leg on Alyn's fifth voyage, and wrote the vulgar life *Hard as Oak* — quote: "squired for his lordship as a youth and was later knighted by him".
