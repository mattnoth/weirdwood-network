## Node Prose
### Jaehaerys Targaryen
King during the Shivers; when the City Watch commander Qarl Corbray died and lawlessness spread, he sent forth his Kingsguard and household knights to restore order but soon had to call them back — quote: "he\n\nsoon had no choice but to call them back." He personally rode to claim Rego Draz's body and extracted the killers' names by threat — quote: "I would have the name of the men who did this." He ruled the killers unworthy of a clean death — quote: "you are lower than rats." He lost his daughter Daenerys to the Shivers, drank dreamwine, and demanded of Septon Barth why the gods took her — quote: "What sin did she commit?" He granted Rogar Baratheon leave and aid to hunt Borys and the Vulture King — quote: "you have my sword." He slew Borys with Blackfyre in the mountains — quote: "Borys Baratheon died at the king's feet." In 62 AC he named Aemon Prince of Dragonstone and launched his road-building project — quote: "My lords, | will have roads!"

### Alysanne
Queen; her beloved Septa Edyth died on Dragonstone. Her daughter Daenerys woke her complaining of cold and died a day and a half later, after which the queen collapsed and was given milk of the poppy — quote: "The queen collapsed in the king's arms." She took in the barefoot girl who had named Rego's killer, having her bathed and fed — quote: "There is a place for you in the castle, if you want it." Within two moons of Daenerys's death she conceived again and bore Alyssa late in 60 AC — quote: "she was delivered of her fifth child." She welcomed her half-sister Jocelyn as a ward — quote: "Jocelyn is our own sister, we have not."

### Rego Draz
Pentoshi master of coin, called the Lord of Air; served the Iron Throne ten years and never resided in the Red Keep, keeping a manse on the Street of Silk — quote: "The Pentoshi preferred his own manse on the Street of Silk." Grown stout, he traveled by gilded palanquin, and on a route through Flea Bottom a hungry mob who blamed him for the price of bread swarmed him — quote: "they held the master of coin to blame for the high cost of bread." His skull was crushed with a cobblestone and his fingers cut off for his rings — quote: "his skull crushed by one of the very cobblestones." He never married but had three bastard sons — quote: "he did have three bastard sons who had learned his business at his knee."

### Daenerys Targaryen
Princess, daughter of Jaehaerys and Alysanne, "the darling of the realm," age six and past weaning. She woke her mother saying she was cold; despite prayers, poultices, a summoned wet nurse and her kitten, she died within a day and a half — quote: "the little princess was dead." Her death struck at the Doctrine of Exceptionalism, for she was Targaryen on both sides — quote: "Princess Daenerys had been Targaryen on both sides."

### Grand Maester Benifer
Grand Maester who died of the Shivers as one of three council lords lost in King's Landing; he had served fifteen years, having come to the Red Keep after Maegor decapitated his three predecessors — quote: "served for fifteen years through times both perilous and prosperous." His successor observed dryly of his tenure — quote: "I would not have lasted three days under Maegor."

### Rogar Baratheon
Lord of Storm's End and one-time King's Hand; untouched by the Shivers though his brother Ronnal died and his children by Queen Alyssa recovered. In 61 AC, grey and gaunt, he brought three girls to court and, dying, asked Jaehaerys to take Jocelyn and her cousins as wards — quote: "accept Jocelyn and her cousins as wards." He meant to die fighting his traitor brother Borys and the Vulture King — quote: "I would not die abed, bleeding out of my arse." He slew the Vulture King in single combat with his axe — quote: "then clove him from shoulder to navel." He died at Storm's End half a year later — quote: "died at Storm's End half a year later."

### Alyssa Targaryen
Fifth child of Jaehaerys and Alysanne, born late 60 AC on Dragonstone and named for the queen's late mother. As a babe she resembled Daenerys but grew long-faced and skinny with mismatched eyes — quote: "one violet, the other a startling green." A wooden sword broke her nose at six, which she did not mind; she took after Baelon, wore boy's clothes, and refused porridge — quote: "it was not Daenerys that she took after, but Baelon."

### Aemon Targaryen
Eldest son of Jaehaerys; in 62 AC, aged seven, he was made Prince of Dragonstone and acknowledged heir — quote: "making him the acknowledged heir to the Iron Throne." Tall and handsome, he trained daily with his brother Baelon — quote: "He still trained every morning in the yard with Prince Baelon." At his investiture feast the queen seated Jocelyn beside him and the two talked and laughed all evening — quote: "the queen sat Lady Jocelyn next to him."

### Borys Baratheon
Ser; brother of Rogar who left the stormlands, spent time in Myr and Volantis, then joined the Vulture King in the Red Mountains and raided his own people — quote: "he has joined up with the Vulture King, and is raiding his own people." Cornered first, he rushed the king and was slain — quote: "Rather name me a kingslayer than him a kinslayer!"

### Vulture King
The second to bear the name; not the army-leading first but an upjumped raider who knew the mountains and used ambush — quote: "the minor son of a minor house." Jaehaerys burned his camps from the sky with Vermithor; brought to bay, he was given a spear and shield and slain by Rogar — quote: "Brought to bay in a burned lair where he had hoped to find refuge."

### Septon Barth
The king's wise councillor, who had no answers about Daenerys's death and later proposed the master-of-coin appointment — quote: "young Martyn Tyrell might prove a useful addition to this council." He named Martyn's able wife as a further reason — quote: "she has increased House Tyrell's" incomes.

### Martyn Tyrell
Young Lord of Highgarden, son of the late Bertrand; called young and eager but of doubtful wits, appointed master of coin — quote: "Let us try the dolt with the clever wife." His wife Florence Fossoway kept all the accounts of Highgarden — quote: "His wife, however, is a Fossoway girl, the Lady Florence."

### Florence Fossoway
Lady of House Tyrell by marriage, a Fossoway girl who had been counting apples since she learned to walk and kept Highgarden's accounts, reputedly increasing its incomes by a third — quote: "keeping all the accounts at Highgarden since her marriage."

### Jocelyn Baratheon
Rogar's daughter by Queen Alyssa, born in the Year of the Stranger; grown into a tall, solemn girl with large dark eyes and black hair — quote: "hair black as sin." Brought to court as a ward, she watched Aemon train and was seated beside him at his investiture feast — quote: "The third was Lady Jocelyn, his lordship's own daughter by Queen Alyssa."

### Grand Maester Elysar
Benifer's sharp-tongued successor, twenty years younger, sent by the Conclave; quipped of Princess Alyssa's naming out of the king's hearing — quote: "An honor Her Grace would have appreciated more had she been alive."

### Eustace Hightower
Survived the Shivers a second time, having earlier returned alive from Alys Westhill's voyage, though his wife and children died; grandson of Donnel the Delayer — quote: "survived once again, but his wife and children were not as fortunate."

### Daemon Velaryon
Lord of the Tides of Driftmark; recovered after being at the point of death, but lost his second son and three daughters to the Shivers — quote: "his second son and three of his daughters were borne away."

### Maegelle Targaryen
Daughter of Jaehaerys and Alysanne born 62 AC; gentle, selfless, sweet-natured and bright, she attached herself to her sister Alyssa — quote: "a daughter they named Maegelle."
