## Node Prose

### Otto Hightower
The Hand's schemes produced the Triarchy alliance; the High Council of the Three Daughters accepted his offer at Tyrosh, sending ninety warships to the Gullet — quote: "The schemes of Otto Hightower had borne fruit". He later emerged from Maegor's Holdfast with Alicent when King's Landing fell, and was the first traitor Rhaenyra beheaded, having served three kings as Hand — quote: "who had served three kings as Hand, was the first traitor to be beheaded".

### Aegon the Younger
Rhaenyra's son, only nine, was aboard the captured cog Gay Abandon and escaped by leaping onto his dragon Stormcloud, reaching Dragonstone white with terror — quote: "The boy was white with terror, Mushroom tells us". He had never flown before and would never fly again, for Stormcloud died of its wounds — quote: "Only nine, he had never flown before". Though he lived, all joy left him and he blamed himself for abandoning his little brother Viserys — quote: "he would never forgive himself for leaping onto Stormcloud".

### Prince Viserys
Rhaenyra's youngest son had no way off the Gay Abandon, so he hid his dragon egg and dressed as a common ship's boy — quote: "pretending to be no more than a common ship's boy". A real ship's boy betrayed him and he was taken captive; a Tyroshi captain first recognized him before Sharako Lohar claimed the prize — quote: "but one of the real ship's boys betrayed him, and he was made a captive". After the battle none were certain which ship he had been on, and both sides presumed him dead — quote: "Men on both sides presumed him dead, drowned or burned or butchered".

### Sharako Lohar
The Lysene admiral of the Triarchy fleet took Viserys as his prize and divided his fleet to attack the Gullet north and south of Dragonstone — quote: "the admiral of the fleet, Sharako Lohar of Lys". Of his ninety warships only twenty-eight limped home, and the widows of Myr and Tyrosh accused him of holding his own fleet back — quote: "accused the admiral of sending their fleets to destruction whilst holding back his own".

### Jacaerys Velaryon
Prince of Dragonstone and heir to the Iron Throne, Rhaenyra's son, he swept down on the Lysene galleys aboard Vermax — quote: "When Prince Jacaerys swept down upon a line of Lysene galleys on Vermax". Several differing tales were told of how his dragon fell; when Vermax went down, Jace leapt free onto wreckage until crossbowmen killed him — quote: "one quarrel took him through the neck, and Jace was swallowed by the sea". His loss was felt more deeply than any other in the battle — quote: "Prince of Dragonstone and heir to the Iron Throne".

### Vermax
Jacaerys Velaryon's dragon at the Gullet. According to one account a crossbowman put an iron bolt through his eye, a version thought suspiciously like the death of Meraxes; another says a Myrish sailor's grapnel tore open his belly — quote: "Several differing tales were told afterward of how and why the dragon fell". He went down smoking and screaming and sank with a burning galley — quote: "When the ship heeled over and sank, Vermax sank with her".

### Corlys Velaryon
The Sea Snake's fleet was caught unawares at the Gullet and Driftmark was ravaged; Spicetown was sacked and never rebuilt, and High Tide burned with all his treasures from the east — quote: "All the treasures the Sea Snake had brought back from the east were consumed by fire". Congratulated on the victory, he answered bitterly — quote: "If this be victory, | pray I never win another." Addam Velaryon sought him out after the battle — quote: "what they / spoke to each other even Mushroom does not say".

### Daeron Targaryen
The youngest of Alicent's three sons, fifteen and Ormund Hightower's squire, once milk brother to Jacaerys — quote: "who had once been milk brother to Prince Jacaerys". His arrival on Tessarion reversed the tide at the Honeywine, after which Ormund knighted him with the Valyrian longsword Vigilance as "Ser Daeron the Daring" — quote: "dubbed him a knight with the storied Valyrian longsword Vigilance". He deflected the honor to his dragon — quote: "but the victory belongs to Tessarion." In the Reach he continued to win victories as Lord Hightower's ward — quote: "Lord Hightower and his ward, Prince Daeron the Daring, continued to win victories".

### Ormund Hightower
Caught between two hosts on the banks of the Honeywine, his lines were crumbling until Daeron and Tessarion arrived — quote: "attacking front and rear at once, Lord Hightower saw his lines crumble". He feasted Daeron and knighted him, then enforced submissions across the Reach — quote: "enforcing the submission of the Rowans of Goldengrove, the Oakhearts of Old Oak".

### Rhaenyra
The queen refused to bend the knee despite the disasters, and Jace's death hardened rather than broke her — quote: "Broken by the loss of one son, Rhaenyra Targaryen seemed to find new strength after the loss of a second". She flew Syrax to take King's Landing, rejected Alicent's plea for a great council, and claimed the Iron Throne — quote: "the queen climbed the iron steps and seated herself where King Viserys had sat before her". Septon Eustace records that afterward cuts were seen on her legs and left palm — quote: "the Iron Throne had spurned her, and her days upon it would be few."

### Aemond
Ruling in his brother Aegon II's name while the king lay abed, Aemond One-Eye saw the greater threat in his uncle Daemon at Harrenhal — quote: "Aemond One-Eye saw a greater threat in his uncle Prince Daemon". Septon Eustace says the Kinslayer wanted the victory for himself alone — quote: "the Kinslayer was determined that this should be his victory". He reached an empty Harrenhal, then in fury put every man and boy of House Strong to death — quote: "until the heap made of their heads stood three feet tall". Refusing Criston's counsel to march south, he parted from the Hand to wage his own war of fire — quote: "Prince Aemond refused to consider this 'craven course.'"

### Prince Daemon
Rhaenyra's husband and prince consort, uncle to Aemond, he had gathered a great host at Harrenhal and long anticipated the attack on King's Landing — quote: "for he had long anticipated / this moment". He flew Caraxes south past Criston's line of march and took the city with the aid of the City Watch, who still loved the Prince of the City — quote: "for the rank and file of the City Watch still loved Daemon Targaryen". He proposed wedding the Rosby and Stokeworth daughters to Hugh Hammer and Ulf White — quote: "keeping their lands black whilst / suitably rewarding the seeds for their valor in battle".

### Criston Cole
The Hand and Kingmaker marched from King's Landing and reached an abandoned Harrenhal a day before Aemond — quote: "Ser Criston's vanguard fought and won a short, sharp battle". He counseled withdrawal south to join Ormund Hightower, judging Vhagar alone no match for six dragons — quote: "One against six is a fight for fools, My Prince". When Aemond refused, they parted ways and Cole led the host south — quote: "Cole would take command of their host and lead them south".

### Jason Lannister
The Lord of Casterly Rock assembled a formidable western host and poured down onto the Red Fork — quote: "Jason Lannister had assembled a formidable host in the western hills". He was dealt a mortal wound by the squire Pate of Longleaf and died before learning the ironmen had sacked Lannisport — quote: "Lord Jason was dealt a mortal wound at the hand of a grizzled squire, Pate of Longleaf".

### Sunfyre
Aegon II's golden dragon, his torn wing left him unable to fly, yet he survived Walys Mooton's attack, slaying threescore — quote: "his torn wing left the great golden wyrm unable to take to the air". Afterward he vanished with no tracks, having taken wing again — quote: "Sunfyre the Golden had taken wing again, it seemed".

### Walys Mooton
The Lord of Maidenpool led a hundred knights to retake Rook's Rest and then to kill Sunfyre — quote: "to put an end / to the dragon Sunfyre". He was among the slain, his body found a fortnight later by his brother Manfryd as charred flesh in melted armor — quote: "Amongst the slain was Walys Mooton, Lord of Maidenpool".

### Dalton Greyjoy
The Red Kraken of Pyke, courted by both claimants, made his choice and fell upon the Lannister shores — quote: "Courted by both claimants to the Iron Throne, the Red Kraken had made his choice". Unable to breach Casterly Rock once Johanna Lannister barred the gates, his ironmen sacked Lannisport and carried off more than six hundred women and girls — quote: "swarmed over the walls of Lannisport to sack the city".

### Alicent
As Queen Dowager she saw to the city's defenses when both Hand and Lord Protector were absent, closing the gates and dispatching riders for Aemond — quote: "it fell to his mother, the Queen Dowager, to see to the city's defenses". Her measures failed as the gold cloaks turned; she surrendered the keys and warned Rhaenyra — quote: "but my son Aemond will return with fire and blood." She was fettered in golden chains, her life spared for their father's sake — quote: "for the sake of our father, who loved you once."

### Luthor Largent
The commander of the gold cloaks killed Alicent's brother Gwayne when he denounced him as a turncloak, then opened the gates — quote: "Then he drove his sword through Ser Gwayne's belly". He was ennobled by Rhaenyra afterward — quote: "Ser Luthor Largent, commander of the gold cloaks, was ennobled".

### Simon Strong
The castellan yielded Harrenhal to Daemon, and Aemond took the swift surrender as proof of treason — quote: "convinced him the old man was a traitor". Given a sword for trial by combat, he was cut to pieces and fed to Vhagar — quote: "the prince cut the old man to pieces, then fed his corpse to Vhagar".

### Alys Rivers
The wet nurse of Harrenhal was the only bastard spared in the slaughter of House Strong, taken to Aemond's bed as a prize of war — quote: "Prince Aemond had taken her into his bed as a prize of war". Mushroom says she used love potions on Aemond and Criston, Munkun credits Aemond's respect for the older man, and Septon Eustace says Aemond alone was besotted with her — quote: "who had used love potions and philtres to inflame their passions".

### Roderick Dustin
The Lord of Barrowton, "Roddy the Ruin," led two thousand Winter Wolves south for Rhaenyra — quote: "a warrior so old and hoary men called / him Roddy the Ruin". His men begged to lead the charge at the Fishfeed and suffered the worst losses — quote: "more than two-thirds of the men who had ridden south with Lord Dustin were / dead or wounded".

### Humfrey Lefford
The aged lord took command of the western host after Adrian Tarbeck died, so wounded he led from a litter — quote: "he commanded / from a litter". Caught between two armies at the Gods Eye, he dug in and was slain at the Fishfeed — quote: "amongst them many notables, including Lord Frey, Lord Lefford".

### Robb Rivers
Red Robb Rivers, the Bowman of Raventree, said the finest archer in Westeros, joined the northmen and shot down every raven Lefford sent begging for aid — quote: "Red Robb Rivers, said to be the finest archer / in all Westeros, took them down on the wing".

### Benjicot Blackwood
The eleven-year-old new Lord of Raventree brought fresh levies to the Fishfeed and wept at the heaps of the dead — quote: "the boy Lord of Raventree, wept when he saw the heaps of the dead".

### Pate of Longleaf
A grizzled squire who dealt Jason Lannister his mortal wound, afterward knighted by Lord Piper as "Longleaf the Lionslayer" — quote: "dubbing him Longleaf the Lionslayer". He later led survivors alongside the lords who joined the Fishfeed — quote: "Longleaf the Lionslayer and a ragged band of survivors".

### Adrian Tarbeck
Took command of the western host and slew Tristan Vance by swimming the Red Fork to take his lines from the rear — quote: "this time it was Lord Vance who fell, slain by Ser Adrian Tarbeck". He was himself slain by the hedge knight Harry Penny at Acorn Hall — quote: "This unlikely hero died soon after, whilst slaying Adrian Tarbeck".

### Gwayne Hightower
Alicent's brother and second in command of the gold cloaks, he rushed to sound the warning but was seized and killed by Luthor Largent — quote: "Queen Alicent's brother Ser Gwayne Hightower, second in command of the gold cloaks".

### Hugh the Hammer
A dragonrider who flew Vermithor and lived to boast of it, the blacksmith's son who called himself Hugh Hammer — quote: "the dragonriders Hugh the Hammer and Ulf the White". He declared they were knights now — quote: "We are knights now, truly".

### Ulf the White
A dragonrider who flew Silverwing at the Gullet; later "Ulf the Sot" and "Ulf White" — quote: "who had flown Vermithor and Silverwing into battle and lived to boast of it". He answered Hugh that they should be lords — quote: "Fie on that. We should be lords."

### Addam Velaryon
Lately Addam of Hull, rider of Seasmoke, who sought out the Sea Snake after the Gullet — quote: "And Addam Velaryon, lately Addam of Hull, sought out the Sea Snake". At King's Landing he remained aloft on Seasmoke as a warning against defiance — quote: "the beat of his dragon's wide leathern wings a caution to those below".

### Nettles
A girl who flew and fought bravely at the Gullet but did not celebrate, returning with a face black with smoke and streaked with tears — quote: "her face was black with smoke and streaked with tears when she returned".

### Larys Strong
The Clubfoot, Lord of Harrenhal and Aegon II's master of whisperers, was the only one of Alicent's party absent when King's Landing fell — quote: "The master of whisperers had somehow contrived to disappear". Aemond judged him a traitor and uncle to Rhaenyra's bastards — quote: "Larys Clubfoot was Breakbones's brother, and thus an uncle to / Rhaenyra's bastards".

### Otto Hightower
See above.

### Tyland Lannister
Ser; he backed Aemond's council, and after the fall was given to the torturers to recover the Crown's treasure — quote: "Ser Tyland Lannister was given to the / torturers instead".

### Orwyle
Grand Maester who urged Aemond to seek Baratheon aid, then was seized by gold cloaks and taken to the black cells when he tried to summon Alicent's loyal lords — quote: "the Grand Maester was escorted down to the black cells".
