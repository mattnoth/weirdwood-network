## Node Prose

### Tyland Lannister
Ser Tyland Lannister, the Hand, died of the Winter Fever, the fever taking his life in two days instead of the usual four — quote: "The fever took his life in two days instead of the usual four." He was born a few heartbeats after his twin brother Jason, which denied him lordship and Casterly Rock — quote: "a few heartbeats after his twin brother, Jason, had denied him the glory of lordship." As Aegon II's master of coin he moved three-quarters of the Crown's gold out of King's Landing, sowing the seeds of Rhaenyra's downfall, a stroke that cost him his eyes, ears, and health — quote: "moving threequarters of the Crown's gold from King’s Landing." He had urged Aegon II to put Rhaenyra's son Aegon to death — quote: "he had urged Aegon II to put her son Aegon to death as well." He never married nor fathered children and wore a veil over a disfigured face — quote: "The veil he wore to conceal his disfigured face." He served Rhaenyra's son well and faithfully as Hand — quote: "he served Rhaenyra’s son well and faithfully as Hand."

### Aegon III Targaryen
The boy king, still shy of his thirteenth nameday, took Tyland's hand as he died and displayed a maturity beyond his years — quote: "Aegon took his hand as he breathed his last." Acting without his regents, he named Robin Massey and Robert Darklyn to the Kingsguard, called Thaddeus Rowan to be his Hand, and wanted Baela and Alyn back at court — quote: "| would have Lord Rowan as my Hand." His appointments were all set aside by the regents on the grounds that no boy of twelve could judge such matters — quote: "no boy of twelve had the judgment to decide." Thereafter he took little part in rule, fixing his signature and seal to Peake's papers and retreating into silence — quote: "retreated back into silence and passivity." He hated Tessario, misliked his new Kingsguard and his master-at-arms, and cared for only one companion, Gaemon Palehair — quote: "only one companion he cared about."

### Unwin Peake
Lord Unwin Peake, a regent, set aside all the king's appointments and combined the offices of Hand and Protector of the Realm in himself, ruling the Seven Kingdoms in all but name for near two years — quote: "filled by none other than Unwin Peake himself." Like his father, who sided with the majority at the Great Council of 101, he did not believe a woman should rule; during the Dance he led a thousand swords for Aegon II as one of the fiercest greens — quote: "amongst the fiercest of the greens." Denied Ormund Hightower's command at Tumbleton, he stabbed the turncloak Owain Bourney and plotted the murders of Hugh Hammer and Ulf White, being foremost of the Caltrops — quote: "Foremost of the Caltrops (though this was not widely known)." He packed the court with his kin — his nephew Amaury and bastard brother Mervyn to the Kingsguard, his aunt Clarice over the queen's household, his uncle Gedmund over the fleet — and kept ten sellsword "Fingers" under Tessario — quote: "ten sellswords loyal only to him." He wielded the Valyrian steel sword OrphanMaker and decreed the mass punishments of the Feast of Our Father Above — quote: "OrphanMaker, last seen in the hands of Bold Jon Roxton." He set a trap for Alyn Velaryon by sending him against the ironmen — quote: "The trap was neatly set."

### Alyn Velaryon
Lord Alyn Velaryon, Lord of the Tides and grandson of Corlys, was young, barely a man grown, and bastard-born, yet wed to the king's half-sister Baela and master of all Velaryon wealth — quote: "wed to the king's half-sister, had all the power and wealth of House Velaryon." Instructed to bring the Velaryon fleets under Gedmund, he bristled but slipped his fleet away in the night and struck the Braavosi at Bloodstone, catching them feasting — quote: "The attack caught the Braavosi unawares." His flagship *Queen Rhaenys* rammed and sank the great dromond *Grand Defiance* — quote: "cutting the massive dromond almost in two." He lost three ships, including his cousin Daeron's *True Heart*, while sinking more than thirty, taking six galleys, eighty-nine hostages, and an elephant — quote: "sinking more than thirty and capturing six galleys." He returned to King's Landing a hero with the new name Oakenfist and was made master of ships, then maneuvered into a perilous voyage against the ironmen — quote: "the name that he would carry for the rest of his long life: Oakenfist."

### Septon Eustace
Septon Eustace attended Tyland at his death and recorded the court's affairs, giving a version in which Aegon received the regents' decrees courteously — quote: "the king received the decrees “courteously”." He was dismissed by the Hand and departed for Stoney Sept, his birthplace, to write *The Reign of King Viserys, First of His Name* — quote: "he devoted himself to the writing of his great... work." He described the king at the Feast of Our Father Above as if made of wax — quote: "The king had as well been made of wax."

### Orwyle
The former Grand Maester Orwyle, imprisoned awaiting execution, was drawn out by Aegon III to send ravens summoning Rowan, perhaps hoping for a royal pardon — quote: "Orwyle, mayhaps hopeful of a royal pardon." The regents returned him to his cell — quote: "Grand Maester Orwyle was returned to his cell, to await execution." A convicted traitor and Night's Watch deserter, he was granted death by the sword for his age, birth, and service — quote: "the honor of dying by the sword, in view of his age, high birth, and long service."

### Munkun
Grand Maester Munkun was down in the city tending Winter Fever victims and rushed back into the Red Keep once its gates were reopened — quote: "who came rushing back inside the Red Keep." He supported Peake in setting aside the king's arrangements, an acquiescence he would later rue — quote: "Munkun would later come to rue his acquiescence." In his *True Telling* he wrote that Alyn would soon dread the Hand's rewards — quote: "he would... soon be given good reason to dread the Hand's rewards."

### Marston Waters
Ser Marston Waters, second in command of the Kingsguard, was passed over by the king but confirmed Lord Commander by the regents, then made to bestow cloaks on Peake's kin — quote: "Marston Waters was confirmed as Lord Commander of the Kingsguard." He had been present at Rhaenyra's death, which the king had not forgotten — quote: "the presence of Ser Marston Waters at his mother's death." He dubbed Alyn a knight at his honoring ceremony — quote: "dubbed him a knight."

### Tessario
Tessario, a Volantene adventurer with tiger stripes tattooed across his face and back, captained Unwin's Fingers — quote: "had tiger stripes tattooed across his face and back." Called Tessario the Tiger to his face, he was named Tessario the Thumb behind his back by Mushroom — quote: "the mocking sobriquet that Mushroom had bestowed upon him." He slew Ser Robin Massey in a quarrel over a horse — quote: "the Volantene slew Ser Robin Massey."

### Gaemon Palehair
Gaemon Palehair, the king's six-year-old cupbearer and food taster and a bastard born of a whore, shared all the king's meals and attended him in the yard — quote: "his six-year-old cupbearer and food taster." At Gareth Long's request Peake made him the king's whipping boy, punished for the king's misbehavior — quote: "make the lad the king’s whipping boy." His blood and tears moved the king to improve where words had not — quote: "Gaemon's blood and Gaemon's tears reached the king." 

### Gareth Long
Ser Gareth Long, master-at-arms at Starpike, was given the same office at the Red Keep and tasked with training Aegon for knighthood — quote: "tasked with training King Aegon for knighthood." A skilled swordsman and harsh taskmaster, his punishments were unavailable against the king's inviolate person — quote: "renowned at Starpike for his harshness toward the boys he instructed."

### Gedmund Peake
Ser Gedmund Peake, Unwin's uncle known as Gedmund Great-Axe, was given command of the royal fleet despite little knowledge of ships — quote: "another uncle, Ser Gedmund Peake, a seasoned battler." So seasick on the voyage that sailors named him Gedmund Green-Sick, he insisted the fleet wait at Tarth for word, parting angrily with Alyn — quote: "the sailors had named him Gedmund Green-Sick." He wrongly judged Alyn had fled back to Driftmark — quote: "Run back to Driftmark, I’d venture."

### Manfryd Mooton
Lord Manfryd Mooton, a bedridden regent still recovering from the fever, asked that decisions wait for the absent regents but was overruled — quote: "Manfryd Mooton was bedridden, still recovering his strength." He was later sent to Braavos to treat with the Sealord and return the elephant — quote: "dispatched fellow regent Lord Manfryd Mooton to Braavos." He haggled peace terms while Follard and Cressey bribed the city's magnates — quote: "Whilst he haggled with Lord Mooton over terms of peace."

### Racallio Ryndoon
Racallio Ryndoon, a rogue whose ships were mostly swept from the sea, still ruled Bloodstone and lesser rocks and made common cause with the Sealord and the Archon — quote: "he still ruled Bloodstone, largest of the islands." His destruction was Peake's objective, but he emerged stronger than ever — quote: "Racallio appeared to have emerged stronger than ever."

### Baela Targaryen
Lady Baela, the king's half-sister and Alyn's wife, was wanted back at court by the king — quote: "He wanted Baela back at court as well." She saw Alyn off from Hull with a kiss and the news she was with child, jesting she would name a daughter Laena after her mother — quote: "I will name her Laena, after my mother." 

### Thaddeus Rowan
Lord Thaddeus Rowan was summoned by Aegon III to be his Hand, trusted because Tyland had offered him the king's sister in marriage — quote: "| would have Lord Rowan as my Hand." The regents instead offered him a seat among them as justiciar and master of laws — quote: "the office of justiciar and master of laws."

### Ned Bean
Ned Bean, a notorious sellsail called Blackbean for his thick black beard, was summoned to be Gedmund's second and advise on naval matters — quote: "the notorious sellsail Ned Bean." Alyn refused his authority, saying he would have hanged him but for wasting good rope on a bean — quote: "loath to waste good hempen rope on a bean."

### Daeron Velaryon
Daeron Velaryon, Alyn's cousin, captained the *True Heart* and perished when she sank in the Stepstones — quote: "captained by his cousin Daeron, who perished when she sank."

### Victor Risley
Ser Victor Risley, Knight of Risley Glade and the sole surviving Caltrop besides Unwin, was made King's Justice and personally beheaded the Pentoshi captain and false Shepherd and put Orwyle to the sword — quote: "The King’s Justice, Ser Victor Risley, attended to each of them himself."

### Septon Bernard
Septon Bernard, a younger man of Unwin's blood, replaced Eustace but preferred composing sacred music to recording court gossip — quote: "Septon Bernard preferred composing sacred music to setting down court gossip."

### Bryndemere
Lord Bryndemere the Evenstar brought a dozen longships off Tarth and word that the Sealord, the Archon, and Racallio had made common cause to rule the Stepstones jointly — quote: "commanded by Lord Bryndemere the Evenstar."

### Sealord of Braavos
The Sealord of Braavos boasted of replacing every ship Alyn had stolen or sunk, then, once placated, made peace and ceded the Stepstones though he did not hold them — quote: "We have already replaced every ship that your boy admiral stole or sank."

### Denys Harte
Ser Denys Harte supposedly engaged the Faceless Men to kill a rival back in King's Landing, according to Mushroom — quote: "engaged the services of the mysterious Faceless Men to kill a rival."
