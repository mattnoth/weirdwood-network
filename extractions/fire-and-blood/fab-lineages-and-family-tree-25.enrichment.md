## Node Prose
### Aegon I
The Conqueror, the Dragon; reigned 1–37 AC, the epoch by which the succession is dated. — quote: "1-37 AegonI"
Father of Aenys I by Rhaenys and of Maegor I by Visenya. — quote: "son of Aegon I and Visenya"

### Aenys I
Son of Aegon I and Rhaenys; reigned 37–42 and was succeeded by his brother Maegor I. — quote: "son of Aegon I and Rhaenys"
Father of Jaehaerys I. — quote: "the Conciliator; Aenys’s son"

### Maegor I
The Cruel; son of Aegon I and Visenya; reigned 42–48. — quote: "the Cruel, son of Aegon I and Visenya"

### Jaehaerys I
The Old King, the Conciliator; Aenys's son; reigned 48–103. — quote: "the Old King, the Conciliator; Aenys’s son"
Grandfather of Viserys I. — quote: "grandson of Jaehaerys"

### Viserys I
Grandson of Jaehaerys I; reigned 103–129; father of Aegon II. — quote: "grandson of Jaehaerys"

### Aegon II
Eldest son of Viserys I; reigned 129–131. His ascent was disputed by his half-sister Rhaenyra, ten years his elder, and both perished in the war between them that singers called the Dance of the Dragons. — quote: "eldest son of Viserys"; quote: "the war between them, called by singers the Dance of the Dragons"

### Rhaenyra
Half-sister of Aegon II, ten years his elder; she disputed his ascent and both perished in the Dance of the Dragons. Mother of Aegon III. — quote: "his half-sister Rhaenyra, ten years his elder"; quote: "the Dragonbane, Rhaenyra’s son"

### Aegon III
The Dragonbane, Rhaenyra's son; reigned 131–157. The last of the Targaryen dragons died during his reign. — quote: "the Dragonbane, Rhaenyra’s son"; quote: "The last of the Targaryen dragons died during the reign of Aegon III"

### Daeron I
The Young Dragon, the Boy King; eldest son of Aegon III; reigned 157–161. He conquered Dorne but was unable to hold it, and died young. — quote: "Daeron conquered Dorne, but was unable to hold it, and died young"

### Baelor I
The Beloved, the Blessed; septon and king; second son of Aegon III; reigned 161–171. He imprisoned his three sisters in the Maidenvault of the Red Keep. — quote: "septon and king, second son of Aegon III"; quote: "he imprisons his three sisters"; quote: "in the Maidenvault"

### Viserys II
Younger brother of Aegon III; reigned 171–172. — quote: "Viserys II younger brother of Aegon III"

### Aegon IV
The Unworthy; eldest son of Viserys II; reigned 172–184. He had nine mistresses. — quote: "the Unworthy, eldest son of Viserys"; quote: "Aegon the Unworthy had nine mistresses"

### Aemon the Dragonknight
Prince, younger brother of Aegon IV; champion to Queen Naerys. According to some, he was also her lover. — quote: "Prince Aemon the Dragonknight, was champion and some say lover to Queen Naerys"

### Naerys
Queen; mother of Daeron II. Daeron II was her son by either Aegon (IV) or Aemon. — quote: "Queen Naerys’s son, by Aegon or Aemon"

### Daeron II
The Good; Queen Naerys's son, fathered by Aegon or Aemon; reigned 184–209. He brought Dorne into the realm by wedding his sister to the Prince of Dorne. — quote: "the Good, Queen Naerys’s son, by Aegon or Aemon"; quote: "Daeron brought Dorne into the realm by wedding his sister to the Prince of Dorne"

### Aerys I
Second son of Daeron II; reigned 209–221 and left no issue. — quote: "second son of Daeron II (left no issue)"

### Maekar I
Fourth son of Daeron II; reigned 221–233; father of Aegon V. — quote: "Maekar I fourth son of Daeron II"

### Aegon V
The Unlikely; Maekar's fourth son; reigned 233–259. — quote: "Aegon V the Unlikely, Maekar’s fourth son"

### Jaehaerys II
Second son of Aegon V (the Unlikely); reigned 259–262; only son Aerys II. — quote: "Jaehaerys II second son of Aegon the Unlikely"

### Aerys II
The Mad King; only son of Jaehaerys II; reigned 262–283. He was dethroned and killed, ending the line of the dragon kings; his heir the crown prince Rhaegar was also slain. — quote: "the Mad King, only son of Jaehaerys II"; quote: "Aerys II was dethroned and killed"

### Rhaegar Targaryen
Crown prince and heir to Aerys II; slain by Robert Baratheon on the Trident. — quote: "the crown prince, Rhaegar Targaryen, slain by Robert Baratheon on the Trident"

### Mushroom
A dwarf of lowly birth, not wellborn like Tyrion; the fool at court during the events leading up to and through much of the Dance of the Dragons, and an observer. Semiliterate, he later dictated his story, giving the most scandalous account of the Dance. — quote: "Mushroom is a dwarf, but he’s not a wellborn one like Tyrion"; quote: "the fool at the court during the events leading up to"

### Archmaester Gyldayn
Archmaester of the Citadel; the in-universe author of Fire & Blood, who wrote its history from primary sources including Grand Maester Orwyle's court records, the septon of King's Landing, and Mushroom. — quote: "written by Archmaester Gyldayn of the Citadel"; quote: "Grand Maester Orwyle's official court records"

### Balerion the Black Dread
The biggest of the dragons; the only one who remembers Valyria. — quote: "Balerion the Black"; quote: "the only one who remembers Valyria"
