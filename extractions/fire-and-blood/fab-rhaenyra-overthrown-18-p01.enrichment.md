## Node Prose
### Rhaenyra Targaryen
Queen holding King's Landing, growing ever more isolated by betrayal — quote: "Queen Rhaenyra was finding herself ever more isolated with every new". By arresting Addam Velaryon she lost a dragon, a dragonrider, and her Queen's Hand, and more than half her army abandoned her — quote: "she had lost not only a dragon and a dragonrider, but her Queen's Hand as well". She imprisoned Corlys Velaryon under the Red Keep — quote: "Lord Corlys languished in a dungeon under the Red Keep". She named Ser Glendon Goode Lord Commander of the Queensguard and gave command of the gold cloaks to Ser Balon Byrch — quote: "named the young Ser Glendon Goode Lord Commander of the Queensguard". She forbade her son Joffrey to leave the castle, then lost Syrax and Joffrey together — quote: "once more, Her Grace forbade the prince to leave the castle". Beset on every side, she fled King's Landing through the Dragon Gate toward Duskendale — quote: "Rhaenyra and a small band of followers slipped out through the Dragon Gate".

### Helaena Targaryen
Sister, wife, and queen to King Aegon II and mother of his children, aged one-and-twenty — quote: "sister, wife, and queen to King Aegon II and mother of his children". She threw herself from a window in Maegor's Holdfast onto the iron spikes below — quote: "threw herself from her window in Maegor's Holdfast to die impaled upon the iron spikes". A street rumor held she had been murdered on Rhaenyra's order, which Gyldayn calls a calumny — quote: "Queen \nHelaena had been murdered, the whispers went". According to Mushroom she was with child; Munkun blames the horror of the knights' deaths; Septon Eustace says Mysaria told her of her son Maelor's death — quote: "Mushroom asserts that Helaena was with child". At her death her dragon Dreamfyre snapped two of her chains atop the Hill of Rhaenys — quote: "her dragon, Dreamfyre, rose suddenly with a roar that shook the Dragonpit".

### Joffrey Velaryon
Prince of Dragonstone and heir to the Iron Throne, ten-and-three, who donned squire's armor and begged to fight — quote: "Prince Joffrey, ten-and-three, donned squire's armor". Forbidden to leave, he stole onto Syrax without saddle or whip, meaning to reach his own dragon Tyraxes — quote: "the prince had vaulted onto Syrax without benefit of saddle or whip". Syrax, wanting no other rider, twisted beneath him and he plunged two hundred feet to his death in Flea Bottom — quote: "Prince Joffrey slid from the dragon's back and plunged to the earth". He is named the last of Rhaenyra's sons by Laenor Velaryon, or her bastard by Ser Harwin Strong, depending which truth one believes — quote: "the last of Queen Rhaenyra's sons by Laenor Velaryon...or the last of her bastards by Ser Harwin Strong".

### Aegon the Younger
Rhaenyra's son, ever at his mother's side and seldom speaking a word — quote: "Aegon the Younger was ever at his mother's side, yet seldom spoke a word". After Joffrey's death he was her last living son, whom she clutched fiercely — quote: "Rhaenyra herself wrapped her arms about her last living son, Aegon the Younger". He fled the city with her — quote: "her last surviving son, Aegon the Younger".

### Luthor Largent
Ser; commander of the gold cloaks, named in rumor as Helaena's killer, though he was demonstrably eating with three hundred gold cloaks at the time — quote: "Ser Luthor Largent, the purported killer, was eating with three hundred of his gold cloaks". He led the City Watch against the Shepherd's mob at Cobbler's Square — quote: "At their head rode Ser Luthor Largent upon an armored warhorse". His men were swept under and he was pulled from his saddle and bludgeoned to death with a cobblestone — quote: "bludgeoned to death with a cobblestone, his helm and head so crushed".

### the Shepherd
One-handed prophet who preached the coming of the Stranger at Cobbler's Square — quote: "the one-handed prophet shrieked his curses at". He directed the mob to storm the Dragonpit and slay the dragons — quote: "first must you bathe in dragon's blood". Though he held sway over tens of thousands he never claimed kingship, styling himself a simple son of the Seven — quote: "the Shepherd never claimed kingship, styling himself a simple son of the Seven". A popular tale named him the slayer of Syrax — quote: "the tale most oft heard in King's Landing named the Shepherd himself as the dragonslayer".

### Perkin the Flea
A hedge knight who crowned his own squire Trystane, declaring him a natural son of King Viserys — quote: "a hedge knight named Ser Perkin the Flea crowned his own squire Trystane". He knighted any sellsword and thief who flocked to Trystane's banner — quote: "Ser Perkin began dubbing every sellsword, thief, and butcher's boy". He took the Red Keep when Garth yielded, beheaded Garth and twenty knights, and freed the prisoners — quote: "The Flea also freed all the prisoners found in the dungeons". He recruited gold cloaks and took four of the city's seven gates for Trystane — quote: "giving him four of the city's seven gates".

### Trystane
Ser Perkin's squire, a stripling of sixteen, crowned king and declared a natural son of the late King Viserys — quote: "a natural son of the late King Viserys". He later styled himself King Trystane Truefyre — quote: "the power of King Trystane Truefyre (as he now styled himself)". As Perkin's catspaw he sat the Iron Throne, repealed Rhaenyra's taxes, cancelled debts, and raised gutter knights to nobility — quote: "Ser Perkin's catspaw Trystane sat the Iron Throne".

### Gaemon
A pale-haired boy of four raised up as king by whores in a brothel, supposedly a bastard of the missing King Aegon II — quote: "a palehaired boy of four named Gaemon, supposedly a bastard of the missing King Aegon II". Known as "King Cunny," his court at the House of Kisses on Visenya's Hill issued outlandish decrees — quote: "“King Cunny” in the vulgar parlance of the city". His edicts were reputedly the work of a Dornish whore, Sylvenna Sand — quote: "These edicts were almost certainly the work of a Dornish whore named Sylvenna Sand".

### Wat
A tanner who announced Lord Celtigar's failure to pay his "cock tax" and led a mob — quote: "a tanner called Wat announced that his lordship had failed to pay his “cock tax". He rode a white horse brandishing Celtigar's severed head and genitals, declaring an end to taxes — quote: "brandishing Lord Celtigar's severed head and bloody genitals and declaring an end to all taxes". He led his mob against the Gate of the Gods and out into the countryside — quote: "Wat the Tanner led his own mob of howling ruffians against the Gate of the Gods".

### Mysaria
The White Worm, Lady Misery, mistress of whisperers, who received the betrayal of the rescue plot — quote: "Their plans were betrayed to Lady Misery by a whore". She warned Rhaenyra against the coming dark — quote: "Lady Mysaria warned her against the coming dark". Taken while fleeing, the White Worm of Lys was whipped naked through the city until she died on the cobblestones — quote: "the White Worm was whipped naked through the city".

### Larys Strong
The Clubfoot, Lord of Harrenhal and King Aegon's master of whisperers, who had never truly left King's Landing — quote: "the Clubfoot had never left King's Landing". According to Mushroom he originated the slander that Rhaenyra murdered Helaena — quote: "A slander so vile could only have been the work of Larys Strong". He emerged intact and took a place of honor beside Perkin's new king — quote: "he came striding through the halls of the Red Keep as if he had never left them".

### Alicent Hightower
Dowager Queen of House Hightower and Helaena's mother, who cursed her rival on learning of her daughter's death — quote: "she rent her garments and pronounced a dire curse upon her rival". Gyldayn reasons that had Rhaenyra meant murder, Alicent would have been the one flung down — quote: "surely it would have been the Dowager Queen Alicent flung down onto the spikes". She was on hand when Trystane mounted the throne — quote: "So too was the Queen Dowager, Alicent of House Hightower".

### Corlys Velaryon
The Sea Snake, imprisoned in a dungeon under the Red Keep, whose captivity drove Velaryon men from Rhaenyra's cause — quote: "the Sea Snake's sworn swords". Two of his sworn swords tried and failed to free him — quote: "cut their way into the dungeons to free their lord". He was freed from the dungeons by Ser Perkin — quote: "amongst them Grand Maester Orwyle and the Sea Snake, Lord Corlys Velaryon".

### Glendon Goode
Ser, aged twenty and a Queensguard for less than a moon, named Lord Commander after distinguishing himself in Flea Bottom and recovering Ser Lorent's body — quote: "named the young Ser Glendon Goode Lord Commander of the Queensguard". One of the Seven Who Rode, he was set afire, thrown, and torn to pieces by the mob — quote: "who set his long white cloak afire". He had been Lord Commander for less than a day — quote: "Lord Commander of the Queensguard for less than a day".

### Bartimos Celtigar
Lord and the queen's master of coin, who proposed a tax on bastards — quote: "proposed a punishing new tax on any child born out of wedlock". His manse was overrun; he was bound to a post and tortured until he revealed his hidden wealth — quote: "bound to a post and tortured until he revealed where all his wealth was hidden". Wat the Tanner claimed his manhood as forfeit for the "cock tax" — quote: "must \nyield his manhood to the Crown as forfeit".

### Arthor Celtigar
A boy of fifteen who made a brave stand in a doorway of the Celtigar manse — quote: "Arthor Celtigar, a boy of fifteen, made a brave stand in a doorway". A treacherous serving girl let the rioters in, and he was slain by a spear through the back — quote: "The brave lad was slain by a spear thrust through the back".

### Lorent Marbrand

### Medrick Manderly

He fled the city with the queen — quote: "With her rode the brothers Manderly".

### Torrhen Manderly

### Garth the Harelip

He was dragged before Perkin and beheaded — quote: "Garth the Harelip was dragged before him and beheaded".

### Balon Byrch

### Tyland Lannister

### Hobb the Hewer
A woodsman who leapt onto Shrykos's neck and drove his axe into the beast's skull, striking seven blows for the Seven — quote: "slain by a woodsman known as Hobb the Hewer". ### the Seven Who Rode
Seven champions who rode into the city after Prince Joffrey: Ser Medrick Manderly, Ser Loreth Lansdale, Ser Harrold Darke, Ser Harmon of the Reeds, Ser Gyles Yronwood, Ser Willam Royce, and Ser Glendon Goode — quote: "The Seven Who Rode were Ser Medrick Manderly, the heir to White Harbor". Three of the seven fell in the battle for Joffrey's body — quote: "three of the seven fell in the fighting".

### Dreamfyre
Helaena's dragon, atop the Hill of Rhaenys, who snapped two chains at the queen's death — quote: "snapping two of the chains that bound her". The last of the four pit dragons to die, she slew more than the other three combined before bringing down the Dragonpit's dome upon herself — quote: "Dreamfyre slew more than all three of them combined". A crossbow bolt in her eye drove her into the dome, which collapsed and crushed her — quote: "the dome cracked under the force of impact".

### Syrax
Queen Rhaenyra's yellow she-dragon, who had never known another rider — quote: "Syrax was the queen's dragon. She had never known another rider". She twisted beneath Joffrey and cast him off to his death — quote: "Syrax twisted beneath him, fighting to be free of this unfamiliar rider". Riderless, she descended on the Shepherd's mobs at the Dragonpit and was slain by conflicting accounts — quote: "Many a conflicting tale is told of the death of the queen's dragon".

### Tyraxes
Prince Joffrey's dragon, housed in the Dragonpit, who retreated into his lair roasting his attackers — quote: "Prince Joffrey's Tyraxes retreated back into his lair". His chains fouled and he was slain; his wings were cut into strips for dragonskin cloaks — quote: "sliced the membranes from his wings and tore them into ragged \nstrips to fashion dragonskin cloaks".

### Shrykos
A pit dragon, the first to succumb, slain by Hobb the Hewer's axe if Eustace is believed — quote: "Shrykos was the first dragon to succumb".

### Morghul
A pit dragon slain by the Burning Knight, who thrust a spear into the beast's eye even as its flame devoured him — quote: "Morghul, it is written, was slain by the Burning Knight".

### Denys Woodwright
Ser, one of the Sea Snake's sworn swords who cut into the dungeons to free Corlys and was hanged at dawn — quote: "Ser Denys Woodwright and Ser Thoron True, cut their way into the dungeons".

### Thoron True
Ser, sworn sword of the Sea Snake, whose plot was betrayed by a whore he had been bedding — quote: "betrayed to Lady Misery by a whore Ser Thoron had been bedding".

### Bean

### Sylvenna Sand
A Dornish whore, reputed author of Gaemon's edicts and paramour of the king's mother Essie, if Mushroom is believed — quote: "a Dornish whore named Sylvenna Sand, reputedly the paramour".

### Addam Velaryon

### the Burning Knight
