## Node Prose

### Jaehaerys Targaryen
Jaehaerys returned in triumph from Oldtown to King's Landing and pardoned the Poor Fellows on condition they take the black — quote: "This Jaehaerys granted them, on the condition that they agreed to journey north". Though a boy in his regency, he sat most councils and let his voice be heard, and he personally received sixscore lords in audience in his solar — quote: "he encouraged each man to speak freely and share his views". He selected councillors on a principle of conciliation, rewarding both loyalists and Maegor's former men — quote: "Conciliation remained their guiding principle". On learning of the plan to wed Alysanne to Orryn, he flew with her to Dragonstone and wed her secretly there — quote: "Jaehaerys Targaryen, the First of His Name, took to wife his sister Alysanne". He faced down Septon Mattheus and Lord Rogar at the castle gates and refused to unmake the marriage — quote: "neither gods nor men shall part us". Dragonstone became his residence for the rest of his minority, until his sixteenth nameday — quote: "Dragonstone would remain their refuge and their residence".

### Alysanne Targaryen
Alysanne, thirteen and newly flowered in 49 AC, was the youngest child of Aenys and Alyssa, once called "the little maid" and "the other daughter" — quote: "the youngest child of King Aenys and Queen Alyssa". Blue-eyed with honey-colored curls, she was pretty but seldom called beautiful, and no man questioned her wits — quote: "No man ever questioned her wits". She played hostess to the highborn women during the Golden Wedding festivities to great acclaim — quote: "the young princess rose to the challenge brilliantly". When told she was to wed Orryn Baratheon, she went straight to Jaehaerys, flew to Dragonstone, and wed him — quote: "Instead she went directly to Jaehaerys". She vowed she would always return to him — quote: "Silverwing will always bring me back to Jaehaerys".

### Alyssa Velaryon
Alyssa, the Queen Regent and Dowager Queen, was daughter of Aethan Velaryon and Alarra Massey, forty-two years old and thought past childbearing — quote: "The Queen Regent Alyssa was forty-two years old". She wed Prince Aenys in 22 AC and bore him five healthy children, becoming queen when he took the crown in 37 AC — quote: "she married Prince Aenys Targaryen, the unquestioned heir". She saw her husband's reign fall to ruin, two sons and a marriage forced upon her daughter under Maegor — quote: "the man who had brought about the death of her sons". She wed Lord Rogar Baratheon in the Golden Wedding — quote: "Lord Rogar and Queen Alyssa were to marry". She opposed marrying Jaehaerys to his sister, fearing the reaction of the pious — quote: "persuaded Queen Alyssa to think elsewise". At Dragonstone she halted the standoff and accepted the marriage — quote: "What is done is done, and now we all must needs live with it".

### Rogar Baratheon
Rogar Baratheon, Lord of Storm's End and Hand of the King, was blue-eyed, black-bearded, and eldest of five brothers descended from Orys One-Hand — quote: "Lord Rogar was the eldest of five brothers". No swordsman, he favored a double-bladed axe he boasted could cleave a dragon's skull — quote: "large and heavy enough to cleave through a dragon's skull". He sheltered Alyssa and her children and was first to proclaim Jaehaerys king, becoming kingmaker rather than kingslayer — quote: "he was the first to proclaim Prince Jaehaerys king". He wed the Queen Regent in the Golden Wedding and pushed a Tyroshi match for the king — quote: "the notion of forging an alliance with Tyrosh". At Dragonstone he demanded to know if the marriage was consummated and ordered the children separated, but was thwarted — quote: "Separate these children, gently if you please". Afterward he kept the marriage secret, still meaning to part them — quote: "remained determined to find a way to part Jaehaerys and Alysanne".

### Rhaena Targaryen
Rhaena, twice widowed at twenty-six, wed Androw Farman on Fair Isle in a small secret ceremony, her third marriage — quote: "Rhaena Targaryen wed Androw Farman". King and court knew nothing until a raven came from the Rock — quote: "King and court remained entirely ignorant of the marriage". Her mother Alyssa was deeply offended by her exclusion, and relations cooled — quote: "Queen Alyssa was deeply offended by her exclusion". According to whispers about court, Rhaena was a sorceress who used dark arts to murder Maegor — quote: "Rhaena was a sorceress, who had used the dark arts to murder Maegor". She chose not to attend the Golden Wedding, remaining on Fair Isle — quote: "had chosen not to attend the nuptials". Her daughters Aerea and Rhaella remained the king's heirs while he was unwed — quote: "the daughters of his sister Rhaena would remain his heirs".

### Androw Farman
Androw Farman, second son of Marq Farman, was ten-and-seven, comely and amiable, and said to be besotted with Rhaena — quote: "utterly besotted with his new wife". Many thought a second son of a minor lord far from worthy of a twice-crowned queen — quote: "far from worthy of the hand of a woman who had been twice a queen".

### Daemon Velaryon
Daemon Velaryon, Lord of the Tides and Alyssa's brother, was among the first great lords to abandon Maegor and was made lord admiral and master of ships — quote: "one of the first great lords to abandon Maegor the Cruel". A prideful man, he suggested Jaehaerys wed the widowed Queen Elinor Costayne — quote: "suggested that Jaehaerys might marry the widowed Queen Elinor". Lord Rogar suspected him of leaking the betrothal plan to the princess — quote: "Lord Rogar himself was suspicious of Daemon Velaryon".

### Edwell Celtigar
Edwell Celtigar, Lord of Claw Isle, had been Hand of the King under Maegor and was recalled to serve as lord treasurer and master of coin — quote: "who had been Hand of the King under Maegor". He had two unwed daughters and offered them to Jaehaerys as he once had to Maegor — quote: "now he offered the same girls again for Jaehaerys". Rogar dismissed the girls scornfully — quote: "They have no chins, no teats, and no sense".

### Prentys Tully
Prentys Tully, Lord of Riverrun, was summoned to court as master of laws and royal justiciar — quote: "to serve as master of laws". He nominated a younger sister of his wife Lucinda as a bride for the king — quote: "nominated a younger sister of his own wife, Lucinda".

### Grand Maester Benifer
Benifer chronicled the reign and praised Jaehaerys's reconciliation with the Faith — quote: "King Jaehaerys had reconciled the Iron Throne to the Faith". His letters give the fullest account of the confrontation at Dragonstone, which he witnessed — quote: "whose letters give us the most complete accounting". On Rogar's order he burned Mattheus's letter rather than send word to Oldtown — quote: "Grand Maester Benifer burned his letter rather than dispatch a raven".

### Septon Mattheus
Septon Mattheus of the Most Devout, a great corpulent man claiming descent from the Gardener kings, was reckoned a near certainty to be the next High Septon — quote: "claimed descent from the Gardener kings of old". He warned the queen against a brother-sister match, invoking "other Moons" — quote: "there are other Moons out there". At Dragonstone he raved that the incest would plunge Westeros into war until the king threatened to have his lips sewn shut — quote: "prophesized that this abomination would once more plunge all of Westeros into war".

### Oswyck
Oswyck, an old septon of Dragonstone, had known Jaehaerys and Alysanne since birth and instructed them in the Seven — quote: "who had known Jaehaerys and Alysanne since their births". Familiar with Targaryen sibling marriage, he assented at once and performed the rites — quote: "he assented at once".

### Samgood of Sour Hill
Samgood of Sour Hill, called Sour Sam, was a scarred one-eyed hedge knight of sixty-three, the eldest champion, who fought with the quickness of a younger man — quote: "a scarred and battered man of three-and-sixty". At Dragonstone he answered Rogar that the Kingsguard served the king, not the Hand — quote: "we're the Kingsguard, not the Hand's guard".

### Pate the Woodcock
Pate the Woodcock, the youngest champion, fought with a spear and was dubbed a knight by Ser Joffrey Doggett — quote: "Ser Joffrey Doggett settled the matter by dubbing the lad himself". At Dragonstone he warned Rogar he would be first to die — quote: "die, m'lord, you have my word".

### Gyles Morrigen
Ser Gyles Morrigen, the king’s champion and sworn shield, was among the first Kingsguard named and announced the king's open audiences — quote: "the king’s champion and sworn shield". At Dragonstone he warned any man who touched the king and queen would die — quote: "Any man who lays a hand upon our king and queen".

### Jonquil Darke
The mystery knight known as the Serpent in Scarlet was unmasked as a woman, Jonquil Darke, a bastard daughter of the Lord of Duskendale — quote: "a woman, Jonquil Darke, a bastard daughter of the Lord of Duskendale".

### Orys One-Hand
Orys One-Hand was the first Baratheon Lord of Storm's End, a bastard brother and trusted commander of Aegon the Conqueror — quote: "Orys had been a bastard brother to Aegon the Conqueror". He slew Argilac the Arrogant and wed Argilac's daughter — quote: "After slaying Argilac the Arrogant, last of the Durrandon".

### Orryn Baratheon
Orryn Baratheon, the youngest of Lord Rogar's brothers, was chosen by the council to wed Princess Alysanne — quote: "to Orryn Baratheon, the youngest of Lord Rogar's brothers".

### Elinor Costayne
Queen Elinor of House Costayne, one of Maegor's Black Brides with three sons and proven fertility, was proposed as a bride for Jaehaerys — quote: "the widowed Queen Elinor, of House Costayne".

### Marq Farman
Marq Farman, Lord of Fair Isle, presided over his son Androw's wedding to Rhaena, conducted by his own septon — quote: "presided over by the groom's father, Marq Farman".

### Brandon Stark
Brandon Stark, the ailing Lord of Winterfell, made the journey south with his sons Walton and Alaric and gave the highest praise of the king — quote: "| see his grandsire in him".

### Septon Barth
Septon Barth, writing years later, held that Jaehaerys privately opposed his mother's marriage to Rogar but held his tongue — quote: "Septon Barth tells us that Jaehaerys was opposed to the marriage". Barth esteemed Alysanne and served her husband long — quote: "esteemed her even more than her husband, whom he served for so long".
