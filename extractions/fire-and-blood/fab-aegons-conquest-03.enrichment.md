## Node Prose
### Aegon I Targaryen
Aegon was the only son and second child of Aerion, Lord of Dragonstone, and Lady Valaena of House Velaryon, born on Dragonstone in 27 BC — quote: "was born on Dragonstone in 27 BC". He took both his trueborn sisters, Visenya and Rhaenys, to wife — quote: "Aegon took both his sisters to bride". Some said he wed Visenya out of duty and Rhaenys out of desire — quote: "Aegon wed Visenya out of duty and Rhaenys". He bore the Valyrian steel blade Blackfyre and rode Balerion the Black Dread, yet took no pleasure in feats of arms and flew only to battle or travel — quote: "Armed with the Valyrian steel blade Blackfyre". He dealt harshly with rebels but was open-handed with foes who bent the knee — quote: "he was open-handed with former foes". He was crowned first at the Aegonfort and again at the Starry Sept of Oldtown, where the High Septon proclaimed him King of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men — quote: "King of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men".

### Visenya Targaryen
Visenya, eldest of the three siblings, was as much a warrior as Aegon, carrying the Valyrian longsword Dark Sister — quote: "She carried the Valyrian longsword Dark Sister". Some said she played with poisons and dabbled in dark sorceries — quote: "she played with poisons and dabbled in dark sorceries". She rode Vhagar, burned Stokeworth into submission, later bought the Vale bloodlessly by charming Ronnel Arryn, and took an arrow in the shoulder at the Field of Fire — quote: "Queen Visenya took an arrow in one shoulder".

### Rhaenys Targaryen
Rhaenys, the youngest, was playful and curious, loving music, dancing, and poetry, and no true warrior — quote: "No true warrior, Rhaenys loved music, dancing, and poetry". She rode Meraxes and loved to fly above all things — quote: "astride Meraxes of the golden eyes and silver scales". It was whispered she entertained young men in her bedchambers, yet the king spent ten nights with her for every one with Visenya — quote: "even entertained some in her bedchambers". She won the stormlands from the sky, then flew into Dorne and warned Meria Martell they would return with fire and blood — quote: "the next time we shall come with fire and blood".

### Orys Baratheon
Orys was Aegon's childhood friend and champion, and whispered to be his baseborn halfbrother — quote: "Orys Baratheon was a baseborn halfbrother to Lord Aegon, it was whispered". Aegon proclaimed him "my shield, my stalwart, my strong right hand," and the maesters reckon him the first King's Hand — quote: "the first King's Hand". He crushed Darklyn and Mooton, then slew Argilac in single combat at the Last Storm — quote: "died with a sword in his hand and a curse on his lips". Afterward he unchained Argilac's daughter Argella, took the Durrandon arms and words, and made her his wife — quote: "Lady Argella his wife".

### Argilac Durrandon
Argilac the Arrogant was the last Storm King of House Durrandon, whose only heir was his maiden daughter — quote: "whose only heir was his maiden daughter". In his youth he slew Garse VII Gardener at the Battle of Summerfield — quote: "slaying Garse VII Gardener, King of the Reach, in the Battle of Summerfield". He offered his daughter and dower lands to Aegon, then had the hands of Aegon's envoy cut off and returned in a box — quote: "Argilac had the hands of Aegon's envoy cut off and returned to him in a box". Refusing to burn in his castle, he rode out to the Last Storm and died sword in hand — quote: "died with a sword in his hand and a curse on his lips".

### Harren Hoare
Black Harren of House Hoare was King of the Isles and the Rivers, most feared for his cruelty — quote: "No king in Westeros was more feared than Black Harren". He spent close on forty years building Harrenhal beside the Gods Eye, beggaring the riverlands — quote: "devoted most of his long reign, close on forty years, to building a gigantic castle". Trusting his walls of stone, he defied Aegon's parley and boasted "Stone does not burn" — quote: "| built in stone,". He and his last sons burned in Harrenhal, and House Hoare died with him — quote: "Harren and his last sons died in the fires that engulfed his monstrous fortress".

### Torrhen Stark
Torrhen Stark, King in the North of Winterfell, crossed the Neck with thirty thousand northmen — quote: "leading an army of savage northmen thirty thousand strong". Facing three dragons and a larger host at the Trident, he sent his bastard brother Brandon Snow across to treat, then knelt himself — quote: "laid the ancient crown of the Kings of Winter at Aegon's feet". He rose as Lord of Winterfell and Warden of the North, remembered ever after as the King Who Knelt — quote: "Torrhen Stark is remembered as the King Who Knelt".

### Brandon Snow
Brandon Snow, Torrhen's bastard brother, offered to cross the Trident alone to slay the dragons as they slept — quote: "The king's bastard brother Brandon Snow offered to cross the Trident". Instead Torrhen sent him with three maesters to treat, not to kill — quote: "he crossed with three maesters by his side, not to kill but to treat".

### Meria Martell
Meria Martell, Princess of Dorne, was eighty years old and had ruled the Dornishmen for sixty years — quote: "had ruled the Dornishmen for sixty of those years". Argilac had named her "the Yellow Toad of Dorne," but her wits were undulled — quote: "Argilac the Arrogant had named her "the Yellow Toad of Dorne"". She refused to fight or kneel to Rhaenys, declaring Dorne had no king — quote: "nor will | kneel to you. Dorne has no king." — quote: "Ours are Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken".

### Edmyn Tully
Edmyn Tully, Lord of Riverrun, led the riverlands in revolt against Harren — quote: "led by Lord Edmyn Tully of Riverrun". Summoned to defend Harrenhal, he declared for Aegon instead and raised the dragon banner — quote: "Tully declared for House Targaryen instead". Aegon named him Lord Paramount of the Trident — quote: "named him Lord Paramount of the Trident".

### Loren I Lannister
Loren, King of the Rock, joined Mern of the Reach to field the mightiest host ever seen in Westeros — quote: "the two great western kings had made common cause". He survived the Field of Fire by riding through the flames — quote: "riding through a wall of flame and smoke to safety". Captured the next day, he bent the knee and was confirmed as Lord of Casterly Rock and Warden of the West — quote: "naming him Lord of Casterly Rock and Warden of the West".

### Mern IX Gardener
Mern IX of House Gardener, King of the Reach, marched from Highgarden and demanded the honor of the center at the Field of Fire — quote: "From Highgarden marched Mern IX of House Gardener, King of the Reach". He led the charge on his golden stallion and was slain with all his kin — quote: "King Mern IX was amongst the dead". His last surviving nephew died three days later, and House Gardener died with him — quote: "When he died of his burns, House Gardener died with him".

### Sharra Arryn
Queen Sharra ruled the Vale as regent for her son Ronnel and had been lauded as "the Flower of the Mountain" — quote: "lauded as "the Flower of the Mountain"". She sent Aegon a portrait and offered herself in marriage if he named Ronnel his heir — quote: "offered herself to him in marriage, provided he named her son Ronnel as his heir". Her defenses proved useless when Visenya landed Vhagar in the Eyrie's courtyard — quote: "landed in the Eyrie's inner courtyard".

### Ronnel Arryn
Ronnel Arryn was the boy king of the Eyrie, son of Sharra — quote: "the boy king of the Eyrie, Ronnel Arryn". He was found on Visenya's knee begging to fly, and after three circuits of the Giant's Lance he landed a lord — quote: "Mother, can | go flying with the lady?".

### Argella Durrandon
Argella, Argilac's daughter, barred the gates of Storm's End and declared herself the Storm Queen — quote: "declared herself the Storm Queen". Her garrison delivered her gagged, chained, and naked to Orys, who unchained her and made her his wife — quote: "delivered Lady Argella gagged, chained, and naked to the camp of Orys Baratheon".

### Harlan Tyrell
Harlan Tyrell was the steward of Highgarden whose forebears had served the Gardeners for centuries — quote: "in the hands of its steward, Harlan Tyrell". He yielded the castle without a fight and was made Warden of the South and Lord Paramount of the Mander — quote: "naming him Warden of the South and Lord Paramount of the Mander".

### Manfred Hightower
Manfred Hightower, Lord of Oldtown, was cautious and godly — quote: "Manfred Hightower, Lord of Oldtown, was a cautious lord and godly". Warned by the High Septon's vision, he opened Oldtown's gates and offered his sword, city, and oath — quote: "to offer up his sword, his city, and his oath". Some say he also offered his youngest daughter's hand, which Aegon declined — quote: "Some say that Lord Hightower also offered up the hand of his youngest daughter".

### Daemon Velaryon
Daemon Velaryon, Lord of the Tides, was made master of ships in command of the royal fleet — quote: "Daemon Velaryon, Lord of the Tides, was made master of ships". As Aegon's admiral he was slain when the Arryn fleet defeated the Targaryens off Gulltown — quote: "Amongst the dead was Aegon's admiral, Daemon Velaryon".

### Aenar Targaryen
Aenar the Exile sold his holdings in the Freehold and moved his House to Dragonstone twelve years before the Doom — quote: "Aenar Targaryen sold his holdings in the Freehold". His flight, prompted by his daughter Daenys's dream, left the Targaryens the only surviving dragonlords — quote: "the Targaryens were the only dragonlords to survive".

### Daenys
Daenys the Dreamer, Aenar's maiden daughter, foresaw the destruction of Valyria by fire — quote: "Daenys, known forever afterward as Daenys the Dreamer, had foreseen the destruction". She was sister and wife to Gaemon the Glorious — quote: "Gaemon Targaryen, brother and husband to Daenys the Dreamer".

### Gaemon Targaryen
Gaemon the Glorious followed Aenar as Lord of Dragonstone and wed his sister Daenys — quote: "followed Aenar the Exile as Lord of Dragonstone". His son Aegon and daughter Elaena ruled together after his death — quote: "Gaemon's son Aegon and his daughter Elaena ruled together after his death".

### High Septon
The High Septon, Father of the Faithful, locked himself in the Starry Sept for seven days seeking guidance — quote: "the High Septon had locked himself within the Starry Sept for seven days". The Crone showed him that resisting Aegon would see Oldtown burn, and he later anointed Aegon with the seven oils — quote: "His High Holiness himself anointed Aegon with the seven oils".

### Balerion
Balerion the Black Dread was the sole survivor of the five dragons Aenar brought from Valyria and served as Aegon's mount — quote: "only one survived to Aegon's day: the great beast called Balerion". His black fire, shot through with red, melted the towers of Harrenhal — quote: "the dragon roared his fury and bathed them in black fire".

### Garse VII Gardener
Garse VII Gardener, King of the Reach, was slain by Argilac at the Battle of Summerfield — quote: "slaying Garse VII Gardener, King of the Reach, in the Battle of Summerfield".
