## Node Prose

### Aegon III
A solemn boy of ten at his coronation, crowned atop Visenya's Hill with a simple circlet of yellow gold and proclaimed Aegon the Third — quote: "proclaimed Aegon of House Targaryen, the Third of His Name". He was handsome, lean, silver-haired and purple-eyed, but joyless — quote: "the new king was undeniably handsome, lean of face and form". Munkun judged him broken and hollow, roused to rage only by mention of dragons — quote: "the very mention of which sends him into a rare rage". His only friend was the bastard Gaemon Palehair — quote: "He has no friends save for the bastard boy Gaemon Palehair". During the Winter Fever he visited the sick, and survivors credited his "healing hands" — quote: "attribute their survival to the touch of the king's 'healing hands.'"

### Tyland Lannister
Ser Tyland, recently returned from Myr, was named Hand of the King — quote: "Ser Tyland Lannister, recently returned from Myr, was made Hand of the King". Blinded and disfigured by Queen Rhaenyra's torturers, he wore a silken hood, earning the name the Hooded Hand and smallfolk tales of a masked sorcerer — quote: "began to whisper tales of the malign masked sorcerer in the Red Keep". He claimed to have forgotten who was black or green and served the son of the queen who had tortured him — quote: "a curious failure of memory". He came to dominate Leowyn Corbray and gathered power as the regents met less often — quote: "gathered more and more power to himself". He confined the Dowager Queen, abolished Rhaenyra's taxes, set aside a million dragons in loans, and funded granaries and war galleys — quote: "he abolished the taxes enacted by Queen Rhaenyra and Lord Celtigar". He died at the end, after the fever had passed, his last words a complaint of cold — quote: "It is very cold in here".

### Baela Targaryen
Baela, Prince Daemon's daughter by Laena Velaryon and King Aegon's half-sister, was sixteen, wild and willful, keeping her hair cropped short as a boy's — quote: "as wild and willful a young woman as any in the realm". Her dragon had died on Dragonstone — quote: "her dragon died". She took part in horse races, moonlight swims, and gathered unsuitable companions — quote: "Baela had a taste for unsuitable companions". Refusing the match to Lord Rowan, she fled the castle in a washerwoman's clothes and married her cousin Alyn Velaryon in the sept on Dragonstone — quote: "Alyn Velaryon and Baela Targaryen were married in the sept on Dragonstone". According to Baela's own provocation, she had bedded two of Rowan's sons, though Gyldayn notes the truth of it cannot be said — quote: "I've bedded two of his sons".

### Rhaena Targaryen
Rhaena, Baela's twin and Aegon's half-sister, was tractable where her sister was willful, delighting in court life — quote: "as tractable as her sister had been willful". She had been Lady Jeyne Arryn's ward in the Vale — quote: "Rhaena had enjoyed a life of comfort and privilege as Lady Jeyne's ward". She kept a young dragon, Morning, coiled about her shoulders — quote: "Morning, her young dragon". She confessed a fondness for Ser Corwyn Corbray, and the match was made — quote: "'especially fond' of Ser Corwyn Corbray".

### Corlys Velaryon
The Sea Snake, Lord of the Tides, sat on the council of regents as first among equals and the only man young Aegon trusted — quote: "the only man the young king was willing to trust". On the sixth day of the third moon of 132 AC he collapsed on the serpentine steps and died at seventy-nine — quote: "the Sea Snake was dead". His remains were carried to Driftmark aboard the Mermaid's Kiss and buried at sea aboard the refloated hull of the Sea Snake — quote: "Corlys Velaryon was buried at sea aboard the very ship". It was said the Cannibal swept over the burial, though Gyldayn calls this most likely a later embroidery — quote: "most likely a later embroidery".

### Alicent Hightower
The Dowager Queen, second wife of Viserys I and mother of Aegon, Aemond, Daeron, and Helaena, refused to reconcile with the new king — quote: "who refused to reconcile herself to the new king". She raged, once telling little Jaehaera to cut her sleeping husband's throat — quote: "she had told Jaehaera to cut her husband's throat". Tyland confined her to Maegor's Holdfast — quote: "confine the Queen Dowager to her own apartments in Maegor's Holdfast". She grew averse to the color green and, more lucid at the last, spoke of her children and of the Old King Jaehaerys — quote: "come to have a deep aversion to the color green". She died of Winter Fever the same night as Lord Westerling — quote: "died on the same night as Lord Westerling".

### Alyn Velaryon
Baseborn Alyn of Hull, son of Marilda ("Mouse"), was the Sea Snake's chosen heir; his succession was contested by Velaryon nephews — quote: "the Sea Snake's chosen heir, but his succession was not uncontested". Installed as Lord of the Tides and Master of Driftmark, he rode to King's Landing to claim Corlys's regent seat but was sent home at sixteen — quote: "Alyn Velaryon was but sixteen in 132 AC". He wed the fugitive Baela Targaryen at Dragonstone — quote: "poured out her woes to him". He rewarded the reconciled claimants Daemion and Daeron with lands for ships — quote: "rewarded them with lands on Driftmark".

### Orwyle
Former Grand Maester, condemned to the Wall, Orwyle bribed a guard and fled into the city as a beggar — quote: "he had changed into a beggar's rags and disappeared". Discovered in a brothel called Mother's under the name Old Wyl, he was arrested after teaching girls to read — quote: "going by the name Old Wyl". Tyland, an old green-council comrade, spared him and confined him to a comfortable cell, where he wrote confessions that fed Munkun's True Telling — quote: "such an invaluable source for his successor's True Telling". During the fever he worked tirelessly to save the sick — quote: "Orwyle worked tirelessly to save those he could".

### Munkun
Newly chosen Grand Maester, succeeding Orwyle, and named a regent — quote: "newly chosen by the Citadel to take up Grand Maester Orwyle's chain of office". He wrote to the Conclave that king and queen were both broken — quote: "'Both of them are broken,' Grand Maester Munkun declared". He ended the succession debate by invoking the Great Council of 101 and male primogeniture, but had no answer when asked who the male claimant was — quote: "'And who is this male claimant, my lord? We seem to have killed them all'". He survived the Winter Fever — quote: "one of the fortunate fourth and recovered".

### Alys Rivers
The baseborn wet nurse who had been Prince Aemond's prisoner then paramour, now claiming to be his widow, ruled Harrenhal as the "witch queen" — quote: "the baseborn wet nurse who had been the prisoner and then the paramour". She presented a child she named Aemond's trueborn son and heir — quote: "'His trueborn son and heir,' Alys Rivers spat back". When Ser Regis Groves refused to kneel, he died before the walls; accounts differ on whether by sorcery, crossbow, or sling — quote: "What happened next remains a matter of some dispute". A freed messenger reported she claimed a dragon and cursed those who laughed — quote: "she has a dragon. \| seen it".

### Cregan Stark
Lord Cregan Stark had stepped down as Hand and meant to return to Winterfell, but faced the problem of his unwanted northmen — quote: "he faced a thorny problem". He was offered a place among the regents but refused — quote: "Lord Cregan Stark was also offered a place amongst the regents, but refused". He led his diminished host north within a fortnight of the coronation — quote: "led his much-diminished host north along the kingsroad". Later that year he wed Alysanne Blackwood before the heart tree of Winterfell — quote: "pledged their troth before the heart tree in Winterfell's icy godswood".

### Alysanne Blackwood
Black Aly, aunt of Lord Benjicot, was credited (by legend and Mushroom) with proposing the Widow Fairs to resettle Cregan's northmen among riverland widows — quote: "it was Lady Alysanne who suggested an answer". Her matchmaking was largely a great success, strengthening the riverlords and spreading worship of the old gods — quote: "Lady Alysanne's matchmaking was a great success". She wed Cregan Stark at Winterfell — quote: "the marriage of his aunt Alysanne to Lord Cregan Stark".

### Johanna Lannister
Widow of Casterly Rock, daughter of Roland Westerling, Johanna ruled for her son Lord Loreon and resisted Dalton Greyjoy's ironborn — quote: "continued to resist the depredations of Dalton Greyjoy's ironborn". She appealed repeatedly to the Hand, her late husband's twin, for aid — quote: "her late lord husband's twin, for aid against the reavers". Donning a man's mail, she led the westermen to rout the raiders and save Kayce, though the songs of her slaying a dozen ironmen are dismissed — quote: "Johanna carried a banner into battle, not a sword".

### Jaehaera Targaryen
The child queen, wed to Aegon III, granddaughter of Alicent, had witnessed her twin brother's murder by Blood and Cheese — quote: "witnessed the murder of her twin brother at the hands of Blood and Cheese". Munkun described her as fragile and regressed — quote: "she is eight, going on four". She remained hale through the fever that killed two of her maids — quote: "the little queen herself remained hale and healthy".

### Leowyn Corbray
Named Protector of the Realm, a "black" and brother of Ser Corwyn — quote: "Lord Leowyn Corbray was named Protector of the Realm". Mushroom mocked him as thick of wit — quote: "'thick of neck and thick of wit'". Dominated by Tyland, he died of Winter Fever in his chambers — quote: "He died, along with his mistress and several of his servants".

### Samantha
Lady Sam, widow of Lord Ormund Hightower and paramour of her stepson Lord Lyonel, defied the High Septon's command to take vows as a silent sister — quote: "she ignored His High Holiness's command". She rode her warhorse into the Starry Sept and barred its doors until the High Septon relented — quote: "'this harlot on a horse'".

### Dalton Greyjoy
The Red Kraken paid little heed to the Iron Throne's edicts, continuing to raid and refusing to return women or Fair Isle — quote: "Dalton Greyjoy paid little heed to any of the edicts". He burned Johanna's shipyards and carried off a hundred more women — quote: "put them to the torch, and made off with another hundred women". He boasted a hundred salt wives but never a rock wife — quote: "boasted of having a hundred salt wives".

### Thaddeus Rowan
Lord of Goldengrove, twice-widowed with seven sons, chosen as Baela's match for the value of his all-male issue — quote: "Thaddeus Rowan, Lord of Goldengrove". Rejected by Baela's flight, his pride was appeased by betrothal to Floris Baratheon — quote: "appeased by a betrothal to Floris Baratheon".

### Corwyn Corbray
Ser Corwyn, a second son of thirty-two with two daughters, wielder of the Valyrian blade Lady Forlorn and brother of the Protector Leowyn, wed Rhaena Targaryen — quote: "his late father had given him Lady Forlorn". The match followed a quick betrothal and hasty wedding — quote: "a quick betrothal, followed by a hasty wedding".

### Regis Groves
Ser Regis, a Kingsguard knight, was entrusted with reclaiming Harrenhal and set out with fifty men, joined by Ser Damon Darry — quote: "This task he entrusted to a knight of the Kingsguard, Ser Regis Groves". Refusing to kneel to Alys Rivers's boy, he was killed instantly before the walls — quote: "Ser Regis Groves was dead in an instant".

### Sharis Footly
Widow of Tumbleton, ruling for her infant son whom she proclaimed her late husband's heir though likely sired by Bold Jon Roxton — quote: "far more likely that the boy had been sired by Bold Jon Roxton". She rebuilt the town and displayed the mounted heads of Seasmoke and Vermithor for coin — quote: "the heads of the dragons Seasmoke and Vermithor cleaned and mounted".

### Marston Waters
Ser Marston returned alone from Lys, accepted a pardon, and reported the Triarchy's collapse — quote: "Ser Marston Waters returned alone from Lys". He was made second to the Lord Commander of the Kingsguard — quote: "with Ser Marston Waters as his second".

### Willis Fell
The sole survivor of Viserys's Kingsguard, made Lord Commander of the White Swords — quote: "the sole survivor of the Kingsguard of King Viserys's time". He argued for Rhaena as heir and died of the fever — quote: "Ser Willis Fell, Lord Commander of the Kingsguard, was struck down".

### Racallio Ryndoon
Flamboyant captain-general who once fought Daemon Targaryen, called up by the Archon of Tyrosh to seize the Stepstones — quote: "the flamboyant captain-general who had once commanded the Triarchy's forces". He put the King of the Narrow Sea to death, then betrayed the Archon to claim the crown himself — quote: "betraying the Archon and his native city".
