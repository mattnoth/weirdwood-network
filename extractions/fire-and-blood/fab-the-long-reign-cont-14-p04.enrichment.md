## Node Prose
### Jaehaerys Targaryen
Jaehaerys celebrated his fiftieth nameday in 84 AC and men began calling him "the Old King" rather than "the Conciliator" — quote: "men were calling him “the Old King” rather than “the Conciliator”". He personally answered Braxton Beesbury's trial by combat, bearing Blackfyre against the younger man — quote: "The king bore Blackfyre". He killed the seducer of his daughter by driving the sword into his eye — quote: "laid Blackfyre’s point against his eye, and drove it deep". He refused all appeals to bring Saera home from Lys — quote: "She is a Lyseni whore,” His Grace replied". After Aemon's death he grieved to the end of his days and drank honeyed wine to sleep — quote: "His Grace grieved for Prince Aemon until the end of his days".

### Alysanne Targaryen
Good Queen Alysanne loved her subjects, her women's courts, music, dancing, reading, and flying on Silverwing — quote: "Good Queen Alysanne had loved her subjects". After her losses she withdrew from the governance of the realm — quote: "withdrew more and more from the governance of the realm". She never quite forgave the king over Saera and went to Dragonstone alone to grieve — quote: "She was going back to Dragonstone, alone, to grieve". Age broke her: she fell on the serpentine steps in 95 AC and lost her hearing — quote: "in 95 AC she slipped and fell on the serpentine steps". She said of her decline — quote: "but age is crueler". She died on Dragonstone in 100 AC, a century after the Conquest, aged sixty-four — quote: "Alysanne Targaryen died on Dragonstone on the first day of the seventh moon in 100 AC".

### Saera Targaryen
Saera was sent to the Faith at Oldtown, her sister Septa Maegelle charged with her instruction, to be a novice with the silent sisters — quote: "She would be a novice, it was announced, with the silent sisters". After a year and a half she fled the motherhouse in 85 AC — quote: "in 85 AC, she seized it, fleeing from the motherhouse". In her escape she threw an older sister down the stairs — quote: "she knocked the woman down a flight of steps". A year later she was found in a Lysene pleasure garden, still dressed as a novice — quote: "seen in a Lysene pleasure garden, still clad as a novice". She later moved to Volantis, an infamous but wealthy woman, and left her mother's secret letters unanswered — quote: "Saera still lived, somewhere in Volantis".

### Viserra Targaryen
Viserra, the most beautiful of the queen's daughters, was betrothed at fifteen in 86 AC to old Lord Theomore Manderly — quote: "the betrothal of her daughter Viserra". She wanted her brother Baelon and the queenship, not the match — quote: "That is the husband she desires". She once offered her maidenhead to any boy who put his head in a dragon's mouth — quote: "she would give her maidenhead to whoever put his head in a dragon's mouth". She slipped drunk and naked into Baelon's bed and was sent away — quote: "he found her drunk and naked in his bed and sent her on her way". On a wild night ride she was thrown from her palfrey at the foot of Aegon's High Hill in 87 AC — quote: "The princess was thrown from the saddle headfirst into a wall. Her neck was broken".

### Baelon Targaryen
Baelon the Brave, "the Spring Prince," was fourteen years older than Viserra and widowed of his sister Alyssa — quote: "Prince Baelon was fourteen years older than Viserra, twenty-nine to her fifteen". When Aemon was slain he came to Tarth on Vhagar with Dark Sister and slaughtered the Myrish — quote: "he came down upon Tarth on Vhagar, howling for vengeance". Returning with his brother's corpse he wept in his mother's arms — quote: "| slew a thousand of them". He became Prince of Dragonstone and Hand of the King and remained near the queen to the end — quote: "Baelon was the Prince of Dragonstone, Hand of the King". Alysanne told him — quote: "You will be a great king, even greater".

### Aemon Targaryen
Aemon, Prince of Dragonstone, husband of Lady Jocelyn and father of Rhaenys, was sent to lead the assault on Tarth with Caraxes — quote: "Prince Aemon would have Caraxes". He bade his pregnant daughter stay behind, his last words to her about wanting a grandson — quote: "Those were the last words he would ever speak to his daughter". A Myrish crossbow bolt aimed at the Evenstar struck him instead — quote: "The iron bolt punched through the prince’s throat". He died in 92 AC, drowned on his own blood, aged thirty-seven — quote: "Aemon Targaryen died struggling to speak, drowned on his own blood".

### Rhaenys Targaryen
Rhaenys, eldest grandchild of Jaehaerys and a dragonrider since thirteen, flew to the king's side on Meleys — quote: "his granddaughter, Princess Rhaenys, who flew to his side on Meleys". She won the king's blessing for her marriage on Greenshield — quote: "Princess Rhaenys told him of her plans to marry". She wed Corlys Velaryon in 90 AC, arriving on Meleys — quote: "the wedding of their eldest grandchild, Princess Rhaenys, to Corlys Velaryon". By the time of Aemon's death she had just learned she was expecting — quote: "The princess had just learned she was expecting".

### Corlys Velaryon
Corlys Velaryon of Driftmark, Lord of the Tides, "the Sea Snake," was hailed as the greatest seafarer Westeros had known — quote: "the Sea Snake was already hailed as the greatest seafarer Westeros had ever known". He came home after nine voyages to wed Rhaenys at thirty-seven — quote: "with his nine great voyages behind him". In the Tarth campaign he was to bring the Velaryon fleet south to guard Lord Boremund's crossing — quote: "Lord Corlys and his fleet set sail from Driftmark".

### Gael Targaryen
Princess Gael, "the Winter Child," was born in 80 AC when Alysanne was forty-four, a sweet but frail and simple girl — quote: "Princess Gael, the Winter Child, born in 80 AC". She vanished from court in 99 AC and was said to have died of a summer fever — quote: "in 99 AC she vanished from court". The true tale, told after her parents' deaths, was that a traveling singer seduced and abandoned her — quote: "Seduced and abandoned by a traveling singer". After bearing a stillborn son she drowned herself — quote: "walked into the waters of Blackwater Bay and drowned".

### Maegelle Targaryen
Septa Maegelle took charge of Saera's instruction at Oldtown — quote: "Septa Maegelle was to take charge of her instruction". She bridged the estrangement between her parents — quote: "It was Septa Maegelle who bridged the Rift". She died in 96 AC of greyscale after nursing the afflicted — quote: "died in 96 AC, her arms and legs turned to stone by greyscale".

### Braxton Beesbury
Braxton Beesbury, called Stinger, heir to Honeyholt, drew the harshest punishment for seducing Saera — quote: "the proud young knight called Stinger". He chose trial by combat, sure of his skill at arms — quote: "Then | choose trial by combat". At nineteen he wielded a morningstar against the king but tired and fell — quote: "The heir to Honeyholt was nineteen years of age".

### Viserys Targaryen
Prince Baelon's sixteen-year-old son Viserys entered the Dragonpit in 93 AC and claimed the aged Balerion — quote: "Prince Baelon’s sixteen-year-old son, Viserys, entered the Dragonpit and claimed Balerion". He flew thrice around the city but judged the Black Dread too weak to reach Dragonstone — quote: "flew thrice around the city before landing again".

### Theomore Manderly
Theomore Manderly, the fierce old Lord of White Harbor, was betrothed to Viserra to bind the North to the Iron Throne — quote: "Theomore Manderly, the fierce old Lord of White Harbor". He had outlived four wives and grown very stout — quote: "His lordship had outlived four wives".

### Roy Connington
Red Roy Connington refused to wed Alys Turnberry or claim Stinger's bastard as heir to Griffin's Roost — quote: "Red Roy Connington refused to marry her". Given a choice of the Wall or exile, he chose exile across the narrow sea — quote: "he chose exile and made his way across the narrow sea". He was killed in Myr — quote: "by a whore in a Myrish gambling den".

### Cameron
Lord Cameron, the Evenstar of Tarth, fell back into the island's mountains and made camp in a hidden valley — quote: "Lord Cameron, the Evenstar of Tarth, had fallen back into the spine of mountains". A Myrish scout's bolt aimed at him missed and killed Aemon instead — quote: "missed Lord Cameron...and struck Prince Aemon".

### Vaegon Targaryen
Vaegon, an archmaester at the Citadel, had grown a cold and distant man who wrote dutifully but without warmth — quote: "Vaegon was an archmaester at the Citadel".

### Alys Turnberry
Alys Turnberry, called Sweetberry and pregnant, was sent to the Vale to bear a red-haired girl, then wed to Dunstan Pryor — quote: "she was married to Dunstan Pryor".

### Balerion
Balerion, the Black Dread, had stopped growing and grown sluggish; claimed by Viserys, he died less than a year later — quote: "Less than a year later, Balerion was gone". Septon Barth wrote of him — quote: "The last living creature in all the world who saw Valyria in its glory".
