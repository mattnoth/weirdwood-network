## Node Prose
### Hugh Hammer
Hard Hugh Hammer, a bastard of blacksmith origin who "undeniably possessed some Targaryen blood," desired a crown for himself and declared the throne should be his by the same right as the Conqueror — a dragon — for he rode Vermithor. — quote: "The same right as the Conqueror. A dragon." He wore a crown of black iron over the fury of Prince Daeron. — quote: "Hard Hugh was wearing a crown of black iron". At his command his men held down Ser Roger Corne while he nailed three horseshoes to the knight's skull. — quote: "nailed not one but three horseshoes to the knight's skull". During the Second Battle of Tumbleton he was cut open by Bold Jon Roxton before he could reach Vermithor. — quote: "opening the bastard from groin to throat".

### Ulf White
Ulf White, "Lord White" and "Ulf the Sot," one of the Two Betrayers, was drunk more often than not and had never shown great prowess at arms. — quote: "who was drunk more oft than not". During the battle he could not be roused and slept through it under a table. — quote: "he rolled under a table and snored through the entire battle". Waking to find himself the last dragonrider with the last dragon, he demanded the throne. — quote: "You take the city, I'll take the bloody throne, how's that?" He was poisoned by Ser Hobert Hightower's wine and never woke. — quote: "When Lord Ulf went to sleep, never to awaken".

### Prince Daeron Targaryen
Daeron Targaryen, "Daeron the Daring," youngest son of King Viserys I by Queen Alicent, stood next in the line of succession after Aegon II and Aemond. — quote: "Prince Daeron stood next in the line of succession". He threw a cup of wine into Hard Hugh's face over the bastard's claim. — quote: "he threw a cup of wine into Hard Hugh's face". He sealed the warrants for the execution of Hammer and White. — quote: "eagerly affixed his seal". He died at the Second Battle of Tumbleton; three conflicting accounts describe his death. According to Black Trombo, the prince stumbled out with his nightclothes afire and was killed with a spiked morningstar. — quote: "smashed his face in with a swing of his spiked morningstar". A second account holds he was killed by an unknown man-at-arms with a sword. — quote: "his slayer was not Black Trombo, but some unknown man-at-arms". Munkun's True Telling holds instead that he died when his burning pavilion collapsed on him. — quote: "his burning pavilion collapsed upon".

### Unwin Peake
Lord Unwin Peake summoned twelve other lords and landed knights to a secret council in the cellar of a Tumbleton inn to curb the baseborn dragonriders. — quote: "summoned eleven other lords and landed knights to a secret council". He argued that Hard Hugh must die before Ulf White. — quote: "Hard Hugh must needs die first". He brought warrants for the executions to Prince Daeron. — quote: "Lord Unwin Peake presented him with warrants for the execution". After the battle he offered a thousand golden dragons to any knight of noble birth who could claim Silverwing, then ordered a retreat. — quote: "offered a thousand golden dragons to any knight of noble birth".

### Jon Roxton
Bold Jon Roxton settled the Caltrops' dispute, urging they kill the Betrayers at once. — quote: "We kill the bastards now". Wielding the Valyrian steel OrphanMaker, he slew Hard Hugh Hammer in Lord Footly's yard. — quote: "thrusting deep into Hammer's belly". He slew three of Hammer's men before being slain in turn; it is said he died when his foot slipped on Hammer's entrails, but Gyldayn doubts the detail. — quote: "his foot slipped on a coil of Hugh Hammer's entrails".

### Hobert Hightower
Ser Hobert Hightower, a reluctant Caltrop, asked at the council "what of the dragons?" — quote: "but what of the dragons?" Rather than betray his fellow Caltrops he drank the poisoned Arbor gold to induce Ulf White to drink, dying within the hour. — quote: "His heart stopped within the hour". Mushroom mocked him, saying no man feared his sword but his wine cup was deadlier than Valyrian steel. — quote: "but his wine cup was deadlier than Valyrian steel."

### Addam Velaryon
Ser Addam Velaryon, "born Addam of Hull," rode Seasmoke and was determined to prove not all bastards are turncloaks. — quote: "not all bastards need be turncloaks". Singers say he flew to the Isle of Faces to take counsel with the Green Men, but Gyldayn confines himself to the known: Addam gathered an army of near four thousand from the riverlands. — quote: "he had near four thousand men at his back". He led the night attack that began the Second Battle of Tumbleton and died beside Seasmoke; his bones rested at Raventree Hall until his brother Alyn returned them to Hull in 138 AC. — quote: "found dead beside his dragon". His tomb reads LOYAL. — quote: "On his tomb is engraved a single word".

### Rhaenyra Targaryen
Queen Rhaenyra, "the Realm’s Delight" and "Half-Year Queen," fled King's Landing in terror and found gates barred against her at Rosby and grudging welcome at Duskendale. — quote: "flight from King’s Landing had been beset". Having lost her fleet when she sent Corlys to the dungeons, she had neither gold nor ships and sold her crown to buy passage on the Violande. — quote: "forced to sell her crown to raise the coin". Set on returning to Dragonstone for dragon's eggs, she came ashore into her brother's trap. — quote: "have another dragon, or all was lost". Aegon II delivered her to Sunfyre, who devoured her; she died on the twenty-second day of the tenth moon of 130 AC, aged thirty-three. — quote: "passed from this veil of tears upon the twenty-second day of the tenth moon".

### Aegon II Targaryen
King Aegon II had been spirited out of King's Landing by Larys Strong and hidden on Dragonstone in a fisherman's cloak amid a load of codfish. — quote: "concealed amongst a load of codfish on a fishing skiff". Reunited with the maimed Sunfyre, he retook Dragonstone in under an hour, shattering both legs leaping from the saddle. — quote: "shattering both legs". He had Grand Maester Gerardys slowly hanged and disembowelled, and refused milk of the poppy from him. — quote: "I shall not walk that road again". He delivered his half-sister to his dragon and spared her son Prince Aegon as a hostage. — quote: "the boy might yet have value as a hostage".

### Alfred Broome
Ser Alfred Broome, most senior knight at Dragonstone since the Old King's reign, was passed over for castellan and betrayed the queen for a promise of lordship, lands, and gold. — quote: "willing to betray his queen in return for a promise of lordship". He advised the king's men on the castle's weaknesses and drove a spear through Ser Robert Quince. — quote: "Broome drove a spear into his huge pale belly". He commanded the queen's ambush at the harbour and pricked her breast with a dagger to rouse Sunfyre. — quote: "until Broome pricked the queen's breast with his dagger". He argued for killing Prince Aegon but was overruled. — quote: "Ser Alfred Broome argued for killing Prince Aegon as well".

### Larys Strong
Lord Larys Strong the Clubfoot spirited King Aegon II and his children out of King's Landing through a secret passage of Maegor the Cruel. — quote: "led them out through some secret passage of". He decreed the fugitives part company so no one taken could betray the rest, sending Maelor with Rickard Thorne and Jaehaera with Willis Fell. — quote: "the fugitives should part company". Only he knew the king was hidden on Dragonstone; Orwyle had all this from his own lips. — quote: "few hunters would ever think to look for Aegon on his sister's own island".

### Robert Quince
Ser Robert Quince, "an able man grown old and fat," was the steadfast castellan Rhaenyra set over Dragonstone's small garrison. — quote: "an able man grown old and fat". He had blamed the Cannibal for Grey Ghost's death. — quote: "Ser Robert Quince had blamed the Cannibal". He was killed in his bed by Ser Alfred Broome's spear thrust, which pinned him to the floor. — quote: "the spear went out Ser Robert's back". His charred corpse, burned beyond recognition, was later found hanging from the gatehouse. — quote: "burned beyond all recognition".

### Marston Waters
Ser Marston Waters was the bastard son of Tom Tanglebeard’s sister by the knight who took her maidenhead. — quote: "being the bastard son of Tom Tanglebeard’s sister". He surprised Grand Maester Gerardys in the rookery so no raven could escape. — quote: "Ser Marston surprised Grand Maester Gerardys in his rookery". He wrenched Broome's blade away to spare Lady Baela, and King Aegon named him to the Kingsguard for his valor. — quote: "King Aegon had named him to his Kingsguard for his valor".

### Baela Targaryen
Baela Targaryen, Prince Daemon's daughter by the Lady, escaped her captors through a window and loosed her dragon. — quote: "the girl slipped out her window". As fearless as her father, she rose on Moondancer to meet King Aegon on Sunfyre. — quote: "as fearless as her father". Burned and battered after the dragons fell, she crawled free of Moondancer and was carried to the maester. — quote: "carried her to the maester".

### Gerardys
Grand Maester Gerardys served on Dragonstone; Rhaenyra sent to him for a ship and, receiving no reply, again questioned his loyalty. — quote: "she began to question her Grand". Surprised in his rookery by Marston Waters, he was later hanged with the chain Rhaenyra had given him, thrice let down, then disembowelled and fed to Sunfyre. — quote: "he was disemboweled and dangled before Sunfyre".

### Benjicot Blackwood
Benjicot Blackwood, "the boy Ben Blackwood," the twelve-year-old Lord of Raventree, came to Addam's host. — quote: "the twelve-year-old Lord of Raventree". He watched the dragons fight fifty yards off and told Munkun of it years later, and ordered his archer to end the dying Tessarion. — quote: "Lord Blackwood summoned his best archer". He carried away Addam Velaryon's broken body. — quote: "carried with him the broken body of Ser Addam Velaryon".

### Elmo Tully
Ser Elmo Tully, "that reluctant warrior," called House Tully's banners for the queen in defiance of his bedridden grandsire Lord Grover, after Seasmoke descended on Riverrun. — quote: "in defiance of the wishes of his bedridden grandsire, Lord Grover". He is reported to have quipped about a dragon in the courtyard. — quote: "does wonders to resolve one's doubts".

### Sunfyre
Sunfyre the Golden, once "the most magnificent dragon ever to fly," had his wing torn by Meleys and was reduced to hopping between hills after Rook's Rest; Mushroom mocked him as a "great golden fire-breathing chicken." — quote: "a great golden fire-breathing chicken". He crossed Blackwater Bay to Dragonstone, killing Grey Ghost on the way. — quote: "it was Sunfyre that the sailors on the Nessaria had seen attacking Grey Ghost". He slew Moondancer in the yard and devoured both her carcass and Queen Rhaenyra. — quote: "the golden dragon devoured the queen in six bites".

### Vermithor
Vermithor, "the Bronze Fury," once the Old King's mount and then Hard Hugh's, was almost a hundred years old and as large as the two young dragons put together. — quote: "as large as the two young dragons put together". Riderless in the battle, he knew not friend from foe and burned Lords Piper and Deddings. — quote: "he loosed his wroth on all". He ripped off Seasmoke's head, then collapsed and died unable to fly with his prize. — quote: "when Vermithor locked his teeth into his neck and ripped his head off".

### Tessarion
Tessarion, "the Blue Queen," Prince Daeron's she-dragon, danced with Seasmoke in the air without ever closing. — quote: "only at Tumbleton did the dragons ever truly dance". She fell from the sky to join the fight of Seasmoke and Vermithor, and, unable to regain the sky, was killed by three shafts to the eye from Billy Burley. — quote: "sent three shafts into her eye as she lay helpless".

### Seasmoke
Seasmoke, Ser Addam Velaryon's silver-grey mount, opened the Second Battle of Tumbleton with fire. — quote: "a dragon was swooping down upon them". He fought Tessarion in a dance, then slammed into Vermithor to protect Addam's men. — quote: "Seasmoke slammed into him from above". He was the first of the three to die when Vermithor tore his head off. — quote: "Seasmoke was first to die".

### Moondancer
Moondancer, a young pale-green dragon with pearl horns and crest, was no larger than a warhorse but very quick. — quote: "no larger than a warhorse". Ridden by Baela, she wounded Sunfyre repeatedly but was blinded and borne down. — quote: "a blast that took Moondancer full in the eyes". Grounded, her quickness failed her and she soon lay still. — quote: "The green dragon soon lay still".

### Silverwing
Silverwing, Good Queen Alysanne’s mount of old and Ulf White's dragon, took to the sky as the carnage began and circled for hours. — quote: "circling the battlefield for hours". Singers tell she thrice lifted Vermithor's wing with her nose, though Gyldayn deems this a fable. — quote: "as if to make him fly again, but this is most like a fable". Three men who tried to claim her were maimed or killed. — quote: "the first had his arm torn off and the second burned to death".

### Munkun
Grand Maester Munkun's True Telling, based largely on Orwyle's account, revealed how Aegon II came to Dragonstone. — quote: "it was his True Telling, based in large part on the account of Grand Maester Orwyle". Lord Blackwood told him of the dragon fight years later. — quote: "Lord Blackwood told Grand Maester Munkun many years later".

### Meredyth Darklyn
Lady Meredyth, widow of Lord Gunthor Darklyn, was less than overjoyed at the queen's arrival at Duskendale and admitted her only through Ser Harrold Darke's intercession. — quote: "less than overjoyed when Her". She required the queen not remain long. — quote: "only upon the condition that she would not remain for long".

### Harrold Darke
Ser Harrold Darke, distant kin to the Darklyns and once squire to the late Ser Steffon, persuaded Lady Meredyth to admit the queen and urged her to seek refuge in the Vale. — quote: "Only the intercession of Ser Harrold Darke persuaded Lady Meredyth". At Dragonstone an axe split his head before his sword could clear its scabbard. — quote: "An axe split Ser Harrold Darke’s head".

### Cregan Stark
Cregan Stark wrote from Winterfell promising to bring a host south once the harvest was in, offering ten thousand men. — quote: "The northman promised the queen ten thousand men". He warned it would take time to gather his men. — quote: "for my realms are large, and with winter upon us".

### Jeyne Arryn
Lady Jeyne, "the Maiden of the Vale," wrote from the Gates of the Moon promising aid but, with the passes snowed shut, needing House Velaryon's ships at Gulltown or coin to hire ships. — quote: "If House Velaryon would send its ships to Gulltown". 

### Prince Aegon (the Younger)
Prince Aegon, "Aegon the Younger," Rhaenyra's last living son, remained by her side "like a small pale shadow" through her despair. — quote: "like a small pale shadow". He cried "Mother, flee" too late and watched her death in horror before being manacled to the dungeons as a hostage. — quote: "watched in horror, unable to move".

### Owen Fossoway
Owen Fossoway, Lord of Cider Hall, was dispatched under cover of darkness to bring Prince Daeron to the conspirators' cellar; he was among the eight Caltrops slain. — quote: "was dispatched under cover of darkness to wake the prince".

### Black Trombo
Black Trombo, a Myrish sellsword who came with Hugo Vance's men, claimed far and wide to have slain Prince Daeron with his morningstar. — quote: "This version was the one preferred by Black Trombo, who told it far and wide".
