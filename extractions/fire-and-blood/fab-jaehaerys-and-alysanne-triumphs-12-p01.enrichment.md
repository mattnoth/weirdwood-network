## Node Prose

### Jaehaerys I Targaryen
King on the Iron Throne, called the Conciliator, whose reign brought long peace and prosperity — quote: "the population of Westeros north of Dorne doubled during the Conciliator's reign". With his "even smaller council" he set out to codify and reform all the realm's laws, beginning the Great Code in autumn 55 AC — quote: "Jaehaerys set out to codify, organize, and reform all the kingdom's laws". He defended his sister Rhaena to his lords even as he chafed at her — quote: "the blood of the dragon, and my sister". Fearing Aerea dead, he set Rego's agents hunting for her and Balerion, and when Elissa Farman's ship escaped he weighed and abandoned a dragon-back chase — quote: "reluctantly put the idea aside". After Aerea's death he silenced Ser Lucamore and, later that year, banned ships from the Valyrian islands — quote: "issued a royal edict forbidding any ship suspected of having visited the Valyrian islands".

### Alysanne Targaryen
Queen and wife of Jaehaerys, who prayed daily for her niece Aerea and blamed both herself and her sister — quote: "Queen Alysanne prayed daily for her niece Aerea". Early in 55 AC she was again with child and, delighting in matchmaking, arranged betrothals for two of her Wise Women — quote: "betrothals she arranged for two of the Wise Women". She withdrew to Dragonstone to bear her son despite courtiers' misgivings — quote: "so happy on Dragonstone". She was terrified by the king's notion of chasing Elissa on Vermithor, and later recalled the horn-blast of Balerion's return — quote: "The very notion terrified Queen Alysanne, however".

### Rhaena Targaryen
Sister of King Jaehaerys and rider of Dreamfyre, searching the realm for her lost daughter Aerea — quote: "Rhaena Targaryen flew to Oldtown first". She visited her novice daughter Rhaella in Oldtown, then confronted Lord Franklyn Farman on Fair Isle with threats of Balerion — quote: "Balerion quite enjoyed your brother, by now he may desire another course". Afterward the histories lose track of her wanderings across the wild places — quote: "After Fair Isle, history loses track of Rhaena Targaryen". Later she was seen at Greenstone, drawn there by her childhood cousin Larissa Velaryon — quote: "his sister Rhaena had been seen again, this time at Greenstone".

### Aerea Targaryen
Princess and daughter of Rhaena, near thirteen, who had fled Dragonstone atop Balerion and vanished for more than a year — quote: "Aerea and Balerion still vanished from human ken". She returned to King's Landing on Balerion's back, near naked and dying — quote: "Princess Aerea slid from his back". She spoke only fragments before collapsing — quote: "and collapsed" — and died the same night of a monstrous fever — quote: "Aerea Targaryen was dead". Her body was burned at sunrise the next day — quote: "The princess was consigned to the flames the very next day at sunrise".

### Septon Barth
Septon and the king's foremost counselor, contributing more than any man to the Great Code — quote: "the Great Code of Septon Barth". He weighed in on every court question, from the source of Targaryen power to whether an Essosi "dragon" was truly a wyvern — quote: "A wyvern, beyond a doubt". Admitted alone to the dying Aerea, he recorded in sealed privy papers the horrors he and Benifer witnessed and admitted lying to the king — quote: "that Aerea never spoke, but that is a lie". According to Barth's own surmise, Balerion had borne Aerea to Valyria — quote: "we have all been asking the wrong question". The ordeal drove him to write Dragons, Wyrms, and Wyverns — quote: "provocative but unsound".

### Grand Maester Benifer
Grand Maester to Jaehaerys, and to his uncle Maegor before him, a diligent keeper of records — quote: "in service to King Jaehaerys and his uncle Maegor before him". He sent a storm of ravens seeking Aerea, and later barred even the king from the dying princess's chamber — quote: "Benifer denied them entry". He treated her with milk of the poppy and a tub of ice, to no avail — quote: "giving her milk of the poppy and immersing her in a tub of ice". Tellingly, his writings never once mention Aerea's return or death — quote: "there is not a single word to be found concerning the return of Aerea Targaryen".

### Elissa Farman
Lady and sister of Lord Franklyn Farman, who stole the dragon eggs and bought the carrack Sun Chaser, sailing under the false name Alys Westhill — quote: "the dream Elissa Farman had purchased with her stolen dragon's eggs". Warned by the Sealord of Braavos, she made her way through the Stepstones and on to Oldtown seeking a crew to cross the Sunset Sea — quote: "to sail beyond the sunset in search of lands undreamed of". She believed the world far larger than the maesters held — quote: "far larger and far stranger than the maesters imagine". The Hightower grandsons joined her, and the Sun Chaser slipped away before the king's ravens could seize her — quote: "The Sun Chaser departed Oldtown on the twenty-third day of the third moon of 56 AC".

### Balerion
The Black Dread, largest and oldest of the dragons, once ridden by Aegon the Conqueror and by Maegor the Cruel — quote: "Balerion was the largest and oldest of the three dragons that King Aegon and his sisters rode to conquest". Barth held he alone knew Valyria before the Doom — quote: "the only living creature in the world that knew Valyria before the Doom". He returned to King's Landing bearing the dying Aerea, his own body freshly torn — quote: "a jagged rent down his left side almost nine feet long". By Rhaena's telling he had earlier devoured a Farman — quote: "Balerion quite enjoyed your brother".

### Rhaella Targaryen
Daughter of Rhaena and twin of Aerea, a novice septa in Oldtown, so like and unlike her sister — quote: "so like and yet so unlike her twin". She comforted her mother with words of faith — quote: "the best mother any child could wish for".

### Donnel Hightower
Lord of the Hightower, styled "Donnel the Delayer," who received Rhaena in Oldtown and was commanded to seize Elissa Farman — quote: "commanding him to take this woman into custody". His ravens came too late, and some hold he delayed on purpose — quote: "Donnel the Delayer delayed again". His own ships could not catch the Sun Chaser — quote: "none of Lord Donnel’s vessels could match the Sun".

### Willam the Wasp
Ser, of the Kingsguard, sent with a dozen men to hunt the beast of Andalos — quote: "led by Ser Willam the Wasp of his Kingsguard". Betrayed by a Pentoshi guide into an ambush in the Velvet Hills, he and his men were slain, he the last to fall — quote: "they were overwhelmed and slain. Ser Willam had been the last to fall". His fate was written in the White Book of the Kingsguard — quote: "inscribed in the White Book of the Kingsguard".

### Lucamore Strong
Ser, a broad-shouldered blond knight of the riverlands, sole survivor of the grand melee in the Dragonpit — quote: "called Ser Lucamore Strong". For it he was given Willam's white cloak — quote: "Jaehaerys awarded a white cloak to Ser Lucamore Strong". He carried the dying Aerea to Benifer and told of the fever and stirrings within her, until the king commanded his silence — quote: "commanded him to speak no more of the princess".

### Myles Smallwood
Lord Smallwood, the King's Hand, quick to blame a dragon and to urge vengeance — quote: "Myles Smallwood, the King's Hand, urged the king to punish Pentos". He pinned the Disputed Lands fire on Balerion — quote: "Ser Myles Smallwood declared".

### Rego Draz
The king's agent-master whose informers hunted first the dragon eggs and then Aerea across the narrow sea — quote: "All the agents and informers that Rego Draz had engaged". On the morning of Balerion's return he was in his manse below the Dragonpit — quote: "Rego Draz entertained a young lady of negotiable virtue".

### Franklyn Farman
Lord of Fair Isle and brother of Elissa, no fonder of Rhaena than before and unwise in his speech to her — quote: "The years had not made his lordship any fonder of the queen".

### Larissa Velaryon
Rhaena's cousin and earliest favorite, widow of the Evenstar of Tarth's second son, now living with her married daughter on Greenstone — quote: "her cousin Larissa Velaryon". Her presence drew Rhaena to the poor isle of Estermont — quote: "a companion of her childhood".

### Daenerys Targaryen
Young princess who wanted a little sister rather than a brother — quote: "wanted a little sister". Ser Simon Dondarrion crowned her his queen of love and beauty — quote: "crowned Princess Daenerys as his queen of love and beauty".

### Aemon Targaryen
Newborn son of Jaehaerys and Alysanne, pale of eye and hair, hailed as the new heir — quote: "a boy, clean-limbed and healthy, with eyes as pale as lilac". A dragon's egg was laid in his cradle — quote: "he placed a dragon's egg in the prince's cradle".

### Sealord of Braavos
Ruler of Braavos who fed Elissa caution and warned her to flee before her hunters found her — quote: "Men are hunting you, all up and down the narrow sea".

### Simon Dondarrion
Ser, of Blackhaven, gallant tourney champion beloved of commons and queen — quote: "Ser Simon Dondarrion of Blackhaven, who won the love of the commons and queen alike".

### Brandon the Shipwright
A King of Winter who built a fleet to cross the Sunset Sea and sailed west, never to return — quote: "He took them west himself, never to return".

### Brandon the Burner
Son and heir of Brandon the Shipwright, who burned the shipyards and earned his name — quote: "burned the yards where they were built".
