## Node Prose
### Aegon III Targaryen
The king reigned as a boy under regents; his court was called Aegon III's court — quote: "many of the other lords and ladies of Aegon III's court". He could not be persuaded even by his brother to come see his sister fly her dragon — quote: "persuade his brother the king to come see his sister fly". When his cupbearer Gaemon was poisoned he was left inconsolable and sank back into gloom — quote: "Gaemon Palehair’s death nonetheless left Aegon inconsolable". He barred all dragon's eggs from his castle after the monstrous hatching — quote: "Aegon commanded that no dragon's eggs were to be allowed in his castle". When the coup came he defended Maegor's Holdfast, disavowing Marston Waters — quote: "and | never chose Ser Marston for my Hand". He recalled that the Kingsguard had watched his mother die — quote: "when the dragon ate my mother. All you did was watch".

### Viserys Targaryen
The king's clever, gallant brother, a boy of thirteen who the court doted upon — quote: "that clever, gallant boy Viserys". He announced Larra was with child and later fathered a son by her — quote: "Prince Viserys thrilled the court by announcing that the Lady Larra was with child". He kept his own dragon's egg through exile and captivity and refused to speak to the king for a moon after being forced to yield it — quote: "the prince had kept it with him throughout his years of exile and captivity". He barred the drawbridge of Maegor's Holdfast with a battleaxe to defend his wife — quote: "for you shall not pass whilst I still stand".

### Larra Rogare
Prince Viserys's Lysene wife, who remained a lady of Lys in her heart and would not learn the Common Tongue — quote: "Lady Larra made no effort to learn the Common Tongue". She kept her own Lysene gods and rites — quote: "Lady Larra not also insisted upon keeping her own gods". Dark rumors held that she could turn into a cat and visited brothels as a man; according to the whispers her worship fed such tales — quote: "Larra herself could transform into a cat". She gave birth to Viserys's firstborn son at the age of twenty — quote: "Larra of Lys gave birth to a son, Prince Viserys's firstborn child".

### Rhaena Targaryen
Lady Rhaena, wife of Ser Corwyn Corbray and twin of Lady Baela, first flew her dragon Morning at nineteen — quote: "Lady Rhaena, at the age of nineteen, was flying her dragon, Morning, for the first time". She carried Morning to Dragonstone, saying dragons were more welcome there — quote: "Dragons and those who ride them are more welcome". After her husband's death her twin came to guard against her seeking vengeance — quote: "did not attempt to avenge her husband's death herself on Morning".

### Baela Targaryen
Lady Baela, twin of Rhaena and wife of Lord Alyn, returned to Driftmark with her husband and their daughter — quote: "Lady Baela, meanwhile returned to Driftmark with Lord Alyn and their daughter". She went to Dragonstone to comfort her widowed twin — quote: "went to Dragonstone to comfort her widowed twin".

### Alyn Velaryon
Lord Oakenfist refused to join House Lannister's vengeance on the ironmen — quote: "Lord Oakenfist had refused to be a part of House Lannister’s vengeance". He tore the monstrous wyrm off his infant daughter Laena and hacked it apart — quote: "Lord Oakenfist ripped the “dragon” off her, flung it to the floor, and hacked it into". He commanded the Velaryon fleet against Gulltown for the Hand — quote: "Oakenfist would command the fleet himself".

### Thaddeus Rowan
Lord Rowan, Hand and regent, tried to relieve the realm's woes but was obeyed by none — quote: "his efforts were largely ineffectual". Well-liked but not feared, he was whispered against as having sold himself to the Lyseni — quote: "neither Thaddeus Rowan nor the boy he served were feared". His young wife Lady Floris died in childbirth — quote: "his young wife, the Lady Floris, died in childbirth". He was removed from office and taken into captivity during the coup — quote: "Lord Rowan himself joined the Rogares in captivity".

### Corwyn Corbray
Ser Corwyn, husband of Lady Rhaena, took a council seat after Mooton's death — quote: "Lord Rowan turned to Ser Corwyn Corbray, Lady Rhaena’s husband". He ruled for Jeyne's will and declared Joffrey the rightful Lord of the Eyrie — quote: "declared Ser Joffrey Arryn the rightful Lord of the Eyrie". Drawing Lady Forlorn at Runestone, he was shot through the breast by a crossbowman — quote: "a crossbowman on Runestone's battlements loosed a quarrel and pierced him through the breast". He was uncle to Quenton Corbray and by marriage kin to Alyn Oakenfist — quote: "goodbrother to her twin, Lady Baela, and thus by marriage kin to Alyn Oakenfist".

### Jeyne Arryn
The Maiden of the Vale died at Gulltown of a chill at forty, in the arms of Jessamyn Redfort — quote: "wrapped in the arms of Jessamyn Redfort, her “dear companion.”". On her deathbed she named her cousin Ser Joffrey Arryn her heir — quote: "naming her cousin Ser Joffrey Arryn as her heir".

### Joffrey Arryn
Ser Joffrey served ten years as Knight of the Bloody Gate before being named heir — quote: "Knight of the Bloody Gate, defending the Vale against the savage wildlings". Only a fourth cousin, his claim was contested by nearer kin — quote: "Ser Joffrey was only a fourth cousin by degree".

### Arnold Arryn
Jeyne's first cousin, "the Mad Heir," had twice tried to depose her and was driven mad by long imprisonment — quote: "Ser Arnold Arryn, who had twice attempted to depose her". He escaped Corbray and fled to Runestone where he had squired as a boy — quote: "fled to Runestone, where he had served as a squire in his boyhood".

### Isembard Arryn
Patriarch of the rich Gulltown Arryns, known as the Gilded Falcon, he pressed a claim with gold — quote: "he soon became known as the Gilded Falcon". He bribed lesser lords and brought sellswords across the sea — quote: "bribing lesser lords to support his claim and bringing sellswords".

### Johanna Lannister
Lady Johanna sought to put every ironman to the sword in vengeance for the westerlands — quote: "Lady Johanna’s wish to put every ironman to the sword". According to the narration she swayed the old Sea Lion, mayhaps by promising to wed him — quote: "swayed, mayhaps, by her promise to marry him". She had the captive Rodrik Greyjoy gelded and made her son's fool — quote: "Lady Johanna had him gelded and made him her son's fool".

### Leo Costayne
The old Sea Lion landed thousands of Lannister swords on the Iron Islands — quote: "landing thousands of Lannister swords and spears on Pyke, Great Wyk, and Harlaw". He was cut down on Great Wyk by Arthur Goodbrother, and most of his ships were lost — quote: "Costayne died amidst the stony hills of Great Wyk, cut down by the hand of Arthur Goodbrother".

### Lysandro Rogare
Father of Larra and her brothers, he rose to rule Lys as First Magister for Life under a "velvet tyranny" — quote: "the “velvet tyranny” of Lysandro Rogare, who had taken on himself the style of First Magister for Life". His ties to the Iron Throne made the Rogares princes of Lys in all but name — quote: "Lysandro's to the Iron Throne made the Rogares the princes of Lys". He drowned when his barge sank, one day apart from his brother — quote: "Lysandro drowned when his opulent barge sank". He had sired six trueborn sons, three daughters, and sixteen bastards — quote: "he had sired six trueborn sons, three daughters, and sixteen bastards".

### Drazenko Rogare
Brother of Lysandro and architect of the Dornish alliance, he wed Princess Aliandra Martell and was named Prince Consort and Lord of the Stepstones — quote: "Drazenko married Princess Aliandra Martell of Dorne, and was named by her Prince Consort". He died at Sunspear, choking on a piece of bacon — quote: "Drazenko perished first, choking to death upon a piece of bacon".

### Lysaro Rogare
Lysandro's eldest son took effective control of the Rogare Bank — quote: "Effective control of the bank, however, was vested in Lysandro’s eldest son". Said to have twice his father's ambition and half his ability, he bought a thousand Unsullied and diverted the bank's gold — quote: "he had twice his father's ambition and half his father's ability". His scheme collapsed and he fled Lys in the night, leaving the bank a hollow shell — quote: "he fled Lys in the dead of night with three bed slaves".

### Moredo Rogare
Larra's brother commanded her guards and bore a Valyrian steel longsword called Truth — quote: "a longsword of Valyrian steel he called Truth". Given command of the Vale army despite poor Common Tongue, his appointment was deeply unpopular — quote: "the Lyseni's appointment was deeply unpopular". When his translator was slain in Gulltown he could not command his troops — quote: "Moredo Rogare had great difficulty communicating with his own troops". He later yielded his command and took ship for Braavos — quote: "yielding up his command to Alyn Oakenfist and taking ship for Braavos".

### Roggerio Rogare
The youngest brother opened the opulent Mermaid pillow house by the River Gate — quote: "opened an opulent Lysene pillow house called the Mermaid beside the River Gate". He was marched at spearpoint through a jeering crowd when the Rogares fell — quote: "Lord Roggerio was marched at spearpoint through a jeering crowd".

### Lotho Rogare
He established a branch of the Rogare Bank atop Visenya's Hill — quote: "establishing a branch of the Rogare Bank atop Visenya’s Hill". He was arrested by the gold cloaks as he tried to leave King's Landing — quote: "Lotho Rogare was arrested by Ser Lucas Leygood and his gold cloaks".

### Sandoq the Shadow
A towering mute from the fighting pits of Meereen, given to Larra by her father Lysandro — quote: "a gift from her father the Magister Lysandro". His lips and tongue had been cut away, leaving him mute — quote: "his lips and tongue had been removed, leaving him both mute and hideous". At Maegor's Holdfast he slew Ser Amaury Peake's men and finally the knight himself with the prince's axe — quote: "split Ser Amaury's head and helm in half from crest to gorget".

### Gaemon Palehair
The bastard boy "King Cunny" served Aegon III as cupbearer, food taster and friend — quote: "served Aegon III as cupbearer, whipping boy, and friend". He was poisoned by tainted apple tarts and died within the hour, thought but nine — quote: "He was thought to be but nine years old at his death".

### Munkun
The Grand Maester declared the supper poisoned and saved the queen with a purgative — quote: "Munkun soon declared that both he and the little queen were the victims of poisoning". He determined the poison had been baked into the apple tarts — quote: "the poison had been baked into the crust of the apple tarts". He named the era the "Rogare Ascendency," a term used only at Oldtown — quote: "Munkun refers to this period as the Rogare Ascendency".

### Amaury Peake
Ser Amaury came to Maegor's Holdfast to seize Larra for questioning — quote: "Your lady is wanted for questioning in connection with the treason of her brothers". Refusing to be balked by two boys, he was slain by Sandoq at the drawbridge — quote: "his dying did not disgrace the Kingsguard".

### Marston Waters
The Kingsguard Lord Commander was made the new King's Hand after Rowan's removal — quote: "Ser Marston Waters is the new King's Hand". He swore to protect the king, but Aegon reminded him he had watched his mother die — quote: "I swear upon my sword in the sight of gods and men that none shall do you harm".

### Robert Rowan
Lord Thaddeus's eldest son led the host up the kingsroad into the Mountains of the Moon — quote: "under the command of Ser Robert Rowan, Lord Thaddeus’s eldest son". He was crushed by a falling boulder toppled by the clansmen — quote: "crushed by a falling boulder when the clansmen toppled half a mountainside".

### Benjicot Blackwood
"Bloody Ben" led a thousand Blackwoods and had much experience of war despite his youth — quote: "as much experience of war as men four times his age". He assumed command of the host after Robert Rowan's death — quote: "Bloody Ben Blackwood assumed command upon his death".

### Nettles
A ragged woman seen clinging to the back of the dragon Sheepstealer in the mountains — quote: "with “a ragged woman clinging to its back.”". The wildlings' legend tells of a "fire witch" worshipped by a mountain clan — quote: "tell tales of a “fire witch” who once dwelled in a hidden vale".

### Gunthor Royce
The Bronze Giant of Runestone, old and stubborn, rode out in bronze armor to confront Corbray — quote: "Lord Gunthor donned his ancient bronze armor and rode out to confront him".

### Aliandra Martell
The Princess of Dorne wed Drazenko Rogare and named him Prince Consort and Lord of the Stepstones — quote: "when his brother Drazenko married Princess Aliandra Martell of Dorne".

### Daenaera Velaryon
The king's little queen was poisoned alongside Gaemon but saved by Munkun's purgative — quote: "gave Daenaera a powerful purgative, which most like saved her life". She was so delighted by Morning that she wished for a dragon of her own — quote: "she wanted a dragon of her own".

### Toron Greyjoy
The boy upon the Seastone Chair, seized by the Red Kraken's sisters, who put his mother to death — quote: "seized Toron Greyjoy, the boy upon the Seastone Chair, and put his mother to death". He held the Chair when his defenders beat off the Lannister assault on Pyke — quote: "Toron Greyjoy remained upon the Seastone Chair".

### Rodrik Greyjoy
Toron's half-brother, raised up by Harlaw and Blacktyde, was taken to Casterly Rock — quote: "his cousins joined with the lords of Harlaw and Blacktyde to raise up Toron’s halfbrother Rodrik". He was gelded and made Lady Johanna's son's fool — quote: "had him gelded and made him her son's fool".
