## Node Prose

### Aenys Targaryen
King Aenys, obsessed with building the Red Keep, declared his descendants would rule from it for a thousand years — quote: "My descendants shall rule from here for a thousand years". In 41 AC he announced the marriage of his daughter Rhaena to her brother Aegon, whom he named heir — quote: "give the hand of his daughter Rhaena in marriage to her brother Aegon". Infamous for indecision, he neither abandoned the match nor burned the Starry Sept, but simply persisted — quote: "he stiffened and grew stubborn". At the wedding feast he granted Aegon the title Prince of Dragonstone, which had belonged to Maegor — quote: "granting the title Prince of Dragonstone to his presumptive heir, Prince Aegon". Denounced as "King Abomination," he fled to Dragonstone, fell sick with fear, and died — quote: "Three days later, the king was dead".

### Rhaena Targaryen
Rhaena, eldest child of Aenys at eighteen, wed her brother Aegon and rode the dragon Dreamfyre, once declaring herself free to fly where she willed — quote: "free to fly". It was whispered that on one flight she took a lowborn lover, though it was never proved — quote: "It was whispered (though never proved)". She defied the Poor Fellows who pelted the royal couple — quote: "You are fearless when facing a girl on a horse, | see". Sheltered at Casterly Rock, she grew great with child and gave birth to twin daughters, then begged Aegon to take the family across the narrow sea — quote: "I would gladly give up my own life to make you king".

### Aegon Targaryen (son of Aenys)
Aegon, fifteen and heir presumptive, was reckoned the best young lance in the realm and the image of his grandsire — quote: "the best young lance in all the realm". He wed his sister Rhaena and was named Prince of Dragonstone, though he had never ridden a dragon — quote: "As Prince Aegon had not yet ridden a dragon". Besieged and abandoned, he was mocked as his father's weak son and came to be called "the pretender" and "Aegon the Uncrowned" — quote: "being called "the pretender" and "Aegon the Uncrowned."". He refused Rhaena's plea to flee, determined to claim his birthright — quote: "Prince Aegon was determined to claim his birthright".

### Maegor Targaryen
Maegor, son of Visenya, returned from across the narrow sea on Balerion after Aenys's death and claimed the crown with his father's iron crown — quote: "the iron crown of their father set with its blood-red rubies". He declared the throne goes to the strongest and beheaded Grand Maester Gawen who objected — quote: "The Iron Throne will go to the man who has the strength to seize it". He won the Trial of Seven against the Warrior's Sons, though grievously wounded — quote: "The victory was his". Recovering, he burned seven hundred Warrior's Sons in their sept, waged war on the Faith Militant, took Tyanna as his third wife, and slew Grand Maester Myros with Blackfyre — quote: "drew Blackfyre, and slew him where he stood".

### Visenya Targaryen
The Dowager Queen Visenya, mother of Maegor, urged Aenys to abandon the marriage or burn the Starry Sept, and stalked from the wedding feast when Aegon was named Prince of Dragonstone — quote: "Queen Visenya rose and stalked from the hall without the king's leave". Confined by Aenys to Sea Dragon Tower, she flew east on Vhagar within an hour of his death and returned with Maegor — quote: "Prince Maegor was with her, on Balerion". She crowned her son, commanded loyalist forces, and later burned the seats of five pious riverlands houses in a single night — quote: "were set aflame". She was among those rumored to have caused the High Septon's death — quote: "lay the High Septon's death at the door of another rumored sorceress".

### Alyssa Velaryon
Queen Alyssa, widow of Aenys, sang his dirge at Dragonstone and took her younger children to her father's castle on Driftmark within hours of the funeral — quote: "crossing to her lord father's castle on nearby Driftmark". She proclaimed her son Aegon the true king, but few heeded her — quote: "proclaimed her own son Aegon the true king, but few heeded her call". Persuaded by Visenya, she returned to court, did homage to Maegor, and was compelled to join his bedding — quote: "did homage to Maegor as the true king".

### Tyanna of the Tower
Tyanna, a pale raven-haired beauty from Pentos, arrived with Alys Harroway and six hundred sellswords — quote: "a pale raven-haired beauty known only as Tyanna of the Tower". The natural daughter of a Pentoshi magister and a courtesan risen from tavern dancer, she was rumored a poisoner and sorceress; some called her Maegor's concubine, others Alys's paramour — quote: "rumored to be a poisoner and sorceress". Given charge of the dying king, she nursed him to recovery, became his third wife, and was called "the mistress of whispers" — quote: ""The mistress of whispers," Tyanna was called".

### Alys Harroway
Alys of House Harroway, daughter of Lucas, was Maegor's second wife; her father demanded the royal couple acknowledge her as their uncle's lawful wife — quote: "acknowledge his daughter Alys as their uncle's true and lawful wife". She returned from Pentos with Tyanna and presided over Maegor's bedding with Tyanna — quote: "a bedding ceremony presided over by the king's second wife, Alys Harroway".

### Ceryse Hightower
Ceryse of House Hightower, Maegor's first wife, insisted throughout that she remained his only lawful queen — quote: "she was Maegor's only lawful queen". Grand Maester Myros invoked her before Maegor killed him — quote: "Your one true wife awaits you in the Hightower".

### High Septon
The High Septon at the Starry Sept condemned the sibling marriage as an obscenity and called Aenys "King Abomination" — quote: "declaring him a pretender and a tyrant". An Iron Bank envoy called him the true king of Westeros in all but name — quote: "the true king of Westeros, in all but name". Renowned for his tireless strength at three-and-fifty, he died suddenly in Oldtown on the night the dragons came, his cause of death debated — quote: "Its causes are debated to this day".

### Warrior's Sons
The Warrior's Sons, an order in silver armor, lined the wedding streets, fortified the Sept of Remembrance, and seized the gates of King's Landing — quote: "The Warrior's Sons seized the gates of King's Landing". Seven of their finest died in the Trial of Seven, and seven hundred were burned at prayer by Maegor on Balerion — quote: "the cream of the Warrior's Sons meet their fiery end". They later chose Joffrey Doggett as new grand captain — quote: "chosen a new grand captain".

### Poor Fellows
The Poor Fellows, ragged followers of the Faith, hacked Septon Murmison to pieces, pelted the royal couple, and besieged Crakehall — quote: "hacked him to pieces with their axes". They marched on King's Landing under Wat the Hewer and Ser Horys Hill and were shattered at Stonebridge and the Great Fork — quote: "Thousands of Poor Fellows streamed toward King's Landing".

### Wat the Hewer
Wat the Hewer, a gigantic axeman, led nine thousand Poor Fellows caught crossing the Mander and cut to pieces at Stonebridge — quote: "a gigantic axeman called Wat the Hewer". Taken alive after slaying Lord Meadows and others, Maegor took off his limbs with his own axe but kept him alive to witness the royal wedding — quote: "Maegor took off his limbs with the giant's own axe".

### Damon Morrigen
Ser Damon Morrigen, called Damon the Devout, was Grand Captain of the Warrior's Sons who led seven hundred knights down the Hill of Rhaenys and fought Maegor in the Trial of Seven, where he died — quote: "led by their grand captain, Ser Damon Morrigen".

### Dick Bean
Dick Bean, a simple man-at-arms and no knight, was the first to stand beside Maegor at the Trial and the first to die — quote: "a simple man-at-arms who called himself Dick Bean". He declared he meant to die a king's man — quote: "I mean to die a king's man".

### Bernarr Brune
Ser Bernarr Brune, the squire who had slain Harren the Red and been knighted by Aenys, shamed the crowd into offering their swords for Maegor — quote: "the squire who had slain Harren the Red and been knighted by King Aenys himself".

### Grand Maester Gawen
Grand Maester Gawen reported Aenys's sickness and alone objected to Maegor's usurpation, citing the laws of inheritance — quote: "the Iron Throne should pass to King Aenys's son Aegon". Maegor beheaded him with Blackfyre — quote: "taking off Gawen's old grey head himself with a single swing of Blackfyre".

### Myros
Grand Maester Myros alone dared speak openly against Maegor's marriage to Tyanna and was slain on the spot — quote: "drew Blackfyre, and slew him where he stood".

### Septon Murmison
Septon Murmison, the King's Hand, performed the forbidden nuptials and was expelled from the Faith, then killed by Poor Fellows in his litter — quote: "Septon Murmison, the King's Hand, was expelled from the Faith".

### Lyman Lannister
Lord Lyman Lannister of Casterly Rock offered Aegon and Rhaena his protection and stood firm against Maegor's demand to return them in chains — quote: "where Lord Lyman Lannister offered them his protection".

### Martyn Hightower
Lord Martyn Hightower, new Lord of the Hightower and voice of Oldtown after his father Manfred's death, called his banners as the dragons approached, then swiftly disarmed the Warrior's Sons — including his own brother — and opened the gates — quote: "he dispatched his own knights to disarm and arrest the Warrior's Sons".

### Morgan Hightower
Ser Morgan Hightower, younger brother of Lord Martyn, led two hundred Warrior's Sons to defend the Starry Sept and was among those suspected of the High Septon's death — quote: "Ser Morgan Hightower did the deed at the command of his lord brother, some say".

### Joffrey Doggett
Ser Joffrey Doggett, the Red Dog of the Hills, was chosen new grand captain of the Warrior's Sons and gathered two thousand men; Maegor later burned his family's seat and killed his kin — quote: "Ser Joffrey Doggett, the Red Dog of the Hills".

### Septon Pater
Septon Pater, ninety, blind, stooped and feeble but amiable, was elected the new High Septon on a single ballot and blessed Maegor as king — quote: "the wise men and women of the Faith turned to a certain Septon Pater".

### Horys Hill
Ser Horys Hill, a hedge knight, led the western Poor Fellows and reached the Great Fork with thirteen thousand men; he escaped the battle but his following dwindled — quote: "Ser Horys Hill had reached the Great Fork of the Blackwater".

### Lucas Harroway
Lord Lucas Harroway of Harrenhal, father of Alys, refused the royal couple entry to his castle and was later made Maegor's Hand in 43 AC — quote: "Maegor made Lord Lucas Harroway, father of his wife Queen Alys, his new Hand".

### Rupert Falwell
Lord Rupert Falwell, "the Fighting Fool," led the pious at the Great Fork and slew two Kingsguard before he was cut down by the Lord of Maidenpool — quote: "The Fighting Fool slew two knights of the Kingsguard".

### Jocasta Lannister
Lady Jocasta, wife of Lyman Lannister, first discerned that Princess Rhaena was with child — quote: "who first discerned that Princess Rhaena was with child".
