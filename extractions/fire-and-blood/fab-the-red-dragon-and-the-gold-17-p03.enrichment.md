## Node Prose
### Rhaenyra Targaryen
The queen, in possession of city, castle, and throne, defended by six dragons — quote: "defended by no fewer than six dragons." She appointed Bartimos Celtigar her lord treasurer and master of coin to meet a desperate need for coin — quote: "her lord treasurer and master of coin: her longtime supporter Bartimos Celtigar." She replenished her coffers at grievous cost, and Kingslanders came to name her cruel — quote: "a queen as cruel as any king before her." One wit dubbed her "King Maegor with teats" — quote: "One wit named Rhaenyra “King Maegor with teats”." She sent for her sons and made Aegon the Younger her cupbearer, and planned Joffrey's installation as Prince of Dragonstone and heir — quote: "Joffrey's formal installation as / Prince of Dragonstone and heir to the Iron Throne." Every time she sat the Iron Throne, its blades drew fresh blood from her — quote: "its cruel blades drew fresh blood from her hands and arms and legs." She rejected all peace terms and demanded her half-brothers' heads on spikes — quote: "the blood of / my sweet sons is on their hands."

### Prince Daemon Targaryen
The queen's consort; Munkun says the queen decided in favor of Lord Corlys rather than Prince Daemon out of fear of losing lords' support — quote: "led the queen to decide in favor of Lord Corlys rather than Prince Daemon." He remained in Mysaria's thrall and called on her every evening — quote: "Prince Daemon remained in her thrall, and called upon her every evening." He echoed the queen against pardons, urging traitors' heads on spikes — quote: "The war will end when the heads of the traitors are mounted on spikes." He would take Caraxes to the Trident with Nettles and Sheepstealer to destroy Aemond and Vhagar — quote: "take Caraxes to the Trident, together with the girl Nettles and Sheepstealer."

### Corlys Velaryon
The Sea Snake, the queen's Hand; Munkun says the queen decided in his favor over Daemon — quote: "to decide in favor of Lord Corlys rather than Prince Daemon." He counseled peace: pardons to Lords Baratheon, Hightower, and Lannister, and sending the usurper's brothers to the Wall — quote: "Spare them, and send them to the Wall." He was horrified at granting Storm's End and Casterly Rock to White and Hammer — quote: "Half the lords of Westeros will turn against us if we are so cruel."

### Bartimos Celtigar
Lord of Claw Isle, the queen's master of coin, called staunch, incorruptible, ingenious, and wealthy — quote: "he was unrelenting, incorruptible, and ingenious, all agreed." He restored his ancestor Lord Edwell's taxes and heaped on new levies, doubling wine and ale taxes and tripling port fees — quote: "Taxes on wine and ale were doubled, port fees tripled." He made executions a source of coin in the Dragonpit at three pennies to enter — quote: "each must pay three pennies at the gates to be admitted."

### Aegon II Targaryen
Rhaenyra's half-brother and rival king; he had seized the treasury with the crown, leaving her empty vaults — quote: "Aegon II had seized the treasury along with the crown." He had Lord Caswell beheaded for refusing to renounce the queen — quote: "beheaded by Aegon II at King's Landing when he refused to renounce the queen." He remained alive and free, the king Criston Cole had made — quote: "Aegon II, the king he had made, remained alive and free."

### Tyland Lannister
Aegon II's master of coin, who shipped away three-quarters of Viserys's wealth "for safekeeping" — quote: "had shipped off threequarters of the late king's wealth “for safekeeping.”"

### Mysaria
The White Worm, a Lysene harlot who came from the shadows to reside in the Red Keep, known as Lady Misery, mistress of whisperers in all but name — quote: "became the mistress of whisperers in all but name." Prince Daemon remained in her thrall — quote: "Prince Daemon remained in her thrall." Mushroom claims she proposed selling Alicent and Helaena in a brothel — quote: "sold to any man who wished to have his pleasure of them."

### Rickard Thorne
Ser of the Kingsguard, Maelor's sworn shield, who disguised himself as a traveler with a staff and a boy on his back — quote: "a man appeared from the north with a staff in one hand and a small boy on his back." When his pack was searched and a dragon's egg found, he opened Ben Buttercakes from neck to groin and cut his way out — quote: "opened Buttercakes from neck to groin." Crossbows brought him down on the old stone bridge — quote: "One bolt took him in the arm, the next through the throat." He clung to Maelor to the end — quote: "To the end he clung to the boy he had sworn to defend."

### Maelor Targaryen
The younger son of King Aegon II, carried by Rickard Thorne disguised as the knight's "son" — quote: "the traveler's “son” was Maelor Targaryen, the younger son of King Aegon II." A washerwoman, Willow Pound-Stone, tore him from the dying knight's arms — quote: "tore the weeping prince from his arms." His manner of death is disputed; all that is certain is he was dead before Lady Caswell arrived — quote: "by the time Lady Caswell and her knights appeared... the prince was dead." His head was sent to King's Landing with the knight's corpse — quote: "together with Prince Maelor's head."

### Ben Buttercakes
Proprietor of the Hogs Head, a scoundrel who set his stableboy to search the traveler's pack for silver — quote: "his host had instructed his stableboy, known to us only as Sly, to search his pack for silver." He got no joy of his deceit, slain by Rickard Thorne — quote: "Ben Buttercakes got no joy from his deceit."

### Sly
The Hogs Head stableboy who found the white cloak and dragon's egg, then claimed the prince as his prize — quote: "to declare the prince was his, as he'd been the one to find the egg." Lady Caswell had him hanged from the bridge — quote: "Sly the stableboy and Willow Pound-Stone were hanged from the center span."

### Willow Pound-Stone
A washerwoman who took Maelor from the dying knight, calling him her new son — quote: "no one was going to hurt her new son." Mushroom calls her a monster of thirty stone, simpleminded and half-mad — quote: "a monster thirty stone in weight, simpleminded and half-mad." Mushroom says she crushed the boy to death; Lady Caswell had her hanged — quote: "she broke his back and crushed him to death."

### Lady Caswell
The widowed lady of Bitterbridge, whose husband Aegon II beheaded; she closed her gates against refugees — quote: "closed her castle gates, turning away even anointed knights and lords." She hanged those responsible for Maelor's death and sent the knight's corpse and the prince's head to King's Landing — quote: "along with the man who had owned the horse." When Bitterbridge was sacked she yielded, begging mercy for her children before hanging herself — quote: "Have mercy on my children, lord." Her young sons and daughter were spared and sent in chains to Oldtown — quote: "her ladyship's young sons and daughter were / spared."

### Ormund Hightower
Lord leading the great southron army, besieging Longtable then advancing on King's Landing — quote: "the great southron army led by Lord Ormund Hightower." He spared Lady Merryweather but stripped Longtable of wealth and food — quote: "he did strip her castle of its wealth and every scrap of food." He let Prince Daeron pronounce Bitterbridge's doom and had the town burned — quote: "You shall receive the same terms you gave my nephew Maelor." He was slain by Roderick Dustin at Tumbleton — quote: "slaying both Ser Bryndon and Lord Ormund before he died."

### Prince Daeron
Daeron the Daring, Aemond's younger brother, riding Tessarion as scout for Ormund's host — quote: "Flying Tessarion ahead of the main column, Prince Daeron had proved invaluable / as a scout." He named Maelor his nephew and answered Lady Caswell with vengeance — quote: "the same terms you gave my nephew Maelor." He was named the greatest threat to Rhaenyra, above Aemond — quote: "not Aemond One-Eye, but his younger brother, Prince / Daeron the Daring."

### Criston Cole
Ser, King Aegon's Hand, the Kingmaker, who abandoned Harrenhal with thirty-six-hundred men — quote: "Ser Criston Cole abandoned Harrenhal." Beset by fires and ambushes, he found the Lords of the Trident waiting on a stony ridge and rode out under a peace banner — quote: "Calling for a peace banner, King Aegon's Hand rode out to treat with them." He was shot down at the parley, three arrows in belly, neck, and breast — quote: "striking Cole in belly, neck, and breast." His head was later borne on a spear to Tumbleton — quote: "with the head of Ser Criston Cole upon a spear."

### Prince Aemond
Aemond One-Eye, the Kinslayer, freed of host and castle to wage war with dragonflame on Vhagar — quote: "the oneeyed prince was free to fly where he would." He burned House Darry, Lord Harroway's Town, and many villages, becoming the terror of the Trident — quote: "House Darry was the first to know the prince's wroth." He returned to burn Harrenhal and escaped with Alys Rivers — quote: "her prize captive, the witch woman Alys Rivers, escaped with Prince Aemond."

### Alys Rivers
Wet nurse and purported witch who warmed Aemond's bed at Harrenhal and claimed to carry his child — quote: "now claimed to be carrying his child." She was captured by Sabitha Frey, then escaped with Aemond — quote: "I have the dragon's bastard in me."

### Sabitha Frey
Lady who seized empty Harrenhal three days after Criston Cole left, finding Alys Rivers within — quote: "Lady Sabitha Frey swooped down to seize it." She saved herself from Aemond's fire in a privy and fled to the Twins — quote: "only saved herself from the flames by hiding in a privy."

### Garibald Grey
Ser who met Criston Cole at the parley, vowing to build a sept of traitors' bones — quote: "I would build a sept for them out of / traitors' bones." He commanded the queen's men and called the rout "butchery, not battle" — quote: "Today was butchery, not battle." He led the queen's men again at Tumbleton — quote: "under the command of Ser Garibald Grey."

### Pate of Longleaf
Longleaf the Lionslayer, who had cut down Jason Lannister; at the parley he called for archers and mocked the dying Kingmaker — quote: "the Lionslayer who had cut down Jason Lannister." He declared he'd have no songs of Cole's brave death — quote: "I'll have no songs about how brave you died, Kingmaker."

### Roderick Dustin
Roddy the Ruin, Lord of Barrowton, who bore scars from the Fishfeed and led the Winter Wolves — quote: "Roddy the Ruin, bearing the scars he had taken at the Fishfeed." At Tumbleton, though his shield arm was struck off, he slew both Bryndon Hightower and Lord Ormund before dying — quote: "the savage Lord of Barrowton fought on, slaying both Ser Bryndon and Lord Ormund."

### Red Robb Rivers
Bowman whose arrows, with two archers, struck down Criston Cole at the parley — quote: "Red Robb Rivers and two of his archers raised their longbows." He covered the queen's retreat at Tumbleton with his bowmen — quote: "There Red Robb Rivers and / his bowmen stood, covering the retreat."

### Byron Swann
Ser, second son of the Lord of Stonehelm, who set out to slay a dragon as Serwyn slew Urrax — quote: "he set out to slay a dragon just as Serwyn did." The accounts dispute which dragon; all agree the ruse brought only his death — quote: "brought only death for Ser Byron Swann." The dragon melted his mirrored shield and roasted him — quote: "melting the mirrored shield and roasting the man crouched behind it."

### Hugh Hammer
Hard Hugh Hammer, a dragonrider knighted and granted a small holding on Driftmark; Mushroom says he beat a household knight to death over a virgin — quote: "beating one of the queen's household knights to death in a brothel." He rode Vermithor or Silverwing to Tumbleton and there changed his allegiance, one of the Two Betrayers — quote: "the Two Betrayers (as history has named them)."

### Ulf White
A dragonrider knighted and granted a small holding on Driftmark; Mushroom says he rode drunk through Flea Bottom clad only in golden spurs — quote: "clad in naught but his golden spurs." He turned traitor at Tumbleton, one of the Two Betrayers — quote: "also / chosen this moment to change their allegiance."

### Alicent
The Dowager Queen, the Queen in Chains, Aegon II's mother, who went to the Iron Throne on her knees to plead for peace and division of the realm — quote: "Queen Alicent went to the Iron Throne upon her knees, to plead for peace." She named the murdered boys innocent and warned against the queen's thirst for vengeance — quote: "My son's sons were innocent boys, cruelly murdered."

### Dalton Greyjoy
The Red Kraken, who took Faircastle and Fair Isle, claimed four of Lord Farman's daughters as salt wives and gave the fifth to his brother Veron — quote: "The Red Kraken claimed four of Lord Farman's daughters as salt wives."

### Larys Strong
The Clubfoot, most enigmatic member of the green council, who vanished — quote: "the most enigmatic and cunning member of the green council, had vanished." Mushroom puts the false chamberpot tale down as the Clubfoot's work — quote: "A man who gathers whispers can spread them just as well."

### Bryndon Hightower
Ser, Lord Ormund's cousin, who put himself between Roderick Dustin and his liege and took off the Ruin's shield arm — quote: "taking off the Ruin's shield arm at the shoulder with one terrible blow." He was slain by Roderick Dustin at Tumbleton — quote: "slaying both Ser Bryndon and Lord Ormund."
