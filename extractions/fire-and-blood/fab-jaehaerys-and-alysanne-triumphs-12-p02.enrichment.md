## Node Prose

### Rhaena Targaryen
Rhaena returned from Estermont after her daughter's death, reaching Greenstone by raven only after the princess was already dead and burned — quote: "the princess had already died and been burned." Dreamfyre bore her and the ashes to the Red Keep, and she scattered the ashes from dragonback rather than inter them on Dragonstone — quote: "she took her child’s ashes high into" / "scattered them upon the winds." She refused Dragonstone, Greenstone, a council seat, and any new husband — quote: "| fed my last husband / to my dragon." Jaehaerys settled her at Harrenhal, in the Widow's Tower — quote: "the aptly named Widow's Tower of Harrenhal." She flew to Oldtown yearly to see her daughter Rhaella, and the riverlands smallfolk feared her as a witch — quote: "the smallfolk of the riverlands feared her as a witch." She died in 73 AC at fifty and was burned at Harrenhal — quote: "Rhaena Targaryen died in 73 AC, at fifty years of age."

### Alysanne Targaryen
Alysanne bore Baelon in 57 AC and pressed the king that Daenerys, being older, should be queen — quote: "She is first in line; she should be queen." She won the wells and "queen's fountains" for King's Landing by daring the council to drink river water — quote: "served each of them a tankard of river water." She made the northern progress alone, charming White Harbor and eventually Winterfell, and won the Night's Watch by funding Deep Lake with her jewels — quote: "She would pay for the castle herself." From tales heard at Mole's Town she moved the council to abolish the lord's right to the first night — quote: "it is time we put an end to this. | beg you."

### Jaehaerys I Targaryen
Jaehaerys decreed all dragons be guarded night and day, creating the Dragonkeepers — quote: "the king decreed that all the dragons should be guarded night and day." He named Aemon his heir from birth while planning Aemon and Daenerys should marry and rule together — quote: "She shall be queen, when she and Aemon marry." He dismissed Myles Smallwood and made Septon Barth his Hand — quote: "This time he would have the Hand he wanted: Septon Barth." He mediated the Treaty of Eternal Peace between Tyrosh and Pentos and, at his tenth anniversary, was "every inch a king" — quote: "every inch a king."

### Aemon Targaryen
Aemon was a serious, cautious, obedient boy who loved being read to, two years older than Baelon — quote: "a very serious boy, cautious, careful, and obedient." His father spoke of him as heir from birth — quote: "the king began to speak of him as his heir."

### Baelon Targaryen
Baelon, born 57 AC, was dubbed the Spring Prince when white ravens announced spring two days before his birth — quote: "Baelon was immediately dubbed the Spring Prince." He followed and imitated Aemon everywhere and made a stick-sword to join his training — quote: "Baelon followed his brother, Aemon, everywhere." According to report he struck Balerion on the snout, earning the name Baelon the Brave — quote: "reportedly smote Balerion on the snout."

### Daenerys Targaryen
Daenerys, eldest of the three children, was a lively, laughing child who "flew" on a broomstick dragon — quote: "a lively, laughing child who bounced about the Red Keep." She delighted in her brothers, "especially in telling them what to do."

### Barth
Septon Barth, low-born son of a smith, was made Hand and sent to Braavos to treat with the Sealord over three stolen dragon eggs — quote: "We are speaking of / three eggs." He returned with no egg but with the Iron Bank's forgiveness of the Crown's loan — quote: "the Iron Bank of Braavos forgave the entire remaining principal." He proposed the wells and clean-water works, and finally argued the queen's case against the first night — quote: "I believe Her Grace has the right of this."

### Sealord of Braavos
The Sealord bargained with Barth, denying possession of the eggs while offering gold and the loan's forgiveness — quote: "so instead of stones, let me offer...gold." He met the king's veiled dragon-threat with a veiled threat of his own about a certain guild of assassins — quote: "But they could kill...a few. A well-chosen few."

### Maegor Towers
Maegor Towers, last of his line and named for Maegor the Cruel, dwelt sickly and near-alone at Harrenhal — quote: "Maegor Towers was the last of his line, and sickly and impoverished." We are told he and Rhaena came to forge a friendship; he died in 61 AC — quote: "After his death in 61 AC."

### Theomore Manderly
Lord Theomore Manderly feasted Alysanne lavishly at White Harbor with a roasted aurochs and a tourney — quote: "an entire aurochs was roasted." His Reach-born house keeps the Seven and knighthood though loyal to the Starks — quote: "still worshipped the Seven and kept the traditions of knighthood."

### Jonquil Darke
Jonquil Darke, the Scarlet Shadow, was Alysanne's sworn shield and dueled the captured wildling girl spear against sword — quote: "the wildling and the Scarlet Shadow dueled spear against sword."

### Alaric Stark
Lord Alaric Stark, a flinty widower, greeted Alysanne coldly, refused to have her dragon within his walls and rebuffed southern marriages for his sons — quote: "he did not want her dragon inside his walls." He warmed to her over time and later assented to the doubling of the Gift, but blamed the king for his brother Walton's death — quote: "Walton lies down here in darkness in no small part thanks to you." He keeps the old gods — quote: "We keep the old gods in the North."

### Walton Stark
Walton Stark, Alaric's brother, died putting down oathbreakers among the men Jaehaerys had sent to the Wall — quote: "it cost my brother's life to / put them down."

### Lothor Burley
Lord Commander Lothor Burley assembled eight hundred men to receive the queen and showed her the top of the Wall — quote: "assembled eight hundred of his finest men." He renamed Snowgate as Queensgate in her honor — quote: "renamed Snowgate castle in her / honor, as Queensgate."

### Rego Draz
Rego Draz, the king's master of coin, reckoned the freed Braavosi coin could fund improvements to King's Landing — quote: "the king’s shrewd master of coin, / Rego Draz."

### Orys Baratheon
Orys Baratheon was cited as a dragonseed, a bastard brother to the king's grandsire, fathered by Lord Aerion — quote: "Orys Baratheon himself was a dragonseed."

### Ryam Redwyne
Ser Ryam Redwyne, youngest son of the lord admiral Manfryd Redwyne, unhorsed six opponents and crowned Alysanne queen of love and beauty — quote: "crowned Good Queen Alysanne as / his queen of love and beauty."

### Albin Massey
The justiciar Lord Albin Massey argued the first night was older than the Andals, a First Men custom of a warlord's blessing — quote: "The practice is an ancient one, older than the Andals."
