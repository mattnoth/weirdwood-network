## Node Prose

### Rhaenyra Targaryen
Crowned on Dragonstone by her husband Daemon with the Old King's stolen crown before three hundred witnesses, proclaimed Queen of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men — quote: "placed the Old King’s crown on the head of his wife". Her first act as queen was to declare Otto Hightower and Queen Alicent traitors and rebels — quote: "declare Ser Otto Hightower and Queen Alicent traitors and rebels". She named her eldest son Jacaerys her heir — quote: "named her eldest son, Jacaerys, the Prince of Dragonstone and heir". Hearing Aegon's peace terms in silence, she stripped Orwyle's chain and dismissed his embassy with a threat — quote: "I will have my throne, or | will have his head". She collapsed on learning of Luke's death — quote: "Queen Rhaenyra collapsed when told of Luke's death".

### Daemon Targaryen
Rhaenyra's husband; crowned her and claimed for himself the style Protector of the Realm — quote: "The prince claimed for himself the style Protector of the Realm". Flying Caraxes to the Trident, he took Harrenhal when its castellan struck banners, capturing House Strong's wealth and a dozen hostages — quote: "Prince Daemon at a stroke had captured the not-inconsiderable wealth of House Strong". In his youth he had been the "Prince of the City," known in Flea Bottom — quote: "Daemon Targaryen had been the “Prince of the City”". After Luke's death he sent a raven promising vengeance — quote: "“An eye for an eye, a son for a son,” Prince Daemon wrote" — and reached out to a trusted pale go-between to set it in motion — quote: "To this pale stranger he reached\nout now, by secret ways".

### Jacaerys Velaryon
Rhaenyra's 14-year-old eldest son, named Prince of Dragonstone and heir; sent north as an envoy under sworn oath not to fight. Flying Vermax, he won the Vale from Lady Jeyne Arryn — quote: "Jacaerys Velaryon descended upon the Eyrie on his young dragon, Vermax". At White Harbor he sealed a compact betrothing his brother Joffrey to a Manderly daughter — quote: "a compact was drawn up and signed". At Winterfell he reached the Pact of Ice and Fire with Cregan Stark — quote: "the agreement that Grand Maester Munkun calls “the Pact of Ice and Fire”". He had been betrothed to his cousin Baela since he was four — quote: "betrothed to his cousin Baela since he was four".

### Lucerys Velaryon
Rhaenyra's 13-year-old second son; sent to Storm's End on Arrax on the "shorter, safer" mission. He found Aemond already there and addressed only Lord Borros — quote: "Lord Borros, | have brought you a message". Asked to marry a Baratheon daughter, he declined as betrothed — quote: "I am betrothed to my cousin Rhaena". He refused Aemond's challenge — quote: "I will not fight you. | came here as an envoy, not a knight". He died when Vhagar destroyed Arrax over Shipbreaker Bay — quote: "Lucerys Velaryon died with his dragon, Munkun insists".

### Aegon II Targaryen
King at 22, quick to anger; on hearing of Rhaenyra's coronation demanded his half-sister and uncle be attainted, arrested, and killed — quote: "My half-sister and my uncle are guilty of high treason". He grudgingly permitted Orwyle's peace embassy after his mother and wife urged it, then declared war after Rhaenyra's refusal — quote: "I! offered her an honorable peace, and the whore spat in my face". He welcomed Aemond home after Luke's death with a feast — quote: "Aegon II welcomed Prince Aemond home with a great feast". In grief and fury he had all the city's ratcatchers hanged — quote: "all the city's ratcatchers be taken out and hanged".

### Aemond Targaryen
Aegon II's brother, rider of Vhagar; came to Storm's End to win Borros's support and entertain a marriage suit. He confronted Lucerys, mocking him as "Little Luke Strong, the bastard" — quote: "“Little Luke Strong, the bastard.”". He demanded Luke's eye in payment, baring his sapphire — quote: "he tore off his eye patch and flung it to the floor, to show the sapphire beneath". Provoked by Maris, he pursued Luke on Vhagar and the dragons met above Shipbreaker Bay — quote: "Prince Aemond mounted Vhagar and went after him". Thereafter he was known to his foes as Aemond the Kinslayer — quote: "Aemond the Kinslayer to his foes".

### Otto Hightower
Ser Otto, Hand of the King and father of Alicent; branded the loyalist defectors "turncloaks" and offered a reward for their capture — quote: "(“turncloaks,” Ser Otto would name them, when offering a reward". He rebuked Aemond after Luke's death — quote: "You only lost one eye,” he is reported to have said". He later brought a hundred cats into the Red Keep after the ratcatchers were hanged — quote: "brought one hundred cats into the Red Keep".

### Grand Maester Orwyle
Green council maester who urged parley and was dispatched as Aegon's envoy — quote: "Send me to her, that we may talk". Confronted by Rhaenyra, he admitted King Viserys had named her heir — quote: "“You, Your Grace," Orwyle replied". Rhaenyra stripped his chain of office and had it given to Gerardys — quote: "stripped his chain of office from his neck".

### Maester Gerardys
Rhaenyra's own man, given Orwyle's chain of office and named a true servant of the realm — quote: "bestowed the chain upon her own man, Maester Gerardys".

### Corlys Velaryon
The Sea Snake; his ships sailed from Hull and Spicetown to close the Gullet and choke King's Landing's trade — quote: "the Sea Snake's ships set sail from Hull and Spicetown to close the Gullet". With Princess Rhaenys he kept Joffrey from mounting his dragon after Luke's death — quote: "the Sea Snake and Princess Rhaenys kept the boy from mounting his own dragon".

### Simon Strong
Elderly castellan of Harrenhal, uncle to the late Lord Lyonel and greatuncle to Lord Larys; he struck his banners rather than share Black Harren's fate when Caraxes landed — quote: "quick to strike his banners when Caraxes lighted atop Kingspyre Tower". He and his grandsons were taken hostage — quote: "amongst them Ser Simon and his grandsons".

### Alys Rivers
Wet nurse captured among the Harrenhal smallfolk; at least forty during the Dance, said to look younger than her years — quote: "Alys Rivers was at least forty years of age during the Dance". According to Munkun and Eustace she was sired by Lord Lyonel Strong, making her half-sister to Harwin and Larys — quote: "Munkun and Eustace tell us she was sired by Lord Lyonel Strong". Mushroom instead insists she was much older and had been wet nurse to both boys — quote: "Mushroom insists that she\nwas much older, that she was wet nurse to both boys". Daemon appeared immune to her supposed powers — quote: "Daemon Targaryen was immune to them".

### Grover Tully
Aged, bedridden Lord Paramount of the Trident who had spoken for the male claimant at the Great Council of 101 and insisted Riverrun fight for Aegon — quote: "Lord Grover insisted that Riverrun would fight for young King Aegon". His grandson Elmo overrode him, keeping Riverrun's gates barred — quote: "declared Ser Elmo Tully, his grandson".

### Jeyne Arryn
The Maiden of the Vale, 35 and never wed, ruling since her father and brothers were slain by the Stone Crows when she was three — quote: "The Maiden of the Vale, Lady Jeyne Arryn, was five-and-thirty". She held her cousin Arnold in a sky cell for saying women were too soft to rule — quote: "I have him in one of my sky cells". She pledged the Vale to Rhaenyra in exchange for dragonriders — quote: "Send me dragonriders". Mushroom's and Eustace's tales of her private life the narration declines to credit over Munkun — quote: "he alone confines himself to the High Hall of the Eyrie".

### Desmond Manderly
Lord of White Harbor, a shrewd bargainer who received Jacaerys in the Merman's Court and recalled the Old King's broken promise of Princess Viserra's hand to his great-grandsire — quote: "Lord Desmond Manderly met with him in his Merman’s Court". He sealed a compact for his youngest daughter to wed Joffrey after the war — quote: "Lord Manderly's youngest daughter would be wed to the prince's brother\nJoffrey".

### Cregan Stark
Lord of Winterfell at 21, who would become the Old Man of the North; came into his lordship at 13 on his father Rickon's death in 121 AC — quote: "upon the death of his father, Lord Rickon, in 121 AC". After his uncle Bennard ruled as regent, Cregan rose up in 126 AC and imprisoned Bennard and his three sons — quote: "Cregan Stark rose up, imprisoned Bennard and his three sons". He wed Arra Norrey, who died in 128 AC bearing his son Rickon — quote: "he wed Lady Arra Norrey, a beloved companion since childhood". He gave Jacaerys a warm welcome and sealed the Pact of Ice and Fire — quote: "Cregan Stark and Jacaerys Velaryon reached an accord". Munkun says they swore an oath of brotherhood, while Eustace claims Jacaerys spent his visit trying to convert Cregan to the Seven — quote: "swore an oath of brotherhood, sealed in blood".

### Sara Snow
A "wolf girl" of Mushroom's account, said to be a bastard daughter of the late Lord Rickon Stark. According to Mushroom, Jacaerys lay with her and wed her in Winterfell's godswood — quote: "with the name of Sara Snow". The narration doubts she existed and calls the marriage tale preposterous — quote: "to talk of\nmarriage is preposterous".

### Borros Baratheon
Lord of Storm's End, son of Boremund, likened to the changeable wind; father of four daughters he offered to Aemond — quote: "I have four daughters,” he told the prince". He rejected Rhaenyra's suit once Luke refused to marry a daughter — quote: "the Lord of Storm's End is not a dog that she can whistle up at need". Uneasy at bloodshed, he forbade it beneath his roof but let Aemond pursue Luke outside — quote: "It is not for me to tell you what to do when you are not\nbeneath my roof".

### Maris Baratheon
Borros's less comely secondborn daughter, angry at being passed over, who taunted Aemond and goaded him into pursuit — quote: "Was it one of your eyes he took, or one of your balls?".

### Helaena Targaryen
Queen, wife and sister of Aegon II, mother of Jaehaerys, Jaehaera, and Maelor; she brought her children each evening to see their grandmother — quote: "the custom of Queen Helaena to bring her children to see their grandmother". Cornered by Blood and Cheese and forced to choose a son to die, on her knees she named her youngest, Maelor — quote: "On her knees, weeping, Helaena named her youngest, Maelor". Blood then slew her heir Jaehaerys instead — quote: "the hulking swordsman slew Prince Jaehaerys".

### Alicent Hightower
Queen Dowager, mother of Aegon II; declared a traitor by Rhaenyra. She took residence in the Tower of the Hand after King Viserys's death — quote: "Queen Alicent had taken up residence there after the death of King Viserys". She went pale at Aemond's deed — quote: "Queen Alicent went pale". She commanded Larys Clubfoot to learn Blood's true name so she might bathe in his family's blood — quote: "Queen Alicent had commanded Larys Clubfoot to learn his true name".

### Mysaria
Daemon's trusted pale go-between, an older foreign-spoken woman called "Misery" by the harlots and known as the White Worm; never found despite the gold cloaks' search — quote: "The other harlots called her Misery".

### Blood
Former City Watch serjeant, big and brutal, who had lost his gold cloak for beating a whore to death; led by Cheese into the castle, he slew the guardsman and struck off Prince Jaehaerys's head — quote: "One had been a serjeant in the City Watch". Seized at the Gate of the Gods with the head, he confessed under torture before being allowed to die — quote: "Under torture, he confessed".

### Cheese
Ratcatcher of the Red Keep who knew its hidden doors and secret tunnels; he led Blood in unseen, bound Queen Alicent, and made the choice-demand of Helaena — quote: "The other was a ratcatcher in the Red Keep". He escaped and was never found — quote: "no trace of Cheese or the White Worm was ever found".

### Ser Steffon Darklyn
Late of Aegon's Kingsguard; a loyalist defector whose arrival with the stolen crown of Jaehaerys was greeted with joy on Dragonstone — quote: "The arrival of Ser Steffon Darklyn, late of Aegon's Kingsguard".

### Ser Arryk Cargyll
Of the Kingsguard, sworn to Aegon; twin of Erryk; in Orwyle's envoy retinue — quote: "Ser Arryk had sworn his sword to Aegon".

### Ser Erryk Cargyll
Twin of Arryk, sworn to Rhaenyra; subject with his brother of Luceon of Tarth's ballad — quote: "Ser Erryk to Rhaenyra".

### Ser Forrest Frey
"Fool Frey" of the Twins, who had once begged for Rhaenyra's hand and now rode to her cause — quote: "the very same “Fool Frey” who had once begged for Rhaenyra’s hand".

### Ser Amos Bracken
Won a duel for Rhaenyra's favor but followed his father when House Bracken declared for Aegon — quote: "followed his lord father when House Bracken declared for Aegon".

### Petyr Piper
Grizzled Lord of Pinkmaiden who kept his old oath to Rhaenyra — quote: "I still have the sword".

### Jaehaerys (son of Aegon II)
Aegon II's firstborn son and heir, six years old, twin of Jaehaera; killed by Blood, beheaded with a single blow — quote: "King Aegon’s firstborn son and heir".
