## Node Prose
### Aegon III Targaryen
The fourthborn son of Rhaenyra Targaryen, and her eldest by Prince Daemon, he came to the Iron Throne in 131 AC as a boy of ten and reigned twenty-six years until dying of consumption in 157 AC — quote: "Aegon came to the Iron Throne in 131 AC and reigned for twenty-six years". Grand Maester Munkun calls him the Broken King, and the smallfolk name him the Dragonbane; he was a joyless man who drank no wine and wore a hair shirt beneath his velvets — quote: "was known to wear a hair shirt under the velvets and satins required of a king". As his reign began he was confined to Maegor's Holdfast by Cregan Stark with only the boy Gaemon Palehair for company — quote: "found himself confined to Maegor's Holdfast with no companions save the boy Gaemon Palehair". Though uncrowned and only ten, he moved to spare Corlys Velaryon and restore him to the small council — quote: "restore him to his offices and honors, including a place on the small council".

### Cregan Stark
Lord of Winterfell, aged twenty-three, the Wolf of the North came before King's Landing with a host twice the size of the Lads' to make good a two-years-old promise to Prince Jacaerys — quote: "Cregan Stark had made a promise to Prince Jacaerys". He seized Corlys Velaryon, Larys Strong, Orwyle, Perkin, and Eustace, and pressed to punish the king's poisoners — quote: "dragged him to the dungeons". Made Hand of the Uncrowned King, he sat on a wooden bench beneath the Iron Throne and passed the Judgment of the Wolf, then wielded Ice himself as the custom of the North decreed — quote: "the man who passed the sentence must also wield the sword". A widower of three years, he agreed to marry Black Aly in a bargain trading her a boon for a head — quote: "I claim your hand in marriage". He resigned the Handship the next day, saying his place was at Winterfell — quote: "and my place is at Winterfell".

### Corlys Velaryon
The aged Sea Snake, Master of Driftmark and Lord of the Tides, yielded up King's Landing to the riverlords under a peace banner — quote: "as he yielded up the city to their mercy". Cregan Stark accused him of removing Aegon II by poison, which he did not deny — quote: "What | did, | did for the good of the realm". He was condemned as guilty of murder, regicide, and high treason, but spared by the prince and restored to his offices — quote: "guilty of murder, regicide, and high treason". He had once served Rhaenyra as Queen's Hand and been imprisoned by her on suspicion of treason — quote: "she had imprisoned him on suspicion of treason".

### Larys Strong
Larys the Clubfoot, Lord of Harrenhal, was named one of the two great lords at the heart of the poisoning conspiracy — quote: "the two great lords at the heart of the conspiracy". He would neither confirm nor deny the accusations of Orwyle and Perkin, and stood friendless once condemned — quote: "Lord Larys would neither confirm nor deny the accusations". Cregan Stark took his head with Ice, ending the ancient house — quote: "Thus perished the last Strong". He asked that his clubfoot be hacked off in death, and it later disappeared, said to be sold to a sorcerer — quote: "hack off my clubfoot with that great sword of yours".

### Benjicot Blackwood
"Bloody Ben", Lord of Raventree, was only thirteen, lordship having fallen to him when his father Samwell was slain at the Burning Mill — quote: "when his father Lord Samwell Blackwood had been slain". He commanded the center at Second Tumbleton and led the flank attack that broke Lord Baratheon's stormlanders at the Muddy Mess — quote: "broken Lord Baratheon's stormlanders and won the day". With the brothers Tully he was one of "the Lads", and shrank in Cregan Stark's presence — quote: "the three youths had emerged from the Muddy Mess as the undoubted leaders".

### Kermit Tully
Lord of Riverrun, aged nineteen and "green as summer grass", he was liege lord and nominal commander of the riverlords' host — quote: "Kermit Tully, Lord of Riverrun, was their liege lord". He warned Stark that Storm's End, Oldtown, and Casterly Rock would not fall easily, but ultimately yielded and joined his power to Stark's — quote: "I want no part of this, but | will not have it said".

### Oscar Tully
Ser Oscar, Kermit's brother and a second son, had slain three men during the Muddy Mess and been knighted on the battlefield — quote: "had slain three men during the Muddy Mess and been knighted".

### Alysanne Blackwood
"Black Aly", a sister to the late Lord Samwell and thus aunt to Bloody Ben, was a huntress and archer without peer who had lost her maidenhead in the saddle at thirteen — quote: "a sister to the late Lord Samwell Blackwood, and thus aunt to Bloody Ben". She conceived a liking for Cregan Stark and, according to Mushroom, struck the bargain that stayed Corlys Velaryon's execution — quote: "a bargain struck with Lady Alysanne of House Blackwood". Cregan claimed her hand in marriage in return — quote: "A hand for a head".

### Sabitha Frey
Lady of the Twins, widow of Lord Forrest Frey and mother of his heir; of House Vypren. According to Mushroom she was a sharp-tongued harridan who wore mail and was fond of killing men and kissing women — quote: "was fond of killing men and kissing women".

### Perkin the Flea
Ser Perkin, a gutter knight who had granted dubious knighthoods during the turmoil, was condemned for rebellion and for abandoning the squire he had raised up — quote: "raised up your own squire in her place, then abandoned him". He escaped death by asking to take the black, sparking a chorus of the condemned making the same plea — quote: "Ser Perkin declared that he wished to take the black".

### Orwyle
Grand Maester who spoke for Aegon II's court hailing the riverlords, then confessed under torture to supplying the poison to Larys Strong — quote: "confessed under torture to having given the poison to the Clubfoot". Sentenced to death, he too was spared to take the black, and in the dungeons had begun the confessions that founded Munkun's *True Telling* — quote: "where Orwyle had begun writing his confessions".

### Gyles Belgrave
Ser Gyles of the Kingsguard was condemned for allowing the poisoning through carelessness or blindness, and refused to exchange his white cloak for black — quote: "A knight of the Kingsguard should not outlive his king". He was one of only two men to die that day, beheaded with Ice — quote: "took his head off with a single swift swing of Ice".

### Lyonel Hightower
Ormund's eldest son, a youth of fifteen, inherited the Hightower and first swore to write his reply in the Sea Snake's blood — quote: "swearing to write his reply in the Sea Snake's blood". According to Mushroom he capitulated after his father's widow Lady Sam seduced him and promised marriage only if he made peace — quote: "the boy showed surprising sense... and chose love over honor". He kept her as his paramour thirteen years, fathering six children, before finally wedding her — quote: "kept the Lady Sam by his side as his paramour". Munkun tells it differently: the Tyrells forbade him to go to war on pain of his brother Garmund's life — quote: "his brother would pay for that defiance with his life".

### Samantha Tarly
"Lady Sam", daughter of Donald Tarly and Jeyne Rowan, was Lord Ormund's young widow and had no intention of giving up the Hightower — quote: "the daughter of Lord Donald Tarly of Horn Hill". Mushroom says she yielded to Lyonel's advances and used marriage to bind him to peace — quote: "allowing him to seduce her, and afterward promising to marry him".

### Johanna Lannister
Jason Lannister's widow and daughter of Roland Westerling, she ruled the west and, pressed by the Red Kraken's raids, agreed to all the Sea Snake's terms — quote: "Lady Johanna agreed to all the Sea Snake's terms". She promised to deliver two daughters as companions and hostages and to restore the Crown's gold — quote: "deliver two daughters to the Red Keep, to serve as companions".

### Elenda Baratheon
Lord Borros's widow, unwilling to risk her newborn son Royce, agreed to terms and sent her father to do homage and three daughters as hostages — quote: "she would send her own lord father to do homage in her stead". Her eldest daughter Cassandra wept that she was not to be a queen — quote: "wept bitter tears when she learned she was not to be a queen".

### Baela Targaryen
Half-sister of the prince and Rhaena's twin, she had been freed from imprisonment by men in service to Larys Strong — quote: "had been in service to Lord Larys Strong". Brandishing a sword, she moved Cregan Stark to spare her rescuers, and joined Rhaena in appealing to the prince to spare Corlys — quote: "she would cut off the hand of any man who sought to harm the men who had saved her".

### Rhaena Targaryen
Lady Arryn's ward, she arrived from Gulltown with her dragon Morning on her shoulder — quote: "bringing her own ward, the Lady Rhaena Targaryen". She and her twin Baela became the darlings of King's Landing and pressed the prince to spare the Sea Snake — quote: "Lady Rhaena and her twin sister, Baela, became the darlings of the city".

### Aegon II Targaryen
The dead king, poisoned; his body lay upon a bier beneath the Iron Throne with his mother Queen Alicent weeping beside it, and was consigned to the flames — quote: "The corpse of King Aegon II was consigned to the flames". His murder was judged high treason, the crux of Cregan Stark's inquiries — quote: "his murder was high treason".

### Willis Fell
Ser Willis Fell was the guardian escorting the eight-year-old Princess Jaehaera to King's Landing — quote: "accompanied by Ser Willis Fell, together with his".
