## Node Prose
### Daemon Targaryen
Prince Daemon based himself at Maidenpool with the girl Nettles, hunting Aemond One-Eye and Vhagar without success — quote: "hunting Aemond One-Eye without success". He refused Lord Mooton's counsel to divide the search, arguing only Caraxes and Sheepstealer together could withstand the aged Vhagar — quote: "Prince Daemon refused". Warned by Maester Norren of the queen's order to behead Nettles, he parted from the girl at dawn and declared he would fly for Harrenhal to face Aemond alone — quote: "| fly for Harrenhal". He claimed the ruined Harrenhal, slashing the godswood heart tree each dusk of a fourteen-day vigil — quote: "he slashed the heart tree in the godswood". At the battle's climax he leapt from Caraxes to Vhagar and drove Dark Sister through Aemond's blind eye; both princes died, Daemon nine-and-forty, his body never found — quote: "drove the sword down into his blind eye".

### Nettles
The small brown bastard girl, no more than ten-and-seven, who bound the dragon Sheepstealer to her by feeding him sheep — quote: "the sheep she fed to Sheepstealer to bind him to her". Munkun described her dismissively — quote: "A skinny brown girl on a skinny brown dragon" — and Eustace said her teeth were crooked and her nose scarred for thieving; she was also called Netty — quote: "Nor could Netty truly be called pretty". Warned by Norren, she fed Sheepstealer a black ram at dawn, mounted with blood-stained leathers and tear-stained cheeks, and flew toward the Bay of Crabs — quote: "her cheeks were stained with tears". She vanished into the mists and was never seen again at court or castle — quote: "vanished in the morning mists, never to be seen again".

### Aemond Targaryen
The one-eyed prince ranged the riverlands on Vhagar, striking Stonyhead, Sweetwillow, and other towns before vanishing back into the sky — quote: "Prince Aemond struck at Stonyhead". He came to Harrenhal with Alys Rivers, who had told him where Daemon waited, and confronted his uncle — quote: "on her back rode the oneeyed Prince Aemond Targaryen". Chained to his saddle, he was ambushed out of the setting sun and slain when Daemon leapt aboard Vhagar; he had only turned twenty — quote: "Prince Aemond had only turned twenty". His armored bones were found years later still chained to Vhagar's saddle, Dark Sister thrust hilt-deep through his eye socket — quote: "Dark Sister thrust hilt-deep through his eye socket".

### Rhaenyra Targaryen
On hearing of the betrayal at Tumbleton the queen barred the city gates against turncloaks — quote: "no one was to be allowed to enter or leave King's Landing". Suspicion aroused, she ordered Ser Addam arrested and had Mysaria questioned about the dragonseeds — quote: "commanded Ser Luthor Largent to take twenty gold cloaks". Believing Nettles had bewitched both a dragon and her husband, she sent Lord Mooton a warrant to strike off the girl's head — quote: "deliver her the head of the bastard girl Nettles". She imprisoned Lord Corlys and dismissed Grand Maester Gerardys for defending the dragonseeds — quote: "chose instead to dismiss him from her council".

### Robert Quince
The amiable and famously obese knight whom the queen named castellan of Dragonstone on her departure — quote: "the queen had named castellan of Dragonstone". He was quick to name the Cannibal as Grey Ghost's killer and forbade fishing beneath the Dragonmont's eastern face — quote: "quick to name the Cannibal as the killer". He confined his restless charge Baela to her chambers when she tried to fly to the dead dragon — quote: "the angry maiden was confined to her chambers".

### Baela Targaryen
Daemon's daughter by Laena Velaryon, a wild and willful maiden of ten-and-four, more boyish than ladylike — quote: "Prince Daemon's daughter by his second wife, Laena Velaryon". She rode her dragon Moondancer daily across Dragonstone and even to Driftmark — quote: "riding her dragon Moondancer into the sky". Fearless of the Cannibal, she proposed to investigate the dragon-fight herself and was confined when she defied Quince's ban — quote: "She had no fear of the Cannibal".

### Mysaria
The pale harlot and dancing girl who served as Rhaenyra's mistress of whisperers in all but name, called Lady Misery and the White Worm — quote: "her mistress of whisperers in all but name". Summoned before the council, she told the queen Nettles had already betrayed her and shared Daemon's bed — quote: "Lady Misery appeared before the council". Septon Eustace also names her as a woman whose bed Daemon shared at court — quote: "his nightly visits to Lady Mysaria, whose bed he oft shared".

### Corlys Velaryon
The Sea Snake and Hand, who with Gerardys alone spoke in defense of the dragonseeds — quote: "spoke in defense of the dragonseeds". He declared Addam and his brother Alyn "true Velaryons" and worthy heirs to Driftmark — quote: "Ser Addam and his brother, Alyn". When Addam escaped, Luthor Largent seized the aged Corlys for treachery and cast him into a black cell — quote: "laid rough hands on the aged Lord Corlys".

### Addam Velaryon
The baseborn dragonrider of Seasmoke who resided at the Dragonpit to defend the city — quote: "duty fell to Addam Velaryon". Called Addam of Hull, his loyalty was questioned on the black council alongside Nettles — quote: "What of Addam of Hull and the girl Nettles?". Forewarned of his arrest, he flew off on Seasmoke and escaped — quote: "Ser Addam Velaryon had been forewarned in time to make his escape".

### The Shepherd
A one-handed, barefoot itinerant brother in a hair shirt who rose up in Cobbler's Square, his name lost to history — quote: "a barefoot scarecrow of a man". Munkun suggests he was an outlawed Poor Fellow, while Mushroom calls him "the Dead Shepherd" — quote: "Grand Maester Munkun suggests he might have been a Poor Fellow". He preached that dragons were demons and called down doom on Rhaenyra and Aegon, drawing thousands into the square — quote: "calling down doom and destruction on Queen Rhaenyra".

### Hugh Hammer
The huge son of a common blacksmith, strong enough to twist steel bars into torcs, who rode Vermithor into battle — quote: "he rode Vermithor, once the mount of the Old King". Untrained but fearsome with his warhammer, he began to dream of crowns — quote: "Why be a lord when you can be a king?". A prophecy circulated in camp foretelling a new king when the hammer falls upon the dragon — quote: "When the hammer shall fall upon the dragon, a new king shall arise".

### Ulf White
One of the Two Betrayers, who gave himself to drunkenness after turning his cloak — quote: "drowning himself in wine and flesh". Not content with the knighthood Rhaenyra had given him nor the lordship of Bitterbridge that Daeron granted, he desired Highgarden itself — quote: "he desired no less a seat than Highgarden".

### Jon Roxton
Ser Jon "the Bold," as feared for his black temper as his black Valyrian blade Orphan-Maker — quote: "the Valyrian steel sword called OrphanMaker". He claimed Lady Sharis Footly as a prize of war and cut her protesting husband nigh in two — quote: "cut him nigh in two with OrphanMaker".

### Hobert Hightower
Ormund's cousin, sixty years old and hitherto entrusted only with the baggage train, "as stout as he was slow" — quote: "another of Lord Ormund's cousins". He presumed to take command of the leaderless host by right of his kinship to Queen Alicent — quote: "kinship to Queen Alicent".

### Maester Norren
Keeper of the Chronicles of Maidenpool, a young man of two-and-twenty — quote: "a young man of two-and-twenty". He carried the queen's letter to Daemon and Nettles at supper and lied that no man of Maidenpool knew its contents — quote: "declaring falsely that neither his lordship nor any other man". Daemon named him a bad maester but a good man, and Norren offered himself for delivery to the queen — quote: "You are a bad maester, but a good man".

### Manfryd Mooton
Lord of Maidenpool, host to Daemon and Nettles, who lived in terror of Vhagar — quote: "who lived in terror of Vhagar descending on his town". Ordered by the queen to behead Nettles, he was so shaken he lost his voice, then took counsel and refused to condemn either guest — quote: "The girl is a guest". After the pair departed he raised King Aegon II's banners over Maidenpool — quote: "King Aegon II raised in their stead".

### Alys Rivers
Aemond's woman, her long black hair streaming and her belly swollen with child — quote: "her belly swollen with child". She claimed the sight, telling Aemond she saw Daemon in storm clouds and cook-fires, and watched the final battle from Kingspyre Tower — quote: "She saw you in a storm cloud".

### Daeron Targaryen
Prince "Daeron the Daring," youngest of Alicent's sons, who rode Tessarion down upon Tumbleton — quote: "Prince Daeron swooped down upon Tessarion". Left leaderless after Ormund's death and a boy used to following commands, he was sickened by the sack and bade Hobert stop it — quote: "The youngest of Queen Alicent's sons".

### Joffrey Velaryon
Rhaenyra's son, flush with youth and eager to avenge his fallen brothers on Tyraxes — quote: "I will meet them on Tyraxes". His mother forbade it, judging him too young for battle — quote: "Such talk alarmed his mother".

### Owain Bourney
Lord "the Betrayer," who opened Tumbleton's castle and speared Ser Merrell the Bold through the back — quote: "Lord Owain Bourney did the same within the castle". He boasted his cunning had won the town, then was stabbed through the eye by Lord Peake — quote: "stabbed Bourney through the eye".

### Grey Ghost
A Dragonstone dragon found dead beneath the Dragonmont, torn into two pieces and partly devoured — quote: "the carcass was that of Grey Ghost".

### Ormund Hightower
Lord who commanded King Aegon's host and lay dead at Tumbleton, leaving the loyalists leaderless — quote: "Ormund Hightower lay dead".

### Caraxes
Daemon's dragon, the Blood Wyrm, who tore out Vhagar's throat and crawled ashore to die beneath Harrenhal — quote: "expiring beneath the walls of Harrenhal".
