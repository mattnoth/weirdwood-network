## Node Prose

### Rhea Royce
The "bronze bitch" of Runestone, Daemon's wife, who fell from her horse while hawking in 115 AC — quote: "fell from her horse whilst hawking and cracked her skull upon a stone". She lingered nine days, then collapsed and died within an hour of rising — quote: "collapse and die within an hour of rising". Her lands passed not to Daemon but to her nephew — quote: "Runestone passed instead to Lady Rhea’s nephew".

### Daemon Targaryen
The king's brother, who flew to the Vale on his wife's death, ostensibly to bury her but hoping for her lands — quote: "To put my wife to rest". Lady Jeyne dismissed his claim and warned him from the Vale — quote: "his presence in the Vale was unwelcome". At High Tide his eye fell on Laena Velaryon; he asked Corlys for her hand and slew her Braavosi betrothed with Dark Sister — quote: "the prince made short work of his rival". He wed Laena and left the Stepstones — quote: "wed Lady Laena Velaryon a fortnight later". Widowed again in 120 AC, he wed his niece Rhaenyra secretly on Dragonstone and at last fathered trueborn sons — quote: "Prince Daemon had at last a living son of his own blood".

### Laena Velaryon
Corlys's daughter, a maid of two-and-twenty, tall and lovely, long betrothed to a wastrel son of the Sealord of Braavos — quote: "betrothed from the age of twelve to a son of the Sealord of Braavos". She wed Daemon and bore him twin daughters in Pentos in 116 AC — quote: "Lady Laena gave birth to twin daughters". In 120 AC she bore a twisted son who died within the hour and succumbed to childbed fever herself at twenty-seven — quote: "making her helpless before the onset of childbed fever". In her final hour she tried to reach Vhagar to fly a last time — quote: "intent on reaching Vhagar that she might fly one last time".

### Rhaenyra Targaryen
The king's heir, who bore Lucerys late in 115 AC and Joffrey in 117 AC — quote: "given birth to a second son late in the year 115 AC". She grew estranged from Queen Alicent and kept to Dragonstone — quote: "the princess spent her days on Dragonstone". Widowed in 120 AC, she wed her uncle Daemon and bore him Aegon the Younger and Viserys — quote: "taking to husband her uncle, Daemon Targaryen". She had Vaemond Velaryon beheaded for calling her sons bastards — quote: "had his head removed, and fed his carcass to her dragon, Syrax". Her healer Gerardys later saved the wounded king's life — quote: "acted swiftly to remove two fingers from His".

### Viserys I Targaryen
The king, who still meant his daughter to follow him and her sons after her — quote: "King Viserys still meant for his daughter to follow him upon the Iron Throne". He forgave Daemon a second time out of love — quote: "the king still loved the brother who had been the companion of his youth". After maiming a hand on the Iron Throne and losing two fingers, he shunned the throne room — quote: "Viserys | never sat upon the Iron Throne again". Grown fat, gouty, and short of breath, he recalled Otto Hightower as Hand — quote: "recalled to court Ser Otto Hightower, the queen’s father". He died in his sleep in 129 AC, aged fifty-two after twenty-six years — quote: "closed his eyes and went to sleep".

### Alicent Hightower
The queen, whose rivalry with Rhaenyra deepened past bearing — quote: "the queen and the princess could scarce suffer each other's presence". According to Mushroom she taunted Laenor over his sons' looks — quote: "soon or late, you may get one who looks like you". After Aemond's maiming she demanded one of Lucerys's eyes — quote: "one of Lucerys Velaryon’s eyes should be put out". She grew wroth at the naming of Rhaenyra's son Aegon and pressed Maester Alfador for Grand Maester — quote: "She urged the appointment of one Maester Alfador".

### Laenor Velaryon
Rhaenyra's husband and the putative father of her children — quote: "the putative father of her children". He was slain at a fair in Spicetown in 120 AC by his companion Ser Qarl Correy — quote: "stabbed to death by his friend and companion Ser Qarl Correy". According to Septon Eustace, jealousy was the motive, Laenor having taken a new favorite — quote: "grown enamored of a new favorite, a handsome young squire of six-and-ten".

### Harwin Strong
Ser, called Breakbones, Rhaenyra's champion and sworn shield, sent back to Harrenhal — quote: "whilst Breakbones returned to Harrenhal". Amongst the greens it was an article of faith he, not Laenor, fathered Rhaenyra's sons — quote: "not her husband, Laenor, but her champion, Harwin Strong". He died with his father in the fire at Harrenhal in 120 AC — quote: "both father and son were killed".

### Lyonel Strong
Lord of Harrenhal and Hand of the King, on whom Viserys relied — quote: "Lord Strong had been the King’s Hand". He returned to Harrenhal with his son and died there when fire broke out in their tower — quote: "a fire broke out in the tower where they were sleeping". The cause was never determined and many blamed foul play — quote: "The cause of the fire was never determined".

### Larys Strong
Larys the Clubfoot, who became Lord of Harrenhal on the deaths of his father and elder brother — quote: "Larys Strong became the Lord of Harrenhal". Some suspected him of the fire; he later sat the small council as Lord Confessor and master of whisperers — quote: "the Lord Confessor and master of whisperers, Larys Strong".

### Aemond Targaryen
Alicent's second son, dragonless at ten, who resolved to claim Vhagar in secret — quote: "he resolved not to wait for Dragonstone". He shoved Joffrey into dragon droppings and mounted Vhagar, becoming a dragonrider — quote: "the boy prince Aemond Targaryen became a dragonrider". In the fight after, he broke Luke's nose and mocked the boys as "the Strongs" before Luke took his eye — quote: "calling them “the Strongs.”". He counted the trade a fair one — quote: "he lost an eye and gained a dragon that day". He grew into a dangerous swordsman under Criston Cole — quote: "under the tutelage of Ser Criston Cole".

### Lucerys Velaryon
Luke, Rhaenyra's brown-haired second son who rode Arrax — quote: "given a dragon's egg whilst in the cradle". In the yard at High Tide he slashed Aemond across the face and took his right eye — quote: "slashed Aemond across the face, taking out his right eye". Rhaenyra later urged Corlys to name him heir to Driftmark over Jacaerys — quote: "urged her good-father to name instead her second son, Lucerys".

### Corlys Velaryon
The Sea Snake, Lord of the Tides, who mourned first his daughter Laena then his son Laenor in 120 AC — quote: "still in mourning for their beloved daughter when the Stranger came again, to carry off their son". He offered ten thousand golden dragons for Laenor's killer — quote: "offered a reward of ten thousand". Stricken by fever in 126 AC, he raised the question of who should succeed him — quote: "the Sea Snake was stricken by a sudden fever".

### Rhaenys
Princess, wife of Corlys and mother of Laena and Laenor, "she who might have been a queen" — quote: "she who might have". She mourned both her children in 120 AC — quote: "still in mourning for their beloved daughter".

### Joffrey Velaryon
Rhaenyra's third son, born 117 AC, brown-haired like his brothers — quote: "brown eyes, brown hair, and features that some at court called “common.”". At three he saw Aemond at Vhagar, was shoved into dung, and ran to fetch his brothers — quote: "Afraid that the boy would raise the alarm". His own young dragon was Tyraxes — quote: "his own young dragon, Tyraxes".

### Qarl Correy
Ser, a household knight of low birth with expensive tastes, who stabbed Laenor at Spicetown and fled, never seen again — quote: "Correy had fled by then". Mushroom holds that Daemon paid him to kill Laenor, then had him killed at sea — quote: "then cut his throat and fed him to the sea".

### Otto Hightower
Ser, the queen's father, recalled to the Handship after Lyonel Strong's death — quote: "recalled to court Ser Otto Hightower, the queen’s father". He remained the King's Hand and led the council as Viserys's strength failed — quote: "The King's Hand remained Ser Otto Hightower".

### Vaemond Velaryon
Ser, eldest of Corlys's nephews, who claimed Driftmark on the grounds that Rhaenyra's sons were bastards — quote: "the inheritance by rights should pass to him". Rhaenyra sent Daemon to seize him and had him beheaded and fed to Syrax — quote: "She dispatched Prince Daemon to seize Ser".

### Aegon the Elder
Alicent's eldest son, wed to his sister Helaena in 122 AC at fifteen, a lazy glutton and lecher — quote: "a glutton at table, given to swilling ale and strongwine". He rode Sunfyre, said the most beautiful dragon ever seen — quote: "Aegon's young Sunfyre was said to be the most beautiful dragon ever seen upon the earth". If Mushroom is believed, he fathered two bastards the year of his twins — quote: "he fathered two bastard children the same year as the twins".

### Helaena Targaryen
Alicent's daughter, a dragonrider of Dreamfyre, wed to her brother Aegon at thirteen — quote: "The bride, his sister, was but thirteen". She bore the twins Jaehaerys and Jaehaera in 123 AC and Maelor in 127 AC — quote: "gave birth to twins, a boy she named Jaehaerys and a girl called Jaehaera".

### Mellos
Grand Maester and moderating council voice, who hinted at the sons' bastardy and mused the king himself might have ordered the Harrenhal fire — quote: "the king himself might have given the command". He washed and bound the king's cut hand, then collapsed and died on the serpentine steps — quote: "Grand Maester Mellos collapsed one night whilst he".

### Gerardys
Maester, Rhaenyra's experienced healer on Dragonstone, who came too late to save Laena — quote: "Maester Gerardys came too late". His swift work removing two of the king's fingers later saved Viserys's life — quote: "it was only his healing skills that had saved the king's life".

### Orwyle
Archmaester chosen by the Conclave to be Grand Maester, thought to favor neither faction — quote: "the Conclave bestowed the chain of office upon Archmaester Orwyle". His potions were held more efficacious than Mellos's leechings — quote: "Orwyle’s potions and tinctures were more efficacious".

### Erryk Cargyll
Ser, of the Kingsguard, appointed to serve as Rhaenyra's sworn shield on Dragonstone — quote: "Ser Erryk Cargyll of the Kingsguard would serve as her sworn shield".

### Daeron Targaryen (son of Viserys I)
Alicent's youngest and most popular son, clever and courteous, who had the dragon Tessarion but had yet to ride — quote: "a lovely blue she-dragon named Tessarion, though he had yet to ride". At twelve in 126 AC he was sent to Oldtown to squire for Lord Hightower — quote: "sent to Oldtown to serve as cupbearer and squire to Lord Hightower".

### Baela Targaryen
Twin daughter of Daemon and Laena, born 116 AC in Pentos, named after Daemon's father — quote: "Baela (after his father)". Betrothed while a toddler to one of Rhaenyra's eldest sons — quote: "the betrothal of her two eldest sons to the daughters of Prince Daemon and Lady Laena".

### Rhaena Targaryen (daughter of Daemon)
Twin daughter of Daemon and Laena, born 116 AC in Pentos, small and sickly but fine-featured — quote: "silver-white hair, and purple eyes".

### Jasper Wylde
Lord, master of laws, called "Ironrod" — by Eustace for his unbending rulings, by Mushroom for another reason — quote: "known amongst the smallfolk as “Ironrod”".

### Tyland Lannister
Ser, the lord admiral and master of ships, brother to the Lord of Casterly Rock — quote: "Ser Tyland Lannister, brother to the Lord of Casterly Rock".

### Lyman Beesbury
The aged Lord, master of coin almost without interruption since the Old King's day — quote: "master of coin, in which capacity he had served almost uninterrupted".

### Criston Cole
Ser, Lord Commander of the Kingsguard and a bitter foe to Rhaenyra, who tutored Aemond in arms — quote: "in him Rhaenyra had a bitter foe".
