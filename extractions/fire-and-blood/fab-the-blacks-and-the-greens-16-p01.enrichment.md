## Node Prose

### Rhaenyra Targaryen
Rhaenyra was the only surviving child of King Viserys's first marriage and one of the two principal claimants to the Iron Throne — quote: "his daughter Rhaenyra, the only surviving child of his first marriage". She was confined on Dragonstone, about to give birth, when her father died, and the news drove her into a black fury that brought on her labor early — quote: "Rhaenyra Targaryen strained and shuddered in her third day of labor". The child came forth a stillborn, malformed girl she named Visenya — quote: "The dead girl had been named Visenya". She then convened her own council and swore vengeance — quote: "They stole my crown and murdered my daughter". Once well-loved as the Realm's Delight, she was now a woman aged by six childbirths, with the wealth of House Velaryon and dragons to her side — quote: "her body aged and thickened by six childbirths".

### Aegon II Targaryen
Aegon was Viserys's eldest son by his second wife and the greens' candidate for the throne — quote: "Aegon, his eldest son by his second wife". In Eustace's account he at first refused, protesting his sister's better right — quote: "My sister is the heir, not me". He was crowned in the Dragonpit with the Conqueror's crown and proclaimed Second of His Name — quote: "proclaiming him Aegon of House Targaryen, Second of His Name". Impatient in the days before, he had demanded the crown — quote: "Am | a king or no?". After the crowning he mounted his dragon Sunfyre and then the Iron Throne before a thousand lords — quote: "Ser Arryk Cargyll led His Grace into the torchlit throne room".

### Viserys I Targaryen
The ailing, bedridden king died in his sleep in the Red Keep on the third day of the third moon of 129 AC — quote: "closed his eyes for a nap in the Red Keep of King’s Landing and died without waking". His body was found by a serving man at the hour of the bat, who ran to inform Queen Alicent — quote: "The servant ran to inform Queen Alicent". His corpse lay unattended for days, swollen and rotting, while the greens delayed the announcement — quote: "the stink from the dead king's bedchamber had wafted all through Maegor's Holdfast".

### Alicent Hightower
Queen Alicent, Viserys's widow and leader of the greens, took charge the moment the king was found dead, sealing his chamber and silencing witnesses — quote: "Queen Alicent, accompanied by Ser Criston Cole". She argued the throne must pass to her eldest trueborn son and feared for her children under Rhaenyra — quote: "The Iron Throne by rights must pass to His Grace's eldest trueborn son". Alone among the conspirators she was excused the blood oath — quote: "excused from the oath, on account of her womanhood". She crowned her daughter Helaena queen and knelt before her — quote: "the mother knelt before the daughter".

### Otto Hightower
Ser Otto Hightower, the queen's father and Hand of the King, was empowered to rule in the king's absence and steered the green council toward Aegon's coronation — quote: "her father, Ser Otto Hightower, Hand of the King". He ordered Lord Beesbury seized (per Orwyle) and set the greens' preparations in motion, dividing the treasury and courting allies — quote: "Ser Otto Hightower, as methodical a man as ever served as Hand". He sent his grandson Aemond to win Storm's End by marriage — quote: "Your purpose is to win the hand of one of Lord Baratheon’s daughters".

### Lyman Beesbury
Lord Lyman Beesbury, master of coin and lord treasurer, a man of eighty, was the only councillor to speak for Rhaenyra — quote: "only Lord Beesbury spoke on behalf of Princess Rhaenyra". He rose to denounce the plot and was killed; the manner is disputed among the sources — quote: "the first blood shed in the Dance of the Dragons belonged to". All three chronicles agree his was the first blood of the Dance — quote: "Lord Lyman Beesbury, master of coin and lord treasurer of the Seven Kingdoms".

### Criston Cole
Ser Criston Cole, Lord Commander of the Kingsguard, confirmed the king's death and roused the small council — quote: "Ser Criston Cole, Lord Commander of the Kingsguard". Two sources charge him with Lord Beesbury's death, and he persuaded a reluctant Aegon to take the crown by warning of certain execution — quote: "Rhaenyra has no choice but to take your heads". He placed the Conqueror's crown on Aegon's brow — quote: "Ser Criston Cole placed the steel-and-ruby crown of Aegon the Conqueror".

### Larys Strong
Larys Strong, called Larys Clubfoot, Lord of Harrenhal and master of whisperers, kept silent through the debate — quote: "the master of whisperers hoarded his words like a miser hoarding coins". He spoke only once, to propose the binding blood oath — quote: "Let us be the first to swear".

### Jasper Wylde
Lord Jasper Wylde, called Ironrod, master of laws, argued precedent and Andal tradition against Rhaenyra — quote: "Lord Jasper Wylde, called Ironrod, master of laws". He pronounced any who would not swear to Aegon traitors — quote: "and must die a traitor's death".

### Tyland Lannister
Ser Tyland Lannister, master of ships and brother to the Lord of Casterly Rock, dismissed the old oaths to Rhaenyra as void by death and time — quote: "many of the lords who had sworn to defend the succession of Princess Rhaenyra were long dead". Named master of coin after Beesbury, he seized and divided the royal treasury — quote: "The Crown's gold was divided into four parts".

### Grand Maester Orwyle
Grand Maester Orwyle sat on the green council and opened it by reviewing the customary rites for a dead king — quote: "Grand Maester Orwyle opened the meeting". His confessions before execution underlie Munkun's history, though he wrote to absolve himself — quote: "Orwyle was most anxious to show himself in a favorable light".

### Grand Maester Munkun
Grand Maester Munkun wrote The Dance of the Dragons, A True Telling, the most detailed account, though composed a generation later and relying on Orwyle — quote: "the most detailed and authoritative of them is Grand Maester Munkun’s". He burned the poetic name of the war into history — quote: "tradition, time, and Grand Maester Munkun have burned the".

### Septon Eustace
Septon Eustace, writing years later, held the king's death anticipated and the greens prepared, and gave a gentler account of Aegon — quote: "Septon Eustace, writing on these events some years later". In his telling Criston Cole cut Beesbury's throat — quote: "opened his throat with a dagger".

### Mushroom
Mushroom the dwarf, author of the Testimony, was on Dragonstone with Rhaenyra, not in King's Landing at the king's death — quote: "Mushroom was not in King’s Landing the". He alleges Alicent poisoned the king and paints the crudest pictures of Aegon and Beesbury's death — quote: "a pinch of poison in his hippocras".

### Daemon Targaryen
Prince Daemon, Rhaenyra's uncle and husband, called Lord Flea Bottom by his foes, was the war's most experienced commander — quote: "her consort, Prince Daemon, tried and tempered in the Stepstones". He counseled winning lords with words before battle and warned against pitting dragons against dragons — quote: "We must fight this war with words before we go to battle". He took charge of the assault on Harrenhal — quote: "Prince Daemon would lead the assault on Harrenhal, riding Caraxes".

### Aemond Targaryen
One-eyed Prince Aemond, nineteen, second son of Alicent, was found arming for practice and asked whether Aegon was king — quote: "Oneeyed Prince Aemond, nineteen, was found in the armory". He flew to Storm's End on Vhagar, a sapphire set in his empty socket, charged to wed a Baratheon daughter — quote: "with a sapphire in the place of his missing eye". He vowed he would not fail — quote: "Aegon will have Storm's End, and | will have this girl".

### Daeron Targaryen
Prince Daeron, the youngest and gentlest of Alicent's children, was away in Oldtown serving Lord Hightower as squire — quote: "Prince Daeron, the youngest and gentlest of her children, was in Oldtown". His dragon was the cobalt-and-copper Tessarion — quote: "Prince Daeron’s dragon was Tessarion".

### Helaena Targaryen
Princess Helaena, Aegon's wife and sister, was breaking her fast with her children when the Kingsguard came seeking her husband — quote: "Feel free to search beneath the blankets". Her mother crowned her queen at the coronation — quote: "placed her own crown upon the head of her daughter, Helaena". Her mount was Dreamfyre and her twins had hatchling dragons — quote: "Queen Helaena’s mount was Dreamfyre".

### Corlys Velaryon
Corlys Velaryon of Driftmark, the Sea Snake, grown old, was the greatest lord to pledge his strength to Rhaenyra — quote: "the greatest lord to pledge his strength to the princess was Corlys Velaryon of Driftmark". He argued against burning King's Landing and would command the fleet closing the Gullet — quote: "We want to rule the city, not burn it to the ground". He grumbled but smiled that the three boys were Velaryons — quote: "the three boys were Velaryons".

### Rhaenys Targaryen
Princess Rhaenys, Corlys's wife, five-and-fifty, the Queen Who Never Was, was still fierce and had been a dragonrider longer than any at the black council — quote: "his wife, Princess Rhaenys, five-and-fifty". She enumerated the two sides' dragons and urged finding riders for the Dragonmont beasts — quote: "That is how we shall win this". Long ago the Old King chose Baelon over her, and House Baratheon remained staunch for her claims — quote: "the Old King's choice of Baelon rather than Rhaenys in 92".

### Jacaerys Velaryon
Jacaerys, Rhaenyra's eldest son at fourteen, rode Vermax and sat the black council — quote: "Jace was fourteen, Luke thirteen, Joffrey eleven". He proposed that he and his brothers bear the messages to the lords on dragonback — quote: "Dragons will win the lords over quicker than ravens".

### Lucerys Velaryon
Lucerys, Rhaenyra's son of thirteen, rode Arrax and backed his brother's plan, insisting dragonback would prove them no Strongs — quote: "Only Targaryens ride dragons".

### Aegon the Younger
Aegon the Younger, eldest of Rhaenyra's two sons by Daemon, commanded the young dragon Stormcloud but had not yet mounted him — quote: "commanded the young dragon Stormcloud, though he had yet to mount him".

### Gerardys
Maester Gerardys, Rhaenyra's trusted counselor, tried to restrain her in her labor and noted that Aegon too had dragons — quote: "Maester Gerardys and her midwife tried to restrain her".

### Luthor Largent
Ser Luthor Largent, most fearsome of the "leal five," a bull near seven feet tall, was made commander of the gold cloaks — quote: "the most fearsome of the “leal five,”". He was rumored to have killed a warhorse with one punch — quote: "rumored to have once killed a warhorse with a single punch".

### Gwayne Hightower
Ser Gwayne Hightower, Otto's son and the queen's brother, was named Largent's second to watch him for disloyalty — quote: "his own son Ser Gwayne Hightower (the queen's brother)".

### Dalton Greyjoy
Dalton Greyjoy, the Red Kraken, the daring and bloodthirsty sixteen-year-old Lord Reaper of Pyke, was offered the admiralty and a council seat by Otto — quote: "the daring and bloodthirsty sixteen-year-old Lord Reaper of Pyke". Daemon judged the ironmen fickle and thought Dalton might as easily support Rhaenyra — quote: "Dalton Greyjoy loved blood and battle".

### Steffon Darklyn
Ser Steffon Darklyn of the Kingsguard slipped from King's Landing the night before the coronation, fleeing to Dragonstone — quote: "Ser Steffon Darklyn of the Kingsguard had slipped from the city". His party carried away a stolen crown, the one Viserys and the Old King had worn — quote: "They brought with them a stolen crown".

### Visenya Targaryen (daughter of Rhaenyra)
Rhaenyra's stillborn daughter came forth malformed, with a hole in her chest and a scaled tail, and was named Visenya — quote: "a stillborn girl, twisted and malformed, with a hole in her chest". Mushroom claims he carried the body to the yard for burning — quote: "The dead girl had been named Visenya".

### Borros Baratheon
Borros Baratheon, son of the late Boremund and Lord of Storm's End, was even more belligerent than his father — quote: "his son Borros was even more belligerent than his father". Aemond was sent to wed one of his four daughters and so win the stormlands — quote: "Lord Borros will deliver the stormlands for".

### Boremund Baratheon
Old Lord Boremund of Storm's End, lately dead, had been the staunchest of friends to Rhaenys — quote: "the late Lord Boremund had always been the staunchest of friends".

### Lady Jeyne
Lady Jeyne, the Maiden of the Vale, ruled the Eyrie, and her own rights might be questioned if Rhaenyra were set aside — quote: "the Eyrie was presently ruled by a woman, Lady Jeyne, the Maiden of the Vale".

### Arryk Cargyll
Ser Arryk Cargyll, twin to Erryk, was among the Kingsguard in King's Landing and led Aegon II into the throne room — quote: "Ser Arryk Cargyll led His Grace into the torchlit throne room".

### Erryk Cargyll
Ser Erryk Cargyll, twin to Arryk, was on Dragonstone with Rhaenyra and stood at her black council — quote: "Ser Erryk Cargyll, twin to Ser Arryk".

### Vhagar
Vhagar, oldest and largest of the dragons of Westeros, bore Aemond to Storm's End and had once been Queen Visenya's mount — quote: "not a raven who took flight for Storm's End that day, but Vhagar".

### Vermithor
Hoary old Vermithor lairs in the Dragonmont, unridden since King Jaehaerys died — quote: "hoary old Vermithor, unridden since the death of King Jaehaerys".

### Meleys
Meleys the Red Queen, Rhaenys's she-dragon, had grown lazy but stayed fearsome when roused — quote: "Rhaenys's own she-dragon, Meleys the Red Queen, had grown lazy".
