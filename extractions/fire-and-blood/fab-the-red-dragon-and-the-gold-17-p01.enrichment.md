## Node Prose
### Helaena
Queen Helaena was spared by Blood and Cheese but broken by the ordeal; afterward she would not eat, bathe, or leave her chambers — quote: "she would not eat, nor bathe, nor leave her chambers". She could no longer bear to look upon her son Maelor, whom she had named to die — quote: "she could no longer stand to look upon her son Maelor". She and Aegon slept apart thereafter and she sank into madness — quote: "Aegon and his wife slept separately thereafter". She was the rider of Dreamfyre but spent her days weeping — quote: "Dreamfyre's rider, Queen Helaena".

### Aegon II Targaryen
The king took Maelor from Helaena and gave him to their mother Alicent to raise, and thereafter raged and drank — quote: "give him over to their mother, the Dowager Queen Alicent". He lost faith in Otto and dismissed him, tossing the Hand's chain to Criston Cole — quote: "he tore the chain of office from his neck". At Rook's Rest he rode Sunfyre into the trap and suffered grievous burns — quote: "the king's armor had melted into his flesh". Carried home in a closed litter, he did not rise from bed for the rest of the year — quote: "Carried back to King's Landing in a closed litter".

### Otto Hightower
Ser Otto, Hand and grandsire of the king, won over lords, hired sellswords, and sought alliances after Orwyle's peace overtures were rejected — quote: "winning over lords, hiring sellswords, strengthening the defenses". He reached to Dorne's Qoren Martell, who spurned him, and to the Kingdom of the Three Daughters to break the Velaryon blockade — quote: "reached across the narrow sea to another of the prince's enemies". Losing his king's trust, he was stripped of office in favor of Criston Cole — quote: "mistook his efforts for inaction, and his caution for cowardice".

### Criston Cole
Ser Criston, Lord Commander of the Kingsguard, was made Hand as a "steel fist" — quote: "My new Hand is a steel fist". He counselled repaying Rhaenyra in stealth and chose his Sworn Brother Arryk Cargyll for the deed — quote: "We will pay the princess back in her own bloody coin". He beheaded captive lords, sacked Duskendale, and laid a trap at Rook's Rest that lured Rhaenys to her death — quote: "Aegon's Hand had expected this, counted on it". He remained Hand under the new Prince Regent — quote: "Ser Criston Cole remained Hand of the King".

### Aemond Targaryen
Prince Aemond rode Vhagar to Rook's Rest and helped destroy Meleys and Rhaenys — quote: "his brother Aemond upon Vhagar". Bidden to rule until Aegon recovered, he took up the Conqueror's crown but named himself only Protector of the Realm and Prince Regent — quote: "named himself only Protector of the Realm and Prince Regent". He boasted the crown suited him and named Daemon the true danger — quote: "It looks better on me than it ever did on him".

### Rhaenyra Targaryen
The queen, called "Aegon the Usurper's" foe, was broken by pregnancy, labor, and the death of Lucerys — quote: "a woman already broken by pregnancy, labor, and stillbirth". She was reluctant to attack King's Landing; the chroniclers dispute why — Munkun cites her horror of kinslaying, Septon Eustace says she had "a mother's heart," and Mushroom claims she was too griefsick to command — quote: "Rhaenyra was still so griefsick over the death of her son Lucerys". She counted four living sons her strength and consolation — quote: "Four living sons remained to her". At Jacaerys's urging she legitimized Addam and Alyn of Hull.

### Rhaenys Targaryen
Rhaenys, the Queen Who Never Was, took up her husband's and Rhaenyra's command and flew Meleys to answer Lord Staunton's plea — quote: "rode Rhaenys Targaryen, the Queen Who Never Was". She turned to fight even against Vhagar and Sunfyre together, and died in the fall — quote: "against Vhagar and Sunfyre together, doom was certain". Daughter of Jocelyn Baratheon and Prince Aemon, faithful wife to Corlys, she died at fifty-five — quote: "She was fifty-five years old".

### Corlys Velaryon
The Sea Snake's fleets closed Blackwater Bay and the Gullet — quote: "The Sea Snake's fleets closed Blackwater Bay". After Rhaenys's death he blamed Rhaenyra bitterly — quote: "It should have been you". Jacaerys brought him back into the fold as Hand of the Queen — quote: "naming him the Hand of the Queen". He brought the Hull boys forward as his grandsons and had Addam legitimized as heir to Driftmark — quote: "who brought the boys to Prince Jacaerys for the Sowing".

### Daemon Targaryen
Prince Daemon, riding Caraxes, took Harrenhal and then Stone Hedge by storm with a host of Darrys, Rootes, Pipers, and Freys — quote: "Led by Prince Daemon on Caraxes, a strong host". Rhaenyra's consort, he was both a pillar and, as a maker of enemies, a weakness of her cause — quote: "The prince had made more foes than friends". Aemond named him the true danger — quote: "The danger is my uncle".

### Jacaerys Velaryon
Jace, Prince of Dragonstone and on the cusp of fifteen, came to the fore late in 129 AC — quote: "was on the cusp of his fifteenth nameday". He sent Joffrey and Rhaena to the Vale, dispatched his young halfbrothers to Pentos, and named Corlys Hand of the Queen — quote: "the safety of his halfbrothers, Aegon the Younger and Viserys". At Mushroom's urging he called on the dragonseeds to master the masterless dragons — quote: "any man who could master a dragon would be granted lands and riches". Mushroom alone claims Jace was secretly wed to Sara Snow of Winterfell.

### Arryk Cargyll
Ser Arryk, Kingsguard and identical twin of Erryk, was sent to Dragonstone disguised as his brother — quote: "in the guise of his twin brother". His true target is disputed: Munkun says Rhaenyra, Mushroom says her sons — quote: "Cargyll had been commanded to slay Rhaenyra". He met Erryk and the twins slew each other, the manner of it disputed by singers and by Mushroom — quote: "the Cargyll twins slew each other".

### Erryk Cargyll
Ser Erryk, twin at Rhaenyra's court, knew his brother's presence at once — quote: "who knew at once what his brother's presence meant". In Munkun's telling they embraced and died with tears; in Mushroom's, Erryk struck first but took four days to die of a gut wound — quote: "Ser Erryk took four days to die of his gut wound".

### Marilda
Marilda of Hull, called "Mouse," was a shipwright's daughter who bore Addam in 114 AC and Alyn in 115 — quote: "gave birth to Addam in 114 AC". She became a canny trader owning seven ships by 130 AC — quote: "by 130 AC Marilda of Hull owned seven ships". She long refused to name the boys' father, at last claiming the late Ser Laenor — quote: "claiming both boys were the natural sons of the late Ser Laenor Velaryon".

### Addam of Hull
Addam, a boy of fifteen of silver hair and purple eyes, claimed Seasmoke, seeming to prove his Velaryon blood — quote: "took onto his back a boy of ten-and-five". Munkun and Eustace name Laenor his father, but Mushroom insists Corlys himself sired him — quote: "sired not by the Sea Snake's son, but by the Sea Snake himself". He was legitimized as Addam Velaryon, heir to Driftmark — quote: "became Addam Velaryon, heir to Driftmark".

### Alyn of Hull
Alyn, one year younger than Addam, hoped to tame Sheepstealer and Grey Ghost but was badly burned; his brother saved him — quote: "only his brother's swift action saved his life". He carried the scars the rest of his long life and became Alyn Velaryon — quote: "Alyn Velaryon would carry the scars of the encounter".

### Nettles
A "small brown girl" of sixteen tamed the wild Sheepstealer by feeding him a slaughtered sheep each morning — quote: "a 'small brown girl' of six-and-ten". Munkun names her Nettles; Mushroom calls her Netty, a dockside whore's bastard — quote: "the girl was a bastard of uncertain birth called Netty".

### Baela Targaryen
Daughter of Daemon by Laena and twin of Rhaena, Baela refused to leave Dragonstone or Jacaerys — quote: "Long betrothed to Prince Jacaerys, she refused to leave him". Her dragon Moondancer was too small to bear her, and no wedding to Jace was held — quote: "Moondancer was too small to bear her weight".

### Rhaena Targaryen
Rhaena of Pentos, thirteen, was sent to the Vale with Joffrey; a landless dragonrider, she carried three eggs and prayed for their hatching — quote: "she brought three dragon's eggs with her to the Vale".

### Samwell Blackwood
Lord Samwell surprised the Brackens at a riverside mill and was slain in single combat by Ser Amos Bracken — quote: "Lord Samwell Blackwood surprised them on the march".

### Amos Bracken
Ser Amos led the Bracken host from Stone Hedge and slew Lord Blackwood before a weirwood arrow found his eye slit — quote: "a weirwood arrow found the eye slit of his helm".

### Alysanne Blackwood
Lord Samwell's sixteen-year-old sister, later called Black Aly, is supposed by family legend to have loosed the arrow that killed Amos Bracken — quote: "that shaft was loosed by Lord Samwell's sixteen-year-old sister, Alysanne".

### Ormund Hightower
Otto's nephew marched from Oldtown with a great host and was harassed by the two Alans and Lord Costayne — quote: "Lord Ormund Hightower had issued forth from Oldtown". He begged King's Landing for dragons — quote: "We have need of your dragons".

### Larys Strong
Aegon II's master of whisperers drew up a list of all the lords who had attended Rhaenyra's coronation — quote: "master of whisperers, Larys Strong the Clubfoot, had drawn up a list".

### Steffon Darklyn
Lord Commander of the Queensguard, he offered himself as a dragonrider and was burned to death by Seasmoke — quote: "Steffon Darklyn was burned to death whilst attempting to mount the dragon Seasmoke".

### Silver Denys
His hair and eyes lent credence to a claim of descent from a bastard son of Maegor; Sheepstealer tore off his arm and the Cannibal devoured him and his sons — quote: "had an arm torn off by Sheepstealer".

### Hugh the Hammer
A towering blacksmith's bastard, also called Hard Hugh, who claimed the Old King's dragon Vermithor — quote: "bent his neck to a blacksmith's bastard".

### Ulf the White
A pale-haired man-at-arms, also called Ulf the Sot, who mounted Silverwing — quote: "named Ulf the White (for his hair) or Ulf the Sot".

### Meleys
"The Red Queen," Rhaenys's scarlet mount, was hit by many arrows but killed and broken in the fall; her head was drawn through King's Landing on a cart — quote: "the head of the dragon Meleys, drawn through the city on a cart".

### Sunfyre
"Sunfyre the Golden," Aegon II's mount, had a wing half torn away and was left crawling in the fields, too heavy to move — quote: "crawling through the ashes like some great golden wyrm".

### Cannibal
The largest and oldest wild dragon, said by some smallfolk to have laired on Dragonstone before the Targaryens; he devoured Silver Denys and his sons — quote: "The largest and oldest of the wild dragons was the Cannibal".

### Sheepstealer
An ugly "mud brown" wild dragon with a taste for mutton, who killed more seeds than the three castle dragons together before Nettles tamed him — quote: "a notably ugly 'mud brown' dragon".

### Grey Ghost
A shy, elusive pale grey-white wild dragon of the eastern Dragonmont who preferred fish — quote: "a notably shy dragon who avoided men".

### Qoren Martell
Dorne's ruling prince, who had once warred against Daemon and refused Otto's offer — quote: "I would sooner sleep with scorpions".

### Lord Staunton
Lord of Rook's Rest, who defied Criston and sent a raven to Dragonstone pleading for succor — quote: "he dispatched a raven to Dragonstone, pleading for succor".
