## Node Prose

### Jaehaerys I Targaryen
King of the Seven Kingdoms under his mother's regency; he wed his sister Alysanne in a sudden secret marriage that took the Hand and Queen Regent unawares — quote: "the king’s sudden and secret marriage to his sister". He remained on Dragonstone, unwilling to be ruled by Rogar Baratheon though grateful for his help to the throne — quote: "I do not require a second." He trained daily at arms against the garrison, "dying" so often the men made a game of it — quote: "shouting “The king is dead” every time he fell". He expected to be tested as his father had been — quote: "They will test me as well."

### Alysanne Targaryen
Fifthborn child of Aenys and Alyssa, a maid of thirteen who pledged her troth to her brother against her mother's wishes — quote: "pledged her troth to her brother King Jaehaerys". Remembered as a bright, biddable child without her sister's timidity — quote: "a bright but unremarkable girl". She was in no haste to leave Dragonstone and welcomed the arriving Wise Women with gifts — quote: "welcomed each of them with glad smiles and gifts".

### Alyssa Velaryon
Queen Regent, widow of King Aenys, wife of the Hand Rogar Baratheon, and mother of the three queens' generation — quote: "widow of the late King Aenys, mother to his son Jaehaerys". She wished the king's rash marriage undone, fearing it would set the Faith against the Crown, yet could seldom bring herself to act; as Septon Barth wrote, she "desired above all to be loved" — quote: "She desired above all to be loved, admired, and praised". She saw the sending of companions as a chance to undo the marriage — quote: "an opportunity to undermine, and mayhaps undo, her marriage".

### Rogar Baratheon
Hand of the King and husband of the Queen Regent; humiliated when forced to back down at the gates of Dragonstone — quote: "humiliated when forced to back down at the gates of Dragonstone". His first impulse was to take Dragonstone by storm until Alyssa dissuaded him — quote: "my children ride dragons, and we do not." He wanted eyes and ears on Dragonstone and the marriage's consummation prevented — quote: "the Hand needed eyes and ears on Dragonstone". Septon Barth's charity is invoked for him — quote: "he had been a hero once".

### Rhaena Targaryen
Eldest daughter of Aenys and Alyssa, a dragonrider and widow of Aegon the Uncrowned, now wed to Androw Farman and called the Queen in the West — quote: "men were already calling her the Queen in the West". She grew resentful that her claim, and her daughters', had been passed over for her "baby brother" — quote: "resentful that her own claim to the Iron Throne". She could neither forget nor forgive Rogar's inaction while her husband faced Maegor — quote: "his inaction when her own husband, Prince Aegon, faced Maegor".

### Coryanne Wylde
Younger daughter of Morgan Wylde of the Rain House; seduced at thirteen by a stable groom and confined to bear a bastard son in 48 AC — quote: "give birth to his baseborn son". She was among the companions sent to Dragonstone and is the alleged author of A Caution for Young Girls — quote: "the alleged author of A Caution for Young Girls is none other than Coryanne Wylde". According to that book, a great lord met her at an inn and charged her to beguile the king into bed, though the man's identity is disputed — quote: "the identity of the man at the inn is a matter of some dispute".

### Androw Farman
Second son of Lord Farman, nine years younger than Rhaena, mocked even at his father's court as "half a girl" and never made a knight — quote: "he was soft of speech and gentle of nature". Rhaena said she chose him for his kindness — quote: "He was kind to me."

### Elissa Farman
Androw's elder sister, three years his senior, sharp of wit and tongue, whose great love was sailing and who longed to sail beyond the Sunset Sea — quote: "her great love was sailing". Lord Farman's maester wrote that Rhaena's true love on Fair Isle was Elissa, not Androw — quote: "not with Androw, but with his sister, Lady Elissa".

### A Caution for Young Girls
A distasteful, banned book that appeared some forty years after these events, circulating under several titles — quote: "known under various titles". Hundreds of copies exist though many more were burned by Baelor the Blessed — quote: "as many more were burned by Baelor the Blessed". Its versions disagree on the man at the inn: the older, shorter Sins of the Flesh and The High and the Low name Ser Borys Baratheon, while A Wanton's Tale and The Wickedness of Men name Lord Rogar himself — quote: "the man is Lord Rogar himself".

### Maegor I Targaryen
Maegor the Cruel, uncle of Jaehaerys and Rhaena, brother of Aenys, son of Visenya, who returned from across the narrow sea to seize the Iron Throne — quote: "returned from across the narrow sea to seize the Iron Throne". He made Jaehaerys and Alysanne wards of Visenya and demanded Rhaena's capture — quote: "King Maegor, was demanding her capture".

### Aenys I Targaryen
King, first husband of Alyssa and father of Jaehaerys, slighted as weak beside his warrior brother Maegor — quote: "His father, King Aenys, had been slighted as weak". He died on Dragonstone, after which Maegor seized the throne — quote: "when King Aenys died on Dragonstone".

### Visenya Targaryen
Dowager Queen and mother of Maegor, who enforced his will and held Jaehaerys and Alysanne as wards until her death in 44 AC freed Alyssa to flee — quote: "made wards of their great-aunt, the fearsome Visenya".

### Merrell Bullock
Commander of the Dragonstone castle garrison and father of Ser Alyn and Ser Howard — quote: "Ser Merrell Bullock, commander of the castle garrison".

### Elyas Scales
Seasoned master-at-arms on Dragonstone who trained the king and judged he might best a risen Maegor — quote: "a seasoned master-at-arms in Ser Elyas Scales".

### Pate the Woodcock
Young garrison man who won the contest to "kill" the king most in training bouts — quote: "the victor, we are told, was young Ser Pate the Woodcock".

### Culiper
Aged Maester of Dragonstone, eighty and serving since Visenya's day — quote: "Maester Culiper, still spry at eighty, had been serving".

### Barth
Septon Barth, called the wisest man ever to serve as Hand, quoted on human nature and on Queen Alyssa's yearnings — quote: "the wisest man ever to serve as the Hand of the King".

### Mattheus
Septon Mattheus, who spoke of little but how decent folk would condemn the king's incestuous union — quote: "how “all decent folk” would condemn the king's".

### Lucinda Tully
Fiercely pious wife of the Lord of Riverrun, set over Alysanne's maids and charged by Rogar to report on the king and queen — quote: "Lady Lucinda Tully, the wife of the Lord of Riverrun".

### Ella Broome
Lucinda's younger sister, a modest maid whose name had briefly been offered as a match for Jaehaerys — quote: "her younger sister, Ella of House Broome".

### Borys Baratheon
Eldest of Lord Rogar's four brothers, named in some versions of the book as the man at the inn and later blamed by Archmaester Crey for the calumny — quote: "eldest of Lord Rogar's four brothers".

### Morgan Wylde
Lord of the Rain House and father of Coryanne — quote: "Morgan Wylde, Lord of the Rain House".

### Lord Farman
Lord of Fair Isle, father of Androw and Elissa, who gave the widowed Rhaena refuge when Maegor demanded her capture — quote: "offering her refuge on Fair Isle".

### Crey
Archmaester writing late in Jaehaerys's reign, who held the inn meeting a calumny meant to blacken Lord Rogar — quote: "a clumsy calumny intended to blacken the name of Lord Rogar".

### Ryben
Maester and the Citadel's foremost expert on obscene texts, who dismissed the tale as a bawdy invention — quote: "the Citadel's foremost expert on banned, forbidden, fraudulent, and obscene texts".

### Smike
Maester serving Lord Farman, who wrote to the Citadel that Rhaena found her true love in Elissa — quote: "Maester Smike wrote to the Citadel".

### Benifer
Grand Maester who attributes the plan of sending companions to Queen Alyssa — quote: "Grand Maester Benifer assures us".

### Oswyck
Aged Septon who kept the sept on Dragonstone and performed the royal wedding — quote: "who had performed the wedding rites for Jaehaerys and Alysanne".

### Ysabel
Formidable Septa sent to see to Alysanne's religious instruction — quote: "the formidable Septa Ysabel".

### Lord Tully
Lord of Riverrun on the regency council, who warned Jaehaerys could simply close Dragonstone's gates — quote: "When Lord Tully pointed out that Jaehaerys could simply close the gates".

### Aerea Targaryen
Rhaena's willful and stubborn daughter — quote: "the willful and stubborn temperament of Rhaena’s daughter Aerea".

### Aegon the Uncrowned
Rhaena's late first husband, Prince Aegon, who faced Maegor at the Battle Beneath the Gods Eye — quote: "widow of Prince Aegon the Uncrowned".
