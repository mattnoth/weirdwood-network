## Node Prose
### Jaehaerys I Targaryen
He returned to King's Landing alone on Vermithor, having grown from a stripling into a man since flying to Dragonstone — quote: "returned to King’s Landing alone, on the wings of his dragon". He bore his grandsire's sword Blackfyre on his swordbelt — quote: "he bore Blackfyre...his grandsire's sword, the sword of kings." He remade the small council, dismissing men who had proved unequal and cleaning out lesser offices and the dungeons — quote: "began his realm by remaking the council". He pardoned Lord Rogar under strict conditions rather than execute or exile him — quote: "Then | pardon your offenses". He resolved the empty treasury through foreign loans and new taxes on luxury goods and crenellations, and answered his sister-marriage's opponents with preachers rather than steel — quote: "We shall fight words with words".

### Alysanne Targaryen
She remained on Dragonstone while Jaehaerys returned, then came to King's Landing on Silverwing after half a year apart — quote: "apart from the king for nigh on half a year." She was wed to Jaehaerys a second time and, at her own insistence, the marriage was consummated with a bedding — quote: "the marriage of Jaehaerys Targaryen and his sister Alysanne was consummated". She began attending council meetings and set about making the Red Keep glitter with singers, mummers, and art, and later told the king she was with child — quote: "she told him she was with child."

### Alyssa Velaryon
The Dowager Queen, no longer a regent, was found weeping in Maegor's Holdfast and lost her seat at council — quote: "The Dowager Queen, a regent no more". As a condition of Rogar's pardon she returned to Storm's End to live as his wife again — quote: "you will live as husband and wife once again." She had made her late husband Aenys's court a splendid place of song and beauty — quote: "had made the court a splendid place".

### Rhaena Targaryen
The widowed Queen in the West flew to King's Landing on Dreamfyre for the wedding and to visit her daughter Aerea — quote: "to visit her daughter Aerea." After a fruitless progress through the westerlands, she demanded a seat of her own, claiming Dragonstone as her birthright — quote: "I want Dragonstone." Jaehaerys granted it as a gift and restored Aerea to her, and she became the Queen in the East — quote: "the Queen in the West at a stroke became the Queen in the East."

### Aerea Targaryen
Rhaena's daughter, a girl of eight, was Jaehaerys's acknowledged successor and heir apparent to the Iron Throne — quote: "his own acknowledged successor, heir apparent to the Iron Throne." Jaehaerys restored her to Rhaena, perhaps too hastily — quote: "mayhaps too hastily".

### Rogar Baratheon
Summoned back to King's Landing, the Lord of Storm's End came with only six old knights and humbled himself before the Iron Throne — quote: "fell to one knee, bowed his head, and laid his sword". Jaehaerys pardoned him despite his treasonous words and plans, requiring him to champion the queen and honor Alyssa — quote: "you shall be her loudest champion". Roaring drunk at the wedding, he led the men who disrobed the queen at the bedding — quote: "Lord Rogar himself, roaring drunk, led the men who disrobed her".

### Rego Draz
A Pentoshi merchant who rose from nothing to be the richest man in Pentos, shunned there for low birth, he answered the king's call and was named master of coin and a lord — quote: "the richest man in Pentos". Landless, he was dubbed "the Lord of Air" — quote: "dubbed him “the Lord of Air."" He secured three loans by playing the banks against one another and devised the king's luxury and spice taxes — quote: "not one but three substantial loans."

### Albin Massey
Lord of Stonedance, named master of laws, he had been forging a maester's chain at the Citadel until fever killed his father and elder brothers — quote: "forging a maester's chain at the Citadel only three years earlier". A twisted spine left him with a limp — quote: "A twisted spine condemned him to walk with a limp".

### Manfryd Redwyne
The Lord of the Arbor was named lord admiral and master of ships, coming to court with his squire sons Robert, Rickard, and Ryam — quote: "his young sons Robert, Rickard, and Ryam, squires all". His appointment ended House Velaryon's hold on the admiralty — quote: "the first time the admiralty had gone to any man not of House Velaryon."

### Edwell Celtigar
Dismissed as master of coin to the realm's joy, he left for Claw Isle while his taxes were struck down — quote: "dismissed Edwell Celtigar as master of coin". The king praised his daughters as "two treasures" — quote: "name them “two treasures.”"

### Septon Mattheus
The corpulent prelate who had railed against the sibling marriage was dismissed and took it poorly — quote: "fulminated so loudly against incestuous unions". He departed for Oldtown in a gilded wheelhouse — quote: "he traveled in a gilded wheelhouse".

### Barth
A young man summoned from Highgarden to see to the royal library — quote: "a young man coming from Highgarden to see to our library." He pronounced Jaehaerys and Alysanne man and wife and helped craft the Doctrine of Exceptionalism — quote: "it was Septon Barth who pronounced them man and wife". Legend says he passed the departing Mattheus at Bitterbridge, riding alone on a donkey — quote: "Barth was alone, riding on a donkey."

### Benifer
Grand Maester who narrates the king's return and reign; he had fled to Pentos when Maegor died and feared his return — quote: "The day was not an auspicious one, Grand Maester Benifer tells us."

### Orryn Baratheon
Rogar's brother, sent to Oldtown to remove Rhaella and held there by Hightower, then sentenced to ten years' exile — quote: "free your brother Ser Orryn". He never returned to Westeros, taking service with the Archon of Tyrosh and marrying his daughter — quote: "he married the Archon's daughter". He later joined the Maiden's Men in Myr and was killed in the Disputed Lands — quote: "He was killed soon afterward in the Disputed Lands".

### Elinor Costayne
Queen Elinor, "the Black Bride" who found Maegor dead upon the Iron Throne, was the only Speaker known to the commons — quote: "the Black Bride who had found Maegor dead". She traveled the Reach preaching the goodness of Maegor's successors and later became Mother Elinor at Lannisport — quote: "rising eventually to become Mother Elinor".

### Vermithor
Jaehaerys's growing bronze dragon that he flew home and fed a slaughtered bull; the king let his mount stand as surety over the stormlands — quote: "He grows larger every day".

### Septon Alfyn
One of the Seven Speakers, legless and carried in a litter, who preached the Doctrine that Targaryens are exceptional — quote: "fierce old Septon Alfyn, who had lost his legs".

### Tom the Strummer
A singer from Flea Bottom whose mocking songs had amused all during the War for the White Cloaks; Alysanne made him the court singer — quote: "Alysanne made him the court singer".
