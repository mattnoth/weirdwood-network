## Node Prose
### Aegon III Targaryen
Through the "secret siege" the boy king met every appeal with sullen, stubborn silence, roused to anger only once by his master-at-arms — quote: "who will you punish, ser?" When Rowan's tortured confession was heard he was left bereft of speech — quote: "The confession left King Aegon III bereft of speech." He then declared Rowan innocent and ordered the Lord Confessor seized — quote: "this man is my Hand and innocent of treason." On his sixteenth nameday he took the governance of the realm, cancelling the progress and dismissing his regents and Hand — quote: "There will be no progress." He did not leave Maegor's Holdfast for six days after the confession was unravelled — quote: "Aegon III did not leave Maegor's Holdfast for six more days."

### Viserys Targaryen
It fell to Prince Viserys to answer the confession, and by mock-questions he exposed it as false, drawing Rowan into confessing that Gaemon and Viserys himself were among the guilty — quote: "Gaemon was as guilty as the rest of us...of nothing." He supported Munkun's proposal to choose regents by lot, and shut down calls to restore Unwin Peake as Hand — quote: "his brother would prefer a younger man." He attended the trials throughout, often with his wife — quote: "Prince Viserys was present throughout."

### Larra of Lys
Lady Larra, wife of Prince Viserys, was named a conspirator in Rowan's false confession — quote: "And my wife, Lady Larra." During the trials her belly was swelling with a second child — quote: "her belly swelling with their second child." She wept when Lord Torrhen handed down his verdict on her brothers, and later bore Viserys a second son and disciplined her elder son — quote: "she wept when Lord Torrhen handed down his verdict."

### Marston Waters
Ser Marston Waters, bastard-born, had risen from King Aegon II's retinue to Lord Commander of the Kingsguard and Hand — quote: "a modest place in the retinue of King Aegon II." Larys Strong had chosen him to hide the king during Rhaenyra's ascendancy — quote: "to hide the king during Rhaenyra's ascendancy." He held back from storming Maegor's, weighed down by his vow to the king he had sworn to protect — quote: "Unwilling to dishonor the white cloak he wore." He obeyed Aegon's command to seize Graceford, then was mortally stabbed by Mervyn Flowers and died that night — quote: "pronounced the wound mortal."

### Thaddeus Rowan
Lord Rowan was brought forth in chains, his face bruised beyond recognition and his teeth gone, to confess to crimes recited by Septon Bernard — quote: "Thaddeus Rowan was brought forth in chains." Prince Viserys exposed the confession as the product of torture — quote: "those who tortured him to bring forth this false confession." Broken by the dungeons, he was relieved of office and died on the road to Goldengrove with two of his sons — quote: "he died upon the road in the company of two of his sons."

### Munkun
Grand Maester Munkun made repeated appeals to the king, confirmed that the poison was the Tears of Lys, and examined Marston Waters's mortal wound — quote: "an assertion that Grand Maester Munkun then confirmed." He summoned the lords by raven, proposed that three regents be chosen by lot, and served as both regent and Hand for the rest of the year — quote: "the Grand Maester served as both regent and Hand." He urged the appointment of Maester Rowley as Lord Confessor — quote: "A man who knows how to ease pain will also know how to inflict it."

### Mushroom
The fool claims the queen's ladies donned mail to feign more defenders — quote: "Queen Daenaera's ladies donned mail and took up spears." In his *Testimony* he names Unwin Peake as the hidden "player in the shadows" — quote: "player in the shadows," Mushroom calls him" He confesses to tormenting the broken Rowan into absurd confessions — quote: "made him confess to the Doom of Valyria." He accepted Manderly's offer of a place at White Harbor rather than stay with the king — quote: "I was a fool but never such a fool as to stay with that fool."

### George Graceford
The Lord Confessor tortured Rowan to produce the false confession, and was seized on the king's command — quote: "beckoned to the Lord Confessor, George Graceford, to bring the prisoner forward." Faced with the instruments, he named the other conspirators — quote: "the sight of the instruments was all." Condemned to die, he chose to take the black. Mushroom says he and Lady Clarice Osgrey were lovers — quote: "she and George Graceford were lovers."

### Mervyn Flowers
Ser Mervyn Flowers, a bastard knight of the Kingsguard, was named among the conspirators by Graceford — quote: "Ser Mervyn Flowers of the Kingsguard." When Marston Waters came for him he offered his hilt in feigned surrender, then drew a dagger and stabbed him — quote: "plunged it into Waters's belly." He killed a man-at-arms and two stableboys before being overwhelmed and beaten to death — quote: "overwhelmed and beaten to death, still clad in the white cloak."

### Gareth Long
Ser Gareth Long, the king's master-at-arms, tried and failed to make the king yield, and was named a conspirator — quote: "his master-at-arms, Ser Gareth Long." At trial he declared the king unfit — quote: "Aegon Ill was a weakling unfit to hold a sword." Condemned, he took the black, but was brought back to slay Ser Victor Risley in a trial by battle — quote: "The former master-at-arms dispatched the former headsman quickly."

### Septon Bernard
Septon Bernard prayed for the king and recited Lord Rowan's alleged crimes at the confession — quote: "Septon Bernard detailed Lord Rowan’s alleged crimes." As a plotter he argued from his Faith that the Lyseni had no place in the Seven Kingdoms and that Larra was to die with her brothers. The High Septon won him clemency; he was gelded and made to walk to Oldtown with his manhood about his neck — quote: "Bernard was gelded and condemned to walk barefoot from King's Landing to Oldtown." He lived as a silent scribe — quote: "copying holy books at the Starry Sept."

### Tessario
Tessario the Thumb, a Volantene, was the frankest of the plotters, having acted for gold, girls, and vengeance after Roggerio banned him from the Mermaid — quote: "Roggerio Rogare had banned him from the Mermaid for striking one of his whores." Fleeing, he was taken in a dockside tavern near the River Gate — quote: "was taken in a dockside tavern." Under torture he named Marston Waters, then Graceford, then Flowers, dying before a fourth name — quote: "The first name he gave was that of Marston Waters."

### Victor Risley
Ser Victor Risley, once the King's Justice, was named a conspirator and demanded a trial by battle to prove his innocence — quote: "Ser Victor Risley, once the King’s Justice, stood upon his right." Gareth Long killed him quickly, then turned to the throne room and asked, "Anyone else?" — quote: "You always were a bloody fool, Victor."

### Lucas Leygood
Ser Lucas Leygood commanded the gold cloaks, more than two thousand strong, and was named among the plotters — quote: "Ser Lucas Leygood’s gold cloaks numbered more than two thousand." A short, savage battle at the Gate of the Gods, when men came for him, left him dead — quote: "leaving nine dead, amongst them Leygood himself."

### Unwin Peake
Once Hand and Lord of Starpike, Dunstonbury, and Whitegrove, Peake is named by Mushroom as the hidden master of the plot — quote: "Dunstonbury, Lord of Whitegrove, once Hand of the King." Several traitors had blood ties to him or owed him their places, and he had once planned to murder two dragonriders under the sign of the Bloody Caltrops, but he was at Starpike during the siege and his involvement stayed unproven — quote: "his involvement remained unproven, then as now." He brought the largest retinue to the gathering — quote: "a thousand of his own men and five hundred sellswords."

### Torrhen Manderly
The lords chose Torrhen Manderly, Lord of White Harbor, as Hand for having no enemies south of the Neck — quote: "the lords turned to the northman, Torrhen Manderly, Lord of White Harbor." He judged the treason trials, reformed the taxes with Isembard Arryn's help, filled the Kingsguard, and planned a royal progress — quote: "Torrhen Manderly proved to be an honest and able Hand." He never warmed to the king, calling him "that sullen boy," and was curtly dismissed on the king's nameday — quote: "You may return to White Harbor." He left for White Harbor an enemy of the king — quote: "brusque and gluttonous, but fair."

### Alyn Velaryon
Lord Alyn, called Oakenfist, and Bloody Ben Blackwood forced the Vale lords to bend the knee to Joffrey Arryn — quote: "forced the Gilded Falcon, the Mad Heir, the Bronze Giant." He put forward Isembard Arryn's name for master of coin — quote: "Then up stood Oakenfist, to offer the name of Isembard Arryn." Having lost half the Velaryon wealth entrusted to Lotho Rogare, he planned the second of his six great voyages and quarreled then reconciled with Baela before sailing at midyear — quote: "began to make plans for the second of his six great voyages."

### Baela Targaryen
Lady Baela, of the blood of the dragon and quick to anger, quarreled with her husband over his talk of Princess Aliandra of Dorne — quote: "Lady Baela was of the blood of the dragon and quick to anger." She remained on Driftmark carrying his second child, and declared she would join the royal progress unwanted or not — quote: "Lady Baela remained on Driftmark with Lord Alyn’s second child."

### Cassandra Baratheon
Lady Cassandra, late Lord Borros's eldest child and once betrothed to Aegon II, confessed to sharing her bed with Mervyn Flowers and, at his behest, with Tessario — quote: "she had often shared her bed with Ser Mervyn Flowers." Her high birth spared her mutilation; she was instead wed to Ser Walter Brownhill of Cape Wrath — quote: "wed a minor knight named Ser Walter Brownhill."

### Lucinda Penrose
Lady Lucinda, attacked and her nose slit while hawking before the Maiden's Day Ball, admitted wanting Daenaera dead — quote: "If my nose had not been slit, it would be her serving me." She was condemned to have her nose cut off unless she gave herself to the Faith — quote: "condemned to have their noses cut off."

### Priscella Hogg
Lady Priscella, a simple girl of fourteen, believed Prince Viserys would marry her if Larra of Lys were dead — quote: "Prince Viserys would marry her if only Larra of Lys were dead." Like Lucinda she was condemned to lose her nose unless she took the Faith.

### Clarice Osgrey
Lady Clarice, widowed aunt of Unwin Peake, supervised Queen Daenaera's attendants as she had Queen Jaehaera's before — quote: "Lady Clarice Osgrey, widowed aunt of Lord Unwin Peake." The condemned women denied she was complicit; Mushroom says she and George Graceford were lovers.

### Lysaro Rogare
Named the architect of his family's doom, Lysaro was captured at Volon Therys on the Rhoyne as his Unsullied died protecting him — quote: "captured in the town of Volon Therys on the Rhoyne." Returned to Lys chained to an oar, at trial he named the magisters he had bribed but was condemned all the same — quote: "I used it to bribe him, and him, and him, and him." He was scourged to death at the Temple of Trade, his sister and brother among those wielding the whip — quote: "Lysaro expired in the seventh hour of the first day of his scourging."

### Lotho Rogare
The banker Lotho was adjudged guilty of theft and given the choice of the black or the loss of his right hand — quote: "having his right hand removed as if he were a common thief." He chose mutilation — quote: "Then praise Yndros, | am left-handed." Short a hand, he was taken up by Lady Samantha to help found the Bank of Oldtown.

### Roggerio Rogare
Nothing could be proved against Roggerio, but Manderly gave him seven lashes anyway — quote: "For being a thrice-damned Lyseni." He closed his brothel, bought a great cog named the Mermaid's Daughter, and traded up and down the narrow sea — quote: "a great cog he named the Mermaid’s Daughter."

### Moredo Rogare
Eldest of the three brothers who came with Larra, Moredo was in Braavos during the trials, treating with the Iron Bank's keyholders — quote: "treating with the keyholders of the Iron Bank." He would sail for Tyrosh to hire ships and swords for an attack on Lys, and years later pulled down Lysaro's bones for burial — quote: "to hire ships and swords for an attack on Lys."

### Isembard Arryn
The Gilded Falcon of the Vale, forced to bend the knee to Joffrey Arryn, was put forward by Oakenfist and made master of coin, helping Manderly reform the taxes — quote: "to offer the name of Isembard Arryn, the Gilded Falcon of the Vale."

### Samantha
Lady Sam, paramour of Lord Lyonel Hightower, took up the maimed Lotho Rogare to learn how to put Hightower gold to use, founding the Bank of Oldtown — quote: "taken up by Lady Samantha, the paramour of Lord Lyonel Hightower."

### Raynard Ruskyn
Ser Raynard was the one Kingsguard within the holdfast, held prisoner after being wounded by the Lyseni — quote: "Ser Raynard Ruskyn was a prisoner." He later became Lord Commander of the Kingsguard — quote: "Ser Raynard Ruskyn became Lord Commander of the Kingsguard."

### Gaemon Palehair
Aegon III's young friend, killed by the poison meant for the king — quote: "Lord Rowan’s foul plot took the life of your young."

### Sandoq the Shadow
The Lysene giant who guarded Lady Larra; only he and six Lyseni remained at her side during the siege, and he later stood veiled and silent behind the king when Aegon claimed his rule — quote: "So did Sandog the Shadow, veiled and silent, carrying his great sword."

### Walter Brownhill
A minor, thrice-widowed knight of Cape Wrath who had fathered sixteen children by his previous wives, wed to Cassandra Baratheon — quote: "fathered sixteen children by his previous wives, thirteen of whom still lived."

### Aemon (son of Viserys)
On the eve of Smith's Day Larra bore Prince Viserys a large and lusty second son named Aemon — quote: "a large and lusty boy that the prince named Aemon."

### Aegon (son of Viserys)
Viserys's toddler son, discovered hitting the newborn Aemon with the dragon's egg set in the cradle — quote: "hitting the babe with the dragon's egg that had been placed inside the cradle."
