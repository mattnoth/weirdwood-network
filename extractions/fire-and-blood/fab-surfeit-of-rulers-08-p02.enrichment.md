## Node Prose
### Rhaena Targaryen
Rhaena, the Queen in the West, kept a court-within-the-court at Faircastle centered on herself, Elissa Farman, Alayne Royce, and Samantha Stokeworth — the "Four-Headed Beast" — while her husband Androw was only admitted from time to time — quote: "a court within the court...dubbed “the Four-Headed Beast.”" She flew her dragon Dreamfyre with her lady companions but never took Androw — quote: "Queen Rhaena never took him flying with her on the back of her dragon, Dreamfyre". When the new Lord Franklyn ordered her off Fair Isle she departed that night for Casterly Rock — quote: "She departed Fair Isle that very night, taking wing for Casterly Rock". She dissuaded Androw from fighting his brother, saying a thrice-widowing would see her named a witch — quote: "were | to be thrice widowed, men would name me a witch or worse". At Casterly Rock she came to realize Lord Lyman wanted an alliance with the Iron Throne and dragons of his own, and resolved to leave — quote: "“We cannot remain here,” she confided to her dear companions".

### Elissa Farman
Elissa Farman, daughter of Lord Marq, had been twice betrothed but frightened off both boys — quote: "she had frightened off both...boys, as her own father admitted ruefully". She found a like-minded companion in Queen Rhaena — quote: "In Rhaena Targaryen, however, she found a like-minded companion". Her brother Franklyn tried to keep her on Fair Isle to be wed to a man of his choosing, but the dockside smallfolk overwhelmed his men and she sailed away on her ship the Maiden's Fancy — quote: "his sister would remain on Fair Isle to be wed".

### Androw Farman
Androw Farman, Rhaena's husband and Lord Marq's weakling son, was admitted to the Four-Headed Beast only occasionally and was not of an adventurous disposition — quote: "he was not of an adventurous disposition". He offered to face his brother Franklyn in single combat but the queen dissuaded him — quote: "offered to face his brother in single combat, the queen quickly dissuaded him".

### Franklyn Farman
Ser Franklyn, Lord Marq's elder son, fought and was wounded at the Battle Beneath the Gods Eye, and brought home the corpses of his grandsire and eldest uncle who died there — quote: "Ser Franklyn had fought in the Battle Beneath the Gods Eye and taken a wound there". He resented Rhaena's lack of gratitude and her friendship with his sister Elissa — quote: "He also resented her friendship with his sister, Elissa". On becoming Lord of Fair Isle he summoned Rhaena and commanded her to leave the island — quote: "commanded her to remove herself from his island". At the docks he was overwhelmed by the crowd and thrown into a ship's hold of cod — quote: "Lord Franklyn himself was thrown into a ship’s hold full of fresh-caught cod".

### Marq Farman
Lord Marq Farman was an amiable, good-natured man who loved all his children and loved Rhaena for loving them — quote: "his lordship was an amiable and good-natured man who loved all his children". Less than a fortnight after the couple's first anniversary he choked to death on a fish bone at his own table at the age of six-and-forty — quote: "Lord Marq died suddenly at his own table, choking to death upon a fish bone".

### Lyman Lannister
Lyman Lannister, Lord of Casterly Rock, had earlier given Rhaena and Aegon the Uncrowned refuge from Maegor, and welcomed her again with a lavish feast — quote: "had given Rhaena and her husband Aegon the Uncrowned refuge". He grew interested in the queen's three dragon eggs and offered to buy them outright for a staggering sum — quote: "Lord Lyman offered to buy the eggs...outright for a staggering sum of gold". Rhaena judged he sought an alliance with the Iron Throne and dragonriders of his own — quote: "He wanted an alliance with the Iron Throne".

### Tyler Hill
Ser Tyler Hill, Lord Lyman's comely bastard son, fought under Prince Aegon at the Gods Eye and was openly scornful of Androw while trying to ingratiate himself with Rhaena — quote: "openly scornful of Androw, even whilst doing all he could to ingratiate himself to Rhaena".

### Jocasta Lannister
Lady Jocasta of House Tarbeck, Lord Lyman's wife, had befriended Rhaena at the Rock and been first to discern she was with child — quote: "the first to discern that she was with child". She privately suggested a dragon egg would make a fine gift to House Lannister — quote: "one or more of the eggs would make a fine gift".

### Alyssa Velaryon
Queen Alyssa, the Queen Regent, hoped her Wise Women and piety would break Jaehaerys and Alysanne apart, but at last accepted the marriage — quote: "“It is done and cannot be undone, Seven save us."". When Rogar demanded they put Jaehaerys aside, she dismissed him from the Hand's office — quote: "“Your service no longer pleases me, Lord Rogar."". She had Lord Corbray spirit Princess Aerea away and named her brother Daemon Velaryon Hand, then withdrew from public life — quote: "named her brother Daemon Velaryon as Hand of the King". Septon Barth wrote that this final rift shattered her — quote: "“It shattered her.”"

### Rogar Baratheon
Lord Rogar, Hand of the King, was determined to complete the Dragonpit and wroth at the lords crawling to Dragonstone — quote: "Lord Rogar was determined to complete work on the Dragonpit". When his plan to break the royal marriage failed he urged the council to set Jaehaerys aside and crown Princess Aerea, whose mother had a dragon — quote: "“We must act now and put him aside.”". Faced with Corbray's drawn sword he was dismissed; he flung down his brooch of office and left King's Landing — quote: "removed the golden brooch...flung it at her contemptuously". He then plotted through his brother Orryn to seize the novice Rhaella and pass her off as Aerea, and on that failing fell into despair and drafted a will and confession — quote: "named Ser Borys as heir to Storm's End".

### Jaehaerys
King Jaehaerys, the boy king, kept a self-imposed exile on Dragonstone, training with his Kingsguard and studying the reign of his grandsire Aegon the Conqueror — quote: "poring over accounts of the reign of his grandsire Aegon the Conqueror". More and more lords came to Dragonstone to talk with him — quote: "more and more visitors made their way to Dragonstone to talk with the king". On the twentieth day of the ninth moon of 50 AC he reached his sixteenth nameday and became a man grown, ending the regency — quote: "Jaehaerys Targaryen reached his sixteenth nameday and became a man grown".

### Alysanne
Queen Alysanne remained on Dragonstone convinced the gods meant her to marry her brother Jaehaerys, and won over the Wise Women sent to dissuade her — quote: "“He will be a great king,” she told Septa Ysabel...“and | will be a great queen.”".

### Edwell Celtigar
Edwell Celtigar, Lord of Claw Isle, had been an ineffectual Hand for Maegor and proved an equally ineffectual master of coin, imposing new taxes on the smallfolk of King's Landing — quote: "decided to impose new taxes on the smallfolk of King’s Landing". His levies emptied inns, slowed building, drove trade to other ports, and made him loathed throughout the city — quote: "serve to make Lord Celtigar loathed throughout the city".

### Brandon Stark
Brandon Stark, Lord of Winterfell, died in 49 AC not long after returning from the Golden Wedding, the journey said to have asked too much of him — quote: "Brandon Stark, Lord of Winterfell, had died in 49 AC".

### Walton Stark
Walton Stark succeeded his father and rode to the Wall to put down the Night's Watch rebellion — quote: "His son Walton succeeded him". Pursuing rebels into the haunted forest he was set upon by giants, slew two, and was torn apart — quote: "he and his men were set upon by giants".

### Alaric Stark
Alaric Stark, Brandon's second son, became Lord of Winterfell and ruled the North twenty-three years, an able but stern man who long blamed Jaehaerys's clemency for Walton's death — quote: "he blamed the king's clemency for his brother Walton's death".

### Olyver Bracken
Ser Olyver Bracken, a turncloak knight who had left Maegor's Kingsguard for Jaehaerys, was given a crumbling fort to restore and instead rebelled with Raymund Mallery — quote: "the two turncloak knights who had served in Maegor's Kingsguard before abandoning him". He was beheaded by Lord Stark with Ice — quote: "beheaded by Lord Stark with his celebrated blade Ice".

### Raymund Mallery
Ser Raymund Mallery co-led the Night's Watch rebellion, fled beyond the Wall to the wildlings, who killed him and his men — quote: "Ser Raymund’s head was delivered to Eastwatch half a year later".

### Daemon Velaryon
Daemon Velaryon, the Crown's lord admiral and master of ships and Alyssa's brother, bluntly refused Rogar's request to bar lords from Dragonstone, and was later named Hand of the King — quote: "“No,” he said. The Hand took this as a further sign of disrespect".

### Coryanne Wylde
Lady Coryanne of House Wylde, putative author of A Caution for Young Girls, is the subject of contradictory lewd tales about bedding the king. According to A Wanton's Tale she bedded Jaehaerys and all seven Kingsguard — quote: "Lady Coryanne not only bedded the king, but also all seven members of the Kingsguard". The High and the Low has Alysanne join them, while Sins of the Flesh tells it more plausibly, with Coryanne tutoring the fumbling boy king — quote: "as many boys of his age are known to be when first abed with a maid". History records only that on the fifteenth day of the sixth moon of 50 AC she fled Dragonstone by night with Ser Howard Bullock — quote: "she departed Dragonstone under the cover of night in the company of Ser Howard Bullock".

### Aerea Targaryen
Princess Aerea, eight years old and daughter of Rhaena and Aegon the Uncrowned, was named by Rogar as his preferred claimant over Jaehaerys — quote: "“Princess Aerea,” Lord Rogar answered at once". Alyssa had Lord Corbray spirit her away disguised as a common girl with her hair dyed brown; she spent the regency working in a stable near the King's Gate — quote: "Princess Aerea would spend the rest of the regency working in a stable".

### Qarl Corbray
Lord Qarl Corbray, Commander of the City Watch, had fought at the Battle Beneath the Gods Eye — quote: "Qarl Corbray had fought in the Battle Beneath the Gods Eye". He rose against Rogar's treason, laying his Valyrian steel blade Lady Forlorn on the table toward him — quote: "drew his sword, the Valyrian steel blade called Lady Forlorn". He also removed Princess Aerea at the Queen Regent's command — quote: "Lord Corbray had removed her, at the Queen Regent's command".

### Grand Maester Benifer
Grand Maester Benifer sat the regency council and recorded the confrontation between Rogar and Alyssa, including her obsidian-hard eyes and Corbray's drawn sword — quote: "Benifer tells us that her eyes looked as hard and dark as obsidian".

### Orryn Baratheon
Ser Orryn Baratheon appeared at the Oldtown motherhouse with a letter under Rogar's seal demanding the novice Rhaella, meaning to pass her off as Aerea and name her queen — quote: "demanding that the novice Rhaella Targaryen be turned over to them immediately". Confronted by thirty armed septons he surrendered and confessed the plot — quote: "Ser Orryn confessed the entire plot".

### Ronnal Baratheon
Ser Ronnal Baratheon forced his way into Aerea's chambers with a dozen men on the night of Rogar's dismissal, only to find her already gone — quote: "forced their way into her chambers in the Red Keep". He also assembled the Baratheon knights for the march home — quote: "his brother Ronnal assembled their knights and men-at-arms".

### Rhaella Targaryen
Rhaella Targaryen, a novice at the Oldtown motherhouse, was the girl Rogar plotted to seize and force to confess she was the true Princess Aerea — quote: "she was the actual Princess Aerea, not...Rhaella".

### Casper Straw
Casper Straw, steward to the High Septon and a former Warrior's Sons captain, mustered thirty armed septons and bluffed the Baratheons into surrender with a claim that Hightower knights were coming — quote: "twoscore of Lord Hightower's knights were on their way (a lie, as it happened)".

### Donnel Hightower
Lord Donnel Hightower, called Donnel the Delayer, imprisoned the captive Baratheons and sent a full account to the Red Keep and Dragonstone, unafraid of Storm's End — quote: "Donnel Hightower, who had rightly been named Donnel the Delayer".

### Elinor Costayne
The twicewidowed Queen Elinor of House Costayne left King's Landing after Jaehaerys's ascent to visit her three sons by Ser Theo Bolling, then settled at her father's seat of Three Towers to live quietly — quote: "The twicewidowed Queen Elinor of House Costayne...had departed King's Landing".

### Septa Karolyn
Septa Karolyn held the motherhouse door, and her spine of steel and suspicious nature led her to stall Orryn and send secretly to the High Septon — quote: "had a spine of steel and a suspicious nature".

### Maester Smike
Maester Smike of Faircastle noted the highborn lords flocking to see the Queen in the West and wrote to the Citadel of Franklyn's lack of sense before the queen's wrath — quote: "Franklyn Farman did not lack for courage, as Maester Smike wrote".
