## Node Prose

### Aegon II Targaryen
King Aegon II, Second of His Name, proclaimed victory on Dragonstone after Sunfyre devoured Rhaenyra — quote: "after Sunfyre had feasted on his sister". His leap from Sunfyre's back at Dragonstone had broken his right leg in two places and shattered the left, leaving him unable to walk, and burns from Rook's Rest scarred half his body — quote: "His leap from Sunfyre’s back at Dragonstone had broken his right leg in two places". He returned to King's Landing aboard the cog Mouse in the waning days of 130 AC and, unable to climb the Iron Throne, held court from a cushioned wooden seat at its base — quote: "hold court from a carved, cushioned wooden seat at the base of the true throne". He judged and destroyed the three "dayfly kings," burned the Shepherd with his own hand, and pursued vengeance on the crownlands — quote: "set him aflame with his own hand". He was found dead of poison in his litter, having lived four-and-twenty years and reigned two — quote: "He had lived four-andtwenty years and reigned for two". According to Mushroom, his burns had rendered him impotent, though he still watched his favorites couple from behind a curtain and wept afterward — quote: "Mushroom says they had rendered him impotent as well".

### Corlys Velaryon
Corlys Velaryon, the Sea Snake, had been made a captive by Rhaenyra and was a "guest" of Trystane in King's Landing — quote: "that same queen had made their lord a captive". Offered freedom, a pardon, and a council seat, he proved intractable, setting his own terms — quote: "My knees are old and stiff and do not bend easily". He demanded pardons for all who fought for Rhaenyra and that Aegon the Younger wed Jaehaera and be named heir — quote: "Aegon the Younger be given Princess Jaehaera’s hand in marriage". He knelt before Alicent as proxy and was restored as admiral and master of ships — quote: "restored him to his old place on the small council, as admiral and master of ships". In council he favored reconciliation and, when the king refused, denounced them all before storming out — quote: "accused king and council of being “fools, liars, and oathbreakers,”". After Aegon II's death he rode out with Prince Aegon to hail the new king — quote: "The king is dead,” the Sea Snake announced gravely, “long live the king".

### Larys Strong
Larys Strong the Clubfoot advised the squire Trystane to parley and was sent as envoy across the Blackwater — quote: "Send me to parley with them". He brokered the accord in Borros's tent and was betrothed to Floris Baratheon — quote: "an accord was reached between Lord Borros, Lord Larys, and Queen Alicent". His honeyed words swayed king and council, and he calmed Alicent into accepting the Sea Snake's terms — quote: "His words swayed the king and council in their course". Secretly he went to Corlys and revealed the king's plan to grant his demands then murder him — quote: "told him of the king's intent to grant him all he had requested and murder him later". Gyldayn holds his loyalties an unsolvable enigma but concludes Aegon II's poisoning was done at his behest — quote: "we can have no doubt that it was done at the behest of Larys Strong".

### Alicent Hightower
The Dowager Queen Alicent went with Larys and Orwyle to parley with Borros, and wept with joy at news that her granddaughter Jaehaera was safe at Storm's End — quote: "her grandaughter Jaehaera, the only surviving child of her son Aegon and daughter Helaena". She wanted the Sea Snake dead and was outraged by his demands, Munkun tells us, having lost two of three sons and her only daughter in the Dance — quote: "she had suffered the loss of two of her three sons and her only daughter during the Dance". She ruled as proxy, proclaiming a curfew in her son's name and pardoning Corlys — quote: "The City Watch was reformed under the command of Ser Perkin the Flea". She urged her son to vengeance and to mutilate his hostage Prince Aegon — quote: "Cut off one of the boy’s ears and send it to Lord Tully". She was arrested by Velaryon men and returned to the dungeons as her last son died — quote: "The Queen in Chains was chained again and taken to the dungeons".

### Borros Baratheon
Borros Baratheon, Lord of Storm's End, left his pregnant wife and four daughters to march north on hearing of the queen's death — quote: "left his newly pregnant wife and four daughters to strike north". He received the envoys, agreed to wed his daughters to king and Clubfoot, then retook King's Landing and put down the two remaining kings — quote: "an accord was reached between Lord Borros, Lord Larys, and Queen Alicent". In council he scorned reconciliation and urged war — quote: "Casterly Rock and Oldtown should be commanded to raise fresh armies at once". He led the doomed charge at the Muddy Mess, was unhorsed, and slew a dozen knights and the Lords Mallister and Darry before Kermit Tully killed him — quote: "The Lord of Storm's End died in the mud along the kingsroad". Seven days later his widow bore his heir, naming him Royce rather than Aegon — quote: "Lady Baratheon named the child Royce, after her own father".

### Trystane Truefyre
Trystane was a squire who styled himself King Trystane Truefyre and held the battlements with Larys and Perkin — quote: "the squire now calling himself King Trystane Truefyre". Taken and chained when the stormlanders came, he was sentenced to die for high treason — quote: "was sentenced to die for high treason". His courage failed only when he saw Perkin standing with the king; he asked to be knighted before dying — quote: "asked only that he might be made a knight before he died". Marston Waters dubbed him Ser Trystane Fyre and Alfred Broome beheaded him with Blackfyre — quote: "Ser Alfred Broome struck his head off with Blackfyre".

### Gaemon Palehair
Gaemon Palehair, the "Cunny King," was a boy who had just turned five and was routed on Visenya's Hill — quote: "the “Cunny King,” Gaemon Palehair". Spared for his youth, he was made a ward of the Crown — quote: "the boy was spared on account of his youth and made a ward of the Crown". His mother Essie confessed under torture that his father was not the king but a Lyseni oarsman — quote: "Gaemon's father was not the king, as she had previously claimed".

### Shepherd
The Shepherd, a one-handed prophet, called his "barefoot army" to defend the Hill of Rhaenys but few obeyed — quote: "But the Shepherd's star had fallen". Taken alive in the ruined Dragonpit, he cursed Borros with words he later repeated to the king — quote: "We shall meet in hell before this year is done". For his insolence Aegon had his tongue torn out with hot pincers, then burned him among the dragon skulls with 241 of his followers — quote: "the Shepherd's tongue torn out with hot pincers".

### Perkin the Flea
Ser Perkin the Flea, a gutter knight, had raised the squire Trystane as king but abandoned him to join the stormlanders — quote: "Ser Perkin and his gutter knights would join the stormlanders". He reported the pretender taken, was given command of the reformed City Watch, and later turned coat again in the coup — quote: "We have a new king now,” answered Ser Perkin". He barred Alfred Broome from Prince Aegon and shoved him to his death on the drawbridge spikes — quote: "sending him staggering off the drawbridge onto the iron spikes below".

### Alyn Velaryon
Alyn of Hull, younger son of Mouse and brother of the dead Addam, took command of the Velaryon fleets at fifteen — quote: "command of the Velaryon fleets now rested with Addam’s brother, Alyn". His allegiance uncertain, he was courted with pardons then threatened with Baela's execution, and he gathered ships to attack Dragonstone — quote: "young Alyn Velaryon gathering his ships for an attack on Dragonstone". The Sea Snake's heir, he sent the king home aboard the humble cog Mouse — a deliberate slight, Mushroom claims — quote: "he wanted him seen to be creeping into the city on a mouse".

### Baela Targaryen
Lady Baela Targaryen, Aegon II's cousin and Rhaena's twin, was held in the Dragonstone dungeons — quote: "his cousin Lady Baela be brought up from the dungeons". Twice the king ordered her death, staying his hand only when reminded her mother was a Velaryon, the Sea Snake's daughter — quote: "the girl’s mother had been a Velaryon, the Sea Snake's own daughter". Brought to King's Landing in chains, she was spirited to safety by Larys's agents during the coup — quote: "spirited away to safety by agents of Lord Larys the Clubfoot".

### Rhaena Targaryen
Lady Rhaena, Baela's twin, had carried a dragon's egg to the Vale — quote: "Lady Rhaena of House Targaryen, brave Baela’s twin, had brought a dragon's egg with her to the Vale". The egg proved fertile, hatching a pale pink dragon she named Morning, whose birth alarmed the green council — quote: "an egg that had proved fertile, bringing forth a pale pink hatchling with black horns and crest".

### Kermit Tully
Ser Kermit Tully became Lord of Riverrun on his father Elmo's death, a wild and headstrong youth — quote: "the lordship had passed to his eldest son, Ser Kermit Tully". At the Muddy Mess he called on Borros to yield and, when the lord charged, killed him with his morningstar — quote: "straight into the spiked iron ball at the end of Lord Kermit's morningstar".

### Elmo Tully
Elmo, grandson of Grover Tully, became Lord of Riverrun and called the lords of the Trident to war — quote: "his grandson Elmo, now at last the Lord of Riverrun, had called the lords of the Trident to war". He died on the march after only nine-and-forty days as lord — quote: "Lord Elmo himself had expired on the march after drinking some bad water".

### Benjicot Blackwood
Benjicot Blackwood of Raventree joined the riverlords, a seasoned warrior at three-and-ten — quote: "Benjicot Blackwood of Raventree, already a seasoned warrior at three-and-ten". His flanking charge from the wood broke the battle and earned him the name Bloody Ben — quote: "led by that mad boy Benjicot Blackwood, who would this day earn the name Bloody Ben".

### Tyland Lannister
Ser Tyland Lannister, the blind master of coin, had placed three-quarters of the Crown's wealth beyond Rhaenyra's reach — quote: "had placed threequarters of the Crown's wealth safely in the hands of Casterly Rock, Oldtown, and the Iron Bank". He urged hiring sellswords and, more harshly, the immediate execution of Prince Aegon — quote: "argued for the immediate execution of Prince Aegon the Younger". He was dispatched across the narrow sea to seek sellswords and had not returned — quote: "Ser Tyland Lannister, Ser Marston Waters, and Ser Julian Wormwood had been dispatched across the narrow sea".

### Aegon III Targaryen
Prince Aegon the Younger, Rhaenyra's son, was held captive at Aegon II's court and named as a proposed heir — quote: "her only surviving son a captive at his own court". The king refused to let him wed Jaehaera or father children, decreeing his line must end — quote: "My sister's line must end". Freed in the coup, he was proclaimed king on his uncle's death and wed Jaehaera in 131 AC, beginning his melancholy reign — quote: "the melancholy reign of King Aegon III Targaryen had begun".

### Alfred Broome
Ser Alfred Broome had served Aegon II on Dragonstone and beheaded Trystane with Blackfyre — quote: "Ser Alfred Broome struck his head off with Blackfyre". Sent to mutilate Prince Aegon, he was barred by Perkin and shoved off the drawbridge to die on the spikes below — quote: "As Ser Alfred Broome crossed the drawbridge to Maegor's Holdfast".

### Sunfyre
Sunfyre, the golden dragon that was Aegon II's glory, had feasted on Rhaenyra — quote: "after Sunfyre had feasted on his sister". His neck wounds festered and stank, and he died in the outer yard of Dragonstone on the ninth day of the twelfth moon of 130 AC — quote: "the magnificent golden dragon that had been King Aegon’s glory died".

### Marilda of Hull
Marilda of Hull, called Mouse, was the shipwright's daughter and mother of Addam and Alyn — quote: "the younger son of Mouse, the shipwright’s daughter". She owned and captained the battered cog Mouse that bore the king home — quote: "owned and captained by Marilda of Hull".

### Essie
Essie, Gaemon's mother, styled herself Lady Esselyn during her son's reign — quote: "who had presumed to style herself Lady Esselyn during her son's reign". Under torture she recanted her claim that the king was Gaemon's father, and was hanged with Sylvenna Sand and twenty-seven others — quote: "Essie and the Dornish whore Sylvenna Sand were hanged from the battlements".

### Grover Tully
Lord Grover Tully, aged and bedridden, had finally died at Riverrun — quote: "the aged and bedridden Lord Grover Tully had finally died". Mushroom says it was apoplexy from his house fighting the king at Second Tumbleton — quote: "of apoplexy from having his house fight against the rightful king".

### Gyles Belgrave
Ser Gyles Belgrave of the Kingsguard commanded the king's escort and testified to his grey and ashen look — quote: "Ser Gyles Belgrave, the Kingsguard knight commanding his escort". He found the king dead in the litter with blood on his lips — quote: "found the king dead upon his cushions".

### Tom Tanglebeard
Tom Tanglebeard, father of Tom Tangletongue, was absent from the castle during the coup — quote: "His father Tom Tanglebeard was absent from the castle". Found in a tavern on Eel Alley protesting he was a simple fisherman, his captors drowned him in a cask of ale — quote: "his captors drowned him in a cask of same".

### Ummet
Ummet, the king's grossly fat food taster, was murdered in the coup according to Mushroom — quote: "the murder of the king's food taster, a grossly fat man named Ummet". Gyldayn names him a likelier poisoner of the king than the Kingsguard — quote: "seems a more likely candidate".

### Willis Fell
Ser Willis Fell of the Kingsguard delivered Princess Jaehaera safely to Storm's End — quote: "delivered safely to Storm's End by Ser Willis Fell of the Kingsguard".

### Cregan Stark
Cregan Stark, Lord of Winterfell, brought a great host down the Neck — quote: "Down the Neck came Cregan Stark, Lord of Winterfell, with a great host". Its size is disputed: Septon Eustace names twenty thousand while Munkun lowers it to eight thousand — quote: "Septon Eustace speaks of “twenty thousand howling savages in shaggy pelts,”".
