- **chapter_summary:** Eddard Stark is awakened at dawn by Lannister men drilling in the yard. He breaks his fast with his daughters, telling them to prepare to leave by midday. Grand Maester Pycelle arrives to announce King Robert's death, and Ned summons the small council to his solar, where Robert's sealed letter naming him Protector of the Realm is read aloud. Varys reveals that Renly has fled the city with Loras Tyrell and fifty retainers. The council proceeds to the throne room, where Joffrey sits the Iron Throne and demands oaths of fealty. Ned presents Robert's letter, which Cersei tears apart, and Ned publicly declares Joffrey has no claim and names Stannis as Robert's true heir. When Ned orders Janos Slynt to take the queen into custody, the gold cloaks betray him — killing Tomard, Varly, and Cayn — and Littlefinger holds Ned's own dagger to his throat.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned awakens at dawn** — Grey light and hoofbeats wake him; he sees Lannister guardsmen drilling in the yard below, including Sandor Clegane driving a lance through a straw dummy.
2. **Breakfast with daughters** — Ned breaks his fast with Sansa, Arya, and Septa Mordane. Sansa refuses to eat and begs to see Joffrey; Ned refuses. Arya asks for a final lesson with Syrio; Ned grants it with the condition she be ready by midday. Sansa runs out weeping.
3. **Pycelle announces Robert's death** — Grand Maester Pycelle arrives an hour later and reports the king is dead. Ned feels empty but resolves to act.
4. **Small council summoned to Ned's solar** — Ned insists on an immediate meeting. Pycelle accepts sweet beer and a chair. Ser Barristan arrives first and wants to attend Joffrey; Ned tells him his place is here.
5. **Littlefinger reports his task complete** — Arrives in last night's clothes with dusty boots, confirming "that little task" Ned set him is accomplished.
6. **Varys reports Renly's flight** — Renly left through a postern gate an hour before dawn with Ser Loras Tyrell and some fifty retainers, galloping south toward Storm's End or Highgarden.
7. **Robert's letter is read** — Ned produces the sealed letter. Ser Barristan confirms the seal is Robert's and unbroken, reads aloud: Ned is named Protector of the Realm to rule as regent until the heir comes of age. Ned internally reflects on not revealing the succession issue yet — he plans to wait until his daughters are safe and Stannis has arrived.
8. **Ned asks the council to confirm him as Lord Protector** — He watches the councillors' faces for reactions.
9. **Royal steward summons them to the throne room** — Fat Tom announces the steward, who says "the king" demands the small council's immediate presence in the throne room.
10. **Procession to the throne room** — Littlefinger gives Ned his arm; a double column of eight men-at-arms escorts them. Grey cloaks, no Lannister crimson visible; gold cloaks on the ramparts and gates reassure Ned.
11. **Janos Slynt meets them at the throne room door** — Armored in ornate black-and-gold plate; his men push open the great oaken doors.
12. **Joffrey proclaimed king** — The royal steward announces Joffrey's full style: "Joffrey of the Houses Baratheon and Lannister, the First of his Name, King of the Andals and the Rhoynar and the First Men, Lord of the Seven Kingdoms and Protector of the Realm."
13. **Ned limps to the Iron Throne** — Supported by Littlefinger, he traverses the long hall. Five Kingsguard in full armor arrayed around the base; Cersei and her two younger children behind Ser Boros and Ser Meryn; twenty Lannister guardsmen behind the throne; Sandor Clegane at the foot of the stairs. Gold cloaks line the walls with spears, outnumbering Lannisters five to one.
14. **Joffrey demands coronation and fealty** — Standing on the throne, Joffrey commands the council to arrange his coronation within the fortnight and says he will accept oaths of fealty today.
15. **Ned presents Robert's letter** — Asks Varys to show it to Cersei. She reads "Protector of the Realm," mocks it as "a piece of paper," and tears it into quarters.
16. **Cersei demands Ned bend the knee** — Offers to let him step down as Hand and return to Winterfell if he swears fealty to Joffrey.
17. **Ned declares Joffrey has no claim** — States publicly that Stannis is Robert's true heir. Joffrey screams "Liar!" Myrcella asks Cersei what Ned means.
18. **Cersei orders Ser Barristan to seize Ned** — Ser Barristan hesitates; Stark guardsmen surround him with bare steel.
19. **Armed standoff escalates** — The Hound draws his longsword; Kingsguard and Lannister guardsmen move to support him. Joffrey screams "Kill all of them!"
20. **Ned orders Janos Slynt to take the queen into custody** — Commands the City Watch to arrest Cersei and her children, do them no harm, escort them to the royal apartments.
21. **The gold cloaks betray Ned** — Slynt dons his helm, gold cloaks level their spears and close. A gold cloak drives a spear through Tomard's back, killing him. Slynt personally slashes Varly's throat. Cayn fights back but Sandor Clegane cuts off his sword hand and kills him.
22. **Littlefinger's betrayal** — Littlefinger slides Ned's dagger from its sheath and holds it under Ned's chin, saying "I did warn you not to trust me, you know."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Exhausted at dawn, then empty grief at Robert's death ("something died within him"); bitter about the necessity of deception; grimly determined; pain from his injured leg throughout; shock and desperation in the final moments of betrayal
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing the regency peacefully; getting his daughters safely out of King's Landing; keeping his plans about the succession secret until Stannis arrives
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses Sansa's request to see Joffrey; insists on immediate council meeting; keeps silent about the true succession (that "the heir" is Stannis, not Joffrey); asks the council to confirm him as Lord Protector; publicly declares Joffrey illegitimate when Cersei forces the issue; orders Janos Slynt to arrest Cersei
- **Self-deception flags:** Trusts that Littlefinger's "accomplished" task means the gold cloaks are on his side; reassured by the number of gold cloaks visible, not recognizing that their loyalty is compromised; believes he can "tread softly" and play the political game despite having no aptitude for it; thinks there will be "time enough" to deal with the succession later
