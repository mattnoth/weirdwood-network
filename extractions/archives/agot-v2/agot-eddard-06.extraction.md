# AGOT — Eddard VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 28
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard VI
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard VI
- **location_primary:** King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Red Keep (council chambers, Tower of the Hand), Street of Steel, Tobho Mott's shop and forge
- **approximate_timeline:** During preparations for the Hand's tourney; the city is swelling with visitors
- **time_markers:** Council session takes place during daytime; Tomard and Desmond left the castle "early this morning"; the ride to the Street of Steel and visit to the armorer occur same day; "last night" referenced for crimes in the city; "the night before" a woman's head was found in the Great Sept; "a few days past" Renly showed Ned the locket with Margaery's portrait
- **chapter_summary:** Ned presides over a small council session where Janos Slynt reports surging crime ahead of the tourney; Ned authorizes fifty new watchmen and twenty of his own guard. Afterward, Ned reviews Jon Arryn's investigation through Jory's interviews with four witnesses — a stableboy/watchman, Ser Hugh, a serving girl, and a former potboy — learning that Jon Arryn and Stannis Baratheon visited a brothel and an armorer together. Ned rides to Tobho Mott's shop at the top of the Street of Steel, where the master armorer introduces him to Gendry, a muscular apprentice boy with thick black hair and blue eyes. Ned recognizes something in the boy's features and departs, wondering what Jon Arryn wanted with a king's bastard and why it cost him his life.

## Events & Actions
1. **Council session on city crime** — Janos Slynt reports to the small council about surging crime from tourney visitors: drownings, riots, knife fights, rape, fires, robberies, a horse race down the Street of the Sisters, and a severed head found in the Great Sept's rainbow pool.
2. **Renly threatens Slynt** — Renly suggests replacing Slynt if he cannot keep the peace; Slynt bristles.
3. **Ned authorizes reinforcements** — Ned orders fifty new City Watchmen hired (Littlefinger to fund them) and commits twenty of his own household guard under Alyn's command.
4. **Council discusses the tourney** — Pycelle and Littlefinger defend the tourney's benefits; Renly jokes about Stannis wanting to outlaw brothels; Ned asks about Stannis's absence from the council.
5. **Ned returns to Tower of the Hand** — Ned orders Harwin to summon Jory and have Hullen (Harwin's father) saddle his horse.
6. **Ned reads the Malleon book** — In his chambers, Ned changes out of council silks and studies *The Lineages and Histories*, focusing on House Lannister, searching for whatever Jon Arryn sought.
7. **Jory reports on witness interviews** — Jory relays what the four witnesses said: Ser Hugh was brusque; a serving girl said Jon Arryn was troubled about his son's frailty and gruff with his wife; a potboy (now cordwainer) reported Jon Arryn quarreling with the king, commissioning silvered armor with Stannis, taking interest in hunting hound breeding, and planning to foster his son on Dragonstone; the stableboy/watchman said Jon Arryn and Stannis visited a brothel together.
8. **Ned and Jory discuss Stannis** — Ned wonders why Stannis left for Dragonstone and whether he played a part in Jon Arryn's death or was afraid; decides not to summon Stannis yet.
9. **Ned recalls Renly's locket** — Ned remembers Renly showing him a portrait of Margaery Tyrell, asking if she reminded him of anyone; Ned found the encounter queer.
10. **Ned dresses and departs** — Ned puts on the grey doublet with direwolf sigil, longsword, and cloak with Hand's badge; rides out through the King's Gate with Varly and Jacks.
11. **Ned rides through the crowded city** — He observes mummer on stilts, boys dueling with sticks, vendors at the market square; watches for anyone following, aware of Varys's spies; Tomard and Desmond posted along the route.
12. **Lord Beric Dondarrion arrives** — Beric rides through the Mud Gate with his escort carrying a black banner with purple lightning; declares he's come to win the Hand's tourney.
13. **Ned ascends the Street of Steel** — Ned rides up the winding hill past blacksmiths, freeriders, and ironmongers to Tobho Mott's large house at the top.
14. **Ned meets Tobho Mott** — The master armorer receives Ned warmly, serves wine, boasts of his craftsmanship; mentions clients including Loras Tyrell and Renly; claims he can work Valyrian steel, learned at Qohor.
15. **Ned asks about Jon Arryn's visit** — Mott confirms Jon Arryn and Stannis came but did not commission armor; they asked to see "the boy."
16. **Ned meets Gendry** — Mott takes Ned to the forge and introduces Gendry, a tall muscular boy with thick black hair and blue eyes. Gendry shows his bull helm but refuses to sell it.
17. **Gendry describes Jon Arryn's questions** — Jon Arryn asked about Gendry's wellbeing, his treatment, his work, and especially his mother — who she was and what she looked like. Gendry says his mother had yellow hair, worked in an alehouse, sang to him, and died when he was little. Stannis ("the bald one") just glared silently.
18. **Ned recognizes Gendry's parentage** — Ned studies Gendry's jaw and ice-blue eyes and thinks "Yes, I see it."
19. **Ned questions Mott about Gendry's apprentice fee** — Mott initially claims he took the boy free, then admits a lord paid in gold — twice the usual fee — for the boy and for Mott's silence. The lord was stout, round-shouldered, with a brown beard with some red, wearing a heavy purple velvet cloak with silver threads, hood shadowing his face.
20. **Ned departs** — Ned tells Mott to send Gendry to him if the boy ever wants to wield a sword; rides away wondering what Jon Arryn wanted with a king's bastard and why it was worth his life.
