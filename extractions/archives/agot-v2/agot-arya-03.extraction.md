# AGOT — Arya III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 33
- **pov_character:** Arya Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya III
- **first_available:** AGOT Arya III
- **location_primary:** The Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Dungeons beneath the Red Keep; underground tunnels; sewer outlet at the river; the Tower of the Hand (Eddard's solar)
- **approximate_timeline:** During the Hand's tourney period; after Catelyn has seized Tyrion Lannister; Arya has been training with Syrio Forel for some time
- **time_markers:** The chapter spans from daytime (cat-chasing) through "full dark" when Arya emerges from the sewer; moonlight visible when she washes in the river; the portcullis is down and gates barred (nighttime); Yoren arrives at an urgent late hour ("My pardons for the hour")
- **chapter_summary:** Arya catches the one-eared black tomcat after a long chase through the Red Keep, but is discovered by Myrcella and Tommen, who mistake her for a boy. She flees from their guards and gets lost in the castle's dungeons, where she discovers a room full of dragon skulls. She overhears two men discussing the imminent conflict between Stark and Lannister, the need for delay, a princess with child, and the possibility of killing a second Hand. She follows them through tunnels and emerges from a sewer outside the city walls. After returning to the castle and convincing the gold cloaks of her identity, she reports to her father, who dismisses her account as mummers. Yoren of the Night's Watch arrives with urgent private news.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya catches the one-eared black tomcat** — After a long chase through the Red Keep (twice around the Tower of the Hand, through multiple areas of the castle), Arya corners and catches the tomcat in a dead-end alley, hugging him to her chest and kissing him between the eyes.
2. **Myrcella and Tommen discover Arya** — The princess and prince, accompanied by a septa and two Lannister guards in crimson cloaks, find Arya holding the cat and mistake her for a ragged boy. The septa demands to know who she belongs to.
3. **Arya escapes from the guards** — Using her water-dancing training, Arya evades Godwyn's grasp, slides between the septa's legs, bowls over Tommen, spins past the second guard, and flees. She drops and rolls to evade a pursuing red cloak, then climbs through a high narrow window (scarcely more than an arrow slit) and loses pursuit.
4. **Arya becomes lost in the dungeons** — Hiding in a pitch-dark cellar, she hunkered against a damp stone wall and began counting to ten thousand.
5. **Arya discovers the dragon skull room** — As her eyes adjust, she sees massive skulls with huge empty eyes and jagged teeth. She overcomes her fear using Syrio's mantras, touches the bones (smooth, cold, hard) and teeth (black, sharp as daggers). She flees through the room when a skull's fang catches her jerkin, leaping over the biggest skull's teeth (tall as swords) and throwing herself against a heavy iron-ring door.
6. **Arya reaches the top of a great well shaft** — Following a pitch-black hallway by touch, she reaches a twenty-foot-wide shaft with stone steps spiraling downward. She sees a torch far below and hears two voices.
7. **Arya overhears a secret conversation** — Two men ascend the shaft discussing: "found one bastard," the wolf and lion being at each other's throats, the need to delay war ("Too soon, too soon"), a princess being with child, and the khal waiting for his son's birth.
8. **The conspirators discuss assassination** — The fat man asks "If one Hand can die, why not a second?" The stout man replies "Before is not now, and this Hand is not the other."
9. **The stout man details the political situation** — He names Stannis Baratheon and Lysa Arryn fleeing and gathering swords, Ser Loras urging his father to send his sister to court for Robert, Littlefinger's unknown game, Lord Stark having the bastard, the book, and approaching the truth, and Catelyn's abduction of Tyrion thanks to Littlefinger's meddling.
10. **The stout man seals the shaft entrance** — He pushes at something, causing a huge red stone slab to slide down from the ceiling with a resounding crash, sealing the well entrance completely.
11. **The stout man requests resources** — "I must have gold, and another fifty birds." Fragments follow about young ones who know their letters, treating them gently, keeping tongues, the risk.
12. **Arya follows the men through the tunnels** — She trails them at a distance for miles, guided by torchlight. The tunnel transitions from dressed stone to raw earth with timber supports.
13. **Arya emerges from a sewer** — She exits at the mouth of a sewer emptying into the river, knee-deep in foul-smelling water. She strips, dives into the river, swims until clean, and washes her clothes in the moonlight. Riders pass on the river road but take no notice.
14. **Arya is refused entry at the gatehouse** — The gold cloaks mistake her for a beggar boy, refuse entry, and one swings his fist at her. She dodges ("She saw the blow coming even before it began") and identifies herself as Arya Stark, threatening that her father will have their heads on spikes, and demands they fetch Jory Cassel or Vayon Poole.
15. **Arya reports to her father** — Harwin and Fat Tom bring her to Eddard's solar. She tells him about the two men, the threats against him, the monsters, and the wizard. Ned closes the great thick book to listen. He dismisses them as mummers performing for the tourney.
16. **Yoren arrives with urgent news** — A Night's Watch brother arrives, mistakes Arya for Eddard's son ("He has your look"), and reports that he rode hard from an inn where sellswords and freeriders witnessed something. Some rode for King's Landing, some for Casterly Rock ("the Rock lies closer. Lord Tywin will have gotten the word by now"). He came for Benjen Stark's sake and asks to speak privately.
17. **Arya is sent to her chambers** — Eddard kisses her brow, says they'll finish their talk on the morrow. Desmond escorts Arya to her bedchamber. She asks about her father's guards (fifty) and whether they could protect him from Lannisters and wizards. Desmond draws his longsword and reassures her.
