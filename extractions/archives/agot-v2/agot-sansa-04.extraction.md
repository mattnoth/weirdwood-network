# AGOT — Sansa IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT

- **time_markers:** "the third day" (when Sansa is summoned); "the first day" (fighting began, Jeyne Poole brought to her that night); "the second day" (silence, meals brought, sunset bells ring); "late that night" (Jeyne thrust inside); "at sunset on the second day" (bells begin); "that night" (Sansa dreams of Joffrey); "the next morning, the morning of the third day" (Ser Boros comes); "later that night" (Sansa drifting off to sleep)
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa Stark has been confined in Maegor's Holdfast for three days following violent fighting at the Red Keep. On the third day, Ser Boros Blount escorts her to the council chambers where Queen Cersei, Lord Varys, Grand Maester Pycelle, and Lord Petyr Baelish inform her that her father Eddard Stark has been arrested as a traitor. They pressure Sansa into writing letters to her mother Catelyn, brother Robb, aunt Lysa Arryn, and grandfather Hoster Tully urging them to swear fealty to King Joffrey. Sansa agrees, hoping to preserve her betrothal to Joffrey and eventually plead for her father's pardon. Jeyne Poole is removed from Sansa's room and sent to Lord Petyr's apartments.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa dresses herself on the third day of confinement** — She chooses a dark grey wool dress with silver fastenings; struggles without servants; Jeyne Poole is useless from crying.
2. **Sansa recalls the first day's violence** — Fighting broke out with real steel; sounds of combat, pain, and death filled Maegor's Holdfast; Sansa wept and called for her father, Septa Mordane, the king, and Joffrey; guards did not respond.
3. **Jeyne Poole thrust into Sansa's room late on the first night** — Bruised and shaking, Jeyne reports the Hound broke down her door with a warhammer and there were bodies on the stair of the Tower of the Hand, steps slick with blood.
4. **Second day: silence and confinement continue** — Iron portcullis down, drawbridge up, Lannister guardsmen on walls; three meals brought; servants refuse to answer questions; women bring clothes from Tower of the Hand but flee from Sansa; guards refuse to let them leave.
5. **Sansa repeatedly asks to see the queen or Joffrey** — She tells everyone she sees; no response.
6. **Bells ring at sunset on the second day** — A great bell with deep sonorous voice; answered by bells from the Great Sept of Baelor on Visenya's Hill; Sansa knows the king is dead.
7. **Sansa dreams of Joffrey on the throne** — She sits beside him in a gown of woven gold with a crown, and everyone bends the knee.
8. **Ser Boros Blount escorts Sansa to the queen** — Morning of the third day; they cross the drawbridge (now down); Sansa sees a body impaled on iron spikes in the dry moat and workmen lowering another man on ropes.
9. **Sansa meets Queen Cersei and small council** — Cersei is in the council chambers with Littlefinger, Pycelle, and Varys, all in mourning black; Ser Mandon Moore ushers them in.
10. **Cersei orders Jeyne Poole removed** — Ser Boros is sent to take Jeyne to Littlefinger's apartments; told to say Littlefinger will take her to see her father.
11. **Sansa told her father is a traitor** — Varys announces it directly; Pycelle elaborates that Eddard tried to steal Joffrey's throne; Cersei shows a bloodstained letter from Eddard to Stannis inviting him to take the crown.
12. **Sansa's betrothal to Joffrey threatened** — Council members argue Sansa carries traitor's blood; Cersei says she cannot allow the marriage unless the Stark family demonstrates loyalty.
13. **Sansa reveals she went to Cersei before the fighting** — She confesses she told Cersei of Eddard's plan to send her away from King's Landing; she did so out of love for Joffrey and desire to remain.
14. **Littlefinger compares Sansa to young Catelyn** — He says Sansa is "the very image of Cat at the same age" in hair and eyes.
15. **Sansa agrees to write letters** — After pressure and the promise of eventual marriage to Joffrey, Sansa agrees to write to her family urging fealty to Joffrey.
16. **Sansa writes four letters** — To Catelyn Stark, Robb Stark, Lysa Arryn, and Hoster Tully; Varys stamps each with Eddard's direwolf seal in pale white beeswax.
17. **Ser Mandon Moore returns Sansa to Maegor's Holdfast** — Jeyne and all her things are gone; Sansa builds a fire, reads stories of Florian and Jonquil, Lady Shella and the Rainbow Knight, Prince Aemon.
18. **Sansa realizes she forgot to ask about Arya** — As she drifts off to sleep that night.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terror and confusion giving way to desperate hopefulness and self-deception; Sansa oscillates between fear for her father, desire to marry Joffrey, and the need to be a "good girl" who is polite and ladylike; she resolves to be a lady "no matter what"
- **Primary preoccupation:** Preserving her betrothal to Joffrey; she frames everything through the lens of romantic fantasy — believing Joffrey will be merciful, imagining herself as queen, reading stories of knights and fair maidens
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees to write four letters urging her family to swear fealty to Joffrey; this is made under duress (threat of losing Joffrey) but Sansa partly convinces herself it is the right thing
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes Joffrey is "good and kind" and will show mercy to her father; believes the council's performance is genuine concern rather than manipulation; believes she can eventually become queen and pardon her father; rationalizes her betrayal of Eddard's plans as an act of love; tells herself Jeyne is "such a child" while being equally powerless; dismisses the gravity of the fighting as resolvable through courtly manners
