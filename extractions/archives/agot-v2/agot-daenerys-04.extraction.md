# AGOT — Daenerys IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 37
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys IV
- **first_available:** AGOT Daenerys IV
- **location_primary:** Vaes Dothrak
- **location_secondary:** Mother of Mountains, Western Market, Eastern Market, the godsway
- **approximate_timeline:** After extended travel across the Dothraki sea; Daenerys is visibly pregnant; the khalasar arrives at Vaes Dothrak
- **time_markers:** Arrival during daylight (long shadows from bronze stallions); Drogo must ascend Mother of Mountains "this night"; bloodriders will "return at dawn"; Dany takes a bath and prepares supper that evening; she falls asleep holding the dragon egg; twenty years since Cohollo saved young Drogo from sellswords
- **chapter_summary:** Khal Drogo's khalasar arrives at Vaes Dothrak, passing through the Horse Gate and down the godsway lined with plundered statues. Dany and Ser Jorah discuss whether the Dothraki could conquer the Seven Kingdoms, with Jorah providing detailed military analysis and naming key Westerosi leaders. Drogo departs to sacrifice on the Mother of Mountains. Dany prepares Dothraki-style clothing as gifts for Viserys and invites him to supper, but he reacts with fury, striking Doreah and grabbing Dany. She strikes him across the face with a bronze medallion belt, drawing blood, and orders him out. Alone afterward, she curls up with a dragon egg and feels her unborn child move as if reaching toward it.

## Events & Actions
1. **Khalasar enters Vaes Dothrak** — Khal Drogo leads the khalasar through the Horse Gate, two gigantic bronze stallions forming an arch a hundred feet above the road, and down the godsway with his bloodriders beside him.
2. **Procession along the godsway** — Dany rides her silver behind Drogo, escorted by Ser Jorah and Viserys, with Aggo and Rakharo behind them. They pass plundered statues and monuments from sacked cities lining both sides of the road.
3. **Viserys insults the Dothraki** — Speaking in the Common Tongue so the Dothraki cannot understand, Viserys calls them savages and their city "the trash of dead cities." Dany tells him they are her people now.
4. **Viserys mocks the Dothraki riders** — He favors Aggo and Rakharo with a mocking smile and says the "savages lack the wit to understand the speech of civilized men."
5. **Viserys demands his army** — He asks how long before Drogo gives him his army. Ser Jorah tells him the Western Market will have food to his taste and Drogo will honor his promise in his own time.
6. **Jorah explains the gift/sale distinction** — He tells Dany that Viserys thinks he sold her and wants his price, but Drogo views her as a gift and will give a return gift in his own time. "You do not demand a gift, not of a khal."
7. **Dany asks about Dothraki military capability** — She asks what if someone other than Viserys, "someone stronger," led the Dothraki. Jorah says Dothraki are superior mounted archers, Drogo alone commands forty thousand riders, and they could defeat Robert in open field but cannot take castles.
8. **Jorah discusses Westerosi leadership** — He compares Rhaegar's army at the Trident (forty thousand, only a tenth knights) to Drogo's forces. He says Robert is brave but rash, while Stannis, Tywin Lannister, and Eddard Stark would counsel against open battle.
9. **Jorah reveals personal bitterness** — He says Eddard Stark took "all I loved, for the sake of a few lice-ridden poachers and his precious honor," then abruptly changes the subject.
10. **Arrival in the city** — They ride through the Western Market bazaar (full of running children and shouting men) and down the broad ways beyond. Dany observes the strange, diverse architecture of the sprawling, mostly empty city.
11. **Halt near the Eastern Market** — Drogo calls a halt near the Eastern Market beneath the Mother of Mountains. Dany sees the "palace" — actually a cavernous wooden feasting hall with a sewn-silk roof, surrounded by horse yards and earthen houses.
12. **Weapons surrendered** — All riders including Drogo unbelt their arakhs and hand them to waiting slaves, as required by sacred law.
13. **Dany reflects on bloodrider customs** — She recalls Jhiqui's teachings: bloodriders are the khal's brothers who share his life and death; in some khalasars they share wine, tent, and wives, but never horses. She is glad Drogo does not share his wives.
14. **Dany reflects on the individual bloodriders** — Old Cohollo is kind; Haggo is huge, silent, and glowering; Qotho has cruel eyes and quick hands, bruises Doreah and makes Irri sob at night.
15. **Dany wishes for bloodriders for her son** — She reflects that King Aerys's Kingsguard failed him (the Kingslayer murdered him, Barristan went to the Usurper) and decides her son will have bloodriders to protect against treachery.
16. **Cohollo delivers message** — Drogo's oldest bloodrider tells Dany in Dothraki that Drogo must ascend the Mother of Mountains to sacrifice for his safe return. Only men are allowed on the Mother.
17. **Dany takes a bath** — Doreah leads her to the earthen dwelling. She orders a scalding hot bath and Jhiqui washes her hair.
18. **Supper and gifts prepared** — Dany sends Irri to buy goat and fruit (anything but horseflesh) from the bazaar. She lays out Dothraki-style clothing she had made for Viserys: white linen tunic and leggings, leather sandals, a painted leather vest, bronze medallion belt, and a sandsilk cloak green as grass with pale grey border.
19. **Doreah sent to invite Viserys** — Dany sends Doreah to invite Viserys to supper. Viserys is nicer to the Lysene girl than to the Dothraki handmaids, perhaps because Illyrio let him bed her in Pentos.
20. **Viserys arrives furious** — He drags Doreah by the arm; her eye is red where he hit her. He is enraged at being "commanded" by "this whore" and says he should have sent Dany back her head.
21. **Dany tries to calm him** — She apologizes, says Doreah misspoke, and shows Viserys the gifts. He sneers at them as "Dothraki rags" and says the cloak stinks of manure.
22. **Viserys refuses the clothing** — When Dany suggests he would be more comfortable and the Dothraki would respect him more, he asks if she'll want to braid his hair next. She says he has no right to a braid, having won no victories.
23. **Confrontation escalates** — Fury shows in Viserys's lilac eyes but he does not strike her with handmaids watching and khas warriors outside. He picks up the cloak, sniffs it, threatens to use it as a horse blanket.
24. **Viserys grabs Dany** — He grabs her arm painfully and threatens her: "Do you think that big belly will protect you if you wake the dragon?" He calls her "slut."
25. **Dany strikes Viserys** — She grabs the bronze medallion belt and swings it into his face, cutting his cheek. She tells him he is the one who forgets himself, orders him to leave before she summons her khas, and warns that Drogo will cut him open and feed him his own entrails.
26. **Viserys retreats** — He scrambles to his feet, threatens "When I come into my kingdom, you will rue this day, slut," and walks off holding his torn face. He leaves all the gifts behind. His blood spatters the sandsilk cloak.
27. **Dany declines supper** — She tells her handmaids to share the food among themselves and send some to Ser Jorah.
28. **Dany requests a dragon egg** — Irri fetches the egg with the deep green shell and bronze flecks. Dany curls up on her side, pulling the blood-spattered sandsilk cloak across her, cradling the egg between her belly and breasts.
29. **The child moves** — Dany feels her unborn child move as if reaching toward the egg, "brother to brother, blood to blood." She whispers "You are the dragon, the true dragon. I know it." She smiles and falls asleep dreaming of home.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Shifting throughout — wonder and awe at Vaes Dothrak's strangeness; anxiety about Viserys's behavior and Drogo's patience; relief when Viserys rides away to inspect a statue; tenderness and hope when preparing gifts; shock then decisive anger when Viserys attacks; deep sadness after the confrontation ("I'm not hungry," she said sadly); comfort, strength, and peace when holding the dragon egg; ends with a smile and dreaming of home
- **Primary preoccupation:** Managing Viserys — trying to improve his standing with the Dothraki, reconcile after the grass incident, placate his impatience; increasingly contemplating who might actually lead a Dothraki invasion ("someone stronger"); growing identification with Dothraki as "my people"
- **Key decisions made:** Strikes Viserys with the bronze medallion belt rather than submitting to his violence; orders him to leave and threatens Drogo's wrath; declines supper and asks for the dragon egg for comfort
- **Self-deception flags:** Still tells herself Viserys is "still her king" and her brother, and defends his right to impatience about the army to Jorah, while simultaneously recognizing his cruelty and incompetence; whispers to her unborn child that he is "the true dragon," implicitly transferring the Targaryen claim away from Viserys even while maintaining outward loyalty
