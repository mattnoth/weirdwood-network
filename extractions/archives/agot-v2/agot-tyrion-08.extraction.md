# AGOT — Tyrion VIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 63
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion VIII
- **first_available:** AGOT Tyrion VIII
- **location_primary:** Lannister camp along the kingsroad (between the river and the kingsroad)
- **location_secondary:** Battlefield (the Green Fork)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly before and during the Battle of the Green Fork; Robb Stark's host has moved south from the Twins
- **time_markers:** Twilight/dusk at chapter opening (evening meal); predawn darkness when trumpets sound battle assembly; dawn/sunrise as the army forms up; rising sun during the battle
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion dines with his father Lord Tywin and the Lannister lords, where he learns he and his clansmen will serve in the vanguard under Ser Gregor Clegane. Humiliated, Tyrion leaves dinner and meets Shae, a camp follower Bronn has procured for him. During the night, trumpets sound battle assembly as the Stark host has arrived sooner than expected. Tyrion leads the mountain clansmen on the left flank of the vanguard in the ensuing battle, fights fiercely, and survives with a wounded elbow. After the Lannister victory, Tyrion learns from Ser Addam Marbrand that Robb Stark was not with this army — he crossed at the Twins with most of his cavalry, riding for Riverrun.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion arrives late to Lord Tywin's evening meal** — He is saddlesore and takes his place on the bench beside Ser Kevan. Lord Tywin mocks his lateness.
2. **Tywin reports Stark movements** — Ser Addam's outriders report the Stark host has moved south from the Twins with Lord Frey's levies, likely no more than a day's march north.
3. **Lord Lefford complains about arming the clansmen** — He questions whether the mountain clans will fight, expressing displeasure at the steel given them. Tyrion defends his men.
4. **Ser Kevan assigns Tyrion to the vanguard** — Ser Kevan (speaking for Lord Tywin) tells Tyrion his clansmen will serve in the vanguard. Tyrion suspects this is meant to get him killed.
5. **Lord Tywin places Tyrion under Ser Gregor** — When Tyrion angrily accepts the van assignment, Tywin clarifies Tyrion will serve under Ser Gregor, not command. Tyrion storms away from dinner.
6. **Tyrion wanders the camp alone** — He walks through the vast Lannister camp, surrounded by twenty thousand men but feeling isolated. No one acknowledges him.
7. **Tyrion encounters the Stone Crows** — Conn invites him to share their ox. Tyrion declines, noting the ox is far from cooked.
8. **Tyrion arrives at his tent and meets Shae** — Bronn has procured a camp follower as Tyrion requested. Tyrion negotiates terms with Shae: she will share his tent and take no other men.
9. **Tyrion and Shae sleep together** — Their first sexual encounter. Tyrion reflects it has been nearly a year since he last lay with a woman. He whistles a song he learned as a boy.
10. **Tyrion speaks with Bronn outside the tent** — Bronn reveals he took Shae from a knight at dirkpoint. Tyrion asks Bronn to fight beside him and promises a reward for keeping him alive.
11. **Battle assembly trumpets sound in predawn** — Robb Stark's host has arrived sooner than expected, less than a mile north, forming in battle array. The camp scrambles to prepare.
12. **Tyrion arms for battle** — Pod lays out his mismatched armor; Shae and Pod help him dress. He is given a massive ironwood shield and battle-axe.
13. **The Lannister army deploys** — Ser Kevan commands the center with archers, pikemen, and men-at-arms plus 300 heavy horse. Ser Addam Marbrand commands the right wing (4,000 cavalry). Lord Tywin takes the reserve (5,000) on the high ground. The vanguard/left under Ser Gregor is composed of mounted archers, freeriders, sellswords, poorly armed levies, and Tyrion's clansmen.
14. **Tyrion recognizes the vanguard is expendable** — He and Bronn observe the vanguard is "crow food" — no pikes, few bowmen, barely any knights, poorly armed, commanded by Gregor Clegane.
15. **Ser Gregor orders Tyrion to hold the river on the far left** — The leftmost position of the left flank.
16. **The battle begins** — Lannister archers loose volleys; the vanguard charges. Ser Gregor leads the wedge into a Karstark spear formation. His horse dies but the Mountain fights on untouched.
17. **The clansmen break through the enemy lines** — Shagga bursts through the gap in the shield wall. Tyrion follows with Burned Men and Moon Brothers. Arrows fall on both sides.
18. **Tyrion fights in personal combat** — He knocks aside a spear thrust, rides down a man, chases a spear-thrower, and kills a tall northerner by burying his axe in the man's head after his horse bites the man's cheek.
19. **Tyrion is unhorsed by a knight with a morningstar** — A knight shouting "For Eddard and Winterfell!" strikes Tyrion's elbow, destroying his axe, then knocks him from his horse. The knight demands his yield.
20. **Tyrion headbutts the knight's horse** — He drives his head into the horse's belly; the horse falls on its rider. The knight, trapped and with a broken arm, yields to Tyrion instead.
21. **The battle turns in Lannister favor** — Ser Kevan's pikemen push the northerners back. Lord Tywin commits the reserve, shattering the Stark lines.
22. **Tyrion surveys his losses** — Ulf son of Umar is dead (arm gone at elbow). Conn son of Coratt is dead (spear thrust to chest). Shagga is wounded but alive (riddled with arrows). Chella has four ears. Timett loots bodies. Roughly half the three hundred clansmen survive.
23. **Tyrion confronts his father** — He accuses Tywin of deliberately placing him where he would be killed. Tywin admits he placed the least disciplined men on the left hoping a collapse would lure Robb Stark into a trap.
24. **Ser Addam Marbrand reports** — Several northern commanders captured (Lord Cerwyn, Ser Wylis Manderly, Harrion Karstark, four Freys). Lord Hornwood is dead. Roose Bolton escaped. Robb Stark was not with this army — he crossed at the Twins with most of his horse, riding hard for Riverrun.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Sour and humiliated at dinner; bitter resentment toward his father; lonely and isolated wandering the camp; briefly happy and tender with Shae; terrified before battle (queasy bowels, stomach in knots, brow cold with sweat); grimly determined during combat; pain-dazed after being unhorsed; bitterly vindicated when confronting his father after the battle
- **Primary preoccupation:** His father's contempt and the likelihood that Tywin has placed him in the vanguard to be killed; survival in the coming battle
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts the vanguard assignment rather than suffer the humiliation of guarding the baggage train; takes Shae as his companion with terms of exclusivity; leads his clansmen to hold the river on the far left
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion suspects Shae's delight in bed is feigned but tells himself "she did it so well that it did not matter" — "That much truth he did not crave." He recognizes his own willful self-deception about the relationship.
