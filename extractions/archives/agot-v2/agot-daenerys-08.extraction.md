# AGOT — Daenerys VIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 65
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys VIII
- **first_available:** AGOT Daenerys VIII
- **location_primary:** Dothraki Sea (unnamed halt along the march)
- **location_secondary:** Khal Drogo's tent
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after the sack of the Lamb Men's town; Drogo's wound has been festering for at least six days since he tore off Mirri Maz Duur's poultice
- **time_markers:** "since they had mounted up at dawn"; "not even that much since midday"; "heat of the afternoon sun"; "the sun was low on the horizon" at chapter's end; "He will be gone by morning" (Mirri's prognosis); six days since Drogo tore off the poultice; three days of heavy poppy wine drinking; fires burning after sunset; "the sun had set" at chapter's close
- **chapter_summary:** Khal Drogo, weakened by his festering wound, falls from his horse during the march. Daenerys orders the khalasar to halt and camp, commanding the bloodriders over their objections. Ser Jorah examines the wound and warns Dany that Drogo is dying and they must flee, explaining that rival kos will fight for power and kill her son. Mirri Maz Duur offers bloodmagic — sacrificing Drogo's red stallion — to save him, warning that only death can pay for life. When the ritual begins, Qotho attempts to stop it and is killed by Ser Jorah in combat; Haggo is killed by Rakharo and Jhogo; Cohollo is killed by Aggo. Dany goes into premature labor during the fighting and, with the birthing women fled, Ser Jorah carries her into the tent where Mirri Maz Duur is performing the ritual.

## Events & Actions
1. **Drogo deteriorating on the march** — Bloodflies circle the khal as he rides in silence, unresponsive to Dany. His wound is covered with a fig-leaf-and-mud plaster made by herbwomen after he tore off Mirri Maz Duur's poultice six days ago. He has been drinking poppy wine heavily for three days, scarcely touching food, thrashing and groaning at night.
2. **Drogo falls from his horse** — He sways in the saddle and falls heavily when Dany touches his arm. He does not recognize her. He gasps "My horse." The bloodriders witness it.
3. **Dany orders the halt** — She commands the bloodriders to make camp, telling Haggo to claim Drogo ordered it and her pregnancy prevented continuing. Qotho resists but Dany sends him to fetch Mirri Maz Duur, threatening him with Drogo's wrath.
4. **Tent erected** — Slaves set up Drogo's tent beneath a jagged outcrop of black rock for shade. Irri and Doreah help walk Drogo inside. Eroeh sets up a brazier. Doreah removes his medallion belt and clothes; Jhiqui unlaces his riding sandals. Dany posts her khas as guards and forbids anyone to enter.
5. **Eroeh declares Drogo dying** — She whispers "He dies." Dany slaps her and insists the khal cannot die.
6. **Jhiqui speaks the unspeakable** — She says "he fell from his horse," forcing Dany to confront the implications: a khal who cannot ride cannot rule.
7. **Dany orders bathing** — She orders the copper tub brought and water fetched to cool Drogo's fever. She wets silk to lay across his brow.
8. **Dany unbinds Drogo's braid** — She removes his bells one by one, as she had on their wedding night, telling herself he will want them again when he is well.
9. **Ser Jorah arrives and examines Drogo** — Announced by Aggo as "the Andal." He sends the handmaids away, then scrapes off the mud plaster with his dagger, revealing the wound black and glistening with corruption, seeping blood and pus, with a foul sweet smell.
10. **Ser Jorah declares Drogo dying** — "Your khal is good as dead, Princess." He urges flight south to Asshai and then by ship to Pentos.
11. **Ser Jorah warns about succession** — He explains that Jhaqo and Pono and other kos will fight for power, the winner will kill Drogo's son as a potential rival, and the bloodriders will take Dany to Vaes Dothrak to join the dosh khaleen before dying themselves.
12. **Dany remembers Rhaegar's children** — She recalls the story Viserys told of the Usurper's dogs ripping Rhaegar's son from his mother's breast and dashing his head against a wall.
13. **Dany refuses to leave Drogo** — She insists she will not abandon him. "I will not leave him."
14. **Mirri Maz Duur arrives** — She enters limping and haggard, bowing low. Qotho and Haggo carry her chest. When the bloodriders see the wound, Haggo drops the chest and Qotho swears.
15. **Mirri examines the wound** — She declares it has festered and is beyond a healer's skills.
16. **Qotho and Haggo assault Mirri** — Haggo strikes her across the face and kicks her. Qotho threatens to stake her out for rape, dogs, and carrion. Dany orders them to stop.
17. **Qotho threatens Dany** — He suggests she should be staked out beside Mirri. Ser Jorah steps between them, loosening his longsword. Qotho warns Dany is nothing when Drogo dies.
18. **Dany invokes her Targaryen identity** — "Before I was khaleesi, I was the blood of the dragon." She summons her khas. The bloodriders withdraw.
19. **Ser Jorah warns about Qotho** — "That one means you no good, Princess. A dead man is beyond fear." He goes to don armor at Dany's request.
20. **Mirri offers bloodmagic** — She tells Dany there is a spell learned in Asshai from a bloodmage of the Shadow Lands. She warns it is hard and dark, and that "only death may pay for life."
21. **Mirri clarifies her own nature** — When Dany calls her a maegi, Mirri responds: "Only a maegi can save your rider now, Silver Lady."
22. **Dany agrees to the price** — Mirri assures her it will not be Dany's death. Dany thinks of Rhaegar dying for the woman he loved.
23. **Drogo lowered into the bath** — Rakharo and Quaro lower him into the copper tub. He mutters "Must ride."
24. **The red stallion is brought** — Jhogo leads it into the tent. The horse screams and rears at the scent of death; three men subdue it.
25. **Jhogo pleads against bloodmagic** — He kneels before Dany calling it forbidden. Dany overrules him, comparing it to eating the stallion heart in Vaes Dothrak.
26. **Mirri sacrifices the stallion** — She chants in an unknown tongue, produces the bronze knife, and slashes the stallion's throat. Horse blood fills the bath. She chants: "Strength of the mount, go into the rider. Strength of the beast, go into the man."
27. **Dany orders the stallion carcass burned** — Following Dothraki funeral custom for a man's mount.
28. **Mirri begins the full ritual** — She lights braziers and throws red powder on the coals. She orders everyone out: "My song will wake powers old and dark. The dead will dance here this night. No living man must look on them."
29. **Dany exits the tent** — She kisses Drogo's brow and asks Mirri to bring him back. She leaves bloody footprints.
30. **Ser Jorah confronts Dany outside** — Now wearing mail, leather, chainmail, gauntlets, greaves, and gorget. He sees the bloody footprints: "What have you done, you little fool?" He says he would have seen her safe to Asshai.
31. **Mirri's wailing and dancing shadows** — Her voice rises to a high ululating wail. Shadows move inside the glowing tent. Dothraki begin to back away in fear.
32. **The bloodriders return** — Qotho, Haggo, and Cohollo arrive with the hairless men (eunuch healers). Cohollo spits in Dany's face.
33. **Qotho attacks toward the tent** — He draws his arakh to stop the ritual. Dany tries to stop him; he shoves her to her knees. She orders her khas to kill him.
34. **Qotho kills Quaro** — His arakh catches Quaro low under the arm, through leather, skin, muscle, and rib bone. Quaro dies pleading for water.
35. **Ser Jorah fights and kills Qotho** — Extended combat: Qotho's arakh speed vs. Jorah's armor and longsword. Qotho wounds Jorah's face (half his ear gone), hip (gash through mail). Qotho taunts him as "a craven, a milk man, a eunuch in an iron suit." When Qotho's arakh catches bone, Jorah severs his forearm and delivers a killing blow that nearly explodes his face.
36. **Rakharo and Jhogo kill Haggo** — Jhogo's whip coils around Haggo's throat and yanks him down; Rakharo drives his arakh through the top of Haggo's head between his eyes.
37. **Dany goes into labor** — Sharp pain in her belly and wetness on her thighs during the fighting.
38. **Crowd throws stones at Dany** — Her shoulder is torn and bloody from a thrown stone.
39. **Cohollo seizes Dany** — He grabs her hair, pulls her head back, and puts a knife to her throat. She screams "My baby."
40. **Aggo kills Cohollo** — His arrow takes Cohollo under the arm, piercing lungs and heart.
41. **The khalasar begins to disperse** — Dothraki steal back to tents or saddle horses and ride off. The crowd disperses.
42. **Dany collapses in agony** — Labor pains seize her like a giant's fist. She cannot rise.
43. **Dany glimpses shadow shapes** — Through the sandsilk she sees shadows dancing, some not human — a great wolf and a man wreathed in flames.
44. **Ser Jorah carries Dany** — He lifts her, face sticky with blood, half his ear gone. He calls for handmaids and birthing women.
45. **Birthing women refuse to come** — Doreah reports they say Dany is accursed and have fled.
46. **Dany taken into the tent** — Someone (possibly Aggo) suggests taking her to the maegi. Irri and Doreah recall Mirri claimed knowledge of birthing. Ser Jorah carries Dany inside the tent where Mirri's ritual is underway, against Mirri's prohibition.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Escalating dread and fear throughout. Begins with anxiety watching the bloodflies and Drogo's silence. Transitions to desperate grief and denial when Drogo falls. Fierce determination when commanding the bloodriders. Hopelessness when Ser Jorah declares Drogo dying. Desperation when agreeing to bloodmagic. Terror during the combat. Physical agony when labor begins.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Saving Khal Drogo's life at any cost; secondarily, protecting her unborn son Rhaego
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Orders the khalasar to halt rather than let Drogo's fall be seen further; (2) Sends for Mirri Maz Duur despite bloodrider resistance; (3) Refuses Ser Jorah's plan to flee to Asshai; (4) Authorizes Mirri's bloodmagic, agreeing that "only death may pay for life"; (5) Commands her khas to kill Qotho when he moves to stop the ritual
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself Drogo "cannot die" despite all evidence; sets aside his bells telling herself "He would want them again when he was well"; rationalizes bloodmagic as "the same" as eating the stallion heart — a false equivalence; is "more frightened than she dared admit, even to herself"; screams "the price is too high" only after it is too late to turn back
