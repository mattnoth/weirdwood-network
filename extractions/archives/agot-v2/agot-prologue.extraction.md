- **chapter_summary:** Three Night's Watch rangers — Will, Ser Waymar Royce, and Gared — track a band of wildling raiders through the haunted forest beyond the Wall. Will had scouted ahead and found eight wildlings dead at their camp. When the three return to the campsite, the bodies have vanished. The Others appear from the woods — pale, silent figures in shifting armor — and one engages Ser Waymar in single combat. The Other's crystal blade shatters Royce's steel longsword, and the remaining Others butcher him. Will climbs down from his hiding place in a tree to find Royce's body, but the dead knight rises with one burning blue eye and strangles Will.

## Events & Actions
1. **Gared urges turning back** — As darkness falls, Gared argues the wildlings are dead and there is no reason to continue. He cites the eight-to-nine day ride back and worsening weather.
2. **Royce overrules the veterans** — Ser Waymar dismisses Gared's concerns and Will's report, questioning whether the wildlings are truly dead. He asks Will to recount what he saw.
3. **Will describes the wildling camp** — Will reports finding eight wildlings (men and women, no children) dead at a camp beside a stream, with a lean-to against a rock, weapons nearby, no fire, no movement.
4. **Royce deduces the cold didn't kill them** — Using Will's observation that the Wall was weeping (too warm for death by freezing), Royce concludes something else killed the wildlings and orders Will to lead them to the camp.
5. **Gared shows his frostbite scars** — Gared pulls back his hood to show the stumps where his ears were, revealing he lost two ears, three toes, and a finger to cold. Mentions his brother found frozen with a smile.
6. **The party advances toward the camp** — Will leads on foot, Royce follows on his destrier, Gared brings up the rear. They ride through deepening twilight into full night.
7. **The party dismounts at the ridge** — Will stops under an ironwood tree and says they should proceed on foot. Royce dismounts and draws his longsword. Gared asks for a fire; Royce forbids it. Gared is ordered to guard the horses.
8. **Will and Royce reach the clearing** — Will crawls under the sentinel tree at the ridge top and sees the clearing is empty. All the wildling bodies are gone. The double-bladed axe remains.
9. **Royce orders Will up a tree** — Refusing to return to Castle Black a failure, Royce commands Will to climb the sentinel tree and look for a fire.
10. **Will climbs the sentinel tree** — Will climbs, hands sticky with sap, praying to the nameless gods of the wood. He puts his dirk between his teeth.
11. **The Others appear** — Pale shapes glide through the wood. A shadow emerges — tall, gaunt, flesh pale as milk, in color-shifting armor.
12. **The Other duels Ser Waymar** — One Other engages Royce in single combat with a crystal blade. Five or more Others watch silently in a circle. Their blades produce a high thin keening when they meet. Royce's blade frosts over; the Other's dances with pale blue light.
13. **Royce is wounded** — The pale sword bites through ringmail beneath his arm. Blood wells and steams.
14. **The Other mocks Royce** — It speaks in an unknown language; its voice is like cracking ice on a winter lake.
15. **Royce's sword shatters** — Royce shouts "For Robert!" and swings with full force. When blades touch, the steel shatters into a hundred brittle pieces. Shards fly; blood wells from Royce's eyes.
16. **The Others butcher Royce** — The watchers move forward together and cut Royce apart with their pale blades, slicing through ringmail as if silk. Their laughter is sharp as icicles.
17. **Will waits in the tree** — He stays hidden while the moon crosses the sky, muscles cramping, fingers numb.
18. **Will descends and finds Royce's body** — Royce lies facedown, one arm outflung, cloak slashed, looking very young in death.
19. **Will picks up the broken sword** — He finds the shattered remains of the longsword, splintered and twisted, and takes it as proof.
20. **Royce reanimates** — Will stands and finds Ser Waymar standing over him. Royce's face is ruined; a sword shard transfixes his left eye (blind white pupil); his right eye burns blue. He strangles Will with icy cold hands.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deep, escalating fear throughout — begins as unease, builds to terror. Will shares Gared's nervousness from the start. Something about the darkness feels wrong: "an edge to this darkness that made his hackles rise." He feels watched by "something cold and implacable that loved him not." After climbing the tree, "fear filled his gut like a meal he could not digest." During the attack he is paralyzed — unable to call out a warning, choosing survival over duty.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival; the wrongness of the environment; the desire to ride back to the Wall
- **Key decisions made:** Will chooses not to call out a warning to Royce when the Others surround him ("It was his duty. And his death, if he did. He shivered, and hugged the tree, and kept the silence"). He later descends and picks up the broken sword as proof rather than fleeing immediately.
- **Self-deception flags:** Will briefly tries to convince himself the pale shapes might be "a bird, a reflection on the snow, some trick of the moonlight" — grasping for a rational explanation before acknowledging the supernatural reality.
