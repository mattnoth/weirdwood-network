# AGOT — Sansa III

## Chapter Metadata

- **location_primary:** Tower of the Hand, King's Landing

- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Eddard's judgment on the Gregor Clegane matter; Lord Beric's party rides out the next morning
- **time_markers:** "that night" (cold supper by lamplight); "the next morning she woke before first light"; "dawn was breaking over the city"; breakfast time; "it was midday when Septa Mordane knocked upon her door"
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa and Jeyne Poole discuss Lord Eddard's decision to send Lord Beric rather than Ser Loras to deal with Ser Gregor Clegane. Sansa recalls Lord Baelish overhearing her complaints and telling her "life is not a song." The next morning she watches Lord Beric's party ride out, then at breakfast quarrels with Arya, who throws a blood orange at her and ruins her ivory silk dress. At midday Eddard summons both daughters and announces he is sending them back to Winterfell by ship for their safety. Sansa inadvertently says Joffrey's son will have golden hair, prompting a visible reaction from Eddard.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa and Jeyne Poole share cold supper** — They discuss Lord Eddard's decision to send Lord Beric instead of Ser Loras against Ser Gregor Clegane.
2. **Sansa recalls Littlefinger's overheard comment** — After court, Lord Baelish overheard Sansa questioning her father's decision on the stairs. He touched her cheek and told her "life is not a song."
3. **Sansa and Jeyne discuss Joffrey and the white hart** — Sansa shares her "dream" (actually a wish) of Joffrey shooting the white hart with a golden arrow.
4. **Sansa recounts the court session** — She describes Yoren begging men for the Wall, Eddard giving Yoren his pick of the dungeons, and two Dornish freeriders pledging their swords.
5. **Sansa and Jeyne search the kitchen for lemon cakes** — They find no lemon cakes but eat half a cold strawberry pie on the tower steps.
6. **Lord Beric's party rides out at dawn** — Sansa watches from her window before first light. Three banners go before them: the crowned stag, the Stark direwolf (carried by Alyn), and Lord Beric's forked lightning standard.
7. **Sansa and Arya quarrel at breakfast** — Arya says Jaime Lannister murdered Jory, Heward, and Wyl, and the Hound murdered Mycah. Sansa defends the Hound as Joffrey's sworn shield. Arya calls Sansa a liar and throws a blood orange at her face.
8. **Sansa's ivory silk dress is ruined** — The blood orange stains the silk; the stain bleeds through to her underskirt. Sansa throws the dress into the cold hearth and cries herself to sleep.
9. **Sansa dreams of Lady** — At midday when woken, Sansa whispers "Lady" and recalls dreaming of running with her direwolf, but the dream fades.
10. **Eddard summons both daughters** — He is bent over a huge leather-bound book in his solar. Sansa arrives in a pale green damask gown; Arya in ratty leathers.
11. **Arya apologizes to Sansa** — Arya says she was wrong and begs forgiveness; Sansa is startled.
12. **Eddard announces he is sending both daughters back to Winterfell** — He cites safety concerns: three of his men killed, Robert goes hunting instead of acting. He plans to send them by trading galley with Septa Mordane, guards, and possibly Syrio Forel.
13. **Sansa pleads to stay** — She professes love for Joffrey, comparing it to Queen Naerys and Prince Aemon, and to Jonquil and Ser Florian. She says she will give Joffrey "a son with golden hair."
14. **Eddard reacts to Sansa's words** — Upon hearing "a son with golden hair," Eddard swears "Gods… out of the mouth of babes." He then urgently arranges the departure and insists on secrecy.
15. **Sansa cries as she is escorted away** — She feels her life is over. Arya tries to comfort her by mentioning the galley adventure and seeing Bran, Robb, Old Nan, and Hodor. Sansa lashes out, telling Arya she should marry Hodor.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bewildered and disappointed by her father's choices at court; uneasy about Littlefinger's attention; romantically infatuated with Joffrey; disgusted by Arya; devastated at the prospect of leaving King's Landing; grief-stricken — she cries multiple times throughout the chapter
- **Primary preoccupation:** Her betrothal to Joffrey and the courtly life she fears losing; she filters everything through songs and romantic stories
- **Key decisions made:** None — Sansa is acted upon throughout this chapter, pleading but unable to change outcomes
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes Joffrey had no part in the attack on Eddard's men, blaming Jaime entirely; calls her wish about Joffrey and the white hart a "dream" because "everyone knew that dreams were prophetic"; believes her love for Joffrey mirrors the great romances of song; believes Joffrey is nothing like Robert (unwittingly supporting the truth about his parentage); convinced queens do not cry
