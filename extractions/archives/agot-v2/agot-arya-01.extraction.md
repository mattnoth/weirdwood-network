- **time_markers:** "the afternoon quiet of the tower room"; "the hour of play is done" (Joffrey); references to a prior feast (no specific days given)
- **chapter_summary:** Arya struggles with her needlework in a sewing lesson alongside Sansa, Jeyne Poole, Beth Cassel, and Princess Myrcella under Septa Mordane's supervision. After being humiliated by the septa's criticism of her stitches, Arya flees the room and goes with her direwolf Nymeria to a covered bridge window overlooking the practice yard. She watches Bran spar with Prince Tommen, then witnesses a confrontation between Robb and Joffrey when Joffrey demands live steel and insults the Starks. Jon Snow, watching from the same window, provides sardonic commentary on Joffrey's heraldry and the unfairness of rules governing bastards and girls. Arya eventually returns to her room to find both Septa Mordane and her mother waiting.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya struggles with needlework** — Arya frowns at her crooked stitches while Sansa sews expertly nearby; Septa Mordane ignores Arya in favor of Princess Myrcella
2. **Girls discuss Prince Joffrey** — Sansa, Jeyne Poole, and Beth Cassel whisper about Joffrey; Jeyne reports Joffrey told Sansa she was beautiful at the feast; Beth says he will marry Sansa and she will be queen
3. **Arya provokes tension** — Arya reports Jon's opinion that Joffrey "looks like a girl"; Sansa calls Jon jealous because he's a bastard; Arya loudly insists "He's our brother," cutting through the quiet room
4. **Sansa smoothly deflects** — Sansa corrects to "our half brother" and tells the septa they were praising the princess's presence
5. **Septa Mordane inspects Arya's work** — The septa crosses the room, examines Arya's stitches, and declares "This will not do at all"; everyone stares; Jeyne smirks
6. **Arya flees the sewing lesson** — Humiliated and in tears, Arya pushes out of her chair and bolts for the door; she stops to bow stiffly to Myrcella and delivers the parting line "I have to go shoe a horse"
7. **Arya retrieves Nymeria** — Arya finds Nymeria waiting in the guardroom at the base of the stairs, unties her, and hugs her
8. **Arya reflects on Sansa's superiority** — Internal monologue: Sansa can sew, dance, sing, write poetry, play the high harp and bells, and is beautiful with Tully coloring; Arya has a long solemn face, lusterless brown hair, and can only ride and manage a household better; Jeyne used to call her "Arya Horseface"
9. **Arya and Nymeria go to the covered bridge** — They run to a window between the armory and the Great Keep that overlooks the whole practice yard
10. **Arya finds Jon and Ghost at the window** — Jon is sitting on the sill watching the yard; Ghost greets Nymeria cautiously; Arya climbs up beside Jon
11. **Bran spars with Tommen** — The younger boys drill under Ser Rodrik's supervision with padded wooden swords; both are staggering; Bran knocks Tommen down
12. **Arya and Jon discuss fighting** — Arya says she could fight as well as Bran; Jon says she's too skinny to lift a longsword; she glares at him
13. **Jon points out Joffrey's heraldry** — Joffrey stands at the back under the shade of the high stone wall, surrounded by Lannister and Baratheon squires; his surcoat bears both the crowned stag and the Lannister lion; Jon calls the Lannisters proud
14. **Arya and Jon discuss arms for girls and bastards** — Jon says girls get the arms but not the swords, bastards get the swords but not the arms; Arya suggests wedding Tully to Stark — "a wolf with a fish in its mouth"
15. **Ser Rodrik calls for Joffrey and Robb to spar** — Robb agrees eagerly; Joffrey dismisses it as a children's game and demands live steel
16. **Sandor Clegane confronts Ser Rodrik** — The burned man challenges Ser Rodrik's authority: "This is your prince. Who are you to tell him he may not have an edge on his sword, ser?"; asks "Are you training women here?"; boasts he killed a man at twelve
17. **Ser Rodrik insists on safety** — He asserts his title as master-at-arms of Winterfell; offers tourney swords with blunted edges as a compromise; says they will have steel "when they are of an age"
18. **Joffrey refuses and departs with a taunt** — Joffrey shrugs, tells Robb "Come and see me when you're older, Stark. If you're not too old," and leaves with Tommen and the Lannister party amid laughter
19. **Robb rages; Theon restrains him** — Robb's curses ring through the yard; Theon Greyjoy seizes his arm and holds him in an iron grip until the princes are safely away; Ser Rodrik's face is beet-red
20. **Jon departs** — Jon tells Arya to go back before her punishment worsens; warns she'll be sewing all through winter; says "Nothing is fair"; walks away with Ghost
21. **Arya returns to her room** — Arya reluctantly turns the other direction; she finds Septa Mordane and her mother both waiting for her

- **Key decisions made:** Flees the sewing lesson rather than endure further humiliation; goes to the practice yard instead of hiding in her room
- **Self-deception flags:** None observed — Arya is remarkably honest about her own shortcomings and feelings throughout
