# AGOT — Catelyn II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 7
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn II
- **first_available:** AGOT Catelyn II
- **location_primary:** Winterfell — Catelyn's bedchambers in the Great Keep
- **location_secondary:** Riverrun (recalled), the Eyrie (referenced), Starfall (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** The night after Robert's arrival at Winterfell; Ned is deliberating whether to accept the position of Hand of the King
- **time_markers:** Night; Ned and Catelyn have just finished lovemaking; "fifteen long years" since their wedding; "three years since Rickon"; Ned was eight when fostered at the Eyrie; "a fortnight before we are ready to depart"; Bran is seven; Sansa is eleven; Jon and Robb are fourteen; Catelyn was twelve when betrothed to Brandon
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn and Ned discuss whether he should accept Robert's offer to become Hand of the King, with Catelyn urging acceptance and Ned reluctant. Maester Luwin arrives with a secret letter from Lysa Arryn, hidden in a carved wooden box containing a Myrish lens. The letter, written in a childhood cipher, accuses the Lannisters and Queen Cersei of murdering Jon Arryn. This tips the decision: Ned agrees to go south as Hand. They discuss which children will go — Sansa and Arya to court, Bran to befriend the princes, Rickon and Robb to remain at Winterfell with Catelyn. The question of Jon Snow's future arises; Catelyn refuses to keep him, and Maester Luwin reveals that Benjen Stark has raised the possibility of Jon joining the Night's Watch. Ned reluctantly agrees.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn and Ned lie in bed after lovemaking** — Catelyn reflects on the warmth of Winterfell's hot springs and her bedchamber. She hopes Ned's seed will quicken and give her another son.
2. **Ned opens the windows** — He rises from bed naked, pulls back the tapestries, and opens the high narrow windows, letting cold night air into the chamber.
3. **Ned declares he will refuse the Handship** — He tells Catelyn he has no wish to be Robert's Hand; his duties are in the north.
4. **Catelyn argues Ned must accept** — She warns that refusing a king is dangerous, invokes the betrothal of Sansa to Joffrey as an honor, and reminds Ned that Brandon is dead and the responsibility has passed to him.
5. **Ned reflects bitterly on Brandon** — He says everything was meant for Brandon — Catelyn, Winterfell, everything — and he never asked for this burden.
6. **A knock at the door interrupts them** — Desmond announces that Maester Luwin begs urgent audience despite Ned's orders not to be disturbed.
7. **Ned admits Maester Luwin** — Ned puts on a robe; Catelyn pulls furs to her chin against the cold.
8. **Luwin presents the carved wooden box** — He explains a box with a Myrish lens was left in his observatory by someone in the king's party, with no rider and no witnesses.
9. **Catelyn realizes the lens is a coded message** — She says "A lens is an instrument to help us see."
10. **Luwin produces the concealed letter** — He found it in a false bottom of the box; it is marked for Lady Catelyn's eyes only, sealed with blue wax and the moon-and-falcon seal of House Arryn.
11. **Catelyn identifies the letter as from Lysa** — She recognizes the seal and says it will not make them glad.
12. **Catelyn reads the letter** — It is written in a childhood cipher she shared with Lysa; she deciphers it.
13. **Catelyn burns the letter** — She crosses the room naked, lights a fire in the cold hearth, and slides the letter into the kindling.
14. **Catelyn reveals the letter's contents** — Lysa accuses the Lannisters and the Queen of murdering Jon Arryn.
15. **Ned resists going south** — He says "The south is a nest of adders" and prefers to stay in the north.
16. **Luwin counsels acceptance** — He argues the Hand has power to find the truth, bring killers to justice, and protect Lysa and her son.
17. **Catelyn presses Ned** — She asks if he would leave Robert surrounded by Lannisters.
18. **Ned accepts the Handship** — After gazing out the window in farewell, he agrees, noting his father went south to answer a king's summons and never came home.
19. **Ned assigns Catelyn to govern the north** — She must stay at Winterfell and make Robb part of her councils; "There must always be a Stark in Winterfell."
20. **Ned trusts Luwin with his family** — He asks Luwin to give Catelyn his voice and teach Robb what he needs to know.
21. **They decide which children go south** — Sansa must wed Joffrey; Arya must learn southron ways; Bran will befriend the princes. Rickon and Robb stay with Catelyn.
22. **Catelyn asks Ned to keep Bran off the walls** — Knowing Bran loves to climb, she asks this as a condition.
23. **Luwin raises the question of Jon Snow** — Catelyn reacts with tension and anger.
24. **Catelyn's internal reflection on Jon Snow** — She recalls Ned bringing his bastard home, the rumors of Ashara Dayne, the one time she asked Ned about Jon's mother and he frightened her into silence.
25. **Catelyn refuses to keep Jon at Winterfell** — She says "He is your son, not mine. I will not have him."
26. **Ned objects to taking Jon south** — He says there will be no place for a bastard at court.
27. **Luwin proposes the Night's Watch** — He reveals that Benjen Stark approached him about Jon aspiring to take the black.
28. **Ned reluctantly agrees** — He will speak to Benjen; he wants to tell Jon himself and let the boy enjoy his remaining days before departure, estimated at a fortnight.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Shifts through the chapter — begins with warmth, sexual satisfaction, and hope (praying Ned's seed quickens); moves through fear, urgency, and dread upon receiving the letter; experiences grief at losing four children to the south; hardens into cold resolve regarding Jon Snow
- **Primary preoccupation:** Protecting her family by persuading Ned to accept the Handship; securing her children's futures; removing Jon Snow from Winterfell
- **Key decisions made:** Urges Ned to accept Hand of the King; agrees to let Sansa, Arya, and Bran go south; demands Jon Snow leave Winterfell; burns Lysa's letter
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn rationalizes her hostility toward Jon as pragmatism ("Ned would do the boy no kindness by leaving him here") while her true motivation is jealousy and resentment; she tells herself "the gods knew that Arya needed refinement" to ease letting her go
