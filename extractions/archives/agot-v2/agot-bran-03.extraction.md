# AGOT — Bran III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 18

- **location_primary:** Dreamscape (falling/flying vision), then Winterfell tower room
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell (seen from above), the Bite (sea), the Trident, the narrow sea, Free Cities, Dothraki sea, Vaes Dothrak, Jade Sea, Asshai by the Shadow, the Wall, lands beyond the Wall
- **approximate_timeline:** While Bran lies comatose at Winterfell; ends with his awakening
- **time_markers:** No specific time-of-day markers in the dream sequence; cold in the tower room when he wakes; window is open
- **chapter_summary:** Bran experiences a prolonged falling dream in which a three-eyed crow urges him to fly rather than fall, warning he will die if he hits the ground. During the fall, Bran has sweeping visions of Winterfell, his mother on a galley, his father and sisters in the south, lands across the narrow sea, and the far north beyond the Wall. The crow tells him "winter is coming" and commands him to choose: fly or die. Bran spreads his arms and flies, then the crow pecks him between the eyes and the dream dissolves. Bran wakes in a tower room in Winterfell, discovers he cannot move his legs, and names his direwolf Summer.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** A storm is gathering ahead of Catelyn's galley (seen in vision) — "a vast dark roaring lashed by lightning." Cold in the tower room when Bran wakes; the window is open.
- **Season indicators:** "Because winter is coming" (the crow's words). Snow on mountain peaks seen during the fall. The far north described as snow, cold, death, frozen wasteland, blue-white spires of ice.
- **Time of day:** Not specified in the dream. Not specified at waking.
- **Lighting:** Darkness throughout the dream — "no sun, no stars." Grey mists. The golden face shines with light. The Wall shines "like blue crystal." A "curtain of light at the end of the world" in the far north. Yellow eyes of the direwolf shine "like the sun."
- **Sounds:** The grey mists howl around Bran as he plunges. The crow caws and shrieks. Death screams as it reaches for him. The serving woman shouts "He's awake" repeatedly.
- **Smells:** None described.
- **Notable sensory details:** Cold in the darkness of the dream. Bran feels how fast he is falling. Golden kernels of corn slide between his fingers. The crow pecks at him — a "sudden blinding pain" between his eyes, which continues burning after waking. Bran feels weak and dizzy upon waking. He cannot feel his legs — "nothing happened" when he tries to move; he feels nothing when the wolf lands on his legs. The warmth from the direwolf "enfolded him like a hot bath." His hand trembles "like a leaf." A basin of water shatters on the floor.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran falls through darkness** — Bran is falling through a void of grey mists with no sun or stars, aware he will die if he hits the ground.
2. **A voice urges Bran to fly** — A high, thin voice tells Bran to fly instead of cry; Bran protests he cannot fly.
3. **The crow appears** — A crow spirals down with Bran, asks for corn, and lands on his hand to eat golden kernels from Bran's pocket.
4. **The crow challenges Bran** — The crow questions whether Bran is really falling and whether the dream is just a dream, warning "You'll die when you hit the ground."
5. **The crow tells Bran about different wings** — When Bran points out the crow has wings, the crow says "Maybe you do too" and mentions "different kinds of wings."
6. **A golden face appears** — A shining golden face emerges from the mist and says "The things I do for love." Bran screams.
7. **The crow banishes the memory** — The crow shrieks "Not that" and "Forget that, you do not need it now," pecks at Bran, and the golden face vanishes.
8. **The crow commands Bran to look down** — Bran resists, but the crow insists. Bran sees the whole world spread out below him.
9. **Bran's vision of Winterfell** — He sees Winterfell from above: Maester Luwin studying the sky, Robb practicing with real steel, Hodor carrying an anvil to the forge, the weirwood in the godswood staring back at him.
10. **Bran's vision to the east** — He sees a galley on the Bite; his mother alone in a cabin with a bloodstained knife; Ser Rodrik seasick at the rail; a storm ahead they cannot see.
11. **Bran's vision to the south** — He sees the Trident; his father pleading with the king; Sansa crying herself to sleep; Arya watching in silence. Shadows surround them: one dark as ash with a hound's face, one armored golden and beautiful, and a giant in stone armor with nothing inside but darkness and thick black blood.
12. **Bran's vision across the narrow sea** — He sees the Free Cities, the Dothraki sea, Vaes Dothrak, the Jade Sea, and Asshai by the Shadow where "dragons stirred beneath the sunrise."
13. **Bran's vision to the north** — He sees the Wall like blue crystal, Jon sleeping alone in a cold bed growing pale and hard. Beyond the Wall: forests, frozen shore, rivers of ice, dead plains, a curtain of light, and then beyond it — the heart of winter. Bran cries out in fear.
14. **The crow reveals its purpose** — The crow whispers "Now you know why you must live" and says "Because winter is coming." Bran sees the crow has three eyes; the third eye is "full of a terrible knowledge."
15. **Bran sees the frozen wasteland below** — Jagged spires of ice and bones of a thousand other dreamers impaled on them.
16. **Bran remembers his father's words** — He hears his own voice asking "Can a man still be brave if he's afraid?" and his father's answer: "That is the only time a man can be brave."
17. **The crow commands Bran to choose** — "Fly or die." Death reaches for him, screaming.
18. **Bran flies** — He spreads his arms and soars upward on unseen wings. He cries out in delight.
19. **The crow pecks Bran between the eyes** — The three-eyed crow flaps in his face, slows him, and stabs its beak at his forehead, causing blinding pain between his eyes.
20. **Bran wakes** — The grey mists dissolve; the crow becomes a serving woman with long black hair. Bran realizes he is in Winterfell, in a bed in a tower room. The woman drops a basin and runs shouting "He's awake."
21. **Bran discovers he cannot move** — He touches his forehead (burning but no wound), feels weak and dizzy, tries to get out of bed, but "nothing happened." He feels nothing when Summer lands on his legs.
22. **Bran names his direwolf** — Summer lands on his legs; yellow eyes look into Bran's; the wolf radiates warmth. Bran realizes his pup has grown large.
23. **Robb arrives** — Robb bursts into the room breathless. Summer is licking Bran's face. Bran looks up and says "His name is Summer."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terrified throughout the falling dream — crying, desperate, afraid. Brief wonder and awe during the aerial visions. Delight when he successfully flies. Disoriented and weak upon waking. Calm when he names Summer.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival — the dream is framed as a literal life-or-death choice between flying and dying. Upon waking, he is disoriented by his inability to move.
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to fly rather than die, spreading his arms at the crow's urging. Names his direwolf Summer.
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran initially insists "It's just a dream" and "I'll wake up when I hit the ground" — the crow contradicts both. The golden face memory surfaces but the crow forces Bran to suppress it.
