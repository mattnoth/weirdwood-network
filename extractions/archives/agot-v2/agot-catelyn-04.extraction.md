# AGOT — Catelyn IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 19
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn IV
- **first_available:** AGOT Catelyn IV
- **location_primary:** King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Storm Dancer (galley on the narrow sea approaching King's Landing), the inn on Eel Alley (Visenya's Hill), the Red Keep (tower room)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Catelyn and Ser Rodrik's departure from White Harbor; arrives King's Landing by ship; Ned Stark has already departed Winterfell as Hand
- **time_markers:** Arrival within the hour (opening); Catelyn sleeps and wakes at sunset (rooftops red in setting sun light); lamps being lit along streets when she rides to castle; gates sealed for the night at Red Keep; Ser Rodrik visited the castle "early this afternoon" per Varys; "about sunset" Ser Rodrik and Ser Aron left the castle together; Moreo has plied the narrow sea for thirty years; Ser Rodrik's mother is forty years dead; Petyr's father died "several years before"
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn and Ser Rodrik arrive at King's Landing aboard the Tyroshi galley Storm Dancer after a rough voyage from White Harbor. They take rooms at an inn on Eel Alley rather than going to the castle, and Ser Rodrik departs to find Ser Aron Santagar, the king's master-at-arms, to identify the dagger used in the attempt on Bran's life. While Catelyn sleeps, gold cloaks of the City Watch arrive bearing Littlefinger's mockingbird seal and escort her to the Red Keep. There, Petyr Baelish and Lord Varys question her; Varys already knows about the dagger through his intelligence network. Littlefinger identifies the blade as Valyrian steel and claims it was his until he lost it in a wager at Prince Joffrey's name day tourney — the winner, he says, was Tyrion Lannister.

## Events & Actions
1. **Storm Dancer approaches King's Landing** — Captain Moreo announces they will arrive within the hour. Catelyn promises silver stags to each oarman. Moreo favors her with a half bow.
2. **Catelyn reflects on the voyage** — She recalls choosing the galley over a fishing sloop at White Harbor despite Ser Rodrik's objections. The winds were against them and a storm hit off Dragonstone. Ser Rodrik nearly went overboard but was rescued by three of Moreo's men.
3. **Catelyn's injured hands** — Beneath linen bandages, her fingers throb where the dagger bit. She cannot bend the last two fingers on her left hand. She considers the pain her scourge, a small price for Bran's life.
4. **Ser Rodrik appears on deck** — He looks thinner and has shaved off his great white side whiskers after repeated seasickness. Moreo greets him as "my good friend" through his forked green beard.
5. **Catelyn and Ser Rodrik discuss their plan** — They agree Catelyn must not enter the castle. Ser Rodrik will go to find Ser Aron Santagar. They discuss the risk of being recognized, particularly by Littlefinger.
6. **Catelyn recalls Littlefinger** — She remembers Petyr Baelish as her father's ward at Riverrun, his feelings for her, his challenge to Brandon Stark for her hand at age fifteen against Brandon at twenty, Brandon sparing him with a scar at Catelyn's plea, her father sending him away, and her burning his letter unread after Brandon's death. She knew Ned would marry her in Brandon's place.
7. **King's Landing comes into view** — The far-eyes calls from the rigging. Catelyn observes the city's three hills, the Great Sept of Baelor with seven crystal towers, the Dragonpit with its collapsing dome, the Street of the Sisters, the harbor full of ships, and the Red Keep with Baratheon banners replacing Targaryen.
8. **Ser Rodrik's plan** — He will enter the castle unrecognized since his shaved face is unrecognizable even to himself — his mother was the last to see him without whiskers, forty years dead. Catelyn will wait at an inn.
9. **Arrival at the docks** — The Storm Dancer docks with sixty oars lifting, reversing, and backing water in unison. Tyroshi seamen leap to tie up. Moreo offers to help carry things to the castle; Catelyn declines, asking for an inn instead.
10. **Payment negotiations** — Moreo asks for the second half of their agreed payment plus the sixty silver stags for oarmen. He suggests holding the silver for the oarmen "for the sake of their wives and children." Ser Rodrik says "Winter is coming." Catelyn insists on paying the oarmen directly.
11. **Catelyn pays the oarmen directly** — She gives each man a silver stag personally. She pays two porters a copper each to carry their chests halfway up Visenya's Hill.
12. **Settling at the inn on Eel Alley** — They take rooms at a rambling inn owned by a suspicious crone with a wandering eye who bites the coin to test it. The rooms are large and airy; Moreo praises the fish stew. The innkeeper has no interest in their names.
13. **Ser Rodrik departs** — Armed with ringmail, dagger, longsword, and a dark hooded cloak, he sets off. Ser Rodrik advises Catelyn to stay away from the common room. He promises to return before nightfall with Ser Aron.
14. **Catelyn watches and sleeps** — She watches Ser Rodrik stride through the busy streets until lost in crowds, then falls asleep on straw-stuffed bedding.
15. **Gold cloaks arrive** — Catelyn wakes at sunset to pounding on her door. Men of the City Watch in black ringmail and golden cloaks demand entry in the name of the king, bearing a ribbon sealed with Littlefinger's grey wax mockingbird.
16. **Catelyn dresses and deduces betrayal** — She bathes her hands, wraps them in clean linen, dresses with difficulty. She suspects Moreo sold their identities, hoping he got a good price.
17. **Escort to the Red Keep** — A horse is provided. The City Watch escorts Catelyn through the lamplit streets. At the Red Keep, the portcullis is down and great gates sealed for the night. They enter through a narrow postern door and climb endless steps to a tower.
18. **Meeting with Littlefinger** — Petyr is alone, writing at a heavy wooden table by oil lamp. He greets her as "Cat." He dismisses the guards. Catelyn is angry at being summoned like a serving wench.
19. **Catelyn observes Littlefinger** — He looks contrite, a gift he had as a child. He has changed little: still small, slender, sharp-featured, grey-green eyes, now with a pointed chin beard and silver threads in dark hair. His silver mockingbird fastens his cloak — "even as a child, he had always loved his silver."
20. **Littlefinger explains Varys detected her** — Varys came to him first. He notes Selmy and Renly rode north, Stannis is at Dragonstone, leaving only himself and Pycelle on the council. He claims friendship with Lysa Arryn.
21. **Littlefinger tests Catelyn's story** — She claims she came for her husband and daughters. He sees through it, asking for the Tully words. She recites "Family, Duty, Honor," noting he knows her too well.
22. **Varys arrives** — Lord Varys enters with a soft knock: plump, perfumed, powdered, hairless as an egg. Purple silk gown, woven gold vest, soft velvet slippers. He takes Catelyn's hand in both of his; his flesh is soft and moist, his breath smells of lilacs.
23. **Varys offers sympathy and aid** — He expresses sorrow about Bran. Offers Maester Pycelle's salve and healers from the Free Cities. Catelyn deflects, saying Maester Luwin has seen to her hurts. She refuses to speak of Bran before these men.
24. **Varys reveals he knows about the dagger** — He asks to see the dagger without being told about it, shocking Catelyn. He reports that Ser Rodrik visited Ser Aron Santagar at the armory, they discussed the dagger, left the castle together about sunset, and are now drinking in the inn's common room.
25. **The dagger is examined** — Catelyn throws the dagger on the table. Varys cuts his thumb testing the edge, squeals, and drops it. Littlefinger identifies the blade as Valyrian steel, hefts it, tests the balance, flips it in the air, then throws it across the room into the oak door where it buries deep and quivers.
26. **Littlefinger identifies the dagger's owner** — He claims it was his until he lost it in a wager at the tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day. He backed Ser Jaime in the jousting along with half the court. Loras Tyrell unhorsed Jaime. Jaime lost a hundred golden dragons, the queen lost an emerald pendant (later recovered), and Littlefinger lost his knife. The winner — and claimed current owner — was Tyrion Lannister, "the Imp." Varys watches Catelyn's face as the name is spoken.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined and urgent at the start; pain from her wounded fingers serves as constant reminder of purpose ("the pain was her scourge, lest she forget"). Nostalgic and somewhat wistful when recalling Petyr and Riverrun — lifts her face to the spray "as if the brisk wind could blow the memories away." Anxious about being recognized in King's Landing. Alarmed when gold cloaks arrive — grabs the dagger before opening the door. Angry and indignant at being summoned by Littlefinger. Guarded and suspicious in the meeting — trusts Littlefinger "only a little" and Varys "not at all"; will not let them see her grief or speak of Bran. Fear mounts when the dagger's owner is about to be named — "her mouth dry with fear," "her fingers ached with remembered pain."
- **Primary preoccupation:** Identifying the owner of the Valyrian steel dagger used in the attempt on Bran's life; maintaining secrecy about her presence and purpose in King's Landing.
- **Key decisions made:** Insisted on the faster galley over a fishing sloop from the Three Sisters; chose not to enter the Red Keep; paid oarmen directly rather than through Moreo; went with the gold cloaks after recognizing Littlefinger's seal; showed the dagger to Littlefinger and Varys despite not trusting them; refused to speak of Bran before these men.
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn believes she knows Littlefinger well from childhood ("I thought of him as a brother"), yet simultaneously wonders "what the years have done to him." She accepts his identification of the dagger's owner without independent verification. She maintains that he "did know her too well" — a mutual assumption of familiarity that may be outdated after years apart.
