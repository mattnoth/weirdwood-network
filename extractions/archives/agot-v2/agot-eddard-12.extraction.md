# AGOT — Eddard XII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 46
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard XII
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard XII
- **location_primary:** Tower of the Hand, Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Godswood of the Red Keep
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after the fight with Jaime Lannister in the streets; Robert is away on a hunt; Ned is bedridden with a broken leg; three days before Sansa and Arya are scheduled to sail on the Wind Witch
- **time_markers:** "an hour after the Grand Maester had left" (Littlefinger's visit); "Late that afternoon" (Ned summons Fat Tom); "She came to him at sunset, as the clouds reddened above the walls and towers"; "three days hence" (Sansa and Arya to sail); "A dozen years" (length of Robert and Cersei's marriage); "some ninety years ago" (Tya Lannister wed Gowen Baratheon); "Thirty years before that" (another Lannister-Baratheon union)
- **chapter_summary:** Ned Stark, bedridden with a broken leg, receives visits from Grand Maester Pycelle and Littlefinger, learning that Lord Tywin is mustering forces at Casterly Rock and that Prince Joffrey has returned from the hunt while Robert continues deeper into the forest. Ned reflects on Sansa's innocent observation that confirmed the truth about Cersei's children — that none are Robert's. He summons Cersei to the godswood and confronts her directly: all three of her children are Jaime's. Cersei confirms the incest and her contempt for Robert. Ned warns her to flee with her children before Robert returns, but Cersei responds with the threat "When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die."

## Events & Actions
1. **Pycelle visits Ned** — Grand Maester Pycelle treats Ned's broken leg, leaves milk of the poppy, and reveals that Lord Tywin sent a raven to Cersei expressing fury about the men sent after Gregor Clegane.
2. **Ned dismisses Tywin's threat** — Ned tells Pycelle that Lord Beric rides under the king's banner and Robert will deal with Tywin if he interferes. Pycelle departs, and Ned suspects he goes straight to Cersei.
3. **Ned reflects on the truth** — Ned drinks honeyed wine and recalls how Sansa's innocent remark ("He's not the least bit like that old drunken king") made the truth about Cersei's children clear. He recognizes this truth killed Jon Arryn and will destroy Robert.
4. **Littlefinger visits Ned** — Littlefinger reports that freeriders and sellswords are flocking to Casterly Rock, that Robert found the white hart killed by wolves and now pursues a monstrous boar, and that Prince Joffrey has returned with part of the hunting party including the Hound.
5. **Littlefinger notices Malleon's book** — Littlefinger sees the genealogy tome and remarks on its tedium. Ned tells him Jon Arryn was studying it when he fell ill. Littlefinger quips and departs.
6. **Ned assesses his allies** — Ned reflects that he trusts almost no one in King's Landing: Littlefinger is self-interested, Varys does too little, Pycelle serves Cersei, and Ser Barristan is rigid.
7. **Ned contemplates his duty and the danger** — Ned knows he must tell Robert the truth when the king returns; Vayon Poole has arranged for Sansa and Arya to sail in three days. He dreams of Rhaegar's murdered children and fears Robert will kill Cersei's children.
8. **Ned weighs Robert's capacity for mercy** — He recalls Robert pardoning Pycelle, Varys, and Balon Greyjoy but concludes this betrayal Robert could never forgive.
9. **Ned summons Fat Tom** — Late in the afternoon, Ned summons Tomard, orders the guard doubled on the Tower of the Hand, and asks to be carried to the godswood.
10. **Ned sends for Cersei** — In the godswood beside the faceless oak heart tree, Ned gives Fat Tom a sealed letter for delivery.
11. **Cersei arrives at sunset** — Cersei comes alone, dressed simply in hunting greens, with a faded bruise on her face from Robert.
12. **Ned confronts Cersei about incest** — Ned tells Cersei he knows the truth Jon Arryn died for. He states all three children are Jaime's. Cersei confirms the incest, saying she and Jaime have been lovers since childhood, comparing themselves to Targaryens.
13. **Cersei confirms Bran saw them** — When Ned mentions Bran, Cersei admits Bran saw them together.
14. **Cersei reveals she aborted Robert's child** — Cersei says Robert got her with child once and Jaime found a woman to "cleanse" her. She has not let Robert inside her for years.
15. **Cersei reveals Robert's wedding night** — Cersei tells Ned that Robert called her "Lyanna" on their wedding night, the origin of her hatred.
16. **Cersei offers Ned a bargain** — Cersei suggests Ned could rule as Hand through Joffrey's minority, touching his leg and face suggestively, offering alliance and intimacy.
17. **Ned rejects her and gives warning** — Ned tells Cersei she must flee with her children before Robert returns — to the Free Cities, Summer Isles, or Port of Ibben. He advises Tywin and her brothers to go as well.
18. **Cersei threatens Ned** — Cersei tells Ned he should have seized the throne himself when he found Jaime sitting on it. She delivers the line: "When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die. There is no middle ground."
19. **Cersei departs** — Cersei raises her hood and leaves Ned alone in the godswood as stars come out.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Defiant but increasingly anxious; pained physically and emotionally; haunted by dreams of Rhaegar's murdered children; conflicted between duty and mercy; briefly tempted to confide in Littlefinger; sickened by the truth about Cersei's children; moved to near-weeping when Cersei mentions Lyanna; resigned to a course of action he knows is dangerous
- **Primary preoccupation:** How to handle the truth about Cersei's children — duty to Robert vs. desire to protect the children from Robert's wrath; getting his own daughters out of King's Landing safely
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to warn Cersei before telling Robert (giving her a chance to flee); decides to confront Robert with the truth when he returns from the hunt; has already arranged for Sansa and Arya to sail on the Wind Witch in three days
- **Self-deception flags:** "Not near as confident of Robert as he pretended" — deliberately projecting confidence about Robert's support to Cersei and Pycelle while privately doubting it; persuades himself Robert might show mercy while knowing deep down Robert "will kill them all"
