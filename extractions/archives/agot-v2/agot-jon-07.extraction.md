# AGOT — Jon VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 53
- **pov_character:** Jon Snow
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon VII
- **first_available:** AGOT Jon VII
- **location_primary:** The haunted forest beyond the Wall; Castle Black
- **location_secondary:** Lord Commander's Tower, common hall, storeroom along the Wall's base, stable
- **approximate_timeline:** Mid-AGOT; close on half a year since Benjen Stark departed; spirit summer ending
- **time_markers:** "The morning was unnaturally warm"; "well past midday by the time they started back"; "the rest of the afternoon passed as if in a dream"; "by the time the sun went down"; night (candle burns out, moonlight); last night Jon dreamed the Winterfell dream; "close on half a year since Benjen left us"; summer has lasted ten years; Jon was a babe in arms when summer began
- **chapter_summary:** Rangers discover the corpses of Othor and Jafer Flowers — two of Benjen Stark's missing men — in the woods beyond the Wall. Sam observes the bodies show no rot, no blood, and have changed eye color to blue. Mormont orders the bodies brought back to Castle Black. Jon learns that King Robert is dead and Lord Eddard has been imprisoned for treason. After attacking Ser Alliser Thorne for calling him "a traitor's bastard," Jon is confined to his cell. That night the corpse of Othor reanimates as a wight, kills Jon's guard, and enters the Lord Commander's Tower. Jon and Ghost fight the wight and Jon sets it ablaze with an oil lamp.

## Events & Actions
1. **Discovery of corpses** — Ser Jaremy Rykker identifies two dead rangers as Othor and Jafer Flowers, both of Benjen Stark's missing party, found in the woods beyond the Wall.
2. **Mormont questions Ser Jaremy** — The Lord Commander demands to know how two men died within half a day's ride of Castle Black without rangers detecting anything.
3. **Ser Jaremy suggests wildling attack** — He argues the men were the last survivors of Benjen's party, killed on their way back.
4. **Sam observes anomalies** — Sam notes the bodies show no fresh blood, no rot, no maggots, no animal scavenging, and the blood is dry black dust despite apparently fresh-looking corpses.
5. **Jon corroborates Sam** — Jon notes Ghost is different from the dogs and horses, which refuse to approach the bodies.
6. **Chett fails to bring hounds near the corpses** — A bitch lunges at Chett and escapes into the trees.
7. **Sam notes absence of blood on the ground** — Despite terrible wounds, there is no blood at the scene, suggesting they were killed elsewhere.
8. **Dywen observes the blue eyes** — Notes Othor never had blue eyes before; Ser Jaremy confirms the same for Jafer Flowers.
9. **Rangers call to burn the bodies** — Mormont overrules them, wanting Maester Aemon to examine the corpses first.
10. **Corpses transported on foot** — Horses refuse to carry the dead, forcing rangers to fashion crude slings from branches.
11. **Mormont orders a search** — Commands Ser Jaremy to search every inch of forest within ten leagues for Ben Stark's remaining men or any other presence.
12. **Return to Castle Black** — The column returns; Ghost rejoins them with a bloody muzzle from prey; the watchman's horn sounds one blast for returning rangers.
13. **Bowen Marsh delivers urgent news** — The Lord Steward meets them at the gate, agitated, saying a bird has come and Maester Aemon waits in the solar.
14. **Bodies stored in ice cell** — Jon has the dead men placed in a storeroom chiseled from the Wall's ice.
15. **Jon learns the king is dead** — Pyp tells Jon in the common hall that Robert Baratheon has died.
16. **Mormont tells Jon of Eddard's arrest** — In his solar, Mormont reveals Lord Eddard is imprisoned for treason, charged with plotting with Robert's brothers to deny Joffrey the throne.
17. **Mormont pledges to write letters** — Says he will write to king's councillors (Pycelle, Stannis, Barristan) urging that Eddard be allowed to take the black.
18. **Jon's friends rally to him** — Matthar says they lit a candle; Pyp and Grenn insist it's a lie; Sam offers to pray at the weirwoods with Jon.
19. **Ser Alliser taunts Jon** — Calls him "not only a bastard, but a traitor's bastard."
20. **Jon attacks Ser Alliser** — Vaults onto the table with a dagger, kicks Thorne's bowl away, slashes at his face; Sam, Pyp, Grenn, and Toad restrain him.
21. **Jon is confined** — Stripped of knife and sword, confined to his sleeping cell with a guard posted; Mormont expresses disgust and disappointment.
22. **Jon dozes and wakes to Ghost scratching at the door** — Ghost has grown noticeably tall; he bares fangs and gouges the door.
23. **Jon discovers dead guard** — Opens door to find the guard dead, head twisted completely around.
24. **Jon takes the guard's sword** — Arms himself and follows Ghost up the tower steps.
25. **Wight enters Mormont's solar** — A man-shape in black with icy blue glowing eyes moves toward Mormont's sleeping cell door.
26. **Ghost attacks the wight** — Wolf and wight grapple; the wight gets its black hands around Ghost's throat.
27. **Jon rips down the curtain** — Moonlight floods the room, revealing the wight choking Ghost.
28. **Jon severs the wight's arm** — Cuts through sleeve, skin, and bone; the severed arm continues to move, black fingers wriggling.
29. **Jon recognizes Othor** — Sees the wight's face and realizes it is the corpse they brought back.
30. **Severed arm attacks Jon's leg** — The dismembered arm crawls up his leg, clawing at flesh.
31. **Jon hacks at the wight's neck** — Cuts deep but the wight feels nothing and has no blood.
32. **Wight overpowers Jon** — Slams into him, knocks him down, jams black fingers into Jon's throat, choking him.
33. **Ghost saves Jon** — Pulls the wight off; buries teeth in its gut, ripping and tearing.
34. **Mormont appears with oil lamp** — Emerges from his sleeping cell naked and groggy.
35. **Jon sets the wight on fire** — Snatches the lamp from Mormont, throws it into the puddled curtain drapes; grabs burning drapes and whips them at the corpse.
36. **Raven screams "Burn!"** — Mormont's raven cries "Burn, burn, burn!" just before Jon acts.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Numbness and dread at the discovery of the corpses; unease in the forest; shock and disbelief at news of Eddard's arrest and Robert's death; fierce protectiveness of his father's honor; blind rage when Thorne taunts him; despair and isolation in confinement; terror during the wight attack; desperate determination when fighting
- **Primary preoccupation:** His father's imprisonment and the injustice of the treason charge; guilt and inner conflict about his identity as a bastard ("He fathered a bastard… Where was the honor in that?"); resolve to ask Eddard about his mother ("I am a man now, it is past time he told me. Even if she was a whore, I don't care, I want to know"); worry for Arya and Sansa
- **Key decisions made:** Encourages Sam to look at the bodies and speak up; attacks Ser Alliser Thorne with a dagger; takes the dead guard's sword and follows Ghost up the tower; rips down the curtain for moonlight; sets the wight on fire using Mormont's oil lamp
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself "It is only a wood… and they're only dead men" while clearly terrified; insists "I am a brother of the Night's Watch now, not a frightened boy" after his nightmare; thinks "The Others are only a story" while knowing otherwise; the small inner voice questioning Eddard's honor regarding the bastard
