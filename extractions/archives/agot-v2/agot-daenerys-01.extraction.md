- **time_markers:** Setting sun visible from Dany's window; red priests lighting night fires; streets pitch-dark when they depart for Drogo's manse; moonlight in the courtyard at the feast
- **chapter_summary:** Viserys presents Daenerys with a gown for her inspection by Khal Drogo. While being bathed and dressed by Illyrio's slaves, Dany reflects on her life as an exile — their flight from Dragonstone, years with Ser Willem Darry in Braavos, and wandering through the Free Cities. She, Viserys, and Illyrio travel by palanquin to Drogo's manse, where Dany sees the khal for the first time. When she begs Viserys not to make her marry Drogo, he threatens her and insists the marriage is necessary to gain an army. The chapter ends with Illyrio bringing Drogo over to meet them.

## Events & Actions
1. **Viserys presents the gown** — Viserys shows Daenerys a gift from Illyrio, a deep plum silk gown, telling her she must look like a princess tonight.
2. **Viserys instructs and threatens Dany** — He tells her to wash off the stink of the stables, pushes back her shoulders, touches her breasts, and pinches her nipple cruelly, warning her not to fail him tonight or "wake the dragon."
3. **Dany looks out her window** — After Viserys leaves, Dany looks at the bay, the black silhouettes of Pentos's towers against the setting sun, and wishes she could be among the ragged children playing outside.
4. **Dany reflects on exile** — She thinks about Westeros (a land she has never seen), Viserys's stories of their family's fall, her birth on Dragonstone during a storm, her mother's death, Ser Willem Darry and the house with the red door in Braavos, and their years of wandering through the Free Cities.
5. **Slaves bathe and dress Dany** — Illyrio's slaves fill a scalding bath; Dany does not flinch from the heat. The old woman washes her hair; the girl scrubs her back and chatters about Drogo's wealth. They anoint her with spiceflower perfume and dress her in the gown, sandals, tiara, bracelets, and golden torc.
6. **Dany sees herself in the looking glass** — She looks like a princess but notices the golden collar and feels a chill, thinking of the slave girl's comment that even Drogo's slaves wear golden collars.
7. **Viserys and Illyrio inspect Dany** — Viserys says she is too skinny; Illyrio pronounces her a vision and says Drogo will be enraptured. Illyrio assures them she is old enough since she has had her blood.
8. **Travel by palanquin** — They ride through pitch-dark streets in Illyrio's palanquin. Viserys fantasizes about sweeping the Seven Kingdoms with ten thousand Dothraki and names houses he believes will support him. Illyrio flatters him about support in Westeros. Viserys vows to kill the Usurper and the Kingslayer.
9. **Arrival at Drogo's manse** — An Unsullied guard stops the palanquin and inspects them before waving them through. Viserys mutters about the "insolent eunuch."
10. **Entry into the feast** — A eunuch announces them with full Targaryen titles. They enter a pillared courtyard with Dothraki horselords, sellswords, a red priest, men from Ibben, and lords from the Summer Isles. Dany realizes she is the only woman present.
11. **Illyrio identifies guests** — He points out Drogo's three bloodriders, Khal Moro and his son Rhogoro, the brother of the Archon of Tyrosh (green-bearded), and Ser Jorah Mormont.
12. **Dany notices Ser Jorah** — She is surprised to find a knight here. Illyrio explains he fled after selling poachers to a slaver. Viserys wants to speak with him. Dany studies Jorah curiously.
13. **Dany sees Khal Drogo** — Illyrio points out the khal. Dany observes his height, grace, copper skin, onyx eyes, long uncut braid with bells, and finds him frightening.
14. **Dany begs Viserys not to marry her to Drogo** — She says she wants to go home. Viserys furiously tells her "home" requires an army, and he would let Drogo's entire khalasar have her if necessary.
15. **Illyrio brings Drogo to meet them** — Dany brushes away tears. Viserys nervously tells her to smile and stand up straight. Daenerys smiles and stands up straight.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear dominates — fear of the gown's beauty and what it represents, fear of Khal Drogo, fear of Viserys's anger. Also wistfulness, longing for a normal childhood, and deep homesickness for the house with the red door. She is resigned and compliant by chapter's end.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The impending presentation to Khal Drogo and what it means for her future. Underneath, a longing for the house with the red door in Braavos — the only home she remembers.
- **Key decisions made:** None — Dany is entirely passive in this chapter, carried along by Viserys's and Illyrio's plans. Her one act of resistance (begging Viserys not to make her marry Drogo) is immediately crushed.
- **Self-deception flags:** None apparent. Dany is notably clear-eyed: she distrusts Illyrio's motives, recognizes Viserys's delusions, knows the slaves are slaves despite Pentos's laws, and understands her own powerlessness.
