- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion Lannister finishes a night of reading in Winterfell's library and descends to the yard, where he overhears Joffrey and Sandor Clegane discussing Bran's lingering death and the howling direwolf. Tyrion slaps Joffrey twice and orders him to pay his respects to the Starks. He then joins Jaime, Cersei, and the royal children for breakfast, where he reports that Bran may survive but will never walk again. Cersei and Jaime exchange a meaningful glance at the news. Tyrion announces his intention to travel to the Wall with Benjen Stark and Jon Snow rather than return south with the royal party.

## Events & Actions
1. **Tyrion reads through the night in Winterfell's library** — He has been reading all night, including a hundred-year-old discourse on the seasons. His reading lamp runs low on oil as dawn arrives.
2. **Direwolf howling** — A wolf howls over Winterfell, the sound "like a flag of mourning." The howl recurs, prompting Tyrion to close his book.
3. **Tyrion wakes Septon Chayle** — He instructs the septon to return books to shelves, be gentle with the Valyrian scrolls, and take care with the rare copy of Ayrmidon's Engines of War.
4. **Tyrion descends the library tower** — He exits and descends the steep, corkscrewing exterior steps, struggling with the high, narrow steps and his short, twisted legs.
5. **Tyrion overhears Joffrey and the Hound** — From above, he hears Sandor say Bran is "a long time dying" and offer to kill the direwolf. Joffrey replies "Send a dog to kill a dog!" and says the Starks would never miss one wolf.
6. **Tyrion rebukes Joffrey** — He enters the yard and corrects Joffrey, noting the Starks can count past six, unlike some princes.
7. **The Hound mocks Tyrion's height** — Sandor pretends not to see Tyrion, calling him "spirits of the air" and then looking down to notice "the little lord Tyrion." Joffrey laughs.
8. **Tyrion slaps Joffrey twice** — After Joffrey refuses to offer condolences to the Starks, calling Bran "nothing to me," Tyrion slaps him. When Joffrey threatens to tell his mother, Tyrion slaps him again.
9. **Tyrion orders Joffrey to the Starks** — He instructs Joffrey to go to Lord and Lady Stark, kneel, express sorrow, and offer service. Joffrey flees the yard holding his cheek.
10. **The Hound warns Tyrion** — Sandor tells Tyrion that "the prince will remember that." Tyrion replies, "I pray he does."
11. **Tyrion asks after Jaime** — The Hound tells him Jaime is breaking fast with the queen.
12. **Tyrion joins breakfast in the Guest House** — He seats himself uninvited at the table with Jaime, Cersei, Tommen, and Myrcella. A cold, cheerless meal is laid out.
13. **Tyrion reports on Bran's condition** — He tells the family he visited the sickroom last night; the maester thinks Bran may survive but will never walk again. His back is broken, his legs shattered, and he is kept alive with honey and water.
14. **Jaime and Cersei exchange a glance** — Upon hearing Bran may live, Jaime and Cersei share a brief, significant look. Cersei then drops her gaze and says it is "no mercy" to let the child linger.
15. **Tyrion describes the direwolf's vigil** — He tells the family that Bran's direwolf stays outside his window howling day and night; when the window was closed, Bran weakened, and when reopened, his heart beat stronger.
16. **Cersei denounces the direwolves** — She calls them "unnatural" and "dangerous," refusing to have any go south. Jaime notes they follow "those girls" everywhere.
17. **Tyrion announces his Wall plan** — He says he will travel north with Benjen Stark and Jon Snow to see the Wall. Jaime jokes about taking the black. Tyrion says he wants to "stand on top of the Wall and piss off the edge of the world."
18. **Cersei departs with the children** — She declares the children don't need to hear "this filth" and leaves with Tommen and Myrcella.
19. **Jaime and Tyrion discuss Bran's fate** — Jaime says Eddard will never leave while Bran lingers; Tyrion says Robert will command it. Jaime says he would give a crippled son "a good clean death." Tyrion replies that "death is so terribly final, while life is full of possibilities."
20. **Tyrion provokes Jaime** — He says he hopes Bran wakes because he'd be "most interested to hear what he might have to say." Jaime's smile curdles and he warns Tyrion there are times he wonders whose side Tyrion is on. Tyrion grins and says, "You know how much I love my family."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Wry detachment mixed with genuine concern for Bran; disgust at Joffrey's callousness; affection for Jaime tempered by suspicion; habitual self-deprecation about his physical form
- **Primary preoccupation:** Bran Stark's survival and its implications; the behavior and reactions of his siblings
- **Key decisions made:** To slap Joffrey and force him to pay condolences to the Starks; to travel to the Wall with Benjen and Jon Snow rather than return south; to deliberately provoke Jaime by expressing hope that Bran wakes and speaks
- **Self-deception flags:** None obvious — Tyrion appears unusually self-aware in this chapter, openly acknowledging his physical limitations and deliberately testing his siblings' reactions
