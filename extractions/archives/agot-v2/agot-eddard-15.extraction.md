# AGOT — Eddard XV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 59
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard XV
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard XV
- **location_primary:** Black cells beneath the Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Harrenhal (recalled), Winterfell crypts (recalled), the Eyrie (recalled), Tower of Joy (implied in memory)
- **approximate_timeline:** Days to weeks after Ned's arrest in the throne room; he has been imprisoned long enough to lose track of time, develop fever, and suffer starvation
- **time_markers:** No sun or moon visible; Ned cannot mark days; he counts water deliveries as days passing; "hours turned to days, or so it seemed"; he was "half-asleep" when Varys arrived; Varys says the queen will visit "on the morrow"; Ned recalls being "eighteen again" at the tourney at Harrenhal during "the year of false spring"; Sansa came to court "a few days ago" to plead for Ned
- **chapter_summary:** Eddard Stark lies imprisoned in the black cells beneath the Red Keep, injured, feverish, and starving in total darkness. He reflects on his failures, blames himself for trusting Littlefinger and others, and hallucinates or dreams of Robert Baratheon. He relives a vivid memory of the tourney at Harrenhal — Rhaegar crowning Lyanna with winter roses — and wakes recalling Lyanna's deathbed words. Varys visits disguised as a turnkey, reveals that Arya has escaped, that Tyrion has been lost by Catelyn, that Robb marches south with a northern army, and that Cersei will offer Ned his life if he publicly confesses treason and takes the black. When Ned refuses to give false confession, Varys leverages Sansa's safety to compel his cooperation.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned imprisoned in darkness** — Eddard Stark lies in the black cells beneath the Red Keep in absolute darkness, his shattered leg throbbing, remembering being shoved into the cell.
2. **Ned blames himself** — He damns Littlefinger, Janos Slynt, the gold cloaks, Cersei, Jaime, Pycelle, Varys, Barristan, and Renly, but ultimately blames himself, calling himself a "thrice-damned blind fool."
3. **Ned recalls Cersei's warning** — He envisions Cersei's face and remembers her words: "When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die."
4. **Ned thinks of his daughters** — He would weep for his daughters but cannot; his grief and rage freeze inside him as a Stark of Winterfell.
5. **Time passes in darkness** — Ned sleeps and wakes repeatedly, unable to distinguish the two; he dreams of blood and broken promises; his waking thoughts are worse than nightmares.
6. **Ned makes plans to sustain hope** — He talks aloud to hear a voice; he counts on Robert's brothers raising armies, his household guard returning, and Catelyn raising the north.
7. **Ned dreams/hallucinates Robert** — He sees Robert as a young man, then Robert speaks to him; Robert's face cracks and becomes Littlefinger's face, grinning and mocking, with lies turning to pale grey moths.
8. **Gaoler brings water** — A rat-faced gaoler in mail and leather brings a clay water jug; Ned drinks desperately and asks about his daughters; the gaoler refuses to speak and takes the jug back.
9. **Ned's sense of smell fades** — The straw no longer smells; he can no longer distinguish waking from sleeping.
10. **Ned recalls the tourney at Harrenhal** — A vivid memory: Jaime Lannister's Kingsguard investiture, Rhaegar Targaryen winning the jousting in black ruby-studded armor, Brandon and Bronze Yohn Royce and Ser Arthur Dayne all unhorsed.
11. **Rhaegar crowns Lyanna** — Ned recalls the moment all smiles died when Rhaegar rode past his wife Elia Martell to lay the crown of winter roses in Lyanna's lap.
12. **Ned wakes from the memory** — He reaches for the crown of roses but thorns cut his skin; he wakes trembling, recalling Lyanna's deathbed words: "Promise me, Ned." She had loved winter roses.
13. **Ned weeps and cries out** — He says "Gods save me, I am going mad."
14. **Continued starvation** — Each water delivery marks a day; Ned begs for word of his daughters, then for food; he is not fed; stomach cramps begin.
15. **Ned reasons about his survival** — He concludes Cersei wants him alive because Catelyn holds her brother; he will not be killed while Tyrion is a hostage.
16. **Varys arrives disguised** — A second gaoler appears, stouter and shorter, offering wine; Ned recognizes Varys's voice and touches his false-stubbled face.
17. **Varys shares wine and information** — Varys drinks first to prove the wine is safe; they share the wineskin.
18. **Varys reveals Arya's escape** — The younger girl escaped Ser Meryn and fled; neither Varys nor the Lannisters can find her.
19. **Varys reveals Sansa's situation** — Sansa is still betrothed to Joffrey; Cersei keeps her close; Sansa came to court to plead for Ned's life.
20. **Varys tells Ned he is a dead man** — He states bluntly that Ned should realize his situation.
21. **Varys reveals Tyrion's escape** — Catelyn held "the wrong brother" and let the Imp slip; Varys expects Tyrion is dead in the Mountains of the Moon.
22. **Ned confronts Varys about inaction during the arrest** — Varys says he was unarmed and surrounded by Lannister swords; he explains his philosophy of playing a role at court.
23. **Ned asks to be freed** — Varys says he could but won't, as questions would lead back to him.
24. **Ned asks Varys to carry a message** — Varys agrees to provide paper and ink but says he will read the letter and deliver it or not as serves his own ends.
25. **Varys reveals his goal is peace** — He says he was the one person desperate to keep Robert alive; he protected Robert for fifteen years from enemies but not from friends.
26. **Varys reveals Lancel's role in Robert's death** — Cersei gave Lancel the wineskins with Robert's favorite vintage; Varys says it was Ned's mercy (warning Cersei) that killed the king, not the wine.
27. **Varys describes the political situation** — Jaime fights the river lords; Lysa Arryn sits in the Eyrie; the Martells brood in Dorne; Robb marches south with a northern host; Stannis gathers forces on Dragonstone and is Cersei's greatest fear.
28. **Ned declares Stannis the true heir** — He says the throne is Stannis's by rights.
29. **Varys presents Cersei's offer** — Ned must confess treason, command Robb to stand down, proclaim Joffrey true heir, denounce Stannis and Renly; in exchange he may take the black and live at the Wall.
30. **Ned refuses** — He says his word would be hollow; his life is not so precious.
31. **Varys threatens Sansa** — He asks how precious Ned's daughter's life is; invokes the fate of Rhaenys Targaryen and her kitten Balerion; says the next visitor will bring bread and cheese and milk of the poppy — or Sansa's head. The choice is Ned's.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Despair, self-blame, grief, shame; oscillates between desperate hope (counting on allies) and crushing guilt; weeps when recalling Lyanna; feverish delirium blurs memory and hallucination; rage frozen inside him "as a Stark of Winterfell"
- **Primary preoccupation:** His daughters' safety (Sansa and Arya); his own failure and responsibility for Robert's death; the memory of Lyanna and his promise to her; Jon Snow (thought of with shame and deep sorrow)
- **Key decisions made:** Ned initially refuses to give a false confession even to save his own life — but Varys's threat to Sansa's life leaves the decision open at chapter's end
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned's hope that allies will rescue him (Robert's brothers, his household guard, Catelyn raising the north) is presented as castle-building — "built castles of hope in the dark"; his belief that Cersei won't kill him because Catelyn holds Tyrion is revealed as wrong by Varys (Catelyn lost Tyrion, and it was the wrong brother anyway)
