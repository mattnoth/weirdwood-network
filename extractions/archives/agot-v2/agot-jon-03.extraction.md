- **approximate_timeline:** Jon has been at Castle Black for several weeks; his name day has passed a fortnight ago; Benjen Stark departed on a ranging three days after their arrival and has not returned
- **time_markers:** "dawn the next day" (when Jon watched Benjen leave — recalled event); "almost midday" when Jon leaves the armory; "this morning" when the bird arrived from Winterfell; Jon's name day came and went "a fortnight past"; Benjen left "three days after their arrival"; Jon states "I'll be fifteen on my name day" (recalled dialogue)
- **chapter_summary:** Jon trains in the yard at Castle Black, easily defeating Grenn under Ser Alliser Thorne's contemptuous supervision. After the session, Grenn and three other recruits confront Jon in the armory, and the armorer Donal Noye breaks up the fight. Noye forces Jon to recognize that his castle-bred training gives him an unfair advantage over the other recruits, calling him a bully. Jon walks outside, encounters Tyrion Lannister, and they share a meal of stew in the common hall. Ser Alliser summons Jon to the Lord Commander; Jeor Mormont gives him a letter from Robb reporting that Bran has woken from his coma. Elated, Jon returns to the hall, offers to help train Grenn, and makes a joke at Ser Alliser's expense that earns him the laughter of the hall — and Thorne's enmity.

- **Notable sensory details:** Sweat trickling "icily" down Jon's chest under armor; Jon shivering in the armory despite coal braziers; the weight of all the ice pressing down on Jon when he looks up at the Wall; taste of mockery in Tyrion's words

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon defeats Grenn in training** — In the courtyard, Jon overpowers Grenn with superior swordwork, hamstringing him, denting his helm, and knocking the sword from his hand.
2. **Ser Alliser mocks the recruits** — Thorne dismisses Grenn as fit only for stableboy duty and insults the entire group's fighting ability.
3. **Jon reflects on his isolation** — In the armory, Jon changes clothes and thinks about how cold and lonely Castle Black is compared to Winterfell.
4. **Jon recalls Benjen's refusal** — Three days after arriving, Jon asked to join Benjen's ranging and was refused; Benjen told him he was still a boy and must earn his place.
5. **Jon watches Benjen depart** — At dawn, Jon watched his uncle lead his horse into the tunnel; Benjen had no smile for him.
6. **Jon imagines Benjen dead** — After Benjen's departure, Jon envisioned his uncle lying dead in the snow and was disturbed by the thought.
7. **Jon misses his siblings** — He recalls Rickon, Robb, Bran, Sansa, and Arya with deep affection, missing Arya most after Robb.
8. **Grenn and three others confront Jon in the armory** — Grenn, Toad, and two rapers from the Fingers threaten Jon; Toad insults Jon's mother.
9. **Jon fights back** — Jon breaks free, attacks Toad, slams his head against the ground; the rapers pull him off and Grenn kicks him.
10. **Donal Noye breaks up the fight** — The armorer orders them to stop, sends Grenn to Maester Aemon and Toad to have his head looked at, keeps Jon behind.
11. **Noye lectures Jon** — The armorer tells Jon he is a bully who shames untrained boys with his castle-bred advantages, and warns his "brothers" may slit his throat.
12. **Jon encounters Tyrion outside** — Tyrion surprises Jon while he gazes at the Wall; they discuss what lies beyond.
13. **Tyrion advises Jon on mockery** — Tyrion counsels Jon to own the name "Lord Snow" so it cannot hurt him.
14. **Jon and Tyrion share a meal** — They eat stew in the common hall; Jon sits far from the other recruits.
15. **Tyrion mentions Benjen is overdue** — Tyrion notes the talk that Benjen has been away too long; Jon reveals Benjen was searching for Ser Waymar Royce.
16. **Ser Alliser summons Jon to the Lord Commander** — Thorne tells Jon the Lord Commander wants him; a bird arrived from Winterfell about "his brother."
17. **Tyrion intervenes with Thorne** — Tyrion threatens to use his court influence if Thorne doesn't explain, forcing Alliser to reveal it's about Bran.
18. **Jon races to the Commander's Keep** — Jon runs through the snow, takes the stairs two at a time, arrives wild-eyed and panting.
19. **Mormont gives Jon the letter** — The Lord Commander hands Jon a letter sealed with a direwolf in white wax; Jon reads that Bran has woken, though he is crippled.
20. **Jon celebrates** — Jon runs back to the common hall, lifts Tyrion and spins him, announces Bran will live.
21. **Jon offers to help train Grenn** — Jon apologizes for the wrist injury and offers to teach Grenn how to defend the move.
22. **Jon jokes at Ser Alliser's expense** — When Thorne sneers about teaching an aurochs, Jon quips he'd love to see Ghost juggle; the hall erupts in laughter.
23. **Ser Alliser threatens Jon** — Thorne's face darkens and he tells Jon it was "a grievous error."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Shifts dramatically through the chapter — proud after victories, lonely and cold in isolation, ashamed after Noye's lecture, awed by the Wall, fearful when summoned by Mormont, euphoric upon learning Bran lives, generous and open at the end
- **Primary preoccupation:** His isolation at Castle Black; missing his family (especially Arya and Robb); his unknown mother; Benjen's absence and overdue return
- **Key decisions made:** After Noye's lecture and Bran's news, Jon decides to help train Grenn rather than continue shaming the other recruits — a fundamental shift from contempt to generosity
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon believes "They hate me because I'm better than they are" — Noye corrects this to "They hate you because you act like you're better than they are"; Jon feels the accusation of being a bully is unjust, but begins to accept it; Jon stubbornly insists his mother was not a whore while admitting he knows nothing about her
