# AGOT — Sansa II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 30
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Sansa II
- **first_available:** AGOT Sansa II
- **location_primary:** Tourney grounds outside King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Riverside feast grounds, King's Landing streets, Red Keep
- **approximate_timeline:** During the Hand's tourney, day one of jousting through the evening feast and return to the castle
- **time_markers:** Jousting lasts "all day and into the dusk"; moon is "well up" when the king ends the last matches; feast goes into the night; Septa Mordane falls asleep at the table; the walk back is in darkness with torchlight
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa attends the Hand's tourney with Septa Mordane and Jeyne Poole, watching a full day of jousting that includes Ser Gregor Clegane killing a young knight from the Vale. The field narrows to four semifinalists: the Hound, Gregor, Jaime Lannister, and Ser Loras Tyrell, who gives Sansa a red rose. Petyr Baelish introduces himself to Sansa, remarking on her resemblance to her mother. At the evening feast, Joffrey is charming and attentive to Sansa until King Robert drunkenly quarrels with Queen Cersei and the evening disintegrates. Sandor Clegane escorts Sansa back to the Red Keep and tells her the true story of how his brother burned his face, threatening to kill her if she ever repeats it.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa rides to tourney** — Sansa travels to the Hand's tourney in a litter with yellow silk curtains alongside Septa Mordane and Jeyne Poole.
2. **Jousting begins** — Knights enter the lists, including the Kingsguard, Jaime Lannister, Gregor Clegane, various lords and knights from across the realm.
3. **Winterfell riders compete** — Jory unhorses Horas Redwyne and a Frey but loses to Lothor Brune; Harwin is unhorsed by Ser Meryn; Alyn falls to Ser Balon Swann.
4. **Gregor kills a young knight** — Ser Gregor's lance rides up under a young Vale knight's improperly fastened gorget, driving through his throat and killing him instantly. He dies ten feet from Sansa.
5. **Sansa's reaction to the death** — Sansa watches with "strange fascination" rather than crying; reflects that she may have used up her tears for Lady and Bran. Jeyne weeps hysterically and is taken away by Septa Mordane.
6. **Body removed and jousting resumes** — A boy shovels dirt over the blood and the tourney continues.
7. **Renly unhorsed by the Hound** — Renly flies off his horse; golden antler on his helm snaps. He gives the broken tine to the Hound, who tosses it to the crowd. Renly walks among the commons to restore order.
8. **Hedge knight disgraced** — A hedge knight in a checkered cloak kills Beric Dondarrion's horse and is declared forfeit.
9. **Field narrows to four** — The Hound, Gregor, Jaime, and Ser Loras Tyrell remain.
10. **Ser Loras gives Sansa a red rose** — After defeating Ser Robar Royce, Loras rides to Sansa and gives her a red rose (white roses for all other maidens), calling her "sweet lady" and saying no victory is half so beautiful as her.
11. **Petyr Baelish approaches Sansa** — Littlefinger tells Sansa she has "the Tully look," says her mother was his queen of beauty once, touches her hair, and abruptly walks away.
12. **King decrees final matches for next morning** — With the moon up and crowd tired, Robert postpones the last three matches to before the melee.
13. **Feast begins** — The court moves to the riverside for an elaborate feast with aurochs, multiple courses, entertainment.
14. **Joffrey is charming to Sansa** — He sits beside her, compliments her, talks all night, shows her how to eat snails, serves her food, pours her wine.
15. **King Robert quarrels with Cersei** — Robert, very drunk, shouts that he will fight in the melee tomorrow. Cersei storms off in silence. Robert shoves Jaime to the ground and mocks him, referencing the Kingslayer name and his own former prowess with the hammer.
16. **Joffrey sends Sansa away with the Hound** — Joffrey abruptly tells Sansa it grows late, then calls Sandor to escort her, leaving without a farewell.
17. **The Hound escorts Sansa** — Sandor leads Sansa through the dark tourney grounds back toward King's Landing.
18. **Sandor tells the story of his burns** — He stops in a dark field, forces Sansa to look at his face, and tells her how Gregor burned him with a brazier over a stolen wooden knight toy when Sandor was six or seven. He reveals his father lied about the cause, and that Gregor was later knighted by Rhaegar Targaryen.
19. **Sansa reaches out to Sandor** — Sansa's fear turns to sadness; she touches his shoulder and whispers "He was no true knight." Sandor agrees.
20. **Return to the Red Keep** — They ride in silence through the King's Gate and up to Sansa's bedchamber corridor.
21. **Sandor threatens Sansa** — He warns her that if she ever tells anyone what he told her tonight, he will kill her.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Enchanted and dazzled by the tourney's splendor; unsettled by the young knight's death but strangely fascinated rather than weeping; anxious about Joffrey's attitude toward her, then swept up in happiness when he is charming; terrified of the Hound during the walk home, then shifting to sadness and compassion for him
- **Primary preoccupation:** The beauty and romance of the tourney and feast; her relationship with Joffrey; performing as a proper lady
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses not to cry at the young knight's death, believing a great lady should show composure; decides to blame Arya rather than Joffrey for Lady's death; promises the Hound she will keep his secret
- **Self-deception flags:** Sansa tells herself it was "not Joffrey's doing, not truly" regarding Lady's death, blaming the queen and Arya instead; she convinces herself she cannot hate Joffrey because "he was too beautiful to hate"; she frames her lack of tears at the death as proper ladylike composure rather than examining what it might mean
