- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion rides north toward the Wall with Benjen Stark, Jon Snow, Ghost, his own two men, and Yoren with two recruits picked up at a forest holdfast. On the eighteenth night, Tyrion reads about dragons by a stream and reflects on the dragon skulls he once visited beneath the Red Keep. Jon Snow approaches and asks why he reads so much; Tyrion delivers a candid speech about his dwarfism, his mind as his weapon, and his childhood dreams of dragons. When Tyrion provokes Jon about the reality of the Night's Watch, Ghost knocks Tyrion down, and Jon helps him up. They share wine and return to camp, where the party eats squirrel stew with black bread and hard cheese. Jon takes the first watch, staring into the flames.

## Events & Actions
1. **Departure from Winterfell** — Tyrion recalls leaving Winterfell on the same day as the king's party, riding north with Benjen Stark and Jon Snow while the royal party turned south.
2. **Journey north along the kingsroad** — The party rides north through increasingly wild and cold terrain; farmland gives way to dense wolfswood; hills become mountains.
3. **Yoren joins the party** — At a forest holdfast at the edge of the wolfswood, the party is joined by Yoren of the Night's Watch and his two peasant boy recruits from the Fingers (rapers).
4. **Jon observes Yoren and recruits** — Tyrion notices Jon watching Yoren and his companions with an expression "uncomfortably like dismay."
5. **Tyrion reads by the stream** — On the eighteenth night, Tyrion takes his bearskin, wineskin, and dragon book to a spot by a stream under an ancient oak, away from camp.
6. **Tyrion recalls dragon skulls** — While reading about dragonbone properties, Tyrion remembers visiting the nineteen dragon skulls in the Red Keep cellar during his first visit to King's Landing for Cersei's wedding.
7. **Tyrion recalls the Field of Fire** — Tyrion reflects on his ancestor King Loren's defeat by Aegon and his three dragons.
8. **Jon approaches Tyrion** — Jon finds Tyrion reading and asks why he reads so much.
9. **Tyrion's speech about his mind as his weapon** — Tyrion explains his dwarfism, his limitations, and why he reads: "My mind is my weapon... a mind needs books as a sword needs a whetstone."
10. **Tyrion reveals his childhood dragon dreams** — Tyrion tells Jon he dreamed of having a dragon and used to start fires in Casterly Rock imagining his father and sister burning.
11. **Tyrion provokes Jon about the Night's Watch** — Tyrion tells Jon the Watch is "a midden heap for all the misfits of the realm," provoking Jon to anger and near-tears.
12. **Ghost attacks Tyrion** — When Tyrion steps toward Jon (intending comfort), Ghost appears silently and knocks Tyrion flat on his back; Tyrion wrenches his back.
13. **Ghost stands between them** — Ghost shows teeth silently, blocking Tyrion from approaching Jon; Tyrion sags to the ground.
14. **Jon helps Tyrion up** — After Tyrion asks nicely, Jon commands Ghost down, lifts Tyrion to his feet, and returns his book.
15. **They share wine and reconcile** — Jon asks if what Tyrion said about the Night's Watch is true; Tyrion confirms it; Jon accepts: "If that's what it is, that's what it is." Tyrion praises him: "Most men would rather deny a hard truth than face it."
16. **Return to camp** — Tyrion refuses Jon's help over rough ground; they return to camp where stew is cooking.
17. **Grumkins joke** — Tyrion tells Benjen "it was the grumkins," making Jon smile; Benjen is baffled.
18. **Supper** — The party eats squirrel stew with black bread and hard cheese; Tyrion shares his wine.
19. **Party retires** — The company drifts off to sleep; Jon draws first watch; Tyrion is last to retire, pauses to look at Jon standing near the fire staring into the flames, then smiles sadly and goes to bed.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Complex mixture — physically uncomfortable (cold, cramping, raw thighs), intellectually stimulated (reading about dragons), nostalgic (childhood dragon dreams), guilty (after provoking Jon), humiliated (knocked down by Ghost, unable to rise), wryly amused (grumkins joke), and sad (final moment watching Jon stare into the flames). A persistent undercurrent of determination to not show weakness.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Dragons — their history, their lost magnificence, his childhood dreams of them; also observing and testing Jon Snow's character.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides not to complain about the hard riding (won't give Benjen satisfaction); accepts the bearskin fur deliberately; chooses to provoke Jon about the Night's Watch's reality; crushes his anger when humiliated by Ghost rather than escalating; refuses Jon's help over the roots on the way back to camp.
- **Self-deception flags:** None apparent — Tyrion presents himself as unusually self-aware: "I have a realistic grasp of my own strengths and weaknesses." His narration is consistent with this self-assessment. The statement "I seldom even dream of dragons anymore" may contain more wistfulness than he acknowledges.
