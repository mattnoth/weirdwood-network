# AGOT — Eddard XIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 48
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard XIII
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard XIII
- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Maegor's Holdfast (royal bedchamber), Winterfell crypts (dream)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Robert returns from his hunt in the kingswood; deep into Robert's reign as king, near the end of Ned's time as Hand
- **time_markers:** Deep night (room "black as pitch" when Ned is woken); moonlight on the ramparts ("the moon hung low over the walls, ripening toward full"); events span from the middle of the night through to before break of day; Robert took two days to be brought back from the hunt; the Wind Witch sails on the evening tide; Ned instructs Cayn to bring Littlefinger "before break of day"
- **chapter_summary:** Ned is woken from a dream of Winterfell's crypts and Lyanna's statue to be summoned to the dying King Robert, who was gored by a boar during his hunt. Robert dictates his will naming Ned as Lord Regent and Protector of the Realm, and Ned secretly substitutes "my heir" for "my son Joffrey." Robert asks Ned to countermand the assassination of Daenerys Targaryen and to care for his children. After Robert is given milk of the poppy, Ned refuses Lord Renly's proposal to seize the royal children by force, then summons Littlefinger and reveals the secret of Joffrey's parentage. Ned rejects Littlefinger's scheme to keep Joffrey on the throne and instead declares Stannis the rightful heir, arranging for the City Watch to be bought and a letter sent to Stannis at Dragonstone.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned dreams of the Winterfell crypts** — He walks past the Kings of Winter to find his father, Brandon, and Lyanna. Lyanna's statue whispers "Promise me, Ned" while wearing blue roses and weeping blood from her eyes.
2. **Ned is woken and summoned to the king** — The royal steward, accompanied by Tomard and Cayn, hammers on his door with orders to come at once. Ned dresses with Cayn's help, including the Valyrian dagger.
3. **Ned crosses to Maegor's Holdfast** — He is escorted across the inner bailey and over the drawbridge, passing three Kingsguard (Ser Boros Blount, Ser Preston Greenfield, Ser Barristan Selmy).
4. **Ned sees the dying Robert** — The king is in his bedchamber with Cersei, Renly, Pycelle, and servants. Robert has been gored from groin to nipple by a boar. His wound is bandaged but already black with blood.
5. **Robert recounts the hunt** — He blames too much wine, says he missed his thrust, but drove a knife through the boar's eye. Renly confirms they brought the carcass back.
6. **Robert clears the room** — He dismisses Cersei, Renly, Pycelle, and the servants. Robert refuses the milk of the poppy, knocking the cup from Pycelle's hand.
7. **Robert dictates his will** — He commands Ned to write: Ned is named Lord Regent and Protector of the Realm until "my son Joffrey" comes of age. Ned secretly writes "my heir" instead of "my son Joffrey."
8. **Robert expresses regret about Daenerys** — He tells Ned she is "only a child," that Ned was right, and asks Ned to stop the assassination.
9. **Robert asks Ned to help his son** — He says "help my son, Ned. Make him be … better than me."
10. **Robert says "Promise me, Ned"** — He asks Ned to serve the boar at his funeral feast. Ned promises, and internally echoes Lyanna's voice saying the same words.
11. **Robert asks Ned to give Lyanna his love** — He says he will see Lyanna in death and asks Ned to take care of his children.
12. **Ned pledges to guard Robert's children** — Ned thinks of the bastards (Barra, Mya, Gendry) and carefully says he will guard Robert's children "as if they were my own."
13. **The will is sealed** — Robert presses his seal into golden wax on the letter, witnessed by Renly and Pycelle.
14. **Robert drinks milk of the poppy and sleeps** — This time he accepts and drinks deeply. He asks if he will dream, and Ned says yes.
15. **Pycelle reports the wound is mortal** — He says it took two days to bring Robert back and the wound has mortified; only the gods can heal him now.
16. **Renly marvels at Robert's strength** — He notes Robert slew the boar even with his entrails sliding from his belly.
17. **Ned instructs Ser Barristan** — He tells the knight to let no one disturb Robert's rest without Ned's leave. Ser Barristan expresses guilt over failing to protect the king.
18. **Varys appears and questions the wine** — He asks who gave the king wine during the hunt. Ser Barristan identifies Lancel Lannister as the squire who kept Robert supplied.
19. **Ned orders Varys to stop the Daenerys assassination** — Varys says it may be too late but he will try.
20. **Renly proposes a coup** — On the bridge, Renly offers Ned a hundred swords and urges him to seize Joffrey, Myrcella, and Tommen from Cersei immediately.
21. **Ned refuses Renly's plan** — He says he will not shed blood in Robert's halls or drag children from their beds while Robert still lives.
22. **Ned reflects and considers his position** — Back in his chambers, he recalls Cersei's words about the game of thrones and wonders if he was right to refuse Renly.
23. **Ned arranges the escort for his daughters** — He assigns Tomard to command twenty men on the Wind Witch, sailing on the evening tide, replacing Porther.
24. **Ned gives Tomard a letter for Stannis** — The ship will pass near Dragonstone; Tomard is to deliver the letter to Lord Stannis personally.
25. **Ned writes the letter to Stannis** — He informs Stannis of Robert's impending death and urges him to sail for King's Landing with all his power before the Lannisters can march.
26. **Ned seals the letter with the direwolf seal** — He reflects that his regency will be short and longs for home.
27. **Littlefinger is brought to Ned** — Cayn and Desmond bring him. Littlefinger already knows about the regency via Varys.
28. **Ned reveals Joffrey's parentage to Littlefinger** — He tells Littlefinger that Joffrey and Tommen are Jaime Lannister's bastards from incest with Cersei.
29. **Littlefinger proposes keeping Joffrey** — He argues Stannis would bring war, and suggests Ned rule as regent, make peace with the Lannisters, arrange marriages, and eventually put Renly on the throne if Joffrey proves troublesome.
30. **Ned rejects Littlefinger's plan as treason** — He declares Stannis is the heir and reminds Littlefinger of Jon Arryn's murder, Jory Cassel's death, and the dagger used to attack Bran.
31. **Ned asks Littlefinger to buy the City Watch** — He says he needs the two-thousand-strong gold cloaks to counter the queen's forces.
32. **Littlefinger agrees to bribe Janos Slynt** — He proposes six thousand gold pieces: a third for the Commander, a third for the officers, a third for the men. He offers Ned the dagger back, hilt first.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief-stricken over Robert's dying; anger at the situation; heartsick and weary; torn between honor and pragmatism; disgusted by Littlefinger's proposals; longing for home and family
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing the succession for Stannis while protecting his family; managing the immediate crisis of Robert's death; countering the Lannister threat
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Substitutes "my heir" for "my son Joffrey" in Robert's will; (2) Refuses Renly's proposal to seize the royal children; (3) Declares Stannis the rightful heir to Littlefinger; (4) Rejects Littlefinger's scheme to keep Joffrey; (5) Arranges to send his daughters away on the Wind Witch; (6) Writes to Stannis urging him to come with all his power; (7) Agrees to buy the City Watch through Littlefinger
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned feels "soiled" by writing "my heir" instead of "my son Joffrey" but tells himself "the lies we tell for love"; he wonders if he did the right thing refusing Renly but does not revisit the decision; he places his trust in Littlefinger to buy the City Watch despite deep misgivings, because he has no other option; he assumes Cersei will have the sense to flee rather than fight
