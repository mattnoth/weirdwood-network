- **chapter_summary:** Sansa attends the first court session of King Joffrey's reign. Grand Maester Pycelle reads a long list of lords commanded to swear fealty or be declared traitors, including Sansa's own family. Tywin Lannister is named Hand of the King, Cersei is seated on the small council, and Janos Slynt is elevated to lord with the seat of Harrenhal. Ser Barristan Selmy is dismissed from the Kingsguard and replaced as Lord Commander by Jaime Lannister, while Sandor Clegane is named to fill the seventh Kingsguard slot despite refusing to take knight's vows. Sansa steps forward to plead for mercy for her father; Joffrey agrees on the condition that Eddard confess his crimes and acknowledge Joffrey as king.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa enters the throne room** — Ser Mandon Moore takes his place; Sansa enters unguarded for once, observing the stripped walls and sparse attendance.
2. **Sansa attempts to mingle with courtiers** — She recognizes Jalabhar Xho, Ser Aron Santagar, the Redwyne twins, Lord Gyles, and Ser Dontos, but all avoid her or pretend not to see her.
3. **Small council members arrive** — Pycelle is already seated and appears asleep; Varys hurries in silently; Littlefinger enters smiling and chats with Ser Balon and Ser Dontos.
4. **Joffrey and Cersei enter in procession** — Ser Barristan leads the way; Ser Arys escorts Cersei; Ser Boros walks beside Joffrey. Six of seven Kingsguard are present (Jaime absent). Joffrey takes the Iron Throne.
5. **Pycelle reads the list of those commanded to swear fealty** — A long list of lords and ladies across the realm, including the Starks (Catelyn, Robb, Bran, Rickon, Arya), Baratheon brothers, Tullys, Arryns, Tyrells, Martells, and many others. Failure to appear means being declared traitor with lands and titles forfeit.
6. **Tywin Lannister named Hand of the King** — Replacing "the traitor Eddard Stark."
7. **Cersei named to the small council** — As Queen Regent, to help Joffrey rule.
8. **Janos Slynt elevated to lord with seat of Harrenhal** — Also placed on the small council. Lords mutter angrily as this commoner takes precedence. He enters with his two sons carrying his new shield bearing a bloody spear sigil.
9. **Ser Barristan Selmy dismissed from Kingsguard** — Cersei announces his service is at an end. Janos Slynt bluntly tells him he is relieved as Lord Commander. Barristan protests that Kingsguard vows are for life.
10. **Cersei names Jaime Lannister as new Lord Commander** — Barristan calls Jaime "the Kingslayer" and "the false knight."
11. **Varys offers Barristan land near Lannisport** — Barristan spits upon their pity.
12. **Barristan strips off his armor and cloak** — Removes his white cloak, breastplate, and helmet, letting them fall to the floor. Declares he is a knight and will die a knight.
13. **Barristan threatens and defies the court** — Draws his sword; Ser Boros and Ser Meryn move to confront him but are frozen by his contempt. He boasts he could cut through all five remaining Kingsguard. He flings his sword at the foot of the Iron Throne, telling Joffrey to melt it down, and warns that Stannis may sit the throne.
14. **Barristan departs** — Walks out slowly; lords and ladies part for him; the great oak-and-bronze doors close behind him.
15. **Joffrey orders Barristan seized** — Janos Slynt volunteers his gold cloaks and leaves to carry out the order, sons in tow.
16. **Sandor Clegane named to the Kingsguard** — Filling the seventh slot. He accepts but refuses to take knight's vows. Ser Boros objects but is silenced.
17. **Sansa steps forward to plead for Eddard** — She addresses Joffrey from the foot of the Iron Throne, kneeling on Barristan's discarded cloak. She asks for mercy for her father.
18. **Council members respond to Sansa** — Pycelle calls treason a "noxious weed"; Varys calls her a "babe" who doesn't know what she asks; Littlefinger asks if she denies her father's crime.
19. **Sansa makes her case** — She argues Ned was lied to by Renly, Stannis, or others; that milk of the poppy clouded his judgment when he denied Joffrey's kingship.
20. **Cersei states terms** — If Ned confesses his crime, they would know he had repented.
21. **Joffrey conditionally grants mercy** — He agrees to show mercy if Eddard confesses and acknowledges Joffrey as king. Sansa believes he will.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Nervous, afraid, hopeful, isolated; butterflies in her stomach before proceedings; heart goes out to Barristan; heart soars when Joffrey conditionally agrees to mercy
- **Primary preoccupation:** Pleading for her father's life; maintaining composure in a hostile court
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to step forward and publicly plead for Ned's mercy despite her fear; chooses her words carefully (has practiced them "a hundred times"); decides to attribute Ned's words to milk of the poppy
- **Self-deception flags:** Repeatedly tells herself Joffrey loves her and the queen loves her too; interprets Joffrey's smile as proof of love; believes "all would be well" when Joffrey nods; convinced Ned will confess; tells herself "I must be as strong as my lady mother" while operating within a system designed to control her
