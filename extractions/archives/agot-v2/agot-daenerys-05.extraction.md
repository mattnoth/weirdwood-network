# AGOT — Daenerys V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 47
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys V
- **first_available:** AGOT Daenerys V
- **location_primary:** Vaes Dothrak
- **location_secondary:** The Womb of the World (lake), Khal Drogo's hall, the godsway
- **approximate_timeline:** During Daenerys's pregnancy; she has been with the Dothraki for some time and has learned considerable Dothraki language
- **time_markers:** "cool evening air"; ceremony occurs at night under moon and stars; torchlight; moonlight on the lake; the feast continues into the night; Dany's pregnancy described as showing (swollen breasts, curve of belly); "tender mother's stomach that had afflicted her these past two moons"; she "starved herself for a day and a night before the ceremony"
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys completes the Dothraki heart-eating ceremony, consuming a raw stallion's heart before the dosh khaleen. The crones prophesy her unborn son will be "the stallion who mounts the world," and Dany names him Rhaego. After ritual bathing in the Womb of the World, a procession leads to a great feast in Khal Drogo's hall. Viserys arrives drunk and armed with a sword — forbidden in Vaes Dothrak — demands his promised crown, and threatens Daenerys and her unborn child at swordpoint. Khal Drogo kills Viserys by pouring molten gold over his head.

## Events & Actions
1. **Heart-eating ceremony** — Khal Drogo presents a raw stallion heart to Daenerys. She eats the entire heart with teeth and nails (no steel permitted) while the dosh khaleen watch.
2. **Dany proclaims the prince** — After finishing the heart, Dany announces "Khalakka dothrae mr'anha!" (A prince rides inside me) in Dothraki. The crones and crowd affirm it is a boy.
3. **Prophecy of the stallion who mounts the world** — The one-eyed crone peers into fragrant smoke and prophesies that Dany's son will be "the stallion who mounts the world" — the khal of khals who will unite all Dothraki and conquer all peoples.
4. **Naming of Rhaego** — The crone asks what the child shall be called. Dany names him Rhaego, after her brother Rhaegar.
5. **Procession along the godsway** — Khal Drogo leads Dany from the pit along the godsway, followed by dosh khaleen, visiting khals, and the khalasar. Drums beat and bells ring.
6. **Ritual bathing in the Womb of the World** — Dany strips naked and wades into the sacred lake to cleanse herself and her unborn child while the assembled Dothraki watch.
7. **Sexual intercourse at the lakeshore** — After Dany emerges from the lake, Khal Drogo takes her publicly, lifting her in the air. He whispers "the stallion who mounts the world" and bites her throat.
8. **Ride to the feast hall** — Cohollo helps Dany onto her silver. Drogo and Dany ride down the godsway to his hall.
9. **Arrival at the feast** — Dany rides through the hall's arched entry and up the center aisle. The Dothraki hail her belly and chant about the stallion who mounts the world. Three khals sit on the high bench.
10. **Dany searches for Viserys** — Dany looks for her brother but cannot find him. She spots Ser Jorah near the middle firepit and summons him via Jhiqui.
11. **Ser Jorah reports on Viserys** — Jorah tells Dany that Viserys went to the Western Market seeking wine and hoping to recruit sellswords from caravan guards. He also reveals Viserys planned to steal the dragon's eggs until Jorah threatened to cut off his hand.
12. **Dany offers the dragon's eggs freely** — Shocked, Dany says Viserys could simply have asked and she would have given them.
13. **Dany reflects on her family** — She tells Ser Jorah that Viserys is all she has left — mother died giving birth, father and Rhaegar died before she was born. Jorah tells her she now belongs to the Dothraki.
14. **Viserys arrives drunk and armed** — Viserys enters the hall wearing a sword (forbidden in Vaes Dothrak), drunk, shouting for his sister and demanding to eat before others.
15. **Ser Jorah attempts to restrain Viserys** — Jorah goes to Viserys, whispers to him, takes his arm. Viserys wrenches free.
16. **Drogo insults Viserys** — Drogo tells Viserys his place is at the back with the lowest. He calls him "Sorefoot King" and orders a cart brought for "Khal Rhaggat" (Cart King).
17. **Viserys draws his sword** — After being knocked to the floor by Ser Jorah during their struggle, Viserys draws his borrowed longsword — an ultimate violation of Vaes Dothrak's sacred laws.
18. **Viserys threatens Daenerys** — He puts his sword point between Dany's breasts, slides it down over her belly, pricks her navel. He demands his promised crown and threatens to cut out the unborn child. He weeps and laughs simultaneously.
19. **Dany translates for Drogo** — Despite not knowing if she has enough Dothraki words, Dany tells Drogo what Viserys demands. Drogo understands.
20. **The golden crown** — Drogo's bloodriders seize Viserys: Qotho holds his arms, Haggo shatters his wrist, Cohollo takes the sword. Drogo melts his gold belt medallions in an iron pot over the firepit, then pours the molten gold over Viserys's head, killing him. No blood is shed.
21. **Dany watches without flinching** — She refuses Ser Jorah's plea to turn away. After Viserys dies, she thinks: "He was no dragon. Fire cannot kill a dragon."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined and fearful during the heart-eating ceremony (forces herself through it); pride and connection to Drogo during the ritual; shock upon learning Viserys planned to steal her eggs; protective loyalty toward Viserys despite his failings; dread when Viserys arrives armed ("a sense of dread closed around her heart"); terror when he draws the sword; calm detachment at his death ("curiously calm")

- **Key decisions made:** Names her son Rhaego after Rhaegar; offers Viserys the dragon's eggs freely; refuses to turn away from Viserys's execution; translates Viserys's demands to Drogo
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself "I am the blood of the dragon" as a mantra for courage; maintains loyalty to Viserys as "my true king" despite evidence of his weakness and cruelty; her final thought — "He was no dragon. Fire cannot kill a dragon" — represents a belief about dragons and fire that she applies to herself
