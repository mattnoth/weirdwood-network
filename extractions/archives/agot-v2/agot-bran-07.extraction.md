# AGOT — Bran VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 67
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran VII
- **first_available:** AGOT Bran VII
- **location_primary:** Winterfell
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell crypts, Maester Luwin's turret
- **approximate_timeline:** Late in AGOT; Eddard is a captive of the queen in King's Landing; Robb has marched south with the Stark forces and all the likely lads for leagues around
- **time_markers:** Morning (comet visible in the morning sky; Luwin measuring shadows); "last night" (Bran's crow dream and Rickon's dream); Rickon states he is four years old; Bran recalls holding a wooden sword "when the king had come to Winterfell"; the raven arrives during the turret scene
- **chapter_summary:** Bran watches raw recruits training in Winterfell's yard while Maester Luwin measures a comet. Bran tells Luwin about a three-eyed crow dream that led him to the crypts, where his father was sad about something to do with Jon. When Hodor refuses to descend the crypt stairs, Osha carries Bran down instead. Bran recounts the history of the Kings of Winter to Osha. Shaggydog attacks Luwin from inside Eddard's empty tomb, and Rickon is discovered hiding there — he has had the same dream. Back in Luwin's turret, the maester lectures on the children of the forest and shows obsidian arrowheads, while Osha asserts the children still live beyond the Wall. The direwolves howl and a wounded raven arrives bearing news that Eddard Stark is dead.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran watches recruits train** — From Luwin's turret balcony, Bran watches raw recruits (oldest 17-20, most 16 or less) spar with staves and wooden swords while Ser Rodrik berates them, saying "No" repeatedly and comparing their fighting to a goose and a hedgehog.
2. **Luwin measures the comet** — The maester peers through his Myrish lens tube, measuring shadows and noting the position of a comet low in the morning sky, marking numbers in a book.
3. **Bran expresses resentment and desire to fight** — Bran says he could beat the recruits if he had his legs, recalls knocking Prince Tommen down half a hundred times with a wooden sword, and proposes using a poleaxe with Hodor as his legs. Luwin dismisses this as unlikely.
4. **Bran recounts Symeon Star-Eyes** — Bran cites Old Nan's story of a blind knight with a bladed staff. Luwin identifies him as Symeon Star-Eyes and dismisses the tale as a fable from the Age of Heroes.
5. **Bran tells Luwin about the three-eyed crow dream** — The crow flew into his bedchamber, told him to come, and led him to the crypts. Father was there, they talked, and he was sad. It was "something to do about Jon." The dream was deeply disturbing, more so than the other crow dreams.
6. **Hodor refuses the crypts** — When Bran told Hodor to take him down to the crypts that morning, Hodor stood at the top step and would not descend, saying only "Hodor," apparently afraid of the dark despite Bran having a torch. Bran almost swatted him but did not.
7. **Luwin dismisses the dream** — He tells Bran that Eddard is a prisoner in King's Landing and will not sit in stone for many years. Bran insists he was there last night. Luwin offers to take Bran down to see.
8. **Osha carries Bran to the crypts** — The wildling woman is summoned; she lifts Bran in wiry-strong arms. Summer follows but refuses to go deep. Luwin leads with a torch. Bran notes Osha's chain was struck off by Ser Rodrik's order but ankle shackles remain.
9. **Summer stops at the crypt steps** — Summer stalks into the gloom, lifts his head, sniffs the chill dead air, bares his teeth, and creeps backward with golden eyes glowing.
10. **Osha eyes the Stark statues** — She calls them "grim folk" and smiles when Bran whispers they were the Kings of Winter, saying "Winter's got no king. If you'd seen it, you'd know that, summer boy."
11. **Luwin describes the statues** — Some hairy and bearded, shaggy men fierce as wolves crouching by their feet. Others shaved clean, features gaunt and sharp-edged as the iron longswords across their laps. "Hard men for a hard time."
12. **Bran recounts Stark history** — Walking through the crypts, he identifies Jon Stark, Rickard Stark (ancient), Theon Stark the Hungry Wolf, Brandon the Shipwright, Brandon the Burner, Rodrik Stark, Torrhen Stark the King Who Knelt, Cregan Stark, Lord Rickard (grandfather), Lyanna, and Brandon (uncle).
13. **Bran tells Lyanna's story** — Robert was betrothed to Lyanna; Rhaegar carried her off and raped her; Robert killed Rhaegar on the Trident with his hammer; Lyanna died and Robert never got her back.
14. **Osha comments on the empty tombs** — "A sad tale, but those empty holes are sadder." Luwin identifies one as Lord Eddard's tomb, prepared for when his time comes.
15. **Shaggydog attacks from Eddard's tomb** — As Luwin thrusts his arm into the empty sepulchre to prove it empty, darkness springs at him snarling. Shaggydog, with green fire eyes and black fur, bites and savages Luwin's arm. The torch flies from his fingers and falls against Brandon Stark's statue, flames licking up its legs.
16. **Summer fights Shaggydog** — Summer shoots from the dimness, slams into Shaggydog, and the two direwolves roll in a tangle of grey and black fur, snapping and biting. Their shadows, twenty feet tall, fight on walls and roof. Osha props Bran against Lord Rickard's stone wolf to help Luwin.
17. **Rickon is discovered** — Standing in the mouth of Eddard's tomb, Rickon warns Luwin to leave his father alone. Tears glisten on his face. He says he saw his father last night in a dream and that father is coming home as he promised.
18. **Bran confirms the shared dream** — Both boys dreamed of their father in the crypts the same night. Luwin looks more uncertain than Bran has ever seen him.
19. **Luwin warns about Shaggydog** — He says Shaggydog is too wild, the third man he's savaged, and must be chained or killed. Bran thinks "or killed" but says aloud: "He was not made for chains. We will wait in your tower, all of us."
20. **Osha affirms Bran's authority** — "The boy's the lordling here, as I recall." She carries Bran; Rickon follows with Shaggydog; Luwin has no choice but to follow, keeping a wary eye on the wolves.
21. **Osha tends Luwin's wounds in the turret** — She washes, cleans, and bandages the bites with stinging ointment and pale red firemilk, under Luwin's terse instruction. Both direwolves sit on opposite sides of the room, licking wounds and gnawing on bones.
22. **Luwin rationalizes the shared dream** — He says fear can fever a man's mind and that Rickon is too young to understand. Rickon interjects "I'm four now" while peering through the lens tube at gargoyles on the First Keep.
23. **Luwin shows obsidian arrowheads** — He produces a green jar of shiny black arrowheads to make a point about trusting in spells ("dueling with a glass sword"). Osha names them "dragonglass"; Luwin insists "obsidian." He explains the children of the forest hunted with them — "forged in the fires of the gods, far below the earth." They wore woven leaves and bark instead of mail, obsidian blades instead of swords. Bran keeps one; Rickon takes four ("I want four. I'm four."). Luwin makes Rickon count them out.
24. **Osha says the children still use obsidian** — "And still do," she says in the present tense while bandaging Luwin.
25. **Luwin lectures on the children of the forest** — People of the Dawn Age, dark and beautiful, small of stature. Lived in woods, caves, crannogs, and secret tree towns. Quick and graceful. Hunted with weirwood bows and flying snares. Their gods were the old gods. Their wise men were greenseers who carved faces in weirwoods.
26. **Luwin tells of the First Men** — Appeared from the east twelve thousand years ago, crossing the Broken Arm of Dorne. Came with bronze swords, great leathern shields, and horses. Cut down the weirwood faces and burned them. The children went to war. Greenseers used dark magic to shatter the Arm and make the seas rise, but too late. Wars continued, more children than men dying. Wood, stone, and obsidian are a poor match for bronze.
27. **Luwin tells of the Pact** — The wise of both races prevailed. Chiefs and heroes of the First Men met greenseers and wood dancers on the Isle of Faces in Gods Eye. First Men got coastlands, high plains, meadows, mountains, and bogs; deep woods remained forever the children's. No more weirwoods to be cut. Every tree on the island given a face. The sacred order of green men formed to watch over the Isle. Four thousand years of friendship followed. First Men took up the old gods. The Dawn Age ended; the Age of Heroes began.
28. **Osha asserts children survive beyond the Wall** — "North of the Wall, things are different. That's where the children went, and the giants, and the other old races." Luwin dismisses this as folly, saying the Starks have treated Osha gently and she repays their kindness by filling the boys' heads with nonsense.
29. **Luwin tells of the Andal invasion** — A race of tall, fair-haired warriors with steel, fire, and the seven-pointed star. Wars lasted hundreds of years. Six southron kingdoms fell. Only in the North, where the King in the North threw back every army at the Neck, did the First Men's rule endure. The Andals burned weirwood groves, hacked the faces, slaughtered the children, and proclaimed the triumph of the Seven. The children fled north—
30. **The direwolves howl** — Summer begins to howl, then Shaggydog joins. Dread clutches Bran. He realizes he has known since the crow dream that something terrible was coming — "He had known it, but he had not believed. He had wanted Maester Luwin to be right."
31. **The howling stops** — Summer pads to Shaggydog and licks at the bloody fur on his brother's neck. A raven arrives at the window.
32. **A wounded raven arrives** — A raven with dried blood on its wings lands on the grey stone sill, gives a harsh raucous rattle of distress. Luwin whistles it onto his bandaged forearm. He notes a hawk or owl attacked it: "Poor thing, a wonder it got through." He takes the letter from its leg.
33. **Rickon cries** — His arrowheads fall from his hand one by one, clattering on the floor.
34. **Osha speaks to Bran** — "You know what it is, boy," she says, not unkindly, putting her hand on his head.
35. **Luwin delivers the news** — The small grey man with blood on his sleeve and tears in his bright grey eyes tells them: "My lords … we shall need to find a stonecarver who knew his likeness well …" — implying Eddard Stark is dead.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Resentful of his disability early in the chapter; stubborn and determined about wanting to fight; deeply disturbed by the crow dream (more so than any prior crow dream); curious and engaged while recounting Stark history in the crypts; sadness creeping over him as he reaches the end of the statues; growing dread and certainty when the wolves howl; shivering fear as the letter is unrolled; grief at the end
- **Primary preoccupation:** The three-eyed crow dream about his father being sad in the crypts; wanting to go down and verify whether his father is truly there; his crippled state and desire to be a knight
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to go to the crypts to verify the dream; asserts lordly authority to keep Shaggydog unchained and bring everyone to Luwin's turret; keeps an obsidian arrowhead
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran acknowledges internally that he had known since the crow dream that something terrible was coming — "He had known it, but he had not believed. He had wanted Maester Luwin to be right." He chose to hope the maester's rationalism was correct over his own instinct and the crow's message.
