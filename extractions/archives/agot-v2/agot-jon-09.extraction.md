# AGOT — Jon IX

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 71

- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon IX
- **first_available:** AGOT Jon IX
- **location_primary:** The kingsroad south of Castle Black
- **location_secondary:** Castle Black (stables, King's Tower), Mole's Town
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after news of Eddard Stark's death reaches the Wall; Jon attempts desertion at night and is returned before dawn the same night
- **time_markers:** Night (full moon); moonlight throughout the ride south; dawn still an hour off when returning to Castle Black; day breaks when Jon goes to the kitchens; "first light" referenced as when the Old Bear rises; "on the morrow" referenced for future plans
- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow attempts to desert the Night's Watch to join Robb's war after learning of their father's death. He rides south from Castle Black at night, passing through Mole's Town. His friends — Sam, Pyp, Grenn, Halder, Toad, Matthar, and a seventh rider — catch up to him and surround him, reciting the Night's Watch oath until he yields. They ride back to Castle Black before dawn. The next morning, Lord Commander Mormont reveals he knew of Jon's attempt, lectures him on the greater threat beyond the Wall, announces a great ranging, and demands Jon's commitment. Jon swears to stay.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon saddles a mare in the Castle Black stables at night** — He straps his roll to the saddle, calls Ghost to him, and prepares to desert.
2. **Sam Tarly confronts Jon in the stable door** — Sam begs Jon not to leave, standing in his way. Jon threatens to ride him down.
3. **Jon rides past Sam** — The mare bolts; Sam jumps aside at the last moment, stumbles and falls. Jon rides out of Castle Black with Ghost racing alongside.
4. **Jon follows the kingsroad south under moonlight** — He plans to leave the road the next day to throw off pursuit. He reflects on honor, his father, and his decision.
5. **Jon considers his situation as a deserter** — He knows Maester Aemon's ravens will spread the word; strangers in black are viewed with suspicion. He would not be safe even at Winterfell.
6. **Jon recalls Winterfell with longing** — He remembers the castle, the Great Hall, Bran, Gage's pies, and Old Nan's tales.
7. **Jon reflects on his motivations** — He left to be his father's son and Robb's brother. He recalls Maester Aemon's lesson about choosing, and Tyrion's words about denying hard truths.
8. **Jon remembers the beheading of the deserter** — He recalls Lord Eddard's words about vows, Desmond and Fat Tom dragging the man, Bran watching, Theon bringing forth Ice and kicking the head.
9. **Jon slows the mare to a walk past Mole's Town** — He rides through the sleeping village, noting the underground structures and the red lantern over the brothel.
10. **Jon stops to rest and water himself** — He drinks icy snowmelt, splashes it on his face. His burned hand and head throb.
11. **Jon walks the mare to cool her down** — He notes the road is narrow and littered with stone. He calls for Ghost, who does not appear.
12. **Jon eats his provisions** — He sits under trees eating biscuit, cheese, and an apple while the mare grazes.
13. **Riders approach from the north** — Jon hears hooves and voices; he hides behind sentinel trees with the mare.
14. **Jon identifies the riders as his Night's Watch friends** — He recognizes Grenn, Pyp, Toad, and Halder by their voices. There are at least seven riders total.
15. **Ghost reappears and gives away Jon's position** — The direwolf bounds out of the shadows, startling the mare into whinnying. The riders hear it.
16. **The friends surround Jon** — Jon draws his sword and threatens them, but Halder signals the group to encircle him. Jon is outnumbered seven to one.
17. **The friends recite the Night's Watch oath** — Pyp, Grenn, Halder, Toad, and Matthar each recite portions of the oath, drowning out Jon's protests and curses.
18. **Jon yields** — Pyp reaches for his reins and gives him the choice: kill him or come back. Jon lowers his sword and agrees to return.
19. **The group rides back to Castle Black** — Pyp sets the pace. They arrive before dawn. Sam is waiting in the stables.
20. **Jon internally plans to desert again** — He tells himself he will bide his time and try a different route: east along the Wall to the sea, or west through the mountains.
21. **Jon brings Mormont's breakfast the next morning** — Three-Finger Hobb gives him the food. Jon carries it to the King's Tower and prepares Mormont's beer with lemon.
22. **Mormont reveals he knew about Jon's desertion attempt** — He tells Jon that Aemon predicted it and Mormont expected him back. A watch had been kept; Jon would have been caught regardless.
23. **Mormont lectures Jon on duty vs. personal grief** — He speaks of his own sister Maege marching with Robb. He emphasizes that the Night's Watch's war matters more than the conflict in the south.
24. **Mormont shares alarming reports from beyond the Wall** — Elk and mammoth herds moving south, misshapen footprints near Eastwatch, abandoned villages, mountain fires, Mance Rayder massing his people in a secret stronghold.
25. **Mormont announces a great ranging beyond the Wall** — He intends to command it personally, to find Benjen Stark and confront whatever threatens. He wants Jon and Ghost with him.
26. **Jon swears to stay** — He asks forgiveness of his family internally and pledges himself to Mormont as his man. Mormont tells him to go put on his sword (Longclaw).

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Torn between duty and family; guilt over abandoning the Night's Watch; grief and rage over his father's murder; frustration and anger when his friends catch him; shame when Mormont reveals he was anticipated; ultimately resolved but heavy-hearted when he recommits
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether deserting is the right thing to do; the tension between his vows and his desire to avenge his father and fight beside Robb
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Decides to desert the Night's Watch at the chapter's opening; (2) Yields to his friends rather than hurting them; (3) Internally plans to desert again after being brought back; (4) Ultimately swears to stay after Mormont's speech about the threat beyond the Wall
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells himself "I warned him" about Sam to ease his guilt; plans to pretend contentment then desert again (abandoned by chapter's end); tells himself "I am doing the right thing" while his body and emotions contradict him; initially cannot imagine Robb's welcoming smile, suggesting he knows the reality may differ from his fantasy
