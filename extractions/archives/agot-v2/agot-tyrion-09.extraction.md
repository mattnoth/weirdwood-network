- **chapter_summary:** A blood-stained Crakehall messenger arrives at the inn where Tywin's host is resting to report the catastrophic defeat at Riverrun: Robb Stark and the Tullys destroyed Jaime's three camps, freed Edmure Tully, and captured Jaime. Tywin's bannermen argue about what to do, with suggestions ranging from suing for peace to marching to battle. After dismissing his lords, Tywin confers privately with Kevan and Tyrion, revealing that Renly Baratheon has crowned himself king and wed Margaery Tyrell. Tywin announces plans to march to Harrenhal and unleash Gregor Clegane, Vargo Hoat, and Amory Lorch to burn the riverlands. He then tells Tyrion he is sending him to King's Landing to rule as Hand in his stead and curb Joffrey's follies. Tyrion realizes his father has given up Jaime for lost.

## Events & Actions
1. **Messenger arrives with news of Riverrun** — A blood-stained Crakehall courier reports to Tywin's war council that Robb Stark and the Tullys have destroyed Jaime's besieging army at Riverrun.
2. **Report of the battle** — The messenger describes the night attack: the Blackfish led the van against the north camp, Lord Brax drowned trying to cross the river on a raft, the Starks swept in from the west with Robb and his wolf, the Tullys sortied from Riverrun, Greatjon Umber fired the siege towers, and Edmure Tully was freed.
3. **South camp retreats** — Ser Forley Prester retreated in good order with 2,000 spears and as many bowmen, but lost his Tyroshi sellsword freeriders to defection.
4. **Council debates response** — Ser Harys Swyft advocates suing for peace; Ser Addam Marbrand argues for marching to battle; Lord Lefford suggests prisoner exchange or ransom; Tyrion dismisses all options as futile given Joffrey's execution of Eddard Stark.
5. **Tyrion smashes his wine cup** — Tyrion hurls his cup to the floor, shattering it, to illustrate that peace is impossible after Eddard's execution.
6. **Tywin dismisses the council** — Tywin orders everyone out except Tyrion and Kevan.
7. **Tywin shares additional intelligence** — He reveals Renly Baratheon has crowned himself king and married Margaery Tyrell, and that Cersei commands them to ride to King's Landing.
8. **Strategic discussion** — Tywin, Kevan, and Tyrion discuss the dire strategic situation: enemies on all sides, Stannis building forces on Dragonstone, Bolton behind them.
9. **Tywin announces plan to march to Harrenhal** — He orders Ser Addam's outriders to screen their movements and unleashes Gregor Clegane, Vargo Hoat, and Amory Lorch with 300 horse each to burn the riverlands from the Gods Eye to the Red Fork.
10. **Tywin offers Tyrion's clansmen for raiding** — Tyrion declines, preferring to keep Shagga and Timett with him.
11. **Tywin sends Tyrion to King's Landing** — He tells Tyrion to go to court to "rule" — to curb Joffrey and manage the council. He condemns Joffrey's follies, the elevation of Janos Slynt, the dismissal of Barristan Selmy, and the incompetence of the small council.
12. **Tyrion realizes Tywin has given up Jaime** — When Tywin says "You are my son" to explain why he's sending Tyrion, Tyrion understands his father believes Jaime is as good as dead and Tyrion is all he has left.
13. **Tywin forbids Tyrion from bringing Shae** — His parting words: "You will not take the whore to court."
14. **Tyrion observes the hanged innkeep** — From his garret window, he sees the innkeep's body on the gibbet, flesh thin and ragged.
15. **Tyrion tells Shae his plan** — He wakes Shae and tells her he has a mind to take her to King's Landing.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Complex mixture — bitter amusement, anger, grudging pleasure, pain (physical from battle wound), cynicism, and deep hurt at his father's conditional regard. Torn between being pleased at the trust/responsibility and knowing he's only chosen because Jaime is lost.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The military catastrophe and what it means for his family; then the shock and implications of being sent to rule in King's Landing; his relationship with Shae.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to hand over his clansmen (Shagga, Timett) to ride with Vargo Hoat; decides to bring Shae to King's Landing despite his father's explicit prohibition.
- **Self-deception flags:** None overt — Tyrion is remarkably clear-eyed in this chapter. He sees through his father's motives ("You have given him up for lost"), recognizes the impossible military situation, and acknowledges his own fear of battle. His decision to bring Shae is made with full awareness that it defies Tywin.
