- **chapter_summary:** Sansa grieves in isolation in Maegor's Holdfast after her father's execution, refusing food and contemplating throwing herself from the window. Joffrey forces her to attend court, where Ser Meryn Trant strikes her when she protests. After bathing and dressing, she watches Joffrey dispense cruel justice in the throne room. Joffrey then takes her to the gatehouse battlements to view the tar-preserved heads of her father and Septa Mordane mounted on spikes. When Sansa defiantly suggests her brother might give her Joffrey's head, Ser Meryn strikes her again, and Sansa briefly contemplates pushing Joffrey from the rampart before Sandor Clegane intervenes by kneeling to clean her bloodied lip.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa grieves in isolation** — After her father's execution, Sansa withdraws to her bed in Maegor's Holdfast, refusing food, sleeping and weeping, haunted by memories of the beheading.
2. **Sansa contemplates suicide** — She considers flinging herself from the window but loses her nerve and returns to bed sobbing.
3. **Pycelle examines Sansa** — Grand Maester Pycelle visits, examines her, and gives her a sleeping potion of honeywater and herbs.
4. **Sansa dreams of Ser Ilyn** — She has a nightmare of Ser Ilyn Payne climbing the tower stairs with Ice, coming for her head; she wakes murmuring pleas.
5. **Joffrey forces Sansa from bed** — Joffrey arrives with Sandor Clegane and two Kingsguard, demands she attend court. When she refuses, Sandor lifts her from bed.
6. **Sansa pleads to go home** — She reminds Joffrey she wrote the letters as asked and begs to be allowed to leave; Joffrey refuses, saying his mother insists they still marry.
7. **Sansa tells Joffrey she hates him** — She says "I hate you" after truly seeing him for the first time as cruel.
8. **Ser Meryn strikes Sansa (first time)** — On Joffrey's command, Ser Meryn backhands Sansa across the ear with a gloved fist, knocking her to the floor and bloodying her.
9. **Sandor Clegane advises Sansa** — After Joffrey leaves, the Hound tells Sansa to give Joffrey what he wants: smiles, pretty words, love, and fear.
10. **Sansa bathes and dresses** — She has her maids prepare hot water, washes for the first time since her father's death; her hair springs back in thick auburn curls. She chooses the green silk gown from the tourney and uses powder to hide the bruise.
11. **Ser Meryn escorts Sansa to court** — At midday, Ser Meryn returns in full white armor to escort her; Sansa calls him "no true knight."
12. **Joffrey holds court** — From a deserted balcony, Sansa watches Joffrey on the Iron Throne: he has a thief's hand chopped off, orders two knights to duel to the death, sends a grieving woman to the dungeons.
13. **The tavern singer's punishment** — A singer who mocked Robert is forced to perform his song, then told he must choose between losing his fingers or his tongue.
14. **Joffrey walks with Sansa after court** — He talks about his upcoming name day feast, calls Sansa stupid (quoting his mother), and threatens to get her with child and behead her if the child is stupid.
15. **Joffrey shows Sansa the severed heads** — He takes her up to the gatehouse battlements and forces her to look at her father's tar-preserved head and Septa Mordane's decayed head on spikes, plus two empty spikes saved for Stannis and Renly.
16. **Joffrey threatens Robb** — He vows to raise a host after his name day and kill Robb himself, offering Robb's head as a gift to Sansa.
17. **Sansa defies Joffrey** — She retorts that perhaps her brother will give her Joffrey's head instead.
18. **Ser Meryn strikes Sansa (second time)** — On Joffrey's order, Ser Meryn holds her jaw and strikes her twice, splitting her lip.
19. **Sansa contemplates pushing Joffrey** — She considers shoving him over the inner edge of the wallwalk (a 70-80 foot drop), thinking it wouldn't matter if she went over with him.
20. **Sandor Clegane cleans Sansa's face** — The Hound kneels before her with surprising delicacy and dabs the blood from her broken lip, positioning himself between her and Joffrey. The moment of impulse passes.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief-stricken, traumatized, fearful, but with flashes of defiance and rage. Moves between despair (contemplating suicide), numbness (looking at her father's head "calmly, not seeing it at all"), hatred ("I hate you"), and murderous impulse (considering pushing Joffrey from the rampart). She is learning to mask her true feelings.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Surviving Joffrey's cruelty; grieving her father; yearning for home (Winterfell). She thinks of the north when looking from the battlements.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to comply with Joffrey's demands after the Hound's advice — to give him what he wants, to smile, to use courtesies as armor. Chooses the green silk gown deliberately to remind Joffrey of his former gallantry. Ultimately chooses survival over suicide (twice: at the window, and when the impulse to push Joffrey passes).
- **Self-deception flags:** Earlier self-deception about Joffrey has fully collapsed — she now sees his lips as "soft and red as the worms you found after a rain" and his eyes as "vain and cruel," wondering "how she could ever have thought him handsome." She tells herself "he can make me look at the heads, but he can't make me see them" — a coping mechanism of deliberate dissociation.
