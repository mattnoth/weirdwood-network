- **time_markers:** No specific time of day stated; travel occurs during daylight (watchers seen from towers, visual identification of banners and camp); events appear to take place over a single day of riding down from the foothills to the camp
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion descends from the Mountains of the Moon with a band of roughly three hundred mountain clansmen and the sellsword Bronn, heading toward a Lannister army camped at the crossroads. He rides ahead with representatives from each clan to meet his father. At the inn at the crossroads, Tyrion reunites with Lord Tywin and Ser Kevan, learns of the war's progress including Robert Baratheon's death, and introduces the clansmen. A messenger arrives reporting that the Stark host is marching south down the causeway. Lord Tywin orders his army to march against Robb Stark, and the clansmen agree to fight — on the condition that Tyrion rides with them.

## Events & Actions
1. **Chella scouts ahead** — Chella daughter of Cheyk reports a Lannister army of approximately twenty thousand at the crossroads, flying red banners with a golden lion.
2. **Tyrion proposes riding down alone** — He suggests going to the camp by himself, but the clansmen insist on sending representatives.
3. **Delegation assembled** — Tyrion selects Chella, Shagga, Conn, Ulf, and Timett to ride with him, with Bronn also accompanying. The rest of the clansmen (~300) wait behind.
4. **Descent through the foothills** — The party rides past watchers in unmortared stone towers; a raven takes wing.
5. **First checkpoint encountered** — At a strong point where the high road narrows between rocky outcrops, crossbowmen and a low earthen wall block the road. The captain recognizes Tyrion and provides an escort.
6. **Passage through war-ravaged land** — Tyrion sees blackened fields, burned holdfasts, no bodies but many ravens and carrion crows — evidence of recent fighting.
7. **Arrival at the Lannister camp** — The party reaches the barricade of sharpened stakes; Ser Flement Brax challenges them, recognizes Tyrion with astonishment, and lets them through.
8. **Ride through the camp** — Tyrion notes the camp's enormous scale (spread over leagues); identifies banners of Presters, Crakehall, Marbrand, Lydden. The clansmen are visibly awed.
9. **Arrival at the inn** — Tyrion sees the gibbet with a woman's corpse (the former innkeep, birds having eaten her face). He addresses her corpse wryly.
10. **Shagga resists giving up his horse** — Tyrion convinces Shagga to let a stableboy tend his mare.
11. **Tyrion enters the inn** — He meets Tywin and Kevan in the common room. Tywin gives him a long, searching look.
12. **Reunion dialogue** — Tywin shows no warmth; says "the rumors of your demise were unfounded." Blames Tyrion for starting the conflict by getting captured. Notes Jaime would never have submitted to capture by a woman.
13. **War update delivered** — Kevan and Tywin brief Tyrion: Edmure's border forces destroyed piecemeal; Jaime smashed Vance and Piper at the Golden Tooth; Jaime besieging Riverrun with Edmure captured; Raventree fell; Lady Whent yielded Harrenhal; Ser Gregor burned out the Pipers and Brackens.
14. **Remaining opposition identified** — Mallisters hold Seagard; Walder Frey marshals at the Twins but is expected to wait and see. Tywin dismisses both as threats unless Starks or Arryns intervene.
15. **Robert's death revealed** — Tywin tells Tyrion that Robert Baratheon is dead and "your nephew" reigns. Tyrion is taken aback, internally concluding Cersei is the real power.
16. **Tyrion dismisses Arryn threat** — He tells his father not to worry about the Arryns.
17. **Tywin offers Tyrion commands** — First to deal with Marq Piper and Karyl Vance raiding across the Red Fork; then to handle Beric Dondarrion and Thoros. Tyrion deflects both.
18. **Tyrion lists his promises to the clansmen** — He begins enumerating 3,000 helms, hauberks, swords, pikes, spearheads, maces, battle-axes, gauntlets, gorgets, greaves, breastplates, and wagons.
19. **Shagga bursts in** — He crashes through the door, throws the guard captain across the room into the hearth, snaps the man's sword over his knee, and threatens him.
20. **Clansmen enter the common room** — All five clansmen and Bronn enter. Kevan demands to know who they are.
21. **Tyrion introduces the clansmen to Tywin** — He names each one with mock ceremony and introduces Bronn as a sellsword who has "already changed sides twice."
22. **Tywin introduces himself formally** — He addresses the clansmen with calculated respect, acknowledging their prowess.
23. **Tywin manipulates the clansmen** — He mentions the fearsome winterland men, implying the clansmen need not join. The reverse psychology works: Timett, Conn, Shagga, and Chella all declare they will ride.
24. **Chella's condition** — She agrees to ride but demands Tyrion go with them, as his life is theirs until he delivers the promised steel.
25. **Tywin turns his eyes on Tyrion** — Tyrion responds with "Joy" and a resigned smile.
26. **Messenger arrives** — Reports that the Stark host is moving down the causeway, sent by Ser Addam.
27. **Tywin orders the march** — He tells the messenger to have Ser Addam fall back, harass the Stark flanks, and draw them south. Orders drummers to beat assembly and sends word to Jaime that he marches against Robb Stark.
28. **Kevan suggests defensive posture** — He argues they are well situated with ford, pits, and spikes. Tywin overrules him, wanting to break the Starks quickly to deal with Stannis Baratheon.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Sardonic and guarded; resigned amusement at his father's contempt; darkly humorous throughout; a flash of genuine surprise at Robert's death; "grim fascination" watching Tywin manipulate the clansmen
- **Primary preoccupation:** Managing the clansmen (keeping their loyalty while controlling their wildness); navigating his father's dismissiveness; processing the war's rapid developments; privately scheming to use the clansmen against the Vale
- **Key decisions made:** Selects which clansmen to bring as representatives; decides not to ride down alone; declines Tywin's offered commands (Piper/Vance and Dondarrion/Thoros); accepts (with resignation) that he must ride with the clansmen into battle
- **Self-deception flags:** Wonders whether he is the clansmen's commander or captive, acknowledging "most of the time, it seemed to be a little of both" — some self-awareness about his uncertain position; becomes "uncomfortably aware of all his deformities and shortcomings" under his father's gaze, suggesting internalized shame
