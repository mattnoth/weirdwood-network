- **chapter_summary:** While King Robert hunts a white hart in the kingswood, Ned sits the Iron Throne and hears petitions. Riverlands knights Ser Raymun Darry, Ser Karyl Vance, and Ser Marq Piper bring surviving villagers from Sherrer, Wendish Town, and the Mummer's Ford who testify that a large, armored raiding force — identified as Gregor Clegane — burned their homes, killed their people, and butchered livestock without looting. Ned rejects Ser Loras Tyrell's offer to lead the response and instead commands Lord Beric Dondarrion, Thoros of Myr, Ser Gladden, and Lord Lothar to ride west with eighty men to bring the king's justice to Clegane. Ned formally denounces, attaints, and sentences Gregor Clegane to death. Afterward, Varys privately suggests Ned's decisions may have political costs.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned sits the Iron Throne** — With Robert away hunting a white hart in the kingswood, Ned holds court as Hand, despite his shattered leg causing severe pain.
2. **Riverlands knights present survivors** — Ser Raymun Darry, Ser Karyl Vance, and Ser Marq Piper bring surviving villagers from Sherrer to testify before the throne about Lannister raids.
3. **Villagers testify about the raids** — Joss the brewer, a farmer, a smith, a girl, and an old man describe the attacks on Sherrer, Wendish Town, and the Mummer's Ford: burning homes and fields, butchering livestock, killing civilians including women and children, riding down an apprentice boy with lances as sport, burning people alive inside the Wendish Town holdfast, and shooting those who fled with arrows.
4. **The Mountain is identified** — Witnesses describe an enormous leader "big as an ox" with "a voice like stone breaking"; Ser Marq Piper names him as Gregor Clegane.
5. **Pycelle argues for inaction** — Grand Maester Pycelle urges that the matter be deferred to Lord Tywin's justice or until the king returns, reminding the court that Tywin is father to the queen.
6. **Ned observes courtiers leaving** — Ned spots men slipping out the hall door, suspecting them of going to inform the queen. He also sees Sansa and Septa Mordane in the gallery.
7. **Littlefinger questions the Riverlands knights** — Asks where they were during the raids; Ser Karyl explains they were at the Golden Tooth pass, Ser Raymun was summoned to Riverrun.
8. **Ned reflects on Tywin's strategy** — Internally recognizes that Tywin sent Clegane without banners so that any Tully retaliation could be blamed on Riverrun for breaking the king's peace; thinks Edmure is young and more gallant than wise.
9. **Ser Loras Tyrell volunteers** — Offers to lead the force against Clegane; Littlefinger mocks this as suicidal; Ned declines.
10. **Ned commissions Beric Dondarrion's force** — Names Lord Beric Dondarrion, Thoros of Myr, Ser Gladden, and Lord Lothar to each assemble twenty men, plus twenty of Ned's own guards, to ride to Clegane's keep. Lord Beric given overall command.
11. **Ned formally sentences Gregor Clegane** — In Robert's name, Ned denounces, attaints, and strips Gregor Clegane of all rank, titles, lands, incomes, and holdings, and sentences him to death.
12. **Ser Robar Royce dispatched to inform the king** — Ned sends Ser Robar to carry word of the proceedings to Robert on his hunt.
13. **Ned descends the throne** — Alyn and Porther help him down the steep iron steps while Ser Loras stares sullenly before stalking away.
14. **Varys counsels Ned privately** — Varys suggests Ned should have sent Ser Loras (to make the Tyrells allies against the Lannisters) and warns that Ser Ilyn Payne appeared displeased at being bypassed as King's Justice.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pain from his shattered leg dominates physically; politically he feels the weight of responsibility and unease; experiences fury at the raiders' atrocities that he struggles to control; flashes anger at seeing Sansa in the gallery; feels sullen resentment at Aegon for building the throne and Robert for leaving him to sit it; grim determination when pronouncing sentence
- **Primary preoccupation:** Dispensing justice while navigating the political minefield between Lannisters and Tullys; recognizing Tywin's strategic trap; maintaining the king's peace rather than enabling vengeance
- **Key decisions made:** Rejects Ser Loras's offer (avoids making this about vengeance and avoids political entanglement with the Tyrells); sends Lord Beric Dondarrion with a mixed force of 80 men; bypasses Ser Ilyn Payne as King's Justice because the Paynes are Lannister bannermen; formally attaints and sentences Gregor Clegane to death in the king's name; sends Ser Robar Royce to inform Robert
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned dismisses Varys's advice about courting the Tyrells as an ally, focused on justice over political strategy; tells himself Ser Loras "will outgrow the disappointment" without weighing the political cost; does not fully reckon with how his actions provoke Tywin further
