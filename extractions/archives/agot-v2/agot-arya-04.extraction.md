# AGOT — Arya IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 51
- **pov_character:** Arya Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya IV
- **first_available:** AGOT Arya IV
- **location_primary:** The Red Keep, King's Landing (Small Hall, kitchens, cellars, stables, yards, dungeons)
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell (recalled), Braavos (referenced in Syrio's story)
- **approximate_timeline:** Immediately following the political crisis — Lannister men sent to seize Stark household members; a departure by ship had been planned for that evening
- **time_markers:** Daylight throughout the above-ground scenes (Arya sees daylight through cellar window, crosses the bailey in daylight); Arya notes she will have a bruise "by the time she went to sleep, somewhere out at sea" — implying departure was planned for that day/evening; "more than an hour" spent navigating back to the dragon skull dungeon; chapter ends in total darkness underground
- **chapter_summary:** Arya trains with Syrio Forel in the Small Hall when Ser Meryn Trant arrives with five Lannister guardsmen to seize her. Syrio fights them with his wooden sword, defeating the five guardsmen but facing Ser Meryn in full armor as Arya flees. Arya runs through the castle, finding dead Stark household men at the stables, including the dying Hullen and the slain Desmond. A stableboy tries to capture her for the queen, and Arya kills him with Needle — her first kill. Unable to leave through the gates, she makes her way to the dragon skull dungeon and enters the underground passages to escape.

## Events & Actions
1. **Syrio trains Arya in the Small Hall** — Fast-paced drill calling out directions; Arya retreats before him, checking each blow. She nearly touches him with a shoulder slash but is struck when Syrio says "left" and goes right. "A bruise is a lesson, and each lesson makes us better."
2. **Syrio teaches Arya the lesson of "true seeing"** — Tells the story of how he became first sword to the Sealord of Braavos by correctly identifying an ordinary fat tomcat when others saw a fabulous beast. The key teaching: senses first, then thinking.
3. **Syrio mentions traveling to Winterfell and putting Needle in Arya's hand** — Implies he plans to accompany the Stark household north; Arya excitedly begins to mention Jon.
4. **Ser Meryn Trant and five Lannister guardsmen crash through the doors** — Knight of the Kingsguard in full armor with visor up; five red cloaks in mail and steel caps with lion crests. Demands Arya come with them, claiming her father sent for her.
5. **Syrio challenges the claim** — Holds Arya's arm and questions why Lord Eddard would send Lannister men instead of his own.
6. **Arya refuses to go** — Snatches up her stick sword; invokes the Kingslayer precedent: "So was the Kingslayer when he killed the old king."
7. **Ser Meryn orders his men to take Arya** — Lowers his visor; three guardsmen advance, chainmail clinking.
8. **Syrio intervenes** — Steps between Arya and the guardsmen; taps his wooden sword against his boot; strikes the first man's helm; breaks a guardsman's sword-hand fingers, disarming him.
9. **Syrio fights all five Lannister guardsmen** — Defeats them using speed, the water dancer's stance (presenting only his side), and improvised weapons (a stolen steel cap). One guardsman loses his left eye to an upward thrust. One guardsman's own ally accidentally kills him with a misdirected longsword blow (crunching through mail, leather, flesh between neck and shoulder). One is jabbed in the throat and staggers back choking, face blackening. One's kneecap is shattered. One's fingers are broken.
10. **Ser Meryn curses his men and draws his longsword** — "Bloody oafs."
11. **Syrio tells Arya to run** — "Arya child, we are done with dancing for the day. Best you are going now. Run to your father." Never takes his eyes off the Lannisters.
12. **Arya observes Syrio had been toying with her in their training** — Realizes his true speed only now.
13. **Arya observes the guardsmen's armor weaknesses** — Notes: leather on legs, bare hands, noseguards but no eye visors.
14. **Syrio faces Ser Meryn** — Bounces blows off temple, elbow, and throat; wood rings against metal but does no damage to the full plate armor. Ser Meryn advances; Syrio backs away. The fourth blow slices Syrio's stick in two, splintering wood and shearing through the lead core.
15. **Arya flees, sobbing** — Plunges through kitchens and buttery; bowls over a baker's helper (scattering fragrant bread); passes a portly butcher with a cleaver (arms red to the elbow).
16. **Arya reaches a turret stair and goes down** — Remembers Syrio's advice: "Never do what they expect." Up would lead to the covered bridge to the Tower of the Hand.
17. **Arya reaches a dead-end vaulted cellar** — Casks of ale stacked twenty feet tall. She climbs them and pulls herself through a window (wall three feet thick, window a tunnel slanting up and out).
18. **Arya observes the Tower of the Hand under attack** — From ground level across the bailey: stout wooden door splintered as if by axes; a dead man facedown on the steps in grey wool cloak trimmed with white satin, mailed shirt soaked red; sounds of fighting within.
19. **Arya remembers the dead and the yellow-bearded man's warning** — Thinks of Jory, Wyl, Heward; recalls "If one Hand can die, why not a second?"
20. **Arya acknowledges Syrio's probable death** — "the white knight had probably killed him."
21. **Arya moves through the yard** — Keeps close to walls, slides from shadow to shadow; pretends she is a cat being chased.
22. **Gold cloaks pass through the inner bailey** — A dozen in mail and plate; Arya hunches down in shadows, unsure whose side they are on.
23. **Arya finds Hullen dying at the stable door** — Master of horse, stabbed so many times his tunic looks patterned with scarlet flowers. Whispers "Arya Underfoot" and tells her to warn her lord father. Frothy red spittle; closes his eyes and says no more.
24. **Arya finds more dead in the stables** — A groom she had played with; three household guards; Desmond, staring blindly at the ceiling, flies on his eyes. One dead Lannister man nearby.
25. **Arya discovers an abandoned wagon and broken chest** — Wagon laden with crates and chests near the stable door, being loaded for departure to the docks. A fallen chest has split open, spilling Arya's clothes.
26. **Arya retrieves Needle from the chest** — Finds it hidden at the bottom beneath her belongings. Also finds: a heavy woolen cloak, a velvet skirt, a silk tunic, smallclothes, a dress her mother had embroidered for her, a silver baby bracelet.
27. **The stableboy confronts Arya** — Recognizes her as "the wolf girl"; says "Father's dead"; says the queen will reward him for capturing her. Grabs her arm hard.
28. **Arya kills the stableboy with Needle** — Drives the blade upward through his leather jerkin and belly, out between his shoulder blades. He gasps "Oh, gods … Take it out." When she takes it out, he dies. Her first kill.
29. **Arya stands frozen over the body** — Blood gushes from his mouth; more seeps from his belly wound, pools beneath him; his palms are cut where he grabbed the blade.
30. **Arya abandons her plan to ride out on horseback** — Realizes the castle gates would be closed and guarded; even postern doors likely guarded; decides on the underground passages instead.
31. **Arya gathers supplies and conceals Needle** — Takes a heavy woolen cloak, hides Needle beneath it, bundles the rest of her things.
32. **Arya crosses the yard to the royal sept** — Walks slowly, forcing calm, eyes on the ground, never looking up at the gold cloaks on the walls. "Cold with sweat" but no alarm is raised.
33. **Arya steals two prayer candles from the sept** — Stuffs them up her sleeves; exits through a back window.
34. **Arya navigates through the castle to the underground entrance** — Crawls through windows, hops over walls, moves through dark cellars; hears a woman weeping; takes more than an hour; gets lost before finding the low narrow window.
35. **Arya enters the dragon skull dungeon** — Tosses her bundle through the window; doubles back to light her candle from dying embers; hears voices as she exits through the window while unknown people enter through the door.
36. **Arya passes the dragon skulls** — No longer frightened; they seem like old friends. She whispers "Dragons" and draws Needle; the slender blade seems small against the dragons but she feels better with steel in hand.
37. **Arya enters the long windowless hall** — Goes right (away from the well entrance); holds Needle in her left hand (sword hand), candle in right; hot wax runs down her knuckles.
38. **Arya recalls the Winterfell crypts** — Memory of Robb leading the children down; Jon covered in flour scaring them from an open tomb; Sansa ran screaming, Bran clung to Robb, Arya punched the "spirit." They all laughed. The memory makes her smile and gives her courage.
39. **Arya resolves to go home** — "Everything would be better once she was home again, safe behind Winterfell's grey granite walls." Plunges deeper into the darkness.

- **Emotional state:** Progresses from playful confidence during training → shocked and confused (Lannister arrival) → suddenly afraid ("Arya was suddenly afraid") → panicked (fleeing through kitchens, "blind with panic") → horrified and grief-stricken (seeing the attack's aftermath, tears in her eyes) → terrified and frozen (considering the stableboy's dead eyes, "still and frightened in the face of death") → cold determination (forcing herself to walk calmly across the yard, "cold with sweat") → emboldened by memory (Winterfell crypts flashback makes her smile; the stableboy's ghost no longer frightens her) → resolute ("she was going home")
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and escape; finding her father to warn him; getting back to Winterfell
