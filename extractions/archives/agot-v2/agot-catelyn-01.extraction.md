## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn enters the godswood** — She seeks Ned, knowing he always goes to the godswood after taking a man's life. She reflects on the differences between the Winterfell godswood and the one at Riverrun.
2. **Catelyn finds Ned beneath the heart tree** — He is seated on a moss-covered stone, cleaning Ice in the black pool. He asks where the children are.
3. **Discussion of the wolf pups** — Catelyn reports the children are naming the pups: Arya is in love, Sansa is charmed, Rickon is unsure. Ned says Rickon must learn to face his fears and invokes "Winter is coming."
4. **Ned discusses the execution** — He says the man died well and he was glad for Bran's sake. The deserter was the fourth this year and was "half-mad" with fear. Ned says something had put a deep fear in him.
5. **Ned shares news from Benjen** — The Night's Watch strength is below a thousand due to desertions and losses on rangings. Ned attributes the problem to wildlings and mentions he may need to call the banners to deal with Mance Rayder.
6. **Catelyn raises the topic of the Others** — She says there are "darker things beyond the Wall." Ned dismisses this, saying the Others have been gone eight thousand years and Maester Luwin would say they never existed. Catelyn counters that until this morning no one had seen a direwolf either.
7. **Catelyn delivers news of Jon Arryn's death** — She tells Ned directly. He is deeply affected. She relays details from Robert's letter: Jon was taken quickly, Maester Pycelle was helpless, milk of the poppy eased his passing.
8. **Discussion of Lysa and Robert Arryn** — Ned asks about Catelyn's sister and Jon's son. Catelyn says they have returned to the Eyrie. She wishes they had gone to Riverrun instead. Ned mentions Brynden Tully is in the Vale as Knight of the Gate.
9. **Ned urges Catelyn to go to Lysa** — He suggests she take the children to fill Lysa's halls with life. Catelyn says she cannot.
10. **Catelyn announces the king's visit** — Robert is riding to Winterfell. Ned's grief lifts and he smiles. Catelyn notes the omen of the dead direwolf with a broken antler in its throat and feels dread, but forces herself to smile.
11. **Planning for the royal visit** — Ned begins planning: he will tell Maester Luwin to send a bird to Benjen; he will send Jory south with an honor guard to escort the party. Catelyn estimates a hundred knights at least, their retainers, and half again as many freeriders. Cersei and the children travel with them, as do the queen's brothers.
12. **Ned reacts to the Lannisters** — He grimaces at the news of the queen's brothers. The Lannisters came late to Robert's cause and Ned has never forgiven them. He calls it "an infestation of Lannisters."
13. **Discussion of Prince Tommen** — Ned recalls the youngest royal child was still nursing last time he saw him. Catelyn corrects him: Tommen is seven, and warns Ned to guard his tongue about the queen.
