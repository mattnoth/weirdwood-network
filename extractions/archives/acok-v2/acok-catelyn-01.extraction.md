- **time_markers:** Morning (morning light glimmered against the steel); a fortnight past since the white raven arrived announcing autumn; Theon leaves on the morrow; autumn declared by the Conclave; reference to forty years of Harrenhal's construction; three centuries since the ancient crown of the Kings of Winter was yielded to Aegon
- **chapter_summary:** Robb holds court at Riverrun and presents peace terms to Ser Cleos Frey for delivery to Cersei Lannister in King's Landing. The terms demand the return of Sansa and Arya, Ned's bones, the greatsword Ice, release of captured lords, and recognition of northern and riverlands independence. After court, Catelyn argues with Robb over releasing Jaime Lannister to secure the girls' return and warns against sending Theon to the Iron Islands. Catelyn visits her dying father Lord Hoster Tully, where she meets with Brynden Blackfish, who reports devastation across the riverlands and news of a new Lannister host assembling at Casterly Rock under Ser Stafford Lannister. They discuss the possibility of seeking alliance with Renly Baratheon.

## Events & Actions
1. **Robb holds court in Riverrun's Great Hall** — Wearing his new crown, Robb sits with his longsword drawn across his knees as Ser Cleos Frey is brought before him.
2. **Ser Cleos is forced to kneel** — Theon Greyjoy demands he kneel; Ser Robin Ryger forces him to his knees.
3. **Grey Wind intimidates Ser Cleos** — The direwolf pads forward and sniffs the captive knight, who edges away in fear, drawing laughter from watchers.
4. **Greatjon Umber corrects Ser Cleos's address** — Insists on "Your Grace" rather than "my lord."
5. **Robb presents peace terms** — Demands: return of Sansa and Arya by sea to White Harbor (Sansa's betrothal to Joffrey ended); return of Ned's bones; delivery of Ice; release of lords captured at Green Fork; recognition of northern and riverlands independence; Lannister withdrawal; ten highborn hostages.
6. **Robb states Jaime Lannister will be kept** — All other captives from Whispering Wood and Battle of the Camps to be released, save Jaime alone.
7. **Greatjon leads chanting** — "The King in the North!" / "Stark! Stark! King in the North!" Grey Wind howls.
8. **Robb threatens with Grey Wind** — Whistles for Grey Wind to snarl, saying he'll give Cersei "another Whispering Wood" if terms are refused.
9. **Lord Rickard Karstark walks out** — Shoves through guards and leaves during the presentation of terms.
10. **Robb orders Ser Cleos given meal, clothing, and departure at first light** — Ser Robin Ryger to see to it.
11. **Court ends; Robb departs with Grey Wind and Olyvar** — Catelyn and Edmure follow.
12. **Catelyn critiques Robb's wolf theatrics** — Tells him it was "japery more befitting a boy than a king."
13. **Catelyn notes Karstark's departure** — Robb acknowledges it, gives crown to Olyvar to return to bedchamber.
14. **Edmure argues for marching on Harrenhal** — Robb says they lack the strength; Edmure says they grow no stronger sitting idle.
15. **Catelyn blames Edmure for dwindling host** — It was Edmure's insistence that river lords were given leave to depart after crowning.
16. **Robb says he'll speak with Karstark** — Notes Karstark lost two sons in the Whispering Wood.
17. **Catelyn argues Cersei won't trade sisters for cousins** — She wants Jaime released; Robb refuses, says his lords would never abide it.
18. **Catelyn and Robb clash over Jaime's value vs. the girls** — Catelyn suggests they should pay even the crown to get the girls back; Robb says Lannister won't die but won't be freed.
19. **Catelyn questions if Robb fears Jaime in the field** — Grey Wind growls; Edmure intervenes; Robb angrily tells Edmure not to call him "the boy."
20. **Robb regrets he could not trade Jaime for Ned** — Implies the girls are not equivalent in bargaining value; Catelyn is stung.
21. **Robb suggests Catelyn go to the Twins or with Theon** — To choose his Frey bride or return to Winterfell for Bran and Rickon. Catelyn refuses both.
22. **Catelyn warns against sending Theon to Pyke** — Suggests Jason Mallister, Tytos Blackwood, or Stevron Frey instead. Notes Balon Greyjoy wore a crown and may want one again.
23. **Robb dismisses Catelyn's counsel on Theon** — Says Theon fought bravely, saved Bran, and he needs Greyjoy longships. Walks off with Grey Wind.
24. **Catelyn decides to visit her dying father** — Asks Edmure to come; he declines, says he needs to see Ser Desmond's bowmen.
25. **Catelyn walks through the godswood to the central keep** — Notes the trees still have leaves despite autumn being declared.
26. **Catelyn finds Hoster Tully sleeping in his solar** — He is frail, white-haired, dying; moans and rolls in his sleep.
27. **Brynden Blackfish is at Hoster's bedside** — Just returned from scouting, still in mail and travel-stained cloak.
28. **Brynden reports on Hoster's condition** — Catelyn tells him Hoster sleeps most of the time, speaks with less sense, sometimes doesn't recognize her, once called her by her mother's name.
29. **Brynden says Catelyn has her mother's face** — In cheekbones and jaw.
30. **Brynden reports war news** — Riverlands awash in blood and flame around Gods Eye; fighting spread south to Blackwater and north to the Twins; Marq Piper and Karyl Vance winning small victories; Beric Dondarrion raiding Lannister foragers.
31. **Brynden reports Ser Burton Crakehall killed** — Boasted of slaying Dondarrion, then led his column into Dondarrion's trap.
32. **Brynden reports bannerman losses** — Jonos Bracken wounded, nephew Hendry slain; Tytos Blackwood's lands scorched; Darry retaken then lost to Gregor Clegane who killed entire garrison including boy lord Darry, last of his line.
33. **Catelyn and Brynden discuss Tywin's strategy** — Tywin sits safe at Harrenhal, using terror to provoke battle; has loosed Clegane, Amory Lorch, and a sellsword from Qohor.
34. **Brynden reports new Lannister host at Casterly Rock** — Under Ser Stafford Lannister, cousin to Tywin, brother to Tywin's late wife; his son Ser Daven is more formidable.
35. **Catelyn and Brynden discuss the comet** — Visible by day; Greatjon calls it red flag of vengeance for Ned; Edmure sees Tully fish; Brynden says it's blood.
36. **Catelyn proposes seeking alliance with Renly** — To force Tywin from Harrenhal; Brynden notes Renly will want homage.

- **Emotional state:** Grief-stricken and weary (mourning Ned, sometimes wakes expecting him beside her); anxious for Sansa and Arya; frustrated by Robb's refusal to trade Jaime; conflicted between pride in her son and anguish at his choices; guilt over having urged Ned to go south ("my doing, mine, no other"); fearful about the war's trajectory
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting Sansa and Arya back safely; the political and military situation; Robb's stubbornness and youth; her dying father
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to go to the Twins or return to Winterfell; stays at Riverrun to be with her dying father; the chapter ends with the implicit decision to seek out Renly Baratheon as an ally
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn blames herself entirely for Ned's death ("Go, I told him... my doing, mine, no other") — she takes sole responsibility while ignoring that many factors contributed. She also thinks "Kings are not supposed to have mothers" as rationalization for Robb pushing her away, when the tension is more about policy disagreement.
