# ACOK — Sansa I

## Chapter Metadata

- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Outer bailey (tourney grounds)
- **approximate_timeline:** Early in ACOK; Joffrey's name day, shortly after news of Robb's proclamation as King in the North
- **time_markers:** Morning ("The morning of King Joffrey's name day dawned bright and windy"); the great comet is visible; the gates of the Red Keep have been closed for days due to troubles in the city
- **chapter_summary:** On King Joffrey's name day, Sansa is escorted by Ser Arys Oakheart to a small tourney held in the Red Keep's outer bailey. The tourney is poorly attended and features weak competitors. When the drunken Ser Dontos Hollard appears half-naked and unable to mount his horse, Joffrey orders him drowned in a cask of wine. Sansa intervenes, claiming it is ill luck to kill on a name day, and the Hound supports her claim. Joffrey instead makes Dontos his fool. The tourney is cut short, and Tyrion Lannister arrives at the Red Keep with a ragged column of sellswords and clansmen, greeting the royal children and exchanging barbed words with Joffrey before departing to meet the queen and council.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa watches the comet** — From her tower window on the morning of Joffrey's name day, Sansa observes the great comet. Ser Arys Oakheart arrives to escort her and claims the comet heralds Joffrey's glory.
2. **Sansa prepares carefully** — She dresses in pale purple silk with a moonstone hair net, choosing long sleeves to hide bruises inflicted at Joffrey's command by Ser Boros after Robb's proclamation.
3. **Ser Arys shares gossip** — While escorting Sansa, he reveals that Cersei is furious because Lord Tywin has gone to ground at Harrenhal rather than bringing his army to King's Landing. The queen will not attend the tourney due to an urgent council meeting.
4. **Arrival at the tourney** — Sansa and Ser Arys reach the outer bailey where a poor tourney has been assembled. The crowd is thin, mostly guardsmen.
5. **Sansa greets the royal family** — Tommen excitedly tells Sansa he will ride in the tourney. Joffrey is pleased she wore the moonstone hair net.
6. **Joffrey tells Sansa of Viserys's death** — The king recounts how the Dothraki crowned Viserys with molten gold. He threatens to feed Robb to wolves and claims he will challenge Robb to single combat.
7. **Ser Meryn defeats Ser Hobber Redwyne** — First tilt; Ser Meryn unhorses Hobber on the second pass. Ser Horas helps his brother from the field.
8. **Ser Balon Swann defeats Morros Slynt** — Morros cannot manage his lance and shield; he is unhorsed and dragged by his horse, bloodied but alive. Joffrey mocks him.
9. **Ser Horas Redwyne defeats an elderly knight** — Horas vanquishes an old man with silver griffin heraldry. Joffrey calls the show feeble.
10. **Ser Dontos appears drunk and half-naked** — Called to face Lothor Brune, Dontos enters on foot, wearing only breastplate and helm, unable to mount his horse. He sits in the dirt and declares "I lose."
11. **Joffrey orders Dontos drowned** — The king demands a cask of wine to drown Dontos in.
12. **Sansa intervenes** — She blurts out that it would be ill luck to kill on a name day. The Hound supports her claim. Joffrey relents.
13. **Dontos is made court fool** — Sansa suggests Dontos is better suited as a fool than a knight. Joffrey agrees and declares Dontos his new fool, to dress in motley and sleep with Moon Boy.
14. **Joffrey ends the tourney** — Disgusted with the quality of the competitors, Joffrey cancels the remaining tilts.
15. **Tommen rides against the quintain** — Myrcella and Tommen insist he be allowed to ride as Cersei promised. Tommen strikes the quintain but is knocked from his pony by the padded mace. He gets up to try again.
16. **Sansa advises Joffrey to comfort Tommen** — Joffrey refuses, saying falling in dirt is not riding well. The Hound notes Tommen's courage.
17. **Tyrion Lannister arrives** — A column of riders enters the Red Keep: Lannister men-at-arms, freeriders, sellswords, and wild mountain clansmen. Tyrion rides among them on a tall red horse, arm in a sling, beard grown out.
18. **Tyrion greets the royal children** — Tommen gallops to greet him; a hairy clansman lifts Tommen from his saddle. Tyrion and Tommen are the same height. Tyrion picks up Myrcella and spins her.
19. **Tyrion exchanges words with Joffrey** — Tyrion kneels, Joffrey is cold. Tyrion makes a pointed remark about Robert's death. Joffrey complains about Jaime's capture and Robb's rebellion.
20. **Tyrion offers condolences to Sansa** — He says he is sorry for her losses. Sansa does not know how to respond.
21. **Tyrion departs to meet the council** — He squeezes Sansa's hand, vows he will not savage her, and leaves. Sansa resolves never to trust a Lannister again.
22. **The Hound warns Tyrion** — Before departing, Clegane tells Tyrion to guard his tongue.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear, anxiety, revulsion at Joffrey's touch, moments of desperate courage, deep grief for her father, longing for home and family (Bran), wariness and distrust of all Lannisters, grim resolve
- **Primary preoccupation:** Surviving Joffrey's unpredictable cruelty; managing her outward appearance of loyalty while internally loathing her situation
- **Key decisions made:** Intervenes to save Ser Dontos from execution (impulsive, then improvises the name-day superstition and the fool suggestion); resolves never to trust a Lannister again after Tyrion's kind words
- **Self-deception flags:** None — Sansa is clear-eyed about her situation. She explicitly acknowledges that her declaration of loyalty to Joffrey is a learned reflex, not belief. She recognizes her own moment of "queer giddy courage" and is surprised by it.
