- **chapter_summary:** Arya recalls the eight days of imprisonment at the storehouse beside the Gods Eye, where the Mountain's men tortured and killed prisoners daily for information about Lord Beric Dondarrion. The captives are marched toward Harrenhal to serve Lord Tywin Lannister, enduring killings, rapes, and brutality along the way. Arya develops a nightly ritual of reciting the names of those she hates. Upon arrival at Harrenhal, the captives are processed in a bathhouse, Arya adopts the name "Weasel," and she is assigned to serve under Weese in the Wailing Tower.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya recalls the storehouse imprisonment** — Eight days at the storehouse beside the Gods Eye; the Mountain selects prisoners daily for the Tickler's questioning; all die.
2. **Girl sharing soldier's bed is picked** — A girl who slept with a soldier for three nights is chosen on the fourth day; the soldier says nothing.
3. **All-for-Joffrey picked on the fifth day** — A smiley old man who constantly proclaimed loyalty to Joffrey is selected for questioning.
4. **Mother offers information to protect daughter** — A pox-scarred woman volunteers information freely; the Mountain picks her daughter the next morning to verify.
5. **Small amounts of gold, silver, and copper found** — Interrogations yield little treasure and contradictory information about Lord Beric.
6. **Bodies hung beyond fires for wolves** — Dead prisoners are displayed after questioning.
7. **The march begins** — Ser Gregor commands the captives to march to Harrenhal with captured livestock and salt fish.
8. **Arya's sex is revealed** — Unable to maintain her disguise on the road, Arya must relieve herself publicly; Hot Pie notices she is a girl, but the soldiers don't care.
9. **Boy of three killed** — A boy won't stop calling for his father; soldiers smash his face with a spiked mace.
10. **Boy's mother killed** — She starts screaming after her son's death; Raff the Sweetling kills her.
11. **Brave woman dies screaming** — One woman picked for questioning tried to be brave but died like all the rest.
12. **Gendry spared for smithing** — His skill as an apprentice smith saves his life.
13. **Girl beheaded by Ser Gregor** — A pretty girl taken by four or five men nightly hits one with a rock; Gregor beheads her with his greatsword in front of all captives.
14. **Arya develops her nightly kill list** — She recites names every night: Ser Gregor, Dunsen, Polliver, Chiswyck, Raff the Sweetling, the Tickler, the Hound, Ser Amory, Ser Ilyn, Ser Meryn, King Joffrey, Queen Cersei.
15. **Old women discuss the war** — Captives whisper about Beric, Tullys, Lannisters, and Starks; an old man praises King Aerys and loses his teeth when a guard hears.
16. **Captives arrive at Lannister encampment and Harrenhal** — After days of marching, they reach the army camp west of the castle and enter through the gatehouse.
17. **Captives processed in the bathhouse** — Stripped, scrubbed in scalding water; supervised by Goodwife Amabel and Goodwife Harra.
18. **Arya adopts the name "Weasel"** — She cannot use her real name or "Arry"; names herself after the orphan girl.
19. **Arya requests stable work** — She asks to tend horses, thinking she could steal one to escape.
20. **Goodwife Harra slaps Arya** — For speaking out of turn; her swollen lip breaks open again.
21. **Arya assigned to Weese** — The goodwives send her to Weese, understeward of the Wailing Tower, instead of the kitchens.
22. **Weese addresses new servants** — Lectures them on obedience, not looking highborn in the eye, and boasts he can smell defiance.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pervasive fear throughout; self-hatred for her passivity and perceived cowardice; hatred toward her captors crystallizing into a structured ritual; resignation mixed with survival instinct; sullen defiance suppressed by violence
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival; cataloging who is dangerous and who is less so; maintaining her concealed identity; nurturing her list of names as a form of psychological resistance
- **Key decisions made:** Adopts the alias "Weasel" at Harrenhal; asks for stable duty (hoping to steal a horse — denied); does not fight back when slapped, choosing survival over defiance
- **Self-deception flags:** Arya tells herself "Fear cuts deeper than swords" (Syrio's lesson) but acknowledges it doesn't make the fear go away; she judges herself against Syrio's standard and concludes she is "no water dancer" — she measures herself against an idealized standard rather than accepting the reality of her situation; she thinks "It's good that they took [Needle] away" rationalizing her helplessness as protective
