# ACOK — Davos II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 43
- **pov_character:** Davos Seaworth
- **pov_chapter_number:** Davos II
- **first_available:** ACOK Davos II
- **location_primary:** Storm's End (exterior walls, then Stannis's camp)
- **location_secondary:** Shipbreaker Bay, the sea cavern beneath Storm's End
- **approximate_timeline:** During the siege of Storm's End, after Renly's death and Black Betha's arrival eight days prior; a fortnight after Stannis's initial offer to Ser Cortnay
- **time_markers:** "eight days since Black Betha had joined the rest of the fleet off Storm's End"; "You have had a fortnight to consider my offer"; morning sun during the parley; "one hour hence" for war council; the smuggling mission takes place at night, during high tide; "sixteen years before" references Davos's original smuggling run during the earlier siege; "a moon ago they were Renly's"; "Since Lord Renly died" — recent event; dawn was nigh when Renly died
- **chapter_summary:** Davos accompanies King Stannis and his lords to a parley beneath the walls of Storm's End, where Ser Cortnay Penrose refuses to surrender the castle or hand over Edric Storm, and challenges Stannis to single combat. Stannis refuses and withdraws to camp, where he confides in Davos about the unreliability of his bannermen, his grief over Renly, and Melisandre's visions. Stannis orders Davos to smuggle Melisandre beneath Storm's End by boat that night. In a sea cavern beneath the castle, Melisandre gives birth to a shadow creature that passes through the portcullis — a shadow Davos recognizes.

## Events & Actions
1. **Parley at Storm's End** — Stannis and his lords ride to the walls of Storm's End to meet Ser Cortnay Penrose. Ser Cortnay refuses to yield the castle or surrender Edric Storm.
2. **Challenge to single combat** — Ser Cortnay throws his glove at Stannis, challenging him to single combat or to name a champion. Ser Guyard Morrigen and Lord Caron volunteer, but Stannis refuses.
3. **Debate over Renly's death** — The lords argue over who killed Renly. Ser Guyard and Lord Caron blame Brienne of Tarth. Lord Florent accuses Catelyn Stark. Ser Cortnay defends Brienne and shames the Rainbow Guard for failing to protect Renly.
4. **Stannis threatens storm** — Stannis warns he will take the castle by storm with no mercy. Ser Cortnay invokes the castle's name as defiance and rides back through the gate.
5. **Ride back to camp** — Lord Estermont, Ser Jon Fossoway, and Lord Caron debate strategy as they return. Stannis silences them and summons Davos to ride alongside him.
6. **Stannis confides in Davos** — Stannis and Davos discuss the loyalty of his bannermen, Ser Cortnay's stubbornness, and Renly's death. Stannis admits he loved Renly and is haunted by the peach.
7. **Stannis describes his dream of Renly's death** — He recounts dreaming of a green tent, candles, a woman screaming, and blood, while Renly died. He insists he was asleep and his hands were clean.
8. **Discussion of propaganda** — Stannis asks how the smallfolk received news of Cersei's incest and is frustrated that the tale of Selyse and Patchface has spread.
9. **Private conference in pavilion** — Stannis dismisses the lords and brings only Davos into his tent. He predicts how his war council will go and asks Davos for advice.
10. **Davos advises striking King's Landing** — Davos urges bypassing Storm's End to attack King's Landing directly, but Stannis insists the castle must fall quickly to prevent appearing defeated.
11. **Stannis reveals the strategic situation** — Doran Martell has fortified the mountain passes; Highgarden retains strength; Renly's sixty thousand foot at Bitterbridge may be lost to Ser Loras; Ser Errol and Ser Parmen sent to claim them have not returned.
12. **Stannis reveals Melisandre's prophecy** — He tells Davos that Ser Cortnay will be dead within the day, as Melisandre has seen it in her flames, and insists he must have Edric Storm.
13. **Stannis orders the smuggling mission** — He commands Davos to land a small boat beneath the castle unseen, at night. Davos reluctantly agrees.
14. **Night crossing of Shipbreaker Bay** — Davos sails Melisandre across the bay in a small boat with a black sail, threading through rocks toward the cave mouth beneath Storm's End.
15. **Conversation at sea** — Melisandre and Davos discuss good and evil; Davos accuses her of killing Maester Cressen and Renly; she denies killing Renly but laughs; she explains shadows are servants of light.
16. **Entry into the sea cavern** — Davos navigates the boat through the treacherous cave passage during high tide, past jagged rocks, into total darkness beneath the castle walls, reaching the portcullis.
17. **Melisandre births the shadow** — Melisandre disrobes, revealing she is hugely pregnant. She gives birth to a shadow creature that is tall as the tunnel, with black grasping fingers. The shadow passes through the portcullis bars and races across the water. Davos recognizes whose shadow it is.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply conflicted and increasingly distressed. Begins feeling out of place among highborn lords, grows sad and disturbed as Stannis reveals the plan, and feels outright moral revulsion during the night mission. "I never felt evil until tonight."
- **Primary preoccupation:** The wrongness of what Stannis is asking him to do — he is a knight now, not a smuggler or assassin. Also concerned about the reliability of Stannis's bannermen and what Melisandre has done to the king.
- **Key decisions made:** Despite deep misgivings, Davos agrees to smuggle Melisandre beneath Storm's End. He tries to argue for alternatives but ultimately obeys.
- **Self-deception flags:** Davos tells himself "It is not for me to question the king's commands" while clearly questioning them. He notes Stannis's insistence that his hands were clean regarding Renly's death, and his phantom fingertips itch — he senses something deeply wrong but does not push further. He rationalizes obedience through loyalty and his sons' futures.
