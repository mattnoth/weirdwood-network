- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn and Brienne eat supper alone in Riverrun's Great Hall while the garrison celebrates Edmure's return and Robb's conquest of the Crag. Catelyn reveals to Brienne that a raven from Ser Rodrik reports Bran and Rickon have been killed by Theon Greyjoy. After sitting vigil with her dying father Lord Hoster, Catelyn descends to the dungeons at midnight with Brienne to interrogate Jaime Lannister. Jaime confirms he is the father of Cersei's children and that he pushed Bran from the window, but denies sending the catspaw assassin with the Valyrian steel dagger. He recounts the gruesome deaths of Rickard and Brandon Stark at the hands of Aerys Targaryen. The chapter ends with Catelyn calling for Brienne's sword.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn and Brienne eat supper alone** — The Great Hall is empty; servants given leave to join the celebration outside. Deep shadows and only three torches.
2. **Garrison celebrates** — Ser Desmond has brought twenty casks of ale; smallfolk celebrate Edmure's imminent return and Robb's conquest of the Crag.
3. **Catelyn reveals Bran and Rickon's reported deaths** — She tells Brienne about the raven from Ser Rodrik at Castle Cerwyn: Bran and Rickon tried to escape, were taken at a mill on the Acorn Water, and Theon Greyjoy has mounted their heads on Winterfell's walls.
4. **Catelyn describes her daughters** — She speaks lovingly of Sansa and Arya; says she thinks Arya must be dead; declares she wants Theon, Jaime, Cersei, and Tyrion all dead.
5. **Catelyn mentions sending wine to Jaime** — She reveals to Brienne she has sent wine to the Kingslayer and asks Brienne to come with her at midnight.
6. **Catelyn visits Lord Hoster** — She climbs to her father's solar; Maester Vyman reports he was given dreamwine for pain and will not know she is there. Vyman offers a sleeping draught; Catelyn declines.
7. **Catelyn sits vigil with Lord Hoster** — She holds his hand, speaks to him about having no one to talk to, remembers getting lost with Lysa riding from Seagard and Petyr finding them. She speaks of wanting the war to end, wanting her children back.
8. **Catelyn hears Rymund singing** — From the yard: "I loved a maid as red as autumn, with sunset in her hair."
9. **Brienne arrives at midnight** — She comes to the door of the solar as arranged.
10. **Catelyn confronts the gaoler** — He initially refuses entry citing Edmure's orders requiring his sealed writing. Catelyn threatens to take him to Lord Hoster. He relents.
11. **Catelyn enters Jaime's cell** — She takes the oil lamp, turns up the flame. The wine flagon is untouched. The cell is foul, dark, with straw, nitre, and overflowing waste.
12. **Jaime and Catelyn exchange barbs** — He comments on her appearance, makes crude sexual suggestions, and rattles his chains. She finds him vile but still powerful and beautiful.
13. **Catelyn proposes a bargain** — She offers information in exchange for answers. Jaime agrees to trade answers.
14. **Jaime confirms he is Joffrey's father** — He admits Joffrey and all of Cersei's children are his.
15. **Jaime confirms he pushed Bran** — He says plainly "I flung him from a window" and "I meant for him to die" because Bran was spying on him and Cersei.
16. **Jaime denies sending the catspaw** — He says he never hires anyone to do his killing. Claims if he wanted Bran dead he'd have done it himself.
17. **Catelyn and Jaime discuss the Valyrian steel dagger** — Catelyn describes it; Jaime recalls Robert showed it to him at a feast, having won it. Both Jaime and Tyrion (separately) point away from Tyrion as the dagger's wagerer. Catelyn realizes her original understanding may have been wrong.
18. **Information exchange about the war** — Catelyn tells Jaime that Stafford Lannister is dead at Oxcross; Cersei, Tyrion, and Tywin live; Stannis marches on King's Landing; Renly is dead at Bitterbridge; Robb has taken the Crag; Robb recently turned sixteen.
19. **Jaime discusses his vows** — He reflects on the impossibility of keeping all the oaths of knighthood and the Kingsguard simultaneously; says he was the youngest man to wear the white cloak.
20. **Jaime recounts the deaths of Rickard and Brandon Stark** — He describes Aerys's pyromancers roasting Lord Rickard alive in his armor while Brandon strangled himself with a Tyroshi device trying to reach a sword. Jaime stood at the foot of the Iron Throne in his white armor, thinking of Cersei. Gerold Hightower told him afterward: "You swore a vow to guard the king, not to judge him."
21. **Jaime provokes Catelyn about Ned** — He questions Ned's love for Catelyn vs. Robert; references Littlefinger and Jon Snow; says he has been truer than Ned in his own way, having never lain with any woman but Cersei.
22. **Catelyn calls for Brienne's sword** — She calls Brienne into the cell and demands her sword. The chapter ends.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deep grief over Bran and Rickon's reported deaths; bitter, self-described as "a creature of grief and dust and bitter longings" with "an empty place within me where my heart was once"; murderous rage toward Theon, Jaime, Cersei, and Tyrion; fear for Sansa and Arya; exhaustion and isolation; revulsion toward Jaime mixed with reluctant acknowledgment of his beauty; growing doubt about Littlefinger's account of the dagger
- **Primary preoccupation:** The deaths of Bran and Rickon; her helplessness; the fate of her remaining children; extracting information from Jaime
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to interrogate Jaime at midnight despite Edmure's orders against visitors; overrides the gaoler's objection by invoking Lord Hoster's authority; at chapter's end, calls for Brienne's sword — an impulsive decision whose purpose is left ambiguous
- **Self-deception flags:** "I have said it and made it true" — Catelyn treats the act of speaking the news as what makes it real, suggesting she was trying to deny it by not saying it; she clings to grief as duty ("I will not sleep away my grief. Bran and Rickon deserve better from me"); she begins to doubt Littlefinger's story about the dagger but resists ("Somewhere there was a trap here")
