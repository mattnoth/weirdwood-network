# ACOK — Bran II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 17
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran II
- **first_available:** ACOK Bran II
- **location_primary:** Winterfell
- **location_secondary:** White Harbor (mentioned), the Twins (mentioned), the Dreadfort (mentioned), Deepwood Motte (mentioned), Torrhen's Square (mentioned), Harrenhal (mentioned), Eastwatch (mentioned), King's Landing (mentioned), Dragonstone (mentioned), Widow's Watch (mentioned), Bay of Seals (mentioned), Last River (mentioned), Whispering Wood (mentioned), Green Fork (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** During the harvest feast at Winterfell; several days pass across the chapter as different lords arrive and are given audience
- **time_markers:** "two days past" (Lord Manderly's arrival before the first audience day); "midday came and went" (during Manderly audience); evening arrival of Lady Hornwood ("that night, as he sat to supper, a horn sounded"); "the next day" and "the morrow" (Lady Hornwood's audience); "the following day" (Leobald Tallhart's audience); "in the days that followed" (ravens arriving); "one bright, blustery morning" (Cley Cerwyn's arrival); "that night" (Bran's nightmare); "long before the first pale fingers of light" (chapter opening, pre-dawn); "a few hours of daylight left" (after Lady Hornwood's audience)
- **chapter_summary:** Bran presides over the harvest feast audiences at Winterfell in his father's solar, acting as the Stark in Winterfell. Lord Wyman Manderly proposes new customs officers, a royal mint, and a warfleet for Robb. Lady Hornwood raises the threat of Roose Bolton's bastard at the Dreadfort and the question of who will inherit her lands, which becomes the central political problem as multiple lords make competing claims. Cley Cerwyn arrives with news that Stannis Baratheon has declared himself king and accused Cersei and Jaime of incest, which triggers a panic attack in Bran. The chapter closes with Bran dreaming of the three-eyed crow pecking out his eyes and reliving his fall from the tower.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** One morning described as "bright, blustery" (Cley Cerwyn's arrival)
- **Season indicators:** Maesters in the Citadel have proclaimed the first of autumn; harvest feast underway; lords discuss storing portions of the harvest for winter; a hedge wizard predicted "a bountiful spirit summer before the cold set in"
- **Time of day:** Opens pre-dawn ("long before the first pale fingers of light"); progresses through morning (quintain practice), midday (dining during Manderly audience), afternoon (visiting godswood), evening (Lady Hornwood's arrival at supper), night (dreams); spans multiple days
- **Lighting:** Pre-dawn darkness in Bran's chamber at opening; daylight in the courtyard; natural light through windows of the solar
- **Sounds:** Clash of sword and shield and horse in the yard; ring of steel on steel from squires at swordplay; blast of horns announcing Umber arrival; horn heralding Lady Hornwood's arrival; horses and boisterous shouting from arriving Umbers
- **Smells:** Bread baking from the kitchens (recalled memory of Hodor running to the kitchens)
- **Notable sensory details:** Bran feels the rough stone ceiling above his head while lying in bed; runs his hand across the rough wood of the plank-and-trestle table; his shoulders grow stiff from sitting in the same chair; water ripples in the godswood pool with no wind; Osha's skin bumpy with gooseprickles from the cold pool; the pain in Bran's dream described as "an axe splitting his head apart"

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran wakes before dawn** — lies awake dreading the day of audiences, wishing he could joust instead of playing the prince
2. **Hodor dresses and carries Bran** — Bran wears a white wool doublet and silver brooch; Hodor carries him in the wicker basket down the winding stair
3. **Quintain practice in the yard** — The Walders and White Harbor squires tilt at quintains painted with Lannister lions; Bran watches from Hodor's back
4. **Walders mock Hodor** — Little Walder calls Hodor an ugly horse; both Freys taunt Hodor's intelligence; Bran threatens them with Summer
5. **Maester Luwin intervenes** — scolds the Freys for cruelty toward Hodor; warns Little Walder to stay out of the godswood and away from the wolves
6. **Luwin rebukes Bran** — tells Bran he should not have been in the yard; Ser Rodrik and Lord Wyman have been waiting
7. **Manderly audience begins** — Bran sits in his father's oak chair; Lord Manderly proposes new customs officers for White Harbor, replacing those sending silver to King's Landing
8. **Manderly proposes a royal mint** — suggests White Harbor as the site to mint King Robb's coinage
9. **Manderly proposes a warfleet** — offers to build galleys sufficient to take Dragonstone and King's Landing, citing Brandon the Burner's destruction of the old Stark fleet; Ser Rodrik promises to relay it to Robb
10. **Midday meal** — they dine on cheese, capons, and brown oatbread in the solar
11. **Manderly raises Lady Hornwood's situation** — inquires after her as a cousin; proposes himself or his son Wendel as husband
12. **Manderly reveals Tywin's letter** — Lord Tywin offers to return Ser Wylis from Harrenhal without ransom if Manderly withdraws from the war; Manderly declares his loyalty but hopes for a captive exchange
13. **Lady Hornwood arrives at supper** — comes with only six tired men-at-arms with moosehead badges on dusty orange livery; Bran offers condolences for her husband and son
14. **Lady Hornwood's audience** — discusses harvest storage (a fifth, increased to a quarter at Luwin's suggestion); warns of Bolton's bastard massing men at the Dreadfort
15. **Lady Hornwood describes Bolton's bastard** — tells of Ramsay's cruelty, his servant Reek, their hunting of people; says the bastard looks hungrily at her lands
16. **Ser Rodrik suggests Lady Hornwood remarry** — she rejects Mors Crowfood and Lord Manderly; hints she fancies Ser Rodrik himself
17. **Succession debate** — Luwin, Ser Rodrik, and Bran discuss claimants to Hornwood lands: Tallharts, Flints, Karstarks, Glovers (fostering the bastard Larence Snow), Bolton
18. **Bran suggests legitimizing Lord Hornwood's bastard** — thinking of Jon Snow
19. **Bran visits the godswood** — Hodor carries him to the pool beneath the heart tree; Summer emerges from under an oak; Shaggydog is glimpsed but vanishes
20. **Osha surfaces from the godswood pool** — she has been swimming naked; startles Summer and Hodor
21. **Osha talks to Bran** — mentions kitchen gossip about Bran defending Hodor from the Freys; warns that Little Walder is mean to the bones; asks about Bran's wolf dreams; Bran denies having them
22. **Umber party arrives** — announced by horns at night; banner shows a giant in shattered chains
23. **Mors and Hother Umber audience** — Mors asks to wed Lady Hornwood; Hother reports wildling incursions across the Bay of Seals and requests ships
24. **Ser Rodrik proposes Manderly-Umber cooperation on shipbuilding** — Mors dismisses Manderly as "Lord Lamprey" but the Umbers are made to comply
25. **Glover party arrives from Deepwood Motte** — steward comes instead of Lady Glover; admits only setting aside a tenth of harvest; Ser Rodrik commands a fifth; steward praises Larence Snow
26. **Tallhart party arrives from Torrhen's Square** — Leobald tells of nephew Benfred's unauthorized company of young lances, the "Wild Hares"
27. **Leobald proposes fostering his son Beren with Lady Hornwood** — suggests the boy could take the Hornwood name and be named heir; Bran speaks up to accept the proposal for consideration
28. **Maester Luwin praises Bran** — tells him he will be a good lord; Bran insists Robb will have sons who will inherit
29. **Ser Rodrik reflects on mortality and succession** — tells Bran of his three wives who gave only daughters, and his brother Martyn's line ending with Jory's death
30. **Ravens arrive from absent lords** — Bolton's bastard declines; Mormonts and Karstarks are south with Robb; Lord Locke too old; Lady Flint heavy with child; sickness at Widow's Watch; Howland Reed never heard from
31. **Cley Cerwyn arrives** — fourteen-year-old lord arrives with two dozen lances; greets Bran cheerfully while Bran rides Dancer
32. **Cley reveals Stannis's accusation** — tells Bran that Stannis claims Cersei bedded Jaime and Joffrey is a bastard; Cerwyn knights discuss it
33. **Bran has a panic attack** — hearing of incest and being called a bastard triggers a visceral reaction; feels as if he cannot breathe, a giant hand crushing his chest, as though he is falling
34. **Bran has the three-eyed crow nightmare** — the crow pecks out both his eyes, then drives its beak into his skull; Bran can then see himself clinging to a tower; a golden man pulls him up and throws him into empty air, saying "The things I do for love"

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Frustrated and resentful at being confined to lordly duties when he wants to ride and fight; defensive about Hodor; ashamed when Maester Luwin rebukes him; briefly proud when praised; triggered into terror by the incest accusation (which echoes his own fall); tormented by nightmares
- **Primary preoccupation:** The gap between what he wants (knighthood, physical ability) and what he must do (act as lord); he constantly hears the sounds of practice in the yard and wishes he were there
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to defend Hodor against the Walders; suggests legitimizing Lord Hornwood's bastard (thinking of Jon); speaks up to accept Leobald Tallhart's proposal on behalf of Winterfell; fights against sleep to avoid wolf dreams
- **Self-deception flags:** Denies having wolf dreams when Osha asks ("No." — Osha immediately identifies this as a lie); tells himself he'll just have "a quick look" at the quintain practice when he knows he's supposed to go straight to the audience
