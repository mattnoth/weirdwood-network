She prays in the crowded sept, singing hymns to the Mother. As sounds of battle reach her, she crosses to Maegor's Holdfast where the highborn women are sheltered. She discovers Ser Ilyn Payne standing guard with Ice, and Cersei explains he is there to deal with treason. Cersei begins the evening with cutting remarks about the unreliability of their guards and the realities of a city being sacked.

## Events & Actions
1. **Singing in the sept** — The castle's inhabitants have been singing hymns since the first report of enemy sails, praying to the Mother for mercy.
2. **Joffrey departs for battle** — Joffrey mounts his blood bay horse in gilded armor. Ser Meryn Trant holds his horse. Tyrion is also mounted, armored, with a battle-axe.
3. **Tyrion speaks to Sansa** — Tyrion tells Sansa she should be safe in Maegor's and says he should have sent her with Tommen. He says the day may change things for her as well as for House Lannister.
4. **Joffrey summons Sansa** — Joffrey calls Sansa over and shows her his new sword Hearteater, commanding her to kiss the blade. She complies.
5. **Sansa provokes Joffrey** — Sansa tells Joffrey that Robb always goes where the fighting is thickest, calling Robb "a man grown." Joffrey frowns and threatens to gut Robb with Hearteater.
6. **Troops depart** — Joffrey, Ser Meryn, Ser Osmund, gold cloaks, Tyrion, and Ser Mandon Moore ride out through the gates. Guards cheer them off. Sudden stillness falls.
7. **Sansa goes to the sept** — She joins the crowded sept where rainbow light pours through crystal windows and candles burn everywhere. She lights a candle at each of the Seven altars.
8. **Sansa sings the hymn** — She joins the singing, a hymn to the Mother ("Gentle Mother, font of mercy") that her mother taught her in Winterfell.
9. **Sansa prays for many people** — She sings for family members (Bran, Rickon, Robb, Arya, Jon, Catelyn, Eddard implied through others), for friends and retainers (Jeyne Poole, Ser Dontos, Jory Cassel, Maester Luwin, Septa Mordane), for Robert, and finally even for Tyrion and the Hound.
10. **Sansa leaves the sept in anger** — When the septon prays for Joffrey's victory, Sansa walks out, thinking coldly "Let his sword break and his shield shatter."
11. **Sounds of battle reach Sansa** — She hears warhorns, catapults, burning pitch, scorpions, and dying men from outside the castle.
12. **Encounter with the Stokeworths** — At the drawbridge to Maegor's Holdfast, Sansa meets Lady Tanda, Falyse, and Lollys. Lollys is sobbing and refusing to cross the bridge. Shae and Falyse half-drag her across.
13. **Sansa enters the Queen's Ballroom** — She notes the room's mirrors, carved wood, rushes, music, and draped windows. Almost every highborn woman in the city is present, along with some old men and young boys.
14. **Sansa sees Ser Ilyn with Ice** — She spots Ser Ilyn Payne standing in shadows holding her father's greatsword Ice.
15. **Osfryd explains Ser Ilyn's presence** — He says the queen expects to have need of the headsman before the night is done.
16. **Cersei arrives and is announced** — The royal steward announces her as "Queen Regent and Protector of the Realm." She wears a white gown with diamonds and emeralds.
17. **Cersei questions Sansa about her "red flower"** — Cersei asks if Sansa's menstrual period is still occurring. Sansa confirms. Cersei remarks "The men will bleed out there, and you in here."
18. **Cersei explains Ser Ilyn's role** — She says he is there "to deal with treason" and to defend them. She notes her sellsword guards are unreliable and would flee if the battle is lost.
19. **Cersei mocks Sansa's idealism** — When Sansa says "True knights would never harm women and children," Cersei sarcastically invokes Symeon Star-Eyes and Prince Aemon the Dragonknight.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fearful but controlled; cycling between dread, defiance, and resignation. Shows flashes of quiet hatred for Joffrey and moments of compassion (praying even for the Hound and Tyrion). Growing awareness of real danger.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The battle's outcome and what it means for her survival. The presence of Ser Ilyn with her father's sword creates acute dread.
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to provoke Joffrey by comparing him to Robb ("a man grown"); leaves the sept when the septon prays for Joffrey; prays for the Hound despite his not being a knight.
- **Self-deception flags:** Says "True knights would never harm women and children" but immediately recognizes her own words ring hollow — she is aware this is self-deception even as she says it.
