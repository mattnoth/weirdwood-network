# ACOK — Davos I

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 11
- **pov_character:** Davos Seaworth
- **pov_chapter_number:** Davos I
- **first_available:** ACOK Davos I
- **location_primary:** Dragonstone (castle gates, port, inn on stone pier, Chamber of the Painted Table in the Stone Drum)
- **location_secondary:** King's Landing (discussed), Storm's End (discussed), Lys (referenced), Flea Bottom (recalled)
- **approximate_timeline:** Early in ACOK; Stannis preparing letters to declare his claim and planning distribution by ship; Renly has left Highgarden and is marching up the Roseroad
- **time_markers:** Morning (burning of the Seven); "well after dark" when Devan summons Davos; a year ago Davos was with Stannis in King's Landing for Joffrey's name day tourney; Salladhor Saan's Bird of Thousand Colors arrived "yesterday"; Salladhor mentions Stannis will owe another thirty thousand dragons "come the black of the moon"
- **chapter_summary:** Davos witnesses the burning of the statues of the Seven on the shore outside Dragonstone, where Melisandre declares Stannis to be Azor Ahai reborn after he pulls a sword from the pyre. Davos then visits an inn at the port where Salladhor Saan shares intelligence about King's Landing's weak defenses and Renly's march north with his host. That evening, Davos is summoned to the Chamber of the Painted Table where Stannis orders him to distribute letters declaring his claim to the Iron Throne and Cersei's children's illegitimacy. Davos counsels Stannis against using R'hllor's name in the letters, and Stannis explains his pragmatic embrace of Melisandre's power.

## Events & Actions
1. **Burning of the Seven** — The statues of the Seven, carved from ancient Targaryen ship masts, are burned outside the castle gates of Dragonstone at Melisandre's direction. Hundreds witness the ceremony.
2. **Melisandre's ceremony** — Melisandre walks around the fire three times, praying in the tongue of Asshai, High Valyrian, and the Common Tongue, calling on R'hllor to accept the "false gods."
3. **Stannis draws the sword** — Stannis strides into the fire with a padded glove and leather cloak, pulls a longsword from the burning Mother's heart, and retreats with the blade wreathed in jade-green flames. His glove begins to smolder and he thrusts the sword into the earth.
4. **Melisandre proclaims Azor Ahai reborn** — Melisandre declares the sword is Lightbringer and Stannis is Azor Ahai come again. Queen Selyse and the queen's men echo the proclamation.
5. **Stannis and Selyse withdraw** — Stannis loses patience, takes the queen's elbow, and escorts her back into Dragonstone, leaving Lightbringer stuck in the ground. The squires roll up the now-dimmed sword in the king's burnt leather cloak.
6. **Davos goes to the port** — Davos parts from his sons Allard and Dale and enters the inn on the stone pier to drink.
7. **Meeting with Salladhor Saan** — Salladhor Saan shares intelligence: Tyrion has been sent to King's Landing and replaced the gold cloak commander; the city is poorly defended; Renly has left Highgarden with his queen and host marching up the Roseroad. Saan tells the tale of Lightbringer's forging and says the sword Stannis pulled from the fire is merely burnt, not magical.
8. **Salladhor Saan departs** — Saan reminds Davos that Stannis owes him another thirty thousand dragons by the black of the moon, then leaves for dinner aboard his ship.
9. **Davos reflects on Thoros of Myr** — Davos recalls the tourney a year ago where Thoros fought with a flaming sword that had no true magic; Bronze Yohn Royce defeated him. He thinks about the cost of Nissa Nissa's sacrifice and pictures his own wife Marya.
10. **Devan summons Davos** — After dark, Devan arrives at Black Betha with a white palfrey and summons Davos to the Chamber of the Painted Table.
11. **Davos encounters Ser Axell Florent** — At the Stone Drum, Ser Axell tells Davos he saw dancing maidens in the flames, which he interprets as a vision of glory. Davos deflects and moves on.
12. **Stannis gives Davos the letter** — Stannis shows Davos the letter declaring his claim and the illegitimacy of Cersei's children. Maester Pylos reads it aloud since Davos cannot read. Stannis insists on removing "beloved brother" as a lie and adding "Ser" before Jaime the Kingslayer.
13. **Stannis assigns the distribution mission** — Davos is to sail Black Betha north (Gulltown, the Fingers, Three Sisters, White Harbor) distributing letters. Dale will sail south in Wraith (past Cape Wrath, the Broken Arm, coast of Dorne to the Arbor). Allard will take Lady Marya across the narrow sea to Braavos and the Free Cities.
14. **Davos counsels against R'hllor's name in the letter** — After Pylos leaves, Davos objects to "Done in the Light of the Lord," arguing the people will reject a foreign god. He suggests "Done in the sight of gods and men" instead.
15. **Stannis explains his pragmatic use of Melisandre** — Stannis reveals he stopped believing in gods when his parents drowned on the Windproud. He says he doesn't know Melisandre's god but recognizes her power. He tells the story of Proudwing, his injured goshawk that would never soar, and his great-uncle's advice to try a different bird. He declares R'hllor is his "red hawk."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply uneasy about the burning of the Seven; conflicted between loyalty to Stannis and discomfort with Melisandre's religion; gratitude and obligation toward Stannis for raising him from nothing; isolated among the highborn lords who scorn him; protective of his sons' futures; pragmatic but morally troubled
- **Primary preoccupation:** The tension between duty to Stannis and the wrongness of burning the old gods; whether Stannis can actually win the throne against three rivals with more men and gold; his family's future depending on Stannis's success
- **Key decisions made:** Promises to speak to the king about the water cask issue; advises Stannis privately against using R'hllor's name in the letter; accepts the mission to distribute letters by ship
- **Self-deception flags:** Davos tells Salladhor Saan "King Stannis is my god" as a careful public answer, immediately recognizing it as a dodge rather than his true feeling; he tells himself he is "not made of the stuff of heroes" while actively counseling a king and taking on dangerous missions; he rationalizes Stannis's justice in taking his fingers ("It was just, what he did to me") while the text suggests this is rehearsed conviction rather than spontaneous feeling
