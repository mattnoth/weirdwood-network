- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys, expelled from Xaro's hospitality and threatened by warlocks and Qartheen factions, rides to Qarth's waterfront seeking passage for her khalasar and dragons. She is saved by two strangers — Arstan Whitebeard (an old Westerosi man) and Strong Belwas (a eunuch pit fighter) — who reveal they were sent by Magister Illyrio from Pentos with three ships. Daenerys accepts and orders the ships renamed Vhagar, Meraxes, and Balerion.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dany refuses Qartheen clothing** — She declines the ivory samite gown Irri brings, choosing Dothraki garb instead for the waterfront.
2. **Dany rides out with escort** — She departs on her silver mare with Ser Jorah, Jhogo, and Aggo, leaving Rakharo to guard her people and dragons.
3. **They ride through Qarth's poorer districts** — Pale men watch from arched doorways; the people do not love Dany.
4. **Dany reflects on needing to flee** — She recalls Xaro's warning about warlocks gathering against her, and his final offer of marriage (refused) and demand that she leave.
5. **Xaro offers ships for a dragon** — In recalled conversation, Xaro offers ten ships for one dragon, escalating to thirty. Dany refuses all offers. She calculates a third of all ships in the world as fair price.
6. **Xaro expels Dany** — His seneschal sends increasingly cold messages demanding she leave, stop eating his food, and return his gifts.
7. **Dany reflects on the three treasons** — She identifies Mirri Maz Duur as the first traitor (for blood) but decides Pyat Pree and Xaro do not fit the second and third.
8. **Dany discusses visions with Jorah** — They discuss the three-headed dragon, the Targaryen sigil, Aegon and his sisters, Rhaegar's vision with the babe named Aegon, the song of ice and fire, and the mummer's dragon.
9. **They arrive at the waterfront** — The harbor is described; they pass through the Thirteen's quays to the foreign ships' docks.
10. **Dany is refused by multiple captains** — Captain of Ardent Friend laughs; Lyseni on Trumpeteer mocks her; Silken Spirit says dragons are too dangerous; Lord Faro's Belly will take dragons but not Dothraki; Quicksilver and Greyhound captains are courteous but too expensive; other ships are too small, wrong destination, or unseaworthy.
11. **Jorah spots followers** — Using a brass platter as a mirror, they identify a fat brown man and an old man with a staff following them.
12. **Dany haggles with the brass merchant** — The price drops from thirty honors to two; Jorah buys it.
13. **Assassination attempt by a Sorrowful Man** — A Qartheen man kneels and gives Dany a jewel box; inside the emerald scarab is a manticore. It unfolds with a hiss.
14. **Arstan saves Dany** — The old man knocks the box from her hand with his staff, crushing the manticore. Dany's fingers are hurt but not broken.
15. **Brief melee** — Aggo rides through an eggseller's stall, Jhogo cracks his whip, Jorah hits Belwas with the brass platter, Belwas shoves Jorah down. Weapons are drawn.
16. **Dany stops the fight** — She orders her men to stand down, recognizing Arstan and Belwas as her defenders.
17. **Dany tips the brass merchant** — She gives him a silver for his trouble after the manticore landed on his arm (he soiled himself in terror).
18. **Arstan and Belwas introduce themselves** — Arstan Whitebeard is from the Dornish Marches, squired for Lord Swann's household, now serves Belwas. Belwas is a former pit fighter from Meereen who was sold to Qohor, then Pentos, then sent by Illyrio.
19. **They reveal Illyrio sent three ships** — The cog Saduleon, and galleys Summer Sun and Joso's Prank, to bring Dany to Pentos. Robert is dead, four kings in Westeros, and the realm bleeds.
20. **Dany accepts and renames the ships** — She orders the ships renamed Vhagar, Meraxes, and Balerion, with names painted in golden letters three feet high.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined but beleaguered; defiant when dressing Dothraki; frustrated by her expulsion from Xaro's; anxious about the warlocks; reflective and troubled by the House of the Undying visions she cannot stop thinking about (compared to a tongue returning to a missing tooth); joyful at the news of ships from Illyrio (but keeps it from her face); resolute at the end when renaming the ships
- **Primary preoccupation:** Finding passage out of Qarth — she needs ships for her people, horses, and dragons; secondary preoccupation is the meaning of her visions (three heads, three treasons, song of ice and fire)
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to marry Xaro or trade a dragon for ships; decides to accept Illyrio's ships and leave Qarth; renames the ships after the Conqueror's dragons as a statement of intent
- **Self-deception flags:** None clearly present; she is relatively clear-eyed about her situation and the motives of those around her
