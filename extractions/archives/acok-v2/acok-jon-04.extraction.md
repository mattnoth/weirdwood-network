- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow rides with Lord Commander Mormont and officers to the summit of the Fist of the First Men, where they decide to make camp and await Qhorin Halfhand. Ghost refuses to enter the ringfort, unsettling Jon. The officers debate strategy regarding the Frostfangs, the Milkwater, and Mance Rayder's host. Mormont explains to Jon that Benjen Stark, if alive, will come to them rather than the other way around. After dark, Ghost returns inside the camp and leads Jon down the hill, where Jon discovers a cache of dragonglass weapons and an old warhorn wrapped in a black cloak of the Night's Watch, buried in the earth.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at the Fist** — Jon rides to the summit of the Fist of the First Men with Lord Mormont and officers. Ghost is left below after running off three times during the climb.
2. **Camp decision** — Mormont declares the Fist good ground and orders camp to be made while awaiting Qhorin Halfhand.
3. **Camp construction** — Brothers set up tents, tether horses, cut firewood, dig latrines, and prepare fire-hardened stakes. Mormont orders all ringwall gaps ditched and staked before dark.
4. **Ghost refuses the ringfort** — Jon descends to find Ghost, who comes willingly but balks at entering the stone ring, sniffing at the gap and retreating. Jon cannot force him inside and lets him go.
5. **Jon surveys the landscape** — From atop the ringwall, Jon observes the Milkwater, surrounding forests, stony hills, and the Frostfangs on the horizon. He feels a growing sense of foreboding.
6. **Jon speaks with Sam** — Sam reports he fared well; Jon tells him Mormont plans to wait for the Halfhand. Sam wishes he could release the caged ravens.
7. **Officers' strategy meeting** — Jon prepares hot spiced wine and serves it while Buckwell, Locke, Wythers, and Smallwood debate routes into the Frostfangs. Mormont decides to hold position at the Fist and let the wildlings come to them along the Milkwater.
8. **Smallwood's rangers restricted** — Mormont orders rangers limited to their side of the river until the Halfhand arrives; Smallwood objects but complies.
9. **Jon and Mormont discuss Benjen** — Mormont explains the logic of staying put: Benjen, if alive, will come to them. If dead, he may come as a wight, as Othor and Jafer Flowers did.
10. **Camp at night** — Jon hears laughter, pipes, sees the great campfire and the red comet overhead. He joins Dywen, Edd, Grenn, and Hake by the fire.
11. **Dywen smells "cold"** — Dywen claims the forest smells cold; Jon privately associates the smell with death, remembering the Lord Commander's chambers, and loses his appetite, giving his stew to Grenn.
12. **Wolf howling** — Wolves howl in the darkness; Ghost appears by the fire with glowing red eyes, behaving restlessly.
13. **Ghost leads Jon out of camp** — Ghost leads Jon past the ringwall (Jon lies to the guard about fetching water) and down the hill into the forest with a torch.
14. **Discovery of the cache** — Ghost digs furiously behind a fallen tree. Jon unearths a bundle wrapped in a black Night's Watch cloak containing dragonglass weapons (knives, spearheads, arrowheads, a dagger blade) and an old warhorn made from an auroch's horn banded in bronze.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Growing foreboding and unease throughout the chapter; disquiet he doesn't share with Sam; alarm when Ghost behaves strangely at night; determination mixed with self-doubt as he follows Ghost into the dark
- **Primary preoccupation:** The safety of their position at the Fist; the fate of his uncle Benjen Stark; Ghost's unsettled behavior and what it means
- **Key decisions made:** Lets Ghost roam free rather than force him into the ringfort; decides not to share his unease with Sam; lies to the guard to follow Ghost outside the camp; follows Ghost into the dark forest alone
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself "They ought to be safe here" while simultaneously feeling deep foreboding; tells himself "Stop acting the boy" to suppress legitimate instincts
