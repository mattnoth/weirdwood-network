- **chapter_summary:** Arya travels north on the kingsroad disguised as an orphan boy named "Arry," part of Yoren's group of recruits and prisoners bound for the Wall. Yoren punishes her with three strokes of the stick and reveals that Lord Eddard was supposed to take the black and travel with them, but "something went queer." That night Arya sees the great red comet and thinks of her father's Valyrian steel sword Ice, then dreams of reaching Winterfell and reuniting with Jon Snow at the Wall.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arya recalls Yoren cutting her hair** — In an alley in King's Landing, Yoren hacked off Arya's hair with his dagger and told her she would be "Arry the orphan boy" from there to Winterfell.
2. **Yoren warns Arya about the company** — He tells her half the men would turn her over to the queen for a pardon, and the other half would rape her first; she must keep to herself and make water alone in the woods.
3. **The party departs King's Landing** — Five wagons laden with supplies for the Wall, with teams of plow horses, two coursers, and half-dozen donkeys; Lannister guardsmen at the gate wave them through after Yoren calls one by name.
4. **Arya wishes King's Landing would wash away** — She wishes the Rush would rise and destroy the city, but remembers Sansa is still there and decides to wish for Winterfell instead.
5. **Lommy and Hot Pie bully Arya** — The orphan boys mock her as "Lumpyhead," question the authenticity of her sword, and demand she hand it over.
6. **The Bull intervenes verbally** — He tells the boys to leave Arya alone; Lommy does not dare mock him because he is bigger and older.
7. **Hot Pie tries to take Needle** — He rides closer and reaches for the hilt of Needle.
8. **Arya beats Hot Pie with her practice sword** — She strikes his donkey, dumps him on the ground, pokes him in the gut, whacks him across the face (breaking his nose), and continues beating him; she uses a water dancer's stance and lunges between his legs.
9. **The Bull warns Arya of Hot Pie's attack from behind** — Hot Pie grabs a jagged rock and throws it; Arya ducks and continues the fight.
10. **Yoren breaks up the fight** — He pulls Arya off Hot Pie, confiscates the practice sword, and threatens to tie them all behind the wagons and drag them to the Wall.
11. **Yoren punishes Arya** — He takes her off the road into a tangle of trees, makes her bare her thighs and hug an oak, and strikes her three times with the practice stick, drawing blood.
12. **Yoren reveals Lord Eddard was meant to take the black** — He tells Arya that he was waiting for Lord Eddard to join the party; a man came with a boy, a purse of coin, and a message saying Eddard would take the black, but "something went queer."
13. **Arya blames Joffrey** — She says someone should kill Joffrey; Yoren says someone will but it won't be either of them.
14. **Yoren returns the practice sword and gives Arya sourleaf** — The sourleaf helps with the pain but tastes foul.
15. **Arya walks for three days** — Too raw to sit a donkey after the beating; Hot Pie rides in the back of a wagon on barley sacks, whimpering.
16. **Lommy avoids Arya** — He twitches every time she looks at him; the Bull reports this to her.
17. **Arya sees the red comet** — That night she stares at it from her blanket on the ground; the Bull calls it "the Red Sword"; Arya sees it as Ice covered in her father's blood.
18. **Arya dreams of home** — She dreams of the kingsroad leading past Winterfell; she yearns to see her mother and siblings, but thinks most of Jon Snow and wishes they could reach the Wall first so he could call her "little sister."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grieving, angry, and defensive. Arya cries in her sleep dreaming of her father but wakes dry-eyed. She channels grief into aggression when provoked. Beneath the toughness, she is deeply homesick and lonely.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and maintaining her disguise; defending Needle (her connection to Jon); longing for home and family, especially Jon Snow.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides not to go crying to Yoren for help against the bullies; decides to fight Hot Pie rather than surrender Needle; chooses to stop talking to people after the incident ("It seemed safer not to talk to anyone").
- **Self-deception flags:** None clearly observed. Arya is fairly clear-eyed about her situation, though she suppresses her grief and fear behind Syrio's "calm as still water" mantra.
