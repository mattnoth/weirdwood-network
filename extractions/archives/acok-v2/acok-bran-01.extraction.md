He reflects on his inability to walk or climb, his resentment of the Frey wards, and his wolf dreams. He argues with Maester Luwin about the Walders and his confinement. Luwin gives him a sleeping draught, and Osha hints that the gods may be speaking to him through his dreams. Bran falls asleep and enters a wolf dream as Summer, prowling the godswood and sensing Shaggydog circling the walls, feeling the call of the world beyond Winterfell.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran sits at his window seat** — He prefers the hard stone to his featherbed; watches Winterfell's windows light up and listens to the direwolves howl at the comet.
2. **Bran asks various people about the howling and the comet** — Ser Rodrik dismisses it; Farlen says freedom; Gage says hunting; Maester Luwin says the wolves think the comet is the moon; Osha says "blood and fire"; Septon Chayle says "the sword that slays the season"; Old Nan says "dragons"; Hodor says "Hodor."
3. **White raven arrives from Oldtown** — Brings word of autumn, seemingly confirming Septon Chayle's interpretation.
4. **Bran howls at the comet** — Cups his hands and howls; Summer and Shaggydog join in; guard Hayhead arrives and fetches Maester Luwin.
5. **Luwin visits Bran about the howling** — Tells Bran it's late; Bran says he's talking to the wolves and mentions his wolf dreams and tree dreams; tells Luwin he dreams of a weirwood that calls to him.
6. **Bran argues with Luwin about the Walders** — Bran says he hates them and commanded them sent away; Luwin insists they are his mother's wards; Bran complains Summer was confined because of them and that Alebelly won't let him ride Dancer outside.
7. **Bran reflects on the Walders' arrival (flashback)** — Rickon screamed and wanted them gone; Bran welcomed them properly with meat and mead; they played lord of the crossing in the godswood.
8. **Lord of the crossing game (flashback)** — Played with a log across a hot pool in the godswood; Little Walder was lord more often than not; Shaggydog attacked Little Walder after he smacked Rickon with the stick, drawing blood.
9. **Aftermath of the game (flashback)** — Rickon befriended the Walders; they plundered kitchens, raced walls, trained with wooden swords; Rickon showed them the crypts, angering Bran.
10. **Ser Rodrik confines the direwolves to the godswood** — After Shaggydog bit Little Walder.
11. **Luwin returns with sleeping draught** — Accompanied by Osha and Hayhead; Osha carries Bran to bed; Bran drinks the thick chalky draught with honey.
12. **Osha speaks privately with Bran** — Tells him not to fight the wolf dreams; says the gods may be trying to talk to him through the heart tree; Bran falls asleep.
13. **Bran enters a wolf dream as Summer** — Prowls the godswood on four legs; smells the hot pools, earth, squirrels; senses Shaggydog circling the walls in fury; perceives Winterfell as confining man-rock; feels the call of the world beyond.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Lonely, frustrated, resentful, wistful. Bran feels imprisoned — his room is a cell, Winterfell his prison. He resents his disability, the Walders, and being denied freedom. He feels a queer tight feeling in his belly when he sees where he fell. He feels excluded from the other children's play. Exultation when walking in the wolf dream.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The wolf dreams and his connection to Summer; his inability to walk or participate in normal activities; the comet and what it means; resentment of the Walders taking over spaces that belong to Starks (Jon's room, the crypts).
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to stop howling (defies Luwin); drinks the sleeping draught; has been talking to the heart tree in the godswood.
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran tells himself the sleeping draught will give him dreamless sleep ("'It will?' Bran said, wanting to believe") — but the chapter ends with him entering a wolf dream despite the draught, suggesting either the draught doesn't work on these dreams or they are more than ordinary dreams.
