- **chapter_summary:** Bran experiences the burning of Winterfell through Summer's eyes in a wolf dream lasting three days. He wakes in the crypts beneath Winterfell where he, Rickon, Hodor, Osha, Meera, and Jojen have been hiding. They emerge to find the castle sacked and burned, with dead ironmen and Bolton men among the corpses. They discover Maester Luwin dying beneath the heart tree in the godswood. Luwin counsels that the princes must be separated for safety, then the group splits: Osha takes Rickon and Shaggydog east via the kingsroad, while the Reeds take Bran and Hodor north through the Hunter's Gate with Summer.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran experiences Winterfell's destruction through Summer** — In a wolf dream lasting three days, Bran sees through Summer's eyes as Winterfell burns through the night. Summer and Shaggydog watch from the wolfswood edge, smell blood, burnt meat, and steel. They see a fiery shape in the sky ("a great winged snake whose roar was a river of flame"). After the fires die, they enter the winter town ruins, finding only corpses and crows. Summer and Shaggydog fight over a dying horse's carcass; Summer dominates.
2. **Bran resists returning to his body** — The "dark place" (the crypts/his body) pulls at him. He fights it, wanting to stay as wolf, but the darkness overwhelms him.
3. **Bran wakes in the crypts** — Meera calls him back. He reports what he saw: Winterfell burning, everyone killed. He has been gone three days; Meera kept him alive with dribbled water and honey.
4. **Jojen warns Bran about staying too long in wolf dreams** — Says Bran will starve himself; tells him to remember who he is.
5. **Bran tells Osha about the burning** — Osha is reluctant to go above, fearing Theon's men. Meera volunteers to go.
6. **Osha lights a torch** — Reveals the crypts: statues of dead Starks on stone chairs with stone wolves. The group is huddled in Lord Eddard's empty tomb with their remaining food.
7. **Bran uses his third eye to confirm daylight above** — He briefly sees double: the crypts and Winterfell's surface simultaneously. Sees the gates charred, drawbridge down, bodies in the moat.
8. **The group climbs the crypt stairs** — Osha scouts ahead; finds the door blocked. Hodor forces it open with tremendous effort after Bran's encouragement.
9. **They emerge at the First Keep** — One side of the building has collapsed. Shattered gargoyles litter the yard. Bran notes they fell where he once did.
10. **Summer and Shaggydog rejoin the group** — The direwolves emerge from behind the broken tower. Rickon greets Shaggydog happily.
11. **The group surveys the destroyed castle** — The Great Hall's roof has collapsed; glass gardens shattered; stables burned with dead horses; Library Tower has hot water gushing from a crack; the bridge between Bell Tower and rookery has collapsed; Maester Luwin's turret is gone; fires still burn in cellars and a storehouse.
12. **They find bodies and identify combatants** — Bran recognizes Poxy Tym (axed in the face). Osha identifies dead ironmen and Theon Greyjoy's warhorse (black, with arrows). She finds Black Lorren's body, badly hacked. She turns over a corpse bearing a flayed man badge — Bolton men.
13. **Summer leads them to the godswood** — They find Maester Luwin dying beneath the heart tree by the black pool, having crawled there leaving a blood trail.
14. **Maester Luwin's final counsel** — He reveals he knew the burned boys were not Bran and Rickon ("the legs... the muscles in his legs"). He tells Osha the princes must be separated. He reports Ser Rodrik, Leobald Tallhart, Lady Hornwood, and the Cerwyn boy are dead; Deepwood has fallen, Moat Cailin too, Torrhen's Square soon; ironmen on the Stony Shore; the Bastard of Bolton in the east. He suggests White Harbor or the Umbers. He tells Bran to be strong — "your father's son."
15. **Luwin asks for water and "another boon"** — Osha understands; she sends the children away and stays with him. She rejoins them shortly, saying nothing of Luwin.
16. **The group splits** — Osha takes Rickon and Shaggydog, planning to exit via the East Gate and follow the kingsroad. Meera and Jojen take Bran and Hodor, exiting through the Hunter's Gate. Jojen declares their road is north.
17. **They scavenge food from the kitchens** — Burned bread, cold roast fowl, honey, apples.
18. **Rickon's tearful farewell** — He sobs and clings to Hodor until Osha smacks him with her spear butt. The last Bran sees is Shaggydog's tail vanishing behind the broken tower.
19. **Bran's group exits through the Hunter's Gate** — The portcullis is warped by heat; they squeeze under its spikes one by one.
20. **Bran looks back at Winterfell** — From the wolfswood edge, the castle looks almost intact from a distance. He reflects: "The stone is strong... the roots of the trees go deep, and under the ground the Kings of Winter sit their thrones." He thinks: "It was not dead, just broken. Like me... I'm not dead either."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief, fear, resolve. Bran grieves the destruction of Winterfell, the deaths of Maester Luwin and Ser Rodrik and others. He fears leaving the safety of the crypts and the unknown ahead. By the end, he arrives at a quiet determination — identifying himself with the broken but surviving castle.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The pull between his wolf identity and his human identity. He prefers being Summer — "Better Bran the beastling" — and resents being called back. Also preoccupied with the destruction of his home and the loss of everyone he knew.
- **Key decisions made:** Tells the group they must go see Winterfell; agrees to the separation from Rickon; accepts going north with the Reeds rather than to Greywater Watch.
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran tells himself carrying a sword means something ("the blade felt good in his hand") but immediately admits "it was only a game" and he knows it. He also convinces himself the horse meat he ate as Summer counts as his own eating ("I ate").
