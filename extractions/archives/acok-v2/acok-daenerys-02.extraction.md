# ACOK — Daenerys II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 28
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys II
- **first_available:** ACOK Daenerys II
- **location_primary:** Qarth
- **location_secondary:** Xaro Xhoan Daxos's palace; docks of Qarth (referenced, Jorah visits offscreen)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after arrival at Qarth from Vaes Tolorro; roughly half a year since Dany last had news from Westeros
- **time_markers:** Entrance to the city during daytime; "near evenfall" when Jorah returns with Quhuru Mo; dusk settling over Qarth during their audience; "half a year" since Dany last heard tidings from the Seven Kingdoms; Quhuru Mo called at Oldtown "not half a year past"
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys enters Qarth in a ceremonial procession, escorted by Pyat Pree, Xaro Xhoan Daxos, and Quaithe. She is installed in a wing of Xaro's vast palace. After the three seekers depart — Pyat promising an audience with the Undying, Xaro promising feasts, and Quaithe issuing a cryptic warning — Dany dispatches Rakharo to scout the city and Ser Jorah to the docks. While bathing, she reflects on her need for armies and ships and her fears about conquering Westeros. At evenfall Jorah returns with Quhuru Mo, captain of the Cinnamon Wind, who brings news that Robert Baratheon is dead, Joffrey sits the Iron Throne, the Lannisters rule, Robert's brothers have fled to claim the crown, and Ned Stark has been seized for treason.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Not described explicitly; implied warm climate consistent with Qarth
- **Season indicators:** None stated directly
- **Time of day:** Daytime during the entry procession; transitions to evenfall and dusk by chapter's end
- **Lighting:** Daylight during the procession; dusk/evening light at chapter's end; pale smoke rising from Drogon described like a veil before Dany's face
- **Sounds:** Men beating gongs on the walls of Qarth; curious horns that encircle the players' bodies like great bronze snakes; a thousand gaily colored birds in the bazaar's latticework ceiling; Drogon hissing; dragons screeching and wingtips scrabbling on marble

- **Notable sensory details:** Pool water described as "deliciously cool"; tiny golden fish nibble at Dany's skin and make her giggle; the water later feels cold to her as her doubts rise; marble floors, silk hangings that shimmer with every breath of air

## Events & Actions
1. **Daenerys enters Qarth in ceremonial procession** — Gongs beat, horns sound, camelry in copper armor escort her through three carved walls and three sets of gates
2. **Pyat Pree, Xaro, and Quaithe compete for Dany's attention during the procession** — Pyat offers the House of the Undying; Xaro offers material wealth; they snipe at each other
3. **Dany declares her desire for the Red Keep and asks for ships and swords** — She tells Pyat the only palace she wants is King's Landing; asks Xaro for military aid
4. **Ser Jorah warns Dany against both Pyat Pree and Xaro** — He calls both men untrustworthy in the Common Tongue
5. **Dany is installed in Xaro's palace** — Given an entire wing with gardens, bathing pool, scrying tower, and warlock's maze
6. **Xaro promises a feast for the morrow** — Peacock and lark's tongue; the Thirteen will attend
7. **The three seekers depart** — Pyat vows to petition the Undying for an audience and gives Dany a jar of ointment, kissing her feet; Quaithe warns "beware of all" and says people will lust for her dragons; Xaro departs
8. **Dany orders guards and scouts** — Tells bloodriders to guard the palace wing and watch over dragons; sends Rakharo to scout the city (with women for places men cannot go); sends Jorah to the docks
9. **Jorah resists being sent away** — Argues his place is at her side; Dany insists he has the languages needed; he reluctantly agrees
10. **Dany bathes in the marble pool** — Her handmaids strip her travel-stained silks; she floats in cool water with golden fish; reflects on needing to conquer Westeros
11. **Dany reflects on her enemies and her doubts** — Thinks of Robert, Eddard Stark, the Lannisters, her dead husband Drogo, Viserys's foolishness; doubts whether she can succeed
12. **Ser Jorah returns at evenfall with Quhuru Mo** — Captain of the Cinnamon Wind, a Summer Islander from Tall Trees Town
13. **Quhuru Mo delivers news: Robert Baratheon is dead** — Torn by a boar while hunting; some say his queen or brother or Lord Stark betrayed him; Joffrey now sits the Iron Throne; Lannisters rule; Robert's brothers have fled to claim the crown; Ned Stark seized for treason
14. **Jorah defends Ned Stark's honor** — Snorts that Stark would never commit treason
15. **Dany tells Quhuru Mo of Viserys's death** — Killed by Khal Drogo with a crown of molten gold
16. **Dany asks about passage to Westeros; Quhuru Mo declines** — The Cinnamon Wind sails east for the trader's circle round the Jade Sea, won't return for a year or more
17. **Dany and Jorah debate the significance of Robert's death** — Jorah urges caution; Dany insists this changes everything, comparing Westeros to a khalasar losing its khal; Jorah says she will need a fleet, gold, armies, alliances
18. **Dany asserts her identity and resolve** — Tells Jorah she is not the girl he met in Pentos, not Viserys; Jorah says she has more of Rhaegar in her; she kisses his cheek and says dragonslayers also die

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Shifts from wonder and mild wariness during the entry to Qarth, through comfort and private reflection during the bath, to doubt and shivering fear about conquering Westeros, then to blazing hope and fierce resolve upon learning of Robert's death ("a sun had risen in Dany's heart")
- **Primary preoccupation:** How to reach and reclaim the Iron Throne; need for ships, armies, and allies; distrust of those offering help (Pyat, Xaro, Quaithe); the significance of Robert's death
- **Key decisions made:** To accept Xaro's hospitality while remaining guarded; to post her own guards on her wing; to send Rakharo to scout Qarth independently; to send Jorah to the docks for news and ships; to openly declare her plans despite Jorah's caution
- **Self-deception flags:** Dany wishes she could love Jorah "better than she did" — acknowledges his devotion without recognizing all its dimensions; she dismisses Jorah's caution about Robert's death changing nothing, insisting "this changes everything" with a confidence that may not be warranted
