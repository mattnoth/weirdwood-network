- **chapter_summary:** Edmure Tully rides out from Riverrun to defend the fords of the Red Fork against Lord Tywin's forces, leaving Catelyn behind with a skeleton garrison. The Lannisters probe the fords repeatedly — first a small cavalry thrust repelled by Lord Jason Mallister's men, then a nighttime assault also beaten back. Catelyn receives a letter reporting Storm's End has yielded to Stannis after Ser Cortnay Penrose's death, and she interrogates Ser Cleos Frey about Tyrion's peace terms, learning Sansa was seen at court but not Arya. Days later, word arrives that Edmure has won a decisive victory at Stone Mill, throwing back Ser Gregor Clegane and forcing Lord Tywin to march southeast — yet Catelyn feels deep unease despite the string of victories.

## Events & Actions
1. **Edmure departs Riverrun** — Edmure Tully rides out with his forces to defend the fords of the Red Fork, leaving with trumpets, drums, lances raised, and banners streaming. He asks Catelyn to tell their father he has gone to make him proud.
2. **Catelyn surveys the garrison** — She notes Edmure has taken every able-bodied man, leaving Ser Desmond Grell to command a garrison of the wounded, old, sick, squires, and untrained peasant boys, defending a castle full of women and children.
3. **Catelyn prays in the sept** — She kneels before the Warrior, lights scented candles for Edmure and Robb, and stays for the septon's celebration. She reflects on wanting Septon Osmynd's counsel about the shadow she witnessed in Renly's pavilion.
4. **Rymund sings in the yard** — Rymund the Rhymer sings of Lord Deremond at the Bloody Meadow by the brewhouse. Boys play at war with sticks nearby.
5. **Catelyn and Brienne discuss war and motherhood** — Brienne says fighting is better than waiting; Catelyn compares childbearing to battle. Brienne reveals she never knew her mother. Catelyn asks Brienne to fill the protective role of the men of her house while they are away.
6. **Letter from Storm's End arrives** — Maester Vyman brings a letter from Lord Meadows, castellan of Storm's End, announcing Ser Cortnay Penrose is dead and Storm's End has yielded to Stannis. Catelyn orders a raven sent to Ashemark to relay news to Robb at the Crag.
7. **Catelyn reflects on bastards** — She discusses Edric Storm's appearance with Brienne, deduces Stannis wants to parade the boy to undermine Joffrey's legitimacy. She thinks of Jon Snow and his unknown mother. She recalls Roose Bolton's cold letter about Ramsay's death and his march on Harrenhal.
8. **First ford battle (daytime)** — A column of Lannister riders (about fifty, flying Brax purple unicorn under the Lannister lion) attempts to cross the nearest ford. Lord Jason Mallister's men repel them from behind rocks, grass, and hillocks. Dead horses and men float past Riverrun. The Lannisters retreat.
9. **Second ford battle (night)** — Well after midnight, Lannisters attack the ford again. Defenders' watchfires illuminate the battle. Men step in hidden pools and are cut by caltrops. Mallister bowmen send fire arrows. A man afire dances in the water. The attack fails and survivors melt into the night.
10. **Brienne's military analysis** — Brienne tells Catelyn the probing attacks are Lord Tywin "feeling for a weak point" and predicts he will concentrate his forces if he finds no undefended crossing.
11. **Catelyn interrogates Ser Cleos Frey** — After having wine sent to loosen his tongue, she questions him about Tyrion's peace terms. He confirms Tyrion offered to exchange Sansa and Arya for the Kingslayer. He saw Sansa at court (beautiful but wan) but not Arya. Catelyn threatens to hang him if he lies.
12. **Catelyn's bitter reflection on the dagger** — She examines her scarred fingers and reasserts that Tyrion's dagger was used in the attempt on Bran's life, despite his denials even in Lysa's sky cells.
13. **Late supper in the Great Hall** — Catelyn sups with the garrison. Rymund sings of Robb's victory at Oxcross with wolf-howling between verses. Half the hall howls along. Brienne mentions a singer from across the narrow sea at Evenfall Hall. Catelyn asks if Brienne sang for her father or for Renly; Brienne, embarrassed, leaves.
14. **Mallister rider brings report** — A rider with the Mallister eagle reports Ser Flement Brax tried to force a different ford six leagues south and was repelled with scorpions and archers. Lord Karyl Vance also held the fords farther upstream.
15. **Stone Mill victory reported** — Three days after the battle (five before news arrives), Edmure's messenger brings word of victory. Ser Gregor Clegane led the fiercest assault at Stone Mill but was thrown back. Lord Lefford drowned, Strongboar captured, Ser Addam Marbrand thrice retreated. Lord Tywin is marching southeast.
16. **Celebration at Riverrun** — Casks are opened, smallfolk shout "Riverrun!" and "Tully!", Rymund plays harp with drummers and a piper. Catelyn cannot share the joy.
17. **Catelyn studies the maps** — In her father's solar, she traces the Red Fork on a map and concludes Lord Tywin has likely reached the headwaters of the Blackwater Rush. Despite the victories, she feels deep unease.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious and fearful despite victories; burdened by duty; envious of Brienne's simplicity; grieving for Ned and her lost sense of certainty; guilt about Sansa and the promise of singers at court; unease growing as she traces Lord Tywin's likely path southeast. She cannot share in the celebration. "If we are winning, why am I so afraid?"
- **Primary preoccupation:** The safety of her children (Robb at war, Sansa captive, Arya missing); the meaning of her duty when she cannot determine what it requires; Lord Tywin's movements and what they portend despite the victories.
- **Key decisions made:** Orders wine sent to Ser Cleos to loosen his tongue before interrogation; orders raven sent to Ashemark to relay Storm's End news to Robb; allows Ser Desmond to spread word of victories and open casks.
- **Self-deception flags:** Continues to assert with certainty that the dagger was Tyrion's and that he hired the assassin for Bran, despite acknowledging he denied it repeatedly and that he defended her against the clansmen.
