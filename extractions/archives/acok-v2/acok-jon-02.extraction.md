- **approximate_timeline:** During the Great Ranging; the Night's Watch column is moving north through the haunted forest, visiting a series of empty wildling villages
- **time_markers:** Three hours of daylight remaining when departing Whitetree; days of travel implied ("by day they followed game trails," "at night they camped"); references to "a year ago" when wildlings were last at Whitetree; Castle Black departure described as recent past; the comet is visible at night
- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow and the Night's Watch ranging party arrive at Whitetree, the fourth empty wildling village they have found. They search the houses and find nothing but bones and ashes inside the great weirwood's mouth. Mormont dictates a message to Maester Aemon and decides to press north toward a lake rather than camp at Whitetree. Jon delivers the message to Sam Tarly, who sends it by raven. The column rides on, and Mormont tells Jon they will be three hundred strong when Qhorin Halfhand joins them.

## Events & Actions
1. **Arrival at Whitetree** — The Night's Watch ranging party arrives at Whitetree, the fourth empty wildling village. Jon notes the tumbledown houses and the monstrous weirwood with its jagged mouth.
2. **Examination of the weirwood** — Mormont, Smallwood, and Jon discuss the great weirwood. Smallwood expresses hostility toward it. Jon recalls his father's belief about heart trees and lying.
3. **Discovery of bones in the weirwood** — Jon reaches into the weirwood's mouth and finds a human skull, then a second smaller skull with a broken jaw, amid ashes and bone fragments.
4. **Mormont examines the skull** — The Old Bear holds the skull, reflects on wildlings burning their dead, wishes he'd asked them why. He references the children of the forest's ability to speak to the dead. He tosses the skull back into the tree.
5. **Search of the houses** — Mormont orders the houses searched. Jon is paired with Dolorous Edd. They search a house and find nothing but ashes, a straw nest, and old dung smell. All houses yield nothing.
6. **Giant scouts from the weirwood** — Bedwyck (Giant) climbs the weirwood and reports water to the north (a lake), flint hills to the west, nothing else.
7. **Debate about camping** — Smallwood suggests camping at Whitetree. Mormont declines, asks Giant about remaining daylight (three hours), decides to press north toward the lake.
8. **Discussion of missing wildlings** — Buckwell notes a year ago Robert was king and things were different. Ser Mallador Locke dismisses concern about the missing wildlings. The rangers are uneasy about four empty villages.
9. **Mormont writes to Maester Aemon** — Mormont dictates a brief message noting the fourth empty village and that the wildlings are gone.
10. **Jon delivers message to Sam** — Jon rides to find Sam among the stewards. Sam sends the message by raven. Sam describes his improving courage and his map work.
11. **The column moves out** — The march order is described: Buckwell's scouts, Smallwood's vanguard, Mormont's main force, Locke's baggage train, Wythers's rear guard. Two hundred men, half again as many mounts.
12. **Jon reflects on the journey** — The narrative describes days of travel through ranger's roads, nights under stars with the comet, declining morale and growing unease among the brothers.
13. **Jon flexes his burned hand** — Jon peels off his glove to air his burned fingers, remembers mussing Arya's hair, feels sad he may never do so again, and flexes his sword hand to prevent stiffness.
14. **Ghost appears** — Ghost emerges suddenly from undergrowth, startling Jon's garron. The direwolf has been hunting away from the column with no better luck than the foragers — the woods are empty.
15. **Jon rides with Mormont** — Jon catches up to the Old Bear. They discuss Benjen Stark's likely actions upon finding empty villages. Mormont says they'll be three hundred when Qhorin joins them and promises they will find the enemy.
