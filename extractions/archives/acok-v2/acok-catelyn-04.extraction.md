As Brienne armors Renly, a shadow with a sword kills the king by cutting his throat through his gorget. Brienne is blamed by Renly's guards; Catelyn intervenes, convincing Ser Robar Royce of Brienne's innocence. Catelyn and Brienne escape the burning pavilion and flee the disintegrating camp with Catelyn's escort.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn arrives at abandoned village sept** — Ser Wendel lights a torch and leads her inside; he and Ser Robar wait outside.
2. **Catelyn prays before the Mother** — She studies the charcoal drawings of the Seven, prays for her sons Robb, Bran, and Rickon.
3. **Catelyn reflects on her family** — She sees her father, mother (Lady Minisa), Arya, Lysa, and Cersei in the shifting shadows of the charcoal faces. She thinks about Cersei's children and the incest.
4. **Catelyn reasons through Bran's fall** — She concludes Bran must have seen Jaime and Cersei together, which is why they tried to kill him.
5. **Catelyn prays to each of the Seven** — She prays to the Smith for Bran, the Maid for Arya and Sansa, the Father for justice, the Warrior for Robb, and the Crone for guidance.
6. **Ser Robar summons Catelyn** — He tells her they must return before dawn.
7. **Catelyn rides back to camp** — They ride through sparse woodland back to Renly's camp; the army waits in predawn darkness, lances pointing upward.
8. **Catelyn enters Renly's pavilion** — She finds Brienne armoring Renly while Lords Tarly and Rowan discuss battle tactics.
9. **Battle tactics discussed** — Lord Rowan urges an immediate attack; Renly insists on waiting for dawn to avoid accusations of treachery; Tarly notes the sun will be in their eyes; Renly is confident Ser Loras will break the enemy.
10. **Renly recounts the siege of Storm's End** — He tells of Ser Gawen Wylde's failed attempt to surrender and Maester Cressen's warning about eating the dead, and how the Onion Knight saved them.
11. **Renly orders Stannis's corpse treated with respect and Barristan Selmy spared** — The lords depart.
12. **Catelyn proposes a Great Council** — She tells Renly that Bran caught Jaime and Cersei at their incest, and asks leave to go to Stannis to propose a Great Council where all three kings would set aside their crowns.
13. **Renly dismisses the proposal** — He compares it to direwolves voting on pack leadership and puts on his gauntlets.
14. **The shadow assassinates Renly** — A gust of wind opens the tent door; a shadow with a sword cuts through Renly's gorget; he chokes on his blood and dies in Brienne's arms.
15. **Brienne is accused** — Ser Robar, Ser Emmon, and men-at-arms burst in and see Brienne drenched in blood holding the king; Ser Emmon attacks her.
16. **Brienne fights back** — She snatches Renly's sword, shatters Emmon's axe haft, cuts off a man-at-arms' hand with torch, fights both Emmon and another guardsman.
17. **Catelyn convinces Ser Robar** — She tells him Stannis killed Renly through sorcery and a shadow; she swears on her husband's grave and her honor as a Stark; Robar believes her and agrees to hold off pursuers.
18. **Catelyn strikes Ser Emmon** — She crashes an iron brazier into the back of his head, knocking him to his knees.
19. **Catelyn and Brienne escape** — They slash through the tent silk, walk calmly through the chaotic camp; the pavilion burns behind them.
20. **Brienne vows to kill Stannis** — She swears three times to kill Stannis with Renly's own sword.
21. **They reach the escort and flee** — Hal Mollen, Ser Wendel, and Ser Perwyn Frey are waiting with horses. Brienne takes a spare mount, leaving her own horse and armor behind. They ride away as dawn breaks.
22. **Catelyn observes the army's fate** — She realizes all of Renly's power — Storm's End and Highgarden — now belongs to Stannis. She recalls Stannis's words declaring himself the rightful king and calling Robb a traitor.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief-stricken (still mourning Ned — "When they took his head off, they killed me too"); weary and hungry (hasn't eaten); aching knees; protective of her children; horror at Renly's assassination; desperate urgency during the escape
- **Primary preoccupation:** Her children's safety (prays for each by name); the futility of the war; guilt over being absent from her children and from Ned's death ("I was not even with Ned when he died")
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to ask Renly for a Great Council compromise; instantly names Stannis as the killer and defends Brienne; decides to flee with Brienne rather than stay to explain; commands her escort to cut down anyone who tries to stop them
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself she didn't eat because there was no time, then admits "the truth was that food had lost its savor"; says "hoping it was true" about Robb setting aside his crown, then admits "She would make it true if she must" — aware she may be overriding Robb's wishes
