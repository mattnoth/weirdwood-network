- **approximate_timeline:** The Great Ranging continues north; the Night's Watch has passed seven empty wildling villages and now reaches Craster's Keep. They stay one night and depart the next morning.
- **time_markers:** Six days of rain prior to arrival; midafternoon when Jon rides through the wood; dusk approaching as Jon reaches the gate; overnight stay; pale pink light of dawn the next morning; departure within the hour after breaking fast; "three years" since Craster last saw Benjen Stark
- **chapter_summary:** The Night's Watch column reaches Craster's Keep after six days of rain and seven empty villages. Lord Commander Mormont negotiates shelter and information from Craster, who confirms Mance Rayder is gathering wildlings in the Frostfangs. One of Craster's wives, Gilly, approaches Jon through Sam and begs to be taken south, revealing that Craster sacrifices his sons to "the cold gods" — white shadows with bright blue eyes. Jon tells Mormont what he has learned; Mormont admits the rangers have long known. The Watch departs north and west, with Mormont declaring they will find, fight, and stop Mance Rayder.

## Events & Actions
1. **March through rain** — The Night's Watch column rides through the haunted forest in blowing rain, on their sixth day of wet weather, having passed seven empty wildling villages.
2. **Buckwell's horn signals** — Jarman Buckwell sounds a hunting horn indicating Craster's Keep has been found and is still inhabited.
3. **Jon relays orders** — Mormont sends Jon back along the column to spread word and remind officers that men must not trouble Craster's wives.
4. **Jon checks on Sam** — Jon passes Sam in the baggage train with the ravens; Sam reports he is wet but alive.
5. **Jon informs Ser Ottyn** — Jon delivers the news to the rear guard commander, who is relieved.
6. **Jon rides through the wood** — Jon takes a shortcut through the forest; hears rustling and calls for Ghost.
7. **Encounter with Dywen and Grenn** — Instead of Ghost, Jon finds the outriders Dywen and Grenn; they joke about Craster's wives.
8. **Ghost appears** — Ghost trots alongside Jon silently; horses are uneasy around him.
9. **Arrival at Craster's Keep** — Jon observes the squalid compound: midden heap, pigsty, empty sheepfold, daub-and-wattle hall, earthen dike in disrepair.
10. **Camp is established** — Buckwell's scouts and Smallwood's van set up horse lines and tents; most men must camp outside the hall.
11. **Jon enters the hall** — Jon passes through deerhide doors into the smoky, foul-smelling hall where two dozen chief rangers are gathered.
12. **Craster negotiates with Mormont** — Craster denies knowledge of Benjen Stark; demands wine and a new axe; refuses Mormont's offer of escort to the Wall; claims to be "a godly man" whom the gods keep safe.
13. **Craster discusses missing rangers** — Craster recalls Waymar Royce, Gared, and Will; describes Waymar as a proud lordling.
14. **Mormont describes the wights** — Mormont tells Craster about the reanimated dead at Castle Black who killed Ser Jaremy Rykker.
15. **Craster offers intelligence on Mance** — Craster reveals he has information on Mance Rayder's location and hints he'll share it; shows a tongue nailed to his wall from Mance's messenger.
16. **Craster agrees to host the Watch** — One night only, hall floor for officers, meat and beer for twenty; Mormont offers to share Watch supplies.
17. **Jon volunteers Sam as mapmaker** — Jon tells Craster that Sam can draw maps; Mormont sends Jon to fetch Sam and Edd with the axe.
18. **Craster identifies Jon as a Stark** — Craster notes Jon has "the look of a Stark"; learns he is Ned Stark's bastard.
19. **Jon speaks with Dolorous Edd** — Edd delivers sardonic commentary about Craster, the axe gift, and the rain while tending horses.
20. **Ghost kills Craster's rabbits** — Ghost breaks into a wooden hutch and kills two rabbits; one of Craster's wives (Gilly) is frightened.
21. **Jon meets Gilly** — Jon calms Gilly, learns she is fifteen or sixteen, pregnant, one of Craster's daughter-wives; she was going to breed the rabbits since there are no sheep left.
22. **Chett and Lark confront Jon** — They mock Jon as "Lord Snow" and "bastard of Winterfell"; Lark threatens Gilly about the wolf; Chett blames Jon for his reassignment from Maester Aemon to hounds.
23. **Jon stands down from confrontation** — Jon declares he won't fight a brother beyond the Wall and walks away.
24. **Jon and Sam make camp and cook rabbit** — Jon finds dry wood, starts a fire under a rock outcrop, skins and roasts the rabbit; they eat together.
25. **Jon tells Sam about Craster** — Jon describes the hall, the negotiations, and what he observed.
26. **Sam goes to map for Craster** — Sam takes quill and parchment to the hall despite reluctance.
27. **Jon sleeps by the fire** — Jon falls asleep beside Ghost; wakes to a frozen, crystalline morning.
28. **Gilly approaches Jon at dawn** — Wearing Sam's cloak, Gilly finds Jon and begs to be taken along when the Watch leaves. She reveals Craster gives his sons to "the cold gods" — white shadows with bright blue eyes. Jon refuses, saying they ride north, not south.
29. **Jon confronts Sam** — Jon finds Sam with Gilly behind the hall; Sam admits he promised Gilly they'd take her on the way home. Jon is angry but also feels ashamed.
30. **The Watch departs Craster's Keep** — The column rides north and west along a crooked game trail; Ghost swims the swollen brook.
31. **Jon tells Mormont about Craster's sons** — Jon reveals Craster has no sons and gives his boys to the wood. Mormont admits the rangers have always known.
32. **Mormont explains his pragmatism** — Mormont says Craster is useful to the Watch despite his practices; the Watch has "other wars to fight."
33. **Mormont reveals Mance's plan** — Craster confirmed Mance is gathering all wildlings in the Frostfangs, matching intelligence from the Shadow Tower. Mormont concludes Mance means to strike south into the Seven Kingdoms.
34. **Mormont declares the mission** — "Find him. Fight him. Stop him." Jon thinks: three hundred against the fury of the wild.
