- **chapter_summary:** Jon finds Sam in the Castle Black library vault, where Sam has spent the night searching for maps for the Lord Commander. They discuss the upcoming great ranging beyond the Wall. After emerging, Jon speaks with Donal Noye at the armory about Robb's crowning as King in the North and the Baratheon brothers. Jon and Sam deliver the maps to Lord Commander Mormont, who dismisses Thoren Smallwood's claim to command the ranging. Mormont tells Jon the history of Maester Aemon's royal lineage and his refusal of the Iron Throne, drawing a parallel to Jon's own situation — having a king for a brother while bound by vows to the Night's Watch.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon finds Sam in the library vault** — Jon descends into the Castle Black vault looking for Sam, who has spent the entire night searching for maps at Maester Aemon's request.
2. **Sam shows Jon the maps and books** — Sam displays the maps he found, including one with wildling village sites and Redwyn's pre-Conquest account of ranging to Lorn Point, mentioning giants and children of the forest.
3. **Jon tries to reassure Sam about the ranging** — Sam is frightened; Jon reminds him the force will be large (200 men plus Qhorin Halfhand's 100 from the Shadow Tower) and that Sam is needed for the ravens.
4. **Jon and Sam exit through the wormwalks** — They climb steep steps to the surface; Jon reflects on the white raven's arrival signaling summer's end and the coming winter.
5. **Ghost joins them outside** — The direwolf was sleeping by the granary wall, wakes and trots to Jon with tail held stiffly upright.
6. **Jon and Sam observe the Wall and the comet** — The Wall looms above them, described in shifting colors; the comet (Mormont's Torch) is visible even by day.
7. **They cross the grounds past the sept and armory** — Voices sing in the sept; Ser Endrew Tarth trains new recruits brought by Conwy from near Gulltown.
8. **Jon speaks with Donal Noye** — Noye asks about the recruits, then about Robb's crowning. Noye offers his assessment of the three Baratheon brothers using a metalworking metaphor. He wishes Jon well and asks him to bring back Benjen.
9. **Jon and Sam deliver maps to Mormont in the King's Tower** — Mormont's raven greets Jon with "Snow!"; Mormont inspects the maps and finds them old.
10. **Thoren Smallwood argues for command of the ranging** — Smallwood claims he is First Ranger with Ben Stark lost and Ser Jaremy killed. Mormont firmly rejects this, stating Benjen remains First Ranger until confirmed dead and that he himself will lead the ranging.
11. **Mormont discusses diplomatic missions** — He considers sending Sam to Renly because of Randyll Tarly's influence, but decides on Ser Arnell instead (whose mother was a green-apple Fossoway). Notes Alliser Thorne is headed to King's Landing with the wight's hand. Laments that House Lannister has never been a friend to the Watch.
12. **Jon shows Mormont his healing hand** — Scars to the elbow, mottled pink flesh, itching; he can wield Longclaw. Maester Aemon gave him salve and exercises.
13. **Mormont tells Jon the history of Maester Aemon** — Full Targaryen succession from Daeron II through Maekar's sons: the heir's tourney death, the Great Spring Sickness, Aerys I's reign, Aerion Brightflame drinking wildfire, Prince Daeron's pox death, the Great Council, Aemon's refusal of the crown, Aegon V's accession, and Aemon's voluntary exile to the Wall.
14. **Mormont draws the parallel to Jon** — Both Jon and Aemon have a king for a brother. Mormont lays out what Jon's vows mean: black versus silk, no wife or children, service versus rule. He challenges Jon to admit it troubles him.
15. **Jon affirms his vows** — "Be troubled, and keep my vows."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Conflicted about Robb's crowning — loyal pride mixed with awareness of what he's given up; steady resolve about his vows; underlying unease about the ranging and what lies beyond the Wall; haunted by dreams of the wight Othor
- **Primary preoccupation:** The upcoming great ranging; Robb's new kingship and what it means for Jon's identity as a bastard bound to the Night's Watch
- **Key decisions made:** Affirms his commitment to his Night's Watch vows despite the emotional cost; chose not to go to Mole's Town brothel ("I took a vow")
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon envisions Robb sipping summerwine from jeweled goblets — a romanticized contrast suggesting he may be idealizing what he's missing. He tells Mormont "I've always known that Robb would be Lord of Winterfell" as if kingship is the same thing, which Mormont directly challenges ("A lord's one thing, a king's another").
