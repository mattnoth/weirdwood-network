- **time_markers:** Moon rising/sun sinking at chapter opening; half-moon during night ride; dawn the next day; references to "days and nights" blurring together during the retreat; nightfall when Ebben departs; "every dawn and every dusk" seeing the eagle; "come dawn" for Qhorin's plan to press on through the mountain; "break of day" when they eat and saddle up; "long hours later" emerging from the cave; "before the sun is down" (wildling taunt)
- **chapter_summary:** Jon and Qhorin Halfhand are the last two survivors of the five-ranger scouting party that fled the Skirling Pass. After losing Squire Dalbridge, sending Ebben east with a message for Mormont, and parting with Stonesnake, Qhorin orders Jon to yield to the wildlings if captured and infiltrate their ranks. They attempt to evade pursuit by riding back past their own campfire and hiding in a cave behind a waterfall, but the eagle tracking them finds them again when they emerge. Cornered by Rattleshirt and fourteen wildlings, Jon yields, and Qhorin forces a fight in which Jon kills him — with Ghost's help — to prove himself to the wildlings. Ygritte vouches for Jon and persuades the wildlings to spare him. They burn Qhorin's body and Ygritte tells Jon that Mance Rayder is already marching on the Wall.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon builds a fire** — Jon hacks branches from a dead tree and starts a fire with flint and dagger while Ghost watches.
2. **Qhorin and Jon sit by the fire** — Only two of the original five rangers remain; Jon reflects on the losses.
3. **Squire Dalbridge falls (recalled)** — A far-off horn signals Dalbridge's death at the Skirling Pass.
4. **Eagle spotted (recalled)** — The party sees the eagle soaring through dusk; Stonesnake cannot get off a shot. Ebben mutters of wargs and skinchangers.
5. **Ebben sent east (recalled)** — Qhorin orders Ebben to ride east for Mormont with both garrons. Ebben urges sending Jon instead, but Qhorin says Jon "has a different part to play."
6. **Stonesnake covers tracks and departs with Ebben (recalled)** — Stonesnake goes east a short way with Ebben, doubles back, then the three remaining turn southwest.
7. **Days of flight (recalled)** — The three ride continuously, sleeping in saddles, over bare rock, pine forests, snow, ridges, rivers. Watched constantly by the eagle.
8. **Shadowcat encounter and horse loss (recalled)** — A shadowcat startles Stonesnake's mare; she breaks a leg. They butcher the horse for blood porridge and raw meat, feed Ghost.
9. **Stonesnake departs on foot (recalled)** — With no spare horse, Stonesnake volunteers to go afoot through the Frostfangs. Qhorin sends him to the Fist to tell Mormont about the old powers, giants, wargs, and that "the trees have eyes again."
10. **Lonely riding with Qhorin** — Jon and Qhorin ride in near silence for hours. Ghost comes and goes but is always near. Jon does not dream.
11. **Qhorin asks if Jon's sword is sharp** — The Halfhand begins a significant conversation by the fire.
12. **Jon and Qhorin recite the Night's Watch oath together** — They say the full oath in unison beneath the rising moon, with Ghost and the mountains as witnesses.
13. **Qhorin orders Jon to yield if captured** — Qhorin commands Jon to go over to the wildlings, do whatever they ask, and spy on them — learning what they sought in the Milkwater valley and whether they found it.
14. **Jon reluctantly agrees** — Jon accepts but asks Qhorin to tell the Old Bear he never truly broke his oath. Qhorin swears he will.
15. **Jon builds fire higher** — Qhorin orders more wood; Jon feeds the fire with broken branches.
16. **Night ride back past the campfire** — Qhorin's plan: leave the fire burning to draw pursuit past, ride back the way they came by moonlight.
17. **Entering the defile** — They retrace steps to a narrow defile with an icy stream; Qhorin notes the icing prevents them from riding in the streambed.
18. **Discovery of the cave behind the waterfall** — Qhorin rides through the curtain of falling water; Jon follows through the shock of cold. Ghost bursts through after them.
19. **Sheltering in the cave** — They unsaddle horses; Qhorin takes first watch with drawn sword; Jon sleeps with Ghost beside him.
20. **Jon's nightmares** — He dreams of burning castles and dead men rising from graves.
21. **Morning preparation** — They eat half-frozen horsemeat, saddle up; Qhorin makes torches from dry moss soaked in oil.
22. **Passage through the mountain** — Qhorin leads with torch through twisting dark passages; Jon follows with horses.
23. **Emergence and eagle discovery** — They come out the other side, but the eagle is waiting on a dead tree. Ghost chases it but it flies away.
24. **Qhorin decides to make a stand** — The cave mouth provides defensive advantage; they feed the horses their last oats.
25. **Wildlings arrive** — Hunting horn sounds; hounds bay; fourteen wildlings with eight dogs appear over a ridge.
26. **Rattleshirt confronts Qhorin** — Riding in bone armor on a goat-like mount, Rattleshirt exchanges insults with Qhorin.
27. **Ebben's head displayed** — A spearwife pulls Ebben's bald head from a bloodstained sack, saying "He died brave."
28. **Wildlings taunt Jon** — A skinny youth threatens to make Ghost into a cloak; a spearwife bares a breast and mocks Jon as a baby.
29. **Jon yields** — Before the archers can loose, Jon steps forward and shouts "We yield!" Qhorin calls him a craven and coward, playing the role.
30. **Ygritte reveals herself and vouches for Jon** — She removes her sheepskin helm, shows her red hair, identifies Jon as "the Bastard o' Winterfell" who once spared her.
31. **Rattleshirt demands Jon be killed** — He insists Jon is untrustworthy; the eagle screams from atop his helm.
32. **Ygritte tells Jon the eagle was once a man he killed** — She says the bird hates him because it was a man before Jon killed him.
33. **Ragwyle proposes Jon prove himself** — The big spearwife suggests Jon demonstrate his loyalty.
34. **Rattleshirt orders Jon to kill Qhorin** — Jon agrees to do "whatever you ask."
35. **Jon and Qhorin fight** — Qhorin attacks Jon; they duel with swords, Qhorin's left-hand power against Jon's quickness.
36. **Ghost bites Qhorin's calf** — The direwolf's attack creates an opening.
37. **Jon kills Qhorin** — Jon plants, pivots, and slashes Qhorin's throat. A "string of red tears" like a "ruby necklace" appears; Qhorin says "...sharp" and dies.
38. **Jon's grief** — Jon kneels with Ghost, realizing Qhorin knew what would be demanded. He thinks of Sam, Grenn, Edd, Pyp, Toad, Bran, Rickon, Robb.
39. **Jon taken by wildlings** — Rough hands drag him up; Ygritte identifies him as Jon Snow, Eddard Stark's blood.
40. **Debate over Jon's fate** — Rattleshirt wants him gutted; Ygritte argues he yielded; a short man notes Jon slew his brother; Rattleshirt claims Ghost did the work; Ragwyle mocks Rattleshirt's own reluctance. Ygritte says being a warg "has never frightened us." Others shout agreement. Rattleshirt grudgingly yields.
41. **Qhorin's body burned** — They build a pyre of pine needles, brush, and green branches; black smoke rises. Rattleshirt claims charred bones; wildlings dice for gear; Ygritte wins Qhorin's cloak.
42. **Ygritte tells Jon Mance is marching on the Wall** — She says they will not return by the Skirling Pass; Mance is "well down the Milkwater, marching on your Wall."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Resigned to death at the start; conflicted about Qhorin's order to play oathbreaker; reluctant but obedient; numb with grief after killing Qhorin; identity crisis — "Who was he now? What was he?"
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and duty; whether he can bring himself to fake an oath-breaking; loss of companions one by one; at the end, grappling with having killed Qhorin and what it means for his identity
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees (reluctantly) to Qhorin's order to yield and infiltrate the wildlings; steps forward to yield before archers can shoot; fights and kills Qhorin when demanded
- **Self-deception flags:** "I'm not afraid to die" — narrator notes "It was only half a lie"; Jon tells himself they will escape after the cave passage ("By the time we come out we will have lost them") — but they don't
