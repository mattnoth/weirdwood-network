- **chapter_summary:** Jon and Qhorin Halfhand's small scouting party rest in the Skirling Pass. Qhorin reveals he knew Jon spared Ygritte and explains he wanted to learn Jon's character. Jon falls asleep and has a wolf dream through Ghost's eyes, seeing the wildling host gathered at the source of the Milkwater with giants riding mammoths, and a weirwood with his brother's three-eyed face. An eagle attacks Ghost, wounding him. Qhorin takes the dream seriously and orders a retreat. Squire Dalbridge volunteers to stay behind as a rearguard. At dawn a hunting horn sounds behind them — the wildlings are pursuing.

## Events & Actions
1. **Scouting party rides through the Skirling Pass** — Jon, Qhorin, Ghost, Stonesnake, Ebben, and Squire Dalbridge traverse the narrow, shadowed pass. Dalbridge leads through the narrowest sections, scanning the heights.
2. **Rest at the summit** — Qhorin orders a halt at a wind-carved stone arch at the highest point of the pass, to wait until shadows return. Stonesnake sleeps; Jon feeds Ghost; Ebben and Dalbridge feed horses.
3. **Jon and Qhorin discuss Ygritte** — Jon confesses he spared Ygritte. Qhorin reveals he already knew (via Stonesnake's report). Qhorin explains he deliberately left the decision to Jon as a test of character.
4. **Qhorin discusses Mance Rayder** — Qhorin reveals Mance was wildling-born, taken as a child, and deserted to return home. He calls Mance "the best of us and the worst" and praises wildling bravery while noting their lack of discipline.
5. **Qhorin assesses Jon** — Qhorin explains he uses different men for different tasks (Stonesnake for climbing, Dalbridge for archery, Ebben for interrogation) and says he now knows Jon better than before.
6. **Jon attempts to sleep; Ghost departs** — Jon settles under a rock overhang. Ghost looks at him, then pads away to hunt. Jon worries about shadowcats.
7. **Jon has a wolf dream** — Through Ghost's eyes, he perceives five direwolves scattered (should be six), feels incompleteness. He sees a weirwood sapling growing from rock with his brother's three-eyed face that says "Not always... not before the crow." The tree smells of death. The brother says "Don't be afraid, I like it in the dark" and touches him.
8. **Wolf dream: Jon/Ghost sees the wildling host** — From a precipice, Ghost sees the Milkwater valley with a massive wildling encampment — thousands of people, riders training, crude shelters, cookfires. He sees a mammoth with a giant riding it across the lake.
9. **Eagle attacks Ghost** — An eagle plummets from the sky and attacks Ghost with talons. Jon wakes shouting.
10. **Jon reports the dream** — Ebben shakes Jon awake. Jon describes the frozen river, the wildling host, giants on mammoths, and the weirwood. Qhorin takes it seriously, calling it a "wolf dream." Ebben mentions "skinchanger."
11. **Party resumes march; eagle spotted** — They descend the pass at twilight. An eagle is perched on a rock spine above them. Ebben wants to shoot it but Dalbridge says it's out of bowshot.
12. **Jon finds Ghost wounded** — Jon spots a flash of white between boulders. Ghost's neck is torn and bloody from the eagle's talons but he lives. Qhorin, Ebben, and Stonesnake help wash and bandage the wounds with water, wine, and a strip from Jon's cloak.
13. **Qhorin orders retreat** — Realizing the eagle has spotted them (implying a skinchanger controls it), Qhorin orders the party to turn back. "Eagles have sharper eyes than men. We are seen. So now we run."
14. **Night ride back through the pass** — They ride all night in darkness, no torches allowed. They pass the stone bridge at the summit and descend. A shadowcat screams; Jon sees glowing eyes on a ledge.
15. **Squire Dalbridge volunteers as rearguard** — At the black hour before dawn, near where the wildlings died, Qhorin identifies a defensible point where one man could hold a hundred. Dalbridge volunteers, asking only for arrows and an apple for his horse. Qhorin clasps his forearm. Jon watches Dalbridge climb away — his last sight of the man.
16. **Dawn: eagle and hunting horn** — At dawn, the eagle is visible as a speck in the cloudless sky. A hunting horn sounds behind them. Qhorin says "And now they come."
