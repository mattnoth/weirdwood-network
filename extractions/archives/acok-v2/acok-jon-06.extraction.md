- **chapter_summary:** Jon and a small Night's Watch scouting party under Qhorin Halfhand spot a wildling watchfire in the Skirling Pass. Qhorin sends Stonesnake and Jon to climb the mountain and eliminate the watchers. After a dangerous nighttime ascent, they attack three wildlings — killing two but Jon takes the third, a woman named Ygritte, as captive rather than killing her. Ygritte tells Jon the tale of Bael the Bard. At dawn, Qhorin and the others arrive and tell Jon he must execute the captive, but Jon lets Ygritte go free instead.

## Events & Actions
1. **Wildling watchfire spotted** — The scouting party sees a fire burning against the mountainside in the Skirling Pass, identified as wildling watchers.
2. **Discussion of the threat** — Squire Dalbridge, Ebben, Stonesnake, and Qhorin discuss the watchers; Qhorin determines there are likely two and they must not be allowed to blow their horn.
3. **Climbers selected** — Stonesnake volunteers as the best climber; Jon volunteers as the second. Qhorin orders Ghost to stay behind because white fur is visible in moonlight.
4. **Preparation for climb** — Jon and Stonesnake take ropes; Stonesnake carries iron spikes and felt-headed hammer. They leave garrons, helms, and mail behind. Jon says goodbye to Ghost.
5. **Nighttime ascent begins** — Stonesnake leads; they follow the trail upward, then leave it for a direct rock-face climb. Jon struggles with the difficult terrain, torn thumbnail, stiffening burned hand.
6. **Dangerous climbing** — They use ropes, spikes, and bare-handed climbing up a moonlit rock wall. Jon nearly slips once. They reach a crooked fissure two-thirds up and crawl along a shelf to get above the wildlings.
7. **Wildling camp observed** — Three wildlings seen (not two as expected): one asleep with red hair under furs, one feeding the fire, one watching the pass and wearing the horn.
8. **Attack** — Stonesnake leaps down; Jon follows with Longclaw. Stonesnake knocks the horn aside before the watcher can blow it. Jon's man fights with a burning brand; Jon kills him with Longclaw but the sword gets stuck in the falling body.
9. **Ygritte discovered** — The sleeper sits up and Jon grabs her with his dirk at her throat, then realizes the third wildling is a woman.
10. **Jon takes Ygritte captive** — Despite Stonesnake urging him to kill her, Jon accepts her yield and takes her prisoner.
11. **Signal sent** — Stonesnake throws a burning brand off the cliff as signal to the rangers below.
12. **Ygritte urges burning the dead** — She warns they should burn the killed wildlings. Jon remembers dead Othor and considers it. Stonesnake instead strips the bodies and throws them off the cliff.
13. **Interrogation attempt** — Jon and Stonesnake question Ygritte about wildling numbers, intentions, and Benjen Stark. She claims "hundreds and thousands" of free folk but refuses most questions.
14. **Shadowcats arrive** — Growling heard; Ygritte explains shadowcats have come for the dead bodies and will eat them.
15. **Ygritte tells the tale of Bael the Bard** — She recounts the legend of Bael the Bard who infiltrated Winterfell disguised as a singer, took Lord Brandon's daughter, hid with her in the crypts, and fathered the next Lord Stark. She tells the darker ending: Bael's son killed him at the Frozen Ford, and Bael's lover threw herself from a tower; the son was later flayed by one of his lords.
16. **Dawn arrives; Qhorin's party reunites** — Qhorin, Ebben, Squire Dalbridge arrive with garrons at dawn. Ghost reunites with Jon. Ygritte is frightened by Ghost.
17. **Qhorin orders Jon to execute Ygritte** — Qhorin, Ebben, and Dalbridge all argue against keeping a captive. Qhorin tells Jon to "do what needs be done" and leads the others away.
18. **Ygritte's final plea** — Left alone with Jon, Ygritte suggests he could join Mance Rayder. Jon refuses. She asks to be burned after; he says the smoke would be seen. She kneels and bares her neck.
19. **Jon releases Ygritte** — Jon raises Longclaw but cannot bring himself to execute her. He lowers the sword and tells her to go. She goes.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Tense and focused during the climb; admiring the natural beauty of the Frostfangs despite danger; conflicted about killing; shocked at discovering the third wildling is a woman; increasingly torn between duty and conscience; ultimately unable to kill Ygritte and acts against orders
- **Primary preoccupation:** The dangerous climb and mission to kill the watchers; then the moral weight of executing a captive who yielded to him
- **Key decisions made:** Volunteers for the climb; takes Ygritte captive instead of killing her; refuses Ygritte's offer to desert to Mance Rayder; releases Ygritte against Qhorin's explicit orders
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells himself "One step and then another, and I will not fall" repeatedly as a mantra to manage fear; thinks "He was his father's son. Wasn't he? Wasn't he?" — questioning whether he has the resolve to execute like Ned Stark, then proving he does not
