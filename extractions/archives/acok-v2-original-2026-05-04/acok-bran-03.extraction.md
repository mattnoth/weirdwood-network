# ACOK — Bran III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 22
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran III
- **first_available:** ACOK Bran III
- **location_primary:** Winterfell — Great Hall
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell — Bran's bedchamber tower, godswood
- **approximate_timeline:** Harvest feast at Winterfell during Robb's southern campaign
- **time_markers:** The feast runs from evening into night; music and dancing follow the meal; Bran retires and blows out his bedside candle; the wolf dream occurs at night while the feast continues ("this night was wildly alive, full of the howling of the man-pack at their play")
- **chapter_summary:** Bran rides Dancer into Winterfell's Great Hall to preside over a harvest feast as the Stark in Winterfell. He greets lords and guests, distributes food from the lord's portion, and recalls how many familiar faces are gone — dead or at war. Meera and Jojen Reed arrive unexpectedly and swear fealty to the Starks, pledging Greywater Watch. Bran, saddened by the dancing he cannot join, retires to his bedchamber and recalls his father's words about Ser Arthur Dayne and Howland Reed. He falls asleep and enters a wolf dream as Summer, encountering Meera and Jojen in the godswood before the dream dissolves at Jojen's touch.

## Physical Environment
- **Weather:** Cold outside — a gust of cold air enters when the hall doors open
- **Season indicators:** Harvest time; references to autumn ale; Bran's memory notes "Summer still reigned" during the welcoming feast for King Robert, implying that is no longer the case
- **Time of day:** Evening into night; the feast begins in the evening, dancing follows, Bran retires and blows out his candle in darkness; moonrise anticipated
- **Lighting:** Torchlight in the Great Hall (torches flame brighter when the doors open); dim-lit gallery outside the hall; candlelight in Bran's bedchamber; darkness after the candle is extinguished
- **Sounds:** Harp, fiddle, horn, and later drums and bladder-and-woodharp; singing ("Iron Lances," "The Burning of the Ships," "The Bear and the Maiden Fair," "The Night That Ended"); clash of cups and plates; snarling of hounds fighting for scraps; roar of talk and laughter; a huge curved warhorn blast that sets dogs barking; Hodor shouting "Hodor"; faint music drifting through shuttered window; howling of wolves in the godswood; rattle of iron
- **Smells:** Kitchen and Great Hall food smells so strong they carry into the godswood (in the wolf dream); stranger-scent mixed with leather, earth, and iron (wolf dream)
- **Notable sensory details:** Hot inside the Great Hall; Bran itches under his woolens; wine's "hot snaky fingers wriggling through his chest"; silver direwolf muzzle pressing against his palm on the goblet; the wolf dream touch "as light as a summer breeze" that dissolves the scene; steam rising off hot pools in the godswood (in Bran's waking vision)

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran rides Dancer into the Great Hall** — Dancer takes the low stone steps easily; men rise and cheer "Stark!" and "Winterfell!" as Bran rides the length of the hall.
2. **Bran is lifted to the high seat** — Osha and Hodor undo his straps and buckles, lift him from Dancer, and carry him to his father's seat on the dais.
3. **Bran opens the feast** — He welcomes guests in Robb's name, asks them to thank the gods for Robb's victories and the harvest, and raises his father's silver goblet. The hall toasts "A hundred more!"
4. **The feast is served** — Enormous quantities of food are served course after course; Lord Wyman contributed twenty casks of fish from White Harbor.
5. **Music and entertainment** — Lord Wyman's musicians play; songs include "Iron Lances," "The Burning of the Ships," "The Bear and the Maiden Fair," and later "The Night That Ended." Hodor listens and hops beside the piper.
6. **Bran distributes the lord's portion** — He sends specific dishes to Lady Hornwood, the Umbers, Cley Cerwyn, Joseth, Hodor, Old Nan, and (at Ser Rodrik's prompting) beets and turnips to the Frey wards.
7. **Osha breaks a flagon over a man's head** — One of Leobald Tallhart's men slides a hand under her skirts; she breaks the ale flagon on him to roars of laughter.
8. **Bran has a waking vision of the godswood** — He briefly experiences the godswood's coolness, steam, smells, and anticipates the moon rising and "my brother" singing to it.
9. **Meera and Jojen Reed arrive** — Alebelly announces them at the door. They walk the length of the hall and kneel before the dais.
10. **The Reeds swear fealty** — They recite the ancient oath of Greywater Watch, swearing by earth and water, bronze and iron, ice and fire. Bran accepts and offers the meat and mead of Winterfell.
11. **Jojen asks about the direwolves** — Bran says they're in the godswood and that Summer won't bite.
12. **Bran commands food for the Reeds** — He sends them mutton chops, aurochs, and beef-and-barley stew.
13. **Dancing begins** — Tables shoved back; Hother Umber blows his silver-banded warhorn during "The Night That Ended"; Mors Umber dances first with a serving girl; Lord Wyman dances with Beth Cassel, then Cley Cerwyn takes over; Hodor dances alone; Lady Hornwood declines Ser Rodrik and leaves.
14. **Bran asks to leave** — Saddened by the dancing he cannot do, he has Hodor summoned.
15. **Bran is carried out** — Maester Luwin and Hayhead lift him into Hodor's basket; guests stare; they exit through the lord's door.
16. **Joseth encountered in the gallery** — Joseth has a woman pressed against the wall; she screams when Hodor stops to watch. Bran tells Hodor to leave them be.
17. **Bran is put to bed** — Hodor carries him up the winding steps, Bran uses the iron bars to get to bed, Hodor removes his boots and breeches and is dismissed.
18. **Bran remembers Ned's words about Ser Arthur Dayne** — Ned called Dayne the finest knight he ever saw, said Dawn was "forged from the heart of a fallen star," and that Howland Reed saved him from Dayne. Ned grew sad and would say no more.
19. **Bran enters a wolf dream** — He becomes Summer, prowling the godswood with Shaggydog. They smell kitchen and feast smells, hear the man-pack howling.
20. **Summer and Shaggydog encounter the Reeds** — They hear iron rattling, race to find a female and young male (Meera and Jojen) who show no fear. Jojen observes that "the grey is strong... stronger than he knows" and asks if Meera can "feel him."
21. **Jojen touches Summer** — He reaches out and touches Summer's muzzle; at the contact, the dream dissolves and Bran falls.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pride swelling when cheered, quickly tempered by awareness that the cheers are not truly for him; sadness and loneliness pervading the feast as he catalogs who is gone; feeling like an observer watching from a distance, "seeing everything yet a part of nothing"; hot, tired, and flushed from wine; deep sadness when watching the dancing he can never do; wistful longing for the godswood's peace
- **Primary preoccupation:** The weight of performing as the Stark in Winterfell; acute consciousness of his broken body; grief for lost family and household members; curiosity about the Reed newcomers
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to send specific food gifts to lords as political gestures (with Maester Luwin's coaching); accepts the Reeds' fealty and improvises a response; decides to leave the feast when the dancing begins
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself "my belly's full to bursting" when actually he left the feast because the waking vision disoriented him and the heat/noise overwhelmed him; suppresses crying — "he couldn't" because "he was the Stark in Winterfell... and almost a man grown"
