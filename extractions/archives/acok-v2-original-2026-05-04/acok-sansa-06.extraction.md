Osney and Osfryd Kettleblack bring periodic battle reports as the fighting escalates. Cersei drinks heavily and delivers increasingly bitter commentary on women's powerlessness, her resentment of Jaime's advantages, and the realities awaiting them if the castle falls. After accusing Sansa of praying for Stannis's victory, Cersei reveals that Ser Ilyn Payne stands ready to execute them both rather than let Stannis take them alive.

## Events & Actions
1. **Feast underway during battle** — Sansa attends Cersei's feast in the Queen's Ballroom while the Battle of the Blackwater rages outside.
2. **First battle report** — Osney Kettleblack reports: fleets locked in battle, archers ashore but cut to pieces by the Hound, chain being raised, drunkards causing trouble in Flea Bottom (gold cloaks dispatched), Baelor's Sept full of people praying, Joffrey walking the walls with the Hand.
3. **Cersei reflects on women's weapons** — After a weeping knight's wife is removed, Cersei quotes her mother about tears being a woman's weapon vs. swords.
4. **Cersei lectures Sansa on queenship** — Cersei explains why she hosts the women (political necessity), what to expect if the castle falls (rape, murder), and that fear is better than gentleness for maintaining loyalty.
5. **Traitors caught** — Osfryd reports a groom and two maidservants caught sneaking out a postern with three of the king's horses. Cersei orders Ser Ilyn to execute them and display their heads on pikes outside the stables.
6. **Second battle report** — Osney reports hulks have gone up, the whole Blackwater awash with wildfire, a hundred ships or more burning.
7. **Third battle report** — Osney reports Stannis has landed men on tourney grounds with more coming, Mud Gate under attack, ram at King's Gate, Tyrion has gone out to lead defense.
8. **Cersei orders Joffrey withdrawn** — Learning Joffrey is at the Whores flinging Antler Men into the river, Cersei orders Ser Osmund to fetch him back to the castle, threatening him and Osney if they disobey.
9. **Cersei's childhood memory** — Cersei recalls being identical to Jaime as children, dressing in each other's clothes, but being denied a sword while Jaime received one; she laments the different fates assigned by gender.
10. **Merchants refused refuge** — Osfryd reports people gathering in the square seeking castle refuge; Cersei orders them turned away, with crossbowmen to kill any who won't leave.
11. **Songs after the meal** — A singer performs sad songs (Jonquil and Florian, Prince Aemon the Dragonknight, Nymeria's ten thousand ships); women weep; Sansa's eyes grow moist.
12. **Cersei accuses Sansa of treason** — Cersei confronts Sansa about her godswood visits, accusing her of praying for Stannis or Robb's victory. Sansa denies it, insisting she prays for Joffrey.
13. **Cersei forces Sansa to drink** — Cersei fills Sansa's cup with sweet plum wine and commands her to drain it.
14. **Ser Ilyn's true purpose revealed** — Cersei summons Ser Ilyn, who appears carrying Ice with blood on it. Cersei reveals he is there to execute them both if Stannis takes the castle — she will not let Stannis judge her or take them alive. She touches Sansa's hair, brushing it from her neck.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear permeates Sansa's experience — she perceives darkness despite the light, senses the joyless laughter, finds all food "flavored with fear." She grows increasingly terrified through Cersei's lectures about rape, murder, and the realities of a fallen city. She panics when Cersei mentions the godswood, desperately telling herself not to look at Dontos. The chapter ends with her in mortal terror as Cersei reveals Ser Ilyn's purpose.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and maintaining her facade of loyalty to Joffrey; concealing her connection to Ser Dontos and her godswood meetings; absorbing Cersei's bitter lessons about power and womanhood.
- **Key decisions made:** Sansa decides to maintain her denial about the godswood ("I only visit the godswood to pray") and silently resolves that if she is ever queen, she will rule through love rather than fear.
- **Self-deception flags:** Sansa's internal assurance that "Dontos promised me, my Florian would never fail me" shows her clinging to an idealized view of her would-be rescuer, casting him in the romantic role of Florian.
