# ACOK — Daenerys III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 41
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys III
- **first_available:** ACOK Daenerys III
- **location_primary:** Qarth
- **location_secondary:** Hall of a Thousand Thrones (recalled), bazaar, Xaro's manse
- **approximate_timeline:** Some time after Dany's arrival in Qarth; she has been receiving gifts from seekers and has spent considerable gold on bribes to the Pureborn
- **time_markers:** The chapter begins after the audience with the Pureborn (returning from the Hall); sun going down when Ser Jorah visits; "half a year gone" referenced by Quaithe regarding the firemage's former abilities; Dany mentions being "a child" when she left Pentos; "come the morrow" at chapter's end
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys returns from a failed appeal to the Pureborn of Qarth, who refused her request for ships and soldiers. Riding in Xaro Xhoan Daxos's palanquin, she discusses her dwindling options with the merchant prince, who again proposes marriage instead of military aid. In the bazaar, she witnesses a firemage performing feats of growing power, and Quaithe warns her that she must leave Qarth soon and cryptically advises her to go south to go north, east to go west. Back at her chambers, Ser Jorah explains Xaro's true motive — a Qartheen wedding custom that would let him claim a dragon — and advises against returning west to Illyrio Mopatis. Dany resolves to send Jorah to the warlock Pyat Pree the next day.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dany returns from the Pureborn** — Daenerys climbs into Xaro's palanquin after the Pureborn refuse her request for ships and soldiers, despite her having performed all the traditional protocols (sacrifice at the Temple of Memory, bribe to the Keeper of the Long List, persimmon to the Opener of the Door, blue silk slippers summoning).
2. **Dany removes the amethyst collar** — Frustrated by the Pureborn's refusal, Dany unfastens and flings aside the silver collar with the enchanted amethyst that Xaro claimed would ward against poisons.
3. **Dany and Xaro discuss the failed appeal** — Xaro asks about the audience; Dany reports that Mathos said nothing, Wendello praised her speech, and the Exquisite wept but refused her along with the rest.
4. **Dany considers recovering her bribes** — She suggests sending Ser Jorah to demand the return of her gifts, but Xaro warns that the Sorrowful Men would assassinate her in retaliation.
5. **Dany reflects on her situation** — She considers the political landscape of Qarth (Spicers, Tourmaline Brotherhood, Thirteen, Pureborn, warlocks) and recalls the gifts seekers have brought, including the dragon crown she kept.
6. **Xaro proposes marriage again** — He offers Dany a golden throne, travel to Yi Ti, and the dreaming city of poets. Dany refuses, insisting she will sail to Westeros.
7. **Dany asks the Thirteen for ships** — She proposes each of the Thirteen lend her ten ships; Xaro deflects, claiming she would have no crews and could not afford to pay sailors.
8. **Dany threatens to go to the warlocks** — She mentions heeding Pyat Pree; Xaro reacts sharply, warning that blue lips speak only lies and warlocks eat dust and drink shadows.
9. **Xaro enumerates his gifts** — He lists perfume, pomegranates, tumbling monkeys, spitting snakes, scrolls from lost Valyria, an idol's head, a serpent's foot, the palanquin, and the miniature army of a thousand jeweled knights.
10. **The procession stops at the bazaar** — The oxen halt as the crowd has gathered to watch a firemage.
11. **Dany watches the firemage** — Jhogo lifts Dany onto his horse. The firemage conjures a crackling orange ladder of swirling flame rising unsupported toward the latticed roof, then climbs it, each rung dissolving behind him in wisps of silver smoke until both ladder and mage vanish.
12. **Jhogo spots a cutpurse** — He points out a girl in a felt hat working the crowd; Dany finishes his sentence, having seen cutpurses in the Free Cities during her youth.
13. **Quaithe appears and warns Dany** — Quaithe declares the firemage's growing power is real, not a trick, and that Dany is the cause. She warns Dany must leave Qarth soon or never be permitted to leave. She gives cryptic directional advice: "To go north, you must journey south. To reach the west, you must go east. To go forward you must go back, and to touch the light you must pass beneath the shadow."
14. **Dany interprets Quaithe's words** — She thinks Quaithe means Asshai and asks what she would find there; Quaithe answers "Truth" and disappears into the crowd.
15. **Rakharo and Aggo dismiss Quaithe** — Rakharo calls her "spawn of shadows" who dare not show their face beneath the sun; both agree she is not to be trusted.
16. **Xaro warns against Asshai** — He says Quaithe's truths are not likely to make Dany smile, then presses more wine on her and speaks of love and trifles for the rest of the ride.
17. **Dany feeds her dragons** — In her chambers, she strips off her finery, puts on a purple silk robe, chops a snake, and chars the pieces over a brazier for the dragons, noting they have doubled in weight since Vaes Tolorro.
18. **Ser Jorah reveals the Qartheen wedding custom** — He explains that on the wedding day, each spouse may ask one thing of the other's worldly goods that cannot be denied. Xaro's true motive is to claim a dragon through marriage.
19. **Dany and Jorah discuss Illyrio** — Jorah warns Illyrio is untrustworthy ("he sold you to Khal Drogo"); Dany defends Illyrio but acknowledges the point. Jorah argues Illyrio cannot enthrone her alone and that sellswords won't win Westeros.
20. **Jorah counsels going east** — He warns that staying in one place makes Dany easier for enemies to find, and that the Targaryens' enemies will react when they learn of the dragons.
21. **Dany resolves to visit Pyat Pree** — She concludes the comet led her to Qarth for a reason, acknowledges her fear, and tells Ser Jorah to go to Pyat Pree on the morrow.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Weary, bitter, and frustrated after the Pureborn's refusal; oscillating between defiance ("the blood of the dragon does not weep") and self-doubt; afraid but forcing bravery; rueful about the faithlessness of men; sympathetic toward Viserys's bitter exile for the first time
- **Primary preoccupation:** How to acquire ships and soldiers to take Westeros; running out of allies and options in Qarth
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses Xaro's marriage proposal; refuses to sell the dragon crown; decides to send Ser Jorah to Pyat Pree and the warlocks as a last resort
- **Self-deception flags:** Dany acknowledges she is not as certain as she sounds when declaring nothing will turn her from her course; she wonders if Lord Redwyne will fight for her but has no basis for confidence; she defends Illyrio despite Jorah's valid points
