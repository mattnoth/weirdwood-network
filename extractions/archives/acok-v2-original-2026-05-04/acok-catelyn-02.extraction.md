# ACOK — Catelyn II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 23
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn II
- **first_available:** ACOK Catelyn II
- **location_primary:** Bitterbridge (Lord Caswell's castle and surrounding encampment)
- **location_secondary:** Rolling grasslands (travel), the Mander river, roseroad
- **approximate_timeline:** Mid-ACOK; Catelyn is traveling south from Riverrun on Robb's orders to treat with Renly Baratheon
- **time_markers:** Dawn at chapter opening; travel lasting multiple days ("for the past four days, they had seen no signs of war"); "still a half day's ride from Renly's camp" when intercepted; melee has been ongoing "a day or more"; feast at night; music "seeping out into the night" during rooftop conversation; messenger arrives at night after the feast
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn wakes at dawn on the grasslands while traveling south to treat with Renly Baratheon on Robb's behalf. She recalls her reluctance and her farewell to her dying father at Riverrun. Her party is intercepted by Ser Colen of Greenpools and escorted to Renly's massive encampment at Bitterbridge, where a great melee is underway. Brienne of Tarth wins the melee, defeating Ser Loras Tyrell, and is granted a place in Renly's Rainbow Guard. Catelyn is presented to Renly and attends a lavish farewell feast at Lord Caswell's castle. After the feast, Renly takes Catelyn to the castle roof to display his vast host and presses her to convince Robb to bend the knee. A rider arrives with news that Storm's End is besieged by Stannis Baratheon.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn wakes at dawn on the grasslands** — She dreams of her children whole and safe, and Ned alive, then wakes aching, weary, and alone.
2. **Morning camp routine** — Shadd cooks oats and brews nettle tea; Ser Wendel offers to hunt quail but Catelyn insists on simple fare for speed.
3. **Catelyn recalls her departure from Riverrun** — She remembers arguing with Robb about the mission to Renly, Robb's insistence, and her painful farewell to her dying father Hoster, who called her "Minisa."
4. **The party rides south toward the upper Mander** — Catelyn is first mounted and sets the pace; Hal Mollen bears the Stark banner.
5. **Robin Flint spots a far-eyes at a windmill** — A scout watching from a distant windmill roof; by the time Catelyn's party reaches it, the man is gone.
6. **Renly's outriders intercept Catelyn's party** — Twenty mailed and mounted men led by Ser Colen of Greenpools; Catelyn identifies herself as envoy from "Robb Stark, the King in the North."
7. **Ser Colen escorts Catelyn to Renly's camp at Bitterbridge** — His men form a double column flanking her party.
8. **Catelyn observes Renly's enormous host** — Thousands of cookfires, siege engines, pikes, pavilions, banners of numerous southern houses on both sides of the Mander.
9. **A melee is in progress below the castle** — Fewer than a score of knights remain from an original 116; the grounds are torn and muddy.
10. **Brienne of Tarth defeats multiple opponents including Red Ronnet** — The knight in cobalt blue armor fights with a morningstar; unhorsed earlier combatants.
11. **Brienne defeats Ser Loras Tyrell** — After losing her morningstar, Brienne charges into Loras's axe blow, grapples him off his horse, and forces his yield with a dirk at his visor.
12. **Brienne is revealed to be a woman** — Ser Colen explains to Catelyn; Brienne removes her helm revealing her appearance.
13. **Brienne asks to join the Rainbow Guard** — Renly grants her request; she swears her life to his protection.
14. **Ser Colen presents Catelyn to Renly** — Catelyn corrects him: "Lord of Winterfell and King in the North."
15. **Renly and his lords react to Catelyn's embassy** — Renly promises justice for Ned's murder; Margaery offers condolences; Lord Randyll Tarly criticizes Robb for sending his mother; Catelyn retorts that Robb is "warring, not playing at tourney."
16. **Renly asks about Jaime Lannister** — Catelyn confirms Jaime is held prisoner at Riverrun; Lord Rowan seems dismayed he's still alive.
17. **Renly assigns Catelyn his pavilion and invites her to Lord Caswell's feast** — A steward in Storm's End livery arranges for her companions.
18. **Catelyn examines Renly's lavish pavilion** — She notes the extravagance of its furnishings; sees her reflection in his polished armor and thinks "the face of a drowned woman."
19. **The farewell feast at Lord Caswell's castle** — Catelyn seated on the dais between Lord Rowan and Ser Jon Fossoway; lavish food served; singers and tumblers perform.
20. **Catelyn observes the young lords' boasting and folly** — Josua and Elyas argue about King's Landing; Ser Tanton vows to slay the Hound with his foot in a gravy boat; a fool in lion costume chases a dwarf ("Kinslayer" jest).
21. **Lord Rowan and Catelyn reflect on the youth of the knights** — "They are all so young"; Catelyn calls them "knights of summer."
22. **Brienne speaks to Catelyn about death and songs** — Claims "winter will never come for the likes of us" because "it's always summer in the songs."
23. **Renly takes Catelyn to the castle rooftop** — Brienne offers to escort but Renly dismisses her, visibly hurting Brienne.
24. **Renly asks about Barristan Selmy** — Reveals Selmy was dismissed by the Lannisters, that Brienne's Rainbow Guard cloak was kept for Selmy; Selmy has not appeared at Highgarden or Riverrun.
25. **Renly recounts the night of Robert's death** — He offered Ned a hundred swords to seize Joffrey; Ned refused; Renly fled King's Landing.
26. **Renly displays his host from the rooftop** — Claims to have twice Robb's forty thousand, plus Mace Tyrell's ten thousand at Highgarden, Storm's End garrison, and expects Dornish support; also counts on Stannis's strength.
27. **Renly proposes terms** — Will confirm Robb in lands, titles, and honors; Robb may call himself King in the North if he bends the knee; references the Stark king who knelt to Aegon.
28. **A messenger arrives from Storm's End** — A rider in a winged helm reports that Storm's End is besieged — not by Lannisters, but by Stannis Baratheon, who now calls himself king.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Weary, grief-stricken, homesick. She aches for her children and Ned. She is tired of being strong but forces herself to continue. At the feast, queasy from rich food. On the rooftop, she refuses to count the stars/fires — a moment of dread. Throughout, she maintains sharp political awareness despite her emotional exhaustion.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting home to her children; completing the mission to Renly as quickly as possible; assessing whether Renly is friend or foe. She yearns to go north but is driven south.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses Ser Wendel's quail for speed; does not reveal Robb's military dispositions to Renly; does not kneel; maintains diplomatic composure despite provocation from Lord Tarly.
- **Self-deception flags:** None overt. Catelyn is lucid about her situation, though she clings to the hope of returning home soon despite evidence that circumstances keep driving her further from it ("Fate drives me south and south again... when it is north I should be going").
