- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Maegor's Holdfast, the godswood, the serpentine steps
- **approximate_timeline:** Early-to-mid ACOK; after Tyrion's arrival as Hand and dismissal of Janos Slynt; before Stannis's assault on King's Landing; preparations underway for Tyrek Lannister's wedding feast
- **time_markers:** Night; the chapter takes place entirely at night — Sansa reads the note during the evening, a fire is lit, she waits in bed, hears shouting, then ventures out in "the black of night"; moonlight mentioned; Ser Preston paces the drawbridge at night
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa finds an anonymous note under her pillow telling her to come to the godswood if she wants to go home. She agonizes over whether it is genuine or a trap, burns the note, and nearly stays in bed. When a disturbance draws the guards away from the drawbridge, she seizes the opportunity and slips out to the godswood. There she meets Ser Dontos Hollard, who reveals himself as the note's author and vows before the heart tree to help her escape King's Landing. On her way back, she encounters Sandor Clegane on the serpentine steps, who escorts her back to Maegor's Holdfast, where Ser Boros Blount now guards the bridge and explains the disturbance was hungry smallfolk at the gates.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa reads the anonymous note** — She discovers a folded parchment under her pillow reading "Come to the godswood tonight, if you want to go home." She reads it repeatedly, agonizing over its meaning and whether it is genuine or a trap.
2. **Sansa reflects on potential rescuers** — She considers the Redwyne twins, Ser Balon Swann, and Beric Dondarrion as possible senders.
3. **Sansa considers showing the note to Cersei** — She thinks about taking it to the queen to prove her loyalty but does not.
4. **The bedmaid enters and lights a fire** — Sansa hides the note and requests a fire. She suspects the maid spies for the queen or Varys.
5. **Sansa burns the note** — She thrusts the parchment into the flames and watches it curl and blacken.
6. **Sansa observes Ser Preston Greenfield on the drawbridge** — She sees the short knight guarding the bridge from her window and realizes she cannot pass him unnoticed.
7. **Sansa goes to bed but cannot sleep** — She lies awake, thinking of Septa Mordane, Jeyne Poole, her siblings, and her captivity.
8. **Disturbance erupts in the castle** — Distant shouting grows louder; men run on the walls with spears and torches; horses and pounding feet are heard.
9. **Sansa sees the drawbridge unguarded** — Ser Preston has left his post; the bridge is down and undefended.
10. **Sansa dresses and arms herself** — She throws on a grey cloak and takes her meat knife, resolving that death is better than continued abuse.
11. **Sansa slips out of Maegor's Holdfast** — She waits for red-cloaked swordsmen to pass, then darts across the drawbridge.
12. **Sansa sees Joffrey arming in the yard** — She glimpses Ser Preston and three other Kingsguard helping Joffrey into armor; Joffrey shouts for his sword and crossbow. Sansa avoids detection.
13. **Sansa climbs the serpentine steps** — She is panting at the top; she presses against a wall in a shadowy colonnade; a ragged black cat with a chewed-off ear brushes her leg and spits at her.
14. **Sansa reaches the godswood** — She reflects on the old gods, prays for a true knight, and moves from tree to tree feeling the bark.
15. **Ser Dontos reveals himself** — He steps from the shadows and identifies himself as the note's sender. Sansa is heartbroken that it is Dontos rather than a true knight.
16. **Dontos vows to help Sansa escape** — He promises to find a way to get her out of the castle and onto a ship home, but says it cannot happen tonight. He places his hand on the heart tree and swears a solemn oath.
17. **Dontos establishes the godswood as their meeting place** — He tells Sansa to come to the godswood as often as she can; nowhere else is safe to talk because "the stones have ears in the Red Keep."
18. **Dontos warns of the need for deception** — He tells Sansa he may seem cruel or mocking in public and asks forgiveness; both must play roles.
19. **Sansa kisses Dontos on the cheek** — She calls him "my Florian" and departs.
20. **Sansa runs back through the pig yard and river walk** — She thinks of home and the songs of Florian and Jonquil.
21. **Sandor Clegane intercepts Sansa on the serpentine steps** — He lurches from a hidden doorway; she collides with him; he grabs her wrist with iron fingers.
22. **Sansa lies about her purpose** — She tells Sandor she was praying in the godswood for her father and the king's safety. He does not believe her.
23. **Sandor comments on Sansa's appearance** — He tells her she looks "almost a woman" and demands a song.
24. **Sandor tells the Clegane family origin story** — His grandfather was kennelmaster at the Rock; saved Lord Tytos from a lioness, lost a leg, was rewarded with lands and a towerhouse. The three dogs on their sigil are the three that died.
25. **Sandor delivers his philosophy** — "A hound will die for you, but never lie to you. And he'll look you straight in the face."
26. **Sandor escorts Sansa to Maegor's Holdfast** — He gives her an oddly gentle push and walks her back in brooding silence.
27. **Ser Boros Blount challenges them at the drawbridge** — He now guards the bridge; Sandor dismisses him ("Fuck your ser, Boros").
28. **Ser Boros explains the disturbance** — Hungry smallfolk gathered at the gate after hearing of Tyrek's wedding feast preparations; Joffrey led a sortie to disperse them.
29. **Sansa thinks of Stannis facing Joffrey** — She silently thinks "Let us see how brave he is when he faces my brother" (referring to Stannis).
30. **Sandor demands a song on the stairs** — Sansa offers Florian and Jonquil; he rejects it ("A fool and his cunt") but says he'll have a song from her someday. He calls her a bad liar and says everyone in the Red Keep lies better than she does.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious, fearful, hopeful, and conflicted. Sansa oscillates between desperate hope that someone will rescue her and fear that the note is a trap from Joffrey or Cersei. She is deeply lonely, missing Jeyne Poole, Septa Mordane, and even Arya. By chapter's end she feels renewed hope calling Dontos "my Florian," but is shaken by the encounter with Sandor.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Whether to trust the note and go to the godswood; whether escape from King's Landing is possible; survival through deception and compliance.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to burn the note (destroying evidence); decides to go to the godswood despite her fears; decides to trust Dontos and accept his help; lies to Sandor about her purpose in the godswood.
- **Self-deception flags:** Sansa believes Arya is safe at Winterfell "dancing and sewing, playing with Bran and baby Rickon." She romanticizes Dontos as "my Florian" despite recognizing he is a drunken fool — she clings to the songs and their promise of noble rescue. She rationalizes the bruise from Ser Meryn as "her own fault" for not hiding her feelings better.
