## Chapter Metadata

- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing

- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly before the Battle of the Blackwater; Stannis's vanguard arrived two nights prior; the city is preparing for siege
- **time_markers:** "two nights ago during the black of the moon" (vanguard arrival); nighttime (godswood meeting, rooftop scene); Sansa wakes in "pale light of morning"; Cersei is "breaking her fast" when Sansa is brought to her
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa meets Ser Dontos in the godswood at night, where he reports a friend will arrange a ship for her escape but urges patience. She climbs to the roof and encounters the Hound, who delivers a bitter speech about killing, knighthood, and the nature of the world. That night Sansa dreams of the riot. She wakes to discover she has had her first flowering and panics, trying to burn the bloodstained bedclothes before servants discover her. She is brought to Queen Cersei, who confirms her womanhood and offers cynical counsel about love, marriage, and Joffrey.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa meets Dontos in the godswood at night** — Dontos reports that a friend will hire a swift ship for their escape but insists the time is not yet right; Sansa urges immediate action but Dontos explains the city gates are heavily guarded and the river closed
2. **Dontos shares intelligence** — Reports that Stannis burned the godswood at Storm's End at the red priestess's command; that Varys pays for information; that Moon Boy may be Varys's agent; that Stannis has vowed to burn the Great Sept of Baelor
3. **Sansa reflects on the approaching threat** — She observes that Stannis's vanguard (5,000 men under Ser Guyard Morrigen) arrived two nights ago; the city is terrified; the Blackwater is empty of civilian traffic
4. **Dontos tries to kiss Sansa** — She dodges his wet lips and kisses him lightly on an unshaven cheek instead; bids him good night
5. **Sansa crosses the unguarded drawbridge to Maegor's Holdfast** — Gold cloaks have been moved to city walls; Kingsguard have other duties; Sansa can go where she wants within the castle
6. **Sansa climbs to the roof instead of entering her bedchamber** — She feels trapped by her room; from the roof she can see the whole city, the fires, the catapults at the Mud Gate, soldiers on the walls
7. **Sansa experiences a sharp pain in her belly and nearly falls** — The Hound catches her arm and steadies her
8. **The Hound confronts Sansa on the rooftop** — He reminds her he saved her during the riot; mocks her gratitude; delivers a nihilistic speech about killing; draws his longsword and presses it to her throat; declares knighthood and gods are lies; tells her to leave
9. **Sansa flees the rooftop** — She reflects that she is afraid of the Hound but wishes Dontos had some of his ferocity; she affirms to herself that gods and true knights exist
10. **Sansa has a nightmare about the riot** — She dreams of being dragged from her horse, beaten, and stabbed in the belly; calls for heroes who never come
11. **Sansa wakes to discover her first flowering** — She finds blood on her thighs and the bedclothes; panics
12. **Sansa attempts to destroy the evidence** — She cuts the stain from the sheet with a knife, then tries to burn the sheet, blanket, and featherbed in the fireplace; smoke fills the room
13. **Servants discover Sansa** — Three women pull her away from the fire; the bedclothes are burnt but her thighs are bloody again; servants bathe her, wash her hair, give her a cloth and a green wool shift
14. **Sansa is brought to Queen Cersei** — Cersei confirms Sansa has had her first flowering; discusses what it means for her marriage to Joffrey
15. **Cersei shares personal history** — She recounts Joffrey's difficult birth, Robert's absence during her labors, Jaime's insistence on being present; she discusses Joffrey's cruelty toward Sansa, blaming Arya's shaming of him at the Trident
16. **Cersei delivers her closing counsel** — "Love is poison. A sweet poison, yes, but it will kill you all the same."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fear predominates — fear of the approaching battle, of Joffrey, of marriage, of her own body's betrayal. Also shame (at her panicked attempt to burn the bedclothes), grief (for her father, her wolf, her lost life), and a flicker of defiance (wanting the Sept burned). Light-headed and feverish during the godswood scene. Calm resignation by the end.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Escape from King's Landing; dread of being married to Joffrey now that she has flowered; the approaching siege
- **Key decisions made:** Continues to rely on Dontos's escape plan despite frustration with his pace and drunkenness; attempts to destroy evidence of her flowering (fails)
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells Cersei "I love His Grace with all my heart" — a deliberate survival lie, not self-deception. Her internal affirmation that "there are gods, and there are true knights too. All the stories can't be lies" may be willful self-reassurance against the Hound's nihilism.
