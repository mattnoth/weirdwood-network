- **chapter_summary:** Theon Greyjoy returns to Pyke after ten years as a ward of Eddard Stark, bearing a letter from Robb Stark proposing an alliance against the Lannisters. He arrives at Lordsport aboard the merchant ship Myraham, where he is met not by an honor guard but by his uncle Aeron "Damphair" Greyjoy, now a priest of the Drowned God. Aeron anoints Theon with seawater and rides with him to Pyke in cold silence. At the castle, Theon finds no warm welcome — his father Lord Balon criticizes his mainlander clothing and gold chain, burns Robb's letter, and reveals he has his own plans for war involving a different, undefended target.

## Events & Actions
1. **Theon views Pyke from the sea** — The Myraham sails past Pyke, and Theon observes his family's castle from the deck, seeing the Greyjoy banner and the red comet in the sky. He touches the oilskin pouch containing Robb Stark's letter.
2. **Theon takes the captain's daughter below** — He leads her to his cabin for sex, instructing the captain to summon him when they reach Lordsport.
3. **Sexual encounter in the cabin** — Theon has oral sex with the captain's daughter while reflecting on the Old Way and his ambitions. He muses that this is his season and his hour.
4. **Captain's daughter begs to stay** — She asks to be his salt wife or serve in his castle. He refuses and tells her she's likely pregnant with a king's bastard.
5. **The Myraham rounds the point to Lordsport** — Theon observes the rebuilt Botley castle, the village of Lordsport, the harbor full of longships (fifty or sixty), and searches for the Silence (absent) and the Great Kraken (present).
6. **Theon arrives at Lordsport unrecognized** — No one at the dock knows who he is. He pays the captain a golden dragon and demands a horse from the innkeeper.
7. **Aeron Greyjoy reveals himself** — The Drowned God priest announces he will take his nephew to Pyke. The smallfolk kneel; the innkeeper murmurs "Damphair."
8. **Aeron anoints Theon with seawater** — He pours seawater over Theon's head and they recite the Drowned God's prayer: "What is dead may never die, but rises again, harder and stronger."
9. **Theon and Aeron ride to Pyke** — A long, mostly silent ride through bare stony hills. Aeron refuses to discuss why longships are hosting. He reveals: Theon's mother is on Harlaw with a cough; Asha is on Great Wyk; Dagmer is on Old Wyk rousting bannermen. He dismisses Theon's talk of Robb and the comet.
10. **Aeron challenges Theon's inheritance** — When Theon insists he is heir, Aeron says "we shall see" and notes Asha lives. He warns Balon will never hand the islands to "a Stark."
11. **Theon arrives at Pyke at sunset** — He observes the scars of Robert's war on the walls and the rebuilt south tower. Guards watch with strangers' eyes.
12. **Aeron departs without staying** — Refuses meat and mead, rides back out under the portcullis.
13. **Helya shows Theon to the Bloody Keep** — Not his old Sea Tower rooms. The chambers are damp, mildewed, musty, long-unopened. He demands hot water, fire, and clean rushes.
14. **Theon dresses to meet his father** — He carefully selects mainlander-style finery: black velvet doublet with golden kraken, gold chain, silk gloves with golden tracery.
15. **Theon crosses bridges to the Sea Tower** — Three bridges, each narrower than the last; the final one of rope and wood, swaying in the wind. His heart is in his mouth.
16. **Theon meets Lord Balon in the Sea Tower solar** — Balon sits beneath sealskins by a brazier. He is gaunt, bone-thin, aged, smaller than Theon remembered.
17. **Balon criticizes Theon's appearance** — He mocks the velvet and silk as feminine/mainlander. He asks whether the gold chain was paid for with gold or iron price.
18. **Balon rips off Theon's gold chain** — Yanks it off Theon's neck, snapping it, and drops it on the brazier. Declares he won't have his son "bedeck himself like a whore."
19. **Theon presents Robb's proposal** — An alliance: ironborn attack the west by sea while Robb attacks through the Golden Tooth. Theon asks for Casterly Rock as his seat in reward.
20. **Balon rejects the alliance and burns the letter** — He objects to Robb "giving" him a crown. "No man gives me a crown. I pay the iron price." He burns the letter on the brazier.
21. **Balon strikes Theon** — A stinging backhand across Theon's cheek when Theon asks if he's gone mad.
22. **Balon reveals his own war plans** — He intends to carve a kingdom, but not from the west. He hungers for a "different plum" — not so juicy sweet, but "ripe and undefended." Theon realizes what he means (the target is left unstated).

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Anxious anticipation giving way to wounded pride and growing anger. Theon is eager to impress his father, proud of his plan and his position with Robb, but is systematically humiliated — unrecognized at Lordsport, treated coldly by Aeron, housed in damp forgotten chambers, mocked and struck by his father. He oscillates between hurt and defiance.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Proving himself worthy as Balon's heir and securing the alliance with Robb, which he sees as *his* plan, *his* glory, and eventually *his* crown. He craves recognition from his father and the ironborn.
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to dress in fine mainlander clothing to meet his father (a critical misjudgment); abandons the captain's daughter without hesitation; presents Robb's proposal despite Balon's hostility; stands his ground verbally even after being struck.
- **Self-deception flags:** Theon tells himself the comet is "my comet" and this is "my hour." He imagines himself as the man who succeeded where Balon failed. He assumes Robb's letter is "paper as good as a crown." He fails to anticipate how thoroughly the ironborn will see him as a mainlander. He dismisses Aeron's warning about his inheritance. He does not recognize that dressing in gold and velvet will antagonize his father.
