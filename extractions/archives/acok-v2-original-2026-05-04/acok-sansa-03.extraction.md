# ACOK — Sansa III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 33

- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Tower of the Hand, Maegor's Holdfast (mentioned), Oxcross (referenced), Lannisport (referenced), Casterly Rock (referenced), Golden Tooth (referenced), Winterfell (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the Battle of Oxcross; word of the battle reached King's Landing that morning
- **time_markers:** "not three days ride from Lannisport" (describing Oxcross location); "six nights gone" (when Robb attacked Stafford's camp); "word only this morning" (news of the battle); dark when Sansa wakes after sleeping with dreamwine; "last night" (when Joffrey claims to have shot a man)
- **chapter_summary:** Sandor Clegane summons Sansa to Joffrey, who is enraged by Robb Stark's victory at Oxcross over Ser Stafford Lannister's host. Joffrey publicly humiliates and has Sansa beaten by Ser Boros Blount and stripped to the waist before the court. Tyrion Lannister intervenes with Bronn and Timett, stopping the abuse and rebuking Joffrey. Sansa is taken to the Tower of the Hand, tended by Maester Frenken, and given dreamwine. Later, Tyrion visits her to discuss the battle, hints he does not intend for her to marry Joffrey, and offers her a guard of clansmen, which she refuses, wanting to preserve her secret escape plan with Ser Dontos.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sandor Clegane summons Sansa** — The Hound warns her the king is waiting and tells her the cause is Robb, not something she did.
2. **Sansa dresses hastily** — She chooses a gown Joffrey likes, worrying he discovered her meetings with Ser Dontos.
3. **Procession through the lower bailey** — Sansa walks with the Hound past courtiers; she notices Lord Gyles coughing, the Redwyne twins avoiding her gaze, and a yellow cat dying with a crossbow quarrel through its ribs.
4. **Ser Dontos whispers encouragement** — He approaches on his broomstick horse and tells Sansa to "be brave."
5. **Joffrey confronts Sansa** — He orders her to answer for Robb's treasons, wielding his crossbow.
6. **Lancel Lannister reports the Battle of Oxcross** — He describes Robb's attack as sorcery with wargs and claims northmen feasted on the slain.
7. **Joffrey threatens Sansa with his crossbow** — He points the crossbow at her face and boasts of killing a man and wounding a woman the previous night who came to the gates begging for bread.
8. **Joffrey reveals constraint on harming Sansa** — He says his mother (Cersei) forbids him from shooting Sansa because it would endanger Jaime's life.
9. **Dontos attempts to shield Sansa** — He offers to "beat" her himself, whacking her with his melon morningstar while she staggers and the crowd laughs.
10. **Joffrey orders Boros and Meryn** — Unsatisfied by the comic beating, Joffrey commands his Kingsguard to do it properly.
11. **Ser Boros beats Sansa** — He punches her in the belly, grabs her hair, hits her across the thighs with the flat of his sword repeatedly.
12. **The Hound says "Enough"** — Sandor Clegane verbally protests the beating.
13. **Joffrey orders Sansa stripped** — He commands Boros to "make her naked"; Boros tears her bodice, baring her to the waist.
14. **Tyrion Lannister intervenes** — He arrives with Bronn and Timett, stopping the abuse and confronting Joffrey and the Kingsguard.
15. **Tyrion rebukes Joffrey** — He compares Joffrey to Aerys Targaryen, warns that wanton brutality will not win love, and threatens Ser Boros through Bronn and Timett.
16. **Sandor Clegane gives Sansa his cloak** — At Tyrion's request for someone to cover the girl, the Hound unfastens his cloak and tosses it to her.
17. **Sansa is taken to the Tower of the Hand** — Tyrion's men escort her there instead of to Maegor's Holdfast.
18. **Serving girls bathe and tend to Sansa** — They strip the ruined gown, wash melon juice from her hair and face.
19. **Maester Frenken treats Sansa's wounds** — He applies salve to welts on her legs and gives her dreamwine with honey.
20. **Sansa sleeps and wakes in darkness** — She wakes in pain, recognizes the room as Arya's old bedchamber.
21. **Clanwoman guard blocks Sansa** — A hard-faced woman with a necklace of human ears prevents Sansa from going to the godswood.
22. **Food is brought to Sansa** — She refuses it but drinks water and nibbles an olive.
23. **Tyrion visits Sansa privately** — He gives her a frank account of the Battle of Oxcross, debunking the sorcery claims.
24. **Tyrion reveals he does not intend Sansa to wed Joffrey** — He says no marriage will reconcile Stark and Lannister now.
25. **Tyrion probes Sansa's feelings** — He asks about her prayers in the godswood and whether she has flowered; she has not.
26. **Tyrion offers clansmen as guards** — He suggests Stone Crows, then Chella of the Black Ears; Sansa refuses, fearing it would prevent Dontos from rescuing her.
27. **Sansa fabricates a lie to leave** — She claims ghosts of her father's men would give her nightmares in the Tower of the Hand.
28. **Tyrion escorts Sansa back to her chambers** — He accepts her request and offers to walk her back.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terror during the summons and public beating; desperate hope that Joffrey hasn't discovered her meetings with Dontos; humiliation at being stripped before the court; relief when Tyrion intervenes; bitter disillusionment with knights and chivalry; secret exultation at Robb's victory; wariness and distrust during her conversation with Tyrion; pain from her wounds throughout
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival — performing loyalty to Joffrey while hiding her true feelings and protecting her secret escape plan with Ser Dontos
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses Tyrion's offer to stay in the Tower of the Hand with clansmen guards, prioritizing her escape plan with Dontos over physical safety; fabricates a convincing lie about ghosts to justify leaving
- **Self-deception flags:** None — Sansa is unusually clear-eyed in this chapter. She sees through the performative cruelty, recognizes that no true knight helped her, and is fully aware of the gap between her spoken words and her real feelings.
