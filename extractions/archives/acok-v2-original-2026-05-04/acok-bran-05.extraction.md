Bran tries to warn the potential drowning victims but is not believed. Ser Rodrik returns with the prisoner Reek, having killed the Bastard of Bolton and discovered Lady Hornwood's horrible death.

## Events & Actions
1. **Alebelly fetches Bran from the forge** — Bran is working the bellows for Mikken when Alebelly summons him to the maester's turret for a message from King Robb.
2. **Maester Luwin delivers news of Oxcross** — Robb has won a great victory, shattering a Lannister army under Ser Stafford Lannister, and taken several castles. He writes from Ashemark, the former Marbrand stronghold.
3. **Death of Ser Stevron Frey announced** — Ser Stevron took a wound at Oxcross not thought serious but died three days later in his sleep.
4. **The Walders callously discuss Frey succession** — Big Walder and Little Walder show no grief, instead calculating who is now heir. Big Walder recites the line: Ser Ryman, then Edwyn, Black Walder, Petyr Pimple, Aegon and his sons. Big Walder declares he will be lord regardless.
5. **Bran is carried out by Osha** — Bran asks to be excused; Osha carries him since Hodor is busy in the stables.
6. **Bran asks Osha about traveling north** — She tells him to follow the Ice Dragon constellation, chasing the blue star in the rider's eye. She confirms she has seen giants; mentions the children of the forest and white walkers.
7. **Bran asks about the three-eyed crow** — Osha says she has never seen one and wouldn't want to.
8. **Jojen and Meera visit Bran's bedchamber** — Jojen asks Bran to trust his words. Bran agrees.
9. **Jojen reveals the green dream of the sea** — He dreamed the sea flooded Winterfell, with black waves crashing against gates and towers. He identifies the drowned men: Alebelly, Septon Chayle, and Mikken.
10. **Bran describes his own dreams to Jojen** — Wolf dreams where he runs and hunts; dreams of the crow telling him to fly; a tree calling his name; and worst, falling dreams that began after his actual fall.
11. **Jojen names Bran a warg** — He tells Bran he is a warg/shapechanger/beastling, that his wolf dreams are real, that the power is strong in him, and that he is "the winged wolf" who will never fly unless he opens his third eye. He pokes Bran in the forehead.
12. **Bran attempts to open his third eye** — He tries wrinkling his forehead and poking at it but cannot see differently.
13. **Bran warns Mikken, Septon Chayle, and Alebelly of drowning** — Mikken laughs it off. Septon Chayle dismisses it, noting he is a strong swimmer. Only Alebelly takes it seriously, stops bathing, and refuses to go near the well.
14. **Alebelly is forcibly bathed** — Six guards throw him into a tub of scalding water and scrub him raw while he screams they're drowning him.
15. **Ser Rodrik returns with Reek** — The prisoner is identified as Reek, who served the Bastard of Bolton and helped murder Lady Hornwood.
16. **The Bastard of Bolton's death reported** — Ser Rodrik's men caught him on Hornwood land and shot him with arrows as he tried to flee. Lady Hornwood was found dead in a tower, starving, with bloody mouth and chewed-off fingers.
17. **Ser Rodrik discusses the political knot** — The Bastard married Lady Hornwood with vows before septon and heart tree, bedded her before witnesses, and had her sign a will naming him heir. Manderly knights and Dreadfort men are killing each other in Hornwood forests. Ser Rodrik must keep Reek alive as a witness.
18. **Bran tells Ser Rodrik about Jojen's dream** — Ser Rodrik says he won't take Alebelly if he rides against the raiders.
19. **Three-sided tile game** — Bran, Jojen, and Meera play tiles that night. Jojen insists green dreams cannot be changed. Meera argues the gods wouldn't send warnings if they couldn't be heeded.
20. **Jojen reveals the second green dream** — Reek skins the faces off Bran and Rickon with a long red blade; Bran and Rickon lie dead at his feet.
21. **Jojen's final vision** — He saw Bran and Rickon in the Winterfell crypts, down in the dark with all the dead kings and their stone wolves.
22. **Meera proposes killing Reek** — She suggests driving a spear through his heart in the dungeon, but Jojen says gaolers and guards will stop her.
23. **Bran considers fleeing** — He suggests going to Greywater Watch or to "the crow," but Jojen says it will not matter; the green dreams do not lie.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Excited at Robb's victory, then disquieted remembering Osha's warning that Robb was "marching the wrong way." Sickened by the Walders' callous reaction to their uncle's death. Frightened by Jojen's green dreams — both the drowning vision and especially the vision of Reek skinning his and Rickon's faces. Muddled and confused after Jojen's talk of the third eye. Hopeful briefly when Ser Rodrik says he won't take Alebelly. Desperate at the chapter's end, considering fleeing.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The green dreams — Jojen's prophecy of Winterfell flooding and of Bran and Rickon's deaths; also grappling with being named a warg.
- **Key decisions made:** Agrees to trust Jojen's words; attempts to warn Mikken, Septon Chayle, and Alebelly about the drowning vision; considers fleeing to Greywater Watch or "the crow."
- **Self-deception flags:** None clearly present — Bran is unusually open to uncomfortable truths in this chapter, though he briefly tries to rationalize that the prophecy can be avoided ("If they stay away from the sea").
