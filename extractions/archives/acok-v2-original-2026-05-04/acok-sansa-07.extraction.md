# ACOK — Sansa VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 63
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Sansa VII
- **first_available:** ACOK Sansa VII
- **location_primary:** Red Keep — Queen's Ballroom (Maegor's Holdfast)
- **location_secondary:** Sansa's bedchamber (Red Keep); King's Landing (streets, gates, river); Blackwater
- **approximate_timeline:** During and immediately after the Battle of the Blackwater; chapter spans from mid-battle through dawn of the following day
- **time_markers:** Battle ongoing at chapter start (night); torches burning low during the vigil in the ballroom; sky dark with fire reflections; "the first faint hint of dawn was visible in the east" at chapter end; bells ringing at dawn
- **chapter_summary:** The battle of the Blackwater reaches its crisis. Ser Lancel and Osney Kettleblack bring reports of collapse to Cersei in the Queen's Ballroom; Cersei orders the drawbridge raised and Joffrey brought inside Maegor's Holdfast, then strikes Lancel and abandons the hall. Sansa calms the frightened women and children before retreating to her bedchamber, where she finds the Hound waiting. He drunkenly demands a song at knifepoint, announces he is leaving, and departs after she sings the hymn to the Mother. At dawn, bells ring across King's Landing and Ser Dontos brings news that Lord Tywin, Mace Tyrell, and Randyll Tarly have routed Stannis's host, with a figure in Renly's green armor leading the vanguard.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ser Lancel reports the battle is lost** — He tells Cersei the bridge of boats broke, Tyrion is likely dead, Ser Mandon is gone, the Hound has disappeared, and gold cloaks are deserting after Joffrey was pulled back from the Mud Gate.
2. **Osney Kettleblack reports broader collapse** — Fighting on both sides of the river, some of Stannis's lords possibly fighting each other, Ser Balon fallen back inside the city, the riverside lost, King's Gate being rammed, mobs at Iron Gate and Gate of the Gods, Flea Bottom rioting.
3. **Cersei orders Maegor's sealed** — She commands Osfryd Kettleblack to raise the drawbridge and bar the doors, allowing no one in or out.
4. **Cersei orders Joffrey brought inside** — She tells Osfryd to bring Joffrey from the castle gatehouse into Maegor's Holdfast.
5. **Lancel argues against pulling Joffrey back** — He shouts that it will repeat the Mud Gate debacle, that Joffrey is the king and should stay.
6. **Cersei strikes Lancel** — She slams her open palm into his wound; he cries out and nearly faints.
7. **Cersei abandons the Queen's Ballroom** — She sweeps from the room without looking at Sansa.
8. **Panic spreads among the guests** — Women wail that the battle is lost, children cry, some guests rush out, some call for more wine.
9. **Sansa calms the hall** — She stands on the dais and tells the women not to be afraid, reassures them about the walls and moat, tells them Joffrey is unhurt and the queen will return.
10. **Moon Boy performs** — He does cartwheels, climbs a table, juggles wine cups, hits himself in the head with them, draws nervous laughter.
11. **Sansa helps the wounded Lancel** — She and a serving man get him to his feet; she commands he be taken to Maester Frenken.
12. **Ser Dontos advises Sansa to go to her bedchamber** — He whispers that she should lock herself in and he will come for her when the battle is done.
13. **Sansa leaves the ballroom and runs to her bedchamber** — She walks slowly out then runs up the stairs; a guard knocks into her on the stair, dropping looted items.
14. **Sansa sees the burning sky from her window** — The southern sky is aswirl with wildfire green and common flame colors; embers drift like fireflies; the air smells burnt.
15. **Sansa whispers "Lady"** — She wonders if she will meet her direwolf when she is dead.
16. **The Hound grabs Sansa in the dark** — He seizes her wrist and covers her mouth; identifies himself; he is drunk and blood-covered.
17. **The Hound announces he is leaving** — He says he is going north through the Iron Gate, away from the fires; he has his white cloak and sword to get past anyone.
18. **The Hound demands a song** — He reminds Sansa she promised him a song; he draws his dagger and holds it to her throat, ordering her to sing.
19. **Sansa sings the hymn to the Mother** — Unable to remember the song of Florian and Jonquil, she sings "Gentle Mother, font of mercy."
20. **The Hound relents** — He takes the blade from her throat; Sansa touches his cheek and feels blood and tears; he says "Little bird" and rises.
21. **The Hound departs** — Sansa hears cloth ripping and retreating footsteps; she finds his white cloak on the floor, twisted and stained with blood and fire.
22. **Sansa huddles under the Hound's cloak** — She wraps herself in it on the floor, shivering, as the sky darkens and the wind blows.
23. **Bells ring across King's Landing at dawn** — Multiple bells join in, a joyful thunder unlike the death knell for King Robert; men shout and cheer in the streets.
24. **Ser Dontos brings news of victory** — He bursts in drunk and ecstatic, whirling Sansa around; reports that Stannis's host is broken.
25. **Dontos describes the relief force** — Lord Tywin on the right, Randyll Tarly in the center, Mace Tyrell on the left; they came up through ashes and along the riverbank; Stannis's men fled, bent the knee, or were slaughtered; many shouted for Lord Renly.
26. **Dontos reports the "ghost of Renly"** — A figure in Renly's green armor with golden antlers led the vanguard, killed Ser Guyard Morrigen and a dozen other knights.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Terror throughout — fear of Ser Ilyn, fear of death, fear of the Hound; brief resolve when she calms the hall; grief when she whispers "Lady"; pity and instinctive compassion when she helps Lancel and touches the Hound's face; relief implicit at the news of victory
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival — whether Ser Ilyn will execute her; whether the city will fall; whether the Hound will kill her
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to stand and calm the hall instead of fleeing; chooses to help the wounded Lancel despite him being an enemy; chooses to sing for the Hound rather than resist; touches the Hound's face instinctively
- **Self-deception flags:** "I am soft and weak and stupid, just as Joffrey says" — internalizing her abuser's characterization of her while demonstrating courage and compassion
