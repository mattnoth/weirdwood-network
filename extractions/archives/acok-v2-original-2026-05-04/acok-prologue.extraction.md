- **chapter_summary:** Maester Cressen observes the red comet from his balcony at Dragonstone and receives Shireen and Patchface, showing them the white raven that signals summer's end. He learns from Pylos that Ser Davos has returned, and on the stairs Davos tells him the storm lords will not rally to Stannis. Cressen counsels Stannis to ally with the Starks or Lady Arryn, but Lady Selyse and the influence of Melisandre push Stannis toward relying on R'hllor's power. Dismissed from his position and replaced by Pylos at the feast, Cressen attempts to poison Melisandre with the strangler dissolved in wine, but she drinks it unharmed and Cressen dies.

- **Season indicators:** White raven from the Citadel declaring summer's end; "Ten years, two turns, and sixteen days" — the longest summer in living memory; Shireen is a "summer child" who has "never known true cold"; smallfolk say a long summer means an even longer winter; pale grey steam rises from the hot vents of Dragonmont

## Events & Actions
1. **Cressen observes the red comet at dawn** — Standing on his balcony between gargoyles, the old maester watches the comet and reflects on omens, the steam from Dragonmont, and the white raven's arrival yestermorn signaling summer's end.
2. **Pylos announces Shireen's visit** — Pylos informs Cressen that the princess and her fool wish to see the white raven.
3. **Shireen and Patchface visit Cressen** — Shireen tells Cressen about her dragon nightmares and the servants' talk of the comet being dragonsbreath. Cressen reassures her about the stone dragons and the comet.
4. **Pylos shows the white raven** — The large white raven is brought down from the rookery; it speaks ("Lady, lady"). Cressen explains to Shireen about the Citadel's white ravens and the end of the longest summer in living memory.
5. **Patchface sings "The shadows come to dance"** — The song frightens Shireen and startles the white raven. Cressen recalls Patchface's backstory: Lord Steffon found him in Volantis, the shipwreck in Shipbreaker Bay, Patchface's drowning and recovery, his broken mind.
6. **Pylos reports Davos's return** — Pylos returns without the porridge and tells Cressen that Ser Davos returned in the night and has been with the king since.
7. **Cressen encounters Davos on the stairs** — While climbing to the Chamber of the Painted Table, Cressen meets Davos descending. Davos reports that the storm lords will not rise for Stannis — they do not love him. He names specific lords and reports on Renly's Rainbow Guard.
8. **Cressen counsels Stannis** — In the Chamber of the Painted Table, Cressen urges Stannis to ally with the Starks or Lady Arryn, possibly betrothing Shireen to Robert Arryn. Stannis considers sending Shireen to the Eyrie.
9. **Lady Selyse intervenes** — She enters and argues against alliances, urging Stannis to embrace R'hllor's power. She reports that Melisandre has seen Renly dead in her flames. Cressen is horrified at the prospect of fratricide.
10. **Stannis dismisses Cressen** — Stannis chooses to hear Selyse's counsel over Cressen's and sends the maester away.
11. **Cressen retrieves the strangler** — Back in his chambers, Cressen finds an indigo glass vial containing purple crystals of the strangler poison, planning to use it on Melisandre at the feast.
12. **Cressen sleeps and wakes to find the feast already begun** — He oversleeps; no one woke him. Pylos is absent. Servants help him to the Great Hall.
13. **Cressen arrives at the feast** — He enters the Great Hall and is knocked down by Patchface in the rushes, causing laughter.
14. **Melisandre helps Cressen up and mocks him** — She lifts him to his feet, places Patchface's helm on his head, and calls it "a crown to match your chain."
15. **Cressen discovers Pylos in his seat** — Stannis has replaced Cressen with Pylos at the high table, declaring the old maester too ill and confused to serve.
16. **Davos offers Cressen a seat beside him** — Cressen sits with Davos at the lower bench, too far from Melisandre to reach her cup easily.
17. **Cressen makes his attempt** — He dissolves a strangler crystal in Davos's cup of sour red wine, approaches Melisandre, and offers to share a cup to toast R'hllor. She drinks first and is unharmed. Cressen drinks the remainder.
18. **Cressen dies** — The strangler closes his throat. He sinks to his knees, still denying Melisandre's power, as the cowbells ring and Melisandre looks down on him.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deep foreboding shifting to despair and finally to grim resolve. Cressen feels old, frail, and useless. He loves Stannis as a son and is anguished at being dismissed. He fears Melisandre's influence and is horrified at the prospect of fratricide. By the end he is desperate and determined.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Protecting Stannis from Melisandre's influence and preventing the murder of Renly. Secondary: grief at being replaced and rendered irrelevant.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to poison Melisandre with the strangler at the feast; follows through despite being seated too far away and despite her warning.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself he does not believe in omens, yet dwells on them repeatedly. Rationalizes murder ("If there are gods, surely they will forgive me") while simultaneously seeing himself as the voice of reason and wisdom. Believes his steady hands mean the gods favor his act.
