# ACOK — Bran IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 29
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran IV
- **first_available:** ACOK Bran IV
- **location_primary:** Winterfell (godswood)
- **location_secondary:** Maester Luwin's turret, Winterfell great hall (supper)
- **approximate_timeline:** Days or weeks after the harvest feast; Ser Rodrik is away dealing with the Hornwood crisis
- **time_markers:** Afternoon (Bran is "early for lessons today," with lessons scheduled for afternoon); dusk (Meera comes to Bran "at dusk as he sat in his window seat watching the lights flicker to life"); night (darkness of his bed at chapter's end); the harvest feast is referenced as a past event; Bran's ninth name day has "finally come and gone"
- **chapter_summary:** Bran watches Meera Reed spar with Summer in the godswood, where Jojen reveals his green dream of a winged wolf bound in stone chains and a three-eyed crow trying to free it. Jojen presses Bran about his wolf dreams and his connection to Summer, provoking Bran's anger and causing both Summer and Shaggydog to threaten the Reeds until Hodor chases the wolves off. Bran seeks counsel from Maester Luwin, who dismisses the greensight and magic as extinct forces. At dusk, Meera shares Jojen's prophetic dream about supper — the king's cut served to Bran, grey dead meat to the Freys — but Bran finds pigeon pie at supper with nothing visibly wrong. Bran goes to bed trying to convince himself there is no magic.

## Events & Actions
1. **Meera spars with Summer** — Meera circles with net and frog spear; Summer leaps into the net and knocks her down but becomes entangled; Jojen declares Meera the winner
2. **Summer freed from the net** — Meera laughs and rolls with the tangled wolf, then deftly undoes the net to free him
3. **Bran wrestles with Summer** — Bran calls Summer and they wrestle in the grass; Bran ends up on top; Summer licks his ear
4. **Bran discusses Greywater Watch with Meera** — Learns there are no knights, no master-at-arms, and no maester; ravens can't find it because it moves
5. **Bran considers visiting Greywater Watch** — Says he could ask Ser Rodrik when he returns; notes Maester Luwin would never allow it
6. **Jojen reveals his green dream** — Describes a winged wolf bound to earth with grey stone chains and a three-eyed crow trying to peck through them; tells Bran he is the winged wolf
7. **Bran reveals the three-eyed crow came to him** — Blurts out that the crow came after his fall, told him to fly or die; he woke but was broken
8. **Jojen says the crow is beyond the Wall** — Tells Bran the crow sent them to Winterfell to break his chains; urges Bran to open his third eye
9. **Bran deflects and grows angry** — Tries to change the subject to lizard-lions; Jojen persists about wolf dreams
10. **Jojen presses Bran about the harvest feast dream** — States Bran dreamed he was Summer in the godswood that night; says he felt Bran in Summer when he touched the wolf
11. **Summer and Shaggydog threaten the Reeds** — Summer growls and stalks toward Jojen; Meera interposes with spear; Shaggydog emerges from behind the weirwood with teeth bared
12. **Meera and Jojen climb the weirwood** — Jojen climbs first using the weirwood face as handholds; Meera jumps and swings up; Shaggydog's jaws snap beneath her ankle
13. **Bran calls for Hodor** — Shouts for Hodor, badly frightened and ashamed
14. **Hodor chases off the wolves** — Arrives half-dressed from the hot pools; waves arms and stamps feet; Shaggydog slinks away first, then Summer returns to Bran
15. **Jojen promises they will talk again** — The Reeds descend; Meera takes up her weapons
16. **Bran visits Maester Luwin** — Hodor carries him to Luwin's turret; Bran asks about the greensight
17. **Luwin explains the greenseers** — Describes the children of the forest's greenseers who could see through weirwood eyes and had power over beasts; says those powers are gone
18. **Luwin shows his Valyrian steel link** — Reveals he studied the higher mysteries (magic) at the Citadel; confesses he tried spells as a boy but magic does not work
19. **Luwin dismisses magic** — Argues magic was once powerful but is now gone; Valyria was the last ember; dragons are no more, giants dead, children of the forest forgotten
20. **Meera visits Bran at dusk** — Bran apologizes for the wolves; tells her the crow lied about flying and Jojen lied too
21. **Meera shares Jojen's prophetic dream about supper** — Bran served the king's cut (rare, bloody, savory) by Luwin; the Freys served old grey dead meat; the Freys liked theirs better
22. **Bran observes actual supper** — Pigeon pie for everyone, nothing visibly wrong with the Walders' food; Bran decides Luwin was right
23. **Bran despairs in bed** — Tells himself there is no magic and the stories are just stories; concludes he will never walk, fly, or be a knight

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Playful and happy at the start (wrestling with Summer); increasingly anxious and angry as Jojen presses about dreams; frightened and ashamed when the wolves attack the Reeds; seeking reassurance from Luwin; conflicted between wanting magic to be real and accepting Luwin's dismissal; ultimately despairing
- **Primary preoccupation:** His dreams — the falling, the golden man, the wolf dreams — and whether they mean something; whether he has powers or the crow told the truth
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to believe Maester Luwin over Jojen; tells Meera the crow lied; tries to convince himself there is no magic
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran insists "I don't have dreams" when he clearly does; claims "It was the wolves, it wasn't me" about Summer's aggression when Jojen correctly identifies it as Bran's anger; insists "I'm not a wolf" while acknowledging he's howled with them and tasted blood in wolf dreams; forces himself to accept Luwin's worldview despite his own experiences contradicting it
