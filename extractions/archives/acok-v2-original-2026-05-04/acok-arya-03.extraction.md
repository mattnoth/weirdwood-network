- **chapter_summary:** Yoren leads his party off the kingsroad onto winding back roads to avoid pursuit, heading west toward the Gods Eye before turning north. Food runs short and they live off hunting and foraging. They detour around an armed camp of wounded soldiers bearing a spotted treecat sigil. They arrive at a burned and destroyed village where they rescue a mute toddler and a one-armed woman who dies that evening. That night Arya encounters a wolf pack in the woods, and she and Yoren share a tense conversation about safety, wolves, and whether the road was the right choice.

## Events & Actions
1. **Party travels winding back roads** — Yoren leads the group off the kingsroad onto narrow, winding paths to avoid detection. The wagons slow them severely, getting stuck in ruts and mud.
2. **Arya tests the sentries** — She sneaks past the watch at night, including climbing an oak tree directly above Lommy Greenhands without being detected.
3. **Gendry rejects speculation about his parentage** — The orphan boys treat Gendry as special because the queen wants his head. He insists he was just an armorer's apprentice. Lommy speculates he is Ned Stark's bastard; Arya internally denies this.
4. **Yoren announces route change** — He tells the party they will circle the Gods Eye along its western shore to avoid the kingsroad until they cross the Trident. He turns the wagons west at a crossroads.
5. **Provisions exhausted; party lives off the land** — Yoren sends Koss and Kurz to hunt. Younger boys forage for blackberries and apples. Arya catches a rabbit.
6. **Rabbit feast** — Arya kills a rabbit with her stick. Yoren stews it with mushrooms and wild onions. Arya gets a whole leg, shares with Gendry. Even the three prisoners get a spoonful. Jaqen thanks her politely; Rorge mocks her as "Lumpyface Lumpyhead Rabbitkiller."
7. **Confrontation at Briarwhite** — Fieldhands surround the party in a cornfield demanding payment. Yoren pays coppers. He bitterly laments the decline in respect for the Night's Watch. A fieldhand threatens to stake them in the corn.
8. **Sweetcorn roast** — They cook the corn that night; Yoren is too angry to eat and paces the camp.
9. **Koss reports armed camp ahead** — Twenty or thirty wounded soldiers in mail and halfhelms with a spotted treecat sigil (yellow and black on mud-brown). One is dying. They have spears, shields, one lame horse.
10. **Yoren decides to detour** — Unable to identify the soldiers' allegiance, and fearing they'd take the party's horses, Yoren leads them miles out of the way, costing two days.
11. **Increasing hostility in the countryside** — Armed men guard fields; riders patrol fences with axes; a man in a dead tree tracks them with a nocked arrow. Yoren curses them all.
12. **Distant fire spotted** — Dobber sees a red glow in the north. Yoren checks the wind. The fire grows brighter through the night, casting the whole northern sky ablaze. They can smell smoke. None sleep well.
13. **Arrival at the burned village** — At midday they find a destroyed settlement: charred fields for miles, blackened houses, butchered animal carcasses covered in carrion crows, burnt bodies impaled on stakes atop the holdfast's timber palisade.
14. **Yoren, Murch, and Cutjack enter the holdfast** — Arya and the boys guard the wagons. Ravens rise from inside. Gendry puts on his bull helm.
15. **Rescue of survivors** — Yoren carries out a mute toddler girl; Murch and Cutjack carry a one-armed woman in a makeshift sling. The woman repeats only "Please." Rorge and Biter laugh at her until Murch curses them silent.
16. **Hot Pie and Arya confess fear** — Hot Pie admits he never truly kicked a boy to death; he just sold his mother's pies. Arya says she's scared too.
17. **Death and burial of the woman** — She dies at evenfall. Gendry and Cutjack dig her grave on a hillside beneath a weeping willow. The willow's branches seem to whisper "Please."
18. **Cold camp — no fire** — Supper is radishes, dry beans, and brook water. Lommy claims the water tastes of corpses. Hot Pie and Lommy nearly fight; Reysen separates them.
19. **Arya's wolf encounter** — She wakes at night, needing to relieve herself. Hot Pie tries to stop her. She tricks him and sneaks out. She spots twelve glowing eyes — a wolf pack. One wolf approaches, bares its teeth, then turns and runs. The pack vanishes.
20. **Arya and Yoren's nighttime conversation** — Arya reports the wolves. Yoren is unsurprised. Arya mentions Nymeria and her father's death. She says she wishes she were home. Yoren reflects on thirty years of service, having lost only three men; he wonders if a ship would have been wiser.
21. **Arya tries to sleep** — She hears wolves howling and what might be screams on the wind.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Fearful and homesick; oscillating between practiced bravery ("fierce as a wolverine") and feeling like "just a little girl." Grief for her father surfaces when discussing Nymeria. Genuine terror during the wolf encounter. Deep unease at the burned village.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Safety — constantly watching for gold cloaks behind them and dangers ahead. Also occupied with maintaining her disguise and proving her competence (sneaking past sentries, catching rabbits).
- **Key decisions made:** Chooses to stay with Yoren's group rather than ride off alone (recognizes safety in numbers). Decides to sneak past Hot Pie to relieve herself in the woods despite being told to stay close.
- **Self-deception flags:** Arya tells herself she tries hard to be brave "fierce as a wolverine and all" but acknowledges she sometimes feels like just a little girl — a rare moment of honest self-assessment rather than self-deception.
