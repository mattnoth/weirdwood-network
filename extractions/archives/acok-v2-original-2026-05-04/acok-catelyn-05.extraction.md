# ACOK — Catelyn V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 40
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn V
- **first_available:** ACOK Catelyn V
- **location_primary:** Riverrun
- **location_secondary:** Riverlands (road to Riverrun), Red Fork crossing, godswood of Riverrun
- **approximate_timeline:** Mid-ACOK; after Renly's death, during Robb's western campaign; Lord Tywin has left Harrenhal and marches west
- **time_markers:** "Two days ride from Riverrun" at opening; "two hours ride" to Rivers' camp; scout encounter at muddy stream while watering horses; camp made that night (Brienne's oath scene); they ford the Red Fork "late the next day"; Edmure mentions "Two days ago he was making plans for your marriage to Brandon Stark" regarding Hoster's confusion; the attempted Kingslayer escape occurred "on the fourth night" after Ser Cleos arrived; "the hour before dawn" when Edmure returned and the escape was caught; "a fortnight" is the deadline Stannis gave Storm's End garrison
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn returns toward Riverrun from the south, encountering Frey scouts and learning of Robb's victory at Oxcross. Brienne swears her sword to Catelyn's service. At Riverrun, Catelyn learns of the failed attempt to free Jaime Lannister, Edmure's plan to defend the Red Fork against Lord Tywin's march, Ser Cortnay Penrose's pleas from Storm's End, and the return of Ned Stark's bones. She visits her dying father Lord Hoster, who confuses her with Lysa and speaks of past marriage arrangements, then spends the night alone with Ned's remains.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn's party encounters a Frey scout** — Two days from Riverrun, a scout wearing the twin tower badge of House Frey finds Catelyn watering horses by a muddy stream. He informs her the Blackfish has gone west with Robb and Martyn Rivers commands the outriders.
2. **News of Oxcross** — The scout reports Robb's great victory at Oxcross and the death of Ser Stafford Lannister.
3. **Arrival at Rivers' camp** — Catelyn's party reaches Martyn Rivers' camp at a shattered holdfast with a hundred fresh graves. Rivers reports Lord Tywin has left Harrenhal and marches west; they must not linger.
4. **Combined party rides for Riverrun** — Rivers breaks camp and joins Catelyn's escort, now near fifty strong, flying the direwolf, leaping trout, and twin towers banners.
5. **Rivers recounts Robb's campaign** — He tells of Robb bypassing the Golden Tooth via a goat track found by Grey Wind, the Lannister remnants falling back on Lannisport, lords raiding the Westerlands, and the tale of Robb feeding Stafford's heart to Grey Wind (which Catelyn sharply dismisses).
6. **Catelyn remembers the direwolf pups** — She recalls finding five pups for the five trueborn Stark children and a sixth white-furred, red-eyed pup for Jon Snow.
7. **Brienne seeks to leave** — That night at camp, Brienne asks Catelyn's leave to go to Storm's End alone to kill Stannis.
8. **Catelyn and Brienne debate duty and vengeance** — Catelyn argues against a suicidal mission; Brienne speaks of Renly's goodness and the injustice of the world; they discuss rightful kingship.
9. **Brienne swears her sword to Catelyn** — Brienne kneels, lays Renly's longsword at Catelyn's feet, and swears to shield her back and give her life. Catelyn accepts with the reciprocal oath of hearth, meat, mead, and honorable service. Catelyn promises not to hold Brienne back from Stannis when the time comes.
10. **Crossing the Red Fork** — The next day, they ford the Red Fork upstream of Riverrun, guided through underwater iron spikes and caltrops by Mallister soldiers guarding the fortified crossing.
11. **Passage through camps and refugees** — Between the rivers, Catelyn passes through smallfolk heading for Riverrun, a Blackwood camp, and camps of Piper, Darry, and Paege forces — all her father's bannermen recalled by Edmure.
12. **Dead Lannisters on Riverrun's walls** — Catelyn sees dead men in crimson cloaks hanging from the battlements, crow-pecked and swollen.
13. **Catelyn fears for Jaime and her daughters** — She thinks: "If they have slain the Kingslayer, then my daughters are dead as well."
14. **Edmure rides out to meet Catelyn** — Accompanied by Ser Desmond Grell, Utherydes Wayn, and Ser Robin Ryger.
15. **News exchanged at the gate** — Edmure reports on Lord Hoster's condition, asks about Renly's death; Catelyn introduces Brienne; Utherydes asks about alliance with Stannis.
16. **Edmure recounts the Kingslayer's escape attempt** — False envoys tried to free Jaime on the fourth night; a big man killed two guards by smashing their skulls, a skinny man picked the lock, and a mummer impersonated Edmure's voice to open the River Gate. Jaime got a sword, killed Poul Pemford and squire Myles, wounded Delp. Edmure caught them returning at dawn. The conspirators were hanged; Jaime is now chained in the dark. Cleos Frey imprisoned.
17. **Storm's End crisis discussed** — Three identical pleas from Ser Cortnay Penrose; Stannis besieges by land and sea; Penrose refuses to hand over Edric Storm; Edmure sent no answer.
18. **Edmure tells of Renly accusations** — Some say Catelyn killed Renly, others a "southron woman"; Brienne defends Catelyn.
19. **Catelyn sees the refugee-packed castle** — Hundreds of smallfolk with livestock crowded inside Riverrun's walls by Edmure's order.
20. **Robb's instructions relayed** — Utherydes says Robb wants Catelyn sent to the Twins to evaluate Lord Walder's daughters for his bride selection.
21. **Catelyn refuses to leave** — She insists on staying at Riverrun rather than going to the Twins.
22. **Catelyn and Edmure argue in the godswood** — Catelyn says he lacks the strength to meet Tywin; Edmure outlines his plan: defend the fords with his eleven thousand while Bolton (with Tallhart reinforcements) retakes Harrenhal, trapping Tywin. He cites Blackwood and Bracken agreement. Catelyn remains skeptical but kisses him.
23. **Catelyn visits Lord Hoster** — She finds her father bedridden and delirious. He mistakes her for Lysa, speaks of Jon Arryn's virtues, rages about a "wretched stripling," begs forgiveness, and is sedated with milk of the poppy.
24. **Maester Vyman warns of Hoster's impending death** — He says they should send for Brynden Tully and possibly Lysa; Catelyn agrees to write Lysa but doubts she will come.
25. **Catelyn ponders Lysa's past suitor** — She wonders who the "wretched stripling" was — perhaps a squire, hedge knight, tradesman's son, or singer, given Lysa was "always too fond of singers."
26. **Discovery of Ned's bones** — Outside her chambers, Utherydes and two silent sisters present Ned Stark's bones, returned by Ser Cleos from King's Landing.
27. **Catelyn views Ned's remains** — She sees the skeleton dressed in his white velvet surcoat, head reattached with silver wire, skeletal fingers clasping an unfamiliar longsword. She notes Ice was not returned. Utherydes credits Tyrion for the return.
28. **Catelyn orders Ned's bones sent to Winterfell** — She assigns Hal Mollen and the silent sisters to escort the bones north.
29. **Catelyn spends the night with Ned's bones** — She dismisses everyone to be alone with her husband's remains.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Weary, grieving, anxious. Catelyn carries the weight of Renly's death, her father's decline, the war's devastation, and now the return of Ned's bones. She oscillates between pragmatic resolve and deep sorrow. Relief at reaching Riverrun is quickly replaced by new worries about Edmure's battle plans.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The safety of her children and family; whether Edmure's plan to fight Tywin at the fords is wise; her dying father; and the return of Ned's remains. She also calculates the political landscape — Stannis's march, Jaime's captivity, and the implications for Sansa and Arya.
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses to go to the Twins as Robb instructed, choosing to stay at Riverrun. Accepts Brienne's oath of service. Promises not to hold Brienne back from pursuing Stannis. Orders Ned's bones escorted to Winterfell by Hal Mollen. Agrees to write Lysa though expects no reply.
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn dismisses the tale of Robb feeding Stafford's heart to Grey Wind with "My son is no savage," but then reflects that the direwolves are "no common wolves" — she resists acknowledging the increasingly feral reputation her son is acquiring. She also second-guesses her own military instincts: "Perhaps she was wrong to oppose him. Perhaps it was a splendid plan, and her misgivings only a woman's fears" — she undermines her own sound strategic judgment by attributing it to gender.
