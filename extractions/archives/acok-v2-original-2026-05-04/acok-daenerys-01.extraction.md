## Events & Actions
1. **Dany follows the red comet** — Daenerys decides to lead her khalasar into the red waste, following the comet the Dothraki call shierak qiya, the Bleeding Star. It is the only direction open to her.
2. **Strategic options eliminated** — The Dothraki sea (north) risks capture by khalasars; the Lhazareen lands (south) are hostile; downriver toward Slaver's Bay is blocked by Khal Pono's khalasar.
3. **Dany garbs herself and mounts** — She dresses in the hrakkar skin as cloak and hood over her burned-away hair, mounts her silver mare, with Viserion on her shoulder and Ser Jorah at her side.
4. **March begins through the red waste** — The khalasar rides by night and shelters by day; horses die; the land is desolate with dry riverbeds and bitter pools.
5. **First death: toothless old man** — Three days in, an old man falls from his saddle and dies. Blood flies swarm. Dany orders a horse killed so he may ride into the night lands.
6. **Second death: infant girl** — Two nights later, an infant too young to ride perishes. Her mother wails all day.
7. **Continued attrition** — Wine, mare's milk, flatbread, and dried meat are exhausted. Only dead horse flesh remains. Doreah grows gaunt; Dany herself wastes away; her breast milk dries up.
8. **Dragons refuse raw meat** — The hatchlings hiss and spit at raw horsemeat. Dany remembers Viserys saying only dragons and men eat cooked meat.
9. **Dragons eat charred meat and thrive** — When handmaids char the horsemeat, the dragons eat voraciously and begin to grow.
10. **Dany names the dragons** — She names the black one Drogon (for Drogo), the green one Rhaegal (for Rhaegar), and the cream-and-gold one Viserion (for Viserys).
11. **Doreah dies** — Doreah takes a fever; blood blisters on lips and hands; hair falls out; loses strength to mount. Dany gives her water, cools her brow, holds her hand until she dies.
12. **Dany asks Jorah if they are lost** — He says maps show great kingdoms to the east (Yi Ti, Qarth, Asshai) but admits the way is harder than expected. He says they are doomed if they turn back.
13. **Dany kisses Jorah on the cheek** — A gesture of encouragement; she resolves to be strong for him too.
14. **Brimstone pool** — They find a scalding, foul-smelling pool and cool the water to drink, though the taste is terrible.
15. **Discovery of Vaes Tolorro** — Outriders report a white city; bloodriders scout ahead and report it deserted, nameless, godless, with broken gates.
16. **Dany enters the city** — Despite Irri and Jhiqui's fear of ghosts, Dany rides in first, followed by Jorah, bloodriders, then the rest.
17. **Camp established** — They camp on a windswept plaza before a gutted palace; searchers find figs, fruit trees, grapes, and a well with cold pure water; also bones and skulls.
18. **Dany dismisses ghost fears** — "Dragons are more powerful than ghosts."
19. **Sand bath** — Irri and Jhiqui scrub Dany clean with white sand in her tent; Jhiqui notes her hair is growing back.
20. **Rhaegal attempts flight** — In the tent, Rhaegal unfolds green wings and flaps half a foot before landing; screams; lashes tail in fury.
21. **Ser Jorah brings a peach** — Dany savors it; they discuss resting at Vaes Tolorro.
22. **Jorah tells his story** — He describes Bear Island, his first wife (a Glover), the Greyjoy Rebellion, winning knighthood at Pyke, the Lannisport tourney where he won and crowned Lynesse Hightower queen of love and beauty, their marriage, Bear Island disappointing Lynesse, his debts and dishonor, fleeing Ned Stark's judgment, exile in Lys, Lynesse leaving him for Tregar Ormollen.
23. **Jorah reveals Lynesse looked like Dany** — As he leaves, he tells Dany that Lynesse "looked a bit like you, Daenerys."
24. **Dany realizes Jorah's feelings** — She understands he loves her as a man loves a woman, not just as a knight serves a queen. She cannot imagine him that way; she still thinks of Drogo.
25. **Dany resolves to give Jorah back his home** — She thinks she can restore his home and honor someday.
26. **Dragon-riding dream** — Dany dreams of riding dragons with Drogo on their wedding night.
27. **Bloodriders sent to scout** — Aggo southwest, Rakharo due south, Jhogo southeast following the comet.
28. **Rakharo returns** — Reports red waste continues south to a poison-water shore; found immense dragon bones he rode through.
29. **Dany orders plaza pulled up** — Sets Rakharo and twelve men to remove paving stones so grass can grow; plans to farm.
30. **Aggo returns** — Reports barren southwest; found two more ruined cities; one warded by skull-topped iron spears; explored the other; brought back iron bracelet with fire opal; left crumbling scrolls.
31. **Dany orders gate repairs** — Tells Aggo to repair Vaes Tolorro's gates against possible enemies.
32. **Jhogo returns from Qarth** — Arrives with three strangers on ugly humped creatures (camels): Pyat Pree, Xaro Xhoan Daxos, and Quaithe of the Shadow.
33. **The three introduce themselves** — Pyat Pree (warlock, blue lips, speaks Dothraki), Xaro Xhoan Daxos (merchant prince of the Thirteen, jeweled nose, speaks Valyrian), Quaithe of the Shadow (lacquered mask, speaks Common Tongue). All say they come seeking dragons.
34. **Dany declares they have found them** — "Seek no more. You have found them."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined and resolute on the surface, but privately fearful and grieving. She forces herself to show no weakness ("when they look upon my face they must see only Drogo's queen"). Despair creeps in during the worst of the crossing ("The comet mocks my hopes"). Relief and wonder upon finding Vaes Tolorro. Complicated emotions upon learning of Jorah's feelings — she cannot reciprocate.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Keeping her people and her dragons alive through the red waste; finding a safe destination. Secondarily, the weight of leadership and the need to project strength she does not always feel.
- **Key decisions made:** Following the comet into the red waste; entering the dead city despite Dothraki fear of ghosts; settling at Vaes Tolorro to rest and recover; sending bloodriders to scout in three directions before moving on; ordering gate repairs and plaza cleared for farming.
- **Self-deception flags:** "It is the herald of my coming" — Dany tells herself the comet was sent by the gods for her, while the narration notes it was simply the only direction open. "She felt older than her fourteen years. If ever she had truly been a girl, that time was done" — she constructs herself as beyond girlhood, a necessary self-myth for leadership.
