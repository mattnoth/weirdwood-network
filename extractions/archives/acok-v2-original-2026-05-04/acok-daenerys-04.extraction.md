# ACOK — Daenerys IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** ACOK
- **chapter_number:** 49
- **pov_character:** Daenerys Targaryen
- **pov_chapter_number:** Daenerys IV
- **first_available:** ACOK Daenerys IV
- **location_primary:** House of the Undying Ones (Palace of Dust), Qarth
- **location_secondary:** None
- **approximate_timeline:** Mid-to-late ACOK; Daenerys has been in Qarth long enough to have sought counsel from multiple parties and decided to visit the warlocks
- **time_markers:** Morning (the building "drinks the morning sun"); the visit feels like hours inside ("I've walked for hours") but appears brief to those outside ("Can it be that the Undying are done with you so soon?")
- **chapter_summary:** Daenerys arrives at the House of the Undying Ones despite warnings from Jhogo, Aggo, Ser Jorah, and Xaro Xhoan Daxos. Pyat Pree instructs her to always take the first door to the right and always go up. After drinking shade of the evening, Dany enters alone with Drogon and navigates a labyrinthine interior, passing disturbing visions including a feast of corpses with a wolf-headed king, a house with a red door, a silver-haired man with a harp speaking of "the prince that was promised," and an old king on a barbed throne. She resists a false audience chamber of glamoured wizards, finds the true Undying — ancient blue corpses gathered around a floating corrupt heart — and receives cryptic prophecies about three fires, three mounts, and three treasons, along with visions. The Undying attack her, but Drogon burns the heart and the Undying, and Dany escapes the collapsing palace. Outside, Pyat Pree attacks her with a knife but is disarmed by Jhogo's whip and tackled by Rakharo.

## Events & Actions
1. **Dany arrives at the House of the Undying** — She exits her palanquin and sees the grey, ancient ruin coiling through a grove of black-barked trees. Drogon hisses at the sight.
2. **Companions warn Dany against entering** — Jhogo calls it an evil place of ghosts and maegi. Ser Jorah questions the warlocks' power. Xaro urges her to his pleasure barge instead. Aggo offers to walk with her.
3. **Pyat Pree appears and insists Dany enter alone** — He states she must enter alone or not at all, or the doors of wisdom will be closed forever.
4. **Ser Jorah reminds Dany of Mirri Maz Duur** — Dany takes this as encouragement, remembering Mirri had knowledge and was only a maegi.
5. **Pyat Pree gives navigation instructions** — Always take the first door to the right; always go up, never down; do not enter any room; pass through to the audience chamber.
6. **Dany drinks shade of the evening** — A dwarf servitor presents a crystal glass of thick blue liquid. The taste transforms from foul to transcendent.
7. **Dany enters the House of the Undying** — She navigates through identical square rooms, each time taking the door to the right.
8. **Dany enters the long hall** — She finds a high-ceilinged corridor with torches on the right and doors only on the left. Drogon attempts to fly but crashes after twenty feet.
9. **Vision: the naked woman and the four dwarfs** — Through an open door, Dany sees four rattish little men crawling over a naked woman, one between her thighs, another savaging her breasts.
10. **Vision: the feast of corpses with the wolf-headed king** — Slaughtered feasters lie across tables; a dead man with a wolf's head sits on a throne wearing an iron crown, holding a leg of lamb, his eyes following Dany with mute appeal.
11. **Vision: the house with the red door** — Dany recognizes the room from Braavos with its wooden beams, carved animal faces, and lemon tree outside. Ser Willem appears and beckons her. She almost goes to him but remembers he is dead and runs.
12. **Dany runs through the long hall** — She passes countless doors of every type. Drogon urges her on from her back.
13. **Vision: the king on the barbed throne** — Through grand bronze doors, Dany sees a cavernous hall with dragon skulls on the walls. An old man with dark eyes and long silver-grey hair in rich robes sits on a towering barbed throne and says "Let him be king over charred bones and cooked meat. Let him be the king of ashes."
14. **Vision: the silver-haired man, the woman, and the newborn** — A tall man with dark indigo eyes and Targaryen hair names the babe "Aegon" and calls him "the prince that was promised" whose song is "the song of ice and fire." He says "there must be one more" and "the dragon has three heads," then plays a harp with silvery strings before the vision fades.
15. **Dany reaches a dead end** — The hall ends at a descending stair. Torches are going out behind her. Something shuffles toward her in the darkness.
16. **Dany realizes the trick** — The first door on the right is the last door on the left. She flings herself through.
17. **Dany encounters a false exit with Pyat Pree** — After navigating more rooms, she finds Pyat Pree outside through a mouth-shaped door. He tries to lead her away, but she remembers his own words and takes the door to her right. His face crumbles to something pale and wormlike.
18. **Dany climbs the stairwell** — She ascends despite the House appearing to have no towers.
19. **Dany encounters the false audience chamber** — Through ebony-and-weirwood doors, she sees a splendid hall of glamoured wizards in rich robes and armor. They welcome her as "Daenerys of House Targaryen" and offer knowledge, magic weapons, and "the food of forever." They claim to have sent the comet.
20. **Drogon alerts Dany to the deception** — He flies to the top of the door and bites the wood instead of entering. Dany finds a hidden plain grey door behind the grand one and runs through it.
21. **Dany enters the true audience chamber** — A gloomy room with a long stone table and a floating, swollen, corrupt blue heart. The Undying are blue shadows who do not stir or speak aloud.
22. **The Undying deliver prophecies** — "Three heads has the dragon"; "child of three"; "three fires must you light — one for life and one for death and one to love"; "three mounts must you ride — one to bed and one to dread and one to love"; "three treasons will you know — once for blood and once for gold and once for love."
23. **The Undying show visions** — Daughter of death: Viserys dying in gold, a tall lord beneath a fiery stallion banner, rubies flying from a dying prince. Slayer of lies: a red sword and shadow-casting blue-eyed king, a cloth dragon on poles, a stone beast breathing shadow fire. Bride of fire: her silver trotting to a darkling stream, a corpse at a ship's prow with bright eyes, a blue flower in a wall of ice.
24. **Further rapid visions** — Shadows in a tent, a little girl running to a red-doored house, Mirri Maz Duur burning with a dragon from her brow, a bloody corpse dragged behind a silver horse, a white lion in tall grass, naked crones from a lake beneath the Mother of Mountains, ten thousand slaves calling "Mother."
25. **The Undying attack Dany** — They surround her, pulling, stroking, touching with dry cold hands, twining fingers through her hair. Her heart ceases to beat. A hand twists her nipple, teeth find her throat, a mouth descends on her eye.
26. **Drogon destroys the Undying** — He spreads his wings, tears the rotten heart to ribbons, and breathes fire. The Undying burn — their flesh is crumbling parchment, their bones dry wood soaked in tallow.
27. **Dany escapes the burning palace** — She pushes through the burning husks, runs through a serpentine passageway, and bursts out into the sun. Smoke rises through cracks in the walls and roof.
28. **Pyat Pree attacks and is subdued** — Outside, Pyat Pree gibbers and draws a knife. Drogon flies at his face, Jhogo's whip disarms him, and Rakharo slams him to the ground. Ser Jorah kneels beside Dany and puts his arm around her.
