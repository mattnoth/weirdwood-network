- **approximate_timeline:** During the Battle of the Blackwater; the royal fleet approaches King's Landing from the sea and engages Joffrey's defenders on the river
- **time_markers:** Three days past — war council aboard Fury at the mouth of the Wendwater; two days before — sighted fishing skiffs off Merling Rock; westering sun during battle approach (late afternoon); the battle unfolds over a continuous period from approach through the wildfire explosion
- **chapter_summary:** Davos Seaworth commands Black Betha in the second line of Stannis Baratheon's fleet as it enters the Blackwater Rush to assault King's Landing. Despite his misgivings about Ser Imry Florent's aggressive battle plan, the fleet engages Joffrey's smaller defensive fleet. Davos successfully rams and sinks Lady's Shame, then boards and captures White Hart. He notices the chain boom towers and a hulk leaking wildfire, but too late — Swordfish rams the hulk, triggering a massive wildfire explosion that destroys ships on both sides. The defenders raise the chain boom across the river mouth, trapping the burning fleet. Davos is thrown into the water and swept toward the flaming barrier.

## Events & Actions
1. **Fleet forms battle lines** — Stannis's fleet, under Ser Imry Florent's command, forms ten lines of twenty ships each as it approaches King's Landing on the flood tide. Davos commands Black Betha in the second line on the starboard wing.
2. **War council recalled** — Three days prior, Ser Imry summoned captains to Fury at the mouth of the Wendwater. He organized the fleet into ten lines: first two to destroy Joffrey's fleet, following lines to land troops, rear ships to ferry Stannis's army, Salladhor Saan's Lyseni as rear guard.
3. **Fishing skiffs captured** — Two days before off Merling Rock, six fishing skiffs were overtaken. Captives reported a boom being built across the river mouth, though accounts differed on whether it was complete.
4. **Fleet enters the Blackwater Rush** — The warhorns sound the attack. The first line enters the river under oars. Davos notices the squat new towers at the river mouth and sunlight on chain links — evidence of a chain boom, though it has not been raised.
5. **Defenders fire on the fleet** — Pots of burning pitch are launched from the Red Keep; arrows rain from tower nests. Queen Alysanne and Dragonsbane take early hits. Courageous catches fire. Soldiers and archers begin falling.
6. **Troops attempt shore landing** — Devotion, Prayer, and Piety drive toward the north shore to land archers. Rocks and arrows come down from the castle.
7. **Defenders counterattack the landing** — Knights led by the Hound ride down the archers on the shore. The Hound rides his horse up the plank onto Prayer's deck, hacking men down.
8. **First line engages** — Ships collide and board each other. Stag of the Sea splits an enemy galley in two. Queen Alysanne is locked between Lady of Silk and Lady's Shame. Dog's Nose is afire. Fury fires her catapults.
9. **Davos rams Lady's Shame** — Black Betha and Lady Marya ram Lady's Shame simultaneously, fore and aft. Lady's Shame breaks apart and sinks, spilling men into the river. Davos bites his tongue in the impact.
10. **Wildfire appears** — Green fire erupts from Queen Alysanne's stern. The cry of "Wildfire!" goes up. Queen Alysanne, Lady of Silk, and pieces of Lady's Shame drift downriver as a ranging green inferno; a Myrish galley crashes into them and catches fire.
11. **Trebuchets devastate the center** — The three great trebuchets behind the Mud Gate hurl massive stones. Bold Laughter (a Velaryon galley) is destroyed by a direct hit.
12. **Davos engages the pleasure barge** — Black Betha strikes Queen Cersei's carved-and-gilded pleasure barge, spilling soldiers into the river where archers pick them off.
13. **White Hart attempts to ram Black Betha** — Matthos alerts Davos. Black Betha turns hard to starboard; the impact is a glancing blow, but both ships snap oars.
14. **Davos boards and captures White Hart** — Davos leads his men over the rail. He fights through the press, is struck from behind with an axe (his helm saves him), and kills his attacker by driving his sword into the man's belly. White Hart is taken.
15. **Swordfish rams the wildfire hulk** — Swordfish, her rigging already burning, rams a Lannister hulk leaking green wildfire. The hulk bursts open, releasing thousands of jars of wildfire across the river surface.
16. **Massive wildfire explosion** — A short sharp "woof" precedes a roar. The wildfire ignites across the river. A swirling demon of green flame fifty feet high dances on the water. Ships on both sides are consumed: Black Betha, White Hart, Loyal Man, Piety, Cat, Courageous, Sceptre, Red Raven, Harridan, Faithful, Fury, Kingslander, Godsgrace, Pride of Driftmark all burn.
17. **Davos is thrown into the river** — The explosion throws Davos from Black Betha's deck. He surfaces, grabs debris, and is swept by the current toward the bay.
18. **The chain is raised** — The defenders raise the chain boom across the river mouth. A dozen galleys have already crashed into it. The trapped fleet burns between the chain and the wildfire. The mouth of the Blackwater becomes impassable.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pride in his sons' seamanship, but deep unease and fear about the battle. He feels like a maiden coming to her first battle of this kind. Increasingly alarmed as he spots the chain towers and wildfire. Horror when he realizes the wildfire trap. Final desperation as he is thrown into the water and swept toward the raised chain.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The tactical danger of Ser Imry's aggressive plan; the chain boom and what it means; the safety of his sons; the trap closing around the fleet.
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to ram Lady's Shame when he sees the opportunity; decides to board White Hart after the collision; orders "back water" when he sees the wildfire hulk burst — too late.
- **Self-deception flags:** None. Davos is notably clear-eyed throughout, consistently identifying dangers that others ignore. He is aware of his own fear and does not disguise it from himself. He acknowledges he is "still a smuggler at heart."
