# AGOT — Eddard XV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 59
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard XV
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard XV
- **location_primary:** Black cells beneath the Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Harrenhal (recalled), Winterfell (recalled), the Eyrie (recalled), Tower of Joy (implied through Lyanna memory)
- **approximate_timeline:** Days to weeks after Ned's arrest in the throne room; Robb is marching south with a northern army
- **chapter_summary:** Eddard Stark languishes in the black cells beneath the Red Keep, injured, starving, and feverish. He reflects on his failures and blames himself for trusting Littlefinger, the gold cloaks, and others. He drifts between waking and sleeping, experiencing vivid memories of the tourney at Harrenhal and Lyanna's death. Varys visits him disguised as a gaoler, reveals key information about the political situation, and urges Ned to confess treason and support Joffrey in exchange for being allowed to take the black. When Ned refuses, Varys threatens that Sansa's life hangs in the balance.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned imprisoned in black cells** — Ned is shoved into a lightless cell beneath the Red Keep with an injured leg. He has no food, bed, or light.
2. **Ned blames himself and others** — He damns Littlefinger, Janos Slynt, the gold cloaks, Cersei, Jaime, Pycelle, Varys, Barristan, and Renly, but ultimately blames himself for being a fool.
3. **Ned recalls Cersei's warning** — He remembers her words: "When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die."
4. **Passage of time in darkness** — Ned loses track of time, sleeps and wakes in cycles, develops fever in his injured leg, and begins talking aloud to himself.
5. **Ned builds hopeful plans** — He tells himself Robert's brothers are raising armies, his men will return, and Catelyn will raise the north.
6. **Ned hallucinates/dreams of Robert** — He sees young Robert speaking to him, then Robert's face cracks apart to reveal Littlefinger underneath, whose lies become pale grey moths.
7. **Rat-faced gaoler brings water** — A gaoler brings Ned a jug of water but refuses to speak or answer questions about his daughters.
8. **Ned recalls the tourney at Harrenhal** — Extended memory of the false spring tourney: Jaime's Kingsguard investiture, Rhaegar's jousting victories, and Rhaegar crowning Lyanna queen of beauty instead of Elia.
9. **Ned remembers Lyanna's death** — He reaches for the crown of roses and feels thorns; wakes remembering Lyanna's words "Promise me, Ned" from her bed of blood, and that she loved winter roses.
10. **Varys visits disguised as a gaoler** — Varys enters in disguise, brings wine, and engages Ned in extended conversation about the political situation.
11. **Varys reveals Arya escaped** — Arya fled from Ser Meryn; neither Varys nor the Lannisters can find her. Joffrey "loves her not."
12. **Varys reveals Sansa pleaded for Ned** — Sansa came to court to beg that Ned be spared.
13. **Varys reveals Catelyn lost Tyrion** — The Imp slipped through her fingers; Varys expects he is dead in the Mountains of the Moon.
14. **Varys confirms Cersei's role in Robert's death** — Cersei gave Lancel the fortified wine; the boar was the instrument but Cersei arranged the means.
15. **Varys explains the political landscape** — Stannis gathers forces on Dragonstone; Robb marches south; Jaime fights the river lords; Lysa sits in the Eyrie; Martells brood in Dorne.
16. **Varys proposes a deal** — Ned should confess treason, proclaim Joffrey true heir, denounce Stannis and Renly, command Robb to stand down; in exchange, he may take the black.
17. **Ned refuses the deal** — Says his word would be hollow; his life is not so precious.
18. **Varys threatens Sansa's life** — Tells Ned the next visitor could bring food and milk of the poppy, or Sansa's head. The choice is Ned's.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Despair, self-blame, grief, feverish delirium; briefly hopeful when constructing plans; ultimately anguished when Sansa is threatened
- **Primary preoccupation:** His daughters' safety (Sansa and Arya); his own failure and foolishness; guilt over Robert's death
- **Key decisions made:** Refuses Varys's proposed deal to confess and take the black — but left wavering after the threat to Sansa
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned's hopeful castle-building (Robert's brothers raising armies, Catelyn rallying the north) contrasts with his actual powerlessness. He tells himself Cersei will not kill him because Catelyn holds Tyrion — but Varys reveals Catelyn has lost Tyrion.
