## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 46

- **location_primary:** The Red Keep, King's Landing (Tower of the Hand; godswood)

- **approximate_timeline:** While King Robert is away on his hunt; Ned is bedridden recovering from his broken leg; three days before Sansa and Arya are to sail on the Wind Witch
- **chapter_summary:** Ned, bedridden with his broken leg, receives visits from Grand Maester Pycelle and Littlefinger, learning that Tywin Lannister is wroth about the force sent after Gregor Clegane and that sellswords are gathering at Casterly Rock. Ned reflects on what Sansa's innocent remark revealed to him about the royal children's true parentage. He summons Cersei to the godswood and confronts her directly: all three of her children are Jaime's, not Robert's. Cersei confirms the incest and her hatred of Robert. Ned warns her to flee with her children before Robert returns, but Cersei warns him that in the game of thrones, you win or you die.

## Events & Actions
1. **Pycelle visits Ned** — Treats his leg, reports Tywin's anger via raven, and warns about the mission against Gregor Clegane. Ned suspects Pycelle is relaying Cersei's threats rather than acting as a neutral advisor.
2. **Littlefinger visits Ned** — Reports that sellswords are gathering at Casterly Rock, Robert's hunt found the white hart killed by wolves and Robert is now pursuing a monstrous boar, Joffrey and part of the hunting party have returned, and the Hound went straight to the queen.
3. **Littlefinger notices the Malleon book** — Ned reveals Jon Arryn was studying it when he fell ill; Littlefinger deflects with a quip.
4. **Ned reflects on Sansa's remark** — Recalls Sansa's angry statement that Joffrey is nothing like King Robert, which made the truth of the children's parentage clear to him.
5. **Ned dreams of Rhaegar's children** — Remembers Tywin presenting the murdered children beneath the Iron Throne, wrapped in crimson cloaks.
6. **Ned resolves to act** — Decides he must tell Robert but also must find a way to save Cersei's children from Robert's wrath.
7. **Ned orders the guard doubled** — Tells Fat Tom to double the guard on the Tower of the Hand and let no one enter or leave without his permission.
8. **Ned summons Cersei to the godswood** — Sends a sealed letter via Tomard.
9. **Ned confronts Cersei** — Tells her he knows the truth Jon Arryn died for. States that all three children are Jaime's.
10. **Cersei confirms the incest** — Openly acknowledges her sexual relationship with Jaime since childhood, invoking Targaryen precedent. Confirms Bran saw them.
11. **Cersei reveals Robert's wedding night** — On their wedding night, Robert called her by Lyanna's name while in bed with her.
12. **Cersei reveals she terminated Robert's pregnancy** — States Robert got her with child once, and Jaime found a woman to "cleanse" her. Robert never knew.
13. **Cersei attempts to seduce/bargain with Ned** — Offers an alliance, touching his leg and face, suggesting enemies can become friends.
14. **Ned rejects Cersei's offer** — Asks if she made the same offer to Jon Arryn; she slaps him.
15. **Ned warns Cersei to flee** — Tells her to take her children and leave before Robert returns, suggesting exile to the Free Cities or farther. Warns that Robert's wrath will follow.
16. **Cersei delivers her warning** — "When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die. There is no middle ground."
17. **Cersei departs** — Raises her hood to hide her bruised face and leaves Ned alone in the godswood at nightfall.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Pain (physical from the broken leg), grim determination, moral anguish, pity for both Robert and Cersei, dread about the consequences of revealing the truth, haunted by memories of Rhaegar's murdered children
- **Primary preoccupation:** How to reveal the truth to Robert while preventing the murder of Cersei's children; balancing duty against mercy
- **Key decisions made:** (1) To confront Cersei directly before telling Robert. (2) To give Cersei a chance to flee with her children before Robert returns. (3) To double the guard on the Tower of the Hand. (4) To tell Robert the truth when he returns from the hunt.
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned is "not near as confident of Robert as he pretended" — he projects certainty to Cersei while internally doubting Robert's response. He tells Cersei Robert's wrath will follow her, but his real fear is that Robert will kill the children, suggesting he overestimates his own ability to control the outcome.
