She names the child Rhaego. The procession moves to the Womb of the World where Dany bathes in the sacred lake. Khal Drogo kills Viserys by pouring molten gold over his head.

## Events & Actions
1. **Stallion heart ceremony** — Khal Drogo presents a raw stallion's heart to Daenerys. She eats it entirely before the dosh khaleen and assembled Dothraki. Her success is taken as a favorable omen for the child.
2. **Prophecy of the stallion who mounts the world** — The eldest crone of the dosh khaleen peers into smoke and prophesies that Daenerys's son will be the stallion who mounts the world — the khal of khals who will unite all Dothraki and conquer all peoples.
3. **Naming of Rhaego** — Daenerys names her unborn son Rhaego, after her brother Rhaegar.
4. **Procession along the godsway** — The khals, dosh khaleen, bloodriders, and retinue process from the pit along the godsway to the Womb of the World.
5. **Bathing in the Womb of the World** — Daenerys bathes naked in the sacred lake, cleansing herself and the child, while the assembly watches.
6. **Sexual encounter at the lakeshore** — Khal Drogo has sex with Daenerys publicly at the lakeshore after her bathing.
7. **Feast in Drogo's hall** — The assembly rides to Drogo's hall for a feast. Khals Jommo and Ogo sit at the high bench with Drogo.
8. **Viserys's absence noted** — Dany searches for Viserys at the feast and cannot find him. Ser Jorah reports Viserys went to the Western Market for wine and to recruit sellswords from caravan guards.
9. **Dragon egg theft revealed** — Ser Jorah tells Dany that Viserys had planned to steal her dragon eggs to sell for sellswords, and that Jorah threatened to cut off his hand to stop him.
10. **Viserys arrives armed and drunk** — Viserys enters the feast hall wearing a longsword (forbidden in Vaes Dothrak), visibly drunk, demanding his place at the feast and calling Dany a whore.
11. **Ser Jorah attempts to intervene** — Jorah tries to calm Viserys and physically struggles with him. Viserys draws the sword after being knocked to the floor.
12. **Viserys threatens Daenerys and her unborn child** — Viserys places his sword point on Daenerys's belly, threatening to cut out the child and take Dany and the dragon eggs unless Drogo gives him his crown.
13. **Drogo's bloodriders seize Viserys** — On Drogo's command, Qotho holds Viserys's arms, Haggo breaks his wrist, and Cohollo takes the sword.
14. **Death of Viserys Targaryen** — Drogo melts his gold belt in a stew pot over the firepit and pours the molten gold over Viserys's head, calling it his "golden crown." Viserys dies. No blood is shed.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Determined resolve during the ceremony; growing confidence and pride in Dothraki identity; deep anxiety and dread when Viserys arrives armed; calm detachment at the moment of Viserys's death
- **Primary preoccupation:** The ceremony and its meaning for her child; protecting her unborn son; loyalty to Viserys warring with her emerging Dothraki identity
- **Key decisions made:** Names the child Rhaego; offers Viserys the dragon eggs freely; tells Viserys (translating Drogo) he will receive a golden crown; refuses to look away from Viserys's death; folds her arms protectively over her belly rather than turning away
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells Ser Jorah that Viserys is her true king and "all I have" — but by the end refers to him as "the man who had been her brother," suggesting the loyalty was already dissolving; her final thought ("He was no dragon") reframes his death as a test he failed rather than a murder
