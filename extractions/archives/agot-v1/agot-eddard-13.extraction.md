# AGOT — Eddard XIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 48
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard XIII
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard XIII
- **location_primary:** Red Keep, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Maegor's Holdfast (royal bedchamber); Winterfell crypts (dream)
- **approximate_timeline:** Immediately after Robert's return from his hunting trip in the kingswood; late night into pre-dawn hours
- **chapter_summary:** Ned is woken in the night and summoned to Robert's bedchamber in Maegor's Holdfast, where the king lies dying from a wound inflicted by a boar during his hunt. Robert dictates his will, naming Ned as Lord Regent and Protector of the Realm, and Ned substitutes "my heir" for "my son Joffrey" when writing it. After Robert sleeps, Varys implies Lancel Lannister may have deliberately over-served the king wine. Renly offers Ned a hundred swords to seize Cersei's children immediately, but Ned refuses. Ned writes a letter to Stannis Baratheon informing him of Robert's impending death and summons Littlefinger, who proposes keeping Joffrey on the throne under Ned's regency; Ned rejects this as treason and instead asks Littlefinger to buy the loyalty of the City Watch.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned's dream of the Winterfell crypts** — Ned dreams of walking the crypts, seeing the Kings of Winter, and reaching the tombs of his father, Brandon, and Lyanna, whose statue whispers "Promise me, Ned" while wearing blue roses and weeping blood from her eyes.
2. **Ned summoned to Robert's bedside** — The royal steward, Tomard, and Cayn wake Ned and escort him to Maegor's Holdfast, where three Kingsguard stand watch.
3. **Robert's mortal wound revealed** — Robert has been gored by a boar from groin to nipple. The wound has mortified. Pycelle says the king should already be dead. Robert claims he killed the boar by driving a knife through its eye.
4. **Robert dismisses everyone** — Robert orders Cersei, Renly, Pycelle, and the servants to leave so he can speak privately with Ned.
5. **Robert's change of heart about Daenerys** — Robert expresses regret about ordering Daenerys Targaryen killed, saying Ned was right and asking him to stop the assassination.
6. **Robert dictates his will** — Robert commands Ned to write his will naming Ned as Lord Regent and Protector of the Realm until "my son Joffrey" comes of age.
7. **Ned's deliberate alteration of the will** — Ned writes "my heir" instead of "my son Joffrey," unable to reveal the truth about Joffrey's parentage to his dying friend.
8. **Robert's final requests** — Robert asks Ned to serve the boar at his funeral feast, to let Daenerys live, and to help his son be better than he was. Robert also says he will give Lyanna Ned's love and asks Ned to care for his children.
9. **Will witnessed and sealed** — Renly and Pycelle witness Robert pressing his seal into the wax on the will.
10. **Robert given milk of the poppy** — After the will is sealed, Pycelle administers milk of the poppy and Robert sleeps.
11. **Varys implies Lancel plied Robert with wine** — Outside the bedchamber, Varys questions who gave the king wine during the hunt. Ser Barristan identifies Lancel Lannister as the squire who kept Robert's wineskins full. Varys pointedly notes Lancel's Lannister connections.
12. **Ned orders assassination of Daenerys reversed** — Ned tells Varys to undo whatever arrangements were made to kill Daenerys. Varys says it may be too late, the "birds have flown."
13. **Renly proposes a coup** — On the bridge over the dry moat, Renly urges Ned to seize Joffrey, Myrcella, and Tommen immediately to neutralize Cersei, offering a hundred swords.
14. **Ned refuses Renly's offer** — Ned declares he will not shed blood in Robert's halls or drag children from their beds, and will instead convene the council after Robert dies.
15. **Ned arranges escort for his daughters** — Ned orders Tomard to command a twenty-man escort on the Wind Witch, sailing on the evening tide, and to deliver a letter to Stannis at Dragonstone on the way north.
16. **Ned writes letter to Stannis** — Ned composes a letter informing Stannis of Robert's impending death and the need to sail for King's Landing with all his power, signing as Lord Protector of the Realm.
17. **Ned summons Littlefinger** — Ned sends Cayn to find Littlefinger and bring him before dawn.
18. **Ned reveals the incest secret to Littlefinger** — Ned tells Littlefinger that Joffrey and Tommen are Jaime Lannister's bastards and that Stannis is the rightful heir.
19. **Littlefinger proposes keeping Joffrey on the throne** — Littlefinger argues that supporting Stannis means war and suggests Ned rule as regent, make peace with the Lannisters, and eventually put Renly on the throne if needed.
20. **Ned rejects Littlefinger's plan as treason** — Ned insists Stannis is the heir and the matter is not a choice.
21. **Littlefinger agrees to buy the City Watch** — Littlefinger agrees to go to Janos Slynt and secure the gold cloaks' loyalty for 6,000 gold pieces, invoking his love for Catelyn.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief-stricken, weary, heartsick; oscillating between duty and personal anguish; anger at Littlefinger's amorality; longing for home and family
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing the succession for Stannis while protecting Robert's dying hours from dishonor; arranging to send his daughters to safety; securing the City Watch
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Substitutes "my heir" for "my son Joffrey" in the will; (2) Refuses Renly's offer of swords and immediate seizure of the royal children; (3) Declares Stannis the rightful heir; (4) Rejects Littlefinger's proposal to keep Joffrey on the throne; (5) Writes to Stannis; (6) Sends daughters north on the Wind Witch with twenty-man escort; (7) Agrees to have Littlefinger bribe the City Watch
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned reflects that "the deceit made him feel soiled" about altering the will, yet tells himself it was a lie told for love. He hopes the gods will know his true intentions. He also wonders if he was right to refuse Renly, but pushes doubt aside in favor of honor.
