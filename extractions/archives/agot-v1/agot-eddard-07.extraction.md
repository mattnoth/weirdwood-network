# AGOT — Eddard VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 31
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard VII
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard VII
- **location_primary:** Tourney grounds outside King's Landing (along the river)
- **location_secondary:** King's pavilion, jousting field, archery field, feast hall, Tower of the Hand, Ned's chambers
- **approximate_timeline:** During the Hand's tourney, final day of jousting and melee; continues into the night feast and a late-night visit from Varys
- **chapter_summary:** Ned views the body of Ser Hugh of the Vale with Ser Barristan, then tries to dissuade Robert from fighting in the melee. Robert confides his unhappiness as king, his regrets about Cersei, and his fears about Joffrey. The tournament concludes: Sandor Clegane defeats Jaime Lannister in the joust, Ser Gregor Clegane kills his own horse and attacks Loras Tyrell before Sandor intervenes and Robert stops the fight. Loras concedes the championship to Sandor. That night, Ned reflects on the catspaw dagger and Robert's bastard children, then receives a secret visit from Varys, who reveals that Jon Arryn was poisoned with the tears of Lys, that Ser Hugh may have been the poisoner, and that the Lannisters had plotted to kill Robert during the melee.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned and Barristan view Ser Hugh's body** — Barristan stood vigil; Ned reflects on whether Hugh was killed to prevent him from talking; orders Hugh's armor sent to his mother in the Vale.
2. **Ned and Barristan attempt to dissuade Robert from the melee** — Robert is too fat for his armor; Ned tells him directly. Robert sends squires for a nonexistent "breastplate stretcher" as a joke.
3. **Robert confides in Ned** — Robert expresses regret about kingship, bitterness about Cersei, concern about Joffrey, and nostalgia for his youth. He dreams of abdicating and becoming a sellsword in the Free Cities.
4. **Sandor Clegane defeats Jaime Lannister in the joust** — First pass goes to Jaime, second pass unhorses him. Jaime's helmet is dented shut and he must be led to a blacksmith.
5. **Loras Tyrell defeats Gregor Clegane in the joust** — Loras rides a mare in heat that distracts Gregor's stallion. Gregor is unhorsed without Loras even breaking his lance.
6. **Gregor Clegane kills his horse and attacks Loras** — In rage, Gregor decapitates his stallion with one blow, then charges Loras and knocks him from his saddle.
7. **Sandor Clegane intervenes to save Loras** — Sandor pulls Gregor away and the brothers fight with swords. Sandor notably does not strike at Gregor's unprotected face.
8. **Robert stops the Clegane fight** — Uses his commanding battlefield voice to end the violence; lets Gregor leave.
9. **Loras concedes victory to Sandor** — Returns in a linen doublet and declares the day belongs to Sandor, who wins the champion's purse and the love of the commons.
10. **Anguy wins the archery competition** — An unheralded commoner outshoots Balon Swann and Jalabhar Xho at a hundred paces. Ned offers him a position in the Hand's guard but Anguy refuses.
11. **Thoros of Myr wins the melee** — Near forty men fight for three hours; three broken limbs, a shattered collarbone, two horses put down. Robert does not participate.
12. **Feast that night** — Robert in good humor, Lannisters absent, Sansa and Arya behave well. Arya reports on her training with Syrio Forel.
13. **Ned reflects on his investigation** — Studies the catspaw dagger, considers Robert's bastard children (Gendry, a girl in the Vale, a boy at Storm's End), notes Stannis has not returned and Lysa is silent.
14. **Varys visits Ned in disguise** — Arrives in roughspun at the Tower of the Hand via secret passages. Reveals the Lannisters planned to kill Robert in the melee, that Jon Arryn was poisoned with the tears of Lys, that Ser Hugh may have been the poisoner, and that Jon Arryn died because he was "asking questions."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Tired and troubled at the start (slept badly); briefly hopeful during breakfast with Robert; alarmed and sick during Varys's revelations; overwhelmed by the end ("all too much")
- **Primary preoccupation:** The investigation into Jon Arryn's death and the threat posed by the Lannisters; keeping Robert safe; the catspaw dagger and Bran's fall
- **Key decisions made:** Orders Ser Hugh's armor sent to his mother; attempts to recruit Anguy for the Hand's guard; agrees to work with Varys against the Lannister threat
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned believes that if he can prove Lannister guilt, Robert will act decisively and justice will prevail — "He could see it all so clearly" — an optimism that may not account for the complexity of the situation
