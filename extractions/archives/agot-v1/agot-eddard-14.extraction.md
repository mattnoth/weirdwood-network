## Chapter Metadata

- **chapter_number:** 50

- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard XIV
- **location_primary:** Tower of the Hand, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** Throne room (Red Keep)
- **approximate_timeline:** Morning after Robert's death; same day as the confrontation in the throne room
- **chapter_summary:** Eddard wakes to Lannister guardsmen drilling in the yard. He breakfasts with his daughters and Septa Mordane, ordering them to prepare to leave King's Landing by midday. Grand Maester Pycelle arrives to inform Ned that King Robert has died. Ned summons the small council to his solar and presents Robert's sealed letter naming him Protector of the Realm, but learns that Renly has fled the city with Loras Tyrell and fifty retainers. The council proceeds to the throne room where Joffrey sits the Iron Throne and demands oaths of fealty. Cersei tears up Robert's letter, and Ned publicly declares that Joffrey has no claim and names Stannis as Robert's true heir. When Ned orders Janos Slynt and the gold cloaks to take the queen into custody, the City Watch instead turns on Ned's men — killing Tomard, Varly, and Cayn — revealing Littlefinger's betrayal as he holds a dagger to Ned's throat.

## Events & Actions
1. **Lannister guardsmen drill at dawn** — Ned watches Sandor Clegane and Lannister men training in the yard, jousting and sparring with straw dummies.
2. **Breakfast with daughters** — Ned eats with Sansa, Arya, and Septa Mordane. Sansa is refused permission to see Joffrey and runs away crying. Arya asks permission for a last lesson with Syrio.
3. **Pycelle announces Robert's death** — Grand Maester Pycelle informs Ned that King Robert has died.
4. **Small council convened in Ned's solar** — Ned summons the council members. Ser Barristan, Littlefinger, and Varys arrive. Renly is reported as having fled.
5. **Littlefinger reports task completed** — He tells Ned the "little task" set for him is accomplished (securing the gold cloaks' loyalty).
6. **Varys reports Renly's flight** — Renly left through a postern gate an hour before dawn with Ser Loras Tyrell and fifty retainers, galloping south.
7. **Robert's letter read aloud** — Ser Barristan verifies the seal and reads the letter naming Ned Protector of the Realm and regent.
8. **Ned internally conceals his knowledge of Joffrey's illegitimacy** — He notes the letter says "until the heir comes of age" and reflects that the true heir (Stannis) is of age, but does not share this with the council.
9. **Royal steward summons council to throne room** — Joffrey demands the small council's immediate presence.
10. **Procession to the throne room** — Ned is escorted with the council by a double column of eight men-at-arms in grey cloaks. Gold cloaks are visible on ramparts and at gates.
11. **Joffrey proclaimed king** — The steward announces Joffrey's full titles. Joffrey demands coronation within a fortnight and oaths of fealty.
12. **Cersei destroys Robert's letter** — She reads "Protector of the Realm," then tears the letter to pieces.
13. **Cersei offers Ned terms** — Bend the knee or return to Winterfell.
14. **Ned declares Joffrey illegitimate** — He states Joffrey has no claim and names Stannis as Robert's true heir.
15. **Cersei orders Ned's arrest** — She tells Ser Barristan to seize Ned; Stark guardsmen surround Barristan.
16. **Standoff escalates** — The Hound draws his longsword; Kingsguard knights and Lannister guardsmen move to support him. Joffrey screams to kill them all.
17. **Ned orders the gold cloaks to take Cersei into custody** — He calls on Janos Slynt and the City Watch.
18. **Gold cloaks betray Ned** — The City Watch turns on Ned's men. A gold cloak spears Tomard through the back. Janos Slynt slashes Varly's throat. The Hound kills Cayn.
19. **Littlefinger's betrayal** — Littlefinger draws Ned's own dagger from its sheath and holds it under Ned's chin, saying "I did warn you not to trust me."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief-stricken over Robert's death yet outwardly controlled; feeling empty; burdened by duty; a "bitter taste" from the need for deceit
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing his position as regent while getting his daughters safely out of King's Landing; frustration that Cersei has not fled despite the chances he gave her
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to conceal his knowledge of the succession from the council; chooses to confront Cersei publicly in the throne room by naming Stannis as true heir; orders the gold cloaks to arrest Cersei
- **Self-deception flags:** Trusts Littlefinger's assurance that the gold cloaks are secured; believes Janos Slynt will follow his command; counts on the City Watch outnumbering the Lannisters as sufficient protection
