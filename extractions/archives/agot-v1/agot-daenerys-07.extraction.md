Horrified by the rape and enslavement of the townspeople, she orders her khas to stop the rapes and claims the victims as her own slaves. Drogo, wounded by an arakh cut and an arrow, supports Dany's claims against the protests of his bloodriders and the warrior Mago. A Lhazareen godswife named Mirri Maz Duur, whom Dany saved, offers to treat Drogo's wounds and does so inside the temple. Dany asks Mirri Maz Duur to attend her when she gives birth.

## Events & Actions
1. **Battle aftermath** — Dany rides through the fields after Drogo's khalasar defeated Khal Ogo's khalasar at a Lhazareen town. Jaqqa rhan harvest heads; girls collect arrows; sheep lie dead in thousands.
2. **Drogo kills two khals** — Drogo slew Khal Ogo, then Ogo's son Fogo who became khal upon Ogo's death. Drogo's bloodriders cut the bells from their hair.
3. **Mass enslavement and rape of Lhazareen** — Dothraki herd survivors as slaves; riders rape women throughout the town. Ser Jorah estimates as many as ten thousand captives.
4. **Dany intervenes to stop rapes** — Dany orders her khas and Ser Jorah to stop the rapes. Jhogo beheads one man; Aggo kills another with an arrow. Dany claims the victims as her own slaves.
5. **Dany claims multiple women throughout the town** — She rides through the town, stopping at each instance of rape, claiming the victims. One woman blesses her in the Common Tongue.
6. **Mago's complaint** — Mago, a rider in Ko Jhaqo's khas, complains to Drogo that Dany took his "spoils." Drogo backs Dany, telling Mago to find another.
7. **Dany confronts Qotho** — When Qotho mockingly asks "Does the horse breed with the sheep?", Dany retorts "The dragon feeds on horse and sheep alike." Drogo approves.
8. **Mirri Maz Duur offers to heal Drogo** — The Lhazareen godswife, one of the women Dany saved, offers her services. Despite objections from Qotho and Haggo calling her "maegi," Dany accepts and Drogo consents.
9. **Mirri Maz Duur treats Drogo's wounds** — Inside the temple, she removes the arrow, washes wounds with boiled wine, applies a green paste, stitches the chest wound with silver needle and silk thread, applies red ointment and a lambskin bandage. She instructs him to pray, keep the bandage ten days/nights, and avoid wine and milk of the poppy.
10. **Drogo dismisses medical instructions** — He says he will drink what he likes and spits on pain.
11. **Dany asks Mirri Maz Duur to attend her birth** — Mirri claims she has never lost a babe and knows every secret of the "bloody bed."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Horror at the battle's aftermath, pity for the Lhazareen, determination to intervene despite custom and counsel; underlying fear she may have "dared too much" in challenging Dothraki ways; growing confidence in asserting her authority
- **Primary preoccupation:** Stopping the rapes and claiming victims; ensuring Drogo is healed; her approaching childbirth
- **Key decisions made:** Orders her khas to stop the rapes and claims victims as her slaves; overrules Ser Jorah and her khas who counsel acceptance; accepts Mirri Maz Duur as healer for Drogo over bloodriders' objections; asks Mirri to attend her birth
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells herself "This is war, this is what it looks like, this is the price of the Iron Throne" — rationalizing the conquest as necessary cost; trusts Mirri Maz Duur ("she had saved her from the hard hands of her rapers, after all") — assumes gratitude where other motives may exist
