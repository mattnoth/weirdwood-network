# AGOT — Prologue

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 1
- **pov_character:** Will
- **pov_chapter_number:** Prologue
- **first_available:** AGOT Prologue
- **location_primary:** The haunted forest, beyond the Wall
- **location_secondary:** Castle Black (referenced), the Wall (referenced), Mallister lands (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Nine days into the ranging north of the Wall; the opening event of AGOT
- **chapter_summary:** Three Night's Watch rangers — Will (scout, former poacher), Gared (veteran man-at-arms), and Ser Waymar Royce (young knight, commander) — track wildling raiders into the haunted forest. Will scouted a wildling camp earlier and found eight dead bodies with no apparent cause of death. When the party returns to investigate, the bodies are gone. Pale supernatural beings called the Others emerge and surround Royce; one fights him in single combat and his castle-forged sword shatters against the Other's crystal blade. The remaining Others close in and kill Royce. Will hides in a tree, climbs down after, and is killed by a reanimated Royce whose right eye burns blue.

## Events & Actions
1. **Debate at the treeline** — Gared urges retreat to the Wall; Royce refuses. Will sides with Gared but defers to Royce's command. Royce points out the Wall was weeping that week, disproving cold as cause of death.
2. **Will's earlier scout described** — Will reports finding eight dead wildlings at the camp: no fire, no blood, no movement; one woman up an ironwood tree as a lookout, also motionless.
3. **Royce orders the advance** — Will leads on foot; Gared stays with the horses. Gared requests permission to make a fire; Royce refuses. Will and Royce reach the ridge.
4. **Bodies are gone** — Will surveys the clearing; all eight wildling bodies have vanished. The double-bladed axe remains, untouched. Royce orders Will up the sentinel tree to look for signs of the missing men.
5. **The Others appear** — Pale shapes glide out of the darkness. One Other steps forward to confront Royce. Five or more additional Others materialize silently around him as watchers.
6. **Will stays silent** — Will sees the Others but cannot bring himself to call out a warning, recognizing it as his duty and his likely death. He stays in the tree.
7. **Combat: Royce vs. the lead Other** — The Other fights with a crystal longsword. Steel meeting the Other's blade produces a high, thin screaming sound, not metal-on-metal. Royce is wounded under the arm; his sword becomes white with frost. The Other speaks to Royce in an unknown language after wounding him.
8. **Royce's sword shatters** — Royce shouts "For Robert!" and swings a full-force blow; the Other parries and the steel shatters into a hundred brittle pieces. A shard transfixes Royce's left eye.
9. **The Others close in and kill Royce** — The watching Others advance together; their pale blades slice through ringmail silently. Will hears "voices and laughter sharp as icicles." Royce is slaughtered.
10. **Will descends** — Will climbs down after a long wait. Finds Royce's body facedown, the sable cloak slashed in a dozen places.
11. **Will recovers a sword shard** — Intends it as proof for Gared, Mormont, or Maester Aemon.
12. **Royce reanimates and kills Will** — Royce stands over Will; right eye open and burning blue; fine clothes tattered; face ruined. Will drops the sword shard and closes his eyes to pray. Royce's moleskin-gloved hands close around Will's throat. Chapter ends.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply frightened throughout; suppresses it because sharing fear with his commander is not acceptable; escalates to paralytic terror during the Others' attack; feels guilt at his failure to call out a warning
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival; getting back to the Wall; finding someone to believe and understand what he has seen
- **Key decisions made:** Follows Royce's order to climb the tree; does NOT call out a warning to Royce when the Others appear (explicitly named as his duty and his death if he did); recovers the broken sword shard as proof; descends after the Others leave
- **Self-deception flags:** Will briefly tells himself his silence may have been justified — "Perhaps he was wrong. Perhaps it had only been a bird, a reflection on the snow, some trick of the moonlight" — rationalizing cowardice as uncertainty
