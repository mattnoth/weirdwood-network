- **approximate_timeline:** Evening of the same day as the Night's Watch deserter's execution; King Robert's arrival is still upcoming
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn finds Ned in the godswood cleaning Ice after the execution. They discuss the wolf pups, the declining strength of the Night's Watch, Mance Rayder, and the Others (which Ned dismisses as myth). Catelyn then delivers two pieces of news from a royal letter: Jon Arryn has died, and King Robert is riding to Winterfell. Ned receives Jon Arryn's death with grief and Robert's coming with joy. The composition of the royal party is discussed — Cersei, the royal children including Prince Tommen, and the Lannister brothers — and Ned begins planning the feast and escort response.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned's post-execution vigil** — Ned sits alone at the heart tree cleaning Ice. Catelyn notes this is his habitual behavior: "Whenever he took a man's life, afterward he would seek the quiet of the godswood."
2. **Catelyn enters the godswood** — She approaches and sits beside the weirwood pool, deliberately keeping her back to the weirwood's carved face.
3. **Discussion of wolf pups** — Catelyn reports the children are in the kitchen arguing about names; Arya is in love with hers, Sansa is charmed, Rickon (age three) is a little afraid.
4. **Ned reflects on the execution** — "The man died well." He notes the deserter was the fourth this year and was "half-mad" with fear so deep Ned's words could not reach him. He expresses pride in Bran.
5. **Night's Watch decline discussed** — Ned relays Benjen's letter: strength below a thousand; men lost on rangings as well as to desertion.
6. **Mance Rayder raised as threat** — Ned speculates he may one day have to call his banners and ride north to deal with the King-beyond-the-Wall.
7. **Exchange about the Others** — Catelyn alludes to darker things beyond the Wall. Ned dismisses the Others as myth, gone eight thousand years. Catelyn counters with the direwolf precedent.
8. **Catelyn delivers Jon Arryn's death** — She tells Ned directly, without softening. The letter reports Jon died quickly; Maester Pycelle was helpless but brought milk of the poppy so Jon did not linger in pain.
9. **Ned reacts to Jon Arryn's death** — Visibly struck by grief; asks if the news is certain. Narration establishes Jon Arryn as Ned's second father and later brother-in-law.
10. **Lysa and the Arryn boy discussed** — Reported well; returned to the Eyrie. Ned urges Catelyn to go to Lysa. Catelyn says she cannot, because of the next news.
11. **Robert's coming announced** — Robert is already riding to Winterfell to seek Ned out. Ned's grief gives way to open joy.
12. **Catelyn notes the direwolf omen internally** — She recalls "a direwolf dead in the snow, a broken antler in its throat" and feels dread, but forces herself to smile. She does not speak this aloud to Ned.
13. **Royal party composition discussed** — At least a hundred knights, retainers, freeriders; Cersei and children; the queen's brothers. Ned grimaces at the Lannisters.
14. **Prince Tommen's age corrected** — Ned thinks Tommen is about five; Catelyn tells him Tommen is seven, the same age as Bran.
15. **Ned plans the response** — Feast with singers; hunting for Robert; Jory south with an honor guard to meet the party on the kingsroad; Maester Luwin to send a raven to Benjen.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Apprehensive and dutiful. Catelyn is unsettled by the godswood and the weirwood's watchful eyes. She carries dread about the royal visit (the direwolf omen) that she does not share with Ned. She worries for her sister Lysa. She forces a smile at Ned's joy.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Delivering the two pieces of news — Jon Arryn's death and Robert's coming — and managing Ned's emotional response to both.
- **Key decisions made:** To wait until after Ned had cleansed himself before delivering the news; to tell him straight without softening; not to share her anxiety about the direwolf omen, knowing Ned "put no faith in signs."
- **Self-deception flags:** She deliberately suppresses her own dread and performs calm for Ned's sake. This is characterized as conscious restraint rather than self-deception.
