## Events & Actions
1. **Council debate on Daenerys Targaryen's assassination** — Robert demands Daenerys, her unborn child, and Viserys be killed after learning Daenerys is pregnant. Ned argues against it on moral grounds. The council votes: Renly, Varys, Pycelle, and Littlefinger support assassination; only Ser Barristan sides with Ned.
2. **Ned resigns as Hand of the King** — Ned refuses to seal the assassination order, removes the Hand's badge, and lays it on the council table. Robert furiously orders Ned to leave King's Landing and threatens to put his head on a spike.
3. **Discussion of assassination methods** — After Ned leaves, the council discusses hiring Faceless Men (too expensive) and offering a lordship to anyone who kills Daenerys. Varys suggests poison (tears of Lys).
4. **Ned begins planning departure** — Ned summons Vayon Poole and orders preparations to leave King's Landing immediately. He plans to take his daughters and a few guards, leaving the rest to follow.
5. **Ned considers sailing to Dragonstone** — Ned thinks of traveling by sea and stopping at Dragonstone to question Stannis Baratheon about Jon Arryn's secret.
6. **Ned sends Poole to the docks** — Ned dispatches Poole to find a fast ship for immediate departure.
7. **Littlefinger visits Ned** — Littlefinger reports that he talked the council out of hiring Faceless Men; instead a lordship will be offered. He offers to take Ned to the brothel Jory has been searching for.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Isolated, morally outraged, saddened, increasingly fearful; feels "unclean" after the council session
- **Primary preoccupation:** The assassination of Daenerys (moral crisis); returning to Winterfell; the unresolved mystery of Jon Arryn's death; worry about the Catelyn-Tyrion situation becoming known
- **Key decisions made:** Resigns as Hand of the King; decides to leave King's Landing immediately with his daughters; considers stopping at Dragonstone to speak with Stannis; sends Poole to find a ship
- **Self-deception flags:** Tells himself Robert would not truly harm him ("He did not truly believe the king would harm him, not Robert"), then immediately undercuts this by recalling Robert's enduring hatred of Rhaegar and the unpredictability of a furious king
