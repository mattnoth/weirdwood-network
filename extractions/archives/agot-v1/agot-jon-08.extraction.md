# AGOT — Jon VIII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 61

- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon VIII
- **first_available:** AGOT Jon VIII
- **location_primary:** Castle Black
- **location_secondary:** King's Tower, Hardin's Tower, the rookery, the granary
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the wight attack on Lord Commander Mormont; Jon's burned hand is healing but still bandaged
- **chapter_summary:** Jon visits Lord Commander Mormont, who gifts him the Valyrian steel bastard sword Longclaw, reforged with a white wolf pommel, as reward for saving his life from the wight Othor. Mormont shares news that Ser Barristan Selmy has been removed from the Kingsguard and that Ser Alliser Thorne has been sent to King's Landing with the wight's severed hand. Jon shows the sword to his friends, then is summoned by Maester Aemon, who counsels him about the conflict between love and duty, revealing himself as Aemon Targaryen and describing his own painful choices when his family was destroyed.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon visits Lord Commander Mormont** — Jon reports to Mormont, who asks about his healing hand. Jon's right hand is bandaged in silk to the elbow with cracked skin and blood blisters.
2. **Search for Benjen fails** — Dywen and Hake return with no sign of Benjen Stark, confirming the failure of the rangers' search.
3. **News of Ser Barristan Selmy** — Mormont reports that Selmy has been removed from the Kingsguard, his place given to Clegane; Selmy is wanted for treason but slew two gold cloaks and escaped.
4. **Mormont gives Jon Longclaw** — The Old Bear presents Jon with the Mormont ancestral Valyrian steel sword, reforged with a new white wolf pommel, black leather grip, and silver-banded scabbard, as reward for saving his life from the wight.
5. **History of Longclaw recounted** — Mormont explains the sword was carried by Mormonts for five centuries, passed to his son Jorah who left it behind when he fled in disgrace; Maege Mormont returned it.
6. **Ser Alliser Thorne sent away** — Mormont reveals he sent Thorne to Eastwatch-by-the-Sea and then to King's Landing with the wight's hand, partly to get evidence before the king and partly to separate Thorne from Jon.
7. **Ser Endrew Tarth named new master-at-arms** — Mormont announces Tarth is coming from the Shadow Tower to replace Thorne.
8. **Jon shows Longclaw to friends** — Pyp, Grenn, Toad, Halder, Matt, and others admire the sword; Halder reveals he helped carve the pommel and Sam bought the garnets.
9. **Jon returns to Hardin's Tower** — Jon retreats to his cell, sullen and unable to share his friends' enthusiasm; reunites with Ghost.
10. **Sam summons Jon to Maester Aemon** — Sam nervously tells Jon that Aemon wants to see him; Jon realizes Sam told Aemon about having shared the news of Robb's war.
11. **Maester Aemon counsels Jon** — In the rookery, Aemon speaks about ravens, the Night's Watch's vow of celibacy, and the eternal conflict between love and duty.
12. **Aemon reveals his identity** — Aemon tells Jon he is Aemon Targaryen, son of King Maekar I, brother of King Aegon V, and that he was tested three times by the gods in his vows, including watching helplessly as his family was destroyed.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply conflicted; angry without knowing why; unable to feel the pride he knows he should feel about receiving Longclaw; haunted by nightmares of the burning corpse with his father's face; bitter about being kept from news of Robb's war; feeling craven for not marching south
- **Primary preoccupation:** The tension between his Night's Watch vows and his loyalty to his family — his father's unknown fate, Robb's war, his sisters' safety
- **Key decisions made:** None yet — this chapter sets up the choice between staying and leaving. Jon accepts the sword but makes no decision about desertion.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon tells Mormont he is "well" and knows it is a lie; he tries to sound "pleased and proud as he ought to have felt" when showing the sword; he tells himself "his place was here now" but still feels craven; he internally protests that Eddard "would do whatever was right" but a "small sly voice" questions Eddard's honor regarding his bastard
