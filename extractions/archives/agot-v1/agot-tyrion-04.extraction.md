# AGOT — Tyrion IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 32
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion IV
- **first_available:** AGOT Tyrion IV
- **location_primary:** The high road through the Mountains of the Moon
- **location_secondary:** The crossroads inn (recalled in memory)
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after the arrest at the crossroads inn; the party is traveling the eastern road toward the Vale of Arryn
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion reflects bitterly on his capture by Catelyn Stark at the crossroads inn and her deception in taking the eastern high road toward the Vale instead of north to Winterfell. The party is attacked by mountain clansmen. Tyrion persuades Catelyn to arm him and his men, fights in his first battle with a double-bladed axe, and saves Catelyn's life during the skirmish. Three of their party die (Kurleket, Mohor, Jyck) while nine clansmen are killed. Afterward, Catelyn allows Tyrion to keep his weapons. As they ride on, Tyrion tells Catelyn he never bets against his family, challenging Littlefinger's story about the dagger.

## Events & Actions
1. **Chiggen butchers Tyrion's horse** — At predawn, Chiggen slaughters Tyrion's mare (a gift from Jaime) for meat because they are running low on provisions.
2. **Tyrion recalls his arrest at the crossroads inn** — Extended flashback: Catelyn publicly called on armed men by invoking oaths to her father; approximately a dozen responded; Tyrion ordered Jyck not to draw his sword and agreed to go willingly; Bronn disarmed them.
3. **Catelyn's deception revealed** — After days of hooded travel, Tyrion discovers they are on the eastern high road toward the Vale, not heading north to Winterfell as Catelyn publicly announced.
4. **Tyrion confronts Catelyn about the dagger and Littlefinger** — Tyrion argues he would never arm a footpad with his own blade; accuses Littlefinger of lying; claims Littlefinger boasts of bedding Catelyn.
5. **Catelyn defends Petyr Baelish** — She insists Petyr loved her once and his passion was real and pure; calls Tyrion evil.
6. **Tyrion presses his argument about the tourney wager** — He challenges the story that he won the dagger betting against Jaime at the tourney, stating he never bets against his family.
7. **Mountain clansmen attack** — Lharys spots 20-25 riders (Milk Snakes or Moon Brothers); they have hidden watchers and know the party is there.
8. **Tyrion persuades Catelyn to arm the prisoners** — Argues they need every sword; she extracts a promise they will surrender weapons after the fight.
9. **The battle** — Ser Rodrik, Bronn, Chiggen, and Ser Willis lead the charge; Tyrion fights on foot with the axe, killing at least two clansmen; Morrec uses a bow; Jyck charges bareback.
10. **Tyrion saves Catelyn Stark** — Three clansmen trap Catelyn against the mountainside; Tyrion attacks from behind, striking one in the knee; Catelyn cuts another's throat; the third flees.
11. **Casualties tallied** — Three of their party killed (Kurleket, Mohor, Jyck); nine clansmen killed; Ser Rodrik wounded; Marillion's ribs, hand, and woodharp broken.
12. **Catelyn allows Tyrion to keep his weapons** — After the battle, Lharys tries to reclaim Tyrion's dirk; Catelyn overrides him, letting Tyrion keep the dirk and axe.
13. **Party resumes travel** — They leave the dead unburied despite Catelyn's wishes, pressured by the danger of a second attack; shadowcats are heard fighting over the corpses.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bitter rage at being outwitted by Catelyn; grim determination; fear during the battle masked by dark humor; growing confidence after surviving his first fight
- **Primary preoccupation:** Escaping captivity; discrediting Littlefinger's story about the dagger to undermine the case against him
- **Key decisions made:** Chose not to resist at the inn (ordered Jyck not to draw); persuaded Catelyn to arm him during the attack; chose to save Catelyn's life despite contemplating letting the clansmen have her; chose to press the argument about Littlefinger and the dagger
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion congratulated himself on his "subtlety" at the inn (planting the idea of reward), but Catelyn had already outmaneuvered him entirely; he also tells Catelyn his father may not bother to pursue him, which is "only half a lie" — he downplays Tywin's likely response while knowing Tywin would act to defend House honor
