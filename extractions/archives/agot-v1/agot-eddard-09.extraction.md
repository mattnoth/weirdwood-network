# AGOT — Eddard IX

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 36
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard IX
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard IX
- **location_primary:** King's Landing — streets and a brothel
- **location_secondary:** Chataya's brothel; the Red Keep (at chapter's end)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Ned has resigned as Hand of the King; Catelyn has already seized Tyrion
- **chapter_summary:** Ned visits a brothel run by Chataya, where he meets Robert's youngest bastard daughter Barra and her mother. Riding back through the rain with Littlefinger, he asks about Robert's other bastards and learns of at least three more. They are ambushed by Jaime Lannister and twenty Lannister soldiers demanding to know about Tyrion's abduction. Ned confirms Catelyn took Tyrion at his command. Jaime decides not to kill Ned but orders his men to kill Ned's guards. In the ensuing fight, Jory Cassel, Heward, and Wyl are killed. Ned's horse falls on him, shattering his leg. Littlefinger returns with the City Watch and Ned is carried to the Red Keep, where Grand Maester Pycelle treats him with milk of the poppy.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned concludes visit to Chataya's brothel** — He pulls on his gloves and tells his men it is time to leave after meeting Barra and her mother.
2. **Ned tells Littlefinger he is no longer Hand of the King** — Corrects Littlefinger when called the Hand.
3. **Ned recalls Lyanna's words about Robert** — Remembers her saying Robert would never keep to one bed and love cannot change a man's nature.
4. **Ned meets Barra and her mother** — The young woman shows Ned the infant daughter she named Barra; the child has Robert's dark hair. She asks Ned to tell Robert about the baby and that she is waiting for him.
5. **Ned recalls Jon Snow's face** — While reflecting on bastards, he sees Jon Snow's face and thinks about the gods and men's lusts.
6. **Ned asks Littlefinger about Robert's bastards** — Littlefinger describes the acknowledged boy at Storm's End (fathered on Stannis's wedding night) and rumors of twins at Casterly Rock whom Cersei allegedly had killed.
7. **Ned questions why Jon Arryn investigated the bastards** — Littlefinger dismisses the idea that anyone would kill Jon Arryn over such common knowledge.
8. **Ned briefly thinks of Rhaegar Targaryen** — Wonders if Rhaegar frequented brothels; thinks not.
9. **Jaime Lannister ambushes Ned's party** — Twenty or more Lannister soldiers block the street, front and rear. Jaime demands to know about Tyrion.
10. **Ned confirms Catelyn took Tyrion at his command** — Publicly claims responsibility for Tyrion's arrest.
11. **Jaime threatens Ned but stands down** — Jaime draws his sword and threatens to kill Ned, but Ned warns that Catelyn will kill Tyrion if Ned dies. Jaime sheathes his sword, unwilling to gamble Tyrion's life.
12. **Littlefinger departs to fetch the City Watch** — The Lannister line parts to let him through.
13. **Jaime orders Ned's men killed** — Tells Tregar to ensure no harm comes to Ned, but to kill his men.
14. **Battle in the street** — Jory, Heward, and Wyl fight the Lannister soldiers. Heward is speared in the belly. Jory's horse's legs are cut from under him and he is killed on the ground. Wyl is pulled from his horse and killed. Ned strikes Tregar's helm.
15. **Ned's horse falls on him** — His horse slips in the mud, crushing his leg. Ned sees splintered bone protruding from his calf.
16. **Ned cradles Jory's body** — He drags himself through mud to Jory's body. No bystanders help.
17. **Littlefinger returns with the City Watch** — They find Ned in the street holding Jory.
18. **Ned is taken to the Red Keep** — Carried on a litter; loses consciousness repeatedly. Pycelle treats him with milk of the poppy and prepares to tend the wound.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grim, sorrowful, burdened by guilt. The visit to the brothel and the sight of Robert's bastard deepens his melancholy. He is haunted by memories of Lyanna and by thoughts of Jon Snow.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Why Jon Arryn was investigating Robert's bastards; the connection between this investigation and Jon Arryn's death. Also burdened by promises — to Barra's mother, to Lyanna.
- **Key decisions made:** Publicly claims responsibility for Tyrion's arrest to Jaime Lannister; uses Catelyn's custody of Tyrion as a deterrent against Jaime killing him.
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned promises Barra's mother he will tell Robert and ensure the child does not go wanting, while internally knowing Robert will forget. He keeps promises others would not, framing it as a curse rather than examining its costs.
