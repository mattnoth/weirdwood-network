- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after Tyrion's release from the Eyrie; he and Bronn have been riding since daybreak after being ushered through the Bloody Gate by Ser Lyn Corbray
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion and Bronn travel through the Mountains of the Moon after being expelled from the Vale through the Bloody Gate. They debate whether to travel stealthily or openly, with Tyrion insisting on building a fire and making camp. Over a meal of roasted goat, Tyrion tells Bronn the story of Tysha, the girl he married as a boy and how Tywin and Jaime destroyed the marriage. During the night, mountain clansmen led by Gunthor of the Stone Crows surround them. Tyrion negotiates with the clansmen, and when threatened with death, offers them the Vale of Arryn in exchange for safe passage.

## Events & Actions
1. **Departure through the Bloody Gate** — Ser Lyn Corbray ushers Tyrion and Bronn through the Bloody Gate at daybreak, commanding them never to return.
2. **Debate over travel strategy** — Bronn advocates stealth (travel by night, no fires, avoid roads), while Tyrion insists on building a fire, arguing stealth will not save them from the clans.
3. **Negotiation of Bronn's loyalty** — Tyrion and Bronn frankly discuss their transactional relationship. Tyrion promises gold, land, and women in exchange for continued protection, and warns Bronn he will always outbid any rival offer.
4. **Camp established** — They build a fire, Bronn hunts and returns with a young goat, and they eat roasted kid with stale bread.
5. **Tyrion tells the story of Tysha** — Tyrion recounts meeting a girl on the road near Lannisport, marrying her secretly with a drunken septon, and then Tywin's punishment: Jaime confessed the girl was a hired whore, and Tywin gave her to his guards, making Tyrion watch and go last.
6. **Tyrion's dream** — He dreams of the sky cells, but this time he is the gaoler beating his father toward the abyss.
7. **Mountain clansmen arrive** — At least ten Stone Crows, led by Gunthor son of Gurn, surround the camp. They claim the goat, the horses, and all possessions.
8. **Tyrion negotiates with the Stone Crows** — When threatened with death, Tyrion mocks their weapons and provokes them, then offers arms, steel, and ultimately the Vale of Arryn in exchange for safe passage.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Outwardly confident and witty, but the Tysha story reveals deep pain and vulnerability; desolation surfaces when recounting Tywin's punishment despite years of distance
- **Primary preoccupation:** Immediate survival in the mountains; securing Bronn's loyalty through gold; underlying drive to investigate the dagger and settle debts
- **Key decisions made:** Chose to build a fire openly (deliberate gamble to attract the clans rather than flee); chose to negotiate with the Stone Crows rather than fight; offered them the Vale of Arryn
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion accepts Jaime's confession that Tysha was a whore without question — he repeats "the girl was a whore" as established fact
