## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT

- **pov_chapter_number:** Arya I
- **first_available:** AGOT Arya I
- **location_primary:** Winterfell (tower sewing room; covered bridge between armory and Great Keep; guardroom)
- **location_secondary:** The yard (training yard at Winterfell)
- **approximate_timeline:** During the royal court's visit to Winterfell; after the feast at which Joffrey sat with Sansa and Arya sat with Tommen
- **chapter_summary:** Arya struggles with needlework in a tower room alongside Sansa, Septa Mordane, and Princess Myrcella. After her poor stitching is publicly noted and she is humiliated, she flees and collects her direwolf Nymeria. She finds Jon watching the training yard from a window in the covered bridge, and together they observe Bran and Prince Tommen spar, followed by a confrontation between Joffrey and Robb over live-steel practice. The Hound (Sandor Clegane, identified by name) intervenes on Joffrey's behalf but Ser Rodrik refuses live steel. Joffrey and his party depart, mocking the Starks. Jon advises Arya to return to face her punishment; when she does, she finds both Septa Mordane and Lady Catelyn waiting.

## Events & Actions
1. **Needlework humiliation** — Arya's crooked stitches are examined by Septa Mordane in front of Sansa, Jeyne, Beth, and Princess Myrcella. Arya flees the room in tears after Septa Mordane publicly criticizes her work.
2. **Arya's exit** — Arya curtsies to Myrcella and quips "I have to go shoe a horse" before running out. Septa Mordane threatens to tell Lady Catelyn.
3. **Arya collects Nymeria** — She retrieves her direwolf from the guardroom at the base of the stairs.
4. **Arya finds Jon** — She climbs to the covered bridge window, where Jon is already watching the yard with Ghost.
5. **Bran vs. Tommen sparring bout** — Both boys are heavily padded and fighting with padded wooden swords. Tommen is knocked down; Bran stands over him ready to strike again. Ser Rodrik calls a halt and helps Tommen up.
6. **Ser Rodrik calls for the next round** — He invites Prince Joffrey and Robb to spar.
7. **Live steel confrontation** — Joffrey declares practice "a game for children" and demands live steel. Ser Rodrik refuses and offers tourney blunted swords instead. Sandor Clegane speaks up in support of Joffrey against Ser Rodrik.
8. **Joffrey mocks Robb and departs** — Joffrey declines to fight with blunted swords and leaves with Tommen and his Lannister men, mocking Robb. Robb curses; Theon physically restrains him.
9. **Jon advises Arya to return** — Jon tells her the longer she hides the worse the penance, then walks away with Ghost. Arya follows Nymeria back toward her room.
10. **Arya returns to her room** — She finds not only Septa Mordane but Lady Catelyn waiting.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Ashamed, resentful, and angry at the start (needlework failure, Sansa comparison); briefly cheered by Nymeria and Jon; still stung by the unfairness of her position; resigned and anxious at the end
- **Primary preoccupation:** The unfairness of her situation — she cannot do needlework well, she is excluded from the swordsmanship she would prefer, and she is constantly compared unfavorably to Sansa
- **Key decisions made:** Flees the sewing room rather than endure further humiliation; chooses to watch the yard rather than return to her room; eventually decides to go back and face the consequences
- **Self-deception flags:** Arya frames everything as "not fair," but does not examine any ways she might change her circumstances; her fantasy of seeing Robb beat Joffrey drives her to the yard but produces no satisfaction
