# AGOT — Eddard IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 21
- **pov_character:** Eddard Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Eddard IV
- **first_available:** AGOT Eddard IV
- **location_primary:** King's Landing (Red Keep council chamber; brothel owned by Littlefinger)
- **location_secondary:** Moat Cailin (mentioned), White Harbor (mentioned), Winterfell (mentioned), Dragonstone (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the Stark party arrives at King's Landing; Robert has not yet entered the city
- **chapter_summary:** Eddard arrives at the Red Keep exhausted and is immediately summoned to a small council meeting, where he learns the Crown is over six million gold dragons in debt and Robert wants a costly tournament held in Ned's honor. After adjourning the council, Littlefinger leads Ned outside the castle walls and to a brothel he owns, where Catelyn has been secretly sheltering. Catelyn presents the Valyrian-steel dagger used in the assassination attempt on Bran and reveals she suspects Tyrion Lannister ordered the attack. Ned instructs Catelyn to return to Winterfell and issues covert military precautions — fortifying Moat Cailin, strengthening White Harbor, and watching Theon Greyjoy — while resolving to find proof of Lannister involvement in Jon Arryn's death before going to Robert.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned arrives at the Red Keep** — Riding through the bronze gates, exhausted from the kingsroad journey; he is immediately told the small council has convened an urgent meeting.
2. **Ned instructs Vayon Poole** — To settle his daughters into their chambers; tells Jory to keep them there and not let Arya explore.
3. **Small council meets** — Five members present (Ned, Varys, Renly, Littlefinger, Pycelle); Stannis and Ser Barristan are absent.
4. **Tournament command read** — Robert's letter orders a tournament in Ned's honor: 40,000 gold dragons to the joust champion, 20,000 for second, 20,000 for the melee winner, 10,000 for archery victor (90,000 total prize money).
5. **Crown debt revealed** — Littlefinger discloses the Crown is over six million gold dragons in debt: primarily to the Lannisters (~3 million), but also to Lord Tyrell, the Iron Bank of Braavos, Tyroshi trading cartels, and the Faith.
6. **Ned adjourns the council** — Abruptly, over implicit objections; resolves to speak with Robert about canceling the tournament.
7. **Littlefinger intercepts Ned** — Outside the council chamber, redirects him away from the Tower of the Hand.
8. **Littlefinger leads Ned out of the castle** — Through a corridor of Targaryen armor, down a stair, out through a door in the bluff, and down rock-cut niches to the river trail below.
9. **Ned confronts Littlefinger at the brothel door** — Seizes him and puts a dagger to his chin when he realizes they are at a brothel, not his chambers; Ser Rodrik's voice stops him.
10. **Ned reunites with Catelyn** — Inside the brothel's third floor; Catelyn embraces him and presents the assassin's dagger.
11. **Catelyn recounts Bran's assassination attempt** — Tells Ned everything: the library fire, the assassin, Bran's wolf killing the man, her own hand wounds, and the intelligence trail pointing to Tyrion Lannister.
12. **The dagger debated** — Ned identifies it as the Imp's dagger; Littlefinger warns the dagger is weak evidence because Tyrion can claim it was lost or stolen, and suggests dropping it in the river.
13. **Ned refuses to discard the dagger** — Cites his duty to Bran and Catelyn; keeps it.
14. **Ned orders Catelyn to return to Winterfell immediately** — Citing continued assassination risk; forbids her from seeing the girls.
15. **Ned issues covert military orders** — Instructs Catelyn to relay: Tallhart and Glover to raise 100 bowmen each and fortify Moat Cailin; Manderly to strengthen White Harbor; watch kept on Theon Greyjoy.
16. **Ned resolves to find proof of Lannister involvement** — Plans to bring it to Robert; internally fears Robert may not be the man he once was.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Exhausted, irritable on arrival; increasingly alarmed at the Crown's financial state; stunned and grieved upon hearing Catelyn's full account of Bran's assassination attempt; cold fury toward Littlefinger; deeply uneasy about the Lannisters and about Robert's fitness as king
- **Primary preoccupation:** The attempt on Bran's life and who ordered it; his distrust of nearly everyone on the small council; how to protect his family while navigating King's Landing politics
- **Key decisions made:** Keeps the dagger rather than discarding it; orders Catelyn home immediately; issues covert military precautions (Moat Cailin, White Harbor, Theon watch); resolves to find proof of Lannister guilt before approaching Robert
- **Self-deception flags:** Ned tells himself "It will not come to war" while simultaneously ordering military fortifications; he says he will not believe the king was involved in Bran's assassination attempt while his internal thoughts immediately recall Robert ordering a hired knife against Daenerys Targaryen and turning away from violence he had sanctioned
