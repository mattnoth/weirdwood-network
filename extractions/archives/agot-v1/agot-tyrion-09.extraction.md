# AGOT — Tyrion IX

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 70
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion IX
- **first_available:** AGOT Tyrion IX
- **location_primary:** An inn on the road south (the same inn Tyrion has stayed at before)
- **location_secondary:** Riverrun (discussed), King's Landing (discussed), Harrenhal (discussed), Casterly Rock (mentioned), Dragonstone (mentioned), Highgarden (mentioned), Storm's End (mentioned), the Whispering Wood (mentioned), the Green Fork (mentioned), Moat Cailin (mentioned), the Twins (mentioned)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the Battle of the Camps at Riverrun; Tywin's army has been marching south from the Green Fork
- **chapter_summary:** A Crakehall messenger brings news to Tywin Lannister's war council that Robb Stark has broken the siege of Riverrun, capturing Jaime Lannister. The assembled lords debate what to do, with some urging peace and others wanting to fight on. After dismissing everyone except Tyrion and Kevan, Tywin reveals that Renly Baratheon has married Margaery Tyrell and claimed the crown. Tywin announces plans to march to Harrenhal and unleash Gregor Clegane, Vargo Hoat, and Amory Lorch to burn the riverlands. He then orders Tyrion to go to King's Landing to serve as Hand of the King in his stead, reining in Joffrey and the council. Tyrion realizes his father has given up Jaime for lost.

## Events & Actions
1. **Crakehall messenger delivers battle report** — A bloodstained courier arrives and reports the fall of Riverrun and Jaime's capture to Tywin's war council.
2. **Battle of the Camps described** — The Blackfish led the van against the north camp at night; the Starks swept in from the west in two columns led by Robb with his direwolf; Lord Brax drowned trying to cross the river on a raft; Tully catapults sank rafts; Tytos Blackwood sortied from Riverrun and freed Edmure Tully; Greatjon Umber fired siege towers; the Tyroshi sellsword defected; Ser Forley Prester retreated with survivors.
3. **War council debate** — Ser Harys Swyft urges suing for peace; Ser Addam Marbrand wants to march against the Starks; Lord Lefford suggests prisoner exchange or ransom; Tyrion argues peace is impossible after Eddard Stark's execution.
4. **Tyrion shatters his wine cup** — Throws his cup to the floor to illustrate that peace is broken beyond repair.
5. **Tywin dismisses council, keeps Tyrion and Kevan** — Tywin clears the room but asks Tyrion and Kevan to remain, which shocks Tyrion.
6. **Tywin offers Tyrion his untouched wine** — A gesture that surprises Tyrion.
7. **Tywin reveals Renly's claim** — Renly Baratheon has married Margaery Tyrell and claimed the crown, with the Tyrells' support.
8. **Cersei's command revealed** — Cersei has ordered Tywin to ride to King's Landing to defend against Renly, but has not told Joffrey about Renly's claim.
9. **Tywin announces march to Harrenhal** — Plans to move to Harrenhal with Ser Addam's outriders screening movements in groups of four.
10. **Tywin orders the burning of the riverlands** — Ser Gregor Clegane, Vargo Hoat, and Ser Amory Lorch each given 300 horse to burn from the Gods Eye to the Red Fork.
11. **Tywin appoints Tyrion as acting Hand** — Orders Tyrion to go to King's Landing to "rule" — rein in Joffrey and the council.
12. **Tywin criticizes Joffrey's court** — Condemns the elevation of Janos Slynt, the dismissal of Barristan Selmy, and the poor counsel of Littlefinger, Pycelle, and Varys.
13. **Tywin forbids Tyrion from taking Shae to court** — His final command: "You will not take the whore to court."
14. **Tyrion decides to take Shae anyway** — Tells Shae he has "a mind to take you to King's Landing."

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Complex mixture — sardonic detachment masking grief for Jaime; pain from battle wound; bitter amusement at the political chaos; shock and grudging pleasure at being chosen for King's Landing; deep resentment toward his father
- **Primary preoccupation:** Jaime's capture and the family's collapsing military position; his relationship with his father
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to keep his clansmen (Shagga, Timett) with him rather than sending them with Vargo Hoat; decides to defy his father's order and take Shae to King's Landing
- **Self-deception flags:** None overt — Tyrion's internal assessments appear clear-eyed in this chapter
