Ser Raymun Darry, Ser Karyl Vance, and Ser Marq Piper present evidence and ask leave to retaliate. Ned refuses to let the Riverlands lords take vengeance, instead commissioning Lord Beric Dondarrion to lead a force to bring the king's justice to Clegane. Ned formally denounces, attaints, and sentences Gregor Clegane to death in Robert's name.

## Events & Actions
1. **Ned sits the Iron Throne** — In Robert's absence (hunting a white hart), Ned presides over court despite his shattered leg.
2. **Villagers from Sherrer petition for justice** — Survivors of raids on Sherrer, Wendish Town, and Mummer's Ford are brought before the throne by three Riverlands knights.
3. **Testimony identifies Gregor Clegane** — Witnesses describe the raiders as mounted, armored men led by a giant with a voice like stone breaking. Ser Marq Piper names him as the Mountain.
4. **Pycelle argues for deferral** — Grand Maester Pycelle urges sending complaints to Lord Tywin and waiting for the king's return.
5. **Ned refuses to defer** — Ned asserts that all justice is the king's justice and that Robert commanded him to act in his place.
6. **Ned dispatches Ser Robar Royce** — Sends him to inform the hunting party of the day's proceedings.
7. **Ser Loras Tyrell volunteers** — Offers to lead the mission against Clegane; Ned refuses him.
8. **Ned commissions Lord Beric Dondarrion** — Assigns Beric, Thoros of Myr, Ser Gladden, Lord Lothar, and twenty of his own guards to ride to the westlands.
9. **Ned formally sentences Gregor Clegane** — Denounces, attaints, and strips Clegane of all rank, titles, lands, incomes, and holdings; sentences him to death in Robert's name.
10. **Varys privately counsels Ned** — Suggests Ned should have sent Ser Loras (to court Tyrell friendship) and notes Ser Ilyn Payne appeared displeased at being bypassed.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Angry, pained, reluctant, grimly determined. His leg is in constant agony. He is furious at the raids but controls it. He resents being in this position ("What Eddard Stark was doing sitting there he would never comprehend").
- **Primary preoccupation:** Delivering justice while avoiding escalation into open war; seeing through Tywin Lannister's strategy of provoking Riverrun.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Refuses to let the Riverlands knights retaliate directly. (2) Refuses Ser Loras's offer. (3) Commissions Lord Beric Dondarrion instead. (4) Formally attaints and sentences Gregor Clegane to death. (5) Sends Ser Robar to notify the king. (6) Deliberately bypasses Ser Ilyn Payne (Lannister bannerman).
- **Self-deception flags:** None apparent. Ned's analysis of Tywin's strategy is clear-eyed. His preference for his own Northern customs ("the judge who called for death should wield the sword") is acknowledged as impractical given his injury.
