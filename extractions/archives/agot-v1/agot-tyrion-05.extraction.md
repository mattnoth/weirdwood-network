# AGOT — Tyrion V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 39
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister
- **pov_chapter_number:** Tyrion V
- **first_available:** AGOT Tyrion V
- **location_primary:** The Eyrie (sky cells; High Hall of the Arryns)
- **location_secondary:** None (Casterly Rock, King's Landing, Mountains of the Moon, Winterfell, the Bloody Gate mentioned in dialogue/thought only)
- **approximate_timeline:** Days after Tyrion's arrival at the Eyrie; follows his imprisonment in the sky cells
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion Lannister languishes in a sky cell at the Eyrie, tormented by the turnkey Mord who withholds food and beats him. After days of deterioration, Tyrion bribes Mord with promises of gold to deliver a message to Lady Lysa saying he wishes to confess his crimes. Brought before the assembled court in the High Hall, Tyrion delivers a mocking confession of petty sins rather than admitting to the murders he is accused of. When Lysa orders him returned to a worse cell, Tyrion demands a trial, then specifically demands trial by combat. After Lysa names Ser Vardis Egen as her champion, Tyrion requests Jaime as his champion but is denied the delay. The sellsword Bronn volunteers to stand as Tyrion's champion.

## Events & Actions
1. **Mord taunts Tyrion with food** — Mord offers beans then holds them out of reach over the sky cell edge, drops the plate into the void when Tyrion refuses to approach
2. **Mord kicks Tyrion** — After Tyrion curses him, Mord drives a steel-toed boot into Tyrion's ribs
3. **Tyrion recalls arrival at the Eyrie** — In extended flashback, Tyrion remembers Bronn carrying him up the final climb, being presented before Lysa and Robert, trading barbs, Robert demanding "I want to see him fly," Catelyn intervening, and Ser Vardis hauling him to the sky cells
4. **Tyrion's physical deterioration** — Growing weaker daily from cold, hunger, beatings; fears he cannot last much longer
5. **Tyrion reflects on his situation and suspects a third party** — Wonders who sent the footpad to kill Bran, suspects the murder of Jon Arryn was deft while the attempt on Bran was clumsy, raises the possibility someone else is using him as a catspaw
6. **Tyrion bribes Mord** — Offers gold in exchange for delivering a message; Mord beats him with the leather strap multiple times during the negotiation; Tyrion nearly falls off the cell edge; eventually Mord agrees after Tyrion writes a promise of gold
7. **Tyrion brought before the court** — Ser Vardis Egen retrieves Tyrion; Tyrion reclaims his shadowskin cloak from Mord
8. **Tyrion delivers mock confession** — Confesses to whoring, gambling, cheating, wishing his father and sister dead, and cruelty — drawing laughter — but denies the murder charges
9. **Tyrion demands a trial** — When Lysa orders him back to a worse cell, Tyrion invokes his right to a trial, appealing to king's justice and the rights of the Seven Kingdoms
10. **Lysa opens the Moon Door** — Orders the Moon Door opened as a threat, announces Robert will judge Tyrion
11. **Tyrion demands trial by combat** — Instead of a trial before Robert, Tyrion invokes the gods' judgment through trial by combat
12. **Multiple lords volunteer to champion Lysa** — Unnamed knight with green viper sigil, Lord Hunter, Ser Albar Royce, Ser Lyn Corbray, and a dozen others compete for the honor
13. **Lysa names Ser Vardis Egen as champion** — Ser Vardis reluctantly accepts, calling it shameful to fight a dwarf
14. **Tyrion requests Jaime as champion** — Asks for a raven to be sent; Lysa refuses the delay and demands the combat happen on the morrow
15. **Bronn volunteers as Tyrion's champion** — After a long silence where no one moves, Bronn calls out from the rear of the hall

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Shifts from despair and physical misery in the sky cell to calculated determination when he devises his plan; fearful but refusing to show it; humor as armor throughout
- **Primary preoccupation:** Survival and escape from the Eyrie; specifically, how to talk his way out since physical escape is impossible
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Bribes Mord to deliver a false confession message; (2) Delivers a mocking confession instead of a real one; (3) Demands trial when threatened with return to cells; (4) Demands trial by combat instead of trial by Lord Robert; (5) Requests a champion when Ser Vardis is named
- **Self-deception flags:** None apparent — Tyrion is notably clear-eyed about his situation, his family's flaws, and the risks of his own mouth
