# AGOT — Sansa III

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 45
- **pov_character:** Sansa Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Sansa III
- **first_available:** AGOT Sansa III
- **location_primary:** Tower of the Hand, King's Landing
- **location_secondary:** The Red Keep (throne room gallery, kitchens, solar), King's Landing streets/gates
- **approximate_timeline:** Day of and day after the court session where Eddard dispatches Lord Beric against Gregor Clegane; Eddard's leg is still broken and healing
- **chapter_summary:** Sansa and Jeyne Poole share supper discussing the day's court events, particularly Eddard's refusal to send Ser Loras against Gregor Clegane. Sansa recalls Littlefinger's unsettling remark that "life is not a song." The next morning, Lord Beric rides out with a mixed force bearing royal, Stark, and Dondarrion banners. After a breakfast quarrel with Arya that ruins Sansa's ivory silk dress, Eddard summons both daughters and tells them he is sending them back to Winterfell by ship for their safety. Sansa protests desperately, declaring her love for Joffrey and insisting their children will be golden-haired; Eddard reacts strangely to this remark and orders preparations for a fast galley, commanding secrecy.

## Events & Actions
1. **Sansa and Jeyne share supper** — They eat a cold supper by lamplight, discussing court events while Eddard sups separately with Alyn, Harwin, and Vayon Poole.
2. **Sansa recalls Littlefinger's encounter on the stairs** — After court, Baelish overheard Sansa questioning Eddard's decision not to send Loras. He touched her cheek and told her "life is not a song."
3. **Sansa and Jeyne search for lemon cakes** — They find half a cold strawberry pie instead and eat it on the tower steps.
4. **Lord Beric rides out at dawn** — His force departs with three banners (royal, Stark, Dondarrion) as dawn breaks. Alyn carries the Stark banner.
5. **Breakfast quarrel between Sansa and Arya** — They argue about Joffrey, the Hound, and Mycah's death. Arya throws a blood orange at Sansa, ruining her ivory silk dress.
6. **Sansa retreats to her room in grief** — She strips off the ruined dress, throws it in the hearth, and cries herself to sleep.
7. **Sansa dreams of Lady** — She dreams of running with Lady; the wolf has golden eyes, sad and knowing. The dream fades when she wakes.
8. **Eddard summons both daughters** — He tells them he is sending them both back to Winterfell by fast trading galley for their safety, with Septa Mordane, guards, and possibly Syrio Forel.
9. **Sansa declares her love for Joffrey** — She pleads to stay, saying she loves Joffrey and wants to give him "a son with golden hair."
10. **Eddard reacts strangely** — After Sansa says her son will be golden-haired and Arya says Joffrey is "a stag, not a lion," Eddard swears "Gods… out of the mouth of babes" and immediately calls for Septa Mordane.
11. **Eddard orders secrecy** — He instructs the girls to say nothing about the planned departure.
12. **Sansa insults Arya** — After Arya tries to comfort her, Sansa tells her she should marry Hodor.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Bewildered and disappointed by her father's court decisions; uneasy after Littlefinger's encounter; proud watching Lord Beric ride out; furious and grief-stricken after Arya ruins her dress; desperate and devastated when told she must leave King's Landing
- **Primary preoccupation:** Maintaining her romantic vision of court life, her betrothal to Joffrey, and her place in King's Landing — all of which are under threat
- **Key decisions made:** None — Sansa is acted upon throughout this chapter; she protests but has no agency over the outcomes
- **Self-deception flags:** Believes Joffrey had no part in Jory's murder and blames Jaime entirely; sees court life through the lens of songs and stories despite Littlefinger's warning; convinces herself her dream about Joffrey killing the white hart is prophetic when she admits internally it was "more of a wish"; insists Joffrey is a true prince despite evidence to the contrary
