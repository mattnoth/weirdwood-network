# AGOT — Tyrion II

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 14
- **pov_character:** Tyrion Lannister

- **location_primary:** The kingsroad north of Winterfell (the wolfswood)
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell (departure point, recalled); King's Landing (recalled); Casterly Rock (recalled); a cellar beneath King's Landing (recalled); the Summer Isles (mentioned as origin of wine); the Fingers (mentioned as origin of Yoren's recruits); the Port of Ibben (mentioned); the Reach (recalled — Field of Fire)
- **approximate_timeline:** Approximately eighteen days after departing Winterfell; the royal party has already turned south on the kingsroad
- **chapter_summary:** Tyrion Lannister rides north through the wolfswood with Benjen Stark, Jon Snow, Yoren, two recruits, and two of Tyrion's own men. On the eighteenth night of the journey Tyrion reads alone beside a stream — a book on dragons — and reflects at length on the dragon skulls he once examined in a cellar beneath King's Landing and on the Field of Fire. Jon Snow approaches and the two have a prolonged conversation: Tyrion explains why he reads, confesses his childhood longing for a dragon, and bluntly tells Jon that the Night's Watch is not the noble institution Jon imagines. Ghost knocks Tyrion down and pins him; Jon helps him up after Tyrion asks politely. The chapter ends with Tyrion watching Jon stare into the campfire.

## Events & Actions
1. **Departure from Winterfell** — Tyrion's party left on the same day as the royal party; the king's procession turned south on the kingsroad while Tyrion turned north with Benjen Stark and Jon Snow.
2. **Joining Yoren** — At the edge of the wolfswood, the party stayed at a forest holdfast and joined up with Yoren and his two recruits from the Fingers.
3. **Nightly reading** — On the eighteenth night, Tyrion settles alone beside a stream with wine and a book about dragons.
4. **Tyrion recalls the dragon skulls** — Extended flashback: Tyrion previously visited a cellar beneath the Red Keep during Cersei's wedding to Robert and examined the nineteen dragon skulls; he found them beautiful.
5. **Tyrion recalls the Field of Fire** — Reads about or mentally revisits the history: Aegon, with his sisters and three dragons, defeated the combined armies of King Loren and King Mern; roughly four thousand men burned; King Mern died; King Loren survived and swore fealty.
6. **Jon Snow approaches Tyrion** — Jon finds Tyrion reading beside the stream and asks why he reads so much.
7. **Tyrion's speech on self-knowledge** — Tyrion catalogues his physical limitations and explains that his mind is his weapon; books are its whetstone.
8. **Exchange about dragons** — Jon says there are no more dragons; Tyrion says he used to dream of having one and imagined flying above the world on dragonback.
9. **Tyrion provokes Jon** — Tyrion implies Jon must also dream of revenge or escape; Jon denies it angrily; Tyrion taunts him about Catelyn's treatment of him, Robb inheriting Winterfell, and being sent to the Wall.
10. **Tyrion denounces the Night's Watch** — Tells Jon the Watch is "a midden heap for all the misfits of the realm" composed of sullen peasants, debtors, poachers, rapers, thieves, and bastards.
11. **Ghost attacks Tyrion** — Ghost knocks Tyrion flat without warning and without making a sound; holds him pinned with a show of teeth and red eyes.
12. **Jon forces Tyrion to ask politely** — Jon makes Tyrion request help graciously before calling Ghost off.
13. **Jon helps Tyrion up and returns the book** — Jon lifts Tyrion to his feet and hands back the fallen book.
14. **Wine shared** — Tyrion offers the wineskin to Jon; Jon takes a cautious swallow; Jon acknowledges the truth of Tyrion's words about the Night's Watch.
15. **Return to camp** — Both return; Tyrion eats stew with black bread and hard cheese; Tyrion's wine skin is shared around until even Yoren mellows.
16. **Night watch** — Jon draws the first watch; stands staring into the fire; Tyrion watches him before retiring.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Physically uncomfortable (cramping legs, sore back, cold); stoic about his discomfort; privately proud; briefly guilty after provoking Jon; capable of genuine warmth at the chapter's end (smiles "sadly" watching Jon at the fire)
- **Primary preoccupation:** Dragons (reading about them; recalling the skulls; discussing them with Jon); his own identity as a dwarf and a Lannister; assessing Jon Snow
- **Key decisions made:** Accepts Benjen's bearskin deliberately, knowing Stark expected him to decline — a small act of Lannister stubbornness. Chooses to share wine and soften toward Jon after the Ghost incident.
- **Self-deception flags:** Tyrion frames his mocking of Jon as truth-telling and even care ("he had a realistic grasp of his own strengths and weaknesses"), but the text notes he "felt guilty" immediately after — he acknowledges the cruelty even as he couches it in honesty.
