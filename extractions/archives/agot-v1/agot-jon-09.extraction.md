- **location_primary:** The kingsroad south of Castle Black

- **chapter_summary:** Jon Snow deserts the Night's Watch at night, riding south on the kingsroad toward Robb's army to avenge his father's death. Sam Tarly tries to stop him at the stables but fails, then alerts their friends. Grenn, Pyp, Halder, Toad, Matthar, and others catch Jon south of Mole's Town and persuade him to return by reciting the Night's Watch oath. The next morning, Lord Commander Mormont reveals he knew of Jon's attempt and had ordered a watch on him. Mormont describes alarming reports from beyond the Wall — wildlings massing, villages abandoned, strange signs — and announces the Night's Watch will ride beyond the Wall in force. Jon pledges himself to Mormont and the Watch.

## Events & Actions
1. **Jon deserts Castle Black** — Jon saddles a mare at night, packs supplies, and rides south on the kingsroad with Ghost, intending to join Robb's army to avenge their father.
2. **Sam tries to stop Jon** — Sam stands in the stable door and pleads with Jon not to leave. Jon rides past him, and Sam jumps aside at the last moment, falling.
3. **Jon rides through Mole's Town** — Jon passes through the mostly underground village without stopping; a dog barks.
4. **Jon stops to rest and eat** — South of Mole's Town, Jon dismounts, walks the mare, drinks snowmelt, and eats a biscuit, cheese, and apple.
5. **Ghost disappears and returns** — Ghost falls behind during a gallop, does not respond to calls, then reappears from the trees just as Jon is hiding from pursuers.
6. **Jon's friends catch him** — A group of seven (including Pyp, Grenn, Halder, Toad, and Matthar) surround Jon south of Mole's Town. Jon draws his sword but cannot bring himself to use it.
7. **The friends recite the Night's Watch oath** — Each friend recites a portion of the oath, persuading Jon to lower his sword and return.
8. **Jon returns to Castle Black** — The group rides back before dawn. Jon privately resolves to try deserting again later.
9. **Jon serves Mormont breakfast** — Jon fetches breakfast from Three-Finger Hobb and brings it to Mormont in the King's Tower.
10. **Mormont confronts Jon** — Mormont reveals he knew of Jon's desertion attempt; Maester Aemon predicted it and Mormont ordered a watch set on Jon.
11. **Mormont reports threats beyond the Wall** — Mormont describes reports from Cotter Pyke (elk herds, mammoths, misshapen footprints near Eastwatch), Ser Denys (abandoned villages, mountain fires), and Qhorin Halfhand (captive who says Mance Rayder is massing people in a secret stronghold).
12. **Mormont announces the Great Ranging** — Mormont declares the Night's Watch will ride beyond the Wall in force against Mance Rayder, the Others, and whatever else is out there. He will command personally.
13. **Jon pledges to stay** — Jon swears himself to Mormont and the Night's Watch, abandoning his plan to desert. Mormont tells him to go put on Longclaw.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Torn, guilty, angry, then resigned and recommitted. Jon cycles through determination (riding south), guilt (about Mormont, Sam, his oath), anger (at friends for stopping him), hopelessness, and finally a sense of belonging when he pledges to Mormont.
- **Primary preoccupation:** The conflict between family duty (avenging his father, joining Robb) and his Night's Watch oath. Secondary: his identity as a bastard and outsider.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Decides to desert the Night's Watch. (2) Lowers his sword and returns with his friends. (3) Privately plans to try deserting again. (4) Abandons that plan and pledges himself fully to Mormont after hearing of the ranging.
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon briefly tells himself he'll try to desert again and plans alternative routes, but this resolve seems hollow given his inability to harm his friends. He tells himself "I warned him" about Sam as justification for nearly riding him down.
