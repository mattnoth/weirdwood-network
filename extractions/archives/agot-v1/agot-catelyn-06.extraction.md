# AGOT — Catelyn VI

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 35
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn VI
- **first_available:** AGOT Catelyn VI
- **location_primary:** The Vale of Arryn (the high road, the Bloody Gate, the Gates of the Moon, the ascent to the Eyrie, the Eyrie)
- **location_secondary:** Riverrun (recalled), King's Landing (referenced)
- **approximate_timeline:** Shortly after the mountain road journey; Catelyn arrives at the Bloody Gate and ascends to the Eyrie within approximately one day and one night
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn and her diminished party are met by Ser Donnel Waynwood on the high road and escorted to the Bloody Gate, where her uncle Brynden Tully serves as Knight of the Gate. Brynden warns her that Lysa has become fearful and may not be helpful. They descend into the Vale and reach the Gates of the Moon, where Lord Nestor Royce hosts them. Catelyn ascends the mountain to the Eyrie overnight with the guide Mya Stone. At the Eyrie, Lysa greets her warmly at first but turns furious when she learns Catelyn has brought Tyrion Lannister, refusing to be drawn into conflict with the Lannisters. The chapter ends with Lysa's sickly son Robert Arryn suggesting they make Tyrion "fly."

## Events & Actions
1. **Ser Donnel meets Catelyn's party on the high road** — He informs her the road is unsafe and the clans have grown bolder since Jon Arryn's death
2. **Catelyn recalls the mountain clan attacks** — Three men died in the first attack, two in the second, and Lannister's serving man died of wound-fever; six total dead
3. **Arrival at the Bloody Gate** — Brynden Tully, Knight of the Gate, recognizes Catelyn and grants passage in the name of Robert Arryn
4. **Catelyn tells Brynden the full story** — She recounts Lysa's letter, Bran's fall, the assassin's dagger, Littlefinger, and the chance meeting with Tyrion at the crossroads inn
5. **Brynden warns Catelyn about Lysa** — He tells her Lysa has changed, is fearful, may not be helpful, and that bringing Tyrion is bringing "the lion to her door"
6. **Brynden describes Vale politics** — Anger over Jaime being named Warden of the East; suspicion about Jon Arryn's death; Robert Arryn's weakness; Nestor Royce as high steward for fourteen years; suitors competing for Lysa's hand
7. **Descent into the Vale and ride to the Gates of the Moon** — The party rides through the valley, arriving at the Gates of the Moon after dark
8. **Lysa orders Catelyn brought up immediately** — Lord Nestor relays that Lysa wants to see Catelyn at once, despite it being night
9. **Tyrion is confined to a tower cell** — Lord Nestor orders him taken to a tower cell with meat and mead
10. **Mya Stone guides Catelyn up the mountain** — Night ascent on mules through three waycastles (Stone, Snow, Sky)
11. **Catelyn freezes on the exposed saddle between rock spires** — Overcome by vertigo and fear; Mya leads her across blind and trembling
12. **Catelyn takes the basket for the final ascent** — From Sky, she rides the winch basket to the Eyrie rather than climbing the chimney
13. **Arrival at the Eyrie** — Ser Vardis Egen and Maester Colemon receive Catelyn; dawn has broken
14. **Lysa's reception** — Initially warm embrace, then furious accusation that Catelyn has endangered them by bringing Tyrion
15. **Robert Arryn appears** — The sickly six-year-old enters; Lysa breastfeeds him; he suggests making Tyrion "fly"

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Exhausted, guilt-ridden over the six dead men, increasingly doubtful about Tyrion's guilt, frustrated and angry with Lysa's cowardice and refusal to act
- **Primary preoccupation:** Getting Tyrion to the Eyrie to answer for his alleged crimes; hoping Lysa will be an ally against the Lannisters
- **Key decisions made:** Decides to leave Ser Rodrik at the Bloody Gate to recover; allows Marillion and Bronn to continue to the Eyrie (reluctantly for Bronn); takes the basket rather than climbing the final ascent; charges Lord Nestor to guard Tyrion
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn "pushed her doubts away" about Tyrion's guilt despite acknowledging she might be wrong; she expected Lysa to be a willing ally despite Brynden's clear warnings
