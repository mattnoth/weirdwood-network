# AGOT — Bran IV

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 25
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran IV
- **first_available:** AGOT Bran IV
- **location_primary:** Winterfell (tower room, great hall, Bran's bedchamber)
- **location_secondary:** King's Landing (mentioned), the Wall (mentioned), the haunted forest (mentioned, in Old Nan's story)
- **approximate_timeline:** After Eddard, Catelyn, and the rest of the Stark household have departed Winterfell; Robb is acting lord; Bran has recently turned eight
- **chapter_summary:** Bran watches Rickon play with the direwolves from his window, unable to join them. Old Nan tells him the story of the Long Night and the Others until Maester Luwin arrives with Hodor to bring Bran to meet Tyrion Lannister and a party of Night's Watch brothers. Tyrion presents Bran with a saddle design that will allow him to ride despite his paralysis, and the direwolves menace Tyrion before Bran calls them off. At the evening feast, the Night's Watch brothers reveal that Benjen Stark has gone missing and is feared dead. Robb carries Bran to bed himself and the two brothers share a quiet, emotional moment, Robb promising Bran a horse and plans to visit Jon at the Wall.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran watches from window** — Bran observes Rickon running with Grey Wind and Shaggydog in the yard below; Summer is near Bran. Bran weeps at his inability to join them.
2. **Old Nan attempts to tell stories** — Old Nan offers various stories; Bran initially refuses but relents when she offers scary ones.
3. **Old Nan tells the story of the Long Night** — She describes the Others, the dead lands, the last hero's quest to find the children of the forest, and the pale white spiders big as hounds that hunted him.
4. **Maester Luwin arrives with Hodor** — He announces that Tyrion Lannister and Night's Watch brothers have come with word from Jon Snow; Bran's presence is required.
5. **Hodor carries Bran to the great hall** — Old Nan's real name for Hodor is revealed: Walder.
6. **Robb confronts Tyrion** — Robb sits in the lord's high seat with sword bare; the two exchange tense words about courtesy and Robb's authority.
7. **Bran is seated in the high seat of the Starks** — Hodor places Bran in the carved stone seat; Bran grips the direwolf-headed arms.
8. **Tyrion presents the saddle design** — Tyrion gives Maester Luwin a rolled paper with a drawing for a specialized saddle; Maester Luwin approves it.
9. **Tyrion explains his motivation** — He states Jon asked him to help Bran, and that he has "a tender spot in my heart for cripples and bastards and broken things."
10. **Rickon and direwolves enter the hall** — Rickon bursts in; all three direwolves (Summer, Grey Wind, Shaggydog) converge on Tyrion and menace him.
11. **Bran calls off the wolves** — Bran commands Summer; Robb calls Grey Wind; Bran directs Rickon to call Shaggydog. Tyrion is unharmed but shaken; Grey Wind tears a scrap from his sleeve.
12. **Tyrion declines hospitality** — Robb (advised by Maester Luwin) offers Tyrion hospitality; Tyrion declines and says he'll stay at the inn in the winter town. He instructs Yoren to meet him on the road at daybreak.
13. **Robb hosts the Night's Watch brothers at feast** — Suckling pig, pigeon pie, turnips in butter, and honeycomb served; direwolves present; Winterfell's dogs absent from the hall.
14. **Yoren delivers news of Benjen Stark** — Reports that Benjen was sent out to look for Waymar Royce and is overdue; likely dead.
15. **Robb reacts with anger** — Stands, hand on sword hilt, insists his uncle is not dead.
16. **Bran mentions the children of the forest** — Suggests the children might help Benjen; Maester Luwin says they have been dead for thousands of years; Yoren hedges, saying past the Wall you can't always tell what's alive and what's dead.
17. **Robb carries Bran to bed** — Grey Wind leads the way, Summer follows. Robb is breathing hard by the top of the stairs.
18. **Robb and Bran's private moment** — Robb promises Bran a horse; speaks of Catelyn coming home, riding out to meet her, and later riding north to surprise Jon at the Wall. Bran hears Robb sob and reaches for his hand in the dark.
19. **Bran's nightmare** — Bran dreams of climbing an ancient windowless tower; gargoyles with red glowing eyes pull free from the stone and close in on him; he wakes in terror. Hodor soothes him, washes him, and dresses him for the feast.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Grief and resentment at his paralysis and isolation; anger that everyone has left; fear when he thinks of Benjen and the Others; cautious hope about riding; affection toward Summer and Robb
- **Primary preoccupation:** His inability to move and run; feeling abandoned by his family; clinging to the hope that Robb's promises (horse, seeing Jon) will come true
- **Key decisions made:** Calls off Summer during the wolf confrontation with Tyrion; chooses to believe he can ride again
- **Self-deception flags:** Bran insists "I never fell, never never never" — he has no memory of the climb or fall but asserts agency over what happened
