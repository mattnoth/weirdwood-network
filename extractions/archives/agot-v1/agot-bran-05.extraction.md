# AGOT — Bran V

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 38
- **pov_character:** Bran Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Bran V
- **first_available:** AGOT Bran V
- **location_primary:** Wolfswood (near Winterfell)
- **location_secondary:** Winterfell, winter town, the kingsroad, a stream/ford in the wolfswood
- **approximate_timeline:** After the news of Catelyn taking Tyrion prisoner; after Ned's injury in King's Landing
- **chapter_summary:** Bran rides out of Winterfell for the first time since his fall, accompanied by Robb, Theon Greyjoy, Maester Luwin, Joseth, and guardsmen. Robb tells Bran that a raven brought news of Jory Cassel's murder by the Kingslayer and Ned's serious leg injury. After separating from the group in the wolfswood, Bran is ambushed by six wildlings and Night's Watch deserters who attempt to rob and kidnap him. Robb returns with the direwolves and a violent fight ensues, killing most of the attackers. Theon kills the last man (Stiv) with an arrow as he holds a knife to Bran's throat. The surviving wildling woman Osha surrenders and is taken prisoner back to Winterfell.

## Events & Actions
1. **Bran rides outside Winterfell for the first time since his fall** — He rides Dancer, his specially trained filly, through the gatehouse and winter town, accompanied by Robb, Theon, Maester Luwin, Joseth, and four guardsmen.
2. **Robb delivers news from King's Landing** — Robb tells Bran that Jory Cassel, Wyl, and Heward were murdered by the Kingslayer, and Ned's leg was shattered when a horse fell on him. Ned is unconscious on milk of the poppy.
3. **Discussion of calling the banners** — Theon urges Robb to call the banners ("Blood for blood"). Bran notes that only the lord can call the banners. Robb is torn between Theon's counsel and Maester Luwin's caution.
4. **Bran and Robb separate from the party** — They ride into the wolfswood to find the direwolves. Robb leaves Bran alone at the stream to track the wolves.
5. **Wildling ambush** — Six ragged strangers (including two Night's Watch deserters) confront Bran at the stream. They demand his silver pin, his horse, and attempt to strip him from the saddle. Stiv cuts Bran's thigh strap, also cutting Bran's leg (Bran cannot feel the wound).
6. **Osha proposes taking Bran as hostage** — She suggests Mance Rayder would value Benjen Stark's blood as a hostage. Stiv dismisses this, referencing "white walkers."
7. **Robb returns and battle ensues** — Robb charges with his sword, killing a man with an axe. Grey Wind kills Wallen in the stream. Robb fights and rides down Osha. Summer attacks and kills Hali. Grey Wind kills the sixth man who tries to flee.
8. **Stiv takes Bran hostage** — Stiv pulls Bran from the saddle, holds a dagger to his throat, and demands Robb kill the wolves.
9. **Theon kills Stiv with an arrow** — A single broadhead arrow through the back/chest kills Stiv.
10. **Osha surrenders** — She throws down her spear and begs for mercy. Robb takes her prisoner at Maester Luwin's suggestion.
11. **Robb rebukes Theon** — Robb angrily criticizes Theon for the risky shot that could have killed Bran, despite it saving his life.
12. **Robb orders the deserters' heads sent to the Wall** — He commands the heads be hacked off and sent to the Night's Watch; the rest left for carrion crows.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Begins with anxious excitement at riding outside Winterfell for the first time since his fall; transitions to joy and exhilaration as he gallops; shifts to grief and dread upon hearing news of Jory's death and Ned's injury; terror during the ambush; relief at rescue
- **Primary preoccupation:** Proving he can ride despite his disability; processing the grim news from King's Landing; fear for his father's life
- **Key decisions made:** Insists on continuing the ride despite cold and discomfort (not wanting to show weakness); identifies himself as Brandon Stark to the attackers (bold but dangerous); tells Robb that Osha mentioned Mance Rayder
- **Self-deception flags:** None observed. Bran notes he tries to suppress his fear but is honest about his emotions internally.
