# AGOT — Catelyn IX

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 60
- **pov_character:** Catelyn Stark
- **pov_chapter_number:** Catelyn IX
- **first_available:** AGOT Catelyn IX
- **location_primary:** The Twins (both castles and the bridge)
- **location_secondary:** The Neck (bogs), the riverlands, the Green Fork, the kingsroad (referenced as landmark)
- **approximate_timeline:** The northern host has crossed south through the Neck and reached the Twins; Edmure's host has already been defeated by the Kingslayer at Riverrun
- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn rides south with Robb's army through the Neck into the riverlands, growing anxious about the war. The Blackfish scouts ahead and reports that Walder Frey has massed four thousand men at the Twins. News arrives that Jaime Lannister has defeated Edmure's host and besieged Riverrun. Catelyn volunteers to negotiate with Lord Walder Frey for passage. Inside the Twins, Lord Walder haggles and reveals that Jon Arryn had planned to foster Robert Arryn with Stannis Baratheon at Dragonstone. Catelyn secures crossing rights and Frey military support in exchange for wardships, a squire, and marriage pacts binding Arya and Robb to Frey children. The army splits: Robb's cavalry crosses west, while Roose Bolton marches the infantry south to confront Lord Tywin.

## Events & Actions
1. **March through the Neck** — The northern host crosses south through the bogs of the Neck along the causeway into the riverlands.
2. **Blackfish scouts ahead** — Ser Brynden takes a hundred men and horses to screen movements and scout.
3. **Skirmish with Lannister scouts** — The Blackfish's riders engage Lannister outriders; a dozen scouts are killed. Ser Addam Marbrand pulls back south, burning as he goes.
4. **Discovery of Frey resistance** — Walder Frey has massed nearly four thousand men at the Twins and closed his gates.
5. **Raven interception ordered** — Catelyn orders bowmen placed around the Twins to shoot down any ravens, but the Blackfish has already done so.
6. **Battle of Riverrun reported** — Brynden brings news from a captured Lannister outrider: the Kingslayer has destroyed Edmure's host, Edmure is wounded and captured, and Riverrun is besieged.
7. **Catelyn volunteers to negotiate** — As the northern lords argue, Catelyn offers to enter the Twins alone to treat with Lord Walder.
8. **Perwyn Frey left as hostage** — Ser Perwyn remains with Robb's host as surety for Catelyn's safe return.
9. **Catelyn negotiates with Lord Walder** — Extended negotiation in the great hall of the eastern castle; Walder complains of slights from Tullys and Arryns.
10. **Walder reveals Jon Arryn's fostering plan** — Lord Walder states that Jon Arryn planned to send Robert Arryn to Dragonstone to foster with Stannis Baratheon, contradicting what Catelyn had understood (fostering at Casterly Rock with Tywin).
11. **Terms agreed** — Catelyn secures passage and Frey military support in exchange for: two Frey grandsons (both named Walder) as wards at Winterfell; Olyvar Frey as Robb's squire; Arya to marry Elmar Frey; Robb to marry a Frey daughter of his choosing.
12. **Army crosses the Twins** — The cavalry crosses at evenfall through both castles and the bridge.
13. **Army splits** — Robb takes the cavalry west toward Riverrun; Roose Bolton commands the infantry continuing south on the east bank to confront Lord Tywin's host.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Deeply anxious and fearful — for her father, brother, husband, daughters, and sons. She masks fear behind a still, stern face. Anger at Walder Frey's recalcitrance is controlled.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Securing the crossing at the Twins so Robb's army can relieve Riverrun; protecting Robb from making rash military or diplomatic errors.
- **Key decisions made:** Volunteers to enter the Twins alone to negotiate; agrees to extensive marriage and wardship terms on behalf of her family without consulting Robb first; counsels Robb to use words over force.
- **Self-deception flags:** Lies to Robb and the lords that Walder Frey "would never offer me any harm," while internally acknowledging he would harm her if he saw profit in it. Tells herself to "be a Stark for true now" while relying on her Tully identity and connections to negotiate.
