- **chapter_summary:** Catelyn arrives at Moat Cailin with the Manderly levies and Ser Brynden Tully, joining Robb's assembled northern host. She reunites with Robb in the Gatehouse Tower, where he is meeting with his bannermen. After dismissing the lords, Catelyn and Robb discuss Sansa's letter (which Catelyn identifies as dictated by Cersei), the lack of news about Arya, Lysa's refusal to aid them, and the military situation in the riverlands. Robb presents his battle plan to split his host at the Twins, sending foot down the kingsroad and horse across the Green Fork to relieve Riverrun. Catelyn counsels him to give the eastern infantry command to Roose Bolton rather than the Greatjon. Catelyn announces she will ride to Riverrun rather than return to Winterfell.

## Events & Actions
1. **Catelyn arrives at Moat Cailin** — She rides in with Ser Brynden Tully, Ser Wylis, Ser Wendel, and approximately 1,500 Manderly troops.
2. **Manderly forces make camp** — Ser Wylis halts his men on high ground to set fires and tend horses; Ser Wendel rides ahead with Catelyn and Brynden.
3. **Catelyn reunites with Robb** — She enters the Gatehouse Tower where Robb is meeting with Roose Bolton and the Greatjon. Grey Wind notices her first. Robb greets her emotionally.
4. **Bannermen pay respects** — Ser Helman Tallhart, the Glovers, Greatjon Umber, Lord Hornwood, Theon Greyjoy, and others greet Catelyn formally.
5. **Catelyn introduces Brynden and Wendel** — Robb welcomes the Blackfish and Ser Wendel to the host.
6. **Bolton asks about Tyrion** — Roose Bolton inquires whether Catelyn brought Tyrion Lannister as hostage; she admits he was freed, blaming her sister.
7. **Catelyn dismisses the lords** — She requests to speak with Robb alone; all depart, with Theon lingering until pointedly told to leave.
8. **Catelyn reads Sansa's letter** — She identifies it as dictated by Cersei and notes Arya is not mentioned at all.
9. **Discussion of Lysa's refusal** — Robb learns the Vale will not join them; only Brynden came.
10. **Robb reports on the military situation** — He relays the defeat at the Golden Tooth, the ambush at Mummer's Ford, and Tywin's march toward Harrenhal.
11. **Catelyn counsels Robb on leadership** — She tells him he cannot be sent home now but warns him he must command decisively and not appear indecisive before his lords.
12. **Robb presents his battle plan** — He proposes splitting the host at the Twins: foot continues down the kingsroad as a decoy, horse crosses the Green Fork to relieve Riverrun.
13. **Catelyn advises on command assignments** — She steers Robb away from giving the Greatjon the infantry command and toward choosing Roose Bolton for his "cold cunning."
14. **Catelyn decides to go to Riverrun** — Rather than return to Winterfell, she announces she will ride to Riverrun where her father may be dying and her brother is surrounded.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** A complex mix of maternal fear, pride, grief, and resolve. Desperately afraid for Robb but also proud of the man he is becoming. Near tears at the end when deciding to go to Riverrun.
- **Primary preoccupation:** Assessing whether Robb is ready to lead; ensuring the campaign has sound strategy; worry for Ned, Sansa, Arya, her father, and Edmure.
- **Key decisions made:** (1) Not to send Robb home despite his youth, recognizing it would destroy his standing with his lords. (2) To guide Robb toward choosing Roose Bolton for the infantry command. (3) To ride to Riverrun instead of returning to Winterfell.
- **Self-deception flags:** Catelyn notes she had "put despair and fear aside, as if they were garments she did not choose to wear … but now she saw that she had donned them after all." She recognizes her own suppression of emotion throughout the chapter.
