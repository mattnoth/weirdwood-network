# AGOT — Jon VII

## Chapter Metadata
- **book:** AGOT
- **chapter_number:** 53
- **pov_character:** Jon Snow
- **pov_chapter_number:** Jon VII
- **first_available:** AGOT Jon VII
- **location_primary:** The haunted forest near Castle Black; Castle Black
- **location_secondary:** Lord Commander's Tower, common hall, storeroom along the base of the Wall
- **approximate_timeline:** Follows an extended period since Benjen Stark's departure (close to half a year); occurs during "spirit summer" as warm weather breaks; King Robert has recently died
- **chapter_summary:** Rangers discover the corpses of Othor and Jafer Flowers, two of Benjen Stark's men, in the woods near Castle Black. The bodies show anomalies: no blood on the ground, no rot, no animal scavenging, and blue eyes neither man had in life. Sam identifies the forensic inconsistencies. The bodies are brought back to the Wall. Jon learns that King Robert is dead and Lord Eddard has been imprisoned for treason. Jon attacks Ser Alliser Thorne after Thorne calls him "a traitor's bastard" and is confined to his cell. That night, the corpse of Othor reanimates as a wight and kills Jon's guard; Jon fights it in Mormont's solar and sets it on fire.

## Events & Actions
1. **Discovery of Othor and Jafer Flowers' corpses** — Ghost leads the ranging party to the bodies of two of Benjen Stark's men in the woods near Castle Black. Ser Jaremy identifies them.
2. **Forensic examination in the field** — Sam identifies that the bodies have not bled despite massive wounds, show no rot or maggot activity, and animals refuse to approach. Dywen notes both corpses have blue eyes they did not have in life.
3. **Debate over cause of death** — Ser Jaremy argues wildlings killed the men. Mormont is skeptical, noting Benjen would have fought or returned for help. The anomalies go unexplained.
4. **Mormont orders a search** — Commands Ser Jaremy to search every foot of ground within ten leagues, using all available men, to find Ben Stark and the other four missing rangers.
5. **Return to Castle Black with corpses** — Horses refuse to carry the dead; crude slings are fashioned. Bodies are stored in an ice cell along the Wall's base.
6. **Mormont commends Sam and Jon** — Tells Sam "You're fat but you're not stupid" and acknowledges Jon as well.
7. **News of King Robert's death** — A raven brings word. Pyp tells Jon, having heard it from a guard who overheard Clydas reading to Maester Aemon.
8. **Mormont informs Jon of Eddard's arrest** — Lord Eddard is charged with treason for allegedly plotting with Robert's brothers to deny the throne to Joffrey. Mormont says he will write to councillors seeking mercy.
9. **Jon attacks Ser Alliser Thorne** — After Thorne publicly calls Jon "a traitor's bastard," Jon leaps onto the table with a dagger and slashes at Thorne. His friends restrain him.
10. **Jon is confined to his cell** — Weapons confiscated, guard posted, forbidden visitors. Ghost is allowed to stay.
11. **The wight attack** — In the night, the reanimated corpse of Othor kills Jon's guard (twisting his head around) and enters Mormont's tower heading for the Lord Commander's chambers.
12. **Jon fights the wight** — Jon takes the dead guard's sword, follows Ghost upstairs, and fights the animated corpse of Othor in Mormont's solar. Severing its arm does not stop it; the arm continues to move independently.
13. **Jon burns the wight** — After Mormont appears with an oil lamp, prompted by the raven screaming "Burn!", Jon sets the curtains on fire and uses them to ignite the wight. He burns his hand in the process.

## POV Character's Internal State
- **Emotional state:** Progresses from unease (at the corpses) to shock (learning of Eddard's arrest) to rage (attacking Thorne) to despair (confined to his cell) to terror and desperate courage (fighting the wight)
- **Primary preoccupation:** His father's imprisonment and the safety of his family, especially Arya and Sansa
- **Key decisions made:** Defends Sam's right to speak before the officers; attacks Ser Alliser Thorne; fights the wight and chooses to use fire
- **Self-deception flags:** Jon briefly wonders if his father could have been dishonorable, thinking of his own bastardy — "He fathered a bastard, a small voice whispered inside him. Where was the honor in that?" He immediately suppresses this doubt.
