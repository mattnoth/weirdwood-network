## Identity

Ser Quentyn Ball, known as Fireball, was a knight from House Ball during the reigns of kings Aegon IV and Daeron II Targaryen. He served as master-at-arms at the Red Keep and became one of the key supporters of Daemon Blackfyre during the First Blackfyre Rebellion. His personal coat-of-arms was a fireball blazing red and yellow across a night black field. (wiki:Quentyn_Ball.cite_ref-Rtmk-2)

## Appearances & Description

Red-haired with a fiery temper, hence the moniker Fireball. (wiki:Quentyn_Ball.cite_ref-Rtmk-2) Considered one of the greatest knights of his day. (wiki:Quentyn_Ball.cite_ref-Rtss-3)

## Narrative Arc

As master-at-arms of the Red Keep under King Aegon IV, Quentyn trained both the sons of Prince Daeron (Baelor, Aerys, Rhaegel, and Maekar) and the Great Bastards (Daemon Waters, Aegor Rivers, and Brynden Rivers). (wiki:Quentyn_Ball.cite_ref-Rtmk-2) Aegon IV promised Quentyn a place in the Kingsguard; to facilitate this, Quentyn had his wife join the silent sisters. Aegon died before an opening became available. (wiki:Quentyn_Ball.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf-4)

When a Kingsguard vacancy arose under Daeron II, Quentyn was refused the white cloak, which went to Ser Willem Wylde instead. This betrayal caused him to support Daemon Blackfyre's rebellion. He was rumored to have had as much a part as Bittersteel in convincing Daemon to claim the Iron Throne. (wiki:Quentyn_Ball.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf-4)

During the First Blackfyre Rebellion, Fireball rescued Daemon from arrest, killed Lord Lefford at Lannisport, repulsed Lord Damon Lannister, and slew all the sons of Lady Penrose at the crossing of the Mander save the youngest. He was slain by a common archer on the eve of the Battle of the Redgrass Field when he stopped for a drink of water, in 196 AC. (wiki:Quentyn_Ball.cite_ref-Rtss-3)(wiki:Quentyn_Ball.cite_ref-Rtmk-2)
