## Identity

The miller's wife is an unnamed woman from the North, mother of Ramsay Snow. Not to be confused with the Miller's wife from Acorn Water. (wiki:Miller's_wife.cite_ref-Radwd32)

## Origins

Born in the North. Married an old miller who worked a mill by the Weeping Water. (track_b: Born; wiki:Miller's_wife.cite_ref-Radwd32)

## Appearances & Description

Tall and willowy, with long legs and small firm breasts. According to Roose Bolton, she was pretty "in a common sort of way" and very healthy-looking. (wiki:Miller's_wife.cite_ref-Radwd32)

## Narrative Arc

The miller's wife married an old miller by the Weeping Water without Lord Roose Bolton's knowledge or permission. While hunting a fox, Roose came upon the mill, saw her washing clothes, and desired her. Invoking the outlawed first night custom, Roose hanged the miller and raped his wife beneath the tree. (wiki:Miller's_wife.cite_ref-Radwd32)

A year later, she came to the Dreadfort with her infant son Ramsay, claiming he was Roose's bastard. Roose considered killing the boy until he saw the child had the same pale eyes as himself. He gave her possession of the mill, sent annual supplies, and cut out the tongue of the miller's brother to prevent Lord Rickard Stark from learning what had happened. (wiki:Miller's_wife.cite_ref-Radwd32)

Despite Roose's orders never to tell Ramsay who his father was, she disobeyed and told Ramsay about his rights. She later returned to the Dreadfort demanding a servant for her wild, unruly son. Roose gave her his foul-smelling servant Reek as a joke, but Ramsay and Reek became inseparable. (wiki:Miller's_wife.cite_ref-Radwd32)
