## Identity

The Battle of the Bells was a battle fought during Robert's Rebellion at Stoney Sept in the southwestern Riverlands in 283 AC. Named for the town's sept tolling its bells to warn citizens at the battle's start (wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells).

## Allegiances

Part of `FIGHTS_IN` Robert's Rebellion. Rebel forces (armies of the North, Riverlands, and Vale) fought against the royalist army commanded by Jon Connington (track_b: Conflict).

## Narrative Arc

After defeat at the Battle of Ashford, Robert Baratheon fled north to Stoney Sept, where his wounds were tended by sympathetic townspeople. Hand of the King Jon Connington occupied the town with a royalist force and searched for Robert, who remained hidden — his last hiding place the brothel called the Peach. The rebel armies of Eddard Stark and Hoster Tully reached Stoney Sept before Robert was found. The town's septons rang bronze and silver bells as warning. The rebels stormed the walls; fighting raged in streets, alleys, and on rooftops. Jon Connington wounded Hoster Tully and killed Ser Denys Arryn with an axe. Robert emerged from the Peach and slew six men including Ser Myles Mooton. Jon retreated when he saw the battle was lost. Robert later said Eddard won the battle for him (wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells, cite: ASOS Ch. 29, ADWD Ch. 24, Ch. 61).

The battle forced Aerys II to take the rebellion seriously. Jon Connington was exiled, his lands seized. Prince Rhaegar returned from the Tower of Joy. The rebel leaders traveled to Riverrun for the weddings of Catelyn and Lysa Tully (wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells).
