## Identity

Lyanna Stark was the only daughter of Lord Rickard Stark and Lady Lyarra Stark (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). She had two older brothers, Brandon and Eddard, and one younger brother, Benjen (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). Her abduction by Prince Rhaegar Targaryen ultimately triggered Robert's Rebellion and the downfall of the Targaryen dynasty (wiki:Lyanna_Stark).

## Origins

Born 266–267 AC at Winterfell, the North (track_b: Born). Died 283 AC, aged 16, at the tower of joy, Dorne (track_b: Died). Buried in the Crypt of Winterfell (track_b: Buried). Father: Rickard Stark (track_b: Father). Mother: Lyarra Stark (track_b: Mother).

## Appearances & Description

Slim of frame (wiki:Lyanna_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12). Arya Stark — brown-haired, long-faced, grey-eyed — resembles Lyanna in appearance according to Eddard (wiki:Lyanna_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot35). Described by Maester Yandel as "a wild and boyish young thing," while Ser Kevan Lannister recalls a "wild beauty" about her (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). Eddard calls her "a child-woman of surpassing loveliness" (wiki:Lyanna_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot30). She had "the wolf blood" according to her father Rickard; Eddard compares her to Arya in personality (wiki:Lyanna_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot35). She was a noted and skilled rider — described as riding "like a northman" by Harwin, a "centaur" by Lady Barbrey Dustin, and "half a horse herself" by Roose Bolton (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). She loved the scent of winter roses (wiki:Lyanna_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66).

## Narrative Arc

At the tourney at Harrenhal, Lyanna defended Howland Reed from bullying squires with a tourney sword, roaring "That's my father's man you're kicking" (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). Rhaegar Targaryen performed a sad song that made her weep; when Benjen teased her, she poured wine on his head (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). A mystery knight — the Knight of the Laughing Tree — appeared at the tourney and defeated the bullies' knights. Prince Rhaegar won the tournament but shocked the assembly by crowning Lyanna, not his wife Elia Martell, as queen of love and beauty with a laurel of blue winter roses (wiki:Lyanna_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66).

The following year, Rhaegar took Lyanna with the help of Ser Arthur Dayne and Ser Oswell Whent (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). Brandon Stark rode to King's Landing demanding Rhaegar "come out and die" and was arrested. King Aerys summoned the captives' fathers; Rickard demanded trial by combat but Aerys chose fire as champion. Rickard burned in his armor while Brandon strangled himself trying to reach a sword. Jon Arryn raised his banners in revolt, starting the war (wiki:Lyanna_Stark).

Eddard found Lyanna dying in the tower of joy in a "bed of blood," guarded by three Kingsguard: Ser Arthur Dayne, Ser Oswell Whent, and Lord Commander Gerold Hightower. After a fight in which all three Kingsguard died, Eddard reached Lyanna. She made him promise something — "Promise me, Ned" — and died with rose petals in her hand as Eddard held her (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). Her body was brought home and interred in the crypts of Winterfell with a stone statue — unusual for a woman, as typically only Lords of Winterfell receive statues (wiki:Lyanna_Stark).

Robert Baratheon remains obsessed with Lyanna's memory fifteen years after her death, straining his marriage to Cersei (wiki:Lyanna_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot30). Lyanna had been less enthusiastic about the betrothal, telling Eddard that Robert's love would not prevent his unfaithfulness (wiki:Lyanna_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot45).
