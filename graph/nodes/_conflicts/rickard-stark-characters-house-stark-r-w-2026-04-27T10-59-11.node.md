## Identity

Rickard Stark was Lord of Winterfell and Warden of the North (track_b: Titles), head of House Stark during the lead-up to Robert's Rebellion. He was the son of Edwyle Stark and Marna Locke (track_b: Father, Mother). Born between 230 and 250 AC at Winterfell in the North (track_b: Born).

## Appearances & Description

His tomb statue in the Winterfell crypts shows a long, stern face with a stone sword across his lap (agot-eddard-01). Described as having been forced to watch his son Brandon die before being killed himself by the Mad King Aerys (agot-eddard-01).

## Narrative Arc

Rickard Stark was a politically active lord who arranged a betrothal between his eldest son Brandon and Catelyn Tully of Riverrun, and promised his daughter Lyanna's hand to Robert Baratheon (agot-eddard-01, agot-eddard-09). He coined the term "the wolf blood" to describe the wild Stark temperament (agot-arya-02). When Prince Rhaegar Targaryen took Lyanna, Brandon rode to King's Landing to demand her return. Rickard followed, answering King Aerys's summons. Aerys had Rickard burned alive in his armor while forcing Brandon to watch (agot-eddard-01, agot-bran-07). Their deaths, along with Aerys's demand for Ned's and Robert's heads, triggered Robert's Rebellion (agot-eddard-02). Rickard was buried in the Crypt of Winterfell alongside Brandon and Lyanna, with statues that Ned commissioned despite the tradition being reserved for kings and lords (agot-bran-07). Ned's children heard him referred to with the warning that he "went south once, to answer the summons of a king. He never came home again" (agot-catelyn-02).
