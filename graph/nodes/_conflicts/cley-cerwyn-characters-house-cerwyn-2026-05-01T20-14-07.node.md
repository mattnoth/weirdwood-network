## Identity

Cley Cerwyn is a member of House Cerwyn, son of Lord Medger Cerwyn and heir to Castle Cerwyn. He briefly held the title Lord of Cerwyn in 299 AC before his death. (wiki:Cley_Cerwyn)

## Origins

Born in 284 or 285 AC at Castle Cerwyn. (wiki:Cley_Cerwyn.cite_ref-1) Son of Lord Medger Cerwyn. (track_b: Father)

## Allegiances

`SWORN_TO` House Cerwyn. (track_b: Allegiance) `CULTURE_OF` Northmen. (track_b: Culture) Cley was a supporter of Robb Stark in the War of the Five Kings. (wiki:Cley_Cerwyn)

## Appearances & Description

Cley has always been friendly with Bran Stark and his brothers. (wiki:Cley_Cerwyn.cite_ref-Racok16)

## Narrative Arc

While his father Lord Medger was held captive at Harrenhal, Cley attended the harvest feast at Winterfell in his place, bringing two dozen lances and news of Stannis Baratheon's letter concerning the illegitimacy of Cersei Lannister's children. (wiki:Cley_Cerwyn.cite_ref-Racok16) He danced with Beth Cassel at the feast. (wiki:Cley_Cerwyn.cite_ref-Racok21) Cley became Lord of Cerwyn when his father died of wounds at Harrenhal. (wiki:Cley_Cerwyn.cite_ref-Racok30)

When the ironmen attacked Torrhen's Square, Lord Cley brought three hundred men as part of the relief force. (wiki:Cley_Cerwyn.cite_ref-Racok46) He then joined Ser Rodrik Cassel's march to relieve Winterfell. Ramsay Snow led a Bolton force in betraying Rodrik's army; Cley was killed by an arrow through the eye. (wiki:Cley_Cerwyn.cite_ref-Racok66) He was succeeded as head of House Cerwyn by his older sister Jonelle. (wiki:Cley_Cerwyn.cite_ref-Rasosappendix)
