## Identity

Ser Criston Cole, known as the Kingmaker, was a knight from House Cole who served as Lord Commander of the Kingsguard under King Viserys I Targaryen and as Hand of the King for Aegon II Targaryen during the Dance of the Dragons. (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Ragot13)

## Origins

Criston was born in 82 AC at Blackhaven. He was the son of Lord Dondarrion's steward and of Marcher culture. (track_b: Born) (Tier 2 — semi-canon source describes him as common-born.) (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rheirs4)

## Allegiances

Criston was `SWORN_TO` House Cole by birth. (track_b: Allegiances) He served as sworn shield to Princess Rhaenyra Targaryen before becoming her bitterest foe. (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rheirs4) He became a staunch supporter of Queen Alicent Hightower and the `SWORN_TO` Greens faction. (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rheirs4) He was a member of the `SWORN_TO` Kingsguard from 105 AC until his death. (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rheirs4)

## Appearances & Description

Criston had coal black hair and pale green eyes. Many ladies described him as charming. He was an exceptionally skilled warrior, particularly with his morningstar, and also wielded a longsword. He rode a white horse. His arms bore ten black pellets on a scarlet field. (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Ragot16) (affc-jaime-02)

## Narrative Arc

In 104 AC, Criston won a melee at the tourney at Maidenpool, drawing attention by knocking Dark Sister from Prince Daemon Targaryen's hands with his morningstar. He gave the victor's laurel to the seven-year-old Princess Rhaenyra and was named her personal sworn shield. In 105 AC he joined the Kingsguard, replacing Ser Ryam Redwyne. (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rheirs4)

Named Lord Commander after Ser Harrold Westerling's death in 112 AC, Criston's relationship with Rhaenyra soured around 113 AC — conflicting accounts from Septon Eustace and Mushroom describe either his rejected proposal to elope or her rejected attempt at seduction. Criston turned from her staunchest defender to her bitterest foe and became Queen Alicent Hightower's sworn shield. He tutored Prince Aemond Targaryen in swordsmanship. (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rheirs4) (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rblacks8)

When Viserys I died in 129 AC, Criston and Alicent convened the small council to install Prince Aegon. He killed Lord Lyman Beesbury — accounts differ on whether by slitting his throat or defenestration. Criston crowned Aegon II with the Conqueror's crown in the Dragonpit. Named Hand of the King after Otto Hightower, he led the sack of Duskendale and planned the ambush at the Battle at Rook's Rest, where Princess Rhaenys and Meleys were slain. (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rred5) (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Ragot9)

Criston marched to Harrenhal with Aemond but found it abandoned. Marching south with thirty-six hundred men, his force was harried by rivermen. At the Butcher's Ball in 130 AC, Criston offered to yield to spare his men, was refused, and was struck down by three arrows from Robb Rivers's archers. His head was carried on a spear to the First Battle of Tumbleton. (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rblacks8) (wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rred5)

## Notes

Criston's motives for supporting Aegon II are debated: ambition and a desire to control a more pliable king, personal bitterness from Rhaenyra's spurning, defense of Andal succession customs favoring sons over daughters, or opposition to the Strong bastards inheriting the throne. The wiki presents all four without adjudicating. (Tier 2 — wiki presents competing interpretations without resolution.)

The killing of Lyman Beesbury has two conflicting accounts — throat-slitting (Septon Eustace) vs. defenestration (Mushroom) — with neither source present at the event. (Tier 2 — wiki hedges with "neither Eustace or Mushroom was present.")

The nature of Criston's relationship with Rhaenyra is similarly contested between Eustace and Mushroom, with accounts diverging on who propositioned whom. (Tier 2 — wiki presents both versions without resolution.)

Criston may be a homage by GRRM to Richard Neville, 16th Earl of Warwick (Warwick the Kingmaker).
