## Identity

Ramsay Snow, later legitimized as Ramsay Bolton, is the bastard son of Roose Bolton, Lord of the Dreadfort. He is known as the Bastard of Bolton, the Bastard of the Dreadfort, and Red Helm. He assumes the identity "Reek" to deceive Ser Rodrik Cassel and later forces Theon Greyjoy to take on the Reek identity in turn. Following legitimization by royal decree from King Tommen I Baratheon, he styles himself Ramsay Bolton and holds the titles Lord of the Hornwood, Lord of Winterfell, and Castellan of the Dreadfort. (wiki:Ramsay_Snow)

## Origins

Ramsay is the product of rape. While hunting along the Weeping Water, Roose Bolton saw a miller's wife and practiced the banned tradition of the first night, hanging the miller and raping the bride beneath his swaying body. A year later the woman arrived at the Dreadfort with Ramsay. Roose nearly killed mother and child, but the boy had his eyes, and the taboo of kinslaying stayed his hand. Born in or before 282 AC in the North. (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd32) (track_b: Born) (track_b: Father)

## Appearances & Description

Ramsay is ugly, big-boned and slope-shouldered, with a fleshiness suggesting he will be fat later in life. His skin is pink and blotchy, his nose broad, his hair long, dark, and dry. His mouth is small but his lips are wide, meaty, and wormy-looking. His distinctive eyes resemble Roose's — small, close-set, and oddly pale, like two chips of dirty ice. He sometimes wears a garnet cut in the shape of a drop of blood in his right ear. (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Racok35) (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd12)

For battle he wears dark armor with a red helm and a pale pink cloak. His rounded helm and gorget resemble the face and shoulders of a skinless, bloody man whose mouth is open in a silent scream. His weapons include a falchion, a dagger, and a flaying knife, all with hilts of yellow bone. (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Racok66) (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd32)

His personal steed is named Blood, and he keeps a pack of female hunting hounds called the Bastard's girls. (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd20) (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd32)

## Narrative Arc

Ramsay was raised by his mother at the mill with a servant called Reek sent by Roose as a cruel jest. The two became inseparable, and Ramsay eventually learned his true parentage. In 297 AC, Roose's trueborn heir Domeric Bolton died of a suspicious illness; Roose believes Ramsay poisoned him. (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd32)

During the War of the Five Kings, Ramsay abducts and forcibly marries Lady Donella Hornwood, who later starves to death in captivity after eating her own fingers. Ramsay escapes capture by switching clothes with Reek, who is killed in his place, while Ramsay-as-"Reek" is imprisoned at Winterfell. (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Racok35) (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Racok46)

After Theon Greyjoy captures Winterfell, "Reek" is released into Theon's service. He orchestrates the murder of two miller's sons whose flayed corpses are presented as Bran and Rickon Stark. When Ser Rodrik Cassel's host arrives to retake Winterfell, Ramsay brings Dreadfort forces, betrays and kills Rodrik, then sacks Winterfell and captures Theon. (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Racok56) (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Racok66)

Ramsay tortures Theon extensively at the Dreadfort — flaying fingers and toes, breaking teeth, and forcing him to adopt the "Reek" identity. He is legitimized as Ramsay Bolton and betrothed to "Arya Stark" (actually Jeyne Poole). He resolves the siege of Moat Cailin by sending Theon as envoy, then flays the surrendered ironborn alive. (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd12) (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd19)

Ramsay weds "Arya" in Winterfell's godswood, establishing House Bolton of Winterfell. He abuses Jeyne and forces Theon to participate. After Jeyne and Theon escape with the aid of Mance Rayder's spearwives, Ramsay sends a letter to Jon Snow at Castle Black claiming victory over Stannis and demanding the return of his bride and his Reek. (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd41) (wiki:Ramsay_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd69)
