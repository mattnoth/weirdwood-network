## Identity

Nan, generally referred to as Old Nan, is a servant woman at Winterfell and the oldest person in the castle (wiki:Old_Nan). She came to Winterfell to nurse a Brandon Stark but does not recall which one (wiki:Old_Nan).

## Origins

Born 185–211 AC (track_b: Born). She lost both sons during Robert's Rebellion, her grandson at the siege of Pyke during Greyjoy's Rebellion, and her daughters moved away and died (wiki:Old_Nan). Her only living relative is her great-grandson Walder, called "Hodor" (wiki:Old_Nan).

`SWORN_TO` House Stark (track_b: Allegiance). `CULTURE_OF` Northmen (track_b: Culture).

## Appearances & Description

An ugly old woman — toothless, shrunken, and wrinkled. Almost blind, she is too weak to climb stairs. She has only a small amount of hair left (wiki:Old_Nan). She is fond of needlework and telling stories (wiki:Old_Nan). She occasionally gives her great-grandson Hodor a swat in the head when annoyed (wiki:Old_Nan).

## Narrative Arc

Old Nan has served the Starks for decades, telling stories to Eddard's children and his ward Theon Greyjoy (wiki:Old_Nan). She sometimes confuses Bran with other Brandon Starks she has known (wiki:Old_Nan). When Bran would not stop climbing, Nan told him a story about a boy struck by lightning (wiki:Old_Nan). After Bran's fall, she cares for him and tells him stories — about the Long Night, the Others, the last hero, and the Night's King — which later prove eerily resonant (wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot24). She tells Bran that the Wall marks the end of the world (wiki:Old_Nan). During the harvest feast at Winterfell, Old Nan remains in the castle (wiki:Old_Nan). After Winterfell falls to the ironborn, Nan is among those taken by Ramsay Snow's men. Her ultimate fate is unknown; Wyman Manderly later asks after her (wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Radwd29). Theon recalls her among the people of Winterfell (wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Radwd51).
