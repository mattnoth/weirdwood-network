## Identity

The Battle of the Trident was the decisive battle of Robert's Rebellion, fought in 283 AC at the Trident, across what would thereafter be known as the ruby ford. It ended in a decisive rebel victory and the death of Prince Rhaegar Targaryen (wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident).

## Allegiances

Part of `FIGHTS_IN` Robert's Rebellion. Rebels (Robert Baratheon, Eddard Stark, Jon Arryn, Hoster Tully, with forces from the North, Vale, Riverlands, and Stormlands — Houses Stark, Bolton, Karstark, Manderly, Mormont, Umber, Arryn, Corbray, Tully, Mallister, Baratheon, Grandison) fought against Loyalists (Rhaegar Targaryen, Lewyn Martell, Barristan Selmy, Jonothor Darry, with ~40,000 men from the Crownlands, Dorne, Reach — Houses Targaryen, Martell, Darry, Mooton, Ryger, Boggs, Brune, Crabb, Goodbrook) (track_b: Conflict).

## Narrative Arc

After Jon Connington's defeat at the Battle of the Bells, Aerys II finally recognized the rebellion as a mortal threat. Rhaegar returned from Dorne to command the royalist army. Aerys dispatched Prince Lewyn Martell with ten thousand Dornishmen. The royalists numbered nearly forty thousand; the rebels were fewer but more battle-hardened. At the chief crossing of the Trident, Dornishmen under Lewyn threatened Robert's left flank; when Lord Corbray fell wounded, his son Lyn Corbray took up the Valyrian steel sword Lady Forlorn and led a charge that broke the Dornish, killing the already-wounded Lewyn. Ser Barristan Selmy killed a dozen friends of Robert and Ned. Lord Jason Mallister slew three of Rhaegar's bannermen. Robert and Rhaegar met in single combat in the shallows of the ford. Robert crushed Rhaegar's chest with his warhammer, scattering the rubies from his armor into the river — giving the ruby ford its name. The royal army broke and fled. Barristan, grievously wounded, was spared by Robert over Roose Bolton's counsel to execute him (wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident, cite: AGOT Ch. 4, 15, 30, 33, 39; ASOS Ch. 67; ADWD Ch. 61).

The battle opened the way to King's Landing. Aerys sent Queen Rhaella and Prince Viserys to Dragonstone. Ned Stark led the vanguard south, but Tywin Lannister arrived first, resulting in the Sack of King's Landing (wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident).
