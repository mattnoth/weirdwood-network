## Identity

Ser Jacelyn Bywater, called Ironhand, is a knight of House Bywater and an officer of the City Watch of King's Landing. He serves as captain of the Mud Gate before being elevated to Commander of the City Watch by Tyrion Lannister in 299 AC. (wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater.cite_ref-Racok8)

## Origins

Born in the Crownlands. (wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater.cite_ref-Rawoiafjacelyn_bywater)

## Appearances & Description

Tall, lantern-jawed, with deep-set eyes, a prominent brow, and salt-and-pepper hair. He has an iron hand strapped to his right wrist where he lost his hand during the Siege of Pyke, hence his nickname Ironhand. (wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater.cite_ref-Racok8) Varys describes him as courageous, honorable, obedient, and grateful. Janos Slynt considers him rigid and overly proud of his honor. (wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater.cite_ref-Racok8)

## Narrative Arc

Jacelyn received his knighthood from King Robert I Baratheon for valor at the Siege of Pyke during Greyjoy's Rebellion, where he lost his right hand. (wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater.cite_ref-Racok8) He served as captain of the Mud Gate for three years before Tyrion Lannister arrived as Hand of the King. Tyrion dismissed Janos Slynt and appointed Jacelyn as Commander of the City Watch. (wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater.cite_ref-Racok8) Jacelyn carried out Tyrion's orders faithfully: ensuring Allar Deem was thrown overboard, receiving Cleos Frey's peace terms from Robb Stark (wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater.cite_ref-Racok20), and defying Cersei's attempt to free Grand Maester Pycelle (wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater.cite_ref-Racok29). He led mounted lancers during the Riot of King's Landing and reported the spread of bloody flux and growing unrest to Tyrion. (wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater.cite_ref-Racok41) On Tyrion's orders, Jacelyn seized Prince Tommen Baratheon on the Rosby road, earning Cersei's enmity and a lordship from Tyrion. (wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater.cite_ref-Racok49) During the Battle of the Blackwater, when King Joffrey retreated, half the gold cloaks broke. Jacelyn tried to rally them but was struck in the throat by an arrow; the fleeing gold cloaks dragged him from his horse and killed him. (wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater.cite_ref-Rasos4) Jaime Lannister later considers wearing a golden hand similar to Jacelyn's iron hand. (wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater.cite_ref-Rasos67)
