## Identity

Ardrian Celtigar, known as the Red Crab, is the Lord of Claw Isle and head of House Celtigar. (wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Racokappendix) Born in or after 229 AC at Claw Isle. (wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rawoiafardrian_celtigar)

## Origins

Of Valyrian descent, from the Crownlands. Born at Claw Isle. (track_b: Race, Culture, Born)

## Allegiances

`SWORN_TO` House Celtigar. (track_b: Allegiance)

## Appearances & Description

Ardrian is a sour old man with a wrinkled face. (wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Racokprologue; wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Racok10) He is known for his avarice and wealth. (wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rasos36) He owns a mantle patterned with red crabs picked out in garnets and a square of linen embroidered in red crabs. (wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Racok0; wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Racok10) His ship is the ornate Red Claw. (wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Racok10) His treasures at Claw Isle are said to include a Valyrian steel axe and a horn capable of summoning krakens. (wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rasos36; wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rasos54)

## Narrative Arc

Ardrian supports Stannis Baratheon's claim to the Iron Throne during the War of the Five Kings. He attends Stannis's feast at Dragonstone and has a coughing fit when Melisandre burns the statues of the Seven. (wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Racokprologue; wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Racok10) He participates in Stannis's siege of Storm's End. (wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rasos36)

Captured at the Battle of the Blackwater, Ardrian publicly bends the knee to King Joffrey I Baratheon. (wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rasos25; wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Racok65) He remains in King's Landing, attends the Purple Wedding, and later testifies against Tyrion Lannister at his trial for regicide. (wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rasos66)

Ser Axell Florent proposes sacking Claw Isle in Ardrian's absence, but Davos Seaworth objects and Stannis sides with Davos. (wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rasos36)

## Notes

Ardrian looks down upon the lowborn Davos Seaworth. Stannis is dismissive of Ardrian's opinions. His turning from Stannis to Joffrey after the Blackwater is treated by Axell Florent as treason, though Davos defends him.
