## Identity

Ser Aegor Rivers, known as Bittersteel, was one of the Great Bastards, a legitimized natural son of King Aegon IV Targaryen by his fifth mistress, Lady Barba Bracken. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-1) He was a knight, a major supporter of the Blackfyre pretenders, and the founder of the Golden Company. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-2) In his personal arms he combined the red stallion of House Bracken with black dragon wings from House Blackfyre on a golden field. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-7)

## Origins

Born in 172 AC at King's Landing, Aegor was sent away from court as an infant after Queen Naerys Targaryen recovered from a near-fatal childbirth. He was raised at Stone Hedge in the riverlands by the Brackens. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-1) His father was King Aegon IV Targaryen (track_b: Father) and his mother was Barba Bracken (track_b: Mother).

## Appearances & Description

Being half-Targaryen, Aegor had purple eyes but black hair. He had a close-cropped beard, little more than a shadow on his face. He was tall and muscular but lean and lithe like a panther. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-7) His armor was plain grey-steel and black rings with a horsehead crest helm. Angry all his life, Aegor never smiled. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-7) He was a hard man who had little use for anything beyond war. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-8)

## Narrative Arc

Aegor especially loathed his half-brother Brynden Rivers (Bloodraven), whose mother Melissa Blackwood had replaced Barba as Aegon IV's mistress. His enmity deepened when their half-sister Shiera Seastar chose Brynden over him. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-7) Aegor was betrothed to Calla Blackfyre, daughter of the pretender Daemon Blackfyre. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-6)

In the First Blackfyre Rebellion (196 AC), Aegor commanded the right of Daemon's host at the Battle of the Redgrass Field. When Daemon fell, Aegor picked up the Valyrian steel sword Blackfyre, rallied his troops, and charged the Raven's Teeth. He dueled Bloodraven and took out his eye, but ultimately fled the field. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-9) (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-2) He fled to Tyrosh with Daemon's widow Rohanne, the surviving Blackfyre children, and the sword Blackfyre.

After a year with the Second Sons, Aegor founded the Golden Company to bind the Blackfyre exiles together. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-11) The company's war cry, "Beneath the gold, the bitter steel," refers to his nickname. He supported the Third and Fourth Blackfyre Rebellions but refused to back the Second, possibly distrusting Daemon II Blackfyre's plans. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-8)

Aegor died in 241 AC in the Disputed Lands. Per his wishes, his skull was dipped in gold and carried before the Golden Company into battle. (wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-5)
