## Identity

Roose Bolton is the Lord of the Dreadfort and head of House Bolton. He becomes Warden of the North after the Red Wedding. Known as "Lord Leech" or "The Leech Lord" for his practice of regular leeching. (wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos; track_b: Titles, Aliases)

## Appearances & Description

Unremarkable body — neither plump, thin, nor muscular. Pasty skin, pallid and hairless chest. Short, strong fingers. Plain, beardless face with strange pale eyes — paler than stone, darker than milk, sometimes called "ghost grey." His eyes are paler than his son Ramsay's. (wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok64; wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32; agot-catelyn-08)

Mild-mannered but remorseless and implacable. Cold and cunning. His voice is small and soft; he rarely raises it, forcing listeners to fall silent. (wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos; agot-catelyn-08)

Convinced that regular leeching improves health. Often wears black ringmail and a red-spotted pale pink cloak trimmed with white fur. Also owns dark grey plate armor with rondels shaped like agonized human heads. His personal motto is "A peaceful land, a quiet people." Sips his alcohol, preferring hippocras. (wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32; wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok64)

## Narrative Arc

Roose practices the outlawed first night tradition but discretely to avoid the attention of House Stark. While hunting, he raped a miller's wife after hanging her husband, fathering Ramsay Snow. (wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32)

After the death of his first wife, Roose married Bethany Ryswell. Their only son to survive infancy was Domeric. When Domeric died in 297 AC — suspected poisoned by Ramsay — Roose lacked trueborn heirs. (wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32)

At Robert's Rebellion, Roose counselled killing the wounded Ser Barristan Selmy after the Battle of the Trident; Robert refused. (wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot; agot-eddard-08)

In the War of the Five Kings, Roose serves as one of Robb Stark's commanders. Robb gives him command of the diversionary foot force — Catelyn steers Robb toward Bolton's "cold cunning" rather than the Greatjon's courage. (agot-catelyn-08) Defeated at the battle on the Green Fork by Tywin Lannister, Roose retreats in good order. (wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot)

Roose captures Harrenhal through a deal with Vargo Hoat and the Brave Companions. He takes Arya Stark (disguised as "Nan") as his cupbearer. When Jaime Lannister is brought to Harrenhal with his hand cut off, Roose secures Jaime's assurance that he will not be blamed for the maiming. Roose releases Jaime with the message "give my regards to Lord Tywin." (wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos)

Roose secretly collaborates with the Freys to betray Robb Stark. At Edmure Tully's wedding at the Twins, Roose personally kills Robb, saying "Jaime Lannister sends his regards." For this betrayal, Tywin Lannister grants Roose the title Warden of the North and legitimizes Ramsay. (wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos)

As Warden, Roose marches north and gathers reluctant allies — the Dustins, Ryswells, Umbers (Whoresbane faction), Cerwyns, Lockes, and Manderlys. Arnolf Karstark is secretly in league with Roose. After Ramsay takes Moat Cailin, Roose moves the wedding of Ramsay to "Arya Stark" (Jeyne Poole) from Barrowton to Winterfell to bait Stannis. (wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd)

At Winterfell, Roose uses squatters as labor to rebuild the castle, then has them hanged. He is cautious at the wedding feast, eating only food Lord Wyman Manderly eats first. As murders mount inside the castle, tensions rise between Freys and Manderlys. (wiki:Roose_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd)
