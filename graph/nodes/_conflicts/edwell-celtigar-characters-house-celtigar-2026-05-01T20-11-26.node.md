## Identity

Edwell Celtigar was the Lord of Claw Isle and head of House Celtigar during the reigns of kings Maegor I and Jaehaerys I Targaryen. (wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings_maegor_i; wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king)

## Narrative Arc

Edwell was named Hand of the King by Maegor I in 44 AC, following the execution of the previous Hand, Lord Lucas Harroway. (wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings_maegor_i) King Maegor left Edwell to rule King's Landing alongside Queen Tyanna of the Tower when Maegor departed to campaign against the Faith Militant uprising in 45 AC. (wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon)

In 47 AC Edwell suggested Maegor take more wives to produce an heir, offering his own maiden daughters Prunella and Prudence. The king took the advice but did not choose Edwell's daughters. Edwell resigned as Hand in 48 AC as lords turned against the king, and safely returned to Claw Isle. (wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon)

Upon Jaehaerys I's ascension, Edwell was pardoned and named lord treasurer and master of coin by Lord Rogar Baratheon. (wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides) Edwell's tax policies were deeply unpopular: he tripled port fees, taxed goods both coming and going, and levied new charges on innkeepers and builders. The Lannisters and Hightowers ignored his demands. (wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers) King Jaehaerys kindly dismissed Edwell upon his sixteenth name day and undid most of the taxes. (wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing)

Edwell died during the winter of 59 AC from the Shivers at Claw Isle. (wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign)

His descendant Lord Bartimos Celtigar later reinstated the same unpopular taxes during the Dance of the Dragons in 130 AC. (wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant)
