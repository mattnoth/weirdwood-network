## Appearances & Description

A small grey man with grey eyes and thinning hair (wiki:Luwin). He wears a robe of grey wool with voluminous sleeves sewn with pockets, from which he is always drawing things (wiki:Luwin). His maester's collar is a simple choker; he often tugs at the chain when uncomfortable (wiki:Luwin). He owns a bronze far-eye from Myr (wiki:Luwin). Luwin is one of the few maesters who earned the Valyrian steel link, signifying the study of the higher mysteries, though he is skeptical of magic, hedge wizards, and Old Nan's stories (wiki:Luwin).

## Narrative Arc

Luwin delivered all of Lord Eddard and Lady Catelyn's children and educated the Stark children, Jon Snow, and Theon Greyjoy (wiki:Luwin). He receives Lysa Arryn's letter warning that the Lannisters murdered Jon Arryn, and joins Catelyn in urging Eddard to accept the post of Hand (wiki:Luwin). After Bran's fall, Luwin tends the boy and treats Catelyn's wounds from the catspaw attack (wiki:Luwin). He creates the wicker basket Hodor uses to carry Bran, and builds a saddle from Tyrion's plans (wiki:Luwin). When Robb marches south, Luwin and Ser Rodrik Cassel govern Winterfell with Bran's voice. He provides sleeping draughts for Bran's troubled dreams and assists during the harvest feast (wiki:Luwin). With Rodrik's departure, governance falls completely on Luwin. He is wounded during Theon Greyjoy's capture of Winterfell but manages to send a raven to White Harbor (wiki:Luwin). After the sack, Bran and his companions find the mortally wounded Luwin in the godswood beneath the heart tree. Luwin counsels Osha to split the boys — Bran north and Rickon to safety — and asks Osha to end his suffering with mercy (wiki:Luwin).
