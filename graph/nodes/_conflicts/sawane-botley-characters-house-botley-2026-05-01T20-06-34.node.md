## Identity

Sawane Botley is the Lord of Lordsport and head of House Botley. He is the captain of the *Swiftfin*. (wiki:Sawane_Botley.cite_ref-Raffc11)

## Origins

Sawane has six sons: Harren, Tristifer, Symond, Harlon, Vickon, and Bennarion. (track_b: Issue) His brother is Germund Botley, and his half-brothers are Lucimore Botley and Sargon Botley. (wiki:Sawane_Botley)

## Allegiances

`SWORN_TO` House Botley (track_b: Allegiance). Sawane is a loyal supporter of House Greyjoy's line of succession, declaring that the Seastone Chair belongs to Theon Greyjoy by rights. (wiki:Sawane_Botley.cite_ref-Raffc1)

## Appearances & Description

During Greyjoy's Rebellion, the timber-and-wattle castle of House Botley was burned to the ground by King Robert I Baratheon during the battle at Lordsport. Lord Sawane rebuilt in stone after the war. (wiki:Sawane_Botley.cite_ref-Racok11)

## Narrative Arc

Sawane buys a difficult horse at Lannisport in 298 AC, which he later sells to Theon Greyjoy, who names it Smiler. As part of the transaction, Theon takes on Wex Pyke, the bastard son of Sawane's half-brother Sargon, as his squire. (wiki:Sawane_Botley.cite_ref-Racok24)

After King Balon Greyjoy's death, his brother Euron returns to proclaim himself King of the Iron Islands. Sawane refuses to do him fealty, stating that the Seastone Chair belongs to Theon Greyjoy by rights. Euron has Lord Sawane drowned. (wiki:Sawane_Botley.cite_ref-Raffc1, wiki:Sawane_Botley.cite_ref-Raffc11, wiki:Sawane_Botley.cite_ref-Raffc18)

Sawane's sons are dispossessed of the lordship. Half of the lands are given to House Wynch, while the remnants are left to Sawane's brother Germund, the new Lord of Lordsport. Asha Greyjoy intends to restore Sawane's lands to his eldest surviving son, Tristifer. (wiki:Sawane_Botley.cite_ref-Raffc11)
