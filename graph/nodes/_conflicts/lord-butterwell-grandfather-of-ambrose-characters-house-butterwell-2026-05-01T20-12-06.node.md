## Identity

Lord Butterwell was the Lord of Whitewalls and head of House Butterwell during the reign of King Aegon IV Targaryen. He served as Hand of the King to Aegon IV. His personal name is unknown. (wiki:Lord_Butterwell_(grandfather_of_Ambrose).cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_aegon_iv-2)

## Origins

Lord Butterwell was of Rivermen culture. He had a son and three daughters. (track_b: Culture, track_b: Issue) He was the grandfather of Lord Ambrose Butterwell. (wiki:Lord_Butterwell_(grandfather_of_Ambrose))

## Narrative Arc

Lord Butterwell built Whitewalls, a pale castle commonly called the Milkhouse by the smallfolk. Its walls, keep, and towers were made of white stone quarried in the Vale of Arryn, with floors and pillars of white marble veined with gold and rafters carved from weirwood trunks. (wiki:Lord_Butterwell_(grandfather_of_Ambrose).cite_ref-Rtmk-1)

When King Aegon IV visited Lord Butterwell's castle, Aegon gifted him a dragon egg in return for "access" to his three maiden daughters. According to tales, Aegon impregnated all three girls the same night. (wiki:Lord_Butterwell_(grandfather_of_Ambrose).cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_aegon_iv-6, wiki:Lord_Butterwell_(grandfather_of_Ambrose).cite_ref-Rtmk-1)

He was among the men Aegon IV chose as Hand not for nobility, honesty, or wisdom, but for their ability to amuse and flatter him. (wiki:Lord_Butterwell_(grandfather_of_Ambrose), note N1)
