## Identity

Maelys I Blackfyre, better known as Maelys the Monstrous, was the last of the Blackfyre Pretenders and the last known male-line member of House Blackfyre (wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre). He was of Valyrian race and Free Cities culture (track_b: Race, Culture).

## Origins

Born in the 3rd century AC in exile in Essos. He was a descendant of the male line of Daemon I Blackfyre. According to a semi-canon source, he supposedly devoured his twin in the womb, resulting in his grotesquely large upper body and vestigial second head (wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre).

## Appearances & Description

Maelys had a grotesquely huge torso and arms. His skull was huge and malformed, with a second head sprouting from his neck about the size of a child's fist. He was inhumanly strong, able to kill a destrier with a single punch and tear a man's head from his shoulders (wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre).

## Narrative Arc

Maelys seized command of the Golden Company by killing his cousin Daemon, punching his destrier dead and tearing Daemon's head from his shoulders. In 258 AC, Maelys gathered with eight other outlaws, exiles, and sellsword captains beneath the Tree of Crowns in the Disputed Lands, forming the Band of Nine. They pledged mutual aid, with the alliance promising to help Maelys conquer the Seven Kingdoms. The Band sacked Tyrosh and seized the Stepstones, becoming a real threat to Westeros (wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre).

In 260 AC, King Jaehaerys II Targaryen sent an army to the Stepstones. The War of the Ninepenny Kings was fought entirely there. Maelys personally killed Lord Ormund Baratheon, the Hand of the King, early in the war. Towards the war's end, Maelys was slain by Ser Barristan Selmy, ending the Blackfyre threat to the Iron Throne and the male line of House Blackfyre (wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre).

In ASOS, Jaime Lannister reads Barristan's entry in the White Book recording the deed (wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-asos). In ADWD, Illyrio Mopatis tells Tyrion that when Maelys died, the male line of House Blackfyre ended. At the Golden Company camp, Jon Connington sees Maelys's malformed skull among the gilded skulls of former captain-generals, with the tiny skull of his vestigial twin below it (wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-adwd).
