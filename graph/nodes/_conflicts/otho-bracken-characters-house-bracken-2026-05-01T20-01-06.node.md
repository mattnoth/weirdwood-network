## Identity

Ser Otho Bracken, called the Brute of Bracken, was a knight of House Bracken during the reigns of kings Daeron II and Aerys I Targaryen. He was the second son of Lord Bracken of Stone Hedge and eventually succeeded him. (wiki:Otho_Bracken.cite_ref-1) (wiki:Otho_Bracken.cite_ref-2)

## Narrative Arc

In 206 AC, Otho slew Lord Quentyn Blackwood in a tourney at King's Landing with a blunted longaxe, striking so hard he stove in the visor of Quentyn's helm and the face beneath. He earned the name "the Brute of Bracken" for this act. (wiki:Otho_Bracken.cite_ref-1)

At the tourney at Ashford Meadow in 209 AC, Otho erected his brown tent beneath a red stallion far from the Blackwood tents. When Ser Duncan the Tall desperately sought a seventh knight for his trial of seven, he pleaded with Otho, who had the grace to respond but rejected him, not knowing the hedge knight. (wiki:Otho_Bracken.cite_ref-1)

Otho's older brother died in the Great Spring Sickness, making Otho the heir. By 211 AC, Septon Sefton predicted "the Blackwoods will never stomach the Brute of Bracken as a neighbor," insisting it would mean war. (wiki:Otho_Bracken.cite_ref-4)
