## Identity

Daemon I Blackfyre, born Daemon Waters, was the bastard son of Princess Daena Targaryen and King Aegon IV Targaryen (track_b: Father, Mother). He was the founder of House Blackfyre, named after the Targaryen Valyrian steel sword. He was the first and greatest of the five Blackfyre Pretenders, known as "The Black Dragon" for his reversed Targaryen arms — a black three-headed dragon on red (wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre).

## Origins

Born in late 170 AC in King's Landing (track_b: Born). His mother named him for her grandfather, Prince Daemon Targaryen. He was of Valyrian race and Crownlands culture (track_b: Race, Culture). One of the Great Bastards legitimized by Aegon IV on his deathbed in 184 AC (wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre).

## Allegiances

He claimed the titles Ser, Prince, King of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men (claimant), Lord of the Seven Kingdoms (claimant), and Protector of the Realm (claimant) (track_b: Titles).

## Appearances & Description

Daemon was tall and powerful, with broad shoulders, muscular arms, and a flat stomach. He had deep purple eyes and silver-gold hair worn long to his shoulders. He was almost inhumanly beautiful, clean-shaven, and many thought he strongly resembled portraits of Aegon the Conqueror. His armor was ornate black and red plate with batlike steel "dragon wings" on his warhelm (wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre).

He mastered all weapons of the battlefield, but with the Valyrian steel sword Blackfyre in hand he was said to fight like the Warrior himself. He won friends easily and women were drawn to him (wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre).

## Narrative Arc

Raised in the Red Keep, Daemon was taught arms by Ser Quentyn Ball. At twelve, he won a squire's tourney and was knighted by King Aegon IV, who publicly bestowed the sword Blackfyre on him — an act some saw as symbolizing the monarchy itself. Aegon IV arranged his betrothal to Rohanne of Tyrosh; supporters later claimed Daemon had desired to marry his half-sister Daenerys instead. In 184 AC, Aegon IV legitimized all his bastards on his deathbed (wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre).

Growing resentment toward King Daeron II — fueled by Dornish influence at court, rumors of Daeron's illegitimacy, and the symbolic gift of Blackfyre — led nobles and knights to rally to Daemon. It took years to convince him to declare his claim; Maester Yandel considers it likely that Aegor Rivers (Bittersteel) was the key persuader. After Daemon agreed to a betrothal between his daughter Calla and Aegor, support increased. In 196 AC, Daemon began the First Blackfyre Rebellion (wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre).

At the Battle of the Redgrass Field, Daemon shattered Lord Donnel Arryn's vanguard and duelled Ser Gwayne Corbray of the Kingsguard for nearly an hour, both wielding Valyrian steel swords. After gaining the upper hand, Daemon dismounted to prevent the wounded Corbray from being trampled. In that pause, Brynden Rivers and his Raven's Teeth archers rained arrows from the Weeping Ridge, killing Daemon and his eldest sons Aegon and Aemon (wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre).

After Daemon's death, Bittersteel fled to Tyrosh with Daemon's remaining children and widow. Four more Blackfyre Rebellions would follow until the male line ended with Maelys I Blackfyre in 260 AC (wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre).
