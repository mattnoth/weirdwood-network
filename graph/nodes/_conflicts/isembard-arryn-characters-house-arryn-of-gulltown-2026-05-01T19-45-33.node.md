## Identity

Isembard Arryn, called the Gilded Falcon, was the head of House Arryn of Gulltown and a claimant to the Eyrie during the regency of King Aegon III Targaryen. He later served as master of coin. (wiki:Isembard_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency_1)

## Origins

A member of House Arryn of Gulltown, a cadet branch of House Arryn of the Eyrie. His exact parentage is not stated, but the family tree shows him as a cousin of Jeyne Arryn, Arnold Arryn, and Joffrey Arryn. (wiki:Isembard_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency_1)

## Appearances & Description

Isembard was known as the Gilded Falcon due to his wealth; men jested that the falcon on his personal arms was made of gold. His personal arms displayed a golden falcon soaring against a white moon, on a sky-blue field. (wiki:Isembard_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency_1)

## Narrative Arc

After the death of Jeyne Arryn, Lady of the Eyrie, a succession dispute arose between her chosen heir Ser Joffrey Arryn and a closer cousin Ser Arnold Arryn. Isembard emerged as a third claimant, using his wealth to bribe lesser lords and hire sellswords. Regent Ser Corwyn Corbray ruled in Joffrey's favor; Isembard and his sons were imprisoned. House Grafton continued to support his claim until Lord Alyn Velaryon defeated his sellsails and captured Gulltown's harbor. (wiki:Isembard_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency_1)

By 136 AC, Alyn and Lord Benjicot Blackwood forced Isembard's supporters to do homage to Lord Joffrey. Isembard traveled to King's Landing for the regent lottery. After Lord Torrhen Manderly was chosen Hand, Alyn suggested Isembard be named master of coin. Isembard and Torrhen reformed taxes and provided relief for those suffering from the collapse of the Rogare Bank. (wiki:Isembard_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency_1)
