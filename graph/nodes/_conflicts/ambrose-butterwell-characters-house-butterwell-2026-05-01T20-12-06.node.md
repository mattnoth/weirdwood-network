## Identity

Lord Ambrose Butterwell was the Lord of Whitewalls and head of House Butterwell during the reigns of Kings Aegon IV, Daeron II, and Aerys I Targaryen. He served as master of coin for Aegon IV and later as Hand of the King for Daeron II. (wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell)

## Origins

Ambrose was the grandson of the Lord Butterwell who built Whitewalls and served as Hand to Aegon IV. He was of Rivermen culture. (track_b: Culture, wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell)

## Appearances & Description

When he married his second wife in 212 AC, the fifty-year-old Ambrose had heavy jowls and thin flaxen hair. (wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk-1) He was very rich and said to be a pious man. King Maekar I Targaryen disliked him, considering him a craven, as did Ser Brynden Rivers. Lord Butterwell disliked taking risks and was indecisive, letting himself be swayed by those around him, namely his son-in-law Ser Tommard Heddle. (wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell)

## Narrative Arc

Lord Ambrose served as master of coin under King Aegon IV Targaryen and was raised to Hand of the King under Daeron II, but his ability to lead was questioned when his attempt to deal with the burgeoning Blackfyre Rebellion failed miserably. He was replaced by Lord Hayford. During the First Blackfyre Rebellion, Butterwell attempted to keep one foot in each camp: he sent his eldest son to fight for Daeron II and his second son to fight for Daemon Blackfyre, while he remained home with his full strength. Both sons perished at the Battle of the Redgrass Field, and his youngest son died of the Great Spring Sickness. (wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell)

With his sons dead and daughters married off, the recently-widowed Lord Butterwell hosted a wedding tourney at Whitewalls in 212 AC to celebrate his marriage to a daughter of House Frey. The tourney was secretly an attempt to foment the Second Blackfyre Rebellion in the name of Daemon II Blackfyre. The winner would receive the dragon egg given to Ambrose's grandfather by King Aegon IV, but the egg was stolen before it could be awarded. (wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell)

When Prince Aegon Targaryen (disguised as "Egg," Duncan the Tall's squire) was discovered within the castle, Lord Ambrose brought the boy to the sept. Aegon bluffed Ambrose by claiming he was a spy and that an army was coming, so Lord Frey departed with his retinue and Ambrose recanted his allegiance to House Blackfyre. Ser Duncan slew Tommard Heddle in the sept when he attempted to take Aegon into custody. (wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell)

Lord Brynden Rivers appeared with an army to end the nascent rebellion. Lord Ambrose lost Whitewalls (which was razed and the earth salted) and was only allowed to keep a tenth of House Butterwell's fortunes along with his head and his new bride. (wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell)
