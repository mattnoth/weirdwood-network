## Identity

The Coming of the Andals was the centuries-long migration and invasion of Westeros by the Andals, originating from Andalos in Essos. It is one of the most transformative events in Westerosi history, resulting in the conquest of all the southern kingdoms and the replacement of the old gods with the Faith of the Seven south of the Neck. (wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals)

## Narrative Arc

The Andals crossed the narrow sea and landed first in the Vale, where they conquered the First Men kingdom of the Arryns' predecessors. Led by warlords bearing the seven-pointed star carved into their flesh, the Andals spread across the south over generations. The Vale fell first; then the riverlands, stormlands, Reach, westerlands, and Iron Islands followed. The children of the forest were driven further into retreat. Dorne saw intermarriage between Andals and local populations rather than outright conquest. Only the North, defended by Moat Cailin and the Neck, resisted the Andal invasion entirely, with House Stark maintaining the old ways. The Andals brought the Faith of the Seven, steel weapons, and new customs of knighthood to Westeros. (wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals, agot-40, agot-60, agot-66, asos-45, asos-56, asos-68, asos-70, affc-20, adwd-48)

## Notes

Cited across multiple books: AGOT 40, 60, 66; ASOS 45, 56, 68, 70; AFFC 20; ADWD 48. Also extensively covered in TWOIAF. The timing is debated — maesters place it approximately 6,000 years before the events of the main series, though the True History argues for a much shorter timeframe.
