## Identity

Prudence Celtigar was a noblewoman from House Celtigar during the reigns of kings Maegor I and Jaehaerys I Targaryen. She later became the Lady of Gulltown by marriage to Lord Grafton. (wiki:Prudence_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon; wiki:Prudence_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabbirth_death_and_betrayal)

## Origins

Daughter of Lord Edwell Celtigar of Claw Isle. Of Valyrian descent, from the Crownlands. Born 34 or 35 AC. (wiki:Prudence_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon; track_b: Father, Race, Culture)

## Allegiances

`SWORN_TO` House Celtigar (by birth) and House Grafton (by marriage). (track_b: Allegiances)

## Appearances & Description

Lord Rogar Baratheon scornfully claimed that Prudence and her sister Prunella had "no chins, no teats, and no sense." (wiki:Prudence_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides)

## Narrative Arc

In 47 AC, Prudence's father urged King Maegor I to marry either Prudence or her sister Prunella; the king instead married the Black Brides. (wiki:Prudence_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon) In 49 AC, her father made the same offer to Lord Rogar Baratheon for King Jaehaerys I; Baratheon rebuffed it. (wiki:Prudence_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides)

Prudence became a lady companion to Queen Alysanne Targaryen during her time on Dragonstone. (wiki:Prudence_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides) She participated in the bedding ceremony at Jaehaerys and Alysanne's second wedding. (wiki:Prudence_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing)

At Maidenpool, Prudence was stabbed through the shoulder defending Queen Alysanne from septas who wished to kill the child the queen was carrying. (wiki:Prudence_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabbirth_death_and_betrayal)

In 52 AC, her betrothal to Lord Grafton was arranged. (wiki:Prudence_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabbirth_death_and_betrayal)
