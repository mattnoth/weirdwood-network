## Identity

Ser Balman Byrch is a knight from House Byrch and the husband of Lady Falyse Stokeworth, heir to Stokeworth. (wiki:Balman_Byrch)

## Origins

Born in the Crownlands. (track_b: Born) (wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Rawoiafbalman_byrch)

## Appearances & Description

Balman has a thick blond mustache, wavy blond hair that is receding, and a pot-belly. He enjoys food and drink, including hippocras. (wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc24) In his youth he was considered one of the most handsome knights and was a renowned tourney jouster. (wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc24) Queen Cersei Lannister considers him a fool. (wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc24)

## Narrative Arc

Balman jousted in the tourney at Lannisport in 289 AC after Greyjoy's Rebellion and in a tourney at Duskendale in 294 AC. (wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc24) He has been married to Falyse for ten years with no children; it is rumored he shuns his wife's bed, preferring maidens. (Tier 2 -- wiki hedges with "it is rumored") (wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Rasos66)

Balman and Falyse attend Lord Tywin Lannister's funeral in King's Landing, where they ask to name Lollys Stokeworth's bastard child Tywin, offending Cersei. (wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc7) (affc-cersei-02) When Bronn instead names the child Tyrion Tanner, Cersei manipulates Balman into a conspiracy to kill Bronn. (wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc24) (affc-cersei-05)

Rather than arranging a hunting accident as Cersei intended, Balman challenges Bronn to a duel at Castle Stokeworth. Bronn drives his lance through the chest of Balman's horse, crushing Balman's legs beneath it. Balman confesses Cersei's involvement in exchange for mercy, but Bronn kills him with a knife to the eye. (wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc32) (affc-cersei-07)
