## Identity

Walton, known as Steelshanks Walton, is the captain of the guards at the Dreadfort, in service to Lord Roose Bolton. (wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Rasos3)

## Origins

## Appearances & Description

Tall and dour. (wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Racok64; wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Rasos44) Known as Steelshanks for the steel greaves he wears over his long legs. (wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Rasos3) Also wears a mail shirt and wields a spear. (wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Racok64; wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Radwd51) Loyal and brutal, but not cruel — a simple soldier who obeys without question but lacks the malignant cruelty of men like the Brave Companions. (wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Rasos44)

## Narrative Arc

Walton is part of Roose Bolton's force that captures Harrenhal, assisting with executions of Lannister servants. (wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Racok64) He commands Jaime Lannister's escort of two hundred men from Harrenhal to King's Landing. (wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Rasos37) When Jaime persuades him to return to Harrenhal, Walton's crossbowmen slay a bear to save Jaime and Brienne of Tarth from the bear pit. (wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Rasos44)

At King's Landing, Walton and his men receive their promised gold. (wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Rasos62) He later takes possession of a girl claimed to be Arya Stark (actually Jeyne Poole) and departs to bring her north for marriage to Ramsay Bolton. (wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Rasos72)

In ADWD, Walton commands Roose's guards at Barrowton and Winterfell. (wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Radwd32) He escorts Theon to meetings and his men break up the fight between Manderlys and Freys when Hosteen Frey attacks Wyman Manderly. (wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Radwd46; wiki:Steelshanks_Walton.cite_ref-Radwd51)
