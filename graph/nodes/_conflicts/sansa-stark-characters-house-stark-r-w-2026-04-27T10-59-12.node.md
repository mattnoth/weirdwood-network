## Identity

Sansa Stark is the eldest daughter of Lord Eddard Stark and Lady Catelyn Stark, and a POV character across all five main novels (track_b: Father, Mother). She holds the titles of Princess and Lady of Winterfell (track_b: Titles). Born in 286 AC at Winterfell (track_b: Born), she is approximately eleven years old at the start of AGOT. Her aliases include "Little bird" (Sandor Clegane's name for her), "Alayne Stone" (her disguise identity in the Vale), and "Lady Lannister" (through her forced marriage to Tyrion) (track_b: Aliases).

## Appearances & Description

Long auburn hair, brushed until it shone (agot-sansa-01, agot-sansa-02). Thick auburn curls after washing (agot-sansa-06). Described as tall for her age and beautiful, the "very image of Cat at the same age" by Littlefinger (agot-sansa-04). Dresses finely: blue velvets with silver chain at the hearing (agot-eddard-03), green gown at the tourney (agot-sansa-02), ivory silk dyed black for mourning (agot-sansa-05). She is bonded to the direwolf Lady, who is killed on Cersei's orders early in AGOT (agot-bran-02, agot-sansa-03).

## Narrative Arc

Sansa begins as a romantic, courtly girl who dreams of marrying Prince Joffrey and becoming queen. She is betrothed to Joffrey and travels south with the royal party (agot-sansa-01). When conflict erupts between Arya and Joffrey, Sansa claims she cannot remember what happened, alienating her sister (agot-eddard-03). She becomes increasingly enmeshed in court life, attending the Hand's tourney, receiving a rose from Ser Loras (agot-eddard-07), and idealizing Joffrey despite warning signs. After her father discovers the truth about Cersei's children, Sansa goes to Cersei and tells her of Ned's plans to send them home — inadvertently contributing to his arrest (agot-sansa-04). Confined in Maegor's Holdfast, she is manipulated by Cersei into writing letters to Robb demanding fealty (agot-sansa-04). She pleads for her father's life before the court, kneeling to beg mercy (agot-sansa-05). After Joffrey orders Ned's execution despite promises of mercy, Sansa is held hostage. In her final AGOT chapter, Joffrey forces her to view the heads on the battlements, and she contemplates pushing him off the walkway before the Hound stops her (agot-sansa-06). Across later books, she is married to Tyrion Lannister, smuggled out of King's Landing by Littlefinger after Joffrey's death, and disguised as his bastard daughter Alayne Stone in the Vale.
