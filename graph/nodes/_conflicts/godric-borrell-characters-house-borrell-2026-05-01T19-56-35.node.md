## Identity

Lord Godric Borrell is the Lord of Sweetsister, Shield of Sisterton, Master of Breakwater Castle, and Keeper of the Night Lamp. He is the head of House Borrell. (wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9-1) (wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Raffcappendix-3)

## Origins

Godric was born on Sweetsister. (wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Rawoiafgodric_borrell-2) His father was the previous Lord Borrell, who held the same titles during Robert's Rebellion. (wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9-1)

## Appearances & Description

Godric is a big, fleshy man with massive shoulders and a blunt, brutal face with a massive brow. He is bald on the top of his scalp, with coarse grey and white hair growing from his cheeks and chin. He has black eyes, thick lips, and a lumpy, red-veined nose. Most of his teeth are yellow and one is blackened. He has "the mark" — webbing between the middle three fingers of his right hand. (wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9-1)

Lord Borrell is fond of storms and sister's stew, but not of northmen. (wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9-1) He is described as pragmatic. (Tier 2 — semi-canon source: wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Rawoiafgodric_borrell-2)

## Narrative Arc

Godric was present when his father freed Eddard Stark during Robert's Rebellion rather than turning him over to King Aerys II Targaryen for a reward. (wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9-1)

Stannis Baratheon, as master of ships, once sent a fleet to Sisterton without Godric's leave and forced him to hang a dozen smuggler friends, threatening to hang Godric himself should the Night Lamp go dark. (wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9-1)

Godric has daughters but no sons; his granddaughters include Gella. (wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9-1)

In *A Dance with Dragons*, Godric claims the cargo of the *Sloe-Eyed Maid* after it crashes into rocks in the Bite. Freys led by Rhaegar Frey arrive at Sweetsister en route to White Harbor with Lord Wyman Manderly's son Wendel's bones; Godric states he would have hanged them. When Davos Seaworth is brought before him, Godric recounts the story of Eddard Stark's visit and the fisherman's daughter who brought Ned across the Bite, leaving her with a bastard she named Jon Snow. (wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd9-1) Godric then allows Davos to leave for White Harbor. (wiki:Godric_Borrell.cite_ref-Radwd15-4)
