## Identity

The Battle of the Honeywine was a battle fought during the Dance of the Dragons in 130 AC on the banks of the Honeywine river in the Reach. It occurred a fortnight after the Battle of the Gullet (wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine).

## Allegiances

Part of `FIGHTS_IN` Dance of the Dragons. Blacks (northern force: Thaddeus Rowan and Tom Flowers with Rowan/Caswell knights; southern force: Alan Beesbury, Alan Tarly, Owen Costayne — Houses Beesbury, Caswell, Costayne, Rowan, Tarly) fought against Greens (Ormund Hightower's army with Houses Hightower, Peake, Roxton; Prince Daeron Targaryen on Tessarion) (track_b: Conflict).

## Narrative Arc

Lord Ormund Hightower marched out of Oldtown with a large army but was harassed for months by the "Two Alans" (Alan Tarly and Alan Beesbury) raiding his camps and Lord Owen Costayne attacking his rear. A large force under Thaddeus Rowan descended from north of the Mander. Ormund halted along the Honeywine, caught between two armies. He sent for dragon support from King's Landing but was ignored — Aemond took Vhagar north instead. In the third week of 130 AC, Rowan and Tom Flowers attacked from the northeast while the Two Alans and Costayne attacked from behind. The Hightower lines were crumbling when Prince Daeron arrived on Tessarion and turned the battle decisively. Tom Flowers was killed. Lord Owen Costayne was mortally wounded by Jon Roxton wielding the Valyrian steel sword Orphan-Maker. Alan Tarly and Alan Beesbury were captured. Only Thaddeus Rowan escaped with the remains of his host. Daeron was knighted by Ormund with the Valyrian steel longsword Vigilance and dubbed "Daeron the Daring" (wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine).
