## Identity

Jonos Bracken is the Lord of the Stone Hedge and head of House Bracken, a riverlord house. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken)

## Origins

Born at Stone Hedge. (track_b: Born) He has had three wives and five daughters, plus a bastard son Harry Rivers whose paternity is disputed by Lord Tytos Blackwood. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-2)

## Allegiances

`SWORN_TO` House Bracken (track_b: Allegiance). `HOLDS_TITLE` Lord of the Stone Hedge (track_b: Title). `CULTURE_OF` Rivermen (track_b: Culture).

## Appearances & Description

Jonos is heavier than Ser Jaime Lannister but not as tall. He has brown eyes, coarse brown hair (including on his back), and thick shoulders and arms. He often glowers and blusters. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-2) He wears a brown woolen tunic embroidered with the red stallion of House Bracken. In battle, he wears plate and mail with a grey steel greathelm bearing a horsehair crest. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-2)

## Narrative Arc

Before Tyrion's arrest at the crossroads inn, Catelyn Stark confirms Jonos is loyal to her father Hoster Tully. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-4) (agot-catelyn-05) After Robert I's death, the Iron Throne decrees Jonos and other river lords swear fealty to Joffrey. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-5) Tywin Lannister sends Gregor Clegane to attack Stone Hedge, and the Mountain rapes one of Jonos's daughters during the burning. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-2)

After the siege of Riverrun breaks, Jonos arrives from ruined Stone Hedge. At Robb's war council he sits as far from rival Tytos Blackwood as possible. He suggests allying with Renly against the Lannisters and joins in declaring Robb King in the North and King of the Trident. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-3) (agot-catelyn-11)

During the liberation of Stone Hedge, Jonos is wounded and his nephew Hendry is slain. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-7) His bastard son Harry Rivers is killed by Lannisters. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-9) A dozen of Jonos's kin die at the Red Wedding. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-2) After Joffrey's death, Tommen pardons Jonos for his part in the war. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-13)

Jonos besieges Raventree Hall on behalf of the Iron Throne. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-14) When Jaime arrives to end the siege, he finds Jonos abed with a common woman named Hildy. Jonos demands lands and compensation from the Blackwoods; Jaime forces a modest settlement and orders Jonos to send one of his daughters to attend Queen Regent Cersei at court. (wiki:Jonos_Bracken.cite_ref-2)
