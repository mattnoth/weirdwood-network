## Narrative Arc

Ser Gyles served King Aegon II during the Dance of the Dragons. Following the defeat of Lord Borros Baratheon at the Battle of the Kingsroad, Gyles commanded Aegon's escort after a small council meeting. The king drank Arbor red and ordered Gyles to take his palanquin to the Red Keep's sept. When Aegon did not respond upon arrival, Gyles found the king dead with blood on his lips. (wiki:Gyles_Belgrave.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons-2)

Two days later, when the victorious riverlords arrived, Gyles and four other Kingsguard stood as Aegon's body lay on a bier beneath the Iron Throne. During the Hour of the Wolf, Lord Cregan Stark accused Gyles of either poisoning the king's wine or failing to prevent it. On the day of executions, most condemned chose to take the black, but Gyles accepted death, stating he had failed in his duty. Lord Stark personally beheaded him with Ice, and his head was placed on a pike by the gates of the Red Keep. (wiki:Gyles_Belgrave.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf-3)
