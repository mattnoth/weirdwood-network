## Identity

Daemon II Blackfyre, also known as Daemon the Younger, was the third son of Daemon I Blackfyre and Rohanne of Tyrosh (track_b: Father, Mother). He used the alias Ser John the Fiddler, a hedge knight, while traveling incognito. He led the brief Second Blackfyre Rebellion (wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre).

## Origins

Born in 189 AC in Westeros (track_b: Born). Of Crownlands culture (track_b: Culture). His father was Daemon I Blackfyre and his mother was Rohanne of Tyrosh (track_b: Father, Mother).

## Allegiances

`SWORN_TO` House Blackfyre (track_b: Allegiance). Predecessor as Blackfyre Pretender: Daemon I Blackfyre; successor: Haegon I Blackfyre (track_b: Predecessor, Successor).

## Appearances & Description

As "Ser John the Fiddler," Daemon appeared as a lean, lithe young man with a handsome clean-shaven face and fine features. He had collar-length black hair (dyed) and dark purple eyes — his natural hair was silver-gold, which he revealed after unmasking. He wore a dark blue silk doublet edged in gold satin, embroidered with an engrailed cross, golden fiddle, and golden sword. He smelled of oranges and limes with a hint of some strange eastern spice (wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre).

Daemon had prophetic dreams and a love of music. George R. R. Martin confirmed that Daemon was gay. He had a close relationship with Lord Alyn Cockshaw (track_b: Lover).

## Narrative Arc

As a child, Daemon was bullied by his older twin brothers Aegon and Aemon. He dreamed prophetically that they would die, but they laughed at him. After his father's death at the Redgrass Field in 196 AC, seven-year-old Daemon went into exile in Tyrosh with his surviving family and uncle Bittersteel (wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre).

In Tyrosh, Daemon dreamed that a dragon would be born at Whitewalls. Lord Gormon Peake convinced him to cross the narrow sea and make a play for the throne, using a wedding tourney at Lord Butterwell's castle as cover. Bittersteel withheld support and the sword Blackfyre. Under the alias "Ser John the Fiddler," Daemon traveled to Whitewalls and befriended Ser Duncan the Tall. During the tourney, Daemon unseated opponents but Peake had been bribing his rivals. When Ser Glendon Flowers was falsely accused of stealing a dragon egg, Duncan exposed the plot. Daemon challenged Glendon to a trial joust and was unhorsed in the first tilt, landing in a mud puddle — earning the epithet "the Brown Dragon" (wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre).

Brynden Rivers (Bloodraven) marched on Whitewalls to end the Second Blackfyre Rebellion. Daemon rode out alone to challenge Bloodraven to single combat but was arrested instead. Bloodraven kept Daemon alive as a hostage to prevent Bittersteel from crowning Haegon. Daemon died a few years later while imprisoned in the Red Keep (wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre).
