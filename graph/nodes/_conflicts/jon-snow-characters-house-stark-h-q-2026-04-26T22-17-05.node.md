## Identity

Jon Snow is the bastard son of Lord Eddard Stark of Winterfell (wiki:Jon_Snow). He has five half-siblings: Robb, Sansa, Arya, Bran, and Rickon Stark. Unaware of the identity of his mother, Jon was raised at Winterfell (wiki:Jon_Snow). He is one of the major POV characters in A Song of Ice and Fire.

## Origins

Born 283 AC, near the end of Robert's Rebellion (track_b: Born). Father: Eddard Stark (track_b: Father). Mother claimed variously as Wylla (supposedly) or Ashara Dayne (rumored) or a fisherman's daughter (rumored) (track_b: Mothers; wiki:Jon_Snow). Named possibly after Jon Arryn, Eddard's foster father (wiki:Jon_Snow).

## Appearances & Description

Jon has the long face of the Starks, dark brown hair, and grey eyes so dark they almost seem black (wiki:Jon_Snow). He is graceful, quick, and lean. He resembles his father Eddard more than any of his half-brothers; Arya is said to resemble him most of all the Stark children (wiki:Jon_Snow). He looks solemn and guarded, considered sullen and quick to sense a slight (wiki:Jon_Snow). After joining the Night's Watch he dresses in black. He wields Longclaw, the Valyrian steel bastard sword of House Mormont, given to him by Lord Commander Jeor Mormont with a white wolf-head pommel (wiki:Jon_Snow). His left hand is scarred from burns received fighting a wight (wiki:Jon_Snow). His direwolf is Ghost, an albino with red eyes.

## Narrative Arc

Jon accompanies his father to the execution of a Night's Watch deserter, and on the return discovers a litter of direwolf pups. He argues that five pups for five Stark children must be a sign, then finds a sixth — an albino runt — and claims it as Ghost (wiki:Jon_Snow). Catelyn Stark refuses to let Jon remain at Winterfell when Eddard goes south, so Jon joins the Night's Watch. He gives Arya a small sword, Needle, forged by Mikken (wiki:Jon_Snow).

At Castle Black, Jon overcomes initial arrogance, befriends Samwell Tarly, and saves Lord Commander Mormont from a wight, burning his hand. Mormont rewards him with Longclaw. After Eddard's execution, Jon attempts to desert but is brought back by his friends (wiki:Jon_Snow).

During the great ranging, Jon captures the wildling Ygritte but secretly lets her go. Qhorin Halfhand orders Jon to infiltrate the wildlings; Jon kills Qhorin to prove his loyalty and is brought to Mance Rayder (wiki:Jon_Snow). Jon convinces Mance of his desertion and falls in love with Ygritte. He escapes at Queenscrown to warn Castle Black of the wildling attack, taking an arrow in the leg (wiki:Jon_Snow).

Jon helps defend Castle Black against Styr's raiders; Ygritte dies in his arms. He then holds the Wall against Mance's main assault for several days. Arrested by Alliser Thorne and Janos Slynt, Jon is sent to parley with Mance, but Stannis Baratheon's forces arrive and rout the wildlings (wiki:Jon_Snow).

Jon refuses Stannis's offer of legitimization as Jon Stark, Lord of Winterfell, because it would require burning the heart tree. Elected 998th Lord Commander of the Night's Watch through Sam's efforts (wiki:Jon_Snow).

As Lord Commander, Jon executes Janos Slynt for insubordination, swapping Mance's infant with Gilly's son to protect both from Melisandre, and sends Sam to the Citadel. He allows Tormund's wildlings through the Wall, garrisons abandoned castles, and negotiates a loan from the Iron Bank of Braavos (wiki:Jon_Snow). After receiving Ramsay Bolton's taunting letter claiming Stannis's defeat, Jon announces his intention to ride south against the Boltons. In the resulting mutiny at Castle Black, Bowen Marsh and others stab Jon repeatedly, stating "for the Watch" (wiki:Jon_Snow).

## Notes

Jon's true parentage is a major unresolved question in the series. Multiple candidates for his mother are presented: Wylla (Eddard's claim to Robert), Ashara Dayne (Catelyn's maids' gossip), and a fisherman's daughter (Lord Godric Borrell's tale). Robb Stark's legitimization decree is semi-canon. Jon's fate after the mutiny at Castle Black is unresolved. All theory and speculation is deferred to Pass 5+.
