## Identity

The battles at Summerhall were three engagements fought by Robert Baratheon in a single day during Robert's Rebellion in 282 AC, near the ruined castle of Summerhall in the stormlands. (wiki:Battles_at_Summerhall)

## Narrative Arc

After the rebels' initial call to arms, Lords Grandison, Cafferen, and Fell marched to join the royal forces but were caught separately by Robert Baratheon. Robert defeated all three hosts in succession on the same day — a feat that became legendary. He killed Lord Fell in single combat, took his son Silveraxe prisoner, and won Lords Cafferen and Grandison to his cause. The triple victory secured Robert's hold on the stormlands and established his reputation as a formidable battlefield commander. (wiki:Battles_at_Summerhall, asos-36, asos-54)

## Quotes

"Robert emerged from his castle with a stag's head on his helm and smashed the Targaryen forces in three battles in a single day." — Recalled in A Storm of Swords (wiki:Battles_at_Summerhall)
