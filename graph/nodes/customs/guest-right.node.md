## Origins

The legend of the [Rat Cook](wiki:Rat_Cook) of the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort) concerns guest right.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Its song is used to represent the repercussions that await those who violate this sacred law of hospitality.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
Maester [Egbert](wiki:Egbert) wrote about [northern](wiki:North) violations of guest right in [Justice and Injustice in the North: Judgments of Three Stark Lords](wiki:Justice_and_Injustice_in_the_North:_Judgments_of_Three_Stark_Lords), with only [kinslaying](wiki:Kinslaying) being an equivalent crime.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)
Lord [Lyman Lannister](wiki:Lyman_Lannister) provided guest right to Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) and Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), protecting them from King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) The royal couple had previously been denied guest right by Lord [Lucas Harroway](wiki:Lucas_Harroway), who refused them entry to [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) so long as they did not acknowledge his daughter [Alys](wiki:Alys_Harroway) as Maegor's true and lawful wife.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)
On the [Feast Day of Our Father Above](wiki:Feast_Day_of_Our_Father_Above) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), the dungeons of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) were emptied and all the offenders punished publicly, among them an innkeeper who had been killing some of his guests for years and had stolen their valuables.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
During his dispute with [House Webber](wiki:House_Webber), Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey) refused hospitality to Lady [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber) when she neared the lands of [House Osgrey](wiki:House_Osgrey).(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

When [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) returns to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) after the injury of [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark), [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) greets Tyrion with an unsheathed sword upon his knees. After Tyrion gives the design for a special saddle for Bran to be able to ride a [horse](wiki:Horse) despite his injury, Robb sheathes his sword and offers Tyrion the hospitality of Winterfell. Tyrion rejects it and chooses to stay in the [winter town](wiki:Winter_town).(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) agrees to protect Jon as a guest when they meet [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall).(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
[Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), gifts [Craster](wiki:Craster) with an [axe](wiki:Armament) and a crossbow when the [great ranging](wiki:Great_ranging) stays at [Craster's Keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep). The pregnant [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) wants [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) to take her away. When Jon protests that he is a guest, Gilly argues that Jon did not eat Craster's food or sleep in Craster's hall.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Craster and Jeor are murdered by members of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) during the [mutiny at Craster's Keep](wiki:Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep).(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)
Robb and his mother [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) receive bread and salt when they arrive at the [Twins](wiki:Twins) for the wedding of Lord [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully).(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), the [Lord of the Crossing](wiki:Lord_of_the_Crossing), betrays them in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), however.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Despite having orchestrated the massacre and offered protection to Lord Walder, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) places the blame on him.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) When [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) hears of the Red Wedding, he believes that [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) is cursed for violating guest right.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

The massacre ruins the Freys' reputation even among allies.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) The thousands of [sparrows](wiki:Sparrow) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) are especially furious about the Red Wedding and violation of guest right. The [small council](wiki:Small_council) discusses how to blame the Freys, and not the Iron Throne, for the massacre.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Trust in guest right has declined in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) in the aftermath of the Red Wedding. The [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) hangs some outlaws and soldiers who expect safety at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn).(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
[Wine](wiki:Wine), cheese, bread, and salt are offered to the [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant) when they come to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), since their acceptance of the good is a recognition of guest right.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Davos Seaworth is grateful to eat bread and [sister's stew](wiki:Sister's_stew) while at [Breakwater](wiki:Breakwater), since it is a recognition of guest right by Lord [Godric Borrell](wiki:Godric_Borrell).(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)
Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) grants palfreys as guest gifts to [Jared](wiki:Jared_Frey), [Rhaegar Frey](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey), and [Symond Frey](wiki:Symond_Frey) when they depart [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Wyman desires vengeance against [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) for the death of his son, [Wendel](wiki:Wendel_Manderly), who was killed at the Twins as a guest during the Red Wedding.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) The three Frey envoys disappear while traveling to [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton).(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Wyman serves [three large pork pies](wiki:Frey_Pies/Theories) during the wedding feast of [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) and "[Arya Stark](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)" at Winterfell.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
Despite [Obara Sand](wiki:Obara_Sand)'s calls for vengeance, [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), refuses to harm his guest, Ser [Balon Swann](wiki:Balon_Swann).(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)
Jon Snow, now Lord Commander, accepts [Alys Karstark](wiki:Alys_Karstark) as a guest at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) and has [Cregan Karstark](wiki:Cregan_Karstark) apprehended before he can request guest right.(wiki:Guest_right.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)
