## Origins

In the [Reach](wiki:Reach), during the days of the [House Gardener](wiki:House_Gardener) [Kings of the Reach](wiki:Kings_of_the_Reach), you could pay a man a blood price if you had slain one of his animals or [peasants](wiki:Smallfolk).(wiki:Blood_price.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
In [211 AC](wiki:211_AC), near [Standfast](wiki:Standfast), Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey)'s [sworn sword](wiki:Sworn_sword), Ser [Bennis of the Brown Shield](wiki:Bennis_of_the_Brown_Shield), attacked [Wolmer](wiki:Wolmer), a peasant in service to Lady [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber), cutting his cheek to the bone. Hoping to prevent Lady Rohanne avenging the assault by killing Bennis, Ser Eustace recalled the old tradition of the blood price. He sent his other sworn sword, Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), to [Coldmoat](wiki:Coldmoat) with a [silver](wiki:Silver) [stag](wiki:Currency) for Wolmer, and three stags for Lady Rohanne, for the insult. However, Rohanne rejected the offer.(wiki:Blood_price.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
According to a semi-canon source, in [255 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) sent his good-father, Ser [Denys Marbrand](wiki:Denys_Marbrand), to capture Lord [Walderan](wiki:Walderan_Tarbeck) and Lady [Ellyn Tarbeck](wiki:Ellyn_Reyne) and bring them to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) to answer for their crimes of taking neighboring holdings by force. However, [Roger Reyne](wiki:Roger_Reyne), [Lord of Castamere](wiki:Lord_of_Castamere) and Ellyn's brother, led a host that attacked the Marbrand men in the night, slaying hundreds including Denys. Though calls went out for war, Ser [Reynard Reyne](wiki:Reynard_Reyne) convinced Tytos that the attack was a misunderstanding, claiming that Roger was unaware of Denys's identity and thought he was attacking [outlaws](wiki:Outlaw). After Reynard offered apologies and a blood price to [House Marbrand](wiki:House_Marbrand), Tytos pardoned Roger Reyne, and Lord and Lady Tarbeck as well.(wiki:Blood_price.cite_ref-sample_3-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

In [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), Queen [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) frequently receives petitioners asking for repayment for the sheep that her [dragons](wiki:Dragon) have killed and eaten. One day [a farmer](wiki:Hazzea's_father) comes to the [audience chamber](wiki:Audience_chamber_(Great_Pyramid)), carrying a sack of burned and cracked bones, claiming they are the remains of his child [Hazzea](wiki:Hazzea), and that [Drogon](wiki:Drogon) had killed and eaten her.(wiki:Blood_price.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) While one of Daenerys's advisors, [Skahaz mo Kandaq](wiki:Skahaz_mo_Kandaq), tells her to kill the man or at least [have his tongue out](wiki:Tongue_mutilation) to keep the girl's death a secret, instead Daenerys chooses to pay a blood price. No one can tell her the value of a daughter, so she pays the man one hundred times the value of a lamb, with more money to be given each year on Hazzea's [nameday](wiki:Nameday), as long as the farmer never tells anyone a dragon killed her.(wiki:Blood_price.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
At [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), arranges with [Tormund Giantsbane](wiki:Tormund_Giantsbane) to allow three thousand [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) through the [Wall](wiki:Wall) to safety. Tormund has the free folk hand over their wealth or anything of value to the Watch, along with one hundred boys as [hostages](wiki:Hostage). Tormund calls this Jon's "blood price".(wiki:Blood_price.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Blood_price.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
