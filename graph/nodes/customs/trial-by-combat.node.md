## Origins

[Trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven), originally a religious ceremony,(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rssm1020.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) is a form of trial by combat which was brought across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) during the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
In [42 AC](wiki:42_AC), during the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) after the death of his brother, King [Aenys I](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen). When Maegor arrived with his mother, Dowager Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), she challenged all in the city who opposed Maegor's right to the throne. Ser [Damon Morrigen](wiki:Damon_Morrigen), the [Grand Captain](wiki:Grand_Captain) of the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons), accepted her challenge. After Maegor insisted upon a trial by combat, the king accepted Damon's proposal of a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven).(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-6) Maegor was the only survivor of the ensuing battle.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf43.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
In [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), Ser [Harrold Langward](wiki:Harrold_Langward), one of Maegor's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), demanded a trial by combat instead of joining the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). The fourteen-year-old King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) wished to face the knight himself, but his mother, Queen Regent [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), forbade it. Ser [Gyles Morrigen](wiki:Gyles_Morrigen) acted as the crown's champion instead and slew Harrold.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rfab5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
In [84 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Ser [Braxton Beesbury](wiki:Braxton_Beesbury) asked for trial by combat after being accused of seducing Jaehaerys's daughter, [Saera Targaryen](wiki:Saera_Targaryen). Jaehaerys decided to face the younger Braxton personally. After Braxton tired himself during the trial, Jaehaerys slew him with [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre).(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
In the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), Ser [Victor Risley](wiki:Victor_Risley) demanded a trial by combat after being accused of [treason](wiki:Treason). He was slain by Ser [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long).(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
During the reign of King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), Queen [Naerys Targaryen](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen) was accused of adultery and treason by Ser [Morgil Hastwyck](wiki:Morgil_Hastwyck). Naerys's brother, Prince [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), defended his sister's honor in a trial by combat and slew Morgil, thereby disproving the accusations.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) This event became famous and inspired many songs, stories and fables by bards, furthering Aemon the Dragonknight's renown, much to Aegon's annoyance.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)
In [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), during the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow), several events led to a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) in which Prince [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen) and Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) accused a [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight), Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall). Although both princes eventually withdrew their accusations, the trial ended with the crown prince, [Baelor Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), among the deceased.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
In [211 AC](wiki:211_AC), a trial by combat took place in the [Chequy Water](wiki:Chequy_Water) in the [Reach](wiki:Reach) to resolve the conflict between Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey) of [Standfast](wiki:Standfast) and Lady [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber) of [Coldmoat](wiki:Coldmoat). Ser Duncan the Tall, Eustace's [sworn sword](wiki:Sworn_sword), served as his champion, while Ser [Lucas Inchfield](wiki:Lucas_Inchfield), Rohanne's [castellan](wiki:Castellan), served as hers. [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Sefton](wiki:Sefton_Staunton) prayed over the combatants before the battle. In the fight, Duncan slew Lucas, but in the process drowned in the stream. However, Coldmoat's [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Cerrick](wiki:Cerrick) was able to revive Duncan using the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) technique of the [kiss of life](wiki:Kiss_of_life).(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
In [212 AC](wiki:212_AC), a trial by combat was held at [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls) in the [Riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) after Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell)'s [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) was stolen during his [wedding tourney](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls). Ser [Glendon Flowers](wiki:Glendon_Flowers) was accused of the crime, but Duncan the Tall claimed he had been framed by Lord [Gormon Peake](wiki:Gormon_Peake) to remove him from [Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre)'s path to victory. Daemon allowed Glendon to face him in a [joust](wiki:Joust), and Glendon unhorsed Daemon in the first tilt, proving his innocence. Daemon's humiliating loss was a factor in the quick defeat of the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion).(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
[Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End) during the reign of King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), rebelled against the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) when Prince [Duncan Targaryen](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen) broke his [betrothal](wiki:Betrothal) to Lyonel's daughter. The rebellion was eventually settled in a trial by combat between Lyonel and Aegon's champion, Duncan the Tall, by then a knight of the Kingsguard.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
In [282 AC](wiki:282_AC), Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark) demanded a trial by combat when called to answer for the alleged crimes of his son, [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark), and King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) granted the request. When Rickard, dressed in his armor, was brought to the throne room, however, Aerys named fire as the champion of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). The king declared that in order to prove his innocence, all Rickard had to do was not burn. Aerys then had Rickard suspended from the rafters while a fire was lit beneath him. Rickard cooked in his armor as his cloak burned away and his armor melted in the heat.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

When accused of the murder of Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) by Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) and of the attempted murder of [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) by Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) requests a trial by combat at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). [Bronn](wiki:Bronn) champions for Tyrion and Ser [Vardis Egen](wiki:Vardis_Egen), captain of the [Arryn guard](wiki:House_Arryn_guards), champions for Lysa.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Bronn slays Vardis in the duel and Tyrion is released.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) often rules that disputes should be resolved by trial by combat, usually to the death.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) is accused of murdering [Mycah](wiki:Mycah). Ser [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion) decides to [judge him](wiki:Sandor_Clegane's_trial_by_combat) by combat and loses,(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) therefore decreeing Sandor innocent of that crime.
When Tyrion is accused in [the murder](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of King Joffrey, Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) offers to be his champion in trial by combat.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) represents Tyrion's accuser, Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister).(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Although Oberyn severely wounds Gregor and forces the Mountain to admit to having murdered [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell) and [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) in the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing), Gregor manages to crush his opponent's skull.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

The accusations directed towards [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) and Cersei by the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) make both consider demanding trial by combat. Their status as queens makes it necessary for both to choose from the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) at a time when the order's ranks are depleted. Cersei writes a letter to Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), her brother and the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), currently in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), asking him to return to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to be her champion, but Jaime has the letter burned.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

After learning of Jaime's disappearance, Cersei must choose a new champion from the Kingsguard for her trial by combat.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Ser [Robert Strong](wiki:Robert_Strong) is the eventual choice.(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Trial_by_combat.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
