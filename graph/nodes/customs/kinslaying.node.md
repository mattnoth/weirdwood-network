## Culture

Many nobles and [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) believe this, and thus those who slay their own blood are usually looked down upon or shunned. Across [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), in the religions of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods), the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), and even of the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God), men state that "no man is so accursed as the kinslayer" and that "the kinslayer is accursed in the eyes of gods and men".(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
The stigma and notoriety of kinslaying can go both ways. For example, [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) hates his brother [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) and thinks about killing him, but the stigma and curse associated with kinslaying is what restrains him from doing so.(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) However, when [Tristifer Botley](wiki:Tristifer_Botley), who believes [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) was killed by his brother Euron, says Euron is afraid of being seen as a kinslayer, [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) responds that if Euron heard this, he would murder one of his sons to prove them wrong.(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
In the rare case of twin brothers [Arryk](wiki:Arryk_Cargyll) and [Erryk Cargyll](wiki:Erryk_Cargyll), kinslaying is a subject of romance and songs. While [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom)'s testimony asserts the brothers met a brutal and hate-filled end, [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) claimed they professed their love for one another and died weeping in each other's arms. The singers much prefer the latter tale,(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and it is the version [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) is familiar with long after their deaths.(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Rather than being reviled, the Cargyll twins' names are remembered alongside exalted knights of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) such as [Ryam Redwyne](wiki:Ryam_Redwyne) and Prince [Aemon the Dragonknight](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)).(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
To be a kinslayer does not inherently indicate a deliberate act. After Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) accidentally killed his elder brother [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)) in a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) at [Ashford Meadow](wiki:Ashford_Meadow), he was reckoned a kinslayer by many in the Realm. Although Maekar's son [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) bristled at the accusations, Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) privately admitted that Maekar was a kinslayer, even though he had not meant it.(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Another such instance is the death of Prince [Aelor Targaryen](wiki:Aelor_Targaryen) in a terrible mishap at the hand of his wife and twin sister [Aelora](wiki:Aelora_Targaryen), though it's unknown if she was considered a kinslayer for the incident.(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
According to [George R.R. Martin](wiki:George_R.R._Martin):
There are degrees in kinslaying, as in anything else. Fighting a battle in which a brother dies might be frowned upon, but killing him with your own hand would be considered far worse.(wiki:Kinslaying.cite_ref-grrm_11-0)
