## Origins

The [custom](wiki:Custom) of fosterage is common among the nobility of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). It is seen as a way of establishing friendships and alliances.(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-ssm_1-0) For example, Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) became a second father to his two wards, [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) and [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), during the years they were fostered by Arryn at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Additionally, fosterage is seen as a means of education.(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-ssm_1-1) For example, boys can be fostered out as [squires](wiki:Squire), training for [knighthood](wiki:Knight).(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Most commonly the fostered child is male,(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-ssm_1-2) although it is not unheard of for females to be fostered. The fostered child can be trueborn or [bastard](wiki:Bastard) born.(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Children taken as [hostages](wiki:Hostage) may be called wards; for example, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy).(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) In the [Free City](wiki:Free_City) of [Norvos](wiki:Norvos), children are usually not fostered out.(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
According to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin), a child who is fostered by a lord or lady can make visits home during the years of his fosterage, although these visits would be minimal while the child is still young. After reaching the [age of majority](wiki:Age_of_majority), the fostered child is free to come and go as he or she likes.(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-9)

## Origins

In his youth, King [Harmund II Hoare](wiki:Harmund_II_Hoare) was brought up at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
The [kings of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock), [Tyrion III](wiki:Tyrion_III_Lannister) and [Gerold II Lannister](wiki:Gerold_II_Lannister), took the sons and daughters of newly ennobled [Andal](wiki:Andal) lords as wards and fosterlings.(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
The fourth [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale) from [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn), [Roland I Arryn](wiki:Roland_I_Arryn), was fostered as a boy by an Andal king in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_the_eyrie.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Shortly before his death in early [298 AC](wiki:298_AC), Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) planned to have his young son [Robert](wiki:Robert_Arryn) foster with Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Following Jon's death, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) planned to foster Robert with Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1) Robert's mother, Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), took her son back to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), preventing both plans from coming to fruition.(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

As part of the terms allowing [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) to cross at the [Twins](wiki:Twins), [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) agrees to foster [Big Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)) and [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)) at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Racok4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm), [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son of the late King [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_Baratheon), is a ward of [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) After the death of Renly, [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) [besieges Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), but offers to let the garrison go free if Edric is surrendered. However, Renly's castellan, Ser [Cortnay Penrose](wiki:Cortnay_Penrose), resists in fear of what Stannis would do to Edric.(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) After Cortnay falls to his death,(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Stannis takes the castle and ships Edric to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

The [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant) demand that young Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) be removed from the Eyrie and the care of [Lord Protector](wiki:Lord_Protector) [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), and to be made a ward of Lord [Yohn Royce](wiki:Yohn_Royce). As part of the negotiations, Petyr offers to foster [Harrold Hardyng](wiki:Harrold_Hardyng), Robert's heir, but Harrold's foster-mother, Lady [Anya Waynwood](wiki:Anya_Waynwood), rejects the offer.(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) Petyr convinces his rivals to grant him a year before any decision is made, and later fosters [Gyles Grafton](wiki:Gyles_Grafton) and [Terrance Lynderly](wiki:Terrance_Lynderly) as companions for Robert.(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1)(wiki:Fosterage.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)
