## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), brother of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), becomes suspicious about the paternity of Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)’s three children, [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), and [Tommen](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), believing they had not been fathered by her husband, King Robert I, but instead by her brother, [Ser](wiki:Ser) [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). He investigates the matter with the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), who discovers proof for Cersei's infidelity.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) Before Jon can reveal the secret to the king, he dies.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)
When the king visits [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) with half his court, [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) accidentally witnesses Queen Cersei and Ser Jaime having sex in the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep). In an attempt to keep their secret, Jaime throws Bran from the window of the tower.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)
In [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) discovers Queen Cersei's secret. He informs Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) of the fact, but Baelish's reaction suggests he had been aware for some time already.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Eddard goes to confront Cersei, who confirms her relationship with Jaime.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1) When King Robert I dies, Eddard attempts to prevent Joffrey from taking the [throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), but is betrayed, arrested, and subsequently executed.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)
During his captivity, Eddard is visited by [Varys](wiki:Varys), who reveals he was aware of the relationship between Cersei and Jaime.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)
Before being presented to the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) [khal](wiki:Khal) [Drogo](wiki:Drogo), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) recalls how she had always assumed that she would marry her brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) once she came [of age](wiki:Age_of_majority).(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Stannis Baratheon proclaims himself to be King on Dragonstone, and thereby lays claim to the Iron Throne.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) He has his [maester](wiki:Maester), [Pylos](wiki:Pylos), write numerous copies of a letter in which he proclaims Cersei's three children to be the products of her incestuous relationship with her brother, Jaime.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) These letters are sent to the lords of the Seven Kingdoms, including the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) in [King’s Landing](wiki:King’s_Landing).(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)
While in captivity at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) confirms to [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) that Joffrey, Myrcella, and Tommen are his biological children.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

At [Craster's Keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep), [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) gives birth to her father's son.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) She flees her father's keep with [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly),(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Rasos46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) and eventually reaches [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) with the child.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) makes it clear to both Cersei(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) and Jaime(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) that he is aware of their relationship and the paternity of Cersei's children.
As part of her plot to discredit Queen [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), Dowager Queen Cersei Lannister has disgraced former [maester](wiki:Maester) [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) [torture](wiki:Torture) the [Blue Bard](wiki:Blue_Bard) into confessing how he witnessed Margaery having affairs with lovers. The names he speaks include Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell), Margaery’s brother. However, Cersei knows that the lie will not be believed, on account of Loras's sexual preferences, nor be taken kindly, following his recent military success at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), and insists the Blue Bard changes his confession.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

When [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) discovers Gilly was not only Craster's daughter, but also his wife who gave birth to his son, he agrees to [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow)'s plan to send Gilly south, stating he will not suffer "such abominations" at the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)
Having been taken captive by the Faith, Dowager Queen Cersei Lannister is charged with multiple crimes, including incest. She denies to the [High Septon](wiki:High_Sparrow) that her children were fathered by her brother instead of her husband.(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) During her [walk of atonement](wiki:Walk_of_atonement), the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) call Cersei names, including "brotherfucker".(wiki:Incest.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)
