## Identity

Fish stew appears in two distinct contexts: as the humble soldier's fare at Dragonstone (men soaking black bread in their fish stew at the ACOK prologue feast), and as a heartier form at the Quiet Isle septry in AFFC (thick fish-and-shellfish stew alongside the septry's famous mead and cider). In both cases it marks plainness and austerity — the food of common soldiers and austere monks, not of nobles' feasts.

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

- (Prologue, ACOK — `sources/chapters/acok/acok-prologue.md:287`) Stannis's Dragonstone feast: the lower tables are "crowded with knights, archers, and sellsword captains, tearing apart loaves of black bread to soak in their fish stew." No loud laughter; Stannis "did not permit such."

### A Feast for Crows

- (Brienne VI, AFFC — `sources/chapters/affc/affc-brienne-06.md`) Quiet Isle septry supper: "a thick fish-and-shellfish stew" alongside mead, sweet cider, crusty bread, fresh-churned butter, and honey — the septry's famous mead in its native context.
