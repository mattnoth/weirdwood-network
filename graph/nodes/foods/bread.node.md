## Identity

Bread is the most common staple food across the Seven Kingdoms — served at nearly every meal, from a lord's high table to a peasant's board. Together with salt, bread is the central token of [guest right](guest-right): to eat a host's bread and salt under his roof is to invoke the sacred hospitality compact. Bread also appears with cheese as a basic, humble offering of charity or care.

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

- (Theon II, ACOK — `sources/chapters/acok/acok-theon-02.md:365`) Pyke ironborn feast featuring "a succession of fish stews, black bread, and spiceless goat" — black bread as the emblematic bread of the Iron Islands, austere and unremarkable against the meager feast.
- (Theon VI, ACOK — `sources/chapters/acok/acok-theon-06.md:11`) Theon's untouched pre-sack breakfast: "a platter of oakcakes, honey, and blood sausage" he knocks aside before the taking of Winterfell — food left uneaten at a moment of violence.

### A Game of Thrones

- (Eddard XV, AGOT — `sources/chapters/agot/agot-eddard-15.md:35`) Ned in the black cells: a gaoler brings a clay water jug, "cool and beaded with moisture"; Ned drinks until he nearly vomits. No food is mentioned. He counts days by the water deliveries. When stomach cramps begin, he begs for food — "he was not fed" (`:55`). The Lannisters are using deliberate starvation to weaken him; his only nourishment is water.

As Eddard Stark lies a prisoner under sentence and his daughter Sansa's life hangs in the balance, Varys frames the choice he is offering with a homely image of mercy: "The next visitor who calls on you could bring you bread and cheese and the milk of the poppy for your pain … or he could bring you Sansa's head." Bread and cheese stand here for ordinary comfort and survival, set against the threat of execution. (Eddard XV, AGOT — `sources/chapters/agot/agot-eddard-15.md:155`)

### A Feast for Crows

- (Jaime VI, AFFC — `sources/chapters/affc/affc-jaime-06.md:309`) Hospitality-as-coercion for captive Edmure Tully: Jaime orders "wine and food" plus a singer for the prisoner; "Lew, heat some bathwater for my guest … Peck, wine for Lord Tully. Are you hungry?" Meanwhile Little Lew Piper hollows a stale-bread trencher — bread here as the container for Edmure's first proper meal as prisoner, the trencher's staleness marking his humiliation.

### A Storm of Swords

- (Epilogue, ASOS — `sources/chapters/asos/asos-epilogue.md:11`) Riverlands famine signal: "Between rains, floods, fire, and war, they had lost two harvests" — bread absent as the staple it should be; macro famine registered through its absence.

On the morning of his trial by combat against Ser Gregor Clegane, Tyrion Lannister rises with a hearty appetite and breaks his fast on fried bread, blood sausage, applecakes, and a double helping of eggs cooked with onions and fiery Dornish peppers — a meal that comes "boiling back up" when he watches the Mountain crush Prince Oberyn's skull. (Tyrion X, ASOS — `sources/chapters/asos/asos-tyrion-10.md:101`)

### A Dance with Dragons

- (A Ghost in Winterfell I, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-a-ghost-in-winterfell-01.md:15`) Snowbound garrison rationing-by-rank: "stale bread fried in bacon grease (the lords and knights ate the bacon)" — the parenthetical captures class distinction in a single clause; common soldiers get the grease, lords get the meat.
- (Jon X, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-jon-10.md:195`) Alys/Sigorn wedding feast: "coarse brown bread" as one of the humble wedding provisions, with note that "the last of the butter would be gone within a moon's turn."
- (The Griffin Reborn I, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-griffin-reborn-01.md:135`) JonCon's first breakfast as restored lord: boiled eggs, fried bread, and beans with "the worst wine in the cellar" (wine is for greyscale vinegar-soak, not drinking) — fried bread here as cover for a medical ritual.
- (The Griffin Reborn I, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-griffin-reborn-01.md:81`) Victory feast: "roast meats and fresh-caught fish, washed down with rich red wines" — bread implicit in a celebration meal.
- (Epilogue, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-epilogue.md:197`) Kevan's KL regency supper includes "hot bread and butter" as the final course alongside beef-and-barley soup, quail, pike, turnips, and mushrooms — bread bookending a great man's last meal.
- (The Spurned Suitor I, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-spurned-suitor-01.md:103`) Purple Lotus tavern, Meereen siege: the Tattered Prince's observation that the food at Zahrina's stall is "stale bread" with "unspeakable stew. Grease and salt, with a morsel or two of meat. Dog, she says, but I think rat is more likely." Stale bread as the baseline of siege-poverty food; the stew is more notable than the bread (see rat node).
- (Melisandre I, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-melisandre-01.md:71`) Melisandre orders "nettle tea, a boiled egg, and bread with butter. Fresh bread, if you please, not fried" — a notably plain, precise breakfast from a woman who admits "it is R'hllor who provides nourishment." The insistence on fresh (not fried) captures her fastidiousness; she sends Devan for it while noting she actually needs Jon Snow, not bread.
