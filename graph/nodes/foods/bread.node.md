## Identity

Bread is the most common staple food across the Seven Kingdoms — served at nearly every meal, from a lord's high table to a peasant's board. Together with salt, bread is the central token of [guest right](guest-right): to eat a host's bread and salt under his roof is to invoke the sacred hospitality compact. Bread also appears with cheese as a basic, humble offering of charity or care.

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

As Eddard Stark lies a prisoner under sentence and his daughter Sansa's life hangs in the balance, Varys frames the choice he is offering with a homely image of mercy: "The next visitor who calls on you could bring you bread and cheese and the milk of the poppy for your pain … or he could bring you Sansa's head." Bread and cheese stand here for ordinary comfort and survival, set against the threat of execution. (Eddard XV, AGOT — `sources/chapters/agot/agot-eddard-15.md:155`)

### A Storm of Swords

On the morning of his trial by combat against Ser Gregor Clegane, Tyrion Lannister rises with a hearty appetite and breaks his fast on fried bread, blood sausage, applecakes, and a double helping of eggs cooked with onions and fiery Dornish peppers — a meal that comes "boiling back up" when he watches the Mountain crush Prince Oberyn's skull. (Tyrion X, ASOS — `sources/chapters/asos/asos-tyrion-10.md:101`)
