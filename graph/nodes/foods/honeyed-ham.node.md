## Identity

Honeyed ham is a glazed or honey-cured pork dish served at feasts. In ACOK it appears as a gift from Lady Tanda Stokeworth to Tyrion and Cersei's pre-Blackwater supper — a bribe wrapped in hospitality.

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

- (Tyrion XII, ACOK — `sources/chapters/acok/acok-tyrion-12.md:49`) Lady Tanda Stokeworth's pre-Blackwater supper gift: honeyed ham amid lamprey pie, buttered carrots, white beans and bacon, and roast swan — a bribe of hospitality. "We have Lady Tanda to thank for the pig."

### A Dance with Dragons

- (Tyrion III, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-tyrion-03.md:147`) Road supper in an overgrown yard near Ghoyan Drohe: salt pork and cold white beans washed down with ale — the austere travelling fare Tyrion finds "a pleasant change from all the rich food he had eaten with Illyrio." Pork in its preservation form, stripped of all ceremony.
- (Tyrion IV, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-tyrion-04.md:65`) Shy Maid daily breakfast: Ysilla fries bacon and bakes biscuits over the brazier, "some days bacon and biscuits; some days biscuits and bacon." Tyrion steals a biscuit and gets narrowly missed by Ysilla's wooden spoon; Duck sniffs over the brazier and gets swacked. Bacon as the recurring small comfort of river-journey domesticity.

### A Feast for Crows

- (Arya II, AFFC — `sources/chapters/affc/affc-arya-02.md:91`) Arya stares at "a slice of pale white meat" at supper and is seized by "a terrible suspicion" — she fears she has been fed human flesh from the vaults below the House of Black and White. The kindly man sees her horror and says: "It is pork, child. Only pork." The horror of mistaken identity is pork's sharpest moment in the corpus.
