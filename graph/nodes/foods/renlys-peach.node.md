## Identity

The peach is the single most narratively charged food item in the series — Renly Baratheon produces one at the failed parley with Stannis outside Storm's End and eats it deliberately as a taunt, juice running down his chin. Stannis later says he will "go to my grave thinking of my brother's peach," making it the emblem of everything Renly represented: pleasure, ease, the south's abundance, and the brother Stannis could never understand. The moment is both a character-defining beat for both brothers and a dark foreshadow, since Renly dies that very night.

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

- (Catelyn III, ACOK — `sources/chapters/acok/acok-catelyn-03.md:155`) Renly-Stannis parley outside Storm's End: Renly produces a peach "from Highgarden" and takes a deliberate bite, juice running "from the corner of his mouth," while Stannis fumes — "I did not come here to eat fruit." Renly: "A man should never refuse to taste a peach. He may never get the chance again." That night, Renly is killed by a shadow.

During their meeting near [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) offers his brother [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) a peach as they are parlaying. As his brother's hand slides into his cloak Stannis reaches at once for the hilt of his sword, but before he can draw steel Renly produces a peach. Smiling, Renly offers the peach to Stannis, promising him he has never tasted anything so sweet. When Stannis refuses the peach, Renly advises him,
A man should never refuse to taste a peach. He may never get the chance again. Life is short, Stannis. Remember what the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) say. Winter is coming.(wiki:Renly's_peach.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)
Following [Renly's death](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), Stannis is vexed by the offering of the peach.(wiki:Renly's_peach.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

### A Storm of Swords

- (Davos V, ASOS — `sources/chapters/asos/asos-davos-05.md`) Stannis remembers the peach: "I will go to my grave thinking of my brother's peach" — the fruit becomes Stannis's lifelong emblem of what he lost in Renly.

The memory of Renly with his peach continues to haunt Stannis.(wiki:Renly's_peach.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
