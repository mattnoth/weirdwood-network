## Culture

## Origins

In some stories about [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister)'s acquisition of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), [Lann the Clever](wiki:Lann_the_Clever) filled a cleft in the castle with [mice](wiki:Mice), rats, and other vermin to chase out [House Casterly](wiki:House_Casterly).(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
According to legend, the [Rat Cook](wiki:Rat_Cook) was turned into a monstrous white rat who could only eat his own young.(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
[Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower) is said to have been able to speak with rats and [spiders](wiki:Spider).(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
Large grey rats are common in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown). The [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) was brought to the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), however, by smaller black rats common on ships. [Cats](wiki:Cat) were highly sought after in order to exterminate the vermin.(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysane:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
[Cheese](wiki:Cheese_(ratcatcher)) was a ratcatcher who became an assassin during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Rfaba_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
Rats temporarily disappeared from King's Landing after bodies were burned during the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness).(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)
[The Rat, the Hawk, and the Pig](wiki:The_Rat,_the_Hawk,_and_the_Pig) were a notorious trio.(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
Rats from [Treasure](wiki:Treasure) brought the [grey death](wiki:Grey_death) to [Pentos](wiki:Pentos).(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
The garrison of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) survived on rats, roots, and boot leather during the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End).(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

While [skinchanging](wiki:Skinchanging) into [Summer](wiki:Summer), [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) detects a rat creeping through bush in the [godswood of Winterfell](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
[Xaro Xhoan Daxos](wiki:Xaro_Xhoan_Daxos) informs [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) that the rats of [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) are chewing off their tails in the aftermath of the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying)'s destruction.(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

### A Clash of Kings (ACOK-specific)

- (Catelyn IV, ACOK — `sources/chapters/acok/acok-catelyn-04.md:61`) Storm's End siege backstory: "Stannis ate rats rather than open his gates"; Maester Cressen had warned they "might be forced to eat our dead." Stannis's rat-eating is the canonical emblem of his endurance during Robert's Rebellion siege.

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) eats a rat in the dungeon of the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort).(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Radwd12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)
[Meera Reed](wiki:Meera_Reed) thinks the chunks of meat in blood stew served in the [cave of the three-eyed crow](wiki:Cave_of_the_three-eyed_crow) is rat.(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

- (Reek I, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-reek-01.md:11`) GRIM: Theon eats a live rat in the Dreadfort dungeon — "the warm blood running over his lips," weeping at the sweetness. It had been two or three days since he had last eaten. The belly is "the softest part."
- (Bran III, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-bran-03.md:89`) Cave larder blood stew "thickened with barley and onions and chunks of meat" — Jojen thought it was squirrel, Meera said rat. Almost daily meal in the three-eyed crow's cave.
- (Arya I, AFFC — `sources/chapters/affc/affc-arya-01.md:197`) Arya's failed-consumption courage test in the House of Black and White: she plucks a worm from a skull's eye socket to eat it, but it "melts like a shadow in her hand" (closest failed-food attempt node).
- (Bran III, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-bran-03.md:111`) GRIM/UNCANNY: Summer digs up a severed wight arm "black and covered with hoarfrost" in the snow north of the cave, tears the meat from the bone and cracks the bones for the marrow — wolf instinct overriding the uncanny. "Only then did the arm remember it was dead." Undead meat as the outermost edge of the food register; the wolf does not share Bran's horror.
- (Daenerys V, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-daenerys-05.md:171`) Fall of Astapor: survivors report "we ate cats and rats and leather. A horsehide was a feast" — rat as the last refuge before starvation; the Tattered Prince also suspects the Purple Lotus stew is rat rather than dog (ADWD The Spurned Suitor I, `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-spurned-suitor-01.md:103`).
Lady [Barbrey Dustin](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin) thinks of [maesters](wiki:Maesters) as being grey rats.(wiki:Rat.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)
