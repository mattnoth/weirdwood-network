## Culture

The [spiceflower](wiki:Spiceflower) is a scented [flower](wiki:Plants) found in the [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea) that is used in perfumes.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
The [Ancient Guild of Spicers](wiki:Ancient_Guild_of_Spicers) is one of the three prominent organization of merchant princes within the city of [Qarth](wiki:Qarth).(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
The [Saffron Straits](wiki:Saffron_Straits) are the eastern exit of the [Jade Sea](wiki:Jade_Sea), dividing the continents of [Essos](wiki:Essos) and [Ulthos](wiki:Ulthos).(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rtloiafthe_known_world.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
[Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) is known for its spiced honey [wine](wiki:Wine).(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) At the [Wall](wiki:Wall), the brothers of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) drink hot mulled wine, spiced with cloves and nutmeg.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) [Hippocras](wiki:Hippocras) is made from wine mixed with sugar and spices, most notably cinnamon, and possibly heated.
[Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) like to eat their food with fiery peppers and strange hot spices.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) People from the [Reach](wiki:Reach) claim these spices make Dornishmen hot-tempered, and Dornishwomen wild and wanton.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)
The candles in the [solar](wiki:Solar) of [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) in the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) smell of nutmeg and other costly spices.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
[Sister's stew](wiki:Sister's_stew) is normally spiced with salt and pepper, but wealthy people can also add cloves, nutmeg, and saffron.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
Spices from the Summer Isles are used in the preparation of the [strangler](wiki:Strangler).(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)
[Meereen](wiki:Meereen) has a spice market.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Radwd60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

## Origins

Two of the [Rhoynish Wars](wiki:Rhoynish_Wars), fought between the [Valyrians](wiki:Valyria) and the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar), were named the [first](wiki:Spice_War) and [Second Spice War](wiki:Second_Spice_War).(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
In [50 AC](wiki:50_AC), King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and his [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), a former [Pentoshi](wiki:Pentos) spicemonger named [Rego Draz](wiki:Rego_Draz), instituted a series of luxury taxes on expensive foreign imports, including spices.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
[Elissa Farman](wiki:Elissa_Farman) and the [Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower) brothers, [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_Hightower) and [Norman](wiki:Norman_Hightower), attempted to find a western route to eastern Essos in [56 AC](wiki:56_AC).(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) After facing fierce storms, Elissa and Eustace found new spices on [three small uninhabited islands](wiki:Aegon,_Rhaenys,_and_Visenya_(islands)). Elissa continued her voyage west from there, but Eustace desired to return home and sailed east.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) It is unknown if Elissa succeeded in finding a western route, but many years later, [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) saw an old ship he believed was the [Sun Chaser](wiki:Sun_Chaser) in [Asshai](wiki:Asshai).(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) Unlike Elissa, Corlys made several [voyages](wiki:Great_Voyages) by sailing east to the eastern part of [Essos](wiki:Essos) via the traders' route. Those voyages made Corlys and [House Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) extremely wealthy, to the point of becoming the wealthiest House in Westeros.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) One of the villages on Corlys's home island of [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) developed into a prosperous port town named [Spicetown](wiki:Spicetown),(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) and the Velaryon warship [Spicetown Girl](wiki:Spicetown_Girl) was [named after](wiki:Named_after) the town.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
The [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) hit Westeros in the [winter](wiki:Winter) of [59](wiki:59_AC)–[60 AC](wiki:60_AC). As no remedy was known to cure the deadly [disease](wiki:Disease), several options were tested, such as drinking wine spiced with [snake](wiki:Snake) [venom](wiki:Poison).(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)
Trade boomed in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring). Spices from the east were arriving to the city more frequently than before.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
[Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre) smelled of oranges and limes, with a hint of some strange eastern spice beneath.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)
[House Spicer](wiki:House_Spicer) was founded by a petty [lord](wiki:Lord), a wealthy merchant elevated to [nobility](wiki:Nobility) by Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) The Spicer sigil displays three black pepperpots on a saffron bend.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-27) The merchant's wife, [Maggy the Frog](wiki:Maggy_the_Frog), later kept a fortune-telling tent that was redolent with queer eastern spices.(wiki:Spices.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

Ginger is a costly eastern import, and Illyrio as spice-merchant makes the gift doubly characteristic.
