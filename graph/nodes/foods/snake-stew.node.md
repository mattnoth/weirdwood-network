## Identity

Snake stew is a signature Dornish dish combining seven varieties of snake slow-simmered with dragon peppers, blood oranges, and a dash of actual venom "to give it a good bite." Grilled street-snake with a drop-of-venom sauce is the cheaper street-vendor version. Both forms are culturally charged: they represent Dornish willingness to consume the dangerous and exotic, and they serve as a litmus test for outsider tolerance of Dornish cuisine.

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

- (The Watcher I, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-watcher-01.md:51`) The 7-course feast welcoming Ser Balon Swann to Sunspear: "a savory snake stew, chunks of seven different sorts of snake slow-simmered with dragon peppers and blood oranges and a dash of venom to give it a good bite." Ser Balon Swann tries "only one small spoonful of the stew. Even that made his brow break out in sweat." Areo Hotah tasted none of it but notes he was once equally overwhelmed.

### A Feast for Crows

- (The Soiled Knight I, AFFC — `sources/chapters/affc/affc-the-soiled-knight-01.md:21`) Shadow-city street vendor: "a short man stood in an arched doorway grilling chunks of snake over a brazier." The sauce: "the best snake sauce had a drop of venom in it, he had heard, along with mustard seeds and dragon peppers." Ser Arys Oakheart notes that Myrcella "had taken to Dornish food as quick as she had to her Dornish prince."
