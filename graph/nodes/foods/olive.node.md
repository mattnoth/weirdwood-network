## Identity

Olives appear throughout the series as a marker of Mediterranean-inflected Dornish and Lyseni cuisine, and as a Castle Black provision staple. They appear pickled, in oil, fresh, and in various colors (green, purple, black); the "cracked green olives" detail is specific to Salladhor Saan's Lyseni table.

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

- (Davos II, ASOS — `sources/chapters/asos/asos-davos-02.md:49`) Salladhor Saan's feast aboard ship: "white cheese and a bowl of those cracked green olives we counted earlier" — Lyseni pirate hospitality; Davos is told not to eat all the olives.

### A Dance with Dragons

- (Jon IV, ADWD — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-jon-04.md:23`) Castle Black stores census: "clay jars packed with olives in oil and sealed with wax" among dried peas, figs, walnuts, potted hare, and pickled goods — winter provisions inventory.

### A Feast for Crows

- (The Captain of Guards I, AFFC — `sources/chapters/affc/affc-the-captain-of-guards-01.md:153`) Doran Martell's Water Gardens supper: "a bowl of purple olives, with flatbread, cheese, and chickpea paste" — characteristic Dornish simplicity at the prince's private table.
- (The Princess in the Tower I, AFFC — `sources/chapters/affc/affc-the-princess-in-the-tower-01.md:59`) Arianne's prison meals: "figs or olives or peppers stuffed with cheese" on request from her bed-maid Belandra — comfortable captivity contrasting her total isolation from information.
- (The Queenmaker I, AFFC — `sources/chapters/affc/affc-the-queenmaker-01.md:239`) Desert rest stop: "cheese and fruit … Garin ate olives and spit the stones at Drey" — travel food on the sands.
