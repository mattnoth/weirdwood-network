## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

When he is brought before Lord [Godric Borrell](wiki:Godric_Borrell) on [Sweetsister](wiki:Sweetsister), [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) is served sister's stew in a trencher hollowed out of a stale loaf. Davos has tasted sister's stew before, as it is served in every inn and tavern of the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters). Godric insists this stew made by his granddaughter [Gella](wiki:Gella) is better. Davos is grateful for the stew, as he should now have [guest right](wiki:Guest_right).(wiki:Sister's_stew.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Davos notes that there are stranger [spices](wiki:Spices) than salt in this particular stew. Godric confirms it has saffron from [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) and cracked black pepper from [Volantis](wiki:Volantis). Lord Borrell laughs, explaining he had taken pepper, cloves, nutmeg, and saffron off a [sloe-eyed maid](wiki:Sloe-Eyed_Maid).(wiki:Sister's_stew.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

## Provenance — exotic spices (book-cite overlay)

Godric Borrell's sister's stew is spiced with **saffron from Qarth** and **cracked black pepper from Volantis** — both far costlier than the salt of ordinary stew. The spices come from a looted hoard: Lord Godric took "pepper, cloves, nutmeg, and saffron" off a wrecked Braavos-bound ship (the [sloe-eyed maid](wiki:Sloe-Eyed_Maid)) that a gale smashed against his rocks. He boasts of having "forty chests" of the pepper and "a pound of saffron." (ADWD, Davos I — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-davos-01.md:121` and `:123`)

> "Aye. From Qarth. There's pepper too." … "Cracked black pepper from Volantis, nothing finer. Take as much as you require if you're feeling peppery. I've got forty chests of it. Not to mention cloves and nutmeg, and a pound of saffron. Took it off a sloe-eyed maid."

— ADWD (Davos I), `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-davos-01.md:123` · the wreckers' pepper/saffron hoard that flavors the stew.  _(capture: harvest pass, food)_
