## Origins

The most famous Unsullied were the [Three Thousand of Qohor](wiki:Three_Thousand_of_Qohor), who held back a [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* over fifty thousand strong. Only six hundred Unsullied survived, having killed twelve thousand of the Dothraki. To honor the Unsullied, the surviving Dothraki rode in before the Unsullied line, throwing their cut braids down in front of them.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Unsullied have since made up, or at least been supported by, the [city watch](wiki:City_watch_(Qohor)) of [Qohor](wiki:Qohor).(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

[Lysaro Rogare](wiki:Lysaro_Rogare) bought a thousand Unsullied from [Astapor](wiki:Astapor) in [135 AC](wiki:135_AC) after the death of his father, [Lysandro Rogare](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare). When Lysaro fled [Lys](wiki:Lys), he surrounded himself with one hundred of the [slave](wiki:Slave) soldiers. Lysaro was later caught in [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys) with only twenty Unsullied, as he had been forced to sell the rest along the way. The twenty Unsullied fought to the end on the docks of the town and perished. Lysaro was captured and sent back to Lys in chains.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

The three brothers of [Missandei](wiki:Missandei),(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) including [Mossador](wiki:Mossador) and [Marselen](wiki:Marselen), were taken from [Naath](wiki:Naath) by raiders from the [Basilisk Isles](wiki:Basilisk_Isles) and sold into slavery in Astapor,(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) where they were trained as Unsullied.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

During the voyage to [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) to join up with Magister [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis), Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) convinces [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) to change their destination to [Astapor](wiki:Astapor) to buy Unsullied. He tells her about the [Three Thousand of Qohor](wiki:Three_Thousand_of_Qohor), and to buy the Unsullied they could use the trade goods being carried onboard.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

Dany is shown Unsullied by [Kraznys mo Nakloz](wiki:Kraznys_mo_Nakloz), one of the [Good Masters](wiki:Good_Masters), in the Astapori [Plaza of Pride](wiki:Plaza_of_Pride), where ten ranks of a hundred Unsullied stand. More than half are of [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) stock, while others appear to be from [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) and even the same ethnicity as their slavers, while others are from peoples she does not recognize.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

After [Arstan Whitebeard](wiki:Arstan_Whitebeard) tells Kraznys that even brave men show fear when faced with death or maiming, the slaver whips and mutilates Unsullied who do not react. Daenerys is horrified by the training regime which the Unsullied endure. Dany is told eight thousand Unsullied are available, but they are not cheap because their training takes years. Arstan tells her that [slavery](wiki:Slavery) is held to be an abomination in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), and using slave soldiers would do great dishonor to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). Kraznys says that a [corsair king](wiki:Corsair_king) was also recently interested in purchasing Unsullied.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

Dany later tells Kraznys through the translating slave [Missandei](wiki:Missandei) that she wants all the Unsullied, including six centuries, the eight thousand, and those still in training. Eight slavers are hesitant to sell those in training since if they fail in battle they will shame them, and they would not have any to sell to the next buyer. Dany tells them that she will pay the same for those in training. This provokes an argument among the slavers, some liking having the gold now instead of the future, and others worried about the reputation of the Unsullied if they fail. The eldest of the slavers, [Grazdan](wiki:Grazdan_mo_Ullhor), agrees to sell Daenerys all the Unsullied for her black [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Drogon](wiki:Drogon), her trade goods, and her ships. Dany is given Missandei as a token. Missandei tells Dany of the Unsullied's unflinching discipline, and the slave girl reveals that three were her brothers.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

When Dany brings her whole company to the Plaza of Pride for the transaction, Kraznys advises her that her Unsullied are inexperienced. They exchange Drogon's chain and a whip with nine ends, the harpy's fingers. Daenerys, however, then has Drogon kill the slaver with [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire). During the ensuing [fall of Astapor](wiki:Fall_of_Astapor), the Unsullied refuse the slavers' orders to defend their masters. Dany commands the Unsullied to slay the Good Masters, their soldiers, and any man that wears a *[tokar](wiki:Tokar)* or holds a whip, and to strike the chains off every slave.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

When Dany later arrives outside [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai), it is the Unsullied under [Grey Worm](wiki:Grey_Worm) that will attack the Yunkai'i from right and left while her horse attack the center. The [battle near Yunkai](wiki:Battle_near_Yunkai) is won with less than a dozen losses in part because the [Stormcrows](wiki:Stormcrows) defect to Dany and the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons) are drunk.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

The Unsullied commander, [Grey Worm](wiki:Grey_Worm), is among those present during planning for the [siege of Meereen](wiki:Siege_of_Meereen). Daenerys tells him she will not throw away Unsullied lives in storming the city walls. When a plan is presented to invade [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) through the sewers, Daenerys thinks it would not make use of the Unsullied's discipline.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Some Unsullied are among those that invade Meereen through the sewers and free [pit fighters](wiki:Pit_fighters) in an uprising of slaves.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) The Unsullied crush Meereenese resistance and punish murderers, looters, and rapers.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

The [Qartheen](wiki:Qarth) master of the *[Indigo Star](wiki:Indigo_Star)* tells Daenerys that [Cleon](wiki:Cleon), the new king in Astapor, is training new Unsullied from the highborn boys in Astapor.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)

### A Dance with Dragons

In [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell) sees Unsullied spearmen in ornate armor and [tiger](wiki:Tiger)-skin cloaks guarding the [triarchs of Volantis](wiki:Triarchs_of_Volantis).(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Cleon marches against Yunkai with his new Unsullied, but they are defeated in a [battle at the Horns of Hazzat](wiki:Battle_at_the_Horns_of_Hazzat).(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) During the [siege of Astapor](wiki:Siege_of_Astapor), these Unsullied are butchered by the [Windblown](wiki:Windblown), the [Company of the Cat](wiki:Company_of_the_Cat), and a [Ghiscari legion](wiki:Ghiscari_legion) from [New Ghis](wiki:New_Ghis).(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

Some of the Unsullied in Daenerys's service reclaim their birth names, while others choose new names. Several Unsullied, such as [Stalwart Shield](wiki:Stalwart_Shield), [Duran](wiki:Duran), and [Mossador](wiki:Mossador), are murdered in Meereen by the [Sons of the Harpy](wiki:Sons_of_the_Harpy).(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) The queen assigns her Unsullied to guard the city walls and her person, and directs [Skahaz mo Kandaq](wiki:Skahaz_mo_Kandaq) to create a Meereenese city watch, the [Brazen Beasts](wiki:Brazen_Beasts),(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) consisting of [freedmen](wiki:Freedmen) and [shavepates](wiki:Shavepates).(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)

Grey Worm and fifty of the Unsullied aid refugees outside of Meereen's walls and help with burning the dead.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) [Hero](wiki:Hero), a captain of the Unsullied, is among the hostages sent to the Yunkish camp when Daenerys makes peace with the Yunkai'i.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Daenerys disappears from [Daznak's Pit](wiki:Daznak's_Pit) on [Drogon](wiki:Drogon)'s back.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Her new husband, King [Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq), attempts to gain control of the Unsullied but they refuse to obey him.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) is able to convince them to follow him if Yunkai should attack Meereen.(wiki:Unsullied.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
