## Origins

### Establishment

The City Watch was established under the [Targaryen dynasty](wiki:House_Targaryen) as early as the reign of [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). After Maegor's death, Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) arrested the Commander of the City Watch.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Command of the City Watch was later given to Lord [Qarl Corbray](wiki:Qarl_Corbray).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) At the command of Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), the City Watch combed [Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom) in search of [Tom the Strummer](wiki:Tom_the_Strummer) to make him the court singer.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Lord Qarl later succumbed to the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) in [59 AC](wiki:59_AC), with many of the City Watch stricken as well, leading to the streets of King's Landing falling into anarchy.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### Daemon and the Dance of the Dragons

Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) commanded the watch from [104](wiki:104_AC)–[105 AC](wiki:105_AC). He instituted their uniform use of equipment, including the golden cloaks from which they get their name. The weapons he issued were dirk, short sword, and cudgel. There were two thousand gold cloaks at the time.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the officers of the City Watch were loyal to Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) and the [greens](wiki:Greens), but the rank-and-file gold cloaks supported their former commander, Daemon, a leader of the [blacks](wiki:Blacks). Ser [Luthor Largent](wiki:Luthor_Largent) was appointed commander at the start of the war, but Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), named his son Ser [Gwayne](wiki:Gwayne_Hightower) as Luthor's second.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) During the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing), Luthor led a coup in support of the blacks and personally killed Gwayne Hightower.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

After Luthor was killed during the [riots in King's Landing](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), Ser [Balon Byrch](wiki:Balon_Byrch) became the new commander.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) The "[Mudfoot](wiki:Mudfoot)" garrison, stationed at the [River Gate](wiki:River_Gate) or "Mud Gate", joined the [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight) and would-be kingmaker [Perkin the Flea](wiki:Perkin_the_Flea), declared their allegiance to [Trystane Truefyre](wiki:Trystane_Truefyre) and flew his banner above the River Gate. The garrison stationed at the [Gate of the Gods](wiki:Gate_of_the_Gods) was overwhelmed by Perkin's men.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) Twenty gold cloaks accompanied Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) when she fled the capital, but half of them deserted her on the [Rosby road](wiki:Rosby_road) and the remainder stayed at [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) when she sailed for [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2) Perkin became commander of the City Watch after a pardon from King [Aegon II](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Many gold cloaks died during the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) and the [Moon of Madness](wiki:Moon_of_Madness), so Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) added five hundred of his own men to the City Watch.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) During the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring), [George Graceford](wiki:George_Graceford) admitted that Ser [Lucas Leygood](wiki:Lucas_Leygood), the gold cloaks' commander, and six of the seven gate captains had conspired against King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) and Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

### Dragonless Targaryens

The reputation of the City Watch hit an all time low during the reign of King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen). Aegon promoted his lickspittles within the Watch and used them as go-betweens to make sure the brothels were available for his lusts. It took more than a year for Aegon's son and sucessor, [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), to repair the City Watch after Aegon's death.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

### House Baratheon

When the Targaryens were overthrown during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), the new king, [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), continued the institution of the gold cloaks. [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt) became their commander after the death of [Manly Stokeworth](wiki:Manly_Stokeworth).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) During his rule as commander of the gold cloaks, Janos was known for taking bribes and selling positions and promotions. By the end of his tenure, over half the officers were paying him part of their salaries. Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), discovered this, and had two men who were willing to come forward to testify against Janos, but both were found dead a short time later. Robert recognized Janos's corruption, but allowed him to remain in his position, arguing his successor might be worse. The king's brother, Lord [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), felt Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) had convinced Robert to retain Janos.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

During the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney), the gold cloaks are hard-pressed to keep the king's peace. The [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), commands Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) to find funds to hire more watchmen, and lends twenty of his own [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) guardsmen to help for the tourney's duration.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

After Robert's death, Petyr tells Eddard he will make certain the gold cloaks are loyal to him in the power struggle against Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), with a bribe of six thousand gold coins.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) In truth, Petyr bribes Janos to support the queen, which is ultimately successful when Janos and one hundred gold cloaks accompany Eddard to the throne room and betray him, killing the [Winterfell men](wiki:House_Stark_guards).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Janos is rewarded by being named [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

After being dismissed from the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) kills two of the watchmen(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) that Janos sends to arrest him.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) Gold cloaks bring Eddard to the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), where King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) has him executed.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

Joffrey considers leading the City Watch into the field against his uncle, [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Six gold cloaks come upon [Yoren](wiki:Yoren)'s band of [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) recruits on the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) with the intent of seizing [Gendry](wiki:Gendry), a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), but they are driven away.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

On Cersei's orders, Janos sends his right hand man, [Allar Deem](wiki:Allar_Deem), to murder the infant [Barra](wiki:Barra), another royal bastard.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

Commander Slynt is exiled to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) by the acting Hand, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), for his corruption and next potential betrayal, and Tyrion arranges for Allar to be thrown overboard.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1) Janos's replacement is Ser [Jacelyn Bywater](wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater), an honorable veteran of [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), who helps Tyrion prepare the city's defenses in the face of impending invasion by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

Along with forty knights and squires including [Hobber Redwyne](wiki:Hobber_Redwyne), three hundred gold cloaks escort Petyr Baelish to [Bitterbridge](wiki:Bitterbridge) on his mission to treat with the [Tyrells](wiki:House_Tyrell).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

During the [riots in King's Landing](wiki:Riots_in_King's_Landing), Ser Jacelyn and a wedge of mounted lancers charge down the streets in an effort to defend the royal party, while numerous others form lines of spears before the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk). In total, nine gold cloaks are slain and forty wounded.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

Tyrion sends Jacelyn to take Prince [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) into custody at [Rosby](wiki:Rosby), confronting his [sworn shield](wiki:Sworn_shield), Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount), without bloodshed, and seizing Rosby to hold him there.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

Towards the end of his term, Janos had tripled the number of the gold cloaks, making them six thousand strong.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) Despite this, the new watchmen are poorly trained, and many are drunks, brutes, or cravens who joined for the promise of bread. Also, as Kingslanders, few have love for [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) after the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) Tyrion believes that only a quarter of them (fifteen hundred) are reliable as actual soldiers.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

When Stannis arrives and the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater) begins, the Watch constitutes the bulk of the defending force.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) When King Joffrey withdraws from the walls, the men break. Jacelyn attempts to rally them, but the Commander is killed by his own men on the cusp of success.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Janos Slynt uses his experience leading the gold cloaks during his candidacy to become the new [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) is eventually chosen instead, however.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Twoscore gold cloaks guard the plaza of the Great Sept during Tywin's funeral.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

Ser Addam is relieved to leave his troubling duties to join Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) in a mopping-up campaign in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) In his place Cersei appoints the illiterate hedge knight [Osfryd Kettleblack](wiki:Osfryd_Kettleblack),(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) who is later removed by the [small council](wiki:Small_council) after Cersei's arrest by the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). In Osfryd's place, they promote [Humfrey Waters](wiki:Humfrey_Waters) to lead the gold cloaks.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

The new Hand of the King, Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell), adds a hundred [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) men to the City Watch.(wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)
