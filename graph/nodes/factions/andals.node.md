## Culture

### Religion[ ]

The Andals followed the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), believing that the Seven once walked the hills of [Andalos](wiki:Andalos) in human form.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Displaying a seven-pointed star,(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) they brought their religion across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) They chiseled axes and stars in the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn),(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) although this practice declined as they expanded beyond the Vale.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### Social hierarchy[ ]

The Andals were led by war chiefs, warlords, and petty kings.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) The [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) opposed [incest](wiki:Incest)(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) and [slavery](wiki:Slavery).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Regarding [inheritance](wiki:Inheritance), Andal tradition holds that the rights of a trueborn son come before those of a daughter.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
[Knighthood](wiki:Knight) came to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) with the Andals.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) [Trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) was practiced in Andalos.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

### Military[ ]

The Andals are said to have learned [ironworking](wiki:Iron) from the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Andals used iron weapons and plate against the native tribes of what would become [Andalos](wiki:Andalos),(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) and their [steel](wiki:Steel) swords and iron ringmail(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) also surpassed the [bronze](wiki:Bronze) equipment of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) Andals were skilled craftsmen, especially their blacksmiths and stonemasons.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)
Ancient Andal warriors painted and carved seven-pointed stars on their flesh.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) The Andals brought the warrior tradition of the [knight](wiki:Knight) to Westeros.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)
Unlike the First Men, the Andals were fearless(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) seafarers who used longships.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)
Like the First Men, the early Andals built square towers and keeps, with round fortifications coming later.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Andals built walled towns and stout stone-and-timber castles to protect their coasts.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) Andals also fortified some of their [septs](wiki:Sept).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)

## Origins

### Andals in Essos[ ]

The Andals were a tall and fair-haired people(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) said to have originated in the [Axe](wiki:Axe) of northern [Essos](wiki:Essos),(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4) although some say they came from south of the [Silver Sea](wiki:Silver_Sea).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) They migrated and established [Andalos](wiki:Andalos) in northwestern Essos, stretching south as far as the [Flatlands](wiki:Flatlands) and the [Velvet Hills](wiki:Velvet_Hills) and engaging in conflict with the tribes that inhabited those lands, such as the so-called "[hairy men](wiki:Hairy_men)".(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5) Legend tells that they were visited by the incarnations of the [Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), who crowned their first king, [Hugor of the Hill](wiki:Hugor_of_the_Hill).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) They learned the art of working [iron](wiki:Iron) from the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar) who lived along the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)
[Septons](wiki:Septon) and [septas](wiki:Septa) teach that the Seven had promised Hugor and his descendants great kingdoms in a foreign land, and this is why the Andals eventually left Essos for [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6) Maesters, however, generally believe that the Andals fled because of the expanding [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-7) The Valyrians established [Myr](wiki:Myr) in place of a walled Andal town,(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) and many of the Valyrian colonists of [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) intermarried with local Andals.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_pentos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Archmaester [Perestan](wiki:Perestan) suggests that [Norvos](wiki:Norvos) was originally founded by Andals.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) Before the [Scouring of Lorath](wiki:Scouring_of_Lorath) by Valyrian [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlord), the [Lorathi](wiki:Lorath) isles were ruled by [Qarlon the Great](wiki:Qarlon_the_Great), who desired to be [King of All Andals](wiki:King_of_All_Andals).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

### Migration to Westeros[ ]

It is unknown when the Andals sailed west across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) from Andalos; some sources date the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) to six thousand years ago.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rrpgguardiansorder.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) According to the [True History](wiki:True_History), it occurred four thousand years ago, and some [maesters](wiki:Maesters) instead claim it was only two thousand years ago.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)
Andals first landed in the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers) and attacked the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) living in the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn). They burned [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) groves, hacked down [heart trees](wiki:Heart_tree), and slaughtered the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest). Everywhere they proclaimed the triumph of [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) over the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2)
A hill in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), now known to the Westerosi as [High Heart](wiki:High_Heart), was sacred to the children of the forest. There, the Andal king [Erreg](wiki:Erreg) the [Kinslayer](wiki:Kinslayer) cut down the children’s grove of thirty-one [weirwoods](wiki:Weirwood).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) It is said that the First Men killed half of the children of the forest with [bronze](wiki:Bronze) blades, and the Andals finished the job with [iron](wiki:Iron).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)
After several hundred years of war, Andal warlords and adventurers conquered or married into the southern kingdoms of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) and created their own. They eventually conquered even the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands), though the Faith of the Seven was unable to take root in the archipelago.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-8) Only the [Kings of Winter](wiki:King_in_the_North) resisted the Andals successfully, stopping them at [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin) and preventing them from conquering the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3)(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-9) After fending off an Andal invasion led by [Argos Sevenstar](wiki:Argos_Sevenstar) in the [Battle of the Weeping Water](wiki:Battle_of_the_Weeping_Water), King [Theon Stark](wiki:Theon_Stark) responded by raiding the coast of Andalos.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2)
Due to migration and invasion, little is left of the original Andal culture in Andalos in Essos.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-33)

### Andal Dominance[ ]

Despite their victory in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), the Andals were outnumbered by the native [First Men](wiki:First_Men). The early Andal kings thus often tolerated worship of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) in [godswoods](wiki:Godswood) to prevent religious turmoil,(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-10) although the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) came to dominate southern [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) over time. To solidify their right to rule, Andal kings often married the wives and daughters of defeated First Men kings.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-11) The Andals adopted the First Men's practice of the [first night](wiki:First_night).(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) According to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin), hardly any Westerosi are pure Andal or First Man after thousands of years of interbreeding.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-35)
[Runes](wiki:Runes) were used by [First Men](wiki:First_Men) in antiquity, but not by the Andals. Histories of the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age) and the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes) were written by [septons](wiki:Septon) and [maesters](wiki:Maesters) after the Andals came to Westeros, sometimes thousands of years after the times described.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-12)(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)
According to legend, at some point in time at the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort), the [Rat Cook](wiki:Rat_Cook) served an Andal king, possibly [Tywell II Lannister](wiki:Tywell_II_Lannister) or [Oswell I Arryn](wiki:Oswell_I_Arryn), a pie made of bacon and, unknown to the king, the king's son.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) King [Sherrit](wiki:Sherrit) is said to have cursed Andals while at the Nightfort.(wiki:Andals.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)
