## Origins

The [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) [rebelled](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) against King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) when the king married [his eldest daughter](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) to [his eldest son and heir](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC).(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) After Aenys and his family fled King's Landing to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), the [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) chapter of the Warrior's Sons seized the gates of the city, giving them control over who may enter or leave the capital, and drove the workers from the unfinished [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). They also fortified the [Hill of Rhaenys](wiki:Hill_of_Rhaenys), ensuring the [Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Sept_of_Remembrance) could withstand the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8)(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Following the death of King Aenys I on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) in [42 AC](wiki:42_AC), his half-brother, [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), claimed the crown. Maegor traveled to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), where Dowager Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) challenged those who questioned her son's right to rule. The captain of the Warrior's Sons, Ser [Damon Morrigen](wiki:Damon_Morrigen), met this challenge, and the parties agreed to a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven). Maegor was the only survivor of the fourteen combatants.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

With Maegor declared as the winner, the Warrior's Sons who witnessed the fight dropped their knees in submission and returned to the Sept of Remembrance, where they began to pray and debate whether to accept Maegor as their king, since the [gods](wiki:The_Seven) had granted him victory, or to fight on as they were bound by oath to the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I)). Twenty-nine days after the trial of seven, the Sept of Remembrance and all the Warrior's Sons in it were [burned](wiki:Burning_of_the_Sept_of_Remembrance) down by Maegor with his [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Balerion](wiki:Balerion).(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9)

Thirteen thousand Poor Fellows and hundreds of knights of the Warrior's Sons joined forces with rebel lords of the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) and [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) to fight against Maegor's forces at the [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept). During [this fierce battle](wiki:Battle_at_the_Great_Fork) at the Great Fork of the [Blackwater](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), the Faith lost when Maegor and his [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), left death in their wake.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

In [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), the order elected Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett) as its new [Grand Captain](wiki:Grand_Captain), who rode for Oldtown to seek the blessing of the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I)). Meanwhile, Maegor raised [a set of laws](wiki:King_Maegor's_laws) which outlawed the orders of the Faith Militant,(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-10) and forbade holy men from carrying arms.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) That same year,(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-11) the Warrior's Sons of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) surrounded the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept) to protect it from the impending arrival of the King Maegor, Queen Dowager [Visenya](wiki:Visenya), their dragons, and the royal army. However, when they arrived by dawn, they found the city gates open and the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Maegor_I)) suddenly dead. All of the Warrior's Sons were disarmed and arrested under orders of Lord [Martyn Hightower](wiki:Martyn_Hightower), who opened the gates before Balerion and [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) could burn the city.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-11)

The captive Warrior's Sons were given a choice by Maegor: renounce the order and be permitted to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) or die as martyrs of the Faith. Three-quarters of the captive chose [the Wall](wiki:The_Wall), while the remainder died. Seven of their number, famous knights and sons of lords were given the honor of having Maegor behead them himself with [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre). The rest were beheaded by their own former sworn brothers. Only one of the condemned received a pardon: Ser [Morgan Hightower](wiki:Morgan_Hightower), brother of Lord Martyn and commander of the local chapter of the order. The pardon has led to rumors still persisting today, suggesting he had murdered the High Septon at his brother's request,(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) as he had been seen entering and leaving the chambers of the High Septon in the night of his death.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-12)

The [new High Septon](wiki:Pater) disbanded the Stars and Swords, while Maegor granted the surviving members of the Faith Militant til the year's end to surrender their weapons and give up their rebellion.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-13) Afterward, those who remained defiant would find bounties on their heads: a [gold dragon](wiki:Currency) for the head of any Warrior's Son, and a silver stag for the scalp of each [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows).(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

In [46 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Maegor brought two thousand skulls from his campaign against the Faith back to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), claiming they were the heads of Warrior's Sons and [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows). However, many suspected they were the skulls of innocent [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk).(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)

The end of the Warrior's Sons began following the death of Maegor I and the ascension of [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC). Although much reduced in numbers, the Warrior's Sons and Poor Fellows were still present. They remained restless and eager to restore their orders. King Jaehaerys sent [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Barth](wiki:Barth) to Oldtown to speak with the High Septon. Eventually, it was agreed that the Iron Throne would always protect and defend the Faith, if the last few Stars and Swords would put down their weapons, and if the Faith agreed to accept justice from the Iron Throne instead of the Faith. As a result, the Faith Militant was disbanded.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

## Organization

Before their disbandment during the reign of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), the Warrior's Sons were permanently stationed at chapterhouses in several major cities and towns. The Warrior's Sons had one overall commander known as a [Grand Captain](wiki:Grand_Captain).

At the outbreak of the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC), the major chapters were:(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

- **[Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown)**(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-7)
- **[King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing)**: Home to seven hundred Warrior's Sons, this chapterhouse was commanded by Ser [Damon Morrigen](wiki:Damon_Morrigen), [Grand Captain](wiki:Grand_Captain) of the Warrior's Sons, at the start of the reign of King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). All were killed by Maegor during the [burning of the Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Burning_of_the_Sept_of_Remembrance).(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)
- **[Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport)**: commanded by Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett), who later replaced Damon Morrigen as Grand Captain after Morrigen died.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) Home to at least one hundred Warrior's Sons, as Ser Joffrey rode with a hundred men to seek the blessing of the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I)).(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)
- **[Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown)**: Home to at least two hundred Warrior's Sons. In [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), when King Maegor and Dowager Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) threatened to burn [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) and the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept), two hundred Warrior's Sons spilled from their chapterhouse to defend the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I)) at the Starry Sept, under the command of Ser [Morgan Hightower](wiki:Morgan_Hightower). Arrested on the orders of Lord [Martyn Hightower](wiki:Martyn_Hightower), and given a trial at Oldtown by Maegor.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) Three-quarters decided to [take the black](wiki:Night's_Watch), the others were executed.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)
- **[Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept)**: Home to at least two hundred Warrior's Sons, who marched to the [Battle at the Great Fork](wiki:Battle_at_the_Great_Fork) in [43 AC](wiki:43_AC).(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7)

[White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), despite being one of the only five settlements in Westeros large enough to be considered a "city", did not host a chapterhouse of the Warrior's Sons, even though [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) follow the Seven.

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

After the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) in [300 AC](wiki:300_AC), the ruling King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), under the edict of his mother and [Regent](wiki:Regent) [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), overturns [Maegor the Cruel](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen)'s law which forbade holy men from being armed, and the orders of the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) are reborn.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Over a hundred knights have so far pledged their swords and lives to the Warrior's Sons and obey the new High Septon, known by many as the [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow).(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister), Lord of [Darry](wiki:Darry), renounce his titles, land, and [bride](wiki:Amerei_Frey) to serve the Warrior's Sons and the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Cersei observes the Warrior's Sons' rainbow-striped robes,(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) rainbow stripes on their swordbelts,(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) and crystals on the crests of their greathelms.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

### A Dance with Dragons

Warrior's Sons and [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) escort Cersei during her [walk of atonement](wiki:Walk_of_atonement). The knights of Ser [Theodan Wells](wiki:Theodan_Wells) wear silver plate atop [hair shirts](wiki:Hair_shirt), and their kite shields depict the crystal sword.(wiki:Warrior's_Sons.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)
