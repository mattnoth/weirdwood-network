## Culture

The crannogmen are so called for their habit of living in small villages formed of reeds and thatch that sit atop floating islands in the mire (crannogs). They are a poor people and seldom leave their lands, subsisting on fishing and [frogging](wiki:Frog).(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) Some claim the crannogmen's smaller size is because of intermarriage with the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest), while others attribute their size to poor [nutrition](wiki:Food).(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_crannogmen_of_the_neck.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
Crannogmen are talented hunters and warriors. Despite their short stature(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Rasos9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) and somewhat primitive lifestyle, they have proven a notoriously difficult people to conquer. They wield nets, [bronze](wiki:Bronze) knives, three-pronged frog spears, and round leather shields.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Rasos9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) They use guerrilla tactics and have a mastery of their difficult terrain. According to those who have fought them, their houses, even their castles, move.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Crannogmen are looked down upon by most other people of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) for their tactics, which are viewed as cowardly.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_crannogmen_of_the_neck.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) [Ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) claim that crannogmen [poison](wiki:Poison) their weapons and smear them with feces and worse things. Even a graze by a poisoned arrow can be deadly.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) The swamp-dwellers are disparaged as "frogeaters" and "mudman",(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) while ironborn refer to them as bog devils.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Crannogmen are well acquainted with [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) of the [Twins](wiki:Twins), who control the crossing of the [Green Fork](wiki:Green_Fork).(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) [Riverfolk](wiki:Rivermen) allege that crannogmen can breathe water, have green teeth, and have webbed hands and feet like frogs.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_crannogmen_of_the_neck.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

## Origins

The histories say that the crannogmen grew close to the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) in the days when the [greenseers](wiki:Greenseer) tried to bring the [hammer of the waters](wiki:Hammer_of_the_waters) down upon the Neck.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) The crannogmen and their [Marsh Kings](wiki:Marsh_King) held [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin) against [southron](wiki:Southron) invaders.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) They swore oaths of fealty to the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) thousands of years ago, when King [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark_(king)) slew the last Marsh King and took his daughter as wife.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_crannogmen_of_the_neck.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
The crannogmen are now ruled by [House Reed](wiki:House_Reed) from [Greywater Watch](wiki:Greywater_Watch). Other families of crannogmen are Houses [Blackmyre](wiki:House_Blackmyre), [Boggs](wiki:House_Boggs_(north)), [Cray](wiki:House_Cray), [Fenn](wiki:House_Fenn), [Greengood](wiki:House_Greengood), [Peat](wiki:House_Peat), and [Quagg](wiki:House_Quagg).(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
They have a feud with [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey), who have ruled the lands immediately to the south in the northern [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) for the last six centuries.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

[Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) leads a host of [northmen](wiki:North) south to rescue his father, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Robb sends a message to [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed), Lord of [Greywater Watch](wiki:Greywater_Watch), tasking the crannogmen with guarding the [causeway](wiki:Causeway) in case [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) march north.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Meera](wiki:Meera_Reed) and [Jojen Reed](wiki:Jojen_Reed) travel to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to attend the [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast_(299)).(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
[Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), [Lord Captain of the Iron Fleet](wiki:Lord_Captain_of_the_Iron_Fleet), achieves the [fall of Moat Cailin](wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin),(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) while his nephew, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), leads the [capture of Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Theon intends to hold Meera and Jojen as hostages to stay Howland's hand, but the Reed children vanish from Winterfell.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Meera and Jojen tell [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) about [a crannogman](wiki:Howland_Reed) and the [Knight of the Laughing Tree](wiki:Knight_of_the_Laughing_Tree).(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)
Robb, now [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), tasks [Galbart Glover](wiki:Galbart_Glover) and [Maege Mormont](wiki:Maege_Mormont) with sailing on [Mallister](wiki:House_Mallister) longships into the [Neck](wiki:Neck). The envoys are to inform Howland and the crannogmen of Robb's plan to recover [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin).(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Robb is murdered by Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) during the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), however.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

The [poisoned](wiki:Poison) arrows of crannogmen weaken the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) garrison at Moat Cailin, so Victarion wears heavy chain mail over boiled leather for protection.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) After the death of his brother [Balon](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), the [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North), Victarion leads most of the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) back to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) for the [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk).(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Ralf Kenning](wiki:Ralf_Kenning), left in command of Moat Cailin by Victarion, is mortally wounded by a poisoned arrow.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) sends [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) to resolve the [siege of Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin), offering the remaining ironborn food and safe passage if they surrender unarmed. As Theon approaches arrows fly at him from unseen attackers in the bog, but he is able to enter the gatehouse without harm.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) Theon convinces the ironmen to surrender, but Ramsay executes the captives anyway.(wiki:Crannogmen.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)
