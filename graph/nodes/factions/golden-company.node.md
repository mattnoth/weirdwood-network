## Origins

### Foundation

The Golden Company was founded by Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers), called "Bittersteel", a legitimized [bastard](wiki:Bastard) of King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen). Aegor had fled [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) with the younger sons of [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) at the end of the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion). When Bittersteel saw the support of [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) ebb away as exiled [lords](wiki:Lord) and [knights](wiki:Knight) signed on with sellsword companies, such as the [Ragged Standard](wiki:Ragged_Standard) or the [Maiden's Men](wiki:Maiden's_Men), he decided to create his own sellsword company in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC) in order to bind the exiles together.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

The Golden Company's reputation was quickly established when [Qohor](wiki:Qohor) refused to honor the contract it had made. The sellswords [sacked](wiki:Sack_of_Qohor) Qohor as an answer to the [Free City](wiki:Free_City)'s refusal.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Rawoiafaegor_rivers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) The Golden Company has mainly fought in the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands) for [Lys](wiki:Lys), [Myr](wiki:Myr), or [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh).(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

### Blackfyre Rebellions

Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) did not provide [Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre) with the support of the Golden Company for the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC).(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) He aided [Haegon I Blackfyre](wiki:Haegon_I_Blackfyre) in the [Third Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Third_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [219 AC](wiki:219_AC), but Bittersteel was captured and Haegon was slain during the campaign. Although Aegor was allowed to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), his ship was taken while sailing to [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea) and Bittersteel rejoined the Golden Company.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The Golden Company invaded [Massey's Hook](wiki:Massey's_Hook) in [236 AC](wiki:236_AC) during the [Fourth Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Fourth_Blackfyre_Rebellion), but they were defeated by King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen).(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Bittersteel died while leading the Golden Company in a skirmish between Myr and Tyrosh in the Disputed Lands in [241 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

[Maelys Blackfyre](wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre) won command of the Golden Company by twisting the head of his cousin, [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_Blackfyre_(cousin_of_Maelys_I)), until it was torn from his shoulders.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) One of the [Band of Nine](wiki:Band_of_Nine) in [258 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) Maelys I led the Golden Company during the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings). Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) cut a bloody path though the Golden Company's ranks to slay Maelys the Monstrous in single combat on the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones).(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) [Tygett Lannister](wiki:Tygett_Lannister) slew a knight of the Golden Company during the war.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### Recent History

When [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) was a little girl, her brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) once feasted the captains of the Golden Company in the hopes they might take up his cause to reclaim the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) from King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). They ate his food and heard his pleas and laughed at him.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), exiled from Westeros during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), served five years with the Golden Company,(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) but he is said to have drank himself to death.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

The Golden Company is said to now be made up of exiles and the sons of exiles.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) Some have been born into the company, but still consider themselves Westerosi.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Ar2_1-2)

## Culture

The men of the Golden Company prefer to call themselves a free brotherhood of exiles, rather than [sellswords](wiki:Sellswords).(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Ar2_1-1) Unlike most hosts of [household knights](wiki:Household_knight) and [feudal](wiki:Feudal) levies, the Golden Company are highly disciplined soldiers able to quickly establish orderly camps.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

The high officers display a rude splendor. Like many in their trade they keep their worldly wealth upon their person, including jewelled swords, inlaid armor, heavy torcs, and fine [silks](wiki:Silk). Many wear a lord's ransom in [golden](wiki:Gold) arm rings, with each ring signifying one year's service with the Golden Company.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

Jon Connington's POV at the first officers' muster at Griffin's Roost confirms this — navigable cite:

> "Like many in their trade, they kept their worldly wealth upon their persons: jeweled swords, inlaid armor, heavy torcs, and fine silks were much in evidence, and every man there wore a lord's ransom in golden arm rings."

— Jon Connington POV, ADWD The Lost Lord I (`sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-lost-lord-01.md:107`) — confirms each arm ring = one year's service; the wealth-display at the command level is structural, not individual vanity.

**Bow hierarchy:** The Golden Company's archer arm commanded by Black Balaq uses a ranked hierarchy of weapons by range and power:

> "A third of Balaq's men used crossbows, another third the double-curved horn-and-sinew bows of the east. Better than these were the big yew longbows borne by the archers of Westerosi blood, and best of all were the great bows of goldenheart treasured by Black Balaq himself and his fifty Summer Islanders. Only a dragonbone bow could outrange one made of goldenheart."

— Jon Connington POV, ADWD The Griffin Reborn I (`sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-griffin-reborn-01.md:19`) — establishes the book hierarchy: crossbow < horn-and-sinew eastern bow < yew longbow < goldenheart < dragonbone; the goldenheart superiority is the reason Black Balaq's fifty are elite skirmishers. The [captain-general](wiki:Captain-general)'s tent, made of cloth-of-gold, is surrounded by a ring of pikes topped with the [gilded skulls](wiki:Golden_skulls) of previous captains-general.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

While the core of the Golden Company is still made up of descendants of men who fought for [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre), over the past century a steady trickle of outlaws or men from the losing side of other wars has joined their ranks. It has also been joined by other sellswords simply seeking better opportunities whose ancestors were never from [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), so that soldiers from a dozen different lands can now be found in the Golden Company. This trend has only increased since the [Fifth Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Fifth_Blackfyre_Rebellion), over forty years ago. In the present day, some high-ranking officers like [Black Balaq](wiki:Black_Balaq), [Gorys Edoryen](wiki:Gorys_Edoryen), and [Lysono Maar](wiki:Lysono_Maar) do not descend from Blackfyre supporters, though all of the known captains-general are descended from Blackfyre loyalists. Several members boast family names that were once prominent in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), such as [Cole](wiki:House_Cole), [Lothston](wiki:House_Lothston), [Mandrake](wiki:House_Mandrake), [Mudd](wiki:House_Mudd), and [Strong](wiki:House_Strong), though men in the free companies may call themselves however they choose.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

The Golden Company breaks its contract with the [Free City](wiki:Free_City) of [Myr](wiki:Myr), despite looming war with neighboring [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh). According to sailors in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), they are making for [Volantis](wiki:Volantis).(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

In [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) tells Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) that the Golden Company has broken its contract with Myr. She uses this information in her attempt to convince him to help with her scheme to crown Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) as [Queen of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men](wiki:Queen_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men).(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

During Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s small council meeting, [Aurane Waters](wiki:Aurane_Waters) reveals that the Golden Company has broken its contract with Myr. He has heard many men say that [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) has hired them and is bringing them across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). Cersei then tells the council that [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) heard from a Myrish galley that the Golden Company are making their way to [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), so if they mean to cross to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) they are marching the wrong way.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

Before the Golden Company broke their contract, the [Yunkish](wiki:Yunkai) attempted to contract the Golden Company for their campaign on [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay). An envoy offered twice what Myr was paying them, plus a [slave](wiki:Slave) for every man in the company, ten for every officer, and a hundred choice maidens for the commander, [Harry Strickland](wiki:Harry_Strickland). Harry told the envoy he would think on his proposal; he thought a blunt refusal might have been unwise as his men would think he had taken leave of his wits.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

While traveling on [Valyrian roads](wiki:Valyrian_roads) to [Ghoyan Drohe](wiki:Ghoyan_Drohe), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) learns from [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis) that the Golden Company is marching to Volantis to join with [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), Queen of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen).(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) The exiled Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), now calling himself "Griff", recalls secret plans with the previous captain-general, Ser [Myles Toyne](wiki:Myles_Toyne). They plotted with Illyrio to eventually have "Young Griff", whom they claim is Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), marry Daenerys and claim the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Jon is unsure if Myles's successor, Homeless Harry, is aware of the plan.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)

The Golden Company encamps south of [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys) after Triarch [Malaquo Maegyr](wiki:Malaquo_Maegyr) closes the delta road to Volantis.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8) The men of the company are frustrated by Illyrio's constantly changing plans when they learn that the unaware Daenerys has stayed in Meereen. They decide to swear allegiance to Aegon VI Targaryen and join him in an invasion of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-9)

The company sails with Jon and Aegon from Volon Therys for Westeros, but the Volantene fleet transporting them is scattered by a storm after leaving [Lys](wiki:Lys). During the [landing of the Golden Company](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company) in the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) and the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), the sellswords quickly take the castles of [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost), [Rain House](wiki:Taking_of_Rain_House), [Crow's Nest](wiki:Taking_of_Crow's_Nest), and [Greenstone](wiki:Taking_of_Greenstone). Their next plan is to take [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End),(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) which is held by Ser [Gilbert Farring](wiki:Gilbert_Farring) for [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### The Winds of Winter

[Chain](wiki:Chain) and [Young John Mudd](wiki:Young_John_Mudd) hold [Mistwood](wiki:Mistwood) for the Golden Company. They keep Lady [Mary Mertyns](wiki:Mary_Mertyns) under house arrest and host Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell)'s party for a night. Chain tells Arianne many stories about the company and its members, including that of [Little Pussy](wiki:Little_Pussy) and his lucky cat, as well as the time someone made off with Harry Strickland's favorite [elephant](wiki:Elephant). At [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost), [Haldon Halfmaester](wiki:Haldon_Halfmaester) informs Arianne that the Golden Company has taken [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) and that the [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) awaits her there. He tells her that Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell)'s army is descending on the castle from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and that she will want to be safe inside the walls before the battle.(wiki:Golden_Company.cite_ref-Ar2_1-3)
