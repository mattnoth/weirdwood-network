## Culture

### Religion[ ]

Most northmen still follow the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods), and they have little inclination for newer religions. The worship of the old gods is not accompanied by priests, holy texts, songs of worship, and barely any rites.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Ragot70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Prayers are done in silence.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) Worshipers of the old gods visit [godswoods](wiki:Godswood), groves contained within castles throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), where a [heart tree](wiki:Heart_tree), usually a [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood), can be found. These trees, which have faces carved into them, are considered to be sacred. Worshipers believe the old gods watch through the trees.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)
There are only a few houses who follow the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven),(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) with [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) the most prominent.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Due to its religious aspect, most northmen refuse to take holy orders and thus cannot become [knights](wiki:Knight), although some [northern cavalry](wiki:Northern_cavalry) are knights who still follow the old gods instead of the Seven.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Most knights of the north live in the region's southern lands, such as [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor)(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-36) and the [barrowlands](wiki:Barrowlands).(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1) [Heraldry](wiki:Heraldry) in the north is simpler than that in southern Westeros, showing the lesser influence that chivalry has had there.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-37)
Since most northmen pray before heart trees instead of following the Seven in [septs](wiki:Sept), some [southrons](wiki:Southron) consider them to be godless(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) or tree worshipers.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)
In the aftermath of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), many northmen from the host of Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) remained in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), marrying widows at so-called [Widow Fairs](wiki:Widow_Fairs). They strengthened Houses [Tully](wiki:House_Tully) and [Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood) in particular, and helped to revive worship of the old gods south of the [Neck](wiki:Neck).(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

### Customs[ ]

[Guest right](wiki:Guest_right) is treasured among northmen.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) The people of the north have long memories, and a [lord](wiki:Lord) who does not seek his rightful vengeance threatens to have his own men turn on him.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) A northern custom is that when someone is condemned to death, he who passes that sentence should swing the sword.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3)(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)
During harsh winters, many farmers and younger northmen seek refuge in the [winter town](wiki:Winter_town) nearby [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) or in the castles of lords.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) To preserve resources for the younger generations, many older northmen leave their home to ostensibly "hunt" during winter, with most not surviving until spring.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Men who are old, childless, homeless, or younger sons also traditionally joined winter armies which marched for adventure and plunder with no expectation of survival.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4)
The [first night](wiki:First_night) was allowed in the north until Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) convinced King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) to abolish it.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1) Some northern lords, such as the [Umbers](wiki:House_Umber) still discretely practice it, however.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)
Northmen hold the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) in high regard,(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-5) and many lords send annual gifts to support the black brothers.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)
[Bastards](wiki:Bastard) of noble origin from the north may be given the surname "Snow".(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)
Northmen have been referred to by other Westerosi as [wolves](wiki:Wolves)(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2)(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) or wolf folk.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)
Greatswords are used by northmen, including Umbers(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-2) and [clan](wiki:Northern_mountain_clans) champions.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) have used battle-axes in addition to greatswords.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) Mail is a common armor in the north,(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-46) with many northern warriors wearing mail hauberks or shirts beneath heavy cloaks of wool or fur.(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-6)(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) [Karstarks](wiki:House_Karstark) wear cloaks made from the pelts of [bears](wiki:Bear), [seals](wiki:Seal), and [wolves](wiki:Wolf).(wiki:Northmen.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-3)
