## Edges


## Culture

The quarrelsome clans of the rugged northwest are a hardy people who, like most other [northmen](wiki:Northmen), worship the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) According to a semi-canon source, the clansmen have lands and [holdfasts](wiki:Holdfast). Some reside in fastnesses large enough to be called castles, though they would be considered small and crudely constructed in comparison to the great castles of the south.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-13)
There are roughly forty clans, large and small,(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) the most prominent of which are the [Wulls](wiki:House_Wull) along the [Bay of Ice](wiki:Bay_of_Ice),(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) the [Norreys](wiki:House_Norrey) near the [Gift](wiki:Gift),(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) the [Harclays](wiki:House_Harclay) in the southern hills,(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) and the [Burleys](wiki:House_Burley), the [First Flints](wiki:House_Flint_of_the_mountains), the [Knotts](wiki:House_Knott), and the [Liddles](wiki:House_Liddle) in the high places.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Some clans also live along rivers.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_mountain_clans.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) The most powerful of the clans are the Wulls.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_mountain_clans.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)
The Norreys,(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) the Flints,(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) and the Wulls(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_mountain_clans.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) hate [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) because of raids on their lands, and the Norreys are noted friends of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) who control the nearby Gift.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) The Wulls have also clashed with the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) in the past.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_mountain_clans.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5) These threats and their own inter-clan feuds have instilled a fierce martial spirit in the mountain folk.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_mountain_clans.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6)
In battle, clan champions fight with huge two-handed greatswords or axes and sometimes armor themselves with mail byrnies.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) The common men sling stones or wield staffs of mountain ash. Clansmen wear ragged skins and studded leathers,(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) and some disguise themselves with leaves and brush for arboreal fighting.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) They breed hardy mounts in their high valleys and mountain meadows.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5)
The chiefs of these clans are given the treatment of "[lord](wiki:Lord)" by their lieges, the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). Among themselves, however, the clansmen style their leaders as "the [clan name]"; i.e. the chieftain of Clan Liddle would be known as the Liddle. This practice extends to other usage; Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) is called "the Ned" by the clans.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) This tradition seems to stem from the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), as it is also practiced sometimes by House Stark ("the Stark in Winterfell"),(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) by some noble houses among the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn),(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) and by some tribes of [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) (referring to Mance Rayder as "The Mance").(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
The mountain clans are noted for their hospitality, with chiefs attempting to outdo their rivals in generosity,(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6)(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_mountain_clans.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7) and they are traditionally loyal to the Starks of Winterfell.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_mountain_clans.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8)(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-19) Given their high latitude and sparse resources, the clans suffer population strain during winter years. The young are sent to the Starks' [winter town](wiki:Winter_town), and old men often announce that they are “going hunting.” Some are found the next spring; more are never seen again.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)
According to Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), some of the mountain clans still continue the tradition of the [first night](wiki:First_night).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Origins

Descending from the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) like other northmen,(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) the clansmen were eventually included within the domain of the [Kings of Winter](wiki:Kings_of_Winter) from [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_mountain_clans.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-9) The clans' territory has been historically raided by [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) and [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_mountain_clans.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-10)
The clans have quarreled with each other for centuries,(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_stoneborn_of_skagos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) leading the Starks to settle the disputes by force or by calling the chiefs to Winterfell. These feuds are recalled in songs, such as "[Black Pines](wiki:Black_Pines)" and "[Wolves in the Hills](wiki:Wolves_in_the_Hills)".(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_mountain_clans.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-11) The clansmen have traditionally only banded together under the leadership of a Stark.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
The clans have not been visited by a king in centuries, as [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark), the last [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), knelt to [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-7)
[Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell) and [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), has been hosted generously whenever he visits the mountain clans.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-8)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

While traveling through the [northern mountains](wiki:Northern_mountains) toward the [Wall](wiki:Wall), [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) tells [Meera](wiki:Meera_Reed) and [Jojen Reed](wiki:Jojen_Reed) that the hills and mountains are inhabited. Through the eyes of [Summer](wiki:Summer), Bran knows that the clansmen are aware of their presence. Bran's party meets a [a Liddle](wiki:House_Liddle) during their journey.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)
Clansmen fight for [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). [Norreys](wiki:House_Norrey) and [Burleys](wiki:House_Burley) participate in the [fighting at the fords of the Trident](wiki:Fighting_at_the_fords_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) hears the toasts and songs of mountain clansmen in the tents outside the [Twins](wiki:Twins).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rasos50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) [Owen Norrey](wiki:Owen_Norrey) is slain at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) sends [ravens](wiki:Raven) to the Burleys, the [Harclays](wiki:House_Harclay), the Liddles, the Norreys, and the [Wulls](wiki:House_Wull) asking for aid against the [wildling](wiki:Wildling) host of [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

After the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) estimates the clans could muster two or three thousand fighting men. Rather than attack the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort), Jon advises [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) to seek the aid of the clansmen.(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-9)
When Stannis tries to curry favor with the northern lords by freeing their western strongholds from the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn), he begins by enlisting the aid of [Old Torghen Flint](wiki:Torghen_Flint) and [Big Bucket Wull](wiki:Hugo_Wull). Stannis is the first king the clans have seen since [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark) knelt before [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-10)(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)
By observing the courtesies suggested by Jon, Stannis is able to rally the mountain folk and [liberate Deepwood](wiki:Fight_by_Deepwood_Motte), earning the loyalty of Houses [Glover](wiki:House_Glover) and [Mormont](wiki:House_Mormont).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) During the army's snow-plagued [march on Winterfell](wiki:March_on_Winterfell) to take the castle from [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton), Stannis's [southron](wiki:Southron) knights struggle to survive, while the snowshoe-clad clansmen are able to soldier on with relative ease. They remain fiercely loyal to the memory of "[the Ned](wiki:Eddard_Stark)" and want to free "[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark)" (actually [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
Old Flint and [The Norrey](wiki:Brandon_Norrey) travel to [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) to attend the wedding of [Alys Karstark](wiki:Alys_Karstark) and [Sigorn](wiki:Sigorn).(wiki:Northern_mountain_clans.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)
