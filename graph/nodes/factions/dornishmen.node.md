## Origins

The Dornish have often fought across the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) and the [Dornish Marches](wiki:Dornish_Marches) with the [Reach](wiki:Reach) and the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands).(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) The Dornish fought [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) in several wars, including the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War) and the [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne). They finally joined the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) during the reign of King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen). The presence of Dornishmen in Daeron's court was one of the causes of the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), however.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

## Culture

Dornish culture has [First Men](wiki:First_Men) and [Andal](wiki:Andal) origins, but is also strongly influenced by the mass immigration of the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar) who settled in [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) during the [Rhoynar migration](wiki:Rhoynar_migration). Their influence can be observed in many Dornish [customs](wiki:Customs),(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-dornishmen_14-0) including the [laws of inheritance](wiki:Inheritance). In Dorne, unlike the other six kingdoms, no distinction is made between sons and daughters. Instead, children inherit in order of birth regardless of gender, as per [Rhoynish](wiki:Rhoynish) custom.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) In the case of an inheriting female, her last name will be passed on to her children, instead of the name of her husband.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-17)

Furthermore, views on [bastardy](wiki:Bastardy) are milder in Dorne compared to the rest of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). Especially when a child is born to a [paramour](wiki:Paramour), the Dornish are not greatly concerned if a child is born in wedlock or out of it.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Many lords and some ladies in Dorne have paramours,(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-dornishmen_14-1) and [homosexuality](wiki:Gender_and_sexuality) is more accepted.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

### Language

The Dornish speak the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue), as does the rest of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). But unlike in the other kingdoms, their speech is marked by the influences of the language of the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar).(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Radwd1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) This influence can be found all over Dorne,(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Radwd1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) but most strongly among the salty Dornishmen,(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) who have the most Rhoynish blood.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) The Dornish speak with a Dornish drawl,(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) stretching, rolling, or lilting some words.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) Some describe the Dornish speech as charming.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8) The [Marcher](wiki:Dornish_Marches) lords often claim to find the Dornish accent impossible to understand, however.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)

### Religion

The Dornish primarily follow the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven),(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) although [red temples](wiki:Red_temple) of the [Lord of Light](wiki:Lord_of_Light), R'hllor, can also be found within Dorne.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)

### Fashion

The Dornish climate favors loose, layered robes.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10) Dornish nobles prefer to wear robes of linen, satin, silk, or samite,(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) accessorized with jeweled belts.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)

Dornish armor is heavily enameled and inlaid with burnished [copper](wiki:Copper), shining [silver](wiki:Silver), and soft red [gold](wiki:Gold),(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9) with cloaks over it to keep the sun off.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-3) Similarly, it is Dornish fashion to wrap helmets in long scarfs, to prevent the sun from beating down upon it.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-10)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-4)

When crossing the deserts of Dorne during daytime, veils are used to keep the sands out of their faces.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)

### Culinary arts

Dornish prefer spicy food.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) [Snake](wiki:Snake) sauce preferably has a drop of venom in it, as well as mustard seeds and dragon peppers.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) The Dornish produce their own wines, often called "Dornish reds".(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Radwd1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2) These wines are generally sour,(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) though they occasionally can be more rich of taste.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Strongwines from Dorne are as dark as blood, with a sweet taste.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

### Military

Dornishmen prefer round metal shields, spears, short throwing spears or double-curved bows.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-11)

Fabled are the Dornish [sand steeds](wiki:Sand_steed). These horses are slim and swift, with elegant heads, long necks, and flowing manes.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-12) They are considered to be the most beautiful horses in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11) They are smaller than regular warhorses and lightboned, and so are unable to bear the weight of armor.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-13)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-12) However, they can run for near two days and a night before tiring,(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-14) needing only a few drinks of water over the course of a day and a night.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-13)

The least populous of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms),(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) the Dornish seem to favor different tactics in battle than the rest of the realm. The warm climate of Dorne and the scarcity of water in the desert make the movement and supply of a large army difficult. This has proven to have been an advantage during wars, as many hosts which have entered Dorne over time have withered and broiled on the hot red Dornish sands.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) For example, many of the men from Lord [Harlan Tyrell](wiki:Harlan_Tyrell)'s host died of sun and thirst during their march on [Hellholt](wiki:Hellholt) during the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War).(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

The Dornish have not been a seafaring culture since Princess [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria)'s burning of her ships,(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) with [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) never being a sea power.(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Radwd50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Dornishmen have a reputation for using [poison](wiki:Poison).(wiki:Dornishmen.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

### A Storm of Swords
