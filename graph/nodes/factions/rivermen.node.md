### Religion[ ]

Brought to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) by the [Andals](wiki:Andals),(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) has become the most common religion in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) There are several [septries](wiki:Septries) in the territory,(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and many [septs](wiki:Sept) and [motherhouses](wiki:Motherhouse) were established during the reign of [Humfrey I Teague](wiki:Humfrey_I_Teague), [King of the Rivers and the Hills](wiki:King_of_the_Rivers_and_the_Hills).(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) The [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) was once strong among the rivermen, and [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept) was known for its chapter of [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons).(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)
Unlike most river lords, [House Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood) of [Raventree Hall](wiki:Raventree_Hall) continue to follow the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men).(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) [Northmen](wiki:Northmen) who remained after the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) also continued to worship the old gods.(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

### Customs[ ]

Much of life in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) is centered on its namesake rivers, such as the three forks of the [Trident](wiki:Trident) and the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush).(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Fishermen, farmers, merchants, and [mummers](wiki:Mummers) use the rivers as part of their livelihood.(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rasos1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
[Guest right](wiki:Guest_right) is an important custom in the riverlands.(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
The quarrelsome river lords(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) have a fractious history.(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully)'s funeral customs have the deceased member released into the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork) on a small boat, which is then set on fire with a fire arrow. The burning boat and its occupant then sink into the river.(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) [Bastards](wiki:Bastard) of noble origin from the riverlands may be given the surname "Rivers".(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Racok4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
[Smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) shun [High Heart](wiki:High_Heart), believing that the hill is haunted by [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest).(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
The [Lady of the Leaves](wiki:Lady_of_the_Leaves) leads a community living in the branches of a forest.(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) The [green men](wiki:Green_men) tend to the [weirwoods](wiki:Weirwood) on the [Isle of Faces](wiki:Isle_of_Faces) within the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye), which is rarely visited by anyone from the mainland.(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
Rivermen and [westermen](wiki:Westermen) of yore referred to the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) as "[wolves](wiki:Wolves) of the sea".(wiki:Rivermen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)
