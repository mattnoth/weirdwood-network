## Origins

The [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) were [knights](wiki:Knight) who renounced their titles, lands, gold, and possessions to fight for the Seven. They swore their swords to the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon). They wore inlaid [silver](wiki:Silver) armor over [hair shirts](wiki:Hair_shirt), rainbow cloaks, and swords with star-shaped crystals in their pommels. They developed a reputation for fanaticism and implacable hatred for enemies of the Faith.

The [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) were a more humble order generally for commoners and women. Acting as a militant counterpart to [begging brothers](wiki:Begging_brothers), Poor Fellows wandered the realm and escorted pilgrims between septs. They were lightly-armed footmen, who carried whatever weapons they could make or find, often axes or cudgels, and wore star badges, red on white.

The Faith Militant supported [House Teague](wiki:House_Teague) when they tried to suppress the worship of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) by [House Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood). Houses [Tully](wiki:House_Tully) and [Vance](wiki:House_Vance_of_Atranta) supported the Blackwoods, however, and the resulting war led to the control of the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) by the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_King) of [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon).(wiki:Faith_Militant.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

The [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) against [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) began during the reign of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) after several events the Faith took as slights.(wiki:Faith_Militant.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) His successor, King [Maegor the Cruel](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), outlawed armed holy men and took brutal steps to put down the rebelling orders and the houses supporting them, including offering bounties for their heads.(wiki:Faith_Militant.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Not even the death of the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Maegor_I)) and his replacement for a more passive one stopped the determination of the Faith Militant.(wiki:Faith_Militant.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

After Maegor's death, his successor, [Jaehaerys the Conciliator](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), offered amnesty to the rebels in exchange for the disbandment of the Faith Militant.(wiki:Faith_Militant.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

The [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) and its followers are incensed by attacks on holy men and women and the sacking of septs and septries carried out by foragers, such as the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions), during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). The new [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon), known as the [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow), manipulates Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) into overturning Maegor's law and the Faith Militant is born again.(wiki:Faith_Militant.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
