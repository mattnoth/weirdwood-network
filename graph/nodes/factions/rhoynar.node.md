## Appearances & Description

The Rhoynar were a slender people with smooth olive skin, black hair, and dark eyes.(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Some [maesters](wiki:Maesters) have controversially theorized that the people of [Myr](wiki:Myr) are related to the Rhoynar because of their similar olive skin.(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

## Culture

The Rhoynar practiced a number of customs that differ from mainstream [Andal](wiki:Andal) culture. These included equal primogeniture, granting inheritances to the eldest child regardless of gender. They also tolerated homosexuality. Due to their origin in city-states, the Rhoynar titled their rulers [Princes and Princesses](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne). Their influence made these customs standard in Dorne as well.
The Rhoynar worshipped a number of river-themed nature gods. Their primary god was [Mother Rhoyne](wiki:Mother_Rhoyne), or Mother River, the personification of the river Rhoyne itself. Other gods include the [Old Man of the River](wiki:Old_Man_of_the_River), a turtle god, and his adversary, the [Crab King](wiki:Crab_King).
Art and music flourished in the ancient cities of the Rhoynar. They were by and large a peaceful people, but were fiercely independent, and could be formidable when roused to wroth.(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Origins

### Ancient Rhoynar[ ]

The Rhoynar lived in city-states along the vast network of the river Rhoyne, including [Ar Noy](wiki:Ar_Noy), [Chroyane](wiki:Chroyane), [Ghoyan Drohe](wiki:Ghoyan_Drohe), [Ny Sar](wiki:Ny_Sar), [Sar Mell](wiki:Sar_Mell), and [Sarhoy](wiki:Sarhoy).(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Rtloiaf_the_free_cities.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) The Rhoynish civilization was as storied and ancient as the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis).(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
The Rhoynar taught the [Andals](wiki:Andals) how to work iron, although the [Seven-Pointed Star](wiki:Seven-Pointed_Star) of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) teaches that the Andals received this gift from [the Smith](wiki:Smith_(the_Seven)).(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) The Rhoynar used [water magic](wiki:Water_magic) to defend themselves from enemies.(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) [Garris the Grey](wiki:Garris_the_Grey), Prince of Ny Sar, is said to have driven [hairy men](wiki:Hairy_men) from the [Noyne](wiki:Noyne).(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
However, the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold)'s expansion from the east threatened to overtake the Rhoynar hundreds of years before [Aegon's Landing](wiki:Aegon's_Landing). For some two and a half centuries, the [Rhoynish Wars](wiki:Rhoynish_Wars) were fought between the Valyrians and the Rhoynar. In the last of the these, [Garin the Great](wiki:Garin_(prince)), Prince of Chroyane, led two hundred and fifty thousand Rhoynar to their deaths in battle against the Valyrians. [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria), Princess of Ny Sar, led the surviving Rhoynar, mostly women and children, in fleeing Essos in [ten thousand ships](wiki:Ten_thousand_ships).(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) During and after the Rhoynar evacuation, the Valyrians destroyed the great cities of the Rhoynish realm; their massive ruins are still easily observed from the Rhoyne.(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### Rhoynar in Dorne[ ]

Nymeria led her followers on dangerous journeys to the [Basilisk Isles](wiki:Basilisk_Isles), [Sothoryos](wiki:Sothoryos), [Naath](wiki:Naath), the [Summer Isles](wiki:Summer_Isles), the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), and finally [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) in southern [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Nymeria married a Dornish lord, [Mors Martell](wiki:Mors_Martell) of the [Sandship](wiki:Sandship), and helped him consolidate Dorne under his rule through [Nymeria's War](wiki:Nymeria's_War).(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Under the rule of [House Nymeros Martell](wiki:House_Martell) of [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), the Rhoynar have lived and intermingled with the native Dornish since that time.(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
Having abandoned the Rhoyne, most Rhoynar adopted the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) in Westeros. In [The Conquest of Dorne](wiki:The_Conquest_of_Dorne), King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen) differentiated between three sorts of [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen), so-called stony, sandy, and salty Dornishmen.(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) The salty Dornishmen are held to have the most Rhoynish blood, being dark haired, olive skinned, and living close to the shore. The salty Dornishmen no longer speak the language of the ancient Rhoynar,(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_dorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) although they do speak the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue) with a distinctive drawl.(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Unlike the rest of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), equal primogeniture is practiced in Dorne as it was along the Rhoyne.
Some descendants of the Rhoynar did not assimilate and continue to practice the traditions of their ancestors, however. Called the "[orphans of the Greenblood](wiki:Orphans_of_the_Greenblood)", these individuals live on rafts along the river [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood) and consider themselves orphaned from their Mother Rhoyne.(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) They are of pure Rhoynar blood and still speak the Rhoynish language in secret.(wiki:Rhoynar.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_dorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
