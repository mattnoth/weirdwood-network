## Origins

### Antiquity

The most accepted origin for the ironborn states they descend from [First Men](wiki:First_Men) who settled upon the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) thousands of years ago. Their [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair), carved from [oily black stone](wiki:Oily_black_stone), is said to have been found on the shores of [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk) when they first arrived.(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-18) Somewhat isolated from the rest of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), the ironborn created their own religion based around the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God). According to their priests, the ironborn came from beneath the oceans and are more kin to fish and [merlings](wiki:Merling) than the rest of mankind. Archmaester [Haereg](wiki:Haereg) theorized that the ancestors of the ironborn came from a land west of the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea).(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-19)

The salt kings and reavers who raided the shores of the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea) in antiquity are fondly remembered by singers and priests.(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-20) The First Men of the mainland did not have a strong seafaring tradition, so the ironmen controlled the seas from the [Frozen Shore](wiki:Frozen_Shore) to the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor).(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-21) It is said that the ironmen of old had oft been blood-drunk in battle, so berserk that they felt no pain and feared no foe.(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

### Age of Heroes

According to legend, the ironborn of old were ruled by the [Grey King](wiki:Grey_King) during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes). After his death, his hundred sons [killed each other](wiki:Kinslaying) in order to determine who should succeed him. The sixteen who eventually remained divided up the islands between them.(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

According to the oldest surviving records at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), each of the Iron Islands was ruled by two kings: a [rock king](wiki:Rock_king) who ruled the island, and a [salt king](wiki:Salt_king) who held the command at sea. These kings were chosen in a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot), called for by a priest of the Drowned God whenever a king died. Eventually, a [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands) was chosen via kingsmoot to rule over all of the ironborn.(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) While previous generations usually raided the mainland and then returned to the islands, the driftwood kings began to conquer territory on the mainland.(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2)

### Kings of the Iron Islands

The ironborn eventually declined as dynasties such as the [Gardeners](wiki:House_Gardener) and [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) grew more powerful, and many ironborn conquests were lost to the mainland. [House Greyiron](wiki:House_Greyiron) eventually claimed the throne as hereditary [Kings of the Iron Islands](wiki:Kings_of_the_Iron_Islands), ending the practice of a High King being chosen through [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot).(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-3)(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

The [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) brought the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) to the ironborn. While some ironmen converted, this new religion did not fully take hold and worship of the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God) continued.(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) The Greyirons were eventually overthrown by an alliance of [Andal](wiki:Andals) and ironborn families, with [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) claiming the throne with Andal support.(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1)

Many Hoare kings sought closer relations with the mainland, bringing the dynasty into conflict with priests of the Drowned God. Since the Westerosi kingdoms had grown stronger, daring ironborn sailors reaved as far away as the [Basilisk Isles](wiki:Basilisk_Isles), [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), and the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands).(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

King [Harwyn Hardhand](wiki:Harwyn_Hoare) captured the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) from the [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King) [Arrec Durrandon](wiki:Arrec_Durrandon), greatly expanding the ironborn's territory, and Harwyn's grandson, [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare), constructed [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) by the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye).(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1)

### Iron Throne

[Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare) was [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers) when King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) arrived in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) and began [the Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest) by [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). The ironborn were thrown back to their islands when [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) was extinguished in the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-2)(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) Following Aegon's [invasion of the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands), Lord [Vickon Greyjoy](wiki:Vickon_Greyjoy) was chosen by the surviving ironborn lords to have primacy over them.(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragons_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

Guided by [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy), the ironborn have been mostly-peaceful subjects of the [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms) and the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) for almost three hundred years(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)—aside from reavings by Lords [Dalton](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy)(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_red_kraken.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) and [Dagon Greyjoy](wiki:Dagon_Greyjoy)(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)—although none of them could ever be called a leal and faithful servant to the throne.(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

In [289 AC](wiki:289_AC) the ironborn revolted in [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), led by Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), in an attempt to restore the [Old Way](wiki:Old_Way). However, the rebellion was crushed by King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon),(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1)(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_glorious_reign.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) and Balon's new [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) was defeated by Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

Archmaester [Haereg](wiki:Haereg) recorded the history of the ironmen in his exhaustive *[History of the Ironborn](wiki:History_of_the_Ironborn)*, and Archmaester [Hake](wiki:Hake_(archmaester)) also wrote of their history.(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-3) Some maesters believe the blood of [Cape Kraken](wiki:Cape_Kraken)'s people is closer to that of the ironmen than the [northmen](wiki:North).(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_mountain_clans.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

### A Feast for Crows

[Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) calls for a [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk) to decide Balon's successor.(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) After winning the support of the gathered ironmen,(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) sends them to attack the [Shield Islands](wiki:Taking_of_the_Shields)(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-4) and the [Mander](wiki:Mander).(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

Euron sends [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) to retrieve [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) and her [dragons](wiki:Dragons) from [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) with the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet).(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-5) In Euron's absence while at war, [Erik Ironmaker](wiki:Erik_Ironmaker) rules as [Lord Steward of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_Steward_of_the_Iron_Islands) and castellan of [Pyke](wiki:Pyke).(wiki:Ironborn.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)
