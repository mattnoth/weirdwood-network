## Identity

The Dragonkeepers were an order of seventy-seven guards tasked with protecting the dragons of House Targaryen at the Dragonpit in King's Landing and on Dragonstone (wiki:Dragonkeepers).

## Origins

Founded in 56 AC by King Jaehaerys I Targaryen after Princess Aerea Targaryen stole the dragon Balerion and died upon returning to Westeros (wiki:Dragonkeepers). The order was created to prevent unauthorized access to dragons.

## Allegiances

`SWORN_TO` House Targaryen (track_b: Allegiance). `FOUNDED` by Jaehaerys I Targaryen (track_b: Founder).

## Appearances & Description

The Dragonkeepers wore suits of gleaming black armor with helms crested by a row of dragon scales that continued down their backs, diminishing downward (wiki:Dragonkeepers). They wielded swords (wiki:Dragonkeepers).

## Narrative Arc

The Dragonkeepers guarded dragons at the Dragonpit and Dragonstone for over seventy years. They named Caraxes "the Blood Wyrm," convinced Princess Alyssa Targaryen to claim Meleys instead of Balerion in 75 AC, caught Princess Saera Targaryen trying to enter the Dragonpit in 84 AC, and stopped Princess Viserra Targaryen's reckless dare involving dragons. During the Dance of the Dragons, fewer than fifty Dragonkeepers were slaughtered when a mob led by the Shepherd stormed the Dragonpit; the dragons Tyraxes, Shrykos, Morghul, and Dreamfyre died in the fighting and the dome collapsed (wiki:Dragonkeepers). In AGOT, Eddard Stark sees empty suits of their distinctive armor in a corridor of the Red Keep (wiki:Ragot20). In ASOS, Sansa Stark passes through the same gallery (wiki:Rasos61).
