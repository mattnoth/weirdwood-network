## Origins

The High Council was composed of thirty-three [magisters](wiki:Magister), eleven each from [Lys](wiki:Lys), [Myr](wiki:Myr), and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh). The egotistic and self-centered magisters often sought to advance the causes of their home cities. Topics were usually subject to endless debate.(wiki:High_Council_of_the_Triarchy.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower) sent envoys to the High Council on behalf of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). In return for the Triarchy fighting the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) of Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), Otto offered exclusive trading rights at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and promised the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) to the Triarchy. The magisters were not in session, however, and the delay led to Aegon replacing Otto as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) with Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole). The High Council in Tyrosh eventually allied with Aegon and attacked the blacks with the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet). The Triarchy eventually collapsed into the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War).(wiki:High_Council_of_the_Triarchy.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
