## Origins

### Prelude[ ]

Seven-year-old Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) was taken captive at the end of [129 AC](wiki:129_AC) (shortly before the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet) the following year) and then held captive in [Lys](wiki:Lys) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
[House Rogare](wiki:House_Rogare) rose to prominence in [Lys](wiki:Lys) during the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War), and their patriarch, [Lysandro](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare), wed Viserys to his youngest daughter, [Larra of Lys](wiki:Larra_Rogare). In [134 AC](wiki:134_AC) after meeting with Lysandro's brother, [Drazenko](wiki:Drazenko_Rogare), in [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) traveled to Lys to negotiate with Lysandro a ransom for Viserys's return to the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). Alyn agreed that the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) would pay one hundred thousand [golden dragons](wiki:Currency), utilize the [Rogare Bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank), uphold Viserys's marriage with Larra, and grant [lordships](wiki:Lord) to Lysandro's youngest sons.(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

### Prosperity[ ]

The return of Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC) delighted his elder brother, King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), who had felt guilty since the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet), and caused the court at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to rejoice. The size of the ransom outraged Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), however. Despite Unwin's objections, [Aegon's regents](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) agreed to pay the ransom in ten annual payments. They also agreed to transfer funds from the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos) to the [Rogare Bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank) of [Lysandro](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare). Lord Peake resigned as Hand and was replaced with Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan).(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
Some of [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare)'s brothers accompanied her to King's Landing, with [Moredo](wiki:Moredo_Rogare) leading her guards, [Lotho](wiki:Lotho_Rogare) opening a branch of the bank on [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill), and [Roggerio](wiki:Roggerio_Rogare) establishing the [Mermaid](wiki:Mermaid_(pillow_house)), a [pillow house](wiki:Pillow_house) at the [River Gate](wiki:River_Gate). Her uncle [Drazenko](wiki:Drazenko_Rogare) married [Aliandra Martell](wiki:Aliandra_Martell), [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne).(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
King's Landing prospered due to the Rogare Bank and increased trade with the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), [Lys](wiki:Lys), [Myr](wiki:Myr), [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh). Other cities and towns of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) benefited as well, including [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown), [Hull](wiki:Hull), [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), and [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
For a time the Rogare Bank was stronger than even the Iron Bank of Braavos. The Rogare family's power helped to decrease the power of [Aegon's regents](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III).(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) The Seven Kingdoms rejoiced first at the sight of Lady [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)) flying atop [Morning](wiki:Morning) and then the birth of Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), the son of Viserys and Larra.(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

### Downfall[ ]

The [Lyseni](wiki:Lys) were distrusted by some in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) due to their foreign customs and belief in Lysene gods instead of [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare)'s guards and companions were all Lyseni. King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) and his [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan), were seen as ineffective at dealing with famine in the [north](wiki:North) and fighting in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) and the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands). The hatching of a monster from [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon_(daughter_of_Alyn))'s [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) was seen as a bad omen, as was the [poisoning](wiki:Poison) of Aegon's friend, [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair).(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
[Lysandro](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare) and [Drazenko Rogare](wiki:Drazenko_Rogare) died a day apart from each other, with Drazenko choking on bacon and Lysandro drowning when his barge sank. Some attributed their abrupt deaths to the [Faceless Men](wiki:Faceless_Men) of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos). The deaths of the brothers led to a series of assassinations by the various [magisters](wiki:Magisters) and merchant princes of [Lys](wiki:Lys). Lysandro's eldest son, [Lysaro Rogare](wiki:Lysaro_Rogare), was an ineffective successor, and depositors began withdrawing their funds after rumors spread that the Rogare Bank was unsound. Lysaro fled Lys, but, after being caught in [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys), he was returned to Lys and scourged to death. [Drako](wiki:Drako_Rogare) reached [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) and [Marra](wiki:Marra_Rogare) claimed sanctuary in Lys's temple of [Yndros](wiki:Yndros), but their other kin lost their wealth or were sold into [slavery](wiki:Slavery).(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
In Westeros, Moredo surrendered his leadership of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne)'s army in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) and sailed for Braavos. Lotho and Roggerio were imprisoned in the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand) by Ser [Lucas Leygood](wiki:Lucas_Leygood) and Ser [Marston Waters](wiki:Marston_Waters), respectively, and the bank's branch on Visenya's Hill was seized. Lord Rowan was arrested, as were one of his nephews and twoscore grooms, servants, and knights retainer. King Aegon and Prince Viserys refused to allow Ser [Amaury Peake](wiki:Amaury_Peake) from entering [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) and seizing Viserys's wife Larra, however. [Sandoq the Shadow](wiki:Sandoq_the_Shadow) slew Amaury and a dozen guards, allowing the royal brothers to seek refuge in Maegor's Holdfast for eighteen days.(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)
Ser Marston, the new Hand, refused to storm the keep during the so-called [secret siege](wiki:Secret_siege). Among other confessions, Thaddeus admitted to having helped loot the Rogare Bank and thereby defrauding Westerosi noblemen, as well as conspiring with Larra and her brothers to poison Aegon with the [Tears of Lys](wiki:Tears_of_Lys) in order to place Viserys on the Iron Throne. Aegon and Viserys determined that Lord Rowan was an innocent man tortured into making false confessions, however.(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)
Lord [George Graceford](wiki:George_Graceford), the [Lord Confessor](wiki:Lord_Confessor), named Marston, Amaury, Ser [Mervyn Flowers](wiki:Mervyn_Flowers) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) as conspirators, as well as [Tessario the Tiger](wiki:Tessario_the_Tiger), Septon [Bernard](wiki:Bernard), Ser [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long), Ser [Victor Risley](wiki:Victor_Risley), Lucas Leygood, and six captains of the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing). Also implicated were [Cassandra Baratheon](wiki:Cassandra_Baratheon), [Priscella Hogg](wiki:Priscella_Hogg), and [Lucinda Penrose](wiki:Lucinda_Penrose), all ladies of Queen [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon). [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) suggested that Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) and his aunt, [Clarice Osgrey](wiki:Clarice_Osgrey), had orchestrated the plot against the Rogares in Westeros, although their involvement was never proven.(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)
Lucas was slain while being apprehended, and three of his captains fled the city. Marston, who claimed innocence, was mortally wounded by Mervyn, who was then overwhelmed and beaten to death. Gareth admitted that he thought Aegon III to be unworthy of being king, while Bernard was opposed to the foreign influence of the Rogares. Before dying of torture, Tessario revealed that he had wanted to replace Roggerio.(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)
Thaddeus Rowan, having been weakened by torture, was unable to remain as Hand, and he died while returning to [Goldengrove](wiki:Goldengrove).(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

### Aftermath[ ]

In the aftermath of the attempted coup against King [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) and the fall of the [Rogares](wiki:House_Rogare), nobles from throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) traveled to King's Landing in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), the largest gathering since the [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) in [101 AC](wiki:101_AC). [Willam Stackspear](wiki:Willam_Stackspear), [Marq Merryweather](wiki:Marq_Merryweather), and [Lorent Grandison](wiki:Lorent_Grandison) were chosen as regents by lot, with Lord [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly) becoming Hand, [Isembard Arryn](wiki:Isembard_Arryn) the [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), [Gedmund Peake](wiki:Gedmund_Peake) the [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships), Ser [Raynard Ruskyn](wiki:Raynard_Ruskyn) as [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), and Ser [Adrian Thorne](wiki:Adrian_Thorne) as [Commander of the City Watch](wiki:Commander_of_the_City_Watch).(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)
Of the forty-two people accused of plotting against Aegon and the Rogares, eight had died, sixteen had fled, thirteen confessed to some involvement, and five were put on trial after professing their innocence. Ser [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long), Lord [George Graceford](wiki:George_Graceford), and several [gold cloaks](wiki:Gold_cloaks) and [Fingers](wiki:Fingers_(guards)) agreed to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), and Septon [Bernard](wiki:Bernard) lived a life of silent penance at the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept). Ser [Victor Risley](wiki:Victor_Risley) was slain in a duel by Gareth after demanding [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle). [Lucinda Penrose](wiki:Lucinda_Penrose) and [Priscella Hogg](wiki:Priscella_Hogg) joined the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), and [Cassandra Baratheon](wiki:Cassandra_Baratheon) was wed to Ser [Walter Brownhill](wiki:Walter_Brownhill).(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)
Since the fall of the [Rogare Bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank) had ruined thousands of nobles and tradesmen, [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare)'s brothers in Westeros were also punished by Lord Manderly. After having his right hand removed for theft, [Lotho](wiki:Lotho_Rogare) traveled to the [Hightower](wiki:Hightower) and helped found the [Bank of Oldtown](wiki:Bank_of_Oldtown) with [Samantha Tarly](wiki:Samantha_Tarly) and Lord [Lyonel Hightower](wiki:Lyonel_Hightower). After being whipped seven times "for being a thrice-damned Lyseni", [Roggerio](wiki:Roggerio_Rogare) sold his brothel in King's Landing and reestablished it on [Mermaid's Daughter](wiki:Mermaid's_Daughter) on the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). [Moredo](wiki:Moredo_Rogare) hired mercenaries in [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) for a campaign against Lys.(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14)
Isembard and Lord Torrhen reformed the collection of taxes, which provided some relief for those harmed by the Rogare Bank.(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15) Larra remained in King's Landing, although she never felt comfortable in the city.(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) She moved back to [Lys](wiki:Lys) in [139 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) after giving birth to Princess [Naerys](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen) and Prince [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)).(wiki:Lysene_Spring.cite_ref-AMA_5-0)
