## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

When meeting Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell)'s traveling party outside [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) recalls King Daeron's description of the three types of Dornishmen.(wiki:The_Conquest_of_Dorne.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
[Maester](wiki:Maester) [Pylos](wiki:Pylos) educates the [noble](wiki:Nobility) children of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), Princess [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon), [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm), and [Devan Seaworth](wiki:Devan_Seaworth), having them read from Conquest of Dorne to teach them about about King Daeron I Targaryen. The book is a favorite of Devan Seaworth. Pylos offers the book to [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) when teaching him to read, but Davos demurs.(wiki:The_Conquest_of_Dorne.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

At the [Wall](wiki:Wall), [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) and King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) discuss battle strategy, referring to Daeron's book and his use of goat tracks during the [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne). Stannis is dismissive of the strategy, and of the book.(wiki:The_Conquest_of_Dorne.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
