## Origins

The plague killed tens of thousands in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) with the major cities affected the most; it was bad in [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), worse in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), but worst of all in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), where four in ten succumbed to it. A strong man could wake up healthy in the morning and die by the evening, so swiftly did the plague strike.(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
[Dorne](wiki:Dorne) and the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) did not suffer from the Great Spring Sickness, as they closed off their access roads and their ports to all travellers. Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) and his squire, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), were in Dorne for the duration of the plague so were unaffected by it.(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
The plague killed many people both nobles and [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), including King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) and his two most immediate heirs, Princes [Valarr](wiki:Valarr_Targaryen) and [Matarys](wiki:Matarys_Targaryen), as well as the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon), a third of the [Most Devout](wiki:Most_Devout), nearly all of the [silent sisters](wiki:Silent_sisters) in King's Landing,(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) and possibly Daeron's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King)(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-3) Ser [Rolland Uffering](wiki:Rolland_Uffering), the fourth husband of Lady [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber), died in the spring,(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) as did the youngest son of Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell).(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Other notable victims include the heir and eldest son of Lord [Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken),(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) and in [210 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Lord [Damon Lannister](wiki:Damon_Lannister_(lord)).(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to Daeron's successor [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), ordered the many bodies to be brought to the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) and burned by the [pyromancers](wiki:Alchemists'_Guild). The light of their [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) pyres could be seen as a dark green glow throughout King's Landing during the night.(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) A quarter of the city burned to the ground.(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Many hostages of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), taken from [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre)'s supporters in the aftermath of the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), died in the spring. With these kinsmen of their supporters already dead, Lord [Gormon Peake](wiki:Gormon_Peake) and [Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre) felt more comfortable attempting the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion).(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)(wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
