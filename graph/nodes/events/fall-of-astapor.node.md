## Origins

Following a suggestion from Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont),(wiki:Fall_of_Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) sails from [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) to [Astapor](wiki:Astapor) on *[Balerion](wiki:Balerion_(ship))* to purchase [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied). There, Daenerys negotiates with [Kraznys mo Nakloz](wiki:Kraznys_mo_Nakloz) through the translator [Missandei](wiki:Missandei). Daenerys understands the [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian) of Astapor, but keeps Kraznys ignorant of this fact, as he continually insults her. Daenerys is impressed with the martial skill of the Unsullied, but is horrified by their mistreatment.(wiki:Fall_of_Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) She agreed to purchase all of the Unsullied soldiers in exchange for her ships, all of the trade goods she possessed, and [Drogon](wiki:Drogon), her largest dragon.(wiki:Fall_of_Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Other than the Unsullied, Astapor is poorly defended; its walls are old and crumbling, with no guards in the towers. Even a modest [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* could probably take the city, but none has ever tried because of the Unsullied.(wiki:Fall_of_Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Apparently, no one thought it was a bad idea to turn control of all of their best soldiers to an ambitious buyer such as the desperate queen inside their walls.

## Narrative Arc

The exchange takes place the next morning, at the [Plaza of Punishment](wiki:Plaza_of_Punishment). Having closed the deal, Daenerys receives the [harpy's fingers](wiki:Harpy's_fingers), a nine-lashed whip that symbolizes her ownership of the Unsullied. She betrayed the terms of the deal and unleashes her dragons on the masters and the few remaining soldiers in the plaza, ordering the Unsullied to kill the remaining slave-masters of the city and freeing all of the slaves.(wiki:Fall_of_Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

## Aftermath

Before leaving for [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai), Daenerys leaves a council of three men—a healer, a scholar and a priest—to rule. They are later deposed by [Cleon](wiki:Cleon), a former butcher, who leads the people to believe that the three intend to return the [Good Masters](wiki:Good_Masters) to their positions of power. Naming himself king after having the council executed, Cleon has all the highborn boys seized to begin training new Unsullied. Each pyramid within Astapor is now an armed camp and the dead lie in the streets and the markets of devoid of food and slaves to purchase.(wiki:Fall_of_Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) The city is later [besieged by the Yunkai'i](wiki:Siege_of_Astapor).(wiki:Fall_of_Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
