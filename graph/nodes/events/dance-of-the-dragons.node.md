## Origins

King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) had three children by his first queen, [Aemma Arryn](wiki:Aemma_Arryn), but only one, Princess [Rhaenyra](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), survived to adulthood. Lacking a son to succeed him, Viserys began to train Rhaenyra to be his heir. Young Rhaenyra was included in discussions of the affairs of state, and was allowed to participate in meetings of the [small council](wiki:Small_council). Many of the nobles took note, and Rhaenyra soon acquired a clique of adherents and supporters. After the passing of Queen Aemma in [105 AC](wiki:105_AC), Viserys named Rhaenyra his heir and hundreds of [lords](wiki:Lord) and [landed knights](wiki:Landed_knight) paid obeisance to her.(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Viserys's declaration disregarded precedents from [92 AC](wiki:92_AC) and [101 AC](wiki:101_AC).(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The king remarried in [106 AC](wiki:106_AC), this time to [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower), and had four more children: [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), [Helaena](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen), [Aemond](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), and [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)).

In [111 AC](wiki:111_AC), a great tourney was held at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) on the fifth anniversary of Viserys's marriage to Alicent. During the opening feast, Queen Alicent wore a green gown, while Princess Rhaenyra dressed in the red and black of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). Note was taken, and thereafter it became the custom to refer to "[greens](wiki:Greens)" and "[blacks](wiki:Blacks)" when talking of the queen's party and the party of the princess, respectively. Wearing Rhaenyra's favor, Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) unhorsed all of Alicent's champions in the tourney, including two of her cousins and her youngest brother, Ser [Gwayne Hightower](wiki:Gwayne_Hightower).(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Viserys I strengthened Rhaenyra's place in the succession by marrying her in [114 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) to Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon), who had Targaryen blood through his mother, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)). The marriage caused a falling out between Rhaenyra and Criston. Rhaenyra gave birth to three sons—[Jacaerys](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon), [Lucerys](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon), and [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon)—during her marriage, although there were rumors that the father of these princes was not Laenor, but Ser [Harwin Strong](wiki:Harwin_Strong).(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

After Laenor was murdered in [Spicetown](wiki:Spicetown) in [120 AC](wiki:120_AC), Rhaenyra married her uncle, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), younger brother to Viserys I. Her sons by him were [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen)—called so to distinguish him from his uncle, Alicent's Aegon, who on occasion was called Aegon the Elder—and [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen).

The relationship between Rhaenyra and Alicent declined early in Alicent's marriage to Viserys, since both ladies had been trying to be the realm's first lady, and there could only be one. As a consequence of their bad relation, Alicent's sons did not take to Rhaenyra's sons. Alicent's father, Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower), had also disliked Rhaenyra's second husband, Prince Daemon, since the beginning of Viserys's reign.(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

## Aftermath

### The False Dawn

With King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) dead, Aegon the Younger was crowned as King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). Although Aegon II's host had been defeated and the [Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) fleet once more served the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), the period that followed was named the [False Dawn](wiki:False_Dawn).(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2)

The realm faced numerous problems. The cruel winter that had begun the year before on Maiden's Day of [130 AC](wiki:130_AC) would last until [135 AC](wiki:135_AC). Much of the realm had been burned out by [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire) - in particular, [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen)'s one-man war riding [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), which had burned out most of the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands). [Broken men](wiki:Broken_men) and outlaws roamed the countryside in the hundreds and thousands, and public order had collapsed in many places. The [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn), under the command of [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy)—who had only nominally sided with the blacks as a pretext to attack wealthy green kingdoms—now refused to acknowledge the boy-king Aegon III's commands to stop raiding.(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3)

Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) and his [northern](wiki:North) army of childless, homeless, and younger men had marched to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with hopes of adventure. The sudden death of King Aegon II had robbed them of that chance, leaving Cregan furious. He had wanted to punish [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), and [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) for having supported Aegon II over [Rhaenyra](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), but upon his arrival, Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) had already sent out ravens, suing for peace.(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-4)

### Hour of the Wolf

Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) could not be dissuaded from punishing the betrayers and prisoners of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). He found the poisoning foul murder, and had twenty-two men arrested in the name of young [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), including Lords [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong) and [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon). He then made Aegon name him [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-5)

Cregan reigned as Hand during a day filled with trials and executions. Most of those accused chose to [take the black](wiki:Night's_Watch)—chief among them Ser [Perkin the Flea](wiki:Perkin_the_Flea)—but Ser [Gyles Belgrave](wiki:Gyles_Belgrave) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) and [Larys](wiki:Larys_Strong) chose death. Corlys was spared a trial thanks to the intervention of [Baela](wiki:Baela_Targaryen) and [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)). The day after the executions, Cregan resigned as Hand.(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### Regency of Aegon III

The [wedding of Aegon III Targaryen and Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen), attended by nobles from throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), joined the two branches of House Targaryen.(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Cregan returned to the north after the wedding and coronation, though many of his [northmen](wiki:Northmen) remained in the south and married riverwomen at [Widow Fairs](wiki:Widow_Fairs).(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

With the war over, a council of seven [regents](wiki:Regent) was established for the young [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). The first seven regents were Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), Lady [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn), Lord [Royce Caron](wiki:Royce_Caron), Ser [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly), Lord [Manfryd Mooton](wiki:Manfryd_Mooton), Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun), and Lord [Roland Westerling](wiki:Roland_Westerling). The regency period was filled with many troubles as the realm struggled to rebuild from the war. Out of the seven original regents, only Munkun serveed the full term. (wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-6)

In [135 AC](wiki:135_AC) the harsh winter finally ended, and in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), when he came of age, King Aegon III dismissed his final regents and Hand.(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-7)

### Changed views on inheritance

The Dance of the Dragons further had influences upon the future Targaryen succession. Upon the death of King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) in [171 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the succession of the throne was unclear. Since Baelor had no children and had not appointed an heir, there were some lords and smallfolk who felt the Iron Throne should pass to the eldest of his sisters, Princess [Daena Targaryen](wiki:Daena_Targaryen). However, other recalled the troubled time when [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) sat the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). The Dance was part of the reason why Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), Baelor's uncle, was chosen to ascend the throne over the wild Daena.(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) By choosing Viserys over Daena, a precedent was set for women coming after all men in Targaryen succession since the Dance.(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-22) During the reign of King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), his niece, Princess [Aelora Targaryen](wiki:Aelora_Targaryen), was made [Princess of Dragonstone](wiki:Princess_of_Dragonstone) even while her uncle and male cousins still lived, though her own suicide prevented her accession to the throne. Following her death, Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) was made heir over Aelora's younger sister, Princess [Daenora](wiki:Daenora_Targaryen).(wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons.cite_ref-23)
