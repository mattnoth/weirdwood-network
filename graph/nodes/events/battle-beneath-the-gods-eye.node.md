## Identity

Battle Beneath the Gods Eye is a event.battle from the AWOIAF wiki.

## Edges

- FIGHTS_IN: Aegon the Uncrowned's rebellion (track_b: Conflict)
- DEFEATS: Prince Aegon is slain (track_b: Result)

## Origins

King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), the half-brother of the late King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [42 AC](wiki:42_AC). At the time Aenys's eldest son, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), was besieged at [Crakehall Castle](wiki:Crakehall_Castle) by thousands of [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows), leaving Aegon unable to act. After Maegor had won his [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) against the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Aegon's mother, Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), proclaimed Aegon king on [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark), to no avail. When the Poor Fellows besieging Crakehall finally marched to King's Landing, Prince Aegon and his sister-wife, Princess [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), were able to depart. They fled to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), where they were given protected by Lord [Lyman Lannister](wiki:Lyman_Lannister). Lady [Jocasta](wiki:Jocasta_Lannister), Lyman's wife, discerned that Rhaena was with child. The princess gave birth to her twin daughters, [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) and [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon)), that year.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Aegon remained adamant about his claim to the throne, but was unable to find many lords who were willing to risk Maegor's wroth by supporting Aegon's claim. Not even Lord Lannister was willing to give his support to Aegon, although he did refuse Maegor's demands that Aegon and Rhaena be expelled from Casterly Rock. Additionally, it was said that Aegon's own mother had abandoned his cause, as she had been present at King's Landing for King Maegor's third marriage.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Many of the young nobles that had accompanied Aegon and Rhaena on their [progress](wiki:Progress) abandoned them to bend the knee to Maegor, the prince gaining the name "Aegon the Uncrowned". However, Lord [Jon Piper](wiki:Jon_Piper) and his sister [Melony](wiki:Melony_Piper), a former favorite of Rhaena, arrived at [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) with their brothers to swear the loyalty of [House Piper](wiki:House_Piper).(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

While Maegor was at [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), Aegon and Rhaena used their uncle's absence at King's Landing to covertly enter the capital, aided by members of Maegor's court that had grown weary of the king's cruelty. In the city, Rhaena retrieved her [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre), while Aegon claimed his father's dragon, [Quicksilver](wiki:Quicksilver).(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) The prince returned to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) on dragonback to gather an army, though Lord Lyman was still reluctant to offer his full support. Instead, Aegon and Rhaena gathered their support at [Pinkmaiden Castle](wiki:Pinkmaiden_Castle), seat of House Piper.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) Eventually, Aegon gained support from lords from the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) and [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), including Lords [Tarbeck](wiki:Alyn_Tarbeck), [Piper](wiki:Jon_Piper), [Roote](wiki:House_Roote), [Vance](wiki:Ronnel_Vance), [Frey](wiki:House_Frey), [Paege](wiki:House_Paege), [Parren](wiki:House_Parren), [Farman](wiki:House_Farman), and [Westerling](wiki:House_Westerling). Additionally, Lord [Corbray](wiki:Qarl_Corbray), [Denys Snow](wiki:Denys_Snow), and the fourth son of Lord [Connington](wiki:House_Connington) joined Aegon's side.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) An additional five hundred men came from Lannisport, led by Ser [Tyler Hill](wiki:Tyler_Hill), Lyman's bastard son.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

Though Aegon's ranks included seasoned commanders and noble knights, (such as Lord [Myles Smallwood](wiki:Myles_Smallwood)(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) and Lord [Qarl Corbray](wiki:Qarl_Corbray)) no great houses came to his side. When Aegon eventually marched in [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), King Maegor was informed by his [mistress of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers), [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower), that Dowager Queen Alyssa Velaryon had been in secret correspondence with the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark), [Arryns](wiki:House_Arryn), [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister), and [Baratheons](wiki:House_Baratheon). However, the great lords wished to be certain of Aegon's ability to win before they pledged their support.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

## Narrative Arc

In [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), Aegon asserted his claim to the throne and denounced King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) as a tyrant and usurper.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) He marched through the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) leading an army of fifteen thousand strong from atop his [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Quicksilver](wiki:Quicksilver).(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) Lord [Lucas Harroway](wiki:Lucas_Harroway) and Lord [Tully](wiki:House_Tully) supported Maegor with their levies, however,(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and Ser [Davos Darklyn](wiki:Davos_Darklyn) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) marched from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with five thousand men.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) From the [Reach](wiki:Reach) came Lords [Peake](wiki:House_Peake), [Merryweather](wiki:House_Merryweather), and [Caswell](wiki:House_Caswell) and their levies.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11) Although each of these armies were smaller than Aegon's force, they were coming from all sides. Only seventeen years old, Aegon was yet an inexperienced commander. He refused to attack and defeat each of Maegor's forces separately before they could join their powers, as Lord Qarl Corbray advised, as he was loathe to divide his army. Instead, he continued his march on the capital.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)

Just south of the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye), Prince Aegon found Davos's force blocking his path sitting on high ground behind a wall of spears, as the forces of the Reach were advancing from the south, and the forces from Lords Tully and Harroway were advancing from the north. Aegon ordered a charge against the hill where the Kingslanders had gathered, hoping to shatter them before the other hosts could flank him. Aegon mounted Quicksilver, intend to lead the attack himself. However, King Maegor I appeared from the south upon the back of [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), who was much larger and stronger than Quicksilver. Balerion the Black Dread fell upon the younger dragon from above, ripping a wing from her body. Quicksilver fell to her death, with Prince Aegon upon her back.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)

Once Aegon fell, the rebels saw their cause lost and ran. They were slaughtered by the loyalists surrounding them as they attempted to escape, and by the end two thousand of Aegon's men had died, including Lord Alyn Tarbeck, Denys Snow, Melony Piper and three of her brothers, Lord Ronnel Vance, Ser [Willam Whistler](wiki:Willam_Whistler), and Lord Farman and his heir. Among Maegor's forces, the losses were fewer. In total a hundred men died, and the only notable loss among them was Ser [Davos Darklyn](wiki:Davos_Darklyn), who had been slain by Lord Corbray with [Lady Forlorn](wiki:Lady_Forlorn).(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10)

## Aftermath

The battle beneath the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye) in the latter half of [43 AC](wiki:43_AC) was followed by a half a year of trials and executions. Dowager Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) managed to persuade King [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) to spare some of the rebellious lords. Maegor demanded lands and titles from the rebels, and took hostages as well to ensure the loyalty of those he had spared.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11)

However, Princess Rhaena had avoided capture, having remained at Pinkmaiden Castle with her daughters and dragon. Upon hearing of the outcome of the battle, Rhaena gathered her daughters and fled on dragonback from the riverlands, fearing her uncle's wroth. While first arriving at Lannisport, she would later flee to [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle) on [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle), the seat of Lord [Marq Farman](wiki:Marq_Farman). His father and brother having perished in the battle, Farman offered Rhaena sanctuary beneath his roof.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13) Meanwhile, with the death of Lord Tarbeck, Maegor married his wife, Lady [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling_(wife_of_Maegor_I)).(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-12)

During the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) led by Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett) targeted and assassinated some of Maegor's loyalists that had participated in the battle, including Old Lord Merryweather, the heir of Lord Peake, and Ser Davos Darklyn's aged father.(wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14)
