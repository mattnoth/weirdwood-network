## Origins

Late during the reign of King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), in [258 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), news reached [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) that the so-called [Band of Nine](wiki:Band_of_Nine), a group of ambitious power-seekers in Essos, had come together under the [Tree of Crowns](wiki:Tree_of_Crowns) where they had vowed to aid one another in carving out kingdoms for each individual member. Among them was [Maelys I Blackfyre](wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre), better known as Maelys the Monstrous, the last of the [Blackfyre Pretenders](wiki:Blackfyre_Pretenders), who had won the command of the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) by killing his cousin [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_Blackfyre_(cousin_of_Maelys_I)) a few years before. His desired kingdom, as [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre)'s last descendant, was the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

When told of these events, Prince [Duncan Targaryen](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen) famously quipped that "crowns were being sold nine a penny", and afterwards the Band of Nine became known in the Seven Kingdoms as the Ninepenny Kings.(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Most men, including King [Aegon V](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) and later King [Jaehaerys II Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen), thought that the threat posed by these pretenders would be countered by the might of the Free Cities, or otherwise founder in Essos.(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Nonetheless, preparations were made to make sure the Blackfyres could not land on Westerosi soil.(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

The Band of Nine their goals met with initial success, conquering the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands) and [securing](wiki:Sack_of_Tyrosh) the Free City of [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), setting up [Alequo Adarys](wiki:Alequo_Adarys), the Silvertongue, as its ruler. Second, they conquered the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones). From there, they stood ready to threaten the Seven Kingdoms.(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

## Aftermath

It took another six years before the [Band of Nine](wiki:Band_of_Nine)'s holdings in [Essos](wiki:Essos) were lost. [Alequo Adarys](wiki:Alequo_Adarys) was eventually poisoned by his queen and the [Archon](wiki:Archon) returned to power in [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh).(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)

With [Ormund](wiki:Ormund_Baratheon) slain, [Steffon Baratheon](wiki:Steffon_Baratheon) became the new [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End). He remained close friends with both Prince [Aerys Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) and Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). Tywin, himself a new-made knight, received the honor of giving Prince Aerys his spurs, after fighting gallantly in the war.(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Tywin became bitter and hardened, particularly against his ineffectual father [Tytos](wiki:Tytos_Lannister), who had not taken part in the war, rather remaining at home with his mistress. These tensions soon erupted in the [Reyne-Tarbeck revolt](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt) in [261 AC](wiki:261_AC).(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) became acquainted with Lord [Baelish](wiki:House_Baelish) during the war, where they fought side by side. Lord Baelish did a service for Hoster, which was later repayed when Hoster accepted to take Baelish's son, [Petyr](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), as a ward in [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-15) [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) also became acquainted with Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and Lord [Steffon Baratheon](wiki:Steffon_Baratheon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), which resulted several years later in Jon taking their sons, [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) and [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), as his wards at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Rawoiafjon_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

The Blackfyres were extinguished in the male line thanks to Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy). When the next opening presented itself, Barristan was named to the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard),(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) an opening he accepted despite being heir to [Harvest Hall](wiki:Harvest_Hall) and being betrothed.(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

The war is described in *[Account of the War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:Account_of_the_War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings)* by [Eon](wiki:Eon) and *[Observations Upon the Recent Blood-Letting on the Stepstones](wiki:Observations_Upon_the_Recent_Blood-Letting_on_the_Stepstones)* by [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle). According to Septon [Meribald](wiki:Meribald), who participated in the war, many of the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) who fought in the war became [broken men](wiki:Broken_men).(wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)
