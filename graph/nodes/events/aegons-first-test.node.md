## Origins

[Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), Lord of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), landed at the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), where he built the [Aegonfort](wiki:Aegonfort) on the northern bank of the river. Aegon's manpower varied from hundreds to three thousand depending on the source. Nearby [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) and [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth) [both](wiki:Yielding_of_Rosby) [submitted](wiki:Submission_of_Stokeworth) to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) after minimal resistance.(wiki:Aegon's_first_test.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Narrative Arc

Opposition came from Lord [Darklyn](wiki:House_Darklyn) of [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) and Lord [Mooton](wiki:House_Mooton) of [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), who joined their power and marched south with three thousand men. Aegon sent [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon) to meet them on the march with the Targaryen soldiers, whilst he descended on them from above with [Balerion](wiki:Balerion). Lords Darklyn and Mooton were both killed in the overwhelming Targaryen victory.(wiki:Aegon's_first_test.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

## Aftermath

After the deaths of their lords, Mooton's brother, [Jon](wiki:Jon_Mooton), and Darklyn's son yielded their castles and swore their swords to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), one of Aegon's sisters, claimed the riches of Duskendale, which at the time was the principal port on the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), greatly expanding the Targaryens' resources. After the submission of a dozen local castles, Lord Aegon was proclaimed King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) at the [Aegonfort](wiki:Aegonfort).(wiki:Aegon's_first_test.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
