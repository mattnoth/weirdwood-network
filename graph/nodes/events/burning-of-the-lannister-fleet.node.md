## Origins

[Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), desired to restore the [Old Way](wiki:Old_Way) after he succeeded his father, Lord [Quellon Greyjoy](wiki:Quellon_Greyjoy), during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). Over five years he constructed the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet),(wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf756_1-0) one hundred warships controlled by the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair) instead of local [lords](wiki:Lord).(wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) One of Balon's younger brothers, [Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), became [Lord Captain of the Iron Fleet](wiki:Lord_Captain_of_the_Iron_Fleet).(wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Aftermath

Balon hoped that the destruction of Tywin Lannister's ships would give the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) naval supremacy over the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea). [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), the [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships), eventually defeated Victarion's Iron Fleet in a [sea battle off Fair Isle](wiki:Sea_battle_off_Fair_Isle), however,(wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) allowing his brother, King Robert, to invade the rebellious [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf756_1-1) Tywin rebuilt the Lannister fleet after the war.(wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet.cite_ref-Rssm1049.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
