## Identity

In the dungeon corridor, moments after freeing him, Jaime confesses to Tyrion the truth about Tysha — Tyrion's first wife, whom Jaime had told him years ago was a paid whore hired to make a man of him. It was a lie Tywin commanded Jaime to tell. Tysha was in fact a real crofter's daughter who loved Tyrion; Tywin had her gang-raped by his guardsmen as a "lesson" and forced Tyrion to watch. The revelation converts Tyrion's gratitude into rage and is the explicit motive-hinge of the whole arc: it is why Tyrion, already free and bound for the sewers, instead climbs 230 rungs to his father's bedchamber to kill him. The agency-collapse principle is satisfied here — the patricide is Tyrion's own choice, driven by (MOTIVATES) this revelation, not a mechanical consequence of it.
