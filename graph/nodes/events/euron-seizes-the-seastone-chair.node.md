## Identity

The day after [Balon Greyjoy's death](death-of-balon-greyjoy), [Euron Greyjoy](euron-greyjoy) — the "Crow's Eye," whom Balon had sent into exile two years earlier on pain of death — sailed into [Lordsport](lordsport) and claimed both [Pyke](pyke) castle and the [Seastone Chair](seastone-chair) as Balon's eldest living brother, sending ravens to summon the captains and the kings of every isle to do him homage as their king. By the law of the green lands the chair belonged to [Theon](theon-greyjoy), or to [Asha](asha-greyjoy) if the prince were dead, but Euron seized it before any kingsmoot could be called. When Lord [Sawane Botley](sawane-botley) objected that the Seastone Chair belonged by rights to Theon, Euron had him drowned in seawater — "If he was drowned, no blood was shed," the pious [Aeron Damphair](aeron-greyjoy) noted, a distinction that mattered under ironborn custom. Euron's premature, godless usurpation is the act that provokes Aeron, who deems him unworthy, into summoning the kingsmoot to deny him.
