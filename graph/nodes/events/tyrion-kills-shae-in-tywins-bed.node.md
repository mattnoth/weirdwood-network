## Identity

Climbing to his father's bedchamber to confront him, Tyrion finds his former lover Shae asleep in Tywin's bed, naked but for a chain of linked golden hands — the Hand of the King's chain — about her throat. She had testified falsely against Tyrion at his trial; now she calls Tywin "m'lord" and, seeing Tyrion, pleads and calls him "my giant of Lannister." Tyrion strangles her with the gold chain. The killing is a co-located beat of the same chamber rampage as the [assassination of Tywin](assassination-of-tywin-lannister) — a separate victim and instrument (the chain, not the crossbow) — and is the moment Tyrion takes the crossbow from the wall before going on to find his father in the privy. It is modeled as a sibling beat on the same causal path (caused by the Tysha revelation that sent Tyrion up the ladder), not as a cause of the patricide; the patricide's motive is Tysha.
