## Origins

When the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) breaks out after the death of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), his brother [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) marries [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) at [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) and claims the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) with the support of [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell).(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Margaery's father, Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell), is eager for her to be a queen,(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) and he also wants to be the grandfather of a king.(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) [Renly is assassinated](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), however, leaving Margaery a widow.(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Robert's brother [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) is defeated in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater),(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) and Margaery agrees to marry Robert's supposed son, King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), as part of an alliance between Houses Tyrell and [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) After Joffrey chokes to death during [their wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding) in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep),(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) the Tyrells insist that Margaery wed Joffrey's younger brother, Prince [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) At the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers), Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) tells [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) that he had conspired with Margaery's grandmother, Lady [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell), to assassinate the volatile Joffrey so that Margaery can marry Joffrey's younger brother, Prince [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
During [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister)'s trial for murder, Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) testifies that Joffrey was [poisoned](wiki:Strangler).(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Tyrion, falsely convicted of killing Joffrey, murders [Shae](wiki:Shae) and his father, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), in the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand) before disappearing from the city.(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
Although [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) is held by Stannis's men, Mace refuses to depart [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) until Margaery weds King Tommen.(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Tommen's mother, the increasingly-paranoid Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), prefers a betrothal and thinks the marriage should be delayed for a year or two. She reluctantly agrees to a wedding to avoid offending [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), however.(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) The Tyrells insist against Cersei's wishes that Margaery and Tommen share a bed, with Cersei agreeing that they can do so for just the wedding night. Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) and the actual father of Joffrey and Tommen, explains to his sister Cersei that Margaery's cousins—[Megga](wiki:Megga_Tyrell), [Alla](wiki:Alla_Tyrell), and [Elinor Tyrell](wiki:Elinor_Tyrell)—will be with the married youths that night, but Cersei orders Jaime to be in the room as well.(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Cersei wants Tommen to wear the red silk cloak which Joffrey had worn at his wedding, it was the same cloak which Cersei's father, Lord Tywin, had used when he married [Joanna Lannister](wiki:Joanna_Lannister). Olenna objects to Tommen wearing the colors of House Lannister, however, and the boy king instead wears the heavy cloth-of-gold cloak worn by Robert [when he married Cersei](wiki:Wedding_of_Robert_I_Baratheon_and_Cersei_Lannister).(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Aftermath

Lady Merryweather becomes Cersei's confidant.(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) The Queen Regent plots against Queen Margaery, eventually having her accused of adultery.(wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
