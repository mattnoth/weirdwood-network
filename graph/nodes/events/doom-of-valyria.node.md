## Identity

Doom of Valyria is a battle/event from the AWOIAF wiki.

## Edges


## Origins

### Foreshadowing[ ]

Twelve years before the Doom, Lord [Aenar Targaryen](wiki:Aenar_Targaryen)'s maiden daughter, [Daenys the Dreamer](wiki:Daenys_the_Dreamer), had a powerful prophetic dream along with visions that [Valyria](wiki:Valyria) would be destroyed.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Foreseeing the Doom, she convinced her father to leave Valyria before the cataclysm. Aenar set sail, moving [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) and their property and their five [dragons](wiki:Dragon) to the island [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)).(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Other Houses of the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) of Valyrian descent include the [Velaryons](wiki:House_Velaryon) of [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) and the [Celtigars](wiki:House_Celtigar) of [Claw Isle](wiki:Claw_Isle).(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### The Doom[ ]

On the day of the Doom, every hill for five hundred miles exploded, filling the air with ash and smoke and fire, which killed even [dragons](wiki:Dragon). Earthquakes destroyed palaces, temples, and towns, while lakes boiled or turned to acid. The [Fourteen Flames](wiki:Fourteen_Flames), the fiery mountains of Valyria, sent molten rock a thousand feet into the air, and red clouds rained down [dragonglass](wiki:Dragonglass). The cataclysm fragmented the [Valyrian peninsula](wiki:Valyrian_peninsula) surrounding [Valyria](wiki:Valyria) into numerous smaller islands and creating the [Smoking Sea](wiki:Smoking_Sea) between them.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) East of Valyria, [Velos](wiki:Velos) and [Ghozai](wiki:Ghozai) on the [Isle of Cedars](wiki:Isle_of_Cedars) were destroyed by a tsunami.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
It is commonly believed that the Doom was a natural calamity caused by the eruption of the Fourteen Flames, although some [septons](wiki:Septons) believe the polytheistic Valyrians delved too deep to the [seven hells](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). Alternatively, Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth) and some [maesters](wiki:Maesters) believe that Valyrian [spells](wiki:Magic) controlling the Fourteen Flames faltered. Other explanations include the curse of [Garin the Great](wiki:Garin_the_Great), the fire of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor), or infighting [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) assassinating fire mages.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

### Aftermath[ ]

Much of [Essos](wiki:Essos) fell into chaos after the Doom. During the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood), the [Volantenes](wiki:Volantis) unsuccessfully considered themselves the heirs to Valyria, often waging war against other [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) The [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) conquered many of the lands surrounding the [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea),(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and the last [Ibbenese](wiki:Ibben) king was thrown down.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) The [Slaver Cities](wiki:Slaver_Cities) of [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) regained their independence.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
Some [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) in [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) survived the Doom, but they were soon killed along with their [dragons](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) The dragonlord [Aurion](wiki:Aurion) declared himself [Emperor of Valyria](wiki:Emperor_of_Valyria) and led a host from [Qohor](wiki:Qohor) toward Valyria, but he and his followers were not seen again.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) [Tommen II Lannister](wiki:Tommen_II_Lannister), [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock), vanished while sailing to Valyria with his blade, [Brightroar](wiki:Brightroar), and [Gerion Lannister](wiki:Gerion_Lannister) later disappeared while searching for Brightroar.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) It is thus said that no one has returned from a journey to the ruins of Valyria, and even the most hardened sailors shudder at the thought of sailing to ruined Valyria.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
Many of [Valyria](wiki:Valyria)'s secrets, such as [magic](wiki:Magic),(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) the method for creating [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel),(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) and ancient records(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) were lost in the Doom. The disaster is represented in art across the [known world](wiki:Known_world).(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Songs are sung of the Doom.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
In [54 AC](wiki:54_AC) during the reign of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), Princess [Aerea Targaryen](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) disappeared from [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) with the dragon [Balerion](wiki:Balerion).(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Balerion returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in [56 AC](wiki:56_AC) with a dying Aerea. Septon Barth, who helped treat her, believed that Balerion had taken Aerea to Valyria, its birthplace. Because of her bizarre and horrific death and the powerful dragon's mysterious wounds, Jaehaerys made traveling to Valyria punishable by execution, and prohibited ships that had visited the area from docking in Westeros.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

As she walks into the entry hall of [Drogo's manse](wiki:Drogo's_manse) in [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) notices a mosaic of colored glass depicting the Doom of Valyria.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

At the [wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), [Collio Quaynis](wiki:Collio_Quaynis) sings a haunting [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian) ballad of two lovers dying amidst the Doom.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

[Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) claims to have reaved all over [the world](wiki:Known_world) during his exile, including a trip to the feared ruins of [Valyria](wiki:Valyria). During the [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk), he claims to have found his [dragon horn](wiki:Dragonbinder) among the smoking ruins that were Valyria, where no man has dared to walk but him.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

In [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), the merchant prince [Xaro Xhoan Daxos](wiki:Xaro_Xhoan_Daxos) gifts Daenerys a [tapestry](wiki:Tapestry) that has been in his family vaults since before the Doom that took Valyria. The old tapestry depicts the [Valyrian peninsula](wiki:Valyrian_peninsula) before its destruction.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
When the [Selaesori Qhoran](wiki:Selaesori_Qhoran) sails east near the coast of Valyria, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) sees a dull red glow lighting the sky.(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
[Ralf the Limper](wiki:Ralf_the_Limper)'s squadron of [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) ships are driven near blighted Valyria by storms while sailing to [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Doom_of_Valyria.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
