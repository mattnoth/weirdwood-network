## Origins

In [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell) and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), tasks his wife [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) with having Ser [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart) and [Galbart Glover](wiki:Galbart_Glover) raise a hundred bowmen each to garrison [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin).(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Catelyn sends a raven from the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) to their son [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark) at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Eddard is imprisoned after the death of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), so Robb marches south with a host to rescue his father. Once at Moat Cailin, Robb decides to leave a small force of mostly archers at the ruined castle.(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) After Eddard is executed in King's Landing, Robb is declared [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

King Robb, seeking allies in what becomes the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), sends [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) as an envoy to his father to offer an alliance between the [north](wiki:North) and the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands). [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), rejects Robb's offer, however, and instead intends to strike at the north, which is weakly defended with the northern forces fighting in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) and the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands). Balon sends Theon, his brother [Aeron Damphair](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), and [Dagmer Cleftjaw](wiki:Dagmer) to [harry the Stony Shore](wiki:Harrying_of_the_Stony_Shore) in hopes that the remaining northern lords will march from their castles. Balon sends his daughter [Asha](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) to [seize Deepwood Motte](wiki:Taking_of_Deepwood_Motte) and his brother [Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) with the majority of the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) to capture Moat Cailin and prevent the northern lords in the south from returning to their seats.(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

## Narrative Arc

Victarion and the Iron Fleet sail up the [Saltspear](wiki:Saltspear) and the [Fever River](wiki:Fever_River) to its headwaters, from which Moat Cailin is less than twenty miles away.(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) The Victarion-led [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) seize the ruined fortifications,(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) which are held by the small northern force.(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Other captains with Victarion include [Red Ralf Stonehouse](wiki:Ralf_Stonehouse), [Ralf Kenning](wiki:Ralf_Kenning), and [Ralf the Limper](wiki:Ralf_the_Limper).(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

## Aftermath

After [harrying the Stony Shore](wiki:Harrying_of_the_Stony_Shore), Theon changes Balon's plan and [captures Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Balon styles himself [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

The news of the seizures of Winterfell and Moat Cailin changes Robb's plans and forces him to return to the north.(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Robb is killed at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), however.(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Moat Cailin is eventually [besieged](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin) and taken by a Bolton-led northern army after Victarion brings most of his men back to the Iron Islands for the [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk).(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)(wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
