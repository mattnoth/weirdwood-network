## Narrative Arc

[Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) is woken from a nightmare, in which he is being chased by [large wolves](wiki:Direwolf) with the faces of children, by his squire, [Wex Pyke](wiki:Wex_Pyke), with [[Reek](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) standing behind him. Reek informs Theon that [Asha](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) has arrived at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). As Theon prepares to see his sister, he thinks back about the nightmares he has been having lately, ever since the night he and Reek killed the two boys at the mill. After washing his face, Theon dresses in rich clothing, only to realize that Asha cares more for blades. He changes again, this time dressing in felted black wool and ringmail. Lastly, he dons his crown.
Theon has taken to using guards for his own safety, while his own men have become sullen and anxious following the murders of [Gelmarr](wiki:Gelmarr), [Aggar](wiki:Aggar), and [Gynir](wiki:Gynir). Although the three [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) had been killed by Reek with Theon's permission, to ensure they would not be able to reveal what had happened at the mill the night they returned with the corpses of the children, Theon had felt the need to blame someone for their deaths. To that end, he had executed [Farlen](wiki:Farlen), going as far as to personally execute him like [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) would have done. The memory is a sour one, as it had taken him four difficult blows to sever Farlen's head, afterward feeling sick about it.
His men would prefer to leave Winterfell, a thought that makes Theon angry. He refuses to be driven from his own seat, being the [Prince of Winterfell](wiki:Prince_of_Winterfell). He blames Asha, feeling she had taken this long to respond to his commands on purpose. Reek, [Urzen](wiki:Urzen) and [Kromm](wiki:Kromm) escort Theon to the hall, where he finds Asha in the high seat of the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark). Theon is dismayed to discover that Asha brought only twenty men. In turn, she scolds him for having killed [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) and [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark) and having mounted their heads upon Winterfell's gates. The memory of returning the bodies to Winterfell is a sour one for Theon as well. He recalls forbidding [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) from sewing the heads back onto the bodies and placing them in the [crypts of Winterfell](wiki:Crypts_of_Winterfell). Theon leads Asha to Eddard Stark's solar, where he tells her that [Dagmer](wiki:Dagmer) has lost the fight at [Torrhen's Square](wiki:Torrhen's_Square). Asha tells him she was not surprised to learn about it, and informs him that Dagmer is currently leading the survivors back to the [Stony Shore](wiki:Stony_Shore), making Theon realize that she is better informed than he is.
Theon is furious about the fact that Asha is planning on leaving only ten of her men behind for him. [Leobald Tallhart](wiki:Leobald_Tallhart) has joined Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel), Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) has sent a dozen barges upriver, and the [Umbers](wiki:House_Umber) are gathering beyond the [Last River](wiki:Last_River). Asha compliments her brother for the clever way he [took Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell), but tells him that the wise course of action would have been razing the castle and taking the two princes as hostages back to [Pyke](wiki:Pyke). Winterfell is too far from the sea, and every northman is now his enemy. Theon counters her, telling her that he killed two of Eddard Stark's sons to lay to rest the ghosts of their brothers, [Rodrik](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy) and [Maron](wiki:Maron_Greyjoy). Asha tells him that Rodrik might feel the need to avenge Eddard's sons and asks Theon to return with her to [Deepwood Motte](wiki:Deepwood_Motte). When Theon refuses, Asha leaves, telling him he shall hold the castle for the remainder of his life.
Asha departs the castle with her men and Theon watches her go from atop the wall, where he is approached by Reek. Reek offers to find Theon two hundred good men if Theon provides a horse and a bag of coin. He asks for [Palla](wiki:Palla) as a reward. Although Reek makes Theon uneasy, he does not dare kill the man, as he is literate and cunning, and could easily have left a hidden account of what they had done behind. Theon agrees to give him the girl if he can deliver two hundred men, and Reek leaves the castle before the sun goes down. That night Theon dreams about the welcoming feast Eddard Stark had thrown King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) when the king visited Winterfell. Although the feast is jolly at first, the room grows darker and suddenly Theon finds himself dining with the dead. Robert's guts are spilling out on the table from a great gash in his belly, while Eddard Stark is headless. Lining the benches below are the corpses of men he knows: [Jory Cassel](wiki:Jory_Cassel), [Fat Tom](wiki:Tomard), [Porther](wiki:Porther), [Cayn](wiki:Cayn), [Hullen](wiki:Hullen), and all the others who had gone south to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). [Mikken](wiki:Mikken), [Chayle](wiki:Chayle), [Benfred Tallhart](wiki:Benfred_Tallhart) and his [Wild Hares](wiki:Wild_Hares), the [miller's wife](wiki:Miller's_wife_from_Acorn_Water), [Farlen](wiki:Farlen), and the [wildling](wiki:Wildling) Theon had killed to rescue Bran are among them. There are also people who Theon has never met, including [Rickard](wiki:Rickard_Stark), [Lyanna](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), and [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark). Then, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) enters through the doors with [Grey Wind](wiki:Grey_Wind) beside him, both of them bleeding "from half a hundred savage wounds".
Theon wakes screaming, and Wex and his guards fetch Luwin, but Theon does not dare drink the sleeping draught that the [maester](wiki:Maester) leaves him. Instead, he summons [Kyra](wiki:Kyra) and fucks her, but even that cannot help him sleep again, and she leaves sobbing of pain. At dawn, Theon goes outside. Seeing everything belonging to the Starks around him, he thinks to himself he should have gone with Asha. In silence, he looks at the heads mounted atop the iron spikes of the gatehouse, reflecting on what fools people could be. Although everyone had accepted his story, the heads on the wall actually belong to the miller's sons, who had been of an age and coloring with the two missing Starks. With their faces flayed, no one had been able to tell the difference.(wiki:A_Clash_of_Kings-Chapter_56.cite_ref-1) Theon never found Bran and Rickon.
