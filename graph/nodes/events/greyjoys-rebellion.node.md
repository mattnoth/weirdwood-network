## Aftermath

[Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) surrendered, his two eldest sons [Rodrik](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy) and [Maron](wiki:Maron_Greyjoy) were killed, and his last surviving son, [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), was made a ward of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) as hostage. [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s victory cemented his hold on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), having overthrown the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen) six years before. He threw a great [tourney at Lannisport](wiki:Tourney_at_Lannisport) to celebrate his victory.(wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

The rebellion resulted in the [castle of Pyke](wiki:Pyke) being severely damaged and partially rebuilt. The [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) suffered abuse in the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) at the hands of fanatics who worship the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God). [Baelor Blacktyde](wiki:Baelor_Blacktyde), [Lord of Blacktyde](wiki:Lord_of_Blacktyde), was a child when [his father](wiki:Lord_Blacktyde) died in the war. As a result he was taken away to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) as a hostage following the end of the rebellion. He returned eight years later as a follower of the Seven.(wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)
