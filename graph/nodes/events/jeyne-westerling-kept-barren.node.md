# Sybell Spicer keeps Jeyne Westerling barren

On [Tywin Lannister](tywin-lannister)'s orders, [Lady Sybell Spicer](sybell-spicer) ensured that her
daughter [Jeyne Westerling](jeyne-westerling) bore [Robb Stark](robb-stark) no child during their
marriage — a covert sabotage that denied the Young Wolf an heir who could have carried his cause and
claim beyond his death. Sybell confirmed the act directly to [Jaime Lannister](jaime-lannister) at
Riverrun after Robb's death at the [Red Wedding](red-wedding): when Jaime observed that Jeyne was not
with child, Sybell answered, "I made certain of that, as your lord father bid me." The act was one
piece of the broader understanding between Sybell and Tywin — the price of which was a royal pardon for
House Westerling, [Castamere](castamere) for her brother [Ser Rolph Spicer](rolph-spicer), and worthy
marriages for her daughters.

Jeyne herself was a victim, not a party, to her mother's scheme: she grieved Robb sincerely and rode
from Riverrun in torn mourning clothes. The **specific means** Sybell used are never stated in the
published books.

This betrayal is the hidden mechanism beneath the Westerling marriage trap — the marriage that broke
Robb's [Frey pact](robb-weds-jeyne-westerling) and led to the Red Wedding was also engineered to leave
no Stark heir behind it.
