## Origins

During the [riots of King's Landing](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd) led thousands in the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit). Prince [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon) pleaded that [knights](wiki:Knight) should be used to help the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) in dispersing the mob, but the boy's mother, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), insisted on keeping her knights back to defend the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) if needed. In order to save the [dragons](wiki:Dragon) of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), Joffrey departed the castle on Rhaenyra's dragon, [Syrax](wiki:Syrax).(wiki:Seven_Who_Rode.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) wrote about the seven knights as men of honor, bound by duty to obey their queen. Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) proposes they had been moved by Rhaenyra's love for her son. According to [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom), however, they were dolts and dastards, eager for reward, and too dull to believe they might die. Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), for his part, agrees in part with each the three chroniclers.(wiki:Seven_Who_Rode.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Joffrey was thrown off Syrax's back during his flight over [Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom), with the fall killing him. The Seven recovered most of the prince's remains from the mob which looted the boy's corpse, although Ser [Gyles Yronwood](wiki:Gyles_Yronwood), Ser [Willam Royce](wiki:Willam_Royce), and Lord Commander [Glendon Goode](wiki:Glendon_Goode) were killed during their mission.(wiki:Seven_Who_Rode.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
