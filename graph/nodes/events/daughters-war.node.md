## Origins

The [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy), called the Kingdom of the Three Daughters by [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros), was an alliance formed in [96 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) by [Lys](wiki:Lys), [Myr](wiki:Myr), [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), all former colonies of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold).(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) The Triarchy supported King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and the [greens](wiki:Greens) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), sacking [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) but suffering major losses against the [Velaryon fleet](wiki:Velaryon_fleet) in the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet) in early [130 AC](wiki:130_AC).(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), taken captive in the battle, was held first by the Triarchy's admiral, [Sharako Lohar](wiki:Sharako_Lohar) of Lys, and later by the Lysene [magister](wiki:Magister) [Bambarro Bazanne](wiki:Bambarro_Bazanne).(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Sharako was criticized by Myrmen and Tyroshi for sending their ships into the bloody battle and sparing Lysene ships from heavy casualties.(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) In late [131 AC](wiki:131_AC), Sharako was slain by a rival for the favor of [Johanna Swann](wiki:Johanna_Swann), the courtesan called the Black Swan. The Lyseni thought it was a political murder orchestrated by the Myrmen,(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) however, and the murders which followed led to war.(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) After the end of the Dance in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC), Ser [Marston Waters](wiki:Marston_Waters) reported that the Triarchy was collapsing and that the three [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) were hiring [free companies](wiki:Free_companies). The [Wolf Pack](wiki:Wolf_Pack) and the [Stormbreakers](wiki:Stormbreakers) were Westerosi sellswords who traveled to [Essos](wiki:Essos) for the coming conflict.(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

### War

While [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Myr](wiki:Myr) fought each other, the [Archon of Tyrosh](wiki:Archon_of_Tyrosh) sent [Racallio Ryndoon](wiki:Racallio_Ryndoon) to conquer the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones). After killing the reigning [King of the Narrow Sea](wiki:King_of_the_Narrow_Sea), however, Racallio claimed the Stepstones for himself. The war hampered trade in [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown), [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), and [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), but Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), declined the entreating of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), [Lorath](wiki:Lorath), and [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) to involve the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in the war.(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

The [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) and the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands) were engulfed in war between Lyseni, Myrmen, Tyroshi, Racallio, and the triple alliance of Braavosi, Lorathi, and Pentoshi in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC).(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) The Lyseni suffered a series of defeats, and Magister [Bambarro Bazanne](wiki:Bambarro_Bazanne) was betrayed by sellswords in the Disputed Lands in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC). Bambarro's captive, [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), came under the control of [Lysandro Rogare](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare), who wed the prince to his daughter, [Larra of Lys](wiki:Larra_Rogare).(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

By [133 AC](wiki:133_AC) most of Racallio's naval strength had been defeated, although he still controlled [Bloodstone](wiki:Bloodstone) and some smaller islands. When it appeared that the Tyroshi would soon defeat Racallio, the Lyseni and Myrmen made peace and jointly attacked Tyrosh. Although Lorath had left the war, Pentos occupied the remaining Stepstones and their Braavosi allies controlled the nearby waters.(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Desiring to reopen trade in the narrow sea, the new Hand of the King, Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake), tasked Ser [Gedmund Peake](wiki:Gedmund_Peake), [Ned Bean](wiki:Ned_Bean), and Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) with defeating Racallio and establishing a Westerosi presence on Bloodstone. When the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) was at [Tarth](wiki:Tarth), Lord [Bryndemere Tarth](wiki:Bryndemere_Tarth) informed that Racallio had allied with the Archon of Tyrosh and the [Sealord of Braavos](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos), discarding the [Prince of Pentos](wiki:Prince_of_Pentos). This new alliance agreed to jointly rule the Stepstones and control trade. While Gedmund, the admiral of the fleet, waited for further instructions from Unwin, Alyn took it upon himself to lead the Velaryon ships south. The Braavosi fleet among the Stepstones was crushed in [Lord Alyn's attack](wiki:Lord_Alyn's_attack), with Alyn sinking *[Grand Defiance](wiki:Grand_Defiance)* with the ram of *[Queen Rhaenys](wiki:Queen_Rhaenys)*.(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Although Alyn returned to King's Landing as a hero, he had failed to capture any of the islands and Racallio remained powerful. Lord Unwin sent Lord [Manfryd Mooton](wiki:Manfryd_Mooton) to Braavos to negotiate the avoidance of open war between the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) and the formidable Free City. In return for a large indemnity, the Sealord ended its alliance with Tyrosh. The Sealord also ceded the Stepstones to Westeros, although the islands remained occupied by Racallio and Pentos.(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

Unwin sent his rival, Lord Alyn, to resolve the threat of Lord [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy) and the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) to the western coast of Westeros. Doing so, however, would mean that Alyn would need to sail past the dangerous Stepstones. When Alyn sailed south on *[Lady Baela](wiki:Lady_Baela)*, Racallio controlled Bloodstone and the southern Stepstones, while the northern and eastern isles were controlled by Pentoshi sellswords hired by Tyrosh. Alyn sailed to Bloodstone under a parley flag but was held as a captive and guest by Racallio for more than a fortnight. Racallio eventually allowed Alyn to sail his fleet past the Stepstones in return for three ships, an alliance, and a future kiss from Alyn's wife, Lady [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen).(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

While at [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) during his return from the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), Alyn Oakenfist learned from Princess [Aliandra Martell](wiki:Aliandra_Martell) that [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) had allied with Lys and Tyrosh against Racallio.(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Sailing to Lys, Alyn negotiated with [House Rogare](wiki:House_Rogare) for the return of Prince Viserys Targaryen to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC).(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

### Aftermath

The Daughters' War ended in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC). After [Racallio Ryndoon](wiki:Racallio_Ryndoon) fled to the [Basilisk Isles](wiki:Basilisk_Isles), [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) took control over most of the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones). The [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands) were divided between the former Triarchy, [Lys](wiki:Lys), [Myr](wiki:Myr), and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh). [Aliandra Martell](wiki:Aliandra_Martell), [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne), and the [Archon of Tyrosh](wiki:Archon_of_Tyrosh) had the greatest gains from the war, while Myr had lost the most. Lys had begun the war in the strongest position out of the Three Daughters, and for a brief period of about one year afterwards its power waxed during the so-called [Lyseni Spring](wiki:Lyseni_Spring) of 135 AC. By 136 AC, however, the [Rogare Bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank) collapsed and Lys fell into internal strife as houses and [magisters](wiki:Magisters) fought one another.(wiki:Daughters'_War.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
