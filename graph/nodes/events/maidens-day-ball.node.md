## Origins

Young King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen)'s first marriage to his cousin Princess [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen) had ended tragically in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), with Jaehaera's apparent suicide. Afterwards, Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake), who was the king's [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and [regent](wiki:Regent), planned a betrothal between Aegon and his daughter, [Myrielle Peake](wiki:Myrielle_Peake). [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) and [Kermit Tully](wiki:Kermit_Tully) were among the lords that disapproved of the match, and [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun)'s support began to waver, acknowledging that the match would be seen as advancing [House Peake](wiki:House_Peake)'s interests rather than being for the good of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). Lord [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly), Lady [Johanna Lannister](wiki:Johanna_Lannister), Lady [Elenda Baratheon](wiki:Elenda_Baratheon), and Lady [Sharis Footly](wiki:Sharis_Footly) wrote to the crown, proposing their own relatives as Aegon's bride (or themselves, in Lady Footly's case). Lady [Samantha Hightower](wiki:Samantha_Tarly) not only named her sister and sister-in-law, who she suggested could [both marry](wiki:Polygamy) Aegon, but thirty-one other eligible maidens of the [Reach](wiki:Reach). ([Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) unverifiably adds that she said she could name pretty boys as well, if Aegon should be so inclined, though that would not solve the issue of heirs.) Due to the opposition, Peake announced a ball in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), where the king himself would be able to choose his own bride.
To limit the attendance, Peake restricted the ball to [maidens](wiki:Maiden) of [nobility](wiki:Nobility) under the age of thirty. Additionally, many likely maidens were injured, scandalized, or even died before the ball, and men spoke of the "[Maiden's Day curse](wiki:Maiden's_Day_curse)". Many of these events were probably engineered by Lord Peake, who still hoped to have his daughter be chosen queen. Nevertheless, in hopes of marrying the king, more than one thousand young women from throughout [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), and even [Essos](wiki:Essos), crowded into the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) on the day of the ball.(wiki:Maiden's_Day_Ball.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
