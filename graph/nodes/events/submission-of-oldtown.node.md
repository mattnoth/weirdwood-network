## Origins

As a result of the defiance of many "pious lords", Dowager Queen [Viseyna](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) and King Maegor I [burned](wiki:Burning_of_the_seats_of_the_pious_lords) down their seats in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) and [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) with [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) and [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), respectively. Then they turned south and flew to Oldtown, as the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I)) had continued his denunciation against the King and had even issued a proclamation commanding the monarch to present himself before the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept) and beg the forgiveness of the gods.(wiki:Submission_of_Oldtown.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

## Aftermath

[Pater](wiki:High_Septon_(Pater)), an amiable [septon](wiki:Septon) of ninety years, blind, stooped and feeble was chosen as the new head of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). The aged man, despite forgetting the words, eagerly blessed and annointed Maegor with holy oils.

The King would remain Oldtown for almost half the year, holding court and presiding trials. The captive Warrior's Sons were given the choice of taking the black or being executed. Three quarters of the knights chose the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). Of those who chose death, seven were beheaded by the king himself wielding [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre), on account of their status as sons of lords or renowned knights; the rest were condemned to be decapitated by their own former brothers. Ser [Morgan Hightower](wiki:Morgan_Hightower), meanwhile, was pardoned by Maegor.(wiki:Submission_of_Oldtown.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

After the executions, the High Septon announced the dissolution of both Warrior's Sons and [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows), as the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) would protect the Faith thereafter. King Maegor granted the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) until the end of the year to surrender their arms return to the [king's peace](wiki:King's_peace). Afterwards, the remaining rebels would be subject of a bounty: a gold dragon for the head of a Warrior's Son and a silver stag for the scalp of a Poor Fellow.(wiki:Submission_of_Oldtown.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

The king also reconciled with his first wife, [Ceryse Hightower](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower), who agreed to accept the king's other wives, to honor and respect them, whilst she was restored to all her rights, incomes and privileges as wife and queen. A great feast, a bedding and a "second consummation" were held at the Hightower to celebrate the reconciliation.(wiki:Submission_of_Oldtown.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)
