## Origins

Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) was one of the leaders of the [rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) that overthrew King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), the Mad King, ending the Targaryen Dynasty.(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.E2.80.99s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) Following the defeat of the Targaryen forces and Aerys's death in [283 AC](wiki:283_AC), Robert was crowned and reigned as king over all the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Securing his power, Robert wed [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) in [284 AC](wiki:284_AC) following the advice of his foster-father and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn).(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_glorious_reign.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-5) Within a few years the royal succession was secured by three heirs: [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) (b. [286 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) (b. [290 AC](wiki:290_AC)), and [Tommen](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) (b. [291 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)).(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Cal_6-0) However, whilst Robert and the realm believed Cersei's three children to have been fathered by Robert, they had actually been fathered by [Ser](wiki:Knight) [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), Queen Cersei's twin brother, with whom she had been having an incestuous relationship for years.(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

However, King Robert's brother, [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), eventually became suspicious of the legitimacy of Robert’s three children. He shared his suspicions with Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), still serving as the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Together, they investigated the case and concluded that the children were not Robert's, but the product of incest between Queen Cersei and her twin brother Jaime. Before they are able to address the issue to the King, however, Lord Arryn unexpectedly dies. While King Robert traveled [North](wiki:North) to appoint a new Hand of the King, Stannis, suspecting that Arryn had been murdered by the Lannisters because of their discovery regarding Cersei’s children, fled to [his own stronghold](wiki:Dragonstone) on the island of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)).

Thus, Robert remained oblivious to these suspicions concerning his children, and traveled with a large part of the royal court to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to appoint his old friend, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), as Arryn's successor as Hand of the King.(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Lord Eddard had originally considered to turn down the offer, but changed his mind upon the arrival of a secret message from [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), Jon Arryn's widow and the sister to Lord Eddard's wife, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark). Lysa claimed that the Lannisters had murdered her husband, and causing Eddard to feel forced to accept Robert's appointment, as to be able to investigate Arryn's death and the involvement of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Upon Robert’s suggestion, Eddard also accepts to a betrothal between his daughter [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

While the royal court is at Winterfell, Lord Eddard's younger son [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) accidentally witnesses a romantic moment between Queen Cersei and Ser Jaime, and is thrown from a tower window to silence him.(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Bran's condition is severe, and he lies comatose for several weeks.(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) After the royal court (including Bran's father, Lord Eddard, and his sisters [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark)) have left Winterfell for [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), an assassin attempts to murder Bran in his bed. He is halted by Bran's mother, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Tully), injuring her in the process, before being killed by Bran's direwolf pet, [Summer](wiki:Summer).(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) To inform her husband of the attempt on Bran's life, Catelyn travels south to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), where her childhood friend [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) claims that the assassin's weapon used belonged to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), the younger brother of Queen Cersei.(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

While Lord Eddard begins investigating Arryn's death, Catelyn crosses paths with Tyrion Lannister at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn). Deciding to arrest him, Catelyn charges him with the attempt on Bran's life, which leads to the start of conflicts in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Meanwhile, in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Eddard’s investigation into Arryn's death has eventually independently led him to discover the truth about Queen Cersei's children. He confronts her about the matter, offering her the option to flee, which she refuses.(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Eddard intends to share his discoveries with the king when Robert returns from his [boar](wiki:Boar) hunt. However, Robert returns having been fatally wounded by the boar, in a hunting incident which Cersei had orchestrated).(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Robert's younger brother and [master of laws](wiki:Master_of_laws), [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), unaware of the illegitimacy of Cersei's children, proposes to Eddard that they take Cersei's children hostage, as to prevent the Lannisters from seizing power after Robert's death. Eddard refuses him, however, and Renly departs the city for [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), the seat of [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell).(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

After Robert's death, Eddard attempts a coup, sharing with the small council that Robert's brother [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), and not [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), is Robert's true heir. He is betrayed by [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) and the [City Watch](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing), however, and arrested and imprisoned by the Lannisters.(wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)
