## Origins

Although the uprising began during the reign of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), the first seeds were already planted during the reign of his father, King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen). Before the birth of Aenys's children, his younger half-brother [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) was regarded as his heir. However, Aenys was wed in [22 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and his first child, Princess [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), was born the next year. Her birth sparked the discussion of the line of succession, as it was unclear whether Maegor remained second in the line of succession or whether he fell behind the newborn Rhaena. In [24 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), shortly after Maegor turned twelve, his mother Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) proposed a betrothal between Rhaena and Maegor to settle the issue of the royal succession. However, both Prince Aenys and [his wife](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) and the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon_I)) protested.(wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The High Septon suggested his own niece, Lady [Ceryse Hightower](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower), as a bride for the young prince instead. Maegor and Ceryse were subsequently wed in [25 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) at the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept) in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), in a ceremony officiated by the High Septon himself.(wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

After fourteen years of childless marriage, Maegor suddenly announced in [39 AC](wiki:39_AC) that Ceryse was barren and that he had taken another wife, Lady [Alys Harroway](wiki:Alys_Harroway), the daughter of the new [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal), [Lucas Harroway](wiki:Lucas_Harroway). Unable to convince the [septon](wiki:Septon) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) to perform the wedding, Maegor had taken Alys to wife in a Valyrian wedding ceremony led by the Dowager Queen, Visenya.

Maegor's second marriage angered many. Ceryse's father, Lord [Martyn Hightower](wiki:Martyn_Hightower), protested the marriage to King Aenys and demanded that Maegor set Alys aside. The High Septon denounced the marriage as sin and fornication and called Alys "this whore of Harroway." Many of the pious lords in the realm similarly condemned the marriage, and openly began to call Alys "Maegor's Whore". Maegor remained defiant, however, citing that his father had taken two wives as well, claiming that the strictures of the Faith did not rule [the blood of the dragon](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

Maegor quarreled bitterly with his brother over the marriage, as the king had neither known of the marriage prior to the ceremony, nor given his leave for it. Eventually, Aenys made Maegor choose between setting Alys aside or going into exile for five years. Maegor chose the latter, and left for [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) in [40 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) The High Septon was not yet satisfied, however, so Aenys appointed Septon [Murmison](wiki:Murmison) as his new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), but even he could not heal the rift between the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and the Faith.(wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

The situation worsened in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC), when Aenys wed his daughter [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) and son [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) to one another. The High Septon sent Aenys a denunciation, addressing him as "King Abomination". Even the pious lords and [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) who had loved Aenys turned against the king.(wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

## Aftermath

Prince Jaehaerys ascended the throne in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC) following Maegor's death. He was crowned by the High Septon, and he declared an amnesty for the surviving Faith Militant. While he wished to disband the holy orders, Jaehaerys appointed [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett) to his [kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) where he served faithfully for many years. Septon [Moon](wiki:Moon) and his less reputable rabble of followers marched on [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) to try to have him formally declared High Septon, but he received no support from major lords. Moon was assassinated in the night and his followers quickly dispersed (many suspect Moon's death was ordered by the Hightowers but accounts differ).

The first dozen years of young Jaehaerys's reign would be spent trying to reunite a realm strained by his uncle's tyranny, though thankfully Jaehaerys proved to be an adept diplomat. Moreover the royal treasury was seriously depleted, so Jaehaerys had to take out massive loans from the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos) and then spend years adjusting the tax code to try to pay off the interest without inflaming the lords of the realm to rebellion again.

About a decade into his reign, he became acquainted with [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Barth](wiki:Barth), who tended to the books and records in the Red Keep's library. Jaehaerys soon named him [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), a position he kept for forty years.(wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)(wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Together with Barth, Jaehaerys formally re-conciliated the Iron Throne with the Faith. The Poor Fellows and Warrior's Sons, much reduced in number, were still present and restless, eager to restore their orders. Jaehaerys sent Septon Barth to Oldtown to speak with the High Septon. It was agreed that the Iron Throne would always protect and defend the Faith, if the last few Stars and Swords would put down their weapons, and if the Faith agreed to accept justice from the Iron Throne instead of the Faith.(wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) The Faith accepted Targaryen marriage practices in the [Doctrine of Exceptionalism](wiki:Doctrine_of_Exceptionalism).(wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Two years after Jaehaerys ascended the throne, members of the Faith Militant and Maegor's loyalists who had been exiled to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) made common cause in a [rebellion against the leadership](wiki:Night's_Watch_rebellion_of_50_AC) of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) to try to establish their own lordships, requiring the intervention of Lord [Walton Stark](wiki:Walton_Stark) to crush the traitors.(wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
