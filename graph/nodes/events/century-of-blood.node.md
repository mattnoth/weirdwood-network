## Origins

The [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) defeated the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis) and took control of the [Slaver Cities](wiki:Slaver_Cities),(wiki:Century_of_Blood.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) forced the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar) into exile,(wiki:Century_of_Blood.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ten_thousand_ship.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and founded eight of the nine [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities)—[Braavos](wiki:Braavos), the ninth and youngest of the Free Cities, was founded by escaped [slaves](wiki:Slavery) from Valyria.(wiki:Century_of_Blood.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) The power of Valyria eventually encompassed the western half of [Essos](wiki:Essos), ranging from the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) to [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Century_of_Blood.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
In [102 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest)(wiki:Century_of_Blood.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) the Valyrian Freehold collapsed when the [Valyrian peninsula](wiki:Valyrian_peninsula) was destroyed in the [Doom](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria). Within mere hours, [Valyria](wiki:Valyria) was gone and the Freehold was destroyed. It is said that the fire and smoke was so hot that even [dragons](wiki:Dragon) were consumed. The destruction of Valyria strongly reduced the number of [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) left in the [known world](wiki:Known_world).(wiki:Century_of_Blood.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) had relocated to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), the westernmost outpost of Valyria located in the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), twelve years before with their dragons, thereby ensuring their survival.(wiki:Century_of_Blood.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Some other dragonlords — those who had not been in Valyria when the Doom occurred — survived as well. In [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Qohor](wiki:Qohor), it is said that dragonlords were spared, but they did not long outlive the Doom.(wiki:Century_of_Blood.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)
