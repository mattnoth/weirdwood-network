## Origins

King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) led an [invasion of the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands) as part of the [Wars of Conquest](wiki:Wars_of_Conquest). One of the claimants opposing Aegon was [Lodos](wiki:Lodos), a barefoot holy man who claimed to be the son of the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God).(wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned's_revolt.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragons_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) Lodos, unsuccessful in his call for [krakens](wiki:Kraken) to destroy the fleets from the [green lands](wiki:Green_lands), filled his robes with stones and walked into the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea), claiming he would take counsel with the Drowned God. Thousands of his followers did the the same, and their drowned bodies continued to wash up on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk) in the following years, although the body of Lodos was never found.(wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned's_revolt.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragons_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned's_revolt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Following the death of Aegon the Conqueror and the coronation of his son, [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), in [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), revolts broke out throughout [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), including [Harren the Red's rebellion](wiki:Harren_the_Red's_rebellion) in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), [Jonos Arryn's rebellion](wiki:Jonos_Arryn's_rebellion) in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), and the [Second Dornish War](wiki:Second_Dornish_War). In the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands), a priest-king calling himself [Lodos the Twice-Drowned](wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned) claimed to have returned at last from visiting the Drowned God.(wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned's_revolt.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

[Goren Greyjoy](wiki:Goren_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), dealt with "King" Lodos's rebellion himself. Lord Greyjoy descended on Old Wyk and [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk), where Lodos's support was strongest, with a hundred longships, and the pretender's followers were cut down by the thousands. Goren had Lodos's head pickled in brine and sent to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) as a gift for Aenys.(wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned's_revolt.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Pleased by Lord Greyjoy's actions, King Aenys offered his vassal a boon of his chance. Goren requested permission to expel [septons](wiki:Septon) and [septas](wiki:Septa) from the Iron Islands and thereby halt conversions from the Drowned God to the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), to which Aenys agreed.(wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned's_revolt.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
