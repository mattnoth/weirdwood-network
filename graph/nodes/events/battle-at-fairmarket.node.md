## Origins

Harwyn's [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) defeated first [Samwell Rivers](wiki:Samwell_Rivers) and then Lady [Agnes Blackwood](wiki:Agnes_Blackwood), being aided in the latter battle by Agnes's rival, Lord [Lothar Bracken](wiki:Lothar_Bracken). The rivermen resistance to the ironborn ended, but King [Arrec Durrandon](wiki:Arrec_Durrandon) marched from the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) with a great host. A score of river lords, including the [Brackens](wiki:House_Bracken), [Charltons](wiki:House_Charlton), [Goodbrooks](wiki:House_Goodbrook), [Paeges](wiki:House_Paege), and [Vyprens](wiki:House_Vypren) allied with Harwyn in hopes of an independent riverlands. Arrec's stormlanders were thus harassed and denied supplies as they marched north.(wiki:Battle_at_Fairmarket.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

## Narrative Arc

Harwyn met Arrec at [Fairmarket](wiki:Fairmarket) on the [Blue Fork](wiki:Blue_Fork). Arrec had half again as many stormlanders as Harwyn's ironborn and rivermen, but the former were ill-supplied and tired. Although outnumbered, the longships of the ironborn allowed them to seize the river crossing. Arrec was headstrong and indecisive in the battle, which turned into a shattering defeat for the Durrandons, who lost half their men. Two of Arrec's brothers fell in the fighting, although Arrec was able to escape.(wiki:Battle_at_Fairmarket.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Battle_at_Fairmarket.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Aftermath

The rivermen were initially joyous after the battle. Instead of restoring them independence, however, Harwyn claimed the riverlands, making him the first [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers).
