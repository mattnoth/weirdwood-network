## Origins

[Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), the queen consort of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), was widowed after the death of her husband in [42 AC](wiki:42_AC). Their son, [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), became king in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC). Meanwhile, Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) had been a widower since the death of his young wife. Jaehaerys was privately opposed to Rogar marrying his mother without asking for royal permission. The king did not publicly object to the marriage, however, as Jaehaerys had not opposed the recent [marriage](wiki:Wedding_of_Rhaena_Targaryen_and_Androw_Farman) of his sister, the widowed Queen [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), to [Androw Farman](wiki:Androw_Farman).(wiki:Golden_Wedding.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Lords and knights from throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), as well as envoys from [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) and the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), came to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) for the lavish wedding. Many nobles used the wedding as an opportunity to meet the new king, who sat the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) after King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). Rogar entertained guests with drinking, feasting, gambling, hawking, and hunting. According to an unconfirmed tale spread by [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), Rogar and his kin celebrated with seven maidens and twoscore other women from [Lysene](wiki:Lys) [pleasure housess](wiki:Pleasure_houses). Princess [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) hosted with grace the noblewomen who came for the wedding.(wiki:Golden_Wedding.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
The ceremony occurred on the seventh day of the seventh month of [49 AC](wiki:49_AC), and at least forty thousand smallfolk are said to have gathered in the partially-constructed [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) at the [Hill of Rhaenys](wiki:Hill_of_Rhaenys). Rogar wore gold and an antlered halfhelm, while Alyssa wore a [gemstone](wiki:Gemstone)-covered greatcloak. Jaehaerys and Alysanne impressed the crowd by arriving on their dragons, [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor) and [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing). After the [High Septon](wiki:High_Lickspittle) performed the marriage rites inside the Dragonpit, the wedding party proceeded to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). The most prestigious attendees feasted in the throne room, while lesser nobles gathered in yards and small halls.(wiki:Golden_Wedding.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
The wedding was followed by seven days of feasting and [tourneys](wiki:Tourney), including the famous [War for the White Cloaks](wiki:War_for_the_White_Cloaks). A mock sea battle was also staged in [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay). The Golden Wedding delighted the realm, for Jaehaerys was seen as a promising young ruler and Rogar as a generous Hand. The smallfolk and merchants of King's Landing also profited greatly from the visitors.(wiki:Golden_Wedding.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Rogar's indulgent behavior before the wedding was remembered by the cautious Jaehaerys, however, and the two had a falling out after the king chose to marry Alysanne.(wiki:Golden_Wedding.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
