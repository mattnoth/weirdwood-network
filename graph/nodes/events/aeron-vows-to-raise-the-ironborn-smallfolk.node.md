## Identity

After [Euron wins the kingsmoot](kingsmoot-on-old-wyk), [Aeron Damphair](aeron-greyjoy) — who had crowned Euron with his own hands — refuses to accept the godless king. On Old Wyk he confronts [Victarion](victarion-greyjoy), declaring that since he has no ships or swords he will use his prophet's voice instead: he will rouse not the great lords but the **common folk** of the Iron Islands, "tillers of the soil and fishers of the sea," against Euron, town by town and village by village. He casts himself and the smallfolk as the patient, unstoppable sea wearing down a mountain. Victarion refuses to help; Aeron stalks off into the night, and by the next dawn he has **vanished from Old Wyk** — even his drowned men know not where, and the Crow's Eye only laughs when told. This vow-and-disappearance is the hinge that seeds Aeron's entire later arc (his eventual capture by Euron).
