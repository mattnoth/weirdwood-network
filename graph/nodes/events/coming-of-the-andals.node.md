## Origins

[Generational warfare](wiki:War_of_the_First_Men_and_the_children_of_the_forest) had occurred in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) between the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) and the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest), but the [Pact](wiki:Pact) between the two peoples led to four thousand years of peace and worship of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) by the First Men.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) The [Andals](wiki:Andals), the first new people of Westeros since the Pact, came from the hills of [Andalos](wiki:Andalos) in western [Essos](wiki:Essos). They were tall and fair-haired warriors who carried steel weapons and the seven-pointed star of their gods painted on their bodies.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Their religion, the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), teaches that [Hugor of the Hill](wiki:Hugor_of_the_Hill) and his Andals were promised kingdoms in a foreign land by the Seven, which led to the Andals sailing to Westeros. [Maesters](wiki:Maesters) instead believe the Andals traveled west under pressure from the expanding [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold),(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) which conquered with [dragons](wiki:Dragon) and forced subjugated peoples into [slavery](wiki:Slavery).(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The Andals wielded iron in contrast to the bronze of the First Men, who were gradually defeated in generational wars over hundreds of years.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

### Vale

The [Fingers](wiki:Fingers) in what is now the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) was where the Andals first landed to wrest land from the First Men.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) The First Men of the Vale were ruled by numerous petty kings, some of whom allied with the Andals instead of resisting them. The [Shells](wiki:House_Shell_(Vale)) and [Brightstones](wiki:House_Brightstone) were betrayed by Andal allies, and the Andal [Corbrays](wiki:House_Corbray) claimed the Fingers. The [Shetts](wiki:House_Shett) of [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown) allied with the Andal [Graftons](wiki:House_Grafton) against the [Royces](wiki:House_Royce), but their conflict ended with Gulltown controlled by the Andals.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

King [Yorwyck VI Royce](wiki:Yorwyck_VI_Royce) and his heirs led much of the resistance of the First Men to the Andals. King [Robar II Royce](wiki:Robar_II_Royce) gained the support of the [Redforts](wiki:House_Redfort), [Hunters](wiki:House_Hunter), [Belmores](wiki:House_Belmore), [Coldwaters](wiki:House_Coldwater), and [Upcliffs](wiki:House_Upcliff). These united First Men were able to successively defeat several Andal warlords, including the Corbrays, Graftons, and the [Hammer of the Hills](wiki:Hammer_of_the_Hills). However, Robar's army was routed in the [Battle of the Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_the_Seven_Stars), and control of the Vale was claimed by the Andals of [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn).(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Those First Men who did not submit to the Arryns fled into the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) and became the [Vale mountain clans](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans).(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) The legend of [Alyssa Arryn](wiki:Alyssa_Arryn) and [Alyssa's Tears](wiki:Alyssa's_Tears) is said to be between six thousand(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) and two thousand years old.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_vale:_the_eyrie.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### Riverlands

After conquering the Vale, the Andal warlords continued west through the [Bloody Gate](wiki:Bloody_Gate) or sailed up the [Trident](wiki:Trident) into the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), where they established their own small kingdoms. Tales from the era include the [Fall of Maidenpool](wiki:Fall_of_Maidenpool) and the death of its young king [Florian V Mooton](wiki:Florian_V_Mooton); the [Widow's Ford](wiki:Widow's_Ford) where Lord [Darry's](wiki:House_Darry) three sons held off [Vorian Vypren](wiki:Vorian_Vypren) and his Andals for a day and a night, slaying hundreds before they were themselves slain; and the events of the night in the [White Wood](wiki:White_Wood). The [Blackwoods](wiki:House_Blackwood) and [Brackens](wiki:House_Bracken) allied to oppose the Andals, but were shattered by 777 charging Andal [knights](wiki:Knight) and seven [septons](wiki:Septon), at the great [Battle of Bitter River](wiki:Battle_of_Bitter_River).

The greatest of the [river kings](wiki:River_kings) to oppose the Andals was King [Tristifer IV Mudd](wiki:Tristifer_IV_Mudd), whom the [Tullys](wiki:House_Tully) fought alongside in many of his campaigns. Tristifer is said to have fought one hundred battles, winning ninety-nine and losing only one. When [Roland II Arryn](wiki:Roland_II_Arryn), the [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale), invaded the riverlands and won small victories over several petty kings, he found himself facing Tristifer, who smashed his forces and forced the [Valemen](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) into retreat. One of Roland's allies then betrayed him to the river king, and Tristifer beheaded Roland at [House Mudd](wiki:House_Mudd)'s seat, [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones).(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_vale:_the_eyrie.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) Tristifer was defeated in his hundredth and final battle, when seven Andal kings attacked simultaneously and defeated him; the greatest of these conquerors was [Armistead Vance](wiki:Armistead_Vance).(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) His heir, [Tristifer V](wiki:Tristifer_V_Mudd) was unable to stem the Andal tide and failed to hold his own people together, and so the Mudd kingdom fell to the Andals. To avoid being slaughtered, many of the First Men houses submitted and intermarried with the Andals.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

The Andals had mixed results when they invaded [Crackclaw Point](wiki:Crackclaw_Point), which is now part of the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands). While the native Crackclaws had success fighting in their valleys and bogs, eventually, the Andals learned that what they could not conquer with swords they could through marriage.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

During their conquest the Andals burned [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) groves and slew the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) when they found them,(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) believing them abominations, although *[True History](wiki:True_History)* states the children had already fled the riverlands before the Andals invaded.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) The Andal [Erreg the Kinslayer](wiki:Erreg) attacked [High Heart](wiki:High_Heart), a sacred place to the children of the forest of the riverlands, killing the children and their First Men allies and cutting down High Heart's grove of weirwood trees.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

The Andals failed to conquer the [Isle of Faces](wiki:Isle_of_Faces), leading to conjecture that some children of the forest survived on the Isle with the aid of the [green men](wiki:Green_men).(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)

After centuries of infighting between the Andal kings, [House Justman](wiki:House_Justman) was the first house to regain control over the riverlands.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

### Stormlands

When the Andals first began crossing the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), [Erich the Unready](wiki:Erich_VII_Durrandon) was the [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King) from [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). He took little interest in the invaders as he was embroiled in his own wars at the time, and he died while the Andals completed their conquest of the Vale.

Shortly after the Andals conquered the Vale, they began sailing to [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay) and the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands). The Andal [Togarion Bar Emmon](wiki:Togarion_Bar_Emmon) allied with the First Men of [House Massey](wiki:House_Massey) and expelled the stormlanders from [Massey's Hook](wiki:Massey's_Hook) and established Andal control over the peninsula. The grandson of Erich the Unready, King [Qarlton the Conqueror](wiki:Qarlton_II_Durrandon), was the first Storm King to face the Andals in battle, and his reign and the reigns of his successors, [Qarlton III](wiki:Qarlton_III_Durrandon) and [Monfryd V](wiki:Monfryd_V_Durrandon), would be spent continually warring against the Andals. The Storm Kings won at least six major battles against the Andals, including the great [Battle of Bronzegate](wiki:Battle_of_Bronzegate) when King Monfryd V Durrandon defeated the [Holy Brotherhood of the Andals](wiki:Holy_Brotherhood_of_the_Andals) at the cost of his own life, but the Andals continued to invade, conquering [Tarth](wiki:Tarth) and [Estermont](wiki:Estermont).

To prevent the Andals from conquering all of the [rainwood](wiki:Rainwood), King [Baldric the Cunning](wiki:Baldric_I_Durrandon) manipulated the petty Andal kings and warlords of [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath) into attacking each other. King [Durran XXI](wiki:Durran_XXI_Durrandon) allied with the children of the forest, and this [Weirwood Alliance](wiki:Weirwood_Alliance) achieved victories over the Andals at [Black Bog](wiki:Battle_at_Black_Bog), the [Misty Wood](wiki:Misty_Wood), and the [Howling Hill](wiki:Howling_Hill), and helped to check the decline of the Kingdom of the Storm for a time. A generation later, King [Cleoden I](wiki:Cleoden_I_Durrandon) allied with three [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) kings and defeated the Andal [Drox the Corpse-Maker](wiki:Drox_the_Corpse-Maker) in a [battle on the river Slayne](wiki:Battle_on_the_river_Slayne).(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Eventually the Durrandons and Andals came to an accord when the Andals failed in a seventh attempt to conquer the great castle of Storm's End. The Andals instead intermarried with the First Men storm lords; King [Maldon IV](wiki:Maldon_IV_Durrandon) and his son, [Durran XXIV](wiki:Durran_XXIV_Durrandon), for instance, married Andal maidens. The Andals swore to serve the Storm Kings, while King [Ormund III](wiki:Ormund_III_Durrandon) and his queen converted to the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

### The North

When the Andals attacked the eastern shores of the [north](wiki:North), they were aggressively met by the [Kings of Winter](wiki:Kings_of_Winter), [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), and their First Men bannermen.

The Hungry Wolf, King [Theon Stark](wiki:Theon_Stark), was supported by [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) when the Andal warlord [Argos Sevenstar](wiki:Argos_Sevenstar) attacked and was slain in the [Battle of the Weeping Water](wiki:Battle_of_the_Weeping_Water). Theon then sailed east, raided [Andalos](wiki:Andalos), and displayed the heads of his Andal victims along the shore's of the north.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

After the conquest of the [Trident](wiki:Trident) and the riverlands, the Andals began to attack the north from its south over land. However, every attack was thrown back by the [crannogmen](wiki:Crannogmen) of the [Neck](wiki:Neck) or the strong fortifications of [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin); it is unknown how many Andal armies were destroyed in the Neck.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8) King [Sherrit](wiki:Sherrit) is said to have called down a curse upon the Andals while at the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort), which is also where the legendary [Rat Cook](wiki:Rat_Cook) served "bacon-and-prince" pie to an Andal king.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Eventually, the Andals relented and the north was allowed to remain in peace,(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4) although over succeeding millennia Andal blood entered the kingdom through dynastic marriages.

### Dorne

The influence of the Andals was less in [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) than in the other southern kingdoms of Westeros. Most Andals focused on the nearer lands on the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), rather than the Dornish sands. Some, such as the [Ullers](wiki:House_Uller), [Qorgyles](wiki:House_Qorgyle), and [Vaiths](wiki:House_Vaith), adventured into more inhospitable regions of Dorne. The [Allyrions](wiki:House_Allyrion), [Jordaynes](wiki:House_Jordayne), and [Santagars](wiki:House_Santagar) also established their own realms. The [Martells](wiki:House_Martell) defeated two First Men houses, the [Wades](wiki:House_Wade) and [Shells](wiki:House_Shell_(Dorne)), claiming territory near the mouth of the Greenblood.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_dorne:_the_andals_arrive.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### Westerlands

The Andals began to invade the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) after they conquered the Vale and the riverlands. [Tybolt Lannister](wiki:Tybolt_Lannister_(king)), the [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock), defeated the first Andal warlord, and the next few attacks were also successfully defended against by [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

As the Andals continued to march west, however, Kings [Tyrion III](wiki:Tyrion_III_Lannister) and [Gerold II](wiki:Gerold_II_Lannister) arranged marriages between their bannermen and the most powerful of the warlords. The children of the Andals were brought to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) to serve as wards but also hostages. After the death of King [Gerold III](wiki:Gerold_III_Lannister), his daughter's husband, the Andal Ser [Joffrey Lydden](wiki:Joffrey_Lydden), took the Lannister name. Other houses formed by the intermarriages included Houses [Brax](wiki:House_Brax), [Drox](wiki:House_Drox), [Jast](wiki:House_Jast), [Kyndall](wiki:House_Kyndall), [Lefford](wiki:House_Lefford), [Marbrand](wiki:House_Marbrand), [Parren](wiki:House_Parren), [Sarsfield](wiki:House_Sarsfield), and [Serrett](wiki:House_Serrett). In contrast to most First Men kings, the support of the Andals allowed the Kings of the Rock to expand their power.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

### The Reach

The Andals arrived late in the [Reach](wiki:Reach), after the invasions of the Vale, the riverlands, and the stormlands, as they were previously prevented from sailing there by the fleets of [House Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower) of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) and [Redwyne](wiki:Redwyne_fleet) of the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor). The [Gardener](wiki:House_Gardener) kings of [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) prepared the defenses of the [Kingdom of the Reach](wiki:Kingdom_of_the_Reach). King [Gwayne IV](wiki:Gwayne_IV_Gardener) sought help from the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest), while King [Mern II](wiki:Mern_II_Gardener) focused on fortification. King [Mern III](wiki:Mern_III_Gardener) bestowed honors on a [woods witch](wiki:Woods_witch) who claimed she could raise armies of the dead. The anticipated Andal attacks never occurred, however.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

When the disunited Andals eventually reached the realm of the Gardeners generations later, they were welcomed by the [Three Sage Kings](wiki:Three_Sage_Kings): [Garth IX](wiki:Garth_IX_Gardener), son [Merle I](wiki:Merle_I_Gardener), and grandson [Gwayne V](wiki:Gwayne_V_Gardener). Rather than resist, they granted lands, wives, and lordships to the most powerful Andals. Garth IX brought a [septon](wiki:Septon) to his court and built the first [sept](wiki:Sept) at Highgarden. Merle I, a convert to the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), promoted the construction of septs, septries, and motherhouses across the Reach. Gwayne V was the first Gardener born into the new Faith and the first to be [knighted](wiki:Knight). In order to integrate the newcomers, Merle I and Gwayne V took Andal brides, and all three kings accepted Andal knights and retainers into their service. For instance, Ser [Alester Tyrell](wiki:Alester_Tyrell), the founder of [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell), was made Gwayne V's champion and [sworn shield](wiki:Sworn_shield).(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

House Hightower of Oldtown were amongst the first lords to welcome the Andals.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Lord [Dorian Hightower](wiki:Dorian_Hightower) set aside his wife for an Andal princess and the stability of trade, and Lord [Damon](wiki:Damon_Hightower) was the first Hightower to accept the Faith. After Damon's premature death, his son, the young Lord [Triston](wiki:Triston_Hightower), was raised and trained by Septon [Robeson](wiki:Robeson), who eventually became the first [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon). Triston honored Robeson by building the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept), while Triston's son, Lord [Barris Hightower](wiki:Barris_Hightower), gave the High Septon his first crystal crown.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

The Gardeners encouraged Andal craftsmen, aspecially blacksmiths and stonemasons, to settle throughout the Reach, supplying their bannermen with iron instead of bronze and strengthening their castles with Andal masonry.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2)

Families formed by the intermarriage of First Men and Andal nobles include Houses [Cuy](wiki:House_Cuy), [Graceford](wiki:House_Graceford), [Leygood](wiki:House_Leygood), [Orme](wiki:House_Orme), [Roxton](wiki:House_Roxton), [Uffering](wiki:House_Uffering), and [Varner](wiki:House_Varner). The Andals and First Men of the Reach became closely integrated, and most of the newcomers aided the Gardeners against future Andal invaders. [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) writes that "seldom has a conquest been achieved with less bloodshed."(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-3)

### Iron Islands

The Andals turned their attention to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) a thousand years after their landing on the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers).(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-9) As the Andals invaded and settled in the riverlands, westerlands, and the Reach, they began constructing new settlements and stout castles. These stronger defenses, combined with the Andals' sailing ability, weakened the grip of the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) on the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea). The Andals then began to invade the Iron Islands in waves, often with the support of rival ironborn lords.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

[Rognar II Greyiron](wiki:Rognar_II_Greyiron), [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands), was defeated by an alliance of Andals with Houses [Drumm](wiki:House_Drumm), [Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy), [Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare), and [Orkwood](wiki:House_Orkwood). According to legend, [Harras Hoare](wiki:Harras_Hoare) was chosen as king through the [finger dance](wiki:Finger_dance), but Archmaester [Haereg](wiki:Haereg) states Harras became king by marrying the daughter of an Andal warlord.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) The [Hoare dynasty](wiki:House_Hoare) were often denigrated by the ironborn, especially by the [priests](wiki:Priests_of_the_Drowned_God) of the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God), for marrying and allying with Andals, tolerating the Faith and promoting trade instead of reaving.(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) The new religion never became popular in the Iron Islands and most families of Andal origin eventually converted to the native [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God).(wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-10)
