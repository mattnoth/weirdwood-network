## Origins

### 37 AC[ ]

King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) considered calling a great council in [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), when four rebels—[Harren the Red](wiki:Harren_the_Red), [Jonos Arryn](wiki:Jonos_Arryn), [Lodos the Twice-Drowned](wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned), and the [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Aenys_I))—began to harass the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) and the king could not decide how to handle them. During this proposed gathering, the assembled nobles would have discussed how best to deal with the situations. A council was never called, however, as others acted more swiftly than the king, thereby solving the troubles.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### Great Council of 101 AC[ ]

The Great Council of [101 AC](wiki:101_AC) (also known as the Great Council of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal)) was convened by old King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) after the unexpected death of his son and heir [Baelon Targaryen](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), so that the lords of the Seven Kingdoms could settle on who should succeed him on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).

### 130 AC[ ]

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) was contested by Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). After the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), Dowager Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) suggested assembling a great council to decide which claimant should ascend to the throne. Rhaenyra, however, refused to grant the wish.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

### Great Council of 136 AC[ ]

King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) ascended the throne in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC) at the age of eleven at the conclusion of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). It would be five years before the king would be able to rule in his own right, but instead of naming one regent, a council of seven regents was named to rule with the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
During the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), the original council of seven consisted of Lady [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn), Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), Lord [Roland Westerling](wiki:Roland_Westerling), Lord [Royce Caron](wiki:Royce_Caron), Lord [Manfryd Mooton](wiki:Manfryd_Mooton), Ser [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly), and Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun). Out of these seven, only Munkun served the entire term ([131 AC](wiki:131_AC) to [136 AC](wiki:136_AC)).(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
In the aftermath of the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring), a gathering of the lords to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) was called in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC) to name new members after deaths and resignations. Although not formally called a Great Council according to [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn),(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) considers it as such.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) This was the largest gathering of nobles since the Great Council of [101 AC](wiki:101_AC). Most attendees originated from the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands), [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands), and the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), while others included [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) envoys from the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), Lord [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly), Lord [Lyonel Hightower](wiki:Lyonel_Hightower) with Lady [Samantha Tarly](wiki:Samantha_Tarly), and a large contingent with Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake).(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)
The gathering selected [Willam Stackspear](wiki:Willam_Stackspear), [Marq Merryweather](wiki:Marq_Merryweather) and [Lorent Grandison](wiki:Lorent_Grandison) by lot,(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) while Lord Manderly was chosen as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). King Aegon III dismissed the regents and Hand once he reached his sixteenth nameday.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Other royal appointments made by the assembled [nobility](wiki:Nobility) include [Isembard Arryn](wiki:Isembard_Arryn) as [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), [Gedmund Peake](wiki:Gedmund_Peake) as [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships), Ser [Raynard Ruskyn](wiki:Raynard_Ruskyn) as [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), and Ser [Adrian Thorne](wiki:Adrian_Thorne) as [Commander of the City Watch](wiki:Commander_of_the_City_Watch).(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

### Great Council of 233 AC[ ]

King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen)'s heir, Prince [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), died after a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC),(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) making his eldest son [Valarr](wiki:Valarr_Targaryen) the heir. However, later that year the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness) killed King Daeron and both of Baelor's sons, Valarr and [Matarys](wiki:Matarys_Targaryen).(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) This in turn left Daeron's second son, Prince [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), to inherit, and he was thus crowned as King Aerys I Targaryen.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Aerys left no issue, and upon his death in [221 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) the crown was passed to his youngest brother, who reigned as King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) (Aerys's other heirs, Prince [Rhaegel](wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen) and Rhaegel's son, Prince [Aelor](wiki:Aelor_Targaryen), had predeceased Aerys). The inheritance was unclear after Maekar's death in the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising) of [233 AC](wiki:233_AC), so a council was called in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) by Maekar's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), to decide who would inherit the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
Maekar's eldest son, Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), called "Daeron the Drunken", had died of a sexually transmitted pox prior to [233 AC](wiki:233_AC). Daeron's only issue was Princess [Vaella](wiki:Vaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daeron)), a feeble-witted, simple girl.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
Maekar's next son, Prince [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen), had died in [232 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), when he drank [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) in the mistaken belief it would turn him into a dragon.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Aerion's only issue was an infant son, Prince [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerion)), who had been born in [232 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), not long before Aerion's death.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)
Maekar's third son, [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), had taken his maester's vows in [217 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)
Maekar's youngest son, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), had served as a squire of Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) in his youth, which had led to some of the lords mistrusting him, as they felt that Aegon's wandering with the [hedge knight had](wiki:Hedge_knight) left the prince "half a [peasant](wiki:Peasant)".(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5)
The claim of Princess Vaella was immediately dismissed. Only a few spoke up for the claim of Prince Maegor, but his ascension would mean a long regency, and in addition, it was feared that Maegor might have inherited Aerion's madness and cruelty. Thus, Maegor's claim was dismissed as well.
Prince Aegon's claim seemed as the obvious choice, but due to the prince's wanderings in his youth, there were enough lords who hated him, and thus, an effort was made to offer the throne,(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-6) quietly, to Aemon. Maester Aemon, however, refused the crown.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)
As the Great Council was debating the matter of succession, a fifth claimant appeared. [Aenys Blackfyre](wiki:Aenys_Blackfyre), the fifth son of the Pretender [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), had written to King's Landing from [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) when the Great Council had first been announced, hoping to gain the throne with words, as swords had failed his family three times already. The [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), had offered him safe conduct to Westeros. Upon arrival, however, Aenys was immediately arrested and dragged to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), where he was executed. His head was presented to the council to serve as a warning against every lord who might still have sympathies for [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre).(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-7)
A majority of the council elected Prince Aegon soon thereafter, leading to the ascension of King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), called "Aegon the Unlikely".(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-8)
As his first act as King, Aegon V had [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) arrested and sentenced to death for breaking the word of the Iron Throne, having arranged the execution of Aenys Blackfyre. Aegon later offered Bloodraven the option of going to the [Wall](wiki:Wall), which Brynden accepted. He traveled to the Wall together with Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), serving in the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) from [233 AC](wiki:233_AC) until he disappeared [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall) in [252 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### 281 AC[ ]

The announcement of the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal) by Lord [Walter Whent](wiki:Walter_Whent) gave reason for suspicion. It was rumored that there had been a "shadow host", who had been providing Walter with the funds to pay the magnificent prizes which were promised. These rumors were widely believed in [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), and are still believed at the present. Whilst a dozen names have been put forward for the identity of this shadow host, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) is the most compelling.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
Rhaegar allegedly arranged the tourney by sending Ser [Oswell Whent](wiki:Oswell_Whent), his friend, and Lord Walter's brother, as a go-between. Providing the gold for prizes, the prince wanted to lure as many lords and knights as possible to [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) to call an informal Great Council. Hoping to address the madness of his father, King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), Rhaegar possibly desired a regency or a forced abdication.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)
Aerys, warned of these rumors by his [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers), Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys), decided to attend the tourney personally, despite not having left the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) for four years.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)

### 283 AC[ ]

Before departing [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) told Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) that he intended to call a council to determine what should be done about King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Rhaegar was slain by Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) at the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), however.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

After realizing that King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) is not the biological son of the late King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) suggests to [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) that he, his brother [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), and [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), who have all been crowned during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), should set aside their claims and call for a Great Council to determine who should rule. Renly laughs at the suggestion, however.(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

A [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) is called in late [299 AC](wiki:299_AC) by [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), a [priest of the Drowned God](wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God), after the death of [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) In early [300 AC](wiki:300_AC), [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) is chosen as the new king by the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) assembled at [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk).(wiki:Great_Council.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
