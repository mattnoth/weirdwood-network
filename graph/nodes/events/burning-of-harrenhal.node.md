## Origins

[House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) had ruled the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) and the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) as the [Kings of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:Kings_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers) for three generations. King [Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare), also known as Harren the Black, had devoted most of his reign, nearly forty years, to the construction of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), which would be the largest castle in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), on the northern shore of the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye). Thousands had died while building the castle, and the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) had been depleted in terms of materials. On the day the new castle was finally completed, however, [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) landed at the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), to start his [Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Supported by many great [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) lords and warriors,(wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Black Harren raised his banners. His men fought Aegon's host in the [Battle of the Reeds](wiki:Battle_of_the_Reeds), which was a Targaryen victory, albeit with heavy losses, and the [Wailing Willows](wiki:Wailing_Willows), in which [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) was victorious. However, in the meantime, after years of plundering and beggaring the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), Harren the Black had to face the consequences of those actions. Lord [Edmyn Tully](wiki:Edmyn_Tully) of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), the most powerful of the [riverlords](wiki:Rivermen) following Harren's harsh treatment of [House Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken) and [House Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood), refused to support Harren and instead joined the Targaryens. Following the [Tully's](wiki:House_Tully) example, numerous other riverlords, including the [Blackwoods](wiki:House_Blackwood), [Mallisters](wiki:House_Mallister), [Vances](wiki:House_Vance), [Brackens](wiki:House_Bracken), [Pipers](wiki:House_Piper), [Freys](wiki:House_Frey), and [Strongs](wiki:House_Strong), rebelled against Harren's ironmen and marched to surround Harrenhal. Outnumbered, Harren closed the gates of well-stocked Harrenhal in response.(wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

## Aftermath

Once Harrenhal cooled, the ruined swords of the ironmen were taken to the [Aegonfort](wiki:Aegonfort), where they eventually became part of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) Only a few of Harren's supporters survived the burning or the uprising of the rivermen to make it back to the Iron Islands.(wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

The day following the burning of Harrenhal, Lord [Edmyn Tully](wiki:Edmyn_Tully) swore fealthy to Aegon, who accepted and named him [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident),(wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) as he had been the first riverlord to support him.(wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Aegon next accepted oaths of fealthy from the other riverlords.(wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) Aegon also granted partially-ruined Harrenhal to [Quenton Qoherys](wiki:Quenton_Qoherys), who had been his master-at-arms at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

It is said by some that Harrenhal's [Wailing Tower](wiki:Wailing_Tower) is occupied by the ghosts of Harren and his sons.(wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
