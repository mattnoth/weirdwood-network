## Origins

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) initially rise in rebellion against the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), securing an invaluable strategic position for the [northmen](wiki:North). The rebellion is contingent on [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark)'s betrothal to a daughter of [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), [Lord of the Crossing](wiki:Lord_of_the_Crossing).(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Robb becomes [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) and [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident) after the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps).(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

After Robb is wounded during the [storming of the Crag](wiki:Storming_of_the_Crag), Lady [Sybell Spicer](wiki:Sybell_Spicer) and her brother, Ser [Rolph](wiki:Rolph_Spicer), maneuver Sybell's [maiden](wiki:Maiden) daughter, [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling), into treating the fevered king in her own bed.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rawoiafsybell_spicer.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) After learning of the alleged deaths of his brothers [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) and [Rickon](wiki:Rickon_Stark) in the aftermath of the [capture of Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell), Robb sleeps with Jeyne and then marries her, a slight that Walder is unlikely to overlook.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

The Freys at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) with [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), [Lord of the Dreadfort](wiki:Lord_of_the_Dreadfort), are outraged after learning of Robb's marriage to Jeyne,(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) and the Freys at the Crag(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) and [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun)(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) angrily return to the [Twins](wiki:Twins). After learning of the broken pact, Walder secretly begins corresponding with Roose, who has wed Walder's granddaughter [Walda](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Merrett)),(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) and with Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), for terms to bring House Frey back as a supporter of the Iron Throne and King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Separately, Robb's mother, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), releases Tywin's son, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), from imprisonment at Riverrun, hoping [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) will exchange Jaime for Robb's sisters, [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark).(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) En route, however, [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat) has his [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions) chop off Jaime's sword hand and bring him to Lord Bolton at Harrenhal.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Disavowing the Brave Companions' actions,(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Roose, whose son [Ramsay](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) was responsible for the [sack of Winterfell](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell),(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) does not return Jaime to Robb or [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully), but instead sends Jaime back to his family in King's Landing. Before parting, Jaime and Roose agree to give the other's regards to Robb and Tywin.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

After Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) passes away, Lord Frey sends his son [Lame Lothar](wiki:Lothar_Frey) to Riverrun with an offer to have Edmure, now [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun), marry Walder's daughter [Roslin](wiki:Roslin_Frey) at the Twins, and thereby restore the alliance between Houses Stark and Frey.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Robb cannot decline after already slighting his allies once, since he needs House Frey's assistance for his planned [siege of Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin).(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8) Robb leaves Jeyne at Riverrun with his great uncle, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), while he journeys north with an army of thirty-five hundred, mostly northmen but with some rivermen in Edmure's retinue.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-9)

The [ghost of High Heart](wiki:Ghost_of_High_Heart) tells the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) about [dreams](wiki:Dreams) she had involving a howling wolf, tumultuous music, and sad bells.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

## Aftermath

According to author [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin), Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) had [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) and his followers killed in such a bloody manner to wash out the dishonor done to him.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-34) The massacre ends the [northern](wiki:North) rebellion against the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), and [Frey](wiki:House_Frey) soldiers ride along the [Green Fork](wiki:Green_Fork) in search of stray [Stark](wiki:House_Stark) survivors.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) is named [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North) for his aid, and Walder's second son, [Emmon](wiki:Emmon_Frey), is named [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun).(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) When [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan) tells [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) of the massacre, he reports that the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) call it the Red Wedding.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

According to [Merrett Frey](wiki:Merrett_Frey), the Red Wedding was ordered by Walder and then arranged by [Lame Lothar](wiki:Lothar_Frey) and Roose, with [Ryman Frey](wiki:Ryman_Frey) involved as well.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) takes no personal responsibility for the slaughter when speaking with his son [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), although the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) admits he countenanced Walder's actions to prevent the Freys from rejoining Robb's cause and further extending the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). According to Tywin, Robb was always going to be killed at the feast, although Walder had intended for Catelyn to be captured.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

The [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) gains a number of valuable prisoners which help resolve hostilities around Westeros, and most river lords bend the knee.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1) [Black Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman)) threatens to hang [Patrek Mallister](wiki:Patrek_Mallister) outside his father's castle which results in Lord [Jason's](wiki:Jason_Mallister) surrender at the [siege of Seagard](wiki:Siege_of_Seagard). [Greatjon Umber](wiki:Jon_Umber) and Ser [Marq Piper](wiki:Marq_Piper) are held as hostages to ensure their houses' loyalty; [Hother Umber](wiki:Hother_Umber) allies with Lord Bolton, and Lord [Clement Piper](wiki:Clement_Piper) is forced to help the Freys in their [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun). Lord [Jonos Bracken](wiki:Jonos_Bracken) besieges Lord [Tytos Blackwood](wiki:Tytos_Blackwood) at [Raventree](wiki:Siege_of_Raventree).(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) With [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) captive, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) is able to eventually negotiate a peaceful resolution to the siege of Riverrun. To the displeasure of the Freys, they are informed by Jaime that King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) requires that all prisoners taken during the Red Wedding be turned over to the Iron Throne.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2)

The Red Wedding stains the ancient custom of [guest right](wiki:Guest_right), with safety and security in an another's home no longer being considered guaranteed.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) The massacre ruins House Frey's reputation, and they are treated with antipathy and disgust by many [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros),(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) including their own allies.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1) When [Edwyn Frey](wiki:Edwyn_Frey) calls those of [House Piper](wiki:House_Piper) who died at the Twins "traitors and rebels", Jaime Lannister tells his Frey allies that they are "twice as treacherous as Piper".(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) Lord [Yohn Royce](wiki:Yohn_Royce) mentions the Freys as an example of guest right violators.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-1)

Still traveling with [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark), [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) gives the [gift of mercy](wiki:Gift_of_mercy) to a dying Piper bowman who had been betrayed by a Bolton soldier. Arya has a dream of her direwolf, [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria_(direwolf)), recovering a body from a river.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) This turns out to be the corpse of [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), and Lord [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion) gives his life to revive her with the [last kiss](wiki:Last_kiss).(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1) Catelyn, now the vengeful Lady Stoneheart, takes leadership of Beric's [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners), and they kill [Petyr](wiki:Petyr_Frey) and Merrett Frey.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) During the siege of Riverrun, Jaime sees Robb's crown worn by [Ryman's whore](wiki:Queen_of_whores),(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4) and the crown ends up in the grasp of Stoneheart(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-2) after Ryman's party is ambushed by the outlaws while returning to the Twins.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3)

Ser [Jared Frey](wiki:Jared_Frey) tells Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly), [Lord of White Harbor](wiki:Lord_of_White_Harbor), that the Red Wedding was actually Robb Stark's work. According to the Frey story, Robb changed into a [wolf](wiki:Wolf) as a [warg](wiki:Warg) before the eyes of the Freys and tore out the throat of [Jinglebell](wiki:Aegon_Frey_(son_of_Stevron)), a harmless [simpleton](wiki:Simpleton). Furthermore, he allegedly would have slain Lord Walder if Wyman's son, Ser [Wendel Manderly](wiki:Wendel_Manderly), had not put himself in the way. According to Jared, the other northmen also turned into wolves to join Robb's attack.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1) Wyman supports the Freys and Boltons publicly, but he privately disbelieves their lies.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) When Jared and his kin, [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey) and [Symond](wiki:Symond_Frey), go missing after departing [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), Wyman pleads ignorance about [their fates](wiki:Frey_Pies/Theories).(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) Since many lost kin at the Red Wedding, northmen resent the presence of Freys who travel with Lord Bolton to Winterfell for the wedding of his son [Ramsay](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) to "[Arya Stark](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)".(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8) [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), a captive of the Boltons, wishes he had died by Robb's side.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

Thousands of [sparrows](wiki:Sparrow) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) are furious about the massacre and its violation of guest right. The [small council](wiki:Small_council) of King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) discusses how to blame the Freys, and not the Iron Throne, for the Red Wedding,(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) as did Tywin before his death.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2) [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) suggests once Lord Walder dies, blame should be placed on the Freys that lose the impending fight over succession.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1)

I wrote [the entire book](wiki:A_Storm_of_Swords), I skipped over the Red Wedding and wrote all the way to the end, and then I came back and did the Red Wedding, because it was just emotionally difficult to do that. But you know, hopefully, if it's hard to write, it'll be hard to read, too. It’ll affect the reader emotionally. I mean if the reader is just reading the book and terrible things happen, and they just put it aside and say, “What’s for dinner,” you’ve kind of failed. Your characters haven't achieved any reality here. If sad things happen in the book, the readers should be sad about them. And that does involve a certain amount of emotional vulnerability on the part of the writer.(wiki:Red_Wedding.cite_ref-44)

> —[George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin)
