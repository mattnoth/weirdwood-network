## Origins

[House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) aligns itself with King [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) at the start of the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), in [299 AC](wiki:299_AC), with Renly marrying [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell).(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Following [Renly's death](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), the [small council](wiki:Small_council) sends Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) to negotiate an alliance between Houses Tyrell and [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister), supporting the claim of King [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) After the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Joffrey discards [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and is betrothed to Margaery.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) set the first day of the new year, [300 AC](wiki:300_AC), as the date for the great wedding, so that the ceremony herald the dawn of a new era.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-6)

Ser [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard) tells Sansa she will be able to escape captivity during the upcoming wedding, and he gives her a silver hair net decorated with black [amethysts](wiki:Amethyst) from [Asshai](wiki:Asshai).(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Dontos insists that Sansa wear the hair net during the wedding.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

After arriving in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Margaery's grandmother, [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell), questions Sansa about Joffrey to see if the stories of the boy king's cruelty are true.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Instead of Joffrey, Sansa is forced by Tywin Lannister to marry [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), Joffrey's uncle and Tywin's son.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) overhears the [ghost of High Heart](wiki:Ghost_of_High_Heart) telling the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) that "she dreamt of a maid at a feast with purple serpents in her hair, venom dripping from their fangs".(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

## Aftermath

A drunken Ser [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard) helps [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) escape the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), and [Oswell Kettleblack](wiki:Oswell_Kettleblack) rows them into [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay). When they reach a waiting ship, however, Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) has Dontos killed by Ser [Lothor Brune](wiki:Lothor_Brune)'s men.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Petyr and Sansa sail on *[Merling King](wiki:Merling_King)* to the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers). Once at [Petyr's tower](wiki:Drearfort), Sansa learns that her silver hair net secretly contained poison within its amethysts. [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell) took the poison from an unsuspecting Sansa when she straightened the girl's hair during the celebration. Sansa determines that the poison was then put into Joffrey's wine, and Petyr explains he had plotted Joffrey's death so that [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) could marry Joffrey's younger brother, [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) According to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin), the conspirators wanted Joffrey to appear to die accidentally by choking, rather than a public assassination like the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-EW_1-1)

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), Sansa's husband, is also falsely implicated. While in custody, he believes that Sansa had poisoned Joffrey with other conspirators.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) At the start of his trial, Tyrion states that Joffrey had accidentally choked to death on the pie.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) Maesters [Ballabar](wiki:Ballabar) and [Frenken](wiki:Frenken) find no food in Joffrey's throat and concur that he was poisoned, however, and Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) testifies that Tyrion killed the king with the [strangler](wiki:Strangler).(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) Lady [Taena Merryweather](wiki:Taena_Merryweather) swears she saw Tyrion drop something into Joffrey's [wedding chalice](wiki:Wedding_chalice), and [Lord Estermont](wiki:Lord_Estermont), [Josmyn Peckledon](wiki:Josmyn_Peckledon), [Galyeon of Cuy](wiki:Galyeon_of_Cuy), and [Morros](wiki:Morros_Slynt) and [Jothos Slynt](wiki:Jothos_Slynt) all recall having seen Tyrion empty the chalice of wine while the king died.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3)

Tyrion eventually insists upon [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat), but he is found guilty after Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) kills [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell).(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) has Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) help him free Tyrion from the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells) the night before his execution, however. After Jaime tells his brother about [Tysha](wiki:Tysha), the angry Tyrion falsely claims responsibility for Joffrey's death. Before escaping the castle, Tyrion kills his father, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

[Smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) tell various stories about Joffrey's death, including that Tyrion slit the boy's throat with a dagger and drank his blood from a chalice,(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) or that Sansa killed Joffrey with a [spell](wiki:Magic) and then escaped the Red Keep by turning into a [wolf](wiki:Wolf) with [bat](wiki:Bat) wings.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos74.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

With the exception of his mother, [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), Joffrey is not particularly mourned by anyone; even his biological father, Jaime, feels that Joffrey deserved his fate.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Tyrion states that Joffrey would have become a worse king than the Mad King, [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) thinks that the only good thing that could be said of Joffrey was that he was tall and strong for his age.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) With poison in mind, Jaime, the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), orders Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount) to taste all food for King Tommen I.(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) The twice-widowed Margaery eventually [marries Tommen](wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell).(wiki:Purple_Wedding.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
