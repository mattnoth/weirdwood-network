## Origins

Because there were five vacancies in the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) after young king [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) succeeded his uncle [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Queen Regent [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) suggested that her son award white cloaks to champions of the tourney which would follow her [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) to Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon). Jaehaerys decided that the contenders would fight on foot instead of jousting, as foes seldom strike a king from [horseback](wiki:Horse).(wiki:War_for_the_White_Cloaks.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
The tourney lasted seven days, with the jousting tilts overshadowed by wild melees and bloody duels. Hundreds of [knights](wiki:Knight) competed with [axe](wiki:Armament), spear, and sword for the honor of serving in the Kingsguard. Several colorful competitors became favorites of the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), such as Ser [Willam Stafford](wiki:Willam_Stafford), Ser [Tom the Strummer](wiki:Tom_the_Strummer), and a [mystery knight](wiki:Mystery_knight) revealed to be [Jonquil Darke](wiki:Jonquil_Darke).(wiki:War_for_the_White_Cloaks.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
