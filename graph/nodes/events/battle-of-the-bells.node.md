## Origins

After being defeated by Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) in the [Battle of Ashford](wiki:Battle_of_Ashford),(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) near the border between the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) and the [Reach](wiki:Reach), Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) turned north to rejoin his friend, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark). Robert's wounds were tended to by friends in [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

When the glory-seeking [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King),(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), occupied Stoney Sept with a mighty force,(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) he ordered his soldiers to begin searching the town, even its sewers,(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) for Robert. Jon offered pardons and rewards, and kept hostages in [crow cages](wiki:Crow_cage), but Robert remained hidden in various places by the townspeople.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) His last hiding place was the [Peach](wiki:Peach),(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) where Robert is said to have fathered [Bella](wiki:Bella).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)

## Narrative Arc

The royalists had not found Robert when the forces of Lords [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) and [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) reached Stoney Sept.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9) According to a semi-canon source, Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) was also part of the rebel host.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rawoiafstoney_sept.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The town's septons rang bronze and silver bells to warn the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) to remain indoors.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) The rebels stormed Stoney Sept's walls and attacked [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington)'s army, who responded by fighting back fiercely in streets, in alleys, and on rooftops. Jon wounded Hoster(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10) and killed Lord Arryn's gallant cousin and heir,(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Ser [Denys Arryn](wiki:Denys_Arryn), with an axe.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Emerging from the [Peach](wiki:Peach) when the bells began ringing, Robert slew six men, including Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen)'s friend and former squire, Ser [Myles Mooton](wiki:Myles_Mooton).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11) Robert and Jon did not meet on the battlefield according to [Harwin](wiki:Harwin),(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-12) while Jon recalled that he was nearly slain by Robert on the steps of the town's sept.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) Jon realized the battle was lost and retreated.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-13) Robert later maintained that Eddard won the battle for him.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-14)

### A Storm of Swords

[Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) thinks that Ser [Denys Arryn](wiki:Denys_Arryn)'s death in the Battle of the Bells contributed to Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) agreeing to [a hasty marriage](wiki:Weddings_of_Catelyn_and_Lysa_Tully) with [Lysa Tully](wiki:Lysa_Arryn).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

When they arrive at [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept), [Harwin](wiki:Harwin) tells [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) about the victory of her father, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), in the Battle of the Bells.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-15)

### A Feast for Crows

Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) tells [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) about the death of Denys, Jon's heir, in the Battle of the Bells.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), who had not died of drink but instead allowed that story to be spread so he could help educate [Young Griff](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) in secret, is still haunted by the Battle of the Bells and its outcome.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Following the [taking of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost), he recalls a conversation with the late Ser [Myles Toyne](wiki:Myles_Toyne) from his first year of exile, in which Jon explained he could not have done more at Stoney Sept. Myles responded that Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) would have burned down the town and brought an immediate end to [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), as Robert's death would have led to Eddard and Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) accepting pardons. With his failure at Stoney Sept in mind, Jon now intends to assure the victory of Young Griff, who is claimed to be Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), the son of Jon's late friend [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

## Aftermath

According to a semi-canon source, the rebel leaders traveled from Stoney Sept to [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) for the [weddings of Catelyn and Lysa Tully](wiki:Weddings_of_Catelyn_and_Lysa_Tully).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rawoiafeddard_stark.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rawoiafrobert_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

The Battle of the Bells forced King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) to take [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) seriously.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) He seized the lands of Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) for his failure to deal with rebels, and Jon, who for years insisted he was not responsible for the defeat,(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) is said to have drank himself to death in exile.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) The king sent Ser [Jonothor Darry](wiki:Jonothor_Darry) and Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) to [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept) to rally what they could of the loyalist forces.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) returned from the [tower of joy](wiki:Tower_of_joy) and advised his father to seek assistance from Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) Aerys instead trusted in Lords [Qarlton Chelsted](wiki:Qarlton_Chelsted) and [Rossart](wiki:Rossart).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Having survived Stoney Sept, [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) eventually slew Rhaegar in the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) and was crowned after Aerys's death in the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)
