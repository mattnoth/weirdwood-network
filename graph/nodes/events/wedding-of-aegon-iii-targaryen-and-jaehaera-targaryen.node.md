## Identity

Wedding of Aegon III Targaryen and Jaehaera Targaryen is a war from the AWOIAF wiki.

## Edges


## Origins

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), leader of the [greens](wiki:Greens), had his half sister and rival, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), leader of the [blacks](wiki:Blacks), fed to his [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre), after the [fall of Dragonstone](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone). Aegon the Elder took Rhaenyra's son, Prince [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), as a hostage.(wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)
Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) insisted that Aegon the Younger be wed to King Aegon's only remaining child, the eight-year-old Princess [Jaehaera](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen), to join the two branches of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). Lord [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong) convinced King Aegon to agree to both the betrothal, as well as to naming Aegon the Younger as the heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), insisting that, after the war was done and won, the situation could be dealt with.(wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
King Aegon II was [poisoned](wiki:Poison) in the aftermath of the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad).(wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Aegon the Younger succeeded to the Iron Throne at the age of ten.(wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
Plans were made for a lavish coronation and wedding.(wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Guests came to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) from throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), and even the [Summer Isles](wiki:Summer_Isles). Tents and pavilions were raised outside of the capital since the inns and stables within were full. Tavernkeepers throughout King's Landing and [prostitutes](wiki:Prostitutes) from [Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom) and the [Street of Silk](wiki:Street_of_Silk) profited handsomely from the guests.(wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

## Aftermath

Aegon's firsts act as king were to name replacements to the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) and to appoint [regents](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) who would rule for him until he came of age.(wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) The various great lords who attended the celebration gradually returned to their homes after the wedding and coronation.(wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)
Aegon and Jaehaera had a troubled marriage with little contact between the two aside from rare formal occasions.(wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8) Jaehaera eventually perished in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC) at the age of ten, throwing herself onto the spikes below [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast).(wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Recalling her wedding day, some smallfolk of King's Landing speculated that the young queen had actually been murdered, with Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) considered the most likely suspect.(wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) The [Maiden's Day Ball](wiki:Maiden's_Day_Ball) was eventually held so that Aegon could choose a new queen.(wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)
