## Identity

At the [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](kingsmoot-on-old-wyk), after [Victarion](victarion-greyjoy) makes his claim, [Asha Greyjoy](asha-greyjoy) interrupts the acclamation, mounts Nagga's steps with a golden collar set on her head as a makeshift crown, and stakes her own claim — pointedly calling it "my queensmoot." Backed by her champions ([Qarl the Maid](qarl-the-maid), [Tristifer Botley](tristifer-botley), and Ser [Harras Harlaw](harras-harlaw) with the sword [Nightfall](nightfall)), she argues that Balon's daughter outranks Balon's brothers ("Balon's brother cannot come before Balon's son!") and offers the ironborn a third course: **peace** and alliance with the North rather than more doomed conquest. She dramatizes the worthlessness of the conquered north by spilling three chests before the captains — pebbles (the Stony Shore), pinecones (Deepwood), and turnips (Winterfell) — and warns that her uncle would give them "more war and more defeat" as her father did. She wins real but insufficient support before Euron's [dragon horn](dragonbinder) ends the contest. This is Asha's only political stand in the saga, and the seed of her ADWD arc.
