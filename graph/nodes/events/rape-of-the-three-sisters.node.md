## Origins

Previously a free people ruled by their own [kings](wiki:King_of_the_Three_Sisters), the islands of the Three Sisters were conquered by [northmen](wiki:North) who had tired of their [pirating](wiki:Pirate) and pillaging. According to chronicles written by Sistermen and men of the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), the northmen committed numerous atrocities. Supposedly, they killed children and cooked them in pots, disemboweled men and wound their entrails around spits, executed three thousand warriors in a single day at the [Headman's Mount](wiki:Headman's_Mount), and [Belthasar Bolton](wiki:Belthasar_Bolton) made a "[Pink Pavilion](wiki:Pink_Pavilion)" out of the [flayed](wiki:Flaying) skins of a hundred Sistermen. Northern accounts of the war do not mention these actions.(wiki:Rape_of_the_Three_Sisters.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

King [Theon Stark](wiki:Theon_Stark) once conquered the islands,(wiki:Rape_of_the_Three_Sisters.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) although it is unknown if this was part of the Rape of the Three Sisters or during a different time.

## Aftermath

In response to the invasion, the Sistermen turned to [Mathos II Arryn](wiki:Mathos_II_Arryn), [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale), for assistance in ousting the northmen. In return, the previously independent Sistermen bent the knee to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). The Rape of the Three Sisters became the first act in a thousand years of conflict between the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) and [Arryns](wiki:House_Arryn) over the islands, known as the [War Across the Water](wiki:War_Across_the_Water), or the Worthless War.(wiki:Rape_of_the_Three_Sisters.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
