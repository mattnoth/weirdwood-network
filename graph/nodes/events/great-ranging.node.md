## Identity

The Great Ranging is a large-scale military expedition mounted by the Night's Watch, led by Lord Commander Jeor Mormont, which departs Castle Black with roughly two hundred brothers and adds men from the Shadow Tower to bring the total to approximately three hundred. The stated objectives are to locate the missing First Ranger Benjen Stark, find and assess the wildling host gathering under Mance Rayder in the Frostfangs, and determine what threat, if any, is advancing from beyond the Wall. The expedition proceeds north through a succession of abandoned wildling villages, stops at Craster's Keep where Mormont learns that Mance is massing his people for a march on the Wall, and then establishes a fortified base camp atop the Fist of the First Men. There, Mormont waits for three scouting parties — led by Qhorin Halfhand, Jarman Buckwell, and Thoren Smallwood — to return with intelligence. Jarman Buckwell's scouts are sent to climb the Giant's Stair; Mormont strengthens the position at the Fist with ditches, stakes, caltrops, and a ring of sentries.

The ranging ends in catastrophe at the Fist when a massive force of wights — both human and animal — attacks the camp at night. Fire arrows slow but cannot stop the undead, and the Watch is overwhelmed; most of the men who fight on the Fist are killed. Mormont forms up perhaps fifty survivors and breaks out, leading a grim retreat south through the haunted forest to Craster's Keep. Samwell Tarly, serving as Mormont's aide and keeper of the ravens, sends warning birds to Castle Black and the Shadow Tower during the battle. Around sixty men initially retreat from the Fist; further deaths en route leave roughly forty-four who reach Craster's Keep. A subsequent mutiny there kills both Craster and Mormont, scattering the remnants: some loyalists (including Grenn, Dolorous Edd, and Bedwyck) return to Castle Black, some men desert into the haunted forest, and a handful — including Jarman Buckwell's returning scouts — straggle back to the Wall separately. The Great Ranging is thus a total failure, marking the worst disaster to befall the Night's Watch in living memory and prefacing the broader conflict beyond the Wall.

Preparations for the ranging include Maester Aemon dispatching Samwell into the vaults of Castle Black to copy maps, and Jon Snow distributing a cache of dragonglass weapons discovered near the Fist among his brothers. The Builders accompany the main column, with a score of them tasked on arrival at the Fist with clearing brush, digging latrines, and planting fire-hardened stakes. After the ranging's collapse, most of the four hundred men who had remained at Castle Black are builders and stewards — underscoring how heavily the ranging drew on the rangers.
