## Origins

[Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), the maester at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), sends [ravens](wiki:Raven) seeking aid for the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) after the disastrous [fight at the Fist](wiki:Fight_at_the_Fist), and Lord [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) informs King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Having learned that the bulk of the Watch's fighting force has been wiped out at the Fist by [Others](wiki:Others) and their [wights](wiki:Wights), [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) plans to lead his main host of [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) through the gate in the [Wall](wiki:Wall) at Castle Black. He lures the main strength still remaining at the castle away by having smaller raiding parties show up at multiple places at the Wall, leaving Castle Black to be weakly defended.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Next, Mance sends [Styr](wiki:Styr), the Magnar of [Thenn](wiki:Thenn_(valley)), and [Jarl](wiki:Jarl) as joint commanders with a force of a hundred [Thenns](wiki:Thenns) and twenty of Jarl's raiders to scale the Wall and take Castle Black unaware from the south.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Jarl dies during the ascent,(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) however, and the survivors descend using the abandoned castle of [Greyguard](wiki:Greyguard).(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) They travel through the [Gift](wiki:Gift) to Castle Black.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) While at [Queenscrown](wiki:Queenscrown), [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), a black brother who had pretended to have deserted the Night's Watch, escapes the raiding party.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) He travels back to Castle Black by the way of [Mole's Town](wiki:Mole's_Town) in time to warn the remaining brothers of the Watch of the on-coming attack from the south.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

[Donal Noye](wiki:Donal_Noye) and [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) repel the [attack on Castle Black](wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black) led by [Styr](wiki:Styr).(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Since the Thenns failed to capture the castle and open the gate for the [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall)'s main host, Mance is forced to attack the Wall in an attempt to breach the gate.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Meanwhile, the fleet of [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan) transports Stannis's army north to [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea).(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) [Cotter Pyke](wiki:Cotter_Pyke) leads Stannis's knights west on [ranger](wiki:Ranger)'s roads close to the Wall.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

## Narrative Arc

### Battle for the gate

Mance has his host encamped half a mile north from the Wall.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) The host is first noticed in the black of the night, when the sentries of the Night's Watch at Castle Black blow their horns two times to alert the brothers of the presence of the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk). Per the command of [Donal Noye](wiki:Donal_Noye), barrels of burning pitch are thrown down the Wall by trebuchet in order to get a view of the enemy. Next, the brothers set ablaze a dozen jars of lamp oil, which they shove over the edge one by one, followed by a barrel of pitch.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

Donal takes two archers and two spearmen with him down the Wall to hold the gate from within the tunnel, leaving Jon Snow in command of the forces above. From atop the Wall, the brothers blindly loose arrows and throw rocks with small catapults. After several hours, one of the trebuchets breaks down, while the free folk down below learn to avoid the places the rocks of the other trebuchet land. They fight blindly all throughout the night.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

When the sun has come up, the free folk bring in a ram. The wildling line is centered by a hundred or more [mammoths](wiki:Mammoth) with [giants](wiki:Giants) on their backs, and more giants beside them. Horsemen, archers, and men with spears, slings, clubs, and leathern shields march on either side of the giants, and bone [chariots](wiki:Chariot) from the [Frozen Shore](wiki:Frozen_Shore) make up the flanks. Once they are within range, the black brothers on the Wall rain down arrows and spiked steel caltrops upon them, until the giants who had been carrying the ram are dead or unable to go on. With fire arrows and barrels of burning oil the advancing host is scared into retreating, giving the day's victory to the Night's Watch.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

Despite the successful defense of the Night's Watch, the giants below manage to break the outer door of studded [oak](wiki:Oak) of the gate. [Mag Mar Tun Doh Weg](wiki:Mag_Mar_Tun_Doh_Weg) crawls through, withstanding the arrows and reaching the iron gate behind the men of the Night's Watch were standing. Of the five below, Donal is the last defender to die, either killing or mortally wounding Mag in the process by stabbing him in the throat with his sword. Donal dies when the giant crushes his spine.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

Jon, [Pypar](wiki:Pypar), Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), and [Clydas](wiki:Clydas) enter the tunnel after the battle is over, finding the five dead brothers and the dead giant. Beyond the tunnel, they find the carcass of a dead mammoth partially blocking the entrance, and three more dead giants outside. Jon suggests blocking up the tunnel up to the second gate with rubble, ice, and anything else they can find. However, when he proclaims that Ser Wynton Stout, the last knight remaining at Castle Black, should take over command, Aemon informs him that Wynton is no longer able to do so, and that command has passed to Jon himself.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

### The turtle

The struggle continues for many days afterwards.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-26) During that time, the Night's Watch runs low of supplies. They have no more oil or barrels of pitch, and are soon to run out of arrows as well. When they receive news from Ser [Denys Mallister](wiki:Denys_Mallister) about the [fight at the Bridge of Skulls](wiki:Fight_at_the_Bridge_of_Skulls), Jon dispatches [Zei](wiki:Zei) to Mole's Town to plead with the villagers to aid the black brothers at the Wall. When she fails to return, Jon sends [Mully](wiki:Mully) after her, who returns to tell them that the entire village has been deserted.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

During that time, the free folk construct a “[turtle](wiki:Turtle_(siege_engine))” to breach the gate. Its layers protect the turtle from fire arrows shot down by the Watch, making Jon realize that they can only stop it by crushing it once it is close enough. Together with [Grenn](wiki:Grenn), [Owen the Oaf](wiki:Owen_the_Oaf), and [Kegs](wiki:Kegs), Jon pushes four of the dozen stout oaken barrels full of crushed rock submerged in frozen water off the Wall as the wildlings begin to clear away the dead giants. They succeed in splintering the front of the turtle, and the free folk beneath it flee.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

### Jon's arrest and parley

Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne), who had been sent to King's Landing by [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), the late [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), returns to Castle Black from [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea), bringing with him [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt) and some reinforcements from Eastwatch. Janos takes over command at Castle Black and has Jon taken into custody, accusing him of desertion. Janos and Alliser bring forth [Rattleshirt](wiki:Rattleshirt),(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4) who had been taken captive by [Cotter Pyke](wiki:Cotter_Pyke) at [Long Barrow](wiki:Long_Barrow).(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) Rattleshirt confirms Jon had taken up arms against his brothers when he killed [Qhorin Halfhand](wiki:Qhorin_Halfhand) in the [Frostfangs](wiki:Frostfangs), and neither Janos nor Alliser accept Jon's explanation. Only Maester Aemon comes to Jon's aid, explaining how Jon has held the Wall against Mance Rayder during the struggle thus far. Regardless, Janos orders Jon confined to an ice cell,(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-5) where he sits for four days.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) Although Janos wishes to hang Jon, Aemon protests and writes to Cotter at Eastwatch, preventing Janos from being able to perform the execution.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)

Janos has Jon taken out of the ice cell four days later, proclaiming that Mance requested a parley, asking the Night's Watch to send him an envoy. Alliser and Janos select Jon, with the secret command of killing Mance once Jon has reached the camp. Jon is brought beyond the Wall by the use of the winch cage, and is met by [Tormund](wiki:Tormund) on his slow way to Mance's tent. Mance waits outside his tent, attended by [Harma](wiki:Harma) and [Varamyr Sixskins](wiki:Varamyr_Sixskins), but agrees to speak with Jon privately in his tent. He reveals that Varamyr, through [Orell](wiki:Orell)'s eagle, has seen how few men of the Night's Watch remain, and he shows Jon the [Horn of Winter](wiki:Horn_of_Winter), admitting that it is not his desire to sound the horn and have the Wall fall, as he wishes to use the Wall to protect his people from the [Others](wiki:Others). In the presence of his wife [Dalla](wiki:Dalla) and her sister [Val](wiki:Val), Mance informs Jon that he will give the Night's Watch the horn if he and his people are allowed to pass through the gate, but insists that his people will not yield to the laws of the south. If the Night's Watch refuses the offer, Mance will have Tormund sound the horn at dawn three days later.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)

### Battle beneath the Wall

While contemplating whether to kill Mance now or return to the Wall to bring his offer before his superiors, Jon and Mance are interrupted by the sound of a warhorn, alerting them of something coming towards them, first only from the east, but later also from the north. Mance immediately orders their defenses up, but a combined force of [rangers](wiki:Ranger) from Eastwatch and the [southron](wiki:Southron) knights of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) sweep down upon the camp.

Stannis's knights wash over the wildlings. The [red priestess](wiki:Red_priest) [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) burns [Varamyr](wiki:Varamyr)'s eagle, taking out the [skinchanger](wiki:Skinchanger) as he falls to the ground in pain. As battle is joined, Dalla goes into labor, with only Val and Jon to assist her. [Harma Dogshead](wiki:Harma_Dogshead) is killed in battle,(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9) while [Dormund](wiki:Dormund) is slain by a [knight](wiki:Knight) with moths on his shield,(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) possibly Ser [Richard Horpe](wiki:Richard_Horpe). When Mance's horse is slain in battle, the wildlings break, with only the giants still holding.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-10) Ser [Godry Farring](wiki:Godry_Farring) kills an escaping giant, earning himself the nickname Giantslayer.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

## Aftermath

[Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) is captured and imprisoned by the forces of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon),(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) alongside of another thousand of the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk),(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) including Mance's [newborn son](wiki:Aemon_Steelsong) and his sister-in-law, [Val](wiki:Val).(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) Stannis remains at Castle Black, where he occupies the [King's Tower](wiki:King's_Tower).(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6) Stannis offers to legitimize [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) and name him [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell) in exchange for Jon's help in winning the [north](wiki:North) to his side. He additionally plans to wed Val to his new Lord of Winterfell in order to assure the loyalty of his new subjects. Jon is given time to think about the offer.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3)

The [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), having been without a [lord commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) since the death of [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont) during the [mutiny at Craster's Keep](wiki:Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep), start the vote for Jeor's successor.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7) Stannis, desiring to negotiate with the lord commander over possession of the Gift and all castles but Castle Black, the [Shadow Tower](wiki:Shadow_Tower), and [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea), is displeased by the fact that after ten days of voting the black brothers still have not yet chosen a new commander. He threatens to several of the higher ranking black brothers that he will choose a lord commander for them if they do not come to a decision that same day.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly), realizing that of the three main contesters, [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt) is coming dangerously close to being elected,(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8) convinces the two other contenders, Ser [Denys Mallister](wiki:Denys_Mallister) and [Cotter Pyke](wiki:Cotter_Pyke), the commanders of the [Shadow Tower](wiki:Shadow_Tower) and [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea), respectively, to give their support to Jon instead.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) Jon is elected as lord commander later that day.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

Stannis sends word to Ser [Rolland Storm](wiki:Rolland_Storm), his castellan at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), to begin mining [dragonglass](wiki:Dragonglass) for use in fighting the [Others](wiki:Others).(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2) Stannis is willing to allow free folk through the [Wall](wiki:Wall) and settle them on the [Gift](wiki:Gift) if they swear to [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor), including the [Lord of Bones](wiki:Lord_of_Bones) and [Sigorn](wiki:Sigorn), the new leader of the [Thenns](wiki:Thenns).(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4) Stannis burns Mance for treason,(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) but this is actually a [glamoured](wiki:Glamour) Rattleshirt.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

The wildlings who escaped capture flee into the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest).(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwdprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Many follow a rider who leads them to the [Milkwater](wiki:Milkwater), while hundreds more follow a dour warrior to [Thenn](wiki:Thenn_(valley)).(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwdprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) Even more follow [Mother Mole](wiki:Mother_Mole) to [Hardhome](wiki:Hardhome); [Varamyr Sixskins](wiki:Varamyr_Sixskins) believes the number to have been hundreds,(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwdprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-2) but [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh) later reports that more than six thousand wildlings are at Hardhome with Mother Mole.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) Another three thousand follow [Tormund](wiki:Tormund).(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3)(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Lord Commander Snow sends Val into the forest as an envoy to find Tormund.(wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)
