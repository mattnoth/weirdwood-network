## Origins

During the thousands of years that the Night's Watch has existed, there have been various rebellions by local garrisons. Several infamous, semi-legendary Lord Commanders are alleged to have even tried to set themselves up as kings of the Wall and lands south of it, but these are figures from the mists of ancient times such as the "[Night's King](wiki:Night's_King)" who purportedly lived not long after the Wall was first constructed.(wiki:Night's_Watch_rebellion_of_50_AC.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

By the time of [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), though the Watch had diminished somewhat on the scale of millennia, it still numbered about ten thousand black brothers in total. [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen)'s unification ended the petty wars between the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) (save with [Dorne](wiki:Dorne)), and the supply of new recruits from the losing sides of those conflicts slowed down for the next half-century onward. During the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) and other conflicts of the tyrant [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), however, a new stream of defeated rebels chose to join the Watch to avoid execution. For example, in [43 AC](wiki:43_AC) the [Hightowers](wiki:House_Hightower) arrested the [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) chapter of the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) and delivered them to Maegor: out of 200 knights, three quarters chose to take the black to avoid the sentence of death. As Maegor's reign advanced, many more hundreds of [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) were sent to the Wall as well (such as [Rob the Starvling](wiki:Rob_the_Starvling) at the end of the conflict).(wiki:Night's_Watch_rebellion_of_50_AC.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Ultimately, Maegor's younger nephew, Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) rose up against him, and in rapid succession all the [Great Houses](wiki:Great_Houses) abandoned him, after which he was found dead on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC). King Jaehaerys I brought peace between the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) and the crown, and now sent several of Maegor's loyalists to the Wall. Out of Maegor's five surviving [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), four chose to take the black, including Ser [Olyver Bracken](wiki:Olyver_Bracken) and Ser [Raymund Mallery](wiki:Raymund_Mallery). These two knights had switched sides to Jaehaerys, though only when Maegor's defeat was obvious. Jaehaerys observed that they were willing to break their Kingsguard vows once before while serving Maegor, so he could not trust them to serve him now.(wiki:Night's_Watch_rebellion_of_50_AC.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

## Aftermath

As it turned out, the wildlings had no more love for deserters from the Night's Watch than for loyalists. Half a year later, a wildling chieftain delivered Ser Raymund Mallery's head to Eastwatch. When asked what befell Raymund's remaining traitors, the chieftain laughed and said they [ate them](wiki:Cannibalism).

Walton Stark died childless, so rule of Winterfell passed to his younger brother [Alaric Stark](wiki:Alaric_Stark). Lord Alaric blamed King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) for his brother's death, as the mutineers' lives had been spared by the young king after the wars of [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). Alaric showed Walton's tomb in the [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell) to Jaehaerys when the king visited Winterfell in [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Night's_Watch_rebellion_of_50_AC.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)

Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) flew with Jaehaerys on his [royal progress](wiki:Royal_progress) to Winterfell in [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and decided to fly farther north to inspect the status of the Night's Watch. Whatever short-term benefit the influx of new recruits from the Faith Militant uprising had brought to the Watch had apparently been offset by losses in the rebellion of 50 AC. Alysanne was impressed with the bravery of the black brothers, but as their own Lord Commander admitted, many of their castles were crumbling into disrepair and the Watch lacked the [coin](wiki:Currency) to build new ones. Not only was the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort) too expensive to repair, the Watch's numbers were so low that they could not hope to fully garrison it. Alysanne donated her royal [jewelry](wiki:Jewelry) to pay for the construction of [Deep Lake](wiki:Deep_Lake) as a replacement for the Nightfort, and convinced Jaehaerys to double the size of the [Gift](wiki:Gift), the lands south of the Wall held directly by the Watch.(wiki:Night's_Watch_rebellion_of_50_AC.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)
