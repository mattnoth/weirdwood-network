## Narrative Arc

Down in the library beneath [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), [Sam](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) is trying to dig up any information on the [Others](wiki:Others) for his Lord Commander, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow). Returning to the surface, Sam considers how hard Jon has been working the men of [the Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). He meets [Dolorous Edd](wiki:Edd_Tollett), [Pyp](wiki:Pypar), and [Grenn](wiki:Grenn), and they mention how much Jon has changed, acting different and barely spending any time with his friends; although he never misses a day practising his sword work. We learn that [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) has plans for [Val](wiki:Val), [Dalla](wiki:Dalla)'s sister, seeking to use her in order to forge an alliance between the [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) and the [northmen](wiki:North). Thus far only the [Karstarks](wiki:House_Karstark), who have no other choice, have agreed to ally with Stannis.
Sam arrives at Jon's solar, and Jon shows him a letter he plans to send to King [Tommen](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) which declares that Stannis is aiding them in their battle, but the Night's Watch is not sworn to his cause. They discuss how [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) means to sacrifice [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) for his king's blood, Jon states, "Mance's blood is no more royal than mine own". He reveals to Sam that he is sending [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) and "the boy" far away from the Wall. Sam tells Jon that he has uncovered little on the Others, but one book mentioned [dragonsteel](wiki:Dragonsteel) swords as being effective against them. However, neither is sure if dragonsteel and [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) are the same thing.
Jon breaks the news that he is sending Sam to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) so he can train to become a [maester](wiki:Maester) and replace [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)). Sam will travel by sea from [Eastwatch](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-sea), along with Gilly, the baby, and Maester Aemon. The thought of becoming a maester frightens Sam, bringing back memories of [his father](wiki:Randyll_Tarly)'s wrath when he had mentioned training at the Citadel to him when he was a boy. His father had chained him by the neck in a cell and left him there for three days, telling him "no son of [House Tarly](wiki:House_Tarly) will ever wear a chain". Lastly, Jon commands Sam to never call himself craven again.
The next morning, as the party prepares to set off for Eastwatch where they will join up with [Dareon](wiki:Dareon), Gilly begs Jon to find a good [wetnurse](wiki:Wetnurse) for the other baby.
