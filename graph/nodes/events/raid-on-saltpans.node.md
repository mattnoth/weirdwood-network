## Origins

Using [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) as their base, the brutal [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions) raid the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) on behalf of first Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) and then Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton). When Roose decides to abandon King [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) for the cause of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), he leaves [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat) of the Brave Companions with control of Harrenhal. His lieutenants, in particular [Urswyck](wiki:Urswyck) and [Rorge](wiki:Rorge), argue for abandoning the castle and moving to regions that have not been wiped clean by the war yet. Abandoned by almost all of the Brave Companions, Vargo is killed by Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) after the [capture of Harrenhal](wiki:Capture_of_Harrenhal).

Some time before the Brave Companions leave Harrenhal, [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane), holding [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) captive, also heads towards Saltpans. He intends to find a ship that will bring them to the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), where he hopes he can ransom Arya to her aunt, [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn). However, a fight breaks out at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn) with some of [his brother Gregor's men](wiki:Mountain's_men)—the [Tickler](wiki:Tickler), [Polliver](wiki:Polliver) and a squire from [House Sarsfield](wiki:House_Sarsfield)—and the Hound is gravely wounded. Arya abandons the dying man on the banks of the [Trident](wiki:Trident) and reaches [Saltpans](wiki:Saltpans) on her own, where she secures passage on a ship to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos).(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Rasos74.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Sandor is found by the [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)) who tends to him till he reportedly dies and is buried under a stone heap. The Elder Brother put Sandor's distinctive hound's helm on top of the grave.(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

One band of fleeing Brave Companions is led by Rorge, who has his constant companion [Biter](wiki:Biter) at his side. They head towards Saltpans in the hope of finding a ship to take them from [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Along the way they find the Hound's helm.

## Aftermath

### Randyll Tarly

The reports by survivors are passed on from the [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle) to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and soon become widely distributed. The references to a brutal man wearing the [Hound](wiki:Hound)'s distinctive helm led people to the misperception that [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) led the raid.(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly), trying to restore order in the eastern [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) from nearby [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), spreads rumors that blame the raid on [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion)'s [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) in an attempt to turn the smallfolk against them. The term "Mad Dog of Saltpans" circulates,(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) and as the tales spread, the number of murders that Sandor is accused of rises from twelve to twenty.(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

### House Frey

The suggestion that Beric's band had been responsible for the raid leads Ser [Arwood Frey](wiki:Arwood_Frey) to gather a force of fifty soldiers and archers, including Ser [Harys](wiki:Harys_Haigh) and Ser [Donnel Haigh](wiki:Donnel_Haigh), and ride from the [Twins](wiki:Twins) to Saltpans to find the trail of the outlaws. When they arrive, they find the town reduced to bones and ashes. Ser [Quincy Cox](wiki:Quincy_Cox) does open his holdfast to them, but tells what saw from the battlements. The Frey men find a survivor trapped beneath a beam. However, all references are to the [Hound](wiki:Hound) leading the raid, making Arwood skeptical that Beric was involved.(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

### House Lannister

Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) suggests that [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister)'s son, Lord [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister), go after [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion) and [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) to restore order around the [Darry](wiki:Darry) lands, but Kevan tells her his son is not the man to deal with someone like the [Hound](wiki:Hound). Cersei proposes that Kevan hunt down Sandor, but he says the Hound was Cersei's creature and that it is her responsibility.(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) refuses to hunt down the Hound and suggests Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) deal with reestablishing order in the region. As Kevan heads towards Darry for the wedding of Lancel and [Amerei Frey](wiki:Amerei_Frey), Jaime cautions Kevan to keep his knights close, thinking of how dangerous the Hound was. However, Kevan interprets this as an implied threat by his nephew.(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

When Cersei meets the [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow) at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), this new [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon) refers to the death of Septon [Bennet](wiki:Bennet) and the rape of the twelve-year-old girl promised to the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) as examples of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) not being able to protect the faithful any longer. Cersei blames Sandor joining the band of Beric and insists King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) cannot be blamed for all crimes committed by people once employed by [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister). Cersei agrees to the restoration of the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) in exchange for the High Septon acknowledging Tommen as king and forgiving the crown's debt to the Faith.(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

At Darry, Ser [Arwood Frey](wiki:Arwood_Frey) tells Jaime of his futile attempt to find traces of the brotherhood without banners at Saltpans. The atrocities make Jaime skeptical that this was the Hound's doing, as, despite all Sandor's brutality, he did not consider him quite the monster his brother [Gregor](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) had been. Arwood assures Jaime that the identification of Sandor as leader of the raid is unmistakable according to all accounts. Jaime refuses the appeal by Amerei to hunt down the Hound for her, citing his obligations at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun). However, Ser [Lyle Crakehall](wiki:Lyle_Crakehall) promises her to return and kill the Hound for her once the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun) is complete, making Jaime muse that the Strongboar is not strong enough to defeat Sandor.(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

### Brienne and the Crossroads Inn

In her search for [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), information that she misinterprets leads [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) to the [Whispers](wiki:Whispers), where she comes across three former Brave Companions who had not participated in the raid, [Shagwell](wiki:Shagwell), [Timeon](wiki:Timeon) and [Pyg](wiki:Pyg). Before killing the three, she learns from Timeon that "the Stark girl" was actually in the [Hound](wiki:Hound)'s hands and that the [brotherhood](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) was searching for the two along the [Trident](wiki:Trident).(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Believing this to refer to Sansa rather than to [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark), Brienne decides to look for the Hound herself. Upon her return to [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), she learns from Ser [Hyle Hunt](wiki:Hyle_Hunt) about [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane)'s alleged responsibility for the raid and the attempts to blame it on the brotherhood.(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Septon [Meribald](wiki:Meribald) leads them to the [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle), where the [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)) shares his detailed knowledge of the raid of Saltpans, including the cowardice exhibited by Ser [Quincy Cox](wiki:Quincy_Cox). He informs Brienne that the Hound could not have been responsible, as he had buried the man himself, and that the "Stark girl" who had accompanied Sandor Clegane earlier had actually not been Sansa but Arya. The Elder Brother knows nothing of the girl's whereabouts, but suspects she might have been among those killed at Saltpans.(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) Brienne's small band finds nothing but death and destruction in Saltpans and they are refused entry to the holdfast. They head west towards the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn).(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Meanwhile, the influence of the vengeful [Lady Stoneheart](wiki:Catelyn_Tully) has changed the brotherhood into a band that kills in large numbers. They use the inn to capture assumed offenders and hang them along nearby roads, often shoving pieces of salt into their mouths as reference to the raid on Saltpans. However, it is unclear whether the hanged were guilty of crimes or had just fought for the wrong side in the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Decorations worn by condemned [broken men](wiki:Broken_men) include axes ([Byrch](wiki:House_Byrch) or [Cerwyn](wiki:House_Cerwyn)), arrows ([Norridge](wiki:House_Norridge) or [Sarsfield](wiki:House_Sarsfield)), salmon ([Mooton](wiki:House_Mooton)), pine trees ([Mollen](wiki:House_Mollen)), [oak](wiki:Oak) leaves ([Oakheart](wiki:House_Oakheart)), beetles ([Bettley](wiki:House_Bettley)), bantams ([Swyft](wiki:House_Swyft)), boars' heads ([Vikary](wiki:House_Vikary)), and tridents ([Condon](wiki:House_Condon) or [Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly)).

Some perpetrators of the raid escape the brotherhood. When [Lem Lemoncloak](wiki:Lem) hears the "Mad Dog of Saltpans" is heading north along the [Green Fork](wiki:Green_Fork), he and his men follow the trail of the marauders, although it is actually a trick by Rorge's band to lure them from the inn. Lem's men are delayed by a [column](wiki:Column) of [Frey](wiki:House_Frey) knights when they try to return.(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

Brienne and her small band arrive at the inn before the Saltpans marauders. Led by Rorge, who is still wearing the Hound's helm, seven riders approach the inn. In an attempt to protect the children at the inn, Brienne provokes Rorge to a fight and manages to kill him, only to be attacked in turn by [Biter](wiki:Biter) who savages her face. Before he can eat her alive, Biter is killed by [Gendry](wiki:Gendry). When Lem's men return, they take the severely-injured Brienne as well as her companions [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne) and Ser Lyle Hunt captive for assumed crimes. Lem's men let Brother Meribald go his way while Brienne, Podrick and Lyle are taken to be judged by Lady Stoneheart. Lem claims the hound helm for himself.(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)(wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)
