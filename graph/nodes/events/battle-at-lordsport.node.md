## Origins

Rejecting the rule of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) declared himself [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands). The defeat of the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) in the [sea battle off Fair Isle](wiki:Sea_battle_off_Fair_Isle) allowed royal forces to cross [Ironman's Bay](wiki:Ironman's_Bay) from [Seagard](wiki:Seagard) and [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), however. Aided by Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), [Warden of the West](wiki:Warden_of_the_West), and Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), Robert's men landed on the various islands of the archipelago.(wiki:Battle_at_Lordsport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Narrative Arc

King Robert led a mighty host(wiki:Battle_at_Lordsport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_glorious_reign.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) of thousands on the [isle of Pyke](wiki:Pyke_(island))(wiki:Battle_at_Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) which attacked [Lordsport](wiki:Lordsport), the largest town of the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn).(wiki:Battle_at_Lordsport.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

The fighting left numerous longships and galleys wrecked on the strand of Lordsport. Buildings in the town were broken and burned, including the [sept](wiki:Sept) and [Otter Gimpknee's inn](wiki:Otter_Gimpknee's_inn), and Robert had the timber-and-wattle castle of [House Botley](wiki:House_Botley) razed to the ground.(wiki:Battle_at_Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

After conquering Lordsport, Robert continued on to the final fighting of the war, the [battle at Pyke](wiki:Battle_at_Pyke).(wiki:Battle_at_Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Aftermath

The townspeople of Lordsport used the stones of their destroyed structures to rebuild. Otter's inn doubled in size, and [Sawane Botley](wiki:Sawane_Botley), [Lord of Lordsport](wiki:Lord_of_Lordsport), rebuilt his castle in stone instead of wood. The sept of the town was never rebuilt, however.(wiki:Battle_at_Lordsport.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
