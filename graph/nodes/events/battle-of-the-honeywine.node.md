## Origins

Given that [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen)'s main supporters were [House Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower), the family of his mother [Alicent](wiki:Alicent_Hightower), the [greens](wiki:Greens) assumed the lords and lands of the [Reach](wiki:Reach) would side with them. Instead, many of the major lords from the north and east of the Reach declared for [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and her [blacks](wiki:Blacks), including Houses [Caswell](wiki:House_Caswell), [Rowan](wiki:House_Rowan), and [Tarly](wiki:House_Tarly). Several of the Hightowers' own vassals declared for Rhaenyra, such as Houses [Beesbury](wiki:House_Beesbury) and [Costayne](wiki:House_Costayne). With the Reach divided between blacks and greens and Lord [Lyonel Tyrell](wiki:Lyonel_Tyrell_(lord)) of [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) only a baby, his regent mother declared [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) neutral in the civil war. Instead of quickly marching armies from the Reach to join with the other green armies in the north to overwhelm the blacks, the Hightowers and their allies had to slowly fight through the Reach itself.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Following the early black victories in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Assault_on_Harrenhal) and [Stone Hedge](wiki:Battle_of_the_Burning_Mill), Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), sent messages to his nephew, Lord [Ormund Hightower](wiki:Ormund_Hightower), to deploy the large Hightower army to put down these rebellions in the Reach. Ormund assembled a great host and marched out of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), only to be quickly set upon by black forces. While they were far outnumbered by the Hightower army, the black forces faced them from multiple directions, and used harassment tactics to slow their advance. Lord [Alan Tarly](wiki:Alan_Tarly) and Ser [Alan Beesbury](wiki:Alan_Beesbury) (the "[Two Alans](wiki:Two_Alans)") joined forces to raid Ormund's camps day and night, kill his scouts, and set fires in his line of march. Farther south, Lord [Owen Costayne](wiki:Owen_Costayne) came up from [Three Towers](wiki:Three_Towers) to harass the Hightowers' baggage train in their rear. These hit-and-run attacks from multiple directions lasted for several months, but the Hightowers failed to pin down the rebels.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

By late [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), worse news reached Ormund that a large host equal in size to his own was descending from north of the [Mander](wiki:Mander), led by [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan), [Lord of Goldengrove](wiki:Lord_of_Goldengrove). (The host apparently consisted of the combined strength of the Rowans, Caswells, and other blacks in the northern Reach). Faced with enemies in front and behind, rather than advance Ormund decided to halt and camp in a defensive position along the [Honeywine](wiki:Honeywine). He remained there for several weeks, while sending messages to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) urging King Aegon II and his brother Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) to send one of their [dragons](wiki:Dragon) to aid them. However, Aegon II had been badly injured at the [Battle of Rook's Rest](wiki:Battle_of_Rook's_Rest), and the hot-heated Aemond decided that the greatest threat was from his uncle Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), so he ignored Ormund's calls for aid and took [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) with his remaining army [north into the riverlands](wiki:Aemond's_march_on_Harrenhal).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

The Hightower army left Oldtown soon after the war began in the riverlands, around mid-[129 AC](wiki:129_AC), while Ormund halted his advance soon after the Battle of Rook's Rest in late [129 AC](wiki:129_AC). Rowan's army reached the Honeywine roughly three weeks into the new year [130 AC](wiki:130_AC) ("a fortnight" after the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet), which took place on the fifth day of [130 AC](wiki:130_AC)).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

## Aftermath

The battle was a significant blow to the blacks, ending their offensive operations in the Reach. In addition to their armies being destroyed, most of the major black commanders in the Reach were either killed or captured in the battle. Thaddeus Rowan was the only exception, though he also apparently bent the knee several months later.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

Following only a fortnight after the devastating [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet), Lord [Bar Emmon](wiki:House_Bar_Emmon) suggested that the time had come for a negotiated peace. Instead, the recent tragedies pushed [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) to make a risky strike on King's Landing itself, as Daemon's strategic plan had lured Aemond and most of the city's defenders into the riverlands.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

[Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)) was hailed as a hero that day, and knighted by Lord [Ormund Hightower](wiki:Ormund_Hightower) with the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) longsword, [Vigilance](wiki:Vigilance). Daeron was first called "Daeron the Daring" after the battle, though Daeron modestly said [Tessarion](wiki:Tessarion) was the true hero of the battle.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

The battle did, however, succeed in slowing the advance of the large Hightower army, indirectly preventing the greens in the Reach from aiding the greens to the north. The [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands)' armies were annihilated in the [Battle by the Lakeshore](wiki:Battle_by_the_Lakeshore), while the green armies from the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) were destroyed in the [Butcher's Ball](wiki:Butcher's_Ball).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

With young Tessarion now joining the Hightower army, none of the remaining blacks in the Reach could stand against them. Lord Ormund and Prince Daeron enforced the submission of the [Rowans](wiki:House_Rowan) of [Goldengrove](wiki:Goldengrove), the [Oakhearts](wiki:House_Oakheart) of [Old Oak](wiki:Old_Oak), and the lords of the [Shield Islands](wiki:Shield_Islands). The Hightower army spent the next months slowly advancing up the [Roseroad](wiki:Roseroad), smashing any black forces that attempted to harass them. Daeron on Tessarion proved invaluable in scouting ahead of the main host from the air, to give them advance warning of enemy traps and ambushes.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Eventually they [besieged Longtable](wiki:Siege_of_Longtable), then [sacked Bitterbridge](wiki:Sack_of_Bitterbridge), where they crossed the Mander again and proceeded east towards King's Landing, leading to the [First Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:First_Battle_of_Tumbleton).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
