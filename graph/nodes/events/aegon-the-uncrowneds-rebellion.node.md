## Origins

In [42 AC](wiki:42_AC), Aegon and his sister-wife, Princess [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) were in the middle of a [royal progress](wiki:Royal_progress), but found themselves trapped at [Crakehall](wiki:Crakehall) by members of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), which had [risen in rebellion](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) against [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). At the same time, Aegon's father, King Aenys, died on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). Dowager Queen [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) brought her son and Aenys's half-brother, Prince [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), back from his exile in [Essos](wiki:Essos), and he declared himself king ahead of Aegon.(wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) When the [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) who had besieged Crakehall departed to march on [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Aegon and Rhaena fled to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), where Lord [Lyman Lannister](wiki:Lyman_Lannister) protected them by extending [guest right](wiki:Guest_right). There, Aegon's twin daughters [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) and [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon)) were born the following year.(wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

After Maegor's coronation, Aegon found himself abandoned by many who he once considered his loyal friends. Throughout the realm, it was whispered that Aegon was weak like his father was, pointing out Aegon had never taken a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) of his own, whereas Maegor had claimed [Balerion the Black Dread](wiki:Balerion). In [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), while Maegor was in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), Rhaena and Aegon took note of his long absence from the capital, and after a long journey managed to successfully infiltrate [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). With several companions aiding them, Aegon and Rhaena entered one of the city gates in a [mule](wiki:Mule) [wagon](wiki:Wagon), concealed in the back under sacks of corn. Once in the city, they were given further aid by several collaborators who had grown weary of Maegor's persecutions. However, Aegon and Rhaena did not seize the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), as they lacked the strength to hold it; they had come for dragons. Rhaena was reunited with her [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre), and Aegon finally claimed a dragon, his late father's mount [Quicksilver](wiki:Quicksilver). The pair left the city on dragonback, and returned to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) to raise an army and exact Aegon's claim to the Iron Throne.(wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Aftermath

The battle beneath the Gods Eye in the latter half of [43 AC](wiki:43_AC) was followed by a half a year of trials and executions. Dowager Queen Visenya Targaryen managed to persuade King Maegor to spare some of the rebellious lords. Maegor demanded lands and titles from the rebels, and took [hostages](wiki:Hostage) as well to ensure the loyalty of those he had spared.(wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15)

However, Princess Rhaena avoided capture, having remained at Pinkmaiden Castle with her daughters and dragon. Upon hearing of the outcome of the battle, Rhaena gathered her daughters and fled on dragonback from the riverlands, fearing her uncle's wroth. While first arriving at Lannisport, she would later flee to [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle) on [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle), the seat of Lord [Marq Farman](wiki:Marq_Farman). His father and brother had perished in the battle, but Farman offered Rhaena sanctuary beneath his roof.(wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16)

Later, Maegor married Lord Alan Tarbeck's widow, Lady [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling_(wife_of_Maegor_I)), along with Princess Rhaena, in a ceremony where the two women and [another widow](wiki:Elinor_Costayne) were called the "[Black Brides](wiki:Black_Brides)".(wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-17)

During the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) led by Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett) targeted and [assassinated](wiki:Assassin) some of Maegor's loyalists that had participated in the battle, including Old Lord Merryweather, the heir of Lord Peake, and Ser Davos Darklyn's aged father.(wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-18)
