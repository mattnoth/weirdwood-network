## Origins

After taking [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) in [158 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne_(father_of_Maron)) and forty Dornish [lords](wiki:Lord) bent the knee to King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen). To ensure their loyalties, Daeron had fourteen highborn hostages—one of whom was [Cassella Vaith](wiki:Cassella_Vaith)(wiki:Submission_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)—brought back to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) by his cousin, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen). While the hostages secured the loyalty of the highborn Dornish, Daeron did not anticipate the tenacity of the Dornish [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) in resisting the invaders.(wiki:Submission_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
Massive revolts began after the assassination of Lord [Lyonel Tyrell](wiki:Lyonel_Tyrell_(lord)), who had been left behind as governor of Dorne by Daeron, at [Sandstone](wiki:Sandstone); undoing the Young Dragon's work.(wiki:Submission_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Submission_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Submission_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The young king was then slain trying to suppress the uprising, and King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) ascended the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [161 AC](wiki:161_AC). Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) wanted to put several of the hostages to death, but Baelor forbade any harm to come to the hostages. Baelor walked barefoot to Sunspear to forge a peace.(wiki:Submission_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
