## Origins

### Baelor I and Viserys II

After King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) made peace with [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), he returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and annulled his marriage to his eldest sister, [Daena](wiki:Daena_Targaryen). He then locked Daena with his other sisters, [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_III)) and [Elaena](wiki:Elaena_Targaryen), in the [Maidenvault](wiki:Maidenvault), so they could not tempt the lusts of men. However, Daena contrived to escape her imprisonment on several occasions, and towards the end of her brother's reign became pregnant by her cousin, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen).(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) In late [170 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Daena gave birth to a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son whom she named [Daemon Waters](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre).(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Daena refused to reveal the identity of the father of her child, though Aegon was already suspected to have been involved.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) King Baelor, in response to Daemon's birth, began a forty-day fast, which eventually caused his death in [171 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) After the claims of Daena and her sisters were dismissed, Daena's uncle was crowned as King [Viserys II Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen). Viserys ruled only for a year, until his own death, after which his eldest son, King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), ascended the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [172 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### Aegon IV

In addition to his unacknowledged bastard son by [Daena](wiki:Daena_Targaryen), King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) had by his marriage to his sister, [Naerys](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen), a trueborn son named [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), born in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and a daughter, [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)), born in [172 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Aegon often clashed with Prince Daeron during his reign, with Daeron opposing the ideas and the corrupt style of rule of his father. Aegon, on the other hand, despised his sister-wife, Naerys, and his younger brother, Prince [Aemon the Dragonknight](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), to both of whom Prince Daeron was close. When Aegon, in [174 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), planned to launch an unprovoked attack against [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), the homeland of his daughter-in-law [Myriah Martell](wiki:Myriah_Martell), Daeron opposed his ideas. Around the same time, rumors began to circulate, instigated by King Aegon himself, that Daeron was a bastard born of adultery between his siblings, Naerys and Aemon. For the first time, though not the last, Aegon threatened to name one of his bastard sons ([Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) and [Balerion Otherys](wiki:Balerion_Otherys) being the only sons Aegon had, at this point, acknowledged) as his heir over Daeron.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) The rumors about Daeron's parentage forever remained, but despite Aegon's threats and japes, Daeron was never formally disowned. Meanwhile, two factions began to form at court, one supporting the corrupt reign of Aegon IV, the other supporting Daeron.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

In [182 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aegon knighted his bastard son, [Daemon Waters](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), after the boy won a squire's tourney at the age of twelve. Aegon publicly bestowed [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre), one of the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) swords of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), on Daemon as well,(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4) finally acknowledging him.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-greatbastards_13-0) There were some who felt that the sword symbolized the monarchy(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-14) (not unlike rumors that were spread when King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) bestowed the sword on his half-brother, Prince [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen)),(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-15) as it had been the sword of King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), the Conqueror, and had been passed on from king to king ever since. Aegon's public gift of the sword eventually started talk that Daemon should be king.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-targaryenhistory_16-0) Following the acquisition of the sword, Daemon took the name "[Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre)" for himself.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-5)

### Daeron II

In early [184 AC](wiki:184_AC), King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) legitimized all of his bastards on his deathbed. Prince Daeron ascended the throne as King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), and did all he could to keep the nobly-born [Great Bastards](wiki:Great_Bastards) close. He paid the [dowry](wiki:Dowry) his late father had promised to the [Archon of Tyrosh](wiki:Archon_of_Tyrosh), seeing his half-brother [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) wed to [Rohanne of Tyrosh](wiki:Rohanne_of_Tyrosh), as per the late Aegon's desire.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Daeron was a cultured and scholarly man who swept the court clear of Aegon the Unworthy's corruption, depriving several people of their advantageous positions. Daeron placed men of his own in the positions of power, and he began negotiations with his brother-in-law, [Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell), the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne). This resulted in the [wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Maron_Martell_and_Daenerys_Targaryen) of Maron to Daeron's younger sister, Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)), in [187 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and the joining of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) into the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Dissatisfaction grew from the fact that Dorne had won several concessions during Maron's negotiations that the other regions did not have (e.g. the right to keep their [royal title](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), autonomy to maintain their own laws, the right to assess and gather the taxes due to the Iron Throne with only irregular oversight from the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)). Additionally, it was the belief that the Dornish held too much power at court, as many Dornishmen were brought to Daeron's court, and several filled important offices.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

While Daeron had chosen to be crowned with his father's crown, rumors about his legitimacy remained. Daeron was not a warrior, and while Prince [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), Daeron's eldest son by his Dornish wife [Myriah Martell](wiki:Myriah_Martell), was all that could be desired in a warrior, Baelor had the looks of a [Martell](wiki:House_Martell), and not, like Daemon Blackfyre, of a Targaryen. Nobles and knights from the [Dornish Marches](wiki:Dornish_Marches) began to mistrust both Daeron and Baelor, and began to long for the days when the Dornish were still their enemies.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

Meanwhile, Daemon Blackfyre had grown into a great warrior of his own, trained by Ser [Quentyn Ball](wiki:Quentyn_Ball),(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) and had come to resemble [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) in appearance according to his supporters, with silver-gold hair and deep [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes).(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-greatbastards_13-1) Married to Rohanne of Tyrosh in [184 AC](wiki:184_AC), his twin sons [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Blackfyre) and [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Blackfyre) were born later that same year.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-17) Five other sons, and at least two daughters followed during the years to come.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Daemon's supporters claimed that Daemon had desired his half-sister, Princess Daenerys, and the singers claim that she loved him as well. It is said that Aegon, who had seen more profit in the match with Tyrosh, had promised Daemon that he could have more than one bride. Daeron, however did not agree. Nor has it ever been proven that Princess Daenerys indeed loved Daemon, as the singers and Daemon's supporters claimed.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

The Dornish concessions and influence, the sudden change of Dornish enemies to Dornish allies, Daeron's rumored illegitimacy, and Aegon IV's public gift of the sword of kings to Daemon, all were part of the seeds from which the first Blackfyre Rebellion grew. Still, it took many years for the actual rebellion to begin.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) There was no final insult that resulted in the declaration of Daemon's claim.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) Daemon had come to resent having the status of bastard, and what it represented.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-santafe_18-0) Additionally, he had councillors who urged him to rebel, and many famous warriors who disagreed with the peace with Dorne sought him out. Years passed between the first man approaching him, and Daemon finally declaring his claim. However, Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) considers it likely that it had been [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers), Daemon's younger half-brother, who managed to convince Daemon in the end.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) According to [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), it had been both Bittersteel and Fireball, the master-at-arms at the Red Keep, who had played the largest role in convincing Daemon.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Aegor pressed him to proclaim, increasing his efforts after Daemon agreed to wed his eldest daughter, [Calla](wiki:Calla_Blackfyre), to Bittersteel himself, and many lords and knights joined in.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

## Aftermath

King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen)'s punishment of the rebels was harsher than had been previously expected of him.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-20) Those who had supported [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) lost lands, titles or wealth, and all were forced to give hostages.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10) Lord [Gormon Peake](wiki:Gormon_Peake) lost two of his three castles for his support of Daemon's cause,(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-11)(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) for instance. Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey), whose three sons [Edwyn](wiki:Edwyn_Osgrey), [Harrold](wiki:Harrold_Osgrey) and [Addam](wiki:Addam_Osgrey) had died upon the [Redgrass Field](wiki:Redgrass_Field), had to give up his daughter [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Osgrey) as a hostage, and lost a great amount of land to [House Webber](wiki:House_Webber).(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-14)(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-21)

Aegor Rivers was able to recover the sword [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre) from the battlefield and escape to the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), together with Daemon's widow, [Calla Blackfyre](wiki:Calla_Blackfyre), and remaining children, eventually ending up in [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh).(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-12)(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-15) Daemon's descendants continued their struggle for the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in exile as the [Blackfyre Pretenders](wiki:Blackfyre_Pretenders).(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-22)

The First Blackfyre Rebellion left its mark upon [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) for years to come. Asking a man whether he had fought for the black dragon or the red was considered a dangerous question,(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-13)(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-16) Singers immortalized the Battle of Redgrass Field in a song about [the hammer and the anvil](wiki:The_hammer_and_the_anvil), referring to the strategy of [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)) and [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen).(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-17)

The [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness), which began in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), created the possibility for another rebellion to arise. Many of those who had been taken hostage by King Daeron II had died,(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-14) as had Daeron himself.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-15)(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-18)(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-23) Baelor Breakspear, Daeron's heir and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), had died shortly before during the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow).(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Baelor's two sons, [Valarr](wiki:Valarr_Targaryen) and [Matarys](wiki:Matarys_Targaryen) perished in the Spring Sickness as well,(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-19) leading to the coronation of Daeron's second son, the bookish [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), who named Ser [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) as his Hand.(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-16)(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-20)(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) Under these circumstances did Daemon Blackfyre's eldest surviving son, [Daemon the Younger](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre), arrive in Westeros from Tyrosh, and the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion) was attempted in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC).(wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-17)
