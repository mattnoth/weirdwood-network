## Origins

In the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) it had been custom among the [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlord) to marry brother to sister, or, if that was not possible, an uncle to a niece, or an aunt to a nephew. Also accepted were [polygamous](wiki:Polygamy) marriages, though this practice was less common. The Valyrians had their own religious ceremonies, though a priest does not seem to be a necessity here, as Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) was capable of officiating a wedding performed per this Valyrian ceremony.(wiki:Marriage.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-3)
In [12 AC](wiki:12_AC) during the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), the [Wyl of Wyl](wiki:Wyl_of_Wyl) and his men interrupted the wedding of Ser [Jon Cafferen](wiki:Jon_Cafferen) and [Alys Oakheart](wiki:Alys_Oakheart), killing the guests, [castrating](wiki:Eunuch) the groom and gang-raping the bride and her [handmaids](wiki:Handmaid), and selling the women into [slavery](wiki:Slavery).(wiki:Marriage.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_87-0)
[House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) continued their marriage customs after [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), even though the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) saw [incest](wiki:Incest) as a vile sin.(wiki:Marriage.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-4) The Faith also objected to [Maegor Targaryen's](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) multiple marriages. The marriage of Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) to his sister, Princess [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), contributed to [an uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) by the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant)(wiki:Marriage.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-3) which began during the reign of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen),(wiki:Marriage.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-5) and continued during the reign of King Maegor the Cruel.(wiki:Marriage.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_88-0)(wiki:Marriage.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-4)
In [49 AC](wiki:49_AC), the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) between Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), the mother and [regent](wiki:Regent) of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), and [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End) and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), was considered the most glorious event in the history of Westeros.(wiki:Marriage.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-1)
