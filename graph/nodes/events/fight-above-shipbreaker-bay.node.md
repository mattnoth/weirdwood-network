## Origins

### History

In [120 AC](wiki:120_AC), Prince [Lucerys Velaryon](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon) confronted his half-uncle, Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), together with his brothers [Jacaerys](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) and [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon) when Aemond claimed the [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) on [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark). The three Velaryon siblings attacked Aemond with wooden swords from the training yard. In the fighting, Aemond broke Lucerys's nose and smacked one of the swords against the back of Jacerys's head. As the three younger boys scrambled away, Aemond began to mock them, calling them "the Strongs". Jacaerys, the eldest of the three Velaryon brothers, understood what the insult meant, and attacked Amond once more. Aemond began pummeling Jacaerys savagely. In an attempt to help his brother, Lucerys drew his dagger and slashed Aemond across the face, taking out his right eye.(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rfabthe_heirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) died abed on the third day of the third month of the year.(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Although Viserys had appointed his daughter [Rhaenyra](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), his only surviving child from his first [marriage](wiki:Marriage), as his heir, the supporters of his second wife, [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower), crowned instead Viserys's eldest son by Alicent, [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). Rhaenyra and Aegon fought over the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) over the course of the following two and a half years in the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

During the first few months of the year, both factions began to seek support for their cause. Both Aegon II's [greens](wiki:Greens) and Rhaenyra's [blacks](wiki:Blacks) sought to secure an alliance with [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), ruled by Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon). Within days of Viserys I's death, Dowager Queen Alicent sent her second son, [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), to Storm's End to propose a betrothal between himself and one of Borros's four daughters, to secure the allegiance. Aemond was uncertain how he would be received, but found a warm welcome at Storm's End, where Borros hosted him feasts, hunts, and jousts. Aemond remained at Storm’s End for a while, during which time Lord Borros, feeling Rhaenyra had taken [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon) for granted for too long, agreed on a betrothal and gave Aemond a choice from his four daughters.(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

The [blacks](wiki:Blacks) remained unaware of Aemond's presence at the Baratheon stronghold. Princess [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)), a supporter of Rhaenyra, believed that Borros would support Rhaenyra, as Rhaenys's uncle and Borros's father, [Boremund](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon), had always been a staunch supporter of Rhaenys and her son [Laenor](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon). Because they believed their envoy would receive a warm welcome, Rhaenyra agreed to send her second son, [Lucerys Velaryon](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon), to Storm's End as an envoy, to personally deliver a message from her to Lord Borros.(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### Confrontation at Storm's End

[Lucerys Velaryon](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon) arrived at Storm's End before a gathering storm. By the time Lucerys arrived, however, Aemond and Borros were already haggling over dates and dowries. Lucerys found Aemond in the Round Hall, together with Lord Borros, his four daughters [Cassandra](wiki:Cassandra_Baratheon), [Maris](wiki:Maris_Baratheon), [Floris](wiki:Floris_Baratheon), and [Ellyn](wiki:Ellyn_Baratheon), Borros's septon and maester, and twoscore knights, guards, and servants, including Ser [Byron Swann](wiki:Byron_Swann).

Aemond greeted Lucerys with an insult but was ignored. When Lucerys tried to give Rhaenyra's message to Lord Borros, Aemond tried to grasp it and Baratheon's men intervened. Upon learning the contents of the letter, Lord Borros scowled, asking which of his daughters Lucerys would marry, if Borros were to do as Rhaenyra wished of him. When Lucerys admitted that he was not free to marry, Borros send him away.(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Upon that, Aemond drew his sword, insisting Lucerys put out his own eye to repay him for the eye Aemond had lost years before. Lucerys refused to fight, however, at which Aemond grew more angry. Baratheon's men kept Aemond inside as Lucerys was escorted outside, mounted his dragon, and departed Storm's End.(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

## Aftermath

Three days after the encounter, the head and neck of [Arrax](wiki:Arrax) washed up beneath the cliffs below [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). According to the dwarf [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom)'s scurrilous *[Testimony](wiki:The_Testimony_of_Mushroom)*, the body of Lucerys Velaryon washed ashore as well. Mushroom claims that Prince Aemond Targaryen cut out the eyes of the boy and presented them on a bed of seaweed to Maris Baratheon. Other accounts state that Lucerys had been snatched up by Vhagar, who subsequently swallowed him. Yet other accounts claim that Lucerys survived the fall, swam ashore, but suffered from amnesia and lived the rest of his life as a [simpleminded](wiki:Simple) fisherman. In his *[True Telling](wiki:True_Telling)*, [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) claims that Lucerys died with his dragon, a fact which Archmaester Gyldayn agrees with, claiming that Lucerys's body was never found.(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

For his role in Lucerys's death, Aemond was henceforth known as Aemond the [Kinslayer](wiki:Kinslayer). Aemond did not receive a hero's welcome in King's Landing when he returned with the news. His mother, Dowager Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower), paled and cried out for [the Mother](wiki:Mother_(the_Seven))'s mercy. His grandfather and the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower), was also displeased, telling Aemond, *"You only lost one eye, how could you be so blind?"* However, Aemond's brother King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) welcomed him home with a great feast, and announced that he had made *"a good beginning."*(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

Lucerys's mother, [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), collapsed when told of her son's death, and his younger brother Joffrey swore an oath of vengeance against Aemond and Borros. Rhaenyra's husband and uncle, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), learned of Lucerys's death and sent a raven to Rhaenyra, informing her *"An eye for an eye, a son for a son."* As revenge, Daemon sent "[Blood and Cheese](wiki:Blood_and_Cheese)" to murder Prince [Jaehaerys Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_II)), the eldest son and heir of King Aegon II, in the presence of his siblings, his mother, Queen [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen), and grandmother, Dowager Queen Alicent Hightower.(wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7)
