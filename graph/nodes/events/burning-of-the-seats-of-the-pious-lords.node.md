## Origins

In the wake of the victory of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) at the [Battle at the Great Fork](wiki:Battle_at_the_Great_Fork), the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) remained defiant against the crown. The [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I)) made denunciations both against Maegor and his wives as "the abomination and his whores" as well as the daughters of [Aegon the Uncrowned](wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned), regarded as abominations and fruits of lust and [incest](wiki:Incest).(wiki:Burning_of_the_seats_of_the_pious_lords.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

For their part, the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) remained active. Thousands of [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) haunted roads and remote locations in the [Reach](wiki:Reach) and the [Vale](wiki:Vale), as well as the lands surrounding the [Trident](wiki:Trident), in small bands that fell upon travelers and swarmed towns, villages and poorly defended castles, killing whatever king's loyalists they came upon. With Ser [Horys Hill](wiki:Horys_Hill) lost much of his support after Faith Militant's defeat at the Great Fork, new leaders arose among the Poor Fellows, such as [Ragged Silas](wiki:Ragged_Silas), [Septon Moon](wiki:Septon_Moon) and [Dennis the Lame](wiki:Dennis_the_Lame). Meanwhile, the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) elected Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett) as their new [grand captain](wiki:Grand_captain). Ser Joffrey and a hundred men set out from their chapterhouse in [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) to seek the blessing of the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon). By the time the Warrior's Sons arrived at [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), their numbers rose to two thousand, among [knights](wiki:Knight), [squires](wiki:Squire) and [freeriders](wiki:Freerider). Elsewhere, lords and followers of the Faith gathered men and plotted ways to bring down the crown.(wiki:Burning_of_the_seats_of_the_pious_lords.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

The crown was aware of these events, and [ravens](wiki:Raven) were sent to summon lords and [landed knights](wiki:Landed_knight) suspected of disloyalty to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to bend the knee, do homage to the king and present a son or daughter as [hostage](wiki:Hostage). The Faith Militant, for their part, were [outlawed](wiki:Outlaw), with [membership declared punishable by death](wiki:King_Maegor's_laws), and the High Septon was commanded to deliver himself to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) to stand trial under charges of high treason. The High Septon's response was a summon to the king, commanding him to present himself to the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept) to beg the forgiveness of the [gods](wiki:Seven-Faced_God) for his sins and cruelties. And while some of the pious lords summoned to the capital obeyed the king and presented themselves to do homage and deliver hostages, the majority refused and remained at their seats.

King Maegor, however, was devoted to the completion of the Red Keep. He remained inactive for almost half a year.(wiki:Burning_of_the_seats_of_the_pious_lords.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

## Aftermath

After the destruction of the seats of the defiant lords, Maegor and Visenya [set their sights](wiki:Submission_of_Oldtown) on [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) and flew south.(wiki:Burning_of_the_seats_of_the_pious_lords.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
