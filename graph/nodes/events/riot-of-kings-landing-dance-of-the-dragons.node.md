## Origins

When the people of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) learned of the slaughter during the [First Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:First_Battle_of_Tumbleton), terror began to hold grip in the city, as the citizens became convinced that their city would burn once the [Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower) army would arrive. A man known only as the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd) began preaching against the Targaryens and their [dragons](wiki:Dragons) on [Cobbler's Square](wiki:Cobbler's_Square), claiming that "only by cleansing King's Landing of dragons and their masters" could the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) avoid a doom similar to [that of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria).(wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Following the betrayal of [Hugh Hammer](wiki:Hugh_Hammer) and [Ulf White](wiki:Ulf_White) during the First Battle of Tumbleton, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) became convinced that none of the [dragonseeds](wiki:Dragonseeds) could be trusted. In [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), she ordered the arrest of Ser [Addam Velaryon](wiki:Addam_Velaryon), the legitimized heir of Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), Rhaenyra's [Hand of the Queen](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Corlys warned Addam, who fled from the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) on his [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Seasmoke](wiki:Seasmoke). For aiding Addam in his escape, Corlys was arrested, and placed in the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells).(wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) As a result, the Velaryon forces, which made up more than half of Rhaenyra's army, began to abandon her cause. Some joined the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd), others returned to [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark). Those who remained could no longer be trusted. Ser [Denys Woodwright](wiki:Denys_Woodwright) and Ser [Thoron True](wiki:Thoron_True), two of Corlys's sworn swords, attempted to break Corlys out of the dungeons, but were caught and hanged from the walls of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep).(wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

That same day, Queen [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen) committed suicide by jumping from her window on top of the spikes that lined the dry moat of [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast). That night, the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) of King's Landing began to tell each other that Helaena had been murdered, and placed the blame at the feet of Queen Rhaenyra, insisting she had killed Helaena because Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)) and the [Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower) army would soon be at the gates. The people whispered that Ser [Luthor Largent](wiki:Luthor_Largent) had flung Helaena from her window on Rhaenyra's order, despite the fact that Largent had been at the barracks of the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) at the [Gate of the Gods](wiki:Gate_of_the_Gods) when Helaena died. According to Grand Maester [Munkun ](wiki:Munkun), it was the Shepherd who spread the tale, although [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) believes that story originated from Lord [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong).(wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

The rumor of the beloved Helaena's murder was quickly believed by the citizens, who had completely turned against the once-beloved Rhaenyra and who were still mindful of the terrible murders of Helaena's sons, [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_II)) and [Maelor](wiki:Maelor_Targaryen).(wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

## Aftermath

[Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen)'s loyalists agreed that the city was lost to them, and convinced her to abandon King's Landing. Rhaenyra, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), and a few loyalists [slipped](wiki:Flight_to_Dragonstone) from the city the next morning through the [Dragon Gate](wiki:Dragon_Gate), intend on reaching [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale).

King's Landing descended into anarchy, a chaotic period known as the "[Moon of the Three Kings](wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings)", or the "Moon of Madness". Different factions divided up sections of the city. Ser Perkin and "King" Trystane ruled the Red Keep, the Shepherd ruled from the Dragonpit, and [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair)'s followers held [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill).(wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9) The Moon of the Three Kings was eventually brought to an end by Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon), who marched on King's Landing.(wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

After much troubles and efforts, Rhaenyra and her son eventually reached [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), where they discovered the castle had [previously fallen](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone) to King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and his supporters. Rhaenyra was fed by Aegon to his dragon, [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre), as her son Aegon the Younger looked on. King Aegon II subsequently had Prince Aegon chained and brought to the dungeons.(wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10)(wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons).cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
