## Origins

[Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), which had once been an important port on [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay) and a seat of kings, had seen its wealth shrink and trade dwindle due to the growth of nearby [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). [Denys Darklyn](wiki:Denys_Darklyn), Lord of Duskendale, wished to halt this decline. He desired to win a charter for Duskendale, similar as [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) had received nearly a century before. Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), however, firmly rejected the proposal. Infuriated at Tywin's refusal, Denys devised a new plan, which would eventually have severe consequences. Some say Lord Denys had been driven by the influence of his [Myrish](wiki:Myr) wife, Lady [Serala](wiki:Serala).(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

## Aftermath

King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen)'s vengeance was terrible. Though Lord [Denys Darklyn](wiki:Denys_Darklyn) begged for mercy, the king demanded the deaths of Denys and his immediate family, as well as his uncles, aunts, and distant [House Darklyn](wiki:House_Darklyn) kinsmen living in Duskendale. [House Hollard](wiki:House_Hollard), Lord Denys's goodkin, were attainted and executed as well. Ser [Jon Hollard](wiki:Jon_Hollard), who had been married to Denys's sister, died together with his wife and young son. [Robin Hollard](wiki:Robin_Hollard), the squire who had pulled Aerys's beard when he had first been seized, died upon the rack. The only exception was young [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard), whose life was spared when Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) requested it. Aerys, unable to refuse the man who had saved his life, granted this boon. Denys's wife, Lady [Serala](wiki:Serala), had her tongue and womanly parts torn out, after which she was burned alive.(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) The lands and incomes of [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) were granted to [House Rykker](wiki:House_Rykker), while the lands of [House Hollard](wiki:House_Hollard) were taken away, their villages burned, and their castle torn down.(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)
Despite what happened, the people of Duskendale still love Lord Denys. They blame his foreign wife, Serala, and half of them still feel that the punishment Aerys gave her was too kind by half.(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)
Following his half year of captivity at Duskendale, Aerys II was forever a changed man. It is believed by some that it was the Defiance which caused the king's madness.(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) Barristan, however, claims that it had always been in him, with the lapses of madness growing more frequent over time,(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and it is considered that the Defiance shattered what little sanity Aerys had left.(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) His madness grew significantly worse in the years that followed. He would no longer allow others to touch him, and as a result, his hair and fingernails grew longer and longer. No blades were allowed near him, except for the swords of his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), and his judgments became more severe and crueler. From the end of the Defiance in [277 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), until the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal) in [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), Aerys refused to leave the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep).(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
Furthermore, his experience at Duskendale had convinced Aerys that his son and heir, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), had conspired with Tywin to have Aerys killed during the Defiance by storming the walls, which would have allowed for Rhaegar to ascend the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and marry Lord Tywin's daughter, [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister). Aerys grew mistrustful of Tywin as a consequence, going as far as to refuse to meet with Tywin without the presence of all his Kingsguard knights. Relations with his son Rhaegar became more strained, and the king began to mistrust everyone, from the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) to his own wife, Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen).(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

The story of the Defiance is recounted to [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) when she travels to [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) in search of [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark).(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Now in [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), a regretful Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) considers what might have happened had he not rescued King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) from Duskendale.(wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
