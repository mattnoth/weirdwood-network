## Origins

The fearful citizens of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) broke out in [a riot](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) the night following Queen [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen)'s suicide. During the first night of these riots, the mobs plundered and killed all over the city, hacked the [River Gate](wiki:River_Gate) to pieces, and killed Ser [Luthor Largent](wiki:Luthor_Largent) and his five hundred [gold cloaks](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) on [Cobbler's Square](wiki:Cobbler's_Square) when they arrived to arrest the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd). The Shepherd himself fled the gold cloaks.(wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

Although most of the mobs fled at dawn, they returned in greater numbers the following night, dubbed the [Last Day](wiki:Last_Day) by [Munkun](wiki:Munkun). The Shepherd appeared once again at [Cobbler's Square](wiki:Cobbler's_Square) to preach against the large crowd which had formed on the square. The people feared the arrival of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen)'s dragons and army, but the Shepherd told his "lambs" that everyone would burn when the dragons arrived, and that prayers would not be able to stay the wroth of the [Stranger](wiki:Stranger_(the_Seven)). Instead, the Shepherd claimed that the only way the city would be able to achieve salvation was by killing the dragons in the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit).(wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

### The Seven Who Rode

Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and her sons, [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon) and [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), looked on with their loyalists from atop the roof of [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast). Although Rhaenyra had sent riders to Ser [Balon Byrch](wiki:Balon_Byrch) at the [Old Gate](wiki:Old_Gate) and Ser [Garth the Harelip](wiki:Garth_the_Harelip) at the [Dragon Gate](wiki:Dragon_Gate) to seize the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd), disperse his followers, and protect the dragons, they did not know whether the riders had been able to reach their destinations. Prince Joffrey wished to ride forth to protect the dragons, but Rhaenyra refused to allow it, insisting that they would need every man to defend [Aegon's High Hill](wiki:Aegon's_High_Hill) after the [Hill of Rhaenys](wiki:Hill_of_Rhaenys) fell. Joffrey, fearing his own dragon, [Tyraxes](wiki:Tyraxes), would be killed, slipped away to the yard and loosed his mother's dragon [Syrax](wiki:Syrax). Mounted on Syrax's back, the prince flew out over the city. Whether it was his intent to fly the dragon into battle or to fly to the Dragonpit to fetch his own dragon, Tyraxes, and perhaps the other dragons as well, is unknown.(wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Fearful, Rhaenyra ordered men to return her son to the castle. Ser [Medrick Manderly](wiki:Medrick_Manderly), Ser [Loreth Lansdale](wiki:Loreth_Lansdale), and Ser [Harrold Darke](wiki:Harrold_Darke), Ser [Harmon of the Reeds](wiki:Harmon_of_the_Reeds), Ser [Gyles Yronwood](wiki:Gyles_Yronwood), Ser [Willam Royce](wiki:Willam_Royce), Ser [Glendon Goode](wiki:Glendon_Goode), six squires, eight gold cloaks, and twenty men-at-arms rode forth from the Red Keep and fought their way through the city in an attempt to reach the prince. Joffrey, however, was unable to remain on Syrax's back once they were high in the air, and slid from her back two hundred feet above [Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom) and plunged to his death. The *Seven Who Rode* came upon his body as the mob was cutting it to pieces, and managed to reclaim every part of his except for a foot.(wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

## Aftermath

### Syrax's descent

Following the destruction of the roof, the storming of the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) ended. Yet [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen)'s [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Syrax](wiki:Syrax), freed from the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s stables by her son, [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon), now descended on the survivors who came forth from the smoking ruins. According to [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom), "a thousand shrieks and shouts echoed across the city, mingling with the dragon's roar." The [Hill of Rhaenys](wiki:Hill_of_Rhaenys) looked as if it wore "a crown of yellow fire", burning so bright that it seemed the sun was rising. Instead of breathing [fire](wiki:Dragonflame) onto the mobs from above, Syrax flew down and attacked the mob with her teeth and claws, devouring the people below. Rhaenyra watches Syrax's end from atop the roof of [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast), clutching her only surviving son(wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit.cite_ref-4) [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) in her arms until Syrax fell to the ground.

Mushroom, Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), and Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) have written down accounts of Syrax's death. According to Munkun, [Hobb the Hewer](wiki:Hobb_the_Hewer) killed the dragon with his axe, although Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) believes this is almost certainly mistaken, as he considers it unlikely that the same man could have slain two dragons the same night and in the same manner. Others have suggested an unnamed spearman, "a blood-soaked giant" who leapt onto the dragon's back, while others tell of Ser [Warrick Wheaton](wiki:Warrick_Wheaton), who supposedly slashed a wing from Syrax with a [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword ([Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) believes [Lamentation](wiki:Lamentation_(sword)) to have been the most likely option). [Bean](wiki:Bean), a crossbowman, claimed responsibility for Syrax's death afterward, and boasted of it frequently - until one of Rhaenyra's supporters grew tired of his stories and cut our his tongue.(wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)

Gyldayn argues that it is possible that all the candidates, except for Hobb the Hewer, played some role in the dragon's death. However, the tale most frequently told in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) is that of the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd). According to the tale, as others fled, the Shepherd faced Syrax on his own, calling upon the [Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) for succor, until the [Warrior](wiki:Warrior_(the_Seven)) himself took form, thirty feet tall, with a black blade made of smoke that turned to steel. Even Septon Eustace repeats the tale in his account, while singers sang about it for many years.(wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)

### Rhaenyra's flight

Shaken by the loss of her [dragon](wiki:Dragon) and her son [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon), [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) was inconsolable, according to [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom). Her counselors agreed that [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) was lost to them, and they managed to convince Rhaenyra to abandon the city. Rhaenyra agreed reluctantly and [fled](wiki:Flight_to_Dragonstone) the next day, sneaking out of the city with some of her loyalists and son [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). Rhaenyra reached, after great difficulty, her own castle [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), where she was subsequently captured by King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), whose people had [taken the castle](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone) during the previous weeks.(wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9)(wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
