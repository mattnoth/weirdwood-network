## Origins

The [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) expects that Queen [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) will leave [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) with her army and [dragons](wiki:Dragon), join the sellswords at [Volantis](wiki:Volantis),(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) wed the long-lost Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), and help him claim the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). The plan was orchestrated by [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis) and [Varys](wiki:Varys).(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

The timing for their invasion of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) is ideal, as the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) is massively indebted thanks to the late King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s spending, which is made worse by Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s refusal to pay back the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos).(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Thus the Iron Throne would be unable to afford [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword) or to try and buy off the Golden Company.(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) The Iron Throne is also preoccupied with [ironmen](wiki:Ironmen) in the [Reach](wiki:Reach) and [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s forces in the [north](wiki:North) and at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Unaware of the Golden Company's intentions, Daenerys remains in Meereen ruling as a queen.(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), traveling on *[Shy Maid](wiki:Shy_Maid)* in Aegon's company, suggests the youth prove himself by invading Westeros without Daenerys's aid.(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Meanwhile, the Golden Company has encamped near [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys) and is frustrated that Daenerys has not left [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay). Against the advice of their captain-general, [Harry Strickland](wiki:Harry_Strickland), the Golden Company is convinced by Aegon to depart [Essos](wiki:Essos) for Westeros.(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Numerous captains sail up the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne) from Volantis to Volon Therys to offer their ships to the sellswords. While in Volantis, Tyrion is shocked when he hears of Aegon's decision, as he had considered hiring the Golden Company himself to aid him in claiming [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

The exile Jon Connington, once [Lord of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Lord_of_Griffin's_Roost), decides that [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath) in the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) should be where the Golden Company should begin their invasion. King Robert and Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) are dead, and their brother Stannis, who is away in the north, is not as well liked by the storm lords.(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Aftermath

In [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), the [small council](wiki:Small_council) of King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) receives reports that sellswords have landed all over the south, including [Tarth](wiki:Tarth), the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), and [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath). Tommen's regent, Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), mistakenly believes that [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) has hired the sellswords.(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

The Golden Company plans to next take [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), which is held by Ser [Gilbert Farring](wiki:Gilbert_Farring) for [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon),(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) and raise Aegon's banner upon its battlements. To [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington)'s surprise, Aegon intends to lead the attack.(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) Additionally, nearly all the [Great Houses](wiki:Great_Houses) of Westeros but [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) in the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) and [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell) in [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) have suffered significant losses in the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). While the Vale is far away, Jon hopes to win Dorne to Aegon's cause.(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

The small council later hears reports of sellswords incursions all along the coast and the islands, that Jon Connington is advancing on Storm's End, and that the Golden Company have the feigned Prince Aegon with them. Ser [Ronnet Connington](wiki:Ronnet_Connington) offers to prove his loyalty to Tommen by killing his exiled uncle. After the meeting, [Varys](wiki:Varys) slays Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) and Kevan Lannister in order to keep the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) in the chaotic state that [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) had brought it to, which will work in Aegon's favor.(wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
