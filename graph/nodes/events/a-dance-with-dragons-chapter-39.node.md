## Narrative Arc

[Jon](wiki:Jon_Snow) sends [Val](wiki:Val) north into the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest) to find [Tormund Giantsbane](wiki:Tormund_Giantsbane) and offer him and his followers shelter south of the [Wall](wiki:Wall). Jon explains that Val's lover [Jarl](wiki:Jarl) had been killed by the Wall, not by him. Val reveals that she has given the milk name [Monster](wiki:Monster) to the son of [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) and [Craster](wiki:Craster), and she advises Jon to keep the child away from [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre). Val says she will return the first night of the full [moon](wiki:Moon).
[Dolorous Edd](wiki:Dolorous_Edd) brings Jon his dinner in the new [Lord Commander's](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) chambers behind the armory of [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black). [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh), Septon [Cellador](wiki:Cellador), and [Othell Yarwyck](wiki:Othell_Yarwyck) talk to Jon with various complaints; they disapprove of [Leathers](wiki:Leathers) as Castle Black's [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms), [Satin](wiki:Satin) as Jon's steward, and Jon's decision to negotiate with Tormund and allow him and his men through the Wall. They are also nervous about the fact Jon is keeping two corpses in the [ice cells](wiki:Ice_cell), and react with outrage and horror when Jon reveals he is waiting for them to rise, ignoring Jon's argument that the Watch knows next to nothing about the [wights](wiki:Wights) or their masters, and need to learn more if they are to defeat them.
Overlooking the officers' protests, Jon tells them of a large group of [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) making for [Hardhome](wiki:Hardhome) on the eastern coast of the lands [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall), following the [woods witch](wiki:Woods_witch) [Mother Mole](wiki:Mother_Mole) who claims they will find ships there to bring them across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea).(wiki:A_Dance_with_Dragons-Chapter_39.cite_ref-1) Jon fears they will die there, but when he gets only indifference from the others at the prospect of thousands of dead wildlings, Jon finally loses patience. Angrily asking if they do not realize the truth or are just ignoring it, Jon points out that if they do nothing, the wildlings at Hardhome will die in their thousands, and when they are dead, in time they will rise up again with blue eyes and cold hands, and the [Others](wiki:Others) will have thousands more wights under their control to send at the Wall.
