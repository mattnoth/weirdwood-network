## Origins

In his campaign to place [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) joins the [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) in invading the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands). Their [Volantene](wiki:Volantis) fleet is scattered by a storm after [Lys](wiki:Lys), and only six of the sellswords' ten ships arrive at the rendezvous near the [rainwood](wiki:Rainwood) in [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath), land formerly owned by [House Connington](wiki:House_Connington). The gathered force is not quite half the Golden Company's full strength of ten thousand.(wiki:Fall_of_Mistwood.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Narrative Arc

During her journey to meet Aegon, Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) learns that Mistwood has fallen to the Golden Company.(wiki:Fall_of_Mistwood.cite_ref-arianne1_1-1) Half a mile from Mistwood, her party is ambushed by three Company men who escort them to the castle. There she meets with [Young John Mudd](wiki:Young_John_Mudd) and [Chains](wiki:Chains) who held the castle for the Company. She later has supper in the Tower of Owls with them and the dowager Lady [Mary Mertyns](wiki:Mary_Mertyns) and her [maester](wiki:Maester) who have been made prisoners in their own castle. Lady Mary explains to Arianne that the castle fell easily as the castle was lightly garrisoned since her sons and grandsons took most of the men to war in support of King [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon). Arianne and her companions leave for [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost) in the morning.(wiki:Fall_of_Mistwood.cite_ref-arianne2_3-0)
