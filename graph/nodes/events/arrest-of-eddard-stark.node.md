## Origins

After the unexpected death of the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) journeys north to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) with the royal family and half his court, to ask his boyhood friend, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), to be his new Hand.(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Eddard agrees and journeys south to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Once in the capital, Lord Eddard investigates the death of his predecessor.(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) He finds out that the king's three children, Prince [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), Princess [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), and Prince [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), are in fact [bastards](wiki:Bastard) from Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s affair with her twin brother, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Lord Stark decides to tell the truth to his friend, but he is too late as Robert is dying due to an hunting accident in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood) with a [boar](wiki:Boar).(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) On his deathbed, Robert names Eddard [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm) and [Lord Regent](wiki:Regent). Eddard alters Robert's will, changing Joffrey's name for "the heir".(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Eddard intends for his daughters, [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark), to escape King's Landing on *[Wind Witch](wiki:Wind_Witch)*, and he instructs [Tomard](wiki:Tomard) with personally delivering a letter to [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), during the ship's voyage to the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Sansa, who is betrothed to Prince Joffrey, informs Cersei that she is going to be sent away.(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

While Robert lies dying, Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) approaches Eddard and suggests they seize control of the royal children before Queen Cersei can strike.(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Eddard refuses the offer, however, and instead meets with Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin). Littlefinger agrees to bribe the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) so that [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt) and his gold cloaks will support Eddard against Cersei.(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)

## Aftermath

Lord Stark is imprisoned in the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells) of the Red Keep.(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Sansa is brought before Cersei in the small council chambers on the third day. The queen informs Sansa that her father was a traitor who tried to steal Joffrey's throne, showing her Eddard's letter to Stannis. The council has Sansa, who still desires to wed Joffrey, write letters urging her family to remain peaceful. Lord Baelish takes custody of Jeyne.(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)

Eddard's eldest son and heir, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), calls the [banners](wiki:Bannerman) of the [north](wiki:North) to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell),(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) and he marches south to rescue his father.(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Joffrey's grandfather, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), invades the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) from the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands).(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

In Joffrey's first court session, Tywin is appointed Hand of the King.(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Janos is named [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal), creating [House Slynt](wiki:House_Slynt), and he is given an advisory seat on the small council.(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Barristan is dismissed from the Kingsguard, with Cersei's brother Jaime promoted to Lord Commander in his place. Though not a [knight](wiki:Knight), Sandor Clegane is sworn in as member of Joffrey's Kingsguard, taking Barristan's slot.(wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2)
