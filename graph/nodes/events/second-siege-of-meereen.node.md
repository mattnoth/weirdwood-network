## Identity

The Second Siege of Meereen is a military engagement depicted in *A Dance with Dragons* and in sample chapters from *The Winds of Winter*. Following the collapse of the peace brokered at Daznak's Pit — triggered by Drogon's disruption and the death of Yunkish commander Yurkhaz zo Yunzak — the coalition assembled by Yunkai resumes its siege of the city. The besieging force includes Yunkish slave soldiers, iron legions from New Ghis, and several sellsword companies (the Windblown, the Company of the Cat, and initially the Second Sons), supported by a naval blockade of galleys from Qarth, New Ghis, and Tolos sealing the Skahazadhan river mouth. The besiegers also employ six trebuchets — the Six Sisters, individually named (including the Harpy's Daughter and Ghost of Astapor) — to hurl plague-corpses of Astapori refugees over the city walls, introducing the bloody flux into Meereen.

Ser Barristan Selmy, acting as regent after deposing Hizdahr zo Loraq with the Shavepate, chooses to launch an offensive sortie rather than allow the epidemic to destroy the city from within. He constructs a beacon atop the Great Pyramid — its lighting is the signal for the Meereenese forces to pour out the gates. In parallel, Victarion Greyjoy's Iron Fleet arrives in the bay and attacks the Yunkish ships; the Windblown turns its cloak to Daenerys's side (the Tattered Prince having slain Gorzhak zo Eraz the night before), and the Second Sons subsequently follow. Tyrion Lannister and Jorah Mormont are enlisted into the Second Sons as the siege reaches its climax. The outcome — a Meereenese victory — is anticipated in the released Winds of Winter Barristan chapter.
