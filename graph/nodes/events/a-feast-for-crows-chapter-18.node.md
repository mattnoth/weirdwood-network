## Narrative Arc

[Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) has left the defense of [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin) to [Ralf Kenning](wiki:Ralf_Kenning), and sailed the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) to [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk) for the [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk). When the fleet arrives at [Nagga's Cradle](wiki:Nagga's_Cradle), Victarion has them blockade [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy)'s [Silence](wiki:Silence). The first to greet him on shore is [Aeron](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), and later that night the priest is at his side for the feast. Several captains pledge their voice to Victarion's claim, but [Baelor Blacktyde](wiki:Baelor_Blacktyde) seeks a king who will end the wars, saying,
Soon, the captain finds [his niece](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy), advising her not to lay forth a claim, because a woman has no place ruling the ironborn. Asha stands by her claim, and notes that the Damphair may have underestimated the appeal of the kingsmoot, since [the Drumm](wiki:Dunstan_Drumm) will present his claim and there is talk of [Maron Volmark](wiki:Maron_Volmark) being presented as heir of the throne by [Tarle the Thrice-Drowned](wiki:Tarle_the_Thrice-Drowned), while it is apparent that Aeron's intent was to legitimize Victarion as Balon's successor.
Their chat is interrupted by the arrival of Euron. Victarion notes Euron does not seem to have aged in the almost three years that he has been gone.(wiki:A_Feast_for_Crows-Chapter_18.cite_ref-1) Aeron confronts Euron with the accusation that "no godless man may sit the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair)", but Euron cows his brother into leaving the hall. Asha then accuses her uncle of the death of her father, but Euron denies he had anything to do with it. When a fight nearly breaks out between Asha and one of Euron's men, Victarion steps in and demands that Euron leave. After his departure, Victarion and his niece walk outside, and Asha asks why Euron was banished by her father, for he refused to ever speak of it. Victarion eventually tells her that Euron impregnated [Victarion's wife](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy's_third_wife). Victarion wanted to kill his brother but Balon forbid it and sent Euron to exile. Later Victarion killed his wife with his own hands.
Asha offers to set aside her claim if Victarion will name her his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and end the war. She feels that the Ironborn have overextended themselves and tells her uncle that she has reached an agreement with Lady [Sybelle Glover](wiki:Sybelle_Glover), and that their people may keep [Sea Dragon Point](wiki:Sea_Dragon_Point) and the [Stony Shore](wiki:Stony_Shore) in return for the release of [Deepwood Motte](wiki:Deepwood_Motte), [Torrhen's Square](wiki:Torrhen's_Square), and [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin). Victarion is not receptive. Asha replies that she then will claim the throne.
