## Origins

### Background

During the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), the loyalist forces of King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) defeated the rebel forces of his half-brother, [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), one of the legitimized [Great Bastards](wiki:Great_Bastards) of King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen). Daemon was killed in the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field), but Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers), another Great Bastard and one of Daemon's strongest supporters, was able to escape to the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) with Daemon's surviving family and [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre), the famed [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Many of those who had fought for the Black Dragon in the rebellion had their children taken to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) as hostages. Most of these hostages died during the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness), as did King Daeron, allowing the rebellious families to plot once more.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

### Wedding tourney

The marriage of Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell) to [a daughter](wiki:Lady_Butterwell) of [Lord Frey](wiki:Lord_Frey) was used by some Blackfyre supporters to join their forces with those who resented the rule of Ser [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), another Great Bastard and the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to Daeron's son, King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen). [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre), son of the late Daemon I, [dreamed](wiki:Dream) that a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) would hatch, and Lord [Gormon Peake](wiki:Gormon_Peake) believed that a living [dragon](wiki:Dragon) would gain the rebels the support of the [Butterwells](wiki:House_Butterwell) of [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls), the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) of the [Twins](wiki:Twins), the [Lothstons](wiki:House_Lothston) of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), and the [Brackens](wiki:House_Bracken) of [Stone Hedge](wiki:Stone_Hedge). Ser [Tommard Heddle](wiki:Tommard_Heddle) was skeptical of Daemon's dreams, however, and he wished that they had the support of Aegor Rivers, as Bittersteel remained in [Essos](wiki:Essos) with the sword Blackfyre.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

The organizers of the new rebellion convinced Ambrose to name his prized dragon egg as the prize for the winner of his [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls). Daemon entered the tourney disguised as a [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight), Ser John the Fiddler, and the plotters attempted to rig the competition so that Daemon would win the egg. A drunken Daemon attempted to gain the support of Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) during the festivities, with "John" telling Dunk he had dreamed of him in the white armor of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).

Because of his prior involvement in the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow), Duncan decided to enter the tourney as a [mystery knight](wiki:Mystery_knight) called the Gallows Knight. He was defeated in his joust by Ser [Uthor Underleaf](wiki:Uthor_Underleaf), however. Uthor later pointed out to Duncan that one of the gold coins he had won had been coined during the Blackfyre Rebellion, and that an unknown party wanted him to kill Duncan during the competition.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

### Rebellion

When the dragon egg disappeared from Lord Butterwell's bedchamber, Ser [Glendon Flowers](wiki:Glendon_Flowers) was framed by Lord Peake for the theft because of his refusal to lose to Daemon in the lists. Glendon was thrown in a cell when the missing egg was found in his possessions.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

When Lord [Alyn Cockshaw](wiki:Alyn_Cockshaw), Daemon's friend, attempted to kill Duncan out of jealousy, Duncan kicked Alyn into a well to drown, an event observed by Ser [Maynard Plumm](wiki:Maynard_Plumm). Meanwhile, Duncan's squire Egg, actually Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), revealed his identity to Maester [Lothar](wiki:Lothar), who then informed Lords Butterwell and Frey. The Frey retinue immediately departed Whitewalls to return to the Twins, and Ambrose admitted his doubts about the conspiracy to Duncan and insisted that Lord Peake had plotted everything. Duncan then slew Ser Tommard in single combat when he tried to take Prince Aegon into captivity.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

Within the great hall of Whitewalls, Duncan publicly accused John the Fiddler of being Daemon and alleged that the egg that had been found in Glendon's saddlebags was actually a painted stone planted by order of Lord Peake; the real dragon egg was still missing. Daemon had been unaware of the plots, and was unsure who to believe. He ordered a trial by joust, declaring that he would personally face Glendon.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

Many wedding guests disappeared before the joust the next morning, including Ser Maynard. Daemon washed the black dye from his hair to reveal silver and gold [Valyrian](wiki:Valyrians) hair, and his horse bore the sigil of [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre). Glendon, who had been tortured in the dungeon, unhorsed Daemon on the first pass.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)

As the joust was ending, Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), called Bloodraven, arrived outside of Whitewalls with three of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), his three hundred [Raven's Teeth](wiki:Raven's_Teeth), and five hundred knights and five thousand spears from the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) and the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands). These were drawn from Houses [Darklyn](wiki:House_Darklyn), [Hayford](wiki:House_Hayford), [Massey](wiki:House_Massey), [Rosby](wiki:House_Rosby), [Stokeworth](wiki:House_Stokeworth), [Mooton](wiki:House_Mooton), [Lothston](wiki:House_Lothston), and finally [House Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood), the house of Brynden's mother, [Melissa](wiki:Melissa_Blackwood). Daemon called for those within the castle to fight to the death but was laughed off by the fighting men. Daemon then marched out and offered single combat, but was promptly taken prisoner by Lord Rivers, thus ending the rebellion before it could begin. The only man killed was a servant of Lord [Vyrwel](wiki:House_Vyrwel) who revealed that he had been spying for Bloodraven and was then slain by a [Costayne](wiki:House_Costayne) knight.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)

### Aftermath

Ser [Roland Crakehall](wiki:Roland_Crakehall_(Kingsguard)) informed Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) that the conspirators would be taken to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) for trial. Soldiers following their liege's commands would presumably be lightly punished, and lords who told the truth would be pardoned after giving up a hostage. Lords who had already been pardoned after the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), however, would be imprisoned or attainted.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9)

Duncan found [Lord Frey](wiki:Lord_Frey) in the company of Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) within the [Hand's](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) pavilion.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10) Brynden had the ringleader, Lord [Gormon Peake](wiki:Gormon_Peake), beheaded for his crimes,(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-11) and other unspecified conspirators were also executed.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Bloodraven allowed Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell) to keep only a tenth of his fortune, and Lord Rivers declared that [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls) was forfeit to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and would be razed to the ground to prevent it from becoming a pilgrimage site like the [Redgrass Field](wiki:Redgrass_Field). Lord Frey was dismissed without punishment, although Brynden told him they would speak later. The would-be [Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre) was kept as a hostage in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) to prevent Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) from crowning Daemon's younger brother, [Haegon](wiki:Haegon_I_Blackfyre), in exile.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-12)(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Duncan realized that [dwarfs](wiki:Dwarf) who had provided entertainment at the wedding had discretely removed the [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) from Ambrose's bedchamber by order of Bloodraven.(wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-13)
