## Origins

Led by [Wat the Hewer](wiki:Wat_the_Hewer), a force of nine thousand [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) marched against the forces of King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). As they attempted to cross the [Mander](wiki:Mander), they were caught between six different royalist hosts. The Poor Fellows were untrained and undisciplined, clad in boiled leather, roughspun, and scraps of rusted steel. Armed largely with woodsmen's axes, sharpened sticks, and farm implements, they did not stand a chance against the [knights](wiki:Knight) of the royalist armies. With Wat's forces divided by the river, the Poor Fellows were cut to pieces.(wiki:Battle_at_Stonebridge.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) The sheer brutality of the carnage made the Mander run red with blood for twenty leagues, resulting in the bridge and castle that commanded it forever after being known as [Bitterbridge](wiki:Bitterbridge).(wiki:Battle_at_Stonebridge.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Wat fought Lord [Myles Smallwood](wiki:Myles_Smallwood) in single combat, with both men surviving the fight. (wiki:Battle_at_Stonebridge.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Wat later managed to kill half a dozen lords, including Lord [Meadows](wiki:House_Meadows), the commander of the king's army. Wat was eventually taken alive himself and delivered to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in chains.(wiki:Battle_at_Stonebridge.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Battle_at_Stonebridge.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
