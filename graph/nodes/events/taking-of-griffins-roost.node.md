## Origins

In his campaign to place [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) joins the [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) in invading the [Stormlands](wiki:Stormlands). Their [Volantene](wiki:Volantis) fleet is scattered by a storm after [Lys](wiki:Lys), and only six of the sellswords' ten ships arrive at the rendezvous near the [rainwood](wiki:Rainwood) in [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath), land formerly owned by [House Connington](wiki:House_Connington). The gathered force is not quite half the Golden Company's full strength of ten thousand.(wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

Jon leads a quarter of the available strength to take [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost), the ancestral seat of the Conningtons. Jon was the Lord of Griffin's Roost during the [War of the Usurper](wiki:War_of_the_Usurper), but he was forced into exile after the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells) and the castle passed to his cousin [Ronald Connington](wiki:Ronald_Connington). The current Knight of Griffin's Roost, Ronald's son Ser [Ronnet Connington](wiki:Ronnet_Connington), is away in the [Riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) with Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister). Prior to the battle, the sellswords make a battering ram at their camp.(wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Narrative Arc

The nearby woods have grown close to Griffin's Roost, which allows Ser [Franklyn Flowers](wiki:Franklyn_Flowers) to lead his men within twenty yards of the castle's gatehouse. The crash of the ram brings two defenders to the battlements, but they are quickly brought down by the archers of [Black Balaq](wiki:Black_Balaq). He commands two hundred veterans wielding crossbows, horn-and-sinew bows from [Essos](wiki:Essos), yew longbows, and goldenheart bows from the [Summer Islands](wiki:Summer_Islands). The gate is not barred, and the ram breaks through on the second strike.

Franklyn's men advance past the gatehouse and are halfway up the narrow ridge called the griffin's throat before the castle is alerted by a warhorn. The archers quickly bring down two ravens released from the [maester](wiki:Maester)'s tower. The Golden Company uses grapnels to climb the curtain wall and attack at six places along the battlements. A defender pours oil on the sellswords attacking the gates, but the unheated oil does little damage. Ronnet's defenders are also confused by the sellswords shouting the words of House Connington, "*A griffin! A griffin!*"

The battle only takes a few minutes. When Jon Connington rides up the griffin's throat with [Harry Strickland](wiki:Harry_Strickland), they see Black Balaq bring down a raven himself. Jon tasks Franklyn with preventing any further ravens from flying, and he or his men toss the maester from his tower. The remaining guards throw down their weapons.(wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

## Aftermath

Jon expected to lose at least one hundred soldiers attacking Griffin's Roost, but the Golden Company only suffers four casualties. Jon has Ronnet's siblings, [Raymund](wiki:Raymund_Connington) and [Alynne Connington](wiki:Alynne_Connington), and bastard son, [Ronald Storm](wiki:Ronald_Storm), secured as hostages. The servants and the four surviving members of Ronnet's garrison swear allegiance to Jon Connington, Lord of Griffin's Roost. When Aegon arrives four days later, he announces his intention to lead the attack on [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

After returning to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) from the Riverlands, Red Ronnet asks King [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon)'s [small council](wiki:Small_council) to allow him to prove his loyalty by bringing them the heads of Jon and Aegon. Tommen's regent, Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), has Ronnet kept under guard, however, as he thinks Ronnet may sympathize with his kinsman Jon.(wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
