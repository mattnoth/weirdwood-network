## Narrative Arc

Ser [Barristan](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) attends [Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq)'s court under the unease that [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen)'s absence brings.
The [Brazen Beasts](wiki:Brazen_Beasts) now have a new commander - the king's cousin, [Marghaz zo Loraq](wiki:Marghaz_zo_Loraq). [Reznak mo Reznak](wiki:Reznak_mo_Reznak) keeps a strong voice in court, counseling the new King.
[Harghaz](wiki:Harghaz) is now acclaimed as a hero after his fight against Drogon.(wiki:A_Dance_with_Dragons-Chapter_59.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)
Ser Barristan notices the presence of [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell) and his two Dornish companions at court, remembering his former companionship to Prince [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell), his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) colleague and also Quentyn's uncle. He considers the possibility that the [poisoned locusts](wiki:Poisoned_locusts) that [Strong Belwas](wiki:Belwas) ate might have been meant for Hizdhar, as part of a plot to direct Daenerys into marrying Quentyn, given that [another of the prince's uncles](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) was notorious for poisoning those who got in his way.
Before he makes up his mind on the matter, three [Wise Masters](wiki:Wise_Masters) envoys from [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) arrive, along with their sellsword [Bloodbeard](wiki:Bloodbeard), to talk with Hizdahr. They show Admiral [Groleo](wiki:Groleo)'s severed head as a gesture of intimidation, claiming his death as payback for the death of [Yurkhaz zo Yunzak](wiki:Yurkhaz_zo_Yunzak), who was trampled to death during the panic when [Drogon](wiki:Drogon) attacked the [Fighting pits](wiki:Fighting_pits).(wiki:A_Dance_with_Dragons-Chapter_59.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Ser Barristan reminds King Hizdahr that Yurkhaz's death was accidental, while the Yunkai representatives free three of the six remaining hostages. They demand that the dragons be destroyed as a condition for the release of the last three hostages. Barristan is also suspicious of Hizdahr's lack of reaction, given that all three of the [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) kings he served under- [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), [Robert](wiki:Robert_Baratheon) and even the supposedly weak [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen)- would have responded to such an insult by ordering every member of the Yunkish delegation killed; he also believe Bloodbeard is trying to start violence in order to get the plunder the peace has denied him.
As the court is ended for further deliberation of the King's council, Ser Barristan decides to counsel the [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen). Despite appearing weak, the King has taken notice of Quentyn, and Ser Barristan considers it dangerous for them to remain in [Meereen](wiki:Meereen). He advises them to return immediately to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), going so far as offering the coin for the trip, and reveals that there was an attempt at poisoning either Daenerys or her consort during the fighting pits reopening.(wiki:A_Dance_with_Dragons-Chapter_59.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Quentyn's shocked reaction reassures Ser Barristan that the Dornishmen had no part in the poisoning, but he still counsels Quentyn and his companions to leave Meereen and return to Dorne, for he considers his prospects of marriage unpromissing and his situation dangerous.
