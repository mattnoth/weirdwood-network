## Origins

Ser [Borys Baratheon](wiki:Borys_Baratheon) was the heir to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) until his older brother, Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon), finally had a son, [Boremund](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon), in [52 AC](wiki:52_AC). Borys became bitter and traveled to [Myr](wiki:Myr) and [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) before returning to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), where he joined forces with the [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Jaehaerys_I)) of the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains). Using his knowledge of the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands), he assisted the rebel in raiding and pillaging his former home.(wiki:Third_Dornish_War.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

In [61 AC](wiki:61_AC) Lord Rogar Baratheon traveled to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), asking King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) for his leave to end the threat. Jaehaerys replied that Lord Rogar not only had his leave, but the king's own sword too.(wiki:Third_Dornish_War.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Narrative Arc

When pursued, the [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Jaehaerys_I)) would disappear into the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) to evade capture, retreating to one of his camps high in the mountains. However, his tactics were to little avail against the Targaryen forces. King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) flew upon [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor), setting aflame each of the Vulture King's crude camps in the mountains. Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon)'s [column](wiki:Column) traveled into the mountain from the east, at first by horse but soon afoot along goat tracks. Lord [Simon Dondarrion](wiki:Simon_Dondarrion) of [Blackhaven](wiki:Blackhaven) led a small host of marcher knights into the mountains from the west, sealing off a potential route of escape for the Vulture King.(wiki:Third_Dornish_War.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Ser [Borys Baratheon](wiki:Borys_Baratheon) was the first to be cornered. Lord Rogar's men made short work of Borys's men, but when Rogar and Borys came face to face, Jaehaerys refused to let Rogar become a [kinslayer](wiki:Kinslaying) and personally challenged Borys to single combat. Borys accepted the challenge, and was cut down by Jaehaerys with [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre).(wiki:Third_Dornish_War.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

The Vulture King was eventually captured in a previously burned lair. Led before Jaehaerys and Rogar in chains, Lord Rogar ordered the captive's chains removed and that he be given a spear and shield. Lord Rogar commanded that if the Vulture King managed to slay him in single combat, he would be allowed to go free. The Vulture King proved unable to defeat the Stormlord, however, and was slain by Lord Rogar.(wiki:Third_Dornish_War.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

In total, the war lasted not even half a year.(wiki:Third_Dornish_War.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

## Aftermath

Following the defeat of the Vulture King, raiding fell off sharply along the Dornish Marches for a time.(wiki:Third_Dornish_War.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

As the actions of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) during the conflict became known throughout the Seven Kingdoms, the lords of the realm grew to respect their king more, and any doubts they still might have had about Jaehaerys's bravery were removed.(wiki:Third_Dornish_War.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

Many years later in [83 AC](wiki:83_AC), [Morion Martell](wiki:Morion_Martell) succeeded his father as Prince of Dorne, but scorned how two decades before his (unnamed) father had allowed Jaehaerys to enter Dornish lands unmolested and without his leave. Rash and foolish, Morion sought to avenge what he perceived as a slight upon Dornish honor by launching the [Fourth Dornish War](wiki:Fourth_Dornish_War), which ended in his humiliation.(wiki:Third_Dornish_War.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)
