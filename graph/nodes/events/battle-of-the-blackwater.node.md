## Identity

The Battle of the Blackwater was the largest battle in the War of the Five Kings, fought in 299 AC at the mouth of the Blackwater Rush and King's Landing. It ended in a decisive victory for House Baratheon of King's Landing (wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater).

## Allegiances

Defenders: King's Landing garrison (Tyrion Lannister commanding gold cloaks, sellswords, Vale mountain clansmen, Sandor Clegane; Kingsguard), relief force (Tywin Lannister, Mace Tyrell, Randyll Tarly, Garlan Tyrell with westermen and Reach forces). Attackers: Stannis Baratheon (Ser Imry Florent commanding fleet, Ser Guyard Morrigen commanding van; stormlanders, crownlanders, Florents, Fossoways, Salladhor Saan's Lysene pirates, Myrish sellsails) (track_b: Conflict).

## Narrative Arc

After assassinating Renly and taking Storm's End, Stannis advanced on King's Landing with ~200 ships and forces from the Stormlands, Crownlands, and defected Reach houses. Tyrion Lannister prepared the defense: a chain boom across the Blackwater Rush, massive stores of wildfire, and tripled gold cloaks. Stannis's fleet entered the river under Ser Imry Florent. Tyrion's wildfire trap — hulks filled with wildfire pots — was ignited, creating a wall of green flame that consumed much of both fleets. The chain was raised behind Stannis's ships, trapping them. Survivors came ashore and assaulted the city gates. Sandor Clegane led three sorties but the wildfire robbed him of his courage. Tyrion led a sortie himself at the King's Gate. When Joffrey was taken back to the Red Keep on Cersei's orders, defender morale collapsed. Tyrion was wounded — slashed across the face by Ser Mandon Moore of the Kingsguard, who was killed by Podrick Payne. At dusk, the Lannister-Tyrell relief force arrived, taking Stannis's forces in the rear. Tywin led the right, Tarly the center, Mace Tyrell the left. The vanguard under Ser Garlan Tyrell, wearing Renly's distinctive green armor, shattered Stannis's lines — former Renly supporters, believing their lord had returned, abandoned Stannis. Ser Guyard Morrigen was slain. Stannis escaped with several hundred men on Salladhor Saan's ships; thousands were left behind (wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater, cite: ACOK Ch. 58, 59, 61, 62; ASOS Ch. 4, 60).

## Origins

Convinced that [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) is not King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s son, [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) believes his claim to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) is the true one. However, he lacks the strength to attack [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), as few [Houses are sworn to Dragonstone](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_Dragonstone) and the rocky, volcanic islands surrounding [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) have a sparse population, and the men of the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) have given their support to his younger brother [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), the [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End), who has proclaimed himself king as well. Additionally, Renly has taken [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) to wife, which has earned him the support of the men from the [Reach](wiki:Reach) as well.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), a [red priestess](wiki:Red_priest) at Dragonstone, tells Stannis of two futures she has seen in her flames, one in which Stannis is defeated in King's Landing by his brother Renly, and one in which he sails to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), where his brother shall die, and his men will flock to Stannis.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-4)

Stannis arrives in the stormlands with his five thousand men and [lays siege](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)) to the coastal fortress Storm's End, Renly's seat.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-3)(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) Renly is at [Bitterbridge](wiki:Bitterbridge) with his army when news reaches his camp. He splits his forces and rides with some twenty thousand cavalry to Storm's End to break the siege.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1) After a failed parlay in which neither brother will yield, the battle is set at dawn.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-2) However, that night Renly is killed by a [shadow assassin](wiki:Shadow_assassin) summoned by Melisandre.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1) With Renly dead, the majority of the host that had accompanied him to Storm's End swears their allegiance to Stannis.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-4) [House Florent](wiki:House_Florent) and both branches of [House Fossoway](wiki:House_Fossoway) also defect to Stannis, but a fifth of Renly's knights returns to Bitterbridge with Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-5)

The siege of Storm's End continues, as its castellan, Ser [Cortnay Penrose](wiki:Cortnay_Penrose), refuses to yield the castle to Stannis(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-6) because he fears for the safety of King Robert's [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son, [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-5) To end the siege quickly, Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) smuggles Melisandre under the castle by boat, where she summons another shadow to assassinate Cortnay.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-6) After Cortnay's death, the castle yields, leaving Stannis free to advance on King's Landing.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1)(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0) Stannis sends Melisandre and [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm), Robert's [bastard](wiki:Bastard), to the safety of Dragonstone.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-13)

Sixty thousand men from Renly's army are still encamped at Bitterbridge, and when news of Renly's death reaches them,(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-7) fighting breaks out between Stannis's supporters and Renly's loyalists led by the vengeful Loras Tyrell. [Parmen Crane](wiki:Parmen_Crane) and [Erren Florent](wiki:Erren_Florent)—the two knights sent by Stannis to retrieve the army for him(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-8)—are taken prisoner,(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) while Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) seizes the supplies and puts many men, mostly Florents, to death in the [fighting at Bitterbridge](wiki:Fighting_at_Bitterbridge).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-2)

In King's Landing, the [small council](wiki:Small_council) of King Joffrey decides to attempt to make an alliance with [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) by offering to marry Joffrey to Margaery. [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), representing King Joffrey I,(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-7) travels to Bitterbridge and wins the allegiance of House Tyrell. Riders are sent north to warn Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) of Stannis's threat to the capital.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)

Meanwhile, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) raids the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), hoping to lure Tywin, who had been residing at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) with his twenty thousand men, away from King's Landing while King Stannis attacks the city from the south. Stannis's victory would mean the end of King Joffrey, Queen [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), and the acting [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), breaking Lannister power.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-1) Tywin begins his march west,(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) but Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully), unaware of Robb's plan,(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-2) refuses to allow the Lannisters to cross the [Trident](wiki:Trident) without giving battle and places an army of eleven thousand men at the key fords along the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-1) After several probing attacks, Lord Tywin attempts to force a crossing in a dozen places. Unable to cross the river, Tywin is forced to retreat from the [Battle of the Fords](wiki:Battle_of_the_Fords).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-1) This delays Tywin just long enough for riders out of Bitterbridge to reach him with word of Stannis's threat to King's Landing.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-3)

In preparation of the defense of King's Landing, Queen Cersei has arranged for the production of vast amounts of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire),(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) while Tyrion has commissioned a great chain boom across the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) The [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) is tripled in size,(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-1) sellswords are hired,(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) contingents of soldiers are send from [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) and [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth),(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-11)(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) and hundreds of [scorpions](wiki:Scorpion_(weapon)), spitfires, and catapults are constructed and placed on the walls.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-2)(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-3) Tyrion further sends out his hundred and fifty [Vale mountain clansmen](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans) to harass Stannis's flanks and kill his scouts in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)

Stannis's vanguard arrives at the city early, and Joffrey's war galleys are dispatched to exchange arrows with his forces. The vanguard is said to have five thousand men, flying the banners of Houses [Fossoway](wiki:House_Fossoway), [Estermont](wiki:House_Estermont), and [Florent](wiki:House_Florent), under the command of Ser [Guyard Morrigen](wiki:Guyard_Morrigen).(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2)

Treacherous waters and inclement weather delay Stannis's fleet on its northward journey from Storm's End. A dozen ships are lost on the journey to [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay). Stannis gives command of the fleet to Ser [Imry Florent](wiki:Imry_Florent), who commands from Stannis's galley, *[Fury](wiki:Fury)*. Stannis himself is on the south side of the Blackwater Rush with his host, making them create arrows and rafts.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-14)

## Aftermath

The Battle of the Blackwater is a major defeat for [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon). He retreats to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) with only a fraction of his army, and only [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan)'s ships. Melisandre tries to convince the brooding king that she can wake a stone [dragon](wiki:Dragon) by sacrificing [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm) because of his king's blood.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0)

Most of those captured during the battle bend the knee to King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), including men sworn to Dragonstone. During a court session in the [throne room](wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep)), Ser [Garlan Tyrell](wiki:Garlan_Tyrell) publicly asks Joffrey to take his sister [Margaery](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) to bride, and Joffrey, as previously agreed upon, fakes unwillingness to break his betrothal to [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) until the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Tyrion's)) and [small council](wiki:Small_council) agree that he is allowed to do so. Multiple honors and rewards are given to the participants and survivors of the battle by King Joffrey and his small council.(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-17)(wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)
