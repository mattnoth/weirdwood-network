## Origins

Lord [Robert Reyne](wiki:Robert_Reyne), the Lord of [Castamere](wiki:Castamere), was succesful in arranging a betrothal between his daughter [Ellyn](wiki:Ellyn_Reyne) and [Tywald Lannister](wiki:Tywald_Lannister), the heir of Lord [Gerold Lannister](wiki:Gerold_Lannister) of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock). However, both Ellyn's betrothed and father were slain during the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising) in [233 AC](wiki:233_AC). Ellyn, strong-willed and hot-tempered, was not willing to give up on her dream of becoming Lady of Casterly Rock, something which she had been anticipating for years. She convinced Lord Gerold's second son, [Tion](wiki:Tion_Lannister), to break his own betrothal and marry her instead. In this she was succesfull; Ellyn and Tion were wed in [235 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), causing Ellyn to become the Lady of Casterly Rock in all but name, as Lord Gerold was widowed.(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Ellyn used her position to support the position of [her own family](wiki:House_Reyne). In [236 AC](wiki:236_AC), she became a widow when Tion died in the [Fourth Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Fourth_Blackfyre_Rebellion). Ellyn's influence dwindled as Lord Gerold assumed more control over Casterly Rock and began to prepare his younger son Tytos for his future lordship. According to a semi-canon source, Ellyn at first claimed to have been pregnant shortly after Tion's death, but as time passed and her belly remained flat it was concluded the pregnancy had been a lie.(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-sample_2-2) Thereafter, most of the Reynes departed Casterly Rock for Castamere. Due to the [Widow's Law](wiki:Widow's_Law), Ellyn remained at the Rock, but her influence was declining sharply. In [239 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Ellyn made another claim to the position she so desired and attempted to bed Tytos, Lord Gerold's new heir. The young Tytos was so intimidated by Ellyn that he was unable to perform and confessed what had happened to his wife, Lady [Jeyne Marbrand](wiki:Jeyne_Marbrand), who related the tale to Lord Gerold. Gerold acted at once, and Ellyn was hastily married to the twice-widowed [Walderan Tarbeck](wiki:Walderan_Tarbeck), Lord of [Tarbeck Hall](wiki:Tarbeck_Hall).(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) The [rivalry](wiki:War_of_the_Wombs) between Ellyn and Jeyne, which had begun years past, would continue for many years to come.

[Tytos](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) became the [Lord of Casterly Rock](wiki:Lord_of_Casterly_Rock) and [Warden of the West](wiki:Warden_of_the_West) in [244 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) at the death of Lord Gerold. Tytos desired to be loved, and as such was willing to forgive people quickly. Many saw this as a weakness, and as such, many of Tytos's bannermen and merchants from [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) and [Kayce](wiki:Kayce) borrowed money from him without paying their debts. Through her brothers, Lady Ellyn borrowed gold from [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) as well, which she used to restore the crumbling [Tarbeck Hall](wiki:Tarbeck_Hall). The power of House Lannister grew less, and even beyond the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) people began to realize the Lannisters were no longer to be feared.(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

Lord Tytos's eldest three sons left to fight in the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings) in [260 AC](wiki:260_AC). When [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), Tytos's heir, returned from the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) with his brothers, [Kevan](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) and [Tygett](wiki:Tygett_Lannister), he took upon himself to restore the power of House Lannister, despite Tytos's reluctance. Tywin demanded repayment of his father's loans and all who could not pay were ordered to send hostages to Casterly Rock. Kevan formed a new company of five hundred veterans to aid Tywin. Lord [Roger Reyne](wiki:Roger_Reyne), Ellyn's older brother, laughed when he read Tywin's edicts and advised his friends and vassals to do nothing. Lord Walderan Tarbeck hoped to convince Tytos to rescind Tywin's edicts and traveled to Casterly Rock, but was instead imprisoned by Tywin.(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) In return, his wife Ellyn seized three Lannisters – two [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister_of_Lannisport) of [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), and [Stafford Lannister](wiki:Stafford_Lannister), whose sister [Joanna](wiki:Joanna_Lannister) was betrothed to Tywin – and threatened them harm unless her husband was returned. Tytos ignored Tywin's suggestion of sending Lord Walderan back to his wife in three pieces, one for every Lannister taken, and instead returned Walderan unharmed. In addition, he forgave the Tarbeck debt to House Lannister.(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

## Aftermath

By the end of the campaign the rebellious Houses [Reyne](wiki:House_Reyne) and [Tarbeck](wiki:House_Tarbeck) were completely obliterated. The ruined, blackened, and crumbling castles of [Castamere](wiki:Castamere) and [Tarbeck Hall](wiki:Tarbeck_Hall) stand empty to this day as a reminder to those who dare scorn the power of the Rock.(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Castamere's mines have remained sealed.(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16)

Tywin's manner of dealing with the revolt had made him respected and feared throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-17) Though the brutality of Tywin's methods drew censure from some, everyone agreed that order had been restored to the westerlands.(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Tywin's actions to restore [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister)'s power are immortalized in the song "[The Rains of Castamere](wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere)", and he is known to have used the song as a threat against troublesome bannermen.(wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)
