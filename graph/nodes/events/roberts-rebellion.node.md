## Origins

### Tourney at Harrenhal

In [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), a grand [tourney](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal) was held by Lord [Walter Whent](wiki:Walter_Whent) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Many nobles from all over the Seven Kingdoms came to the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) to take part in the festivities. Amongst them were the heir to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark), and his younger siblings, [Eddard](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Lyanna](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) and [Benjen](wiki:Benjen_Stark).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

The most prominent visitor of the tourney was King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), however. Aerys had not left the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) for many years, ever since his half-year capture during the [Defiance of Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale) in [277 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) but he was inspired to come to the tourney by words from his [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers), the eunuch [Varys](wiki:Varys).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

The tourney was cause for great celebration, as spring had come to Westeros, or so men thought.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-12) In addition, all of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) had gathered to welcome their newest member, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister). However, the tourney's ending was less joyful. After winning the final tilt, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) was to crown the new [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty). Instead of crowning his wife, the Dornish princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), Rhaegar rode past her and crowned Lyanna Stark of Winterfell instead. Eddard Stark would later recall this moment as "the moment when all smiles died".(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### Abduction of Lyanna Stark

At the coming of the new year, [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) was abducted by Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) near [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Her brother, [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark), was on his way to [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) to wed [Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), when the news reached him. Brandon rode at once to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with his companions: [Ethan Glover](wiki:Ethan_Glover), Brandon's squire, [Elbert Arryn](wiki:Elbert_Arryn), nephew to Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) and the heir of the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), [Kyle Royce](wiki:Kyle_Royce), and [Jeffory Mallister](wiki:Jeffory_Mallister).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Brandon rode for the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), and outside its gates, unaware that Rhaegar was not present, shouted for him to come out and die. King [Aerys II](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) had them all arrested for plotting the murder of the crown prince, and summoned the fathers of all those who stood accused to court.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark), who had been on his way south with the wedding party for Brandon's wedding,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_catelyn_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) went to King's Landing with two hundred men.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) None of them returned north. In King's Landing, all of those who stood accused were executed,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) with the sole exception of Ethan Glover.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

King Aerys did allow for trials, of a sort. Lord Rickard Stark demanded a [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle), and the king granted his request. Rickard dressed for battle, but Aerys declared fire to be the champion of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). He had Rickard suspended from the rafters in the throne room, where his [pyromancers](wiki:Pyromancers), [Rossart](wiki:Rossart) amongst them, kindled a blaze beneath the Lord of Winterfell. Brandon Stark was brought in as well, and placed in a [Tyroshi](wiki:Tyrosh) strangling device, while a longsword was placed just outside his reach. Brandon was allowed to try and free his father, but the more he struggled to reach the sword, the tighter the cord around his neck became. Brandon strangled himself in an attempt to reach the sword and save his father, while Lord Rickard cooked in his armor.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

Next, Aerys demanded that Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) send him the heads of Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_Baratheon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), Lyanna's betrothed,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) and [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), now the Lord of Winterfell after the deaths of his father and older brother.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Robert and Eddard were Jon's wards at the Eyrie, however, and Jon instead raised his banners in revolt.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) This act is seen as the start of the war,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) which would last "close to a year".(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

## Aftermath

While Robert's Rebellion is considered to have ended at the Sack of King's Landing,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_end.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-1) not all battles were fought. In the south, the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End) continued, while [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) was still missing.

### End of the Siege of Storm's End

The [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End) (6 on the map) had begun after the [Battle of Ashford](wiki:Battle_of_Ashford).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3) Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) had led his forces to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), where they had laid siege to the castle for the better part of a year. Storm's End was being held by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), the younger brother of Robert Baratheon. While the castle garrison was starving and ate rats to survive,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) the forces of the [Reach](wiki:Reach) feasted in sight of the castle.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1) A smuggler named [Davos](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) managed to sail past the blockade of the Redwyne fleet and sold his cargo of onions and fish to the residents of Storm's End,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-2) thereby preventing them from resorting to [cannibalism](wiki:Cannibalism).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-1) The new food supply allowed Storm's End to hold out long enough until Eddard Stark arrived from King's Landing to lift the siege. The lords of the Reach were quick to dip their banners.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)

### Combat at the Tower of Joy

After lifting the siege at Storm's End, Lord Eddard Stark rode further south with six companions: [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed), Lord [Willam Dustin](wiki:Willam_Dustin), [Ethan Glover](wiki:Ethan_Glover), [Martyn Cassel](wiki:Martyn_Cassel), [Theo Wull](wiki:Theo_Wull), and Ser [Mark Ryswell](wiki:Mark_Ryswell).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3) According to a semi-canon source, Ned's army did not accompany him south to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-BB_37-1)

Near the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) of Dorne, they found three knights of Aerys II's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), Ser [Arthur Dayne](wiki:Arthur_Dayne), Ser [Oswell Whent](wiki:Oswell_Whent), and the [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), Ser [Gerold Hightower](wiki:Gerold_Hightower).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-4) They were guarding a tower which Rhaegar is said to have named the [tower of joy](wiki:Tower_of_joy). The northmen and Kingsguard fought, with only Eddard and Howland surviving the [combat at the tower of joy](wiki:Combat_at_the_tower_of_joy).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5) Inside the tower, Eddard found his sister, [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), who extracted a promise from Eddard, and Lyanna died shortly after with her brother by her side.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-4) Eddard later tore down the tower, so he could use the stones to make cairns for the eight dead men.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-6)

Afterwards, Eddard travelled further south, to [Starfall](wiki:Starfall), where he delivered the famed sword [Dawn](wiki:Dawn), which had been in the possession of the [Sword of the Morning](wiki:Sword_of_the_Morning), to Arthur's sister, Lady [Ashara Dayne](wiki:Ashara_Dayne).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_dorne:_the_andals_arrive.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) The woman jumped into the sea to her death.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)

### Assault on Dragonstone

In between the Battle of the Trident and the Sack of King's Landing, Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) and her son and Aerys's new heir, Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), had been sent to the [island of Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island))(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-11)(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_end.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-2) with the Red Keep's master-at-arms, Ser [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-7) There, Viserys was crowned by his mother,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_viserys_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) while on the mainland, [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) began constructing a new [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) to lead an assault on Dragonstone.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-3)

In [284 AC](wiki:284_AC), nine months after the flight to Dragonstone,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-3) Rhaella died giving birth to Princess [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) during a great storm that destroyed the Targaryen fleet at anchor at the island.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-4) With most of the Targaryen fleet destroyed, and with the Baratheon fleet ready to take Dragonstone, the household of the last Targaryen stronghold was willing to sell the children to the new king, [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). Shortly before Stannis sailed to Dragonstone, however, Willem Darry and four loyal men smuggled Viserys, Daenerys, and her wet nurse from the nursery and sailed for the [Braavosian coast](wiki:Braavosian_coast), and the children subsequently lived in exile in the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-5) While Stannis was successful in leading the [assault on Dragonstone](wiki:Assault_on_Dragonstone), Robert scorned him for allowing Willem and the children to escape.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-4)

### Establishment of Baratheon Dynasty

Following the conclusion of the rebellion, [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) was successfully installed as the new [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms). Robert named his foster father, [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), as his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), and he named his middle brother, [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), as [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-5) Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle), and Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) were pardoned during [Robert's coronation](wiki:Coronation_of_Robert_I_Baratheon),(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) and the new king pardoned Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) as well.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

Robert had no wish to marry following the death of his betrothed, [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), but Lord Arryn convinced him otherwise by informing Robert that the realm was in need of an heir. Jon suggested Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s daughter, [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), as the marriage would ensure Tywin's support, should [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) ever attempt to win his throne back.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1) Robert and Cersei married in [284 AC](wiki:284_AC).(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-56) Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) described the marriage as Robert's "first act" as king.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_glorious_reign.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)

### Rumored Dornish Resistance

The deaths of Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell) and her two young children, [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) and [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), infuriated [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell). Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), Elia's younger brother, attempted to raise [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) for the exiled Viserys. Messages were sent by both raven and messenger, though their contents have remained a secret.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), went to Dorne the year after the rebellion,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) returned the bones of Prince [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell), and sat down with [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne) and the older brother of Elia and Oberyn. During his stay in [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), Jon was "questioned closely" regarding the fates of Elia and her children,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-1) and a hundred others were questioned as well.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-2) While all talk of war ended in Dorne after Jon's visit,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-1) Doran continued to plot a Targaryen restoration in secret,(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) working closely together with Oberyn.(wiki:Robert's_Rebellion.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0)
