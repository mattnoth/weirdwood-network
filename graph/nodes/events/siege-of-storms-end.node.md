## Origins

[Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End), took the majority of the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands)' forces with him after the [battles at Summerhall](wiki:Battles_at_Summerhall). He left his younger brother, [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), and a small garrison to hold [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

Following Robert's defeat by Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) at the [Battle of Ashford](wiki:Battle_of_Ashford),(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) driving Robert away from his base of power, the Tyrell host defeated resistance in the stormlands and marched on Storm's End. The castle's granaries and storehouses were only half full, as war had been unexpected.(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_storm.27s_end.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Narrative Arc

Soon after Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) began besieging Storm's End with the [Reach](wiki:Reach)'s host, Lord [Paxter Redwyne](wiki:Paxter_Redwyne) arrived with the [Redwyne fleet](wiki:Redwyne_fleet) from the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor) and closed [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay) to all trade, essentially cutting Storm's End off.(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

The siege continued for the better part of a year, with the garrison having to eat their [horses](wiki:Horse), [dogs](wiki:Dog) and [cats](wiki:Cat), and were nearly forced to eat their own dead.(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) [Donal Noye](wiki:Donal_Noye) lost an arm during the siege(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) after an axe wound festered. Meanwhile, Mace and his bannermen, such as Paxter and Lord [Mathis Rowan](wiki:Mathis_Rowan),(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) feasted within sight of the walls.(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Some of Mace's strength joined the army of Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen).(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-SSM_7-0)

There was at least one breach in discipline within Storm's End, when the castle's master-at-arms, Ser [Gawen Wylde](wiki:Gawen_Wylde), and three knights were caught trying to sneak out a postern gate to surrender. [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) prepared to have them hurled at the [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) host via catapult. Maester [Cressen](wiki:Cressen) convinced Stannis to imprison the men, however, since the garrison might need to eat the dead if their food ran out. Gawen eventually died in his cell during the siege.(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

[Davos](wiki:Davos_Seaworth), a notorious smuggler, eventually slipped through the Redwyne fleet's lines one night and entered a tunnel beneath Storm's End(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) with a ship loaded with onions and salt fish for the starving garrison.(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) This food allowed the garrison to survive long enough for Robert's friend and ally, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), to march south after the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), in which Rhaegar was killed by Stannis's elder brother [Robert](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), and the ensuing [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

With King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) dead and the surviving Targaryens fled to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)), Mace dipped his banners as soon as Eddard and the rebels appeared on the field outside Storm's End.(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

## Aftermath

According to a semi-canon source, the successful defense of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) prevented [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) and [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) from falling into loyalist hands, and it helped the rebel [storm lords](wiki:Stormlands) remain loyal to their liege, [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-SSM_7-1)

After Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) surrendered, Stannis was ordered by the new king, his brother Robert I, to build [a new fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) and [assault Dragonstone](wiki:Assault_on_Dragonstone).(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) The majority of the Reach's lords and knights were later pardoned by Robert for their part in the war.(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Stannis granted [Davos](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) a [knighthood](wiki:Knight) for assisting the garrison, as well as a keep and lands on [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath), [a war galley](wiki:Black_Betha) to be commanded by himself, and later two more to be commanded by his two eldest sons.(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Stannis personally removed the first joint of each finger on Davos Seaworth's left hand as punishment for his smuggling past, however.(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) Davos's younger son [Devan](wiki:Devan_Seaworth) would later be taken as a squire by Stannis.(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Having lost an arm, the blacksmith [Donal Noye](wiki:Donal_Noye) left Storm's End to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) During a visit to [Greenstone](wiki:Greenstone), King Robert slept with a widowed [Estermont cousin](wiki:Estermont_(cousin_of_Robert)) whose husband and father died during the siege.(wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
