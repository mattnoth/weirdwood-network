## Origins

### Kings in the North

The Starks are an ancient house of [First Men](wiki:First_Men) descent.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) House Stark was founded by [Brandon the Builder](wiki:Brandon_the_Builder), a legendary figure who lived during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes). Bran the Builder is said to have raised [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell),(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) the seat of the Starks, and the [Wall](wiki:Wall),(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) and other legends connect him with [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_durrandon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) and the [Hightower](wiki:Hightower).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) The Starks were [Kings of Winter](wiki:King_in_the_North) in the north for eight thousand years(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) since the Age of Heroes, possibly beginning with Bran the Builder. The [Night's King](wiki:Night's_King), the attainted thirteenth [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), has been suggested to have been a Stark, among other possibilities.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) The Night's King is said to have been defeated by [Brandon the Breaker](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(Breaker)) and [Joramun](wiki:Joramun).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

The ancient Starks gradually defeated rival kings, such as the [Barrow Kings](wiki:Barrow_Kings) to their south and the [Red Kings](wiki:Red_King) to their east.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) For several millennia, the Starks were not the uncontested Kings in the North. Their primary antagonists, the Red Kings from [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) of the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort), swore fealty some thousand years ago, ending their [flaying](wiki:Flaying) ways. Meanwhile, King [Jon Stark](wiki:Jon_Stark) drove raiders from the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife), and the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den) was built at its mouth. This stronghold was often granted to sons and grandsons of the King in the North; one such branch, the [Greystarks](wiki:House_Greystark), was extinguished after allying with the Boltons against the Starks.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) King Jon's son, [Rickard](wiki:Rickard_Stark_(king)), defeated the [Marsh King](wiki:Marsh_King) and married his daughter, bringing the [Neck](wiki:Neck) into Winterfell's realm under the lordship of [House Reed](wiki:House_Reed).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2)

When [Andals](wiki:Andals) attempted to conquer the north, King [Theon Stark](wiki:Theon_Stark) made common cause with House Bolton and defeated [Argos Sevenstar](wiki:Argos_Sevenstar) in the [Battle of the Weeping Water](wiki:Battle_of_the_Weeping_Water), after which he raised a fleet and sailed to [Andalos](wiki:Andalos). There he took revenge by sacking and putting hundreds to death. He later conquered the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters), attacked the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers), and battled against the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn), driving them away from [Cape Kraken](wiki:Cape_Kraken) and [Bear Island](wiki:Bear_Island) and slaying [Ravos Hoare](wiki:Ravos_Hoare). Both places were later taken back by King [Loron Greyjoy](wiki:Loron_Greyjoy), and King [Rodrik Stark](wiki:Rodrik_Stark) took Bear Island back after Loron's death. It is said that he did so by winning a wrestling match, though some scholars doubt the truth of this tale.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_mountain_clans.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-3) Rodrik's sons and grandsons took back Cape Kraken.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-1) King Theon also smashed a rebellion in the [Rills](wiki:Rills), and gave aid to the Night's Watch against [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-2)

Thousands of years before [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1) King [Brandon the Shipwright](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(Shipwright)) attempted to sail across the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea), but never returned back home. His son, also named [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(Burner)), burned the northern shipyards in his grief.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-4)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-2) The north has since lacked strength at sea.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)

House Stark attempted to conquer the Three Sisters, which began a conflict between the Starks and [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) from the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn). In response to the [Rape of the Three Sisters](wiki:Rape_of_the_Three_Sisters), the [Sistermen](wiki:Sistermen) asked for help from [Mathos II Arryn](wiki:Mathos_II_Arryn), [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale). This conflict, the [War Across the Water](wiki:War_Across_the_Water), lasted for a thousand years, during which time the Starks thrice landed on the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) During King [Edrick Stark](wiki:Edrick_Stark)'s hundred-year long reign, the Wolf's Den, already torched by House Arryn, was captured by slavers from the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones). Edrick's great-grandson, King [Brandon "Ice Eyes"](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(Ice_Eyes)), took back the Wolf's Den.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-1) After a thousand years of war, the Arryns were victorious over the Starks, and the Valemen have ruled over the Three Sisters ever since.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-1)

Several centuries before Aegon's Conquest,(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-55) [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) was driven into exile from the [Mander](wiki:Mander) by Lord [Lorimar Peake](wiki:Lorimar_Peake) during the reign of [Perceon III Gardener](wiki:Perceon_III_Gardener), [King of the Reach](wiki:King_of_the_Reach).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-3) The Starks welcomed them to the north, and accepted oaths of fealthy from the Manderlys, giving them the Wolf's Den and the surrounding lands, leading to the creation of [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) around the Wolf's Den.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-4)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rawoiafwolf.27s_den.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)

Possibly around 700 BC,(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-58) [Karlon Stark](wiki:Karlon_Stark), a younger son of Winterfell, helped crush a rebel lord. As a reward, he was granted lands on which he built a keep named Karl's Hold. Over time, the keep became known as [Karhold](wiki:Karhold) and Karlon's descendants became [House Karstark](wiki:House_Karstark).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0)

The Starks helped repel several major [wildling](wiki:Wildling) invasions, such as when they and their [Umber](wiki:House_Umber) bannermen defeated the brother [Kings-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall) [Gendel](wiki:Gendel) and [Gorne](wiki:Gorne) three thousand years ago.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_wildlings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) [Bael the Bard](wiki:Bael_the_Bard) is said to have sired a son on the maiden daughter of [Brandon the Daughterless](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_daughterless)). Bael was later slain by his own son, who was unaware of the fact that Bael was his father.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1)

### Targaryen Conquest and Aftermath

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), King [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark) raised thirty thousand [northmen](wiki:Northmen) and marched south. With the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal) and the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire) in mind, however, Torrhen submitted without battle to [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) Since the time of the King Who Knelt, the Starks have held the [north](wiki:North) for the [Lords of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lords_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms) as [Wardens of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0) Queen [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen) arranged the marriage of Torrhen's daughter to [Ronnel Arryn](wiki:Ronnel_Arryn_(king)), the [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie), in an attempt to knit the new realm together.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0) There are letters at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) which suggest that Torrhen only agreed to this match after much protest, and that his sons had refused to attend the wedding.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-1) Torrhen's sons did not agree with the rule of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). Some spoke of rebelling and raising the Stark banner.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-2)

Torrhen was succeeded by [Brandon the Boisterous](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_Boisterous)). At some point rule of the north passed to [Roderick Stark](wiki:Roderick_Stark), and Roderick was succeeded by [Brandon the Boastful](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(father_of_Walton)). Brandon the Boastful attended the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) in [49 AC](wiki:49_AC), along with his sons [Walton](wiki:Walton_Stark_(son_of_Brandon)) and [Alaric](wiki:Alaric_Stark), though Brandon died soon after his return to the north.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0)

Despite earlier rumblings of dissent by Torrhen's sons, the Starks did not rise like the rebels [Jonos Arryn](wiki:Jonos_Arryn), [Lodos the Twice-Drowned](wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned), and [Harren the Red](wiki:Harren_the_Red) against Aegon the Conqueror's son, King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen). As the north follows the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods), they did not heed the [High Septon's](wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I)) calls for rebellion, and the Starks took no part in the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) against Aenys and then his brother, King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). Captured members of the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) had been exiled to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) by Maegor the Cruel, and Aenys's son, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), exiled many of Maegor's supporters to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) as well, including surviving members of his old [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). Neither group had love for Jaehaerys, so in [50 AC](wiki:50_AC) the two groups made common cause to [rebel against the leadership of the Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch_rebellion_of_50_AC). Lord Walton Stark called his bannermen to intervene and crushed the deserters, but survivors fled beyond the Wall; Walton pursued them but was ambushed by wildlings and killed by [giants](wiki:Giants). Walton was succeeded by his brother Alaric.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-1)

In [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) visited Winterfell and was received by Lord Alaric, who was initially cold to her and Jaehaerys because of Walton's fate. Alaric was gradually charmed by Alysanne's visit, however, and his daughter ​[Alarra](wiki:Alarra_Stark) grew particularly close to the queen during her visit.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-3) Two of Alaric's sons attended the [tourney in King's Landing](wiki:Tourney_at_King's_Landing_on_the_anniversary_of_the_king's_coronation) which celebrated the tenth anniversary of Jaehaerys's coronation.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-4) Alaric ordered his bannermen to put aside half of their harvests in anticipation of the winter of [59](wiki:59_AC)–[60 AC](wiki:60_AC), but not all of the northmen obeyed, resulting in famine.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0) The [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) followed later that winter, though no specific casualties are mentioned in the north and Alaric survived. Lord Alaric died in [72 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and was succeeded by his grandson, [Edric Stark](wiki:Edric_Stark), as Alaric's two sons had preceded him in death.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-1)

Alaric's relationship with the Targaryens remained complicated throughout his reign. According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), Alysanne charmed a reluctant Alaric(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-67) into granting the [New Gift](wiki:New_Gift) to the Night's Watch because, even though Alaric was a strong friend to the Watch, he knew the northern lords who already held the lands in question would not be happy to give them away.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-5) According to Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), however, the Starks were opposed to the royal demand for a different reason: the Starks feared that, under the control of [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), the New Gift would inevitably decline, for the Night's Watch would always look northward and never give much thought to their new tenants to the south. Time proved this criticism correct, for as the decades passed the Night's Watch neglected the New Gift and it fell into disuse. Yandel cites that there are archived letters in the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) sent by Lord Stark’s sons, asking the [maesters](wiki:Maesters) about precedents against forced donation of property.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-3) Whatever the case, on the whole Jaehaerys and Alysanne became popular in the north due to their other generous donations to the Night's Watch, and their construction of the new [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) extending from the [Neck](wiki:Neck) to Winterfell and the Wall.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-4)

### The Dance of the Dragons

Edric was succeeded by Lord [Ellard Stark](wiki:Ellard_Stark), who attended the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC) to determine the new heir of the elderly King Jaehaerys. The final vote came to a choice between [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) (son of Jaehaerys's second son, Prince [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I))) and [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon) (grandson of Jaehaerys through [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)), daughter of his first son, Prince [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I))). Viserys was the candidate Jaehaerys himself preferred, and he won the final vote by a landslide, though the Starks were among the minority of lords who had supported Laenor (and one of only two [Great Houses](wiki:Great_Houses), the other being the [Baratheons](wiki:House_Baratheon)). Yandel that Ellard voted against Jaehaerys's preferred successor because the Starks were still bitter about the king forcing them to donate the [New Gift](wiki:New_Gift) to the Night's Watch, leading to its mismanagement. The pushed the Starks closer to the Velaryon faction at King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen)'s court, and through them to ultimately side with [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-5)

At some point after [101 AC](wiki:101_AC), Ellard was succeeded by [Benjen Stark](wiki:Benjen_Stark_(lord)), and later Benjen was succeeded by his son [Rickon](wiki:Rickon_Stark_(son_of_Benjen)). Rickon died in [121 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and was succeeded as the nominal Lord of Winterfell by his only surviving son, the thirteen-year-old [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark). Rickon's brother, [Bennard](wiki:Bennard_Stark), served as [regent](wiki:Regent) during Cregan's minority. When Cregan came of age in [126 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), however, Bennard was slow to surrender his power. Relations grew strained between Bennard and Cregan as the young Lord of Winterfell chafed under the limits imposed on him by his uncle. Ultimately, Cregan rose up against his uncle, imprisoned Bennard and his sons, and took rule of the north into his own hands.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0)

In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) began, Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) recruited Lord Cregan's aid for his mother, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), known as the [Pact of Ice and Fire](wiki:Pact_of_Ice_and_Fire),(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-6) even though the north was considered to be too remote to be of much influence in the war.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0) Cregan initially sent the [Winter Wolves](wiki:Winter_Wolves) under [Roderick Dustin](wiki:Roderick_Dustin), [Lord of Barrowton](wiki:Lord_of_Barrowton), and knights from [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) led by Ser [Medrick](wiki:Medrick_Manderly) and Ser [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0)

After the gathering of one last harvest before winter,(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0) Cregan led a great army of childless, homeless, unwed, or old men, and younger sons, to King's Landing. He arrived after King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) had been [poisoned](wiki:Poison), however, and though he had hoped to punish those lords who had supported the late king, [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), [Lord of the Tides](wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides), had already sent out ravens suing for peace. While the court awaited replies, Cregan held sway and arrested all those involved in Aegon II's death, as he objected to the murder of a king. The as-yet-uncrowned [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) was cowed into naming Cregan as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), though Cregan served only a day in that office, a time known as the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf). Lord Stark held trials for twenty-one out of twenty-two of his prisoners, with Corlys the only one spared a trial. Aegon III pardoned Lord Velaryon and restored his titles to him, which Cregan allowed when [Alysanne Blackwood](wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood) offered the Hand her own hand in marriage. Cregan returned north the next day.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0)

Cregan struggled to hold the north together against several threats during the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III). A devastating winter had already begun late in the war which ultimately lasted six years, and in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC) the north was one of the first regions in Westeros struck by the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever), which continued through [133 AC](wiki:133_AC). With the north weakened by winter, famine, and plague, a large [wildling](wiki:Free_folk) war-band led by [Sylas the Grim](wiki:Sylas_the_Grim) seized on the opportunity to force their way south of the Wall. His force of 3,000 raiders overwhelmed the Night's Watch at [Queensgate](wiki:Queensgate) and proceeded to plunder the lands to the south. Lord Cregan rallied what few remaining able-bodied men were left among his bannermen, then rode out to hunt down and destroy Sylas's raiding bands.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-0)

Cregan's eldest son and heir was [Rickon](wiki:Rickon_Stark_(son_of_Cregan)), named after his own father. Rickon predeceased his father when he died in one of the final battles of the [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne), during the reign of King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen). The north lamented Rickon's death, for the subsequent reigns of Cregan's younger sons after him were troublesome.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-7)

### Cregan's successors

Cregan Stark had five sons: [Rickon](wiki:Rickon_Stark_(son_of_Cregan)), [Jonnel](wiki:Jonnel_Stark), [Edric](wiki:Edric_Stark_(son_of_Cregan)), [Barthogan](wiki:Barthogan_Stark), and [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(son_of_Cregan)). Though Rickon predeceased his father, he was survived by his own two daughters, [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark_(daughter_of_Rickon)) and [Serena](wiki:Serena_Stark), and by the known [laws](wiki:Laws_and_justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms) of succession they should have been ahead of Rickon's younger brothers in line of inheritance. Northern succession laws may be different,(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-HW_74-0) there has never been a ruling Queen of Winter or ruling Lady of Winterfell for example,(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-75) however, and primogeniture is not necessarily binding elsewhere in the Seven Kingdoms either.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-76) Cregan's second son Jonnel succeeded Cregan upon his death, marrying his half-niece Sansa.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_stark_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0) Cregan's third son Edric married Rickon's other daughter Serena.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_stark_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-1) Jonnel's marriage to Sansa was childless, but upon his death rule passed not to Cregan's third son Edric, but to his fourth son, Barthogan.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_stoneborn_of_skagos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-0) While it is possible that Edric predeceased his older brother, Edric did have two sons and two daughters with Serena, and the daughters had children of their own,(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_stark_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-2) and all of these normally would have been ahead of Barthogan in the line of succession. The exact reasons and circumstances under which Barthogan became lord are as yet unknown.

Barthogan himself later died childless, when he was slain in the [Skagosi](wiki:Skagos) rebellion during the reign of King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen),(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_stoneborn_of_skagos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-1) and he was succeeded by Cregan's fifth and final son, Brandon, from whom the later generations of House Stark descend. Convoluted succession challenges remained for decades, possibly through Serena and Edric's children and between Brandon's own rival grandchildren. Brandon was succeeded by his eldest son [Rodwell](wiki:Rodwell_Stark), who died without issue, after which rule passed to his younger brother [Beron](wiki:Beron_Stark). The grandson of Cregan, Beron was mortally wounded fighting off an incursion by [Dagon Greyjoy](wiki:Dagon_Greyjoy)'s [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn), and as he lay dying in Winterfell [multiple Stark widows](wiki:The_She-Wolves_of_Winterfell) pushed their children's rival succession claims against the others.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-79)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-80)

Ultimately Beron was succeeded by his eldest son, [Donnor Stark](wiki:Donnor_Stark), but when he died without issue rule passed to second son, [Willam Stark](wiki:Willam_Stark). In [226 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [Raymun Redbeard](wiki:Raymun_Redbeard), the [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall), led a large [wildling](wiki:Wildling) invasion. Lord Willam and [Harmond Umber](wiki:Harmond_Umber), [Lord of the Last Hearth](wiki:Lord_of_the_Last_Hearth), met the wildling host in battle on the shores of [Long Lake](wiki:Battle_at_Long_Lake). Although Willam was killed by Raymun, his younger brother, [Artos](wiki:Artos_Stark), avenged him by slaying the King-Beyond-the-Wall.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_wildlings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-1)

Willam was succeeded upon his death by his son [Edwyle Stark](wiki:Edwyle_Stark), father of [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark) and grandfather of [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark).

### Robert's Rebellion

Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark), urged on by his [maester](wiki:Maester), [Walys](wiki:Walys_Flowers), allegedly sought to further [southron](wiki:Southron) alliances.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-0) He sent his second son, [Eddard](wiki:Eddard_Stark), to be fostered with Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) at the age of eight. There, Eddard befriended fellow ward [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1) Rickard betrothed his heir, [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark), to [Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), the daughter of [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully), [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun),(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-2) and his only daughter, [Lyanna](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), to Robert, [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-0) Rickard's four children attended the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal) in [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), where they met [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed). After winning the jousting competition at the tourney, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) crowned Lyanna as his [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty), passing over his own wife, Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_84-0)

In [282 AC](wiki:282_AC), Brandon Stark was at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) when the date of his wedding to Catelyn Tully was announced. [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), Lord Hoster's ward, had been in love with Catelyn for years, and challenged Brandon to a duel for Catelyn's hand. Brandon won.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_85-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_86-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_87-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) After the duel, Brandon left Riverrun, promising his bride-to-be that he would return soon.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) On his way back to Riverrun, however, Brandon heard about Lyanna's abduction by Prince Rhaegar, and rode for King's Landing, where he was arrested by Aerys II for threatening the life of the crown prince. Rickard, who had been on his way to Riverrun with the wedding party, was summoned to court to answer for Brandon's crime. Brandon and Rickard were executed by Aerys II; Rickard was burned alive, while Brandon strangled himself in his attempt to rescue his father.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Racok5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_88-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rawoiafcatelyn_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_89-0) After the executions, King Aerys demanded the heads of Eddard Stark and Robert Baratheon from their former foster father, Jon Arryn. The Lord of the Eyrie refused, however, and raised his banners in revolt.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-3)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_90-0) This act is seen as the start of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_90-1)

Eddard made an arduous journey through the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) and the [Bite](wiki:Bite) to call his banners in the north.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-1) The new lord then marched south and won an important victory during the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells), rescuing his friend Robert with the aid of Hoster Tully.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_91-0) Next, he went to Riverrun, where he honored his late brother Brandon's betrothal by marrying Catelyn as part of the alliance to bring [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) into the rebel fold.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_92-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-3)

One of the principal commanders in the war, Eddard took command of the rebel army after the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), following the remnants of Rhaegar's army to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_93-0) Ned arrived at the capital after the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing) by Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), however, and Lord Stark found Aerys II dead and Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) sitting on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_93-1) He had a falling out with Robert when Robert accepted the aid of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) and condoned the murders of Rhaegar's [wife](wiki:Elia_Martell), [young daughter](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)), and [infant son](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)). Eddard rode south the next day to relieve the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_93-2)

After Storm's End, Eddard and six companions went to retrieve Lyanna at the building Rhaegar had named the [tower of joy](wiki:Tower_of_joy), finding it defended by three knights of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). Eddard and Howland Reed were the only survivors of the ensuing [combat at the tower of joy](wiki:Combat_at_the_tower_of_joy).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_94-0) Eddard found Lyanna dying in a bed of blood, clutching rose petals, dead and black, in her palm.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) She forced him to make a promise to her, the contents of which are unknown, but which have haunted Eddard since. Eddard and Robert were reconciled in their shared grief over Lyanna's death.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_93-3)

Eddard returned from the war with his [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow),(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-4) said to have been birthed by [Wylla](wiki:Wylla),(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_93-4) and Ned was presented with his trueborn son, [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark), by Catelyn when she arrived from Riverrun. Lord Stark fought in [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion) in [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), and he took [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy)'s son [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) with him to Winterfell, a hostage to ensure the loyalty of the [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-4) Eddard has ruled the [north](wiki:North) in Robert's name, keeping a distance from the intrigue-ridden politics of King's Landing.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_98-0)

The statues of the kings and lords have stone wolves by their feet(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) and have swords placed upon their lap,(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) which are said to keep the spirits of the dead at rest, locked within their tombs.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

House Stark traditionally follows the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Racok43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Following the marriage of Eddard to Lady [Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), a follower of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), a [small sept](wiki:Lady_Catelyn's_sept) was constructed for her at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Following the death of his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) journeys to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to offer Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) the office.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-4)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-5) A letter arrives from [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), Jon's widow and Eddard's wife [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark)'s sister, who claims the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) were behind Lord Arryn's sudden death. At Catelyn's urging, Ned grudgingly accepts the Handship and a betrothal of their daughter [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) to Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-5) During the royal party's stay, Eddard's second son, [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark), accidentally witnesses Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) having sex with her twin brother, Ser [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_98-1) Jaime throws Bran out of the tower window, hoping to kill him to keep their relationship a secret.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_98-2) Bran survives, but is crippled and comatose.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_99-0)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_100-0)

Eddard rides to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) along with his daughters, Sansa and [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark), while Catelyn remains at Winterfell with their sons. Meanwhile, Ned's bastard son, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), rides north with his uncle, [Benjen](wiki:Benjen_Stark), to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_97-1) Catelyn is inconsolable in her worry for Bran, ignoring her duties as Lady of Winterfell, but she is jolted back to reality by [an attempt](wiki:Catspaw) on Bran's life thwarted only by [his direwolf](wiki:Summer).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1) As Bran lies comatose, he begins to have visions of a [three-eyed crow](wiki:Three-eyed_crow).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_101-0)

Near the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford) and the [crossroads](wiki:Crossroads), Arya defends her friend [Mycah](wiki:Mycah) from the aggressions of Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), upsetting Sansa.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) This is exacerbated when Sansa's direwolf, [Lady](wiki:Lady_(direwolf)), is ordered killed in place of Arya's, [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria_(direwolf)), which has escaped.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_102-0)

In [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Eddard is outraged to find that the crown is heavily in debt (largely to Robert's goodfather, [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), [Lord of Casterly Rock](wiki:Lord_of_Casterly_Rock)) and that the king intends to throw a [lavish tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney) in his honor.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) After taking up his duties as Hand, Eddard assigns twenty of [his household guard](wiki:House_Stark_guards) to assist the [gold cloaks](wiki:Gold_cloaks) in keeping order in King's Landing.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_103-0)

Ned investigates the death of Jon Arryn, determining that he was looking into the king's [bastard](wiki:Bastard) brood. When Robert holds a [small council](wiki:Small_council) meeting to arrange the death of the exiled [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), who has wed [Drogo](wiki:Drogo), a mighty *[khal](wiki:Khal)* of the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki), Ned refuses to condone the assassination and resigns as Hand.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_104-0) In the streets of the capital, he is waylaid by Jaime Lannister and [red cloaks](wiki:House_Lannister_guards) in retaliation for Catelyn's abduction of [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), whom her foster-brother, [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), had told her was responsible for the attempt on Bran's life.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_105-0) Ned's leg is broken and his best men slain, but Ned survives and Robert re-confirms his appointment as Hand before going on a hunt and leaving Ned to sit the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_94-1) In this capacity he sends men, including twenty Stark guards, under [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion), [Lord of Blackhaven](wiki:Lord_of_Blackhaven), to stop Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) from raiding the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_106-0) Gregor's attacks were ordered by Lord Lannister; since Lord Stark was wounded he was not lured into the field and thus could not be captured and traded for Tyrion. The men sent after Gregor are [ambushed](wiki:Battle_at_the_Mummer's_Ford) and many are slain.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_107-0) Catelyn brings Tyrion to her sister Lysa at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), but Tyrion is released after [Bronn](wiki:Bronn) is victorious in a [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

At the [Wall](wiki:Wall), Jon Snow deals with the cruel tutelage of Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne) while making friends such as [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) and learning lessons from his elders.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_108-0) Jon becomes the personal [steward](wiki:Steward) of [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_109-0) The youth encounters a hint of the evil that supposedly lies [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall) when the corpses of two of his uncle's men [rise from the dead](wiki:Wight) and go on the rampage at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black); Jon saves the Lord Commander from being killed by [one of the wights](wiki:Othor).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_110-0)

Eventually, Ned arrives at the same conclusion at which Jon Arryn did: that Queen Cersei's children are not Robert's, but bastards born of [incest](wiki:Incest) between the Lannister twins, and determines that it is this secret for which Jon was murdered. He approaches Cersei with this truth, warning her to flee the city.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_111-0) Enlisting Lord Baelish to recruit the gold cloaks,(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_112-0) Ned confronts Cersei after Robert's death from a hunting accident, insisting that [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) is now king by rights. Littlefinger betrays Ned, however, and the Hand's men are slaughtered by [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt)'s gold cloaks and Ned is thrown into a [black cell](wiki:Black_cell).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_113-0) Sansa is held hostage in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep),(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_114-0) but Arya manages to escape thanks to [Syrio Forel](wiki:Syrio_Forel), the fencing master Ned had hired to train her in swordplay.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3)

[Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) calls the banners to Winterfell and marches south to aid his mother's family, [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), against the Lannisters, and to rescue his father and sisters from the capital.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2) At the [Twins](wiki:Twins), Catelyn arranges an alliance with [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), [Lord of the Crossing](wiki:Lord_of_the_Crossing), to negotiate passage over the [Green Fork](wiki:Green_Fork), promising that Robb and Arya will wed [Freys](wiki:House_Frey).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_115-0) Robb captures Jaime in the [Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_of_the_Whispering_Wood)(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_116-0) and then relieves the Lannisters' siege of Riverrun with the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_117-0) The rest of the Stark army, led by [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), [Lord of the Dreadfort](wiki:Lord_of_the_Dreadfort), is defeated by Tywin in the [battle on the Green Fork](wiki:Battle_on_the_Green_Fork), however.(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_118-0)

Benjen has gone missing [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall), and Jon Snow, after briefly deserting the Watch to join his half-brother's army, rides as part of the [great ranging](wiki:Great_ranging) meant to determine his fate and the threats of the [Others](wiki:Others) and [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings).(wiki:House_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)
