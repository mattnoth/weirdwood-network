## Origins

The Barrow Kings claimed descent from the [First King](wiki:First_King) of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), who is said to be buried at the [Great Barrow](wiki:Great_Barrow) in [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton).(wiki:Barrow_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Barrow_Kings.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Some Barrow Kings allied with [Marsh Kings](wiki:Marsh_King) against [southron](wiki:Southron) invaders who tried to pass the [Neck](wiki:Neck).(wiki:Barrow_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Singers claim the Barrow Kings fought against the [Kings of Winter](wiki:Kings_of_Winter), the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), north of the barrowlands, in the [Thousand Years War](wiki:Thousand_Years_War), but [runes](wiki:Runes) of the First Men indicates the conflict lasted about two hundred years. When the last Barrow King submitted to Winterfell, the Stark king received his daughter in marriage. The [Dustins](wiki:House_Dustin) of Barrowton claim descent from the First King and the Barrow Kings.(wiki:Barrow_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
Maester [Kennet's](wiki:Kennet_(maester)) [Passages of the Dead](wiki:Passages_of_the_Dead) mentions a curse was supposedly placed on the Great Barrow, weakening and making corpselike any living man who dared to equal the First King. Some [maesters](wiki:Maesters) think the [corpse queen](wiki:Corpse_queen) of the [Night's King](wiki:Night's_King) may have actually been a daughter of a Barrow King, as they were often connected with graves.(wiki:Barrow_Kings.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
