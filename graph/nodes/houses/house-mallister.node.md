## Identity

House Mallister of Seagard is one of the most prominent noble houses from the riverlands, with a prouder lineage than their liege lords, House Tully (wiki:House_Mallister). Their blazon is a silver eagle displayed on an indigo (purple) field, and their motto is "Above the rest" (wiki:House_Mallister).

## Origins

The Mallisters conquered most of the Cape of Eagles from the ironborn during the reign of King Torgon Greyiron (wiki:House_Mallister). After the fall of House Justman, the Mallisters were among the petty river kings; Lord Petyr Mallister briefly and ingloriously reigned as King of the Trident before his defeat (wiki:House_Mallister).

## Allegiances

`SWORN_TO` House Tully as overlord during AGOT (track_b: Overlords). `SWORN_TO` House Baelish as overlord by ADWD (track_b: Overlords). Seagard was built to defend the coast against ironborn reavers, and the Mallisters distrust the ironborn (wiki:House_Mallister).

## Appearances & Description

Sigil: A silver eagle displayed on indigo (wiki:House_Mallister). Some Mallisters wear winged helms; Mallister guardsmen wear indigo cloaks (wiki:House_Mallister).

## Narrative Arc

In AGOT, Lord Jason Mallister is present at the Hand's tourney and later fights in the War of the Five Kings, slaying three of Rhaegar Frey's sons in the Battle of the Camps (wiki:House_Mallister). He supports Robb Stark's crowning as King in the North. In ACOK-ASOS, Jason continues to fight for the Stark-Tully cause. His son Patrek is captured. In AFFC, Jason eventually yields Seagard to the Iron Throne and bends the knee, his banner seen among those besieging Riverrun (wiki:House_Mallister). Ser Denys Mallister of the Night's Watch is a candidate for Lord Commander (agot-prologue).
