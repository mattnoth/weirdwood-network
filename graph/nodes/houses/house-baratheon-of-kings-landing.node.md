## Identity

House Baratheon of King's Landing is the royal line of House Baratheon of Storm's End. Founded by Robert I Baratheon after his successful rebellion against King Aerys II Targaryen. The house sits the Iron Throne from the Red Keep in King's Landing and receives the support of the mainland crownlands houses (wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing).

## Heraldry & Sigil

Per pale crimson and gold, a golden lion rampant and a black stag (Per pale gules and or, a lion rampant of the second, a stag sable) Formerly: A crowned black stag on a golden field (Or, a stag crowned sable) (wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing)

## Origins

Founded in 283 AC when Robert Baratheon overthrew the Targaryen dynasty and moved from Storm's End to King's Landing to rule the Seven Kingdoms (wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing).

## Allegiances

Sovereign house, though sovereignty is disputed by House Baratheon of Dragonstone and House Targaryen (track_b: Overlord). The house's claim to the Iron Throne is complicated by the secret that Cersei's children — Joffrey, Myrcella, and Tommen — are not Robert's, but the product of incest between Cersei and Jaime Lannister (wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing).

## Appearances & Description

Sigil under Robert: a crowned black stag on gold. Under Joffrey and Tommen: per pale crimson and gold, a golden lion rampant and a black stag — combining the Baratheon stag with the Lannister lion (wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing).

## Narrative Arc

In AGOT, King Robert appoints Eddard Stark as Hand and offers Joffrey to Sansa. Robert dies from a boar wound, arranged by Cersei who fears discovery of her children's true parentage. In ACOK, Joffrey I takes the throne; his claim is disputed by uncles Stannis and Renly. Tyrion serves as Hand, betrothing Myrcella to Trystane Martell. The house defends King's Landing at the Blackwater and Joffrey is betrothed to Margaery Tyrell. In ASOS, Joffrey is assassinated at his wedding feast and Tommen I inherits the throne. In AFFC, Tommen weds Margaery; the house is effectively controlled by House Lannister. Arianne Martell plots to crown Myrcella under Dornish succession law but fails. In ADWD, Cersei's arrest by the Faith increases Tyrell influence over Tommen (wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing).

## Notes

The house's legitimacy is fundamentally undermined by the incest secret — all three of Cersei's children (Joffrey, Myrcella, Tommen) are Jaime's, not Robert's. This is known to Stannis, Eddard, Jon Arryn, and eventually widely rumored. The claim to Storm's End is disputed with House Baratheon of Dragonstone.
