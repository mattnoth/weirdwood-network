## Origins

### Origin[ ]

According to legend, the founder of House Casterly was the huntsman [Corlos](wiki:Corlos), son of [Caster](wiki:Caster), who lived in a village near where [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) eventually sprang up. When a [lion](wiki:Lion) began preying upon the village's sheep, Corlos tracked him back to his den, a cave in the base of the [nearby mountain](wiki:Casterly_Rock). Armed only with a spear, Corlos slew the lion and his mate, but spared her newborn cubs. This act of mercy pleased the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods), who took favor on Corlos and sent a shaft of sunlight deep into the cave, which shone upon a huge vein of [gold](wiki:Gold).(wiki:House_Casterly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
Whether or not the legend is true, Corlos or some other progenitor of House Casterly began to mine the gold within the Rock. To defend his treasure, he moved inside the cave and fortified the entrance. As time passed, the Casterlys delved deeper into the earth, creating halls, galleries, stairways, and tunnels, into what became known as Casterly Rock. Although they never ruled as [kings](wiki:King), the Casterlys became the most powerful house of the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) and the richest lords in all of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:House_Casterly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

### Disappearance[ ]

The Casterlys disappeared from history during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), having been replaced by [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister). Legends claim that the [Andal](wiki:Andal) adventurer [Lann the Clever](wiki:Lann_the_Clever) tricked [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) from the Casterlys, although his methods vary in different stories. The most common tale is that Lann coated himself with butter and squeezed into the Rock through a narrow gap. Once inside, he played tricks on the Casterlys to convince them their home was haunted so they would flee. Another story claims Lann filled the Rock with vermin to drive out the Casterlys. Some stories have Lann smuggling lions into the Rock to kill the male Casterlys, and the rogue then secretly impregnated the Casterly women.(wiki:House_Casterly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)
In contrast to the legends, [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Perestan](wiki:Perestan) suggests that Lann was a retainer or household guard of the Casterlys who married the daughter of Lord Casterly. Perestan theorizes that if Lord Casterly had no trueborn sons, then after his death the Rock passed to the daughter and Lann.(wiki:House_Casterly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)
