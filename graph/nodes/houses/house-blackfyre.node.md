## Identity

House Blackfyre is an extinct noble house from the crownlands, a cadet branch of House Targaryen founded by Daemon Blackfyre, a legitimized Great Bastard of King Aegon IV Targaryen. Named for the Valyrian steel sword Blackfyre. The male line went extinct in 260 AC (wiki:House_Blackfyre).

## Heraldry & Sigil

A black three-headed dragon, breathing black fire on red (Gules, a dragon thrice-headed sable flammant of the last) (wiki:House_Blackfyre)

## Origins

Daemon Waters, bastard son of Aegon IV, won a squires' tourney at age twelve, was knighted, and given the Valyrian steel sword Blackfyre — wielded by every Targaryen king since the Conquest. He took the name Blackfyre for himself. When Aegon IV legitimized his bastards on his deathbed, Daemon became a legitimate claimant. He married Rohanne of Tyrosh at fourteen (wiki:House_Blackfyre).

## Appearances & Description

Sigil: a black three-headed dragon breathing black fire on red — the Targaryen sigil with colors reversed (wiki:House_Blackfyre). Inherited Targaryen traits: platinum blond hair, violet eyes, and dragon dreams (wiki:House_Blackfyre).

## Narrative Arc

The house's history spans five rebellions against the Targaryens. The First Blackfyre Rebellion saw Daemon Blackfyre's bid for the throne end in defeat. Subsequent rebellions — Second, Third, Fourth, and the War of the Ninepenny Kings — progressively weakened the house. The male line is considered extinct as of 260 AC. In the main ASOIAF narrative, the Blackfyre legacy surfaces through discussions of legitimized bastards and the question of whether the Golden Company's support for "Aegon" Targaryen represents a hidden Blackfyre connection (wiki:House_Blackfyre).
