## Origins

After King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) exterminated [House Harroway](wiki:House_Harroway) in [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), he divided the Harroway holdings among his remaining supporters. The king granted [Lord Harroway's Town](wiki:Lord_Harroway's_Town) to Lord [Alton Butterwell](wiki:Alton_Butterwell), [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) to Lord [Walton Towers](wiki:Walton_Towers), and the rest of the Harroway holdings to Lord [Darnold Darry](wiki:Darnold_Darry).(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) When Maegor decided to take more wives as he needed an heir, Alton, the king's [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), suggested his widowed stout sister as a match, who while no great beauty had given birth to seven children so her fertility was beyond doubt. Maegor was scornful of Alton's offer, but remembering his words he eventually chose women of proven fertility when he wed three new [brides](wiki:Black_Brides) in [47 AC](wiki:47_AC).(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Lord Butterwell initially supported Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen)'s claim to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and declared for the [blacks](wiki:Blacks). He was captured and sent to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s dungeons. When he was faced with the choice between swearing fealty to [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) or death, Lord Butterwell chose to defect to the [greens](wiki:Greens).(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
[Lord Butterwell](wiki:Lord_Butterwell_(grandfather_of_Ambrose)) was [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen). King Aegon stayed one night at Butterwell's castle,(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) and gave his Hand a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) in return for "access" to his three [maiden](wiki:Maiden) daughters.(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) By the next day all three girls had royal [bastards](wiki:Bastards) on the way, according to the tales.(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) He had [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls) built, the pale castle was commonly called the Milkhouse by the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) who lived near it. Its walls, keep, and towers were made of white stone quarried in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), and its floors and pillars were crafted from white [marble](wiki:Marble) veined with [gold](wiki:Gold). The rafters were carved from the trunks of [weirwoods](wiki:Weirwood).(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
Lord Butterwell's grandson, [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell), was a [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) under King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen)(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) and was also later a Hand of the King, for [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) At the beginning of the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), Lord Ambrose performed so poorly that some thought he was in league with [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre).(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) He was replaced by [Lord Hayford](wiki:Lord_Hayford) as Hand.(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)
The Butterwells attempted to keep one foot in each camp during the First Blackfyre Rebellion.(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) Lord Ambrose's eldest son fought for Daeron while his second son fought for Daemon, but both sons died at the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field). His youngest son died of the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness).(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)
In [212 AC](wiki:212_AC), Lord Ambrose held a [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls), for a [marriage](wiki:Marriage) between himself and [the daughter](wiki:Lady_Butterwell) of [Lord Frey](wiki:Lord_Frey), which became the setting for the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion). As punishment, Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) allowed Ambrose to keep only a tenth of his wealth. Whitewalls was stripped from the Butterwells, and Bloodraven decided to have it dismantled to prevent it from becoming a [Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) monument.(wiki:House_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)
