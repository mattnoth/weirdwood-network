## Origins

### Age of Heroes[ ]

The Blackwoods are an ancient house descended of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men). Family traditions state they ruled most of the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood) in the [north](wiki:North) before being driven south by the [Kings of Winter](wiki:Kings_of_Winter) from [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark), which Maester [Barneby](wiki:Barneby)'s [runic](wiki:Runes) translations support.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
Petty [river kings](wiki:River_kings) from House Blackwood claimed dominion over the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) in the [Age of the Hundred Kingdoms](wiki:Age_of_the_Hundred_Kingdoms).(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
The Blackwoods have an old and bitter feud against their neighbors in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), the [Brackens](wiki:House_Bracken) of the [Stone Hedge](wiki:Stone_Hedge), coming from the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes) when both houses ruled as kings. According to the Blackwoods, the Brackens were petty lords and horse breeders who hired swords to usurp the Blackwood kings. The Brackens, in contrast, claim the Blackwoods were vassals who betrayed and usurped Bracken kings.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) The Blackwoods believe these events occurred five hundred years before the arrival of the [Andals](wiki:Andals), but the [True History](wiki:True_History) states it was one thousand years before the Andals.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)

### Andal Riverlands[ ]

The Blackwoods and Brackens allied together against the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to the riverlands, but they were defeated in the [Battle of the Bitter River](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bitter_River).(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The feud was aided by House Bracken's conversion to the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) after the Andal invasion.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-12) In contrast, the Blackwoods kept the religion of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods), being one of the few houses south of the [Neck](wiki:Neck) to do so. The Blackwoods accuse the Brackens of having poisoned the [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) of [Raventree Hall](wiki:Raventree_Hall).(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8) The [Teats](wiki:Teats), [Battle Valley](wiki:Battle_Valley), and the village of [Blackbuckle](wiki:Blackbuckle) are part of the disputed land. There have been numerous peaces between the Blackwoods and Brackens, many sealed by marriage, but the feud always restarts.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)
Benedict Rivers was a bastard born from both houses who eventually became King [Benedict I Justman](wiki:Benedict_I_Justman), with [House Justman](wiki:House_Justman) ruling the riverlands for about three centuries. There were Blackwoods among the many petty kings who disputed the riverlands in the tumultuous century after King [Bernarr II Justman](wiki:Bernarr_II_Justman)'s death.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)
With the support of [Arlan III Durrandon](wiki:Arlan_III_Durrandon), a [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King) from [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), Lord [Roderick Blackwood](wiki:Roderick_Blackwood) rebelled against [Humfrey I Teague](wiki:Humfrey_I_Teague), [King of the Rivers and the Hills](wiki:King_of_the_Rivers_and_the_Hills). Roderick was killed in the [Battle of Six Kings](wiki:Battle_of_Six_Kings), ending Arlan's attempt to restore the riverlands to the Blackwoods. After the river lords rejected the possibility of being ruled by Roderick's daughter, [Shiera Blackwood](wiki:Shiera_Blackwood), Arlan added the riverlands to the realm of the Storm Kings.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)
When the [Kings of the Iron Islands](wiki:Kings_of_the_Iron_Islands) from [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) invaded the riverlands three centuries later, Lady [Agnes Blackwood](wiki:Agnes_Blackwood) led the rivermen's resistance. She was betrayed by Lord [Lothar Bracken](wiki:Lothar_Bracken), however, and then slain by King [Harwyn Hoare](wiki:Harwyn_Hoare). The [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) then conquered the riverlands from the Storm King, [Arrec Durrandon](wiki:Arrec_Durrandon).(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

### Aegon's Conquest[ ]

House Blackwood aided King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) during [the Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest) and rebelled against Harwyn's grandson, [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare) of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers).(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) The Blackwoods and Brackens had fought a private war a decade before the rebellion, however, and Harren had dealt with them harshly. Instead of the weakened Blackwoods or Brackens, Aegon instead granted the new title of [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident) to Lord [Edmyn Tully](wiki:Edmyn_Tully) of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)

After the civil war ended, Black Aly married Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) in exchange for him confirming King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen)'s pardon of Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon).(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kingss:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) The [Tullys](wiki:House_Tully) and Blackwoods in particular were strengthened by [northmen](wiki:Northmen) who married widows at so-called [Widow Fairs](wiki:Widow_Fairs) and remained in the riverlands after the war.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

### Great Bastards[ ]

Lady [Melissa Blackwood](wiki:Melissa_Blackwood) was the sixth mistress of King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) and the mother of three [Great Bastards](wiki:Great_Bastards), [Mya](wiki:Mya_Rivers), [Gwenys](wiki:Gwenys_Rivers), and [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), the last of whom became known as Lord Bloodraven.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Melissa was well liked at court, so the Blackwoods did not greatly suffer when Aegon dismissed her.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)
Ser [Otho Bracken](wiki:Otho_Bracken), the Brute of Bracken, slew Lord [Quentyn Blackwood](wiki:Quentyn_Blackwood) in a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) three years before the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow), reinvigorating the old feud.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) [Bennifer](wiki:Bennifer_Blackwood), [Robert](wiki:Robert_Blackwood_(209)), [Roland](wiki:Roland_Blackwood), and [Roger Blackwood](wiki:Roger_Blackwood) participated at Ashford. Ser [Steffon Fossoway](wiki:Steffon_Fossoway) considered convincing Otho or the Blackwoods to fight for Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) in a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven), but decided it would be impossible to make them fight on the same side.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)
When [Lord Bracken](wiki:Lord_Bracken_(father_of_Otho)) lay dying, Septon [Sefton](wiki:Sefton_Staunton) explained that Otho would inherit and the Blackwoods would not suffer him, meaning war. The then-[Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Brynden Rivers, was half-Blackwood, so he was unlikely to stop it.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) When Bloodraven marched on the [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) to quash the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion), among the river lords who came in support was Lord Blackwood.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

### Later History[ ]

Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey) boasted that in the past members of important families married [Osgreys](wiki:House_Osgrey), citing Blackwoods as one of the examples.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) The queen consort of King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) was [Betha Blackwood](wiki:Betha_Blackwood),(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) and [Alyssa Blackwood](wiki:Alyssa_Blackwood) was the fourth wife of [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), [Lord of the Crossing](wiki:Lord_of_the_Crossing).
Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), decided to grant a mill to the Blackwoods to resolve a border dispute, but King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) instead decided to give it to the Brackens.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
Lords Blackwood and Bracken came to Riverrun when [Lysa Tully](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) was young to put their feud before Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully)'s judgment.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

When war breaks out after the death of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) defeats the river lords and captures Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) in a [battle under the walls of Riverrun](wiki:Battle_under_the_walls_of_Riverrun). Lord [Tytos Blackwood](wiki:Tytos_Blackwood) leads the survivors inside [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), however, and defends the castle during Jaime's siege. With Tytos absent, Lord Blackwood's seat of [Raventree Hall](wiki:Raventree_Hall) surrenders to the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Tytos is named among the nobles whom the new king, [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), insists must swear their loyalty or be declared traitors.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)
When [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) attacks the Lannister camps outside Riverrun, Tytos leads a sortie from Riverrun and frees Edmure. Lord Blackwood and his feathered cloak are seen by [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) in the [godswood](wiki:Godswood) of Riverrun, praying with her son to the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) after their victory in the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps). At the war council in Riverrun, Tytos urges to continue fighting the Lannisters and march against Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal). He joins the other river lords in declaring Robb to be [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North).(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

Tytos's second-eldest son, [Lucas Blackwood](wiki:Lucas_Blackwood), is one of the highborn companions of Catelyn in the delegation to King [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon)'s camp.(wiki:House_Blackwood.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)
