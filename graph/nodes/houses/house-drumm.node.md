## Origins

Some Drumms were [rock kings](wiki:Rock_king) of [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk) in antiquity. For instance, the rock king [Regnar Drumm](wiki:Regnar_Drumm), called Raven-feeder, was chosen [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands) by [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) after [Erich I Greyiron](wiki:Erich_I_Greyiron) renounced his crown.(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
The Drumms later joined with the [Greyjoys](wiki:House_Greyjoy), [Hoares](wiki:House_Hoare), [Orkwoods](wiki:House_Orkwood), and [Andals](wiki:Andals) to bring down the hereditary [House Greyiron](wiki:House_Greyiron).(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
Glorious Drumms of old include [Dale the Dread](wiki:Dale_Drumm) and [Roryn the Reaver](wiki:Roryn_Drumm).(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) [Dagon Drumm](wiki:Dagon_Drumm) was allegedly a [necromancer](wiki:Necromancer),(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) while [Gormond the Oldfather](wiki:Gormond_Drumm) is claimed to have fathered a hundred sons.(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) [Hilmar the Cunning](wiki:Hilmar_Drumm) is said to have taken [Red Rain](wiki:Red_Rain), a [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword, from a [knight](wiki:Knight) with his wits and a wooden cudgel.(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
Lord Drumm of Old Wyk was put to the sword by [westermen](wiki:Westerlands) during Lady [Johanna Lannister](wiki:Johanna_Lannister)'s invasion of the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC).(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

When [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) arrives at [Pyke](wiki:Pyke), [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) tells him that [Dagmer Cleftjaw](wiki:Dagmer) has gone to [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk) to summon the [Stonehouses](wiki:House_Stonehouse) and the Drumms.(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) The houses of Old Wyk agree to support [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

After Balon falls to his death at Pyke, Aeron calls for a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) on Old Wyk to choose Balon's successor as [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Lord [Dunstan Drumm](wiki:Dunstan_Drumm)'s ship, [Thunderer](wiki:Thunderer), is spotted by [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) on Old Wyk's shores.(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
Wielding [Red Rain](wiki:Red_Rain), Dunstan claims the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair) for himself at the kingsmoot, his champions being his sons, [Denys](wiki:Denys_Drumm) and [Donnel Drumm](wiki:Donnel_Drumm), and [Andrik the Unsmiling](wiki:Andrik). Aeron thinks his champions are notable and Dunstan starts speaking well. However, the fact that Dunstan talks too much of his family's past glories, and that the contents of his chests only contain bronze, diminish his support. [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) is instead chosen king.(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)
After the [taking of the Shields](wiki:Taking_of_the_Shields), Victarion sees Lords [Rodrik Harlaw](wiki:Rodrik_Harlaw), [Gorold Goodbrother](wiki:Gorold_Goodbrother), and Dunstan Drumm talking in the yard of [Oakenshield](wiki:Oakenshield). Euron names Andrik Lord of [Southshield](wiki:Southshield), a move understood by Victarion as a strategy of depriving The Drumm of his strong right hand.(wiki:House_Drumm.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
