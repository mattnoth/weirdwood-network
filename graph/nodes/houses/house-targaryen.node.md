## Origins

A complete history of House Targaryen is planned for *[Fire and Blood](wiki:Fire_and_Blood)*,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-44) to be published several years after the completion of *[A Song of Ice and Fire](wiki:A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire)*.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-45)

### Origins and relocation

House Targaryen was one of the forty ancient noble houses known as [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) who ruled the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold), a great empire spanning much of [Essos](wiki:Essos). The Targaryens were not one of the most powerful houses, however. The family resided in Valyria until [Daenys Targaryen](wiki:Daenys_Targaryen), also known as Daenys the Dreamer, the daughter of Lord [Aenar Targaryen](wiki:Aenar_Targaryen), had visions of a cataclysm that would come over Valyria. Aenar sold his estates in Valyria and moved his wives, children, other relatives, slaves, wealth, and five dragons (including [Balerion](wiki:Balerion)) to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)), an island at the entrance of [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay) and the westernmost outpost of Valyrian influence, where he took possession of a [castle of the same name](wiki:Dragonstone), in [114 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest). Twelve years later the [Doom](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria) descended on the city of [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), leading to the collapse of the Freehold. The Targaryens were the only dragonriders of Valyria to survive. In the century that followed, four of the five dragons of House Targaryen died, with only Balerion surviving. Two more dragons, [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) and [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes), were hatched from eggs on Dragonstone, however.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

During the century after the Doom, which became known as the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood) due to the violent struggles for power in Essos, [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) attempted to seize power over Valyria's colonies. Most of the other [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) rebelled against Volantis, and towards the end of the Century of Blood, the young Lord [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), rider of the dragon Balerion, was approached by [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) to form an alliance. Aegon, mounted on his dragon Balerion, flew to meet the [Prince of Pentos](wiki:Prince_of_Pentos), and then to [Lys](wiki:Lys), where he set the Volantene fleet aflame. With Volantis's campaign at an end, he returned to Dragonstone and focused his attention to the lands west, which had always been of interest to him.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)

### Aegon's Conquest

[Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), Lord of Dragonstone, aspired to unite the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) under one ruler. Slightly more than a hundred years after the Doom of Valyria, in [2 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest), he began his invasion, in which he was aided by his two sister-wives, [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen) and [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen). All three were dragonriders, and used their dragons, [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes), and [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) in battle. [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), which lasted for two years, began with [Aegon's Landing](wiki:Aegon's_Landing) at the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) with a small force, and ended when Aegon was anointed king by the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon's_Conquest)) in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).

[Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), which lasted for two years, began with [Aegon's Landing](wiki:Aegon's_Landing) at the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) with a small force. [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare), the [Kings of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:Kings_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), were extinguished in the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal), and Aegon granted the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) to [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully). [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon), the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_Kings), were defeated in the [Last Storm](wiki:Last_Storm) and replaced by [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon), whose founder [Orys](wiki:Orys_Baratheon) was Aegon's half brother. Visenya achieved the submission of [Crackclaw Point](wiki:Crackclaw_Point), but [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) refused to yield to Rhaenys. [House Gardener](wiki:House_Gardener), the [Kings of the Reach](wiki:Kings_of_the_Reach), were extinguished at the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire) and replaced by [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell), and [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister), the [Kings of the Rock](wiki:Kings_of_the_Rock), submitted the following day. Rather than face House Targaryen's dragons, [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark), the [Kings in the North](wiki:Kings_in_the_North), and [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn), the [Kings of Mountain and Vale](wiki:Kings_of_Mountain_and_Vale), also submitted to the invaders. The Conquest ended when Aegon was anointed king by the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon's_Conquest)) in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1)

From the swords of his enemies, Aegon created the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), and the dynasty he began would rule for nearly three hundred years.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) Aegon had constructed a wooden castle, the [Aegonfort](wiki:Aegonfort), at the place where he had first come to shore, and the town that grew around it became [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), the kingdom's new capital.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) Dragonstone remained in the possession of the Targaryens as well, and was one of Aegon's favorite places.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2) The region around King's Landing became known as the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands). During his [invasion of the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands), King Aegon allowed the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) to choose [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy) as their new rulers.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

### First Dornish War

In [4 AC](wiki:4_AC), Aegon's [Wars of Conquest](wiki:Wars_of_Conquest) continued with the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), during which he and Rhaenys attempted to bring the Dornish under Targaryen rule as well.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) This was a bloody war, during which Queen [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen) was killed with her dragon, [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes), at [Hellholt](wiki:Hellholt) in [10 AC](wiki:10_AC).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-1) An assassination attempt on Aegon that same year led to the creation of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), who would guard the kings of the realm for centuries to come.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-4)

The First Dornish War eventually ended in [13 AC](wiki:13_AC), after [Nymor Martell](wiki:Nymor_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), had sent his daughter [Deria](wiki:Deria_Martell) to King's Landing with a letter and the skull of Meraxes. Though the contents of the letter have never been revealed, Aegon agreed to end the war the next day.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-2)

### The Sons and Grandsons of the Dragon

[Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) died in [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), and was succeeded by his eldest son, [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen). Almost immediately, Aenys faced four rebellions which had grown out of dissent, dreams of the old days or of vengeance, and of the fact that Aenys and his younger brother, [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), had both been born from incestuous marriages. A bandit named [Harren the Red](wiki:Harren_the_Red), claiming to be a grandson of [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare), took [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) and killed Lord [Gargon Qoherys](wiki:Gargon_Qoherys). In the Vale, Lord [Ronnel Arryn](wiki:Ronnel_Arryn_(king)) and his family were taken hostage by his younger brother, [Jonos Arryn](wiki:Jonos_Arryn). On the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands), a man claimed to be the priest-king [Lodos the Twice-Drowned](wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned), and in Dorne, the [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Aenys_I)) raised thirty thousand followers against the Targaryens.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)

Lord [Goren Greyjoy](wiki:Goren_Greyjoy) killed the Lodos and sent his head to King Aenys. [Lord Royce](wiki:House_Royce) of [Runestone](wiki:Runestone) defeated the supporters of Jonos Arryn, though this caused the death of Lord Ronnel and his family when Jonos threw them out of the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door) in the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-1) Prince [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), who had claimed the dragon [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) after his father's death, eventually flew up to the Eyrie and had Jonos and his followers executed.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-2)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Sons_50-0) The Vulture King had made the mistake of splitting his large army, and was defeated during the [Vulture Hunt](wiki:Vulture_Hunt), in which Lord [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon) and several other [Marcher lords](wiki:Marcher_lords), including Lord [Dondarrion](wiki:House_Dondarrion), Lady [Caron](wiki:House_Caron), and Lord [Sam Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly_(lord)), marched against him.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-3)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Sons_50-1) And the last rebel, Harren the Red, was eventually caught by Aenys's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Alyn Stokeworth](wiki:Alyn_Stokeworth). Though Alyn was killed by Harren in the fighting that ensued, Harren himself was slain shortly after. As Alyn's replacement, King Aenys named Maegor as his Hand.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-4)

The next challenges Aenys faced came from the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). Even though the Targaryens had been followers of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) since before the [Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), the Targaryens held themselves apart from the laws of gods and men. By marrying both his sisters, Aegon I had continued both the Valyrian practice of incestuous [marriage](wiki:Marriage), which was considered a sin in the eyes of the Seven, as well as the more rarely practiced custom of [polygamy](wiki:Polygamy), even though the Faith of the Seven considered marriage only possible between one man and one wife.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-5) Even though Aegon I had always been careful regarding the Faith, the High Septons had never declared his marriages lawful, though they had not spoken up against Aegon's polygamous and incestuous marriages either.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Sons_50-2) However, when Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) had suggested, in [23 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), that her son Maegor should be married to Aenys's infant daughter, Princess [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), the High Septon had protested, and Maegor had been married to the High Septon's niece, [Ceryse Hightower](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower), instead.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-6) After sixteen years of marriage, however, Maegor remained childless. Following the birth of Aenys I's sixth child in [39 AC](wiki:39_AC),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Sons_50-3) Maegor took a second wife, [Alys Harroway](wiki:Alys_Harroway). This greatly angered the Faith, and Aenys gave his brother the choice between abandoning Alys, or going in exile. Maegor chose the latter option, after which he left for [Pentos](wiki:Pentos).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-7)

The Faith remained discontent. Though they had not objected to Aenys's own marriage in [22 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), to his cousin [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), new troubles arose in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC) when Aenys married his eldest daughter, Rhaena, to his eldest son and heir, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)). The uproar this marriage caused led to the start of the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-8) Aenys fled to Dragonstone, but fell sick and died in [42 AC](wiki:42_AC). Dowager Queen [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) immediately called Maegor back from his exile, allowing him to claim the throne. Maegor took King's Landing back from the Faith Militant, though he would spent his entire reign fighting against them. In addition, he fought against Aenys's heir, Aegon, and killed both him and his dragon [Quicksilver](wiki:Quicksilver) in [43 AC](wiki:43_AC).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1)

Maegor's reign was a cruel one. He finished the construction of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), which had begun in [35 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), during his reign,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-5)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-2) though he honored his moniker "the Cruel" when he killed all the construction workers after work was completed. He had come back from his exile with a third wife, [Tyanna](wiki:Tyanna), but was still without an heir towards the end of his reign. Denouncing the claims of Aenys's only surviving son, [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), Maegor married his three [Black Brides](wiki:Black_Brides), one of whom was Jaehaerys's sister, Rhaena, and named Rhaena's daughter [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) as his heir until he had a child of his own. While support for Jaehaerys grew, support for Maegor dwindled. When Maegor died mysteriously in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), Jaehaerys succeeded him.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-3)

### The Old King

A wise ruler known as the Conciliator and the Old King, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) ruled for fifty-five years, during which time he made peace with the Faith. The [Doctrine of Exceptionalism](wiki:Doctrine_of_Exceptionalism) was promulgated to have the Faith accept House Targaryen's incestuous marriages.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_for_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) The king had his councillors begin work on what would become the [Books of Law](wiki:Books_of_Law),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-2) and he created or expanded several roads through the Seven Kingdoms, including the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad), the [roseroad](wiki:Roseroad), the [ocean road](wiki:Ocean_road), the [river road](wiki:River_road), and the [goldroad](wiki:Goldroad).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaeaherys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)

Jaehaerys faced a difficult decision in [92 AC](wiki:92_AC) following the death of his heir, Prince [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), when he had to name either Aemon's daughter, [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)), or Aemon's brother, [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), as his heir. Jaehaerys chose Baelon, but Baelon died in [101 AC](wiki:101_AC), which caused Jaehaerys to become the first king to call the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), where Baelon's son was chosen as Jaehaerys's heir. Jaehaerys died two years later, in [103 AC](wiki:103_AC), and was succeeded by his grandson, [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)

### Death of the Dragons

King [Viserys I's](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) reign had troubles of its own. By his first wife, Lady [Aemma Arryn](wiki:Aemma_Arryn), to whom he had been married since [93 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), he had one surviving daughter, [Rhaenyra](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen). Aemma died in [105 AC](wiki:105_AC) in childbed, as did her son, [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)), after which Viserys officially declared Rhaenyra to be his heir, having the lords of the realm swear fealty to her. Though Viserys remarried in [106 AC](wiki:106_AC), and had three sons, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), [Aemond](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), and [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)), and one daughter, [Helaena](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen), by his new wife, Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower), he insisted that Rhaenyra remained his heir. Rivalry between Rhaenyra and Alicent resulted in two fractions being formed at court, called the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) and the [greens](wiki:Greens). Alicent's three sons loathed Rhaenyra's three eldest sons, [Jacaerys](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon), [Lucerys](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon) and [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon), all three rumored to have been bastards fathered by Ser [Harwin Strong](wiki:Harwin_Strong), as they considered themselves to have been robbed of their birthright to the Iron Throne.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-3)

Both Rhaenyra and her uncle, Prince [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), the younger brother of Viserys I, had been married into [House Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon), and both had children by their respective spouses. When both Rhaenyra's husband [Laenor](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon) and Daemon's wife [Laena](wiki:Laena_Velaryon) died in [120 AC](wiki:120_AC), they married each other, and had three more children ([Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), and the stillborn [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon))). Meanwhile, King Viserys's son [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and daughter [Helaena](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen) had been married as well, with three children resulting from their marriage ([Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_II)), [Jaehaera](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen), and [Maelor](wiki:Maelor_Targaryen)).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-4)

When Viserys died in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), his widow, Dowager Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower), and the [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) of [his Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole), defied Viserys's last will, and crowned Viserys's son [Aegon II](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) while Rhaenyra, unaware of her father's death, resided on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). Unwilling to submit to her brother, Rhaenyra declared war, and the resulting conflict over the succession, called the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) ([129](wiki:129_AC)–[131 AC](wiki:131_AC)), divided the kingdom. During this bloody war, thousands died, as did most of the Targaryen [dragons](wiki:Dragon). Rhaenyra herself was devoured by [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre), Aegon II's dragon, in late [130 AC](wiki:130_AC) while her son [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) looked on.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0) Despite her death and Aegon the Younger's capture, the war raged on for another half year.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) Aegon II was forced into betrothing his daughter and only remaining child, [Jaehaera](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen), to Rhaenyra's eldest surviving son, [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), and name them his heirs,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-57)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-58) though he planned to remarry.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-1) The was lasted until mid-[131 AC](wiki:131_AC), when Aegon II was poisoned by his own councilors.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-2)

With the war at an end, the eleven-year old Aegon the Younger ascended the throne as [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). Until Aegon reached the [age of sixteen](wiki:Age_of_majority), the realm was ruled by a [regency](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III). Aegon's younger brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), formerly thought to have died during the Dance, returned to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), married to Lady [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare). Viserys served as his brother's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) towards the end of his reign. The Targaryen dragons had been greatly diminished in numbers because of the war, and when the [last dragon](wiki:Last_dragon) died in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Viserys convinced Aegon to sent for nine mages from [Essos](wiki:Essos), to allow them to use their arts to hatch a clutch of [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_egg). Sadly, their attempt failed.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-2)

### Conquest of Dorne

When [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) died in [157 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) from consumption, he was succeeded by his eldest son, [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen), born to him by his second wife, Queen [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon) (his first marriage having ended both quickly and childless with the death of Queen [Jaehaera](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC)).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-3) Though Daeron was only fourteen years of age, his uncle, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), decided not to insist on a regency, and continued to serve his new king as [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) Daeron I had great ambition, and wished to "complete the conquest" which his ancestor [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) had begun a century and a half before.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-1) During Aegon's Conquest, [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) had been the only kingdom to resist the Targaryen forces,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-7) and following the conclusion of the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War) in [13 AC](wiki:13_AC), a peace had been agreed upon by the Targaryens and Martells which had long lasted.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-3) Daeron I managed to convince his councilors of his plans, and managed to [conquer Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne) within a year. In [258 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne) and several other Dornish lords bent their knees at the [Submission of Sunspear](wiki:Submission_of_Sunspear). The hinterlands were pacified by [159 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and Daeron returned to King's Landing with fourteen highborn Dornish hostages, leaving Lord [Harlan Tyrell](wiki:Harlan_Tyrell) behind to keep the peace.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-2)

However, Daeron's conquest did not last. The [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) rebelled, and though Lord Tyrell attempted to fight back, traveling from holdfast to holdfast, the rebels always struck back.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-3) After he was murdered in his bed at [Sandstone](wiki:Sandstone), all of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) rose in open rebellion, and they managed to liberate themselves within a fortnight.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) Daeron returned to Dorne in [160 AC](wiki:160_AC), and when the Dornish requested to discuss peace terms the next year, they betrayed Daeron by attacking him and his retinue beneath the peace banner. One of Daeron's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) knights surrendered, while another, his cousin [Aemon the Dragonknight](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), was captured. Three other Kingsguard knights were slain, as was Daeron I himself.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-4) Though his reign had lasted only four years, it had been a bloody one. It is said that ten thousand men died conquering Dorne, and another forty or fifty thousand died trying to keep it.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-5)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) Only eighteen years old at the time of his death, Daeron I had never been married(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-62) and left no issue. He was succeeded by his younger brother, [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-6)

Though in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) Prince Viserys argued for the execution of the Dornish hostages, the new King Baelor I decided to free them instead. He walked barefoot from King's Landing to Sunspear, escorting the hostages home, and met with the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne). They forged a peace, and Baelor agreed to betroth his cousin, Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) (his uncle Viserys's grandson), to the eldest child of the Prince of Dorne, Princess [Myriah Martell](wiki:Myriah_Martell). During his journey back home, Baelor passed by [Wyl](wiki:Wyl_(castle)), where he freed his cousin [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), though he was bitten by venomous snakes in the process. When he finally returned to King's Landing, Baelor convinced the High Septon to dissolve his marriage to his sister, [Daena](wiki:Daena_Targaryen), whom he had wed in [160 AC](wiki:160_AC), as it had never been consummated. He then locked Daena and her sisters [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_III)) and [Elaena](wiki:Elaena_Targaryen) in the [Maidenvault](wiki:Maidenvault), to "preserve their innocence". Baelor I was focused mostly on the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), and even took a [septon](wiki:Septon)'s vows, ensuring he would never father heirs. His edicts became more focused on spiritual matters as his reign continued, causing unease among the highborn. Baelor began the construction of [a new sept](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), though it would not be completed until after his death. Baelor also began to fast more frequently and for longer periods of time towards the end of his reign. And when his sister and former wife [Daena](wiki:Daena_Targaryen), who had escaped the Maidenvault on several occasions during Baelor's reign, gave birth to a bastard son she named [Daemon Waters](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) in late [170 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Baelor began a forty-day fast (even though another lengthy fast several years before had nearly killed him). Baelor was found collapsed on the forty-first day, and died soon after, in [171 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0)

As the next male in line, Prince Viserys, who had served as Hand of the King for [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), [Daeron I](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen), and [Baelor I](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen), ascended the throne after Baelor's sisters were passed over.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0) Though he ruled for only a year, [Viserys II Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) made several reforms in the royal household, established a new royal mint, attempted to increase trade across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), and revised the [code of laws](wiki:Books_of_Law) established by Jaehaerys I years before.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-1)

### The Blackfyre Pretenders

Viserys II was succeeded by his eldest son, [Aegon IV](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), who was rumored to have had a hand in his father's death.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0) Aegon's reign was filled with corruption, and he gave and took from noble houses as he wished, whenever he wanted to.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-1) He had been married to his sister [Naerys](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen) in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-2) They differed greatly in personalities,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-3) and childbirth was hard on her. When their son [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) was born in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) warned that another childbirth could kill Naerys. However, Aegon took no heed, and impregnated Naerys three more times. Only one child, however, survived: Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)), born in [172 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Naerys died in childbirth several years later.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-2)

Aegon was very promiscuous, both before and during his reign. He had [bastard](wiki:Bastard) children by several women, though he only acknowledged those fathered upon his nine official mistresses, with one exception: his cousin Daena's son, [Daemon Waters](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre). In [182 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aegon openly recognized the boy as his own son(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-greatbastards_66-0) and gifted him the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-3) There were some who felt that the sword symbolized the monarchy,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-67) as the sword had been passed on from king to king since Aegon the Conqueror (with one exception(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Aenys_68-0)). Aegon's public gift of the sword to his bastard son was one of the seeds from which the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) would eventually grow. Following the acquisition of the sword, Daemon took the surname "Blackfyre" for himself.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-4)

Aegon IV's relationship with his trueborn heir was more troublesome. Once he was old enough to voice his own opinions, Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) and Aegon IV quarreled more often, and towards the end of Aegon's reign, Daeron became his chief obstacle. When Aegon attempted to invade [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) with wooden dragons and a fleet in [174 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the homeland of Daeron's wife, [Myriah Martell](wiki:Myriah_Martell), rumors were heard for the first time that Daeron had been fathered by Aegon's brother [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)) instead of Aegon. Though Aemon defeated Ser [Morgil Hastwyck](wiki:Morgil_Hastwyck) in a trial by combat when he accused Naerys of adultery, the rumors remained, and it was believed that they had originated with King Aegon himself, though he denied it. Following the death of both Aemon and Naerys, Aegon began more openly to refer to Daeron's illegitimacy, though he never disowned him. Finally, on his death bed in [184 AC](wiki:184_AC), Aegon IV legitimized all of his [bastards](wiki:Bastards).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-5) Though his successor, his son [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), could not undo this, he made attempts to keep his half-siblings close.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)

Daeron II began his reign by negotiating with his brother-in-law, [Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell), who had [inherited the rule](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne) of Dorne. He arranged a marriage between Maron and his sister, [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)), which occurred in [187 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Following the wedding, Dorne officially joined the realm, and did fealty to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). However, while the Seven Kingdoms were now finally united, dissatisfaction grew with the concessions that Dorne had won, as well as the growing Dornish influence at Daeron's court. Daeron II's heir, Prince [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), favored his Dornish mother in his physical appearance, and more and more lords began to wish for "the old days", when the Dornish had been the enemy in battle, and not rivals for power at court.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2)

These lords looked at Daemon Blackfyre, and saw in him a warrior, tall and powerful, with the hallmarks of Old [Valyria](wiki:Valyria) (deep purple eyes, silver-gold hair down to his shoulders), who was described as having had an "almost inhumane beauty", being "half a god among mortal men".(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-greatbastards_66-1) Though it took many years, talk eventually turned into treason when Daemon planned to make his claim for the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). The [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) began in early [196 AC](wiki:196_AC) and lasted nigh on a year, ending late in the year with the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-4) Daemon and his two eldest sons, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Blackfyre) and [Aemon Blackfyre](wiki:Aemon_Blackfyre), were killed,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-5)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) whereas [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers), another one of Daeron II's half-brothers, fled into exile with Daemon's remaining children and the sword Blackfyre. For a while, Aegor resided in [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) with Daemon's family,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3) until he formed the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0)

### The Great Spring Sickness

The remainder of Daeron II's reign was peaceful. However, in its final year, [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), problems began when Daeron's heir and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Prince [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), died when defending a hedge knight, Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), in a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) at the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-6)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) It is believed that Baelor's youngest brother [Maekar](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), who had fought on the side of the two accusers, his own eldest two sons [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) and [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen), had been the one to fatally his Baelor's head.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-7)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) Following Baelor's cremation, Maekar sent Aerion into exile, while he allowed his youngest son, nicknamed "[Egg](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen)", to squire for Duncan, so he could be taught humility and honor.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)

Not long after the death of Baelor, the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness) began. Thousands died as a result, and among the victims were Daeron II and his two most immediate heirs, Baelor's two sons [Valarr](wiki:Valarr_Targaryen) and [Matarys](wiki:Matarys_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-8)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3) As such, Daeron II was succeeded by his second son, [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), whose reign began with a two-year drought.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-4) The Spring Sickness had hit the cities of the Seven Kingdoms most, especially King's Landing, and most of the hostages King Daeron II had once taken after the First Blackfyre Rebellion had died, leaving their relatives feeling secure enough about attempting a new rebellion.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-4) Lord [Gormon Peake](wiki:Gormon_Peake) convinced [Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre) to cross the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) to claim the throne.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-1) However, the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion) was far less successful than the first, as Daemon II was arrested at [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls) before he could even gather an army.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5) He was kept alive as a hostage at King's Landing, but died within years, allowing [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) to crown [Haegon I Blackfyre](wiki:Haegon_I_Blackfyre) the new [Blackfyre pretender](wiki:Blackfyre_Pretenders). Haegon attempted [his invasion](wiki:Third_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [219 AC](wiki:219_AC), when he crossed the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) with the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company), led by Aegor Rivers. Haegon was slain after he had surrendered his sword, while Aegor was arrested, and sentenced to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). He managed to escape, and returned to [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), where he crowned Haegon's son, [Daemon III Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_III_Blackfyre).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-2)

### Succession problems

In addition to the two Blackfyre Rebellions, Aerys's reign knew multiple other troubles, including the [plague](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness) and drought, dwindling trade, rising banditry, and the reaving of [Dagon Greyjoy](wiki:Dagon_Greyjoy).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-5)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-6)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-3) Additionally, Aerys had difficulties with his heirs. Though he had been married to Lady [Aelinor Penrose](wiki:Aelinor_Penrose) before his reign began, his marriage remained unconsummated. He additionally refused to take another to wife. As he had no issue of his own, he firstly acknowledged his younger brother [Rhaegel](wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen) as his heir. Following Rhaegel's death in [215 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Rhaegel's son [Aelor](wiki:Aelor_Targaryen) was named [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone). Aelor died in [217 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), after which Aerys I's youngest brother, [Maekar](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) was named heir.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-4)

Maekar succeeded Aerys I in [221 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and reigned for twelve years.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0) However, when Maekar himself died while leading an attack on [Starpike](wiki:Starpike) in the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-71) in [233 AC](wiki:233_AC), the succession once more became a problem. Maekar I's eldest son, Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), had predeceased his father, and left behind only one child: a simple-minded daughter, [Vaella](wiki:Vaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daeron)). Maekar's second son, [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen), had died in [232 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) when drinking a cup of wildfire. Aerion's only issue was an infant son named [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerion)), born the same year Aerion had died. Maekar's third son, [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), had been sent to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) at [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) by King Daeron II at a young age,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0) where he had completed his [maester](wiki:Maester)'s chain at the age of nineteen,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_aemon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-0) and his youngest son, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) (also known as "Egg"), was disliked by several lords, as he had often meddled in their affairs.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-1)

A [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) was called by [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), who had served as Maekar's Hand of the King. The council passed over [Daeron's](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) daughter and [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen)'s infant son, and because of the dislike some had for Maekar's youngest son, the crown was quietly offered to Aemon first. Following Aemon's refusal, they crowned Maekar I's youngest son as [Aegon V](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen). Aegon V became known as "The Unlikely", as he had been the fourth son of a fourth son.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-2)

### The Unlikely Reign

The reign of [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) began troubled. He felt forced to arrest [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), who had served both Maekar I and Aerys I as Hand, after Brynden, who had offered safe passage to [Aenys Blackfyre](wiki:Aenys_Blackfyre), the fifth son of the late [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), from Tyrosh to King's Landing, so Aenys could present his own claim during the Great Council, betrayed Aenys by arresting and executing him upon arrival. Rivers was sent to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) and joined the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). Aegon V's brother Aemon accompanied Rivers, intent on joining the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) as well so his claim to the throne could not be used against the king.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-0)

Aegon spend much of his reign in battle. When winter ended in [236 AC](wiki:236_AC), [Daemon III Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_III_Blackfyre) and [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) crossed the narrow sea to begin the [Fourth Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Fourth_Blackfyre_Rebellion). They were supported by few, however, and Daemon III was eventually slain by a knight of Aegon V's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1)

Further opposition came from the lords of Aegon V's realm, as many disagreed with his policies, or distrusted and hated him, as they felt that his wanderings with the [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight) [Duncan](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) had left him "half a peasant".(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-3) Aegon's reign had begun in the middle of a cruel six-year long winter, which had led to starvation and suffering in the north. Concerned for the welfare of the poor and weak, Aegon V made attempts to increase the flow of food to the north. However, some disagreed, as they felt Aegon did too much in this regard.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-2) Aegon further wanted to diminish the powers of the lords, and enacted numerous reforms while giving new rights and protections to the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk). For this, he faced fierce opposition and at times even open defiance. One lord even went as far as to denounce Aegon V as a "bloody-handed tyrant intent on depriving us of our gods-given rights and liberties."(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-3)

A major rebellion in [239 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) was caused by Aegon's eldest son. Aegon V had five children, [Duncan](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen), [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen), [Shaera](wiki:Shaera_Targaryen), [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_V)), and [Rhaelle](wiki:Rhaelle_Targaryen), and for the eldest four betrothals had been arranged to several great Houses ([House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon), [Tully](wiki:House_Tully), [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) and [Redwyne](wiki:House_Redwyne) respectively). When Duncan, in [239 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), broke his betrothal to a daughter of Lord [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon) to marry [Jenny of Oldstones](wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones), going as far as to renounce his rights to the throne for her, Lord Lyonel declared the independence of the [Stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) in response. Only after his defeat in a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) against the [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) of Aegon's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), and the promise of the hand of Aegon V's daughter Rhaelle in marriage to Lyonel's heir, [Ormund Baratheon](wiki:Ormund_Baratheon), did Lyonel join the King's peace again.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-4) Aegon's other children (except Rhaelle) all broke their own betrothals as well. While Jaehaerys and Shaera secretly married each other in [240 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Daeron chose to remain unwed.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-5)

Towards the end of his reign, Aegon V began to dream of dragons with whom he could bend the lords of Westeros to his will. As several Targaryen kings had tried before him, seems to have attempted to hatch dragon eggs at [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall), where he had gathered those closest to him to celebrate the upcoming birth of his first great-grandchild in [259 AC](wiki:259_AC). However, in a [great tragedy](wiki:Tragedy_of_Summerhall) occurred, causing the death of Aegon V, Prince Duncan, and many more. Though the survivors would not speak of what had happened, the histories of [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), note that the [last maester](wiki:Corso) to serve at the castle, spoke of seven eggs and [pyromancers](wiki:Pyromancers).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-6) As Summerhall was burning down, Princess [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen), the only daughter of Prince Jaehaerys and Princess Shaera, gave birth to a healthy son named [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)

### War of the Ninepenny Kings

Aegon V's second son [Jaehaerys II Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen) ascended the throne in [259 AC](wiki:259_AC) following the [Tragedy of Summerhall](wiki:Tragedy_of_Summerhall), and was immediately faced with a threat from the east when the [Band of Nine](wiki:Band_of_Nine) conquered first [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) and then the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-0) The Band of Nine had formed in the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands) in [258 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) from nine outlaws, exiles, pirates, and sellsword captains. They swore to aid one another in conquering a kingdom for each member. One of these members was [Maelys I Blackfyre](wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre), known as "the Monstrous", who held command over the Golden Company.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-7)

To face the "Ninepenny Kings", as Prince [Duncan Targaryen](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen) had named them before his death,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-8) King Jaehaerys II sent an army, including his own heir Prince [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) and his Hand, Lord [Ormund Baratheon](wiki:Ormund_Baratheon), which landed on the shores of the Stepstones in [260 AC](wiki:260_AC). For most of the year, the war continued, but ended when a young knight called [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) killed Maelys, thereby ending the male line of the Blackfyres after five generations.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-0)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-0)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-1) As the remainders of the Band of Nine had little interest in Westeros, this victory ended the war.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-2)

In total, Jaehaerys II only reigned for three years, and died after a short sickbed in [262 AC](wiki:262_AC). He was succeeded by his only son, [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-3)

### Mad King Aerys

While the reign of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) began with much promise, his would become the reign which ended the Targaryen dynasty. Upon ascending the throne, he replaced all of his father's elderly councilors with younger men, and appointed a friend from his youth, the twenty-year old [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), as his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). While Aerys had great plans for his realm, his attention span was short and most plans were quickly forgotten. While Tywin would be Aerys II's Hand for nineteen years, tensions between the two friends began early in Aerys's reign. Aerys grew increasingly jealous of Tywin and over time began to insult him, while the deaths of several of his children contributed further to his madness. When in [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), after three miscarriages, two stillbirths, and three deaths in the cradle, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) was born, Tywin hosted a [tournament in honor of Viserys's birth](wiki:Tournament_in_honor_of_Viserys's_birth) at [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), which Aerys II and Rhaegar attended.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-2) There, Tywin suggested his ten-year-old daughter [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) as a bride for Rhaegar, and her twin [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) as Rhaegar's [squire](wiki:Squire).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-3)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-0) Aerys refused him, stating he would not wed his son to the daughter of his servant.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-1)

Aerys's jealousy for Tywin increased further when he learned of the saying that Tywin was the true ruler of the kingdom, making his suspicious of his subjects at court. His jealousy and desire to show himself as a leader led to his rash action of accepting the invitation of Lord [Darklyn](wiki:House_Darklyn) to travel to [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), as to discuss Darklyn's petition. Aerys went to Duskendale with only a small escort, and was captured upon arrival. This revolt, known as the [Defiance of Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale), lasted for half a year, during which time Aerys II was kept in the dungeons, while Lord Tywin besieged the city. Though he was eventually freed, the last of Aerys's sanity had died during his captivity. He became increasingly suspicious everyone, including his own heir, [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), and refused to leave the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) for four years. Tensions between Aerys and Rhaegar grew, more so after Rhaegar and his new bride [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell) moved to his seat of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) in [280 AC](wiki:280_AC).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_80-0)

In [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), Aerys appointed Tywin's heir, Ser Jaime, to his Kingsguard, leaving Tywin with [a dwarf](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) for an heir. Tywin resigned as Hand and left King's Landing. Convinced that his son Rhaegar planned to gather as much lords as possible to depose him at the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal) held later that same year, Aerys personally attended the tourney. When the victorious Prince Rhaegar passed by his own wife, Elia, and named [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), the betrothed of his cousin [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), as his [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty), it caused fury and tensions among those present, and inflamed Aerys's council against Rhaegar further.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_80-1)

The next year, Prince Rhaegar left his wife and newborn son [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) and went on a journey. Ultimately, he ended up in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) where he disappeared with Lyanna Stark. Lyanna's brother [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark) went to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), threatening Rhaegar's life. Though the prince was not present, King Aerys had Brandon arrested and summoned his father, Lord [Rickard](wiki:Rickard_Stark), to court. Aerys had both Rickard and Brandon killed at their "trial", after which he demanded the heads of Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Lyanna's betrothed, and [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), Lyanna's younger brother, from the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). Their foster father, Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), refused Aerys's commands and raised his banners, thereby starting a [civil war](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-0)

### War of the Usurper

Aerys's acts triggered a chain of events that would become known as the War of the Usurper to Targaryen loyalists and [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) to those who fought on the rebel side. Houses [Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-0) [Stark](wiki:House_Stark),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-0) and [Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-1) and later on in the war also Houses [Tully](wiki:House_Tully)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_84-0) and [Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_85-0) united in rebellion against the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_80-2) Houses [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_86-0) and [Martell](wiki:House_Martell) remained loyal to Aerys,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_87-0) however, and [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) stayed neutral for most of the conflict.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-2) In the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), Houses [Darry](wiki:House_Darry), [Goodbrook](wiki:House_Goodbrook), [Mooton](wiki:House_Mooton), and [Ryger](wiki:House_Ryger) defied the Tullys and remained loyal to the Targaryens.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_88-0)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_89-0)

Towards the end of the war, Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) proclaimed his intent to claim the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-90) Robert had the better claim than [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) and [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_91-0) as his grandmother had been Princess [Rhaelle Targaryen](wiki:Rhaelle_Targaryen), the youngest daughter of King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-2) Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) eventually returned from the south and took command of the royal troops, leading them during the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), where he was killed by Robert.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_87-1)

Aerys sent his new heir, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), and the pregnant Queen [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) to Dragonstone, while keeping Rhaegar's wife and two children as hostages at King's Landing.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_87-2)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_end.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_92-0) Though he had ignored calls to arms from both the loyalists and the rebels, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) appeared with an army at the gates of King's Landing hours before the arrival of a rebel army. Feigning loyalty to House Targaryen, the Lannister forces began the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing) once allowed into the city,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-3)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_87-3) slaying Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)), and the infant Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_93-0)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_94-0) Aerys's only Kingsguard knight in the city, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), killed the king in the [throne room](wiki:Throne_room).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_end.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_92-1)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_87-4)

The war was at an end, and on the mainland the final opposition to the reign of Robert I Baratheon was resolved. On Dragonstone, however, the last Targaryens held their last stronghold, and Dowager Queen Rhaella crowned her son Viserys.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafrhaella_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_95-0) Nine months after her flight from King's Landing, howeer, Rhaella died giving birth to Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) in [284 AC](wiki:284_AC) during a great storm which destroyed the [Targaryen fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) protecting the island. With [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) approaching the island and the garrison willing to surrender, Ser [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry) spirited young Viserys and Daenerys to the [Free City](wiki:Free_City) of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), where they remained until Willem's death near [289 AC](wiki:289_AC).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Afterwards, Viserys and Daenerys traveled from city to city for years,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_96-0) forced to sell the last of their belongings, until they were taken in by [Magister](wiki:Magister) [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis) in [297 AC](wiki:297_AC).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

And so, following two hundred and eighty-three years of rule, the Targaryen dynasty ended in [283 AC](wiki:283_AC) with the death of Aerys II and the exile of his young son Viserys.

## Culture

### Traits

The phrase "blood of the dragon" refers to the [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) descent of the Targaryens, and more specifically to the fact that they were one of forty [dragonlord](wiki:Dragonlord) families of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

The Targaryens are renowned for their beauty.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) They usually have typical Valyrian features: pale skin, [silver](wiki:Silver), [platinum](wiki:Platinum), or [gold](wiki:Gold) hair and eyes in various [shades of purple](wiki:Purple_eyes) or light blue.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) A few Targaryens had darker hair, such as Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)), Prince [Baelor Breakspear](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), Prince [Valarr](wiki:Valarr_Targaryen), and Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)).

According to semi-canon sources, the Targaryens can tolerate more heat than most ordinary people, but they are not immune to fire.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-13)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-egg_14-0) The Targaryens long believed themselves immune to illness, but Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Jaehaerys_I)) then died of the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) and the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness) claimed the lives of King [Daeron II](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), Prince Valarr, and Prince [Matarys](wiki:Matarys_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Another trait typical to Targaryens is the ability to have [premonition-like dreams](wiki:Dragon_dreams).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) The trait has been in House Targaryen since before the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), with the dreams of [Daenys Targaryen](wiki:Daenys_Targaryen) compelling the family to relocate from Valyria to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) in [114 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) This trait was passed on to a branch of the family, [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre), as well.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

The Targaryens are seen as [prone to madness](wiki:Targaryen_madness), presumably caused by their [inbreeding](wiki:Incest).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-egg_14-1) According to King [Jaehaerys II Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen), "madness and greatness are two sides of the same coin. Every time a new Targaryen is born [...] the gods toss the coin in the air and the world holds its breath to see how it will land".(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### Customs

House Targaryen's words are "Fire and Blood."(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Their sigil is a three-headed [dragon](wiki:Dragon) breathing flames, red on black.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The three-headed dragon represents [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and his two sisters, [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen) and [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) used a golden dragon to rally his supporters during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Some younger sons of the house used variations of the standard sigil. ### Dragons

As one of the forty [dragonlord](wiki:Dragonlord) families of Valyria, the Targaryens were closer to [dragons](wiki:Dragon) than other men. [Aenar Targaryen](wiki:Aenar_Targaryen) brought five dragons with him when he moved from Valyria to Dragonstone, and [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and his sister-wives, [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen) and [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) rode them to battle during the [Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest) of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) in [2 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest) - [1 AC](wiki:1_AC).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) The Targaryens rode their dragons during the first century and a half following Aegon's Conquest. Deaths in battle, especially during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), and a growing infirmity in each following generation caused the family's stable to dwindle. The [last dragon](wiki:Last_dragon) was a deformed and sterile creature, who died young in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), during the reign of [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), the Dragonbane.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

The Targaryens housed their dragons in an immense domed structure in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit), which was constructed during the reign of [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) Not all dragons which hatched were ridden, however. On the island of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)), several dragons, both wild and previously ridden, resided at least during the reigns of King [Jaehaerys I](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), [Viserys I](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), and [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

The Targaryens often gifted a newborn child with a [dragon's egg](wiki:Dragon's_egg) in the cradle.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-2)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) This practice continued even after the death of the last dragon, with the last Targaryens confirmed to have been given an egg having been the sons of Prince [Maekar](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) during the reign of [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) Whether the tradition was continued beyond this point is unknown, but the Targaryens still possessed eggs as recently as the reign of [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), the last king of the Targaryen dynasty.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Several attempts have been made, since the death of the last dragon, to hatch these eggs by members of House Targaryen, including by [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) and his younger brother and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) [Baelor I](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) [Aegon V](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) and Aerys II.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), the [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen)'s surviving son is claiming legitimacy as "[Viserys III](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen)". He and his sister, [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), having wandered the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) for years seeking aid from various merchant princes to restore House Targaryen to the throne, have been taken in by [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis), a [magister](wiki:Magister) of [Pentos](wiki:Pentos).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) With Illyrio's help, Viserys betroths his thirteen-year-old sister Daenerys to [Drogo](wiki:Drogo),a *[khal](wiki:Khal)* of the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki), in exchange for the promise of warriors to reclaim his throne.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_97-0) Daenerys marries Drogo,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_97-1) and the two accompany the *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* in the journey to [Vaes Dothrak](wiki:Vaes_Dothrak).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_98-0) During this time, Daenerys begins to learn to stand up to her brother, while Viserys's behavior leads to him being mocked by the other Dothraki.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_99-0) Viserys is killed by Drogo after threatening the lives of Daenerys and her unborn child with a sword, despite weapons being forbidden in the sacred city.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_100-0)

King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) promises a lordship to whoever manages to kill Daenerys, her unborn child, or Viserys.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_101-0) After Daenerys escapes an assassination attempt by poison, Drogo vows to invade the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_102-0) When Drogo is injured in battle against another *khalasar*, Daenerys has one of her newly acquired slaves, [Mirri Maz Duur](wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur), see to his wounds,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_103-0) but Drogo's wound infects nonetheless.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_104-0) Daenerys attempts to save his life by having Mirri perform [blood magic](wiki:Blood_magic), but this causes disagreement among the warriors in Drogo's *khalasar*, and ensues in fighting. Daenerys goes into labor(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_104-1) and eventually wakes to find her child was stillborn and deformed, Drogo's *khalasar* broken up, and Drogo himself an empty shell of the man he once was.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_104-2) Daenerys has Mirri arrested and smothers Drogo with a pillow to end his suffering.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_104-3) She places her three [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_egg) on Drogo's funeral pyre, as well as Mirri. When the fire is burning, Daenerys enters the pyre herself, and when the flames have died out the next morning is found unharmed, with three newly-hatched [dragons](wiki:Dragons) in her arms. They are the first dragons in the [known world](wiki:Known_world) in a century and a half.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_105-0)

At the [Wall](wiki:Wall) in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), the hundred-year-old Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), brother to the late King [Aegon V](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), still resides at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black). Aemon intervenes when a new recruit, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), asks for his help with a fellow recruit, [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly), naming Sam his personal servant to prevent him from remaining in training, which would have meant his death.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_106-0)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_107-0) Aemon later advises Jon regarding the conflict of loyalty between family and duty to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), revealing he had felt such conflicting feelings himself trice, most recently when he was informed of the [deaths of the last of his house](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_108-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Daenerys has named her three dragons [Drogon](wiki:Drogon), [Rhaegal](wiki:Rhaegal), and [Viserion](wiki:Viserion), after her late husband and brothers.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_109-0) She leads the few remaining people of her *khalasar* through the [red waste](wiki:Red_waste),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_109-1) and is given entrance to [Qarth](wiki:Qarth).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_110-0) There, she resides with merchant prince [Xaro Xhoan Daxos](wiki:Xaro_Xhoan_Daxos).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_110-1) Once in Qarth, Daenerys learns of the death of King Robert I Baratheon in Westeros and the [war](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) that erupted as a result.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_110-2)

After having been denied help by the [Pureborn](wiki:Pureborn) of Qarth, Daenerys decides to request aid from the [warlocks](wiki:Warlocks).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_111-0) She drinks [shade-of-the-evening](wiki:Shade-of-the-evening) and enters the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying), where she meets the [Undying Ones](wiki:Undying_Ones) after seeing many visions. The Undying Ones give her cryptic information before attempting to consume her. Drogon rescues her, setting the House of the Undying on fire.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_112-0) Unwelcome in Qarth following the destruction of the home of the warlocks, Daenerys begins to seek passage on a ship. She is rescued from an assassination attempt by a [Sorrowful Man](wiki:Sorrowful_Man) by [Arstan Whitebeard](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) and [Belwas](wiki:Belwas), who inform her that they have been sent by Magister Illyrio with three ships to escort Daenerys and her people to Pentos.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

### A Storm of Swords

On route to Pentos, Daenerys is convinced by [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) to change course towards [Astapor](wiki:Astapor), one of the three [Slaver Cities](wiki:Slaver_Cities) in [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_96-1) There, she acquires eight thousand six hundred [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied) and five thousand boys in training.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_110-3) She uses her new army to conquer and sack [Astapor](wiki:Fall_of_Astapor),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_113-0) beginning her [campaign to abolish slavery in Slaver's Bay](wiki:Targaryen_campaign_in_Slaver's_Bay), after which she frees the slaves, inviting them to join her.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_114-0)

Daenerys marches on [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai), where she faces the slave soldiers of Yunkai, and two sellsword companies, the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons) and the [Stormcrows](wiki:Stormcrows). Daenerys [defeats](wiki:Battle_near_Yunkai) the Yunkish soldiers, while the Stormcrows join her cause.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_114-1) With their leader gone, the Second Sons also join Daenerys and elect a new commander.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_115-0) Daenerys frees the slaves of Yunkai and marches on [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), the last of the slaver cities.

Daenerys [conquers Meereen](wiki:Siege_of_Meereen) with the help of the city's slaves, whom she frees. After she learns how the council she had created to rule Astapor had been replaced by the butcher [Cleon](wiki:Cleon), who now rules Astapor as its king and has enslaved all the nobility in the city, Daenerys decides to prevent Meereen from the same fate. Believing she needs to learn how to rule before conquering Westeros, Daenerys decides to remain in Meereen and rule as its queen.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) Having discovered that Jorah Mormont had been spying on her for months for the Iron Throne and that his information had led to the poisoning attempt, and that Arstan is actually Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), a former [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) knight who had accepted pardons from Robert Baratheon, Daenerys furiously gives them one chance to redeem themselves.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_115-1) While she is later able to forgive Barristan, and takes him into her service, she finds herself unable to forgive Jorah, and sends him into exile.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)

[Ben Plumm](wiki:Ben_Plumm), who is chosen as the leader of the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons) following the disappearance of [Mero](wiki:Mero) at the battle near Yunkai, claims to have Targaryen ancestry, as his ancestor [Ossifer Plumm](wiki:Ossifer_Plumm) was married to [Elaena Targaryen](wiki:Elaena_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_115-2)

At [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), Maester Aemon believes [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) when he claims he was ordered to join the [wildlings](wiki:Free_folk) by [Qhorin Halfhand](wiki:Qhorin_Halfhand).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_116-0) Following the death of [Donal Noye](wiki:Donal_Noye), Aemon tells Jon to take command of Castle Black during the [attack by wildlings](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_117-0) When Jon is arrested on suspicious of treason, only Aemon's intervention prevents Jon from being executed.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_118-0) When ordered before King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), Aemon requests to see Stannis's sword [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer), and orders [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) to describe the sword to him, as he cannot see for himself.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_119-0) Aemon is in charge of the [choosing](wiki:Choosing) of a new Lord Commander, but as a maester is not allowed to influence the choosing. Instead, he subtly suggests that Samwell could,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_119-1) leading to the election of Jon Snow as [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_120-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Lord Commander Jon Snow, fearing that the priestess [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) will wish to sacrifice Aemon to [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) for his king's blood, orders Aemon to travel to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), accompanied by [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly), singer [Dareon](wiki:Dareon), wildling [Gilly](wiki:Gilly), and [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder)'s [son](wiki:Aemon_Battleborn).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1) Aemon's health deteriorates during the first part of the journey,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-1) and becomes worse once they are in [Braavos](wiki:Braavos).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_121-0) After Dareon abandons them, Samwell manages to arrange passage to Oldtown on the *[Cinnamon Wind](wiki:Cinnamon_Wind)*.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_121-1) Though talk of dragons and Daenerys Targaryen temporarily increases Aemon's health, it worsens again during the journey, and he eventually dies at the age of a hundred and two off the coast of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-3) His body is preserved in a cask of rum, so he can be cremated once the ship makes port.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-4) Upon arrival in Oldtown, Samwell informs [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Marwyn](wiki:Marwyn) of Aemon's passing, and his desire to join Daenerys, causing Marwyn to travel to Slaver's Bay in Aemon's stead.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_122-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Daenerys rules Meereen, but meets with resistance from several parties. A resistance group of [Ghiscari](wiki:Ghiscar) noblemen, the [Sons of the Harpy](wiki:Sons_of_the_Harpy), engage in a shadow war against her,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_123-0) while Yunkai resumes its slavery and starts to make alliances against her. Daenerys forms a city watch,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_124-0) the [Brazen Beasts](wiki:Brazen_Beasts),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_125-0) to combat the Harpy's Sons, killings continue. Daenerys promises to web [Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq) if he can stop the murders for ninety days.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_126-0) Meanwhile, the Yunkai'i defeat the [Astapori](wiki:Astapor) in [battle](wiki:Battle_at_the_Horns_of_Hazzat)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_125-1) and [besiege and later sack](wiki:Siege_of_Astapor) the city,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_126-1)(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_127-0) leaving them free to march against Daenerys. The Yunkai'i besiege Meereen,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_128-0) and the Second Sons desert Daenerys(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_128-1) after she is unwilling to use her dragons in battle following the death of the young girl [Hazzea](wiki:Hazzea),(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_129-0) who was killed by Drogon.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_123-1) Daenerys had [Viserion](wiki:Viserion) and [Rhaegal](wiki:Rhaegal) locked up after learning about Hazzea, though [Drogon](wiki:Drogon) escaped capture and left Meereen.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_124-1)

Hizdahr aids Daenerys by negotiating a peace with Yunkai, in which their marriage plays a large role.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_130-0) Seven leaders of Yunkai come to Meereen to sign the peace and celebrate the opening of [Daznak's Pit](wiki:Daznak's_Pit), the largest of the fighting pits in Meereen.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_131-0) Daenerys is sickened by the battles, and after [a female warrior](wiki:Barsena) is slain by a [boar](wiki:Boar) decides to leave. At the same time, [Belwas](wiki:Belwas) falls ill due to eating poisoned locusts which had been meant for Daenerys. Just as Belwas falls to his feat, Drogon returns to the city and lands in the Pit. While the public panics and attempts to flee, Daenerys rushes to Drogon's side and flies away from Meereen on his back.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_132-0) Drogon flies her to the [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea), where the dragon makes a lair at "[Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(hill))". Daenerys eventually encounters the *khalasar* of Khal [Jhaqo](wiki:Jhaqo).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_133-0)

As Daenerys is still in the east, Prince Aegon proposes traveling west to start conquering Westeros without her,(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_139-0) a plan previously suggested to him by the exiled [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_138-2) reasoning that Daenerys will come west once she learns about his purpose.(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_139-1) The Golden Company agrees, and they travel to Westeros, where Aegon, after [the first few victories in the stormlands](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company), decides to personally lead an attack on [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:House_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_140-0)
