## Origins

An old house,(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-7) the Conningtons were a significantly wealthy House during the rule of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Ser [Alyn Connington](wiki:Alyn_Connington) was an exalted [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
During the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), Lord Connington was killed by Dornish assassins while hunting.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Rfabthe_reign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) The fourth son of the ruling Lord Connington joined Prince [Aegon the Uncrowned](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I))'s forces in his campaign against his uncle King [Maegor the Cruel](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) and fought for him in the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
Lord [Kyle Connington](wiki:Kyle_Connington) was a guest at the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) The young Lord [Roy Connington](wiki:Roy_Connington) became a companion of Princess [Saera Targaryen](wiki:Saera_Targaryen) during his stay at court. After being involved in a sexual scandal, Roy was condemned to a ten-year exile from [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) by Saera's father, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen). Roy died shortly before the end of his sentence, however.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
With the death of Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) and his heir being an [infant](wiki:Royce_Baratheon), the [Stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) became vulnerable to [Dornish](wiki:Dornish) incursions. Borros's widow, Lady [Elenda Baratheon](wiki:Elenda_Baratheon), married Ser [Steffon Connington](wiki:Steffon_Connington) hoping the fierce warrior would fight off the Dornishmen and bring stability to the region.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Steffon was slain by [Wyland Wyl](wiki:Wyland_Wyl), however.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### Recent History[ ]

Lord [Armond Connington](wiki:Armond_Connington) always desired land, and he once sought assistance from Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) regarding a dispute with Lord [Morrigen](wiki:House_Morrigen).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) The [White Book](wiki:White_Book) entry on [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) records how he defeated Armond's son and successor, Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), in a [tourney at Storm's End](wiki:Tourney_at_Storm's_End).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) [Ashara Dayne](wiki:Ashara_Dayne) danced with Jon at the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
Jon was later named [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) by the Mad King, [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen). The previous Hand, [Owen Merryweather](wiki:Owen_Merryweather), had been exiled for his failure to face [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), and Lord Connington was a noted warrior of proven loyalty, being one of Prince Rhaegar's best friends. During the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells) at [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept),(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Jon wounded Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) and killed Ser [Denys Arryn](wiki:Denys_Arryn), the Darling of the Vale, with an axe.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) After being defeated by the rebels at Stoney Sept, Jon was stripped of his lands and sent into exile in the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), where he is said to have drank himself to death.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)
Aerys allowed the Connington lands to remain with Jon's cousin, [Ronald Connington](wiki:Ronald_Connington), the castellan of [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost),(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) but Ronald joined the rebellion after the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) When [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) was crowned king after the death of Aerys, he allowed Ronald to keep Griffin's Roost, but the Conningtons lost their [lordship](wiki:Lord) and nine-tenths of their land was distributed among neighbors who were more fervent in their support of Robert.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) House Connington's status thus fell from being a full noble house to [landed knights](wiki:Landed_knight).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)
Through the initiative of Ronald, his son [Ronnet](wiki:Ronnet_Connington) was betrothed when he was sixteen to twelve-year-old [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_Tarth). Ronnet rejected her when he met her, however, as he did not find Brienne attractive.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) Ronnet now rules as the [Knight of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Knight_of_Griffin's_Roost).

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) sees Ser [Ronnet Connington](wiki:Ronnet_Connington), a helmetless red-bearded man with a [griffin](wiki:Griffin) on his shield, falling to [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_Tarth) during the [melee at Bitterbridge](wiki:Melee_at_Bitterbridge).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) After riding for [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), Ronnet turns to [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), and fights for him in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater). He is made prisoner and bends the knee to King [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Arstan Whitebeard](wiki:Arstan_Whitebeard) tells [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) that the young Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) was dear to Daenerys's late brother, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Brienne remembers the day she was presented to Ronnet Connington, who refused to marry her for being too ugly. He gave her a rose, saying that that was all she was going to get from him.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) In a dream, Brienne chops off Ronnet's hand when he offers her the rose.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
Red Ronnet, the [Knight of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Knight_of_Griffin's_Roost), is part of Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister)'s army assembled in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to resolve the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun). At [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), Jaime punches Ronnet with his golden hand for speaking of Brienne without due respect. Jaime sends Ronnet to deliver Ser [Wylis Manderly](wiki:Wylis_Manderly) to [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) so that he does not have to look at him anymore.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Despite rumors that he drank himself to death in [Essos](wiki:Essos), [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) is revealed to be alive, having gone into hiding under the alias "Griff" with the help of [Varys](wiki:Varys). He has raised [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), the son of his friend Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), with the hope of one day placing him on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Jon and his companions are attacked by [stone men](wiki:Stone_men) in [Chroyane](wiki:Chroyane) while traveling via barge down the River [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne). Jon contracts [greyscale](wiki:Greyscale) during the ambush while saving [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
After the [landing of the Golden Company](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company) at [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath), Jon leads the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) to [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost) in the night. The [taking of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost) only takes a few minutes and Jon declares himself to again be the [Lord of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Lord_of_Griffin's_Roost). Although Red Ronnet is away serving under Jaime in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), Ronnet's bastard son [Ronald Storm](wiki:Ronald_Storm) and his siblings [Raymund](wiki:Raymund_Connington) and [Alynne Connington](wiki:Alynne_Connington) are kept as prisoners at Griffin's Roost.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
After returning to King's Landing from Maidenpool, Ronnet wants to prove his loyalty to King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) and vows to bring the head of his uncle Jon to Tommen's [small council](wiki:Small_council). Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), the regent for Tommen, keeps Ronnet under guard in King's Landing for the time being.(wiki:House_Connington.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
