## Origins

### Age of Heroes[ ]

The Farmans are an ancient line, dating back to the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age)(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) and the days of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) when they ruled as petty kings of [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle) and guarded the western coast of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) from [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) reavers with their longships.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
The Farmans became famous for hatred of the ironborn.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) During the era of the [driftwood kings](wiki:Driftwood_kings), Fair Isle fell to the domains of the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands). However, as the strength of the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) grew while the power of the driftwood kings dwindled, Fair Isle was the first to be liberated from ironborn occupation when [Gylbert Farman](wiki:Gylbert_Farman) incited a [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) uprising. King [Erich V Harlaw](wiki:Erich_V_Harlaw) later retook Fair Isle in his youth, but lost it in his old age.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdriftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
In the decades that followed the [arrival](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) of the [Andals](wiki:Andals) in the westerlands, the Farmans knelt to [Tommen I Lannister](wiki:Tommen_I_Lannister), [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock), when he raised a great fleet and married the daughter of the last Farman king. As [bannermen](wiki:Bannermen) of the Lannister kings, the Farmans helped shield the western coast of the [Kingdom of the Rock](wiki:Kingdom_of_the_Rock).(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

### Rhaena Targaryen[ ]

When Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) rebelled against his uncle [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), House Farman joined the prince against the king in the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye) in [43 AC](wiki:43_AC). In the ensuring battle, Lord Farman and his eldest son perished alongside Aegon the Uncrowned. Farman's second son [Marq](wiki:Marq_Farman) succeeded him as Lord of Fair Isle. The widowed [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) fled from Maegor to Fair Isle, where Lord Marq granted her the hospitality of [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle).(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) When Rhaena was summoned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) by Maegor the Cruel in [47 AC](wiki:47_AC), she went as she had grown fond of Lord Farman and even more than fond of his second son [Androw](wiki:Androw_Farman), and ignoring Maegor's summon would bring the king with his [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) down on Fair Isle.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
After Maegor's death and the coronation of King [Jaehaerys I](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), Rhaena returned to Fair Isle,(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) where she quietly [married](wiki:Wedding_of_Rhaena_Targaryen_and_Androw_Farman) Androw Farman. The marriage had been conducted without the Crown's leave, and angered many at court believing Rhaena had married below her station.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) While Rhaena insisted she married Androw as he was kind to her, Lord Farman's [maester](wiki:Maester) [Smike](wiki:Smike) suggested that Rhaena had fallen in love with Androw's sister, [Elissa](wiki:Elissa_Farman). The marriage caused discord on Fair Isle, with some Fair Islanders fearing her dragon [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre), while court life at Faircastle became centered around Rhaena and her companions, earning them the resentment of Lord Farman's heir, Ser [Franklyn](wiki:Franklyn_Farman). While Lord Marq was a happy host of Rhaena, he died choking on a fish bone in [50 AC](wiki:50_AC) and Franklyn succeeded him as Lord of Fair Isle. Franklyn was quick to dispel Rhaena and Androw from Fair Isle, and Elissa followed them despite his efforts to keep her at Faircastle.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)
Rhaena's party eventually settled on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), where Elissa became bored remaining on the island, longing to travel at sea again. With Rhaena's reluctant leave, Elissa set sail for [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), but not before stealing three dragon eggs.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Elissa would sell the eggs to the [Sealord of Braavos](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos) and use the money to build a ship to sail the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea).(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Rhaena was enraged at the betrayal, arguing with Androw when she accused him of aiding his sister. Though never passionate, Rhaena's marriage with Androw continued to decline, reaching a low in [54 AC](wiki:54_AC). He often found himself ignored and sidelined at court and by other nobles, with Rhaena even declaring "The wrong Farman ran away." Furious at his own treatment, Androw poisoned Rhaena's companions with [tears of Lys](wiki:Tears_of_Lys). When confronted by Rhaena, he committed suicide by leaping from a window.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) When her daughter [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) went missing, Rhaena searched for her on Fair Isle, where Rhaena threatened Lord Franklyn after a heated conversation, appalling King Jaehaerys and his lords alike. In his next [progress](wiki:Progress), the king would omit visiting Fair Isle,(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) which he would not visit until [88 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) when Lord Franklyn had passed.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### Later Targaryen reign[ ]

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Lord [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy) landed longships on Fair Isle while [Lord Farman](wiki:Lord_Farman_(father_of_Lysa))'s pleas for help were left unanswered. Faircastle eventually fell to the ironborn and the Red Kraken claimed four of Lord Farman's daughters as [salt wives](wiki:Salt_wives), while giving the fifth "homely one" to his brother, [Veron](wiki:Veron_Greyjoy). Lord Farman and his sons were ransomed back to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_red_kraken.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Lord Farman later perished in the Dance, as did two of his daughters. After the war, Greyjoy was instructed by the Crown to return Fair Isle to the Farmans, though the Red Kraken ignored the edicts.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) was dispatched to recapture Fair Isle with the Velaryon fleet in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC). While Velaryon was making his way west, the Red Kraken was murdered by a girl named [Tess](wiki:Tess). Much of the Greyjoy fleet fled the island, and the Fair Isle smallfolk and remaining [knights](wiki:Knight) rebelled against the ironborn. Only Faircastle remained under ironborn control, until its garrison commanders quarreled over [Lysa Farman](wiki:Lysa_Farman), one of Dalton's salt wives. When Velaryon arrived, the ironborn had abandoned Fair Isle and it was restored to the Farmans. The Farmans were left to rebuild their fleets.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
Two years after [the Great Spring Sickness](wiki:The_Great_Spring_Sickness), Lord [Dagon Greyjoy](wiki:Dagon_Greyjoy) sacked Fair Isle and took a hundred women away along with half its wealth. Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) considered leaving the service of Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey) and pledging his sword to Lord Farman, who was reinforcing the defenses of his island.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### Recent history[ ]

Some years after Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) defeated the [Reyne-Tarbeck rebellion](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_rebellion), Lord Farman of Faircastle grew truculent. Tywin sent him an envoy, a musician who played "[The Rains of Castamere](wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere)" in his hall, and that was enough to make Lord Farman reconsider his position.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
[Jeyne Farman](wiki:Jeyne_Farman) was [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s [bedmaid](wiki:Bedmaid) at Casterly Rock during her youth, along with another bedmaid and friend, [Melara Hetherspoon](wiki:Melara_Hetherspoon). In [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the girls attended the [tourney](wiki:Tourney) hosted by Lord Tywin Lannister in [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) [in honor of Prince Viserys's birth](wiki:Tournament_in_honor_of_Viserys's_birth). Jeyne accompanied Cersei and Melara to visit [witch woman](wiki:Woods_witch) [Maggy the Frog](wiki:Maggy_the_Frog) on the tourney grounds to have their fortunes told. However, when the [maegi](wiki:Maegi) opened her yellow and crusted eyes, the sight of them caused Jeyne to flee in fear, and she never heard her future.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) has a nightmare about the night when she, [Jeyne Farman](wiki:Jeyne_Farman), and [Melara Hetherspoon](wiki:Melara_Hetherspoon) went together to visit [Maggy the Frog](wiki:Maggy_the_Frog) in [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport). Cersei thinks that perhaps Jeyne was the wisest of all of them when she fled without hearing her fortune, since afterwards she had a pleasant life, marrying one of her brother Lord [Sebaston](wiki:Sebaston_Farman)'s [bannermen](wiki:Bannermen) and whelping a dozen children.(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)(wiki:House_Farman.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
