## Origins

### Hundred Kingdoms[ ]

The Hunters of [Longbow Hall](wiki:Longbow_Hall) are an ancient house of [First Man](wiki:First_Man) origin.(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_the_eyrie.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
During the [Andal invasion](wiki:Andal_invasion), the Hunters allied with High King [Robar II Royce](wiki:Robar_II_Royce) against the [Andals](wiki:Andals). In a celebrated tale, Robar outshot Lord Hunter in an archery contest to gain the Hunters as allies, although some legends claim Robar cheated. Robar's forces were defeated in the [Battle of the Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_the_Seven_Stars), however, and the Hunters submitted to [Artys I Arryn](wiki:Artys_I_Arryn), the new [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale). Along with the other surviving houses of First Men origin, the Hunters had to deliver up gold, land, and hostages to their conquerors.(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
[Teora Hunter](wiki:Teora_Hunter), the daughter of a Lord of Longbow Hall, married Artys's grandson, King [Roland I Arryn](wiki:Roland_I_Arryn). Queen Teora was responsible for inspiring Roland I to build the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), the seat of [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn).(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_the_eyrie.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

### Targaryen dynasty[ ]

Lord [Alec Hunter](wiki:Alec_Hunter) attended the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) between Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) and Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) in [49 AC](wiki:49_AC), using it as an opportunity to meet King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), whom Alec found to be stern.(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Jaehaerys I later visited the Hunters at Longbow Hall in [52 AC](wiki:52_AC).(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
When [a war of succession](wiki:Arryn_Succession_Conflict_(134_AC)) ensued in the Vale upon the death of Lady [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn) in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC), House Hunter supported her chosen heir, Ser [Joffrey Arryn](wiki:Joffrey_Arryn).(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Old Lord [Eon Hunter](wiki:Eon_Hunter) is seen by [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), staying there as one of the many suitors of the recently-widowed Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn).(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Despite his age, Lord Hunter offers himself as Lysa's champion in Tyrion's [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat).(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
[Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark)'s memories of the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal) include old Lord Hunter jesting with [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) and [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Lord Eon dies suddenly, and his two younger sons, [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_Hunter) and [Harlan](wiki:Harlan_Hunter), accuse the elder brother and new Lord Hunter, [Gilwood](wiki:Gilwood_Hunter), of having murdered him.(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Eon reigned for nearly sixty years before his abrupt death.(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Lord Gilwood is one of the six [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant);(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) he is the last to arrive at the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon) to block the food supplies.(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), the [Lord Protector of the Vale](wiki:Lord_Protector_of_the_Vale), considers that Gilwood is not a threat to him in the long term, since he predicts Gilwood will be soon murdered by one of his young brothers. According to him, Harlan was responsible for their father's death.(wiki:House_Hunter.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)
