## Origins

### Kingdom of the Reach[ ]

The Manderlys are an ancient line who once lived along the banks of the mighty river [Mander](wiki:Mander) in the [Kingdom of the Reach](wiki:Kingdom_of_the_Reach), and some claim the river was named after them.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rawoiafmander.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) A noble house of great lords descended from the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), they held the castle of [Dunstonbury](wiki:Dunstonbury) as their seat and had a fierce rivalry with [House Peake](wiki:House_Peake).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
During the reign of the [Gardeners](wiki:House_Gardener) in the Reach, King [Gwayne III Gardener](wiki:Gwayne_III_Gardener) persuaded Lords Manderly and Peake to accept his judgment on their quarrel and do fealty for their lands, without any bloodshed.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Like other houses of the Reach, the Manderlys presumably converted from the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) of the First Men to the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) after the Gardeners welcomed [Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) into their kingdom.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)
Near the end of the long reign of King [Garth X Gardener](wiki:Garth_X_Gardener), a problem arose with the succession as the elderly and senile Garth had sired no sons and only daughters, one of whom had married Lord Manderly, and another to Lord Peake. Both lords were determined that their own wife should succeed to the throne, and the rivalry between them was marked by betrayal, conspiracy and murder, and finally escalated into open war with other lords joining the cause on both sides. The [anarchy](wiki:Anarchy_in_the_Reach) that followed lasted almost a decade until Ser [Osmund Tyrell](wiki:Osmund_Tyrell), the [High Steward](wiki:High_Steward), made common cause with the other lords of the Reach and defeated the Peakes and Manderlys. Osmund then placed a distant cousin of the late Garth X on the throne as King [Mern VI Gardener](wiki:Mern_VI_Gardener).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

### Exile and White Harbor[ ]

At some point, House Manderly overreached itself, and was driven from the Reach by the [Gardeners](wiki:House_Gardener).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rawoiafwhite_harbor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) According to Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), the exile of House Manderly is credited to Lord [Lorimar Peake](wiki:Lorimar_Peake) upon the behest of King [Perceon III Gardener](wiki:Perceon_III_Gardener), who feared the Manderlys' growing influence and power in the Reach.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) This allowed House Peake to acquire the Manderlys' seat of [Dunstonbury](wiki:Dunstonbury).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)
Bringing their wealth with them, the Manderlys fled to the [north](wiki:North), where they were welcomed by the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) as their own bannermen.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) The Starks awarded the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den) to the Manderlys and tasked them with defending the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife) in return for swearing an oath that they would always be loyal subjects of House Stark. This history instilled the Manderlys with great loyalty to their new liege lords.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)
When exactly House Manderly came north is unknown. In [211 AC](wiki:211_AC), Lady [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber) dated the flight of the Manderlys as having occurred "a thousand years" ago.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Lord [Godric Borrell](wiki:Godric_Borrell) defines the time period to "no more than nine hundred years" before [300 AC](wiki:300_AC).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) However, both [Wylla Manderly](wiki:Wylla_Manderly), as well as Maester Yandel date the arrival of the Manderlys in the north back a bit further, "a thousand years before [the Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest)"(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) and "some thousand years before the Conquest",(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2) respectively. A semi-canon source lists "a thousand years before the Conquest" as well.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rawoiafwhite_harbor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) These accounts thus place the arrival of the Manderlys in the north between 1000 BC and 600 BC.
House Manderly built the city of [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) with the wealth they had brought from the Reach.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) They further developed it over the following centuries.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8) It is the smallest of the five cities of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), and is the main northern port for commerce and naval transport.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-3) Until the construction of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during the Conquest (by then still known as the [Aegonfort](wiki:Aegonfort)), White Harbor was also the newest city of the Seven Kingdoms.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-4) The [New Castle](wiki:New_Castle), built to replace the aging Wolf's Den, resembles their previous castle Dunstonbury in the Reach.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-5) The Manderlys are one of the few great houses of the north to follow the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) instead of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) and thus have a strong tradition of [knighthood](wiki:Knight).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### Targaryen Era[ ]

In the aftermath of [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), Ser [Warrick Manderly](wiki:Warrick_Manderly) and Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) suppressed the [Sistermen's Rebellion](wiki:Sistermen's_Rebellion). Lord [Steffon Sunderland](wiki:Steffon_Sunderland) sent a son to be fostered with the Manderlys.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
[Mara Manderly](wiki:Mara_Manderly) was a companion to Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) in [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Her father, Lord [Theomore Manderly](wiki:Theomore_Manderly), hosted Alysanne in [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) when the queen visited [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) as part of her [royal progress](wiki:Royal_progress), with Mara's sister, [Jessamyn](wiki:Jessamyn_Manderly), serving as her cupbearer.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Theomore was to marry Princess [Viserra Targaryen](wiki:Viserra_Targaryen) in [86 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) but the high-spirited girl died after falling from a horse while racing drunkenly through the streets of King's Landing.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) House Manderly supported Princess [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)) in the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) traveled to White Harbor and [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and convinced the north to join the [blacks](wiki:Blacks). After agreeing that his youngest daughter should wed Prince [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon) once fighting ended, Lord [Desmond Manderly](wiki:Desmond_Manderly) sent warriors led by his sons, Ser [Medrick](wiki:Medrick_Manderly) and Ser [Torrhen](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly), to support [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) The courtly brothers were well regarded in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), especially when compared to the northmen who served in the host of Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Torrhen served as one of the regents of King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) after the war, resigning in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC) after the deaths of his father and brother from [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) When Aegon came of age in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), he ended [the regency](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) and dismissed Lord Manderly, his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)
Lady [Jeyne Manderly](wiki:Jeyne_Manderly) wed [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark_(son_of_Cregan)), eldest son and supposed heir to Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) and gave him two daughters, [Serena](wiki:Serena_Stark) and [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark_(daughter_of_Rickon)).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_stark_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Lady [Myriame Manderly](wiki:Myriame_Manderly) married [Rodwell Stark](wiki:Rodwell_Stark) and became Lady of Winterfell upon her husband's accession.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_stark_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1)

### Recent History[ ]

Members of House Manderly were present at the [Tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal) of [281 AC](wiki:281_AC).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Citadel_28-0)
When Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) called the northern [banners](wiki:Bannerman) to join [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) against King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) led the Manderlys south. Wyman fought at the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), rewarding his retainer Ser [Bartimus](wiki:Bartimus) for saving his life by making him castellan of the Wolf's Den, now White Harbor's prison.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
Lady Manderly, the wife of Lord Wyman, died in around [291 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

After learning of the [assassination attempt](wiki:Catspaw) on his son [Bran's](wiki:Bran_Stark) life, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) instructs his wife [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) to have Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) strengthen [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)
After leaving the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), Catelyn meets the Manderlys at White Harbor. Because of Eddard's arrest in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), a Manderly army, led by Ser [Wylis](wiki:Wylis_Manderly) and Ser [Wendel](wiki:Wendel_Manderly), joins [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) at [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin) when marching south against the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister). Their force consists of near fifteen hundred men, including some twenty knights with their squires, 200 mounted lances, swordsmen and freeriders, and the remaining foot armed with spears, pikes and tridents. Wyman remains at White Harbor to watch over the city.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
When the northern army splits at the [Twins](wiki:Twins), Wendel accompanies Robb to free [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), and although he is already a mature man, he is counted among the thirty young sons of lords that form [Robb's personal guard](wiki:Robb's_personal_guard). Meanwhile, Wylis leads the White Harbor infantry with Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) on his march south, and is captured at the [battle on the Green Fork](wiki:Battle_on_the_Green_Fork).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

While at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) for the [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast), Lord Wyman meets with [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark), Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel), and Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin). He offers to [mint](wiki:Old_Mint) coins for Robb, informs them of White Harbor's defenses, and proposes to build the king a new warfleet of galleys, with Rodrik saying he will inform Robb of the proposal. Later during the feast, Rodrik commands [Hother](wiki:Hother_Umber) and [Mors Umber](wiki:Mors_Umber) to work with Wyman in building longships to combat [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1)
Lady [Donella Hornwood](wiki:Donella_Hornwood) (née Manderly) is widowed by the death of Lord [Halys Hornwood](wiki:Halys_Hornwood) on the Green Fork. During the harvest feast, Lord Wyman advances his claim for the [House Hornwood](wiki:House_Hornwood) lands, going on to offer to wed his cousin. However, Donella is forcefully wed when she returns to her lands and is locked away to die by [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), the Bastard of [Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton). In response, Wyman seizes [Hornwood](wiki:Hornwood) to prevent Ramsay from occupying it,(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) and Manderly knights attack Bolton men in the [Hornwood forests](wiki:Hornwood_(forest)). News reaches Winterfell via Rodrik, but he does not have the manpower to stop them.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)
In the south, Wendel accompanies Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) to negotiate with Kings [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) and [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), bearing the Stark banner at the parley before [Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)). Meanwhile, Wylis has been held prisoner at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), where he frequents the kitchens. Wylis is one of the northmen [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) is willing to trade for his cousins captured at the [battle in the Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Wylis is ultimately freed when Roose Bolton [captures Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal).
After [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) [seizes Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell), he receives reports that Lord Wyman sent a dozen barges up the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife) packed with knights and siege engines to help recapture the Starks' seat.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) During his parley with Rodrik outside the gates of Winterfell, Theon observes a line of mounted Manderly knights. Rodrik's forces are betrayed by [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) in the [battle at Winterfell](wiki:Battle_at_Winterfell), however.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

As Robb marches to the [Twins](wiki:Twins) to attend the wedding of [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully), Ser Wendel Manderly is placed in charge of protecting his baggage train. Ser Wylis commands Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton)'s rearguard as they march to the same destination. Wylis is captured for a second time when [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane)'s men [attack](wiki:Fighting_at_the_fords_of_the_Trident) the army as they cross the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford) of the [Trident](wiki:Trident), with Lord Bolton unable to prevent the Mountain from destroying his rearguard.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) Wylis is returned to captivity at Harrenhal.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) At the Twins, Wendel dies at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), with an arrow going into his open mouth.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)
Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) sends a raven to White Harbor in a plea for help from the Manderlys to defend [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) from the [wildlings](wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

According to Queen [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), the Manderlys are on the verge of allying themselves with the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton), with Lord Wyman agreeing to marry both of his granddaughters to members of [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) and open his port to Lannister ships. The [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) receives reports that Wyman executed Davos when he attempted to negotiate an alliance with Stannis; Cersei responds by allowing the release of Wyman's surviving son, Wylis Manderly. At Harrenhal, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) informs a grateful Wylis he will have an escort to [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) and be put on a ship home to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).
[Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) and her companions see the corpses of the perpetrators of the [raid on Saltpans](wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans) hanged from trees. Among the dead are men with tridents badges sewn on their breasts, presumably [Condon](wiki:House_Condon) or Manderly men-at-arms who became [broken men](wiki:Broken_men) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

When [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) arrives in White Harbor hoping to ally Lord Wyman to King [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), he sees that twenty-three war galleys have been built without the Lannisters' knowledge. White Harbor is awash with refugees, including those from the Hornwood lands.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) Despite the objections of his granddaughter, [Wylla](wiki:Wylla_Manderly), Wyman denounces Davos in front of three Frey envoys—[Jared](wiki:Jared_Frey), [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey), and [Symond](wiki:Symond_Frey)—indicating he is ready to make peace with the Iron Throne and pay allegiance to [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), who requires that Wyman give up his claim to the Hornwood lands.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9) Wyman fakes Davos's death, however, killing a criminal in his place and altering the body so it resembles the Onion Knight. After Wylis returns safely to White Harbor, Wyman has [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) release Davos from the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den). Lord Manderly sends Davos on a mission to find [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark), known to be alive thanks to [Wex Pyke](wiki:Wex_Pyke), in a secret bid to unite the northern lords in resistance to Houses Bolton and Frey.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
Keeping up his ruse of loyalty, Lord Wyman arrives at Winterfell with three hundred men(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) to answer Roose Bolton's call to arms, and to attend [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow)'s wedding. At the wedding, he provides some of the food, including [three pies](wiki:Frey_Pies/Theories) implied to be made from the Frey envoys who disappeared after leaving White Harbor.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)
While quartered in Winterfell, there is tension within the garrison between the Manderlys and Freys. When Wyman japes about the death of [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)), it boils over into bloodshed, with Wyman wounded and several men-at-arms dead on both sides. Lord Bolton, hoping to reduce tensions and hungry mouths within the castle, sends the White Harbor men to engage Stannis's army, which is slowly [approaching Winterfell](wiki:March_on_Winterfell).(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

### The Winds of Winter[ ]

Having escaped from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and been brought to the [crofters' village](wiki:Crofters'_village), [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) tells [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) that [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) has sent the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) and Manderlys to confront him. Stannis is angered at Wyman's support of the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton), but Theon does not believe Wyman would coordinate with the Freys against Stannis.(wiki:House_Manderly.cite_ref-TheonI_47-0)
