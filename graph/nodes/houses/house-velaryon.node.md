## Origins

### Origins

House Velaryon is an ancient and proud family, with the blood of [old Valyria](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) in in their veins.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) According to their family histories, the Velaryons have a storied Valyrian lineage and came to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) before [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), settling on the [island of Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) in the [Gullet](wiki:Gullet).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) The [castle of Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark_(castle)) housed their ancient high seat, the [Driftwood Throne](wiki:Driftwood_Throne), which according to legend had been given to them by the [Merling King](wiki:Merling_King_(god)) to conclude a pact.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Their close proximity to the Gullet enabled the Velaryons to fill their coffers from trade in the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) and [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay). They allied with House Targaryen of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) and [House Celtigar](wiki:House_Celtigar) of [Claw Isle](wiki:Claw_Isle), both houses of Valyrian descent; while the Velaryons and Celtigars dominated the middle reaches of the narrow sea with their ships, the Targaryens were [dragonriders](wiki:Dragonrider) who ruled the skies with their [dragons](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) The Velaryons were the oldest and closest allies of the Targaryens for centuries.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

### The Conquest

The close relations between Houses Velaryon and Targaryen resulted in multiple marriages between the families, both before and after the [Wars of Conquest](wiki:Wars_of_Conquest). From one such marriage was born [Valaena Velaryon](wiki:Valaena_Velaryon).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) She married [Aerion Targaryen](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen_(son_of_Daemion)), the [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), with whom she had three children: [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), Lord [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), and [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)

House Velaryon allied with the Targaryens at the start of [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), and the [Velaryon fleet](wiki:Velaryon_fleet) transported Aegon the Conqueror's men across [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay) for [Aegon's Landing](wiki:Aegon's_Landing).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4) Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon) was made [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships) by Aegon after the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) had been secured. Daemon died fighting for Aegon in the [battle in the waters off Gulltown](wiki:Battle_in_the_waters_off_Gulltown), however.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)

### Aegon I

For the first century after [the Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), the position of [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships) was seen as almost hereditary within House Velaryon since so many from the family were named to the office.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-5) The [Velaryon fleet](wiki:Velaryon_fleet) also came to make up the bulk of the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-6)

In [10 AC](wiki:10_AC) Ser [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon_(son_of_Daemon)) became the first [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) His elder brother, Lord [Aethan Velaryon](wiki:Aethan_Velaryon) became [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships) for King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Lord [Aethan](wiki:Aethan_Velaryon) thus succeeded his father as lord admiral, and in turn was succeeded by his own son, Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) Lord Aethan fought against [Sargoso Saan](wiki:Sargoso_Saan), a pirate-lord, in the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)

Aethan's daughter, [Alyssa](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), was married to Aegon's elder son, Prince [Aenys Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), in [22 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), with whom she had six children ([Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), and [Vaella](wiki:Vaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I))).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

### Aenys, Maegor, and Jaehaerys

[Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) became queen at the coronation of her husband, King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen). When Aenys died in [42 AC](wiki:42_AC), his younger brother claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) as King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) over Aenys's sons by Alyssa, who became Maegor's prisoner on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)). In [44 AC](wiki:44_AC) Alyssa fled in the confusion that followed the death of Dowager Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), and Alyssa's last surviving son by Aenys, [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), put forward his claim for the throne. Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)), Maegor's admiral of the fleet, who had previously supported Maegor, turned against the cruel king, and many of the [great houses](wiki:Great_houses) of Westeros joined with him.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

Alyssa's son Jaehaerys became king at the age of fourteen, and Alyssa ruled as his regent, aided by Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) After Jaehaerys reached the [age of majority](wiki:Age_of_majority), the regency ended, and Alyssa married Rogar,(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) giving birth to two more children: [Boremund](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon) and [Jocelyn Baratheon](wiki:Jocelyn_Baratheon).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) The Velaryons of the Old King's reign were a house even more influential than [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### The Sea Snake

After [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)) died, he was succeeded as [Lord of the Tides](wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides) by his grandson [Corlys](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), also known as the Sea Snake, who had risen to fame with his [great voyages](wiki:Great_voyages) across the seas of the [known world](wiki:Known_world). Corlys became the first man from Westeros to sail to [Yi Ti](wiki:Yi_Ti), returning rich with [spices](wiki:Spices), [silks](wiki:Silk), and other treasures. He accumulated so much wealth that House Velaryon became the richest house in the realm, and himself the wealthiest man in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), waxing even greater than the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) or [Hightowers](wiki:House_Hightower). Because their ancestral castle, [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark_(castle)), was damp and crowded, Corlys constructed a new seat for the Velaryons, [High Tide](wiki:High_Tide).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Alyssa's daughter, [Jocelyn Baratheon](wiki:Jocelyn_Baratheon), wed Alyssa's grandson, Prince [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), at the time the heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Their child, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)), was in turn wed to Lord Corlys, and gave birth to two children by him: [Laena](wiki:Laena_Velaryon) and [Laenor](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon), both of whom became [dragonriders](wiki:Dragonrider). During the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC), Laenor's claim to the throne was presented before the lords of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) who had gathered to [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal). Although primogeniture favored him, and his father's wealth, fame and reputation resulted in much support for Laenor, in the end he lost to Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen),(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) causing a rift between the Velaryons and the Iron Throne.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-7) After Queen [Aemma Arryn](wiki:Aemma_Arryn) died, King Viserys I's [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester), [Runciter](wiki:Runciter), suggested Corlys's twelve-year-old daughter Laena as a new bride, but House Velaryon was passed over once more. In response, Corlys and his family shunned Viserys's wedding to [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-8)

Corlys eventually befriended Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), with whom he [invaded the Stepstones](wiki:War_for_the_Stepstones) in [106 AC](wiki:106_AC). When Daemon's wife, Lady [Rhea Royce](wiki:Rhea_Royce), died in [115 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Corlys happily agreed to wed Laena to Daemon, following the death of Laena's betrothed. Laena's brother, Laenor, had married [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), the [Princess of Dragonstone](wiki:Princess_of_Dragonstone), the year before. Although that marriage resulted in three sons, it was rumored that all three boys were [bastards](wiki:Bastard) of Ser [Harwin Strong](wiki:Harwin_Strong), as they looked nothing like Rhaenyra and Laenor.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-9) Laena gave birth to twin girls fathered by Daemon, [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)) and [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen), and she died in childbirth several years later, in early [120 AC](wiki:120_AC). Laenor was killed shortly after by Ser [Qarl Correy](wiki:Qarl_Correy).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-10)

### The Dance of the Dragons

Corlys and his wife, [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)), were major supporters of [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), in which Rhaenyra's half-brother [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), despite Rhaenyra having been the proclaimed heir of the late King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen). Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) flew to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and negotiated the [Pact of Ice and Fire](wiki:Pact_of_Ice_and_Fire).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Prince [Lucerys Velaryon](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon) traveled as an envoy to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), where he was killed by Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen),(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) and Corlys grew furious with Rhaenyra when Rhaenys died at [Rook's Rest](wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest). Lord Velaryon was brought back into the fold by Rhaenyra naming him [Hand of the Queen](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

When Prince Jacaerys offered the people of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) to attempt and claim a [dragon](wiki:Dragon), to increase the number of dragonriders, [Addam of Hull](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) and his brother [Alyn](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) made their attempt during the [Sowing](wiki:Sowing). Addam succeeded in claiming the dragon [Seasmoke](wiki:Seasmoke), who had previously been ridden by Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon). According to their mother, [Marilda](wiki:Marilda), Laenor had fathered the two boys, which many found a dubious claim, as Laenor had preferred men. The court fool [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) suggests that Addam and Alyn had actually been fathered by Corlys, who had kept them far away from his fiery-tempered wife. With his wife now dead, and the paternity of his son Laenor's children in question, Corlys agreed to adopt both boys into House Velaryon, petitioning Rhaenyra to remove the taint of bastardy from them, enabling him to name them his heirs.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

Corlys used his fleets to blockade [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay). However, Jacaerys was killed and the blockade was broken in the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet). The Velaryons lost nearly a third of their ships and the [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy) sacked [Spicetown](wiki:Spicetown) and [High Tide](wiki:High_Tide), with Spicetown never rebuilt.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2)

When Rhaenyra became paranoid following [the betrayal of the new dragonriders](wiki:Two_Betrayers) later on in the war, she ordered Addam's arrest. Corlys objected, and when ignored, warned Addam, allowing him to flee [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Addam later fought at the [Second Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:Second_Battle_of_Tumbleton), proving his loyalty to Rhaenyra by defeating her foes at the cost of his own life. Meanwhile, Corlys was thrown in the dungeon, and so the Velaryon fleet abandoned Rhaenyra's cause.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Prince [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon) fell to his death during the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Rhaenyra fled the capital, but was fed to [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre) after the [fall of Dragonstone](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

Corlys was freed when Aegon II took King's Landing after the [Moon of the Three Kings](wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) In exchange for a pardon,(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) Corlys used his wealth and power to support Aegon II. However, he refused to accept the execution of Rhaenyra's surviving son, Prince [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), and insisted the boy should be betrothed to Aegon II's only remaining child, Princess [Jaehaera](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen), as a requirement for his support.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Corlys served on Aegon II's [small council](wiki:Small_council) until the king's death.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)

With Aegon the Younger by his side, Lord Velaryon surrendered King's Landing to the [Lads](wiki:Lads) when they arrived at the capital, and peace offers were sent throughout the realm.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) During the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf), however, Corlys was arrested on the charge of poisoning Aegon II by Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark). The Sea Snake's granddaughters and Lady [Alysanne Blackwood](wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood) convinced Cregan to free Corlys,(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) who then served the new king, [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), as [a regent](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) until his death in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

### The Oakenfist

Following the death of his first wife, [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen), King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) married Lady [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon), the daughter of [Daeron Velaryon](wiki:Daeron_Velaryon), a cousin to Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon). Daenaera gave birth to five children: [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen), [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen), [Daena](wiki:Daena_Targaryen), [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_III)), and [Elaena](wiki:Elaena_Targaryen). Although both of her sons would rule as kings, both died childless. Through her daughter Daena, however, Daenaera is an ancestor of [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

Alyn Velaryon, formerly Alyn of Hull, became of Lord of Driftmark upon the death of Corlys the Sea Snake. During the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), Alyn was the greatest rival to Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake), then [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Alyn was refused his grandfather's place as regent for the young king, and then was made to sail against the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones); [Lord Alyn's attack](wiki:Lord_Alyn's_attack) during the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War) earned him the name Oakenfist. Alyn was later dispatched to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) to defeat [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), the Red Kraken. Lord Unwin had hoped that Alyn would fail, but instead, this expedition became the first of Alyn's six [great voyages](wiki:Great_voyages).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) Alyn later played an important role in the return of Aegon III's brother, [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), having negotiated his release from captivity in [Lys](wiki:Lys).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2)

In [157 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Alyn Oakenfist took a decisive part in King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen)'s [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne) as [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) He commanded a fleet that broke the [Planky Town](wiki:Planky_Town), and swept halfway up the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood) while the main Dornish strength was engaged with Daeron in the [Prince's Pass](wiki:Prince's_Pass).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Alyn returned to subdue the Planky Town and the Greenblood in [160 AC](wiki:160_AC) after the [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) rebelled.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1)

Alyn had an unknown number of children with his wife, Lady [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen), the eldest daughter of Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) and Lady [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) Although he was married, he loved the Princess [Elaena Targaryen](wiki:Elaena_Targaryen),(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) and had two bastards by her, the twins [Jon](wiki:Jon_Waters) and [Jeyne Waters](wiki:Jeyne_Waters).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-32) Elaena hoped to marry Alyn, but he disappeared at sea.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1) Their son Jon earned knighthood, married, and had a trueborn son of his own who founded [House Longwaters](wiki:House_Longwaters).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1)

### Robert's Rebellion

[Lucerys Velaryon](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon_(master_of_ships)) served as master of ships for King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), being one of the king's bigger supporters against his son [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

After [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), the new king, [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), granted the castle [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) to his brother [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon). The Velaryons have been sworn to [House Baratheon of Dragonstone](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_Dragonstone) ever since.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

## Culture

### Traits

House Velaryon is of [Valyrian](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) descent, and its members often have Valyrian features, such as silver-gold or silver hair and [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Some Velaryons have blue(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) or grey-green(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) eyes.

### Customs

A traditional name of the Velaryons is Jacaerys.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

It is traditional for the sons of House Velaryon to be given a taste of a seafarer's life when young. The positions of [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships) and [lord admiral](wiki:Lord_admiral) are often filled by a member of House Velaryon, as the family provides most of the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Lord [Monford Velaryon](wiki:Monford_Velaryon) answers [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s summons when he crowns himself king and calls his banners. Stannis expects Monford will threaten to take his levies home unless they attack at once.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1) Monford's contribution to Stannis's [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) includes the large ships *[Pride of Driftmark](wiki:Pride_of_Driftmark)*, *[Bold Laughter](wiki:Bold_Laughter)*, *[Harridan](wiki:Harridan)*, and *[Seahorse](wiki:Seahorse)*.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) When Stannis makes public the accusations of [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) being born from incest, Monford states that only steel will decide the matter.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

When Stannis [besieges Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Lord Monford urges him to storm the castle walls as soon as possible.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1) Monford burns with his ship at the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) Before sinking, the *Pride of Driftmark* manages to take down two ships of the royal fleet. When leading his sortie, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) finds himself surrounded with Ser [Balon Swann](wiki:Balon_Swann) and Ser [Mandon Moore](wiki:Mandon_Moore) by a circle of Velaryon spearmen.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Racok61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) Monford's half-brother, [Aurane Waters](wiki:Aurane_Waters), the Bastard of Driftmark, is made prisoner during the battle and surrenders to King Joffrey.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Monford is succeeded by his six-year-old son, [Monterys](wiki:Monterys_Velaryon).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Despite Stannis's defeat at the Blackwater, the Velaryons are still counted among his supporters.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

The seahorse arms of House Velaryon are spotted by [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) during the [attack by Stannis](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall) on the [wildling](wiki:Wildling) camp north of the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

### A Feast for Crows

[Aurane Waters](wiki:Aurane_Waters) wins the favor of Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), who finds that Aurane reminds her of Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) due to his [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) appearance.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Regardless of his youth and Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister)'s objections, Cersei names Aurane as the new [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships). In that position, Aurane proposes the construction of [new dromonds](wiki:Cersei's_dromonds) and also suggests to crew them with thieves and poachers.(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

When Queen [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) is accused of adultery, Aurane suggests setting the new ships on the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) to impose order and be an unspoken threat against Margaery's father, Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) Once he receives notice of Cersei's imprisonment, however, Aurane flees to sea and disappears. Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) believes the Bastard of Driftmark has the idea to set himself up as a [pirate](wiki:Pirate) in the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones).(wiki:House_Velaryon.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-1)
