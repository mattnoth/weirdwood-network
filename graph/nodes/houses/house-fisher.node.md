## Origins

Some chronicles state the Fishers were the oldest dynasty of [First Men](wiki:First_Men) [river kings](wiki:River_king) or the second dynasty, while the [Annals of the Rivers](wiki:Annals_of_the_Rivers) from [Peasedale](wiki:Peasedale) suggest they were the third dynasty.(wiki:House_Fisher.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Petty kings from House Fisher claimed dominion over the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) during the days of the [Hundred Kingdoms](wiki:Hundred_Kingdoms).(wiki:House_Fisher.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
The Fishers were possibly destroyed in wars with the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_King) or the [ironmen](wiki:Ironmen).(wiki:House_Fisher.cite_ref-4) [House Mudd](wiki:House_Mudd) ruled the [Trident](wiki:Trident) at some point after the Fishers.(wiki:House_Fisher.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
After the Storm King [Arlan III Durrandon](wiki:Arlan_III_Durrandon) conquered the riverlands, various pretenders unsuccessfully claimed to be river kings. One such was Ser [Lymond Fisher](wiki:Lymond_Fisher), Knight of [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones), although his lineage with the ancient Fishers is unconfirmed.(wiki:House_Fisher.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
