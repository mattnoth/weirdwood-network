## Origins

### Origin

House Frey dates back six centuries, making it one of the younger noble houses in Westeros. The first Lord Frey was awarded lands and a noble status, and began the construction of a great bridge spanning the [Green Fork](wiki:Green_Fork) of the [Trident](wiki:Trident). Construction was finished under the rule of his grandson, who added wooden keeps on both sides of the river. The timber keeps were eventually replaced by stone keeps, which received the name "the Twins".(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Due to their strategic location, House Frey prospered by exacting tolls for passage across the river, greatly increasing their wealth. House Frey has grown into the most powerful bannermen of [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun),(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) and the Twins are among the strongest castles of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) However, some older houses look down upon the Freys as upstarts(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) and toll collectors.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

Warriors from House Frey tried and failed to conquer [Greywater Watch](wiki:Greywater_Watch) in the [Neck](wiki:Neck).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### Targaryen Era

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), House Frey aided [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and rebelled against [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare) of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), the [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers). After the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal), the Freys joined the other river lords in swearing fealty to [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully), the new [Lords Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lords_Paramount_of_the_Trident) for [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

In [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), Lord Frey supported [Aegon the Uncrowned](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) against his uncle, King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

A young [Forrest Frey](wiki:Forrest_Frey) was mocked as fool Frey for asking for the hand of Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), House Frey sided with the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) of Queen Rhaenyra against the [greens](wiki:Greens) of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen),(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) and Freys aided Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) during the [taking of Stone Hedge](wiki:Taking_of_Stone_Hedge).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Lord Forrest died fighting [westermen](wiki:Westerlands) in the [Battle by the Lakeshore](wiki:Battle_by_the_Lakeshore),(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) but his widow [Sabitha](wiki:Sabitha_Frey) continued House Frey's support of the blacks.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) After the Dance, six hundred Freys joined Ser [Robert Rowan](wiki:Robert_Rowan) when he marched to the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) in support of Ser [Joffrey Arryn](wiki:Joffrey_Arryn).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) recognized the Frey banner among the arms displayed at the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell) held a [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) to celebrate his marriage to his new lady wife who was [a member of House Frey](wiki:Lady_Frey_(wife_of_Ambrose_Butterwell)). [Lord Frey](wiki:Lord_Frey) attended the tourney, accompanied by his daughters, his four-year-old son (the current Lord [Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey)), his well-regarded brother Ser [Franklyn](wiki:Franklyn_Frey), and his nephew Ser [Addam](wiki:Addam_Frey).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) Lord Frey and his retinue abandoned Whitewalls before [Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre) could be crowned in the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3)

During the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal), the squire of a Frey knight accosted [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed). The Frey knight was later defeated by the [Knight of the Laughing Tree](wiki:Knight_of_the_Laughing_Tree) and told to teach his squire honor.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

During the long reign of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), the house exploded in size as he and his descendants sired many offspring.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) He offered [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully) his choice of three Frey girls in marriage.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) When Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) acquiesced to Walder's proposal to marry his second son, [Emmon](wiki:Emmon_Frey), to Tytos's daughter [Genna](wiki:Genna_Lannister), the ten-year old [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) spoke out against the match.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

During [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Lord Walder arrived at the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) after the outcome was already decided. Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) has since referred to his bannerman as the Late Lord Frey.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

### Customs

[Lord of the crossing](wiki:Lord_of_the_crossing) is a game played at the [Twins](wiki:Twins) by Freys.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Racok4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

The Freys do not like their northern neighbors, the [crannogmen](wiki:Crannogmen) of the [Neck](wiki:Neck) ruled by [House Reed](wiki:House_Reed) of [Greywater Watch](wiki:Greywater_Watch), calling them pejorative names like "frogeaters" or "mudmen".(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

The Freys are considered by some to be upstarts(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) and toll collectors.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Some in the service of House Frey wear blue steel ringmail and capes of silvery grey.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) and [Joyeuse Erenford](wiki:Joyeuse_Erenford) are [to wed](wiki:Wedding_of_Walder_Frey_and_Joyeuse_Erenford) on Walder's ninetieth [name day](wiki:Name_day).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)

A score of Frey men are at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn) when Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) seizes [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3) Rather than joining her to bring him to justice, the men report the event to Lord Frey.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

Six Freys attend the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing): Ser [Jared](wiki:Jared_Frey), Ser [Hosteen](wiki:Hosteen_Frey), Ser [Danwell](wiki:Danwell_Frey), Ser [Emmon](wiki:Emmon_Frey), Ser [Theo](wiki:Theo_Frey), and Ser [Perwyn](wiki:Perwyn_Frey), as well as one of Lord Walder's bastards, [Martyn Rivers](wiki:Martyn_Rivers).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

As he had done in [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Lord Walder delays his response to Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully)'s calling of banners, although he keeps near four thousand men at the [Twins](wiki:Twins) and defends their lands from [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) outriders commanded by Ser [Addam Marbrand](wiki:Addam_Marbrand).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-11) When [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) arrives at the Twins on his march south to free Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), he is received by Walder's heir, Ser [Stevron](wiki:Stevron_Frey). Walder negotiates with Robb's mother, Lady Catelyn, securing a marriage contract with Robb and linking the Freys with the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). Furthermore, [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) will wed [Elmar](wiki:Elmar_Frey) when the fighting is done, Catelyn will take two more ([Big](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)) and [Little Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett))) as wards, and Robb will take yet another ([Olyvar](wiki:Olyvar_Frey)) as his squire. In exchange the Freys lend their swords to his cause and grant the northern army safe passage.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-12)

After the death of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Walder and Stevron are named among the nobles the new king, [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), insists must do him homage or be named traitors to the realm.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

Robb's army split at the Twins, each force heading to deal with a different Lannister army; Freys ride with both forces. Robb acknowledges that the Freys under Stevron's command fought bravely at the [battle in the Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood); Perwyn fights there as part of [Robb's battle guard](wiki:Robb_Stark's_battle_guard). Ser [Cleos](wiki:Cleos_Frey) and [Tion](wiki:Tion_Frey) fight on the other side of that battle under their cousin, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), and are captured along with him. Their father, Emmon Frey, also fights on the Lannister side of the war. Other Freys, including Ser [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_Frey), Jared, Hosteen, Danwell, Elmar, and [Ronel Rivers](wiki:Ronel_Rivers) are part of Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton)'s army. Jared, Hosteen, Danwell, and Ronel are captured by Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s forces during the [battle on the Green Fork](wiki:Battle_on_the_Green_Fork). Stevron attends the war council at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) at which Robb Stark is declared [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)

Stevron dies after receiving a minor wound in the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross), making his son Ryman the heir to the Twins.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) In the [storming of the Crag](wiki:Storming_of_the_Crag), Black Walder scales the walls of the castle along with [Smalljon Umber](wiki:Smalljon_Umber).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Ser Perwyn accompanies Lady Catelyn on her voyage south to treat with kings [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) and [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) Martyn Rivers commands the outriders in the riverlands while Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully) is campaigning in the west with Robb Stark, [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident). Martyn meets Catelyn's party when she nears [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) on her return trip.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) Perwyn and Martyn fight in the [Battle of the Fords](wiki:Battle_of_the_Fords).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

To solidify wartime alliances, Roose Bolton marries one of Walder's granddaughters, [Walda](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Merrett)). After the fall of Winterfell and the Lannister victory over Stannis in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), the Freys in Lord Bolton's army at Harrenhal believe that Robb should bend the knee.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

### A Storm of Swords

At Riverrun, Catelyn frees Jaime from captivity in an effort to exchange him for her daughters, allowing his cousin, Cleos Frey, to accompany him along with her sworn sword, [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) On the journey to King's Landing, Cleos proves to be a useless fighter, and he is killed by [outlaws](wiki:Outlaws) near [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

Robb Stark's marriage to [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling) outrages House Frey and breaks their alliance. Ryman and Black Walder angrily depart Robb's army at the Crag,(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) as do Freys led by Perwyn and Martyn Rivers at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) The Freys with Ser [Frey](wiki:Aenys_Frey) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) are also enraged,(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1) but they remain with Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

After the passing of [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully), the [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident), [Lame Lothar](wiki:Lothar_Frey) and [Bastard Walder](wiki:Walder_Rivers) arrive at Riverrun with forty men from the Twins. Their father, Lord Walder, suggests that their new liege lord, Robb's uncle [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully), stand in Robb's place for the marriage alliance and marry Walder's daughter, [Roslin](wiki:Roslin_Frey).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

While Robb leads his northmen and some rivermen from Riverrun to the Twins, Roose leads his own host north from Harrenhal. They are welcomed and given [guest right](wiki:Guest_right) by the Freys.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) However, Walder has secretly negotiated with Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), and Lord Bolton. They betray the Starks and Tullys at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), breaking [guest right](wiki:Guest_right) and slaughtering their former allies. [Jinglebell](wiki:Aegon_Frey_(son_of_Stevron)), [Benfrey](wiki:Benfrey_Frey), and [Tytos](wiki:Tytos_Frey) are killed in the process.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) Perwyn, Olyvar, and [Alesander](wiki:Alesander_Frey) had been sent away beforehand, they being too friendly with the Starks.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-1)

The Freys are rewarded by the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) for killing King Robb and capturing Lord Edmure. Walder Frey's second son, [Emmon](wiki:Emmon_Frey), is given the lordship of a junior branch, [House Frey of Riverrun](wiki:House_Frey_of_Riverrun), when House Tully is divested of their seat.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) Ryman and his son, [Edwyn](wiki:Edwyn_Frey), lead two thousand men to join Ser [Daven Lannister](wiki:Daven_Lannister) in the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), while Black Walder resolves the [siege of Seagard](wiki:Siege_of_Seagard) by threatening to kill [Patrek Mallister](wiki:Patrek_Mallister).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

Other nobles and the smallfolk scorn House Frey for breaking the sacred guest right. [Petyr](wiki:Petyr_Frey) and [Merrett](wiki:Merrett_Frey) are seized and hanged by the revived Lady Catelyn and her incarnation of the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners).

### A Feast for Crows

To solidify the alliance with the Lannisters, [Amerei Frey](wiki:Amerei_Frey) is paired with Lord [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister),(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) and [Daven Lannister](wiki:Daven_Lannister) is to wed another Frey.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) Violating guest right has ruined the Freys' reputation, however, with even Jaime Lannister calling them treacherous.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) House Frey influence in the riverlands increases further by way of the newly-created [House Lannister of Darry](wiki:House_Lannister_of_Darry), the household of which is provided by Lancel's wife, Ami. A score of Freys, including Danwell, [Arwood](wiki:Arwood_Frey), and [Marissa](wiki:Marissa_Frey), accompany her to [Castle Darry](wiki:Darry). After "[the Hound's](wiki:Rorge)" attack on [Saltpans](wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans), Arwood and his kinsmen [Harys](wiki:Harys_Haigh) and [Donnel Haigh](wiki:Donnel_Haigh) ride from Darry to investigate, finding Ser [Quincy Cox](wiki:Quincy_Cox) hiding behind his walls.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)

Dead soldiers bearing the colors of House Frey have washed up on the [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle). According to the [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)), they are buried next to their enemies of [House Darry](wiki:House_Darry). It is hinted that the enmity between Frey and Darry is not new, but has significant history.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)

After arriving at Riverrun, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) informs Emmon that although he has been granted Riverrun, Emmon is not the liege of the riverlords. The Freys instead owe allegiance to Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), head of [House Baelish of Harrenhal](wiki:House_Baelish_of_Harrenhal) and [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) Jaime removes Ryman from command of the siege of Riverrun in favor of his son Edwyn.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) When informing Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully) of what will happen if he does not surrender, Jaime says he will send the Freys as a second wave, after the other rivermen but before the westermen. On his journey back to the Twins, Ryman is hanged by outlaws near [Fairmarket](wiki:Fairmarket). Ryman's death leaves his son Edwyn as heir to the Crossing.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

According to Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) is on the verge of allying himself with the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) after he agrees to marry both of his granddaughters to members of House Frey and open his port of [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) to Lannister shipping.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

[Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey), [Jared](wiki:Jared_Frey), and [Symond](wiki:Symond_Frey) arrive in White Harbor to solidify the alliance between Houses Frey and Manderly.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) The Manderlys only pretend to accept the peace of King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon),(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0) however, and the Frey envoys disappear while traveling from [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) to [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) pleads ignorance about [their fate](wiki:Frey_Pies/Theories).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0)

Big Walder and Little Walder remain squires of [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) at the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) After the [siege of Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin) is resolved by [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) and Ramsay, they are joined by Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton)'s northmen and Ser [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_Frey) and Ser [Hosteen's](wiki:Hosteen_Frey) Freys, who number between fourteen hundred(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) and two thousand,(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0) to help Roose in pacifying the north. Roose moves the wedding of Ramsay and "[Arya Stark](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)" from Barrowton to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-1)

While quartered at Winterfell during a blizzard, which the Freys are ill prepared for,(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0) tensions rise in Roose's army when the Freys place the blame for their missing envoys on Wyman.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-1) A brawl results in the deaths of several men when Wyman insults the Freys after Little Walder, the brother of Roose's wife [Walda](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Merrett)), is found dead in the snow. To relieve the pressure, Roose sends Aenys and Hosteen as a vanguard against Stannis,(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0) who is encamped at a [crofters' village](wiki:Crofters'_village) in the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood).(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0)

### The Winds of Winter

The Frey force at Winterfell falls prey to a trap set by [Mors Umber](wiki:Mors_Umber) outside the walls, killing Aenys. This leaves Hosteen, who loses his horse in the trap, in command of the force.(wiki:House_Frey.cite_ref-66)
