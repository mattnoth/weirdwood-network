## Identity

House Seaworth is a knightly house from the Stormlands, newly founded in 284 AC. They blazon their arms with a white ship with an onion sail on pale grey, with a black chief and a crowned stag on the chief. (wiki:House_Seaworth)

## Origins

House Seaworth was founded when Davos, a smuggler, ran a cargo of onions and salt fish through the Redwyne fleet's blockade to feed the besieged Storm's End during Robert's Rebellion. Stannis Baratheon rewarded Davos with a knighthood and lands in the Stormlands, creating House Seaworth — but also punished the smuggling by removing the first joint of each finger on Davos's left hand. (wiki:House_Seaworth.cite_ref-Racok10)

## Narrative Arc

Davos Seaworth `RULES` House Seaworth. (track_b: Head) His heir is Devan Seaworth. (track_b: Heir) Davos married Marya, a carpenter's daughter, and fathered seven sons: Dale, Allard, Matthos, Maric, Devan, Stannis, and Steffon. (wiki:House_Seaworth)

During the War of the Five Kings, Davos served as Stannis Baratheon's chief advisor and smuggler-turned-lord. Dale, Allard, Matthos, and Maric all perished in the Battle of the Blackwater when the wildfire trap destroyed the fleet. (wiki:House_Seaworth.cite_ref-Racok58) Davos survived and was eventually named Hand of the King by Stannis. (wiki:House_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos36) Devan serves as Stannis's squire. Stannis and Steffon remain at home with their mother. (wiki:House_Seaworth)

Davos was later sent by Stannis to White Harbor to treat with Lord Wyman Manderly, who publicly condemned Davos but secretly sent him to Skagos to retrieve Rickon Stark. (wiki:House_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd29)
