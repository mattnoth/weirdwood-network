## Origins

### Age of Heroes[ ]

The Gardeners claimed descent from the mythical [Garth Greenhand](wiki:Garth_Greenhand), who, according to legends from the [Reach](wiki:Reach), is said to have been the [High King of the First Men](wiki:High_King_of_the_First_Men) and common ancestor of numerous noble houses from the Reach.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_garth_greenhand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) His firstborn child was [Garth the Gardener](wiki:Garth_the_Gardener), the legendary first [King of the Reach](wiki:King_of_the_Reach) who reigned in the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_garth_greenhand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) According to legend, Garth the Gardener made his home on the hill atop the [Mander](wiki:Mander) river that in time would become known as [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), and he wore a crown of vines and flowers. From his loins sprang House Gardener, whose kings would rule the Reach for many thousands of years.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_garth_greenhand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Their [Oakenseat](wiki:Oakenseat) grew from an [oak](wiki:Oak) that Garth Greenhand himself was reputedly said to have planted.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
The Gardener kings initially ruled the [Reach proper](wiki:Reach_proper), although over time they expanded their territory. The [western marches](wiki:Western_marches) between [Horn Hill](wiki:Horn_Hill) and [Nightsong](wiki:Nightsong) were eventually added to the Gardeners' domain. [Old Oak](wiki:Old_Oak), [Red Lake](wiki:Red_Lake), and [Goldengrove](wiki:Goldengrove) joined the realm with pacts of friendship and mutual defense with [Garth III](wiki:Garth_III_Gardener) (the Great).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) [Meryn III](wiki:Meryn_III_Gardener) brought the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor) into the kingdom and made [House Redwyne](wiki:House_Redwyne) his vassals after his cousin, the last [King of the Arbor](wiki:King_of_the_Arbor), was lost at sea.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Not long after, King [Garland II](wiki:Garland_II_Gardener) (the Bridegroom) brought [House Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower) of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) and their domains into the realm by wedding his daughter to Lord [Lymond Hightower](wiki:Lymond_Hightower) and putting aside his own [wives](wiki:Polygamy) to marry Lymond's daughter.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) King [Gwayne III](wiki:Gwayne_III_Gardener) (the Fat) resolved the quarrel between Lords [Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) and [Peake](wiki:House_Peake) and accepted fealty for their lands without bloodshed. [John II](wiki:John_II_Gardener) (the Tall) received homage from lords and petty kings as he sailed his barge to the headwaters of the Mander.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)
Legendary warrior kings from this time include [Garth V](wiki:Garth_V_Gardener) (Hammer of the Dornish), [Gwayne I](wiki:Gwayne_I_Gardener) (the Gallant), [Gyles I](wiki:Gyles_I_Gardener) (the Woe), and [Gordan I](wiki:Gordan_I_Gardener) (Grey-Eyes).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) The Gardener kings often warred with the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn), sometimes in alliance with the [Kings of the Rock](wiki:Kings_of_the_Rock) and the [Lords of Oldtown](wiki:Lord_of_Oldtown).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) At least six Gardener kings died in battle against the ironborn, including [Gareth II](wiki:Gareth_II_Gardener) (the Grim), [Garth VI](wiki:Garth_VI_Gardener) (the Morningstar), and [Gyles II](wiki:Gyles_II_Gardener), the last of whom was tortured to death by [Joron I Blacktyde](wiki:Joron_I_Blacktyde).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### The Golden Reign[ ]

The greatest of the Gardeners was King [Garth VII Gardener](wiki:Garth_VII_Gardener), known as the Goldenhand, for his deeds both in war and in peace.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) Ascending to the Oakenseat at age twelve, Garth ruled for eighty-one years, of which less than ten were spent at war. As a boy he defeated an invasion of [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) by King [Ferris Fowler](wiki:Ferris_Fowler),(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8) then drove the ironborn from the Misty Islands and fortified them as the [Shield Islands](wiki:Shield_Islands) with men from the Reach.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Garth VII divided an alliance between the King of the Rock and [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King), defeating both in the [Battle of Three Armies](wiki:Battle_of_Three_Armies), and he dictated the boundaries of their three kingdoms in the aftermath.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9)
Garth's greatest accomplishment was giving his people three-quarters of a century of golden peace, bringing an unprecedented prosperity. Garth's rule became known as the [Golden Reign](wiki:Golden_Reign), and was when the Reach truly flowered.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10)

### Arrival of the Andals[ ]

The Kings of the Reach observed the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands), and the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) from afar. Instead of allying with the invaders against other [First Men](wiki:First_Men), they took precautions against possible [Andal](wiki:Andals) invasions. King [Gwayne IV](wiki:Gwayne_IV_Gardener) (the Gods-fearing) sought aid from the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest), while [Mern II](wiki:Mern_II_Gardener) (the Mason) constructed a new curtain wall around Highgarden and encouraged his bannermen to do the same. [Mern III](wiki:Mern_III_Gardener) (the Madling) supported a [woods witch](wiki:Woods_witch) who claimed she could raise armies of the dead. The anticipated Andal attacks never occurred, however. When the disunited Andals eventually reached the realm of the Gardeners generations later, however, they were welcomed by the [Three Sage Kings](wiki:Three_Sage_Kings).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
The Three Sage Kings who followed one another on the Oakenseat—[Garth IX](wiki:Garth_IX_Gardener), his son [Merle I](wiki:Merle_I_Gardener) (the Meek), and his grandson [Gwayne V](wiki:Gwayne_V_Gardener)—were a trio of cunning and wise kings who used a policy of accord and assimilation with the Andals rather than armed resistance. Although he continued to worship the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods), Garth IX made a [septon](wiki:Septon) part of his councils and the first [sept](wiki:Sept) at Highgarden was built during his reign. Merle I converted to the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) and become its patron, helping to fund and build [septs](wiki:Sept), [septries](wiki:Septry), and [motherhouses](wiki:Motherhouse) across the Reach. Gwayne V was the first Gardener king born into the new Faith, as well as the first to be made a [knight](wiki:Knight).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
The Three Sage Kings took Andals into their service as knights and retainers. Ser [Alester Tyrell](wiki:Alester_Tyrell), the founder of [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell), was made Gwayne V's champion and [sworn shield](wiki:Sworn_shield).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Merle I and Gwayne V married Andal maidens to bind their fathers to the Gardener realm.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) The three kings also bequeathed lands, wives, and lordships for the more powerful of the Andals descending on the Reach in exchange for pledges of fealty; numerous houses of the Reach trace their ancestry back to these Andal adventurers.
The Gardeners also encouraged Andal craftsmen, specially blacksmiths and stonemasons, to settle throughout the Reach, supplying them and their bannermen with [iron](wiki:Iron) instead of [bronze](wiki:Bronze) and strengthening their castles with Andal masonry. Most new lords remained loyal and helped the Gardeners against internal and external threats. Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) writes that seldom had a conquest been achieved with less bloodshed.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

### Andal Kings[ ]

The centuries that followed the assimilation with the Andals were less peaceful, and the Gardener kings (and one queen) varied in wisdom and strength. The Kings of the Reach constantly warred against and allied with the [Kings of the Rock](wiki:Kings_of_the_Rock), the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_Kings), the many kings from Dorne, and the [Kings of the Rivers and the Hills](wiki:Kings_of_the_Rivers_and_the_Hills).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)
During the minority of King [Garland VI Gardener](wiki:Garland_VI_Gardener), a member of House Tyrell ruled the Reach in his name as [regent](wiki:Regent).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)
King [Gyles III Gardener](wiki:Gyles_III_Gardener) conquered from the Storm King all the lands north of the [rainwood](wiki:Rainwood) save for [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). While he was away, the Reach was attacked by [Lancel IV Lannister](wiki:Lancel_IV_Lannister), forcing Gyles to deal with the [westermen](wiki:Westermen).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) A new broader war followed, involving three Dornish kings and two river kings, and ended with Gyles III dead of a [bloody flux](wiki:Bloody_flux) and his conquests abandoned.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)
The long reign of King [Garth X](wiki:Garth_X_Gardener), known as Garth Greybeard, witnessed the nadir of Gardener power. The elderly and senile Garth had sired only daughters, who were married to the rival Lords [Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) and [Peake](wiki:House_Peake). Once the rival lords began warring, the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister), [Durrandons](wiki:House_Durrandon), and Dornish attacked the kingdom. A Dornish king sacked Highgarden, killed Garth Greybeard, and destroyed the [Oakenseat](wiki:Oakenseat).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)
The anarchy that followed lasted almost a decade until Ser [Osmund Tyrell](wiki:Osmund_Tyrell), the [High Steward](wiki:High_Steward), made common cause with the other lords of the Reach and defeated both the Peakes and the Manderlys, reclaimed the ruins of Highgarden, and supported [Mern VI Gardener](wiki:Mern_VI_Gardener), a second cousin of Garth, as king. Although a man of only modest gifts, Mern VI was able to rebuild Highgarden and restore the power of the Reach and House Gardener, relying on the counsel of his stewards: Osmund Tyrell, Osmund's son Ser [Robert](wiki:Robert_Tyrell)—who was allowed to marry his youngest daughter in gratitude—and Robert's son [Lorent](wiki:Lorent_Tyrell_(steward)). Mern's son [Garth XI](wiki:Garth_XI_Gardener) (the Painter) retaliated against the Dornish by invading the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9)
Approximately a thousand years ago, Lord [Lorimar Peake](wiki:Lorimar_Peake) drove House Manderly from the Reach at the behest of King [Perceon III Gardener](wiki:Perceon_III_Gardener), who feared the Manderlys' growing power in the Reach.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) The Manderlys fled from the [Mander](wiki:Mander) for the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife) in the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) King [Greydon Gardener](wiki:Greydon_Gardener) attempted an invasion of Dorne, but was thrown back by [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria), [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) King [Garse VII](wiki:Garse_VII_Gardener) was slain by the Storm King, [Argilac the Arrogant](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon), at the [Battle of Summerfield](wiki:Battle_of_Summerfield).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

### Aegon's Conquest and Extinction[ ]

When [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) invaded [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) three centuries ago, King [Mern IX Gardener](wiki:Mern_IX_Gardener) joined with [Loren I Lannister](wiki:Loren_I_Lannister), [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock), to put an end to [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest) at what would become known as the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Mern commanded the center of the combined army and led the charge against Aegon's forces. The host of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) eventually broke, but by then Aegon and his sisters, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) and [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen), had taken to the air on their [dragons](wiki:Dragons). [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes), and [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) killed four thousand men of the combined army, among them Mern and his sons, grandsons, brothers, uncles, cousins, and other kin.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_landing_and_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) One nephew of King Mern survived, but he died of his burns three days later, ending House Gardener.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
Mern's High Steward, [Harlan Tyrell](wiki:Harlan_Tyrell), surrendered [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) to Aegon I Targaryen, pledging fealty. Aegon named Harlan as [Lord of Highgarden](wiki:Lord_of_Highgarden), [Lord Paramount of the Mander](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Mander), and [Warden of the South](wiki:Warden_of_the_South), and he granted Harlan dominion over the Reach in exchange for his submission to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### Legacy[ ]

Most noble houses from the [Reach](wiki:Reach) trace their descent back to [Garth Greenhand](wiki:Garth_Greenhand),(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_garth_greenhand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) a fact that is often used to boast of a better claim than [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) as the rightful rulers of the Reach, as the Tyrells were stewards who descended from the Gardeners only through the female line.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) The green hand of the Gardeners is included in the sigils of Houses [Chester](wiki:House_Chester) and [Uffering](wiki:House_Uffering), and a white hand on green is depicted in the arms of [House Oldflowers](wiki:House_Oldflowers). Despite now living in the [north](wiki:North), knights of [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) continue to claim membership in the [Order of the Green Hand](wiki:Order_of_the_Green_Hand).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_highgarden.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)
Golden [currency](wiki:Currency) from the time of the Gardeners is still in use, albeit rare.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) The coins are named hands, each worth roughly half a gold dragon,(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) and feature the hand sigil of House Gardener on one side and the likeness of the current king on the other side. They are known to have been made with [Garth XII](wiki:Garth_XII_Gardener)'s effigy.(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)
Septon [Mattheus](wiki:Mattheus) claimed descent from the Gardener kings and was expected to be chosen as [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon),(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) but [Alfyn](wiki:Alfyn) was elected instead in [54 AC](wiki:54_AC).(wiki:House_Gardener.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
