## Origins

House Rogare was a rich and powerful banking dynasty whose bloodlines could be traced back to [Valyria](wiki:Valyria) before the [Doom](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria).(wiki:House_Rogare.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
The Rogare family from the [Free City](wiki:Free_City) of [Lys](wiki:Lys) was once closely tied to the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). Lady [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare) was the wife of Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) and her uncle [Drazenko](wiki:Drazenko_Rogare) was the consort of [Aliandra Martell](wiki:Aliandra_Martell), [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne).(wiki:House_Rogare.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
For some years after the [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) civil war known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the [Rogare Bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank) rivaled the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos) in size(wiki:House_Rogare.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) and the family became heavily involved in Westerosi court intrigues during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring).(wiki:House_Rogare.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
Larra's father, [Lysandro the Magnificent](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare), proclaimed himself [First Magister for Life](wiki:First_Magister_for_Life), but the Rogares began to weaken when he and Drazenko died with a day of each other in [135 AC](wiki:135_AC). Lysandro's heir, the ambitious [Lysaro Rogare](wiki:Lysaro_Rogare), was a spendthrift who angered rival [magisters](wiki:Magister): Lysaro's embezzlement caused the Rogare Bank to fail, and he was scourged to death at the [Temple of Trade](wiki:Temple_of_Trade) the same year. Lysaro's siblings were not executed, however, and [Moredo Rogare](wiki:Moredo_Rogare), carrier of the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) [longsword](wiki:Longsword) [Truth](wiki:Truth_(sword)), once led a campaign against Lys.(wiki:House_Rogare.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) It is implied that Moredo was somewhat successful in his campaign against Lys, because in [138 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) he was able to put the bones of his dead brother, that had been left chained to a pillar at the Temple of Trade, into the family crypt.(wiki:House_Rogare.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Although the Rogares at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) were apparently guilty of some crimes, their rivals in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) blamed them for many more. In 135 AC Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan) was tortured in an attempt to find evidence against the Rogares, and Ser [Marston Waters](wiki:Marston_Waters), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), sent men to capture Larra after arresting her brothers. Aegon and Viserys refused to surrender Larra, however, and the case against the Rogares collapsed.(wiki:House_Rogare.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Larra left the court at King's Landing after she grew homesick(wiki:House_Rogare.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and returned to [Lys](wiki:Lys) in [139 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:House_Rogare.cite_ref-6)
