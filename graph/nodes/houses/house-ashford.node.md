## Origins

Ser [Alyn Ashford](wiki:Alyn_Ashford) participated in a [tourney at Old Oak](wiki:Tourney_at_Old_Oak) in [73 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), where he was defeated by Prince [Baelon Targaryen](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)).(wiki:House_Ashford.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
House Ashford held [a tourney](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow) at [Ashford Meadow](wiki:Ashford_Meadow) in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC). [Lord Ashford](wiki:Lord_Ashford)'s sons, Ser [Androw](wiki:Androw_Ashford) and Ser [Robert](wiki:Robert_Ashford), were two of the original champions of their [thirteen-year-old sister](wiki:Lord_Ashford's_daughter). During the tourney an [altercation occurred](wiki:Tanselle) between Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) and Prince [Aerion Targaryen](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen). This resulted in Duncan striking the prince, claiming that he was only following the [knight](wiki:Knight)'s vows to defend the weak. Aerion demanded a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) to clear his name. The tourney became the theater of the first trial of seven in more than a hundred years. During the trial King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen)'s son and heir, Prince [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), was slain by his own brother, Prince [Maekar](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Ashford.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) was defeated at the [Battle of Ashford](wiki:Battle_of_Ashford) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:House_Ashford.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) As bannermen of [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell), House Ashford can be presumed to have remained loyal to King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) during Robert's Rebellion.
