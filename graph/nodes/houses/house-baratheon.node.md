## Origins

### Targaryen Era

House Baratheon is the youngest of the [Great Houses](wiki:Great_House) of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). It was created when [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon), one of [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen)'s generals, his closest companion, and his rumored [bastard](wiki:Bastard) half-brother, took [Argella Durrandon](wiki:Argella_Durrandon), the only daughter of [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon), the last [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King), to wife. Orys took the sigil and words of [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon) for his own and became the first [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Through the female line, House Baratheon descents from King [Durran Godsgrief](wiki:Durran_Godsgrief), who founded the kingdom of the Storm Kings during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_durrandon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

[Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon), the lord of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) at the time of the reigns of kings [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) and [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), served as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm) during the minority of Jaehaerys I.(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) A grandson of Lord Orys, Rogar would marry Queen Dowager [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) and father two children, [Boremund](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon) and [Jocelyn](wiki:Jocelyn_Baratheon). Boremund would succeed his father, while Jocelyn married Prince [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) sided the House with [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and the [greens](wiki:Greens), despite his late father, Lord Boremund's support of [the blacks](wiki:The_blacks) and relation to [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)) (through his sister, [Jocelyn Baratheon](wiki:Jocelyn_Baratheon)).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) was sent to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), to get Lord Borros to side with Aegon II, by offering a betrothal between himself and one of Lord Borros' four daughters. It is unknown whether this marriage occurred prior to Aemond's [death](wiki:Dance_over_Harrenhal) the next year. Lord Borros was reluctant to face the dragons during the war, but marched with his army towards the end of the war, seizing [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) for Aegon II during the Moon of the Three Kings, restoring the city to order. He was promised that his eldest daughter would marry King Aegon II, who had lost both his Queen, [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen), as well as both of his sons, during the war. Borros then marched against the approaching riverlander army, and fought in the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad), where he was killed by young Lord [Kermit Tully](wiki:Kermit_Tully).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Having been injured in [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) stayed at Storm's End for more than half a year to regain his strength, before traveling on to King's Landing.(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

In [200 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Lord Baratheon held a tourney to celebrate the birth of his grandson. In [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), the heir to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), Ser [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon), called the Laughing Storm, participated in the Ashford tourney and fought on the side of Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) during a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

A daughter of Lord Lyonel was betrothed to crown prince [Duncan Targaryen](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen) in [237 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Duncan broke the betrothal when he married [Jenny of Oldstones](wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones) in [239 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). This angered Lord Lyonel, causing him to declare independence, naming himself the new Storm King. His short, bloody rebellion ended when he was defeated by Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) in single combat, Duncan renounced his claim to the throne, and King Aegon promised that Princess [Rhaelle Targaryen](wiki:Rhaelle_Targaryen), his youngest daughter, would marry Lyonel's heir, [Ormund Baratheon](wiki:Ormund_Baratheon). Rhaelle was send to Storm's End to serve as cupbearer to Lord Lyonel, and as lady companion to Lord Lyonel's wife. The wedding took place in [245 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and a son, [Steffon](wiki:Steffon_Baratheon), was born the next year.(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

Steffon served in his youth at the court of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), and became a close friend to his cousin, Prince [Aerys Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen). He fought in the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings) in [260 AC](wiki:260_AC), where his father, Lord Ormund, died in his arms, slain by [Maelys I Blackfyre](wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

Lord Steffon's eldest son, [Robert](wiki:Robert_Baratheon), was fostered by Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), together with [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) After the [Defiance of Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale), King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) summoned Lord Steffon to King's Landing and named him to the small council. In [278 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Steffon was sent to [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), to find a bride for Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen). He was accompanied by his wife, Lady [Cassana Estermont](wiki:Cassana_Estermont). They failed their mission, and died upon their return, when their ship *[Windproud](wiki:Windproud)* sank in [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### Robert's Rebellion

Steffon's eldest son, Robert, inherited Storm's End upon Steffon's death, and in the years that followed, was betrothed to [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot_4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) defeated Lord Robert in a [tourney at Storm's End](wiki:Tourney_at_Storm's_End), winning the joust in the end.(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) In [282 AC](wiki:282_AC), Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), Robert's cousin, disappeared with Lyanna Stark, causing Lyanna's eldest brother [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark) to ride to King's Landing in anger. He was arrested, and killed together with his father, Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark), on the command of King Aerys II. The king then demanded the heads of both Lord Robert, as well as [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), from Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), as both men were in the Vale. Jon refused and raised his banners, marking the start of the war that would become known as Robert's Rebellion. Robert became a figure-head, and he personally killed Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) during the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) in [283 AC](wiki:283_AC). In the early parts of the war, the [battles at Summerhall](wiki:Battles_at_Summerhall) and the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End) were fixtures of the stormlands theater.(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

### Baratheon Dynasty

Lord Robert was crowned King Robert I. House Baratheon was split into three branches: [House Baratheon of King's Landing](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing), under King Robert I, [House Baratheon of Dragonstone](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_Dragonstone), headed by Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), and House Baratheon of Storm's End, headed by Lord Renly Baratheon, Robert's youngest brother. During [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion) in [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), the combined Baratheon fleet defeated the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) in a [sea battle off Fair Isle](wiki:Sea_battle_off_Fair_Isle), commanded by Stannis, the [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships). Stannis later subdued [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk) in his brother's name. Some time after he achieved manhood, Renly Baratheon was appointed to Robert's [small council](wiki:Small_council) as [master of laws](wiki:Master_of_laws).

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), dies suddenly in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and his retinue travel to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to offer the position to Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), Robert's friend from his youth. Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) rides north to meet King Robert on his return trip after Eddard accepts the position, while Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) returns to his seat at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). Renly participates in [the Hand's tourney](wiki:The_Hand's_tourney), riding well before losing to [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane).

Later, as Robert lies dying from a [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood) [boar](wiki:Boar)'s tusk, Renly asks Eddard to support him in a bid for the throne. Lord Stark has discovered that Queen [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s children, [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), and [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), are not Robert's legitimate children, but they are instead bastards born of incest with Cersei's brother, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister). Eddard insists that it is Lord Stannis who should be Robert's successor as king. After Eddard refuses to support Renly's bid for power, Renly, his thirty men-at-arms, and his former squire Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) ride south out of the capital. King Joffrey I names Lord Renly and Ser Loras among the nobles who must do him homage or be declared traitors, and soon thereafter has Eddard executed for treason.

### A Clash of Kings

Despite Stannis's better claim to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Renly is able to rally the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) to his cause, as well as [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) and much of the [Reach](wiki:Reach). Stannis has a significantly smaller force drawn mostly from the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) and the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet). When Renly is [assassinated](wiki:Shadow_assassin) beneath the walls of Storm's End as the brothers' hosts [prepare to clash](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), he leaves no heirs. Most of the houses sworn to Storm's End, as well as some from the Reach, join Stannis, who unsuccessfully attacks King's Landing in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater). Following their defeat, most houses sworn to Stannis kneel to King Joffrey and the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).

### A Storm of Swords

Stannis's garrison at Storm's End commanded by Ser [Gilbert Farring](wiki:Gilbert_Farring) remains loyal to his cause.

[Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm) is smuggled out of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) by Lord [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) to prevent him from being burned alive by Stannis's advisor, [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre).

Renly's widow, [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), marries King [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon). After [Joffrey's death](wiki:Purple_Wedding), the current head of the house is the young king [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) - a title disputed by King Stannis.

Aside from a few garrisons, Stannis leads his remaining forces north to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) and saves the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) from [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) in the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall).

### A Feast for Crows

Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) serves as [regent](wiki:Regent) for her son, King Tommen I. She sends Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) to [take Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(300)) from Stannis's garrison, although when Mace hears his daughter [Margaery](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) has been arrested he returns to the capital with most of his army,(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) leaving Lord [Mathis Rowan](wiki:Mathis_Rowan) to continue the siege with a token force.(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_mathis_rowan.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Cersei is replaced as Tommen's regent by Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) after she is also arrested by the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) is said to have taken [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) from Stannis's garrison in a [bloody assault](wiki:Siege_of_Dragonstone).

### A Dance with Dragons

After leaving [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), Stannis remains in the [north](wiki:North), [liberates Deepwood Motte](wiki:Fight_by_Deepwood_Motte),(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) and [marches on Winterfell](wiki:March_on_Winterfell).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) His wife, Queen [Selyse Florent](wiki:Selyse_Florent), and heir, Princess [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon), travel to Castle Black from [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

The garrison at Storm's End is Stannis's last force in the south, but it is threatened by Mathis Rowan as well as [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) and the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_mathis_rowan.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

The regent of King Tommen I Baratheon, Ser Kevan Lannister, is murdered in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) by [Varys](wiki:Varys).(wiki:House_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)
