## Identity

House Reed of Greywater Watch is a noble house from Greywater Watch and one of the principal families in the North. They rule the crannogmen, small folk who live in the swamps and marshes of the Neck (wiki:House_Reed). Their arms are a black lizard-lion on grey-green (Tier 2 — semi-canon heraldry source) (wiki:House_Reed).

## Heraldry & Sigil

A black lizard-lion on grey-green (Vert-cendrée, a lizard-lion sable) (wiki:House_Reed)

## Origins

The Reeds descend from the First Men (Tier 2 — semi-canon source) (wiki:House_Reed). They first swore fealty to House Stark when the Starks were Kings in the North, thousands of years ago (wiki:House_Reed.cite_ref-Racok21). House Reed has led the crannogmen of the Neck since the defeat of the Marsh Kings by the Starks (wiki:House_Reed.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf).

## Narrative Arc

Howland Reed was one of Eddard Stark's six companions at the combat at the tower of joy and the only one who survived; Ned credited Howland with saving his life (wiki:House_Reed.cite_ref-Ragot39, wiki:House_Reed.cite_ref-Racok21). Howland has reportedly remained in the Neck since Robert's Rebellion (wiki:House_Reed.cite_ref-Rawoiaf). In AGOT, the crannogmen did not join Robb Stark's army but were instructed to defend the Neck against Lannister invasion via the causeway (wiki:House_Reed.cite_ref-Ragot55). In ACOK, Howland sent his children Meera and Jojen to Winterfell for the harvest feast to renew the oath to the Starks (wiki:House_Reed.cite_ref-Racok21). They escaped the sacked Winterfell with Bran and Rickon Stark (wiki:House_Reed.cite_ref-Racok69). In ASOS, Robb commanded Maege Mormont and Galbart Glover to seek out Howland to coordinate a flanking attack on Moat Cailin, but Robb was killed at the Red Wedding before the plan could be executed (wiki:House_Reed.cite_ref-Rasos45, wiki:House_Reed.cite_ref-Rasos51). In ADWD, Meera and Jojen accompany Bran beyond the Wall seeking the three-eyed crow (wiki:House_Reed.cite_ref-Radwd13). Roose Bolton does not trust the crannogmen, traveling with a decoy wagon through the Neck (wiki:House_Reed.cite_ref-Radwd20).
