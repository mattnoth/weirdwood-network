## Identity

House Hoare of Orkmont is an extinct noble house originally from the Iron Islands, known as the "black line" or the "black blood" (wiki:House_Hoare.cite_ref-Ragot60). The Hoares became Kings of the Iron Islands after Andal settlers ended the rule of House Greyiron (wiki:House_Hoare.cite_ref-History).

## Origins

The Hoares originated on Orkmont during the Age of Heroes (track_b: Seats). Archmaester Hake described them as "black of hair, black of eye, and black of heart" (wiki:House_Hoare.cite_ref-Raffc1). They built Hoare Castle on Great Wyk before eventually moving their seat to the Riverlands (wiki:House_Hoare.cite_ref-History).

## Appearances & Description

Their sigil depicted per saltire with two heavy silver chains crossing between a gold longship on black, a dark green pine on white, a cluster of red grapes on gold, and a black raven flying in a blue sky — representing the distant lands under their rule: the Iron Islands, Bear Island, the Arbor, and Oldtown (wiki:House_Hoare.cite_ref-semi-canon).

## Narrative Arc

The Hoares rose to power after allying with Andal invaders to overthrow House Greyiron. Under Qhored I Hoare, ironborn expansion reached its peak (wiki:House_Hoare.cite_ref-History). The later "Andal Hoares" were criticized for having "Andal taint" through Andal queens. King Harwyn Hardhand conquered the Riverlands from Storm King Arrec Durrandon at the battle of Fairmarket (wiki:House_Hoare.cite_ref-Racok11). His grandson Harren the Black built Harrenhal, the greatest castle in the Seven Kingdoms, completing it the day Aegon I Targaryen landed in Westeros. The rivermen rebelled and joined the Targaryens; Aegon burned Harrenhal with Balerion's dragonflame, incinerating Harren and his sons and ending the Hoare line (wiki:House_Hoare.cite_ref-Ragot60). After Harren's death, Qhorin Volmark claimed the black line's legacy but was slain by Aegon. Harren the Red later seized Harrenhal claiming to be Harren's son but was defeated (wiki:House_Hoare.cite_ref-History).
