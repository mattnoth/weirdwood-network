## Identity

House Staunton of Rook's Rest is a lordly house from the crownlands. Their arms (semi-canon) depict two black wings upon a white fess on checkered black and grey (*Chequy sable and cendrée, on a fess argent two wings of the first*) (wiki:House_Staunton). Their seat is Rook's Rest (track_b: Seat).

## Narrative Arc

House Staunton has a rich history tied to the Targaryens. A Lord Staunton was among King Maegor I's last supporters, then yielded the Red Keep to Jaehaerys I — he was pardoned after surrendering some land (wiki:House_Staunton.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon, wiki:House_Staunton.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king). Lord Staunton and family died of the Shivers in 59 AC (wiki:House_Staunton.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign).

During the Dance of the Dragons, the Stauntons supported the blacks. Lord Staunton was killed at the Battle at Rook's Rest and his head taken to King's Landing (wiki:House_Staunton.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons). Lady Staunton later joined Corwyn Corbray's host during the Hour of the Wolf (wiki:House_Staunton.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath).

Lord Symond Staunton served as master of laws under Aerys II Targaryen. He tried to ban tourneys and inflamed Aerys's suspicions against Prince Rhaegar (wiki:House_Staunton.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons).

In AFFC, a maester tells Brienne that smallfolk believe the Defiance of Duskendale could have been avoided if Lord Denys Darklyn had married a Staunton instead of Serala of Myr (wiki:House_Staunton.cite_ref-Raffc9). No living members have appeared in the main series.
