## Origins

### Knights[ ]

In [42 AC](wiki:42_AC), Ser [Guy Lothston](wiki:Guy_Lothston) was slain defending [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) during the king's [Trial of Seven](wiki:Trial_of_Seven).(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
Ser [Lucas Lothston](wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena)) was the grandson of a [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight). In [135 AC](wiki:135_AC), after the [secret siege](wiki:Secret_siege) and the failed plot against King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), Lucas was named [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms) of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) by Lord [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly) to replace Ser [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long). Lucas earned the respect of Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) and his elder brother, King Aegon III.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### Lords of Harrenhal[ ]

In [149 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [Falena Stokeworth](wiki:Falena_Stokeworth) became the first [mistress](wiki:Mistress) of Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), making him "a man" when the prince was fourteen. When a [knight](wiki:Knight) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) found them abed together in [151 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), his father, Prince Viserys, desired to remove Falena from court at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Viserys married her to Ser Lucas, and convinced King Aegon III to gift his master-at-arms with [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), as the previous holders of the castle, [House Strong](wiki:House_Strong), had died out as a result of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) House Lothston became the sixth noble house to rule as [Lords of Harrenhal](wiki:Lords_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
In [178 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Falena came to court with her daughter (by Lucas or by Aegon), [Jeyne](wiki:Jeyne_Lothston), who became the eighth of King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen)'s [mistresses](wiki:Mistress). Lord Lucas was made [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), and it was rumored that the king enjoyed mother and daughter in the same bed.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) This possibly resulted in Lucas receiving the derogatory title "The Pander".(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-pander_11-0) However, after Jeyne caught a [pox](wiki:Pox) from the king, Lucas was removed as Hand and the Lothstons were all sent away from court.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3)
The [Bastard of Harrenhal](wiki:Bastard_of_Harrenhal) was defeated by Ser [Arlan of Pennytree](wiki:Arlan_of_Pennytree) in the [melee](wiki:Melee) at a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) held at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in [193 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
The Lothstons of Harrenhal had a bad reputation; Lord [Lucas Lothston](wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(father_of_Manfryd)) and his son [Manfryd](wiki:Manfryd_Lothston), for example, were renowned for their black deeds.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) According to Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey), [Manfred Lothston](wiki:Manfred_Lothston) betrayed [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) in the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion).(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Lady [Danelle Lothston](wiki:Danelle_Lothston), called Mad Danelle, was said to send giant [bats](wiki:Bat) out to capture children for her cookpots, to have bathed in blood, and to have feasted on human flesh.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
In [212 AC](wiki:212_AC), when Lord [Bloodraven](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) marched on the [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) to quash the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion), among the [river lords](wiki:River_lords) who came in support was Lady Danelle.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
The downfall of House Lothston came when Danelle turned to the [black arts](wiki:Sorcery), causing chaos.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) The last Lothston was killed with the help of an ancestor of Ser [Illifer the Penniless](wiki:Illifer_the_Penniless) in unknown circumstances.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) After the line ended during the reign of King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), the lordship of Harrenhal was given to [House Whent](wiki:House_Whent), [knights](wiki:Knight) who had first served the Lothstons but then helped to defeat the mad family.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) The blacksmith [Ben Blackthumb](wiki:Ben_Blackthumb) worked as a boy for Lord Lothston, and later for the grandfather and father of Lady [Shella Whent](wiki:Shella_Whent).(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

For his ride back to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) uses a [shield](wiki:Shield) found in the armory of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) with the [arms](wiki:Arms) of House Lothston, thinking that because the Lothstons are all dead, no one will bother him for using arms that are not his own.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Jaime gives the heavy oaken shield to [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) when she departs King's Landing to find [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark).(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) On the road, Brienne encounters a [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight), Ser [Illifer the Penniless](wiki:Illifer_the_Penniless), who chastises her for using the Lothston arms, and mentions an ancestor who helped kill the last of the Lothstons.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6) When Brienne gets to [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), a captain at the gate tells her that the black bat arms are of ill repute. Brienne has the shield re-painted by the [captain's sister](wiki:Captain's_sister), who is not pleased to see the black bat either, as she is reminded of her mother's frightening tale about [Mad Danelle](wiki:Danelle_Lothston).(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)
Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) uses the extinction of House Lothston as an example of the fate of those who claim to own Harrenhal.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
Jaime Lannister returns to Harrenhal, and learns that Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) forced some of his captives to commit [cannibalism](wiki:Cannibalism) there. Jaime remembers the stories of Lady [Danelle Lothston](wiki:Danelle_Lothston), who bathed in tubs of blood and presided over feasts of human flesh.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Jon Lothston](wiki:Jon_Lothston) serves with the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) in [Essos](wiki:Essos). He may or not be genuinely of House Lothston, as in the [free companies](wiki:Free_companies) a man can call himself whatever he chooses.(wiki:House_Lothston.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
