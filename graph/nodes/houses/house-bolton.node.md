## Origins

### Red Kings

The Boltons are an ancient and powerful house of the [north](wiki:North) who once ruled as [Red Kings](wiki:Red_King) from the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort), with their land reaching from the [Last River](wiki:Last_River) and the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife) to the [Sheepshead Hills](wiki:Sheepshead_Hills).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf711_1-1) It has been suggested that the legendary [Night's King](wiki:Night's_King) was a Bolton.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Some Red Kings allied with [Marsh Kings](wiki:Marsh_Kings) to defend against [southron](wiki:Southron) invaders.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf711_1-2)

The Boltons have a sinister reputation(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf711_1-3) because of their tradition of [flaying](wiki:Flaying) their enemies,(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf74.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) with the skins then hung in a room of the Dreadfort.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) After the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night), the Red Kings were bitter rivals of the [Kings of Winter](wiki:King_in_the_North), the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). The Boltons achieved some successes against the Starks, with Kings [Royce II](wiki:Royce_II_Bolton) and [Royce IV](wiki:Royce_IV_Bolton) burning Winterfell.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf711_1-4) According to rumor, some Boltons wore the flayed skins of Stark princes and lords, such as the son of [Bael the Bard](wiki:Bael_the_Bard),(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) as cloaks(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf711_1-5) during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

The last Red King, [Rogar the Huntsman](wiki:Rogar_Bolton), submitted to Winterfell when the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) was beginning. King [Theon Stark](wiki:Theon_Stark), the Hungry Wolf, and the Boltons defeated [Argos Sevenstar](wiki:Argos_Sevenstar) and his [Andals](wiki:Andals) in the [Battle of the Weeping Water](wiki:Battle_of_the_Weeping_Water).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf711_1-6)

### Vassals of Winterfell

Thousands of years ago,(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-22) the Dreadfort rose in an unsuccessful rebellion with the [Greystarks](wiki:House_Greystark) of the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den) against the Starks. The Boltons survived the rebellion, although the Greystarks did not.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3)

*[The Chronicles of Longsister](wiki:The_Chronicles_of_Longsister)* state that during the [Rape of the Three Sisters](wiki:Rape_of_the_Three_Sisters) by the [Kings of Winter](wiki:Kings_of_Winter), which occurred two thousand years ago,(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) [Belthasar Bolton](wiki:Belthasar_Bolton) had a [Pink Pavilion](wiki:Pink_Pavilion) made from the [flayed](wiki:Flaying) skins of a hundred [Sistermen](wiki:Sistermen).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf74.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

The Boltons threatened Winterfell during the reign of King [Edrick Snowbeard](wiki:Edrick_Stark).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf716_23-0) At some point unspecified centuries ago, the Boltons rebelled against the Starks. The Stark armies besieged the Dreadfort for two years before the Boltons capitulated to King [Harlon Stark](wiki:Harlon_Stark) and dipped their banners once more.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

"A thousand years ago",(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-24) the Boltons bent their knees to Winterfell and agreed to abandon their practice of flaying.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) It is rumored the skins of their enemies hang in a room within the Dreadfort, however.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

### Iron Throne Era

[Barba Bolton](wiki:Barba_Bolton) attended the [Maiden's Day Ball](wiki:Maiden's_Day_Ball) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), but King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) chose to wed [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon) instead. Barba asked the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) for aid in feeding [northmen](wiki:Northmen) during winter.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rfab21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

When Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) called the banners of the north to fight in [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) took up arms, fighting in the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident). Afterward, he advised Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) to slash the throat of Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), a knight in the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), but Robert instead sent for his [maesters](wiki:Maesters) to treat the wounds of the legendary Barristan the Bold.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

When [Domeric Bolton](wiki:Domeric_Bolton), Roose's accomplished son and heir, learned that he had a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) half brother, he rode up the [Weeping Water](wiki:Weeping_Water) to befriend [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow). According to Lord Bolton, Domeric was then [poisoned](wiki:Poison) by Ramsay.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Now heirless, Roose brought Ramsay to the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

## Culture

The Boltons are known for their historical practice of [flaying](wiki:Flaying) their enemies,(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and they are said to hang the skins of their enemies in a room of the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The family has a reputation for being dishonorable,(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) treacherous,(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) cruel,(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) and cunning.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) According to [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), [House Umber](wiki:House_Umber) has no love for the Boltons.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

Boltons use pink sealing wax for their messages.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) [Ramsay](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), the Bastard of Bolton, has written in blood instead of [maesters](wiki:Maesters)' ink.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

After [the arrest](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark) of Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) responds to [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark)'s calling of the banners to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). A Bolton man knifes a [Cerwyn](wiki:House_Cerwyn) man at the [Smoking Log](wiki:Smoking_Log) in the [winter town](wiki:Winter_town) outside the castle. Roose and [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) demand a command.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

When the [northern](wiki:North) host splits at the [Twins](wiki:Twins), Roose receives his wish, commanding an army of mostly infantry to engage Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Lord Bolton hopes to catch Tywin unawares by forcing a night march, but the northmen are forced to retreat at the [battle on the Green Fork](wiki:Battle_on_the_Green_Fork).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Roose reforms his army at the mouth of the [causeway](wiki:Causeway).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

Robb is able to relieve [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), where he is proclaimed [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) after the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)

### A Clash of Kings

Encamped at the [Twins](wiki:Twins),(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) Roose marries [Walda Frey](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Merrett)), a granddaughter of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Lord Bolton takes the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford) and the [crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads) in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1)

Following orders from Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully), Roose and Ser [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart) march on [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-2) Bolton is able to [seize the fortress](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal) by making a deal with the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions) within, and he is indirectly aided by [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) and [Jaqen H'ghar](wiki:Jaqen_H'ghar).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) At Harrenhal, Roose unwittingly takes the incognito Arya as his cupbearer. He and the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) under Ser [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_Frey) learn of [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy)'s [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) taking much of the north and of the [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister)-[Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) victory over [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater). Roose orders Helman to first [burn Darry](wiki:Siege_of_Darry) and then join [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) in attacking [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) in Robb's name.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

Meanwhile, Roose's bastard son, [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), marries and kills Lady [Donella Hornwood](wiki:Donella_Hornwood), taking the [Hornwood](wiki:Hornwood) lands for himself.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) responds with [fighting in the Hornwood](wiki:Fighting_in_the_Hornwood).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1) Ramsay then leads the Dreadfort men in betraying the northmen under Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) in a [battle at Winterfell](wiki:Battle_at_Winterfell). After dispersing Rodrik's host, Ramsay has his men [burn Winterfell](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell), capturing or slaughtering all within.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### A Storm of Swords

In the aftermath of the sack of Winterfell, Bolton men search the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) in the north for the missing [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) and [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

The northern army sent by Roose into the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) is crushed in the [battle at Duskendale](wiki:Battle_at_Duskendale).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

When Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) is captured by the Brave Companions and brought to Harrenhal, Roose allows him to go on his way if he agrees to place all blame for the loss of his hand on the Brave Companions. Roose sends his captain, [Steelshanks Walton](wiki:Steelshanks_Walton), with two hundred men to secure Jaime's passage back to King's Landing.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1)(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

The same day that Jaime leaves Harrenhal, Roose rides north to attend the wedding of [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully), [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun), to [Roslin Frey](wiki:Roslin_Frey) at the [Twins](wiki:Twins). He watches as his rearguard under Ser [Wylis Manderly](wiki:Wylis_Manderly) is chewed up by Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) in the [fighting at the fords of the Trident](wiki:Fighting_at_the_fords_of_the_Trident). Lord Bolton leaves six hundred men under the command of Ser [Kyle Condon](wiki:Kyle_Condon) and [Ronnel Stout](wiki:Ronnel_Stout) to guard the [Trident](wiki:Trident).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

Roose arrives at the Twins with three thousand foot and five hundred horse, most of whom are Bolton or [Karstark](wiki:House_Karstark) men.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), intends for Roose to command the rearguard during his upcoming campaign against the ironborn-held [Moat Cailin](wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1)

Roose's actions culminate with his personal killing of Robb at the Twins in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), which he planned with [Lame Lothar Frey](wiki:Lothar_Frey) with the approval of Lords [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) and [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) House Bolton usurps House Stark, with Roose being named the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North) by the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Meanwhile, Ramsay Snow is granted a royal writ of legitimacy by King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), becoming Ramsay Bolton.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) Ramsay has been amusing himself with the torture of the captive [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1)

### A Feast for Crows

[Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), the [regent](wiki:Regent) for the young Tommen, relies on House Bolton to hold the north for the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) Her [small council](wiki:Small_council) considers aiding the Boltons against [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy), the self-styled [Kings of the Isles and the North](wiki:Kings_of_the_Isles_and_the_North), if Roose is able to defeat [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

The Boltons' authority is contested by Stannis and several northern lords, and is begrudged by nearly all others.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) Lord Roose's strongest supporters are his relatives of [House Dustin](wiki:House_Dustin) and [House Ryswell](wiki:House_Ryswell).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)

Roose's horse marches north along with two thousand men from the Twins. His passage is secured thanks to the forced efforts of [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), whose will has been broken by Ramsay. At the [siege of Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin), Theon is able to convince its ironborn garrison to surrender, and Ramsay has them flayed alive.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) At [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton) in the north, Roose instructs Ramsay to behave more discreetly, and arranges the marriage of Ramsay to "[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark)", who is actually [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole), in order to secure dynastic legitimacy.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2)

While there, Roose deals with high tensions between the different factions, particularly between the Freys and Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly).(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) Theon manages to escape with Jeyne, much to Ramsay's chagrin.(wiki:House_Bolton.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)
