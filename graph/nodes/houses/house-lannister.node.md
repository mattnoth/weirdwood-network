## Origins

### First Men Kings

The Lannisters suddenly appear as [First Men](wiki:First_Men) in historical records of the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), ruling large portions of the westerlands from [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) just as the [Casterlys](wiki:House_Casterly) vanish from the chronicles. They claim descent from [Lann the Clever](wiki:Lann_the_Clever), the legendary figure who tricked the Casterlys from Casterly Rock.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

According to a semi-canon source, members of lesser branches of the family left Casterly Rock and developed a nearby village into the city of [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), forming [House Lannister of Lannisport](wiki:House_Lannister_of_Lannisport). Meanwhile, the Lannisters of Casterly Rock grew to become [Kings of the Rock](wiki:Kings_of_the_Rock).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-sample_15-0) The first known King of the Rock was [Loreon I Lannister](wiki:Loreon_I_Lannister), although Lann the Clever has posthumously been called by the same title. Loreon gained [House Reyne](wiki:House_Reyne) as his vassals and defeated the [Hooded King](wiki:Hooded_King), [Morgon Banefort](wiki:Morgon_Banefort).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

### Andal Kings

During the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), King [Tybolt Lannister](wiki:Tybolt_Lannister_(king)) initially fought the invading [Andal](wiki:Andal) warlords and adventurers who ventured into the westerlands, but Kings [Tyrion III](wiki:Tyrion_III_Lannister) and [Gerold](wiki:Gerold_II_Lannister) eventually took a policy of arranging marriages between them and the daughters of the local [First Men](wiki:First_Men) houses. They also took Andal sons and daughters as wards and fosterlings at Casterly Rock to prevent betrayals such as those that had happened in the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn). In time, the Lannister kings also wed their children to Andals, and when King [Gerold III](wiki:Gerold_III_Lannister) died without male issue, a council crowned the Andal husband of Gerold's only daughter, Ser [Joffrey Lydden](wiki:Joffrey_Lannister), who took the Lannister name. Thus the Lannisters became an Andal house, though their First Men name lived on.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

[Cerion](wiki:Cerion_Lannister) extended his rule to the [Golden Tooth](wiki:Golden_Tooth), and [Tommen I](wiki:Tommen_I_Lannister) gained [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle). [Lancel I](wiki:Lancel_I_Lannister) and [Lancel IV](wiki:Lancel_IV_Lannister) fell while campaigning against the [Kings of the Reach](wiki:Kings_of_the_Reach) from [House Gardener](wiki:House_Gardener), while [Gerold the Great](wiki:Gerold_Lannister_(king)) raided the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)

[Hagon Hoare](wiki:Hagon_Hoare), [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands), allowed his mother, the dowager queen [Lelia Lannister](wiki:Lelia_Lannister), to be mutilated by the [Shrike](wiki:Shrike). Her nephew, the King of the Rock, began a war which left the Iron Islands impoverished.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

House Lannister possessed an ancestral [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) [greatsword](wiki:Greatsword) called [Brightroar](wiki:Brightroar), but it was lost when King [Tommen II Lannister](wiki:Tommen_II_Lannister) went on a quest to [Valyria](wiki:Valyria) and never returned.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) The Lannisters have been looking for a replacement ever since, though they have not stopped looking for their own lost ancestral sword.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

### Targaryen Conquest

When the [Wars of Conquest](wiki:Wars_of_Conquest) began and [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) swept through [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), [Loren I](wiki:Loren_I_Lannister), King of the Rock, sided with [Mern IX Gardener](wiki:Mern_IX_Gardener), [King of the Reach](wiki:King_of_the_Reach), against the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen). The host of the Two Kings numbered fifty-five thousand men, five thousand of them mounted knights, while the Targaryens had only ten thousand men, many of uncertain loyalty and reliability. The two armies met in the plains of the Reach south of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush). With their smaller force severely weakened and in danger of routing, Aegon and his sisters, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) and [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen), each deployed their three dragons, the only time this happened during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest). Their combined fires scoured the battlefield, immolating more than four thousand men (including King Mern and his heirs) in the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

After he was captured the following day, King Loren the Last bent the knee to Aegon and was allowed to remain Lord of Casterly Rock. Loren rose as the first [Warden of the West](wiki:Warden_of_the_West).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Queen Visenya is said to have been grateful that Loren rode to war instead of seeking refuge in his castle, as Casterly Rock may have withstood even [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

To encourage greater ties throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) brokered marriages between the new vassals sworn to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). The eldest son of Lord [Loren Lannister](wiki:Loren_I_Lannister), for instance, was wed to a girl from [House Redwyne](wiki:House_Redwyne).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

### First Century of Targaryen Rule

While the great wealth of Casterly Rock was untouched by the Targaryen Conquest, the Lannisters were reduced from kings to lords, and they did not have close ties to the new Targaryen kings. The Lannisters had little presence at the royal court for the first century of Targaryen rule, and no Lannister is mentioned as a member of the [small council](wiki:Small_council) until the lead-up to the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). During this first century, the noble houses with the closest political connections to the Targaryen dynasty were the [Velaryons](wiki:House_Velaryon), [Baratheons](wiki:House_Baratheon), [Tullys](wiki:House_Tully), [Hightowers](wiki:House_Hightower), and [Arryns](wiki:House_Arryn).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

By the time of the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), [Lyman Lannister](wiki:Lyman_Lannister) was Lord of Casterly Rock, and was wed to Lady [Jocasta Tarbeck](wiki:Jocasta_Lannister). Lord Lyman resented the Lannisters' lack of influence under the new Targaryen regime, but could do little about it. When the conflict began in [42 AC](wiki:42_AC), Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) and his sister-wife, Princess [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) were in the middle of a royal progress through the westerlands, and were briefly trapped at [Crakehall](wiki:Crakehall) by Poor Fellows, until Lord Lyman offered the couple refuge at Casterly Rock. Lyman refused to surrender them to King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), although he declined to support Aegon's claim militarily. A shrewd ruler, Lyman carefully weighed his actions and played both sides: when young Aegon the Uncrowned eventually raised a rebel army to march against his uncle Maegor, Lyman's [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son Ser [Tyler Hill](wiki:Tyler_Hill) led five hundred men from [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) to join his forces - thus allowing Lyman himself to plausibly deny that he had directly ordered a force of westermen to join the rebels. As it happened, Aegon was slain by Maegor in the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye) in [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), though Tyler Hill survived.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

In the second half of Maegor's reign the surviving [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) rebounded, now led by Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett), leader of Lannisport chapter of the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons). They could no longer face Maegor in open battle but resorted to ambush and harassing his forces. By late [45 AC](wiki:45_AC), Joffrey ruled the hill country north of the [Golden Tooth](wiki:Golden_Tooth) in all but name. Maegor also later sent the son of [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling_(wife_of_Maegor_I)) to be Lyman's ward.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

Throughout all this the Lannisters took no direct action in favor of one side or the other. At the very end of the conflict in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), Maegor's nephew [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) declared against him, and after his brutal reign the Great Houses could finally stand him no longer. First the Velaryons declared for Jaehaerys, rapidly followed by most of the Great Houses including the Lannisters. Abandoned by his supporters and with rebel armies advancing on [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) unopposed, Maegor ran out of options, and was found dead on the Iron Throne by the time the rebels arrived.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2)

Lord Lyman led three hundred knights to King's Landing to attend the famed [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) in [49 AC](wiki:49_AC), a grand event which by extension celebrated [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen)'s succession to the throne and reconciliation with the Great Houses.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) The Lannisters hosted Rhaena and her husband, [Androw Farman](wiki:Androw_Farman), for a time after they were sent away from [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle). Rhaena eventually departed, however, after determining that Lyman and his wife Lady Jocasta Lannister coveted her [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_egg), hoping to become the "second house" in the realm (displacing the Targaryens' current favorites, the Velaryons and Baratheons).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) King Jaehaerys never fully trusted Lyman either, accurately judging that he was an ambitious man who wanted to increase Lannister power by edging his way into the Targaryens' inner circle. At multiple points Jaehaerys therefore passed over Lyman for several royal offices and favors: he decided not to name him [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) in [50 AC](wiki:50_AC), or to name him as the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) in [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), or to consider him as master of coin again in [59 AC](wiki:59_AC) upon the death of [Rego Draz](wiki:Rego_Draz).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Lyman and many of his [bannermen](wiki:Bannermen) died months later in 59 AC from the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) plague.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

In [80 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Jaehaerys once again passed over the Lannisters, this time for a royal marriage between his younger daughter Princess [Daella](wiki:Daella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Jaehaerys_I)) and young [Tymond Lannister](wiki:Tymond_Lannister), the new heir to Casterly Rock. This had more to do with the fact, however, that Daella personally disliked Tymond, for he was a noted womanizer and overly fond of [wine](wiki:Wine). Nonetheless Jaehaerys indulged Daella and let her marry whoever she chose instead of insisting on a political marriage alliance, and ultimately she chose to wed Lord [Rodrik Arryn](wiki:Rodrik_Arryn).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

Twenty years later, Lord Tymond Lannister and three hundred westermen attended the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal). Despite the fact that Jaehaerys never granted the Lannisters any royal favors, like the overwhelming majority of lords at the council they voted in favor of Jaehaerys's preferred candidate [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) (son of his second son) over [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon) (Jaehaerys's grandson through his first son's daughter). It was said that a major factor in the decision for many lords was that male proximity predominate in the inheritance, regardless of their current political allegiance.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Ser [Jason](wiki:Jason_Lannister) and Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), twin knights, wooed [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) during the princess's visit to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) in [112 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), but without success.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)

### The Dance and Regency

As King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen)'s reign continued, the Lannisters aligned with the emerging political faction led by [House Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower), specifically Viserys's second wife Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower). While the Targaryens treated the Hightowers with respect due to their connections to the leadership of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), they were not as influential as the Velaryons or Baratheons and rarely served on the [small council](wiki:Small_council) in the first century of Targaryen rule. Both the Hightowers and Lannisters ruled powerful port cities on the west coast of Westeros, and increasingly came to resent the Velaryons and their allies who were then pre-eminent at the royal court. Over time these two factions coalesced into "the [greens](wiki:Greens)" (the Hightowers, Lannisters, and their allies) led by Queen Alicent, and "the [blacks](wiki:Blacks)" (the Velaryons and their allies) led by Viserys's eldest child, Princess [Rhaenyra](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).

In the third and last decade of Viserys I's rule, the Hightowers came back into power on the small council after the mysterious death of Lord [Lyonel Strong](wiki:Lyonel_Strong) in [120 AC](wiki:120_AC). Alicent's father Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower) was brought back to replace him as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), and he spent the next decade stacking the small council with green loyalists. Among them, Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister) became the new [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

Upon Viserys I's death, a civil war broke out between the two rival branches of House Targaryen, known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). Lord [Jason Lannister](wiki:Jason_Lannister) declared for Alicent's eldest son, [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), while his twin brother Ser Tyland (who had been master of ships) was named as [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) after the removal of Lord [Lyman Beesbury](wiki:Lyman_Beesbury) (the only member of the small council who didn't support Aegon II). Tyland seized the royal treasury, swelled by years of peace and prosperity under Viserys, and divided the Crown's gold into four parts. One part was entrusted to the care of the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos) for safekeeping, another was sent under strong guard to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), and a third went to Oldtown. The remaining wealth was used by the greens for bribes and gifts and hiring [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1)

The bulk of Rhaenyra's supporters in mainland Westeros were in the North, the Vale, and the riverlands, and they began amassing their forces at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) for an eventual strike on [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). To counter this, the main Lannister army invaded the riverlands from the west, while Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen)'s forces from King's Landing invaded the riverlands from the south, hoping to catch their enemy on two fronts. As the war progressed, Lord Jason was killed by [Longleaf the Lionslayer](wiki:Pate_of_Longleaf) in the [Battle of the Red Fork](wiki:Battle_of_the_Red_Fork),(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) and the remaining Lannister host under first Ser [Adrian Tarbeck](wiki:Adrian_Tarbeck) and then Lord [Humfrey Lefford](wiki:Humfrey_Lefford) was then shattered in the [Battle by the Lakeshore](wiki:Battle_by_the_Lakeshore), the bloodiest battle of the civil war.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

After the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing), Daemon and Rhaenyra executed several members of Aegon II's small council, but spared Tyland - because they hoped they could force him to confess where he sent the Crown's treasury. Tyland was given over to the torturers, but despite being blinded and gelded, even after months of torture he refused to give up his secret.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) Rhaenyra's husband, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), suggested giving Casterly Rock to [Hugh Hammer](wiki:Hugh_Hammer), which horrified Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), the [Hand of the Queen](wiki:Hand_of_the_Queen).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1)

With Casterly Rock in disarray, [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), attacked the westerlands to support Rhaenyra's blacks. [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) was sacked by the ironborn, but they were unable to conquer the Rock from Jason's widow, Lady [Johanna Lannister](wiki:Johanna_Lannister), who was ruling on behalf of her four-year-old son, Lord [Loreon Lannister](wiki:Loreon_Lannister).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1)

Near the end of the war, Aegon II retook King's Landing, where they freed the mutilated Tyland - who continued to serve on Aegon II's small council until his death. Many assumed that Tyland would have gone insane from his extensive torture, but surprisingly he retained all his wits and cunning - nor did his blindness affect his shrewd mind. At the end of the war he was sent to the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) to try to hire new sellsword armies, but the final defeat of Aegon II's armies and his death by [poisoning](wiki:Poison) then ended the war.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

Ser Tyland returned to Westeros, and was named the new Hand of the King to Rhaenyra's eldest surviving son, King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), whose execution Tyland had once proposed. Tyland had an odd sense of honor, in that he only felt compelled to obey oaths he had personally sworn, not those made by his ancestors or his allies, but once he had personally sworn an oath he would keep it even to his enemies. Ser Tyland served loyally and ably as Hand early in the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), trying to hold the realm together after the devastation of the war and the harsh winter that followed. Both Tyland and his sister-in-law Lady Johanna (current regent for House Lannister) attended Aegon III's coronation, and his wedding to Aegon II's daughter, Princess [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

Although the Dance of the Dragons had ended with Aegon III on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Dalton refused to make peace with the westerlands. Lady Johanna won [a victory over the ironborn](wiki:Battle_at_Kayce) at [Kayce](wiki:Kayce), but her plan to recover Fair Isle ended with the deaths of Ser [Erwin Lannister](wiki:Erwin_Lannister) and Lords [Prester](wiki:House_Prester) and [Tarbeck](wiki:House_Tarbeck).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake), Tyland's successor as Aegon's Hand, tasked Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) with bringing peace to the west. Dalton was killed by [Tess](wiki:Tess), one of his many salt wives, however, and the ironmen began to retreat. Johanna presented Alyn Oakenfist with a [golden](wiki:Gold) seahorse when his fleet finally arrived in the west, but the admiral declined her suggestion to attack the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

Johanna took advantage of the [struggle for power in the Iron Islands](wiki:Struggle_for_power_in_the_Iron_Islands) by having Ser [Leo Costayne](wiki:Leo_Costayne) invade the archipelago with a Lannister army. In return for conquering the isles for young Lord Loreon, Leo hoped to win Johanna's hand. The Lannister army eventually faltered and Leo was slain on [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk), but hundreds of ironborn ships and villages were destroyed during the invasion. Many ironborn nobles were killed in the campaign, and Johanna had the captured [Rodrik Greyjoy](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy_(son_of_Dalton)) [gelded](wiki:Eunuch) and made him a [fool](wiki:Fool) for Loreon.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

Meanwhile, after the Dance ended in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC) Tyland Lannister was heavily involved in dealing with the outbreak of the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War) in the Free Cities, and the complicated political maneuverings that followed as the multi-sided war spilled over into the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) and involved the fleets of Westeros. Two years into the regency of Aegon III, however, Westeros was further devastated by the outbreak of the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever). While it never spread to the west coasts of the continent, King's Landing was hard hit. Ser Tyland ordered a quarantine and was locked inside the city along with the rest of its inhabitants, where he was one of the last to die from the plague in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC).

### Rebellions and Uprisings

Lord [Damon Lannister](wiki:Damon_Lannister_(lord)) remained loyal to the Targaryens in [196 AC](wiki:196_AC) during the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), but he was defeated by Ser [Quentyn Ball](wiki:Quentyn_Ball) outside the gates of Lannisport.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2)

Damon and his heir, Ser [Tybolt Lannister](wiki:Tybolt_Lannister), participated in the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow). Damon was defeated in the first tilt by Lord [Leo "Longthorn" Tyrell](wiki:Leo_Tyrell_(Longthorn)), while his son defeated his opponent to become a champion.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) Tybolt inherited the lordship from Damon when the latter died of the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

[Gerold Lannister](wiki:Gerold_Lannister) was a suitor to [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber), [Lady of Coldmoat](wiki:Lady_of_Coldmoat), but much preferred to remain at Casterly Rock with his brother Tybolt.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) When Lord [Dagon Greyjoy](wiki:Dagon_Greyjoy) raided the western coast during the early reign of [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), House Lannister built ships to fight him at sea.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-1) Gerold succeeded as lord after the deaths of Lord Tybolt and his daughter, Lady [Cerelle Lannister](wiki:Cerelle_Lannister), under mysterious circumstances, but the new lord became known as Gerold the Golden for his prosperous rule.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

Gerold's second wife, Lady Rohanne, disappeared in [230 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and his twin sons, Ser [Tywald](wiki:Tywald_Lannister) and Ser [Tion](wiki:Tion_Lannister), were killed in the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising) in [233 AC](wiki:233_AC) and the [Fourth Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Fourth_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [236 AC](wiki:236_AC), respectively. Tion's widow, [Ellyn Reyne](wiki:Ellyn_Reyne), bickered with [Jeyne Marbrand](wiki:Jeyne_Marbrand), the wife of Gerold's new heir, [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister), and Ellyn eventually remarried to [Walderan Tarbeck](wiki:Walderan_Tarbeck), [Lord of Tarbeck Hall](wiki:Lord_of_Tarbeck_Hall).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-1)

Tytos became lord after his father Gerold's death in [244 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Although a kind man, Tytos was a weak ruler, and corruption and disloyalty spread among his [bannermen](wiki:Bannermen), including Houses [Reyne](wiki:House_Reyne) and [Tarbeck](wiki:House_Tarbeck). On three occasions King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) sent knights to bring order to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-2) Tytos's younger brother, Ser [Jason Lannister](wiki:Jason_Lannister_(son_of_Gerold)), was killed in the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings) in [260 AC](wiki:260_AC), but Tytos's older sons—[Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), [Kevan](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), and [Tygett](wiki:Tygett_Lannister)—won honor for their House in the war.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-3)

Ser Tywin sought to restore House Lannister to their former glory, so he crushed the [Reyne-Tarbeck revolt](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt) in [261 AC](wiki:261_AC).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-4)

### Tywin

When [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) succeeded to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [262 AC](wiki:262_AC), his friend, the twenty-year-old Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), became the youngest man to serve as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Tywin then became [Lord of Casterly Rock](wiki:Lord_of_Casterly_Rock) after the death of [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) in [267 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-5) Like his grandfather [Gerold](wiki:Gerold_Lannister), Tywin became a skilled steward and ruler.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

When Aerys was held hostage by Lord [Denys Darklyn](wiki:Denys_Darklyn), Tywin's conservative approach took six months to resolve the [Defiance of Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale) in [277 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). This instigated Aerys's paranoia and drove a wedge between the two, along with the king's lusting after [Joanna Lannister](wiki:Joanna_Lannister), Tywin's wife, cousin, and trusted confidante.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1)

Despite Tywin's attempts to ensure a strong legacy, life at Casterly Rock was dysfunctional at best. His twin children, [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) and [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), began an incestuous relationship at an early age.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) Tywin was never a jovial man, but in [273 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) when Joanna died birthing their third child, [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), he became even more stern.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) His brother, [Kevan](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), became his right hand, and their sister, [Genna](wiki:Genna_Lannister), took a maternal role over the children. His second brother, [Tygett](wiki:Tygett_Lannister), died of a [pox](wiki:Pox),(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) and their youngest brother, [Gerion](wiki:Gerion_Lannister), sailed off on a journey to [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), never to be seen again.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

Cersei, while a young maid, visited a [woods witch](wiki:Maegi) called [Maggy the Frog](wiki:Maggy_the_Frog), who shared portents of Cersei's future that planted seeds of her paranoia.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) Cersei possibly murdered her friend [Melara Hetherspoon](wiki:Melara_Hetherspoon), who had witnessed the prophecy.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) Tyrion, when thirteen years old, was out with Jaime when he met and married a girl named [Tysha](wiki:Tysha), a match which was quickly and cruelly ended by their father.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Tywin offered Tyrion to Houses [Martell](wiki:House_Martell), [Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower), [Royce](wiki:House_Royce), [Florent](wiki:House_Florent) and [Tully](wiki:House_Tully), but he was rejected by all.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) Jaime was a promising young knight, dubbed by Ser [Arthur Dayne](wiki:Arthur_Dayne). Tywin was devastated when Jaime joined King Aery's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), breaking off a betrothal to [Lysa Tully](wiki:Lysa_Arryn). Upon this action (which he perceived as Aerys robbing him of his heir),(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) coupled with the Mad King's refusal to wed Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) to Cersei,(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) Tywin resigned the Handship.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-6) This flew in the face of Jaime's plan, which was to be closer to Cersei; he was kept in King's Landing while Cersei returned to Casterly Rock.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

### Robert's Rebellion

The Lannisters stayed neutral for the majority of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). Only after the killing of [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), by [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End), in the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) did Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) bestir himself from [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

Tywin led an army of twelve thousand to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), where he professed loyalty to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-1) Although Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) objected, Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle), a Lannister loyalist, convinced King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) to open the capital's gates. Tywin betrayed the Targaryens, however, and had his host conquer the city for Robert. During the ensuing [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) slew Aerys to avert the Mad King's [wildfire plot](wiki:Wildfire_plot).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) Meanwhile, Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) and Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch), two of Tywin's knights, killed Rhaegar's wife, [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), and children(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) to prove House Lannister's commitment to Robert's cause.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-2) The bodies of Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) and Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) were wrapped in [crimson cloaks](wiki:House_Lannister_guards) to hide their blood.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

Afterwards, Tywin's daughter Cersei was wed to the new king, Robert I. [Tya Lannister](wiki:Tya_Lannister) had been the last Lannister to marry a member of [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon), [Gowen Baratheon](wiki:Gowen_Baratheon), before the marriage of Robert and Cersei.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-1)

### Baratheon Era

The royal marriage and the increasing amount of gold King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) owes to Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) have only increased Lannister power.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) Much of Tywin's energy is directed inward, ensuring all of his relatives do their part to uphold the family name.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)

On behalf of [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) and [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) began [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion) with the [burning of the Lannister fleet](wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) Tywin joined Robert and [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Warden of the West](wiki:Warden_of_the_West), in invading the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands), however, and the rebellion was defeated.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0)

Tywin attended the [tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_Prince_Joffrey's_name_day) in King's Landing. Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) and her children accompanied Tywin during his return to Casterly Rock; en route they were informed of the death of [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), Robert's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) travels to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to ask his childhood friend, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), to succeed Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). ### A Clash of Kings

[Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s brothers, [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) and [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), each contest [Joffrey I Baratheon's](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) right to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), while [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) has been named [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) and [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident). The conflict becomes known as the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). After her ship leaves port, the royal party is caught up in the [riot of King's Landing](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing) provoked by King Joffrey.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_90-0) [Tyrek Lannister](wiki:Tyrek_Lannister) has wed to [Ermesande Hayford](wiki:Ermesande_Hayford) so that the Lannisters can claim [her lands](wiki:House_Hayford),(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) but Tyrek disappears during the riot.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_90-1)

They are nearly killed by lions in the [Great Pit of Daznak](wiki:Great_Pit_of_Daznak) before Queen Daenerys puts an end to it.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_136-0) When their master, [Yezzan zo Qaggaz](wiki:Yezzan_zo_Qaggaz), dies of the [pale mare](wiki:Pale_mare), the trio joins the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons), as Tyrion plans to have [Ben Plumm](wiki:Ben_Plumm) switch sides again and to have the company aid Daenerys.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_137-0)(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_138-0)

Jaime brokers peace at the [siege of Raventree](wiki:Siege_of_Raventree), the last southron stronghold loyal to [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark)'s lost cause. While at [Pennytree](wiki:Pennytree) he unexpectedly meets [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth), who tells him that he must go with her to save [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) from the [Hound](wiki:Hound).(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_139-0)

Kevan and the [small council](wiki:Small_council) try to deal with the resurgent Faith Militant, as well as the [landing of the Golden Company](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company) and Aegon Targaryen in the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands). [Varys](wiki:Varys) and his [little birds](wiki:Little_birds) murder [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) and Kevan in the [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester)'s chambers, however. A supporter of Aegon, Varys hopes the death of the competent Lord Regent will cast suspicion between [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) and [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) and continue the rifts created during Cersei's rule.(wiki:House_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-2)
