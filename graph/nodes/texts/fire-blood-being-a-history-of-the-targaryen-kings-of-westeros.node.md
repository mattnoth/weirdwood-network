## Origins

Only the first volume of [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn)'s work is known to have been completed, covering the reigns of the [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) kings from [Aegon I](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) to [the regency](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) of [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen).(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) The book incorporates the contents of several of the [archmaester](wiki:Archmaester)'s earlier works with only [minor amendments](wiki:Errata_of_history_novellas), such as [The Princess and the Queen](wiki:The_Princess_and_the_Queen,_or,_the_Blacks_and_the_Greens),(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) [The Rogue Prince](wiki:The_Rogue_Prince,_or,_A_King's_Brother),(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and [The Sons of the Dragon](wiki:The_Sons_of_the_Dragon_(Gyldayn)).(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
In the original texts, after completing the first chapter, covering [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), Archmaester Gyldayn sent a copy to Archmaester [Gerold](wiki:Gerold_(archmaester)), perhaps for his commentary and approval. However, this manuscript was lost until Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) discovered it along with other fragments written by Gyldayn among Gerold's papers.(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Yandel included this chapter into his work [The World of Ice & Fire](wiki:The_World_of_Ice_&_Fire_(Yandel)), in which he wrote that he hoped the rest of Gyldayn's masterwork would one day be discovered.(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_glorious_reign.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Yandel's usage of Gyldayn's text was done with the latter's blessing.(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-GyldaynRetcon_2-1)
Gyldayn drew upon many sources for his work. These included official court records(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) and the writings of various [maesters](wiki:Maester), especially the [Grand Maesters](wiki:Grand_Maester), who kept written accounts with varying degrees of diligence. Another key source was the writings of [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Barth](wiki:Barth), the long-time [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
In covering the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Gyldayn most heavily referenced three main sources, [The Reign of King Viserys, First of His Name, and the Dance of the Dragons That Came After](wiki:The_Reign_of_King_Viserys,_First_of_His_Name,_and_the_Dance_of_the_Dragons_That_Came_After) by Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace),(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) [The Dance of the Dragons, A True Telling](wiki:The_Dance_of_the_Dragons,_A_True_Telling) by Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun),(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) and [The Testimony of Mushroom](wiki:The_Testimony_of_Mushroom) as dictated by the court jester [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom). Both Mushroom and Eustace had close ties to the Targaryens and were well-placed to know much of their goings-on, though Gyldayn noted their accounts were often contradictory and filled with salacious rumors and accusations.(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) Munkun, though not an eye-witness himself, drew heavily upon the confessions of the former Grand Maester [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle), who was, as well as interviews and other sources. Gyldayn considered Munkun's history " by far the most detailed and authoritative" and thus referenced him frequently.(wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
