## Origins

Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), borrowed the book from Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle),(wiki:The_Lineages_and_Histories_of_the_Great_Houses_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) but he fell ill the following day(wiki:The_Lineages_and_Histories_of_the_Great_Houses_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) and quickly died.(wiki:The_Lineages_and_Histories_of_the_Great_Houses_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) uses the book while looking into the death of his predecessor as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn).(wiki:The_Lineages_and_Histories_of_the_Great_Houses_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
After reading that historical marriages listed in the book between [Baratheons](wiki:House_Baratheon) and [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) all resulted in black-haired children, Eddard concludes that the children of Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)—[Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), and [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon)—were not actually fathered by King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:The_Lineages_and_Histories_of_the_Great_Houses_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
