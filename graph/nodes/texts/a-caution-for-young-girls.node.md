## Origins

A Caution for Young Girls first appeared in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) around [90 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), forty years after [Coryanne Wylde](wiki:Coryanne_Wylde) was Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen)'s handmaid.(wiki:A_Caution_for_Young_Girls.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Several hundred copies were ordered burned by King [Baelor the Blessed](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) during his reign, but this failed to destroy all of them. Hundreds more have since been recopied, circulating around the [brothels](wiki:Brothel) and other low places of Westeros.(wiki:A_Caution_for_Young_Girls.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
There are several variants of the book in circulation, due to its nature as a book of erotica: most of the population in Westeros is illiterate, and professional book-copiers only serve the elite. [Maesters](wiki:Maester) are strictly trained to reproduce books exactly, while [septons](wiki:Septon) are encouraged to strike out passages considered obscene or offensive. Such professionals would not transcribe erotica, so A Caution for Young Girls was probably copied by various less reliable sources such as expelled drunken septons, failed students from the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), and [mummers](wiki:Mummer). In particular, mummers are deemed the most unreliable, since they add increasingly more lascivious incidents to works they are reproducing.(wiki:A_Caution_for_Young_Girls.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
These inaccurate transmissions and intentional additions had the combined effect of producing different versions which are truly different books, and indeed, do not even share the same title - through all do share the subtitle "A Caution for Young Girls", by which maesters refer to them collectively. The four primary variants are Sins of the Flesh, The High and Low, A Wanton's Tale, and The Wickedness of Men. Sins of the Flesh and The High and Low are apparently the earlier versions, and they are shorter, while A Wanton's Tale and The Wickedness of Men appear to be later versions, especially because they are longer with additions not present in the others.(wiki:A_Caution_for_Young_Girls.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
[Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) used A Caution for Young Girls as a source for his book [Fire & Blood](wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros), for details regarding an incident during the reign of [Jaehaerys the Conciliator](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), as all official sources of the time considered the event too scandalous to remark upon at all.(wiki:A_Caution_for_Young_Girls.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) has the choice of three books aboard ship, including "a well-thumbed tome about the erotic adventures of a young slave girl in a [Lysene](wiki:Lys) [pillow house](wiki:Pillow_house)", which he considers "the worst written but the most engrossing".(wiki:A_Caution_for_Young_Girls.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) It is possible that the book is a variant of A Caution for Young Girls.
