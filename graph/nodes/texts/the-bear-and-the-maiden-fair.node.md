## Origins

The only song [Dunk](wiki:Dunk) knows all the way through is "The Bear and the Maiden Fair".(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) He heard the song performed at the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow) in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), while a dancing bear danced to the music.(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) It was also sung by Lord [Frey](wiki:House_Frey)'s nephew at the [Whitewalls tourney](wiki:Whitewalls_tourney).(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

The song is sung at the [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast) at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

It is sung by [Butterbumps](wiki:Butterbumps) at [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark)'s dinner with [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) and [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell), in order to cover their conversation about King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
[Tom of Sevenstreams](wiki:Tom_of_Sevenstreams) sings it with [Hot Pie](wiki:Hot_Pie) on the ride to the [Inn of the Kneeling Man](wiki:Inn_of_the_Kneeling_Man). When Tom wants someone to sing with, Hot Pie tells him he knows the song about the bear. Tom starts to sing it and Hot Pie joins in lustily, even bouncing in his saddle a little on the rhymes. The astonished [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) thought Hot Pie could only bake, but he has a good voice and sings well.(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Rasos13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
During the grim march back to [Craster's Keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep) after the [fight at the Fist](wiki:Fight_at_the_Fist), [Grenn](wiki:Grenn) tries to encourage [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) to keep marching by telling him to sing a song in his head. Sam knows a hundred songs, but is so terrified he cannot think of any. Grenn suggests "The Bear and the Maiden Fair" and starts to sing it, but Sam begs him not to, as it makes him think of the hideous [wight](wiki:Wight)-bear that had come at them at the [Fist](wiki:Fist_of_the_First_Men).(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
During the [battle near Yunkai](wiki:Battle_near_Yunkai) Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) tells [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) that King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) liked songs that made him laugh. Barristan adds that Robert only sang when he was drunk, and then by that point one of the songs was likely to be "The Bear and the Maiden Fair".(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
[Greatjon Umber](wiki:Jon_Umber) sings "The Bear and the Maiden Fair", drunkenly, at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), at the same time as musicians perform "[Flowers of Spring](wiki:Flowers_of_Spring)". The songs do not sound good together.(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### Feast for Crows[ ]

It is sung by [Dareon](wiki:Dareon) on the [Blackbird](wiki:Blackbird), on his voyage to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) with Samwell Tarly, [Gilly](wiki:Gilly), and [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)).(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
[Ronnet Connington](wiki:Ronnet_Connington) refers to the song at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), when visiting its bear pit to see where [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) had fought with a bear.(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Dance With Dragons[ ]

The song is sung by [Abel](wiki:Abel) for [Barbrey Dustin](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin) during a meal at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). The [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) and some [northmen](wiki:Northmen) join in with the singing, until the noise frightens the [horses](wiki:Horse) kept in [the hall](wiki:Great_Hall_of_Winterfell).(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
At a [slave](wiki:Slave) auction in [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) realizes that Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) is doomed if he is purchased by [Zahrina](wiki:Zahrina). Tyrion manages to get the slave overseer [Nurse](wiki:Nurse) to convince [Yezzan zo Qaggaz](wiki:Yezzan_zo_Qaggaz) to purchase Jorah by lying and telling Nurse that Jorah is part of their show - the bear and the maiden fair. Jorah is the bear, [Penny](wiki:Penny) is the maiden, and Tyrion is the brave knight who rescues her. During their enslavement, Tyrion notes that Jorah has not adapted well. When called upon to play the bear and carry off the maiden fair, he has been sullen and uncooperative, shuffling lifelessly through his paces when he deigns to take part in their [mummery](wiki:Mummer) at all.(wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
