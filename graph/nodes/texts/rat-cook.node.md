## Origins

According to legend, the man who would later be known as the Rat Cook was a simple cook at the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort). He became infamous when he served an [Andal](wiki:Andal) king (identified either as [Tywell II Lannister](wiki:Tywell_II_Lannister), [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock), or [Oswell I Arryn](wiki:Oswell_I_Arryn), [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale))(wiki:Rat_Cook.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) a pie that was made of bacon and, unknown to the king, the king's son. The cook killed the prince in revenge for a wrong the king supposedly did to him. The king was unaware of this, however, as he ate and praised the taste and asked for a second piece. The gods were angered—not because the cook had committed murder, nor because he had made the king a [cannibal](wiki:Cannibalism)—but because the cook had slain a guest beneath his roof. They cursed the cook and transformed him into a massive [rat](wiki:Rat) who was doomed to be unable to eat anything but his own young.(wiki:Rat_Cook.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
According to the story, the Rat Cook is an enormous white rat who still lives in the Nightfort today, and all the other rats that inhabit the Nightfort are his descendants.(wiki:Rat_Cook.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Such is the infamy of the tale that there is a song about the Rat Cook that is still sung in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), despite the fact that this incident supposedly happened hundreds of years before [Aegon's Landing](wiki:Aegon's_Landing). The song is used to represent the repercussions to those who violate [guest right](wiki:Guest_right), the sacred laws of hospitality.(wiki:Rat_Cook.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

At the [wedding of Ramsay Bolton and Arya Stark](wiki:Wedding_of_Ramsay_Bolton_and_Arya_Stark) at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) asks [Abel the Bard](wiki:Mance_Rayder) to sing about the Rat Cook after he serves [three large pork pies](wiki:Frey_Pies/Theories).(wiki:Rat_Cook.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
