## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

At [Acorn Hall](wiki:Acorn_Hall), Lady [Ravella Smallwood](wiki:Ravella_Smallwood) accuses [Tom of Sevenstreams](wiki:Tom_of_Sevenstreams) of singing "Oh, Lay My Sweet Lass Down in the Grass" to seduce every milkmaid in the area. He defensively corrects her, saying he actually sings "[Let Me Drink Your Beauty](wiki:Let_Me_Drink_Your_Beauty)", which women are always pleased to hear.(wiki:Oh,_Lay_My_Sweet_Lass_Down_in_the_Grass.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
The lyrics for both of these songs are unknown, but not long afterwards,(wiki:Oh,_Lay_My_Sweet_Lass_Down_in_the_Grass.cite_ref-2) Tom sings what may be(wiki:Oh,_Lay_My_Sweet_Lass_Down_in_the_Grass.cite_ref-3) the lyrics of "Oh, Lay My Sweet Lass Down in the Grass":
My featherbed is deep and soft, and there I'll lay you down, I'll dress you all in yellow silk, and on your head a crown. For you shall be my lady love, and I shall be your lord. I'll always keep you warm and safe, and guard you with my sword. And how she smiled and how she laughed, the maiden of the tree. She spun away and said to him, no featherbed for me. I'll wear a gown of golden leaves, and bind my hair with grass, But you can be my forest love, and me your forest lass.
