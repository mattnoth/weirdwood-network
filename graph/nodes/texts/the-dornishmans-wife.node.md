## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

When [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) meets with [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) in the [Frostfangs](wiki:Frostfangs) under pretext of joining his campaign, the [King-beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-beyond-the-Wall) is singing "The Dornishman's Wife".(wiki:The_Dornishman's_Wife.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
The song comes to Jon's mind during the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall).(wiki:The_Dornishman's_Wife.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

The song comes to Jon's mind again while he attends the apparent burning of Mance by [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre).(wiki:The_Dornishman's_Wife.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Later, when Melisandre reveals to Jon that Mance is still alive, the former King announces: "I've sung my songs, fought my battles, drunk summer wine, tasted the Dornishman's wife. A man should die the way he's lived. For me that's steel in hand."(wiki:The_Dornishman's_Wife.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
After Mance arrives at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) as a bard under the assumed name Abel, he performs several songs throughout the [wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Ramsay_Bolton_and_Arya_Stark) of [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) and "[Arya Stark](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)" and subsequent feasts at the castle. When he plays "The Dornishman's Wife", Mance switches the lyrics to a "northman's daughter" instead, to the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton)' amusement.(wiki:The_Dornishman's_Wife.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
