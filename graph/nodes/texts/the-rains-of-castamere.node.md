## Origins

[Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister), [Lord of Casterly Rock](wiki:Lord_of_Casterly_Rock), was perceived as weak by his own vassals, and [House Reyne](wiki:House_Reyne) and [House Tarbeck](wiki:House_Tarbeck) had long taken advantage of this. Tytos's eldest son and heir, Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), was determined to restore [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) dominance. In [261 AC](wiki:261_AC), Tywin sent [ravens](wiki:Raven) to [Tarbeck Hall](wiki:Tarbeck_Hall) and [Castamere](wiki:Castamere), demanding that they answer for their crimes at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock). As Tywin had expected, Lord [Roger](wiki:Roger_Reyne) and Ser [Reynard Reyne](wiki:Reynard_Reyne), as well Lord [Walderan](wiki:Walderan_Tarbeck) and Lady [Ellyn Tarbeck](wiki:Ellyn_Reyne), [rose in rebellion](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt), renouncing their fealty to Casterly Rock. Without the permission of his father, Tywin marched against the upstart vassals with three thousand [men-at-arms](wiki:Men-at-arms) and crossbowmen and five hundred [knights](wiki:Knight). The Lannister army first approached Tarbeck Hall, where Lady Ellyn Tarbeck closed her gates and sent ravens to Castamere to summon her brothers, Roger and Reynard Reyne.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_2-0) According to a semi-canon source, when Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) approached under a peace banner to demand House Tarbeck's surrender, Lady Ellyn laughed at him, saying that her brothers were also [lions](wiki:Lion), with claws as sharp as his.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-sample_3-0) Tywin had [siege engines](wiki:Catapult) throw a huge boulder onto Tarbeck Hall, bringing it crashing down upon Lady Ellyn, and then had the castle put to the torch.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_2-1)
Lord Roger Reyne, the "Red Lion of Castamere", arrived with his army in time to witness Tarbeck Hall aflame. Roger launched a surprise attack, but fell back due to the Lannister army's overwhelming numbers. Injured, Roger fled back to Castamere, where his younger brother Reynard led their people into the great mines beneath the castle, believing that Tywin could not possibly fight his way inside. When his people were all inside, Reynard sent terms for surrender to Tywin, stating that the Reynes would be loyal vassals in return for Tywin's brothers serving within Castamere as [hostages](wiki:Hostage). Tywin ignored Reynard's terms and instead ordered his men to seal the entrances of the mines. When all entrances were blocked with tons of stone, earth, and soil, leaving no way in or out, Tywin had his men dam a nearby stream and divert it to the nearest mine entrance. Water easily found its way through the tiny gaps in the rubble that blocked the mouth of the entrance. Lannister men stationed at the most distant entrances claimed they could hear faint screaming and shouting, but by daybreak, there was nothing but silence. None of the three hundred men, women, and children within ever emerged from the mines again.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_2-2)
Afterwards, singers created a "rather gloomy" song to celebrate Tywin's victory and the obliteration of Houses Reyne and Tarbeck.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The lyrics heavily reference the fact that the [sigils](wiki:Sigil) of House Lannister and House Reyne are both [lions](wiki:Lion), though House Lannister's is [golden](wiki:Gold) and House Reyne's is red.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Lady Ellyn Tarbeck's words describing her brothers as lions, with "claws as long and sharp as yours",(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-sample_3-1) were attributed to the "proud lord" of the song, Roger Reyne the Red Lion. Furthermore, the title and lyrics are a play on words, as the rains (Reynes) weep over the empty halls of the slaughtered Reynes of Castamere.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Some years later, [Lord Farman](wiki:House_Farman) of [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle), a Lannister vassal, became belligerent. Lord Tywin sent him an envoy with a message, but instead of a letter, he brought a [lute](wiki:Lute). Once "The Rains of Castamere" was played in his hall, Lord Farman reconsidered his position.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) hears that [House Westerling](wiki:House_Westerling), Lannister vassals, have joined with rebel King [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) after he married [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling). Tyrion wonders at this, thinking that the [Crag](wiki:Crag) is not far from the shattered remains of Tarbeck Hall and Castamere, and that the Westerlings might have heeded the lesson of the song. His father, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), replies that the Westerlings are well aware of Castamere.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
When Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) is captured by the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions), he threatens to sing a verse of "The Rains of Castamere" as a warning that his father Tywin will not react well to the [turncloak](wiki:Turncloak) [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat).(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
After the [battle at the burning septry](wiki:Battle_at_the_burning_septry), the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) shelter in the [septry](wiki:Septry)'s brewhouse while it storms. [Tom of Sevenstreams](wiki:Tom_of_Sevenstreams) sings several songs about rain, including "The Rains of Castamere".(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
The [wedding](wiki:Wedding) of Lord [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) and [Roslin Frey](wiki:Roslin_Frey) is held at the [Twins](wiki:Twins), attended by King Robb Stark, [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), his mother [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), most of his vassals, and his entire army. The wedding is meant to to symbolize the restoration of the alliance between [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) and [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey), broken earlier when Robb married Jeyne Westerling instead of his Frey [betrothed](wiki:Betrothed).(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) However, the Starks are unaware that Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) and Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) have plotted a betrayal with Tywin Lannister.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Lord Bolton and [Lothar Frey](wiki:Lothar_Frey) have planned the wedding and betrayal in detail, including the song selection and placing crossbowmen among the musicians.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) After the [bedding](wiki:Bedding) ceremony, the musicians begin playing "The Rains of Castamere" as a signal for the forces of House Frey and [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) to turn on the Starks and [Tullys](wiki:House_Tully) and their bannermen and slaughter them. Lady Catelyn Stark recognizes the tune and is alarmed, but it is already far too late; as the song begins, so does the bloodbath that ends Robb's life and her own, as well as many of his vassals.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Outside the Twins, [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) hears the song playing as she struggles to reach her family and sees the massacre of the soldiers outside.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) With the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding) and the death of Robb Stark, the rebellions of the [north](wiki:North) and the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) against the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) end.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
The song is performed by both [Hamish the Harper](wiki:Hamish_the_Harper) and [Collio Quaynis](wiki:Collio_Quaynis) to honor Lord Tywin Lannister at the [wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) and Queen [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell). When [Alaric of Eysen](wiki:Alaric_of_Eysen) is about to perform, Lady [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell) sarcastically comments she hopes for another rendition of the song to remember how it goes.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

At the [Water Gardens](wiki:Water_Gardens) in [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), [Obara Sand](wiki:Obara_Sand), angered at the death of her father [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), wishes she could kill Tywin Lannister while making him sing "The Rains of Castamere".(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
[Marillion](wiki:Marillion) sings the song while imprisoned in a [sky cell](wiki:Sky_cell) at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
[Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell) sarcastically comments she is disappointed that the [Blue Bard](wiki:Blue_Bard) does not sing "The Rains of Castamere" at the [wedding feast](wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell) of Margaery and King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
At the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), Ser Jaime Lannister has Tom of Sevenstreams play the song to the captive Lord [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully), to remind him what would happen to his family if he does not surrender [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Tyrion Lannister describes the song "[Hands of Gold](wiki:Hands_of_Gold)" to [Penny](wiki:Penny), saying that it does not compare to "The Rains of Castamere", but some parts were good.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
Tyrion, a captive [slave](wiki:Slave) outside [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), sees another slave playing music on a [bone](wiki:Bone) [flute](wiki:Flute), and almost wants to ask him if he knows "The Rains of Castamere".(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Radwd47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Later, Tyrion bleakly wonders if he can play the song himself with his slave collar's bells.(wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
