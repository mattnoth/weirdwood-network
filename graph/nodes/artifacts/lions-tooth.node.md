## Appearances & Description

Lion's Tooth is a [longsword](wiki:Longsword) proportioned to fit a twelve-year-old boy, made of gleaming blue steel, castle-forged and double-edged, with a leather grip and a [gold](wiki:Gold) lion's-head pommel.(wiki:Lion's_Tooth.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

While out riding with [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) comes across [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) and a common boy, [Mycah](wiki:Mycah), playing at swords with wooden sticks. He threatens Mycah and cuts his cheek, which leads Arya to attack the prince with her stick. Joffrey then threatens Arya with his sword, slashing at her, until [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria_(direwolf)) bites him to protect her. Arya throws Lion's Tooth into the [Trident](wiki:Trident).(wiki:Lion's_Tooth.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
When Arya is questioned at [Darry](wiki:Darry), [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) laughs at Joffrey being disarmed by the girl, as well as at the sword's name.(wiki:Lion's_Tooth.cite_ref-Ragot16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Now king, Joffrey receives a replacement sword, [Hearteater](wiki:Hearteater). Sansa recalls how Arya had thrown Lion's Tooth in the river, and she hopes that [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) will do the same with Joffrey's new sword.(wiki:Lion's_Tooth.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Within the [hollow hill](wiki:Hollow_hill), Arya misremembers the sword's name as "Lion's Paw".(wiki:Lion's_Tooth.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Lion's_Tooth.cite_ref-6)
Joffrey receives [Widow's Wail](wiki:Widow's_Wail) as a present before his wedding with [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), and Sansa remembers his previous swords, Lion's Tooth and Hearteater.(wiki:Lion's_Tooth.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
