## Origins

### Aegon the Conqueror[ ]

In the aftermath of [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) used Blackfyre to kill [Qhorin Volmark](wiki:Qhorin_Volmark) during the [invasion of the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) During the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), Aegon killed the champion of [House Toland](wiki:House_Toland) with Blackfyre, not knowing it was actually the fool of [Ghost Hill](wiki:Ghost_Hill).(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
Even though Aegon the Conqueror was a great warrior with Blackfyre,(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) his sister, Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), feared the blade was not enough to protect the new king from danger. Aegon was convinced to allow the formation of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) in [10 AC](wiki:10_AC).(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
Aegon had his elder son, [Aenys Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), handle Blackfyre from time to time, but the prince was no more than an adequate swordsman. Aegon bestowed [knighthood](wiki:Knight) on his younger son, [Maegor Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), during a tourney at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) in [28 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), dubbing the prince with Blackfyre.(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
After Aegon died at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) in [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), the king's body was burned in a funeral pyre with his hands folded over the hilt of Blackfyre. Prince Maegor then retrieved Blackfyre, which had been made darker in color by the flames but was otherwise unharmed.(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

### Aenys and Maegor[ ]

Aegon the Conqueror was succeeded by King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), but the new king granted Blackfyre to his more martial younger brother, Prince [Maegor Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). Maegor henceforth only wielded Blackfyre and kept his previous [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword, [Dark Sister](wiki:Dark_Sister), at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). When Maegor refused to put aside his second wife, [Alys Harroway](wiki:Alys_Harroway), the polygamous prince chose exile in [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) and refused Aenys' request to leave Blackfyre behind.(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)
Maegor flew to Dragonstone to claim the crown after Aenys died in [42 AC](wiki:42_AC). When Grand Maester [Gawen](wiki:Gawen) insisted that the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) should pass to Aenys's son, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), Maegor beheaded Gawen with Blackfyre. Maegor wielded Blackfyre during his [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) against the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons), with the new king slaying Ser [Damon Morrigen](wiki:Damon_Morrigen) and Ser [Willam the Wanderer](wiki:Willam_the_Wanderer). Maegor later used Blackfyre to kill [Myros](wiki:Myros) when the Grand Maester spoke out against Maegor marrying [Tyanna of Pentos](wiki:Tyanna_of_Pentos). In [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), seven prominent Warrior's Sons who refused to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) were beheaded by Maegor with Blackfyre. After Tyanna confessed to [poisoning](wiki:Poison) the children of Maegor's other queens, the king used Blackfyre to cut out her heart and feed it to his dogs.(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)
When the realm began to unite against Maegor the Cruel, Queen [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) stole the sword from the sleeping Maegor at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) for Aenys's surviving son, Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), who had announced his own claim at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). Maegor was eventually found dead on the Iron Throne.(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)

### Jaehaerys I[ ]

King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) bore Blackfyre on his swordbelt.(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Jaehaerys used the sword to personally slay the rebel [Borys Baratheon](wiki:Borys_Baratheon) during the [Third Dornish War](wiki:Third_Dornish_War).(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Jaehaerys also killed Ser [Braxton Beesbury](wiki:Braxton_Beesbury) in a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) after Stinger had seduced the king's daughter, Princess [Saera](wiki:Saera_Targaryen).(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### Aegon II[ ]

[Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen)'s possession of Blackfyre enhanced his legitimacy against his rival, [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
When Aegon returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) near the end of the Targaryen civil war, he allowed the condemned [Trystane Truefyre](wiki:Trystane_Truefyre) to be granted knighthood before his death. Ser [Marston Waters](wiki:Marston_Waters) dubbed the youth Ser Trystane Fyre, and Ser [Alfred Broome](wiki:Alfred_Broome) then beheaded the newly-made knight with Blackfyre.(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### The Young Dragon[ ]

[Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen) wielded Blackfyre during his [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne), and he was eventually killed by [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) with the sword in hand.(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
While it is unknown when Blackfyre was returned to the possession of the Targaryens, it had been returned at least by the reign of King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen). However, as, according to Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey), Blackfyre had been wielded by every Targaryen king since Aegon's Conquest,(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) the sword might have already been returned during the reigns of [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) or [Viserys II Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen).

### House Blackfyre[ ]

In [182 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) chose to bestow Blackfyre on his [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), a born warrior, instead of his scholarly legitimate son, the future [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen). Ser Daemon, who had been known by the bastard surname Waters until then, took the name "Blackfyre" thereafter, creating [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre).(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Some felt that the Conqueror's sword symbolized the monarchy, so the gift was one of the seeds from which rebellion grew.(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-17)
Daemon I Blackfyre wielded the sword during the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [196 AC](wiki:196_AC), and he fought an epic duel with Ser [Gwayne Corbray](wiki:Gwayne_Corbray) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) at the [Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field), pitted against Gwayne's own Valyrian blade, [Lady Forlorn](wiki:Lady_Forlorn). [Aemon Blackfyre](wiki:Aemon_Blackfyre) briefly wielded the sword after the [Raven's Teeth](wiki:Raven's_Teeth) slew Daemon, but Aemon was also killed in the battle.(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Daemon's half brother, [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers), known as Bittersteel, attempted to rally the rebels with Blackfyre in hand, but Bittersteel withdrew after dueling another half brother, [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), a rival called Bloodraven.(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)
With Daemon and his twin sons dead at the Redgrass Field, Bittersteel took Blackfyre with him in exile to the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), where he eventually founded the sellsword [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company). Aegor refused to give the Valyrian sword to [Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre) for the ill-fated [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion), and Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell) doubted Daemon's potential after learning he lacked Blackfyre.(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
Possession of Blackfyre after Bittersteel is unknown, although [maesters](wiki:Maesters) consider the [Blackfyre Pretenders](wiki:Blackfyre_Pretenders) to have ended with the death of [Maelys Blackfyre](wiki:Maelys_Blackfyre).(wiki:Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
