## Appearances & Description

The Moon Door is a narrow door that stands between two slender pillars in the [High Hall](wiki:High_Hall) of the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). It is made of [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) and has a crescent [moon](wiki:Moon) carved into it.(wiki:Moon_Door.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Three heavy [bronze](wiki:Bronze) bars hold in place the Moon Door, which opens inward. The door opens into thin air, with the waycastle [Sky](wiki:Sky) visible six hundred feet below on the side of the [Giant's Lance](wiki:Giant's_Lance).(wiki:Moon_Door.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) The wind can be heard near the door even when it is closed.(wiki:Moon_Door.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Moon_Door.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

## Origins

In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), at the start of the reign of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), [Jonos Arryn](wiki:Jonos_Arryn) rose in rebellion. He deposed and imprisoned his own brother, Lord [Ronnel Arryn](wiki:Ronnel_Arryn_(king)), and declared himself [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale). Jonos executed Ronnel by send him flying out of the Moon Door.(wiki:Moon_Door.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) When Prince [Maegor Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) arrived at the Eyrie on his [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), the [Eyrie's garrison](wiki:House_Arryn_guards) seized Jonos and pushed him through the Moon Door to share his brother's fate.(wiki:Moon_Door.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Young Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) wants [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), who has been accused by Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) of having murdered Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), to fly out the Moon Door. Lysa has the door opened to intimidate the prisoner. In response, Tyrion publicly demands [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat).(wiki:Moon_Door.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), Lysa's new husband, kisses her niece, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), in the Eyrie's garden; unbeknownst to them, the act is seen by Lysa. The Lady of the Eyrie later accuses Sansa of having tried to seduce Petyr, and she drags the girl to the Moon Door and has her open it. As Lysa threatens to force Sansa through the door, Petyr enters the High Hall and interrupts the fighting. Petyr promises never to leave his wife's side again, Lysa falls into his arms, and Sansa hugs a nearby pillar for safety. Petyr tells Lysa he has only ever loved one woman, her sister [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), and then shoves Lysa out the Moon Door to her death. When guards arrive, Petyr blames Lysa's singer, [Marillion](wiki:Marillion).(wiki:Moon_Door.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

When Lord [Nestor Royce](wiki:Nestor_Royce), Ser [Albar Royce](wiki:Albar_Royce), and Ser [Marwyn Belmore](wiki:Marwyn_Belmore) come to the Eyrie to inquire about Lysa's death, Sansa—disguised as Petyr's bastard, "Alayne Stone"—repeats the lie that Marillion pushed Lysa to her death. Marwyn thinks Marillion should be pushed through the door as well, but Petyr has the singer returned to the [sky cells](wiki:Sky_cells).(wiki:Moon_Door.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
