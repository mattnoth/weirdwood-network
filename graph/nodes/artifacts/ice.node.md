## Appearances & Description

Ice is a greatsword as wide across as a man's hand(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) and six feet long.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The blade is taller than an adolescent [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark)(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) and near as tall as Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne).(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Its [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) is dark and smoky(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) with deep rippling.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Origins

The name "Ice" is a legacy from the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes) and predates the current sword. About four hundred years before King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s reign, the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) greatsword was spell-forged in [Valyria](wiki:Valyria) and acquired by [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark), who named it after that legacy.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
In [50 AC](wiki:50_AC) Lord [Walton Stark](wiki:Walton_Stark_(son_of_Brandon)) used Ice to execute Ser [Olyver Bracken](wiki:Olyver_Bracken), who had rebelled against the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) used Ice to behead Ser [Gyles Belgrave](wiki:Gyles_Belgrave) and Lord [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf).(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) uses Ice whenever to condemns a man to death.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Eddard has allowed his bastard son, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), to handle Ice; Jon has sometimes dreamed of being regarded as a Stark and being given Ice.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) uses Ice to execute [Gared](wiki:Gared), a deserter from the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), at a [holdfast in the hills](wiki:Holdfast_in_the_hills).(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Ned then cleans the blade with a swatch of oiled leather in the [godswood of Winterfell](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
After Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) is attacked by [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria_(direwolf)), the [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf) of [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark), Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) insists that a direwolf be killed. Since Nymeria has gone missing, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) approves of Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne), the [King's Justice](wiki:King's_Justice), killing [Lady](wiki:Lady), the direwolf of [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark). Eddard instead volunteers to kill his daughter's direwolf with Ice, and he orders [Jory Cassel](wiki:Jory_Cassel) to have men bring Lady's bones to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to be buried.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
[Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), gifts [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) with [Longclaw](wiki:Longclaw), a Valyrian steel sword which reminds the youth of Ice.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
After Eddard is arrested for treason in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Ilyn wears Ice in a scabbard on his back.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Joffrey, now king after Robert's death, orders Ilyn to behead Ned at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor). A screaming Sansa is present in the royal retinue, but in the assembled crowd [Yoren](wiki:Yoren) prevents her sister Arya from witnessing the beheading.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) While imprisoned in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), Sansa has a nightmare of Ilyn coming to behead her with Ice.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

When [Gendry](wiki:Gendry) refers to the [red comet](wiki:Red_comet) as the Red Sword, Arya thinks of Ice and her father's blood.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Racok1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
Eddard's heir [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark), now the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), sends Ser [Cleos Frey](wiki:Cleos_Frey) as an envoy during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). Robb has Cleos ask for the return of Ice, but [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), the acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), informs Cleos that the greatsword will only be returned when Robb makes peace with the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
During the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Sansa is scared when she sees Ilyn in the Queen's Ballroom of [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) with the highborn women of the capital.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Queen Cersei claims the knight is there for protection,(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) but she later reveals she wants Ilyn to execute Sansa and herself if [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) is victorious in the battle.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Cersei has Ilyn execute three servants who attempt to flee the castle. When he returns, Sansa notices blood drying on Ice, as Ilyn has not yet cleaned the blade.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Stannis is defeated and forced to retreat.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) When Joffrey receives prisoners in the throne room after the battle, the king orders Ilyn to behead a [Florent](wiki:House_Florent) bastard who remains loyal to Stannis.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) takes Ice from Ilyn and has [Tobho Mott](wiki:Tobho_Mott) re-forge the greatsword into two Valyrian steel longswords.(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) When Tyrion sees Ilyn with a new silver greatsword, he regrets not having sent Ice back to Robb,(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) who was killed in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
King Joffrey receives one of the new longswords from his grandfather Tywin as a [wedding gift](wiki:Purple_Wedding) and names it [Widow's Wail](wiki:Widow's_Wail).(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Tywin gives the other new blade to his elder son, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), but Jaime rejects the gift. Jaime instead gives the sword to [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) and, sending her to find the missing Sansa, asks her to call the sword [Oathkeeper](wiki:Oathkeeper).(wiki:Ice.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
