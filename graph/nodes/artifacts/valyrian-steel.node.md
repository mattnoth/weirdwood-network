## Appearances & Description

The properties of Valyrian steel are the result of both folding [iron](wiki:Iron) many times to balance and remove impurities, and the use of unknown [spells](wiki:Magic), giving unnatural strength to the resulting steel.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) [Bloodmagic](wiki:Bloodmagic) and/or [fire magic](wiki:Fire_magic) presumably plays a role in spells used to make Valyrian steel, as all Valyrian sorcery came from blood or fire.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
Valyrian steel blades are lighter, stronger, harder, and sharper than even the best castle-forged steel.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Nothing holds an edge like Valyrian steel, therefore swords hundreds of years old are sharp as the day they were forged.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) The blades feature distinctive rippled patterns (similar to real-world Damascus steel), the mark of steel that has been folded back on itself many thousands of times.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Most Valyrian steel is dark grey in color, almost appearing black.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) No common fire, not even [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame), can damage Valyrian steel.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

## Origins

Valyrian steel was manufactured in the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold), spell-forged with the assistance of [dragons](wiki:Dragon) and [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame).(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) The costly material became even more expensive when the method of making new Valyrian steel was lost with the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), and existing weapons became highly treasured and extremely rare.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-valyria_22-0) In all the world, only the [Free City](wiki:Free_City) of [Qohor](wiki:Qohor) has preserved the art of reworking Valyrian steel.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) The master smiths of Qohor jealously guard their secrets, the [spells](wiki:Spells) needed to reforge Valyrian steel without losing its strength or unsurpassed ability to hold an edge.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)
[Maester](wiki:Maester) [Pol](wiki:Pol) spent many years of residence in Qohor, investigating the secrets of their blacksmiths for his treatise on Qohorik metalworking. He was thrice publicly whipped, and cast out for making too many inquiries. The final time, his hand was also cut off, per the allegation he had stolen a Valyrian steel blade. According to Pol, the true reason for his final exile was his discovery of [blood sacrifices](wiki:Bloodmagic)—including that of infant [slaves](wiki:Slavery)—which the Qohorik smiths use in their efforts to produce a steel equal to the original Valyrian steel.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)
There may be thousands of Valyrian steel blades remaining in the [known world](wiki:Known_world), but in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) there are only 227 such weapons according to Archmaester [Thurgood](wiki:Thurgood)'s [Inventories](wiki:Inventories), some of which have since been lost or have disappeared from recorded history.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Most Valyrian steel blades in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) are treasured heirlooms of [noble houses](wiki:Nobility), each with its own name and storied history, and even impoverished lesser houses are unwilling to give them up.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4) Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) attempted to purchase Valyrian steel swords from poorer houses on at least three occasions, but was refused each time.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5)
After the death of King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) in [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), the king's body was [burned in a funeral pyre](wiki:House_Targaryen), with his hands folded over the hilt of his Valyrian steel sword, [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre). Blackfyre's blade was darkened by the dragon-lit flames, but the sword was otherwise unharmed.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) has [Tobho Mott](wiki:Tobho_Mott), a [Qohorik](wiki:Qohor) master blacksmith who knows the [spells](wiki:Spells) needed to rework Valyrian steel, reforge [Ice](wiki:Ice) into two new Valyrian blades. Tobho Mott attempts to tint the swords crimson, for the color of House Lannister, but the metal absorbs the color, resulting in ripples of nearly-black grey, and red almost as dark as the grey.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6) The first sword, [Widow's Wail](wiki:Widow's_Wail), is given to King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) as a wedding gift by his grandfather, Tywin. Joffrey chooses its name from the shouted suggestion of an unnamed guest.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Following Joffrey's death at his [wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding), ownership of Widow's Wail passes to his younger brother, King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-30)
Tywin gives the second blade to his son, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister). Jaime then gives the sword to [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) to use in her quest to locate [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), and he asks her to name it [Oathkeeper](wiki:Oathkeeper).(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

When looking in the Night's Watch's archives at Castle Black for a way to combat the [Others](wiki:Others), [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) reads about the [last hero](wiki:Last_hero) slaying them with a blade of [dragonsteel](wiki:Dragonsteel), which he and Jon Snow think might be Valyrian steel.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)
Daggers of Valyrian steel are among the treasures which [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) gives to [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) captains during the [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk).(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)
When Brienne is captured by the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners), they use the sword's appearance as evidence that Brienne serves [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister), and [Lady Stoneheart](wiki:Lady_Stoneheart) says it should be better named "Oathbreaker". She demands that Brienne take up the sword to keep her oath to [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) and slay Jaime Lannister, or be hanged. Brienne initially refuses, but when she is being hanged and sees her friends dying, she screams "sword".(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-34)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Tormund Giantsbane](wiki:Tormund_Giantsbane) says that fighting the Others is impossible, and asks Jon Snow if his sword can "cut cold". However, he admits the true test must come in battle.(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Radwd60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)
During the [siege of Astapor](wiki:Siege_of_Astapor), the sellsword [Caggo](wiki:Caggo) fights with a Valyrian steel [arakh](wiki:Arakh).(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Radwd60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1)

### The Winds of Winter[ ]

[Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North), is seen by his captive brother, [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), in a suit of black scale armor which Aeron believes to be Valyrian steel. Aeron Damphair believes it would have cost a kingdom even before the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria).(wiki:Valyrian_steel.cite_ref-Forsaken_15-1)
