## Origins

Brightroar came into the possession of the [Kings of the Rock](wiki:Kings_of_the_Rock) from [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) in the century before the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), and it is said that the weight in [gold](wiki:Gold) they paid for it would have been enough to raise an army.(wiki:Brightroar.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)
[Lancel IV Lannister](wiki:Lancel_IV_Lannister) is said to have beheaded the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) king [Harrald Halfdrowned](wiki:Harrald_Halfdrowned) with Brightroar,(wiki:Brightroar.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) and wielded his [Valyrian](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword against Ser [Wilbert Osgrey](wiki:Wilbert_Osgrey).(wiki:Brightroar.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) However, it was probably a common steel sword of the same name, which has been conflated with the Valyrian blade.(wiki:Brightroar.cite_ref-6)
Brightroar was lost little more than a century after it was acquired, when King [Tommen II Lannister](wiki:Tommen_II_Lannister) carried it with him when he sailed with his great fleet to ruined [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), with the intention of plundering the wealth and [sorcery](wiki:Sorcery) he was sure still remained. The fleet never returned, nor Tommen, nor Brightroar.(wiki:Brightroar.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
House Lannister has been searching for a replacement ever since Brightroar's disappearance. Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) attempted to buy a replacement from lesser, poorer houses on at least three occasions, but was always rebuffed.(wiki:Brightroar.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Tywin's brother [Gerion](wiki:Gerion_Lannister) went in search of the sword circa [291 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), but he never returned from Valyria.(wiki:Brightroar.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
