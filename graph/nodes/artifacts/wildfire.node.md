## Appearances & Description

Wildfire is a murky green liquid.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Once set aflame, the wildfire will burn with dark, emerald and jade green flames.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rasos5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Cold will thicken liquid wildfire, but heating it will make the liquid flow.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)
Wildfire is considered a treacherous substance.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) The [pyromancers](wiki:Pyromancer) claim the only things that burn hotter than wildfire are [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame), the fires beneath the earth, and the summer sun.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Wildfire cannot be quenched by water, but can be smothered by sand.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) Once it takes fire, it will burn until it is exhausted,(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) and in large amounts, beyond the control of men.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Even a thin layer of wildfire will burn for an hour.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) Wildfire can burn on water,(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and it seeps into most materials, like cloth, wood, leather, and [steel](wiki:Steel), which will then go up in flames.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8) Clay pottery jars are used to contain wildfire, but it eventually seeps into that material as well.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)
Aging impacts the characteristics of wildfire.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10) While it is implied that new wildfire can still somewhat be controlled, as claimed by the alchemists,(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) old wildfire is "fickle"; any flame or spark can set it off. Too much heat—such as being exposed to sunlight for even a short time—could cause a jar to take fire on its own. Once the fire begins within the jar, the heat will make the liquid wildfire expand violently. Jars of such aged wildfire, if stored together, will go off in a chain reaction.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11) Older jars of wildfire are transported from place to place only by night, in carts filled with sand to lessen any jostling, and then sealed in [wax](wiki:Wax) and placed in rooms pumped full of water.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-12)

## Origins

In [174 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) tried to conquer [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), using seven wooden "[dragons](wiki:Dragons_(mechanical))", siege engines created by the pyromancers, fitted with pumps that shot jets of wildfire. The attempt was a complete failure, as these "dragons" went up in flames in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood) en route to Dorne. Hundreds of men died, a quarter of the kingswood burned, and Aegon never spoke of Dorne again.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
During the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness) ([209](wiki:209_AC)–[210 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), so many in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) died so quickly that there was no time to bury them, and the bodies were placed in the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit). When the corpse piles reached ten feet deep, the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), commanded the pyromancers to burn them with wildfire. The light of the fires shone through the windows of the Dragonpit, making it look like it did when dragons lived there. At night, the green glow of wildfire could be seen throughout the city.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) A quarter of the city went up in flames along with the corpses, but there was nothing else to be done.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
In [232 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Prince [Aerion Targaryen](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen) drank wildfire, believing it would transform him into a [dragon](wiki:Dragon), and died screaming.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
Wildfire may have been involved in the [tragedy at Summerhall](wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall) of [259 AC](wiki:259_AC), as "wild fire" is mentioned by Maester [Corso](wiki:Corso).(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
By the end of his reign, King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) became fascinated with wildfire.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) The pyromancers became a regular fixture at court. By [280 AC](wiki:280_AC), Aerys was executing [traitors](wiki:Traitor), murderers, and plotters by burning, rather than hanging or beheading them.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) In [282 AC](wiki:282_AC), when King's Landing was struck by a massive blizzard, Aerys charged his pyromancers to drive off [winter](wiki:Winter) with their magic. Huge amounts of wildfire burned along the walls of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) for a month.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
After the defeat of the royal army at the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells) in [283 AC](wiki:283_AC), Aerys realized that [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) was the greatest threat to his rule and to all of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). Aerys commanded his pyromancers [to place caches of wildfire all over King's Landing](wiki:Wildfire_plot), as the king was willing to burn the whole city rather than letting the rebels take it.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) When his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Qarlton Chelsted](wiki:Qarlton_Chelsted), discovered the plan, he tried to reason with the king, without success. Finally, Chelsted resigned his office, and Aerys had him dipped in wildfire and burned him alive, and made the pyromancer [Rossart](wiki:Rossart) his new Hand.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) The plot was stopped when Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) slew King Aerys, as well as the pyromancers involved, Wisdoms Rossart, [Garigus](wiki:Garigus), and [Belis](wiki:Belis). The only person remaining alive who knew of the plot was Ser Jaime.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)
Remnants of King Aerys II's wildfire plot were discovered at various times during the reign of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). In [298 AC](wiki:298_AC), two hundred jars were discovered in a storeroom beneath the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), and safely removed by the pyromancers, though they had no idea how the jars came to be there.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-17)
The [red priest](wiki:Red_priest) [Thoros](wiki:Thoros) of [Myr](wiki:Myr), when battling in [tourney](wiki:Tourney) [melees](wiki:Melee), would dip his sword in wildfire and fight with a flaming sword,(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) despite the damage it did to the metal.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Thoros also bore his wildfire-flaming sword when he was the first over the wall at the [siege of](wiki:Siege_of_Pyke) [Pyke](wiki:Pyke), during [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion) in [289 AC](wiki:289_AC).(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)
At the [tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_Prince_Joffrey's_name_day) in [298 AC](wiki:298_AC), Thoros was defeated by Lord [Yohn Royce](wiki:Yohn_Royce) with a common mace, after his sword's wildfire guttered out.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Thoros of Myr wins the melee at the [Hand's Tourney](wiki:Hand's_Tourney), his flaming sword frightening the [horses](wiki:Horse) of the other competitors.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

The [Alchemists' Guild](wiki:Alchemists'_Guild) pledges ten thousand jars of wildfire to [Queen Regent](wiki:Queen_Regent) [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) for the defense of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)
[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) for [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon), learns of the properties of wildfire from Wisdom [Hallyne](wiki:Hallyne) at the Guildhall. He also learns that the pyromancers have hundred of aged wildfire pots from the era of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen). There are men training to defend the city with [spitfires](wiki:Armament), and Tyrion has them practice with pots filled with green paint, dismissing anyone who spatters. After the men have mastered loading and firing, they are to use pots of lamp oil and practice at lighting and firing flaming jars.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-18)
According to [Quaithe](wiki:Quaithe), the [firemage](wiki:Firemage) that [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) sees in [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) previously employed wildfire as part of his act, but due to the birth of her [dragons](wiki:Dragon), now he can use true fire magic.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)
One of Lord [Rossart](wiki:Rossart)'s caches of wildfire, containing more than three hundred jars, is found below the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit), when a client of the [whores](wiki:Whore) working there falls through a patch of rotted floor into an underground cellar. The man is so drunk that he mistakes the jars for [wine](wiki:Wine), drinks some, and dies.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)
Hallyne notes to Tyrion that the Guild is producing much more wildfire much faster than expected, and wonders if there could be any dragons around to make the alchemists' spells so much more efficient.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)
[Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) prepares to attack King's Landing via [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay) and the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush). Ser [Imry Florent](wiki:Imry_Florent), admiral of his fleet, warns his men to expect the city to be defended with wildfire, but assures them that the defenders will soon run out.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)
Tyrion Lannister uses the thousands of jars of wildfire provided by the pyromancers to defend King's Landing during the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater). Spitfires rain wildfire on the fleet, though that has little effect. However, Tyrion has filled an old, rotting hulk of a [ship](wiki:Ship) with the old jars of wildfire, and when it is rammed by the [Swordfish](wiki:Swordfish_(ship)), it explodes violently, setting nearly all of Stannis's fleet aflame.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) Other hulks filled with the aged wildfire also explode, until the entire mouth of the river becomes an inferno. Hundreds of ships and thousands of men burn.(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)(wiki:Wildfire.cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)
