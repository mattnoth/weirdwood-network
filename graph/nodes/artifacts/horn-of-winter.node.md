## Origins

Thousands of years ago,(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) [Joramun](wiki:Joramun), a [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall), supposedly blew the horn and woke [giants](wiki:Giants) from the earth.(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) [Free folk](wiki:Free_folk) believe that blowing the horn can bring down the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Rasos30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Some [maesters](wiki:Maesters) are skeptical of the horn's existence.(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_wildlings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte) tells [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) that [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) and the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) have been searching for the Horn of Winter along the [Milkwater](wiki:Milkwater).(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) They open graves of heroes and kings, but are unable to find the horn.(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Rasos30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
During a parley amidst the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall), Mance shows [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) a large warhorn eight feet long.(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) It is black in color(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) with gold bands and engraven with [runes](wiki:Runes) of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men).(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) It is said to be a thousand years old(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) and may have come from an [aurochs](wiki:Aurochs).(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)
While some free folk want Mance to bring down the [Wall](wiki:Wall) with the horn, Mance resists for fear that nothing could then stop the [Others](wiki:Others). Mance plans to offer the horn in exchange for letting his people pass through the Wall. If the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) refuses, [Tormund Giantsbane](wiki:Tormund) is to sound the Horn at dawn three days hence. Mance is interrupted by the arrival of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s knights, however, and the free folk host is defeated.(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

The warhorn, found in a glacier in the [Frostfangs](wiki:Frostfangs),(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) is burned by [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) when she has "Mance", actually a glamoured [Rattleshirt](wiki:Rattleshirt),(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) put to death by fire. The horn bursts into green and yellow flame and its [runes](wiki:Runes) seem to briefly shimmer in the air. The [queen's men](wiki:Queen's_men) then heave the horn into the fire pit.(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)
Later, as the free folk pass through the Wall, Tormund mentions to Jon that Mance never found the Horn of Winter, instead claiming the horn burned by Melisandre as Joramun's because of its size.(wiki:Horn_of_Winter.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
