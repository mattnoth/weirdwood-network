## Origins

The name "Lady Forlorn" is a legacy from the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals), and predates the current Valyrian steel sword. [Robar II Royce](wiki:Robar_II_Royce), the [High King](wiki:High_King_of_the_Vale,_the_Fingers_and_the_Mountains_of_the_Moon) of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) of the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), claimed the first Lady Forlorn (which was not Valyrian steel)(wiki:Lady_Forlorn.cite_ref-7) after slaying [Qyle Corbray](wiki:Qyle_Corbray), the [King of the Fingers](wiki:King_of_the_Fingers) and an [Andal](wiki:Andals) leader. Robar wielded Lady Forlorn during the [Battle of the Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_the_Seven_Stars), using it to kill [Torgold Tollett](wiki:Torgold_Tollett) and a decoy dressed as Ser [Artys Arryn](wiki:Artys_I_Arryn). The [Corbrays](wiki:House_Corbray) claim that Ser [Jaime Corbray](wiki:Jaime_Corbray) slew Robar in the battle, allowing them to reclaim the first Lady Forlorn.(wiki:Lady_Forlorn.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
At some point the Corbrays acquired a [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword, which they named Lady Forlorn in honor of the ancient sword. Lord [Qarl Corbray](wiki:Qarl_Corbray) used the new blade to kill Ser [Davos Darklyn](wiki:Davos_Darklyn) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) during the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye) in [43 AC](wiki:43_AC).(wiki:Lady_Forlorn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Qarl threatened Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) with Lady Forlorn when Rogar suggested replacing King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) with Princess [Aerea Targaryen](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) in [50 AC](wiki:50_AC).(wiki:Lady_Forlorn.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
Ser [Corwyn Corbray](wiki:Corwyn_Corbray), the brother of Lord [Leowyn Corbray](wiki:Leowyn_Corbray), wielded Lady Forlorn during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Lady_Forlorn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Corwyn, one of the [regents for Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), was slain by a crossbowman at [Runestone](wiki:Runestone) after drawing Lady Forlorn on Lord [Gunthor Royce](wiki:Gunthor_Royce) in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC).(wiki:Lady_Forlorn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_-_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
At the time of the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), Lady Forlorn was wielded by Ser [Gwayne Corbray](wiki:Gwayne_Corbray) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). In [196 AC](wiki:196_AC) during the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field), Gwayne fought [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), who wielded the Valyrian sword [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre). Eventually, after nearly an hour of fighting Daemon defeated Gwayne.(wiki:Lady_Forlorn.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
[Lord Corbray](wiki:Lord_Corbray_(father_of_Lyonel)) wielded Lady Forlorn during the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) in [283 AC](wiki:283_AC). When Lord Corbray was wounded, his younger son, Ser [Lyn](wiki:Lyn_Corbray), took up the sword and killed the man who had wounded his father. Lyn then led the charge against the [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) and killed the already-wounded Prince [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell) of the Kingsguard. Because of this feat, when their lord father died, the sword was left to Lyn instead of his elder brother, [Lyonel](wiki:Lyonel_Corbray). Although he is now [Lord of Heart's Home](wiki:Lord_of_Heart's_Home), Lyonel still feels cheated of his birthright.(wiki:Lady_Forlorn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

During Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish)'s meeting with the [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant) at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), Ser [Lyn Corbray](wiki:Lyn_Corbray) threatens Ser [Lothor Brune](wiki:Lothor_Brune) with Lady Forlorn. Petyr uses the aggression to demand concessions from the Lords Declarant. After they leave, Petyr informs [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) that Lyn is secretly working on his behalf.(wiki:Lady_Forlorn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
