## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

The mummers of the Ship intend to put on a performance of [Seven Drunken Oarsmen](wiki:Seven_Drunken_Oarsmen) as an answer to the [Blue Lantern](wiki:Blue_Lantern)'s performance of [The Lord of the Woeful Countenance](wiki:The_Lord_of_the_Woeful_Countenance).(wiki:Ship_(playhouse).cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark), disguised as the street urchin and fish peddler Cat of the Canals, makes friends with the mummers of the Ship. The mummers show Cat how a hero stands, and they teach her the speeches from [The Song of the Rhoyne](wiki:The_Song_of_the_Rhoyne), [The Conqueror's Two Wives](wiki:The_Conqueror's_Two_Wives), and [The Merchant's Lusty Lady](wiki:The_Merchant's_Lusty_Lady). [Quill](wiki:Quill), the man who writes bawdy farces for the Ship, offers to teach Cat how a woman kisses, but [Tagganaro](wiki:Tagganaro) hits him with a codfish and puts an end to that.(wiki:Ship_(playhouse).cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
[Joss the Gloom](wiki:Joss_the_Gloom) tells Cat that [Quence](wiki:Quence) found [Allaquo](wiki:Allaquo) in bed with [Sloey](wiki:Sloey). The two men fought with mummer swords and both left the Ship, leaving the performance of Seven Drunken Oarsmen without two oarsmen.(wiki:Ship_(playhouse).cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

After becoming the blind beggar Beth, Arya avoids the Ship and other places where Cat had been well-known.(wiki:Ship_(playhouse).cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
