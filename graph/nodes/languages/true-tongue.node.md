## Origins

The True Tongue is said either to have originated or drawn inspiration from the sounds of the natural world that the children of the forest were surrounded by, such as wind through leaves, rain on water, or stones in a brook.(wiki:True_Tongue.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) The songs of the children were said to sound as beautiful as they were,(wiki:True_Tongue.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) with their voices as pure as winter air.(wiki:True_Tongue.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
In his [Unnatural History](wiki:Unnatural_History), Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth) theorized that the children could speak with [ravens](wiki:Raven) and teach them words, and that the children taught this skill to the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) of antiquity so that the birds could transport messages over great distances. This would thus have been the origin of [maesters](wiki:Maesters) sending written messages by raven, though the scholars can now only communicate on a rudimentary level with the birds. Most maesters disbelieve Barth's theory, however.(wiki:True_Tongue.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
Humans are unable to speak the True Tongue,(wiki:True_Tongue.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) but an ancient ballad tells the tale of how [Brandon the Builder](wiki:Brandon_the_Builder) was able to learn to understand the language while seeking the children's help to build the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:True_Tongue.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) This account, recorded in the book [Winter's Kings](wiki:Winter's_Kings,_or_the_Legends_and_Lineages_of_the_Starks_of_Winterfell), is one of the only fragments thought to have survived from the time of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) that records what the songs of the children were about.(wiki:True_Tongue.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)
The children called [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) the "Empty Land".(wiki:True_Tongue.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Thus the word "Dorne" may have its origin in the True Tongue or it may be a derivitive made up by the First Men who were unable to speak the True Tongue properly. However, it may also be entirely unrelated to the True Tongue and instead be a word in the Old Tongue of the First Men.

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) hears [Coldhands](wiki:Coldhands) murmur a blessing in a strange tongue when the ranger slits the throat of his fallen elk.(wiki:True_Tongue.cite_ref-Radwd13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Within the [cave of the three-eyed crow](wiki:Cave_of_the_three-eyed_crow), Bran hears [Leaf](wiki:Leaf) and other [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) sing in the True Tongue.(wiki:True_Tongue.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Bran learns that [ravens](wiki:Raven) can speak the language as well.(wiki:True_Tongue.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

## Quotes

Maester [Childer](wiki:Childer)'s [Winter's Kings, or the Legends and Lineages of the Starks of Winterfell](wiki:Winter's_Kings,_or_the_Legends_and_Lineages_of_the_Starks_of_Winterfell) contains a part of a ballad alleged to tell of the time [Brandon the Builder](wiki:Brandon_the_Builder) sought the aid of the children while raising the [Wall](wiki:Wall).
