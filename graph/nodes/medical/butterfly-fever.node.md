## Origins

[Ghiscari](wiki:Ghiscari) seized [Naath](wiki:Naath) thrice in the times of the [Old Empire](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis). The [Valyrians](wiki:Valyrian) erected a fort, a company of [Volantene](wiki:Volantis) adventurers built a trade town, and corsairs of the [Basilisk Isles](wiki:Basilisk_Isles) have landed on Naath countless times. All of these invaders fell to the plague, however.(wiki:Butterfly_fever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_naath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Likewise, when [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria)'s [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar) landed on the island, they too fell to the disease, even though they had been welcomed with open arms by the Naathi.(wiki:Butterfly_fever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
Corsairs learned that the chances of dying of butterfly fever are low as long as they do not remain on the islands for more than a few hours, and lower still if they land at night, as the butterflies that carry it are day creatures. Thus, slavers now often attack Naath during the dark of night, forcing the Naathi inland.(wiki:Butterfly_fever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_naath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
