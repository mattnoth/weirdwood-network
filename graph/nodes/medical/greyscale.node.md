## Origins

Prince [Garin the Great](wiki:Garin_the_Great) of the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar) went to war against [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) and [Valyria](wiki:Valyria) in the [Second Spice War](wiki:Second_Spice_War). He was captured in battle, however, and hung in a cage at [Chroyane](wiki:Chroyane) to see the enslavement of his people. Using [water magic](wiki:Water_magic), Garin is said to have called [a curse](wiki:Garin's_Curse) upon the conquerors, entreating [Mother Rhoyne](wiki:Mother_Rhoyne) to avenge her children. That night the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne) flooded out of season and a thick fog fell upon the river, and the Valyrian conquerors began to die of greyscale. To this day, the [Sorrows](wiki:Sorrows) is known for its foul fogs and waters, and the ruins of Chroyane are haunted by [stone men](wiki:Stone_men), people suffering from the later stages of greyscale.(wiki:Greyscale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Greyscale.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)
The eldest son of [Qhorwyn Hoare](wiki:Qhorwyn_Hoare), [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands), died of greyscale.(wiki:Greyscale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
Septa [Maegelle Targaryen](wiki:Maegelle_Targaryen) treated children afflicted with greyscale and died of the disease herself, with her arms and legs turned to stone.(wiki:Greyscale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Greyscale.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
[Harlon Greyjoy](wiki:Harlon_Greyjoy) was isolated in a tower because of his greyscale.(wiki:Greyscale.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

## Disease Progression

Tyrion's POV on approaching the Bridge of Dream provides the clearest in-text account of the disease's stages:

> "The mortal form of greyscale began in the extremities, he knew: a tingling in a fingertip, a toenail turning black, a loss of feeling. As the numbness crept into the hand, or stole past the foot and up the leg, the flesh stiffened and grew cold and the victim's skin took on a greyish hue, resembling stone. He had heard it said that there were three good cures for greyscale: axe and sword and cleaver. Hacking off afflicted parts did sometimes stop the spread of the disease, Tyrion knew, but not always. Many a man had sacrificed one arm or foot, only to find the other going grey. Once that happened, hope was gone. Blindness was common when the stone reached the face. In the final stages the curse turned inward, to muscles, bones, and inner organs."

— Tyrion's POV, ADWD Tyrion V (`sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-tyrion-05.md:125`)

> [Jon Connington](jon-connington)'s death-clock — the greyscale he contracts pulling Tyrion from the Rhoyne during the stone-men attack: "The nail on his middle finger had turned as black as jet, he saw, and the grey had crept up almost to the first knuckle... Death, he knew, but slow. I still have time."

— ADWD, The Lost Lord (`sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-lost-lord-01.md:237`)
