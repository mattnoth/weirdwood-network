## Origins

At the end of [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), a [maester](wiki:Maester) and several women died on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) from a mysterious epidemic, that save for the maester seemed to only attack women. The first sign was a bloody stool, followed by a terrible cramping in the gut. Lord [Rego Draz](wiki:Rego_Draz) recognized the symptoms as those of the tears of Lys, and realized that the experienced maester had to die first so that the poison would not be recognized. The poisoner was [Androw Farman](wiki:Androw_Farman), jealous of his wife [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I))'s friendships with her ladies.(wiki:Tears_of_Lys.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
In [135 AC](wiki:135_AC), King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) was sharing a quiet dinner with his young queen, [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon), as well as his friend and [food taster](wiki:Food_taster) [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair), when Gaemon collapsed from belly cramps and Daenaera also suffered from stomach pains. [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) gave Daenaera a powerful purgative, which probably saved her life, but he came too late for Gaemon, who died within the hour. Munkun determined that the poison had been baked into the crust of the apple tarts, and identified it as the tears of Lys. While seven of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s kitchen staff confessed under [torture](wiki:Torture) to attempting to poison the king, none could say where they got the poison, nor could they correctly identify what dish had been poisoned, so the confessions were dismissed by the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan). Later, Thaddeus Rowan himself was arrested, and tortured until he falsely confessed to plotting with the [Lyseni](wiki:Lys) [Rogares](wiki:House_Rogare) to poison both King Aegon and Queen Daenaera, to replace them with Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) and his wife [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare). However, as King Aegon was known to not eat sweets, evidence pointed towards a frame of the Rogares by [another culprit](wiki:Unwin_Peake) seeking the elimination of Queen Daenaera only.(wiki:Tears_of_Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

The [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) of King [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_Baratheon), Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), dies from what his [maester](wiki:Maester), [Colemon](wiki:Colemon), believes was a chill on the stomach.(wiki:Tears_of_Lys.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Tears_of_Lys.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) However, Jon's widow, Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), sends a message to her sister [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) alleging he was killed by Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister).(wiki:Tears_of_Lys.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) [Varys](wiki:Varys) tells [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) that Jon Arryn was poisoned with the tears of Lys.(wiki:Tears_of_Lys.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
When the [small council](wiki:Small_council) considers assassinating [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), Varys suggests using the tears of Lys.(wiki:Tears_of_Lys.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) sees the tears of Lys in [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle)'s collection of [medicines](wiki:Medicine) and [poisons](wiki:Poison).(wiki:Tears_of_Lys.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Pycelle confesses to Tyrion that Colemon had been purging Jon Arryn in hopes of curing the [illness](wiki:Illness), but that he sent Colemon away because he believed Queen Cersei wanted Jon dead. However, Pycelle swears he was not the one who poisoned Lord Arryn, and thinks it must have been Jon's squire [Hugh](wiki:Hugh_of_the_Vale).(wiki:Tears_of_Lys.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

During [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister)'s trial for the death of King [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) claims that Tyrion stole a sample of the tears of Lys, as well as other poisons from Pycelle's collection.(wiki:Tears_of_Lys.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
[Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) learns that [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) put the tears of Lys in the [wine](wiki:Wine) of her husband, Jon Arryn, at the behest of her lover [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish).(wiki:Tears_of_Lys.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

In the [Faceless Men](wiki:Faceless_Men)'s [House of Black and White](wiki:House_of_Black_and_White) in [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), the [waif](wiki:Waif) teaches [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) about the properties of the tears of Lys.(wiki:Tears_of_Lys.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
