## Origins

After being exiled from the [Reach](wiki:Reach) "a thousand years" before [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) was awarded the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den) by [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and tasked with defending the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife). As the city of [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) developed under the Manderlys' leadership, they built the New Castle, a new seat inspired by [Dunstonbury](wiki:Dunstonbury), their lost home in southern [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). The Wolf's Den is now used as a prison.(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) held court in White Harbor three times during his [royal progresses](wiki:Royal_progress).(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Lord [Theomore Manderly](wiki:Theomore_Manderly) hosted [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) in [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), providing a welcoming feast for the queen. In the small [tourney](wiki:Tourney) which was held in Alysanne's honor, among the fighters was a skilled [wildling](wiki:Wildling) girl who had been captured [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall) and given to one of Theomore's [household knights](wiki:Household_knight) for fostering.(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Alysanne also held one of her [women's courts](wiki:Women's_courts) in the [Merman's Court](wiki:Merman's_Court).(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

In [131 AC](wiki:131_AC) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) was received by Lord [Desmond Manderly](wiki:Desmond_Manderly). To join the Manderlys' to the cause of the [blacks](wiki:Blacks), they agreed that Desmond's youngest daughter would wed Prince [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon) once the civil war ended.(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

## Appearances & Description

The New Castle is reminiscent of [Dunstonbury](wiki:Dunstonbury), the [Manderlys'](wiki:House_Manderly) seat from when they lived along the [Mander](wiki:Mander) in the [Reach](wiki:Reach).(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) The pale castle is built atop a hill rising above [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor)'s white walls, and the merman sigil of House Manderly flies from its towers. There is a clear view of both of the city's harbors from the hilltop.(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

The broad, stepped [Castle Stair](wiki:Castle_Stair) is a white stone street that runs from the ancient [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den) up the hill to the New Castle.(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) There is a passage beneath the Castle Stair which secretly connects the two castles.(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

The Manderlys decorate their hallways with faded banners, broken shields and rusted swords from ancient victories, and wooden figures from the prows of ships.(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) The [Merman's Court](wiki:Merman's_Court) is the great hall where the Manderlys hold court. Two [mermen](wiki:Mermen) made of [marble](wiki:Marble) stand outside the hall's doors.(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

The large and airy chambers of the castle are finely furnished.(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) The room which secretly leads to the Castle Stair passage has wooden beams and walls of lath and plaster. It contains a [Myrish](wiki:Myr) carpet on the floor, beeswax candles on a table, and a sheepskin map of the [north](wiki:North) hanging on a wall.(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

Lord [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) visits the New Castle to gain the support of [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) for King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon). [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly), [Lord of White Harbor](wiki:Lord_of_White_Harbor), publicly rejects Davos in the [Merman's Court](wiki:Merman's_Court), however, and has Davos sent to the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den).(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

A feast is held in the Merman's Court to celebrate the safe return of Wyman's heir, Ser [Wylis Manderly](wiki:Wylis_Manderly). Meanwhile, [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) releases Davos from imprisonment in the Wolf's Den and leads him through the secret passage underneath the [Castle Stair](wiki:Castle_Stair). Excusing himself from the rest of the court to use the privy, Wyman instead secretly meets with Davos and Robett in the chamber connecting to the secret passage. Wyman reveals to Davos that he is plotting against the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) and the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey). Robett brings [Wex Pyke](wiki:Wex_Pyke) into the room, and Wyman tasks Davos with finding [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark).(wiki:New_Castle.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
