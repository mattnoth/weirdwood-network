## Origins

The waterfall [receives its name](wiki:Namesake) from [Alyssa Arryn](wiki:Alyssa_Arryn), an ancient member of [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn). According to legend, Alyssa saw her husband, her brothers, and her children killed in front of her, but she did not shed a tear. As a consequence, after her own death, the [gods](wiki:Gods) decreed that she would not know rest until her tears reached the earth of the Vale of Arryn, where all those she had loved were buried. Per the legend, for six thousand years, no drop of the waterfall has ever reached the valley below.(wiki:Alyssa's_Tears.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Alyssa's_Tears.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf742_3-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

[Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) enters the Vale through the Bloody Gate, and sees Alyssa's Tears flowing down the Giant's Lance in the distance.(wiki:Alyssa's_Tears.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Later, in the Eyrie, Catelyn looks at the waterfall from the window of her bedchamber, and thinks of the legend of Alyssa Arryn.(wiki:Alyssa's_Tears.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

From the balcony of [Alayne Stone](wiki:Sansa_Stark)'s apartments in the Eyrie's [Maiden's Tower](wiki:Maiden's_Tower), she can see icicles hanging from the precipice where Alyssa's Tears usually flows. She recalls hearing the sound of the waterfall when she first arrived at the Eyrie, but by mid-autumn it has frozen. [Gretchel](wiki:Gretchel) has told her it will stay silent until spring.(wiki:Alyssa's_Tears.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
