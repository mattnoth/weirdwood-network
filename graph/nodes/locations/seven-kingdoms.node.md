## Origins

### Wars of Conquest

The continent of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) was once divided among a multitude of monarchs in the [Age of the Hundred Kingdoms](wiki:Age_of_the_Hundred_Kingdoms), but over time the land was consolidated into seven major realms.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), and his sisters, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) and [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen), invaded the continent, with Aegon demanding the submission of the mainland's monarchs.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

[Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest) began with [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) landing at the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), and Aegon was crowned [King of All Westeros](wiki:King_of_All_Westeros) at the [Aegonfort](wiki:Aegonfort). Aegon then extinguished [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare) and [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) with the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal), ending the [Kings of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:Kings_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers). Aegon granted the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) to [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully). Meanwhile, [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon), the [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King), was killed in the [Last Storm](wiki:Last_Storm) by [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon) in the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands), with [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon) replacing [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

At the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire), the Targaryen siblings used their [dragons](wiki:Dragon)—[Balerion](wiki:Balerion), [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), and [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes)—to defeat the armies of the [Two Kings](wiki:Two_Kings). [Mern IX](wiki:Mern_IX_Gardener), [King of the Reach](wiki:King_of_the_Reach), and his family from [House Gardener](wiki:House_Gardener) were extinguished in the [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame), while [Loren I](wiki:Loren_I_Lannister), [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock), surrendered the next day. Aegon granted [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) and the [Reach](wiki:Reach) to [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell), and he allowed the defeated [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) to retain their rule in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands). Aegon then met the host of [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), at the [Trident](wiki:Trident). Aware of Harrenhal's fate, Torrhen knelt to Aegon instead of engaging in a pitched battle.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

Visenya flew to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), where she achieved the submission of [Ronnel](wiki:Ronnel_Arryn_(king)), the last [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale) from [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn). Rhaenys was sent south to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), but [Meria Martell](wiki:Meria_Martell), [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne), refused to join Aegon's realm.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

The [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon's_Conquest)) advised Lord [Manfred Hightower](wiki:Manfred_Hightower) to welcome Aegon to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), and the new king's reign is regarded by [maesters](wiki:Maesters) as having begun with his coronation in that city as [King of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men](wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men), [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms), and [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7)

The [Sistermen's Rebellion](wiki:Sistermen's_Rebellion) in the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters) was quickly defeated by Visenya and Ser [Warrick Manderly](wiki:Warrick_Manderly), and Aegon led an [invasion of the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands) to put an end to the civil war among the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn). In [2 AC](wiki:2_AC), Aegon allowed [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy) to rule the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Since Dorne had refused to submit during Aegon's Conquest, the Targaryens began the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War) in [4 AC](wiki:4_AC). Queen Rhaenys and Meraxes were killed at the [Hellholt](wiki:Hellholt) in the bloody war, and Orys was mutilated by [House Wyl](wiki:House_Wyl). Despite the burning of the Dornish castles with dragonflame, the Dornish refused to surrender. After the passing of Meria in [13 AC](wiki:13_AC), Aegon finally agreed to a peace with Prince [Nymor Martell](wiki:Nymor_Martell), with Dorne remaining independent.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

### Iron Throne

Instead of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) or [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) made the new city of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) the capital of his kingdom, with the swords of his defeated foes made into the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) The [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) were created out of territory near the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) and along [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay).

Aegon encouraged included nobles and their children from throughout the land to serve at court, and the Targaryens encouraged marriages between disparate families in an effort to unite the formerly-independent realms. Aegon the Conqueror spent much of his reign learning about the peculiarities of each province during his many royal progresses, and he often left alone the laws and customs already in place.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)

### Aenys and Maegor

Soon after ascending the throne, King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) was faced with the rebellions of [Jonos Arryn](wiki:Jonos_Arryn) in the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), [Harren the Red](wiki:Harren_the_Red) in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), [Lodos the Twice-Drowned](wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned) in the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands). Lord [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon) helped suppress the [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Aenys_I)) in the [Second Dornish War](wiki:Second_Dornish_War).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

The [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) began during the reign of Aenys I and continued through the reign of his brother, [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). Aenys's heir, [Aegon the Uncrowned](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), was defeated by Maegor in the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye). Most of the realm eventually turned against Maegor the Cruel, however, and the king was found dead on the Iron Throne.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2)

### Jaehaerys the Conciliator

The uprising of the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) finally ended after the ascension of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) In [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Jaehaerys and his advisers began work on the first unified code of law for the realm; Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth) contributed greatly to the [Great Code](wiki:Great_Code).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2) A terrible case of the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) spread through the realm in [59](wiki:59_AC)–[60 AC](wiki:60_AC).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) Construction of the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) began in [62 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) during Jaehaerys's reign.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-1) The Old King dealt with another [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Jaehaerys_I)) in the [Third Dornish War](wiki:Third_Dornish_War) in [61 AC](wiki:61_AC),(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-2) and then defeated the [Fourth Dornish War](wiki:Fourth_Dornish_War) in [83 AC](wiki:83_AC).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-3)

More than a thousand lords traveled to [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) for the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC), during which Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) was chosen to be Jaehaerys's heir.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragons_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

Most [archmaesters](wiki:Archmaester) believe that the population of the realm doubled during the reign of the Old King.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-3)

### The Dance of the Dragons

Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), the younger brother of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), began his [war for the Stepstones](wiki:War_for_the_Stepstones) in [106 AC](wiki:106_AC).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragons_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-1)

The death of Viserys in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC) began the civil war known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). Throughout the realm, the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) of [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) battled the [greens](wiki:Greens) of [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). Both claimants died during the war, which ended with [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), Rhaenyra's son, sitting the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), continued to occupy parts of the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) after the war ended, and a [succession struggle](wiki:Iron_Islands_succession_struggle) broke out in the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) after his death.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)

The [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) struck the realm in [132](wiki:132_AC)–[133 AC](wiki:133_AC) during the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-1) The last [dragon](wiki:Dragon) of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) died in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) during Aegon's reign, so he became known as the Dragonbane.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)

### The Young Dragon and Baelor the Blessed

King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen) began his [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne) in [157 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and he achieved the [Submission of Sunspear](wiki:Submission_of_Sunspear) the following year. The [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) rose in rebellion, however, and the Young Dragon was killed while meeting under a peace banner.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)

King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) traveled to [Dorne to](wiki:Dorne) make peace and secure the release of Prince [Aemon the Dragonknight](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0) Baelor the Blessed began construction of the [Great Sept](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) in King's Landing, which would became the new seat for the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-1)

### Aegon the Unworthy and Daeron II

King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) is often regarded as one of the worst rulers of the Seven Kingdoms. The king granted [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre), one of the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) swords of House Targaryen to Ser [Daemon Waters](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), one of his [bastards](wiki:Bastards), instead of awarding it to his heir. On his deathbed, Aegon the Unworthy legitimized all of his bastards, including the Great Bastards, those born to noble women.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0)

In [187 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) peacefully joined the Seven Kingdoms through the [marriage](wiki:Wedding_of_Maron_Martell_and_Daenerys_Targaryen) of Princess [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)), sister of King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), to [Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)

[Barthogan Stark](wiki:Barthogan_Stark), [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell), was killed while suppressing a rebellion of the [Skagosi](wiki:Skagos).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_stoneborn_of_skagos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0)

### Blackfyre Rebellions

Some lords hated the inclusion of Dornishmen at court, and they preferred the legitimized [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) over the peaceable King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen). Supported by Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers), another of the Great Bastards, Daemon led the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [196 AC](wiki:196_AC). The civil war ended with Daemon being struck down by [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), a Great Bastard called Bloodraven, in the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-1)(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) Aegor, called Bittersteel, survived the battle and went into exile in the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-1)

The [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness) killed tens of thousands in the realm in [209](wiki:209_AC)–[210 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-2)

Bittersteel did not participate in the ill-fated [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion), which occurred at the [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls),(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) but he did lead the [Third Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Third_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in support of [Haegon I Blackfyre](wiki:Haegon_I_Blackfyre). The Targaryens triumphed over [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) again, however. After being freed while en route to the [Wall](wiki:Wall), Aegor returned to [Essos](wiki:Essos) and founded the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0)

[Raymun Redbeard](wiki:Raymun_Redbeard) led a host of [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) past the [Wall](wiki:Wall) in [226 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), but they were eventually defeated in a [battle at Long Lake](wiki:Battle_at_Long_Lake).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_wildlings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0)

### Aegon the Unlikely

After the death of King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) in the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising), a Great Council was called in [233 AC](wiki:233_AC) to choose his successor. [Aenys Blackfyre](wiki:Aenys_Blackfyre) arrived in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to peacefully put forward his claim, but Bloodraven had him seized and executed. Maekar's fourth son was chosen, becoming King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), and the new king sent Bloodraven to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-1)

The [Fourth Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Fourth_Blackfyre_Rebellion) was one of several rebellions which Aegon the Unlikely was forced to confront. His efforts to help [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) through reform and rights made him popular with commoners, but his plans often provoked resistance from the realm's [lords](wiki:Lord).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1)

Aegon and many of those closest to him were killed in the [tragedy at Summerhall](wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall) in [259 AC](wiki:259_AC).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-2)

### War of the Ninepenny Kings

After conquering [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) and the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), the [Band of Nine](wiki:Band_of_Nine) set the Seven Kingdoms as their next target. Lord [Ormund Baratheon](wiki:Ormund_Baratheon) led the defense of the realm on behalf of King [Jaehaerys II Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen) in the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings). Ormund was killed by [Maelys I Blackfyre](wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre) in the conflict, but Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) avenged him by slaying Maelys on the Stepstones.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0)

### Aerys and Tywin

King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) named his friend Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), who had swiftly crushed the [Reyne-Tarbeck revolt](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt), to be his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). ## Culture

The people of the Seven Kingdoms are a mixture of several ethnic groups which have migrated to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) over the centuries and intermarried over hundreds and thousands of years.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-29) As such these original ethnicities are more of a blurred trend than firm political units. Today they are mostly identified through their place of residence, where each kingdom retains its unique flavor.

The influence of the [Andals](wiki:Andals) is strongest in the peoples of the south, including [crownlands men](wiki:Crownlands_men), [rivermen](wiki:Rivermen), [stormlanders](wiki:Stormlanders), [Valemen](wiki:Valemen), [westermen](wiki:Westermen), and [men of the Reach](wiki:Men_of_the_Reach). [Northmen](wiki:Northmen), however, still follow many customs of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men). The traditions of chivalric [knighthood](wiki:Knight) and courtly singers are said to have originated in the [Reach](wiki:Reach).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) are heavily influenced by the mass immigration of the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar), who according to legend were led to Westeros by [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria) on [ten thousand ships](wiki:Ten_thousand_ships).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Small groups, such as the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) of the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) and the [Vale mountain clans](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans) in the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon), maintain different cultures from the rest of the realm.

The most common language in the kingdom is the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) Unlike their counterparts in [Essos](wiki:Essos), Westerosi nobles take pride in their [heraldry](wiki:Heraldry).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Radwd1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) Exiled and adventurous Westerosi warriors have been known to serve in [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) companies in Essos, such as the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company),(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) the [Windblown](wiki:Windblown),(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons),(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Radwd66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) the [Company of the Rose](wiki:Company_of_the_Rose),(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) and the [Wolf Pack](wiki:Wolf_Pack).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

The people of the Seven Kingdoms follow several different faiths.

- The [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), the primary religion of the Seven Kingdoms, was brought to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) by the Andals thousands of years ago. The Faith worships the Seven, a single deity with separate aspects: the [Mother](wiki:Mother_(the_Seven)), the [Father](wiki:Father_(the_Seven)), the [Warrior](wiki:Warrior_(the_Seven)), the [Smith](wiki:Smith_(the_Seven)), the [Maiden](wiki:Maiden_(the_Seven)), the [Crone](wiki:Crone_(the_Seven)), and the [Stranger](wiki:Stranger_(the_Seven)).(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) Knighthood is popular among the warrior class.

- The [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) are worshiped in the [north](wiki:North) and the lands [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall). It was the faith of the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest), who used to inhabit the land thousands of years ago. Followers worship numerous and nameless gods of the forests, who are always watching through the faces carved into the bark of [heart trees](wiki:Heart_tree), most which are ancient [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) trees.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

- The [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God), the indigenous deity of the Iron Islands, is a harsh deity who rewards those who plunder in his name.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

- [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor), the red god, is a deity popular in [Essos](wiki:Essos) but little-known in Westeros, although gaining in popularity. The followers of R'hllor claim their deity is the Lord of Light and will stand against the [Great Other](wiki:Great_Other), the god of darkness and cold, in a war that is to come.(wiki:Seven_Kingdoms.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)
