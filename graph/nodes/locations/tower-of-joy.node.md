## Identity

The Tower of Joy is a tower located in the Red Mountains of Dorne, named by Prince Rhaegar Targaryen. It became the site of one of the final and most consequential events of Robert's Rebellion. After the Battle of the Trident and the Sack of King's Landing, Lord Eddard Stark rode south with six companions — Howland Reed, Lord Willam Dustin, Ethan Glover, Martyn Cassel, Theo Wull, and Ser Mark Ryswell — to find his sister Lyanna Stark.

At the tower, they were confronted by three knights of the Kingsguard: Ser Arthur Dayne (the Sword of the Morning), Ser Oswell Whent, and Lord Commander Ser Gerold Hightower. These three had remained at the tower guarding Lyanna rather than fighting in the wider rebellion. After a short exchange in which the Kingsguard refused opportunities to leave unharmed, combat ensued. Of the seven Stark companions and three Kingsguard, only Eddard Stark and Howland Reed survived. Inside the tower, Eddard found Lyanna, who extracted an undisclosed promise from him before dying. The details of what Eddard found there are not fully revealed in the narrative, giving the Tower of Joy profound significance in fan theories about Jon Snow's parentage.

The tower appears in Eddard Stark's recurring fever dream, a fragmented memory of the event, and is referenced by multiple characters including Willam Dustin's widow, who notes that Ned brought back Willam's horse but not his body.
