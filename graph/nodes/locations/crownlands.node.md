## Origins

### Hundred Kingdoms

In the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), the petty kings of [House Darklyn](wiki:House_Darklyn) ruled much of the future crownlands, from [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) to [Crackclaw Point](wiki:Crackclaw_Point). Although neighboring realms forced them to swear fealty and their lands changed hands many times over the years, the Darklyns remained one of the more influential houses of the region. In the days of the [Hundred Kingdoms](wiki:Hundred_Kingdoms), the mouth of the Blackwater was claimed by the Darklyns, the [Masseys](wiki:House_Massey) of [Stonedance](wiki:Stonedance), and the [river kings](wiki:River_kings) of Houses [Mudd](wiki:House_Mudd), [Fisher](wiki:House_Fisher), [Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken), [Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood), and [Hook](wiki:House_Hook).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Two centuries before the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), [Valyrians](wiki:Valyria) settled [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) and built [a citadel](wiki:Dragonstone) as the westernmost outpost of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold), becoming another power in the region.(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf29.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) In time, the middle of the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) came to be controlled by the [dragons](wiki:Dragon) of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) of Dragonstone, as well as the ships of [House Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) of [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) and [House Celtigar](wiki:House_Celtigar) of [Claw Isle](wiki:Claw_Isle).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

### The Conquest

It was at the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), the later site of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), where [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and his sisters, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) and [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen), landed with their initial army. At that time the petty lords of the region had been subjugated by [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare). [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) and [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth) surrendered to Visenya and Rhaenys without fighting, while Aegon and his half-brother [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon) defeated the Darklyns and [Mootons](wiki:House_Mooton).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) [Massey's Hook](wiki:Massey's_Hook) and the southern shore of the Blackwater Rush were controlled by the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_Kings) of [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon), but the [Bar Emmons](wiki:House_Bar_Emmon) and Masseys allied with Aegon the Conqueror against King [Argilac the Arrogant](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

### Targaryen Era

Since the Conquest, the crownlands houses have historically been a strong source of support for House Targaryen, with Crackclaw Point being particularly fierce supporters. The Conqueror's [councillors](wiki:Small_council) included several crownlands men, including [Crispian Celtigar](wiki:Crispian_Celtigar), [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon), and [Tristan Massey](wiki:Tristan_Massey). Nobles from the region such as [Qarlton Chelsted](wiki:Qarlton_Chelsted), a [Hayford](wiki:House_Hayford) lord, and several Targaryens have served as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), and even smallfolk of the region are near enough to catch the royal eye for such service, such as [Rossart](wiki:Rossart) and presumably Septons [Barth](wiki:Barth) and [Murmison](wiki:Murmison). Nobles and smallfolk alike also provide much of the manpower for the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing). The [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) has also included many men of the crownlands, such as [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Dunk), [Donnel of Duskendale](wiki:Donnel_of_Duskendale), the [Cargyll](wiki:House_Cargyll) twins, Ser [Rickard Thorne](wiki:Rickard_Thorne), at least six men of Crackclaw Point, and seven Darklyn [knights](wiki:Knight).

At the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the [Velaryons](wiki:House_Velaryon), supporters of Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and the [blacks](wiki:Blacks), used their fleet to seal the [Gullet](wiki:Gullet) and blockade King's Landing. King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen)'s supporters, the [greens](wiki:Greens), retaliated by sacking [Duskendale](wiki:Sack_of_Duskendale) and [Rook's Rest](wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest). The greens later broke the blockade with the [Battle in the Gullet](wiki:Battle_in_the_Gullet) and sacked [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark). Rhaenyra was eventually fed to Aegon II's dragon, [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre), at Dragonstone.(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) At the end of the war, the [Lads](wiki:Lads) defeated Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) in the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf46.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

The [Defiance of Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale), a rising by the Darklyns during the reign of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), was a sign that even the normally loyal crownlands were losing respect for the dragonlords. Later, the outlaws of the [Kingswood Brotherhood](wiki:Kingswood_Brotherhood) robbed nobles in the forest before being suppressed by the Kingsguard.

### Robert's Rebellion

King's Landing was sacked by the forces of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), also known as the War of the Usurper.(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) After the death of Aerys, the new king, [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), tasked his younger brother [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) with building a new [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) and leading the [assault on Dragonstone](wiki:Assault_on_Dragonstone).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Ser [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry) fled the island with [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) and [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), however.(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) Robert named Stannis the new [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), although Stannis felt slighted that their youngest brother, [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), received the more prestigious [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1)(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) With several [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) having died during the war, Robert named Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount) to the White Cloaks.(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rssm1027.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

The crownlands are sworn to King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), and the lords of the region are often seen at court. There is lingering anti-[Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) sentiment from the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing), with Lady [Tanda Stokeworth](wiki:Tanda_Stokeworth) being among those listed by [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) as possible supporters in a hypothetical coup after Robert's death.(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Robert is succeeded by his supposed son, [Joffrey I](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

### A Clash of Kings

At the outbreak of [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), the lords of the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) gather their fleets to support Robert's brother, [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), the [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone) and [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships). Stannis sails most of the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) in [298 AC](wiki:298_AC),(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) leaving only some fifty ships behind in [King’s Landing](wiki:King’s_Landing).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2) While Stannis later sets sail with some two hundred ships,(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) it is unknown how many of these originally belonged to the royal fleet, and how many ships belong to the lords who had sworn Stannis fealthy. In [299 AC](wiki:299_AC), a call-to-arms from [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) gathers a host of three thousand men, including [sellswords](wiki:Sellswords) from [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Myr](wiki:Myr),(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-2) which later swells to five thousand men, with less than four hundred horse.(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

The mainland lords support Joffrey, who is backed by the Lannisters. Ser [Jacelyn Bywater](wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater) augments the [gold cloaks](wiki:Gold_cloaks) to six thousand in early [299 AC](wiki:299_AC).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) The food supplies of [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth) and [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) help prevent King's Landing from starving during the [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) embargo.(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

The split of the crownlands effectively ends after the [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister)-Tyrell victory for Joffrey in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

### A Storm of Swords

The gold cloaks decline to some forty-four hundred men due to deaths and desertions following the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater) in the second half of [299 AC](wiki:299_AC).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Later that same year, numbers have increased to nearly five thousand.(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) sends a [northern](wiki:North) army led by [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) and Ser [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart) to attack [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale),(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) where they are [crushed](wiki:Battle_at_Duskendale) by Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) and Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) Joffrey dies at [his own wedding](wiki:Purple_Wedding)(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) and is succeeded by his younger brother, [Tommen I](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

### A Feast for Crows

[Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) visits [Rosby](wiki:Rosby),(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale),(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) and the [Whispers](wiki:Whispers)(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) during her search for [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark).

[Queen Regent](wiki:Regent) [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) appoints [Gyles Rosby](wiki:Gyles_Rosby) and [Aurane Waters](wiki:Aurane_Waters), two natives of the crownlands, to her small council,(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) although Gyles dies of natural causes(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) and Aurane flees the city after the queen's arrest.(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) takes command of the [siege of Dragonstone](wiki:Siege_of_Dragonstone), Stannis's last remaining base in the crownlands, although the Knight of Flowers is reported to have been gravely wounded in his assault.(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Lords [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) and [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) bring their armies back to King's Landing after the arrest of Mace's daughter, Queen [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell).(wiki:Crownlands.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)
