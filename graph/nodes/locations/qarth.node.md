## Origins

Qarth claims to be the birthplace of civilization, although [maesters](wiki:Maesters) are skeptical.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) It is the sole surviving city-state established by the [Qaathi](wiki:Qaathi) people, who rose in the grasslands of central [Essos](wiki:Essos)—known today as the [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea)—and founded city-states as they moved southeast following defeat in wars against the [Sarnori](wiki:Sarnor).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Ancient Qarth was ruled by [kings](wiki:King_of_Qarth) and queens, the ancestors of the [Pureborn](wiki:Pureborn).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-11) [Jar Han](wiki:Jar_Han), the eighth of the sea-green emperors of [Yi Ti](wiki:Yi_Ti), exacted tribute from Qarth.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

The new lands of the Qaathi turned into the [red waste](wiki:Red_waste), however, and their civilization declined.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) During the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood), [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) pushed into the red waste and destroyed the remaining Qaathi cities, leaving Qarth as the sole surviving Qaathi city.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-2) The ruins of [Vaes Orvik](wiki:Vaes_Orvik), [Vaes Qosar](wiki:Vaes_Qosar), and [Vaes Shirosi](wiki:Vaes_Shirosi) still stand today.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rawoiafvaes_orvik.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rawoiafvaes_qosar.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rawoiafvaes_shirosi.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

Looking to the sea, the Pureborn constructed a fleet and took control of the [Jade Gates](wiki:Jade_Gates), the strait between Qarth and [Great Moraq](wiki:Great_Moraq). The Qartheen prospered from trade and tolls on the most direct route between the [Summer Sea](wiki:Summer_Sea) and the [Jade Sea](wiki:Jade_Sea).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-3)

The protagonist of *[A Caution for Young Girls](wiki:A_Caution_for_Young_Girls)* allegedly served as the [handmaid](wiki:Handmaid) of a Qartheen [warlock](wiki:Warlock) during part of her life.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

In the last of his [nine voyages](wiki:The_Nine_Voyages) aboard the *[Sea Snake](wiki:Sea_Snake)*, [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) brought a shipload of gold to Qarth, where he purchased twenty more ships. Filled with spices, [elephants](wiki:Elephant), and silk, most of the ships returned to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2) [Jade](wiki:Jade) from Qarth was popular in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

[Lomas Longstrider](wiki:Lomas_Longstrider) visited the [walls of Qarth](wiki:Walls_of_Qarth).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) [Xandarro Xhore](wiki:Xandarro_Xhore) was a Qartheen pirate active in the [Basilisk Isles](wiki:Basilisk_Isles).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) has allegedly plundered as far as Qarth and [Tall Trees Town](wiki:Tall_Trees_Town).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

[Colloquo Votar](wiki:Colloquo_Votar)'s *[Jade Compendium](wiki:Jade_Compendium)* describes Qarth and the [Jade Sea](wiki:Jade_Sea).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafother_lands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

## Appearances & Description

### Location

Qarth is located on the [Jade Gates](wiki:Jade_Gates), which link the [Summer Sea](wiki:Summer_Sea) to the fabled [Jade Sea](wiki:Jade_Sea). The city is located to the southeast of [Lhazar](wiki:Lhazar) and the [red waste](wiki:Red_waste) and southwest of the [Bone Mountains](wiki:Bone_Mountains). Just east of Qarth is the small island of [Qal](wiki:Qal), while the large island [Great Moraq](wiki:Great_Moraq) is to the south. The great [Slaver Cities](wiki:Slaver_Cities) of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), [Astapor](wiki:Astapor), and [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) are to the west of Qarth.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtloiaf.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

Four main travel routes originate from Qarth. The western route leads to [Qarkash](wiki:Qarkash), the northwestern route to [Vaes Qosar](wiki:Vaes_Qosar) and [Vaes Tolorro](wiki:Vaes_Tolorro), while the eastern road leads to [Asabhad](wiki:Asabhad).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtloiafthe_known_world.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The fourth route, the [Sand Road](wiki:Sand_Road), leads through the southern [Bone Mountains](wiki:Bone_Mountains), eventually passing [Bayasabhad](wiki:Bayasabhad), leading to the market city [Tiqui](wiki:Tiqui).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtloiafthe_known_world.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Qarth's position makes it the gateway between the lands of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) and [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay), and more eastern lands such as [Asshai](wiki:Asshai), the [Shadow Lands](wiki:Shadow_Lands), and [Yi Ti](wiki:Yi_Ti). Qarth has been described as the center of the world.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

The power of Qarth extends west to [Port Yhos](wiki:Port_Yhos),(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_port_yhos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and east to the island [Qal](wiki:Qal), where the Qartheen have raised fortified harbor cities on either side of the island to reinforce their control of the passage of the [Jade Gates](wiki:Jade_Gates).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rawoiafqal.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) The nearby town [Qarkash](wiki:Qarkash) is a Qartheen city,(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rawoiafqarkash.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and [Vaes Orvik](wiki:Vaes_Orvik), [Vaes Qosar](wiki:Vaes_Qosar), and [Vaes Shirosi](wiki:Vaes_Shirosi), all in ruins, were Qartheen in origin as well.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rawoiafvaes_orvik.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rawoiafvaes_qosar.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rawoiafvaes_shirosi.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### Layout

Qarth is surrounded by three thick walls of thirty, forty, and fifty feet in height, respectively engraved with portraits of animals, war, and lovemaking.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) The [walls of Qarth](wiki:Walls_of_Qarth) are one of the nine wonders made by man reported in the book, *[Wonders Made by Man](wiki:Wonders_Made_by_Man)*, by [Lomas Longstrider](wiki:Lomas_Longstrider).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) The [port of Qarth](wiki:Port_of_Qarth) is one of the greatest in the [known world](wiki:Known_world).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

The buildings in Qarth are colorful. Decorations include a bronze arch fashioned in the likeness of two snakes mating, their scales delicate flakes of [jade](wiki:Jade), [obsidian](wiki:Obsidian), and [lapis lazuli](wiki:Lapis_lazuli). The city has slim towers which stand tall, and balconies of houses are delicate and frail. Each square has an elaborate fountain in the shape of creatures like [griffins](wiki:Griffin), [dragons](wiki:Dragon), and [manticores](wiki:Manticore).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

There is a great arcade where the heroes of the city stand atop columns of green and white [marble](wiki:Marble). The statues are three times as big as a man.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

The [Pureborn](wiki:Pureborn) of Qarth rule from the [Hall of a Thousand Thrones](wiki:Hall_of_a_Thousand_Thrones),(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) and the [warlocks](wiki:Warlocks) of Qarth have their center of power in the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Other locations include the [Temple of Memory](wiki:Temple_of_Memory), where traditional sacrifices are made,(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) the [Garden of Gehane](wiki:Garden_of_Gehane), and [Warlock's Way](wiki:Warlock's_Way).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

Larger than most market towns, the [palace of Xaro Xhoan Daxos](wiki:Xaro's_home) includes gardens, a marble bathing pool, a scrying tower, and warlock's maze.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) Located on the route between Xaro's palace and the Hall of a Thousand Thrones is a cavernous building home to a bazaar.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) A thousand gaily colored birds live on the latticework ceiling. On the terraced walls above stalls, trees and flowers bloom.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

### People

Descending from the [Qaathi](wiki:Qaathi), Qartheen are a tall and pale people, called [Milk Men](wiki:Milk_Men) by the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) for their complexion.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) They pride themselves on their sophistication and consider weeping in times of strong emotion to be a mark of civilization.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3) Qartheen politeness goes as far having the Qartheen guild of assassins known as the [Sorrowful Men](wiki:Sorrowful_Men) say "I'm so sorry" to their victim before killing them.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-4)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) The syllables of the Qartheen language sound liquid to a [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) ear.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

The Qartheen dress in linen, samite and [tiger](wiki:Tiger) fur. As per Qartheen fashion, women wear [Qartheen gowns](wiki:Qartheen_gown), which leave one breast exposed.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-5) Meanwhile, men favor beaded silk skirts. Children might go about naked, wearing only golden sandals and [body paint](wiki:Cosmetics).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9)

The Qartheen ride [camels](wiki:Camel).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-10)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Warriors wear scaled [copper](wiki:Copper) armor and snouted helms with copper tusks and long black silk plumes. They sit high on saddles inlaid with [rubies](wiki:Rubies) and [garnets](wiki:Garnet). Their camels are dressed in blankets of a hundred different hues.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-11)

Qartheen men and women retain their possessions after [marriage](wiki:Marriage). However, there is a marriage custom where, on the day of the wedding, the groom may ask for any one item from his bride's possessions and the bride may ask for any one item of the groom's possessions. Whatever is asked by either cannot be denied. This is seen as a gift of devotion by the bride and groom to each other.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-6)

Qarth is nominally ruled by the [Pureborn](wiki:Pureborn), who are seated in the [Hall of a Thousand Thrones](wiki:Hall_of_a_Thousand_Thrones). They deal with all matters of civic importance, commanding the [Civic Guard](wiki:Civic_Guard) and the fleet of ornate war galleys which rule the [Jade Gates](wiki:Jade_Gates) between the seas.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-7) However, the powerful guilds of the [Thirteen](wiki:Thirteen), the [Tourmaline Brotherhood](wiki:Tourmaline_Brotherhood), and the [Ancient Guild of Spicers](wiki:Ancient_Guild_of_Spicers) all contend endlessly with the Pureborn.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-8)

The [warlocks](wiki:Warlocks) of Qarth are feared and respected throughout [Essos](wiki:Essos).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-12)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-9) Like the [Alchemists' Guild](wiki:Alchemists'_Guild) of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), however, the warlocks' power and prestige have waned over the years.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-13) Most warlocks have blue lips(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-14) from drinking [shade of the evening](wiki:Shade_of_the_evening),(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) which is derived from a [black-barked tree](wiki:Black-barked_tree).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) The warlocks' stronghold and seat of power is the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying), which houses the [Undying Ones](wiki:Undying_Ones).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-15)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3)

The Qartheen practice [slavery](wiki:Slavery) in the city.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

### Economy

A city of merchants.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Qarth has become wealthy due to its control of the [Jade Gates](wiki:Jade_Gates).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rawoiafqarth.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Because of its location, Qarth is seen as a gateway of commerce and culture between the east and west, and the north and south.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-16) [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) traders from [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) and the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor) sail to Qarth, for instance.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Qarth houses three guilds of competing merchant princes, the [Thirteen](wiki:Thirteen), the [Tourmaline Brotherhood](wiki:Tourmaline_Brotherhood), and the [Ancient Guild of Spicers](wiki:Ancient_Guild_of_Spicers).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-10)

Trade goods from Qarth include saffron,(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) [dreamwine](wiki:Dreamwine) and liqueur, strongly spiced,(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) [silk](wiki:Silk),(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) [spices](wiki:Spices), and [elephants](wiki:Elephant).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) Qartheen spice ships can be as big as palaces.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Qartheen ships travel as far as [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) to trade.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

Qartheen adventurers search for [gold](wiki:Gold), [gems](wiki:Gemstone), and [ivory](wiki:Ivory) along the eastern coasts of [Sothoryos](wiki:Sothoryos), hungry to make a profit. However, they have never been able to reach the southern end of that continent.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_sothoryos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

While Qarth depends upon its [slaves](wiki:Slaves),(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) *[khalasars](wiki:Khalasar)* of the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) never cross the [red waste](wiki:Red_waste) to sell their slaves at Qarth. The Qartheen do not want them to do so, either, as they strongly dislike the smell of the *khalasars*.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

[Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) mentions the city to [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), telling Jon that in one of his efforts to cure Sam of his timidity Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) once brought two [warlocks](wiki:Warlocks) from Qarth to his castle at [Horn Hill](wiki:Horn_Hill).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

Before Khal [Drogo](wiki:Drogo)'s body is burnt on his funeral pyre, Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) begs [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) to come east with him, to [Yi Ti](wiki:Yi_Ti), Qarth, the [Jade Sea](wiki:Jade_Sea), and [Asshai by the Shadow](wiki:Asshai), to see all the wonders yet unseen.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Daenerys recalls how Drogo dreamed of the day when he might sack the great cities of the east, including Qarth.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-17)

Three representatives of Qarth—[Pyat Pree](wiki:Pyat_Pree), [Xaro Xhoan Daxos](wiki:Xaro_Xhoan_Daxos), and [Quaithe](wiki:Quaithe)—accompany [Jhogo](wiki:Jhogo) back to [Vaes Tolorro](wiki:Vaes_Tolorro) after he is sent by Daenerys to discover what lies near the abandoned city.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) The emissaries escort Daenerys and her *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* to Qarth. A [column](wiki:Column) of [camelry](wiki:Camel) emerge from the city as Daenerys's honor guards upon her arrival.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-18) Her three young [dragons](wiki:Dragon) are a marvel in the ancient city.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-19)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-12)(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)

Xaro, a member of the [Thirteen](wiki:Thirteen), offers Daenerys the hospitality of [his palace](wiki:Xaro's_home) while she and her *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* are in Qarth. Under his guidance Daenerys seeks an audience with the [Pureborn](wiki:Pureborn) to plea with them in the [Hall of a Thousand Thrones](wiki:Hall_of_a_Thousand_Thrones) for their fleet of galleys, or part of it and some of their soldiers. However, the Pureborn deny her.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-13)

Xaro sees to it that the great and the humble alike offer some token to Daenerys, the Mother of Dragons. She receives gifts from throughout the [known world](wiki:Known_world), selling all except for a crown wrought in the shape of a three-headed dragon given to her by the [Tourmaline Brotherhood](wiki:Tourmaline_Brotherhood).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-14)

After having been refused by the Pureborn, Daenerys agrees to go with Pyat to the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-15) [Drogon](wiki:Drogon) destroys the building when Daenerys is attacked by the [Undying Ones](wiki:Undying_Ones).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-4) After its destruction, [ghost grass](wiki:Ghost_grass) is found growing in the [Garden of Gehane](wiki:Garden_of_Gehane) and [phantom tortoises](wiki:Phantom_tortoises) are seen carrying messages between the windowless houses on [Warlock's Way](wiki:Warlock's_Way). In the home of [Urrathon Night-Walker](wiki:Urrathon_Night-Walker), [glass candles](wiki:Glass_candle) burn for the first time in a century.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3)

Daenerys is soon forced to flee Qarth after the Tourmaline Brotherhood calls for her expulsion and the [Ancient Guild of Spicers](wiki:Ancient_Guild_of_Spicers) call for her death. Only Xaro's influence prevents the Thirteen from adding their voices.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4) As Daenerys searches for transport at the [port of Qarth](wiki:Port_of_Qarth), [Strong Belwas](wiki:Strong_Belwas) and [Arstan Whitebeard](wiki:Arstan_Whitebeard) save her from being assassinated by the [manticore](wiki:Manticore) of a [Sorrowful Man](wiki:Sorrowful_Man).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-5)

### A Storm of Swords

In [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), Daenerys learns that Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) had been spying on her on behalf of Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys), the [spymaster](wiki:Spymaster) of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). Jorah admits that the last report that he made to Varys had been made from Qarth. Daenerys is taken aback, as she hoped that his spying had ended much earlier.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

### A Feast For Crows

At the [Quill and Tankard](wiki:Quill_and_Tankard) in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) [Leo Tyrell](wiki:Leo_Tyrell_(son_of_Moryn)) informs his fellow students—[Pate](wiki:Pate_(novice)), [Mollander](wiki:Mollander), [Alleras](wiki:Alleras), [Armen](wiki:Armen), and [Roone](wiki:Roone)—that sailors who have visited Qarth are speaking of Daenerys's dragons.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

Seeking vengeance against Daenerys,(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Rawoiafqarth.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) a galleas of [warlocks](wiki:Warlocks) sails from Qarth but is captured by *[Silence](wiki:Silence)*, the galley of [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Xaro arrives in Meereen as a representative of Qarth to visit Queen Daenerys. He attempts to persuade her to sail for Westeros. [Her campaign](wiki:Targaryen_campaign_in_Slaver's_Bay) has disrupted the [slave](wiki:Slave) trade upon which depend many cities of [Essos](wiki:Essos), including Qarth. He brings a gift of thirteen ships, stating that they are hers on the condition she sails from [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) to Westeros. Daenerys has the thirteen ships Xaro brought inspected, but later refuses the offer. Xaro's reply is to leave a bloodstained glove on a satin pillow, indicating Qarth has declared war on her.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Xaro leaves the thirteen ships at Slaver's Bay, where they give aid in closing off the bay to Meereen. Three Qartheen galleys sail up the [Skahazadhan](wiki:Skahazadhan) at night, in an attempt to close off the river as well. The [Mother's Men](wiki:Mother's_Men) loose flights of fire arrows at their sails and fling pots of burning pitch onto their decks, but the galleys manage to slip by quickly and succeed in closing off the river. The Qartheen drive off a third of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen)'s fishing fleet and manage to seize another third, leaving Meereen without trade.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) Qarth sends a corps of Qartheen camelry to aid the [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai)'i against Daenerys.(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

Pale Qartheen are among those who come to Meereen to battle in [Daznak's Pit](wiki:Daznak's_Pit).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

### The Winds of Winter

As the [second siege of Meereen](wiki:Second_siege_of_Meereen) resumes, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) hears high Qartheen whistles as their ships battle the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) of the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn).(wiki:Qarth.cite_ref-43)
