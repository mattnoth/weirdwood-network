## Appearances & Description

### Village[ ]

The stone [holdfast](wiki:Holdfast) tower of Queenscrown is described both as a roundtower and a towerhouse.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) It stands upon an rocky island in a lake, with many [oak](wiki:Oak) trees growing thick along the shore.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) On the lakeshore opposite the tower's entrance are the remnants of a village surrounded by oak and apple trees. Its inn is set among a dozen apple trees, but all that remains of the building are a stone chimney and two cracked walls, with an apple tree growing through the common room.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) The inn used to be small and was made from timber.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The houses of the village are tumbledown cottages made from daub-and-wattle, but most have collapsed.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

### Causeway[ ]

A stone causeway runs beneath the waters from the lake to the island. It starts as a stone pathway three feet wide, leading out into the lake. Where the path of the causeway comes climbing out of the water onto the island, it turns into a short flight of stone steps that lead to the holdfast's door. Path, steps, and door are in a straight line, which makes one incorrectly think the causeway runs straight. The path under the water instead zigs and zags, going a third of the way around the island before jagging back. The turns are treacherous, and the long path means that anyone approaching would be exposed to arrow fire from the tower for a long time.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) The hidden stones of the path are slimy and slippery, and the water over the path is roughly three and a half feet deep.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-5)

### Tower[ ]

The stout door to the tower has a low lintel, and it can no longer be closed completely as its heavy oak planks have warped. It leads to a small, gloomy strongroom with a murder hole above it. Steps built into the inner wall of the tower curve away upward to the left to the upper stories, and downward to the right into an undervault; both directions are locked behind [iron](wiki:Iron) grates. The second floor of Queenscrown is a maze of small cells, the third floor has arrow slits, and the fourth has windows.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
The fifth floor is a large round chamber with arched doors on three sides which open onto small stone balconies. Along the fourth side is a privy chamber perched above a sewer chute that drops straight down into the lake. The north-facing balcony looks out toward the village. There is access to the roof, which gives a high view of the surrounding countryside. The [northern mountains](wiki:Northern_mountains) are visible to the south, as are the plains of the [Gift](wiki:Gift) in other directions, but the [Wall](wiki:Wall) is too far away to be seen.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) A beacon on the roof could be lit by the tower's lordling in case of a [wildling](wiki:Wildling) attack.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) The merlons atop Queenscrown are painted [gold](wiki:Gold), but are now flaking,(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) and have faded to yellow.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

## Origins

During a visit to the [north](wiki:North),(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-8) Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) flew to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) on her dragon, [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing). She stayed at this holdfast on her way, so the villagers painted the merlons atop the tower gold in imitation of her crown to honor her.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)
Queenscrown has been abandoned for many years, as many of the [Gift](wiki:Gift)'s inhabitants have fled from [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings).(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark), [Hodor](wiki:Hodor), and [Meera Reed](wiki:Meera_Reed) and [Jojen Reed](wiki:Jojen_Reed) stop at Queenscrown on their march to the [Wall](wiki:Wall). The roofless inn is uninhabitable with a storm approaching, so they take refuge in the tower; Hodor nearly slips twice while they cross the causeway over the lake. As they wait out the storm, Hodor becomes frightened by lightning and thunder.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)
While they wait out the storm, a traveling horseman takes shelter in the ruined inn. When Bran's party observes men entering the village, Bran enters Hodor's mind as a [skinchanger](wiki:Skinchanger) to prevent the scared stableboy from alerting the strangers of their presence.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11) Unbeknownst to Bran, the newcomers are a party of [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) who crossed the [Wall](wiki:Wall) at [Greyguard](wiki:Greyguard).(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), Bran's half-brother, is among the wildlings, who believe he has deserted the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). [Styr](wiki:Styr) orders Jon to kill the traveler they captured in the inn, but the youth balks at murdering the old northman. [Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte) kills the man anyway with her knife.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8) Bran enters the mind of his [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf), [Summer](wiki:Summer),(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and attacks in the dark, allowing Jon to escape on the travelers horse,(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9) although Summer is struck by an arrow.(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)
Two of the wildlings nearly drown while attempting to cross the causeway the next morning,(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) so Styr eventually has his wildlings depart for [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black).(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) The wounded Summer swims out to the island, allowing Meera to treat his wound with nearby plants. Bran and his companions wait another day and then continue north toward the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort).(wiki:Queenscrown.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10)
