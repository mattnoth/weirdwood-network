## Origins

The roseroad may have existed in some form in earlier centuries, but it and other routes of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) were remade or built anew during the reign of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Roseroad.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_politics.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) takes the roseroad to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), where she swears her service to [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon).(wiki:Roseroad.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_journeys_map_-_brienne_tarth.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) closes the roseroad to all shipments of food to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), as he supports Renly's claim to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Renly slowly marches his host down the roseroad from Highgarden towards King's Landing.(wiki:Roseroad.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

After the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater) and the betrothal of his daughter, [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), to King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), Mace reopens the roseroad.(wiki:Roseroad.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
