## Origins

### Foundation

The initial settlement of Qohor was a lumber camp along the [Forest of Qohor](wiki:Forest_of_Qohor).(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14) Qohor was colonized by followers of the [Black Goat](wiki:Black_Goat_of_Qohor). The religious dissidents abandoned [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), rejecting the religious tolerance practiced by the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold).(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

### Century of Blood

Shortly after the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), the [dragonlord](wiki:Dragonlords) [Aurion](wiki:Aurion) raised a host of Qohorik colonists and proclaimed himself the [Emperor of Valyria](wiki:Emperor_of_Valyria). He marched with thirty thousand men to claim what remained of the [Valyrian peninsula](wiki:Valyrian_peninsula), but none were seen again.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

At the start of the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood) that followed the Doom, Khal [Temmo](wiki:Temmo) rode from the east with a [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* of fifty thousand. The Dothraki crushed the Qohorik heavy horse, and the sellswords in the city's service fled. Three thousand [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied), slave soldiers from [Astapor](wiki:Astapor), withstood the Dothraki charges, however, and saved the city. Since the victory of the famed [Three Thousand of Qohor](wiki:Three_Thousand_of_Qohor), the [city's defenses](wiki:City_watch_(Qohor)) have been mostly left to the Unsullied.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15)

Towards the end of the Century of Blood, Qohor formed an alliance with [Norvos](wiki:Norvos) and broke [Volantene](wiki:Volantis) power on the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne) in a battle involving fire galleys on [Dagger Lake](wiki:Dagger_Lake).(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) The Qohorik and Norvoshi are usually peaceful toward each other, although the [bearded priests](wiki:Bearded_priests) of Norvos consider the [Black Goat](wiki:Black_Goat_of_Qohor) to be demonic.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16)

### Recent History

Shortly after being created in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC),(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) sacked Qohor, after the city refused to honor the contract it had made with the company. Qohor is visited every three or four years by Khal [Zekko](wiki:Zekko). The Qohorik give him gifts, however, and he moves his [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* on.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

## Appearances & Description

### Location

Yhe city stands on the banks of the river [Qhoyne](wiki:Qhoyne), a tributary of the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne), on the western edge of the [Forest of Qohor](wiki:Forest_of_Qohor). It is seen as a gateway to the east.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Qohor is surrounded by strong stone walls.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Northwest of Qohor is [Norvos](wiki:Norvos), and [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) is to the south.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-map_3-0)(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtloiafthe_known_world.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The Qohorik rule the Qhoyne north of the ruins of [Ar Noy](wiki:Ar_Noy)(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and the eastern bank of the [Darkwash](wiki:Darkwash), although that river's western bank is within the domains of Norvos.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

### People

[Maesters](wiki:Maesters) consider Qohor the most exotic of the Free Cities, due to its status as the western terminus for overland trade networks stretching all the way to [Yi Ti](wiki:Yi_Ti) (though this contact is often indirect). The [dark arts](wiki:Magic), such as divination, blood magic, and necromancy, are believed to be practiced in the City of Sorcerers.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

The Qohorik believe in a dark god, the [Black Goat of Qohor](wiki:Black_Goat_of_Qohor), who demands a daily [blood sacrifice](wiki:Blood_sacrifice). Sacrifices can be calves, bullocks and horses, on regular days, but condemned criminals on holy days. In times of crisis, the nobles of the city are willing to sacrifice their own children in the hopes that their god will defend the city.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Followers of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) can also be found in Qohor.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Qohor is also famous for its hunters, foresters, and artisans.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) The Qohorik have a small [city watch](wiki:City_watch_(Qohor)). Ever since the events of the [Three Thousand of Qohor](wiki:Three_Thousand_of_Qohor), the city's defenses have instead been entrusted to [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied) from [Astapor](wiki:Astapor), each carrying a spear with a braid of human hair.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Occasionally, Qohor also hires free companies, and is one of several Free Cities to offer gifts to [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) *[khals](wiki:Khal)* so that *[khalasars](wiki:Khalasar)* will pass on.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

Like the tongues of all [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), the [Qohorik language](wiki:Qohorik_language) is a form of [bastard Valyrian](wiki:Valyrian_languages).(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### Economy

Due to its location, Qohor functions as the gateway to the [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea) and the lands beyond. Trading caravans bound for or returning from [Vaes Dothrak](wiki:Vaes_Dothrak) provision themselves in Qohor before heading towards the Dothraki sea. In turn, overland merchant caravans from [Yi Ti](wiki:Yi_Ti) head west over the [Bone Mountains](wiki:Bone_Mountains) to Vaes Dothrak, where they trade with merchants from the Free Cities. The overland trade routes are long and can be dangerous, but they are preferred by merchants who wish to avoid the exorbitant taxes that [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) imposes on the shipping lanes that pass through its straits to the south.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) Trade has helped make Qohor one of the most richest of the Free Cities, although the city was richer before the [Kingdom of Sarnor](wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor) was destroyed.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

The [Forest of Qohor](wiki:Forest_of_Qohor) is the principal source of wealth of the city. Timber is shipped down the [Qhoyne](wiki:Qhoyne) to [Dagger Lake](wiki:Dagger_Lake), [Selhorys](wiki:Selhorys), [Valysar](wiki:Valysar), [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys), and [Volantis](wiki:Volantis).(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) Many other valuables can be found in the Forest of Qohor.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Qohorik tapestries are comparable in to those made by the [Myrish](wiki:Myr), though cheaper. Exquisite wooden carvings are sold in the city's markets as well.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

Qohorik forges are considered to have no equal. The armor they create is seen as superior to the armor made in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). They are capable of infusing a deep color into the metal with beautiful results. Additionally, the Qohorik are the only ones in the world who still possess the knowledge on how to rework [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel), a secret they guard strictly. According to Maester [Pol](wiki:Pol), [blood sacrifices](wiki:Blood_sacrifice) are used when the Qohorik rework Valyrian steel.(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

[Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) passes Qohor during the journey of Khal [Drogo](wiki:Drogo)'s *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* from [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) to [Vaes Dothrak](wiki:Vaes_Dothrak).(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Rtloiafjourneys.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

The [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions), a sellsword company also known as the Bloody Mummers, are led by the Qohorik [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat). Following the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) orders that they be sent out to pillage and burn the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

### A Clash of Kings

The Brave Companions take part in the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) on the side of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Vargo changes sides during the conflict, supporting [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) instead, and aids Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) against the Lannisters in the [fall of Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:Qohor.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)
