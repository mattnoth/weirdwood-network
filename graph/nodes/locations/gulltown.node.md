## Origins

### First Men and Andals

Prior to the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals), Gulltown was a wealthy harbor town of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) ruled by [House Shett](wiki:House_Shett_of_Gulltown). Because he was threatened by King [Yorwyck VI Royce](wiki:Yorwyck_VI_Royce) of [Runestone](wiki:Runestone), King [Osgood III Shett](wiki:Osgood_III_Shett) allied with the Andal knight [Gerold Grafton](wiki:Gerold_Grafton_(king)). Osgood was killed in battle, however, and [House Grafton](wiki:House_Grafton) replaced House Shett as the kings of Gulltown. Although a First Men rebellion had to be bloodily suppressed, Gulltown grew into the Vale's only city.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

The sister of King [Robar II Royce](wiki:Robar_II_Royce) later convinced the Shetts to rebel against the Graftons, allowing Robar's men to storm the city. Like most of the Vale, however, Gulltown submitted to [Artys I Arryn](wiki:Artys_I_Arryn), [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale), after the [Battle of the Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_the_Seven_Stars). The Arryns and Graftons developed Gulltown into one of the greatest cities of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

During the [War Across the Water](wiki:War_Across_the_Water), the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) burned hundreds of Vale ships after Gulltown's stone walls resisted the [northmen](wiki:North)'s attempt at conquest.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

### Targaryen Era

Gulltown's harbor was the site of a [naval battle](wiki:Battle_in_the_waters_off_Gulltown) between the [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) and [Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) fleets during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), a draw which delayed the submission of the Vale to King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The new king visited Gulltown during his royal progresss.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) The city grew during the king's reign.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Gulltown had a chapter of the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) during the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) When King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) visited the city in [52 AC](wiki:52_AC), Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) held a [women's court](wiki:Women's_court).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) [Alys Turnberry](wiki:Alys_Turnberry) gave birth to her child at the [Motherhouse of Maris](wiki:Motherhouse_of_Maris).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) gained the Vale for Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and the [blacks](wiki:Blacks). The Maiden of the Vale, [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn), assembled forces at Gulltown and the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon). Jacaerys sent [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon) and [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)) to Gulltown for their safekeeping, although Joffrey was told he was to defend the Vale from King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). After the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing), Joffrey sailed with a fleet from Gulltown to the capital.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) After Rhaenyra's death, Jeyne sent ten thousand men under the leadership of Lord [Leowyn Corbray](wiki:Leowyn_Corbray) and Ser [Corwyn Corbray](wiki:Corwyn_Corbray) from Gulltown on [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) ships against King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), ordered the construction of a fortified granary at Gulltown.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Trade withered in the city during the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War), and Gulltown suffered from [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) Lady Jeyne died at the Motherhouse of Maris in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC). [Isembard Arryn](wiki:Isembard_Arryn) of the [Gulltown Arryns](wiki:House_Arryn_of_Gulltown) opposed her preferred heir, Ser [Joffrey Arryn](wiki:Joffrey_Arryn). Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan) sent a thousand men with Corwyn to settle the issue, and [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) and [Moredo Rogare](wiki:Moredo_Rogare) lost hundreds of men during the capture of Gulltown and its harbor.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Gulltown prospered during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

During [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Lord Grafton tried to prevent Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) from sailing to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). The rebels led by Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn)(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) overcame the royalists, and Marq was slain by Robert beneath the city walls in the [taking of Gulltown](wiki:Taking_of_Gulltown).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) [Lyn Corbray](wiki:Lyn_Corbray) fought beside Marq at Gulltown and later fought under Jon Arryn at the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### Baratheon Era

For a few years between [283 AC](wiki:283_AC) and [298 AC](wiki:298_AC), [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) was in charge of customs at the port of Gulltown. He increased the income tenfold.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

## Culture

Gulltown is an important port and portal to the Vale, its sheltered harbor offering anchorage to ships traveling from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) or the [north](wiki:North). Its strategic location means it never lacks for trade in many of the exotic goods making their way from across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), keeping the Vale supplied even when the passes of the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) are closed in winter. Gulltown has fine seamstresses.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

An old song begins with the lyrics *[off to Gulltown](wiki:Off_to_Gulltown)*.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rasos13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

After the [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle) of [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) and Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) sail from Gulltown(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

### A Clash of Kings

[Conwy](wiki:Conwy) brings men from a lord's dungeon near Gulltown to [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) as recruits for the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) is sent by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) with a chest of letters to spread word of King [Joffrey I Baratheon's](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) illegitimacy. His route is to sail to Gulltown and other harbors along the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

After speaking with Tyrion, Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) sails from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to Gulltown on his mission to sway Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) to Joffrey's cause.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

### A Storm of Swords

[Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), asks permission to return to the [north](wiki:North) by passing the [Bloody Gate](wiki:Bloody_Gate) and then sailing from Gulltown for [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor). His fearful aunt, Lysa, declines, however.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

When [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) takes on the identity of Alayne Stone, Petyr creates the backstory that Alayne is the daughter of a [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) woman whom he met while administering Gulltown's port years ago.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

### A Feast for Crows

[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) spots trading ships from Gulltown, including *[Brazen Monkey](wiki:Brazen_Monkey)* at [Ragman's Harbor](wiki:Ragman's_Harbor) in Braavos. [Luco Prestayn](wiki:Luco_Prestayn)'s *[Lady Bright](wiki:Lady_Bright)* sails from Braavos for Gulltown, [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), King's Landing, and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) The master of *[Gulltown Girl](wiki:Gulltown_Girl)* at [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) refuses to transport [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) from the town.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) *[Seastrider](wiki:Seastrider)* sails from Maidenpool for Gulltown, the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers), [Sisterton](wiki:Sisterton), and [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1)

Lord [Lyonel Corbray](wiki:Lyonel_Corbray) marries the [daughter](wiki:Lady_Corbray) of a rich Gulltown merchant in [a wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Lyonel_Corbray_and_Lady_Corbray) arranged by Lord Baelish.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) *[Merling King](wiki:Merling_King)* returns to Gulltown from Braavos.(wiki:Gulltown.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)
