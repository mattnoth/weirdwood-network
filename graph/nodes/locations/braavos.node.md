## Origins

### Foundation

Unlike the other [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), Braavos was never part of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold). The youngest of these cities, Braavos was founded by escaped [slaves](wiki:Slaves), fleeing the grasp of the Freehold. The slaves rebelled on their way from Valyria to a new colony in [Sothoryos](wiki:Sothoryos), took control of the ships, and turned north instead of south, travelling as far as they could. According to the histories of Braavos, it was a group of slave women from the lands of [Jogos Nhai](wiki:Jogos_Nhai), the [moonsingers](wiki:Moonsingers), who predicted where shelter could be found. Thus, the slaves travelled to the distant lagoon, hidden away from sight of the [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) by the wall of hills covered with pine trees and the fog covering the lagoon.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-17) The founders of Braavos risked their lives for freedom, and swore a vow that no one in their city would ever be a slave, [thrall](wiki:Thrall), or bondsman. This vow became the [First Law of Braavos](wiki:First_Law_of_Braavos), and is engraved on the arch spanning the [Long Canal](wiki:Long_Canal).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-18)

Some of the fugitives who helped found Braavos hid their valuables in an abandoned [iron](wiki:Iron) mine, and the chambers of the mine filled as the city grew. Richer Braavosi began lending their wealth, and thus began the famous [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-19) The secretive [Faceless Men](wiki:Faceless_Men) are also connected with Braavos, but the order of assassins are rumored to be even older than the city.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-20)

The exact founding date of Braavos is unknown,(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-founding_2-1) and the existence of the city was kept secret for a century.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-21)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-17) While Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) states that [Sealord](wiki:Sealord) [Uthero Zalyne](wiki:Uthero_Zalyne) had sent forth his ships to every corner of the world in order to proclaim the existence and location of Braavos during the [Uncloaking (or Unmasking) of Uthero](wiki:Uncloaking_of_Uthero) at the 111th festival celebrating the city's founding,(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-22) the city's location remained hidden for another two centuries.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-18) Settlements were paid to the descendants of the former masters of the escaped slaves to compensate for the ships that had been stolen, though the Braavosi refused to pay for the value of the slaves.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-23)

At [Bitterweed Bay](wiki:Bitterweed_Bay), the Sealord of Braavos sent the last great war fleet of the [Kingdom of Sarnor](wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor) to the bottom.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_shivering_sea.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)

[Harwyn Hoare](wiki:Harwyn_Hoare) visited Braavos and other Free Cities before succeeding his father [Qhorwyn](wiki:Qhorwyn_Hoare) as [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

### After the Doom

During the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood) following the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) attempted to take control of Valyria's daughters. The Volantenes managed to take control over [Myr](wiki:Myr) and [Lys](wiki:Lys), but when they attempted to take [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) they met with resistance. In the rebellion against Volantis, the [Sealord of Braavos](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos) sent a hundred warships to the aid of Lys.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), a dozen Braavosi warships helped the [Kingdom of Mountain and Vale](wiki:Kingdom_of_Mountain_and_Vale) in battle against the Targaryens in the [Gullet](wiki:Gullet).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_conquest:_the_reign_of_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) During the [Sistermen's Rebellion](wiki:Sistermen's_Rebellion) after the Conquest, the [north](wiki:North) hired a Braavosi fleet to transport Ser [Warrick Manderly](wiki:Warrick_Manderly)'s host to the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters). Queen [Marla Sunderland](wiki:Marla_Sunderland) was deposed when the fleet came into sight, and her brother [Steffon](wiki:Steffon_Sunderland) yielded the islands peacefully.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragons_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

### Jaehaerys I

Following the death of Septon [Moon](wiki:Moon_(septon)) in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), some speculated that his murderer had been a [Faceless Man](wiki:Faceless_Man) from Braavos.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

In [49 AC](wiki:49_AC), the [Sealord of Braavos](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos) sent his son to attend the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) of the Queen Regent, [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), and the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) The next year, [Rego Draz](wiki:Rego_Draz), the [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), took out a loan at several banks, including the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) brought a company of mummers from Braavos to entertain the court of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

In [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), Lady [Elissa Farman](wiki:Elissa_Farman) traveled to Braavos with three [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_eggs) she had stolen from [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). By the time she arrived, she had taken on the name "Alys Westhill". Under this alias, she secured an audience with the Sealord, who bought the dragon eggs from her.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) She used the gold she received in return for the construction of her *[Sun Chaser](wiki:Sun_Chaser)*, built by the Braavosi shipwrights.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) The next year, the night before Alys set sail from Braavos with her new ship, the Sealord summoned her to [his palace](wiki:Sealord's_Palace) and warned her to leave Braavos, so the Braavosi would not become involved in another struggle with Valyrians.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

In King's Landing in [56 AC](wiki:56_AC), King Jaehaerys asked the Iron Bank's envoy if the bank had financed the construction of *Sun Chaser* or had knowledge of the dragon eggs, but the envoy denied all.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) In [57 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth) traveled to Braavos to speak with the Sealord about the three dragon eggs. The Sealord refused to admit he had them in his possession, and was amused by Jaehaerys's veiled threat on [dragons](wiki:Dragon). After threatening to employ the [Faceless Men](wiki:Faceless_Men) to assassinate "a well-chosen few" in King's Landing, the Sealord suggested a trade to which Barth eventually consented. Claiming that the eggs were only "pretty stones" until they hatched, the Sealord convinced the Iron Bank to cancel the Iron Throne's large debts. According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), upon learning of Barth's visit, Jaehaerys reluctantly agreed to the deal, as long as the eggs remained unhatched.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Ser [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) sailed his *[Ice Wolf](wiki:Ice_Wolf)* to Braavos, and from there to [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea), [Hardhome](wiki:Hardhome), [Lorath](wiki:Lorath), and the [Port of Ibben](wiki:Port_of_Ibben).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabthe_heirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

### Viserys and the Dance

During the reign of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), Lady [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon) was betrothed to the son of the [Sealord of Braavos](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos). The Sealord died before the marriage could take place, however, and his son had proven to be a wastrel and fool, who spent all of his father's wealth and remained on [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) permanently. Laena's father, Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), repeatedly postponed the wedding, as he did not want it to continue, yet saw no way to gracefully rid himself of the man. Thus, they were betrothed for a decade, until Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) in [115 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) spoke privately with Corlys about a marriage to Laena, provoked her betrothed until he demanded single combat, and then killed the Braavosi man.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister) entrusted one fourth of the Crown's gold to the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos), to keep it out of the hands of Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) After the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit), Rhaenyra sailed from [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) on a Braavosi ship, *[Violande](wiki:Violande)*.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) Close to war's end, Lady [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn) sent envoys to Braavos to hire ships so she could bring her army to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) from [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown) in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragon_-_the_sad.2C_short_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) The Braavosi eventually landed Lord [Leowyn Corbray](wiki:Leowyn_Corbray) at Duskendale and Ser [Corwyn Corbray](wiki:Corwyn_Corbray) at [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)

Envoys from Braavos attended the coronation of King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) and his wedding to [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

### Daughters' War and the Lysene Spring

When the [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy) collapsed into the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War), Braavos joined an alliance with [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) and [Lorath](wiki:Lorath) against the feuding cities of [Lys](wiki:Lys), [Myr](wiki:Myr), and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-1) Braavos suffered from [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-2) Although Lorath eventually left the alliance, Pentoshi sellswords gained most of the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) and Braavosi ships controlled the nearby sea.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), sent Ser [Gedmund Peake](wiki:Gedmund_Peake) with the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) to expel [Racallio Ryndoon](wiki:Racallio_Ryndoon) from the remaining Stepstones and establish a [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) presence on the islands. At [Tarth](wiki:Tarth), however, Gedmund paused after learning that the [Sealord of Braavos](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos) had allied with Racallio and the [Archon of Tyrosh](wiki:Archon_of_Tyrosh) to jointly rule the Stepstones. While Gedmund was waiting at Tarth for Unwin's instructions, Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) led his own fleets south and surprised the Braavosi ships in the Stepstones. The sinking of *[Grand Defiance](wiki:Grand_Defiance)*, the pride of the Sealord's fleet, during [Lord Alyn's attack](wiki:Lord_Alyn's_attack) earned him the nickname of Alyn Oakenfist. Lord Velaryon returned to King's Landing as a hero, but his independent action outraged the King's Hand.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-1)

Lord [Manfryd Mooton](wiki:Manfryd_Mooton) led a diplomatic mission to Braavos to prevent war between the Free City and the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), as well as return a captured [elephant](wiki:Elephant) to the [Sealord's menagerie](wiki:Sealord's_menagerie). After displaying the mighty [Arsenal](wiki:Arsenal) to Manfryd, the Sealord agreed to dissolve the alliance with Tyrosh and Racallio in return for a large indemnity. Lord [Follard](wiki:House_Follard) became infatuated with a [courtesan](wiki:Courtesan), Ser [Herman Rollingford](wiki:Herman_Rollingford) was killed by a dueling [bravo](wiki:Bravo), and Ser [Denys Harte](wiki:Denys_Harte) allegedly hired the [Faceless Men](wiki:Faceless_Men) to assassinate a rival in King's Landing. [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) refused the Sealord's offer to remain in the city.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-2)

The Daughters' War eventually ended with the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands) divided between Lys, Myr, and Tyrosh, and most of the Stepstones controlled by [Dorne](wiki:Dorne).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) Braavosi ships frequented King's Landing during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring). The sudden deaths of [Lysandro](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare) and [Drazenko Rogare](wiki:Drazenko_Rogare) were widely believed to have been at the hands of Faceless Men, with the [Iron Bank](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos) considered as one of many possible employers. When [House Rogare](wiki:House_Rogare) collapsed, [Moredo Rogare](wiki:Moredo_Rogare) fled the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) for Braavos. After receiving gold from the Iron Bank, Moredo hired mercenaries in Tyrosh for an attack on Lys.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-1)

### Recent History

Over the past two centuries, Braavos has fought six wars against [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) concerning the practice of slavery and the lands located in between the two cities. Four of these wars were won by Braavos. The last war ended in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), after [Nevio Narratys](wiki:Nevio_Narratys), [Prince of Pentos](wiki:Prince_of_Pentos), convinced the [magisters](wiki:Magister) of Pentos to sue for peace after winning a rare victory. In the peace accords, Pentos was forced by Braavos to make several concessions, including the termination of [slavery](wiki:Slavery) in Pentos and their withdrawal from the slave trade.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_pentos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)

After Princess [Naerys Targaryen](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen) almost died in labor in [161 AC](wiki:161_AC), her brother and husband, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) was sent on a diplomatic mission to Braavos. There he met [Bellegere Otherys](wiki:Bellegere_Otherys) the daughter of a Sealord's son and a princess from the [Summer Isles](wiki:Summer_Isles), with whom he started an affair that would last for a decade and yield three children.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Mercy_18-6)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-55) Bellegere was called the [Black Pearl of Braavos](wiki:Black_Pearl_of_Braavos), and her daughter [Bellenora](wiki:Bellenora_Otherys) became the first [courtesan](wiki:Courtesan) by that name; her descendants have been Black Pearls until the present.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-19)

During the reign of [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), Ser [Michael Manwoody](wiki:Michael_Manwoody) was sent to Braavos on several occasions to negotiate with the [Iron Bank](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0)

In [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) announced he was going to "bring the [Titan](wiki:Titan_of_Braavos) to its knees" after a dispute with the Iron Bank. However, [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), settled the dispute by repaying the loans made by Aerys's father, King [Jaehaerys II](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen), with his own money.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0) After [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), the last two surviving children of Aerys, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) and Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), were taken to Braavos by Ser [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry) and four other loyal men, where they lived in a [house with a red door](wiki:House_with_the_red_door).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) While they were staying there, Willem signed a [secret marriage pact](wiki:Secret_marriage_pact) with [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell). This betrothed [Arianne](wiki:Arianne_Martell), the daughter of [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), to Viserys, in return for [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) supporting Viserys's claim to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) However, Willem fell ill around [289 AC](wiki:289_AC) and died soon after.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rawoiafdaenerys_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) After his death, servants stole the money remaining to the Targaryens, who were forced to leave the house. Daenerys and Viserys began to wander the Free Cities,(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-1) although they visited Braavos at least once more during later years.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0)

The great-grandfather of [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) was born in Braavos. He came to the [Vale](wiki:Vale) as a sellsword in hire of Lord [Corbray](wiki:House_Corbray). His son, upon receiving knighthood, took the Titan of Braavos for his sigil.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0)

Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) once visited Braavos with his second wife, [Lynesse Hightower](wiki:Lynesse_Hightower). While there, he borrowed heavily from moneylenders from the city.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0) When he was exiled from [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) several years later, he wanted to return to Braavos, but as per his wife's request of a warmer location, he went to [Lys](wiki:Lys) instead, and as a sellsword, fought on the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne) against the Braavosi.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0) [Allard Seaworth](wiki:Allard_Seaworth) has a girl in Braavos.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0)

## Appearances & Description

### Location

Braavos sprawls across a hundred islands in a vast lagoon(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) known as the [Great Lagoon](wiki:Great_Lagoon),(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Bmercy_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Cmercy.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) found where the [Shivering Sea](wiki:Shivering_Sea) and the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) come together.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) The [Volantenes](wiki:Volantis) are fond of boasting that the hundred isles of Braavos can be dropped and drowned in their own harbor at the mouth of the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

The nearby mainland is mostly marshland, whilst the seaward sides of Braavos are protected by tall, mountainous islands which form a semi-circle around the city, such as [Sellagoro's Shield](wiki:Sellagoro's_Shield). Firewood is brought in by barge, while pines on the islands surrounding the city are left to act as windbreaks to protect the harbors, and are illegal to cut down.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

To enter the Great Lagoon, one must pass beneath the [Titan of Braavos](wiki:Titan_of_Braavos), a stone fortress in the shape of a huge warrior.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) The Titan roars every dawn(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) and every sunset,(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) and appears to roar every hour as well,(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Mercy_18-0) as well as when a ship is approaching.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) Its roar can be heard across the city, faint, but capable of waking the sleeping city.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Just beyond the Titan is located the [Arsenal](wiki:Arsenal), a vast shipyard where a warship can be built in a day and which is warned by the Titan.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)

In [autumn](wiki:Autumn), Braavos generally knows three types of weather: fog, rain, and freezing rain. However, there are also days that are clear, crisp, and bright.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) Close to winter, the canals can have ice on them.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

There is a stretch of land to the south claimed by Braavos, the [Braavosian Coastland](wiki:Braavosian_Coastland).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-20) The western coast of [Lorath Bay](wiki:Lorath_Bay) is also under Braavosi control.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) The Braavosi fleets dominate the seas northwest of [Essos](wiki:Essos), protected by the warlords belonging to the [Sealord](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_shivering_sea.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

### Canals

Braavos is comprised of a hundred islands linked together by small stone bridges spanning the many canals throughout the city. The houses in the humbler regions are tightly packed together, even over the canals.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Mercy_18-1) One of the bigger canals is the [Canal of Heroes](wiki:Canal_of_Heroes), located closest to the [Titan of Braavos](wiki:Titan_of_Braavos), the [Chequy Port](wiki:Chequy_Port), and the [Arsenal](wiki:Arsenal). Multiple bridges cross the Canal of Heroes, and along both sides are rows of statues of deceased [Sealords](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) The canal leads directly to the center of the city, where the [Isle of the Gods](wiki:Isle_of_the_Gods) is located.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-4)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtloiafbraavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) The [Green Canal](wiki:Green_Canal) leads from the [Palace of Truth](wiki:Palace_of_Truth) to the east side of the lagoon,(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtloiafbraavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) and the [Black Canal](wiki:Black_Canal) can be crossed when traveling to the [Ragman's Harbor](wiki:Ragman's_Harbor).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

Also of note is the [Long Canal](wiki:Long_Canal), the broadest of the major waterways, leading from the center of the city to the south of the lagoon, where a fishmarket is located.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtloiafbraavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2) The [First Law of Braavos](wiki:First_Law_of_Braavos) has been engraved in stone on an arch spanning this canal.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) Along the Long Canal, the Palace of Truth can be found, as well as the tall square towers of the [Prestayns](wiki:Prestayns) to the west and the [Antaryons](wiki:Antaryons) to the east. South of these towers, the large grey aqueduct of the [sweetwater river](wiki:Sweetwater_river) crosses the Long Canal, after the [Silty Town](wiki:Silty_Town) is passed on the east side of the canal. Here, the houses are smaller, and less grand.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3)

### Nature

There are no trees to be found within the city (except in the courts and gardens of the mighty)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) making Braavos a flat city of stone architecture and granite monuments.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-5) The streets are lined with houses made of grey stone, built so close that they lean upon one another. Some houses are built above waterways. They are usually slim buildings with peaked tile roofs, raised four to five stories tall.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-6) Braavos has no walls.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-7) The islands like [Sellagoro's Shield](wiki:Sellagoro's_Shield) surrounding Braavos are covered with [soldier pines](wiki:Soldier_pine) and black spruce,(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-8) which act as windbreaks to protect the harbor. It is illegal to cut these trees down. Because of this, and the lack of wood in the remainder of the city, the price of firewood, which has to be brought in by barge, is high.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

As the water from the lagoon is brackish and briny and the canal water is not drinkable without first boiling it,(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-9) the Braavosi have build the [sweetwater river](wiki:Sweetwater_river) to supply themselves with clean water. A grey stone roadway supported by three tiers of arches traveling from the south, looming above the canals and houses, it brings fresh water from the mainland across the mudflats and the briny shallows. Rich men pipe it directly into their homes, while commoners fill buckets at public fountains.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) The sweetwater river ends at the [Moon Pool](wiki:Moon_Pool).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-4)

### Drowned Town

The [Drowned Town](wiki:Drowned_Town) is the eldest part of the city.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) It is located in an area in the north-west of the city,(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtloiafbraavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3) directly north of the [Ragman's Harbor](wiki:Ragman's_Harbor),(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) where the town has fallen into the lagoon and only the domes and towers of the old buildings are visible above the surface of the water.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-5) In the higher part of the half-submerged buildings, some of the poorer people still live.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9) The [Spotted Cellar](wiki:Spotted_Cellar) is located near the gates to the Drowned Town. There, eel fights are held.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-6) East of the Drowned Town, the [Gate](wiki:Gate_(playhouse)) can be found.

### Ports

Scattered throughout the city are small wharves and landings for fishermen and ferries.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4) Some of the wharves are located below the Drowned Town.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Further, the city is served by three main harbors: the [Chequy Port](wiki:Chequy_Port), located directly behind the [Arsenal](wiki:Arsenal), where ships arriving in Braavos have to submit to inspection by the Sealord's customs officers upon arrival in the city;(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-10) the [Purple Harbor](wiki:Purple_Harbor), located in the north of the city beneath the towers and domes of the [Sealord's Palace](wiki:Sealord's_Palace), a harbor for Braavosi ships only;(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-5)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) and the [Ragman's Harbor](wiki:Ragman's_Harbor),(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-6) also called the Outer Harbor,(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Mercy_18-2) located in the west of the city, where all foreign ships are allowed to dock. Ragman's is poorer, rougher, dirtier, and noisier than the Purple Harbor.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-7)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-7)

### Temples

All gods are honored in Braavos, and therefore, there are many temples and shrines to be found in the city. Most of the temples are located on the [Isle of the Gods](wiki:Isle_of_the_Gods) in the center of the city.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-11)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtloiafbraavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-4) Amongst them is the [Temple of the Moonsingers](wiki:Temple_of_the_Moonsingers), who led the original Braavosi refugees to the islands. Theirs is the biggest temple, built of white [marble](wiki:Marble) capped with a silvered dome, milk glass windows showing all the phases of the moon, and a pair of marble maidens flanks its gates. Other temples of note are the temple of the [Father of Waters](wiki:Father_of_Waters), which is rebuilt whenever he takes a new bride, the [Temple of the Lord of Light](wiki:Temple_of_the_Lord_of_Light_(Braavos)), and the [Sept-Beyond-the-Sea](wiki:Sept-Beyond-the-Sea), where only [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) come to pray.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-12)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) Homage is done to the [Many-Faced God](wiki:Many-Faced_God) at the [House of Black and White](wiki:House_of_Black_and_White), the home of the secretive [Faceless Men](wiki:Faceless_Men).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2)

Other sites include the shrine of the [Weeping Lady of Lys](wiki:Weeping_Lady_of_Lys), the [Gardens of Gelenei](wiki:Gardens_of_Gelenei), the wooden hall of the [Lord of Harmony](wiki:Lord_of_Harmony), the house of the [Great Shepherd](wiki:Great_Shepherd), a three-turreted tower honoring [Trios](wiki:Trios), the [Stones of the Silent God](wiki:Stones_of_the_Silent_God), the [Patternmaker's Maze](wiki:Patternmaker's_Maze), the temple of [Aquan the Red Bull](wiki:Aquan_the_Red_Bull), and twin temples honoring [Semosh and Selloso](wiki:Semosh_and_Selloso),(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3) and the temple of the [Cult of Starry Wisdom](wiki:Church_of_Starry_Wisdom).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) Even gods that have no more followers are honored in a temple called the [Holy Refuge](wiki:Holy_Refuge) or, colloquially, the Warren.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-13)

### People

By and large, Braavosi are a kind people,(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5) great lovers of song.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Wealthy Braavosi dress in charcoal grey, purple, blues so dark that are almost black, and blacks as dark as moonlight. Sword-wielding [bravos](wiki:Bravos), in contrast, dress in flamboyant colors.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-13) [Keyholders](wiki:Keyholders) of the [Iron Bank](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos) wear drab coats of brown and grey.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-11) Instead of traveling by horse, the Braavosi use boats.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) The Braavosi tongue is a bastard version of [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian),(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) and the [trade tongue](wiki:Trade_tongue) is also spoken among the wharves, docks, and sailor's taverns.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-12) The Braavosi calendar is different from that of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Braavos is renowned worldwide for its [courtesans](wiki:Courtesan). Every courtesan has her own barge and servants to work them. Songs are sung about them, and they are showered with gifts from goldsmiths and craftsmen beg for their custom.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-13) Additionally, pugnacious bravos fill the city, frequently dueling to display their skill. Visibly wearing a sword at night means that you can be challenged to a fight, but the bravos do not bother anyone who does not wear a sword. Bravos are known to kill each other in the name of courtesans, and some take it as a challenge when another claims that another woman than the [Nightingale](wiki:Nightingale_(courtesan)) is the most beautiful woman in the world.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-14)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-14)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) The [Black Pearl](wiki:Black_Pearl) is considered by some to be the most famous courtesan.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-15)

Braavosi culture has created a unique form of sword fighting, the [water dance](wiki:Water_dance). The style is a refined form of fencing in which the practitioner stands sideways and wields a slender blade.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Ragot22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) The greatest of the bravos call themselves water dancers, and they always duel at night. They take their name from the custom of dueling on the [Moon Pool](wiki:Moon_Pool), near the Sealord's Palace.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10)

The ruler of the city is the [Sealord](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos), who lives in the [Sealord's Palace](wiki:Sealord's_Palace) in the northeast of the city. Instead of hereditary succession, the Sealord, currently [Ferrego Antaryon](wiki:Ferrego_Antaryon),(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-6) is chosen by Braavosi [magisters](wiki:Magister) and [keyholders](wiki:Keyholders) through a convoluted process,(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-11) and he serves for life. The selection of his successor can be highly contested and can resort to violence.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-7) The Sealord's personal guard is commanded by the [First Sword](wiki:First_Sword) of Braavos.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-12)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtloiafbraavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-10) The exact position of the [Third Sword](wiki:Third_Sword)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Mercy_18-3) is not known. The [city watch](wiki:City_watch_(Braavos)) also patrol the city.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-4)

Instead of making farces, Braavosi [mummers](wiki:Mummers) perform plays of written stories.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) Braavosi mummers can be found at such playhouses as the [Ship](wiki:Ship_(playhouse)), the [Gate](wiki:Gate_(playhouse)), the [Blue Lantern](wiki:Blue_Lantern), and the [Dome](wiki:Dome).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-15)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-16)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Mercy_18-4) Only in Braavos may mummers openly mock authorities such as the Sealord or portray them in humiliating acts without persecution or punishment.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Mercy_18-5)

The anniversary of the [Uncloaking](wiki:Uncloaking_of_Uthero) is celebrated annually. The masked feasts last for ten days, and culminate in the midnight of the tenth day, when the [Titan](wiki:Titan_of_Braavos) roars and all of the celebrants remove their masks at the same time.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-13)

### Economy

Braavos is the wealthiest of the Free Cities, in part due to the wealth and influence of the [Iron Bank](wiki:Iron_Bank), they are rivalled in power by Volantis only.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rawoiafbraavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) The Iron Bank lends money to foreign nations, including the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Braavosi coins are square and made from iron.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-5)

During the years when Braavos was as of yet undiscovered, the city prospered. The Braavosi discovered a sea [snail](wiki:Snail) similar to those that could be found at [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), and whose [dyes](wiki:Hair_dye) had made the city famous. The snail found by the Braavosi yielded dark purple dye, which the Braavosi used to color the sails of the ships the founders had stolen from the Valyrians.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-14) They paint their hulls purple too.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) At first, Braavos began to trade with [Ib](wiki:Ib), and later the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-15)

Braavos has a seafaring trade culture. Much of the city's early wealth came from the brackish waters of the lagoon. These waters yielded oysters, eels, crabs, crawfish, clams, rays, and many sorts of fish. Braavosi [ships](wiki:Ships) paint their hulls purple and their merchant ships sail to many distant lands and bring their trade and wealth back home. One hears of purple sails as far away as [Asshai](wiki:Asshai) and the islands of the [Jade Sea](wiki:Jade_Sea). In the present, Braavos is one of the greatest ports of the world, where all but slavers are allowed to dock. Even ships from [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) and the [Summer Isles](wiki:Summer_Isles) come to Braavos to trade.(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-16)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)(wiki:Braavos.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-14)
