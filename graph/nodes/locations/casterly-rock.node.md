## Origins

### House Casterly

The region was probably inhabited by [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) and [giants](wiki:Giants) before the arrival of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), and numerous animals have made it their home as well.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9)

Legend says that Casterly Rock is named for the family of First Men who ruled it in the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age) and the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), the [Casterlys](wiki:House_Casterly). After their founder, [Corlos](wiki:Corlos), discovered [gold](wiki:Gold) within the Rock, the Casterlys fortified the entrance and carved halls and tunnels deep into the stone.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

The [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) trace their rule over the Rock back to [Lann the Clever](wiki:Lann_the_Clever), who tricked the Casterlys out of their castle and claimed it for his own.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Some claim that Lann's [ghost](wiki:Ghosts) still haunts the fortress,(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) which has never fallen.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-10)

### Kings of the Rock

The [Kings of the Rock](wiki:Kings_of_the_Rock) from House Lannister took their title from their seat, and members of the family founded nearby [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport). During the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to Westeros, the Lannisters eventually invited [Andals](wiki:Andals) into their realm and brought Andal youths to serve at Casterly Rock as wards and potential hostages.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) According to a lost scroll, [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) sorcerers believed their downfall would come from the Rock's gold.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)

Impressed by earlier visits to Casterly Rock and the [Hightower](wiki:Hightower), [Roland I Arryn](wiki:Roland_I_Arryn) ordered the construction of the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) as a replacement for the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon) as the seat of [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_the_eyrie.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

King [Gerold Lannister](wiki:Gerold_Lannister_(king)) raided the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) and held one hundred captives in the Rock. Gerold the Great then hanged a captive whenever the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) threatened his lands.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Before becoming [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands), [Harmund II Hoare](wiki:Harmund_II_Hoare) spent his youth at Casterly Rock as a [ward](wiki:Fosterage).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), King [Loren I](wiki:Loren_I_Lannister) formed an alliance at Casterly Rock with [Mern IX Gardener](wiki:Mern_IX_Gardener), [King of the Reach](wiki:King_of_the_Reach), against [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen). [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) defeated the Lannisters and Gardeners at the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire), however. After the battle, Loren the Last surrendered his crown to Aegon and was confirmed as Lord of Casterly Rock and [Warden of the West](wiki:Warden_of_the_West).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) is said to have been grateful that Loren rode to war instead of seeking refuge in his castle, as the Rock may have withstood even [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-11)

### Iron Throne

Casterly Rock hosted King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) several times during his royal progresses.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

After the ascension of King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), Lord [Lyman Lannister](wiki:Lyman_Lannister) hosted Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) and Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) at Casterly Rock, where the princess gave birth to [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) and [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon)) at the Rock.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) The son of [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling_(wife_of_Maegor_I)) was sent to Casterly Rock to be Lyman's ward.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1)

After being expelled from [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle) during the reign of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), Rhaena flew from [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle) to the Rock on [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre). Lyman gave the [Four-Headed Beast](wiki:Four-Headed_Beast) a suite within his castle, but Rhaena eventually left after becoming convinced that the Lord of Casterly Rock wanted her [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_eggs).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Rhaena visited the Rock during her search throughout the Seven Kingdoms for the missing Aerea.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Ser [Alastor Reyne](wiki:Alastor_Reyne), master-at-arms of the Rock, participated in the [tourney for the completion of the Dragonpit](wiki:Tourney_for_the_completion_of_the_Dragonpit).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_.E2.80.93_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Jaehaerys visited Casterly Rock during his progresses in [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and [88 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_.E2.80.93_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1)

### Dalton and the Dance

The young Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) was once courted during a feast at Casterly Rock by the twins [Jason Lannister](wiki:Jason_Lannister) and [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

[House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) declared for the [greens](wiki:Greens) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). At the start of the civil war, Ser Tyland, [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) to King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), sent a quarter of the royal treasury from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to the Rock for safekeeping.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.5D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) The castle was left in disarray after the death of Lord Jason in the [Battle of the Red Fork](wiki:Battle_of_the_Red_Fork) and the Lannister defeat in the [Battle by the Lakeshore](wiki:Battle_by_the_Lakeshore), however.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), husband to Aegon's rival Rhaenyra, suggested that Casterly Rock be stripped from the Lannisters and granted to Ser [Hugh Hammer](wiki:Hugh_Hammer), but the queen declined.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

The [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) declared for Rhaenyra's [blacks](wiki:Blacks) during the Dance. Although [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), was able to sack [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), Casterly Rock was too strong for the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) once Jason's widow, Lady [Johanna Lannister](wiki:Johanna_Lannister), barred its gates.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1) Johanna approved an expedition to recover [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle) from the ironborn, but the Red Kraken sent the heads of Ser [Erwin Lannister](wiki:Erwin_Lannister) and Lords [Prester](wiki:House_Prester) and [Tarbeck](wiki:House_Tarbeck) to Johanna at the Rock after the plan was betrayed.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

As the Dance wound down, Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) considered marching to subdue [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), and Casterly Rock. Lady Johanna agreed to peace terms previously sent by Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), however.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

Dalton was slain at [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC). When the fleet of Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) arrived in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), Johanna presented him with a seahorse wrought in gold.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfirst.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) Ser [Leo Costayne](wiki:Leo_Costayne) invaded the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands), and the captured [Rodrik Greyjoy](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy_(son_of_Dalton)) was made into a [fool](wiki:Fool) at Casterly Rock.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

### Lord Tytos

Lady [Ellyn Reyne](wiki:Ellyn_Reyne), the wife of Ser [Tion Lannister](wiki:Tion_Lannister), held a splendid court at the Rock as her good-father, Lord [Gerold Lannister](wiki:Gerold_Lannister), declined in health. Ellyn's influence declined after Tion's death in the [Fourth Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Fourth_Blackfyre_Rebellion), however, and she left the Rock in [239 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) after Gerold had her wed Lord [Walderan Tarbeck](wiki:Walderan_Tarbeck).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

Gerold was succeeded by his ineffectual son [Tytos](wiki:Tytos_Lannister), a lord easily taken advantage of and mocked at feasts and balls.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1) When Lord Tytos announced at a feast that his daughter [Genna](wiki:Genna_Lannister) would wed [Emmon Frey](wiki:Emmon_Frey), the second son of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), the western lords were shocked and Tytos's ten-year-old heir, [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), publicly denounced the betrothal.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

After returning from the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings), young Ser Tywin sought to restore the power of Casterly Rock. Those who were unable to repay loans were required to send hostages to the Rock; [Dorna Swyft](wiki:Dorna_Swyft) was one such hostage. Lord Tarbeck rode to Casterly Rock to speak with Lord Tytos, but Tywin had him imprisoned in a dungeon before Walderan could meet with his father. Ellyn Tarbeck captured [Stafford Lannister](wiki:Stafford_Lannister) and two [Lannisters of Lannisport](wiki:House_Lannister_of_Lannisport), so Lord Tytos forced the feuding factions to release their hostages.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-2)

In [261 AC](wiki:261_AC), Ser Tywin ordered Lord [Roger Reyne](wiki:Roger_Reyne), Ser [Reynard Reyne](wiki:Reynard_Reyne), and the Tarbecks to present themselves at Casterly Rock. The bannermen refused, however, as Tywin expected. Without Tytos's leave, the Lannister heir gathered a host and quickly crushed the [Reyne-Tarbeck revolt](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt). Tywin was named [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) by [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) the following year.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-3)

[House Clegane](wiki:House_Clegane) was founded after the kennelmaster of the Rock saved Tytos from a lioness attack.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Racok18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) Late in his life, Tytos allowed [his mistress](wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress) to sit beside him in his hall.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Tytos died in [267 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) while climbing the stairs to her bedchamber.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-4)

### Lord Tywin

For much of [267 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) resided at Casterly Rock with Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Tywin repaid the late [Jaehaerys II Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen)'s debts to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) with gold from the Rock.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) When Tywin's children, the twins [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) and [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), were caught experimenting with each other by a maid, Lady [Joanna Lannister](wiki:Joanna_Lannister) had her children sleep in distant bedchambers within the Rock.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

Shortly after the birth of [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), the young [Elia](wiki:Elia_Martell) and [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) accompanied their mother, the [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne), to Casterly Rock when she met with Lord Tywin. The [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) could hear the howling baby from the depths of the Rock.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) Tyrion and his elder brother, Jaime, played in the Hall of Heroes as boys.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Tyrion, who was interested in [dragons](wiki:Dragon), would start fires in the bowels of Casterly Rock and pretend they were [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

Aerys's decision to name Ser Jaime to the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) in [281 AC](wiki:281_AC) angered Tywin, who resigned as Hand and returned to Casterly Rock with his daughter, [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-1) During [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Tywin led a host from the Rock to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) following the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), and Jaime slew Aerys during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_end.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

Although outlaws are not common near Casterly Rock, Jaime and his younger brother Tyrion saved [Tysha](wiki:Tysha) from two bandits on the road between [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) and the Rock around [286 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

[Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) was captured after the [sea battle off Fair Isle](wiki:Sea_battle_off_Fair_Isle) in [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), taken to Lannisport in chains, and spent the rest of [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion) imprisoned in Casterly Rock.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

When Tyrion became a man at sixteen, he was forbidden by Tywin from taking a tour of the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) as his uncles [Gerion](wiki:Gerion_Lannister) and [Tygett](wiki:Tygett_Lannister) had done. Instead Tywin gave his son charge of the cisterns and drains at Casterly Rock.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) Tyrion learned a few of the customs of [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) from a [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) at the Rock.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Radwd1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

According to Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) fathered twins on a serving woman at Casterly Rock when Tywin hosted a tourney in [295 AC](wiki:295_AC), but the two were killed on the orders of Queen Cersei.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) Following the [tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_Prince_Joffrey's_name_day) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in [298 AC](wiki:298_AC), Cersei and her children accompanied her father, Tywin, during his return to Casterly Rock.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Casterly Rock](wiki:Category:Images_of_Casterly_Rock)*

Casterly Rock is carved out of a colossal stone hill beside the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rawoiafcasterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) It is popularly believed to resemble a [lion](wiki:Lion) in repose at sunset.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) According to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin), the Rock is two leagues (six miles) long from west to east and has a width of two miles from north to south. Its peak is about 2,100 feet high which makes it three times as high as the highest point of the [Wall](wiki:Wall) and taller than the [Hightower](wiki:Hightower) of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) too.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-A_Couple_of_Rocks_7-0)(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

The [Casterlys](wiki:House_Casterly) of antiquity built a [ringfort](wiki:Ringfort) on the peak, and as millennia have passed its natural defenses have been expanded with walls, gates, and watchtowers.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) The base of the Rock contains large sea-carved caverns. The stone has been mined for thousands of years, so there are hundreds of mineshafts in the depths of the Rock, as well as yet untouched [gold](wiki:Gold) veins.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

The face of the mount has windows and arrow slits scattered all over, they look small from the outside but it is only an illusion due to the huge size of the Rock. The entire stronghold—tunnels, dungeons, storerooms, barracks, halls, grand halls, stables, stairways, courtyards, balconies, gardens, a [sept](wiki:Sept), passages, caves, mines, galleries, chutes, wells, barracks, armories, bedchambers, servant's quarters, etc.—lies within the Rock itself. The only exception being the watchtower on the top of the mount that is also used by the [maesters](wiki:Maester) as a [rookery](wiki:Rookery).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-A_Couple_of_Rocks_7-1) According to George R. R. Martin, this particularity makes Casterly Rock the strongest and most impregnable seat in all of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-A_Couple_of_Rocks_7-2)

In the bowels of the Rock are rooms where caged lions were once kept,(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) cells for the worst prisoners,(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Radwd66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) as well as [oubliettes](wiki:Oubliette) so small that a man cannot even sit down in one.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

The Lion's Mouth, the main entry to Casterly Rock, is an enormous natural cavern reaching two hundred feet high located on the south face of the Rock. It is accessible via a great stone stairway with steps wide enough for twenty riders. There are sea gates at the base of the west face, large enough for galleys and cogs to sail into the caverns under the stone, where the Lannisters have their own protected docks, as well as wharves, and shipyards.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-A_Couple_of_Rocks_7-3)(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) However, the bulk of the Lannister fleet anchors in the harbor of [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport),(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) the port city that lies close by to the south of the Rock.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-13) From below the Rock thunder can be heard, where the sea comes in.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

The Golden Gallery contains treasures of the Lannisters, including gilded ornaments and walls.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6) The Hall of Heroes is where the Lannisters and their close kin who have died valiantly are interred.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) The armor of Lannisters of old are also displayed in the hall.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) The Stone Garden is a [godswood](wiki:Godswood) in a cave within the Rock. It contains a twisted [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood), a tenth the size of [Raventree Hall](wiki:Raventree_Hall)'s, whose tangled roots have almost filled the cave, choking out all other growth.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8)(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) tells Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) that he wanted young [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) to foster at Casterly Rock with Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), but the boy's mother took him to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) after the death of Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

After the capture of [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) by Eddard's wife, Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), Tyrion's brother [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) wounds Ned in the streets of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and then rides for the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) Jaime raises a host at Casterly Rock(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) and Tywin raises another,(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) and they invade the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) after the death of King Robert.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-1)

After the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), Ser [Marq Piper](wiki:Marq_Piper) suggests attacking Casterly Rock but receives little support from the rebel nobles at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Ser [Stafford Lannister](wiki:Stafford_Lannister) begins raising a new host at Casterly Rock during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings),(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) but Stafford and his westermen are defeated by [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), in the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)

Expecting the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) to ally with the [northmen](wiki:North), [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) desires to conquer Casterly Rock and claim it for himself. His father, [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), states the Rock is too strong, however, and instead attacks the north.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Tyrion demands his birthright of the lordship of Casterly Rock from his father, Tywin, and is harshly rejected.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0)

Tyrion suggests to his new wife, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), that they visit the Rock, wanting to show her where he and Jaime grew up, but also desiring to keep her away from the abuses of his nephew, King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon). She remains impassive, however, so Tyrion withdraws his offer.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

Resting on a [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) stump en route to King's Landing, Jaime dreams of watery caverns beneath the Rock.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0)

King Joffrey dies at his own [royal wedding](wiki:Purple_Wedding).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) Lord Tywin intends to remove his son Jaime from the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) so he can again be heir to Casterly Rock, and Tywin wants the new king, his grandson [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), to serve as Jaime's ward and squire at the Rock where he can learn to be a Lannister. Jaime opposes the plan, however.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) Tywin's daughter, Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), believes she is to be sent to the Rock so she cannot influence her royal son.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0) Tywin is killed in the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand) by Tyrion.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Tywin's brother, Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), advises Cersei to return to the westerlands to take her place as Lady of Casterly Rock. Cersei insists on remaining in King's Landing and refuses to name Kevan as Tommen's regent,(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0) and she names Ser [Damion Lannister](wiki:Damion_Lannister), a distant cousin of the main branch, as castellan of the Rock instead of Kevan.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0) Tywin's body is escorted back to Casterly Rock by an honor guard of western knights and nobles.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0)

After Cersei's arrest by the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), Ser [Harys Swyft](wiki:Harys_Swyft) and Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) invite Kevan to return from the Rock and assume the regency.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0)

As a condition of Jaime's ending of the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), Lord [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) is to become an honored captive at the Rock. His pregnant wife [Roslin](wiki:Roslin_Frey) is to join him from the [Twins](wiki:Twins) once she gives birth.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0)(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

### A Dance with Dragons

An exile in [Essos](wiki:Essos), Tyrion considers hiring the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) to help him gain Casterly Rock, but the sellswords follow Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)).(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0) Tyrion instead joins the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons) and gains their support by promising them [gold](wiki:Gold) from the Rock.(wiki:Casterly_Rock.cite_ref-Radwd66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)
