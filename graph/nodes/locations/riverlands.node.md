## Origins

### First Men

During the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age) the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) settled in the riverlands, coming into conflict with the native [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest). After centuries of fighting, a [pact](wiki:Pact) was signed at the [Isle of Faces](wiki:Isle_of_Faces), the children withdrew to their forests, while the First Men raised their kingdoms in the lands ceded by the mysterious folk.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

During the thousands of years that followed, various families ruled the riverlands as river kings, claiming the titles [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident) or King of the Rivers and the Hills. Many dynasties claimed these lands during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), including [Fishers](wiki:House_Fisher), [Brackens](wiki:House_Bracken), [Blackwoods](wiki:House_Blackwood), and [Mudds](wiki:House_Mudd), who were the last of the First Men to claim the riverlands as their own.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rssm1243.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rssm1028.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

The time of the First Men river kings came to an end with the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals). Remembered in song are the [Fall of Maidenpool](wiki:Fall_of_Maidenpool), the [Widow's Ford](wiki:Widow's_Ford), the [White Wood](wiki:White_Wood), and the [Battle of Bitter River](wiki:Battle_of_Bitter_River).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8) King [Tristifer IV Mudd](wiki:Tristifer_IV_Mudd) raised his armies and met the Andal invaders. Tristifer was said to have won ninety-nine out of his hundred battles against the Andals, but in the final battle he was killed. His son and successor, [Tristifer V](wiki:Tristifer_V_Mudd), was not as successful as his father and the kingdom fell.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

### Andals

The victorious Andals raised their own kingdoms and dynasties. [House Justman](wiki:House_Justman) united the riverlands for almost three centuries, and King [Benedict II Justman](wiki:Benedict_II_Justman) expanded their realm east to [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), [Rosby](wiki:Rosby), and the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9) However, the sons of King [Bernarr II Justman](wiki:Bernarr_II_Justman) were murdered by [Qhored Hoare](wiki:Qhored_Hoare) in the [Bloody Keep](wiki:Bloody_Keep) of [Pyke](wiki:Pyke) in the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands), and the riverlands fell into anarchy when Qhored sacrificed Bernarr to the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God), ending the Justman line.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

The riverlands were then disputed by Houses Blackwood, Bracken, [Vance](wiki:House_Vance), [Mallister](wiki:House_Mallister), and [Charlton](wiki:House_Charlton) for a century. The [Hooks](wiki:House_Hook) were also river kings of old who claimed the mouth of the Blackwater.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) The various kings were pressured by the [Kings of the Iron Islands](wiki:Kings_of_the_Iron_Islands), the [Kings of the Rock](wiki:Kings_of_the_Rock), the [Vale mountain clans](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans), the [Kings of the Reach](wiki:Kings_of_the_Reach), the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_King), and pirates from the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) and the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters). [Torrence Teague](wiki:Torrence_Teague) finally reunited the riverlands, but he and his heirs were disliked by their rebellious subjects.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10) According to a semi-canon source, [House Teague](wiki:House_Teague) may not have had an uninterrupted rule, instead being contested by other river dynasties for generations.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-32)

The last native river kings were [Humfrey I Teague](wiki:Humfrey_I_Teague) and his kin. Humfrey founded numerous [septs](wiki:Sept) and [motherhouses](wiki:Motherhouse) in his kingdom, and he repressed worship of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods). A rebellion began by Lord [Roderick Blackwood](wiki:Roderick_Blackwood) led to the deaths of the Teagues in the [Battle of Six Kings](wiki:Battle_of_Six_Kings). Some maesters believe that [Arlan III Durrandon](wiki:Arlan_III_Durrandon), the [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King) and Roderick's ally, had intended to restore the Blackwoods as river kings, but Roderick's death in the war led to Arlan being the first invader to claim the riverlands in their entirety.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-11)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

### Storm Kings

The Storm Kings of [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon) ruled the riverlands for at least three centuries.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-12) The Durrandons had difficulty ruling their vast realm,(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1) however, and the riverlords rose in rebellion each generation. [Lucifer Justman](wiki:Lucifer_Justman), [Marq Mudd](wiki:Marq_Mudd), Lord [Robert Vance](wiki:Robert_Vance), Lord [Petyr Mallister](wiki:Petyr_Mallister), Lady [Jeyne Nutt](wiki:Jeyne_Nutt), Ser [Addam Rivers](wiki:Addam_Rivers), [Pate of Fairmarket](wiki:Pate_of_Fairmarket), Ser [Lymond Fisher](wiki:Lymond_Fisher), and a dozen others all ruled briefly as petty kings before being put down by the might of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-13) Some members of [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) filled prominent offices and trusted positions for the Storm Kings.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

### House Hoare

The Storm Kings in turn were defeated by the [Iron King](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands), [Harwyn Hardhand](wiki:Harwyn_Hoare), who established his own kingdom from the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) to the riverlands.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-14) The era of the [Kings of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:Kings_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers) is included in *[The Iron Chronicle](wiki:The_Iron_Chronicle)*.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) Harwyn's son, [Halleck Hoare](wiki:Halleck_Hoare), made his seat in [Fairmarket](wiki:Fairmarket) instead of the Iron Islands.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-15)

Harwyn's grandson, [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_the_Black), ordered the construction of an immense castle, [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), as a display of his wealth and power. Construction of Harrenhal took forty years and a huge amount of resources and money to build. Feuding between the [Blackwoods](wiki:House_Blackwood) and the [Brackens](wiki:House_Bracken) during the construction led Harren to punish both houses. Ironically, the same day the most immense castle in history was finished, [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), the [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), [landed in Westeros](wiki:Aegon's_Landing) with his [dragons](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-16)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

### The Conquest

King [Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare) was unpopular within the riverlands when [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest) began. Rather than support [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare), the river lords rose in rebellion and joined [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). The first to do so was [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully), followed by the [Blackwoods](wiki:House_Blackwood), [Mallisters](wiki:House_Mallister), [Vances](wiki:House_Vance), [Brackens](wiki:House_Bracken), [Pipers](wiki:House_Piper), [Freys](wiki:House_Frey), and [Strongs](wiki:House_Strong).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

Harren the Black thought if he refused battle to [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), the invaders would have to besiege Harrenhal with the river lords. Instead, Aegon let his dragon attack from above. The heat produced by the [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame) of [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) was so great that much of Harrenhal burned and melted, killing Harren and his children. After the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal), Aegon raised Lord [Edmyn Tully](wiki:Edmyn_Tully) to dominion over the riverlands, below the sovereignty of the Targaryens, as the [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-17)

Rivermen made up the bulk of Aegon's army at the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) The [Inn of the Kneeling Man](wiki:Inn_of_the_Kneeling_Man) on the Red Fork was built where [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), is believed to have submitted to Aegon.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

### Targaryen Dynasty

Having never been [river kings](wiki:River_kings), [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) has sometimes been overshadowed by their bannermen.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) Lord [Edmyn Tully](wiki:Edmyn_Tully), however, repaired much of the damage caused by the rule of [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-18) In [31 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) Prince [Maegor Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) slew the [Giant of the Trident](wiki:Giant_of_the_Trident), a robber knight.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Shortly after his coronation, King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) visited [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun). [Harren the Red](wiki:Harren_the_Red) rebelled against Aenys and killed [Gargon Qoherys](wiki:Gargon_Qoherys), the [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-19)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

During the reign of Maegor the Cruel, several river lords joined the king in the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye) against his nephew, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-20)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett) led former [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) in the riverlands and the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

### Dance of the Dragons

When the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) began in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), the notoriously-quarrelsome river lords were nominally under the rule of House Tully. Most supported [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) against [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and the [greens](wiki:Greens). Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) led the [assault on Harrenhal](wiki:Assault_on_Harrenhal) and followed up with the [Battle of the Burning Mill](wiki:Battle_of_the_Burning_Mill) and the [taking of Stone Hedge](wiki:Taking_of_Stone_Hedge).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

The green army from the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) was victorious in the [Battle of the Red Fork](wiki:Battle_of_the_Red_Fork)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-1) and at [Acorn Hall](wiki:Battle_at_Acorn_Hall) in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), but was then destroyed by rivermen and [northmen](wiki:North) in the [Battle by the Lakeshore](wiki:Battle_by_the_Lakeshore).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) and his dragon, [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), burned much of the riverlands in response,(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-41) but Aemond was eventually killed in the [Battle Above the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Above_the_Gods_Eye) by Daemon.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1) Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole), the Kingmaker, led an army of greens through the riverlands, but they were hampered by scorched earth tactics. Rivermen and their northern allies crushed Criston and his greens in the [Butcher's Ball](wiki:Butcher's_Ball), an ambush south of the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-2) Riverlords, northmen, and [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) men from the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) continued south, but the blacks were defeated in the [First Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:First_Battle_of_Tumbleton).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-3) Ser [Addam Velaryon](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) led near four thousand rivermen to the [Second Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:Second_Battle_of_Tumbleton).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-4)

Lords [Kermit Tully](wiki:Kermit_Tully) and [Benjicot Blackwood](wiki:Benjicot_Blackwood) led the rivermen who defeated Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) in the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad) in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) Supporters of [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), the [Lads](wiki:Lads) were welcomed when they marched into [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) after Aegon II's death.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2) In the aftermath of the war, more than a thousand northmen settled in the riverlands, marrying women in [Widow Fairs](wiki:Widow_Fairs) and taking service as guards and men-at-arms. The northmen strengthened the riverlords and led a partial revival of worship of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) in the region.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

### Robert's Rebellion

Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) betrothed his daughter, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), to [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark), the heir to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). Brandon and his father, Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark), were executed by King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), however, and Hoster supported the rebels during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) Following the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells) at [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept) in the southern riverlands, Catelyn wed Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) and her sister, [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), wed Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) in a dual wedding at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

A number of Lord Tully's vassals remained loyal to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), including Lords [Darry](wiki:House_Darry), [Goodbrook](wiki:House_Goodbrook), [Mooton](wiki:House_Mooton), and [Ryger](wiki:House_Ryger), however, and Hoster punished the Goodbrooks with fire and sword.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Ragot16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) The [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), which ultimately determined the outcome of the war, was fought in the riverlands. Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) arrived late to the Trident in support of the rebels.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

After [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) abducts [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister),(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) retaliates by sending Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) to raid the riverlands. The [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), tasks [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion) with stopping the raiders.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) Beric's force is ambushed in a [battle at the Mummer's Ford](wiki:Battle_at_the_Mummer's_Ford), however, and Gregor continues killing and plundering.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

### A Clash of Kings

In [299 AC](wiki:299_AC), during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) believes that a host of twenty thousand riverlanders has joined Robb. However, according to [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) the actual number is much less.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)

The acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), strips Lord Janos of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) and exiles him to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) After the defeat of Jaime, Tywin brings his army to Harrenhal instead of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) Robb Stark allows the river lords to leave Riverrun and retake their castles from the Lannisters.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) Sending Ser [Cleos Frey](wiki:Cleos_Frey) as an envoy, Robb claims the lands watered by the Trident and its tributaries, bounded by the [Golden Tooth](wiki:Golden_Tooth) to the west and the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) to the east.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3) However, Tyrion rejects Robb's territorial demands.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) The river lords burn their crops to prevent the Lannisters from taking them, while Tywin has foragers burn villages and slay [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-1)

Robb takes the northern horse and some [rivermen](wiki:Rivermen), including [Freys](wiki:House_Frey)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) and Ser [Wilbert](wiki:Wilbert)'s sons from [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept),(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0) to raid the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) While Robb is away, his mother Catelyn frees Jaime in the hope that he will free her daughters, [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark), whom she believes are held hostage in King's Landing.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) Unknown to Catelyn, however, Arya has been traveling incognito through the war-torn riverlands.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0)

Roose Bolton is able to [recover Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal) and other territory in the riverlands when Tywin leaves to defend the westerlands from Robb.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-1)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-1)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0) Ser [Aenys Frey](wiki:Aenys_Frey) commands fifteen hundred Freys with Roose's host.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-1) Edmure raises eleven thousand soldiers, including eight thousand foot and three thousand horse.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-2) Tywin marches west, but is turned back by Edmure at the [Battle of the Fords](wiki:Battle_of_the_Fords).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0) Tywin then heads southeast to King's Landing to defend his grandson, King Joffrey, from the boy king's uncle, [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

As a reward for negotiating the alliance between House Lannister and [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell), the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) grants Harrenhal to [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) and names him [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident). Petyr remains in King's Landing, however. Ser [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister) receives [Darry](wiki:Darry),(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0) as Lord [Lyman Darry](wiki:Lyman_Darry) had been killed in the [sack of Darry](wiki:Sack_of_Darry).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4)

### A Storm of Swords

Arya Stark meets the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners), a band of outlaws led by a revived Lord [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion). In the name of the late King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), they engage in a guerrilla campaign against House Lannister and the Iron Throne.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0)

Robb, King in the North and [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident), returns to Riverrun after successfully raiding the westerlands, but has lost the support of the Freys after marrying [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling) instead of a daughter of Walder Frey.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) Soldiers of [House Karstark](wiki:House_Karstark) roam the riverlands in search of Jaime Lannister and abandon Robb when he executes their lord, [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark), for treason.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0) [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully), the longtime Lord of Riverrun, passes away and is succeeded by his son, [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0) In order to return the Freys to King Robb's side, Lord Edmure agrees to marry a Frey.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-1)

Leaving Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully) at Riverrun as the [Warden of the Southern Marches](wiki:Warden_of_the_Southern_Marches), Robb leads his [northmen](wiki:Northmen) to the [Twins](wiki:Twins) for Edmure's wedding to [Roslin Frey](wiki:Roslin_Frey). Most of the river lords remain behind, with Edmure only bringing a modest retinue of his friends.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1) Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) leads his own contingent to the Twins from Harrenhal, but his rearguard is defeated by Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) in [fighting at the fords of the Trident](wiki:Fighting_at_the_fords_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-0) At the Twins, the Freys and [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) betray their king in the massacre known as the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding). Robb and Catelyn are killed, while Edmure is taken captive.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-0)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-0)

Most of the river lords surrender to the Iron Throne in the aftermath of the Red Wedding;(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-0) for instance, [Seagard](wiki:Siege_of_Seagard) surrenders to [Black Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman)).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0) Gregor takes the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford) and [retakes Harrenhal](wiki:Capture_of_Harrenhal) for the Iron Throne.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-0) The majority of river lords are welcomed back into the king's peace, but the Tullys are stripped of their titles and lands, with Riverrun being granted to the Freys.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-1) Ser [Ryman Frey](wiki:Ryman_Frey) leads two thousand Freys toward Riverrun.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) for [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), departs [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to bring peace to the riverlands, visiting [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) and [Darry](wiki:Darry).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-0) Brynden Tully continues to hold out, despite the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun) by House Frey.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_80-0) Jaime negotiates the surrender of the castle with Edmure,(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-1) though Brynden escapes during the night.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-0) Jaime reminds Lord [Emmon Frey](wiki:Emmon_Frey) that although [his house](wiki:House_Frey_of_Riverrun) possesses Riverrun, lordship over the riverlands has passed to [House Baelish](wiki:House_Baelish) of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-1) [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), Lord Paramount of the Trident, remains in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), however, and has not visited his seat at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).

Even though the War of the Five Kings has seemingly ended, the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) and broken men still roam the riverlands. Led by [Lady Stoneheart](wiki:Lady_Stoneheart), the outlaws of the brotherhood engage in a campaign of retribution against House Frey.(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-0)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-2) The town of [Saltpans](wiki:Saltpans) is [raided](wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans) by survivors of the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions).(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-0)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_84-0)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-1)(wiki:Riverlands.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)
