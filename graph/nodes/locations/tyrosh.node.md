## Origins

### Foundation

Tyrosh began as a military outpost of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) on a bleak stony isle to control shipping through the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones). They soon discovered in the nearby waters a unique variety of sea [snail](wiki:Snail) whose secretions were used to create a dye popular with [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) nobles. Thousands of merchants developed Tyrosh into a major city within a generation. Tyroshi dyers learned to produce different colors of dyes by varying the diet of the snails.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)

### After the Doom

Some [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) in Tyrosh survived the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), but the citizens killed the [dragons](wiki:Dragon) and their [dragonriders](wiki:Dragonriders).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) During the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood), the Free City of [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) thought to add Tyrosh to her conquests, after they had conquered [Myr](wiki:Myr) and [Lys](wiki:Lys). Their attempt to take Tyrosh, however, made their empire collapse. [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) came to the aid of the Tyroshi, as well as two [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros), the [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King) [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) and [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Myr](wiki:Myr) both rose in rebellion against Volantis.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)

After the flight of Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) from [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) in [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), Tyrosh was one of the places where she was rumored to have gone into hiding with her youngest two children.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) The Archon of Tyrosh traveled to the Seven Kingdoms in [49 AC](wiki:49_AC) for the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) of [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) and Alyssa Velaryon, accompanied by his maiden daughter. When it was later suggested that King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) should take the Archon's daughter as his bride, Alyssa argued against the match, fearing the Westerosi would never accept a foreign queen.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) In [50 AC](wiki:50_AC) Ser [Orryn Baratheon](wiki:Orryn_Baratheon) took service with the [Archon of Tyrosh](wiki:Archon_of_Tyrosh) and wed his daughter a year later.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2) Tyrosh and [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) began a war in [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) which significantly disrupted trade in the narrow sea. In [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) both sides requested that King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) to act as a mediator in negotiations to end the dispute. The [Prince of Pentos](wiki:Prince_of_Pentos) and the Archon traveled to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to conduct negotiations in person, and they eventually agreed upon "A Treaty of Eternal Peace".(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

[Elissa Farman](wiki:Elissa_Farman) visited Tyrosh while sailing *[Sun Chaser](wiki:Sun_Chaser)* from [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1)

### The Triarchy and the Daughters' War

After the defeat of [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) in the [Battle of the Borderland](wiki:Battle_of_the_Borderland), Tyrosh formed the Triarchy with [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Myr](wiki:Myr) in [96 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). The Triarchy, also known as the Kingdom of the Three Daughters, fought with Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) over the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) in [106 AC](wiki:106_AC).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) [Racallio Ryndoon](wiki:Racallio_Ryndoon) of Tyrosh led an invasion fleet against Daemon's island kingdom in [110 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1)

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the [High Council of the Triarchy](wiki:High_Council_of_the_Triarchy) met in Tyrosh and accepted Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister)'s offer of alliance with the [greens](wiki:Greens). A Tyroshi captain captured Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) before the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet), but the captive was claimed by the Triarchy's admiral, [Sharako Lohar](wiki:Sharako_Lohar) of Lys. Since most of the Triarchy's casualties during the battle were from Myrish and Tyrosh ships, the widows of those cities blamed Sharako for protecting his own Lysene ships.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

The [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War) broke out after the murder of Sharako. Although the admiral was killed in a personal dispute over the [Black Swan](wiki:Johanna_Swann), it was rumored that he had been assassinated by Myr. Tyrosh claimed the Stepstones when Lys and Myr began fighting. Racallio claimed the Stepstones for himself as [King of the Narrow Sea](wiki:King_of_the_Narrow_Sea), however. With the Dance ended, Ser [Oscar Tully](wiki:Oscar_Tully) led the [Stormbreakers](wiki:Stormbreakers) to Tyrosh and the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

Lys and Myr made peace and began attacking Tyrosh, forcing the Archon to recall his men from the Stepstones.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1) Tyrosh allied with Racallio and [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) to jointly rule the Stepstones, but the alliance collapsed after [Lord Alyn's attack](wiki:Lord_Alyn's_attack).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) In the aftermath of the battle, [Bloodstone](wiki:Bloodstone) and the southern Stepstones were held by Racallio, while [Pentoshi](wiki:Pentos) sellswords hired by the Archon controlled the northern and eastern islands. When Racallio accused Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) of being a Tyroshi spy, Alyn Oakenfist was forced to kill three Tyroshi prisoners at Bloodstone to prove his innocence.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

[Dorne](wiki:Dorne) eventually joined the war on the side of Tyrosh and Lys against Racallio.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1) When the Daughters' War ended, Tyrosh gained much of the Disputed Lands.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Tyroshi ships frequented [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring). During the fall of the [Rogare Bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank), [Moredo Rogare](wiki:Moredo_Rogare) fled the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) for Braavos. After negotiating with the [Iron Bank](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos), Moredo sailed for Tyrosh and hired mercenaries for an attack on Lys.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1) In [136 AC](wiki:136_AC) Lord Velaryon gathered a fleet of merchantmen for a voyage to Tyrosh and other Free Cities.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-2)

Aegon died before the marriage could take place, so his successor, King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), paid the [dowry](wiki:Dowry) to the [Archon of Tyrosh](wiki:Archon_of_Tyrosh) and saw Daemon and Rohanne wed.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) Daemon and Rohanne had seven sons (including [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Blackfyre), [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Blackfyre), [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre), [Haegon](wiki:Haegon_I_Blackfyre) and [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_Blackfyre)), and at least two daughters.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

After the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) led to the defeat and death of Daemon and his eldest two sons, Aegon and Aemon, Daemon's half-brother [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) gathered Daemon's surviving sons, daughters, and wife and fled to Tyrosh, where they remained for years.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1)(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Haegon was eventually crowned in Tyrosh as a rival to [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), the king on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1)

[Kiera of Tyrosh](wiki:Kiera_of_Tyrosh) married Prince [Valarr Targaryen](wiki:Valarr_Targaryen) in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros),(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) with whom she had several stillborn sons.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-2) Valarr died in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), and Kiera eventually remarried, this time to Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), Valarr's cousin.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-1) Both Valarr and Daeron had been directly in line for the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) at the time of their marriages, respectively, as their fathers were crown princes at the time. With Daeron, Kiera had one child, Princess [Vaella](wiki:Vaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daeron)).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-1)(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-2)

### War of the Ninepenny Kings

The [Band of Nine](wiki:Band_of_Nine), a group of [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword), [pirates](wiki:Pirate), and merchants who had conquered the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands), sacked Tyrosh and continued on to the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones). The wealthy merchant [Alequo Adarys](wiki:Alequo_Adarys) was placed in charge of Tyrosh, replacing the Archon. While the Band of Nine was defeated in the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings) in late [260 AC](wiki:260_AC), and the fighting against the Band of Nine in the Stepstones and the Disputed Lands lasted only another half year, Alequo remained in charge of Tyrosh for another six years. In the end, he was poisoned by his queen, and the Archon was restored.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

[Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark) was strangled with a Tyroshi device when King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) executed his father, Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) When [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) was a girl, she and her brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) spent some time in Tyrosh when they were wandering from place to place.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) She thus speaks [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian) with a Tyroshi accent.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)

Rather than face Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) for having sold poachers to a Tyroshi [slaver](wiki:Slaver), Lord [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) fled [Bear Island](wiki:Bear_Island) for the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

[Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), had planned to send his daughter, Princess [Arianne](wiki:Arianne_Martell), to Tyrosh to serve as the cupbearer of the archon, so she could secretly meet the exiled Viserys Targaryen, to whom she had been secretly betrothed. When his wife, Lady [Mellario](wiki:Mellario) of [Norvos](wiki:Norvos), threatened to kill herself if Arianne was sent away, Doran kept his daughter in [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). The daughter of the Archon of Tyrosh, however, spent some years in the [Water Gardens](wiki:Water_Gardens) of [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

[Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) has visited Tyrosh.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

## Appearances & Description

### Location and Layout

Tyrosh, a fortress city protected by high walls, is on the northernmost and easternmost of the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones). Its inner walls are [fused black dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(material)).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Tyrosh is far larger than the shadow city of [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

Tyrosh has a large fleet.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) At the mouth of its harbor lies the [Bleeding Tower](wiki:Bleeding_Tower).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

In the city lies the [Fountain of the Drunken God](wiki:Fountain_of_the_Drunken_God). There is no established faith in Tyrosh, and temples and shrines in honor of many different gods line the streets and waterfronts of the city.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) The [Temple of Trios](wiki:Temple_of_Trios) has a large statue of the three-headed [Trios](wiki:Trios) beside the temple's doors.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) is worshipped in Tyrosh,(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) as well as the [Patternmaker](wiki:Pattern) and other gods.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

Like [Lys](wiki:Lys), Tyrosh has [pleasure houses](wiki:Pleasure_houses).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

### People

### Economy

Tyrosh is a merchantile city, where trade is considered a much more honorable profession than arms. Shortly after its founding, Tyrosh quickly grew out into a rich city, due to the discovery of a certain variety of sea [snail](wiki:Snail).(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) Tyrosh has at least one bank.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

Tyrosh is deeply involved in the [slave trade](wiki:Slavery), and Tyroshi slavers are known to be especially aggressive. They even sail north [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall), in search of [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) to enslave. Slaves outnumber freeborn in Tyrosh three to one.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) [Sellswords](wiki:Sellswords) are also common in the city.

Pear brandy and baked [honeyfingers](wiki:Honeyfingers) are produced in Tyrosh,(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) and the city's craftsmen design gilded helms and filigreed armor.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) The fantastic helmets can be shaped like birds and animals and are chased with precious metals.(wiki:Tyrosh.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
