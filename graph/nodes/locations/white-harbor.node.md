## Origins

### Antiquity

The [First Men](wiki:First_Men) constructed an ancient [ringfort](wiki:Ringfort) atop [Seal Rock](wiki:Seal_Rock) at the entrance to the harbor.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-21)

King [Jon Stark](wiki:Jon_Stark) founded the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den),(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) a castle at the mouth of the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife), after he drove out sea raiders who were using the harbor as a base for raids inland.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) For centuries the castle was held by various [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) and offshoot branches, such as the [Greystarks](wiki:House_Greystark). Other families who were lords of the Wolf's Den in the past included the [Flints](wiki:House_Flint), [Lockes](wiki:House_Locke), [Slates](wiki:House_Slate), [Longs](wiki:House_Long), [Holts](wiki:House_Holt_(north)), and [Ashwoods](wiki:House_Ashwood), all tasked by [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to defend the White Knife.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)

### Foundation

Between [1,000 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest) and [600 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-15) [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) was driven into exile from the [Mander](wiki:Mander) in southern [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) by Lord [Lorimar Peake](wiki:Lorimar_Peake) during the reign of [Perceon III Gardener](wiki:Perceon_III_Gardener), [King of the Reach](wiki:King_of_the_Reach).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) The Starks welcomed the Manderlys to the north and accepted oaths of fealty from them in the Wolf's Den, giving them the ancient castle and the surrounding lands. This led to the creation of White Harbor around the Wolf's Den.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rawoiafwolf.27s_den.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

The Manderlys used their wealth from the [Reach](wiki:Reach) to build the new city, the architecture of which more closely resembles that of the Reach than other northern settlements.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) The Manderlys eventually built the [New Castle](wiki:New_Castle), said to be inspired by their lost southern seat of [Dunstonbury](wiki:Dunstonbury),(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) and they receive visitors in its [Merman's Court](wiki:Merman's_Court).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) The older, crumbling Wolf's Den is now used as a prison.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

### Aegon the Conqueror

Ser [Warrick Manderly](wiki:Warrick_Manderly) led a fleet of hired [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) galleys from White Harbor to suppress the [Sistermen's Rebellion](wiki:Sistermen's_Rebellion).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) held court in White Harbor three times during his reign.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Queen [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen) arranged a marriage between a White Harbor knight and a girl from [House Rowan](wiki:House_Rowan) who had been impregnated by a scullion.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

### Good Queen Alysanne

White Harbor prospered during the reign of [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and under the rule of Lord [Theomore Manderly](wiki:Theomore_Manderly).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) The king and his queen, [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), planned to make a great [royal progress](wiki:Royal_progress) to the [north](wiki:North) in [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). The royal retinue traveled in three ships to White Harbor before proceeding to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). As Jaehaerys was delayed in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Alysanne and the retinue went ahead without him. Tens of thousands of [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) came to see Alysanne and her [dragon](wiki:Dragons), [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing). Lord [Theomore Manderly](wiki:Theomore_Manderly) was surprised by the amount of smallfolk residing in his city.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

Alysanne's social and courtly skills were welcome in White Harbor, as the Manderlys enjoyed similar courtesies and pageantry on account of their [southron](wiki:Southron) origins. Theomore held a small [tourney](wiki:Tourney) to honor the queen and to display the prowess of his [knights](wiki:Knight). A [wildling](wiki:Wildling) woman, having been fostered as a girl with one of Theomore's household knights, dueled the queen's sworn shield, [Jonquil Darke](wiki:Jonquil_Darke). A few days after the tournament, Queen Alysanne convened a southron-style "[women's court](wiki:Women's_court)" in Lord Manderly's hall, at which more than two hundred women and girls gathered to share their thoughts, concerns, and grievances with the queen.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

### The Dance and Its Aftermath

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) traveled to White Harbor and Winterfell on [Vermax](wiki:Vermax) and convinced the north to join the [blacks](wiki:Blacks). Lord [Desmond Manderly](wiki:Desmond_Manderly) sent his sons, [Medrick](wiki:Medrick_Manderly) and [Torrhen](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly), to support [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) After the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing), the two knights urged Rhaenyra to accompany them on the *[Violande](wiki:Violande)* back to White Harbor, but the queen refused.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Medrick and Torrhen returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with a hundred White Harbor knights for the wedding of [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) to [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Thousands died of the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) in White Harbor in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), including Lord Desmond and Ser Medrick.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) The city suffered during the harsh winter of [134 AC](wiki:134_AC), with some men selling themselves or family members into [slavery](wiki:Slavery) in return for food. Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), sent food to aid the [northmen](wiki:Northmen), but more was always needed. White Harbor prospered during the brief rise of the [Rogare Bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank). [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) chose to accompany Lord Torrhen back to White Harbor after the [regent](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) was dismissed by King Aegon III.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_-_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

### Recent History

White Harbor has not seen [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) in centuries and has never been attacked by the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4) Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) last visited White Harbor in [294 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-22)

[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) accompanied her father, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), on two of his visits of the city.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

The city's history is described in [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Yorrick](wiki:Yorrick)'s *[Wed to the Sea, Being an Account of the History of White Harbor from Its Earliest Days](wiki:Wed_to_the_Sea,_Being_an_Account_of_the_History_of_White_Harbor_from_Its_Earliest_Days)*.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Maester [Heriston](wiki:Heriston) also served in the city.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_shivering_sea.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

## Appearances & Description

White Harbor is located on the eastern shore of the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife), where the river empties into a firth of the [Bite](wiki:Bite).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) The city is clean and well-ordered, with wide straight cobbled streets that make it easy to walk around. Its houses are built of whitewashed stone, with steeply-pitched roofs of dark grey [slate](wiki:Slate).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) According to a semi-canon source, there may be an offshoot branch of the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) in the city.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-5) White Harbor is defended by city guardsmen.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) Seen from the sea, the city's walls of whitewashed stone rise on the eastern shore where the White Knife plunges into the firth; the jetty dividing the inner and outer harbors is fortified with a long stone wall, thirty feet tall and almost a mile long, with towers every hundred yards. (adwd-davos-02:19)

The city has access to a variety of seafood,(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) and it is also the home of many [shipwrights](wiki:Ship) and [silversmiths](wiki:Silver).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) White Harbor is well regarded for its [beer](wiki:Beer), which includes ale,(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) yellow beer,(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and a thick black(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) stout(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) prized as far away as [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) and the [Port of Ibben](wiki:Port_of_Ibben).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) Ships frequently travel the Bite between White Harbor and the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters). White Harbor sells wood, wool, and hides, and in return the [Sistermen](wiki:Sistermen) sell crab, fish, and goat cheese.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### Outskirts

- [Seal Rock](wiki:Seal_Rock) is a massive stone dominating the approaches to the Outer Harbor. It is crowned with a ringfort of weathered stones of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) that stood desolate and abandoned for centuries. However, the Manderlys fortify it with crossbowmen, scorpions, and spitfires. The stone looms fifty feet above the waters, grey-green in color. [Seals](wiki:Seal) often rest on it.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)

- The harbor is divided into the inner and outer harbors. The outer harbor is larger, but the inner harbor offers better anchorage and shelter by the city wall on one side and the looming mass of the Wolf's Den on another. A mile-long, thirty foot wall, with towers every hundred yards, is located on the jetty that separates the two harbors.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)

- The [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den) is an ancient fortress that now serves as a prison. It is located by the water and adjoins the city walls. Houses cling like barnacles to the walls of the Wolf's Den; one is a brewhouse which makes well-regarded black [beer](wiki:Beer). The [godswood of White Harbor](wiki:Godswood_of_White_Harbor) is found within the prison's walls.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9)

- A fish market is located between the outer harbor and the Seal Gate. Seafood available at White Harbor include clams,(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) [cod](wiki:Cod),(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-10) [crabs](wiki:Crab),(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) [eels](wiki:Eel),(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) [herring](wiki:Herring),(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-11) [lampreys](wiki:Lamprey),(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) [lobster](wiki:Lobster),(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6) mussels,(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) oysters,(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) [salmon](wiki:Salmon),(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8) [whitefish](wiki:Whitefish),(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9) and [winkles](wiki:Winkle).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-10)

### Inside the walls

The city is protected by thick walls. The Seal Gate opens into the harbor.

- [Fishfoot Yard](wiki:Fishfoot_Yard), a cobbled square with a fountain at its center, located just outside the Seal Gate. There is an alley leading to a brothel.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-12) A stone merman the locals call Old Fishfoot rises twenty feet from tail to crown out of the fountain; his curly beard is green and white with lichen, and one of the prongs of his trident broke off before Davos was born. (adwd-davos-02:57)
- The [Old Mint](wiki:Old_Mint), located in Fishfoot Yard.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-13)
- The [Lazy Eel](wiki:Lazy_Eel), a winesink renowned for offering the oldest whores and vilest wine in White Harbor, along with meat pies full of lard and gristle that are inedible on their best days and poisonous on their worst.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-14)
- [Castle Stair](wiki:Castle_Stair) is a stone street with steps which connects the Wolf's Den and the New Castle. It is lined with marble mermaids that light the way with bowls of burning whale oil cradled in their arms.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-15)
- The [Sept of the Snows](wiki:Sept_of_the_Snows) is a large sept with a domed roof surmounted by tall statues of [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-16)
- A [mummer](wiki:Mummer)'s hall where bawdy entertainments can be had for a few [pennies](wiki:Currency).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-17)
- An inn famous for its lamprey pies.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-18)
- An [alehouse](wiki:Ale) where the wool factors and the customs men of White Harbor are known to come to drink.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-19)

### New Castle

- The [New Castle](wiki:New_Castle) is the seat of House Manderly. It is located on a hill inside the city wall, and the Castle Stair leads to its gates from the Wolf's Den below.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-20) There is a secret passage beneath the Castle Stair connecting the New Castle with the cellars of the Wolf's Den.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

- The [Merman's Court](wiki:Merman's_Court) is the great hall of the New Castle where Lord Manderly holds court and feasts. Its walls, floor, and ceiling are made of wooden planks notched cunningly together and decorated with all the creatures of the sea.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) hires *[Storm Dancer](wiki:Storm_Dancer)* to take her and Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) from White Harbor. Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) instructs Catelyn to have Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) repair and strengthen the defenses of White Harbor and insure they are well-manned.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

Catelyn returns to the north from the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) with Rodrik and her uncle, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), arriving at White Harbor. She and her uncle leave with Ser [Wylis Manderly](wiki:Wylis_Manderly) and his levies of nearly 1,500 men to meet her son, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), at [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin). Lord Wyman stays behind to see to White Harbor's defenses.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Robb is later proclaimed [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) is sent by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) with a chest of letters to spread word of [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon)'s illegitimacy. His route is to sail to White Harbor and other harbors along the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Davos does not visit White Harbor, however.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-23)

While at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) for the [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast), Lord Wyman meets with [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark), Ser Rodrik Cassel, and Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin). He has appointed new customs officers at White Harbor, as the previous officials had held back [silver](wiki:Silver) for the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) instead of Robb, the new [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North). Wyman offers to [mint](wiki:Old_Mint) coins for Robb, informs of White Harbor's defenses, and proposes to build the king a new warfleet of galleys, with Rodrik saying he will inform Robb of the proposal. Rodrik commands [Hother](wiki:Hother_Umber) and [Mors Umber](wiki:Mors_Umber) to work with Wyman in building longships to combat [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) Twenty casks of seafood are brought from White Harbor for the feast.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-11)

After hearing of the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) threat to [Torrhen's Square](wiki:Torrhen's_Square), Maester Luwin sends [ravens](wiki:Raven) to White Harbor, the [barrowlands](wiki:Barrowlands), and the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood) with instructions to summon their levies. While [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) is [capturing Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell), Luwin is able to send another [raven](wiki:Raven) to alert White Harbor.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) Lord Wyman then sends a dozen barges up the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife) packed with knights, warhorses, and siege engines.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) The Manderly contingent is betrayed and defeated by [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) in the [battle at Winterfell](wiki:Battle_at_Winterfell), however.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) sends a raven to White Harbor in a plea for help to defend [Castle Black](wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black) from [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

### A Feast for Crows

It is reported to Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) that [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) has been executed in White Harbor and that his head and hand grace the walls of White Harbor.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Sent by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) to negotiate with [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly), Lord [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) returns to White Harbor for the first time in six years.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-24) The Onion Lord notes the large navy when he arrives in White Harbor, with the inner harbor filled with twenty-three war galleys, and that [Seal Rock](wiki:Seal_Rock) has been occupied.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-25) Davos is preceded, however, by envoys of [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey). [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey), [Jared](wiki:Jared_Frey) and [Symond Frey](wiki:Symond_Frey) have come with the remains of Ser [Wendel Manderly](wiki:Wendel_Manderly), who died at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding). Davos notices that the [Old Mint](wiki:Old_Mint) is now being used to shelter refugees, and he visits the [Lazy Eel](wiki:Lazy_Eel) to gather information.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-26)

Davos appears before the [Merman's Court](wiki:Merman's_Court) in the [New Castle](wiki:New_Castle) to present the case for the Manderlys to support Stannis. To save his living son and heir, Ser [Wylis Manderly](wiki:Wylis_Manderly), who is a captive at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), Lord Manderly has Davos dragged to prison as a traitor, in the presence of the Freys.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-5)

Wyman has Davos imprisoned in the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den), but has a different prisoner in the cell executed. The prisoner is beheaded, his head dipped in tar. The fingers of one of his hands are also cut off to resemble Davos. The head and hands are then put up on spikes on the walls of White Harbor to give the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) and Freys reason to believe in House Manderly's loyalty to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)

Once Wyman has sent a false report to the Iron Throne and has Wylis returned, the Lord of White Harbor has [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) free Davos and bring him through a secret passage to the New Castle. He tells the Onion Knight that he will swear his allegiance to Stannis Baratheon if Davos retrieves [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark). Via [Wex Pyke](wiki:Wex_Pyke), Wyman has discovered the boy is alive, but needs a smuggler to retrieve him from [Skagos](wiki:Skagos).(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)

Wyman travels to [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton) and then Winterfell for the [wedding of Ramsay Bolton and Arya Stark](wiki:Wedding_of_Ramsay_Bolton_and_Arya_Stark) with three hundred men, including one hundred knights.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) His retinue includes musicians from White Harbor(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) and forty wayns of foodstuffs.(wiki:White_Harbor.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)
