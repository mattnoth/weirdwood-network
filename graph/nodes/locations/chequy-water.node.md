## Origins

The Chequy Water has been known by its name for over a thousand years. Its name comes from the chequy [lion](wiki:Lion) [sigil](wiki:Sigil) of House Osgrey.(wiki:Chequy_Water.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey) first kissed a girl on the banks of the Chequy Water, and he and his kin enjoyed fishing and playing in the stream. However, because of Eustace's [treasonous](wiki:Treason) support of [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) in the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) granted ownership of the stream to their neighbors, House Webber of [Coldmoat](wiki:Coldmoat). The Webbers cleared trees on both banks of the stream to make way for pastures.(wiki:Chequy_Water.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
In [211 AC](wiki:211_AC), [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber), [Lady of Coldmoat](wiki:Lady_of_Coldmoat), had the stream dammed to irrigate her crops and provide water for her moat. This caused a confrontation with Ser Eustace, which was only settled after a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) between their champions, Ser [Lucas Inchfield](wiki:Lucas_Inchfield) and Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall). During the battle, held within the Chequy Water itself, Duncan killed Lucas, but he nearly drowned in the stream.(wiki:Chequy_Water.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
