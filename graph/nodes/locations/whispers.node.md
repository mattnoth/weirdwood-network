## Origins

According to legend, the ancient castle received its name from the whispering heads of the victims of Ser [Clarence Crabb](wiki:Clarence_Crabb), a fabled knight from [Crackclaw Point](wiki:Crackclaw_Point). Whenever Clarence would kill a man—including [lords](wiki:Lord), wizards, [knights](wiki:Knight), [pirates](wiki:Pirate), and a king of [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale)—he would cut off their head and bring it back to his castle to his wife, a [woods witch](wiki:Woods_witch). There, she would kiss the head and bring it back to life. The heads would then talk to each other and give Clarence counsel, creating the whispering sound.(wiki:Whispers.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

The castle has been a ruin for a thousand years.(wiki:Whispers.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)(wiki:Whispers.cite_ref-Crabb_4-0)

## Appearances & Description

The downtrodden castle sits on the edge of a cliff overlooking the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). The castle, which is triangular with square towers at its corners, was built with unmortared stone. It is now overgrown with forest from its [godswood](wiki:Godswood), which contains [soldier pines](wiki:Soldier_pine) and a young [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood). Poisonous red ivy grows over the fallen curtain wall. The beacon tower collapsed when [Dick Crabb](wiki:Dick_Crabb) was a boy and now sits in the sea fifty feet below.(wiki:Whispers.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) There is a large smuggler's cove nearby.(wiki:Whispers.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) The Whispers is named for the sound of the sea pouring into holes made in the cliff and echoing in caverns below the castle.(wiki:Whispers.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

[Dick Crabb](wiki:Dick_Crabb) suggests the smuggler's cove at the Whispers to [Shagwell](wiki:Shagwell), [Pyg](wiki:Pyg), and [Timeon](wiki:Timeon), former members of the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions) who are seeking transport from [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). Dick neglects to tell Shagwell the Fool that ships have not visited the cove for thirty years, however.(wiki:Whispers.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) The three sellswords camp at the ruined castle.(wiki:Whispers.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

[Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) is in search of [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), who had been in the company of another fool, [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard). Thinking that the fool fooled by Dick had possibly been Dontos, Brienne hires Dick to escort her and [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne) to the Whispers.(wiki:Whispers.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) After Brienne's party arrives at the ruins, Shagwell jumps down from the young [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) and kills Dick. While Brienne slays Pyg and Timeon, Pod throws stones at Shagwell until he submits. Brienne has Shagwell dig a grave for Dick beneath the weirwood, and she kills the fool with her dagger when he suddenly attacks with a rock. Afterward, Ser [Hyle Hunt](wiki:Hyle_Hunt) joins Brienne and Podrick at the Whispers.(wiki:Whispers.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Brienne brings the heads of the three sellswords to [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) and leaves their bodies for [crows](wiki:Crow).(wiki:Whispers.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)(wiki:Whispers.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
