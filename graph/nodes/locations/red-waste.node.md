## Identity

The Red Waste is a vast, desolate geographic region in Essos, located to the southeast of Lhazar and to the west/northwest of Qarth. It is a harsh, inhospitable wasteland — dry, barren, and largely uninhabitable — whose ruins testify to the civilization that once flourished there before its destruction. The region borders the Dothraki sea and lies between the grasslands of the Dothraki and the city of Qarth near the Jade Gates.

Historically, the Red Waste was the territory of the Qaathi, an ancient people whose civilization declined as their fertile lands turned into the wasteland. During the Century of Blood following the Doom of Valyria, the Dothraki pushed deep into the red waste and destroyed the remaining Qaathi cities, leaving Qarth as the sole surviving city of that culture. The ruins of Vaes Orvik, Vaes Qosar, and Vaes Shirosi still stand in the waste today. Dothraki khalasars do not cross the red waste to reach Qarth, and Jorah Mormont specifically notes he has not traveled there.

In the narrative, Daenerys Targaryen's small khalasar is forced to cross through the red waste during A Clash of Kings after being abandoned following Khal Drogo's death. They struggle south through the wasteland and eventually reach the safety of Qarth. It is during this crossing that Daenerys names the green dragon Rhaegal (after her brother Rhaegar) and sends out her bloodriders as scouts. The red waste thus serves as a crucible for Daenerys's early leadership and is tied to the moment her dragons are first confirmed as alive and growing.
