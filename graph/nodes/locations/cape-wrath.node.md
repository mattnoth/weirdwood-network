## Origins

The woods of Cape Wrath were inhabited in antiquity by the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest),(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) but the cape came to be controlled by the [First Men](wiki:First_Men).
A [woods witch](wiki:Woods_witch) called the [Green Queen](wiki:Green_Queen) rebelled against the [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King) [Durwald I Durrandon](wiki:Durwald_I_Durrandon), controlling the [rainwood](wiki:Rainwood) for a generation.(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_durrandon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) During the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), the [Andals](wiki:Andals) would have conquered all of Cape Wrath from the First Men if not for their own disunity.(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
With King [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon) preoccupied with the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen), pirates from the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) raided the cape during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) In [8 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) during the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), a pirate king from the Stepstones transported [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) across the [Sea of Dorne](wiki:Sea_of_Dorne) to Cape Wrath. The raiders attacked six towns and villages and set fires in the rainwood.(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
During her search for the missing [Aerea Targaryen](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen), [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) and her dragon, [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre), were seen flying over Cape Wrath.(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
[Morion Martell](wiki:Morion_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), vowed to burn a hundred towns and a hundred castles on Cape Wrath during the ill-fated [Fourth Dornish War](wiki:Fourth_Dornish_War) in [83 AC](wiki:83_AC), although the peninsula does not have a third that number of such settlements. Lord [Boremund Baratheon](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon) readied a host to defend Cape Wrath against the invaders. [Caraxes](wiki:Caraxes), [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), and [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor) burned Morion's ships as they crossed the sea, however, and Dornish corpses washed ashore for months.(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
The [Weeping Town](wiki:Weeping_Town) is so named because it is where the body of King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen) was first brought after his death in Dorne.(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)
Following the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End), [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) granted [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) a small keep and choice lands on Cape Wrath,(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) including woods in which he can hunt [red deer](wiki:Red_deer).(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) sends [Dale Seaworth](wiki:Dale_Seaworth) to sail [Wraith](wiki:Wraith) along Cape Wrath, the [Broken Arm](wiki:Broken_Arm), the coast of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), and the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor), informing the populace of Stannis's claim to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Cape Wrath is invaded during the [landing of the Golden Company](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company) in the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) and the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones). While a quarter of [the sellswords'](wiki:Golden_Company) available strength is taking [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost), Ser [Tristan Rivers](wiki:Tristan_Rivers) sets off simultaneously for the seat of [House Morrigen](wiki:House_Morrigen) at [Crow's Nest](wiki:Taking_of_Crow's_Nest), and Ser [Laswell Peake](wiki:Laswell_Peake) for [Rain House](wiki:Taking_of_Rain_House), the stronghold of [House Wylde](wiki:House_Wylde). [Harry Strickland](wiki:Harry_Strickland) argues that if Tristan and Laswell are successful the Golden Company will control a better part of Cape Wrath.(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) The castles fall quickly.(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Rawoiafcrow.27s_nest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Cape_Wrath.cite_ref-Rawoiafrain_house.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)
