## Origins

The Summer Isles have a history dating back thousands of years. Much of this was recorded in carvings made by priestesses on the famous [Talking Trees](wiki:Talking_Trees) of [Tall Trees Town](wiki:Tall_Trees_Town). Even more is recorded in oral tradition, because the Summer Islanders rely heavily upon histories recorded in highly complicated formal verse and memorized by rote.(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-24) In Westeros, Maester [Gallard](wiki:Gallard) painstakingly translated and recorded many of these in his [Children of Summer](wiki:Children_of_Summer), which remains the chief historical text on the Summer Isles.(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-23)
For much of their recorded history, the Summer Islanders lived in isolation from the rest of the [known world](wiki:Known_world), and they were apparently unaware that other lands and peoples existed. Maps included in the ancient histories carved into the [Talking Trees](wiki:Talking_Trees) depict only the Summer Islands themselves, surrounded by a world-encompassing ocean. In antiquity the islanders used oared coracles and then larger ships with woven hemp sails.(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-24)

### The Age of Exploration[ ]

The first recorded contact between the Summer Isles and the outside world occurred when the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis) was at its height, between eight and five thousand years ago. A Ghiscari merchant ship was blown off course by a storm and landed on [Walano](wiki:Walano), but the crew and ship fled when they saw the inhabitants. This introduced the islanders to the idea that there were other lands and peoples beyond their home waters, so their princes began building larger and sturdier ships capable of making long sea voyages. The greatest of these was [Malthar Xaq](wiki:Malthar_Xaq), prince of [Koj](wiki:Koj).(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-25)
The Summer Islanders first encountered the peaceful inhabitants of [Naath](wiki:Naath) to their east, then the [Basilisk Isles](wiki:Basilisk_Isles), the northern coasts of [Sothoryos](wiki:Sothoryos), and the southern coasts of [Essos](wiki:Essos) and [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). According to [Lomas Longstrider](wiki:Lomas_Longstrider), sages from the islands claimed that their ancestors founded ill-fated colonial cities on the coast of [Sothoryos](wiki:Sothoryos).(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-26)
Less than half a century after their first contact, the isles had established a trade network with the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold).(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-27) Merchant ships may have reached [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) in the [Reach](wiki:Reach) generations before the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) or even the [First Men](wiki:First_Men). Maester [Jellicoe](wiki:Jellicoe) speculated that Oldtown may have first risen in the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age) as a trading post where ships from [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), [Old Ghis](wiki:Old_Ghis), and the Summer Isles would put in to replenish their supplies and trade with each other and even the elder races.(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

### Slavers' Wars[ ]

Although the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) did not attempt to conquer the archipelago, islanders were raided by [slavers](wiki:Slavers) from [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), [Old Ghis](wiki:Old_Ghis), and the [Basilisk Isles](wiki:Basilisk_Isles). Native princes also sold defeated rivals to the slavers. These [Years of Shame](wiki:Years_of_Shame) lasted near two centuries.(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-28)
[Xanda Qo](wiki:Xanda_Qo), Princess of [Sweet Lotus Vale](wiki:Sweet_Lotus_Vale) once a slave herself, united the islands under her rule. Because [iron](wiki:Iron) for swords, spears, or armor was scarce, Xanda armed her sailors with tall [goldenheart](wiki:Goldenheart) bows superior to those of the slavers. Xanda also had new large and sturdy ships designed, known as "[swan ships](wiki:Swan_ships)" for the long necks of their prows, to provide high platforms for archers to fire upon approaching slaver ships. Led by Xanda's daughter and eventual successor, [Chatana Qo](wiki:Chatana_Qo), the Slavers' Wars lasted a generation, but ultimately the Summer Islanders prevailed. Slavery has been strictly outlawed in the Summer Islands ever since. Chatana, the so-called Arrow of Jhahar, was less accomplished as a ruler, however, and the islands fell into disunity after her death.(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-29)

### Recent History[ ]

The Summer Islands have only been briefly united under a single ruler about half a dozen times.(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-30)
During the voyage of the [ten thousand ships](wiki:Ten_thousand_ships) of the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar), Princess [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria) and her refugees briefly stopped on the island of Abulu, which subsequently became known as the [Isle of Women](wiki:Isle_of_Women). The isle's soil was poor, however, and most of the Rhoynar left with Nymeria when she continued her journey.(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
The sacking of [Tall Trees Town](wiki:Tall_Trees_Town) by a large pirate fleet in [19 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) inspired King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) to build walls around his new city, [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), the capital of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Three Summer Islander princes attended the [wedding of Aegon III Targaryen and Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen).(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)
[Bellegere Otherys](wiki:Bellegere_Otherys), the first Black Pearl, was the daughter of a princess of the Summer Islands and the son of a [Sealord of Braavos](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos).(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-29)
[Xhobar Qhoqua](wiki:Xhobar_Qhoqua), an exiled prince from the Summer Isles, was one of the infamous [Band of Nine](wiki:Band_of_Nine) during the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings).(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)
[Jalabhar Xho](wiki:Jalabhar_Xho), Prince of the [Red Flower Vale](wiki:Red_Flower_Vale), received refuge in King's Landing after being forced into exile. He spent many years petitioning King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) for military assistance in retaking his homeland. Robert was attracted by the idea of invading the Summer Islands, but his advisors convinced him it was a foolhardy venture.(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)(wiki:Summer_Isles.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)
