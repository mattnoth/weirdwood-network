## Appearances & Description

The ruined stronghold sits on a hill above the [Blue Fork](wiki:Blue_Fork) of the [Trident](wiki:Trident).(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) The overgrown and stony road slowly goes twice around the hill before reaching the summit.(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Beneath the castle ruins, the lower slopes of the hill are thickly forested with ash, elms, [oaks](wiki:Oak), [pines](wiki:Pine), and [sentinels](wiki:Sentinel). The landscape also contains blackberry, bracken, gorse, sedge, and thistle.(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
Only the foundations remain among the weeds to show where the dismantled walls of Oldstones had once stood.(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) The curtain wall which once encircled the brow of the hill now consists of waist-high piles of crumbling stone spotted with lichen, and the ruins are extensive near the former gatehouse.(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) The only remnants of the keep are mossy hummocks, behind which is the [godswood](wiki:Godswood).(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos81.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
A great carved sepulcher rests in the castle yard, half-hidden in waist-high brown grass among of ash trees. The weathered sepulcher entombs [Tristifer IV Mudd](wiki:Tristifer_IV_Mudd), [King of the Rivers and the Hills](wiki:King_of_the_Rivers_and_the_Hills). The stone is cracked and crumbling at the corners and discolored by splotches of white lichen, while wild [roses](wiki:Rose) creep up over the king's feet almost to his chest.(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
Near Oldstones is a bridge across the Blue Fork, older and smaller than the bridge downstream at [Fairmarket](wiki:Fairmarket).(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

## Origins

The castle now called Oldstones was the seat of [House Mudd](wiki:House_Mudd), who succeeded in unifying more of the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) than previous [First Men](wiki:First_Men) dynasties.(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) [Tristifer IV Mudd](wiki:Tristifer_IV_Mudd), [King of the Rivers and Hills](wiki:King_of_the_Rivers_and_Hills), beheaded [Roland II Arryn](wiki:Roland_II_Arryn), [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale), at Oldstones during the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals).(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_the_eyrie.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) However, the Mudds were eventually overwhelmed by [Andal](wiki:Andal) warlords such as [Armistead Vance](wiki:Armistead_Vance),(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) and their castle fell to ruin over the centuries.(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) [Smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) made off with most of the stones to raise barns, [holdfasts](wiki:Holdfast), and [septs](wiki:Sept).(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
During the occupation of the riverlands by the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_Kings) from [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon), Ser [Lymond Fisher](wiki:Lymond_Fisher), the [Knight of Oldstones](wiki:Knight_of_Oldstones), unsuccessfully claimed to be a [river king](wiki:River_king).(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
There is [a song](wiki:My_Jenny's_song) about [Jenny of Oldstones](wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones),(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) a mysterious girl who lived in the ruined castle and married [Duncan Targaryen](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen), Prince of Dragonflies.(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
[Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully), the [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident), once camped at Oldstones with his daughter, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), and his ward, [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), while traveling to [Seagard](wiki:Seagard). Catelyn played at being Jenny, while Petyr pretended to be the Prince of Dragonflies.(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

The rising waters of the [Blue Fork](wiki:Blue_Fork) wash away the bridge near Oldstones.(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) On the way to the [Twins](wiki:Twins), [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), the [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident), encamps upon the ruins of Oldstone atop the hill. recalling the fate of King [Tristifer IV Mudd](wiki:Tristifer_IV_Mudd), Robb discusses with his mother, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), whom he will name has his successor, as he and his queen, [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling), have not yet any children. Against his mother's wishes he plans to legitimize his half-brother, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), and name him his heir.(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) Robb and Catelyn are later betrayed by Houses [Frey](wiki:House_Frey) and [Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) at the [Twins](wiki:Twins) and killed in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
In the aftermath of the Red Wedding, the outlaws of the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) abduct [Petyr Frey](wiki:Petyr_Frey). [Merrett Frey](wiki:Merrett_Frey) travels from the Twins to the snowy ruins of Oldstones with the ransom for Petyr Pimple, the grandson of his half-brother [Stevron](wiki:Stevron_Frey). When Merrett enters Oldstones, he hears faint music drifting through the trees from somewhere deep within the castle. As he reaches the site of Tristifer's sepulchre, Merrett finds [Tom of Sevenstreams](wiki:Tom_of_Sevenstreams) sitting cross-legged atop it, fingering the strings of his woodharp, along with other outlaws. Petyr is revealed to have already been hanged in the [godswood](wiki:Godswood). A revived Catelyn allows the outlaws to surround Merrett, take his [gold](wiki:Gold), and hang him for his own involvement in the Red Wedding.(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

The murderers of Merrett flee Oldstones, with one band escaping Lord [Lucias Vypren](wiki:Lucias_Vypren) at [Fairmarket](wiki:Fairmarket).(wiki:Oldstones.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
