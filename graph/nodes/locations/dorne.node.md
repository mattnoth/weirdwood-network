## Origins

### Dawn Age

During the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age), the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) referred to desolate Dorne as the **Empty Land**.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

### First Men

The [First Men](wiki:First_Men) settled Westeros by crossing the land bridge across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) called the [Arm of Dorne](wiki:Arm_of_Dorne). During [their great war](wiki:War_of_the_First_Men_and_the_children_of_the_forest) with the First Men, the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) allegedly broke the Arm with the [hammer of the waters](wiki:Hammer_of_the_waters) in an attempt to stop them coming.

First Men houses who established themselves as prominent kings included [House Dayne](wiki:House_Dayne), the [Kings of the Torrentine](wiki:Kings_of_the_Torrentine); [House Fowler](wiki:House_Fowler), the [Kings of Stone and Sky](wiki:Kings_of_Stone_and_Sky); and [House Yronwood](wiki:House_Yronwood), the Bloodroyals who styled themselves [High King of Dorne](wiki:High_King_of_Dorne). A rival High King of Dorne was also chosen from a dozen houses along the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_kingdoms_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1)

Dorne has had a violent history, particularly with the houses of the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains), who lived by raiding across the borders into the [Dornish Marches](wiki:Dornish_Marches), feuding with each other endlessly. The various Dornish kings, the [Kings of the Reach](wiki:Kings_of_the_Reach), and the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_King) fought border wars beyond count and made countless raids across mountains and marches even when at peace, leading to their great enmity.

### Andal Adventurers

During the [Andal invasion](wiki:Andal_invasion) of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), most [Andals](wiki:Andals) avoided Dorne, aside from adventurers such as the [Ullers](wiki:House_Uller), [Qorgyles](wiki:House_Qorgyle), and [Vaiths](wiki:House_Vaith). The [Allyrions](wiki:House_Allyrion), [Jordaynes](wiki:House_Jordayne), and [Santagars](wiki:House_Santagar) also established their own realms. The [Martells](wiki:House_Martell) defeated two First Men houses, the [Wades](wiki:House_Wade) and [Shells](wiki:House_Shell_(Dorne)), claiming territory near the mouth of the Greenblood.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_the_andals_arrive.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

### House Nymeros Martell

Centuries ago, Dorne was a coalition of [First Men](wiki:First_Men) and [Andal](wiki:Andal) petty kings and lords, with no ruler strong enough to seize control of the entire region. About seven hundred years before [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_the_coming_of_the_rhoynar.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-42)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-43) the [Rhoynish Wars](wiki:Rhoynish_Wars) forced the people of the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar) to flee their homeland along the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne) in [Essos](wiki:Essos). Led by their legendary warrior-queen, [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria), they left in a fleet of [ten thousand ships](wiki:Ten_thousand_ships), eventually making landfall at the mouth of the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood) in Dorne. Nymeria allied with Lord [Mors Martell](wiki:Mors_Martell) of the [Sandship](wiki:Sandship), and with his support conquered the entire peninsula in [Nymeria's War](wiki:Nymeria's_War), uniting it with [House Nymeros Martell](wiki:House_Martell) of [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) as its ruler.

While the monarchs from the rest of Westeros used the title "king", the new rulers of Dorne used the Rhoynish title "[prince](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne)" instead. Although no longer kings, the Yronwoods, the second most powerful house in Dorne, continued to call themselves "the Bloodroyal".(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5)

The Rhoynar brought their own gods with them to Dorne, but they have largely disappeared in favor of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). Nevertheless, many Rhoynish customs, including inheritance regardless of gender, have been absorbed into Dornish society.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-44) Descendants of the Rhoynar who have not assimilated into Dornish society are known as [orphans of the Greenblood](wiki:Orphans_of_the_Greenblood).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

### House Targaryen

Three centuries ago, [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) claimed the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) and invaded [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), he subjugated each of the Westerosi monarchs one by one, except Dorne. One of Aegon's sisters, [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen), flew on her dragon, [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes), above the Dornishmen guarding the [Prince's Pass](wiki:Prince's_Pass), but each Dornish castle she visited was abandoned. When she came to Sunspear, she was met by [Meria Martell](wiki:Meria_Martell), Princess of Dorne, who warned her that the Targaryens would face peril if they attacked Dorne.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

In [4 AC](wiki:4_AC), however, Aegon the Conqueror announced another campaign, which became the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War). Whereas other kings and lords had taken to the field against Aegon, or clustered in castles, the Dornish refused to give open battle and allow Aegon to deploy his dragons. Instead, they turned to ambush and raids, striking quickly and then slipping back into the desert or through the mountain passes, where even the dragons could not find them. Aegon's younger sister-wife, Queen Rhaenys, participated in this war, which eventually lead to her death. Meraxes was also killed, being hit in the eye by a [scorpion](wiki:Scorpion_(weapon)) at the [Hellholt](wiki:Hellholt).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) In [13 AC](wiki:13_AC) a peace was agreed(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) between Dorne and the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), which held for seventy years, barring conflicts with raiders in the mountains, the misleadingly named [Second](wiki:Second_Dornish_War) and [Third](wiki:Third_Dornish_War) Dornish Wars.

Despite Dornish independence, [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) continued to claim the Iron Throne's control over the land as part of the Seven Kingdoms. In [83 AC](wiki:83_AC) the foolish Prince [Morion Martell](wiki:Morion_Martell) launched the [Fourth Dornish War](wiki:Fourth_Dornish_War) and was defeated in a day by King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and his sons. On his death in [103 AC](wiki:103_AC) it was said that even Dorne mourned. (wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

In [110 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Prince [Qoren Martell](wiki:Qoren_Martell) led Dorne to join the [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy) in the [war](wiki:War_for_the_Stepstones) against Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) for control of the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-1) King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) considered wedding his daughter, Princess [Rhaenyra](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), to the Prince of Dorne, and thereby bring Dorne under control of the Iron Throne, but Rhaenyra eventually wed Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon) instead.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-2) At the outbreak of the great Targaryen civil war known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC) both sides sought Dorne as a potential ally, but remembering the devastation of the First Dornish War, Prince Qoren chose to reject both. Rhaenyra's faction was co-led by Daemon, who had recently been fighting against Dorne, but the [opposing faction](wiki:Greens) of Rhaenyra's half-brother Aegon II included Dorne's ancestral enemies in the stormlands and the southern Reach. Dorne therefore remained neutral throughout the Targaryen civil war, content to sit back and watch them destroy each other.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

Qoren had died by [132 AC](wiki:132_AC) and was succeeded by his fiery seventeen year-old daughter [Aliandra Martell](wiki:Aliandra_Martell), who considered herself "the new Nymeria". Dorne's power waxed during her reign, as the Targaryen realm was left badly weakened by the Dance of the Dragons and would take a full generation to recover. Aliandra encouraged her knights and lords to prove themselves with renewed raids in the Dornish Marches. Eventually conflict in the Stepstones flared up again when the Triarchy collapsed in the chaotic [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War) of 131-134 AC, and Aliandra actively sought to expand Dorne's influence east there as well. Towards the end of the war Aliandra allied with Tyrosh and Lys against the other factions competing over the region, and when the dust settled, at the end of the war Dorne ended up in control of almost all of the Stepstones.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)

### The Conquest of Dorne

In [157 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), shortly after his ascension, King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen) began to plan his [invasion of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne), intent on "completing [the Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest)". Daeron attacked Dorne with three separate hosts; One led by Lord [Lyonel Tyrell](wiki:Lyonel_Tyrell_(lord)), which marched through the [Prince's Pass](wiki:Prince's_Pass), entering Dorne at the western end of the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains); One led by [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon), who came by sea; And one by the Targaryen king himself, which came through the [Boneway](wiki:Boneway). Within a year, the Targaryen armies arrived at the gates of [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) and battled their way through the [shadow city](wiki:Shadow_city). In [158 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne_(father_of_Maron)) and forty of the most powerful Dornish [lords](wiki:Lord) bent their knees in the [Submission of Sunspear](wiki:Submission_of_Sunspear).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) However, rebels continued to cause trouble. In [159 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), after he had consolidated his rule, Daeron I returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), leaving Lord [Lyonel Tyrell](wiki:Lyonel_Tyrell_(lord)) to keep the peace in Dorne.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-1) Although the loyalty of the dornish nobility was ensured by taking fourteen highborn hostages,(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-2) the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) continued to rebel against Targaryen rule.

Lord Lyonel was eventually murdered at [Sandstone](wiki:Sandstone), the seat of [House Qorgyle](wiki:House_Qorgyle), and within a fortnight the Targaryen conquest was undone.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-3)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) Daeron returned to Dorne in [160 AC](wiki:160_AC), winning several victories in the Boneway, while Lord Alyn Velaryon descended on the [Planky Town](wiki:Planky_Town) and the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood) as he had done the first time.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-4) In [161 AC](wiki:161_AC), the Dornishmen agreed to meet to discuss terms and renew their fealty. However, the meeting was a ploy to kill King Daeron I. Daeron died together with three of his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) knights, while [another](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)) was captured, and a fifty yielded.

With Daeron's death, the conquest of Dorne came to an end.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) It is said that ten thousand men had died conquering Dorne,(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-1)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-5) but many more died trying to hold it. According to Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), Daeron lost another forty thousand men during the three years after his conquest had completed,(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-6) but according to [Benjen Stark](wiki:Benjen_Stark) the true number was fifty thousand.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-2)

### Unifying the Seven Kingdoms

With the death of his brother, the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) passed to King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen), whose first act as king was to grant pardon to the Dornish hostages. Baelor walked barefoot from King's Landing to Sunspear, personally escorting the hostages (who rode on fine horses) back home. The [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne) and Baelor came to an agreement regarding a peace. As part of the terms of the agreement, Baelor agreed to betroth his young cousin [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) to Princess [Myriah Martell](wiki:Myriah_Martell), the eldest child of the Prince of Dorne.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) As both were still young children, the marriage took place later during Baelor's reign.

Prince Daeron's father, King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), a veteran of Daeron I's conquest of Dorne, planned to launch an unprovoked war against Dorne. In [174 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), he made his attempt. He turned to the [pyromancers](wiki:Pyromancers) of the ancient [Guild of Alchemists](wiki:Guild_of_Alchemists) to built "[dragons](wiki:Dragons)" of his own design, wood-and-iron monstrosities fitted with pumps that shot jets of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire). Aegon wanted to drag them through the [Boneway](wiki:Boneway), but they never came that far. All seven burned down in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood). Likewise, the huge fleet Aegon IV had constructed for his invasion never reached Dorne either, but instead was broken and scattered by storms on route to Dorne.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0) After these failures, Aegon IV never spoke of Dorne again.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-1)

Prince Daeron inherited the Iron Throne in [184 AC](wiki:184_AC), and ascended the Iron Throne as King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen). One of his earliest significant acts after assuming the throne was to begin negotiations to unify Dorne under Targaryen rule. Negotiations lasted two years, but eventually Daeron and his good-brother, Prince [Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell), now the ruling Prince of Dorne, reached an agreement. Maron was betrothed to Daeron's younger sister, [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)). When they were [wed](wiki:Wedding_of_Maron_Martell_and_Daenerys_Targaryen) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in [187 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Prince Maron knelt before the Iron Throne and swore his oaths of fealty, placing Dorne under Targaryen rule.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0)

Due to the peacefully negotiated unification, the Martells were allowed to continue using the title [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), unlike their [lordly](wiki:Lord) counterparts in the rest of the Seven Kingdoms.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) They additionally had won several concessions, resulting in significant rights and privileges for the Dornish lords that the other great houses did not. Additionally, Dornish influence at the royal court of King's Landing grew. All of these concessions resulted in dissatisfaction among some lords, and was one of the seeds from which the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) eventually sprang.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-1)

During the First Blackfyre Rebellion, Prince [Baelor Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), the eldest son of King Daeron II and Queen Myriah, commanded a host from Dorne and the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands),(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-2) with whom he achieved a decisive victory in the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

### Recent History

The Dornishmen closed their borders and successfully prevented the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness) from reaching Dorne.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) and Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) visited Dorne after the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

The mother of [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell) had served at the Targaryen court as a lady-in-waiting to Princess [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-3) prior to inheriting the rule to Dorne.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rawoiafjoanna_lannister.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) In [279 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), she betrothed her daughter [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell) to the crown prince, [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen). In early [280 AC](wiki:280_AC), Prince [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell) saw his sister wed to Rhaegar at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) Later that year, Elia gave birth to her first child, [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)). Her second child, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), was born near the new year of [282 AC](wiki:282_AC).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-1)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0) However, Rhaegar disappeared with [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark),(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-1) one of the events which sparked [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).

### Robert's Rebellion

When Rhaegar disappeared with [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) in [282 AC](wiki:282_AC), not long after Aegon's birth, it started a chain of events leading to [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion),(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-2) in which the [North](wiki:North), and several houses from the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) and [Vale](wiki:Vale) (later joined by several houses from the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands)) fought against the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0) Prince [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell) was furious about the way his sister had been treated by Rhaegar. Consequently, he was slow to lend any aid to the Targaryens.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rawoiafdoran_martell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0) Late in the war, Doran agreed to send ten thousand Dornishmen to fight in the loyalist army, under the command of his uncle, Prince [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard),(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1) understanding that Elia, as the wife to the crown prince, would one day become queen, and that her son Aegon would one day be king.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rawoiafdoran_martell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-1)

However, Rhaegar died at the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident),(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0) as did Doran's uncle, Lewyn.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0) King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), convinced that Lewyn had betrayed Rhaegar on the Trident, refused to send Elia and her children to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) alongside [his own wife](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) and [new heir](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-2) Not long after, during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing), Elia and her children were brutally killed.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-1)(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0)

After lifting the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) travelled to the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) of Dorne to liberate his sister [Lyanna](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), who apparently was held at the [tower of joy](wiki:Tower_of_joy). There, Eddard and his six companions [battled](wiki:Combat_at_the_tower_of_joy) three [knights](wiki:Knight) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard): [Arthur Dayne](wiki:Arthur_Dayne), [Oswell Whent](wiki:Oswell_Whent), and Lord Commander [Gerold Hightower](wiki:Gerold_Hightower). Eddard and his friend [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed) were the only survivors.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0) Eddard had the tower torn down to build cairns for the eight deceased.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-1) According to a semi-canon source, [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) was found dying inside the tower by her brother, Eddard, after the fight.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rawoiaflyanna_stark.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0)

Furious after King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) proved to be accepting of the deaths of Elia and her children,(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0) Doran's younger brother [Oberyn](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) send messages all over Dorne in an attempt to raise a rebellion for [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) during the year after the rebellion. Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), traveled to [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) to return Prince Lewyn's bones. There he spoke with Doran, ending all talks of rebellion.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-1)

Texts about Dornish history include *[Ten Thousand Ships](wiki:Ten_Thousand_Ships)* and *[The Loves of Queen Nymeria](wiki:The_Loves_of_Queen_Nymeria)*.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

The acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), sends Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) to [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) to gain [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell) as an ally for [House Baratheon of King's Landing](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0)

### A Storm of Swords

[Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), remains at Sunspear because of his health. His younger brother, Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), leads a Dornish retinue to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to take Doran's place on the [small council](wiki:Small_council).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-4) Seeking vengeance for his murdered sister [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), Oberyn is killed by Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) during Tyrion's [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Oberyn's daughters, the [Sand Snakes](wiki:Sand_Snakes), demand vengeance for their father and want to lead the angry Dornish against the Baratheons, [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister), and [Tyrells](wiki:House_Tyrell). Oberyn's cautious brother, Prince Doran, has the Sand Snakes seized, however.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4)

Doran's daughter, Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell), plots to crown Myrcella and place her on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), since Myrcella is older than her brother, King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3) Her plan falters on the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood), however. Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) is killed by [Areo Hotah](wiki:Areo_Hotah), while Myrcella is wounded by Darkstar, Ser [Gerold Dayne](wiki:Gerold_Dayne).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-1) With the exception of Darkstar, Arianne and her fellow conspirators are taken into custody.

Later, Doran reveals to Arianne there was a [secret marriage pact](wiki:Secret_marriage_pact) to wed her to the exiled Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) and her brother, Prince [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell), to Viserys's sister, Princess [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-7) Viserys was killed at [Vaes Dothrak](wiki:Vaes_Dothrak), however.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Ragot46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Doran sends [Obara Sand](wiki:Obara_Sand) with Ser [Balon Swann](wiki:Balon_Swann) to apprehend Darkstar, while [Tyene Sand](wiki:Tyene_Sand) and [Nymeria Sand](wiki:Nymeria_Sand) are sent to King's Landing.(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)

Quentyn travels across [Essos](wiki:Essos) to Daenerys, now the Queen of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen). Daenerys rejects him,(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1) however, and Quentyn is killed while attempting to tame one of her [dragons](wiki:Dragon) amidst the [second siege of Meereen](wiki:Second_siege_of_Meereen).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-0)

### The Winds of Winter

Arianne departs [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) on a mission from her father to discover the truth about the supposed [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)). Dornish hosts are massed in the [Prince's Pass](wiki:Prince's_Pass) and the [Boneway](wiki:Boneway).(wiki:Dorne.cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Barianne_i_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Carianne_i.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-0)
