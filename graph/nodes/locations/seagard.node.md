## Origins

The castle of Seagard was built to defend the coast from [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) reavers, especially [Greyjoys](wiki:House_Greyjoy) from [Pyke](wiki:Pyke).(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) After the fall of [House Justman](wiki:House_Justman), the [Mallisters](wiki:House_Mallister) of Seagard contested rule of the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) with rival petty kings.(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

[Harwyn Hardhand](wiki:Harwyn_Hoare), [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands), landed his longships forty leagues south of Seagard at the start of his campaign to conquer the riverlands from the [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King) [Arrec Durrandon](wiki:Arrec_Durrandon).(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

During [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), the bronze bell of the Booming Tower rang for the first time in three hundred years, when [Rodrik Greyjoy](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy) led the [storming of Seagard](wiki:Storming_of_Seagard). Lord [Jason Mallister](wiki:Jason_Mallister) killed Rodrik beneath the castle walls and threw the ironmen back into the sea.(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s army sailed from Seagard and [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) to subdue the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) and end the rebellion.(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

[Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), [Lysa Tully](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), and [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) visited Seagard in their youth, once camping at [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones) on the way there with Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully).(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) The Tully sisters briefly became lost during a foggy ride back to [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), but they were found by their father's ward, Petyr.(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) notes that the [Mallisters](wiki:House_Mallister) of Seagard and the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) of the [Twins](wiki:Twins) are the only standing threats to the [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) campaign in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands). Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) disregards them, stating that Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) will not take the field when victory is doubtful and that Lord [Jason Mallister](wiki:Jason_Mallister) is not strong enough to fight alone.(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Seagard is said to be one of the only riverland keeps still mercifully untouched by the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) [Wendel Frey](wiki:Wendel_Frey) serves as a page at the castle.(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Some Lannister captives from the [Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood) and [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), including Lord [Gawen Westerling](wiki:Gawen_Westerling),(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) are brought from [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) to Seagard by Lord Jason and his son, [Patrek](wiki:Patrek_Mallister).(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) accompanies the Mallisters during their ride to Seagard, and Jason warns his son against befriending an [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn). Theon boards the *[Myraham](wiki:Myraham)* at Seagard and sails for the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

### A Storm of Swords

After [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) and [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident), weds [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling) at the [Crag](wiki:Crag), the king orders the release of Jeyne's father, Gawen, from captivity at Seagard.(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

The *Myraham* returns to Seagard from [Lordsport](wiki:Lordsport), bringing word of the death of [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), the [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North) and Theon's father, and the return of Balon's exiled brother, [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), who has claimed the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair).(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

Robb intends to have his mother, Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), travel to Seagard for protection,(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) but both are killed beforehand at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) expects [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) will pen up Lord Jason in the [siege of Seagard](wiki:Siege_of_Seagard).(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) The [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) later hears that Seagard has yielded to [Black Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman)).(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) learns from Ser [Ryman Frey](wiki:Ryman_Frey) that Lord Jason surrendered Seagard and swore fealty to the Iron Throne only when Black Walder threatened to hang Patrek Mallister, a captive from the Red Wedding, if his father refused to surrender.(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

After Ryman is hanged south of [Fairmarket](wiki:Fairmarket), [Edwyn Frey](wiki:Edwyn_Frey) places blame for his father's death on Black Walder, even though his younger brother is away at Seagard.(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Jason and Patrek are imprisoned in their own castle.(wiki:Seagard.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
