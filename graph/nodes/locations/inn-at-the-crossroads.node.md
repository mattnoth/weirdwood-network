## Identity

The Inn at the Crossroads is a prominent inn located in the Riverlands at the junction of the kingsroad and the river road, near the ruby ford of the Trident. It is one of the most frequently visited and narratively significant locations in the series precisely because its crossroads position makes it a hub of travel throughout the realm. The inn was built during the reign of Jaehaerys I Targaryen, and King Jaehaerys and Queen Alysanne stayed there so often during their royal progresses that it was renamed "the Two Crowns" in their honor for a period, reverting to the simpler crossroads name in common usage.

During *A Game of Thrones*, the inn is where Catelyn Stark arrests Tyrion Lannister on Petyr Baelish's false accusation about the catspaw dagger, setting off the chain of events leading to the War of the Five Kings; Arya Stark and Mycah the butcher's boy depart from the inn to search for rubies at the ruby ford. In *A Storm of Swords*, the inn becomes the site of the confrontation between Sandor Clegane, Arya, Polliver, and the Tickler — Arya retrieves Needle there and kills the pimply squire. During *A Feast for Crows*, Brienne of Tarth kills Rorge at the inn, and the brotherhood without banners hangs several outlaws there (violating the inn's traditional safe-conduct). The innkeeper during the War of the Five Kings period is Masha Heddle; later the inn is managed by a young girl and her family.
