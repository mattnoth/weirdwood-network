## Origins

### Storm Kings

During the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age), the woods of the stormlands were inhabited by [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest), while [giants](wiki:Giants) dwelled in the foothills of the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains). The land was then settled by [First Men](wiki:First_Men) who came to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) across the [Arm of Dorne](wiki:Arm_of_Dorne). Deities worshipped by the stormlanders of antiquity included the [sea god](wiki:Sea_god) and the [goddess of the wind](wiki:Goddess_of_the_wind).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) [War ensued](wiki:War_of_the_First_Men_and_the_children_of_the_forest) between the elder races and the humans until the [Pact](wiki:Pact).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) The First Men of old often carved their tales into wood.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_durrandon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

The stormlands were consolidated by the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_King), [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), who were founded by [Durran Godsgrief](wiki:Durran_Godsgrief). In time, the Durrandons gained dominion over [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath) and the [rainwood](wiki:Rainwood), what would become the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), [Massey's Hook](wiki:Massey's_Hook), and islands in the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), such as [Tarth](wiki:Tarth) and [Estermont](wiki:Estermont).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) Storm Kings fought [river kings](wiki:River_kings) for control of lands north of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), and sometimes Storm's End controlled lands as far as [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) and the [Bay of Crabs](wiki:Bay_of_Crabs).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) The [Dornish Marches](wiki:Dornish_Marches) were created as bulwarks against [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) to the south and the [Kingdom of the Reach](wiki:Kingdom_of_the_Reach) to the west.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)

The Storm Kings defended the stormlands during the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals), but eventually the First Men intermarried with the Andal newcomers.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Himself aided by Andals, [Gyles III Gardener](wiki:Gyles_III_Gardener), King of the Reach, conquered the lands north of the rainwood, but he failed to capture Storm's End after besieging it for two years.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) King [Arlan III Durrandon](wiki:Arlan_III_Durrandon) vanquished the river kings of [House Teague](wiki:House_Teague), extending the Storm Kings' domain north to the [Neck](wiki:Neck) for three centuries.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

Stretched thin by their conquests,(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) the power of the Storm Kings gradually declined due to pressure from [House Gardener](wiki:House_Gardener) and [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) King [Arrec Durrandon](wiki:Arrec_Durrandon) lost the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) to [Harwyn Hardhand](wiki:Harwyn_Hoare), who became [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), although the stormlands fended off Harwyn's son, King [Halleck Hoare](wiki:Halleck_Hoare).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) The rivermen found the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) to be crueler masters than the stormlanders.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

Arrec's grandson, King [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon), killed King [Garse VII Gardener](wiki:Garse_VII_Gardener) at [Summerfield](wiki:Battle_of_Summerfield) and also fought alongside [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) against [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) during the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)

### House Baratheon

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), Lords [Buckler](wiki:House_Buckler), [House Errol](wiki:House_Errol), and [Fell](wiki:House_Fell) fought against [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen)'s host at the [Wendwater](wiki:Wendwater) and then retreated into their forests. [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen) burned the woods with [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes), however.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) King [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon) and his stormlords confronted [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon), the half brother of [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), near [Bronzegate](wiki:Bronzegate). Orys slew Argilac the Arrogent in the bloody battle known as the [Last Storm](wiki:Last_Storm).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

The last [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King)'s lands, titles, sigil, and house words were claimed by Orys, who married Argilac's daughter, [Argella](wiki:Argella_Durrandon). King Aegon I named Orys as [Lord Paramount of the Stormlands](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Stormlands), and Orys became the first [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Traditionally part of the stormlands, the northern [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood) and [Massey's Hook](wiki:Massey's_Hook) became part of the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands), a new province sworn to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)

### Dornish Wars

The storm lords participated in the bloody [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), which saw [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath) raided by [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) and parts of the [rainwood](wiki:Rainwood) burned.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) The [marcher lords](wiki:Marcher_lords) participated in the [Second Dornish War](wiki:Second_Dornish_War) against the [first Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Aenys_I)),(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) and they fended off attacks by the [second Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Jaehaerys_I)) in the [Third Dornish War](wiki:Third_Dornish_War).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) visited the stormlands while returning to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) from [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) in [54 AC](wiki:54_AC).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) [Morion Martell](wiki:Morion_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), desired to conquer the stormlands as far as Storm's End, but Jaehaerys crushed the Dornishmen in the [Fourth Dornish War](wiki:Fourth_Dornish_War).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1)

Prince [Lucerys Velaryon](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon) was slain by Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) at the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) declared for the [greens](wiki:Greens) of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). Instead of fighting the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) of Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), however, Borros led a host of six thousand into the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) to retaliate against the [third Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Aegon_II)) for Dornish raids.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) After Rhaenyra's death at [Dragonstone](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone), Borros and his stormlanders secured [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) for the greens and ended the [Moon of the Three Kings](wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings). They were then defeated by the [Lads](wiki:Lads) in the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

After [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) joined the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), many stormlords, especially the [marcher lords](wiki:Marcher_lords), resented the Dornish presence in the court of King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

In [206 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Lords [Caron](wiki:House_Caron) and [Dondarrion](wiki:House_Dondarrion) suppressed a [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Daeron_II)), with the father of Ser [Manfred Dondarrion](wiki:Manfred_Dondarrion) taking nearly four thousand infantry and eight hundred cavalry into the Red Mountains.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

### Robert's Rebellion

[Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) saw [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon) raise a rebel alliance against King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), who called for the head of Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). After Robert returned from the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) to call his banners, he defeated recalcitrant bannermen at [Summerhall](wiki:Battles_at_Summerhall) and eventually marched for [Ashford](wiki:Ashford).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell), Lord of [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) and a Targaryen loyalist, invaded the stormlands from the [Reach](wiki:Reach). Robert's middle brother, [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), defended their family's seat during the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End), and [Davos](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) smuggled food past the [Redwyne fleet](wiki:Redwyne_fleet) to aid Stannis's garrison.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

Robert claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), forming the royal [House Baratheon of King's Landing](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing), after the deaths of Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), killed in the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), and King Aerys, betrayed by Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) The siege of Storm's End was lifted when Lord Tyrell knelt to Robert's friend, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Stannis then led the [assault on Dragonstone](wiki:Assault_on_Dragonstone) and was given [that castle](wiki:Dragonstone) by Robert, although Stannis resents that their youngest brother, [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), eventually succeeded Robert as Lord of Storm's End.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) dies after hunting [boar](wiki:Boar) in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) Rather than kneel to the new king, Robert's alleged son [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), Lord of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), escapes [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and allies with the [Tyrells](wiki:House_Tyrell) of [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

### A Clash of Kings

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), sends Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) to court the storm lords, but they remain loyal to their liege lord, Renly.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3) Possibly, as many as some twenty thousand stormlanders join Renly.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-38)

While Renly's host is slowly progressing toward King's Landing, Stannis sails from [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) and besieges [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) After [Renly's death](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)),(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) the stormlords change their allegiance to Stannis.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) After [a shadow](wiki:Shadow_assassin) kills Ser [Cortnay Penrose](wiki:Cortnay_Penrose), Storm's End opens its gates to Stannis.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) The king divides his forces, with Stannis marching north on the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) for the capital and Ser [Imry Florent](wiki:Imry_Florent) leading the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) which sails for [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Stannis is defeated by the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) and [Tyrells](wiki:House_Tyrell) in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), however.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) Many of the stormlords kneel to King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Aside from the garrisons of Ser [Gilbert Farring](wiki:Gilbert_Farring) at Storm's End and Ser [Rolland Storm](wiki:Rolland_Storm) at Dragonstone, the remaining lords loyal to Stannis travel with him to defend the [Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall) and [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) from [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) King Joffrey dies at [his wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding),(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) and he is succeeded by his younger brother, [Tommen](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Lords [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) and [Mathis Rowan](wiki:Mathis_Rowan) lay siege to Stannis's garrison at Storm's End. Mace returns to King's Landing when he hears that his daughter, Queen [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), has been arrested, however, leaving Mathis to continue the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(300)).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_mathis_rowan.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Many of the nobles with Stannis, such as Ser [Patrek of King's Mountain](wiki:Patrek_of_King's_Mountain) and Ser [Justin Massey](wiki:Justin_Massey), hope to win lands in the [north](wiki:North), as their lands in the south are lost to them.(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

Led by [Harry Strickland](wiki:Harry_Strickland) and [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) sails from [Essos](wiki:Essos) for [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) in support of [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)). The [landing of the Golden Company](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company) occurs in the stormlands, with the sellswords quickly taking [Estermont](wiki:Estermont), [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath), [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost), [Greenstone](wiki:Greenstone), [Rain House](wiki:Taking_of_the_Rain_House), and [Crow's Nest](wiki:Taking_of_Crow's_Nest).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

### The Winds of Winter

Sellswords are said to have taken [Tarth](wiki:Tarth), most of Cape Wrath, and half the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones).(wiki:Stormlands.cite_ref-52)
