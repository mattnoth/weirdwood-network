## Appearances & Description

### Island[ ]

The isle is colder and wilder on its east side than on the sheltered side. The hill is steeper and the path meanders back and forth through weeds and briars, wind carved rocks, and twisted, thorny trees that cling to the stony hillside. [Saltpans](wiki:Saltpans) can be seen on clear nights.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
Thick mudflats surround the island when the swift tide goes out. To get to the island from the mainland by foot one must cross the mudflats when the tide is out. Because of the dangers of quicksand, tidal pools, and drowning, the crooked approach is called the path of faith.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)
When the tide is in the brothers use a ferry to get to the mainland. The isle's slopes are covered with terraced fields, with fishponds down below and a windmill above, its wood-and-sailcloth blades turning slowly in the breeze off the bay. Sheep graze on the hillside and storks wade in the shallow waters around the ferry landing.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)
Where the river meets the bay, numerous objects are washed toward the Quiet Isle. The brothers have found driftwood, silver cups, iron pots, sacks of wool and bolts of silk, rusted helms and shining swords, and rubies. Drowned creatures and slain bodies also wash up on the island.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

### Septry[ ]

The Quiet Isle's prosperous [septry](wiki:Septry) stands on a island half a mile from the shore, where the wide mouth of the [Trident](wiki:Trident) widens further to the [Bay of Crabs](wiki:Bay_of_Crabs). A pebbled path from the shore leads through a grove of apple trees to a whitewashed stable with a peaked thatch roof.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)
The brothers built a flight of wooden steps that wander back and forth across the steep hillside and among the buildings. There is a low barn on a lower slope, while a [lichyard](wiki:Lichyard) is found on a higher slope. A summer arbor produces small, tart grapes used for [wine](wiki:Wine). The brothers brew [ale](wiki:Ale), and their [mead](wiki:Mead) and sweet [cider](wiki:Cider) are famous. The brothers provide themselves with their own food and appear to be self-sufficient. Food includes bread, fresh churned butter, [honey](wiki:Honey), and a thick stew of [crabs](wiki:Crab). They also have sheep and milk cows. (wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)
The top of the hill has a low wall of unmortared stone encircling a cluster of buildings, including a windmill, the cloisters where the brothers sleep, and the common hall where they eat there meals. The wooden [sept](wiki:Sept) has leaded glass windows and wide doors carved with the likenesses of the [Mother](wiki:Mother_(the_Seven)) and the [Father](wiki:Father_(the_Seven)) and a seven-sided steeple with a walk on top. Behind the sept is a vegetable garden where some of the older brothers pull weeds.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)

### Hermit's Hole[ ]

The Hermit's Hole is a cave located in the side of a hill with a wooden door at its entrance, near a chestnut tree. It is the original residence of the first holy man to live on the Quiet Isle roughly two thousand years before the events of [A Song of Ice and Fire](wiki:A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire). Currently the [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)) occupies the cave.
Woolen carpets cover the ground and tapestries cover the walls of the cozy sanctum. The cave contains a long table, a settle, a chest, cups, and several tall cases full of books and chairs. All are made of polished driftwood which appears golden in the light from tall beeswax candles.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9)

### Women's cottages[ ]

Set aside for the women who visit the isle, be they noble ladies or [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), are modest cottages on the eastern side of the isle, looking out over a broad expanse of mud and the distant waters of the [Bay of Crabs](wiki:Bay_of_Crabs). The cottages resemble beehives made of stone, low and rounded, windowless with smokeholes in the center of their roofs. A typical cottage includes a dirt floor, a straw pallet, furs and blankets to keep her warm, a basin of water, a flagon of [cider](wiki:Cider), some bread and cheese, a small fire and two low chairs.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10)
The cottages are not often used but the brothers keep them clean and dry. On the Quiet Isle men and women do not sleep beneath the same roof unless they are wed.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-11)

## Origins

Six rubies from Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen)'s armor, knocked loose by Lord [Robert Baratheon's](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) warhammer during the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), have washed ashore at the Quiet Isle and been recovered by the brothers. Believing in the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), the brothers await the appearance of a seventh ruby.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-12)

Verbatim from Brienne's visit, spoken by the Elder Brother:

> "Six have been found. We are all waiting for the seventh."

— Elder Brother to Ser Hyle Hunt, AFFC Brienne VI (`sources/chapters/affc/affc-brienne-06.md:117`) — Rhaegar's rubies as a Chekhov's gun: six recovered from the river; the seventh outstanding. The waiting-for-the-seventh framing is the Quiet Isle's particular way of holding expectation about Rhaegar's legacy. ## Supper & Hospitality

Brienne's account of the community meal in the septry hall:

> "Their supper in the septry was as strange a meal as Brienne had ever eaten, though not at all unpleasant. The food was plain, but very good; there were loaves of crusty bread still warm from the ovens, crocks of fresh-churned butter, honey from the septry's hives, and a thick stew of crabs, mussels, and at least three different kinds of fish. Septon Meribald and Ser Hyle drank the mead the brothers made, and pronounced it excellent, whilst she and Podrick contented themselves with more sweet cider."

— Brienne's POV, AFFC Brienne VI (`sources/chapters/affc/affc-brienne-06.md:141`) — the Quiet Isle's silent communal table: bread from their ovens, butter, hive honey, crab-and-fish stew, their famous mead and sweet cider; one brother plays the high harp, proctors read from *The Seven-Pointed Star*. Model of monastic self-sufficiency and hospitality by example (no words needed).

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

The [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) has not come to the Quiet Isle, although the bodies of combatants have washed ashore and Brother [Clement](wiki:Clement) dies after the nearby [raid on Saltpans](wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans).(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-13)
[Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth), Septon [Meribald](wiki:Meribald), [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne), and Ser [Hyle Hunt](wiki:Hyle_Hunt) visit the island during Brienne's quest to find [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark). The stable is more than three quarters empty, with only half a dozen mules. Away from the other animals is Driftwood, a huge black stallion which was known as [Stranger](wiki:Stranger_(horse)) when it was owned by [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane). Brienne's group also sees a lame [gravedigger](wiki:Gravedigger) in the lichyard.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-14)
They speak with the [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)) and have a discussion on the war and Saltpans. After the others leave, the Elder Brother and Brienne discuss Brienne's mission and why she has to find Sansa.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-15) Her party then takes a ferry from the Quiet Isle to the ruins of Saltpans.(wiki:Quiet_Isle.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
