## Appearances & Description

The Gardens are located on a beach next to the [Summer Sea](wiki:Summer_Sea), three leagues to the west of [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) on a coastal road.(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)
Pale pink [marble](wiki:Marble) paves the gardens and courtyard. Terraces overlooking the numerous pools and fountains of the Water Gardens, shaded by [blood orange](wiki:Blood_orange) trees, can be reached via a fluted pillar gallery leading to a triple archway.(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
The Water Gardens are pleasant in [autumn](wiki:Autumn): hot days, cool nights, the salt breeze blowing in from the sea, and fountains and pools to admire and play in.(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Children from all stations and areas of Dorne are sent to the Water Gardens to foster, where they play together at the beach, pools and fountains, and in the water.(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The [shoulder-riding game](wiki:Shoulder-riding_game) is popular among the children in the pools.(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

## Origins

The Water Gardens were raised by [Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), as a gift for his new bride, Princess [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)), to mark the [union](wiki:Wedding_of_Maron_Martell_and_Daenerys_Targaryen) of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) with the rest of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Daenerys began the tradition of hosting children at the Water Gardens, initially the kin of [lords](wiki:Lord) but eventually also [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk).(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
The young [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) often played in the pools and fountains of the Water Gardens with [Tyene Sand](wiki:Tyene_Sand), [Garin](wiki:Garin_(orphan)), [Andrey Dalt](wiki:Andrey_Dalt), and [Sylva Santagar](wiki:Sylva_Santagar). Sometimes they were joined by [Nymeria Sand](wiki:Nymeria_Sand) and [Sarella Sand](wiki:Sarella_Sand), as well as the [Fowler twins](wiki:Fowler_twins) and the [Archon's green-haired daughter](wiki:Archon's_green-haired_daughter).(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

[Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), resides at the tranquil Water Gardens, having moved there from [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) in [298 AC](wiki:298_AC). The prince watches generations of children play there as he relaxes and thinks. He is protected by thirty [guards](wiki:House_Martell_guards) and their captain, [Areo Hotah](wiki:Areo_Hotah).(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) In Doran's absence, Sunspear is administered by Ser [Manfrey Martell](wiki:Manfrey_Martell), [Ricasso](wiki:Ricasso), [Alyse Ladybright](wiki:Alyse_Ladybright), Maester [Myles](wiki:Myles), and the prince's brother, [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), the Red Viper. Oberyn visits Doran at the Water Gardens twice a fortnight.(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
After the death of Oberyn fighting Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane), his eldest daughter, [Obara Sand](wiki:Obara_Sand), pressures Doran to seek vengeance. The gout-ridden prince reluctantly leaves the Water Gardens to return to the [Old Palace](wiki:Old_Palace) of Sunspear.(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Doran later tells Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) that he intends to return to the Gardens with Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon).(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Doran's daughter, Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell), takes Myrcella away from Sunspear in a failed attempt to crown her, however.(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
After [Cedra](wiki:Cedra) sneaks a message from Arianne, who is imprisoned in Sunspear, Doran has the servant reassigned to the Water Gardens. Doran tells Arianne that [Ellaria Sand](wiki:Ellaria_Sand) and her daughters are ensconced at the Water Gardens.(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Prince [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell) recalls that while visiting his father, Doran, at the Water Gardens, Doran explained his plan to wed Quentyn to [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen).(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
After a feast welcoming Ser [Balon Swann](wiki:Balon_Swann) to Sunspear, Prince Doran intends to bring him to the Water Gardens where he will hear the story of the wounded Myrcella.(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

### The Winds of Winter[ ]

Doran sends Arianne from the Water Gardens as an envoy to [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) in the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands). She is joined by [Nate](wiki:Nate), who tends seven [ravens](wiki:Raven) from the Water Gardens. [Dorea Sand](wiki:Dorea_Sand) remains at the palace.(wiki:Water_Gardens.cite_ref-7)

— Areo Hotah's opening line, Water Gardens opening scene, AFFC The Captain of Guards I (`sources/chapters/affc/affc-the-captain-of-guards-01.md:11`)

She was sister to King Daeron the Good, and it was her marriage that made Dorne part of the Seven Kingdoms ... It was Daenerys who filled the gardens with laughing children." Later she invited the sons and daughters of grooms, cooks, and serving men to use the pools and fountains — a tradition that endures.

— Doran Martell to Ser Balon Swann, ADWD The Watcher I (`sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-watcher-01.md:93`)
