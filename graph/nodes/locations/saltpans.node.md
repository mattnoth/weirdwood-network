## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) arrives at Saltpans six days after abandoning the dying [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) by the banks of the [Trident](wiki:Trident). Arya sees that most of the shops and inns and alehouses around the harbor have been plundered or burned from the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), though some still look inhabited. After trading [her horse](wiki:Craven) for silver, she manages to procure passage on a [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) ship, using the [iron coin](wiki:Faceless_Men) given to her by [Jaqen H'ghar](wiki:Jaqen_H'ghar).(wiki:Saltpans.cite_ref-Rasos74.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

A [raven](wiki:Raven) arrives in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) from the [septry](wiki:Septry) at the [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle), saying that [Saltpans has been savagely raided by a band of outlaws](wiki:Sack_of_Saltpans), and some of the survivors claim [a roaring brute in a hound's head helm](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) was amongst the raiders. Supposedly the Hound killed a dozen men and raped a twelve-year-old girl who had been promised to the [Faith](wiki:Faith).(wiki:Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Later reports say that all that remains of the town is the castle of Ser [Quincy Cox](wiki:Quincy_Cox), and the rest is bones and ashes.(wiki:Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)(wiki:Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Those who were at sea during the raid only returned to bury their kin.(wiki:Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)
On the [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle), the [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)) informs [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) more of what happened at Saltpans. He has treated some of the survivors, when the fisherfolk brought them across the bay to him, after the flames had gone out and they deemed it safe to land. The Elder Brother tells Brienne that the raiders wanted a galley or a cog to carry them across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). When none was at hand, they took their rage and desperation out upon the townsfolk.(wiki:Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)
The Elder Brother tells Brienne that it was not Sandor Clegane who had led the massacre, as the Hound had died by the Trident, but another man who found his hound's head helm that the Elder Brother had left on his grave.(wiki:Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) Later, Brienne learns that it was in fact [Rorge](wiki:Rorge), wearing the helm, who committed the atrocities, along with his companion [Biter](wiki:Biter). The rest of the raiders were members of the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions).(wiki:Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)(wiki:Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
The fishermen and others who escaped the assault eventually abandon the town, with most of them going to [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), leaving nothing behind in Saltpans other than the holdfast.(wiki:Saltpans.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)
