## Appearances & Description

The walls of the city are black [basalt](wiki:Basalt), black [iron](wiki:Iron), and yellow bone.(wiki:Kayakayanaya.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) The [Steel Road](wiki:Steel_Road) leads from [Vaes Dothrak](wiki:Vaes_Dothrak) and the [Mother of Mountains](wiki:Mother_of_Mountains) through the [Bone Mountains](wiki:Bone_Mountains) to Kayakayanaya.(wiki:Kayakayanaya.cite_ref-Rtloiafthe_east.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Fur-clad warriors ride the Steel Road escorting caravans to and from Kayakayanaya.(wiki:Kayakayanaya.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

## Origins

Kayakayanaya is one of three fortress cities that were once part of the [Patrimony of Hyrkoon](wiki:Patrimony_of_Hyrkoon) and were founded by the [Patriarchs of Hyrkoon](wiki:Patriarchs_of_Hyrkoon).(wiki:Kayakayanaya.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)
Over the centuries the city has faced countless assaults from the [Jogos Nhai](wiki:Jogos_Nhai), a result of an ancient war between the Patrimony of Hyrkoon and the [zorse](wiki:Zorse)-riders of the plains, which saw thousands of Hyrkoon carried off into [slavery](wiki:Slavery) and many more Jogos Nhai sacrificed to the gods of Hyrkoon.(wiki:Kayakayanaya.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_plains_of_the_jogos_nhai.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
