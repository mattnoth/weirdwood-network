## Origins

Astapor was a colony of the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis). According to an old saying, *"Bricks and blood built Astapor, and bricks and blood her people"*, referring to the red bricks gaining their color from the blood of the slaves who had made them.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-21)(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) Astapor survived the destruction of [Old Ghis](wiki:Old_Ghis) at the hands of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold), but it was conquered by the Valyrians, who learned [slavery](wiki:Slavery) from the conquered [Ghiscari](wiki:Ghiscari) and used slaves to expand their empire.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) As a result, the Ghiscari gods and the Ghiscari tongue are now largely forgotten.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-22) After the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria) and the fall of the Freehold, the Ghiscari of [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) took control of the slave trade.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

The reputation of Astapor's Unsullied was solidified when the [Three Thousand of Qohor](wiki:Three_Thousand_of_Qohor) fended off a [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* four centuries ago, during the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Astapor's eunuch soldiers are often hired by the rulers of the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

## Appearances & Description

### Location

Astapor is at the mouth of a meandering stream that the Astapori call the [Worm](wiki:Worm), which flows in from the east. To the south are the [Ghiscari](wiki:Ghiscar) hills, with the ruins of [Old Ghis](wiki:Old_Ghis). West of Astapor, beyond the hills, is the [Isle of Cedars](wiki:Isle_of_Cedars), with the cities [Ghozai](wiki:Ghozai) and [Velos](wiki:Velos), on the [Gulf of Grief](wiki:Gulf_of_Grief).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rtloiafslaver.27s_bay.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-4)

Astapor is the southernmost of the slaver cities on [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rtloiafslaver.27s_bay.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) is a hundred leagues to the north, by the old Ghiscari coastal road, and [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) is fifty more. Well-mounted travellers, riding hard, can reach Yunkai from Astapor in six days.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

### Layout

> *See also: [Images of Astapor](wiki:Category:Images_of_Astapor)*

Astapor is a port-city on [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) An old crumbling statue portraying the Astapori [harpy](wiki:Harpies) is atop the harbor gate. The bay shore is dominated by great stepped [pyramids](wiki:Pyramid), the largest of which is four hundred feet high. All manner of trees, vines, and flowers grow on the terraces of the pyramids.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Known pyramids include the [pyramid of Nakloz](wiki:Pyramid_of_Nakloz) and the [pyramid of Ullhor](wiki:Pyramid_of_Ullhor).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) The city has a [Temple of the Graces](wiki:Temple_of_the_Graces_(Astapor)).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

Astapor's streets, plazas, pyramids, [fighting pits](wiki:Fighting_pits), sulfurous fountains, gloomy [wine](wiki:Wine) caves, and city walls have all been constructed with the same kind of red brick.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) An old rhyme says "Bricks and blood built Astapor, and bricks and blood her people." This rhyme refers to the red-bricked walls of the city taking their color from the blood of the [slaves](wiki:Slaves) who made them.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) The bricks are old and crumbling, causing red dust to be all over the city.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) The city's walls are old and decaying, and its watch towers are unmanned.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

The [Plaza of Pride](wiki:Plaza_of_Pride) is an open market where the slavers take their customers to view their purchases. In the center of the plaza is a red brick fountain with a hammered bronze version of the Astapori harpy standing in its center.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) In the [Plaza of Punishment](wiki:Plaza_of_Punishment), inside the main gate of Astapor, new slaves are brought into the city. There they go past rebellious slaves who have been punished or executed.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

In the city's fighting pits, dug deep and surrounded by descending rings of seats, all manner of entertainments are held. These include putting children up against animals like bulls and [bears](wiki:Bear). The pits are named after their owners; two known pits in Astapor are [Douquor's Pit](wiki:Douquor's_Pit) and [Jothiel's Pit](wiki:Jothiel's_Pit).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)

### People

Astapor is an old city, but is not as populous as it once was. [Lys](wiki:Lys), [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), and [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) are more populous.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9) The Astapori are of mixed ethnic origins, and [their language](wiki:Astapori_language) is [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian) spoken with a growl of the [Old Ghiscari](wiki:Old_Ghiscari) tongue,(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10) similar to [the dialect](wiki:Yunkish_language) spoken in [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) The Ghiscari gods who were worshiped when the Old Empire ruled are now also gone from memory.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11) However, the Astapori still use Ghiscari glyphs.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-12)

Descending from the Ghiscari of old, most Astapori have amber skins, broad noses, dark eyes, and black or dark red hair, or a mixture of red and black hair which is distinctive to the ancient Ghiscari.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-13)(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Men might oil their hair and beards, while women might wear accessories in their hair (e.g., [ivory](wiki:Ivory) combs).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) They dredge themselves in sweet perfumes.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Freeborn men and women wear *[tokars](wiki:Tokar)*. The fringe on the tokar signifies a person's status.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) To keep the red dust of the city's bricks, which stings worse than sand, out of their eyes, Astapori women veil their faces.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-14).

Mounted Astapori guards wear tunics embroidered with linen, pleated linen skirts, sandals, and a cloak of yellow silk with [copper](wiki:Copper) disks sewn onto it.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5) They go about bareheaded, and oil and twist their hair into fantastic shapes (e.g., horn, wings, blades, and grasping hands), giving them a demonic look.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-15)(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6) These highborn warriors are wealthy young men with old names and riches, who pretend they still rule the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis) by dressing up as scourges. On feastdays, they fight mock wars in the fighting pits.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-16)

Astapor is ruled by the [Good Masters](wiki:Good_Masters), wealthy slavers.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-7)(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) At dusk the Good Masters light silk lanterns, making the pyramids glowing with colored lights.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-17)

Although the city is poorly defended and could be taken by a modest [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)*, none try, as they know they would be facing the Unsullied. Also, the sons of the harpy give lavishly to the Dothraki *[khals](wiki:Khal)*, feasting them and giving them gifts, earning their friendship.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-18)

### Economy

Astapor is a slaver city, and has over a hundred slave traders.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-8) Stories told about the flesh marts of the slave cities is dire and frightening.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) The Astapori sell bed slaves, fieldhands, scribes, craftsmen, tutors, and soldiers. Only when dealing in the latter they function as allies.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-9) The city takes a tenth of the price whenever a slave changes hands.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The greatest of the slave traders are those who sell [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied), the far-famed eunuch slave soldiers. Trained from a young age, these young soldiers are trained to be obedient, fearless warriors who feel no pain. The latter is accomplished by giving them [wine of courage](wiki:Wine_of_courage).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-19) The Astapori claim that the Unsullied are the lockstep legions of the Old Empire come again, but unlike these ancient legions, the Unsullied are not free men.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-20)(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

The Dothraki deliver many of the slaves trained and sold on the slave markets of Astapor and her sister cities.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Khal [Pono](wiki:Pono)'s *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* rides to the [Slaver Cities](wiki:Slaver_Cities) to sell thousands of slaves.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

The *[Sunblaze](wiki:Sunblaze)* sails from Astapor to [Qarth](wiki:Qarth).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### A Storm of Swords

As urged by Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont),(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) sails her ships to Astapor. There she purchases all the [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied) within its walls, including those who have yet to complete their training. After the Unsullied have been transferred to her ownership, Daenerys has her soldiers [sack the city](wiki:Fall_of_Astapor), beginning [her campaign in Slaver's Bay](wiki:Targaryen_campaign_in_Slaver's_Bay). She orders her Unsullied to strike the chains of all the slaves the Unsullied come across, and kill every man who wears a tokar.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-10)

Upon Daenerys's departure from Astapor for [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai), she leaves a [council](wiki:Ruling_council_of_Astapor) of three men, a healer, a scholar and a priest to rule. They are deposed by [Cleon](wiki:Cleon), a former butcher, who leads the people to believe that the three intend to return the Good Masters to their positions of power. Naming himself [king](wiki:King_of_Astapor) after he has the council executed, he has all the highborn boys seized to begin training new Unsullied. Each pyramid within Astapor is now an armed camp and the dead lie in the streets and the markets devoid of food and slaves to purchase.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) learns about a slave revolt in Astapor.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) reports to Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) that the revolt has spread to [Meereen](wiki:Meereen).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Lord [Ghael](wiki:Ghael) brings news to Daenerys, now the queen in [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), that the [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai)'i have attacked Astapor.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) He requests the queen aid them with her [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied) warriors, but she refuses and tells him that the freed city must defend itself.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) Yunkai besieges Astapor and Cleon is killed by a rival who called himself [Cleon the Second](wiki:Cleon_the_Second). Cleon the Second in turn is killed and a civil war erupts between Cleon the Second's murderer, [King Cutthroat](wiki:King_Cutthroat), and his concubine, [Queen Whore](wiki:Queen_Whore).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

In an attempt to break the [siege of Astapor](wiki:Siege_of_Astapor), the city's new and inferior Unsullied march against the forces of Yunkai and its allies and sellswords but are defeated.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) The [bloody flux](wiki:Bloody_flux) breaks out in the city and when people open the gates to escape, the Yunkai'i and their armies are able to get in. Queen Whore dies fighting and King Cutthroat is ripped apart by dogs. To prevent the dead and dying and the flux they carry from escaping, the Yunkai'i seal Astapor's gates.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

[Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell) reflects that the sights he saw riding down the red brick streets of Astapor will haunt him forever and that the Red City is the closest thing to hell he ever hopes to know. Astapor's burning pyramids are larger than any castle he has ever seen.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) [Sellswords](wiki:Sellswords) hunt the hills north of Astapor for those who escape the city.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) Two [Ghiscari](wiki:New_Ghis) [legions](wiki:Lockstep_legions) land at Astapor in preparation for the [second siege of Meereen](wiki:Second_siege_of_Meereen).(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

According to the peace treaty between Meereen and Yunkai, Astapor is to be restored in its old form as a slaver city.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Daenerys provides some food and shelter to Astapori refugees who reach Meereen, but her city's gates are sealed in an attempt prevent the bloody flux from spreading inside.(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)(wiki:Astapor.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
