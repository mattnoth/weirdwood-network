## Origins

### Foundation

Lys was founded as a colony of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) on an island in the [Summer Sea](wiki:Summer_Sea), near the [Broken Arm](wiki:Broken_Arm) of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) and the [Orange Shore](wiki:Orange_Shore) of [Essos](wiki:Essos).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rawoiaflys.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Founded by wealthy merchants and nobles, Lys was mostly a trading colony.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) Due to its climate, Lys soon became a resort destination of sorts for the [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlord) of old [Valyria](wiki:Valyria). The sunny island is fertile with palm and fruit trees, and the surrounding blue-green waters are filled with fish.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

### After the Doom

Some [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlord) in Lys survived the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), but the citizens killed the [dragons](wiki:Dragon) and their [dragonriders](wiki:Dragonriders).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) During the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood), [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) took Lys and [Myr](wiki:Myr), and for two generations all three cities were ruled from within the [Black Walls](wiki:Black_Wall) of Volantis. When Volantis tried to take [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), however, [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) came into the war on the Tyroshi side and Lys and Myr rose in rebellion. The [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King), [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon), led a host which defeated Volantenes threatening Myr. Lord [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) flew forth from [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) on his dragon, [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), and defeated a Volantene fleet attacking Lys.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) The Bleeding Years left the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands) a waste and freed Lys and Myr from the yoke of Volantis.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

Seven maidens and twoscore other beauties traveled from Lysene [pleasure houses](wiki:Pleasure_house) for the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) to entertain Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) and his entourage.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Some tales claim [Coryanne Wylde](wiki:Coryanne_Wylde) became the mistress of a Lysene pillow house.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) [Saera Targaryen](wiki:Saera_Targaryen) served in a Lysene pleasure garden after fleeing the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), brother to King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), had a paramour from Lys, a dancer named [Mysaria](wiki:Mysaria).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) visited Lys during his voyages.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

### The Triarchy and the Daughters' War

After the defeat of [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) in the [Battle of the Borderland](wiki:Battle_of_the_Borderland) in [96 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Lys united with [Myr](wiki:Myr) and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) to form the [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy), also known as the Kingdom of the Three Daughters. While the Triarchy was initially appreciated for subduing [pirates](wiki:Pirate) from the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), Lysene captains became particularly resented for claiming women and comely children from passing ships to serve in [pillow houses](wiki:Pillow_house). These victims included [Johanna Swann](wiki:Johanna_Swann), whose uncle refused to pay her ransom. Johanna rose to become the Black Swan, a powerful courtesan who practically ruled Lys. The Triarchy fought with Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) during his [war for the Stepstones](wiki:War_for_the_Stepstones).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the [High Council of the Triarchy](wiki:High_Council_of_the_Triarchy) accepted Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister)'s offer of alliance with the [greens](wiki:Greens). Because most of the Triarchy's losses during the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet) came from Myrish and Tyroshi ships, their admiral, [Sharako Lohar](wiki:Sharako_Lohar) of Lys, was accused by widows of having held back his own Lysene ships.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) Sharako took Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) into his custody after the battle, but the embattled admiral eventually sold his captive to [Bambarro Bazanne](wiki:Bambarro_Bazanne), a Lysene magister.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

The death of Sharako led to the collapse of the Triarchy in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC). Although the admiral had actually been killed by a rival for the affections of the [Black Swan](wiki:Johanna_Swann), it was believed that Sharako had been assassinated by [Myr](wiki:Myr). When Lys declared war on Myr, Tyrosh sent [Racallio Ryndoon](wiki:Racallio_Ryndoon) to claim the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones). Racallio declared himself [King of the Narrow Sea](wiki:King_of_the_Narrow_Sea) instead. An alliance between [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), [Lorath](wiki:Lorath), and [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) also joined the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War) in the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) and the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Many ancient Lysene houses and noble magisters fell or were killed in the fighting. The war eventually ended with the Disputed Lands divided between Lys, Myr, and Tyrosh.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)

### Lysene Spring

Of ancient lineage, [House Rogare](wiki:House_Rogare) rose to power during the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War). [Lysandro Rogare](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare), patriarch of the family and head of the [Rogare Bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank), acquired Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) after the death of his captor, [Bambarro Bazanne](wiki:Bambarro_Bazanne), in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC). Lysandro wed the prince to his beautiful daughter, [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare). While in [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) during his return from the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) was informed by Lysandro's brother, [Drazenko](wiki:Drazenko_Rogare), of Viserys's captivity. Alyn Oakenfist sailed *[Lady Baela](wiki:Lady_Baela)* to Lys and negotiated with Lysandro for the prince's release. Viserys, the younger brother of King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with Larra and three of her brothers.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1) Drazenko eventually married [Aliandra Martell](wiki:Aliandra_Martell), [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne), and was named [Lord of the Stepstones](wiki:Lord_of_the_Stepstones) by her.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5)

The Rogares' connections with the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) essentially made them princes of Lys, and the Rogare Bank grew more influential than the rival [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos). Lysandro named himself [First Magister for Life](wiki:First_Magister_for_Life). Trade flourished in the cities of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) during the so-called [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring) and the "velvet tyranny" of Lysandro the Magnificent.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6) Despite living in King's Landing, Larra refused to speak the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue). She wore Lysene fashions and was surrounded by Lysene ladies, servants, and protectors, including [Sandoq the Shadow](wiki:Sandoq_the_Shadow).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-7)

When Lysandro and Drazenko abruptly died one day apart, the [Faceless Men](wiki:Faceless_Men) were widely held responsible. The magisters and merchant princes of Lys began infighting, with [Torreo Haen](wiki:Torreo_Haen), [Silvario](wiki:Silvario_Pendaerys) and [Pereno Pendaerys](wiki:Pereno_Pendaerys), [Moreo Dagareon](wiki:Moreo_Dagareon), and [Matteno Orthys](wiki:Matteno_Orthys) all falling. Lysandro's eldest son, [Lysaro](wiki:Lysaro_Rogare), attempted to rule Lys, but he was less effective than his father. When rumors spread that the Rogare Bank was unsound, Lysaro fled with its wealth and a thousand [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied). The emptied bank was seized by Lys's magisters, as were the Rogare palace, manses, wharves, storehouses, the [Perfumed Garden](wiki:Perfumed_Garden), and most of their galleys. Several Rogares were sold into [slavery](wiki:Slavery), and Lysaro was scourged to death in Lys after being caught in [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-8)

Disliked by many [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros), the Rogares in the Seven Kingdoms also lost power. [Lotho](wiki:Lotho_Rogare) lost his right hand after having been found guilty of stealing from the King's Landing branch of the Rogare Bank, but he went on to help establish the [Bank of Oldtown](wiki:Bank_of_Oldtown). [Roggerio](wiki:Roggerio_Rogare) established the cog *[Mermaid's Daughter](wiki:Mermaid's_Daughter)* as a seaborne pillow house. After receiving gold from the Iron Bank, [Moredo Rogare](wiki:Moredo_Rogare) hired mercenaries in [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) for an attack on Lys. He eventually buried the remains of Lysaro in the family crypt.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-9) Larra gave birth to Prince [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)) and Princess [Naerys Targaryen](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen) in Westeros, but eventually returned to her native Lys.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

### Recent History

King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), half-Lysene through his mother [Larra](wiki:Larra_Rogare), had a Lysene mistress towards the end of his reign: [Serenei of Lys](wiki:Serenei_of_Lys), the last daughter of an ancient but impoverished noble family of Valyrian blood. Serenei bore the king one child, the famously beautiful [Shiera Seastar](wiki:Shiera_Seastar), whom Aegon IV legitimized on his deathbed as one of his [Great Bastards](wiki:Great_Bastards).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

In [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), Prince [Aerion Targaryen](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen) was temporarily exiled to Lys, following his role in the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow) which indirectly resulted in the death of his uncle, Prince [Baelor Breakspear](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)). Aerion brightflame stayed in Lys for a few years, and it is possible that he fathered some children while staying there.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-37)

Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) went to Lys during the reign of [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-38) Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) was sent to Lys after being suspected of using poison on Lord [Edgar Yronwood](wiki:Edgar_Yronwood), but no one dared call it exile.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-39)

[Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) supposedly drank himself to death in Lys after being driven from the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) in disgrace for stealing from their war chest.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) When [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) was a young girl she and her brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) spent some time in Lys when they were wandering from place to place.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) fled in exile to Lys with his wife [Lynesse Hightower](wiki:Lynesse_Hightower), after which he began to sell his sword to make coin. By the time Jorah returned to Lys after fighting the [Braavosi](wiki:Braavosi) on the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne), his wife had taken [Tregar Ormollen](wiki:Tregar_Ormollen) as a lover. She now rules his household as his concubine.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

[Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq) has visited Lys.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) [Varys](wiki:Varys), [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers) for King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), originates from Lys,(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rawoiafvarys.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) and according to Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle), Varys was born a [slave](wiki:Slave) there.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), Robert's [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), trades for Lysene lace.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

[Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) lost her virginity when she was sixteen to a beautiful blond Lysene sailor.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

## Appearances & Description

### Location and Layout

A mercantile city, Lys is built on a fertile island considered a paradise by [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlord). The sunny island is fertile with palm and fruit trees, and the surrounding blue-green waters are filled with fish.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Lys is protected by high walls and hired [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword). A city dominated by wealth rather than by birth, Lys is where trade is considered a profession more honorable than arms. The city is known for its [pillow houses](wiki:Pillow_house)(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) and pleasure gardens.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Temples and shrines can be found in the streets and waterfronts of the city,(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) including the [Temple of Trade](wiki:Temple_of_Trade),(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) the temple of [Yndros](wiki:Yndros),(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and a [red temple](wiki:Red_temple) of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) The hulls of Lysene galleys are often striped in bright hues.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### People

More than anywhere else in the [known world](wiki:Known_world), the blood of [Old Valyria](wiki:Valyria) still runs strong in the Lyseni, who are regarded as beautiful. Even the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) in Lys have the pale skin, silver-gold hair, and [purple](wiki:Purple_eyes), lilac, and pale blue(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) eyes of the [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlord) of old.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Many of the nobility in Lys have produced infamous beauties, and the Lysene nobility values the purity of their blood above all. [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) kings and princes are known to have looked at Lys for wives and paramours, for their blood as well as their beauty.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) The people of Lys are known to curl and perfume their hair.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

The [Lysene tongue](wiki:Lysene_language) is a corruption of [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin) has described the Lysene dialect to be a musical, flowing, liquid tongue.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-14) Like the [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) and [Pentoshi](wiki:Pentos), the Lyseni are great lovers of song.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

Lys is ruled by conclaves of [magisters](wiki:Magister), chosen from amongst the wealthiest and noblest men of the city,(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) with the [First Magister](wiki:First_Magister) as the preeminent figure.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) The *[gonfaloniere](wiki:Gonfaloniere)* is an elected military leader.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

Lys is a port city with no established faith. [Pantera](wiki:Pantera), [Yndros of the Twilight](wiki:Yndros_of_the_Twilight), [Bakkalon](wiki:Bakkalon), [Saagael](wiki:Saagael), and [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) are some of the manifold deities of Lys.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) The city is home to a love goddess(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) whose naked figure is on Lysene [coinage](wiki:Currency);(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) it is unknown if she is the same as the [Weeping Lady of Lys](wiki:Weeping_Lady_of_Lys).

### Economy

Lys is deeply involved in the [slave](wiki:Slave) trade, with slaves outnumbering freeborn Lyseni three to one. Lys is most famous for their bed slaves, and Lyseni are known to be famously voracious in their search for comely young boys and fair maids for their [pillow houses](wiki:Pillow_house). Lys is also known for the breeding of slaves, mating beauty with beauty, hoping to produce the most lovely [courtesans](wiki:Courtesan) and bedslaves.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

Lysene [coins](wiki:Currency) are oval in shape and have a naked woman stamped on them,(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) the native love goddess of Lys.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

Lys makes red and white [wine](wiki:Wine),(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) as well as fine tapestries.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) The [alchemists of Lys](wiki:Alchemists_of_Lys) are known to make [poisons](wiki:Poison), including the [strangler](wiki:Strangler)(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) and the [tears of Lys](wiki:Tears_of_Lys).(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Heady sweet perfumes also come from the city.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) The Lyseni also make fine long dirks.(wiki:Lys.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)
