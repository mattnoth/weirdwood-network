## Origins

Lord [Armond Connington](wiki:Armond_Connington) enjoyed looking at the nearby forests and crags from atop the east tower of the castle. When Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) and his escort visited Griffin's Roost for a fortnight while returning from [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), Armond's son, [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), showed his princely friend the view as well. During the welcoming feast, Rhaegar played a song on his harp which made women in the hall weep.(wiki:Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

During [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Lord Jon was raised to the position of [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), Rhaegar's father. After his failure at the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells), Jon was stripped of his titles and sent into exile, where he is believed to have drank himself to death.(wiki:Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Griffin's Roost and the [lordship](wiki:Lord) that went with it passed to Jon's cousin and castellan, Ser [Ronald Connington](wiki:Ronald_Connington). When Aerys II was replaced on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) by [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Ronald was demoted from lordship to be merely the [Knight of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Knight_of_Griffin's_Roost). Nine-tenths of his lands were then distributed to neighboring lords who had supported Robert in his rebellion.(wiki:Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

## Appearances & Description

Small but strong, Griffin's Roost is located on a lofty crag jutting out from the shores of [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath). The castle lies surrounded by red stone cliffs on three sides, which descend into the stormy waters of [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay). The land-facing approach is a long natural ridge called the griffin's throat. The entrance to the griffin's throat is guarded on one end by a gatehouse, and by the castle's main gate and two round towers on the other end.(wiki:Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Besides faded tapestries, Griffin's Roost is also decorated with arched windows displaying myriad diamond-shaped panes of red and white glass. The bed in the lord's chambers sits below a canopy of red and white velvet. The great hall contains the carved and gilded [Griffin Seat](wiki:Griffin_Seat) where fifty generations of Conningtons have ruled.(wiki:Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

The east tower, the tallest of the castle's, offers a view of the surrounding countryside. The castle also contains stables, armory, barracks, and a maester's tower with a [rookery](wiki:Rookery). A secret stair beneath the sept's altar of [the Mother](wiki:Mother_(the_Seven)) leads to a bolt-hole, while another stair under the northwest tower goes to a hidden cove beneath the crag which appears when the tide is out. A well-provisioned garrison can hold the castle against twenty times as many men.(wiki:Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

The current [Knight of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Knight_of_Griffin's_Roost) is Ser [Ronnet Connington](wiki:Ronnet_Connington), the son of Ser [Ronald Connington](wiki:Ronald_Connington). Red Ronnet supports [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings),(wiki:Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) but he joins [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) after [Renly's death](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)). Ronnet submits to King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) after the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater).(wiki:Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Ronnet joins the host of Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) to subdue the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

While Red Ronnet is away, his exiled uncle, [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), leads the [taking of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost) by the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company). Only four members of Ronnet's garrison survive the attack. Jon confines Ronnet's bastard son, [Ronald Storm](wiki:Ronald_Storm), and his siblings, [Raymund](wiki:Raymund_Connington) and [Alynne](wiki:Alynne_Connington), to the west tower.(wiki:Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

Jon holds a war council in the castle's [solar](wiki:Solar) with [Harry Strickland](wiki:Harry_Strickland), [Black Balaq](wiki:Black_Balaq), [Franklyn Flowers](wiki:Franklyn_Flowers), [Haldon Halfmaester](wiki:Haldon_Halfmaester), [Malo Jayn](wiki:Malo_Jayn), [Brendel Byrne](wiki:Brendel_Byrne), [Dick Cole](wiki:Dick_Cole), and [Lymond Pease](wiki:Lymond_Pease). Harry advises waiting at the strong castle so the Golden Company can regroup, but Jon and Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) decide to advance on [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)

### The Winds of Winter

Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) stops at Griffin's Roost during her mission to meet with Jon and Aegon as an envoy of her father, [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell).(wiki:Griffin's_Roost.cite_ref-Winds_1-1)
