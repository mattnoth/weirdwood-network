## Origins

### Old Empire

The ancient city of [Ghis](wiki:Old_Ghis), in [Ghiscar](wiki:Ghiscar) along the [Gulf of Grief](wiki:Gulf_of_Grief), was one of the oldest known cultures and nations in the world. Its founding and golden age predate that of its successor, [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), by thousands of years. The city grew into a military, economic and cultural powerhouse, the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis). From Ghiscar, the empire conquered and colonized the east of Slaver's Bay.(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-9)

At its height, Ghis fielded highly-disciplined [lockstep legions](wiki:Lockstep_legions) reputed to have been nearly unbeatable on the battlefield. The architecture of Ghis was dominated by massive brick towers and [pyramids](wiki:Pyramid) built on the back of [slave](wiki:Slave) labor. Its symbol was the harpy, a fanged woman with leathery wings for arms, the legs of an eagle, and the tail of a scorpion, clutching a thunderbolt in her talons.(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-13)

### Valyrian Freehold

The Old Empire fell over five thousand years ago after warring with the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) to the west, losing a series of five wars with Valyria before toppling. Despite its wealth and nearly invincible legions, Ghis could not stand against Valyria's ultimate weapon, [dragons](wiki:Dragons).(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-10)

As the Old Empire collapsed, the city of [Ghis](wiki:Old_Ghis) toppled into ruin and most Ghiscari bloodlines were extinguished or enslaved. The Ghiscari language was replaced with [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian)(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-11) spoken in local dialects.(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-13)

### Slaving Cities

The [Valyrian peninsula](wiki:Valyrian_peninsula) was destroyed in the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), causing chaos in the rest of the Freehold.(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) [Astapor](wiki:Astapor), [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai), and [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), once lowly colonies along Slaver's Bay, rose into prominence and thrived after the Doom. These cities reestablished themselves as hubs of the global [slave](wiki:Slave) trade and became wealthy.(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-12)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

[Astapor](wiki:Astapor) is conquered by [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) after she purchases the city's entire population of [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied) and sends them against the helpless [Good Masters](wiki:Good_Masters).(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-7) Daenerys sets up a new government headed by a healer, a priest and a scholar. After she leaves, however, a former butcher named [Cleon](wiki:Cleon) seizes control of the city and reinstates slavery, only with the former slaves as the new masters. Cleon the Butcher King sends Daenerys gifts and wedding invitations, though shes does not approve of his rule.(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3)

After the [fall of Astapor](wiki:Fall_of_Astapor), [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) girds itself with a slave army of approximately four thousand and purchases the services of the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons) and [Stormcrows](wiki:Stormcrows), totaling over one thousand sellswords. Daenerys lures the Stormcrows to her service and ambushes the Second Sons, [scattering](wiki:Battle_of_Yunkai) them. The Wise Masters surrender and release all of their slaves.(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-14)

[Meereen](wiki:Meereen) utilizes a scorched earth defense against Daenerys, razing the land surrounding the city and nailing slave children onto posts with their fingers pointing to the city. Outside the city's gates, [Oznak zo Pahl](wiki:Oznak_zo_Pahl) challenges Daenerys's forces but is slain by [Strong Belwas](wiki:Belwas).(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8) In order to avoid an extended [siege](wiki:Siege_of_Meereen), Daenerys has a small group infiltrate the city and free the enslaved [pit fighters](wiki:Pit_fighters) of the [fighting pits](wiki:Fighting_pits). They use the ensuing chaos to open the gates and force a surrender. Daenerys has a number of the Great Masters executed, then linger in the city to rule as its queen.(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4)

### A Dance with Dragons

While Daenerys attempts to rule in Meereen, Astapor is [conquered](wiki:Siege_of_Astapor) by Yunkai and [New Ghis](wiki:New_Ghis).(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) The Yunkai'i then lead [another siege of Meereen](wiki:Second_Siege_of_Meereen).

[Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) leads the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) to Slaver's Bay.(wiki:Slaver's_Bay.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
