## Appearances & Description

Located near willows, the inn is a sprawling grey building with timber and turrets. Half of it is built over the lake on pilings, and rough-cut planks lead over the muddy shore to the ferry landing. The inn's stable has a thatched roof, and the yard, which is enclosed by a gated drystone wall, contains a well and a watering trough. Brown [ale](wiki:Beer) and roast [boar](wiki:Boar) are served at the inn.(wiki:Old_inn_by_the_lakeshore.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Origins

In [212 AC](wiki:212_AC), many travelers visited the inn while traveling to attend the [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls). The ferryman [Ned](wiki:Ned_(ferryman)) led Ser [John the Fiddler](wiki:John_the_Fiddler) and his companions across the water, and the inn was filled with the retinues of [Lord Shawney](wiki:Lord_Shawney) and [Lord Costayne](wiki:Lord_Costayne_(Aerys_I)). Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) asked the innkeep if he and his squire, [Egg](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), could sleep in the stable, but the woman refused. Dunk and Egg instead camped for the night with [hedge knights](wiki:Hedge_knight) by [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) stumps along the shore. Lords Shawney and Costayne argued the next day over who should be ferried first, and the hedge knights then had to wait even longer to cross because of the arrival of Lord [Smallwood](wiki:House_Smallwood)'s retinue.(wiki:Old_inn_by_the_lakeshore.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
