## Appearances & Description

The common room is a cavernous warren, larger than the great halls of many [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) castles.(wiki:Merchant's_House.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) The maze of the common room is built around a central courtyard. A trellis of flowering vines throws intricate patterns across its flagstone floor, and green and purple moss grows between the stones.(wiki:Merchant's_House.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) The dimly-lit interior of the common room has numerous private alcoves and hidden nooks which are ideal for discrete business. The [widow of the waterfront](wiki:Widow_of_the_waterfront) has a customary table back by the courtyard where she is steeped in shadow. [Slave](wiki:Slave) girls serve flagons of [ale](wiki:Ale), [wine](wiki:Wine), and an iced green drink that smells of [mint](wiki:Mint).(wiki:Merchant's_House.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
The Merchant's House is famous for its locks, which are as secure as a gaol.(wiki:Merchant's_House.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) There are cheap rooms on the fourth floor, with iron rings set in the walls for chaining up slaves. One of the rooms, edged into a corner of the building beneath a sloping roof, has a low ceiling, a sagging feather bed with an unpleasant odor, and a slanting wood-plank floor. One window looks toward the [Long Bridge](wiki:Long_Bridge) and the [Black Walls](wiki:Black_Walls), and the other opens on [Fishermonger's Square](wiki:Fishermonger's_Square) below.(wiki:Merchant's_House.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

## Origins

After the death of Triarch [Vogarro](wiki:Vogarro), [his widow](wiki:Widow_of_the_waterfront) took up residence at the Merchant's House west and set up her business in its common room.(wiki:Merchant's_House.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Prince [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell) and his companions, Ser [Gerris Drinkwater](wiki:Gerris_Drinkwater) and Ser [Archibald Yronwood](wiki:Archibald_Yronwood), stay at second floor of the Merchant's House while in [Volantis](wiki:Volantis). The innkeep advises them against traveling by foot, as persons of quality are expected to travel by [hathay](wiki:Hathay) or [palanquin](wiki:Palanquin).(wiki:Merchant's_House.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Prior to leaving for [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai), four [Windblown](wiki:Windblown) serjeants set up a trestle table outside of the Merchant's House calling to every passing man and boy, looking for fresh meat to fill their ranks. Their presence hatches Archibald's plan for himself and his companions to join the sellswords as a means to get to [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) and thereby [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen).(wiki:Merchant's_House.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) takes his hostage, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), to the Merchant's House. He chains Tyrion to the wall in a room on the fourth floor and later takes the [dwarf](wiki:Dwarf) with him when he meets with the [widow of the waterfront](wiki:Widow_of_the_waterfront) in the common room. Jorah seeks assistance in finding a ship to take himself and Tyrion to Meereen to meet Daenerys. The widow is reluctant to aid them due to Jorah's secretive attitude, but she ultimately offers them the information they need after [Penny](wiki:Penny) attacks Tyrion. The widow has the inn's landlord bring Penny up to her rooms.(wiki:Merchant's_House.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)

Their destination proved to be the Merchant's House, a four-story monstrosity that squatted amongst the warehouses, [brothels](wiki:Brothel), and taverns of the waterside like some enormous fat man surrounded by children. Its common room was larger than the great halls of half the castles in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), a dim-lit maze of a place with a hundred private alcoves and hidden nooks whose blackened beams and cracked ceilings echoed to the din of sailors, traders, captains, money changers, shippers, and [slavers](wiki:Slavers), lying, cursing, and cheating each other in half a hundred different tongues.(wiki:Merchant's_House.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)
