## Origins

### House Gardener

The beginnings of the Reach can be traced back to [Garth Greenhand](wiki:Garth_Greenhand), the legendary High King of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) who lived in the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_garth_greenhand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The royal line of [House Gardener](wiki:House_Gardener) of [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) claimed descent from Garth as do many current houses in the region.(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) What is now the [Ravenry](wiki:Ravenry) of the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) is said to have been a pirate stronghold during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Beginning from the [Reach proper](wiki:Reach_proper), the Gardener [Kings of the Reach](wiki:Kings_of_the_Reach) expanded north to [Old Oak](wiki:Old_Oak), [Red Lake](wiki:Red_Lake), and [Goldengrove](wiki:Goldengrove), and south to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor), and [Horn Hill](wiki:Horn_Hill).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

The Reach is the home of mythical warriors and knights such as [Serwyn of the Mirror Shield](wiki:Serwyn_of_the_Mirror_Shield), [Davos the Dragonslayer](wiki:Davos_the_Dragonslayer), [Roland of the Horn](wiki:Roland_of_the_Horn), and the [Knight Without Armor](wiki:Knight_Without_Armor).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1)

The Reach is the heart of the chivalric tradition in the Seven Kingdoms, the place where knighthood is most universally esteemed. [Tourneys](wiki:Tourney) here have the most elaborate and varied rules, and are the most likely to be held for [knights](wiki:Knight) only. The greatest champions of the Reach became knights in the [Order of the Green Hand](wiki:Order_of_the_Green_Hand).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_highgarden.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) [House Peake](wiki:House_Peake) expelled [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) from the Reach during the reign of King [Perceon III Gardener](wiki:Perceon_III_Gardener).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

[Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) have warred against the Reach for thousands of years; rivalries include the feud between [House Oakheart](wiki:House_Oakheart) and [House Dayne](wiki:House_Dayne).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Ser [Wilbert Osgrey](wiki:Wilbert_Osgrey) fell defending against an invasion of the Reach by [Lancel IV Lannister](wiki:Lancel_IV_Lannister), [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) The Kings of the Reach also warred with the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_Kings) of [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon), gradually conquering land from the western [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands). However, King [Garse VII Gardener](wiki:Garse_VII_Gardener) was slain by King [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon) in the [Battle of Summerfield](wiki:Battle_of_Summerfield).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) The [Dornish Marches](wiki:Dornish_Marches), the lands just north of the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), have long been disputed. The lords of Highgarden count [Defender of the Marches](wiki:Defender_of_the_Marches) amongst their titles,(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) although most of the [marcher lords](wiki:Marcher_lords) now owe allegiance to [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon) of the stormlands.(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

### House Tyrell

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), King [Mern IX Gardener](wiki:Mern_IX_Gardener) led the army of the Reach against the invading [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), but he was killed at the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire) and his line was ended. [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell), the stewards of the Gardeners, surrendered Highgarden to [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen). They were rewarded with both the castle and the title of [Lord Paramount of the Mander](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Mander). The [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon's_Conquest)) and Lord [Manfred Hightower](wiki:Manfred_Hightower_(Aegon's_Conquest)) did not oppose Aegon when he approached [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), increasing the new king's legitimacy.(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Rather than remain independent, the Reach became part of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) ruled by the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the Reach was split between the [greens](wiki:Greens) and the [blacks](wiki:Blacks), with the [Hightowers](wiki:House_Hightower) providing much of the support for the greens (along with the [Redwynes](wiki:House_Redwyne)), but with the [Rowans](wiki:House_Rowan), [Tarlys](wiki:House_Tarly), [Costaynes](wiki:House_Costayne), [Mullendores](wiki:House_Mullendore), and [Grimms](wiki:House_Grimm) supporting the blacks. The [Battle on the Honeywine](wiki:Battle_on_the_Honeywine) and [First](wiki:First_Battle_of_Tumbleton) and [Second Tumbleton](wiki:Second_Battle_of_Tumbleton) were fought in the Reach.(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

In the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), several of [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre)'s greatest supporters were men of the Reach, including Ser [Quentyn Ball](wiki:Quentyn_Ball) and Lord [Gormon Peake](wiki:Gormon_Peake), while others supported [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) The Reach suffered from a terrible drought in [211 AC](wiki:211_AC).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-2) The [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) have not raided the Reach since the time of [Dagon Greyjoy](wiki:Dagon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

[Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) saw the Reach provide much of the pro-Targaryen strength. Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) led the van of Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) to victory against Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_Baratheon) in the [Battle of Ashford](wiki:Battle_of_Ashford).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) After Ashford, Mace led the unsuccessful [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

With the death of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and the ascension of his supposed son, [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) and Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) flee [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) for [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), where Renly weds [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) and claims the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

### A Clash of Kings

The lords of the Reach join the [Tyrells](wiki:House_Tyrell) in declaring for Renly, aside from the neutral [Redwynes](wiki:House_Redwyne).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) closes the [roseroad](wiki:Roseroad) and Renly holds a [melee at Bitterbridge](wiki:Melee_at_Bitterbridge) during his slow march on King's Landing.(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

Some lords join [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) after Renly's [death at Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)),(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) although there is [fighting at Bitterbridge](wiki:Fighting_at_Bitterbridge) between [Florents](wiki:House_Florent) and [Tarlys](wiki:House_Tarly).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) hurries from King's Landing to Bitterbridge to meet with Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell),(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) continuing on to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) to negotiate with Mace and [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) The Tyrells join with Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) to defeat Stannis in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Hundreds of wayns deliver food from the Reach to King's Landing.(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) The lords of the Reach provide much of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne)'s manpower, under the uneasy alliance between Houses [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) and Tyrell.(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1)(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Olenna returns to Highgarden, while Ser [Garlan Tyrell](wiki:Garlan_Tyrell) marches on [Brightwater Keep](wiki:Brightwater_Keep) to claim it from the Florents.(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

The Reach remains untouched by the war until the [taking of the Shields](wiki:Taking_of_the_Shields) by [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) The [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) raiders then [invade the Arbor](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Arbor), attack Whispering Sound, and sail up the [Mander](wiki:Mander).(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) Ser [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Hightower) and Ser [Gunthor Hightower](wiki:Gunthor_Hightower) lead the defense of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown),(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-1) while Garlan Tyrell gathers men to retake the Shields.(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2)

[Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) sails on *[Cinnamon Wind](wiki:Cinnamon_Wind)* from [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) to Oldtown to study at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel). Archmaester [Marwyn](wiki:Marwyn) departs Oldtown for [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) after hearing Sam's story.(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-2)

### A Dance with Dragons

With the Reach preoccupied with the ironborn, [Obara Sand](wiki:Obara_Sand) wants [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), to attack the Reach as well.(wiki:Reach.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)
