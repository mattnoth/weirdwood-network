## Appearances & Description

The [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort)'s kitchens, a stone octagon with a broken dome, contain a huge central well which descends into the earth. A skinny [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) has grown through the kitchens' slate floor. The well has stone steps which circle down to its depths,(wiki:Black_Gate.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) and the Black Gate at its bottom is said to be findable and accessible only by members of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Black_Gate.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Set deep in the wall of the well, the Black Gate is made of faintly-glowing white [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood). The old face on the wood is pale, shrunken, and wrinkled with blind white eyes. When someone approaches the Black Gate, the blind eyes open and the face asks them to identify themselves. If a man of the Night's Watch repeats a part of his [vow](wiki:Night's_Watch), the Black Gate will allow admittance. The door's lips open wider and wider still until nothing remains but a great gaping mouth in a ring of wrinkles.(wiki:Black_Gate.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Coldhands](wiki:Coldhands) guides [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly), [Gilly](wiki:Gilly), and [her baby](wiki:Monster) from [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall) to the Black Gate. While Coldhands, unable to pass through the [Wall](wiki:Wall), remains on the northern side with his [elk](wiki:Elk), the other three pass through the Black Gate to reach the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort).(wiki:Black_Gate.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark), [Meera](wiki:Meera_Reed) and [Jojen Reed](wiki:Jojen_Reed), [Hodor](wiki:Hodor), and [Summer](wiki:Summer_(direwolf)) encounter Sam when the black brother ascends the well's steps to the Nightfort's kitchens. Sam leads them down the well to the Black Gate, and he recites part of his [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) vows to allow the travelers to pass through the Wall.(wiki:Black_Gate.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
