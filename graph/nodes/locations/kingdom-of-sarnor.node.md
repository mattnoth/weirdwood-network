## Origins

### Early History[ ]

Sarnor was an ancient kingdom centered around the great river [Sarne](wiki:Sarne) and its tributaries and some of the lake remnants of the [Silver Sea](wiki:Silver_Sea). According to distant legend, the Silver Sea was once truly a large inland body of water, its coasts ruled by the [Fisher Queens](wiki:Fisher_Queens). Over the eons the sea dried out into lakes and their realm collapsed. The Sarnori, who call themselves the Tall Men (Tagaez Fen in their language) trace their lineage to the king [Huzhor Amai](wiki:Huzhor_Amai), descended from the last of the Fisher Queens. He took as wives the daughters of the greatest lords and kings of neighboring peoples: the [Cymmeri](wiki:Cymmeri), [Zoqora](wiki:Zoqora), and [Gipps](wiki:Gipps). However, it is uncertain whether Huzhor Amai ever actually existed; the heroic figure may be a mythological retelling of how the ancient Sarnori conquered and assimilated the Cymmeri, Zoqora, and Gipps, merging into one people.(wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
By the time recorded history began, the Sarnori had established a series of city-states along the Sarne River and its tributaries, and became well-known for using chariot teams in battle. Always a proud and quarrelsome people, they were seldom unified, but engaged in long wars against the [Qaathi](wiki:Qaathi) for domination of central Essos. The Qaathi lost these wars more than they won them, until they were driven past [Lhazar](wiki:Lhazar) to the southern end of the [Bone Mountains](wiki:Bone_Mountains). Later, Dothraki expansion and desertification destroyed the Qaathi cities one by one, until only one outpost remained: [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), which ironically then flourished as a major passageway for sea trade into the [Jade Sea](wiki:Jade_Sea).
At their height, the Sarnori traded with [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), [Yi Ti](wiki:Yi_Ti), [Leng](wiki:Leng), and [Asshai](wiki:Asshai). They also sailed the [Shivering Sea](wiki:Shivering_Sea) to [Ib](wiki:Ib), the [Thousand Islands](wiki:Thousand_Islands), and Mossovy; warred against the [Qaathi](wiki:Qaathi) and the [Ghiscari Empire](wiki:Ghiscari_Empire); and led forays against bands of [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki), nomadic horsemen who roamed the steppes to their east.(wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)
Eventually, the Sarnori became involved in the [wars between](wiki:Ghiscari_Wars) the ancient [Ghiscari Empire](wiki:Ghiscari_Empire) and the rising [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold). The Sarnori allied with Valyria during the [Second](wiki:Second_Ghiscari_War) and [Third Ghiscari Wars](wiki:Third_Ghiscari_War), though during the [Fourth](wiki:Fourth_Ghiscari_War), the rival Sarnori kings supported both sides.(wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)
After the fifth and final Ghiscari War some 5,000 years ago, the Valyrians did not move on to conquer the Sarnori - just as they were in no hurry to conquer the still-independent [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar) city-states to the west, and the dragon-lords were always busy with internal rivalries in Valyria itself. Sarnor remained a trading partner with Valyria, and perhaps a useful buffer against raids by the nascent [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) mounted raiders from the northeast of Essos's central grasslands.(wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

### Century of Blood[ ]

After the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), the Sarnori kingdoms took less than a century to fall. While the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) fought to become the successor to [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), the [Tall Men](wiki:Tall_Men) ignored the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) threat, even as [khalasars](wiki:Khalasar) began to raid across their eastern borders. Accustomed to them being little more than a nuisance, some Sarnori kings tried to use the Dothraki in their own wars, offering them gold and slaves and other gifts to fight against their rivals. [Khal](wiki:Khal) [Mengo](wiki:Mengo), who had united all khalasars under his rule, accepted the gifts but then burnt fields, farms, and towns to return the grasslands to nature.(wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)
The Tall Men did not realize the danger until Mengo's son, Khal [Moro](wiki:Moro_(Century_of_Blood)), led his khalasar to the gates of [Sathar](wiki:Sathar). The men of Sathar were put to the sword, while the women and children carried off as slaves; three-quarters of them dying on the march to the [Ghiscari](wiki:Ghiscari) city of [Hazdahn Mo](wiki:Hazdahn_Mo). Sathar was burned to ash. Rather than uniting against the Dothraki, the Sarnori kings of [Kasath](wiki:Kasath) and [Gornath](wiki:Gornath) fought each other for Sathar's plunder.(wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)
Six years after the fall of Sathar, Khal [Moro](wiki:Moro) razed Kasath, with the aid of the King of Gornath, who had made common cause with the Dothraki and taken one of Moro's daughters to wife. A dozen years afterward Gornath fell to Khal [Horro](wiki:Horro), who had killed Moro. The King of Gornath was killed by his wife, who was then taken by Horro. The slaying of Horro by a rival several years later led to the splintering of his great khalasar into a dozen lesser hosts.(wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)
The Sarnori cities fell to new khals who sought to outdo one another in conquest and carrying the broken gods of the defeated to [Vaes Dothrak](wiki:Vaes_Dothrak). Much of the Tall Men's history was lost with the burning of [Sallosh](wiki:Sallosh) and its great library. [Kyth](wiki:Kyth) and [Hornoth](wiki:Hornoth) soon fell to rival khals. The fortress city of [Mardosh](wiki:Mardosh) resisted for nearly six years, but its starving defenders eventually slew their women and children and rode to certain defeat.(wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9)
The fall of Mardosh led the Sarnori to unite under [Mazor Alexi](wiki:Mazor_Alexi), last of the High Kings. His great host was trapped and destroyed by four khalasars at the [Field of Crows](wiki:Field_of_Crows), however. [Sarnath](wiki:Sarnath) was put to the torch by Khal [Loso](wiki:Loso) less than a fortnight later, and the remaining cities continued to fall. [Sarys](wiki:Sarys), the last Sarnori city to be conquered, had already been mostly abandoned when Khal [Zeggo](wiki:Zeggo) reached it. The support of [Ibben](wiki:Ibben) and [Lorath](wiki:Lorath) have allowed the Sarnori city of [Saath](wiki:Saath) to survive, however.(wiki:Kingdom_of_Sarnor.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10)
