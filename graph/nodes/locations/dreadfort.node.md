## Origins

### Ancient History

The Dreadfort was the seat of the [Red Kings](wiki:Red_King) from [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes). The Boltons eventually submitted to the [Kings in the North](wiki:Kings_in_the_North) from [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) near the start of the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). The Boltons aided King [Theon Stark](wiki:Theon_Stark) in repelling the invasion of [Argos Sevenstar](wiki:Argos_Sevenstar).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

The Dreadfort once joined [House Greystark](wiki:House_Greystark) of the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den) in rebellion against the Starks.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Centuries ago, the Boltons rose up against King [Harlon Stark](wiki:Harlon_Stark) and the Dreadfort held out for two years under siege before the Boltons surrendered.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

### Recent History

[Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) has possibly visited the Dreadfort.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

The servant [Reek](wiki:Reek) twice stole perfume from Lady [Bethany Bolton](wiki:Bethany_Bolton)'s bedchamber because of his horrid smell.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

[Domeric Bolton](wiki:Domeric_Bolton), the heir of Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), died at the Dreadfort(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Rawoiafdomeric_bolton.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) after visiting his baseborn brother, [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), along the [Weeping Water](wiki:Weeping_Water).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) Maester [Uthor](wiki:Uthor) called it a sickness of the bowels, but Roose blamed [poison](wiki:Poison).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3) Lord Bolton subsequently brought Ramsay to live at the Dreadfort.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) The Bastard of Bolton has earned a dark reputation from hunting women, and victims who give him good sport are sometimes the namesake for [hounds in the Dreadfort's kennel](wiki:Bastard's_girls).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-4)

## Appearances & Description

The Dreadfort is a strong fortress with thick stone walls and massive towers.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) The castle has triangular merlons which look like sharp stone teeth.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

The great hall of the Dreadfort is dim and smoky, with rows of torches grasped by skeletal human hands jutting from the walls. Long tables stand before a dais with a high table and the seat of the [Lord of the Dreadfort](wiki:Lord_of_the_Dreadfort). The hall has a vaulted ceiling and wooden rafters turned black from smoke.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Kennels are located in the Dreadfort's yard, and steep steps lead down to the dungeons. The cells beneath the castle have cold damp stone walls.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) The castle is ill omened, for it is said the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) keep [torture](wiki:Torture) chambers and a special room where they hang the [flayed](wiki:Flaying) skins of their enemies, including several [Kings in the North](wiki:Kings_in_the_North).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Boltons are buried beneath the Dreadfort.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Dreadfort men under the command of Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) join the northern host called by [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### A Clash of Kings

Lady [Donella Hornwood](wiki:Donella_Hornwood) learns that [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), the bastard son of Roose, is massing men at the Dreadfort. She is concerned, as the lands of [House Hornwood](wiki:House_Hornwood) lands adjoin those of the Boltons.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) While returning from Winterfell's [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast), Donella is seized and forced to wed Ramsay. In response, Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) seizes [Hornwood](wiki:Hornwood) to prevent Ramsay from occupying it,(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) and [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) knights battle Dreadfort men in the [Hornwood forests](wiki:Hornwood_(forest)). News reaches Winterfell via Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel), but he does not have the manpower to stop the [fighting in the Hornwood](wiki:Fighting_in_the_Hornwood).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

"[Reek](wiki:Reek)", a servant of House Bolton, is captured by Rodrik after the supposed death of Ramsay. After the [capture of Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell) by [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), "Reek" aligns himself with Theon. Because Theon lacks the manpower to hold Winterfell, he allows "Reek" to leave to hopefully gather a few hundred reinforcements.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) However, "Reek" returns to Winterfell with six hundred men from the Dreadfort. He attacks Rodrik's army of [Stark](wiki:House_Stark) loyalists in a [battle at Winterfell](wiki:Battle_at_Winterfell), reveals himself to be the still-living Ramsay Snow, and orders the [sack of Winterfell](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

### A Storm of Swords

[Lothar Frey](wiki:Lothar_Frey) reads a letter to [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), now [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun). According to [Big Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)), the women and children who survived the sack of Winterfell, which allegedly was caused by Theon's [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn), were taken to safety at the Dreadfort by the Bastard of Bolton.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) sends a raven to the Boltons in a plea for help to defend [Castle Black](wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black) from [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

The hosts of Roose and Robb meet at the [Twins](wiki:Twins) for the wedding of Lord [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully). Robb agrees with Roose to keep Theon captive at the Dreadfort.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) The Boltons turn on their Stark allies, however, and aid the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) in slaughtering them at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) signs decrees which name Roose the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North) and which legitimize Ramsay Snow as Ramsay Bolton.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) The [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), intends for the Dreadfort to battle the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) for control of the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Ramsay serves as castellan of the Dreadfort.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

[Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) is kept in a dungeon below the castle where he has been subjected to Ramsay's flaying since the [sack of Winterfell](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) [Kyra](wiki:Kyra) and Theon eventually escape through an unguarded postern gate, but this turns out to be a ruse by Ramsay; Kyra is hunted and killed by Ramsay and Theon is returned to captivity.(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) [Big Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)) and [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)) eventually bring the mutilated Theon, now called "Reek", to Ramsay's feast in the great hall with [Arnolf Karstark](wiki:Arnolf_Karstark) and [Hother Umber](wiki:Hother_Umber).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

Ramsay marches with a host to resolve the [siege of Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Arnolf writes to [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) at Castle Black that the Dreadfort is weakly garrisoned with fewer than fifty men, half of them servants. However, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), explains that the Dreadfort is a strong castle, and he convinces Stannis to instead march from [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) to [Deepwood Motte](wiki:Fight_by_Deepwood_Motte).(wiki:Dreadfort.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)
