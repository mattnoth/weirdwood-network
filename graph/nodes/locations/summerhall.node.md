## Origins

### Prince of Summerhall

Summerhall was built in the [Dornish Marches](wiki:Dornish_Marches) during the notably pro-Dornish reign of King [Daeron II](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen). It was a [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) summer castle and royal residence used frequently by Daeron in his younger years. As he grew older, he left King's Landing less frequently and Summerhall passed to his youngest son, Prince [Maekar](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen). The prince maintained a household there after the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion).(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-4) This was possible since Daeron's heir, [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), had [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), and [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen) and [Rhaegel](wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen) seldom left the court. Maester [Melaquin](wiki:Melaquin) served at Summerhall.(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rawoiafmelaquin.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) The holder of the castle became known as the [Prince of Summerhall](wiki:Prince_of_Summerhall).(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

After the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow), Maekar wanted his son, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), to return to Summerhall. Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), a [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight), took Aegon as his squire, however.(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Aegon had a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) at Summerhall.(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Maekar brooded at Summerhall after his brother, King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), named [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

### Tragedy at Summerhall

A great fire broke out in the castle in [259 AC](wiki:259_AC), which was a serious blow to House Targaryen. The fire left the castle ruined and resulted in several deaths, including King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), his eldest son and once heir, Prince [Duncan the Small](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen), and the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall).(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rawoiafduncan.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-9) It is sometimes blamed on one of the many Targaryen attempts to bring dragons back by hatching ancient [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_egg). [Corso](wiki:Corso) was the last maester to serve at Summerhall.(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-11)

Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) was born at Summerhall(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rawoiafrhaegar_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) on the day of the tragedy.(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) The crown prince grew up with a complex fascination for the ruins. Here he was happiest, yet also saddened by the memory of the tragedy.(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) He liked to visit by himself and sleep in the ruined hall of Summerhall beneath the [moon](wiki:Moon) and the stars. Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) thought Rhaegar's birth was associated with [the prince that was promised](wiki:The_prince_that_was_promised), but Rhaegar later thought the legend was associated with his own son, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)).(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

### Robert's Rebellion

Upon the outbreak of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), the lords of the stormlands varied in their allegiance to their king, [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), and their lord, [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). The loyalist Lords [Fell](wiki:Lord_Fell), [Cafferen](wiki:Lord_Cafferen), and [Grandison](wiki:Lord_Grandison) planned to join their strength at Summerhall and march on [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). Robert was informed of their march, however, and defeated the three armies in succession in three [battles at Summerhall](wiki:Battles_at_Summerhall) before they could coordinate.(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) expects that when [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell) comes to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), he will take the [Boneway](wiki:Boneway) from [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) to Summerhall, and then head east to the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad).(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Tyrion is later surprised when [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) arrives instead of Doran.(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

At [High Heart](wiki:High_Heart), the [dwarf woman](wiki:Ghost_of_High_Heart) tells [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) that she experienced grief at Summerhall.(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

In [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) recalls that the children of King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) wed for love. Treason and turmoil followed, ending at Summerhall in [sorcery, fire, and grief](wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall).(wiki:Summerhall.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
