## Origins

### Ancient History

Ancient Lorath was inhabited by a people known as the [mazemakers](wiki:Mazemakers), who created colossal mazes of carved stone on the islands and on mainland Essos to the south. After the mazemakers died out, they were followed first by a race of [hairy men](wiki:Hairy_men), similar to the [Ibbenese](wiki:Ibben), and then by the [Andals](wiki:Andals), with each island having its own king. The largest of the islands even had four kings.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

For the next thousand years, the Andals fought among each other, until the Andal warrior [Qarlon the Great](wiki:Qarlon) united the Lorathi isles under his rule. On [Lorassyon](wiki:Lorassyon), in the center of the vast haunted maze, he raised a great wooden keep decorated with the heads of those foes he had slain. Qarlon wanted to become king of all Andals, and he conquered petty kingdoms over twenty years. [Norvos](wiki:Norvos) eventually closed the rivers against him, causing Qarlon to march against the city. Norvos called to the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) for help, and as Qarlon laid siege to Norvos, the [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) came to the aid of their daughter. Qarlon and his army were burned, and the dragonlords flew to Lorath where the city was destroyed in the [Scouring of Lorath](wiki:Scouring_of_Lorath). It is said that no one survived.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

For more than a century, the Lorathi isles remained uninhabited. It was said that the islands were haunted, and the whalers from [Ib](wiki:Ib) who went ashore to find freshwater did not dare to venture inland.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

### Valyrian Colony

In [1,436 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest), [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) worshippers of the Blind God [Boash](wiki:Boash) travelled to Lorath and founded a temple on the main isle, settling in the ancient mazes of the first Lorathi, turning them into their towns, temples, and tombs. For three-quarters of a century, they dominated the islands. However, more and more people who did not share their faith travelled to Lorath and built villages. In time, the islands became a refuge for [freedmen](wiki:Freedmen) and escaped [slaves](wiki:Slave), as the priests of Boash taught that every man was equal. Three of such fishing villages on the western part of the largest isle grew so populous and prosperous that they merged into a town. In time, the town became a city.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

The numbers of newcomers swelled, while the numbers of the followers of Boash dwindled. As the worship fell away, the remaining priests became more corrupt. Eventually, the colonists rose in rebellion. The acolytes of Boash who remained were killed, though several managed to flee to the great temple maze on [Lorassyon](wiki:Lorassyon), where they remained until the last of them died, the best part of a century later.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13) The Cult of Boash died out roughly around 700 BC.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14)

Afterward, Lorath became a freehold owing allegiance to the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold), ruled by a council of [three princes](wiki:Princes_of_Lorath): the Harvest Prince, the Fisher Prince, and the Prince of the Streets. The princes continue to sit today, though their titles are purely ceremonial. The true authority now lies in a council of [magisters](wiki:Magister), made up of nobles, priests, and merchants.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15)

### Recent History

The relative isolation of Lorath led it to be little involved in the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood) after the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), save for those Lorathi who sold their swords to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) and [Norvos](wiki:Norvos).(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16)

[Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) visited Lorath and the [Port of Ibben](wiki:Port_of_Ibben) during a voyage on *[Ice Wolf](wiki:Ice_Wolf)*.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Lorath's trade was affected when the [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy) collapsed and the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War) broke out in the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). Lorath allied with Braavos and [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) during the war,(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) but the Lorathi eventually withdrew.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

## Appearances & Description

### Location

Lorath is on the west side of the largest of three islands surrounded by stormy seas.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) It is at the mouth of [Lorath Bay](wiki:Lorath_Bay) and, except [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), is the northernmost of the Free Cities. The second-largest island is [Lorassyon](wiki:Lorassyon), but there are a score of smaller isles.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

To the south of the isles is a thickly forested peninsula. The Lorathi also claim dominion over the waters of Lorath Bay, but fishing fleets from Braavos and whalers and sealers out of [Ib](wiki:Ib) often venture into the bay, for Lorath does not have sufficient strength to make good its claim.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) To the east is the [Axe](wiki:Axe) and the west [Braavos](wiki:Braavos).(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-3) [Morosh](wiki:Morosh) at the [Sarne](wiki:Sarne) delta is a Lorathi colony.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rawoiafmorosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

The Lorathi isles are bleak and stony, but in their waters plenty of [cod](wiki:Cod), whales, and grey [leviathans](wiki:Leviathan) can be found, and colonies of [walrus](wiki:Walrus) and [seal](wiki:Seal).(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

### People

The followers of [Boash](wiki:Boash) did not eat flesh, nor drink [wine](wiki:Wine). They went about barefoot, dressing in [hair shirts](wiki:Hair_shirt) and hides, and their priests were [eunuchs](wiki:Eunuchs) who wore eyeless hoods. The cult of Boash believed in extreme self-abnegation, and because all humans were equal before their [god](wiki:Religion), they considered women to be equal to men in all matters, nor did they practice [slavery](wiki:Slavery). This denial of the self extended to the point that adherents came to refer to themselves and others using indefinite pronouns; they did not use names, and referred to themselves as "a man" and "a woman", instead of saying "I", "me", or "mine". While the cult of Boash has long gone extinct, certain of these speaking habits are still used in Lorath today. Nobles regard it as vulgar to speak of one's self directly.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Lorath used to be ruled by [three princes](wiki:Princes_of_Lorath): the Harvest Prince, the Fisher Prince, and the Prince of the Streets. These princes, once chosen, served for life. All are chosen by vote: the Harvest Prince by those who own land upon the islands, the Fisher Prince by all who own ships, and the Prince of the Streets by the acclamation of the free men of the city. While the council of princes still exists today, their titles have become ceremonial. The actual rule of Lorath now resides with a council of [magisters](wiki:Magister) made up of nobles, priests, and merchants.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

Like the tongues of all [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), the [Lorathi language](wiki:Lorathi_language) is a form of [bastard Valyrian](wiki:Valyrian_languages).(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

### Economy

Salt [cod](wiki:Cod), [walrus](wiki:Walrus) tusks, [sealskins](wiki:Seal), and whale oil form the greater part of Lorath's trade.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

Though the Lorathi possess a large fleet of fishing vessels, they have little military power, and build few [warships](wiki:Watercraft). Not many Lorathi ever leave the islands. Even fewer Lorathi make their way to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). Instead, Lorath prefers to trade with their near neighbors, [Norvos](wiki:Norvos), [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), and [Ib](wiki:Ib).(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

Lorath is accounted as the poorest of the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), and the most isolated and backward.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) believes the city to be insignificant economically.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### A Clash of Kings

[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) meets [Jaqen H'ghar](wiki:Jaqen_H'ghar), who claims to be Lorathi. He dyes one half of his hair red and the other white,(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Racok5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) and he uses the speech pattern of Lorathi.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Racok5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) Amid the [fall of Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal), Arya learns that "Jaqen" is actually a [Faceless Man](wiki:Faceless_Man) of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos).(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

In Braavos, Arya observes a fat Lorathi man at [Pynto's](wiki:Pynto's) complaining about the size of his booth.(wiki:Lorath.cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
