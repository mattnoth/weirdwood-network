## Origins

### Founding

Before [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), the area around the future King's Landing was just hills and forests, claimed by both the [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King), [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), and the [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), [Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare) of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) During the days of the [Hundred Kingdoms](wiki:Hundred_Kingdoms), the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) had been claimed by many petty kings, including the [Darklyns](wiki:House_Darklyn) of [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), the [Masseys](wiki:House_Massey) of [Stonedance](wiki:Stonedance), and various [river kings](wiki:River_kings) including [Blackwoods](wiki:House_Blackwood), [Brackens](wiki:House_Bracken), [Fishers](wiki:House_Fisher), [Hooks](wiki:House_Hook), and [Mudds](wiki:House_Mudd). Towers and forts had crowned the three hills multiple times, but all had been destroyed during various wars. Only broken stones and overgrown ruins remained when Lord [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) led his army from [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) and landed on the north bank of the Blackwater Rush in [2 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-1)(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-1), where a handful of fisherfolk lived in a small village.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-4) Aegon quickly built his first fortress of wood and earth, the [Aegonfort](wiki:Aegonfort),(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rawoiafking.27s_landing.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) on top of the [highest hill](wiki:Aegon's_High_Hill).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-2)(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-2)

When Aegon finalized his conquest and was crowned [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms), many expected that he would make [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) or [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) his capital. He unexpectedly decided to build his capital in the new town growing around the Aegonfort,(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-3)(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-3) since it was more centrally located than any other and not part of the former kingdoms, setting the king apart from his new [vassals](wiki:Feudalism).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-5) Aegon foresaw that King's Landing, which became a city in [10 AC](wiki:10_AC), would surpass Oldtown or [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport). Trade shifted from [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) and [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), and by [25 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) King's Landing had become the realm's third-largest city, ahead of [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) and [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown). The following year Grand Maester [Gawen](wiki:Gawen) and Ser [Osmund Strong](wiki:Osmund_Strong) completed the city walls and seven gates.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf41.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

### Maegor the Cruel

The city was expanded by Aegon's successors. The [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), which replaced the Aegonfort, was completed during the reign of [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). During the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) and [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) seized the [Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Sept_of_Remembrance) and the Red Keep, which was then under construction. Maegor the Cruel used [Balerion](wiki:Balerion)'s [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire) to destroy the sept, and began construction of the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) atop its ruins.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf43.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

### Jaehaerys the Conciliator

During the early reign of [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), the city hosted the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding), an event unlike any seen in the history of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) that saw the city prosper.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfab6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) When Jaehaerys came of age, great work was done to improve the city of King's Landing. Work on the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) was completed in [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). A massive system of drains and sewers was constructed to carry the waste from the city. The muddied streets were widened and eventually paved with cobblestone. The [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Septon Barth pointed out that the river water which was the only source of water for the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) was fit for only swine and horses. So wells were sunk in the city and other wells were dug outside the city, a series of glazed pipes and tunnels would carry fresh water into the city, where it would be stored in four huge cisterns and made available at fountains and public squares to provide clean drinking water for the smallfolk. The construction of the fountains was approved thanks to the actions of good Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), so they became known as "the queen's fountains".(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf44.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) [A tourney](wiki:Tourney_at_King's_Landing_on_the_anniversary_of_the_king's_coronation) to celebrate the king's first decade of rule was held in [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)

In the winter of [59 AC](wiki:59_AC) a plague known as the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) spread throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), King's Landing was hit hardest of all. Thousands died, including half the [small council](wiki:Small_council), two of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) and Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Jaehaerys_I)). The [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) succumbed to the disease as well, the streets became lawless and unsafe during the plague. The [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), [Rego Draz](wiki:Rego_Draz) was murdered by a starving mob who blamed him for the price of bread and for being a foreigner.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

The city later became connected with other regions of the realm with a network of roads the king ordered constructed such as the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad), [Goldroad](wiki:Goldroad), and [roseroad](wiki:Roseroad). In [98 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) King's Landing held a great tourney to celebrate the fiftieth year of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen)'s reign.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf44.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-1)

### Viserys the First

The [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) became a place of song and splendor during the prosperous reign of [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen). The young kings generosity was legendary and King's Landing flourished as Viserys hosted countless balls, feasts and [tournaments](wiki:Tourney) within the city.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

### Dance of the Dragons

At the beginning of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), King's Landing was held by King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), but in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC) the [city fell](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) to Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and her [blacks](wiki:Blacks). Later in the same year, [riots](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(the_Dance_of_the_Dragons)) broke out against the rule of Rhaenyra. These culminated in the mass [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit), the deaths of five Targaryen dragons and thousands of smallfolk, and the ruination of the Dragonpit.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) Rhaenyra abandoned the city, which fell into chaos during the [Moon of the Three Kings](wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings), and met her doom at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). The civil war ended with [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) succeeding Aegon II, and Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) briefly held court during the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf46.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf47.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0) The [wedding of Aegon III Targaryen and Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen) was held atop [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0)

### Reign of Aegon III

During the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister) ordered all the city gates to be strengthened so they could better resist attacks from within the city walls. King's Landing, like the rest of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), fell prey to the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) in early [133 AC](wiki:133_AC).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0)

### Baelor the Blessed

King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen), brought hostages to King's Landing after his [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne) and the [Submission of Sunspear](wiki:Submission_of_Sunspear).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf48.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) Daeron, the Young Dragon, was killed trying to subdue Dornish rebellions, however. His successor, King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen), pardoned the hostages and walked barefoot to [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear). After recovering from his journey, Baelor the Blessed attempted to outlaw [prostitution](wiki:Prostitution) in the capital and began construction of the [Great Sept](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), which was completed after his death.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf49.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0)

### First Blackfyre Rebellion

During the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [196 AC](wiki:196_AC), the army of [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), the bastard half-brother of King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), threatened King's Landing, but they were stopped at the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### Great Spring Sickness

When the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness) engulfed [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), the worst effects were in King's Landing, where four in ten inhabitants were killed by the outbreak. The Hand of the King, Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), ordered funeral pyres for the victims to curtail the disease. Corpses were piled in the ruins of the Dragonpit until they reached ten feet high, and the pyres burned nonstop, painting the night sky in the unnatural green of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) In the end, Lord Rivers had the [pyromancers](wiki:Pyromancers) burn the corpses where they laid, and a quarter of the city burned to the ground.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0)

### Aegon the Unlikely

After the death of [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) in [233 AC](wiki:233_AC), a [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) was held in King's Landing to choose the new king. [Aenys Blackfyre](wiki:Aenys_Blackfyre) was given safe conduct to attend the council, but after the [pretender](wiki:Blackfyre_Pretenders) arrived, Lord Brynden Rivers had him arrested and executed. The assembled lords chose [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf414.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0)

[Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) was knighted for his deeds during the [winter tourney at King's Landing](wiki:Winter_tourney_at_King's_Landing).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0)

### The Mad King

In [265 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), disgusted with King's Landing, considered building a new marble city on the southern shore of the Blackwater Rush. The Mad King largely remained within the Red Keep after the [Defiance of Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0) Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), hosted the [Anniversary Tourney](wiki:Anniversary_Tourney) for Aerys in King's Landing.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-1)

During [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), also called the War of the Usurper, Aerys sent Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen)—pregnant with Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen)—with Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) after King's Landing was informed of Prince [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen)'s death at the [Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) advised Aerys to open the gates to the king's former Hand, Lord Tywin, but the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) host instead began the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0)(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) The Mad King ordered his [pyromancers](wiki:Pyromancers) to destroy the city with caches of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire), but Tywin's son, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) of the Kingsguard, stopped the [wildfire plot](wiki:Wildfire_plot) and killed Aerys.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-1) [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) became the new king on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), forming [House Baratheon of King's Landing](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0)

## Appearances & Description

King's Landing is roughly square-shaped, sprawling across several miles and defended by tall walls. It is dotted with manses, arbors, granaries, brick storehouses, timbered inns, merchant stalls, taverns, graveyards and brothels. There is a [fish market](wiki:Fish_market). Hundreds of quays can be found in [the harbor](wiki:Harbor_of_King's_Landing). Between buildings the roads are broad, lined with trees and branching alleys and streets.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

The city covers the north shore of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) and covers three tall hills, named after [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and his two sisters, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) and [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen). [Aegon's High Hill](wiki:Aegon's_High_Hill), surmounted by the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), the royal castle located in the south-eastern corner of the city, overlooks the bay directly, whilst [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill) to the west is crowned by the [marble](wiki:Marble)-walled [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) and its seven crystal towers. The [Hill of Rhaenys](wiki:Hill_of_Rhaenys) in the north is capped by the collapsed ruins of the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) dome, its bronze doors shut for a century and a half since the last dragon died.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

Behind the quays outside of the walls, there tend to be ramshackle buildings which extend to the walls. These include bait shacks, pot shops, warehouses, merchant's stalls, alehouses, and [prostitution](wiki:Prostitution) rooms.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Generally, the areas on the higher elevations of the three hills tend to be the wealthier parts of the city, while the poorer areas are the low places in between them.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rfab3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) The worst slum in the city is [Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom), a maze of narrow streets and alleys at the bottom of the east side of Rhaenys's Hill (the side facing Aegon's High Hill). There the poorest [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) eke out a meager existence, subsisting largely on "[bowls of brown](wiki:Bowl_of_brown)", a mystery stew that can include the meat of rats and murder victims, among other things. In contrast, the top of Rhaenys's Hill is the location of the Street of Silk, filled with expensive high-end brothels catering to lords and rich merchants.

According to semi-canon sources the richer areas of King's Landing lie near the Old Gate.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rrpgguardiansorder.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

The [tourney grounds](wiki:Tourney_grounds_(King's_Landing)) outside the city are near the Blackwater(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) and the [King's Gate](wiki:King's_Gate).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtloiafking.27s_landing.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### City gates

Seven is a sacred number for the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), thus the reason [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) had seven huge gates built as the entrances into the capital of King's Landing.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf41.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Each is protected by portcullis, heavy doors, and armed guard.

- [Dragon Gate](wiki:Dragon_Gate), leads north to the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad).
- [Iron Gate](wiki:Iron_Gate), leads to the [Rosby road](wiki:Rosby_road).
- [Old Gate](wiki:Old_Gate).
- [Gate of the Gods](wiki:Gate_of_the_Gods).
- [River Gate](wiki:River_Gate), known as the Mud Gate by the population of the city, leads south to the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) and the [roseroad](wiki:Roseroad).
- [Lion Gate](wiki:Lion_Gate), leads west to the [Goldroad](wiki:Goldroad).
- [King's Gate](wiki:King's_Gate), outside of which are [tourney grounds](wiki:Tourney_grounds_(King's_Landing)).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rtloiafking.27s_landing.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

### Notable locations

- The [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), the royal castle located on top of Aegon's High Hill. Within the castle are [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) and the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand).
- The [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), where the [Most Devout](wiki:Most_Devout) convene with the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon). It is the holiest sept of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). It is located on Visenya's Hill.
- The [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit), a huge dome, now collapsed, that used to hold the Targaryen dragons. ## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Following the sudden death of Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) travels to King's Landing to become the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). Robert's brother, Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), leaves the capital to return to his seat of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0) The [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney) is held at the [tourney grounds](wiki:Tourney_grounds_(King's_Landing)) outside the city walls to honor Ned Stark, who does not approve of the extravagance.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-1)

Ned investigates Jon Arryn's death and learns that Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s children were fathered not by Robert, but by her twin brother, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-1) When Robert is mortally wounded while hunting in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), his youngest brother, [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), flees the city with Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0) Following Robert's death, Cersei has Ned imprisoned for plotting against Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), now Robert's successor as king.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-1)(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0)

To the surprise of his [small council](wiki:Small_council), Joffrey orders Ned's execution at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) rather than allow him to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3) [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) is held captive by the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister),(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1) but [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) escapes the city with the [wandering crow](wiki:Wandering_crow) [Yoren](wiki:Yoren).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-4) Because Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) is campaigning against [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), he sends [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) to act as Hand to Joffrey.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0)

### A Clash of Kings

King's Landing hosts a [tourney on King Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_King_Joffrey's_name_day).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-0) During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), the capital is cut off from trade and food supplies by the blockade of Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell), a supporter of Renly,(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-0)(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-0) as well as by fighting in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1) This leads to famine and a [massive riot](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing) in which the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(fat_one)) is killed.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-5) [Lollys Stokeworth](wiki:Lollys_Stokeworth) is found wandering Sowbelly Row after she had been raped half a hundred times behind a tanner's shop.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-6) The [bloody flux](wiki:Bloody_flux) spreads through the stews of Pisswater Bend.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-7)

Just within the [Mud Gate](wiki:Mud_Gate) lie the three great trebuchets called the [Three Whores](wiki:Three_Whores), a new addition to the defense of the city.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

The city is besieged by Stannis during the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater),(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-0) but Joffrey's reign is secured by the timely arrival of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) and his new ally, Mace.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0)

### A Storm of Swords

The armies of Lords Tywin and Mace encamp at King's Landing, and other nobles arrive for the upcoming nuptials of King Joffrey I.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Hundreds of wayns bring food from [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) in the name of Mace's daughter, Lady [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) The city's master [armorer](wiki:Armorer), [Tobho Mott](wiki:Tobho_Mott), smiths two [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) swords from Ned Stark's [Ice](wiki:Ice) for Lord Tywin.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Tyrion welcomes a delegation from [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), and he tells Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) that there are half a million people in the stinking city, including tens of thousands of soldiers from various hosts.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

Joffrey weds Margaery at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), but he [dies during the reception](wiki:Purple_Wedding) at the Red Keep.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-1) He is succeeded by his younger brother, [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon). Sansa flees the capital with the assistance of Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-0) Tyrion is condemned to death after Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) defeats Oberyn in a [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle),(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-0) but he escapes King's Landing with the assistance of Jaime and Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_80-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Queen Cersei serves as the regent for young King Tommen. With Tyrion and Varys missing after the death of Tywin, Cersei uses [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) to burn the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-0) Pious [sparrows](wiki:Sparrows) travel to the capital in support of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-0)

Amidst growing unrest after the arrest of Queen Margaery by the Faith, Cersei allows Lord [Aurane Waters](wiki:Aurane_Waters), the master of ships, to launch [new dromonds](wiki:Cersei's_dromonds) in a show of force. However, Aurane sails from King's Landing when Cersei is herself arrested.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Traveling in [Essos](wiki:Essos), Tyrion is told that the [pisswater prince](wiki:Pisswater_prince) came from Pisswater Bend.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1)

As atonement for her confessed sins, Cersei [walks](wiki:Walk_of_atonement) from the Great Sept of Baelor to the Red Keep amidst the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) of King's Landing.(wiki:King's_Landing.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_84-0)
