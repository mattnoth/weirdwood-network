## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

The Skirling Pass is considered by the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) as a probable place to find [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder)'s [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings).(wiki:Skirling_Pass.cite_ref-Racok34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) [Qhorin Halfhand](wiki:Qhorin_Halfhand)'s group is sent to scout it for signs of wildlings.(wiki:Skirling_Pass.cite_ref-Racok53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) [Stonesnake](wiki:Stonesnake) and [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) scale the Skirling Pass at night to ambush a trio of wildling watchers, where they kill two of the sentries.(wiki:Skirling_Pass.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Jon captures [Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte), but releases the wildling rather than execute her.(wiki:Skirling_Pass.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
While retreating back to the [Fist of the First Men](wiki:Fist_of_the_First_Men), the scouts are overtaken by [Rattleshirt](wiki:Rattleshirt)'s wildlings, with Jon the only confirmed survivor while Stonesnake is ordered by Qhorin to climb through the [Frostfangs](wiki:Frostfangs) on foot to warn the Night's Watch of the wildling invasion.(wiki:Skirling_Pass.cite_ref-Racok68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Stonesnake is lost in the Skirling Pass.(wiki:Skirling_Pass.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Several wildlings attempt to follow the ranger up the mountain, but cease pursuit once the terrain becomes too rough for them and their horses to follow him.(wiki:Skirling_Pass.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

There were wonders here as well. He had seen sunlight flashing on icy thin waterfalls as they plunged over the lips of sheer stone cliffs, and a mountain meadow full of autumn wildflowers, blue [coldsnaps](wiki:Coldsnaps) and bright scarlet [frostfires](wiki:Frostfires) and stands of [piper's grass](wiki:Piper's_grass) in russet and gold. He had peered down ravines so deep and black they seemed certain to end in some hell, and he had ridden his garron over a wind-eaten bridge of natural stone with nothing but sky to either side. [Eagles](wiki:Eagle) nested in the heights and came down to hunt the valleys, circling effortlessly on great blue-grey wings that seemed almost part of the sky. Once he had watched a [shadowcat](wiki:Shadowcat) stalk a ram, flowing down the mountainside like liquid smoke until it was ready to pounce.(wiki:Skirling_Pass.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)
