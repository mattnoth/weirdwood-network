## Origins

According to Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), it has been "a thousand years" since Meereen last had [a king](wiki:King_of_Meereen).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-date_27-0)

Meereen was a colony of the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis). Cedars once grew tall along the coast but grow no more; they were felled by the axes of the Old Empire or consumed by [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire) when Ghis made war against [Valyria](wiki:Valyria). Once the trees had gone, the soil baked beneath the hot sun and blew away in thick red clouds. According to [Galazza Galare](wiki:Galazza_Galare), it was these calamities that transformed her people into slavers.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-7) Meereen survived the destruction of [Old Ghis](wiki:Old_Ghis) at the hands of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) and was subsequently was conquered by the Valyrians, who learned [slavery](wiki:Slavery) from the conquered [Ghiscari](wiki:Ghiscari) and used slaves to expand their empire.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

The modern Ghiscari in Meereen now speak a blend of the High Valyrian of their conquerors and Old Ghiscari.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-16)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3) Following the fall of the Freehold during the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), the Ghiscari of [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) took control of the slave trade.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)

The slave [Belwas](wiki:Belwas) was bred and trained in the fighting pits of Meereen.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) From Meereen, he was eventually sold to [Qohor](wiki:Qohor).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

## Appearances & Description

### Location

> Meereen was as large as Astapor and Yunkai combined. Like her sister cities she was built of brick, but where Astapor had been red and Yunkai yellow, Meereen was made with bricks of many colors. Her walls were higher than Yunkai's and in better repair, studded with bastions and anchored by great defensive towers at every angle. Behind them, huge against the sky, could be seen the top of the Great Pyramid, a monstrous thing eight hundred feet tall with a towering bronze harpy at its top.

— Daenerys first sees Meereen from the siege lines, ASOS Daenerys V (`sources/chapters/asos/asos-daenerys-05.md:11`)

Meereen, the northernmost of the [slaver cities](wiki:Slaver_cities) of [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay), is located on the northeast coast at the mouth of the [Skahazadhan](wiki:Skahazadhan).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-3) The [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea) lies to the north, beyond the river. To reach [Lhazar](wiki:Lhazar) from Meereen one must pass through the [Khyzai Pass](wiki:Khyzai_Pass).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) By the old Ghiscari coastal road, [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) is somewhat more than fifty leagues to the south, and Astapor another hundred more; in total, the distance between Meereen and [Astapor](wiki:Astapor) is between a hundred-and-fifty and two hundred leagues.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-7)

Meereen’s hinterlands consist of all the land west of Lhazar, which includes hills and a range of rounded sandstone mountains,(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) as far south as the Yunkish hills.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### Layout

Meereen is the largest(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) and most formidable of the cities along [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay),(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) being as large as [Astapor](wiki:Astapor) and [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) combined. Like them, the city is made of brick, but unlike Astapor and Yunkai, Meereen's bricks are of many different colors.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Within the city there are narrow twisty alleys and wide brick streets, temples, granaries, hovels, palaces, brothels, baths, gardens, fountains, and fighting pits.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

#### Defenses

Meereen's walls are higher than Yunkai's and Astapor's,(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) thicker than Astapor's(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) and better maintained than Yunkai's(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5), studded with bastions and anchored by great defensive towers at every angle.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) The city's north wall runs along the bank of the [Skahazadhan](wiki:Skahazadhan), its west along the bay shore.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) The river wall is a few feet higher than the others.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) Upon the walls, rows of [bronze](wiki:Bronze) harpy heads with open mouths are positioned, from which the Meereenese can squirt boiling oil out upon any attacker below.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

The city's wastes are carried into the Skahazadhan by great brick sewers. These sewers are closed off with iron gates, although some have rusted through.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) The Meereenese do not drink the water from the river, drinking instead water gathered from wells.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

#### Pyramids

The nobility of Meereen lives in the stepped [pyramids](wiki:Pyramid). The greatest of them all is the [Great Pyramid](wiki:Great_Pyramid), located on Meereen's central [plaza](wiki:Plaza),(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) which stands eight hundred feet tall.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12) There are a score of lesser pyramids in the city, but none stand even half as high.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) The Great Pyramid has a great bronze harpy on top of its apex.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

The pyramids are built in the colors of the noble house occupying them: the [pyramid of Pahl](wiki:Pyramid_of_Pahl) is pink-and-white, the [pyramid of Naqqan](wiki:Pyramid_of_Naqqan) is green-and-black,(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) and the great [pyramid of Rhazdar](wiki:Pyramid_of_Rhazdar) is yellow-and-green.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) The [pyramid of Kandaq](wiki:Pyramid_of_Kandaq) is said to be modest.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Poorer districts are located between the pyramids.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

#### Fighting pits

Meereen has multiple [fighting pits](wiki:Fighting_pits_of_Meereen) scattered across the city.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) These include [Daznak's Pit](wiki:Daznak's_Pit), the [Pit of Ghrazz](wiki:Pit_of_Ghrazz), and the [Golden Pit](wiki:Golden_Pit).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Daznak's Pit, the largest of all,(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) is located west from the Great Pyramid.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)

#### Markets

Meereen has several markets,(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) including a spice market where the [Purple Lotus](wiki:Purple_Lotus) is located, near the pyramid of Rhazdar.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

A market square inside the great western gate houses Meereen's ancient Slave Exchange, the merchant's arcade that fronts on the southern side of the square, and the weathered bronze statue of the [Chainmaker](wiki:Chainmaker). Not far from the statue is the ghastly monument the Great Masters call the [Spire of Skulls](wiki:Spire_of_Skulls).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Barristan_16-0)

#### Other locations

The [Temple of the Graces](wiki:Temple_of_the_Graces), home to the priestesses of the city, is located west of the Great Pyramid. It is a huge structure topped with golden domes.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

A great bronze harpy can be found in the [Plaza of Purification](wiki:Plaza_of_Purification).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

Another location in Meereen is [Mazdhan's Maze](wiki:Mazdhan's_Maze).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

### Nature

Olives grow along the shores of [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Tall cedars once grew along the coast, but were either cut down the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis) or burned by [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire) during the wars against the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) On the terraces of Meereen's pyramids grow night-blooming flowers, lemon trees, and persimmon trees.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Multiple farmer's fields and wells can be found near the city. Numerous plants, including [waspwillow](wiki:Waspwillow), dusky roses, wild mint, lady's lace, [daggerleaf](wiki:Daggerleaf), broom, [prickly ben](wiki:Prickly_ben), and [harpy's gold](wiki:Harpy's_gold) can be found beside the coastal road leading from Yunkai to Meereen.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14)

### People

Meereen's population is a fraction of what it was at the height of the power of the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) The Meereenese are a sly and stubborn people, and [their language](wiki:Meereenese_language) is a bastard form of [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian) blended with [Old Ghiscari](wiki:Old_Ghiscari).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) They use the styles of "Your Worship",(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) "Magnificence",(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) and "His Radiance"(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) to address their royalty.

#### Nobility

Meereen is ruled by the [Great Masters](wiki:Great_Masters), members of the old slaving families(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5) who reside in formidable pyramids.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) These families include the Houses of [Dhazak](wiki:House_of_Dhazak), [Galare](wiki:House_of_Galare), [Ghazeen](wiki:House_of_Ghazeen), [Hazkar](wiki:House_of_Hazkar), [Kandaq](wiki:House_of_Kandaq), [Loraq](wiki:House_of_Loraq), [Merreq](wiki:House_of_Merreq), [Naqqan](wiki:House_of_Naqqan), [Pahl](wiki:House_of_Pahl), [Quazzar](wiki:House_of_Quazzar), [Reznak](wiki:House_of_Reznak), [Rhazdar](wiki:House_of_Rhazdar), [Uhlez](wiki:House_of_Uhlez), [Yherizan](wiki:House_of_Yherizan), and [Zhak](wiki:House_of_Zhak).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) The richest man of Meereen is from the House of Pahl, and another Pahl commands Meereen's city guard.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16) Some nobles, like Zhak and Merreq, have estates in the hills.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)

Meereenese nobility wear the Ghiscari *[tokar](wiki:Tokar)*, a sign of wealth and power.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7) Some old lines might prefer *tokars* of a specific color. For example, family members of the ancient line of Loraq favor *tokars* of purple, indigo, and lilac, and family members of the ancient line of [Pahl](wiki:House_of_Pahl) *tokars* of pink and white, the latter similar to the color of their pyramid.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5) Women might [paint their nails](wiki:Cosmetics),(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-6) while the men, like other Ghiscari men, tease their hair into horns, spikes, and wings using combs, [wax](wiki:Wax) and [irons](wiki:Iron).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Meereenese seldom ride within the city, preferring to use [palanquins](wiki:Palanquin), litters, and sedan chairs instead, carried by their slaves.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) Ghiscari inter their honored dead in crypts below their manses.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6) The bones of the deceased might otherwise be laid to rest in the Temple of the Graces, where men might light candles in their memory.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3)

#### Graces

The priestesses of Meereen are known as [Graces](wiki:Graces). The color of their robes indicates role they fulfill. There are green, white, pink, red, blue, gold, and purple graces.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2) [White Graces](wiki:White_Graces) are girls of noble birth who are still too young to serve in the temple's pleasure gardens.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) The [Red Graces](wiki:Red_Graces) serve in the gardens, where they wait every night until a man chooses them; should they not be chosen, they must remain until the sun comes up.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) The [Blue Graces](wiki:Blue_Graces) take care of the sick.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3)

### Customs

The Ghiscari love a course of [dog](wiki:Dog), and have multiple different ways to prepare it.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

#### Fighting pits

Combat which is profoundly religious in nature takes place in the [fighting pits of Meereen](wiki:Fighting_pits_of_Meereen). They are seen as a blood sacrifice to the gods of Ghis, as well as a display of courage, skill, and strength most pleasing to the gods. Victorious fighters are pampered and acclaimed, and the slain are honored and remembered, their names graven on the Gates of Fate amongst the other valiant fallen.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-4) Additionally, criminals can be condemned to die in the pits.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-7) For them, the pits represent a [judgment by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle), which gives them a last chance to prove their innocence.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-10)

Within the fighting pits, contests are held between men, women, children, and wild animals. These are alternated by follies. All animals killed during the contests and follies in the pits are made into a healthful stew for the hungry, and a bowl is given to every man who presents himself at the Gates of Fate.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-8)

#### Weddings

[Ghiscari](wiki:Ghiscari) wedding [customs](wiki:Custom) are still practiced in Meereen. The female relatives of the groom examine the bride's womb and female parts, to ensure her fertility. Three [Graces](wiki:Graces) witness this ancient ritual, reciting prayers. The bride is to be stripped completely naked for this purpose. After the examinations are done, the women eat a cake baked specially for betrothals, which men are forbidden to taste.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2) Usually, the bride is to wash the groom's feet, signifying that she will be her husband's [handmaid](wiki:Handmaid).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-3) Under special circumstances, it might be the groom who washes the feet of the bride.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3)

When [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) marries [Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq), she sits in an [ivory](wiki:Ivory) chair whilst he washes her feet with water from a golden bowl, while fifty eunuchs sing to them.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-4) The bride traditionally wears a *tokar* of white silk, with dark red veils. The *tokar* is fringed with baby pearls, which symbolize fertility.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-4) Weddings of the highest nobility in Meereen takes place at the [Temple of the Graces](wiki:Temple_of_the_Graces). The ceremony might last up to four hours, and when husband and wife emerge from the temple, they are bound together wrist and ankle with chains of yellow gold.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-5)

### Economy

Meereen is a wealthy city,(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-7) deeply involved in the [slave](wiki:Slave) trade.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5) As in Yunkai, slave swordsmen in Meereen are cheaper than their swords.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Warriors are bred and trained in the fighting pits,(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-8) and young boys and girls are trained in the brothels.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) In Meereen, as in Yunkai, eunuchs are often made by removing only the testicles, leaving the penis.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

The [fighting pits of Meereen](wiki:Fighting_pits_of_Meereen) are far-famed across the world, drawing trade to the city.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-11)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2) Meereen claims one-tenth of all the profits from the fighting pits, after expenses, as a tax, through which way the pits help to fill the city's coffers.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-12)

A Meereenese coin is called an "honor".(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-13)

[Copper](wiki:Copper) is plentiful in the Ghiscari hills, and was valuable "when bronze ruled the world".(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6) The small pale yellow grapes native to the region produce a notably inferior [wine](wiki:Wine).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-14)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Some nobles have estates in the Ghiscari hills, where slaves grow wheat and olives, herd sheep and goats, and mine salt and copper.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-15)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) advises Khal [Drogo](wiki:Drogo) to drive his slaves to Meereen since it has suffered from a plague last year.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)

### A Clash of Kings

Khal [Pono](wiki:Pono)'s *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* rides to the [Slaver Cities](wiki:Slaver_Cities) to sell thousands of slaves.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) In [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) meets [Belwas](wiki:Belwas), a veteran of the [fighting pits of Meereen](wiki:Fighting_pits_of_Meereen).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1)

### A Storm of Swords

The [Great Masters](wiki:Great_Masters) prepare for Daenerys's arrival by scorching the earth around their city(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-17) and having the Meereenese fleet set sails.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-8) The masters also nail a hundred and sixty-three slave children on mileposts along the coastal road.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-18) Once Daenerys's forces have arrived, Meereen sends out their champion [Oznak zo Pahl](wiki:Oznak_zo_Pahl). Oznak is slain in single combat by Strong Belwas.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-19)

Daenerys's [siege of Meereen](wiki:Siege_of_Meereen) eventually comes to an end after she sends men through the sewers to strike the chains of the slaves within the city, while having her own forces battering down the eastern gate. The Meereenese slaves rise up, and Daenerys and her army gain access to the city, resulting in its sack.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-9)

Daenerys takes up residence in the [Great Pyramid](wiki:Great_Pyramid) of Meereen, and she orders a hundred and sixty-three of the Great Masters to be nailed on posts upon the city's central plaza to avenge the slave children.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-10) King [Cleon](wiki:Cleon) of [Astapor](wiki:Astapor) sends Lord [Ghael](wiki:Ghael) to Meereen as an envoy, offering an alliance between Astapor and Meereen. Since Astapor has not fared well since [its earlier capture](wiki:Fall_of_Astapor), Daenerys decides to remain and learn to rule in Meereen instead of spreading her rule elsewhere.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-11)

### A Dance with Dragons

Ruling as the [Queen of Meereen](wiki:Queen_of_Meereen), Daenerys abolishes slavery within the city, although the practice is continued in the hills where slaves still work on the estates of the wealthy. Instead of having slaves, former slavers hire freed slaves at meager wages.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-17) Because Daenerys has abolished the slave trade and closed the fighting pits, merchant ships rarely travel to Meereen anymore.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) [Skahaz mo Kandaq](wiki:Skahaz_mo_Kandaq) and his followers, the [shavepates](wiki:Shavepates), are strong supporters of the queen, who is also advised by the seneschal [Reznak mo Reznak](wiki:Reznak_mo_Reznak).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-18)

While several Great Masters accept the rule of Daenerys, a group of [Ghiscari](wiki:Ghiscari) noblemen, known as the [Sons of the Harpy](wiki:Sons_of_the_Harpy) and the Harpy's Sons, engage in a [shadow war](wiki:Shadow_war) against Daenerys. They slay [freedmen](wiki:Freedmen), [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied) and shavepates during the night, and leave a mark over the body of their victim: a harpy, drawn in blood.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-19) To combat this movement, Daenerys orders the creation of a new city watch, made up from the Meereenese themselves:(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5) the [Brazen Beasts](wiki:Brazen_Beasts).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-9) Additionally, Daenerys takes children from Meereen's old slaving families hostage, having them serve as [her cupbearers](wiki:Cupbearers_of_Daenerys_Targaryen).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-6)

[Lhazar](wiki:Lhazar) allies with Meereen, but [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) gathers a coalition of slaving cities against Daenerys.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-10) The [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) serving Daenerys free the slaves in the hills near Meereen.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) After the [siege of Astapor](wiki:Siege_of_Astapor), the Yunkish host marches on Meereen(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) and the city is blockaded by sea.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) Astapori refugees outside of Meereen's walls suffer from the [bloody flux](wiki:Bloody_flux).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-5)

[Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell) arrives in Meereen as part of the [secret marriage pact](wiki:Secret_marriage_pact) arranged by his father, Prince [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), but Daenerys rejects the [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) offer. The queen instead [weds Hizdahr](wiki:Wedding_of_Hizdahr_zo_Loraq_and_Daenerys_Targaryen) in an attempt to bring peace to Meereen,(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-6) and a truce is made between Meereen and Yunkai.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4)

Daenerys flees from [Daznak's Pit](wiki:Daznak's_Pit) on [Drogon](wiki:Drogon)'s back when the [fighting pits](wiki:Fighting_pits) are reopened, and the Yunkish leader [Yurkhaz zo Yunzak](wiki:Yurkhaz_zo_Yunzak) is killed in the chaos.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-9)

Quentyn is mortally burned by [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire) while trying to tame [Rhaegal](wiki:Rhaegal) and [Viserion](wiki:Viserion),(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) and the [dragons](wiki:Dragon) escape from the Great Pyramid.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), the [Queen's Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), deposes King Hizdahr with the aid of Skahaz Shavepate, and the [ruling council of Meereen](wiki:Ruling_council_of_Meereen) assumes command of the overseeing of city affairs.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) Headed by Barristan, they meet to discuss how to best deal with the Yunkish army.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

[Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) leads the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) toward Meereen on a mission to bring back Daenerys for his brother [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) joins the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons), who are part of the Yunkish coalition.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) The Yunkishmen resume the [second siege of Meereen](wiki:Second_siege_of_Meereen), using trebuchets to launch corpses into the city.(wiki:Meereen.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)
