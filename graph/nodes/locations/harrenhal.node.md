## Origins

### House Hoare

The seat of [Halleck Hoare](wiki:Halleck_Hoare), [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), was a modest tower house at [Fairmarket](wiki:Fairmarket) on the [Blue Fork](wiki:Blue_Fork).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) His son, [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare), built Harrenhal along the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye) as a monument to himself, intending it to be the greatest of all castles in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) and for it to dwarf any other.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

The construction of Harren's dream took forty years. Thousands of captives died in the quarries chained to sledges or laboring on the five huge towers. Men froze by winter and sweltered in summer. [Weirwoods](wiki:Weirwood) that had stood three thousand years were cut down to provide rafters and beams. Harren beggared the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) and the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) alike to ornament his dream.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

Upon its completion, Harren boasted that his new fortress was impregnable. However, he did not account for [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and his [dragons](wiki:Dragon) invading Westeros. On the day Harren took up residence, Aegon came ashore at what would become [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Aegon flew his dragon [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), the Black Dread, above the high walls and forbidding towers and then roasted Harren alive in the tallest of the towers, now known as [Kingspyre](wiki:Kingspyre). Harren and all his line perished in the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal). Due to the extreme heat of dragonflame, the castle took on a charred, melted appearance.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2)

Since [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), the castle has become a white elephant. It is too big to garrison effectively and too expensive to maintain.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Harren allegedly mixed human blood into the mortar for the stonework;(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) some believe that the castle is cursed and haunted due to Harren's hubris and the horrors that have occurred within the castle's walls, with the early deaths of its many rulers as proof.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-3) The curse is thought to prevent any lord from holding Harrenhal indefinitely.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

### House Qoherys

King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) granted Harrenhal to [Quenton Qoherys](wiki:Quenton_Qoherys), although Lord [Edmyn Tully](wiki:Edmyn_Tully) of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) was named [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) [House Qoherys](wiki:House_Qoherys) died out during the reign of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), however, when Lord [Gargon the Guest](wiki:Gargon_Qoherys) was slain during [Harren the Red](wiki:Harren_the_Red)'s rebellion.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

### House Harroway

Lord [Lucas Harroway](wiki:Lucas_Harroway) succeeded Gargon as Lord of Harrenhal, and [House Harroway](wiki:House_Harroway) helped to defeat Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) in the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye). The Harroways of Harrenhal and [Lord Harroway's Town](wiki:Lord_Harroway's_Town) were extinguished by King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) in [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), however.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-25) It began to be said that the castle was cursed.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

### House Towers

Ser [Walton Towers](wiki:Walton_Towers) was named the new Lord of Harrenhal after winning a melee announced by Maegor, though he died of his wounds within two weeks of the event. [Jordan Towers](wiki:Jordan_Towers) received Harrenhal, though not all of the lands formerly belonging to House Harroway, as [Lord Harroway's Town](wiki:Lord_Harroway's_Town) was granted to Lord [Alton Butterwell](wiki:Alton_Butterwell) and the rest of the Harroway holdings to Lord [Darnold Darry](wiki:Darnold_Darry).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) [House Towers](wiki:House_Towers_of_Harrenhal) became extinct when Lord [Maegor Towers](wiki:Maegor_Towers) died without heirs in [61 AC](wiki:61_AC).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

### Rhaena Targaryen

Since the sickly and impoverished Lord Maegor only utilized one of Harrenhal's five towers, the [Tower of Dread](wiki:Tower_of_Dread), King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) settled his widowed sister, Queen [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) in Harrenhal. Rhaena, who lived in the [Widow's Tower](wiki:Widow's_Tower), became friends with poor Maegor, and she took care of his household after their lord's passing. After Rhaena died at Harrenhal in [73 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the king had her remains burned and interred in the castle.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)

### House Strong

King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) granted Harrenhal to Ser [Bywin Strong](wiki:Bywin_Strong) after Rhaena's death.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2) The [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC), in which Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) was chosen as the Old King's successor, was hosted by [House Strong](wiki:House_Strong) of Harrenhal.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Lord Lyonel and his heir [Harwin](wiki:Harwin_Strong) died in a fire at Harrenhal in [120 AC](wiki:120_AC). It was blamed on the curse of Harrenhal, although non-mystical suspects included Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), Lyonel's younger son [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong), and even King Viserys I.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

Early in the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Ser [Simon Strong](wiki:Simon_Strong) surrendered the castle to Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) during his [assault on Harrenhal](wiki:Assault_on_Harrenhal). Daemon used it as a base for the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) to launch attacks in the nearby area. Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) and Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) led an army of [greens](wiki:Greens) to retake the great castle, but found it abandoned by the blacks when they arrived. Aemond killed Simon and his kin.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

After the [Battle by the Lakeshore](wiki:Battle_by_the_Lakeshore), Criston led his soldiers south along the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye). Aemond rode [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) and ravaged the riverlands, including burning all wooden structures in Harrenhal. Lady [Sabitha Frey](wiki:Sabitha_Frey), who had occupied Harrenhal in the meantime, was forced to hide in a privy to survive the [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) Daemon and Aemond later fought in the [Battle Above the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Above_the_Gods_Eye), a duel which killed both princes and their dragons, [Caraxes](wiki:Caraxes) and Vhagar, although Daemon's body was never found.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3) Lord [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong) was executed during the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf) in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC), ending House Strong.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

### Alys Rivers

[Alys Rivers](wiki:Alys_Rivers) was the so-called "witch queen" who ruled Harrenhal during the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), following the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). She claimed to Ser [Regis Groves](wiki:Regis_Groves) and Ser [Damon Darry](wiki:Damon_Darry) that she was the widow of Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), and that [their young son](wiki:Alys_Rivers's_son) was trueborn and the rightful heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Regis did not survive the encounter and Damon fled back to [Darry](wiki:Darry). An ill-fated messenger sent to Darry claimed that he had seen a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) inside Harrenhal. The regents in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) planned to gather a large army to recover Harrenhal, but their plans were interrupted by the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) of [133 AC](wiki:133_AC).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

### House Lothston

King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) gave the ruined castle to Ser [Lucas Lothston](wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena)) in [151 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) The [Bastard of Harrenhal](wiki:Bastard_of_Harrenhal) was defeated by Ser [Arlan of Pennytree](wiki:Arlan_of_Pennytree) in a melee in [193 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

[Manfred Lothston](wiki:Manfred_Lothston) was involved in the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [196 AC](wiki:196_AC),(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) and Lady [Danelle Lothston](wiki:Danelle_Lothston) helped to suppress the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion) with the might of Harrenhal in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) [House Lothston](wiki:House_Lothston) was cast down during the reign of King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) ([221](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)–[233 AC](wiki:233_AC)), however, as Danelle descended into madness.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

### House Whent

[House Whent](wiki:House_Whent), originally knights sworn to Harrenhal, became lords of the castle for their part in defeating the Lothstons.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

Lord [Walter Whent](wiki:Walter_Whent) hosted a great [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal) in [281 AC](wiki:281_AC). The famous tournament resulted in the crowning of [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), as its champion, and is also noted for the participation of the mysterious [Knight of the Laughing Tree](wiki:Knight_of_the_Laughing_Tree).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) was named to the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) of [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) during the tourney.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

Prior to the outbreak of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Rhaegar abducted [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) less than ten leagues from Harrenhal.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1) The Whents have been noted for their own tragedies.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Harrenhal](wiki:Category:Images_of_Harrenhal)*

According to a semi-canon source, Harrenhal is immensely strong.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-5) The castle has five towers of dizzying size, with equally monstrous curtain walls. The walls are incredibly thick and its rooms are built on a scale that would be more comfortable for [giants](wiki:Giants) than humans.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) thinks of the castle as [Black Harren's](wiki:Harren_Hoare) folly.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Harrenhal covers three times as much ground as [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and its buildings are so much larger that they can scarcely be compared.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) chose the castle as the location of the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC) because it was not known how many would attend, and the crown wanted space for at least five hundred lords and their entourages; more than a thousand came.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Much of Harrenhal has far gone into decay, however, and many places in the castle have not been used in decades.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### Walls

Harrenhal has massive walls of black stone.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Its gatehouse is as large as the [Great Keep of Winterfell](wiki:Great_Keep_(Winterfell)), and its stone is discolored and fissured.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) From outside the gatehouse, only the tops of the towers can be seen because of the great height of the walls.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

The main gate has walls so thick that no less than a dozen murder holes are passed before one reaches the yard on the other side.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The east gate is smaller than the main gate(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and is located near the [Tower of Ghosts](wiki:Tower_of_Ghosts).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### Towers

Like its walls, the five immense round(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) towers of Harrenhal are made of black stone.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Harrenhal's shortest tower is half again as high as the tallest one in Winterfell, yet none of the towers are proper, being bent, lumped, and cracked from the melting of the stone during the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal) by [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), the [dragon](wiki:Dragon) of King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), three centuries earlier. Their original names were lost with the death of [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare), and the towers are said to be haunted by [fiery spirits](wiki:Ghosts).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

- The [Kingspyre Tower](wiki:Kingspyre_Tower) contains the castellan's chambers. It connects to the Widow's Tower via a stone bridge.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
- The [Tower of Dread](wiki:Tower_of_Dread) is near the middle bailey and the mews.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
- The [Tower of Ghosts](wiki:Tower_of_Ghosts) is near the postern gate on the east wall and the collapsed [sept](wiki:Sept).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)
- The [Wailing Tower](wiki:Wailing_Tower) contains storerooms on the ground floor and cavernous vaults beneath.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)
- The [Widow's Tower](wiki:Widow's_Tower) connects to the Kingspyre Tower via a stone bridge.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Underneath there is a great cell that is used to keep prisoners.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

[House Whent](wiki:House_Whent) uses only the lower thirds of two of the five towers, letting the rest go to ruin. Bats infest the tops of some of the towers.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7)

### Buildings

- The [Hall of a Hundred Hearths](wiki:Hall_of_a_Hundred_Hearths) is the castle's great hall. It is the largest chamber in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), even larger than the [throne room](wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep)) of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) The hall has only thirty-three or thirty-five hearths, but is said to be able to entertain an army.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8) Its floors are smooth [slate](wiki:Slate) and there are steps to two galleries above.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Ser [Willis Wode](wiki:Willis_Wode), a landed knight sworn to Lady [Shella Whent](wiki:Shella_Whent) of Harrenhal, supports [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) when she arrests [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

When war breaks out after the death of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Shella [yields Harrenhal](wiki:Yielding_of_Harrenhal) to Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) grants the castle to [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt), Commander of the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing), in return for his service in the [arrest of Eddard Stark](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark). Many nobles at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) are angered that the lowborn Janos has been named [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) Tywin is also shocked when he hears of Janos receiving Harrenhal, and he returns to the castle from the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn) after learning of the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Tyrion, acting as [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) as Tywin is still in the field during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), revokes Janos's title and sends him to the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)

The [Mountain's men](wiki:Mountain's_men) capture [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark), [Gendry](wiki:Gendry), and [Hot Pie](wiki:Hot_Pie) and bring the youths to Harrenhal.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) Tywin's garrison include bannermen lead by Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch) and Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane), and they are bolstered by the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions). The men treat the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) of the castle cruelly. Amory's men and the Brave Companions feud, resulting in more deaths. Three dozen prisoners from the [battle on the Green Fork](wiki:Battle_on_the_Green_Fork) are kept prisoner in the [Tower of Dread](wiki:Tower_of_Dread); six [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) are ransomed, but [northern](wiki:North) prisoners remain. The wounded Lord [Medger Cerwyn](wiki:Medger_Cerwyn) dies in captivity.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-13) Arya has [Jaqen H'ghar](wiki:Jaqen_H'ghar) assassinate [Chiswyck](wiki:Chiswyck) and [Weese](wiki:Weese), and some attribute the deaths to the ghost of [Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-14)(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

Tywin departs Harrenhal, leaving Amory as castellan.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4) Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) tasks Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) and Ser [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart) with taking Harrenhal for [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), so that Tywin can be blocked by [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork), and Harrenhal.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

A new group of northern prisoners, including [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover), are brought to Harrenhal by the Brave Companions. Arya convinces Jaqen to help her free the prisoners and revolt against Amory's Lannisters. At the same time, the Brave Companions butcher the Lannister bannermen and all who are loyal to them, then hand the castle to Lord Bolton. Amory is killed within the bear pit for entertainment after the [fall of Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-7) Arya serves Roose as his cupbearer.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-5)

Following the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) awards Harrenhal to Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) for his role in securing an alliance between the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) and [Tyrells](wiki:House_Tyrell) through the engagement of Joffrey and [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell). Petyr is also named [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident), replacing [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) of Riverrun; Hoster is a supporter of [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

Harrenhal remains occupied by Roose Bolton's northmen and Ser [Aenys Frey](wiki:Aenys_Frey)'s rivermen. Roose punishes and executes some of the castle's servants for having served House Lannister.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-6) Arya practices her [water dance](wiki:Water_dance) in the [godswood of Harrenhal](wiki:Godswood_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-7) Arya thinks of herself as the ghost in Harrenhal,(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-8) but she flees with Gendry and Hot Pie from the castle when she learns Roose intends to grant it to [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat), the leader of the Brave Companions.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-8)

### A Storm of Swords

Vargo had changed his allegiance from [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) to [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) in order to receive Harrenhal from [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton). After Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) escapes [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), Vargo, fearing the vengeance of Jaime's father [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), desires to wed Lord [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark)'s daughter, [Alys](wiki:Alys_Karstark), and claim the safer and more distant [Karhold](wiki:Karhold).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) Vargo's [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions) capture Jaime near [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool). Fearing that Roose will return Jaime to Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) at Riverrun or even to Tywin in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Vargo has [Zollo](wiki:Zollo) maim Jaime by cutting off the knight's sword hand.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) Lord Karstark is eventually executed by Robb Stark for his own treason.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

The Bloody Mummers bring Jaime and [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) to Harrenhal,(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) and within the castle's bathhouse Jaime reveals the [wildfire plot](wiki:Wildfire_plot) to Brienne.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3) While departing Harrenhal for Edmure's wedding at the [Twins](wiki:Twins), Lord Bolton sends [Steelshanks Walton](wiki:Steelshanks_Walton) to escort Jaime to King's Landing. Brienne remains in Vargo's control.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-4) After a dream during his journey to the capital, however, Jaime insists on returning to Harrenhal, arriving in time to rescue Brienne from a bear in the castle's bear pit. Jaime brings Brienne with him to safety in King's Landing.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)

Following Robb's death in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), Tywin Lannister, the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) for [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), intends to be lenient toward any opponent who surrenders, with the exception of Vargo at Harrenhal.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) Most Brave Companions flee the castle.(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) After the [capture of Harrenhal](wiki:Capture_of_Harrenhal),(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) Gregor departs to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to participate in the [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) of [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos74.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

Though he has never set foot within Harrenhal's walls, Petyr Baelish remains its lord. The castle's large dominion elevates Petyr's social position enough to marry [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), the widowed-Lady Regent of the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn).(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)(wiki:Harrenhal.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)
