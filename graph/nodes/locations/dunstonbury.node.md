## Origins

Dunstonbury was once a castle of [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) when they lived along the [Mander](wiki:Mander) in the [Kingdom of the Reach](wiki:Kingdom_of_the_Reach). When the Manderlys fled the Reach a thousand years before the [War of Conquest](wiki:War_of_Conquest), Dunstonbury passed to their rivals, [House Peake](wiki:House_Peake). Exiled in the [North](wiki:North), the Manderlys built the [New Keep](wiki:New_Castle) at [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) to reflect Dunstonbury.(wiki:Dunstonbury.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

For siding against King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) in the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), the Peakes were stripped of two of their three castles,(wiki:Dunstonbury.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Dunstonbury and [Whitegrove](wiki:Whitegrove), leaving them only [Starpike](wiki:Starpike).(wiki:Dunstonbury.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) It is unknown to which houses the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) granted the stripped castles.
