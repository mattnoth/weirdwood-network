## Origins

### Origin

The mighty fortress called Storm's End has stood for centuries, facing the terrible weather of [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay) with little wear to show for it. Legend claims that it was built by [Durran Godsgrief](wiki:Durran_Godsgrief), the first [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King) during the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age), who declared a war against the [sea god](wiki:Sea_god) and the [goddess of the wind](wiki:Goddess_of_the_wind) after they killed his family and guests, ruining his wedding to their daughter, [Elenei](wiki:Elenei). It was said Durran raised six castles, each larger and more formidable than the last, but all were destroyed by storms. The massive seventh castle, Storm's End, is also called Durran's Defiance(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) because it was able to endure the storms.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf786_1-2) Some believe this was because the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) took a hand in its construction, using their magics in raising the castle walls which allow it to resist the storms. Others believe that a young boy who grew up to be [Bran the Builder](wiki:Bran_the_Builder) advised Durran on its construction.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

The [maesters](wiki:Maesters) serving at Storm's End have recorded fierce storms nearly every year especially in [autumn](wiki:Autumn). The storms of [166 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and [221 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) were particularly powerful.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf786_1-3)

### House Durrandon

The [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_King), Durran's descendants from [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon), used Storm's End as their seat as the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) consolidated control over the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf78.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) In its long history the formidable fortress has never fallen to siege or storm.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf786_1-4)

During the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals), the invaders are said to have abandoned attempts to capture Storm's End from the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) Storm Kings after seven failed sieges and assaults. Instead, the Durrandons and [Andals](wiki:Andals) eventually came together through marriage.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf783_12-0) Archmaester [Vyron](wiki:Vyron) speculates in his *[Triumphs and Defeats](wiki:Triumphs_and_Defeats)* that the final form of the castle being the seventh shows a clear Andal influence and suggests the possibility that the final form was achieved in Andal times. The great tower and curtain wall appear beyond the capability of the ancient First Men, at least, as they often built ringforts which are now left in ruins.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf786_1-5)

[Gyles III Gardener](wiki:Gyles_III_Gardener), [King of the Reach](wiki:King_of_the_Reach), conquered much of the stormlands but failed to capture Storm's End after a siege of two years.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf773_13-0)

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), the last Storm King, [Argilac the Arrogant](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon), abandoned his impressive defenses to meet [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon) in open battle and suffered accordingly in the [Last Storm](wiki:Last_Storm). Taking Argilac's daughter, [Argella](wiki:Argella_Durrandon), as his bride, Orys became Aegon's [Lord Paramount of the Stormlands](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Stormlands) and [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon) replaced the Durrandons as lords of Storm's End.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### House Baratheon

Storm's End hosted [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) several times during the king's royal progresses.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rfab3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) visited the castle after becoming king.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

[Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) was proclaimed king by Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) at Storm's End in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), hastening the downfall of [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) In the same year a steward at Storm's End and his barren wife fostered the baseborn son of [Coryanne Wylde](wiki:Coryanne_Wylde).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rfab7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) The Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) died giving birth to [Jocelyn Baratheon](wiki:Jocelyn_Baratheon) at Storm's End in [54 AC](wiki:54_AC).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rfab9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Near the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) enjoyed feasts, hunting, and jousting at Storm's End when he visited Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) as an envoy of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). Borros hosted Aemon and Prince [Lucerys Velaryon](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon), an envoy of Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), in the [Round Hall](wiki:Round_Hall). Lucerys and his dragon, [Arrax](wiki:Arrax), were slain by Aemond and [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) outside Storm's End when Lucerys attempted to depart.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rfab14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), Rhaenyra's husband, suggested that Storm's End be granted to the lowborn Ser [Ulf White](wiki:Ulf_White), an idea which shocked Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rfab16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Ser [Willis Fell](wiki:Willis_Fell) escorted Princess [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen) to Storm's End after the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rfab18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Lord [Olyver Baratheon](wiki:Olyver_Baratheon) was born at Storm's End seven days after his father Borros was killed in the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rfab18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) recuperated at Storm's End for half a year after he returned from [Dorne](wiki:Dorne).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf49.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### Recent History

Princess [Rhaelle Targaryen](wiki:Rhaelle_Targaryen) served at Storm's End as Lord [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon)'s cupbearer and companion to his lady wife,(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) and she wed Lyonel's heir, Lord [Ormund Baratheon](wiki:Ormund_Baratheon).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf784_22-0)

The *[Windproud](wiki:Windproud)* of Lord [Steffon Baratheon](wiki:Steffon_Baratheon), Ormund's son, sank in [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay) within sight of Storm's End. Bodies washed up on the strand below the castle, with the only survivor being [Patchface](wiki:Patchface). The castellan at that time was Ser [Harbert](wiki:Harbert), while the castle's maester was [Cressen](wiki:Cressen).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

There was a [tourney at Storm's End](wiki:Tourney_at_Storm's_End) prior to [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). During the rebellion, Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Steffon's son, feasted and hunted with Lords [Cafferen](wiki:Lord_Cafferen) and [Grandison](wiki:Lord_Grandison) at Storm's End after defeating them at [Summerhall](wiki:Battles_at_Summerhall); Robert hung the banners of the defeated in his hall as trophies.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

Storm's End was then besieged for near a year by the host of Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell), who commanded the landward forces, whilst Lord [Paxter Redwyne](wiki:Paxter_Redwyne)'s [fleet of the Arbor](wiki:Redwyne_fleet) kept the castle cut off by sea. However, [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), Robert's brother in command of the defense, refused to yield and his men were reduced to eating rats. The [armorer](wiki:Armorer) [Donal Noye](wiki:Donal_Noye) lost an arm during the siege.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) A smuggler named [Davos](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) ran the blockade and resupplied the castle, allowing the garrison to outlast the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End). Stannis rewarded Davos with [knighthood](wiki:Knight), creating [House Seaworth](wiki:House_Seaworth).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

After Robert became king, he gave the castle to his youngest brother, [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), and granted Stannis the much poorer [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), which led to many years of bitterness on Stannis's part.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2)(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) squired at Storm's End, where he became acquainted with Renly.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) When Renly is away at court in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) fulfilling his duties as Robert's [master of laws](wiki:Master_of_laws), Storm's End is held by its castellan, Ser [Cortnay Penrose](wiki:Cortnay_Penrose). Robert's bastard, [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm), is fostered by Cortnay at the castle.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

## Appearances & Description

Storm's End is surrounded by a massive outer curtain wall, one hundred feet high and forty feet thick on its thinnest side and nearly eighty feet thick on its seaward side. It is composed of a double course of pale grey stone with an inner core of sand and rubble. The wall is smooth and curving, the stones so well placed and so perfectly fit together that the wind can find no purchase. On the seaward side, there is a one hundred fifty foot drop below the wall into the sea.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) There is no safe anchorage by the castle.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf786_1-1) The curtain wall protects the castle's kitchens, stables, and yard.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Storm's End has only one tower,(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rfab9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) a colossal drum tower crowned with formidable battlements, making it look like a huge, spiked fist thrusting towards the sky from afar.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) The tower is so large that it comfortably contains the granary, barracks, armory, feast hall, and [lord](wiki:Lord)'s chambers all at once.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) The main hall of the castle is called the [Round Hall](wiki:Round_Hall).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rfab14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) The maester's cell and the [rookery](wiki:Rookery) are located at the top of the tower,(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rfab9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) which is windowless on the side facing the sea.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) It is said that there are [spells](wiki:Magic) woven into the stonework of Storm's End.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

The seaward side of Storm's End stands upon [Durran's Point](wiki:Durran's_Point),(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf78.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) a high white cliff overlooking the sea.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) A tunnel, accessible only at high tide and treacherous because of jagged rocks, leads to a cavern beneath the castle where the storm lords of old built their landing. The passageway is guarded by murder holes in its ceiling and can be lit by torches. A portcullis with narrow bars guards entry from the tunnel to the cavern.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

The [godswood](wiki:Godswood) of Storm's End has a [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood), and the [heart tree](wiki:Heart_tree) is carved with a solemn face.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rasos5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Despite a good wind off [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay), the weather is hot at Storm's End in summertime.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) There are fields and stony ridges in the vicinity.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

[Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End), flees [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) after the death of his brother, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys), the [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers), thinks Renly is riding to Storm's End or [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) in the [Reach](wiki:Reach).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Renly weds [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) at Highgarden and is proclaimed [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms) by Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell). Storm's End calls the banners for Renly.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

### A Clash of Kings

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), the many castles sworn to Storm's End help Renly field a sizable force to support his claim on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). His elder brother, [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), lays [siege to Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)) instead of attacking King's Landing. Stannis's men cut down a nearby grove of trees for siege weapons, and it is where the rival kings hold an unsuccessful parley.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7) The night before the brothers are to battle, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) prays at the sept of a village located in sparse woodland near the castle. Despite the presence of his [Rainbow Guard](wiki:Rainbow_Guard), Renly is assassinated in his camp by [a shadow](wiki:Shadow_child) that night.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

Following Renly's death, the castellan Ser [Cortnay Penrose](wiki:Cortnay_Penrose) refuses to yield in a parley beneath Storm's End's walls, believing that Robert's bastard, [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm), fostered within the castle, would be harmed.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Stannis has Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth), a former smuggler, row [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) into the tunnel beneath the castle so that the spells of Storm's End do not ward out her [magic](wiki:Magic). Once past the walls, Melisandre births a shadow which bypasses the portcullis(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) and throws Cortnay from the tower, an apparent suicide.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

Soon after Cortnay's death, his lieutenant, Lord [Elwood Meadows](wiki:Elwood_Meadows), names himself castellan and surrenders the castle to Stannis.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) Melisandre has Stannis burn the [godswood](wiki:Godswood) as an offering to the [Lord of the Light](wiki:R'hllor).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Following the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Stannis's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Alester Florent](wiki:Alester_Florent), negotiates with the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), suggesting that Stannis relinquish his royal claim and remain Lord of Dragonstone and Storm's End. Stannis has Alester imprisoned for treason.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

Ser [Gilbert Farring](wiki:Gilbert_Farring) serves Stannis as the castellan of Storm's End, with Elwood as his second-in-command and a garrison of two hundred(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) or three hundred.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

Loras admits to Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) that he buried Renly at a place known to them near Storm's End.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) believes that Storm's End belongs to [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) by rights, but that the Iron Throne belongs to his elder sister, [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

In [300 AC](wiki:300_AC), Mace and Lord [Mathis Rowan](wiki:Mathis_Rowan) bring half the [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) strength to [besiege Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(300)).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Some of the [Redwyne fleet](wiki:Redwyne_fleet) blockade [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay), preventing the castle from being resupplied.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) Mace abandons the siege to return to King's Landing after the arrest of Margaery, Mace's daughter and Tommen's queen, by the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) Mace leaves a token force with Lord Rowan to continue the siege but the castle continues to support Stannis's claim to the throne.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rawoiafmathis_rowan.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) Its defenders include Gilbert, Elwood, and Stannis's uncle, Ser [Lomas Estermont](wiki:Lomas_Estermont).(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

[Griffin's Roost is captured](wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost) during the [landing of the Golden Company](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company) in the stormlands. Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) next plans to take Storm's End by guile and use it as a base for further campaigns of the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company). After learning of the planned attack, [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) tells Jon of his intention to lead the assault.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

### The Winds of Winter

At [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost), [Haldon Halfmaester](wiki:Haldon_Halfmaester) informs Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) that the Golden Company has taken Storm's End and that the [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) awaits her there. He tells her that [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell)'s army is descending on the castle from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and that she will want to be safe inside the walls before the battle. Arianne decides to sail to Storm's End.(wiki:Storm's_End.cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Barianne_ii_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Carianne_ii.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)
