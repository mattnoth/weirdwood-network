## Origins

During the reign of King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), a [sept](wiki:Sept) for the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) was made in a sunken cog in the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), while the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon's_Conquest)) in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) sponsored a grander sept atop [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) The [Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Sept_of_Remembrance) was then built on the [Hill of Rhaenys](wiki:Hill_of_Rhaenys) to honor the late Queen [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) destroyed the Sept of Remembrance during the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), however, and replaced it with the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Inspired by a vision, [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) ordered the construction of a great sept atop Visenya's Hill; the fate of the grand sept atop the hill from Aegon the Conqueror's reign is unknown. The Great Sept was completed years after the death of Baelor the Blessed,(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) and it replaced the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept) of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) as the center of the Faith in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).

When [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) join the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) during the reign of [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) with the [wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Maron_Martell_and_Daenerys_Targaryen) of [Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell) and [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)), the king rode with Maron, [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), from the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) to the Great Sept. The two men placed a wreath before the statue of Baelor the Blessed.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), wed his cousin, [Joanna Lannister](wiki:Joanna_Lannister), within the Great Sept in [263 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Aerys made a [walk of atonement](wiki:Walk_of_atonement) through King's Landing to the Great Sept after the death of his infant son, Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerys_II)), in [274 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Aerys's heir, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), and Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell) had a lavish wedding within the Great Sept in [280 AC](wiki:280_AC).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) The [wedding of King Robert I Baratheon and Queen Cersei Lannister](wiki:Wedding_of_Robert_I_Baratheon_and_Cersei_Lannister) was held in the sept after [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-6)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of the Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Category:Images_of_the_Great_Sept_of_Baelor)*

The Great Sept of Baelor is located at the top of [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). The plaza of white [marble](wiki:Marble) which surrounds the [sept](wiki:Sept) features a statue of King [Baelor the Blessed](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) standing tall and serene upon a plinth, his face a study of benevolence.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Large gardens, capable of holding hundreds of people, surround the sept,(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) and marble steps lead up to the building.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

The Great Sept is an impressive marble structure with a great dome(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) and seven slender crystal towers,(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) the bells of which can be rung in mourning(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) or to gather crowds.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) The lofty dome is made of leaded glass,(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) [gold](wiki:Gold),(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) and crystal.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Before the doors leading into the building is a raised marble pulpit from which a [septon](wiki:Septon) can address a gathering crowd.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Past the towering outer doors(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) is the [Hall of Lamps](wiki:Hall_of_Lamps), with its suspended globes of colored leaded glass.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) The sept's floor is polished marble.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Past the entrance hall, through inner doors, is the cavernous sept proper, with seven broad aisles meeting beneath the dome.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) The interior contains benches for worshippers and a stepped marble bier upon which bodies can be placed for funerals.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5) The building has high windows, and light is reflected into rainbows by hanging crystals and the glass dome.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6)(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) Incense sweetens the air.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

The sept has towering(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) stone statues of the Seven.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Scented candles can be placed by worshippers at each altar,(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) with most placed before those of [the Mother](wiki:Mother_(the_Seven)) and [the Maiden](wiki:Maiden_(the_Seven)) but only a few before [the Stranger](wiki:Stranger_(the_Seven)).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4) [The Warrior](wiki:Warrior_(the_Seven)) stands on an altar of red marble.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-5) Different altars are used for different ceremonies, depending on their purpose. For example, marriages are conducted at the altar between two towering gilded statues of [the Father](wiki:Father_(the_Seven)) and the Mother.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) There are several sets of doors leading into the sept proper; septons use the Father's Doors, [septas](wiki:Septa) the Mother's Doors, and [silent sisters](wiki:Silent_sisters) the Stranger's Steps.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5)

The Great Sept has seven transepts,(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7) various crypts and chapels,(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8) and a rainbow pool.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) The sept has tombs where kings are laid to rest,(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) and its vaults hold costly vestments, rings, crystal crowns, and other treasures of the Faith.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Below ground is a seven-sided audience chamber with the faces of the Seven carved into bare stone walls, with their eyes made of [onyx](wiki:Onyx), [malachite](wiki:Malachite), yellow [moonstone](wiki:Moonstone), and [jade](wiki:Jade).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Cells of varying sizes(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) for penitents(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) are found in the towers,(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) which have rough stone steps.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) Individuals accused by the Faith can also be held in chambers below the sept.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

Thrice yearly septons from Baelor's Sept create heraldic drawings and illuminations for the [White Book](wiki:White_Book) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

With the influx of people in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) before the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney), a woman's head is found floating in the rainbow pool of the Great Sept.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

The sept's bells ring dolorously for a day and a night after the death of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) is taken to the steps of the Great Sept of Baelor where, unbeknownst to him, his daughter [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) and [Yoren](wiki:Yoren) are amongst the crowd. Eddard makes a false public confession, but the plan goes awry when the new king, [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), declares that Eddard must be executed, to the evident shock of Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), [Varys](wiki:Varys), and the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(fat_one)). Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne) beheads Eddard on the steps with his own greatsword, [Ice](wiki:Ice). Yoren prevents Arya from seeing her father die.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), in disguise after being dismissed from the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) by Joffrey, is also amongst the crowd. After Eddard's death, Barristan enters the sept and thanks the [Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) that his white cloak had been stripped by Joffrey.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

### A Clash of Kings

The [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(fat_one)) is outraged at Lord Stark's execution, claiming it profaned the Great Sept with blood.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) often meets with the High Septon at the sept, but after these brief visits she dons a plain cloak and secretly meets with the [Kettleblack](wiki:House_Kettleblack) brothers.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

Wisdom [Hallyne](wiki:Hallyne) informs [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) that two hundred jars of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) were discovered in a storeroom below the sept in [298 AC](wiki:298_AC).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) To turn public opinion against [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), now a supporter of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor), Tyrion spreads the word that Stannis intends to burn the Great Sept.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

Thousands jam the Great Sept of Baelor prior to the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) The sept's bells joyously thunder after Stannis's attack on the city is defeated.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) More than six hundred new [knights](wiki:Knight) stand vigil in the Great Sept before walking barefoot to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

### A Storm of Swords

The [royal wedding](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of Joffrey and [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) takes place at midday on the first day of the year [300 AC](wiki:300_AC) in the Great Sept of Baelor.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) After Joffrey dies during his wedding feast, his body is entombed in the Great Sept.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)

### A Feast for Crows

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) stands vigil for his late father, Lord Tywin, whose body rests under the Great Sept's lofty dome of glass and gold and crystal, upon a stepped marble bier. Tywin's corpse stinks so badly no one is able to enter the sept for days afterward.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6)

Instead of a lavish ceremony in the Great Sept, King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) weds Joffrey's widow, Margaery, in the [castle sept](wiki:Castle_sept) of the Red Keep.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

[Sparrows](wiki:Sparrows) gather in the Great Sept's plaza and beneath its doors, piling up bones and skulls beneath the statue of [Baelor the Blessed](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-7) After the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Tyrion's)) dies, the [Most Devout](wiki:Most_Devout) come close to selecting Septon [Luceon](wiki:Luceon_Frey) as the successor. Sparrows enter the Great Sept, however, and force the elevation of the so-called [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-8) Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) meets with the High Sparrow within the sept and agrees to allow the reestablishment of the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant).(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-9)

The [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) confines Margaery in a cell atop one of the Great Sept's towers, although a crowd gathers outside the sept in protest.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) Cersei is pleased at her rival's arrest, but the High Sparrow also has the queen regent imprisoned in a tower cell.(wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

### A Dance with Dragons
