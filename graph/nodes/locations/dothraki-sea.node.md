## Identity

The Dothraki Sea is the vast, open grassland steppe that dominates the interior of the continent of Essos, stretching from the edges of the Free Cities in the west to the Bone Mountains in the east, and from the ruins of the Sarnori kingdoms in the north to the red waste in the south. It is so called because it is as wide as an ocean and as difficult to traverse without the Dothraki as the sea is without ships. The grassland is broken by no major rivers or mountains, offering unobstructed travel to those on horseback — which explains the Dothraki's dominance of its expanse. Vaes Dothrak, the only city of the Dothraki people, sits near the base of the Mother of Mountains at the eastern edge of the sea. Ruined cities and regions ring the Dothraki sea — remnants of the Kingdom of Sarnor, the Kingdom of the Ifequevron, and other civilizations ground to dust by generations of Dothraki raiding.

In the narrative, the Dothraki Sea is the primary setting of Daenerys Targaryen's first arc: she crosses it with Khal Drogo's khalasar, traveling through Ghoyan Drohe, Norvos, Qohor, and Vaes Khadokh to Vaes Dothrak, where she names her unborn son Rhaego. After Drogo's death, her small khalasar struggles south through the red waste to Qarth. In *A Dance with Dragons*, Drogon flies Daenerys to a hill in the Dothraki sea that she names Dragonstone, where Khal Jhaqo's khalasar eventually finds her standing beside her dragon. The sea also provides the terrain context for the legendary Three Thousand of Qohor — the Unsullied force that held off a Dothraki khalasar — and for references to the broader reach of any power claiming to control "the known world."
