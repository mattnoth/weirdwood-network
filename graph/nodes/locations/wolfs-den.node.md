## Origins

[Jon Stark](wiki:Jon_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), raised the Wolf's Den to defend the mouth of the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife) from raiders from the sea. It became the seat of younger sons, brothers, uncles and cousins to the Kings in the North. Some passed the castle to their sons and grandsons, and offshoot branches of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) rose. The [Greystarks](wiki:House_Greystark) lasted the longest, holding the Wolf's Den for five centuries, until they joined [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) of the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort) in rebellion against the Starks of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

After the Greystarks, the castle switched many hands. [House Flint](wiki:House_Flint_of_Widow's_Watch) held it for a century, and [House Locke](wiki:House_Locke) for almost two. [Slates](wiki:House_Slate), [Longs](wiki:House_Long), [Holts](wiki:House_Holt_(north)) and [Ashwoods](wiki:House_Ashwood) then held the Wolf's Den and kept the White Knife safe. Reavers from the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters) took the castle once, and made it their toehold in the north. During the [War Across the Water](wiki:War_Across_the_Water) between the Starks and the [Arryns](wiki:House_Arryn), it was besieged by [King](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale) [Osgood Arryn](wiki:Osgood_Arryn), the Old Falcon, and burned by his son, [Oswin the Talon](wiki:Oswin_Arryn).(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

The Wolf's Den was later attacked by [pirate](wiki:Pirate) lords from the Three Sisters and slavers out of the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones).(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) The castle was captured by slavers from the Stepstones when King [Edrick Stark](wiki:Edrick_Stark) had grown too feeble to defend his realm. During a long and cruel [winter](wiki:Winter) which froze the White Knife, when the slavers were huddled around their fires, Edrick's great-grandson, the new king called "[Ice Eyes](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(Ice_Eyes))", took back the Wolf's Den. He stripped the slavers naked and gave them to the [slaves](wiki:Slave) chained up in the dungeon. The freed slaves hung the slavers' entrails in the branches of the [heart tree](wiki:Heart_tree) as an offering to the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods).(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

A thousand years before [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), the [Manderlys](wiki:House_Manderly) received the Wolf's Den from the Starks. Inside the ancient castle, they took oaths before the old gods and [the new](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) to be the Starks' men forever in return for protection from their enemies and the land that became the city of [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) The Manderlys eventually built the [New Castle](wiki:New_Castle) as a replacement for the aging Wolf's Den and converted the latter to a prison.(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

Ser [Bartimus](wiki:Bartimus) received the Wolf's Den from Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) as a reward for saving his life at the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

Ser Bartimus recounts the castle's long, bloody history to Davos: the ancient First Men fortress was raised by King Jon Stark, then held in turn by the Greystarks and by Houses Flint, Locke, Slate, Long, Holt, and Ashwood; reavers from the Three Sisters took it once as their toehold in the north; it was besieged by Osgood Arryn and burned by his son Oswin the Talon; and it was captured by slavers out of the Stepstones when King Edrick Stark grew too feeble to defend his realm. (adwd-davos-04:35–37)

## Appearances & Description

The Wolf's Den looms over the inner harbor of [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), and houses cling to its crumbling black walls like barnacles.(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The [Castle Stair](wiki:Castle_Stair) is a stepped street which runs from the ancient castle to the [New Castle](wiki:New_Castle) on a nearby hill.(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

The ground floor of the Wolf's Den contains a barracks. One of the castle's cells, large and unexpectedly comfortable, may once have been a lordling's bedchamber. It contains a hearth large enough to hold a kettle and a privy in a corner nook. This cell's floor consists of warped planks. The walls are solid stone and the door is made of [oak](wiki:Oak) and [iron](wiki:Iron). In the castle's cellars are true dungeons, including [oubliettes](wiki:Oubliette), torture chambers, and dank pits in which huge black rats scrabble.(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Located within the Den, the [godswood of White Harbor](wiki:Godswood_of_White_Harbor) contains oaks, elms, and birches. Its [heart tree](wiki:Heart_tree) is a [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) so large that its limbs pass through walls and windows.(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

A door in the godswood leads to a barrel-vaulted cellar with walls covered with salt and a floor sloshing with seawater. Past other cellars are rows of smaller, foul-smelling cells. Pushing on a certain blank wall leads to a long tunnel that runs below the Castle Stair, secretly connecting the Wolf's Den with the New Castle.(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

In the [New Castle](wiki:New_Castle), Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) publicly rejects Lord [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth)'s attempt to gain [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) for the cause of King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon). Wyman orders Ser [Marlon Manderly](wiki:Marlon_Manderly) to take Davos to the Wolf's Den and have his head and hands cut off.(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

To deceive the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Wyman has Davos confined to a cell in the Wolf's Den, and the body of a criminal is instead publicly claimed to be that of Stannis's envoy. Unaware of this, Davos finds his imprisonment in the Wolf's Den is more comfortable than expected. According to Ser [Bartimus](wiki:Bartimus), Davos is the only prisoner in the castle. While [Garth](wiki:Garth_(Wolf's_Den)) tries to intimidate Davos, Bartimus treats the Onion Lord courteously and tells him the history of the prison. Davos writes letters to send to [his family](wiki:House_Seaworth).(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

After Ser [Wylis Manderly](wiki:Wylis_Manderly) is safely returned to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) releases Davos from his cell and brings him to Wyman in the [New Castle](wiki:New_Castle) through the secret passage connecting the two castles. After apologizing to Davos for his poor treatment, Wyman tasks him with finding [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark).(wiki:Wolf's_Den.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)
