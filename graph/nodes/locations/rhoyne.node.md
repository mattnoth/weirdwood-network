## Origins

During the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night), the Rhoyne may have frozen as far south as its confluence with the [Selhoru](wiki:Selhoru).(wiki:Rhoyne.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_long_night.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
The watershed of the Rhoyne was the homeland of the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar), an iron-working people who founded several city-states along its banks.(wiki:Rhoyne.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) The hills and forests contained savage peoples, but the powerful Rhoynar ensured peace along the river and its tributaries.(wiki:Rhoyne.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
After conquering the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis), the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) began expanding west and founded their first colony, [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), at the mouth of the Rhoyne. The Rhoynar served as a buffer against Valyrian expansion to the northwest, however, so the Valyrians instead pressured the [Andals](wiki:Andals) to the west.(wiki:Rhoyne.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
The Rhoynar wielded [water magic](wiki:Water_magic) during the later [Rhoynish Wars](wiki:Rhoynish_Wars), but they were overwhelmed by the [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) of [Valyria](wiki:Valyria). Queen [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria) led the surviving Rhoynar in [ten thousand ships](wiki:Ten_thousand_ships) from their homeland to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) in southern [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), and the Rhoynar cities fell into ruin. Nymeria wed Lord [Mors Martell](wiki:Mors_Martell) in Dorne, forming [House Nymeros Martell](wiki:House_Martell). Their new seat, [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), was named after the spear of the Martells and the sun of the Rhoyne.(wiki:Rhoyne.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_dorne:_sunspear.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
There are few Rhoynar remaining along the Rhoyne's banks. Among them the river holds a god-like status, and in their faith it is called "[Mother Rhoyne](wiki:Mother_Rhoyne)".(wiki:Rhoyne.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
During the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood) after the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), [Norvos](wiki:Norvos) and [Qohor](wiki:Qohor) used fire galleys at [Dagger Lake](wiki:Dagger_Lake) to end Volantene control over the northern Rhoyne.(wiki:Rhoyne.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) The Rhoyne south of the [Sorrows](wiki:Sorrows) is still patrolled by Volantene galleys,(wiki:Rhoyne.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) while the headwaters of the Rhoyne are dominated by Great Norvos(wiki:Rhoyne.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) and Qohor controls the river north of [Ar Noy](wiki:Ar_Noy). The waters between Ar Noy and the Sorrows have been left to [pirates](wiki:Pirates).(wiki:Rhoyne.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)
