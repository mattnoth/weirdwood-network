## Origins

During their [ancient war](wiki:War_of_the_First_Men_and_the_children_of_the_forest) with the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) may have used the [hammer of the waters](wiki:Hammer_of_the_waters) from this island to break the [Arm of Dorne](wiki:Arm_of_Dorne).(wiki:Isle_of_Faces.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_dorne:_the_breaking.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) At the end of the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age), following many centuries of fighting between them, the isle is where the First Men and the children signed the [Pact](wiki:Pact) ending their wars against each other. In celebration, every weirwood on the island was given a face, so that the [gods](wiki:Old_gods) would witness the pact.(wiki:Isle_of_Faces.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
With the signing of the Pact, the order of the [green men](wiki:Green_men) was formed to tend the last remaining weirwoods in the south.(wiki:Isle_of_Faces.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The [Andals](wiki:Andals) failed to conquer the green men, so some [maesters](wiki:Maesters) have conjectured that children of the forest survived the [Andal invasion](wiki:Andal_invasion) on the isle.(wiki:Isle_of_Faces.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Because the island has been considered sacred for millennia, it is not visited by outsiders: in truth, there is no reliable proof that the order of the green men continued to exist after the Andal Invasions. Occasionally over the generations there have been accounts of some young foolhardy riverlord or another attempting to take a boat there, but they are inevitably driven away by sudden windstorms or flocks of ravens.(wiki:Isle_of_Faces.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_ancient_history:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), [Addam Velaryon](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) was said to have landed with his dragon on the Isle of Faces and taken counsel with the green men.(wiki:Isle_of_Faces.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Meera Reed](wiki:Meera_Reed) tells [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) a story about a [crannogman](wiki:Crannogman) who went to the Isle of Faces in the [year of the false spring](wiki:Year_of_the_false_spring). He remained there for a while before travelling to a [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal) in which the [Knight of the Laughing Tree](wiki:Knight_of_the_Laughing_Tree) participated.(wiki:Isle_of_Faces.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
