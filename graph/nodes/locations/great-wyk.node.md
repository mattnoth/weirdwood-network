## Origins

### Older History[ ]

Like the other [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands), Great Wyk was ruled in antiquity by [rock](wiki:Rock_king) and [salt kings](wiki:Salt_king) chosen in [kingsmoots](wiki:Kingsmoot), and the numerous petty kings of the ironborn eventually came together to choose a [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) King [Harrag Hoare](wiki:Harrag_Hoare) included longships from Great Wyk in his forces when he attacked the [north](wiki:North) of [Theon Stark](wiki:Theon_Stark).(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The rock and salt kings were reduced to [lords](wiki:Lord) when [Urron Greyiron](wiki:Urron_Greyiron) ended the kingsmoots and ruled henceforth as hereditary [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)
Thousands of years ago, sailors from Great Wyk discovered the [Lonely Light](wiki:Lonely_Light) after being blown off course.(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
The first [sept](wiki:Sept) in the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) was built on Great Wyk during the reign of King [Wulfgar Hoare](wiki:Wulfgar_Hoare).(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) [Hoare Castle](wiki:Hoare_Castle), the seat of [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) on Great Wyk, was destroyed by Ser [Aubrey Crakehall](wiki:Aubrey_Crakehall) after the defeat of King [Hagon Hoare](wiki:Hagon_Hoare).(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) During the rule of later Hoare kings, merchants and traders from Great Wyk were known to call at [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), and the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

### Iron Throne[ ]

Following [the death](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal) of [Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare), [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), rival claimants rose on Great Wyk, [Harlaw](wiki:Harlaw), [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk), [Orkmont](wiki:Orkmont), and [Pyke](wiki:Pyke_(island)). King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) ended the fighting with his [invasion of the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands) in [2 AC](wiki:2_AC).(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) Aegon the Conqueror visited Great Wyk during one of his [royal progresses](wiki:Royal_progress).(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
When [Lodos the Twice-Drowned](wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned) rose in rebellion, [Goren Greyjoy](wiki:Goren_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), led a hundred longships to Old Wyk and Great Wyk to defeat Lodos on behalf of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
After the death of Lord [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), Great Wyk supported the pretender [Sam Salt](wiki:Sam_Salt), who claimed to be descended from [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare), in a [struggle for power in the Iron Islands](wiki:Struggle_for_power_in_the_Iron_Islands). Ser [Leo Costayne](wiki:Leo_Costayne) invaded the isles on behalf of Lady [Johanna Lannister](wiki:Johanna_Lannister), but he was slain by [Arthur Goodbrother](wiki:Arthur_Goodbrother) on Great Wyk.(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
[Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), subdued Great Wyk on behalf of his brother, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), during [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion).(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), sends his daughter [Asha](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) to Great Wyk to meet with Lord [Gorold Goodbrother](wiki:Gorold_Goodbrother).(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) [Sparrs](wiki:House_Sparr), [Merlyns](wiki:House_Merlyn), and [Goodbrothers](wiki:House_Goodbrother) travel from Great Wyk to [Pyke](wiki:Pyke) when Balon calls the banners.(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Balon declares himself first [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands)(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) and then [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

[Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), a [priest of the Drowned God](wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God), is [drowning men](wiki:Drowned_men) on the shores of Great Wyk when he learns from [the Sparr](wiki:The_Sparr), [Steffarion Sparr](wiki:Steffarion_Sparr), and [Gormond Goodbrother](wiki:Gormond_Goodbrother) of the death of his eldest brother, King Balon, and the priest travels to [Hammerhorn](wiki:Hammerhorn) to meet with Lord Goodbrother. At [Pebbleton](wiki:Pebbleton), Aeron Damphair calls for a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) to be held to choose Balon's successor.(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
Goodbrothers,(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Sparrs,(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) [Humbles](wiki:House_Humble),(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2) and Merlyns(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) depart Great Wyk for the [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk), in which [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) is chosen king against Aeron's intentions.(wiki:Great_Wyk.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)
