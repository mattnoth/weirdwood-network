## Origins

Castle Black is an ancient stronghold,(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) although the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort), the first castle of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), is said to be twice as old.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Castle Black at some point became the seat of the [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), taking over that role from the Nightfort(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rawoiafcastle_black.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) at least six hundred years ago.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)

Lord Commander [Lothor Burley](wiki:Lothor_Burley) hosted Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) and her [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing), at Castle Black in [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). The dragon refused to fly over the [Wall](wiki:Wall) despite Alysanne's efforts.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

Castle Black once held over five thousand fighting men along with their servants, but that has dwindled to a tenth of that number.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) According to Lord Commander [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), Castle Black now holds six hundred brothers.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

[Craster](wiki:Craster) is said to be the son of a member of the Watch and a woman from [Whitetree](wiki:Whitetree). When the wildling tried to show their child to the Sworn Brother, she was chased away from Castle Black.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

## Appearances & Description

Castle Black is not a true castle, as it has no walls to defend it to the west, east, or south. Only the Wall stands to the north. It consists of several stone towers and timber keeps.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Beneath the keeps and towers, there is a series of subterranean passages which connect all of the buildings. They are rarely used during the summer, but in the winter it is the only way to travel to different parts of the castle.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Castle Black has a small [sept](wiki:Sept) run by Septon [Cellador](wiki:Cellador) but no [godswood](wiki:Godswood).(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Men who follow the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) can travel half a league [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall) into the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest), where a small grove contains a rough circle of nine [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) [heart trees](wiki:Heart_tree).(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

### Towers

- The [Lord Commander's Tower](wiki:Lord_Commander's_Tower), also called "Lord Commander's Keep", is where the [Lord Commander's](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) quarters are located.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
- The [King's Tower](wiki:King's_Tower) is reserved for honored guests, though no king has visited the tower in over a hundred years.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
- [Hardin's Tower](wiki:Hardin's_Tower) has a broken battlement, from which stone has spilled into the yard below. It has a severe lean.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
- The [Lance](wiki:Lance_(tower)) is the tallest tower at the castle, though only a third of the height of the Wall. It is slim and crumbling.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
- The [Tower of Guards](wiki:Tower_of_Guards) is the strongest of the towers. It lies next to the kingsroad.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
- The [Silent Tower](wiki:Silent_Tower) is a tower at Castle Black.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)
- [Maester Aemon's tower](wiki:Maester_Aemon's_tower) is a stout timber keep which contains quarters for Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) as well as the [rookery](wiki:Rookery) and a library.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

### Buildings

- The [common hall](wiki:Common_hall) is a great timbered keep where the brothers take their meals. Crows nest in the rafters.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
- The [armory](wiki:Armory_(Castle_Black)) is where the equipment for weapons practice is kept. [Donal Noye](wiki:Donal_Noye) forges weapons and armor here.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
- The bathhouse is found near the armory.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
- The old [Flint Barracks](wiki:Flint_Barracks) is where most of the brothers reside.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
- The subterranean [vaults of Castle Black](wiki:Vaults_of_Castle_Black) contain stored food(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) as well as records and old books that even the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) does not have.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Amongst other topics, they have drawings of the faces in the weirwoods, [the language](wiki:True_Tongue) of the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest), and scrolls from [Valyria](wiki:Valyria).(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
- The [Shieldhall](wiki:Shieldhall) is a feast hall of dark stone. In years past, when the Night's Watch was much larger in number, its walls had been hung with rows of brightly colored wooden shields. When a knight took the black, his shield would adorn its wall and he would take up the plain black shield of the brotherhood.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
- The [Grey Keep](wiki:Grey_Keep) is another building at Castle Black.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### Wormways

There are a series of tunnels underneath Castle Black called [wormways](wiki:Wormways)(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) or wormwalks,(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) which connect the various keeps and towers. These tunnels are mostly used during the winter, when the cold and snow, sometimes forty or fifty feet deep, make it impossible to travel above ground.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) The large archive in [the vaults](wiki:Vaults_of_Castle_Black) lies along one of these tunnels.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

### Lichyard

Beside the eastern road from Castle Black is a [lichyard](wiki:Lichyard) with the graves of some members of the Night's Watch.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) Beside the lichyard are ancient tombs.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

### The Wall

The gate guards the tunnel through the [Wall](wiki:Wall), which is long, twisting and narrow. Three iron gates block the inner passage. Each of these gates is locked and has a murder hole above it. The outer door to the tunnel is solid oak, about nine inches thick.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

The great switchback stair climbs its way up from Castle Black to the top of the Wall. It is made of wooden stairs, anchored by huge beams frozen into the Wall itself.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

An iron cage attached to a winch is used to ascend and descend the face of the Wall. The cage can hold ten men(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) and is located near Castle Black's well.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Supplies and barrels of gravel can be transported to the top via the cage.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

Facilities on top of the Wall include a small warming shed for the men on watch located next to the crane.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

[Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) and [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) are trained by Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne) as recruits of the Night's Watch at Castle Black. [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) sleeps in the [King's Tower](wiki:King's_Tower) during his visit to the Wall, while Jon sleeps in [Hardin's Tower](wiki:Hardin's_Tower).(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11) Jon and Sam swear their vows at the nearby godswood north of the Wall and are sworn in as new brothers of the Watch.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) The bodies of [Jafer Flowers](wiki:Jafer_Flowers) and [Othor](wiki:Othor), rangers killed beyond the Wall, reanimate as [wights](wiki:Wights) at Castle Black. The [Lord Commander's Tower](wiki:Lord_Commander's_Tower) burns during Jon's fight with the undead Othor when it tries to kill Lord Commander [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont).(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Ragot52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

### A Clash of Kings

Two hundred men from Castle Black accompany Lord Commander Mormont during the [great ranging](wiki:Great_ranging) north of the Wall. Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) remains at the castle because of his age. [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) reads a report in the library about [Redwyn](wiki:Redwyn), a brother who led a ranging to the [Frozen Shore](wiki:Frozen_Shore) during the reign of [Dorren Stark](wiki:Dorren_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North).(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

### A Storm of Swords

Jon returns to Castle Black after being wounded by [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) at [Queenscrown](wiki:Queenscrown) south of the Wall.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) Jon and [Donal Noye](wiki:Donal_Noye) fortify Castle Black as best they can in anticipation of [Styr](wiki:Styr), Magnar of [Thenn](wiki:Thenn_(valley)). Styr's wildling raiders are defeated in their [attack on Castle Black](wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black) by a fire trap at the wooden stairs of the castle.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

[Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) attacks the Wall with multiple waves of wildlings during the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall). Jon leads the defenders atop the Wall while Donal protects the tunnel through the Wall. After the death of Donal, Aemon tells Jon that he must lead Castle Black until its garrison returns.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) Jon is sent as an envoy to meet with Mance north of the Wall. As they converse, the knights of King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) and [rangers](wiki:Rangers) from [Eastwatch](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea) appear and rout the wildling host.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

In the aftermath of the battle, Sam finds the [common hall](wiki:Common_hall) burned to the ground and the wooden stair a mound of broken ice and scorched timbers.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Stannis occupies the King's Tower, while the Lance, Hardin's Tower, the Grey Keep, the [Shieldhall](wiki:Shieldhall), and other buildings are also crowded with men. Jon is selected as the new Lord Commander to replace Jeor Mormont, who was killed at [Craster's Keep](wiki:Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep).(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Samwell searches in Castle Black's library for information about the [Others](wiki:Others). Sam learns from Lord Commander Snow that he must escort Maester Aemon to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) and then train at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) to become Castle Black's new maester.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) Because Stannis Baratheon has claimed the King's Tower, and the Lord Commander's Tower has been gutted by fire, Jon establishes himself in Donal Noye's modest quarters behind the [armory](wiki:Armory_(Castle_Black)).(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)

### A Dance with Dragons

Castle Black hosts the army of King Stannis. Jon Snow executes [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt) for disobedience, while [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) burns "[Mance Rayder](wiki:Rattleshirt)". Jon allows thousands of captive wildlings to cross through the Wall to Castle Black, much to the consternation of some members of the Night's Watch.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) The officers worry there will not be enough rations for the additional mouths. Stannis departs to recruit the [northern mountain clans](wiki:Northern_mountain_clans), although Melisandre remains at Castle Black.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) Some wildlings are recruited by Jon to help the Night's Watch defend the Wall.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

Queen [Selyse](wiki:Selyse_Florent) and her [queen's men](wiki:Queen's_men) stop at Castle Black on their way to the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort).(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) [Alys Karstark](wiki:Alys_Karstark) arrives seeking refuge and is married to [Sigorn](wiki:Sigorn) of Thenn in a [a ceremony performed by Melisandre](wiki:Wedding_of_Sigorn_and_Alys_Karstark).(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Jon has Alys's relative [Cregan Karstark](wiki:Cregan_Karstark) imprisoned in an [ice cell](wiki:Ice_cell). [Val](wiki:Val) brings [Tormund Giantsbane](wiki:Tormund_Giantsbane) and his four thousand wildlings to Castle Black, but [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh) and the mountain clans disapprove of the wildlings settling south of the Wall.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

Lord Commander Snow agrees to disperse the wildlings amongst the previously abandoned castles along the Wall.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) Jon receives a [belligerent letter](wiki:Bastard_Letter) from [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) and, choosing to break his vow to the Night's Watch to remain neutral, decides to go to Winterfell to confront Ramsay. As he prepares, the giant [Wun Wun](wiki:Wun_Weg_Wun_Dar_Wun) kills one of the queen's men, Ser [Patrek of King's Mountain](wiki:Patrek_of_King's_Mountain). While Jon is trying to make sense of the situation, he is [attacked](wiki:Mutiny_at_Castle_Black) by Bowen and other members of the Night's Watch.(wiki:Castle_Black.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)
