## Origins

### Arrival of the Andals[ ]

The hills of Andalos were the homeland of the [Andals](wiki:Andals) prior to [their invasion](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) of the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers) in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). Possibly originating from the [Axe](wiki:Axe),(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rawoiafaxe.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) the Andals took Andalos from a race of [hairy men](wiki:Hairy_men) believed by some to have been cousins to the [Ibbenese](wiki:Ibbenese).(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
The [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) originates in Andalos.(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) It is said that the Seven made themselves manifest in the hills, and thus the Andals felt blessed by the gods. The [Father](wiki:Father_(the_Seven)) is believed to have pulled seven stars from the heavens to make the crown of King [Hugor of the Hill](wiki:Hugor_of_the_Hill), the first king of the Andals.(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) The tradition of the [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) originated in Andalos.(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Unlike the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) in Westeros, the Andals of Andalos did not practice the [first night](wiki:First_night).(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
The [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar) are believed to have taught [ironworking](wiki:Iron) to the Andals,(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) and the historian [Doro Golathis](wiki:Doro_Golathis) of [Norvos](wiki:Norvos) wrote about Rhoynish outposts in Andalos.(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Living in Andalos for thousands of years, the Andals spread their influence possibly as far as [Lorath](wiki:Lorath)(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and [Myr](wiki:Myr).(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
Various petty kings ruled in Andalos, though [Qarlon the Great](wiki:Qarlon_the_Great) desired to become [King of All Andals](wiki:King_of_All_Andals).(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

### Departure of the Andals[ ]

The [Andals](wiki:Andals) eventually migrated to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) and conquered most of the kingdoms of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men). According to Andal tradition, they traveled to Westeros because [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) had promised them kingdoms in a foreign land. [Maesters](wiki:Maesters), however, believe the Andals in [Essos](wiki:Essos) were instead threatened by the expanding [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold).(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) The [True History](wiki:True_History) states the [Andals invaded Westeros](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) four thousand years ago, but some maesters claim it was only two thousand years ago.(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
After repelling Andal attacks on the eastern coast of the [north](wiki:North), King [Theon Stark](wiki:Theon_Stark) raided a coastal section of Andalos in retaliation.(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
Most of Andalos eventually became controlled by [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), a [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) colony and now one of the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_pentos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### Recent History[ ]

After learning of reports that [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) had been seen by shepherds in the hills of Andalos, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) sent a dozen men led by Ser [Willam the Wasp](wiki:Willam_the_Wasp) to investigate. Willam's party were ambushed and slain in the [Velvet Hills](wiki:Velvet_Hills), however, by brigands who had been spreading tales of a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) to ward others away from their sheepstealing.(wiki:Andalos.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)
