## Origins

Whitewalls was built forty years before the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion), about [171 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), by Lord [Butterwell](wiki:Butterwell_(Hand_of_the_King)), who had been [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen).(wiki:Whitewalls.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) Some seventy to eighty years before the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), one could take a ferry across the [Trident](wiki:Trident) from the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn) to the road that led to [Lord Harroway's Town](wiki:Lord_Harroway's_Town) and Whitewalls. However, the river moved and the ferry landing at the crossroads inn was abandoned.(wiki:Whitewalls.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

The castle was the site of the [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC). While journeying from [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept) to the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad), Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) and Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) took [Ned's ferry](wiki:Ned_(ferryman)) from the [old inn by the lakeshore](wiki:Old_inn_by_the_lakeshore) across the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye) to attend the festivities. Due to the overflow of injured during the [tourney](wiki:Tourney), [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Lothar](wiki:Lothar) tended to the wounded in the castle's cellar.(wiki:Whitewalls.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

The Second Blackfyre Rebellion failed at the tourney, and for his [treason](wiki:Treason), Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell) forfeited Whitewalls to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), ordered the castle pulled down and its earth salted.(wiki:Whitewalls.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

## Appearances & Description

Costly to build, the castle's walls, keep, and towers were made of white stone quarried in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), and its floors and pillars were crafted from white [marble](wiki:Marble) veined with [gold](wiki:Gold). The rafters were carved from the trunks of [weirwoods](wiki:Weirwood). The pale castle was commonly called the "Milkhouse" by those who lived near it, for its color and for House Butterwell's income from dairy cows.(wiki:Whitewalls.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

The arms of [House Butterwell](wiki:House_Butterwell) were displayed from the keep and the gatehouse. Whitewalls had postern gates, including one in the north wall. Whitewalls had barracks, stables, dungeons, kennels, and kitchens. The castle's cellar was full of casks of [wine](wiki:Wine) and [ale](wiki:Ale) and had a barrel-vaulted ceiling. A closed courtyard contained a well ringed with a low stone wall. The castle [sept](wiki:Sept) had inner and outer doors, and altars to each of the [Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). Viewing stands for [tourneys](wiki:Tourney) could be raised in the castle's outer yard.(wiki:Whitewalls.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

The [great hall](wiki:Great_Hall) of Whitewalls was a long white hall with a dais and a minstrel's gallery. In contrast to the castle's marble opulence, the hall was modest in size. Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell) and [his wife](wiki:Lady_Frey_(wife_of_Ambrose_Butterwell)) had a double throne of gilded [oak](wiki:Oak), and esteemed guests could sit in tall chairs with carved arms. Banners hung from the hall's rafters.(wiki:Whitewalls.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

Located in a tower, the large bedchamber of Lord Butterwell was finely decorated with [Myrish](wiki:Myr) carpets, scented candles, and a set of plate [armor](wiki:Armor) inlaid with [gold](wiki:Gold) and gems. Ambrose kept his [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) on a black velvet cushion on the top of a marble plinth. The chamber had its own privy as well.(wiki:Whitewalls.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

Half a mile from Whitewalls was a spreading elm tree with cows grazing nearby.(wiki:Whitewalls.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)
