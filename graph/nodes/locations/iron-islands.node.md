## Origins

### Settlement

[Maesters](wiki:Maesters) believe the Iron Islands were settled by the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) many thousands of years ago. Legends claim that the First Men discovered what would be called the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair) upon the shores of [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) There is no evidence the islands were inhabited by [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) or [giants](wiki:Giants). [Weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) trees do not grow in their poor soils, so the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) did not have significant followers there. Instead, the humans who came to inhabit the islands worship their own local religion of the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)

Differing from maesters, the [priests of the isles](wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God) claim the [ironmen](wiki:Ironmen) are not First Men in origin but were created in the image of the Drowned God, and they therefore may have a connection with [merlings](wiki:Merling). Some also suspect that the isles were originally inhabited by the [Deep Ones](wiki:Deep_Ones), and that they are the ones who left the Seastone Chair behind.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9)

Most ironborn believe that their ancestors were an offshoot of the First Men who simply crossed to the isles on boats, where their culture developed differently from their mainland cousins.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-10) The ancient First Men kept [thralls](wiki:Thrall), hinting that the ironborn were also First Men who retained the practice in their isolation.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-11)

### Kingsmoots

According to legend, the islands were ruled by the [Grey King](wiki:Grey_King) during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) For much of their history, each island was its own kingdom and had its own kings, a [rock king](wiki:Rock_king) who ruled the land and a [salt king](wiki:Salt_king) who commanded at sea. Chosen by [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot), these petty kings constantly fought with each other, and they raided the First Men of mainland Westeros for timber, crops, and thralls.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)

The islands were first unified when a powerful priest of the Drowned God, [Galon Whitestaff](wiki:Galon_Whitestaff), decreed it was sinful for ironborn to make war upon other ironborn. The other priests preached his word throughout the isles, until the various kings and longship captains convened on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk) at [Nagga's Bones](wiki:Nagga's_Bones) for the first kingsmoot to select one [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands) to rule over all. Galon decreed the title was not hereditary, but upon the death of each High King a new kingsmoot would be convened to elect another. The new high kings were also called driftwood kings because of their crowns of driftwood.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-12)

The kingsmoots on Old Wyk ended the petty wars between each of the isles, and with their new unified strength, the High Kings began to conquer other lands instead of just raiding them. Under the rule of King [Qhored the Cruel](wiki:Qhored_I_Hoare), the ironborn brought much of the western coast of Westeros under the rule of the Iron Island, including lands as far as [Bear Island](wiki:Bear_Island), the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor), and [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) They were gradually lost by his successors, however, as mainland houses such as the [Hightowers](wiki:House_Hightower), [Gardeners](wiki:House_Gardener), and [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) increased in strength. The High Kings came from numerous houses, with most coming from Houses [Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy), [Goodbrother](wiki:House_Goodbrother), and especially [Greyiron](wiki:House_Greyiron).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2)(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

### The Greyiron dynasty

Upon the death of High King [Urragon III Greyiron](wiki:Urragon_III_Greyiron), his younger sons convened a kingsmoot which chose [Urrathon IV Goodbrother](wiki:Urrathon_IV_Goodbrother), although Urragon's elder son, [Torgon Greyiron](wiki:Torgon_Greyiron), was away raiding the [Reach](wiki:Reach). Supported by priests unhappy with Urrathon's tyranny, Torgon declared the kingsmoot invalid when he returned to the Iron Islands, and the so-called Badbrother was overthrown. Although Torgon the Latecomer was wise, the ironborn were still in decline and the [Cape of Eagles](wiki:Cape_of_Eagles) was lost to the [Mallisters](wiki:House_Mallister) of [Seagard](wiki:Seagard). Torgon had his son rule alongside him for several years, and [Urragon IV Greyiron](wiki:Urragon_IV_Greyiron) thus also became High King without being chosen in a kingsmoot.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-3)

The dying wish of Urragon IV was that the high kingship pass to his great-nephew [Urron Greyiron](wiki:Urron_Greyiron), salt king of Orkmont. When the priests insisted that a kingsmoot be held at [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk), Urron had his men slaughter those who attended, including thirteen other kings and half a hundred priests.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8) Urron Redhand established the hereditary rule of [House Greyiron](wiki:House_Greyiron) over the Iron Islands. Instead of being called High Kings, they titled themselves [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands) and wore iron crowns. Abolishing the system of rock and salt kings, the Greyirons reduced other ironborn kings to vassal [lords](wiki:Lord) and extinguished families who refused to submit.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

While the driftwood kings were elected by consent of the lords and captains, the iron kings led to infighting among the ironborn, which the priests were unable to stop. The Greyirons faced half a dozen major rebellions, numerous smaller insurrections and insubordinations, and at least two major [thrall](wiki:Thrall) uprisings.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

The stronger and larger kingdoms of the [green lands](wiki:Green_lands) took advantage of the ironborn's disunity to take back conquests on mainland [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). For instance, [Garth VII Gardener](wiki:Garth_VII_Gardener), [King of the Reach](wiki:King_of_the_Reach), drove the ironmen from the [Shield Islands](wiki:Shield_Islands) and fortified them to prevent ironborn raids up the [Mander](wiki:Mander).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2)

Unlike the First Men, the [Andals](wiki:Andals) built strong ships capable of fighting ironborn longships. During the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals), wooden stockades of the First Men with replaced with stone castles throughout the [Reach](wiki:Reach), the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), and the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), new defenses against the lightning raids of the ironborn. All of their possessions lost, the Greyirons barely held on to power, and the isles increasingly divided into civil wars. After a thousand years of hereditary rule, the Greyirons fell to a coalition of ironborn lords and Andal adventurers, who often intermarried with the natives of the Iron Islands.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3)

### The Hoare dynasty

The [Greyirons](wiki:House_Greyiron) were replaced as hereditary Kings of the Iron Islands by [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare), who intermarried with the [Andals](wiki:Andals) when they came to the isles. The [priests of the Drowned God](wiki:Priests_of_the_Drowned_God) considered the Hoares ungodly and false kings, which Archmaester [Hake](wiki:Hake_(archmaester)) agreed with. Archmaester [Haereg](wiki:Haereg), however, believed the Hoares were disliked for tolerating the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), discouraging reaving, and promoting trade.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

The priests eventually rebelled against King [Harmund the Handsome](wiki:Harmund_III_Hoare), led by a priest remembered as the [Shrike](wiki:Shrike). They overthrew Harmund within a fortnight and mutilated his mother, Dowager Queen [Lelia Lannister](wiki:Lelia_Lannister), which led to a long war with the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) which left the Iron Islands impoverished and ill-prepared for the [Famine Winter](wiki:Famine_Winter).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) Ser [Aubrey Crakehall](wiki:Aubrey_Crakehall) claimed the islands for himself during the war, but he was swiftly overthrown by the ironmen.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2)

It took centuries for the Iron Islands to recover, during which ironmen began to trade with coastal Westeros and the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities). Only a shadow of what they once were, the ironborn no longer held territories on the fortified mainland and instead reaved in distant places, such as the [Basilisk Isles](wiki:Basilisk_Isles), [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), and [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-3)

### Kings of the Isles and the Rivers

A few generations before the [Wars of Conquest](wiki:Wars_of_Conquest), the peaceful King [Qhorwyn the Cunning](wiki:Qhorwyn_Hoare) built a strong fleet to deter attack.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-4) His ambitious son, King [Harwyn Hardhand](wiki:Harwyn_Hoare), then conquered the [Trident](wiki:Trident) on the mainland from the [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King) [Arrec Durrandon](wiki:Arrec_Durrandon).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) Instead of relinquishing control of the territory to rivermen, the Hoares ruled both the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) and the Iron Islands as [Kings of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:Kings_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers). Harwyn's son, King [Halleck](wiki:Halleck_Hoare), ruled from [Fairmarket](wiki:Fairmarket) on the [Blue Fork](wiki:Blue_Fork) of the Trident rather than from the Iron Islands, and he led unsuccessful campaigns against the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands), the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), and the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-5)

Halleck's son, King [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare), ordered the construction of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), an enormous castle on the northern shore of the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye) in central Westeros. The building of Harrenhal over forty years beggared both the Iron Islands and the riverlands.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

The Hoare line ended with the deaths of Harren and his sons during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest). Inspired by [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), Lord [Edmyn Tully](wiki:Edmyn_Tully) led the river lords in rebellion against the Hoares at Harrenhal.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Harren refused to yield to Aegon and the castle was too strong to storm, so Aegon flew his [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), over the walls and roasted Harren and his sons in [their tower](wiki:Kingspyre_Tower).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Racok26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Most of Harren's supporters were killed in the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal) or by river lords as they retreated back to [Ironman's Bay](wiki:Ironman's_Bay),(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) and Aegon granted rule over the riverlands to [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

### The Greyjoys under the Iron Throne

In [2 AC](wiki:2_AC), King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) led an [invasion of the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands) to defeat the various pretenders to [Harren the Black's](wiki:Harren_Hoare) throne, including [Qhorin Volmark](wiki:Qhorin_Volmark) and [Lodos](wiki:Lodos). Aegon then allowed the defeated ironborn to choose Lord [Vickon Greyjoy](wiki:Vickon_Greyjoy) of [Pyke](wiki:Pyke) to rule as the new [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), a vassal of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) which now ruled the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) from the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy) relinquished all claims to the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), and Aegon granted the ruins of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) to [House Qoherys](wiki:House_Qoherys).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

Lord Vickon was a cautious ruler who did not provoke [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) and its [dragons](wiki:Dragon), and he allowed the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) to return to the Iron Islands. His son, Lord [Goren Greyjoy](wiki:Goren_Greyjoy), suppressed a conspiracy to crown [Qhorin Volmark](wiki:Qhorin_Volmark) and a revolt by a priest calling himself [Lodos the Twice-Drowned](wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned), and in return King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) allowed Goren to expel the Faith from the archipelago for another century. After Goren, the weakened Iron Islands remained aloof from mainland politics for the next hundred years.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Lord [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), the Red Kraken, raided the western shores during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), capturing land from the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands). He was murdered at [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC) by a [salt wife](wiki:Salt_wife), [Tess](wiki:Tess), and a [struggle for power](wiki:Struggle_for_power_in_the_Iron_Islands) broke out between his two infant [salt sons](wiki:Salt_son), [Toron](wiki:Toron_Greyjoy) and [Rodrik](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy_(son_of_Dalton)), his sisters and good-brothers, his cousins, and the pretender [Sam Salt](wiki:Sam_Salt).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) In [134 AC](wiki:134_AC), Lady [Johanna Lannister](wiki:Johanna_Lannister) allied with Ser [Leo Costayne](wiki:Leo_Costayne) to invade the islands in reprisal and conquer them for her son, Lord [Loreon Lannister](wiki:Loreon_Lannister_(son_of_Jason)), though Lord Toron remained on the Seastone Chair by the war's end.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1)

During the reign of King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), Lord [Dagon Greyjoy](wiki:Dagon_Greyjoy), the Last Reaver, led the ironborn in again raiding the western coast of Westeros.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

The Iron Islands supported the Iron Throne during the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings), with Lord [Quellon Greyjoy](wiki:Quellon_Greyjoy) leading a hundred longships to fight in the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Quellon outlawed [thralldom](wiki:Thrall) and wanted to integrate the Iron Islands into the rest of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), and he pursued neutrality during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). Encouraged by his sons, however, Quellon joined the rebels after Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen)'s death at the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident). Quellon made a token display of support for [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon) against the Targaryens, but the Lord of the Iron Islands was killed in [battle at the Mander](wiki:Battle_at_the_Mander).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

Quellon was succeeded by his eldest surviving son, Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), who rejected his father's works and wanted to return to the [Old Way](wiki:Old_Way) of paying the [iron price](wiki:Iron_price). Balon spent five years building the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet), believing that the usurper [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s reign was weak. In [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), Balon declared himself [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands) in a bid for independence from the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion) had some early victories, [destroying the Lannister fleet](wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet) at anchor and raiding the western coasts, but Balon had misjudged Robert's position. The mainland rallied their support for Robert, and the Iron Islands were invaded by the king, his brother Lord [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), and Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) Balon lost his two eldest sons in the war, and though Robert pardoned the would-be king in return for bending the knee, Balon's surviving son, [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), was sent to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to be the ward and hostage of Lord Stark.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), now [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), advises [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) to watch his ward, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy). With tensions increasing in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) may need the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) of Theon's father, [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

### A Clash of Kings

[Warfare](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) erupts in Westeros after the death of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms). Seeking an alliance, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), sends Theon, his childhood friend, back to the Iron Islands as an envoy to Theon's father, Balon.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-9)

Balon takes advantage of the instability and declares independence for the Iron Islands. Rather than ally with Robb against [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) and King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), Balon sends the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) to occupy key positions in the [north](wiki:North) and declares himself [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

### A Storm of Swords

[Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) claims the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair) the day after Balon falls to his death at [Pyke](wiki:Pyke).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) [Sawane Botley](wiki:Sawane_Botley), [Lord of Lordsport](wiki:Lord_of_Lordsport), is drowned by Euron for stating that the throne belongs to Theon as Balon's son.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1)(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

### A Feast for Crows

[Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) declares a [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk) as the proper way to raise the next king after Balon's death.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) This move is made by Aeron to end a potential civil war between the ironborn and to prevent Euron from taking the Seastone Chair. [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), [Lord Captain of the Iron Fleet](wiki:Lord_Captain_of_the_Iron_Fleet), returns from the [Saltspear](wiki:Saltspear) with most longships of the Iron Fleet.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) However, Euron is chosen king despite the efforts of Aeron, Victarion, and [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

Euron promises to conquer all of Westeros for the ironborn and expands their war by attacking the [Shield Islands](wiki:Shield_Islands),(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor),(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) and the [Mander](wiki:Mander).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) Euron offers to give the Seastone Chair and the Iron Islands to Victarion once the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) is claimed.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2) [Baelor Blacktyde](wiki:Baelor_Blacktyde), [Lord of Blacktyde](wiki:Lord_of_Blacktyde), is killed for not accepting Euron as king.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3)

Euron sails from the Iron Islands to attack the [Reach](wiki:Reach), beginning with the [taking of the Shields](wiki:Taking_of_the_Shields).(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-4)

### A Dance with Dragons

The castellan of Pyke, [Erik Ironmaker](wiki:Erik_Ironmaker), rules the Iron Islands in Euron's absence while he is on campaign.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

Victarion leads the Iron Fleet to [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) on a mission to gain [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) and her [dragons](wiki:Dragons) for Euron's cause.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) Victarion intends to claim Daenerys for himself, however.(wiki:Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)
