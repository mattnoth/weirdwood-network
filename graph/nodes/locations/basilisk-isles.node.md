## Culture

The Basilisk Isles are named after the [basilisks](wiki:Basilisk) that once infested them.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
Corsair towns and [pirate](wiki:Pirate) lairs—which reuse names like [Black Pudding](wiki:Black_Pudding), [Port Plunder](wiki:Port_Plunder), [Sty](wiki:Sty), and [Whore's Gash](wiki:Whore's_Gash)—spring up in the Basilisks but are quickly abandoned.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) The mud-and-blood towns of the Basilisk Isles teem with escaped [slaves](wiki:Slaves), slavers, [skinners](wiki:Flaying), [whores](wiki:Whores), hunters, [brindled men](wiki:Brindled_men), [sellswords](wiki:Sellswords), corsairs, and pirates.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) describes life in the Basilisks as nasty, brutal, and often short. The isles are hot, humid, and swarming with stinging flies, sand fleas, and bloodworms, making them unhealthy for both man and beast.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)
Corsairs from the Basilisks have often raided [Naath](wiki:Naath)(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_naath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and [Sothoryos](wiki:Sothoryos).(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_sothoryos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) For centuries,(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) corsairs have brought their captives to holding pens on [Talon](wiki:Talon) and the [Isle of Tears](wiki:Isle_of_Tears) before selling them in the flesh markets of [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) or the [pillow houses](wiki:Pillow_house) and [pleasure gardens](wiki:Pleasure_garden) of [Lys](wiki:Lys).(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_sothoryos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Many corsairs decorate their ships with severed heads dangling from hempen rope, and they deliver the [skulls](wiki:Bone) to [Skull Isle](wiki:Skull_Isle) once all the flesh is gone.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

## Origins

### Ancient History[ ]

Ruins on the [Isle of Tears](wiki:Isle_of_Tears), the [Isle of Toads](wiki:Isle_of_Toads), and [Ax Isle](wiki:Ax_Isle) indicate the presence of an ancient civilization, now lost, established during the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age). Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) speculates the inhabitants of the Isle of Toads may have a link to this vanished people.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)
The [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis) founded Gorgai on the Isle of Tears. The [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) captured it during the [Third Ghiscari War](wiki:Third_Ghiscari_War) and renamed it [Gogossos](wiki:Gogossos). The [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) used it as a twisted penal colony for the worst criminals.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)
During the migration of their [ten thousand ships](wiki:Ten_thousand_ships), the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar) stopped in the Basilisks for provisions but were attacked by corsair kings of Ax Isle, [Talon](wiki:Talon), and the [Howling Mountain](wiki:Howling_Mountain), who put twoscore ships to the torch and carried off hundreds into slavery. The corsairs offered to allow the Rhoynar to settle on the Isle of Toads, provided they gave up their boats and sent each king thirty virgin girls and thirty pretty boys each year as tribute. Princess [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria) refused, however, and took her fleet to sea again.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
[Swan ships](wiki:Swan_ships) from the [Summer Isles](wiki:Summer_Isles) explored the Basilisks, though pirates and slavers from the Basilisks then began to raid the Summer Islanders.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Some Summer Islanders joined the pirates and became feared captains.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)
[Ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) from the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) have reaved in the Basilisk Isles.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Pirates from the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) and the Basilisks have fought the fleets of [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn).(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### Recent History[ ]

Powerful Gogossos fell into ruin after an outbreak of the [Red Death](wiki:Red_Death) during the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood), and the Basilisks were shunned for a century until corsairs returned. [Xandarro Xhore](wiki:Xandarro_Xhore) of [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) eventually built a black fort at [Ax Isle](wiki:Ax_Isle), while the [Brotherhood of Bones](wiki:Brotherhood_of_Bones) settled on the [Isle of Flies](wiki:Isle_of_Flies). In the aftermath of the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), the islands became nests for [pirates](wiki:Pirates) who attacked merchants attempting to avoid the [Valyrian peninsula](wiki:Valyrian_peninsula) and [Smoking Sea](wiki:Smoking_Sea).(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) Slavers from the Basilisks have raided [Naath](wiki:Naath) so frequently since the Century of Blood that the [Naathi](wiki:Naathi) have largely moved inland.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_naath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
[Volantis](wiki:Volantis) and other [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) periodically send fleets to clear out these pirates, with varying success. Instead of suppressing them, [Saathos Saan](wiki:Saathos_Saan) of [Lys](wiki:Lys) joined the pirates and reigned as [King of the Basilisk Isles](wiki:King_of_the_Basilisk_Isles) for three decades.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8)
[Harwyn Hoare](wiki:Harwyn_Hoare) spent two years in the isles as the captive of a pirate king.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)
The author of [A Caution for Young Girls](wiki:A_Caution_for_Young_Girls) claims to have spent time as the plaything of a corsair queen in the isles.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
[Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy) raided the pirate towns of the Basilisks with his uncle when he was fifteen.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_red_kraken.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
Highborn maidens from Volantis sailing to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to attend the [Maiden's Day Cattle Show](wiki:Maiden's_Day_Cattle_Show) for King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) were captured en route by corsairs from the Basilisks.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) [Racallio Ryndoon](wiki:Racallio_Ryndoon) fled to the isles after the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War).(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

According to [Haldon Halfmaester](wiki:Haldon_Halfmaester), the current [Shrouded Lord](wiki:Shrouded_Lord) is a corsair from the Basilisk Islands.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

[Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) gives [Red Ralf Stonehouse](wiki:Red_Ralf_Stonehouse) the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet)'s swiftest ships to sail the [corsair's road](wiki:Corsair's_road) along the northern coast of [Sothoryos](wiki:Sothoryos) and seek provisions through the [iron price](wiki:Iron_price) from towns in the Basilisk Isles. Victarion gave orders to reunite off the southern tip of the [Isle of Cedars](wiki:Isle_of_Cedars); only nine of the ships that had sailed with Ralf turn up, with Ralf himself among the missing.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

## Quotes

Named for the [fierce beasts that once infested them](wiki:Basilisk), the Basilisks have for long centuries been the festering sore of the [Summer Sea](wiki:Summer_Sea), inhabited only by corsairs, [pirates](wiki:Pirates), [slavers](wiki:Slavers), [sellswords](wiki:Sellswords), murderers, and monsters, the worst of humanity. They come from every land beneath the sun, it is said, for only here can such men hope to find others of their own ilk.(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9)
The swiftest ships he gave to [Red Ralf Stonehouse](wiki:Ralf_Stonehouse) to sail the [corsair's road](wiki:Corsair's_road) along the northern coast of [Sothoryos](wiki:Sothoryos). The dead cities rotting on that fervid, sweltering shore were best avoided, every seamen knew, but in the mud-and-blood towns of the Basilisks Isles, teeming with escaped [slaves](wiki:Slaves), slavers, [skinners](wiki:Flaying), [whores](wiki:Whores), hunters, [brindled men](wiki:Brindled_men), and worse, there were always provisions to be had for men who were not afraid to pay the [iron price](wiki:Iron_price).(wiki:Basilisk_Isles.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)
