## Origins

### Antiquity[ ]

According to legend, the Neck turned into a swamp when [greenseers](wiki:Greenseers) used the [hammer of the waters](wiki:Hammer_of_the_waters). Some [maesters](wiki:Maesters) instead believe the landscape was caused by a natural event.(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
The Neck was originally an independent realm ruled by the [Marsh Kings](wiki:Marsh_King), until King [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark_(king)) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) defeated the last Marsh King and married his daughter, annexing the Neck to the domain of the [Kings of Winter](wiki:King_in_the_North).(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) was able to preserve northern independence by holding the Neck and [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin). Some [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) may have fled to the Neck during the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals).(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
King [Benedict the Just](wiki:Benedict_I_Justman), founder of [House Justman](wiki:House_Justman), extended his domains to border the Neck during his long reign.(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### Targaryen Era[ ]

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) marched an army of thirty thousand south through the Neck to the [Trident](wiki:Trident),(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) but he ultimately choose to kneel in submission to [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
Orchards were planted throughout southern Westeros from the [Reach](wiki:Reach) to the Neck during the reign of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) The right of the [first night](wiki:First_night), an ancient custom of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), was rarely practiced south of the Neck during Jaehaerys's time, but it was more common in the [north](wiki:North). Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) convinced Jaehaerys to outlaw the first night throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) Jaehaerys commanded the construction of the kingsroad to connect [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) with the [Wall](wiki:Wall),(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain..7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) with the portion running through the Neck becoming known as the [causeway](wiki:Causeway).(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)
During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) marched a great host down the Neck to oppose [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) in support of first [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and then her son, [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen).(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) called the northern banners during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) and marched south against King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

The retinue of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) traverses the Neck by taking the [causeway](wiki:Causeway); the southern leg takes twelve days of travel.(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)
[Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) sends word to Lord [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed) to have the [crannogmen](wiki:Crannogmen) defend the Neck against the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Robb's host of [northmen](wiki:North) marches from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin), and then through the Neck to the [Twins](wiki:Twins).(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Avoiding the Neck, the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) under [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) sail the [Fever River](wiki:Fever_River) and [seize Moat Cailin](wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin) from the north, claiming it for [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

As part of his plan to [retake Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin) after the death of Balon, Robb orders [Galbart Glover](wiki:Galbart_Glover) and [Maege Mormont](wiki:Maege_Mormont) to [Seagard](wiki:Seagard). They are then to sail up the Neck on [Mallister](wiki:House_Mallister) longships to find [Greywater Watch](wiki:Greywater_Watch) so that the crannogmen can direct Robb's army around the rear of the ancient fortress.(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Robb's plan is ruined by the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), however.

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

[Black Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman)) tracks a [hooded woman](wiki:Catelyn_Stark)'s [outlaws](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) north of [Hag's Mire](wiki:Hag's_Mire) until they disappear into the Neck.(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

The ironborn garrison at Moat Cailin is weakened by guerrilla warfare conducted by the crannogmen. The host of Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) travels through the Neck and past Moat Cailin after it is [surrendered](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin) to his son, [Ramsay](wiki:Ramsay_Snow).(wiki:Neck.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)
