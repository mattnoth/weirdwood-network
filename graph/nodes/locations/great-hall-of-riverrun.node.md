## Appearances & Description

The Great Hall contains the high seat of [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully).(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) The hall has timber rafters,(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) and a gallery leads from its rear.(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) The castle's garrison can take their meals(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) in the great stone hall.(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
There is a small, private audience chamber above the Great Hall with another high seat for the [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun) and a bell to ring for servants.(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Seated on a bench in the Great Hall, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) tells the [Tully garrison](wiki:House_Tully_guards) about the [battle in the Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood).(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
[Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) holds a war council in the aftermath of the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), with [northmen](wiki:Northmen) and [river lords](wiki:Riverlands) sitting in the Great Hall at four long trestle tables arranged in a broken square. [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully), [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident), is ill, so Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) sits the high seat instead. The gathered nobles eventually proclaim Robb to be [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North).(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

In the Great Hall, King Robb publicly announces his peace offer to Ser [Cleos Frey](wiki:Cleos_Frey), who will deliver the message to Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Lord [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark), angry at the deaths of his sons [Eddard](wiki:Eddard_Karstark) and [Torrhen Karstark](wiki:Torrhen_Karstark) at the hands of Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) in the Whispering Wood, disapproves of making peace and instead departs the hall.(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) eats with the garrison in the Great Hall. [Rymund the Rhymer](wiki:Rymund_the_Rhymer) sings during each course, including a performance of "[Wolf in the Night](wiki:Wolf_in_the_Night)".(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
While the rest of Riverrun celebrates Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully)'s victory on the fords and Robb's [conquest of the Crag](wiki:Storming_of_the_Crag), Catelyn and [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) eat by themselves in the Great Hall.(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Catelyn and Brienne release Jaime from captivity at Riverrun in hopes he can be exchanged for Catelyn's daughters.(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
After returning to Riverrun, Robb praises Edmure in the Great Hall for his defense of the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork). When the king greets his mother, Catelyn, Lord Karstark leaves the hall in anger. Catelyn meets the [Westerlings](wiki:House_Westerling) after most nobles depart the hall.(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) In the audience chamber above the Great Hall, Robb and Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully) criticize an unknowing Edmure for having ruined the young king's attempt to trap Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands).(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
Lord Karstark kills two of Robb's prisoners, [Willem Lannister](wiki:Willem_Lannister) and [Tion Frey](wiki:Tion_Frey), after having been denied vengeance against Jaime. Robb has the victims' bodies brought before him in the torch-lit Great Hall, where Rickard openly challenges his authority. Robb orders Rickard's conspirators to be hanged, and he beheads Lord Karstark the next day in Riverrun's [godswood](wiki:Godswood).(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
Robb meets with [Lothar Frey](wiki:Lothar_Frey) and [Walder Rivers](wiki:Walder_Rivers) at supper.(wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
