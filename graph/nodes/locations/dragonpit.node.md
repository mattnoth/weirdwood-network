## Appearances & Description

The Dragonpit is a huge domed castle(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) at the crown of [Rhaenys's Hill](wiki:Rhaenys's_Hill).(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) The main gate consisted of massive doors, mainly [bronze](wiki:Bronze) with some [iron](wiki:Iron),(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) and were so wide that thirty [knights](wiki:Knight) could ride through it at once.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) A score of lesser entrances were also present, some of them were [oak](wiki:Oak)-and-iron doors.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) The building's walls were thick and the roof strong.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
A huge dome was constructed above the pit.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Benches were located in the pit itself, which can seat eighty thousand.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)
Within the structure, long brick-lined tunnels have been dug deep into the hillside, fashioned like caves, five times as large as the dragon's lairs on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)).(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Beneath the dome, there were forty huge undervaults which had been carved in a great ring. These man-made caves were closed off at both sides by thick iron doors. The inner doors opened on the sands of the pit itself, and the outer doors opened to the hillside.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
When living dragons still nested beneath the dome, light would shine through the windows at night.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
During the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the Dragonpit was destroyed. The huge dome is now broken,(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) the building ruined, the pit's walls blackened by fire.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) The pit has been abandoned for a century and a half,(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) and its huge doors have been sealed for a century.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

## Origins

### Construction[ ]

In [42 AC](wiki:42_AC), during the early phase of the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) used the [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame) of [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) in the [burning of the Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Burning_of_the_Sept_of_Remembrance) on the top of [Rhaenys's Hill](wiki:Rhaenys's_Hill). Three years later, shortly after the completion of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s construction, Maegor ordered the construction of the Dragonpit, which he called "a great stone stable for [dragons](wiki:Dragon)".(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Because Maegor had killed all those who had built the Red Keep to hide its secrets, many of those hired to construct the building fled. Maegor eventually had to use city prisoners, with supervisors from [Myr](wiki:Myr) and [Volantis](wiki:Volantis).(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)
Construction of the Dragonpit continued during the reign of Maegor's successor, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), initially led by Jaehaerys's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). In [49 AC](wiki:49_AC), Rogar [married](wiki:Golden_Wedding) the [Queen Regent](wiki:Regent), [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), in the Dragonpit, witnessed by forty thousand people.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) Rogar wished to complete the Dragonpit before Jaehaerys came [of age](wiki:Age_of_majority) and would start to rule on his own, but found himself lacking the proper funds to continue construction by [50 AC](wiki:50_AC).(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) After Jaehaerys assumed rule on his own, his newly-appointed [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), [Rego Draz](wiki:Rego_Draz), secured funds by loaning gold from the banks of [Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos), [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) and [Myr](wiki:Myr).(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) By [52 AC](wiki:52_AC), King Jaehaerys would often visit the Dragonpit to keep an eye on the progress of its construction.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Eventually, due to all of the taxes imposed by the master of coin to pay for the Dragonpit's completion and the other improvements on King's Landing, Rego's popularity plummeted significantly.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)
Construction on the pit was finally completed in [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and [a great tourney](wiki:Tourney_for_the_completion_of_the_Dragonpit) was held in celebration. Ser [Lucamore Strong](wiki:Lucamore_Strong), the victor of the melee, was rewarded with the white cloak of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### First uses[ ]

When [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) returned to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) in [56 AC](wiki:56_AC), King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) had the dragon confined to the Dragonpit. He also created a new order of seventy-seven warriors, the [Dragonkeepers](wiki:Dragonkeepers), to guard the royal dragons.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) Three younger dragons were soon housed in the Dragonpit along with Balerion, though Jaehaerys and Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) kept their own dragons, [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor) and [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing), at the Red Keep.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)
By [72 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [Caraxes](wiki:Caraxes) was considered the fiercest of the young dragons living at the Dragonpit. In [75 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Princess [Alyssa Targaryen](wiki:Alyssa_Targaryen) claimed [Meleys](wiki:Meleys) as her mount in the Dragonpit. In [84 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Princess [Saera Targaryen](wiki:Saera_Targaryen) attempted to sneak into the Dragonpit, but was caught by the Dragonkeepers and returned to the Red Keep.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) In [93 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) claimed Balerion from the Dragonpit.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)
King Jaehaerys I was cremated in the Dragonpit in [103 AC](wiki:103_AC).(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### Dance of the Dragons[ ]

In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC) at the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) was [crowned in the Dragonpit](wiki:Coronation_of_Aegon_II_Targaryen). According to Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun), more than a hundred thousand [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) were jammed into the building, while the fool [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) claims the stone benches were only half-filled.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)
During the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) to Aegon II's rival, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), the dragon [Sheepstealer](wiki:Sheepstealer) landed atop of the Dragonpit.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
When Rhaenyra ruled the city, the Dragonpit housed nine dragons: [Caraxes](wiki:Caraxes), [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor), [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing), and Sheepstealer, who eventually flew off to battle, and [Shrykos](wiki:Shrykos), [Morghul](wiki:Morghul), [Tyraxes](wiki:Tyraxes), and [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre). The Dragonpit became the site of executions of traitors, rebels and murderers. For the price of three pennies, the people of the city were allowed to watch criminals be beheaded and their corpses fed to Rhaenyra's dragons.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) By [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), it had become the custom that at least one [dragonrider](wiki:Dragonrider) resided in the Dragonpit, to rise to the defense of the city should this be necessary.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)
Near the end of Rhaenyra's hold over the city, the five dragons that remained in the city were killed during the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit), when tens of thousands of crazed and starving smallfolk led by the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd) stormed the Dragonpit to kill the dragons. All four dragons remaining inside, as well as Rhaenyra's [Syrax](wiki:Syrax), were killed in the struggle, as well as thousands of smallfolk. The Dragonpit was reduced to flaming ruins.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)
During the [Moon of the Three Kings](wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings), the Shepherd held sway over tens of thousands of people from the ruins of the Dragonpit. The heads of the five slain dragons were displayed on posts, between which the Shepherd preached every night.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) Two days after Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) reclaimed the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) in the name of Aegon II, the Shepherd was arrested amidst the ruins of the Dragonpit.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) After Aegon II returned to the city, he commanded the Dragonpit be restored and rebuilt.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)

### Ruined Dragonpit[ ]

Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) in the first years of [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen)'s reign, continued the restoration of the Dragonpit by setting hundreds of stonemasons, carpenters, and builders to work.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
By [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), the dragon [Morning](wiki:Morning) had made its lair in the ruined Dragonpit.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
During the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness), so many people from King's Landing died so quickly that there was no time to bury the bodies. Instead they were piled up in the Dragonpit, and when the corpses were ten feet deep, the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), ordered the [pyromancers](wiki:Pyromancer) to burn them. The light of the fires shone through the windows, and by night citizens could see the dark green glow of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) all through [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)
King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) had [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) stored underneath the Dragonpit, as part of his [wildfire plot](wiki:Wildfire_plot) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Aboard the [Storm Dancer](wiki:Storm_Dancer) as she is about to dock in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) takes in the view of the capital. Across the city she can see atop [Rhaenys's Hill](wiki:Rhaenys's_Hill) the blackened walls of the Dragonpit, its huge dome collapsed in ruin.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Some [whores](wiki:Whore) use the Dragonpit as a place to entertain their customers and one of them, along with their patron, falls through the rotting floor into a cellar. There they find a hidden stash of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire), placed by Lord [Rossart](wiki:Rossart) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Aboard the [Balerion](wiki:Balerion_(ship)), [Arstan Whitebeard](wiki:Arstan_Whitebeard) tells Queen [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) about the Dragonpit.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

In [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), just before attempting to steal [Rhaegal](wiki:Rhaegal) and [Viserion](wiki:Viserion) from the [Great Pyramid](wiki:Great_Pyramid), Prince [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell) recalls reading that the Dragonpit had restricted the size of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen)'s dragons.(wiki:Dragonpit.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
