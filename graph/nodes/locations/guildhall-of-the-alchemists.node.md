## Appearances & Description

Made of black [marble](wiki:Marble), the Guildhall is is a warren of twists and turns. Outside, it has broad curving steps that front on the [Street of the Sisters](wiki:Street_of_the_Sisters).(wiki:Guildhall_of_the_Alchemists.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)
The Gallery of the Iron Torches is the receiving area for guests. It is a long chamber with black metal columns which can be lit with [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire). These [iron](wiki:Iron) torches are rarely lit, usually save for important guests. The walls and floor of polished black marble allow the green flames to reflect on their surface and bathe the hall in emerald radiance.(wiki:Guildhall_of_the_Alchemists.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Wildfire, the material that makes the Alchemists' Guild infamous, is prepared in bare stone cells by trained acolytes. Above each cell is a room filled with sand. The floor below has [protective enchantments](wiki:Magic) placed upon it. Any fire within the cell will cause both floors to fall and the sand to drop, smothering the fire instantly.(wiki:Guildhall_of_the_Alchemists.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Two vaults are found behind the Guildhall of the Alchemists, under the [Hill of Rhaenys](wiki:Hill_of_Rhaenys). The upper vault has damp stone walls splotchy with nitre. Once a pot of wildfire has been readied by an acolyte, an apprentice carries it to the upper vault. Pots are then placed on tables arranged in orderly rows. Older pots of wildlfire from the reign of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) are stored in the lower vault, which has been pumped full of water.(wiki:Guildhall_of_the_Alchemists.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Visitors to the upper vault are advised to dress warmly, as heat is avoided and the only light available comes from carried sealed iron-and-glass oil lamps. The vaults are accessible from a cellar of the guildhall.(wiki:Guildhall_of_the_Alchemists.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

## Origins

The last [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to visit the Guildhall of the Alchemists was Lord [Rossart](wiki:Rossart) during the reign of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Guildhall_of_the_Alchemists.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) tours the Guildhall of the Alchemists and the upper vault as part of his inspection to make sure the preparations for the defense of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) are coming along at the proper pace. Wisdom [Hallyne](wiki:Hallyne) hopes to one day host King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) at the guildhall.(wiki:Guildhall_of_the_Alchemists.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
[Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom) is set afire during the [riots in King's Landing](wiki:Riots_in_King's_Landing). Tyrion tasks [Bronn](wiki:Bronn) and [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) with having the water wagons protected so that the fire cannot reach the Guildhall of the Alchemists.(wiki:Guildhall_of_the_Alchemists.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
