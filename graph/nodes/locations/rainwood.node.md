## Origins

### House Durrandon[ ]

The rainwood was once part of a primeval forest which also included what is now the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood) and was inhabited by [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest). The children tolerated the building of [First Men](wiki:First_Men) villages along the forest's streams, but conflict broke out over the First Men's use of timber.(wiki:Rainwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
[Durran Godsgrief](wiki:Durran_Godsgrief) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) claimed the forest for the First Men, leading to generations of warfare between [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon) and the children. The [woods witch](wiki:Woods_witch) known as the [Green Queen](wiki:Green_Queen) held the forest for almost a generation.(wiki:Rainwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_durrandon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
Much of the rainwood was conquered during the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals), but King [Baldric I Durrandon](wiki:Baldric_I_Durrandon) encouraged the [Andal](wiki:Andal) warlords to fight amongst each other. The children of the forest gradually disappeared from the rainwood after the First Men and Andals made peace.(wiki:Rainwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### House Targaryen[ ]

Half of the rainwood burned in [8 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) after an attack by [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) during the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War).(wiki:Rainwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
Drought during the reign of King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen) led to fires in the rainwood.(wiki:Rainwood.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
Among King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen)'s unrealized grandiose schemes was the idea to bring water from the rainwood to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) via a canal beneath the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains).(wiki:Rainwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) receives the title "[Lord of the Rainwood](wiki:Lord_of_the_Rainwood)" from King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Rainwood.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Ser [Dermot of the Rainwood](wiki:Dermot) tells knightly stories of the forest while at [Darry](wiki:Darry).(wiki:Rainwood.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

During the [landing of the Golden Company](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company), their gathering point is a deserted coastal stretch near the edge of the rainwood. The [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) then marches against nearby [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost).(wiki:Rainwood.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### The Winds of Winter[ ]

At [Ghost Hill](wiki:Ghost_Hill) [Nymella Toland](wiki:Nymella_Toland) and [Valena Toland](wiki:Valena_Toland) tell Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) there is talk of [elephants](wiki:Elephant) in the rainwood.(wiki:Rainwood.cite_ref-Ar1_16-0)
Arianne and her party travel through the rainwood on the way to the [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost). While staying overnight in a cave, they find a cavern with stone columns decorated with faces; Ser [Daemon Sand](wiki:Daemon_Sand) explains it was once populated by the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest). The travelers find that [Mistwood](wiki:Mistwood) has been occupied by the Golden Company, and the sellswords escort them to Griffin's Roost.(wiki:Rainwood.cite_ref-Ar2_6-3)
