## Identity

The Narrow Sea is the body of water that separates the continent of Westeros to the west from the continent of Essos to the east. It functions as the primary maritime boundary between the Seven Kingdoms and the Free Cities, and is described as "the vast continent across the narrow sea" in reference to Essos. The sea is characterized by regular mercantile traffic: ships pass daily between Pentos and King's Landing, and Gulltown on the Vale coast serves as a major hub for exotic goods crossing from the Essosi side. Dragonstone, the westernmost outpost of the Valyrian Freehold before the Doom, is an island within the Narrow Sea. The Stepstones — a chain of rocky islands between Dorne and the Disputed Lands of Essos — lie at its southern end and historically control the sea lanes into and out of the sea itself.

Control of the Narrow Sea's trade lanes has been a recurring source of conflict throughout Westerosi and Essosi history. The Triarchy of Lys, Myr, and Tyrosh cleared pirate dens from the Stepstones in 96 AC to make the trade lanes safe, but later imposed tolls that drove resentment in the Seven Kingdoms, ultimately provoking the War for the Stepstones. During that conflict, Daemon Targaryen was crowned King of the Stepstones and the Narrow Sea in 109 AC, and five men followed him in that title before the sellsword kingdom ended. The Daughters' War of 131–133 AC engulfed the Narrow Sea again, disrupting trade at Duskendale, Gulltown, King's Landing, and Maidenpool. Warden and Admiral of the Narrow Sea are formal Westerosi titles tied to its defense.

The Narrow Sea also carries a deep symbolic weight as the barrier between the familiar Westerosi world and the alien eastern one. Characters routinely frame crossing it as a transformative or desperate act: Aegor Rivers commanded his golden skull be carried across the narrow sea when the Golden Company retook Westeros; exiled Blackfyre pretenders returned across it to launch successive rebellions; the Andals originally crossed it to escape Valyrian expansion in Andalos; and Dothraki culture regards the sea with fear, making Khal Drogo's pledge to cross "the black salt water" on wooden horses a mark of extraordinary commitment. Jaqen H'ghar uses the sea as a threshold when he invites Arya to travel east with him.
