## Origins

Hull arose from three modest fishing villages which grew together at the time in which [House Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) grew in wealth and power from the treasures brought by Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) from his voyages.(wiki:Hull.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_.E2.80.93_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) The Sea Snake frequently visited the town's shipyards.(wiki:Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

At the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Corlys the Sea Snake launched his ships from Hull and [Spicetown](wiki:Spicetown) to close the [Gullet](wiki:Gullet).(wiki:Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) [Addam](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) and [Alyn](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) were legitimized [bastards](wiki:Bastards) from the town. The boys' mother, [Marilda of Hull](wiki:Marilda_of_Hull), was a shipwright's daughter and a canny trader who owned seven ships by [130 AC](wiki:130_AC). Marilda claimed their father was Corlys's son, the late Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon), who had been known to visit Hull's shipyard on occasion. [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) claimed the brothers were actually Corlys's bastard sons, however. Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) legitimized the two as the Sea Snake's Velaryon heirs after Addam was able to ride the dragon [Seasmoke](wiki:Seasmoke). While Spicetown and High Tide were sacked during the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet), never to be rebuilt, Hull and Castle Driftmark on the opposite side of the island were unharmed.(wiki:Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Another [dragonseed](wiki:Dragonseed), [Nettles](wiki:Nettles), grew up on the streets of Hull and Spicetown.(wiki:Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

When King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) from [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC), Alyn had him transported on *[Mouse](wiki:Mouse)*, Marilda's old cog.(wiki:Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Before setting forth to confront the Red Kraken, Lord [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), Alyn Oakenfist gathered his fleet at Hull in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC).(wiki:Hull.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Hull prospered during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring), with scores of new ships built and Marilda expanded her trading fleets. She built above the harbor a palatial manse which Mushroom called the [Mouse House](wiki:Mouse_House).(wiki:Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

In [138 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) Lord Alyn entombed at Hull the bones of his brother Addam, which had been returned by Lord [Benjicot Blackwood](wiki:Benjicot_Blackwood) from [Raventree](wiki:Raventree_Hall). Addam had died in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC) during the [Second Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:Second_Battle_of_Tumbleton) after being called a traitor by Rhaenyra. Alyn had his brother's tomb engraved with the word *LOYAL* and decorated with carvings of a seahorse and a mouse.(wiki:Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
