## Origins

### Andal era

[House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) had served [House Mudd](wiki:House_Mudd) of [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones), but King [Tristifer IV Mudd](wiki:Tristifer_IV_Mudd) was killed during the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals). After the king's death, Lord [Axel Tully](wiki:Axel_Tully) constructed the red castle at the juncture of the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork) with the [Tumblestone](wiki:Tumblestone), land which he had received from an [Andal](wiki:Andal) king, [Armistead Vance](wiki:Armistead_Vance).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

The strategic position of Riverrun brought wealth to the Tullys, who guarded the western riverlands from the [Kingdom of the Rock](wiki:Kingdom_of_the_Rock). The castle has rarely been surrendered and has never fallen by storm.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_riverrun.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) The Tullys have ruled Riverrun and its rich lands for at least a thousand years.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

When [Harwyn Hoare](wiki:Harwyn_Hoare), [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands), invaded the riverlands, Riverrun was abandoned by Lord Tully, who joined the host of Lady [Agnes Blackwood](wiki:Agnes_Blackwood). Agnes's forces were betrayed by Lord [Lothar Bracken](wiki:Lothar_Bracken), however.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

### Targaryen Era

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest) a few generations later, Lord [Edmyn Tully](wiki:Edmyn_Tully) raised the standard of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) above Riverrun, beginning the rebellion of the river lords against Harwyn Hoare's grandson [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare), the [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers). Following the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal), King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) named Edmyn the first [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Aegon visited Riverrun during his royal progresses.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Prince [Maegor Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) was [knighted](wiki:Knight) by Aegon during a great tourney at Riverrun in [28 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) The rebellion of [Harren the Red](wiki:Harren_the_Red) began while King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) was guesting at Riverrun.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Aenys and Lord Tully gathered a thousand men to Riverrun before marching to Harrenhal.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)

House Tully supported King Maegor I against Aenys's son, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), in the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2) Riverrun turned against Maegor in favor of [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and the [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), however.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Lord [Prentys Tully](wiki:Prentys_Tully) and his wife, [Lucinda](wiki:Lucinda_Tully), hosted Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett), a leader of the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3)(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) When King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) visited Riverrun in [53 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [Jennis Templeton](wiki:Jennis_Templeton) held a [women's court](wiki:Women's_court) in the town on behalf of Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Prentys and Lucinda died from the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) at Riverrun in [59 AC](wiki:59_AC).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

The aged Lord [Grover Tully](wiki:Grover_Tully) wanted to support the [greens](wiki:Greens) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), but his grandson, Ser [Elmo Tully](wiki:Elmo_Tully), feared [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire) and instead barred their castle's gates.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) House Tully ended their neutrality when Ser [Addam Velaryon](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) visited Riverrun atop his dragon, [Seasmoke](wiki:Seasmoke), and Elmo and sons, [Kermit](wiki:Kermit_Tully) and [Oscar](wiki:Oscar_Tully), declared for the [blacks](wiki:Blacks).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) Elmo gathered near six thousand rivermen to Riverrun, and Kermit led them to victory in the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) [Widow Fairs](wiki:Widow_Fairs) were held at Riverrun after the war.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

### Recent History

[Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) spent part of his youth at Riverrun with the Tully siblings, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), and [Edmure](wiki:Edmure_Tully).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Retainers at Riverrun during Cat's youth included Maester [Kym](wiki:Kym) and Septon [Osmynd](wiki:Osmynd).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5) When Lord [Sumner Crakehall](wiki:Sumner_Crakehall) sent [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) to deliver a message to Riverrun, Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) unsuccessfully attempted to interest Jaime in his daughter, Lysa.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Lord [Andros Brax](wiki:Andros_Brax) once visited Riverrun in hopes of making an offer for the hand of Catelyn or Lysa for one of his sons.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-6) Lords [Tytos Blackwood](wiki:Tytos_Blackwood) and [Jonos Bracken](wiki:Jonos_Bracken) came to Riverrun to bring their feud before Hoster.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark) defeated Petyr in a duel over Catelyn in the lower bailey of the castle.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

During [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Lords [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) and [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) married Catelyn and Lysa, respectively, in a [double wedding](wiki:Weddings_of_Catelyn_and_Lysa_Tully) at Riverrun's [sept](wiki:Sept).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Ned and Cat's eldest child, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), was born at Riverrun nine months later(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) with the assistance of Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

[Jack-Be-Lucky](wiki:Jack-Be-Lucky) was once imprisoned in Riverrun's dungeons.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Riverrun](wiki:Category:Images_of_Riverrun)*

Riverrun is a strong three-sided castle, although not especially large. It is a tenth the size of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_.E2.80.94_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Riverrun is bordered on the north by the [Tumblestone](wiki:Tumblestone) and on the south by the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork), while on the west a third side faces a massive man-made ditch. In time of danger the sluice gates can be opened to fill a wide moat and leave the castle surrounded on all three sides by water, turning Riverrun into an island and leaving it practically unassailable. It commands a view of many leagues.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

The castle has red(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) sandstone walls which rise sheer from the water, its battlements are crenelated and have arrow loops, and its towers command the opposite shores.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Riverrun's keep is located inside.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Properly garrisoned, Riverrun can hold supplies for men and horses for as long as two years;(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) a garrison of two hundred men is larger than Riverrun requires in most circumstances.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) [Guardsmen](wiki:House_Tully_guards) wear fish-crest helms.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) The castle's doors are made of heavy redwood.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

The Wheel Tower has a great waterwheel turned by the Tumblestone. It has ivy climbing alongside it, and below one makes a wide turn and ends up in churning waters. The Tumblestone leads to the Water Gate, a wide arch and a heavy iron portcullis, red with rust in its lower half. It is named so for being half in the water, and one must use a boat to go through it. Many boats are tied up within the Water Gate, secured to iron rings in the walls. The water stair leads from the lower bailey up to the castle.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)

The keep is triangular, like Riverrun itself, and the [solar](wiki:Solar) of the [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun) is triangular as well with a triangular stone balcony jutting eastwards. The solar can be reached by a spiral stairway.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) The pillars of Hoster's great canopied bed are carved as leaping trout.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

The [Great Hall of Riverrun](wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun) contains the high seat of the Tullys and can host councils and meals.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3)(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) There is a private audience chamber above the Great Hall with another high seat for the lord and a bell to ring for servants.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)

The shortest way from the Great Hall to the keep is through [the godswood](wiki:Godswood_of_Riverrun),(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) a bright and airy garden(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) with elms, redwoods, wildflowers, nesting birds, and streams.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Its [heart tree](wiki:Heart_tree) is a slender [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood), with a carved sad face.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-4)

The [sept](wiki:Sept) at Riverrun is a seven-sided sandstone building amidst the gardens of the late [Minisa Tully](wiki:Minisa_Tully). Inside, images of [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) are painted on [marble](wiki:Marble).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) A rainbow of light fills the sept.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

The dungeons of Riverrun are windowless(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) and damp,(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) their doors heavy and made of wood and iron.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) The castle also contains kennels,(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) stables,(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) and a [brewhouse](wiki:Beer).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

With Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) ill at the start of the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) is defeated by Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) in a [battle under the walls of Riverrun](wiki:Battle_under_the_walls_of_Riverrun).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) The castle is then besieged by a [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) host commanded by Jaime. It is relieved in the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps) by a [Stark](wiki:House_Stark) force commanded by [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), Lord of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)

In the [Great Hall of Riverrun](wiki:Great_Hall_of_Riverrun), Robb is elevated to [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) by his [northern bannerman](wiki:Northmen) as well as the [river lords](wiki:Rivermen), who were never ruled by the old Kings of Winter.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-5)

### A Clash of Kings

Lord Hoster's [solar](wiki:Solar) is used as his [sickroom](wiki:Sickroom).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

Robb allows the gathered riverlords to disperse from Riverrun to defend their own lands.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) When Robb, the [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident), leaves the riverlands to attack the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), he leaves Edmure to protect Riverrun.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), the acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), sends a group of envoys to Riverrun. Some are involved in a [plot to free Jaime Lannister](wiki:Plot_to_free_Jaime_Lannister), a prisoner since the [battle in the Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood), but these false envoys are hanged at the castle when the rescue attempt fails.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)

Edmure admits [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) within the walls of Riverrun to protect them from war. The knight regathers riverlords(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-3) to defend the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork) from Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s army,(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-7) with [Blackwoods](wiki:House_Blackwood) and [Mallisters](wiki:House_Mallister) encamped between the rivers and [Darrys](wiki:House_Darry), [Paeges](wiki:House_Paege), and [Pipers](wiki:House_Piper) established north of the [Tumblestone](wiki:Tumblestone).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-4) After Tywin retreats from the [Battle of the Fords](wiki:Battle_of_the_Fords), Riverrun celebrates Edmure's victory and Robb's [storming of the Crag](wiki:Storming_of_the_Crag).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

Following the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) names [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) the [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident), the new liege lord of the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), with the castle of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) as his seat.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) However, the [Tullys](wiki:House_Tully) of Riverrun and their bannermen remain loyal to Robb.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

### A Storm of Swords

[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) hopes that the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) will escort her to her family at Riverrun,(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) but the outlaws instead bring her to Lord [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion) in the [hollow hill](wiki:Hollow_hill).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

Robb's mother, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), releases Jaime from Riverrun's dungeon in an attempt to exchange him with the Iron Throne for her daughters, [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark). Ser [Robin Ryger](wiki:Robin_Ryger), the captain of [Riverrun's guards](wiki:House_Tully_guards), tries to recover Jaime but is stopped by Jaime's escort, [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) Catelyn remains in house arrest in the chambers of her ailing father, Lord Hoster.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

Having been informed that Robb has married [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling) instead of one of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey)'s daughters, the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) at Riverrun angrily return to the [Twins](wiki:Twins).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

Livid with Catelyn's release of Jaime, Lord [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark) sends his riders from Riverrun in search of the escapee, and he murders two of Robb's captives, the squires [Willem Lannister](wiki:Willem_Lannister) and [Tion Frey](wiki:Tion_Frey). Robb beheads Rickard in the [castle's godswood](wiki:Godswood_of_Riverrun).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

Once Hoster dies, a funerary boat with his body is launched by Robb, [Tytos Blackwood](wiki:Tytos_Blackwood), [Jonos Bracken](wiki:Jonos_Bracken), [Jason Mallister](wiki:Jason_Mallister), [Marq Piper](wiki:Marq_Piper), [Karyl Vance](wiki:Karyl_Vance), and [Lothar Frey](wiki:Lothar_Frey).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

When Robb marches north to attend the wedding of Lord Edmure to [Roslin Frey](wiki:Roslin_Frey) at the [Twins](wiki:Twins), he leaves Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully) at Riverrun as the [Warden of the Southern Marches](wiki:Warden_of_the_Southern_Marches). Jeyne, Robb's queen, also remains at Riverrun.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

Edmure is captured and Robb is killed at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), however, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the Hand of the King, strips Riverrun from House Tully and grants it to Ser [Emmon Frey](wiki:Emmon_Frey), forming [House Frey of Riverrun](wiki:House_Frey_of_Riverrun). Brynden the Blackfish continues to hold the castle against a Frey siege.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Brynden expels the smallfolk from Riverrun and replenishes its provisions from the countryside.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) sends her brother, Jaime, to resolve the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) Since the siege has become a stalemate, Jaime takes command from Ser [Daven Lannister](wiki:Daven_Lannister) and Ser [Ryman Frey](wiki:Ryman_Frey).(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)

During a meeting at the castle's drawbridge, Brynden rejects Jaime's terms of surrender. After Jaime tells the captive Edmure he will sacrifice rivermen in an assault and launch Edmure's unborn child into the castle,(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) Edmure negotiates Riverrun's surrender from House Tully to House Frey.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Jaime resides in Lord Hoster's chambers, while Edmure is placed in another tower.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

Brynden escapes through the Water Gate, however, and presumably swims down the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork). Lord Emmon has a large garrison of two hundred to defend his new castle, while the Blackfish's garrison is allowed to depart without arms and armor. Robin Ryger and Ser [Desmond Grell](wiki:Desmond_Grell) agree to [take the black](wiki:Night's_Watch). Ser [Forley Prester](wiki:Forley_Prester) commands the escort taking Edmure and the [Westerlings](wiki:House_Westerling) from Riverrun to the westerlands. When snow falls at Riverrun, its people engage in a snowball war along the battlements. Maester [Vyman](wiki:Vyman) receives Cersei's request for aid from Jaime, but the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) refuses to answer his sister.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

### A Dance with Dragons

After resolving the [siege of Raventree](wiki:Siege_of_Raventree), Jaime is said to have disappeared while returning to Riverrun.(wiki:Riverrun.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)
