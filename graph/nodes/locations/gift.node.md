## Origins

### Brandon's Gift[ ]

A Brandon Stark, usually mentioned as [Bran the Builder](wiki:Bran_the_Builder) but sometimes another Brandon, gave the land up to twenty-five leagues south of the Wall to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) for their sustenance and support.(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Brandon's Gift was farmed for thousands of years, but nature reclaimed many buildings and orchards as the Night's Watch decreased in number.(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
Compared to lands further south, the soil of Brandon's Gift is thin and stony.(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) [Mole's Town](wiki:Mole's_Town) is located along the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) half a league south of [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black).(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

### New Gift[ ]

In [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and his sister-wife, Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), made a royal progress to the [north](wiki:North). Flying on her [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing), Alysanne visited [Alaric Stark](wiki:Alaric_Stark), the [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell) who warmed to the queen during her stay at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), and then [Lothor Burley](wiki:Lothor_Burley), the [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black).(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Holding the Night's Watch in great esteem,(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Alysanne felt Brandon's Gift was not sufficient to support the Night's Watch during her tour of the region. The queen suggested to Jaehaerys that he double the land by extending it a further twenty-five leagues south. Lord Alaric was wary of the idea of the New Gift, as the northmen who already held those lands would oppose having them taken away. According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), the charming Alysanne eventually persuaded Lord Stark to agree to the plan,(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) and it is said that the Starks were glad to aid the Watch.(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
According to Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), however, the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) were angered at being forced to surrender the territory, as they believed the Night's Watch would focus their attention [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall) and would neglect the New Gift. The brother of a Lord Stark sought legal guidance from the [maesters](wiki:Maesters) of the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) to avoid the forced donation to no avail. This resentment toward Jaehaerys and Alysanne might have contributed to Lord [Ellard Stark](wiki:Ellard_Stark) supporting Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) and [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)) in the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC).(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
The villages that exist in the New Gift pay their taxes in goods and labor to the Night's Watch, helping feed and provide food for the brothers. The New Gift contains many roundtowers, towerhouses, and holdfasts, such as [Queenscrown](wiki:Queenscrown).(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Of the [northern mountain clans](wiki:Northern_mountain_clans), the [Norreys](wiki:House_Norrey) live closest to the New Gift.(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

### Wildings[ ]

The [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) often enter the Gift to raid, steal, and carry off women of the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) In [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), for instance, three thousand wildling raiders led by [Sylas the Grim](wiki:Sylas_the_Grim) spread out across the Gift until being hunted down by Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark), [Flints](wiki:House_Flint_of_the_mountains), [Glovers](wiki:House_Glover), [Norreys](wiki:House_Norrey), and [rangers](wiki:Rangers).(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Because of the frequency of raids, many people have fled south into the mountains or the lands of [House Umber](wiki:House_Umber) east of the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad), leaving the villages and holdfasts abandoned.(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) has considered having new lords settle in the abandoned holdfasts of the Gift to protect against the raids. His brother [Benjen Stark](wiki:Benjen_Stark), the [First Ranger](wiki:First_Ranger), thinks the [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) would approve the idea if the new lords pay taxes to [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) instead of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), but Eddard believes the plan would have to wait until after winter.(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

The [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) through the Gift is used by [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) and [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow).(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

During their journey to find the [three-eyed crow](wiki:Three-eyed_crow), [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) and his companions seek refuge at [Queenscrown](wiki:Queenscrown) in the New Gift, which [Jojen Reed](wiki:Jojen_Reed) observes as having good land.(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) While they are hiding in the tower, they witness a fight between [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) and [Styr](wiki:Styr)'s party of [wildling](wiki:Free_folk) raiders.(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)
After the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall), [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) informs Jon that he intends to settle wildlings in the Gift, if they swear fealty.(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) The officers of the Watch react negatively when Stannis makes his request.(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Jon, the new [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), agrees to allow Stannis to settle [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) on the Gift,(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) but the [northern mountain clans](wiki:Northern_mountain_clans) are wary of the decision.(wiki:Gift.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
