## Origins

### Great Empire of the Dawn[ ]

According to scribes of [Yin](wiki:Yin), all the lands between the [Jade Sea](wiki:Jade_Sea) and the [Shivering Sea](wiki:Shivering_Sea), the [Bone Mountains](wiki:Bone_Mountains) and the [Grey Waste](wiki:Grey_Waste) were part of the [Great Empire of the Dawn](wiki:Great_Empire_of_the_Dawn) and fell under the control of the [God-on-Earth](wiki:God-on-Earth), the only son of The [Lion of Night](wiki:Lion_of_Night_(god)) and the [Maiden-Made-of-Light](wiki:Maiden-Made-of-Light). He is said to have had a hundred wives and to have reigned for ten thousand years, before ascending into the stars to join his forebears. He was succeeded by his eldest son, the [Pearl Emperor](wiki:Pearl_Emperor), who ruled for a thousand years. He was followed by the [Jade Emperor](wiki:Jade_Emperor), the [Tourmaline Emperor](wiki:Tourmaline_Emperor), the [Onyx Emperor](wiki:Onyx_Emperor), the [Topaz Emperor](wiki:Topaz_Emperor), and the [Opal Emperor](wiki:Opal_Emperor), who all reigned for centuries. Each successive ruler had a shorter reign and faced rising problems, as mankind grew greedy and corrupt.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-13)
The Opal Emperor was succeeded by his daughter, the [Amethyst Empress](wiki:Amethyst_Empress). Her younger brother slew her and usurped the throne, and event which is remembered as the Blood Betrayal. He proclaimed himself the [Bloodstone Emperor](wiki:Bloodstone_Emperor) and began a reign of terror, practicing dark magical arts and necromancy. He cast down the true gods to worship a black stone which had fallen from the sky. According to the annals of the east, the Blood Betrayal is attributed as the cause of the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night), as it caused the Maiden-Made-of-Light to despair and turn her face away from mankind, while the Lion of Night sent the generation-long winter to punish mankind. In the chaos that followed, the Great Empire of the Dawn collapsed.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-14)
According to [Colloquo Votar](wiki:Colloquo_Votar), as recounted in his [Jade Compendium](wiki:Jade_Compendium), a legend from Yi Ti claims that disaster during the Long Night was only averted by "the deeds of a woman with a monkey's tail".(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_long_night.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
The [maesters](wiki:Maester) of the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) doubt that the Great Empire of the Dawn ever truly existed, but most do agree that Yi Ti's culture is very ancient, and a few believe that they were contemporary with the [Fisher Queens](wiki:Fisher_Queens) on the western side of the Bone Mountains.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-15)

### Golden Empire[ ]

After the collapse of the [Great Empire of the Dawn](wiki:Great_Empire_of_the_Dawn), the Golden Empire of Yi Ti began. Since the end of the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night) eleven dynasties of emperors have ruled over Yi Ti, each known by a color honorific. Some of them ruled no more than half a century, but the longest ruled for seven hundred years. The transitions of power between dynasties did not always occur peacefully, and on four occasions, the end of a dynasty led to a prolonged period of civil war and anarchy. The longest of these lasted more than a hundred years.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-16)
Under the sea-green emperors, Yi Ti reached the apex of its power. During the reign of god-emperor [Jar Har](wiki:Jar_Har), [Leng](wiki:Leng) was conquered. His sons further expanded the power of Yi Ti; [Jar Joq](wiki:Jar_Joq) [Great Moraq](wiki:Great_Moraq), and [Jar Han](wiki:Jar_Han) exacted tribute from [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), [Old Ghis](wiki:Old_Ghis), [Asshai](wiki:Asshai), and other lands.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-17)
[Chai Duq](wiki:Chai_Duq), the fourth of the yellow emperors, married a noblewoman of [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), and kept a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) at his court.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-18)
During the past two thousand years, Yi Ti has often fought against raiders of the [Jogos Nhai](wiki:Jogos_Nhai), whose lands are to the north. According to Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), the raiders of the Jogos Nhai have reduced a dozen YiTish cities, a hundred towns, and farms and fields beyond counting to ruins. To combat these attacks, many imperial generals and three god-emperors invaded the [Plains of the Jogos Nhai](wiki:Plains_of_the_Jogos_Nhai), but these attacks usually did not end well, as the Jogos Nhai would usually evade capture for such long periods of time that the YiTish invaders would give up and go home.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-19)
[Lo Han](wiki:Lo_Han), forty-second scarlet emperor, invaded the plains three times without success, returning home after being unable to catch his enemies. After Lo Han's death, his son, [Lo Bu](wiki:Lo_Bu), assembled a mighty host, supposedly of three hundred thousand men, and led his force into the plains, leaving a burning wasteland behind everywhere he went. During his campaign, millions of Jogos Nhai were killed. During the last two years of the struggle, the rival clans of the Jogos Nhai united against Lo Bu under a woman called [Zhea](wiki:Zhea), whose riders eventually descended upon Lo Bu's host, slaughtering them all. Lo Bu was among those slain. Zhea had his head stripped of its flesh and his skull dipped in gold, so it could serve as her drinking cup. Every [jhattar](wiki:Jhattar) of the Jogos Nhai since Zhea has drunk fermented [zorse](wiki:Zorse) milk from Lo Bu's skull.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)
Around [100 BC](wiki:100_BC), the island [Leng](wiki:Leng) broke free of Yi Ti, although the northern two-thirds of the island are still under the control of the descendants of the original YiTish invaders.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)
On the first of his nine great voyages, [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) sailed to Yi Ti and Leng(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) on the [Sea Snake](wiki:Sea_Snake),(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rfabthe_heir_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) returning with such a large amount of spices, silks, and jade, which made [House Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) the wealthiest house in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) for a while.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)
In the present day, Yi Ti is ruled from [Yin](wiki:Yin) by the seventeenth azure emperor, [Bu Gai](wiki:Bu_Gai). However, the amount of power he has is weak compared to older god-emperors, and the local governors and tax-collectors have carved out a hundred princedoms among the provinces. In [Carcosa](wiki:Carcosa), on the [Hidden Sea](wiki:Hidden_Sea), an exiled sorcerer lord resides, who claims to be the 69th yellow emperor. In addition, a general called [Pol Qo](wiki:Pol_Qo) has named himself the first of the orange emperors, using [Trader Town](wiki:Trader_Town) as his capital.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-20)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

In the Western Market of [Vaes Dothrak](wiki:Vaes_Dothrak), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) sees a fat cloth trader from Yi Ti haggling with a [Pentoshi](wiki:Pentos) over the price of some green [dye](wiki:Hair_dye).(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)
Before Khal [Drogo](wiki:Drogo)'s body is burnt on his funeral pyre, Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) begs Daenerys to come east with him, to Yi Ti, [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), the [Jade Sea](wiki:Jade_Sea) and [Asshai-by-the-Shadow](wiki:Asshai), to see all the wonders yet unseen. (wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

In [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), [Xaro Xhoan Daxos](wiki:Xaro_Xhoan_Daxos) tries to entreat Daenerys to accompany him on his pleasure barge to the Jade Sea. He suggests that they journey around Yi Ti to search for the dreaming city of poets and to sip the [wine](wiki:Wine) of wisdom from a dead man's skull.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

In [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), Daenerys reminds Jorah that after Drogo's death he had asked her to go with him to Yi Ti and the Jade Sea.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

[Garin](wiki:Garin_(orphan)) tells Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) that according to sailors in [Planky Town](wiki:Planky_Town), the [grey plague](wiki:Grey_plague) has broken out in Yi Ti.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

When in the cellar of [Illyrio's manse](wiki:Illyrio's_manse) in Pentos, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) notices that [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis) has wines from the fabled east in his stock, including wines from Yi Ti.(wiki:Yi_Ti.cite_ref-Radwd1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)
