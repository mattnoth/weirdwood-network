## Origins

Hardhome was close to becoming the only true town north of the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Before its destruction, Maester [Wyllis](wiki:Wyllis) journeyed to Hardhome aboard a [Pentoshi](wiki:Pentos) trader and established himself as a healer and counselor under the protection of [Gorm the Wolf](wiki:Gorm_the_Wolf), one of the four chieftains who controlled the settlement, so that he may write of the local customs. However, Gorm was murdered and Wyllis, in mortal danger, fled back to Oldtown, where he wrote *[Hardhome: An Account of Three Years Spent Beyond-the-Wall among Savages, Raiders, and Woods-witches](wiki:Hardhome:_An_Account_of_Three_Years_Spent_Beyond-the-Wall_among_Savages,_Raiders,_and_Woods-witches)*.

Hardhome was abruptly destroyed six centuries ago.(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) Something terrible happened that night, though the details are uncertain. The homes of the inhabitants of Hardhome are said to have burned with flames so high and hot that the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) on the Wall thought that the sun was rising in from the north. Afterwards, ashes rained down on the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest) and the [Shivering Sea](wiki:Shivering_Sea) alike for almost half a year.(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) Hardhome's people are said to have been carried off into slavery by slavers from across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) or slaughtered for meat by [cannibals](wiki:Cannibalism) out of [Skagos](wiki:Skagos), depending on the tale one chooses to believe.(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_wildlings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

Traders investigating Hardhome reported a landscape of charred trees and burned bones, waters choked with swollen corpses, and shrieks echoing from the cave mouths.(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) A ship sent by the Night's Watch also reported the strange screams.(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_wildlings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

The free folk never again settled the site, which became overgrown by wilderness. [Rangers](wiki:Ranger) roaming north of the Wall told tales of Hardhome being haunted by [ghouls](wiki:Ghouls), [demons](wiki:Demon), and burning [ghosts](wiki:Ghosts) with an unhealthy taste for blood.(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)

[Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) sailed *[Ice Wolf](wiki:Ice_Wolf)* to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea), and Hardhome, then ventured east to [Lorath](wiki:Lorath) and the [Port of Ibben](wiki:Port_of_Ibben).(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

After [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder)'s force trying to get south of the [Wall](wiki:Wall) is defeated by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) and the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) in the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall), [Mother Mole](wiki:Mother_Mole) leads thousands of [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) to Hardhome. Mother Mole preaches to the free folk that they will find salvation where they once found damnation, and she claims to have had a vision of ships coming to Hardhome to sail them south.(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Radwdprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

[Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) may have seen a vision of Hardhome when she looks into the fire in her chambers at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black).(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

Lord Commander [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) sends [Cotter Pyke](wiki:Cotter_Pyke) with eleven ships from [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea) to bring the wildlings south from Hardhome. Many ships are wrecked by storms during the voyage. When Cotter arrives at Hardhome with six ships left, the situation has grown very bad. Wildlings are eating their own dead and there are presumably [wights](wiki:Wights) in the forest and in the sea. Slavers have already taken some of the wildlings to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), and the wildlings try to take *[Storm Crow](wiki:Storm_Crow)* by force which costs Cotter six of his crew. Cotter sends a [raven](wiki:Raven) to plead for help via land from Jon, since traveling by the sea is far too dangerous.(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)

In Braavos, [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) tells the [kindly man](wiki:Kindly_man) that she knows why the [Sealord of Braavos](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos) seized the *[Goodheart](wiki:Goodheart)* and that the slaves on the ship are wildlings from Hardhome. She tells him that it is an old ruined place, accursed. Arya remembers [Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan)'s tales of Hardhome.(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

While [Selyse Florent](wiki:Selyse_Florent) and [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh) advocate letting those at Hardhome die, Jon intends to lead a ranging by land. After Jon receives [a letter](wiki:Bastard_Letter), [Tormund](wiki:Tormund) instead receives command of the planned ranging.(wiki:Hardhome.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
