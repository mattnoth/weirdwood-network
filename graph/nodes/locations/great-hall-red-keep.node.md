## Appearances & Description

The Great Hall is the largest hall in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), aside from the [Hall of a Hundred Hearths](wiki:Hall_of_a_Hundred_Hearths) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) The [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s throne room can feast a thousand men, while the [Small Hall](wiki:Small_Hall) in the nearby [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand), in comparison, can seat two hundred.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
The cavernous hall has high, narrow windows and a gallery.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Tall [oak](wiki:Oak)-and-[bronze](wiki:Bronze) doors and the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) are located at opposite ends of the long hall,(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) which has a carpet.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) A table for the [small council](wiki:Small_council) is located beneath the towering chair.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Behind the throne is an exit for the king,(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) with the practice yard nearby.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) often stand guard at the foot of the Iron Throne, and household guards and [gold cloaks](wiki:Gold_cloaks) can be aligned along the walls.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)
[Dragon skulls](wiki:Skulls_of_the_Targaryen_dragons) were displayed on the walls of the throne room during the reign of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), but King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) had them replaced with banners and hunting tapestries after [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

## Origins

When Grand Maester [Myros](wiki:Myros) spoke against the intention of [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) to wed [Tyanna of Pentos](wiki:Tyanna_of_Pentos), the king beheaded him with [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre) in the throne room.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Once the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) began to turn against Maegor the Cruel, he was found dead on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Some conjectured that Maegor had been assassinated by culprits who had accessed the throne room by a hidden passage.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)
During the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding), King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) met with visitors in his solar instead of the great hall.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) and Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), the king's mother, held their wedding feast in the throne room.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) When Jaehaerys made peace with Rogar after a later quarrel, they celebrated with a feast in the great hall.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
After losing two fingers to cuts from the Iron Throne, King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) held court in first his solar and then his bedchamber instead of in the throne room.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) sat the Iron Throne at the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons),(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) but Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) then claimed it after Aegon fled during the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) According to Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), the armor-clad Rhaenyra was cut by the throne and dripped blood as she left the hall, allegedly a symbol she had been rejected by the Iron Throne.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)
The [Maiden's Day Ball](wiki:Maiden's_Day_Ball) was held in the throne room during the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III).(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
In the aftermath of Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen)'s abduction of [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), the executions of her father [Rickard](wiki:Rickard_Stark) and brother [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark) lead to [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) had Lord Rickard suspended from the throne room's rafters and burned to death by his [pyromancers](wiki:Pyromancers), and Brandon was strangled while trying to save his father.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) During the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) killed Aerys, whom he was sworn to protect,(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) to prevent the Mad King's [wildfire plot](wiki:Wildfire_plot).(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) When Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) entered the hall, he found Jaime seated on the Iron Throne and Aerys's body on the floor.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

While King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) is away hunting in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) hears petitions from the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) as Robert's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)
[Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) succeeds to the throne after Robert's death. Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), Robert's widow and Joffrey's mother, has Lord Stark [arrested in the throne room](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark).(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)
The hunting tapestries are removed from the throne room's walls. Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) is publicly dismissed as [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) during Joffrey's first court session.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) while his father [Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) is on campaign during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), holds court and hears from Ser [Cleos Frey](wiki:Cleos_Frey) and Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne).(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)
While in the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying) in [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) has a vision of her late father, King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), speaking to [a man](wiki:Rossart)(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) from atop the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in a cavernous stone hall.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)
After the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Joffrey holds court and grants honors to his supporters.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

After Joffrey weds [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) in the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), their wedding feast is held in the throne room. The boy king [chokes to death](wiki:Purple_Wedding) during the celebration, however.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Accused of having murdered Joffrey, Tyrion is put on trial in the throne room.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) forbids young King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) from sitting on the Iron Throne or attending meetings of the [small council](wiki:Small_council).(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Cersei sits on a seat below the throne while holding court.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), now Tommen's Lord Regent, and the small council hold court in the throne room. Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell), the new Hand, sits on an oaken throne carved in the shape of a hand.(wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep).cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)
