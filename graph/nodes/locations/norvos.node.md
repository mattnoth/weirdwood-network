## Origins

### Foundation

The original settlers of Norvos are unknown, but they were not [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar). Some theorize they were [mazemakers](wiki:Mazemakers) or [Ibbenese](wiki:Ibbenese), but most believe they were [Andals](wiki:Andals). [Hairy men](wiki:Hairy_men) out of the east are said to have driven these original villagers away, but these hairy men were eventually expelled by Prince [Garris](wiki:Garris) of [Ny Sar](wiki:Ny_Sar). The Rhoynar did not remain, however, preferring instead the warmer lower [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne).(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14)

Modern Norvos, like [Qohor](wiki:Qohor) and [Lorath](wiki:Lorath), was founded following a religious schism in the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold). Dissidents found the religious tolerance of the Freehold intolerable, so the sect of the [bearded priests](wiki:Bearded_priests) founded Norvos as a theocracy where the "true faith" would be practiced.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15)(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

### Recent History

Norvos came into conflict with [Lorath](wiki:Lorath) when [Qarlon the Great](wiki:Qarlon_the_Great), trying to become [King of All Andals](wiki:King_of_All_Andals), expanded his domain to the [Axe](wiki:Axe) and the headwaters of the [Upper Rhoyne](wiki:Upper_Rhoyne) and [Noyne](wiki:Noyne). The Norvoshi closed the river, and Qarlon came forth to attack them. Two battles were lost by Norvos in the hills, leading Qarlon to march against the city. According to *[The Fires of the Freehold](wiki:The_Fires_of_the_Freehold)*, a hundred [dragons](wiki:Dragon) from the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) descended upon the [Andals](wiki:Andals) laying siege to Norvos. Qarlon was burned with his army, and Lorath followed shortly thereafter, in the [Scouring of Lorath](wiki:Scouring_of_Lorath).(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

During the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood) after the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), Norvos and [Qohor](wiki:Qohor) allied together against [Volantis](wiki:Volantis). They destroyed much of the Volantene fleet which had control of the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne) in a battle involving fire galleys on [Dagger Lake](wiki:Dagger_Lake).(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Since then, though the bearded priests are known to regard the [Black Goat of Qohor](wiki:Black_Goat_of_Qohor) as a demon, Norvos and Qohor have been allies more often than they have been enemies.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) and Lady [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon) visited Norvos and other [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) after their wedding.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

[Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne) has visited Norvos,(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) and he is married to Lady [Mellario](wiki:Mellario) of Norvos, with whom he had three children.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Doran is guarded by [Areo Hotah](wiki:Areo_Hotah), a Norvoshi who was trained by the [bearded priests](wiki:Bearded_priests).(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6) Already angered by the fostering of Prince [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell) at [Yronwood](wiki:Yronwood), Mellario became estranged from Doran and returned to Norvos when her husband planned to send Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) to [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh).(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_doran_martell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Areo remained with Doran at [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), however.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)

## Appearances & Description

### Location

Norvos sits among the [Hills of Norvos](wiki:Hills_of_Norvos), upon the eastern banks of the river [Noyne](wiki:Noyne), a tributary of the massive [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne), and is surrounded by limestone hills, dark forests of [oak](wiki:Oak), pine, and beech.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Located between Pentos and Qohor, it serves as a gateway for caravans and travelers going east-west on the ancient [Valyrian roads](wiki:Valyrian_roads), with the ruins of [Ghoyan Drohe](wiki:Ghoyan_Drohe) to the southwest and Qohor to the southeast.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-2) Norvos is located in a land of rolling hills and terraced farms, surrounded by small walled villages.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

The domains of Norvos stretch as far as the western bank of the [Darkwash](wiki:Darkwash) to the east and the [Upper Rhoyne](wiki:Upper_Rhoyne) to the west. The [Noyne](wiki:Noyne) is ruled by Norvoshi river galleys as far south as the ruins of [Ny Sar](wiki:Ny_Sar), where she joins the Rhoyne. The city also claims dominion over the [Axe](wiki:Axe) upon the [Shivering Sea](wiki:Shivering_Sea), though the [Ibbenese](wiki:Ibbenese) are known to dispute this claim, often bloodily.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Norvos's climate contains sweltering summers and bitter winters, with harsh, cold winds,(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) and a pale, wan sun.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

### Layout

Norvos is made up out of two parts, joined only by a massive stone stair, known as the [Sinner's Steps](wiki:Sinner's_Steps). The high city, where the ancient nobility of Norvos lives, is ringed about by mighty stone walls,(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) and located on the tallest hill in the region.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Here, the great fortress-temple of the bearded priests is located.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

The lower city is located three hundred feet below, at the base of the hill, by the river,(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) spread out along the muddy shores. It is defended by moats, ditches, and a timber palisade, overgrown with moss. The riverfront is lined with wharves, brothels, and beer halls.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) While the upper city is grim, the lower city is lively.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

#### Bells of Norvos

Norvos's [three bells](wiki:Three_bells) govern every aspect of the lives of those in the city. The bells tell the Norvoshi when to rise, sleep, work, rest, when to take arms, when to pray, and when they are permitted to have carnal relations.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) Each bell has its own distinctive 'voice' and name:(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

- Noom, with a deep sound
- Narrah, with a strong sound
- Nyel, with a higher pitched sound.

[Lomas Longstrider](wiki:Lomas_Longstrider) counts the bells of Norvos as one of his nine *[Wonders Made by Man](wiki:Wonders_Made_by_Man)*.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

### People

Norvos is a theocracy. While it has a [council of magisters](wiki:Council_of_magisters_(Norvos)), its members are chosen by the [bearded priests](wiki:Bearded_priests), who speak for their god and wield the true power in the Free City.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) The magisters give lavishly to passing [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) *[khals](wiki:Khal)*, so their *[khalasars](wiki:Khalasar)* will not savage the city.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Among the Norvoshi only the priests are allowed beards. The priests wear [hair shirts](wiki:Hair_shirts) and untanned hides. Once initiated, these men are forbidden to cut or shave their hair. They practice ritual flagellation as part of their worship. The name of the god worshipped by the Norvoshi is known only to initiates.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

The freeborn of the city, both noble and lowborn, favor long mustachios; [Byan Votyris](wiki:Byan_Votyris), for instance, dyes his mustachio blue and sweeps it to his ears.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Norvoshi women shave off all of their hair, though noblewomen don wigs, especially when in the company of men from other lands and cities.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12) Some Norvoshi women wear dresses with squirrel collars.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) The Norvoshi do not foster children.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

[Slaves](wiki:Slaves) are also shaved bare. There are whispers of torchlit cellars where slave women mate with wolves.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)

Norvoshi enjoy [wintercakes](wiki:Wintercake) and *[nahsa](wiki:Nahsa)*, a drink of fermented goat's milk which can be laced with honey.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) Once a year, there is a festival in Norvos that features [bears](wiki:Bear) dancing down the [Sinner's Steps](wiki:Sinner's_Steps).(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

[Doro Golathis](wiki:Doro_Golathis) is a historian from Norvos.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Like the tongues of all [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), the [Norvoshi language](wiki:Norvoshi_language) is a form of [bastard Valyrian](wiki:Valyrian_languages).(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### Economy

The Norvoshi are well known for their [tapestries](wiki:Tapestries).(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Norvos is involved in trade with [Lorath](wiki:Lorath),(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) but rarely the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Caravans are known to pass through the city.(wiki:Norvos.cite_ref-Ragot36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
