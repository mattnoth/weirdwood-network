## Origins

### Dawn Age

During the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age), [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) was inhabited by [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) and by [giants](wiki:Giants).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) Maester [Kennet](wiki:Kennet_(maester)) has found evidence that giants who lived in what is now the north buried their dead.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-1)

The human peoples of the north are nearly all descended from the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), who settled the land between eight thousand and twelve thousand years ago.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) Little is known of that time, but cryptic [runes](wiki:Runes) carved in old stones and the [barrows](wiki:Barrow) of the First Men can still be found in the [barrowlands](wiki:Barrowlands). Ancient forts of the First Men are scattered throughout the north, including a ringfort atop Seal Rock near [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor)(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-5) and ruins in [Sea Dragon Point](wiki:Sea_Dragon_Point).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The children of the forest also made [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) circles.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

### Kings of Winter

About eight thousand years ago, the legendary [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night) occurred when the [Others](wiki:Others) invaded from the [Lands of Always Winter](wiki:Lands_of_Always_Winter). The event defined and shaped the north, leading to the founding of the [Wall](wiki:Wall), the order of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), the castle of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), and the first [Kings of Winter](wiki:Kings_of_Winter) from [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark). [Brandon the Breaker](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(Breaker)) is said to have allied with [Joramun](wiki:Joramun), a [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall), to bring down the [Night's King](wiki:Night's_King).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1)

The Starks gradually defeated rival kings, such as the [Barrow Kings](wiki:Barrow_Kings) to their south and the [Red Kings](wiki:Red_King) to their east.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) During the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals), the Kings of Winter stopped [Andals](wiki:Andals) at [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin) and the eastern shores, the only kingdom in Westeros to do so.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0)(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

King [Jon Stark](wiki:Jon_Stark) founded the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den) at the mouth of the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife) after driving out sea raiders.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3) His son, [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark_(king)), conquered the [Neck](wiki:Neck) from the [Marsh King](wiki:Marsh_King) and married his daughter.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-1) King [Rodrik Stark](wiki:Rodrik_Stark) is said to have won [Bear Island](wiki:Bear_Island) from the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) in a wrestling match.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-2) Two thousand years ago the north warred with the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) after the [Rape of the Three Sisters](wiki:Rape_of_the_Three_Sisters), with the Arryns eventually gaining control of the islands in this [War Across the Water](wiki:War_Across_the_Water).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0)(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0)

Thousands of years before [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-2) King [Brandon the Shipwright](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(Shipwright)) attempted to sail across the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea), but never returned back home. His son, also named [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(Burner)), burned the northern shipyards in his grief.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-3) The north has since lacked strength at sea.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-4)

For many centuries the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) were bitter rivals of the Starks of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). The practice of flaying their enemies gave the Boltons a sinister reputation. It is said that a thousand years ago, the Boltons finally swore fealty to the Kings in the North and agreed to abandon their practice of flaying their enemies.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0)

Around a thousand years ago, the [Manderlys](wiki:House_Manderly) were driven from the river [Mander](wiki:Mander) by [Perceon III Gardener](wiki:Perceon_III_Gardener),(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_kings_of_winter.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) [King of the Reach](wiki:King_of_the_Reach), and fled to the north, where they were welcomed by the Starks of Winterfell as their own bannermen.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0) The Manderlys received the Wolf's Den and developed [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), one of the five cities of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) and the main northern port for commerce and naval transport. The Manderlys are the most prominent of the few northern noble houses to follow the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) instead of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-3)

### Targaryen Era

The Starks led the north to war during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest). After the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire), however, King [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark) knelt to [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) rather than face his [dragons](wiki:Dragons). The north was included in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) and owed allegiance to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). The Stark [Kings in the North](wiki:Kings_in_the_North) became the Lords of Winterfell and [Wardens of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North). Some northmen who refused to bend the knee fled into exile in [Essos](wiki:Essos) and formed the [Company of the Rose](wiki:Company_of_the_Rose).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0)

Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) convinced her husband, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), to grant the [New Gift](wiki:New_Gift) to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch)(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0) despite the objections of Lord [Ellard Stark](wiki:Ellard_Stark).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0)

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the Starks supported the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) against the [greens](wiki:Greens). Lord [Roderick Dustin](wiki:Roderick_Dustin) led two thousand northern soldiers, known as the [Winter Wolves](wiki:Winter_Wolves), during the war, while the Manderlys had knights from White Harbor also support the claim of [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0) Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) led a great host of northmen to King's Landing, where he briefly ruled as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) for [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) during the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-1)

Lord [Dagon Greyjoy](wiki:Dagon_Greyjoy) led the ironborn in raiding the north during the reign of King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen); Lord [Beron Stark](wiki:Beron_Stark) gathered swords to repel them.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-1)

The people of the north must be vigilant against [wildlings](wiki:Free_folk) who manage to bypass the [Wall](wiki:Wall). One [King-beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-beyond-the-Wall), [Raymun Redbeard](wiki:Raymun_Redbeard), was defeated by the Starks and [Umbers](wiki:House_Umber) at [Long Lake](wiki:Battle_at_Long_Lake).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0)

A century ago the [Skagosi](wiki:Skagos) unsuccessfully rebelled against the Starks, costing the lives of Lord [Barthogan Stark](wiki:Barthogan_Stark) and hundreds of his supporters.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

### Robert's Rebellion

The northmen composed much of the rebel forces during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), also known as the War of the Usurper. After King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) caused the deaths of Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark) and his heir, [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark), their successor, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), led armies alongside Lord Robert Baratheon, who was crowned King [Robert I](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) at war's end.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) travels from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to offer the position of [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to his old friend, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), which the [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North) reluctantly accepts.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0)(wiki:North.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-0)

When Eddard is later imprisoned in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) after the death of Robert, his heir, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), calls the northern banners to Winterfell and marches south to rescue Eddard(wiki:North.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-2) with a strength of nearly twenty thousand(wiki:North.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) close to the start of autumn.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-1) [House Karstark](wiki:House_Karstark) contributes at least two thousand three hundred men, mostly infantry.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-3) [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) provides at least fifteen hundred,(wiki:North.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) but it is not their full strength.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-4) [House Dustin](wiki:House_Dustin) sends a minimal amount.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-0) Of the four thousand armored lances who come to Winterfell, only three or four hundred are [knights](wiki:Knight).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-4)

Robb is victorious over [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) forces in the [Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood) and the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), but Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) is defeated by Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) on the [Green Fork](wiki:Battle_on_the_Green_Fork). After hearing that King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) ordered the execution of Eddard, the assembled northern and river lords in [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) reject the sovereignty of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and proclaim Robb to be [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-0)

### A Clash of Kings

With Robb campaigning south of the [Neck](wiki:Neck), his younger brother [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) remains as the Stark in Winterfell. Bran hosts various bannermen at Winterfell's [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast) in [299 AC](wiki:299_AC).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-5)(wiki:North.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-0) Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel), the castellan of Winterfell, allows [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) to begin construction of a fleet with the aid of [House Umber](wiki:House_Umber).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-6)

[Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), the Bastard of [Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), abducts the widowed Lady [Donella Hornwood](wiki:Donella_Hornwood) and claims the lands of the [Hornwoods](wiki:House_Hornwood). After hearing that Ramsay starved Donella, the Manderlys skirmish with the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) in the [Hornwood](wiki:Hornwood_(forest)).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)(wiki:North.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0)

The [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) also declare their independence from the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-0) With most of the northern soldiers fighting alongside Robb in the south,(wiki:North.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-0) [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), claims the north and sends the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) to raid the [Stony Shore](wiki:Harrying_of_the_Stony_Shore) and capture [Deepwood Motte](wiki:Taking_of_Deepwood_Motte), [Torrhen's Square](wiki:Capture_of_Torrhen's_Square), and [Moat Cailin](wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin). [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) captures [Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell),(wiki:North.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_80-0) but Rodrik Cassel assembles near two thousand men to retake the castle from the ironborn. Rodrik's loyalists are betrayed by Ramsay Snow and six hundred Boltons from the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort) in a [battle at Winterfell](wiki:Battle_at_Winterfell), however. The northern capital is then [sacked](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell) by Ramsay and the Boltons.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-0)

Bran Stark flees with [Hodor](wiki:Hodor), [Meera Reed](wiki:Meera_Reed), and [Jojen Reed](wiki:Jojen_Reed) from Winterfell. They travel through the [northern mountains](wiki:Northern_mountains) and the [Gift](wiki:Gift) to the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-0)

Balon Greyjoy, the self-declared [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North), falls to his death at [Pyke](wiki:Pyke).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-0)

Robb Stark, King in the North and [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident), intends to march north from the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) to expel the ironborn.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-1) However, he is [betrayed and murdered](wiki:Red_Wedding) at the [Twins](wiki:Twins) by his bannermen, Lords [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) and [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton),(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_84-0) who have their forces slaughter most of the northmen in the south.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rasos52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_85-0) In return for his services to [House Baratheon of King's Landing](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing), Roose is named the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North) for the Iron Throne.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_86-0)

King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) sails from [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) to aid the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) in defeating the [wildling](wiki:Wildling) army of [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder), [King-beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-beyond-the-Wall), in the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

In [300 AC](wiki:300_AC), Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) has completed some forty-six warships.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-6)(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-5)(wiki:North.cite_ref-88) Wyman secretly tasks [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) with bringing [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark) back from [Skagos](wiki:Skagos).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-6)

The [Ryswells](wiki:House_Ryswell) and [Dustins](wiki:House_Dustin) defeat the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) at the [Fever River](wiki:Fever_River).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-7) [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) uses [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) to resolve the [siege of Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin) and thereby allow Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) to march north with his Frey allies.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_89-0) From the roughly twenty thousand northmen who marched south with [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) at the start of war, only one in five returns north, most of them men from [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

Roose has a tenuous grasp over the north, considering only the Ryswells and Dustins to be loyal allies because of marriage ties. The [Cerwyns](wiki:House_Cerwyn), [Tallharts](wiki:House_Tallhart), [Umbers](wiki:House_Umber), and [Manderlys](wiki:House_Manderly) are reluctant supporters or are outright plotting against Roose.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-1) Ramsay marries "[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark)", who is actually [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole), at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) in order to claim the ancient capital of the north.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_89-1)

Rather than attacking the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort), [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) is convinced by [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), to travel west from [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) to the [northern mountains](wiki:Northern_mountains). The [mountain clans](wiki:Northern_mountain_clans) field him three and a half thousand men,(wiki:North.cite_ref-90) and Stannis [liberates Deepwood Moote](wiki:Fight_by_Deepwood_Motte) for the [Glovers](wiki:House_Glover).(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) The king marches through the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood) to take Winterfell from Roose. Stannis's [march on Winterfell](wiki:March_on_Winterfell) is slowed, however, by strong blizzards(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_91-0) which also hamper Roose.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_92-0)

After receiving [an inflammatory letter](wiki:Bastard_Letter) from Ramsay, Jon asks for volunteers from the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) to help him march south toward Winterfell to confront the Bastard of Bolton. Jon is betrayed in the [mutiny at Castle Black](wiki:Mutiny_at_Castle_Black), however.(wiki:North.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_93-0)
