## Origins

Back in [Davos](wiki:Davos)'s smuggling days, the Eel had been renowned for offering the oldest whores and vilest [wine](wiki:Wine) in [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), along with meat pies full of lard and gristle that were inedible to eat on their best days and poisonous on their worst.
Most locals shunned the place, leaving it for sailors who did not know any better. City guardsmen and customs officers were never seen down in the Lazy Eel. Not much has changed.(wiki:Lazy_Eel.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Narrative Arc

### Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) goes to the Lazy Eel to try and catch up on tidings concerning [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) and beyond. Amongst the patrons various their talk turns to the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen) and the [Mad King](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen)'s daughter, none of the patrons are sure what the name of the princess was. Davos mentions that her name was [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) and that he doesn’t know what became of her. A [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) oarsman immediately says he does know what became of her, and recounts a (true) tale he heard in [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) from the captain's steward of the [Sloe-Eyed Maid](wiki:Sloe-Eyed_Maid).(wiki:Lazy_Eel.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) In [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) did try to gain passage on the Sloe-Eyed Maid, but the captain rejected her.(wiki:Lazy_Eel.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
