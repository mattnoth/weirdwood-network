## Appearances & Description

The Book Tower is made of large blocks of stone and is octagonal in shape. It is the fattest of the ten towers of the castle. Its staircase is enclosed within the thick walls.(wiki:Book_Tower.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)
The fifth story of the Book Tower contains the reading room for [Rodrik Harlaw](wiki:Rodrik_Harlaw), [Lord of Harlaw](wiki:Lord_of_Harlaw). Rodrik sits at a table by a window with the aid of [beeswax](wiki:Bee) candles in ornate [iron](wiki:Iron) holders.(wiki:Book_Tower.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) He does not allow food or drink in his library.(wiki:Book_Tower.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
There is a well in the yard beneath the tower.(wiki:Book_Tower.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

[Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) visits her uncle, Lord [Rodrik Harlaw](wiki:Rodrik_Harlaw), in his reading room when she returns to [Ten Towers](wiki:Ten_Towers) after the death of her father, Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy). Instead of hosting [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) in his hall, Rodrik reads a copy of Archmaester [Marwyn](wiki:Marwyn)'s [Book of Lost Books](wiki:Book_of_Lost_Books) which had been brought by [Hotho Harlaw](wiki:Hotho_Harlaw).(wiki:Book_Tower.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
