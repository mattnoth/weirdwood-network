## Appearances & Description

The [holdfast](wiki:Holdfast) lies behind walls twelve feet thick.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) A dry moat lined with a bed of formidable iron spikes surrounds it, with a drawbridge spanning the moat.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) The only way in or out of Maegor's is across the drawbridge. A knight of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) is usually, but not always,(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) posted at its far end.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) However according to Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) there is a secret egress built at the behest of King [Maegor I](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
The royal apartments are located in Maegor's Holdfast. The king's bedchamber has a canopied bed and twin hearths.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
The [Queen's Ballroom](wiki:Queen's_Ballroom) within Maegor's Holdfast is much smaller than the [Great Hall](wiki:Great_Hall) of the Red Keep or the Small Hall of the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand). The graceful ballroom seats one hundred and has beaten silver mirrors behind the wall sconces which makes the torchlight seem twice as bright. Its walls are paneled with richly carved wood and it has a gallery above the main floor. High arched windows sit along the south wall.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

## Origins

### Early History[ ]

Construction of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) began in [35 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) during the reign of King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and continued into the reign of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen). In [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) took charge of the castle's construction and ordered the building of a redoubt within the Red Keep.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Members of [House Harroway](wiki:House_Harroway) were thrown onto the spikes surrounding Maegor's Holdfast during the downfall of Queen [Alys Harroway](wiki:Alys_Harroway).(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)
The Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) secluded herself in Maegor's Holdfast when her son, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), resided at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). Jaehaerys landed [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor) in the yard before Maegor's Holdfast upon his return in [50 AC](wiki:50_AC). Jaehaerys and his sister, Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), consummated their marriage in Maegor's Holdfast the same year.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
Ser [Lucamore Strong](wiki:Lucamore_Strong) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) was guarding the bridge across the dry moat when the dying Princess [Aerea Targaryen](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) returned on [Balerion](wiki:Balerion).(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
Queen [Aemma Arryn](wiki:Aemma_Arryn) died in Maegor's Holdfast while giving birth to Prince [Baelon Targaryen](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)).(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### Dance of the Dragons[ ]

The death of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) in his apartments in Maegor's Holdfast led to the civil war known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). The [small council](wiki:Small_council) gathered in the apartments of Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) and supported the claim of her son, [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), with the exception of Lord [Lyman Beesbury](wiki:Lyman_Beesbury). By the time Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) took his leave on [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) for [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) the stink from the dead king's bedchamber had wafted all through the holdfast.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Aegon II moved into Maegor's Holdfast with his queen and sister-wife, [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen).(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) [Cheese](wiki:Cheese_(ratcatcher)) knew of no way in and out of Maegor's save over the drawbridge, so he and [Blood](wiki:Blood_(butcher)) instead infiltrated the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand) to murder Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_II)).(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)
After the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) to Aegon II's rival, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), Alicent emerged from Maegor's and attempted to treat with her stepdaughter but Rhaenyra rejected her proposal. Rhaenyra's men found Aegon's wife, the mad Queen Helaena, locked in her bedchamber. When they broke down the doors of Aegon's apartments they discovered only his empty bed and a full chamberpot.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
As Rhaenyra was losing her grip on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Helaena threw herself from her window in Maegor's Holdfast. She died impaled upon the iron spikes that lined the dry moat below. During the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit), Queen Rhaenyra watched the attack unfold from the roof of Maegor's Holdfast with her sons and members of her court, including [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom).(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
Aegon II lost much of his support after the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad). Turncloaks turned against Aegon the Elder, and Ser [Perkin the Flea](wiki:Perkin_the_Flea) pushed Ser [Alfred Broome](wiki:Alfred_Broome) off the drawbridge before Maegor's Holdfast onto the spikes below.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### Regency of Aegon III[ ]

Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark), briefly [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), confined King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) and [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair) in Maegor's Holdfast during the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf).(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) After Cregan's departure, Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), the new Hand, confined [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) to gentle imprisonment in Maegor's.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
During the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring), the [Lyseni](wiki:Lys) at court were rumored to perform [blood sacrifice](wiki:Bloodmagic) within Maegor's Holdfast.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) King Aegon and his brother, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), sought refuge in the redoubt during the so-called [secret siege](wiki:Secret_siege). [Sandoq the Shadow](wiki:Sandoq_the_Shadow) slew Ser [Amaury Peake](wiki:Amaury_Peake) and his guards on the drawbridge of the holdfast.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

### Recent History[ ]

In [270 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) forbade Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) from leaving Maegor's Holdfast after she lost several children.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
Amid [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) and Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch) scaled the walls of Maegor's Holdfast during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) They made their way to the royal nursery within, where they slew Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell) and her children, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) and Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)).(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) dies in Maegor's Holdfast after being gored by a [boar](wiki:Boar) while hunting in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood). After the downfall of Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), his eldest daughter, [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark), resides at the top of the highest tower of Maegor's Holdfast.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

During the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater) almost every highborn woman in the city takes refuge in the [Queen's Ballroom](wiki:Queen's_Ballroom) in Maegor's Holdfast, along with a handful of old men and boys.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) recalls her son, King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), during the battle and closes Maegor's.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) recovers from the Blackwater in a chamber above the Queen's Ballroom, as Lord [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) has taken his son's former chamber in the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand).(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) After marrying Sansa, Tyrion moves from Maegor's Holdfast and displaces Lord [Gyles Rosby](wiki:Gyles_Rosby) in spacious apartments atop the Kitchen Keep.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rasos58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
On the morning of his [royal wedding](wiki:Purple_Wedding), King Joffrey receives gifts in the Queen's Ballroom after breakfast is cleared.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) notes that the dry moat surrounding Maegor's Holdfast is three feet deep in snow and that the iron spikes that line it are glistening with frost. Kevan sups with his niece, Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), and her son, King [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), in the royal chambers. Tommen tells Kevan that the [bad cat](wiki:Balerion_(cat)) was outside his window the previous night.(wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
