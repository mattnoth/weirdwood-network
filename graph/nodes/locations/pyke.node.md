## Origins

### Antiquity

Pyke is so old that its original builders and lords are unknown.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) The castle was once larger and located on the [island of Pyke](wiki:Pyke_(island)), but unstable earth led to some portions of the cliff falling into the sea. The surviving structures are built upon stone stacks.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

[Qhored I Hoare](wiki:Qhored_I_Hoare), [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands), murdered the sons of [Bernarr II Justman](wiki:Bernarr_II_Justman), [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident), in the [Bloody Keep](wiki:Bloody_Keep) of Pyke while they were his hostages.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-14)(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) That may imply Pyke has been the ruling seat of the Iron Islands since the times of the [driftwood kings](wiki:Driftwood_kings).

### Iron Throne

In the aftermath of [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), [Vickon Greyjoy](wiki:Vickon_Greyjoy), [Lord Reaper of Pyke](wiki:Lord_Reaper_of_Pyke), became the new [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), ruling from the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair) at Pyke.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

The [black council](wiki:Black_council) of [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) sent [ravens](wiki:Raven) to Pyke seeking the aid of Lord [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The overtures to Pyke from [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) were ignored, and the Greyjoys eventually aided Rhaenyra by attacking the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands).(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Dalton was killed at [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC).(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

When the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) were invaded by [westermen](wiki:Westermen) amid the ironborn's [struggle for power](wiki:Struggle_for_power) in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC), Pyke resisted [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister)'s assault. [Rodrik Greyjoy](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy_(son_of_Dalton)), the half-brother of Lord [Toron Greyjoy](wiki:Toron_Greyjoy), was captured and taken to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) to be a [fool](wiki:Fool), however.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### Balon Greyjoy

Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) killed Pyke's [maester](wiki:Maester) after the man failed to heal his brother [Urrigon](wiki:Urrigon_Greyjoy),(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) although Balon then requested another maester from the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel),(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) possibly [Qalen](wiki:Qalen). Balon's youngest surviving brother, [Aeron](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), sang reaving songs atop the Great Keep's tables.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-15)

A [battle at Pyke](wiki:Battle_at_Pyke) was the final engagement of [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion). King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), along with Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), mustered thousands of men and invaded the island of Pyke thorough [Lordsport](wiki:Lordsport). They captured the castle after bringing down the southern part of the outer wall of Pyke using siege engines.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-16)(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), the last surviving son of Lord Balon, was taken from Pyke to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) as a hostage by Lord Stark.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) A new south tower was raised after the war.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-17)

Having had two sons, [Maron](wiki:Maron_Greyjoy) and [Rodrik](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy), killed in the rebellion and Theon taken away, Balon's wife, [Alannys Harlaw](wiki:Alannys_Harlaw), brought five boys to Pyke as wards. One of them, [Tristifer Botley](wiki:Tristifer_Botley), was sent to [Blacktyde](wiki:Blacktyde) after Maester [Qalen](wiki:Qalen) found him kissing Lord Balon's daughter, [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy).(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) After wandering the halls of Pyke in search of her dead sons, Alannys now lives with her brother, Lord [Rodrik Harlaw](wiki:Rodrik_Harlaw), at [Ten Towers](wiki:Ten_Towers), which is less raw than Pyke.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-18) Asha has felt more at home at Ten Towers than at Pyke.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Pyke](wiki:Category:Images_of_Pyke)*

Ancient Pyke was originally built on a cliff jutting out into the sea, but over time the cliff has eroded, leaving the castle's keeps and towers standing on three barren islands and a dozen small stacks of rock, surrounded by water. The towers are connected by swaying rope bridges. The keep, its towers, and walls are made of the same grey-black stone of which the rest of the island is composed. In the thousands of years the castle has stood, it has become covered with green lichen. Since there is no safe anchorage at Pyke, ships sail to nearby [Lordsport](wiki:Lordsport).(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

A curtain wall encloses the headland of fifty acres and the cliffs around the foot of the wide stone bridge which extend out to the largest islet. The stables, kennels and livestock are located on the headland. The Great Keep, the Kitchen Keep, and the Bloody Keep each sit on their own islands. Towers and outbuildings are located on stacks beyond them, linked to each other by covered archways where the pillars stood close and by long, swaying walks of wood and rope when they did not.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

- The [Great Keep of Pyke](wiki:Great_Keep_of_Pyke) is massive and caps the largest islet. It is connected to the mainland by the great stone bridge and to the Bloody Keep by a covered stone walkway.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) The long, smoky Great Hall contains the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair).(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

- The [Bloody Keep](wiki:Bloody_Keep) is one of the largest sections of the castle. It lies on its own island, further out than the Great Keep. Its halls are larger and better furnished, with some suites having high and gloomy ceilings.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) To get to the Great Keep one must use a covered stone walkway.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)

- The Kitchen Keep sits on its own island. It lies further out from the mainland than the Great Keep.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8) Dishes include fish stews, black bread, spiceless goat, and onion pie.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) [Ale](wiki:Ale), [mead](wiki:Mead), and [wine](wiki:Wine) are served at Pyke.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

- The [Sea Tower](wiki:Sea_Tower) rises from the outermost stack, which is sheer and crooked. It is the oldest part of the castle, round and tall. The base of the tower is white from centuries of salt spray, the upper stories are green with lichen, and the top is black with soot from [nightfires](wiki:Nightfire). To get to the Sea Tower, one must cross three bridges, the last of which is made of rope. The tower's door is moldy grey wood with rusted iron studs. Twisting stairs lead to the damp and drafty [solar](wiki:Solar) of Lord Greyjoy, which is warmed by a brazier.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10)(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) The [kraken](wiki:Kraken) banner of [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy) flies from an iron mast above the Sea Tower.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11)

- The gatehouse is separated from the Great Keep by the high bridge.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-12) The walls of Pyke run in a crescent moon from cliff to cliff, with the gatehouse providing entry, located in the center portion of the wall and including an iron portcullis to provide additional protection should the wall be attacked. There are three towers to either side; the south tower is new, made of a paler grey stone and still lacking lichen, after the old south tower had been destroyed during [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion) when King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) breached the walls.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-13)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

[Ravens](wiki:Raven) are sent from [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) and [Seagard](wiki:Seagard) to Pyke informing Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) that his son, [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), is being sent as an envoy of [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), but there is no reply.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-19)

Theon returns to Pyke for the first time since being taken away as a child after [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion). Instead of an honor guard led by [Dagmer](wiki:Dagmer), Theon is escorted from [Lordsport](wiki:Lordsport) to Pyke by his uncle, [Aeron Damphair](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy).(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-20) Instead of staying in the [Sea Tower](wiki:Sea_Tower), where he slept as a child, Theon is given a suite in the [Bloody Keep](wiki:Bloody_Keep). Within the Sea Tower, Balon rejects Robb's offer of alliance.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-21)

From the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair), Greyjoy hosts a feast in the [Great Keep](wiki:Great_Keep_of_Pyke) for his assembled captains. He then discusses his plans for independence and invading the [north](wiki:North) with his family in the solar of the Sea Tower.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

### A Storm of Swords

It is reported that Balon, [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North), fell to his death when a bridge of Pyke was torn to pieces by the wind. His exiled brother, [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), returned to Pyke the day after and claimed the Seastone Chair.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Euron Crow's Eye has Maester [Wendamyr](wiki:Wendamyr) summon the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) captains to Pyke to pay homage to their new king, but his brother Aeron instead calls for a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk).(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) Despite the efforts of Aeron, Euron is confirmed king at the gathering.(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

[Erik Ironmaker](wiki:Erik_Ironmaker) serves as castellan of Pyke and [Lord Steward of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_Steward_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Pyke.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)
