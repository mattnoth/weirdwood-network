## Origins

The secret tunnel from the brothel was dug for an unspecified [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

The brothel is a favorite of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) Lords [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) and [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) visited Chataya's brothel when trying to identify [bastards](wiki:Bastards) fathered by Robert.(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Appearances & Description

The [brothel](wiki:Brothel) is two stories tall with a stone ground floor and a timber upper floor. Many of its windows are leaded. Over the door swings an ornate lamp of gilded metal and scarlet glass.(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Inside the entrance the air smells of exotic spice. Its floor displays a mosaic of two women entwined in love. The common room is behind an ornate [Myrish](wiki:Myr) screen that has been carved with [flowers](wiki:Plants), fancies, and dreaming maidens. The common room has a cushioned alcove and a leaded colored glass window where sunlight pours through. An older man plays the [pipes](wiki:Reed_pipes).(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

A round turret rises from one corner of the house. The turret room is located up two flights of stairs from the ground floor, then down a long hall and up another stair to a lone door which opens to the turret room. Within the room is a great canopied bed, a tall wardrobe decorated with erotic carvings, and a narrow window of leaded glass in a pattern of red and yellow diamonds.(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

Inside the turret room's empty wardrobe is a back panel concealing a secret passageway. When the back panel is pushed aside it reveals a metal ladder whose rungs go well below street level. The shaft then opens onto a dark, slanting earthen tunnel. Having come perhaps a distance of three blocks under the [Hill of Rhaenys](wiki:Hill_of_Rhaenys), a patron will then emerge through a trap door at the back of a nearby stable.(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

[One of the whores](wiki:Barra's_mother) has [Barra](wiki:Barra), a bastard daughter of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) During his search for the king's bastards, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) finds the child and her mother at [Chataya](wiki:Chataya)'s brothel.(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

### A Clash of Kings

Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) decides to erase any clue of her adultery and has Barra and her mother killed by men of the [City Watch](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) under the command of [Allar Deem](wiki:Allar_Deem).(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) visits Chataya's as a front for his clandestine visitations with [Shae](wiki:Shae) at a manse nearby. When he enters the common room, he sees an older man playing music on pipes and girls playing tiles near a window. [Alayaya](wiki:Alayaya), Chataya's daughter, shows Tyrion how to access the secret tunnel from the turret room, and [Varys](wiki:Varys) escorts him beneath [Rhaenys's Hill](wiki:Hill_of_Rhaenys). Tyrion then rides from the nearby stable to discretely visit [Shae's manse](wiki:Shae's_manse).(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)

Tyrion returns to the brothel on several occasions so he can visit [Shae](wiki:Shae); each time he only requests the services of Alayaya. During one visit, a patron attempts to spit on Tyrion but the spittle instead lands on Tyrion's companion, [Bronn](wiki:Bronn), who knocks out the patron's teeth.(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) On another occasion, Bronn goes off with a girl from [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). The [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) has led [Chataya](wiki:Chataya) to offer a poorer vintage of [wine](wiki:Wine).(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Tyrion considers bringing King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) to the brothel so he can enjoy the girls.(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) [Marei](wiki:Marei) teaches her fellow prostitutes how to read,(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) and she wagers with [Dancy](wiki:Dancy) over the latter girl's ability to seduce Tyrion.(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

Fearing that Tyrion is plotting against Joffrey, Cersei threatens Tyrion by having Alayaya taken as a hostage. The queen mistakenly believes that the prostitute is Tyrion's lover instead of Shae.(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Racok54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Tyrion sends [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne) to find Ser Bronn, who is with Alayaya and Marei at Chataya's.(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

[Anguy](wiki:Anguy) reveals he spent the small fortune he had won at the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney) on Alayaya, [Dancy](wiki:Dancy), and [Jayde](wiki:Jayde), as well as on roast swan and Arbor [wine](wiki:Wine).(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Tyrion recommends Chataya's to Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell).(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The Red Viper eventually sleeps with Alayaya.(wiki:Chataya's_brothel.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
