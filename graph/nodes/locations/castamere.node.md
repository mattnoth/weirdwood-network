## Origins

The rich veins of [gold](wiki:Gold) and [silver](wiki:Silver) allowed [House Reyne](wiki:House_Reyne) to become nearly as wealthy as [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:Castamere.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

[Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) and her dragon, [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre), briefly stayed at Castamere with the [Reynes](wiki:House_Reyne) in [51 AC](wiki:51_AC).(wiki:Castamere.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) visited the castle in [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Castamere.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

[Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), the second son of Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister), served as first page and then squire to [Robert Reyne](wiki:Robert_Reyne), [Lord of Castamere](wiki:Lord_of_Castamere). Following the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings), tensions rose between the Reynes and Lannisters during the rule of Tytos. Castamere was where the opposing sides met to exchange hostages. Peace was short lived, however.(wiki:Castamere.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

During the ensuing [Reyne-Tarbeck revolt](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt), the Reynes retreated to Castamere after the destruction of [Tarbeck Hall](wiki:Tarbeck_Hall). With Roger wounded and many of their men killed in battle, the Red Lion's brother, Ser [Reynard](wiki:Reynard_Reyne), abandoned the surface castle for the safety of the mine's [oak](wiki:Oak)-and-[iron](wiki:Iron) gate. Tytos's heir, Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), ignored Reynard's offers and had his miners bury Castamere's entrances beneath stone. The Lannisters then dammed [the pool's](wiki:Castamere_(pool)) stream and diverted it into a mine entrance, flooding the underground chambers of Castamere and drowning those trapped inside. Tywin also had the surface buildings put to the torch. The fate of the Reynes and Castamere is remembered in the song "[The Rains of Castamere](wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere)".(wiki:Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Castamere.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

## Appearances & Description

Named after a [nearby pool of water](wiki:Castamere_(pool)),(wiki:Castamere.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Castamere began as a mine like [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock). After building curtain walls to guard the entrance with two stout towers, the Reynes constructed keeps and halls. As the [gold](wiki:Gold) and [silver](wiki:Silver) dried up, the mine below was widened and converted into halls, galleries, bedchambers, and a vast underground ballroom. While the surface fortifications resembled those of a minor [lord](wiki:Lord) or [landed knight](wiki:Landed_knight), nine-tenths of the castle was subterranean.(wiki:Castamere.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), the [gold](wiki:Gold) mines of Castamere [are seized](wiki:Seizure_of_westerlands_gold_mines) by Lord [Jon Umber](wiki:Jon_Umber) after the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross).(wiki:Castamere.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

### A Storm of Swords

For their part in the downfall of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), [House Spicer](wiki:House_Spicer) receives Castamere and Ser [Rolph Spicer](wiki:Rolph_Spicer) is named [Lord of Castamere](wiki:Lord_of_Castamere).(wiki:Castamere.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
