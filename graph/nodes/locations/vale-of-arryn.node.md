## Origins

### First Men

During the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), the Vale was thinly populated and was ruled by a score of petty [First Men](wiki:First_Men) kings. The [Winged Knight](wiki:Winged_Knight) is among the legendary heroes of the Valemen from thousands of years ago.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### Vale of Arryn

The Vale was where the [Andals](wiki:Andals) landed when they began their [invasion of Westeros](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals). Rather than unite against the newcomers, many of the First Men rulers sought alliances with the Andals, often with disastrous results.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) claimed the Vale after defeating the First Men in the [Battle of the Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_the_Seven_Stars).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) As such, the Arryns are one of the oldest lines of Andal nobility in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). The Arryns ruled as [Kings of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale) for approximately six thousand years. Those First Men who did not accept Andal rule fled into the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon), becoming the [mountain clans](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3)

Following the [Rape of the Three Sisters](wiki:Rape_of_the_Three_Sisters) two thousand years ago, the Vale warred with the [north](wiki:North) for a thousand years.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) The [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den) was besieged by [Osgood Arryn](wiki:Osgood_Arryn) and burned by [the Talon](wiki:Oswin_Arryn) during the [War Across the Water](wiki:War_Across_the_Water) between the two kingdoms.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) The [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters) swore fealty to the Arryns, although their loyalty remains tenuous.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) The [Bloody Gate](wiki:Bloody_Gate) withstood three attempts by [Halleck Hoare](wiki:Halleck_Hoare), [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), to conquer the Vale.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### Iron Throne

The Arryns bent the knee to [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) during [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen)'s [Wars of Conquest](wiki:Wars_of_Conquest), bringing the Vale under the control of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) gained the Vale for the cause of the [blacks](wiki:Blacks).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) In [134 AC](wiki:134_AC), a war of succession arose in the Vale after the death of Lady [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn) between her chosen heir, Ser [Joffrey Arryn](wiki:Joffrey_Arryn), a closer cousin, Ser [Arnold Arryn](wiki:Arnold_Arryn), and the head of [House Arryn of Gulltown](wiki:House_Arryn_of_Gulltown), [Isembard Arryn](wiki:Isembard_Arryn), with the Crown ruling Jeyne's testament should be upheld, and sending a force to maintain the [King's Peace](wiki:King's_Peace).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

The Arryns remained loyal to the Targaryens during the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion),(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) although there were battles in the Vale during the civil war.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) The Arryns blocked access to the Vale during the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

[Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) began in the Vale, when Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), [Defender of the Vale](wiki:Defender_of_the_Vale), refused to surrender his wards, Lords [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), to King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen). Robert aided Jon in defeating his royalist bannermen at [Gulltown](wiki:Taking_of_Gulltown), allowing Robert to sail home to the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2) Meanwhile, Eddard crossed the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) to the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers), from which he sailed to the [Bite](wiki:Bite) and then returned home to the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-3) Lord Arryn went on to help Robert secure the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), and was named his [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) after the war.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_glorious_reign.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) In Jon's absence, [Nestor Royce](wiki:Nestor_Royce) has governed as High Steward of the Vale.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

After Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn)'s sudden death in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), his widow [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) flees with their son, Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn), to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Her sister, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), abducts [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn)(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) and brings her captive to Lysa in the Vale instead of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell),(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) which leads Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) to begin warfare in the sisters' homeland of the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

Tyrion recruits [Vale mountain clansmen](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans) while taking the [high road](wiki:High_road) to the riverlands, promising them weapons and armor(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) so they can threaten the Vale.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Lysa refuses to lend the Vale's strength to any side of the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) reports to the [small council](wiki:Small_council) that mountain clansmen have been raiding from the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon), in greater numbers and with better weaponry than normal.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1)

### A Storm of Swords

After [the death](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) helps [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) escape King's Landing(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) and brings her to the Vale. Lysa marries Petyr at [his tower](wiki:Drearfort) at the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Sansa, using the name Alayne Stone,(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1) accompanies Petyr and her aunt, Lady Lysa, to the Eyrie via [Coldwater Burn](wiki:Coldwater_Burn), [Snakewood](wiki:Snakewood), [Heart's Home](wiki:Heart's_Home), [Longbow Hall](wiki:Longbow_Hall), [Old Anchor](wiki:Old_Anchor), and [Ironoaks](wiki:Ironoaks).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtloiafjourneys.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

[Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) attempts to bring [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark), Sansa's sister, from the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) to the Eyrie so she can be ransomed by Lysa, but they turn back when a village elder in the mountain foothills warns them of inclement weather, [shadowcats](wiki:Shadowcat), and mountain clans.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

After Lysa threatens Sansa and reveals that she and Petyr had [poisoned](wiki:Poison) her first husband, Jon Arryn, Petyr pushes Lysa through the Eyrie's [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

### A Feast for Crows

The high road to the Vale is closed by snow.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

Petyr rules as Lord Protector of the Vale and the guardian of the orphaned Lord Robert, to the consternation of the [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Lord [Yohn Royce](wiki:Yohn_Royce) leads the Lords Declarant in surrounding the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon), but Petyr manipulates them into peace during a parley at the Eyrie.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) With the onset of winter, Robert's court descends from the Eyrie to the Gates of the Moon.(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Lord [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) washes up on [Sweetsister](wiki:Sweetsister), but Lord [Godric Borrell](wiki:Godric_Borrell) allows Davos to continue to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) instead of turning him over to Lord [Triston Sunderland](wiki:Triston_Sunderland).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-4)

### The Winds of Winter

Nobles and knights from the Vale travel to the Gates of the Moon to attend the tourney for the [Brotherhood of Winged Knights](wiki:Brotherhood_of_Winged_Knights).(wiki:Vale_of_Arryn.cite_ref-41)
