## Appearances & Description

The High Hall has carved wooden doors protected by [House Arryn guards](wiki:House_Arryn_guards).(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) Long and austere, the hall has walls of blue-veined [marble](wiki:Marble).(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) The hall has fluted, slender pillars and narrow arched windows,(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) and fifty torches can burn in high iron sconces along the milk-white walls.(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) A blue silk carpet runs between the rows of pillars.(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Behind the dais is another doorway for the [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie).(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
The High Hall contains Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn)'s throne of carved [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood), beside which is a smaller matching throne for Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn).(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Above the thrones hang the [moon](wiki:Moon)-and-[falcon](wiki:Falcon) banners of [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn).(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
The [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door) stands between two marble pillars, with three heavy [bronze](wiki:Bronze) bars keeping the dangerous weirwood doorway closed.(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

## Origins

In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), [Jonos Arryn](wiki:Jonos_Arryn) rebelled against [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) and threw his brother [Ronnel](wiki:Ronnel_Arryn_(king)), [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie), out the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door). When Prince [Maegor Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) appeared on [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), Jonos's garrison surrendered and the kinslayer was himself thrown through the Moon Door.(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) met with [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn), [Lady of the Eyrie](wiki:Lady_of_the_Eyrie), in the High Hall during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
While having breakfast in the High Hall, a food fight ensued when [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) struck [Dacks](wiki:Dacks) in the face with an orange.(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) publicly accuses [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) of having murdered [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie), and Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) wants Tyrion to be thrown through the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door). [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) interrupts, however, and Tyrion is instead sent to the [sky cells](wiki:Sky_cells).(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) The prisoner is returned to the High Hall after claiming he wishes to confess, but Tyrion then maintains his innocence in Jon's death. When Lysa threatens to have Tyrion executed through the Moon Door, Tyrion calls for a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat).(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Lysa has [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) brought to the High Hall after having witnessing a kiss between the girl and Lysa's new husband, Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish). Lysa threatens Sansa at the Moon Door, but Petyr arrives and halts the confrontation. After Lysa admits to having [poisoned](wiki:Poison) Jon Arryn on Petyr's orders, Petyr shoves her through the Moon Door and tells guardsmen that [Marillion](wiki:Marillion) is to blame for her death.(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

The High Hall is kept closed after Lysa's death. When Lord [Nestor Royce](wiki:Nestor_Royce) ascends to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), the hall is reopened so that Nestor can inquire about Lysa's fall.(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
Rather than the High Hall, Petyr meets with the [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant) in the Eyrie's solar.(wiki:High_Hall.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
