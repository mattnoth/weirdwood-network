## Appearances & Description

Squat and wide, the Gatehouse Tower is the largest of the remaining towers of [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin).(wiki:Gatehouse_Tower.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) It is covered in green moss,(wiki:Gatehouse_Tower.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) and a gnarled tree grows sideways from the stones of its north side.(wiki:Gatehouse_Tower.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) There are fragments of broken wall still standing to the east and west.(wiki:Gatehouse_Tower.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
The Gatehouse Tower's hall is dark stone, drafty with high ceilings. A massive table of carved stone fills the hall, and pale lichen spot the walls. Peat can be burned in the hall's black hearth. A spiral stair leads up the tower to a dark and smoky chamber.(wiki:Gatehouse_Tower.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Gatehouse_Tower.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

[Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) takes the Gatehouse Tower as his seat when his [northern](wiki:North) host stays at [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin) during their march south. Robb holds a war council with the northern lords in the tower's hall.(wiki:Gatehouse_Tower.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

During the [siege of Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin), [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) sends [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) to offer the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) garrison of Moat Cailin food and safe passage if they surrender to [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) unarmed. Theon sees pale faces at the battlements of the Gatehouse Tower and through the broken masonry crowning the [Children's Tower](wiki:Children's_Tower). Theon succeeds in entering the Gatehouse Tower, where he discovers forty-eight ironmen are still holding the tower. Most are men from [House Codd](wiki:House_Codd).(wiki:Gatehouse_Tower.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Theon is informed by a guard that all of the ironborn within the Children's Tower are dead; [Dagon Codd](wiki:Dagon_Codd) went over a few days ago and discovered two survivors eating the corpses of the deceased, so Dagon killed them both. Some of the pale faces may thus be those of [crannogmen](wiki:Crannogmen) opposing the ironborn.(wiki:Gatehouse_Tower.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
Within the Gatehouse Tower's upper chamber, Theon gives the [gift of mercy](wiki:Gift_of_mercy) to its dying commander, [Ralf Kenning](wiki:Ralf_Kenning). Theon then meets with surviving ironborn in the tower's hall. When Dagon refuses to surrender, he is killed by [Adrack Humble](wiki:Adrack_Humble).(wiki:Gatehouse_Tower.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
