## Origins

### Older History[ ]

[Maesters](wiki:Maesters) believe that the great island kingdom of Tarth joined the realm of the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_King) when King [Durran the Fair](wiki:Durran_Durrandon_(the_Fair)) married the daughter of the island's king, [Edwyn Evenstar](wiki:Edwyn_Tarth). The island rebelled three times during the reign of the Storm King [Durwald the Fat](wiki:Durwald_I_Durrandon), however.(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_durrandon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Tarth was the first region of [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon)'s Kingdom of the Storm to be conquered during the [Andal invasion](wiki:Andal_invasion). The Durrandon Storm Kings and their [First Men](wiki:First_Men) bannermen eventually intermarried with the [Andals](wiki:Andals), however.(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) [House Tarth](wiki:House_Tarth), who rule the island from [Evenfall Hall](wiki:Evenfall_Hall) and were once kings, have Andal ancestry.(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
Now in ruins, [Morne](wiki:Morne) on the eastern coast of Tarth was once the seat of petty kings who were conquered by Storm Kings. Maester [Hubert](wiki:Hubert) believes the site was of Andal origin, not First Men. Nobles and [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) alike from Tarth claim descent from Ser [Galladon of Morne](wiki:Galladon_of_Morne).(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
[Marble](wiki:Marble) from Tarth was used during the construction of the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn).(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_the_eyrie.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
Some monarchs kept fleets along the western coast of Tarth, as its mountains shield against storms and make the [Straits of Tarth](wiki:Straits_of_Tarth) more pacific than the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) or [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay).(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

### Targaryen Era[ ]

During the reign of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), his daughter, Princess [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), flew on [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre) as far as [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown), [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), [Runestone](wiki:Runestone), and Tarth.(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
In the aftermath of the [Myrish Bloodbath](wiki:Myrish_Bloodbath) of [91](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)–[92 AC](wiki:92_AC), pirates from [Myr](wiki:Myr) took over the eastern coast of Tarth. Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) joined his fleets with the soldiers of Lord [Boremund Baratheon](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon) to retake the island. Prince [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)) flew on [Caraxes](wiki:Caraxes) to the hidden mountain camp of Lord [Cameron Tarth](wiki:Cameron_Tarth). A Myrish scout killed the prince with his crossbow, however. Prince [Baelon Targaryen](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)) retaliated by burning the Myrish ships with [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), while Cameron and Boremund slaughtered the thousands of Myrmen on land.(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
In [133 AC](wiki:133_AC) during the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War), Ser [Gedmund Peake](wiki:Gedmund_Peake)'s fleet stopped at Tarth while sailing for the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones). Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) led the [Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) contingent south and defeated [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) ships in a [sudden attack](wiki:Lord_Alyn's_attack). Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) was displeased, however, as Gedmund's soldiers were left behind at Tarth and Alyn was unable to capture [Bloodstone](wiki:Bloodstone).(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
Long after the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), [Luceon of Tarth](wiki:Luceon_of_Tarth) composed "[Farewell, My Brother](wiki:Farewell,_My_Brother)", a song about the twin knights [Arryk](wiki:Arryk_Cargyll) and [Erryk Cargyll](wiki:Erryk_Cargyll).(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Lord [Selwyn Tarth](wiki:Selwyn_Tarth) meets with Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) in a midnight grove, where Davos is informed [House Tarth](wiki:House_Tarth) will not support [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

To prevent [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) from being raped, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) misleads the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions) into believing that Tarth is known as the Sapphire Isle because of an abundance of gemstones. In actuality, the name comes from the color of its waters.(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat) rejects Selwyn's offer of three hundred [golden dragons](wiki:Currency) to ransom his daughter Brienne, as Vargo demands [sapphires](wiki:Sapphire).(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

It is reported that sellswords have landed on Tarth and other islands in the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) after the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) begins [their invasion](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company).(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### The Winds of Winter[ ]

[Smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) claim that Tarth has fallen to the Golden Company.(wiki:Tarth.cite_ref-17)
