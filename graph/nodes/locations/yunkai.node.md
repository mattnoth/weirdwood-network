## Origins

Yunkai was a colony of the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis). The city survived the destruction of [Old Ghis](wiki:Old_Ghis) at the hands of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold), and was subsequently conquered by the Valyrians, who learned [slavery](wiki:Slavery) from the conquered [Ghiscari](wiki:Ghiscari) and used slaves to expand their empire.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

After the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria) and the fall of the Freehold, the Ghiscari of [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) took control of the slave trade.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) During the centuries that followed, Yunkai and her sister cities Meereen and Astapor were the linchpins of the slave trade.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

A [Triarch of Volantis](wiki:Triarch_of_Volantis) named [Vogarro](wiki:Vogarro) purchased a pleasure slave trained in the seven sighs in Yunkai. Vogarro fell in love with her, freed her, and married her, raising a great scandal. When Vogarro died his widow remained in [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), where she still continues to wield power as the [widow of the waterfront](wiki:Widow_of_the_waterfront).(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

## Appearances & Description

### Location

Yunkai is located on [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rtloiaf_slaver.27s_bay.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-mapofvalyria_7-0) One hundred leagues to the south, following the old Ghiscari coastal road, is [Astapor](wiki:Astapor) and about fifty leagues—at least a hundred and sixty-three miles(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)—to the north [Meereen](wiki:Meereen). Well mounted travellers, when riding hard, can reach Yunkai from Astapor in six days.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) To the southwest of Yunkai is the island [Yaros](wiki:Yaros).(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-mapofvalyria_7-1)

Three leagues south of Yunkai lies a birchwood forest near a slanting sandstone ridge.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

### Layout

> *See also: [Images of Yunkai](wiki:Category:Images_of_Yunkai)*

Yunkai is made of yellow bricks, with crumbling walls, towers, and tall, stepped [pyramids](wiki:Pyramid).(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) These include the golden [pyramid of Qaggaz](wiki:Pyramid_of_Qaggaz).(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) A great harpy is mounted above the city's gates.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

### People

The [Yunkish language](wiki:Yunkish_language) is a dialect of [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian), different from [Astapor's](wiki:Astapori_language), but similar enough.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

Yunkai is ruled by slavers called the [Wise Masters](wiki:Wise_Masters),(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) who are considered to be cunning(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and corrupted.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_rise_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

The highborn warriors of Yunkai wear linen skirts and tunics dyed a deep yellow and cloaks sewn with [copper](wiki:Copper) disks. They oil their hair and twist it into towers and shapes, and wear tall helms to preserve the stylings.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8) The nobility wear fringed *[tokars](wiki:Tokar)*,(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9) a sign of power, as they do in [Astapor](wiki:Astapor) and [Meereen](wiki:Meereen).

The Yunkai'i ride [horses](wiki:Horse) and [camels](wiki:Camel).(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10) The city's walls are defended by crossbowmen and slingers.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11)

Yunkai can field an army of roughly five thousand men, all slaves.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-12)

### Economy

Yunkai is known for training bed [slaves](wiki:Slaves) who are taught the way of the seven sighs and the sixteen seats of pleasure.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-13) Dancers are trained to dance since are old enough to walk, and are an expert in all the erotic arts as well.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) In Yunkai, as in [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), [eunuchs](wiki:Eunuch) are made by only cutting the testicles off(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) In Yunkai slave swordsmen are cheaper than the swords they use.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

[Coins](wiki:Currency) from Yunkai include the golden marks, brightly shining coins stamped with a stepped pyramid on one side and the harpy of Ghis on the other.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-14)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Khal [Pono](wiki:Pono)'s *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* rides to the [Slaver Cities](wiki:Slaver_Cities) to sell thousands of [slaves](wiki:Slaves).(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### A Storm of Swords

As part of [her campaign in Slaver's Bay](wiki:Targaryen_campaign_in_Slaver's_Bay), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) marches on Yunkai following the [fall of Astapor](wiki:Fall_of_Astapor). Having learned about the faith of Astapor, the [Wise Masters](wiki:Wise_Masters) of Yunkai supplement their defense with sellswords.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-15) The Wise Masters attempt to convince Daenerys to leave their city by having [Grazdan mo Eraz](wiki:Grazdan_mo_Eraz) offering her fifty thousand golden marks to leave [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) and head to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). Daenerys rejects the offer.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-16)

Daenerys defeats the city in a [battle near Yunkai](wiki:Battle_near_Yunkai), turning the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons) and the [Stormcrows](wiki:Stormcrows) in their service to her side.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-17) After Yunkai's surrender and the liberation of all [slaves](wiki:Slaves) in the city, Daenerys decides not to sack the city but instead to march further towards [Meereen](wiki:Meereen).(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) After having conquered Meereen, Daenerys receives an envoy from Astapor, Lord [Ghael](wiki:Ghael), who informs Daenerys that [Cleon](wiki:Cleon), the new king in [Astapor](wiki:Astapor), desires an alliance between Meereen and Astapor against Yunkai.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

After Daenerys's departure, Yunkai returns to slaving(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) and begins preparing for the next conflict by hiring other sellswords, such as the [Long Lances](wiki:Long_Lances), the [Windblown](wiki:Windblown), and the [Company of the Cat](wiki:Company_of_the_Cat).(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Their envoys travel to [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) and other free cities spreading false tales about Daenerys in order to gain support to cast her down. [Grazdan mo Eraz](wiki:Grazdan_mo_Eraz) travels to [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) to persuade the [triarchs](wiki:Triarchs_of_Volantis) to send their fleet against Daenerys, and other envoys ally Yunkai with [New Ghis](wiki:New_Ghis), [Tolos](wiki:Tolos), [Elyria](wiki:Elyria), and [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki).(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) The queen sends the [Stormcrows](wiki:Stormcrows) to scout the Yunkish hinterlands.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Yunkai besieges Astapor in an effort to topple the regime that had come into power after Daenerys had sacked that city.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Prince [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell), one of the Windblown, witnesses the [siege of Astapor](wiki:Siege_of_Astapor) and its sacking.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

The Yunkish army is commanded by squabbling, wealthy lordlings(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-18) who have brought slave hosts of various sizes. [Yurkhaz zo Yunzak](wiki:Yurkhaz_zo_Yunzak) has the supreme command of the Yunkishmen.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) While some such as [Yezzan zo Qaggaz](wiki:Yezzan_zo_Qaggaz) favor peace with Meereen, most of Yunkai'i want to attack the city, despite the Volantenes not having arrived yet.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) The [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons) defect from Daenerys to the Yunkai'i.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

Daenerys makes peace with Yunkai and exchanges hostages.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Yunkish envoys attend the reopening of Meereen's [fighting pits](wiki:Fighting_pits), but Yurkhaz is killed(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) in the turmoil at the [Great Pit of Daznak](wiki:Great_Pit_of_Daznak) after Dany disappears atop [Drogon](wiki:Drogon).(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) The Yunkai'i, now led by a council of masters, kill three hostages and demand that King [Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq) have the queen's [dragons](wiki:Dragon) killed.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) Yezzan has the most support for supreme command but he then dies of the [pale mare](wiki:Pale_mare). The Wise Masters rotate the supreme command amongst themselves with the generals taking a turn each day.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2) They provide the Windblown, and presumably their other sellsword companies, with a chart for the rotation.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

The [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) captures a few merchant and slaving vessels near [Yaros](wiki:Yaros) and Yunkai.(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

In the [Great Pyramid](wiki:Great_Pyramid) of Meereen, [Skahaz mo Kandaq](wiki:Skahaz_mo_Kandaq) informs Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), [Hand of the Queen](wiki:Hand_of_the_Queen) to the missing Daenerys, that the Yunkai'i have [resumed their siege](wiki:Second_siege_of_Meereen).(wiki:Yunkai.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
