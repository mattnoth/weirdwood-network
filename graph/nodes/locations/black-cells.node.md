## Origins

King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) ordered four levels of dungeons when he completed the construction of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), including the third level of the black cells.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) He ordered his men to put his nephew and squire, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), in the black cells where he was tortured to death.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Lords [Towers](wiki:Jordan_Towers), [Staunton](wiki:Lord_Staunton_(father_of_Cassella)) and [Darklyn](wiki:Lord_Darklyn_(father_of_Jonquil_Darke)) were briefly imprisoned in the black cells at the start of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen)'s reign for their supports to his uncle, King Maegor I. Jaehaerys also imprisoned the men who were involved in the torture and death of his brother Viserys, including the [King's Justice](wiki:King's_Justice), the [Lord Confessor](wiki:Lord_Confessor), the [Chief Gaoler](wiki:Chief_Gaoler), the [Commander of the City Watch](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing), and the remaining [knights](wiki:Knight) of Maegor's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

When King Jaehaerys I learned about his [daughter](wiki:Saera_Targaryen)'s sexual adventures with her three male favorites, [Roy Connington](wiki:Roy_Connington), [Jonah Mooton](wiki:Jonah_Mooton) and [Braxton Beesbury](wiki:Braxton_Beesbury), he imprisoned the three lordlings in the black cells for a night.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) between the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) and the [greens](wiki:Greens), the [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), Lord [Lyman Beesbury](wiki:Lyman_Beesbury), was killed by the greens, the first death of the war.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Some say he died from a chill after being thrown into the black cells,(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) although Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) believed Lyman was murdered by Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole).(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

[Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle) advised King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) of the greens to parlay with Queen Rhaenyra of the blacks, but the king was angered at the suggestion and threatened to have Orwyle thrown into the black cells with his black friends. Others on the green council also supported the idea to parlay, and Orwyle's imprisonment was avoided.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) However, after the blacks [captured King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) and the Red Keep, Orwyle was imprisoned in the black cells.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) He wrote his account of the Dance of the Dragons while in his cell.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Orwyle_12-0)

Rhaenyra also imprisoned Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) in the black cells, for treasonously warning [Addam Velaryon](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) to escape her arrest order.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4) Corlys was later freed and pardoned by Aegon II when he retook the city.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

After the events of the [secret siege](wiki:Secret_siege) and the coup against the [Rogares](wiki:House_Rogare), King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) ordered the perpetrators to be sent in the black cells to await trial.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

## Appearances & Description

The Red Keep has four levels of dungeons. The third level has small cells with no windows and solid wooden doors, which leaves the prisoners in darkness. Because of this, they are called the "black cells".(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) They are reserved for the most vile and dangerous prisoners,(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Racok1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) and therefore are not often used.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) The [gaoler](wiki:Undergaoler) [Rugen](wiki:Rugen) is in charge of the black cells.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

There are no windows or beds in the black cells, and not even a bucket for waste. The straw on the floor smells of urine and feces. The doors are made of wood, four inches thick and studded with iron. The dark is absolute. The only light the prisoners receive is when the door is opened to feed them, or if they receive a visitor with a torch.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) The cells are bitterly cold.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

Varys describes Maegor's four levels in full as he leads Tyrion out: the upper level holds large shared cells for common criminals, with narrow windows set high in the walls; the second level has smaller windowless cells for highborn captives, lit only by torches in the halls; the third level — the black cells proper, with wooden doors — held both Tyrion and Eddard Stark before him; and below all of these is a fourth level, built by Maegor for torment, where "once a man is taken down to the fourth level, he never sees the sun again, nor hears a human voice, nor breathes a breath free of agonizing pain." (Tyrion XI, ASOS — `sources/chapters/asos/asos-tyrion-11.md:145`)

The fourth level opens onto a small round juncture chamber directly below the [Tower of the Hand](tower-of-the-hand). Five iron-barred doors open off it, with an opening in the ceiling and a series of rungs set in the wall leading upward; an ornate brazier fashioned in the shape of a dragon's head holds coals burnt down to a sullen orange glow, and the floor bears a mosaic of a three-headed dragon wrought in red and black tiles. A long-closed, rust-frozen door from this chamber leads out to the river — the route by which Varys spirits Tyrion out of the city. (Tyrion XI, ASOS — `sources/chapters/asos/asos-tyrion-11.md:153`)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

[Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) is held in the black cells when he is arrested for treason.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Varys, in his disguise as the gaoler [Rugen](wiki:Rugen), visits Eddard in his cell and convinces him to confess to treason in exchange for the life of his daughter, [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark).(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

### A Clash of Kings

[Yoren](wiki:Yoren), a [wandering crow](wiki:Wandering_crow) for the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), has taken several men from the dungeons of the Red Keep, to bring to the [Wall](wiki:Wall). Three of them—[Rorge](wiki:Rorge), [Biter](wiki:Biter), and [Jaqen H'ghar](wiki:Jaqen_H'ghar)—were found in the black cells. Yoren keeps them fettered hand and foot in the back of a [wagon](wiki:Wagon), and vows that they will stay in irons all the way to the Wall.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Racok1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

The acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), arrests [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) for treason, and has him thrown into the black cells.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Later, on Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s request, Tyrion allows Pycelle to be removed from the dungeons.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Racok29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Tyrion is held in a tower cell after his arrest for [the murder](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon). After the death of [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), Tyrion's champion in his [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat), Tyrion is taken to the black cells.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) has Varys help Tyrion escape the black cells. Varys, in disguise as Rugen, doses the guards' wine with [sweetsleep](wiki:Sweetsleep), so that they fall asleep. After a conversation with Jaime, Tyrion walks with Varys down past the fourth level of the dungeons and into a [secret passageway](wiki:Secret_passage).(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

### A Feast for Crows

Cersei Lannister learns that Tyrion has escaped the black cells, and that the guards were found asleep. She orders their deaths,(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) and they are eventually killed by Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount) and Ser [Osmund Kettleblack](wiki:Osmund_Kettleblack).(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Cersei is also told that the gaoler Rugen is missing.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Cersei tells the former [maester](wiki:Maester) [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn), who was dismissed from the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) for vivisection and [necromancy](wiki:Magic), that he may experiment on the dying Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane). However, Qyburn must confine his studies to the black cells.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

Jaime Lannister speaks with the [chief undergaoler](wiki:Chief_undergaoler), [Rennifer Longwaters](wiki:Rennifer_Longwaters), who says that the black cells are not often used. Rennifer says that the only recent prisoners have been Tyrion Lannister, Grand Maester Pycelle, Lord Eddard Stark, and three common men that were given to the Night's Watch. Rennifer says he recommended against freeing those three men.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

The secret passages in the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand) are explored, and a passage to the black cells is discovered.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Cersei tells the [small council](wiki:Small_council) that Gregor has died and she is sending [his head](wiki:Mountain's_skull) to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne).(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Nevertheless, Qyburn still experiments on something in the black cells. Cersei allows Qyburn to use various prisoners for his work, including her former handmaiden, [Senelle](wiki:Senelle), and [two female puppeteers](wiki:Two_female_puppeteers). When Qyburn requests the puppeteers for his uses, Cersei recalls how Senelle had come with her to the black cells, unknowing of her fate until Qyburn manacled her. She also recalls hearing a "[foul thing](wiki:Robert_Strong) screaming in the darkness".(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

After the puppeteers die, Cersei tells Qyburn to take Lady [Falyse Stokeworth](wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth) for his experiments, to make her disappear.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Later, Cersei asks if Falyse is still alive, and Qyburn replies that she is, but "not entirely comfortable", and no longer capable of ruling [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth) or even feeding herself.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

Cersei has Qyburn torture the [Blue Bard](wiki:Blue_Bard) in the black cells, to convince him to give evidence against [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell). Cersei later dreams that she is in chains in the black cells, being tortured by Tyrion.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Falyse Stokeworth has died screaming in the black cells.(wiki:Black_cells.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
