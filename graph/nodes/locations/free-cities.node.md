## Origins

### Foundations

The Free Cities were founded as colonies of the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold), with the exception of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), which was founded by [slaves](wiki:Slaves) escaping the Freehold.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8) The reasons for each city's founding varies. What made them "free" is that their leaders purchased contracts from Valyria's [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlord) that gave them limited self-rule over their own affairs, but they called on their "mother" for aid in times of war or other crisis.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

The southern Free Cities, closest to [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), were all founded as trade colonies: [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) (first and oldest of the Free Cities), [Lys](wiki:Lys), [Myr](wiki:Myr), and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh). They had a high degree of contact with Valyria and each other and thus have similar cultures. Tyrosh was first created as a military outpost to guard shipping lanes through the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), though it quickly turned into a major mercantile center as well.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-8)

The northern Free Cities, farther away from Valyria, have more diverse cultures and were mostly founded by religious dissidents seeking to distance themselves from the center of Valyrian power, who disagreed with the Freehold's tolerance for the plethora of different faiths practiced within it. [Norvos](wiki:Norvos) was founded by [Bearded Priests](wiki:Bearded_Priests_of_Norvos),(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2) [Qohor](wiki:Qohor) by the cult of the [Black Goat](wiki:Black_Goat_of_Qohor),(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_qohor.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2) and [Lorath](wiki:Lorath) by followers of the Blind God [Boash](wiki:Boash).(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4) Braavos is also located at the far northwestern edge of the Free Cities because the escaped slaves who created it fled as far from Valyria itself as possible.

Lorath was originally settled by a series of other peoples, from the [Mazemakers](wiki:Mazemakers) in ancient times up to the [Andals](wiki:Andal), before they were burned out by Valyria's [dragons](wiki:Dragons), leaving it totally depopulated. Centuries later, Lorath was re-settled by dissidents from Valyria, though the ruins of its former inhabitants remain.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-5)

Alone among the Free Cities, Pentos might be a continuation of a prior settlement. Some histories say it was an Andal colony where many Valyrian merchants migrated to seek their fortunes, and because they were of low social rank they freely intermingled their bloodlines with the locals. The modern Pentoshi have similar appearances to the Andals of Westeros, but the sources claiming it existed before Valyria are biased and might not be trustworthy.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_pentos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-5)

### Rhoynish Wars

After many centuries, Valyria's expansion of the Free Cities and other colonies into western Essos brought it into conflict with the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar), a loose confederation of six independent city-states along the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne). These trade wars flared off and on for two centuries, but eventually the [Rhoynish Wars](wiki:Rhoynish_Wars) climaxed when [Garin the Great](wiki:Garin_the_Great), [Prince of Chroyane](wiki:Prince_of_Chroyane), rallied the remaining Rhoynar city-states into a grand alliance which threatened Volantis itself. Valyria rushed to the aid of its colony with an army of three hundred dragons, and in the end the Rhoynar city-states were totally defeated and left in ruins.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

### The Doom

The Free Cities continued to thrive for another six centuries, until [Valyria](wiki:Valyria) itself was destroyed in a single day by the volcanic cataclysm known as the [Doom](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria). Their vast empire quickly collapsed as the outer provinces and colonies fought for supremacy, a chaotic time known as the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood).(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

[Volantis](wiki:Volantis), the eldest and largest of the Free Cities, attempted to seize power over the others, managing to take and hold [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Myr](wiki:Myr) for two generations. Eventually the other Free Cities united against it, however, and the Volantenes were repulsed, though the alliance then collapsed. This coincided with the first great expansion of the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) mounted hordes from the [grasslands](wiki:Dothraki_sea) of central Essos, no longer held in check by fear of Valyria's dragons. Struggling to face the attacks from the Dothraki, further attempts to conquer and unify all of the Free Cities ended.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1)(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

Some called the city of [Gogossos](wiki:Gogossos) on the [Basilisk Isles](wiki:Basilisk_Isles) the "Tenth Free City" when it grew rich and powerful during the Century of Blood, until it was destroyed by plague.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_basilisk_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) [Essaria](wiki:Essaria), near the Dothraki grasslands, has sometimes been considered the "Lost Free City" as the colony was reduced to ruins by the horselords.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1)

### Recent History

Over the past two centuries, [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) has fought six wars against [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) concerning the practice of [slavery](wiki:Slavery) and the lands located in between the two cities. Four of these wars were won by Braavos. The last war ended in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC) and in the peace accords, Pentos was forced by Braavos to make several concessions. These included the (nominal) termination of slavery in Pentos and their withdrawal from the slave trade, as well as a drastic reduction of the size of the Pentoshi military force.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_pentos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-6)(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Radwd1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1)

In [96 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [Myr](wiki:Myr), [Lys](wiki:Lys), and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) united as the [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy) after defeating Volantis at the [Battle of the Borderland](wiki:Battle_of_the_Borderland), driving the older city from the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands).(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-3) Next, the Myrish prince-admiral [Craghas Drahar](wiki:Craghas_Drahar) led an invasion of the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), clearing out the pirate dens of the islands and making safe the trade lanes of the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). When the Triarchy began to impose high tolls on trade in the narrow sea, the [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) began to resent the Triarchy's presence on the islands. Foremost was Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) of [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark), who forged an alliance with Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) and with him [fought for dominion](wiki:War_for_the_Stepstones) over the Stepstones against the Triarchy, beginning in [106 AC](wiki:106_AC). The conflict raged on beyond [115 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), when Prince Daemon abandoned the cause.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-4)

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the Triarchy allied with the faction of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) against Aegon's half-sister, [Rhaenyra](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), and her husband, Prince Daemon. They participated in the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet), where they took major losses. By [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), the "eternal alliance" between the three Free Cities had begun to tear itself apart.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons:_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) An alliance between Braavos, Pentos, and Lorath in the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War) eventually brought an end to the Triarchy.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-9)

During the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring), the [Rogare Bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank) in Lys grew more powerful than the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos).(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) This power increased further following the marriage of [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare) to Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), after which [House Rogare](wiki:House_Rogare) became involved in Westerosi politics. However, following the sudden deaths of [Lysandro Rogare](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare), the head of the family, and his brother [Drazenko](wiki:Drazenko_Rogare), the family's power rapidly decreased.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-10)

During the reign of the Westerosi king [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen), Braavos was at war with Pentos and Lys. When Daeron attempted to negotiate a marriage-alliance between the [Sealord of Braavos](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos) and one of his sisters, to help deal with the pirate activity in the Stepstones, Pentos and Lys responded by sending crucial aid to the [rebels in Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne).(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-11)

The surviving Westerosi rebels who had fought on the side of [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) during the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) fled to the Free Cities, where they took work as mercenaries.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers), who had escaped together with Daemon's widow and remaining children, eventually ended up in [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh).(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) When he saw the former strength of [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) scatter, Aegor formed the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC), thereby binding the exiles together.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3)(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) Shortly after its creation, the Golden Company sacked Qohor after the city refused to honor the contract it had made with the company. This event established the reputation of the sellword company,(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rawoiafaegor_rivers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) which became known as the foremost free company of the Disputed Lands.(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1)(wiki:Free_Cities.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-4)
