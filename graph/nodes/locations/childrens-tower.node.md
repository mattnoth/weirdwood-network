## Appearances & Description

The Children's Tower is tall, slender, and green with moss. Half of its crenelations are gone from its crown, and its shattered top is open to the wind and the rain.(wiki:Children's_Tower.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

## Origins

Legend has it that the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) called upon their gods from the top of Children's Tower to send the [hammer of the waters](wiki:Hammer_of_the_waters) and shatter the [Neck](wiki:Neck).(wiki:Children's_Tower.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Children's_Tower.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

[House Umber](wiki:House_Umber) takes the Children's Tower as their seat when the [northern](wiki:North) host of [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) stays at [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin).(wiki:Children's_Tower.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

During the [siege of Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin), [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) sends [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) to offer the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) garrison food and safe passage if they surrender to [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) unarmed. Theon sees pale faces at the battlements of the [Gatehouse Tower](wiki:Gatehouse_Tower) and through the broken masonry crowning the Children's Tower. He is later informed by a guard that all of the ironborn within the Children's Tower are dead; [Dagon Codd](wiki:Dagon_Codd) went over a few days ago and discovered two survivors eating the corpses of the deceased, so Dagon killed them both. The pale faces may thus be [crannogmen](wiki:Crannogmen) who retook the Children's Tower.(wiki:Children's_Tower.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
