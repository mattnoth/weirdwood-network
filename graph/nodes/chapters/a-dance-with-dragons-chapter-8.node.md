## Narrative Arc

As dawn breaks [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) awakes to find the litter has stopped. He meets [Haldon](wiki:Haldon) Halfmaester and Ser [Rolly Duckfield](wiki:Rolly_Duckfield), who take on supplies from [Illyrio](wiki:Illyrio) before leaving with Tyrion. Illyrio tells them the dwarf's name is Yollo, although Tyrion, worried they will realize the name is a lie, claims it is his name in [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) and his name is Hugor Hill. The pair tease Tyrion with tales of river [pirates](wiki:Pirates) and the [Shrouded Lord](wiki:Shrouded_Lord). Tyrion and Haldon test each others' book knowledge while Duck recounts the events that led to his knighting at the hands of [Griff](wiki:Griff) and his unusual name. They ride until they reach the [Little Rhoyne](wiki:Little_Rhoyne) at [Ghoyan Drohe](wiki:Ghoyan_Drohe), where a poleboat, the *[Shy Maid](wiki:Shy_Maid),* is waiting for them.

On board are the boy, [Young Griff](wiki:Young_Griff), and his father, Griff. The older man reads a letter sent to him by [Illyrio](wiki:Illyrio), telling of Tyrion's murder of his father. Griff is mistrustful and Tyrion is convinced he is no ordinary [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) - he can read, and he is a [knight](wiki:Knight), having knighted Duck himself. Tyrion promises to serve [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) faithfully, but asks "What if we should find the queen and discover that this talk of dragons was just some sailor's drunken fancy? This wide world is full of such mad tales. Grumkins and snarks, ghosts and ghouls, mermaids, rock goblins, winged horses, winged pigs...winged lions." Griff tells him to guard his tongue or lose it.
