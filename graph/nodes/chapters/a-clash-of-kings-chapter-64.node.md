## Narrative Arc

After aiding in the [fall of Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal), [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark) is now serving Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) as his cupbearer, "Nan". She is bothered by the killing and head-staking of many of the servants who worked for Lord [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), most of them inherited from Lady [Shella Whent](wiki:Shella_Whent) who held Harrenhal prior to Lord Tywin.(wiki:A_Clash_of_Kings-Chapter_64.cite_ref-1) Arya does not wish to reveal herself to the [Lord of the Dreadfort](wiki:Lord_of_the_Dreadfort), even though she knows Roose to be one of King [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark)'s bannermen.

Arya overhears a meeting between Bolton and his advisors, mainly [Freys](wiki:House_Frey). The Freys believe that Robb will lose, and that they should sue for peace and leave Harrenhal. Roose states that he is not a man to be undone, like [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) was. Arya learns that [Winterfell has fallen](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell) and is horrified when she finds out that her brothers are dead. Roose dismisses the Freys and has Arya remove the leeches.

[Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) reads a letter from the Lady [Walda](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Merrett)), Roose's s new wife. Lord Bolton orders the letter burned, and a message sent to [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart), who has recently taken [Darry](wiki:Darry) from the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister). Roose orders Helman and [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) to burn the castle and put the people within to the sword in the name of King Robb, and then to strike east for [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale). Roose says that both men will wish vengeance for what has transpired to their families and homes in the [north](wiki:North).

Roose announces that he will hunt wolves that day, for he cannot sleep with all the howling at night. Meanwhile, Arya practices her [water dance](wiki:Water_dance) in the [godswood of Harrenhal](wiki:Godswood_of_Harrenhal).

That evening, Lord Bolton returns with nine dead wolves, and he tells Arya that he means to give Harrenhal to [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat) when he returns to the north. Arya is to remain at Harrenhal to serve the goat. Outside the [Wailing Tower](wiki:Wailing_Tower), which is occupied by the Freys, she hears much shouting within and sees [Elmar](wiki:Elmar_Frey) crying on the steps. The young son of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) says, "We’ve been dishonored. There was a bird from the Twins. My lord father says I'll need to marry someone else." Elmar was promised a princess originally, but whether or not he knows it to be Arya(wiki:A_Clash_of_Kings-Chapter_64.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) is unknown.

Arya decides to flee [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), and eventually convinces [Gendry](wiki:Gendry) and [Hot Pie](wiki:Hot_Pie) to come with her that night. They meet at the [Tower of Ghosts](wiki:Tower_of_Ghosts), Gendry says the postern gate is guarded, and Arya says she will get rid of the [Bolton guard](wiki:House_Bolton_guards). She approaches him openly and tells him that Lord Roose has her giving a silver piece to all the guards for their service. She takes out the coin Jaqen gave her and drops it, when the guard reach for it, she slits his throat and the three ride out through a sally port and away from Harrenhal.
