## Narrative Arc

A respite at the falling of snow motivates [Jon](wiki:Jon_Snow) to range [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall), leading the new recruits of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) who follow the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) to the same grove of [weirwoods](wiki:Weirwood) in the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest) where he and [Sam](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) swore their vows.(wiki:A_Dance_with_Dragons-Chapter_35.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) Scouting ahead of them go [Tom Barleycorn](wiki:Tom_Barleycorn), [Big Liddle](wiki:Duncan_Liddle) and [Luke of Longtown](wiki:Luke_(Longtown)).

There are six new recruits - [Hareth the Horse](wiki:Hareth_(Mole's_Town)) from [Mole's Town](wiki:Mole's_Town), [Arron](wiki:Arron) and [Emrick](wiki:Emrick) from [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle), [Satin](wiki:Satin) from [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) and the [wildlings](wiki:Free_folk) [Leathers](wiki:Leathers) and [Jax](wiki:Jax).

Of the sixty-three wildlings that Jon invited to join the Watch, Leathers and Jax are the only two to accept so far. Among the others wildlings come to the wall, nineteen are girls and women and were sent to [Hardin's Tower](wiki:Hardin's_Tower) to avoid trouble with the men in [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black). Their presence makes the men uneasy and there has already been an incident. The [spearwives](wiki:Spearwife) know how to defend themselves and their girls, and three men had to be locked in ice cells.

Jon means to garrison [Deep Lake](wiki:Deep_Lake), [Sable Hall](wiki:Sable_Hall) and [Long Barrow](wiki:Long_Barrow) with wildlings under command of offices of the Night's Watch, and to choose an almost completely female contingent for Long Barrow in order to avoid future harassment. [Iron Emmett](wiki:Iron_Emmett) and [Dolorous Edd](wiki:Dolorous_Edd) will be Long Barrow's commander and chief steward.

Other wildlings are also a cause of tension and incidents with the Brothers of the Night's Watch, most noticeably [Halleck](wiki:Halleck) and his followers. Jon is also worried that the [Thenns](wiki:Thenn_(people)) may blame him for the death of [Styr](wiki:Styr).

Tom Barleycorn finds a small group of wildlings, including a child and his mother, a Thenn, a [Hornfoot](wiki:Hornfoot) and [a giant](wiki:Wun_Weg_Wun_Dar_Wun) in the weirwood grove. With the advantage of surprise and an effort at diplomacy from Leathers, they succeed in avoiding a fight and even convince the survivors to return to Castle Black with them.

The recruits swear their vows in the presence of the wildlings. As he hears the verse about guarding *the realms of men*, Jon realizes that his duties involve protecting the wildlings as well as any other men.

They return to Castle Black, bringing the two wildling corpses they found with them. Jon consigns the corpses to the ice cells and receives a letter from [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), learning that he succeeded at [taking Deepwood Motte](wiki:Retaking_of_Deepwood_Motte) and received support from various northern clans and houses along the way, including survivors from the [battle at Winterfell](wiki:Battle_at_Winterfell). His troops now number five thousand and growing. Stannis has also learned that [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) means to marry [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark)(wiki:A_Dance_with_Dragons-Chapter_35.cite_ref-2) in [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and promises Jon to save her if he can. He means to have the help of [Arnolf Karstark](wiki:Arnolf_Karstark) and [Mors Umber](wiki:Mors_Umber) at his attempt to conquer Winterfell.

Jon wonders if Arya could have escaped without Stannis's help, as predicted by [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre).(wiki:A_Dance_with_Dragons-Chapter_35.cite_ref-Radwd28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) He also wonders if [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) could be lying about having Arya in order to bait Stannis and thinks about [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) and the six spearwives who went with him to rescue Arya.(wiki:A_Dance_with_Dragons-Chapter_35.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
