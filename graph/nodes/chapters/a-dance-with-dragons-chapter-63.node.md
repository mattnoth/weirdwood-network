## Narrative Arc

Despite his crew's unease at [Moqorro](wiki:Moqorro)'s continued presence, [Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) insists that the [red god](wiki:R'hllor) will help them on their journey. Though his arm and hand are now burned, they are stronger than ever. He says, *“Two gods are with me now...No foe can stand before two gods.”*

The Iron Fleet captures a number of trading ships: *[Ghiscari Dawn](wiki:Ghiscari_Dawn)*, *[Dove](wiki:Dove)*, two unnamed ships that he renames as *[Ghost](wiki:Ghost_(ship))* and *[Shade](wiki:Shade)*, an unnamed fat galleas, an unnamed trading galley, an unnamed fishing ketch, and a slaver galley named the *[Willing Maiden](wiki:Willing_Maiden)*. Moqorro's prophecies are instrumental in the capture of these fleets. As a show of his appreciation, Victarion burns seven [Lysene](wiki:Lys) bed slaves as a tribute to both the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God) and R'hllor. After this sacrifice, a favorable wind rises for the Iron Fleet, which now numbers 60 ships with the new additions.

That night, Victarion allows Moqorro to inspect the [dragon horn](wiki:Dragon_horn) given to him by his brother [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy). Moqorro reads the [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) glyphs displayed on the horn to Victarion, telling him that the horn's name is written as *"I am [Dragonbinder](wiki:Dragonbinder)."* Moqorro goes on to explain that no mortal man can blow the horn and live. However, he states that *"Who blows the hellhorn matters not. The [dragons](wiki:Dragon) will come to the horn's master. You must claim the horn. With blood.”*
