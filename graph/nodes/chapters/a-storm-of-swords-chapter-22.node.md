## Narrative Arc

The outlaws first visit Lord [Lymond Lychester](wiki:Lymond_Lychester) and then the [Lady of the Leaves](wiki:Lady_of_the_Leaves) in search of word on Lord [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion)'s whereabouts. But none have seen him, only hearing rumors that he was dead—hanged once, killed by [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat), [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch), and twice by [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane). [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark) heard similar rumors at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), but [Lem](wiki:Lem) and [Tom](wiki:Tom_of_Sevenstreams) know that Beric is still alive.

The outlaws plan to ransom Arya at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) after they meet up with Beric and [Thoros of Myr](wiki:Thoros_of_Myr). They arrive at [High Heart](wiki:High_Heart), an ancient hill sacred to the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest). Thirty-one mighty [weirwoods](wiki:Weirwood) had stood at the top of the hill, but only their stumps remained from after the [Andals](wiki:Andals) chopped them down. Arya can still feel the power of the place. An [old dwarf woman](wiki:Ghost_of_High_Heart) speaks in private to Lem, Tom, and [Greenbeard](wiki:Greenbeard), but Arya overhears the conversation. The woman has dreams while sleeping among the weirwood stumps, and tells them she had dreamed of "a shadow with a burning heart killing a golden stag";(wiki:A_Storm_of_Swords-Chapter_22.cite_ref-1) of "a man without a face, waiting on a swaying bridge, on his shoulder perched a drowned crow with seaweed hanging from its wings";(wiki:A_Storm_of_Swords-Chapter_22.cite_ref-2) and of "a raging river and a woman that was a fish…dead with red tears on her cheeks, but when her eyes did open, I woke from terror."(wiki:A_Storm_of_Swords-Chapter_22.cite_ref-3)

At [Acorn Hall](wiki:Acorn_Hall), [Lady Smallwood](wiki:Ravella_Swann) informs them that Thoros had been through recently, and that [Karstark](wiki:House_Karstark) men were searching for the [Kingslayer](wiki:Jaime_Lannister). Arya and [Gendry](wiki:Gendry) go off to the smithy, and the former apprentice explains that Thoros was a charlatan, using regular swords with [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) to make his flaming sword. After getting in a playful fight with Gendry, Arya tears a dress given to her by Lady Smallwood. As the Brotherhood is getting ready to leave, Lady Smallwood gives Arya clothes that had belonged to her now deceased son. Embarrassed by her actions, Arya apologizes to Lady Smallwood before leaving.
