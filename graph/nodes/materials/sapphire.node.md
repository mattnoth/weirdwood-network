## Culture

The [Summer Isles](wiki:Summer_Isles) are rich in gemstones, including sapphires.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)
[Tarth](wiki:Tarth) is called the Sapphire Isle,(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Rasos1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) but it has no such gemstones. The name is from the blue of its waters.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
[Tobho Mott](wiki:Tobho_Mott) wears a sapphire as large as a pigeon's egg, on a heavy [silver](wiki:Silver) chain around his neck.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) has a slim [coronet](wiki:Crown) made of [gold](wiki:Gold) and sapphires.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

[Dagmer Cleftjaw](wiki:Dagmer_Cleftjaw) has rings set with chunks of sapphire, [garnet](wiki:Garnet), and [dragonglass](wiki:Dragonglass).(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

## Origins

The legendary [Symeon Star-Eyes](wiki:Symeon_Star-Eyes) was a [knight](wiki:Knight) who lost both of his eyes. He replaced them by putting star sapphires in the empty sockets, or so the singers claim.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
The Summer Isles engaged in trade with the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold), sending them gemstones (including sapphires) and other rare and exotic resources, in exchange for gold, [iron](wiki:Iron), [tin](wiki:Tin), and other metals.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) replaced his missing eye with a sapphire.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
In [134 AC](wiki:134_AC), when Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) after the first of his great voyages, young Queen [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon) presented him with heavy golden chain studded with sapphires.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
[Shiera Seastar](wiki:Shiera_Seastar)'s favorite piece of [jewelry](wiki:Jewelry) was a heavy silver necklace, with alternating star sapphires and [emeralds](wiki:Emerald), that complemented her mismatched eyes — one dark blue, the other bright green.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-17)
In [284 AC](wiki:284_AC), when [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) wed King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), her uncle [Gerion](wiki:Gerion_Lannister) gave Robert a gilded dagger with an [ivory](wiki:Ivory) grip and a sapphire pommel.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

When the bodies of [rangers](wiki:Ranger) [Othor](wiki:Othor) and [Jafer Flowers](wiki:Jafer_Flowers) are found by the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), their eyes appear to be blue as sapphires, though they were not that color before. The corpses later rise as [wights](wiki:Wight).(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Ragot52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

To protect [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) tells [Urswyck](wiki:Urswyck) that Tarth is known as the Sapphire Isle. Unaware the island is so named because of the color of its waters instead of being rich in gemstones, the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions) expect that Lord [Selwyn Tarth](wiki:Selwyn_Tarth) will pay a large ransom for his daughter.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Rasos31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

[Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy)'s [Silence](wiki:Silence) returns to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) with holds full of treasure, including sapphires big as eggs.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) imagines the [House of Black and White](wiki:House_of_Black_and_White) has treasure chambers full of precious metals and gemstones, including sapphires as blue as the sea.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)
Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) wears a chain of [gold](wiki:Gold) and sapphires.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
When Brienne of Tarth and her companions are captured by the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) and about to be hanged, she attempts to save her [squire](wiki:Squire), [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne), by claiming her father will ransom him for sapphires, but they do not care.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

When the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) pass through the [Wall](wiki:Wall), the tribute collected from them includes a broken [sword](wiki:Sword) with three sapphires in its hilt.(wiki:Sapphire.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)
