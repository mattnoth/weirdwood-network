## Culture

Deposits of iron ore are plentiful on [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk), [Harlaw](wiki:Harlaw), [Orkmont](wiki:Orkmont), and [Pyke](wiki:Pyke_(island)) in the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) While it is often believed the island chain is named after the ore, the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) themselves insist their lands are instead named after their people's fierce, hard, unbending nature.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Followers of the [Old Way](wiki:Old_Way) desire to claim treasures by paying the [iron price](wiki:Iron_price).(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) [Iron Holt](wiki:Iron_Holt) is the seat of [House Wynch](wiki:House_Wynch) on [Pyke](wiki:Pyke_(island)).(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) [Iron Kiss](wiki:Iron_Kiss), [Iron Lady](wiki:Iron_Lady), [Iron Vengeance](wiki:Iron_Vengeance), [Iron Victory](wiki:Iron_Victory), [Iron Wind](wiki:Iron_Wind), and [Iron Wing](wiki:Iron_Wing) are ships of the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet). Their [Lord Captain](wiki:Lord_Captain_of_the_Iron_Fleet), [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), thinks of himself as an iron captain.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) [Silence](wiki:Silence) has a black iron maiden on its prow.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) King [Urras Greyiron](wiki:Urras_Greyiron) is remembered as Urras Ironfoot.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)
Singers say that [demons](wiki:Demons) can be killed by cold iron;(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) according to [Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan), the legendary [Others](wiki:Others) hated iron.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) "[Iron Lances](wiki:Iron_Lances)" is a popular song.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)
The [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) traditionally place an iron [longsword](wiki:Longsword) on the lap of each statue of the [Lords of Winterfell](wiki:Lords_of_Winterfell) within [their crypt](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell). This is said to keep vengeful spirits in the crypt.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
Ser [Jacelyn Bywater](wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater) is known as Ironhand for his replacement hand.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) [Areo Hotah](wiki:Areo_Hotah) refers to his axe as his his ash-and-iron wife.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) [Roger Ryswell](wiki:Roger_Ryswell) uses an iron horsehead brooch.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

## Origins

### Antiquity[ ]

The [Cymmeri](wiki:Cymmeri) are believed to have been the first people to work iron.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)
[Septons](wiki:Septon) claim that [the Smith](wiki:Smith_(the_Seven)) wrought a suit of iron plates for each of the forty-four sons of [Hugor of the Hill](wiki:Hugor_of_the_Hill).(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) In [Essos](wiki:Essos), however, it is believed that the [Andals](wiki:Andals) learned how to work iron from the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar).(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-1) The Andals used iron weapons and armor to defeat various tribes, including possibly [hairy men](wiki:Hairy_men).(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) During the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), the Andals' usage of iron aided in their triumph over the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), who mostly used [bronze](wiki:Bronze).(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-1)
The [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) used [slavery](wiki:Slavery) to unearth iron for making [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel).(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_valyria.27s_children.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)
[House Yronwood](wiki:House_Yronwood) grew wealthy from deposits of iron, [tin](wiki:Tin), and [silver](wiki:Silver) in their [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) lands.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_kingdoms_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)
Some [Kings of the Reach](wiki:Kings_of_the_Reach) from [House Gardener](wiki:House_Gardener) wore [crowns](wiki:Crown) of iron thorns when they rode to war.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_the_gardener_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) Andals who were welcomed to the [Reach](wiki:Reach) taught First Men how to work iron.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)
After slaughtering rivals on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk), [Urron Greyiron](wiki:Urron_Greyiron) established the tradition of [Kings of the Iron Islands](wiki:Kings_of_the_Iron_Islands) wearing a black iron crown instead of a driftwood crown.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) The [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) traded iron for timber in the era of [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare).(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1)
Northwestern [Essos](wiki:Essos) is rich in iron, tin, [lead](wiki:Lead), and [slate](wiki:Slate). The [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos) was founded in an abandoned iron mine.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) The mountains of [Ib](wiki:Ib) are rich in iron, [gold](wiki:Gold), and tin.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0)

### Targaryen Era[ ]

The [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), the seat of the [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms), was created from the swords of men defeated by [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0)
Lord [Jasper Wylde](wiki:Jasper_Wylde) was known as Ironrod,(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0) while Ser [Harmon of the Reeds](wiki:Harmon_of_the_Reeds) was called Iron-Banger.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Ser [Hugh Hammer](wiki:Hugh_Hammer) infuriated Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)) by wearing a crown of black iron.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)
King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) ordered the construction of artificial [dragon](wiki:Dragon) of wood and iron. The siege engines faltered and burned in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), however.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0) King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) wore a crown with black iron points in a band of red [gold](wiki:Gold).(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0)
Lord [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon) decorated his helm with a rack of iron antlers.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0) While serving as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) wore a brooch in the shape of an iron hand.(wiki:Iron.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0)
