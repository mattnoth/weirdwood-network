## Origins

After the death of her brother-husband, Prince [Aegon the Uncrowned](wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned), Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) dyed the silver hair of their twin daughters, [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) and [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon)), to hide them from King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). However, the ploy did not work, as the girls were found by [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower), one of Maegor's wives and his [mistress of whisperers](wiki:Mistress_of_whisperers).(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
In [50 AC](wiki:50_AC), Aerea's hair was dyed muddy brown by the order of her grandmother, Queen Regent [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), to disguise her as a [common](wiki:Smallfolk) girl and hide her from Ser [Ronnal Baratheon](wiki:Ronnal_Baratheon).(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
[Racallio Ryndoon](wiki:Racallio_Ryndoon) dyed his long hair and beard purple, his favorite color, with orange streaks.(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
In [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_Targaryen) ordered his son, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), that when traveling incognito as the [squire](wiki:Squire) "Egg" with Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), he must keep his distinctive silver-gold hair shaved or dyed.(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
In [212 AC](wiki:212_AC), Duncan and Egg encountered the black-haired [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight), Ser [John the Fiddler](wiki:John_the_Fiddler), who was actually the pretender king Daemon II Blackfyre, dyeing his silver-gold hair to remain incognito while traveling through Westeros.(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Septa](wiki:Septa) [Eglantine](wiki:Eglantine) puts brown dye in Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon)’s hair when they are aboard the [Seaswift](wiki:Seaswift), on the way to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos). She tells Mycella that it is a game, but Myrcella realizes that it is meant to keep her safe in case the ship is taken by her uncle, the rebel King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Queen [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) encounters [Daario Naharis](wiki:Daario_Naharis), the Tyroshi captain of the [Stormcrows](wiki:Stormcrows), who dyes his hair and beard blue, and paints his mustache [gold](wiki:Gold).(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) After the [sack of](wiki:Siege_of_Meereen) [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), Daario dyes his hair and beard a deep rich purple, which also makes his eyes look almost purple.(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
[Lysa Tully](wiki:Lysa_Tully) tells [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) that she looks too much like her mother, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), and that in order to keep her disguise as Alayne Stone, [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish)'s [bastard](wiki:Bastard) daughter, Sansa's hair will have to be darkened before she is brought to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

In the Eyrie, [Alayne Stone](wiki:Alayne_Stone) dyes her hair with a wash given to her by her aunt Lysa. The wash comes from Tyrosh, across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). It changes her rich auburn hair into a burnt brown, but it is seldom long before the red begins creeping back at the roots. Alayne worries what she will do when the dye runs out.(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
Cersei observes the [Blue Bard](wiki:Blue_Bard), who wears all blue clothing, noticing that he even dyes his hair blue.(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)
When [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) arrives at [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), the captain of the [Huntress](wiki:Huntress) tells him that [ironmen](wiki:Ironmen) captured a Tyroshi merchantman on the straits a fortnight ago. They killed her crew, donned their clothes, and used the ship's cargo of dyes to color their whiskers half a hundred colors, meaning to sneak into Oldtown disguised as Tyroshi. However, the oarsmaster of the [Lady of the Tower](wiki:Lady_of_the_Tower) spoke Tyroshi, and discovered the deception when he hailed the ironmen in that language but none could answer.(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) meets [Griff](wiki:Griff) and [Young Griff](wiki:Young_Griff), a father and son with dyed blue hair. Young Griff says his mother was Tyroshi, and they dye their hair in her memory.(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Young Griff is later revealed to be the disguised Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(Young_Griff)), who allegedly survived [his reported death](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) in the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
In [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys), Prince Aegon dyes his hair a deep dark blue, which [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) notes, makes his [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes) look blue as well.(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)
[Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen)'s [paramour](wiki:Paramour), [Daario Naharis](wiki:Daario_Naharis), dyes his hair back to blue to please her.(wiki:Hair_dye.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
