## Appearances & Description

Obsidian is volcanic glass formed by immense heat and pressure in the earth.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Shaw_5-0) [Smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) like to say that dragonglass is made by [dragons](wiki:Dragons), while [maesters](wiki:Maester) say it comes from the fires of the earth.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
Obsidian was used by the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) to fabricate weapons;(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) obsidian blades are sharper than steel but far more brittle.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Racok43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
The [Valyrians](wiki:Valyria) used obsidian to make their [glass candles](wiki:Glass_candle).(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
There are large deposits of dragonglass on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) It is an export product from [Asshai](wiki:Asshai),(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and possibly the [Shivering Sea](wiki:Shivering_Sea),(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) while the [Skagosi](wiki:Skagos) trade obsidian blades and arrowheads.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_stoneborn_of_skagos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Some [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) carry dragonglass knives.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
Dragonglass weapons are one of the few weaknesses of the [Others](wiki:Others).(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
According to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin),
I've given it [magical](wiki:Magic) characteristics that of course real obsidian doesn't necessarily have. After all, we live in a world that has no magic. [My world](wiki:Known_world) does have magic, so it's a little bit different.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Shaw_5-1)

## Culture

Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully) has a brooch depicting a black fish in [gold](wiki:Gold) and obsidian.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
[Mors Umber](wiki:Mors_Umber) wears a chunk of dragonglass in place of a lost eye.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
[Dagmer Cleftjaw](wiki:Dagmer_Cleftjaw) has rings set with chunks of [sapphire](wiki:Sapphire), [garnet](wiki:Garnet), and dragonglass.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
Before acolytes of the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) say their vows, they must spend a night attempting to light a [candle of obsidian](wiki:Glass_candle).(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
A bronze arch in [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) depicts two snakes mating, and their scales are made of delicate flakes of [jade](wiki:Jade), obsidian, and [lapis lazuli](wiki:Lapis_lazuli).(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
The dark eyes of Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) have been compared to obsidian,(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) as have the shiny eyes of Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne).(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

## Origins

The [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) made tools and weapons out of dragonglass, including daggers, blades, and arrowheads.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) During the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age), the children fought against their greatest foes, the [giants](wiki:Giants), with obsidian weapons, as possibly proved by Maester [Kennet](wiki:Kennet_(maester)), whose study describes a giant's burial at a barrow near the [Long Lake](wiki:Long_Lake), who had obsidian arrowheads amidst his extant ribs.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)
In [their war](wiki:War_of_the_First_Men_and_the_children_of_the_forest) against the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), the children with their dragonglass weapons could not stand against the bronze weapons of the First Men.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_the_breaking.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) During the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), it was recorded by the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) that the children of the forest gave the black brothers a hundred obsidian daggers every year.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
During the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), red clouds rained down dragonglass and the black blood of [demons](wiki:Demon).(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
[Roro Uhoris](wiki:Roro_Uhoris) sailed [Cobblecat](wiki:Cobblecat) into the [Shivering Sea](wiki:Shivering_Sea) to trade steel to the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) for [ivory](wiki:Ivory), furs, [amber](wiki:Amber), and obsidian.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

In his turret in [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) shows a handful of arrowheads to [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark), [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark), and [Osha](wiki:Osha). Bran examines an arrowhead, noticing that the black glass is slick and shiny. He thinks it is beautiful, and asks Luwin if he can keep one.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

At the [Fist of the First Men](wiki:Fist_of_the_First_Men), [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) discovers a cache of knives, leaf-shaped spearheads, and numerous arrowheads made of obsidian. Beneath the dragonglass is an [old warhorn](wiki:Old_warhorn), and when Jon shakes the dirt from inside it, a stream of arrowheads falls out. Jon gives [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) a spearhead and a dozen arrowheads as well, and passes the rest out among his other friends for luck.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Racok43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
During [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen)'s stay in [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), dragonglass from [Asshai](wiki:Asshai) is among the gifts she receives from people seeking to learn the truth behind the rumors about living [dragons](wiki:Dragon). The dragonglass is among the many offerings Daenerys sells to gather the wealth she ends up wasting on the [Pureborn](wiki:Pureborn).(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) After the death of the [Undying Ones](wiki:Undying_Ones), [Xaro Xhoan Daxos](wiki:Xaro_Xhoan_Daxos) tells Daenerys that [glass candles](wiki:Glass_candle) that have not burned in a hundred years are now burning in the house of [Urrathon Night-Walker](wiki:Urrathon_Night-Walker).(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Samwell and the few survivors from the [fight at the Fist](wiki:Fight_at_the_Fist) escape south with a heavy snow falling. Upon an [undead horse](wiki:Wight), a lone [Other](wiki:Other) appears. In a blind panic, Sam slays the Other by stabbing it with the dragonglass dagger given to him by Jon. The dagger causes the Other to shrink and puddle, dissolving away in twenty heartbeats. Only the dagger remains, wreathed in steam. [Grenn](wiki:Grenn) goes to scoop up the dragonglass dagger, but has to fling it down again as it is extremely cold.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)
At [Craster's Keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep), Samwell discusses obsidian with [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont). The [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) tells Sam that if dragonglass kills the Others as Sam claims, he wants more of it. Sam remarks that the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) would know where to find obsidian, but Lord Commander Mormont tells him that the children are all dead.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
Sam and [Gilly](wiki:Gilly), escaping from the [mutiny at Craster's Keep](wiki:Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep), are menaced by a wight which was once [Small Paul](wiki:Small_Paul). Sam attempts to stab him with the dragonglass dagger, but it shatters upon contact with Paul's chainmail.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rasos46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)
Having relinquished [Ice](wiki:Ice) to [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne) now wields a greatsword with a chunk of dragonglass carved in the shape of a grinning skull on its pommel.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)
At the [Wall](wiki:Wall), after hearing about the properties of dragonglass from Sam, King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) sends word to Ser [Rolland Storm](wiki:Rolland_Storm), the castellan of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), to begin mining obsidian on the [volcanic isle](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)).(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

### A Feast For Crows[ ]

In [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), [Leo Tyrell](wiki:Leo_Tyrell_(son_of_Moryn)) tells his fellow acolytes and novices of the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) that there is a [glass candle](wiki:Glass_candle) burning in Archmaester [Marwyn](wiki:Marwyn)'s chambers. [Armen](wiki:Armen) explains the vigil an acolyte must hold to become a [maester](wiki:Maester), where one fruitlessly attempts to light one of the Citadel's glass candles, and says that obsidian does not burn. [Pate](wiki:Pate_(novice)) remembers that the smallfolk call obsidian dragonglass, and [Alleras](wiki:Alleras) wonders if the return of [dragons](wiki:Dragons) is what lets the candle burn again.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
At the Wall, Samwell tells Jon, the new Lord Commander, that he has learned the children of the forest used to give the Night's Watch a hundred obsidian daggers every year, during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)
When Sam arrives at the Citadel, there is a black glass candle burning in Marwyn's study on the [Isle of Ravens](wiki:Isle_of_Ravens). Marwyn tells him that it burns but is not consumed.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### A Dance With Dragons[ ]

In [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), [Quaithe](wiki:Quaithe) tells [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) that the glass candles are burning.(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)
Jon arms his [rangers](wiki:Rangers) with obsidian weapons when they venture into the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest).(wiki:Dragonglass.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)
