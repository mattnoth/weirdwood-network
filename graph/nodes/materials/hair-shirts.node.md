## Origins

In [Essos](wiki:Essos), the followers of [Boash](wiki:Boash), the Blind God, ate no flesh and drank no [wine](wiki:Wine). They went about barefoot, clad only in hair shirts and hides. Their priests were [eunuchs](wiki:Eunuch), who wore eyeless hoods in honor of their god. 1322 years before the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria), a sect of this [religion](wiki:Religion) left the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold) to establish a temple and colony in [Lorath](wiki:Lorath). Over time, the worship of Boash dwindled, and the priests grew worldly and corrupt, forsaking their hair shirts, hoods, and piety, and growing fat and rich off the taxes they extracted from those they ruled.(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_lorath.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

[Norvos](wiki:Norvos) was another Valyrian colony founded by religious dissidents. The [bearded priests](wiki:Bearded_priests) are a sect so secretive that even the name of their god is revealed only to initiates. He is a stern god, as the priests wear hair shirts and untanned hides, and practice ritual flagellation as part of their worship. Once initiated, they are forbidden to shave or cut their hair.(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

In [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) were an order of [knights](wiki:Knight) sworn to the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), part of the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant). They wore rainbow cloaks, and inlaid [silver](wiki:Silver) [armor](wiki:Armor) over hair shirts.(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The Warrior's Sons were outlawed during the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) by King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen),(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and officially disbanded in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC) during the reign of his successor, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

In [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), a [begging brother](wiki:Begging_brother) known as the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd) arose in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), a barefoot scarecrow of a man in a hair shirt and roughspun breeches, filthy and unwashed and smelly. The Shepherd preached against Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and [dragons](wiki:Dragon), inspiring the [storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit) during the [riots in King's Landing](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)).(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) He led a "[barefoot army](wiki:Barefoot_lambs)" until he was captured and executed by King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), as a man grown, was known to wear a hair shirt under his velvet and satin royal clothing.(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

At [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan) wonders if King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) would like him better if he wore a hair shirt and never smiled, but rejects the idea and tells [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) that the king must tolerate him in [silk](wiki:Silk) and samite.(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Feast for Crows

In [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), [Queen Regent](wiki:Queen_Regent) [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) tells her companion, [Taena Merryweather](wiki:Taena_Merryweather), about the practices of the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) and the hair shirts they wore under their armor. Cersei arranges with the [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow) to overturn [Maegor the Cruel](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen)'s law which forbade holy men from being armed, and the orders of the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) are reborn.(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

At [Darry](wiki:Darry), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) encounters his cousin [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister) praying in the [sept](wiki:Sept), thin and barefoot, looking like a beggar in his roughspun tunic and hair shirt. When Jaime asks Lancel what he did to deserve such atonement, Lancel confesses that he helped Cersei murder her husband, King [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_Baratheon), and also slept with her. Lancel further explains that he is renouncing his titles, land, and [bride](wiki:Amerei_Frey), and joining the High Sparrow and the Warrior's Sons.(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

As Cersei enters the Hall of Lamps of the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) before she is to begin her [walk of atonement](wiki:Walk_of_atonement), she finds a dozen of the new Warrior's Sons waiting to accompany her. Cersei sees their rainbow cloaks, their crystal-crested greathelms, and their silver plated armor, but she knows that underneath, each knight is wearing a hair shirt.(wiki:Hair_shirt.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
