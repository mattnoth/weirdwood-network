## Identity

Faith of the Seven is a organization.religion from the AWOIAF wiki.

## Edges

## Origins

### Andals

The Faith of the Seven arose in [Essos](wiki:Essos) among the [Andals](wiki:Andals) who lived in the hills of [Andalos](wiki:Andalos). It is claimed that the Seven walked there in human form. According to *[The Seven-Pointed Star](wiki:The_Seven-Pointed_Star)*, [the Father](wiki:Father_(the_Seven)) brought down seven stars from heaven and placed them on the brow of [Hugor of the Hill](wiki:Hugor_of_the_Hill), the first king of the Andals, to form his crown. [The Maid](wiki:Maiden_(the_Seven)) brought forth a girl supple as a willow with eyes like deep blue pools that became Hugor's first wife. The Mother made her fertile, allowing the girl to bear Hugor him forty-four mighty sons as foretold by [the Crone](wiki:Crone_(the_Seven)). [The Warrior](wiki:Warrior_(the_Seven)) gave each son strength of arms, and [the Smith](wiki:Smith_(the_Seven)) wrought each a suit of iron plate.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-2) It is said that when she peered through the door of death, the Crone let the first raven into the world.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)

The Faith was brought to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) with the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) thousands of years ago, and the Faith largely supplanted the local worship of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods). Some of the Andal warriors had the seven-pointed star of the Faith carved into their flesh to show their devotion.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5) Although the Andals came late to the [Reach](wiki:Reach),(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_109-0) the [Hightowers](wiki:House_Hightower) were among the first Westerosi lords to welcome the Andals, considering war bad for trade. Lord [Damon Hightower](wiki:Damon_Hightower) was the first to accept the Faith. To honor the Seven, he constructed the first sept in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), and six more elsewhere in his realm. Following his premature death, Septon [Robeson](wiki:Robeson) became regent for Damon's newborn son, [Triston](wiki:Triston_Hightower). When Robeson eventually died, Triston had the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept) raised in his honor.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_110-0) Oldtown eventually became the center of the Faith, and the Starry Sept home to the High Septon.

[Harmund II Hoare](wiki:Harmund_II_Hoare), [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands), accepted the Faith and spoke of the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God) as one of "the Eight Gods". After opposition from septons and priests of the Drowned God, however, Harmund relented and claimed the Drowned God was an aspect of the Stranger.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_111-0)

### Aegon the Conqueror

When he learned that [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) had landed in Westeros at the start of [the Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon's_Conquest)) locked himself in the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept) for seven days and nights, seeking guidance from the gods. On the seventh day, the High Septon saw a vision given to him by the Crone, showing him that if Oldtown resisted Aegon, the Hightower, Citadel, and Starry Sept would burn. The High Septon warned Lord [Manfred Hightower](wiki:Manfred_Hightower_(Aegon's_Conquest)) of what he had seen, after which the [Lord of the Hightower](wiki:Lord_of_the_Hightower) decided to offer no resistance to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). Aegon dated the start of his reign from the day the High Septon anointed him at the Starry Sept.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-2) Since then, it has been traditional for the High Septon to give their blessing to every new king.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_112-0)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-11)

The Targaryens had converted to the Faith on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) before Aegon's day,(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) and Aegon always trod lightly with the Faith.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-1) The High Septon protested when a marriage was proposed between Prince [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), Aegon's younger son, and Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), the newborn daughter(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-113) of Prince [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), Aegon's elder son.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-2) The High Septon suggested his own niece, Lady [Ceryse Hightower](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower), as a bride for the young prince. Maegor and Ceryse were subsequently wed in [25 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) at the Starry Sept, and matters calmed down.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-3)

### Faith Militant uprising

Issues arose during the reign of Aegon I's successor, [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), when the new king's younger brother, Prince [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) secretly took a second wife, [Alys Harroway](wiki:Alys_Harroway), [39 AC](wiki:39_AC). The [polygamous](wiki:Polygamy) marriage angered the Faith, and Aenys felt forced to exile his brother(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-3) when Maegor refused to set Alys aside.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-4) Although Maegor left for [Pentos](wiki:Pentos),(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-4) the High Septon was not satisfied. Aenys appointed Septon [Murmison](wiki:Murmison) as his new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), but even Murmison could not heal the rift between the Iron Throne and the Faith.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-5)

When Aenys wed his children, Princess [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) and Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), to one another in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC), the High Septon denounced him, calling him "King Abomination".(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-5) This was the start of the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising), during which even the pious lords and [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) who had loved Aenys turned against the king.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-6)

The uprising lasted the remaining year of Aenys I's reign, as well as the reign of his brother and successor, Maegor I. Maegor the Cruel placed a bounty on the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-2) and put out a decree that prohibited the Faith from arming itself.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-12)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_99-2) During Maegor’s campaigns against the Faith, thousands died. Although Maegor’s death resulted in the end of the campaigns, conflicts between the Crown and the Faith lasted until about a decade(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-114) into the reign of Maegor's successor, King [Jaehaerys I](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen). Jaehaerys the Conciliator eventually resolved the issues between the crown and the Faith by promising that the Crown would always protect and defend the Faith. In return, the last few Stars and Swords were to put down their weapons, and the Faith was to agree to accept justice from the Iron Throne from that moment onwards.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-1)

### Baelor the Blessed

The septon-king [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) assumed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [161 AC](wiki:161_AC). The most pious of all Targaryen kings, Baelor the Blessed's interests were the Seven. He convinced the High Septon to dissolve his marriage to his sister, Princess [Daena Targaryen](wiki:Daena_Targaryen), arguing that the marriage had been contracted before he had become king, and in addition had never been consummated. Next, Baelor placed Daena and his other sisters, [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_III)) and [Elaena](wiki:Elaena_Targaryen), in what would become known as the [Maidenvault](wiki:Maidenvault), where only maidens were allowed to join them. The king stated that he wished to preserve their innocence, but some wondered whether Baelor had done so to because he feared the temptation of their beauty.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_115-0)

Baelor's edicts, increasingly concerned with spiritual matters, become more zealous and erratic as his reign continued. He took a septon's vows, preventing himself from marrying again. Meanwhile, the High Septon grew more and more influential. When he died, Baelor declared to the [Most Devout](wiki:Most_Devout) that the Seven had revealed to him the identity of the new High Septon. The Most Devout elected Baelor's choice, [a stonemason](wiki:High_Septon_(stonemason)) who carved his stonework so beautifully that Baelor was convinced he was the [Smith](wiki:Smith_(the_Seven)) in human form. However, the illiterate man was unable to recall even the simplest of prayers. He died of [illness](wiki:Illness) after a year, at which point Baelor declared that [an eight-year-old boy](wiki:High_Septon_(child)), whom he claimed to have seen speaking to doves who answered him in the voices of the Seven, should become the new High Septon. The Most Devout once again did as Baelor desired.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_115-1)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-13)

Baelor also ordered the construction of a new sept atop [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill). Completed many years after Baelor's death,(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-116) the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) eventually became the new seat of the High Septon and the [Most Devout](wiki:Most_Devout), as well as a preeminent center of religious education in the realm.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Various_30-1)

### Recent History

[King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) was one of the areas most affected by the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness). The High Septon, a third of the Most Devout, and nearly all the [silent sisters](wiki:Silent_sisters) died from the sickness.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-6)

Following the death of King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), a [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) was called to settle the issue of succession. The crown was quietly offered to Maekar’s third son, [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), who had taken his [maester](wiki:Maester)'s vows years before. The High Septon offered to absolve Aemon from his vows, but Aemon refused(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_117-0) and the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) passed to his younger brother, King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen).(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_118-0)

Although King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) left a treasury overflowing with gold upon his death in [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) emptied the treasury, leaving the Crown in debt. The Faith is among those of whom the Crown borrows gold.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-1)

## Organization

### Training

According to a semi-canon source, the Faith has great centers of religious training, such as the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor). Not all septons are well educated, however.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Various_30-0)

### Priests

The male and female godsworn of the Faith are called [septons](wiki:Septon) and [septas](wiki:Septa), respectively. Upon taking their vows, they set aside their last names, even if they come from noble families.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Septons often wear white robes, seven-stranded belts of different colors, and a crystal about their necks.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) They lead worship with incense, censers, and songs.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) Septas typically dress in white robes(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) with woven seven-color belts.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) They sometimes wear hoods,(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) but not always,(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1) and their hair can be seen.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) Septas may serve as governesses in noble households. Septa [Mordane](wiki:Mordane), for example, serves [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark).(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

In villages which are too small to support a septon, a septon from a neighboring village might visit twice a year. Other times, a "wandering septon", a septon who travels from village to village without a specific sept at which he serves, might visit these small villages. These septons perform holy services, marriages, and forgive sins. While the septon is visiting the village, the people must provide him with food and a place to sleep.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

### High Septon

The head of the Faith is the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon), the Father of the Faithful, the voice of the new gods on earth.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) The [Most Devout](wiki:Most_Devout), a council of the highest-ranking septons, elect the High Septon, usually from among their own ranks,(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) although there have been notable exceptions. The septon who is elected gives up his name, as the Faith believes that the High Septon no longer has any need of a man’s name, since he has become the avatar of the gods.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) Typically, the High Septon wears long white robes and a crown.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) Septons from the Most Devout wear robes of cloth-of-silver and crystal coronets.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-2)

### Silent sisters

The [silent sisters](wiki:Silent_sisters) are tasked with preparing the deceased for the grave.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) They dress in grey robes, their faces hooded and shawled so only their eyes remain visible,(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-3) as it is "ill fortune to look on the face of death."(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-2) The silent sisters do not speak to the living,(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) and although some claim that the silent sisters have their tongues cut out,(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rasos65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) in truth the silent sisters have simply taken a vow of silence.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

The silent sisters remove the bowels and organs, and drain the blood from the corpses in their care. They may also stuff the body with fragrant herbs and salts to preserve it and hide the smell of decomposition.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) When the deceased is transported back home, one or more silent sisters might accompany the body.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-1)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-3) Due to their task, the silent sisters are also called “death's handmaidens”(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-4) and “handmaidens of the Stranger”.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) Some even say that they are wives to the Stranger.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)

### Holy brothers and sisters

Humbler members of the Faith include "holy brothers"(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) and "holy sisters".(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) Many of the holy brothers wear tonsures, cutting the hair on their scalps as an act of humility and to show the Father that they have nothing to hide.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2) Holy brothers often wear robes of brown, dun, or green.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) Holy sisters can wear robes of white, blue, or grey.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

Brothers can serve at [septries](wiki:Septry),(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-1) monastic communities of the Faith similar to monasteries. Some holy brothers wear the iron hammer of the Smith about their neck.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-5)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-3) The brothers live in penitence, quiet contemplation, and prayer at these septries, and they often take vows of silence. The leader of the community, the [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother), is assisted by [proctors](wiki:Proctor).(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-2) [Motherhouses](wiki:Motherhouse) are corresponding communities for women.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

[Begging brothers](wiki:Begging_brother) travel from place to place, but are not to be confused with wandering septons, as the latter are one rank higher in the Faith's hierarchy.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) The begging brothers are often dressed in threadbare or roughspun robes of undyed wool belted with hempen rope,(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) and some go about barefoot.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2) Some wear a bowl on a leather thong around their necks.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-6)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6) Wandering the realm as a begging brother might be done as a penance.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)

### Faith Militant

The [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) was the military arm of the Faith of the Seven, under the command of the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon). It was composed of two military orders, the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons), an order of knights who gave up their lands and gold, swearing their sword to the High Septon, and the [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows), made up from [common men](wiki:Smallfolk), hedge knights, and the like,(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rawoiafpoor_fellows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) who guarded and escorted travelers.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) The two orders were also known as the "Swords" and the "Stars" for their respective symbols. The Faith Militant is therefore also known as the *Swords and Stars*(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0) or *Stars and Swords*.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) The Faith Militant was outlawed during the reign of King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) and disbanded by the High Septon during the reign of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), is informed during his first [small council](wiki:Small_council) meeting that the Crown has had to borrow money from multiple factions, including the Faith.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-2) Arrested following the death of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon),(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_119-0) Eddard is eventually brought to the steps of the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) to falsely confess his crimes and [take the black](wiki:Take_the_black). King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) unexpectedly orders Eddard's execution, which is immediately performed upon the steps, to the distress of the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(fat_one)).(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1)

### A Clash of Kings

After he has crowned himself king, [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) creates the [Rainbow Guard](wiki:Rainbow_Guard), his form of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard),(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_120-0) to honor the Faith.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-121)

The High Septon believes that the Crown profaned the Great Sept by executing Lord Stark there, and he is convinced that the Lannisters lied about their intent.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_105-1) As the dissatisfaction grows among the population of King's Landing, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) observes a [begging brother](wiki:Begging_brother) preaching that the High Septon has forgotten the gods.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_122-0)

When [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) leaves for [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), the High Septon blesses her before *[Seaswift](wiki:Seaswift)* leaves. As the procession, including the High Septon, travels back to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), a [riot breaks out](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing) in which the High Septon is killed.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-1) [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), the acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), appoints the [new High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Tyrion's)).(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_123-0) Tyrion tells him to tell the people that [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) has vowed to burn the Great Sept.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_123-1)

Following the victory over Stannis at the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), the new High Septon proclaims to King Joffrey that the Faith permits him to break his betrothal to [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), arguing that her father Eddard's treason has made the contract invalid.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_124-0)

### A Storm of Swords

During a meeting of the [small council](wiki:Small_council), it is revealed that the previous High Septon's crown had been looted by rioters. Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), tells the [new High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Tyrion's)) that he must wear a new crown for King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon)'s wedding, and he orders his daughter, Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), to place an order with her goldsmiths.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_125-0)

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) marries [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) in Baelor's Sept.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_88-11) King Joffrey later marries [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) in the Great Sept as well, with the High Septon officiating the wedding.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-11) Joffrey chokes to death during [his wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding).(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-12) The body of Joffrey is placed on a bier beneath the statue of the [Stranger](wiki:Stranger_(the_Seven)) in the Great Sept.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) The High Septon asks [the Father](wiki:Father_(the_Seven)) to lead them to justice when Tyrion's trial for Joffrey's murder begins.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3)

### A Feast for Crows

The death and destruction left behind by the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), as well as the increasing violence towards the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), cause more and more people to turn towards the Faith. Thousands travel to King's Landing, calling themselves "[sparrows](wiki:Sparrows)" after the most common bird, as they are the most common men and women.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-6)

The body of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) is placed on a bier in the Great Sept for seven days.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-11) Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) has the High Septon murdered by Ser [Osney Kettleblack](wiki:Osney_Kettleblack), as she fears the man was a catspaw of Tyrion.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-2)(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-3) During the elections of a new High Septon, the sparrows disgrace Septon [Ollidor](wiki:Ollidor) and prevent the selection of Septon [Luceon](wiki:Luceon_Frey), leading to the rise of a new, pious High Septon, the so-called [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow).(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-14) The High Septon empties the vaults of the Great Sept of Baelor in order to provide for the poor.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-15) The Faith is angered as the Crown refuses to pay off its loan, leading the High Sparrow to refuse to anoint King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-16)

In an attempt to alleviate the crown's debts, gain the Faith's blessing, and gain more protection from her purported enemies, Cersei allows the High Sparrow to revive the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant), ignorant of its history of [causing trouble](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) for monarchs.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-17) Over a hundred knights pledge their swords and lives to the Warrior's Sons,(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-3) including Ser [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister), the newly-wed Lord of [Darry](wiki:Darry), who renounces his titles, land, and new bride, [Amerei Frey](wiki:Amerei_Frey), in order to join the Warrior's Sons in King’s Landing.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_99-3)

Cersei plots to frame Tommen’s queen, [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), for adultery and treason. She seduces Osney Kettleblack and has him falsely confess to the High Sparrow that he had intercourse with Margaery and two of her three cousins, [Megga](wiki:Megga_Tyrell) and [Elinor](wiki:Elinor_Tyrell).(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-1) The High Sparrow acts on the information and has Margaery arrested when she visits the Great Sept. Cersei feigns concern publicly and visits the Great Sept in order to appear to the population that she wishes Margaery released. As part of her plot, she convinces the High Sparrow to have the Faith conduct Margaery's trial, to which he quickly acquiesces. However, the High Sparrow, suspicious of Osney's enthusiastic confession, has had Osney tortured until he spoke the truth. Acting on Osney’s new confession, the High Sparrow has Cersei arrested during her visit for several crimes, including the murder of the previous High Septon, and announces she too will be judged by the godsworn.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-4)

### A Dance with Dragons

The Faith continues to keep Cersei a prisoner in a tower of the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), and has her placed under the care of three septas, [Moelle](wiki:Moelle), [Scolera](wiki:Scolera), and [Unella](wiki:Unella). Cersei confesses to the [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow) that she had relations with her cousin, Ser [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister), and all three of the [Kettleblack](wiki:House_Kettleblack) brothers, knowing that such sins would not earn her an execution. However, she continues to deny involvement in the death of the [previous High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Tyrion's)) and the death of her late husband, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1)

Cersei's uncle, Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), arrives in King's Landing as Lord Regent and comes to an agreement with the High Septon. Intent on drawing “her fangs”,(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_126-0) Kevan does not protest when the Faith demands Cersei to submit to a [walk of atonement](wiki:Walk_of_atonement) from the Great Sept to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), escorted by [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons), [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows), and septas.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_127-0)

When [Varys](wiki:Varys) later murders Kevan, he states that one of the reasons why is because Kevan was trying to bind to Faith to Tommen's rule.(wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_126-1)
