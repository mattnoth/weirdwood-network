## Origins

Followers of R'hllor believe that he made the sun and the stars.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

A [prophecy](wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies) found in ancient books of [Asshai](wiki:Asshai) claims that after a long summer an evil cold darkness shall fall on the world. [Azor Ahai](wiki:Azor_Ahai), wielding the sword [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer), shall be reborn to combat this darkness.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

In an attempt to gain more followers in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), the red priest [Thoros](wiki:Thoros) of [Myr](wiki:Myr) was sent to the royal court at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to convert the fire-obsessed King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen). Thoros was unsuccessful, however, and started to question his own faith.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-5) Thoros remained in King's Landing, and as such was present during the immediate aftermath of the [Sack of King’s Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King’s_Landing).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1) He remained at court during the reign of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), becoming the king's frequent drinking companion.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-6)

The red priestess [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) travels to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) on her own initiative(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Rssm6539.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) in search of the prophesied prince [Azor Ahai](wiki:Azor_Ahai).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Rawoiafmelisandre.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) She is convinced that Dragonstone is the place of smoke and salt amidst which Azor Ahai shall be reborn.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) She comes to believe that [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), the [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), is Azor Ahai reborn,(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Rawoiafmelisandre.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1) and eventually becomes a confidant to Stannis's wife, [Selyse Florent](wiki:Selyse_Florent).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

## Organization

### Priests

[Red priests](wiki:Red_priests) are taught prayers and spells, and are trained to see visions in fires. It takes many years of training to see the shapes beyond the flames, and even more years to learn how to interpret them.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Numerous priests, both male and female, are trained in their [red temples](wiki:Red_temple). The priests of the Lord of Light wear loose red robes.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

### Temple of the Lord of Light

The [Temple of the Lord of Light](wiki:Temple_of_the_Lord_of_Light) in [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) has been called the greatest temple in the [known world](wiki:Known_world).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3) All those who serve there are [slaves](wiki:Slave_of_R'hllor) who were bought as children and trained.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Whether this is also the case at temples in other cities is unknown. [Thoros](wiki:Thoros), a priest from [Myr](wiki:Myr), states he was given over to the red temple as a young child.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)

[Benerro](wiki:Benerro), the [High Priest](wiki:High_Priest_(R'hllor)) in Volantis, also holds the titles [Flame of Truth](wiki:Flame_of_Truth), [Light of Wisdom](wiki:Light_of_Wisdom), [First Servant of the Lord of Light](wiki:First_Servant_of_the_Lord_of_Light), and [Slave of R'hllor](wiki:Slave_of_R'hllor).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) As is the custom of that city, the red priests of the Volantene temple, all slaves, have flames [tattooed](wiki:Tattoo) across their cheeks, chin and forehead.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) Red priests from other places, such as Thoros or [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), are not known to have such tattoos.

The [Fiery Hand](wiki:Fiery_Hand) are a thousand slave soldiers who guard the Temple of the Lord of Light.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) The Volantene temple also trains some of their slaves to become temple [prostitutes](wiki:Prostitution).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-4)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-2)

Many worshippers in Volantis pin scraps of red cloth to their sleeves or tie them around their brow.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

[Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis) tells [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) that the [red priests](wiki:Red_priest) have promised that the Lord of Light would hold [Pentos](wiki:Pentos)'s walls against a million [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1)

### A Clash of Kings

At [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) survives a [poisoning](wiki:Poison) attempt by Maester [Cressen](wiki:Cressen), claiming that it was the power of R'hllor, and that "fire cleanses".(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) The [red priestess](wiki:Red_priest) convinces [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) to claim the mantle of [Azor Ahai](wiki:Azor_Ahai) and wield [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer). Followers of R'hllor at Stannis's court are known as [queen's men](wiki:Queen's_men).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6) The painted statues of [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) from the [sept at Dragonstone](wiki:Sept_at_Dragonstone) are burned outside the castle.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7)

Melisandre, who is also a [shadowbinder](wiki:Shadowbinder), births [shadow assassins](wiki:Shadow_assassin) to kill Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) and Ser [Cortnay Penrose](wiki:Cortnay_Penrose)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) at the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), telling Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) that shadows are servants of light.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) The priestess has Stannis burn the [godswood](wiki:Godswood) at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) as another sacrifice to R'hllor.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Davos recalls that Melisandre had sacrificed Lord [Alester Florent](wiki:Alester_Florent), who had been accused of treason, to R'hllor, in order to have strong winds for Stannis's voyage north to [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) [Free folk](wiki:Free_folk) who have surrendered to Stannis are required by Melisandre to burn [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) branches.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) Jon Snow, now [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), observes at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) that Stannis's sword, which is claimed to be Lightbringer, is cold instead of warm.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-1) Melisandre believes that her power is stronger at the [Wall](wiki:Wall) than it had been in [Asshai](wiki:Asshai).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-4)

Stannis's army is slowed by a blizzard during their [march on Winterfell](wiki:March_on_Winterfell). Some of the queen's men desire to sacrifice [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy), a follower of the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God), so that R'hllor will end the storm.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0)(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) Four [Peasebury](wiki:House_Peasebury) men are burned after being accused of [cannibalism](wiki:Cannibalism).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-1)

In [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) witnesses [Benerro](wiki:Benerro) preaching at the [Temple of the Lord of Light](wiki:Temple_of_the_Lord_of_Light) that [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) is Azor Ahai reborn and encouraging many to support her.(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-4) The [High Priest](wiki:High_Priest_(R'hllor)) sends [Moqorro](wiki:Moqorro) to convert the [Queen of Meereen](wiki:Queen_of_Meereen).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-2)

After Moqorro heals the wounded hand of [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), the [Lord Captain of the Iron Fleet](wiki:Lord_Captain_of_the_Iron_Fleet) decides to honor R'hllor in addition to his Drowned God. Victarion sacrifices slave girls by burning them alive on a fishing ketch set adrift to burn and then sink in [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1)

According to the [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow), the new [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon), Stannis has turned from the truth of the Seven to worship a red [demon](wiki:Demon), and his false faith has no place in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:R'hllor.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0)
