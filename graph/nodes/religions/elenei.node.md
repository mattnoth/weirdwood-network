## Origins

According to legend, in the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), the first [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King), [Durran Godsgrief](wiki:Durran_Godsgrief), won the love of fair Elenei. Her divine parents forbade their love, but Durran and Elenei wed despite them. When she gave her [maidenhead](wiki:Maidenhead) to him on their wedding night, committing herself to a mortal life, the gods' wrath was terrible to behold. They destroyed Durran's keep, killing all his family and the wedding guests, but Elenei protected Durran from the storm. Enraged, Durran declared war on the gods, who replied by hammering his kingdom with massive storms. Each time Durran built a castle to face the sea, the gods destroyed it.(wiki:Elenei.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Elenei.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_durrandon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
King Durran's lords pleaded with him to build inland, his priests told him he must placate the gods by giving Elenei back to the sea, and even his [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) begged him to relent. But Durran refused, and persisted building larger and more powerful fortifications. Finally, the seventh castle stayed in place and resisted the storms of [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay). Some believe this is because the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) took a hand in its construction, shaping the stones with [magic](wiki:Magic). Others believe that a young boy who grew up to be [Bran the Builder](wiki:Bran_the_Builder) advised Durran on its construction. The truth of the matter is unknown. Nevertheless, [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) resisted everything the gods threw at it, and Durran reigned with Elenei at his side for a thousand years.(wiki:Elenei.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Elenei.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_durrandon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### The Winds of Winter[ ]

[Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) recalls her father, Prince [Doran](wiki:Doran_Martell), and Maester [Caleotte](wiki:Caleotte) arguing with a [septon](wiki:Septon) about why the north and south sides of the [Sea of Dorne](wiki:Sea_of_Dorne) were so different. Prince Doran Martell and his [maester](wiki:Maester) favored weather patterns, the way the big storms that formed in the [Summer Sea](wiki:Summer_Sea) would pick up moisture until they crashed into [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath), though Doran thought it was odd that the storms never struck at [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). The septon believed the reason was because Durran Godsgrief stole Elenei from her godly parents and earned their eternal enmity, while no Dornishman ever did so.(wiki:Elenei.cite_ref-3)
