## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

While smuggling [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) past the wards of [Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) is scolded by the [red priestess](wiki:Red_priest) for mentioning a god of darkness.(wiki:Great_Other.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Melisandre tells [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), whom she believes to be [Azor Ahai](wiki:Azor_Ahai) reborn,(wiki:Great_Other.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) that he is the [prince that was promised](wiki:Prince_that_was_promised) and is destined to fight the Other.(wiki:Great_Other.cite_ref-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
At [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), Melisandre refers to the [Others](wiki:Others) as the "cold children" of the Great Other.(wiki:Great_Other.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Melisandre resists sleeping at the [Wall](wiki:Wall), believing that dreams are whisperings of the Other who would drag humanity into his eternal night.(wiki:Great_Other.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) When gazing into her fires, [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) sees [a white wooden face](wiki:Three-eyed_crow), [a thousand red eyes](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), and [a howling boy with a wolf's face](wiki:Bran_Stark) beside him. She thinks they must be the Other's champions, as King Stannis is hers.(wiki:Great_Other.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)
