## Identity

Aerys II Targaryen (244–283 AC) — child of Jaehaerys II Targaryen and Shaera Targaryen; married to Rhaella Targaryen; Prince of Dragonstone. One of 3 characters named Aerys Targaryen.

## Origins

### Early life

Aerys was born to Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen), the second son of King [Aegon V](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), and his sister-wife, Princess [Shaera](wiki:Shaera_Targaryen). While still a teenager, Aerys was married to his sister [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen), in accordance with the family tradition of dynastic [incest](wiki:Incest). While Aerys's grandfather, King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) was against this incestuous marriage (as he had been against incestuous marriages for his own children), Jaehaerys was allowed to arrange it. Jaehaerys's motivation for the marriage was a prophecy made by a [woods witch](wiki:Ghost_of_High_Heart), which had been interpreted to mean that the [prince that was promised](wiki:Prince_that_was_promised) would be born of the line of Aerys and Rhaella.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) According to Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), who was present at the [wedding](wiki:Customs), there was no fondness between the two siblings.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

During his youth, Aerys befriended the young heir to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), who served as a page at the royal court. Aerys also befriended the heir to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), his cousin [Steffon Baratheon](wiki:Steffon_Baratheon). The three became inseparable.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-12)

In [259 AC](wiki:259_AC), Rhaella gave birth to their firstborn son and heir, Prince [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), during the [tragedy of Summerhall](wiki:Tragedy_of_Summerhall),(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-13) which killed King Aegon V, his eldest son Prince [Duncan](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen), and many others.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafrhaegar_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Aerys's father, Jaehaerys, ascended the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) after this tragedy. It would later be rumored that, on the night of Jaehaerys's coronation, Aerys took the maidenhead of Lady [Joanna Lannister](wiki:Joanna_Lannister), who had come to court for the celebrations.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-14)

Aerys was a squire during the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings). He fought on the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), and received his [knighthood](wiki:Knight) there at the age of sixteen. Aerys chose to be dubbed a knight by his friend, Tywin. When King Jaehaerys II passed away from [illness](wiki:Illness) in [262 AC](wiki:262_AC), Aerys was crowned as King Aerys II Targaryen.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-15)

### Early reign

Aerys's reign began in [262 AC](wiki:262_AC) with great promise. The new king removed many of the prominent officials at court, most of them old men brought in during the reign of his father and grandfather. Aerys replaced many of them with younger men, including his friend Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), impressed by the ruthlessness the man had shown in dealing with the [Reyne-Tarbeck revolt](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt) against [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister). Aerys named Tywin his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-16)

Aerys declared upon his coronation that it was his wish to be the greatest king in the history of the Seven Kingdoms. Some of his friends encouraged this by suggesting that one day he would be known as Aerys the Wise or Aerys the Great. During the early years of his reign, Aerys took an active part in his rule. He was ambitious during this time, and boasted about many grand plans, although he lost interest in them quickly as well. His plans, none of which would ever be carried out, included invading the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) and adding them to his kingdom, building a new [Wall](wiki:Wall) hundred of miles north of the current one to extend his kingdom to the north after a visit from Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark) in [264 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), building a city of white [marble](wiki:Marble) on the south bank of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) after complaining of the smell of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in [265 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), building a war fleet to "bring the [Titan](wiki:Titan_of_Braavos) to its knees" after a dispute with the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos) in [267 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and building an underwater canal from the [rainwood](wiki:Rainwood) to make the deserts of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) bloom after visiting the southern region in [270 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-17)

Regardless, the Seven Kingdoms prospered during these years, thanks to Aerys's Hand, Tywin Lannister. Shrewd enough not to make an enemy of the Iron Bank, Tywin settled the dispute with [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) by paying back the loans owed to them by the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) with the gold of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock). He appeased the high lords by repealing all the laws, rights and protections that were granted to the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) by King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen). He also won the support of the wealthy merchants by reducing tariffs on shipping to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), and King's Landing, which in turn increased trade. He sternly punished criminals, improved the infrastructure, and organized [tourneys](wiki:Tourney). Although Tywin remained little loved, he proved to be a brilliant administrator, and as Hand his reputation for brutal effectiveness became so well-known and so widely respected that popular rumor held it was Tywin, not Aerys, who truly ruled the realm, eventually causing Aerys to regret his choice of Hand.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-18)

The relationship between Aerys and Tywin was further complicated due to Tywin's wife, Lady [Joanna Lannister](wiki:Joanna_Lannister). It was rumored that she briefly "reigned as his [paramour](wiki:Paramour)" when Aerys first came to the throne. Whether or not this was true, the following year she married Tywin, with Aerys being present at the wedding. At the feast, a drunken Aerys joked that it was a great pity that the lord's right to the [first night](wiki:First_night) had been abolished and took certain liberties during the [bedding](wiki:Bedding) ceremony. It is said that Tywin did not forget Aerys's behavior during this night.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-19)

Aerys's marriage to his younger sister, [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen), was less happy than Tywin's. Aerys was known to keep many mistresses, and whilst Rhaella turned a blind eye to her husband's infidelities, she objected heavily against "turning my ladies into whores". In [263 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Joanna Lannister was dismissed by Rhaella, shortly after having married Tywin. Whilst no official reason was given for Joanna's abrupt dismissal, she was not the first of Rhaella's ladies to be dismissed in this fashion, nor was she the last.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-20) Aerys's many relationships were not the only strain on his marriage. Rhaella's difficulties with childbirth played their part as well. In [263 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and [264 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Rhaella had miscarriages. In [267 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), whilst Aerys was away in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), Rhaella gave birth to the stillborn Princess [Shaena](wiki:Shaena_Targaryen). Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerys_II)), born in [279 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), survived for only half a year. After Daeron, a second stillbirth followed in [270 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and a miscarriage in [271 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). In [272 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerys_II)) was born two months premature. He died the following year.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-21) While Aerys was compassionate at first, comforting Rhaella in her grief, he turned suspicious in time. By [270 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), he had decided that Rhaella had been unfaithful to him, and that none of the stillbirths, miscarriages and dead princes had been his, as the gods would not want a bastard to sit the Iron Throne. He soon confined Rhaella to [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast), and ordered that two [septas](wiki:Septa) would sleep in her bed from that moment forth, to ensure she would remain faithful.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-22)

Meanwhile, Aerys's relationship with his Hand was breaking down as well, the pair's friendship fraying. Where previously Aerys had heeded Tywin's counsel, he now often disagreed with his Hand and did the opposite of what Tywin suggested. When [Myr](wiki:Myr) and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) became involved in a trade war against [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), Lord Tywin suggested that [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) remained neutral. King Aerys, in response, provided [gold](wiki:Currency) and arms to the Volantenes. When [House Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken) and [House Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood) had a border dispute, Lord Tywin wanted to act in the favor of House Blackwood, leading to Aerys siding with House Bracken. Over Tywin's objections, Aerys doubled the port fees for Oldtown and King's Landing and tripled them at Lannisport and other ports in Westeros. Trade suffered and a delegation of merchants arrived before the Iron Throne to protest. Aerys lied to them and blamed the matter on his Hand of the King.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-23) He restored the port fees to the previous levels, which won him much popularity and acclaim for himself, whilst the king's lies and actions left Tywin with nothing but scorn. Aerys objected to many of Tywin's appointments and filled the offices with men of his own choosing. When Tywin suggested his own brother, Ser [Tygett Lannister](wiki:Tygett_Lannister), as master-at-arms for the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), Aerys appointed Ser [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry) instead.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-24)

By now, Aerys had become aware of the tales being told in his kingdoms; that it was Lord Tywin who truly ruled the Seven Kingdoms and that Aerys was but a hollow figurehead. These statements greatly angered the king. When Lord Tywin's captain of the guard, Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne), had been overheard to repeat this statement, Aerys had the man's tongue torn out with hot pincers.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) The angered king became determined to disprove the tales, and to humble Tywin, his "overmighty servant", putting him back into his place.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-25)

In [272 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), to celebrate Aerys's tenth year on the Iron Throne, Tywin hosted the [Anniversary Tourney](wiki:Anniversary_Tourney) in the capital. Lady Joanna Lannister returned to the court for this event, bringing with her six-year-old twins, [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) and [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister). Drunkenly, Aerys asked Joanna if nursing her children had ruined her breasts. Whilst the question was a great amusement for Lord Tywin's rivals, it humiliated Joanna. Lord Tywin attempted to resign the next morning, but Aerys refused to accept. And thus, Tywin Lannister remained Hand of the King.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-26)

Courtiers at the court who wished to win the king's favor and gain advancement soon learned the best way to gain the king's eye was to mock and make japes about Lord Tywin. King Aerys continued to undermine and slight his Hand, which amused many lords who were rivals of Lord Tywin or who simply disliked the humorless man. Tywin Lannister suffered all these slights in silence, never showing any public display of weakness. When Joanna Lannister died in childbirth in [273 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the king had been heard to remark that Joanna had died, and Tywin had begotten [his dwarf son](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) instead from the gods, "to teach him some humility at last". These reports would reach [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), where Lord Tywin was in mourning. From that moment on, all the old affection between the old childhood friends was gone. Yet still, Lord Tywin continued to serve the realm as Hand of the King, whilst King Aerys grew increasingly violent, suspicious and erratic.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-27)

The king's [madness](wiki:Targaryen_madness) abated slightly with the birth of another son, whom he named [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerys_II)), in [274 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Jaehaerys's birth almost restored the king to his old self, but this changed when Jaehaerys died later that same year. In a black rage, Aerys had the boy's [wet nurse](wiki:Wet_nurse) beheaded, convinced it had been her fault. He soon had a change of heart, and declared that his mistress was to blame, having poisoned the young babe. Aerys had her and her entire family tortured and eventually executed. Under torture, all confessed to murdering Prince Jaehaerys, though their accounts on Jaehaerys's death greatly differed from one another.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-28)

After this deed, however, Aerys changed his mind yet again. He fasted for a fortnight, and made a walk of repentance across the city, to the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor). There, he prayed with the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon). When he returned, he announced that he would remain true to his wife and marital vows from that moment forth. From that day on in [275 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King Aerys II Targaryen lost interest in all other women.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-29)

In [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Rhaella gave birth to a son named [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen). While the child was healthy enough, Aerys was paranoid about the boy's safety. [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) knights were ordered to stand over the babe, day and night. No one, not even Queen Rhaella, was allowed to be alone with Viserys. Gifts that came from all over the realm for the new prince were piled up in the yard and burned on Aerys's orders, as the king feared they might be cursed or ensorcelled. When Rhaella's milk dried up, Aerys made his own food taster suckle on the nipples of the wet nurse, to ensure there was no poison on them.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-30)

Later that year, Lord Tywin Lannister staged a [tournament in honor of Viserys's birth](wiki:Tournament_in_honor_of_Viserys's_birth) at Lannisport. It has been suggested by history that this tourney was arranged as a gesture for reconciliation.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-31) At this tourney, Lord Tywin proposed a marriage between his daughter [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) and Prince Rhaegar to King Aerys. However, Aerys's fear of Tywin's power and ambition led him to reject the offer rudely, saying that Tywin was a mere servant of the crown, and no servant's daughter was fit to marry a prince of royal blood. The tourney ended without a final feast and Aerys departed in a chilly atmosphere. Tywin would not forget the insult.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

### The Defiance of Duskendale

Lord [Denys Darklyn](wiki:Denys_Darklyn) of [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) desired to win a charter for Duskendale, which would give him more autonomy from the crown, and so he made his proposal to the crown in [277 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) refused the proposal, but Denys would not give up so easily. Having noticed the deteriorated relationship between Aerys and Tywin, Denys decided to withhold his payments to the crown, and instead invited the king to Duskendale to hear his petition for a new charter. While Aerys had intended to decline the invitation, the king changed his mind when Tywin advised him to refuse in the strongest possible terms. Eager to demonstrate his ability to handle the situation without the help of his Hand, Aerys decided to settle the issue personally and bring the defiant Lord Darklyn to heel.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-32)

Informing [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) and the [small council](wiki:Small_council) of his decision, Aerys traveled to Duskendale, against Lord Tywin's advice, taking a small force led by Ser [Gwayne Gaunt](wiki:Gwayne_Gaunt) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). The invitation proved to be a trap, however, and Aerys walked right into it. Some of his escort (most notably Gwayne) were killed trying to defend their king, and Aerys was taken prisoner.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-33)

Outrage was the response to this news from Duskendale. As [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), it fell to Tywin to resolve the crisis. He massed an army and laid siege to the town in what became known as the [Defiance of Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale). The situation remained in a stalemate for six months, with Lord Denys threatening to execute the king at the first sign that Tywin intended to storm the town. In the end, it was Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) of the Kingsguard who staged a daring infiltration and managed to rescue the king.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

Lord Denys surrendered and begged for mercy, but the king next ordered [House Darklyn](wiki:House_Darklyn) utterly destroyed, with each Darklyn beheaded and Denys's wife [Serala](wiki:Serala) burned alive after being mutilated. Aerys also ordered the deaths of the Darklyns' allies, [House Hollard](wiki:House_Hollard), with the sole exception of the child [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard), who was spared only at Barristan's pleading.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-34)

### Following the Defiance

The Defiance of Duskendale plunged Aerys deeper into his descent into madness. Deeply shaken by his imprisonment, he refused to leave the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) for the next four years. His jealous and suspicious nature deepened into paranoia and eventually outright delusions, seeing evidence of treachery everywhere.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-35)

Aerys's relations with Lord [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) deteriorated even more rapidly. The king felt his Hand had wanted him to perish in the dungeons of Duskendale. Aerys also no longer trusted his wife or his heir. In his paranoia, Aerys believed that Tywin and [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), had conspired to have him killed by storming Duskendale, so the prince would ascend the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and marry Tywin's daughter.

To prevent such conspiracies between Rhaegar and Tywin, Aerys summoned his old childhood friend, Lord [Steffon Baratheon](wiki:Steffon_Baratheon), to court, making him a member of the [small council](wiki:Small_council). Aerys publicly announced Steffon would go to [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), to find a bride for Rhaegar of "proud [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) blood". The fact that Aerys entrusted this task to Steffon instead of Tywin or Rhaegar caused many to mutter that Aerys intended to make Steffon his new Hand of the King, upon the successful completion of this mission, and that Aerys planned to have Lord Tywin arrested and executed for high treason. Many lords took delight in the prospect.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-36)

Lord Steffon's mission turned out to be a failure and on the return voyage his ship was caught in a storm and sank, just off the coast of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), in sight of his two eldest children, [Robert](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon). Aerys got the notion in his head that Tywin had somehow assassinated Lord Baratheon. He even stated to [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) that he could not dismiss Tywin as Hand, as Tywin would have him killed then as well. In the final years of his reign where Tywin was still Hand, Aerys never met with Lord Tywin unless all seven of the Kingsguard knights were present.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-37)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

His paranoia and hysterical madness ever deepening, Aerys heard word of the talents of a certain eunuch named [Varys](wiki:Varys). The eunuch was brought to court, and appointed as Aerys's [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers) on the small council. The king reasoned that only a foreigner with no competing loyalties in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) could be trusted to alert him of potential threats. The Spider, as Varys became known to the smallfolk, used the crowns gold to form a vast web of informers. Varys was always seen crouching at the king's side whispering in his ear.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-38)

Aerys also resolved the matter of Rhaegar's marriage. In early [279 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Prince Rhaegar was betrothed to Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell). In early [280 AC](wiki:280_AC), the two were wed in a lavish ceremony at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor). Fearing to be assassinated, Aerys refused to attend. Neither did he allow Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) to attend. Rhaegar and his new bride decided to take up residence on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)), instead of remaining in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), which led some to mutter that Rhaegar planned on deposing his father and seizing the throne. Others claimed that Aerys II meant to disinherit Rhaegar and name his younger son, Prince Viserys, the new heir. The birth of Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)), Aerys first grandchild, did not unite the estranged father and son. When Rhaegar presented the girl at court, Queen Rhaella embraced her grandchild warmly, but Aerys refused to touch her, stating that "she smelled [Dornish](wiki:Dornishmen)".(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-39)

Aerys grew brutal, capricious and increasingly fascinated with fire, especially the highly flammable substance known as [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) which was said to be a cousin to [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame). By [280 AC](wiki:280_AC), Aerys had taken to using wildfire for executing alleged traitors, rather than hangings and beheadings. The [pyromancers](wiki:Alchemists'_Guild) became a fixture at the king's court and [Wisdom](wiki:Wisdom) [Rossart](wiki:Rossart), who carried out the executions, was eventually named to the [small council](wiki:Small_council). All the executions by fire aroused the king and, while he and his wife had slept in separate chambers for some years and avoided each other by day as well, Aerys would always claim his marriage rights after such an execution by fire, brutally abusing his wife in bed.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

### Year of the False Spring

Ser [Arthur Dayne](wiki:Arthur_Dayne) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) defeated the [Kingswood Brotherhood](wiki:Kingswood_Brotherhood) and brought the concerns of [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood) to Aerys.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Arthur knighted [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), the eldest (and [only able-bodied](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister)) son of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), for his valor during the campaign.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)

When a place in the Kingsguard became available, after Ser [Harlan Grandison](wiki:Harlan_Grandison) died in his sleep, Aerys decided to appoint Ser Jaime as the youngest knight ever to join the order. Tywin, and later on Jaime, came to believe that Aerys had intended this not as an honor but a slight to [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister), meant to deprive Tywin of his heir and allow Aerys to hold Jaime hostage for his father's loyalty. Unbeknownst to Tywin, this scheme had originally been hatched by Tywin's own daughter, [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), who had proposed the idea to King Aerys as she wished for her twin Jaime to join the order as to prevent Tywin from betrothing him to [Lysa Tully](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), as Tywin and Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) had been discussing.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)

Aerys had made his announcement during court, and Tywin had thanked him openly for the honor the king bestowed upon his House. In reality, he had been furious, and pleading illness, Tywin resign his position as [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and returned to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), with his daughter. Aerys accepted Tywin's resignation and appointed Lord [Owen Merryweather](wiki:Owen_Merryweather) as his replacement, an amiable but not especially competent old man whose main qualifications as Hand were his willingness to throw lavish feasts and offer the king constant flattery. The young knight Jaime would officially join the Kingsguard in a ceremony at a later date.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-40)

With Lord Tywin now gone from court, the new focus of Aerys's mistrust was his own son and heir, Prince [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), who had become wary of his father's behavior.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) At court, there was growing tension between the factions loyal to the king and those loyal to the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone). Aerys may have been mad and cruel to those he deemed his enemies, but he was lavish with wealth and titles to those who enjoyed his trust.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-41) Those loyal to the king were Lord [Qarlton Chelsted](wiki:Qarlton_Chelsted), the [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin); Lord [Lucerys Velaryon](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon_(master_of_ships)); the [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships), Lord [Symond Staunton](wiki:Symond_Staunton), the [master of laws](wiki:Master_of_laws); [Varys](wiki:Varys), the [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers); and Wisdom [Rossart](wiki:Rossart). All enjoyed the king's trust and at court held great power. Having learned how to twist the king's madness to their own benefit, they had good reason to oppose Aerys's removal from the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-42)

Rhaegar's supporters were young Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), Ser [Myles Mooton](wiki:Myles_Mooton), Ser [Richard Lonmouth](wiki:Richard_Lonmouth), and the [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) who accompanied him at court, including Princess [Elia](wiki:Elia_Martell)'s uncle, Prince [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) and Ser [Arthur Dayne](wiki:Arthur_Dayne), the [Sword of the Morning](wiki:Sword_of_the_Morning) also of the Kingsguard and who was Rhaegar's dearest friend.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

It fell to [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) and Lord Merryweather the unenviable task of trying to keep the peace between the factions, Pycelle even dispatched a letter to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) writing that tensions and division at court strongly resembled those before the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Pycelle was fearful of a civil war between those loyal to the king and those who supported the prince would break out unless some accord could be reached that would satisfy both factions.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3)

Lord [Walter Whent](wiki:Walter_Whent) announced a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) would be held at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) in [280 AC](wiki:280_AC)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-36) to rival any previous tournament. The tourney was to be held in [281 AC](wiki:281_AC). It is believed by some that the tournament was secretly arranged and financed by Prince [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), as a pretext, so Rhaegar could meet up with the great lords of the realm to discuss arranging a [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) and the removal of his father. The tournament was announced by Lord Whent shortly after a visit by his younger brother, Ser [Oswell](wiki:Oswell_Whent) of the Kingsguard.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4)

King Aerys at first was counseled by Lord Chelsted to forbid the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal), while Lord Staunton went as far to suggest the king should outlaw all tournaments. Lord Merryweather intervened by pointing out such events were extremely popular with the highborn and lowborn and that attempting to ban or outlaw tourneys would make Aerys unpopular and strengthen Rhaegar. The king decided to attend the tournament, the first time he left the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) since the [Defiance of Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-5)

Aerys also hoped his presence at the tourney would win back the love of his people. If that was his intention, however, it backfired horrifically. Since the Defiance of Duskendale, the king had not taken care of his appearance. His fear of blades meant he would not permit his hair to be cut, or his nails to be groomed. His hair and beard were unwashed, matted and tangled, and his nails were yellow and had grown nine inches in length. His fear and paranoia of [poison](wiki:Poison) had made him thin and gaunt. The [lords](wiki:Lord) and [knights](wiki:Knight) at the tourney were appalled at what their monarch had become. Nor was Aerys's behavior that of a sane man, going from mirth to melancholy at a snap of the fingers. His bouts of hysterical laughter, long silences, sudden rages and constant weeping made all present weary.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-6) In contrast, Prince Rhaegar looked every inch a leader and warrior. Tall, clean, handsome, well groomed, the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) was unstoppable in the lists, winning bout after bout.

Aerys had decided that the tournament would be the perfect place to initiate Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) into the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), and the young knight said his vows before the king with the realm looking on. When a cheer went up as the young handsome knight joined the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), Aerys believed that the cheers believed to be for him.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-7)

A [mystery knight](wiki:Mystery_knight), the [Knight of the Laughing Tree](wiki:Knight_of_the_Laughing_Tree), competed in the tournament. Certain the knight had concealed his identity because he was an enemy of the crown, Aerys ordered Rhaegar to discover his identity, but the mystery knight could not be found. Prince Rhaegar went on to win the tournament, and shocked all those present by crowning [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), rather than his wife, Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), his [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-8)

The next year, in [282 AC](wiki:282_AC), sometime after the birth of Rhaegar's heir, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), Prince Rhaegar disappeared with Lyanna, apparently having kidnapped her.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-9) Her eldest brother, [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark), rode to King's Landing with several friends when word of his sister's disappearance reached him. Upon arriving at the Red Keep, Brandon roared for Rhaegar to "come out and die", but the prince was not present. The king had Brandon and his companions—[Jeffory Mallister](wiki:Jeffory_Mallister), [Kyle Royce](wiki:Kyle_Royce), [Elbert Arryn](wiki:Elbert_Arryn), and [Ethan Glover](wiki:Ethan_Glover)—arrested on charges of conspiring against the life of the crown prince, demanding that their fathers present themselves at court to answer for their sons' crimes. When they did so, Aerys had them all executed without a fair trial, aside from Ethan. When Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark) demanded a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat), King Aerys chose fire as his champion. Rickard was burned alive in the [throne room](wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep)) by [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) as Brandon was forced to watch, strapped into a torture device that caused him to strangle himself in his attempts to save his father.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

His paranoia still unsatisfied, Aerys ordered Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) to send him the heads of Lyanna's next brother, [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), now the Lord of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), and Lyanna's fiancé, Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), who were present with Jon at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). Lord Arryn refused to kill his former wards, however, and raised his banners in revolt.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

### Robert's Rebellion

Aerys's acts triggered a chain of events that would become known as the War of the Usurper to [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) loyalists and [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) to those who fought on the rebel side. Houses [Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn),(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) [Stark](wiki:House_Stark),(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) and [Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon),(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) and later on in the war also Houses [Tully](wiki:House_Tully)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) and [Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy),(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) united in rebellion against the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-10) Houses [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) and [Martell](wiki:House_Martell) remained loyal to Aerys,(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) however, and [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) stayed neutral for most of the conflict.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3)

Aerys's [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) at the start of the rebellion was still Lord [Owen Merryweather](wiki:Owen_Merryweather), but he was quickly replaced for his failure to act to contain the unrest in its early stages. Aerys next turned to Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) of [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost), a close friend of Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen). Jon promised to deliver Robert's head, but he was exiled by Aerys after losing to rebel forces at [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept) in the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1)

As his next Hand, Aerys chose Lord [Qarlton Chelsted](wiki:Qarlton_Chelsted).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) By this point, the king was becoming fearful of a rebel victory, and he hatched the [wildfire plot](wiki:Wildfire_plot), a plan to gain a final revenge against his enemies in the event that they took the capital. He ordered [pyromancers](wiki:Pyromancer) to create a massive reserve of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) and secret it around [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), plotting to burn down the entire city and kill all its inhabitants rather than allow Robert to have it intact. Upon discovering the plot, Lord Chelsted confronted Aerys and, when he realized the king could not be dissuaded, resigned his position in disgust, flinging his chain of office at the king's feet. Aerys had him burned alive. The [Alchemists' Guild](wiki:Alchemists'_Guild) had enjoyed royal favor ever since the Defiance of Duskendale owing to the king's obsession with wildfire, and Aerys appointed the head of the guild, [Rossart](wiki:Rossart), his final Hand.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1)

After the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), in which Prince Rhaegar was slain by Robert and the royal army defeated, a rebel victory was all but certain.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) Aerys sent Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) (then newly pregnant with Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen)) and Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) for their own safety. However, he kept Rhaegar's widow, Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), and her young children, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) and Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), with him at the Red Keep, believing they were necessary as hostages to ensure the loyalty of House Martell and [Dorne](wiki:Dorne).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_end.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

### Sack of King's Landing

Hours before the vanguard of Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark)'s rebel host would arrive, twelve thousand [westermen](wiki:Westermen) under Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) reached [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and pledged their loyalty to King Aerys.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-4) Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) advised the king not to admit them to the city, but [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) argued that the [Warden of the West](wiki:Warden_of_the_West) had come to help Aerys. The king made the fateful choice to listen to Pycelle, and opened the city gates.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-2) The Lannister forces then began the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing), however.

Realizing this was the end, Aerys summoned Lord Rossart and Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), the last remaining Kingsguard knight in the city. Aerys had retained Jaime throughout the war to ensure Tywin's loyalty, though up until that point [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) had been neutral in the rebellion. Jaime would later remark upon the irony of the Mad King's belief that he could not be harmed if he kept Jaime close. Aerys commanded Jaime to kill his father, then gave Lord [Rossart](wiki:Rossart) the long-awaited order to ignite the wildfire caches and burn the city to the ground.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-3) Jaime later suggested that much like his mad great-uncle [Aerion the Monstrous](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen) before him, Aerys held the delusional belief that he would not die in the inferno, but rather would be transformed by the flames into a [dragon](wiki:Dragon), giving him the power to crush his enemies.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-4)

To prevent the wildfire plot from being carried out, Jaime slew Rossart near a postern gate, and he returned to the [throne room](wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep)) to find Aerys pacing by himself. The king released his bowels and fled for the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) when he realized that Jaime had killed Rossart, but the Kingsguard pulled him off the steps and cut his throat.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-5) Meanwhile, Princess [Elia of Dorne](wiki:Elia_Martell) and Aerys's grandchildren, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) and Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), were killed by Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch) and Ser Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) in [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) Jaime later hunted down the pyromancers [Belis](wiki:Belis) and [Garigus](wiki:Garigus), but he never publicly revealed the wildfire plot(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-6) and his killing of Aerys earned him the epithet "Kingslayer".(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

[Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) claimed the Iron Throne, and the reign of the Targaryen dynasty was ended.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) In return for their fealty, those pardoned by King Robert included Jaime,(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Pycelle,(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) Varys,(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-1) and Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-2) Aerys's wife, Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen), died in childbirth at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), but Ser [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry) fled with Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) and the infant [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) for the [Braavosian Coastland](wiki:Braavosian_Coastland) before the [assault on Dragonstone](wiki:Assault_on_Dragonstone) by Robert's brother, Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-1)

## Appearances & Description

Aerys was a handsome young man,(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) with [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) and gold-silver hair.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Later in his life, as his insanity grew, his appearance deteriorated, most notably after his captivity during the [Defiance of Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale). He afterwards refused to wash, and would not allow anyone to cut his hair, trim his beard, or cut his fingernails due to his fear of being touched. As a result, his hair grew past his shoulders to his waist(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) and became matted, his beard tangled, and his fingernails grew to be yellow talons of almost a foot long.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) As he was afraid of being poisoned, Aerys became very thin and gaunt, and he looked much older than he actually was.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-targaryenkings_15-0) Towards the end of his reign, Aerys would often cut himself on the blades of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). His arms and legs were always covered with scabs and half-healed cuts from the Iron Throne, and he became known as King Scab.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)

Aerys wore the crown previously worn by Kings [Aegon IV](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-targaryenkings_15-1) and [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen),(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) a large and heavy crown of red [gold](wiki:Gold), each of its points a [dragon](wiki:Dragon)'s head with [gemstone](wiki:Gemstone) eyes.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-targaryenkings_15-2)

In his youth, Aerys had an undeniable charm. He was generous, resolute, and ambitious, and during the early part of his reign was an active and lively king. Aerys liked music, dancing, masked balls, and young women. However, at the same time, Aerys was not the most intelligent or the most diligent person, and was somewhat quick to anger. He was vain, proud, and changeable, which made him an easy target for lickspittles and flatterers.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)

As his reign continued, Aerys's mood swings became more frequent and his behavior increasingly erratic. He became more and more jealous, suspicious, and violent as the years went on, prone to furious outbursts. Aerys loved fair maidens and took multiple mistresses. Although most historians doubt the accuracy of it, some believe Aerys had as many mistresses as [Aegon the Unworthy](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen). Regardless, he would often quickly lose interest in these women. A notable change in his infidelity occurred in [274 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), when Aerys renounced all women but his wife following the death of [his newborn son](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerys_II)). While [a healthy son](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) was born the following year, this only increased Aerys's obsessive behavior and fear for his child's life.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9)

After the [Defiance of Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale), these characteristics became more pronounced. Aerys began to see every unexplained event or act of minor defiance as evidence of fearful conspiracies against him, and devised sadistic punishments for those he imagined to be his enemies. He developed a fascination with fire, which eventually grew so consuming that he could only become sexually aroused by watching someone burn to death. His marriage to his sister-wife [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) was never a happy one, and Aerys became sexually abusive late in his reign.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10) According to [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), Aerys would go "from mirth to melancholy in the blink of an eye" by [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), including spells of hysterical laughter, long silences, bouts of weeping, and sudden rages.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

Also following Duskendale, the king became more and more obsessed with [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire), and like his ancestors from [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), Aerys too began to attempt to hatch [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_eggs). When these attempts proved futile, he became obsessed with [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire), and he liked the [Alchemists Guild](wiki:Alchemists_Guild) to store the substance in jars shaped like fruits.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) By [280 AC](wiki:280_AC), Aerys took great pleasure in executing criminals by burning them alive.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-11)

Many consider Aerys to have been mad and cruel,(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) especially by those who fought against him in [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), who was raised by her brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), has an idealized understanding of the father she never knew,(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) and [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) are also more likely to have a positive memory of the late king.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) [House Darry](wiki:House_Darry) stood high in Aerys's favor.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
