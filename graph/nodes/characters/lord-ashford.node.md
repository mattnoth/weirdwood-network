## Origins

In [209 AC](wiki:209_AC) Lord Ashford organized the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow) for the thirteenth [name day](wiki:Name_day) of [his daughter](wiki:Lord_Ashford's_daughter). The [Lord of Ashford](wiki:Lord_of_Ashford) gave his chambers to Prince [Baelor Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), during the tourney.(wiki:Lord_Ashford.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) According to a semi-canon source, Lord Ashford hoped to curry favor with Baelor by hosting the tourney.(wiki:Lord_Ashford.cite_ref-3)
Alongside princes from [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), Lord Ashford watched the competition from a viewing stand. His sons, Ser [Androw](wiki:Androw_Ashford) and Ser [Robert Ashford](wiki:Robert_Ashford), were defeated in the lists but competed nobly against Ser [Tybolt Lannister](wiki:Tybolt_Lannister) and Ser [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon), respectively.(wiki:Lord_Ashford.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Lord Ashford enraged Prince [Aerion Targaryen](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen) by declaring Ser [Humfrey Hardyng](wiki:Humfrey_Hardyng) the victor of their bout and awarding him Aerion's courser.(wiki:Lord_Ashford.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)
This tourney entered history by becoming the theater of the first [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) in more than a hundred years. Mud landed on Lord Ashford and his daughter when Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) shook the defeated Aerion before the viewing stand. Baelor Breakspear was slain during the trial.(wiki:Lord_Ashford.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)
