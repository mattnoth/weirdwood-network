## Origins

At a time when Myles would have been captain-general, [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) feasted the captains of the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) in the hopes they might take up his cause to reclaim the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) from King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). His sister [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), then a little girl, witnessed them eat his [food](wiki:Food) and hear his pleas and laugh at him.(wiki:Myles_Toyne.cite_ref-Radwd16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

Myles secretly plotted with [Magister](wiki:Magister) [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis), Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys), and the exiled lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) to have the Golden Company aid [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) in claiming the Iron Throne.(wiki:Myles_Toyne.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

After Myles's death in [296 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), his [skull was dipped in gold](wiki:Golden_skulls) and placed upon a company standard. He was succeeded as captain-general by the company [paymaster](wiki:Paymaster), Ser [Harry Strickland](wiki:Harry_Strickland).(wiki:Myles_Toyne.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

## Appearances & Description

Myles was not dark and handsome like his infamous forebear, Ser [Terrence Toyne](wiki:Terrence_Toyne). Instead, Myles was jug-eared, with a big nose and a crooked jaw.(wiki:Myles_Toyne.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Myles was known as Blackheart because of the sigil of [House Toyne](wiki:House_Toyne), which was depicted on his shield. He enjoyed the nickname and appreciated having a fearsome reputation, but he was not a cruel man. His good friend [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) considered Myles full of life.(wiki:Myles_Toyne.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

When [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) arrives at the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company)'s encampment near [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys), he contemplates the company's [golden skulls](wiki:Golden_skulls) ringing the captain-general's tent. Myles's skull has a glittering grin.(wiki:Myles_Toyne.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

Later, following the [taking of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost), Jon recalls a conversation with Myles, in which Jon explained he could not have done better at [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept) to prevent the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells), that not even Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) could have done more. Myles responded that Tywin would have burned down the town and killed every living creature in it, bringing an immediate end to [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), as Robert's death would have led to Lords [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) and [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) accepting pardons. This recollection strengthens Jon's desire to not make the same mistake in his war to seat Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Myles_Toyne.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
