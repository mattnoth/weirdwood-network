## Appearances & Description

Pykewood wears his worldly [wealth](wiki:Currency) upon his person and a lord’s ransom in golden arm rings. Each ring signifies one year's service with the Golden Company.(wiki:Pykewood_Peake.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Pykewood and his brothers—[Laswell](wiki:Laswell_Peake) and [Torman](wiki:Torman_Peake)—are related to the [Peakes](wiki:House_Peake) of [Starpike](wiki:Starpike).(wiki:Pykewood_Peake.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

The three Peake brothers are among the captains of the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) presented by Ser [Franklyn Flowers](wiki:Franklyn_Flowers) to [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) and his companions upon their arrival in the company's camp located south of [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys). Pykewood and the others swear their allegiance to [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) before they [invade Westeros](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company).(wiki:Pykewood_Peake.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
