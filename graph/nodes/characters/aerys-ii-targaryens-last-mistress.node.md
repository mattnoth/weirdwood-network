## Origins

After the death of his son, Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerys_II)), King Aerys went into a black rage and blamed Jaehaerys' [wet nurse](wiki:Wet_nurse) for the death of the princeling. Aerys later announced Jaehaerys had been [poisoned](wiki:Poison) by his own mistress. She and all her kin were tortured to death.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen's_last_mistress.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Afterward Aerys, who was known for his infidelities, vowed to stay faithful to his sister-wife, Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen). According to the chronicles, Aerys respected his vow and did not take another mistress.(wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen's_last_mistress.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
