## Origins

He was one of the many Andal warriors that gathered round Ser [Artys Arryn](wiki:Artys_Arryn) and fought under his leadership at the [Battle of Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_Seven_Stars). According to accounts, though bleeding from a score of wounds, he fought through [Lord Redfort](wiki:House_Redfort)'s strongest warriors and look the lord's arm with a single cut. He also slew [Ursula Upcliff](wiki:Ursula_Upcliff), barehandedly tearing her head off.(wiki:Torgold_Tollett.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

He was slain by King [Robar II Royce](wiki:Robar_II_Royce). King Robar slashed at his head with [Lady Forlorn](wiki:Lady_Forlorn). Tollett grabbed for the blade, laughing, but the sword sliced through his hands and buried itself in his skull. According to song he died choking on his last laugh.(wiki:Torgold_Tollett.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Appearances & Description

Torgold was ironically called "the Grim" because he rode into battle laughing and naked above the waist. Tollett was a giant of a man who sported a seven-pointed star carved across his chest and fought with an axe on each hand. Songs about him say he knew no fear and felt no pain.(wiki:Torgold_Tollett.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
