## Origins

### Maegor the Cruel

Lord Qarl joined the host of Prince [Aegon the Uncrowned](wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned) at [Pinkmaiden Castle](wiki:Pinkmaiden_Castle) in [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), rallying to his cause against King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Qarl_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Qarl_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

The host marched towards [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), but was intercepted by Maegor I at the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye). During the subsequent [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye), Prince Aegon's host was surrounded by several armies loyal to the king. Lord Corbray advised the young prince to attack each army separately before they could join their power, though Aegon was loath to divide his own strength. The battle turned into a rout and the prince was slain. The only notable casualty among Maegor's royalists was Ser [Davos Darklyn](wiki:Davos_Darklyn) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), slain by Lord Corbray with [Lady Forlorn](wiki:Lady_Forlorn).(wiki:Qarl_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Half a year of trials and executions followed. Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) persuaded her son to spare some of the rebellious lords, though they were stripped of some lands and titles and were forced to give up hostages.(wiki:Qarl_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Lord Qarl was among the survivors and lived through Maegor's reign.(wiki:Qarl_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### Reign of Jaehaerys I

After the death of King Maegor I, he was named Commander of the [City Watch](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) and was awarded a seat on King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen)'s [small council](wiki:Small_council) as part efforts to reconcile the enemies and supporters of his late uncle. The small council helped Queen Regent [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) and Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), rule in the name of the young king, who was still in his minority. Lord Qarl was one of the representatives of the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) at the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding), alongside the Lords [Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) and [Royce](wiki:House_Royce).(wiki:Qarl_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

When searching for a bride for the king, Queen Regent Alyssa considered a noblewoman from House Corbray, on account of Lord Qarl's support of the late Prince Aegon.(wiki:Qarl_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) After the king wed his own sister, Princess [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), in private, Lord Rogar was deeply opposed and sought to cast down Jaehaerys and crown Princess [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen). Lord Corbray refused to support him and declared that the City Watch would never stand for it. Grand Maester [Benifer](wiki:Benifer) noted that Qarl feared Lord Baratheon's intentions would result in another conflict that would pit the Targaryens against each other, recalling the terrible sight of [dragon](wiki:Dragon) against dragon at the Gods Eye. When Queen Alyssa dismissed Lord Baratheon from his office as Hand, Rogar refused and responded that no woman could dismiss him. In response, Qarl drew Lady Forlorn and laid it against the table facing Rogar, replying with a simple "*Yes*". Only then did Rogar concede. Qarl also hid Princess Aerea under the orders of the Queen Regent, thus keeping Ser [Ronnal Baratheon](wiki:Ronnal_Baratheon) from taking her to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) with the departing household of Lord Rogar.(wiki:Qarl_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

After King Jaehaerys I reached the [age of majority](wiki:Age_of_majority) in [50 AC](wiki:50_AC), Lord Corbray was retained as Commander of the City Watch.(wiki:Qarl_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) After the death of the [High Septon](wiki:High_Lickspittle) in [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), Qarl and Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon) advocated for a show of force, with Lord Corbray offering to lead an army to prevent the elevation of Septon [Mattheus](wiki:Mattheus) to the office of [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon), for Mattheus was a great opponent of the Targaryens.(wiki:Qarl_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) When the dragon [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) returned to the capital with the dying Princess Aerea, Lord Corbray was inspecting the East Barracks of the City Watch. When the king thought to name Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth) as his Hand, Qarl reminded Jaehaerys of the cleric's low birth, though the king shrugged this off.(wiki:Qarl_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

When the deadly [winter](wiki:Winter) plague known as the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) struck the realm in [59 AC](wiki:59_AC), Qarl succumbed to the [disease](wiki:Disease).(wiki:Qarl_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
