## Identity

Vulture King (Jaehaerys I) (d. 61 AC). One of 4 characters named Vulture King. The second Vulture King was a self-proclaimed Dornish king who raided in the Third Dornish War during the reign of King Jaehaerys I Targaryen. (wiki:Vulture_King_(Jaehaerys_I))

## Origins

The second Vulture King was believed to be the minor son of a minor house in [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). While the first [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Aenys_I)) had commanded an army of thousands, the second had a force of a few hundred brigands and [outlaws](wiki:Outlaws) who shared his taste for robbery and rape. His band was later joined by Ser [Borys Baratheon](wiki:Borys_Baratheon), an embittered younger brother of [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End). With Borys's knowledge of the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands), the Vulture King raided with more success, always retreating back to the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) before a force could be mustered to attack him. He knew the mountain terrain well and always disappeared when pursued, and he launched cunning ambushes upon would-be pursuers.(wiki:Vulture_King_(Jaehaerys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) used [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor) to burn the Vulture King's scattered mountain camps. When the outlaw king was captured at the end of the [Third Dornish War](wiki:Third_Dornish_War), Lord Rogar ordered his chains removed and that he be given a spear and shield. Rogar commanded that if the Vulture King managed to slay him, he was to be set free. The Vulture proved unable for the task and was cut down by Rogar with ease.(wiki:Vulture_King_(Jaehaerys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

In [83 AC](wiki:83_AC) [Morion Martell](wiki:Morion_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), began the ill-fated [Fourth Dornish War](wiki:Fourth_Dornish_War), as he resented that his princely father had not supported the second Vulture King.(wiki:Vulture_King_(Jaehaerys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
