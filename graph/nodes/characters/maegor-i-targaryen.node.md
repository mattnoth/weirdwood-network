## Identity

Maegor I Targaryen (12–48 AC) — child of Aegon I Targaryen and Visenya Targaryen; married to Alys Harroway and Ceryse Hightower and Elinor Costayne and Jeyne Westerling (wife of Maegor I) and Rhaena Targaryen (daughter of Aenys I) and Tyanna of the Tower; Prince. One of 2 Maegor Targaryens — see the Maegor Targaryen disambiguation entry.

## Origins

### Youth

In [11 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) announced that she was pregnant with a son. That son, Maegor, was born the next year,(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafmaegor_i_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) As a babe, he was a big and healthy child; almost twice as heavy as his half-brother [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) had been. Maegor and Aenys were not close as children; Maegor was raised by his mother on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), and in time became known as the "Prince on Dragonstone". He received his first sword at the age of three, and took to swordplay at once. He supposedly immediately butchered a cat with the sword, although some doubt the truth of this tale. Regardless, when Maegor was eight, he stabbed to death a palfrey which had kicked him, and slashed half the face off the stableboy who came running towards the [animal](wiki:Animal)'s screams. Maegor was trained at arms by Ser [Gawen Corbray](wiki:Gawen_Corbray), the [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms) of Dragonstone. He became a squire at the age of eight. By the age of twelve, he was already unhorsing boys four and five years his elder in the lists and battering seasoned men-at-arms into submission in the castle yard,(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) and by the age of thirteen he was already defeating hardened knights in mêlées.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) His mother gave him her [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword, [Dark Sister](wiki:Dark_Sister), as a [nameday](wiki:Nameday) present when Maegor turned thirteen in [25 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Despite the fact that there were several [dragon](wiki:Dragon) hatchlings on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), Maegor never claimed a dragon of his own. When his good-sister [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) teased him that he was afraid of dragons, Maegor scowled and replied that [only one](wiki:Balerion) was worthy of him.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

Maegor's brother [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) had married Lady [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) in [22 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) and their daughter [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) was born the next year. After Rhaena's birth, the issue arose as to whether Maegor would fall behind her in the line of succession. In [23 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-9) shortly after Maegor had turned eleven, Queen Visenya suggested a betrothal between Rhaena and Maegor to settle the succession issue, but both Rhaena's parents and the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon_I)) protested.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13) Instead, Maegor was betrothed to the High Septon's niece, Lady [Ceryse Hightower](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower), whom he married in [25 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) at the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept) in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) in a ceremony presided over by the High Septon himself.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) Though Maegor boasted that he had conceived a child on his wedding night, the marriage remained barren.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

Maegor had more success in his military accomplishments. In [28 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), he won renown a tourney by defeating three knights of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) in the joust, and next winning the mêlée. He was knighted by his father, King Aegon I, at the age of sixteen, making his the youngest knight in the realm at the time.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) In both [29 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and [30 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) Maegor assisted the [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships), Lord [Aethan Velaryon](wiki:Aethan_Velaryon), and the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Ser [Osmund Strong](wiki:Osmund_Strong), in two campaigns against the pirate king [Sargoso Saan](wiki:Sargoso_Saan) in the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), and in [31 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) defeated the [Giant of the Trident](wiki:Giant_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16)

### Hand and exile

In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), Maegor's father, King Aegon I, died, and his half-brother Aenys ascended the throne as King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen). Admitting to all assembled at Aegon I's funeral pyre that he was not nearly the warrior his half-brother was, Aenys presented Maegor with Aegon's [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) blade, [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre), proclaiming they would rule together.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-17)

After Aegon I's death, Maegor finally claimed a dragon, his father's mount, [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) who Maegor had long coveted as his own. Shortly after Aenys's ascension to the throne, several rebellions broke out. In the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), [Jonos Arryn](wiki:Jonos_Arryn) rose against his brother, Lord [Ronnel](wiki:Ronnel_Arryn_(king)), imprisoning him and his family. Although he arrived too late to rescue Lord Ronnel, Maegor personally crushed this rebellion, flying on Balerion to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). Hoping Maegor would show them mercy, Jonos's followers threw Jonos through the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door). Nonetheless, Maegor had all of them hanged.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-18) To replace Lord [Alyn Stokeworth](wiki:Alyn_Stokeworth), who had died fighting [another rebel](wiki:Harren_the_Red), King Aenys I named Maegor as his new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to reward him for his service, an office Maegor held for two years.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-19)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

Even though they had been married since [25 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Maegor and [Ceryse](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower) still had no children by [39 AC](wiki:39_AC). In [39 AC](wiki:39_AC), Maegor suddenly announced that Ceryse was barren and that he had taken another wife, Lady [Alys Harroway](wiki:Alys_Harroway). Unable to convince the [septon](wiki:Septon) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) to perform the wedding, Maegor and Alys had been wed in a [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) [wedding ceremony](wiki:Marriage) led by Dowager Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen). This marriage angered many. Maegor quarreled bitterly with his brother, King Aenys, while Ceryse's brother, Lord [Martyn Hightower](wiki:Martyn_Hightower), demanded that Maegor set Alys aside. The [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I)) denounced the marriage as sin and fornication and called Alys *"this whore of Harroway."* Many of the pious lords in the realm began to follow his example, openly referring to Alys as *"Maegor's Whore"*. Maegor remained defiant, however, citing that his father had taken two wives as well, claiming that the strictures of the Faith did not rule [the blood of the dragon](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-20)

Feeling he had no other option,(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Aenys furiously gave Maegor a choice: set Alys aside or go into exile for five years. Maegor chose the latter option and in [40 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) left for [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) with Alys and [Balerion](wiki:Balerion). He also took the sword Blackfyre with him, refusing Aenys's request to leave the sword behind.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-21) Septon [Murmison](wiki:Murmison) replaced Maegor as Hand. And in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC), Aenys named his eldest son, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-22)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)

### Claiming the throne

The [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) rose [in rebellion](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) against King Aenys I in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC). Early next year, Aenys died on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) while his eldest daughter and son, [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) and [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), were besieged at [Crakehall](wiki:Crakehall).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5) After Aenys was burned and buried, Visenya flew to Pentos on the back of her dragon, [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), to bring Maegor back to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-23)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8)

Although Aenys's son Aegon was the heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Maegor claimed the throne immediately upon his return. He was crowned at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) with the Valyrian steel crown of his father, King [Aegon I](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), instead of Aenys's ornate one. [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Gawen](wiki:Gawen) protested Maegor's coronation, stating that by all the [laws of inheritance](wiki:Customs), the crown should pass to Aegon. Maegor beheaded Gawen with [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre),(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-24)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9) making Gawen the first of three Grand Maesters who would be executed during Maegor's reign,(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and ending contention against Maegor's ascension.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-10)

Maegor next left for King's Landing, where the Faith had seized the [Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Sept_of_Remembrance) on [Rhaenys's Hill](wiki:Rhaenys's_Hill). Maegor flew to the city on [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) and planted his banner on [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill) to rally men to his side. His mother, Dowager Queen Visenya, challenged those who questioned her son's right to rule to prove themselves. Ser [Damon Morrigen](wiki:Damon_Morrigen), the Grand Captain of the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons), came forth. Maegor quickly decided to [settle the matter with swords](wiki:Trial_by_battle), and Damon demanded a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven), to which Maegor agreed.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-25)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-11)

Maegor had no knights of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) with him, as [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) had taken them to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) when he fled the capital, so Maegor turned to the crowd and asked who would stand with him. At first, most turned away or pretended they did not hear until a common man-at-arms named [Dick Bean](wiki:Dick_Bean) stepped forward. Ser [Bernarr Brune](wiki:Bernarr_Brune) was the next offering to stand beside the king. Shamed by Dick Bean's display of courage and Brune's words, several knights came forward, from whom Maegor chose his last four champions: Ser [Bramm of Blackhull](wiki:Bramm_of_Blackhull), Ser [Rayford Rosby](wiki:Rayford_Rosby), Ser [Guy Lothston](wiki:Guy_Lothston), and Lord [Lucifer Massey](wiki:Lucifer_Massey). Together they faced seven of the Warrior's Sons, led by Ser Damon Morrigen, their [Grand Captain](wiki:Grand_Captain). Maegor was the only survivor of the trial, but took a blow to the head and collapsed just as the last Warrior's Son died. The king was carried off the field by his mother and his supporters and confined to bed where he lay in a deep coma. At the [Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Sept_of_Remembrance), the [Warrior Son's](wiki:Warrior_Son's) debated, prayed and argued about their course. Some felt that Maegor must be accepted as king, since the God's had blessed him with victory; others insisted that they were bound by oath to follow the High Septon and fight on. On the twenty-eighth day of Maegor's coma, Queen [Alys Harroway](wiki:Alys_Harroway) returned from [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), bringing with her [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower). Tyanna took over Maegor's care. On the thirtieth day since the trial, the king woke.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-12)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-26)

### Tyranny

When he woke from his coma, Maegor mounted his [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) and flew to [Rhaenys's Hill](wiki:Rhaenys's_Hill). During the [burning of the Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Burning_of_the_Sept_of_Remembrance) his archers killed all those who attempted to flee the inferno.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-27) That same year, Maegor's forces achieved a victory over the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) in a [battle at Stonebridge](wiki:Battle_at_Stonebridge), and later in a [battle at the Great Fork](wiki:Battle_at_the_Great_Fork) of the [Blackwater](wiki:Blackwater_Rush).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-28) Maegor personally fought in the latter battle, burning men on Balerion's back.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-13) Following these two victories, Maegor declared his intent to marry again, taking [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower) as his third wife. [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Myros](wiki:Myros) objected, stating that Maegor's "[one true wife](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower)" awaited him at the [Hightower](wiki:Hightower), for which Maegor beheaded him with [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre). Maegor wed Tyanna on the Hill of Rhaenys, surrounded by the remains of the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) who had [died there](wiki:Burning_of_the_Sept_of_Remembrance). It was said Maegor had to execute a dozen septons before finding one willing to perform the ceremony. At Oldtown the High Septon loudly denounced "the abomination and his whores", while Ceryse continued to insist that she was Maegor's only lawful queen.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-29)

Construction of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), which had begun in [35 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), continued under Maegor's reign. In early [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), named Lord [Lucas Harroway](wiki:Lucas_Harroway), the father of his wife Alys, as his new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), leaving the administration of the kingdom in his hands while Maegor personally took charge of the construction of the Red Keep. He focused on constructing [secret passages](wiki:Secret_passages) and tunnels built through the depths of Aegon's High Hill, and commanded the construction of a fortified castle within the castle, which would become known as [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-30)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-14) He also ordered four levels of dungeons constructed, and had the fourth level build specifically for [torture](wiki:Torture).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

As the Faith continued to resist his rule,(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-31) Maegor raised [a set of laws](wiki:King_Maegor's_laws) which forbade holy men from carrying arms,(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) and punished lords who spoke out against the suppression of the Faith.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) In response, the High Septon commanded Maegor to present himself in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) and beg the forgiveness of the gods for his sins and cruelties. Still focused on the Red Keep's construction, Maegor allowed this resistance to go on for half a year, until his mother mounted her dragon [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) and burned the seats of several houses in the [Reach](wiki:Reach) in one single night. Maegor flew Balerion to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), burning the seats of houses who had defied his royal summons.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-32)

Next Maegor and Visenya turned towards [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), threatening to incinerate the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept) in response to the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I))'s condemnation of his [polygamous marriages](wiki:Polygamy). When the High Septon suddenly died, Lord [Martyn Hightower](wiki:Martyn_Hightower) opened the city's gates, saving Oldtown from the fire.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) The [new High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Pater)) was a more passive man, who anointed Maegor upon his arrival at the Starry Sept and did not lift a finger as the Warrior's Sons at Oldtown were either sent to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) or executed by Maegor. Maegor remained at Oldtown for half a year, presiding over the trials personally.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-33) Adding to his earlier laws, he now placed bounties on the heads of those faithful who remained defiant: [a gold dragon](wiki:Currency) was given for the scalp of a [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) and a silver stag for the scalp of a [Poor Fellow](wiki:Poor_Fellow).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-34)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

While at Oldtown, Maegor reconciled with Queen [Ceryse Hightower](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower). They celebrated their reunion with a great feast and had a second consummation. Afterwards, Ceryse returned to King's Landing.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-35)

Late that same year, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), [Aenys I's](wiki:Aenys_Targaryen) [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), [announced his claim to the Iron Throne](wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion). Supported by several lords of the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) and [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), Aegon marched at the head of an army of fifteen thousand men. Still at Oldtown, Maegor was warned by Queen Tyanna, his [mistress of whisperers](wiki:Mistress_of_whisperers), that [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon), [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn), [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark), and [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) were considering to join Aegon's cause if he could prove he could prevail. Maegor set forth from Oldtown, ordering his loyal lords to swarm Aegon's host from all sides. In the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye) the forces clashed, and Aegon's rebellion came to an end when Maegor slew him and his dragon, [Quicksilver](wiki:Quicksilver). Afterwards Maegor was known as "Maegor the Cruel" and a [kinslayer](wiki:Kinslaying) throughout the realm.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-36)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-15)

By [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), Maegor had made Aenys's widow, Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), and her youngest two children, [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), his mother's wards on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), while summoning her son [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) to court, making him his personal squire. Still without an heir of his own body, Maegor visited one of his three wives every night. When Queen Alys announced that she was pregnant that year,(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-17) she was confined to her bed. However, she went into labor only three months later, and gave birth to a monstrosity, eyeless and twisted. Convinced the child could not be his, Maegor had everyone involved in the birth executed, sparing only Alys's two sisters [Jeyne](wiki:Jeyne_Harroway) and [Hanna](wiki:Hanna_Harroway). It is said Maegor was seated on the Iron Throne with the head of [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Desmond](wiki:Desmond) in his hand when Queen Tyanna approached him, claiming that the child had been fathered by a secret affair of Alys, who had been desperate to give the king a son and so had slept with men of confirmed fertility. Tyanna provided a list of names as proof. Under torture, all but two of the twenty men confessed to their crime. Maegor had Alys dragged from her bed and given to Tyanna for torture. All Harroways at King's Landing, including Maegor's Hand, Lord [Lucas Harroway](wiki:Lucas_Harroway), were killed, with Alys's death the worst one of all.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-37)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-16) Maegor then departed the capital and marched on Harrenhal, killing its entire garrison, along with every person with even a single drop of Harroway blood. Next, he marched to [Lord Harroway's Town](wiki:Lord_Harroway's_Town) on the Trident and did the same there.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-38) With [House Harroway](wiki:House_Harroway) extinct, Maegor held a melee in Lord Harroway's Town with [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) the prize. Though gravely wounded, Ser [Walton Towers](wiki:Walton_Towers) was the victor and [House Towers](wiki:House_Towers_of_Harrenhal) was granted the ruined castle.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Walton died soon after of his wounds and was succeeded by his eldest son, [Jordan](wiki:Jordan_Towers).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-39) Lord Jordan remained a staunch supporter of King Maegor till his death and even [named](wiki:Named_after) his [youngest son](wiki:Maegor_Towers) after the king.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

When he returned to King's Landing, Maegor learned that his mother, Dowager Queen [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), had died. The news devastated Maegor, as she had been his strongest supporter. [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen)'s wife, Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), had escaped from Dragonstone during the resulting chaos, taking her two youngest children, Jaehaerys and Alysanne, and the Valyrian sword [Dark Sister](wiki:Dark_Sister) with her. In response, Maegor had her eldest surviving son, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), arrested and tortured. Viserys died after nine days of questioning by Queen Tyanna. His mutilated body was left in the courtyard for a fortnight in an unsuccessful attempt to force Alyssa to return.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-40)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-17)

In [45 AC](wiki:45_AC), Maegor saw the Red Keep completed.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-18) In celebration Maegor threw a feast for those who had taken part in the construction, engorging the workers with wines and sweetmeats as well as whores from the cities finest brothels. But after three days of revelry, Maegor had them all put to death in order to protect the secrets of the castle. Their bones were interred beneath the castle they had built.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-19)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Maegor next ordered the construction of the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) for the [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) dragons at the site of the ruined [Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Sept_of_Remembrance) on [Rhaenys's Hill](wiki:Rhaenys's_Hill). However, his treatment of the construction workers of the Red Keep caused all workers to flee the city, leaving the king forced to use prisoners from the dungeons for the construction instead.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-20)

Maegor's first wife, Queen [Ceryse Hightower](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower), suddenly fell ill and died shortly after the Red Keep had been completed. A rumor was spread that she had said a shrewish remark that affronted Maegor, after which he had ordered Ser [Owen Bush](wiki:Owen_Bush) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) to remove her tongue. People claimed that Ceryse had struggled so fiercely that Ser Owens knife slipped and he accidentally slashed her throat. Though the story was never proven and most historians insist it was slander concocted by the king's enemies, it was widely believed at the time.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-41)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-21)

In late [45 AC](wiki:45_AC), Maegor took the field again. In the great wood south of the [Blackwater](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), Maegor hunted down the Poor Fellows who had taken refuge, and ordered the rebel [Poxy Jeyne Poore](wiki:Jeyne_Poore) to be burned alive. Although Poor Fellows and peasants attempted to prevent the execution, Maegor had all the would-be rescuers slaughtered.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-42) The following year, Maegor returned to King's Landing with two thousand skulls of Poor Fellows and Warrior’s Sons. However, it was widely believed that many of the victims had been innocent [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) who had been in the wrong place at the wrong time.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-43)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-22) King Maegor's waging of his war was ruinously expensive, exhausting the royal treasury. Maegor's [Master of Coin](wiki:Master_of_Coin), Lord [Alton Butterwell](wiki:Alton_Butterwell) continuously raised taxes and imposed new ones and when these measures failed, Maegor demanded a tribute of gold from the high lords. Day by day, the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) turned against its king, as both smallfolk and lords began to despise him for his many cruelties.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-44)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-23)

### The Black Brides

By early [47 AC](wiki:47_AC), Maegor still had no heirs, and had stopped sleeping with Queen Tyanna. Maegor's counselors agreed that he needed to wed again, and multiple suggestions were made. Grand Maester [Benifer](wiki:Benifer) suggested [Clarisse Dayne](wiki:Clarisse_Dayne), the Lady of Starfall. Maegor's [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), [Alton Butterwell](wiki:Alton_Butterwell) suggested his widowed sister, a woman of proven fertility. Maegor's Hand, Lord Edwell Celtigar, suggested his two young daughters. Lord Velaryon of Driftmark suggested Maegor's niece, Princess Rhaena, to unite their claims and strengthen the royal bloodline. Maegor eventually decided to marry a woman of proven fertility, and to take three wives, in order to triple his chances of getting a son. He agreed that one of those wives should be his niece, in order to weaken any claim her younger siblings might put forth. And so, in [47 AC](wiki:47_AC), Maegor wed Lady [Elinor Costayne](wiki:Elinor_Costayne), Lady [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling_(wife_of_Maegor_I)), and Princess Rhaena in a single ceremony. All three women had been widowed through Maegor's actions or wars, and became known as the [Black Brides](wiki:Black_Brides). He named [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen), the eldest of Rhaena's two daughters by her deceased brother, Aegon, as his heir, disinheriting Rhaena's brother, Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), while sending Rhaena's other daughter, [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon)), to Oldtown to be raised as a septa. Half a year after the wedding, Lady Jeyne was announced to be pregnant. Not long after, Lady Elinor became pregnant as well. Three months before her due-date, however, Queen Jeyne went into labor, and gave birth to a stillborn child who was legless and armless and had both male and female genitals. Jeyne died not long after. People began to whisper that Maegor was cursed, and that the gods would never grant him a living son. Maegor himself, however, had Queen Tyanna seized and deliver to the dungeons, where she confessed to having poisoned both Alys's child as Jeyne's, even before the torturers began. She promised that Elinor would birth a similar stillborn monster. After her confession, Maegor executed Tyanna himself, but her promise came true two months later, when Elinor went into labor in early [48 AC](wiki:48_AC) and birthed an eyeless child with small wings.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-45)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-24)

### Downfall

By [48 AC](wiki:48_AC) Maegor's tyranny could no longer be borne by the realm. At [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) [Aenys I's](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) last surviving son, Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), [put forth his claim to the throne](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen's_uprising), supported by Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon), who was named [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm) and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) by the prince. Jaehaerys had two dragons on his side, his own mount [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor) and his [sister's](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) mount [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing), against Maegor's [Balerion](wiki:Balerion).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-46)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-25)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Learning of her brother's claim, Queen [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) fled from Maegor in the night, escaping on her dragon [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre) with the Valyrian blade [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre), and her daughter, Princess [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen), adding a third dragon to her brothers cause. Upon discovering Rhaena's disappearance, Maegor sent a rider to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) commanding [House Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower) to behead Princess [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon)) as punishment for her mother's betrayal. Lord Hightower refused and imprisoned the messenger instead. Lord [Edwell Celtigar](wiki:Edwell_Celtigar) resigned his position as Hand and returned to [Claw Isle](wiki:Claw_Isle) and [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Benifer](wiki:Benifer) secretly escaped on a ship to [Pentos](wiki:Pentos). Ser [Olyver Bracken](wiki:Olyver_Bracken) and Ser [Raymund Mallery](wiki:Raymund_Mallery), two of Maegor's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), also deserted him. Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)), the admiral of the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet), was the first of the great lords to forsake Maegor, taking the royal fleet with him, and many other lords followed his example. The great houses of [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister), [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell), and [Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) came out against Maegor and in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) gave support to Septon [Moon](wiki:Moon_(septon)) and Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett), the leaders of the [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-47)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-26)

Maegor called his banners in response, but few answered, giving Maegor an army of barely four thousand soldiers. Despite this, Maegor refused to surrender. When Lord [Hayford](wiki:House_Hayford) suggested that Maegor abdicate and [take the black](wiki:Night's_Watch) instead, Maegor ordered him beheaded and continued his war council with Lord Hayford's head mounted on a lance behind the Iron Throne.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-48)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-27) At the end of the war council, Maegor remained behind alone in the throne room to brood. He was found dead the next morning by Queen [Elinor](wiki:Elinor_Costayne), seated on the Iron Throne with his robes covered in blood and his wrists slashed. A spike from one of the swords on the throne behind him was impaled through the back of his neck. How Maegor died was never discovered. Some say he had been killed by Queen Elinor, others that he had been killed by a knight of his own Kingsguard. Yet others say he had been killed by a builder who escaped the slaughter three years earlier and desired revenge, and many believe that Maegor had been killed by the throne itself. Others believe that Maegor killed himself by opening his wrists on the blades of the Iron Throne.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-49)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-28)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

### Legacy

Maegor ruled for six years and sixty-six days and died without issue. He was succeeded by his nephew, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), the youngest son of the late King Aenys I. The Faith Militant uprising only ended during Jaehaerys' reign.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Jaehaerys agreed to pardon all those of the Faith who would set their swords aside,(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) though he also deprived the Faith of the right to hold trials.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

During the [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) of [101 AC](wiki:101_AC), a strapping red-haired man-at-arms, who claimed to be a bastard of Maegor, put forth his claim to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). His only proof was his mother, an aged innkeep's daughter who said she had once been raped by Maegor. The lords believed the claim of rape, but not that it had impregnated her.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Decades later, during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), [Silver Denys](wiki:Silver_Denys) claimed to be descended from a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son of Maegor, but he was mutilated by the dragon [Sheepstealer](wiki:Sheepstealer) and slain with his sons by the [Cannibal](wiki:Cannibal_(dragon)) when trying to claim a dragon.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower) believed that Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) would be a second Maegor the Cruel if he would ever ascend to the throne,(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and at the start of the civil war his daughter [Alicent](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) claimed that Daemon would be "as cruel and unforgiving as Maegor ever was" as king consort.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Daemon's wife, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), came to be considered a grasping, cruel, vindictive woman, and was dubbed "King Maegor with teats" by a wit in King's Landing. "Maegor's teats" was a common curse amongst Kingslanders for a hundred years thereafter.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

In [232 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), a son was born to [Aerion Targaryen](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen) and [Daenora Targaryen](wiki:Daenora_Targaryen), and Aerion gave him the ominous name of [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerion)).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

## Appearances & Description

Maegor was a tall, broad, and fearsomely strong man,(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) larger than his father, [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-ssm_7-0) He was bull-like, with heavy shoulders, a thick neck, and huge arms. Though he was on the heavy side, he was not fat, but more massive and square. He kept his hair short and his beard trimmed to his jawline. He had angry-looking, suspicious eyes, and a scowling mouth.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-ssm_7-1)

Maegor was a born warrior(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) whose skill with weapons was unmatched.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) He lived for war, [tourneys](wiki:Tourneys), and battle, and rose to become one of the youngest and finest [knights](wiki:Knight) of his time.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) However, he was also a hard and brutal man(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-ssm_7-2) who craved violence, death, and absolute mastery over all he deemed his.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) His savagery in the field and his harshness toward defeated enemies was frequently remarked upon.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

Maegor was quarrelsome, quick to take offense, slow to forgive, and fearsome in his wroth. He was a rigid man, unyielding, and unbending. He preferred fire and steel over settling issues through discussion, and showed cruel tendencies early in his childhood. Although he had many companions throughout his youth, he had no true friends, and even as an adult Maegor trusted no one.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

Maegor wore his father's [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel)-and-ruby [crown](wiki:Crown).(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) His armor, however, was more elaborate than Aegon's. Instead of a shirt of scale, Maegor wore a breastplate, covered by a surcoat that displayed the Targaryen three-headed dragon, red on black.(wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-ssm_7-3)
