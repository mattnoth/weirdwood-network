## Origins

Although Tormund is said to have slain a [giant](wiki:Giant), he claims to have actually cut open the belly of a sleeping giantess and slept in her for warmth during a winter storm. Tormund claims the giantess, thinking he was a babe, then suckled him for three months in the spring.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Tormund also claims to once have drunkenly slept with a [bear](wiki:Bear).(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Tormund once thought to make himself [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall), but he was defeated by [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder).(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Tormund](wiki:Category:Images_of_Tormund)*

Although not tall, Tormund has a broad chest, a massive belly,(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and a beard white as snow.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) On his massive arms he wears golden bands engraved with [runes](wiki:Runes) of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men),(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) which have been passed down by his forefathers.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Tormund is armored with heavy ringmail taken from a dead [ranger](wiki:Ranger).(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

The often jovial Tormund enjoys food and drink, especially [ale](wiki:Ale) and [mead](wiki:Mead).(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) He is styled Tall-talker, Horn-blower and Breaker of Ice, Husband to Bears, the Mead-king of [Ruddy Hall](wiki:Ruddy_Hall), Speaker to Gods and Father of Hosts.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) named him Horn-blower for the power of Tormund's lungs.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) It is said that Tormund can laugh the snow off mountaintops.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) He distrusts the [men of the Frozen Shore](wiki:Men_of_the_Frozen_Shore).(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Tormund does not know how to read.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

Tormund is among those gathered in [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder)'s tent when [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) is presented to him.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6) Jon is attached to Tormund's war party, with [Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte) and [Longspear Ryk](wiki:Ryk) joining as well. Tormund joins in the singing of "[The Last of the Giants](wiki:The_Last_of_the_Giants)", and he advises Jon to sleep with Ygritte.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

During the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall), Jon sees Tormund and two of his sons near a [siege turtle](wiki:Turtle_(siege_engine)).(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) When Jon goes to treat with Mance for the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), Tormund is the first to greet him. He brings Jon to Mance's tent and defends Jon's right to speak to Mance. The [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall) threatens to have Tormund blow the [Horn of Winter](wiki:Horn_of_Winter) to bring down the [Wall](wiki:Wall). When [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) attacks the wildlings, Tormund leads a triple line of spearmen. They are attacked on the flank by Stannis's knights and break.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Tormund escapes capture by Stannis's knights,(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) but his son [Dormund](wiki:Dormund) is killed by Ser [Richard Horpe](wiki:Richard_Horpe).(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

### A Feast for Crows

With Mance a captive at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), Tormund becomes the leader of many free folk [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall).(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

Tormund is forced to end one of his sons, [Torwynd](wiki:Torwynd), after he dies from a chill and rises as a [wight](wiki:Wight). Tormund's daughter, [Munda](wiki:Munda), marries Longspear Ryk.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6)

Jon Snow, now [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), and other officers expect that Tormund will besiege the undermanned Wall again.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Jon sends [Val](wiki:Val) to retrieve Tormund and bring an offer of peace to him.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Tormund agrees to the terms and goes to the [Wall](wiki:Wall). The free folk do not like the terms offered by the Night's Watch, but with attacks of the [Others](wiki:Others) constantly decimating their numbers and their people's suffering only increasing, they consent to the terms.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7) Tormund claims there are four thousand who follow him.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8)

Tormund has the free folk hand over their wealth or anything of value to the Watch, along with a son as a hostage from their chiefs. Tormund calls this Jon's "[blood price](wiki:Blood_price)". Tormund is the first of the free folk to hand over his wealth, giving over his gold wristbands. Jon states he may keep them if he wishes, but Tormund refuses; he will not let it be said he made the rest of his people give up their wealth while he kept his.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-9)

The peace means free folk warriors will garrison the abandoned castles along the Wall to help the Night's Watch defend the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) from the Others, while the non-martial free folk will be settled on the [Gift](wiki:Gift) and the [New Gift](wiki:New_Gift). Jon re-garrisons [Oakenshield](wiki:Oakenshield_(Wall)) as Tormund's seat.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-10) The lord commander takes Tormund's son [Dryn](wiki:Dryn) as his page.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Another son, [Toregg](wiki:Toregg), burns the dead to prevent them from becoming wights.(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

Jon asks Tormund to march with him to rescue [Cotter Pyke](wiki:Cotter_Pyke) and the stranded people at [Hardhome](wiki:Hardhome). However, Jon changes his plans when he receives [a letter](wiki:Bastard_Letter) from [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow). Tormund agrees to lead the ranging to Hardhome instead, after hearing the letter's contents. Jon is then attacked in the [mutiny at Castle Black](wiki:Mutiny_at_Castle_Black).(wiki:Tormund.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
