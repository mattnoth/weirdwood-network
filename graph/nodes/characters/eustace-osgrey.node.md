## Appearances & Description

Eustace was tall, broad, and handsome in his youth. As he grew older, he remained big-boned, broad-shouldered, and barrel-chested, with strong, sharp features. He wore a thick mustache and kept his hair close-cropped. Eustace's hair turned white as milk, but his mustache and eyebrows were ashy gray. His eyes were a paler gray color, and appeared to be full of sadness.(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
Eustace was a stubborn [knight](wiki:Knight) proud of the past glories of [House Osgrey](wiki:House_Osgrey), although he grew forgetful in his old age. He treasured the broken shield of his ancestor, Ser [Wilbert Osgrey](wiki:Wilbert_Osgrey).(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) He admired valorous warriors, such as [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) and Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), and disliked King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) and [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen).(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
The knight wore mail and plate. Eustace's long, woolen surcoat, decorated with the chequy [lion](wiki:Lion) of his family, was faded yellow. A chequy lion also adorned his frayed silk cloak, and he wielded a [sword](wiki:Sword). The knight rode a [grey gelding](wiki:Horse).(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

## Origins

### Eustace and Wyman[ ]

Ser Eustace had been good friends with his neighbor, Lord [Wyman Webber](wiki:Wyman_Webber), before the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), though not without some jealousy, as much of the [House Webber](wiki:House_Webber) lands had once belonged to [House Osgrey](wiki:House_Osgrey), including the Webbers' castle, [Coldmoat](wiki:Coldmoat).(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)
Eustace's youngest son, [Addam](wiki:Addam_Osgrey), served at Coldmoat as Wyman's [page](wiki:Page) and [squire](wiki:Squire), and began a childhood romance with Wyman's daughter, [Rohanne](wiki:Rohanne_Webber), who was near the same age. However, when Eustace proposed that Addam marry Rohanne, Wyman courteously rejected him, though Eustace heard Wyman laughing with Ser [Lucas Inchfield](wiki:Lucas_Inchfield) as he left. Eustace never returned to Coldmoat during Wyman's lifetime.(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

### First Blackfyre Rebellion[ ]

Eustace supported the pretender king [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) during the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), in hopes that he would reverse the lands and castles that [House Osgrey](wiki:House_Osgrey) had lost under [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) rule. Eustace also apparently believed the rumors about King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) being a [bastard](wiki:Bastard), calling him "Daeron the Falseborn" years later, and proclaiming the merit of the "rightful king", Daemon.(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)
In the end the Rebellion failed, and Eustace's three sons, [Edwyn](wiki:Edwyn_Osgrey), [Harrold](wiki:Harrold_Osgrey), and [Addam](wiki:Addam_Osgrey), were killed at the decisive [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field).(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

### Aftermath of the Rebellion[ ]

Eustace was pardoned by King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) after the rebellion, although some lands of [House Osgrey](wiki:House_Osgrey), including the [Chequy Water](wiki:Chequy_Water), were granted to [House Webber](wiki:House_Webber), which had remained loyal to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12) Eustace's daughter [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Osgrey) was sent to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) as a hostage.(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)

- Book-cited overlay, Eustace's own words on burying his sons: "They died good deaths, fighting bravely for the king," Ser Eustace told Dunk, "and I brought them home and buried them among the blackberries" (`sources/chapters/tss/tss-dunk-01.md:251`).

After Eustace brought home the bones of his sons and allowed Alysanne to be taken away, his wife committed suicide by throwing herself from the top of [Standfast](wiki:Standfast). Eustace's wife and sons were buried at Standfast by the blackberry bushes his sons had loved to pick. [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber) blamed Eustace for the death of [Addam Osgrey](wiki:Addam_Osgrey).(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14)
Alysanne stayed in King's Landing for the rest of her life, becoming a [silent sister](wiki:Silent_sisters). Ser Eustace went to visit her once, but she refused to break her vows of silence to speak with him. Alysanne died during the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness) (in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC) or [210 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), which made Eustace the last of his line.(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15)

### Conflict with Rohanne Webber[ ]

In [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), [Dake](wiki:Dake), a [peasant](wiki:Smallfolk) of House Osgrey's villages who had served as a forager for Ser Eustace during the Blackfyre Rebellion, was accused of stealing sheep from the neighboring lands of House Webber. After [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber), [Lady of Coldmoat](wiki:Lady_of_Coldmoat), tried contacting Eustace twice with no response, she sent her [knights](wiki:Knight) across the [Chequy Water](wiki:Chequy_Water) to seize Dake and bring him to [Coldmoat](wiki:Coldmoat) for punishment. When Eustace arrived at Coldmoat to demand Dake's release, he learned the peasant had been sewn into a sack with rocks, which was sunk in the moat of Coldmoat. Eustace then swore a holy vow that he would never return to Coldmoat again, except to take possession of it.(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16)
The aging Eustace took on [hedge knights](wiki:Hedge_knight) to protect his lands, first Ser [Bennis of the Brown Shield](wiki:Bennis_of_the_Brown_Shield) after Dake's death and later Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall). In [211 AC](wiki:211_AC), Bennis, Duncan, and Duncan's squire Egg, who was actually Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), happened upon the Chequy Water in the process of being dammed by peasants of House Webber. Believing that the men were trespassing on Osgrey lands, Bennis cut [Wolmer](wiki:Wolmer)'s face in an attempt to scare them off. Eustace, fearing that Lady Webber would demand Bennis's life as she had with Dake's, ordered Bennis and Duncan to raise troops from the peasant villages of House Osgrey and prepare them for a possible war.(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-17)
However, the meager amount of men of fighting age were untrained, and no match for the knights of House Webber. Seeing this, Eustace decided to pay a [blood price](wiki:Blood_price) to settle the matter, but as he had sworn to never return to Coldmoat, he sent Duncan to make the offer. However, Rohanne rejected the blood price, demanding that Bennis be brought to her to have his nose slit as a punishment, or that she would come to Standfast by the next day with fire and sword. [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Cerrick](wiki:Cerrick) revealed a document to Duncan, sealed by the crown, showing House Webber's possession of the Chequy Water and revealing that Eustace had been a [traitorous](wiki:Traitor) supporter of the [Blackfyres](wiki:House_Blackfyre). Duncan also learned that Rohanne blamed Eustace for the death of her first love, Eustace's son [Addam Osgrey](wiki:Addam_Osgrey).(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-18)
When Duncan returned to Standfast, he confronted Eustace about his Blackfyre loyalties and about lying to him about the deaths of his sons and the status of the Chequy Water. Duncan planned to leave Standfast the next morning, but Standfast was awakened that night by a fire in [Wat's Wood](wiki:Wat's_Wood). Believing that Rohanne had set the fire, Eustace planned to use his peasant militia to make war on her lands and burn one of the Webber mills. However, Duncan realized that all the peasants would die in the conflict. Duncan sent home the smallfolk and told Eustace he would represent him at the confrontation with Rohanne.(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-19)
At the border of their lands, the Chequy Water, Eustace accused Rohanne of setting the fire, while she denied all complicity. Duncan and Rohanne parlayed in the middle of the river. She would not accept Duncan cutting his own cheek as payment for Bennis, and instead demanded an apology for Eustace's slander about the fire. They agreed to a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) between Duncan and Rohanne's champion, Ser [Lucas Inchfield](wiki:Lucas_Inchfield), the [castellan](wiki:Castellan) of Coldmoat. Duncan prevailed, but almost drowned in the river in the process. While Eustace and Duncan were away from Standfast, Bennis tied up Eustace's servants, [Sam Stoops](wiki:Sam_Stoops) and his wife, stole the remaining valuables from Eustace's tower, and fled.(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-20)
While Duncan was unconscious, Eustace and Rohanne reconciled, speaking about Addam and visiting his grave at Standfast. When Rohanne began to weep, Eustace comforted her. The next day, Eustace married Rohanne, as a resolution to the will of her father, Lord [Wyman Webber](wiki:Wyman_Webber), that she be married within two years of his death or lose her ruling rights to a Webber cousin, Ser [Wendell](wiki:Wendell_Webber). Egg attended Eustace, now Lord of Coldmoat, during the wedding feast. Eustace and Rohanne offered to make Ser Duncan their new captain of the guard at Coldmoat, but Duncan refused and left with Egg.(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-21)

### Legacy[ ]

Not long after, while attending the [wedding tourney](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) at [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls), Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) thought of [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber) and Eustace, and wondered if they ever thought of him. Upon encountering some other [Blackfyre rebels](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion) at the tourney, he was also reminded of Eustace.(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Eustace died some time before [219 AC](wiki:219_AC).(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-calc_3-1) Rohanne later married [Gerold Lannister](wiki:Gerold_Lannister).(wiki:Eustace_Osgrey.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
