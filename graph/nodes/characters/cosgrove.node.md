## Origins

During the [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls), Cosgrove acted as the [master of the games](wiki:Master_of_the_games). Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) sent his squire, [Egg](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), to meet with Cosgrove and enter the [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight) in the lists as a [mystery knight](wiki:Mystery_knight) called the Gallows Knight.(wiki:Cosgrove.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) Ser [Uthor Underleaf](wiki:Uthor_Underleaf) bribed Cosgrove for favorable matches.(wiki:Cosgrove.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Cosgrove was reluctant to admit Ser [Glendon Flowers](wiki:Glendon_Flowers) to Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell)'s feast within [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls), insisting that Ser [Quentyn Ball](wiki:Quentyn_Ball) did not have a son and disregarding Glendon's claim of having been knighted by Ser [Morgan Dunstable](wiki:Morgan_Dunstable). Ser [Kirby Pimm](wiki:Kirby_Pimm) spoke with Cosgrove, however, and Glendon was eventually allowed to attend the feast.
