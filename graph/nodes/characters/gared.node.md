## Origins

Gared joined the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) as a boy and spent forty years as a [ranger](wiki:Ranger). When Gared's ears had frostbite one winter, Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) had to cut them off.(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

While drinking [wine](wiki:Wine) in the barracks of [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), Gared joked that Ser [Waymar Royce](wiki:Waymar_Royce) personally killed the [sables](wiki:Sable) for his cloak.(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

Shortly before *[A Song of Ice and Fire](wiki:A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire)* starts, Waymar, [Will](wiki:Will), and Gared were sent ranging into the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest) by [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), in search of a band of [wildling](wiki:Free_folk) raiders. Jeor gave the command to Waymar, although he was the least experienced of the three, whereas Jeor counted Gared and Will among his best men. However, Waymar felt it was his due to have a command because he was a [knight](wiki:Knight), and the Lord Commander accepted because he did not want to offend Waymar's father, Lord [Yohn Royce](wiki:Yohn_Royce).(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

## Appearances & Description

Gared is past fifty. He lost both ears, three toes and the little finger of his left hand to the cold.(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Old and scrawny,(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) he is considered an experienced [ranger](wiki:Ranger).(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

The [man-at-arms](wiki:Man-at-arms) is known to make fun of Ser [Waymar Royce](wiki:Waymar_Royce) because of the fine clothes the inexperienced lordling wears.(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Gared's sword is a short, ugly thing with a nicked edge and a grip discolored by sweat. His garron is smaller than Waymar's destrier.(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Gared, [Will](wiki:Will), and Ser [Waymar Royce](wiki:Waymar_Royce) follow the [wildlings](wiki:Free_folk), finding them dead on the ninth day. Gared wants to return to the [Wall](wiki:Wall), but Waymar insists they discover what killed the wildlings. Gared says he can feel that something is wrong, but Waymar dismisses the suggestion. He mocks the experienced ranger repeatedly, making Gared increasingly angry. Waymar orders Gared to remain with the horses while he and Will walk the last part. Waymar refuses to allow Gared to make a fire. After Waymar is slain by [Others](wiki:Others), the knight rises as a [wight](wiki:Wight) and kills Will.(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

A terrified(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Rawoiafgared.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Gared deserts the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) and flees south of the [Wall](wiki:Wall), but he is arrested outside a small [holdfast in the hills](wiki:Holdfast_in_the_hills) near [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). He is questioned by Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), but Eddard's words do not reach the ragged and distraught Gared.(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragot70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

For his desertion, Gared is sentenced to death by Eddard, who performs the beheading himself with the greatsword [Ice](wiki:Ice), in accordance with the traditions of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men). Afterwards, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) laughs and kicks Gared's head when it lands near his feet, provoking [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow)'s disapproval.(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Eddard sends Gared's head to [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch).(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

When Jeor tells [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) about the conditions on the Wall, he mentions the ranging on which Will and Waymar went missing. Jeor laments giving the command to someone as inexperienced as Waymar. Jeor mentions that Gared had been longer at the Wall than himself and that he would never have believed the man would turn.(wiki:Gared.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)
