## Origins

Marilda was the daughter of a shipwright at [Hull](wiki:Hull). She was frequently seen at the shipyards, often underfoot. Marilda was sixteen years old when she gave birth to [Addam](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) in [114 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and she was barely eighteen when [Alyn](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) was birthed in [115 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). She refused to name the father of her sons.(wiki:Marilda_of_Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

After her father's death a decade later, Marilda sold his shipyard and used the proceeds to captain a trading cog she called *[Mouse](wiki:Mouse_(ship))*. By [130 AC](wiki:130_AC) Marilda owned seven ships, which her sons served on.(wiki:Marilda_of_Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

When Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) called for [dragonseeds](wiki:Dragonseed) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Marilda claimed that her children, who had silver hair and [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes), had been fathered by Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon), who had died in [120 AC](wiki:120_AC). Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) and Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) agree with Marilda's claim, but [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) wrote that Addam and Alyn had actually been fathered by Laenor's own sire, Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), a frequent visitor to Driftmark's shipyards. After Addam became a [dragonrider](wiki:Dragonrider) on [Seasmoke](wiki:Seasmoke), who was Laenor's own mount, Corlys and Jacaerys convinced Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) to legitimize the [bastard](wiki:Bastard) brothers as Velaryons.(wiki:Marilda_of_Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) Addam eventually died in the [Second Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:Second_Battle_of_Tumbleton).(wiki:Marilda_of_Hull.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

After the death of Rhaenyra in the [fall of Dragonstone](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone) and the pardoning of Corlys by Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower), Alyn agreed to transport King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) from Dragonstone back to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Protected by a dozen Velaryon galleys, Marilda sailed the king to the capital aboard *Mouse*.(wiki:Marilda_of_Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

When Corlys died in King's Landing in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), Marilda and Lord Alyn sailed his body back to Driftmark aboard *[Mermaid's Kiss](wiki:Mermaid's_Kiss)*. Corlys's remains were then buried at sea near [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) aboard *[Sea Snake](wiki:Sea_Snake)*.(wiki:Marilda_of_Hull.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Marilda constructed the [Mouse House](wiki:Mouse_House) at Hull during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring).(wiki:Marilda_of_Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

When Alyn Oakenfist led a fleet of merchantmen to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) and the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), the [Lord of the Tides](wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides) commanded from a galley named *[Bold Marilda](wiki:Bold_Marilda)*.(wiki:Marilda_of_Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

## Appearances & Description

A pretty woman, Marilda was known as Mouse because she was "[small, quick, and always underfoot](wiki:Mouse)" as a girl.(wiki:Marilda_of_Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) She was intelligent and adventurous.(wiki:Marilda_of_Hull.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)
