## Origins

At some point, Cressen and [Walgrave](wiki:Walgrave) knew each other.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Maester Cressen served as the maester of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). In [278 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), following the wreck of the *[Windproud](wiki:Windproud)* in [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay), Cressen helped identify the deceased. When the [fool](wiki:Fool) [Patchface](wiki:Patchface) washed ashore three days after the shipwreck, Ser [Harbert](wiki:Harbert), the [castellan](wiki:Castellan) of Storm’s End at the time, suggested that the mad boy be given the [gift of mercy](wiki:Gift_of_mercy) with [milk of the poppy](wiki:Milk_of_the_poppy), but Cressen refused to agree. In the end, Cressen won the argument.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

Because Lord [Steffon Baratheon](wiki:Steffon_Baratheon) and his wife [Cassana Estermont](wiki:Cassana_Estermont) had died in the wreck, Cressen raised the Baratheon children. His favorite was [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), who needed Cressen more because he lacked the charm of his brothers. Cressen came to regard Stannis as the son he never had.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

Cressen was at Storm's End when it was [besieged](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) in [282](wiki:282_AC)–[263 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). When Ser [Gawen Wylde](wiki:Gawen_Wylde) and three other [knights](wiki:Knight) were caught trying to sneak out a postern gate to surrender, Stannis, who held command, prepared to have them hurled at the [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) host via [catapult](wiki:Catapult). Cressen convinced him instead to imprison the men, pointing out that the castle might at some point be forced to [eat their dead](wiki:Cannibalism), and the four knights were "good meat".(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Cressen attended the [knighting](wiki:Knight) of Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) by Stannis after the siege.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

When Stannis was named [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone) by his brother, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Cressen followed him to the ancient stronghold. Circa [289](wiki:289_AC)–[290 AC](wiki:290_AC), Stannis's infant daughter [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon) contracted [greyscale](wiki:Greyscale). Though Cressen was able to save her life, the disease almost killed her, and left her disfigured. Cressen feels that her sadness is a mark of his failure.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

In [297 AC](wiki:297_AC), Cressen fell and broke his hip, and afterwards was no longer able to climb to the [rookery](wiki:Rookery). In [298 AC](wiki:298_AC), when Cressen fell ill, the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) sent young Maester [Pylos](wiki:Pylos) to Dragonstone. It was said that he was to be Cressen's assistant, but Cressen was aware that truly meant that Pylos was to be his replacement after he died.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)

## Appearances & Description

Cressen is nearly eighty years old. He is frail with a bad hip, causing him to have trouble walking and climbing stairs. The maester has wrinkly, thin skin, and trembling hands.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Cressen loves [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) and feels guilty about the sadness of Stannis's daughter, [Shireen](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon). He does not feel at home at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), although he has grown fond of its [gargoyle](wiki:Gargoyle) decorations.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Because of Cressen's long service to Stannis, Stannis has always confided in him.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Cressen receives [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon) and [Patchface](wiki:Patchface) when they come to see the [white raven](wiki:White_raven) the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) has sent to [the island](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) to announce the end of [summer](wiki:Seasons). He attempts to sooth her worries about being eaten by a [dragon](wiki:Dragon), and discusses the [red comet](wiki:Red_comet) with her.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)

After [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) declares himself king, he unsuccessfully sends messengers to gather support for his claim, with only the vassals of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) supporting him. The lords of the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) support Stannis's younger brother, [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), who has also declared for the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Cressen advises Stannis to ally with the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) and the [Arryns](wiki:House_Arryn), but Stannis insists that those crowning [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) as [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) are committing [treason](wiki:Treason). Stannis's queen, Lady [Selyse Florent](wiki:Selyse_Florent), urges Stannis to rely solely on [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) and says that Renly will die soon, according to [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), a [red priestess](wiki:Red_priest). Cressen thinks that Selyse is suggesting [kinslaying](wiki:Kinslaying), and is shocked when Stannis dismisses him to listen to Selyse's counsel. Cressen blames Melisandre and her "madness", and plans to kill her.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9)

Stannis commands Maester [Pylos](wiki:Pylos) to let Cressen rest, but Cressen insists upon attending the feast that night. After Patchface collides with Cressen and causes the old man to fall, Melisandre places the [fool](wiki:Fool)'s belled helm on Cressen's head, which the maester removes. Because of Cressen's age, Stannis announces he will now be taking counsel from the younger Pylos. Cressen is eventually seated beside Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth). He puts the [strangler](wiki:Strangler), a potent [poison](wiki:Poison), in Davos's cup of [wine](wiki:Wine), shaking off the knight's hand and protest. Instead, Cressen invites Melisandre to drink the cup with him. Apparently aware of Cressen's plan, Melisandre suggests he can still spill the wine, but Cressen insists that they share the cup. Melisandre drinks the majority of the wine and remains unaffected. When Cressen drinks the final sip, the poison kills him.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10)

After Cressen's death, the gossip on Dragonstone is that Cressen had challenged R'hllor and been struck down for his impiety.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) When Davos meets with Stannis after the burning of Dragonstone's wooden statues of the [Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), Stannis admits that he had not wanted Cressen at the feast. Despite his anger at the maester's counsel, Stannis had not wanted the old man dead.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

### A Storm of Swords

Aboard the *[Bountiful Harvest](wiki:Bountiful_Harvest)*, Davos wonders if Melisandre can be killed by mortal weapons, recalling Cressen's attempt to kill her with poison.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

When climbing up the narrow twisted steps of Sea Dragon Tower, Davos is reminded of Cressen, who had a lot of trouble climbing the steps of the tower after he had broken his hip.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

When meeting with his the small group of [king's men](wiki:King's_men), just before [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm) is secretly shipped away, Davos reminds them that when Maester Cressen attempted to kill Melisandre she knew at once.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

### A Feast for Crows

In [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), the elderly [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Walgrave](wiki:Walgrave) sometimes confuses the [novice](wiki:Novice) [Pate](wiki:Pate_(novice)) with Cressen.(wiki:Cressen.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
