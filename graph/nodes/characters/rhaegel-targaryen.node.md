## Origins

Like his brothers, Prince Rhaegel learned how to fight from Ser [Quentyn Ball](wiki:Quentyn_Ball), the [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms) of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep),(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) but he lacked martial prowess.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) Rhaegel was married to Lady [Alys Arryn](wiki:Alys_Arryn_(wife_of_Rhaegel)) and had three children: the twins [Aelor](wiki:Aelor_Targaryen) and [Aelora](wiki:Aelora_Targaryen) and daughter [Daenora](wiki:Daenora_Targaryen).(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) He and his brother Aerys seldom left the court, leading their father to pass the castle of [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall) instead to their youngest brother, Maekar.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-8) Rhaegel became [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), when his older brother Prince [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen) ascended the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), as Aerys had no children of his own.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Despite his position as the heir(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) and the common Targaryen practice of naming close blood relations as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King),(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Rhaegel was seemingly not in contention for the Handship when Aerys took the throne, for when Aerys named [Bloodraven](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) as Hand, their younger brother Maekar quarreled bitterly as he had expected the position to be granted to him.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Some even mistook Maekar as Aerys's true heir, forgetting both Rhaegel and his children.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) Those in the realm who mistrusted Bloodraven as Aerys's Hand and felt he held an inordinate amount of power considered Rhaegel impotent to oppose him on account of his meek nature and madness.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Such individuals even suggested that Rhaegel and his children were at risk of being killed by Bloodraven or his brother Maekar, though the claim infuriated Maekar's son, Aegon.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)

Rhaegel died in [215 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), whilst choking on a [lamprey](wiki:Lamprey) pie. His son, Prince Aelor, the new Prince of Dragonstone, would not long outlive him, and his daughter Aelora's death by suicide would soon follow.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

## Appearances & Description

Rhaegel Targaryen was considered a sweet(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) and gentle boy(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) but touched by [madness](wiki:Targaryen_madness).(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) He was furthermore meek, sickly(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) and feeble-minded to the point that some in the realm believed him as good as dead.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) During the reigns of both his father, King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen),(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) and brother, King [Aerys I](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), his condition was well-known in the realm, and rumor claimed that he danced naked through the halls of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). According to his nephew, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), Rhaegel was well-loved by his younger brother, Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Much like his nephew [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) and brother [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), Rhaegel lacked martial prowess and had little place on a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) field. Because of his mental and physical condition, he was unlikely to traverse half the realm to attend a tourney.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

[Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont) of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) recounts the tale of [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I))'s family history and how his brother [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) came to the throne to his personal [steward](wiki:Steward), [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow). Lord Mormont notes that King Daeron II had four sons and that Aegon V was the fourth son of a fourth son.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Rhaegel Targaryen was the third(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) of the four sons of Daeron II mentioned by Mormont.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Discussing both their fathers' objections to them becoming maesters, Maester Aemon tells [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) that his grandfather sent him to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) because too many dragons were as dangerous as too few. He mentions that King Daeron had four sons, three of whom had sons of their own.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Rhaegel was one of these three sons who had sons of their own.(wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)
