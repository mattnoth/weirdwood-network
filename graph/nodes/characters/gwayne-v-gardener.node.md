## Origins

Whereas his father converted to the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), Gwayne V was the first Gardener born into the new Faith, as well as the first to be made a [knight](wiki:Knight).(wiki:Gwayne_V_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Like his father, he took an Andal maiden to wife as a means of binding the bride's father to the realm.(wiki:Gwayne_V_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) He also took Andals into his service as retainers and household knights, among them Ser [Alester Tyrell](wiki:Alester_Tyrell), whose prowess at arms was such that he was made the king's champion and [sworn shield](wiki:Sworn_shield).(wiki:Gwayne_V_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Gwayne bequeathed lands and wives and lordships for the more powerful of the Andal kings and other Andal adventurers descending on the Reach in exchange for pledges of fealty.(wiki:Gwayne_V_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Like his grandfather and father, Gwayne V likewise sought after Andal craftsmen, especially blacksmiths and stonemasons, to settle throughout the Reach, supplying him and his bannermen with iron instead of bronze and strengthening their castles and holdfasts with Andal masonry.(wiki:Gwayne_V_Gardener.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_andals_in_the_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
