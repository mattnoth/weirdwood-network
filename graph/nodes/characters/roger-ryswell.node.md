## Appearances & Description

Roger's look resembles that of his brother [Rickard](wiki:Rickard_Ryswell), to the point [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) struggles to tell them apart.(wiki:Roger_Ryswell.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) He clasps his cloak with an [iron](wiki:Iron) [horsehead](wiki:Horse) brooch.(wiki:Roger_Ryswell.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Roger and his brothers Rickard and [Roose](wiki:Rickard_Ryswell) are said to be quarrelsome. They each take a horsehead of a different color for their sigil, the jape being that the [Ryswells](wiki:House_Ryswell) cannot even agree upon the color of their arms.(wiki:Roger_Ryswell.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

[Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) sees a Ryswell lead a [column](wiki:Column) of riders to the camp of [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) at the [siege of Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin), but is unable to tell the difference between Roger and his brother [Rickard](wiki:Rickard_Ryswell).(wiki:Roger_Ryswell.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Roger later joins his father and brothers in appending his signature on a letter written by Ramsay, declaring the fall of Moat Cailin.(wiki:Roger_Ryswell.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Along with the rest of [House Ryswell](wiki:House_Ryswell), Roger attends [the wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Ramsay_Bolton_and_Arya_Stark) of Ramsay to "[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark)"—actually [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)—in [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), hoping to lure [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) to the castle.(wiki:Roger_Ryswell.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Theon spots Rickard nuzzling at the neck of one of [Abel](wiki:Abel)'s washerwomen—supposedly [Frenya](wiki:Frenya)—near the Burned Tower of Winterfell (however, as mentioned before, it is possible Theon mistakes Roger for Rickard).(wiki:Roger_Ryswell.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

When one of his men-at-arms is found dead at Winterfell's inner wall, Roger calls the groom a drunk and suspects he slipped and fell off Winterfell's walls urinating. When Ser [Hosteen Frey](wiki:Hosteen_Frey) threatens Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly), Roger and his sister [Barbrey](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin) calm six [knights](wiki:Knight) from [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) who rose in their lord's defense. Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) later discusses the murders at Winterfell with Roger, Barbrey, and Ser [Aenys Frey](wiki:Aenys_Frey). Aenys accuses Theon, though Roger is skeptical and declares that Theon is not the killer. He also dismisses the suggestion of Lord Manderly as the killer, instead believing that Stannis has a man inside Winterfell. Roger and Barbrey remind Aenys that the Ryswells and [Dustins](wiki:House_Dustin) both lost men at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding) orchestrated by [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey).(wiki:Roger_Ryswell.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

When Stannis's vanguard reaches Winterfell, Roger accompanies Lord Bolton in the [Great Hall](wiki:Great_Hall_of_Winterfell) to break their fast. Roger later laughs at a jape from Lord [Harwood Stout](wiki:Harwood_Stout).(wiki:Roger_Ryswell.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
