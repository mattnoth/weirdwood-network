## Appearances & Description

The large Garth is bald and taciturn. He always glowers and wears the same greasy leather jerkin.(wiki:Garth_(Wolf's_Den).cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

Garth refers to [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) as a dead man during his imprisonment, constantly serving as a reminder to Davos that his life hangs in the balance now that Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) commanded that his head and hands be cut off.(wiki:Garth_(Wolf's_Den).cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Garth introduces "his ladies" to Davos. The [Whore](wiki:Whore_(weapon)) is a rod of cold black iron, while [Lady Lu](wiki:Lady_Lu) is a large sharp axe. The other keepers tell him that Garth spends his days honing Lady Lu. Davos resolves to go to his death as a [knight](wiki:Knight), asking only that they take his head before his hands.(wiki:Garth_(Wolf's_Den).cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Fortunately for Davos, the command to execute him is a sham; [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) eventually appears at his door and politely asks him to follow him.(wiki:Garth_(Wolf's_Den).cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
