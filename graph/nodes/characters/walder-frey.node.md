## Origins

As a toddler Walder was present at the [wedding](wiki:Wedding) of [his elder sister](wiki:Lady_Frey_(wife_of_Ambrose_Butterwell)) to Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell) at [Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-17) It is said he caused the marriage when he discovered his sister having intimate relations with a servant. Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) thought that Walder was an extremely annoying child, and was sorely tempted to throw him down a well.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

When Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) acquiesced to Walder's proposal to marry his second son, [Emmon](wiki:Emmon_Frey), to Tytos's daughter [Genna](wiki:Genna_Lannister), the ten-year-old [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) spoke out against the match.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

During [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Walder and his levies arrived at the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) after the rebels had already won. [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully), the [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident), has since called his [bannerman](wiki:Bannerman) "the Late Lord Frey",(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7) a name Walder despises.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rawoiafwalder_frey_.28late_lord.29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) refused to [foster](wiki:Foster) Walder's grandsons at court in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and his own son, [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn), at the [Twins](wiki:Twins).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8) Lord Hoster Tully refused a Frey bride for his son [Edmure](wiki:Edmure_Tully), and Hoster did not attend Walder's wedding to his seventh wife, [Annara Farring](wiki:Annara_Farring).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Walder Frey](wiki:Category:Images_of_Walder_Frey)*

As a young child, Walder was chinless and snot-nosed, with a high shrill hiccup of a laugh.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

By the age of ninety, Walder has turned bald, and his head has spotted with age.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) He looks a little like a vulture, but mostly like a weasel.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Many of his descendants have inherited his weasel-like look.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Walder's shoulders are scrawny and his long neck pink. He has a receding chin, under which loose skin dangles. He has runny and clouded eyes, and a toothless mouth, which constantly moves.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Walder is troubled with gout, and can no longer stand without assistance.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

> His bald head, spotted with age, thrust out from his scrawny shoulders on a long pink neck. Loose skin dangled beneath his receding chin, his eyes were runny and clouded, and his toothless mouth moved constantly, sucking at the empty air as a babe sucks at his mother's breast.
>
> — Catelyn's POV (Walder at ninety-one), ASOS Catelyn VI (`sources/chapters/asos/asos-catelyn-06.md:85`)

Walder has always been irascible, sharp of tongue, and blunt of manner, and this has only increased with age.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) He is a cautious man,(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) but also an ambitious one, and is known to only give aid to a winning cause.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) He is peevish,(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) ill-tempered, envious, and prideful,(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) with a long memory,(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) and can brood long about every slight he has ever suffered.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Walder has a prickly manner, and can take insult in the smallest things.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) He is considered untrustworthy by his liege lord, [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

At the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn), a [Frey](wiki:House_Frey) captain tells [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) that Lord Walder is to remarry on his ninetieth [name day](wiki:Name_day).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) After Catelyn abducts [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister),(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) the Frey contingent at the crossroads report back to Walder.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

Catelyn's ailing father, Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully), declines to attend [Walder's wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Walder_Frey_and_Joyeuse_Erenford) to [Joyeuse Erenford](wiki:Joyeuse_Erenford).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-10)

Walder is slow in answering [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully)'s summons when Ser [Edmure](wiki:Edmure_Tully) calls the banners. Aside from skirmishing with Ser [Addam Marbrand](wiki:Addam_Marbrand)'s [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) outriders, the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) mass their strength within the [Twins](wiki:Twins).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-11) Edmure is defeated by Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) at [Riverrun](wiki:Battle_under_the_walls_of_Riverrun)(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) while Walder gathers his forces.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-12)

Because the use of the Twins becomes a strategic necessity for the host of Catelyn's son, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), Walder negotiates marriage contracts with Catelyn. Robb is to marry one of Walder's daughters once there is peace and his sister, [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark), is betrothed to Walder's youngest son, [Elmar](wiki:Elmar_Frey). In addition, two of Lord Frey's grandsons, [Little Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)) and [Big Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)), are sent to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) as wards and one of his sons, [Olyvar](wiki:Olyvar_Frey), is made squire to Robb.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-13)

### A Clash of Kings

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Walder's son and heir, Ser [Stevron Frey](wiki:Stevron_Frey), dies after the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross), having fought bravely for Robb Stark, the new [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) and [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) marries one of Walder's granddaughters, [Walda Frey](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Merrett)),(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) in [a ceremony at the Twins](wiki:Wedding_of_Roose_Bolton_and_Walda_Frey).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Walder had promised Roose to pay the bride's weight in [silver](wiki:Silver) for her [dowry](wiki:Dowry), so Roose chose the fattest one.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) Walder mocks his son [Merrett](wiki:Merrett_Frey) for Walda's weight.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Word eventually reaches the [Twins](wiki:Twins) of Robb Stark's marriage to [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling) instead of a Frey as promised. Ser [Ryman Frey](wiki:Ryman_Frey), Lord Walder's new heir, withdraws House Frey's support from the king in outrage.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Walder sends his son [Lame Lothar](wiki:Lothar_Frey) to [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) to negotiate a rapprochement. Robb and many of his key bannermen return to the Twins under [guest right](wiki:Guest_right) to make amends and marry Lord [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) to Walder's daughter, [Roslin](wiki:Roslin_Frey). During the wedding feast, however, Frey and [Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) men ambush Robb and his entire host, slaughtering King Robb, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), many of their bannermen, and a significant portion of the northern army.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

Walder had secretly colluded with Lords [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) and Roose Bolton to betray the Starks and Tullys in what becomes known as the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Walder swears loyalty to King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), with Walder's second son [Emmon](wiki:Emmon_Frey) receiving Riverrun and its lands as reward.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

According to one of his sons, [Merrett](wiki:Merrett_Frey), Lord Walder is not long for the world and when he dies it will be every other Frey for himself. Walder, though a tyrant, believes in taking care of his own, even the ones who disappoint him. When [Stevron](wiki:Stevron_Frey) was heir it was different, as he was raised by Walder to believe that blood was blood, but his sons and grandsons, now the new heirs, do not feel the same way. Merrett and [Petyr Frey](wiki:Petyr_Frey) are hanged by outlaws, the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners), at [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Walder's new wife, Joyeuse, is pregnant.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

Ser [Daven Lannister](wiki:Daven_Lannister) is promised to a Frey according to an agreement negotiated by Lord Tywin before his death.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) Ser [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister) is betrothed to Walder's granddaughter, [Amerei](wiki:Amerei_Frey).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

Walder sends a [raven](wiki:Raven) to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) putting forth several claims to land. Because the Red Wedding is reviled by [sparrows](wiki:Sparrows), the [small council](wiki:Small_council) of Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) suggests that Walder's eventual successor could name the culprits and thereby rid himself of rivals.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

The [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)) at the [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle) says that Lord Frey is hunting outlaws from the Twins.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

While returning to the Twins from the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), Walder's new heir, [Ryman](wiki:Ryman_Frey), is hanged, presumably by the brotherhood without banners. Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) tasks [Walder Rivers](wiki:Walder_Rivers) with informing Lord Walder that King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) requires all captives taken by the Freys during the Red Wedding.(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Walder agrees to a marriage pact with Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly), betrothing two of Walder's grandsons, [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey) and [Little Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)), to Wyman's granddaughters, [Wynafryd](wiki:Wynafryd_Manderly) and [Wylla](wiki:Wylla_Manderly).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

Walder sends at least 1,400 Frey soldiers under the command of two sons, Ser [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_Frey) and Ser [Hosteen](wiki:Hosteen_Frey), with Lord Bolton when the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North) returns to the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) Rhaegar and two of Walder's sons, [Jared](wiki:Jared_Frey) and [Symond](wiki:Symond_Frey), [vanish](wiki:Frey_Pies/Theories) after leaving [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor). Little Walder, is found slain in [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Walder_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)
