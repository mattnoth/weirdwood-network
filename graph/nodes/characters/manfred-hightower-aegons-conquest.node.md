## Origins

When news arrived in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) of the [landing](wiki:Aegon's_Landing) of [Aegon the Dragon](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and his sisters, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) and [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen), the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon's_Conquest)) fasted and prayed for seven days and nights under the dome of the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept). When he emerged he announced that the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) would not oppose the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen), because the [Crone](wiki:Crone_(the_Seven)) had shown him that to do so would mean the destruction of Oldtown in [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame).(wiki:Manfred_Hightower_(Aegon's_Conquest).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Lord Manfred, who was a godly man, heeded the High Septon's prophecy when he heard it. He kept his forces at Oldtown and when Aegon marched south towards the city, he freely opened his gates submitting to Aegon's authority.(wiki:Manfred_Hightower_(Aegon's_Conquest).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:Manfred_Hightower_(Aegon's_Conquest).cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Some say that Manfred also offered his youngest daughter's hand in marriage to [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), which Aegon declined so as not to offend his queens.(wiki:Manfred_Hightower_(Aegon's_Conquest).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

Lord Manfred died during the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), soon after the death of his son [Garmon](wiki:Garmon_Hightower) in [10 AC](wiki:10_AC), and was succeeded by another son, Ser [Addam Hightower](wiki:Addam_Hightower).(wiki:Manfred_Hightower_(Aegon's_Conquest).cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)
