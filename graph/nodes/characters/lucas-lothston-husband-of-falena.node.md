## Origins

Ser Lucas was the grandson of a [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight). In [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), he was appointed the new [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms) of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) by Lord [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), replacing the harsh and disgraced Ser [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long). The patient Lucas became a favorite of Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), and the knight won grudging respect from the prince's solemn elder brother, King [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen).(wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena).cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

In [149 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [Falena Stokeworth](wiki:Falena_Stokeworth) became the first [mistress](wiki:Mistress) of Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), making him "a man" when the prince was fourteen. When a [knight](wiki:Knight) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) found them abed together in [151 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), his father, Prince Viserys, desired to remove Falena from court at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Viserys arranged for Falena to marry Lucas, and convinced King Aegon III to gift Lucas with [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), as the previous holders of the castle, [House Strong](wiki:House_Strong), had died out as a result of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)(wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) House Lothston became the sixth noble house to rule as [Lords of Harrenhal](wiki:Lords_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Falena gave birth to a daughter, [Jeyne Lothston](wiki:Jeyne_Lothston), in [164 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Although Prince Aegon did not have any acknowledged children with Falena, some suspected Jeyne's father was the prince, not Lucas.(wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

In [172 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Prince Aegon succeeded to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) as King Aegon IV. Falena brought her fourteen-year-old daughter to court in [178 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). The king took Jeyne as his eighth mistress, and named Lord Lucas as Hand of the King. It was rumored but not proven that King Aegon enjoyed mother and daughter together in the same bed.(wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) This possibly resulted in Lucas receiving the derogatory title "The Pander".(wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena).cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena).cite_ref-pander_2-1) However, Aegon soon gave Jeyne a [pox](wiki:Pox), and Lucas was banished from court along with his wife and daughter, having served less than a year as Hand.(wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)(wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)
