## Identity

Lady Joanna Lannister was a member of House Lannister who became Lady of Casterly Rock, wife of Lord Tywin Lannister, and mother of Cersei, Jaime, and Tyrion Lannister (wiki:Joanna_Lannister). She died giving birth to Tyrion in 273 AC.

## Origins

Born 244–252 AC at Casterly Rock in the westerlands (track_b: Born). Father: Ser Jason Lannister, younger brother of Tytos Lannister (track_b: Father). Mother: Marla Prester (track_b: Mother). Joanna had one older sibling, Damon (a son from Jason's first marriage), five younger siblings (including Stafford), and an older bastard sister Lynora (wiki:Joanna_Lannister).

## Narrative Arc

Tywin met his cousin Joanna at Casterly Rock in childhood (wiki:Joanna_Lannister). In 259 AC, Joanna went to King's Landing for the coronation of Jaehaerys II Targaryen and remained as a lady-in-waiting for Princess Rhaella. There were rumors she gave her maidenhead to Prince Aerys and briefly became his paramour after his ascension (wiki:Joanna_Lannister). Joanna was dismissed from Rhaella's service in 263 AC due to "ichertain liberties" taken during Aerys's bedding ceremony. She returned to Casterly Rock and married Tywin in a lavish ceremony that year — the last event where Aerys was known to be happy (wiki:Joanna_Lannister). In 266 AC, she gave birth to twins Cersei and Jaime. Aerys made a crude remark about her nursing at court, which drove Tywin to attempt resigning as Hand. Joanna returned to Casterly Rock and rarely visited the capital again. She died in 273 AC giving birth to Tyrion, who was born a dwarf. Tywin was devastated by her loss and never remarried (wiki:Joanna_Lannister).
