## Origins

### Early life

Joanna's father, Ser [Jason](wiki:Jason_Lannister_(son_of_Gerold)), was the younger brother of [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister), [Lord of Casterly Rock](wiki:Lord_of_Casterly_Rock). She had one older sibling, [Damon](wiki:Damon_Lannister_(son_of_Jason)), a son from her father's first marriage, and five younger siblings (three brothers, two sisters), the eldest of whom was [Stafford](wiki:Stafford_Lannister). She also had an older bastard sister, [Lynora](wiki:Lynora_Hill).(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_lannister_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the eldest son of Lord Tytos, met his cousin Joanna at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) while they were children.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Joanna went to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in [259 AC](wiki:259_AC), for the coronation of King [Jaehaerys II Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen), and remained as a [lady-in-waiting](wiki:Lady-in-waiting) for the future queen, Princess [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen). There were rumors that Joanna gave her [maidenhead](wiki:Maidenhead) to Prince [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) the night of Jaehaerys's coronation, and that she briefly became Aerys's [paramour](wiki:Paramour) after he ascended the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). However, Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) insists these tales are baseless, as Tywin would not have married Joanna if they were true, "for he was ever a proud man and not one accustomed to feasting on another man's leavings."(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

### Married life

Joanna married her first cousin, [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), now serving as Aerys's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), in [263 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) in a lavish ceremony in the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor).(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) On their wedding day, the oft-grim Tywin smiled, a rare occurrence.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) At the wedding feast, Aerys drunkenly japed about how it was a pity the [first night](wiki:First_night) tradition was banned, and he took certain "unwonted liberties" with Joanna in the bedding ritual when the men at the feast had to disrobe the bride.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

Not long thereafter, Joanna was dismissed by Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) from her service in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). While Rhaella turned a blind eye towards most of her husband's infidelities, she did not approve when it concerned one of her own ladies, several of whom were sent away from court. Joanna departed for [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) at once, and she seldom visited the capital afterwards.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

Joanna's marriage to Tywin was reportedly a happy one, and she became Tywin's trusted counselor and companion. Her influence on her husband was such that people say that Tywin ruled the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) as Hand,(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) "but was ruled at home by his lady wife."(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

In [266 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Joanna gave birth to [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) and [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), with Tywin present. King Aerys ordered Tywin to bring Joanna and the twins to King's Landing when the children were old enough to travel, but Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) died before this could occur. Instead, Tywin, Aerys, Prince [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), and half the court went to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) in [267 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), where they remained for most of the next year.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

Joanna attended the [Anniversary Tourney](wiki:Anniversary_Tourney) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in [272 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), held to celebrate Aerys's tenth year on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Aerys insulted Joanna by asking her if nursing her children had ruined her breasts, and the king refused the outraged Tywin's resignation the next day.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

At some point within the next year, Joanna's servant caught Jaime and Cersei together, engaged in some kind of sexual activity. Joanna sent the maid away, moved Jaime's chamber to the other side of Casterly Rock, and put a guard outside of Cersei's chamber. She then warned her twins that they must never do such a thing again, or else she would be forced to tell their father.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### Death

During her time as Queen Rhaella's lady-in-waiting, Joanna had befriended the [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne_(mother_of_Doran)), another member of the court.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) The Dornishwomen had two children, [Oberyn](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) and [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), who were not yet promised in marriage, so the two women planned to have their children be wed to each other.

In [273 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Joanna died birthing her youngest son, the dwarf [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), while the Martells were on their way to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock). Once confronted on the subject, Tywin bluntly refused all the offers (Jaime wed to Elia, Cersei wed to Oberyn, or both), and instead offered newborn Tyrion for Elia, an offer meant to be insulting.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

Tywin seldom spoke of his wife, but he was greatly saddened by her death(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) and never remarried. Tywin has since blamed Tyrion for Joanna's death.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) A brother, [Kevan](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), later took over Joanna's role as Tywin's most trusted counselor.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) drapes [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) with a wife's cloak worn first by Joanna in her wedding to [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) and then by [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) in her wedding to [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### A Feast for Crows

One of [Cersei's dromonds](wiki:Cersei's_dromonds) is named *[Lady Joanna](wiki:Lady_Joanna)* to honor the queen's mother.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

During the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) dreams and sees a woman he barely recognizes. At first, he believes her to be his sister, Cersei, but then realizes it is his mother. She asks if he will forget his father like he has forgotten her. She talks about the future that Tywin had wanted for his children: his son a [knight](wiki:Knight) and his daughter a queen. She cries when Jaime replies that they have indeed become that.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) tells [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) of the desire of her father, King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), for Joanna and the liberties he took with her at her wedding.(wiki:Joanna_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)
