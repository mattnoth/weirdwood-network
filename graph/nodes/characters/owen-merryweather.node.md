## Origins

During a feast King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) held when [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) came to court for the first time, Lord Owen talked about raising taxes on [wine](wiki:Wine), which led to Lord [Rykker](wiki:House_Rykker) joking about Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) shitting gold.(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Ser [Bonifer Hasty](wiki:Bonifer_Hasty) once served Lord Merryweather.(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

When Tywin resigned his position as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) in [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), the aged Owen was named the new Hand by Aerys.(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) With Tywin gone from court, the new focus of Aerys's mistrust and paranoia was his own son and heir, [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone). Lord Merryweather and Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) attempted to keep peace between the factions loyal to the king and those loyal to Rhaegar.(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Owen proved ineffectual in preventing the uprising that came to be known as [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). When Lords [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), and [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) began their rebellion, Owen sent missives to the lords of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) declaring them [outlaws](wiki:Outlaws) and demanding their heads. Some of the [stormlords](wiki:Stormlords) were encouraged by Owen, but they were defeated by Robert at [Summerhall](wiki:Battles_at_Summerhall).(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Owen never stirred from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), and this coupled with the news of Ned and Robert's successful return to their respective seats of power made the paranoid Aerys believe that Lord Merryweather was conspiring with them. Owen was subsequently stripped of lands and titles and exiled for his failure, and was replaced as Hand by the younger [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), Lord of [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost).(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

King Robert I Baratheon eventually restored the Merryweather titles and some of their lands to Owen's grandson, [Orton](wiki:Orton_Merryweather).(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

## Appearances & Description

Owen was considered amiable but not capable.(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) He was famous for lavishing praise upon King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) and laughing at all of the Mad King's jokes.(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Because of the sigil of House Merryweather, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) thought of Owen as "the horn-of-plenty Hand".(wiki:Owen_Merryweather.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
