## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Devan Seaworth](wiki:Category:Images_of_Devan_Seaworth)*

Devan is a skinny boy,(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) with a square plain face, forthright brown eyes, and thin brown flyaway hair.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) He looks very much like his eldest brother [Dale](wiki:Dale_Seaworth) did at that age. Devan's cheeks and chin are covered with a fuzz of blond hair, and though the beard "would have shamed a proper peach" (according to Devan's father [Davos](wiki:Davos_Seaworth)), Devan is fiercely proud of it.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Devan takes great pride in being a [squire](wiki:Squire) of King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) As a royal squire, Devan dresses in a squire's raiment, a cream-colored doublet with Stannis's sigil sewn on the breast. Davos feels Devan looks splendid in his raiment.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Devan also wears Baratheon gold and black, with the flaming heart of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) sewn above his own.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Devan is called brave by [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan)(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) and described as "a good boy" by Stannis Baratheon, who claims the boy is much like his father.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) considers him to be "quick and smart and able".(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) He is a diligent boy, who has learned to read and write.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) According to the [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), Devan is showing promise with sword and lance.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) Davos is sure Devan will be [knighted](wiki:Knight) in the future.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

After Stannis converts to the [Lord of Light](wiki:Lord_of_Light), Devan does the same,(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) and becomes a devout follower.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Melisandre believes that he fears her, but that he is half in love with her too.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Devan is present during [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre)'s burning of the [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) [sept](wiki:Sept)'s statues of the [Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). He equips King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) with a padded glove so that he may draw the sword [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer) from the [Mother](wiki:Mother_(the_Seven))'s breast. After the ceremony, Devan and his fellow squire [Bryen Farring](wiki:Bryen_Farring) collect Lightbringer. Davos tells his sons [Dale](wiki:Dale_Seaworth) and [Allard](wiki:Allard_Seaworth) that Devan handled himself well.{Ref|ACOK|10}}

Devan tries to wake Stannis [the morning he is to battle](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)) his brother [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), but cannot. He sees Stannis thrash and cry out, dreaming.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Devan attends Stannis as his squire when he attacks [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater). According to [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan), Devan bravely stays by Stannis's side throughout the battle. When the battle turns against Stannis and he retreats, Devan is one of the Baratheon men taken away by Saan's [Lyseni](wiki:Lys) galleys.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

### A Storm of Swords

Davos, a survivor of the Battle of the Blackwater, remembers his four sons who died during the battle, and worries that Devan may have been killed as well.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Davos asks [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan) if Dale and Allard had managed to escape the destruction of the battle. Salladhor tells him no, but comforts him with the news that Devan is safe, and reports on his bravery.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

At [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), Devan continues his lessons with [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Pylos](wiki:Pylos), alongside Princess [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon) and [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm), the [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son of the late King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

Davos finds himself thinking about Marya and their two young sons almost every day, and part of him wishes he could take Devan and go home.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) He thinks if he survives telling Stannis he has helped [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm) escape, he will return to [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath) with Devan, raise his living sons to be good men, and speak no more of kings.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

When Stannis Baratheon goes north to the [Wall](wiki:Wall), Devan, as his squire, accompanies him and is part of his retinue at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black).(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

In [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), Davos thinks that when the war is over and Stannis sits the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and has no more need of him, he will take his sons Devan, [Steff](wiki:Steffon_Seaworth), and [Stanny](wiki:Stannis_Seaworth), and travel east to see the wonders of the world.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

At Castle Black, Devan attends King Stannis as his squire.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) He attempts to summon [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) to the king's chambers, but when [Ghost](wiki:Ghost) sniffs him, he freezes in fear until Jon commands the [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf) to leave him alone.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

While imprisoned in the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den), believing he is to be executed, Davos writes letters to his wife and three surviving sons. To Devan, he writes how proud he was to see him as a king's squire, reminds him it's his duty as the eldest son to protect his mother and younger brothers. He asks Devan to tell Stannis that Davos did his best, and is sorry he had failed him.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

When Stannis marches south to face the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton), Devan's fellow squire [Bryen Farring](wiki:Bryen_Farring) travels with him. However, Devan remains at Castle Black to attend Melisandre. Devan is unhappy to be left behind, and Melisandre thinks the boy may believe he is being punished for some mistake he or his father may have made. In truth, Melisandre requested that Stannis leave Devan with her because it would be safer, and she does not wish for Davos to lose any more sons.(wiki:Devan_Seaworth.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)
