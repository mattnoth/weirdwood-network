## Origins

In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), Ser Byron Swann was among the two dozen [knights](wiki:Knight) present while Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) and Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) haggled over the betrothal of the prince to one of Lord Baratheon's daughters. Byron witnessed Prince [Lucerys Velaryon](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon)'s arrival, and the confrontation between the two princes. Shortly after, both princes left, resulting in the [fight above Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay) and the beginning of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Byron_Swann.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

In [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), about the same time of the [Butcher's Ball](wiki:Butcher's_Ball), Byron and his [squire](wiki:Squire) set out to slay a [dragon](wiki:Dragon). The knight tried to accomplish the feat by sneaking up upon the dragon, while hiding behind his silvered shield so the beast would see only its reflection, as [Serwyn of the Mirror Shield](wiki:Serwyn_of_the_Mirror_Shield) legendarily did to the dragon [Urrax](wiki:Urrax).(wiki:Byron_Swann.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)(wiki:Byron_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

The identity of the dragon is disputed. The court [fool](wiki:Fool) [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom), who was with Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), asserted in his *[Testimony](wiki:The_Testimony_of_Mushroom)* that Byron had attempted to kill Rhaenyra's dragon [Syrax](wiki:Syrax);(wiki:Byron_Swann.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) the same thing was written in an unspecified text, presumably a letter, by Ser Byron's squire.(wiki:Byron_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Byron_Swann.cite_ref-4) However, [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) stated in his book *[The Dance of the Dragons, A True Telling](wiki:The_Dance_of_the_Dragons,_A_True_Telling)*, that it was Prince Aemond's dragon [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) who Byron tried to kill, in an effort to end the prince's raids across the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands). Nevertheless, this is unlikely since [House Swann](wiki:House_Swann) supported the prince's brother, King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), and the [greens](wiki:Greens). Munkun largely drew his information from Grand Maester [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle), who was imprisoned at the time.(wiki:Byron_Swann.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)(wiki:Byron_Swann.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) Another chronicler, [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Eustace](wiki:Eustace), claimed that the knight tried to slay [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre), the dragon of King Aegon II, though this is certainly a mistake, as Sunfyre's whereabouts were unknown at the time.(wiki:Byron_Swann.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

All accounts agree that the dragon stirred upon the knight's approach and unleashed its fire, burning through the mirror shield and roasting Ser Byron Swann, who died screaming.(wiki:Byron_Swann.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)
