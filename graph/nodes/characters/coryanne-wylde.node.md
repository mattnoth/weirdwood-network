## Origins

Coryanne Wylde was the daughter of Lord [Morgan Wylde](wiki:Morgan_Wylde) of the [Rain House](wiki:Rain_House). At the age of thirteen, she was [deflowered](wiki:Maiden) by a "[surly lad](wiki:Surly_lad)" from the stables. According to *A Caution for Young Girls*, her lover was a handsome boy her own age, but according to the Rain House [maester](wiki:Maester), he was thirty years old and pox-scarred. Per both accounts, he was [gelded](wiki:Eunuch) and sent to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) shortly afterwards. Coryanne was confined to her chambers, where in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), she gave birth to a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son, who was soon sent away to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) to be [fostered](wiki:Fostered) by a castle steward and his wife. Few beyond the walls of the Rain House knew of her shame.(wiki:Coryanne_Wylde.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

In [50 AC](wiki:50_AC), Coryanne was summoned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). [Queen Regent](wiki:Queen_Regent) [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) was sending [septas and girls of noble birth](wiki:Wise_Women) to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) to be a household for her daughter, Princess [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), and Coryanne was chosen as a representative of the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands). When the [raven](wiki:Raven) came for Coryanne, her mother told her to let everyone believe she was a [maiden](wiki:Maiden), and to never speak of her child. However, the truth was that Queen Alyssa and the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon), were attempting to undo the secret [marriage](wiki:Marriage) of young King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and his sister-wife Alysanne, which at the time was unconsummated, and the ladies' mission was to try and persuade Alysanne to [annul](wiki:Marriage) the marriage. It is not certain why Coryanne was included, but *A Caution for Young Girls* states that Lord Rogar or his brother, Ser [Borys](wiki:Borys_Baratheon), placed Coryanne among these ladies with the instruction to bed King Jaehaerys, in order to anger Alysanne into undoing the marriage. If this was the case, the attempt failed.(wiki:Coryanne_Wylde.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

On the fifteenth day of the sixth moon of [50 AC](wiki:50_AC), Coryanne eloped with the married Ser [Howard Bullock](wiki:Howard_Bullock), the younger son of the [commander of Dragonstone's garrison](wiki:Merrell_Bullock). A fishing boat carried them to [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark), where they took ship to [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), and then to the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands), where Ser Howard joined [the Free Company](wiki:The_Free_Company). In [53 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), he died in [Myr](wiki:Myr) from a fall from his [horse](wiki:Horse) after a night of drinking, and Coryanne was left alone and penniless. The rest of Coryanne's life has not been recorded in history, but it is believed the tales she told to the [sellswords](wiki:Sellswords) of the Free Company and the [magisters](wiki:Magister) of [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) helped spread gossip [across the narrow sea](wiki:Across_the_narrow_sea) about the marriage of Jaehaerys and Alysanne.(wiki:Coryanne_Wylde.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)
