## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Murenmure](wiki:Category:Images_of_Murenmure)*

Murenmure is a slim man who wears his [maester](wiki:Maester)'s chain about his neck and has fine grey robes.(wiki:Murenmure.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Murenmure is knowledgeable about the [succession](wiki:Succession) [laws](wiki:Law) of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Murenmure.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

[Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), a [priest of the Drowned God](wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God), dislikes maesters and thinks of Murenmure as a [thrall](wiki:Thrall).(wiki:Murenmure.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

[Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) travels to [Hammerhorn](wiki:Hammerhorn) to speak about the death of his brother [Balon](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North), with [Gorold Goodbrother](wiki:Gorold_Goodbrother), [Lord of Hammerhorn](wiki:Lord_of_Hammerhorn). The Damphair demands that Murenmure not be present for their conversation, but Gorold insists that his [maester](wiki:Maester) remain. When Aeron decides to leave instead, Murenmure informs him that [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) has returned to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) and claimed the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair). Murenmure debates the rules of [succession](wiki:Succession) with Aeron, with the maester stating that [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) and [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) would be Balon's heirs as his children and Aeron considering Balon's other brothers Euron and [Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) instead.(wiki:Murenmure.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
