## Origins

Long after the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Luceon composed a sad ballad called “[Farewell, My Brother](wiki:Farewell,_My_Brother)”, still sung today. The song purports to relate the last meeting between Ser [Arryk](wiki:Arryk_Cargyll) and Ser [Erryk Cargyll](wiki:Erryk_Cargyll), twin brothers and [knights](wiki:Knights) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). During the Dance of the Dragons, Ser Arryk had sworn his sword to King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), but Ser Erryk sided with his rival, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen). Arryk was in the retinue of [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle) when he traveled from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) in an unsuccessful attempt to reconcile the rival [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen).

In the song, before Arryk boards the ship to return to King's Landing, the brothers try to persuade each other to change sides. When this attempt fails, they exchange declarations of love and part, knowing that when they meet next, it will be as enemies.
