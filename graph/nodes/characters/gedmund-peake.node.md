## Origins

When Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) became [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC) he made his uncle Gedmund commander of the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) with the more experienced [Ned Bean](wiki:Ned_Bean) as his second-in-command. During the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War), Gedmund and Ned were sent against [Racallio Ryndoon](wiki:Racallio_Ryndoon), the self-proclaimed [King of the Narrow Sea](wiki:King_of_the_Narrow_Sea). The commander became so seasick during the voyage, however, that sailors called him "Gedmund Green-Sick". When it was learned that Racallio had allied with the [Sealord of Braavos](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos) and the [Archon of Tyrosh](wiki:Archon_of_Tyrosh), Gedmund remained at [Tarth](wiki:Tarth) to await further instructions from Unwin. Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) protested they would lose time, however, and left Gedmund behind to continue south. The [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) ships in the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) were then defeated in [Lord Alyn's attack](wiki:Lord_Alyn's_attack), an action which outraged Unwin. The Hand recalled Gedmund's fleet from Tarth to defend the [Gullet](wiki:Gullet) and [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay) from Braavosi retaliation.(wiki:Gedmund_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

Gedmund remained in his post after his nephew resigned as Hand in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC).(wiki:Gedmund_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) After the attempted coup against the [Rogare family](wiki:House_Rogare) and the King's Hand, Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan) in [135 AC](wiki:135_AC), Gedmund was named [lord admiral](wiki:Lord_admiral) and [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships) to appease Lord Peake and his supporters.(wiki:Gedmund_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Gedmund_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
