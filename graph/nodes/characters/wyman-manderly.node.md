## Origins

Wyman loved to ride horses as a boy and he won some small acclaim in the lists when he was a young man. However, he has grown too big to ride.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

Wyman participated in the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). He granted the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den) to Ser [Bartimus](wiki:Bartimus) after the latter saved Wyman's life in the battle.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)

Lord Manderly has been a widower since about [291 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

## Appearances & Description

Wyman is nearly sixty years old, with a massive belly and fingers the size of sausages.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Lord Manderly has pale blue eyes(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) and a beard.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) He appears to have four chins.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Wyman is so fat he can no longer ride a [horse](wiki:Horse) and must be carried in a [litter](wiki:Litter), leading to the nickname *Lord Too-Fat-to-Sit-a-Horse*.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Wyman is amiable and has a loud, booming laugh.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) He is mocked by his own people as Lord Lamprey.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) By his own admission, and because of his physical appearance, Wyman is seen by many as craven and foolish. This is a clever front, however, as Wyman is shrewd, calculating, and intelligent. Lord Manderly is also staunchly loyal to [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Wyman wears rich clothing, including a velvet blue-green doublet embroidered with [golden](wiki:Gold) thread. A golden trident pins his mantle to his shoulder.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) A prodigious eater, he enjoys boiled eggs,(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) capons,(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) [eels](wiki:Eel),(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) [lampreys](wiki:Lamprey),(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) [pork pies](wiki:Frey_Pies/Theories),(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) porridge,(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) and sausages.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) Lord Manderly has a large cushioned throne in the [Merman's Court](wiki:Merman's_Court).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

While in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) asks his wife, Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), to instruct Lord Wyman to strengthen the defenses of [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) and keep them well garrisoned.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Wyman meets Catelyn when she comes ashore at White Harbor after her return from the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). He stays to command the defense of the city when [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) calls his banners. He sends his two sons, Ser [Wylis](wiki:Wylis_Manderly) and Ser [Wendel](wiki:Wendel_Manderly), in his absence.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

### A Clash of Kings

Wyman comes to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to partake in the [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast), bringing a retinue of [knights](wiki:Knight), retainers, and entertainers,(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) as well as twenty casks of seafood.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) To [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark), Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel), and Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin), Lord Manderly proposes to mint new coinage for Robb, the new [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), and a new war fleet for the [north](wiki:North). He also offers himself or Wendel as a possible new husband for the widowed Lady [Donella Hornwood](wiki:Donella_Hornwood).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) Despite his size, Wyman dances gracefully with [Beth Cassel](wiki:Beth_Cassel) during the feast.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

When [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), the Bastard of Bolton, kidnaps and forcibly marries Lady Hornwood, Wyman moves quickly to seize [Hornwood](wiki:Hornwood) and prevent [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) from having it.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

In response to [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy)'s [capture of Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell), Wyman sends a dozen barges packed with knights, warhorses, and siege engines up the [White Knife](wiki:White_Knife) to join Rodrik's army.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) However, these Stark loyalists are betrayed by Ramsay and the garrison of the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort) in the [battle at Winterfell](wiki:Battle_at_Winterfell).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### A Storm of Swords

While traveling in the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood), the crippled Bran recalls that during the harvest feast Lord Wyman looked at him with less pity than other northern lords. However, Bran does not know if White Harbor is safe from the Boltons, and he decides instead to journey [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall) to find the [three-eyed crow](wiki:Three-eyed_crow).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Rasos9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Wyman's heir, Ser [Wylis](wiki:Wylis_Manderly), is captured by Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) in the [fighting at the fords of the Trident](wiki:Fighting_at_the_fords_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) His second son, Ser [Wendel](wiki:Wendel_Manderly), is killed by crossbowmen at the [Twins](wiki:Twins) when King Robb is betrayed by [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### A Feast for Crows

King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon)'s [small council](wiki:Small_council) learns that Wyman has imprisoned Lord [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth), an envoy of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), and Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) orders Lord Manderly to execute the Onion Lord.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) It is later reported to Cersei that Wyman has had Davos executed. Members of [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) testify that they have seen his head, confirming Wyman's claim.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) In return, Cersei orders her brother, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), to release Wylis from captivity at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

[Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) sends a letter from [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) demanding fealty to him as king. Wyman responds with obfuscation, speaking of his age and infirmity. [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) informs Stannis that [Val](wiki:Val) would refuse to marry Wyman.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

[Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) comes to White Harbor to try raising men, but it is believed that Lord Manderly is refusing his pleas. Supported by the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey), [Symond](wiki:Symond_Frey), and [Jared](wiki:Jared_Frey) arrive in White Harbor on *[Lionstar](wiki:Lionstar)* as envoys and to return the bones of Ser [Wendel Manderly](wiki:Wendel_Manderly).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) In return for the freedom of his surviving son, Ser [Wylis Manderly](wiki:Wylis_Manderly), for whom he has to pay a ransom of three thousand [dragons](wiki:Currency), and peace with the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Wyman agrees to marry a daughter of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) and to marry his granddaughters [Wynafryd](wiki:Wynafryd_Manderly) and [Wylla](wiki:Wylla_Manderly) to Rhaegar and [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)), respectively. Wyman also agrees to relinquish his claim to [Hornwood](wiki:Hornwood).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

Lord [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) arrives to treat with Wyman on behalf of Stannis and is brought before the [Merman's Court](wiki:Merman's_Court). A large audience is present, with the three Freys and Wylis's family in attendance. A pallid Wyman listens to Jared claim that Wendel sacrificed his life to save Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) from [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), who had [turned into a wolf](wiki:Warg). When Rhaegar insults Robb, claiming he was a vile dog who died like one, and that [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) is the rightful king, Wyman agrees with him. Wylla speaks in favor of Davos, saying they should join Stannis for vengeance and that she will not marry a Frey, but Lord Wyman threatens to send her to the [silent sisters](wiki:Silent_sisters). After Wyman hears Davos's plea to join Stannis's cause, he orders his cousin, Ser [Marlon Manderly](wiki:Marlon_Manderly), to have Davos executed.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)

Instead of executing Davos, Wyman sends Davos to comfortable captivity in the [Wolf's Den](wiki:Wolf's_Den), a prison in White Harbor, and a criminal who resembles Davos is killed instead. The unnamed man's head and hands are removed and dipped in tar, and his fingers are shortened by Ser [Bartimus](wiki:Bartimus) so that the publicly-displayed remains look like Davos's. Once the Freys inform Cersei's small council that Lord Manderly ordered the execution of the Onion Lord, the Iron Throne returns Wylis from captivity at [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)

During the celebration feast welcoming Wylis home to White Harbor, Wyman meets secretly in the [New Castle](wiki:New_Castle) with Davos and Robett. He apologizes for the way in which Davos has been treated, tells him he agrees with Wylla about the debt White Harbor owes the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark), and explains he is acting cowardly to deceive the Lannisters and Freys. Wyman has been building warships at White Harbor and hiding them up the White Knife.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5) He claims there are spies around him, not trusting Maester [Theomore](wiki:Theomore), who was born a [Lannister of Lannisport](wiki:House_Lannister_of_Lannisport). Wyman learned from [Wex Pyke](wiki:Wex_Pyke), a survivor of the [sack of Winterfell](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell), that [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) was responsible for the massacre. Wyman promises Davos he will swear allegiance to Stannis if [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark) is retrieved from [Skagos](wiki:Skagos).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6)(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Rawoiafdavos_seaworth.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

Wyman leaves White Harbor to attend the wedding of Ramsay to "[Arya Stark](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)" with an armed escort. Before doing so, he gives his three Frey guests a palfrey each; guest gifts are given to guests when they leave their host's protection, meaning they are no longer under the protection of [guest right](wiki:Guest_right).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The Frey envoys disappear while en route to [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Wyman claims the Freys rode ahead of him, being that he cannot ride a horse and instead travels slowly by barge and litter. Wyman is distraught and the leaders of the Frey contingent, Ser [Hosteen](wiki:Hosteen_Frey) and Ser [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_Frey), sons of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), are distressed at their kinsmen's disappearance.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) [Big Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)) thinks the envoys were killed by Wyman.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), moves his son [Ramsay's wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Ramsay_Bolton_and_Arya_Stark) from Barrowton to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) Wyman brings a huge supply of food but no hostages, which unsettles Roose.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) Wyman provides food and drink for the wedding feast, during which the jolly lord calls for [Abel](wiki:Mance_Rayder) to sing "[The Night That Ended](wiki:The_Night_That_Ended)" and about [Danny Flint](wiki:Danny_Flint). He serves [three huge pies](wiki:Frey_Pies/Theories) himself, the first portions to Roose and [Walda Bolton](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Merrett)) and the next to Hosteen and Aenys, and Lord Manderly eats two slice from each pie himself.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) Roose does not eat or drink anything unless he sees Wyman taste it first. While being helped from the hall, a drunken Wyman tells Abel to sing of the [Rat Cook](wiki:Rat_Cook).(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3)

Among the snowmen that squires build along the battlements of Winterfell, one resembling Wyman is the fattest snowman [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) has ever seen. Lady [Barbrey Dustin](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin) does not believe that Roose would allow Lord Manderly to survive the upcoming battle with Stannis.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

When tensions arise from the blizzard, Aenys and Hosteen accuse Lord Manderly of being responsible for the disappearance of their kin, but Wyman pleads ignorance.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Aenys suspects the Manderlys are to blame after several men-at-arms in the castle are murdered, but Barbrey and Ser [Roger Ryswell](wiki:Roger_Ryswell) are skeptical.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) When Little Walder Frey is murdered, Hosteen accuses Wyman, who denies the charge yet at the same time insults House Frey, which causes an enraged Hosteen to attack Wyman. Lord Manderly's throat is nearly slit open but his knights intervene, and Wyman's wounds are treated by Maester [Medrick](wiki:Medrick). Roose orders Wyman to have his men march on Stannis from Winterfell's east gate.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

### The Winds of Winter

Having escaped from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and been brought to the [crofters' village](wiki:Crofters'_village), [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) tells [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) that [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) has sent the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) and [Manderlys](wiki:House_Manderly) to confront him. Stannis is angered at Wyman's support of the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton), but Theon does not believe Wyman would coordinate with the Freys against Stannis.(wiki:Wyman_Manderly.cite_ref-TheonI_26-0)
