## Origins

When his elder brother [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark_(son_of_Benjen)), [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell) and [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), died in [121 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Bennard ruled the [north](wiki:North) as [regent](wiki:Regent) during the minority of his lord nephew, [Cregan](wiki:Cregan_Stark). When Cregan came of age in [124 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Bennard was slow to surrender his power. Relations grew strained between Bennard and Cregan as the young Lord of Winterfell chafed under the limits imposed on him by his uncle.(wiki:Bennard_Stark.cite_ref-Rfaba_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Finally, in [126 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Cregan rose up against his uncle, and imprisoned Bennard and his sons, taking the rule of the north into his own hands.(wiki:Bennard_Stark.cite_ref-Rfaba_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
