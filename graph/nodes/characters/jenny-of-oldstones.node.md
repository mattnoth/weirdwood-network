## Origins

Jenny claimed descent from long-vanished kings of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men).(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) The ruined castle of [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones) was once the seat of [House Mudd](wiki:House_Mudd).(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

[Duncan Targaryen](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen), the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), met Jenny while traveling the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) in [239 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). The prince loved Jenny so much he married her against the wishes of his father, King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), breaking his [betrothal](wiki:Betrothal) to the daughter of [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End). King Aegon tried to have the marriage undone, but Duncan refused to give Jenny up, ultimately relinquishing his rights to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) for her, while the outraged Lyonel led [a short-lived rebellion](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon's_rebellion). Duncan became known as the Prince of Dragonflies, while his younger brother [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen) became Prince of Dragonstone and the new heir.(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Jenny was titled "Lady" by courtesy, and was eventually accepted at court. The smallfolk throughout the Seven Kingdoms cherished her, and she and Duncan were a favorite subject of singers for many years.(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Jenny was friends with a [dwarf woman](wiki:Ghost_of_High_Heart), a [woods witch](wiki:Woods_witch), whom she believed to be one of the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest). The woman, whom Jenny brought to court, [prophesied](wiki:Prophecy) that [the prince that was promised](wiki:The_prince_that_was_promised) would be born from the line of Prince [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) and Princess [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen). When Prince Jaehaerys heard the prophecy, he arranged a marriage between his two children.(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

King Aegon and Prince Duncan were killed in the [tragedy at Summerhall](wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall) in [259 AC](wiki:259_AC).(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rawoiafjaehaerys_ii_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) It is uncertain if Jenny died in the fire or survived; according to a song, Jenny danced with her ghosts in the hall of kings,(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) though this may be speaking of her life at Oldstones.

When [Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) was twelve, she camped at Oldstones while traveling to [Seagard](wiki:Seagard) with her father, Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully). Catelyn and [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), her father's [ward](wiki:Ward), pretended to be Jenny and Duncan, with Catelyn winding flowers in her hair.(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

## Appearances & Description

Jenny is said to have been a strange and lovely girl(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) who wore flowers in her hair.(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Local villagers considered the wild Jenny a half-mad peasant, with some calling her a [witch](wiki:Witch). She became popular with [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), however.(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

While at [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones), [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) recalls a song about Jenny.(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

### A Feast for Crows

Among other sad songs, [Marillion](wiki:Marillion) sings of Jenny and Prince [Duncan Targaryen](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen) while imprisoned at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

In [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) recalls how all three sons of Aegon V Targaryen had married for love, including Duncan and Jenny, and that this resulted in tragedy and death.(wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
