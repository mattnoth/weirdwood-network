## Origins

The [Kings of Yronwood](wiki:Kings_of_Yronwood) ruled half of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne),(wiki:Yorick_V_Yronwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_queer_customs_of_the_south.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) and Yorick was the richest and most powerful of the [Dornish](wiki:Dornish) kings at the start of [Nymeria's War](wiki:Nymeria's_War), when Lord [Mors Martell](wiki:Mors_Martell) and Princess [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria) warred to conquer Dorne. The Martells met and subdued all the petty kings of Dorne, until only Yorick V remained, the greatest of the Dornish kings.(wiki:Yorick_V_Yronwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

For nine years, the Yronwoods warred against the Martells and their supporters; Yorick's own [bannermen](wiki:Bannermen) included the [Jordaynes](wiki:House_Jordayne) and the [Wyls](wiki:House_Wyl), as well as the [Blackmonts](wiki:House_Blackmont) and [Qorgyles](wiki:House_Qorgyle). In the [Third Battle of the Boneway](wiki:Third_Battle_of_the_Boneway). Yorick V himself slew Mors and Princess Nymeria assumed command of the Martell armies. Two years of battle followed, though ultimately Yorick V was forced to submit and bend the knee to Nymeria, and the Yronwoods became bannermen of House Martell.(wiki:Yorick_V_Yronwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

After his defeat, Yorick was sent to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), the same fate that befell the five kings that submitted before him.(wiki:Yorick_V_Yronwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_the_coming_of_the_rhoynar.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
