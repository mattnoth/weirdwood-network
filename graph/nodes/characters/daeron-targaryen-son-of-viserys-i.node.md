## Identity

## Appearances & Description

Daeron had the coloring of [the blood of the dragon](wiki:House_Targaryen). A handsome boy, Daeron was the most popular of the three sons of King Viserys I Targaryen and Queen Alicent Hightower. He was courteous and clever, and was described as "the gentlest" of his brothers.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Daeron grew up in the shadow of his brothers, and therefore became more accustomed to following orders than giving them.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
Daeron had a splendid [silken](wiki:Silk) pavilion.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

## Origins

### Early life[ ]

Late in [114 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Daeron was born to King Viserys I Targaryen and his second wife, Queen Alicent Hightower. He had two elder brothers, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and [Aemond](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), and an older sister, [Helaena](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen), as well as an older half-sister, [Rhaenyra](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen). Daeron was born only shortly after Rhaenyra's eldest son, Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon). However, Rhaenyra did not get along with Daeron's mother, Alicent. Trying to prevent a similar enmity between his youngest son and eldest grandson, Viserys had the two children share a [wet nurse](wiki:Wet_nurse) until they were weaned, hoping that them being [milk brothers](wiki:Milk_brother) would make a difference.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
The king's hope was in vain, because just as his older siblings, Daeron grew up to be a bitter rival to Jacaerys and his younger siblings, [Lucerys](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon) and [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon). While Daeron and his older siblings were forced to spend time with their cousins (i.e. attending the same feasts, and occasionally training at arms or studying with a [maester](wiki:Maester) together), it is said that this only increased the enmity.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
By the age of six, Daeron had a dragon of his own, [Tessarion](wiki:Tessarion), though he had yet to ride her. When Daeron was twelve in [126 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), he was sent to serve as a [cupbearer](wiki:Cupbearer) and [squire](wiki:Squire) to Lord [Ormund Hightower](wiki:Ormund_Hightower), his mother Alicent's cousin, in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

### The Dance of the Dragons[ ]

In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), after the death of Daeron's father, King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) began as Daeron's older brother claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) as Aegon II Targaryen, despite the fact that their half-sister, Rhaenyra Targaryen, had been Viserys's declared heir. Daeron was still in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), squiring for Lord Ormund Hightower.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Daeron accompanied Lord Ormund Hightower's army as it marched from Oldtown. During the [Battle of the Honeywine](wiki:Battle_of_the_Honeywine), Ormund faced defeat until Daeron arrived on [Tessarion](wiki:Tessarion), saving his cousin and his army. For this act, Daeron was feasted by Ormund, who [knighted](wiki:Knight) him with the [Valyrian sword](wiki:Valyrian_sword) [Vigilance](wiki:Vigilance), dubbing the young prince "Daeron the Daring".(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
After King Aegon II was injured in the [battle at Rook's Rest](wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest), Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) took command. Aemond saw his uncle, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), Rhaenyra's husband, as the biggest threat to their rule, and was determined to defeat him in battle. Wanting sole glory for defeating Daemon and considering Daeron too young, Aemond did not ask Daeron for help when preparing to retake [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) from Daemon.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
After the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) to Rhaenyra, Daeron was considered the greatest threat to her rule. He remained with Lord Ormund's army, and scouted ahead for them on his dragon. The [Shield Islands](wiki:Shield_Islands), [House Oakheart](wiki:House_Oakheart), and [House Rowan](wiki:House_Rowan) submitted to the greens to avoid facing Daeron's Tessarion. Because his nephew, Prince [Maelor Targaryen](wiki:Maelor_Targaryen), had died at [Bitterbridge](wiki:Bitterbridge), Daeron refused to be merciful to Lady [Caswell](wiki:House_Caswell). Daeron had Tessarion burn the town during the [sack of Bitterbridge](wiki:Sack_of_Bitterbridge).(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
[Ulf White](wiki:Ulf_White) and [Hugh Hammer](wiki:Hugh_Hammer) were sent to [Tumbleton](wiki:Tumbleton) to defend the town against Daeron, but the [Two Betrayers](wiki:Two_Betrayers) defected to the greens during the [First Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:First_Battle_of_Tumbleton). Since Ormund had died during the battle, command of the greens' host was disputed by several nobles. Daeron ordered Ser [Hobert Hightower](wiki:Hobert_Hightower) to stop the sickening sack of Tumbleton, but Hobert was ineffective. Although Daeron named Ulf as Lord of Bitterbridge, the [Two Betrayers](wiki:Two_Betrayers) refused to join Daeron in an attack on [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) if their demands were not met.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
With Aegon II missing after the fall of King's Landing and with Aemond killed in the [Battle Above the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Above_the_Gods_Eye), Prince Daeron became the first in line for the throne. Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake), one of the leaders at Tumbleton, insisted Daeron should be proclaimed [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) immediately. Others, who believed Aegon II to be dead, wished Daeron to be crowned king. When Hugh declared that he should become king, Daeron threw [wine](wiki:Wine) in his face. Hugh was then seen wearing an iron crown. Offended by the arrogance of the Betrayers, the lords and knights of the Reach, later known as the [Caltrops](wiki:Caltrops), began to plot their deaths. While Daeron was not a member of the Caltrops, the men did not wish to proceed without his consent, which the prince gave swiftly.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
Two days later, the [Second Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:Second_Battle_of_Tumbleton) began. Prince Daeron died during the battle, though it is not known exactly how he died. The story most often told claims Daeron was cut down by [Black Trombo](wiki:Black_Trombo) with a morningstar whilst stumbling out of his tent with his clothes on fire, after the attacks of the dragon [Seasmoke](wiki:Seasmoke) had begun. Another claims the same, except that the prince was slain by a man-at-arms with a sword, who was unaware of the identity of his victim. The third account, agreed upon by [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) and [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), states that Daeron's burning tent collapsed on top of him.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

### Legacy[ ]

Prince Daeron's death is regarded as mysterious, and his body was never identified.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Because of this opportunity, several [pretenders](wiki:Daeron_Pretenders) were able to claim to be Daeron during the reign of King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). According to Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), it has been conclusively proven that these men were imposters.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
After briefly regaining the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) ordered the building of giant statues of his fallen brothers, [Aemond](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) and Daeron, in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister) halted the statues during the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), however.(wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I).cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
