## Origins

The eldest son of Prince Maron and Princess Daenerys was their heir,(wiki:Maron_Martell's_son.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) and he had multiple younger siblings.(wiki:Maron_Martell's_son.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) After observing the children at play in the [Water Gardens](wiki:Water_Gardens), his mother came to the realization that she could not tell the [high-born](wiki:Nobility) children from the [low-born](wiki:Smallfolk) and concluded that all the children were deserving of long life, love, and protection. Showing her son the children in the Water Gardens, Daenerys told him that they were his realm and he must remember them in everything that he did. He presumably succeeded his father as Prince of Dorne.(wiki:Maron_Martell's_son.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

His descendant, Prince [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), tells the tale of Daenerys's lesson for her son to his niece, [Obara Sand](wiki:Obara_Sand). He also tells her of how that lesson was passed through the generations of [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell), and that his [own mother](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne_(mother_of_Doran)) repeated the same words to him in his youth when he was old enough to leave the pools.(wiki:Maron_Martell's_son.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
