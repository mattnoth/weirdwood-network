## Identity

Tyrion Lannister is the third and youngest child of Lord Tywin Lannister and the late Lady Joanna Lannister. His older siblings are Queen Cersei and Ser Jaime. Tyrion is a dwarf, called the Imp and the Halfman. He is a major POV character across five books (wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).

## Origins

Born in 273 AC at Casterly Rock, the westerlands (track_b: Born). Son of Tywin Lannister and Joanna Lannister (track_b: Father, Mother). His mother died giving birth to him, for which his father never forgave him (wiki:Tyrion_Lannister.cite_ref-TWOIAF).

## Appearances & Description

Tyrion is a dwarf with stunted legs, stubby fingers, and a jutting forehead. He has mismatched eyes — one green, one black — and thin hair so blond it appears white. After the Battle of the Blackwater, he loses most of his nose to a sword slash (wiki:Tyrion_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot5). He is intelligent, well-educated, sharp of wit, and a ferocious reader. Despite being no warrior, he shows great bravery in battle and knows some tumbling tricks (wiki:Tyrion_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot5).

## Narrative Arc

At thirteen, Tyrion fell in love with Tysha, a common girl he and Jaime rescued from bandits. They married in secret, but Tywin forced Jaime to claim Tysha was a prostitute. Tywin had his guards rape Tysha, then forced Tyrion to do the same — a formative trauma (wiki:Tyrion_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot42).

Tyrion travels north with the royal party to Winterfell, then to the Wall, where he befriends Jon Snow. Returning south, he is arrested by Catelyn Stark and taken to the Eyrie, where he demands trial by combat and wins his freedom through the sellsword Bronn (wiki:Tyrion_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot42). He fights in the Battle on the Green Fork commanding the mountain clansmen (wiki:Tyrion_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot62).

As acting Hand of the King, Tyrion governs King's Landing, outmaneuvers Cersei politically, and fortifies the city against Stannis Baratheon. He commands the defense during the Battle of the Blackwater using wildfire, but is gravely wounded and receives no credit when his father Tywin arrives to win the battle (wiki:Tyrion_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok59).

Tyrion is forced to marry Sansa Stark but refuses to consummate the marriage. He serves as Master of coin. At Joffrey's wedding, the king is poisoned and Tyrion is accused of murder. After trial by combat fails when Oberyn Martell is killed by Gregor Clegane, Tyrion is sentenced to death. Jaime frees him and reveals the truth about Tysha. Tyrion murders Shae and his father Tywin with a crossbow before fleeing Westeros (wiki:Tyrion_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos70, cite_ref-Rasos77).

In exile, Tyrion travels with Illyrio Mopatis, is kidnapped by Jorah Mormont, sold into slavery, and eventually joins the Second Sons mercenary company near Meereen (wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).
