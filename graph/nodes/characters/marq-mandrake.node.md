## Appearances & Description

Marq has a [pox](wiki:Pox)-scarred face that has a hole in one cheek where a [slaver’s mark](wiki:Tattoo) had been burned away. He wears his worldly wealth upon his person, including a chain of golden skulls and a lord's ransom in [golden](wiki:Gold) arm rings. Each arm ring signifies one year's service with the Golden Company.(wiki:Marq_Mandrake.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Origins

Marq is a [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) exile and [knight](wiki:Knight) that escaped from [slavery](wiki:Slavery) at some point.(wiki:Marq_Mandrake.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) How he came to be enslaved, where he was a slave, and for how long, is not known. His surname Mandrake is of a house that once loomed large in the histories of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). He may or may not be of [House Mandrake](wiki:House_Mandrake), since in the [free companies](wiki:Free_companies) a man can call himself whatever he chooses.(wiki:Marq_Mandrake.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

During his rendezvous with the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) three miles south of [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys), [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) reveals that [Young Griff](wiki:Young_Griff) is in fact Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)). Silence greets his announcement. He notices Marq Mandrake and [Jon Lothston](wiki:Jon_Lothston) exchange a glance, and Jon Connington realizes they have already known. Marq, like the rest of the company, swears his allegiance to Aegon before they [invade Westeros](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company).
In [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), during Jon Connington and the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company)'s war council at [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost), it is disclosed that Marq was put ashore by the [Volantenes](wiki:Volantene) on what turns out to be [Estermont](wiki:Estermont) with close to five hundred men, although the island was never one of their objectives. He leads his troops and takes [Greenstone](wiki:Greenstone).(wiki:Marq_Mandrake.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
Connington figures there should be ships on Estermont as it is an island. He instructs [Haldon](wiki:Haldon) to send word to Marq to leave a garrison behind and bring the rest of his men to [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath), along with any noble captives.(wiki:Marq_Mandrake.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
