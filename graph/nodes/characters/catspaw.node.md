## Appearances & Description

The catspaw is described as being a small, dirty man in filthy brown clothing that smells of [horses](wiki:Horse). He has a gaunt face, limp blond hair, and pale deep-sunk eyes. He wields a [Valyrian steel dagger](wiki:Littlefinger's_blade) with a [dragonbone](wiki:Dragonbone) hilt. [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) notes that he moves unexpectedly fast.(wiki:Catspaw.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

The assassin arranges for a fire to start in the [library tower](wiki:Library_tower) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) as a diversion. This succeeds in drawing [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) away from the comatose [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) in order to attend to the fire, leaving Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) behind. Upon entering the [sickroom](wiki:Sickroom), the assassin finds Catelyn and claims that she was not supposed to be there. As he moves to Bran, the footpad states that killing him would be a mercy. As she attempts to scream for help, Catelyn is attacked by the assassin, who moves to try and attempt to slit her throat. They struggle and he injures her hand, as well as pulling out some of her hair. When the assassin is about to attack again, Bran's [unnamed direwolf](wiki:Summer) attacks him and tears out his throat.(wiki:Catspaw.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

Following this, [Hallis Mollen](wiki:Hallis_Mollen) informs Catelyn that the assassin was not a servant or stableboy and had been seen wandering around the castle following the weeks after Bran's fall from the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep). Hal also reports that the assassin had been sleeping in the stables, explaining why he had smelled of [horses](wiki:Horse). Amongst the possessions found in the stables, Robb mentions that the assassin had been paid ninety [silver stags](wiki:Currency) to kill Bran.(wiki:Catspaw.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

Catelyn decides to travel to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to inform her husband, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), of the attempt on their son's life, with Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) agreeing to accompany her.(wiki:Catspaw.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) In the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) tells Catelyn that he had once owned the Valyrian steel dagger but lost it to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) during the [tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_Prince_Joffrey's_name_day).(wiki:Catspaw.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) After being arrested by Catelyn, Tyrion denies ever having owned the dagger.(wiki:Catspaw.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Tyrion recalls a memory whilst he was at Winterfell, in which he overheard Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) jesting with the [Hound](wiki:Hound), [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane), to send a dog to kill a wolf. He guesses that Joffrey hired the catspaw from among those who joined King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s party as it made its way [north](wiki:North), and that he had found the dagger amongst his father's weapons and had simply taken it. However, Tyrion is unable to figure out why Joffrey wanted Bran dead, and he attributes it to the boy's innate cruelty.(wiki:Catspaw.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) realizes that Joffrey sent the assassin in an attempt to impress his father, after overhearing a drunken King Robert say it would be kinder to put the crippled Bran out of his misery.(wiki:Catspaw.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
