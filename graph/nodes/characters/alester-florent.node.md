## Appearances & Description

Alester is an older man in his middle years,(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rawoiafalester_florent.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) tall and slender.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) His long, elegant face has silvery grey hair and a pointed beard.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) He appears pale and pampered.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

Alester is an influential man, wealthy and powerful.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) The courtly lord considers himself to be a man of honor, but some consider him ambitious.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rawoiafalester_florent.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

In battle, Lord Florent wears [red-gold](wiki:Gold) armor,(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) with the sigil of [House Florent](wiki:House_Florent) on his breastplate displayed as a red-gold [fox](wiki:Fox) whose shining snout pokes through a circle of [lapis lazuli](wiki:Lapis_lazuli) flowers.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) Alester also owns a slashed velvet doublet and jeweled cloak.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

When Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) declares himself [King of Westeros](wiki:King_of_Westeros), his wife [Selyse](wiki:Selyse_Florent) believes that her uncle, Lord Alester, and her brothers will pledge their swords to his cause. Stannis believes otherwise, as Florent lands lie too close to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) for Lord Alester to risk the wrath of Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell).(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Lord Alester follows [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) in supporting Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) as king.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) When Renly marches on [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Alester's banners are spotted when Renly's host camps outside [Bitterbridge](wiki:Bitterbridge).(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

After [Renly's death](wiki:Shadow_assassin), Lord Florent believes it was Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) who slew the king.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) Alester rallies his banners, and is the first of those who swore to Renly to go over to Stannis's cause.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) He is also the first of the new lords to discard the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) and take up the worship of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor), and he becomes a leader of the [queen's men](wiki:Queen's_men).(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

When Stannis's host resumes the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), the castle's [castellan](wiki:Castellan), Ser [Cortnay Penrose](wiki:Cortnay_Penrose), accuses Lord Florent of being a [turncloak](wiki:Turncloak). Alester urges Stannis to bring old [Lord Penrose](wiki:Lord_Penrose) to the castle and hang him before his son.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

Stannis gives command of his [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) to Alester's nephew, Ser [Imry](wiki:Imry_Florent),(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) and the Florent banners are seen outside King's Landing when Stannis's vanguard arrives.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Stannis's host is defeated in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater),(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) however, and Alester flees when [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) overrun his camp, leaving behind all but the mail on his back and the rings on his fingers.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

### A Storm of Swords

For their support of Stannis, the Florents are stripped of [Brightwater Keep](wiki:Brightwater_Keep) by the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), with their lands instead being granted to Ser [Garlan Tyrell](wiki:Garlan_Tyrell).(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

Lord Alester becomes Stannis's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King),(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) in part because he is Selyse's uncle.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rawoiafalester_florent.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) After Stannis's defeat at the Blackwater, it is left to Selyse and Alester to rule in Stannis's name while the king broods. Alester is given the king's seal by Selyse, to speak in Stannis's name.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) When Maester [Pylos](wiki:Pylos) shows Alester a request from the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) for aid against a [wildling](wiki:Wildling) host, Alester dismisses the plea for help because Stannis lacks manpower.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Alester advises his brother, Ser [Axell Florent](wiki:Axell_Florent), the [castellan](wiki:Castellan) of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), against [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre)'s idea for Stannis to wake a stone [dragon](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6)

Alester tries to make peace with [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) by having Stannis give up his claim to the Iron Throne in exchange for him being named [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End) and [Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), with Alester vowing to do the same in exchange for Brightwater Keep and all his lands. He offers to seal the bargain by wedding Stannis's daughter [Shireen](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon) to Prince [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon). However, Alester's plans are discovered by the queen's men, and Stannis is furious with the terms. For his accused [treason](wiki:Treason), Alester is imprisoned in the same cell in [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone)'s dungeons as Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth).(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7)

When Davos is retrieved from the dungeons, Axell denounces his brother as a traitor and takes the torch from his cell, causing Alester to appeal to [the gods](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). Axell desires for Alester and Davos to be burned as traitors. Stannis, however, names Davos as his new King's Hand.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Ser [Denys Mallister](wiki:Denys_Mallister) recalls that he once unhorsed both of [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly)'s grandfathers in the same tourney.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Lord Alester is reported to have been sacrificed by [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) to [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) as a traitor.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) His son [Alekyne](wiki:Alekyne_Florent) succeeds him as the attained [Lord of Brightwater Keep](wiki:Lord_of_Brightwater_Keep), though he has fled to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

Ser Davos recalls that when Stannis decided to aid the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) at the [Wall](wiki:Wall), Melisandre burned Alester alive in order to obtain favorable winds for their expedition to [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea). When bound to a post, Lord Florent had been silent and dignified, but as the flames began to touch him, he began to scream.(wiki:Alester_Florent.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
