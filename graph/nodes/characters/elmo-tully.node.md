## Identity

Elmo Tully — Ser Elmo Tully, "that reluctant warrior," called House Tully's banners for the queen in defiance of his bedridden grandsire Lord Grover, after Seasmoke descended on Riverrun.

## Origins

At the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Lord [Grover Tully](wiki:Grover_Tully) wanted to support King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and the [greens](wiki:Greens), but no word of support left [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun). Fearing the wroth of the [dragons](wiki:Dragon) that both the greens and [blacks](wiki:Blacks) held, Ser Elmo kept the Tully banners at Riverrun. While Grover was furious with his grandson, he was too old and bedridden to oppose him.(wiki:Elmo_Tully.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)(wiki:Elmo_Tully.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

The Tullys finally joined the war in support of Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), after Ser [Addam Velaryon](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) visited Riverrun atop [Seasmoke](wiki:Seasmoke), much to Lord Grover's wroth.(wiki:Elmo_Tully.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Joining Ser Addam's army, Elmo led the riverlords to victory at the [Second Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:Second_Battle_of_Tumbleton). Grover passed away soon after the battle, with Elmo succeeding his grandfather as Lord of Riverrun. As the host of Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) began attacking the late Rhaenyra's loyalists around King's Landing, Elmo summoned the riverlords to war and marched a host of six thousand men towards the capital.(wiki:Elmo_Tully.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)(wiki:Elmo_Tully.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

While marching to King's Landing, Elmo died from drinking bad water, only forty-nine days after succeeding his grandfather.(wiki:Elmo_Tully.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) He was succeeded by his eldest son, Ser [Kermit Tully](wiki:Kermit_Tully), who led the riverlords to victory against the Baratheon host.(wiki:Elmo_Tully.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)(wiki:Elmo_Tully.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

## Appearances & Description

A reluctant and pragmatic warrior, [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) wrote that the undistinguished history of [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) was only exacerbated by the character of Elmo and his grandfather, Lord [Grover Tully](wiki:Grover_Tully).(wiki:Elmo_Tully.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
