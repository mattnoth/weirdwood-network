## Origins

### Dance of the Dragons

Unwin supported the [greens](wiki:Greens) during the [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) civil war known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). He marched forth with a thousand men(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-12) and joined Lord [Ormund Hightower](wiki:Ormund_Hightower)'s great host, which slowly marched towards [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). After the [First Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:First_Battle_of_Tumbleton), Unwin was one of the men who attempted to claim leadership over the host following Ormund's death, and he quarreled with the other would-be successors. At a war council, he stabbed Lord [Owain Bourney](wiki:Owain_Bourney) through the eye with his dagger, declaring him a turncloak.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Unwin wanted to wait at the [sacked town](wiki:Tumbleton) in hopes of reinforcement from Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon). Upon learning of the [unrest](wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings) in King's Landing, Unwin wished to march on the capital immediately, but Ser [Hobert Hightower](wiki:Hobert_Hightower) counseled caution, and [Hugh Hammer](wiki:Hugh_Hammer) and [Ulf White](wiki:Ulf_White) refused to join any attack. When the host learned of the death of Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), Unwin advocated for declaring Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)) to be the new [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone). Together with Ser Hobert, Unwin called together eleven other [lords](wiki:Lord) and [landed knights](wiki:Landed_knight), with whom they formed the [Caltrops](wiki:Caltrops). At a secret war council, the Caltrops plotted to kill the [Two Betrayers](wiki:Two_Betrayers).(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Lord Peake's tent was burned by [Seasmoke](wiki:Seasmoke) during the [Second Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:Second_Battle_of_Tumbleton). Unwin was one of the four Caltrops to survive the battle. He asked Ulf White about his intentions after the battle, to which Ulf replied that he would take the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) after the greens captured King's Landing. As planned by the Caltrops, Ulf died shortly later, after drinking [poisoned](wiki:Poison) [wine](wiki:Wine) with Hobert. Unwin offered one thousand [golden dragons](wiki:Currency) to any knight of noble birth who could claim [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing), Ulf's [dragon](wiki:Dragon), but none succeeded. Seeing that his army was disappearing through desertion, Unwin decided to abandon the campaign toward King's Landing and retreat from Tumbleton.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

### Regency of Aegon III

When the original council of King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen)'s [regents](wiki:Regent) was formed in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), Unwin was not offered a place amongst them. According to [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), Unwin was angered by his exclusion.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Following the death of Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), the other regents offered the empty seat to Unwin, who accepted.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) After the death of Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), Unwin was one of the three regents who remained in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), besides the ailing Lord [Manfryd Mooton](wiki:Manfryd_Mooton) and [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun). Unwin insisted that the other regents, who had departed the capital before Tyland's death, had given up their place on the council by doing so. Supported by Munkun, Unwin undid all of King Aegon III's appointments, as the king had not consulted his regents in making them. Unwin instead selected his own people for the vacant offices, and named himself as Hand and [Lord Protector of the Realm](wiki:Lord_Protector_of_the_Realm).(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-13)

Many of the appointments Unwin made concerned his own family members, or close friends. He gave white cloaks of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) to his nephew, Ser [Amaury Peake](wiki:Amaury_Peake), and his [bastard](wiki:Bastard) half-brother, Ser [Mervyn Flowers](wiki:Mervyn_Flowers). Lord Peake named Ser [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long), the former [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms) at [Starpike](wiki:Starpike), as the new master-at-arms to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), and he appointed Ser [Lucas Leygood](wiki:Lucas_Leygood) as [Commander of the City Watch](wiki:Commander_of_the_City_Watch). Unwin placed his widowed aunt, [Clarice Osgrey](wiki:Clarice_Osgrey), in charge of Queen [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen)'s household. Lord [George Graceford](wiki:George_Graceford) and Ser [Victor Risley](wiki:Victor_Risley), two [Caltrops](wiki:Caltrops), were appointed to the offices of [Lord Confessor](wiki:Lord_Confessor) and [King's Justice](wiki:King's_Justice), respectively. Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) was dismissed and replaced by Septon [Bernard](wiki:Bernard), who was descended from [House Peake](wiki:House_Peake). In addition, Unwin surrounded himself with a personal guard made up of ten [sellswords](wiki:Sellswords), who soon became known as the "[Fingers](wiki:Fingers_(guards))".(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-14) By [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), he had taken possession of the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword [Orphan-Maker](wiki:Orphan-Maker).(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-15)

Unwin wished to demonstrate his strength and rectitude during his time as Hand. He had the crowded dungeons of the Red Keep emptied and all the offenders punished publicly on the [Feast Day of Our Father Above](wiki:Feast_Day_of_Our_Father_Above) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC).(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-16) By mid-year, Unwin's hold on power was firm. Intent on demonstrating the power of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Unwin sent his uncle, Ser [Gedmund Peake](wiki:Gedmund_Peake), with the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) to end the pirate kingdom of [Racallio Ryndoon](wiki:Racallio_Ryndoon) and establish a presence upon [Bloodstone](wiki:Bloodstone) in the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), so as to restore trade during the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War). However, as the royal fleet was not large enough to accomplish this, Unwin sent forth a [raven](wiki:Raven) to [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark), commanding Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) to join his forces to Gedmund's. However, while Gedmund delayed on [Tarth](wiki:Tarth), Alyn defied orders and sailed to the Stepstones with his own fleet, [crushing the Braavosi fleet](wiki:Lord_Alyn's_attack) at Bloodstone. This left Unwin furious, as Racallio's forces were unharmed, while the attack on the Braavosi forces could easily lead to a war with [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) itself. Although he publicly rewarded Alyn for his actions, Unwin instructed King Aegon III to send forth Velaryon to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) to deal with [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), hoping that Alyn would either suffer severe losses on the perilous journey, or would free the westerlands from the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn). Meanwhile, he sent Lord Mooton to Braavos to negotiate a truce and prevent war.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-17)

In [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), Queen [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen) died, apparently having committed suicide. It was rumored, however, that she had been murdered. Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) considers Unwin as the only possible candidate for having ordered Jaehaera's death, if indeed she had been murdered, and suggests Ser Mervyn Flowers, the white cloak guarding Jaehaera's door the night of her death, or one of the Fingers as the possible killers. According to Gyldayn, Unwin's motive would have been the succession of the Iron Throne, as Aegon III was not like to father an heir on the queen, leaving only the potential son of Aegon III's half-sister [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen), who was expecting with her husband, Alyn Velaryon.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-18)

Seven days after Jaehaera's death, Unwin declared that Aegon would wed [Myrielle Peake](wiki:Myrielle_Peake), Unwin's daughter. Many [lords](wiki:Lord) disapproved of the match and voiced their criticism. Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) suggested the [north](wiki:North) would look on the match with disfavor, Lord [Kermit Tully](wiki:Kermit_Tully) called it presumptuous, Lord [Benjicot Blackwood](wiki:Benjicot_Blackwood) questioned the haste of the match, and Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun)'s support began to waver, acknowledging that the match would be seen as advancing [Peake](wiki:House_Peake) interests rather than being for the good of [the realm](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). Several highborn ladies wrote to the crown, proposing their own relatives as Aegon's bride (or themselves, in some cases). Due to pressure from the lords and ladies of the realm, Unwin instead announced what became known as the [Maiden's Day Ball](wiki:Maiden's_Day_Ball) in King's Landing, where the king would be able to choose his own bride.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-19)

Lord Peake restricted the ball to [maidens](wiki:Maiden) of [nobility](wiki:Nobility) under the age of thirty to limit the attendance, but more than one thousand young women from throughout [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) and even [Essos](wiki:Essos) came to King's Landing in hopes of marrying the king. Many women were injured or scandalized before the ball, and lords spoke of the "[Maiden's Day curse](wiki:Maiden's_Day_curse)". Many of these events were likely engineered by Lord Peake, who hoped to have his daughter be queen.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-20)

Unwin had Myrielle arrive at court a fortnight before the ball, making sure she spent as much time as possible with the king. Aegon and Myrielle dined together half a dozen times and it seemed like the king was fonder of Myrielle than he ever was of Jaehaera. When the day of the ball finally arrived, each maid was presented before the king. King Aegon quickly grew bored and his growing disinterest as hours passed only benefited Lord Peake further. When only a few maidens remained, Lady [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen) and Lady [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)) suddenly arrived with their kinswoman, [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon). Afterwards, the king summoned his cupbearer, [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair), who announced that the king would marry Lady Daenaera. His plans foiled by the twins, Lord Peake developed a special hatred for Baela and Rhaena. According to [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom), Unwin was paranoid and convinced that the pregnant Baela was plotting to kill the king if she had a boy and said as much to Ser [Marston Waters](wiki:Marston_Waters) once.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-21)

When Lord Unwin threatened to resign his position in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC), his threat was accepted by his co-regents. Although he left King's Landing, all of Unwin's appointments kept their posts after his departure.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-22)

### Later Life

Amid the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring) in [135 AC](wiki:135_AC), there was a conspiracy against [House Rogare](wiki:House_Rogare) in King's Landing. It was claimed that the Rogares were plotting to assassinate King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) in order for his brother, Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), who was married to Lady [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare), to ascend the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). This eventually led to the [secret siege](wiki:Secret_siege) within the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). When the plot unraveled and the conspirators were arrested, many suspected Lord Unwin was the mastermind behind it, for all the conspirators were men and women he had named to positions of power when he was [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Although it was never conclusively proven, few doubt Unwin was involved.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Appearances & Description

Unwin was proud, brusque, hard, ambitious, and cunning. Like his [lord father](wiki:Lord_Peake_(father_of_Unwin)), Unwin did not believe a woman should rule over men.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11) Unwin had a splendid [silken](wiki:Silk) pavilion.(wiki:Unwin_Peake.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
