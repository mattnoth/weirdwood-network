## Origins

Ser Lyn won his [knighthood](wiki:Knight) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-10) At the start of the war, he fought for [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) alongside Lord [Marq Grafton](wiki:Marq_Grafton) against Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) at the gates of [Gulltown](wiki:Taking_of_Gulltown).(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-11) After being pardoned, Lyn joined Jon against the Targaryens.(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-12)

[Lyn's father](wiki:Lord_Corbray_(father_of_Lyonel)) was badly wounded during the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident). As [Lyonel Corbray](wiki:Lyonel_Corbray) led their father back to the rear, Lyn took up the family's [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) longsword, [Lady Forlorn](wiki:Lady_Forlorn), led a charge on the [Dornish](wiki:Dornishmen) lines, and cut down the previously-wounded Prince [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-13)

Honoring Lyn's achievements on the battlefield, his father bequeathed him Lady Forlorn upon his death. Lyn's elder brother, Lyonel inherited [Heart's Home](wiki:Heart's_Home) and the rest of the family estate and titles, but still resented the loss of the family blade.(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-14)

Lyn has slain many men in duels.(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-15)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Ser Lyn is one of Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn)'s suitors after the death of Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn).(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) His squire is [Mychel Redfort](wiki:Mychel_Redfort).(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Ragot36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Lyn attends the trial of [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). He offers himself as champion of the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) when Tyrion demands [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat), but is rebuffed by Lysa.(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Lyn suggests beheading Tyrion and sending his head to his brother, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), but is again refused.(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

Following [Bronn](wiki:Bronn)'s victory over Ser [Vardis Egen](wiki:Vardis_Egen) in the trial,(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) Lyn escorts Tyrion and Bronn to the [Bloody Gate](wiki:Bloody_Gate), where he commands them never to return to the Vale.(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Lyn remains a suitor of Lysa.(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) prefers having Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) ruling the Eyrie than any of Lysa's suitors, including Lyn.(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)

Having [married Lysa](wiki:Wedding_of_Petyr_Baelish_and_Lysa_Arryn) and been named [Lord Protector of the Vale](wiki:Lord_Protector_of_the_Vale), Petyr travels to meet with the [Corbrays](wiki:House_Corbray).(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Ser Lyn accompanies the [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant) to their parley with Petyr and young Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) at the Eyrie. Lord [Nestor Royce](wiki:Nestor_Royce) claims that Lyn rarely left the side of Lysa for nearly half a year. During the parley, Ser Lyn grows tired of the conversation and threatens Petyr with his sword, [Lady Forlorn](wiki:Lady_Forlorn), violating the laws of hospitality. Ser [Lothor Brune](wiki:Lothor_Brune) moves to intercept, but it is the intervention of the other Lords Declarant that causes Lyn to put up his sword. Lyn leaves the parley shortly thereafter.(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-16)

Later, Petyr admits to [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) that Lyn is in his employ, and it is his task to feign hatred for Petyr and to join every conspiracy against him and inform on them. Petyr states that Lyn's price is [gold](wiki:Gold) and boys.(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-17)

With Mychel Redfort now a knight, Lyn's new squire is a lad considered a lout by [Myranda Royce](wiki:Myranda_Royce).(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### The Winds of Winter

Prior to the [tourney](wiki:Tourney) for the [Brotherhood of Winged Knights](wiki:Brotherhood_of_Winged_Knights) at the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon), Ser Lyn knocks down Ser [Owen](wiki:Owen_(knight)) during a practice match. The buxom Myranda japes that Lyn would not be interested in her because of her gender.(wiki:Lyn_Corbray.cite_ref-sample_8-4)
