## Origins

As the maester in service to [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie), Colemon stayed in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during Jon's time in the capital as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). When Jon became ill with stomach pain, Colemon purged him with wasting potions and pepper juice.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle), the [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), sent Colemon away, but Lord Arryn's condition continued to worsen and he soon died.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

After Jon's death, Colemon returned to the Eyrie with the rest of Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn)'s household.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Colemon](wiki:Category:Images_of_Colemon)*

Colemon is a thin(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) man with little hair(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) and a long, skinny neck.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) The apple of his throat is visible.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Colemon is much taller than Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish).(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Colemon wears a threadbare grey cloak lined with [squirrel](wiki:Squirrel) fur.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) The chain worn by [maesters](wiki:Maesters) hangs loose around his neck(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) and clinks softly when he walks.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Colemon is often nervous,(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) especially in violent situations.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) considers Colemon to be young,(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and Lady [Anya Waynwood](wiki:Anya_Waynwood) considers Maester [Helliweg](wiki:Helliweg) of [Runestone](wiki:Runestone) to be more experienced.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) commands Maester Colemon to remain at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) to care for her son, Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn).(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), investigates the death of his predecessor, Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn). Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) explains that he dismissed Colemon from treating Jon out of fears that the young maester would inadvertently kill his older patient.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

Colemon and Ser [Vardis Egen](wiki:Vardis_Egen) greet Lysa's sister, Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), upon her arrival at the Eyrie.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) When a letter from Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) arrives to inform about the conflict between [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) and [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), Colemon tells the sisters' uncle, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), about its contents.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Anxious about the upcoming [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) to determine the fate of [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), Colemon drinks [wine](wiki:Wine) brought by Lord [Eon Hunter](wiki:Eon_Hunter). Colemon lets slip to Catelyn and Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) that Jon Arryn had planned to send his son Robert to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), to be fostered by Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon). When Catelyn responds that Robert was to be fostered at Casterly Rock, Colemon insists that Jon had chosen Dragonstone.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

### A Clash of Kings

During his interrogation by [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), now the acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) reveals his belief that Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) had wanted Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) dead. Colemon had been purging Jon to treat his sudden [illness](wiki:Illness), so Pycelle sent Colemon away and allowed Lord Arryn's condition to worsen.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### A Storm of Swords

A shivering Colemon watches for a bit as Alayne Stone ([Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark)) builds a snow castle of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) in the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie)'s garden. When Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) begins smashing the snow castle with his doll, Sansa rips the doll apart in an effort to stop him. Colemon provides half a cup of [dreamwine](wiki:Dreamwine) to calm the outraged boy, and Robert is taken to the maester's chambers for [leeching](wiki:Leech) to treat his [shaking sickness](wiki:Shaking_sickness).(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Colemon is present when Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) welcomes Lord [Nestor Royce](wiki:Nestor_Royce) and Ser [Marwyn Belmore](wiki:Marwyn_Belmore) to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) He is in attendance when [Alayne Stone](wiki:Alayne_Stone) tells the visitors how Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) had been killed by her singer, [Marillion](wiki:Marillion),(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) although Petyr was the actual murderer.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) When Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn), upon hearing the tale, suffers another attack from his shaking sickness, a grim-faced Colemon has the boy taken back to bed so he can be bled.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) Colemon later gives Robert [dreamwine](wiki:Dreamwine) to help him sleep when the boy claims to hear Marillion singing in the night.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

Lord Robert is upset on the day when the [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant) are to come to the Eyrie, and Maester Colemon is struck when the boy throws his bowl of porridge in anger. Colemon expresses his worries about the treatments he gives Robert, as the boy's spells have grown worse since his mother Lysa died. Petyr orders him to add a pinch of [sweetsleep](wiki:Sweetsleep) to the boy's milk.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

During the parley with the Lords Declarant, Lady [Anya Waynwood](wiki:Anya_Waynwood) attempts to persuade Petyr to send Robert to [Runestone](wiki:Runestone), where Lord [Yohn Royce](wiki:Yohn_Royce)'s maester, [Helliweg](wiki:Helliweg), could treat Robert, claiming that Helliweg is more experienced and better suited to treat Robert's frailties than Colemon.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

The day the inhabitants of the Eyrie are to descend to the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon) in preparation for winter, Robert throws his [chamber pot](wiki:Night_soil) at Colemon and dirties his hair and robe. The maester goes to Ser [Lothor Brune](wiki:Lothor_Brune), who informs Alayne. Robert complains to Alayne that Colemon had put something "vile" in his milk. Colemon suggests giving Robert some [milk of the poppy](wiki:Milk_of_the_poppy) so the boy will slumber during the descent, but Alayne refuses the notion. She tells him instead to give Robert a cup of [sweetmilk](wiki:Sweetmilk), to which the maester objects, fearing the [poison](wiki:Poison) is building up too much in Robert's blood. Colemon reveals that Robert's blood has grown so thin that he had begun to fear leeching the boy, and he fears the boy will bleed from the nose. When Alayne insists that Colemon should give Robert a cup of sweetmilk before the descent, and one shortly after, Colemon insists that it must be the last cup for at least half a year.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

When Colemon accompanies Robert and Alayne down the [Giant's Lance](wiki:Giant's_Lance), he leads a mule laden with chests of herbs and potions.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

### The Winds of Winter

During the feast for the [Brotherhood of Winged Knights](wiki:Brotherhood_of_Winged_Knights) at the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon), Alayne assumes that Maester Colemon has provided a strong dose of [sweetmilk](wiki:Sweetmilk) to Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) to keep the boy calm.(wiki:Colemon.cite_ref-twow_10-0)
