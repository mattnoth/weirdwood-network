## Identity

Sansa Stark (b. 286 AC) — child of Catelyn Stark; sworn to House Stark; Princess. One of 2 characters named Sansa Stark.

## Origins

Sansa was born at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell),(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_sansa_stark.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) with [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) aiding [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) during the delivery.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) In her eleven years, she has been tutored by [Septa](wiki:Septa) [Mordane](wiki:Mordane) in the traditional womanly activities.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-12) Luwin has also educated the Stark children.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Sansa shared a bedchamber with her younger sister, [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark), before getting her own.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

[Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), having covered himself with flour to appear as a ghost, once tried to scare his younger siblings in the [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell). While Sansa and [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) were frightened, Arya instead punched their half-brother.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

Sansa became infatuated with Ser [Waymar Royce](wiki:Waymar_Royce) when the young man stopped at Winterfell on his way to [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), the ward of Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), has pondered marrying Sansa and being accepted by Eddard as a son.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

## Appearances & Description

Sansa is a beautiful girl who resembles her mother, Lady [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) of [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) She has the Tully features(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) of deep blue eyes(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) and thick, soft auburn hair,(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) which is lighter than her mother's.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) Sansa also has fine high cheekbones like Catelyn,(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) which they share with Catelyn's mother, Lady [Minisa](wiki:Minisa_Tully) of [House Whent](wiki:House_Whent).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Sansa is eleven years old at the start of *[A Song of Ice and Fire](wiki:A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire)*.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) As she has grown up, her figure has been described as tall,(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) graceful,(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-twow_20-0) and womanly.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Sansa is described as soft-spoken and sweet-smelling.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

Raised as a lady, the courteous(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Sansa is adept at sewing(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) and dancing.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4) The youth has learned numerous songs by heart(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) and sings sweetly,(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) and she can play the high [harp](wiki:Harp) and the bells.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5) Sansa dresses herself appropriately.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-6)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) She can read and write better than her brothers(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) and is skilled at poetry,(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-7) but has difficulty with sums(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) and figures.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-8)

Like many girls her age, Sansa is enthralled by songs and stories of romance(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) and adventure, particularly those depicting handsome princes, honorable [knights](wiki:Knight), chivalry, and love.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-9) Sansa keeps faith with both the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) and the [Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven),(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) though she favors the new gods of her mother.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Sansa's relationship with her younger sister [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark) is often strained, and the two are opposites in most respects.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-10) She always refers to the [bastard](wiki:Bastard)-born [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) as her half-brother.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Sansa's dearest friend is [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole), the daughter of [Vayon Poole](wiki:Vayon_Poole), the steward of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-11)

Sansa is fond of [lemon cakes](wiki:Lemon_cake),(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) with [Gage](wiki:Gage) making the treats whenever possible.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-31)

As Alayne Stone she dyes her auburn hair: "The wash her aunt had given her changed her own rich auburn into Alayne's burnt brown, but it was seldom long before the red began creeping back at the roots." AFFC Alayne I (`sources/chapters/affc/affc-alayne-01.md:25`).

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Sansa names her [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf) pup [Lady](wiki:Lady_(direwolf)).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-13) With her father [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark)'s appointment as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Sansa is delighted at the prospect of life at court in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and is thrilled by her [betrothal](wiki:Marriage) to the handsome Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-14)

On their journey to the capital, Sansa is frightened when she meets Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne), the [King's Justice](wiki:King's_Justice), at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn). Joffrey courts Sansa at the insistence of his mother, Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister). The two are walking together near the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford) when they come upon her sister, [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark), playing at swords with the boy [Mycah](wiki:Mycah). Joffrey draws his sword, [Lion's Tooth](wiki:Lion's_Tooth), and threatens Mycah, but Arya fights with him; her direwolf [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria_(direwolf)) disarms and superficially wounds him.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Later recounting the tale to Joffrey's father, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), at [Darry](wiki:Darry), Arya tells the truth while Joffrey lies, saying that Mycah, Arya, and Nymeria attacked him without provocation. Sansa, not wishing to betray her future husband, lies and refuses to verify either story, pleading that she does not remember. Cersei uses the opportunity to insist that the direwolf that attacked her son be killed. Arya, having anticipated this, had driven Nymeria away. To assuage the queen, Sansa's direwolf Lady is killed by Eddard instead.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

Sansa feuds with Arya in King's Landing, placing the majority of the blame on her sister for Lady's death. Sansa remains infatuated with Joffrey, largely overlooking his violent mood swings, and she develops a close relationship with Queen Cersei as well. Attending the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney) celebrating her father's appointment, Sansa finds many of her notions of [knightly](wiki:Knight) valor and chivalry reinforced, seeing the events as worthy of one of her favored stories. To warn Sansa about dangers she faces, [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) tells the girl how his brother, Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane), burned his face. She also develops a crush on Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell), the Knight of Flowers; when he gives her a red [rose](wiki:Rose), she is certain she is living in a romantic song.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

After Eddard discovers the truth of Joffrey's parentage and tells his daughters that he is returning them to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) on the *[Wind Witch](wiki:Wind_Witch)*, Sansa, in an act of defiance, runs to the queen and tells her of her father's plans, pleading that she might be allowed to stay and marry Joffrey. Her doing so unwittingly aids Cersei's plot against her father.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1) After [Eddard's arrest](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark), Sansa is detained in [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast), and her friend [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole) is taken away by Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) Sansa is forced to call her father a traitor. She pleads in front of the king and his retainers for mercy. Joffrey, now king after Robert's death, promises to be merciful,(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-2) but on the steps of the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) he orders Ilyn to behead Eddard as Sansa helplessly looks on and screams hysterically.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

Even after Eddard's execution, Cersei declares that Joffrey should still be married to Sansa upon her flowering. Sansa, horrified at the prospect and only wanting to return home, has no choice but to remain in King's Landing during the deteriorating situation, as a hostage, and denounce her father and brother as traitors. Joffrey shows Sansa her father's head, which has been placed on a spike in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). Sansa briefly considers pushing the prince to his death, even if it means she will die as well, but Sandor subtly stops her.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

After the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), Sansa's brother [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark) is proclaimed [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Now effectively a hostage in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), Sansa armors herself in her lady's courtesies as she dutifully goes through the motions of denouncing her family as traitors and pleading to them for peace. King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon) frequently orders her to be beaten by his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) as punishment for actions taken by her rebellious brother [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) At the [tourney on King Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_King_Joffrey's_name_day), Sansa realizes she has lost her romantic notions about knightly and courtly love. Ser [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard) disgraces himself by showing up too drunk to compete. Joffrey decides he should be killed, causing a horrified Sansa to make up a convincing lie which [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) backs up. Sansa pleads successfully with the king to spare Dontos, and a placated Joffrey instead strips him of his knighthood and makes him a court [fool](wiki:Fool), as Sansa suggested.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1)

When [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) arrives in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and assumes the position of acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), he sees an end to the more overt abuses directed at Sansa, including stopping Ser [Meryn Trant](wiki:Meryn_Trant) from stripping the girl naked and beating her.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-1) Sansa develops a unique relationship with Sandor, who despite his brusque words and hideous demeanor treats her gently, protects her, and refuses to beat her at Joffrey's command. He privately mocks her for her naive nature but also displays sympathy for her predicament.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

One night Sansa finds a note in her room, directing her to come to the [godswood of the Red Keep](wiki:Godswood_of_the_Red_Keep) if she wants to go home. It is from Dontos, who promises that a plan is in place to free her from the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) and bring her to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). Wary of a trap, she agrees to Dontos's promise, who in turn counsels patience and perseverance. Feigning a newfound piety to the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods), Sansa makes frequent trips to the godswood to meet with Dontos, at first seeing him as a gallant rescuer who seeks redemption for the shame he brought upon himself, though she eventually comes to doubt his competence. They refer to each other as [Florian](wiki:Florian_the_Fool) and [Jonquil](wiki:Jonquil).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

During the [riots in King's Landing](wiki:Riots_in_King's_Landing) after the departure of Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), Ser [Mandon Moore](wiki:Mandon_Moore) abandons Sansa to protect Joffrey and the girl is separated from the royal party. Sandor rescues the wounded Sansa from the mob and returns her to the Red Keep.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

As [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s army approaches the city, Sansa experiences her first [flowering](wiki:Flowering), making her officially a woman.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-1) During the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater) Sansa takes refuge with many of the other ladies of the court in the [royal sept](wiki:Royal_sept) of the Red Keep and the [Queen's Ballroom](wiki:Queen's_Ballroom) in Maegor's Holdfast. She remains strong and attempts to calm many of the other frightened women, despite the bitter and cynical words of a drunk Cersei, who spends her time sniping at Sansa and the other attendant ladies before storming out.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-3)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

When Sansa returns to her chambers, she finds Sandor hidden in her room, broken from the threat of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire). He drunkenly offers to take Sansa with him as he flees the city. She refuses, and he makes her sing for him at knifepoint.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-1) After he leaves, Sansa puts on Sandor's bloody cloak, then keeps it in a chest with her summer dresses. Later on, Sansa remembers [something more](wiki:UnKiss) happening, she believes Sandor kissed her that night. Dontos later informs Sansa that Stannis was defeated by the arrival of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) and [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-2)

### A Storm of Swords

Since [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) and [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark) are believed to have been murdered by [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) releases Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) from captivity at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), in return for his promise to return with her daughters, Sansa and [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

Sansa remembers the night Sandor left, and she wishes he was still there. Sansa is asked to sup with [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) and her grandmother [Olenna](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell). Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) takes her breath away with his beauty and compliments, but he does not appear to remember her or the rose from the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney). When Olenna tries to find out more about Joffrey, Sansa confesses that the king is a monster and begs Margaery not to marry him. Margaery insists that she will be safe with her brother Loras's addition to the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), and brushes off the concerns. The Tyrell women raise the possibility of Sansa being taken to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) to be married to the Tyrell heir, [Willas](wiki:Willas_Tyrell), who is crippled, but kind, intelligent, and capable. Sansa readily agrees, anxious to be free of the Lannisters, and she becomes friends with Margaery for a time.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1)

Sansa shares the Tyrell plot with Ser [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard), but the fool advises against it, fearfully noting that the Tyrells are not as gentle as they seem. Sansa, frustrated with the apparent lack of progress in Dontos's own plans, disregards his warnings.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) Unbeknownst to Sansa, Dontos informs his employer, Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), and Petyr in turn informs the Lannisters.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)

Not wishing to lose their hostage, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) acts quickly to marry Sansa to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) against the wishes of both parties.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2) Sansa, now thirteen, does her best to hide her feelings toward her disfigured [dwarf](wiki:Dwarf) husband, remaining courteous despite her disgust with his appearance and her deep unhappiness with the marriage. The [wedding of Tyrion and Sansa](wiki:Wedding_of_Tyrion_Lannister_and_Sansa_Stark) is held in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). Tyrion does not require that she consummate the marriage, for which Sansa is grateful,(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) particularly after learning of the deaths of her mother, Catelyn, and brother, King [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark), at the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) Sansa's maids include [Brella](wiki:Brella) and [Shae](wiki:Shae), the latter of whom is Tyrion's secret lover.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

Littlefinger also has Dontos killed and dumped overboard, explaining that while he had played his part well, a drunk such as Dontos could never be entrusted to keep such sensitive information secret for long.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-1)

### A Feast for Crows

[Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) searches the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) and the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) for Sansa, whom she claims is her sister.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0) Along the road to [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), Brienne meets Ser [Shadrich of the Shady Glen](wiki:Shadrich), a hedge knight seeking the girl for a gold bounty.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-1) Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) regrets having followed King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon)'s commands to beat Sansa.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0)

During a meeting of the new [small council](wiki:Small_council) of King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) is angered that Sansa, whom Cersei believes she tried to mentor, helped to [murder her son](wiki:Purple_Wedding), and threatens [torture](wiki:Torture) and worse should Sansa be found.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0)

Sansa, still in the guise of "Alayne Stone", becomes the *de facto* Lady of the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). She takes care of and comforts her sickly young cousin, Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn), who she calls Sweetrobin. Robert, who is no longer able to share his mother's bed since her death, wanders the Eyrie at night looking for other beds to share. He frequently crawls into Sansa's bed, which she would not mind, except that he tries to nuzzle at her breasts, and his [shaking sickness](wiki:Shaking_sickness) causes him to wet her bed. Therefore, she has Ser [Lothor Brune](wiki:Lothor_Brune) lock Robert in his own room at night.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-1)

[Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), [Lord Protector of the Vale](wiki:Lord_Protector_of_the_Vale), tutors Sansa in some of the finer points of courtly intrigue, and convinces her that lies are not harmful if meant kindly. Petyr also convinces Sansa that if he were to be found out as Lysa Arryn's killer, she would be accused of the crime as well. When Lord [Nestor Royce](wiki:Nestor_Royce) visits the Eyrie, she tells him that [Marillion](wiki:Marillion) killed Lysa, not Littlefinger. She also deduces that Petyr grants the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon) to Nestor to gain his political support.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-2) When Ser [Lyn Corbray](wiki:Lyn_Corbray) draws his sword during a parlay at the Eyrie between Petyr and the [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant), Littlefinger leverages the breach of etiquette to demand a trial period of [regency](wiki:Regency) over the little Lord Robert. She correctly surmises that Lyn is under Petyr's employ, which Littlefinger confirms.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3)

Lysa's jewels and wardrobe of silks, satins, velvets, and furs are given by Petyr to Alayne, although most of the clothing is too large for her. She also must be careful not to wear Arryn colors or anything too luxurious, lest Alayne appear to be stepping above her [bastard](wiki:Bastard) status. Sansa also must avoid Tully colors, or her resemblance to her mother Catelyn might be noticed. Alayne's hair dye turns her auburn hair into a burnt brown, or chestnut, but it is seldom long before the red begins creeping back at the roots. Alayne worries what she will do when the dye runs out.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-4)

The Arryn court departs the Eyrie for the Gates of the Moon because of the onset of [winter](wiki:Winter). Preparing for departure, Alayne lets Sweetrobin kiss her, but when his lips touch hers, she remembers Sandor kissing her. Alayne befriends Lord Nestor's daughter, [Myranda Royce](wiki:Myranda_Royce), and helps Robert during the journey down the [Giant's Lance](wiki:Giant's_Lance). Alayne tells Myranda that she is fourteen, a year older than Sansa actually is at the time.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2)

Once at the Gates of the Moon, Alayne is introduced to Petyr's newly hired hedge knights, Shadrich, Ser [Byron the Beautiful](wiki:Byron_the_Beautiful), and Ser [Morgarth the Merry](wiki:Morgarth_the_Merry). Petyr later reveals his plan to Alayne for the eventual marriage of Sansa to the heir of the Vale of Arryn, Ser [Harrold Hardyng](wiki:Harrold_Hardyng), and to reveal her true identity to the Lords of the Vale and to reclaim [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) in her name.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-3)

### A Dance with Dragons

[Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) wants to bestow [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) on [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), now [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), but Jon insists the castle is his half-sister Sansa's birthright.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Stannis discounts the claim of "Lady Lannister", however.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0)

### The Winds of Winter

To find a means of meeting her betrothed, Alayne suggests that a personal guard be formed to protect Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn), named the [Brotherhood of Winged Knights](wiki:Brotherhood_of_Winged_Knights), whose initial members would be selected in a [tourney](wiki:Tourney). She meets the newly-knighted [Harrold Hardyng](wiki:Harrold_Hardyng) when he comes to the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon) to compete for a position in the brotherhood. When he first meets Alayne, he treats her with contempt for her [bastard](wiki:Bastard) status. Sansa remembers that [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) was attractive as well, but cruel, while the ugly [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) was kinder. At the behest of Petyr, she attempts to beguile Harrold during a feast held that evening.(wiki:Sansa_Stark.cite_ref-twow_20-1)
