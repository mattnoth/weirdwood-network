## Origins

### Early life

Prince Baelor was born in [170 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-ages_4-1) the first child of King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) and [Myriah Martell](wiki:Myriah_Martell).(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) After the death of his grandfather, King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), in [184 AC](wiki:184_AC),(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Prince Baelor became the heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and was later given the title of [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) by his father.(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

Baelor learned how to fight under the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms), Ser [Quentyn Ball](wiki:Quentyn_Ball), alongside his brothers and the [Great Bastards](wiki:Great_Bastards).(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) In [187 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the seventeen-year-old Baelor won the name "Breakspear", following his famous victory at his [aunt Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV))'s [wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Maron_Martell_and_Daenerys_Targaryen) [tourney](wiki:Tourney), where he defeated Ser [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), Daeron's [bastard](wiki:Bastard)-born half-brother, in the final tilt.(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

### First Blackfyre Rebellion

Baelor's dark hair and eyes led to wariness from those who disliked [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell). Nobles from the [Dornish Marches](wiki:Dornish_Marches) especially distrusted King Daeron and his heir, as the [marcher lords](wiki:Marcher_lords) were historical foes of [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) and disliked their presence at court.(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

Baelor distinguished himself in the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) by leading the Dornish and [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) force which attacked the rearguard of Daemon's rebel army during the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field) in [196 AC](wiki:196_AC). Baelor's forces smashed the rebels against the shield-wall of his brother, Prince [Maekar](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), inspiring a song about [the hammer and the anvil](wiki:The_hammer_and_the_anvil).(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### Hand of the King

Baelor was named [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm).(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12) Ser [Arlan of Pennytree](wiki:Arlan_of_Pennytree) had a high regard for Baelor, who was considered chivalrous and wise.(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

When Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) served at meetings of the [small council](wiki:Small_council), he observed that Baelor recommended clemency for honorable foes to prevent them from fighting to the death. In contrast, [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) believed that pardoning rebels would only lead to future rebellions.(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

### Tourney at Ashford Meadow

In [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), at the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow), Prince Baelor vouched for Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) because he remembered Duncan's old master, Ser [Arlan of Pennytree](wiki:Arlan_of_Pennytree). Baelor had jousted with Arlan in a tourney at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) and had unhorsed him after breaking four lances. Due to Baelor's intervention, Duncan was allowed to enter the lists.(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14)

When Duncan attacked Prince [Aerion Brightfire](wiki:Aerion_Brightfire) to defend [Tanselle](wiki:Tanselle), an innocent puppeteer, Baelor had a private word with Duncan and advised him. The next day Baelor sat together with Prince [Maekar](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), Lord [Leo Tyrell](wiki:Leo_Tyrell_(Longthorn)), and [Lord Ashford](wiki:Lord_Ashford) in judgment of Duncan. Prince Aerion demanded a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven), which was granted.(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15)

With the help of his squire, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), and Ser [Raymun Fossoway](wiki:Raymun_Fossoway), Duncan was able to recruit five men to fight for him. They needed one more, and because Baelor believed Duncan, he fought for him even though his nephew was the accuser.(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16)

Before the melee began, Baelor advised Dunk's champions to use tourney lances, which would strike before their opponents' shorter war lances in the opening tilt. He also noted that the three knights of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) on the opposing side would be sworn not to harm him, allowing him to safely engage them. Dunk's defending party won the trial, but Baelor had received a mortal blow to his head from the mace of his brother, Maekar, and the prince died in Dunk's arms. Baelor was cremated at Ashford, per [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) funeral traditions.(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-17)

## Appearances & Description

Prince Baelor had the dark hair of his mother, [Myriah Martell](wiki:Myriah_Martell), a [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) princess. He kept it cut short, and by his late thirties, his hair was peppered with grey. He had a strong jaw which was clean-shaven. His nose had been broken twice. He was a tall,(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) lean, and broad-shouldered(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rawoiafbaelor_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) man with long, straight legs and stood about a head taller than his youngest brother, Maekar. Although Baelor dressed simply, he had a weight about him that evoked a sense of power and certainty.(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

A warrior, Baelor seemed to be all that could be wished in a [knight](wiki:Knight), [lord](wiki:Lord), or heir. He was a man who could win respect with ease, and he was as open-handed and just as his father, King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) He was one of the most powerful warriors in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), rivalling the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), and was famed for his prowess with sword and lance.(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) Baelor treated honorable foes with clemency.(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Many men looked upon Baelor's dark hair and eyes and muttered that he was more [Martell](wiki:House_Martell) than [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) Also, some [Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) sympathizers were bothered by his namesake, [Baelor the Blessed](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen), "the feeblest king who ever sat the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne)".(wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II).cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
