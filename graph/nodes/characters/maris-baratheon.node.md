## Appearances & Description

According to her father, Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon), Maris was the cleverest of the [Four Storms](wiki:Four_Storms). She was less comely than her sisters, and grew angry that Prince [Aemond](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) preferred them to her.(wiki:Maris_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Origins

At the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) was sent by the [green council](wiki:Green_council) to Storm's End to gain the support of its lord for the [greens](wiki:Greens). Lord Borros offered the hand of any of his four daughters to the prince, noting that Maris would make a clever wife. Aemond agreed to a [betrothal](wiki:Betrothal), though the identity of the daughter is not known, save it was not Maris. The lord and prince were haggling over dates and [dowries](wiki:Dowry) the morning Prince [Lucerys](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon) arrived as an envoy representing the [blacks](wiki:Blacks). Lucerys claimed he could not marry Maris or any of her sisters due to his betrothal to his cousin, [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)), and was sent away by Lord Borros.(wiki:Maris_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
After Aemond and Lucerys started to confront one another, Maris taunted Aemond, asking if it was his eye that Lucerys had taken, "or one of [his] balls."(wiki:Maris_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Aemond was enraged, and took his leave. [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) claims that Prince Aemond cut out the eyes from Lucerys's corpse after killing him, and presented them to Maris on a bed of seaweed, though Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) notes that this seems excessive.(wiki:Maris_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)
By the time of King [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen)'s coronation, Maris had joined the [silent sisters](wiki:Silent_sisters). Mushroom claims that her mother, Lady [Elenda](wiki:Elenda_Baratheon), had Maris's tongue removed, but Gyldayn notes that this can be discounted, as the belief all silent sisters are tongueless is no more than a myth.(wiki:Maris_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
