## Origins

### Youth

In [175 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Brynden was born in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing)(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rawoiafbrynden_rivers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) as the [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son of King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) and his sixth mistress, Lady [Melissa Blackwood](wiki:Melissa_Blackwood), who had replaced the king's fifth mistress, Lady [Barba Bracken](wiki:Barba_Bracken).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) He was named Brynden as an infant by his mother.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Brynden's older sisters were [Mya](wiki:Mya_Rivers) and [Gwenys Rivers](wiki:Gwenys_Rivers).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Although Melissa was eventually dismissed by Aegon IV in favor of his seventh mistress, Lady [Bethany Bracken](wiki:Bethany_Bracken), Brynden was able to maintain close relations at court because of Melissa's popularity.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) He learned how the fight under the tutelage of Ser [Quentyn Ball](wiki:Quentyn_Ball), who also taught the other Great Bastards and the sons of their half-brother Daeron.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-4)

Brynden was resented by Barba's bastard son, [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers), nicknamed "Bittersteel". At some point, Brynden became a lover to his half-sister, [Shiera Seastar](wiki:Shiera_Seastar). As Aegor desired her as well, Shiera became another point of rivalry between the two half-siblings.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Brynden would become Shiera's most ardent admirer.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Shiera_20-0)

Like his other bastard siblings, Brynden was legitimized by his father Aegon IV in [184 AC](wiki:184_AC), when the king was on his deathbed.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) After succeeding as king, Brynden's half-brother King Daeron II maintained all the incomes that King Aegon had bestowed upon the Great Bastards.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

### First Blackfyre Rebellion

During the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), Brynden remained loyal to his trueborn half-brother King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) instead of joining his bastard-born half-brother, [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) Brynden commanded his private guards, a group of longbowmen called the [Raven's Teeth](wiki:Raven's_Teeth).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

At the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field) in [196 AC](wiki:196_AC), the Raven's Teeth gained the [Weeping Ridge](wiki:Weeping_Ridge) and rained arrows down onto the [Blackfyres](wiki:House_Blackfyre) from three hundred yards away, killing first Daemon's eldest son [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Blackfyre), then Daemon himself, and finally Aegon's twin, [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Blackfyre).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) The arrows are said to have been driven by Brynden's sorcery,(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) and people later named Brynden a [kinslayer](wiki:Kinslaying).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5) Daemon's death led the rebels to rout, but some were rallied by [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) in a mad charge.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11) The resulting duel between the rival half brothers left Brynden blinded in one eye and Aegor retreating.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5)

With Daemon and his eldest sons dead, Aegor fled into exile in the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) with Daemon's wife, daughters, and remaining sons.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6) After the war Brynden advocated a hard line against the rebels, winning out over Prince [Baelor Targaryen's](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)) call for leniency.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

### Reign of King Aerys I Targaryen

By the end of King Daeron II's reign, Brynden had risen to a place of prominence at court. However, few anticipated that his nephew, King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), would name him his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) upon taking the throne in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC). At the time of his appointment, Brynden's studies of the higher mysteries were an open secret and suggested by some as the reason Aerys chose him, for he shared that interest. He would also serve as Aerys's [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers) for the duration of his reign.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) His first major trial was the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness), an epidemic that had killed King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), Princes [Valarr](wiki:Valarr_Targaryen) and [Matarys Targaryen](wiki:Matarys_Targaryen), and tens of thousands more. Seeing the corpses pile up in the streets of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Brynden ordered the pyromancers of the [Alchemists' Guild](wiki:Alchemists'_Guild) to burn them in the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit). At night, the green glow of the [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) pyres could be seen throughout the whole city.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)

Around the same time, a drought began that lasted a little more than a year,(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-21) causing many [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) to leave their lands in search of somewhere the rain still fell. Brynden ordered them to return to their own lands but few obeyed. Indeed they blamed Brynden, cursing him as a kinslayer. Many of them became robbers. Prices increased as trade dwindled, and it was widely held that travel was less safe under Aerys's reign than it had been under that of his father.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14) Some people alleged that Brynden and his paramour, his half-sister [Shiera Seastar](wiki:Shiera_Seastar), used sorcery to uncover secrets.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) Brynden proposed to Shiera numerous times, but she always refused his offers of marriage.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Shiera_20-1)

In [211 AC](wiki:211_AC), Ser [Otho Bracken](wiki:Otho_Bracken), known as the Brute of [Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken), was expected to succeed to the [lordship](wiki:Lord) of [Stone Hedge](wiki:Stone_Hedge). Not wanting to see such a man in power, the traditional Bracken rivals of [House Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood) were expected to start a war to root him out. It was suggested that Brynden, being half-Blackwood himself, would not be inclined to hear any complaints of a member of House Bracken. Septon [Sefton](wiki:Sefton_Staunton) believed that Brynden would most likely do nothing, and that *if* he did decide to act, it would only be to help his Blackwood cousins to bring Otho to bay.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15)

In [211 AC](wiki:211_AC) Lord [Dagon Greyjoy](wiki:Dagon_Greyjoy) raided the western coast, including [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle), the shipping of the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor), and the village of [Little Dosk](wiki:Little_Dosk) in the [Reach](wiki:Reach).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16) By [212 AC](wiki:212_AC), Lord [Beron Stark](wiki:Beron_Stark) had called his banners to repulse the [ironmen](wiki:Ironmen) from the [Stony Shore](wiki:Stony_Shore), while [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) was building [ships](wiki:Ships) to strike at the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-6) During all of this Brynden kept his eye on [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), where his half-brother [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) remained with the surviving sons of Daemon Blackfyre in exile.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-7) However, [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) recalls that, "...even Dagon could not defeat the dragons",(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) suggesting that the Targaryens eventually intervened.

### Second Blackfyre Rebellion

During the [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC), Ser [Maynard Plumm](wiki:Maynard_Plumm) defended Brynden's actions as Hand to Ser [Kyle the Cat](wiki:Kyle_the_Cat).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-8) Through his informants,(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) including a [Vyrwel](wiki:House_Vyrwel) man-at-arms and a troupe of comic [dwarfs](wiki:Dwarf), Brynden was aware of a plot at [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls) to put [Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre) forward as the legitimate [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms). Brynden marched on the tourney with three hundred Raven's Teeth, three brothers of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), five hundred other [knights](wiki:Knight), and five thousand infantry drawn from the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) and the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands). In the face of such a force Daemon tried to rally the tourney-goers to battle, but found little support. Daemon then challenged the Hand to single combat; Brynden refused and arrested the pretender, thus containing the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-9) Daemon was kept in captivity rather than executed with the other rebel leaders, ensuring that Aegor Rivers could not name his brother [Haegon](wiki:Haegon_Blackfyre) as a serious pretender.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

### Third Blackfyre Rebellion

In the aftermath of the [Third Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Third_Blackfyre_Rebellion), Brynden argued that the captive [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) should be put to death, but King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen) decided to send Aegor to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) instead. However, Aegor Rivers escaped back to [Essos](wiki:Essos) when his ship was intercepted en route to [Eastwatch](wiki:Eastwatch).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

### Reign of King Maekar I Targaryen

Despite their history of frequent quarrels, when [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) succeeded his brother(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rawoiafbrynden_rivers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) in [221 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Brynden's history of success as Aerys's Hand made him grudgingly accept him as his own.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rawoiafbrynden_rivers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3)

### Great Council of 233 AC

After Maekar's death in [233 AC](wiki:233_AC), Brynden, as the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), called a [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to discuss the disputed matter of succession. [Aenys Blackfyre](wiki:Aenys_Blackfyre) wanted to peacefully participate in the Great Council, and Brynden offered him safe conduct to King's Landing from [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh). Once Aenys arrived in the capital, however, he was arrested by the [gold cloaks](wiki:Gold_cloaks) and then beheaded in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

The Great Council chose Maekar's youngest son to succeed as King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), whose first act was to arrest Brynden for the murder of Aenys.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) It is suggested by [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) that Brynden was imprisoned in the Red Keep's dungeons.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Brynden argued that he had sacrificed his honor for the good of the realm, but Aegon refused to set Brynden free. He instead offered the option of [taking the black](wiki:Night's_Watch) instead of death, which Brynden accepted.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

### Night's Watch

When Maester Aemon sailed for the [Wall](wiki:Wall) in [233 AC](wiki:233_AC), he was escorted by King Aegon V's friend, Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), and accompanied by an honor guard of recruits for the Night's Watch. These were two hundred men and prisoners, including Brynden Rivers. Many of the men were archers from Brynden's [Raven's Teeth](wiki:Raven's_Teeth).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6) According to a semi-canon source, Brynden took [Dark Sister](wiki:Dark_Sister) with him when he went to the Wall.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-23)

Brynden was elected as [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) in [239 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) However, in [252 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), he disappeared while ranging [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall).(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8)

## Appearances & Description

Brynden was an albino, and thus had milk-white skin, long white hair, and red eyes.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-GreatBastards_14-0) On the right side of his face he had a red winestain birthmark, that extended from his throat up to his right cheek. The birthmark was the reason why he was called *"Bloodraven"*, as it was said to look somewhat like a [raven](wiki:Raven) drawn in blood. However, Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) thought the birthmark just a blotch.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) According to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin), it is more a suggestion of a raven shape, like an ink blot.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Descriptions_15-0)

Brynden was not as tall or muscular as his half brothers, [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) and [Aegor](wiki:Aegor_Rivers),(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-GreatBastards_14-1) but he was considered more cunning.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) A shade under six feet tall, Brynden was thin and gaunt. He had a grim forbidding aspect, and sharp, vaguely sinister features.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-GreatBastards_14-2)(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Concerning_16-0) He typically wore the colors of scarlet and smoke, a dark grey mottled and streaked with black.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-GreatBastards_14-3) For instance, one of his outfits included a scarlet tunic with black boots and a smoke-colored cloak.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Because his skin was sensitive to light, Bloodraven usually went about cloaked and hooded.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-GreatBastards_14-4)

Brynden lost an eye during the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), and rarely covered the empty socket with a patch, preferring to display his scar and empty socket to the world.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Descriptions_15-1) He wore his white hair straight and to his shoulders, with the front brushed forward to cover his missing eye.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-GreatBastards_14-5) Although Brynden carried the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) [longsword](wiki:Longsword) [Dark Sister](wiki:Dark_Sister) and was a good swordsman, he was also an expert bowman who preferred to use his tall [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) longbow.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Concerning_16-1)

Brynden was rumored to be a sinister [sorcerer](wiki:Sorcerer) and [spymaster](wiki:Espionage),(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-GreatBastards_14-6)(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) who effectively ruled the kingdom "with spies and spells" and as "Lord Bloodraven".(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) A popular riddle asked was, "*How many eyes does Lord Bloodraven have? A thousand eyes, and one*".(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3) The song "[A Thousand Eyes, and One](wiki:A_Thousand_Eyes,_and_One)" was written about Brynden.(wiki:Brynden_Rivers.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
