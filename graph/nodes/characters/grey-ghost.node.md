## Origins

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) [sought new dragonriders](wiki:Sowing) to fight the [greens](wiki:Greens). Some of the [dragonseeds](wiki:Dragonseed) looked for the Grey Ghost, but could not find him, as he was ever an elusive [creature](wiki:Bestiary). [Alyn of Hull](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) first sought out Grey Ghost before attempting (and failing) to claim Sheepstealer.(wiki:Grey_Ghost.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

In [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), the crew of the *[Nessaria](wiki:Nessaria)* saw Grey Ghost and another dragon fighting in the sky above the Dragonmont. Grey Ghost was slain in the struggle. When Ser [Robert Quince](wiki:Robert_Quince) heard of Grey Ghost's death, he wrongly assumed this was the work of the Cannibal. In truth, Grey Ghost was vanquished and partially devoured by [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre) upon his return to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Grey_Ghost.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

## Appearances & Description

Grey Ghost was pale grey-white, the color of morning mist. He was a notably shy dragon, who avoided men and their works for years at a time. His avoidance of people, and his ability to hide in clouds and mist, gave him his name.(wiki:Grey_Ghost.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

Grey Ghost preferred to feed on fish, and was often glimpsed flying low over the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), snatching prey from the waters. He made his lair in a smoking vent high on the eastern side of the [Dragonmont](wiki:Dragonmont). He was never claimed nor ridden by any man, and was one of the three wild dragons of Dragonstone, along with [Sheepstealer](wiki:Sheepstealer) and the [Cannibal](wiki:Cannibal_(dragon)).(wiki:Grey_Ghost.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
