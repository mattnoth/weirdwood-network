## Origins

Following the lead of Lord [Edmyn Tully](wiki:Edmyn_Tully) of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), Lord Blackwood was among the [riverlords](wiki:Riverlands) who rose up against the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) king [Harren the Black](wiki:Harren_Hoare) and joined the siege of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) with [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) during [his conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest). Before the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal), Harren vowed that whoever could [slay](wiki:Dragonslayer) Aegon's [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) would have his choice of the riverlords' daughters to [marry](wiki:Marriage), including those of Lord Blackwood.(wiki:Lord_Blackwood_(Aegon's_Conquest).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

After the destruction of [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare), Aegon the Conqueror granted lordship of the riverlands to Edmyn Tully, and Lord Blackwood was required to swear fealty to him.(wiki:Lord_Blackwood_(Aegon's_Conquest).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Although the Blackwoods ruled over wider domains and historically fielded a much larger army than [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully),(wiki:Lord_Blackwood_(Aegon's_Conquest).cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) a private war with [House Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken) and the subsequent intervention of Harren the Black a decade prior to the conquest had greatly diminished the Blackwoods' strength, leaving Edmyn Tully the strongest of the riverlords.(wiki:Lord_Blackwood_(Aegon's_Conquest).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
