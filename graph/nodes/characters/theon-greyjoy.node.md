## Identity

Theon Greyjoy is a member of House Greyjoy, sole surviving son and heir apparent of Balon Greyjoy, Lord of the Iron Islands. Taken as a hostage and ward to Winterfell after Greyjoy's Rebellion, he was raised alongside the Stark children. He becomes a POV character in A Clash of Kings. (wiki:Theon_Greyjoy)

## Origins

Born 278–279 AC at Pyke in the Iron Islands. Son of Lord Balon Greyjoy and Lady Alannys Harlaw. (track_b: Born, Father, Mother) His older brothers Rodrik and Maron were killed during Greyjoy's Rebellion, making Theon the heir. He was taken from Pyke at age ten as a hostage by Lord Eddard Stark. (wiki:Theon_Greyjoy)

## Appearances & Description

Theon is lean, dark, and handsome with black hair and a lean dark face. He is known for his confidence and cocky smile. He is promiscuous and vain, but insecure about his familial and social identity. He wears a black velvet doublet embroidered with the Greyjoy kraken and carries a longsword in a scabbard striped black and gold. He is accomplished with the longbow. (wiki:Theon_Greyjoy)

## Narrative Arc

At Winterfell, Theon served as Robb Stark's companion and advisor. He participated in the execution of Gared, presenting Ice to Lord Eddard. He killed the wildling Stiv with an arrow to save Bran, though Robb criticized his risky shot. When war came, Theon rode south with Robb, serving as a scout under Brynden Tully and fighting at the Whispering Wood. He was present when Robb was proclaimed King in the North at Riverrun. (wiki:Theon_Greyjoy; agot-bran-01, agot-bran-05, agot-catelyn-11)

Robb sent Theon to the Iron Islands to forge an alliance with Balon Greyjoy. Balon rejected the offer and instead planned to invade the North. Humiliated by his father and sister Asha, Theon was given a minor command to harry the Stony Shore. Seeking glory, he deviated from his orders and captured Winterfell with a small force. When Bran and Rickon Stark escaped, Theon killed two miller's boys and passed them off as the Stark children. His occupation grew increasingly desperate as men abandoned him. He was betrayed when Bolton forces sacked Winterfell. (wiki:Theon_Greyjoy)

Theon was captured by Ramsay Bolton (then Snow) and subjected to prolonged torture at the Dreadfort. Ramsay broke Theon's identity, renaming him "Reek." Theon was physically mutilated — losing fingers, toes, and other body parts — and psychologically shattered. He was forced to participate in Ramsay's marriage to "Arya Stark" (actually Jeyne Poole) at Winterfell. Theon ultimately helped Jeyne escape by leaping from the walls of Winterfell into the snow, rescued by Mors Umber's men. He was brought before Stannis Baratheon at a crofter's village. (wiki:Theon_Greyjoy)
