## Origins

### Birth

Princess Daenerys was born in [172 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), some nineteen years after the birth of her brother, Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), who had been born in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the year their parents had been wed.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Cal_5-1) Daenerys had a twin brother, who was stillborn. Her mother, Queen [Naerys](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen), lingered near death due to the difficult birth, but eventually recovered.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

### Maron and Daemon

It was said that Daenerys loved her half-brother, [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre),(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and their passion was known by the whole realm.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) Neither their father, King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), nor her brother, King Daeron II, were willing to let their love dictate political matters, however. Instead, both kings negotiated matches for Daemon and Daenerys to secure ties with [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) and [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), respectively.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

Daenerys's betrothal to [Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), was brokered by King Daeron as part of the treaty bringing Dorne into the realm of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne),(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) after negotiations lasting two years.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) A year later in [187 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), when she was [of age](wiki:Age_of_majority), Daenerys's [wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Maron_Martell_and_Daenerys_Targaryen) to Maron was held in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). After the ceremony, Maron and Daeron went to pay their respects to the statue of King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) at the [sept bearing his name](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), as Baelor had once brokered the peace between the Iron Throne and Dorne (with the marriage of King Daeron II and Princess [Myriah Martell](wiki:Myriah_Martell), Maron's older sister, as a result). To celebrate the marriage, a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) was held, where Daeron's son, Prince [Baelor Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), won the name "Breakspear" and the victory, defeating Daemon in [the lists](wiki:Joust) in the final tilt.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

Daeron's arranging of Daenerys to marry Maron led to Daemon becoming the first of the [Blackfyre Pretenders](wiki:Blackfyre_Pretenders).(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-11)(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) By the time of the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion), Daemon had been married to [Rohanne of Tyrosh](wiki:Rohanne_of_Tyrosh) for years and had at least nine children with her, and Daenerys had already given Maron multiple heirs.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) Daemon's supporters claimed that he rebelled because Daenerys was denied to him.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Some Blackfyre partisans even advanced the claim that King Aegon had promised to let Daemon have Daenerys as a second wife in the [polygamous](wiki:Polygamy) tradition of their ancestors, [Aegon I](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and [Maegor I](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). Daemon did not rebel until eight years after the wedding, however, and Daenerys is not recorded to have ever mourned Daemon's death in the war.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)

### Water Gardens

[Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), built the [Water Gardens](wiki:Water_Gardens), a place of beauty made of colored [marble](wiki:Marble), with pools, fountains, and trees, to free Daenerys from the heat and dust of [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear). Their children played in the pools, along with the children of other [highborn](wiki:Nobility) [lords](wiki:Lord) and ladies. One day, when the [sun](wiki:Sun) was hot, Daenerys took pity on the children of the servants and guards, and allowed them to play in the pools with the highborn children, starting a tradition that is still kept in Dorne to this day.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) As Daenerys watched the children playing, she realized that she could not tell the highborn ones from the [lowborn](wiki:Lowborn). Naked, they were only innocent and vulnerable children, who all deserved life, love, and protection. Daenerys told [her eldest son](wiki:Maron_Martell's_son), Maron's heir, to also look at the children of the Water Gardens, and realize that they were the realm of Dorne, and to remember them in everything he did. Those words may be a tradition kept from one [Prince or Princess of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne) to their heir, to this day.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

### Legacy

Princess Daenerys was the namesake for Princess [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), the now-exiled daughter of the late King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) and Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen).(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

When she was younger, Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) spent hours gazing at the portrait of Daenerys hanging in [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear).(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Barianne_i_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Carianne_i.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

While [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) is at the [Lazy Eel](wiki:Lazy_Eel) in [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), talk amongst the patrons turns to the daughter of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), but none of the patrons are sure if her name. Davos mentions it is [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), as she was named for the Daenerys who wed [Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell) during the reign of King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Aerys's Daenerys now rules as [Queen of Meereen](wiki:Queen_of_Meereen).(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

During a feast at [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), Prince [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell) tells Ser [Balon Swann](wiki:Balon_Swann) about the [Water Gardens](wiki:Water_Gardens) and that one of his ancestors (Maron) built them to please his Targaryen bride, Daenerys. He adds that it was her marriage that made Dorne part of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). Doran also mentions how Daenerys invited the [lowborn](wiki:Lowborn) children of her servants to play with the [highborn](wiki:Highborn) children in the pools.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6) Later, in a private conversation with his daughter [Arianne](wiki:Arianne_Martell) and his nieces [Obara](wiki:Obara_Sand), [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria_Sand), and [Tyene Sand](wiki:Tyene_Sand), Doran continues the story of Daenerys and [her eldest son](wiki:Maron_Martell's_son) at the Water Gardens. Doran adds that [his mother](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne_(mother_of_Doran)) told him the same story when he was a youth.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7)

In [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), Arianne's brother, Prince [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell), tells Queen Daenerys that he can trace his lineage back to the first Daenerys.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-first_3-1) He tells her that Prince Maron had built the [Water Gardens](wiki:Water_Gardens) for his bride, though Daenerys admits to never having heard of the palace before. As they make their way back from [the pit](wiki:Pit_(Great_Pyramid)) of the [Great Pyramid](wiki:Great_Pyramid), Daenerys asks Quentyn to tell her of her namesake. She confesses that she knows less than she should of the history of her father's kingdom, as she never had a [maester](wiki:Maester) to teach her.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Radwd50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

[Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) recalls some of the great love affairs of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) history that had led to war, including the first Daenerys(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-first_3-2) and Daemon Blackfyre.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

### The Winds of Winter

Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) recalls that in her younger days, she spent hours gazing at the portrait of Princess Daenerys hanging in Sunspear, back when she was just a pudgy flat-chested girl on the cusp of [maidenhood](wiki:Maiden), who prayed every night for the [gods](wiki:Gods) to make her pretty.(wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV).cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Barianne_i_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Carianne_i.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)
