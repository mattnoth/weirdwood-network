## Origins

Some of Craster's wives are [also his daughters](wiki:Incest).(wiki:Craster's_wives.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Any [sons](wiki:Craster's_sons) are sacrificed by Craster to the [cold gods](wiki:Others) of the wood after they are born,(wiki:Craster's_wives.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) while he keeps his daughters and marries them once they are of age.(wiki:Craster's_wives.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The women wear ragged deerskins and help Craster on his homestead.(wiki:Craster's_wives.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Craster begrudgingly allows the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) to stay at [his keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep) overnight during their [great ranging](wiki:Great_ranging) in the lands [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall). The black brothers are warned beforehand not to talk to Craster's wives, although [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) beseeches [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) to help her. Sam sends her to [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), who tells her that the Night's Watch is pressing north, heading towards danger.(wiki:Craster's_wives.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) [Dywen](wiki:Dywen) boasts of having slept with three wives during the night.(wiki:Craster's_wives.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Chett](wiki:Chett) considers having Craster killed and claiming the wives for himself, but his plotting is interrupted by the [fight at the Fist](wiki:Fight_at_the_Fist).(wiki:Craster's_wives.cite_ref-Rasosprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
After the [mutiny at Craster's Keep](wiki:Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep), some of the younger wives are raped in the loft by mutineers. [Rolley of Sisterton](wiki:Rolley_of_Sisterton) falls and breaks his neck while climbing toward the women.(wiki:Craster's_wives.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) [Dyah](wiki:Dyah) catches two horses, and [Ferny](wiki:Ferny) and [Gilly's mother](wiki:Gilly's_mother) encourage Sam to flee with Gilly and her [newborn son](wiki:Monster) for the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Craster's_wives.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) The fate of the other wives is unknown.

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Aboard the [Cinnamon Wind](wiki:Cinnamon_Wind), Gilly helps Sam comfort the dying Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) by singing a nonsense song she learned from some of Craster's other wives. It soothes Aemon and helps him sleep.(wiki:Craster's_wives.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Gilly and her son make it to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) with Samwell.(wiki:Craster's_wives.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
