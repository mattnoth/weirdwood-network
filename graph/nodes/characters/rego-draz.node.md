## Origins

Rego started out with nothing, but rose to become the richest man in [Pentos](wiki:Pentos). However, due to his low birth he was largely shunned by the other wealthy Pentoshi, and denied a seat on the city's council of [magisters](wiki:Magister). Because he had enough of the scorn of the other Pentoshi, in [50 AC](wiki:50_AC) Rego was glad to accept the offer of the [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) to come to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and become the new [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) on the king's [small council](wiki:Small_council). Rego moved to King's Landing with his family and fortune, and was granted the title of [lord](wiki:Lord) by Jaehaerys. As he was a lord without lands, castle or sworn men, some wit japed he was the "Lord of Air", which amused Rego.(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

Although offered residence within the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) on many occasions,(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) Rego chose to live in a [manse](wiki:Manse) on the [Street of Silk](wiki:Street_of_Silk), below the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit). There, he regaled his concubines without having to deal with the court's condemnation of his activities.(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Rego excelled as master of coin, and was one of the key men responsible for rebuilding the realm's economy after the wars of King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) had left the crown severely in debt. He reached out to the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos), and its rivals in [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) and [Myr](wiki:Myr), arranging three substantial loans. With these loans, the construction of the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) could finally continue. Together with King Jaehaerys, Rego abolished the deeply unpopular trade taxes of Lord [Edwell Celtigar](wiki:Edwell_Celtigar), who had been the previous master of coin. In their place, they instituted a series of luxury taxes on expensive foreign imports, as well as on castle crenellations.(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

However, Draz quickly grew to be as unpopular as his predecessor had been, on account of his alleged corruption, his [religion](wiki:Religion), his ancestry, his wealth, and his foreign origins.(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) When the taxes on silk, [spice](wiki:Spice), and crenellations proved to be insufficient to cover all of the crown's expenses, Lord Rego imposed a new levy: a gate fee, required of passing the [gates of the city](wiki:King's_Landing), with additional fees for [animals](wiki:Animal), [wagons](wiki:Wagon), and [carts](wiki:Cart).(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

After [Elissa Farman](wiki:Elissa_Farman) stole three [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_eggs) from [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) and fled [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) in [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), Rego aided [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Benifer](wiki:Benifer) by reaching out to his agents across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) for any news on the eggs, [dragons](wiki:Dragon), or Elissa herself, to no avail. That same year, Rego suggested bribing some of the [Most Devout](wiki:Most_Devout) to assure that the new [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon) elected would be favorable for the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), but the suggestion was rejected by the king.(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

When several mysterious deaths on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) were put before the small council, Rego read [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Anselm](wiki:Anselm)'s accounts of the illness, and declared it to be no sickness but caused by the [tears of Lys](wiki:Tears_of_Lys), a [poison](wiki:Poison). After Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)) stepped down as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) (after his niece [Lianna](wiki:Lianna_Velaryon) was murdered on Dragonstone), King Jaehaerys initially looked to members of his small council for his replacement. However, he knew that despite Rego's qualifications, his low birth and foreign origin made him unsuitable.(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

Near the end of [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), when Princess [Aerea Targaryen](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) disappeared with the [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), Rego's agents were reassigned to find them, but not even a trace of information was found. Rego was entertaining a [prostitute](wiki:Prostitute) at his manse the day that Aerea and Balerion returned to King's Landing in [56 AC](wiki:56_AC).(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

In [57 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), after Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth) returned from [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) with a deal from the [Iron Bank](wiki:Iron_Bank) to forgive the crown's loans, Rego calculated that the coin could now be used for King Jaehaerys's project to improve King's Landing. However, Rego was reluctant to pay for Barth and Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen)'s project to dig wells and build fountains for drinking water, until Alysanne served him a tankard of foul water from the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) and dared him to drink it.(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

During the harsh [winter](wiki:Winter) of [59 AC](wiki:59_AC) and the ensuing plague of the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers), famine set in throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). So many of the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) had died of the sickness that the city became lawless outside of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). One day, as he was traveling in his palanquin through [Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom), Rego came upon a starving mob who became enraged at the sight of him. They dragged him from his palanquin and killed him, blaming him (as a foreigner) for bringing the plague to Westeros. Rego's head was bashed in using a cobblestone from one of the new streets he himself had helped pay for, and his fingers were cut off to take his jewels. Learning of Rego's death, an enraged King Jaehaerys went forth into the city with what few guards were left, found the men responsible, and had them hung from the walls of the castle with their entrails hanging out.(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

## Appearances & Description

Rego was of the typical [Pentoshi](wiki:Pentos) ethnicity: mixed [Valyrian](wiki:Valyrian), [Andal](wiki:Andal), [slaves](wiki:Slave), and ancient peoples. He showed off his wealth by wearing [silken](wiki:Silk) robes and [ruby](wiki:Ruby) rings.(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) By [59 AC](wiki:59_AC), Rego had grown quite stout, and had taken to moving between his manse and the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) in an ornate [gilded](wiki:Gold) [palanquin](wiki:Palanquin).(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

Rego was a shrewd(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) man of great ability, who earned King [Jaehaerys I](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen)'s trust and gratitude.(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Nevertheless, Rego's critics accused him of being corrupt, stealing from the king, charges which he denied, stating he himself was wealthier than the king was. He was accused of being "[godless](wiki:Religion)" because he did not worship [the Seven](wiki:The_Seven); instead, Rego worshipped a small household idol that looked like a pregnant woman with swollen breasts and a [bat](wiki:Bat)'s head.(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) He never married, but he fathered three [bastard](wiki:Bastard) sons, who learned their father's business at his knee.(wiki:Rego_Draz.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)
