## Origins

### Early life

Born in [262 AC](wiki:262_AC),(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Robert was the first born child of [Cassana Estermont](wiki:Cassana_Estermont) and [Steffon Baratheon](wiki:Steffon_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End), [Lord Paramount of the Stormlands](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Stormlands), and head of [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon). He was a scant year older than his brother, [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), who was born in [264 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-46) When Robert was already in his teens, his youngest brother, [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), was born.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-47)

According to Stannis, when Robert was five or six years old, the two brothers were brought to court by their father, Steffon, where they witnessed the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), sitting on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and holding court.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) Young Robert also played with an [Estermont](wiki:House_Estermont) [cousin](wiki:Estermont_(cousin_of_Robert)) whose father was good to the future king.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-2) Robert had a gyrfalcon named [Thunderclap](wiki:Thunderclap) who never missed her strike, and he mocked Stannis's goshawk, [Proudwing](wiki:Proudwing), as "Weakwing".(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)

From an early age onwards, Robert had been fostered by Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), together with [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). The two boys became fast friends, and Jon, who had no children of his own, became like a second father to them.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rawoiafrobert_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) During the period in which he was fostered, Robert visited [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) on multiple occasions.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rawoiafrobert_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-1) At some point, Robert was [knighted](wiki:Knight).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) During a [tourney at Storm's End](wiki:Tourney_at_Storm's_End), Robert was defeated by Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

After reaching manhood, Robert divided his time between Storm's End and the Eyrie.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rssm1077.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) In [278 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Robert's father was sent on a mission by King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), to travel to [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) to find a bride for Robert's cousin, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen). The mission failed, and Steffon, accompanied by his wife, returned home.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) Robert and Stannis watched their parents' ship, *[Windproud](wiki:Windproud)*, sink in a storm in [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay) while nearing Storm's End.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2) With his father's death, Robert became the Lord of Storm's End.

### Lyanna Stark

Robert fell in love with [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), [Eddard's](wiki:Eddard_Stark) younger sister.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-9) In time, Lyanna's father, Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), agreed to betroth her to Robert. At the time of their betrothal, Robert already had a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) daughter, [Mya Stone](wiki:Mya_Stone), in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), which led to Lyanna commenting that Robert would never keep to one bed, despite his love for her.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-1) Eddard would later state that Robert saw only Lyanna's beauty, and never recognized her boldness and strength of will.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-5)

In [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), Robert was present at the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal), where he took part in the melee.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4) He held a drinking contest with Ser [Richard Lonmouth](wiki:Richard_Lonmouth), and swore to unmask the [mystery knight](wiki:Mystery_knight) known as the [Knight of the Laughing Tree](wiki:Knight_of_the_Laughing_Tree), although he failed to do so.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) It was Robert's cousin, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), who won the final joust of the tourney. The prince crowned Lyanna—by then long betrothed to Robert—the [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty), instead of his wife, Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell). There are some who say that Robert had laughed, stating that Rhaegar had only paid Lyanna her due, though according to men who knew Robert better, he felt it had been an insult, on which he brooded long, causing him to harden against Rhaegar.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)

The next year, Prince Rhaegar kidnapped Lyanna,(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-10)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-1) which set in motion a chain of events leading to [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

### Robert's Rebellion

News of [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark)'s kidnapping led her eldest brother, [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark), and later her father, Lord [Rickard](wiki:Rickard_Stark), to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) demanding justice. Both were executed by order of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), who then demanded that the heads of Robert and [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), wards of Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). Jon refused, however, and rose up in rebellion instead, with Eddard (now [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell) following the deaths of his father and older brother) and Robert rallying the [north](wiki:North) and the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) respectively.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-2) Not all of Arryn's bannermen supported the rebellion, however. Robert [fought at Gulltown](wiki:Taking_of_Gulltown), where [Marq Grafton](wiki:Marq_Grafton) and other loyalists from the Vale blocked the port. Robert slew Marq(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) and sailed back home to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) to call his banners.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

However, some of his own remained loyal to the throne. When Robert learned that Lords [Grandison](wiki:Lord_Grandison), [Cafferen](wiki:Lord_Cafferen), and [Fell](wiki:Lord_Fell) planned to gather their hosts at [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall) in order to march on Storm's End, he rode ahead with his knights. He [fought each of the lords](wiki:Battles_at_Summerhall) in turn, defeating all three.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-1) Robert returned to Storm's End with his captives, and there won the loyalty of Lords Cafferen and Grandison, as well as, [Silveraxe Fell](wiki:Silveraxe_Fell), the son of Lord Fell whom Robert had slain.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-2)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1)

Free to march north so he could join forces with Jon Arryn and the northmen,(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Robert marched out again, leaving his younger brother [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) in charge of Storm's End. Stannis was torn between loyalty to his brother or to the king, ultimately siding with his family.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-2) Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) fell upon Robert with his van and defeated Robert's forces at the [Battle of Ashford](wiki:Battle_of_Ashford), before Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) could arrive with the main force from the [Reach](wiki:Reach).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0) Robert escaped capture with the bulk of his forces,(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rawoiafrobert_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-2) however, and continued his journey north. Aerys wanted someone young and vigorous to match Robert, and so replaced Lord [Owen Merryweather](wiki:Owen_Merryweather) with Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) as his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0) Connington personally led a mighty army into the field, giving chase to Robert, whose march became grueling.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rawoiafrobert_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-3) Wounded and separated from his men,(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1) Robert took refuge at [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-1) When Connington and his army arrived at the town and began searching the houses, the residents actively aided Robert, keeping him hidden from the king's men. In the end, they hid him in a brothel. When Lords [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) and [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) arrived with a rebel army, Robert came out of hiding and fought with Connington, almost killing him.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-2) One of Robert's [bastard](wiki:Bastard) children, [Bella](wiki:Bella), fathered upon a whore in the whorehouse, was named after the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-2)

King Aerys began to realize that Robert was the greatest threat to the Targaryen dynasty since [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), and exiled Connington, naming Lord [Qarlton Chelsted](wiki:Qarlton_Chelsted) as his Hand in Connington's stead.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) To secure the aid of [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully), Jon Arryn and Eddard Stark married Lord Hoster's daughters, [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) and [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-11) According to a semi-canon source, Robert attended [the double wedding](wiki:Weddings_of_Catelyn_and_Lysa_Tully) at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rawoiafrobert_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-4)

Robert led the rebel forces during the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), the decisive battle near war's end. Around this time, Robert proclaimed his intention to claim the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rssm1384.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) Out of the three leaders of the rebellion, Robert had the better claim,(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-6) due to the fact that his grandmother had been Princess [Rhaelle Targaryen](wiki:Rhaelle_Targaryen), the youngest daughter of King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) However, some feel that it was conquest which truly won Robert the throne, despite the [maesters](wiki:Maester) citing his blood ties to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) as justification.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0)

In [283 AC](wiki:283_AC)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-64) the rebel and loyalist forces fought at a place that became known as the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford), located on the northern bank of the [Trident](wiki:Trident).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0) Robert and Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) met on horseback in single combat, with the battle raging on all around them.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-12) Rhaegar wounded Robert(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-4) before Robert killed Rhaegar with a blow to the chest from [his warhammer](wiki:Robert's_warhammer).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-13) Eventually, the Targaryen host broke and ran.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-5) When Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) advised Robert to execute Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), a captured knight of Aerys's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), Robert refused, and instead had his own maester treat the wounds of Barristan the Bold.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-4)

Himself wounded and unable to chase the loyalists,(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-6)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Robert gave the pursuit of the remnants of the Targaryen army to Lord Stark. Leading the van, Eddard followed the loyalists back to King's Landing.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-7)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-1) However, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) had arrived earlier with an army, which began attacking the city after Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) convinced Aerys to admit them within the gates.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-2) Aerys was slain in the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing) by Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), Tywin's son and a member of the Kingsguard.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-8) After Robert arrived in King's Landing, Tywin presented him with the mutilated bodies of Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), Rhaegar's wife, and their two young children, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) and Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)). While Robert was pleased they were dead, Eddard was outraged and disgusted by the act, calling it murder, and saying the young prince and princess were no more than babes. Robert's response was, "I see no babes, only [dragonspawn](wiki:Dragon)". The argument led to Eddard leaving King's Landing the next day, to fight the final battles alone. It took the death of Lyanna and their shared grief to reunite the pair in friendship.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-9) Robert was thereafter haunted by Lyanna's memory and the desire for further vengeance against the slain Rhaegar.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-14)

### Rebellion's Aftermath

In late [283 AC](wiki:283_AC), Robert was crowned king. He chose his second father,(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-3) Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), as his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_glorious_reign.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0) Pardoning many of his former enemies, Robert appointed Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) as the [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) of his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). Despite [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) urging Robert to strip Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) of his white cloak, Robert listened to Jon's advice, and pardoned Jaime together with the [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers), Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys), and the [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester), [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle),(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0) and Jaime continued to serve in the Kingsguard along with Barristan.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) At his [coronation feast](wiki:Coronation_of_Robert_I_Baratheon), Robert joked to Jaime about the fact that Jaime was already being called "Kingslayer".(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-3) Robert allowed Ser [Ronald Connington](wiki:Ronald_Connington) to keep [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost), although Robert kept the gold(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0) and most of [House Connington](wiki:House_Connington)'s lands were granted to neighbors.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-3) Robert replaced the [skulls of the Targaryen dragons](wiki:Skulls_of_the_Targaryen_dragons) in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s throne room with banners and [tapestries](wiki:Tapestries) depicting hunting and battle.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0)

Robert tasked his brother [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) with building a new [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) and capturing [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), the last Targaryen-loyalist stronghold, where the pregnant Dowager Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) and her son [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) remained. Stannis led the [assault on Dragonstone](wiki:Assault_on_Dragonstone) some nine months(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-72) after the Trident, but Viserys and the newly-born [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) had been smuggled from the island shortly before by Ser [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry). Robert blamed Stannis for letting the Targaryen children escape, even though the two children had been spirited to safety by Targaryen loyalists shortly before Stannis had set sail.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-3)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-0)

As he now resided in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Robert granted the ancestral Baratheon seat of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) to his youngest brother, [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), while naming Stannis as [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone). Both Stannis and Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) believe that Robert intended this as a slight against Stannis,(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-4)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_73-1) but according to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin) Robert had not necessarily meant it as such.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rssm1046.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Dragonstone had traditionally been the seat of the heir to the Iron Throne, the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), during most of the Targaryen dynasty, so the then-childless Robert was granting it to his heir at that time, Stannis.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rssm1046.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Stannis became the head of [House Baratheon of Dragonstone](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_Dragonstone), with Robert heading [House Baratheon of King's Landing](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing)

Although peace returned to the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), many of those who did not support Robert referred to him as the [Usurper](wiki:Usurper). [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) never forgot or forgave that Robert condoned the murders of Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell) and her two young children, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) and Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)). The year after Robert took the throne, Elia's younger brother, Prince [Oberyn](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), began plotting a rebellion in the name of the exiled Viserys. Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), Robert's new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), travelled to Dorne, sat down with [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), and ended all talk of rebellion. Regardless, Robert never visited Dorne.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-0)

### Marriage

Though Robert had no wish to marry after [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark)'s death, the realm needed an heir, and Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) suggested that Robert marry Lady [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), the only daughter of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), to ensure political stability and Tywin's support should [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) ever attempt to win back his throne.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-7) Robert and Cersei were married in [284 AC](wiki:284_AC)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-75) in the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor),(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-1) with Robert draping her with a heavy golden cloak decorated with an [onyx](wiki:Onyx) stag.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-0) A tourney was held to celebrate [the royal wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Robert_I_Baratheon_and_Cersei_Lannister),(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-1) and [Gerion Lannister](wiki:Gerion_Lannister) gave the king a gilded dagger with an [ivory](wiki:Ivory) grip and a sapphire pommel.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-1) Robert named Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne) as [King's Justice](wiki:King's_Justice) as a gift for Tywin.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-1) On their wedding night, however, a drunken Robert accidentally whispered, "Lyanna" into Cersei's ear while [consummating the marriage](wiki:Bedding), an act that would set the tone for their marriage.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1) That was the only night when sex between Robert and Cersei was consensual.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-4)

While Robert "claimed his rights" frequently during the early years of their marriage, his [drinking](wiki:Alcohol) led to him hurting Cersei during those encounters. When she confronted him once during their first year of marriage, Robert claimed it was because of the drink, and he was not to blame. According to Robert, he did not remember anything of those nights, but according to Cersei, Robert did recall what he did to her, but felt that pretending to have forgotten was easier than facing the truth. Over time, Robert came to Cersei's bed less frequently, not even once a year.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-5) Cersei began to loathe her husband,(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2) and her cold attitude towards him created a distance between them.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-8) Robert remained in love with Lyanna for years after her death,(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0) placing further strain upon the marriage.

Cersei has had an incestuous relationship with her twin brother, [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), for years, and they continued their illicit relationship after Cersei's marriage to Robert. Although Robert often urged Cersei to join his hunts in the early years of their marriage, Cersei refused so she could discretely be with her brother.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-0) Two of Robert's [Estermont](wiki:House_Estermont) uncles had spent half a year at court following his wedding, and Robert insisted on repaying the visit. They remained on [Greenstone](wiki:Greenstone) for a fortnight. There, Robert slept with a widowed [cousin](wiki:Estermont_(cousin_of_Robert)), and upon learning this, Cersei had sex with Jaime whilst in the castle.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-3)

Cersei gave birth to three children: Prince [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), born in [286 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Princess [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), born in [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), and Prince [Tommen](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), born in [290 AC](wiki:290_AC).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-79) Though everyone, including Robert, believed them to be Robert's children, Jaime is the biological father of all three.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-2) Robert did not attend any of the births; whenever Cersei's time was due, he left for a hunt, returning only after the birth.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_80-0) Cersei became pregnant by Robert only once. Refusing to give birth to Robert's child, she had Jaime find a woman to cleanse her.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-3)

### Greyjoy's Rebellion

Six years after taking the crown, Robert joined forces with his [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), and his [Warden of the West](wiki:Warden_of_the_West), Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), to crush [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), which lasted less than a year. Robert burned down the castle of [House Botley](wiki:House_Botley) during the [battle at Lordsport](wiki:Battle_at_Lordsport).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-0) He and Ned then fought side by side during the conclusive [battle at Pyke](wiki:Battle_at_Pyke),(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-1) after which the king knighted [Jacelyn Bywater](wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-0) and Lord [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-0)

[Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), self-proclaimed [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands), submitted to Robert, who allowed the [ironman](wiki:Ironborn) to remain [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), although Eddard took Balon's heir, [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), as hostage.(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-15)(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_84-0) To celebrate his victory, Robert ordained that [a tourney](wiki:Tourney_at_Lannisport) should be held outside [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport).(wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-1)
