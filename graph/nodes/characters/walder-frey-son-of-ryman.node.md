## Origins

Black Walder is known to be resolute and easy to anger, feared by many including among his own family.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Enmity runs deep between Walder and his elder brother, [Edwyn Frey](wiki:Edwyn_Frey).(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

Black Walder refuses to marry, according to [Merrett Frey](wiki:Merrett_Frey).(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Walder is rumored to instead have maintained numerous affairs with several women, including cousins and the wives of his close relations. There is talk that he [bedded](wiki:Bedded) the wives of his brothers Edwyn ([Janyce Hunter](wiki:Janyce_Hunter)) and [Petyr](wiki:Petyr_Frey) ([Mylenda Caron](wiki:Mylenda_Caron)), his cousin [Fair Walda](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Walton)), and his half-cousin once removed [Amerei](wiki:Amerei_Frey), among others.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Black Walder is even rumored to have been unusually close with Lord Walder's seventh wife, [Annara Farring](wiki:Annara_Farring).(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) The illicit relations may cast doubt over the paternity of many of the younger Freys, including Edwyn's daughter, the young heiress [Walda Frey](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Edwyn)), as well as her cousin [Perra](wiki:Perra_Frey) (Petyr's daughter), and Lord Walder's six children with Annara.

## Appearances & Description

Black Walder is a wiry man with a black beard.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Some think he did not earn his nickname due to his hair color, but because of his irascible temper and stern attitude, inspiring terror in those around him.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

When [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark)'s army finds it necessary to cross the [Green Fork](wiki:Green_Fork), the delicate task of negotiating with Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) falls to Robb's mother, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark). Allegedly(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-8) in attendance at the [Twins](wiki:Twins) are all of Walder's sons, grandsons, and great-grandsons, including Black Walder. Catelyn negotiates passage and an alliance with [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) by promising, among other things, Robb's hand in marriage to one of Lord Walder's daughters.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### A Clash of Kings

When explaining the abundance of Walders in the [Frey family](wiki:House_Frey) to [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark), [Big Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)) mentions Black Walder, referring to him as the fourth in the line of succession.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Racok4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) After receiving the news of Ser [Stevron Frey](wiki:Stevron_Frey)'s death at [Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross), Big Walder updates the line of succession, placing Black Walder third.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Black Walder fights in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) with the army of King [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark).(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Storm of Swords

When Robb's army [storms](wiki:Storming_of_the_Crag) the [Crag](wiki:Crag), castle of [House Westerling](wiki:House_Westerling), Black Walder and [Smalljon Umber](wiki:Jon_Umber_(Smalljon)) lead scaling parties over the walls.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

After Robb marries [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling), the Freys are in uproar. Black Walder is one of the most infuriated, threatening Jeyne by telling Robb that his sisters would not mind marrying a widower. Robb wants to kill Walder for that, but Jeyne convinces him to be merciful. Afterwards, Black Walder's father, [Ryman Frey](wiki:Ryman_Frey), strikes Robb's banners, and leads his forces from the [Crag](wiki:Crag) back to the [Twins](wiki:Twins). Robb later explains to his mother, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), that if [Stevron Frey](wiki:Stevron_Frey) had still been alive, he might have been able to make amends with the Freys in his host, but that was not possible with Ryman and Black Walder leading them.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

Black Walder rides out with his father and brothers to receive the guests at the [Twins](wiki:Twins) for the wedding of [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) and [Roslin Frey](wiki:Roslin_Frey). Robb's [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf), [Grey Wind](wiki:Grey_Wind), tries to attack them, scaring the greeters' [horses](wiki:Horse) and dropping Petyr into the mud. Only Black Walder keeps his horse under control, and draws his sword until Catelyn intervenes. Ryman wonders why Jeyne is not with them, and Black Walder says that [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) will be displeased at her absence, as he had told his great-grandfather much about her.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

During the wedding, Robb comments to Catelyn that he is surprised that Black Walder behaved like a lamb. This changes when the [slaughter](wiki:Red_Wedding) begins; during the fighting, Catelyn sees Black Walder hamstring a [Vance](wiki:House_Vance) who had been wrestling Ser [Harys Haigh](wiki:Harys_Haigh).(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Afterwards, news arrives at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) that [Seagard](wiki:Seagard) surrendered to Black Walder.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

When riding to [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones), [Merrett Frey](wiki:Merrett_Frey) thinks on the ill repute of Black Walder, and of his illicit relationships with his brothers' wives and other female family members.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

### A Feast for Crows

After resolving the [siege of Seagard](wiki:Siege_of_Seagard) by threatening to kill the captive [Patrek Mallister](wiki:Patrek_Mallister),(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Black Walder leads the search for the [outlaws](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) that hanged [Petyr](wiki:Petyr_Frey) and [Merrett Frey](wiki:Merrett_Frey) at Oldstones. He [sharply questions](wiki:Torture) some [peasants](wiki:Smallfolk) in [Hag's Mire](wiki:Hag's_Mire) until they confess that the outlaws' leader is [Lady Stoneheart](wiki:Lady_Stoneheart). Black Walder and his [hounds](wiki:Dog) track her and her men north of Hag's Mire, but the [outlaws](wiki:Outlaw) disappear into the [Neck](wiki:Neck).(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

When [Daven Lannister](wiki:Daven_Lannister) talks with his cousin, [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), about Daven's upcoming wedding to a Frey girl, he comments that he has learned from [Edwyn Frey](wiki:Edwyn_Frey) that he should pick one not yet [flowered](wiki:Flowered), or else Black Walder will have had her first.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

When Ser Jaime Lannister attempts to end the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), he suggests to Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully) that they resolve it with single combat, and Brynden wonders if Black Walder will be Jaime's champion, among other options.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

After Ser [Ryman Frey](wiki:Ryman_Frey)'s death while returning to the Twins, Ser Edwyn Frey confesses to Jaime that he suspects Black Walder had a hand in their father's death, and he is afraid that he will be the next, as he is now the only one between Black Walder and the succession to [Lord of the Crossing](wiki:Lord_of_the_Crossing). He also thinks Black Walder let the outlaws escape on purpose.(wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman).cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
