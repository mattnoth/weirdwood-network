## Origins

Lord Bartimos was a member of Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen)'s [black council](wiki:Black_council) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). He urged the queen to use her numerical advantage in [dragons](wiki:Dragon) to take [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) from King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), but Rhaenyra agreed with the cautious Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen).(wiki:Bartimos_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) After Rhaenyra [seized control of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing), Bartimos was named [lord treasurer](wiki:Lord_treasurer) and [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) on her [small council](wiki:Small_council).(wiki:Bartimos_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
Despite the fact that King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) had left the treasure vaults full of gold upon his death, the treasury was completely empty by the time Rhaenyra took the capital, for the [greens](wiki:Greens) had sent three quarters of the gold away for safekeeping and spent the rest prior to the fall of the capital. Rhaenyra was in desperate need of [coin](wiki:Coin) and to rectify this, Lord Bartimos put in place the ruinous taxes his ancestor Lord [Edwell Celtigar](wiki:Edwell_Celtigar) once enacted, although Bartimos went a step further. Taxes on [wine](wiki:Wine) and [ale](wiki:Ale) were doubled, port fees tripled. Every shopkeeper was assessed a fee to keep his doors open. Innkeepers were required to pay a silver stag for each bed in their inn. The entry and exit fees once put on the seven city gates by [Rego Draz](wiki:Rego_Draz), the "Lord of Air", were brought back, but at triple the cost. A property tax was put in place that [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) and wealthy alike were obliged to pay depending on how much space they took up. Bartimos even made executions a source of coin, by having them take place in the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) and charging those wishing to see the spectacle three pennies. Merchants and traders who couldn't pay had their property, [ships](wiki:Ships), or cargo seized. Though these taxes replenished Queen Rhaenyra's coffers, they quickly made both the queen and Lord Bartimos despised by the populace.(wiki:Bartimos_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
After the [Treasons of Tumbleton](wiki:Treasons_of_Tumbleton) by the [Two Betrayers](wiki:Two_Betrayers), Bartimos urged Rhaenyra to arrest the other two [baseborn](wiki:Baseborn) [dragonriders](wiki:Dragonrider), Ser [Addam Velaryon](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) and [Nettles](wiki:Nettles).(wiki:Bartimos_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Bartimos_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) He later argued for a tax to be placed for any child born out of wedlock, which would help fill the royal coffers and would discourage more bastards being born. When [riots](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) broke out in King's Landing, Lord Bartimos's [manse](wiki:Manse) was overrun by a mob, his servants turned on him or fled, and his kinsman [Arthor](wiki:Arthor_Celtigar) was cut down. Bartimos himself was bound to a post and tortured until he revealed all the locations of his wealth. Then a tanner named [Wat](wiki:Wat_the_Tanner) declared to Bartimos that he had not paid the "cock tax" and thus his manhood was forfeit. Wat the Tanner later rode through the streets upon a stolen horse, displaying Lord Bartimos's severed head and genitals to the crowds and declaring an end to all taxes.(wiki:Bartimos_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
