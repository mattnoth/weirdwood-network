## Origins

Hugh squired for Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) for four years. He desperately wanted to be a [knight](wiki:Knight) and was knighted by King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) in Jon's memory upon his death.(wiki:Hugh_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Ser Hugh stayed in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) after Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn)'s return to the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn).(wiki:Hugh_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) He has nobody close to him, except his mother in the Vale.(wiki:Hugh_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

When Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) complains to Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) that his investigation into [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn)'s death is hindered by the fact that [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) took most of her household back to the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), Petyr tells him that, among others, Jon's squire is still in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), a newly-knighted boy called Ser Hugh of the Vale. Warning that Eddard is watched by spies of Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) and Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), Petyr advises the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to send someone he trusts to question Hugh, rather than go himself.(wiki:Hugh_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Hugh is brusque and unhelpful to the more experienced [Jory Cassel](wiki:Jory_Cassel), [captain](wiki:Captain_of_the_guard) of Ned's guard, and the arrogant knight insists he will only speak with the Hand.(wiki:Hugh_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Hugh takes part in the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney), wearing armor that he has had forged for the occasion,(wiki:Hugh_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) and he tilts against Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) in the Mountain's second joust. Gregor's lance rides up and drives through Hugh's throat, killing him instantly and causing [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole) to weep hysterically.(wiki:Hugh_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) stands vigil for Hugh in lack of any others being close to the youth. Barristan tells Eddard that, as a squire, Hugh desperately wanted to be knighted, but was probably not ready for it. The death by the hand of a [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) bannerman makes Eddard wonder whether Hugh has died because of his attempt to question him, but he doubts that he will ever find out the truth. Eddard orders that the armor be sent with Hugh's remains to the mother in the Vale, and Eddard announces he will make sure the armor's [smith](wiki:Smith) has been paid.(wiki:Hugh_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

In a conversation with Eddard, Varys mentions Hugh as a possible suspect for [poisoning](wiki:Poison) Jon. Varys points out that, although he owed everything to Lord Arryn, Hugh did not return to the Vale when Lysa fled with her household, instead remaining in King's Landing and rising to knighthood.(wiki:Hugh_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

### A Clash of Kings

When [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) questions [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) about his role in the death of Lord Arryn, Pycelle denies it was he who poisoned the Hand of the King. Pycelle admits he withheld care from Jon, as he knew Queen Cersei wanted Jon dead. Pycelle believes Varys's version that it was Hugh who slipped the poison to Jon and suspects that he acted on Cersei's orders.(wiki:Hugh_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
