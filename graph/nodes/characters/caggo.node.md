## Origins

Ser [Lucifer Long](wiki:Lucifer_Long) resents that Caggo once took a [slave](wiki:Slave) girl from the [knight](wiki:Knight), and the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) then killed the girl because he considered her ugly.(wiki:Caggo.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

## Appearances & Description

Caggo is a huge man with a seamed, scarred face.(wiki:Caggo.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Although Caggo is quick to anger, he is well regarded by the [Tattered Prince](wiki:Tattered_Prince), the leader of the [Windblown](wiki:Windblown).(wiki:Caggo.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Caggo rides a monstrous warhorse and wields a rare *[arakh](wiki:Arakh)* of [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel).(wiki:Caggo.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

Caggo participates in the [siege of Astapor](wiki:Siege_of_Astapor), during which he hacks down [Cleon](wiki:Cleon), the Butcher King of [that city](wiki:Astapor). Caggo becomes known as the Corpsekiller, however, when it is revealed that Cleon was just a corpse in armor tied to a horse.(wiki:Caggo.cite_ref-Radwd25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

Caggo, [Denzo D'han](wiki:Denzo_D'han), and [Meris](wiki:Meris) are present when the [Tattered Prince](wiki:Tattered_Prince) meets Prince [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell) and his companions, Ser [Archibald Yronwood](wiki:Archibald_Yronwood) and Ser [Gerris Drinkwater](wiki:Gerris_Drinkwater), in the undercellar of the [Purple Lotus](wiki:Purple_Lotus) in [Meereen](wiki:Meereen). They agree to Quentyn's proposal to steal one of the [dragons](wiki:Dragon) of [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), Queen of Meereen.(wiki:Caggo.cite_ref-Radwd60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Disguised with the cobra mask of a [Brazen Beast](wiki:Brazen_Beast), Caggo beheads a guard and calls for his sellswords to bring chains for the dragons.(wiki:Caggo.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) The plan is ruined, however, when Quentyn is mortally wounded by [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire), and the sellswords scatter to safety.(wiki:Caggo.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Caggo.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
