## Identity

Alysanne Targaryen (36–100 AC) — child of Aenys I Targaryen and Alyssa Velaryon; married to Jaehaerys I Targaryen; sworn to House Targaryen; Princess.

## Origins

### Youth

Alysanne was born to Prince [Aenys Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) and Lady [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) in [36 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). She had four older siblings - [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), and [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) - and one younger sibling, [Vaella](wiki:Vaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) According to legend, her sister Rhaena placed a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) in Alysanne's cradle, from which eventually the [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing) hatched.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), following the death of her grandfather, King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone),(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) her father Aenys ascended the throne.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Followers of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) began an uprising after King [Aenys I](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) married Alysanne's two eldest siblings, Rhaena and Aegon, to one another in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC). After [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) scaled the walls of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) in an attempt to murder the royal family, Alysanne fled with her family to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) By the end of the year most of the realm had joined the side of the Faith, and King Aenys, unable to decide how to deal with the rebels, fell ill. In [42 AC](wiki:42_AC), he collapsed when learning that Rhaena and Aegon were besieged at [Crakehall](wiki:Crakehall), and died three days later.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) Alysanne was present on Dragonstone for her father's cremation.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) Following Aenys's cremation,(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) Dowager Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) brought her son [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) back from his exile. At Dragonstone, Maegor claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) for his own, ignoring the claim of Alysanne's eldest brother Aegon. Before he could arrive, however, Alysanne's mother took her and her brothers Viserys and Jaehaerys to [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark). Late that year, they traveled to King's Landing for the wedding of King Maegor I to [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower). Following the wedding, Alysanne, Jaehaerys, and their mother were kept on Dragonstone, prisoners in all but name, by Dowager Queen Visenya, while Alysanne's brother Viserys was kept in King's Landing by Maegor.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

Alysanne, Jaehaerys, and Dowager Queen Alyssa escaped Dragonstone in [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), in the chaos following Visenya's death.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) As the realm slowly began to turn against Maegor, Alysanne's only brother [Jaehaerys put forward his claim for the throne](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen's_uprising), as their elder brothers had both died during the years before; Aegon had been killed in [battle](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye) by Maegor in [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), Viserys had been [tortured](wiki:Torture) to death by Maegor in retaliation for the escape of Alysanne, Jaehaerys and Alyssa from Dragonstone.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) Alysanne stood beside Jaehaerys as he vowed to end Maegor's reign at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). Following Maegor's mysterious death in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), Alysanne, Jaehaerys, and their sister Rhaena flew on their dragons to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to claim the Iron Throne for Jaehaerys.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

Alysanne would serve as a counselor to her brother throughout his reign. She urged him to allow the five surviving [knights](wiki:Knight) of Maegor's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), instead of executing them. When Jaehaerys allowed it, four of five took the black.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Alysanne was one of many of the royal court who accompanied Jaehaerys to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) in late [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), where she attended his coronation in the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) The next year, Alysanne entertained the noble ladies who came to visit King's Landing for [the wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) of her mother, Dowager Queen Alyssa Velaryon, to Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

### Early marriage

Following Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) and Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon)'s [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding), the [small council](wiki:Small_council) began to debate about potential spouses for Jaehaerys and Alysanne. Still recalling the [uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) of the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) that had followed the wedding of her children [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) and [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), Alyssa decreed Alysanne and Jaehaerys should not be wed to one another, and after Septon [Mattheus](wiki:Mattheus) agreed that such a marriage would likely again inflame the pious of the realm, the council agreed to find other spouses for the siblings. The council was quick to agree on a husband for Alysanne: Ser [Orryn Baratheon](wiki:Orryn_Baratheon).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

Alysanne soon learned about the council's decision. According to some, a servant who had been present for the council's discussions warned her, while others claim that it had been her uncle, Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)). Alysanne informed Jaehaerys, who acted immediately, ordering his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) to discreetly travel to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) during the night, while he and Alysanne flew together to the island upon their [dragons](wiki:Dragon). On Dragonstone, Alysanne and Jaehaerys were married in secret, with Septon [Oswyck](wiki:Oswyck) performing the ceremony. As Jaehaerys refused to consummate the marriage, believing Alysanne to still be too young, Alysanne remained a maiden. Lord Rogar and Queen Alyssa arrived on the island soon after the wedding. Upon learning that the marriage had not yet been consummated, Rogar ordered his men to secure the two children, but was defied by the Kingsguard, who formed a wall in front of their king and queen. While Alyssa and Rogar departed, defeated, Alysanne and Jaehaerys remained behind on Dragonstone, where they would spend the rest of the king's minority, spending nearly every hour in each other's company.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)

Although they had the means to do so, Alysanne and Jaehaerys did not announce their marriage to the realm. Meanwhile, the king's council at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) made no announcement either, but instead began to consider ways to undo the marriage.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8) On the seventh day of the second moon of [50 AC](wiki:50_AC), several ladies-in-waiting departed King's Landing to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) on the orders of Dowager Queen Alyssa. Although Alyssa sent them under the cover of serving her daughter and being her household, their true purpose was to try and persuade Alysanne to rescind her marriage. Lord Rogar approved his wife's plans, as he wanted eyes and ears on Dragonstone, and saw these women as an opportunity to learn all Jaehaerys was doing. Despite her youth, however, Queen Alysanne charmed all her ladies-in-waiting, who soon became completely loyal to her.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)

On the twentieth day of the ninth month of [50 AC](wiki:50_AC), Jaehaerys [came of age](wiki:Age_of_majority).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5) He flew his dragon to King's Landing to claim the throne soon thereafter, while Alysanne remained on Dragonstone. She followed him nigh on half a year later, flying from Dragonstone to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) on [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing). A month later, Alysanne and Jaehaerys married in a small, public ceremony; this time the ceremony was followed by a consummation.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

### First royal progress

In [51 AC](wiki:51_AC), a pregnant Alysanne accompanied [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) on his first royal progress, touring the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands). At [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) Alysanne held the first of her famous women's courts, where she heard women of all ages and births bring her their fears, hopes and consternations. These courts would be held every time the queen visited a castle, town or city. The progress continued to the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), where Alysanne and Jaehaerys eventually arrived at [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool). There, the pregnant Alysanne wished to bathe in [Jonquil's Pool](wiki:Jonquil's_Pool). Jaehaerys's [Doctrine of Exceptionalism](wiki:Doctrine_of_Exceptionalism) had won over most of the pious in the realm, but not all. Some of the women who tended Jonquil's Pool believed that the pool's sacred waters would become polluted if the queen, pregnant with the king's ["abomination"](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), were to enter the waters. While she was inside, Alysanne was attacked by three of these women with daggers. As only women were allowed in the bathhouse, Alysanne was accompanied only by her maids and septas, who did not hesitate to protect Alysanne by stepping between Alysanne and her would-be assassins. Their shouts alarmed the two [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) knights who stood outside, who burst in and slew two of the attackers, sparing the third for questioning. Under questioning, the remaining would-be assassin revealed the names of half a dozen more in the order who had helped plan the assault. Lord [Mooton](wiki:House_Mooton) hanged the guilty women of the order, and planned to hang the innocent ones as well; Alysanne convinced him otherwise.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Alysanne returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), where she remained until she went into early labor in [52 AC](wiki:52_AC), giving birth to a [son](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), who died three days after being born. Alysanne blamed the early birth and subsequent death of her child on the women who had attacked her at Jonquil's Pool at [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), believing that if she had she been allowed to bathe in the pool and its healing waters, her babe would have lived. Alysanne insisted that she needed a female protector, who could accompany her in places where men were not allowed to go. As such, Jaehaerys sent a raven to [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) to summon [Jonquil Darke](wiki:Jonquil_Darke), who was subsequently appointed as Alysanne's [sworn shield](wiki:Sworn_shield).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

### Early challenges

When the [High Septon](wiki:High_Lickspittle) passed away in [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), Alysanne and King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) flew to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) on their [dragons](wiki:Dragons), accompanied by [Jonquil Darke](wiki:Jonquil_Darke) and Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett). There, they hoped to influence the election the new [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon). In this they succeeded. They visited several castles on their way home. While at [Blackhaven](wiki:Blackhaven), Alysanne and Jaehaerys learned that their pregnant mother, [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), was on her deathbed. Alysanne and Jaehaerys immediately flew to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), where they arrived in time to see their mother before she died delivering a [daughter](wiki:Jocelyn_Baratheon).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)

That same year, after several of close friends of [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) were poisoned on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) by Rhaena's husband [Androw Farman](wiki:Androw_Farman), Alysanne traveled to the island in an attempt to comfort her sister. Instead, Rhaena attempted to send her away on three occasions. When Alysanne refused to leave, Rhaena retreated to her chambers, while Alysanne met with her niece, [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen), who begged Alysanne to take her back to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with her. Alysanne brought Aerea's request to Rhaena, who refused coldly, stating that Rhaena had nothing and Alysanne had everything, but would not have Rhaena's daughter too, concluding that "*you have my throne, content yourself with that*". Alysanne left soon after.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-3)

After Aerea had escaped Dragonstone atop [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), Alysanne prayed daily for her, blaming herself, but mostly Rhaena, for Aerea's flight. In [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Alysanne, together with Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth), Grand Maester [Benifer](wiki:Benifer), and Lord [Albin Massey](wiki:Albin_Massey), began to aid Jaehaerys with codifying, organizing, and reforming all the kingdom's laws. The project would take decades. That same year, Alysanne traveled to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) where she gave birth to [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)). Alysanne was displeased when Jaehaerys began to refer to Aemon as his heir, as she believed their daughter [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Jaehaerys_I)) should be the first in line for the throne, as she was the eldest of the two. Although Jaehaerys would always reply that Daenerys would be queen when she wed Aemon, the answer never truly pleased Alysanne.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

In [56 AC](wiki:56_AC), Balerion suddenly appeared at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with the severely ill Princess Aerea on his back. As Grand Maester [Benifer](wiki:Benifer) and Septon Barth attempted to save her, Alysanne and Jaehaerys stood vigil outside the chamber.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) The next year, Alysanne supported Septon Barth's plan of constructing wells, pipes, tunnels, and cisterns to provide King's Landing with fresh, clean water. Barth pointed out to Jaehaerys that the defouled river water was the only source of water for the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), suggesting his plans would provide the smallfolk with clean drinking water instead. When the king and his [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), [Rego Draz](wiki:Rego_Draz), balked at the costs, Alysanne served them a tankard of river water and challenged them to drink it. Instead of drinking the water, the king and his master of coin approved the construction of the fountains, which would become known as "*the queen's fountains*".(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) That same year, Alysanne gave birth to another son, [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), just before spring broke.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3)

### Progress to the North

In [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) planned to visit the [north](wiki:North) on a royal progress together with Alysanne, intent on visiting [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). However, when Jaehaerys was detained at King's Landing, Alysanne suggested she could go on the progress as planned to prevent Lord [Alaric Stark](wiki:Alaric_Stark) from taking offense, with Jaehaerys catching up with her as soon as possible. As such, Alysanne traveled upon the back of [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing) to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), with the royal retinue later joining her by ship. At [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), Alysanne was hosted by Lord [Theomore Manderly](wiki:Theomore_Manderly). She held a women's court at Lord Manderly's hall, where two hundred women laid their grievances before her. A [tourney](wiki:Tourney) was held in her honor, and Alysanne's sworn sword [Jonquil Darke](wiki:Jonquil_Darke) sparred with a [wildling](wiki:Wildling) woman during the event, much to the approval of the northmen. Next, Alysanne traveled to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), where she was coldly received by Lord Alaric Stark. However, due to the queen's charm and wit, he warmed to her during her stay, while Alysanne in turn grew close to Alaric's daughter, [Alarra Stark](wiki:Alarra_Stark).

According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), Alysanne grew restless of waiting for Jaehaerys at Winterfell and decided to travel to [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) at the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-4) [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) however, claims that Alysanne grew bored only after Jaehaerys had arrived at the north, and flew to the Wall while Jaehaerys discussed matters with his [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) According to Bran Stark and his [bastard](wiki:Bastard) brother [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), Alysanne stayed in several local villages while flying north. One such establishment, [Queenscrown](wiki:Queenscrown), changed its name following Alysanne's visit in her honor, and painted the merlons atop the holdfast golden to look like the golden crown she had worn during her visit.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

During her time with the Night's Watch, Alysanne saw groups of [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) when the Lord Commander presented her with his captives. She insisted on visiting other castles alongside the Wall as well. In addition, she attempted to fly Silverwing north [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall) on three occasions. The dragon's refusal to go was troubling to Alysanne.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-5)

Alysanne suggested to the [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) that the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort), the oldest and largest castle at the Wall, which was too costly to maintain, be replaced with a smaller castle nearby. She used her own jewels (including her own crown) to finance the construction of the castle, which was named [Deep Lake](wiki:Deep_Lake).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-6) King Jaehaerys later sent men north to build the castle.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) A statue of Alysanne was constructed in front of the castle. Furthermore, Alysanne pressured Lord Commander Burley to escort her to [Mole's Town](wiki:Mole's_Town), where she held one of her women's courts amongst the whores who lived there.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-7)

Lord Alaric was not pleased by the notion of having to force his bannermen to give up parts of their lands, but Alysanne charmed him into doing so either way.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-8) According to Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), however, this granting of the [New Gift](wiki:New_Gift) was not taken well by all the Starks,(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-22) and [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) believes that this might have influenced Lord [Ellard Stark](wiki:Ellard_Stark)'s choice of support during the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) The Night's Watch, on the other hand, was grateful. To thank Alysanne for financing Deep Lake and winning them the New Gift, the Night's Watch renamed the castle Snowgate, dubbing it [Queensgate](wiki:Queensgate) in her honor.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

From Winterfell, Alysanne and Jaehaerys traveled to [Torrhen's Square](wiki:Torrhen's_Square) and [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton), after which they returned to King's Landing.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-9) Back at King's Landing, Alysanne made a plea before the [small council](wiki:Small_council). During her time in the [North](wiki:North), she had heard tales from women during her women's courts concerning the [first night](wiki:First_night), and the horrifying experience it was for the women who suffered through it. During the council session, with the help of Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth), she convinced the king and his counselors, to abolish the [right](wiki:Custom) of the [first night](wiki:First_night).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-10) This became known as the second of [Queen Alysanne's laws](wiki:Queen_Alysanne's_laws).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-11) By this time, Alysanne was overwhelming loved by the people in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), especially the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-5)

### Motherhood

In late [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Alysanne attended the [tournament held in celebration of King Jaehaerys's tenth year on the throne](wiki:Tourney_at_King's_Landing_on_the_anniversary_of_the_king's_coronation). She was crowned as the [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty) by the victor, Ser [Ryam Redwyne](wiki:Ryam_Redwyne).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-12) The next year, the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) were struck with a harsh winter and the deadly plague known as the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers). The plague lasted for more than a year and many succumbed to the disease, including Alysanne's daughter [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Jaehaerys_I)), who died in [60 AC](wiki:60_AC). Alysanne stopped attending council meetings for a while after this. Within two months of Daenerys's death, Alysanne discovered she was pregnant, and in late [60 AC](wiki:60_AC) gave birth on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) to another daughter, [Alyssa](wiki:Alyssa_Targaryen), named after Alysanne's [mother](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)

Other children followed during the next few years. In [62 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Alysanne gave birth to a daughter, named [Maegelle](wiki:Maegelle_Targaryen). The next year, a son, [Vaegon](wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen), followed, and in [64 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) another daughter, [Daella](wiki:Daella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Jaehaerys_I)). Two more daughters followed a few years later: [Saera](wiki:Saera_Targaryen) in [67 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and [Viserra](wiki:Viserra_Targaryen) in [71 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). In [73 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Alysanne and Jaehaerys announced that their ten year-old daughter Maegelle would join the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). That same year, Alysanne gave birth to another son, [Gaemon](wiki:Gaemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)). However, Gaemon was born early, and the labor was so long and difficult that the maesters feared for Alysanne's life. Although Alysanne recovered from the birth, Gaemon died within three months of his birth, in the early days of [74 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Three years later, Alysanne gave birth to her twelfth child, a son who she named [Valerion](wiki:Valerion_Targaryen). This labor was difficult as well, and as a result Alysanne was bedridden for half a year. Valerion himself was small and sickly, like Gaemon had been, and died the next year, a fortnight before his first [nameday](wiki:Nameday). Alysanne took his death with resignation and declared that she was more suited to be a grandmother now than a mother. Regardless, in [80 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), during winter, Alysanne gave birth to her thirteenth and final child, [Gael](wiki:Gael_Targaryen). Though small and frail at birth as well, Gael lived, to Alysanne's relieve and joy.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8)

Alysanne became a grandmother for the first time with the birth of Princess [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)), the daughter of Alysanne's son [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)) and her half-sister [Jocelyn Baratheon](wiki:Jocelyn_Baratheon), in [74 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). The marriage of her children [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)) and [Alyssa](wiki:Alyssa_Targaryen) yielded three additional grandchildren: [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), born in [77 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), born in [81 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Baelon)), born in [84 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-9)

### First and Second Quarrel

In [80 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), following threats made by King [Jaehaerys I](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), Alysanne finally succeeded, after several failed attempts, in arranging a marriage for her daughter Daella, who was wed to Lord [Rodrik Arryn](wiki:Rodrik_Arryn) in early [81 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). After a year and a half of marriage, Daella wrote to Alysanne to inform her of her pregnancy, telling her mother that she was frightened and asking her to come. Alysanne flew to the [Vale](wiki:Vale), arriving three months before Daella was due. Alysanne remained with Daella until she gave birth. Daella went into labor a fortnight too early, and had a long and troubled labor. Although her daughter, [Aemma Arryn](wiki:Aemma_Arryn), was healthy, Daella soon fell ill and died. According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), Daella’s death was the first hint of the rift that was to open between Alysanne and Jaehaerys, as Alysanne believed Daella had been pushed into marriage at too early an age, and blamed Jaehaerys for insisting their daughter was wed this young. Alysanne's older daughter [Alyssa](wiki:Alyssa_Targaryen) died in childbirth two years later.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-10)

In [84 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Alysanne and Jaehaerys learned that their daughter [Saera](wiki:Saera_Targaryen) had bedded three young [knights](wiki:Knight). When Saera told her parents that she could wed all three of them, comparing herself to King [Maegor the Cruel](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) and his many wives, Jaehaerys had her confined to her bedchamber. Furious and ashamed, Jaehaerys was said to have stated more than once that "*she is no longer my daughter*". Alysanne agreed that Saera had to be punished for her actions, but argued for Jaehaerys to allow for her to redeem herself as well with soothing words. That night, however, Saera escaped the castle and fled to the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit), intent on stealing a [dragon](wiki:Dragon). As punishment, Saera was sent to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) to serve the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) as a novice with the [silent sisters](wiki:Silent_sisters). Saera escaped to [Lys](wiki:Lys) the next year, where she became a [prostitute](wiki:Prostitute) working in a pleasure garden. Alysanne despaired when she heard the news and proclaimed that the Lyseni had turned their daughter into a whore, Jaehaerys replied: "*She always was*", which further devasted Alysanne.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-11)

In [87 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Alysanne arranged the betrothal of her daughter [Viserra](wiki:Viserra_Targaryen) to Lord [Theomore Manderly](wiki:Theomore_Manderly). The night before she was to take ship, however, Viserra died in a drunken race through the city. In pain over the deaths of three daughters (Daella, Alyssa, and Viserra) in five years time, Alysanne begged Jaehaerys to bring Saera home, saying that she needed her daughter. Jaehaerys refused, however, and warned her against flying to Lys to retrieve Saera herself. Jaehaerys instead told her that "*[Saera] is dead. bury her*". This led to the couple's [first big rift](wiki:First_Quarrel), which would last for two years, during which time Alysanne resided on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) while Jaehaerys traveled the realm. They were finally reconciled after their daughter Maegelle persuaded King Jaehaerys to reconcile with her. A fortnight later, both Jaehaerys and Alysanne finally returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-12)

In [92 AC](wiki:92_AC), Prince [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), Alysanne's eldest son and the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), was killed by a [Myrish](wiki:Myr) pirate on [Tarth](wiki:Tarth). When King Jaehaerys named Aemon's brother [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)) as the new Prince of Dragonstone and heir to the throne instead of Aemon's only child, Princess Rhaenys, there were some who voiced their objections. Alysanne, angered about the fact that Rhaenys was passed over on account of her sex, was the most prominent amongst them. The disagreement over Jaehaerys's decision led to the [Second Quarrel](wiki:Second_Quarrel), which began when Alysanne left for Dragonstone after telling Jaehaerys that, if he indeed believed women lacked the wit to rule, he had no need of her. Although they were reconciled again two years later, once more by their daughter Maegelle, they never agreed on the matter of the succession.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-6)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Despite all of this, before Alysanne's death, she told Baelon that he would be a great king, greater even than Jaehaerys.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-13)

### Final Years

As she aged, the joys in Alysanne's life were slowly taken from her. She became too unsteady to fly; Alysanne flew [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing) for the final time in [93 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and wept in pain when she climbed from the dragon's back. Two years later, Alysanne slipped and broke her hip, and had to use a cane to walk thereafter. Her hearing began to fail, leaving her unable to enjoy music anymore, or participate in discussions of the king's [small council](wiki:Small_council). Her memory also began to have trouble, leaving her unable to recall the names of all the new people in the court.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-14)

In [98 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), a great tourney was held in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to celebrate King Jaehaerys's fiftieth year on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Most(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-26) of Alysanne and Jaehaerys's living children, grandchildren, and great-grandchildren were present, gladdening Alysanne's heart.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-7)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Alysanne's youngest and most favored daughter, [Gael](wiki:Gael_Targaryen), was her only true companion in her declining years. Besides Gael, Alysanne had outlived all but three of her children ([Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), [Vaegon](wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen) and [Saera](wiki:Saera_Targaryen)). However, Alysanne's letters to Saera all went unanswered, and Vaegon wrote to his mother dutifully if coldly, but she had not seen him in years. Baelon visited Alysanne often, but as the king's heir he was frequently busy and away to treat with lords. Tragically, Gael was seduced and impregnated by a traveling singer, and after the birth of a stillborn son, she committed suicide in [99 AC](wiki:99_AC). Gael's death broke Alysanne, and she was no longer able to bear living at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). Alysanne returned to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), where she had spent the happiest days of her life, and Jaehaerys joined her there when he could.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-15)

Queen Alysanne Targaryen died of a wasting [illness](wiki:Illness)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) on the first day of the seventh month of [100 AC](wiki:100_AC).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-16) She was cremated, and her ashes were interred on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-ashes_7-1)

### Legacy

Jaehaerys outlived Alysanne by a few years. She had been his great love, and it was said that the grief caused by their parting hung over his court,(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-8) reducing him to a shell of the man he had once been.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) After his death in [103 AC](wiki:103_AC), he too was cremated, and his ashes interred with hers.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-9)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

There is a sad song, "[Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_(song))", that may be about the queen.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) The fleet of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) contains a war galley called *[Queen Alysanne](wiki:Queen_Alysanne)*.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Good Queen Alysanne is remembered in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) with great love by the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) and others,(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2) and [her name](wiki:Alysanne) is one of the most popular in Westeros. However, some nobles, such as Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), still bear a grudge against Alysanne for her abolition of the [first night](wiki:First_night).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

## Appearances & Description

Alysanne was a small woman,(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) slim of waist and slight of frame.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) According to a semi-canon source, she was small of breast, with a long neck, a fair complexion, a high forehead, and high cheekbones.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-GoodQueen_9-0) Alysanne was described as "pretty", but seldom as "beautiful". She had blue eyes and honey-colored curls.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-10)(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Alysanne often wore a slimmer, more feminine version of her brother-husband's [crown](wiki:Crown) at court.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-GoodQueen_9-1)

Alysanne was bright but unremarkable as a young girl. She was courteous and biddable, and had a sweet smile and a pleasing voice. As a child, she was neither timid nor did she have a willful and stubborn temperament.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) According to Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon), Alysanne was a proud girl at the age of thirteen.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) She was considered to be gentle, kind and loving.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Alysanne loved her subjects, both highborn and lowborn,(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) and was well loved throughout the Seven Kingdoms in return.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) She loved her [women's courts](wiki:Women's_courts), where she listened to and learned from the women of the realm. She loved music, dancing, reading, and flying on her dragon.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) She was a fearless woman.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) According to a semi-canon source, Alysanne was a fine archer and hunter in her youth, and became renowned for her charities.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-GoodQueen_9-2)

Later in life, it was said that Alysanne had learned to read before she had been weaned. According to Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth), she would have been sent to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), had she been male. She had a great wit, and all the chronicles agree that she made a powerful impression on those who met her.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) Alysanne was high-spirited, charming and keenly intelligent.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) However, Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) considers Alysanne to have been shrewish.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

According to a semi-canon source, Alysanne's hair turned white as snow in her old age. She wore it in a bun, pulled back and pinned behind her ears.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-GoodQueen_9-3) Additionally, age left crow's feet around her eyes and laugh lines about her mouth, although her face never lost its strength.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-GoodQueen_9-4) Alysanne became thin and frail after the death of her son [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)) in [92 AC](wiki:92_AC), and began to have difficulty in climbing hills. After she broke her hip in [95 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), she walked with a cane. Further, as she aged, her hearing began to fail, and she became too unsteady to fly.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

[Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) recalls a tale told to him by [Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan), in which Jaehaerys and Alysanne traveled to Winterfell together in a great royal progress that contained six dragons.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-3) It is unknown if this was a second progress made by the King and Queen, or if it was just a fable.

As they travel north, [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) tells [Jojen](wiki:Jojen_Reed) and [Meera Reed](wiki:Meera_Reed) about Queen Alysanne and the New Gift. As they arrive at [Queenscrown](wiki:Queenscrown), Bran tells them the story from Old Nan about Alysanne's visit to the village on her way to the [Wall](wiki:Wall), which Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) had mostly confirmed.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) When Bran's [bastard](wiki:Bastard) half-brother [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) arrives at the village not long after, he tells the same story to [Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte).(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-4)

### A Feast For Crows

[Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) recalls how Alysanne and Jaehaerys had visited the Wall on their dragons, and wonders if Silverwing could have left an [egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) there.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) recalls that when she was a child, she had once drawn a picture of herself and Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) flying on a dragon, and that when her brother [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) had found it, she told him the picture was Queen Alysanne and King Jaehaerys.(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

### A Dance With Dragons

When talking to [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) speaks of Alysanne with contempt over her involvement in making the [first night](wiki:First_night) illegal, calling her Jaehaerys's "shrewish queen".(wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)
