## Origins

Jon was born in [283 AC](wiki:283_AC), near the end of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion),(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-33) and he was named by Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark).(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rssm1202.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) One tale says Jon was [named after](wiki:Named_after) Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn),(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Eddard's [foster father](wiki:Fosterage).(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) The identity of Jon's mother is a mystery, and several suggestions have been made by those who know the Starks. Jon is unaware of his mother's identity(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) and Eddard refuses to speak of her.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) When Eddard returned from the war, he brought the newborn Jon to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), insisting on raising him with the rest of his family. Jon and his [wet nurse](wiki:Wet_nurse) were installed in the castle before the arrival of Eddard's new wife, [Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), and his young son and heir, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), from [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), which Catelyn did not take well.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-6)

Lord Eddard was fiercely protective of Jon(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) and refused to send him away.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-7) Jon was raised at Winterfell with his half-siblings. He was tutored by [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Luwin](wiki:Luwin),(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) including learning sums(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-2) and [astronomy](wiki:Astronomy),(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) and was trained at arms by the [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms), Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel). Jon has trained at swordplay since he was old enough to walk, together with Robb,(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-3) whom he came to view as his "best friend, rival and constant companion,"(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) and later also with [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy),(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) after the latter came to Winterfell following the conclusion of [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion). Jon learned how to swim in the great moat at Winterfell; he is considered a strong swimmer.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) Jon has possibly visited the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort).(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

Jon grew close to his true-born siblings, especially Robb and [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark). Because they look alike, Arya briefly believed that she was bastard-born as well, but Jon reassured his half-sister this was not the case.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

When Jon was a young child,(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-44) he and Robb built a great mountain of snow on top of a gate, hoping to push it on someone passing by. They were discovered by [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder), a [ranger](wiki:Ranger) from the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) who had accompanied [Lord Commander Qorgyle](wiki:Lord_Commander_Qorgyle) to Winterfell. Mance promised not to tell anyone, allowing Jon and Robb to succeed in their ploy. The boys dumped the snow upon [Fat Tom](wiki:Fat_Tom), who chased the boys around the yard afterwards until all three were tired.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Since he was young, Jon's hero was King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen), who had [conquered Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne) at the age of fourteen.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) Jon and Robb would often play a game of swordplay in which they would pretend to be great heroes (including [Florian the Fool](wiki:Florian_the_Fool), Prince [Aemon the Dragonknight](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), King Daeron the Young Dragon, and Ser [Ryam Redwyne](wiki:Ryam_Redwyne)). Once, when Jon called out that he was "[Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell)," Robb informed him that was impossible due to his [bastardy](wiki:Bastardy), which became a sore memory for Jon.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-4)

Jon once covered himself in flour and hid in one of the empty tombs in the [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell), and when Robb brought [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark), Arya, and [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) down to the crypts, Jon jumped out of the tomb and moaned, pretending to be a [spirit](wiki:Ghosts). Sansa screamed and ran back upstairs, Bran clung to Robb's leg and cried, and Arya punched Jon for scaring baby Bran with his prank. Jon and Robb laughed, however, until Bran and Arya began laughing as well.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

Lord Eddard had dreamed about raising new [lords](wiki:Lord) and settling them in the abandoned [holdfasts](wiki:Holdfast) in the [New Gift](wiki:New_Gift), and Jon believes that if [winter](wiki:Winter) had come and gone more quickly, he might have been chosen to hold one of the settlements in his father's name.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-1)

## Appearances & Description

Jon has the long face of the Starks.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) He has dark(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) brown hair(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) and grey eyes,(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) so dark they almost seem black.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) He is graceful and quick, and has a lean build.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) Jon resembles his father, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark),(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) and has more [Stark](wiki:House_Stark)-like features than any of his half-brothers.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) Because he looks so much like a Stark, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) notes that whoever Jon's mother was, she left little of herself in her son's appearance.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Out of all the Stark children, [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) is said to resemble Jon the most, as [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark), [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark), [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) and [Rickon](wiki:Rickon_Stark) take after their [Tully](wiki:House_Tully) mother, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark).(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Jon looks solemn and guarded,(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) and is considered sullen and quick to sense a slight.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Due to having been raised in a castle and trained by a [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms), Jon is seen by some [lower-born members](wiki:Smallfolk) of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) as arrogant at first,(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) though this changes when they become more friendly towards one another, as Jon is reminded of his nonetheless privileged background and decides to pass on his knowledge to them. Jon is observant,(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3) a trait he developed on account of being a bastard. He is a capable [horseback](wiki:Horse) rider and is well practiced in fighting with a sword. Jon has resented his bastard status most of the time.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-4) He desires to be viewed as honorable,(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) and wants to prove he can be as good and true as his half-brother, Robb.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) As a child, Jon dreamed he would one day lead men to glory, or even become a conqueror.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) He feels strongly about not fathering a bastard himself.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin) has described Jon as "the brooding, Byronic, romantic hero whom all the girls love".(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-25)

While Lord Eddard openly acknowledged Jon as his son and allowed him to live at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) with his half-siblings, Jon felt like an outsider nonetheless. While Jon has good relationships with his siblings, especially Robb, with whom he trained since they were children,(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) and Arya, whom he sees as somewhat of an outsider as well,(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Eddard's wife Catelyn ensured that Jon was never truly one of them.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Catelyn was especially annoyed when Jon bested Robb in martial training or their studies.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) Jon is not close to [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), Eddard's [ward](wiki:Ward).(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) Lord Stark refuses to speak of Jon's mother, and the boy grew up unaware of her identity, something which has wounded and haunted him.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-5)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) When Jon dreams of her, he considers her to be beautiful, highborn, and kind.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1)

Raised at Winterfell as a descendant of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), Jon keeps faith with the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods),(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) and he feels no connection to [the Seven](wiki:The_Seven).(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)

After joining the Night's Watch, Jon dresses in their official black garb.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) While there are no indications in the books that Jon has adopted a personal [coat of arms](wiki:Coat_of_arms), Martin told the company Valyrian Steel, which makes replicas of [Jon's sword](wiki:Longclaw), to use the reversed Stark colors on the plaque that goes with the sword.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-vsteel_19-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Jon accompanies his father, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), his brothers [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark) and [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark), his father's [ward](wiki:Ward) [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), and others from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to the execution of [Gared](wiki:Gared), a deserter from the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). On their way back to Winterfell, Jon and Robb race ahead and find a litter of [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf) pups. When Eddard states that killing the pups quickly would forestall a painful and slow death, Jon points out that there are five pups—one for each of Eddard's legitimate children—and the direwolf is the sigil of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark), indicating that they must be meant to have the wolves. The comparison only works out because Jon is not claiming a pup for himself, and Eddard gives in. As they leave, Jon discovers an albino pup, cast away from its litter. He claims the pup for his own,(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4) eventually naming it "[Ghost](wiki:Ghost)."(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3)

Because he is a [bastard](wiki:Bastard), Jon is not allowed sit with his siblings and the royal children during [the feast](wiki:Feast_in_honor_of_King_Robert's_visit_to_Winterfell) welcoming King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and the royal family to the [north](wiki:North), and instead sits with the [squires](wiki:Squire), further down the room. At the feast, Jon speaks with his uncle, [Benjen Stark](wiki:Benjen_Stark), the [First Ranger](wiki:First_Ranger) of the Night's Watch. When Benjen suggests that the Watch could use a man as observant as Jon, Jon quickly requests to accompany him to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) when he leaves, as even a bastard can rise to a position of honor there. Benjen is hesitant about having Jon join at such a young age, resulting in an argument that causes Jon to storm out. Outside, he runs into [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), the youngest brother of Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), who counsels him to "never forget [what you are](wiki:Bastard)" and tells Jon to make it his strength instead.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-4)

Although hesitant about it at the feast, Benjen eventually approaches [Luwin](wiki:Luwin), telling the [maester](wiki:Maester) that Jon had expressed interest in joining the Night's Watch. When Lord Eddard decides to accept the position as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), his wife, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), refuses to allow Jon to remain at Winterfell while Eddard is away. As Eddard feels he cannot take Jon south with him, Luwin suggests the Night's Watch for Jon, and Eddard eventually agrees. Catelyn is pleased, because as a member of the Watch Jon will never father children who might contest her grandchildren's inheritance of Winterfell.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-8)

The decision for him to go to the Wall leaves Jon angry in the days before he is set to leave.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) After Bran falls from the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep), the departure day is postponed, but a fortnight after Bran's accident Jon and Benjen are ready to leave Winterfell. Jon says his last goodbyes, first to the comatose Bran, then to Robb, and finally to [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark), to whom he gives a small, slender sword which they name [Needle](wiki:Needle).(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

On his way to the Wall, Jon quickly becomes disillusioned with the Night's Watch, after meeting [Yoren](wiki:Yoren), a so-called [wandering crow](wiki:Wandering_crow), and his new recruits. During the journey, Jon befriends Tyrion Lannister, who had decided to travel further north to see the Wall for himself.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

At [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), Jon first remains aloof and distant, making no friends; he scorns his fellow recruits, who return the feeling, resenting him due to his attitude. Days after their arrival, Benjen leaves to lead a ranging, and while Jon requests to accompany him, Benjen refuses to allow it, leaving Jon angry. After a fight between Jon and several other recruits, Jon speaks with [Donal Noye](wiki:Donal_Noye), the [armorer](wiki:Armorer) at Castle Black, who points that Jon has been a bully to the other recruits. When a letter arrives from Winterfell informing Jon that Bran, though crippled, has awoken and will live, Jon is ecstatic, and when he returns to the Common Hall, he offers his fellow recruits advice on their swordplay.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) Jon begins spending some of his time assisting Donal in the [armory of Castle Black](wiki:Armory_(Castle_Black)).(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

Jon soon becomes a natural leader, mentor, and friend to most of his fellow trainees, earning him the enmity of the [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms), Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne). When [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) arrives at the Wall, Jon reaches out to him, and helps him to be accepted by the majority of the recruits, even threatening [Rast](wiki:Rast) with Ghost to prevent him from further bullying Sam.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-1)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1)

As new recruits are about to arrive at Castle Black, eight recruits, including Jon, are chosen to take their vows. Sam is not chosen, and Jon realizes that it will only be a matter of time that Sam will be hurt or killed in training without his friends present to protect him. Sam points out to Jon that Mormont has given him the position in order to groom him for command, which quells his anger.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2)

Jon and Sam decide to say their vows at the [weirwood grove](wiki:Weirwood_grove) located north of the Wall. After they have said the words, Ghost returns with the severed hand of [Jafer Flowers](wiki:Jafer_Flowers),(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-3) alerting the men of the corpses of Jafer and [Othor](wiki:Othor). The next day, Lord Commander Mormont chastises him for running, and Jon agrees to fully commit to the Watch. He accepts his place as Jeor's squire and prepares for the journey [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall) which Mormont plans to lead.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Ragot70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-1)

### A Clash of Kings

Jon brings his direwolf, [Ghost](wiki:Ghost). Jon fetches [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) for Lord Commander [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), who has been waiting for maps of the lands further north. Jeor and Jon discuss his hand, which still troubles him, but is slowly getting better. They discuss Maester Aemon, and Jeor reveals that Aemon had [once been offered the Iron Throne](wiki:Great_Council_of_233_AC), but instead had decided to remain a maester, and the throne passed to his younger brother, [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen). After the arrival of [Qhorin Halfhand](wiki:Qhorin_Halfhand) with the men from the [Shadow Tower](wiki:Shadow_Tower), Jon is picked by Qhorin to accompany one of the three scouting parties into the mountains.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) Jon grows a beard after leaving the Fist.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0)(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0)

In the [Skirling Pass](wiki:Skirling_Pass), Qhorin's party comes across a group of [wildling](wiki:Wildling) sentries, and Jon is one of those assigned to take them out. He kills one of the man, but discovers his second target is a woman. Jon instead decides to take prisoner the girl, who is called [Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte). He reveals he is the bastard son of [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), and during the night, Ygritte tells Jon the story of "[Bael the Bard](wiki:Bael_the_Bard)," a song which insinuates that, through Bael, the Starks too have wildling blood. Before she leaves, Ygritte informs Jon that Mance Rayder would accept him, if he wanted to join the free folk.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-1) Jon tells Qhorin about this, who confirms that Mance would be willing, and tells Jon that Mance had been a man of the Night's Watch himself once. Jon informs the group, who recognize Jon for a [warg](wiki:Warg). They later see the eagle, and when they find a wounded Ghost, Qhorin decides they return to the [Fist of the First Men](wiki:Fist_of_the_First_Men).(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0)

With the help of Ghost, Jon kills Qhorin, and the wildlings agree to bring him to Mance Rayder.(wiki:Jon_Snow.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0)

Most wildlings in the [Shieldhall](wiki:Shieldhall) agree to support him, but Jon's decision causes great discontent within the Watch's upper leadership. In the confusion resulting from Wun Wun's killing of Ser [Patrek of King's Mountain](wiki:Patrek_of_King's_Mountain), Jon is attacked in the [mutiny at Castle Black](wiki:Mutiny_at_Castle_Black).
