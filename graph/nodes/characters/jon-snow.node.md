## Identity

Jon Snow is the acknowledged bastard son of Lord Eddard Stark and one of the major POV characters in A Song of Ice and Fire. He has five half-siblings: Robb, Sansa, Arya, Bran, and Rickon Stark. The identity of his mother is a central mystery of the series (wiki:Jon_Snow).

## Origins

Born 283 AC (track_b: Born), near the end of Robert's Rebellion. Father: Eddard Stark (track_b: Father). Mothers listed as Wylla (supposedly) and Ashara Dayne (rumored) (track_b: Mothers). Named by Eddard, possibly after Jon Arryn (wiki:Jon_Snow). Eddard brought the newborn Jon to Winterfell before the arrival of Catelyn Tully and Robb from Riverrun (wiki:Jon_Snow).

## Appearances & Description

Jon has the long face of the Starks, dark brown hair, and grey eyes so dark they almost seem black. He is graceful, quick, with a lean build. He resembles Eddard more than any of his half-siblings; Tyrion notes that whoever Jon's mother was, she left little of herself in her son's appearance. Of all the Stark children, Arya resembles Jon the most (wiki:Jon_Snow). Jon looks solemn and guarded, considered sullen and quick to sense a slight. He is observant — a trait developed on account of his bastard status (wiki:Jon_Snow). His companion is the albino direwolf Ghost (wiki:Jon_Snow). After joining the Watch, he receives the Valyrian steel bastard sword Longclaw from Lord Commander Jeor Mormont (wiki:Jon_Snow).

## Narrative Arc

Jon is raised at Winterfell alongside his half-siblings, trained by Ser Rodrik Cassel and tutored by Maester Luwin. He is closest to Robb and Arya but never fully accepted due to Catelyn Stark's resentment. At fourteen, Jon joins the Night's Watch, partly inspired by his uncle Benjen Stark (wiki:Jon_Snow).

At the Wall, Jon initially struggles with his privileged background clashing with the Watch's lower-born recruits but learns humility. He is assigned to the stewards under Lord Commander Mormont rather than the rangers, serving as Mormont's personal steward. During the wight attack at Castle Black, Jon saves Mormont's life and receives Longclaw as reward (wiki:Jon_Snow).

In ACOK, Jon joins the great ranging beyond the Wall under Mormont. He is sent with Qhorin Halfhand to scout the Frostfangs, where they are captured by wildlings. On Qhorin's orders, Jon kills the Halfhand to infiltrate Mance Rayder's host (wiki:Jon_Snow).

In ASOS, Jon lives among the wildlings and becomes Ygritte's lover. He learns of Mance's plan to assault the Wall and escapes south to warn Castle Black. Jon leads the defense of Castle Black against the wildling assault from the south, then helps hold the Wall during the main attack. After Stannis Baratheon arrives to crush Mance's host, Jon is offered legitimization as Jon Stark and lordship of Winterfell by Stannis, but declines. He is elected the 998th Lord Commander of the Night's Watch (wiki:Jon_Snow).

In ADWD, Jon attempts to balance the Watch's traditions with the existential threat of the Others. He allows the wildlings through the Wall, garrisons abandoned castles, and negotiates with Stannis and the Iron Bank of Braavos. His decisions create growing opposition among his brothers. After receiving a provocative letter from Ramsay Bolton, Jon announces his intention to march on Winterfell — breaking his vows — and is stabbed repeatedly by a group of mutinous Night's Watch brothers led by Bowen Marsh (wiki:Jon_Snow).
