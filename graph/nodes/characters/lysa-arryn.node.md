## Origins

### Early life

Lysa was born as the second daughter of Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully), head of [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) and [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun), and his wife, Lady [Minisa Whent](wiki:Minisa_Whent). She has one older sister, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), and one younger brother, [Edmure](wiki:Edmure_Tully). Her mother, who had given birth to two boys previously (who had both died in infancy),(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) died giving birth to her sixth child, who died with her.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

Lysa was raised at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) alongside her two siblings and her father's young ward, [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) As a young girl, Lysa would run and hide whenever she had done something wrong, so her father could not find her.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Just as her siblings and her father's ward, Petyr, were wont to do, Lysa always went to her uncle, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), with her tears and tales, whenever Lord Hoster was too busy, and Lady Minisa too ill.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6) Lysa and Catelyn played together and even had a secret language.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) Once they made mud pies in the [godswood of Riverrun](wiki:Godswood_of_Riverrun) and served them to Petyr, who was sick for a week after eating them.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) On one occasion, Lysa and Catelyn accompanied their father to [Seagard](wiki:Seagard), but on their way back to Riverrun, the sisters became lost due to the mist and Lysa started to cry. Petyr, who had accompanied Lord Hoster as well, eventually found the girls.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

Being raised alongside Petyr, Lysa fell in love with him, but her feelings were not reciprocated. Instead, Petyr fell in love with Lysa's older sister Catelyn.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) When they were still young Lysa and Catelyn had first learnt to kiss by taking turns kissing Petyr,(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) making Petyr the first man Lysa ever kissed.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) Catelyn recalls it as having been innocent, though Petyr attempted to kiss them with tongue, which Lysa allowed.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)

When Lords [Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood) and [Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken) were at Riverrun to settle a feud with Hoster's help, Catelyn and Petyr danced six dances. However, when he tried to kiss her, she pushed him away and laughed at him. Petyr, wounded by the rejection, became drunk and had to be brought to bed by Lysa's uncle, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully). Lysa followed, and climbed into Petyr's bed to "give him comfort." Petyr took her [maidenhead](wiki:Maidenhead) that night, telling her he loved her whilst he drunkenly believed that she was Catelyn, going as far as calling her "Cat" before he fell asleep. Despite this, Lysa stayed with him until sunrise, and would later come to remember the night fondly.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-30)

When Catelyn's wedding date to Brandon was eventually announced in [282 AC](wiki:282_AC), Petyr challenged the [northman](wiki:North) for Catelyn's hand.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1) Brandon wounded Petyr severely, after which Lysa helped nurse him. In the following fortnight, they slept together again. Lysa became pregnant with Petyr's child as a result of this.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-4) After Hoster had Petyr sent back to the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers), Lysa revealed her pregnancy to her father, hoping that Hoster would relent and let her wed Petyr. Hoster, who had always felt Petyr was too lowborn,(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) instead forced Lysa to abort the child with [moon tea](wiki:Moon_tea) provided by Maester [Kym](wiki:Kym).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-5)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-2) Lysa nearly died from this.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rawoiaflysa_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Lords [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) and [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) had been discussing a betrothal between Lysa and [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), the heir to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock). This potential betrothal was off the table when King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) named Jaime to his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) in [281 AC](wiki:281_AC).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) Lord Tywin offered his younger son, [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), instead but was rebuffed by Lord Hoster, who stated that he wanted "a whole man" for Lysa instead of a [dwarf](wiki:Dwarf).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1)

### Robert's Rebellion

Lysa was married to [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie) and [Defender of the Vale](wiki:Defender_of_the_Vale), during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Lord Arryn was already an old man and their marriage forged a useful wartime alliance with [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully). In addition, Jon needed a wife known to be fertile, as his last heir, Ser [Denys Arryn](wiki:Denys_Arryn), had died during the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Lysa married Jon at Riverrun, in the same ceremony as her sister [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) married [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1) Unlike Catelyn's marriage, Lysa's had been hastily arranged.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) While she had wept much at the morning of [their double wedding](wiki:Weddings_of_Catelyn_and_Lysa_Tully), Lysa had been dry-eyed and radiant during the ceremony itself.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

Lords Arryn and Stark soon left to continue fighting. Lysa and Catelyn both remained at Riverrun, and when their moon blood did not come at the accustomed time, Lysa was convinced they were both pregnant with sons. While for Catelyn this was indeed the case, Lysa's moon blood came not much later, and "all the joy" went out of her. After Catelyn gave birth to [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), she gave him to Lysa to hold, but, dissolving into tears, Lysa immediately gave the boy back to her sister.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)

After the war ended, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) named Jon Arryn as his [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) on his coronation,(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rawoiafjon_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) a position that took him away to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) for long periods of time. Additionally, Lysa's uncle, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), who had been quarreling with his older brother Hoster for years, took Lysa's marriage as an opportunity to enter the service of [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn). During Lysa's wedding feast, Brynden informed Hoster he would be serving Lysa and her new husband instead.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-7)

### Marriage

[Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) was already a fairly old man when Lysa married him, and he was proud. Due to Jon's position as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), the pair lived in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Because of Lysa's soiled state and the age difference between them, theirs was a loveless marriage.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4) In addition, Lysa had many troubles with childbirth. Including two boys and three girls,(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7) Lysa suffered two miscarriages in the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) and three in King's Landing, and she gave birth to two stillborn children.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-8)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5) After suffering her last stillbirth, Lysa became paranoid, seeing enemies everywhere.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) She eventually gave birth to a son, [Robert](wiki:Robert_Arryn), in [292 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-39) However, Robert is a sickly child, suffering from the 'shaking sickness'. Being her only living child, Lysa became overprotective, and would still be breastfeeding him when he was six years old.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-9)

Despite her marriage, Lysa did not forget [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish). She convinced her new husband to give Petyr the customs for [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown) around [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), where Petyr performed so well that Arryn eventually brought him to court, eventually rising to the position of [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) During this time, Lysa and Petyr begin a secret affair.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rawoiaflysa_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Lysa and her sister Catelyn last saw each other in [293 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-10) Lysa also met Catelyn's eldest daughter, [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark),(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9) though it is unknown whether these two events occurred at the same time. Lysa once sent [Tom of Sevenstreams](wiki:Tom_of_Sevenstreams) up the [high road](wiki:High_road).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

Lysa commissioned for her husband a double-edged [longsword](wiki:Longsword) decorated to honor [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-5) When Lord Jon grew concerned about the weakness of his son Robert, he arranged for the boy to be [fostered](wiki:Fostered) with Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) Lysa was furious upon learning about this arrangement.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1)

Lord Arryn fell ill while investigating [a book](wiki:Lineages) in King's Landing. Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) personally treated Jon after sending away Maester [Colemon](wiki:Colemon), but Lord Arryn still passed away quickly. Lysa prevented young Robert from visiting his father in the sickroom.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) After Jon's abrupt death, King Robert attempted to arrange for Lysa's son Robert to become a ward of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), as he feared that being raised by his mother would make his namesake weak. However, Lysa refused the suggestion brusquely and, against the wishes of the king, fled in the middle of the night with her son back to the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), without bidding farewell.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

## Appearances & Description

Lysa was a pretty, slender, high-breasted girl in her youth, dimpled and delicate.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) She has grown thick of body by her early thirties, however, as a result of her many pregnancies, miscarriages and stillbirths.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Despite being two years younger than her sister [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), Lysa looks ten years older; her face has become pale and puffy, and she now paints and powders it.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) While Lysa uses scents to smell more sweet, she cannot completely hide the sour milky smell.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) She has the blue eyes of [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully), but they are pale and watery.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Lysa has a small, petulant mouth, and her beautiful auburn hair falls thickly down to her waist.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

Her [marriage](wiki:Marriage) to Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) was purely political, and while he was dutiful and kind to her, the marriage lacked the warmth Lysa required.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Lysa had been a soft, shy,(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and timid girl in her youth, prone to tongue-tied silences and fits of giggles.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) Her years at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) changed her significantly,(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5) however, turning her into a woman who is by turns proud, fearful, cruel, dreamy, reckless, impulsive, timid, stubborn, vain, and inconstant.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Lysa had never been brave in her youth, which did not change during adulthood.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Lysa is fiercely protective of her young son, [Robert](wiki:Robert_Arryn), and goes through great lengths to keep him by her side.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-17) [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) dislikes Lysa.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Lysa prefers rich [silks](wiki:Silk), satins, velvets, furs, samites, and linens.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) She likes [Myrish](wiki:Myr) lace(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) and is fond of wearing a cream-colored gown with a necklace of [sapphires](wiki:Sapphire) and [moonstones](wiki:Moonstone).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Lysa's mourning clothes include black silk, with the moon-and-falcon of [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) sewn in [pearls](wiki:Pearl).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) She also has a blue velvet robe trimmed with fox fur.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) The Lady of the Eyrie is fond of singers.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Lysa's apartments at the Eyrie are located above the garden which passes for the castle's [godswood](wiki:Godswood).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

After her husband dies, Lysa flees [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and returns to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) with her young son, Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn), and her household.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) Lysa sends to her sister, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) a secret letter in which she accuses the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) and Queen [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) in particular of having murdered her husband.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3)

After King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) names Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) as [Warden of the East](wiki:Warden_of_the_East), Lysa commands that Lord Robert be addressed as the True Warden of the East.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-11) The widowed Lysa is courted by various Valemen, including Ser [Lyn Corbray](wiki:Lyn_Corbray),(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-6) Lord [Eon Hunter](wiki:Eon_Hunter),(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-7) Lord [Nestor Royce](wiki:Nestor_Royce),(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) and Ser [Morton Waynwood](wiki:Morton_Waynwood).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-8) Lord [Waxley](wiki:House_Waxley) sends Lysa scented candles from [Wickenden](wiki:Wickenden).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) states Lysa is being courted by powerful men such as Lord [Yohn Royce](wiki:Yohn_Royce), Lord [Horton Redfort](wiki:Horton_Redfort), and Lyn.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-2) Lysa rejects Nestor and a dozen other suitable men, as she wants to choose her next husband herself.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-12)

Catelyn's husband, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), replaces Jon as the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and investigate his predecessor's death.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1) Lysa forbids her knights from fighting in the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-13) [Varys](wiki:Varys) informs Ned that Lord Arryn was [poisoned](wiki:Poison) with the [tears of Lys](wiki:Tears_of_Lys).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

Catelyn takes [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) prisoner when she accidentally meets him at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn) during her journey from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) back to Winterfell.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) To shake off possible pursuers, Catelyn publicly states her intent to take Tyrion to Winterfell, but in truth travels to the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) instead, where her younger sister Lysa rules as regent for her six-year-old son.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) Catelyn has not seen her sister Lysa in years and is shocked by the changes in Lysa. She has grown fat, emotionally unstable and sees enemies everywhere.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-14)

Lysa has Tyrion thrown in the [sky cells](wiki:Sky_cells) and tries to make him confess to the murder of her husband. Tyrion outsmarts her by demanding a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) His champion, [Bronn](wiki:Bronn), is able to win against Ser [Vardis Egen](wiki:Vardis_Egen), captain of [the guard](wiki:House_Arryn_guards) at the Eyrie, whom Lysa has urged into the duel. Lysa orders Tyrion and Bronn to depart on the dangerous [high road](wiki:High_road).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-9)

Catelyn tries to persuade her sister to mobilize an army and join with their father, Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully), and the [north](wiki:North) against the Lannisters, but Lysa refuses. Their uncle, Ser [Brynden the Blackfish](wiki:Brynden_Tully), disgusted by Lysa's behavior, resigns as [Knight of the Gate](wiki:Knight_of_the_Gate) and leaves with Catelyn to fight for [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-10) Before Catelyn leaves, she offers to foster Robert at Winterfell. Lysa responds by threatening to throw Catelyn out the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

After King Robert dies, Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) sends [ravens](wiki:Raven) demanding that Lysa and Lord Robert swear allegiance to the new king, [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Despite her kin fighting the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister), Lysa keeps the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) neutral in the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) is willing to restore the title of [Warden of the East](wiki:Warden_of_the_East) to Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) if his mother Lysa returns to the [king's peace](wiki:King's_peace).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2) During his attempt to discover who on the [small council](wiki:Small_council) is informing on him to [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), Tyrion tells Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) that he wishes for Petyr to convince Lysa to agree to a betrothal between Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) and Robert. Littlefinger reveals he previously slept with Lysa.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3) In truth, however, Tyrion's suggestion is a ruse, and Petyr never leaves King's Landing to propose the match to Lysa.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

At [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), a feverish Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) mistakes his elder daughter, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), for Lysa.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

### A Storm of Swords

Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) passes away at Riverrun,(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) feverishly asking forgiveness for having forced his daughter to take [tansy tea](wiki:Tansy_tea) in her youth. [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) does not understand Hoster's meaning, however.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6)(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-6)

[Marillion](wiki:Marillion) becomes a favorite of Lysa, who gives the singer gold and gifts at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-10)

Lysa's sister, Catelyn, and nephew, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), are killed in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) By the time of the defeat of the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark), Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) has risen high in the counsel of the Lannisters and has been named [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident) and [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal). With this rank, he is now able to marry Lysa and gain a position of influence over her son, Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-3)

Following [Joffrey's death](wiki:Purple_Wedding), Petyr brings [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) with him to the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), disguised as his [natural daughter](wiki:Bastardy) "Alayne Stone," but he informs Lysa the girl is actually her niece. Lysa brings Marillion and her own [septon](wiki:Septon) to the [Baelish ancestral seat](wiki:Drearfort), where [she weds Petyr](wiki:Wedding_of_Petyr_Baelish_and_Lysa_Arryn). On their wedding night, Lysa hopes to become pregnant with Petyr's child. Lysa suggests that Sansa can one day wed Lord Robert, Sansa's cousin.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-11)

Many of the Valemen are upset with Lysa marrying Petyr and by her naming of him as [Lord Protector of the Vale](wiki:Lord_Protector_of_the_Vale). Additionally, the [Royces](wiki:House_Royce) are angered at her refusal to aid House Stark during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). Lysa becomes lonely whenever Petyr departs the Eyrie to conduct business.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-7)

Sansa strongly resembles her late mother, Catelyn.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-12) One snowy morning, Lysa catches Petyr kissing Sansa in the [godswood of the Eyrie](wiki:Godswood_of_the_Eyrie). Summoning Sansa to the [High Hall](wiki:High_Hall), Lysa blames the girl and during an emotional row almost throws her from the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door). Sansa is saved by the timely intervention of Petyr, who stops Lysa from sending Sansa to her death. Lysa is hysterical about the idea that Petyr might desire Sansa, and confesses how she and Petyr had maintained a relationship during their time in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), and she had convinced her husband, Jon, to appoint Petyr as manager of customs at [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown) (which led to Petyr being brought to court and eventually to his appointment as [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin)).(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-4) She also proclaims how she had poisoned her husband with the [tears of Lys](wiki:Tears_of_Lys), as instructed by Petyr, and how she had sent her sister a letter accusing the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) of the murder, again as per Petyr's instructions.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-8)

Petyr calms Lysa down, but confesses to her that he has only ever loved Lysa's sister, Catelyn. He then throws Lysa from the [Giant's Lance](wiki:Giant's_Lance) through the Moon Door, framing her singer Marillion for the deed.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-9)

### A Feast for Crows

Following Lysa's death, Petyr remains [Lord Protector of the Vale](wiki:Lord_Protector_of_the_Eyrie_and_the_Vale_of_Arryn). He replaces Ser [Marwyn Belmore](wiki:Marwyn_Belmore), Lysa's [captain of guards](wiki:House_Arryn_guards), with Ser [Lothor Brune](wiki:Lothor_Brune). [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) has difficulty sleeping, and Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) refuses to sleep in his own bed after his mother's death.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) Petyr gives Sansa her pick of Lysa's wardrobe and [jewelry](wiki:Jewelry); most gowns are too large, but a few from Lysa's youth fit the girl.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

Lord [Nestor Royce](wiki:Nestor_Royce), [High Steward of the Vale](wiki:High_Steward_of_the_Vale), visits the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) to ask about Lysa's death. Petyr has Marillion confess to murdering Lysa, and he convinces Sansa—disguised as Alayne Stone—to place the blame on the singer as well. Petyr grants the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon) to [Nestor's family](wiki:House_Royce_of_the_Gates_of_the_Moon), claiming it was Lysa's desire before Marillion murdered her.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-1)

When the [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant) arrive at the Eyrie, Petyr blames misrule in the Vale on Lysa. Petyr convinces his adversaries to grant him a year to set the Vale to rights.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7)

It is reported at [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) that Lysa was pushed off a mountain by a singer,(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) and [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) learns in [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) that her aunt was killed by a singer.(wiki:Lysa_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8)
