## Origins

### Early life

Jon Connington was the only son of [Armond Connington](wiki:Armond_Connington), [Lord](wiki:Lord_of_Griffin's_Roost) of [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost), and his wife.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Jon served as a [squire](wiki:Squire) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), first alongside Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen),(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) and later for the crown prince.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Winds1_9-2) Jon was considered to be one of Prince Rhaegar's dear friends.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Some felt Jon was the most headstrong of the young lordlings who had gathered around Rhaegar, seeking his favor.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2)

During a time when Jon's father was still alive, Rhaegar was returning from a trip to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), and visited Griffin's Roost for a fortnight. At the welcoming feast, Rhaegar played a song on his [harp](wiki:Harp) that made every woman in the hall cry, but per Jon's recollection, none of the men. Rhaegar visited the tallest tower of the castle with Jon, and complimented the beauty of the Connington lands.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) After Armond died, Jon inherited the rule to Griffin's Roost. When Jon left to serve at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), he named his cousin, Ser [Ronald Connington](wiki:Ronald_Connington), as [castellan](wiki:Castellan).(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4) In [280 AC](wiki:280_AC), Jon was present for the wedding of Rhaegar to Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell) of Dorne.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5) In [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), Jon was present at the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal), where he danced with [Ashara Dayne](wiki:Ashara_Dayne).(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) In a [tourney at Storm's End](wiki:Tourney_at_Storm's_End), he was unhorsed in a [joust](wiki:Joust) by Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### Robert's Rebellion

During [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) dismissed his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Owen Merryweather](wiki:Owen_Merryweather), believing him ineffectual, and suspecting foul play by Owen in favor of the rebels.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rssm1118.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) To match [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Lord of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) and one of the leaders of the rebellion, Aerys wanted someone young and vigorous as his next Hand, and so named Jon to the office.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), a former Hand to Aerys, considered Jon to be too young and impetuous.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-4)

Jon promised King Aerys he would deliver Robert's head,(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-5) and he led an army into the field. After the [Battle of Ashford](wiki:Battle_of_Ashford), Jon took over the pursuit of Robert from the [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) army, and tracked him to [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept) in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands). Jon offered rewards and put [hostages](wiki:Hostage) in [crow cages](wiki:Crow_cage),(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-6) while his soldiers began a house-to-house search for Robert. They were unable to locate him before his rebel allies, Lords [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) and [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully), arrived at Stoney Sept to oust Jon and his forces. Lord Connington fought back fiercely, killing Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn)'s nephew and heir, Ser [Denys Arryn](wiki:Denys_Arryn), and wounding Lord Tully.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Jon was nearly slain by Robert at the town's [sept](wiki:Sept),(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-7) and retreated when he saw that the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells) was lost.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

King Aerys held Jon responsible for his defeat at Stoney Sept. The king exiled Jon, stripping him of his titles, lands, and wealth, and passing the rule of [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost) to Ser Ronald Connington.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-8) After Jon's exile, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) was killed by Robert at the [Trident](wiki:Trident), and Aerys was killed in the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing). Jon felt that both losses were his fault, as the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) would never had been fought had he killed Robert in Stoney Sept.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-7)

### Exile

After the war, the new king, Robert I Baratheon, was not willing to recall Jon from exile, since he had been among Rhaegar's closest friends.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rssm1203.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Robert allowed Ronald Connington to keep his castle, but he took away the [lordship](wiki:Lord) from [House Connington](wiki:House_Connington), making them a house of [landed knights](wiki:Landed_knight). Robert also kept the Connington wealth in the treasury, and took away nine-tenths of the Connington lands, distributing them to his more loyal supporters.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-9)

The exiled Jon joined the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company),(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rawoiafjon_connington.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-8) and rose quickly in the ranks to a place of honor at the right hand of Ser [Myles Toyne](wiki:Myles_Toyne), the company's [captain-general](wiki:Captain-general). Jon believes that, had his exile gone otherwise, he might have been the successor to his friend Myles instead of [Harry Strickland](wiki:Harry_Strickland).(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-9) When Jon reflected on the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells) in his first year of exile, he defended his actions, telling Myles that [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) himself could not have done more. Myles replied that Tywin would have not bothered searching for Robert; he would have burned down [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept), burning everything and everybody within the town to kill Robert, despite the civilian casualties.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-10)

After Jon had served with the Golden Company for five years, he and Myles were approached by [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis) and Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys), who informed them that Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen)'s infant son, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), had survived the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rawoiafjon_connington.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) In order to remove Jon from the Golden Company, they concocted a plan where Jon was driven from the company in disgrace after he was allegedly caught stealing from the war chest. Afterwards, news was spread that Jon drank himself to death in [Lys](wiki:Lys). The remainder of the Golden Company was left unaware of the truth, so that they could not divulge anything.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-10)

While Jon went along with Varys's scheme for Prince Aegon's sake, he believed it a dishonorable slight, and resented the shameful lie of his death. Varys, however, was adamant about the need for secrecy, and felt that the lie would help people forget Jon's existence.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-11) The rumors of Jon's disgrace and death eventually reached Westeros, and convinced everyone that he was dead.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Jon focused on raising and protecting "[Young Griff](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(Young_Griff))" in the guise of his father, "Griff", for twelve years.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-12)

## Appearances & Description

In his youth, Jon's hair and beard were fiery red.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Now nearing forty, Jon is clean shaven with a lined, leathery face.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) He has crow's feet at the corners of his pale blue eyes.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) When disguised as the sellsword "Griff", he dyes his hair blue, though his eyebrows are still red and his red roots show.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Jon's hair has turned grey,(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) though when he grows a beard, it is still mostly red, with ash showing here and there.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) While pretending to be Griff, Jon prefers to wear a [red wolf](wiki:Red_wolf)-skin cloak.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

Jon is a capable warrior and commander. He was described in his youth as proud, arrogant, energetic, reckless, and thirsty for glory.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) However, his years spent in exile have made him more cautious, dangerous, and experienced.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) Though [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost) is sworn to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), the humorless(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4) Jon disliked serving Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Winds1_9-1) Jon is haunted by his failure at the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells), and the sound of bells ringing greatly disturbs him. He often dreams of bells, and of the battle.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4)

### Greyscale — the death-clock (book-cite overlay)

Jon contracts [greyscale](wiki:Greyscale) in his right hand while rescuing Tyrion in the Sorrows, and hides it through the whole ADWD campaign. It is a slow death sentence — the condition that **MOTIVATES his haste** to seat Aegon on the Iron Throne before he dies ("All he asked was time"). On-page progression and concealment:

> The nail on his middle finger had turned as black as jet, he saw, and the grey had crept up almost to the first knuckle. The tip of his ring finger had begun to darken too, and when he touched it with the point of his dagger, he felt nothing.

— Jon's thoughts, alone in his tent (`sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-lost-lord-01.md:237`)

> The nails on all four fingers were black now, though not yet on his thumb. On the middle finger, the grey had crept up past the second knuckle. ... men who would cheerfully face battle and risk death to rescue a companion would abandon that same companion in a heartbeat if he were known to have greyscale.

— Connington soaks his hand at Griffin's Roost, hiding the disease behind a daily request for wine rather than the tell-tale vinegar (`sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-griffin-reborn-01.md:141`)

> Boiled eggs, fried bread, and beans. And a jug of wine. The worst wine in the cellar.

— Connington's first meal in his reclaimed seat. He asks for "the worst wine" because the jug is for soaking his greyscaled hand, not for drinking — a hospitality detail that doubles as a concealment beat (`sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-the-griffin-reborn-01.md:135`)

### A Storm of Swords

[Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), in disguise as the [squire](wiki:Squire) "Arstan", tells [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) that Jon Connington was one of her brother Rhaegar's dear friends.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

The [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) recalls that Jon was one of a series of Hands of the King for Aerys who served so briefly that he remembers their [heraldry](wiki:Heraldry) better than their faces.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

At Stoney Sept, [Harwin](wiki:Harwin) tells [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) about the Battle of the Bells.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2)

### A Feast for Crows

Jaime Lannister meets Ser [Ronnet Connington](wiki:Ronnet_Connington) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), and discusses Jon with him.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3)

### A Dance with Dragons

Griff and Young Griff have [dyed their hair](wiki:Hair_dye) blue, supposedly in memory of Young Griff's mother, a [Tyroshi](wiki:Tyroshi) woman. They travel on the *[Shy Maid](wiki:Shy_Maid)* alongside [Haldon](wiki:Haldon), [Rolly Duckfield](wiki:Rolly_Duckfield), [Lemore](wiki:Lemore), [Ysilla](wiki:Ysilla), and [Yandry](wiki:Yandry).(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5) Jon plans to meet with [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), Queen of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), expecting her to journey towards [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) with her army after her [conquest of the cities](wiki:Targaryen_campaign_in_Slaver's_Bay) in [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay). Knowing that, whichever route she chooses, Daenerys needs to pass [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), it has been decided that the company will wait for her at the [Free City](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis) sends them [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), who has fled [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) after he killed his father [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) and was unfairly condemned for [the death](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1) While Illyrio informs Jon of Tyrion's identity, the secret is kept from the rest of the company, who only know him as "Yollo" or "Hugor Hill".(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7)

While the *Shy Maid* journeys down the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne), Jon forbids Tyrion from drinking [wine](wiki:Wine) after the latter becomes drunk during one of his first nights on the ship, and orders him to [write down everything](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister's_dragonlore) he still recalls from his extensive reading about [dragons](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-5) When they reach the [Sorrows](wiki:Sorrows), Jon orders Young Griff to safety below deck, but the boy protests. Tyrion reveals he is aware of Jon's true identity, and states he should be lucky that [Varys](wiki:Varys) is part of their conspiracy, as the name "Griff" would never have fooled the [spymaster](wiki:Spymaster).(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Tyrion also reveals that he is aware of the true identity of Young Griff. When [stone men](wiki:Stone_men) attack the company in [Chroyane](wiki:Chroyane), Jon rescues Tyrion after the latter is knocked into the water.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) Jon contracts [greyscale](wiki:Greyscale) in his right hand while rescuing Tyrion, but he keeps it a secret for fear he would be left to die.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-13)

The company soon reaches [Selhorys](wiki:Selhorys), where Jon sends Haldon with Tyrion to learn about rumors that Daenerys has not yet left Meereen. Tyrion is kidnapped by [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont),(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3) but Jon believes Tyrion simply escaped to drink and plot some new infamy. Although it is true that Daenerys is still in Meereen, Jon decides to continue their journey to [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys), where the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) is currently stationed. Jon reveals the identity of the young Prince Aegon to the Golden Company, but the highest-ranking officers have already been informed by [Harry Strickland](wiki:Harry_Strickland), their captain-general. The officers are dismayed that Daenerys stayed in Meereen, and voice their opinions on the constantly changing plans of Illyrio. Aegon suggests they go to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) themselves, where they will begin their rebellion, reasoning that once Daenerys learns of their purpose, she will come west and join them. Aegon's proposal is met with great enthusiasm, and Jon later reflects on his satisfaction with the plan. As the greyscale is beginning to spread across his hand, Jon hopes he will live long enough to seat Aegon on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-14)

The journey of the Golden Company to Westeros is difficult, due to the [autumn](wiki:Autumn) storms. The ships are scattered, and the [Volantene](wiki:Volantene) fleet drops them off on multiple different locations, leaving the [landing of the Golden Company](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company) with a smaller host. Despite this, Jon leads a group of men in the successful [taking of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost), his own former seat. Jon reminisces over Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) visit to [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost), his defeat in the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells), his exile by King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), and the taking of his lands by King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). Jon is glad that his cousin [Ronald](wiki:Ronald_Connington), who had received rule over Griffin's Roost after Jon's exile, is dead, as it means he will not have to fight him. He is also glad that Ronald's son, [Red Ronnet](wiki:Ronnet_Connington), is away. Jon has his remaining family members taken into custody. When he tries to get to know his young cousins, their fear and anger make him quickly give up on that plan, and they are returned to their guarded rooms. Jon tells Haldon to prepare a letter to [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), telling the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne) that his sister [Elia's](wiki:Elia_Martell) son, Prince Aegon, is still alive. When Haldon discusses rewards to hand out to lords in order to convince them to join Aegon's cause, Jon refuses to marry Aegon off, insisting he must be free to marry Daenerys once she returns to Westeros. He also refuses to present himself as a marriage candidate, for fear of having his greyscale become known.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-11)

When Prince Aegon arrives at Griffin's Roost four days later, Jon recalls how he had disagreed with the boy's decision to name Rolly to the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), as Jon had hoped to fill the White Swords with younger sons of important lords to bind them to their cause. Jon informs the prince that they are planning to take [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) in ten days, having previously refused the suggestion of making an alliance with [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon). Aegon agrees with Jon's plan, but demands he lead the attack himself.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-12)

Jon wishes to let the Iron Throne believe that the invading force is simply an exiled lord attempting to retake his old seat, and he plans to write King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) for a pardon to support the ruse.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-13) News of Jon and Aegon arrives in King's Landing not too much later. Red Ronnet tells Tommen's [small council](wiki:Small_council) that he is intent on retaking his seat, but they are in doubt about his loyalty. Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), Tommen's regent, realizes that Jon could be a dangerous threat.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-6)

### The Winds of Winter

Ser [Addam Whitehead](wiki:Addam_Whitehead) is among a group of men from the [Weeping Town](wiki:Weeping_Town) who seek out Jon at Griffin's Roost.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Winds2_6-1) Jon's letter reaches Doran, who sends his daughter, Princess [Arianne](wiki:Arianne_Martell), to seek out Aegon and Jon, who calls himself the Hand of the True King.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Winds1_9-3) Arianne learns that Jon has marched from Griffin's Roost, and that he has forbidden the sellswords of the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) from raping during their invasion. Lady [Mary Mertyns](wiki:Mary_Mertyns) is skeptical of their conduct, however. Once Arianne reaches Griffin's Roost, Haldon informs her that Jon and Aegon have taken Storm's End, where Arianne is awaited.(wiki:Jon_Connington.cite_ref-Winds2_6-2)
