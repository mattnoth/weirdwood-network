## Origins

Born at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) in [290 AC](wiki:290_AC) at the height of summer, Bran is the second son and fourth child of [Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) and [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), head of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark). He is [named after](wiki:Named_after) his late uncle, Eddard's elder brother [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark), whose death was one of the major events that led to [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) in [282 AC](wiki:282_AC).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

Bran has three older siblings ([Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark), [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark), and [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark)) and one younger ([Rickon](wiki:Rickon_Stark)). He also has a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) half-brother, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow). As a child, Bran was educated by Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) and trained with [weapons](wiki:Armament) by the master-at-arms, Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel). A year before Bran's birth, in [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), Eddard brought to Winterfell a hostage, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), the last surviving son of [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands). Although Robb and Theon became close after [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), Bran never warmed to his father's ward.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Bran enjoys exploring the castle, as he believes it allows him to know its secrets. Since he was a babe, Bran often climbed the walls of Winterfell, scaring Catelyn to death. Once, she pleaded for him to stop, and extracted a promise for him to do so, which he kept for barely a fortnight. When Bran, wracked with guilt, confessed this to Eddard, he ordered his son to the [Winterfell godswood](wiki:Winterfell_godswood) to cleanse himself, where Bran was eventually found asleep in the tallest [sentinel tree](wiki:Sentinel_tree). Ned then accepted his climbing, but told him to try to not let his lady mother see him.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Lady Stark then turned to others in the castle: first, Maester Luwin, who once made a boy out of pottery and threw it from Winterfell's walls to show Bran what would happen if the boy were to fall while climbing, then, to [Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan), who told him a story of a boy who climbed too high, was struck by lightning and had his eyes eaten by [crows](wiki:Crow), and finally the castle guards, who chased Bran from tower to tower in an attempt to haul him down. Each attempt was fruitless, and Bran continued his climbing.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) The boy enjoys swinging from the [gargoyles](wiki:Gargoyle) of the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep) and feeding corn to the crows atop the [broken tower](wiki:Broken_tower).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)

Bran loves hearing Old Nan's stories, preferring the scary ones above all else concerning the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest), the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night), and the [Others](wiki:Others).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) He is also enthralled by tales of [mystery knights](wiki:Mystery_knight) and the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), and asks to hear their stories over and over until their names become like music to him. However, he is disappointed to be informed by his father that the new Kingsguard is not as it once was.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3)

## Appearances & Description

Bran favors his mother [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) in appearance, having the thick auburn hair and deep blue eyes of the [Tullys](wiki:House_Tully).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Bran is a sweet and thoughtful boy, well-loved by everyone at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). He has a fascination with climbing and exploring along the walls and ramparts of the castle. Catelyn once jested that Bran could climb before he could walk, however, his climbing often distresses her.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Like his siblings, Bran is also dutiful and tough-minded, but also possesses a propensity for adventure and excitement; he yearns to see far off places one day and dreams of becoming a [knight](wiki:Knight).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) His half-brother [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) fondly thinks to himself that Bran was always "stubborn and curious", and in like manner constantly wanted to join in the play of the older boys, believing himself an adult at seven.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Bran owns grey breeches and a white doublet, the sleeves and collar of which are trimmed with vair.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) When riding, he wears a dark grey woolen surcoat with [silver](wiki:Silver) buttons, as well as fur-lined boots and gloves.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Bran clasps his fur-trimmed cloak with a heavy silver pin,(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) but the boy's favorite clasp(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) is a [wolf](wiki:Wolf)'s-head brooch of silver and polished [jet](wiki:Jet) which makes him look [lordly](wiki:Lord).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Bran is among a group of twenty who witness his father, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), dispense the king's justice to [Gared](wiki:Gared), a deserter of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), the first time Bran is allowed to accompany such a group.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) When they find a dead [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf) and her pups on the way back to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), Bran passionately protests against killing the pups. Bran and each of the other Stark siblings receives one of the young animals as a pet.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

During the [feast in honor of King Robert's visit to Winterfell](wiki:Feast_in_honor_of_King_Robert's_visit_to_Winterfell), Bran recites the castles of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) in order to his uncle, [Benjen Stark](wiki:Benjen_Stark).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) When Eddard is named [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Bran is excited at the prospect of seeing the knights and royalty of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), yet he is still apprehensive about leaving.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-4) During the royal visit to Winterfell, Bran fights Prince [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) in the yard under the supervision of the master-at-arms, Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel), which ends in Bran's victory.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

When King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and the older men leave on a hunting trip, Bran begins climbing to Winterfell's [broken tower](wiki:Broken_tower) so he can feed the [crows](wiki:Crow) one last time. When near a window in the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep), however, he discovers Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) and her brother, Ser [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), having sex, although the boy does not understand their actions. Jaime pushes Bran from the window, intending to kill the boy to keep the incestuous love affair a secret,(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5) but Bran survives and enters a coma.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

While Bran is comatose in the [sickroom](wiki:Sickroom), an attempt is made on his life by an unnamed [catspaw](wiki:Catspaw) with a [Valyrian steel dagger](wiki:Valyrian_steel_dagger). His mother, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), delays the assassin long enough for Bran's unnamed direwolf to arrive and tear out his throat.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) In the meantime, Bran has many visions in his coma, including a hazy memory of falling from the tower, a [three-eyed crow](wiki:Three-eyed_crow) that tells him it can teach him to fly, and the [heart of winter](wiki:Land_of_Always_Winter). With the crow's guidance, Bran wakes and immediately names his direwolf "[Summer](wiki:Summer)".(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

Crippled by his fall, Bran is confined to a tower chamber where [Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan) keeps him company. She tells Bran stories of the [north](wiki:North) and the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) When [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) stops at Winterfell on his way back from the [Wall](wiki:Wall), he gives schematics for a saddle designed for Bran's crippled form, despite threats from Bran's brother [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark), acting as Lord of Winterfell, and the direwolves. Bran, however, is overjoyed at the prospect of learning to ride again.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) [Joseth](wiki:Joseth) trains [Dancer](wiki:Dancer) to obey Bran's commands.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) Later, while testing Tyrion's suggestion out on a ride in the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood), Bran is ambushed by a band of [wildlings](wiki:Free_folk) and [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) deserters, but is rescued by Robb, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), [Grey Wind](wiki:Grey_Wind), and Summer.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

Afterwards, Bran begins to bond with the sole survivor of that band, [Osha](wiki:Osha), due to their shared faith in the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods). When Robb calls the northern banners following Lord Eddard's arrest in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Bran witnesses the incident with the [Greatjon](wiki:Jon_Umber), and is the acting Lord of Winterfell when Robb rides south.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Later, Bran has a dream of his father in the [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell). He ventures there with Osha, finding nothing. [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark) also reveals that he dreamed the same dream last night. Almost immediately after leaving the crypts, Maester Luwin receives a raven-borne message informing Winterfell of Ned's execution by King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon)'s command.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

### A Clash of Kings

After [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark) becomes [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), Bran, as Robb's heir, becomes the [Prince of Winterfell](wiki:Prince_of_Winterfell), and he rules the castle in his brother's absence.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) Bran dislikes his mother Catelyn's new wards, [Big Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)) and [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Bran meets several bannermen during the [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast_(299)) and he befriends [Meera](wiki:Meera_Reed) and [Jojen Reed](wiki:Jojen_Reed), children of his father Eddard's most trusted friend, Lord [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

While sleeping, Bran frequently enters [Summer](wiki:Summer)'s mind—though the boy thinks he is simply dreaming—and begins to become more bestial. Jojen recognizes Bran's ability as [skinchanging](wiki:Skinchanger), and instructs him on how to use it properly. After learning of Bran's visions, Jojen claims that if Bran goes north [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall), he can find the [three-eyed crow](wiki:Three-eyed_crow) from his dreams. Maester Luwin, however, does not believe that Jojen has [greensight](wiki:Greensight).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

[Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), Eddard's ward, [captures Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell) and, claiming to be the Prince of Winterfell, holds Bran and Rickon as hostages. Bran, Rickon, Osha, Hodor, and the Reeds escape one night(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) and hide in the [crypts](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) Unable to find them in the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood),(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) Theon has two miller's boys murdered and skinned by "[Reek](wiki:Reek)" and proclaims them to be Bran and Rickon.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Theon prevents Luwin from burying the bodies in the crypts.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1)

At [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), Catelyn is informed that Bran and Rickon were captured by Theon on the [Acorn Water](wiki:Acorn_Water) and their heads are now displayed at Winterfell.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), a captive since the [Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood), admits to Catelyn that he flung Bran from a window but insists he was not involved with the [catspaw](wiki:Catspaw)'s attempt on Bran's life.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)

In the [Skirling Pass](wiki:Skirling_Pass), [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), Bran's half-brother, dreams he is his own direwolf, [Ghost](wiki:Ghost). A dreaming Jon sees a three-eyed Bran in the face of a [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

Theon is eventually betrayed by Reek, actually [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), the bastard of Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton). Men of [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) scatter Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel)'s men in a [battle in the winter town](wiki:Battle_at_Winterfell). Ramsay then orders the killing of Theon's [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) and the [sack of Winterfell](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell), although he spares the Freys.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

Bran and his companions emerge from the crypts to find Winterfell in ruins. The brothers find a mortally-wounded Maester Luwin, who advises their traveling party to split. Rickon and Osha set off in the direction of [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor). Bran, Hodor, and the Reed siblings set off north to seek the [three-eyed crow](wiki:Three-eyed_crow).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)

### A Storm of Swords

The burning of Winterfell is attributed to Theon, and Ramsay is said to be flaying Theon in vengeance for the supposed deaths of Bran and Rickon.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Ramsay conspired with Theon regarding the miller's children, so Bolton men search the [north](wiki:North) for the real Bran and Rickon.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) Bran travels in secret with [Meera](wiki:Meera_Reed), [Jojen](wiki:Jojen_Reed), and [Hodor](wiki:Hodor), avoiding the main roads. They stay at the so-called [Tumbledown Tower](wiki:Tumbledown_Tower) and Jojen warns Bran against staying too long in [Summer](wiki:Summer).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

When the weather turns colder in the [northern mountains](wiki:Northern_mountains), Summer finds shelter and they meet [a Liddle](wiki:The_Liddle) who shares his food with them. When they ask if they should take the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad), the [clansman](wiki:Northern_mountain_clans) tells them that it is treacherous for travelers, as [the war](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) has made everywhere dangerous. The group decide to head to the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort) instead of [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) due to Jojen's [green dream](wiki:Greensight). Meera then tells Bran about the [Knight of the Laughing Tree](wiki:Knight_of_the_Laughing_Tree), a story which the Reeds are surprised that Bran has never heard before.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)

En route to the [Wall](wiki:Wall), the party stops in the [Gift](wiki:Gift) at [Queenscrown](wiki:Queenscrown), an abandoned holdfast surrounded by water, which they are able to get to due to a concealed passageway through the moat remembered by Bran from one of [Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan)'s stories. While hidden inside the tower they spot men in the village. Hodor is terrified by a thunderstorm, so Bran briefly enters the stableboy's mind to calm him.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Summer attacks the men, among whom is Bran's half-brother, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow). Bran then enters Summer's mind and attacks the men trying to slay Jon, giving him the opportunity to escape his [wildling](wiki:Wildling) companions. Summer is wounded by an arrow in the skirmish, but manages to recover after being treated by Meera.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

The troupe eventually reaches the Wall at the Nightfort, and, while sleeping, Bran is awoken by footsteps. At first, the group are terrified to have been discovered, and Bran again enters Hodor's mind. As Meera attacks the intruders, Bran recognizes one as a brother of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) and meets [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly), accompanied by the wildling [Gilly](wiki:Gilly). Sam leads them through a secret door at the bottom of a well that takes them through to the north side of the Wall. There they meet [Coldhands](wiki:Coldhands), an enigmatic figure of uncertain allegiance. Bran, Jojen and Coldhands all extract a promise from Sam that he will tell no one that they are alive. Sam reluctantly agrees, even keeping the secret from Jon Snow, his sworn brother of the Night's Watch.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

Bran's mother, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), and brother, King [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark), are slain in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) The [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) legitimizes Ramsay and names his father, Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

### A Feast For Crows

At the Wall, Samwell is summoned to Jon, the new [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), who mentions his message to the new king, [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), requesting aid for the Watch. He notes bitterly that Bran once bested Tommen at swords in the yard at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell); now Bran is dead, and Tommen sits upon the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Sam wants to tell Jon his brother is alive and [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall), but he keeps his promise and does not say a word.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Bran, [Meera](wiki:Meera_Reed), [Jojen](wiki:Jojen_Reed), [Hodor](wiki:Hodor), and [Coldhands](wiki:Coldhands) continue their journey through the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest) in search of the [three-eyed crow](wiki:Three-eyed_crow). Bran begins to become infatuated with Meera, while Jojen grows thin and weak. The journey is long and cold, and they suffer on the brink of starvation. The group is briefly left by Coldhands, who returns after killing [oathbreakers](wiki:Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep) of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). To try and sate his hunger, Bran feasts on the flesh of the deserters while inside [Summer](wiki:Summer).(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0). Returning to his own skin, Bran eats meat which Coldhands claims to be pig.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1)

As they continue north, Coldhands' elk collapses and is butchered by the ranger and Meera for meat. Despite promising himself he would rather go hungry than feast on a friend, Bran eats the meat twice; once in his own skin, and once in Summer's. The steaks sustain the group for seven days.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

In the depths of the haunted forest, the group is ambushed by [wights](wiki:Wights) outside the [cave of the three-eyed crow](wiki:Cave_of_the_three-eyed_crow), and Bran enters Hodor's mind to fight off the wights. Inside they meet one of the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest), [Leaf](wiki:Leaf), who reveals she saved them by burning the wights. However, the cave is warded against [white walkers](wiki:White_walkers) and wights, so Coldhands cannot accompany them inside. Leaf takes them deeper into the cave, underneath [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) roots and across a floor of bones, until they reach a skeletal body sitting on a throne made of roots - the three-eyed crow, the last [greenseer](wiki:Greenseer). The pale lord tells Bran that the boy will not walk again, but he will fly.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-1)

The three-eyed crow, whom Bran learns was named [Brynden](wiki:Brynden_Rivers),(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) begins to teach Bran about [greensight](wiki:Greensight) and [skinchanging](wiki:Skinchanger). Bran is fed [weirwood paste](wiki:Weirwood_paste), which awakens his greenseeing gifts. He then enters the weirwood roots around them and sees his father, [Eddard](wiki:Eddard_Stark), through [Winterfell's heart tree](wiki:Winterfell_godswood) in the past. Grief-stricken, Bran tries to speak to Ned, but all that is heard is a whisper on the wind. Brynden explains that Bran can learn from the past through the weirwoods, but it is impossible to change it.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1)

Bran continues to enter Hodor to explore the deeper parts of the cave, with the stableboy no longer resisting and the Reeds unaware. When the crow sends him to rest, Bran dreams through the [heart tree](wiki:Heart_tree) in the [godswood of Winterfell](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell) and sees flashes of the past as far back as the old [Kings of Winter](wiki:King_in_the_North):(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-2)

- His [young father](wiki:Eddard_Stark) praying with a bowed head "…let them grow up close as brothers, with only love between them, and let [my lady wife](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) find it in her heart to forgive.";(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-41)
- A girl and a younger boy play fighting with branches;(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-42)
- A pregnant woman coming out of the black pool praying for a son to avenge her;
- A slender girl on her toes kissing a [knight](wiki:Knight) as tall as Hodor;(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-43)
- A pale, dark-eyed youth cutting three branches from the weirwood and shaping them into arrows;(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-44)
- Other lords of Winterfell: tall, hard, stern men in fur and chain mail;
- A bearded man forcing a captive down on his knees, and a white-haired woman killing the captive with a bronze sickle.

A mutilated Theon is brought back to Winterfell by [Roose](wiki:Roose_Bolton) and [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow). After Ramsay weds "[Arya Stark](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)" before the castle's [heart tree](wiki:Heart_tree), Theon thinks he hears a voice whisper his name.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) Later, Theon thinks he sees Bran's face in the weirwood and hears the boy's name spoken by the tree.(wiki:Bran_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)
