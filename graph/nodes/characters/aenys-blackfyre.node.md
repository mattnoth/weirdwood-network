## Origins

After the death of King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) in [233 AC](wiki:233_AC), there was no definitive heir to ascend the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). When the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Brynden "Bloodraven" Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), called a [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council), Aenys put forth his claim to the throne by writing from exile. Bloodraven offered him safe conduct to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), so Aenys could present his claim in person. Aenys accepted this proposal, but as soon as he entered the capital he was seized by the [gold cloaks](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) and dragged to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), where he was executed. His head was presented to the lords of the Great Council, as a warning to any who might still have [Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) sympathies.(wiki:Aenys_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

After King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) was crowned king, he had Bloodraven arrested for the murder of Aenys, as Brynden had broken the word of the Iron Throne with the act. Brynden was sentenced to death but later sent to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) instead, to live out the remainder of his life as a Brother of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Aenys_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
