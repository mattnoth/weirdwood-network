## Origins

Toron was born a [salt son](wiki:Salt_son) of Lord [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), the Red Kraken.(wiki:Toron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Born in [127 AC](wiki:127_AC), Dalton had just claimed his first four [salt wives](wiki:Salt_wife) earlier that year.(wiki:Toron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_red_kraken.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Toron resided with his mother on [Pyke](wiki:Pyke).(wiki:Toron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

In [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), Dalton was killed by one of his salt wives, [Tess](wiki:Tess), at [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle). As he had not taken a [rock wife](wiki:Rock_wife), the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair) passed to Toron by law. A [power struggle](wiki:Iron_Islands_succession_struggle) was inevitable, however, as Toron was not yet six and his salt wife mother could not hope to act as a [regent](wiki:Regent) as a rock wife could.(wiki:Toron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)(wiki:Toron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_red_kraken.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Lord Toron was seized by his aunts and their husbands, and his mother was put to death. Meanwhile, his father's cousins and the lords of [Harlaw](wiki:Harlaw) and [Blacktyde](wiki:Blacktyde) raised up his half-brother [Rodrik](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy_(son_of_Dalton)), while another pretender rose up in the form of [Sam Salt](wiki:Sam_Salt), supposedly descended from the [black line](wiki:House_Hoare). The bloody struggle had raged for half a year when Lady [Johanna Lannister](wiki:Johanna_Lannister) and Ser [Leo Costayne](wiki:Leo_Costayne)'s forces invaded the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC) in retribution for the Red Kraken's invasion of the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands).(wiki:Toron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Lord Toron's defenders beat off the Lannister assault on Pyke, and thus he remained upon the Seastone Chair. However, two of his aunts had died in the conflict, and his brother Rodrik was taken and brought back to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), where he was [gelded](wiki:Eunuch) and made a [fool](wiki:Fool).(wiki:Toron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
