## Appearances & Description

Ser Manfred was a thin man with an unruly mane of red-gold hair. He wore a black wool surcoat slashed with the purple lightning of [House Dondarrion](wiki:House_Dondarrion).(wiki:Manfred_Dondarrion.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

## Origins

In [206 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Ser Manfred accompanied the host of his [lord father](wiki:Lord_Dondarrion_(father_of_Manfred)) and Lord [Pearse Caron](wiki:Pearse_Caron) that defeated the [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Daeron_II)), burning him out of the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains).(wiki:Manfred_Dondarrion.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
Manfred attended the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow) in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC). Having served with the Dondarrions against the Vulture King, Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) thought Manfred could vouch for his [knighthood](wiki:Knight) to the [master of games](wiki:Plummer) at the tourney, however Manfred did not remember him. Ser Duncan recited the origins of House Dondarrion hoping to impress him, but Manfred was uninterested and claimed that he could not help. Later, when Ser Duncan searched for a seventh knight for his [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven), Manfred ignored his call for help, making a comment to the noblewoman beside him.(wiki:Manfred_Dondarrion.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
