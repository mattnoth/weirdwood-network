## Origins

Ser Rodrik Cassel was married three times, and his wives gave birth to several daughters, including Beth's mother. However, he was widowed, and Beth was his only surviving child.(wiki:Beth_Cassel.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Beth was raised at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) with the [Stark](wiki:House_Stark) children. Though closer in age to [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark), she tends to hover around [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark)'s more glamorous retinue.(wiki:Beth_Cassel.cite_ref-Rawoiafbeth_cassel.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Beth is present during [Septa](wiki:Septa) [Mordane](wiki:Mordane)'s session of needlework with Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). She participates in the conversation between [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole), and [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) about Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon). Beth dreamily says that Joffrey will marry Sansa and she will be queen, but as the [betrothal](wiki:Betrothal) has not yet been announced, Sansa gently tells her not to make up stories.(wiki:Beth_Cassel.cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

### A Clash of Kings

When Lady [Donella Hornwood](wiki:Donella_Hornwood) comes to Winterfell, [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) asks Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) why he cannot marry her to solve the problems of her security, as he knows Rodrik thinks Lady Donella is pretty, and Beth would have a mother. However, Rodrik explains to Bran that he's only a [knight](wiki:Knight) and also is too old, and that after his death Donella would have the same issues, and Beth's prospects could also be endangered.(wiki:Beth_Cassel.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

During the closing banquet of the [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast_(299)), Beth is seated at the main table beside her father, Ser Rodrik. After the dinner, she is invited to dance by Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) and [Cley Cerwyn](wiki:Cley_Cerwyn).(wiki:Beth_Cassel.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

When [Winterfell is captured](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell) by [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) raiders led by [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), Beth becomes one of the prisoners. Theon has the people of Winterfell brought to the [Great Hall](wiki:Great_Hall_of_Winterfell) so they can hear Bran Stark yield the castle, and Bran sees Beth crying with fear.(wiki:Beth_Cassel.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

When Ser Rodrik tries to take back Winterfell, Theon uses Beth as a [hostage](wiki:Hostage), displaying her to her father standing atop the walls of the castle with a noose around her neck. Theon says she will hang if Rodrik attacks, or if his army still stands outside Winterfell by sunset, followed by more hanged hostages the following sunrise and sunset. Rodrik offers himself in Beth's place, but Theon knows the threat to her is more valuable than having Rodrik as a hostage. Rodrik is anguished, but still determined to attack. However, [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow)'s [treasonous](wiki:Treason) [attack on Rodrik and his men](wiki:Battle_outside_the_gates_of_Winterfell) prevents any harm coming to Beth from Theon.(wiki:Beth_Cassel.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

After [Winterfell's sack](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell) by the men of [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton), the orphaned Beth is taken to the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort).(wiki:Beth_Cassel.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Beth_Cassel.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Storm of Swords

The sack of Winterfell is blamed on Theon's ironborn, who allegedly killed many of Winterfell's people. Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) is heartsick at the thought of the deaths of so many, including Beth, but she is told that Ramsay Snow took the women and children to the safety of the Dreadfort.(wiki:Beth_Cassel.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Beth remains a captive at the Dreadfort.(wiki:Beth_Cassel.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

In [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), Arya Stark, blinded by the [Faceless Men](wiki:Faceless_Men), goes by the name "Blind Beth", possibly because she knew Beth Cassel in Winterfell.(wiki:Beth_Cassel.cite_ref-Radwd45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
