## Origins

Old Nan is the oldest person at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). She came to the castle to [nurse](wiki:Wet_nurse) a [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(son_of_Willam)); she does not recall which one, only that [his mother](wiki:Lyanne_Glover) had died birthing him. Sometimes she says that Brandon Stark was the older brother of Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark), or perhaps his younger brother, or a brother of [Rickard's father](wiki:Edwyle_Stark).(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-7) Nan lost both of her sons during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), her grandson at the [siege of Pyke](wiki:Siege_of_Pyke) during [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), and her daughters moved away and died. Her only living relative is her great-grandson, [Walder](wiki:Hodor), who was called "Hodor" after he started to say that word.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

When Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) was young, Old Nan taught him the proverb "[dark wings, dark words](wiki:Ravens)".(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Old Nan sometimes eats at the table of Lord Eddard.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) The elderly servant often tells stories to Eddard's children,(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) as well as his [ward](wiki:Ward), [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy).(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Theon once carelessly leapt down the stairs of the [Great Keep](wiki:Great_Keep_(Winterfell)) and accidentally knocked Old Nan to the ground, for which he was punished with a thrashing, the worst he ever had at Winterfell.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) The elderly woman sometimes confuses [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) with other Brandon Starks she has known, as well as the various [Brandon Starks](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(disambiguation)) with each other.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) When Lord Eddard would not forbid Bran from climbing the walls of Winterfell, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), Bran's mother, instead turned to others to convince him otherwise, and Old Nan told him a story about a boy who climbed too high and was struck by lightning.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

## Appearances & Description

Nan is an ugly old woman. She is toothless, shrunken and wrinkled. Almost blind, she is too weak to climb stairs. She has only a small amount of hair left to cover her head.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Old Nan is fond of needlework and telling stories.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) According to her, the [Wall](wiki:Wall) marks the end of [the world](wiki:Known_world).(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Occasionally, when annoyed with her great-grandson, [Hodor](wiki:Hodor), she may give him a swat in the head.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

While watching the execution of [Gared](wiki:Gared), a deserter of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) remembers the stories that Old Nan told him about those who live [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall).(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) tells Bran he should make his farewells to the people of Winterfell, and Bran intends to visit them, including Nan, but he becomes too sad, and goes climbing instead.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

Old Nan helps treat Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) after she is attacked with [a dagger](wiki:Valyrian_steel_dagger) by a [catspaw](wiki:Catspaw).(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Nan also cares for Bran as he recovers from his fall from the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep). Her great-grandson, [Hodor](wiki:Hodor), carries around the crippled boy.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

Young [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark) becomes upset when he learns that [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) is departing [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), and the boy punches Nan when she tries to sing him to sleep.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Robb Stark has been acclaimed [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) and [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident), making his brother Bran a prince. However, Nan does not address him with that title, only "boy" and "my little lord", as usual.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Racok4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Bran, curious about the sudden appearance of the [red comet](wiki:Red_comet) in the sky, asks Old Nan what she believes it signifies. She replies that the comet means one thing: [dragons](wiki:Dragon). She is too blind to see the comet, but claims to be able to smell it.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Racok4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

Bran sends sweets to Nan and Hodor during the [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast). At the feast, Nan also picks at a hot pie.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) After [Jojen Reed](wiki:Jojen_Reed) arrives at Winterfell, Nan begins calling him "little grandfather", for his solemn way of talking that makes him seem older and wiser than his age.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Rasos9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

After the [capture of Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell) by thirty [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) raiders led by [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), Old Nan is among the servants forced to serve Theon, who calls himself [Prince of Winterfell](wiki:Prince_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Nan is shocked by Theon's apparent murder of of Bran and Rickon.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

After the [fall of Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal), [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) does not reveal her true identity, but introduces herself to Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) using the alias *Nymeria*, or *Nan* for short.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

[Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) betrays Theon, and orders the garrison of the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort) to burn Winterfell.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Bran worries that Old Nan may have been killed in the [sack of Winterfell](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell), but remembers that he saw no women among the slain.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

[Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), who has not yet heard about the sack of Winterfell, thinks he may have to kill [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) to prevent the wildlings from breaching the [Wall](wiki:Wall) and killing everyone he knows and loves at Winterfell, including Old Nan.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) When [Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte) tells Jon the tale of [Gendel](wiki:Gendel) and [Gorne](wiki:Gorne) and Gendel's [cannibalistic](wiki:Cannibal) children, he tells her she sounds like Old Nan telling Bran a monster story.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

The sack of Winterfell is blamed on Theon's ironborn, who allegedly killed many of Winterfell's people. Catelyn is heartsick at the thought of the deaths of so many, including Nan, but she is told that Ramsay Snow took the women and children to the safety of the Dreadfort.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Old Nan is among the women of Winterfell held captive at the Dreadfort.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

Arya thinks that Old Nan is probably dead.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Raffc6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) [Needle](wiki:Needle) reminds Arya of Nan's stories.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Bran recalls that Old Nan used to tell him that monsters cannot pass the [Wall](wiki:Wall) as long as it stands and the men of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) stay true. Because [Coldhands](wiki:Coldhands) could not pass the Wall, Bran realizes that he is undead.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Radwd4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

Bran is fed [weirwood paste](wiki:Weirwood_paste) and has [visions](wiki:Vision) of the past, including one of a slender girl on her toes kissing a [knight](wiki:Knight) as tall as Hodor.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-33)(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

According to [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), who was held captive at the Dreadfort, Old Nan is probably dead.(wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

## Notes

Old Nan's stories are a major vehicle for world-building exposition about the Age of Heroes, the Long Night, and the Others. Many of her tales carry potential narrative significance but that analysis belongs to later passes.
