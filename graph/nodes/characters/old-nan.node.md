## Identity

Nan, generally referred to as Old Nan, is a servant woman at Winterfell and the oldest person in the castle (wiki:Old_Nan). She is the great-grandmother of Hodor (Walder) (wiki:Old_Nan).

## Origins

Born between 185–211 AC (track_b: Born). She came to Winterfell to nurse a Brandon Stark whose mother had died birthing him; she no longer remembers which Brandon — perhaps the older or younger brother of Lord Rickard, or a brother of Rickard's father (wiki:Old_Nan).

`SWORN_TO` House Stark (track_b: Allegiance). `CULTURE_OF` Northmen (track_b: Culture).

## Appearances & Description

Nan is an ugly old woman — toothless, shrunken, and wrinkled. Nearly blind, she is too weak to climb stairs and has only a small amount of hair left (wiki:Old_Nan). She is fond of needlework and telling stories (wiki:Old_Nan).

## Narrative Arc

Old Nan lost both sons during Robert's Rebellion, her grandson during the siege of Pyke in Greyjoy's Rebellion, and her daughters moved away and died. Her only living relative is her great-grandson Hodor (wiki:Old_Nan). She is the primary storyteller at Winterfell, telling tales to the Stark children and Theon Greyjoy. Her stories include accounts of the Others, the Night's King, the Last Hero, and Symeon Star-Eyes (wiki:Old_Nan). In AGOT, she tells Bran stories while he recovers from his fall (wiki:Old_Nan.cite_ref-Ragot24). In ACOK, she continues her role as storyteller. After the sack of Winterfell by Ramsay Snow, Old Nan is taken as a captive to the Dreadfort along with other surviving women and children (wiki:Old_Nan). In ADWD, Wyman Manderly tells Davos Seaworth that the Freys and Boltons have not returned Old Nan to the Starks' allies (wiki:Old_Nan).
