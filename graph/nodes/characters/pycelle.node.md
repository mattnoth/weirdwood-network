## Origins

Pycelle forged his maester's chain in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) as a young man during the hot summer in the reign of King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

After Grand Maester [Merion](wiki:Merion) died on his way to serve [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), Pycelle was granted his position. He has been the Grand Maester of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) for forty years, serving Aegon V and [Jaehaerys II](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen) before [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Pycelle wrote *[Observations Upon the Recent Blood-Letting on the Stepstones](wiki:Observations_Upon_the_Recent_Blood-Letting_on_the_Stepstones)* about the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-West_12-0)

During the reign of [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), Pycelle came to greatly admire and respect Aerys's [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). Out of all the members of the [small council](wiki:Small_council), Pycelle assisted Tywin the most in the governance of the realm. Pycelle wrote the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) often, praising Tywin's leadership, stating that the man was born to rule. While Aerys grew increasingly jealous and resentful of Tywin's capability, Pycelle became a staunch ally to the Hand, until Tywn resigned his office in [281 AC](wiki:281_AC).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Aerys also mistrusted his own son and heir, Prince [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), who had become wary of his father's behavior.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) At court tensions were growing between the factions loyal to the king and those loyal to Rhaegar. It fell to Pycelle and the new Hand, Lord [Owen Merryweather](wiki:Owen_Merryweather), to try and keep the peace between the factions. Pycelle even dispatched a letter to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) writing that tensions and division at court strongly resembled those before the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). Pycelle was fearful that a civil war between those loyal to the king and Rhaegar's supporters would break out unless some accord could be reached that would satisfy both factions.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf51.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

Near the end of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Pycelle convinced King Aerys to open his gates to his former Hand, Tywin, in the hope that Tywin would take the throne. This led to the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) During the [coronation of Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Coronation_of_Robert_I_Baratheon), Pycelle was pardoned for his service under Aerys II and allowed to continue on the [small council](wiki:Small_council).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Despite his oath to serve the realm, Pycelle has generally promoted the interests of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) on the council for years.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

## Appearances & Description

Pycelle has only sparse hair that runs around his bald, spotted head.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) He has a long, snowy beard that runs down to his chest(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) and broad stomach.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Pycelle often strokes the beard, which hides his small mouth, missing teeth, warts, and wrinkles.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) He has pale, rheumy eyes(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) and splotchy pink skin.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Pycelle's maester's chain consists of two dozen heavy chains wound together to stretch from neck to breast.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) Its links consist of [brass](wiki:Brass), [bronze](wiki:Bronze), [copper](wiki:Copper), [iron](wiki:Iron), [gold](wiki:Gold), [lead](wiki:Lead), [platinum](wiki:Platinum), [silver](wiki:Silver), [steel](wiki:Steel), and [tin](wiki:Tin), and the chain is decorated with [amethysts](wiki:Amethyst), black [pearls](wiki:Pearl), [emeralds](wiki:Emerald), [garnets](wiki:Garnet), and [rubies](wiki:Rubies).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) Instead of the grey robes worn by most maesters, Pycelle wears a red velvet robe with an ermine collar and golden fastenings.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Pycelle bends the vows of his order to suit his needs, indulging the [needs of the flesh](wiki:Pycelle's_serving_girl)(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and dabbling in [poisons](wiki:Poison)(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and politics with a skill few maesters dare approach. He enjoys sweetened iced milk.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

[Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) reports to her sister, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), that Pycelle was helpless in saving the life of Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

Pycelle convenes an urgent meeting of the [small council](wiki:Small_council) upon Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark)'s arrival from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to take up the role of [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) When Eddard makes inquiries about the death of the previous Hand, Lord Arryn, Pycelle states that, from his experience, Jon's [illness](wiki:Illness) did not seem suspicious. When confronted with Lysa's accusations that [poison](wiki:Poison) was the culprit, he points the finger of suspicion on Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) Pycelle provides Eddard with *[The Lineages and Histories of the Great Houses of the Seven Kingdoms, With Descriptions of Many High Lords and Noble Ladies and Their Children](wiki:The_Lineages_and_Histories_of_the_Great_Houses_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms,_With_Descriptions_of_Many_High_Lords_and_Noble_Ladies_and_Their_Children)*, which Lord Arryn had been reading.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) The Grand Maester supports assassinating [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) before she can provide Khal [Drogo](wiki:Drogo) with an heir.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

After Eddard is injured in a street fight with Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), Pycelle tasks himself with maintaining Eddard's healing.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) When it is discovered Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) had led raids into the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), burning several villages, Pycelle tries to delay any action taken against Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). Eddard becomes more and more convinced that Pycelle is the creature of Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Pycelle tends to King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s wounds after he is injured by a [boar](wiki:Boar) in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), and he reports to Eddard upon Robert's death.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) He is present when Eddard presents Robert's will and is arrested at court.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Pycelle wears black to mourn Robert's death.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) The new king, [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), has the Grand Maester read royal decrees at his first court.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) Under Cersei's direction, Pycelle visits the grief-stricken [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) after her father Eddard's death and examines her intrusively before giving her a potion to calm her.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

### A Clash of Kings

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), serving as Hand, is not sure whom he can trust, so he tells different secrets to [Varys](wiki:Varys), [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), and Pycelle, tasking the Grand Maester with sending two copies of a single letter to [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne). Pycelle does send one copy, but shares the other one with Cersei, who proceeds to confront Tyrion, unwittingly falling in Tyrion's trap. In the time it takes Pycelle to send one of the letter's copies, Tyrion has time to help himself to a mild poison from the maester's cabinet, which he uses later on Cersei. Pycelle is present when Tyrion gives his terms for peace with the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

At midnight, Tyrion takes [Shagga](wiki:Shagga) and [Timett](wiki:Timett) and confronts Pycelle in his chambers, where he is found naked in bed with a [servant girl](wiki:Pycelle's_serving_girl). With help from Shagga, Tyrion intimidates Pycelle, who wets the bed in fear of the [clansmen](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans). While Shagga shaves off Pycelle's signature white beard with his axe, Tyrion learns Pycelle has betrayed the realm over and over in service to House Lannister. He allowed [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) to die when he could have easily saved him, convinced [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) to open the gates to Tywin's forces during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), and would have finished Robert off himself if the wounds from the boar had not been so fatal.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)

Pycelle justifies Jon's death, since he was close to the truth about Cersei's incest with her brother, Ser [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), and King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon)'s parentage. He admits Maester [Colemon](wiki:Colemon) was treating Lord Arryn properly but the queen needed him dead. Pycelle justifies Robert's death, since his brother [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) had been plotting with the [Tyrells](wiki:House_Tyrell) to kill Cersei and wed [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) to Robert. Tyrion has Pycelle thrown in one of the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells) and stripped of his title and seat in the council.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)

### A Storm of Swords

Pycelle is restored to his position by Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) after he learns the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) is planning on naming [Gormon Tyrell](wiki:Gormon_Tyrell) as Pycelle's replacement.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

Pycelle, with his beard shaved off and weakened from his time in the black cells, appears to be a broken man. He falls asleep during the wedding of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) and [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) and is unable to take any action to prevent [Joffrey's death](wiki:Purple_Wedding) at the banquet from poison, something that sours Queen Cersei's opinion of him. During the trial of Tyrion Lannister for Joffrey's murder, Pycelle is called as a witness for the prosecution to discuss poisons and how Tyrion might have killed the King.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

Pycelle continues his service to the newly-crowned King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon). He is ordered by Tywin to heal Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane), who was poisoned by Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) in the [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) and is slowly dying in agony. Tywin plans on having Gregor executed to appease [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell) but needs it to be seen to be the [King's Justice](wiki:King's_Justice) who does the deed, not poison. Pycelle is unsuccessful and Tywin refuses his suggestion of confining the other Dornishmen till they reveal what poison Oberyn used as Tywin cannot risk further offending [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). Tywin fears they might join [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) which would prolong [the war](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) for years.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Following Tywin's death, the relationship between Pycelle and Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) becomes more strained. He opposes the naming of [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn), a disgraced maester, to the [small council](wiki:Small_council). Cersei blames him for the deplorable state of Lord Tywin Lannister's corpse.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) [Taena of Myr](wiki:Taena_of_Myr) casts suspicion on Pycelle when she reports to Cersei that he is found often in the company of Queen [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) Pycelle objects to many of Cersei's edicts, such as her refusing to honor the debts the crown owes the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos) and her decision to allow the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) to re-institute the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant). After pressure from Cersei, Pycelle admits publicly to bringing Margaery [moon tea](wiki:Moon_tea), a contraceptive. This discovery is crucial in Cersei's efforts to frame Margaery for adultery and treason, delivering her to the captivity of the [new High Septon](wiki:High_Sparrow).(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

Cersei considers having him replaced, and Pycelle's future at court looks uncertain. However, when Cersei is arrested by the Faith, Pycelle successfully seizes control of the council, as [Aurane Waters](wiki:Aurane_Waters) and [Orton Merryweather](wiki:Orton_Merryweather) have abandoned the capital. He removes [Osfryd Kettleblack](wiki:Osfryd_Kettleblack) as commander of the [gold cloaks](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) so Osfyrd will not be able to use the Watch to try and free Cersei and his brother [Osney](wiki:Osney_Kettleblack), both of whom are prisoners of the Faith. He also raises [Humfrey Waters](wiki:Humfrey_Waters) of the [Dragon Gate](wiki:Dragon_Gate) to command the Watch. Qyburn states to Cersei that the realm is effectively being ruled by Pycelle and [Harys Swyft](wiki:Harys_Swyft). Pycelle dispatches a message to Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) offering him the [regency](wiki:Regent).

### A Dance with Dragons

Pycelle continues his duty as Grand Maester but is wary of Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell), who is wroth with Pycelle for revealing Maergary's requests for moon tea. He requests guards from Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) out of fear for his personal safety. Pycelle's fears prove justified, as he is [killed in his chambers](wiki:Assassinations_of_Pycelle_and_Kevan_Lannister), not by Tyrell men but by Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys), who removes both him and Kevan to ensure they cannot repair the damage Cersei had done to the kingdom and the Lannister-Tyrell alliance.(wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

Pycelle served six kings during his time as [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester): [Aegon V](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), [Jaehaerys II](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen), [Aerys II](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), [Robert I](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), [Joffrey I](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), and [Tommen I](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).
