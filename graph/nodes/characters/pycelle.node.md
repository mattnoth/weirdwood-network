## Identity

Grand Maester Pycelle is a maester of the Citadel who has served in King's Landing and on the small council for over forty years, serving six kings: Aegon V, Jaehaerys II, Aerys II, Robert I, Joffrey I, and Tommen I (wiki:Pycelle).

## Origins

Born 216 AC (track_b: Born). Died 300 AC, aged 84, in the Red Keep, King's Landing (track_b: Died). Forged his chain at Oldtown during the reign of Maekar I Targaryen (wiki:Pycelle).

## Appearances & Description

Sparse hair around a bald, spotted head. Long snowy beard running to his chest and broad stomach, hiding a small mouth, missing teeth, warts, and wrinkles. Pale, rheumy eyes and splotchy pink skin. His maester's chain consists of two dozen heavy links wound together — brass, bronze, copper, iron, gold, lead, platinum, silver, steel, and tin — decorated with amethysts, black pearls, emeralds, garnets, and rubies. Instead of grey robes, he wears a red velvet robe with ermine collar and golden fastenings (wiki:Pycelle).

## Narrative Arc

Pycelle rose to Grand Maester after Merion died en route to serve Aegon V. He came to greatly admire Tywin Lannister during Aerys II's reign, becoming a staunch Lannister ally. Near the end of Robert's Rebellion, Pycelle convinced Aerys to open his gates to Tywin, leading to the Sack of King's Landing (wiki:Pycelle).

In AGOT, Pycelle deflects suspicion about Jon Arryn's death while secretly having let Arryn die rather than saving him. He serves on Robert's small council, supports assassinating Daenerys, and reads decrees for Joffrey's first court (wiki:Pycelle). In ACOK, Tyrion exposes Pycelle as Cersei's spy through his scheme with different secrets. Pycelle confesses years of treachery for House Lannister — letting Jon Arryn die, convincing Aerys to open the gates to Tywin. Tyrion imprisons him in the black cells and has Shagga shave his beard (wiki:Pycelle).

In ASOS, Tywin restores Pycelle to block the Citadel from naming Gormon Tyrell as replacement. A weakened, beardless Pycelle falls asleep during Joffrey's wedding and is unable to prevent the king's poisoning. He testifies against Tyrion at trial. Tywin orders him to heal the poisoned Gregor Clegane, but he fails (wiki:Pycelle). In AFFC, Pycelle opposes Qyburn's appointment and clashes with Cersei over her decisions. He admits providing Margaery with moon tea, enabling Cersei's case against her. After Cersei's arrest, Pycelle seizes control of the council and offers Kevan the regency (wiki:Pycelle).

In ADWD, Pycelle continues as Grand Maester but is killed by Varys in his chambers — the same night Varys assassinates Kevan Lannister — to ensure neither can repair the realm (wiki:Pycelle.cite_ref-Radwd_epilogue).
