## Identity

Myros — Grand Maester Myros alone dared speak openly against Maegor's marriage to Tyanna and was slain on the spot.

## Origins

After returning to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) following his victory at the [Battle at the Great Fork](wiki:Battle_at_the_Great_Fork), King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) declared his intent to take [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower) as his third wife. Myros dared to speak against the [marriage](wiki:Marriage) openly, stating that Maegor's true wife, [Ceryse Hightower](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower), awaited at [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown). Maegor heard him out in silence before drawing [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre) and killing Myros where he stood.(wiki:Myros.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Myros was one of three [Grand Maesters](wiki:Grand_Maester) were executed during Maegor's reign,(wiki:Myros.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) the others being [Gawen](wiki:Gawen) and [Desmond](wiki:Desmond_(Grand_Maester)).(wiki:Myros.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
