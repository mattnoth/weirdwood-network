## Origins

Janos was born the son of a butcher in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). While captain of the [Iron Gate](wiki:Iron_Gate), he was raised to Commander of the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing), the so-called gold cloaks, after the death of the previous commander, [Manly Stokeworth](wiki:Manly_Stokeworth).(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

During his rule as commander of the gold cloaks, Janos was known for taking bribes and selling positions and promotions. By the end of his tenure, over half the officers were paying him part of their salaries. Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), discovered this, and had two men who were willing to come forward to testify against Janos, but both were found dead a short time later. Robert ackowledged Janos's corruption, but allowed him to remain in his position, arguing his successor might be worse. The king's brother, Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), was certain Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) had convinced Robert to retain Janos.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

## Appearances & Description

Janos is a stout man, with a jowly face and a bald pate.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) He is frog-faced, and built like a keg.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) He wears ornate gold-and-black plate, with a high-crested helm.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Janos is the Commander of the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) when Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) becomes [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) after the death of Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn). Before the start of the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney), Janos comes before the [small council](wiki:Small_council) and reports about the disturbances caused by people flooding the city. He asks for more men to keep the peace. Eddard grants Slynt to hire fifty more men and orders the [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), to come up with the money to pay them. He also promises Janos twenty men of his own household guards as reinforcements for the time of the tourney.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

After King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) is injured while [boar](wiki:Boar) hunting in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), Eddard plans to proclaim Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) as the new king once Robert has died, implying a likely confrontation with Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), who wants to install her son, Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) instead. Given the strength of the [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) force commanded by Cersei, Eddard sees the loyalty of the City Watch as crucial to the success of his plan, so he contacts Petyr, who promises him to buy the loyalty of Janos and his men for six thousand gold pieces.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

When Eddard challenges Joffrey's legitimacy in the throne room and calls on Janos to put Cersei and her children under arrest, Janos betrays him. The gold cloaks begin to murder the [Stark household guards](wiki:House_Stark_guards), and Janos kills [Varly](wiki:Varly) by slashing his throat.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Musing in his cell about why his actions have failed, Eddard damns Janos as one of the people who have betrayed him and [the realm](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), yet he ultimately blames himself the most.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Janos is raised to [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal) for his services to the crown, and is named a member of the small council. The appointment of a commoner to such a high position causes resentment among nobles at court.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Janos chooses a bloody spear on a black field as his sigil. He adds golden scales to his black doublet, and wears a checkered cape of black and gold.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

Janos laughs when Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) is dismissed and replaced as [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard).(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) After recording his dismissal in the [White Book](wiki:White_Book)(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Racok67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) and gather his possessions in the [White Sword Tower](wiki:Red_Keep), Barristan is confronted by Janos and three gold cloaks in the stables. The seasoned knight, only armed with a knife, escapes them, killing one gold cloak and riding through Janos and the other two. The commander sends men after the former Kingsguard, but Barristan the Bold overcomes the guards at the [Mud Gate](wiki:River_Gate) and kills one of his pursuers before fleeing [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

At the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), Janos and Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne) follow King Joffrey's order to execute Eddard,(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) with Janos displaying Ned's head to the crowd.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Afterward, Lord Slynt attends another court session, in which Joffrey hands out harsh sentences in matters brought before him, nodding in approval to every action of the king.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) is aghast at the granting of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) to Janos, the son of a butcher. He orders his son, [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), to go to King's Landing and act as Hand of the King in his absence.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

### A Clash of Kings

Lord Janos sits on the small council when [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) arrives in King's Landing. Janos is later stripped of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) and forcibly sent to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) by Tyrion along with the men he has suggested as replacements for him as Commander of the City Watch. He is replaced by Ser [Jacelyn Bywater](wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater),(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) though Tyrion believes that in getting rid of Slynt for Bywater, he has merely replaced a man loyal to [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) with one loyal to [Varys](wiki:Varys).

Tyrion's action against Janos displeases his sister, Queen [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister).(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Petyr cites Janos's fate when Tyrion offers him Harrenhal as a gift for his services to the Crown, pointing out that Janos had received Harrenhal for the same reason and yet wound up being forced to [take the black](wiki:Night's_Watch). Tyrion replies that, while he had no use for Slynt, the same is not true for Baelish.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) Rumor spreads among the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) that Janos was exiled because he was too bluff and honest for Tyrion.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Not knowing that [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) is dead, [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh) of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) sends an appeal to the [five kings at war](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), in which he asks them to send men to defend the [Wall](wiki:Wall) and the realm against an expected attack by [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder)'s [wildlings](wiki:Free_folk). He also tells them about the possible death of [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) [Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont) during the [great ranging](wiki:Great_ranging). Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), annoyed about the neutrality of the Night's Watch, suggests Janos become the new Lord Commander as a [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) ally against the objections of Tyrion.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Tywin orders Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) to write to Bowen in the name of King [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), in which the demand for men is rejected for the time being but the "fondest regards to his faithful friend and servant Lord Janos Slynt" are also expressed. Tyrion regrets that he did not leave orders for Janos to be disposed of the way his partner in crime [Allar Deem](wiki:Allar_Deem) was.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

Janos arrives at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) and hauls [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) before him on charges of treason and oathbreaking. He allies with Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne) and Ser [Glendon Hewett](wiki:Glendon_Hewett) and has [Rattleshirt](wiki:Rattleshirt) confirm that Jon killed [Qhorin Halfhand](wiki:Qhorin_Halfhand). Janos has Jon arrested and placed in an [ice cell](wiki:Ice_cell).(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

After being prevented by Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) from hanging Jon, Janos sends Jon to assassinate Mance Rayder, hoping that Jon will be killed in the process. [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s army arrives and [captures](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall) Mance, however.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

Janos puts himself forth as a candidate to succeed Jeor Mormont as [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), with the support of Alliser and later [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh).(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Janos also tries to persuade Stannis to support him, but Stannis's personal dislike for Janos over his corruption, and his distaste for the man's sycophantic flattery, puts an end to it. Due to the rivalry between [Cotter Pyke](wiki:Cotter_Pyke) and Ser [Denys Mallister](wiki:Denys_Mallister), Slynt's chances improve. However, in the end, Janos is defeated by the efforts of [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly), who persuades Cotter and Denys to support Jon Snow as a compromise candidate.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Janos informs the Lannister court of events at the Wall, sending a letter to Grand Maester Pycelle telling of Stannis trying to make common cause with the wildlings.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

### A Dance With Dragons

[Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), now Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, decides to give Janos command of the abandoned way castle [Greyguard](wiki:Greyguard), in order to avoid Janos undermining his authority with his scheming and to separate him from his ally Alliser Thorne.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

However, Janos refuses to follow Jon's orders, first in private, and then the next morning refusing him publicly. Jon, tired of Janos's insubordination, and knowing either imprisoning him or forcing him to go would only cause further problems, orders that Janos be taken to the top of the [Wall](wiki:Wall) and hanged. The arrogant Janos threatens [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s retribution, unaware that Tywin has died. As Janos protests, Jon changes his mind, and orders his men to fetch a chopping block so he can carry out the beheading himself, under the laws of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) his father had followed. Janos begs for mercy with his head on the block, but Jon quickly beheads him with [Longclaw](wiki:Longclaw). A watching [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) approves of Jon's decision.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

Janos's accusations toward Jon are remembered by some of the men at Castle Black, hampering the new Lord Commander's support.(wiki:Janos_Slynt.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
