## Origins

### Legend

According to [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) legend, Lord [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_daughterless)), the liege of the [north](wiki:North), once called Bael a coward. To take revenge for this affront and prove his courage, Bael climbed the [Wall](wiki:Wall), took the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad), and entered [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) under the guise of a singer named "Sygerrik of [Skagos](wiki:Skagos)". (*Sygerrik* means "deceiver" in the [Old Tongue](wiki:Old_Tongue).) There, he sang until midnight for the lord.(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Impressed by his skills as a singer, Lord Stark asked Bael what he wanted as a reward, but he requested only the most beautiful flower blooming in [Winterfell's gardens](wiki:Glass_Gardens). As the [winter roses](wiki:Winter_rose) were just blooming, Brandon Stark presented him with one. The following morning, the [maiden](wiki:Maiden) daughter of Lord Stark, his only child, had disappeared, and in her bed was the blue winter rose.(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Lord Brandon sent the members of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) looking for them [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall), but they never found Bael or the girl. The Stark line was on the verge of extinction, when one day the girl was back in her room, holding in her arms an infant: they had actually never left Winterfell, staying hidden in [the crypts](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell). Bael's [bastard](wiki:Bastard) with Brandon's daughter became the new Lord Stark.

Thirty years later, Bael was [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall) and led the [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings)' army south, and he had to fight his own son at the [Frozen Ford](wiki:Frozen_Ford). There, incapable of killing his own blood, he let himself be killed by Lord Stark. His son brought back Bael's head to Winterfell, and his mother who had loved the bard, seeing the trophy, killed herself by leaping from the top of a tower. The son was eventually slain by the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton).(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

### Legacy

The legend of Bael the Bard is recalled in [songs](wiki:Songs) of the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk). [Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte) learned the song from her mother,(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) and [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) often sang it at the [Shadow Tower](wiki:Shadow_Tower) after rangings.(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Racok53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Bael is not mentioned in the old chronicles at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell),(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_wildlings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) and although [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) learned stories from Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) and [Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan) and heard of Bael before,(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) he had never heard of the legend before Ygritte's telling of it and because of its many discrepancies is certain that the story is a lie.(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

Additionally, the timeframe of Lord Brandon's existence is unclear as well. [Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte) claims Brandon lived "a long time back".(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) As both Brandon and his grandson are given the title "Lord" instead of "[King](wiki:King_in_the_North)" this seems to indicate that the disappearance of Brandon's daughter took place after the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) had given up their kingship during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Furthermore, the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) is mentioned, implying it took place during or after the reign of [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), during whose reign the kingsroad was built.(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) On the other hand, the story states that Bael's son was killed by one of his own lords who wore his skin afterwards. The habit of flaying an enemy and wearing his skin was practiced by the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton), but they are said to have given up the practice "a thousand years ago".(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

[Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte) tells [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) about the legends of Bael. Jon had never heard the story at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)

### A Storm of Swords

[Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder), the [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall), tells Jon that the tale of Bael inspired his infiltration of Winterfell to attend the feast with King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

In order to rescue "[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark)" (actually [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)) from Winterfell, Mance pretends to be a musician and takes the alias of "Abel", an anagram of "Bael".(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Bael_the_Bard.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
