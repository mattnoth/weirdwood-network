## Origins

Chain's great-grandsire had fought for [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) in the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field) and fled to exile across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) with [Bittersteel](wiki:Bittersteel). His father was a sellsword of the Company, and fathered Chain on a [camp follower](wiki:Camp_follower). He was raised to speak the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue) and think of himself as [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Chain.cite_ref-Ar2_2-2)

## Appearances & Description

As a [serjeant](wiki:Serjeant), Chain is a high-ranking officer.(wiki:Chain.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) He claims to be a [knight](wiki:Knight). A brusque man, Chain is taller than [Young John Mudd](wiki:Young_John_Mudd), and wears two rusted chains across his chest. Instead of a blade, he uses five feet of heavy linked [iron](wiki:Iron) as a whip.(wiki:Chain.cite_ref-Ar2_2-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

Chain, like the rest of the company, swears his allegiance to [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) in [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys) before they [invade Westeros](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company).(wiki:Chain.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

### The Winds of Winter

Chain and [Young John Mudd](wiki:Young_John_Mudd) hold [Mistwood](wiki:Mistwood) for the Golden Company. They keep Lady [Mary Mertyns](wiki:Mary_Mertyns) under house arrest and host [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell)'s party for a night.(wiki:Chain.cite_ref-Ar2_2-3)

While escorting Arianne part of the way to [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost), Chain reveals the story of his family, and tells her he had never set foot in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) before the invasion. He also reveals some details about his fellow sellswords, including [Young John Mudd](wiki:Young_John_Mudd)'s skill with [dice](wiki:Dice), [Two Swords](wiki:Two_Swords)' fondness for red haired women, [Little Pussy](wiki:Little_Pussy) and his lucky [cat](wiki:Cat), the time someone stole [Harry Strickland](wiki:Harry_Strickland)'s favorite [elephant](wiki:Elephant), and other stories of the members of the Golden Company. He also slips that Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) is planning to seize [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Chain.cite_ref-Ar2_2-4)
