## Identity

Donal Noye is an armorer, smith, and steward of the Night's Watch, formerly a smith and armorer for House Baratheon at Storm's End (wiki:Donal_Noye).

## Origins

Born around 252 AC in the Stormlands (track_b: Born).

## Appearances & Description

Donal has a big gut and a barrel chest, a flat broad nose, and jaws bristling with black stubble. He is muscular and strong despite missing his left arm. He pins the empty sleeve with a silver pin shaped like a longsword. He sometimes works bare-chested under a leather apron. His few possessions include a silver drinking cup, six pennies, a copper star, a niello brooch, and a musty brocade doublet with the Baratheon stag (wiki:Donal_Noye).

## Narrative Arc

Donal served House Baratheon at Storm's End, where he knew Lord Steffon and saw his sons as children. He forged the warhammer that killed Prince Rhaegar Targaryen at the Trident and made the first sword for Stannis Baratheon. He lost an arm to a festering axe wound during the siege of Storm's End in Robert's Rebellion, after which he joined the Night's Watch. At Castle Black, he lectured Jon Snow about fighting unfairly against untrained recruits, helping Jon become a better comrade. He famously characterized the three Baratheon brothers: Robert was the true steel, Stannis was iron that would break before bending, and Renly was copper — bright and shiny but not worth much. He refitted armor for Samwell Tarly and repaired Longclaw after the wight attack. During the wildling assault on Castle Black, Donal took command of the defense at the gate and died fighting a giant in 300 AC (wiki:Donal_Noye, agot-jon-03).
