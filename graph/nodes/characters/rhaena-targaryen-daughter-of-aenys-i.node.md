## Identity

Queen Rhaena Targaryen was the eldest of the six children of King Aenys I Targaryen and Queen Alyssa Velaryon. She was a dragonrider who rode the dragon Dreamfyre (wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)).

## Origins

Born 23 AC at Dragonstone (track_b: Born). Valyrian race, Crownlands culture (track_b: Race, Culture).

## Appearances & Description

Lively lilac eyes and silver-gold hair. Shy and dreamy as a child, more comfortable with animals than people. Had an exquisite singing voice. By eighteen, considered brave, fierce, and headstrong. Later became sometimes stern and sometimes shy, frequently brooding. In her final years, her hair turned completely white (wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)).

## Narrative Arc

Named in honor of Aenys's late mother, Queen Rhaenys. As the first grandchild of Aegon I, her birth raised questions about the succession relative to her uncle Maegor. Queen Visenya proposed betrothing Maegor to infant Rhaena, but both parents and the High Septon refused.

Rhaena bonded with Dreamfyre and overcame her childhood shyness. She married her brother Prince Aegon, but when their father King Aenys died, Maegor usurped the throne and killed Aegon in battle (43 AC). Rhaena disguised her twin daughters Aerea and Rhaella and sent them into hiding. When summoned to King's Landing in 47 AC, Rhaena was forced to marry Maegor, becoming one of his Black Brides. She later fled on Dreamfyre to join her brother Jaehaerys's rebellion against Maegor.

Under Jaehaerys I, Rhaena was called the Queen in the West during her time in the westerlands and the Queen in the East during her years holding Dragonstone. She married Androw Farman as her third husband and was close to Elissa Farman. After numerous betrayals and tragedies, Rhaena became withdrawn and detached, eventually settling at Harrenhal where she died in 73 AC aged 50 (wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)).
