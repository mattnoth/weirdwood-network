## Identity

Aegon I Targaryen (27 BC–37 AC) — child of Valaena Velaryon and Aerion Targaryen (son of Daemion); married to Rhaenys Targaryen and Visenya Targaryen; Lord of Dragonstone. One of 12 Aegon Targaryens — see the Aegon Targaryen disambiguation entry.

## Origins

### Early life

Aegon was born on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island))(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafaegon_i_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) in [27 BC](wiki:27_BC) to Lord [Aerion Targaryen](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen_(son_of_Daemion)) and Lady [Valaena Velaryon](wiki:Valaena_Velaryon). He had an older sister, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), and a younger sister, [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen). According to [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) and [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) tradition, Aegon married [within the family](wiki:Incest), to keep the bloodlines pure; however, instead of taking only one sister to wife, as was expected, Aegon married both of them. It is said that he married Visenya out of duty, and Rhaenys out of desire. The taking of a [second wife](wiki:Polygamy) was unusual, but not without precedent among the [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlord).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)

Aegon had claimed the dragon [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) for his own prior to his marriage.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7) When [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) asked Aegon for his aid in their [struggle](wiki:Century_of_Blood) against [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), Aegon, [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), flew to Pentos atop of Balerion to meet with the [Prince of Pentos](wiki:Prince_of_Pentos) and the city's [magisters](wiki:Magister). Aegon next flew to [Lys](wiki:Lys), where he burned a Volantene fleet before it could attempt to invade the city. Shortly after, Aegon returned to Dragonstone.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### The Conquest

As the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood) came to an end, Aegon's interest in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) grew. He had previously visited the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) and the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor) in the [Kingdom of the Reach](wiki:Kingdom_of_the_Reach) with his elder sister [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), and he might also have visited [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) in the [Kingdom of the Rock](wiki:Kingdom_of_the_Rock).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8) He had a huge wooden table made, cut in the shape of Westeros with a map of the seven kingdoms of Westeros painted upon the surface. No borders were painted upon the [Painted Table](wiki:Painted_Table), however, as Aegon believed it should be one realm instead of seven.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

The [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King), [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon), reached out to Aegon, proposing a [marriage](wiki:Marriage) between Aegon and his only daughter, Princess [Argella](wiki:Argella_Durrandon). All the lands east of the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye) from the [Trident](wiki:Trident) to the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) were offered as her [dowry](wiki:Dowry), although the lands did not belong to Argilac. His offer was an attempt to create a buffer between his own kingdom and that of [Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare), [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers). Aegon spurned the offer, stating he had no need of a [third wife](wiki:Polygamy), and instead offered his close friend and rumored [bastard](wiki:Bastard) half-brother, [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon), as Argella's husband instead, in return for all the lands surrounding the [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay), including those possessed by Argilac. Insulted, Argilac cut off the hands of Aegon's envoy and sent those back to Dragonstone.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Aegon called his supporters together in response to Argilac. After six days of consulting, Aegon sent forth [ravens](wiki:Raven) to every lord in Westeros, declaring his claim to rule the continent as its sole king.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9) Aegon and his sisters prayed to [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) in the [sept at Dragonstone](wiki:Sept_at_Dragonstone) the night before beginning their war.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Aegon [landed with his army](wiki:Aegon's_Landing) on the eastern coast of Westeros at the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), where he constructed the wooden [Aegonfort](wiki:Aegonfort). Houses [Rosby](wiki:Yielding_of_Rosby) and [Stokeworth](wiki:Submission_of_Stokeworth) surrendered to his sisters quickly, and Houses [Darklyn](wiki:House_Darklyn) and [Mooton](wiki:House_Mooton) were quickly defeated in [Aegon's first test](wiki:Aegon's_first_test). With the defeated lords gathered to the new Aegonfort, Aegon was [crowned](wiki:Aegon's_coronations) with a [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) circlet by Visenya and hailed as [King of All Westeros](wiki:King_of_All_Westeros) by [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen). He also displayed the three-headed dragon symbol of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) for the first time.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)

The Targaryen siblings departed the Aegonfort, with Aegon marching northwest into the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) to confront Harren Hoare. [Edmyn Tully](wiki:Edmyn_Tully), [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun), declared for Aegon and led the riverlords in revolt against the ruling [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn). When Harren refused to yield [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), Aegon attacked the gigantic new castle with [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), and [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) was extinguished with the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal). The next day Aegon named Edmyn as [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3) Meanwhile, Rhaenys and Orys defeated the [Durrandons](wiki:House_Durrandon) in the [Last Storm](wiki:Last_Storm), while Visenya fought the [Arryns](wiki:House_Arryn) and secured [Crackclaw Point](wiki:Crackclaw_Point).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-4)

After regathering at [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept), the Targaryen siblings advanced against the larger host of the [Two Kings](wiki:Two_Kings). The Targaryen dragons (Balerion, [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes), and [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar)) allowed Aegon to achieve a great victory at the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire), which resulted in the death of [Mern IX Gardener](wiki:Mern_IX_Gardener), [King of the Reach](wiki:King_of_the_Reach), and the surrender of [Loren I Lannister](wiki:Loren_I_Lannister), [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock). Aegon then marched for [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), where he named [Harlan Tyrell](wiki:Harlan_Tyrell) as [Lord Paramount of the Mander](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Mander) after the castle's surrender. The Targaryens again reunited to face the host of [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), but Torrhen chose to kneel to Aegon along the [Trident](wiki:Trident) instead of fighting a battle.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5)

Visenya returned to the Vale, where she achieved the surrender of the Arryns, while Rhaenys flew to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) in an unsuccessful mission to the [Martells](wiki:House_Martell). Aegon, meanwhile, marched to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), which was surrendered to him by Lord [Manfred Hightower](wiki:Manfred_Hightower_(Aegon's_Conquest)) upon the advice of the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon's_Conquest)). Within the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept), the High Septon [declared](wiki:Aegon's_coronations) Aegon to be [King of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men](wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men), [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms), and [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-6)

### Early Reign

Aegon chose to date the beginning of his reign from the day the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon's_Conquest)) anointed him in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Rather than ruling from Oldtown or Dragonstone, Aegon chose to make his seat [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), the new settlement growing around the Aegonfort. He also took the swords of his defeated enemies, forging them into the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Aegon also began what would become known as the [small council](wiki:Small_council), as well as that of the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), with Lord [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon) becoming his first Hand.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-10) Aegon's first established law was the [King's Peace](wiki:King's_Peace), which forbid conflict in the realm without the leave of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Aegon treated the defeated lords with respect and allowed each region to retain its own laws and customs and for the lords to retain both the [right of pit and gallows](wiki:Right_of_pit_and_gallows) and the [first night](wiki:First_night). Aegon often travelled the realm with six [maesters](wiki:Maesters) who educated him on each region's local customs and history.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), the lords of the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters) had declared their independence and crowned Lady [Marla Sunderland](wiki:Marla_Sunderland) as their queen. Following his coronation at Oldtown, King Aegon commanded Lord [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark), the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), to end the rebellion, and he sent Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) on [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) to accompany the northern army led by Ser [Warrick Manderly](wiki:Warrick_Manderly). At the sight of them, the [Sistermen](wiki:Sistermen) abandoned their queen and installed her younger brother, [Steffon](wiki:Steffon_Sunderland), as their new lord. Steffon soon bent the knee, swore his fealty to [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn), and re-entered the King's Peace, ending the rebellion of the [Sistermen's Rebellion](wiki:Sistermen's_Rebellion).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

In [2 AC](wiki:2_AC), Aegon turned his attention to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands), where the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) had been fighting for two years over the kingship. Leading the [invasion of the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands) by several war fleets, Aegon landed on [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk) with [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) to deal with the contenders.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) Aegon personally cut down [Qhorin Volmark](wiki:Qhorin_Volmark) with [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre), while the priest-king [Lodos](wiki:Lodos) walked into the sea, drowning himself. The other contenders quickly bent the knee. Aegon ignored the suggestions of making the ironborn vassals to the [Tullys](wiki:House_Tully) of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) or the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), as well as the suggestion to exterminate the ironborn by [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame). Instead, Aegon allowed the ironborn to name their own lord paramount, for which the ironborn chose [Vickon Greyjoy](wiki:Vickon_Greyjoy) as [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

### First Dornish War

In [3 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) Aegon turned his attention back on [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), the only kingdom remaining unconquered. He first decided to try and bring Dorne under the rule of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) through diplomacy, after a year of negotiations and no progress to show for it though, Aegon decided to take Dorne by force of arms.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) In [4 AC](wiki:4_AC) Aegon launched a new invasion, continuing the [Wars of Conquest](wiki:Wars_of_Conquest). Though initially the conquest of Dorne seemed to succeed, it unraveled quickly and the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War) prolonged and lasted for nine years, and knew many deaths and tragedies.

One of these tragedies was the capture and mutilation of Aegon's friend and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon), by the [Wyl of Wyl](wiki:Wyl_of_Wyl). Following his release from two years of captivity, Orys returned home lacking a swordhand, as did the men who had been taken captive with him. Aegon, intent on revenge, released his dragons and burned the castles of the defiant Dornish [lords](wiki:Lord).

The greatest loss Aegon faced was the death of Queen [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen) at [Hellholt](wiki:Hellholt) in [10 AC](wiki:10_AC), when [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes) fell from the sky (with Rhaenys upon her back) after a shot from a scorpion took the dragon in the eye. The two years that followed are known as the Dragon's Wroth, as Aegon's wrath after Rhaenys's death knew no bounds.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Aegon and [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) placed bounties on the heads of Dornish lords after Rhaenys's death and in turn the Dornish put bounties on the Targaryens. Aegon was attacked three times, with [his guards](wiki:House_Targaryen) saving his life twice.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2) Visenya also defended her brother twice with [Dark Sister](wiki:Dark_Sister),(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2) with one occasion occurring in [10 AC](wiki:10_AC).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) This led to the forming of the elite royal bodyguard known as the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), with Visenya personally choosing the men herself.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthree_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-3)

The attempted conquest of Dorne was called to an end in [13 AC](wiki:13_AC), after a visit from Princess [Deria Martell](wiki:Deria_Martell), the daughter of [Nymor Martell](wiki:Nymor_Martell), the ruling [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne). Deria brought the skull of Meraxes and a letter with her, which was given to Aegon. After reading the letter, Aegon flew on Balerion to Dragonstone. He returned the next day, and agreed to a peace between Dorne and the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

Aegon kept good relations with Princess Deria. He visited [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) with his eldest son, Prince [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), in [23 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) to celebrate the tenth anniversary of the peace between the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and Dorne.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

### Further Reign

The remaining twenty-four years of Aegon's reign were peaceful, so much that the last two decades of his reign were later called the [Dragon's Peace](wiki:Dragon's_Peace), by the [maesters](wiki:Maesters) of the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-3) He spent much of his time consolidating his power by traveling throughout the Seven Kingdoms and building his capital at King's Landing.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) Aegon devoted half of every year to making these [royal progresses](wiki:Royal_progress).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthre_heads_had_the_dragon_-_governance_under_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) During his rule he tread carefully with the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), so they would not oppose him.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Aegon built a grand sept on [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill) and agreed to the construction of an even grander one known as the [Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Sept_of_Remembrance) on [Rhaenys's Hill](wiki:Rhaenys's_Hill).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

King's Landing initially lacked walls, and the likely reason is that the Targaryens likely believed no one would attack a city which housed dragons. However, when he learned about a pirate fleet sacking [Tall Trees Town](wiki:Tall_Trees_Town) in the [Summer Isles](wiki:Summer_Isles) in [19 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aegon came to the realization that he and Visenya were not always in the city. As such, he ordered the construction of massive walls to be built around the city. Construction began in [20 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and was completed in [26 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

After the year [33 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the royal progresses continued, but were now made by his son Aenys and his wife Alyssa, while the aging king remained at home. By this point late in his reign, Aegon decided that the ramshackle Aegonfort was not a suitable seat for a king, so the structure was torn to the ground in [35 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Aegon moved his family and court back to Dragonstone, while he commanded the construction of what would later be called the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). Visenya was placed in charge of the building of the Red Keep, and it was rumored that Aegon had done this so that he would not have to suffer her presence on Dragonstone.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6) The Iron Throne remained at the build site instead of Dragonstone, since it was too heavy to be moved.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

### The Sons of the Dragon

In [7 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aegon became a father for the first time, when his younger sister-wife Rhaenys gave birth to a son: [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen). A sickly baby, there were rumors that he had been fathered by a singer instead of Aegon; even though, once Aenys bonded with his dragon [Quicksilver](wiki:Quicksilver), his health improved and the rumors faded away.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

However, Rhaenys's death in [10 AC](wiki:10_AC) shattered the boy. Aegon grew desperate, and there were worries about whether or not Aenys would even live. As Visenya by then had not gotten pregnant yet, some believed she was barren, and during the times when Aenys's health was still troubled it was rumored at court that Aegon might take another wife. Although Aegon refused to speak his mind on the matter, many lord and knights brought their young daughters to court. In [11 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Queen Visenya announced her pregnancy, and the next year she gave birth to Aegon's second son, [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

Aegon raised Aenys at King's Landing, and took him with him on his progressions. Occasionally, he let Aenys train with his Valyrian steel sword [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre). Lacking daughters, he married Aenys to a cousin, Lady [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), the daughter of his [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships), in [22 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Aenys would have six children with Alyssa, five of whom were born during Aegon's reign.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

### Death

In [33 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aegon made his final progress throughout Westeros,(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2) during which he visited [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8) Afterwards, Prince [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) and his wife [Alyssa](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) made the progresses in Aegon's stead.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC) on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), while telling his two eldest grandsons [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) and [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) the tales of [his conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest) at the [Painted Table](wiki:Painted_Table), Aegon died from a stroke at the age of sixty-three.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Aegon_10-1) Aegon was cremated on Dragonstone, and his funeral pyre was set aflame by [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-9) [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre) was burned along with him, but later retrieved from the pyre by Maegor.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

## Appearances & Description

Aegon was tall and broad-shouldered, with [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes) and silver-gold hair that he wore short, no longer than the bottom of his ears. He was powerful in appearance, and very charismatic and commanding. During [his Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), Aegon typically wore battle [armor](wiki:Armor) composed of a shirt of black scales, greaves, gauntlets, and a cloak, and wielded [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre), a [bastard sword](wiki:Bastard_sword) made of [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel). His [crown](wiki:Crown) was a simple circlet of Valyrian steel, set with big square-cut [rubies](wiki:Ruby).(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-11)

Aegon was seen as an enigma even to his contemporaries. He was a solitary person whose only friend was [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon), who was widely believed to be his [bastard](wiki:Bastard) half-brother. While Aegon was considered to be among the greatest warriors of his time, he took no pleasure in battle, and never entered [tourneys](wiki:Tourney) or the [melee](wiki:Melee). He only rode his [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), for battle or to travel swiftly, not for pleasure. Though many women were attracted to him, Aegon remained faithful to his sister-wives. He left most of the daily governance of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) to them and to his [small council](wiki:Small_council), but quickly took command when necessary. While Aegon was harsh with those who defied him, he was generous to those that bent the knee.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

Aegon is not considered to have been particularly pious.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) According to a semi-canon source, he followed the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) for political reasons.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-12) Aegon was a [dragon dreamer](wiki:Dragon_dream) according to another semi-canon source.(wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-dream_13-0)
