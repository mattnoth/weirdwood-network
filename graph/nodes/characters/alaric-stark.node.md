## Identity

Alaric Stark, Brandon's second son, became Lord of Winterfell and ruled the North twenty-three years, an able but stern man who long blamed Jaehaerys's clemency for Walton's death.

## Origins

Alaric attended the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with his father, Lord [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(father_of_Walton)), and older brother, [Walton](wiki:Walton_Stark_(son_of_Brandon)), in [49 AC](wiki:49_AC).(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Brandon died soon after returning to the [north](wiki:North) and was succeeded by Walton.(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) While pursuing rebels from the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) in [50 AC](wiki:50_AC), Lord Walton was slain [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall) by [giants](wiki:Giants). Now Lord of Winterfell, Alaric blamed King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) for his brother's death, as the rebels had been spared by the young king and sent to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) after the wars of [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Lord Alaric was wed to a [Mormont](wiki:Lady_Mormont_(wife_of_Alaric_Stark)) from [Bear Island](wiki:Bear_Island). They had two sons and a daughter, [Alarra](wiki:Alarra_Stark). Alaric said his wife had taken an axe to a pack of [wolves](wiki:Wolves) when she had been twelve, had killed two of them and had sewed a cloak from their skins. Alaric's wife died in [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

Alaric and his sons alone received Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) during the [royal progress](wiki:Royal_progress) to Winterfell in [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). He asked the queen not to take her [dragon](wiki:Dragons), [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing), within the castle walls, and declined her initial offers to arrange [marriage](wiki:Marriage) alliances for his sons with daughters of great southern lords, by pointing out that [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) keeps the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods). After Alysanne pointed out that several southern houses kept the old gods, he promised to discuss the matter with his sons. Alaric also scoffed at not wanting to have to feast a large royal delegation, citing that "winter is coming", even though it was summer, because they could not feed a thousand men for long.(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

Alaric was uncertain on how to entertain a queen, and unhappy that Jaehaerys had not come with Alysanne. However, he warmed to the queen, and eventually took her hunting in the [wolfswood](wiki:Wolfswood) for [elk](wiki:Elk) and wild [boar](wiki:Boar), and showed her the bones of a [giant](wiki:Giant). He also allowed her to rummage as she pleased through his modest castle library. When Alysanne continued her journey north, Alaric sent word ahead to [Lothor Burley](wiki:Lothor_Burley), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), informing him of her upcoming arrival at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black). Alaric was cold toward Jaehaerys when the king arrived, showing him Walton's tomb in the [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)

According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), Alysanne charmed a reluctant Alaric into granting the [New Gift](wiki:New_Gift) to the Night's Watch because, even though Alaric was a strong friend to the Watch, he knew the northern lords who already held the lands in question would not be happy to give them away.(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8) According to Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), however, the Starks were opposed to the royal demand, and Alaric's sons wrote letters to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) asking the [maesters](wiki:Maesters) about precedents against forced donation of property.(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Alaric did not attend the [tourney](wiki:Tourney) in King's Landing in which was [celebrated the tenth anniversary of Jaehaerys's coronation](wiki:Tourney_at_King's_Landing_on_the_anniversary_of_the_king's_coronation), sending his two sons and daughter, Alarra, instead.(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9) Alaric ordered his [bannermen](wiki:Bannermen) to put aside half of their harvests in anticipation of the winter of [59](wiki:59_AC)–[60 AC](wiki:60_AC), but not all of the [northmen](wiki:North) obeyed, resulting in famine.(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Lord Alaric died in [72 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and was succeeded by his grandson, [Edric Stark](wiki:Edric_Stark), as Alaric's two sons had preceded him in death.(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

## Appearances & Description

Alaric was notoriously prickly and had a flinty reputation. He was said to be hard, stern and unforgiving, tight-fisted almost to the point of being niggardly, humorless, joyless, and cold. Although he was respected by the [northmen](wiki:Northmen), most did not love him, except for the garrison at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), after spending some time at Winterfell, Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) came to know Alaric as having taciturn humor with an edge to it, and as being not an ill host, despite being careful with his [coin](wiki:Coin).(wiki:Alaric_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)
