## Appearances & Description

Joff was a strapping lad. Like his brothers [Jacaerys](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) and [Lucerys](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon), he had brown eyes and hair, and a pug nose. This caused many at court to suspect that Ser [Harwin Strong](wiki:Harwin_Strong) was their true father.(wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_viserys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

## Origins

He was [named after](wiki:Named_after) his father's favorite [knight](wiki:Knight) and companion, Ser [Joffrey Lonmouth](wiki:Joffrey_Lonmouth).(wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
When Joffrey was three years old, his ten-year-old [dragonless](wiki:Dragon) half-uncle, Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), slapped him in an attempt to claim [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) for himself. Joffrey called for his two older brothers, and in the ensuing fight, Aemond lost an eye.(wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
Joffrey was eleven years old at the start of the civil war which became known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). He asked to ride Tyraxes and help call the lords of the realm to his mother's claim, but was denied on account of his age. However, his elder brothers went forth, and Jacaerys arranged his betrothal to the youngest daughter of Lord [Desmond Manderly](wiki:Desmond_Manderly) in exchange for [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor)'s support for the [blacks](wiki:Blacks). After learning that Lucerys had died in the Dance over [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay), Joffrey swore a terrible oath of vengeance against Prince Aemond and Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon), and only his grandparents, Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) and Princess [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)), kept him from mounting his own dragon at once.(wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
Jacaerys ordered Joff to [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown) to defend the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) against King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen)'s dragons, with [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)) chosen to accompany him. [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) suggests that it was Jacaerys's desire to keep his brother far away from the fighting.(wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) He was named the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) after the death of Jacaerys at the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet).(wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) After the [Fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing), Rhaenyra felt secure enough to send for her sons, and a fleet set out from Gulltown with Joffrey. His mother planned a lavish celebration for Joffrey's formal installation as Prince of Dragonstone and heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
Joffrey was eager to prove himself as a warrior and defeat the armies of the [greens](wiki:Greens), but swore solemnly to help his mother hold the Red Keep in the event of an attack when told to remain in the city with Tyraxes. During the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit), he donned squire's armor and begged the queen to let him ride to the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) and mount Tyraxes, but she forbade him from leaving the castle out of her grief for his brothers' deaths. When the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd) and his savage flock began to march on the Dragonpit, he pleaded with his mother to let him ride forth with knights, anxious for the life of his own dragon, though Rhaenyra refused to believe that such rabble would be able to kill the dragons. [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) claims to have seen Joffrey sulking off, but to have held his tongue. In haste to be away before he could be stopped, Joffrey vaulted onto [Syrax](wiki:Syrax) without benefit of a saddle or whip, never having ridden her before. When the watchers on the roof heard Syrax's roar, they realized that Joffrey was missing, and the queen sent the knights later known as the [Seven Who Rode](wiki:Seven_Who_Rode) after the prince, demanding that they bring him back, as he "did not know". Once in the air, Syrax twisted beneath him, fighting to be free of her unfamiliar rider, and stones and spears flew at him from the Shepherd's lambs below. He plunged down into [Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom) from two hundred feet above, crashing into a steep-pitched roof before rolling off to fall another forty feet amidst a shower of broken tiles. The fall broke his back, shards of slate rained down him like knives, and his own sword tore loose from his hand and pierced him through the belly. According to legend, a candlemaker's daughter named [Robin](wiki:Robin_(Flea_Bottom)) comforted him as he died, and his last words were "Mother, forgive me," with debate over whether he was speaking of Rhaenyra or the [Mother Above](wiki:Mother_(the_Seven)).(wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
The mob descended on Joffrey's corpse, and looters tore the boots from his feet and the sword from his belly. More savage rioters tore at his body, cutting off his hands so that the rings on his fingers may be claimed; his right foot was hacked through the ankle, and a butcher's apprentice was sawing at his neck to claim his head when the seven knights arrived. His remains were reclaimed, aside from his foot, although Ser [Gyles Yronwood](wiki:Gyles_Yronwood), Ser [Willam Royce](wiki:Willam_Royce), and Lord Commander [Glendon Goode](wiki:Glendon_Goode) were killed trying to retrieve the body. His half-brother, [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), became his mother's heir following his death.(wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
