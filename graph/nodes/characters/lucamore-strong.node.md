## Origins

In [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), a great tourney was held to celebrate the completion of the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit). Ser Lucamore was the victor of the great melee in the pit. After the death of Ser [Willam the Wasp](wiki:Willam_the_Wasp) the following year, Jaehaerys awarded a white cloak to Ser Lucamore. Ser Lucamore was on duty when [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in [56 AC](wiki:56_AC) with the afflicted Princess [Aerea Targaryen](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) barely clinging to him. Lucamore shoved through the onlookers and carried the girl to [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Benifer](wiki:Benifer). He later told anyone who'd listen that the princess was stick thin, so hot with fever he could feel the heat through his armor, there was blood in her eyes and something was moving beneath her skin. He was summoned by King Jaehaerys the next day and commanded not to speak of Aerea anymore.(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

After Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), who had ruled as *de facto* Lady of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), died in [73 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King Jaehaerys named Lucamore's brother, Ser [Bywin Strong](wiki:Bywin_Strong), the new [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

It was discovered by Ser [Ryam Redwyne](wiki:Ryam_Redwyne) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) near the end of [73 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) that Ser Lucamore had broken his Kingsguard vows: he had wed in secret, not once, but thrice, with each woman being ignorant of the other two. On these three wives, he had fathered a total of sixteen children. Ser Ryam brought the revelation to the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), Ser [Gyles Morrigen](wiki:Gyles_Morrigen), who in turn had Ser Lucamore seized and brought before the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), along with his three wives and all his children. This scandal shocked both the court and city, although it was eventually a cause of great laughter and merriment among the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk).(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

Ser Gyles requested that Ser Lucamore be put to death. In front of the king, Lucamore fell to his knees, confessed his guilt, and begged for mercy. According to [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Barth](wiki:Barth), the king might have granted it, had Lucamore not been foolish enough to add *"for the sake of my wives and children"* to the end of his plea, as this was tantamount to throwing his crimes in the king's face.(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Jaehaerys declared he would have no oathbreakers serving him, and Queen [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) added that Lucamore had not only broken the vows of the Kingsguard, but also his [marriage](wiki:Marriage) vows. None of the three marriages could be declared lawful, and thus all the children were [bastards](wiki:Bastard). Alysanne also declared that his wives were not entirely innocent: while each woman had been unaware of the other two, they had known that Lucamore was a knight of the Kingsguard and thus not allowed to wed. She declared some mercy would be granted for them, but none for him.(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

Ser Lucamore was [gelded](wiki:Eunuch) by his former sworn brothers of the Kingsguard, after which King Jaehaerys sent Lucamore to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). Two of his elder sons chose to accompany him. A daughter chose to become a [septa](wiki:Septa). His three wives were each sent into exile away from King's Landing, the first given to the charge of Lord Bywin Strong of Harrenhal, Lucamore's brother in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), the second to Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)) of [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) in the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands), and the third wife was sent to Ser [Garon](wiki:Garon_Baratheon) and Lord [Boremund Baratheon](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) in the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands), along with their children, who were forbidden to continue using the surname [Strong](wiki:House_Strong) from that day forth. The children of his first wife were known as Rivers, the children of his second as Waters, and the children of his third as Storm. Lucamore forever more became known as *Lucamore the Lusty*. His deceits are recorded in the [White Book](wiki:White_Book) and are sung about in a humorous song.(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

## Appearances & Description

Lucamore was described as an amiable, strapping, broad-shouldered, young blond bull. He was a great favorite of the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) in [tourneys](wiki:Tourney) and was well loved at court.(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Lucamore_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
