## Identity

Arnold Arryn — Jeyne's first cousin, "the Mad Heir," had twice tried to depose her and was driven mad by long imprisonment.

## Origins

Ser Arnold was a first cousin of [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn), the [Lady of the Eyrie](wiki:Lady_of_the_Eyrie). In his youth, he served as a squire at [Runestone](wiki:Runestone).(wiki:Arnold_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

Arnold twice rose against his cousin to contest her inheritance,(wiki:Arnold_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) claiming that women were too soft to rule.(wiki:Arnold_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) After his second rising sometime before the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), he was imprisoned in one of the [sky cells](wiki:Sky_cell) of the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:Arnold_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Jeyne died in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC). In her last testament, she named her fourth cousin and her [Knight of the Gate](wiki:Knight_of_the_Gate), Ser [Joffrey Arryn](wiki:Joffrey_Arryn), as heir, though Arnold was much closer by blood. Though Arnold had gone mad during his time imprisoned in the sky cells, his sane, shrewd, and ambitious son, Ser [Eldric](wiki:Eldric_Arryn), came forward to press his father's claim. Many [lords](wiki:Lord) supported Arnold's claim, insisting that the [laws](wiki:Law_and_justice) of [inheritance](wiki:Inheritance) could not be put aside by "the whim of a dying woman." Additionally, a third claimant came forward in the person of [Isembard Arryn](wiki:Isembard_Arryn), head of [House Arryn of Gulltown](wiki:House_Arryn_of_Gulltown).(wiki:Arnold_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

Ser [Corwyn Corbray](wiki:Corwyn_Corbray), one of [the regents](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) of young King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), ruled that Jeyne's will must prevail and declared Joffrey the rightful Lord of the Eyrie. Isembard and his sons were imprisoned and Eldric was executed, but Arnold eluded Corwyn and fled to Runestone. When Corwyn arrived at the castle, a crossbowman of Runestone killed Corwyn after he drew [Lady Forlorn](wiki:Lady_Forlorn) against Lord [Gunthor Royce](wiki:Gunthor_Royce), Arnold's protector.(wiki:Arnold_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

Striking down the king's regent was an act of treason, and [war](wiki:Arryn_Succession_Conflict_(134_AC)) began anew across the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn). Houses [Royce](wiki:House_Royce), [Templeton](wiki:House_Templeton), [Tollett](wiki:House_Tollett), [Coldwater](wiki:House_Coldwater), [Dutton](wiki:House_Dutton), and the lords of the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters) supported Arnold's claim, though the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan), commanded the lords supporting Arnold to lay down their arms. When no reply was forthcoming, the crown sent men through the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) and launched a sea attack to support Joffrey's claim.(wiki:Arnold_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

By the time the new regents were chosen by lot in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) and Lord [Benjicot Blackwood](wiki:Benjicot_Blackwood) had at last forced the supporters of Isembard and Arnold to do homage to Lord Joffrey as their liege.(wiki:Arnold_Arryn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)
