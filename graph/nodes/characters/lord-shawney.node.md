## Origins

Lord Shawney supported [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) during the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [196 AC](wiki:196_AC). During the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field), he was on the right of the [Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) host, under the command of Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers). Lord Shawney almost died from his wounds in the battle, and he was pardoned by King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) after the war.(wiki:Lord_Shawney.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

In [212 AC](wiki:212_AC), Lord Shawney came with his retinue to [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls) to attend the [wedding](wiki:Wedding) of Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell) and his second wife, [the daughter](wiki:Lady_Butterwell) of [Lord Frey](wiki:Lord_Frey). On his way, Lord Shawney had to cross the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye) via a ferry. He quarreled with [Lord Costayne](wiki:Lord_Costayne_(Aerys_I)) on the matter of which lord should cross first; Shawney argued that as the older of the two he should go first, whereas Costayne believed that as his House was nobler then Shawney's, he had the precedence.(wiki:Lord_Shawney.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

During the wedding feast, Lord Shawney was seated on the dais. The next day, he attend the [wedding tourney](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) and bet on Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) to win (five to one) against Ser [Uthor Underleaf](wiki:Uthor_Underleaf) in the joust.(wiki:Lord_Shawney.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

The fate of Lord Costayne following the arrival at Whitewalls of the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), and the suppression of the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion) is unknown.
