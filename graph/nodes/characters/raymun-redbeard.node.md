## Origins

Raymun united the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) clans [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall) as [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall). He had seen the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) decline and growing laxness and in [226 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) took advantage of the situation. Sending his reavers over after establishing a toehold for themselves on the [Wall](wiki:Wall), they used thrown ramparts of their own and dropped ropes and ladders for his people to cross over and march south. Because the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) was caught off guard as Redbeard's horde came down upon the north, its lord commander, [Jack Musgood](wiki:Jack_Musgood), was henceforth called Sleepy Jack.(wiki:Raymun_Redbeard.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Eventually Raymun's host met a bloody end on the shores of [Long Lake](wiki:Battle_at_Long_Lake), caught between Lord [Willam Stark](wiki:Willam_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and the Drunken Giant, Lord [Harmond Umber](wiki:Harmond_Umber). During the fight, Willam was slain, and his younger brother, [Artos Stark](wiki:Artos_Stark), known as the Implacable, slew Raymun Redbeard. Raymun's sons were slain as well, however his brother fled from battle and was known after as the [Red Raven](wiki:Red_Raven_(free_folk)). The Watch arrived too late to fight the wildlings but in time to bury them, the task that Artos assigned them in his wroth as he grieved above the headless corpse of his fallen brother Willam.(wiki:Raymun_Redbeard.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
