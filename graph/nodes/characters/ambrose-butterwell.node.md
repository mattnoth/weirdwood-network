## Origins

Lord Ambrose was [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) under King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen).(wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) He was raised to [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) under Aegon's successor, King [Daeron II](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), for a short time, but his ability to lead was questioned when his attempt to deal with the burgeoning [Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) failed miserably.(wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) He was replaced by Lord [Hayford](wiki:Lord_Hayford).(wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) During the First Blackfyre Rebellion Butterwell attempted to keep one foot in each camp, he sent his eldest son to fight for King Daeron II and his second son to fight for [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), while he remained home with his full strength.(wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) Both sons perished at the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field), and his youngest son died of the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness).(wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)

With his sons dead and his daughters married off, Lord Butterwell despaired of siring a new son, or risked his name dying with him. In [212 AC](wiki:212_AC), the recently-widowed Lord Butterwell hosted a [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) to celebrate his [marriage](wiki:Marriage) to [a daughter](wiki:Lady_Butterwell) of [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey), but the tourney was also secretly an attempt to foment the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in the name of [Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre). The winner would receive the [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) that had been given to Ambrose's [grandfather](wiki:Butterwell_(Hand_of_the_King)) by King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen). However, the egg was stolen from [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls) before it could be awarded.(wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)

Egg, the squire of Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) and secretly Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) in disguise, attempted to send a message to his father, Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), by presenting his father's ring to Maester [Lothar](wiki:Lothar) and asking to send a [raven](wiki:Raven). After being informed, Lord Ambrose brought the boy to the castle sept with a few loyal guardsmen; most of the Butterwell garrison were supportive of Ser [Tommard Heddle](wiki:Tommard_Heddle), their commander and Ambrose's treasonous son-in-law. Aegon bluffed Ambrose by claiming that he was a spy and that an army was coming, so [Lord Frey](wiki:Lord_Frey) departed Whitewalls with his retinue and Ambrose recanted his allegiance to [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre). Duncan slew Tom Heddle in the sept when he attempted to take Aegon into custody. Ambrose then departed Whitewalls with Aegon, who was instructed by Duncan to escape when he could.(wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9)

However, before Lords Butterwell and Frey could get far, Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) appeared with an army to put an end to the nascent rebellion. Lord Ambrose lost Whitewalls (which was razed and the earth salted), and was only allowed to keep a tenth of [House Butterwell](wiki:House_Butterwell)'s fortunes along with his head and his new bride.(wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10)

## Appearances & Description

When he married his [second wife](wiki:Lady_Butterwell) in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC), the fifty-year-old Ambrose had heavy jowls and thin flaxen hair.(wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

Either Lord Ambrose Butterwell or his grandfather was renowned for his wit, but considered to be a failure as a Hand.(wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-8) Ambrose was very rich and said to be a pious man. King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) disliked him, considering him to be a craven, as did Ser [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers). Lord Butterwell disliked taking risks and was an indecisive man, letting himself be swayed by those around him, namely his son-in-law Ser [Tommard Heddle](wiki:Tommard_Heddle).(wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)
