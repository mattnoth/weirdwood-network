## Origins

Craster is the [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son of a man of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) and a wildling woman from the village of [Whitetree](wiki:Whitetree). His father abandoned his mother after Craster was conceived, and when his mother tried to bring him to [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), she was chased off by members of the Watch. His father's name is never mentioned.(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

[Craster's Keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep) is located in the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest), where he lives with [his nineteen wives](wiki:Craster's_wives),(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) and he has other daughters too young to wed.(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Rasos0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Any sons he has are sacrificed to [cold gods](wiki:Others).(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

[Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), once gifted Craster with a crossbow.(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) Craster [cut out the tongue](wiki:Tongue_mutilation) of an envoy sent by [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder), [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall).(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8) [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) drank sweet mead when she wed Craster in the summer.(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Rasos46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Craster](wiki:Category:Images_of_Craster)*

Once a powerful-looking man, Craster is nearing the end of his life. His hair is grey turning to white. He has a flat nose and a drooping mouth, which gives him a cruel look. One of Craster's ears is missing, which he has lost to the cold.(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) His teeth are brown and rotten.(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Craster wears a sheepskin jerkin and a cloak of sewn skins, as well as a twisted heavy gold ring about his wrist.(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) He drinks thin yellow [beer](wiki:Beer) from a chipped stone cup, but prefers [wine](wiki:Wine) from the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Craster is one of the few wildlings not openly hostile to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). Although he shows the Watch no malice and offers sanctuary to those north of the [Wall](wiki:Wall) on [rangings](wiki:Rangers), he is regarded as a tenuous ally at best. This is due to his [incestuous](wiki:Incest) relationships with [his daughter-wives](wiki:Craster's_wives) and suspicions of [kinslaying](wiki:Kinslaying), sacrificing his [male children](wiki:Craster's_sons) to the "cold gods".(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Craster is not an ally of [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder),(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) and he dislikes [Rattleshirt](wiki:Rattleshirt).(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Craster begrudgingly allows the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) to stay at his keep overnight during their [great ranging](wiki:Great_ranging) in the lands [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall). He informs [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), that [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) is gathering [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) to the source of the [Milkwater](wiki:Milkwater) in the [Frostfangs](wiki:Frostfangs).(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Although Craster admits to having been visited by Ser [Waymar Royce](wiki:Waymar_Royce)'s ranging party, he claims not to have seen [Benjen Stark](wiki:Benjen_Stark) for three years, yet he is also aware of the fate that befell [Gared](wiki:Gared). [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) reveals to [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) that Craster has been giving his sheep to [the gods](wiki:Others).(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10)

Craster is offered escort south to the [Wall](wiki:Wall), but he refuses. Jeor gives Craster [wine](wiki:Wine), a crossbow, and his own black [steel](wiki:Steel) battle-axe with [gold](wiki:Gold) scrollwork.(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11)(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

### A Storm of Swords

Following the [fight at the Fist](wiki:Fight_at_the_Fist), Craster allows the survivors of the attack to stay overnight at his keep. This time, several members of the Watch, already mutinous, believe that Craster is holding back food and aid to the men. During the farewell feast hosted by Craster, [a mutiny erupts](wiki:Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep). [Dirk](wiki:Dirk) slits Craster's throat, and the dying wildling falls atop the wounded Ser [Byam Flint](wiki:Byam_Flint). Lord Commander Mormont is then mortally wounded by [Ollo Lophand](wiki:Ollo_Lophand). [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) flees with [Gilly](wiki:Gilly), who has recently given birth.(wiki:Craster.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
