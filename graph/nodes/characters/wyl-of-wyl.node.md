## Origins

In the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), Lord Wyl ambushed the forces of Lord [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon) as they tried to assault the [Boneway](wiki:Boneway). The cunning [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) launched a night raid, raining rocks, arrows and spears from above. In the end, the Boneway was blocked both before and behind, and Orys was captured by the Wyl of Wyl.(wiki:Wyl_of_Wyl.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Orys remained the Wyl's captive for years, until the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen) ransomed him and his men back in [7 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) for each man's weight in gold. When the captives were freed, however, the Wyl had their sword hands chopped off so they could not be used again against Dorne. It is said that, after the release of Orys and the other lords, the war entered a new stage, as King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) was by now intent on revenge.(wiki:Wyl_of_Wyl.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

According to Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), the Wyl of Wyl committed infamous deeds which are still remembered in [Fawnton](wiki:Fawnton) and [Old Oak](wiki:Old_Oak).(wiki:Wyl_of_Wyl.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), the Wyl of Wyl turned up uninvited to the wedding of Ser [Jon Cafferen](wiki:Jon_Cafferen), the heir to Fawnton, and [Alys Oakheart](wiki:Alys_Oakheart), the daughter of Lord Oakheart, in [12 AC](wiki:12_AC). He murdered Lord Oakheart and most of the guests, forcing Alys to watch as Jon was castrated. Alys and her handmaids were then raped by the Wyl and his men and sold off to a [Myrish](wiki:Myr) slaver. Gyldayn calls this "the most infamous act" of the First Dornish War.(wiki:Wyl_of_Wyl.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Lord [Walter Wyl](wiki:Walter_Wyl), the son of the Widow-lover, led a host for the [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Aenys_I)) in [37 AC](wiki:37_AC) during the [Second Dornish War](wiki:Second_Dornish_War). After defeating the Dornishmen at [Stonehelm](wiki:Stonehelm), Orys Baratheon chopped off Walter's hands and feet as vengeance for his father's behavior years earlier.(wiki:Wyl_of_Wyl.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
