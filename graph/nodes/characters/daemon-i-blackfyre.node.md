## Identity

Daemon I Blackfyre (170–196 AC) — child of Aegon IV Targaryen and Daena Targaryen; married to Rohanne of Tyrosh; sworn to House Blackfyre; Ser. One of 4 characters named Daemon Blackfyre.

## Origins

### Early life

In late [170 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-7) Princess [Daena Targaryen](wiki:Daena_Targaryen) gave birth to Daemon(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rawoiafdaemon_blackfyre.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) His mother [named him for](wiki:Named_after) her grandfather, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), "for the Prince Daemon who had been the wonder and the terror of his age". In later years, some considered this name a warning of what he became. Although Daena refused to name her son's father,(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) some people suspected the involvement of her cousin, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), even at the time of Daemon's birth.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Daemon was raised in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) and was taught arms by Ser [Quentyn Ball](wiki:Quentyn_Ball), the master-at-arms at the Red Keep.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) In [182 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) or [183 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), when he was twelve years old, Daemon participated in a squire's tourney, which he won. King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) personally [knighted](wiki:Knight) Daemon and publicly bestowed the [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre) on him,(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) finally acknowledging him as his son.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-GreatBastards_6-4) There were some who felt that the sword symbolized the monarchy(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-8) as it had been the sword of King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), the Conqueror, and had been passed on from king to king ever since. Aegon's public gift of the sword would eventually start the first talk that Daemon should be king.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-targaryenhistory_9-0) Following the acquisition of the sword, Daemon took the name "Blackfyre" for himself.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

King Aegon IV negotiated a betrothal for Daemon to [Rohanne of Tyrosh](wiki:Rohanne_of_Tyrosh). Daemon's supporters would later claim that Daemon had desired to marry his younger half-sister, [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)), instead and that King Aegon, who desired the marriage alliance to [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), had promised Daemon that he could have [more than one wife](wiki:Polygamy).(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) In [184 AC](wiki:184_AC), while Aegon IV was on his deathbed, he legitimized all of his bastards; those born to noblewomen, including Daemon, became known as the [Great Bastards](wiki:Great_Bastards).(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) At this point Daemon may have been given the title Prince since he is referred to as such by Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey), however Ser Eustace was one of his followers so this may just be his reverence for Daemon showing instead of Daemon actually being granted the title of Prince.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) Daemon's trueborn half-brother, [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), ascended the throne following Aegon's death. He refused to allow Daemon more than one wife, paid the [dowry](wiki:Dowry) his late father had promised to the [Archon](wiki:Archon) of [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), and saw Daemon wed to Rohanne in early [184 AC](wiki:184_AC), granting him a tract of land near the [Blackwater](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) on his wedding day, with the right to raise a castle.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Daemon and Rohanne had a fertile marriage. Their twin sons, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Blackfyre) and [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Blackfyre), were born later that same year.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-11) They were followed by five more sons, and at least two daughters.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

In [187 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Daemon participated in Princess Daenerys's [wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Maron_Martell_and_Daenerys_Targaryen) tourney. He was defeated in the final tilt by Prince [Baelor Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), Daeron II's eldest son.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

Meanwhile, the growing influence of the [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) at court, the Dornish appearance of Daeron II's heir, the sudden change of Dornish enemies to Dornish allies, Daeron's rumored illegitimacy (started by King Aegon IV during his reign), and Aegon IV's previous public gift of [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre), the "sword of kings",(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) to Daemon caused several nobles and knights to start mistrusting Daeron II and Baelor, while seeing promise in Daemon. Men began to approach Daemon, but it took them many years to convince Daemon to declare his claim. [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) considers it likely that it had been [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers), Daemon's younger half-brother, who had managed to convince Daemon in the end.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) According to King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), it had been both Aegor Rivers and Ser [Quentyn Ball](wiki:Quentyn_Ball), the master-at-arms at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), who had played the largest role in convincing Daemon.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Aegor pressed him to proclaim his claim for the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), and after Daemon agreed to a betrothal between his eldest daughter, [Calla](wiki:Calla_Blackfyre), and Aegor, his efforts increased. Many lords and knights joined in.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4) Besides the influences of his councillors, Daemon had come to resent having the status of [bastard](wiki:Bastardy) and what it represented.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-santafe_14-0)

Following the Blackfyre defeat in the first rebellion (which included the death of Daemon himself and his eldest two children, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Blackfyre) and [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Blackfyre)), Daemon's bastard half-brother, Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers), fled to [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) with Daemon's remaining children and widow. From there, Daemon's descendants continued to plot. Four more rebellions would follow, until [260 AC](wiki:260_AC), when [Maelys I Blackfyre](wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre), the last male of [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre), was slain on the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) during the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings).(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

### First Blackfyre Rebellion

There was no final insult that resulted in the declaration of Daemon's claim.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6) There are some who say that it was the wedding of Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)) to the [Dornish](wiki:Dornish) [Prince](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne) [Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell) that led to the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion). It was said that Daemon rebelled because she had been denied to him by Daeron.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) However, while Daenerys' wedding took place in [187 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Daemon did not rebel until eight years later.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-7) In [196 AC](wiki:196_AC), Daemon began his rebellion, which would last nigh on a year. For his sigil, Daemon took the Targaryen sigil with the colors reversed, a black dragon on red, giving him the nickname "the Black Dragon" (with Daeron being referred as the Red Dragon).(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-8)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

Daemon's decision to claim the throne had been made rashly, and word soon reached Daeron's court that Daemon meant to declare within a month. It has been suggested by [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Merion](wiki:Merion) that [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), another one of Aegon IV's bastard sons, was involved in the news reaching Daeron.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-9) The [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) was sent to arrest Daemon, but, with the aid of the master-at-arms of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), Daemon managed to escape imprisonment.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-10)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3) Daemon's allies used this attempted arrest as a cause for war, claiming that Daeron had acted without reason.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-11)

Besides Aegor Rivers and Quentyn Ball, Daemon's supporters, according to Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey) of [Standfast](wiki:Standfast), included [Robb Reyne](wiki:Robb_Reyne), [Gareth the Grey](wiki:Gareth_the_Grey), Ser [Aubrey Ambrose](wiki:Aubrey_Ambrose), Lord [Gormon Peake](wiki:Gormon_Peake), Black [Byren Flowers](wiki:Byren_Flowers), and [Redtusk](wiki:Redtusk). Daemon's rebellion divided the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). Half the realm declared for him, though not all were eager to pledge their full support. Notably, Houses [Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower), [Butterwell](wiki:House_Butterwell), [Oakheart](wiki:House_Oakheart) and [Tarbeck](wiki:House_Tarbeck) gave support to both Daemon and Daeron, while others (e.g., [Manfred Lothston](wiki:Manfred_Lothston)) were willing to betray Daemon for Daeron. The thief [Quickfinger](wiki:Quickfinger) attempted to steal [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_eggs) for Daemon, but was caught.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9)

Daemon's rebellion ended [on the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field) in late [196 AC](wiki:196_AC).(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-12) According to Ser Eustace Osgrey, who fought on Daemon's side, Daemon fought "like the [Warrior](wiki:Warrior_(the_Seven)) himself".(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10) Daemon shattered the lines of Lord [Donnel Arryn](wiki:Donnel_Arryn)'s vanguard, leaving the loyalists in peril until Ser [Gwayne Corbray](wiki:Gwayne_Corbray) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) arrived with reinforcements.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Daemon and Ser Gwayne duelled, with both men using their Valyrian steel swords. The duel is said to have lasted nearly an hour before Daemon gained the upper hand. Corbray was wounded and Daemon dismounted to prevent Corbray from being trampled. He commanded that Corbray was to be taken off the field.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-11)

In the meantime, [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) with his archers, the [Raven's Teeth](wiki:Raven's_Teeth), had captured and assumed a position atop the [Weeping Ridge](wiki:Weeping_Ridge), which overlooked the battlefield and allowed them to rain arrows down among Daemon and his commanders. Daemon's heir [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Blackfyre) died first, followed by Daemon himself. When Daemon’s second son, Aegon's twin brother [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Blackfyre) took up Blackfyre, he was slain as well.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-12)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-13)

### Legacy

With Daemon dead, the rebels began to rout. [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) claimed Blackfyre and rallied the rebels by leading a charge against the Raven's Teeth, but was eventually forced to flee.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-13)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-14) He took Daemon's widow and remaining children with him to the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities), eventually ending up in [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh).(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-14) In exile, Daemon's descendants continued their struggle for the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), resulting in four more rebellions ([Second](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion), [Third](wiki:Third_Blackfyre_Rebellion) and [Fourth Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Fourth_Blackfyre_Rebellion), and the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings)), as well as one peacefull attempt during the [Great Council of 233 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_233_AC). These attempts led to the arrest of Daemon's third son ([Daemon](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre)), and the deaths of his fourth and fifth sons ([Haegon](wiki:Haegon_I_Blackfyre) and [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_Blackfyre)), as well as the death of a grandson, also named [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_III_Blackfyre). Daemon's last male descendant, [Maelys](wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre), was slain during the War of the Ninepenny Kings by Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), ending the Blackfyre threat(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-5)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) after sixty-four years.

Sixteen years after Daemon's death, some still made pilgrimages to the Redgrass Field to plant flowers on the spot where he died.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-6)

## Appearances & Description

Daemon was tall and powerful, with broad shoulders, muscular arms, and a flat stomach. Targaryen on both sides, Daemon had all the hallmarks of his house. He had strong Targaryen features, with deep [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes) and silver-gold hair which he wore long, flowing down to his shoulders in a silvery-gold mane. He always went clean-shaven, with neither beard nor mustache. Almost inhumanly beautiful, Daemon looked every inch the warrior, and many thought that he strongly resembled portraits of [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-GreatBastards_6-1)

His armor was ornate and costly, all black and red plate, and his warhelm was distinctive, with batlike steel "dragon wings" on the sides.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-GreatBastards_6-2)

Daemon matured into a man of considerable charm and strength, mastering all weapons of the battlefield, but with the sword [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre) in hand he was said to fight like [the Warrior](wiki:Warrior_(the_Seven)) himself. He won friends easily and women were drawn to him.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-GreatBastards_6-3) According to [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey), he was no more pious than a king need be.(wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)
