## Origins

In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), at the start of King Aenys I's reign, [Jonos Arryn](wiki:Jonos_Arryn) rose up in rebellion against the Targaryens and his own older brother, Lord [Ronnel Arryn](wiki:Ronnel_Arryn_(king)). He [imprisoned](wiki:Sky_cell) Ronnel in the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) and declared himself [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale). King Aenys's response was confused and vacillating: first ordering his [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Alyn Stokeworth](wiki:Alyn_Stokeworth), to sail to the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) to deal with the usurper, but then recalling the order at the last moment, for fear that [Harren the Red](wiki:Harren_the_Red), a rebel from the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), might infiltrate [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Allard_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Allard_Royce.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Taking matter into own hands, Lord Allard Royce of [Runestone](wiki:Runestone) gathered "twoscore loyal [lords](wiki:Lord)" and led them to sweep away Jonos's supporters, penning him at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). However, this led Jonos to send his brother flying out of the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door), earning him the nickname Jonos the [Kinslayer](wiki:Kinslaying).(wiki:Allard_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Allard_Royce.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Jonos and his garrison remained defiant until Prince [Maegor Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) flew to the Eyrie atop the [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Balerion](wiki:Balerion). Rather than face the [fire](wiki:Dragonflame), Jonos was seized by his garrison and delivered to Lord Royce through the Moon Door. Surrender saved Jonos's followers from burning, but not from death. All of them were hanged per the orders of Maegor, who denied the [highborn](wiki:Nobility) rebels the honor of beheading.(wiki:Allard_Royce.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)(wiki:Allard_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) continued through a kinsman after Ronnel and Jonos's deaths, a cousin, [Hubert Arryn](wiki:Hubert_Arryn).(wiki:Allard_Royce.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)(wiki:Allard_Royce.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Given that Hubert Arryn's wife was a Royce of Runestone, who had already produced six half-Royce sons to one day rule the Eyrie after him, the succession was seen as secure (and was not contested by Lord Allard). Allard was rewarded by King Aenys with [gold](wiki:Gold), titles, and honors.(wiki:Allard_Royce.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
