## Origins

Randyll married Lady [Melessa Florent](wiki:Melessa_Florent), the eldest daughter of [Alester Florent](wiki:Alester_Florent), [Lord of Brightwater Keep](wiki:Lord_of_Brightwater_Keep).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Their first-born child, [Samwell](wiki:Samwell_Tarly), was born in [283 AC](wiki:283_AC).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-15) They would later have three daughters, born within three years time, and a second son, [Dickon](wiki:Dickon_Tarly).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

As one of Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell)'s bannermen, Randyll fought in [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) on the side of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). He defeated Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s forces at the [Battle of Ashford](wiki:Battle_of_Ashford),(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) cutting down [Lord Cafferen](wiki:Lord_Cafferen) with [Heartsbane](wiki:Heartsbane) during the battle.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

Randyll insisted that young Sam attend him while holding court in the audience chamber at [Horn Hill](wiki:Horn_Hill), and he brought Sam to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) during a visit to see Mace.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) A martinet, Randyll became disappointed in his gentle, timid firstborn son and made several harsh attempts to engage him in manly, martial pursuits, but all to no effect.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) A dozen masters-at-arms failed to toughen Sam. Attempts such as dressing him in his mother's clothes, forcing him to sleep in chainmail, and even being bathed in [aurochs](wiki:Aurochs) blood by two [warlocks](wiki:Warlocks) of [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) failed to raise his valor. Samwell even wept to see a chicken slaughtered.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) When Lord Tarly threw Sam into Horn Hill's fishpond to teach him to swim, Ser [Hyle Hunt](wiki:Hyle_Hunt) had to rescue the boy.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Randyll forbade Sam from sleeping in the same bed as his sisters,(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) and he reprimanded Sam and his mother when he heard them singing "[The Song of the Seven](wiki:The_Song_of_the_Seven)" to the infant Dickon.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) Randyll began to ignore Samwell, leaving him to his own devices and paying more attention to Dickon after his second son was born.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)

When Sam was no more than ten, Lord Tarly brought his heir with him to the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor), where Samwell was mocked endlessly by [Horas](wiki:Horas_Redwyne) and [Hobber Redwyne](wiki:Hobber_Redwyne) as well as by the fool of [House Redwyne](wiki:House_Redwyne). Randyll had planned to leave his son at the Arbor as Lord [Paxter Redwyne](wiki:Paxter_Redwyne)'s page and squire, but he instead brought Samwell back to Horn Hill with him.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) Randyll forbade Sam from traveling to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) to study at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), horrified at the notion of a member of [House Tarly](wiki:House_Tarly) wearing a [maester](wiki:Maester)'s chain. To discourage the boy, Randyll had Sam chained and manacled to a wall for three days.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)

When Samwell turned fifteen, three men-at-arms escorted him to his father in a nearby forest. While skinning a deer, Randyll offered his eldest son the choice of either joining the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), thereby nullifying his claim to Horn Hill and allowing Dickon to become Randyll's lawful heir, or dying in a supposed accident while hunting the following day. Sam agreed to leave for the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Randyll Tarly](wiki:Category:Images_of_Randyll_Tarly)*

Randyll is a lean and balding man with a short, bristly grey beard.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

Hard, narrow-minded, and shrewd,(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rawoiafrandyll_tarly.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Randyll is blunt of speech.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) He is misogynistic and a harsh disciplinarian,(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and he believes in putting idle men to work.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Lord Tarly is not well liked in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Randyll follows the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

A careful tactician(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) always in the van of his liege, Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell),(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Randyll is considered an able soldier by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and the finest soldier in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) by Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Lord Tarly prizes courage and martial ability, which causes him to despise his elder son, [Samwell](wiki:Samwell_Tarly), because of his cowardice and softness, and strongly favor his younger son, [Dickon](wiki:Dickon_Tarly), who is much more fierce and robust.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

In the field Randyll wears mail and boiled leather with a breastplate of grey [steel](wiki:Steel).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) He carries the ancestral [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword [Heartsbane](wiki:Heartsbane) across his back in a [jeweled](wiki:Jewelry) scabbard.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Ser [Hyle Hunt](wiki:Hyle_Hunt) is one of his [household knights](wiki:Household_knight)(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) and captains.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Randyll has men escort his eldest son, [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly), to [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black). Shortly after his arrival at the [Wall](wiki:Wall), Sam tells his new friend, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), how Randyll gave him the choice between joining the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) and death.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-6)

### A Clash of Kings

When [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) calls his banners, Randyll joins with [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) in supporting Renly.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) While encamped by [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), Randyll is told by his son and heir, [Dickon](wiki:Dickon_Tarly), of a cruel game that several knights are playing on [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth). Randyll summons Brienne and tells her that some of the men laid wagers on the first to claim her [maidenhead](wiki:Maidenhead). He then tersely advises her that women have no place on the battlefield.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

Lord Tarly is hosting Renly at [Horn Hill](wiki:Horn_Hill) when they receive [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s letter claiming that the late King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s children are [bastards](wiki:Bastard).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Randyll accompanies Renly's army to [Bitterbridge](wiki:Bitterbridge), and he meets [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) when she arrives during the [melee at Bitterbridge](wiki:Melee_at_Bitterbridge).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

After the parley with Stannis beneath the walls of [Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), Lord Tarly counsels attacking Stannis to make sure he gains no strength while Renly is away.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) Randyll and Lord [Mathis Rowan](wiki:Mathis_Rowan) discuss tactics for the upcoming battle. Before it can take place, however, [Renly is assassinated](wiki:Shadow_assassin).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Randyll continues to follow the Tyrells in forsaking Stannis's cause.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) He captures Renly's stores and puts a great many men to death, mainly those sworn to [House Florent](wiki:House_Florent), his wife's family, in [fighting at Bitterbridge](wiki:Fighting_at_Bitterbridge).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

Randyll is sent out with Lord Rowan to find Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) and bring him to [Tumbler's Falls](wiki:Tumbler's_Falls) where Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) is waiting with a huge host and a fleet of barges.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Once again following the lead of his liege lord, Lord Tarly joins with the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) and fights against Stannis, commanding the center at the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Randyll is honored in the [throne room](wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep)) after the battle.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

### A Storm of Swords

The [small council](wiki:Small_council) of King Joffrey awards lesser tracts of land to Lord Tarly, Lady [Arwyn Oakheart](wiki:Arwyn_Oakheart), and Lord [Leyton Hightower](wiki:Leyton_Hightower) as rewards for the Blackwater.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

Randyll [destroys](wiki:Battle_at_Duskendale) a [northern](wiki:North) force attacking the town of [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), resulting in the capture of [Harrion Karstark](wiki:Harrion_Karstark) and [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover) and the death of [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Afterwards, he takes the town of [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) with Lord [Renfred Rykker](wiki:Renfred_Rykker), locks Lord [William Mooton](wiki:William_Mooton) of Maidenpool in a tower, and secures the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos74.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

At Castle Black, Samwell suggests to Jon that [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) and [her son](wiki:Monster) could be sent to live with his family at [Horn Hill](wiki:Horn_Hill) under the false belief that the baby was fathered by him. Sam thinks Randyll might be secretly proud of his son fathering a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) with the [wildling](wiki:Wildling) girl, but Jon is skeptical of the idea.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

### A Feast for Crows

[Gilly](wiki:Gilly) accompanies Samwell when he departs the [Wall](wiki:Wall) for [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) to study at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel). Though he is nervous about possibly seeing his father, Sam hopes Gilly can become a serving maid at Horn Hill.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3)

Randyll and Lord [Mathis Rowan](wiki:Mathis_Rowan) are suggested by Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) as good candidates to replace his late brother, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) refuses to name a [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) bannerman as Hand, however, failing to understand that such a move would actually weaken the Tyrells by placing one of their most powerful bannermen in a position of authority higher than Lord Mace.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

Randyll begins rebuilding Maidenpool, and he beheads [outlaws](wiki:Outlaws) with [Heartsbane](wiki:Heartsbane).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) His son [Dickon](wiki:Dickon_Tarly) is to marry [Eleanor Mooton](wiki:Eleanor_Mooton), the daughter of the pardoned Lord William.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

Brienne encounters Lord Tarly again while he is dispensing harsh justice in the fishmarket of Maidenpool. He decides that a thief whole stole from an abandoned [sept](wiki:Sept) should have seven fingers removed from his hand, and he orders the lashing of a baker unable to pay a heavy fine of fifty [stags](wiki:Currency) for mixing sawdust in his flour. A [whore](wiki:Whore) accused of giving the [pox](wiki:Pox) to four Tarly soldiers is to be imprisoned and having her private parts washed with lye. Randyll orders an archer from the [Mooton](wiki:House_Mooton) garrison, accused of cheating at dice, to have the little finger of one his hands removed and a nail driven through his other hand. Randyll admonishes Brienne for having let [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) die and for her non-traditional behavior, but he allows her to leave after reading her letter from King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)

Randyll sends Ser [Hyle Hunt](wiki:Hyle_Hunt), one of his captains, to follow an unknowing Brienne in her search for [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), and to bring Sansa back to Maidenpool if Brienne is successful.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Brienne kills [Pyg](wiki:Pyg), [Timeon](wiki:Timeon), and [Shagwell](wiki:Shagwell), former [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions), at the [Whispers](wiki:Whispers), and Hyle helps her bury [Dick Crabb](wiki:Dick_Crabb).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1)

When Brienne returns to Maidenpool, Hyle brings the outlaws' heads with them so they can be displayed on the walls. Lord Randyll in incredulous when Hyle tells him about Brienne killing the outlaws, and he attributes her victory to her [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword, [Oathkeeper](wiki:Oathkeeper). He complains that he has executed many outlaws, but cannot find their leaders. Randyll is dismissive of Brienne's missions and forbids her from returning to Maidenpool while he is there.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

The [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)) of the [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle) tells Brienne that Lord Tarly is hunting outlaws from Maidenpool.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) When Brienne is held captive by the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners), [Jack-Be-Lucky](wiki:Jack-Be-Lucky) says that Lord Tarly has hanged a score of his comrades.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Once word of the imprisonment of [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) reaches Randyll, he marches his army to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Dickon has married Eleanor.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Randyll recounts that when some of the [Mountain's men](wiki:Mountain's_men) began committing crimes in Maidenpool, he had to hang a murderer and geld a rapist.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Randyll's army reaches King's Landing before his liege lord, [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell). The [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) releases [Margaery](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) and her cousins into his custody after Lord Tarly swears a holy oath to return them for trial. To try and undo the damage [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) did to the Tyrell and Lannister alliance, Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), now the Lord Regent, makes Randyll the new [justiciar](wiki:Justiciar) on King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon)'s [small council](wiki:Small_council) and restores the title of [master of laws](wiki:Master_of_laws).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Randyll makes a list of knights who could replace the late Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) on the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard),(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1) but Ser [Robert Strong](wiki:Robert_Strong) is chosen instead.(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

When news of the [taking of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost) and the [landing of the Golden Company](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company) arrive at the capital, Randyll expresses doubts about the identity of Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) as well as that of his [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) pretender, dismissing [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(Young_Griff)) as a "feigned boy". Randyll suggests sending Jon's kinsman, Ser [Ronnet Connington](wiki:Ronnet_Connington), to the [Wall](wiki:Wall), as well as the Mountain's Men who had escorted Red Ronnet to the capital. Lord Tarly is also dismissive of the [sparrows](wiki:Sparrows) who have overtaken the Faith. Kevan thinks that Randyll is more dangerous than Mace Tyrell, the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Randyll_Tarly.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
