## Origins

At the outbreak of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Gunthor was among the lesser lords that gathered at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) and sat on the [black council](wiki:Black_council) in support of Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).(wiki:Gunthor_Darklyn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Lord Darklyn counted himself among Rhaenyra's greatest supporters,(wiki:Gunthor_Darklyn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) well-aware that King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) would name him a traitor, having already received a summons from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) demanding an oath of loyalty from him. Shortly after, his uncle, Ser [Steffon Darklyn](wiki:Steffon_Darklyn), defected from Aegon II's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) with the crown of Rhaenyra's father, King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Gunthor_Darklyn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
Soon after, Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) led a host from King's Landing to move against the nearby lords sworn to Rhaenyra. [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) was among the first targets and the town's defenders were taken by surprise. In the subsequent [sack of Duskendale](wiki:Sack_of_Duskendale), the town was sacked, the harbor was set afire, and Lord Darklyn was beheaded by Cole. Most of Gunthor's garrison chose to swear fealty to Aegon II, although a few followed Lord Darklyn in death.(wiki:Gunthor_Darklyn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
With Gunthor's death, his widow, Lady [Meredyth Darklyn](wiki:Meredyth_Darklyn), took control of Duskendale.(wiki:Gunthor_Darklyn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Although amongst Rhaenyra's greatest supporters, House Darklyn suffered heavily for its loyalty to the princess, with Gunthor and Steffon both dying for her cause. As a result, when Rhaenyra was [forced to flee](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit) to Dragonstone, Lady Meredyth reluctantly hosted her at Duskendale, but only for a brief time.(wiki:Gunthor_Darklyn.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
