## Appearances & Description

Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) describes Luthor as fierce, and measuring almost seven feet tall. Luthor was rumored to have once killed a [warhorse](wiki:Horse) with a single punch.(wiki:Luthor_Largent.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Luthor wielded a [longsword](wiki:Longsword).(wiki:Luthor_Largent.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

## Origins

During the reign of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), Luthor was a captain of the [City Watch](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) commanding one of the seven city gates. He was promoted to the position of [Commander](wiki:Commander_of_the_City_Watch) in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), as he was seen by the [green council](wiki:Green_council) as the more fearsome of the officers sympathetic to Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen)'s cause. The [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower), named his son, Ser [Gwayne Hightower](wiki:Gwayne_Hightower), as Luthor's second to keep an eye on him.(wiki:Luthor_Largent.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
During the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing), Luthor and his gold cloaks turned on the [greens](wiki:Greens), as they were loyal to Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), their former commander. Luthor personally killed his second-in-command, Gwayne.(wiki:Luthor_Largent.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) As a reward for his loyalty, Luthor was ennobled by Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen),(wiki:Luthor_Largent.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) forming [House Largent](wiki:House_Largent).
When Queen [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen) committed suicide, many of the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) believed that Luthor killed her under the orders of Rhaenyra. Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) dismisses the idea, for there was proof Luthor was eating with three hundred gold cloaks at the barracks by the [Gate of the Gods](wiki:Gate_of_the_Gods) at the time of her death.(wiki:Luthor_Largent.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
During the [riots in the city](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), Luthor took four hundred spears to disperse sailors attacking the [River Gate](wiki:River_Gate) in an attempt to return to their ships.(wiki:Luthor_Largent.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Then he led five hundred gold cloaks down [Cobbler's Square](wiki:Cobbler's_Square) to disperse the thousands gathered around the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd). Some say the first victim of the confrontation was a little girl trodden under Luthor's warhorse. When Luthor ordered his men to apprehend the escaping Shepherd, the crowd attacked the guardsmen. The gold cloaks, despite being well-armed and well-disciplined, were no match against ten thousand rioters. Luthor was pulled down from his armored warhorse, stabbed in the stomach, and bludgeoned to death. When his body was recovered by the corpse wagons the next day, his head had been so badly crushed that his body was only recognized by its size.(wiki:Luthor_Largent.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)
The Captain of the [Old Gate](wiki:Old_Gate), Ser [Balon Byrch](wiki:Balon_Byrch), succeeded Luthor as Commander of the City Watch.(wiki:Luthor_Largent.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)
