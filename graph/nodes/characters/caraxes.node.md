## Origins

### Aemon the Prince of Dragonstone

It is unknown when Caraxes hatched from his [egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) but by [72 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) he was a young dragon of rideable size. That year, [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) and heir apparent of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), claimed Caraxes for the first time. The [Dragonkeepers](wiki:Dragonkeepers) considered him to be the fiercest of all the young dragons of the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) and nicknamed him the "Blood Wyrm". The Targaryen siblings [Alyssa](wiki:Alyssa_Targaryen), [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)) and Aemon liked to fly together on their dragons ([Meleys](wiki:Meleys), Vhagar, and Caraxes respectively).(wiki:Caraxes.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_.26_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Aemon used Caraxes against the [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) fleet during the [Fourth Dornish War](wiki:Fourth_Dornish_War) in [83 AC](wiki:83_AC).(wiki:Caraxes.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_.26_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) When rogue [Myrmen](wiki:Myr) [invaded](wiki:Myrish_Bloodbath) parts of the island of [Tarth](wiki:Tarth) in [92 AC](wiki:92_AC), Aemon flew to the island on Caraxes's back to help lords [Cameron Tarth](wiki:Cameron_Tarth) and [Boremund Baratheon](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon) fight back the invaders. Caraxes easily outdistanced the [Velaryon fleet](wiki:Velaryon_fleet) led by Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) in the process. Caraxes was eating goats when Prince Aemon was killed by a crossbow bolt in the throat.(wiki:Caraxes.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_.26_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

### Daemon the Rogue Prince

By [105 AC](wiki:105_AC), Aemon's nephew, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), had claimed Caraxes as his mount. Daemon took his [paramour](wiki:Paramour) [Mysaria](wiki:Mysaria) with him on Caraxes when he retreated from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) for [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). Daemon used Caraxes during the [War for the Stepstones](wiki:War_for_the_Stepstones) ([106](wiki:106_AC)–[115 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)). During those years, Daemon divided his time between the Stepstones and Dragonstone where he would often fly with his niece, Princess [Rhaenyra](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), and her dragon [Syrax](wiki:Syrax).(wiki:Caraxes.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

After Daemon remarried to Lady [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon), the newly wed toured the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) of [Essos](wiki:Essos) with their dragons Caraxes and Vhagar. Huge crowds came to see both dragons everywhere they went.(wiki:Caraxes.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) After their return to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), the couple often flew with Rhaenyra. It is believed that the princess's dragon, [Syrax](wiki:Syrax), mated with Caraxes at that time.(wiki:Caraxes.cite_ref-Rtrotd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

At the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Daemon landed Caraxes atop [Kingspyre Tower](wiki:Kingspyre_Tower) during the [assault on Harrenhal](wiki:Assault_on_Harrenhal).(wiki:Caraxes.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Atop Caraxes later in the civil war, Daemon challenged [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) and [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) at Harrenhal. All four were killed in the ensuing [Battle Above the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Above_the_Gods_Eye). At the end of the fight, Vhagar locked with Caraxes and they fell into the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye). While in freefall, even as Vhagar's claws opened up Caraxes's belly and used her teeth to tear off one of his wing-arms, Caraxes locked his teeth onto the larger dragon's throat and tore it out. Vhagar did not survive the force of the fall. Somehow, Caraxes managed to live long enough to pull himself out of the water and onto the shore, even though his entrails were falling out and one of his arms had been torn clean off. The dragon soon died in front of the walls of Harrenhal.(wiki:Caraxes.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
