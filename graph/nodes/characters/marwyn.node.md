## Appearances & Description

Marwyn has a thick neck and a strong jaw. He is short and squat with enormous hands, a thick chest, and a hard [ale](wiki:Ale)-belly. The [archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) has white hair coming out of his nose and ears. Marwyn is beetle-browed, his nose has been broken more than once, and his teeth are stained red from chewing [sourleaf](wiki:Sourleaf).(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
Though he wears a [maester](wiki:Maester)'s chain of many metals, Marwyn wears a leather jerkin instead of robes.(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) His ring, rod, and mask are made of [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel).(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
Other maesters consider Marwyn disreputable for keeping company with [whores](wiki:Whore), [hedge wizards](wiki:Hedge_wizard), sailors, and other [lowborn](wiki:Lowborn) of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) He can speak the languages of [Ibben](wiki:Ibben) and the [Summer Isles](wiki:Summer_Isles).(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Marwyn has unorthodox views of [magic](wiki:Magic),(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) and he often calls the rest of the archmaesters "the grey [sheep](wiki:Sheep)".(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)
Marwyn's chambers are at the [Ravenry](wiki:Ravenry) on the [Isle of Ravens](wiki:Isle_of_Ravens). They are found at the top of a flight of stairs in the north tower of the castle, below the [rookery](wiki:Rookery) of the black [ravens](wiki:Raven).(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

## Origins

Marwyn has traveled the lands beyond the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). He spent eight years in [the east](wiki:Further_East), mapping distant lands, searching for lost books, and studying with [warlocks](wiki:Warlocks) and [shadowbinders](wiki:Shadowbinder).(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) He encountered [Mirri Maz Duur](wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur) during his travels in [Asshai](wiki:Asshai), and taught her the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue) and the secrets of the human body.(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Marwyn's accounts of his travels confirmed reports that there are no living animals in Asshai.(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Rtwoiafasshai-by-the-shadow.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
Marwyn was nicknamed "Marwyn the Mage" by Archmaester [Vaellyn](wiki:Vaellyn) after his return to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel). The name was soon all over [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), much to Vaellyn's displeasure.(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
Before [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) was expelled from the Citadel for [necromancy](wiki:Necromancy) and vivisection, Marwyn was the only archmaester who liked his thoughts about dead souls leaving some residue in this world.(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
Maryn is rumored to have killed a man with his fists.(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)
Marwyn is the author of a book, [The Book of Lost Books](wiki:The_Book_of_Lost_Books).(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

In [Lhazar](wiki:Lhazar), [Mirri Maz Duur](wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur) mentions to [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) that she was taught about human anatomy, as well as the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue) of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), by a [maester](wiki:Maester) named Marwyn.(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

On the way to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) from [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) explains to Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) how he came to believe in the possibility of [ghosts](wiki:Ghosts), and mentions that of all the [archmaesters](wiki:Archmaesters) only Marwyn gave the matter any thought.(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

In [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), [Leo Tyrell](wiki:Leo_Tyrell_(son_of_Moryn)) tells the group of novices and acolytes assembled at the [Quill and Tankard](wiki:Quill_and_Tankard) that Marwyn believes the tales of [dragons](wiki:Dragons) are true, and that he has managed to light a [glass candle](wiki:Glass_candle). When [Armen](wiki:Armen) and [Roone](wiki:Roone) say that certain archmaesters consider Marwyn to be crazy, Leo shrugs and says it is a simple fact that "the [menagerie](wiki:Menagerie) hates the [mastiff](wiki:Dog)", meaning the archmaesters and Marwyn.(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffcprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)
In King's Landing, Qyburn explains to Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) why he was expelled from the Citadel, calling the archmaesters craven and saying that Marwyn calls them "the grey sheep",(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) a phrase Cersei comes to like.(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
In the [Book Tower](wiki:Book_Tower) at [Ten Towers](wiki:Ten_Towers), [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) finds her uncle, Lord [Rodrik Harlaw](wiki:Rodrik_Harlaw), reading Marwyn's [The Book of Lost Books](wiki:The_Book_of_Lost_Books). Rodrik says that Marwyn claims to have found three pages of [Signs and Portents](wiki:Signs_and_Portents), a book of [visions](wiki:Visions) written down by [Daenys the Dreamer](wiki:Daenys_Targaryen).(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)
Marwyn sees through his glass candle that [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) is coming to the Citadel, and sends [Alleras](wiki:Alleras) to meet him at the [Seneschal's Court](wiki:Seneschal's_Court). Alleras talks with Sam, and brings him to Marwyn's chambers in the [Ravenry](wiki:Ravenry). Marwyn listens to Samwell's story—that the late Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) believed [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) is the [prince that was promised](wiki:Prince_that_was_promised)—and states that he knows the [prophecy](wiki:Prophecy), but does not trust it. After Alleras says that Aemon wanted to go to Daenerys to counsel and protect her, Marwyn says it may be a good thing that Aemon died before he arrived in Oldtown, or the archmaesters might have killed him as part of their efforts to rid the world of [magic](wiki:Magic).(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)
Marwyn decides to travel to [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) in Aemon's place. He orders Samwell to keep quiet about Daenerys and the dragons, finish his studies, and then get back to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) as fast as possible. He orders "[Pate](wiki:Alchemist)" to find Sam a sleeping cell in the Ravenry, where he will help Pate tend Archmaester [Walgrave](wiki:Walgrave)'s [ravens](wiki:Raven), and he orders Alleras to look after Sam. Marwyn then leaves to catch the [swan ship](wiki:Swan_ship) [Cinnamon Wind](wiki:Cinnamon_Wind), in order to travel swiftly to reach Daenerys before the Citadel's man does.(wiki:Marwyn.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)
