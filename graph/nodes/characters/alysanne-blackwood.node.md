## Identity

Alysanne Blackwood — Lord Samwell's sixteen-year-old sister, later called Black Aly, is supposed by family legend to have loosed the arrow that killed Amos Bracken.

## Appearances & Description

Alysanne was tall and thin, with thick black curly hair, long enough to reach below her waist when loose. She had small breasts, but also long legs and strong arms. Although not noted for her beauty or softness, Alysanne was regarded as a fierce, fearless woman, with a bawdy tongue. She was said to smell of woodsmoke, a feature some found attractive.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
Black Aly was an excellent hunter, [horse](wiki:Horse)-breaker, and archer.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) She is rumored to have been fond of Lady [Sabitha Frey](wiki:Sabitha_Frey).(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

## Origins

### Dance of the Dragons[ ]

Alysanne lost her [maidenhead](wiki:Maiden) while horse-riding at the age of thirteen.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)
Alysanne was sixteen years old in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), when [House Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood) declared for the [blacks](wiki:Blacks), the faction loyal to Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) during the civil war called the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). However, the Blackwoods' hereditary enemies, [House Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken), supported King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and the [greens](wiki:Greens). During the [Battle of the Burning Mill](wiki:Battle_of_the_Burning_Mill), Ser [Amos Bracken](wiki:Amos_Bracken) slew Lord [Samwell Blackwood](wiki:Samwell_Blackwood) in single combat, but died immediately afterwards when a [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) arrow was fired into the eye slit of his helm. According to the Blackwoods, that arrow was shot by Alysanne, avenging her brother's death.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
Aly shared her tent with Lady [Sabitha Frey](wiki:Sabitha_Frey) while on campaign.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) Alysanne commanded three hundred archers of the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) during the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad) in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC). After her nephew, Lord [Benjicot Blackwood](wiki:Benjicot_Blackwood), broke the flank of Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon)'s royalist [greens](wiki:Greens), Alysanne's archers slew the royalist [knights](wiki:Knight) and unhorsed Borros, who was then slain by Lord [Kermit Tully](wiki:Kermit_Tully).(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) The [Lads](wiki:Lads) and their armies then advanced on [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing),(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) expecting to besiege the city, but arrived to discover King Aegon II Targaryen was dead, and their presence was welcomed.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)
Alysanne was one of the few commanders on either side who fought in the civil war from its first battle (Burning Mill) to its last (the Muddy Mess).(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-11)

### Aftermath[ ]

Within a fortnight of their arrival at the city, however, Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and his [northmen](wiki:Northmen) reached King's Landing. Though Cregan supported the new king, Rhaenyra's eleven-year-old son [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), he was incensed by the dishonorable [poisoning](wiki:Poison) of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), and had those he suspected of being involved in the plot arrested. This period, when Cregan briefly served as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), dispensed [justice](wiki:Justice), and performed executions of the guilty, became known as the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf). One of those arrested was Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), but he was pardoned by Aegon III, the half-brother of [his granddaughters](wiki:Dragon_Twins). Since Aegon was still under the [age of majority](wiki:Age_of_majority) and not yet anointed king, Cregan was within rights to ignore the pardon and execute Corlys, yet he did not do so.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7) Scholars have wondered why and proposed theories, but according to [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom)'s [Testimony](wiki:The_Testimony_of_Mushroom) has the best explanation: a bargain struck between Alysanne and Cregan. While accompanying her nephew Benjicot at court, Alysanne had met Cregan, and liked him despite his sternness. Cregan responded in kind, attracted to her atypical character and behavior, and soon began seeking her company. Therefore, when Alysanne came to him and asked him to let Aegon III's pardon of Corlys Velaryon stand, Cregan listened. She argued on behalf of the realm, honor, and peace; but in the end, requested it as a personal boon, in exchange for whatever he wished. In return, Cregan asked for her hand in marriage, and Alysanne gladly agreed.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8) According to Mushroom, Alysanne's actions helped to bring peace after the civil war.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-9)
Alysanne was present in King's Landing for the royal wedding of King Aegon III Targaryen and Queen [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen).(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-10) Afterwards, she and Cregan faced a problem — his army was comprised of many childless and homeless men, unwed men, old men, and younger sons,(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) people who might become burdens in the upcoming [winter](wiki:Winter) if they returned north. Alysanne suggested that the northmen remain in the south to help rebuild the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), as many women had lost their husbands during the war. Strength and a willingness to help would be welcome in many homes. In the end, more than a thousand northmen accompanied Alysanne and Lord Benjicot Blackwood when they left for [Raventree](wiki:Raventree) seventeen days after the wedding. Hundreds of marriages were made at the "[Widow Fairs](wiki:Widow_Fairs)" held at Raventree, [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept), the [Twins](wiki:Twins), and [Fairmarket](wiki:Fairmarket), and Alysanne's matchmaking was generally successful. The northmen who preferred to remain unmarried instead became [sworn swords](wiki:Sworn_sword) to lords, to be their guards and [men-at-arms](wiki:Men-at-arms). The resettled northmen strengthened the [riverlords](wiki:Riverlords) who welcomed them, especially [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) and House Blackwood, and helped revive and spread the worship of the [Old Gods](wiki:Old_Gods) in the south.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
Alysanne [married](wiki:Married) Cregan Stark in the [godswood of Winterfell](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell) in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC). Despite howling snowstorms, Benjicot led a retinue up the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to stand witness at his aunt's splendid wedding. Cregan's young son [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark_(son_of_Cregan)) sang a song for his new stepmother during the wedding feast.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Later, Alysanne gave Cregan four daughters: [Sarra](wiki:Sarra_Stark), [Alys](wiki:Alys_Stark), [Raya](wiki:Raya_Stark), and [Myriah Stark](wiki:Myriah_Stark).(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_stark_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)
Near the end of that same year the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) struck Westeros, doing much harm in King's Landing, but dealing its worst damage in the North where the cold weather seemed to aid its spread. Too few able-bodied men were left to harvest grain and a famine set in. With the North so weakened, in the following year the wildling chieftain [Sylas the Grim](wiki:Sylas_the_Grim) seized on the opportunity to lead three thousand of his warriors raiding south of the [Wall](wiki:Wall). Cregan rallied what few remaining healthy men were left in the North to stop the invasion.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-12)
Alysanne died at some unspecified later point in the many decades that Cregan Stark ruled Winterfell, after which he remarried to his distant cousin [Lynara Stark](wiki:Lynara_Stark), who bore him four more sons and another daughter. Rickon only had two daughters before his death, who were both married by sons of Lynara to secure their inheritance. As a result, later generations of the main Stark line do not descend from Alysanne Blackwood.(wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_stark_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)
