## Origins

### Early life

According to [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), Alyn's origins remain a matter of dispute among historians to this day.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) His mother, [Marilda](wiki:Marilda) of [Hull](wiki:Hull), claimed he and his brother [Addam](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) were fathered by Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon)—a fact that many found remarkable due to Laenor's known sexual preference for men. The [court fool](wiki:Court_fool) [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) claimed they were fathered by Laenor's father, Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), but kept unacknowledged and far from court in order to not offend Corlys's fiery-tempered wife, [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf47.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Addam and Alyn grew up in the shipyard of Marilda's father, and the boys served on her trading cog, *[Mouse](wiki:Mouse_(ship))*.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

### The Dance of the Dragons

When it was decided by Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) that the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) needed more [dragonriders](wiki:Dragonrider), many men on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) attempted to ride dragons, including [dragonseeds](wiki:Dragonseeds) such as [Addam](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) of [Hull](wiki:Hull) and his younger brother Alyn. Addam managed to win [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon)'s old dragon [Seasmoke](wiki:Seasmoke), and Alyn sought the wild dragon [Grey Ghost](wiki:Grey_Ghost) but could not find him. He then attempted to tame the wild dragon [Sheepstealer](wiki:Sheepstealer). When he entered his lair, the dragon set fire to his cloak. His brother Addam used his own cloak to beat out the flames, while Seasmoke drove off Sheepstealer. While Alyn was burned and scarred by the experience, he counted himself fortunate to have lived, as other dragonseed and seekers who had aspired to ride Sheepstealer ended being eaten by him instead.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

Not long after Addam had proven himself by flying Seasmoke, Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) petitioned Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) to remove the taint of [bastardy](wiki:Bastardy) from him and Alyn. The queen complied, and legitimized the brothers. Addam Velaryon was named heir to [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

After the treachery of the [Two Betrayers](wiki:Two_Betrayers), many voices in the [black council](wiki:Black_council) questioned Addam's loyalty. Only Lord Corlys spoke in defense of the dragonseed, remarking that Addam and his brother Alyn were "true Velaryons", worthy of Driftmark. After Addam fled Rhaenyra's arrest order, the queen imprisoned Corlys in the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Addam later fought and died in the [Second Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:Second_Battle_of_Tumbleton), proving his loyalty.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) fed Rhaenyra to [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre) during the [fall of Dragonstone](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) However, the fifteen-year-old Alyn commanded the Velaryon ships which prevented the king from returning to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). While Alyn was gathering the [Velaryon fleet](wiki:Velaryon_fleet) to attack Dragonstone, Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) came to an accord with Lord Corlys Velaryon. Alyn transported Aegon II back to the capital on Marilda's old cog, *[Mouse](wiki:Mouse_(ship))*.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) In [131 AC](wiki:131_AC) Alyn's ships were Aegon's only defense at the [Gullet](wiki:Gullet) against [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) ships transporting [Valemen](wiki:Valemen) toward [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), and armies of [rivermen](wiki:Rivermen) and [northmen](wiki:Northmen) were also marching against the king. Aegon II refused Corlys' advice to abdicate, and the king was soon after found dead from [poison](wiki:Poison).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

After a short siege, the servants of Dragonstone overwhelmed Aegon II's men and surrendered the castle to Alyn. During the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf), Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark), who briefly served as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), was convinced by [Alysanne Blackwood](wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood) to spare Corlys' life. [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) suggests Cregan was concerned about provoking Alyn and the Velaryon fleet.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Alyn attended [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen)'s coronation and the new king's wedding to [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

### Lord of the Tides

Alyn became [Lord of the Tides](wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides) after the death of Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC). Alyn and his mother, [Marilda of Hull](wiki:Marilda_of_Hull), transported Corlys' body to [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) on *[Mermaid's Kiss](wiki:Mermaid's_Kiss)*, then buried Corlys at sea aboard his *[Sea Snake](wiki:Sea_Snake)*. [Daemion](wiki:Daemion_Velaryon) and [Daeron Velaryon](wiki:Daeron_Velaryon) advanced their own claims to Driftmark, but the brothers reconciled with Alyn after [Aegon's regents](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) ruled against them. Ser [Malentine](wiki:Malentine_Velaryon) and Ser [Rhogar Velaryon](wiki:Rhogar_Velaryon), two of the [silent five](wiki:Silent_five), attempted to assassinate Alyn at [Castle Driftmark](wiki:Castle_Driftmark), but the former was killed in the attempt and the latter choose to [take the black](wiki:Take_the_black).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

As Corlys's heir, Alyn attempted to join the regents, but Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), rejected him for his youth, and instead selected Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) to replace Corlys.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) Later in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), the regents attempted to marry off Aegon's heir and Alyn's cousin, Lady [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen), to keep her under their control should the throne pass to her. Baela escaped the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) to flee the unwanted marriage, and came to Driftmark, where she confided in Alyn. They were married in the [sept](wiki:Sept) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) a fortnight later. Although some outraged regents wished to appeal to the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Regency_of_Aegon_III)) for an [annulment](wiki:Marriage), the Hand instead had it publicized that the marriage had been arranged by the king and court, as he believed Baela's defiance was the true scandal, not her choice of husband. In the eyes of the regents, however, Baela's wilful actions had confirmed that she should not be allowed to be Aegon's heir, and that Alyn was no suitable prince consort.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

### Daughters' War

After the passing of Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) named Alyn as his [lord admiral](wiki:Lord_admiral). Against the wishes of the king, Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) became the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Since the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) was thrown into turmoil because of the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War), Unwin desired a [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) presence on [Bloodstone](wiki:Bloodstone). Rather than Lord Alyn, Unwin named his uncle, Ser [Gedmund Peake](wiki:Gedmund_Peake), as the commander of the rebuilt royal fleet, with [Ned Bean](wiki:Ned_Bean) as his second. Unwin ordered Alyn to aid the inexperienced Gedmund in his attack against the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones). Alyn's flagship in the campaign was *[Queen Rhaenys](wiki:Queen_Rhaenys)*.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

[Lord Alyn's attack](wiki:Lord_Alyn's_attack) against the [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) in the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) earned him great fame and reputation. He gained the name *Oakenfist* for sinking the *[Grand Defiance](wiki:Grand_Defiance)* with his *Queen Rhaenys* "like a great oaken fist", and was also known as the *Hero of the Stepstones*.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7) Alyn won honors and rewards from the regents despite Lord Peake's protests. Alyn received [knighthood](wiki:Knight) from Ser [Marston Waters](wiki:Marston_Waters), the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), and was named [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships) by the king.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)

### The First Voyage

In [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), Unwin sent Alyn to deal with the Red Kraken, Lord [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), hoping he would be slain in the process. Instead, the expedition to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) around "the arse of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros)" (according to Alyn) became the first of Alyn's six [great voyages](wiki:Great_voyages).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf47i.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

Sailing into [Bloodstone](wiki:Bloodstone) aboard *[Lady Baela](wiki:Lady_Baela)*, Alyn spent a fortnight with [Racallio Ryndoon](wiki:Racallio_Ryndoon). A contradictory man, Racallio alternately treated the Oakenfist as first a friend and then an enemy. On one occasion the pirate king forced Alyn to wrestle him in a mud pit. After being accused of spying for the [Archon of Tyrosh](wiki:Archon_of_Tyrosh), Alyn was forced to kill three [Tyroshi](wiki:Tyrosh) prisoners and thereby prove his innocence. An overjoyed Racallio sent two of his wives to Alyn's chamber, but it is unknown if Alyn slept with the women. Racallio eventually allowed Alyn's fleet to continue past the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)

Alyn gained the favor of [Aliandra Martell](wiki:Aliandra_Martell), [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne), when he stopped at [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear). The book *[Bastard Born](wiki:Bastard_Born)* claims Alyn slept with the princess, whereas *[Hard as Oak](wiki:Hard_as_Oak)* says he refused her.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9) Alyn befriended Lord [Lyonel Hightower](wiki:Lyonel_Hightower) and [Samantha Tarly](wiki:Samantha_Tarly) at [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), where he also visited the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) and was blessed by the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon) at the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-10)

By the time Alyn reached the westerlands, Dalton had been assassinated by [Tess](wiki:Tess). Lady [Johanna Lannister](wiki:Johanna_Lannister) presented Alyn with a [golden](wiki:Gold) seahorse. Alyn left a third of his fleet with Ser [Leo Costayne](wiki:Leo_Costayne) to help protect the westermen, then stopped again at Oldtown during his return voyage. Once at Sunspear, Alyn learned from [Drazenko Rogare](wiki:Drazenko_Rogare) that Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), the younger brother of King [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), had survived the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) and was in the captivity of [House Rogare](wiki:House_Rogare) of [Lys](wiki:Lys). The Oakenfist sailed to the Free City and negotiated with [Lysandro Rogare](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare) for the prince's return.

Baela Targaryen presented Alyn with his daughter [Laena](wiki:Laena_Velaryon_(daughter_of_Alyn)) when *Lady Baela* returned to King's Landing in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC). Alyn was widely praised for having rescued Prince Viserys, although Lord Peake disapproved of the large ransom of one hundred thousand [golden dragons](wiki:Currency). Aegon's regents, however, granted new honors to Alyn. Unwin resigned and was replaced as Hand by Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-11) Alyn returned to [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) with his young family. At Driftmark in [135 AC](wiki:135_AC), Laena's [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) hatched a blind and wingless [wyrm](wiki:Wyrm) that immediately attacked her, and Alyn ripped it off his daughter and hacked it to pieces.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### War in the Vale

After the death of Lady [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn), the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) supported her preferred heir, Ser [Joffrey Arryn](wiki:Joffrey_Arryn), against the claims of Ser [Arnold Arryn](wiki:Arnold_Arryn) and [Isembard Arryn](wiki:Isembard_Arryn). After the death of Ser [Corwyn Corbray](wiki:Corwyn_Corbray) at [Runestone](wiki:Runestone), Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan) sent two armies to the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) to end the rebellion. While Ser [Robert Rowan](wiki:Robert_Rowan) took the [high road](wiki:High_road), Alyn transported [Moredo Rogare](wiki:Moredo_Rogare)'s men by sea. Alyn's ships defeated Isembard's sellsails at [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown), but the loyalists lost hundreds of men during the capture of the city.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

When the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring) ended with the fall of the [Rogares](wiki:House_Rogare), Moredo surrendered his command to Alyn and sailed for [Braavos](wiki:Braavos). Alyn and Lord [Benjicot Blackwood](wiki:Benjicot_Blackwood) eventually secured the Vale for Joffrey. During the gathering of nobles at King's Landing in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), Alyn suggested that Isembard become [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2)

### Later Life

Alyn and Baela's marriage was a stormy one.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) The Velaryons had lost much of their wealth to the [Rogare Bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank), so in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), Alyn assembled a fleet of merchantmen and war galleys led by *[Bold Marilda](wiki:Bold_Marilda)* for a trading expedition to [Volantis](wiki:Volantis). When Alyn first made his plans to leave Driftmark, Baela quarreled with him, as she had heard the rumors of her husband's involvement with Aliandra Martell. However, they reconciled as always before Alyn left Driftmark, where Baela remained, pregnant with their second child.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3)

While Corlys Velaryon had made all of his nine [great voyages](wiki:Great_voyages) on the *[Sea Snake](wiki:Sea_Snake)*, Alyn's six great voyages were made on six different ships, which he called "my ladies".(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6) The Oakenfist achieved great acclaim battling pirates from the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) and the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf824_17-0)

In [138 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [Addam Velaryon](wiki:Addam_Velaryon)'s bones were returned to Driftmark from [Raventree Hall](wiki:Raventree_Hall). Alyn used only a single word, "LOYAL", as the epitaph on his brother's new tomb, along with carvings of a [mouse](wiki:Mouse) and a seahorse.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf47.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

In [157 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Alyn took a decisive part in King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen)'s [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne) as [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf48.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) He commanded a fleet that broke the [Planky Town](wiki:Planky_Town), and swept halfway up the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood) river while the main Dornish strength was engaged with Daeron in the [Prince's Pass](wiki:Prince's_Pass).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Alyn returned to subdue the Planky Town and the Greenblood in [160 AC](wiki:160_AC) after the [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) rebelled, but the Young Dragon Daeron was slain the following year while under a peace banner. His successor, King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen), made peace with the Dornish.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf48.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

Alyn had an unknown number of children with his wife, Lady [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf101.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Although he was married, he loved the Princess [Elaena Targaryen](wiki:Elaena_Targaryen),(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) and would have two bastards by her, the twins [Jon](wiki:Jon_Waters) and [Jeyne Waters](wiki:Jeyne_Waters).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf49.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) Alyn disappeared at sea circa [171](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)–[176 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-calculation_8-1) A year after his disappearance, Elaena, who had hoped to marry him, agreed to wed another.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf49.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)

Two biographies, considered to be vulgar and unreliable, if colorful, were written about Alyn. The first, *[Hard as Oak](wiki:Hard_as_Oak)*, was written by Ser [Russell Stillman](wiki:Russell_Stillman), who [squired](wiki:Squire) for and was knighted by Alyn. The other, *[Bastard Born](wiki:Bastard_Born)*, was written by [Rue](wiki:Rue), who may have been a [septa](wiki:Septa) and may have been one of the Oakenfist's [paramours](wiki:Paramour).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-12) The book *[Six Times to Sea: Being an Account of the Great Voyages of Alyn Oakenfist](wiki:Six_Times_to_Sea:_Being_an_Account_of_the_Great_Voyages_of_Alyn_Oakenfist)*, by Maester [Bendamure](wiki:Bendamure), is considered to be the most complete and authoritative source on Alyn's life and voyages.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-13)

## Appearances & Description

Alyn and his brother [Addam](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) had silver hair and [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes). They were small and quick like their mother, [Marilda of Hull](wiki:Marilda_of_Hull).(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) After his failed attempt to tame the [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Sheepstealer](wiki:Sheepstealer), Alyn carried scars from the [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame) on his back and legs for the rest of his life.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Bold(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Alyn was considered proud and headstrong and at times insolent.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) He was not a good haggler.(wiki:Alyn_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfab22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)
