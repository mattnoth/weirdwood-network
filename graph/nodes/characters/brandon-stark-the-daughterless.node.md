## Identity

Brandon Stark (the Daughterless) — sworn to House Stark; Lord of Winterfell. One of 15 characters named Brandon Stark.

## Origins

### Legend of Bael the Bard

According to the stories as told by the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk), Lord Brandon once called [Bael the Bard](wiki:Bael_the_Bard) a coward. To take revenge for this affront and prove his courage, Bael climbed the [Wall](wiki:Wall) and traveled to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) by the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad). Calling himself *Sygerrik of [Skagos](wiki:Skagos)* ("Sygerrik" means *deceiver* in the [Old Tongue](wiki:Old_Tongue)), he presented himself as a singer, and was allowed into Winterfell. There, he sang for Brandon, who was so impressed by Bael's skills that he allowed him a reward. When Bael asked only for the most beautiful flower blooming in Winterfell's gardens, Lord Brandon sent for the most beautiful [blue winter rose](wiki:Blue_winter_rose) from his [glass gardens](wiki:Garden) as payment. The following morning, Lord Brandon's [maiden](wiki:Maiden) daughter, his only child, was gone, and the blue winter rose was found in her bed.(wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_Daughterless).cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

Lord Brandon sent the members of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) to search for his daughter and her kidnapper. After almost a year, neither Bael nor Brandon's daughter had been found. Brandon fell ill, [his line](wiki:House_Stark) on the verge of extinction. One day, he suddenly heard a child crying, and when he followed the sound, found his daughter nursing her child by Bael in her room. According to the story, Bael had never left Winterfell, but hidden with Brandon's only child in the [crypts](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell) of the castle. The [bastard](wiki:Bastard) Brandon's daughter had birthed eventually became the new [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_Daughterless).cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

There are questions as to whether Bael truly existed. While the [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) say he did, crediting many songs to his name, the old chronicles of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) do not mention him.(wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_Daughterless).cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_wall_and_beyond:_the_wildlings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Additionally, the timeframe of Lord Brandon's existence is unclear as well. [Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte) claims Brandon lived "a long time back."(wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_Daughterless).cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) As both Brandon and his grandson are given the title "[Lord](wiki:Lord)" instead of "[King](wiki:King_in_the_North)" this seems to indicate that the disappearance of Brandon's daughter took place after the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) had given up their kingship during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_Daughterless).cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Furthermore, the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) is mentioned, implying it took place during or after the reign of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), during whose reign the kingsroad was built.(wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_Daughterless).cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) On the other hand, the story states that Bael's son was killed by one of his own lords who wore his skin afterwards. The habit of [flaying](wiki:Flaying) an enemy and wearing his skin was practiced by the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton), but they are said to have given up the practice "a thousand years ago."(wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_Daughterless).cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Although [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) learned stories from Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) and [Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan) and heard of Bael before,(wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_Daughterless).cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) he had never heard of this legend before Ygritte's telling of it and because of its many discrepancies is certain that the story is a lie.(wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_Daughterless).cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
