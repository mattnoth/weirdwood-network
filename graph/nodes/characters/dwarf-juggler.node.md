## Origins

The juggler entertained people every day for years at the square by the [Fountain of the Drunken God](wiki:Fountain_of_the_Drunken_God) in Tyrosh. He was known to a pair of dwarf entertainers, [Oppo](wiki:Oppo) and [Penny](wiki:Penny).(wiki:Dwarf_juggler.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Appearances & Description

The [dwarf](wiki:Dwarf) was an old man.(wiki:Dwarf_juggler.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Dwarf_juggler.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) He was bald, and had brown eyes. He may have had [gold](wiki:Gold) teeth, or they may have fallen out with age.(wiki:Dwarf_juggler.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Because he was old, his hands were not as dexterous as they used to be, so he occasionally dropped the balls he was juggling and had to chase them, but the [Tyroshi](wiki:Tyroshi) would laugh and throw him [coins](wiki:Coin) nevertheless.(wiki:Dwarf_juggler.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

In [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) offers a [lordship](wiki:Lord) to anyone who brings her the head of her dwarf brother, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister),(wiki:Dwarf_juggler.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) who had been found guilty of the murder of her son, King [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), but escaped.(wiki:Dwarf_juggler.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Cersei's bounty results in the murder of a number of innocent dwarfs and children throughout [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) and the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Dwarf_juggler.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

A [Tyroshi](wiki:Tyroshi) with a forked beard [dyed](wiki:Hair_dye) green and pink presents Cersei with an elaborate chest, made of cedar, inlaid with [ivory](wiki:Ivory) in a pattern of vines and flowers, with hinges and clasps of white [gold](wiki:Gold). Inside is a dwarf's head on a bed of blue velvet, which Cersei immediately recognizes as not Tyrion's. The dwarf has brown eyes, whereas Tyrion's are green and black, though the Tyroshi claims he replaced Tyrion's eyes with glass ones when they started to rot. Cersei also notes that the dwarf is twice her brother's age and bald, and his teeth are missing. The Tyroshi claims the dwarf had gold teeth, which he removed. He protests that the dwarf has no nose, the same as Tyrion, and Cersei points out that he cut the dwarf's nose off. For the Tyroshi's fraud, Cersei has him sent to [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) to have his own nose cut off.(wiki:Dwarf_juggler.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

### A Dance with Dragons

On the *[Selaesori Qhoran](wiki:Selaesori_Qhoran)* in the [Summer Sea](wiki:Summer_Sea), Penny tells Tyrion that the night he was arrested for Joffrey's murder at [his wedding](wiki:Purple_Wedding), she and her brother Oppo left King's Landing in fear someone might suspect them of being involved. They went to Tyrosh, which they hoped was far enough away, and where they knew another dwarf, the juggler. One morning, they heard that the juggler had been murdered and his body was found at the [Temple of Trios](wiki:Temple_of_Trios). He had been cut into three parts, each part placed into one of the mouths of the statue of [Trios](wiki:Trios) by the temple doors. However, when the parts were sewn back together, his head was missing.(wiki:Dwarf_juggler.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Penny blames Tyrion for the old man's death, as she does for Oppo, who was also murdered by Cersei's bounty hunters. Tyrion says he is sorry for both of their deaths, but the blame is on Cersei and her bounty hunters, not him. Penny throws her [wine](wiki:Wine) in his face, and leaves the room.(wiki:Dwarf_juggler.cite_ref-Radwd33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
