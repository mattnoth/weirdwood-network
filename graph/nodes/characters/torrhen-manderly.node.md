## Origins

### The Dance of the Dragons[ ]

Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) visited [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) and [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and enlisted the [north](wiki:North) for the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). Torrhen's father, Lord [Desmond Manderly](wiki:Desmond_Manderly), sent Torrhen and his brother, Ser [Medrick](wiki:Medrick_Manderly), south to aid Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen). After Rhaenyra lost two [dragons](wiki:Dragon) to the [Two Betrayers](wiki:Two_Betrayers), Torrhen and his brother argued that the other two [dragonseeds](wiki:Dragonseeds), [Addam Velaryon](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) and [Nettles](wiki:Nettles), were also untrustworthy.(wiki:Torrhen_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
During the [riots at King's Landing](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(the_Dance_of_the_Dragons)), Torrhen led his northmen to [Fishmonger's Square](wiki:Fishmonger's_Square) and [River Row](wiki:River_Row), which they found to be swarming with the gutter knights of Ser [Perkin the Flea](wiki:Perkin_the_Flea). Torrhen's force lost a quarter of their men while retreating back to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). At [Dun Fort](wiki:Dun_Fort) they boarded the [Violande](wiki:Violande), which sailed them to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). Torrhen and his brother remained aboard the ship while Rhaenyra disembarked. From Dragonstone, Torrhen and his brother sailed back to White Harbor on the Violande.(wiki:Torrhen_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### Regency of Aegon III Targaryen[ ]

Ser Torrhen was named as one of King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen)'s seven regents in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC).(wiki:Torrhen_Manderly.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Torrhen_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) When the regents argued over who should be Aegon's heir, Torrhen objected to Lady [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen), the option presented by Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Torrhen_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
In late [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), a disease known as the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) fell on [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). It struck the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters) first and quickly spread to White Harbor. With the death of Torrhen's father, Lord [Desmond](wiki:Desmond_Manderly), followed four days later by the death of his brother, Ser [Medrick](wiki:Medrick_Manderly), Torrhen became the new [Lord](wiki:Lord_of_White_Harbor) of [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor). He gave up his position of regent and sailed back north.(wiki:Torrhen_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
Torrhen sent a letter to King's Landing when Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake), the King's Hand, attempted to betroth his own daughter [Myrielle Peake](wiki:Myrielle_Peake) to King Aegon III following the death of Queen [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen). In his letter, Lord Torrhen argued that Aegon III should marry a Manderly, given the fact that both of the [marriage](wiki:Marriage) pacts between [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) and [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) had been broken "by cruel chance".(wiki:Torrhen_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
When a [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) was called in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC) to choose the new regents and Hand of the King for King Aegon III, Lord Torrhen sailed to the capital with twoscore knights and cousins. Torrhen was chosen by the assembled lords as Aegon's new Hand. He accepted the position and named [Isembard Arryn](wiki:Isembard_Arryn) as the new [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) and [Gedmund Peake](wiki:Gedmund_Peake) as the [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships).(wiki:Torrhen_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
Torrhen's first task as Hand of the King was to sit in judgment at the trials of those accused of [poisoning](wiki:Poison) [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair) and plotting treason against King Aegon III.(wiki:Torrhen_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) During his time as Hand, Torrhen proved to be honest and able. He enacted a major reform of the taxes with the help of Isembard, made several appointments at court and repudiated the pact that Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) had signed to secure the release of Prince [Viserys Targaryens](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen). However, King Aegon III never warmed to Torrhen, and Torrhen never liked the king.(wiki:Torrhen_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
With Aegon III's [sixteenth nameday](wiki:Age_of_majority) approached, Torrhen made preparations for a great [royal progress](wiki:Royal_progress) throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) to mark the occasion. However, on the day Aegon III turned sixteen, the king came into the [council](wiki:Small_council) chambers and canceled the progress. He next dismissed his regents, and relieved Torrhen of his position as Hand. By doing so, Aegon III turned Torrhen in an enemy, as Torrhen considered his dismissal and the abrupt cancellation of the royal progress a deeply humiliating personal insult. Torrhen left King's Landing within a fortnight and sailed back to White Harbor aboard the [Jolly Salt](wiki:Jolly_Salt), accompanied by the [court fool](wiki:Court_fool) [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom), to whom he had offered the position of fool at the [Merman's Court](wiki:Merman's_Court).(wiki:Torrhen_Manderly.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
