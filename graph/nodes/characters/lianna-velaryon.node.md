## Origins

Lianna was the niece of [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)), [Lord of the Tides](wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides). After the death of Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), her daughter Queen [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) retreated to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), and found consolation over her mother's death with new companions, including her young cousin Lianna.(wiki:Lianna_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

In [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), Rhaena's husband [Androw Farman](wiki:Androw_Farman) used the [tears of Lys](wiki:Tears_of_Lys) to [poison](wiki:Poison) his wife's companions, having grown jealous of their friendships and weary of their mockery toward him. Initially believing there to be an [illness](wiki:Illness) spreading across Dragonstone, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) ordered his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)), to blockade the island. The King's Hand did as commanded, though not without grief, for his niece was still on the island. She was the last of Rhaena's companions to be poisoned by Androw. [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Anselm](wiki:Anselm) purged her, bled her, and covered her with ice, but nothing worked. She died in the arms of Rhaena, convulsing, even as her uncle's galleys were pushing off from [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark).(wiki:Lianna_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
