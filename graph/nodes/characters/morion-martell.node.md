## Identity

Morion Martell — Prince of Dorne who succeeded his father in Sunspear, rash and foolish, resentful of Dornish inaction during Lord Rogar's War.

## Origins

In [61 AC](wiki:61_AC), [knights](wiki:Knight) of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) marched into the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) to eliminate a [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Jaehaerys_I)). Morion's father, the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), kept [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) out of the conflict, leaving the Vulture King to his fate in [Lord Rogar's War](wiki:Lord_Rogar's_War). Prince Morion bristled at his father's apparent cowardice.(wiki:Morion_Martell.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

When his elderly father died, Morion succeeded him as the Prince of Dorne and [Lord of Sunspear](wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear) during the reign of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen). He was still angered by his father's inaction during Lord Rogar's War and wished to wash the slight away with an invasion of the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands). Assembling his hosts at [Ghost Hill](wiki:Ghost_Hill) and the [Tor](wiki:Tor), Morion loaded his Dornishmen onto the ships of pirates from the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), [sellsails](wiki:Sellsail) from [Myr](wiki:Myr), and corsairs of the [Pepper Coast](wiki:Pepper_Coast). He intended to land his armies on [Cape Wrath](wiki:Cape_Wrath), hoping to take the stormlords and King Jaehaerys unaware. However, it took the Prince of Dorne nearly a year to load his hosts aboard the ships and, by that time, spies and friends of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) among Morion's court and bannermen had informed the king of the prince's plans.(wiki:Morion_Martell.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

While Lord [Boremund Baratheon](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon) awaited the Dornish on the shores of Cape Wrath, King Jaehaerys and his sons took their [dragons](wiki:Dragon) to burn the Prince of Dorne's fleet. Not a single Dornishmen made it to shore alive, nor did the Iron Throne lose a single man. Morion died during his attempted invasion, called the [Fourth Dornish War](wiki:Fourth_Dornish_War), in [83 AC](wiki:83_AC). He was succeeded by [Mara Martell](wiki:Mara_Martell).(wiki:Morion_Martell.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)
