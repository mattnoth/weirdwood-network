## Origins

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Lord [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy) landed longships on Fair Isle. Lord Farman was unable to repel the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) attack and retreated to his castle, [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle), where he sent out pleas for help. No one came, however, and Faircastle eventually fell to the ironborn in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC).(wiki:Lord_Farman_(father_of_Lysa).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) The Red Kraken claimed four of Lord Farman's daughters as [salt wives](wiki:Salt_wives), including [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Farman), while giving the fifth "homely one" to his brother, [Veron](wiki:Veron_Greyjoy). Lord Farman and his sons were ransomed back to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) for their weights in silver.(wiki:Lord_Farman_(father_of_Lysa).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)(wiki:Lord_Farman_(father_of_Lysa).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_red_kraken.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) After the fall of Faircastle, Lord Dalton took Lord Farman's bedchamber as his own.(wiki:Lord_Farman_(father_of_Lysa).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_red_kraken.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
