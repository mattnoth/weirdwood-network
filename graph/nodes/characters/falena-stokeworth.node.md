## Origins

### Early life

Falena came to court during the early years of the reign of King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), during which time she would sometimes play at dolls with young Queen [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen). Unfortunately, Queen Jaehaera died in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), allegedly by suicide. Afterwards, the [Maiden's Day Ball](wiki:Maiden's_Day_Ball) was held at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) to find a second wife and new queen for King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). Many [maidens](wiki:Maiden) across [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) came to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in hopes of marrying the young king. Falena was one of the many maidens who suffered what was called the "[Maiden's Day curse](wiki:Maiden's_Day_curse)" by being injured or scandalized after the ball was announced. In Falena's case, she took a tumble down the serpentine steps of the Red Keep and broke a leg. According to [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), may have engineered the fall to promote his [own daughter](wiki:Myrielle_Peake) as the king's second wife.(wiki:Falena_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

### Aegon the Unworthy

In [149 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Falena became the first [mistress](wiki:Mistress) of Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), making him "a man" when the prince was fourteen. When a [knight](wiki:Knight) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) found them abed together in [151 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aegon's father, Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), wed Falena to the [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms) at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), Ser [Lucas Lothston](wiki:Lucas_Lothston_(husband_of_Falena)), and persuaded King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) to name Lucas as the [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal) in order to remove Falena from court. However, over the next two years, Aegon paid frequent visits to [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), and it was rumored that he was the father of Falena's daughter, [Jeyne Lothston](wiki:Jeyne_Lothston), not Lucas.(wiki:Falena_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

In [172 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Prince Aegon succeeded to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) as King Aegon IV. Falena brought her fourteen-year-old daughter to court in [178 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). The king took Jeyne as his eighth mistress, and named Lord Lucas as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). It was rumored but not proven that King Aegon enjoyed mother and daughter together in the same bed.(wiki:Falena_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) This possibly resulted in Lucas receiving the derogatory title "The Pander".(wiki:Falena_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Falena_Stokeworth.cite_ref-pander_6-0) However, Aegon soon gave Jeyne a [pox](wiki:Pox) he had caught from the [whores](wiki:Whores) he had seen after Lady [Bethany Bracken](wiki:Bethany_Bracken)'s execution, and the Lothstons were all sent away from court again.(wiki:Falena_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)
