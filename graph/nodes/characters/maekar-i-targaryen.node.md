## Identity

## Origins

### Youth

Maekar was the fourth son of King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) and Queen [Myriah Martell](wiki:Myriah_Martell).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) He had three older brothers: [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen), and [Rhaegel](wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) While King Daeron was no warrior, it was noted that two of his sons—Baelor and Maekar—appeared to be "all that could be wished in a [knight](wiki:Knight), lord, or heir." Baelor was a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) champion at seventeen, and Maekar seemed likely to show similar skills.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) Maekar learned to fight under the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms), Ser [Quentyn Ball](wiki:Quentyn_Ball), alongside his brothers and the [Great Bastards](wiki:Great_Bastards).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

As a fourth son, Maekar was not expected to rule. He married young, to Lady [Dyanna Dayne](wiki:Dyanna_Dayne), and had four sons and two daughters, [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen), [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), [Daella](wiki:Daella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Maekar_I)), [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), and [Rhae](wiki:Rhae_Targaryen).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-6)

Maekar was given the Targaryen summer residence of [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall) for his own seat.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)

### Blackfyre Rebellion

During the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [196 AC](wiki:196_AC), Maekar served as one of the Targaryen commanders. It was his shield wall that held against the "mad" attack of his uncle, Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) (called Bittersteel) in the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) While his brother [Baelor Breakspear](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)) attacked the rebel army from the rear with a host of [stormlords](wiki:Stormlords) and [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen), Maekar rallied what remained of Lord [Donnel Arryn](wiki:Donnel_Arryn)'s vanguard and made an unyielding anvil against which the rebels were hammered and destroyed, and the battle was won.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) Later, a song about "[the hammer and the anvil](wiki:The_hammer_and_the_anvil)" was made about the battle, with Baelor as the hammer and Maekar the anvil.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

Nevertheless, Maekar felt unappreciated. The victory was more attributed to his brother and the errors of [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), than to his efforts.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) In other things he also stood in the shadow of his brothers, wounding his pride.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)

Some time around [207 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) or [208 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) had four grown sons, three with sons of their own. He felt that having too many potential Targaryen heirs was dangerous, and sent Maekar's third son, [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) to become a [maester](wiki:Maester), despite his father's objections.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Maekar's wife, [Dyanna Dayne](wiki:Dyanna_Dayne), died some time before [209 AC](wiki:209_AC).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10)

### Tourney at Ashford

In [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) was held at [Ashford](wiki:Ashford). Maekar attended to see his sons [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) and [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen) compete, and his fourth son [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) was to squire for Daeron. Unfortunately, the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow) proved disastrous for Maekar and his sons. Daeron avoided the tourney and drank himself into a stupor at an [inn near Ashford](wiki:Inn_near_Ashford); Aegon joined the service of a [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight), Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), in disguise as "Egg"; and Aerion shamed himself during the joust by deliberately killing his opponent's horse. When Duncan attacked Aerion to defend [Tanselle](wiki:Tanselle), an innocent puppeteer, and Aerion demanded a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat), Maekar supported Aerion's call for vengeance as well as Daeron's accusation that Duncan had kidnapped Aegon.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11)

Maekar was unhorsed during the [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven), and he fought at the same time against his brother Baelor and Lord [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon), both of whom supported Duncan. Maekar delivered a lethal mace blow to Baelor's head. Baelor survived the trial, but died soon after when [Steely Pate](wiki:Steely_Pate) removed his helm. Maekar suspected that he would thereafter be held in suspicion as a [kinslayer](wiki:Kinslayer).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-12) According to the chronicles, Prince Maekar always bitterly regretted Baelor's passing and marked its anniversary every year.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)

Dealing with the problems of his sons, Maekar banished Aerion to the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) and offered Duncan a position in his personal guard to serve as a role model for Aegon. After Duncan refused, Maekar allowed Aegon to accompany the hedge knight in his travels as his squire.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-13) Maekar made Aegon promise to keep his hair shaved or dyed, to remain incognito, and also gave him a signet ring with his personal seal for his protection in case of emergencies.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

### Later life

Maekar's father, King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), and nephews, [Valarr](wiki:Valarr_Targaryen) and [Matarys](wiki:Matarys_Targaryen) died from the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness) in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) Maekar's brother succeeded to the throne as [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen) and appointed their bastard uncle, [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) (called Bloodraven), as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Maekar had expected to be appointed Hand and could not get along with Lord Bloodraven, so he withdrew from court to his residence at [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7) According to Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey), Lord [Gormon Peake](wiki:Gormon_Peake), and Ser [Kyle the Cat](wiki:Kyle_the_Cat), Maekar sulked and brooded at Summerhall.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) and Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) attended the [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC). When Ser [Mallor](wiki:Mallor)'s squire suggested that Maekar desired to kill his brother Aerys, "Egg" defended his father's reputation by striking the other squire.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) When the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion) began to collapse, Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell) feared Maekar's reaction.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)

After the accidental deaths of Daeron's third son, [Rhaegel](wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen), in [215 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and Rhaegel's son [Aelor](wiki:Aelor_Targaryen) in [217 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the childless King Aerys recognized his sole surviving brother Maekar as his heir, and Maekar became [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

During the [Third Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Third_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [219 AC](wiki:219_AC), Maekar was recognized for his leadership. Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) was captured in battle, and Bloodraven and Maekar's son, [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen), both urged that Aegor be executed. King Aerys was merciful, however, and allowed Bittersteel to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), a fate he managed to escape.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

### Reign

After Aerys's death in [221 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Maekar took the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Desiring to split from the past, he had a new [crown](wiki:Crown) forged, more warlike than previous crowns, since the crown of [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) had been lost upon [Daeron the Young Dragon's](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen) death during the [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne). Nevertheless, Maekar's reign, coming between two [Blackfyre Rebellions](wiki:Blackfyre_Pretenders), is generally regarded as peaceful.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5) And despite his previous strife with Bloodraven, Maekar kept his uncle as Hand of the King.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6)

Maekar summoned all four of his sons,(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) including [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), who had earned the chain of a maester in [217 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafaemon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) to court to serve the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Not wanting to sit on the [small council](wiki:Small_council) as he felt that would displace the [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester), Aemon chose instead to serve his eldest brother, [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), at his keep. This lasted until Daeron died, reputedly from a [pox](wiki:Pox) he caught from a [whore](wiki:Whore).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) In [232 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Maekar's second son [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen), the new heir to the throne,(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-aerion_17-0) also predeceased his father, after drinking a cup of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) in the belief that it would turn him into a [dragon](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7)(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3)

For seven years of Maekar's reign, a hot [summer](wiki:Summer_(season)) persisted. Some thought it was the [Great Summer](wiki:Great_Summer) come at last. When it broke, it was followed by a short [autumn](wiki:Autumn) and a long, cold [winter](wiki:Winter).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

During Maekar's reign, Mad [Danelle Lothston](wiki:Danelle_Lothston), [Lady of Harrenhal](wiki:Lady_of_Harrenhal), reportedly turned to the [black arts](wiki:Magic), causing the downfall of [House Lothston](wiki:House_Lothston). The crown gave the lordship of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) to [House Whent](wiki:House_Whent) as a reward for their service in bringing the Lothstons down.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_the_lords_of_harrenhal.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

In [233 AC](wiki:233_AC), Maekar fell in battle fighting a rebellious lord on the [Dornish Marches](wiki:Dornish_Marches)(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8) during the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) According to a semi-canon source, he died when his helm was crushed by a rock hurled from the battlements of [Starpike](wiki:Starpike) during the [storming of the castle](wiki:Storming_of_Starpike).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-sample_8-1) Maekar was succeeded by his fourth son, [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), after a [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) was convened to resolve the political chaos.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-9)

## Appearances & Description

The thickly-built and stocky Maekar was a powerful man with [violet eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes). He had straight hair and a square-cut beard, both being pale [silver](wiki:Silver) touched with [gold](wiki:Gold). [Pox](wiki:Pox) scars marred his cheeks. He stood about a head shorter than his eldest brother [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), who was a tall man himself.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

Maekar could be prickly and impatient.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) He was a harsh man, quick to judge and to condemn. Unlike his brother Baelor, he was not charismatic and could not make friends and allies easily.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Even so, in his youth, Maekar was regarded as all that could be wished in a knight, lord or heir, just as his eldest brother was.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) After Baelor's accidental death by his hands, Maekar became even more stern and unforgiving.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

Maekar had his own personal coat of arms, the three-headed [Targaryen dragon](wiki:House_Targaryen), quartered.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Beneath a heavy black cloak trimmed with [ermine](wiki:Ermine), he wore a leather brigandine covered with silver studs. Maekar's battle armor was decorated with black curved dragon teeth along the shoulders and down his back, and the crest of his helm had dragon teeth as well. He wielded a huge spiked mace.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) As king, Maekar wore a crown of black [iron](wiki:Iron) points in a band of red gold.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-13)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Speaking to Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) recalls the long and hot summer that encompassed much of Maekar's reign before suddenly changing to a brief autumn and terrible winter in its seventh year.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

When [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) struggles with his loyalty to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) after word of terrible things befalling his father reaches the [Wall](wiki:Wall), Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) tells Jon that he understands and has been similarly tested. When Jon asks who Aemon is, he reveals that he is the son of King Maekar.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) tells [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) about Maekar's objection to Aemon studying at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

As Aemon's health begins to fail in the last days of his life, he would sometimes lose track of his thoughts while speaking to Samwell and ramble about his father or brother. Accepting that he is now dying, Aemon tells Sam that he will be with his brothers and father soon. He remembers that Maekar never thought the throne would pass to him and often said it was punishment for the blow that killed his brother, and he hopes his father found peace after he died.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

After learning of Aemon's death, [Alleras](wiki:Alleras) tells Sam that they could have learned much from him about the reigns of both Aemon's father and [uncle](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

### The Winds of Winter

During the [second siege of Meereen](wiki:Second_siege_of_Meereen), when [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy)'s [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) fleet comes ashore and begins to attack his [Yunkish](wiki:Yunkai) adversaries, Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) celebrates. He likens the arrival of a second force on their side to Baelor Breakspear and Prince Maekar, the hammer and the anvil.(wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Bbarristan_ii_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Cbarristan_ii.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
