## Origins

According to legend, [Night's King](wiki:Night's_King) lived during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), not long after the [Wall](wiki:Wall) was complete. He was a fearless warrior who served as the thirteenth [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch). After viewing the white-skinned woman from atop the Wall, he chased and loved her. Night's King brought her back to the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort) and after the unholy union, he declared himself king and her his queen.(wiki:Corpse_queen.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Night's King ruled the [Nightfort](wiki:Nightfort) as his own castle for thirteen years. During the dark years of his reign, horrific atrocities were committed, of which tales are still told in the [north](wiki:North). It was not until [Brandon the Breaker](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(Breaker)), the [King of Winter](wiki:King_in_the_North), and [Joramun](wiki:Joramun), the [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall), joined forces that Night's King was brought down and the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) freed.(wiki:Corpse_queen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) After his fall, when it was discovered that Night's King had been making sacrifices to the [Others](wiki:Others), all records of him were destroyed and his very name was forbidden and forgotten.(wiki:Corpse_queen.cite_ref-Rasos56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

[Maesters](wiki:Maesters) are skeptical of the legend, thinking the corpse queen was possibly a daughter of a [Barrow King](wiki:Barrow_Kings) from the [barrowlands](wiki:Barrowlands), who were often associated with [graves and burials](wiki:Barrow).(wiki:Corpse_queen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
