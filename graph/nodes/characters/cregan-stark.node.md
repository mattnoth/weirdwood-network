## Identity

Cregan Stark (b. 108 AC) — child of Gilliane Glover and Rickon Stark (son of Benjen); married to Alysanne Blackwood and Arra Norrey and Lynara Stark; sworn to House Stark; Lord of Winterfell.

## Origins

### Early life

Cregan was born in [108 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) as the eldest son of [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark_(son_of_Benjen)), [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell) and [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), and Lady [Gilliane Glover](wiki:Gilliane_Glover).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_stark_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-13) He had a younger brother, who died in [119 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and, according to *[The Testimony of Mushroom](wiki:The_Testimony_of_Mushroom)*, a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) half-sister, [Sara Snow](wiki:Sara_Snow).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

When Lord Rickon passed away in [121 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), he was succeeded by the thirteen-year-old Cregan. Rickon's brother, [Bennard Stark](wiki:Bennard_Stark), ruled the [north](wiki:North) as [regent](wiki:Regent) during Cregan's minority. Bennard was slow to relinquish power when Cregan turned sixteen and [came of age](wiki:Customs) in [124 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), however, and relations were tense between uncle and nephew.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

In [126 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Cregan took up rule of the north by having Bennard and his three sons imprisoned. The young lord then married Lady [Arra Norrey](wiki:Arra_Norrey), a friend from childhood, but she died in [128 AC](wiki:128_AC) while giving birth to a son. Cregan named his infant heir [Rickon](wiki:Rickon_Stark_(son_of_Cregan)) after his late father.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

### The Dance of the Dragons

For over a century after the [Targaryen Conquest](wiki:Targaryen_Conquest), House Stark remained largely disinterested in royal politics and aloof from affairs at court. Upon the death of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) of Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) opposed the [greens](wiki:Greens) of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon), [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), was sent to gain additional support for his mother Rhaenyra's claim to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)

After flying on his [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Vermax](wiki:Vermax) to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), [Sisterton](wiki:Sisterton), and [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), Jacaerys arrived at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to negotiate with Lord Cregan. According to Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun), Cregan and the prince got along well, as Jacaerys reminded Cregan of his late younger brother. After spending time drinking, hunting, and training, Cregan and Jacaerys swore an oath of brotherhood. In contrast to Munkun's writings, Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) claimed that Jacaerys spent his time trying to convert Lord Stark from the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) to the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) claimed that Cregan had a half-sister, [Sara Snow](wiki:Sara_Snow), who slept with Jacaerys, and that an angry Cregan only relented after being told that Jacaerys and Sara had secretly wed in the [godswood of Winterfell](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell). Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) is skeptical of Mushroom's ribald stories, however.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8) Regardless, Lord Cregan and Prince Jacaerys reached an agreement, known by Munkun as the [Pact of Ice and Fire](wiki:Pact_of_Ice_and_Fire), which stated that the future firstborn daughter of Jacaerys would one day marry Cregan's son, [Rickon](wiki:Rickon_Stark_(son_of_Cregan)). Cregan pledged his support to the blacks.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)

[Roderick Dustin](wiki:Roderick_Dustin), [Lord of Barrowton](wiki:Lord_of_Barrowton), commanded the first host of northmen who marched south, known as the [Winter Wolves](wiki:Winter_Wolves), older men who feared a long winter. Roddy the Ruin's host helped the blacks achieve victories over the greens in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), but the Winter Wolves were eventually lost in the [First Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:First_Battle_of_Tumbleton). [Desmond Manderly](wiki:Desmond_Manderly), [Lord of White Harbor](wiki:Lord_of_White_Harbor), also sent soldiers by sea to support Rhaenyra. The White Harbor men were led by Desmond's two sons Ser [Medrick](wiki:Medrick_Manderly) and Ser [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Prince Jacaerys was killed in the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet),(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and Queen Rhaenyra [fled](wiki:Flight_to_Dragonstone) [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) after the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit). While Rhaenyra was at [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), Cregan sent word from Winterfell that he would gather for her a host of ten thousand northmen, although their march south would need to wait until the last harvest before winter was brought in.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Rhaenyra was slain when she returned to [Dragonstone](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

Although King Aegon II returned to King's Landing to rule, Lord Stark honored his word to the blacks and marched south with the second northern host in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC); Eustace claimed Cregan had twenty thousand men, while Munkun believed the number to be eight thousand northmen.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) While the northmen were still on the march, the [rivermen](wiki:Rivermen) defeated [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End), in the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad), and Aegon II was soon [poisoned](wiki:Poison) in the capital.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

### The Hour of the Wolf

Although they were allies, the northmen outnumbered the [rivermen](wiki:Rivermen) when they reached [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), and the [Lads](wiki:Lads) were intimidated by the formidable Cregan. Many of his northmen were childless, homeless, unwed, or younger sons marching to war to spare their families from supporting them in winter. Lord Stark intended to continue the war by marching on [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), and [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), powerful seats of the [greens](wiki:Greens). Cregan had many members of court imprisoned for the murder of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen);(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) although he had been Aegon's foe during the Dance, Lord Stark objected to a monarch being killed by [poison](wiki:Poison) instead of in battle.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

Cregan held court in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) for six days, a time known as the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf), with the eleven-year-old Prince [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), the son of the late Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), confined to [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast). The remaining green lords sent word of their disinterest in continuing the war, and women at court encouraged peace as well. Lord Stark reluctantly agreed to not march on the castles of the greens, although he insisted that the murderers of Aegon the Elder be held accountable. In order to administer justice, Lord Stark was named [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) by Prince Aegon the Younger.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

Ruling from the foot of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Cregan allowed Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) to be released but condemned Grand Maester [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle) and Ser [Gyles Belgrave](wiki:Gyles_Belgrave) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). Twenty-two lesser individuals were also marked for death by Cregan. Lord Stark condemned the soldiers from [House Strong](wiki:House_Strong) who had freed Lady [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen) from imprisonment, but he agreed to spare them when Baela brandished a sword in their defense.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

Cregan also condemned [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), [Lord of the Tides](wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides), who said he had acted for the good of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), and [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong), [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal), who refused to offer a defense. Baela and her sister [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)) convinced Prince Aegon to spare Corlys, however, to which Cregan acquiesced. According to Mushroom, Cregan agreed to the sparing of the Sea Snake through the actions of [Alysanne Blackwood](wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood), whom Cregan had become impressed with during their time at the Red Keep. In return for Corlys's life, Black Aly agreed to marry Cregan, a widower.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

Cregan decided to personally behead the condemned with [Ice](wiki:Ice), the ancestral [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) greatsword of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark). When Lord Stark allowed Ser [Perkin the Flea](wiki:Perkin_the_Flea) to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), however, most of the other condemned had their lives spared by asking to take the black as well. Gyles and Larys were the only two to be executed by Cregan, who resigned as Hand the following day.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)

Within a fortnight of [Aegon the Younger's coronation](wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen) as King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), Cregan began his journey back to the north, having declined an offer to serve in the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Many of the northmen who had marched to King's Landing with him remained in the south, however, choosing to wed women of the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) at [Widow Fairs](wiki:Widow_Fairs) or taking up service as guards and men-at-arms.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

### The Regency era

Upon his return to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), Cregan wed [Alysanne Blackwood](wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood). Her nephew [Benjicot Blackwood](wiki:Benjicot_Blackwood), [Lord of Raventree Hall](wiki:Lord_of_Raventree_Hall), was in attendance.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

Later that same year, however, the North was one of the first regions in Westeros struck by the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever), which continued through [133 AC](wiki:133_AC). The plague eventually spread to most of Westeros as far south as the Blackwater, but it was noted to be much more aggressive in cold climates. When it spread to King's Landing it killed one fifth of the city, thus the death rate was apparently much greater in the North, where [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) and [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton) were hard hit (White Harbor is the North's only city, while Barrowton and the [Winter town](wiki:Winter_town) outside Winterfell are the only two settlements large enough to be called proper "towns").

With the North so badly weakened from winter famine and then the devastating sickness, the [wildlings](wiki:Free_Folk) from beyond the Wall seized on the opportunity to launch major raids into the south. In [133 AC](wiki:133_AC) a wildling chieftain called [Sylas the Grim](wiki:Sylas_the_Grim) led a large force of 3,000 to force their way south of the Wall at [Queensgate](wiki:Queensgate) (overwhelming the Night's Watch garrison). Afterwards they proceeded to plunder the lands of the [Gift](wiki:Gift). Lord Cregan rode forth from Winterfell and rallied what able-bodied men were left to his banner: [Glovers](wiki:House_Glover) of Deepwood Motte, [Flints](wiki:House_Flint_of_the_mountains) and [Norreys](wiki:House_Norrey) from the hill clans, and a hundred [rangers](wiki:Rangers) of the Night's Watch. Cregan's combined force then hunted down and destroyed Sylas's raiding bands.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

After the death of Queen [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen) later in 133 AC, Cregan objected to the suggestion of [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake), [Lord of Starpike](wiki:Lord_of_Starpike) and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), that King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) wed his daughter, [Myrielle Peake](wiki:Myrielle_Peake).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

### Later Reign

Aegon III granted many rewards to Cregan, although a [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) princess never did marry into [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Maester [Kennet](wiki:Kennet_(maester)) served at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) during Cregan's long reign.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Cregan once fought Prince [Aemon the Dragonknight](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), who praised Lord Stark's skill by stating he never faced a finer swordsman.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Cregan's second wife Alysanne Blackwood bore him four daughters, though Rickon - his son and only child by his first wife - was his heir. At some unspecified point in later decades Alysanne died, however, and Cregan married for a third time, to [Lynara Stark](wiki:Lynara_Stark). Lynara was a distant minor Stark cousin, descending from a younger son of Lord [Brandon "the Boisterous" Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_Boisterous)) (who himself succeeded [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark), the last Stark king). Lynara went on to bear Cregan four sons and one daughter: due to male-preference inheritance law, all four sons stood in line of succession ahead of their four older half-sisters through Aly Blackwood.

Cregan had a long life, which earned him the nickname of "The Old Man in the North". The exact date of Cregan's death has not yet been provided, though because he came to rule young (as soon as he turned sixteen in 126 AC) and lived to be quite old, he ruled over the North for a considerable span of decades. Cregan was still alive in 157 AC, and thus he ruled the North for at least thirty years. In 157 AC Cregan would have been 49 years of age, not yet a very old man. It is possible, though unlikely, that Cregan may have lived until the time of the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [196 AC](wiki:196_AC) - as Cregan would have been 88 years old in that year.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Cregan is entombed in the [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

### Legacy

Cregan outlived his eldest son and heir, [Rickon](wiki:Rickon_Stark_(son_of_Cregan)), who died at [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) during the [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne) by King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen) in [157 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) Rickon was survived by two daughters, [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark_(daughter_of_Rickon)) and [Serena](wiki:Serena_Stark), and by the known [laws](wiki:Laws_and_justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms) of succession they should have been ahead of Rickon's younger brothers in line of succession. However, northern succession laws may simply be different(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-HW_19-0); there has never been a ruling Queen of Winter or ruling Lady of Winterfell, for example,(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-20) and primogeniture is not necessarily binding elsewhere in the Seven Kingdoms either.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-21) Whatever the case was, Cregan's second son [Jonnel Stark](wiki:Jonnel_Stark) succeeded Cregan upon his death, marrying his own half-niece Sansa.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_appendix:_stark_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Cregan's third son [Edric stark](wiki:Edric_Stark_(son_of_Cregan)) married Rickon's other daughter Serena.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_appendix:_stark_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) Jonnel's marriage to Sansa was childless, but upon his death rule passed not to Cregan's third son Edric, but to his fourth son [Barthogan Stark](wiki:Barthogan_Stark).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_north:_the_stoneborn_of_skagos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) While it is possible that Edric predeceased his younger brother, Edric did have two sons and two daughters with Serena, and the daughters had children of their own, (wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_appendix:_stark_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2) and all of these should have been ahead in the line of succession to Barthogan. The exact reasons and circumstances under which Barthogan became Lord of Winterfell are as yet unknown.

Barthogan himself later died childless, when he was slain in the [Skagosi](wiki:Skagos) rebellion during the reign of Daeron II Targaryen,(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_north:_the_stoneborn_of_skagos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) and he was succeeded by Cregan's fifth and final son, [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(son_of_Cregan)), from whom the later generations of House Stark descend. Convoluted succession challenges remained for decades, possibly through Serena and Edric's children - and between Brandon's own rival grandchildren. Brandon was succeeded by his eldest son [Rodwell Stark](wiki:Rodwell_Stark), who died without issue, after which rule passed to his younger brother [Beron Stark](wiki:Beron_Stark). The grandson of Cregan, Beron, was mortally wounded fighting off an incursion by [Dagon Greyjoy](wiki:Dagon_Greyjoy)'s [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn), and as he lay dying in Winterfell [multiple Stark widows](wiki:The_She-Wolves_of_Winterfell) pushed their children's rival succession claims against the others - only for the unlikely traveling duo [Dunk and Egg](wiki:Dunk_and_Egg) to become embroiled in the ensuing events.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-24)(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-25)

## Appearances & Description

Cregan had grey eyes which reminded Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) of a winter storm.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), Cregan was a stern and formidable [lord](wiki:Lord).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) Like many other [Starks](wiki:House_Stark), Cregan was a good friend to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) Lord [Cerwyn](wiki:House_Cerwyn) was said to be his closest friend.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) Prince [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), the Dragonknight, claimed he never faced a finer swordsman than Lord Cregan.(wiki:Cregan_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

- Lord of Winterfell at 21, who would become the Old Man of the North; came into his lordship at 13 on his father Rickon's death in 121 AC (fab-the-blacks-and-the-greens-16-p02:137)

- He wed Arra Norrey, who died in 128 AC bearing his son Rickon (fab-the-blacks-and-the-greens-16-p02:137)
- He gave Jacaerys a warm welcome and sealed the Pact of Ice and Fire (fab-the-blacks-and-the-greens-16-p02:159)
- Munkun says they swore an oath of brotherhood, while Eustace claims Jacaerys spent his visit trying to convert Cregan to the Seven (fab-the-blacks-and-the-greens-16-p02:145)
