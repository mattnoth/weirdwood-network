## Identity

Lord Cregan Stark, known as the Wolf of the North and later the Old Man of the North, was the Lord of Winterfell, Warden of the North, and briefly Hand of the King during the reign of Aegon III Targaryen (track_b: Titles, wiki:Cregan_Stark). He was a stern and formidable lord with grey eyes that reminded Septon Eustace of a winter storm (wiki:Cregan_Stark).

## Origins

Born in 108 AC, the son of Lord Rickon Stark and Lady Gilliane Glover (track_b: Father, Mother, Born). Died between 157–209 AC (track_b: Died). Buried in the Crypt of Winterfell (track_b: Buried).

## Narrative Arc

Cregan was Lord of Winterfell during the reigns of Viserys I, Aegon II, Aegon III, and Daeron I Targaryen. During the Dance of the Dragons he sided with the Blacks. After the war he briefly served as Hand of the King for Aegon III — a period remembered as the Hour of the Wolf (wiki:Cregan_Stark). Like many Starks, he was a good friend to the Night's Watch (wiki:Cregan_Stark). Prince Aemon the Dragonknight said he had never faced a finer swordsman than Cregan (agot-bran-07). He married three times: Arra Norrey, Alysanne Blackwood, and Lynara Stark (track_b: Spouses), and fathered ten known children across these marriages (track_b: Issue).
