### Early life

Duncan's earliest memories were of living as a street urchin in the [Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom) slums of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) His closest friends went by the names of [Ferret](wiki:Ferret), [Rafe](wiki:Rafe), and [Pudding](wiki:Pudding). By his own admission, they were "little monsters" with Duncan, at the time still known as "Dunk", the worst of all.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) Sometimes they would sell(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) or even dump(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8) pieces of meat of dubious origin to the local pot shops that made the [bowls of brown](wiki:Bowl_of_brown). ### Tourney at Ashford Meadow

In [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) was to be held at [Ashford Meadow](wiki:Ashford_Meadow) in the [Reach](wiki:Reach). Ser [Arlan of Pennytree](wiki:Arlan_of_Pennytree) died on the way to [Ashford Castle](wiki:Ashford), however, and after Dunk had buried the [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight), he decided to compete in the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow) in Arlan's stead. At [an inn](wiki:Inn_near_Ashford) along the way, Dunk met a [drunken man](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) who claimed to have dreamed about him, and who became distraught at the sight of him. Dunk also met a bald-headed boy who begged Dunk to be allowed to become his [squire](wiki:Squire) when Dunk left. Dunk refused, left, and shortly thereafter arrived at Ashford.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-19)

Duncan made camp, and that evening discovered the bald boy from the inn at his camp. When the boy assumed that Dunk's name was short for Duncan, Dunk agreed, calling himself Ser Duncan the Tall, for his height and because it sounded powerful. The boy claimed to be called "Egg" and said he came from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), causing Dunk to believe the boy was an orphan from [Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom), just like he had been. Because he did not have a squire, Dunk accepted Egg as his squire for the duration of the tourney. That night, in their camp under an [elm](wiki:Elm) tree, Dunk saw a green [falling star](wiki:Astronomy), which he believed was lucky.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-20)

Once at the tourney grounds, Duncan was forced to sell [Sweetfoot](wiki:Sweetfoot), one of his [horses](wiki:Horse), and Arlan's old [armor](wiki:Armor), in order to buy armor of his own from [Steely Pate](wiki:Steely_Pate). He met a puppeteer named [Tanselle](wiki:Tanselle), who was performing at a stall on the tourney grounds. The day after Dunk's arrival, he went to register for the tourney, which required [knighthood](wiki:Knighthood) to participate. Identifying himself as Ser Duncan the Tall, Dunk told [Plummer](wiki:Plummer), [Lord Ashford](wiki:Lord_Ashford)'s steward, how Arlan had knighted him before his death. Several of his thoughts and actions, as well as other facts, however, suggest that his tale is not completely true.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-knighthood_17-0) Plummer suggested that Duncan find someone to vouch for him and return the next day.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-24)

Unable to find anyone, Duncan returned to the castle the next day, where he accidentally interrupted Prince [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)) and Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), who were occupied with the missing Princes [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) and [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), two of Maekar's sons. After Duncan explained his problem to the princes, Baelor recalled jousting against Arlan years before, and vouched for Duncan, allowing his participation. Since Dunk's shield displayed the winged chalice arms of the late Arlan, Baelor said he could not bear the sigil himself, as he was not Arlan's trueborn son. Dunk paid Tanselle to paint over the shield with a new design, a green shooting star above an elm tree at sunset; a sunset because Arlan liked them, an elm for the tree by his camp, and a shooting star for the lucky one he had seen.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-25)

When Tanselle was attacked by Prince [Aerion Targaryen](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen), Duncan rushed to her defense, attacking the prince in the process. When Aerion's guards intervened, Egg protected Duncan from further harm by revealing his identity as Prince Aegon. Duncan was arrested and brought to the castle, where Prince Baelor informed him how Prince Daeron had claimed to his father that the missing Aegon had been kidnapped by a robber knight, while Aerion was twisting the puppeteer story into treason. Due to the lies of both Aerion and Daeron, Maekar became furious with Duncan. Baelor was forced to allow Aerion a trial, and Aerion demanded a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-26)

With help from Aegon, Duncan found six allies: Ser [Robyn Rhysling](wiki:Robyn_Rhysling), Ser [Humfrey Hardyng](wiki:Humfrey_Hardyng), Lord [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon), Ser [Humfrey Beesbury](wiki:Humfrey_Beesbury), the newly-knighted [Raymun Fossoway](wiki:Raymun_Fossoway), and Prince Baelor. Aerion was supported by Daeron, Maekar, Ser [Steffon Fossoway](wiki:Steffon_Fossoway), Ser [Willem Wylde](wiki:Willem_Wylde), Ser [Donnel of Duskendale](wiki:Donnel_of_Duskendale), and Ser [Roland Crakehall](wiki:Roland_Crakehall_(Kingsguard)), the latter three being knights of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). To his surprise, Duncan discovered that the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) supported him, as he had remembered his vows as a knight(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-27) ("protecting the weak and defending the innocent")(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) by protecting Tanselle. During the combat, Duncan eventually found himself facing Aerion one on one, and he managed to best Aerion by using his strength and his fighting skills from Flea Bottom. Duncan forced Aerion to yield. As Daeron had already faked his defeat—as he had promised Duncan the day before—Duncan was declared cleared free of all charges.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-28)

Humfrey Beesbury was slain in the fighting, and Humfrey Hardyng died from his wounds in the night. Prince Baelor was mortally wounded as well by his own brother, Prince Maekar, leaving Duncan feeling extremely guilty. Maekar offered Duncan a place at [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall) so his son Aegon could squire for Duncan. While Duncan agreed to take the prince as his squire, he insisted on remaining a hedge knight, convinced it would teach Egg humility. The next morning Aegon departed with Duncan, saying his father had told him to serve Duncan.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-29)

### On the road

Duncan and Aegon traveled to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), passing through the [Prince's Pass](wiki:Prince's_Pass) and eventually reaching [Vaith](wiki:Vaith), where Duncan accidentally insulted Lady [Cassella Vaith](wiki:Cassella_Vaith), the first highborn woman he had ever met. During the crossing of the desert, Duncan's horse [Chestnut](wiki:Chestnut) died,(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-13) and he and Aegon had to ride double on [Thunder](wiki:Thunder_(horse)).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-14) While in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC) and [210 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness) ran its course through Westeros, Duncan and Aegon were able to avoid encountering this plague as they were in Dorne, one of the two places where the plague had not come.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-15)

Duncan and Aegon took a poleboat down the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood) to [Planky Town](wiki:Planky_Town), where they took passage on the galleas *[White Lady](wiki:White_Lady)* to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-16)(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-13) Aboard the ship, Duncan had his first experience of combat at sea when he helped repel some raiders.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-14) In Oldtown, Aegon's brother [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) measured Duncan, and found he was an inch short of seven feet.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-17)

At some point, Duncan and Aegon were gifted a mule, [Maester](wiki:Maester_(mule)), by one of Aegon's brothers,(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-18) presumably Aemon.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Maester_21-0)

### Service at Standfast

Duncan and Aegon took service with Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey), a poor and broken [landed knight](wiki:Landed_knight), at [Standfast](wiki:Standfast) in the [Reach](wiki:Reach). Also employed was Ser [Bennis of the Brown Shield](wiki:Bennis_of_the_Brown_Shield), an old acquaintance of Duncan from his time with Arlan. Duncan and Bennis discovered that peasants of the widowed [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber), [Lady of Coldmoat](wiki:Lady_of_Coldmoat) had dammed the [Chequy Water](wiki:Chequy_Water). Bennis provoked Rohanne by cutting the cheek of one of her peasants. Duncan and Bennis began training a small levy of peasants for Eustace.

Since Eustace refused to travel to [Coldmoat](wiki:Coldmoat), Duncan went to the castle to try to reason with Lady Webber. Though sexual tension charged their meeting, Rohanne refused Duncan and told him about Eustace's support of [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) during the [Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion). Shocked by the revelation, Duncan attempted to leave Eustace's service, but a fire in the nearby woods caused him to suspect that Rohanne had already started her campaign against Eustace.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-19)

Duncan knew that the battle was a lost cause. He disbanded Eustace's meager levies, but promised to be by his side when he faced Rohanne. Meeting with her forces, Duncan cut his own cheek, claiming he was the one who attacked Rohanne's peasant. Lady Webber and Ser Eustance refused to back down, however, and they decided upon [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat). Duncan represented the elderly Eustace, and Rohanne's champion was her overbearing [castellan](wiki:Castellan), Ser [Lucas Inchfield](wiki:Lucas_Inchfield). Duncan's wrestling ability compensated for his lack of swordsmanship, and he managed to kill Lucas Longinch, though Duncan received major wounds himself and almost drowned in the stream in which they had been fighting.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-20)

After recuperating, Duncan discovered that Eustace and Rohanne had settled their differences and decided to marry. Duncan felt rejected, but was confronted by Rohanne shortly before he left. He took one kiss from Rohanne and cut off a length of her signature braid as a keepsake, after which he and Aegon took their leave.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-21)

### Wedding Tourney at Whitewalls

Duncan and Aegon traveled to the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), where they visited [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept). Though they had been on their way to take up service with Lord [Beron Stark](wiki:Beron_Stark) against the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) on the [northern](wiki:North) coast, they changed their destination after a chance meeting on the road. They encountered a group led by [Gormon Peake](wiki:Gormon_Peake), [Lord of Starpike](wiki:Lord_of_Starpike), Lord [Alyn Cockshaw](wiki:Alyn_Cockshaw), and a hedge knight named Ser [John the Fiddler](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre). The latter invited Duncan to attend the wedding of [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell), [Lord of Whitewalls](wiki:Lord_of_Whitewalls) to [the daughter](wiki:Lady_Frey_(wife_of_Ambrose_Butterwell)) of [Lord Frey](wiki:Lord_Frey), [Lord of the Crossing](wiki:Lord_of_the_Crossing), stating that the victor's prize at the celebratory [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) was to be a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg). Though the company traveled on without him, Duncan decided to go as well, and he befriended the fellow hedge knights Ser [Kyle the Cat](wiki:Kyle_the_Cat), Ser [Maynard Plumm](wiki:Maynard_Plumm), and Ser [Glendon Ball](wiki:Glendon_Ball) on his way to [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-15)

At Whitewalls, Aegon became suspicious about the intentions of the wedding, and he informed Duncan that the majority of the nobles present had fought for [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) during the [Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion). Duncan later had an encounter with John the Fiddler, who told him that Duncan had appeared to him in a dream in which Duncan had worn the all white armor of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). The Fiddler said his dreams always came true, stating that he had dreamed his brothers dead once, and though they had not believed him, they had died. The Fiddler also informed Duncan that he had dreamed about a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) hatching from an egg at Whitewalls.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-16)

Duncan entered the joust as the Gallows Knight, a [mystery knight](wiki:Mystery_knight) named for the hanged man sigil on his shield, which he had bought to replace the one destroyed by the Longinch but had not had time to have painted with his own arms. Duncan was defeated in his first tilt by Ser [Uthor Underleaf](wiki:Uthor_Underleaf), who confessed that someone had attempted to bribe him to kill Duncan in the final tilt. Whilst searching for Aegon, Duncan ran into Glendon, who had been far more successful than Duncan during the tourney, and invited him to come north after the tourney, which Glendon declined. Glendon told Duncan that Lord Peake had offered him a place at [Starpike](wiki:Starpike), on the condition that Glendon would let the Fiddler win. Glendon had refused, angering Gormon. Duncan next ran into the Fiddler, and began to realize John's true identity, as well as that the tourney had been rigged as to make the Fiddler victorious.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-17)

Lord Peake accused Glendon Ball of stealing the dragon egg, and the youth was imprisoned. When Duncan attempted to intervene, Alyn Cockshaw pulled him back, telling Duncan to follow him, if he wanted to find Aegon. Alyn attempted to kill Duncan out of jealousy for the attention the Fiddler had shown Duncan. Though injured, Duncan threw Alyn down a well to drown, an action witnessed by Maynard Plumm. Maynard helped Duncan see to his injuries and informed him of Aegon's location.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-18)

Aegon falsely told Lord Butterwell that he and Duncan were spies sent to investigate the tournament and that his father, Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), was on his way with an army. Frightened, Ambrose pled for his innocence. Ser [Tommard Heddle](wiki:Tommard_Heddle) attempted to seize Aegon in the sept, but was slain by Duncan in the combat that ensued. Duncan ordered Aegon to ride for [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), and he confronted the Fiddler, calling him by his true name, [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre). Duncan accused Lord Peake of bribing the contestants in Daemon's favor, and of falsely charging Glendon with the theft of the dragon egg. Furious about the accusations, Daemon challenged Glendon with a joust as a means of [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-19)

Glendon defeated Daemon Blackfyre easily early the next morning, and moments later a loyalist army led by Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), arrived at Whitewalls. Daemon was arrested, and those who had supported him executed or punished, causing the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion) to be suppressed before it had even begun. Lord Brynden allowed Duncan and Aegon to continue their travels, and hinted as to how the dragon's egg had been stolen, leading Duncan to realize the involvement of the [dwarfs](wiki:Dwarf) who had performed at the wedding.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-22)

### Later life

An unknown amount of time after Whitewalls, Duncan and Aegon visited [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-22)

Presumably in honor of Ser Duncan, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) named his firstborn son and heir [Duncan](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen) (however, when asked about it, [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin) refused to answer).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rssm1105.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Prince Duncan was called "Duncan the Small".(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

Both he and Aegon had some involvement during the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising), where Aegon's father was killed.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-25)

In late [233 AC](wiki:233_AC), after Aegon had ascended the throne as [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), Duncan escorted the king's brother, Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), to [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea), where Aemon was going to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

Duncan eventually joined the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). While it is unknown when exactly he took his vows, the first known mention of him as a Kingsguard knight is in [236 AC](wiki:236_AC).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

In [236 AC](wiki:236_AC), Ser Duncan fought in the [Fourth Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Fourth_Blackfyre_Rebellion) and personally slew [Daemon III Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_III_Blackfyre), the [Blackfyre Pretender](wiki:House_Blackfyre).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

In [239 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), when Prince [Duncan Targaryen](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen) broke his betrothal to Lord [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon)'s daughter and married a commonborn girl known as [Jenny of Oldstones](wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones) instead, the enraged Lyonel renounced his fealty to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Duncan served as King Aegon V's champion in the [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat), in which he bested Lyonel, thereby ending his short rebellion.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf784_27-0)

In [252 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) or [253 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) Duncan, by then [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), participated in a [winter tourney at King's Landing](wiki:Winter_tourney_at_King's_Landing), where he was defeated by a sixteen-year-old [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)

In [259 AC](wiki:259_AC), Duncan apparently perished in the [tragedy of Summerhall](wiki:Tragedy_of_Summerhall), along with King Aegon V and Prince Duncan, among others.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rawoiafduncan.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Little is known about the events of the tragedy, but it seems that the Lord Commander's valor saved some of the survivors from dying in the great fire that consumed [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

## Appearances & Description

Duncan was an inch shy of seven feet tall.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) He had thick, shaggy hair which sun-streaked in the [summer](wiki:Summer_(season)). After the events at [Standfast](wiki:Standfast) in [211 AC](wiki:211_AC), he had a dagger scar on his cheek.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) Dunk was strong and quick, and was taught jousting and swordplay.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) He was better with a sword than with a lance,(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) and even better with an axe or mace.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

Duncan had a humble personality, and frequently thought of himself as thick-headed, recalling how Ser [Arlan](wiki:Arlan_of_Pennytree) had often said, "Dunk the lunk, thick as a castle wall", and referred to him as "slow as an [aurochs](wiki:Aurochs)".(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

Having grown up in the slums of Flea Bottom, Duncan spoke with a recognizable King's Landing [accent](wiki:Common_Tongue).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

While in the Round Room of [White Sword Tower](wiki:White_Sword_Tower), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) admires previous men who served as [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), including Duncan. He also reads Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy)'s entry in the [White Book](wiki:White_Book), which states Barristan defeated Duncan during the [winter tourney at King's Landing](wiki:Winter_tourney_at_King's_Landing) in which Barristan had participated as a [mystery knight](wiki:Mystery_knight).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2)

### A Feast for Crows

For unknown reasons, a shield with Dunk's original arms came to reside in the armory of Lord [Selwyn Tarth](wiki:Selwyn_Tarth) at [Evenfall Hall](wiki:Evenfall_Hall). While at [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), Selwyn's daughter, [Brienne](wiki:Brienne_Tarth), remembering the arms, has them painted on her shield so she can travel the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) and [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) incognito.(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) relates that when he joined the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), his brother [Egg](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) insisted that Ser Duncan accompany him to [Eastwatch](wiki:Eastwatch).(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) Aemon also dreams of his youth in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), a time when he was drinking at the [Quill and Tankard](wiki:Quill_and_Tankard) with Egg and "that big knight he served".(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

While in the [cave of the three-eyed crow](wiki:Cave_of_the_three-eyed_crow), [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) has visions of the past looking through the [heart tree](wiki:Heart_tree) in the [godswood of Winterfell](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell). Among the visions is a slender brown-haired girl kissing a knight as tall as [Hodor](wiki:Hodor),(wiki:Duncan_the_Tall.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) possibly Duncan.
