## Appearances & Description

Val's appearance beside Stannis at the Mance-burning (Jon POV, ADWD Jon III): "Val stood beside him, tall and fair. They had crowned her with a simple circlet of dark bronze, yet she looked more regal in bronze than Stannis did in gold. Her eyes were grey and fearless, unflinching. Beneath an ermine cloak, she wore white and gold. Her honey-blond hair had been done up in a thick braid that hung over her right shoulder to her waist. The chill in the air had put color in her cheeks." (`sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-jon-03.md:35`)

Val is a beautiful young woman with blonde hair,(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) the color of dark honey.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) It reaches to her waist,(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) and she sometimes wears it in a braid across one shoulder.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Val has high sharp cheekbones(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) and eyes which appear pale grey(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) or blue.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) She is slender, with a full bosom.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

Val sometimes wears all white: white woolen breeches tucked into high boots of bleached white leather, white [bearskin](wiki:Bear) cloak pinned at the shoulder with a carved [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) face, white tunic with [bone](wiki:Bone) fastenings.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) She alternatively wears white and [gold](wiki:Gold) beneath an ermine cloak,(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) or a large bearskin cloak.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) She wields a long bone [knife](wiki:Knife).(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

The graceful(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Val is resourceful, brave and capable.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) She is fierce(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) and a capable rider.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

Val is in a relationship with [Jarl](wiki:Jarl), a wildling raider; [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) calls him her "latest pet". Val is present in the tent when [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) is presented to Mance, the [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall).(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Jarl falls to his death while attempting to cross the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Jon uses a [Myrish](wiki:Myr) [far-eye](wiki:Far-eye) during the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall), and he spots Val milking a she-goat near Mance's tent.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) At the same time that the wildling host is attacked by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s men, Dalla goes into labor. Jon has Val act as a midwife since once cannot be found in the chaos, and she protects the tent during the battle.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) Val helps deliver [Mance's son](wiki:Aemon_Steelsong), though Dalla dies in childbirth.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Jon protects Val and the baby against looters when the wildlings are defeated, and he keeps them in the tent until [rangers](wiki:Rangers) arrive to take them prisoner.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Stannis and others assert that Jon had captured Val during the battle, but Jon disagrees, saying she did not need much capturing.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Stannis hopes to gain the support of the [north](wiki:North) and the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk), and he offers to legitimize Jon as Jon Stark, [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell), and wed him to Val. Jon refuses the offer,(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) however, and he is then elected [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black).(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Val watches the black brothers of the Night's Watch from her chamber in the [King's Tower](wiki:King's_Tower). [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) is embarrassed the few times he speaks with her.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Many men from the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) and Stannis's army are smitten with Val's beauty. The men of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) consider her a princess, but to the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) she is merely [Dalla](wiki:Dalla)'s sister.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Ser [Justin Massey](wiki:Justin_Massey) hopes to wed Val and claim [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Confined to her chambers, Val nearly kills a [man-at-arms](wiki:Man-at-arms) with his own dagger while trying to escape, stabbing him in the neck.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

Val unsuccessfully begs Stannis to spare the captive Mance, stating she is willing to marry a [kneeler](wiki:Kneeler).(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) During the burning of "Mance"—actually [Rattleshirt](wiki:Rattleshirt)(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)—a regal-looking Val wears a simple circlet of dark [bronze](wiki:Bronze). She does not join in the prayers to [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor).(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

Jon sends Val with a [half-blind garron](wiki:Half-blind_garron) on a mission to bring peace terms to [Tormund Giantsbane](wiki:Tormund). Before leaving, she calls [Gilly](wiki:Gilly)'s child a [little monster](wiki:Little_monster), and Jon verifies that the Wall was responsible for Jarl's death.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Some men of the Night's Watch disagree with the Lord Commander's decision to release Val, and Jon angers Ser [Axell Florent](wiki:Axell_Florent) by refusing his request to see the so-called princess.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

When Val returns with Tormund, Jon is impressed, as many seasoned [rangers](wiki:Rangers) had failed in finding his wildlings. Jon has her relocated from the King's Tower to the top floor of [Hardin's Tower](wiki:Hardin's_Tower), as the former has been claimed by Queen [Selyse Florent](wiki:Selyse_Florent) and the latter is protected by the giant [Wun Wun](wiki:Wun_Wun).(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

Val kneels when she is presented to Selyse and her daughter, Princess [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon). Val is wary of Shireen because of her [greyscale](wiki:Greyscale), and she mentions to Jon that the child is not clean and should be killed. This horrifies Jon, who is grateful that neither Selyse nor her [queen's men](wiki:Queen's_men) heard Val when she said it.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)

[A letter](wiki:Bastard_Letter) sent by [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) to Jon includes the wildling princess among those whose presence is demanded by the new Lord of Winterfell. Selyse decides to have Val married to Ser [Patrek of King's Mountain](wiki:Patrek_of_King's_Mountain), but the knight is killed near Hardin's Tower by Wun Wun.(wiki:Val.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
