## Identity

Tytos Lannister's second mistress was a lowborn woman, the daughter of a candlemaker. Her name is unknown (wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress).

## Origins

## Narrative Arc

The candlemaker's daughter replaced Tytos's first mistress. Tytos began seating her beside him in the hall, showering her with gifts and honors, and even asking her views on matters of rule. She helped herself to the jewels and clothes of Tytos's deceased wife Jeyne, ordered about household knights, dismissed servants, and sat in attendance when Tytos was absent (wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-TWOIAF).

In 267 AC, Lord Tytos died of a heart attack while climbing stairs to see her. Lord Tywin Lannister had her stripped naked and paraded through the streets of Lannisport for a fortnight, forcing her to confess to every man she met that she was a thief and a harlot. She was then sent into exile from the docks (wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-TWOIAF).

Cersei later recalls how Tywin dealt with the mistress when the body of Shae is found in Tywin's bed (wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-Raffc3). The event parallels Tywin's later treatment of Tysha, who was similarly paraded (wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-Rasos66).
