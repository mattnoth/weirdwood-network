## Origins

Lady [Jeyne Marbrand](wiki:Jeyne_Marbrand), the wife of [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister), Lord of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), died not long after giving birth to [Gerion](wiki:Gerion_Lannister) in [255 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Gerion's [wet nurse](wiki:Wet_nurse) became Tytos's [first mistress](wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_first_mistress). However, the wet nurse was eventually replaced with the comely daughter of a candlemaker.

Tytos began seating his second mistress beside him in the hall, and showering her with gifts and honors, even asking her views on matters of rule in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands). The mistress helped herself to the jewels and clothes of Tytos's deceased wife, Jeyne. She also began ordering about household [knights](wiki:Knight), dismissed servants, and sat in attendance when Tytos was absent.(wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

In [267 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Lord Tytos died of a heart attack while climbing stairs to see his mistress.(wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Abandoned by her allies at court, the mistress lost all of her gifts from Tytos. Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) had her stripped naked and paraded her through the streets of [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) for a fortnight, where she was forced to confess to every man she met that she was a thief and a harlot.(wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) At the end of the two weeks, she was sent into exile from the docks.(wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

[Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) explains to his nephew [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) how the mistress took advantage of his weak father Tytos.(wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Upon the discovery of the dead body of [Shae](wiki:Shae) in the late [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s bed, his daughter [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) is unbelieving that he could have slept with her, recalling how her father had treated the mistress, saying that was how he dealt with [whores](wiki:Whore), and that Shae must have been there for some other purpose.(wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

### A Dance with Dragons

Just prior to making her own [walk of atonement](wiki:Walk_of_atonement), [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) recalls hearing stories from the mouths of washerwomen and guardsmen who had witnessed the walk of her grandfather [Tytos's](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) mistress. They spoke of how the woman wept and begged, of how she desperately clung to all her garments when she was commanded to disrobe, her futile efforts to cover her breasts and her sex with her hands as she hobbled barefoot and naked through the streets to exile. Cersei thinks her uncle, Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), and the [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow) are mistaken if they believe it will be the same with her.(wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Recalling his father's mistress, Kevan tells himself he has no reason to feel guilty about Cersei, as he acted for the good of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
