## Identity

Lyanna Stark was the only daughter of Lord Rickard Stark and Lady Lyarra Stark (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). She had two older brothers, Brandon and Eddard, and one younger brother, Benjen. Her abduction by Prince Rhaegar Targaryen triggered Robert's Rebellion (wiki:Lyanna_Stark).

## Origins

Born 266–267 AC at Winterfell, the North (track_b: Born). Died 283 AC at the Tower of Joy, Dorne, aged 16 (track_b: Died). Buried in the Crypt of Winterfell (track_b: Buried). Father: Rickard Stark (track_b: Father). Mother: Lyarra Stark (track_b: Mother).

## Appearances & Description

Lyanna was slim of frame. Arya Stark, with her brown hair, long face, and grey eyes, resembles Lyanna in appearance according to Eddard (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). Maester Yandel describes her as "a wild and boyish young thing," while Ser Kevan Lannister recalls a "wild beauty" about her (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). Eddard considered her "a child-woman of surpassing loveliness" (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). She had "the wolf blood" according to her father Rickard (wiki:Lyanna_Stark).

## Narrative Arc

Lyanna was betrothed to Robert Baratheon, though she was skeptical of his faithfulness, telling Eddard "Love is sweet, dearest Ned, but it cannot change a man's nature" (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). At the Tourney at Harrenhal, she wept at Rhaegar Targaryen's singing (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). A popular theory identifies her as the Knight of the Laughing Tree at that tourney (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). Prince Rhaegar crowned her the Queen of Love and Beauty, passing over his wife Elia Martell, and later abducted her, triggering the crisis that led to Robert's Rebellion (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). Eddard found Lyanna dying at the Tower of Joy in Dorne, guarded by three Kingsguard. In her final moments she made Eddard promise something — "Promise me, Ned" — a phrase that haunts his memories (wiki:Lyanna_Stark). Robert, who loved Lyanna deeply, never recovered from her death and remained fixated on her throughout his reign (wiki:Lyanna_Stark).
