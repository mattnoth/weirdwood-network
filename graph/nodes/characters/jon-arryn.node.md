## Identity

Jon Arryn was Lord of the Eyrie, Defender of the Vale, Warden of the East, and head of House Arryn. He served as Hand of the King to Robert I Baratheon from 283 AC until his death in 298 AC (wiki:Jon_Arryn). He was the eldest son of Lord Jasper Arryn (track_b: Father).

## Origins

Born approximately 218–220 AC at the Eyrie in the Vale of Arryn (track_b: Born). Son of Lord Jasper Arryn (track_b: Father). He had a younger brother, Ser Ronnel, and a sister, Alys, who married Ser Elys Waynwood (wiki:Jon_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41).

Raised his banners in Robert's Rebellion after refusing King Aerys II's command to execute his wards Eddard Stark and Robert Baratheon (wiki:Jon_Arryn).

## Appearances & Description

Broad-shouldered, with pale blue eyes, sandy blond hair, and an aquiline nose in youth (resembling Harrold Hardyng) (wiki:Jon_Arryn). By the time he married Lysa Tully, half his teeth had fallen out and his breath was poor (wiki:Jon_Arryn). Reputed as prudent, calming, wise, and kindly (wiki:Jon_Arryn). Carried a double-edged longsword with wings on the crossguard and a falcon-head pommel, commissioned by Lysa (wiki:Jon_Arryn). Preferred functional armor over ornamental plate (wiki:Jon_Arryn).

## Narrative Arc

Jon fostered Eddard Stark and Robert Baratheon at the Eyrie, becoming a second father to both. His first wife Jeyne Royce died in childbed; his second wife Rowena Arryn died childless of a winter chill (wiki:Jon_Arryn). When Aerys II murdered Jon's heir Elbert and demanded Jon execute his wards, Jon raised the Vale in revolt, sparking Robert's Rebellion (wiki:Jon_Arryn). After the war, Robert named Jon Hand of the King. Jon brokered peace with Dorne, negotiated Robert's marriage to Cersei Lannister, and bore much of the realm's governance while Robert indulged in excess (wiki:Jon_Arryn). Jon married Lysa Tully as his third wife; after multiple miscarriages, she bore him a sickly son, Robert Arryn (wiki:Jon_Arryn). Jon discovered that Cersei's children were fathered by Jaime Lannister, not King Robert, by investigating Robert's bastards and studying Malleon's lineage book. His dying words were "the seed is strong" (wiki:Jon_Arryn). He was poisoned with tears of Lys by Lysa at Petyr Baelish's instigation — though initially blamed on the Lannisters (wiki:Jon_Arryn).
