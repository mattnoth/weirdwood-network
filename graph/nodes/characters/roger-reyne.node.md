## Origins

### Peake Uprising

Roger became Lord of [Castamere](wiki:Castamere) after the death of his father [Robert](wiki:Robert_Reyne) during the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising) in [233 AC](wiki:233_AC).(wiki:Roger_Reyne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) According to a semi-canon source, Roger executed seven captive [Peakes](wiki:House_Peake) at [Starpike](wiki:Storming_of_Starpike) before being halted by Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen).(wiki:Roger_Reyne.cite_ref-sample_3-0)

### Reynes and Tarbecks

Roger and his younger brother, the cunning [Reynard](wiki:Reynard_Reyne), were often at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) once their sister [Ellyn](wiki:Ellyn_Reyne) married [Tion Lannister](wiki:Tion_Lannister), the son of Lord [Gerold Lannister](wiki:Gerold_Lannister) and heir to the Rock. The brothers returned to [Castamere](wiki:Castamere) after Tion's death, however, as Gerold began to groom [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) for leadership. Ellyn was later rewed to [Walderan Tarbeck](wiki:Walderan_Tarbeck), Lord of [Tarbeck Hall](wiki:Tarbeck_Hall).(wiki:Roger_Reyne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

Ca. [255 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [House Tarbeck](wiki:House_Tarbeck) angered King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) through their territorial demands, according to a semi-canon source.(wiki:Roger_Reyne.cite_ref-sample_3-1) Lord Tytos sent Lord [Denys Marbrand](wiki:Denys_Marbrand) to capture the Tarbecks, but Roger came to his sister's aid and his soldiers slew the [Marbrand](wiki:House_Marbrand) men in the night before they reached Tarbeck Hall. Reynard convinced Tytos that the attack was a misunderstanding, claiming that Roger was unaware of Denys's identity and thought he was attacking [outlaws](wiki:Outlaw). After Reynard offered apologies and a [blood price](wiki:Blood_price) to [House Marbrand](wiki:House_Marbrand), Tytos pardoned Roger Reyne, and Lord and Lady Tarbeck as well.(wiki:Roger_Reyne.cite_ref-sample_3-2)

Roger and his sister were present at the feast in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) where the betrothal of [Genna Lannister](wiki:Genna_Lannister) and [Emmon Frey](wiki:Emmon_Frey) was announced. While Ellyn laughed, the Red Lion left the hall in anger.(wiki:Roger_Reyne.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Roger capably led [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister)'s army in the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings) after the death of [Jason Lannister](wiki:Jason_Lannister_(son_of_Gerold)). Roger [knighted](wiki:Knight) his squire, Tytos's second son [Kevan](wiki:Kevan_Lannister).(wiki:Roger_Reyne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

### Rebellion

In [261 AC](wiki:261_AC) after the war, Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the eldest son and heir of Lord Tytos, desired to reaffirm [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister)'s control over the west. The Reynes and Tarbecks [rose in rebellion](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_rebellion) against their liege lord, Tytos. Roger's host arrived too late to prevent Tywin from exterminating the Tarbecks at Tarbeck Hall,(wiki:Roger_Reyne.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) and the outnumbered Reynes failed to defeat Tywin in a surprise charge. Roger became feverish from a crossbow bolt and Reynard led the surviving Reynes back to [Castamere](wiki:Castamere), where they took refuge in its mines.(wiki:Roger_Reyne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

According to a semi-canon source, Tywin declined Reynard's terms and Roger's offer of single combat.(wiki:Roger_Reyne.cite_ref-sample_3-3) Instead, the Lannisters diverted a nearby stream into Castamere, drowning all within.(wiki:Roger_Reyne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) A song known as "[The Rains of Castamere](wiki:The_Rains_of_Castamere)" was composed as a commemoration of the event.
