## Appearances & Description

Meribald is six feet tall and walks with a slight hunch. He has large, leathery hands and large, bare, weathered feet. His face is lined and windburnt with thick grey hair.(wiki:Meribald.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
Meribald's usual path takes him around most of the countryside, circling the region from [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) to Maidenpool, and is said to take no less than half a year per circuit. Because the [septon](wiki:Septon) usually ministers to the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) in market towns, small villages, and [holdfasts](wiki:Holdfast), he is not as familiar with the [Trident](wiki:Trident) and its castles as he is with the smallroads, tracks, hedges, and unmapped rills and caves.(wiki:Meribald.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
Meribald travels alone in most of his walks with only his dog, [Dog](wiki:Dog_(Meribald)), as his companion. Normally, he also takes a [donkey](wiki:Donkey) to carry his heavy load, including packs of food and goods he means to give to the hungry and poor of the villages he passes by.(wiki:Meribald.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Meribald is fond of [oranges](wiki:Orange).(wiki:Meribald.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

## Origins

Meribald was born and has lived his entire life in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands). As a boy, Meribald went to fight in the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings) with his brothers. His older brother, [Willam](wiki:Willam), agreed to jokingly take Meribald as his squire. During the war, however, both Willam and another brother, [Robin](wiki:Robin), died from a fever. His last brother, [Owen](wiki:Owen_(brother_of_Meribald)), was killed in battle and Owen's friend, [Jon Pox](wiki:Jon_Pox), was hanged for rape. Meribald may have become a [broken man](wiki:Broken_men) because of this. When he returned to the riverlands, he joined the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), but used his position as a world-savvy septon to convince young women to have sex with him. He now walks barefoot as penance for these sins he committed as a youth.(wiki:Meribald.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)
He cannot read or write, but he knows [The Seven-Pointed Star](wiki:The_Seven-Pointed_Star) by memory which seemed to make him capable enough to minister the Faith to [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk). He has been walking the countryside of the riverlands as a wandering septon in exchange for food and shelter for nearly forty years.(wiki:Meribald.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Septon Meribald guides [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth), [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne), and Ser [Hyle Hunt](wiki:Hyle_Hunt) through the riverlands to [Saltpans](wiki:Saltpans), where he took his first stop on his circuit. Along the way, he spooks Podrick with stories of mud along the [Trident](wiki:Trident)'s mudflats that can swallow up ignorant travelers and tales of a large, monstrous, man-eating, demon-like [she-wolf](wiki:Nymeria_(direwolf)) that leads a pack of savage wolves prowling throughout the Trident. Meribald also tells his own story, explaining how he came to be a septon and his duties to the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) of the riverlands.(wiki:Meribald.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)
Meribald guides Brienne and her companions to the [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle) to meet the [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)).(wiki:Meribald.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) The [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) allows Meribald to continue his journey(wiki:Meribald.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) after Brienne's battle with [Biter](wiki:Biter) at the [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads).(wiki:Meribald.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
