## Appearances & Description

Jhogo is young and very thin. He has a faint shadow of a mustache(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Ragot64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) and a raspy voice.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Jhogo's eyes are dark and almond-shaped.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

A brave warrior and a skilled scout,(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Jhogo is fearless and quick to laugh.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Ragot64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Despite his bravery, he is afraid of the sea like most Dothraki, yet is determined not to show fear in front of his compatriots.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Jhogo carries a whip,(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) an *[arakh](wiki:Arakh)*,(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) and a dagger.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Jhogo is one of four [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) warriors assigned to the *[khas](wiki:Khas)* of [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) within the *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* of her husband *Khal* [Drogo](wiki:Drogo). When [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) attacks his sister in the [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea), Jhogo uses his whip to separate them.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

Jhogo accompanies Daenerys during her visit to the Western Market in [Vaes Dothrak](wiki:Vaes_Dothrak). He helps stop an [assassination](wiki:Assassin) attempt on Daenerys's life by a [wineseller](wiki:Wineseller) by ensnaring the man with his whip. Jhogo is given a tall, grey [stallion](wiki:Horse) by Drogo as a reward.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Daenerys orders Jhogo, [Quaro](wiki:Quaro), and Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) to intervene with the rape of [Eroeh](wiki:Eroeh), a [Lhazareen](wiki:Lhazareen) girl. Though all three are initially hesitant, they comply with her orders, and Jhogo beheads one of the men assaulting the girl with his *[arakh](wiki:Arakh)*.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

When Daenerys intends to have Drogo healed using [blood magic](wiki:Blood_magic) by [Mirri Maz Duur](wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur), his [bloodriders](wiki:Bloodrider) attempt to stop it. In the ensuing fight, Jhogo and [Rakharo](wiki:Rakharo) team up to kill [Haggo](wiki:Haggo).(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Ragot64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

After Drogo's death, Daenerys names Jhogo *[ko](wiki:Ko)*, and asks him to become one of her bloodriders, alongside [Aggo](wiki:Aggo) and [Rakharo](wiki:Rakharo). She offers him the silver-handled whip she received as a present during [her wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Drogo_and_Daenerys_Targaryen). Jhogo takes it, but is confused, saying it would be shameful for him to be a bloodrider to a woman.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Jhogo is the first of Daenerys's party to spot the portentous [red comet](wiki:Red_comet) in the sky, and after Daenerys hatches three [dragons](wiki:Dragon) in Drogo's pyre and emerges unharmed from the flames, he is the first of her three bloodriders to accept his role. He lays his *arakh* at her feet, and declares Daenerys "blood of my blood", prostrating himself before her. Aggo and Rakharo follow him.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

### A Clash of Kings

Jhogo serves in the [Queensguard](wiki:Queensguard) of Daenerys.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) During their journey across the [red waste](wiki:Red_waste), Jhogo advises Daenerys to either tie the dying [Doreah](wiki:Doreah) to a horse saddle or leave her.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

While the *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* rests in [Vaes Tolorro](wiki:Vaes_Tolorro) after crossing the red waste, Jhogo finds a well with pure and cold water in the ruined city. Daenerys orders her three bloodriders to scout out the country, each one in a different direction. Jhogo leaves to the south-east and follows the *[shierak qiya](wiki:Red_comet)*. He is the last to return, having found the city of [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), and is accompanied by three representatives of the city, [Pyat Pree](wiki:Pyat_Pree), [Quaithe](wiki:Quaithe), and [Xaro Xhoan Daxos](wiki:Xaro_Xhoan_Daxos).(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

Jhogo leads the procession during Daenerys's visit to the [Hall of a Thousand Thrones](wiki:Hall_of_a_Thousand_Thrones) to petition the [Pureborn](wiki:Pureborn) of Qarth, using his whip to set the crowd aside. He brushes Quaithe away from touching Daenerys when the [shadowbinder](wiki:Shadowbinder) warns Daenerys to leave the city.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

Jhogo accompanies Daenerys to the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying), where he warns her that it is a evil place, a haunt of ghosts and [maegi](wiki:Maegi). He helps disarm [Pyat Pree](wiki:Pyat_Pree) with his whip when the [warlock](wiki:Warlock) tries to murder Daenerys.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

Afterwards, Jhogo, along with [Aggo](wiki:Aggo), rides with Daenerys to the [port of Qarth](wiki:Port_of_Qarth) to seek a [ship](wiki:Ship), where he buys a handful of fat white cherries to eat. When another assassination attempt is made on Daenerys, Jhogo rushes to her aid. He seizes [Arstan Whitebeard](wiki:Arstan_Whitebeard) and puts a dagger to his throat, before releasing him on Daenerys's order.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### A Storm of Swords

Jhogo guards Daenerys when she visits the [Plaza of Pride](wiki:Plaza_of_Pride) in [Astapor](wiki:Astapor) to discuss the purchase of [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied) [slaves](wiki:Slave). He leads the procession back to her ships, and almost pulls his silver-handled whip to move back the crowd, but is stopped by Daenerys before he escalates the situation.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) As one of Daenerys's three bloodriders, Jhogo is often asked to keep her company, to prevent Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) from approaching her when alone.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

Jhogo protects the rear of the litter alongside Rakharo during the [fall of Astapor](wiki:Fall_of_Astapor). He kills several [Good Masters](wiki:Good_Masters) and Astapori slavers on the day of the battle, taking down one slaver soldier with his silver-handled whip.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Rasos27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

On the march to [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai), Jhogo and his fellow bloodriders are also made *kos* by Daenerys and are put in charge of commanding the thirty-odd mounted *khalasar* remaining to her, leaving their guarding duties to [Arstan Whitebeard](wiki:Arstan_Whitebeard) and [Strong Belwas](wiki:Strong_Belwas). Jhogo dismisses the threat of the Yunkish scouts and later participates in the [battle near Yunkai](wiki:Battle_near_Yunkai), successfully leading the Dothraki mounted warriors in wedge formation against the enemy center.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

During the [siege of Meereen](wiki:Siege_of_Meereen), Jhogo almost comes to blows with [Aggo](wiki:Aggo) and [Rakharo](wiki:Rakharo) over who will fight [Oznak zo Pahl](wiki:Oznak_zo_Pahl), the hero of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen). Daenerys ultimately stops her bloodriders from fighting, deeming them brave but too young and valuable to lose. Belwas instead slays Oznak.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) After the conquest of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), Jhogo styles his hair in an oiled braid with [silver](wiki:Silver) bells and wears the [gold](wiki:Gold) and jewels of dead men.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Jhogo and the other bloodriders continue to serve their *[khaleesi](wiki:Khaleesi)*.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Jhogo, [Aggo](wiki:Aggo) and [Rakharo](wiki:Rakharo) are commanded by Daenerys to use her *khalasar* to subdue the hinterlands of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) and end the slavery there.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), [Lord Commander of the Queensguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Queensguard), reports the bloodriders successfully harried the slavers from the hills and struck the shackles off the slaves, and are planting and bringing crops to Meereen to market.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

Daenerys recalls Jhogo and her other bloodriders back to Meereen. They arrive in time to assist her in feeding the thousands of survivors from the [siege of Astapor](wiki:Siege_of_Astapor), where Jhogo tries to stop Daenerys from letting the sick refugees touch her. He and Rakharo are shamed into aiding the sufferers of the [bloody flux](wiki:Bloody_flux) by helping them bathe in nearby [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Radwd36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Queen Daenerys disappears from [Daznak's Pit](wiki:Daznak's_Pit) on the back of [Drogon](wiki:Drogon).(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Radwd50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) When peace is signed between [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) and Meereen, Jhogo—along with [Groleo](wiki:Groleo), [Daario Naharis](wiki:Daario_Naharis), [Hero](wiki:Hero) and three kin to the new King of Meereen, [Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq)—are given over as [hostages](wiki:Hostage) to the Yunkish camp, to vouch for the safety of the seven Yunkai commanders who are allowed to enter the city of Meereen.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) When the decapitated head of [Groleo](wiki:Groleo) is presented to Hizdahr, [Reznak mo Reznak](wiki:Reznak_mo_Reznak) demands the remaining hostages to be returned, though only three of Hizdahr's kin are released.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Radwd59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

At the council called by Barristan, the elderly *[jaqqa rhan](wiki:Jaqqa_rhan)* [Rommo](wiki:Rommo) agrees Jhogo must be freed, as he is Daenerys's "blood of her blood" and the honor of the *khalasar* demands it. Jhogo remains a hostage of the Yunkish.(wiki:Jhogo.cite_ref-Radwd70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)
