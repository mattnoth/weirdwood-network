## Origins

Thoros was born in the Free City of [Myr](wiki:Myr), the youngest of eight children, and he was given to a [red temple](wiki:Red_temple) of R'hllor at a young age. He earned his priesthood but was never overly pious due to his tastes for fighting, drinking, and women. Thoros was sent to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in hopes he could convert the fire-obsessed King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), but he was unsuccessful and started to question his own faith.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

During [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Thoros was present(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) when the corpses of [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) and [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)), clothed in [red cloaks](wiki:House_Lannister_guards) to hide their wounds, were presented by Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) to Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) before the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Thoros has never forgotten Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane)'s brutality during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

Thoros became a frequent drinking companion of the new king, Robert I.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) During [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), Thoros earned recognition for being the first over the wall at [Pyke](wiki:Siege_of_Pyke).(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

Thoros is a frequent [tourney](wiki:Tourney) mêlée champion, using cheap swords set aflame with [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) to spook the other combatants' horses.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Three times Thoros defeated [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) in the mêlées.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) At the [tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_Prince_Joffrey's_name_day), Thoros was defeated by Lord [Yohn Royce](wiki:Yohn_Royce) with a common mace after the fire of his sword guttered out.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) [Tobho Mott](wiki:Tobho_Mott), displeased with the burning of swords, overcharges Thoros for replacements,(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) but Robert is glad to provide for his friend.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

## Appearances & Description

Thoros is a tall, fat man in flapping red robes.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) He shaves his head(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and has a smooth face.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

The genial Thoros enjoys [wine](wiki:Wine),(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) joking that he became a [red priest](wiki:Red_priest) because the robes would hide winestains.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) He is fond of feasts and [tourneys](wiki:Tourney),(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) has a gift for tongues,(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) and has some success seeing visions in flames.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

Thoros has a reputation as a brave madman with an absurd but dangerous firesword.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) The red priest is a great friend of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

[Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole) giggle at Thoros in the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney), where he unhorses Lord [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion).(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Thoros wins the melee, which lasts three hours.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), tasks Thoros with recruiting twenty men to join the party of Beric, which is to seek out Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) and bring him to the king's justice.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

When [war breaks out](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Beric and Thoros raid the foraging parties of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Both are named among those who must swear fealty to the newly-crowned King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) or be adjudged traitors.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### A Clash of Kings

While Beric's band forages in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), Thoros pays [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) with paper notes.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Racok26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### A Storm of Swords

[Harwin](wiki:Harwin) tells [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) of the [battle at the Mummer's Ford](wiki:Battle_at_the_Mummer's_Ford), when Gregor ambushed Beric's party. Thoros rallied Beric's horsemen and allowed Beric to escape. Thoros removed Gregor's lance from Beric and tried to treat the wound,(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) but the Lord of [Blackhaven](wiki:Blackhaven) succumbed. While performing the [last kiss](wiki:Last_kiss), a traditional burial ritual of his faith, Thoros inadvertently resurrected Beric. The two men founded an [outlaw](wiki:Outlaw) group, the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners), to protect the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) from soldiers foraging in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).

The spiritually-reawakened Thoros continues to resurrect Beric through means beyond his understanding,(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) and he has greater success seeing visions in flames.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7) The red priest leads his fellow outlaws in prayers to [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor), the Lord of Light.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

After some time spent in the brotherhood, Thoros becomes loose-skinned and grey-haired, wearing faded robes and piecemeal armor.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) He drives a flock of sheep to [Acorn Hall](wiki:Acorn_Hall) to aid Lady [Ravella Smallwood](wiki:Ravella_Smallwood).(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) The [Peach](wiki:Peach) offers its services to Thoros and his companions without charge.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Thoros revives Beric after the lightning lord is slain by [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) in [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) within the [hollow hill](wiki:Hollow_hill).(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) The red priest fights with a flaming sword during the [battle at the burning septry](wiki:Battle_at_the_burning_septry),(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) and he accompanies Beric to meet the [ghost of High Heart](wiki:Ghost_of_High_Heart).(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8) Thoros has a vision of the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun).(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)

### A Feast for Crows

After fighting [Rorge](wiki:Rorge) and [Biter](wiki:Biter) at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn), [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) is captured by the brotherhood. Thoros explains that when the brotherhood found the body of Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) after the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), he refused to give her the last kiss and revive her. Beric passed the flame of life to Catelyn's corpse instead, and the so-called Lady Stoneheart now leads the brotherhood after Beric's passing. Her motives and methods lower Thoros's spirits.(wiki:Thoros.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
