## Origins

Robert has been sickly all his days.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) calls him cruel names and once slapped Robert with a wooden sword.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) The boy was brought to the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) at the age of six.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

## Appearances & Description

Robert is six years old but small and painfully thin for his age. He is pale with brown hair(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) and big eyes(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) which are always red and runny.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Robert has spindly arms and legs, a soft concave chest, a little belly,(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) and splotchy skin.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Young Robert is spoiled and whiny, prone to crying when his dolls are taken away(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) or damaged.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) He enjoys puppetry shows,(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) as well as songs and stories about animals(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and the [Winged Knight](wiki:Winged_Knight).(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Robert likes to play [come-into-my-castle](wiki:Come-into-my-castle), [hopfrog](wiki:Hopfrog), and [spin-the-sword](wiki:Spin-the-sword), although the court of the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) allows him to win.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) He enjoys being told how strong he is, but does not know how to ride.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

Robert is afflicted with a [disease](wiki:Shaking_sickness) that causes frequent seizures. Maester [Colemon](wiki:Colemon) bleeds him with [leeches](wiki:Leech)(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and administers [dreamwine](wiki:Dreamwine) and [milk of the poppy](wiki:Milk_of_the_poppy) to help Robert sleep.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) [Sweetmilk](wiki:Sweetmilk) helps Robert with shaking, but Colemon is cautious because continuous use of [sweetsleep](wiki:Sweetsleep) has an increasing level of toxicity.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) He is intellectually and physically stunted, and is not weaned from his mother's breast.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Because Robert is delicate, [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) keeps a [whipping boy](wiki:Whipping_boy) at the Eyrie.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) Robert is distrustful of Ser [Lyn Corbray](wiki:Lyn_Corbray).(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

For formal occasions Robert dons a doublet of cream and blue,(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) while in cold weather he wears sky-blue velvet, a chain of gold and sapphires, and a white bearskin cloak.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Robert became [Defender of the Vale](wiki:Defender_of_the_Vale) upon the death of his father, Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), but does not receive his father's title of [Warden of the East](wiki:Warden_of_the_East), as King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) grants it instead to Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister).(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The court at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) instead refers to Robert Arryn as the True Warden of the East.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

Before his ascension, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) offered to take young Robert as his ward, something he had never offered to anyone else. Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) suspects Tywin made this offer in order to seize Robert Arryn as a hostage. King Robert planned to accept the offer but Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) fled from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to the Vale with her son to avoid this.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

Robert's mother, Lady Lysa, rules the Vale as [regent](wiki:Regent) in his name, but makes no effort to school him in the governance of his realm. He presides over the trial of [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) for the attempted murder of [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark). He is upset when [Bronn](wiki:Bronn) defeats Ser [Vardis Egen](wiki:Vardis_Egen) in Tyrion's [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) as he wanted to "make him fly" through the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door).(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Lysa refuses the offer of her sister, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), to foster Robert at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) for a few years.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Tywin, the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), agrees to restores the title of [Warden of the East](wiki:Warden_of_the_East) to [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn), in return for [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) marrying Robert's widowed mother, Lysa, and bringing the Vale to the king's peace.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) [Marillion](wiki:Marillion) sings Robert to sleep each night.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)

Following the death of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) at his [royal wedding](wiki:Purple_Wedding), Petyr smuggles [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) to the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers), where he marries Lysa. Lady Arryn tells Sansa that she intends to marry her to Robert once Sansa's husband, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), is executed for Joffrey's murder.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Ragot68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

In the [godswood of the Eyrie](wiki:Godswood_of_the_Eyrie), Robert uses his doll to destroy the snow castle replica of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) made by his cousin, Sansa, in her guise of Alayne Stone. When Sansa rips the doll, Robert has a fit and is taken away by Maester [Colemon](wiki:Colemon). After an altercation between Sansa and Lysa, Petyr pushes Lysa through the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door) and blames Marillion.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

### A Feast for Crows

Upon the death of Robert's mother, [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) assumes the position of [Lord Protector of the Vale](wiki:Lord_Protector_of_the_Vale), claiming the rule of the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) and the guardianship of Lord Robert through his brief marriage to Lysa.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Robert has become even more emotional and unstable after his mother's death. He has grown highly attached to Alayne, who has taken on a surrogate maternal role with regards to the boy and calls him "Sweetrobin".(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Robert states he is unable to sleep because of the singing of the maimed Marillion.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) At Petyr's suggestion, Maester Colemon has been using [sweetsleep](wiki:Sweetsleep) to treat Robert's seizures, although he is also worried about the potentially fatal side-effects of using it too often.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) Robert is now fearful of blades, so Petyr allows the boy's fine hair to grow as long as a girl's.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8) Robert becomes fond of lemoncakes, possibly because they are a favorite treat of Alayne.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9)

Seated on the [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) throne, Robert hosts Lord [Nestor Royce](wiki:Nestor_Royce). When Sansa, disguised as Alayne Stone, tells Nestor that Lysa had been killed by Marillion, Robert has another fit. Ser [Lothor Brune](wiki:Lothor_Brune) is kicked in the face during the boy's spasms.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

Several of the more prominent nobles of the Vale sign a declaration to oust Petyr from the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) and assume the rearing and training of Robert, calling themselves the [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant).(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4) When they come to the Eyrie, Robert objects to the arrival of Ser [Lyn Corbray](wiki:Lyn_Corbray) and flings his porridge, which strikes Colemon.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5) The Lords Declarant want Robert to be fostered with Lord [Yohn Royce](wiki:Yohn_Royce) at [Runestone](wiki:Runestone), where Robert can be taught by Ser [Samwell Stone](wiki:Samwell_Stone) and Septon [Lucos](wiki:Lucos), healed by Maester [Helliweg](wiki:Helliweg), and befriended by his cousin, [Harrold Hardyng](wiki:Harrold_Hardyng).(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6) Petyr convinces his rivals to grant him a year, however, and Robert later is a gracious host to [Benedar Belmore](wiki:Benedar_Belmore), [Gilwood Hunter](wiki:Gilwood_Hunter), [Horton Redfort](wiki:Horton_Redfort), [Symond Templeton](wiki:Symond_Templeton), and [Anya Waynwood](wiki:Anya_Waynwood).(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-7)

Petyr invites [Gyles Grafton](wiki:Gyles_Grafton) and [Terrance Lynderly](wiki:Terrance_Lynderly) to be companions and squires for Robert.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-8)(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-10) With the approach of winter, the Arryn court descends from the Eyrie to the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon). Sansa and [Mya Stone](wiki:Mya_Stone) help the scared Robert down the [Giant's Lance](wiki:Giant's_Lance). Once at the Gates of the Moon, however, Petyr tells Sansa he intends to marry her to Harry the Heir once the sickly Robert inevitably dies.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-11)

### The Winds of Winter

Robert immediately approves of [Alayne Stone's](wiki:Sansa_Stark) suggestion he be protected by an order inspired by his hero, the [Winged Knight](wiki:Winged_Knight), with a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) choosing the eight members of the new [Brotherhood of Winged Knights](wiki:Brotherhood_of_Winged_Knights). Robert tells Alayne he wants to marry her, but she says he cannot as she is baseborn. Robert dislikes his cousin, Ser [Harrold Hardyng](wiki:Harrold_Hardyng), thinking they are waiting for him to die so Harry the Heir can take over the Eyrie.(wiki:Robert_Arryn.cite_ref-16)

## Notes

Robert's fostering plans are a key plot element: Jon wanted Dragonstone (Stannis), Robert I suggested Casterly Rock (Tywin), Lysa refused both. The Lords Declarant later demand he be fostered at Runestone with Bronze Yohn Royce. His seizure condition and Colemon's careful sweetsleep dosing are potential narrative threads.

Maester Colemon notes that Lady Lysa "would give his lordship her breast whenever he grew overwrought" — she breastfed Robert long past infancy, part of the smothering, coddled upbringing behind his frailty. (Alayne I, AFFC — `sources/chapters/affc/affc-alayne-01.md:81`)
