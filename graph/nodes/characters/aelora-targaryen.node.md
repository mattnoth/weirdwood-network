## Origins

Aelora was married to her twin brother, Prince [Aelor Targaryen](wiki:Aelor_Targaryen). When their father, Prince [Rhaegel Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen), died in [215 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aelor was named [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) and heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) by their uncle, King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Aelora_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) As Rhaegel was often disregarded on account of his mental fragility, both he and his children were sometimes forgotten as heirs to Aerys who came ahead of Aelora's uncle, Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen). Those more knowledgeable of the true line of succession were quick to point out that Rhaegel, Aelor, and Aelora all preceded Maekar.(wiki:Aelora_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

During the reign of Aerys I, his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), was the subject of much mistrust. Bloodraven's detractors worried that nobody in [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) was fit to stand up to him(wiki:Aelora_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) This anti-Bloodraven sentiment ran so deep that some even suggested Rhaegel and his children might be killed by his [magic](wiki:Magic). Even Prince Maekar was not free of such suspicions, though his youngest son, [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), was quick to protest that Maekar loved his brother and would never harm him or his children.(wiki:Aelora_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

In [217 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aelora accidentally caused Aelor's death in a "grotesque mishap", which left her mad with grief.(wiki:Aelora_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Following Aelor's death, King Aerys recognized Aelora as his new heir to the throne.(wiki:Aelora_Targaryen.cite_ref-6) Some time later, three men known as [the Rat, the Hawk, and the Pig](wiki:The_Rat,_the_Hawk,_and_the_Pig) attacked Aelora at a masked ball, after which she committed suicide.(wiki:Aelora_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
