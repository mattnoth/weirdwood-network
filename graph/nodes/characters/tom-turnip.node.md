## Identity

Tom Turnip — The king's half-witted fool, victim and unwitting catspaw of Saera's japes; she once had him climb the Iron Throne, which cut his arms and legs, and later had him brought naked to the Blue Pearl.

## Origins

Tom Turnip was a fool in the court of King [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and his sister-wife, Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen). Their daughter, Princess [Saera Targaryen](wiki:Saera_Targaryen), often played cruel pranks on Tom, such as once telling him it would amuse the court if he performed naked at a great feast, but his actions were poorly received. On another occasion, Saera told Tom that if he climbed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) he would become king, but the fool sliced his arms and legs to pieces when he attempted to do so.(wiki:Tom_Turnip.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

In [84 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Ser [Braxton Beesbury](wiki:Braxton_Beesbury), [Jonah Mooton](wiki:Jonah_Mooton) and Lord [Roy Connington](wiki:Roy_Connington) were found drunk at the [Blue Pearl](wiki:Blue_Pearl), a brothel in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), laughing as Tom was surrounded by six [whores](wiki:Prostitution). When the drunk lordlings were questioned by [gold cloaks](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing), Red Roy admitted they thought it would be funny to see the fool do the deed, and Jonah admitted it was Princess Saera's idea. When brought before King Jaehaerys, the lordlings confessed to bringing Tom to the brothel but remained quiet about Saera's involvement. Saera's activities with the three men were uncovered, however, after Alysanne spoke with [Perianne Moore](wiki:Perianne_Moore) and [Alys Turnberry](wiki:Alys_Turnberry).(wiki:Tom_Turnip.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

## Appearances & Description

Tom was described as a [simpleton](wiki:Simpleton). He was clumsy and prone to tremors.(wiki:Tom_Turnip.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
