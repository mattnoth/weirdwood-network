## Origins

According to [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy), [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) lived in awe and fear of their elder brothers, Rodrik and [Maron](wiki:Maron_Greyjoy).(wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Rodrik was good friends with [Harras Harlaw](wiki:Harras_Harlaw) in their youth.(wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

[Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), rose in rebellion against [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), thinking the new king did not have great support amongst the nobility of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) and that Balon would be allowed to go his own way. Rodrik led longships in the [storming of Seagard](wiki:Storming_of_Seagard), causing the [bronze](wiki:Bronze) bell of the Booming Tower at [Seagard](wiki:Seagard) to be rung for the first time in three hundred years. [Jason Mallister](wiki:Jason_Mallister), [Lord of Seagard](wiki:Lord_of_Seagard), killed Rodrik beneath the castle walls and threw the [ironmen](wiki:Ironmen) back into the sea.(wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Maron was later killed in the [battle at Pyke](wiki:Battle_at_Pyke).(wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Balon blames [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell), for the deaths of Rodrik and Maron during [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), though neither was actually slain by Eddard.(wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) [Alannys Harlaw](wiki:Alannys_Harlaw), Balon's grieving wife, eventually began to roam the halls of [Pyke](wiki:Pyke) in search of her lost sons, even walking the rope bridge to the [Sea Tower](wiki:Sea_Tower) while barefoot; a [maester](wiki:Maester) often had to draw splinters from her heels.(wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

[Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) has little love for his eldest brother, telling [Patrek Mallister](wiki:Patrek_Mallister) he holds no enmity towards Patrek's father, Lord [Jason](wiki:Jason_Mallister), or the [Mallisters](wiki:House_Mallister) for slaying Rodrik during [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion).(wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

After the [capture of Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell) and the supposed deaths of [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) and [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark), two of Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark)'s sons, Theon claims he has laid to rest the [ghosts](wiki:Ghosts) of his own brothers, Rodrik and [Maron](wiki:Maron_Greyjoy).(wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

### A Feast for Crows

[Alannys Harlaw](wiki:Alannys_Harlaw) continues to deeply grieve the loss of her eldest sons.(wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### The Winds of Winter

[Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) informs [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) that the deaths of Rodrik and Maron had shattered their mother, Alannys.(wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy.cite_ref-7)
