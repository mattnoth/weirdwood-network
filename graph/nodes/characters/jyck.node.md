## Origins

In his service for [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), one of Jyck's regular tasks was to hire [prostitutes](wiki:Prostitutes) for him. He sometimes failed to warn the women that Tyrion is a [dwarf](wiki:Dwarf), leading to some unpleasant situations for Tyrion when the prostitutes first saw him.(wiki:Jyck.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Appearances & Description

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) considers Jyck a fairly good sword.(wiki:Jyck.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Jyck arrives with the royal party in [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and later, together with [Morrec](wiki:Morrec), serves as escort to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) when Tyrion travels to the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Jyck.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Jyck is still with Tyrion when they come across Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn). Outnumbered, Tyrion's party is taken captive by [rivermen](wiki:Rivermen).(wiki:Jyck.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Lady Stark wants to bring Tyrion to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) and they reach the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon). As a captive, Jyck is sullen and miserable. When the party is attacked by [Vale mountain clans](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans), Jyck is cut down from behind while he is slicing at a man in front of him. After the skirmish, Bronn pulls the boots off Jyck's feet, as they are much finer than his own, and Tyrion takes Jyck's horse. Reflecting the results of the engagement and Jyck's bareback charge, Tyrion thinks that he was a fool to the end.(wiki:Jyck.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Tyrion later meets his father, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), who comments on how Tyrion's capture led to the fighting in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), stating that no man sheds [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) blood with impunity. Tyrion points out that no Lannister blood has actually been lost and mentions the death of Jyck and Morrec in this context.(wiki:Jyck.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
