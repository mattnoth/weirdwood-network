## Origins

### Early life

Aegon was born the fourth son and fifth child of Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) and his wife, [Dyanna Dayne](wiki:Dyanna_Dayne).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf101.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Aegon served at court in King's Landing for two years as a page, sometimes attending to the [small council](wiki:Small_council).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) Aegon owned a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg), which was colored with green and white swirls and had been placed in his cradle with him when he was a babe. Aegon and his elder brother, Prince [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), hoped their eggs would hatch so they could become [dragonriders](wiki:Dragonriders). Aegon kept his egg at [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) The brothers recreated the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field) with painted soldiers on Maester [Melaquin](wiki:Melaquin)'s table.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3)

Aegon loathed his brother, the cruel and sadistic [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen), who threw Aegon's pet [cat](wiki:Cat) down a well. Aerion once visited Aegon in his bedroom during the dead of night, put a knife to Aegon's privates, and joked about removing his genitals so he would become a girl whom Aerion could marry.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6) Once Aegon's sister [Rhae](wiki:Rhae_Targaryen) slipped a [love potion](wiki:Love_potion) into his drink so that he would marry her and not their sister [Daella](wiki:Daella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Maekar_I)),(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5) to whom he might have been betrothed.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-13) Aegon did not wish to marry, instead dreaming of becoming a [knight](wiki:Knight) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), who are sworn not to wed.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6)

### Tourney at Ashford Meadow

Aegon was supposed to squire for his eldest brother, [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), in the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow), but Daeron had no interest in jousting and instead drank himself into a stupor at an [inn near Ashford](wiki:Inn_near_Ashford). Daeron shaved Aegon's head to prevent the boy's distinctive Targaryen hair from being recognized by men sent by their father, Prince [Maekar](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7) When the hedge knight Dunk visited the inn, he assumed Aegon was a stableboy. Calling himself "Egg", the disguised Aegon assumed that Dunk was short for "Duncan", which Dunk accepted. Aegon was impressed by the humble hedge knight and followed him. Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) agreed to let Egg serve as his squire during the tourney.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8)

Duncan was arrested and sentenced to fight a [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle) after attacking Egg's elder brother, Prince [Aerion Targaryen](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen). Because the battle was to be a [Trial of Seven](wiki:Trial_of_Seven), Aegon convinced Ser [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon) to fight on behalf of Duncan. Aegon's uncle, Prince [Baelor Breakspear](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), also fought alongside Duncan during the trial, but Baelor died of a wound inflicted by Prince Maekar, Baelor's brother and Aegon's father, who had fought on behalf of Aerion and House Targaryen.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9)

Maekar, the [Prince of Summerhall](wiki:Prince_of_Summerhall), wished Aegon to be trained as a knight, but Aegon would only agree if Duncan were the knight who trained him. When Maekar offered Duncan a place at the garrison of [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall), Dunk declined and agreed to take Aegon on only if Aegon accompanied him as his squire. Aegon would live humbly, but Duncan thought it would make Aegon a better man than his brothers, Aerion and Daeron. Thus, Aegon served as a squire to Duncan under his nickname "Egg".(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-10)

### Squire

Aegon kept his identity secret from the public while squiring for Duncan, and he kept hidden in his boot a signet ring from his father, [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), [Prince of Summerhall](wiki:Prince_of_Summerhall).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7)(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-4) While escorting merchants in [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) from the [Prince's Pass](wiki:Prince's_Pass) to [Vaith](wiki:Vaith), [Chestnut](wiki:Chestnut) died while Egg was on the horse's back. The pair traveled aboard a poleboat on the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood) toward the [Planky Town](wiki:Planky_Town), and during the journey [orphan](wiki:Orphans_of_the_Greenblood) girls rubbed Aegon's head for luck. Dunk and Egg then sailed on *[White Lady](wiki:White_Lady)* to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), where Aegon received the mule [Maester](wiki:Maester_(mule)), a gift from his brother, Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8)

A little less than two years after Ashford Meadow, Aegon and Duncan served Ser [Eustace Osgrey](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey) of [Standfast](wiki:Standfast) during his conflict with [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber), [Lady of Coldmoat](wiki:Lady_of_Coldmoat), over the [Chequy Water](wiki:Chequy_Water). At Coldmoat, Egg verified for the illiterate Dunk that a royal parchment from King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) had granted the stream to Lord [Wyman Webber](wiki:Wyman_Webber). When Rohanne demanded that Eustace turn over Ser [Bennis of the Brown Shield](wiki:Bennis_of_the_Brown_Shield), Duncan showed Aegon's signet ring to her in an unsuccessful attempt to avert hostilities. After Duncan slew Ser [Lucas Inchfield](wiki:Lucas_Inchfield) in [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle), Aegon pulled Duncan from the stream and protected the wounded hedge knight during his recovery. Eustace and Rohanne chose to wed to end their conflict, and Egg attended Eustace during the wedding feast.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-9) Rohanne gifted Aegon with a sorrel palfrey which he named [Rain](wiki:Rain).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-5)

### Second Blackfyre Rebellion

After visiting [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept), Aegon and Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) attended the [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC). Egg struck the squire of Ser [Mallor](wiki:Mallor) after an insinuation that Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) was a [kinslayer](wiki:Kinslayer) who might one day intentionally kill King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-6) Aegon attended to Duncan, who called himself the Gallows Knight, during his joust with Ser [Uthor Underleaf](wiki:Uthor_Underleaf). Egg was distraught after Dunk's defeat, as the hedge knight would have to forfeit to Uthor his sword, armor, and horse, [Thunder](wiki:Thunder).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-7)

During the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion), Egg revealed his signet ring to Maester [Lothar](wiki:Lothar), who turned the boy over to Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell) and [Lord Frey](wiki:Lord_Frey). After Aegon claimed that his father was marching on [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls) with an army, Ambrose fled to the castle's sept with Aegon while Frey returned to [the Twins](wiki:The_Twins). Duncan slew Ser [Tommard Heddle](wiki:Tommard_Heddle) when the knight tried to take the prince hostage. Dunk then told Egg to escape Whitewalls if he could. The next morning, the [Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) conspirators surrendered when Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) arrived with a royal host, with Aegon in his company. Aegon convinced Bloodraven to lend gold to Duncan so he could ransom his possessions back from Uthor Underleaf.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-8)

### Later life

In [219 AC](wiki:219_AC), Aegon served alongside his father Prince Maekar and elder brother Prince Aerion in the [Third Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Third_Blackfyre_Rebellion), where he demonstrated great courage.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf413.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

Aegon married Lady [Betha Blackwood](wiki:Betha_Blackwood) in [220 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). The marriage at the time provoked no opposition as Aegon was very low in the line of succession. They married for love and eventually had five children together. Aegon named his first-born son [Duncan](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen), presumably to honor his friend, Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall)(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) (however, when asked about it, [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin) refused to answer).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1105.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

In [221 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aegon's uncle, King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen) died with no issue and was followed on the Iron Throne by his last living brother, Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen). Aegon and his brothers were summoned to court by King Maekar.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

According to a semi-canon source, Aegon participated in the suppression of the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising) in [233 AC](wiki:233_AC), with [Tion Lannister](wiki:Tion_Lannister) as his squire. The prince knighted the mortally wounded [Tywald Lannister](wiki:Tywald_Lannister), Tion's twin, during the battle.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-17)

King Maekar I was also killed at [Starpike](wiki:Storming_of_Starpike) during the rebellion. With Maekar dead, it was unclear who should be king since two of Aegon's older brothers had died before his father. [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) left a feeble-witted daughter named [Vaella](wiki:Vaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daeron)) and [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen) an [infant son](wiki:Maegor_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerion)), so a [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) was called to choose the king. Aegon seemed the logical choice but many lords considered him "half a peasant" due to his youth among the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk). The council approached Aegon's older brother, Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), but he refused, stating the crown should be given to Aegon.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf414.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### King Aegon V

Aegon was crowned [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms) in [233 AC](wiki:233_AC)(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) when he was thirty-three years old.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) From the early years of Aegon V's reign, the king's old friend Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) served in his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard),(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) eventually rising to [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

Aegon's first act as king was the arrest of Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), for the murder of [Aenys Blackfyre](wiki:Aenys_Blackfyre), as Bloodraven had offered Aenys safe conduct to Westeros for the [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) but had Aenys executed when he arrived in the capital.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf414.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) Though all were glad another [Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) pretender was dead, Aegon had to punish Lord Bloodraven to prove the word of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) was not worthless. Although Brynden was sentenced to death, Aegon allowed him to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). Aegon's brother, Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), decided to join the Watch as well, to prevent any plots in which he would be used against his brother. Brynden and Aemon were joined by many of Bloodraven's personal guard, the [Raven's Teeth](wiki:Raven's_Teeth), as well as prisoners released from dungeons by the king.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4) At Aegon's insistence, Ser Duncan the Tall also made the journey north to ensure Aemon's safe arrival to [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Aegon's reign began during a harsh winter which lasted from [230 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) until [236 AC](wiki:236_AC). The benevolent Aegon sent massive shipments of food and grain to aid starving [northmen](wiki:North), though there were those who felt he provided too much aid. The end of winter saw the return of the [Blackfyre Pretenders](wiki:Blackfyre_Pretenders), with the [Fourth Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Fourth_Blackfyre_Rebellion) taking place in [236 AC](wiki:236_AC). [Daemon III Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_III_Blackfyre) attempted to seize the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) with the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company), but few rallied to his side. Aegon and his sons rode to meet and repel the invaders, and Duncan the Tall slew Daemon in single combat, ending the rebellion. [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers), known as Bittersteel, managed to flee with the remnants of the Golden Company [across the narrow sea](wiki:Across_the_narrow_sea).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5)

Aegon V spent much of his reign dealing with uprisings. On three occasions, the Iron Throne had to intervene in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) due to the inferior and inept leadership of Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_20-0) Most of Aegon's troubles resulted from his efforts to improve the lives of the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), whom he had interacted with while squiring for Duncan. Although the smallfolk loved Aegon for his reforms and granting of rights and protections, high lords felt their powers over peasantry was diminished and curtailed by these new reforms. Lacking the [dragons](wiki:Dragon) controlled by early [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) kings, Aegon reluctantly compromised with the recalcitrant lords on several issues.

### Family Betrothals

Aegon faced renewed troubles in his reign caused because of his sons.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiaf.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Convinced the [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) practice of [incestuous marriage](wiki:Incest) was harmful after spending time with [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), Aegon and his queen, [Betha Blackwood](wiki:Betha_Blackwood), betrothed four of their children to some of the [Great Houses](wiki:Great_Houses) of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) in [237 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) Their eldest son and heir, Prince [Duncan](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen), was betrothed to the daughter of Lord [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen), Aegon's second son, was betrothed to Lady [Celia Tully](wiki:Celia_Tully), the daughter of the Lord of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun). Aegon's youngest son, Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_V)), was betrothed to Lady [Olenna Redwyne](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell) of the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor), when both children were nine years old. In addition, Aegon's eldest daughter, Princess [Shaera](wiki:Shaera_Targaryen), was betrothed to [Luthor Tyrell](wiki:Luthor_Tyrell), the heir to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden). The proposed marriages would have won Aegon much support for his reforms, but his children had inherited his willfulness and their mother's stubbornness.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6)(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

Beginning in [239 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Prince Duncan fell in love with and eventually wed the mysterious [Jenny of Oldstones](wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones). King Aegon, the [small council](wiki:Small_council), the [Grand Maester](wiki:Maesters), and the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon) forced Duncan to choose between the peasant girl or the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). The prince choose his wife and abdicated as [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), making his younger brother, Prince Jaehaerys, the new heir. With Duncan refusing to marry the daughter of Lord Baratheon, Storm's End briefly rose in rebellion, which ended when Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) forced Lord Lyonel to yield during a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat). King Aegon pardoned Lyonel and betrothed his daughter, Princess [Rhaelle](wiki:Rhaelle_Targaryen), to Lyonel's heir, [Ormund](wiki:Ormund_Baratheon). Aegon sent Rhaelle to Storm's End as Lyonel's cupbearer and companion to his wife.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-7)

While King Aegon disliked the Targaryen practice of marriage through incest, Prince Jaehaerys and his sister, Princess Shaera, desired each other from a young age. With Duncan marrying Jenny as precedent, Jaehaerys and Shaera secretly wed and consummated their marriage in [240 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), leaving Aegon to deal with the anger of Houses [Tully](wiki:House_Tully) and [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-8)

Though betrothed for nine years to Lady [Olenna Redwyne](wiki:Olenna_Redwyne), Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_V)) broke his betrothal in [246 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) when he was eighteen years old. Daeron remained unwed, instead preferring the companionship of Ser [Jeremy Norridge](wiki:Jeremy_Norridge), a young knight whom he had befriended when they were squires at [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden). Daeron and Jeremy perished in battle in [251 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), crushing a rebellion led by [the Rat, the Hawk, and the Pig](wiki:The_Rat,_the_Hawk,_and_the_Pig).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-9)

### Summerhall

In [258 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), nine outlaws, exiles, pirates and sellswords hoping to carve out individual kingdoms came together in the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands) of [Essos](wiki:Essos). Among this [Band of Nine](wiki:Band_of_Nine) was [Maelys I Blackfyre](wiki:Maelys_I_Blackfyre), the last of the [Blackfyres](wiki:House_Blackfyre). Because the outlaws had no great urgency, King Aegon remained intent on his reign and on [dragons](wiki:Dragon) in particular. Against the advice of his friends and counselors, Aegon V became convinced that only with dragons could he force the [lords](wiki:Lord) of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) to accept his decrees that granted freedoms, rights, and protections to the smallfolk.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-10)

This eventually led to the [tragedy of Summerhall](wiki:Tragedy_of_Summerhall) in [259 AC](wiki:259_AC), in which Aegon perished while trying to hatch [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_egg). His son, Prince [Duncan the Small](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen); his friend and [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall); and others of the royal court also perished in the fire.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafduncan.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-11) Aegon was succeeded by his second son and heir, [Jaehaerys II](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen).

Many of the laws, rights and protections King Aegon V had granted and bestowed upon the smallfolk were later undone during the reign of King [Aerys II](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) by the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

## Appearances & Description

Aegon had large eyes which could appear dark blue,(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) but were actually [deep and dark and purple](wiki:Purple_eyes).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) The boy was pasty faced and skinny.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Because of his silver-gold hair, he shaved his head to hide his identity while a squire, making his eyes appear huge on his bald head.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Aegon wore a wide-brimmed floppy straw hat while squiring for Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall). At ten years old, Aegon was not quite five feet tall.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

According to a semi-canonical source, Aegon in his thirties had shoulder-length hair. Tall, slender, and handsome, he looked strong yet kindly. His [crown](wiki:Crown) was the simple gold circlet worn by [Viserys II](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) and [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) before him.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1253.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Aegon was bold, compassionate toward animals, and well educated in his youth.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) He was known to lie(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) and quick with a retort,(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) however, and was proud of being a noble.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) Some considered him insolent.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) Aegon grew to become a kindly and approachable man(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1253.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) beloved by the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk).(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), who gave the nickname Egg to his brother,(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) believed that Aegon had innocence and sweetness.(wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
