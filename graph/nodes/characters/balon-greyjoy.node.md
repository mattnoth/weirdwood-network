## Origins

### Early life

Balon is the eldest surviving son of [Quellon Greyjoy](wiki:Quellon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), and a lady of [House Sunderly](wiki:House_Sunderly). It is not clear when he was born.

Balon learned to sail when still young and was a very skilled captain. Balon scaled the [Flint Cliffs](wiki:Flint_Cliffs) to the [Blind Lord](wiki:Blind_Lord)'s haunted tower when he was ten. By his thirteenth year, he had become an expert oarsman. At fifteen he sailed with [Dagmer Cleftjaw](wiki:Dagmer) to the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) on a reaving, killing his first man and taking his first two salt wives there. By seventeen he captained his own [longship](wiki:Ships).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) Later he often went raiding and was known for his fierceness and fearlessness.

Balon and two of his brothers, [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) and [Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), wanted their father Quellon to join [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). Quellon was peaceful and cautious, however, and desired neutrality. After Crown Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) was slain in the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), Quellon was convinced by his sons to join the rebellion or else gain nothing from the war. The [ironmen](wiki:Ironmen)'s contribution to the war turned out to be minimal. Quellon was slain fighting the longships of the [Shield Islands](wiki:Shield_Islands) in a [battle at the Mander](wiki:Battle_at_the_Mander), after which Balon brought the Greyjoy ships back to Pyke so he could claim the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

When Balon returned to Pyke he found out his younger brother, [Urrigon](wiki:Urrigon_Greyjoy), had died of gangrene after being injured in the [finger dance](wiki:Finger_dance). The [maester](wiki:Maester) at [Pyke](wiki:Pyke) had tried to save the hand of the boy, but his potions had failed and the fourteen-year-old Urrigon lost his life instead. Balon commanded the same treatment be used on the maester, who died along with Balon's stepmother.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

Balon is married to [Alannys Harlaw](wiki:Alannys_Harlaw), who gave him three sons and a daughter: [Rodrik](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy), [Maron](wiki:Maron_Greyjoy), [Asha](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy), and [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy).

### Greyjoy's Rebellion

Lord [Quellon Greyjoy](wiki:Quellon_Greyjoy) had desired stronger ties with the [green lands](wiki:Green_lands), forbade most reaving, brought [maesters](wiki:Maesters) to the Iron Islands, and outlawed the taking of [thralls](wiki:Thrall). His son Balon, however, wished to return to the [Old Way](wiki:Old_Way), which he considered a better way for the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn). After becoming [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), Balon reversed most of his father's reforms. Over five years he oversaw the construction of the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) to serve the Seastone Chair.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

Balon was quick to seize the chance to revert to the ways of old when he thought that King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s recently-gained rule, through conquest, was unstable. Believing Robert would not have the support, the men, or the will to resist, Balon launched his own rebellion and declared himself [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-15) Beneath the bones of the [Grey King's Hall](wiki:Grey_King's_Hall) on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk), [Tarle the Thrice-Drowned](wiki:Tarle_the_Thrice-Drowned) placed a driftwood crown upon Balon.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

[Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion) won early success with the [burning of the Lannister fleet](wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet) at [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), but Balon's triumph was short lived. His eldest son, [Rodrik](wiki:Rodrik_Greyjoy), died during the [storming of Seagard](wiki:Storming_of_Seagard) and his fleet and forces were defeated, while his second son, [Maron](wiki:Maron_Greyjoy), died while [defending Pyke](wiki:Siege_of_Pyke).

When the defeated Balon was brought before Robert, Balon reminded the king that he had never sworn him an oath after [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). In return for his allegiance now, Balon's life was spared by the generous Robert. Balon's sole surviving son, ten-year-old [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), was taken by Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) as a [ward](wiki:Ward) and hostage.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4) Balon and his wife, Alannys, grew apart after the deaths of Rodrik and Maron and the taking of Theon.

### Recent History

In [297 AC](wiki:297_AC) one of Balon's brothers, [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), seduced the [third wife](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy's_third_wife) of another brother, [Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy). To prevent bloodshed between the brothers Balon sent Euron into exile and swore he would be killed if he returned. To keep his honor Victarion killed his wife.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

## Appearances & Description

Balon had always been thin, but by [299 AC](wiki:299_AC) he is gaunt with a hard face. He has hard black eyes with long grey hair flecked with white that hangs past the small of his back.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) He has a stoop in his shoulder.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) Balon is not as tall or as strong as his father [Quellon](wiki:Quellon_Greyjoy) had been, but he is just as quick and skilled at arms.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Lord Greyjoy wears musty [sealskin](wiki:Seal) robes.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

Balon has become bitter through age and grief.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) According to [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), Balon is not a heavy [drinker](wiki:Alcoholic_beverages).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Though defeated, Balon never gave up on his desire to become king.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) With the death of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and the outbreak of the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Balon assembles his fleet and orders all ships which visit [Lordsport](wiki:Lordsport), including trading vessels, to remain so he will have the element of surprise.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

[Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), sends Balon's surviving son, [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), to [Pyke](wiki:Pyke) as an envoy with an offer to ally with Balon against the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-5) Insulted, Balon spurns the offer and instead declares himself [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Balon hosts his lords and captains in the Great Hall of Pyke.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3)

Balon sends his brother [Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), the [Lord Captain of the Iron Fleet](wiki:Lord_Captain_of_the_Iron_Fleet), to capture [Moat Cailin](wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin). The ironborn's taking of the [Neck](wiki:Neck) cuts off Robb's army, which has been campaigning in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) and the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), from the north. Balon sends his daughter and preferred heir, [Asha](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy), to capture [Deepwood Motte](wiki:Fall_of_Deepwood_Motte). Theon, [Dagmer Cleftjaw](wiki:Dagmer), and Balon's brother [Aeron Damphair](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) are to [harry the Stony Shore](wiki:Harrying_of_the_Stony_Shore).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4) The ironborn forces are able to conquer a number of northern castles because most of their soldiers had left with Robb to fight in the south, and Balon styles himself [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Theon deviates from Balon's plan by sending Dagmer against [Torrhen's Square](wiki:Fight_at_Torrhen's_Square).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) With Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) drawn away from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) by Dagmer, Theon [captures](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell) the seat of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Asha encourages Theon to abandon Winterfell because it is too far inland, but the youth insists upon remaining.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Theon and Rodrik are both betrayed by [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), who orders the [sack of Winterfell](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Balon styles himself the Ninth of His Name Since the [Grey King](wiki:Grey_King), [King of the Iron Islands and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands_and_the_North), [King of Salt and Rock](wiki:King_of_Salt_and_Rock), [Son of the Sea Wind](wiki:Son_of_the_Sea_Wind), and [Lord Reaper of Pyke](wiki:Lord_Reaper_of_Pyke).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

In [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), the [small council](wiki:Small_council) discusses Balon's terms of alliance instead of a pledge of fealty. Lords [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) and [Paxter Redwyne](wiki:Paxter_Redwyne) urge that these terms be accepted in order to free up new resources for dealing with [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon). However, Tywin Lannister rejects them since Balon is already at war with [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

Balon dies in a fall while crossing a bridge at [Pyke](wiki:Pyke) during a storm in [299 AC](wiki:299_AC).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Some [portents](wiki:Ghost_of_High_Heart) and the timely return of his exiled eldest brother, [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), lead to speculation that Balon may have been murdered by [an assassin](wiki:Faceless_Men) at Euron's command.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rasos22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Euron Crow's Eye claims the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair) after Balon's death.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)

### A Feast for Crows

When [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) is informed of his older brother's death, he thinks back to the last time he saw Balon upon returning from the [Stony Shore](wiki:Stony_Shore). Aeron Damphair recalls while Balon's grey hair had turned half-white and the stoop in his shoulder more pronounced, his king had not seemed ill.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

After Balon's death, his daughter, [Asha](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy), and his brothers, [Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) and [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), are among the candidates to succeed him.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2)(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) His surviving son, [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), had been [taken prisoner](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell) by the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) and is not able to press his claim.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Euron Crow's Eye returns to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) the day after Balon's death, but his claim is vehemently opposed by Aeron. Balon's favored successor was Asha, but Aeron does not believe a woman can rule the ironborn. Aeron instead supports Victarion.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8)

The matter is decided during [a kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk) at [Nagga's Hill](wiki:Nagga's_Hill) on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk) organized by the Damphair.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) Despite the efforts of Asha and Victarion, Euron is chosen as Balon's successor after he promises to conquer the whole of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) with the power of [dragons](wiki:Dragons).(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

Euron abandons Balon's conquests in the north, leaving the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) remaining there to fend for themselves.(wiki:Balon_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)
