## Origins

Drogo's father was Khal [Bharbo](wiki:Bharbo).(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) [Cohollo](wiki:Cohollo) was pledged to the *[khalakka](wiki:Khalakka)*, or prince, while Drogo was still a child, and he saved young Drogo's life from [sellswords](wiki:Mercenary) on one occasion.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Bharbo gave his son a whip when Drogo reached manhood.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

By the age of thirty,(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10) Drogo leads a *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* with forty thousand warriors,(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) the largest on the [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea). The *[khal](wiki:Khal)* has never been defeated in battle.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11) He has given a tall grey stallion to [Jhogo](wiki:Jhogo).(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)

Drogo developed a taste for good [wine](wiki:Wine) from his visits to the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Magister [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis), serving as patron to the exiled [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) and [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), arranges a marriage between Princess Daenerys and Khal Drogo during one of Drogo's visits to Pentos. In return for his bride, Drogo is to provide ten thousand Dothraki warriors for her brother's campaign to retake the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-12)

[The wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Drogo_and_Daenerys_Targaryen) takes place in the traditional Dothraki fashion, with Drogo's entire *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* gathering in a field outside Pentos for a day-long feast, punctuated with several deadly fights and couples having intercourse in the open. In accordance with tradition, Drogo's bloodriders offer fine weapons—a whip, an *[arakh](wiki:Arakh)*, and a [dragonbone](wiki:Dragonbone) bow—to the new *[khaleesi](wiki:Khaleesi)*, who then gives them to her husband. Drogo's own gift to his bride is a magnificent [silver mare](wiki:Silver_mare). When they ride off to consummate the marriage, Daenerys is terrified of her bridegroom and expects to be raped. Despite his fierce reputation, however, Drogo proves to be a surprisingly considerate lover. Although he and Dany share no common language, he establishes that he understands the word "no," then begins touching her gently, trying to arouse her. He does not begin to have intercourse with her until Dany expresses her approval and initiates it.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

Drogo initially ignores Daenerys as his *khalasar* rides to [Vaes Dothrak](wiki:Vaes_Dothrak), only visiting the uncomfortable girl in her tent at night to roughly copulate with her. After Daenerys has a [dragon dream](wiki:Dragon_dream), however, she gradually becomes more confident. Daenerys's new role as *khaleesi* takes her out of her abusive brother's control. After learning seduction techniques from [Doreah](wiki:Doreah), Daenerys initiates sex with Drogo outside of her tent, impressing the *khal*.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) As Daenerys's new role as *khaleesi* takes her out of her abusive brother's control, and Daenerys learns from [Jhiqui](wiki:Jhiqui) that she is pregnant.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

As Daenerys learns to speak the [Dothraki language](wiki:Dothraki_language), Drogo begins to learn the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue) of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), though he has a strong accent.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6) Drogo addresses Daenerys affectionately as "moon of my life,"(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-7) while she calls him "my sun and stars."(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) After Daenerys eats a [stallion heart](wiki:Stallion_heart) in front of the *[dosh khaleen](wiki:Dosh_khaleen)* of Vaes Dothrak, she and Drogo have sex by the [Womb of the World](wiki:Womb_of_the_World).(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-8) The *dosh khaleen* proclaim that Drogo and Daenerys's unborn child, [Rhaego](wiki:Rhaego), will be the [stallion who mounts the world](wiki:Stallion_who_mounts_the_world), a prophesied leader in Dothraki legend.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-9)

Unlike Daenerys, Viserys shows nothing but contempt for Dothraki culture and becomes increasingly impatient for the aid Khal Drogo had promised him. A drunken Viserys breaks the ancient taboo against bloodshed in Vaes Dothrak by drawing his blade and threatening the life of Daenerys and her unborn child, demanding his crown. Announcing that Viserys will have the crown of gold he deserves, Drogo sidesteps the prohibition against spilling blood in the sacred city by first melting down his belt of gold medallions Drogo and upending the pot of molten gold over Viserys's head, killing him.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-10)

Drogo and his [bloodriders](wiki:Bloodrider) hunt a *[hrakkar](wiki:Hrakkar)*. It rakes Drogo through his leggings, scarring his leg. He returns to Vaes Dothrak with the carcass slung across a packhorse.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5) Drogo has little interest in invading [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), a land that holds nothing in particular that the Dothraki want and is located across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), which they have always feared. However, he reconsiders after a [wineseller](wiki:Wineseller) tries to [poison](wiki:Poison) Daenerys in the Western Market and claim King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s reward. Enraged by the attempt on his wife's life, Drogo reaffirms his commitment to conquer the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) for her sake and seat their son on the throne of her ancestors.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6)

Drogo pillages several towns in [Lhazar](wiki:Lhazar) to harvest slaves to exchange for warships that will take his horde across the sea. While in Lhazar, Drogo's *khalasar* defeats the rival *khalasar* of Khal [Ogo](wiki:Ogo). Drogo is shot in the arm by an arrow, and one of Ogo's bloodriders cuts off Drogo's left nipple, leaving a flap of bloody skin. Drogo slays the bloodrider and then Ogo himself.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3) Although he does not understand or share her objections to the traditional Dothraki practices of raping and enslaving conquered peoples, Drogo supports Daenerys when she orders his warriors to cease raping the [Lhazareen](wiki:Lhazareen), delighted by his wife's growing courage.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-4)

Though Drogo is unconcerned with his seemingly minor wound, Daenerys convinces him to let [Mirri Maz Duur](wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur), a Lhazareen *[maegi](wiki:Maegi)* she had rescued, make him a poultice. The poultice itches, however, and Drogo tears it off, causing the wound to fester. Drogo's condition deteriorates until he falls from his horse, a symbolic event in Dothraki culture that indicates he is no longer fit to lead.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

As Drogo lingers near death, Daenerys convinces Mirri to use her [bloodmagic](wiki:Bloodmagic) to preserve his life by sacrificing [his stallion](wiki:Drogo's_stallion).(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Alarmed by the ceremony, Drogo's bloodriders try to intervene but are defeated by Daenerys's *[khas](wiki:Khas)* and Ser Jorah.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) The *maegi* betrays Daenerys in revenge for the attack on her village, and the ritual leave Drogo in a catatonic state while causing Rhaego to die in Daenerys's womb.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) Drogo's *khalasar* breaks apart, with [Pono](wiki:Pono) and [Jhaqo](wiki:Jhaqo) leading the largest of the dozen new hosts. Daenerys bathes Drogo, leads him out into the night, and fails in an attempt to arouse him. Realizing that Drogo will never return to his former state, Dany smothers him with a pillow.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)

Drogo and his treasures are piled upon a funeral pyre, and Daenerys places the three petrified [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_egg) given to her by Illyrio about Drogo's body. Daenerys burns Drogo on the platform along with a bound Mirri Maz Duur. Daenerys enters the conflagration as the platform burns and collapses. The flames magically trade life for life, killing the *maegi* and hatching three small [dragons](wiki:Dragon) while leaving Daenerys unharmed.(wiki:Drogo.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)
