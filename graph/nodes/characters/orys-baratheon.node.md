## Identity

Orys Baratheon (d. 37 AC) — married to Argella Durrandon; sworn to House Baratheon; Lord of Storm's End.

## Origins

### Youth

Orys was the rumored [bastard](wiki:Bastard) half-brother of [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) by their father [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen_(son_of_Daemion)), [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone). Orys was one of Aegon's fiercest commanders, and was regarded as Aegon's only true friend and champion of his youth.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3)

According to Aegon I's grandson, [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), it was well-known that Lord Aerion Targaryen was Orys's father, making him a [dragonseed](wiki:Dragonseed) bastard of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). However, Jaehaerys was unaware of the circumstances of his birth, but acknowledged it was possible Orys was conceived as a product of a [first night](wiki:First_night).(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_.E2.80.93_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### Aegon's Conquest

When King [Argilac Durrandon](wiki:Argilac_Durrandon) offered Aegon his daughter [Argella](wiki:Argella_Durrandon) in marriage and some lands as her [dowry](wiki:Dowry), the Lord of Dragonstone spurned the [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King)'s proposal. Instead, he requested significantly more land and offered to seal the pact by wedding Orys to Argilac's daughter. The Storm King angrily rejected these terms, for Argilac the Arrogant believed that Orys was a bastard and that such a match would dishonor his daughter.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-4)

When [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest)'s had begun, Orys had command on the ground during the [first battle](wiki:Aegon's_first_test), defeating Lords [Mooton](wiki:House_Mooton) and [Darklyn](wiki:House_Darklyn). After Aegon's coronation at [Aegonfort](wiki:Aegonfort), Orys was raised to [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Within days of the coronation, Orys and Queen [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen), with her dragon [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes), were then tasked with capturing [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), taking the greater part of Aegon's host with them. While King Argilac gathered his banners, Lords [Fell](wiki:House_Fell), [Buckler](wiki:House_Buckler), and [Errol](wiki:House_Errol) ambushed the advance elements of Orys's host, until they were forced to retreat when Meraxes burned the surrounding woods. Argilac the Arrogant, hearing of the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal), knew his formidable walls would not protect him from [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire). Argilac rode out with his great host to give open battle.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-5)

Orys was made aware of Argilac's march by Rhaenys, who provided him with the Storm King's numbers and dispositions, for she had spotted the host with her dragon. Baratheon took up a strong position on the hills south of [Bronzegate](wiki:Bronzegate), digging in on the high ground while he awaited the stormlanders. Argilac's host was twice the size of Orys's, and when the battle called the [Last Storm](wiki:Last_Storm) begun, the stormlanders managed to break through the Baratheon center, only to be engulfed by dragonflame. With Meraxes and Rhaenys turning the battle into chaos, Orys descended down the hill, where he encountered the Storm King. Ordering his own men to stand aside, he gave Argilac a chance to yield. Instead, Argilac cursed Orys and they fought instead. Though he took a wound, Orys slew Argilac the Arrogant, ending the reign of the Storm Kings. The loss of their king made the stormlanders loss heart, who surrendered or fled.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9)(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-6)

After the battle, Argella Durrandon, the daughter of Argilac, declared herself the Storm Queen and continued to hold Storm's End until her household turned against her to avoid the same fate of Harrenhal. They delivered her to Orys, naked and chained. Orys, however, covered her with his cloak and treated her chivalrously. Aegon rewarded Orys with the lands and incomes of Storm's End and named him [Lord Paramount of the Stormlands](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Stormlands). None dared suggest that he was unworthy of these honors.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) Orys took Argella as his wife and to honor Argliac's valor, he adopted the stag banner, honors and words of the [Durrandons](wiki:House_Durrandon), "*Ours is the Fury*", forming [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon).(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-7)

### First Dornish War

Orys later joined King Aegon I in the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War) in [4 AC](wiki:4_AC), leading an assault on the [Boneway](wiki:Boneway) with one thousand picked [knights](wiki:Knight). His horses struggled up the stony, narrow, and twisty paths, while his knights were hit with boulders from above, the work of defenders the stormlanders never saw. Where the Boneway crossed the river [Wyl](wiki:Wyl_(river)),(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_.E2.80.93_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) the cunning [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) launched a night raid, raining arrows, rocks, and spears from above(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) as the stormlanders crossed a bridge. The bodies of the invading army blocked the Boneway from both ends, and when Lord Orys ordered his men to fall back, a triggered rockfall cut off their retreat. With no way forward or back, the stormlanders were massacred, save for Orys and a dozen other lords thought worth the ransom. However, Orys and his bannermen found themselves the captives of the [Wyl of Wyl](wiki:Wyl_of_Wyl), the savage mountain lord called the Widow-lover.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_.E2.80.93_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

When Aegon eventually agreed to the Dornish ransom demands, Lord Baratheon and his bannermen were ransomed in [7 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) for each man's weight in gold. However, they were freed only after having their sword hands chopped off so they would not be able to use them again against [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). Orys became known as Orys One-Hand thereafter.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) In retaliation for this treachery, King Aegon descended on the mountain fastnesses of [House Wyl](wiki:House_Wyl) with his dragon [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) and burned them to rubble, though the Wyls managed to take refuge in their caves and tunnels beneath the mountains.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_.E2.80.93_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Orys was said to have become crabbed and bitter after that, so he resigned his office as Hand and returned to the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands). He became obsessed with vengeance against the Dornish, especially House Wyl, whose lord had removed his hand.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) When Princess [Deria Martell](wiki:Deria_Martell) came to King's Landing to provide peace terms, Lord Orys argued for sending the princess back to her father [Nymor](wiki:Nymor_Martell), the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), without a hand.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_.E2.80.93_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

### Vulture Hunt

Orys received his opportunity for vengeance against the Wyls in the [Second Dornish War](wiki:Second_Dornish_War), during the early reign of Aegon's son, King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen). In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), a Dornish [outlaw](wiki:Outlaw) known as the [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Aenys_I)) led a huge army to reave the lands north of the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains), attacking [Blackhaven](wiki:Blackhaven) and [Nightsong](wiki:Nightsong). The Vulture King split his host, having one half led by Lord [Walter Wyl](wiki:Walter_Wyl) to besiege [Stonehelm](wiki:Stonehelm), the seat of [House Swann](wiki:House_Swann). The Lord of Storm's End, eager for revenge against the Dornish, led his forces from Storm's End one last time to participate in the [Vulture Hunt](wiki:Vulture_Hunt).(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

While Wyl's host sat besieging Stonehelm, Orys's forces crushed the Dornishmen beneath the walls of the castle.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) Lord Walter Wyl, the son of the Widow-lover, was brought to Orys, wounded but alive. Lord Baratheon chopped off Walter's sword hand, then his other hand, followed by both feet, which he called his "usury". On his trip home to Storm's End, Orys died of the wounds he had taken in the battle. Orys died content, according to his son [Davos](wiki:Davos_Baratheon), smiling at the rotten hands and feet dangling in his tent.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

## Appearances & Description

Orys had black hair and black eyes,(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) as well as a black beard.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

Known for his prowess in battle and his strong loyalty to King [Aegon I](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen),(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) Orys was described as a fierce warrior.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) After the loss of his hand in the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), he became crabbed and bitter, turning his attention to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) and obsessed with the idea of revenge.(wiki:Orys_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)
