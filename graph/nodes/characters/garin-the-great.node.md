## Origins

During the [Second Spice War](wiki:Second_Spice_War), Prince Garin gathered a great army of the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar) at [Chroyane](wiki:Chroyane) to oppose the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold). According to [Beldecar](wiki:Beldecar)'s *[History of the Rhoynish Wars](wiki:History_of_the_Rhoynish_Wars)*, Garin had a quarter of a million men in his command. The prince declared that as long as the army remained beside the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne), they would be protected from the [dragons](wiki:Dragon) of [Valyria](wiki:Valyria) by the Rhoynish [water wizards](wiki:Water_wizard) . Garin began to be called *the Great* after victories at [Selhorys](wiki:Selhorys), [Valysar](wiki:Valysar), and [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys).(wiki:Garin_the_Great.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

However, after Garin the Great threatened [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), his Rhoynish host was defeated by the [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) of [Valyria](wiki:Valyria).(wiki:Garin_the_Great.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) According to legend, the men of Volantis and Valyria hung Garin in a [golden](wiki:Gold) cage, and carried him north to his city of Chroyane, to witness its destruction and the enslavement of his people. Garin called upon [Mother Rhoyne](wiki:Mother_Rhoyne) to destroy his captors, but they only mocked him. Nevertheless, that very night, the waters of the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne) flooded out of season and with great force, drowning the invaders. Additionally, a thick fog fell upon Chroyane, and the Valyrian conquerors began to die of [greyscale](wiki:Greyscale).(wiki:Garin_the_Great.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Garin_the_Great.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Legend says that the fallen conquerors have never rested since that day, down beneath the waters of the [Sorrows](wiki:Sorrows). It is said that their cold breath rises from the murk to make the fogs that infest the ruined city of Chroyane, and their flesh is said to have turned as stony as their hearts.(wiki:Garin_the_Great.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Some also believe that Garin rose from his watery grave and became the [Shrouded Lord](wiki:Shrouded_Lord).(wiki:Garin_the_Great.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Some say [Garin's Curse](wiki:Garin's_Curse) brought about the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria).(wiki:Garin_the_Great.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell), her companions [Andrey Dalt](wiki:Andrey_Dalt) and [Gerold Dayne](wiki:Gerold_Dayne), and her [milk brother](wiki:Milk_brother), [Garin](wiki:Garin_(orphan)) of the [Orphans of the Greenblood](wiki:Orphans_of_the_Greenblood), discuss his [namesake](wiki:Namesake), Prince Garin.(wiki:Garin_the_Great.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Traveling through the [Sorrows](wiki:Sorrows) aboard the *[Shy Maid](wiki:Shy_Maid)*, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) insists that [Garin's Curse](wiki:Garin's_Curse) is merely [greyscale](wiki:Greyscale), caused by dampness and foul humors in the air, not curses. However, [Ysilla](wiki:Ysilla) tells him that the Valyrian conquerors also mocked when Garin cursed them, until the river rose and drowned them. She says that the the Valyrians still remain, restless beneath the river. When Tyrion asks about the [Shrouded Lord](wiki:Shrouded_Lord), [Yandry](wiki:Yandry) tells him that some say he is Garin the Great, but [Haldon Halfmaester](wiki:Haldon_Halfmaester) insists no man rises from the dead or lives for a thousand years, and that there have been over twenty Shrouded Lords.(wiki:Garin_the_Great.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

### The Winds of Winter

[Phario Forel](wiki:Phario_Forel) is a [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) playwright, whose first play, *[Wroth of the Dragonlords](wiki:Wroth_of_the_Dragonlords)*, features a speech made by Prince Garin on the eve of battle. [Izembaro](wiki:Izembaro), the King of the Mummers, quotes Prince Garin's speech on the night he and his group of mummers at [the Gate](wiki:The_Gate) are set to perform *[The Bloody Hand](wiki:The_Bloody_Hand)*, also written by Phario Forel.(wiki:Garin_the_Great.cite_ref-Mercy_5-0)
