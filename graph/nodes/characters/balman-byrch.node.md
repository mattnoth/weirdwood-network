## Origins

Ser Balman was once a renowned [tourney](wiki:Tourney) jouster, and in his youth he was considered one of the most handsome [knights](wiki:Knight).(wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) He jousted in the [tourney at Lannisport](wiki:Tourney_at_Lannisport) in [289 AC](wiki:289_AC) after [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), as well as in a tourney at [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) in [294 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

Balman has been married to Falyse for ten years, and they have no children. It is rumored that Balman shuns his wife's bed, preferring [maidens](wiki:Maiden).(wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Appearances & Description

Balman has a thick blond mustache. His wavy blond hair is receding, and he has a pot-belly.(wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) He enjoys food and drink, including [hippocras](wiki:Hippocras).(wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) thinks that Balman is a fool.(wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

Ser Balman and his wife, [Falyse Stokeworth](wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth), attend the funeral of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) is offended when they inform her that Lady [Tanda Stokeworth](wiki:Tanda_Stokeworth), mother of Falyse and the pregnant [Lollys Stokeworth](wiki:Lollys_Stokeworth), would like to name Lollys's [bastard](wiki:Bastard) child after Cersei's late father if a boy is born.(wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

When Lollys gives birth to a son, her husband, Ser [Bronn of the Blackwater](wiki:Bronn), names the child [Tyrion Tanner](wiki:Tyrion_Tanner).(wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)(wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Cersei is offended that Bronn named the child after her despised younger brother, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister). Falyse and Balman return to the capital from [Castle Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth), and during the journey they are turned away from [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) by order of the late [Gyles Rosby](wiki:Gyles_Rosby)'s [ward](wiki:Ward_of_Gyles_Rosby) and are disgusted by [sparrows](wiki:Sparrows) they encounter. Claiming she fears that Tyrion Lannister will attempt to have Bronn assassinate her son, King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), Cersei manipulates Balman into a conspiracy to kill Bronn.(wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

Although Cersei expects that Bronn will suffer a mortal hunting mishap, the chivalrous Balman instead challenges Bronn to a duel at Castle Stokeworth, believing the former [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword)'s lack of experience on horse will ensure his victory. However, Bronn drives his lance through the chest of Balman's horse, causing the animal to fall and crush Balman's legs. In return for Bronn's promise of mercy, Balman confesses Cersei's involvement. Balman cries out for help from Maester [Frenken](wiki:Frenken), and Bronn kills him with a knife to the eye. Falyse orders for Bronn to be seized, but Bronn's supporters instead force Falyse to flee Stokeworth. A despondent Falyse begs Cersei for aid in recovering Stokeworth, but the queen instead allows [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) to take Falyse and use her in his experiments on to keep her from telling tales.(wiki:Balman_Byrch.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
