## Identity

Vaegon Targaryen — Jaehaerys's surviving son, an archmaester at the Citadel known as Vaegon the Dragonless, pale, frail, and bookish.

## Origins

### Early life

Vaegon was born in [63 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the fourth son and seventh child of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen).(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

King Jaehaerys and Queen Alysanne assumed that Vaegon would one day [marry](wiki:Marriage) his sister [Daella](wiki:Daella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Jaehaerys_I)), who was only one year younger than him. When Vaegon was five years old, Jaehaerys told him to be kind to Daella, as she would one day become “his Alysanne”. However, as time passed it became clear that the two children did not like one another. Vaegon tolerated Daella's presence, but no more, and considered her to be stupid. Daella, on the other hand, was frightened of Vaegon, and considered him to be mean. Although Jaehaerys believed that the two children would eventually warm to one another, in truth their dislike only increased.(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

In [73 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), one of Queen Alysanne's [lady companions](wiki:Lady_companion) asked Vaegon and Daella when they would be wed. Vaegon's public reaction was a grave insult to Daella, insisting that she needed to find a husband who wanted stupid children, as those were the only kind she would be able to birth. After Daella fled the hall in tears, Vaegon's older sister [Alyssa](wiki:Alyssa_Targaryen) emptied a flagon of [wine](wiki:Wine) over Vaegon's head, which did not impress him. Instead, Vaegon simply told his sister that she had wasted [Arbor gold](wiki:Arbor_gold), before leaving to change his clothing.(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

Following this incident, Jaehaerys and Alysanne agreed that they would need to find other spouses for Vaegon and Daella. Vaegon's other sisters, [Saera](wiki:Saera_Targaryen) and [Viserra](wiki:Viserra_Targaryen), were briefly considered, but Alysanne said that Vaegon had never taken any notice of them. King Jaehaerys decided that Vaegon would benefit from spending less time in the library, and asked his elder son [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)) to train Vaegon at arms. Baelon trained Vaegon for a year, during which time both were miserable. One day, Baelon brought their sister Alyssa, dressed in mail, onto the yard as a challenger to Vaegon. Still recalling the incident with the Arbor gold, Alyssa laughed and mocked as she humiliated Vaegon in the yard, as Princess Daella looked on down at them from the castle. When he could take no more, Vaegon threw down his sword and left the yard forever.(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)

As Vaegon grew older and began to receive more attention from the young ladies at court, it became apparent that he was not interested in finding a bride. He was polite to the girls, but also did not pay them any attention. His primary interest continued to be books. Once, [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Elysar](wiki:Elysar) gave Vaegon a volume of erotic drawings in the hope that pictures of “naked maidens comporting with men and beasts and one another” might increase Vaegon's interest in women. Although Vaegon kept the book, he did not show a change in behavior.(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)

### Citadel

On Vaegon's fifteenth [name day](wiki:Name_day) in [78 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Jaehaerys and Alysanne asked Grand Maester Elysar whether Vaegon might have potential to be a [maester](wiki:Maester). Although the Elysar firmly replied "no", as Vaegon had no skills with people or [ravens](wiki:Raven), he stated that Vaegon might have what it takes to become an [archmaester](wiki:Archmaester). As such, three days later, Vaegon was summoned to King Jaehaerys's [solar](wiki:Solar), where he was told that he would be taking ship for [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) within a fortnight. Vaegon responded positively if curtly to his father, though King Jaehaerys would later tell Queen Alysanne that he thought Vaegon had almost smiled.(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)

At the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) of Oldtown, Vaegon would become an archmaester before the age of thirty-six,(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10) holding the ring and rod and mask of yellow [gold](wiki:Gold)(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_heirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) (signifying that he became archmaester of mathematics or economics).(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Although Vaegon wrote to his parents on occasion, his words dutiful, there was no warmth to the letters.(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-11) By the end of Jaehaerys's reign, Vaegon's existence had been largely forgotten by most of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). In [101 AC](wiki:101_AC), after the death of Prince [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), King Jaehaerys I's heir to the throne, King Jaehaerys summoned Vaegon back to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). According to some, Jaehaerys summoned Vaegon to offer him the throne (which Vaegon refused); according to others Jaehaerys only sought Vaegon's counsel on the matter of [succession](wiki:Laws_and_justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms). It was Vaegon who eventually suggested that the king should call a [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) to settle the issue. At the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC), the claim of Archmaester Vaegon was quickly ruled out on account of his vows.(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_heirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

### Later life

No further mention is made of Vaegon after the Great Council of 101 AC, as he apparently returned to obscurity in his studies at the Citadel. It is unknown if he was still alive during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), though it is not implausible: he was only 38 years old at the time of the Great Council, and would have been 66 years old by the outbreak of the civil war in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC). For that matter, the first volume of Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn)'s history, *[Fire & Blood](wiki:Fire_&_Blood,_Being_a_History_of_the_Targaryen_Kings_of_Westeros)*, stops in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), when the long [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) ended, at which time Vaegon would have been 73 years old. It is possible that Vaegon's ultimate fate is detailed in the second volume of Gyldayn's history, at some point later in the reign of King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen).

## Appearances & Description

Vaegon had the silver-gold hair and [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes) of the Targaryens. However, he could not be described as "comely": he had a long face and round shoulders, even at a young age. He always had a pinched sour cast to his mouth.(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Vaegon was never robust. People considered him to be sour, and he had the ability to make everyone around him miserable as well. He was not a coward, but did not enjoy the martial studies of [squires](wiki:Squire) and [pages](wiki:Page). He was a miserable fighter, and not well-trained at arms. He was a bookish boy and much preferred the library, where he could often be found. Vaegon was also remarkably disinterested in sex or romance; books were his only passion.(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Vaegon was quiet and wary as a boy.(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Even as a child, Vaegon was not well-liked, something which did not change over time. When he had to speak, he was often blunt, though never intentionally cruel. He always dutifully performed perfunctory courtesies, but no more.(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_heirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Near the age of forty, Vaegon had become pale and frail. He was devoted to [alchemy](wiki:Alchemy), [astronomy](wiki:Astronomy), mathematics, and other [arcane arts](wiki:Magic).(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_heirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

As an [archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) of the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), Vaegon held the ring, rod, and mask of yellow [gold](wiki:Gold),(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_heirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) signifying his expertise in math or economics.(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
