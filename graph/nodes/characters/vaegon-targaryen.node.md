## Identity

Vaegon, born Prince Vaegon Targaryen, was an archmaester of the Citadel and the seventhborn child of King Jaehaerys I Targaryen and Queen Alysanne Targaryen. Also called Vaegon the Dragonless (wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen, cite: Fire & Blood).

## Origins

Born in 63 AC, the fourth son and seventh child of King Jaehaerys I and Queen Alysanne (wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen).

## Appearances & Description

Vaegon had the silver-gold hair and purple eyes of the Targaryens, but could not be described as comely — he had a long face, round shoulders, and a pinched sour cast to his mouth. He was bookish, never robust, a miserable fighter, and remarkably disinterested in sex or romance. Even as a child he was not well-liked. Near the age of forty, he had become pale and frail, devoted to alchemy, astronomy, mathematics, and arcane arts. As archmaester, he held the ring, rod, and mask of yellow gold, signifying expertise in math or economics (wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen, cite: Fire & Blood).

## Narrative Arc

Jaehaerys and Alysanne assumed Vaegon would marry his sister Daella, but the two children disliked one another — Vaegon considered her stupid and Daella was frightened of him. When the match was proposed, Vaegon refused, declaring he would sooner have a book than a wife. The king was furious and commanded him to go to the Citadel to forge his chain. Vaegon actually thrived there, eventually earning multiple links and becoming an archmaester. He died in or after 101 AC (wiki:Vaegon_Targaryen, cite: Fire & Blood).
