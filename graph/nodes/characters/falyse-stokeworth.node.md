## Appearances & Description

Falyse has an acid tongue.(wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) thinks to herself that Falyse not only looks like a fish but drinks like one too. ## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Falyse remains at the court of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon) after the beginning of [hostilities](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) with the [North](wiki:North). She attends the [tourney for King Joffrey I's thirteenth name day](wiki:Tourney_on_King_Joffrey's_name_day) with her mother, Lady [Tanda](wiki:Tanda_Stokeworth) and her sister, [Lollys](wiki:Lollys_Stokeworth).(wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Falyse arrives in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with a small troop of soldiers the day before the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater). She stays in [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) during the battle with her sister and mother.(wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Falyse attends the funeral of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) with her husband, Ser [Balman Byrch](wiki:Balman_Byrch),(wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) but is not in King's Landing for the [wedding](wiki:Wedding) of King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_I_Baratheon) to Lady [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell).(wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) After the birth of Lollys's [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son, Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) believes Ser [Bronn](wiki:Bronn) to be a traitor because he names the child [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Tanner). While traveling from [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth) to King's Landing, Falyse is refused hospitality at [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) by the [ward of Lord Gyles Rosby](wiki:Ward_of_Lord_Gyles_Rosby) and is also disturbed by [sparrows](wiki:Sparrows) on the road. Once Falyse returns to the capital, Cersei plots with Falyse and her husband, attempting to use them as her tools to get rid of Bronn.(wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

The plan backfires when Ser Balman challenges Ser Bronn to single combat, and Bronn kills Balman in an unchivalrous manner. Bronn hits Falyse and orders her gone. She returns to King's Landing, pleading with Cersei to give her the men to retake her castle. Cersei, fearing that the failure of the plot would undermine her position, gives Falyse to [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) instead and tells him that Falyse should never see the light of day again.(wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) When Cersei contemplates sending Falyse back to [Castle Stokeworth](wiki:Castle_Stokeworth) to reclaim it from Bronn, Qyburn tells her that she is not able to feed herself, much less rule.(wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Dance with Dragons
