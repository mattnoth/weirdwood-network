## Origins

Brandon the Builder is said to have lived during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) Legends from the [Reach](wiki:Reach) claim that he was a descendant of [Brandon of the Bloody Blade](wiki:Brandon_of_the_Bloody_Blade), a son of the mythical [Garth Greenhand](wiki:Garth_Greenhand).(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Tales from the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) claim that when Brandon was a boy, he helped [Durran Godsgrief](wiki:Durran_Godsgrief) build [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

Legends claim that Brandon built the [Wall](wiki:Wall)(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) after the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night).(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) According to a ballad in Maester [Childer](wiki:Childer)'s *[Winter's Kings, or the Legends and Lineages of the Starks of Winterfell](wiki:Winter's_Kings,_or_the_Legends_and_Lineages_of_the_Starks_of_Winterfell)*, Brandon sought assistance from the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) in building the Wall, and he learned to understand the [True Tongue](wiki:True_Tongue) in order to do so.(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) The children are said to have have used [magic](wiki:Magic) during the construction of the Wall, which was composed of huge blocks of ice moved with the aid of [giants](wiki:Giants).(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_night.27s_watch.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Bran the Builder is also said to have raised [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell),(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) the seat of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) in the [north](wiki:North), again with the help of giants,(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) and legends claim that Brandon prayed in the [castle's godswood](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) The [Kings of Winter](wiki:Kings_of_Winter) and [Lords of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell) descend from Brandon the Builder.(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) It is said that he gave [Brandon's Gift](wiki:Brandon's_Gift), a stretch of land twenty-five leagues wide south of the Wall, to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), though some [maesters](wiki:Maesters) argue this was done by [another Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(disambiguation)) of House Stark.(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Rasos40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

Some stories claim King [Uthor of the High Tower](wiki:Uthor_of_the_High_Tower) commissioned Brandon to design the stone [Hightower](wiki:Hightower) at [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), while others state it was Bran the Builder's son, who was also named [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(son_of_Bran_the_Builder)).(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

Brandon the Builder is not as beloved among singers and storytellers as [Lann the Clever](wiki:Lann_the_Clever), the legendary founder of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:Brandon_the_Builder.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
