## Identity

Alyssa Velaryon — Daughter of Aethan Velaryon, wed to Aenys in 22 AC at fifteen; the Velaryons were of Valyrian stock, making it a cousin match.

## Origins

### Early life

Alyssa was born in [7 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) to Lord [Aethan Velaryon](wiki:Aethan_Velaryon), King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen)'s [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships),(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) and Lady [Alarra Massey](wiki:Alarra_Massey).(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) She had several brothers and cousins in House Velaryon.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

Alyssa married her cousin, Prince [Aenys Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), in [22 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) The marriage was a political match.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_aenys_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) She gave birth to six children during this marriage: [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) ([23 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) ([26 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) ([29 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) ([34 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) ([36 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), and [Vaella Targaryen](wiki:Vaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) ([39 AC](wiki:39_AC), died in the cradle).(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

When Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) suggested a betrothal between her own son [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) and Alyssa's firstborn Rhaena in [24 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Alyssa and her husband were opposed the idea. King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) eventually agreed to wed Maegor to [another](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower). In the later years of Aegon I's reign, when Maegor refused half a dozen [dragon](wiki:Dragon) hatchlings, Alyssa teased him, asking him whether he was afraid of dragons.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) Following King Aegon I's sixtieth nameday in [33 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aenys and Alyssa took over the royal progresses, traveling across the realm half of every year.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)

Upon the coronation of her husband Aenys in [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), Alyssa became queen. It has been suggested that the birth of Alyssa's sixth child, Vaella, in [39 AC](wiki:39_AC) is what drove Prince Maegor to [take a second wife](wiki:Polygamy) that same year. Aenys and Alyssa lived in a manse on [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill) during his reign, as the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) was still not yet complete. In [41 AC](wiki:41_AC), following the first [rebellious actions](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) of the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) and [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows), King Aenys I decided that the manse was not safe for his family, and decided to relocate to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). Aenys died the following year on Dragonstone.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) At his funeral, Alyssa sang a dirge for him.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9)

### Reign of Maegor

Alyssa left Dragonstone within hours of the funeral, fleeing to her [father](wiki:Aethan_Velaryon) on [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark). She brought her three youngest children, [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), and [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) with her. There, she proclaimed her eldest son, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), the true king of Westeros while Maegor, who had claimed the Iron Throne and been crowned king, laid in a coma in King's Landing.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-10) Dowager Queen Visenya eventually traveled to Driftmark and convinced Alyssa to return to court with her children. She was present for Maegor's wedding to [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower) in [42 AC](wiki:42_AC), and even took part in the [bedding](wiki:Bedding) ceremony. However, her presence started the rumors that she had abandoned [Aegon's cause](wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion). Regardless of the rumors, in the latter part of [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), Queen Tyanna warned King Maegor that Alyssa had secretly been communicating with [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), and [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) in an attempt to convince them to declare for Aegon.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-11)

By [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), Alyssa had become a prisoner in all but name to Dowager Queen Visenya on Dragonstone, together with her son Jaehaerys and daughter Alysanne, while her son Viserys was summoned to court to serve as Maegor's squire. When Visenya died in [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), the chaos that followed gave her the opportunity to escape with Jaehaerys and Alysanne. She also stole [Dark Sister](wiki:Dark_Sister), Visenya's [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword, from the dead Dowager Queen's chambers.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-12)

Alyssa took her children into hiding, and it was thought that they had crossed the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), possibly to [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) or [Volantis](wiki:Volantis). Alyssa's escape led to the death of her eldest living son, Viserys, who was [tortured](wiki:Torture) by Queen Tyanna for nine days until he died. His body was left in the courtyard of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), as Maegor hoped Alyssa would return to claim it. She did not return, however, and Viserys was eventually cremated.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-13) In [47 AC](wiki:47_AC), Ser [Theo Bolling](wiki:Theo_Bolling), a landed knight who had fought for King Maegor, was arrested by the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). He was charged with conspiring with Alyssa to murder Maegor and place her son Jaehaerys on the throne.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-14)(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

By [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), Alyssa and her children had arrived at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), where [Lord](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End) [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon), the first to give his support to the prince, proclaimed Jaehaerys to be the true and lawful king of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-15) Alyssa stood beside her son as [he vowed to end Maegor's reign](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen's_uprising). Nine days after Maegor's death, Jaehaerys returned with his siblings to King's Landing to claim the throne; Alyssa arrived a fortnight later with Lord Rogar and a large host.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-16)

### Reign of Jaehaerys

Alyssa served her son [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) as [Queen Regent](wiki:Regent) during his minority. Alyssa wished for the prisoners in the dungeons of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) to be executed for having supporter King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). Jaehaerys, however, proclaimed that all of Maegor's supporters would be forgiven, and Alyssa refused to go against his word, as it would make him look weak. Jaehaerys did, however, make an exception for his mother, by executing the headsman, gaolers, and confessors who had taken part in torturing Alyssa's son [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)). Alyssa was afterwards gifted with their heads and hands.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Later that year, Alyssa accompanied her surviving children to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) where Jaehaerys successfully reconciled the Iron Throne and the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), it was widely believed during Jaehaerys's reign that during the visit to Oldtown, [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) and [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon)), the twin daughters of Queen [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) and Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), were switched. Gyldayn suggests that Rhaena or Alyssa might have done so.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

When her daughter Rhaena [married](wiki:Wedding_of_Rhaena_Targaryen_and_Androw_Farman) [Androw Farman](wiki:Androw_Farman) on [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle) without either informing or inviting her mother, Alyssa was angered. This was the start of a rift that began to slowly develop between them. Later that same year, Alyssa was married Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), in the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit). Their wedding became known as the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding). By Alyssa's suggestion, the wedding was followed by the [War for the White Cloaks](wiki:War_for_the_White_Cloaks), a contest the victors of which would be granted places in the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Next, Alyssa and the [small council](wiki:Small_council) began to debate about a bride for Jaehaerys. Jaehaerys was not allowed to be present for these discussions, as Alyssa knew that he would choose his younger sister [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), and Alyssa wished to avoid an uprising similar to [the one](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) that had followed the wedding of her eldest two children, Rhaena and Aegon. Alyssa also refused the idea of wedding Jaehaerys to a foreigner. After Jaehaerys and Alysanne had fled to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), where they had secretly gotten married, Alyssa and Rogar traveled after them. When Rogar and Jaehaerys's Kingsguard were about to cross swords, Alyssa declared they would leave in peace, and returned with her husband and his men to King's Landing.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

Alyssa hoped that Jaehaerys and Alysanne would come to King's Landing to celebrate the start of [50 AC](wiki:50_AC). When they did not, Alyssa planned to travel to Dragonstone again, so she could beg Jaehaerys and Alysanne to come home. She was forbidden to go by her husband, however, and Alyssa stayed at the Red Keep. Instead, she sent [ladies-in-waiting](wiki:Handmaid) to Dragonstone, supposedly to serve as Alysanne's household, but in truth to try and persuade her to rescind the marriage. However, Alysanne charmed them all and gained their complete loyalty. With her plan failed and the news of the king's [marriage](wiki:Marriage) having spread throughout the realm, Alyssa informed the small council they had to accept the marriage. Rogar, however, angrily declared her to be weak, and suggested putting Jaehaerys aside to crown Princess [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen), the eldest daughter and heir of the deceased Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), in his stead. For this suggestion, Alyssa stripped Rogar of his office, which shattered the last bit of affection Rogar had felt for her.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Alyssa informed Jaehaerys by raven about some of what had happened and appointed her brother, Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)), as the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), afterwards retiring from ruling the kingdom for the remainder of Jaehaerys's minority. On the twentieth day of the ninth moon of [50 AC](wiki:50_AC), Jaehaerys [turned sixteen](wiki:Age_of_majority)(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) and returned to King's Landing, where Alyssa reconciled with her son. Jaehaerys summoned Rogar back to court, and instructed him to be an honorable husband to Alyssa. Reconciled, Alyssa and Rogar returned to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) together. They returned to the city when Jaehaerys and Alysanne had their public second marriage.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Much to everyone's surprise, Alyssa became pregnant in [51 AC](wiki:51_AC). While Rogar halted his infidelities to stand by Alyssa during her pregnancy, joyful of the prospect of becoming a father, Alyssa feared this pregnancy would end similar to her previous one. In early [52 AC](wiki:52_AC), however, Alyssa gave birth to a healthy son, [Boremund Baratheon](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon), although she never truly recovered from the childbirth.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### Death

In [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), Alyssa became pregnant again. She went into labor a month early, and because of this was on the brink of death. When King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) arrived at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) to stand by their mother, they found Alyssa's husband, Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) drunk and unwilling to enter his wife's bedroom. According to Maester [Kyrie](wiki:Kyrie), only [sweetsleep](wiki:Sweetsleep) gave Alyssa some respite from her pain; He informed the king and queen that Alyssa was dying, and that there was no more he could do to save her. However, there was a chance he could save her child, although he would have to cut open Alyssa to do so. Jaehaerys forced Rogar into the room and gave him the choice. Unable to look at his wife, Rogar stayed only long enough to tell the maester to save his child.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

As Maester Kyrie cut open Alyssa to save the child, Alysanne remained by her side, holding her hand. Many accounts claim that Alyssa woke before the surgery began, and consented to the plan. Other accounts, however, claim she never woke. Maester Kyrie's surgery was successful, and as she died Alyssa gave birth to a daughter, [Jocelyn Baratheon](wiki:Jocelyn_Baratheon).(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

## Appearances & Description

Alyssa had the silvery hair and [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes) of the [Valyrians](wiki:Valyria).(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

During her wedding to Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon), Alyssa wore a greatcloak with both the [Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) seahorse and the [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) dragon facing each other.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Alyssa had charm, wit, and kindness.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) According to [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Barth](wiki:Barth), above all else Alyssa desired to be loved, admired, and praised.(wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
