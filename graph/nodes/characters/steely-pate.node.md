## Appearances & Description

Steely Pate was a short man with huge hands. He stood no more than five feet tall. The smith's thick chest and arms were covered in coarse black hair, and he had a black beard.(wiki:Steely_Pate.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Pate was proud of his [steel](wiki:Steel), which was plain looking, but strong. Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) did not consider the armorer to be humble.(wiki:Steely_Pate.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
The blacksmith wore a short leather cloak. A [sword](wiki:Sword) and anvil were painted on the side of his wagon.(wiki:Steely_Pate.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Origins

Steely Pate sold Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) new [armor](wiki:Armor) at the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow). Duncan sold the [palfrey](wiki:Horse) [Sweetfoot](wiki:Sweetfoot) to [Henly](wiki:Henly_(House_Ashford)) so that he could afford the armor.(wiki:Steely_Pate.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) After Duncan was arrested for defending the puppeteer [Tanselle](wiki:Tanselle) from Prince [Aerion Targaryen](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen), Pate put a new steel rim on Duncan's [oaken](wiki:Oak) shield, and reinforced the back with steel bands, for the price of just a [copper](wiki:Copper) [penny](wiki:Currency).(wiki:Steely_Pate.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) The smith accompanied Duncan to the [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) held at [Ashford Meadow](wiki:Ashford_Meadow), and he tried to help the mortally-wounded Prince [Baelor Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)) after the battle.(wiki:Steely_Pate.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
Duncan lost his armor, sword, and horse [Thunder](wiki:Thunder) when he lost to Ser [Uthor Underleaf](wiki:Uthor_Underleaf) in the [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls). Uthor was dismissive of the armor's quality, but Duncan defended it, saying it was made by Steely Pate. Later, Duncan paid his ransom to Uthor with [gold](wiki:Gold) loaned by Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), who was grateful to Duncan for his help in suppressing the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion).(wiki:Steely_Pate.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
