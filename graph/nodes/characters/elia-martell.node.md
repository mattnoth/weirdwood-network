## Origins

### Early years

Elia Martell was the fourth child and first daughter born to the future [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne_(mother_of_Doran)), and the second child to live past infancy. She was born a month premature, and her older brother [Doran](wiki:Doran_Martell) did not expect her to live, when he learned about her birth; she survived, however, though her health was always fragile thereafter.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) During her youth she was very close to her younger brother, [Oberyn](wiki:Oberyn_Martell),(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) to whom she was only one year apart.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

When Elia reached the age of marriage, she and her brother Oberyn, together with their mother and their mother's consort, traveled from [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) to several potential suitors for both Elia and Oberyn. Amongst the places they visited were [Starfall](wiki:Starfall), the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor), [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), the [Shield Islands](wiki:Shield_Islands), [Crakehall](wiki:Crakehall), and finally [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock). Oberyn mocked all Elia's suitors. In Oldtown, Elia met [Baelor Hightower](wiki:Baelor_Hightower), whom she liked most of all her suitors. She had been half in love with him, until he farted in the presence of both her and Oberyn, leading to Oberyn naming him "Baelor Breakwind". Elia could not look at him without laughing afterwards.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)

Elia's mother had known Lady [Joanna Lannister](wiki:Joanna_Lannister) from the time they both served as ladies in waiting to Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen). Despite having learned of Joanna's death in [childbirth](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), in [273 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), during their stay at Oldtown, the Martells traveled onwards to the Rock. Oberyn believes that his mother had planned with Joanna to marry either Elia or Oberyn to one of Joanna's twins, [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) and [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), since there had been an empty cabin on their ship, meant for someone of high birth. Waiting until it was decent, Elia's mother eventually broached the subject of marriage to Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), first suggesting a match between Cersei and Oberyn, next a match between Elia and Jaime. Tywin refused both matches brusquely, informing her how Cersei was meant to marry Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), and suggesting the new-born [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) as betrothed to Elia instead, which was taken as an insult.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### Marriage

Elia and Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) were [betrothed](wiki:Marriage) in early [279 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and they married a year later, in [280 AC](wiki:280_AC), in a great ceremony at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) They moved to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) shortly after their wedding, where later in [280 AC](wiki:280_AC), Rhaegar and Elia's first child was born, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)). When the baby was presented to Rhaegar's parents at court, Queen [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) embraced her granddaughter warmly, but King [Aerys II](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) refused to touch or hold the child and complained that she "smells Dornish". Rhaegar and Elia's second child, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), was born near the new year of [282 AC](wiki:282_AC).(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf51.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Their marriage—though not romantic—seemed happy, although Elia's health, which had always been problematic, remained fragile.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Elia was bedridden for half a year after giving birth to Rhaenys, and giving birth to Aegon nearly killed her. Following Aegon's birth, the [maesters](wiki:Maesters) told Rhaegar that Elia would bear no more children.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Elia attended the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal) in [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), during the year of the false spring, together with her husband, Prince Rhaegar, and her father-in-law, King Aerys II Targaryen. Rhaegar won the tournament, and was to crown the [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty). However, he passed over Elia, and crowned Lady [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) instead.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

At the start of the next year, Rhaegar left Elia and their newborn son [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), and took to the road with several companions. Not long thereafter, Rhaegar disappeared with Lyanna,(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf51.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) and this event was one of the events which sparked [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). According to Rhaegar's younger brother, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), Rhaegar was simply not happy with his marriage. He believes that, if Rhaegar had been happy with Elia, he would not have needed Lyanna.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) [George R.R. Martin](wiki:George_R.R._Martin) has described Elia's relationship to Rhaegar as "complex".(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-20)

### Robert's Rebellion

[Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), had been furious at his younger sister's mistreatment, and thus was slow to lend aid to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). Preparing to battle the rebels, King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) threatened Elia's uncle, Prince [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), gracelessly reminding him that he held Elia and her young children. Lewyn took control of the ten thousand [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) who had eventually been sent to aid the Targaryens, and accompanied Elia's husband, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), to fight in the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident). Lewyn and Rhaegar both died at the [Trident](wiki:Trident), and Aerys, due to his paranoia, believed that Lewyn must have betrayed Rhaegar during the battle. As a consequence, he kept Elia and both of her children in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) as hostages against possible [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) betrayal.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rssm1046.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

For most of the rebellion, [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) had remained neutral, but after the Battle of the Trident, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) and twelve thousand of his [westermen](wiki:Westermen) approached the capital. King Aerys opened the gates to them, mistaking them for allied reinforcements. Instead they began the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing) in the name of King [Robert](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) later recollected that as he himself was murdering Aerys to prevent the [wildfire plot](wiki:Wildfire_plot), Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) and Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch) were scaling the walls of [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast), while Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) was leading his own forces through the city gates.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Elia's daughter, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)), was killed by Amory after breaking the door down; he dragged the screaming toddler from under her father's bed and stabbed her over fifty times.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Gregor murdered Elia's son, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), in front of her by smashing his head against a wall. He then raped her, and killed her by crushing her head.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

### Consequences to Elia's murder

Although Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane)'s involvement is common knowledge at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock),(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) the [official histories](wiki:The_World_of_Ice_and_Fire_(Yandel)) (written by the victors) record it is not known who murdered Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) in her bed, or who smashed the infant Prince [Aegon's](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) head against a wall and killed Elia. Some rumors say King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) had commanded it when he learned that Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) had taken up Lord [Robert Baratheon's](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) cause, while others suggest that Elia did it herself for fear of what would happen to her children in the hands of her dead husband's enemies.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf53.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

According to Tywin, he thought the deaths of Elia's children had been necessary to prove the loyalty of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) to the rebel cause after his previous neutrality, so he directly ordered them killed. Tywin claims, however, that he had never ordered the rape, and that Elia, by herself, had been nothing, stating that her death had been unnecessary. He claims that he most likely had neglected to mention Elia when giving orders to Gregor, who killed her because he had not explicitly been told by Tywin to spare her.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) Elia's younger brother, Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), however, believes that Tywin had Elia and her children killed as revenge for the slight he suffered when Aerys II refused the marriage of his son and heir, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), to his daughter, [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister). Aerys married Elia, whom Tywin had refused for his own heir, to Rhaegar instead.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7)

[House Martell](wiki:House_Martell) was enraged by the murder of Elia and her children. Oberyn wanted to continue the war and champion the cause of Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), the only surviving son of Aerys II. Secret messages were sent, but the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), traveled to [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), where he returned the body of Prince [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell), and spoke with [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), ending all talks of war.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2)

## Appearances & Description

Elia Martell was said to be beautiful,(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) slender,(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rawoiafelia_martell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) with black eyes, and a flat chest.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) She always had a delicate health,(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rawoiafelia_martell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) having been born a month premature,(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) which did not permit her much travel in her youth.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) According to [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), Elia was a gentle, good, and gracious lady, but he also describes her as frail, due to her delicate health.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) He also says she was kind and clever, with a sweet wit.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

In [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), Princess [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) thinks of the stories of Robert's Rebellion that her brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) has told her, including Elia of Dorne pleading for mercy as [Rhaegar's](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) heir was ripped from her breast and murdered in front of her. Viserys plans to re-conquer Westeros by marrying Daenerys to *[Khal](wiki:Khal)* [Drogo](wiki:Drogo) of the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki), and assures himself that the realm will rise for him against the [Usurper](wiki:Usurper) Robert Baratheon, including the Dornishmen who "burn to avenge Elia and her children."(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

In the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells) of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) tells Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) that [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell) still broods on the murder of Princess Elia and her babes, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) and Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)).(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### A Clash of Kings

In the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying), Daenerys has a vision of a silver-haired man speaking with a woman nursing a newborn baby, who she does not recognize.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) The man, Rhaegar, discusses with Elia(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rssm15608.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) that he will name their son [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), as there is no better name for a king. Elia asks if Rhaegar will make a song for their son, and he replies that he has a song, as he is [the prince that was promised](wiki:The_prince_that_was_promised) and his is [the song of ice and fire](wiki:The_song_of_ice_and_fire).(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)

### A Storm of Swords

[Ulmer](wiki:Ulmer), a [ranger](wiki:Ranger) of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) and formerly a member of the [outlaw](wiki:Outlaw) [Kingswood Brotherhood](wiki:Kingswood_Brotherhood), claims he once put an arrow through the hand of Ser [Gerold Hightower](wiki:Gerold_Hightower) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) in order to steal a kiss from a [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) princess. Ulmer says he stole the princess's jewels and a chest of [coins](wiki:Coins).(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

While preparing to attack [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai), Daenerys Targaryen asks [Arstan](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) to tell her about Rhaegar, and he speaks of the prince's victories in the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal). Daenerys asks Arstan why Rhaegar named [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) as [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty) instead of his wife, Elia, and why he had kidnapped Lyanna later. She wonders if Elia had done something wrong to Rhaegar, and Arstan replies that it is not for him to say why Rhaegar chose to do what he did, but that Elia was a good and gracious woman, though of delicate health. Daenerys says that her brother Viserys once told her it was her own fault for having been born so late, that Rhaegar could have married her instead of Elia and would have been happy with his wife. Arstan replies that may be true, but he does not feel Rhaegar had the capacity to be happy.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4)

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) has a nightmare involving Rhaegar, who stands between Jaime's deceased sworn brothers of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) and tells Jaime, "I left my wife and children in your hands." Jaime replies that he never thought his father, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), would hurt them.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) arrives in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), asking for [justice](wiki:Justice) for Elia and her children.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3) When [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) stands on trial for [the murder](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon) and learns his adversary in a [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle) will be Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane), Oberyn sees an opportunity for vengeance for Elia, and champions Tyrion.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) Oberyn extracts a confession from Gregor after mortally wounding him, but the Mountain takes advantage of the Red Viper letting his guard down, and kills Oberyn the same way he killed Elia, smashing his head in.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8)

### A Feast for Crows

Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) recalls how her father Tywin had wanted to marry her to Rhaegar, and thinks that Aerys's [madness](wiki:Targaryen_madness) was what caused him to refuse Cersei and instead marry Rhaegar to Elia, who Cersei believes to have been "feeble" and flat-chested.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

[Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), reveals to his daughter [Arianne](wiki:Arianne_Martell) that he has been plotting secretly to restore the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen) and revenge himself on Tywin, whom he and his brother, Oberyn, held responsible for the murders of Elia and her children.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) tells Queen Daenerys Targaryen that Elia was a good woman, "kind and clever, with a gentle heart and a sweet wit." He says that Daenerys's brother, Prince Rhaegar, had been "very fond" of Elia, but Barristan implies Rhaegar married for duty, not love.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), who was in love with Rhaegar,(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) believes that Elia was "never worthy" of the prince, as she was frail and sickly from start of the marriage, and childbirth only left her weaker.(wiki:Elia_Martell.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

Ser Barristan recalls the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal), and thinks that if Rhaegar had chosen the "good and gentle" Elia as [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty), much war and woe could have been avoided.
