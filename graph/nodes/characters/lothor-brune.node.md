## Origins

Lothor is of low birth and is a distant, poorer cousin to House Brune. After his father's death, Lothor went to the [Brunes of Brownhollow](wiki:House_Brune_of_Brownhollow) in an attempt to form a family bond, but they said he was no kin of theirs. He also states that he learned what he knows of arms the hard way.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Lothor Brune](wiki:Category:Images_of_Lothor_Brune)*

Lothor is a small man(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) with a stocky build(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) and a square jaw.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) He has a squashed nose and a mat of woolly grey hair.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Lothor has a rough voice but speaks seldom.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) He is stronger than he appears.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

[Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) thinks that Lothor is not handsome, but also not ugly,(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and she thinks he is stronger than he appears.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Sansa considers Lothor to be honest, and [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) deems him to be loyal.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

Lothor wears patched brown breeches and scuffed boots with a cracked, water-stained leather jerkin.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) He has drab armor(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) and dented plate without device.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Lothor wields a [sword](wiki:Sword)(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and a knife.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lothor takes part in the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) as a [freerider](wiki:Freerider). He defeats [Jory Cassel](wiki:Jory_Cassel) on a judgment by King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), ties with Ser [Aron Santagar](wiki:Aron_Santagar), and finally loses to Ser [Robar Royce](wiki:Robar_Royce).(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

### A Clash of Kings

Lothor is in the lists for the [tourney on King Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_King_Joffrey's_name_day). However, as his opponent, Ser [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard), is too drunk to ride his horse, their tilt is called off.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Lothor fights on behalf of Joffrey during the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), cutting his way through [House Fossoway](wiki:House_Fossoway) men-at-arms to capture Ser [Jon Fossoway](wiki:Jon_Fossoway) and kill Ser [Bryan](wiki:Bryan_Fossoway) and Ser [Edwyd Fossoway](wiki:Edwyd_Fossoway), earning him the nickname "Lothor Apple-Eater"' as the Fossoway sigil is an [apple](wiki:Apple). For his efforts in the battle, Lothor is awarded his [knighthood](wiki:Knight) and is to be granted land and a keep in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) at the end of the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Lothor reassumes the [House Brune](wiki:House_Brune_of_Brownhollow) arms differenced by the addition of his own achievement vis-à-vis the Fossoways.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-8)

### A Storm of Swords

Ser Lothor enters the service of Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), the [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin).(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Taking orders from Petyr, Lothor commands the crossbowmen on *[Merling King](wiki:Merling_King)* who kill Ser [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard) to keep the secret of [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark)'s departure from the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) amid the [Purple Wedding](wiki:Purple_Wedding).(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Rasos61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Lothor and [Oswell Kettleblack](wiki:Oswell_Kettleblack) row Petyr and Sansa to the shore when *Merling King* arrives at the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers). Following [the wedding feast](wiki:Wedding_of_Petyr_Baelish_and_Lysa_Arryn) of [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) and Petyr at the [Baelish keep](wiki:Drearfort), Lothor protects Sansa, now under the alias "Alayne Stone", from the unwanted advances of the singer [Marillion](wiki:Marillion).(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)

Upon his arrival at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), Lord Baelish dismisses the captain of the [House Arryn guards](wiki:House_Arryn_guards), Ser [Marwyn Belmore](wiki:Marwyn_Belmore), and names Lothor as his replacement.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Feast for Crows

To prevent Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) from climbing into her bed, Sansa asks Lothor to lock the boy in his room.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) When Robert has a shaking fit and slips from the Eyrie's [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) throne, Lothor catches the young lord. The spasming Robert accidentally kicks Lothor in the face while being restrained.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

During the parley with the [Lords Declarant](wiki:Lords_Declarant), Ser Lothor stands at the door of Petyr's [solar](wiki:Solar). When Ser [Lyn Corbray](wiki:Lyn_Corbray) draws [Lady Forlorn](wiki:Lady_Forlorn), Lothor goes for his own sword. Before anything can happen, however, Lyn is convinced by the other Lords Declarant to put down his famed blade.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Lord Robert is reluctant to leave the Eyrie when it is time to descend to the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon). After the boy tosses his chamber pot at Maester [Colemon](wiki:Colemon), Lothor seeks out Sansa for help in coaxing the boy from his bed.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) Lothor helps [Mord](wiki:Mord) load supplies in the winch room, and [Ossy](wiki:Ossy) helps the knight descend the [Giant's Lance](wiki:Giant's_Lance) to the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon).(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

Sansa thinks Lothor fancies [Mya Stone](wiki:Mya_Stone), the [bastard](wiki:Bastard) daughter of the late King Robert Baratheon, noticing that Lothor always smiles when he speaks of Mya. [Myranda Royce](wiki:Myranda_Royce) is skeptical of Lothor's prospects with Mya, however.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)

### The Winds of Winter

After Ser [Harrold Hardyng](wiki:Harrold_Hardyng) is rude to Sansa, believing her to be the bastard Alayne, Lothor reassures Sansa by referring to the [newly-made knight](wiki:Mêlée_at_Runestone) as an upjumped squire.(wiki:Lothor_Brune.cite_ref-twow_13-0)
