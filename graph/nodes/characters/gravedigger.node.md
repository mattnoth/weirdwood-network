## Appearances & Description

The gravedigger is bigger than [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) and lame. Like the other brothers he is cowled in dun-and-brown so his face is hidden.(wiki:Gravedigger.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

"a brother bigger than Brienne was struggling to dig a grave. From the way he moved, it was plain to see that he was lame. As he flung a spadeful of the stony soil over one shoulder, some chanced to spatter against their feet … When Dog went to sniff him he dropped his spade and scratched his ear." ## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

When [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) and her companions arrive on the [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle) they walk uphill passing a lichyard on the way. There she notices a brother bigger than her struggling to dig Brother [Clement](wiki:Clement)'s grave. Brother [Narbert](wiki:Narbert) chides him to be more watchful when some flung soil spatters at their feet. The gravedigger also drops his spade and scratches [Dog](wiki:Dog_(Meribald))'s ear.(wiki:Gravedigger.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

When they meet the [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)), he mentions that their gravedigger knows no rest as many corpses are washing up on the isle's shores.(wiki:Gravedigger.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

A little later Brienne and her companions take supper in the Quiet Isle's [septry](wiki:Septry). The novices are tasked with serving and Brienne again notices the limping gravedigger they had encountered on the hill.(wiki:Gravedigger.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
