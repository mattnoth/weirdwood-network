## Identity

Barth (d. 98 AC) — sworn to Faith of the Seven; Septon. One of 2 characters named Barth.

## Origins

### Early life

Barth was a blacksmith's son.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) He was given to the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) when he was young,(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and eventually took a septon's vows. Barth served at first at [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden).(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Thanks to his brilliance,(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) in [50 AC](wiki:50_AC) Barth was sent to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to see to the library of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). According to legend, Barth traveled on his own on a donkey.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

In [51 AC](wiki:51_AC), Barth officiated the second marriage of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), and along with Septon [Oswyck](wiki:Oswyck) he aided the king in writing the [Doctrine of Exceptionalism](wiki:Doctrine_of_Exceptionalism).(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) After Oswyck died in early [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), Barth took over his duties. By [54 AC](wiki:54_AC), Barth had become one of Jaehaerys's most trusted advisors. When the [High Septon](wiki:High_Lickspittle) died that year, Jaehaerys entertained the idea of trying to persuade the [Most Devout](wiki:Most_Devout) to name Barth to the office; Barth personally persuaded him not to do so. Barth was horrified by the suggestions of influencing the [choosing](wiki:Choosing) with gold, force of arms, and secret assassinations, and wrote down an account on how King Jaehaerys managed to influence the choosing by traveling to Oldtown, expressing regret about the corruption among the [Most Devout](wiki:Most_Devout).(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

When Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)) resigned as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), King Jaehaerys at first looked at Barth, among others, to fill the office. However, due to his low birth, and due to the fact that it was suspected that Barth was more loyal to the Faith than to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), he was not chosen.

In [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Barth, King Jaehaerys, Queen Alysanne, Grand Maester [Benifer](wiki:Benifer), and Lord [Albin Massey](wiki:Albin_Massey) began creating the first unified [code of law](wiki:Books_of_Law) for the entire [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). It would take them decades, and Barth alone contributed three times more than any other on his [Great Code](wiki:Great_Code).(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

When the severely-ill Princess [Aerea Targaryen](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) returned to King's Landing in [56 AC](wiki:56_AC) on [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) after having been missing for more than a year, Barth helped Benifer care for her. He announced to Jaehaerys and Alysanne, who waited outside the Grand Maester's chambers, when Aerea had died. Barth wrote two accounts on Aerea's death, which speak about the horrors that came forth from the girl, and in which he speculates about what had happened to her. They were kept in Barth's private papers, and were only discovered a hundred years later. The incident with Aerea left a strong impression on Barth, and afterwards he began research that eventually led to his book *[Dragons, Wyrms, and Wyverns: Their Unnatural History](wiki:Dragons,_Wyrms,_and_Wyverns:_Their_Unnatural_History)*.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

### Hand of the King

After Lord [Myles Smallwood](wiki:Myles_Smallwood) resigned as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) in [57 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) appointed Barth to the office.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) He would hold the office for forty-one years,(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) during which time he proved himself worthy of the position.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

Barth's first task as the King's Hand was a diplomatic journey to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), to treat with the [Sealord](wiki:Sealord) over the three [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_eggs) stolen by [Elissa Farman](wiki:Elissa_Farman). It was suspected that Elissa had sold them to the Sealord to pay for the construction of her ship, *[Sun Chaser](wiki:Sun_Chaser)*, but when Barth confronted the Sealord, the man denied having the eggs. Barth returned to King's Landing without the eggs, causing some lords to claim that he had failed at his first task. However, Barth instead struck a deal with the Sealord, removing the Crown's loans to the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos), and thereby reducing the Iron Throne's debt by half.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

Thanks to the reduction in debt, King Jaehaerys began making improvements to the living conditions of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Barth argued in favor of constructing wells, both within the city and without, pipes and tunnels to bring the water within the city's walls, and four cisterns to store the water in. This way, the drinking water available for the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) of the city would be improved. Barth's proposals were supported by Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), and eventually King Jaehaerys and his [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), [Rego Draz](wiki:Rego_Draz), approved the plan, after Alysanne presented them each with a tankard of foul water from the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) and dared them to drink it.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)

In [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Barth supported Queen Alysanne when she assembled the small council to request them to outlaw the right to the [first night](wiki:First_night). Following a short speech by Barth, in which he explained how the first night collided with the views of the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), insisting that the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon) would no doubt support the plan, Jaehaerys agreed to outlaw the ancient [custom](wiki:Custom).(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-5)

In [60 AC](wiki:60_AC), Barth suggested Lord [Martyn Tyrell](wiki:Martyn_Tyrell) for the office of master of coin, a position which had been empty since the murder of Rego the year before. Although Barth could not praise Martyn's wits, he mentioned that Martyn's wife, Lady [Florence Fossoway](wiki:Florence_Fossoway), had been keeping the accounts at [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) for years and had increased the incomes by a third. Insisting that Barth had never let him wrong, Jaehaerys appointed Martyn as his new master of coin.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-6)

In [84 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Barth stood as one of the witnesses when King Jaehaerys and Queen Alysanne confronted their daughter [Saera](wiki:Saera_Targaryen) with recent events involving her favorites. When Alysanne, following the death of Princess [Viserra Targaryen](wiki:Viserra_Targaryen) in [87 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), pleaded with Jaehaerys to allow the exiled Saera to return home, she brought Barth with her for support. They could not convince Jaehaerys, however, to bring Saera home.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

In [98 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-death_3-1) Barth died peacefully in his sleep. He was succeeded as Hand by Ser [Ryam Redwyne](wiki:Ryam_Redwyne).(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

## Appearances & Description

Barth is considered by historians to have been King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen)'s greatest friend(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and one of his most trusted advisors.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Barth was plainspoken but brilliant.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) He was considered a man of great ability.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) has described him as "the wisest man ever to serve as the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King)."(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) However, Barth's enemies claimed he was more [sorcerer](wiki:Magic) than [septon](wiki:Septon).(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords

When [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) feels insecure about his new position as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) due to his [low birth](wiki:Smallfolk), Maester [Pylos](wiki:Pylos) reassures him by explaining the prosperous and peaceful tenures of the humbly-born Septon Barth and his time as Hand to illustrate that high birth does not equal ability.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### A Feast for Crows

In [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), the dying Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) hears the talk of [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) and her [dragons](wiki:Dragons) emanating from [Qarth](wiki:Qarth). He tells [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) that Daenerys is the one, born amidst salt and smoke and that her [dragons](wiki:Dragon) prove it. Aemon explains to Sam that no one ever looked for a girl, as it was a [prince that was promised](wiki:Prince_that_was_promised), not a princess. Aemon says Barth was correct in believing that dragons are neither male nor female, instead being as changeable as flame.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

Aboard the *[Cinnamon Wind](wiki:Cinnamon_Wind)* Aemon's wits wander and his talk is all a jumble. He asks Sam to read for him from a book by Septon Barth.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

### A Dance with Dragons

Aboard the *[Shy Maid](wiki:Shy_Maid)* while writing [his dragonlore](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister's_dragonlore), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) thinks of the various books about dragons he would like to get his hands on. He is not hopeful concerning Septon Barth's *[Dragons, Wyrms, and Wyverns: Their Unnatural History](wiki:Dragons,_Wyrms,_and_Wyverns:_Their_Unnatural_History)*. He recalls that King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) had ordered all Barth's writings destroyed when he came to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Tyrion had read a surviving fragment of *Unnatural History* ten years ago, but he doubts that any of the septon's work found its way across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea).(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5)

Later, outside of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) on his way to the camp of the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons), the escaped slave Tyrion sees that the [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai)'i besiegers are making preparations in case [Drogon](wiki:Drogon) should return. He knows how hard dragons are to kill and is scornful of these would-be dragonslayers, remembering Barth's writings in *Unnatural History*.(wiki:Barth.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)
