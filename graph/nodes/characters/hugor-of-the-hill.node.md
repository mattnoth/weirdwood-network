## Origins

According to the *[Seven-Pointed Star](wiki:Seven-Pointed_Star)*, King Hugor is a major figure in the history of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). He was supposedly crowned by [the Father](wiki:Father_(the_Seven)) himself, who pulled down seven stars to make his crown. [The Maid](wiki:Maiden_(the_Seven)) brought forth a girl supple as a willow with eyes like deep blue pools that Hugor took as his first wife. [The Mother](wiki:Mother_(the_Seven)) made her fertile, and she bore him forty-four mighty sons as foretold by [the Crone](wiki:Crone_(the_Seven)). [The Warrior](wiki:Warrior_(the_Seven)) gave each son strength of arms and [the Smith](wiki:Smith_(the_Seven)) wrought each a suit of iron plate. His realm was extensive, spreading from the hills of northern [Andalos](wiki:Andalos) to the southern marches, now called the [Flatlands](wiki:Flatlands), in the south.(wiki:Hugor_of_the_Hill.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) The Seven also promised him and his descendants great kingdoms in a foreign land.(wiki:Hugor_of_the_Hill.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Hugor of the Hill may also be the Andal hero whom [Pentoshi](wiki:Pentos) singers call [Hukko](wiki:Hukko).(wiki:Hugor_of_the_Hill.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Some [Kings of Mountain and Vale](wiki:Kings_of_Mountain_and_Vale) from [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) claimed descent from Hugor.(wiki:Hugor_of_the_Hill.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
