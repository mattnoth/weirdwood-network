## Identity

Arryk Cargyll — Ser Arryk Cargyll, twin to Erryk, was among the Kingsguard in King's Landing and led Aegon II into the throne room.

## Origins

Ser Arryk and Ser [Erryk Cargyll](wiki:Erryk_Cargyll), knights of the Kingsguard, were both unhorsed by Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) at the [tourney for King Viserys I's accession](wiki:Tourney_for_King_Viserys_I's_accession) at [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) in [105 AC](wiki:105_AC).(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Arryk visited [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) often during the reign of King Viserys, and was very familiar with the castle.(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

According to [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), Arryk found Prince [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) and Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) in bed together, a few months after the tourney to celebrate King Viserys's and Queen [Alicent's](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) fifth anniversary in [111 AC](wiki:111_AC). [Court fool](wiki:Fool) [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) claims that Daemon was instructing his niece Rhaenyra so she could better seduce Ser Criston. This led to Viserys exiling Daemon from the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

### The Dance of the Dragons

After the death of Viserys in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), Arryk sided with King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and the [greens](wiki:Greens) faction in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). However, his twin Erryk supported Aegon's rival, Queen Rhaenyra, and her [black faction](wiki:Blacks) on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). After the coronation of Aegon in the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit), Arryk led the king into the throne room of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep).(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Arryk was in the retinue of [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle) when he traveled from King's Landing to Dragonstone in an unsuccessful attempt to reconcile the rival [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen). A [song](wiki:Farewell,_My_Brother), composed long after the fact, claims that before Arryk left Dragonstone, the brothers tried to convince each other to change sides. According to the song, when this attempt failed, they exchanged declarations of love, and parted knowing that when they would meet next, it would be as enemies. It is possible that such a farewell did indeed take place that day, but no historical records mention it.(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

After the murder of Aegon's son Prince [Jaehaerys Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_II)) by [Blood and Cheese](wiki:Blood_and_Cheese),(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Aegon wanted revenge on Rhaenyra. Ser Criston, the [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) of Aegon's Kingsguard, ordered Arryk to infiltrate Dragonstone in the guise of his twin, Erryk. It is disputed whether his mission was to kill Rhaenyra or her children, but whichever it was, Arryk did not take the assignment happily, and prayed for forgiveness from [the Mother](wiki:Mother_(the_Seven)) in the Red Keep's [sept](wiki:Sept) the night he left. Arryk arrived on [the island](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) disguised as a fisherman, changed to his Kingsguard armor and white cloak, and easily entered the castle. However, as Arryk was making his way to the royal apartments, he coincidentally encountered Erryk, who immediately understood why his twin was there.(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Accounts of the fight that followed greatly differ. Septon Eustace's [account](wiki:The_Reign_of_King_Viserys,_First_of_His_Name,_and_the_Dance_of_the_Dragons_That_Came_After) simply states that the Cargyll twins slew each other.(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) According to singers, the brothers professed their love for each other as they drew their swords.(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) Per Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun)'s *[The Dance of the Dragons, A True Telling](wiki:The_Dance_of_the_Dragons,_A_True_Telling)*, Erryk and Arryk battled for most of an hour. The noise of the fight woke half of Rhaenyra's court, but they could only watch, as none could tell which twin was which. In the end, Arryk and Erryk mortally wounded each other, and died in each other's arms, weeping.(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) And finally, per *[The Testimony of Mushroom](wiki:The_Testimony_of_Mushroom)*, the fight lasted only moments, and the brothers did not declare their love, but rather called each other [traitor](wiki:Traitor). Erryk struck the first mortal blow, nearly cutting off Arryk's arm at the shoulder. As Arryk collapsed, he grabbed Erryk's cloak and pulled him close to stab him in the belly with a dagger. Arryk was dead before anyone found them, but Erryk took four days to die from his gut wound, screaming in pain and cursing his brother the whole time.(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

## Appearances & Description

Arryk and his twin brother, Ser Erryk, were physically identical, to the point that even their fellow Kingsguards could not differentiate them.(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

[Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) recalls the stories of several legendary members of the Kingsguard, including the twins Ser Erryk and Ser Arryk, and how they killed each other during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Clash of Kings

When King [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) chides his brother Prince [Tommen](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) for crying, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) says that the twins Ser Arryk and Ser Erryk died with tears on their cheeks after each had mortally wounded the other.(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### A Storm of Swords

Sansa Stark encounters Lady [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell)'s personal guard, twins named [Erryk](wiki:Erryk_(guard)) and [Arryk](wiki:Arryk_(guard)), who are evidently [named after](wiki:Named_after) the legendary Kingsguards.(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) thinks that if Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) returns from the [siege of Dragonstone](wiki:Siege_of_Dragonstone), she will send him to deal with [Bronn](wiki:Bronn) at [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth). She hopes that each of them will kill the other, like Ser Arryk and Ser Erryk.(wiki:Arryk_Cargyll.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
