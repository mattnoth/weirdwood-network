## Origins

Following the [battle of Rook's Rest](wiki:Battle_of_Rook's_Rest) in the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Lord Walys desired to [recover](wiki:Retaking_of_Rook's_Rest) [Rook's Rest](wiki:Rook's_Rest) for the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) and Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen). Walys led a hundred [knights](wiki:Knight) from [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) and joined with soldiers from the [Crabbs](wiki:House_Crabb), [Brunes](wiki:House_Brune_of_the_Dyre_Den), and [Celtigars](wiki:House_Celtigar). They took the [greens](wiki:Greens)' garrison at Rook's Rest by surprise and recovered the castle.(wiki:Walys_Mooton.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
Afterwards, Walys led his bravest soldiers to the field west of Rook's Rest to kill the injured [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre). The attackers failed to kill the dragon, however. Three score were killed, including the charred Lord Walys, before the survivors fled. Walys was succeeded as Lord of Maidenpool by his brother, [Manfryd Mooton](wiki:Manfryd_Mooton), who found Walys's body when he arrived at the castle a fortnight later.(wiki:Walys_Mooton.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
