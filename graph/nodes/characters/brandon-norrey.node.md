## Appearances & Description

Brandon is wrinkled and slight of build, but sly-eyed and spry. He resembles an old fox clad in fur and iron.(wiki:Brandon_Norrey.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The bearded Brandon is half a head taller than [Torghen Flint](wiki:Torghen_Flint).(wiki:Brandon_Norrey.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

The Norrey believes in the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods)(wiki:Brandon_Norrey.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and distrusts [free folk](wiki:Free_folk).(wiki:Brandon_Norrey.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons

[House Norrey](wiki:House_Norrey) gives aid to King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) when the king marches to the [northern mountains](wiki:Northern_mountains) and then defeats [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) in the [fight by Deepwood Motte](wiki:Fight_by_Deepwood_Motte).(wiki:Brandon_Norrey.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) The Norrey is too old to go on campaign,(wiki:Brandon_Norrey.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) but his son, [Brandon the Younger](wiki:Brandon_Norrey_(son_of_Brandon)), joins Stannis's [march on Winterfell](wiki:March_on_Winterfell).(wiki:Brandon_Norrey.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Brandon and twelve fighting men visit [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) for [the wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Sigorn_and_Alys_Karstark) of [Alys Karstark](wiki:Alys_Karstark) and [Sigorn](wiki:Sigorn), the new [Magnar of Thenn](wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn). The Norrey also brings a [wet nurse](wiki:Wet_nurse). Brandon is skeptical of the idea of [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) to settle [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) south of the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Brandon_Norrey.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)
