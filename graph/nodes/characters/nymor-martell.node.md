## Identity

Nymor Martell — Nymor was Meria's son, sixty years old and failing, who succeeded her as Lord of Sunspear and Prince of Dorne.

## Origins

During the [War of Conquest](wiki:War_of_Conquest), [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) had defied the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen) and their [dragons](wiki:Dragon), but in [4 AC](wiki:4_AC), King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) began his campaign on Dorne, in an attempt to finalize his Conquest. This [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War) would last until [13 AC](wiki:13_AC).(wiki:Nymor_Martell.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Nymor_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf796_3-1)

In [13 AC](wiki:13_AC), Nymor's mother, Princess [Meria Martell](wiki:Meria_Martell), the Yellow Toad of Dorne, died, and Nymor inherited the rule of Dorne. Already old and failing, Nymor wanted peace. He sent a delegation, led by his daughter and heir, Princess [Deria Martell](wiki:Deria_Martell), to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), to negotiate a peace with King Aegon - a peace between two kingdoms, two equals, not a peace between a vassal and its overlord.(wiki:Nymor_Martell.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Nymor_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf796_3-2)

It is said that King Aegon was about to refuse, when Deria handed him a letter from Nymor. King Aegon read the letter on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), allegedly clenching the bladed seat so hard his hand started to bleed. Once finished, he burned the letter, and flew to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) on [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) without a word. When he returned the next morning, he agreed to the peace and signed a treaty.(wiki:Nymor_Martell.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)(wiki:Nymor_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf796_3-3)

It is unknown what content was in the letter that Nymor had written, but many have speculated. Some claim it was a simple plea that touched Aegon’s heart. Others insist it was a list of all those lords and noble knights who had lost their lives during the war. Some believe it said that Queen [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen) had survived her fall at [Hellholt](wiki:Hellholt), but was being kept captive in extreme torment, and the Dornish promised to end her suffering if Aegon agreed to end the war. Others suspect the letter stated that Nymor was willing to spend all of Dorne's [gold](wiki:Gold) to hire the [Faceless Men](wiki:Faceless_Men) of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) to assassinate Aegon's young son and heir, Prince [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), if Aegon did not sign the peace. Certain [septons](wiki:Septon) even suggest that the missive was [ensorceled](wiki:Magic).(wiki:Nymor_Martell.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)(wiki:Nymor_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf796_3-4)

The answer to those questions is unknown, but the peace that Nymor and Aegon agreed on in [13 AC](wiki:13_AC) would last throughout Aegon's entire reign.(wiki:Nymor_Martell.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)(wiki:Nymor_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf796_3-5)
