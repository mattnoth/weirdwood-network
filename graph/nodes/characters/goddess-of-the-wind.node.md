## Origins

The goddess of the wind and the [sea god](wiki:Sea_god) forbade their daughter [Elenei](wiki:Elenei)'s love with Durran. Regardless, Elenei gave her [maidenhead](wiki:Maidenhead) to Durran, thus yielding to a mortal's death. For this, the gods hated Durran and, in their wroth, sent howling winds and lashing rains on Durran's keep on his wedding night, destroying his keep and killing all his family and guests. Enraged, Duran declared war on the gods, who replied by hammering his kingdom with massive storms. Thus, each new keep Durran built was destroyed by the gods, despite being larger and more heavily fortified.(wiki:Goddess_of_the_wind.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Goddess_of_the_wind.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_stormlands:_house_durrandon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
Durran's seventh keep, however, resisted the wrath of the gods—thus earning the name of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). According to some tales it was built with the advice of a boy who would grow up to become [Bran the Builder](wiki:Bran_the_Builder), though others believe the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) took a hand in the construction.(wiki:Goddess_of_the_wind.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Goddess_of_the_wind.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_stormlands:_house_durrandon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
