## Origins

According to legends from the [Further East](wiki:Further_East), the [Blood Betrayal](wiki:Blood_Betrayal) of the [Bloodstone Emperor](wiki:Bloodstone_Emperor) began an age of darkness—which [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) call the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night)(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)—in ancient times when the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis) was still forming.(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_long_night.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) A great warrior rose to fight this darkness lying over [the world](wiki:Known_world), according to a legend which spread from [Asshai](wiki:Asshai). Followers of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) say the hero's name was Azor Ahai,(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_long_night.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) while other cultures call him Hyrkoon the Hero, Yin Tar, Neferion, and Eldric Shadowchaser.(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) Tales from [Yi Ti](wiki:Yi_Ti) speak of a heroic woman with a monkey's tail,(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_long_night.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) and legends from the [north](wiki:North) regale about a [last hero](wiki:Last_hero) who gained the aid of the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest).(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_long_night.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

Azor Ahai needed to forge a hero's sword, so he labored for thirty days and thirty nights at the sacred fires of a temple until it was done. However, when he went to temper it in water, the sword broke. He was not one to give up easily, so he started over. Azor Ahai took fifty days and fifty nights to make another sword better than the first. To temper it this time, he captured a [lion](wiki:Lion) and drove the sword into its heart, but once more the steel shattered. The third time, with a heavy heart, for he knew beforehand what he must do to finish the blade, Azor Ahai worked for a hundred days and nights until it was finished. This time, he called for his wife, [Nissa Nissa](wiki:Nissa_Nissa), and asked her to bare her breast. He drove his sword into her living heart, her soul combining with the steel of the sword, creating [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer), the Red Sword of Heroes.(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Lightbringer was henceforth always warm to the touch.(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Although he had Lightbringer, Azor Ahai did not fight alone,(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) instead leading virtuous men into battle so that the darkness could be routed.(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) The *[Jade Compendium](wiki:Jade_Compendium)* mentions that when the hero thrust the blade through a monster, the creature burst into flame.(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

According to prophecy, in ancient books of Asshai(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) from over five thousand years ago, Azor Ahai is to be reborn again as a champion sent by [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor).(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) This will occur after a long summer when an evil, cold darkness descends upon the world.(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) It is said that wielding Lightbringer once again, Azor Ahai will stand against the darkness(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) and if he fails, the world fails with him.(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

When Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) was young, he and Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) believed that Rhaegar was [the prince that was promised](wiki:The_prince_that_was_promised).(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Rawoiafazor_ahai.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) When he was older, Rhaegar came to believe that his infant son, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), fit the prophecy, since a comet was seen above [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) when the boy was born.(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

While burning statues of [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), a [red priestess](wiki:Red_priest) from [Asshai](wiki:Asshai), proclaims that [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) is Azor Ahai come again. She also declares that Stannis's sword is [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer). Afterward, [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan) tells [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) about the legends of Azor Ahai and the hero's burning sword, but the sellsail doubts the authenticity of Stannis's blade.(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

### A Storm of Swords

Melisandre continues to claim that Stannis is Azor Ahai reborn, the warrior of fire whose return was heralded by the [red comet](wiki:Red_comet).(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) She tells [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) that Stannis will wake [dragons out of stone](wiki:Stone_dragon).(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Feast for Crows

The dying Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) now believes that [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) fits the prophecy because she was born at Dragonstone amid salt and smoke, and she possesses three [dragons](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

### A Dance with Dragons

[Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), tells [Clydas](wiki:Clydas) that he read the legend of Azor Ahai in the book Maester Aemon left him, the *[Jade Compendium](wiki:Jade_Compendium)*.(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

[Benerro](wiki:Benerro) of [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) proclaims that Daenerys is Azor Ahai returned.(wiki:Azor_Ahai.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
