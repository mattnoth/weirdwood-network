## Origins

Aeron was born the youngest son from Lord [Quellon Greyjoy](wiki:Quellon_Greyjoy)'s second of three marriages. At six-and-ten he called himself a man, but in truth he had been a ribald drunk, who would sing, dance, play pipes, juggle and ride horses. He drank much, and claimed no man could piss longer or farther than him. He once wagered his ship against a flock of goats that he could douse a hearth-fire by urinating on it, and subsequently outraged his brother, Lord [Balon](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), by naming the ship *[Golden Storm](wiki:Golden_Storm)*.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5)

During [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), Aeron joined his brothers [Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) and [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) in raiding the coasts. Aeron nearly drowned when *Golden Storm* was sunk by the war galley *[Fury](wiki:Fury)* during the [sea battle off Fair Isle](wiki:Sea_battle_off_Fair_Isle), when the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) fleet was caught in a trap set by the [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships), Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon). Aeron was initially believed to be lost at sea, but he washed ashore and was discovered by local fishermen, who led him to [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) in chains. Aeron spent the rest of the rebellion as a prisoner of the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister), but he was later returned to the Iron Islands. Aeron held pissing contests with "[lions](wiki:House_Lannister), [boars](wiki:House_Crakehall), and [chickens](wiki:House_Swyft)" while in captivity.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6)

An unknown amount of time after Greyjoy's Rebellion, Aeron went down in a storm, but washed up ashore, safely and unharmed. Due to his near-death experience, he underwent a spiritual reawakening, and dedicated himself as a [priest of the Drowned God](wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God) and [prophet](wiki:Prophet).(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) He became hard, humorless, and ascetic, living as a hermit on the beach, spreading the word of [their god](wiki:Drowned_God), and becoming known primarily by his new name, *Damphair*. The acolytes serving under Aeron, called [drowned men](wiki:Drowned_men), do his bidding without question, and the Damphair is greatly respected by the ironborn in general.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-7) He has never failed to resuscitate any of his followers with the [kiss of life](wiki:Kiss_of_life).(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-8)

Aeron fears his brother, Euron, for reasons that are not made clear. He has recurring memories and nightmares of a door with screeching hinges, which he associates with Euron.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Aeron is still afflicted with guilt over the death of another brother, [Urrigon](wiki:Urrigon_Greyjoy), who died from the infection of a wound that occurred while he and Aeron performed a [finger dance](wiki:Finger_dance), a traditional axe-juggling game among the ironborn.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-9)

## Appearances & Description

Aeron is tall and thin with fierce black eyes and a beak of a nose,(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) but he is shorter than his elder brother [Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy).(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) The sour-faced(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Aeron never cuts his long black hair(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and beard, which falls to his waist and has seaweed woven into it.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

The Damphair garbs himself in roughspun wool robes dyed in the green, grey, and blue of the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God).(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) When drowning his [acolytes](wiki:Drowned_men), he wears only a [sealskin](wiki:Seal) clout to cover his privates.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Aeron carries a waterskin filled with seawater, which he uses to bless those who desire it.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

> Aeron Damphair was waiting for him in the surf … The priest was gaunt and tall, though shorter than Victarion. His nose rose like a shark's fin from a bony face, and his eyes were iron. His beard reached to his waist, and tangled ropes of hair slapped at the back of his legs when the wind blew.

— [Victarion Greyjoy](victarion-greyjoy) arriving at Nagga's Cradle, AFFC The Iron Captain I (`sources/chapters/affc/affc-the-iron-captain-01.md:53`)

Aeron was once an amiable man, fond of songs, [ale](wiki:Ale), and women.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) At the age of sixteen he sang, danced, japed, played the [pipes](wiki:Reed_pipes), juggled, and rode [horses](wiki:Horse).(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Aeron's transformation has made him devoutly devoted to the god in his watery halls, however. He is now a dour, humorless man who views every breath as a gift that is wasted if it is not used to extol the virtues of following the Drowned God's path.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rrpggreenronin.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Aeron considers himself a prophet(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) who can hear the Drowned God.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) He attributes discord in the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) to the hated [Storm God](wiki:Storm_God).(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Aeron sometimes swallows salt water to strength his faith.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

In the early phases of the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), sends his friend, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), back to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) to negotiate an alliance with Theon's father, [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands). Aeron Damphair, Balon's brother, is sent to bring Theon from [Lordsport](wiki:Lordsport) to [Pyke](wiki:Pyke). Since Theon has been living for years with the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), who worship the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods), Aeron blesses Theon with seawater.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

Balon declines Robb's alliance offer, however, and Aeron attends Balon's war council regarding an offensive against the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Aeron and [Dagmer](wiki:Dagmer) accompany Theon during the [harrying of the Stony Shore](wiki:Harrying_of_the_Stony_Shore),(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) and the Damphair blesses Theon's new longship, *[Sea Bitch](wiki:Sea_Bitch)*.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

The ironmen take [Benfred Tallhart](wiki:Benfred_Tallhart) prisoner when they ambush his [Wild Hares](wiki:Wild_Hares). When Benfred refuses to answer questions Aeron and Theon put to him, Aeron commands that the prisoner be sacrificed to the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God). Theon decides to attack [Torrhen's Square](wiki:Fight_at_Torrhen's_Square) and [Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell) with the crew of *Sea Bitch* and Dagmer's *[Foamdrinker](wiki:Foamdrinker)*, granting the six remaining ships to Aeron for future raiding.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

### A Feast for Crows

After finishing the reaving, Aeron returns to his normal duties as a [priest of the Drowned God](wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God) in the Iron Islands. Within the [Sea Tower](wiki:Sea_Tower) of [Pyke](wiki:Pyke), Aeron informs Balon of the defeat of his last son, Theon, at [Winterfell](wiki:Sack_of_Winterfell). Aeron opposes Balon's intention to be succeeded by his daughter, [Asha](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy).(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-10)

While reviving [Emmond](wiki:Emmond) with the [kiss of life](wiki:Kiss_of_life) on the shores of [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk), Aeron learns from [the Sparr](wiki:The_Sparr) and [Gormond Goodbrother](wiki:Gormond_Goodbrother) that Balon, his oldest brother, has fallen to his death from Pyke. At [Hammerhorn](wiki:Hammerhorn) he is told by Maester [Murenmure](wiki:Murenmure) that another older brother, the despised [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), has claimed the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair).(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-11) After preaching at villages and holdfasts on Great Wyk, Aeron rests in a driftwood shelter made by his [drowned men](wiki:Drowned_men) at [Pebbleton](wiki:Pebbleton) and rejects their call for him to claim the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair). Fearing that Euron Crow's Eye will be the ruin of the ironmen, Aeron blesses Lord [Meldred Merlyn](wiki:Meldred_Merlyn) and calls for a [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk) to choose Balon's replacement.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-12)

The ironborn captains answer Aeron's summons, and most of the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) returns from the [Fever River](wiki:Fever_River). On the shores of [Nagga's Cradle](wiki:Nagga's_Cradle), Aeron blesses and prays with his preferred candidate, his third brother, [Victarion](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), whom he knows will continue Balon's traditional ways.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) While the captains feast and drink, Aeron consumes only fish and water, and he departs when Euron's ridicules his piety.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) The Damphair begins the kingsmoot's proceedings and listens to the claims of Lord [Gylbert Farwynd](wiki:Gylbert_Farwynd), [Erik Ironmaker](wiki:Erik_Ironmaker), Lord [Dunstan Drumm](wiki:Dunstan_Drumm), Victarion, and Asha. He is shocked when the captains, impressed with [Euron's dragonhorn](wiki:Dragonbinder) and bold claims, instead choose the Crow's Eye as Balon's successor.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)

Aeron asks Victarion to aid him in opposing Euron, but the obedient Victarion submits to the new king. The Damphair announces plans to try to rouse the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) of the Iron Islands against the "godless" Euron, and he disappears from Old Wyk the next day.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Euron and Victarion lead the ironborn in attacking the [Reach](wiki:Reach).(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

Aeron's niece, [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy), tells [Tristifer Botley](wiki:Tristifer_Botley) that no one has seen the Damphair since the kingsmoot, but his [drowned men](wiki:Drowned_men) claim that he is hiding on [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk) and will soon come forth to call down the wrath of the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God) on Euron Crow's Eye and his minions. [Erik Ironmaker](wiki:Erik_Ironmaker), now [Lord Steward of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_Steward_of_the_Iron_Islands), searches for Aeron by hunting down his drowned men. Tris suspects that Euron slit the Damphair's throat, however.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### The Winds of Winter

Aeron is revealed to have been captured after the kingsmoot by Euron's mutes and chained up in the hold of the *[Silence](wiki:Silence)*. Euron forces him to drink [shade of the evening](wiki:Shade_of_the_evening) and Aeron has numerous nightmarish visions. Aeron remembers how Euron used to sexually abuse him and another brother, [Urri](wiki:Urrigon_Greyjoy), when they were children. On [Oakenshield](wiki:Oakenshield) Aeron encounters [Falia Flowers](wiki:Falia_Flowers). When she feeds him during his imprisonment, Falia believes Euron will make her his [salt wife](wiki:Salt_wife) and her sons will come before Euron's other baseborn children. Aeron warns her to flee but she refuses.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Forsaken_14-0)

Aeron is later taken to a captured island castle located near the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor), presumably the [Isle of Pigs](wiki:Isle_of_Pigs). Several of Euron's lieutenants feast as a dozen bodies of the castle's former occupants hang from the rafters. All take turns mocking Aeron before taking him back to the *Silence*, where Euron commands that his brother to be tied to the prow of the ship as Euron sails to meet the [Redwyne fleet](wiki:Redwyne_fleet). A pregnant and naked Falia is tied next to him, her tongue cut out.(wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Forsaken_14-1)
