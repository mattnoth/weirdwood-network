## Culture

In [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), it is common for [lords](wiki:Lord) and ladies to have paramours, chosen out of love and lust rather than political alliances. [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) are not greatly concerned if a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) is born to a paramour.(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf795_3-0) Dorne gives a degree of social status to paramours, despite the "unchaste" nature of the relationship. Outside of Dorne, a Dornish paramour's status is less certain. They may be afforded the esteem granted in their homeland by the diplomatic-minded, or they may receive treatment ranging from slight social disdain to moral condemnation.(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
That same varying treatment also applies to paramours of non-Dornish nobles, even the mistresses of kings.(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) A paramour can be of the same sex as their lover.(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Each partner in a marriage can have their own paramour, though it depends on the rank of the partners.(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Rssm1285.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
The [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) does not allow its novices to keep wives or paramours, at least not openly.(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

## Origins

When Lord [Lyonel Hightower](wiki:Lyonel_Hightower) was forbidden by the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon) from marrying his widowed step-mother, [Samantha Tarly](wiki:Samantha_Tarly), he kept her as a paramour and fathered six children on her, before the election of a new High Septon allowed their marriage. The relationship was deemed highly scandalous.(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
It was a closely held secret that Prince [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell) kept a paramour despite the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) oaths of chastity.(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) and his other sworn brothers were aware of this, but kept his secret.(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Radwd59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
After Lord [Selwyn Tarth](wiki:Selwyn_Tarth)'s wife died, he kept a new "lady" every year.(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

A degree of social deference is given to [Ellaria Sand](wiki:Ellaria_Sand) by virtue of her relationship with Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell).(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) While esteemed as a high-ranking woman by the [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen), in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) her presence at the high table at the [royal wedding](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) and [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) would be taken as an insult by the noblewomen seated near her. Ellaria is instead placed at a place of honor near the dais.(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) [Olenna Redwyne](wiki:Olenna_Redwyne) refers to her as "the [serpent](wiki:Serpent)'s [whore](wiki:Whore)",(wiki:Paramour.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) in reference to Prince Oberyn's nickname of "the Red Viper".
