## Identity

## Origins

### Early life

Aegon was the eldest son of Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), and his wife, [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare). He was born during the third moon of the year [135 AC](wiki:135_AC) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), less than a fortnight after his aunt, Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)), rode her [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Morning](wiki:Morning), for the first time. Aegon grew up during the rule of his uncle, King [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), after whom he was [named](wiki:Named_after). Septon [Bernard](wiki:Bernard) anointed him with the seven oils in the [royal sept](wiki:Royal_sept), and the bells of the city rang in celebration of his birth and following the custom a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) was placed in his cradle. His grandfather, [Lysandro Rogare](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare) even declared a day of feasting in [Lys](wiki:Lys) in his honor.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

In [139 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), his mother Larra returned to her native [Lys](wiki:Lys), where she died in [145 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-7)(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf47.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) In [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Viserys had Aegon married to his sister, Princess [Naerys](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen), with Aegon III's blessing.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf410.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Their [marriage](wiki:Marriage) was an unhappy one. Naerys had a better relationship with her second brother, Prince [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), for he knew how to make her laugh and had something of the piety she had, while Aegon did not. When Aegon and Naerys were wed in the early months of [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aemon quarreled with Aegon at the wedding feast, and Naerys wept during the [bedding](wiki:Bedding). Aegon's first child with Naerys, Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), was born on the last day of [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

As a young prince, Aegon accompanied his cousin, King [Daeron "the Young Dragon"](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen), in his [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne), as did Aegon's younger brother, Prince [Aemon "the Dragonknight"](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), who had joined the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). After the [submission of Sunspear](wiki:Submission_of_Sunspear), Aegon was tasked by King Daeron I to escort the highborn [Dornish](wiki:Dornish) hostages to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-32) One of these hostages, Lady [Cassella Vaith](wiki:Cassella_Vaith), became one of Aegon's mistresses for a few years.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-33)

When [Baelor the Blessed](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) became king after Daeron's death in Dorne, Baelor dissolved his marriage to his sister-wife, [Daena](wiki:Daena_Targaryen), and imprisoned her and his other sisters in comfortable confinement of the [Maidenvault](wiki:Maidenvault) so the sight of her would not tempt him or the men of his court to carnal thoughts. That did not stop Daena from escaping her confinement on three occasions, one time with the help of her cousin, Aegon. Daena became pregnant, refused to say who the father was, and was dubbed "Daena the Defiant" for her willfulness. In time she gave birth to Aegon's son whom she named [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre).(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-34)(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf49.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1605.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1467.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Daemon was not the first of Aegon's bastards, however. Aegon had already acknowledged multiple children by two of his four mistresses, and more children would follow. In his marriage, however, childbirth went less easily. During the years of their marriage, Princess Naerys had several difficult pregnancies. In [161 AC](wiki:161_AC), Naerys gave birth to twins who died shortly after the birth. This caused the new king, [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen), to fast for a moon's turn.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf49.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Because Naerys nearly died during this pregnancy, King Baelor sent Prince Aegon to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) on a diplomatic mission. Accounts at the time suggest it was an excuse to make certain Aegon left Naerys alone as she recovered from the failed childbirth.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-35) In [172 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), after another troubled labor, Naerys gave birth to a daughter named [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)).(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Daenerys' twin brother, however, was stillborn.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-36)

Eventually Baelor starved himself to death during one of his pious fasts, and Daena and her sisters were passed over in the succession. Aegon's father Viserys became king, but he only ruled for a year before passing away himself. So, in [172 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the throne passed to Aegon, the Fourth of His Name. Some historians suspect that the sudden death of Aegon's father, King Viserys, was [not natural](wiki:Kinslaying) and that his successor and son Aegon [poisoned](wiki:Poison) him in order to hasten his inheritance to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf410.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### Reign

Aegon IV is generally considered to be one of the worst kings in the history of Westeros, and is dubbed "Aegon the Unworthy" in the face of his excess and misrule. Aegon's behavior caused great strife at court, especially with his son [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) and Aegon's brother [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), who was then the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard).

Aegon was a decadent, corrupt ruler who indulged his passions and whims at every opportunity. Being attractive, he was popular with women both [highborn](wiki:Nobility) and [lowborn](wiki:Smallfolk). He had as many as nine mistresses and many bastards. Supposedly, he had any woman he wanted, whether they were married or not.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

Aegon's misrule started with small acts of pleasure, but in time his appetites knew no bounds and his corruption and oppression led to acts that would haunt the realm for generations to come. He filled his court not with men who were noble, wise, or honest, but with those who could flatter and amuse him. The women at court were largely those who could do the same, letting him slake his lusts upon their bodies. On his whims he gave to one House while taking from another. He deprived men of their rightful inheritance, when he desired their wealth, as rumors claim he did following the death of Lord [Ossifer Plumm](wiki:Ossifer_Plumm) during his wedding day.

Aegon gave away priceless treasures to Lords who managed to please him and for the sake of his desires: an example, on one of his many trips he gifted a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) to Lord [Butterwell](wiki:Butterwell_(Hand_of_the_King)), after guesting at his castle and allegedly impregnating his host's three maiden daughters in one night.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) Aegon used promotions of the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) as a way to shower largesse on those he most favored, and they in turn made sure that the brothels - and even the decent women of the city were made available for Aegon's lusts whenever their King desired.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

Aegon once renamed the [Teats](wiki:Teats), two hills that were disputed territories between [House Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken) and [House Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood). They were dubbed by Aegon as *Barba's Teats* in honor of his Bracken mistress, [Barba Bracken](wiki:Barba_Bracken), the mother of [Bittersteel](wiki:Aegor_Rivers). A few years later, he casually appropriated the hills from the Brackens and gifted them to the Blackwoods while renaming the hills for his new Blackwood mistress, [Melissa Blackwood](wiki:Melissa_Blackwood), the mother of [Bloodraven](wiki:Brynden_Rivers). The Blackwoods still call them *Missy's Teats*, while the Brackens call them *Barba's Teats*.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

To the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), Aegon's reign might have been a source of gossip and amusement. To the [lords](wiki:Lord) of the realm who did not stay at his court, and who did not wish Aegon to take liberties with their daughters, he might have seemed strong and decisive, frivolous, but largely harmless. Those who were actually at court (among them Aegon's brother [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II))) saw him for what he truly was. Aegon was too mercurial, too greedy and too cruel to be anything other than dangerous.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-37)

From a young age, Aegon indulged himself with women and continued to do so after his marriage, even during his own reign. He quite openly flaunted his mistresses at court, to the distress of his wife. [Naerys Targaryen](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen) was the only woman Aegon took no pleasure in bedding; he did not love her as she was pious, gentle and frail, everything Aegon loathed. According to Grand Maester [Alford](wiki:Alford), after the birth of Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), he warned Aegon that a second pregnancy could kill Naerys. After giving Aegon an heir, Naerys begged him, "Let us live henceforth as brother and sister." Aegon refused, saying "that is what we are doing", and insisted she still perform her "wifely duties" for the rest of her life.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-38)

Aegon's treatment of Naerys inflamed matters between Aegon and his brother, Aemon. As children, Aemon and Naerys had been inseparable. Aegon's resentment of his younger noble, famed, and celebrated brother was plain for all to see, most likely because Aemon was everything Aegon was not.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-39)

When Queen Naerys was accused of adultery and treason by the knight Ser [Morgil Hastwyck](wiki:Morgil_Hastwyck), Prince Aemon defended his sister's honor in [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) and slew Morgil. This event became famous and inspired many songs, stories, and fables by bards, furthering Prince Aemon's renown, much to King Aegon's annoyance. According to the writings of Maester [Kaeth](wiki:Kaeth) in *[Lives of Four Kings](wiki:Lives_of_Four_Kings)*, it was Aegon who secretly started the rumors of Naerys's adultery and used Morgil to instigate this tale, though at the time Aegon denied this. Strangely enough, there were no known rumors spread about the parentage of [Princess Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)), only about [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen); these accusations also coincidentally started when Aegon and his heir, Prince Daeron, were quarreling. Daeron opposed Aegon's plan for an unprovoked war on [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). The king ignored Daeron's protests, he built a massive fleet, and in [174 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) sent it to launch an invasion by landing on the Dornish coast. The fleet was scattered en route and destroyed by a vicious storm, however.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-40)

In [178 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aegon caught one of his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) knights, Ser [Terrence Toyne](wiki:Terrence_Toyne), sleeping with one of his mistresses, Lady [Bethany Bracken](wiki:Bethany_Bracken). Even though they proclaimed love, Aegon had them both executed.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Terrence was dismembered piece by piece, while Bethany was forced to watch before meeting her own death.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Aegon also had Bethany's father, [Lord Bracken](wiki:Lord_Bracken), who had once served as his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), executed just for spite.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-41) This action led to an assassination attempt against King Aegon by Terrence Toyne's brothers, who desired revenge. Prince [Aemon the Dragonknight](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), who despite their conflicts and Aegon's hatred and disrespect towards him, sacrificed his own life to protect Aegon and saved him from the Toyne brothers' assassination attempt.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) Aegon's wife, Naerys, died in childbirth a year later in [179 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), along with the child. Aegon did little to honor either his brother's or his wife's memories. After the death of both his siblings, Aegon began to make barely veiled references to his son's alleged illegitimacy - something he only dared now because both his wife and the Dragonknight were dead.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-42)

[Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre), the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword of [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), was traditionally carried by the Targaryen kings who succeeded him. Aegon IV, however, gave Blackfyre not to his heir, Daeron, but to one of his bastards, [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), when he knighted Daemon at the age of 12, formally recognizing him as his son. Talk of Daemon becoming Aegon's heir instead of Daeron began after this point.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-43)

Aegon betrothed Daemon to the daughter of the [Archon](wiki:Archon) of [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), a girl named [Rohanne](wiki:Rohanne_of_Tyrosh). This match, however, was not one of Daemon's wishes. It was claimed he wanted to wed his half-sister, Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)). King Aegon, however, refused this, and it was believed that Aegon saw more profit in making ties with Tyrosh, as to have the aid of the Tyroshi fleet available, should he want to make another attempt to conquer Dorne.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)

Other sources claim that Daemon did not mind the marriage to Rohanne, as he had believed he would be allowed to follow the example of Aegon the Conqueror and [Maegor the Cruel](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) and have more than one wife. Some of the [Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre) loyalists would later claim that Aegon IV even promised this to Daemon. Daemon's half-brother Daeron, on the other hand, had different views on the matters, and after his own coronation, made certain that the [dowry](wiki:Dowry) for Daemon's wedding to Rohanne was paid.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)

In the last few years of Aegon's corrupt reign, his heir, Daeron, became one of the biggest obstacles to Aegon's misrule. While some lords saw opportunity in the gluttonous, corpulent king who could easily be convinced to part with honors, offices and treasures for a chance at pleasure, many others who condemned the king's behavior flocked to Daeron. Aegon, despite all the threats, japes and disparities he heaped upon his son, never formally disowned Daeron. Accounts differ as to why, the most likely explanation being that Aegon knew that his hold on his throne would not be secure if he disowned his son. It would mean civil war, as many lords who were sickened by Aegon's depravity would defend Daeron's rights. Chief amongst them were [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell), due to the fact Daeron was married to Princess [Myriah Martell](wiki:Myriah_Martell) of [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear). Aegon tried to use the hatred of the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) and the [Reach](wiki:Reach) toward [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) to his advantage. Aegon decided to go ahead with another plan to invade Dorne, which led to an even greater folly then his first attempted invasion.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-44)

Like many other Targaryen kings, Aegon was obsessed with regaining [dragons](wiki:Dragon) for his house. Instead of trying to resurrect the dead dragons of his ancestors, Aegon commanded the [pyromancers](wiki:Alchemists'_Guild) to "build me dragons". Hundreds of men died while trying to operate the alchemists' seven [mechanical "dragons"](wiki:Dragons_(mechanical)) with jets of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire), destroying a quarter of the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood). Aegon never spoke of Dorne again.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-45)(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### Death

The reign of the unworthy monarch finally ended in early [184 AC](wiki:184_AC).(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-46)(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-20) At only forty-nine years of age, he had become so morbidly obese he could not walk anymore, making many wonder how his last mistress could endure his embrace. Aegon died a horrible death, his bloated body so swollen that he could not even lift himself from his couch that became covered in his feces. Aegon's limbs were rotting and crawling in hosts of flesh worms, and the [maesters](wiki:Maester) said they had never seen the like of this before. The [septons](wiki:Septons), however, pronounced it a judgment of the gods. He was given the [milk of the poppy](wiki:Milk_of_the_poppy) to try to dull the pain but nothing else could be done.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-47)

Aegon's last decree before his death was bitter poison that would lay the seeds to generations of war, bloodshed, death, and woe to the realm. Aegon legitimized all his bastard children, causing five generations of strife as the [Blackfyre Pretenders](wiki:Blackfyre_Pretenders) tried to claim the Iron Throne.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-48)(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

## Appearances & Description

Aegon began his reign when he was young, vigorous, robust, and handsome but ended it old, corrupt and morbidly obese. By the end of it he was bloated and fat. His eyes were almost lost in the fat of his face, his legs too weak to support his belly. He had a small mouth and a large beard used in an attempt to cover the fat of his neck and face.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1253.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) On his [coinage](wiki:Currency), he was depicted with a beard.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

Aegon coveted the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) as a boy. As a prince he was handsome, skilled with lance and sword. He loved to hunt, hawk, and dance. At court he was the brightest prince who dazzled [lords](wiki:Lord) with his wit. He had one major flaw, however: he could not rule himself. His lusts, gluttony and desires ruled him.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf411.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-31)

Aegon dressed in silk and satin, bright and rich, and wore rings and chains of gold and gemstones. His [crown](wiki:Crown) was of red gold, huge and heavy, each of its points a [dragon](wiki:Dragon)'s head with gemstone eyes.(wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1253.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
