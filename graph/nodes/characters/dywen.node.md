## Appearances & Description

Dywen has a leathery, wrinkled face and gnarled hands.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Racok34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) The old ranger has oaken teeth which fit poorly.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) He sometimes sucks on them,(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) and when he laughs the wooden teeth clack.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Dywen is well renowned for his tracking ability within the Night's Watch,(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Rasosprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and he claims he can smell when snow is coming.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

When [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) and [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) go to the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest) to swear their vows, Dywen is among the rangers who escort them. He is the first to notice the human hand that [Ghost](wiki:Ghost) found.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Dywen accompanies [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont) when the [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) goes to look at the corpses of [Othor](wiki:Othor) and [Jafer Flowers](wiki:Jafer_Flowers) in the forest. Dywen fears the bodies were left as a warning, and he notices that Othor now has blue eyes.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Ragot52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Dywen and [Hake](wiki:Hake) unsuccessfully search for the missing [Benjen Stark](wiki:Benjen_Stark).(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### A Clash of Kings

According to Dywen, giant [bears](wiki:Bear) can be found [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall). Lord Commander Mormont doubts this.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Dywen is among [the great ranging](wiki:The_great_ranging), the two hundred brothers of the Night's Watch that leave [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) to search for the [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings). He informs Jon that the woods and villages north of the Wall are empty.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Racok13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The experienced ranger warns Jon and [Grenn](wiki:Grenn) about [Craster](wiki:Craster), and advises the younger members of the Watch to stay away from [Craster's wives](wiki:Craster's_wives). Grenn disbelieves Dywen's claim that he slept with three of the wives in the night.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

While camped out on the [Fist of the First Men](wiki:Fist_of_the_First_Men), Dywen senses the smell of cold, to the disbelief of his brothers with the exception of Jon, who also understands the cold as a sign of [wights](wiki:Wights) and the [Others](wiki:Others).(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Racok34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### A Storm of Swords

Because of their excellent tracking abilities, Dywen and [Bannen](wiki:Bannen) are to be killed by [Lark](wiki:Lark) and his cousins as part of [Chett](wiki:Chett)'s conspiracy against Lord Commander Mormont.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Rasosprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Before the plot can begin, however, the encamped men of the Watch are attacked by [wights](wiki:Wights).(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Rasosprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Dywen guides packhorses during the retreat from the [fight at the Fist](wiki:Fight_at_the_Fist).(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

Once at [Craster's Keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep), Dywen is one of the few men that takes [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) seriously when he tells the men he slew an [Other](wiki:Others) with his [dragonglass](wiki:Dragonglass) dagger. Dywen joins [Dolorous Edd](wiki:Eddison_Tollett) in making Sam and Grenn tell the Lord Commander the tale.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Dywen stays loyal to the Watch during the [mutiny at Craster's Keep](wiki:Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep), in which Lord Commander Mormont is slain. He is one of the few survivors of the ranging to make it all the way back to Castle Black.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

Dywen jokes about Jon's age when the youth is chosen as the new Lord Commander.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Dywen and [Kedge Whiteye](wiki:Kedge_Whiteye) report that two of the [queen's men](wiki:Queen's_men), Ser [Richard Horpe](wiki:Richard_Horpe) and Ser [Justin Massey](wiki:Justin_Massey), rode south along the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad) from Castle Black.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Dywen is one of the most outspoken rangers against the proposal to seal off the gates and other entrances of the [Wall](wiki:Wall) to prevent the [Others](wiki:Others) and more [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) from passing. He argues threats like [Tormund Giantsbane](wiki:Tormund_Giantsbane) and the [Weeper](wiki:Weeper) would either cross the [Bridge of Skulls](wiki:Bridge_of_Skulls) or pass through an unseen gate, trapping the Night's Watch unnecessarily. He rallies most of the rangers under Jon, whereas the builders and stewards side with [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh).(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Lord Commander Snow appoints Dywen as the leader of one of three groups of three rangers sent ranging.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Radwd28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) His group includes Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne). [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) warns Jon soon after their departure that in her visions she has seen three of the nine rangers return eyeless, weeping blood.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Radwd28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

When the decapitated heads of the group led by [Black Jack Bulwer](wiki:Black_Jack_Bulwer) turn up north of Castle Black, [Owen the Oaf](wiki:Owen_the_Oaf) inquiries if Dywen was among the casualties. Jon asks Melisandre if she has also seen the surviving six rangers, including Dywen, in her visions. Melisandre reveals she has not seen their fates just yet.(wiki:Dywen.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
