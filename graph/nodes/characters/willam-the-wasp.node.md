## Origins

Willam was a [sworn sword](wiki:Sworn_sword) to Lord [Myles Smallwood](wiki:Myles_Smallwood) of [Acorn Hall](wiki:Acorn_Hall). In [49 AC](wiki:49_AC), he was one of the five champions in the [War for the White Cloaks](wiki:War_for_the_White_Cloaks) and was given the white cloak of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Willam_the_Wasp.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

When rumors reached [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) that [Balerion the Black Dread](wiki:Balerion) had been sighted in the [Velvet Hills](wiki:Velvet_Hills) north of [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), King [Jaehaerys I](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) sent a dozen men under the command of Willam to investigate. A guide who claimed to know where the beast was offered to escort Willam and his party to the dragon's lair. In truth, [brigands](wiki:Outlaws) had been spreading rumors of a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) in the area in order to turn others away. Willam and his men were led into a trap by the guide and ambushed. Willam was the last of his party to die and it was said he gave a good account of himself. An agent of Lord [Rego Draz](wiki:Rego_Draz), the [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin), managed to retrieve the knight's head, confirming Willam's death. Lord Smallwood, the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), wanted Pentos to be punished, but Jaehaerys declined.(wiki:Willam_the_Wasp.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
