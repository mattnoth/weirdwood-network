## Origins

When Lord [Manfryd Mooton](wiki:Manfryd_Mooton) received a letter from Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), ordering him to execute her husband [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen)'s [paramour](wiki:Paramour) [Nettles](wiki:Nettles), Maester Norren is among those who he asked for their counsel. When Lord Manfryd, deeply disturbed by the request to break the [sacred laws of hospitality](wiki:Guest_right), expressed how he wished he had never read the letter, Norren suggested that "*Mayhaps you never did.*"(wiki:Norren_(House_Mooton).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Norren later showed Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) and Nettles the letter, claiming he had not shown it to anyone else. Daemon called him a bad maester but a good man, and requested that Norren not say a word to anyone until the next day. The following morning, both Nettles and Daemon departed [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool). After they were gone, Norren went to Lord Manfryd, asking him to take his maester's chain off his neck, bind his hands with it, and to deliver him to Rhaenyra for allowing a traitor to escape. Lord Manfryd replied "*Keep your chain. We are all traitors here.*" Maidenpool declared for King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) that night, forsaking Rhaenyra's cause.(wiki:Norren_(House_Mooton).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
