## Origins

Ser Clarence's seat was called the [Whispers](wiki:Whispers), so named due to the disembodied heads that he would collect and bring home to his wife, who would raise them from the dead. The dead heads would give him counsel, and talk among themselves in a whisper. Ser Clarence collected the heads from a variety of people, including [pirates](wiki:Pirate), [lords](wiki:Lord), [wizards](wiki:Magic), [knights](wiki:Knight), and even one king from [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale).(wiki:Clarence_Crabb.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Clarence is also said to have fought the [squisher](wiki:Squisher) king.(wiki:Clarence_Crabb.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Appearances & Description

According to legend, Ser Clarence was eight feet tall and strong enough to uproot trees and throw them half a mile. He was so heavy that only an [aurochs](wiki:Aurochs) could carry him.(wiki:Clarence_Crabb.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

[Dick Crabb](wiki:Dick_Crabb) and [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) discuss the legendary heroes of their homelands, Clarence and Ser [Galladon of Morne](wiki:Galladon_of_Morne).(wiki:Clarence_Crabb.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Brienne is taken into the captivity of the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) after fighting [Rorge](wiki:Rorge) and [Biter](wiki:Biter) at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn). The fevered woman dreams of fighting Clarence at the [Whispers](wiki:Whispers); the huge man's teeth are filed into points (like Biter), and he rides a shaggy aurochs. Brienne is without [Oathkeeper](wiki:Oathkeeper) in the dream, and Clarence strikes off her head.(wiki:Clarence_Crabb.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
