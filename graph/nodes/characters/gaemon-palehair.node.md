## Identity

Gaemon Palehair, the "Cunny King," was a boy who had just turned five and was routed on Visenya's Hill.

## Origins

### Dance of the Dragons

Gaemon was the son of [Essie](wiki:Essie), a [prostitute](wiki:Prostitute) at the [House of Kisses](wiki:House_of_Kisses) on the [Street of Silk](wiki:Street_of_Silk). During the [riot of King's Landing](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), the whores at the brothel claimed that Gaemon was the [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), who had disappeared after the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) to [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), and they raised the four-year-old boy as king. The women bestowed their favors free of charge to any man who swore his sword to the supposed monarch.(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

During the [Moon of the Three Kings](wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings), after Rhaenyra's escape from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Gaemon gathered thousands of followers. Whores, [mummers](wiki:Mummers), and thieves made up his court atop [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill), and his supporters in the streets included gangs, [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword), and drunkards.(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Gaemon's mother began calling herself Lady Esselyn.(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Edicts issued in Gaemon's name included that:(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf795_7-0)(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

- Girls should henceforth be equal with boys in matters of inheritance.
- The poor should be given bread and beer in times of famine.
- Men who lost limbs in war must be fed and housed afterward by whichever [lord](wiki:Lord) they had been fighting for when the loss took place.
- Husbands who beat their wives should themselves be beaten, regardless of the motive for the chastisement.

Gaemon's edicts were considered outrageous, and it is believed they were the work of Essie's [Dornish](wiki:Dornishmen) paramour, [Sylvenna Sand](wiki:Sylvenna_Sand).(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf795_7-1)(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) [Trystane Truefyre](wiki:Trystane_Truefyre), a rival pretender, countered Gaemon's edicts by allowing [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) to hunt in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood).(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

After the [death of Rhaenyra](wiki:Flight_to_Dragonstone) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End), took the capital and the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). He and his knights rode against Visenya's Hill, where the court of the "Cunny King" was gathered, and routed the boy king's followers from three directions. A crying and chained Gaemon, who had celebrated his fifth [nameday](wiki:Nameday) only two days earlier, was taken to the Red Keep slung over the back of a horse. Behind him were Essie, who held Sylvenna's hand, and the remnants of his court, including whores, witch women, criminals, and drunkards. Also defeated and imprisoned to await the return of King Aegon II and his judgement were Trystane and the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd).(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Gaemon was spared by King Aegon on account of his age, and the boy was made a [ward](wiki:Ward) of the Crown. Essie confessed under torture that the boy's true father had been a silver-haired [Lysene](wiki:Lys) oarsman and not Aegon. Essie, Sylvenna, and twenty-seven other members of Gaemon's court were hanged from the battlements of the Red Keep. Aegon ordered that the decrees issued by Gaemon and Trystane be publicly burned.(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Aegon the Elder was eventually killed by [poison](wiki:Poison), and the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) passed to [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen).(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

### Regency of Aegon III

Gaemon was taken into the royal household, befriending young King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). During the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf), Gaemon was Aegon's only companion when he was confined to [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast).(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) reported that Gaemon was the sullen king's only friend,(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and the [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) believed that Gaemon reminded Aegon of his missing younger brother, Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) When [his regents](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) asked if he had a preferred heir, Aegon suggested his [cupbearer](wiki:Cupbearer), as Gaemon had "been a king before".(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

After Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) became [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s new [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms), Ser [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long), recommended that Gaemon, by now also Aegon's [food taster](wiki:Food_taster), become the king's [whipping boy](wiki:Whipping_boy). Gareth punished Gaemon harshly whenever Aegon failed to met Gareth's standards. Although Aegon improved at arms, he resented Gareth's cruelty toward his friend.(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

When Aegon chose [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon) at the [Maiden's Day Ball](wiki:Maiden's_Day_Ball), the king allowed his cupbearer to announce the selection.(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) Munkun reports that Queen Daenaera and Gaemon began to be neglected by Aegon, however, after the unexpected return of Prince Viserys.(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

### Death

In [135 AC](wiki:135_AC) during the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring), Gaemon and Queen [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon) began complaining of stomach pains. Although Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) was able to save the queen's life with a purgative, he was too late to save Gaemon, who quickly died of [poison](wiki:Poison). The [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) brought kitchen servants to the [Lord Confessor](wiki:Lord_Confessor), Lord [George Graceford](wiki:George_Graceford), for questioning and torture, but their admissions conflicted. King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) was left inconsolable by his friend's death.(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf47.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

During the [secret siege](wiki:Secret_siege), Aegon angrily refused Ser [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long), as the master-at-arms had often beaten his late friend.(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Septon [Bernard](wiki:Bernard) announced that Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan), once the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), had poisoned Gaemon and Daenaera with the [tears of Lys](wiki:Tears_of_Lys) in an attempt to assassinate Aegon, but the king recognized that Thaddeus had been tortured into making false confessions. After the coup against Aegon defeated, Lord [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly), the new Hand, sat in judgement against those accused of poisoning Gaemon and conspiring against the king.(wiki:Gaemon_Palehair.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
