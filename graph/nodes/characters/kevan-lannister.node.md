## Origins

### Youth

Kevan was a confidant and companion for his elder brother, [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) since childhood.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Recognizing the competency of Tywin at an early age, Kevan did his best to realize his brother's wishes, though this meant perpetually living in his shadow.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) In contrast, Tywin had stormy relations with his other brothers, [Tygett](wiki:Tygett_Lannister) and [Gerion](wiki:Gerion_Lannister).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) sent young Kevan to [Castamere](wiki:Castamere) to serve as a [page](wiki:Page) and then [squire](wiki:Squire) to Lord [Roger Reyne](wiki:Roger_Reyne).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) Ser Tywin, Kevan, and Tygett fought in the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings), during which Kevan was [knighted](wiki:Knight) by Lord Reyne.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)

At sixteen, Kevan visited the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) on his [coming of age](wiki:Coming_of_age) tour.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### Reyne-Tarbeck Revolt

Upon the return to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), Ser Tywin charged Kevan with the task of ridding the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) of [robber knights](wiki:Robber_knight) and bandit outlaws who had taken advantage of the weak rule of their father, Lord Tytos.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) Tywin also had Kevan collect the outstanding debts owed to Casterly Rock, and those who could not pay immediately had to surrender a family member as a hostage till the debt was settled. As Ser [Harys Swyft](wiki:Harys_Swyft) was not able to pay at the time, he handed his daughter [Dorna](wiki:Dorna_Swyft) into Kevan's custody.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Kevan would eventually fall in love with Dorna(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) and marry her by [266 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

In contrast to Ser Harys, Lord [Roger Reyne](wiki:Roger_Reyne) refused to pay and Lord [Walderan Tarbeck](wiki:Walderan_Tarbeck) sought out Lord Tytos to convince him to countermand Tywin's actions. Tywin had Walderan imprisoned, however, and in response Lady [Ellyn Tarbeck](wiki:Ellyn_Tarbeck) kidnapped [Stafford Lannister](wiki:Stafford_Lannister). Tywin refused to be part of Tytos's negotiations with Ser [Reynard Reyne](wiki:Reynard_Reyne), so Tytos had Kevan exchange Walderan for Stafford at [Castamere](wiki:Castamere).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)

According to a semi-canon source, Kevan aided Tywin in suppressing the [Reyne-Tarbeck revolt](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_revolt) in [261 AC](wiki:261_AC). Ellyn laughed at Kevan when he demanded the surrender of [Tarbeck Hall](wiki:Tarbeck_Hall).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-sample_17-0) Ellyn was slain when Tywin attacked the castle, however.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3)

### Lord Tywin

Ser Kevan has had four children with his wife: [Lancel](wiki:Lancel_Lannister), twin boys [Martyn](wiki:Martyn_Lannister) and [Willem](wiki:Willem_Lannister), and a daughter, [Janei](wiki:Janei_Lannister).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

[Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) died in [267 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), making [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) the new [Lord of Casterly Rock](wiki:Lord_of_Casterly_Rock). Kevan was Tywin's closest confidant, sharing all of his counsels.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) As a second son, Kevan became a [household knight](wiki:Household_knight) and served his older brother, though this meant he held no lands of his own. Kevan did hold certain incomes and was frequently rewarded by Tywin for his good service despite his lack of status, and he set aside chests of coin from this and his generous inheritance from his late father, Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

In [274 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne) and her children, [Oberyn](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) and [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), came to Casterly Rock to see Lady [Joanna Lannister](wiki:Joanna_Lannister). Unfortunately, their visit came shortly after Joanna died giving birth to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister). Tywin, in his grief, placed Kevan in charge of entertaining their [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) guests.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Kevan is remembered by his nephew, [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), as being a stout man with a sword in his youth.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) He fought in a melee at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), but was thrown from his horse by [Thoros of Myr](wiki:Thoros_of_Myr), whose flaming sword terrified his horse.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Kevan has visited [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Kevan has two hundred knights under his command, with gold to hire [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword) and influence to double his numbers.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6) One of his sons, [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister), squires for King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

## Appearances & Description

Kevan is a big man with broad shoulders and a thick waist.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) His short blond hair(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) is balding,(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and he has a close-cropped, yellow beard that follows the line of his massive jaw.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Kevan has green eyes and fair skin.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) A portly man,(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) Kevan is not necessarily considered handsome.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

Kevan is devoted to his elder brother, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) thinks her uncle is tireless, prudent, and reliable,(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) and [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) thinks Kevan is solid and steady.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Kevan considers the [Vale mountain clans](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans) to be savages.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

Despite not being a [lord](wiki:Lord), just a [household knight](wiki:Household_knight), Kevan's strategic thinking, significant retinue, and personal wealth has made him a man of significant standing.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Kevan dresses near as splendidly as Tywin,(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) clasping his crimson wool cloak with a golden lion's head brooch.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Kevan also owns a quilted wool doublet the color of charcoal.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) He wields a [sword](wiki:Sword).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Ser Kevan's eldest son, [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister), provides [strongwine](wiki:Strongwine)(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) to King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) as his squire during a hunt in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), and the king is mortally wounded while drunkenly hunting [boar](wiki:Boar).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Kevan accompanies his elder brother, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), when war breaks out in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) after Robert's death. The [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) capture [Raventree](wiki:Fall_of_Raventree_Hall) and [Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

At the [battle on the Green Fork](wiki:Battle_on_the_Green_Fork), Kevan commands the center, a large mixed force. Tywin intends the left flank to break and for Kevan to wheel around to crush the advancing [northmen](wiki:North) against the [Green Fork](wiki:Green_Fork), but the cautious northern commander, Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), withdraws rather than fall into this trap. Following their victory, the Lannisters discover that [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) was not present in the battle.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

While the Lannister army is hurrying south, Robb captures Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), Tywin's eldest son, and [Willem Lannister](wiki:Willem_Lannister), one of Kevan's twin sons, in the [battle in the Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood),(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) and Robb then relieves [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) with the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps). When word reaches the Lannisters at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn), Kevan defends Jaime's actions to Ser [Harys Swyft](wiki:Harys_Swyft).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Tywin orders Kevan to command Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane), Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch), and [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat) to lead their reavers in raiding the riverlands, while Tywin leads the rest of their host to Harrenhal.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1)

### A Clash of Kings

After the deaths of Ser [Cedric Payne](wiki:Cedric_Payne) and Ser [Lorimer the Belly](wiki:Lorimer_the_Belly), Kevan takes charge of [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne)(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) and sends him to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to squire for his nephew, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), Kevan's niece and Robert's widow, has [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister) granted [knighthood](wiki:Knight)(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) and begins sleeping with her cousin.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) [Martyn Lannister](wiki:Martyn_Lannister), Kevan's other twin son, is captured by [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), now the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), in the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

Kevan accompanies Tywin when the Lannisters depart Harrenhal.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) They attempt to force their way back to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), but they are repelled at the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork) by Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) in the [Battle of the Fords](wiki:Battle_of_the_Fords).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) The Lannisters then join the [Tyrells](wiki:House_Tyrell) and defeat [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), though Ser Lancel is wounded in the fighting.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) After the battle, Kevan announces some of the rewards for the victors on behalf of King Joffrey in the [throne room](wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep)) of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) Tywin has Lancel named [Lord of Darry](wiki:Lord_of_Darry)(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1) per Kevan's request.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7)(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

### A Storm of Swords

King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) appoints Ser Kevan to his [small council](wiki:Small_council) as [master of laws](wiki:Master_of_laws).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) Kevan pays off and dismisses the remaining guards of his wounded nephew, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister),(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) and the uncle warmly greets Tyrion when he rejoins the council.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) Kevan is concerned for Lancel, who was wounded at the Blackwater.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-1) During [the wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Tyrion_Lannister_and_Sansa_Stark) of Tyrion and [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), Kevan dances with Lady [Janna Fossoway](wiki:Janna_Fossoway) and Sansa.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

After [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) releases Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) from captivity at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), Lord [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark) murders [Willem Lannister](wiki:Willem_Lannister) and [Tion Frey](wiki:Tion_Frey), two of Robb's prisoners, to avenge Jaime's killing of [Torrhen](wiki:Torrhen_Karstark) and [Eddard Karstark](wiki:Eddard_Karstark) in the [Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) With Willem now dead, Martyn still a captive, and Lancel weakened, Kevan becomes consumed by worry and grief. As a result, Tywin relies on Tyrion more than he would prefer.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) Robb agrees to exchange Martyn for [Robett Glover](wiki:Robett_Glover).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

Kevan is present when news of the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding) is received. He is appalled by Joffrey's statement that he wants the head of Robb so he can serve it to his sister, Sansa, at his own wedding to [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), a request that Tywin refuses to grant.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

Kevan gives Joffrey a magnificent red leather jousting saddle for his wedding.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) He escorts Lady [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell) during the wedding procession and is pleased at the wedding feast when Sansa praises Lancel's valor.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) Joffrey [chokes to death](wiki:Purple_Wedding) during the feast,(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-1) and Tyrion is accused of regicide and [kinslaying](wiki:Kinslaying).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Kevan reluctantly believes that his nephew is guilty, although Tyrion denies involvement. ### A Feast for Crows

When the body of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) is discovered, Ser Kevan is one of the first to be notified of his brother's death. He prays by the bedside of his brother,(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) shaken and wearied by the murder. He attends Tywin's funeral in the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8) [Lancel](wiki:Lancel_Lannister), contrite for his involvement in King [Robert's](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) death and for having slept with his cousin [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister),(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) prays with the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Tyrion's))(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9) and confesses his sins.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-1)

Cersei, the Queen Regent, offers her uncle the position of [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Kevan doubts Cersei's leadership, however, and he reveals Tywin informed him of his plan to send her back to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) as Lady of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) with a new husband before his death. Kevan states that he will only accept the position if Cersei names him [regent](wiki:Regent) for King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) as well as Hand and she returns to the Rock. He also advises choosing Lord [Mathis Rowan](wiki:Mathis_Rowan) or Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) as Hand of the King instead. Cersei refuses Kevan's demands, however, and throws wine in his face when he insults how she raised Joffrey. Kevan insinuates to Cersei(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-10) and later Jaime(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) that he is aware of the [incest](wiki:Incest) between the twins.

Cersei appoints two Lannister cousins in positions for which Kevan was better suited; [Daven](wiki:Daven_Lannister) is made [Warden of the West](wiki:Warden_of_the_West) and [Damion](wiki:Damion_Lannister) is named [castellan](wiki:Castellan) of Casterly Rock. Kevan is angered that Daven received the title of warden, though he bears Daven no ill will as he knows Daven never asked for the position. At [Tommen's wedding to Margaery](wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell), Cersei suggests that Kevan hunt down the [Hound](wiki:Hound), but he rebuffs her.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

Kevan heads the escort—one hundred crossbowmen, three hundred men-at-arms, and the lords of the west—that is to return Tywin's body to the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) to be buried in Casterly Rock.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2) Cersei appoints Ser [Harys Swyft](wiki:Harys_Swyft), as Hand of the King. Harys is the father of [Dorna Swyft](wiki:Dorna_Swyft), Kevan's wife, so Cersei keeps him close as a threat against Kevan.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

Kevan helps his son Lancel restore [Castle Darry](wiki:Darry), the estate Kevan had won for him.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-3) He arranges for Lancel to wed [Amerei Frey](wiki:Amerei_Frey), who has [Darry](wiki:House_Darry) ancestry, so that the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) will more easily accept [House Lannister of Darry](wiki:House_Lannister_of_Darry).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-4)(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-2) The now-pious Lancel quarrels with his father, however, and Kevan departs after the wedding. Lancel eventually renounces Darry so that he can join the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons),(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-3) as Cersei has allowed the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) to reform.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)

A brooding Kevan spends three days at the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun) while returning west, but he is distant to Daven.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3) Sent by Cersei to pacify the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) and resolve Riverun, Jaime discusses Kevan with his aunt, [Genna Lannister](wiki:Genna_Lannister), the sister of Kevan and Tywin. Genna mentions that Kevan's dutiful nature is evident in Jaime, the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4)

After Cersei is imprisoned by the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), Harys and Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) send a raven to Casterly Rock offering Kevan the regency.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Lord [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) is brought before Lord [Godric Borrell](wiki:Godric_Borrell) at [Breakwater](wiki:Breakwater). Since Kevan is not in control of King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), Godric allows Davos to continue on to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)

Kevan accepts the role of [regent](wiki:Regent) to Tommen, returns to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), and sets about trying to fix the mess that [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) made. As a concession to [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell), Kevan names Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Paxter Redwyne](wiki:Paxter_Redwyne) as [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships), and Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) as [master of laws](wiki:Master_of_laws).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

Kevan visits the imprisoned Cersei and confronts her about her affair with his son, [Lancel](wiki:Lancel_Lannister). He tells her that regardless of the outcome of her trial, her rule is now at an end.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) After Cersei confesses to having slept with the [Kettleblack](wiki:House_Kettleblack) brothers,(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) Kevan sends [Osmund](wiki:Osmund_Kettleblack) and [Osfryd](wiki:Osfryd_Kettleblack) to the dungeons.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) In order to appease the [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow) and redeem the shame to [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister), inspired by what Tywin did to [their father's mistress](wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress),(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) Kevan has Cersei perform a [walk of atonement](wiki:Walk_of_atonement) from the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). Kevan has [Jocelyn Swyft](wiki:Jocelyn_Swyft) cover the nude Cersei when she arrives at the castle.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

Kevan discusses with the [small council](wiki:Small_council) how to deal with [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) and his "feigned dragon", [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(Young_Griff)), who have [invaded the stormlands](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company) with the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company). Kevan sends his good-father, Ser [Harys Swyft](wiki:Harys_Swyft), to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) to negotiate the crown's financial problems with the [Iron Bank](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos). He is suspicious of Ser [Robert Strong](wiki:Robert_Strong), the new member of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) produced by [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn).(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4) He later has supper with the freed Cersei and King Tommen.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5)

After dinner, Kevan is told by a servant that [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) is seeking him, and in the [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester)'s chamber Kevan finds a [white raven](wiki:White_raven) announcing winter's arrival. [Varys](wiki:Varys) suddenly [shoots](wiki:Assassinations_of_Pycelle_and_Kevan_Lannister) Kevan in the chest with a crossbow, and the wounded Kevan notices that Pycelle has already been slain. Varys explains he bears Kevan no malice, but he cannot allow Kevan to undo Cersei's misrule. With his death by crossbow, Cersei will believe that Kevan was murdered by [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) or House Tyrell. The Tyrells, in turn, will suspect Cersei herself, undermining the alliances shoring up Tommen's hold on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). This would open the way for Prince Aegon to take the throne. Varys then commands his "[little birds](wiki:Little_birds)" to finish Kevan off.(wiki:Kevan_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6)
