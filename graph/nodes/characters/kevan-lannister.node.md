## Identity

Ser Kevan Lannister is a knight from House Lannister, the younger brother of Lord Tywin Lannister. He is the POV character for the epilogue of A Dance with Dragons (wiki:Kevan_Lannister).

## Origins

Born 244 AC at Casterly Rock (track_b: Born). Father: Lord Tytos Lannister (track_b: Father). Mother: Lady Jeyne Marbrand (track_b: Mother). Died 300 AC in King's Landing (track_b: Died).

## Appearances & Description

Big man with broad shoulders and a thick waist. Short blond hair, balding, with a close-cropped yellow beard following the line of his massive jaw. Green eyes, fair skin, portly. Not considered handsome (wiki:Kevan_Lannister). Clasps his crimson wool cloak with a golden lion's head brooch. Owns a quilted wool doublet the color of charcoal. Wields a sword (wiki:Kevan_Lannister).

## Narrative Arc

Kevan was Tywin's closest confidant since childhood, perpetually in his shadow but recognizing his brother's competency (wiki:Kevan_Lannister). He served as page and squire to Lord Roger Reyne at Castamere, was knighted by Reyne during the War of the Ninepenny Kings, and visited the Free Cities at sixteen. Tywin later charged him with ridding the westerlands of outlaws and collecting debts — through the latter, Kevan met Dorna Swyft, whom he married by 266 AC (wiki:Kevan_Lannister).

In AGOT, Kevan commands the center at the Battle of the Green Fork while Tywin's left flank attempts to trap Bolton's forces (agot-tyrion-08). His twin son Willem is captured in the Whispering Wood. In ACOK, he sends Podrick Payne to squire for Tyrion and accompanies the army to the Blackwater victory. In ASOS, he serves as master of laws on Joffrey's small council. His son Willem is murdered by Lord Karstark; Martyn remains captive. He reluctantly believes Tyrion guilty of Joffrey's murder (wiki:Kevan_Lannister).

After Tywin's assassination, Cersei offers Kevan the Hand position. He refuses unless named regent as well, saying Cersei should return to the Rock. She throws wine in his face. Kevan departs to escort Tywin's body home, helps Lancel restore Darry, and broods at the Riverrun siege (wiki:Kevan_Lannister). When Cersei is arrested by the Faith, Pycelle offers Kevan the regency. He returns to King's Landing and works to repair Cersei's damage — naming Mace Tyrell as Hand, Paxter Redwyne as master of ships, Randyll Tarly as master of laws. He confronts Cersei about her affair with Lancel and arranges her walk of atonement (wiki:Kevan_Lannister).

Kevan is assassinated by Varys, who shoots him with a crossbow to prevent him from undoing Cersei's misrule — Varys needs the Lannister-Tyrell alliance to fracture to clear the way for Aegon Targaryen. Varys's little birds finish Kevan off (wiki:Kevan_Lannister).
