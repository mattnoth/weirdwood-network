## Identity

Lyman Lannister — Lord Lyman Lannister of Casterly Rock offered Aegon and Rhaena his protection and stood firm against Maegor's demand to return them in chains.

## Origins

### Reign of Maegor

After the death of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), Lord Lyman offered his protection to Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)) and Princess [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), after they abandoned their royal progress in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) in fear of their usurping uncle, [Maegor I](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) While the two were at Casterly Rock, Lyman's own wife, Lady [Jocasta](wiki:Jocasta_Lannister), discerned that Rhaena was with child. Lyman extended [guest right](wiki:Guest_right) and refused King Maegor's demands to turn the prince and princess over, but he did not pledge his swords to the fugitive Aegon's cause. He did not stir himself from Casterly Rock when Aegon gathered his followers at [Pinkmaiden Castle](wiki:Pinkmaiden_Castle), but five hundred men joined Aegon's cause under the banner of Lyman's bastard son Ser [Tyler Hill](wiki:Tyler_Hill), allowing Lyman to lend support to the young prince while keeping his hands clean. However, Aegon perished at his uncle's hands during the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye) in [43 AC](wiki:43_AC).(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

The battle left Lord [Alyn Tarbeck](wiki:Alyn_Tarbeck) slain and his wife [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling_(wife_of_Maegor_I)) widowed. A younger son of Lord Lyman was courting Jeyne when King Maegor summoned her to court and wed her in [47 AC](wiki:47_AC). Maegor confirmed Jeyne's [son](wiki:Lord_Tarbeck_(son_of_Alyn)) by her previous marriage as the [Lord of Tarbeck Hall](wiki:Lord_of_Tarbeck_Hall) and the boy was sent to Casterly Rock to be fostered by Lyman. Meanwhile, Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett) led the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) against Maegor throughout the westerlands, but Lyman had no inclination to move against him.(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) When Prince [Jaehaerys Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) put forward his claim to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Lyman rallied to his support.(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

### Reign of Jaehaerys

Lord Lyman and Lady Jocasta went to [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle) in [49 AC](wiki:49_AC) to assist at the [wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Rhaena_Targaryen_and_Androw_Farman) of Rhaena Targaryen and [Androw Farman](wiki:Androw_Farman), the youngest son of his vassal Lord [Marq Farman](wiki:Marq_Farman); Lyman was the only great lord in attendance for the wedding. Later that year, Lyman traveled to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) along with three hundred knights from Casterly Rock for the wedding of Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) to Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon).(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

The following year, after the death of Lord Farman, Lyman hosted Rhaena and her retinue for more than a month. Lyman held a lavish feast in their honor and provided Rhaena and her companions with regal apartments. Lyman's generous hospitality was not without purpose: he wanted an alliance with the Iron Throne by wedding either his bastard son, [Tyler Hill](wiki:Tyler_Hill), or one of his trueborn sons to Rhaena, and he wanted a dragon of his own. Lyman sought to raise the Lannisters to become the "second house" in the realm. When Rhaena discovered Lyman's intentions, she left Casterly Rock.(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Though Lyman was never part of King Jaehaerys I's [small council](wiki:Small_council), the Lord of Casterly Rock was suggested to fill various offices by the king's advisors. In [50 AC](wiki:50_AC), after the king had dismissed Lord [Edwell Celtigar](wiki:Edwell_Celtigar), several of the king's advisors suggested for Lyman to be named [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) for he was the richest lord in the realm, but Jaehaerys was disinclined and named [Rego Draz](wiki:Rego_Draz) instead.(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Lyman was considered as master of coin a second time in [59 AC](wiki:59_AC) after the murder of Rego Draz, but Jaehaerys thought him too proud. When Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)) stepped down as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lyman was briefly considered as an option for the office, but the king distrusted the lord and passed over him.(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) Lyman traveled a second time to King's Landing in [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) for the tourney celebrating the tenth year of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen)'s reign.(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

In [59 AC](wiki:59_AC), Lyman died from the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) along with the Lords [Tarbeck](wiki:House_Tarbeck), [Marbrand](wiki:House_Marbrand), and [Westerling](wiki:House_Westerling).(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

## Appearances & Description

A cunning and powerful [lord](wiki:Lord),(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) Lyman was an ambitious man who wanted to raise the influence of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) above the [Velaryons](wiki:House_Velaryon), [Baratheons](wiki:House_Baratheon), and [Hightowers](wiki:House_Hightower). [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) believed that he sought a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) of his own to match the power of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

Lyman was alleged to be the richest lord in Westeros.(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) distrusted Lord Lyman and considered him too proud.(wiki:Lyman_Lannister.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
