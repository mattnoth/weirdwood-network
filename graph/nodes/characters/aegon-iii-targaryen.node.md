## Identity

Aegon III Targaryen (120–157 AC) — child of Daemon Targaryen and Rhaenyra Targaryen; married to Daenaera Velaryon and Jaehaera Targaryen; King of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men. One of 12 Aegon Targaryens — see the Aegon Targaryen disambiguation entry.

## Origins

### Youth

Aegon was born towards the end of [120 AC](wiki:120_AC) to Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) and Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Aegon was [named after](wiki:Named_after) the first Targaryen king, [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower), the second wife of his grandfather King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), took offense to Aegon's name, since she had given birth to a son named [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) herself years before, and felt that Rhaenyra's choice of the name was a slight against her own son.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Because two princes during the reign of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) were named Aegon, history refers to Queen Alicent's son as Aegon the Elder, whereas Rhaenyra's son was called Aegon the Younger.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2)

Aegon had three older half-brothers, Princes [Jacaerys](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon), [Lucerys](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon), and [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon), from his mother's first marriage, to Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon), and two older half-sisters, Princesses [Baela](wiki:Baela_Targaryen) and [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)), from his father's second marriage to Lady [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon). Two years after Aegon's birth, Rhaenyra gave birth to another boy, [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3) Aegon lived with his mother, father, and siblings on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)), the ancestral seat of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) and his mother's seat as the [Princess of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone). In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC) on Dragonstone, his mother gave birth to Aegon's sister, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)), who was stillborn.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

Aegon shared his bedchamber with his younger brother Viserys. They were constant companions during their youth on Dragonstone; they took their lessons together, and played together as well.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-3) By [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), Aegon had bonded with the [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Stormcloud](wiki:Stormcloud), but had not yet ridden him.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-21)

### The Dance of the Dragons

A war of succession began with the death of Aegon's grandfather, King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), early in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC). Though Aegon's mother, [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), had been named heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) by Viserys, Aegon the Younger's half-uncle, Aegon the Elder, was crowned King [Aegon II](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). The resulting war became known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)

As part of the terms King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) offered to Prince Aegon's mother Rhaenyra, Aegon the Younger and his brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) would be given places of honor at the royal court, with Aegon becoming a [squire](wiki:Squire) for King Aegon II. Rhaenyra angrily refused the terms.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

Late in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), at the behest of their half-brother [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon), Aegon and his younger brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) were sent to [Essos](wiki:Essos) while their mother Rhaenyra secured the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) During the journey, their ship, the *[Gay Abandon](wiki:Gay_Abandon)*, was captured by ships of the [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy). Aegon escaped capture by flying on his dragon [Stormcloud](wiki:Stormcloud), leaving his brother Viserys behind. He had never flown Stormcloud before, and the experience left him shaken. He clung to the dragon's neck during the journey back to Dragonstone, where he informed the garrison of all that had happened. Stormcloud had been severely wounded during the flight, and died within an hour after arriving on the Targaryen island.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Aegon would never ride a dragon again.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

Aegon would always feel guilty for leaving his younger brother Viserys behind.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) Their brother Jacaerys led an [attack](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet) against the fleet of the Triarchy that had attacked Aegon's ship, but died in the struggle together with his dragon [Vermax](wiki:Vermax) on the fifth day of [130 AC](wiki:130_AC).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5)

In [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), Queen Rhaenyra [took King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing). Once she felt secure enough, Aegon was summoned to the capital. Rhaenyra named him her [cupbearer](wiki:Cupbearer), so he would always be close to her.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Aegon hardly spoke a word during this time.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Although Rhaenyra had secured the capital, her reign was still faced several threats, most notably the missing King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) and his dragon [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), who were terrorizing the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), and the large army under the command of Lord [Ormund Hightower](wiki:Ormund_Hightower) and Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)), which continued to advance on King's Landing, defeating every enemy they met on route. Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), Rhaenyra's [Hand of the Queen](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), suggested terms of peace, which included him taking Aegon II's daughter [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen) as his own [ward](wiki:Ward), until she could be wed to Prince Aegon. Rhaenyra refused.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1)

During Rhaenyra's half-year rule over King's Landing, she slowly lost her grip, eventually resulting in the [riot of King's Landing](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), which lasted two days and was concluded by the [storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit). During the second night of the riots, Aegon and his mother stood on the roof of the Red Keep, from where they witnessed the fall of Prince [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon) from the dragon [Syrax](wiki:Syrax), and the dragon's death. The next morning, Aegon fled with his mother Rhaenyra and a few loyal men and women from the capital, eventually finding refuge at [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale). Rhaenyra, grieving and in despair, refused to be apart from Aegon while at Duskendale, and he became a "small pale shadow" by her side.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

Aegon and Rhaenyra traveled from Duskendale to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) on the *[Violande](wiki:Violande)*. However, in Rhaenyra's absence her seat had secretly [fallen](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone) to King Aegon II, and when Aegon and Rhaenyra arrived at the castle's gates, they found the charred corpses of Rhaenyra's loyalists hanging from the gates. Aegon was the first to realize what it meant, and cried out to his mother to flee, to no avail. Rhaenyra's last three [Queensguard](wiki:Queensguard) [knights](wiki:Knight) were slain, and when Aegon picked up one of their swords, Ser [Alfred Broome](wiki:Alfred_Broome) contemptuously knocked it aside. They were brought to the castle ward, where they found an injured [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre) and King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). After an exchange of conversation, Rhaenyra was forcefully separated from her son, and placed in front of Sunfyre, who burned her with [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire). Prince Aegon watched in horror as Sunfyre devoured his mother.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2) Aegon was forever mentally scarred, and this event cemented his later fierce hatred for and terror of dragons.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-11)

Ser Alfred Broome was in favor of killing Prince Aegon, but King Aegon II refused, insisting that the young prince was valuable as a hostage against Rhaenyra's supporters. Prince Aegon was manacled and brought to the dungeons under Dragonstone.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3)

At King's Landing, King Aegon II's followers enlisted the support of Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon). Lord Corlys insisted that Prince Aegon the Younger was wed to Aegon's only remaining child, Princess Jaehaera, to join the two branches of House Targaryen, and further demanded that Aegon the Younger and Jaehaera would be proclaimed King Aegon II's heirs together. Dowager Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) initially refused, but was later persuaded by Lord [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong) to reluctantly agree to the betrothal.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

King Aegon II, however, upon his arrival at King's Landing, refused the notion, insisting that Rhaenyra's line should end with Aegon the Younger, suggesting that the boy could join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), or otherwise be made a [eunuch](wiki:Eunuch), as to prevent him from fathering children. While Lord [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister) argued for executing Aegon the Younger, Lord Larys Strong convinced King Aegon II to agree to both the betrothal to Princess Jaehaera, as well as to naming Aegon the Younger as the heir to the throne, insisting that, after the war was done and won, the situation could be dealt with.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)

Prince Aegon would remain his uncle's captive for half a year after his mother's death, during which time his mother's supporters continued to fight against King Aegon II and his [greens](wiki:Greens). King Aegon II was eventually murdered by his own men,(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2) after which Lord Corlys Velaryon and Prince Aegon rode forth from the city under a peace banner to yield King's Landing to the army of Lord [Kermit Tully](wiki:Kermit_Tully).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3) Aegon the Younger inherited the throne as King Aegon III Targaryen in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-4)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-10)

### Aegon's regency

In mid-[131 AC](wiki:131_AC), Aegon succeeded to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) at the age of ten.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-11) During the five years of his minority that followed, the realm was therefore ruled by Aegon's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and a council of [regents](wiki:Regent). Numerous political schemes, plots, and assassinations happened in [those five years](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), as the regents and Hands fought one another for political power.

#### Hand of the King: Cregan Stark

During the [False Dawn](wiki:False_Dawn), the [riverlords](wiki:Riverlords) who had marched against King's Landing and the forces of Aegon II swore fealty to the new king. After Aegon II had been cremated, Prince Aegon proclaimed on [Aegon's High Hill](wiki:Aegon's_High_Hill) that peace was at hand, while Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) send forth promises of pardons to Aegon II's former loyalists. Together with Lord Corlys, Lord [Kermit Tully](wiki:Kermit_Tully) and his brother, Ser [Oscar](wiki:Oscar_Tully), and Lord [Benjicot Blackwood](wiki:Benjicot_Blackwood), Aegon welcomed Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) to the city. However, Lord Cregan seized power in King's Landing, in what became known as the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf), to deal with the resolution of the war and the murder of King Aegon II. Rumors throughout King's Landing claimed that Lord Cregan planned to take Aegon to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to wed one of his daughters (although Cregan had no daughters at the time), others claimed that he planned to kill Aegon to claim the Iron Throne by wedding Princess Jaehaera. While Cregan investigated the murder of Aegon II, he confined Prince Aegon to [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) for his safety, with no companion but the boy [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair), a former royal pretender. Cregan did allow Aegon to be visited by his half-sisters, [Baela](wiki:Baela_Targaryen) and [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)), though Aegon paled upon seeing Rhaena's hatchling [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Morning](wiki:Morning), and commanded his guards to remove it.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-12)

Peace was declared, but Cregan still intended to do [justice](wiki:Laws_and_justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms), by putting on trial and executing those he held responsible for the death of Aegon II Targaryen. Lord Cregan arrested twenty-two men, as well as Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), Lord [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong), Ser [Perkin the Flea](wiki:Perkin_the_Flea), and Ser [Gyles Belgrave](wiki:Gyles_Belgrave) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). Intimidating the young Prince Aegon, Cregan Stark was named [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and presided over the trials. The only person spared from condemnation was Corlys Velaryon, on Aegon's authority, who restored Corlys to his offices and honors and gave him a seat on the [small council](wiki:Small_council). Although Aegon was in his minority, still uncrowned, and not yet anointed as king, Cregan agreed to let Aegon's decree stand. Aegon witnessed the executions of those condemned, though in the end only two men were beheaded, while the others [took the black](wiki:Night's_Watch). Afterwards, Cregan Stark returned his chain of office to Aegon, and returned to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-13)

Aegon was married to his cousin, Princess [Jaehaera Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen), on the seventh day of the seventh moon in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC). [The ceremony](wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen), held publicly atop [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill), united the two branches of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). Following the wedding, the couple traveled in an open litter to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), where Aegon was officially crowned as King Aegon III.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-12)

#### Hand of the King: Tyland Lannister

Aegon's first act as king was to fill the five vacancies in the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). Next appointed were the Hand of the King, Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), the [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm), Lord [Leowyn Corbray](wiki:Leowyn_Corbray), and the council of [regents](wiki:Regent) to sit over them. Although Aegon III would sit upon the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) when it was required of him, he was otherwise not often seen. By the end of [131 AC](wiki:131_AC), the people of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) began to grow unhappy with their king, while rumors about the new Hand began to spread.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-13)

The royal marriage was also troubled. Aegon III was now eleven, Jaehaera was only eight, and both children had been greatly traumatized by their losses and by what they had witnessed during the civil war. After their wedding, they had very little contact with one another except for formal occasions, which were rare, as Jaehaera disliked leaving her chambers. Jaehaera's only other living relative, her grandmother Dowager Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower), could not be trusted around the king (as she was likely to snatch a dagger from a guardsman), nor even with Jaehaera, since when Alicent had last shared a meal with her, she told Jaehaera to cut Aegon's throat while he was sleeping, sending the girl screaming. [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) sent a letter to the [Conclave](wiki:Conclave) of the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), declaring both children "broken", "not normal children", and fearing for the future of the kingdom.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-14)

The sudden death of Lord Corlys Velaryon in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC) raised the issue of the king's successor. When King Aegon was asked who his heir should be, the king offered the name of his only friend, [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair), the [bastard](wiki:Bastard)-born pretender during the [Moon of Madness](wiki:Moon_of_Madness) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). The suggestion was ignored by the regents. Although Aegon's half-sisters [Baela](wiki:Baela_Targaryen) and [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)) were dismissed on account of their sex, the regents determined that any son the twins would have, in particular from the elder twin Baela, would be a suitable successor to the king. In accordance, the regents attempted to have the twins married to suitable husbands, unsuccessfully for Baela (who rejected the regents' candidate and eloped to marry her cousin, Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon)), but successfully for Rhaena (who chose one of her suitors, Ser [Corwyn Corbray](wiki:Corwyn_Corbray)).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-15)

When the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) struck Westeros in early [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), Aegon distinguished himself by visiting those stricken by the disease, sitting beside them, and sometimes holding their hands or cooling their fevers with damp cloths. He seldom spoke, but was a great comfort to those afflicted nonetheless, listening to their stories and pleas. Most of those he visted died, but those who survived would later speak of the king's "healing hands". Aegon likewise sat beside his Hand, Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), as he passed away from the disease, taking his hand in his last moments.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-16)

#### Hand of the King: Unwin Peake

In the days following Ser Tyland's death, the twelve-year-old king showed maturity beyond his age, naming Ser [Robert Darklyn](wiki:Robert_Darklyn) and Ser [Robin Massey](wiki:Robin_Massey) to the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). King Aegon further commanded the disgraced former Grand Maester [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle) to send forth [ravens](wiki:Ravens) to summon to court Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan), who he wished to name as his new Hand, Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon), who he wished to name as his admiral, and his cousin [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen). However, Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) and Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun), the two remaining regents healthy enough serve in their office, undid all of the king's appointments. With Munkun's support, Peake named himself [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-4)

Unwin also undid the appointments of Ser Robert Darklyn and Ser Robin Massey to the Kingsguard, and instead gave their white cloaks to his own kin. Aegon had received the decisions of Munkun and Peake with "sullen silence", but he spoke to object to this, pointing out that Kingsguard serve for life. Lord Unwin replied that they do, but only when "properly appointed". Afterwards, Aegon retreated into silence and passivity, refusing to show his true feelings. For the rest of his minority, Aegon did not much participate in the rule of the Seven Kingdoms, only signing and sealing the papers that Lord Peake presented to him. Occasionally Aegon would be brought out to sit the Iron Throne on formal occasions, or to welcome envoys, but otherwise he was not seen much inside the Red Keep, and almost never outside of it.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5)

Nevertheless, Aegon deeply disliked Unwin's appointment of Ser [Marston Waters](wiki:Marston_Waters) to the position of [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), as Waters had stood by silently at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) when King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) had fed Aegon's mother to [his dragon](wiki:Sunfyre). He also hated Unwin Peake's personal guard, the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers_(guards)), whose commander, [Tessario the Tiger](wiki:Tessario_the_Tiger), soon killed Robin Massey in a quarrel. Unwin made further appointments around court, with all positions filled by his supporters, family, and friends. This included the appointment of Ser [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long) as the new [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms) of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), who took over Aegon's training at arms. Aegon quickly grew to despise Ser Gareth, and often ignored his instructions, refused to cooperate, or simply walked away. Since Gareth was not allowed to harm the king, he advised Lord Peake to make Aegon's only friend and companion, [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair), into the king's [whipping boy](wiki:Whipping_boy). Gaemon's tears and blood were motivation enough for Aegon, and his skill at arms improved dramatically.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-6)

Unwin preferred to ignore Aegon whenever possible, but on the [Feast Day of Our Father Above](wiki:Feast_Day_of_Our_Father_Above) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), Aegon was made to watch from the battlements of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) as Lord Peake had its crowded dungeons emptied and had all the offenders punished publicly. Some of the condemned beseeched the king for mercy as they were tormented with their punishments or executions, but Aegon stood still as stone, seemingly not hearing any of the pleas or seeing any of it.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-7)

Later the same year, the king's marriage ended when Queen [Jaehaera](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen) died, seemingly of suicide, although rumors of murder were told all throughout King's Landing.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-8) Lord Unwin attempted to betroth the king to his own daughter, [Myrielle Peake](wiki:Myrielle_Peake), but many [lords](wiki:Lord) disapproved of the match, which took place so soon after the queen's death. Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) suggested the [north](wiki:North) would look on the match with disfavor, Lord [Kermit Tully](wiki:Kermit_Tully) called it presumptuous, and [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun)'s support began to waver, acknowledging that the match would be seen as advancing [Peake](wiki:House_Peake) interests rather than being for the good of the [realm](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). Noble ladies wrote to the crown, proposing their own relatives as Aegon's bride (or themselves, in some cases). Due to the pressure, Peake instead announced a [ball](wiki:Maiden's_Day_Ball) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) where the king himself would be able to choose his own bride.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-9)

In the weeks before the ball, Peake had his daughter spent much time with the king. At the ball itself, held on [Maiden's Day](wiki:Maiden's_Day), all the [maiden](wiki:Maiden) candidates were presented to the king as he was seated on the Iron Throne. Aegon only nodded to each before the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) led her away. Aegon's disinterest increased with every passing hour, which, according to [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom), was exactly what Peake had desired. When only a few maidens remained, Baela and [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)) arrived with their kinswoman [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon), a six-year-old girl, and announced that they had brought their brother his new queen. Aegon responded positively to Daenaera, returning her smile and telling her she looked very pretty. Afterwards, the last few candidates were brought forward hurriedly, and it was so clear that the king wished to end the parade that the final maiden sobbed as she curtsied. Afterwards, the king summoned his cupbearer, [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair), who announced that Aegon would wed Daenaera Velaryon. Although Peake afterwards insisted that Aegon should instead be married to an older girl, as to be able to father heirs, he was overruled by the other regents. Aegon and Daenaera were wed on the last day of the year.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-10)

For a short while following his marriage, Aegon became less gloomy, and was seen far more within the Red Keep. He even left the castle three times, to show Daenaera the sights of King's Landing. Aegon even began to attend the meetings of the [small council](wiki:Small_council), which pleased a few of the regents. However, Lord Peake took the king's presence as a slight, and whenever Aegon asked a question, the Hand would accuse him of wasting the council's time, or tell him the subject was beyond the understanding of a child. Soon, Aegon stopped attending the council meetings, and began to return to his former, passive self.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-4)

#### Hand of the King: Thaddeus Rowan

In [134 AC](wiki:134_AC), Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) returned to King's Landing from his first great voyage. Unwin, who was supsicious of Alyn, had forbidden Aegon from going to greet him, but the regents overruled the Hand, and the king, queen, and court traveled to the docks to welcome *[Lady Baela](wiki:Lady_Baela)* home. There, Lord Velaryon presented Aegon with a "treasure" acquired in [Lys](wiki:Lys): Aegon's younger brother Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), who had been presumed dead since the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet) in early [130 AC](wiki:130_AC). Viserys's survival and return lifted the guilt Aegon had felt since fleeing from the *[Gay Abandon](wiki:Gay_Abandon)*, and did much to restore his confidence. However, Unwin was infuriated by the cost of the ransom that Alyn had paid for Viserys, and threatened to resign his post over it. The regency council quickly accepted his resignation, and appointed Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan) as the new Hand of the King. Nevertheless, Unwin's appointments continued to serve in their positions, and Unwin bequeathed [his guards](wiki:Fingers_(guards)) to Lord Rowan.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-5)

Viserys's return also much reduced Aegon's loneliness, as he resumed his role as Aegon's constant companion, like when they were boys together on Dragonstone. Unfortunately, Aegon's other friend, [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair), was forgotten, and even Queen Daenaera was neglected.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-6) Viserys had come to King's Landing accompanied by his wife, [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare), and several of her brothers. The following year, when Larra gave birth to a son, who was named [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) [in his uncle's honor](wiki:Named_after), King Aegon III's succession seemed secure. Viserys, who had kept his own [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) with him during his captivity, similarly presented his own son with an egg. However, when King Aegon learned that the dragon egg of his niece [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon_(daughter_of_Alyn)) had hatched into a monstrosity, a wingless [wyrm](wiki:Wyrm) which injured the babe in the cradle, he ordered all dragon eggs removed from the Red Keep. Though Viserys was greatly angered by this, the eggs were sent to Dragonstone, and Viserys refused to speak to Aegon for a month.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

With his brother's absence, Aegon began to spend more time with Daenaera again. Unfortunately, it was during a quiet dinner that Aegon shared with his queen, as well as his friend and [food taster](wiki:Food_taster) Gaemon Palehair, that Gaemon collapsed from belly cramps and Daenaera also suffered from stomach pains. [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) gave Daenaera a powerful purgative, which probably saved her life, but Gaemon died within the hour. Viserys mended his rift with his mourning brother as they sat by Daenaera's bedside. Although Aegon was comforted by his brother in his grief, Gaemon's death left him inconsolable, and his old gloom settled over him once more, leaving him uninterested in the court and his kingdom.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8)

It was determined that the [poison](wiki:Poison) was the [tears of Lys](wiki:Tears_of_Lys), baked in the apple tarts. As Aegon usually did not eat sweets, he was not harmed. The Hand, Thaddeus Rowan, investigated the attempted [assassination](wiki:Assassin), but his work was halted when he determined that the confessions of the cooks, who had been [tortured](wiki:Torture) by the [Lord Confessor](wiki:Lord_Confessor) [George Graceford](wiki:George_Graceford), were valueless. However, with the fall of [House Rogare](wiki:House_Rogare) in Lys and the bankruptcy of [their bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank), the Rogares in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) were arrested in swift order. While [one of Larra's brothers](wiki:Moredo_Rogare) fled to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), the other two were arrested by the [gold cloaks](wiki:Gold_cloaks) and by Ser Marston Waters of the Kingsguard. Though it was initially believed that Thaddeus Rowan had ordered the arrests, he himself was arrested by the Kingsguard Ser [Mervyn Flowers](wiki:Mervyn_Flowers), Unwin Peake's half brother, while Peake's [former guards](wiki:Fingers_(guards)) stood aside. Rowan's family and servants were also arrested.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2)

#### Hand of the King: Marston Waters

However, when the Kingsguard Ser [Amaury Peake](wiki:Amaury_Peake) and a dozen [men-at-arms](wiki:Men-at-arms) arrived at [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) to arrest Larra Rogare, they were confronted on the drawbridge by Prince Viserys and King Aegon III. Ser Amaury announed that Ser [Marston Waters](wiki:Marston_Waters) was the new Hand, appointed by the regents, and when King Aegon replied that Thaddeus Rowan was one of his regents, Amaury said that he had been arrested for [treason](wiki:Treason), on authority of the Hand. Prince Viserys laughed at this, but refused the entry of Amaury and his men, planting an axe upon the drawbridge and warning them not to cross it, before he and Aegon retreated back into the castle. When Amaury's men passed the axe, Larra's personal guard [Sandoq the Shadow](wiki:Sandoq) emerged, and slew them all. After Sandoq was done, he returned to the holdfast, and the king commanded the portcullis be lowered and the drawbridge raised. King Aegon, Prince Viserys, Queen Daenaera, Lady Larra Rogare, Sandoq, the [fool](wiki:Fool) [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom), several [ladies-in-waiting](wiki:Ladies-in-waiting), and a few guardsmen would remain within the castle-within-a-castle for eighteen days of the [secret siege](wiki:Secret_siege).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-3)

On the first day, Ser Marston Waters came to swear that he meant no harm to Aegon, and had only acted to protect the king from false friends and traitors. He swore that none would do harm to Aegon while he stood by him. From the battlements, Aegon replied that Marston had stood by him when [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre) ate [his mother](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), and only watched. Aegon would not let Marston stand by and watch while Viserys's wife was killed. On the following days the besiegers brought Grand Maester Munkun and Septon [Bernard](wiki:Bernard), who drew no response from Aegon. However, when [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long) attempted to convince the king to yield, the angered Aegon shouted at the [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms) that Gaemon Palehair was dead, and they would get no blood from beating his bones.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-4)

On the twelfth day, the besiegers brought forth Thaddeus Rowan, in chains, with his face so swollen that he was unrecognizable. With a slurred voice, Rowan confessed to plotting with the Rogares to poison both King Aegon and Queen Daenaera, to replace them with Prince Viserys and his wife Larra Rogare. Aegon was momentarily shocked by this, but then Viserys asked Rowan if he was part of the plot too, and Rowan agreed. Upon further questions, Rowan also agreed that Gaemon Palehair had poisoned the tarts, and even agreed to Mushroom's inquiry if Rowan had poisoned King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen). At this, Aegon declared that Lord Rowan had been [tortured](wiki:Torture) by traitors into falsely confessing, and demanded of Ser Marston Waters, to "seize the [Lord Confessor](wiki:Lord_Confessor), if you love your king… else I will know that you are as false as he is." According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), the fifteen-year-old Aegon seemed "every inch a king" in that moment.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-5)

Though Marston Wasters did act, arresting [George Graceford](wiki:George_Graceford), who named the other conspirators (all appointments of [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake)), he was slain when he went to arrest Mervyn Flowers. The conspirators said they had acted because they considered Aegon weak, or because they did not trust Larra Rogare and her foreign religion, though some claimed that they had acted under the genuine belief that Thaddeus Rowan and the [Lyseni](wiki:Lys) were the real traitors. The mistrust within the Red Keep was so great that Aegon and Viserys did not open the gates of Maegor's Holdfast until six days later, when they saw Munkun send forth [ravens](wiki:Raven) to the king's leal [lords](wiki:Lord). By that point, they had run so short of food within the holdfast that Daenaera cried herself to sleep at night, and two of her ladies were so weak from hunger that they had to be helped across the moat.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-6)

#### Hand of the King: Torrhen Manderly

Aegon restored Lord Thaddeus Rowan to the office as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), but it soon became clear that the torture he had survived had left him unfit to serve as Hand. Subsequently, he was dismissed, and the office was instead temporarily bestowed on Grand Maester Munkun, until a new Hand could be named. At [the gathering of lords](wiki:Great_Council) in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), three new [regents](wiki:Regent) were selected by lot, while Lord [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly) was chosen as the new Hand.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-7)

Lord Manderly presided over the trials of the conspirators, which lasted thirty-three days. King Aegon attended only three times, on the days that judgment was pronounced upon [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long), [George Graceford](wiki:George_Graceford), and Septon [Bernard](wiki:Bernard). He showed no interest in the rest, and never asked about their fates. [Lotho](wiki:Lotho_Rogare) and [Roggerio Rogare](wiki:Roggerio_Rogare) were also put on trial, for the collapse of the [Rogare Bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank), and though Prince Viserys came every day to sit beside his wife Larra as she watched the judgement of her brothers, Aegon never attended.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-8)

### Reign of the Broken King

On Aegon's [sixteenth nameday](wiki:Age_of_majority) in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), the king arrived at the [small council](wiki:Small_council) meeting, where he dismissed his [regents](wiki:Regent) and his [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), and canceled the plans they had been making for him to make a [royal progress](wiki:Royal_progress). Lord [Torrhen Manderly](wiki:Torrhen_Manderly) was greatly affronted by the brusque manner of his dismissal, and according to [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) this act made Manderly Aegon's enemy.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-9)

Aegon was regarded as a broken king who ruled over a broken reign. Aegon was melancholic to the end of his days, found pleasure in almost nothing, and locked himself in his room to brood for days on end. It took ten years until Aegon consummated his marriage, when his wife was sixteen, but eventually Queen [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon) birthed Aegon five children: [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen) (born in [143 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)) and [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) (born in [144 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), [Daena](wiki:Daena_Targaryen) (born in [145 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_III)) (born in [147 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), and [Elaena](wiki:Elaena_Targaryen) (born in [150 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)). Following his birth, Prince Daeron was named [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) by Aegon.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-9)

As king, Aegon III strove to give the realm peace and plenty, but his coldness kept him from courting his lords and people. During the later years of his reign, Aegon's Hand was his brother, Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-10) Together, Aegon and Viserys dealt with the [pretenders](wiki:Daeron_Pretenders) claiming to be Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)), brother to the late King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). All of these pretenders were proven to be imposters.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-11)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-4)

Because Aegon had a great distaste for dragons after the tragic death of [Stormcloud](wiki:Stormcloud) and witnessing [his mother](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) being devoured by [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre), he is often blamed for the death of the [last dragon](wiki:Last_dragon).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Some rumors even claimed he [poisoned](wiki:Poison) her.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) After the last dragon died, Aegon III became known as "Aegon the Dragonbane". Regardless, Aegon became convinced that dragons could be used to cow those who sought to oppose him. At the urging of his brother Viserys, Aegon brought nine [mages](wiki:Mage) from [Essos](wiki:Essos) to attempt to hatch a clutch of [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_eggs) with [magic](wiki:Magic), but this ended in failure.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-12)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

Aegon's reign ended with his death from consumption in [157 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), at the age of thirty-six. Many of his subjects thought him far older, on account of his youth having ended so abruptly. Aegon is not remembered fondly.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-13)

## Appearances & Description

Aegon was a handsome boy(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) with dark [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes) which looked almost black, and silver hair which was so pale that it was almost white.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) He was lean of face and body.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) By the age of ten, Aegon was considered tall for his age.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) According to a semi-canon source, Aegon wore a short beard with no mustache.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1253.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

Aegon dressed simply, and in black (always, according to [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Yandel](wiki:Yandel),(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) very often, according to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn)).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) Under his velvets and satins, he would wear a [hair shirt](wiki:Hair_shirt).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5) He owned black gloves and a golden chain displaying the three-headed dragon of House Targaryen.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1253.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) He wore a [circlet](wiki:Crown) of yellow gold, simple and unadorned.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

Aegon was a joyless man,(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6) severely marked by his experiences during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom)'s accounts state that Aegon seldom smiled and laughed even less, even as a boy. According to the dwarf, Aegon could be graceful and courtly when it was required, but at the same time had a darkness within him that never went away.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7) The guilt he felt over having abandoned his younger brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) when [their ship](wiki:Gay_Abandon) [was attacked](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet) during the Dance of the Dragons, caused him to become somber.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) called Aegon "broken" after he lost his brothers and watched his mother being eaten by his uncle's [dragon](wiki:Dragon), and described him as "dead inside".(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3) Aegon showed little interest in women, did not ride (except for travel), [hawk](wiki:Hawk), hunt, or joust, nor attend [tourneys](wiki:Tourney). He did not enjoy reading, dancing, or singing, and was not interested in [wine](wiki:Wine) or food, so much so that he often had to be reminded to eat.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4) Similarly, he had little interest in swordplay or the arts of war.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) During the [hour of the wolf](wiki:Named_hours), Aegon could often be found standing by a window, gazing up at the stars, but he showed no interest in the study of [astronomy](wiki:Astronomy).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-5)

Aegon rarely displayed emotions, but the mere mention of dragons would send him into a rage,(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-6) and he was unwilling to go near one.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-9)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Although clever, he was an overall silent person, who never started a conversation, and answered questions as curtly as possible.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-7) He was regarded as solemn,(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-8) dour, and gloomy.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) He spoke little, and often retreated into silence, solitude, and a brooding passivity.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) Aegon had few friends, and during the early years of his reign seldom slept a full night.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-9)(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3) He rarely left the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) after his coronation.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-10)

Following his second [marriage](wiki:Marriage) to [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon), Aegon's gloom was lifted for a short while.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) Some joy also returned to Aegon following the return of his brother Viserys.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2) However, he would always remain a melancholy man who found pleasure in almost nothing, who disliked being touched, and who would retreat to his chambers for days on end, brooding alone.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

On the [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) thinks about [dragons](wiki:Dragons) and what her brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) told her of the [last dragons](wiki:Last_dragon). He had said that they died during the reign of Aegon III, the Dragonbane, no more than a century and a half ago, which does not seem so long ago to Daenerys.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

### A Storm of Swords

After King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) has ended his betrothal to [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and has become engaged to [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), he informs Sansa that he can still sleep with her, if he wants to. He tells her that *"One of the Aegons"* had a lot of whores, but fails to remember [which Aegon](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) it was, exactly, causing him to (incorrectly) list Aegon III as a possibility.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

### A Feast for Crows

While discussing dragons, the acolyte [Armen](wiki:Armen) tells the novice [Roone](wiki:Roone) that the last dragon died during the reign of King Aegon III Targaryen. However, [Mollander](wiki:Mollander), another novice, insists it was only the last dragon in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) who had died during Aegon's reign, against which Armen argues that the last dragon in Westeros had been the last dragon anywhere.(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Daenerys Targaryen remembers the stories her brother [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) had told her, including the story about how Aegon III had seen his own mother, [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), devoured by the [dragon](wiki:Sunfyre) of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)
