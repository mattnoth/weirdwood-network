## Appearances & Description

The wineseller is a small man, slender and handsome, with flaxen hair curled and perfumed in the [Lysene](wiki:Lys) style.(wiki:Wineseller.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

The wineseller is part of the trade caravan of [Byan Votyris](wiki:Byan_Votyris), encamped in the Western Market of [Vaes Dothrak](wiki:Vaes_Dothrak). He offers thimble-sized cups of [wine](wiki:Wine) to passersby, calling out a list of his wares in fluent [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki_language). The wineseller offers the pregnant [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) a sample of sweet [Dornish red](wiki:Dornish_red), saying that all she needs is a taste and she will name her child after him. When Daenerys responds affirmatively in [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) [Valyrian](wiki:Bastard_Valyrian), the wineseller gapes surprisedly at her, and asks if she is [Tyroshi](wiki:Tyrosh). Daenerys explains that she is actually [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros), and her [handmaid](wiki:Handmaid) [Doreah](wiki:Doreah) proclaims her full identity as a *[khaleesi](wiki:Khaleesi)* and [princess](wiki:Prince) of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Wineseller.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

The wineseller kneels to Daenerys, and then offers a cask of dry red from the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor), saying it is far more worthy of a princess. In actuality, the [Arbor red](wiki:Arbor_red) is [poisoned](wiki:Poison), and he hopes to reap the reward of a castle and [lordship](wiki:Lord) offered by King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) for the death of [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) or Daenerys and her [unborn child](wiki:Rhaego). However, Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont), who has learned of the assassination offer via a letter from [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis), intervenes and demands a taste. The wineseller tries to stall, but relents and pours him a cup. When Jorah tells him to drink it first, and Daenerys insists that he do so, the wineseller throws the cask at them. He tries to flee, but is quickly captured by Daenerys's warrior, [Jhogo](wiki:Jhogo). Byan Votyris then arrives and orders two of his guard to keep the wineseller imprisoned to be sentenced by Daenerys's husband, Khal [Drogo](wiki:Drogo). As a token of his regret that a member of his caravan should do such a thing, Byan gifts the wineseller's goods to Daenerys.(wiki:Wineseller.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

After Daenerys returns to her camp in Vaes Dothrak, Jorah explains the letter he received, and Robert's desire for the death of her and her son. When Khal Drogo returns from hunting that evening, and Jorah says this wineseller may only be the first such assassin, the *[khal](wiki:Khal)* vows that he will conquer Westeros for his and Daenerys's son. Drogo also gifts Jorah and Jhogo [horses](wiki:Horse) from his own herds. Furthermore, Drogo sentences the wineseller to death, saying that he ran from Daenerys, but better that he should run after her. The day Drogo's *[khalasar](wiki:Khalasar)* departs Vaes Dothrak, the wineseller is stripped naked and chained at his neck and wrists. The chains are attached to Daenerys's [silver mare](wiki:Silver_mare), and the wineseller must run after her to keep up, barefoot and stumbling, until his death.(wiki:Wineseller.cite_ref-Ragot54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

### A Clash of Kings

When Daenerys hears that King Robert Baratheon has died, she recalls the poisoning attempt upon her and her unborn son.(wiki:Wineseller.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

In the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying) in [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), Daenerys sees a [vision](wiki:Vision) of a naked and bloody corpse dragged behind a silver horse.(wiki:Wineseller.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

### A Storm of Swords

While traveling from Qarth on [a ship](wiki:Saduleon) belonging to Illyrio Mopatis, Daenerys and Jorah discuss who she can trust. Jorah says that Illyrio may not be trustworthy, as he is devious and clever. Daenerys argues that she needs clever men around her, and Jorah points out that the wineseller was a clever man too.(wiki:Wineseller.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

In [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), Daenerys learns from Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) that Jorah has been spying on her, sending information to [Varys](wiki:Varys) and thus to Robert Baratheon.(wiki:Wineseller.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Barristan says that he had been present at Robert's [small council](wiki:Small_council) when Varys reported that Jorah said Daenerys was pregnant, and she realizes that Jorah is the reason for the wineseller's assassination attempt. Jorah protests that he stopped Daenerys from drinking the wine, and says she has to forgive him. Daenerys remembers that she dragged the wineseller behind her horse until there was nothing left, and does not know what the man who sent him deserves, who also saved her and cared for her. In the end, Daenerys sentences Jorah to be banished.(wiki:Wineseller.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
