## Appearances & Description

Mirri is a heavyset woman, with black hair, a plain face, and a flat nose. She is about 40 years old when Daenerys first meets her.(wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) According to Mirri, when she was younger, she was more beautiful.(wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
According to Mirri, she knows every secret of childbirth, and has never lost a baby.(wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

## Origins

Mirri was a [godswife](wiki:Godswife) in the temple of the [Great Shepherd](wiki:Great_Shepherd), a [Lhazareen](wiki:Lhazareen) [deity](wiki:God). Her mother had been the godswife before her, and taught her all the [spells](wiki:Spell) and songs, as well as how to make the sacred smokes and ointments. As a young woman, Mirri traveled to [Asshai](wiki:Asshai) to learn the arts of the [shadowbinders](wiki:Shadowbinder). In Asshai, a [moonsinger](wiki:Moonsinger) of the [Jogos Nhai](wiki:Jogos_Nhai) taught her their birthing songs, a woman of the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki) taught her their herb lore, and [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Marwyn](wiki:Marwyn) taught her [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) [medicine](wiki:Medicine) and human anatomy. Marwyn also taught Mirri the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue) of Westeros.(wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

[Khal](wiki:Khal) [Drogo](wiki:Drogo)'s [khalasar](wiki:Khalasar) fights and defeats Khal [Ogo](wiki:Ogo) outside Mirri's home town in [Lhazar](wiki:Lhazar). The Dothraki then attack and conquer Mirri's home, [enslaving](wiki:Slavery) the local people. When [Khaleesi](wiki:Khaleesi) [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) rides through the burned town, she sees Mirri being raped by one of Drogo's warriors, and puts an end to it.(wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur.cite_ref-Ragot68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
While Daenerys gives Mirri and the other Lhazareen rape victims protection from further attacks, Daenerys's husband Drogo is suffering from his battle wounds. Though Drogo is unconcerned, and he and his [bloodriders](wiki:Bloodriders) dislike the Lhazareen slave woman they consider to be a [maegi](wiki:Maegi), Daenerys convinces him to let Mirri Maz Duur treat him. She pulls the arrowhead from Drogo's chest, sews up the wound, and makes him a lambskin poultice of [firepods](wiki:Firepod) and [sting-me-not](wiki:Sting-me-not).(wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)
The poultice itches, and Drogo tears it off, causing the wound to fester. Drogo's condition deteriorates, until he falls from his [horse](wiki:Horse), a symbol to his warriors that he can no longer lead them. As Drogo lingers near death, Daenerys convinces Mirri Maz Duur to use her [blood magic](wiki:Blood_magic) to preserve his life. Though Mirri warns her that "only death may pay for life", Daenerys insists. Mirri performs a [sorcerous](wiki:Sorcery) ritual, sacrificing Drogo's [red stallion](wiki:Drogo's_stallion) in his tent, singing and dancing with dark shadows whirling around her. As the ritual continues, Daenerys goes into labor, and Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) brings her into the tent so that Mirri can help her give birth.(wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur.cite_ref-Ragot64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
However, Mirri betrays Daenerys in revenge for the attack on her village, and the ritual only brings Drogo to a catatonic vegetative state, while causing his son [Rhaego](wiki:Rhaego) to die in Daenerys' womb. Mirri justifies herself, saying that Daenerys had known the true price, and now there will be no "[stallion who mounts the world](wiki:Stallion_who_mounts_the_world)", burning cities and trampling nations to the ground. Mirri also implies, perhaps [prophetically](wiki:Prophecy), that Daenerys will never again bear a living child.(wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur.cite_ref-Ragot68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
Daenerys has Mirri Maz Duur burned to death upon Drogo's funeral pyre. Mirri initially sings, but as the flames engulf her she screams. Eventually, she falls silent, and Daenerys walks into the flames. The next morning, Daenerys is found, unharmed, with three [dragons](wiki:Dragon) hatched from the [eggs](wiki:Dragon_egg) that had been placed on Drogo's pyre.(wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

When Daenerys enters the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying) in [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), she is told in a [vision](wiki:Vision) that she would be betrayed "once for blood, once for gold and once for love".(wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) She believes Mirri was the first betrayal, for blood.(wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur.cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

After Daenerys marries [Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq), he hopes that they will conceive a son. Though Daenerys recalls Mirri's words, she keeps her doubts to herself.(wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur.cite_ref-Radwd50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
