## Origins

### Early life

Ser Criston was the son of [Lord Dondarrion](wiki:Lord_Dondarrion_(82_AC))'s steward;(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) according to a semi-canon source, Criston was common-born.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-7)

In [104 AC](wiki:104_AC), Criston won a melee at [the tourney](wiki:Tourney_for_King_Viserys_I's_accession) at [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) to celebrate the ascension of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), drawing the attention of the royal court by knocking [Dark Sister](wiki:Dark_Sister) from the hands of Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) with his morningstar. He gave the victor's laurel to the seven-year-old Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), whose favor he wore while unhorsing Daemon in the joust, as well as the twins Ser [Arryk](wiki:Arryk_Cargyll) and Ser [Erryk Cargyll](wiki:Erryk_Cargyll) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), before being unhorsed by Lord [Lymond Mallister](wiki:Lymond_Mallister). Thereafter, King Viserys indulged Rhaenyra by naming Criston her personal [sworn shield](wiki:Sworn_shield). In [105 AC](wiki:105_AC), the twenty-three-year-old Criston became a member of the Kingsguard, taking the place of the legendary Ser [Ryam Redwyne](wiki:Ryam_Redwyne).(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

Criston wore Rhaenyra's favor in [a tournament](wiki:History_of_Tourneys_in_Westeros) in [111 AC](wiki:111_AC) where he unhorsed Ser [Gwayne Hightower](wiki:Gwayne_Hightower), younger brother and champion to King Viserys's second wife and queen, [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower).(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) Half a year later, Daemon departed the capital after quarreling with his royal brother. According to [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom), Daemon had given explicit lessons to Rhaenyra so that she could seduce the noble Criston. When Rhaenyra finally approached the knight, however, the horrified Criston rejected the girl.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)

### Lord Commander

When Ser [Harrold Westerling](wiki:Harrold_Westerling), [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), died in [112 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Criston was named his successor to lead the white cloaks.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)

In [113 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) arranged for his reluctant daughter [Rhaenyra](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), [Princess of Dragonstone](wiki:Princess_of_Dragonstone), to wed Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon). According to Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), Criston slipped into Rhaenyra's bedchamber to confess his love, offering to abscond with her to one of the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) where he would pledge his sword to some merchant prince. Rhaenyra refused him, however, saying a [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) was meant to be more than the wife of a [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword), and that if he could forget his Kingsguard vows, he could forget marriage vows.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9) According to [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom), however, it was Rhaenyra who unsuccessfully attempted to seduce Criston in [White Sword Tower](wiki:White_Sword_Tower). The spurned princess took solace in Ser [Harwin Strong](wiki:Harwin_Strong), called Breakbones. Whatever the way of it, Criston went from being her staunchest defender to her bitterest foe, and Rhaenyra soon left for [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) with Harwin as her new sworn shield.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-10)

In [114 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Ser Criston attended the wedding of Rhaenyra to Laenor, which included seven days of feasting and jousting. He fought with Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower)'s token, and during a melee left Harwin with a broken collarbone and shattered elbow, prompting Mushroom to call him "Brokenbones" thereafter. Criston's morningstar also cracked the helm of Laenor's favorite, Ser [Joffrey Lonmouth](wiki:Joffrey_Lonmouth), which the Knight of Kisses died from six days later. While many were angered at Criston for this, including King Viserys, Queen Alicent did not share their displeasure, and asked that he be made her personal sworn shield. Criston became a staunch supporter of Alicent and a member of the [greens](wiki:Greens) ever after.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-11)

Criston tutored Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) to be a skilled swordsman, despite Aemond's one eye.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-12)

### The Dance of the Dragons

#### Aegon II

When King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) died in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), Criston and Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) assembled the [small council](wiki:Small_council) to discuss the succession, keeping the king's death a secret from the public. Criston objected to [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), [Princess of Dragonstone](wiki:Princess_of_Dragonstone), or her husband, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), holding power, and he also repeated the rumor that Rhaenyra's children from Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon) were [bastards](wiki:Bastard). Instead of Rhaenrya, the [greens](wiki:Greens) wanted to crown her younger half-brother, Prince [Aegon the Elder](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). According to Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), Criston cut the throat of Lord [Lyman Beesbury](wiki:Lyman_Beesbury), the only councilor to support Rhaenyra, while [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) claims that Criston instead flung Lyman to his death from a window; neither Eustace or Mushroom was present at the meeting, however.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Any [blacks](wiki:Blacks) who remained at court were taken by Criston into custody. When Criston went to Aegon to convince the prince to ascend the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Mushroom wrote that Criston found him with a young girl in [Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom), while Eustace claimed Aegon was in a more respectable situation with the daughter of a trader. According to the septon, Criston told the prince that he and his siblings would be killed by Rhaenyra if he did not claim the Iron Throne.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

During Aegon's coronation in the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit), Criston placed the crown of [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) on the young king's head.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Criston became known as the Kingmaker due to his support of Aegon II.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

#### Hand of the King

[Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), who was at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) at the time of [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen)'s coronation, refused to support her half-brother, beginning the great armed conflict which engulfed the Seven Kingdoms and became known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Many of the lords of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) were aware of [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen)'s wish to have Rhaenyra succeed him as [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen)'s first ruling queen, and threw their support behind the princess. As the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) gathered their forces and allies, Aegon II became frustrated with Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower), his grandfather and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), and instead awarded the office to Criston.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

In response to [Blood](wiki:Blood_(the_butcher)) and [Cheese](wiki:Cheese_(the_rat-catcher)) having killed young Prince [Jaehaerys Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_II)), the new Hand ordered a reluctant Ser [Arryk Cargyll](wiki:Arryk_Cargyll) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) to infiltrate Dragonstone in the guise of his twin, Ser [Erryk Cargyll](wiki:Erryk_Cargyll), who was supporting Rhaenyra on the island. Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) believed that Criston wanted Arryk to assassinate Rhaenyra herself, while Mushroom wrote that Arryk was supposed to kill Rhaenyra's sons, [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) and [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon). Criston's scheme faltered when Arryk and Erryk came face to face, however, and the twins killed each other in combat.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

Criston marched on [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) and [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth) and forced their levies to join his host of [greens](wiki:Greens), and he then led the [sack of Duskendale](wiki:Sack_of_Duskendale). The Kingmaker was prepared for the arrival of Princess [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)) and [Meleys](wiki:Meleys) at the [Battle at Rook's Rest](wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest), having planned for King Aegon II Targaryen on [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre) and Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) on [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) to wait in ambush. Rhaenys and Meleys were killed in the fighting, and Aegon was left crippled as well. Aemond and Criston killed the garrison of Rook's Rest and carried the head of [Lord Staunton](wiki:Lord_Staunton) back to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). When Aemond became [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm) and [Prince Regent](wiki:Regent) because of Aegon's injuries, Criston remained Hand.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

#### Harrenhal

Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) and Criston desired to retake [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) from Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), the husband of Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), who had gathered an army of [rivermen](wiki:Rivermen) to the great castle. While Lord [Jason Lannister](wiki:Jason_Lannister) marched toward the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye) with a host of [westermen](wiki:Westermen) in support of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), Criston departed [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with another four thousand [greens](wiki:Greens) and Aemond on [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar).(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) Jason was killed in the [Battle at the Red Fork](wiki:Battle_at_the_Red_Fork), however, and his westermen struggled on under Lord [Humfrey Lefford](wiki:Humfrey_Lefford).(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

After defeating Ser [Oswald Wode](wiki:Oswald_Wode) and Lords [Darry](wiki:Lord_Darry_(Dance)) and [Roote](wiki:House_Roote) by the shores of the Gods Eye, Criston's host found Harrenhal to have been abandoned by the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) when they finally arrived at the castle. Aemond saw it as a great victory, but he was outraged upon learning that Daemon had flown away on [Caraxes](wiki:Caraxes) to aid Rhaenyra with the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing). Meanwhile, Humfrey's [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) host was shattered in the [Battle by the Lakeshore](wiki:Battle_by_the_Lakeshore) by the rivermen and the [Winter Wolves](wiki:Winter_Wolves). In response to the blacks' victories, Criston urged Aemond to retreat south and join the host of greens led by Lord [Ormund Hightower](wiki:Ormund_Hightower) and Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)). Aemond refused, however, and decided to instead burn the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) with Vhagar's [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame).(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

#### Butcher's Ball

Criston marched south from [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) with his remaining host of thirty-six-hundred greens to meet the [Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower) host, traveling along the western shore of the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye). The [blacks](wiki:Blacks) destroyed villages and befouled waters on his line of march, denying supplies to the [greens](wiki:Greens). [Rivermen](wiki:Rivermen) such as young [Benjicot Blackwood](wiki:Benjicot_Blackwood), [Lord of Raventree](wiki:Lord_of_Raventree),(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) harried Criston's host as they marched south, and a dozen greens were killed at [Crossed Elms](wiki:Crossed_Elms) in an ambush planned by [Black Trombo](wiki:Black_Trombo).(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

While marching from the Gods Eye to the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), Criston's surviving host was met at a ridge by an army of thousands of blacks led by Ser [Garibald Grey](wiki:Garibald_Grey), Ser [Pate of Longleaf](wiki:Pate_of_Longleaf), and [Roderick Dustin](wiki:Roderick_Dustin), [Lord of Barrowton](wiki:Lord_of_Barrowton). Criston offered to yield if the blacks would spare the lives of his men, but he was refused. When Criston then challenged all three of his counterparts, Pate had [Robb Rivers](wiki:Robb_Rivers) and his archers strike down the Kingmaker with three arrows. Hundreds of Criston's men were then killed by the rivermen and [Winter Wolves](wiki:Winter_Wolves) in the ensuing [Butcher's Ball](wiki:Butcher's_Ball) in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC). Garibald and Pate brought Criston's head on a spear to the [First Battle of Tumbleton](wiki:First_Battle_of_Tumbleton).(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

Criston was eventually succeeded as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) by Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark), who served briefly,(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) and as [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) by Ser [Willis Fell](wiki:Willis_Fell), with both men serving [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen).(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

## Appearances & Description

Criston had coal black hair and pale green eyes. Many ladies described him as charming.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

Criston was an exceptionally skilled warrior, particularly with his [morningstar](wiki:Armament).(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) He is also known to have wielded a [longsword](wiki:Longsword) in battle.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) The Kingmaker rode a white [horse](wiki:Horse).(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

Trying to gain Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) for her plot to crown Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) tells Arys that [Anders Yronwood](wiki:Anders_Yronwood), [Lord of Yronwood](wiki:Lord_of_Yronwood), is Criston Cole reborn, as Arianne believes that Anders supports her younger brother, Prince [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell). Arys recalls a rumor that Criston had been Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen)'s lover before he joined the Kingsguard.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

While showing Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) the [White Book](wiki:White_Book), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), mentions the controversial Criston.(wiki:Criston_Cole.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)
