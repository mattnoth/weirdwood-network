## Origins

Tristifer ruled from the [Trident](wiki:Trident) to [the Neck](wiki:The_Neck) in the era when the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) were being attacked and overrun in the [Andal invasion](wiki:Andal_invasion). He raised the castle that became known as [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones), the strongest in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) at the time of its construction.

Tristifer was the last truly great king of the First Men in the Riverlands. When [Roland II Arryn](wiki:Roland_II_Arryn), the [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale), invaded the riverlands, Tristifer smashed his forces and forced the [Valemen](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) into retreat. One of Roland's allies then betrayed him to the river king, and Tristifer beheaded Roland at Oldstones.(wiki:Tristifer_IV_Mudd.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_vale:_the_eyrie.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Tristifer is said to have fought one hundred battles, winning ninety-nine and losing one. The [Tullys](wiki:House_Tully) fought alongside him in many of his campaigns. The one battle Tristifer lost was his last, where seven [Andal](wiki:Andals) kings attacked simultaneously; the greatest of the conquerors was [Armistead Vance](wiki:Armistead_Vance).(wiki:Tristifer_IV_Mudd.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Tristifer was succeeded by his son, [Tristifer V Mudd](wiki:Tristifer_V_Mudd), the last of [House Mudd](wiki:House_Mudd).(wiki:Tristifer_IV_Mudd.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
