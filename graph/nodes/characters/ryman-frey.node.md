## Origins

Ser Ryman participated in the [tourney at Lannisport](wiki:Tourney_at_Lannisport), the [tourney](wiki:Tourney) that was held to celebrate King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s victory over [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion). He was defeated by the eventual champion, Lord [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont).(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

## Appearances & Description

Ryman is a portly man of great appetites, prone to gluttony, drunkenness and whoring. His face is broad and fleshy and, according to Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), stupid. Ryman has small eyes.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Ryman is in his early fifties,(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Age_1-1) and is reported to have a bad belly.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Unlike his father [Stevron](wiki:Stevron_Frey), Ryman never received Lord [Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey)'s grooming on family values.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) considers Ryman dull-witted as a stone,(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) and [Merrett Frey](wiki:Merrett_Frey) thinks Ryman is stubborn and greedy.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

When Ser [Stevron Frey](wiki:Stevron_Frey) dies following the wounds he received in the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross), his son Ryman becomes heir of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey). Told about the death of Stevron by [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) in [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), [Big Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)) and [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)) discuss the line of succession in [their house](wiki:House_Frey). Little Walder asks Big Walder whether Ryman, whom he thinks is past forty already and has a [bad belly](wiki:Illness), will eventually become [Lord of the Crossing](wiki:Lord_of_the_Crossing), to which he receives an indifferent answer by Big Walder. The two are reprimanded by Luwin for not showing much grief over Stevron.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Ryman participates in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) campaign of [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) and [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident). When [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) criticizes Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) for removing Ser [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart) from the [Twins](wiki:Twins) to aid Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), Edmure includes Ryman in his examples of loyal Frey support for [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark).(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### A Storm of Swords

After Robb marries [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling), slighting [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey), Ryman strikes Robb's banner and leads his forces from the [Crag](wiki:Crag) back to the [Twins](wiki:Twins). He takes Robb's [squire](wiki:Squire) [Olyvar Frey](wiki:Olyvar_Frey) with him, although Olyvar asks to remain with the king. Robb later explains to Catelyn that, if Stevron had still been alive, he might have been able to make amends with the Freys in his host, but that was not possible with Ryman and his son [Black Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman)).(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

When Robb Stark arrives at the Twins for the wedding of [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully), now [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun), and [Roslin Frey](wiki:Roslin_Frey), a curt Ryman and his three sons ride out to welcome him.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

During the wedding feast, Catelyn observes the sour Ryman perspiring while drinking [wine](wiki:Wine), even though Ryman bathed in [lemonwater](wiki:Lemon). Ryman confirms for Catelyn that Olyvar and [Alesander Frey](wiki:Alesander_Frey), who are friendly to House Stark, are not present for the wedding, and he staggers from the hall. When the Freys and [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) betray Robb, an armored Ryman storms back into the hall, followed by soldiers armed with heavy longaxes, and he kills [Dacey Mormont](wiki:Dacey_Mormont) by thrusting his axe into her stomach. When Catelyn holds a dagger to the throat of [Jinglebell](wiki:Aegon_Frey_(son_of_Stevron)) to trade his life for Robb's, Ryman and his son Black Walder circle behind her back. Robb and Catelyn are both slain in what becomes known as the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Ryman takes two thousand spears to meet with Ser [Daven Lannister](wiki:Daven_Lannister) and Ser [Forley Prester](wiki:Forley_Prester) to begin the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun).(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

When Ryman's son, [Petyr Frey](wiki:Petyr_Frey), is taken [hostage](wiki:Hostage) by the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners), Ryman's uncle, [Merrett Frey](wiki:Merrett_Frey), volunteers to deliver the ransom, believing this would put him in the good graces of Ryman and prevent him from kicking Merrett and his family out of the Twins when Ryman succeeds Lord Walder. According to Merrett, Ryman was one of the principal architects of the Red Wedding, together with Lord Walder and Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton). The [outlaws](wiki:Outlaws) hang both Petyr and Merrett for their own involvement in the massacre.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

### A Feast for Crows

As heir of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), Ryman commands the [Frey](wiki:House_Frey) forces at the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun). The disorderly camp is located north of the [Tumblestone](wiki:Tumblestone), and hosts the largest of the three forces besieging the castle. He leaves the planning to his son [Edwyn](wiki:Edwyn_Frey), so he can spend his time drinking and [whoring](wiki:Whore). To upstage [Genna Lannister](wiki:Genna_Lannister), who has brought the singer [Whitesmile Wat](wiki:Whitesmile_Wat) to the [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) camp, Ryman hires a singer for his own camp as well, [Tom of Sevenstreams](wiki:Tom_of_Sevenstreams),(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) who is secretly a member of the outlaw [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners).(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) The Freys have a large stock of provisions, but Ryman refuses to share with the forces led by the new [Warden of the West](wiki:Warden_of_the_West), Ser [Daven Lannister](wiki:Daven_Lannister), and Ser [Forley Prester](wiki:Forley_Prester), leaving them to rely on foraging, during which their men are sometimes caught and killed.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

Ryman's main contribution to the siege is building a large gallows, on which Lord [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) has to stand with a noose around the neck all day. The threat of execution is meant to make Edmure's uncle, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), surrender Riverrun, but instead it hardens the Blackfish's stance. In a half-drunken state, Ryman rides to the gate of Riverrun and tries to threaten Brynden into submission. However, the Blackfish puts an arrow into Ryman's [horse](wiki:Horse), causing the panicked [animal](wiki:Animal) to throw him off, making Ryman a laughing stock to his allies and enemies.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

Ryman does not attend the war council called by Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard). He sends his son Edwyn with the excuse that he is indisposed, which as Daven points out, merely means that he is drunk. Edwyn tells Jaime that his father has stomach problems, and [wine](wiki:Wine) helps him with his digestion.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

Jaime goes to the Frey camp to persuade Edmure into commanding the capitulation of Riverrun. On his way, Jaime notices Ryman entertaining a [whore](wiki:Whore) in his tent; also present is Tom o' Sevens. The woman has [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark)'s [bronze](wiki:Bronze)-and-[iron](wiki:Iron) [crown](wiki:Crown) on her head, and states that Ryman crowned her the [queen o' whores](wiki:Queen_o'_whores). When Ryman ignores the advice to remain silent in Jaime's presence and protests the assumed plan of the Lord Commander to execute Edmure, Jaime strikes Ryman with his [golden](wiki:Gold) hand and removes him from his position, replacing him with Edwyn. Jaime commands Ryman to leave the camp at once and to take his whore with him, but not the crown.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

On his way back to the [Twins](wiki:Twins), Ryman and his escort of three [knights](wiki:Knight) and twelve [men-at-arms](wiki:Men-at-arms) are captured near [Fairmarket](wiki:Fairmarket) by the brotherhood without banners, and hanged. When Jaime learns of this at Riverrun, Edwyn accuses him of having his father's blood on his hands, then suggests his own brother [Black Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman)) might have been involved in the murder, although Black Walder is currently stationed at [Seagard](wiki:Siege_of_Seagard). Jaime notices that Edwyn is not mourning his father particularly, and suspects that might be true for most Freys. Still, Jaime is bothered by the audacity of [outlaws](wiki:Outlaw) who would kill the heir to the [Crossing](wiki:Crossing) so close to the Twins.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) ends the [siege of Raventree](wiki:Siege_of_Raventree), and demands Lord [Tytos Blackwood](wiki:Tytos_Blackwood)'s bookish son [Hoster](wiki:Hoster_Blackwood) as hostage. When Hoster does not seem to take the situation seriously, Jaime reminds Tytos that his son will die, if he does not keep his word. In this context, Jaime stresses that he is no Ryman Frey, meaning that, unlike the farce Ryman staged with Lord [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully), Jaime is ready to execute a prisoner.(wiki:Ryman_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
