## Origins

She was a young woman who married the old miller of the mill by the [Weeping Water](wiki:Weeping_Water) stream, who was twice her age. Soon after the [marriage](wiki:Marriage), Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) of the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort) was hunting a fox along the Weeping Water, when he came upon the mill and saw the miller's wife washing clothes in the stream. The moment Roose saw her, he desired her. According to the outlawed [first night](wiki:First_night) custom, it was Roose's right to bed his vassals' new brides. As the miller had married without his lord's permission or knowledge, Roose felt he had been cheated, and had him hanged. Roose then raped the miller's wife under the tree where her husband was hanging.(wiki:Miller's_wife.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

A year later, the miller's wife came to the Dreadfort with her infant son, [Ramsay](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), who she claimed was Roose's [bastard](wiki:Bastard). Roose considered having her whipped for her impudence, and killing her son, until he saw that the child had the same pale blue eyes as him. The woman claimed that when her dead husband's brother had seen the baby's eyes, he had beaten her and sent her away from the mill. Roose was annoyed by this, so he gave the woman possession of the mill, and [cut out the tongue](wiki:Tongue_mutilation) of the old miller's brother, so that he could not relate Lord Bolton's unlawful activities to his liege lord, [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Miller's_wife.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Every year, Roose Bolton would send the woman some piglets and chickens, and a bag of [stars](wiki:Currency), on the understanding that she was never to tell Ramsay who his father was. However, she disobeyed him, and often told Ramsay about his rights. One day, she returned to the Dreadfort to demand that Roose provide a servant for Ramsay, who was growing up wild and unruly. Roose gave her his foul-smelling servant [Reek](wiki:Reek), as a joke, but the man and Ramsay became inseparable. Ramsay stayed with his mother until [Domeric Bolton](wiki:Domeric_Bolton) returned to the Dreadfort after his [fostering](wiki:Fostering) in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) with [House Redfort](wiki:House_Redfort), and came to the mill by the Weeping Water to seek out his bastard brother for companionship; after Domeric died, Roose brought Ramsay to the Dreadfort as he lacked any other heirs.(wiki:Miller's_wife.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Miller's_wife.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Appearances & Description

She was tall and willowy, with long legs and small firm breasts. According to [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), she was pretty, "in a common sort of way", and very healthy-looking.(wiki:Miller's_wife.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance With Dragons

Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) asks [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) if his [bastard](wiki:Bastard), [Ramsay](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), has told him the story of his birth. Theon replies that Ramsay has said that Roose met his mother while out riding, and was "smitten by her beauty". Roose finds this description amusing, and tells Theon the true account.(wiki:Miller's_wife.cite_ref-Radwd32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
