## Appearances & Description

Robert is eight feet tall, with legs as thick as trees, a chest worthy of a plow [horse](wiki:Horse), and shoulders that would not disgrace an ox. His [armor](wiki:Armor) is [steel](wiki:Steel) plate enameled white, over [gilded](wiki:Gold) mail. A [greathelm](wiki:Greathelm) hides his face, with seven [silken](wiki:Silk) plumes in the rainbow colors of the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) streaming from the crest. A pair of golden seven-pointed stars clasp his white cloak to his shoulders.(wiki:Robert_Strong.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Ser Robert never shows his face, hiding it constantly under his greathelm. He apparently has no physiological necessities, consuming neither food nor drink nor needing to excrete.(wiki:Robert_Strong.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) According to Qyburn, Ser Robert has made a [vow of silence](wiki:Vow_of_silence), supposedly one of not speaking until *"all of [King [Tommen](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon)'s] enemies are dead and evil has been driven from the realm"*.(wiki:Robert_Strong.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) dies of a [poisoned](wiki:Poison) wound, screaming in the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells) of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), under the care of the former [maester](wiki:Maester), [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn),(wiki:Robert_Strong.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) who had been expelled from the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) for unethical experiments and studying [necromancy](wiki:Necromancy).(wiki:Robert_Strong.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Qyburn sends the [Mountain's skull](wiki:Mountain's_skull) to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), as ordered.(wiki:Robert_Strong.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

After Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) leaves for the [siege of Dragonstone](wiki:Siege_of_Dragonstone), Qyburn suggests to Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) that the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) may need a replacement in case Loras dies, and mentions a "champion" who "no living man will be able to withstand". Qyburn orders a massive suit of [armor](wiki:Armor) for his proposed champion, though it is supposedly too large and heavy to be worn by any man.(wiki:Robert_Strong.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

After Cersei is arrested by the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) for adultery and other crimes, Qyburn tells her that his champion is ready, to defend Cersei and prove her innocence in battle. However, Cersei informs Qyburn that as a queen, she can only be represented in a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) by a [knight](wiki:Knight) of the Kingsguard, and there are no open positions available.(wiki:Robert_Strong.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Cersei learns that Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) of the Kingsguard has died in [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). She tells her uncle, Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), [Lord Regent](wiki:Lord_Regent), to bring Qyburn a white cloak and have her son, King [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), appoint the man Qyburn presents to the Kingsguard.(wiki:Robert_Strong.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

When Queen Cersei arrives at the Red Keep after [walking naked](wiki:Walk_of_atonement) from the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), Ser Robert Strong lifts her up and carries her into the castle. Qyburn introduces Robert to Cersei as her and her son's champion, which pleases her greatly.(wiki:Robert_Strong.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

Later, at a meeting of the [small council](wiki:Small_council), Kevan announces that Cersei will be championed by Ser Robert Strong in her trial. Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) grimaces at the idea, and Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) demands to know who Ser Robert is and where he came from. Kevan remembers that Ser [Meryn Trant](wiki:Meryn_Trant) claimed that Robert took neither food nor water, and Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount) said he had never even seen him use the privy. Kevan has inexplicit suspicions about the [identity of the man](wiki:Gregor_Clegane), suspicions he believes are shared by Mace and Randyll. However, Kevan makes clear to the small council that it is in everyone's best interests that Ser Robert's identity not be questioned and that he must prevail in Cersei's trial. If Ser Robert fails and Cersei is found guilty, Tommen's legitimacy as king and, thus, [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell)'s status as queen, would be thrown into question, hurting both [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) and [House Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell).(wiki:Robert_Strong.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
