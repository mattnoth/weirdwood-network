## Origins

Once [lords](wiki:Lord), [House Connington](wiki:House_Connington) were demoted to [landed knights](wiki:Landed_knight) as a result of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) Ser [Ronald Connington](wiki:Ronald_Connington), the [Knight of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Knight_of_Griffin's_Roost), betrothed his eighteen-year-old son Ronnet to [Brienne](wiki:Brienne_Tarth), the daughter of [Selwyn Tarth](wiki:Selwyn_Tarth), the [Lord of Tarth](wiki:Lord_of_Tarth). Ronnet was opposed, having been told that Brienne was ugly.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) When they first met at [Evenfall Hall](wiki:Evenfall_Hall), the two had the same height, although Ronnet was six years older. Brienne remembers how Ronnet brought a [rose](wiki:Rose) to give to his betrothed. She was not able to look him in the eye or speak the simple words of welcome that she had been taught, however.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) When Ronnet handed Brienne the rose, he told her that this was all she would ever have from him, as he had no real interest in marrying her.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6)

## Appearances & Description

Ronnet is a husky man(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) with a long red beard and long red hair.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

The [Knight of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Knight_of_Griffin's_Roost) wears a red-and-white surcoat adorned with [griffins](wiki:Griffin),(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) and his shield is also decorated with a griffin.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Red Ronnet is considered fierce(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) but can be disparaging.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) The knight enjoys [wine](wiki:Wine).(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

The exiled [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) is not resentful toward Ronnet or his late father, [Ronald Connington](wiki:Ronald_Connington).(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Ronnet, now the [Knight of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Knight_of_Griffin's_Roost),(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) is with King [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon)'s host at [Bitterbridge](wiki:Bitterbridge). He participates in a [great mêlée](wiki:Melee_at_Bitterbridge) in which he is defeated by his once-betrothed, [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth),(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) who enjoys her victory.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Ronnet is among the lords and knights who follow Renly to [Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)) and switch sides to King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) after Renly's death.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Ronnet survives the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), either being taken captive or turning sides in the midst of the conflict, and he is presented to King [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) in the [throne room](wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep)) to swear his allegiance.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

### A Storm of Swords

Red Ronnet remains in King's Landing.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Feast for Crows

When [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) meets [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne) for the first time and he is unable to even pronounce his own name, she is reminded of her own shyness when she met Ronnet when she was twelve.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) watches knights riding at a [quintain](wiki:Quintain) in the yard of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), with Ser Ronnet breaking his lance clean.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Ronnet accompanies Jaime as his standard bearer when the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) departs King's Landing to resolve the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), and he joins Jaime and others for supper at [Hayford](wiki:Hayford) on the first night of their voyage.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)

At [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), Jaime comes across Ronnet as he inspects the bear pit; Jaime had rescued Brienne from the pit when she was forced by [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat) to fight a [bear](wiki:Bear).(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) When Ronnet speaks rudely about Brienne, Jaime strikes his mouth with his golden hand, sending Ronnet tumbling down the steps. Jaime demands that Ronnet speak of Brienne respectfully, so the knight responds by calling her "Brienne the Beauty" and spitting blood at Jaime's feet.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8)

To remove Ronnet from his presence, Jaime charges him with escorting Ser [Wylis Manderly](wiki:Wylis_Manderly) to [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool).(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Jaime has some of the [Mountain's men](wiki:Mountain's_men) join Ronnet as he delivers Wylis to Maidenpool,(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) and the heir of Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly) then returns to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) One of the men-at-arms kills a man in Maidenpool and another is accused of rape, so Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) hangs the first and gelds the latter.(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

During the [landing of the Golden Company](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company), the exiled [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) leads the [taking of Griffin's Roost](wiki:Taking_of_Griffin's_Roost). He is relieved to learn that Ronnet is away, said to be fighting in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) with Jaime. Jon takes hostage Ronnet's siblings, [Raymund](wiki:Raymund_Connington) and [Alynne](wiki:Alynne_Connington), as well as his bastard son, [Ronald Storm](wiki:Ronald_Storm).(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Ronnet arrives in King's Landing with twenty men-at-arms, most of them Mountain's men once sworn to Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane). Ronnet is brought before Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell), Lord Tarly, and the other members of the [small council](wiki:Small_council), to determine whether he was involved when the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) attacked the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands). Insisting he is loyal to King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), Ronnet declares he will fight his uncle and the supposed [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(Young_Griff)). The council decides to put Ronnet under guard in his chambers until they march on Jon and the Golden Company, where he will be given a chance to prove his loyalty. Regarding Ronnet's promise to deliver the heads of Jon and Aegon, Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) mentions Jon's failed promise to King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) to bring him the head of [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). Randyll suggests sending Ronnet and all in his company to the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Ronnet_Connington.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)
