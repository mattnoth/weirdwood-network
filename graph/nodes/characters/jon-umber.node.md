## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Jon Umber (Greatjon)](wiki:Category:Images_of_Jon_Umber_(Greatjon))*

Jon is a large man, the same height as [Hodor](wiki:Hodor) (who is seven feet tall)(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-6) but twice as wide.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) He is heavily muscled and is a formidable warrior with fists as large as hams.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) regards him as one of the strongest living men in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) when thinking of who could match him in a fight.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

The Greatjon is proud, boisterous, and fierce,(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) and impressed only by those who earn his respect.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) He does not often respect decorum(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) and he believes that women should not be on a battlefield.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Jon's speaking voice is a bass rumble,(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) but he surprisingly has a decent singing voice.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) The Greatjon is a prodigious drinker(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) considered a sweaty oaf by some.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

Jon wields the biggest, ugliest [greatsword](wiki:Greatsword) that [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) has ever seen, which makes it bigger than [Ice](wiki:Ice).(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) He wears mail(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) and carries a [warhorn](wiki:Horn).(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Jon, accompanied of his sons and brothers, leads his men when [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) calls the [bannermen](wiki:Bannermen) of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). At first he challenges the young lord's authority, threatening to take his forces home if he is placed behind Lords [Halys Hornwood](wiki:Halys_Hornwood) or [Medger Cerwyn](wiki:Medger_Cerwyn) in the order of march. After being called out by Robb, the Greatjon draws his sword, only to have the [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf) [Grey Wind](wiki:Grey_Wind) bite off two of his fingers. Impressed with Robb's courage, the Greatjon makes a joke of it instead of becoming angry and becomes Robb's greatest champion. Jon shoves [Osha](wiki:Osha) out of the way when the [wildling](wiki:Wildling) servant talks to Robb.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

The Umbers reside at the [Children's Tower](wiki:Children's_Tower) when Robb's host camps at [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin).(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Jon suggests that Robb take the fight to Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). Robb decides to split his host in two, with his horse marching southwest to relieve [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) and his foot marching south to confront Tywin. Robb considers giving command of the infantry to Jon, but he instead chooses Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) to lead them after [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) suggests using a more cautious man than the Greatjon.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

When the northern host splits at the [Twins](wiki:Twins), Greatjon goes south with Robb's force along with his son, [the Smalljon](wiki:Jon_Umber_(son_of_Jon)), who is one of [Robb Stark's personal guard](wiki:Robb_Stark's_personal_guard). The Umber lances participate in the [battle in the Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood), and a captured Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) is dragged by the Greatjon and [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy).(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

While Ser [Brynden Blackfish](wiki:Brynden_Blackfish) attacks Jaime's host at Riverrun from the north during the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), Umbers are among the armored horse who attack from the west. The Greatjon sets fire to the siege towers being built by outside the castle.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

After Riverrun is relieved and news of Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark)'s death arrives, the Greatjon prays in the castle's [godswood](wiki:Godswood) with other followers of the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods). The Greatjon and Theon sit closest to Robb during their war council in Riverrun's Great Hall. He calls Ser [Stevron Frey](wiki:Stevron_Frey) a craven for suggesting caution, and he is dismissive of Catelyn's call for peace. The Greatjon is the first of the assembled nobles to declare Robb the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North).(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

### A Clash of Kings

Lord Jon is present when [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) explains his terms for peace with the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in the Great Hall of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun). He barks at Ser [Cleos Frey](wiki:Cleos_Frey) when the envoy does not initially acknowledge Robb as a king. Jon believes that the [red comet](wiki:Red_comet) is a symbol of [northern](wiki:North) vengeance for the execution of Robb's father, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark),(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) and he believes that [direwolves](wiki:Direwolves) were sent to the [Stark](wiki:House_Stark) children by the old gods.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Robb convinces Catelyn to treat with [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) by threatening to send the bombastic Greatjon instead.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Jon's uncles, [Mors Crowfood](wiki:Mors_Umber) and [Hother Whoresbane](wiki:Hother_Umber), remained at the [Last Hearth](wiki:Last_Hearth) as the castle's castellans. During the [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast), Hother complains that the Greatjon took too many soldiers south and the Last Hearth lacks men to gather the harvest.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

After participating in the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross) with Robb, the Greatjon goes on to capture the gold mines at [Castamere](wiki:Castamere), [Nunn's Deep](wiki:Nunn's_Deep), and the [Pendric Hills](wiki:Pendric_Hills).(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Storm of Swords

While at the [Crag](wiki:Crag), the Greatjon informs Robb of the [supposed deaths](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell) of his brothers, [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) and [Rickon](wiki:Rickon_Stark). Robb then sleeps with [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling).(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) When Robb breaks his marriage contract with the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) by wedding Jeyne, the Freys in his host angrily depart the Crag. The Greatjon urges Robb to attack them, but the king declines. The Greatjon offers to make marriage contracts for his uncles, Mors and Hother, in Robb's place.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

The Greatjon returns to Riverrun with Robb after the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater). While a vengeful Lord [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark) is outraged that Catelyn released Jaime from imprisonment at Riverrun, the Greatjon is more understanding and attributes Catelyn's actions to a mother's folly.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) The Greatjon threatens to kill Rickard with a spear after the Lord of [Karhold](wiki:Karhold) murders [Tion Frey](wiki:Tion_Frey) and [Willem Lannister](wiki:Willem_Lannister), prisoners of Robb. The king orders Jon to hang Rickard's accomplices, and Robb beheads Lord Karstark personally.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Lord Umber attends the funeral of [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully), [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun).(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

The Greatjon leads the van on the march to the [Twins](wiki:Twins) for the wedding of Lord [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully) and [Roslin Frey](wiki:Roslin_Frey). In Robb's plan to retake [Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin) from the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn), the Greatjon is given command of the van and told to make his diversionary attack convincing, as Robb's own force is to circle behind Moat Cailin with the guidance of the [crannogmen](wiki:Crannogmen).(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

In preparation for the Freys' betrayal of House Stark, [Merrett Frey](wiki:Merrett_Frey) is tasked by [Lame Lothar Frey](wiki:Lothar_Frey) with getting the Greatjon too drunk to fight.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) During the wedding feast at the Twins, the Greatjon drunkenly sings "[The Bear and the Maiden Fair](wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair)" while musicians perform "[Flowers of Spring](wiki:Flowers_of_Spring)", and he then sings "[The Lusty Lad](wiki:The_Lusty_Lad)" when the musician play "[Iron Lances](wiki:Iron_Lances)". Merrett matches Jon cup for cup, but [Whalen](wiki:Whalen_Frey) and [Petyr Frey](wiki:Petyr_Frey) pass out trying to keep pace with the Lord of the Last Hearth. Jon carries a crying Roslin over his shoulder and takes her from the hall as part of the [bedding](wiki:Bedding) customs.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

Despite the Greatjon having consumed enough [wine](wiki:Wine) to kill three normal men, eight men are needed to subdue him in chains when the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding) begins. He takes the sword of the first man to attack him, kills one, wounds two others, and bites off half the ear of Ser [Leslyn Haigh](wiki:Leslyn_Haigh).(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) The Greatjon's son and heir, [the Smalljon](wiki:Jon_Umber_(son_of_Jon)), is slain during the massacre.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

### A Feast for Crows

The Greatjon is held captive at the Twins.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Following the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), Jaime informs Ser [Edwyn Frey](wiki:Edwyn_Frey) and [Walder Rivers](wiki:Walder_Rivers) that King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) requires the transfer of all captives taken by House Frey in the Red Wedding.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Lord Umber continues to be a prisoner at the Twins.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Half of [House Umber](wiki:House_Umber) under [Hother Whoresbane](wiki:Hother_Umber) swears fealty to [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), though [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) and [Barbrey Dustin](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin) think this is only because the Greatjon is still held captive.(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) The other half of the Umbers, under [Mors Crowfood](wiki:Mors_Umber), take up the cause of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)(wiki:Jon_Umber.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)
