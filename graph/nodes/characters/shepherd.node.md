## Appearances & Description

The Shepherd was an itinerant brother of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), one-handed, religious, and insane. [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) called him "the Dead Shepherd" because he resembled a corpse.(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
The thin man wore a [hair shirt](wiki:Hair_shirt) and roughspun breeches, and he hung a begging bowl about his neck.(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

## Origins

### Origin[ ]

[Munkun](wiki:Munkun) suggests the Shepherd was one of the outlawed [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows). [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) believes the Shepherd lost a hand for thievery.(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

### Dance of the Dragons[ ]

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the Shepherd began to preach against Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) in [Cobbler's Square](wiki:Cobbler's_Square) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), saying that [dragons](wiki:Dragons) were [demons](wiki:Demons), the spawn of godless [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), and the doom of men.(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) believes that the Shepherd spread the story that Rhaenyra sent Ser [Luthor Largent](wiki:Luthor_Largent) to murder Queen [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen).(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
During the [riots in King's Landing](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) the Shepherd led a mob in the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit), where three of the [blacks](wiki:Blacks)' dragons and one of the [greens](wiki:Greens)' dragons dwelled. A tale oft repeated in King's Landing names the Shepherd as the slayer of the dragon [Syrax](wiki:Syrax), Rhaenyra's mount. The Shepherd allegedly stood alone against the dragon, calling upon the Seven for succor, until the [Warrior](wiki:Warrior_(the_Seven)) manifested and beheaded Syrax.(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

### Moon of the Three Kings[ ]

After the [Storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit) and Rhaenyra's flight, the Shepherd and his mob ruled much of the city from the ruins of the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit). This period was dubbed the [Moon of the Three Kings](wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings) by maesters even though the Shepherd never claimed kingship, styling himself a simple son of the Seven. He preached every night among the heads of the five slain dragons, which were set up on posts by his followers.(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
The Shepherd preached against [lords](wiki:Lord), [knights](wiki:Knight), and rich men, commanding his followers to cast off fine attire and walk barefoot with roughspun robes. Although thousands obeyed, his crowds grew smaller with each passing night. Other factions of rioters seized control of other parts of the city, particularly [Trystane Truefyre](wiki:Trystane_Truefyre) at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) on [Aegon's Hill](wiki:Aegon's_Hill) and [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair) on [Visenya's Hill](wiki:Visenya's_Hill).(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

### Arrest and Death[ ]

When Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon)'s army neared [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), the Shepherd ordered his flock to keep him from crossing the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), but few obeyed from his dwindling mob. Forewarned by the fate of [Gaemon Palehair](wiki:Gaemon_Palehair), the Shepherd called upon his "[barefoot army](wiki:Barefoot_lambs)" to assemble around the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) to defend the [Hill of Rhaenys](wiki:Hill_of_Rhaenys). Less than three hundred answered his call, however, and they were easily defeated by Borros and Ser [Perkin the Flea](wiki:Perkin_the_Flea). Cursing Borros, the captured Shepherd was carried to the Red Keep in chains and put in a dungeon alongside the other two false kings to await the return of [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and his judgment.(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
The Shepherd refused to repent when brought before Aegon, cursing the crippled king instead. Aegon ordered the Shepherd's tongue torn out with hot pincers and condemned him and his followers to death by fire. On the last day of [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), two hundred forty-one of the Shepherd's most dedicated followers were covered with pitch and chained to poles on both sides of Hill Street, which ran from [Cobbler's Square](wiki:Cobbler's_Square) to the Dragonpit. As [septs](wiki:Sept) rang bells in celebration, Aegon's knights set each captive ablaze as the king traveled to the Dragonpit. With the aid of two [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), Aegon set aflame the Shepherd, who was chained amongst the heads of the five slain dragons.(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

### Legacy[ ]

Following the burning of the Shepherd and his followers, Hill Street became known as [Shepherd's Way](wiki:Shepherd's_Way).(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) was killed in the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad), and King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) was murdered by [poison](wiki:Poison).(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
During the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf) a two-handed man claiming to be the [Shepherd reborn](wiki:Shepherd_reborn) rose and called for the death of the [northmen](wiki:Northmen) in Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark)'s army, as they believed in the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods) instead of the [Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). Hundreds flocked to hear him,(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) but [gold cloaks](wiki:Gold_cloaks) removed his tongue.(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Another [false Shepherd](wiki:False_Shepherd) claiming to be the "Shepherd Reborn" was executed by Ser [Victor Risley](wiki:Victor_Risley) by order of Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC).(wiki:Shepherd.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
