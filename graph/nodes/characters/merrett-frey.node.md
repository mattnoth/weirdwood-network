## Origins

### Youth

Merrett desired to be a great [knight](wiki:Knight) in his youth. He served with his mother [Amarei's](wiki:Amarei_Crakehall) relative, Lord [Sumner Crakehall](wiki:Sumner_Crakehall), as a [page](wiki:Page) and later a [squire](wiki:Squire). Like many [Crakehall](wiki:House_Crakehall) descendants, he was known for his strength and robustness. During this time, he knew [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), also a squire at [Crakehall Castle](wiki:Crakehall), although the two were not close because of Merrett's habit of bullying others, which was not to Jaime's liking. Jaime is implied to have turned the tables on Merrett when the [Frey](wiki:House_Frey) boy tried to bully him.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### Kingswood Brotherhood

The downfall in Merrett's life began with the expedition against the [Kingswood Brotherhood](wiki:Kingswood_Brotherhood). First he caught a [pox](wiki:Pox) from a [camp follower](wiki:Camp_follower), and later he was captured by [Wenda the White Fawn](wiki:Wenda_the_White_Fawn) and was branded on his arse with her mark, a [fawn](wiki:Fawn). Merrett was freed after Lord Sumner paid a ransom, but he was unable to sit for a fortnight and became the target of cruel jokes by the other squires. In his next fight in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), Merrett was hit with a mace that broke his [helm](wiki:Helm), leaving him insensible for a fortnight. As a result, Merrett began suffering immense pain whenever his head was touched.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

Lord Crakehall gently explained he would never be able to grant knighthood to Merrett because of his injuries, and he sent the young man back to the [Twins](wiki:Twins). Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) treated his son with disdain,(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and Merrett began drinking excessively to numb the pain of his headaches.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

### Family

Merrett married [Mariya Darry](wiki:Mariya_Darry) around [282 AC](wiki:282_AC), and this was considered an excellent match at the time. However, soon after the wedding, [House Darry](wiki:House_Darry) lost most of their influence and power due to [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), in which they declared for King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen). Merrett fathered five daughters before the marriage produced a son, [Little Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)). One daughter died when she was still a child, one was stillborn, and two other daughters shame their parents with promiscuity ([Gatehouse Ami](wiki:Amerei_Frey)) and gluttony ([Fat Walda](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Merrett))). Merrett arranged for Ami to marry Ser [Pate of the Blue Fork](wiki:Pate_of_the_Blue_Fork) after the girl was caught having sex with stablehands, and [Tom of Sevenstreams](wiki:Tom_of_Sevenstreams) performed at their wedding.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

## Appearances & Description

Merrett is a big, broad man of middle height. In his youth, he was almost as robust as his older brother [Hosteen](wiki:Hosteen_Frey), the strongest son of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), but in later years Merrett became soft and fleshy.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) remembers Merrett as slow, clumsy, and stupid in his youth, with strength being his only positive quality.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Merrett is unable to become a [knight](wiki:Knight) because of persistent headaches caused by a head wound in his youth. Merrett tries to prevent the headaches, which can bring blinding pain and reduce him to tears, by drinking [alcohol](wiki:Alcohol) throughout the day. When his headaches become too severe, Merrett rests in a dark room with a damp cloth over his eyes. Fond of [ale](wiki:Ale) and red [wine](wiki:Wine), he has gained the dubious distinction of being the biggest drunk in the [Twins](wiki:Twins).(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Merrett is despised by his wife, [Mariya Darry](wiki:Mariya_Darry), and treated with disdain by his father, Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey).(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Merrett considers his wife a shrew and his children worthless. He hates woods and especially [outlaws](wiki:Outlaws), whom he often drunkenly blames for having ruined his life.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) He is familiar with [Jenny's song](wiki:Jenny's_song).(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

Merrett carries a sword but does not own a [horse](wiki:Horse).(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey)'s kin are present at the [Twins](wiki:Twins) when Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) comes to treat with the [Lord of the Crossing](wiki:Lord_of_the_Crossing) on behalf of her son, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark). As part of the terms allowing Robb to cross at the Twins, Robb agrees to eventually marry one of Walder's daughters, and Merrett's son, [Little Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)), is to be fostered at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Merrett's daughter [Walda](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Merrett)) marries Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) in [a wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Roose_Bolton_and_Walda_Frey) at the Twins.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Racok64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Merrett is pleased at Walda's marriage, believing his luck is changing and that her wedding will gain him favor because of House Frey's alliance with [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton).(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13) However, Merrett learns that Lord Walder had offered Roose the choice of a daughter or a granddaughter with her weight in [silver](wiki:Silver) as [dowry](wiki:Dowry).(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Merrett is once more disappointed when Walder says he is not going to thank his son for fathering a daughter who made him pay a larger dowry than necessary because of her gluttony.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14)

During the planning of the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), Merrett is humiliated when [Lothar Frey](wiki:Lothar_Frey) assigns him to get Lord [Jon Umber](wiki:Jon_Umber) too drunk to fight.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15) Merrett matches the Greatjon cup for cup during much of the feast, and Ser [Whalen Frey](wiki:Whalen_Frey) and [Petyr Frey](wiki:Petyr_Frey) pass out trying to keep pace with their [wine](wiki:Wine) drinking.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) When the Freys try to capture Lord Umber, however, the Greatjon is still sober enough to kill two men, break another's arm, and take part of Ser [Leslyn Haigh](wiki:Leslyn_Haigh)'s ear off before being subdued.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16) Robb Stark, now [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) and [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident), is killed by Lord Bolton during the Red Wedding.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Catelyn Stark's throat is slit by Merrett's brother, Ser [Raymund Frey](wiki:Raymund_Frey), and her body is tossed into the [Green Fork](wiki:Green_Fork).(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-17)

The [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners), the outlaw band led by Lord [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion), kidnaps Petyr in the aftermath of the Red Wedding. Merrett volunteers to deliver the ransom of a hundred [golden dragons](wiki:Currency) for his great-nephew, hoping to gain favor within House Frey as he has no prospect of maintaining an existence on his own once Lord Walder dies. Walder laughs at the suggestion of Merrett dealing with [outlaws](wiki:Outlaw), but eventually gives his consent.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-18)

Merrett nervously drinks wine when traveling to [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones), and the outlaws claim the bag of gold from him once he arrives. Merrett finds Petyr Pimple hanged in the ruined castle's [godswood](wiki:Godswood) for his involvement in the Red Wedding, and the brotherhood prepare to hang Merrett after taking his bag of [gold](wiki:Gold). The outlaws ask Merrett for the whereabouts of [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) and [a young girl](wiki:Arya_Stark), but Merrett knows nothing about them. When the outlaws bring up Robb's death at the Red Wedding, Merrett insists it was not murder, but justified vengeance for Robb insulting House Frey by marrying [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling). Merrett protests that all he did was drink and instead blames Lord Walder, Lame Lothar, [Ryman Frey](wiki:Ryman_Frey), [Walder Rivers](wiki:Walder_Rivers), and Roose Bolton. When Merrett asks for proof of his involvement, the brotherhood reveals a revived Catelyn Stark. Asked whether Merrett was part of the Red Wedding, Catelyn just nods, as she cannot speak anymore. Merrett is then hanged by the brotherhood.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-19)

### A Feast for Crows

Hunting the murderers of Merrett and Petyr, Lord [Lucias Vypren](wiki:Lucias_Vypren) follows one band to [Fairmarket](wiki:Fairmarket) but loses them there. Another group is pursued from [Hag's Mire](wiki:Hag's_Mire) by [hounds](wiki:Dog) and hunters led by [Black Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman)), but their trace is lost when they reach the [Neck](wiki:Neck). [Smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) who are questioned describe a [hooded woman](wiki:Lady_Stoneheart) who is believed to be their leader, but nothing is heard of Beric or [Thoros of Myr](wiki:Thoros_of_Myr). This creates doubts whether the brotherhood is responsible.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

While traveling to [Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) stops at [Darry](wiki:Darry), the new seat of his cousin, Lord [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister). Jaime meets Merrett's widow, [Mariya Darry](wiki:Mariya_Darry), and his eldest daughter, [Amerei](wiki:Amerei_Frey), who is now Lancel's wife. Remembering Merrett to have been a bully, Jaime only praises Merrett as having been strong. When Amerei brings up her father's stories of fighting the [Kingswood Brotherhood](wiki:Kingswood_Brotherhood), Jaime thinks of them as exaggerations and lies. Jaime raises a toast to Merrett, considering it easier to drink to the man than talk about him. Amerei later mentions that Ser [Harwyn Plumm](wiki:Harwyn_Plumm) has promised her Beric's head and she tries to coax Jaime into finding the killers of her father as well, although without success.(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

### A Dance with Dragons

Merrett's son, [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)), serves as a squire to [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow),(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) who is the stepson of Merrett's daughter, [Walda](wiki:Walda_Frey_(daughter_of_Merrett)).(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) However, Walder is later found murdered at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Merrett_Frey.cite_ref-Radwd51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
