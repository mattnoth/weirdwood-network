## Origins

### Dragon Egg

Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen)'s three eldest sons by her husband, Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon), were [Jacaerys](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) (b. [114 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), [Lucerys](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon) (b. [115 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), and [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon) (b. [117 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)). The [rival faction](wiki:Greens) at court, who supported her stepmother [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) and her children, spread the accusation that Rhaenyra's three sons were not really Laenor's, but [bastards](wiki:Bastard) fathered by Ser [Harwin Strong](wiki:Harwin_Strong). Rhaenyra's father, King [Viserys I](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), in an effort to confirm that he considered Rhaenyra and her sons the true heirs to the throne, decreed that a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) would be put in the cradle of each of the boys, to bond with the dragons that hatched from them. Those who doubted the paternity of Rhaenyra's sons also doubted that the eggs would ever hatch, but they were quickly disappointed, as each of the eggs soon hatched in turn. The hatchlings were named Vermax (bonded with Jacaerys), [Arrax](wiki:Arrax) (bonded with Lucerys), and [Tyraxes](wiki:Tyraxes) (bonded with Joffrey). The exact date each hatched is not known, but the last seems to have hatched by no later than about [120 AC](wiki:120_AC).(wiki:Vermax.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### The Dance of the Dragons

By the time the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) began in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), Vermax was big enough to ride. Prince Jacaerys flew [north](wiki:North) on his back, as a messenger for his mother, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), to treat with [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn), the [Lady of the Eyrie](wiki:Lady_of_the_Eyrie), [Desmond Manderly](wiki:Desmond_Manderly), the [Lord of White Harbor](wiki:Lord_of_White_Harbor), and [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark), the [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell). The mission was a success, as all three declared for Rhaenyra and her [blacks](wiki:Blacks).(wiki:Vermax.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Snow, ice, and cold made Vermax irritable, so Jacaerys did not stay in the north for long, despite receiving a warm welcome at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell). In his [testimony](wiki:The_Testimony_of_Mushroom), [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) claims that Vermax laid a clutch of [eggs](wiki:Dragon_egg) in the depths of the [crypts of Winterfell](wiki:Crypts_of_Winterfell), but this tale is most likely false. [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn) states it is difficult to tell the sex of a dragon, but that no other source mentions that Vermax ever produced an egg, and dismisses [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Barth](wiki:Barth)'s theory that dragons can change sex as "ludicrous".(wiki:Vermax.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)(wiki:Vermax.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Prince Jacaerys used Vermax in battle, during the [Battle in the Gullet](wiki:Battle_in_the_Gullet). There, Vermax attacked the [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy)'s fleet, and victory seemed at hand. But Vermax flew too low, possibly wounded from a [crossbow](wiki:Crossbow) bolt to the eye or pulled down by a grapnel, and crashed into a burning [galley](wiki:Galley). Jacaerys leapt free and clung to the smoking wreckage of the ship, but was struck by [Myrish](wiki:Myrish) crossbow quarrels until he sank into the sea, dead.(wiki:Vermax.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Appearances & Description

In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), Vermax was thriving and growing larger every year.(wiki:Vermax.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Vermax became ill-tempered when in proximity of snow, ice, and cold.(wiki:Vermax.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Despite his youth, Vermax was strong.(wiki:Vermax.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Vermax's coloring is not stated in the books. However, he is depicted as green in illustrations from *[The Rise of the Dragon](wiki:The_Rise_of_the_Dragon)*;(wiki:Vermax.cite_ref-Rtrotd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) more specifically, in the television adaptation *[House of the Dragon](wiki:House_of_the_Dragon)*, he has olive green scales and pale orange wing membranes.(wiki:Vermax.cite_ref-6) His [flames](wiki:Dragonflame) were possibly orange, too.(wiki:Vermax.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Vermax.cite_ref-Gullet_7-0)
