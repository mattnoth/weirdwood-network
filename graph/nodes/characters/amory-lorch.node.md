## Origins

According to a semi-canon source, during the [Reyne-Tarbeck rebellion](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_rebellion) in [261 AC](wiki:261_AC), Ser Amory threw the [last Lord Tarbeck](wiki:Last_Lord_Tarbeck), a three-year-old boy, down a well in [Tarbeck Hall](wiki:Tarbeck_Hall). It is disputed whether this was done per the orders of Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), or without his knowledge.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-10)

In [283 AC](wiki:283_AC), Amory was responsible for the murder of the three-year old Princess [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing). Per the order of Tywin Lannister, Amory and [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) scaled the walls of [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) to access the royal apartments.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Alongside and in front of several [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) [men-at-arms](wiki:Men-at-arms),(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) he dragged the screaming toddler from under her father [Rhaegar's](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) bed, and when she kicked him, stabbed her dozens of times.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

## Appearances & Description

Ser Amory is a stout man with a piggy face,(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) little pig's eyes,(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and a high, thin voice.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

The knight is regarded as a cruel and unintelligent, brutal man.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) He is the sort of man to order the deaths of non-combatants as if it bores him.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

Amory's [breastplate](wiki:Armor) is decorated with ornate scrollwork,(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) and a [manticore](wiki:Manticore) is depicted on his shield.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) His [warhorse](wiki:Horse) is armored.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

At the onset of the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) in [299 AC](wiki:299_AC), Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) orders his brother, Ser [Kevan](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), to direct Ser Amory, along with his fellow bannerman Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) and the [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat), to forage the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) and to burn what he does not take. Each of the raider captains is to have three hundred cavalry.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### A Clash of Kings

At a [holdfast](wiki:Holdfast) in [a town](wiki:Gods_Eye_town) by the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye), Amory's raiding party encounters [Yoren](wiki:Yoren)'s group of recruits for the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), with [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) hidden among them. Even though the Watch takes no part in the wars of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), Amory orders his men-at-arms to storm the holdfast,(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6) and Yoren is killed in the [fight at the holdfast](wiki:Fight_at_the_holdfast).(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Because of Yoren's fate, Arya adds Amory's name to the [list of people she wants to kill](wiki:Arya_Stark).(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Amory departs the town without burying any of the dead.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

Amory captures two innocent [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), a beekeeper and his wife, and threatens far and wide to hang them if Lord [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion) does not surrender to him. When Beric surrenders, Amory hangs the beekeeper and his wife anyway, with Beric strung up in the middle. Beric is later resurrected by [Thoros of Myr](wiki:Thoros_of_Myr).(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Rasos39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Arya Stark, disguised as a servant in [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), is offered the deaths of three people by [Jaqen H'ghar](wiki:Jaqen_H'ghar). She considers Amory for one of them, before deciding on [Chiswyck](wiki:Chiswyck).(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

When [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) takes the majority of his army east to engage [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), he makes Amory [castellan](wiki:Castellan) of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), with the [Bloody Mummers](wiki:Bloody_Mummers) also left behind to do the foraging.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) While castellan, Amory resides in the castellan's chambers in [Kingspyre Tower](wiki:Kingspyre_Tower), with only a hundred men left to guard the rest of Harrenhal,(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) and drinks a great deal of the best [wine](wiki:Wine) in the castle's cellars.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

Amory is eventually captured by Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), after Roose persuades [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat) to betray [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) and to seize control of the castle. After the [fall of Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal), Amory is paraded naked before Roose and Vargo. As he pleads and sobs, he is kicked into the [bear](wiki:Bear) pit to be killed by the bear inside it. Arya Stark is present to witness Amory's death, and mentally notes, "The bear is all in black. Like Yoren."(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) The bear tears Amory apart.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

### A Storm of Swords

[Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) finds a human jawbone in the bear pit of Harrenhal. As it still has maggoty flesh on it, it may have been Amory's.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) comes to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to seek justice for the deaths of his sister Princess [Elia](wiki:Elia_Martell) and her children, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) and Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) tells his son [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) his plan to blame all three of the deaths entirely on the deceased Amory (who in truth had only killed Rhaenys), to circumvent giving Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) over to Oberyn. According to Tywin, Amory justified his brutal murder of Rhaenys because the screaming child had kicked him. Tywin plans to tell Oberyn that Amory acted on his own, without orders, to gain favor from the new king, [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), and hopes that Amory's recent brutal death will assuage Oberyn's need for justice.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

Later, to spite his father, Tyrion tells Oberyn that Amory Lorch did kill Rhaenys, but also that Gregor Clegane killed Elia and Aegon.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

### A Feast for Crows

In [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), tells Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) that his charge, Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), may be in danger. When Arys protests that only a beast would hurt a little girl, Doran reminds him of Amory and Rhaenys.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

In [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), Arya Stark sees people peacefully welcoming death at the [House of Black and White](wiki:House_of_Black_and_White), and recalls how Amory had begged in fear when thrown into the bear pit.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

At the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) reminds [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) about the [curse of Harrenhal](wiki:Curse_of_Harrenhal) and of the deaths of all who have held the castle recently, including Amory Lorch.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

Ser [Roger Hogg](wiki:Roger_Hogg) tells [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) how Amory attacked his towerhouse at [Sow's Horn](wiki:Sow's_Horn) and slaughtered his animals, even though he was sworn to [House Hayford](wiki:House_Hayford), who are sworn to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

In Sunspear, [Ellaria Sand](wiki:Ellaria_Sand) wishes for the end of the Martell hostilities against House Lannister, as all those who had a hand in the deaths of Elia and her children are dead, including Amory Lorch.(wiki:Amory_Lorch.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)
