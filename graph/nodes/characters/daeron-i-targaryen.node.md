## Origins

### Youth

Daeron was born in [143 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) as the firstborn son and heir of King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) and Queen [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon). He had a younger brother, Prince [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen), and three younger sisters, Princesses [Daena](wiki:Daena_Targaryen), [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_III)), and [Elaena](wiki:Elaena_Targaryen). When his father died in [157 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the prince was crowned King Daeron I in [157 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) at the age of fourteen. Daeron kept his uncle, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), as his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), as his uncle had held the position for Aegon III as well. Viserys did not insist upon a regency for the young king.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### Conquest of Dorne

Daeron had long felt that the continued independence of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) represented unfinished business for the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen), and upon his ascension he vowed to rectify his ancestors' mistake. Viserys and other councillors objected to the young king's plans, however, reminding Daeron that [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and his sisters had failed twice in trying to conquer Dorne with [dragons](wiki:Dragons), and that the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) now had none. Daeron famously replied, "You have a dragon. He stands before you".(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

Daeron planned his [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne) with the aid of Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon). The campaign showed great promise as Daeron went over and revised the mistakes that were made in the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War). Daeron split his army into three for the invasion and personally led the army down the [Boneway](wiki:Boneway). Learning from the mistakes made by Lord [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon) in the first war, Daeron used goat paths located in the pass to avoid traps and ambushes which [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) usually implemented to repel invaders. A second army led by Lord [Lyonel Tyrell](wiki:Lyonel_Tyrell_(lord)) marched down the [Prince's Pass](wiki:Prince's_Pass). The third army was a naval assault by Alyn Oakenfist along the Dornish coast, and the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) broke the [Planky Town](wiki:Planky_Town).(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

After several battles, the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne_(father_of_Maron)) and two score Dornish lords bent the knee in the [Submission of Sunspear](wiki:Submission_of_Sunspear) in [158 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), within a year of the invasion's beginning. Daeron became the first Targaryen king to conquer Dorne and unite all [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) for the first time, despite the loss of ten thousand men in taking the peninsula.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Daeron wrote *[The Conquest of Dorne](wiki:The_Conquest_of_Dorne)* about his achievement.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) The book was known for its style and simplicity,(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) but in it Daeron exaggerated the numbers of his foes to make his conquest seem greater.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Daeron spent some time in Dorne to consolidate his conquest and eliminate rebels. An assassination attempt on the king was prevented by the actions of his cousin, Prince [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), who threw himself in the path of a poisoned arrow meant for Daeron. Aemon the Dragonknight survived the poison, however, and was sent back to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to recover.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5) Leaving Lord Tyrell in charge in Dorne, Daeron returned to the capital in [159 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), bringing fourteen Dornish nobles with him as hostages.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6) Daeron intended to wed one of his sisters to the [Sealord of Braavos](wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos) to seal an alliance with him, with the aim of removing the [pirates](wiki:Pirates) that were hindering trade with Dorne. According to [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Kaeth](wiki:Kaeth)'s *[Lives of Four Kings](wiki:Lives_of_Four_Kings)*, this talk of a marriage alliance with [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), at the time warring with [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) and [Lys](wiki:Lys), caused those other Free Cities to lend aid to the Dornish rebels.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### Rebellion and Death

Although the Dornish nobility had submitted to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), the Dornish [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) continued to resist. Daeron's steward in Dorne, Lord [Lyonel Tyrell](wiki:Lyonel_Tyrell_(Lord)), attempted to stamp out rebellion, and forty or fifty thousand men are said to have died in the next three years.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-7) A clever trap at [Sandstone](wiki:Sandstone) killed Lyonel and sparked a great uprising, however, leading Daeron to return to Dorne in [160 AC](wiki:160_AC).

The Young Dragon achieved victories along the [Boneway](wiki:Boneway) while Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) dealt with the [Planky Town](wiki:Planky_Town) and the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood).(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-8) Thinking the Dornish were again willing to submit, Daeron met with them under a peace banner in [161 AC](wiki:161_AC). However, in a betrayal, the Dornishmen attacked Daeron and his retinue. The eighteen-year-old king died with [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre) in his hand, surrounded by a dozen enemies. Three knights of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) were slain while defending him — including Ser [Olyvar Oakheart](wiki:Olyvar_Oakheart)(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) — one yielded, and Prince [Aemon the Dragonknight](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)) was captured, though Aemon managed to slay two of the betrayers before being taken into custody.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-9)

Daeron was succeeded by his brother, [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen), and the Dornish hostages in King's Landing were thrown into the dungeons by Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) The [Weeping Town](wiki:Weeping_Town) received its name from the townspeople's reaction when Daeron's corpse arrived from Dorne.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Although most lords were vengeful after Daeron's death, Baelor the Blessed forgave the assassins and brought about a rapprochement with the Dornish.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

In the city of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), there is a statue of King Daeron I astride a tall stone horse, his sword lifted toward Dorne.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Kaeth](wiki:Kaeth) wrote about Daeron's reign in *[Lives of Four Kings](wiki:Lives_of_Four_Kings)*.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

## Appearances & Description

Young Daeron was handsome, being clean-shaven with long hair.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1253.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) A warrior and conqueror, Daeron was self-confident, charismatic,(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1253.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) and possessed prodigious military brilliance.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafdaeron_i_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) He was also an elegant writer.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Daeron has been considered great by some but mad by others.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm945.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) He was idolized by his sister, Princess [Daena Targaryen](wiki:Daena_Targaryen),(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1605.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) considers the Young Dragon as one of his heroes.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Daeron wore the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) [circlet](wiki:Crowns_(object)) of [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and owned an elaborate suit of gold-and-black plate.(wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1253.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)
