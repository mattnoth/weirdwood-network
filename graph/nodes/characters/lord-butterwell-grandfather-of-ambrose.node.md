## Origins

When King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) visited Lord Butterwell's castle he gifted him with a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) in return for "access" to his three [maiden](wiki:Maiden) daughters.(wiki:Lord_Butterwell_(grandfather_of_Ambrose).cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) According to tales, Aegon impregnated all three girls the same night.(wiki:Lord_Butterwell_(grandfather_of_Ambrose).cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

He had [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls) built, the pale castle was commonly called the Milkhouse by the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) who lived near it. Its walls, keep, and towers were made of white stone quarried in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), and its floors and pillars were crafted from white [marble](wiki:Marble) veined with [gold](wiki:Gold). The rafters were carved from the trunks of [weirwoods](wiki:Weirwood).(wiki:Lord_Butterwell_(grandfather_of_Ambrose).cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
