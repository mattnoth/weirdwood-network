## Origins

Brienne was born to Lord [Selwyn Tarth](wiki:Selwyn_Tarth), Lord of [Evenfall Hall](wiki:Evenfall_Hall), on the island of [Tarth](wiki:Tarth). Her mother died when she was so young that she does not remember her.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) She had a single older brother, [Galladon](wiki:Galladon_Tarth), who drowned when he was eight and she was four, and two sisters, both of whom died in the cradle.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2) This makes her the only living offspring of Lord Selwyn, a fact that naturally encouraged him to find a suitable match for her. Given her ugly and ungainly appearance and attitude, however, the task was a difficult one, especially since Brienne was bent on a martial life rather than a marital one, more comfortable with a sword than in the company of court society. She was tutored by her [septa](wiki:Septa), [Roelle](wiki:Roelle), and had the equivalent of a knight's training by a master-at-arms, Ser [Goodwin](wiki:Goodwin).(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) Brienne has known Ser [Cortnay Penrose](wiki:Cortnay_Penrose) since she was a girl.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Brienne has learned many songs by heart since Evenfall Hall always had singers in her youth.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)

Brienne's father successfully found three men willing to betroth her, though all the betrothals fell through. A younger son of Lord [Bryen Caron](wiki:Bryen_Caron) was Brienne's first betrothed. They met only once, on the occasion of their betrothal, when she was seven and he was ten. The boy died two years later from a chill, along with his father, mother and sisters. Had he not died, Brienne would have been wed to him within the year of her first flowering.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) The second betrothal was to Ser [Ronnet Connington](wiki:Ronnet_Connington), who was six years older than her. On their meeting, Red Ronnet gave her a [rose](wiki:Rose), and told her it was all she would have of him, breaking the betrothal as he felt she was a "freak".(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) The last man Brienne was betrothed to, Ser [Humfrey Wagstaff](wiki:Humfrey_Wagstaff), warned her he would insist she act like "a proper woman" once they were wed. Though only sixteen, Brienne replied she would only accept such a demand from a man who could beat her in combat. In the effort, Humfrey ended up with three broken bones and a broken betrothal.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

After that, Lord Selwyn stopped trying to arrange a marriage for Brienne.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) Brienne's only positive encounter with a man occurred when [House Tarth](wiki:House_Tarth)'s liege, Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), visited their island during his [coming of age](wiki:Customs) [progress](wiki:Progress). He crossed paths with Brienne and treated her courteously, and she fell in love with him.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) Sometime later, Lord Selwyn sent his daughter to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) Renly's [fool](wiki:Fool) has made cruel japes about Brienne.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)

## Appearances & Description

Brienne is unfeminine in appearance and is considered unattractive by [Westerosi](wiki:Westeros) standards.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) She is tall, muscular, flat-chested, and ungainly,(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) with long, shoulder-length brittle straw-colored hair(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and broad, coarse features that are covered in freckles. Brienne's teeth are prominent and crooked. Her mouth is wide, her lips are swollen, and her nose has been broken more than once.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) She has large, beautiful blue eyes.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Her fingers are thick and callused.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Brienne is horse-faced.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

When asked about her height, [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin) replied:

> Brienne is well over six feet tall, but not close to seven, no. Just off the top of my head, I would say Brienne is taller than [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) and [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) and significantly heavier than either, but nowhere near the size of [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane), who is the true giant in [the series](wiki:A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire). Shorter than [Hodor](wiki:Hodor) and the [Greatjon](wiki:Jon_Umber), maybe a bit shorter than the [Hound](wiki:Sandor_Clegane), maybe roughly the same height as [Robert](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).*(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rssm1147.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)*

Brienne has spent most of her life as the object of scorn, rejection and—at best—pity. When she tried to dress and act like a proper lady, she was met with mockery and disdain for her unattractive appearance and lack of feminine social graces; when she turned to a career more suited to her talents as a warrior, she received only contempt and resentment for her gender despite her considerable skill.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Perhaps because of this background, Brienne is as awkward in personality as she is in appearance. While Lady [Maege](wiki:Maege_Mormont) and [Dacey Mormont](wiki:Dacey_Mormont) have found respect and acceptance as female warriors,(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Brienne yearns for respect and acceptance from others, and easily gives her love and loyalty to the few people who treat her with any degree of genuine courtesy.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Brienne hates [roses](wiki:Rose)(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) because of her history with [Ronnet Connington](wiki:Ronnet_Connington).(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Brienne can be headstrong and judgmental, but she is also honest, straightforward, loyal and determined. Although she has received poor treatment from most [knights](wiki:Knight), Brienne clings to a naively idealized concept of knighthood. Despite—or perhaps because of—the fact that she has no expectation of ever being recognized by society for her accomplishments, she nevertheless strives to live up to the ideal of a [true knight](wiki:True_knight) at all times.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

Instead of a lady's attire, Brienne wears a velvet doublet quartered rose-and-azure, breeches, boots, and a fine-tooled swordbelt.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) Brienne's dented [steel](wiki:Steel) armor is a brilliant, deep blue [cobalt](wiki:Cobalt), and she also has mail.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) Brienne has a [sword](wiki:Sword),(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) and she wields a blunt morningstar during melees.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) She also knows how to wield an axe.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Her black [destrier](wiki:Horse) is barded with the quartered sun-and-moon heraldry of [House Tarth](wiki:House_Tarth).(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Brienne supports [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) when he declares himself [king](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms). She travels from [Tarth](wiki:Tarth) to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) by way of [Bronzegate](wiki:Bronzegate), [Bitterbridge](wiki:Bitterbridge), and the [roseroad](wiki:Roseroad).(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rawoiafjourneys.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Brienne cries bitter tears(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-3) when Renly is [wed](wiki:Wedding_of_Renly_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell) to [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell) at Highgarden.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) In Renly's camp outside Highgarden, Brienne is continuously mocked. Some of the young knights wager on who will be the first to get her [maidenhead](wiki:Maidenhead) and begin to court her and treat her chivalrously, until Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) puts an end to it, warning Brienne that her presence is the cause of the problem.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Brienne joins Renly in his march to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11) She proves her mettle with a morningstar in a [melee at Bitterbridge](wiki:Melee_at_Bitterbridge), defeating Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell), the Knight of Flowers, as well as many of her mocking suitors. She asks to join the [Rainbow Guard](wiki:Rainbow_Guard) as her prize, becoming Brienne the Blue. Renly awards her the rainbow cloak he had been hoping to give to Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy). Brienne sharply reminds [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), an envoy of [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), the new [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), of the courtesy due Renly as a king. Brienne is later dismayed when Renly denies the need for an escort during his stroll with Catelyn after the night's feast.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

At Renly's parley with his brother, [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), at the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), Brienne is his standard-bearer. After the negotiations fail, she is assigned to do the same in the upcoming clash, riding in the vanguard alongside Loras, the [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) of the Rainbow Guard.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) The next day at dawn, she is arming Renly for battle against his brother when a [shadow](wiki:Shadow_assassin) enters his tent and assassinates him. Brienne is accused of the murder by Ser [Emmon Cuy](wiki:Emmon_Cuy). Since her own sword is absent, Brienne fends off Emmon with Renly's blade. Catelyn convinces Ser [Robar Royce](wiki:Robar_Royce) that Brienne is innocent, and she takes Brienne with her when the [northern](wiki:North) envoys flee the camp.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

When Loras finds Renly's body, he kills Emmon and Robar, both members of the Rainbow Guard, in a rage.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) As Stannis continues his siege of Storm's End, Ser [Cortnay Penrose](wiki:Cortnay_Penrose) defends Brienne's honor after she is accused by Ser [Guyard Morrigen](wiki:Guyard_Morrigen) of having slain Renly.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2)

Brienne leaves her plate armor and horse at Storm's End, but keeps the rainbow cloak. After discarding her other blood-stained clothes, Brienne receives spare garb from the large Ser [Wendel Manderly](wiki:Wendel_Manderly), one of Catelyn's escorts. When they arrive at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), Brienne wants to leave to take revenge with Renly's sword on Stannis for Renly's death, but Catelyn convinces Brienne to enter her service instead.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) Brienne remains within Riverrun to help defend the castle during the [Battle of the Fords](wiki:Battle_of_the_Fords). Catelyn gives her gowns, mail, and boiled leather.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5) Brienne is present when Lady Stark speaks with the Kingslayer, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), in his dungeon cell, and gives Catelyn her sword when it is asked for.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

### A Storm of Swords

At [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), after receiving news that her sons [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) and [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark) have been [killed](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell), Catelyn decides to free Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), making Brienne escort him to King's Landing to fulfill the promise made by Jaime's brother [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), to return her daughters [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark) if the Kingslayer were freed. Ser [Cleos Frey](wiki:Cleos_Frey), Jaime's cousin, accompanies them as an envoy.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

Brienne defends Jaime ably. She learns much of his history that is little known to Westeros at large and the two develop a grudging respect for each other. They make for the capital by boat, throwing off pursuit from Riverrun when Brienne scales a cliff and rolls a boulder onto Ser [Robin Ryger](wiki:Robin_Ryger)'s pursuing galley.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) The trio depart the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork) and continue by land. At the [Inn of the Kneeling Man](wiki:Inn_of_the_Kneeling_Man), Brienne's wariness prevents the group from taking [the innkeeper's](wiki:Husband) travel advice, as he is in league with bandits and is attempting to lead them into ambush.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

Later, they are ambushed by different outlaws on the [Duskendale road](wiki:Duskendale_road) near [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool). Cleos is killed and Jaime tries to escape, dueling Brienne with Cleos's sword. Manacled and out of practice, Jaime is surprised to learn that Brienne is stronger than him.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) Jaime cannot defeat Brienne, who holds his head underwater. Their fighting is interrupted by the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions), however. Four Bloody Mummers beat Brienne, knocking out two of her teeth. [Zollo](wiki:Zollo) then chops off Jaime's sword hand.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) His fighting ability gone, Jaime despairs for his life, but Brienne convinces him to live for revenge. In turn, Jaime prevents Zollo, [Rorge](wiki:Rorge), and [Shagwell](wiki:Shagwell) from raping Brienne.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) The Brave Companions confiscate Brienne's armor and Renly's sword.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

At [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), the two share a bath and Jaime reveals he stopped the [wildfire plot](wiki:Wildfire_plot) of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) brings her a pink gown, which Jaime thinks she looks ridiculous wearing. Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) sends Jaime on his way, but keeps Brienne as [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat)'s prize.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) Jaime returns after dreaming of her, however, and rescues her from the [bear](wiki:Bear) pit of Harrenhal.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Brienne bites [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat)'s ear,(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) which leaves him delirious from infection.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) [Steelshanks Walton](wiki:Steelshanks_Walton) escorts Brienne and Jaime toward King's Landing, and Brienne is shocked when Ser [Bertram Beesbury](wiki:Bertram_Beesbury) informs them at [Brindlewood](wiki:Brindlewood) of Catelyn's death in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)

After their arrival in King's Landing, the pair are confronted by Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell), who still believes Brienne guilty of Renly's regicide. Jaime has Brienne arrested for her own protection,(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-2) and he arranges for Loras to speak to her; this finally dispels Brienne's blame.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

Jaime entrusts Brienne with the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) longsword given to him by his father [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), naming it [Oathkeeper](wiki:Oathkeeper). He sends her to seek the vanished [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), to fulfill his promise to Catelyn. As Oathkeeper was forged from the metal of [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark)'s greatsword, [Ice](wiki:Ice), Brienne is using Stark steel to rescue the Stark girl. Along with this, Jaime gives her two bags full of coin, a mail hauberk, a [Lothston](wiki:House_Lothston) shield, and a letter signed by King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) saying that she is on a royal mission.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) is sent by his sister, Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), to subdue the remaining rebels in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands). At [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) he strikes [Ronnet Connington](wiki:Ronnet_Connington) for mocking Brienne.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

Unbeknownst to Brienne, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) is hiding at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) under the assumed name of Alayne Stone with Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish).(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) Brienne searches for Sansa along the [Rosby road](wiki:Rosby_road) in the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands), meeting [Illifer the Penniless](wiki:Illifer_the_Penniless), [Creighton Longbough](wiki:Creighton_Longbough), and [Shadrich](wiki:Shadrich), although she leaves them behind at the [Old Stone Bridge](wiki:Old_Stone_Bridge).(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3) She visits the [Dun Fort](wiki:Dun_Fort) in [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) and has her shield repainted to the arms of Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall). [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne), once the squire of Sansa's husband [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), tries to discretely follow her on the [Duskendale road](wiki:Duskendale_road), and Brienne allows the boy to join her quest at the [Hollard castle](wiki:Hollard_castle).(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4)

Brienne's next stop is at [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), which she only enters with the unwanted aid of Ser [Hyle Hunt](wiki:Hyle_Hunt), one of the knights who had mockingly courted her in Renly's camp. There she finds Lord [Randyll Tarly](wiki:Randyll_Tarly) dispensing justice; he begrudgingly allows her to continue her search. At the [Stinking Goose](wiki:Stinking_Goose) she meets [Nimble Dick Crabb](wiki:Dick_Crabb), who leads her after a fool, whom she believes is [Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard), who might have Sansa with him.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Brienne wishes Jaime Lannister had come with her, but realizes his place is with the king.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)

Traveling through [Crackclaw Point](wiki:Crackclaw_Point), the trio arrive at the [Whispers](wiki:Whispers), the ruined hall of Nimble Dick's ancestors from [House Crabb](wiki:House_Crabb). They meet a band of [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions) remnants led by the fool [Shagwell](wiki:Shagwell), the psychotic jester. Shagwell kills Dick, but Brienne then kills [Pyg](wiki:Pyg) and [Timeon](wiki:Timeon) with [Oathkeeper](wiki:Oathkeeper). She forces Shagwell to dig a grave for Dick and then kills him as well when the fool turns on her. Hyle joins her party, having followed her from Maidenpool.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3)

Following the [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword)' information, Brienne heads back to the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) to find [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane), who was reported to be with a girl, unbeknownst to her to be [the wrong daughter](wiki:Arya_Stark). At Maidenpool they join with [Meribald](wiki:Meribald), an itinerant septon, who accompanies them to [Saltpans](wiki:Saltpans). From there she visits the nearby [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle), where the [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)) assures her that the [Hound](wiki:Hound) is dead.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-4)

At the [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads) near the [Trident](wiki:Trident), Brienne encounters another group of former Brave Companions led by [Rorge](wiki:Rorge), who has been masquerading as Sandor while raping and murdering in the riverlands. Defending the orphans at the inn, the outnumbered Brienne kills Rorge but is grievously wounded by [Biter](wiki:Biter), who chews the flesh off her cheek.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Before passing out, she sees what she thinks is his foot-long tongue, but in reality is the bloody tip of the spear [Gendry](wiki:Gendry) uses to kill Biter and save her. Despite rescuing her, Gendry and the remaining members of the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners) take her captive, along with Podrick and Hyle. Delirious from the bite, she calls out for Jaime Lannister.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

The undead [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), now leading the brotherhood as "Lady Stoneheart", does not believe Brienne's explanation of her search for Sansa and instead judges her to be a traitor who is serving [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister), due to her sword with a golden lion's head as its pommel, and the letter signed and sealed by King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) stating that Brienne is "about his business".(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1) Brienne is given the choice of "sword or noose" — either prove her loyalty by killing Jaime Lannister, or hang. Brienne refuses to choose and is sentenced to hang, along with her two companions, Podrick and Ser Hyle.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-2) According to a semi-canon source, when she sees Podrick choking, she shouts "sword" to save him.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-3)(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Rssm6425.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Having been spared by the brotherhood, Brienne appears briefly at [Pennytree](wiki:Pennytree) when Jaime camps there. She asks Jaime to help her save [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) from the [Hound](wiki:Hound), claiming they are a day's ride away. She tells Jaime, however, that he must go with her alone, or the Hound will kill Sansa.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) Cersei is skeptical when she is informed that her twin, Jaime, disappeared with a woman in the riverlands.(wiki:Brienne_Tarth.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)
