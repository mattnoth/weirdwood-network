## Identity

Rhaenyra Targaryen (97–130 AC) — child of Aemma Arryn and Viserys I Targaryen; married to Daemon Targaryen and Laenor Velaryon; sworn to Blacks; Princess.

## Origins

### Early life

Born in [97 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Rhaenyra was the only living child of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) by his first wife, Lady [Aemma](wiki:Aemma_Arryn) of [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn). Rhaenyra had two brothers, both of whom died in the cradle.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)

Rhaenyra became a [dragonrider](wiki:Dragonrider) at the age of seven, when she flew into the sky on the back of a young dragon, whom she named [Syrax](wiki:Syrax). A year later, when she was eight, Rhaenyra became a cupbearer for her father, serving him at the table, at tourneys, and at court. From that point onward, King Viserys was seldom seen without Rhaenyra.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

At a young age, Rhaenyra became enamored with her uncle, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen). The roguish prince often brought her exotic gifts from his trips across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). During the [tourney for King Viserys I's accession](wiki:Tourney_for_King_Viserys_I's_accession), Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) won the melee by disarming Daemon. The knight granted the victor's laurel to Rhaenyra and asked the seven-year-old girl for her favor in the lists. Rhaenyra became smitten with Criston when he joined the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) in [105 AC](wiki:105_AC), calling him her white knight, and Viserys allowed the charming knight to become Rhaenyra's personal shield and protector. From that moment on, Cole always wore Rhaenyra's favor in the lists and protected the girl during public events.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6)

Late in [105 AC](wiki:105_AC), Rhaenyra's mother, Aemma, died birthing Rhaenyra's second brother, Prince [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)), who died a day later. King Viserys then proclaimed Rhaenyra his heir, having all the lords of the Seven Kingdoms swear fealty to her, promising to honor and defend her rights of succession. In addition, Rhaenyra was named [Princess of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone).(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7)

King Viserys remarried in [106 AC](wiki:106_AC), to Lady [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower), and stepmother and stepdaughter initially got along well. That changed when Alicent birthed the king two sons, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) in [107 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) and [Aemond](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) in [110 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), yet Viserys did not proclaim either boy his heir. People continued to ask Viserys about the succession, though Viserys was unwilling to discuss it. Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower), Alicent's father and Viserys's [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), was sent away from court for pushing the issue too much. But even after he left, clearly two parties formed at court: the party of the Queen, and the party of the Princess.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8)

In [111 AC](wiki:111_AC), a great tourney was held at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) on the fifth anniversary of King Viserys's marriage to Queen Alicent. At the opening feast, the queen wore a green gown, whilst Princess Rhaenyra dressed dramatically in [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) red and black. Note was taken, and thereafter it became the custom to refer to “[greens](wiki:Greens)” and “[blacks](wiki:Blacks)” when talking of the queen's party and the party of the princess, respectively. The blacks had greater success in the tourney as Criston, wearing Rhaenyra's favor, unhorsed all of the queen's champions, including two of her cousins and her youngest brother, Ser [Gwayne Hightower](wiki:Gwayne_Hightower).(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-9)

According to the accounts of Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), prior to her turning sixteen, Rhaenyra lost her [virginity](wiki:Maidenhead) to her uncle, Prince Daemon, after he seduced her. The account of the fool [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) is more scandalous, however, claiming that Daemon gave Rhaenyra lessons on how to seduce and please a man, lessons in which Mushroom insists he took part as well. Mushroon further claimed that Daemon secretly smuggled Rhaenyra from her rooms dressed as a page boy, and took her to [brothels](wiki:Brothel) on the [Street of Silk](wiki:Street_of_Silk), where she could observe the act of love and learn the "womanly arts" from [prostitutes](wiki:Prostitute). Mushroom's account differs from Eustace's account, in that Mushroom claims Rhaenyra remained a virgin during these lessons. According to Mushroom, Rhaenyra received those lessons from Daemon because she was in love with Criston Cole, and she wanted him to see her as a woman. Whichever account is true, (Grand Maester [Runciter](wiki:Runciter) simply claims that the two brothers quarreled), King Viserys sent his brother into exile.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-9)

### Ruling Princess of Dragonstone

At the age of sixteen, in [113 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Rhaenyra took possession of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). She had been courted by many lords and nobles who sought her hand and her favor. The sons of Lords [Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood) and [Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken) had dueled over the [Princess of Dragonstone](wiki:Princess_of_Dragonstone) when she visited the [Trident](wiki:Trident) in [112 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and the so-called [Fool Frey](wiki:Forrest_Frey) asked for her hand in marriage. The twin knights [Jason](wiki:Jason_Lannister) and [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister) wooed her at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), and she was also courted by the sons of Lords [Oakheart](wiki:House_Oakheart), [Tarly](wiki:House_Tarly), [Tully](wiki:House_Tully), and [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell). Ser [Harwin Strong](wiki:Harwin_Strong), the son of Lord [Lyonel Strong](wiki:Lyonel_Strong), also courted the princess. King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) considered wedding his daughter to [Prince](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne) [Qoren Martell](wiki:Qoren_Martell) to bring [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) into the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), while Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) pondered a marriage between Rhaenyra and her eldest son, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-10)

Rhaenyra instead married Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon) in [114 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), though it took Viserys's threat of removing her status as heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) to convince her to accept the marriage, as Rhaenyra did not feel the need to marry a man with interests like Laenor's. The princess was reported to have stated that "My half brothers would be more to his taste", to her father. Laenor's closest friend, Ser [Joffrey Lonmouth](wiki:Joffrey_Lonmouth), was mortally wounded by Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) during the wedding tourney. Laenor chose to spend most of his time at [High Tide](wiki:High_Tide), while his wife preferred to remain at court. Rhaenyra was very fond of Laenor's sister, Lady [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon), who had wed Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) after the death of his first wife, Lady [Rhea Royce](wiki:Rhea_Royce).(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-11)

Laenor and Rhaenyra had three sons together: Princes [Jacaerys 'Jace'](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon), [Lucerys 'Luke'](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon), and [Joffrey 'Joff' Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon). It was rumored that those sons of Rhaenyra's were actually fathered by her lover, Harwin Strong, called "Breakbones". All three were born with brown hair, brown eyes, and features considered "common" such as a pug nose. However, Laenor and Rhaenyra both had the silver-gold hair and [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes) of [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), and Laenor had an aquiline nose.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-12)(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-10) The true parentage of Rhaenyra's three eldest children is still unclear.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-21) Despite these rumors, there was no doubt that King Viserys meant for Rhaenyra to succeed him as heir, and for her sons to follow her. By royal decree, each of Rhaenyra's Velaryon sons was given a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) in the cradle. Those who doubted their paternity claimed the eggs would never hatch; nevertheless, each one did, and in time the three boys all became dragonriders. Septon Eustace stated that King Viserys sat Jace on his knee while he was holding court on the Iron Throne, and said, "One day this will be your seat, lad."(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-13)

In [120 AC](wiki:120_AC), [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon), the wife of Prince Daemon Targaryen, died after a failed birth. At her funeral, a quarrel broke out between Alicent's son [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) and the sons of Rhaenyra, who he accused of being [bastards](wiki:Bastard) from [House Strong](wiki:House_Strong). In the children's fight, Aemond lost an eye when Lucerys defended Jacaerys with a knife. Though Alicent demanded Luke have an eye removed in recompense, and Rhaenyra demanded that Aemond be questioned "sharply" to learn where he had heard such rumors, King Viserys declared that apologies between the children was all that was needed for peace. The king further issued an edict that anyone spreading the Strong rumor would [ lose their tongue](wiki:Tongue_mutilation), and ordered Harwin Strong to depart from Dragonstone to [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal). Afterward, a massive fire at Harrenhal killed Harwin and his father Lyonel, the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). While some blamed the inferno on the [curse of Harrenhal](wiki:Curse_of_Harrenhal), other rumors blamed Daemon, Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), Harwin's younger brother [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong), or even Viserys for the blaze. That year also saw the death of Rhaenyra's husband, Ser Laenor Velaryon, killed in a fight with his companion and rumored lover, Ser [Qarl Correy](wiki:Qarl_Correy).(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-14)

Less than six months after the deaths of Laena, Laenor, and Harwin, Rhaenyra secretly married her uncle, Prince Daemon, the most experienced warrior of his time, at Dragonstone. Septon Eustace claimed the two had wed in secret to prevent Viserys from prohibiting the marriage, while Mushroom suggested that Daemon, aged thirty-nine, had already impregnated the princess, who was then twenty-three. The news of their wedding caused a great scandal at the court of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), and made Rhaenyra's father livid. King Viserys angrily declared that a wedding so soon after the deaths of Daemon's wife and Rhaenyra's husband was an insult to their memories, and it took some time before Viserys and Daemon reconciled. The year ended with Rhaenyra and Daemon welcoming the birth of their first son, Prince [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), who was born with purple eyes and pale silvery hair. Another son, born in [122 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), was given the name [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) after Rhaenyra's father.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-15)

In [126 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Rhaenyra argued her second oldest son, Lucerys, should officially be named heir to [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark). Several lesser cousins of [House Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon), however, unsuccessfully protested to the Iron Throne that her sons were bastards fathered by Harwin Strong and had no claim to Driftmark. King Viserys heard them in stony silence, and after issuing the order to remove their tongues, slipped and cut his hand to the [bone](wiki:Bone) on the Iron Throne. Grand Maester [Mellos](wiki:Mellos) was ineffective at trying to heal the king, who suffered a fever from an infection and it was feared he would die. Viserys only recovered after Rhaenyra sent her own maester, [Gerardys](wiki:Gerardys), to treat her father's wounds.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-16)(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

### Dance of the Dragons

Rhaenyra was the designated heir of her father, King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), who desired that she succeed him. Viserys's intent had been confirmed by a proclamation of his will.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-17) When Viserys died in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), however, Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), and Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole), [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) and possibly the former lover of Rhaenyra, defied the king's will and instead crowned Rhaenyra's half-brother, Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower)'s son [Aegon II](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), who was ten years younger.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-11) This brought about the Targaryen civil war, known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), in which [dragonriders](wiki:Dragonriders) fought each other.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) During the war, Rhaenyra rode her [dragon](wiki:Dragon), [Syrax](wiki:Syrax).(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-12)

At the time of her father's death, Rhaenyra was on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) awaiting the birth of her sixth child. The [greens](wiki:Greens) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) ensured that the news of the Viserys's death did not reach her until her own supporters in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) were dead or imprisoned and [Aegon II's coronation](wiki:Coronation_of_Aegon_II_Targaryen) had already occurred. When news reached Rhaenyra, she went into a black fury and went into early labor, producing a deformed, stillborn daughter, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)). Rhaenyra blamed this tragedy on the enemies who had stolen her crown. Rhaenyra had a hastily-arranged [coronation of her own](wiki:Coronation_of_Rhaenyra_Targaryen). The arrival of Ser [Steffon Darklyn](wiki:Steffon_Darklyn) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) on Dragonstone, accompanied by some other supporters, was therefore celebrated, as they brought with them the crown worn by [Jaehaerys I](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and Viserys. Rhaenyra was crowned with her father's crown by her husband and uncle, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen). When news of her coronation reached King's Landing, she was offered terms that would let Rhaenyra and her descendants remain rulers of Dragonstone if she renounced her claims, but she refused, knowing that war was inevitable.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

To gain more support to take the [throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) from her brother, Rhaenyra sent her son Jace on his dragon to treat with the [Arryns](wiki:House_Arryn), [Manderlys](wiki:House_Manderly), and [Starks](wiki:House_Stark), and her son Luke to treat with Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). Luke's mission was thought to be the safer journey, but he arrived at Storm's End to find Queen Alicent's younger son [Aemond](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) already there. Aemond, riding [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), killed Luke over [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay) as he attempted to leave after failing to gain Lord Borros' allegiance. The news of Luke's death devastated his mother.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Rhaenyra [took the capital](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) and sat in the throne for roughly half a year. Upon taking the city, Rhaenyra insisted on claiming her father's seat, and so she climbed the Iron Throne and accepted pleas of forgiveness and loyalty from those in the Red Keep all throughout the night. Although Rhaenyra was dressed in armor, Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), who favored Aegon II,(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-24) claimed that those present witnessed the throne leaving several cuts on her legs and left hand. The dripping blood would have been taken as a sign that the throne had rejected her; her days as ruler would be few.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Eustace further claims that Rhaenyra would continuously cut herself more frequently when she sat on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and became more stout from gluttony during her time in King's Landing.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)

Those living in King's Landing initially welcomed Rhaenyra's return as a ruler, for they had always had little love for [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and his brother [Aemond](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen). Rhaenyra once in power sent [knights inquisitor](wiki:Knights_inquisitor) to pursue those who had helped Aegon escape the capital, the spikes of the gates started to be filled with more and more heads each day and although the late king [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) had left the treasure vaults full of [gold](wiki:Currency) upon his death, the treasury was completely empty by the time Rhaenyra had taken the city, for the [greens](wiki:Greens) had sent three quarters of the gold away for safekeeping and spent the rest prior to the fall of the capital. Rhaenyra was in desperate need of coin, Lord [Bartimos Celtigar](wiki:Bartimos_Celtigar), her new [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) placed new taxes on the populace that increased day by day. The [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) slowly started to turn against her, and eventually named her "King [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) with teats." The term Maegor's Teats became a common curse in King's Landing for a hundred years thereafter.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4)

After hearing of the [Two Betrayers](wiki:Two_Betrayers), Rhaenyra grew paranoid and declared the two remaining dragonseeds, Ser [Addam Velaryon](wiki:Addam_Velaryon) and the girl [Nettles](wiki:Nettles) both traitors, a decision with disastrous consequences. Rhaenyra's Hand, Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), who had named Addam his heir, alerted Addam in time for the knight to flee upon the back of the dragon [Seasmoke](wiki:Seasmoke). This led to the arrest of Corlys, which caused the fleet of [House Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) to abandon Rhaenyra's cause.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5)(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)

In order to have the girl Nettles killed, Queen Rhaenyra had sent a letter to Lord [Manfryd Mooton](wiki:Manfryd_Mooton), commanding him to kill the girl. Lord Mooton refused, as Nettles was under the protection of [guest right](wiki:Guest_right). He feared Prince Daemon would kill them if they followed Rhaenyra's orders, but saw there was no winning option, since refusing to obey Rhaenyra's order would make him a traitor to the crown. Insisting that kings Viserys I and Jaehaerys I would never have asked such a thing, Manfryd decided to change his allegiance to Aegon II, after Nettles and Daemon had left his roof.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6)

A massive [riot](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) soon sprang up in King's Landing, after the suicide of [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen) who was beloved of the small folk. Many said, she was killed on Rhaenyra's orders. The fact that this story was so easily believed, showed how much the city had turned against Rhaenyra. The riots soon escalated to the [storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit), and the death of her son [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon).(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1)

After this, Rhaenyra [fled](wiki:Flight_to_Dragonstone) King's Landing, in the aftermath of the deaths of all her other children she refused to let [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) out of her sight. Terrorized and despairing, she grew grey and haggard as she traveled past [Rosby](wiki:Rosby), [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth), and [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale). Rhaenyra was forced to sell her [crown](wiki:Crown) to raise the coin to buy passage on a [Braavosi](wiki:Braavos) merchant ship, the *[Violande](wiki:Violande)*. Once she reached Dragonstone, Rhaenyra [was betrayed](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone) by Ser [Alfred Broome](wiki:Alfred_Broome), whose men slew the remainder of her [Queensguard](wiki:Queensguard). Her half-brother Aegon II then had her fed to his own dragon, [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre), at Dragonstone.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-13)(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-27)

## Appearances & Description

Rhaenyra had her family's [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) looks, wearing her silver-gold hair in a long braid in the manner of Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen).(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM1_19-0) Rhaenyra was a precocious child, bright and bold and beautiful.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) At the age of fourteen, Rhaenyra was declared by her uncle, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), to be the most beautiful [maiden](wiki:Maiden) in all the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Rhaenyra had a large bosom,(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM1_19-1) but never lost the weight she gained from pregnancies, and she had grown stout and thick of waist at the age of twenty after her third pregnancy.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

Rhaenyra was proud and stubborn, and there was a certain petulance to her small mouth. Though she could be charming, Rhaenyra was quick to anger and never forgot a slight.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM1_19-2) She resented her stepmother, Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower),(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-7) but was "fond and more than fond" of Lady [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon).(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

Rhaenyra always dressed richly, favoring purple and maroon velvets and golden [Myrish](wiki:Myr) lace in intricate patterns. Her bodice often glittered with pearls and diamonds, and there were always rings on her fingers. Whenever she was anxious, she would turn them compulsively around her fingers.(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM1_19-3) During the Dance of the Dragons, she wore the [crown](wiki:Crown) of her father, [Viserys I](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen),(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM1_19-4) which had first been worn by King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-8)
