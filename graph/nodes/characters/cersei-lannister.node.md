## Origins

### Early life

Cersei was born in [266 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) as the first-born child and only daughter to Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), heir to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), and his wife, Lady [Joanna](wiki:Joanna_Lannister).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Cersei was born shortly before her twin brother [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), who was holding her foot.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) sent the twins their weights in gold as a [name day](wiki:Name_day) gift, and commanded Tywin to bring the children to court when they were old enough to travel.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) It is currently unknown whether this visit to King's Landing actually occurred, as the royal court came to Casterly Rock following the death of Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) in [267 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) Cersei and Jaime did visit King's Landing in [272 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), when their mother brought them from Casterly Rock for the [Anniversary Tourney](wiki:Anniversary_Tourney) held to celebrate the tenth year of King Aerys's reign.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

During their early childhood, Cersei and Jaime were inseparable.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-1) They looked so similar as children that not even their father was able to keep them apart.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-4) Sometimes they would dress in each other's clothes, and spend a whole day as each other.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-5) Occasionally when Cersei wore Jaime's clothes, she took lessons from the [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms) in his stead, without anyone realizing.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rawoiafcersei_lannister.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) The twins played in the bowels of Casterly Rock, where the caged [lions](wiki:Lion) of Cersei's late grandfather, Tytos, were kept. Cersei and Jaime would dare each other to climb into the cage. Cersei once dared to go as far as touch one of the lions, until her brother pulled her away.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3) Cersei and Jaime slept together in the same bed when they were very young,(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-2) and [experimented sexually](wiki:Incest) at a young age.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-3) During one of these encounters, they were caught by a servant, who informed their mother. A guard was placed outside Cersei's bedchamber, while Jaime's bedchamber was moved to the other side of the castle. The twins were told never to do anything like that ever again, as Joanna would otherwise be forced to tell their father.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

Cersei's mother died not long thereafter,(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1) in [273 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) when giving birth to Cersei's younger brother, [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) Cersei, then seven years old, despised her youngest sibling for killing her mother.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0) Shortly after Joanna's death, while the family was still in mourning, the [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne_(mother_of_Doran)) arrived at Casterly Rock with her two youngest children, [Elia](wiki:Elia_Martell) and [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell). Though Tyrion was kept out of sight during the visit of the [Martells](wiki:House_Martell), Cersei promised to show him to Elia. On the last day of their visit, Cersei threatened her brother's [wet nurse](wiki:Wet_nurse) and sent her away, then undid Tyrion's swaddling clothes to better display his [dwarfism](wiki:Dwarf) to the siblings. When Oberyn remarked that Tyrion was not much of a monster, Cersei replied that he had killed her mother, and then attacked the baby, twisting his penis while he screamed, until Jaime stopped her.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-1)

After being separated from Jaime following their mother's discovery of their sexual experimenting, Cersei had numerous [bedmaids](wiki:Bedmaid) and companions, girls her age who were daughters of Tywin's [bannermen](wiki:Bannermen) and [household knights](wiki:Household_knight). While Cersei occasionally appreciated the company of these girls in keeping her bed warm at night in the cold Rock, she did not like any of them. She felt they were dull, weepy, sneakily telling tales about her, and was convinced they were trying to come between her and Jaime. Few lasted very long.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-3)

### Attempted betrothal to Rhaegar Targaryen

During the Martells' visit to Casterly Rock, the Princess of Dorne proposed a betrothal between Cersei and Oberyn, as had been previously discussed by her and her friend Joanna. However, [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) refused her, and informed her that Cersei was meant as a bride for the crown prince, [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

Lord Tywin first informed Cersei of his wish to betroth her to Prince Rhaegar when she was no older than six or seven, though he told her never to speak of it until a betrothal was officially announced.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1) Cersei once drew a picture of Rhaegar and herself, riding a [dragon](wiki:Dragon), though she claimed it depicted King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and his sister-wife, [Alysanne](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), when Jaime discovered it. In [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), at the age of ten, Cersei became infatuated with Rhaegar after meeting him for the first time during the [tournament in honor of Viserys's birth](wiki:Tournament_in_honor_of_Viserys's_birth) at [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-2)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6) Before the tourney began, Cersei's aunt, Lady [Genna](wiki:Genna_Lannister), informed Cersei that her betrothal to Rhaegar would be announced during the final feast of the tourney.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-3)

On the tourney grounds, Cersei brought her bedmaids, [Melara Hetherspoon](wiki:Melara_Hetherspoon) and [Jeyne Farman](wiki:Jeyne_Farman), to the tent of a [woods witch](wiki:Woods_witch), [Maggy the Frog](wiki:Maggy_the_Frog), so that she could [tell their futures](wiki:Prophecy). After Maggy awoke, causing Jeyne to flee in fear, she required a [taste of a drop of their blood](wiki:Bloodmagic), which Cersei bravely gave her. Cersei then inquired when she and Rhaegar would wed, and Maggy correctly predicted Cersei would not marry "the prince", but rather "the king" ([Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_Baratheon)). When Cersei asked whether she would still be queen, Maggy answered that she would, until another queen, "younger and more beautiful", would cast Cersei down and take all she held dear. Cersei then asked if she and the king would have children, and Maggy predicted that he would have sixteen children, and Cersei three. She went on to prophesy that Cersei would outlive her children, who would die crowned, and that afterwards the *[valonqar](wiki:Valonqar)* would come to "choke the life from [her]." Melara then asked if she would marry Jaime, but Maggy prophesied that she would not marry anyone and would die a [maiden](wiki:Maiden), and that her death was very close. Cersei followed this by throwing a potion into Maggy's face, and the girls fled the tent.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1) Afterwards, Melara suggested that if they never spoke about the prophecies, they would be forgotten and would not come true.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

However, Melara died shortly after their visit to Maggy, drowned in a well, and it is implied that Cersei killed the girl to prevent her from speaking of the prophecies, and out of jealousy over Jaime. After Melara's death, Cersei inquired with her [septa](wiki:Septa), [Saranella](wiki:Saranella), about the meaning of *valonqar*, who informed her it was [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian) for "little brother".(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-1) Cersei eventually became convinced that [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) was the *valonqar* Maggy spoke of, resulting in her despising and mistrusting him even more than she had before. The prophecy of the *valonqar* has continued to haunt Cersei.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1)

After the guests of the tourney left the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), Cersei learned from her aunt that Tywin had proposed the betrothal to King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), but Aerys refused, saying that a man did not marry his heir to his servant's daughter.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-4)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)

### Living at court

At the age of twelve, Cersei was taken to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) by her father, who still served as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), after the failure of Lord [Steffon Baratheon](wiki:Steffon_Baratheon)'s mission to find Prince Rhaegar a suitable bride in [Essos](wiki:Essos). In the following years, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) refused every offer of marriage for Cersei. According to [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), Tywin still had his sights set on a Targaryen match, either hoping to betroth Cersei to young Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), or hoping for Rhaegar's new bride, Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), to die in childbirth.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

In [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), when Cersei was fifteen, Jaime visited King's Landing after receiving his [knighthood](wiki:Knight) for his efforts against the [Kingswood Brotherhood](wiki:Kingswood_Brotherhood). Disguising herself as a simple serving wench, Cersei met with Jaime at an [old inn on Eel Alley](wiki:Old_inn_on_Eel_Alley). Cersei informed her twin that their father had been discussing betrothing Jaime to [Lysa Tully](wiki:Lysa_Arryn). Cersei seduced Jaime and persuaded him to join the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), which would require him to remain unmarried and live near her in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). Cersei knew that Tywin would be opposed to the idea, but that he could not openly object if it was the king's order, and offered to make the arrangements herself. After a night of passionate sex, Jaime consented to her plan.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-1) However, Cersei had no idea how much the relationship between her father and the king had deteriorated, and although Aerys did award Jaime a place in the Kingsguard, Tywin furiously perceived Jaime's appointment to the Kingsguard as a slight by King Aerys intended to rob him of his heir. He resigned as Hand, pleading illness, and moved back to Casterly Rock with Cersei, separating the twins once more.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-2)

### Marriage

After the conclusion of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), a marriage was arranged between Cersei and the new king, [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), in order to seal the [new royal house's](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing) alliance with [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister). Cersei and Robert were wed in [284 AC](wiki:284_AC).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-48) She and Jaime had sex the morning of her wedding.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) Nonetheless, Cersei was at first enthralled by the happy crowds at [the royal wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Robert_I_Baratheon_and_Cersei_Lannister). However, her enthusiasm for the match ended when the drunken Robert called her "[Lyanna](wiki:Lyanna_Stark)", the name of his late betrothed, while having sex with her on their wedding night.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-4)

The marriage rapidly deteriorated, and Cersei resumed her incestuous relationship with Jaime. During the early years of their marriage, Cersei declined Robert's invitations to hunt with him, as Robert's trips allowed her more time with Jaime.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-1) Because two of Robert's [Estermont uncles](wiki:House_Estermont) from [Greenstone](wiki:Greenstone) had remained at court for half a year following the wedding, Robert insisted on repaying the visit, and Cersei and Jaime accompanied him for a two-week stay at [Estermont](wiki:Estermont). While there, Cersei suspected that Robert was sleeping with a [cousin of his](wiki:Estermont_(cousin_of_Robert)), and had Jaime follow Robert to confirms her suspicions. Cersei and Jaime slept together on Greenstone, and Cersei likes to believe that was the night their eldest son [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) was conceived.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-5)

Cersei bore Jaime three children, [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), born in [286 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), born in [290 AC](wiki:290_AC), and [Tommen](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), born in [291 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). Cersei successfully deceived Robert and the entire kingdom, presenting the children as Robert's trueborn heirs. Although the king was away during the births of the children, hunting in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), Jaime was present for at least Joffrey's birth, defying the [maesters](wiki:Maester) and midwives who would have kept him out of the birthing room.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) However, Cersei refused to let her brother ever hold Joffrey, fearing people might start to suspect his true parentage.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

While Robert "claimed his rights" frequently during the early years of their marriage, his drinking led to him hurting Cersei during their sexual intercourse, leaving her sex and breasts sore and painful. Once, during their first year of marriage, when Cersei confronted Robert the morning afterwards, telling him that he had hurt her, he claimed it was because of the drink, and he was not to blame. When he tried to take another horn of ale, Cersei smashed her own horn in his face, chipping his tooth. Robert claimed not to remember anything of those nights, but Cersei believes otherwise, and is certain that Robert did recall what he did to her, but felt that pretending to forget was easier than facing the truth. Cersei, in turn, tried to pretend that Robert was Rhaegar during their sexual encounters, as she could not imagine him as Jaime.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-4) On one occasion, early in her marriage, Cersei became pregnant by Robert. Unwilling to give birth to Robert's child, she sent Jaime out to find a woman to cleanse her.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-5) Having grown to resent Robert over the years, Cersei took further care to ensure he did not impregnate her, and used other means to bring him to completion. Over time, Robert came to Cersei's bed less frequently, not even once a year, preferring to lay with [whores](wiki:Whore).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-6)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-5)

After young Joffrey killed a pregnant cat, Robert suggested bringing [a bastard daughter of his](wiki:Mya_Stone) to court. However, Cersei made unsubtle threats, claiming [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) was a dangerous place for a growing girl. Though Robert hit Cersei for that, the girl was not brought to court, and all of Robert's [bastards](wiki:Bastard) were kept out of sight.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-2) According to [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), however, there are rumors that Robert fathered twins on a serving woman at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) in [295 AC](wiki:295_AC), and that Cersei had the babes killed and the mother sold to a [slaver](wiki:Slavery).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)

Eventually [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), familiar with the appearance of his brother Robert's black-haired, blue-eyed bastard offspring, grew suspicious of the royal children's lack of resemblance to their supposed father.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) He confided in Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), and the two investigated the matter together.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) Cersei lost an [emerald](wiki:Emerald) pendant when Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) defeated Jaime at the [tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_Prince_Joffrey's_name_day), but the jewelry was returned to her.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-1) After the tourney, Cersei and her children travelled with Lord Tywin to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock). During the fortnight following the tourney, Jon was [poisoned](wiki:Tears_of_Lys) and fell ill, eventually dying before he could act.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0) Stannis was convinced that Cersei was responsible for Jon's death, and he fled to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

After the death of the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), Cersei and her children accompany her husband, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), traveling in a huge, lumbering [wheelhouse](wiki:Wheelhouse).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1) At Winterfell, the king offers the position of Hand to Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), and betrothes Joffrey to Eddard's oldest daughter, [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) Jon's widow, Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), sends a coded message to her sister, Eddard's wife [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), alleging that the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) murdered Jon.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0) When Robert and most of the castle go on a hunting trip, Cersei remains behind with her brother, Ser [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister). [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) sees the two having sex in Winterfell's [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep), and when Cersei insists that they must do something to prevent the boy from telling anyone, Jaime pushes him out of the tower window.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) Cersei later berates Jaime for his impulsiveness, arguing that attempting to kill the boy was foolish, when they could have simply intimidated him into silence.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

While the royal procession travels back to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Cersei's eldest son, Prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), bullies a common boy, [Mycah](wiki:Mycah), prompting [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) and her direwolf, [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria_(direwolf)), to attack and disarm him.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) Joffrey tells his parents that Arya and her wolf attacked him without provocation, and Cersei takes her son's side,(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0) arguing to Robert that the girl should lose a hand in accordance with the ancient penalty for striking a prince of royal blood.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0) When Robert refuses to do so, she proclaims her desire regarding maiming or killing Arya to Jaime, using sex as bribe. Jaime searches for the girl, and would later imply that he would have been willing to do his sister's bidding had he found her.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-1) Instead, Arya is found by [House Stark guards](wiki:House_Stark_guards). At [Darry](wiki:Darry), while Robert again resists Cersei's call for severely punishing Arya, the queen successfully pressures him into ordering the execution of Sansa's direwolf, [Lady](wiki:Lady_(direwolf)), as a proxy.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-1)

Having appointed Eddard Stark as Jon Arryn's replacement, Robert orders the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney) be held in honor of Eddard.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0) Cersei forbids Robert from fighting in the melee, inciting a public argument between king and queen.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0) Robert intends to fight anyway, but Eddard and Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) succeed in dissuading him. Later, [Varys](wiki:Varys) claims that Cersei had known publicly forbidding Robert from participating in the melee was the most effective way of convincing him to do so, and had been planning to have him "accidentally" killed in the melee. He also suggests that Ser [Hugh of the Vale](wiki:Hugh), who had been killed by Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) earlier in the tournament, could have poisoned Jon Arryn at the Lannisters' instigation, only to have them arrange his death in order to ensure his silence afterward.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0)

When Cersei's brother [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) is abducted by [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), Cersei argues with Robert, insulting his manhood for his failure to immediately avenge the insult to her family. The king strikes her in response, but Cersei tells him she intends to wear the bruise "as a badge of honor." Eddard does not approve and Robert admits that it was "not kingly," but blames Cersei for provoking him.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0)

Eddard, who has been investigating Jon Arryn's death, discovers the truth about the royal children's parentage. While the king is hunting in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), Eddard confronts Cersei, who admits the accusation is true. She attempts to seduce him in the [godswood of the Red Keep](wiki:Godswood_of_the_Red_Keep), but Ned refuses. Not wanting to see the children harmed, Eddard warns her that he intends to tell Robert the truth and urges her to flee with the princes and princess.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-7) Eddard plans to have his daughters, [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark), sail from King's Landing on the *[Wind Witch](wiki:Wind_Witch)*.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0) Sansa, not understanding the danger and desiring to marry Joffrey, informs Cersei of her father's plan.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1)

A drunken Robert is fatally injured by a [boar](wiki:Boar) during his hunt,(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0) having been given strongwine by his squire, [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0) On his deathbed Robert names Eddard regent until his thirteen-year-old heir Joffrey comes of age, though Eddard secretly changes “my son Joffrey” into “my heir”.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-1) The morning of Robert's death, Cersei seizes power, denouncing Eddard as a traitor who conspired against King Joffrey. She has Eddard imprisoned and is named Queen Regent, heading Joffrey's [small council](wiki:Small_council).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-3)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

### A Clash of Kings

Having escaped [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) adds Cersei's name to the list of those she desires dead.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_74-0)

Cersei sends the gold cloaks after Robert's known bastards, with [Allar Deem](wiki:Allar_Deem) killing [Barra](wiki:Barra) and [her mother](wiki:Barra's_mother) in [Chataya's brothel](wiki:Chataya's_brothel).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_75-0) A group of gold cloaks bearing the queen's seal and warrant try to arrest [Gendry](wiki:Gendry) on the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad), but they are turned away by [Yoren](wiki:Yoren).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-0)

When Tyrion arrives in King's Landing, bearing a letter from their father, Cersei threatens to have him thrown in the dungeon, but he placates her by saying he could rescue Jaime.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-4) As Hand, Tyrion struggles to wrest control from her, finally resorting to [poison](wiki:Poison) to incapacitate her for a few days.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0)

Cersei insists that her cousin [Lancel](wiki:Lancel_Lannister) receive [knighthood](wiki:Knight).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) In Jaime's absence, Cersei begins sleeping with Ser Lancel, which is discerned by Tyrion.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-1) She also reveals to Tyrion that she had given Lancel fortified wine for Robert's hunt, stronger than Robert usually drank.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-5) After Tyrion sends away [Vylarr](wiki:Vylarr) and Cersei's [personal guard](wiki:House_Lannister_guards),(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) she employs three sellswords to replace them—Ser [Osmund](wiki:Osmund_Kettleblack), [Osney](wiki:Osney_Kettleblack), and [Osfryd Kettleblack](wiki:Osfryd_Kettleblack)—but Tyrion finds out and buys their loyalty.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

Tyrion deceives small council members and determines that [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) is Cersei's agent. When Tyrion arrests Pycelle, the Grand Maester tells him that Jon Arryn had been recovering from his poisoning, and that he had sent Jon's maester away in order to prevent Jon's recovery, assuming that Cersei wanted Jon dead.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Tyrion gets Cersei to agree with sending Myrcella to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) to win over [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5) The day Princess Myrcella takes ship for [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), Cersei is among those caught up in the [riots in King's Landing](wiki:Riots_in_King's_Landing), provoked in part by a thoughtless comment she makes to King Joffrey about a dead baby.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)

As King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) moves on King's Landing, Cersei sends Tommen to [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) for safety, but Tyrion's men intercept the party and take the boy into Tyrion's custody.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-0)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-0) Fed up with Tyrion's efforts to rule, Cersei arrests [Alayaya](wiki:Alayaya), a whore she believes Tyrion is sleeping with, and has the girl beaten. She uses Alayaya as a hostage to ensure Tommen's safe return; Tyrion, however, promises her that whatever happens to Alayaya, happens to Tommen as well, rapings and beatings included, frightening Cersei.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) Cersei removes Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount) from the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) for his failure to resist Tyrion's men, and she replaces him with Osmund Kettleblack, a man just as hollow.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

As the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater) begins, Cersei hosts a banquet for noblewomen in the [Queen's Ballroom](wiki:Queen's_Ballroom).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) She claims it is in an effort to keep their minds off the fighting, but has invited Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne), the [King's Justice](wiki:King's_Justice), to be on hand to kill them if the city is taken, to prevent them from becoming hostages.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-6) When the queen hears that the [River Gate](wiki:River_Gate) is under attack, she summons Joffrey back to the Red Keep.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-7) Seeing the king flee back to the castle damages morale and causes the men to rout, nearly costing her the city. This outrages a bleeding Lancel, who feels they could have held the gates if Cersei had not recalled the king. Lancel offers to protect Joffrey if the king returns to the fight, but Cersei insists that her son be brought inside [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) and she strikes Lancel's wound to force him aside.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_80-0)

If not for the timely arrival and attack on Stannis's flank by the army of Lords Tywin Lannister and [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell), the battle would be lost for the Lannisters.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1) Because Tyrion lies wounded and unconscious, Cersei is able to lie to Tywin and turn her father's prejudice against Tyrion to her advantage, stripping Tyrion's power by the time his convalescence is over.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-0)

### A Storm of Swords

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) believes that Cersei is to blame for Ser [Mandon Moore](wiki:Mandon_Moore)'s assassination attempt at the [Blackwater](wiki:Blackwater_Rush).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-0)

Cersei plans the upcoming wedding of her son, King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), to [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-1)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-0) [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) bends Cersei to his will and makes it clear his daughter is no longer wanted on the [small council](wiki:Small_council). Against her wishes, he plans for her to marry her again, considering [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) (whose elderly wife [Alannys](wiki:Alannys_Harlaw) has failing health), [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), [Horas](wiki:Horas_Redwyne) or [Hobber Redwyne](wiki:Hobber_Redwyne), [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell), and [Willas Tyrell](wiki:Willas_Tyrell) as potential matches.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-1) While Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) is initially receptive to Cersei marrying Willas, the offer is rejected after Mace speaks with his mother [Olenna](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell), the Queen of Thorns.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_84-0)

Cersei intends to keep [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) as her hostage,(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-2) but Tywin forces the girl to [marry Tyrion](wiki:Wedding_of_Tyrion_Lannister_and_Sansa_Stark).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_85-0) During the wedding feast, Cersei dances with [Paxter Redwyne](wiki:Paxter_Redwyne), [Mathis Rowan](wiki:Mathis_Rowan), and her father.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_85-1) She also flirts with [Osmund Kettleblack](wiki:Osmund_Kettleblack) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_86-0)

After Joffrey offends Tywin through his ungracious behavior, Tywin is furious and asks where the boy could have learnt such amoral sentiments. Cersei blames Robert but it is clear to all in the room, including Tywin's brother [Kevan](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), that Cersei is responsible.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_87-0)

Joffrey is killed at [his own wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding), and Cersei accuses Tyrion and Sansa, falsely believing them responsible.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_88-0) Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) returns to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with the assistance of [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth), and the Lannister twins have sex in a sept where Joffrey's body rests. Cersei rejects Jaime's suggestion of announcing their incest, like the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen) had done.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-1)

Having escaped King's Landing with the aid of [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), Sansa learns that the [Kettleblacks](wiki:House_Kettleblack) are actually loyal to Petyr, not Cersei or Tyrion.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_89-0)

Cersei finds numerous witnesses to testify against Tyrion,(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2) including his lover [Shae](wiki:Shae),(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-1) and Cersei names Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) champion if there is to be a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat). This leads Prince Oberyn, whose sister [Elia](wiki:Elia_Martell) was murdered by Gregor over a dozen years ago in the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing), to champion Tyrion.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-3) Oberyn is killed and Gregor is severely wounded in the duel.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-2)

Oberyn's death condemns Tyrion, but he is freed from his cell by Jaime and [Varys](wiki:Varys). Tyrion tells Jaime that Cersei has been sleeping with [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister) and Osmund Kettleblack. Before escaping the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), Tyrion kills Shae and his father, Tywin.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos77.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_90-0)

At the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) reveals in Sansa's presence that she had killed [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) and then informed her sister Catelyn that the Lannisters were to blame.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_91-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Cersei's tendencies to paranoia, rash judgement and hysteria increase following the deaths of her son, King [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), and her father, Lord [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). She resumes her position as [regent](wiki:Regent) over her surviving son, the eight-year-old King [Tommen](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), who is obedient to her will. As the eldest child of Tywin, she is also acknowledged as the Lady of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) by her uncle, Ser [Kevan](wiki:Kevan_Lannister).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-4)

Cersei orders Ser [Osney](wiki:Osney_Kettleblack) and Ser [Osfryd Kettleblack](wiki:Osfryd_Kettleblack) to hide Shae's corpse,(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-2) and commands Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount) and Ser [Osmund Kettleblack](wiki:Osmund_Kettleblack) to murder the two guards who had been guarding Tyrion's cell when she hears that they had been found asleep on duty (unknown to Cersei, Varys had drugged them).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) After her father's funeral she has Ser Osney murder the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Tyrion's)), bedding him as a reward.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) After the [wedding of Tommen I Baratheon and Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Wedding_of_Tommen_I_Baratheon_and_Margaery_Tyrell), Cersei burns with [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand), the site of Tywin's death, which her twin [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) considers a folly.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_92-0) Word spreads throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) and the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) that Cersei has promised a [lordship](wiki:Lord) to whomever brings her Tyrion's head,(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-5) but innocents are killed instead.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-3) Cersei's paranoia alienates Jaime,(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) who departs to secure the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) and resolve the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-2)

Cersei surrounds herself with sycophants rather than honest and competent advisers,(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-4) and she fills Tommen's [small council](wiki:Small_council) with her own supporters and agents, disregarding the previous arrangements made by Tywin and the well-meant, if blunt, advice from Kevan. She becomes a restless sleeper, troubled by nightmares of Tyrion, who murdered Tywin, and the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) consuming her.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-3) Cersei also recalls the prophecy of the *[valonqar](wiki:Valonqar)*, whom she considers Tyrion.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-4)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-2) Numerous people, including several family members, consider her to be an ineffectual leader.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-5)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-2) Cersei's growing dependency on alcohol increases her weight, but she blames the washerwomen for shrinking her gowns.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-6)

Cersei suspects her powerful [Tyrell](wiki:House_Tyrell) allies, now relatives through Tommen's marriage to [Margaery](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), are trying to seize control of the kingdom. This suspicion grows from a [Gardener](wiki:House_Gardener) coin found by [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) in the dungeon where Tyrion was being held before his escape(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-6) – a coin similar to the ones Lady [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell) carries with her when traveling, causing Cersei to wrongfully suspect a Tyrell involvement in Tyrion's escape.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) Cersei commences a campaign of intrigue to remove Tyrells in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) from positions of influence and authority, including Margaery and her brother [Loras](wiki:Loras_Tyrell), a knight of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-7)

Cersei refuses to honor the debts owed by the crown, angering powerful institutions such as the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos) and the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-5) This results in the Faith refusing to bless King Tommen, and the Iron Bank calling in all their debts throughout [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) and refusing all new loans.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-2) This causes economic chaos throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). With the monies owed, Cersei constructs a [new royal fleet](wiki:Cersei's_dromonds) of warships and gives command to [Aurane Waters](wiki:Aurane_Waters), the Bastard of [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) and a sellsail of questionable expertise and loyalty.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-6) Two of the dromonds, *[Lioness](wiki:Lioness)* and *[Sweet Cersei](wiki:Sweet_Cersei)*, are named in the Queen Regent's honor.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-3)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-6)

In an attempt to alleviate the crown's debts, gain the Faith's blessing, and gain more protection from her purported enemies, Cersei allows the new High Septon, the so-called [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow), to revive the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant), ignorant of its history of [causing trouble](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) for monarchs.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-4)

Cersei sends Ser [Balon Swann](wiki:Balon_Swann) of the Kingsguard to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) to deliver the head of Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane).(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-7)

Cersei takes the disgraced former [maester](wiki:Maester) Qyburn into her service as [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers), using him as a torturer, and allowing him to conduct immoral experiments on human subjects.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-7) She befriends [Taena Merryweather](wiki:Taena_Merryweather), who informs Cersei that her maid [Senelle](wiki:Senelle) is spying on her for Margaery. ### A Dance with Dragons

Ser [Balon Swann](wiki:Balon_Swann) arrives at [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) with a letter in which Cersei asks [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), to give leave to her daughter [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) to return to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) for a short visit, and to invite Doran to take the Dornish seat on the [small council](wiki:Small_council) that has been left vacant with the death of Prince [Oberyn](wiki:Oberyn_Martell). Doran learns from informers at the royal court, however, that Balon is to invite Prince [Trystane Martell](wiki:Trystane_Martell) to accompany Myrcella but then be killed by outlaws in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), for which Tyrion is supposed to be blamed, with Balon as a witness.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_93-0)

Cersei remains a prisoner of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) in a tower of the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor), under the care of Septas [Unella](wiki:Unella), [Moelle](wiki:Moelle), and [Scolera](wiki:Scolera). To gain access to visitors, Cersei admits to the [High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow) that she had relations with her cousin, [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister), and [Osney Kettleblack](wiki:Osney_Kettleblack); she also falsely confesses to have bedded [Osmund](wiki:Osmund_Kettleblack)(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rawoiafosmund_kettleblack.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_94-0) and [Osfryd Kettleblack](wiki:Osfryd_Kettleblack),(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Rawoiafosfryd_kettleblack.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_95-0) knowing that all such sins would not earn her an execution. She continues to deny having ordered Osney Kettleblack to kill the [previous High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Tyrion's)), or that she was involved in King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s death. The High Septon agrees to allow her one visitor a day. Cersei learns from her uncle, Ser [Kevan](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), that [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) disappeared in the riverlands with a woman, possibly [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth). Kevan also tells his niece about [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon)'s injury and of [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart)'s death at the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood), which leaves a vacancy in the Kingsguard. Cersei sends word to Lord [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) that the time has come.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-1)

Before her trial, the Faith requires Cersei to submit to a [walk of atonement](wiki:Walk_of_atonement) from the Great Sept to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). Cersei is shaved of hair from her entire body, then stripped naked. An escort of [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons), [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows), and septas protect her from the leering and jeering crowds that have flocked to see her, with Septa Scolera ringing a bell and singing the word "shame". Cersei tries to hang on to her pride during the barefoot walk in spite of the crowds projecting filth at her and bawdy insults. Faces in the crowd remind her of [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), [Melara Hetherspoon](wiki:Melara_Hetherspoon), [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), and [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon). She eventually breaks down in tears, however, just before finishing and entering the Red Keep. Upon her entrance, [Jocelyn Swyft](wiki:Jocelyn_Swyft) has her body covered. Cersei is carried into the castle by a giant knight, whom [Qyburn](wiki:Qyburn) introduces as the newest member of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) and her champion, "Ser [Robert Strong](wiki:Robert_Strong)".(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_96-0)

After her walk of atonement, [Lord Regent](wiki:Lord_Regent) Kevan Lannister places Cersei within house arrest in the Red Keep, awaiting her trial. Her guards are dismissed and replaced with Kevan's own men. As part of Kevan's attempts to repair the damage Cersei has done to the Lannister-Tyrell alliance, he tells Mace Tyrell that Cersei will no longer have any voice in the governing of the Seven Kingdoms, nor in Tommen's education. Kevan also intends to send Cersei to Casterly Rock after her trial, and make sure she stays there. Kevan feels that Cersei has been subdued and submissive since her walk, and that he has "pulled her claws".(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_97-0)

While awaiting trial, Cersei is attended by a septa and three novices selected by the High Septon, rather than her usual [ladies-in-waiting](wiki:Ladies-in-waiting). The High Septon insists that no girl spend more than seven days with Cersei, lest she be corrupted. The novices serve as Cersei's [handmaids](wiki:Handmaid), with one sharing her bed every night to make sure she has no other company. The novices report that of the hours Cersei is awake, she spends a third of that time with Tommen, another third praying, and the rest in her tub, bathing four or five times a day, scrubbing herself with [horsehair](wiki:Horse) brushes and lye soap.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_97-1)

After a meeting with the small council, Kevan dines with Cersei and Tommen. Cersei dresses extremely modestly, in a dark brown gown that buttons up to her throat, and a hooded green mantle to cover her shaved head. Cersei requests that once her innocence is proved, that Lady [Taena Merryweather](wiki:Taena_Merryweather) attend her again, and Kevan agrees, thinking Taena could accompany Cersei on her return to Casterly Rock. Although Kevan has no news of Jaime, Cersei seems certain of his safety, believing she would know if he were dead. Kevan begins to chide Cersei about her misjudgment regarding the Kettleblack brothers, but the meal is interrupted when a boy arrives, summoning Kevan to Grand Maester Pycelle's chambers.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_97-2)

In actuality, the boy is one of [Varys](wiki:Varys)'s [little birds](wiki:Little_birds), and Kevan arrives in the maester's chambers to find Pycelle murdered. Varys then assassinates Kevan, since his attempts to unify the Seven Kingdoms under Tommen were "threatening to undo all the queen's good work", and Varys wants the realm in chaos until [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) arrives. Varys shoots Kevan in the chest with a crossbow so that Cersei will believe he was murdered by the Tyrells with Tyrion's help, and then has the little birds end Kevan's suffering with their daggers.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_97-3)

### The Winds of Winter

The [mummers](wiki:Mummer) of [the Gate](wiki:The_Gate) in [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) perform *[The Bloody Hand](wiki:The_Bloody_Hand)*, a play based on recent events in Westeros. The mummer [Lady Stork](wiki:Lady_Stork) plays the role of the Queen, based on Cersei.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-mercy_98-0)

[Harys Swyft](wiki:Harys_Swyft) attends the first performance of the play. The young mummer [Mercy](wiki:Mercy) overhears one of his guards saying that if Harys returns to Westeros without the gold (from the [Iron Bank](wiki:Iron_Bank)), the queen will have his head.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-mercy_98-1)

In the [Vale](wiki:Vale), Petyr Baelish reports to Sansa Stark that Cersei has collected the heads of a dozen dwarfs, but none of them Tyrion's.(wiki:Cersei_Lannister.cite_ref-sansatwow_99-0)
