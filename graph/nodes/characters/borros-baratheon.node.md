## Identity

Borros Baratheon was the Lord of Storm's End and head of House Baratheon during the reigns of King Viserys I and Aegon II Targaryen. He was a key figure in the Dance of the Dragons (wiki:Borros_Baratheon).

## Origins

Born between 64 and 104 AC, Borros was the son of Lord Boremund Baratheon (track_b: Born, Father).

## Appearances & Description

Borros had a beard. He was a seasoned warrior, more temperamental than his stoic father Boremund, and even more belligerent. He was not a man of words — his maester would read his messages for him (wiki:Borros_Baratheon).

## Narrative Arc

Borros clashed with Dornishmen on numerous occasions. He married Elenda Caron, daughter of Lord Royce Caron. At the start of the Dance of the Dragons, the greens sent Prince Aemond Targaryen on Vhagar to Storm's End to win a Baratheon marriage alliance, while the blacks sent Prince Lucerys Velaryon on Arrax. Borros welcomed Aemond with feasts and hunts, and offered him his pick of four eligible daughters, feeling Rhaenyra had taken House Baratheon for granted. When Lucerys arrived, Borros rejected the blacks' overtures, having already committed to the greens. Borros died in 131 AC on the kingsroad (wiki:Borros_Baratheon).
