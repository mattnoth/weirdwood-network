## Origins

The tradition of [knighthood](wiki:Knight) is strong in the [Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn). During the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), the [Andals](wiki:Andals) first landed in the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers).(wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) Legends claim that the first [King of Mountain and Vale](wiki:King_of_Mountain_and_Vale), Ser [Artys I Arryn](wiki:Artys_I_Arryn), was the [Winged Knight](wiki:Winged_Knight).(wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
The knights of the Vale are sent out in search of the [Vale mountain clans](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans) on occasion.(wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) The lesser clansmen tend to be wiped out by these knights.(wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Ser [Hugh](wiki:Hugh), a young knight who squired in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with the late [Defender of the Vale](wiki:Defender_of_the_Vale), Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), is killed in the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Following her return to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), Jon's widow, Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), forbids her knights from traveling to the capital to fight in the tourney.(wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) arrests [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) and takes him to her sister Lysa at the Eyrie. When Tyrion sarcastically replies to the accusation of murdering Jon, Lysa warns him to speak politely, for she is protected by the knights of the Vale. When Tyrion later demands [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat), many knights and nobles ask for the honor of championing Lysa.(wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
After winning his trial by combat against Ser [Vardis Egen](wiki:Vardis_Egen) thanks to his champion [Bronn](wiki:Bronn) and departing the Eyrie, Tyrion appeals to the mountain clans, who reject his offers of [gold](wiki:Gold), by offering them weapons and armor to face the knights of the Vale.(wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
After Catelyn reunites with her son, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), she informs him that the only knight of the Vale that will join them is her uncle Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), formerly the [Knight of the Gate](wiki:Knight_of_the_Gate).(wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Despite their pleas for aid, Lysa ignores the messages urging the Vale to join forces with her nephew Robb, now the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) and [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident). After Robb ponders his next move after the betrayal of Lord [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark) at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), he considers that the knights of the Vale could make all the difference in the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) claims to [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) that the knights of the Vale will all pledge their swords to her to win her back [her birthright](wiki:Winterfell), after she is wed to Ser [Harrold Hardyng](wiki:Harrold_Hardyng).(wiki:Knights_of_the_Vale.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
