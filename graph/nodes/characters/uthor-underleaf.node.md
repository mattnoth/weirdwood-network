## Origins

Uthor was a professional [tourney](wiki:Tourney) knight who made his living wagering on jousts and the ransoms he gained for his wins. He preferred not to become a tourney champion, as he did not want to become famous, but was satisfied with the prize granted to the knight who came second. Uthor would bribe the [master of the games](wiki:Master_of_the_games) so that he would face the opponents he preferred—big men, older champions past their prime, and "village heroes"—where the odds would appear to be against him.(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

Ser Uthor attended the wedding of Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell) and [Lady Frey](wiki:Lady_Frey_(wife_of_Ambrose_Butterwell)). He participated in the [wedding tourney at Whitewalls](wiki:Wedding_tourney_at_Whitewalls) in celebration of the [marriage](wiki:Marriage). He was not part of the conspiracy to start the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion), only attending the tourney for the money he planned to win there.(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

In his first joust, Uthor unhorsed Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) in the first tilt with a blow to the head. In his second joust, he defeated Ser [Addam Frey](wiki:Addam_Frey).(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

Afterwards, Duncan came to Uthor's tent to forfeit his arms, armor, and horse, [Thunder](wiki:Thunder). He saw Uthor chastise his [squire](wiki:Squire), [Will](wiki:Will_(squire)), for accepting "traitor's gold", a [dragon](wiki:Currency) with the face of [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre). Uthor would have preferred to receive a ransom for Dunk's possessions, but Dunk had no money to pay it. Uthor would not accept a promise on parchment, but instead offered Dunk a place in his service. Uthor wanted someone who would appear to be a fearsome knight, who would get excellent odds when faced with Uthor at minor tourneys across [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). Uthor thought that by the time news got around that Dunk was a hopeless jouster, they would have won enough money to buy a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg). Dunk refused the offer, however. Uthor also revealed how Ser [Glendon Flowers](wiki:Glendon_Flowers) had achieved his [knighthood](wiki:Knight), and the fact that Dunk had a [secret enemy](wiki:Alyn_Cockshaw), as someone had bribed Uthor to kill Dunk in the joust.(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

Uthor then defeated Ser [Theomore Bulwer](wiki:Buford_Bulwer) in five tilts. For his next tilt, he unexpectedly was to go against Ser [Tommard Heddle](wiki:Tommard_Heddle), as Lord [Gormon Peake](wiki:Gormon_Peake) had bribed the master of the games with a better offer than Uthor's, to make sure that Uthor would not face Ser [John the Fiddler](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre) or win the tourney. However, the tourney was interrupted by claims that a thief had stolen the grand prize of the tourney, Lord Butterwell's dragon egg.(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

While Uthor was at the feast, Duncan infiltrated the knight's pavilion and reclaimed his sword and shield from Will, using them to defend his own squire, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), from Tom Heddle.(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11) After the defeat of the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion), Aegon convinced Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), to loan Dunk the gold to pay Uthor's ransom.(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

## Appearances & Description

Ser Uthor had small, shrewd, close-set eyes with thin, arching eyebrows. He kept his black beard neat and had a receding hairline.(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) His [squire](wiki:Squire) was named [Will](wiki:Will_(squire)).(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Uthor was a [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight),(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rawoiafuthor_underleaf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) although he preferred not be called as such.(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Uthor was mercenary and practical and, according to [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), was "no [true knight](wiki:True_knight)".(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Uthor's arms depicted a silver [snail](wiki:Snail) on green. Like his sigil, the snail, Uthor "hid in his shell", enjoying comfort while keeping a low profile. His tent's exterior was plain, but its inside was furnished with [Myrish](wiki:Myr) rugs, a featherbed, and ornate furniture. His [wine](wiki:Wine) cups of solid [silver](wiki:Silver) were inlaid with a line of [golden](wiki:Gold) snails around the lip.(wiki:Uthor_Underleaf.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
