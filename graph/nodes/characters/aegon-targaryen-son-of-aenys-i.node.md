## Identity

Aegon Targaryen (son of Aenys I) (26–43 AC) — child of Aenys I Targaryen and Alyssa Velaryon; married to Rhaena Targaryen (daughter of Aenys I); sworn to House Targaryen; Prince of Dragonstone. One of 12 Aegon Targaryens — see the Aegon Targaryen disambiguation entry.

## Origins

### Youth

Aegon was born in [26 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) to Prince [Aenys Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) and Lady [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon). In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), he was present on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) with his younger brother, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), where their grandfather, King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), was telling them tales of his conquests. Aegon the Conqueror suffered a stroke, however, and later died in peace.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

In [41 AC](wiki:41_AC), during the reign of his father Aenys I, Aegon still had not claimed a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) of his own, Although his sister [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) did take him flying with her on [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre).(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13) His father decided it was time his heir was wed and that he would marry his older sister, [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)). At the feast of his wedding, King Aenys officially named him the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), upsetting Dowager Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), who left the feast in protest.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14)

The [incestuous](wiki:Incest) marriage of Aegon and Rhaena enraged the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). After the wedding, Aegon and his bride were sent on a [royal progress](wiki:Royal_progress) by their sire. Rhaena wiser than her father asked his leave to bring her dragon [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre) with them, but the request was denied. As Aegon had not yet claimed a dragon, the king feared that the lords and commons might think him unmanly if they saw his wife on dragonback and him on a palfrey. The moment they set off, their escort were jeered by crowds and even pelted with dirt. Aegon had to be restrained by knights in the escort from trying to chastise the culprits as their party was outnumbered. The [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon) later declared Aenys a tyrant who had no right to rule which launched the [uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) against the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15)

### Fight for the Throne

In [42 AC](wiki:42_AC) Aegon and Rhaena were trapped at [Crakehall](wiki:Crakehall) by members of the [Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). At the same time, Aegon's father, King Aenys, died on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). Dowager Queen [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) brought Prince [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) back from his exile in [Essos](wiki:Essos), and he declared himself king.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16) When the [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) who had besieged Crakehall departed to march on King's Landing, Aegon and Rhaena fled to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), where Lord [Lyman Lannister](wiki:Lyman_Lannister) protected them by extending [guest right](wiki:Guest_right). There, Aegon's twin daughters [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) and [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon)) were born late that year.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-17)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

After Maegor's coronation, Aegon found himself abandoned by many who he once considered his loyal friends. Throughout the realm, it was whispered that Aegon was weak like his father was, pointing out Aegon had never taken a dragon of his own, whereas [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) had claimed [Balerion the Black Dread](wiki:Balerion). In [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), while Maegor was in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), Rhaena and Aegon took note of his long absence from the capital, and after a long journey managed to successfully infiltrate [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). With several companions aiding them, Aegon and Rhaena entered one of the city gates in a mule [wagon](wiki:Wagon), concealed in the back under sacks of corn. Once in the city, they were given further aid by several collaborators who had grown weary of Maegor's persecutions. However, Aegon and Rhaena did not seize the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), as they lacked the strength to hold it; they had come for [dragons](wiki:Dragons). Rhaena was reunited with [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre), and Aegon finally claimed a dragon, his late father's mount [Quicksilver](wiki:Quicksilver). The pair left the city on dragonback, so they could raise an army.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-18)

In [42 AC](wiki:42_AC), Aegon was [declared king](wiki:Aegon_the_Uncrowned's_rebellion) by his mother, Alyssa, but was becoming known as *"Aegon the Uncrowned"* throughout the realm.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-19) In [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), Queen [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower) warned Maegor that Alyssa was secretly communicating with the [Starks](wiki:House_Stark), [Arryns](wiki:House_Arryn), [Baratheons](wiki:House_Baratheon), and [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister). The [great lords](wiki:Great_lord), however, would only publicly support Aegon after a victory. Thus, Aegon denounced his uncle as a tyrant and usurper(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-20)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) and marched through the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) leading an army of fifteen thousand from atop his [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Quicksilver](wiki:Quicksilver).(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-21) Royalist armies marched toward him from three directions. Aegon's army was larger than any one, but the inexperienced young commander did not want to attack and defeat each one separately. During the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye), Maegor attacked while riding [Balerion](wiki:Balerion). The much larger and stronger dragon killed Aegon and Quicksilver.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-22)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

## Appearances & Description

Aegon at fifteen was lean and handsome, he caught the eye of many a [maiden](wiki:Maiden) and Aegon was not indifferent to their charms. Some said he was the very image of his [grandsire](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) at the same age. He served three years as a squire and greatly improved his martial skills with sword and axe, and he was widely regarded as the best young lance in all the realm.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)
