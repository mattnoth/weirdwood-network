## Identity

Jaehaera Targaryen — The child queen, wed to Aegon III, granddaughter of Alicent, had witnessed her twin brother's murder by Blood and Cheese.

## Origins

### Early life

Princess Jaehaera was born in the year [123 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), barely a year after her parents had wed. A [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) was placed in Jaehaera's cradle, and another was placed in the cradle of her twin brother, Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_II)). Both eggs soon hatched, and for Jaehaera, the dragon [Morghul](wiki:Morghul) was born.(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

That same year, two of Jaehaera's half-siblings were born: a boy, whose mother was a [whore](wiki:Whore) in the [Street of Silk](wiki:Street_of_Silk), and a girl, whose mother was a maidservant of Jaehaerys's grandmother, Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower). In [127 AC](wiki:127_AC), Jaehaera's trueborn brother Prince [Maelor](wiki:Maelor_Targaryen) was born.(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), on the third day of the third moon, Jaehaera, her brother Jaehaerys and Maelor, and their mother, Princess [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen), went to visit their grandfather, King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen). There, Jaehaera listened to a fictional tale(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-7) Viserys told, about their great-great-grandsire, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), Jaehaerys's namesake, and how he had flown north of the [Wall](wiki:Wall) on his [dragon](wiki:Dragon) to fight against [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings), [giants](wiki:Giants), and [wargs](wiki:Warg). That night, King Viserys died in his sleep.(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

### The Dance of the Dragons

At the time of the death of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), Jaehaera and her twin were six years old. Her father, [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), was crowned king by the [greens](wiki:Greens) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), but Aegon's older half-sister, [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), who had been Viserys's proclaimed heir, was crowned queen by the [blacks](wiki:Blacks) on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). This provoked a civil war that became known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). With her father now king, Princess Jaehaera became third in line to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

At the start of the war, Jaehaera's uncle, Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), killed Prince [Lucerys Velaryon](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon), the second son of Queen Rhaenyra. Her husband Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) declared vengeance, writing that there would be "An eye for an eye, a son for a son".(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Sometime thereafter, two men, remembered as [Blood](wiki:Blood_(the_butcher)) and [Cheese](wiki:Cheese_(the_rat-catcher)), snuck into the chambers of Dowager Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) in the [Tower of the Hand](wiki:Tower_of_the_Hand). As was her custom, Queen [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen) took her three children to visit their grandmother at dusk, before they went to bed. Once Helaena entered the chambers with Jaehaerys, Jaehaera, and Maelor, Blood barred the door and slew Helaena's guardsman, while Cheese snatched Maelor. Cheese told Helaena that they were two "debt collectors", and asked which one of her sons she wanted to lose. The queen offered herself but the two killers refused, telling her that it had to be a son, a boy. As the queen agonized over the decision, Cheese warned her to make a choice soon, before Blood grew bored and raped Jaehaera. The weeping Helaena eventually named her youngest child, two-year-old Maelor. Cheese told Maelor his mother wanted him dead, then grinned at Blood, who slew Prince Jaehaerys instead, striking off the boy's head with a single blow from his sword. The two men did no further harm to Helaena, Jaehaera, or Maelor, fleeing with Jaehaerys's head in hand while Helaena screamed. The deaths of both Prince Lucerys and Prince Jaehaerys set the civil war ablaze, with both sides calling for vengeance and unwilling to show any mercy.(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

In [130 AC](wiki:130_AC) during the [Fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing), when [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen)'s [dragons](wiki:Dragon) first appeared in the skies above the city, the [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers) Lord [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong) spirited Jaehaera out of the city along with her father, King [Aegon II](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), and younger brother, [Maelor](wiki:Maelor_Targaryen). Larys led them out through a [secret passage](wiki:Secret_passage) of [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast), of which only he had knowledge. Jaehaera was put in the charge of Ser [Willis Fell](wiki:Willis_Fell) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), who swore to bring her safely to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), while the Kingsguard [Rickard Thorne](wiki:Rickard_Thorne) was charged to bring Maelor to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown). After the escape, Rhaenyra posted huge rewards for information leading to the capture of Aegon, Jaehaera, Maelor, Willis Fell, Rickard Thorne, and Larys Strong. When that failed to produce anything, Rhaenyra sent out hunting parties of "[knights inquisitor](wiki:Knights_inquisitor)" to seek out the escapees, and punish anyone who helped them.(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Following the deaths of her parents, brothers, uncles, and dragon in the Dance, and the victory of the [blacks](wiki:Blacks), Jaehaera [was married](wiki:Wedding_of_Aegon_III_Targaryen_and_Jaehaera_Targaryen) to her cousin, King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), as part of the peace agreement. Since she was eight years old at the time of the [marriage](wiki:Marriage) and Aegon was eleven, it was not consummated.(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Jaehaera's grandmother was rarely allowed contact with her at this time. When they finally did share a meal, Alicent told her to slit her husband's throat in his sleep, setting the child to screaming. Jaehaera's marriage was troubled from the start. The children had little contact with each other except for formal occasions, which were rare because Jaehaera disliked leaving her chambers. Munkun observed that both children were broken and joyless after the losing nearly their entire family, some of whose murders they had witnessed. Jaehaera would not laugh or play and was prone to wetting the bed and weeping. Some also feared what might happen if Aegon died without an heir, given that Jaehaera's claim to the throne was as strong as his was.(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

### Death

Two years after her marriage, Jaehaera reportedly threw herself from her window in [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast), and was impaled on the spikes of the dry moat below. She lived for a half hour in agony before her death. With her, ended the line of the marriage of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) and his second wife, Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower).(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Jaehaera's death was officially deemed a suicide, akin to that of her mother, Queen [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen). However, many suspected that she had been murdered at the instigation of Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and one of the [seven regents](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) of Aegon III, as Unwin hoped to marry [his own daughter](wiki:Myrielle_Peake) to Aegon instead. It was variously suggested that Jaehaera had been murdered by Ser [Mervyn Flowers](wiki:Mervyn_Flowers) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), [bastard](wiki:Bastard) half-brother of Lord Peake, who had been guarding at the door to her bedchamber when she died; or that Mervyn had merely stood aside to allow [Tessario the Tiger](wiki:Tessario), a [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) in Lord Peake's service, to do the deed.(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

## Appearances & Description

Jaehaera was born tiny and slow to grow. She did not cry or smile or act as babies normally do.(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Her lack of emotion continued as she grew older.(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Jaehaera is described as a sweet and [simple](wiki:Lackwit) girl at the time of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) In the aftermath of the war, Jaehaera was a joyless child. She would wet the bed and weep uncontrollably when corrected. When she was eight years old, her ladies said she was more like a child nearing four.(wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
