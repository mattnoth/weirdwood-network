## Identity

Walder, commonly called Hodor, is a stableboy and servant at Winterfell in the service of House Stark (wiki:Hodor). His true name is Walder, but he is universally known as "Hodor" because it is the only word he is capable of saying (wiki:Hodor.cite_ref-Ragot24).

## Origins

Born in 283 AC or before (track_b: Born). His great-grandmother is Old Nan, the oldest servant at Winterfell. Old Nan's two sons, including Hodor's grandfather, died in Robert's Rebellion. Hodor's father was Old Nan's grandson (wiki:Hodor).

## Appearances & Description

Hodor is seven feet tall with brown eyes, shaggy hair, a brown beard, and thick brown body hair as dense as a pelt (wiki:Hodor). He has a huge smile and is strong enough to carry an anvil easily. Osha suggests his large size may be the result of giants' blood (wiki:Hodor). He smells faintly of horses and sleeps above the stable (wiki:Hodor). He is gentle, loyal, and slow of wits but capable of performing tasks deftly once taught (wiki:Hodor).

## Narrative Arc

Hodor serves at Winterfell during AGOT, where he helps care for the crippled Bran Stark after his fall (wiki:Hodor.cite_ref-Ragot24). During ACOK, after Theon Greyjoy captures Winterfell, Hodor carries Bran in a basket on his back when Bran and Rickon flee the castle (wiki:Hodor). In ASOS, Hodor travels north with Bran, Meera and Jojen Reed toward the Wall. During their stay at the Nightfort, Hodor is terrified by the darkness but Bran calms him by entering his mind through warging (wiki:Hodor). In ADWD, Hodor accompanies Bran beyond the Wall to the cave of the three-eyed crow. Bran continues to warg into Hodor when needed, though Hodor resists by retreating into a corner of his mind (wiki:Hodor).
