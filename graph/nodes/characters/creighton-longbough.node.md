## Appearances & Description

Ser Creighton has a big belly that strains at the laces of his potted doeskin jerkin. He has a shaggy, untrimmed beard the color of old gold that covers his cheeks and chin. Creighton is nearsighted and squints on occasion. He is younger than Ser [Illifer the Penniless](wiki:Illifer_the_Penniless), who is around sixty. The hedge knight rides a swaybacked brown gelding with rheumy eyes. There is a deep gouge in his shield made by a battle-axe.(wiki:Creighton_Longbough.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Creighton acts like a courteous man who strives to be a [true knight](wiki:True_knight). He is a follower of [the Faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) and is distrustful of the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Creighton_Longbough.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Creighton is boastful, claiming to be able to fight either hand and that singers sing of his fame. However, [Brienne Tarth](wiki:Brienne_Tarth) and Ser [Shadrich](wiki:Shadrich) are skeptical of his prowess.(wiki:Creighton_Longbough.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

According to Creighton, he did great carnage in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater) and won a dozen ransoms. He claims to have slain Ser [Herbert Bolling](wiki:Herbert_Bolling) and eleven other fearsome knights during the battle, and to have fought an epic battle with the [Knight of the Red Chicken](wiki:Knight_of_the_Red_Chicken). His steed served him well.(wiki:Creighton_Longbough.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

While en route to [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), Creighton and his companion, Ser [Illifer the Penniless](wiki:Illifer_the_Penniless), are met by [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_Tarth). Creighton offers to share their trout and fire with her and insists upon escorting her to Duskendale. During the night, Creighton warns away six armed men. The next morning, as they break their fast, Creighton regales Brienne with his exploits at the Blackwater.(wiki:Creighton_Longbough.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

Creighton and Illifer continue on to Duskendale with Brienne. They come upon a merchant named [Hibald](wiki:Hibald), his serving men, and a hedge knight in his employ, Ser [Shadrich](wiki:Shadrich), all heading in the same direction. Creighton suggests riding together as three blades are better than one, to which Hibald agrees.(wiki:Creighton_Longbough.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)

As night gathers the party comes upon the [Old Stone Bridge](wiki:Old_Stone_Bridge), where Creighton owes seven silver stags to the innkeeper, [Naggle](wiki:Naggle). When Creighton and Illifer have no coin, Brienne offers to pay for a room for the two men, and buys them goat to eat. After Creighton and the others have gone to bed and the inn is quiet, Brienne gathers her things and rides out alone for Duskendale, asking a silent pardon of the hedge knights.(wiki:Creighton_Longbough.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8)
