## Origins

It is unknown when Boros joined the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). He may have joined in the aftermath of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), when the White Swords had five vacancies.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-22) Blount is confirmed to have been a Kingsguard by the time of the [Greyjoy Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy_Rebellion) of [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), however.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Boros took part in the [tourney at Lannisport](wiki:Tourney_at_Lannisport) in [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), during which he was defeated by the eventual champion, Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont), [Lord of Bear Island](wiki:Lord_of_Bear_Island).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

## Appearances & Description

Boros is an ugly man, with a broad chest, and short, bandy legs. He has a flat nose, and jowls.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) considers him bald(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) while [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) notices brittle grey hair.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Boros is shorter and heavier than his fellow [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

Boros is short-tempered.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Jaime thinks of him as an adequate fighter, but never more than an ordinary [knight](wiki:Knight).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) considers Boros to be loyal to Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Many characters think of Boros with contempt due to the man's cowardice.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Boros visits [prostitutes](wiki:Prostitute) on the [Street of Silk](wiki:Street_of_Silk).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

The knight usually wears the long white cloak(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) and white steel(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) plate(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) of the Kingsguard. He wields a sword(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) and white shield(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) and has a high white helm.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Boros is sometimes attired in white velvet, with a snowy cloak fastened with a golden [lion](wiki:Lion) brooch with tiny [ruby](wiki:Ruby) eyes.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

With his sworn brothers Ser [Meryn Trant](wiki:Meryn_Trant) and Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), Boros escorts King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) According to Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys), Boros and Meryn, despite their oaths to Robert, are Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s creatures.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

After the death of Robert and the arrest of Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), Boros escorts Ned's daughter, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), from [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) to the [small council](wiki:Small_council) chambers to meet with Cersei. The queen tasks Boros with delivering [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole) to Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

Boros is present in the throne room of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) when Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), is dismissed by Cersei, and he laughs at his former Sworn Brother's shame.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

### A Clash of Kings

Boros does not hesitate to hit [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) on order of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon), including after learning that [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) has been declared [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Sansa considers him the worst member of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) intervenes when Joffrey orders Boros to strip Sansa naked and beat her bloody after hearing of the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

Boros survives the [riots in King's Landing](wiki:Riots_in_King's_Landing), when the royal procession is attacked on its return to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) after seeing Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) off to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). Boros is reluctant to follow Cersei's command to find Sansa in the turmoil, but [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) ends up returning the girl to the castle.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

Concerned for the safety of her younger son, Prince [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon), during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Cersei tasks Boros and Lord [Gyles Rosby](wiki:Gyles_Rosby) with secretly bringing Tommen from the unruly capital to the safety of [Rosby](wiki:Rosby). Ser [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister) informs Tyrion, however, and the acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) orders Ser [Jacelyn Bywater](wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater), Commander of the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing), to take Tommen into custody before the boy reaches Rosby.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) When Jacelyn's gold cloaks stop Gyles's retinue on [the road](wiki:Rosby_road), Boros surrenders Tommen without a fight.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Tommen is watched over at Rosby by men loyal to Tyrion, and Boros is imprisoned in the castle's dungeon.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) Livid at Boros's cowardice, Cersei has King Joffrey strip him of his white cloak. Ser [Osmund Kettleblack](wiki:Osmund_Kettleblack) replaces Boros on the Kingsguard.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

### A Storm of Swords

After Ser [Jacelyn Bywater](wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater) dies in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Cersei sends the [Kettleblacks](wiki:House_Kettleblack) to recover Tommen from Rosby.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), reinstates Boros to the Kingsguard upon his return, to fill the vacancy left by [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) when the [Hound](wiki:Hound) deserted during the Blackwater.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

During the trial of Tyrion for [the poisoning](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), Boros recounts the tale of Tyrion beating Joffrey after the riot, and Tyrion's threat against Joffrey's life.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) He also laughs when [Shae](wiki:Shae) testifies that Tyrion made her call him "my giant of Lannister".(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), the one-handed [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), makes Boros the official [food taster](wiki:Food_taster) of the new king, Tommen I, during a meeting in [White Sword Tower](wiki:White_Sword_Tower). Boros is enraged at this, and states that Jaime should be the food taster since he has lost his sword hand. When Jaime states they can duel if Boros is unhappy, Boros spits at Jaime's feet and leaves the room angrily.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

Boros and the other Kingsguard stand guard to keep the crowd away when Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) duels Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) in Tyrion's [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat). Boros becomes distracted by the duel, however, and Gregor kills a stableboy who stands too close to the fighting.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1)(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

### A Feast for Crows

Boros and Ser [Osmund Kettleblack](wiki:Osmund_Kettleblack) bring the news of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s death to Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), the regent for King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon). Boros learns from the gaolers that [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) disappeared from the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells). Boros and Osmund are ordered by Cersei to murder the two turnkeys who fell asleep during Tyrion's escape;(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Raffc3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) they are later reprimanded by their Lord Commander, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), for mindlessly killing the only two leads to the mystery.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

In Dorne, Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) thinks of Boros and his familiarity with the [Street of Silk](wiki:Street_of_Silk) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

Cersei thinks Boros looks grey when he stands guard outside the [small council](wiki:Small_council)'s chambers.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) When Tommen objects to Cersei speaking ill of his new bride, Queen [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), Cersei tasks Boros with having Tommen bloody his [whipping boy](wiki:Whipping_boy), [Pate](wiki:Pate_(whipping_boy)).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

Cersei, when she frames Margaery for [treason](wiki:Treason), plans on Boros being the only Kingsguard available if Margaery asks for [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat), as Cersei believes Boros would be no match for any champion named by the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) When Cersei inquires Osmund about Boros's fighting skill, Osmund assures her that Boros is an inept fighter.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Boros and ten [House Lannister guards](wiki:House_Lannister_guards) escort Cersei to the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor). While they wait outside unaware, Cersei is arrested by the Faith.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

Boros stands guard at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) during Cersei's [walk of atonement](wiki:Walk_of_atonement).(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

Boros guards Tommen when Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), the [Lord Regent](wiki:Regent), has dinner with Cersei and the young king. Boros appears heavier and in poor health. He has a difficult time standing for the duration of the meal, and he has to lean on a wall for support. Boros escorts Tommen and his [kittens](wiki:Cat) to the royal bedchamber.(wiki:Boros_Blount.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)
