## Origins

Patchface was a [jester](wiki:Jester) [slave](wiki:Slavery) in [Volantis](wiki:Volantis). He was a clever and skilled boy, with astonishing wit. He was reportedly "nimble as a [monkey](wiki:Monkey)", could juggle and tell riddles and do magic tricks, and could sing well in four languages. Patchface's freedom was bought by [Steffon Baratheon](wiki:Steffon_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End), who was impressed with the boy during his trip to the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities). Steffon wrote to his [maester](wiki:Maester), [Cressen](wiki:Cressen), saying he believed his son [Robert](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) would be delighted with Patchface, and that he hoped the fool might be able to make his serious son [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) laugh.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8) However, while Lord Steffon was returning to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), his ship was struck by a sudden storm in [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay). Steffon's eldest sons, Robert and Stannis, watched from the parapets of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) while the *[Windproud](wiki:Windproud)* was smashed against the rocks of the bay. All aboard the ship were believed killed, including Lord Steffon, his wife [Cassana](wiki:Cassana_Estermont), and over a hundred soldiers and sailors.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

Patchface washed up among the dead three days later, naked, his skin white and wrinkled. Maester Cressen believed him to be dead as well, but when the Baratheon's servant [Jommy](wiki:Jommy) grabbed him to put him on the burial wagon, Patchface coughed up water and sat up. Jommy would swear for the rest of his life that the boy's flesh was clammy cold. What happened to Patchface during the two days he was lost in the sea is unknown, but the fisherfolk around Storm's End often tell a story that a [mermaid](wiki:Mermaid) had taught him to breathe water in return for his seed.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

Though Patchface had survived the shipwreck, he was broken in mind and body; the ordeal had taken his memories, and half his wit. He matched none of what Lord Steffon had written in his letter, and was only recognized by his tattooed face. It was suggested by Ser [Harbert](wiki:Harbert), the [castellan](wiki:Castellan) of Storm’s End, that it would be kindest for Cressen to [euthanize](wiki:Gift_of_mercy) the mad boy with [milk of the poppy](wiki:Milk_of_the_poppy), but the maester refused to do so.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

Despite his condition, Patchface is employed as court fool and jester at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), the castle of Lord Stannis Baratheon. He spends most of his time with [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon), Lord Stannis's daughter. He offers strange insights and stranger songs, sometimes touching upon what may have really happened to the boy at the bottom of the bay.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

## Appearances & Description

Patchface takes his name from the [tattoos](wiki:Tattoo) covering his broad face, which is patterned with squares of green and red motley.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) These tattoos are the practice of the the [Free City](wiki:Free_City) of [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), where the faces of [slaves](wiki:Slave) are marked to display their status and their profession.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Rawoiafpatchface.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The obese [fool](wiki:Fool) is dough-soft and slump-shouldered.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Patchface has lost his memory and much of his wits. The fool suffers from twitches and trembles, and he is often incoherent. He has an unusual sideways walk.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) Some consider him a [simpleton](wiki:Lackwit).(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

The fool wears a mock helm fashioned from an old [tin](wiki:Tin) bucket, with a rack of [deer](wiki:Deer) antlers strapped to the crown and hung with cowbells.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) The bells ring frequently, as Patchface seldom remains still(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and often hops back and forth.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Patchface only speaks in songs and rhymes. Some of his words seem to be of a [prophetic](wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies) nature, though none of his listeners understand them. (See [Patchface/Theories](wiki:Patchface/Theories).)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Patchface accompanies [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon) when she visits Maester [Cressen](wiki:Cressen) in his chamber at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) to see the [white raven](wiki:White_raven) that had arrived from the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) to announce the end of summer. Later that day, when Cressen arrives at the castle's dinner feast, a dancing Patchface accidentally collides with the maester and knocks him over. [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), a [red priestess](wiki:Red_priestess) of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor) and adviser to King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), helps Cressen stand up, but after he dismisses her religious beliefs, she places Patchface's [tin](wiki:Tin) helm on Cressen's head, jesting about *"a clever fool and a foolish wise man."* The maester returns the helm to Patchface, but when Cressen again disparages the power of R'hllor, Queen [Selyse Florent](wiki:Selyse_Florent) orders him to wear the fool's helm once more, and Stannis assents to his wife's command. Cressen attempts to kill Melisandre by sharing a cup of [poisoned](wiki:Poison) [wine](wiki:Wine) with her, but her [magic](wiki:Magic) causes [the poison](wiki:Strangler) to have no affect on her. As Cressen dies, he hears the bells of Patchface's helm ring in his antlers, and imagines them saying *"fool, fool."*(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)

Patchface is in attendance when Stannis Baratheon and Melisandre burn the statues of [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) on [Dragonstone's](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) beach, singing about smoke and flames under the sea.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

At the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), the [small council](wiki:Small_council) meets to deal with Stannis's proclamation that the children of Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) were not fathered by her husband, the late King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), but rather her brother, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister). Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) suggests spreading a false tale that Shireen, whose face is disfigured by [greyscale](wiki:Greyscale), was fathered by Patchface instead of Stannis, saying that the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) will believe that Patchface's tattoos caused her disfigurement.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) insults Stannis by mentioning the rumor during [their parley at Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)).(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) delivers Stannis's proclamation throughout the realm, but often finds that the rumor about Selyse and Patchface has arrived before he has, casting doubt on the true tale of Cersei's adultery. He tells Stannis that people gleefully share the rumor, whether they believe it or not.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Storm of Swords

When Davos Seaworth is rescued from being marooned on the [spears of the merling king](wiki:Spears_of_the_merling_king) after nearly drowning during the [battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), he hopes he has not gone mad like Patchface. Later, when Davos returns to Dragonstone, he finds Patchface playing [monsters and maidens](wiki:Monsters_and_maidens) with Shireen and her cousin, [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm).(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

Maester [Pylos](wiki:Pylos) prevents Patchface from attending Shireen's lessons, as the bells of his fool's helm constantly ring loudly. During the lessons, Patchface waits faithfully outside the maester's chamber door.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

### A Dance with Dragons

When Stannis sails north to help the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) defend the realm from the [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) and the [Others](wiki:Others), Patchface accompanies Shireen and Selyse to [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea). The castle's [commander](wiki:Commander_(Night's_Watch)), [Cotter Pyke](wiki:Cotter_Pyke), writes to [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), that Patchface is a [simpleton](wiki:Lackwit). At Eastwatch, Patchface receives a motley cloak of beaver pelts, sheepskins, and rabbit fur. His horned fool's helm now includes long brown flaps of squirrel fur that hang down over his ears.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

When Selyse's entourage arrives at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), she does not bother to introduce Patchface to Jon Snow, but Jon recognizes the fool from Cotter Pyke's letters. The [giant](wiki:Giant) [Wun Wun](wiki:Wun_Wun) is fascinated with the fool and his singing and dancing, but when Wun Wun reaches for him, Patchface backs away in alarm. The giant laughs loudly after Patchface falls into a snowdrift.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Patchface attends the [wedding of Sigorn and Alys Karstark](wiki:Wedding_of_Sigorn_and_Alys_Karstark), and [Owen the Oaf](wiki:Owen_the_Oaf) dances with him during the feast. Melisandre tells Jon that she feels Patchface is dangerous. She says that she has often glimpsed the fool [in her flames](wiki:Vision), sometimes with *"skulls about him, and his lips are red with blood."*(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

When Jon brings [Val](wiki:Val) to meet with Queen Selyse, Patchface is dancing in the queen's chambers. On Selyse's command, Patchface takes Shireen to her room.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

When Jon announces a ranging to rescue [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) at [Hardhome](wiki:Hardhome), Patchface enthusiastically volunteers to lead it.(wiki:Patchface.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
