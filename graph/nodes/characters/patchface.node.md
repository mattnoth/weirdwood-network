## Identity

Patchface, nicknamed Patches, is Lord Stannis Baratheon's court fool and jester at Dragonstone. His given name is unknown. His face is tattooed in motley, from which he takes his name (wiki:Patchface).

## Origins

Born between 262 and 275 AC in Essos. He was a jester slave in Volantis, where his face was tattooed with green and red squares in the Volantene tradition of marking slaves (track_b: Born, wiki:Patchface).

## Appearances & Description

Patchface's broad face is patterned with squares of green and red motley. He is obese, dough-soft, and slump-shouldered. He has lost his memory and much of his wits, suffering from twitches and trembles. He has an unusual sideways walk and seldom remains still. He wears a mock helm fashioned from an old tin bucket with deer antlers hung with cowbells (wiki:Patchface).

## Narrative Arc

Patchface was a clever, skilled jester slave in Volantis — nimble, able to juggle, tell riddles, do magic tricks, and sing in four languages. Lord Steffon Baratheon bought his freedom during a trip to the Free Cities, writing to Maester Cressen that he hoped the fool might make his serious son Stannis laugh. On the return voyage, the Windproud was wrecked in Shipbreaker Bay. All aboard were believed killed. Three days later, Patchface washed ashore among the dead — naked, white, wrinkled. When the servant Jommy grabbed his ankles for the burial wagon, Patchface coughed up water and sat up. Jommy swore the boy's flesh was clammy cold. What happened during his two days in the sea is unknown, though fisherfolk say a mermaid taught him to breathe water. He emerged broken — witless and incoherent. He now speaks only in songs and rhymes, some of which appear to be prophetic, though his listeners do not understand them. He is the companion of Shireen Baratheon (wiki:Patchface).
