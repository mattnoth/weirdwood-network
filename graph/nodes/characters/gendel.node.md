## Origins

Maester [Herryk](wiki:Herryk)'s [History of the Kings-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:History_of_the_Kings-Beyond-the-Wall) has a transcription of a [wildling](wiki:Free_folk) song about Gendel and [Gorne](wiki:Gorne) in which the brothers mediated a dispute between a clan of [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) and a family of [giants](wiki:Giants) over control of a cave. After discovering the cave was part of a larger system of caverns which extended beneath the [Wall](wiki:Wall), Gendel and Gorne resorted to trickery and convinced both parties to abandon the cave. [Maesters](wiki:Maesters) are skeptical of the wildling tale, however.(wiki:Gendel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
Three thousand years before the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Gendel and Gorne ruled as [Kings-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:Kings-Beyond-the-Wall). The brother kings gathered the free folk to them and marched them through [Gorne's Way](wiki:Gorne's_Way), their path through caves beneath the Wall. The wildlings evaded the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), but when they emerged on the far side, the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) was waiting for them and attacked them. Gorne slew the king from [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark), but his son took up his crown and killed Gorne in turn. While the battle raged, the Night's Watch, made aware of the battle, sortied from their castles and took the wildlings in the rear. With the Starks to the south, the [Umbers](wiki:House_Umber) the east, and the Watch to the north, Gendel was also killed, according to the traditions of [northmen](wiki:Northmen)(wiki:Gendel.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) and of [rangers](wiki:Rangers).(wiki:Gendel.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_wildlings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
According to the free folk, however, Gendel survived the fighting. Cutting his way through the Night's Watch, he attempted to return [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall) via the same route the wildlings had come. Gendel did not know the way as well as Gorne, however, and supposedly became lost forever trying to find his way out.(wiki:Gendel.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Free folk legend says that the descendants of Gendel's people still dwell in those caves, attacking anyone who tries to find Gorne's Way and [eating them](wiki:Cannibalism).(wiki:Gendel.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
