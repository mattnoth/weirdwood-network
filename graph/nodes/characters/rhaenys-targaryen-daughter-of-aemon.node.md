## Identity

## Origins

### Early life[ ]

Rhaenys was born in [74 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) on the seventh day of the seventh [moon](wiki:Moon) of the year. As her father was the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), and she was his firstborn child, many considered her as being [next in line](wiki:Customs) for the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), after her father Aemon. Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), when being presented with her granddaughter, called her "our queen to be".(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)
Rhaenys became a [dragonrider](wiki:Dragonrider) in [87 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) when she claimed the [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Meleys](wiki:Meleys), that had been ridden by her late aunt [Alyssa Targaryen](wiki:Alyssa_Targaryen). In [89 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Rhaenys joined King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) for part of his [royal progress](wiki:Royal_progress). She flew on Meleys to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden) to meet up with Jaehaerys, and together they flew to all four of the [Shield Islands](wiki:Shield_Islands). On [Greenshield](wiki:Greenshield) she informed the king about her intention to marry Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), and received her grandfather's blessing.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) Rhaenys married Corlys in [90 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) at the age of sixteen. She insisted on arriving at the [wedding](wiki:Wedding) on the back of Meleys.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)
In early [92 AC](wiki:92_AC), Rhaenys learned that she was pregnant with her first child.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

### The Queen Who Never Was[ ]

Rhaenys's father, Prince [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), died in [92 AC](wiki:92_AC). As Aemon had been the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) had to formally appoint a new heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). The choice was between Rhaenys, Aemon's only child and heir, and Aemon's brother [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)). Jaehaerys appointed Baelon as the new [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), passing over the pregnant Rhaenys and her unborn child. Rhaenys herself was the first to object to the king's decision, and her husband, Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), gave up his position as [lord admiral](wiki:Lord_admiral) and his place on the [small council](wiki:Small_council). Following that, Rhaenys and her husband returned to [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark). Others angered by the king's decision included Rhaenys's mother, Lady [Jocelyn Baratheon](wiki:Jocelyn_Baratheon), her uncle, Lord [Boremund Baratheon](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon), and her grandmother, King Jaehaerys's wife, Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen).(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
Rhaenys gave birth to her first child, a daughter she named [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon), late in the year [92 AC](wiki:92_AC).(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-9) Two years later, she give birth to a son, [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon). Although both [House Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) and [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon) insisted that Laenor had the better claim to the throne, Prince Baelon remained King Jaehaerys's heir.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)
When Baelon died in [101 AC](wiki:101_AC), King Jaehaerys called the first [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) to decide the matter of succession. Fourteen claims were considered, including the claims of Rhaenys and her children.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) Rhaenys's uncle, Lord [Boremund Baratheon](wiki:Boremund_Baratheon), was outspoken in supporting the claims of Rhaenys and her son Laenor during the Great Council,(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and Lord [Ellard Stark](wiki:Ellard_Stark) gave his support to Rhaenys and Corlys as well.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Other supporters included [House Dustin](wiki:House_Dustin) of [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton) and [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly) of [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor).(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) Yet the claims of Rhaenys and her daughter were rejected on account of their [sex](wiki:Gender_and_sexuality). Rhaenys and Corlys used their wealth and influence to persuade the [lords](wiki:Lord) present to vote for their son Laenor, one of the two primary claimants. For this they received support from Lord Boremund, Lord Ellard, Lord [Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly), Lord [Dustin](wiki:House_Dustin), Lord [Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood), Lord [Bar Emmon](wiki:House_Bar_Emmon), Lord [Celtigar](wiki:House_Celtigar), as well as others. However, Laenor was eventually passed over in favor of Baelon's son [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) While the tally was kept secret, it was rumored the assembled lords favored Viserys by twenty to one.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
For having been passed over in the line of succession, the court jester [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) would name her "The Queen Who Never Was",(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) the nickname stuck among the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk).(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
Following the death of Queen [Aemma Arryn](wiki:Aemma_Arryn), in [105 AC](wiki:105_AC), [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Runciter](wiki:Runciter) suggested to King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) that he take Rhaenys's twelve-year-old daughter, Laena, to wife. King Viserys, however, chose [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower), daughter of the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower). Angered by the third time she and her family had been scorned by [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), Rhaenys and her family did not attend the wedding in [106 AC](wiki:106_AC). In [113 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King Viserys I and his [small council](wiki:Small_council) began discussions concerning a betrothal for Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), Viserys's heir. They eventually agreed on Rhaenys' son, Laenor. Rhaenyra and Laenor were wed the next year.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)
Both of Rhaenys's children died in [120 AC](wiki:120_AC); Laena (and her child) died in childbirth during the early days of the year, while Laenor was killed by Ser [Qarl Correy](wiki:Qarl_Correy) while attending a fair in [Spicetown](wiki:Spicetown).(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)

### The Dance of the Dragons[ ]

When the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) began in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), Princess Rhaenys sat on the [black council](wiki:Black_council) of Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), the daughter of the late King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen). When the [Velaryon fleet](wiki:Velaryon_fleet) closed off the [Gullet](wiki:Gullet), sailing forth from [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) and [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) to block all shipping for [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay), the [blacks](wiki:Blacks)' plan was for Rhaenys to fly overhead on [Meleys](wiki:Meleys) to keep the [greens](wiki:Greens) from attacking their ships with [dragons](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)
When news arrived on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) about the death of [Lucerys Velaryon](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon) at the hands of Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), Rhaenys and her husband prevented Lucerys's brother [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon) from mounting his [dragon](wiki:Dragon) to avenge his brother. Grief-stricken following the death of Lucerys, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) gave Rhaenys and Corlys command over her war council.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
Nine days after [Lord Staunton](wiki:Lord_Staunton) dispatched a plea for help, Rhaenys appeared above [Rook's Rest](wiki:Rook's_Rest) upon [Meleys](wiki:Meleys). They attacked the soldiers of Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) during the [battle at Rook's Rest](wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest), but the attack proved to be a ploy. When King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) and Prince Aemond Targaryen suddenly appeared upon [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre) and [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), Rhaenys turned to meet the attack, despite the knowledge that Meleys stood no chance against the two dragons. The three dragons attacked each other and fell to the ground, entangled. Meleys died in the attack, and besides her carcass a body was found which was believed to be Rhaenys, although it was so blackened that no one could be certain it was her. Her husband, [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), was enraged that Rhaenyra had sent Rhaenys instead of going herself, and that Rhaenyra had forbidden her sons [Jacaerys](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) and [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon) from accompanying Rhaenys.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
