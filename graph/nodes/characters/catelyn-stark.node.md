## Identity

Catelyn Stark, née Tully, is the wife of Lord Eddard Stark of Winterfell and a major POV character. Born into House Tully of Riverrun, she is the eldest daughter of Lord Hoster Tully and Lady Minisa Whent. Known as Cat to family and friends (wiki:Catelyn_Stark). After death, she is resurrected as Lady Stoneheart, also called the Silent Sister, Mother Merciless, and the Hangwoman (wiki:Catelyn_Stark).

## Origins

Born 264–265 AC at Riverrun (track_b: Born). Daughter of Hoster Tully and Minisa Whent (track_b: Father, Mother). Originally betrothed to Brandon Stark, she married his brother Eddard after Brandon's execution by Aerys II (wiki:Catelyn_Stark).

## Appearances & Description

Beautiful, with fair skin, thick auburn hair, deep blue Tully eyes, long fingers, and fine high cheekbones. Resembles her mother Minisa in her cheekbones and jaw according to Brynden Tully. A head shorter than Edmure (wiki:Catelyn_Stark). Tries to follow the Tully words: "Family, Duty, Honor." Proud, honorable, and fiercely protective of her family, though she regards Jon Snow as an outsider and has never forgiven Eddard for bringing his bastard into the household (wiki:Catelyn_Stark). Holds to the Faith of the Seven (wiki:Catelyn_Stark).

## Narrative Arc

Catelyn grew up at Riverrun with Lysa, Edmure, and Petyr Baelish, her father's ward. Originally betrothed to Brandon Stark, she married Eddard after Brandon's death, bearing five children: Robb, Sansa, Arya, Bran, and Rickon (track_b: Issue, wiki:Catelyn_Stark).

When Bran is pushed from a tower and an assassin comes for him, Catelyn travels to King's Landing to warn Ned, where Littlefinger identifies the catspaw's dagger as Tyrion Lannister's. She seizes Tyrion at the inn at the crossroads and takes him to the Eyrie for trial (wiki:Catelyn_Stark). She then joins Robb's army, serving as advisor and envoy throughout the War of the Five Kings (agot-catelyn-08).

Catelyn negotiates the crossing at the Twins with Walder Frey, arranges the alliance with House Frey, and later treats with Renly and Stannis Baratheon (wiki:Catelyn_Stark). After learning of Bran and Rickon's apparent deaths, she frees Jaime Lannister from captivity in a desperate attempt to recover Sansa and Arya, an act Robb's bannermen view as treason (wiki:Catelyn_Stark).

At the Red Wedding, Catelyn watches Robb die and is murdered herself — her throat is cut and her body thrown into the river. Three days later, she is resurrected by Lord Beric Dondarrion, who gives his life for hers. As Lady Stoneheart, she leads the Brotherhood without Banners on a campaign of vengeance against the Freys and Lannisters, hanging those she judges guilty (wiki:Catelyn_Stark). Died 299 AC at the Twins (track_b: Died).
