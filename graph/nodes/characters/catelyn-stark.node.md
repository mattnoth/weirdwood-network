## Origins

### Early Life

Catelyn is the eldest daughter of [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully), the [Lord Paramount of the Trident](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Trident), [Lord of Riverrun](wiki:Lord_of_Riverrun), and head of [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully), and his wife, Lady [Minisa Whent](wiki:Minisa_Whent). She was anointed with the seven oils and named in the [sept](wiki:Sept) of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-1) Catelyn had two older brothers, who both died in infancy, causing her father to regard her as his heir until the birth of Catelyn's younger brother, [Edmure](wiki:Edmure_Tully).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1) Catelyn also has a sister, [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), who is two years younger than her.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) Her mother Minisa died in childbirth with her fourth son and sixth child, who also died shortly after.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Catelyn was raised at Riverrun. She was close with her sister Lysa,(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) and would play with her two younger siblings, as well as with her father's young [ward](wiki:Ward), [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-2)(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) Just as her younger siblings and Petyr were wont to do, Catelyn would go to her uncle, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), with her tears and tales, whenever Lord Hoster was too busy, and Lady Minisa was too ill.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) When Catelyn's mother died in childbirth, along with her son, Lord Hoster told his daughter that she had to be the Lady of Riverrun in her mother's stead.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-2) Catelyn does not have many memories of her mother,(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) though she still remembers her warm smile and soft hands.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

Catelyn's relationship with her father was a close one, and according to Catelyn, Lord Hoster cherished her best of all his children.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-3) Hoster taught her how to ride in the [godswood of Riverrun](wiki:Godswood_of_Riverrun).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) Whenever Hoster would ride off to court or a battle, he would tell Catelyn to watch for him from the battlements of the castle, which she did.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) When his eyes tired, Hoster would have Catelyn read to him in his [solar](wiki:Solar).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

Catelyn and Lysa played together as girls, and even had a secret language.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-3) Once, they made mud pies and served them to Petyr, who ate them and as a consequence was sick for a week.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) Catelyn and Lysa also practiced kissing with Petyr in the godswood, where Petyr attempted to stick his tongue in their mouths - an attempt which Catelyn halted, but Lysa both allowed and enjoyed it.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3) Catelyn often accompanied her father on his travels, and had visited the [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads) on multiple occasions.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-2)(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) Once, Catelyn, Lysa and Petyr accompanied Hoster to [Seagard](wiki:Seagard). During the journey to the seat of [House Mallister](wiki:House_Mallister), they stopped at [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones), where Catelyn had played at being [Jenny of Oldstones](wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones), while Petyr had pretended to be her [Prince of Dragonflies](wiki:Duncan_Targaryen).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1) On their way back from Seagard to Riverrun, Catelyn and Lysa got lost in the mist, and were eventually found by Petyr.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3)

### Betrothal to Brandon Stark

In [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) or [277 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), when she was twelve years old, Catelyn was betrothed to [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark), the heir to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell),(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-4)(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rawoiafcatelyn_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) and thanked her father for making such a splendid match for her.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-4) Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully)'s ward, [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), was less pleased, however. While Catelyn had come to regard Petyr as a brother,(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-4) Petyr had fallen in love with Catelyn.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

When Lords [Blackwood](wiki:House_Blackwood) and [Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken) were at Riverrun to settle a feud with Hoster's help, Catelyn and Petyr danced six dances. However, when he tried to kiss her, she pushed him away and laughed at him. Petyr, wounded by the rejection, became drunk and had to be brought to bed by Catelyn's uncle, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully). Catelyn's younger sister, Lysa, who had fallen in love with Petyr, snuck into Petyr's chambers and had sex with him that night. However, the drunken Petyr believed he was having sex with Catelyn, and he called Lysa "Cat" before falling asleep again.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rawoiafpetyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

When the date of Catelyn's wedding was announced in [282 AC](wiki:282_AC),(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-45) Petyr challenged Brandon to a duel for her hand.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) Catelyn refused to give Petyr her favor when he asked, giving it to Brandon instead.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-5)(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-1) She begged Brandon not to kill Petyr, and he agreed. Petyr was severely injured during the duel, and the final cut Brandon gave him was so brutal that Catelyn was convinced the younger boy would die from it.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) Petyr did not die, however, but was given a fortnight to recover at Riverrun before being sent away.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-1)(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-6) Catelyn did not go see him during this time, nor did she say her farewells to him when he left Riverrun.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-7) After the duel, Brandon left Riverrun as well, promising Catelyn to return soon for their wedding.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2)

When Brandon was on his way back to Riverrun(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-5) a few days before the wedding,(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) he learned about the disappearance of his sister, [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), and the involvement of [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone). Furious, Brandon rode to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), where he was arrested by Rhaegar's father, King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen). When Brandon's father, Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark), went to [King's Landing to](wiki:King's_Landing) champion Brandon in his [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle), both men were executed in the [throne room](wiki:Throne_room).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-6) Shortly thereafter, [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) began. After Brandon had died, Petyr sent Catelyn a letter, which she burned without reading it. By then, her father had promised her hand to Brandon's younger brother, [Eddard](wiki:Eddard_Stark), now the Lord of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

### Marriage to Eddard Stark

Catelyn married Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) after the rebels won the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells), without ever having seen him before their wedding day. [She married Eddard in the same ceremony](wiki:Weddings_of_Catelyn_and_Lysa_Tully) as her sister Lysa wed the elder Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn). Eddard and Jon played key roles in the rebellion, and a fortnight after the wedding both men rode off to war again. Catelyn remained at Riverrun, spending the first year of her marriage separated from her husband.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-5)(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) However, Catelyn had conceived a child during their wedding night, and she gave birth to [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark) in [283 AC](wiki:283_AC) at Riverrun, nine months after her wedding,(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-3)(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-2) with the assistance of Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-6)

After the war had ended, Catelyn and young Robb traveled to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), where she was displeased to discover that Eddard's [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), had already been set up in the castle.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-7) While Catelyn had always been aware that men fathered bastards, and had not been surprised to learn of Jon's existence, Jon arriving at Winterfell before Catelyn and Robb had hurt her feelings. Eddard's insistence on raising the boy at Winterfell and his refusal to identify the boy's mother caused frictions between them, leaving Catelyn jealous and angry.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rawoiafcatelyn_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) Catelyn concluded that Eddard must have loved Jon's mother deeply, and she heard rumors at Winterfell about [Ashara Dayne](wiki:Ashara_Dayne) from [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). However, when she finally had gathered enough courage to ask her husband about the Dornishwoman, Eddard demanded to learn where the rumor had come from. Ashara's name was never mentioned in Winterfell again, and Catelyn remembers the night as "the only time in all their years that Ned had ever frightened her".(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-8) Catelyn has never trusted Jon,(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) fearing that the bastard's children might contest her grandchildren's inheritance.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-9)(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-3)

On Eddard's part, the knowledge that Catelyn had actually been his brother Brandon's bride created a rift in their marriage. Despite these issues, however, Catelyn and Eddard grew to deeply love each other over the years.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-10) They had four more children—[Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) (b. [286 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark) (b. [289 AC](wiki:289_AC)), [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) (b. [290 AC](wiki:290_AC)), and [Rickon](wiki:Rickon_Stark) (b. [295 AC](wiki:295_AC))(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-49)—all delivered by Luwin.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-11)

### Recent History

As the Lady of Winterfell, Catelyn became familiar with the lords of the [north](wiki:North), and she once comforted a fellow [southron](wiki:Southron), Lady [Lynesse Hightower](wiki:Lynesse_Hightower), by telling her how she had once felt out of place in the north, but had come to love it all the same.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-4) Eddard had a [small sept](wiki:Lady_Catelyn's_sept) built at Winterfell for Catelyn, so she could pray to [her own gods](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-2)

Catelyn saw her nephew, Lysa's son [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn), when the boy was less than a year old.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4) Catelyn met [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) when he was less bald than he is now.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

## Appearances & Description

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

After the arrival of the [royal](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing) party at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), Catelyn receives a letter from her sister [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), stating that the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) had killed her husband, Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), the late [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). This puts the Starks on guard, as Eddard has just been offered the position of Hand by his friend, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). Catelyn pushes Ned to accept the offer along with a betrothal that would make [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) queen one day. Despite misgivings about going to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Eddard accepts.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-12)

When [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) is left in a coma having apparently fallen while climbing the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep), Catelyn stays by his side for eight days, barely sleeping or eating, ignoring her own affairs and those of Winterfell. On the eighth night, she is attacked by an [assassin](wiki:Catspaw) who had come for Bran, expecting the boy to be alone in the [sickroom](wiki:Sickroom). During the ensuing struggle, she grabs the blade of his dagger to keep him from cutting her throat or killing Bran, before Bran's [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf), [Summer](wiki:Summer), attacks and kills the man. Afterwards, Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) treats Catelyn's wounds; the blade had cut her fingers nearly to the bone, and the assassin had torn her scalp when he pulled out a handful of hair.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-3)

After recovering, Catelyn travels to King's Landing along with Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) to inform Eddard, sailing on the *[Storm Dancer](wiki:Storm_Dancer)*. Despite having traveled in secret they are met by guards upon their arrival, and are taken to a brothel owned by Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), Catelyn's childhood friend, who tells her that the [Valyrian steel dagger](wiki:Littlefinger's_blade) used in the attack belonged to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7)

On their way back to Winterfell, still traveling in secret, Catelyn and Rodrik encounter Tyrion at the [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads). He recognizes Catelyn, exposing her identity to those present, which includes [men-at-arms](wiki:Men-at-arms) in service to bannermen of [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully). Catelyn successfully appeals to them to detain Tyrion for conspiring to murder her son, and assist her in returning him to Winterfell to await justice.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-3)

Despite Catelyn announcing her intention to take him to Winterfell, their party instead makes its way to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) for trial. During the journey they come under attack by [clansmen](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans) on the [high road](wiki:High_road) in the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon). Tyrion successfully persuades Catelyn to allow her captives to take up arms, and goes on to save her during the skirmish.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5) At the Eyrie, Tyrion is thrown into a [sky cell](wiki:Sky_cell), but escapes execution by convincing [a sellsword](wiki:Bronn) to fight in a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-2) Meanwhile, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) orders Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) and other raiders to attack the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) in retribution for his son Tyrion's arrest.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

Her son Robb having marched south with his father's bannermen in response to [Eddard's arrest](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark) in King's Landing, Catelyn joins [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly)'s levies at [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) and meets the northern host in the field at [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) She is accompanied by her uncle, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), who had left [his service](wiki:Knight_of_the_Gate) to [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn), disgusted by Lysa's refusal to help against the Lannisters.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-3) Catelyn advises Robb against placing the hotheaded Lord [Greatjon Umber](wiki:Jon_Umber) in command of the infantry force sent to engage Tywin, and Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), a man of cold calculation, is appointed instead.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-1) Robb wants Catelyn to return to the north, but she stays with the army in order to see her dying father, Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-2)

When the army finds it necessary to cross the [Green Fork](wiki:Green_Fork), the delicate task of negotiating with [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), [Lord of the Crossing](wiki:Lord_of_the_Crossing), falls to Catelyn. She negotiates passage through the [Twins](wiki:Twins) and an alliance with [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) by promising, among other things, Robb's hand in marriage to one of Walder's daughters and [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark)'s hand to Walder's son [Elmar](wiki:Elmar_Frey).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

Catelyn is present for Robb's victory over Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) in the [Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood), where her guard is commanded by [Hallis Mollen](wiki:Hallis_Mollen).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-4)

After the news of Eddard's execution by order of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), Catelyn is filled with grief but maintains composure for the sake of her son. During the war council at Riverrun after the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), Catelyn argues for peace with the south so that Sansa and Arya might be more easily brought to safety. Robb and the assembled lords make clear their desire to continue the war, with the Greatjon declaring Robb the first [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) in three centuries.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4)

### A Clash of Kings

Catelyn advises against Robb's plan to send Eddard's ward, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), on a diplomatic mission to offer terms of an alliance with his father, Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), as she does not trust either.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2) With Robb south of the [Neck](wiki:Neck), [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy) ends up invading the north and Balon declares himself [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)

It is Catelyn's idea to attempt to ally with [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), who has a massive host, but she wishes to remain with Robb.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-3) When her son threatens to send the boisterous Greatjon as his envoy, Catelyn relents and goes herself, accepting the fact that her son is becoming a man. She travels south to [Bitterbridge](wiki:Bitterbridge) to meet King Renly, where she finds him hosting a [great melee](wiki:Melee_at_Bitterbridge).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1) When news arrives that Renly's brother [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) is laying [siege to Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)), the seat of [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon), Catelyn rides with Renly and part of his host.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-1)

Catelyn is present for the inconclusive parley between the two brothers outside [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End),(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-2) and she witnesses Renly's murder later that night by a mysterious [creature of shadow](wiki:Shadow_child). [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth), one of Renly's [Rainbow Guard](wiki:Rainbow_Guard), is the only other person present, so the pair flee in the knowledge that they would be blamed for the murder. They journey to Riverrun, and on the way Catelyn takes Brienne into her service.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

Catelyn disapproves of the admittance of [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) into Riverrun by her brother, Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-2) When [silent sisters](wiki:Silent_sisters) bring the bones of [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) to Riverrun, Catelyn tasks [Hallis Mollen](wiki:Hallis_Mollen) with returning them to Winterfell.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-3) She and Brienne remain within the castle during the [Battle of the Fords](wiki:Battle_of_the_Fords).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-8)

Upon hearing of her two youngest sons' supposed murders at the hand of Theon following his [capture of Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell), Catelyn meets with the imprisoned Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister). The Kingslayer admits to having thrown Bran from the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep) but insists he did not send the [catspaw](wiki:Catspaw) to attack the comatose boy.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-7)

Catelyn's father, Lord Hoster, finally dies at Riverrun, making Edmure the new lord.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) Cat advises Robb to make peace with the Lannisters, but the king declines.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-1)

While traveling to the [Twins](wiki:Twins) for Edmure's marriage with [Roslin Frey](wiki:Roslin_Frey), Catelyn objects to Robb's intention to name [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) his heir.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-5) Against her wishes, Robb intends to have Catelyn remain at [Seagard](wiki:Seagard) under the protection of Lord [Jason Mallister](wiki:Jason_Mallister) for the remainder of the war.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-6)

Catelyn attends Edmure's wedding at the Twins, sitting between Ser [Ryman Frey](wiki:Ryman_Frey) and Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) during the feast.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2) However, the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) and [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) betray the Starks in an act of treachery that becomes known as the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding). In a desperate attempt to save her son's life, Catelyn seizes Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey)'s lackwit grandson, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Frey_(son_of_Stevron)), and tries to trade his life for Robb's. When Robb is killed by Roose, Catelyn saws through Aegon's throat with a knife. Walder had planned to take Catelyn hostage,(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0) but the maddened woman claws her own face and Ser [Raymund Frey](wiki:Raymund_Frey) is allowed to slit her throat.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-3) The Freys dump her body naked into the Green Fork, a mockery of [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully)'s funeral customs,(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) after Catelyn has been dead for a day and a night.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0)

Three days after her death,(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) Catelyn's corpse is retrieved from the Green Fork by [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark)'s [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf), [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria_(direwolf)), who runs away when humans approach.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0) Catelyn is resurrected by Lord [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion), who gives his life for hers through the [last kiss](wiki:Last_kiss).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) Catelyn's wounds only partially heal, however, leaving her mutilated and with an impaired ability to speak. Consumed with the desire for vengeance over her family's betrayal and murder, she assumes command of Beric's [outlaw](wiki:Outlaw) band, the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners), and leads them in mercilessly pursuing and executing everyone she considers a Lannister collaborator, among them [Petyr](wiki:Petyr_Frey) and [Merrett Frey](wiki:Merrett_Frey) at [Oldstones](wiki:Oldstones).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-1)

[Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) brings Sansa to the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) after [the death](wiki:Purple_Wedding) of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), and he marries Catelyn's widowed sister [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) at the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) To keep Sansa's identity secret, she is claimed to be Alayne Stone, Petyr's [bastard](wiki:Bastard) daughter. Petyr kisses Sansa at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), which Lysa interprets as an advance by her niece toward her new husband. When Lysa threatens Sansa, Petyr tells his wife he has only loved one woman, Catelyn, and pushes Lysa through the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door) to her death.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-2)

### A Feast for Crows

A cloaked and hooded woman is spoken of in the vicinity of [Fairmarket](wiki:Fairmarket)(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6) and [Hag's Mire](wiki:Hag's_Mire),(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) but she vanished into the [Neck](wiki:Neck) when pursued by [Black Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Ryman)).(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-1) There are also tales of a woman called Stoneheart leading a band of outlaws different from Beric's brotherhood. According to one story, she was hanged by Freys but revived by Beric, and now the two lovers cannot die.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) The mysterious woman is also known as the Silent Sister, Mother Merciless, and the Hangwoman.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)

After the Red Wedding, Ser [Ryman Frey](wiki:Ryman_Frey) gives Robb's [crown](wiki:Crown) to [a prostitute](wiki:Queen_o'_whores). At the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), Jaime commands Ryman to leave the camp at once, allowing Ryman take his whore with him but not the crown.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1) Ryman's party is ambushed by outlaws while returning to the [Twins](wiki:Twins),(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-3) and Stoneheart ends up with the crown.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-4)

The brotherhood comes to the aid of [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_Tarth) after she defends the [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads) from former members of the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions), and they bring Brienne, Ser [Hyle Hunt](wiki:Hyle_Hunt), and [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne) to a hideout in a cave. Brienne insists she is searching for Sansa at the behest of [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister). Thoros reveals to Brienne that Beric's brotherhood has been broken.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-8)

Stoneheart returns to the cave from Fairmarket. Using a [young northman](wiki:Harwin) as her interpreter, Stoneheart says that Brienne has turned traitor, as she carries [Oathkeeper](wiki:Oathkeeper), a Lannister sword forged from House Stark's [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) greatsword, [Ice](wiki:Ice). Brienne swears she is still faithful but Stoneheart insists she must prove it by killing Jaime. Brienne refuses, stating that Jaime has changed. Stoneheart says she must choose, either kill Jaime or be hanged. Brienne refuses to choose. Just before she, Hyle, and Podrick are to be executed, however, Brienne shouts out a word.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-9)

### A Dance with Dragons

After [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) returns to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) for the [wedding of Ramsay Bolton and Arya Stark](wiki:Wedding_of_Ramsay_Bolton_and_Arya_Stark),(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0) Catelyn's [ruined sept](wiki:Lady_Catelyn's_sept) is among the places he visits.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0) Within the [castle crypts](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell), Lady [Barbrey Dustin](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin) recollects to Theon that her one-time lover, [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark), had not wanted to wed Catelyn. Barbrey had also hoped to wed [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) after Brandon died, but Ned instead [married Catelyn](wiki:Weddings_of_Catelyn_and_Lysa_Tully) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). Barbrey intends to prevent [Hallis Mollen](wiki:Hallis_Mollen) from returning Ned's bones to Winterfell.(wiki:Catelyn_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0)
