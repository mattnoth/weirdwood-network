## Identity

Rhaenys Targaryen (daughter of Rhaegar) (280–283 AC) — child of Rhaegar Targaryen and Elia Martell; sworn to House Targaryen; Princess. One of 3 characters named Rhaenys Targaryen.

## Origins

In [280 AC](wiki:280_AC), Rhaenys was born on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rawoiafrhaenys_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) After her birth, her [mother](wiki:Elia_Martell) was bedridden for half a year.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Rhaenys was presented at court by her father, Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen). Whilst her grandmother, Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen), embraced her warmly, her grandfather, King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), refused to touch or hold her, claiming she "smelled [Dornish](wiki:Dornish)".(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Rhaenys had a small black [kitten](wiki:Kitten) that she named [Balerion](wiki:Balerion_(cat)), after [Balerion the Black Dread](wiki:Balerion), the [dragon](wiki:Dragon) of [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) has been told that she held Rhaenys once, when she was too young to remember.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-9)

During [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Elia, Rhaenys, and her infant brother, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), were brought to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) on orders of King Aerys II Targaryen. In preparation for the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), Aerys used them as [hostages](wiki:Hostage), gracelessly reminding Rhaenys's great-uncle, Prince [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), that the three were in Aerys's hands. Lewyn took command of ten thousand [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen), but died in the battle, as did Rhaenys's father, Rhaegar. Aerys became convinced that the Dornishmen had betrayed him, and refused to allow Rhaenys and her family to flee to Dragonstone alongside Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) and Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen). Rhaenys and her mother and brother were kept at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) as hostages to ensure continued Dornish loyalty.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

During the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing), a fortnight after the royal forces had left for the [Trident](wiki:Trident), Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) of the Kingsguard slew King Aerys in the [throne room](wiki:Throne_room) of the Red Keep.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Though Rhaegar had left the safety of his family to Jaime, the only Kingsguard member left in the city, Jaime did not believe that his father [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), whose troops were attacking King's Landing, would harm Elia or her children.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

However, Tywin commanded the deaths of Rhaenys and Aegon to prove his loyalty to [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) While Jaime was killing Aerys, Tywin's men Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) and Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch) were scaling the walls of [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast).(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Rhaenys hid under her father's bed, a floor above the nursery where her mother and brother were located.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) While Gregor Clegane slew Aegon and Elia, Rhaenys was discovered and dragged out by Amory Lorch, who proceeded to stab her with "half a hundred" thrusts.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) recalls hearing of other [men-at-arms](wiki:Men-at-arms) present during the murder, but does not know their names.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Amory brought Rhaenys's body to Tywin afterwards.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

Tywin claims to have been shocked at the brutality of the deaths of Elia and her children. In Rhaenys's case, he believed Lorch, who tried to justify his savagery by claiming the girl kicked him and would not stop screaming, could simply have offered a few sweet words to calm her, and a pillow to suffocate her.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

After Rhaenys and Aegon's deaths, Tywin Lannister presented their bodies to Robert, laying them beneath the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) wrapped in crimson [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) cloaks, to better conceal the blood.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Rhaenys's body was distinctly recognizable, despite its condition.(wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwd72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
