## Origins

### Youth

Born in [122 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Viserys was the second born son of his father, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), and the fifth born son of his mother, Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen). As per Targaryen tradition, a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) was placed in his cradle.(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Viserys was [named after](wiki:Named_after) his grandfather, King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Viserys had four older brothers; three half-brothers from his mother's first marriage, [Jacaerys](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon), [Lucerys](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon) and [Joffrey Velaryon](wiki:Joffrey_Velaryon), and one full-blood brother, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). He also had two older half-sisers, [Baela](wiki:Baela_Targaryen) and [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)), from his father's second marriage.(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Viserys lived with his mother, father, brothers, and sisters on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), his mother's seat as the [Princess of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone). When his grandfather died in early [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), Viserys and his family were thus away from the capital, since Rhaenyra was pregnant for the sixth time. This pregnancy led to the birth of Viserys's stillborn younger sister, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)).(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

The death of his grandfather sparked a war of succession between Viserys's mother, Rhaenyra, and his half-uncle, Prince Aegon, who was crowned King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), despite Rhaenyra having been the heir to the throne.(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)

### The Dance of the Dragons

In late [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), at the behest of their half-brother Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon), Viserys and his older brother [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) were sent to [Essos](wiki:Essos) aboard the *[Gay Abandon](wiki:Gay_Abandon)* to be [fostered](wiki:Fosterage) by a [prince](wiki:Prince_of_Pentos) of [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) until their mother Rhaenyra secured the Iron Throne. However, the fleet of the [Kingdom of the Three Daughters](wiki:Kingdom_of_the_Three_Daughters), who were allied with the [greens](wiki:Greens) and [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), captured the *Gay Abandon* during the voyage. Aegon flew away atop his dragon, [Stormcloud](wiki:Stormcloud), but Viserys had only a dragon egg. Remaining behind, the clever Viserys hid his egg and disguised himself as a common ship's boy, but his identity was revealed by one of the crew. A [Tyroshi](wiki:Tyrosh) captain brought Viserys to [Sharako Lohar](wiki:Sharako_Lohar), the Triarchy's admiral. Viserys's fate through the remainder of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) was unknown to his family, who believed he had died in [the battle](wiki:Battle_in_the_Gullet).(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Viserys ended up in [Lys](wiki:Lys) at the [manse](wiki:Manse) of the [magister](wiki:Magister) [Bambarro Bazanne](wiki:Bambarro_Bazanne) in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC). After the magister died in debt, his manse and property were seized by creditors. All of Bazanne's valuables, including the captive Viserys, passed into the custody of the wealthy [Lysandro Rogare](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare) of the [Rogare family](wiki:Rogare_family), whose [bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank) at the time was as powerful as the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos). Initially, Lysandro considered putting him to work as a courtesan on account of his striking beauty, but quickly(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-13) changed his plans when the prince revealed his identity. Viserys was wed to the family's youngest daughter, [Larra](wiki:Larra_Rogare), despite her being seven years his senior.(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

### Reign of Aegon III

During the early reign of Viserys's older brother, [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), it became known in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) that Viserys had survived. He was recovered by [Oakenfist](wiki:Oakenfist) in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC), and after a huge ransom was paid to the merchant princes of Lys, Viserys returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Aegon had never forgiven himself for abandoning his brother during the [Battle in the Gullet](wiki:Battle_in_the_Gullet), and so Viserys's return was the only true joy he had in his reign. For the rest of Aegon's life, Viserys was the only person Aegon ever fully trusted.(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

Viserys came to King's Landing with his new wife Larra Rogare and several of her kin. During the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring), the ambitious Rogare family became embroiled in the conflicts of the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III). Although the Rogares at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) were apparently guilty of some crimes, their rivals in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) blamed them for many more. In [135 AC](wiki:135_AC), Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan) was tortured in an attempt to find evidence against the Rogares, and Ser [Marston Waters](wiki:Marston_Waters), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), sent men to capture Larra after arresting her brothers. Aegon and Viserys refused to surrender Larra, however, and defiantly protected her in [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) in what would later become known as the [secret siege](wiki:Secret_siege). Eventually the case against the Rogares collapsed.(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

Viserys and Larra had three children: [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) (born [135 AC](wiki:135_AC)), [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)) (born [136 AC](wiki:136_AC)), and [Naerys](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen) (born [138 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)). Larra never felt happy at court, and in [139 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), she left Viserys and their three children and returned to Lys, an experience that made Viserys stern. Viserys had Aegon and Naerys wed to one another in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), with Aegon III's blessing, while Aemon became a knight of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### Hand of the King

Eventually,(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-16) Viserys served as Hand during the last years of Aegon's reign. Viserys remained in the office during the reigns of both of his brother's sons, [Daeron I](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen) and [Baelor I](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen). Viserys was said to be instrumental in keeping the realm intact while Aegon brooded, Daeron warred, and Baelor prayed. It was rumored that to spare the realm embarrassment, Viserys used his position as Hand to order the [poisoning](wiki:Poison) of [a stonemason](wiki:High_Septon_(stonemason)) whom Baelor had appointed as [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon).(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

## Appearances & Description

Viserys had silver-gold hair.(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) In his youth, Viserys was said to be striking, though he was not as handsome as his older brother, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen).(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) According to a semi-canon source, Viserys II was clean-shaven with long hair. He had a prominent nose, bushy eyebrows and a shrewd, calculating look to him. He wore the simple crown of [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen).(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-5)

Viserys was frailer than his brother Aegon in his youth, but was more mature than Aegon and his [Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) half-brothers.(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) As a boy, Viserys was said to be clever and gallant.(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_the_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) He was well-educated by tutors during his captivity, and excelled at the art of swordsmanship under his [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms).(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Unlike Aegon, Viserys had the gift of charm, but later became stern.(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Due to his intelligence, he was later in life compared to his great-great-grandfather, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), in terms of political capability.(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) According to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), Viserys had been far better suited to rule the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) than the nephews he served loyally as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
