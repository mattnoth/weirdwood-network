## Identity

Deria Martell — Reigning Princess of Dorne; she denounced the Vulture King but was rumored to aid him.

## Origins

### The First Dornish War

Princess Deria was the granddaughter of Princess [Meria Martell](wiki:Meria_Martell), the Yellow Toad of Dorne. Upon Meria's death in [13 AC](wiki:13_AC), Deria's father, Prince [Nymor](wiki:Nymor_Martell), succeeded to the rule of Dorne. The [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War) had been ongoing since [4 AC](wiki:4_AC), and Nymor wanted peace. He sent Deria at the head of a delegation to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), with the skull of the dead [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes) as a peace offer. Many at court took this badly, including Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) and Lord [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon). Lord [Oakheart](wiki:House_Oakheart) suggested that Deria should be sent to "the meanest of [brothels](wiki:Prostitution) to service any man who would have her".(wiki:Deria_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf796_2-1)(wiki:Deria_Martell.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

Deria assured King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) that [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) wanted peace, but would not swear fealty to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). King Aegon's counselors argued against this, claiming Aegon would look weak if he agreed. In addition, they believed the lords of the [Reach](wiki:Reach) and [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands) would feel offended by such a peace.(wiki:Deria_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf796_2-2)(wiki:Deria_Martell.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

King Aegon was ready to refuse the offer when Princess Deria presented him with a letter from [her father](wiki:Nymor_Martell). Aegon read the letter on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), allegedly clenching the bladed seat so hard his hand started to bleed. Once he was done, he burned the letter and immediately flew to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) upon [Balerion](wiki:Balerion). When he returned the next morning, he agreed to the peace and signed a treaty.(wiki:Deria_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf796_2-3)(wiki:Deria_Martell.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

### The Vulture Hunt

At some point in the following decade, Prince Nymor passed away and Deria succeeded him as the [Princess of Dorne](wiki:Princess_of_Dorne). In [23 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King Aegon and his eldest son Prince [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) flew to [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) on their [dragons](wiki:Dragon), [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) and [Quicksilver](wiki:Quicksilver), to attend a "feast of friendship" hosted by Princess Deria to mark the ten year anniversary of the peace accords.(wiki:Deria_Martell.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), Aenys succeeded his father as king, and was almost immediately opposed by four rebels. The largest and most threatening of these rebels was the so-called "[Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Aenys_I))" in the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains). Princess Deria publicly issued denouncements against the Vulture King from [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), but many believed that she supported him in secret.(wiki:Deria_Martell.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Deria_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf42.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
