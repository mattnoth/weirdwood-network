## Origins

Edwell was named [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) by [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) in [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), following the execution of the previous Hand, Lord [Lucas Harroway](wiki:Lucas_Harroway).(wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) King Maegor left Edwell to rule [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) along with Maegor's wife, Queen [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower), when Maegor himself left to embark on a new campaign against the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) in [45 AC](wiki:45_AC).(wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

In [47 AC](wiki:47_AC) Edwell suggested to Maegor that the heirless king should take more than one new wife so as to increase his chances of producing an heir. Edwell suggested his two [maiden](wiki:Maiden) daughters, [Prunella](wiki:Prunella_Celtigar) and [Prudence](wiki:Prudence_Celtigar), who were of twelve and thirteen years. Although Maegor eventually took Edwell's advice, he did not choose Edwell's daughters. Lord Celtigar resigned the office of Hand in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), when even previously loyal [lords](wiki:Lord) of the realm were now turning against the king. He safely returned to [Claw Isle](wiki:Claw_Isle).(wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Upon the ascension of the young [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), Lord Celtigar was pardoned and named [lord treasurer](wiki:Lord_treasurer) and [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) by Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon), the new Hand.(wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The wars fought by Maegor had near bankrupted the crown. Lord Celtigar was unwilling to risk offending the high lords, so he instead enforced a number of new taxes upon the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), such as tripling the port fees of King's Landing, [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), and [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown). Certain goods were taxed both coming in and going out of the city, and new levies were asked of innkeepers and builders. These measures made Edwell deeply despised by the populace and failed to generate sufficient revenue. Building slowed to a halt, the inns emptied, and trade declined significantly in the capital, while the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) and [Hightowers](wiki:House_Hightower) ignored Lord Celtigar's demand to raise their port fees.(wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Upon King Jaehaerys's sixteenth name day, he returned to King's Landing and kindly dismissed Lord Edwell from his office, thanking him for his services. Three days later the king undid most of the taxes Edwell had placed on the smallfolk, much to the populace's delight.(wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

During the winter of [59 AC](wiki:59_AC) when the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) spread to the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands), Lord Edwell was one of the first high lords to die from the disease.(wiki:Edwell_Celtigar.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
