## Origins

### Early Life

According to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin), Hoster served in the [War of the Ninepenny Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings), during which time he met and befriended Lord [Baelish](wiki:Baelish_(lord)). Because of this friendship, Hoster later agreed to foster Lord Baelish’s son, [Petyr](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-11)

### Lord of Riverrun

Following the death of his [father](wiki:Lord_Tully_(father_of_Hoster)), Hoster inherited the rule to [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun),(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) at some point in or after [264 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-12) Following the [Tully](wiki:House_Tully) funeral customs, Hoster's father was sent down the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork) in a boat, which Hoster was to set aflame by firing a fire arrow. While Hoster missed his first shot, his second arrow found sail.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3)

At some point, Hoster attempted to betroth his younger brother [Brynden](wiki:Brynden_Tully) to [Bethany Redwyne](wiki:Bethany_Redwyne). Brynden, however, refused, which began a decades-long feud between the two brothers.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Hoster received other offers for Brynden over the years, including one from Lord [Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken) and one from Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), but Brynden refused them all.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

Hoster married Lady [Minisa Whent](wiki:Minisa_Tully), who gave birth to four sons and two daughters. However, only three children (two daughters and a son) survived infancy. Following the death of his two eldest sons, Hoster came to regard his eldest daughter, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), as his heir, until another son was born to him. Hoster and Minisa had another daughter, [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), who was followed by a son, [Edmure](wiki:Edmure_Tully). Minisa became pregnant a sixth time, but died in childbirth, giving birth to another son who died with her.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Hoster loved his wife and was deeply saddened by her death,(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) still missing her decades later.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

According to Catelyn, she was Hoster's favourite child.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Lord Tully taught Catelyn how to ride in the [godswood of Riverrun](wiki:Godswood_of_Riverrun),(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) and she read to him in his [solar](wiki:Solar) when his eyes tired.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) A restless man in his prime, Hoster was often away from home, frequently staying at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) His daughters and ward sometimes accompanied him.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) When they did not, Hoster always told Catelyn to wait for him at Riverrun when he left on one of his journeys, which she did.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

When Catelyn was eight years old, Hoster and Brynden had one of their larger quarrels, during which Hoster called his brother "the black goat of the Tully flock". Brynden, however, pointed out that the sigil of [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) was a trout, making him a black fish instead of a black goat. Brynden the Blackfish took a black trout for his sigil since that day.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

### Daughters' Betrothals

Lord Hoster wished to make advantageous marriages for his children. He betrothed his eldest daughter, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) to [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark), the heir to Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), when she was only twelve years old.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) He began negotiations with Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), aiming to betroth his younger daughter [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) to [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), the heir to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) When Jaime spent a fortnight at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) in his early teens, Hoster took care to seat him next to Lysa with every meal.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) By [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), Tywin had invited Hoster to travel to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), where Tywin resided as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), to discuss the [dower](wiki:Dowry).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) However, King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) named Jaime to his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) before the agreements were finalized, thereby preventing the betrothal from being made.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2)(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) When Tywin suggested his younger son, the deformed dwarf [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), in Jaime's stead, Hoster declined, informing Tywin that he wanted a "whole man" for his daughter.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

Meanwhile, Lysa had fallen in love with Hoster's ward, [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), over the years,(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) while Petyr had fallen in love with Lysa's older sister, Catelyn,(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) who did not return his feelings.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) Lysa had already lost her virginity to Petyr, though Petyr had been so drunken he was convinced he had slept with Catelyn.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) When, Catelyn's betrothed, Brandon, visited [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) in [282 AC](wiki:282_AC),(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-26) Petyr challenged him for Catelyn's hand(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) following the announcement of the wedding date.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_petyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Brandon won the duel, and Petyr was grievously wounded.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) In the fortnight that Petyr was allowed to remain at Riverrun to recover some of his strength, he and Lysa slept together again, this time conceiving a child.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2)

Once Petyr was strong enough, Hoster sent him back to the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers) in a litter.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1) When Lysa discovered her pregnancy, she informed her father, hoping that Hoster would allow her to marry the father of her child. Hoster, however, considered his former ward to be too low of birth,(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) and with the help of [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Kym](wiki:Kym) gave his daughter [moon tea](wiki:Moon_tea) without informing her what the drink was, and so aborted her child.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3)(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_petyr_baelish.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) Lysa nearly died from the experience.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_lysa_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

### Robert's Rebellion

In [282 AC](wiki:282_AC), [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) began after [Catelyn Tully's](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) betrothed, [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark), and his father, Lord [Rickard](wiki:Rickard_Stark), were executed by King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), and the king demanded the heads of Lords [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), who had been staying at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) with Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn). While unwilling to chose a side in the rebellion at first,(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_lysa_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) Hoster eventually joined the rebellion on the side of the rebels. Hoster arranged Catelyn's betrothal to Eddard, Brandon's younger brother.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2)

In [283 AC](wiki:283_AC), Hoster fought in the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells) at [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept), where he was wounded by Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), Aerys's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) The death of Ser [Denys Arryn](wiki:Denys_Arryn), the heir of Lord Arryn, in the battle gave Hoster a new opportunity. In exchange for Hoster's support, the ageing and heirless Jon Arryn agreed to marry Hoster's younger daughter, [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), who had proven to be fertile following her aborted pregnancy with [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Lysa's betrothal was hastily arranged, and Hoster saw both his daughters [marry in the same ceremony](wiki:Weddings_of_Catelyn_and_Lysa_Tully) at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

During the wedding feast, Hoster's younger brother, Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully), informed Hoster that he had taken service with Lord Arryn and would leave Riverrun for the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) Hoster saw Brynden's decision as a personal rejection.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_brynden_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) He disowned his brother,(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and, angered by Brynden's departure, would not speak his brother's name anymore.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)

While Lords Arryn and Stark returned to the campaign, Hoster remained mostly at Riverrun during the remainder of the war.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Several of his bannermen, such as the [Darrys](wiki:House_Darry), [Goodbrooks](wiki:House_Goodbrook), [Mootons](wiki:House_Mooton), and [Rygers](wiki:House_Ryger),(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0) remained loyal to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), and Hoster put at least one village of House Goodbrook to the sword.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-1)

Because the levies of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey), another of Hoster's bannermen, arrived after the decisive [battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), Hoster has since referred to Walder as the "Late Lord Frey".(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) When Walder married his seventh wife, [Annara Farring](wiki:Annara_Farring), within a few years after the war had ended, the [Lord of the Crossing](wiki:Lord_of_the_Crossing) resented that his liege lord Hoster did not attend the wedding.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

### Recent History

Just as he once sought to make advantageous marriages for his daughters, Hoster also began seeking an advantageous marriage for his son and heir, [Edmure](wiki:Edmure_Tully). Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) offered a daughter who was only a few years older than Edmure, but Hoster declined.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1) Hoster once invited Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell), the heiress to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), to come to [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) to meet Edmure. However, [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), Arianne's father and the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), declined the offer.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

In [296 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Hoster fell ill(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) and eventually became bedridden.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Hoster Tully](wiki:Category:Images_of_Hoster_Tully)*

In his youth Hoster was tall and broad, strong, with blue eyes and brown hair.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) As he grew older, he became portly, and his hair and beard became streaked with grey.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Stricken by [illness](wiki:Illness) since [296 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Hoster has lost weight and now appears to have shrunk. His hair and beard have gone white.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Hoster has always been a proud man,(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) and a follower of the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) When he was younger, he could never stay still, and was often traveling somewhere.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Now aged, he enjoys watching the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork) and the [Tumblestone](wiki:Tumblestone) from his [solar](wiki:Solar) in Riverrun's keep.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) Hoster's daughter, [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), does not believe he would be willing to father [bastards](wiki:Bastardy).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Hoster's armor is [silver](wiki:Silver) plate-and-mail, while his cloak and surcoat are blue and red. The crest atop his greathelm is a [trout](wiki:Trout) scaled in silver and [bronze](wiki:Bronze). His shield is massive [oak](wiki:Oak)-and-[iron](wiki:Iron),(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and he also owns a hunting horn.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) When he was younger, Hoster often rode a brown gelding.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Hoster keeps the severity of his illness a secret,(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) with his son Ser [Edmure](wiki:Edmure_Tully) taking many of his duties, including the defense of [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-2) Hoster declines to attend [the wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Walder_Frey_and_Joyeuse_Erenford) of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey) to [Joyeuse Erenford](wiki:Joyeuse_Erenford).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-2)

When marauders under Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) attack the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) in retribution for [Catelyn's](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) capture of [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), Edmure wants to retaliate. Hoster, however, insists that the matter be brought before the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) by Ser [Marq Piper](wiki:Marq_Piper) and Ser [Raymun Darry](wiki:Raymun_Darry) so that Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) cannot pretend that a counterattack was unprovoked.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

Following the death of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Hoster is included in the list of lords required to swear allegiance to the new king, [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

Hoster is bedridden when Riverrun is besieged by the army of Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) and Edmure is [captured beneath its walls](wiki:Battle_under_the_walls_of_Riverrun).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) Hoster's brother, Ser [Brynden](wiki:Brynden_Tully), and grandson, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), break the siege during the [battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) Hoster’s daughter Catelyn is with them, and visits her father, who is still lucid.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8) Hoster does not attend the war council in which Robb is declared [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) by [northmen](wiki:North) and [rivermen](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9)

### A Clash of Kings

Hoster's [smith](wiki:Smith) creates a new crown for Robb in emulation of the older [Kings in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

After so many years, Hoster reconciles with his brother, Brynden.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) Hoster's [solar](wiki:Solar) is used as his [sickroom](wiki:Sickroom). His mind continues to slip more and more, however, and he constantly loses track of events and time happening all around him. Lord Tully confuses Catelyn for his other daughter, [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), and Maester [Vyman](wiki:Vyman) provides him with [milk of the poppy](wiki:Milk_of_the_poppy)(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) and [dreamwine](wiki:Dreamwine).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)

With Hoster incapacitated, Edmure defends the [Red Fork](wiki:Red_Fork) against Tywin and Gregor in the [battle of the Fords](wiki:Battle_of_the_Fords).(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Racok45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)

### A Storm of Swords

After freeing Jaime Lannister, Catelyn is confined to Hoster's chambers.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) During his last weeks, Hoster is only able to mutter a few feverish words and no longer recognizes his children. His son Edmure has assumed control of Riverrun in his stead. Hoster often speaks in his delirium of Lysa and her aborted pregnancy, initially bewildering Catelyn with his references to "[Tansy](wiki:Tansy_(plant))" and "the blood". Though Catelyn and Edmure write to Lysa asking her to visit her father before his death, she never responds to the ravens.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) Hoster dies in [299 AC](wiki:299_AC) and is succeeded by Edmure.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-4)

At his funeral Hoster's body is put in a boat and set down the Red Fork by [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), [King of the Trident](wiki:King_of_the_Trident), and six of his bannermen: Lord [Jonos Bracken](wiki:Jonos_Bracken), Lord [Tytos Blackwood](wiki:Tytos_Blackwood), Lord [Karyl Vance](wiki:Karyl_Vance), Lord [Jason Mallister](wiki:Jason_Mallister), Ser [Marq Piper](wiki:Marq_Piper), and Lord Walder Frey's son [Lothar](wiki:Lothar_Frey), who replaces Hoster's friend, Ser [Desmond Grell](wiki:Desmond_Grell), at the last minute upon Robb's request. Hoster's armor, shield, hunting horn, and a wooden sword are added to the boat. As part of the funeral custom a flaming arrow is fired to light the sails as the boat travels downriver. Edmure fires the arrows, but misses three times in his grief. Brynden, seeing this, takes the bow from his nephew's hands and succeeds in lighting the sail before the boat disappears downriver.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-5)

Edmure, the new Lord of Riverrun, is affected by his father's death and becomes surly, but Lothar warmly recalls stories about Hoster.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-6) [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) later betrays their liege in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), however, taking Edmure captive and killing Robb and Catelyn.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Following the resolution of the [siege of Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun), Jaime stays in Hoster's solar and drinks his red [wine](wiki:Wine) before continuing on to [Raventree Hall](wiki:Siege_of_Raventree), angering Edmure.(wiki:Hoster_Tully.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)
