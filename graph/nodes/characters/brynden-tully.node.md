## Identity

Ser Brynden Tully, known as the Blackfish, is a knight from House Tully. He is the younger brother of Lord Hoster Tully and uncle of Catelyn Stark, Lysa Arryn, and Edmure Tully (wiki:Brynden_Tully). The nickname "Blackfish" arose when Hoster called him the "black goat of the Tully flock" during an argument about marriage, and Brynden corrected it to "black fish" since their sigil is a trout (wiki:Brynden_Tully).

## Origins

Born 243–245 AC at Riverrun in the riverlands (track_b: Born). Son of Lord Tully (track_b: Father), younger brother of Hoster by five years (wiki:Brynden_Tully).

## Appearances & Description

Tall and lean, with a hoarse smoky voice. Clean-shaven with a craggy, windburnt face, lined and weathered features. Once-auburn hair gone to grey, bushy eyebrows, and deep blue laughing eyes (wiki:Brynden_Tully). Wears a cloak fastened by a clasp of obsidian and gold in the shape of a black fish. His armor includes grey heavy plate when serving as Knight of the Gate, and grey ringmail under blackened steel with a black trout crest when commanding outriders (wiki:Brynden_Tully).

## Narrative Arc

Brynden squired for Lord Darry alongside Norbert Vance and was knighted before fighting with distinction in the War of the Ninepenny Kings in 260 AC (wiki:Brynden_Tully). He refused Hoster's attempts to betroth him, which strained their relationship for decades. After Robert's Rebellion, in which he fought for the rebels, Brynden served as Knight of the Gate for House Arryn in the Vale (wiki:Brynden_Tully).

When the War of the Five Kings begins, Brynden leaves Lysa's service and joins Catelyn at Moat Cailin (agot-catelyn-08). He serves as Robb Stark's chief scout and commander of outriders, playing a key role in the Battle of the Camps that lifts the siege of Riverrun (wiki:Brynden_Tully). Named Warden of the Southern Marches by Robb, he later becomes castellan of Riverrun when Edmure departs for his wedding at the Twins (wiki:Brynden_Tully). After the Red Wedding, Brynden holds Riverrun against the Frey-Lannister siege, refusing to yield. He eventually escapes by swimming under the Water Gate when Edmure is forced to surrender the castle, and his whereabouts remain unknown (wiki:Brynden_Tully).
