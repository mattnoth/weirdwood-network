## Identity

Bronn is a low-born sellsword of great skill and cunning who rises through service to Tyrion Lannister to become a knight and eventually Lord Stokeworth (wiki:Bronn).

## Appearances & Description

Bronn is tall, thin and hard as a bone, with black hair falling over black eyes and a stubble of a beard. He has a wolfish smile and is as quick as a cat. He has a black sense of humor and a pragmatic, thoroughly amoral philosophy. He is illiterate. He wears black oiled ringmail over boiled leather, a round steel halfhelm with a noseguard, and a mail coif. He wields a longsword and a long dirk, can fight with a blade in each hand, and prefers not to use a shield (wiki:Bronn). Pass 1 confirms: bone thin, bone hard, black eyes, black hair (agot-tyrion-04).

## Narrative Arc

Bronn first appears as a sellsword at the inn at the crossroads, answering Catelyn Stark's call to arrest Tyrion. He fights mountain clansmen on the high road and grows close to Tyrion (wiki:Bronn, cite_ref-Ragot28). At the Eyrie, Bronn volunteers to champion Tyrion in trial by combat and kills Ser Vardis Egen, losing a tooth in the duel (wiki:Bronn, cite_ref-Ragot38). He enters Tyrion's permanent service and accompanies him through the Mountains of the Moon, where Tyrion recruits the hill clans (agot-tyrion-06). At Tywin's camp, Bronn finds Shae for Tyrion and they fight in the battle on the Green Fork (wiki:Bronn, cite_ref-Ragot56).

In King's Landing during ACOK, Bronn recruits nearly eight hundred sellswords and becomes captain of Tyrion's guards. He manages the chain across the Blackwater during the battle and receives knighthood afterward, taking the sigil of a flaming green chain on smoke-grey (wiki:Bronn, cite_ref-Racok49). In ASOS, Tywin brings his own guards and Kevan dismisses Bronn's sellswords. Bronn investigates Ser Mandon Moore's assassination attempt on Tyrion and kills the singer Symon Silver Tongue at Tyrion's order. When Tyrion is arrested for Joffrey's murder, Cersei arranges Bronn's marriage to Lollys Stokeworth. Bronn refuses to champion Tyrion a second time against Gregor Clegane (wiki:Bronn, cite_ref-Rasos70).

In AFFC, Bronn names Lollys's bastard son "Tyrion Tanner." He kills Ser Balman Byrch in single combat after Balman challenges him at Cersei's instigation, then claims the title Lord Stokeworth (wiki:Bronn, cite_ref-Raffc36).
