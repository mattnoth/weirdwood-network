## Appearances & Description

Bronn is a tall man,(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) thin and hard as a [bone](wiki:Bone).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) He has black hair which falls over(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) his black eyes, and he has a stubble of a beard.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Bronn has a wolfish smile,(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and is as quick as a cat.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

The [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) has a black sense of humor,(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and a pragmatic, thoroughly amoral philosophy.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) He is illiterate.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Bronn wears black oiled ringmail over boiled leather, a round [steel](wiki:Steel) halfhelm with a noseguard, and a mail coif.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Bronn wields a longsword, which he often hones, and a long dirk.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) He can fight with a blade in each hand(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and prefers not to use a shield.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Bronn and [Chiggen](wiki:Chiggen) are [sellswords](wiki:Sellswords) present when Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) and Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) arrive at the [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads). When Catelyn calls for loyal men to help her arrest [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) for the attempted murder of her son, [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark),(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) the sellswords heed her call and help escort Tyrion along the [high road](wiki:High_road) to the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) Tyrion notes that Bronn and Chiggen only came to Catelyn's aid because they are sellswords and are hoping for a reward.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

When the party comes under attack by [clans](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans) of the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon), Bronn cuts down several of the wildlings on horseback and then fights with two swords after being unhorsed.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) He claims [Jyck](wiki:Jyck)'s fine boots after the [Lannister guard](wiki:House_Lannister_guards) dies.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5) Bronn becomes closer to Tyrion,(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6) to Catelyn's unease.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) When they are attacked again the next night, Chiggen is mortally wounded and Bronn discreetly finishes him off, claiming to Catelyn that Chiggen died from his wounds.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-7)

During Tyrion's trial at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), Bronn volunteers to champion him in [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat),(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) in expectation of Lannister gold.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) Bronn kills Ser [Vardis Egen](wiki:Vardis_Egen), the champion of Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), in the [godswood of the Eyrie](wiki:Godswood_of_the_Eyrie), thereby saving Tyrion's life while losing a tooth in the duel.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

While taking the high road back to the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), Bronn enters into Tyrion's service as a hired sword and Tyrion reveals he was briefly married to [Tysha](wiki:Tysha).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4) They are approached by clansmen, but Tyrion enlists them to his cause.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5)

After they join the host of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), Bronn finds [Shae](wiki:Shae) for Tyrion amongst the camp followers, taking her from a [knight](wiki:Knight).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) He and Tyrion take part in the [battle on the Green Fork](wiki:Battle_on_the_Green_Fork).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) When Tywin sends his son to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to act as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Bronn accompanies Tyrion.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### A Clash of Kings

In [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Bronn recruits a force of sellswords, eventually near eight hundred,(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) to protect [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), alongside the [City Watch](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) and Tyrion's clansmen.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) A freshly-shaven Bronn becomes the [captain](wiki:Captain_of_the_guards) of [Tyrion's guards](wiki:House_Lannister_guards), wearing the crimson cloak of the Lannisters and the black breastplate of the City Watch.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Bronn scoffs at the [wight](wiki:Wight) hand brought to the capital by Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne), which has decomposed.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Tyrion has Bronn find a thief, a poisoner, a mummer, and a murderer for his [plot to free Jaime Lannister](wiki:Plot_to_free_Jaime_Lannister) from captivity at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) The scheme later fails, however, when the four are discovered by Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Bronn pairs with a [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) girl when Tyrion meets [Shae](wiki:Shae) at [Chataya's brothel](wiki:Chataya's_brothel).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) When King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) has the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) beat [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) after the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross), Tyrion has Bronn and [Timett](wiki:Timett) protect the humiliated girl.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

Bronn protects Tyrion during the [riot of King's Landing](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing), as well as afterward from an angry Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount). Tyrion assigns Bronn to protect the water wagons dealing with fires started by the rioters.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

With [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s host traveling north toward King's Landing, Tyrion orders Bronn to have the waterfront burned between the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) and the city walls.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) has Ser [Osmund Kettleblack](wiki:Osmund_Kettleblack) named to the Kingsguard, but the man is actually one of Bronn's informants.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2)

During the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Bronn is put in charge of having oxen pull Tyrion's massive chain tight across the Blackwater, which prevents [Stannis's ships](wiki:Royal_fleet) from escaping [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) He receives [knighthood](wiki:Knight) after the battle,(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) one of the six hundred men knighted by Osmund, Ser [Balon Swann](wiki:Balon_Swann), and Ser [Meryn Trant](wiki:Meryn_Trant) of the Kingsguard.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

### A Storm of Swords

While a wounded [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) recovers from his wounds, Bronn reveals that Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) put him forward for [knighthood](wiki:Knight). Ser Bronn of the Blackwater takes as his sigil a green, flaming chain on a smoking grey field, in commemoration of his role in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater). He wears soft leather boots, a wide with nuggets of silver, a wool doublet, and a pale green silk cloak.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Tywin, the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), brings his own guards to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), and Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) dismisses the sellswords hired by Bronn for Tyrion.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Rather than the cheaper brothels he would previously have patronized, Ser Bronn is now able to visit [Chataya's brothel](wiki:Chataya's_brothel) on his own, sleeping with [Alayaya](wiki:Alayaya) and [Marei](wiki:Marei).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) He investigates the late Ser [Mandon Moore](wiki:Mandon_Moore), who attempted to kill Tyrion at the Blackwater,(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) and he attends the wedding of Tyrion and [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

Tyrion has Bronn discreetly kill the singer [Symon Silver Tongue](wiki:Symon_Silver_Tongue) after Symon tries to blackmail Tyrion, with Symon ending up in a [bowl of brown](wiki:Bowl_of_brown) in one of [Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom)'s pot shops.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Bronn and [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne) accompany Tyrion when he meets the retinue of Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) from [Dorne](wiki:Dorne).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

Tyrion is arrested after the death of his nephew, King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), at his [wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) Cersei deprives her hated brother of an ally by arranging for Ser Bronn to wed [Lollys Stokeworth](wiki:Lollys_Stokeworth), as Lady [Tanda Stokeworth](wiki:Tanda_Stokeworth) is desperate for someone to wed her daughter. Bronn refuses to champion Tyrion a second time in a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat), as it would mean facing the monstrous Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Oberyn volunteers to champion Tyrion, but he is slain by the Mountain in single combat.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

At the [Fingers](wiki:Drearfort), Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) reveals to Sansa that the [Kettleblack](wiki:House_Kettleblack) brothers are actually in his employ, not Bronn's.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Lady [Tanda Stokeworth](wiki:Tanda_Stokeworth) informs Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) that Bronn has given the name of [Tyrion Tanner](wiki:Tyrion_Tanner) to the bastard son of his new wife, [Lollys Stokeworth](wiki:Lollys_Stokeworth), in dubious honor of his former employer, [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

The elderly Tanda shatters her hip while riding.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Bronn takes four knights into his household at [Castle Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth), although Lollys's elder sister, [Falyse Stokeworth](wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth), and her husband, Ser [Balman Byrch](wiki:Balman_Byrch), tell Cersei that they are upjumped [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword). Cersei fears that Bronn is still in league with Tyrion, who vanished after the murder of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). Cersei suggests that Falyse and Balman have Bronn die in a hunting mishap.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1)

The chivalrous Balman instead challenges Bronn to single combat, planning to unhorse Bronn with his lance and then kill the stunned man on the ground. Bronn drives his lance through Balman's horse, however, which upends Balman and leaves him with crushed legs. When Balman asks for mercy, Bronn stabs him in the eye with his dagger and orders Falyse to leave Stokeworth. Claiming to be Lord Stokeworth, Bronn gives Falyse's clothes to Lollys.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) To prevent the queen's involvement from becoming known, Cersei sends Falyse to Qyburn,(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) and the widow dies during his experiments.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

Tanda dies from a chill, with Lollys succeeding her as Lady Stokeworth and Bronn as her lord.(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

Exiled in [Essos](wiki:Essos), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) uses Bronn as an example of his generosity while attempting to gain the favor of the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons).(wiki:Bronn.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)
