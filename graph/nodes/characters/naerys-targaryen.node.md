## Origins

### Youth

Naerys was the only daughter and youngest child of King [Viserys II Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) and [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare).(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) According to semi-canon sources, she almost died in the cradle and was sickly for most of her life, finding most physical activity to be very taxing. She ate but little and was painfully thin, almost emaciated.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM_7-3)

Naerys loved music and poetry, played the [harp](wiki:Harp) well, and enjoyed sewing and embroidering. She was also devout in her [faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), and often found solace in the pages of *[The Seven-Pointed Star](wiki:The_Seven-Pointed_Star)*.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM_7-4) She would have become a [septa](wiki:Septa), had her father allowed it.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Naerys loved Prince [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)) the most out of her two brothers, as he knew how to make her laugh. Aemon was also more like Naerys in character, while Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) was not. Yet, in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Naerys was married to Aegon at their father's orders, with the blessing of his brother, King [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). The singers like to claim that both Aemon and Naerys wept during the ceremony, but the truth is different: Aemon is known to have quarreled with Aegon during the feast, and Naerys wept during the bedding, not the actual wedding.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Prince Aemon joined the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) soon after the wedding, at the age of seventeen.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafaemon_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

### Married life

On the last day of [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Naerys gave birth for the first time, to a son named [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen). His birth was difficult for Naerys, however, and Grand Maester [Alford](wiki:Alford) warned her that another pregnancy could kill her.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) Naerys thus went to her husband, and asked him, as he now had an heir, if they could live as brother and sister, instead of husband and wife. Aegon refused.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM_7-5)

The marriage of [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) and Naerys was an unhappy one. Aegon is known to have stated that he had loved nine women in his entire lifetime, yet Naerys was not counted as being among those.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) While unhappy in her marriage, Naerys still had her brother [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)) and her son Daeron, who both knew how to make her smile.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM_7-6)

Throughout the years, Naerys went through several more difficult pregnancies, despite Alford's warning that another pregnancy could kill her. In [161 AC](wiki:161_AC), Naerys nearly died giving birth to twins,(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6) and the twins' deaths shortly after birth led King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) to fast for a moon's turn.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Baelor also sent Naerys's husband, Aegon, to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) on a diplomatic mission after this pregnancy. Accounts at the time suggest it was an excuse to make sure Aegon left Naerys alone as she recovered from the difficult childbirth.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7)

In [172 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Naerys gave birth to a second set of twins. The boy was stillborn, but the girl, Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)), survived. Yet again, this delivery caused Naerys to linger near death, and Aegon's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Lord Bracken](wiki:Lord_Bracken), went as far as to speak openly about marrying his own daughter [Barba](wiki:Barba_Bracken) (who had given birth to the king's bastard [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) a fortnight before Naerys had gone into labor) to Aegon, after Naerys's expected eventual death. However, Naerys eventually recovered, and the scandal caused Lord Bracken to be removed from his office.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-8)

Naerys died in childbirth a year after Prince [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II))'s death. The child, never mentioned again, most likely died as well.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-9)

### Rumors of Daeron's parentage

After Naerys's husband became King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), rumors that their son [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) had not been fathered by Aegon, but by their brother, Prince [Aemon the Dragonknight](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), started being spread. The truth of this claim has never been proven or disproven, though the singers claim Naerys loved Aemon, and Aemon loved her.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafaemon_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Aemon championed her and defended her honor against the slanders of Ser [Morgil Hastwyck](wiki:Morgil_Hastwyck),(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) when he accused her of treason.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafaemon_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

The writings of Maester [Kaeth](wiki:Kaeth) in the *[Lives of Four Kings](wiki:Lives_of_Four_Kings)* state that it was King Aegon IV who secretly started the rumors of Naerys's adultery, using Morgil to spread this tale, though the king denied this at the time. These accusations only started when Aegon and Daeron quarreled in [174 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), when Daeron opposed his father's plan for an unprovoked war against [Dorne](wiki:Dorne).(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-10)

Prince Aemon once won a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) as a [mystery knight](wiki:Mystery_knight) so he could name Naerys the [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty), instead of said title going to one of Aegon's mistresses.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

Aemon eventually died defending Aegon from two men from [House Toyne](wiki:House_Toyne) who attempted to murder him to avenge their brother, Ser [Terrence Toyne](wiki:Terrence_Toyne) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). Naerys did not long survive Aemon's death, as she died in childbirth a year later. King Aegon, who had already slighted both Naerys and Aemon at every previous chance in the past, did little to honor either of his siblings' memories. Daring to go further now that the Dragonknight was dead, Aegon also began to make more references to Daeron's alleged parentage.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-11)

As far as is known, there are only rumors about Daeron's parentage, with no one having questioned the paternity of [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)), the only other child of Naerys who lived past infancy.

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Naerys Targaryen](wiki:Category:Images_of_Naerys_Targaryen)*

Naerys had the [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) looks, a fine and delicate beauty, almost unworldy. She was slender and small, with big [deep violet in hue](wiki:Purple_eyes) eyes and fine, pale, porcelain skin, near translucent.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM_7-0) She bore a certain resemblance to [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen).(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM_7-1)

Naerys dressed well, but simply, and seldom wore her [crown](wiki:Crown) or any other [jewelry](wiki:Jewelry). Though she had the silver-gold hair of the Targaryens, she often bound it up beneath a hair net or concealed it beneath a cowl.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM_7-2)

Naerys was pious and gentle and frail.(wiki:Naerys_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)
