## Appearances & Description

Thoren is sinewy, and has a weak chin and mouth hidden beneath a thin beard.(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) He wields a [longsword](wiki:Longsword).(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Thoren's demeanor is lordly(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) and aggressive.(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Rasosprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) He is friends with Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne),(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) and hates [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings),(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) with the exception of [Craster](wiki:Craster).(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Thoren is distrustful of [weirwoods](wiki:Weirwood).(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

According to [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), Thoren was a henchman of Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne), the former [master-at-arms](wiki:Master-at-arms) of [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), and Thoren dislikes Jon and [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly).(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

Following the death of Ser [Jaremy Rykker](wiki:Jaremy_Rykker), command of the [rangers](wiki:Rangers) falls to Thoren, who takes Jaremy's heavy breastplate and [sable](wiki:Sable)-trimmed cloak for himself.(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Thoren attempts to convince Lord Commander [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont) that he should command the upcoming [great ranging](wiki:Great_ranging), not Jeor. He is rebuffed by the Lord Commander, who insists that [Benjen Stark](wiki:Benjen_Stark) is still [First Ranger](wiki:First_Ranger) until his fate is confirmed.(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

Thoren is one of the senior officers during the expedition [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall), commanding the rangers. In the [column](wiki:Column), he commands the vanguard.(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) At [Whitetree](wiki:Whitetree), he sends foragers in search of game.(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

Thoren recognizes the advantageous position of the [Fist of the First Men](wiki:Fist_of_the_First_Men), and Jeor restricts Thoren's rangers to patrolling the eastern bank of the [Milkwater](wiki:Milkwater).(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) During a meeting of the officers, Thoren argues that the Night's Watch should advance into the [Frostfangs](wiki:Frostfangs) and battle [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder)'s host of [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings). Lord Commander Mormont decides to send small scouting groups into the mountains to seek out the wildling camp, however.(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) [Qhorin Halfhand](wiki:Qhorin_Halfhand) mentions Thoren as a possible leader for one of the scouting groups.(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Racok43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### A Storm of Swords

Thoren leads one of the search parties looking for the wildling host, finding them along the Milkwater in the Frostfangs. His scouts include [Kedge Whiteye](wiki:Kedge_Whiteye) and [Bannen](wiki:Bannen).(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Rasosprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) After returning to the Fist, Thoren attempts to convince Ser [Mallador Locke](wiki:Mallador_Locke) and Jeor Mormont that the Night's Watch should surprise Mance's host.(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Rasosprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

Before the Night's Watch departs the Fist, however, they are attacked by [wights](wiki:Wights). During the [fight at the Fist](wiki:Fight_at_the_Fist), Thoren coordinates the firing of fire arrows against the undead. As the Night's Watch is being defeated, Thoren advocates sending the reserve to hold the west wall of the Fist, but Lord Commander Mormont has him call them back to cut their way out. When a dead [snow bear](wiki:Snow_bear) comes upon the commanders of the Night's Watch, Thoren charges the [animal](wiki:Animal). He nearly takes the bear's head off with his longsword, but the beast then takes Thoren's own head from his shoulders.(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

As the Night's Watch retreats to [Craster's Keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep), [Ronnel Harclay](wiki:Ronnel_Harclay) replaces Thoren as commander of the remaining rangers.(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) After Jeor Mormont's death in the [mutiny at Craster's Keep](wiki:Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep), [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) briefly contemplates Thoren succeeding as Lord Commander, but Jon remembers that Thoren is dead.(wiki:Thoren_Smallwood.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
