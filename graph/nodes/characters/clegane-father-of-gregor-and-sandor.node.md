## Origins

Clegane was born the son of the [kennelmaster](wiki:Kennelmaster) at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock). One [autumn](wiki:Autumn) year, [his father](wiki:Clegane_(founder)) saved Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) from a [lioness](wiki:Lion). Tytos rewarded the kennelmaster with a [knighthood](wiki:Knighthood), lands and a [towerhouse](wiki:Clegane's_Keep), and took his son as his [squire](wiki:Squire).(wiki:Clegane_(father_of_Gregor_and_Sandor).cite_ref-Racok18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

Some time later, Clegane inherited the keep and lands from his father.(wiki:Clegane_(father_of_Gregor_and_Sandor).cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) When his son [Sandor](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) was seven years old, and his son [Gregor](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) was twelve, a woodcarver set up shop in the village by the keep, and gifted toys to the Cleganes to buy favor. Sandor desired his brother's gift, a jointed wooden [knight](wiki:Knight), and took it to play with, though he was afraid of what the brutal Gregor might do. When Gregor found him, he shoved the side of Sandor's face into the burning coals of a brazier and held him there while he screamed, and only three grown men could drag Gregor off his brother. The Cleganes' [maester](wiki:Maester) attempted to heal the burns with ointments, and Ser Clegane explained the burns to others by claiming that his son's bedding had caught fire.(wiki:Clegane_(father_of_Gregor_and_Sandor).cite_ref-Ragot29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

However, there are still rumors that Gregor was involved in Sandor's burning. Ser Clegane's young daughter died mysteriously, and Gregor is rumored to have been involved with that as well. Finally, the knight himself died in a hunting accident, and it is implied he was actually killed by his son Gregor. Sandor left home the day of his father's death to become a [sworn sword](wiki:Sworn_sword) of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister), and he has never returned.(wiki:Clegane_(father_of_Gregor_and_Sandor).cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
