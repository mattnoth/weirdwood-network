## Origins

Two of Victarion's brothers, [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) and [Aeron](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), often mocked him when they were boys.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Euron later gave Victarion his first woman to make him a man.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

Victarion and his two elder brothers, [Balon](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) and Euron, convinced their father [Quellon](wiki:Quellon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), to join [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), and the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) raided the [Reach](wiki:Reach) late in the war. After Quellon was killed in a [battle at the Mander](wiki:Battle_at_the_Mander), however, the new Lord Balon returned to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) with his younger brothers.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

Victarion's first wife died in childbirth, birthing a stillborn daughter, and his second wife was stricken by a pox.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-11)

Victarion recognized the greatness of Balon and followed him loyally. He commanded the great fleet constructed by Balon, the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet), during [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion). During the war, using a plan Euron had concocted, they sailed into [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) and [burned the Lannister fleet](wiki:Burning_of_the_Lannister_fleet) at anchor.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) Victarion tossed the first torch onto the flagship of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-12) Later in the rebellion, the Iron Fleet was smashed by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) in a [sea battle off Fair Isle](wiki:Sea_battle_off_Fair_Isle), during which *[Golden Storm](wiki:Golden_Storm)*, the vessel of Victarion's younger brother Aeron, was chopped in half by Stannis's warship, *[Fury](wiki:Fury)*.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

Victarion continued to serve Balon faithfully as [Lord Captain of the Iron Fleet](wiki:Lord_Captain_of_the_Iron_Fleet) after the rebellion's end, and he took a [third spouse](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy's_third_wife), a [salt wife](wiki:Salt_wife).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-13) According to a semi-canon source,(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rawoiafeuron_greyjoy.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) she had an affair with Euron, however, and was impregnated by him. When Victarion confronted his brother, Euron laughed and claimed the woman was willing. To retain his honor and prevent the cuckoldry from becoming public,(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-14) a sobbing Victarion beat his love(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6) to death and left her body on the shore for crabs to eat. Only the taboo against [kinslaying](wiki:Kinslaying) kept Victarion from doing the same to Euron.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-15) Victarion has not touched a woman since, nor has he forgiven Euron,(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-16) who was forced into exile by Balon in [297 AC](wiki:297_AC).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2)(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Balon commanded that neither speak of what happened.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-17)

In addition to his third wife, Victarion has beaten four men to death with his fists.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-18) Victarion thrashed [Harras Harlaw](wiki:Harras_Harlaw) on multiple occasions when Harras was young.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7)

## Appearances & Description

Victarion is a large and powerful man, with a bull's broad chest,(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) a boy's flat stomach,(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) and huge hands.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) His hair is flecked with grey.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) considers his uncle to be an old man.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Victarion is less handsome than his brother [Euron](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

Victarion is a follower of the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God),(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) considers Victarion to be more godly than their despised elder brother, Euron Crow's Eye.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Victarion is a capable commander and a fierce warrior in battle, but he has a calm demeanor outside of it, thinking of enemies who show bravery with great respect.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29483.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) He is willing to wear plate armor while sailing because he has no fear of drowning.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Victarion loves the ships of the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet),(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) and he always wants [wine](wiki:Wine) after a battle.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

The Lord Captain wears heavy grey chain mail over boiled black leather.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) When preparing for combat, Victarion also wears heavy plate.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) He wears padded gloves underneath his iron gauntlets of lobstered steel plate.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) Victarion's tall black warhelm is decorated with an iron [kraken](wiki:Kraken), whose tentacles coil down below his jaw.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8) He carries a [longsword](wiki:Longsword) and a dirk,(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9) but prefers to use his fearsome steel axe(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) with a shield.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) Victarion's cloak is made of nine layers of gold cloth sewn like the kraken of his House, and its arms reach his boots.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-10)

After Moqorro heals his sword-wounded hand with fire-magic, Victarion's arm is hideous but stronger: "The arm the priest had healed was hideous to look upon, pork crackling from elbow to fingertips. Sometimes when Victarion closed his hand the skin would split and smoke, yet the arm was stronger than it had ever been." (adwd-victarion-01.md:57) [Moqorro-healed burned arm]

The Myrish eye Victarion claims from the captain of the captured cog *Dove* is a far-seeing glass: "two glass lenses in a series of brass tubes, cunningly wrought so that each section slid into the next, until the eye was no longer than a dirk." (adwd-victarion-01.md:41) [Myrish eye spyglass]

Victarion dislikes laughter, fearing that it means he is being made fun of by others, and the realization of mockery angers him.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Theon and [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) consider their uncle to be a grey bullock, with Theon recalling Victarion as dutiful but unambitious and Asha finding him dim.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) According to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin), Victarion is "dumb as a stump"(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-13) and "a dullard and a brute".(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-14)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

[Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), gather his forces to [Pyke](wiki:Pyke) in preparation for an [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) invasion of the [north](wiki:North) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). Victarion loans his steersman, [Rymolf Stormdrunk](wiki:Rymolf_Stormdrunk), to his nephew, [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), who has returned to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) after years on the [mainland](wiki:Green_lands).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) During a feast in the [Great Hall of Pyke](wiki:Great_Keep_of_Pyke), Victarion laughs when [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) makes fun of her brother, Theon. After the feast, Victarion discusses tides and winds with Balon in the lord's solar.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)

Balon declares himself [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands),(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) and he sends Victarion with the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) to sail up the [Saltspear](wiki:Saltspear) and the [Fever River](wiki:Fever_River).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-5) [Moat Cailin falls](wiki:Fall_of_Moat_Cailin) to Victarion's surprise assault.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

Theon deviates from his [harrying of the Stony Shore](wiki:Harrying_of_the_Stony_Shore) and orchestrates the [capture of Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell),(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) and Balon styles himself [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) Theon intends to hold [Meera](wiki:Meera_Reed) and [Jojen Reed](wiki:Jojen_Reed) captive to prevent their father, Lord [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed), from attacking Victarion's men holding Moat Cailin, but the Reed children vanish from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### A Storm of Swords

[Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), the [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), is informed at [Hag's Mire](wiki:Hag's_Mire) of Balon falling to his death at Pyke and the return of his exiled brother, [Euron Crow's Eye](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy). Robb thinks that Victarion will leave Moat Cailin to claim Balon's throne.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Victarion wears mail and leather day and night at the ruins of Moat Cailin for protection from [crannogmen](wiki:Crannogmen). [Red Ralf Stonehouse](wiki:Ralf_Stonehouse), [Ralf Kenning](wiki:Ralf_Kenning), [Ralf the Limper](wiki:Ralf_the_Limper), and [Nute the Barber](wiki:Nute_the_Barber) support Victarion becoming king. After hearing that his younger brother [Aeron Damphair](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) has called for a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) to determine Balon's successor, Victarion returns to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) with his fleet. He leaves a token force under the command of Ralf Kenning to defend Moat Cailin.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-19)

Victarion makes a claim to the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair) as Balon's brother and second-in-command, and he holds a great feast for famous captains on the shore of [Nagga's Cradle](wiki:Nagga's_Cradle). Victarion receives the support of [Fralegg the Strong](wiki:Fralegg_the_Strong) and [Alvyn Sharp](wiki:Alvyn_Sharp), and [Hotho Harlaw](wiki:Hotho_Harlaw) suggests his twelve-year-old daughter as a queen for the widowed Lord Captain. Lord [Baelor Blacktyde](wiki:Baelor_Blacktyde) is wary, however, as Victarion intends to continue the war. Victarion refuses the offer of his niece, [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy), to share rule with him as king and her as his [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), and he rejects her plan to make peace with [northmen](wiki:Northmen).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-20)

Red Ralf, Ralf the Limper, and Nute serve as Victarion's champions during the [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk), and his men offer [silver](wiki:Silver), [gold](wiki:Gold), and gems to the gathered captains. Victarion gives a brief speech in which he offers to continue Balon's war, but the crowd ultimately favors Euron after he promises to conquer [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) with [dragons](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

Aeron asks for Victarion's help in raising the Iron Islands against Euron, but Victarion's obedient and dutiful nature keeps him following his new king's orders, despite his personal hatred for Euron.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8) Victarion has the Iron Fleet stop Lord Blacktyde from departing Nagga's Cradle, and Euron then has Baelor executed for not accepting his kingship.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-9) Euron gives Victarion a [dusky woman](wiki:Dusky_woman) taken from a [slaving ship](wiki:Slavery) to serve as his bedwarmer. Victarion is reluctant to accept anything from his hated brother, but he relents when Euron threatens to have the woman killed.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-10)

Victarion leads the [taking of the Shields](wiki:Taking_of_the_Shields), slaughtering unarmored sailors and winning a duel with Ser [Talbert Serry](wiki:Talbert_Serry), the son of Lord [Osbert Serry](wiki:Osbert_Serry), but his left hand is wounded during the fight. After his victory, Victarion watches helplessly as Euron steals Nute's allegiance by naming him [Lord of Oakenshield](wiki:Lord_of_Oakenshield). Victarion also deplores the cruelty Euron displays toward the slain lords' women.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-11)

During a private meeting in [Lord Hewett's castle](wiki:Lord_Hewett's_castle), Victarion spits out the [shade-of-the-evening](wiki:Shade-of-the-evening) he is given by his brother. Euron asks Victarion to find [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) in [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay) and deliver Euron's marriage proposal, and the new king explains that Victarion will receive the Seastone Chair once Euron claims the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Victarion agrees, but he secretly plans on courting Daenerys for himself.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-12)

### A Dance with Dragons

After Theon negotiates the [yielding of Moat Cailin](wiki:Siege_of_Moat_Cailin), [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) executes the small garrison left behind by Victarion.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

Victarion's [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) sails east, capturing prizes in the [Redwyne Straits](wiki:Redwyne_Straits) and along the coast of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). After the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), they split into three squadrons and set sail for [Meereen](wiki:Meereen). Victarion's squadron follows the coast of the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands), visits [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), and continues past the [Valyrian peninsula](wiki:Valyrian_peninsula). Also aboard *[Iron Victory](wiki:Iron_Victory)* is the dusky woman, whom Victarion sleeps with. Victarion loses nearly half his ships by the time the Iron Fleet reaches the [Isle of Cedars](wiki:Isle_of_Cedars). Victarion's wounded hand continues to trouble him, and he suspects that [Kerwin](wiki:Kerwin), the [maester](wiki:Maester) from [Greenshield](wiki:Greenshield) that Euron sent to accompany him, is [poisoning](wiki:Poison) him. Victarion also dislikes the [monkeys](wiki:Monkey) which board his ships from the Isle of Cedars.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

A [red priest](wiki:Red_priest) named [Moqorro](wiki:Moqorro) is discovered clinging to wreckage out at sea by *[Grief](wiki:Grief)*. The crew of *Iron Victory* want Moqorro killed when *Grief'*s captain, the [Vole](wiki:Vole), brings him before their leader, but Moqorro convinces Victarion to allow him to treat his wounded hand. Behind closed doors, Moqorro apparently uses [magic](wiki:Magic) on the Lord Captain; when Victarion emerges from his cabin, his left hand is charred and blackened. Victarion has his men kill Maester Kerwin, for Moqorro has claimed his death will bring good winds,(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) and Victarion has [Tom Tidewood](wiki:Tom_Tidewood) garb the red priest in the black and gold colors of [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

The Iron Fleet captures *[Ghiscari Dawn](wiki:Ghiscari_Dawn)* while continuing north toward Meereen, and Victarion has its captain drowned as a sacrifice to the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God) for claiming that [Daenerys has died](wiki:A_Dance_with_Dragons-Chapter_52).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) When the ironborn capture *[Dove](wiki:Dove)*, Victarion claims the [Myrish eye](wiki:Myrish_eye) of its captain for himself.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2) When the captains of two captured galleys repeat the claim that Daenerys is dead, Victarion beheads them and kills most of their crew. Victarion frees their enslaved oarsmen are freed, but then forces them to row for the Iron Fleet.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3) Victarion strangles the master of a fishing ketch after the fisherman laughs at the ironman's lack of knowledge of the [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea).(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-4)

Moqorro, like [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), can apparently see the future in the flames, and he prays before nightfires to aid Victarion in his quest. The *Iron Victory'*s crew live in great fear of Moqorro and call him the Black Flame. Victarion begins making offerings to both the Drowned God and Moqorro's red god, [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor). When the Iron Fleet captures a [Yunkish](wiki:Yunkai) slaver galley, *[Willing Maiden](wiki:Willing_Maiden)*, Victarion has the captive boys join the ironmen as rowers and gives the captive girls to his captains. Victarion claims the seven prettiest girls for himself, sets them adrift in a burning ketch, and has the girls burn and drown as sacrifices to his two gods. Victarion shows [Dragonbinder](wiki:Dragonbinder), the [dragon horn](wiki:Dragon_horn) given to him by Euron, to Moqorro, and the red priest tells the ironman he must claim the horn with blood.(wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-5)

## Foreshadowing

- On the voyage to Meereen, Victarion seems to hear the Drowned God claim him, echoing the dueling-gods motif that runs through his ADWD arc: "You shall serve me well, my captain, the waves seemed to say. It was for this I made you." (adwd-victarion-01.md:55) [Drowned God claims Victarion / dueling-gods motif]

- Moqorro's fire-vision before the prize-taking names a threat behind and a prize ahead — the "tigers" go unresolved in ADWD (commonly read as the Volantene tiger-cloaks / green galleys pursuing the fleet): "Behind come the tigers. Ahead awaits your dragon." (adwd-victarion-01.md:45) [Moqorro fire-vision — tigers unresolved]

- A captured fisherman reports Daenerys's departure on dragonback, marking Victarion's intelligence gap versus Dany's true location: "The silver queen is gone... She flew away upon her dragon, beyond the Dothraki sea." (adwd-victarion-01.md:65) [Victarion's intel-gap re Daenerys]
