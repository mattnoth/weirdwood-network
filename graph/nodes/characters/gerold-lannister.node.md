## Identity

Gerold Lannister (d. 244 AC) — child of Cerissa Brax and Damon Lannister; married to Alysanne Farman and Rohanne Webber; sworn to House Lannister; Regent. One of 4 characters named Gerold Lannister.

## Origins

### Early life

Born at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock), Gerold Lannister was the youngest son of Lord [Damon Lannister](wiki:Damon_Lannister_(lord)), the Grey Lion and his wife, Lady [Cerissa Brax](wiki:Cerissa_Brax).(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rawoiafgerold_lannister.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf103.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) After Damon passed in [210 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Gerold's older brother Ser [Tybolt](wiki:Tybolt_Lannister) became the [Lord of Casterly Rock](wiki:Lord_of_Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-8) By [211 AC](wiki:211_AC), Gerold was a suitor to Lady [Rohanne Webber](wiki:Rohanne_Webber) of [Coldmoat](wiki:Coldmoat), and she was fond of his letters. However, she believed Gerold would never willingly forsake the pleasures of [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) and the splendor of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) for some little lordship, as he wielded more influence at Casterly Rock as his brother's advisor than he could ever hope for as the husband of Lady Rohanne.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) Rohanne Webber would eventually marry [another](wiki:Eustace_Osgrey),(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) but some time after the passing of her fifth husband, she and Gerold were married after all.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-9) Although Gerold had no children by his first wife, Lady [Alysanne Farman](wiki:Alysanne_Farman), he would have four sons with Rohanne: [Tywald](wiki:Tywald_Lannister), [Tion](wiki:Tion_Lannister), [Tytos](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) (born [220 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), and [Jason Lannister](wiki:Jason_Lannister_(son_of_Gerold)) (born [229 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)).(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-10)(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf103.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

Tybolt passed away in [212 AC](wiki:212_AC) under suspicious circumstances, and his young daughter [Cerelle](wiki:Cerelle_Lannister_(daughter_of_Tybolt)) succeeded him as Lady of Casterly Rock, with Gerold serving as her [regent](wiki:Regent). However, in less than a year, Lady Cerelle also passed away. The suddenness of Cerelle's death led to rumors that Gerold had murdered his niece,(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-11) the most common tale claiming that he had smothered her with a pillow. Some of his lords and many of his [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) held no love for him, believing him a [kinslayer](wiki:Kinslayer).(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-sample_6-0) Some said he had murdered his brother Tybolt as well.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-12)

### Lord of Casterly Rock

Following the death of his niece, Gerold succeeded her as [Lord of Casterly Rock](wiki:Lord_of_Casterly_Rock) and [Warden of the West](wiki:Warden_of_the_West). He quickly proved to be a shrewd and able lord. His rule was prosperous, and Gerold was able to greatly increase the wealth and power of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister), as well as the trade at [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport). Gerold ruled the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) for thirty-one years, earning the sobriquet "*Gerold the Golden*".(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-13)

In [230 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Gerold's beloved wife Rohanne disappeared under mysterious circumstances, less than a year after giving birth to Gerold's fourth son [Jason](wiki:Jason_Lannister_(son_of_Gerold)). His eldest son [Tywald](wiki:Tywald_Lannister) died during the [Storming of Starpike](wiki:Storming_of_Starpike) in [233 AC](wiki:233_AC) while [squiring](wiki:Squire) for Lord [Robert Reyne](wiki:Robert_Reyne) in the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising).(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-14) King Maekar had died in the battle as well, after which Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) summoned a [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) to settle the issue of royal succession. With his eloquence, and possibly his gold as well, Gerold aided in swaying the lords at the council into bestowing the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) to Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen).(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-sample_6-1)

Gerold's son Tywald had been long [betrothed](wiki:Betrothal) to Lady [Ellyn Reyne](wiki:Ellyn_Reyne), the sister of [Roger Reyne](wiki:Roger_Reyne), the new [Lord of Castamere](wiki:Lord_of_Castamere). Following Tywald's death, Lady Ellyn persuaded Tywald's twin brother [Tion](wiki:Tion_Lannister) to set aside his own betrothal and marry her instead. It has been said that Gerold opposed the match, but that in the end, due to his grief, age, and [illness](wiki:Illness), he gave way(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-15) when Tion revealed that Tywald's dying words begged his brother to "take care of Lady Ellyn."(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-sample_6-2)

Following the double wedding of his sons Tion and [Tytos](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) in [235 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Gerold retreated to his books and bedchamber. As the wife of the heir to Casterly Rock, Ellyn became Lady of Casterly Rock in all but name, and was quick to bring her brothers and kin to Casterly Rock.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-16) When Gerold's aged [fool](wiki:Fool) [Lord Toad](wiki:Lord_Toad) made a jape at Ellyn's expense, she had him whipped. Gerold was too ill to interfere.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-sample_6-3) When the [Fourth Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Fourth_Blackfyre_Rebellion) broke out in [236 AC](wiki:236_AC), Ser Tion perished in the [Battle of Wendwater Bridge](wiki:Battle_of_Wendwater_Bridge). Tion's death woke a new strength in Gerold, and he took a firm hold of the westerlands once more. He became intent on preparing his thirdborn son, Tytos, for his lordship.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-17)

In [239 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Ellyn attempted to bed Tytos, urging him to set aside his wife, [Jeyne Marbrand](wiki:Jeyne_Marbrand). Tytos confessed to his wife, who informed Gerold. Furious, Gerold married off Ellyn to [Walderan Tarbeck](wiki:Walderan_Tarbeck), the aged Lord of [Tarbeck Hall](wiki:Tarbeck_Hall), and she never returned to Casterly Rock.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-18)

### Final years

Lady Ellyn would give Lord Tarbeck two daughters and a son. She chose all their names carefully, according to Maester [Beldon](wiki:Beldon), who called them *"daggers aimed at Lord Gerold's heart"*. Her first daughter was named [Rohanne](wiki:Rohanne_Tarbeck), after Lord Gerold's lost wife. Her second daughter was named [Cyrelle](wiki:Cyrelle_Tarbeck), after the niece Gerold was rumored to have murdered. Her son was named [Tion](wiki:Tion_Tarbeck), after Gerold's son and Ellyn's late husband.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-sample_6-4) In [242 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Tytos and Jeyne gave Gerold his first grandson, and the only one he lived to see, [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). Legend claims that when Lord Gerold ruffled the infant Tywin's golden hair, the child bit his finger.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-19)

In [244 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Gerold died of a bad bladder, unable to pass water. He was succeeded by his son Tytos.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-20)

## Appearances & Description

Gerold was golden-haired, and over six feet tall.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

Gerold was known to be quick of wit.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) A genial man, Gerold was also exceedingly clever. As [Lord of Casterly Rock](wiki:Lord_of_Casterly_Rock), he proved himself to be an exceptionally shrewd, able, and fair-minded lord. He enjoyed reading, especially later in his life.(wiki:Gerold_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_1-7)
