## Origins

### Early life

Born in [100 AC](wiki:100_AC),(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-birth_2-1) Ser Corwyn was the second son of the [Lord of Heart's Home](wiki:Lord_of_Heart's_Home), and a younger brother of [Leowyn Corbray](wiki:Leowyn_Corbray). A famed [knight](wiki:Knight), his lord father had gifted him [Lady Forlorn](wiki:Lady_Forlorn), the ancient [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword of [House Corbray](wiki:House_Corbray).(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)

Corwyn had two daughters from his first marriage. By the time of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), his wife had passed, however. At the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), he met [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)), a [ward](wiki:Fosterage) of Lady [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn), growing close to the Targaryen princess.(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)

### Dance of the Dragons

In [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), Lady Arryn gave Ser Corwyn and Lord Leowyn command of her [army](wiki:Army) of ten thousand men sent forth from [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown), supporting the claim of the late Queen Rhaenyra's eldest son Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). The Arryn host sailed from Gulltown aboard a [fleet](wiki:Watercraft) hired from [Braavos](wiki:Braavos). Only the [Velaryon fleet](wiki:Velaryon_fleet) posed a threat to the Arryns, though the Corbrays soon witnessed the Velaryon ships tearing down the banners of [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), for the king had died.(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) The Braavosi landed Ser Corwyn at [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool) with half the power Lady Arryn had sent down, the other half under the command of Lord Leowyn at [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale).(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-6) The towns welcomed the Arryn hosts with feasts and flowers, as the surviving supporters of Aegon II surrendered.(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

The young Prince Aegon ascended to the throne as King Aegon III Targaryen. Soon after Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark)'s [hold over court](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf) had begun, word arrived that Ser Corwyn had left Duskendale accompanied by Lord [Clement Celtigar](wiki:Clement_Celtigar) and the widowed [Lady Staunton](wiki:Lady_Staunton).(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) When the Corbray brothers arrived at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), they joined the ruling council and added their voices to those of Lady Arryn and the [Lads](wiki:Lads), desiring peace across the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). This contributed to power beginning to slip from Lord Stark's hands.(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

### Marriage

In [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), Princess [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen) defied the will of the [council of regents](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) of being betrothed to Lord [Thaddeus Rowan](wiki:Thaddeus_Rowan) of [Goldengrove](wiki:Goldengrove), instead marrying her cousin, Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) of [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark). Because of this defiance, the council thought Baela could no longer be considered a potential heir the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and planned to immediate marry off her younger twin, [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Daemon)). Rhaena was asked if she had a favorite among suitors, and she confessed to being "especially fond" of Ser Corwyn. Though Ser Corwyn was a second son and far from an ideal choice, [House Corbray](wiki:House_Corbray) was old and prestigious, and his brother had been made [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm), and thus the council agreed to Rhaena's wish. Although Lord Rowan preferred a longer betrothal, the two were hastily betrothed with a [wedding](wiki:Wedding) a fortnight later, for the regents feared that Princess Baela was already with child by Alyn.(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)

In the latter half of [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), Rhaena announced she was with child by Ser Corwyn, but suffered a miscarriage a month later. Later that year, Ser Corwyn brought Lady [Daenaera Velaryon](wiki:Daenaera_Velaryon) forward at the [Maiden's Day Ball](wiki:Maiden's_Day_Ball) when Baela and Rhaena put her forward as a potential second wife for the king. Favoring the young Daenaera, the king did indeed wed her.(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

In [134 AC](wiki:134_AC), Ser Corwyn descended from the castle to meet the *[Lady Baela](wiki:Lady_Baela)* at the docks, where the presumed dead Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) was returned to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) from [Lys](wiki:Lys) by Lord Alyn.(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### Lord Regent

Lord Rowan made Ser Corwyn one of the seven [regents](wiki:Regent) in [134 AC](wiki:134_AC) after the death of Lord [Manfryd Mooton](wiki:Manfryd_Mooton), who had never fully recovered from the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever).(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Lady Arryn died later that year and a [succession dispute](wiki:Arryn_Succession_Conflict_(134_AC)) broke out in the Vale with three claimants: her fourth cousin and chosen heir, Ser [Joffrey Arryn](wiki:Joffrey_Arryn), a closer cousin, Ser [Arnold Arryn](wiki:Arnold_Arryn), and [Isembard Arryn](wiki:Isembard_Arryn), the wealthy head of [House Arryn of Gulltown](wiki:House_Arryn_of_Gulltown). In the spring of [135 AC](wiki:135_AC), Lord Rowan sent a thousand men to Gulltown under Corwyn's command to restore the [King's Peace](wiki:King's_Peace) and settle the matter of succession.(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Ser Corwyn ruled that Lady Jeyne's last testament must be upheld and that Ser Joffrey was the rightful heir. He imprisoned Isembard Arryn and his sons, and executed Ser [Eldric Arryn](wiki:Eldric_Arryn), Ser Arnold's son, but Ser Arnold himself evaded him, fleeing to [Runestone](wiki:Runestone). When he arrived to winkle him out of his sanctuary, Lord [Gunthor Royce](wiki:Gunthor_Royce) rode out to confront him. Ser Corwyn drew Lady Forlorn and a crossbowman on Runestone's battlements loosed a quarrel and pierced him through the breast, killing him.(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

Ser Corwyn's death was considered an act of treason, for striking down a regent was akin to attacking the king himself. His nephew Lord [Quenton](wiki:Quenton_Corbray) and several other great houses in the Vale rallied to support Ser Joffrey, as war in the Vale renewed.(wiki:Corwyn_Corbray.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)
