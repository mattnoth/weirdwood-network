## Origins

Gwayne came to court at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in [101 AC](wiki:101_AC) with his siblings and mother when his father, Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower), was named [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) by King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Gwayne_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
In [111 AC](wiki:111_AC), Gwayne participated in a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) in King's Landing to celebrate the fifth anniversary of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen)'s marriage to Gwayne's sister, Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower). Gwayne served as one of his sister's champions during the tourney, but he was unhorsed by Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole).(wiki:Gwayne_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
When the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) began in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), Gwayne was named the second-in-command of the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) by his father, who instructed him to keep an eye on Ser [Luthor Largent](wiki:Luthor_Largent), the [Commander of the City Watch](wiki:Commander_of_the_City_Watch), for any signs of disloyalty towards his nephew and the newly crowned King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen). Gwayne and Ser [Arryk Cargyll](wiki:Arryk_Cargyll) were in the retinue of [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle) when he traveled from King's Landing to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) in an unsuccessful attempt to reconcile Aegon II and his half-sister, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).(wiki:Gwayne_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
When King's Landing was [attacked](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) by Queen Rhaenyra's [blacks](wiki:Blacks) in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), Gwayne rushed to the stables to sound the alarm, but he was seized, disarmed, and dragged before his own commander, Luthor Largent, who was still loyal to Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen). When Gwayne denounced him as a [turncloak](wiki:Turncloak), Ser Luthor drove his sword through Hightower's belly and ordered the city gates to be opened to the blacks.(wiki:Gwayne_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
