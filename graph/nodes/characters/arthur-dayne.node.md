## Origins

Arthur wielded the greatsword called [Dawn](wiki:Dawn) as part of his office as [Sword of the Morning](wiki:Sword_of_the_Morning), carrying it slung across his back.(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) To defeat the [Kingswood Brotherhood](wiki:Kingswood_Brotherhood), he gained the trust of the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) of the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood) by paying for what he and his forces took and taking their grievances before King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen). In the end Arthur slew the [Smiling Knight](wiki:Smiling_Knight) in single combat and ended the threat of the outlaws. Following that victory, he knighted [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), who would later become a sworn brother of the Kingsguard.(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Arthur was the champion in the [tournament in honor of Viserys's birth](wiki:Tournament_in_honor_of_Viserys's_birth) in [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), defeating Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen).(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) In another tourney, Arthur broke twelve lances against Rhaegar, losing to the prince.(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-15) At the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal) in [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), he was defeated by Rhaegar once again.(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

According to a semi-canon source,(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Rawoiafrhaegar_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Arthur, along with his other sworn brother Ser [Oswell Whent](wiki:Oswell_Whent), aided Rhaegar in the kidnapping of [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) near Harrenhal as the [year of the false spring](wiki:Year_of_the_false_spring) was ending.(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) began shortly after.

After the end of the rebellion, Arthur, Oswell, and Ser [Gerold Hightower](wiki:Gerold_Hightower) were found by Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) at the [tower of joy](wiki:Tower_of_joy). Arthur died there, [fighting Eddard and his companions](wiki:Combat_at_the_tower_of_joy), but the exact circumstances of his death are unclear.(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) The recorded history describes Arthur as being killed by Lord Eddard in single combat, however.(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_the_andals_arrive.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) recalls his father, Eddard, describing Arthur as the finest knight he ever saw and elaborating further that Arthur would have killed him if not for [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed).(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Stones from the tower were used to build his cairn upon a nearby ridge.(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)

Arthur is remembered as not only a skilled fighter but a valiant and [true knight](wiki:True_knight).(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) After Arthur's death, Eddard rode to [Starfall](wiki:Starfall), the seat of [House Dayne](wiki:House_Dayne), to return the sword [Dawn](wiki:Dawn) to Arthur's sister, Lady [Ashara Dayne](wiki:Ashara_Dayne),(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) as a sign of respect.(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_the_andals_arrive.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Arthur Dayne](wiki:Category:Images_of_Arthur_Dayne)*

According to [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), Arthur was the deadliest of the seven knights of [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen)'s [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), who idolized Arthur,(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) recalls that Arthur was stronger than he.(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Rasos21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) considers Arthur to have been an efficient commander.(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

According to [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin), Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) and Arthur were evenly matched with regular swords. However, if Arthur wielded Dawn he would beat Barristan.(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-10)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) has a fever dream of Arthur, Ser [Oswell Whent](wiki:Oswell_Whent), and Ser [Gerold Hightower](wiki:Gerold_Hightower) at the [tower of joy](wiki:Tower_of_joy).(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

### A Storm of Swords

Resting on a [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) stump, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) dreams of he and [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) being visited by the shades of Arthur, Oswell, Gerold, Ser [Jon Darry](wiki:Jonothor_Darry), Prince [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell), and Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) in the depths of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Arthur_Dayne.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
