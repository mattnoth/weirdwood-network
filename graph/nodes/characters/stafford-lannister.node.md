## Origins

When he was a young squire, Stafford and two [Lannisters of Lannisport](wiki:House_Lannister_of_Lannisport) were captured by Lady [Ellyn Tarbeck](wiki:Ellyn_Reyne) in retaliation for the imprisonment of her husband, Lord [Walderan Tarbeck](wiki:Walderan_Tarbeck), by Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) forced the prisoners to be exchanged, however. Hostilities later erupted with the [Reyne-Tarbeck rebellion](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_rebellion), which Tywin suppressed.(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Stafford has been negotiating to betroth his son, Ser [Daven Lannister](wiki:Daven_Lannister), to [Desmera Redwyne](wiki:Desmera_Redwyne), although an agreement has not been reached with Lord [Paxter Redwyne](wiki:Paxter_Redwyne).(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

## Appearances & Description

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) and Ser [Brynden Tully](wiki:Brynden_Tully) are both unimpressed by Stafford's martial capacities, with Jaime considering his uncle to be blundering.(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

After the capture of Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) in the [Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood) and the defeat of Jaime's host in the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), Stafford begins raising a new army near [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport). Because the force consists of inexperienced men, it takes a long time to train them.(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Feeling safe in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), Stafford does not post sentries.(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

[Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), the new [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), marches west from [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) and avoids the strong [Golden Tooth](wiki:Golden_Tooth) by using a goat track found by his [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf).(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) When Robb's army descends on Stafford's men in the night, the surprised [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) host is destroyed in the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross). Stafford is lanced by Lord [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark) when he tries to reach his horse.(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) According to some stories, Robb fed Stafford's heart to [Grey Wind](wiki:Grey_Wind) after the battle, which [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) finds ridiculous.(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### A Storm of Swords

Stafford's more capable son, Ser [Daven](wiki:Daven_Lannister), reforms the survivors from [Oxcross](wiki:Oxcross) at Lannisport.(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Daven had vowed not to cut his hair until he had avenged his father's death,(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) but Lord Karstark was himself executed by Robb Stark for treason.(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Daven, now the [Warden of the West](wiki:Warden_of_the_West), has continued to grow his hair out.(wiki:Stafford_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)
