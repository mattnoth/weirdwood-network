## Appearances & Description

Marillion is a handsome(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) young man with a boyish look to him.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) His sandy hair is long and curled, and he has a wisp of a mustache.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) The singer has a slender form, smooth skin, and a charming smile.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

After Mord's torture in the sky cells, Marillion appears before the Lords Declarant bathed and elegantly dressed: "Someone had bathed him and dressed him in a pair of sky-blue breeches and a loose-fitting white tunic with puffed sleeves, belted with a silvery sash . . . White silk gloves covered his hands, while a white silk bandage spared the lords the sight of his eyes." AFFC Sansa I (`sources/chapters/affc/affc-sansa-01.md:167`). The costume is Petyr's frame — Marillion presented as almost elegant precisely to defuse the Lords Declarant's pity.
Marillion is vain about his skills(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and his good looks.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Although courteous, he is also arrogant and mocking.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) He dislikes violence.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
The singer's voice is a tenor,(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) strong and sweet,(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) and he also plays the [woodharp](wiki:Harp).(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

## Origins

Marillion was in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) for the [tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_Prince_Joffrey's_name_day). He earned a great deal of [silver](wiki:Silver) with his singing during the tourney, but lost it when he bet that Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) would win his joust against Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell).(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

While en route to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) for the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney), Marillion meets Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) and Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn). Catelyn doubts Marillion's claims that he is friends with her brother, Ser [Edmure Tully](wiki:Edmure_Tully), and that a chamber is kept for him at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)
After Catelyn's capture of [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) at the inn, Marillion rides with them to the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), intending to make a song of the adventure.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) The party is attacked by members of the [Vale mountain clans](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans) on the [high road](wiki:High_road), however, and a horse and rider land on the singer during the fighting. Tyrion steps on Marillion's hand and advises him to play dead. The singer has several broken ribs and four broken fingers on his playing hand, and his woodharp is also destroyed. Marillion takes a fine [shadowskin](wiki:Shadowskin) cloak from the clansmen's dead chief,(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Ragot31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3) which Tyrion later wins from him at [dice](wiki:Dice).(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) While the wounded Rodrik and Ser [Willis Wode](wiki:Willis_Wode) remain at the [Bloody Gate](wiki:Bloody_Gate) to recuperate, Marillion asks Catelyn to continue on with her to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie).(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
Marillion acquires a new woodharp at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie). When Tyrion tells him to make a ballad about how he was not allowed to choose his champion in a [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_combat), Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) acquiesces and allows Tyrion to select [Bronn](wiki:Bronn).(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Ragot38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Following the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Tyrion dreams of a victory feast in which Marillion sings of his valorous deeds.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Racok67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Having remained at the Eyrie, Marillion becomes the favorite of Lady Lysa and her son, Lord [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn), singing the young lord to sleep each night.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Lysa rewards him with costly clothes, a [gold](wiki:Gold) arm ring, a belt studded with [moonstones](wiki:Moonstone), a fine [horse](wiki:Horse), and the favorite [falcon](wiki:Falcon) of her late husband, [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), the [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie).(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) Marillion's songs mock other lords of the Vale, however, and he becomes disliked by many at court.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)
Marillion is present at [the wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Petyr_Baelish_and_Lysa_Arryn) of Lysa and Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) at the [Baelish tower](wiki:Drearfort) in the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers). During the feast, he performs "[The Vow Unspoken](wiki:The_Vow_Unspoken)", "[Seasons of My Love](wiki:Seasons_of_My_Love)", and "[Two Hearts That Beat as One](wiki:Two_Hearts_That_Beat_as_One)", and he then sings a bawdy version of "[Milady's Supper](wiki:Milady's_Supper)" during the [bedding](wiki:Bedding).(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) Marillion, drunk on [mead](wiki:Mead), strikes an [old blind dog](wiki:Old_blind_dog) and tries to seduce an unwilling [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), who is disguised as Petyr's [bastard](wiki:Bastard), "Alayne Stone". Ser [Lothor Brune](wiki:Lothor_Brune), who has been given orders from Petyr to watch Sansa, prevents this and wounds Marillion with his knife.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)
After Lysa's party returns to the Eyrie, Sansa feels that Marillion always sings to her when he performs during supper, and she thinks that he constantly undresses her with his eyes.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5) Marillion claims to be writing a new song about her, "[The Roadside Rose](wiki:The_Roadside_Rose)".(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6) Sansa learns that two serving girls and a page had been banished by Lysa for telling "lies" about her favorite.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-7)
Lysa sees her new husband, Lord Petyr, kissing Sansa in the [godswood of the Eyrie](wiki:Godswood_of_the_Eyrie), so she has Marillion bring Sansa to her in the [High Hall](wiki:High_Hall). She blames Sansa and also accuses her of being interested in Marillion. Lysa threatens to throw Sansa through the [Moon Door](wiki:Moon_Door), and to prevent them from being overheard, Lysa has Marillion perform "[The False and the Fair](wiki:The_False_and_the_Fair)". Petyr arrives to save Sansa from falling, however, and he reassures Lysa. Petyr then throws Lysa through the Moon Door, telling Sansa that Marillion killed Lysa.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Rasos80.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-8)

No matter where she is in the Eyrie, day and night, Sansa thinks she can hear Marillion's rich singing. Lord Robert is unable to sleep at night, and he wants Marillion to be executed by being thrown through the Moon Door.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)
Petyr tells Lord [Nestor Royce](wiki:Nestor_Royce), [High Steward of the Vale](wiki:High_Steward_of_the_Vale), his son Ser [Albar](wiki:Albar_Royce), and Ser [Marwyn Belmore](wiki:Marwyn_Belmore) that Marillion murdered Lysa after learning that she intended to send him away. As Alayne, Sansa also tells the nobles that Marillion had killed Lysa.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5) Lord Baelish has Marillion confess to them that he had killed Lysa out of jealousy. His eyes-sockets are covered with a bandage and his hands are gloved during the meeting, so Sansa cannot see his wounds. According to Nestor, his cousin [Bronze Yohn](wiki:Yohn_Royce), [Lord of Runestone](wiki:Lord_of_Runestone), will want to question Marillion as well.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6) Albar recalls that Marillion had mocked him and his father as pigs in a song, and Marwyn complains that the musician had called him "Ser Ding-Dong".(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Raffc10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-7)
Sansa later assures Lord Robert that a guilt-ridden Marillion has died by walking from the sky cells, based on information from Petyr, but Robert insists that he can still hear Marillion singing at night.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Robert has come to hate singing since his mother's death.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) [Myranda Royce](wiki:Myranda_Royce), Nestor's daughter, tells Sansa that she had willingly slept with Marillion before Lysa's death, and that the singer had been skilled with his fingers.(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

### The Winds of Winter[ ]

At the [Gates of the Moon](wiki:Gates_of_the_Moon), Sansa repeats the lie that Marillion murdered Lysa, telling the story to Ser [Lymond Lynderly](wiki:Lymond_Lynderly) and Ser [Ben Coldwater](wiki:Ben_Coldwater).(wiki:Marillion.cite_ref-14)
