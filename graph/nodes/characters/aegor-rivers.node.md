## Origins

### Early life

In [172 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Aegor was born at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to the fifth of King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen)'s mistresses, [Barba Bracken](wiki:Barba_Bracken). Aegor was born only a fortnight before Aegon's wife, Queen [Naerys Targaryen](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen), nearly died in childbirth. With Naerys lingering near death, [Barba's father](wiki:Lord_Bracken_(Hand_of_the_King)), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), began to speak openly of Barba marrying King Aegon IV. However, his words became a scandal when Queen Naerys recovered her health. Naerys's brother, Prince [Aemon the Dragonknight](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), and her son, Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), forced King Aegon to send both Barba and her [bastard](wiki:Bastard) son away from court. Aegor was raised at [Stone Hedge](wiki:Stone_Hedge) in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) by the Brackens.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

King Aegon IV was visiting Aegor at Stone Hedge in [177 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) when he met Barba's younger sister, [Bethany Bracken](wiki:Bethany_Bracken), and took her for his seventh mistress. However, Bethany's affair with Ser [Terrence Toyne](wiki:Terrence_Toyne), a [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), soon resulted in the execution of Terrence, Bethany, and her father, Aegor's grandfather.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

The angry Aegor reserved particular loathing for his half-brother [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), whose mother, [Melissa Blackwood](wiki:Melissa_Blackwood), had replaced his own as their father's favorite mistress. Aegor loathed Brynden, who was known as Bloodraven, even further when [Shiera Seastar](wiki:Shiera_Seastar), their beautiful half-sister and a fellow [Great Bastard](wiki:Great_Bastard), chose Brynden over him. Their other half-brother, [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), agreed to wed his daughter [Calla](wiki:Calla_Blackfyre) to Aegor; however, it is unknown if and when the marriage took place. Aegor often urged Daemon to press his own claim to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

### First Blackfyre Rebellion

In the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [196 AC](wiki:196_AC), Aegor sided with the pretender king [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) against King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen). During the [battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field), Aegor commanded the right of Daemon's host.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

To prevent a rout upon Daemon's death during the battle, Aegor picked up Daemon's [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword, [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre), rallied his troops, and madly charged into the [Raven's Teeth](wiki:Raven's_Teeth), the archers of Brynden Rivers. Bittersteel met Bloodraven in the middle of his charge, and a mighty duel followed. Though he took out Bloodraven's eye during their duel, Aegor ultimately fled the battlefield.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

Bittersteel fled Westeros with Daemon's widow, [Rohanne of Tyrosh](wiki:Rohanne_of_Tyrosh), her surviving children, the blade Blackfyre, and hundreds of now-landless lords and knights, eventually settling in the [Free City](wiki:Free_Cities) of [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh).(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### The Golden Company

Aegor served a year with the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons) mercenary company.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) However, as other exile lords began to join other mercenary companies and the support for the [Blackfyres](wiki:House_Blackfyre) began to scatter, Bittersteel left the Second Sons to create the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company), to bind the exiles together.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) Shortly after its creation, the Golden Company became one of the most famous and disciplined [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) companies known in the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities). They quickly established their reputation by sacking [Qohor](wiki:Qohor) when the city refused to honor its contract.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rawoiafaegor_rivers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) While sellswords are notoriously unreliable, the Golden Company has a respectable reputation. They are said to have never broken a contract.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) The war cry of the Golden Company is "Beneath the [gold](wiki:Gold), the bitter [steel](wiki:Steel),"(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) which refers to Aegor's nickname, Bittersteel.

The Golden Company was heavily involved in three of the four [Blackfyre Rebellions](wiki:Blackfyre_Pretenders) that followed the [first one](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion). However, Aegor refused to support the pretender king [Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre) during the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [211 AC](wiki:211_AC), and [maesters](wiki:Maester) of the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) often debate why. Many believe that Daemon and Lord [Gormon Peake](wiki:Gormon_Peake) could not convince Aegor that their plan was sensible and could succeed despite the odds. Some think Bittersteel was a man who had little use for anything but war and distrusted Daemon's [dreams](wiki:Dream) and love of fine things, and some think Daemon's homosexuality troubled Aegor enough to deny him his assistance.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) Bittersteel remained in [Essos](wiki:Essos) with the sword [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre), a factor in the failure of the rebellion.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

In [219 AC](wiki:219_AC), [Haegon I Blackfyre](wiki:Haegon_I_Blackfyre) and Bittersteel launched the [Third Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Third_Blackfyre_Rebellion). During the rebellion, Bloodraven and Bittersteel dueled for the second time. At the end of the battle, Haegon was killed after surrendering, but Aegor was captured and brought to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) in chains. Though Brynden Rivers and Prince [Aerion Targaryen](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen) argued that Aegor should be executed, King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen) decided to send him to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). However, the ship taking Aegor north was intercepted, and he was freed and returned to the Golden Company. Before the year was over, Aegor crowned [Daemon III Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_III_Blackfyre), Haegon I's eldest son, and resumed plotting against King Aerys in [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh).(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

During one of the Blackfyre Rebellions, Aegor swore a blood oath with [Torwyn Greyjoy](wiki:Torwyn_Greyjoy), but this [Lord Reaper of Pyke](wiki:Lord_Reaper_of_Pyke) betrayed Bittersteel to his enemies.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

In [236 AC](wiki:236_AC), Aegor landed on [Massey's Hook](wiki:Massey's_Hook) at the head of the Golden Company with Daemon III, starting the [Fourth Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Fourth_Blackfyre_Rebellion). The rebellion was crushed at the [Battle of Wendwater Bridge](wiki:Battle_of_Wendwater_Bridge), though Aegor managed to escape.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) A few years after that battle, Aegor reappeared in the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands), fighting with the Golden Company in a skirmish between Tyrosh and [Myr](wiki:Myr).(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

When Aegor died at the age of sixty-nine in [241 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), it is said he died as he had lived, with a sword in his hand and defiance upon his lips.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) On his deathbed, he commanded the men of the Golden Company to boil the flesh from his skull, [dip it in gold](wiki:Golden_skulls), and carry it before them when they crossed the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) to retake [Westeros](wiki:Westeros). The [captains-general](wiki:Captain-general) who have since led the Golden Company have followed Aegor's example.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

### Legacy

According to a semi-canon source, Aegor had no children.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-18) The war cry of the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company), "Beneath the gold, the bitter steel", pays homage to their founder.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

## Appearances & Description

Being half-[Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), Aegor had [purple eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes), but black hair. As an adult, he had a close-cropped beard, little more than a shadow on his face. He looked like a warrior, tall and muscular, but was lean and lithe like a [panther](wiki:Panther).(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rssm1467.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

Aegor's armor was well-made but plain, grey-steel and black rings. On his helm was a [horsehead](wiki:Horse) crest, with a horse's mane flowing behind. His shield displayed his personal sigil: a stallion with black [dragon](wiki:Dragon) wings, on a golden field, snorting fire.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rssm1467.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

Angry all of his life, Aegor never smiled.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rssm1467.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) Reportedly, he was a hard man who had little use for anything beyond war.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Bittersteel was easily irritated and offended, sometimes attributed to his [Bracken](wiki:House_Bracken) blood.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) Aegor especially loathed his younger half-brother, [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), and Brynden's mother, [Melissa Blackwood](wiki:Melissa_Blackwood), who had replaced Aegor's mother, [Barba Bracken](wiki:Barba_Bracken), as King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen)'s mistress.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Rssm1467.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

In [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) tells Ser [Arys Oakheart](wiki:Arys_Oakheart) that the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) has broken their contract with [Myr](wiki:Myr), mentioning that the exiles are united by the dream of Bittersteel.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

[Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis) discusses the Golden Company with the exiled [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), who recalls the story of Aegor's founding of the [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) company. That night, Tyrion dreams of a battle in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) in which he fights alongside Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) and Bittersteel with [dragons](wiki:Dragon) overhead. Tyrion kills his father, Lord [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), and brother, Ser [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), in the dream.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) When Tyrion joins the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons), [Tybero Istarion](wiki:Tybero_Istarion) informs him that Bittersteel had served with that company before establishing the Golden Company.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

Aegor's [skull](wiki:Golden_skulls), along with those of successive [captains-general](wiki:Captain-general), is still in possession of the Golden Company. All are kept in the tent of the current captain-general, Ser [Harry Strickland](wiki:Harry_Strickland), with Aegor's displayed in the center on a tall pike. [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) sees Aegor's grinning skull when he meets with the Golden Company in [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys).(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) During the [landing of the Golden Company](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company) in the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands), Jon realizes that Harry is a much more cautious leader than Aegor.(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

Barristan recalls how bloodshed resulted from Aegor and [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) both having loved [Shiera Seastar](wiki:Shiera_Seastar).(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### The Winds of Winter

At the [crofters' village](wiki:Crofters'_village), Ser [Justin Massey](wiki:Justin_Massey) advises [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) to regroup across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) and raise a new army as Bittersteel did after the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field). Stannis refuses, however, as Aegor was a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) who never succeeded in putting any of [Daemon Blackfyre's](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre) sons on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Aegor_Rivers.cite_ref-TheonI_21-0)
