## Origins

Aerion was born the second son to Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) and his wife, Lady [Dyanna Dayne](wiki:Dyanna_Dayne). He had one older brother, [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), two younger brothers, [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) and [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), and two younger sisters, [Daella](wiki:Daella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Maekar_I)) and [Rhae](wiki:Rhae_Targaryen).(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

Aerion owned a [dragon egg](wiki:Dragon_egg) that was gold and silver.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Aerion threw Aegon's pet [cat](wiki:Cat) down a well, though he claimed otherwise. Aerion once visited Aegon in his bedroom during the dead of night, and put a knife to Aegon's privates and joked about [removing his genitals](wiki:Eunuch) so Aegon would become a sister he could marry.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-7)

### Ashford Tourney

In [209 AC](wiki:209_AC) Aerion traveled to [Ashford](wiki:Ashford) for the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow), where he initially mistook Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) for a stable boy, ordering him to take his horse, and scoffing at him when Dunk said he was a [knight](wiki:Knight). During the tourney itself, once Aerion entered the field he seemed about to challenge his cousin Prince [Valarr Targaryen](wiki:Valarr_Targaryen) to a joust, but instead challenged Ser [Humfrey Hardyng](wiki:Humfrey_Hardyng). Dunk was surprised to hear his squire, Egg (secretly Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen)), shout at Humfrey to kill Aerion. However, Aerion lowered his lance and instead of striking Humfrey's shield, impaled his horse, causing it to crash and fall atop Humfrey. The horse died in agony, and Humfrey's leg was injured. Though Aerion's actions were called an accident, Egg was certain it was intentional, but Dunk found it difficult to believe that a knight and a Targaryen prince could be so unchivalrous. [Lord Ashford](wiki:Lord_Ashford) awarded Aerion's [charger](wiki:Horse) to Humfrey as payment for the death of his horse, which infuriated Aerion.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-8)

On the tourney grounds that night, a puppet show portraying a [knight](wiki:Knight) slaying a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) offended Aerion, as the prince felt he was a dragon. Aerion had his [men-at-arms](wiki:Man-at-arms) destroy the puppeteers' stall and puppets, and he deliberately broke the fingers of one of the puppeteers, a girl named [Tanselle](wiki:Tanselle). Duncan, who Egg had called for help when the commotion started, then attacked Aerion, punching him and kicking him in the stomach and teeth. Aerion's men apprehended Dunk, and Aerion ordered them to break his teeth with a hammer and cut open his belly. Egg then arrived and revealed himself as Prince Aegon, ordering them to stop. They obeyed, but Duncan was arrested for striking a prince of the blood.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-9)

Prince [Baelor Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), crown prince and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), visited Duncan in his cell, and told him that his nephew Aerion had been telling his father Prince [Maekar](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) that the puppet show was [treason](wiki:Treason) against [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), an incitement to revolt. As Aegon's other brother Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) had been telling his father a falsehood that Aegon had been kidnapped by a robber knight, Duncan's situation was particularly dire. Aerion was demanding his head, which Prince Baelor would not allow, but could not deny Aerion a trial. Baelor reminded Duncan that the last time a man was found guilty of striking one of royal blood, his hand was cut off. Baelor further reminded Duncan that a [knight](wiki:Knight) could request a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) instead of one by judgement.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-10)

However, upon hearing Duncan's request, Aerion demanded a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) instead, which Prince Baelor could not deny. Aerion knew that the [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight) Duncan would find it difficult to find six other knights to fight with him, whereas he would have the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) and two Targaryen princes on his side. Aerion bribed Ser [Steffon Fossoway](wiki:Steffon_Fossoway), one of Duncan's few acquaintances at Ashford, to join his side of the trial by promising him [lordship](wiki:Lord). In total, Aerion's side consisted of his brother Prince Daeron, his father Prince Maekar, Steffon Fossoway, and three knights of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), [Willem Wylde](wiki:Willem_Wylde), [Donnel of Duskendale](wiki:Donnel_of_Duskendale), and [Roland Crakehall](wiki:Roland_Crakehall_(Kingsguard)). Aerion's uncle, Prince Baelor, joined Duncan's side, as did Ser Humfrey Hardyng, Ser [Humfrey Beesbury](wiki:Humfrey_Beesbury) (Hardyng's brother-in-law), Ser [Robyn Rhysling](wiki:Robyn_Rhysling) (an acquaintance of Aegon), Ser [Raymun Fossoway](wiki:Raymun_Fossoway) (Steffon's cousin), and Ser [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon).(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-11)

During the trial, Aerion put his lance through Duncan's side, and struck him from the saddle with a morningstar. However, Duncan refused to yield, and used his skills as a brawler from [Flea Bottom](wiki:Flea_Bottom) to beat the prince down, hitting him in the head with his shield until the crest of his helm broke off. When Duncan threatened his eye with a dagger, Aerion yielded, and withdrew his accusation. Duncan forced Aerion to repeat his submission before [Lord Ashford](wiki:Lord_Ashford), ending the threat to Dunk's other champions. However, Baelor died from injuries sustained in the trial, as did the two Humfreys.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-12)

After the conclusion of the tournament, Maekar sent Aerion to [Lys](wiki:Lys), in hopes that a few years in the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities) would change him for the better.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-13)

### Later Life

Aerion stayed in [Lys](wiki:Lys) for a few years. According to a semi-canon source, he may have fathered some [bastard](wiki:Bastard) children while living there, and his descendants may still be living in Lys.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-12) Aerion served with the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons) for a time while in exile.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Aerion returned to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) at some point, and he fought in the [Third Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Third_Blackfyre_Rebellion) in [219 AC](wiki:219_AC). Aerion performed some actions there that are "known well". After Ser [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) was captured, Aerion and the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), urged King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen) to put Aegor to death, but Aerys decided to send Bittersteel to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) instead.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) According to a semi-canon source, at some point after Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I))'s death, Aerion was appointed as [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) by his father, King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-aerion_5-1)

Aerion was married to his cousin, [Daenora Targaryen](wiki:Daenora_Targaryen).(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Together, they had one child, a son ominously named [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerion)), born in [232 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7) The same year as his son's birth, a drunken Aerion died screaming after drinking a cup of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire), believing it would transform him into a [dragon](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8)(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) A year later, King Maekar died, and in the [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) Aerion's son Maegor was passed over in the succession as it was feared that the boy might have inherited his father's cruelty and madness.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9)(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

### Legacy

Aerion is remembered as having been monstrous.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) enjoyed [Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan)'s gruesome tale about the "The Prince Who Thought He Was a Dragon".(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

## Appearances & Description

In his youth, Aerion was slim and of average height.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) He was the perfect image of a Targaryen: beautiful,(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafaerion_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) with curly silver-gold hair, [deep violet eyes](wiki:Purple_eyes), and pale unblemished skin. Aerion's face was sculptured and imperious, with a high brow, sharp cheekbones, and a straight nose.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Aerion frequently wore clothing as bright as fire, in red and yellow and gold, and a black cloak bordered in scarlet [satin](wiki:Satin). One of his doublets was made of red velvet, with long dagged sleeves.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) In [tourneys](wiki:Tourney), Aerion wore a surcoat woven with a swirling pattern of smoke and fire, and his black helm had a crest of red enamel flames. His lance was gold-tipped, and painted in stripes of red, orange, and yellow. Even his horse was caparisoned in slashed orange and red fabric to look like flames.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

Aerion was one of the most outstanding examples of [the madness](wiki:Targaryen_madness) that can affect the members of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) Although he was a skilled(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafaerion_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) and puissant [knight](wiki:Knight), Aerion was capricious,(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) cruel, and arrogant, even thinking he was a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) in human form.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4)(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) According to [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), Aerion was a dabbler in [the black arts](wiki:Magic).(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)

Aerion was despised by his younger brother [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), who was a target of much of his cruelty and considered Aerion an inveterate liar. Aegon went so far as to shave his head so as not to look like Aerion.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5) According to [Raymun Fossoway](wiki:Raymun_Fossoway), Aerion was all smiles and courtesy in front of his father, [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen), but the vain and cruel Aerion's true nature showed in front of others. Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) thought of his brother as "quite the monster".(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-6) Some at court considered Aerion to be a fright.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

At [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) and [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), discuss why Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) chose to come to the [Wall](wiki:Wall). Jeor mentions Aemon's brother, Aerion, and how he died.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

### A Storm of Swords

In [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), [Olenna Tyrell](wiki:Olenna_Tyrell) mentions Aerion when asking [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) about the personality of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

At [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), [Alester Florent](wiki:Alester_Florent) mentions to [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) the tragedies that have come from those who sought to bring back [dragons](wiki:Dragon), including Aerion.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Later, [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) tells his wife, [Selyse Florent](wiki:Selyse_Florent), of more such tragedies, again including Aerion's death.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

In [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) tells [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) that he thinks King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) believed his [wildfire plot](wiki:Wildfire_plot) would transform him into a dragon, like Aerion also believed.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Nearing death and seeing dragons in his dreams, Maester Aemon recalls how all three of his brothers dreamed of dragons and how the dreams killed each of them.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Outside [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), [Inkpots](wiki:Inkpots) shows [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) the book of the [Second Sons](wiki:Second_Sons) and mentions some of the famous men who have been members of that [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) company, including Aerion.(wiki:Aerion_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Named verbatim in the Second Sons' leather-bound company book — "The Bright Prince, Aerion Targaryen, he was a Second Son." — ADWD Tyrion XII, `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-tyrion-12.md:81`
