## Origins

The constant, though largely unintentional, slights suffered by the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) during the reign of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), led to a revolt by the Faith against the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). The High Septon spoke out against the [polygamous](wiki:Polygamy) marriage of Prince Maegor to [Alys Harroway](wiki:Alys_Harroway). To appease him, Aenys exiled Maegor, and named [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Murmison](wiki:Murmison) as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

The final straw was in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC), when King Aenys had his daughter, Princess [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), marry his son and heir, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)). The High Septon condemned the marriage as an obscenity, warning that children born of it would be "abominations in the sight of gods and men", and expelled Septon Murmison for performing the wedding ceremony. Aenys responded by sending a letter to the High Septon, explaining the history of [incestuous](wiki:Incest) marriage among [Valyrians](wiki:Valyrians), and asking for "good Murmison" to be reinstated. The High Septon responded to the king's request with a blistering denunciation, addressing "King Abomination" as a pretender and a tyrant, with no right to rule the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Pious [lords](wiki:Lord), and even [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) who once loved Aenys, turned against him, beginning the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising).(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) An envoy of the [Iron Bank](wiki:Iron_Bank) of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), sent to treat with Lord [Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower), wrote to the bank that the High Septon was "the true king of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), in all but name".(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

The conflict continued during the reign of King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), who in [42 AC](wiki:42_AC) burned the [Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Sept_of_Remembrance) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to the ground, and killed seven hundred [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons). Maegor then commanded the [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) to disarm, and when this decree had no effect, commanded his loyal lords to disperse them by force. In response, the High Septon called upon "true and pious children of the gods" to take up arms in defense of the Faith, and put an end to the reign of "dragons and monsters and abominations."(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

Later in [42 AC](wiki:42_AC), after the wedding of Maegor to his third wife, [Tyanna of the Tower](wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower), the High Septon denounced "the abomination and his whores."(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) And when Princess [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)) gave birth to her brother [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I))'s daughters, [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) and [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon)), the High Septon proclaimed that they too were abominations, the fruits of lust and [incest](wiki:Incest), and accursed of the gods.(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

In [43 AC](wiki:43_AC), Maegor outlawed the Faith Militant, and commanded the High Septon to deliver himself to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), to stand trial for high treason. In response, His High Holiness commanded the king to present himself to the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept) in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), to beg the forgiveness of the gods for his sins and cruelties.(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) Later that year,(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-5) Maegor and his mother, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), atop [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) and [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), burned down the seats of pious lords in the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) and the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), and then turned towards Oldtown. The High Septon called down the wrath of the gods on the Targaryens, as the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) of Oldtown, led by Ser [Morgan Hightower](wiki:Morgan_Hightower), surrounded the Starry Sept. However, the Targaryens and their [dragons](wiki:Dragon) arrived the next day, to discover the High Septon had died the previous night, and the city welcomed them.(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

The cause of High Septon's death remains debated, especially as he had been a healthy man. Some believe he took his own life, either the act of a craven afraid to face the wroth of Maegor, or a noble sacrifice to spare the people of Oldtown from [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame). Others claim [the Seven](wiki:The_Seven) struck him down for the sin of pride, for heresy, treason, or arrogance. Many more are certain he was murdered. One of the suspects is Ser Morgan Hightower, at the command of Lord [Martyn Hightower](wiki:Martyn_Hightower), who was seen entering and leaving the High Septon's privy chambers the night of his death, and was the only Warrior's Son ever pardoned by Maegor. Others point to [Patrice Hightower](wiki:Patrice_Hightower), Lord Martyn's maiden aunt and a reputed witch, who had sought an audience with His High Holiness at dusk, though he was alive when she departed. Other suspects are the [archmaesters](wiki:Archmaester) of the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), through the use of the [dark arts](wiki:Sorcery), an [assassin](wiki:Assassin), or a [poisoned](wiki:Poison) scroll, as messages were sent between the Citadel and the Stary Sept during the night of the High Septon's demise. The final suspect was Queen Visenya Targaryen, another reputed sorceress.(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

Nevertheless, the death of the High Septon and the election of a new, more passive [figure](wiki:Pater) did not stop the uprising.(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reach:_oldtown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:High_Septon_(Aenys_I).cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

## Appearances & Description
