## Origins

Fredo was the son of Lysandro Rogare. He had five trueborn brothers, [Lysaro](wiki:Lysaro_Rogare), [Moredo](wiki:Moredo_Rogare), [Lotho](wiki:Lotho_Rogare), [Drako](wiki:Drako_Rogare), and [Roggerio](wiki:Roggerio_Rogare), and three trueborn sisters, [Larra](wiki:Larra_Rogare), [Lysara](wiki:Lysara_Rogare), and [Marra](wiki:Marra_Rogare) as well as sixteen bastard siblings.(wiki:Fredo_Rogare.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

After the death of his father, [Lysandro "the Magnificent"](wiki:Lysandro_Rogare), Fredo inherited his [pillow house](wiki:Pleasure_house), the [Perfumed Garden](wiki:Perfumed_Garden). When the [Rogare Bank](wiki:Rogare_Bank) ran dry in [135 AC](wiki:135_AC), Lysaro Rogare's brothers and sisters claimed to have no part in it, but many doubted their claims of innocence, and Fredo was put on trial. The Perfumed Garden was seized, even as Fredo tried to sell it, and his [slaves](wiki:Slavery) were sold. When that proved insufficient to pay more than a tenth of the debts, the Rogares themselves were sold as slaves, and Fredo's daughters were made bed slaves in the Perfumed Garden. It is written that Fredo availed himself of being able to whip his brother Lysaro when he was chained naked to a pillar before the [Temple of Trade](wiki:Temple_of_Trade).(wiki:Fredo_Rogare.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
