## Identity

Prince Aegon Targaryen was the second child and only son of Prince Rhaegar Targaryen and Princess Elia Martell (wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)). It is unconfirmed whether this character and Aegon Targaryen (Young Griff) are the same person (wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)).

## Origins

Born in late 281 AC or early 282 AC at Dragonstone in the crownlands (track_b: Born). Son of Rhaegar Targaryen and Elia Martell (track_b: Father, Mother). A comet was seen above King's Landing on the night Aegon was born (wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)). According to semi-canonical sources, the infant Aegon had fair hair and looked like a Targaryen, taking after his father Rhaegar (wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)).

## Appearances & Description

The infant Aegon had fair hair. He took after his father Rhaegar in appearance, while his sister Rhaenys took after their Dornish mother Elia Martell (wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)).

## Narrative Arc

Rhaegar believed Aegon was "the prince that was promised" and that "his is the song of ice and fire." During the Sack of King's Landing in 283 AC, Aegon was supposedly killed by Gregor Clegane, who dashed the infant's head against a wall in Maegor's Holdfast. His death, along with that of his sister Rhaenys and mother Elia, was a pivotal atrocity of Robert's Rebellion (wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)).
