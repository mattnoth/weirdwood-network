## Origins

### Birth

A comet was seen above [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) on the night Aegon was conceived, which led his father [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), to believe that Aegon was the "[prince that was promised](wiki:Prince_that_was_promised)". The comet was seen as "the bleeding star" of the prophecy. Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) was aware of Rhaegar's beliefs, though it is unclear if he shared them.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### Sack of King's Landing

Aegon was an infant during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion),(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) having been born a few months before its onset. In preparation of the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), Aegon's grandfather, King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) used him, his mother [Elia](wiki:Elia_Martell), and his sister [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) as hostages, gracelessly reminding Aegon's great-uncle, Prince [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), that the three were in Aerys's hands. Lewyn took command of ten thousand Dornishmen, but died in the battle, as did Aegon's father, [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen). Aerys became convinced that the Dornishmen had betrayed him, and refused to allow Aegon and his family to flee to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) alongside Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) and Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen). Aegon, his mother, and his sister were kept at King's Landing as hostages to ensure continued [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) loyalty.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

With Rhaegar's death, Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) became convinced that the war was effectively over, and that [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) could no longer offer a proper king to the realm. Pycelle worked to convince Aerys II to open the gates of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to the Lannister forces, secretly hoping Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) would rise to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

During the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing) a fortnight after the royal forces had left for the Trident, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) killed Aerys in the throne room of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). Jaime, though the only Kingsguard member left in the city, did not think his father Tywin, whose troops were attacking the city, would harm little Aegon and his young sister, Rhaenys. While Jaime was standing over Aerys's corpse, Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) and Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch) scaled the walls of [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) and then killed Aegon, Rhaenys, and Elia. The deaths of the children caused a dispute between Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) and the new king, [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon),(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) and also began the feud between Houses [Martell](wiki:House_Martell) and [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### Death and Aftermath

According to Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s soldiers tore Aegon from his mother's breast and dashed his head against a wall.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) told his sister, Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), that Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell) pleaded for mercy as Aegon was dashed against a wall.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Eddard had heard rumors that Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) was Aegon's murderer, and after the infant was killed Gregor had raped Elia and then put her to the sword.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) According to Eddard, these rumors were not repeated in Gregor's presence.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) According to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), Gregor's role in the deaths of Aegon and his mother was common knowledge at [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock). He heard that Gregor's hands had still been covered with Aegon's blood when he raped Elia.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

According to Tywin Lannister, he required a way to demonstrate his loyalty to the cause of [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), after having remained neutral for most of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). He claims that presenting the bodies of Aegon and his sister to the new king ensured that no one could doubt [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) had forsaken [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). Tywin claims to his son Tyrion that the murders had been done "too brutally", and that Elia's death had not been intended.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Aegon's uncle, Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), however, believes that Tywin had Elia killed as revenge for her having married Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), thereby disrupting Tywin's plans for marrying his own daughter, [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), to Rhaegar.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

Tywin presented King Robert I with the corpses of Elia, Aegon, and Rhaenys as his "token of fealty".(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) He laid the bodies beneath the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) wrapped in the crimson cloaks of [his household guards](wiki:House_Lannister_guards), which helped to hide the blood. Aegon's skull was a "red ruin", which horrified Eddard Stark,(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) and was so terrible that not even Robert could not stand its sight.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) According to Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), Aegon had been unrecognizable, as all that remained of him was a faceless horror of bone, brain, and gore with a few hanks of fair hair.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

The deaths of Aegon, Rhaenys, and Elia were met with mixed responses. Robert Baratheon was relieved that Rhaegar's children were dead, and that he did not have to be the one to give the order.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) Upon the protest of his friend, Eddard Stark, that the two children had been no more than babes, Robert named Aegon and his sister "[dragonspawn](wiki:Dragon)".(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) Eddard called the act murder, and after a quarrel with Robert, Eddard rode out from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in a cold rage, heading south to fight the last battles of the war on his own.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3) Although he had not personally seen the corpses, Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) has been haunted by the "bloody bodies" of Aegon and his sister.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) [Thoros of Myr](wiki:Thoros_of_Myr), who saw the bodies of Aegon and Rhaenys, believes that [House Clegane](wiki:House_Clegane) was "built upon dead children" and their arms should display "two bloody infants".(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

The exiled Viserys Targaryen is convinced that [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) are determined to avenge Elia and her children, and would thus rise to his cause.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) Indeed, [Doran](wiki:Doran_Martell) and Oberyn Martell, Elia's brothers, have plotted against House Lannister.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) According to [Varys](wiki:Varys), Doran's hatred for House Lannister is well known,(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Racok17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) and Oberyn, certain that Gregor had not acted on his own,(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Racok38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) wants vengeance both on Gregor and the man who gave the orders.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) [Nymeria Sand](wiki:Nymeria_Sand), one of [Oberyn's daughters](wiki:Sand_Snakes), believes that the only fitting payment for the death of Elia's children would be the death of "Lord Tywin's golden twins", Cersei and [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister).(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) Doran's daughter [Arianne](wiki:Arianne_Martell), connecting the murders of Aegon and his sister to Robert Baratheon's rise to the throne, calls Robert a monster who climbed onto his throne over the corpses of children.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

The exiled Daenerys Targaryen holds "the [Usurper](wiki:Usurper)'s dogs" responsible for the murders of Rhaenys and Aegon, and sees no difference between the responsibilities of Houses Lannister and [Stark](wiki:House_Stark) in the events.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

In his book *[The World of Ice and Fire](wiki:The_World_of_Ice_&_Fire_(Yandel))*, Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) describes the events of the Sack of King's Landing in a way that shifts the blame from Houses [Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon) and Lannister, by then allied with Robert through marriage, to other candidates, and cites supposed rumors that person responsible was King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), or that it had been Elia, to spare her children from a worse fate.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_robert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

## Appearances & Description

According to semi-canonical sources, Aegon looked like a [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), taking after his father, [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), while his sister, [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)), took after their [Dornish](wiki:Dornishmen) mother, [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell).(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-4)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rawoiafaegon_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rawoiafelia_martell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) The infant Aegon had fair hair.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

According to [Jacelyn Bywater](wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater), there are still whispers in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) that "the butchery of [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen)'s children" is one of the sins of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) and that the [gods](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) are punishing the entire city for the act.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)

In one of her visions within the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) sees a newborn Aegon nursing from the breast of [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), who is seated in a great wooden bed. [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) names the child "Aegon", stating the name is fit for a king. When Elia asks whether Rhaegar will make a song for their son, he replies that Aegon, [the prince that was promised](wiki:The_prince_that_was_promised), already has [the song of ice and fire](wiki:The_song_of_ice_and_fire). Rhaegar then claims that there must be "one more", since "the dragon has three heads".(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rawoiafdaenerys_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) Afterwards, Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) confirms their identities but points out that if Aegon was the promised prince, the promise was broken with his death. Neither have heard of "the song of ice and fire".(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Racok63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

### A Storm of Swords

During [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister)'s [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat), a wounded [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) narrates a brief account of Aegon's death to [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell). He first killed Aegon, the "screaming whelp", and then raped Elia. Gregor claims to have smashed her head with his bare hands, the same method the Mountain then uses to finish off Oberyn.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

While in [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) muses about Aegon, who would have been the sixth of his name to sit on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). She believes that, had Aegon lived, she might have married him, as he would have been closer to her in age than [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen).(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) meets "[Young Griff](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(Young_Griff))", a young man who is introduced to Tyrion as the son of the sellsword "Griff", aboard the *[Shy Maid](wiki:Shy_Maid)*. The company is headed for [Volantis](wiki:Volantis), to await the expected arrival of Daenerys together with the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) and more ships for her cause.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) After some time on the *Shy Maid*, Tyrion concludes that "Griff" is Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), and that "Young Griff" is not his son, but instead Aegon Targaryen. "Young Griff" explains his apparent survival to Tyrion while they play *[cyvasse](wiki:Cyvasse)*. According to his account, the infant killed during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing) was a [tanner's infant son](wiki:Pisswater_prince) born at [Pisswater Bend](wiki:Pisswater_Bend), a street of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). The child's mother had died at birth. The tanner sold his boy to [Varys](wiki:Varys) for a jug of [wine](wiki:Wine), since he already had other sons, but had never tasted Arbor gold. Varys arranged the swap between the two infants. Elia received the tanner's son, whom Tyrion dubs the [pisswater prince](wiki:Pisswater_prince), while Varys took custody of the real Prince Aegon.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

When news of Aegon's supposed survival reaches [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), the small council of King Tommen declare him to be a pretender. Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), however, recalls Jon from his time at [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen)'s court, and wonders whether Aegon could have survived, recalling how the babe killed by Gregor had been unrecognizable.(wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar).cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)
