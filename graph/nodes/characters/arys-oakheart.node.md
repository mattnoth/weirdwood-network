## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Arys Oakheart](wiki:Category:Images_of_Arys_Oakheart)*

Arys is a handsome man(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Rawoiafarys_oakheart.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) with light-brown hair and a comely face.(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) considers him courteous and cordial.(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) He is a fine bold knight who serves faithfully as a member of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Rawoiafarys_oakheart.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

Arys owns a tunic decorated with the golden oak tree of [House Oakheart](wiki:House_Oakheart) and his Kingsguard armor has white-enameled scales.(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Arys owns at least three white cloaks—light wool, heavy wool, and silk—which he pins with the golden leaf of his house.(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Ser Arys competes in the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney) along with the other white cloaks of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). He is present in the [throne room](wiki:Red_Keep) when the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), is dismissed, and he laughs at his former [Sworn Brother](wiki:Sworn_Brother)'s shame.(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### A Clash of Kings

While all of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon)'s Kingsguard [knights](wiki:Knight) (with the exception of [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane)) beat [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) on his orders, Sansa still regards Arys as one of the kinder knights of them, and prefers that he accompanies her over any of the others. Arys speaks politely with Sansa, and when ordered by Joffrey to hit her, he does so more lightly than the others, and once protested the order before doing so.(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) Arys accompanies Sansa to the [tourney on King Joffrey's name day](wiki:Tourney_on_King_Joffrey's_name_day), and expresses to her his confidence in his ability to win, though he admits that the competition is not as fierce as would be desired. Fond of gossip, he informs Sansa of Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s refusal to march his army to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) as Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) had commanded.(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5)

Arys is sent to [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) in [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) as the [sworn shield](wiki:Sworn_shield) of Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), who is betrothed to Prince [Trystane Martell](wiki:Trystane_Martell).(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Arys regrets having struck Sansa, and he lights a candle to [the Warrior](wiki:Warrior_(the_Seven)) in gratitude for having been chosen by [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) for the mission.(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

### A Storm of Swords

Lord Tywin Lannister informs his son Tyrion that Arys has sent a letter from Dorne, revealing Myrcella and Trystane get on well and that Myrcella has taken a great liking to Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell).(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Arys is seduced within half a year of his arrival by Arianne, the daughter and heiress of [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne).(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) She convinces him to rebel against the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and support Myrcella's claim over that of her brother, King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_I_Baratheon),(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) secretly part of an attempt to claim her own birthright.(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Arys brings Myrcella to Arianne's company of friends, and they travel to the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood), where they are confronted by Prince Doran's guards, as their conspiracy has been uncovered. Arys charges into the [Martell](wiki:House_Martell) forces, killing two of them in the process despite being injured. When his horse goes down, Arys manages to struggle free, but [Areo Hotah](wiki:Areo_Hotah), Prince Doran's captain of guards, reaches him and kills him with his axe when Arys raises his sword too slowly.(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

Later, during her time of captivity, Princess Arianne has nightmares about Arys's death. When summoned before her father, Prince Doran informs her that Ser [Balon Swann](wiki:Balon_Swann), Arys's Sworn Brother, is traveling towards Sunspear, and is expecting to see Arys upon arrival. To hide the truth of Arys's death from Swann, Arianne tells her father they should say that Arys died defending Myrcella.(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

The Dornish court informs Ser Balon Swann that Arys was killed by Ser [Gerold Dayne](wiki:Gerold_Dayne).(wiki:Arys_Oakheart.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
