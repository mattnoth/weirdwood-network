## Appearances & Description

One of Larys's feet was twisted at birth, so he dragged it with a limp.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
Larys rarely spoke, preferring to listen. When he did so it was either to be glib or share words of great importance.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) The Clubfoot was an enigmatic and cunning man.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Although he could be convincing and amiable when needed,(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) he kept his own counsel and was largely friendless.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
Historians are still baffled as to exactly what Clubfroot's loyalties and goals were during the Dance of the Dragons. He does not seem to have been absolutely loyal to Aegon II, so much as he aligned with him against their mutual enemy Rhaenyra, who had shamed House Strong in her affair with his older brother.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

## Origins

### Reign of Viserys I[ ]

In [105 AC](wiki:105_AC), Larys was brought to court by his father, Lord [Lyonel Strong](wiki:Lyonel_Strong), alongside his older brother, Ser [Harwin](wiki:Harwin_Strong), and two sisters. Larys joined the confessors of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
In [120 AC](wiki:120_AC), Lyonel and Harwin died in a fire at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), leaving Larys to inherit his father's seat and title as [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal). Although the cause of the fire was never determined, one of the suggestions to have been put forth was that Larys had caused the fire.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands:_house_tully.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Other suggested culprits were Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), King Viserys I himself, and the curse of [Black Harren](wiki:Harren_Hoare).(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)
Larys eventually became the [Lord Confessor](wiki:Lord_Confessor) and [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers) on Viserys's [small council](wiki:Small_council).(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

### Dance of the Dragons[ ]

When King Viserys I died in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), Larys was part of the [green faction](wiki:Greens) at court. He attended the meeting of the [small council](wiki:Small_council) which agreed to support [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) against his older half-sister, [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen). Larys cut his palm and had the male conspirators in attendance swear a blood oath.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
When the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) broke out, Larys' castle was taken by Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) in the [assault on Harrenhal](wiki:Assault_on_Harrenhal). Aegon's mother, Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower), allegedly ordered Larys to torture [Blood](wiki:Blood_(the_butcher)) after the murder of Prince [Jaehaerys Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_II)).(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) The Clubfoot drew up lists of [blacks](wiki:Blacks) whom Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) could march against.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
Larys escaped capture during the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) to Rhaenyra in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC).(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) He spirited Aegon, who was still recovering from the [battle at Rook's Rest](wiki:Battle_at_Rook's_Rest), and the king's two remaining children, Prince [Maelor](wiki:Maelor_Targaryen) and Princess [Jaehaera](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen), from the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) through a [secret passage](wiki:Secret_passage) which led them out of the capital while bypassing the city's gates. Larys insisted the group part ways and ordered Ser [Rickard Thorne](wiki:Rickard_Thorne) of Aegon's [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) to bring Maelor to Lord [Lyonel Hightower](wiki:Lyonel_Hightower) at [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), and Ser [Willis Fell](wiki:Willis_Fell) of the Kingsguard to escort Jaehaera to Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End). Larys disguised King Aegon as a commoner and sent him to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) under the care of Ser [Marston Waters](wiki:Marston_Waters), believing her own stronghold would be the last place Rhaenyra would think to search for her half brother.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
On campaign in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) believed Larys had somehow aided Rhaenyra in the capital's fall. Aegon's brother had every member of [House Strong](wiki:House_Strong) at Harrenhal killed.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
Larys remained in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), strategically working in secret to undermine Rhaenyra's hold on the capital. When Queen [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen) committed suicide, a rumor was spread throughout the city that she was murdered on the orders of Rhaenyra. [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) in his writings accused Larys of being the source for this slander.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)
The massive [riot of King's Landing](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) led to Rhaenyra abandoning the capital. When Ser [Perkin the Flea](wiki:Perkin_the_Flea) and his followers gained control of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) and crowned [Trystane Truefyre](wiki:Trystane_Truefyre) during the [Moon of the Three Kings](wiki:Moon_of_the_Three_Kings), Larys emerged and walked into the Red Keep, where he was greeted by Perkin and given a place of honor at the side of Trystane.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)
Larys feigned support to the pretender Trystane and secured the release of Alicent, Grand Maester [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle), and Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon). When the host of Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) arrived outside King's Landing, Larys offered to treat with the [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End) on Trystane's behalf. Larys instead conspired with Perkin and the greens to betray Trystane and give the city to supporters of Aegon II. A full pardon was promised to Perkin and all his followers in exchange for their aid in restoring the capital to King Aegon, who had killed Rhaenyra after the [fall of Dragonstone](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone). However, the pardon was not extended to Trystane, who was later executed. Larys agreed to a betrothal with one of Borros's daughters, [Floris Baratheon](wiki:Floris_Baratheon). When Alicent advocated that Corlys should be killed for having supported Rhaenrya, Larys pointed out that her son, Aegon, was on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) surrounded by a [Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) fleet. The Clubfoot suggested to Alicent they pardon Corlys and wait until the war's end before quietly disposing of him.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)
During a meeting of the council after Aegon's return, Corlys Velaryon was outraged by the suggestion of Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister) to execute Prince [Aegon the Younger](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), Rhaenyra's surviving son who was held captive by the greens. Lord Strong convinced Aegon II not to punish Corlys, as that would make [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) an enemy. After the meeting, Larys discreetly alerted Corlys of the plot to eventually murder him, and the Clubfoot persuaded the Sea Snake to be part of a scheme of his own devising.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)
After the defeat of Lord Borros and his army at the [Battle of the Kingsroad](wiki:Battle_of_the_Kingsroad), King Aegon declined Lord Velaryon's recommendation to surrender. Larys had Princess [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen) clandestinely spirited away so she could not be used as a hostage against Corlys. Aegon II was soon found dead with blood on his lips, murdered by [poison](wiki:Poison). Larys and Corlys had the few loyalists remaining either killed or imprisoned and persuaded the remaining greens to surrender as the [Lads](wiki:Lads) descended on the capital.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4)
Aegon II was succeeded as king by Aegon the Younger, who wed Princess Jaehaera.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) When the [riverlords](wiki:Riverlands) reached King's Landing, they were joyously greeted by [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk), a welcome presumably devised by the Clubfoot.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

### Hour of the Wolf[ ]

Fortunes turned against Lord Strong and his conspirators with the arrival of the [northern](wiki:North) army of Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark), who served as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to the new King Aegon III Targaryen during the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf). Despite having been a supporter of Rhaenyra, Cregan objected to the murder of Aegon II. Grand Maester Orwyle confessed to having given Larys the [poison](wiki:Poison) used to kill Aegon, and Ser Perkin confessed to being the Clubfoot's agent. While Lord Corlys admitted to having conspired against Aegon for the good of the Seven Kingdoms, Larys refused to confirm or deny Cregan's accusations. Lord Stark condemned both of the great lords, but [Alysanne Blackwood](wiki:Alysanne_Blackwood) convinced him to spare Lord Velaryon.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)
While most of those found guilty followed Perkin in [taking the black](wiki:Night's_Watch), Lord Larys and Ser Gyles of the Kingsguard chose death over the [Wall](wiki:Wall). Cregan beheaded the pair with his [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) greatsword, [Ice](wiki:Ice). Cregan granted Larys' last request, that his clubfoot be removed from his corpse so Larys would at least be free of it in death.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)
The heads of Larys and Gyles were mounted on pikes by the gates of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). Lord Strong's corpse was given to the [silent sisters](wiki:Silent_sisters), and years later his bones were taken to [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) as his final resting place. Cregan ordered that the removed foot be buried in a field, but the limb disappeared before that could occur.(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6) As Larys was the last of his line, his death brought an end to [House Strong](wiki:House_Strong).(wiki:Larys_Strong.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
