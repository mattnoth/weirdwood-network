## Identity

Tyanna of the Tower — Tyanna, a pale raven-haired beauty from Pentos, arrived with Alys Harroway and six hundred sellswords.

## Origins

Tyanna was rumored to be the [natural](wiki:Bastardy) daughter of a [magister](wiki:Magister) of [Pentos](wiki:Pentos). She began as a tavern dancer and rose to become a [courtesan](wiki:Courtesan), although some claimed she was also a [poisoner](wiki:Poison) and a [sorceress](wiki:Magic).(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

Prince [Maegor Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) was exiled to Pentos in [39 AC](wiki:39_AC), but he returned to the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) to claim the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [42 AC](wiki:42_AC) amid the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising). His rule was challenged with a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven); Maegor won but was left grievously wounded. Twenty-eight days later, [Alys Harroway](wiki:Alys_Harroway), Maegor's second wife, returned to Westeros in the company of Tyanna; the Pentoshi woman was rumored to be Maegor's concubine or Alys's [paramour](wiki:Paramour).(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10) The Dowager Queen, [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), after meeting with Tyanna, gave the king over to her care alone, which troubled Maegor's supporters.(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

After awakening from his coma, Maegor burned the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons) at the [Sept of Remembrance](wiki:Sept_of_Remembrance) atop the [Hill of Rhaenys](wiki:Hill_of_Rhaenys) and waged war on the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant). After his victory in the [battle at the Great Fork](wiki:Battle_at_the_Great_Fork) of the [Blackwater](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), Maegor returned to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and announced his intention to take Tyanna as his third wife. When Grand Maester [Myros](wiki:Myros) objected, claiming that the king's only true wife was [Ceryse Hightower](wiki:Ceryse_Hightower), Maegor slew the man with his [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword, [Blackfyre](wiki:Blackfyre). Tyanna married Maegor atop the Hill of Rhaenys, and Alys is said to have joined the pair in bed on their first night of marriage.(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

Tyanna served as [mistress of whisperers](wiki:Mistress_of_whisperers) on her husband's [small council](wiki:Small_council) and was dubbed "the king's [raven](wiki:Raven)". It was said that discussing secrets in the Red Keep was foolish, as rats and other vermin were her spies.(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-12)

The Dowager Queen also died in [44 AC](wiki:44_AC). In the confusion after Visenya's death, [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen)'s widow, Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon), slipped away from [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) with her children, as well as with the sword [Dark Sister](wiki:Dark_Sister). Alyssa and Aenys's second son, Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), had been kept at the Red Keep as Maegor's squire, however, and he suffered for her flight. Viserys died after nine days of questioning at the hands of Tyanna of the Tower.(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-13)

When Queen Alys gave birth to a monstrosity in [44 AC](wiki:44_AC), Queen Tyanna convinced Maegor that it was due to Alys having secret affairs. Maegor disbelieved Tyanna and called her a barren, jealous witch, whereupon Tyanna provided the king with a list of twenty men she claimed had slept with Alys.(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-14) This led to the extinction of [House Harroway](wiki:House_Harroway),(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) and the torture and execution of dozens of alleged lovers. Tyanna tortured Alys herself for near a fortnight until the queen died.(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-15)

Tyanna was unable to give Maegor an heir, so the king wed the three [Black Brides](wiki:Black_Brides)—[Elinor Costayne](wiki:Elinor_Costayne), [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling_(wife_of_Maegor_I)), and [Rhaena Targaryen](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I))—in [47 AC](wiki:47_AC). Tyanna forced Rhaena's obedience by threatening her daughters, [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen) and [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon)). Some stories claim that Tyanna gave Jeyne a fertility potion that night, but the new bride tossed it into Tyanna's face.(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-16)

In [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), Queen Jeyne birthed a stillborn monstrosity, and rumors spread that Maegor was cursed. The angry king had two of his [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), Ser [Owen Bush](wiki:Owen_Bush) and Ser [Maladon Moore](wiki:Maladon_Moore), bring Tyanna to the dungeons. As the torturers prepared their tools, she confessed her responsibility for the two abominations born of Alys Harroway and Jeyne Westerling, claiming she had [poisoned](wiki:Poison) the babies in their mothers' wombs. Tyanna also promised that the same fate would befall the pregnant Elinor Costayne. Tyanna was killed by Maegor's own hand, her heart cut out with Blackfyre and thrown to his dogs;(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-17) Maladon stood witness to her death.(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Two moons later Elinor birthed a stillborn monstrosity.(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-18)

After Maegor died on the Iron Throne in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC), his [headsman](wiki:Headsman), gaolers, and confessors were condemned by King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) for having aided Tyanna in the death of his elder brother, Prince Viserys.(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

## Appearances & Description

Tyanna was a pale beauty with black hair the color of a [raven](wiki:Raven).(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7) Her name may come from the fact that [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) is known for its square brick towers.(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Radwd1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

Tyanna is said to have dabbled in [sorcery](wiki:Magic) and [alchemy](wiki:Alchemy).(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) According to some rumors, she was not loved by her mother-in-law, Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen). She is remembered as a black-hearted woman who was hated and feared.(wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)
