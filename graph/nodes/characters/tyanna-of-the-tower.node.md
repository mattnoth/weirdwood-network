## Identity

Queen Tyanna of the Tower, also known as Tyanna of Pentos, was a courtesan from Pentos who married King Maegor I Targaryen, becoming the third of his six wives (wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower, cite: Fire & Blood).

## Origins

Tyanna was rumored to be the natural daughter of a magister of Pentos. She began as a tavern dancer and rose to become a courtesan, although some claimed she was also a poisoner and a sorceress (wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower).

## Appearances & Description

Tyanna was a pale beauty with black hair the color of a raven (wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower). She is said to have dabbled in sorcery and alchemy. She is remembered as a black-hearted woman who was hated and feared (wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower).

## Narrative Arc

When Prince Maegor returned from exile in Pentos in 42 AC to claim the Iron Throne, his second wife Alys Harroway returned to Westeros in Tyanna's company; Tyanna was rumored to be Maegor's concubine or Alys's paramour. After Maegor was grievously wounded in a trial of seven, Dowager Queen Visenya Targaryen gave the king over to Tyanna's care alone. After Maegor awoke, he announced his intention to take Tyanna as his third wife and named her his mistress of whisperers (wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower, cite: Fire & Blood).

Tyanna served as Maegor's spymaster and interrogator. She eventually confessed under torture to having poisoned and deformed the children of Maegor's other wives. King Maegor killed her himself, cutting out her heart with Blackfyre. She died in 48 AC in King's Landing (wiki:Tyanna_of_the_Tower).
