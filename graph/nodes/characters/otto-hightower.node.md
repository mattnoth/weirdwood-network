## Origins

### Early Service

In [101 AC](wiki:101_AC), after the death of Prince [Baelon Targaryen](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), the son and heir of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Jaehaerys called upon Ser Otto Hightower to be his new Hand. Ser Otto brought his wife and children to court in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing),(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) including his fifteen-year-old daughter [Alicent](wiki:Alicent_Hightower).(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rtrotd64.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) King Jaehaerys was devastated by the deaths of Baelon and his beloved wife, Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), becoming grief-stricken, and with his health failing he became bedridden. Otto effectively ruled the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) as Hand in the final two years of Jaehaerys's reign, while his daughter Alicent nursed the ailing king.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

Otto continued as Hand to [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), Jaehaerys's grandson, who succeeded to the throne in [103 AC](wiki:103_AC). It was said that the longer Otto served, the more arrogant he became, and many lords resented him and envied his access to the king. Otto became a bitter rival of the king's brother and presumptive heir, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen),(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-11) who had been named [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin). Otto convinced the king to remove Daemon from office, and did so again when Viserys named Daemon [master of laws](wiki:Master_of_laws). However, Otto came to regret this when Daemon was named [commander of the City Watch](wiki:Commander_of_the_City_Watch), as this put two thousand men under Daemon's command and the prince thrived in this new office. Otto feared Daemon would become a second [Maegor the Cruel](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) if he ever ascended to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), sharing his fears with his [brother](wiki:Lord_Hightower), the [Lord of the Hightower](wiki:Lord_of_the_Hightower). Thus, at this time, Otto wished for Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) to succeed her father (despite the decision of the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC), which many believed had established the precedent that the throne could not pass to a woman). Viserys often tried to make peace between Otto and Daemon, but the acrimony between the two still continued.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

After the death in childbirth of Queen [Aemma Arryn](wiki:Aemma_Arryn) in [106 AC](wiki:106_AC), King Viserys formally declared Princess Rhaenyra to be the heir to the Iron Throne, and also chose Otto's daughter Alicent to be his second wife. Although Queen Alicent soon gave birth to a son, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), in [107 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Viserys did nothing to change the order of succession, feeling the matter had been settled. In [109 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Otto was removed as Hand for "hectoring" Viserys to replace Rhaenyra as heir with Aegon. Having no place at court anymore, Otto returned to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown). However, Otto's objectives remained at court with the formation of a "queen's party", known as the [greens](wiki:Greens), a group of powerful [lords](wiki:Lord) who were friends with Queen Alicent and who supported the rights of Alicent's sons.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9)

In [120 AC](wiki:120_AC), Otto's replacement as Hand, Lord [Lyonel Strong](wiki:Lyonel_Strong), died at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal). [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Mellos](wiki:Mellos) suggested a younger man to replace him, but King Viserys preferred familiarity, and Otto returned to the position. At court, the enmity between the greens, Queen Alicent's supporters, and the [blacks](wiki:Blacks), Princess Rhaenyra's supporters, continued to grow. On the first day of [127 AC](wiki:127_AC), King Viserys held a feast to celebrate the return of his health. In a show of reconciliation, Rhaenyra and Alicent wore each other's colors, and they and their families made many declarations of love to each other. Prince Daemon toasted Ser Otto, thanking him for his loyal service as Hand, while Otto spoke of Daemon's courage in his toast. However, after the king had left the feast, the conflict re-emerged.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-10)

Over the last decade of Viserys I's reign, Otto largely succeeded in stacking the [small council](wiki:Small_council) with his own supporters. Though the exact timeline of when these men joined the council is unclear, by the last year of Viserys's reign in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), the council was primarily composed of greens: Lord [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong) was [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers), Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister) was [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships), and Lord [Jasper Wylde](wiki:Jasper_Wylde) was [master of laws](wiki:Master_of_laws). Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole), [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), was already a fervent member of Alicent's faction, while Grand Maester [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle) was considered politically neutral (in part due to his vows as a [maester](wiki:Maester)). The only holdout who opposed them was old Lord [Lyman Beesbury](wiki:Lyman_Beesbury), who had served as [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) since late in the reign of Jaehaerys I. By the second month of [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), King Viserys was often too ill to rule, and preferred to leave matters of state to Ser Otto.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-11)

When Viserys died in his sleep on the third day of the third month of [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), Otto played a crucial role in crowning his eldest grandson as King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), in defiance of the last will of Viserys I, which specified Rhaenyra as the successor. This defiance precipitated the civil war known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons).(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

### The Dance of the Dragons

In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), the morning that King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) was found dead, Queen [Alicent](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) and Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) had the [small council](wiki:Small_council) members summoned to the queen's apartments. [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle) began with talk of measures to take upon the king's death and preparations for [Rhaenyra's](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) coronation, but Otto Hightower cut him off, saying they first had to decide the succession. Ser Otto told the council that as Hand of the King, he would rule over the Seven Kingdoms until King [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) was crowned. Otto asserted that Rhaenyra could not be allowed to become queen, as her husband, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), would be the true ruler, and immediately execute Alicent and Otto himself. In the debate that followed, only Lord [Lyman Beesbury](wiki:Lyman_Beesbury) insisted Rhaenyra should be crowned as queen, and attempted to leave. According to Orwyle, Otto then ordered the arrest and confinement of the elderly Lord Beesbury in the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells), where he died of a chill.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

After Beesbury was dealt with, the green council made their plans, and swore a blood oath (on the prompting of Lord [Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong)) asserting their loyalty to each other and to their new king. Ser Otto ordered the arrest of all those in King's Landing who could be loyal to Rhaenyra. Otto appointed his son, Ser [Gwayne Hightower](wiki:Gwayne_Hightower), as the second in command of the [gold cloaks](wiki:Gold_cloaks), instructing him to watch [Commander](wiki:Commander_of_the_City_Watch) [Luthor Largent](wiki:Luthor_Largent) for any signs of disloyalty towards King Aegon II. While Otto judged Rhaenyra's potential supporters and forces, [ravens](wiki:Raven) were sent to all the lords and knights who might be loyal to Aegon. Otto also sent a raven to Lord [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), offering the position of [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships) for his allegiance and the use of his fleet.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

When Ser [Steffon Darklyn](wiki:Steffon_Darklyn) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) and other [black loyalists](wiki:Blacks) escaped from King's Landing with King Viserys's [crown](wiki:Crown), Otto offered a reward for their capture, calling them [turncloaks](wiki:Turncloak). Meanwhile, Rhaenyra remained on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), and only learned of her [usurpation](wiki:Usurper) after Aegon's coronation. After Ser Steffon arrived on Dragonstone, she was crowned herself as Queen Rhaenyra I Targaryen. Her first act was was to declare Ser Otto Hightower and Queen Alicent [traitors](wiki:Traitor) and rebels.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)

Otto sent his grandson, Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen), to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) upon his [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), to secure the allegiance of Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) to the greens by proposing a [betrothal](wiki:Betrothal) to one of Borros's [four daughters](wiki:Four_Storms).(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-6) He was successful, but when Prince [Lucerys Velaryon](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon), Rhaenyra's son, arrived to offer Lord Borros an alliance with the queen, Aemond responded belligerently, as Lucerys had injured him years before. They [fought above](wiki:Fight_above_Shipbreaker_Bay) [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay) upon their dragons, resulting in the death of Lucerys and [Arrax](wiki:Arrax). Otto was not pleased when Aemond returned to King's Landing, saying, “You only lost one eye. How could you be so blind?”(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Prince Daemon Targaryen vowed revenge for his stepson's death, and sent [assassins](wiki:Assassin) to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), the butcher called [Blood](wiki:Blood_(butcher)) and the [ratcatcher](wiki:Rat) known as [Cheese](wiki:Cheese_(ratcatcher)). They killed King Aegon's son, Prince [Jaehaerys Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_II)) before escaping. (While Blood was caught and [tortured](wiki:Torture) to death, Cheese was never found, and in fury, Aegon ordered the execution of all of King's Landing's ratcatchers. Otto brought a hundred [cats](wiki:Cat) to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) to take their place.) After the deaths of the two princes, Lucerys and Jaehaerys, both sides called for vengeance, and the conflict between the rival king and queen escalated into outright war.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)

As the war began its first battles, Ser Otto was busy gaining the support of lords, hiring [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword), strengthening the defenses of King's Landing, and seeking other allies. Ser Otto sent messages to his nephew, Lord [Ormund Hightower](wiki:Ormund_Hightower), begging him to use [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown)'s forces to eliminate a spate of Rhaenyra-allied rebellions in the [Reach](wiki:Reach). Otto tried to to gain the support of [Qoren Martell](wiki:Qoren_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), who had once [fought against Prince Daemon](wiki:War_for_the_Stepstones) in the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), but was unsuccessful. As Dalton Greyjoy had not responded to his offer, Otto reached out to another enemy of Prince Daemon, the [Triarchy](wiki:Triarchy) of [Myr](wiki:Myr), [Lys](wiki:Lys), and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh), promising the [magisters](wiki:Magister) exclusive trading rights at King's Landing if they would break the [Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) blockade of the [Gullet](wiki:Gullet), and also offering control of the Stepstones.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

However, King Aegon II perceived Otto's diplomacy as slothful and cowardly at a time when Prince Daemon had [captured Harrenhal](wiki:Assault_on_Harrenhal) and many houses had declared for the blacks. According to [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), once when Aegon found Otto writing another letter, he knocked the inkpot into his grandfather's lap, telling him to "spill blood, not ink." The merchants of King's Landing complained to the king about the blockade every morning, and the Triarchy had yet to respond to Otto's offer. Eventually, Aegon ran out of patience waiting for Otto to "do something." Despite the pleading of Queen Alicent, King Aegon II tore the chain of office from his grandfather's neck and gave the position of Hand of the King to Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole), the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard).(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)

It was only after his dismissal that Otto's diplomatic efforts bore fruit. The [High Council of the Triarchy](wiki:High_Council_of_the_Triarchy) accepted Otto's offer of alliance, and sent ninety [warships](wiki:Ship) to the Gullet. On the fifth day of [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), the Triarchy's fleet fought the [Velaryon fleet](wiki:Velaryon_fleet) in the bloody [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet), leading to great losses on both sides. However, the Triarchy failed to break the hold of the Velaryon fleet on the east coasts of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)

After Rhaenyra and the blacks [took King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), Otto was the first to be beheaded as a traitor, just as he had predicted.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

## Appearances & Description

Considered a man of great learning in his time,(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Otto Hightower was also proud, haughty, blunt, and domineering.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) He was a cautious, methodical,(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) and ambitious man.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rtrotd71.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) He was resented and envied by many.(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Otto's greatest rival was Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen).(wiki:Otto_Hightower.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
