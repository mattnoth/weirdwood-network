## Appearances & Description

The [dwarf](wiki:Dwarf) is not quite five feet tall. He has a veined, bulbous nose, and his teeth are red from chewing [sourleaf](wiki:Sourleaf). He is dressed in the roughspun robes of a [holy brother](wiki:Holy_brother), with the [iron](wiki:Iron) hammer of [the Smith](wiki:Smith_(the_Seven)) dangling down about his thick neck. He has a tonsure, in the way many holy brothers cut their hair. His speech is coarse, but courteous.(wiki:Dwarf_brother.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

The dwarf used to serve at a holy house near [Maidenpool](wiki:Maidenpool), but [northmen](wiki:Northmen) burned them out. They rebuilt as best they could, but [sellswords](wiki:Sellsword) came, took their pigs, burned them out and killed the brothers. He managed to survive by squeezing in the hollow of a log, hiding until the sellswords left. Afterwards, he buried his brothers, dug up a few [coins](wiki:Coin) the [elder brother](wiki:Elder_Brother) had hid, and set off by himself. He went to Maidenpool, thinking he might find a [ship](wiki:Ship) to take him to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). There he glimpsed a [fool](wiki:Fool) down by the docks and then later at the [Stinking Goose](wiki:Stinking_Goose).(wiki:Dwarf_brother.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

The dwarf brother makes [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth)'s acquaintance in the common room of the [Seven Swords](wiki:Seven_Swords) in [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), courteously letting her sit down on one of the benches. She tells him that she is looking for her sister (actually [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark)), who might be accompanied by a fool ([Dontos Hollard](wiki:Dontos_Hollard)). The dwarf tells Brienne that he had seen a fool at Maidenpool, and overheard [Dick Crabb](wiki:Dick_Crabb) boasting that he had fooled a fool and had the [gold](wiki:Gold) to prove it. After reassuring Brienne and finishing a bowl of crab stew bought by her, the dwarf heads for [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Dwarf_brother.cite_ref-Raffc9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

Sometime afterwards, three men present Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) with a dwarf's head, grey-green and crawling with maggots. Cersei points out that the head has a bulbous nose, whereas [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) has no nose. One of the three men says that the dwarf told them he was a [sparrow](wiki:Sparrows), but they didn't believe him. While Cersei knows that they have killed an innocent man, she does not punish them, for fear it will discourage other such bounty hunters and allow Tyrion to escape.(wiki:Dwarf_brother.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) The beheaded dwarf is likely the same dwarf brother that Brienne encountered.

Brienne has Nimble Dick Crabb escort her to the [Whispers](wiki:Whispers), where she learns the fool observed by the dwarf brother is actually [Shagwell](wiki:Shagwell), not Dontos.(wiki:Dwarf_brother.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
