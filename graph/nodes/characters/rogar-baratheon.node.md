## Identity

Rogar Baratheon was the Lord of Storm's End and head of House Baratheon during the reigns of Maegor I and Jaehaerys I Targaryen. He served as Hand of the King and Protector of the Realm during Jaehaerys I's minority. Grandson of Orys Baratheon and eldest of five brothers (wiki:Rogar_Baratheon).

## Appearances & Description

Rogar had the typical Baratheon blue eyes and black hair, and wore a beard. He was muscled like a bull. Near life's end, his hair went grey and he grew gaunt. He preferred a double-bladed axe to a sword, boasting it was "large and heavy enough to cleave through a dragon's skull." He owned an antlered halfhelm. Prideful, bold, feared for his military prowess, fond of hawking, hunting, gambling, drinking, and feasting. He had no love for mummers (wiki:Rogar_Baratheon).

## Narrative Arc

Rogar proved both loyal friend and brief adversary to King Jaehaerys I. As Hand, he proposed foreign brides for the young king, including the Archon of Tyrosh's daughter. He twice plotted to depose Jaehaerys and crown another in his place — first attempting to use Princess Aerea through his brother Orryn's failed kidnapping of Princess Rhaella. When summoned to King's Landing in 50 AC, his brothers urged defiance, but Rogar submitted. He married Queen Dowager Alyssa Velaryon and fathered two children: Boremund (who succeeded him as Lord of Storm's End) and Jocelyn (who married Prince Aemon Targaryen). Despite his plots, Rogar was not a cruel or evil man nor a fool. He died in 62 AC at Storm's End (wiki:Rogar_Baratheon).
