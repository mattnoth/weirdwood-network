## Culture

The Thenns consider themselves the last of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men). They speak the [Old Tongue](wiki:Old_Tongue) and most have only a few words of the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue).(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) They are led by the [Magnar of Thenn](wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn), who is considered more a [god](wiki:God) than a man by his people.(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Because they have [laws](wiki:Laws) and [lords](wiki:Lord) in their valley, some consider the Thenns more sophisticated than other [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) and closer to the people [south of the Wall](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). They have more dealings with [giants](wiki:Giants) than other men.(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
The Thenns are savage fighters, but because of their belief in the Magnar as their god, they are more obedient and disciplined than other free folk.(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) They are often better equipped than most free folk, with [bronze](wiki:Bronze) [helms](wiki:Helm),(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) axes of bronze and a few of chipped stone,(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) short stabbing [spears](wiki:Spear) with leaf-shaped heads,(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) bronze [swords](wiki:Sword),(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) leather shirts sewn with bronze disks(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and scales,(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) big stone maces,(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) and plain unadorned shields of black boiled leather with bronze rims and bosses.(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) They use bronze-banded [warhorns](wiki:Horn) to warn each other of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

## Origins

The Thenns are unable to withstand the [Others](wiki:Others).(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
[Styr](wiki:Styr), the [Magnar of Thenn](wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn), submits to [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder), [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall), after being defeated by him three times.(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) accompanies one hundred Thenns led by [Styr](wiki:Styr) who climb the [Wall](wiki:Wall) and descend at [Greyguard](wiki:Greyguard), though Jon escapes them at [Queenscrown](wiki:Queenscrown) in the [Gift](wiki:Gift).(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) The raiders [attack Castle Black](wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black) from the south, but are defeated by the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), and Styr dies in the battle.(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) captures the new Magnar, Styr's son [Sigorn](wiki:Sigorn), when Mance's army is defeated in the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall).(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Soon after Mance is defeated at the Wall and his host is broken, a dour warrior in fur and [amber](wiki:Amber) urges the survivors to come with him and take refuge in the valley of the Thenns. Though the Thenns had fled their valley to find safety south of the Wall, hundreds of free folk still follow the warrior northward.(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Radwdprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
The Thenns under Sigorn's leadership refuse to join the Night's Watch in defending the Wall.(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Radwd21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
Jon arranges [a marriage](wiki:Wedding_of_Sigorn_and_Alys_Karstark) between Sigorn and [Alys Karstark](wiki:Alys_Karstark), forming [House Thenn](wiki:House_Thenn).(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Supported by Sigorn's two hundred Thenns, Alys believes that [Karhold](wiki:Karhold) will open its gates to her.(wiki:Thenns.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
