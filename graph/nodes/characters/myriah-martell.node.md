## Origins

Myriah was the eldest daughter of the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne_(father_of_Maron)) during the reign of the Young Dragon, King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Myriah_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf49.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) She had a younger brother, Prince [Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell).(wiki:Myriah_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

In [161 AC](wiki:161_AC), after Daeron was killed during his attempted [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne), King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) freed the [Dornish](wiki:Dornish) [hostages](wiki:Hostage) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and returned them to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) personally, walking barefoot. Once he reached [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), Baelor spoke with the Prince of Dorne, and agreed upon a peace, which included the [betrothal](wiki:Betrothal) of Myriah to Baelor's cousin, Prince [Daeron Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen). As both Daeron and Myriah were children at the time, the two were to marry once they were both [of age](wiki:Age_of_majority).(wiki:Myriah_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf49.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

Princess Myriah eventually wed Prince Daeron, and their marriage was happy and fruitful.(wiki:Myriah_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) They would have four sons together: [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)) (born [170 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen) (born [172](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)–[177 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), [Rhaegel](wiki:Rhaegel_Targaryen) (born [173](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)–[178 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)), and [Maekar](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) (born [174](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)–[179 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)).(wiki:Myriah_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf101.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Myriah's marriage to Daeron would pave the way for [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) to come under the rule of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Myriah_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

In [184 AC](wiki:184_AC), after the death of King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), Daeron succeeded him as King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), and Myriah became queen.(wiki:Myriah_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Many noble Dornishmen and women flocked to Daeron's court, which served to give life in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) a foreign flavor; however, this alienated many of Daeron's other nobles and courtiers. Some also looked upon Prince Baelor's dark hair and eyes and muttered he was more Martell than Targaryen. These seeds of dissent would eventually grow into the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion).(wiki:Myriah_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

It is unknown whether Myriah survived her husband, who died of the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness) in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC).(wiki:Myriah_Martell.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Myriah_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

## Appearances & Description

Myriah had the typical dark hair and eyes of the members of [House Martell](wiki:House_Martell). Those traits were passed on to her eldest son, Prince [Baelor Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)).(wiki:Myriah_Martell.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Myriah_Martell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf412.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
