## Origins

A [crow](wiki:Crow) once took Mors for dead and pecked out his eye.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) According to [Old Nan](wiki:Nan), he grabbed the bird at its feet and bit its head off, earning him the nickname *Crowfood*.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

Mors was a fierce fighter in his youth.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) His wife died in childbirth, his sons were killed in the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), and his only daughter was abducted in [270 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) by wildlings, leaving Mors with a deep hatred of the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk).(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

## Appearances & Description

Mors is an old man, huge and powerful, with a ruddy face and a shaggy white beard.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Btheon_i_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Ctheon_i.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) He wears a chunk of [dragonglass](wiki:Dragonglass) in place of a lost eye(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) or conceals the empty socket with a stained white leather eye patch.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Btheon_i_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Ctheon_i.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) He wears a cloak made of a [snow bear](wiki:Snow_bear) with its head as a hood.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Btheon_i_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Ctheon_i.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Mors enjoys boasting about the [crow](wiki:Crow) which took his eye.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Mors Crowfood is usually [drunk](wiki:Alcoholic_beverages).(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) considers Mors and his brother [Hother](wiki:Hother_Umber) to be hoary old brigands.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

Mors and his brother, [Hother Umber](wiki:Hother_Umber), serve as joint castellans of the [Last Hearth](wiki:Last_Hearth) while their nephew, the [Greatjon](wiki:Jon_Umber), fights in the south during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Rasosappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Mors Crowfood and Hother Whoresbane attend the [harvest feast](wiki:Harvest_feast_(299)) at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), where Mors puts himself forward as a possible match for the recently widowed Lady [Donella Hornwood](wiki:Donella_Hornwood). [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) promises to pass the request on to [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), the new [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), and Donella, who dislikes Mors.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

When Luwin answers Hother's request for longships and men to sail them as protection against the influx of [wildlings](wiki:Free_folk) in the north with the suggestion to strike an alliance with Lord [Wyman Manderly](wiki:Wyman_Manderly), Mors is dismissive, calling Wyman a "waddling sack of suet". However, Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) commands the Umbers in the name of Robb to ally with the [Manderlys](wiki:House_Manderly) and they give in, but not without grumbling.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Racok16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

During the harvest feast, Mors and his brother Hother play a drinking game, slamming their [horns](wiki:Horn) together hard. When a spinning skirl is started, Mors tosses an embarrassed serving girl in the air.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### A Storm of Swords

At [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun), the Greatjon tells Robb that his uncles wish to remarry. Robb considers the possibility of Mors marrying a daughter of Lord [Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey).(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Robb is killed and the Greatjon is captured during the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding) at the [Twins](wiki:Twins).(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Rasosepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Mors is one of the few representatives of the [north](wiki:North) to declare fealty to [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon). He agrees to serve Stannis if he is sent the skull of [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder). Mors also states to Stannis that he will not fight his brother, [Hother Whoresbane](wiki:Hother_Umber), who is supporting the new [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North), Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton). Stannis is advised by some to attack Last Hearth for this, but Jon tells him not to. Hother supports the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton) only because the [Greatjon](wiki:Jon_Umber) is a prisoner of the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). Stannis agrees and promises to place Mors and his host on the opposite side of battle to his brother.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

Mors camps outside [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) during a blizzard, his men repeatedly blowing warhorns to cause confusion and to draw out the Bolton host. There he is able to capture [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) and [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole), when both are fleeing Winterfell and their captor, [Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow), the heir of Roose. Also during this time, the [Braavosi](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos) banker [Tycho Nestoris](wiki:Tycho_Nestoris) comes looking for Stannis but meets Mors instead. Mors sends Theon and Jeyne with Tycho and his escort to Stannis at a [crofters' village](wiki:Crofters'_village).(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### The Winds of Winter

Theon reveals Mors asked Jeyne, who is still claiming she is [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark), questions about Winterfell, such as names of servants of the keep, to confirm her identity. Since Jeyne lived at Winterfell for most of her life, she was able to answer them correctly. Also, Mors was only able to muster a few crippled men and green boys as part of his force, as most of the Umber strength had gone south with the Greatjon and the remaining guard had left with Hother.

Mors had his green boys dig a pit trap outside the Winterfell gates. Due to the blizzard, the sentries' visibility was impaired and they were able to do it without detection. When the [Freys](wiki:House_Frey) rode out of Winterfell's gate, several of them fell into this trap. Ser [Hosteen Frey](wiki:Hosteen_Frey) lost his horse and Ser [Aenys Frey](wiki:Aenys_Frey) broke his neck and died.(wiki:Mors_Umber.cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Btheon_i_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Ctheon_i.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
