## Origins

### Origin[ ]

According to [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun) and Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), Alys was a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) sired by Lord [Lyonel Strong](wiki:Lyonel_Strong) on an unknown woman when he was young. Munkun claims Alys was a serving wench with an interest in potions, while Eustace states she was a [woods witch](wiki:Woods_witch). However, the [fool](wiki:Fool) [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) claims Alys was from an older generation, and that she had served as a [wet nurse](wiki:Wet_nurse) to Lyonel's sons, [Harwin](wiki:Harwin_Strong) and [Larys](wiki:Larys_Strong), and possibly even to Lyonel. Mushroom wrote that Alys was an enchantress who bathed in [maidens](wiki:Maiden)' blood to remain youthful. Both Eustace and Munkun considered Mushroom's claims fantastical, but even they agreed that Alys looked unusually young for her age, as she had to be at least forty by the outbreak of the Dance of the Dragons.(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)
Although all of Alys's children were stillborn, she served as a wet nurse to many children at Harrenhal.(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)

### The Dance of the Dragons[ ]

Alys was at least forty years of age during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), although she appeared younger. She survived the [assault on Harrenhal](wiki:Assault_on_Harrenhal) by Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) and the [blacks](wiki:Blacks).(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_a_son_for_a_son.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)
After the [greens](wiki:Greens) retook [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) ordered the deaths of the remaining members of [House Strong](wiki:House_Strong) at the castle. The [dragonrider](wiki:Dragonrider) spared Alys's life, however, and took the older woman for his bedmate as a spoil of war.(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) When informed of the greens' defeat in the [Battle by the Lakeshore](wiki:Battle_by_the_Lakeshore), Alys prevented an enraged Aemond from strangling the [squire](wiki:Squire) who brought the message to Harrenhal. Eustace disagrees with Mushroom's claim that Alys used [love potions](wiki:Love_potion) and philtres to entice Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole), [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard) and [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), in addition to Aemond.(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)
While Criston marched south along the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye), Aemond began burning the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) with [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar). When Lady [Sabitha Frey](wiki:Sabitha_Frey) seized the undefended Harrenhal, she found only Alys within, with the wet nurse claiming to be pregnant with Aemond's child. Alys eventually escaped with Aemond when the prince returned to Harrenhal and chased away Sabitha.(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5)
When Prince Aemond returned to Harrenhal to confront Prince Daemon and [Caraxes](wiki:Caraxes), he took the pregnant Alys with him. Aemond told Daemon that it was Alys who told him where to find him, as she had seen in her [visions](wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies).(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) Aemond left Alys on the ground, kissing her one last time, before Vhagar and Caraxes leapt as one into the sky above the [Gods Eye](wiki:Gods_Eye) with their dragonriders. The pregnant Alys watched the ensuing [Battle Above the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Above_the_Gods_Eye) atop [Kingspyre Tower](wiki:Kingspyre_Tower) in Harrenhal below. Aemond and Daemon were both slain in their duel in [130 AC](wiki:130_AC).(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)

### Witch Queen[ ]

[Larys Strong](wiki:Larys_Strong), [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal), was executed in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC) during the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf).(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
In [132 AC](wiki:132_AC) during the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), a number of [broken men](wiki:Broken_men) and predatory outlaws began to gather at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) under the rule of a sorcerous witch queen. Tasked by Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), with reclaiming the castle, Ser [Regis Groves](wiki:Regis_Groves) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) and Ser [Damon Darry](wiki:Damon_Darry) marched from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) and [Castle Darry](wiki:Darry) to Harrenhal with a hundred men.(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
The alleged witch queen was revealed to be Alys, who claimed to be the widow of Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen). She presented [a young boy](wiki:Alys_Rivers's_son) as their trueborn son, who she said was the rightful king instead of [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). Regis abruptly died after insulting Alys. Some claim she used [magic](wiki:Magic) to burst his skull, while others state he was killed by crossbowmen or slingers. Alys's rabble then rode from Harrenhal and overwhelmed the loyalist men. Damon led thirty-two survivors back to Castle Darry.(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
Alys sent a captured loyalist to Darry as a messenger the following day. The survivor claimed that a dozen other captives had been tortured to death, and that he had been cursed by Alys so that he would die if anyone laughed at his story. He said Alys had instructed him that any man who approached Harrenhal without submitting to its occupants would die. The man also insisted that he had seen a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) inside. Despite Damon's assurance that no one would laugh at the messenger's story, an onlooker did so. The messenger then choked to death in moments, with some saying they had seen the imprints of a woman's fingers on his throat.(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
Back in King's Landing, Tyland was troubled by Regis's death. Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake) disbelieved Damon's claims of dragons and magic, however, and the other regents agreed with Unwin's conclusion that outlaws were to blame. Aegon's regents concluded they would need to gather a larger force to recapture Harrenhal, but their plans were interrupted by the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC).(wiki:Alys_Rivers.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)
