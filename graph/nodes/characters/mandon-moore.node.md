## Origins

Ser Mandon came from the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn)(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) and is familiar with Ser [Vardis Egen](wiki:Vardis_Egen).(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) He was brought to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) by the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), and King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) named Mandon to the Kingsguard. Neither of the two are particularly fond of Mandon, however.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

## Appearances & Description

Mandon has pale grey eyes, oddly flat and lifeless,(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) which unnerve [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark).(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) [Bronn](wiki:Bronn) thinks Mandon has the eyes of a fish, and Sansa thinks he has a curiously dead face.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Mandon is a skilled warrior,(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) but he is not loved by the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) and he is not close to his fellow brothers of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) considers Mandon dangerous, as his face does not give away his intentions.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) notes that Mandon is a man others would not follow.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

Mandon wields a [sword](wiki:Sword), lance, white shield, and enameled white steel plate.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Wearing the white raiment of the Kingsguard makes him look like a corpse in a shroud.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Mandon rides a coal-black stallion barded in white.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

After the arrest of Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), Ser Mandon guards the doors of the council chamber when his daughter, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), is brought before the [small council](wiki:Small_council) by Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount). Mandon later escorts Sansa back to the high tower of [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast).(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Mandon is present in the [throne room](wiki:Red_Keep) when Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), is dismissed. Mandon, along with the other members of the Kingsguard, laughs at his former Sworn Brother's shame.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Mandon is one of the Kingsguard that King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon) commands to beat Sansa, and obeys without question.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

Mandon is on guard outside the [council chambers](wiki:Red_Keep) when [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) arrives with [Bronn](wiki:Bronn) and [Timett](wiki:Timett), carrying a message from his lord father, [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister). Because Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) had ordered for the meeting to not be disturbed, Mandon refuses to admit the newcomers. The white cloak eventually allows only Tyrion to pass.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

Mandon is among the escort for the party that sees Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) off to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), serving as Sansa bodyguard. When the [riot of King's Landing](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing) breaks out, Mandon guards King Joffrey after [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) is mobbed, abandoning Sansa in the chaos. Someone grabs the king's leg, but Mandon severs the rioter's hand from the wrist. After the royal party reaches the safety of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), Cersei defends Mandon from Tyrion for having protected Joffrey instead of Sansa. Sandor returns Sansa safely to the castle.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

During the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Mandon is assigned to guard King Joffrey and Tyrion, the acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). When the king leaves for the [Three Whores](wiki:Three_Whores), Mandon stays with Tyrion, however. After information has been received that [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s men intend to batter down the [King's Gate](wiki:King's_Gate) with a ram, Mandon, Tyrion, and the Hand's [squire](wiki:Squire), [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne), ride from the [Mud Gate](wiki:River_Gate) to the King's Gate. Tyrion tries to talk Sandor, who is in charge at the King's Gate, into leading his men in a sortie against the enemies, but Sandor refuses, having been frightened by [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire).(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Tyrion decides to lead the charge to the riverfront, with Mandon carrying Joffrey's banner. The [knight](wiki:Knight)'s lance breaks off in an opponent, and his shield is destroyed. Mandon and his Sworn Brother Ser [Balon Swann](wiki:Balon_Swann) skillfully fend off [Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) spearmen. While fighting on a ship, Tyrion is in danger of falling into the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush). Mandon first offers his hand to save Tyrion, but the knight then slashes at the Hand,(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) cutting off part of Tyrion's nose. Tyrion pulls himself onto the deck, but Mandon prepares to stab him in the throat. However, Podrick pushes Mandon into the Blackwater, where he sinks under the weight of his armor(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) and drowns.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

While recuperating from his wounds, a fevered Tyrion has a nightmare of the wildfire and Mandon's assassination attempt. After waking, Tyrion thinks that Mandon was bribed by Cersei to kill him during the battle. Podrick is ashamed to have caused Mandon's death, but Tyrion praises Pod and instructs his squire to keep silent about Mandon.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Racok67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)

### A Storm of Swords

With Mandon dead and Sandor Clegane having fled, Ser [Loras Tyrell](wiki:Loras_Tyrell) is named to the Kingsguard(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) and Ser [Boros Blount](wiki:Boros_Blount) is restored to the white cloaks.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)

Hoping that proof will be found that Mandon was working for his sister, Queen Regent Cersei, Tyrion Lannister asks his friend and [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword), Ser [Bronn of the Blackwater](wiki:Bronn_of_the_Blackwater), to investigate Mandon.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) later tells some of Mandon's history to Tyrion, although it is much the same that Bronn has already uncovered.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

When Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) assumes his duties as [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), he notices that in his absence the [White Book](wiki:White_Book) has not been updated, as that is the Lord Commander's duty, and that the death of Ser Mandon has yet to be recorded.(wiki:Mandon_Moore.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
