## Origins

Ser [Jon Heddle](wiki:Jon_Heddle), known as Long Jon, was a [knight](wiki:Knight) in the 1st century [AC](wiki:AC). When he was crippled and done with adventuring, he took over the old [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads) between the [Kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad), the [river road](wiki:River_road), and the [high road](wiki:High_road). Jon also took up [ironworking](wiki:Iron), creating a sign for his inn in the shape of a three-headed [dragon](wiki:Dragon). The iron sign would clank in the wind, and gave the inn its new name, the Clanking Dragon.(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Many years later in [196 AC](wiki:196_AC), when Long Jon's son was an old man, the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) broke out. Lord [Darry](wiki:House_Darry), then overlord of the inn's lands, was a famous [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) supporter. He saw the inn's black iron dragon sign, and became angry because it resembled the [coat of arms](wiki:Coat_of_arms) of the rebels, [House Blackfyre](wiki:House_Blackfyre); he cut it down, hacked it to pieces, and threw it into the river. Eventually one of the iron dragon's heads washed up on the [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle), red with rust.(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
Some years later, a descendant of Long Jon, Ser [Tommard Heddle](wiki:Tommard_Heddle), sought the hand of Lord [Ambrose Butterwell](wiki:Ambrose_Butterwell)'s daughter, killing three of her other suitors. The two were married, and Black Tom Heddle became a sword of [House Butterwell](wiki:House_Butterwell)'s household and commander of the garrison at [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls). In [212 AC](wiki:212_AC), Lord Butterwell and Black Tom became involved with the [Second Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:Second_Blackfyre_Rebellion). When Black Tom attempted to capture or kill Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), the disguised [squire](wiki:Squire) of Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), he was challenged and killed by Dunk. The rebellion was put down within the day.(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
Over time, the Heddles' inn became known as the River Inn, where one could take a ferry across to [Lord Harroway's Town](wiki:Lord_Harroway's_Town) and Whitewalls. However, the river moved, and soon that name faded. [Masha Heddle](wiki:Masha_Heddle)'s grandfather was the innkeeper at the time the river moved, c.[220 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)-[230 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
When [Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) was a child, Masha was kind to her, and gave her and other children sweet cakes.(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) and Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel) stay at the [inn at the crossroads](wiki:Inn_at_the_crossroads) while returning to the [north](wiki:North) from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). There they encounter [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), whom they arrest and take with them when they leave. [Masha Heddle](wiki:Masha_Heddle) begs the nobles to take their fight elsewhere, but is refused.(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Ragot28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
Later, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) takes revenge on Masha, hanging her on a gibbet in the yard of the inn. He takes the inn as his quarters while he is encamped at the crossroads. Tyrion sees Masha's body when he meets his father at the inn, after leaving the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) with his [clansmen](wiki:Vale_mountain_clans).(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Ragot56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

After Masha's death, one of her nephews reopens the inn, bringing in [whores](wiki:Whore) to attract customers.(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Visiting the inn for wine and information, [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) and [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) happen upon three of Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane)'s men. The innkeep, his girls, and his patrons vacate the common room after Sandor states his expectation the [Mountain's men](wiki:Mountain's_men) will [torture](wiki:Torture) the innkeep for his gold. Sandor and Arya kill [Polliver](wiki:Polliver), the [Tickler](wiki:Tickler), and a [pimply squire](wiki:Pimply_squire), but Sandor is badly wounded in the mêlée.(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Rasos74.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Masha's nephew is eventually killed at the hands of an unidentified noble, and the inn is left vacant. The sisters [Long Jeyne](wiki:Jeyne_Heddle) and [Willow Heddle](wiki:Willow_Heddle) reopen the inn for business, and it serves as a refuge for orphan children. According to the [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother), the girls are Masha's nieces,(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) while a semi-canon source states they are the daughters of Masha's late nephew, and thus her great-nieces.(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Rawoiafjeyne_heddle.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) The Heddles and the inn are now connected with the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners); in addition, [Gendry](wiki:Gendry) now works for them as a [smith](wiki:Smith).(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)
In [300 AC](wiki:300_AC), [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth), [Podrick Payne](wiki:Podrick_Payne), Septon [Meribald](wiki:Meribald), and Ser [Hyle Hunt](wiki:Hyle_Hunt) travel to the crossroads inn. Meribald tells his companions the history of the inn and the Heddle family. The group passes hanged men in the vicinity, alleged perpetrators of the [raid on Saltpans](wiki:Raid_on_Saltpans), which leads Hyle to say the inn should now be called the "Gallows Inn".(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)
Brienne and her companions are greeted at the inn by Willow, Gendry, and the orphans. Jeyne is away with the brotherhood, but Meribald trades his food for rooms at the inn. A fight ensues when outlaws from the [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions) arrive and threaten Willow and the children. Brienne defends them, and Gendry joins in the fight; [Rorge](wiki:Rorge) and [Biter](wiki:Biter) are slain, but Brienne is badly wounded.(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) Jeyne tends to Brienne's wounds after the battle, but Brienne, Hyle and Podrick are all given over to [Lady Stoneheart](wiki:Lady_Stoneheart).(wiki:Heddle_family.cite_ref-Raffc42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
