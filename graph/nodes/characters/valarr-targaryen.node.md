## Origins

Prince Valarr was born the eldest son of Prince [Baelor Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), and his wife, Lady [Jena Dondarrion](wiki:Jena_Dondarrion). He had one younger brother, Prince [Matarys](wiki:Matarys_Targaryen). At some point, Valarr was married to [Kiera of Tyrosh](wiki:Kiera_of_Tyrosh).(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) They had sons, but all were stillborn.(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

### Ashford Tourney

In [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), Valarr was one of the original champions for [Lord Ashford's daughter](wiki:Lord_Ashford's_daughter) at the start of the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow). However, his status as a [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) prince, grandson of King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen), and future heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), prevented him from facing dangerous opponents. He won at least nine jousts against "undistinguished foes", including Ser [Abelar Hightower](wiki:Abelar_Hightower) and Lord [Gawen Swann](wiki:Gawen_Swann), and remained a champion after the first day's jousting.(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

During Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall)'s [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven), Valarr's father, Prince [Baelor Breakspear](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), took part as the seventh [knight](wiki:Knight) on Dunk's side. However, as Baelor had not expected to fight in the tourney, he had not brought any armor to Ashford with him; therefore he borrowed Valarr's armor. Thus when Baelor first appeared on the field, Dunk briefly believed Valarr was coming to champion him.(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4)

Baelor died in the trial, and Valarr stood vigil by his body at the funeral. When Dunk tried to offer his sympathies, Valarr dismissed him.(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

### Death and legacy

Valarr died in [209 AC](wiki:209_AC) from the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness), as did his younger brother [Matarys](wiki:Matarys_Targaryen), and their grandfather, King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) He was likely a teenager at the time of his death.(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-9)

In [211 AC](wiki:211_AC), Duncan had a nightmare where the dead Valarr berated him with the same words he had used at Baelor's funeral.(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3)

In [211 AC](wiki:211_AC), Duncan and Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) heard a [hunchbacked septon](wiki:Hunchbacked_septon) in [Stoney Sept](wiki:Stoney_Sept) preach that the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), had killed Valarr's sons in [their mother's](wiki:Kiera_of_Tyrosh) womb, and suggest that Brynden was responsible for Valarr's death. The [septon](wiki:Septon) was later beheaded for speaking [treason](wiki:Treason).(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

## Appearances & Description

Valarr looked much like his father [Baelor](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), though he was shorter, slimmer, and handsomer. The Young Prince had brown hair, with a streak of silver-gold running through it. He had blue eyes. Unlike his father who was renowned as one of the most powerful warriors in the realm,(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Valarr's jousting skills were modest at best.(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafvalarr_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Valarr wore black [armor](wiki:Armor) and carried a black [lance](wiki:Lance) during [tourneys](wiki:Tourney). His [horse](wiki:Horse) was also black, as well as its trappings. Atop his black helm was the three-headed [dragon](wiki:Dragon) of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), enameled in a rich red. Valarr's pavilion of black silk had a line of pointed scarlet pennons hanging from its roof.(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

After his death, Valarr was remembered as being bold,(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) brave,(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) and full of promise.(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings

While explaining the lineage of Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) to [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont) says that Prince Baelor's two sons soon followed their father to the grave in the Great Spring Sickness.(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Discussing both their fathers' objections to them becoming [maesters](wiki:Maester), Maester Aemon,(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Valarr's cousin,(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) tells [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) that his grandfather sent him to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) because too many dragons were as dangerous as too few. He mentions that King Daeron had four sons, three of whom had sons of their own.(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) As the first-born son of King Daeron's eldest child, Prince Baelor, Valarr was one of the sons of the king's sons(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) alluded to by Aemon.(wiki:Valarr_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2)
