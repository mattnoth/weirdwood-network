## Identity

Prince Valarr Targaryen, known as the Young Prince, was the oldest son of Baelor Breakspear, Prince of Dragonstone, and Lady Jena Dondarrion (wiki:Valarr_Targaryen, cite: The Hedge Knight).

## Origins

Born between 182–193 AC in Westeros to Prince Baelor Targaryen and Lady Jena Dondarrion. He had one younger brother, Prince Matarys (wiki:Valarr_Targaryen).

## Appearances & Description

Valarr looked much like his father Baelor, though shorter, slimmer, and handsomer. He had brown hair with a streak of silver-gold running through it, and blue eyes. His jousting skills were modest at best. He wore black armor with the three-headed dragon of House Targaryen enameled in red atop his helm (wiki:Valarr_Targaryen, cite: The Hedge Knight). After death, he was remembered as bold, brave, and full of promise (wiki:Valarr_Targaryen, cite: AFFC, TWOIAF).

## Narrative Arc

At the tourney at Ashford Meadow in 209 AC, Valarr was one of the original champions for Lord Ashford's daughter. His status as a Targaryen prince prevented him from facing dangerous opponents; he won at least nine jousts against undistinguished foes. When his father Prince Baelor was killed during the trial of seven, Valarr became the new Prince of Dragonstone and heir to King Daeron II. He may have been named Hand of the King. Valarr married Kiera of Tyrosh, but their sons were all stillborn. He died in 209 AC during the Great Spring Sickness, along with his grandfather King Daeron II and his brother Matarys (wiki:Valarr_Targaryen, cite: The Hedge Knight, TWOIAF, ACOK).
