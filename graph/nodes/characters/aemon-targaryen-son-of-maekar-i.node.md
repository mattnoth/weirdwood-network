## Origins

### Youth

Aemon was the third son of Prince [Maekar Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) and his wife, Lady [Dyanna Dayne](wiki:Dyanna_Dayne).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) He was [named after](wiki:Named_after) his great-great-uncle, Prince [Aemon the Dragonknight](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II)), by his grandfather, [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Aemon was a bookish boy.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) He gave his younger brother, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), the nickname of "Egg".(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) He and all of his younger brothers were given [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_egg) when they were young. Aemon and Aegon sometimes pretended that their eggs would hatch so they would be [dragonriders](wiki:Dragonrider).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) The brothers recreated the [Battle of the Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field) with painted soldiers on Maester [Melaquin](wiki:Melaquin)'s table.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) had four grown sons, three with sons of their own, and so felt that having too many potential [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) heirs was dangerous. Daeron sent Aemon to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel)(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) around the age of nine or ten(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) despite Aemon's father Prince Maekar's objections.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-2) Following the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow), Prince Aegon served as the [squire](wiki:Squire) to a [hedge knight](wiki:Hedge_knight), Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall). When Dunk and Egg visited [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) after the [Great Spring Sickness](wiki:Great_Spring_Sickness),(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rawoiafaemon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) Aemon measured the knight's height, and also gave his younger brother a mule, called [Maester](wiki:Maester_(mule)).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### Maester

Aemon forged his chain and was made a [maester](wiki:Maester), finishing his studies at the age of nineteen.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rawoiafaemon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Maester Aemon then served at a lordling's court.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) After succeeding to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) summoned Aemon and his brothers to court. Not wanting to sit on the [small council](wiki:Small_council) as he felt that would displace the [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester), Aemon chose instead to serve at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), the seat of his eldest brother, Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), until Daeron died of a [pox](wiki:Pox).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rawoiafaemon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) Aegon's young daughter, Princess [Rhaelle](wiki:Rhaelle_Targaryen), referred to Aemon as "Uncle Maester".(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

In [233 AC](wiki:233_AC), after Maekar's death in the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising), a [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) was called to decide the succession. Since Prince Aegon was considered by some lords to be "half a [peasant](wiki:Smallfolk)", it was suggested that Aemon could be released from his sworn vows and thereby succeed his late father. However, Aemon quietly refused, ceding rule to his younger brother, who became King Aegon V.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

### Night's Watch

Aemon chose to go to the [Wall](wiki:Wall) and join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) for fear that he might be used in a plot to usurp his brother, King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen). The king provided Aemon with an "honor guard", emptying the dungeons of prisoners to take the vows of the Watch with him. Among these prisoners were [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), who later went on to become [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), and many of the [Raven's Teeth](wiki:Raven's_Teeth). Aegon's friend, Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), accompanied the new recruits aboard the *[Golden Dragon](wiki:Golden_Dragon)* on the way to [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)

Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) sometimes corresponded with his ancient great-great uncle, who believed him to be [the prince that was promised](wiki:The_prince_that_was_promised) due to the [tragic circumstances of his birth](wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall). Rhaegar shared Aemon's belief at first, but later persuaded him that the [prophecy](wiki:Prophecy) applied to his son [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) instead.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Aemon claims that every man who has ever joined the Watch has had his vows tested at least once. Aemon stated that his vows as a maester and to the Night's Watch were tested three times: when he was a boy, as an adult (when he was offered the crown after his father's death),(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4) and when he had grown old. The hardest time was the last, hearing about the destruction of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) and the death and exile of his family during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)

Aemon cut away [Gared](wiki:Gared)'s ears after he lost them to frostbite.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) Before he lost his sight, Aemon used a [far-eye](wiki:Far-eye) from [Myr](wiki:Myr) to study the stars.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) [Clydas](wiki:Clydas) and [Chett](wiki:Chett) are [stewards](wiki:Stewards) who aid Aemon in his duties.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

## Appearances & Description

An ancient man of a hundred years old at the beginning of *[A Song of Ice and Fire](wiki:A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire)*, Aemon is bald, wrinkled, and shrunken. His blind eyes are clouded and milk white.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) He has a thin, fleshless neck.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Aemon was quick witted as a boy.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rawoiafaemon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) The maester speaks softly but his counsel is so valued and respected that many fall silent to hear it.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Despite his age, Aemon's mind is still sharp as is his hearing.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Aemon is calm(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and courteous.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Aemon always wears his maester's chain, which includes links of [gold](wiki:Gold), [iron](wiki:Iron), [lead](wiki:Lead), [silver](wiki:Silver), [tin](wiki:Tin), and other metals.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) The maester walks with a blackthorn cane.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) His writing is small and precise.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) [Maester Aemon's tower](wiki:Maester_Aemon's_tower) at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) is a stout wooden keep which has a library and a [rookery](wiki:Rookery).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Maester Aemon is present at the feast at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) when [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), welcomes [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) to the [Wall](wiki:Wall). Aemon recognizes the intelligence of Tyrion and describes him as "a giant come among us," rendering Tyrion, a [dwarf](wiki:Dwarf), uncharacteristically speechless.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

[Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), one of the new recruits, assists Aemon with the [ravens](wiki:Raven) as part of his training.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) The maester later aids Jon in helping his new friend [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) become a [steward](wiki:Steward) of the Night's Watch, fearing for the boy's safety if he remains a recruit under the brutal training of Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh), the [First Steward](wiki:First_Steward), reassigns [Chett](wiki:Chett) to the kennels so that Sam can aid Aemon in the [rookery](wiki:Rookery) and library at the [maester's tower](wiki:Maester_Aemon's_tower).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)

After Jon saves Lord Commander Mormont from the [wight](wiki:Wight) of [Othor](wiki:Othor), Aemon provides [milk of the poppy](wiki:Milk_of_the_poppy) to the wounded steward.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-3) Aemon sends messages to [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), but the [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) does not reply.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-4) Aemon counsels Jon when he learns his family is in danger, for he knows Jon is having thoughts of deserting to come to [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark)'s aid, just as Aemon had considered helping [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-5)

### A Clash of Kings

Aemon, being too old to accompany the [great ranging](wiki:Great_ranging), remains at Castle Black. The duty of writing and sending messages falls to Sam Tarly. Jeor reveals much of Aemon's history to Jon, including the fact that Aemon could have been king, but refused the crown in favor of his younger brother, [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen). Jeor has a high opinion of Aemon and his abilities, an opinion Jon shares.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5)

### A Storm of Swords

Chett is resentful toward [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) for having replaced him as one of Aemon's stewards.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasosprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

When Jon returns to Castle Black, badly wounded by an arrow, Maester Aemon treats his injury. Despite being blind, his hands are steady and he treats the wound successfully.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) [Donal Noye](wiki:Donal_Noye) and Aemon believe Jon's report of [Thenns](wiki:Thenns) about to attack, and the maester sends [ravens](wiki:Raven) to warn [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea) and the [Shadow Tower](wiki:Shadow_Tower).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3) Aemon also sends ravens throughout the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) asking for help from powerful lords; two ravens are sent to each [northern](wiki:Northmen) lord.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

Aemon suggests that the defenders of Castle Black fill spare clothing with straw, hoping that these straw soldiers will make the approaching Thenns believe that the castle has more defenders than expected.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) Aemon and [Clydas](wiki:Clydas) take the winch cage up the Wall for safety during the [attack on Castle Black](wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black). Jon sees three Thenns hack their way into [Maester Aemon's tower](wiki:Maester_Aemon's_tower) during the fighting.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3)

After Donal is killed defending the gates from [giants](wiki:Giants), Aemon tells Jon he must lead the men while Aemon provides guidance to him and the other men of the Wall. Aemon also comes to Jon's defense when [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt) and Ser [Alliser Thorne](wiki:Alliser_Thorne) want Jon executed for allegedly breaking his oaths and becoming a [wildling](wiki:Wildling).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

Following the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall), only Maester Aemon is allowed to visit the captured [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Aemon is dubious of [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre)'s claim that [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) is [Azor Ahai](wiki:Azor_Ahai) reborn.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) Aemon, Sam, and Clydas help sort token during the selection of a new Lord Commander;(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) Jon Snow is eventually chosen by the assembled brothers.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

### A Feast for Crows

Aemon visits the captive [Val](wiki:Val) and checks on the health of the babes of the wildlings [Gilly](wiki:Gilly) and [Dalla](wiki:Dalla). Aemon has [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) find *[Jade Compendium](wiki:Jade_Compendium)* in the [vaults of Castle Black](wiki:Vaults_of_Castle_Black).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-3) Aemon agrees with the suggestion of Lord Commander Snow to send him to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) with Sam and Gilly, carrying [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder)'s infant "wildling prince", to remove all "royal blood" from Melisandre's grasp. Jon fears that Melisandre might kill Aemon for his "king's blood" to use in a spell.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-4)

The sea voyage on the *[Blackbird](wiki:Blackbird)* is hard for the old man. Sam struggles to tell Aemon what to do, leaving him out on the deck in the rain for too long whilst he is lost in his reminiscences.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) The maester is bedridden and near death when arriving at [Braavos](wiki:Braavos). Aemon has fever dreams of being young and in the company of his late brother, King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), whom he called Egg,(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2) and he also recalls the [Quill and Tankard](wiki:Quill_and_Tankard) in Oldtown.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Sam hires a healer from the [House of the Red Hands](wiki:House_of_the_Red_Hands), but the man is unable to help Aemon recover.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1)

Aemon, upon hearing of [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) and her [dragons](wiki:Dragon), is convinced she is [the prince that was promised](wiki:The_prince_that_was_promised), understanding that the [High Valyrian](wiki:High_Valyrian) word for "prince" is gender neutral and that dragons change sex (per the writings of Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth)).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) He curses his age, gutted that he is too old and feeble to seek her out to help with counsel and dragon lore—a fourth testing of his dual vows. Neverthless, hearing of the dragons strengthens Aemon enough that he is able to board the [Summer Islander](wiki:Summer_Islander) ship *[Cinnamon Wind](wiki:Cinnamon_Wind)* to journey to Oldtown.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

Aemon dies in his sleep at the age of one hundred and two, off the coast of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) on the voyage from Braavos to Oldtown. Targaryens are customarily cremated, but since the *Cinnamon Wind'*s captain [Quhuru Mo](wiki:Quhuru_Mo) will not allow a pyre aboard his ship, Aemon's body is stored in a cask of blackbelly [rum](wiki:Rum) until they can reach Oldtown. At the funeral aboard the ship, Sam speaks a eulogy for Aemon, and the Summer Islanders drink rum and make love to celebrate his life. Gilly decides to name the child of Mance and Dalla [in honor](wiki:Named_after) of the maester, saying the boy will be called "Aemon Battleborn" or "[Aemon Steelsong](wiki:Aemon_Steelsong)" once he turns two.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)

Once at Oldtown, Sam informs Archmaester [Marwyn](wiki:Marwyn) of Aemon's passing and of Aemon's intention to help Daenerys. Marwyn immediately leaves the [Ravenry](wiki:Ravenry) to travel to Daenerys in [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I).cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)
