## Identity

Lord Tywin Lannister is Lord of Casterly Rock, Shield of Lannisport, and Warden of the West. He is the head of House Lannister and the father of Queen Cersei, Ser Jaime, and Tyrion (wiki:Tywin_Lannister).

## Origins

Born in 242 AC at Casterly Rock, the westerlands (track_b: Born). Son of Tytos Lannister and Jeyne Marbrand (track_b: Father, Mother). Died in 300 AC in the Tower of the Hand, King's Landing (track_b: Died). Buried at Casterly Rock (track_b: Buried).

## Appearances & Description

Tywin is tall, slender, and broad-shouldered, with thin but muscled arms. He keeps his head shaved since his golden hair started going bald, but grows out bushy golden side-whiskers. He has pale green eyes flecked with gold (wiki:Tywin_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot56). His steel plate armor is enameled in deep crimson with golden highlights and sunburst rondels. His greatcloak is cloth-of-gold clasped with golden miniature lionesses (wiki:Tywin_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot56).

Tywin is calculating, intelligent, politically astute, ruthless, and controlling. He mistrusts laughter because of how his father Tytos had been mocked. Grand Maester Pycelle was amazed that Joanna could make him laugh three times (wiki:Tywin_Lannister.cite_ref-TWOIAF). He is especially talented at raising funds, leading to the jest that he must "shit gold" (wiki:Tywin_Lannister.cite_ref-Racok30).

## Narrative Arc

As a boy, ten-year-old Tywin alone spoke out against his sister Genna's betrothal to Emmon Frey (wiki:Tywin_Lannister.cite_ref-Raffc33). He served as royal page to King Aegon V and befriended Prince Aerys Targaryen and Steffon Baratheon. He fought in the War of the Ninepenny Kings and earned his knighthood (wiki:Tywin_Lannister.cite_ref-TWOIAF).

After returning from the Stepstones, Tywin sought to restore House Lannister's prestige. He demanded repayment of his father's loans, and when Houses Reyne and Tarbeck rebelled, Tywin crushed them utterly — destroying Tarbeck Hall and flooding the mines of Castamere, extinguishing both houses. The event inspired "The Rains of Castamere" (wiki:Tywin_Lannister.cite_ref-TWOIAF).

The newly-crowned Aerys II appointed Tywin Hand of the King in 262 AC. Tywin proved a brilliant administrator and the Seven Kingdoms prospered for a decade. He married his cousin Joanna in 263 AC; she bore twins Cersei and Jaime in 266 AC and died birthing Tyrion in 273 AC (wiki:Tywin_Lannister.cite_ref-TWOIAF).

As Aerys grew paranoid and jealous, the relationship soured. Aerys refused to betroth Rhaegar to Cersei. When Aerys named Jaime to the Kingsguard — robbing Tywin of his heir — Tywin resigned as Hand (wiki:Tywin_Lannister.cite_ref-TWOIAF).

During Robert's Rebellion, Tywin waited until the outcome was clear, then sacked King's Landing. His forces killed Princess Elia, her children, and the Targaryen remnants. Robert pardoned the brutality (wiki:Tywin_Lannister.cite_ref-Ragot55).

In the War of the Five Kings, Tywin commands the Lannister armies. After setbacks against Robb Stark, he marches from Harrenhal to relieve King's Landing at the Battle of the Blackwater, winning the battle and reclaiming the Handship. He orchestrates the Red Wedding with Walder Frey and Roose Bolton, destroying Robb's cause (wiki:Tywin_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos53).

Tywin is shot by Tyrion with a crossbow on the privy after Tyrion discovers Shae in Tywin's bed. His body is displayed in the Great Sept, where the stench becomes notable (wiki:Tywin_Lannister.cite_ref-Rasos77, cite_ref-Raffc7).
