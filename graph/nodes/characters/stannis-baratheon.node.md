## Origins

### Early life

Born in [264 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Cal_12-1) Stannis is the second son of Lady [Cassana Estermont](wiki:Cassana_Estermont) and [Steffon Baratheon](wiki:Steffon_Baratheon), [Lord of Storm's End](wiki:Lord_of_Storm's_End) and head of [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon). He has an older brother, [Robert](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), and a younger brother, [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_targaryen_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

When Stannis was no more than four years old, his father took him and Robert to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Stannis believed he saw King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), holding court. Stannis was impressed, and he and Robert agreed that the king had been as noble as the [skulls of the Targaryen dragons](wiki:Skulls_of_the_Targaryen_dragons) were fearsome. It was years later that Steffon told him that it had been the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), whom they had seen, as King Aerys had cut himself on the throne earlier that day and Tywin had taken over for him.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

As a lad, Stannis found an injured goshawk and nursed it back to health. He named the bird [Proudwing](wiki:Proudwing), but his brother Robert called it Weakwing, as it would never fly higher than the treetops and never soared. In contrast, Robert owned a gyrfalcon named [Thunderclap](wiki:Thunderclap) which never missed a strike. Eventually, Stannis's great-uncle, Ser [Harbert](wiki:Harbert), convinced him to abandon the bird, stating that Stannis was making a fool of himself with it.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-7)

Growing up, Stannis always stood in the shadow of his older brother. Whatever Stannis did, Robert had done first and better.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-8) These feelings remained until adulthood.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) [Cressen](wiki:Cressen), the [maester](wiki:Maester) at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), came to care deeply for Stannis, moreso than for his brothers, as he felt Stannis was the one who needed him the most, as the most unloved of the three.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-9) Even in his youth, Stannis had been an introverted child, who was mature beyond his years, solemn and joyless.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rawoiafstannis_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

In [278 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Stannis's parents were sent on a mission by King Aerys II to [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) to find a bride for [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), Stannis's cousin and the crown prince, but they were unsuccessful.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Maester Cressen remembers how Lord Steffon had written to him a fortnight before his return, stating that he had found a splendid [fool](wiki:Fool) in Volantis who would delight Robert and who might even teach Stannis how to laugh, in time.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-10) Stannis and Robert were both present at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) when Steffon and Cassana were expected to return from their voyage, looking on from the parapets of the castle. When the *[Windproud](wiki:Windproud)* came in sight of Storm's End, the two brothers witnessed how the ship was smashed against the rocks in [Shipbreaker Bay](wiki:Shipbreaker_Bay) and swallowed by the sea, with Steffon and Cassana drowning.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-11) According to Stannis, he lost his faith in the [Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) that day.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-8) The only survivor of the wreck was the fool, [Patchface](wiki:Patchface), who washed up on the shore on the third day.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-12)

### Robert's Rebellion

When King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) demanded the heads of [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) after the executions of Lord [Rickard](wiki:Rickard_Stark) and [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark), Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) instead raised his banners in revolt. Eddard and Robert returned to their homes to call their own banners during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) against [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). The resulting decision that Stannis faced—whether to be loyal to his older brother and liege, Robert, or to his king, Aerys—was a difficult one.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) Stannis was at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) when Robert returned from the [battles at Summerhall](wiki:Battles_at_Summerhall) with his prisoners, whom he kept at Storm's End, and eventually befriended. Stannis warned his brother that the men he had befriended were men who had wanted to deliver him to Aerys to be burned; while Stannis would have thrown them in a dungeon, Robert made them his allies.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1)

Stannis remained behind at Storm's End when Robert marched again. After Robert was defeated at [Ashford](wiki:Battle_of_Ashford), forces from the [Reach](wiki:Reach) besieged the seat of [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon), surrounding Storm's End on land and blockading it by sea. [Donal Noye](wiki:Donal_Noye) smithed for Stannis and lost an arm during the [siege of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Stannis successfully held Storm's End against the siege by Lords [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) and [Paxter Redwyne](wiki:Paxter_Redwyne),(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2) which lasted a major part of the war. His garrison held under desperate conditions, only avoiding starvation by the timely intervention of [Davos](wiki:Davos_Seaworth), a notorious [smuggler](wiki:Smuggler), whose cargo of onions and salt fish allowed them to hold the fortress.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-13)

After the siege of Storm's End was lifted by Lord Eddard Stark, Stannis [knighted](wiki:Knight) Davos and, taking him into his service, allowed the new [landed knight](wiki:Landed_knight) to choose the name Seaworth for [his new House](wiki:House_Seaworth).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-14) True to Stannis's sense of justice, he also punished Davos for his crimes as a smuggler by removing the first joint from each finger of Davos's left hand, as payment for his past crimes. Davos accepted the punishment on the condition that Stannis carry out the sentence himself, which Stannis did, using a cleaver for a cleaner cut.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-15)

### Lord of Dragonstone

Following the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing) and the surrender of the [Tyrells](wiki:House_Tyrell) at [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), the new king, [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), charged his brother Stannis with building a new [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) to seize the [island of Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)), the last stronghold in the possession of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). A fierce storm destroyed the Targaryen fleet which had been protecting the island, while Dowager Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) died in labor at the [castle of Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone). Targaryen loyalists led by Ser [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry) smuggled the two Targaryen children, [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) and [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), from the island before Stannis's [assault on Dragonstone](wiki:Assault_on_Dragonstone). Robert blamed Stannis for letting the Targaryen heirs escape, although the two children had been spirited to safety in the [Braavosian Coastland](wiki:Braavosian_Coastland) shortly before Stannis had set sail.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-16)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

Stannis was displeased to discover that King Robert had granted him Dragonstone, instead of the wealthier Storm's End, which was given to their younger brother [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon). Stannis resented this and believed it to be an intentional slight,(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-17) which Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) agrees with.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2) According to a semi-canon source, however, Robert had not necessarily meant it as such.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Baratheonbrothers_37-0) The castle had traditionally been the seat of the heir to the Iron Throne, the [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone), during most of the Targaryen dynasty, so the then-childless Robert was granting it to his heir at that time, Stannis.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Baratheonbrothers_37-1) Now the [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), Stannis became the head of [House Baratheon of Dragonstone](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_Dragonstone). Stannis and Maester [Cressen](wiki:Cressen) came to Dragonstone around [287 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-18)

Stannis wed Lady [Selyse](wiki:Selyse_Florent) of [House Florent](wiki:House_Florent). While Stannis and Selyse were dancing during [the feast](wiki:Wedding_of_Stannis_Baratheon_and_Selyse_Florent), King Robert deflowered [Delena Florent](wiki:Delena_Florent), a cousin of Selyse, on the wedding bed. This coupling led to the birth of [Edric Storm](wiki:Edric_Storm), the only officially acknowledged [bastard](wiki:Bastard) of the king. Stannis insisted that the boy was a slight upon the honor of his wife's house and promptly shipped him off to his brother Renly at Storm's End.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0) Stannis only does his duty in the marriage bed once or twice a year, taking no joy in it.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-19) Though he once hoped for sons, Stannis has only had a daughter, [Shireen](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon), with Selyse.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-20)

### Master of Ships

Lord Stannis was appointed to the [small council](wiki:Small_council)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-3) of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) as the [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships), and he left [Selyse](wiki:Selyse_Florent) and [Shireen](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon) on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) when he traveled to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-21). Stannis only occasionally writes to Selyse, and his visits are even fewer.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-22)

In [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), Stannis led the rebuilt [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet) to the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea) to help suppress [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion). Stannis lured [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy), [Lord Captain of the Iron Fleet](wiki:Lord_Captain_of_the_Iron_Fleet), into a trap in the [Straits of Fair Isle](wiki:Straits_of_Fair_Isle), and he destroyed much of the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) in the ensuing [sea battle off Fair Isle](wiki:Sea_battle_off_Fair_Isle).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Afterwards, Stannis subdued [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk) in his brother's name.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

Stannis once led a fleet to [Sisterton](wiki:Sisterton) to suppress smuggling from the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters). He hanged a dozen friends of Lord [Godric Borrell](wiki:Godric_Borrell), and he threatened to hang Godric himself if any ships are wrecked if the [Night Lamp](wiki:Night_Lamp) were to go dark.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

Ser [Axell Florent](wiki:Axell_Florent), Selyse's uncle, has served as the [castellan](wiki:Castellan) of Dragonstone for ten years while Stannis has been away at King's Landing.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-9)

### Recent History

Despite his loyalty and service to his elder brother, Stannis has always felt underappreciated by King [Robert](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon). Instead of thanking Stannis for holding [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) against the [Tyrells](wiki:House_Tyrell) during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), the king thanked his old friend, Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), for lifting the siege. Instead of thanking Stannis for [capturing Dragonstone](wiki:Assault_on_Dragonstone) for him, Robert blamed Stannis for the escape of [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) and [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), even though they left Dragonstone before Stannis had set sail.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-23)

Stannis has helped Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), Robert's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), in ruling the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), but he has received little or no acknowledgment or thanks from his elder brother, who spends his time hunting, drinking, and whoring.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-24) Stannis feels that Storm's End, the ancestral seat of House Baratheon, was his by rights after Robert took the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), and has told Robert so on multiple occasions. In due time, Storm's End was given to young [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon) instead.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-3)

King Robert once rejected Stannis's proposal to outlaw brothels.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-4) Stannis has outlawed [prostitution](wiki:Prostitution) on his island of Dragonstone.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-10) Dragonstone's maester, the elderly [Cressen](wiki:Cressen), fell and broke his hip in [297 AC](wiki:297_AC).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-25)

Eventually, Stannis began to suspect that the children of Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)—Prince [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), Princess [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), and Prince [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon)—were not actually Robert's. Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys), the [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers), suggests the idea had been mentioned to Stannis by an unnamed individual.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) Convinced that Robert would not believe him, Stannis confided his suspicions with Lord Arryn, the King's Hand.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5)

Stannis and Jon investigated the matter, visiting several of Robert's [baseborn](wiki:Bastardy) children in the capital, including visits to the shop of [Tobho Mott](wiki:Tobho_Mott) and to [Chataya's brothel](wiki:Chataya's_brothel).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-5)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-1) According to [Gendry](wiki:Gendry), Tobho's apprentice, Stannis and Jon often went riding together, with Renly excluded.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-6)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2) During their investigations, Jon agreed with Stannis that his young son and heir, [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn), would be [fostered](wiki:Foster) with Stannis on Dragonstone.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-7) Jon had begun to gather proof when he suddenly died,(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-6) with Stannis believing that Cersei had Jon killed.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-7)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)

## Appearances & Description

Like his brothers, King [Robert](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and Lord [Renly](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), Stannis is a large man—tall, broad-shouldered, and sinewy.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) notes that Stannis towers over him.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Stannis has dark blue eyes and a heavy brow. His head has only a fringe of black hair "like the shadow of a crown", and his blue-black beard is cropped closely on his square jaw.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) Stannis's face has a tightness to it like cured leather, and he has hollow cheeks, and thin, pale lips.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2) He often clenches his jaw,(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) and he grinds his teeth when he is angry.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

Stannis is a serious, stubborn, rarely-forgiving, hard man with a strong sense of duty and justice.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) He is proud and protective of his honor.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) In his adult life, Stannis is increasingly embittered by the lack of affection he receives from Robert, even though he serves the king as well as he can.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-4)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rawoiafstannis_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)

Stannis is an accomplished commander, sailor, and warrior(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) with no thirst for battle, instead commanding from the rear.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) He has never had the affection of nobles or [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk),(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-5)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-4)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) but he can command fierce loyalty from soldiers and [knights](wiki:Knight).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) Feasts at his court are quiet and dignified.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-6)

Stannis is not a man for easy courtesies,(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) and he has always been uncomfortable around women, even his own wife, Lady [Selyse](wiki:Selyse_Florent) of [House Florent](wiki:House_Florent). During his years serving on the [small council](wiki:Small_council) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), he has left his wife on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)). Stannis rarely sleeps with Selyse and takes no joy in it, and they have one daughter, [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-7)

Influenced by [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), a [red priestess](wiki:Red_priest) of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor), the Lord of Light, Stannis adopts a new sigil which depicts the [stag](wiki:Stag) of [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon) enclosed within a red heart surrounded by orange flame.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) Stannis dresses more plainly than many of his noble peers.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-5)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3) He sometimes wears a grey wool tunic, a dark red mantle, and a plain black leather belt from which hang his sword and dagger.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) On campaign he wears a studded leather jerkin over quilted doublet, worn boots, breeches of brown roughspun, and a belt studded with [garnets](wiki:Garnet) and yellow [topaz](wiki:Topaz).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-4) Other armor includes wool and boiled leather(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) or grey plate inlaid with a flaming heart.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

Stannis's squires are [Devan Seaworth](wiki:Devan_Seaworth) and [Bryen Farring](wiki:Bryen_Farring),(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-6) and Ser [Andrew Estermont](wiki:Andrew_Estermont) served as his squire years ago.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Stannis is cordial but not friendly with Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), Robert's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) Stannis is also not a friend of Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), though he respects Ned's honor and honesty.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Stannis expects to be named the new [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) after the sudden death of Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn),(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-26)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-8) but King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) instead leaves for the [north](wiki:North) to offer the position to his old friend, [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell) and [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) A resentful Stannis leaves for [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-8) not long after with much of the [royal fleet](wiki:Royal_fleet).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

Stannis plans his next move,(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-1) believing that Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) poisoned Jon.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-27) Rather than have young [Robert Arryn](wiki:Robert_Arryn) fostered with Stannis as planned, Jon's widow [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) flees back to the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) with her son after Jon's death.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) Since Maester [Cressen](wiki:Cressen) has become ill after his fall the previous year, the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) sends [Pylos](wiki:Pylos) to Dragonstone, with the young maester arriving mere days before Stannis closes off the isle.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-28)

Eddard wonders why Stannis left [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), believing it was due to the discovery of whatever secret Jon was allegedly murdered for, although not realizing that Stannis was also slighted by Robert's selection of Eddard as his new Hand. Ned Stark, via Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle), sends a [raven](wiki:Raven) with a polite letter requesting Lord Stannis to return to his seat on the [small council](wiki:Small_council).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) Stannis does not attend the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney) in King's Landing.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-3)

During Eddard's investigation of Jon's death, he discovers that Stannis and Jon had spent a great deal of time together. When he is told Stannis and Jon visited [a brothel](wiki:Chataya's_brothel), when Stannis is normally so righteous to the point of prudish, Eddard gets closer to the truth.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-9) Stannis does not attend the [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney) in King's Landing.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-4)

Robert is mortally wounded by a [boar](wiki:Boar) while hunting in the [kingswood](wiki:Kingswood), and the dying king wants Eddard to rule as [regent](wiki:Regent) for his son, [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1) Since Joffrey is not actually Robert's son, Ned alters the phrasing of Robert's will without his knowledge and plans to bring Stannis to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) instead of Joffrey. Lord Stark tasks [Tomard](wiki:Tomard) with personally delivering a letter to Stannis on Dragonstone when the *[Wind Witch](wiki:Wind_Witch)* sails north.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2) Ned rejects Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon)'s recommendation to take Cersei's children into custody, and Lord Stark instead allies with Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish), who warns that raising Stannis to the throne will bring war.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-3) Eddard is outmaneuvered by Robert's widow, Queen Cersei, and betrayed by Lord Baelish, and Fat Tom is slain by [gold cloaks](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing) during the [arrest of Eddard Stark](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark) in the [throne room](wiki:Great_Hall_(Red_Keep)) before he can depart the capital.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) Cersei shows the letter which would have been brought to Stannis to Ned's captive daughter, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

[War breaks out](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings) in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) after Robert's death.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) During Joffrey's first court session, Stannis, his wife [Selyse](wiki:Selyse_Florent), and daughter [Shireen](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon) are listed by Grand Maester Pycelle among those required to swear fealty to the new king.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) Rather than allowing Eddard to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) after confessing to treason, Joffrey has Lord Stark publicly executed at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) Eddard's eldest son, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), is declared [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) after the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)

Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) reports that Robert's youngest brother, Renly, has been crowned at [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), and that Stannis is said to be building ships, hiring [sellswords](wiki:Sellswords), and bringing a [shadowbinder](wiki:Shadowbinder) from [Asshai](wiki:Asshai) to Dragonstone. Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), Joffrey's Hand, thinks that Stannis is a greater threat the boy king's other rivals.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

### A Clash of Kings

Stannis declares himself king on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)). He sends Ser [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) to gather support for him among the [stormlords](wiki:Stormlords), but none are willing to join him. His supporters from the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) include Lords [Ardrian Celtigar](wiki:Ardrian_Celtigar), [Monford Velaryon](wiki:Monford_Velaryon), [Duram Bar Emmon](wiki:Duram_Bar_Emmon), and [Guncer Sunglass](wiki:Guncer_Sunglass).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Stannis hires [sellswords](wiki:Sellswords) from [Myr](wiki:Myr) and [Lys](wiki:Lys),(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-29) and he also has the support of the sellsails [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan), self-styled as the [Prince of the Narrow Sea](wiki:Prince_of_the_Narrow_Sea), and [Morosh the Myrman](wiki:Morosh_the_Myrman).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) The older [maester](wiki:Maester) at Dragonstone, [Cressen](wiki:Cressen), suggests to Stannis that he makes an alliance against the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) with [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark), who has been declared [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North), but Stannis objects to ruling only half a kingdom. Cressen then proposes that Stannis sends [Shireen](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon), his only child and heir, to the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), to make an alliance with Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn). Stannis's wife, [Selyse Florent](wiki:Selyse_Florent), objects, stating that Stannis should not have to beg for help, and Stannis sides with her.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-30)

Selyse has converted to the faith of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor), the Lord of Light, giving [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre), a [red priestess](wiki:Red_priest), a base of power on Dragonstone. Selyse tells Stannis that the [red comet](wiki:Red_comet) is a sign that Stannis's time has come, and that he must sail from Dragonstone now to conquer.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-31) Selyse claims that [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon)'s men will join Stannis once Renly dies, and that Melisandre has seen Renly dead in her flames. Cressen is horrified by the thought of fratricide and objects, but Stannis dismisses him. Cressen attempts to poison Melisandre with the [strangler](wiki:Strangler), but he does and she survives unscathed.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-32) [Pylos](wiki:Pylos) henceforth serves as Stannis's sole maester at Dragonstone.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-11)

Though Stannis is not a follower of R'hllor,(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-33) he renounces the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) for the Lord of Light. [Queen's men](wiki:Queen's_men) ruin the [sept at Dragonstone](wiki:Sept_at_Dragonstone), and statues of the Seven are publicly burned before Dragonstone's gates. During this sacrifice, Melisandre proclaims that Stannis is [Azor Ahai](wiki:Azor_Ahai) reborn, and has him pull a sword, [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer), out of the burning carving of [the Mother](wiki:Mother_(the_Seven)).(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-12) In private, Sallador tells Davos that the sword is not the real Lightbringer. ### A Storm of Swords

### The Winds of Winter

At the [crofters' village](wiki:Crofters'_village), Stannis prepares for the looming battle against the [Boltons](wiki:House_Bolton). He signs a contract with [Tycho Nestoris](wiki:Tycho_Nestoris) and plans to send the banker back to the Wall for safety. Tycho brought a message from [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) informing Stannis of [Arnolf Karstark](wiki:Arnolf_Karstark)'s planned treachery, so Stannis has Arnolf, his son [Arthor](wiki:Arthor_Karstark), and his three grandsons arrested and plans to execute them. The king sends Ser [Justin Massey](wiki:Justin_Massey) to escort "[Arya Stark](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)" back to Castle Black to reunite her with Jon, in gratitude for the Lord Commander's counsel of gathering the [northern mountain clans](wiki:Northern_mountain_clans). Justin is to then travel to Braavos and use money from the Iron Bank to hire twenty thousand [sellswords](wiki:Sellswords) for Stannis. Stannis tells Justin that he may hear of Stannis's death in Braavos, but even if that news is true, the knight is to follow the orders as to seat his daughter, Princess [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon), on the Iron Throne. Stannis next receives [Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy), and informs her that her brother [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) must be executed, as showing him mercy would mean losing all of the northmen. Asha suggests that Stannis behead Theon with [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer) before a [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) at a nearby isle.(wiki:Stannis_Baratheon.cite_ref-theon1_65-1)
