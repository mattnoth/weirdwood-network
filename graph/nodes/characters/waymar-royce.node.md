## Origins

As a third son of a lord, Waymar had few chances at wealth or land and so joined the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) He was escorted by his father, Lord [Yohn Royce](wiki:Yohn_Royce), north to the [Wall](wiki:Wall). While they stayed at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) as guests on the way, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) fell wildly in love with Waymar.(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Among the brothers of the Night's Watch, Waymar's behavior and appearance earned him some ridicule behind his back, including from [Gared](wiki:Gared) and [Will](wiki:Will).(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)

Shortly before *[A Song of Ice and Fire](wiki:A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire)* begins, Lord Commander [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont) gave Waymar the command of a ranging in the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest) in search of a band of [wildling](wiki:Free_folk) raiders. He was accompanied by Gared and Will. Waymar was the least experienced of the three, having been in the Night's Watch for less than half a year, whereas Jeor counted Gared and Will among his best men. However, Waymar felt it was his due to have a command because he was a [knight](wiki:Knight), and Jeor accepted because he did not want to offend Waymar's father.(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Waymar Royce](wiki:Category:Images_of_Waymar_Royce)*

Waymar is handsome, graceful and slender, with grey eyes. He wears leather boots, woolen pants, moleskin gloves, a [sable](wiki:Sable) cloak, and ringmail over layers of wool and boiled leather, all in black.(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Waymar rides a black [destrier](wiki:Horse) and wields a [longsword](wiki:Longsword), the hilt of which is decorated with jewels.(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

Waymar displays a sense of entitlement as well as arrogance towards more experienced members of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

On the ninth day of ranging north and northest through the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest), [Will](wiki:Will) discovers a camp of eight immobile [wildlings](wiki:Free_folk). [Gared](wiki:Gared) tries to persuade Ser Waymar to head back to the [Wall](wiki:Wall), but without luck. While they approach the camp, Gared says he can feel something is wrong, but Waymar mocks the experienced ranger. Gared intends to make a fire, arguing that fire can keep some enemies away, but Waymar calls him a fool.(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4)

When they are at the wildling camp, Will and Waymar cannot find any bodies. Will climbs a tree to look for them, and both men feel markedly colder. Suddenly, Waymar is surrounded by [Others](wiki:Others). Though Waymar bravely duels an Other, he is blinded when his longsword shatters, and the group of Others swiftly kill the knight. Will stays in the trees until he is sure that the Others have left. When Will descends, he is attacked and strangled by Waymar, who has been turned into a [wight](wiki:Wights) with a blue eye.(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5) Gared escapes south of the [Wall](wiki:Wall), but he is executed by Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) for desertion.(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

[Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), laments to [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) that he gave command to someone as inexperienced as Waymar. Jeor sends [Benjen Stark](wiki:Benjen_Stark) to search for the missing rangers, but Benjen also disappears.(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

### A Clash of Kings

[Craster](wiki:Craster) tells Jeor that the three rangers stopped at [Craster's Keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep) during their search for raiders.(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

As [Tormund](wiki:Tormund)'s free folk pass through the Wall at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), they surrender treasures to the Night's Watch. One man relinquishes a broken sword with three sapphires in the hilt, possibly Waymar's sword.(wiki:Waymar_Royce.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
