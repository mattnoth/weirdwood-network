## Identity

Lann the Clever is the legendary hero who founded House Lannister in the Age of Heroes, winkling Casterly Rock from the Casterlys with only his wits (wiki:Lann_the_Clever). He is popular amongst singers and storytellers. Posthumously given the title King of the Rock (track_b: Title).

## Origins

Most tales connect Lann with the First Men and the dawn of days (wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf). In Reach tales, Lann was a bastard born to Florys the Fox or Rowan Gold-Tree, legendary daughters of Garth Greenhand. In the westerlands, it is claimed Lann deceived Garth by posing as one of his sons and stealing part of the inheritance (wiki:Lann_the_Clever). Some versions state he was an Andal adventurer, though this would predate the Andal arrival by thousands of years (wiki:Lann_the_Clever).

## Narrative Arc

Stories differ on how Lann took Casterly Rock from House Casterly. In the most common version, Lann stripped naked, coated himself in butter, and squeezed through a secret cleft in the stone. Once inside he confused the sleeping Casterlys by whispering threats, howling like a demon, stealing treasures, and generally making them believe the Rock was haunted until they fled (wiki:Lann_the_Clever). He is said to have fathered a hundred sons and a hundred daughters, and to have died in his 312th year (track_b: Issue, Died).
