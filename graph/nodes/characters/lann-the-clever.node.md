## Origins

### Origin

Most tales connect Lann with the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) and the [dawn of days](wiki:Dawn_of_days)(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) or the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) In some tales of the [Reach](wiki:Reach), Lann was a [bastard](wiki:Bastard) born to [Florys the Fox](wiki:Florys_the_Fox) or [Rowan Gold-Tree](wiki:Rowan_Gold-Tree), legendary daughters of [Garth Greenhand](wiki:Garth_Greenhand).(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_garth_greenhand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) In the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), however, it is more commonly claimed that Lann deceived Garth by posing as one of his plentiful sons and making off with part of the inheritance that belonged to Garth's children.(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_garth_greenhand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Some versions state he was an [Andal](wiki:Andal) adventurer from across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), although this would have been thousands of years before the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals).(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

### Casterly Rock

Stories differ in the method by which Lann took [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) from [House Casterly](wiki:House_Casterly). In the most common version, Lann, having stripped naked and coated himself in butter, squeezed through a secret cleft in the stone. Once inside he confused the sleeping Casterlys by whispering threats, howling like a demon, stealing treasures from one brother and placing them in the bedchamber of another, and rigging traps. Thus, he set the Casterlys at odds with one another and convinced them their seat was haunted.(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

In another version of the tale, Lann used the cleft to fill the Rock with [mice](wiki:Mice), rats, and other vermin to drive the Casterlys out. In yet another he smuggled inside a pride of [lions](wiki:Lion) which devoured Lord Casterly and his sons. Lann then claimed the lord's wife and daughters for himself. In the most notorious tale, Lann had his way with Casterly maidens while they slept, and nine months later the women gave birth to golden-haired children while insisting they had never slept with a man.(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)

Lann is said to have stolen gold from the sun to brighten his hair.(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) He supposedly lived to the age of three hundred and twelve, siring a hundred bold sons and a hundred lissome daughters, all fair of face, clean of limb and with hair "as golden as the sun". In a few generations, Lann's descendants were so numerous that Casterly Rock could not contain them all. The members of the lesser branches made their home in a nearby village that grew into the city of [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport).(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3)

### Legacy

[Maesters](wiki:Maesters) are skeptical of Lann the Clever's existence.(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-4) Archmaester [Perestan](wiki:Perestan) proposed that Lann was a retainer or guard in service to House Casterly who impregnated the lord's daughter—or daughters—and persuaded the father to give him the girl in marriage. Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) considers that if this lord had no trueborn sons, the Rock eventually passed to said daughter and Lann. Whatever the case, House Casterly vanished from chronicles and histories and in its place appears the hitherto-unknown [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister).(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-5) Lann is not believed to have claimed the title of [king](wiki:King_of_the_Rock), although some tales have called him such posthumously.(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-6)

Some claim that Lann's [ghost](wiki:Ghosts) still haunts Casterly Rock.(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) [Lann's Point](wiki:Lann's_Point) is presumably named after Lann the Clever.(wiki:Lann_the_Clever.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-7)
