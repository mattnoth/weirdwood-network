## Origins

### Aegon's Conquest

After the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) and [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End) had fallen under the control of [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), Torrhen called his banners as [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North). He was aware that, due to the vast distance of the [north](wiki:North), it would take a long time to assemble his army. After the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire), whilst at [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), Aegon I learned that Torrhen had crossed the [Neck](wiki:Neck) and entered the riverlands, leading an army of thirty thousand men. Aegon abandoned his plans to march south, and at once started his way north, racing ahead of his army on [Balerion](wiki:Balerion), his [dragon](wiki:Dragon). Both [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen) and [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), as well as all the lords who had bent the knee to Aegon after the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal) and the Field of Fire, were sent word of this movement. When Torrhen reached the banks of the [Trident](wiki:Trident), he found a host of forty-five thousand men and three dragons waiting for him.(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Torrhen's scouts had reported seeing the ruins of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), and the king had heard accounts of the Field of Fire as well. Some of Torrhen's lords urged him to attack, but he realized that a similar fate would await him if he tried to force a crossing. Other lords urged the King of the North to fall back to [Moat Cailin](wiki:Moat_Cailin) and make his stand there. Torrhen's bastard half-brother, [Brandon Snow](wiki:Brandon_Snow), offered to cross the Trident at dark to kill the dragons.(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

In the end, Torrhen sent Brandon Snow to Aegon's camp, with three [maesters](wiki:Maesters), to treat with Aegon. Messages went back and forth that night, and the next morning, Torrhen crossed the Trident, knelt and laid his crown at the feet of Aegon I Targaryen, and swore fealty. For his surrender, he was named the [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell) and [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North).(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Ever since, Torrhen is known as the *King Who Knelt*.(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) It is unknown what Aegon did with Torrhen's crown.(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Racok7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The [Inn of the Kneeling Man](wiki:Inn_of_the_Kneeling_Man) later arose where Torrhen had bent the knee.(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

### Warden of the North

After Torrhen's surrender, the [Company of the Rose](wiki:Company_of_the_Rose), a sellsword company, was formed by men and (according to some accounts) women from the [north](wiki:North) who refused to bend the knee to [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen). These men and women willingly chose exile across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea).(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Torrhen's sons did not agree with the rule of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) either, with some speaking of rebelling and raising the banner of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark).(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Since the fleet of [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) had been lost in the [battle in the waters off Gulltown](wiki:Battle_in_the_waters_off_Gulltown), King Aegon relied upon the Starks to suppress the [Sistermen's Rebellion](wiki:Sistermen's_Rebellion). Lord Torrhen sent Ser [Warrick Manderly](wiki:Warrick_Manderly) to the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters) to successfully end the uprising of Queen [Marla Sunderland](wiki:Marla_Sunderland).(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Torrhen also sent longships from [Bear Island](wiki:Bear_Island) to aid Aegon in his [invasion of the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

Lord Torrhen's daughter was married to [Ronnel Arryn](wiki:Ronnel_Arryn_(king)), [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie).(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) This marriage had been arranged by Queen [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen), as an attempt to knit the new realm together. There are letters at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel) which suggest that Lord Stark only agreed to this match after much protest, and that his sons had refused to attend the wedding.(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) Lord Ronnel would be killed early in the reign of King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) by his own brother, [Jonos](wiki:Jonos_Arryn).(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) It is unknown whether Torrhen's daughter was still alive at this point.

Torrhen was entombed in the [crypts of Winterfell](wiki:Crypts_of_Winterfell) after his death.(wiki:Torrhen_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
