## Identity

Vylarr is captain of the House Lannister guards in King's Landing, succeeding Tregar in the role (wiki:Vylarr).

## Allegiances

## Narrative Arc

Vylarr commands one hundred Lannister guardsmen in King's Landing (wiki:Vylarr.cite_ref-Racok3). When Tyrion Lannister arrives as acting Hand, he orders Vylarr to remove the heads of Eddard Stark's retinue from spikes at the Red Keep and give them to the silent sisters (wiki:Vylarr.cite_ref-Racok7). Vylarr escorts Tyrion through the streets and accompanies Cersei touring the city gates (wiki:Vylarr.cite_ref-Racok8). Cersei orders Vylarr's men to imprison begging brothers (wiki:Vylarr.cite_ref-Racok17).

Tyrion later commands Vylarr to escort Ser Cleos Frey to Riverrun with peace terms, taking all Lannister guardsmen — a ploy to deprive Cersei of her red cloaks (wiki:Vylarr.cite_ref-Racok17). Unbeknownst to Vylarr, Tyrion has included instructions for Ser Cleos that serve his own purposes (wiki:Vylarr.cite_ref-Racok25).
