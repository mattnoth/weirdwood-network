## Identity

Prince Rhaegar Targaryen was the firstborn son and heir of King Aerys II Targaryen and Queen Rhaella. As heir-apparent he was Prince of Dragonstone. He was slain by Robert Baratheon at the Battle of the Trident in 283 AC (wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen).

## Origins

Born 259 AC at Summerhall, the stormlands, on the day of the tragedy there (track_b: Born). Valyrian race, Crownlands culture (track_b: Race, Culture).

## Appearances & Description

Beautiful, with deep purple or indigo eyes and silver-blond hair. Tall, with long elegant fingers. Wore night-black plate armor with the three-headed dragon in rubies on the breastplate, golden ringmail beneath, and a helm with gold, orange, and red silk streamers. A skilled musician who loved his silver-stringed harp more than his lance. Bookish as a child — people jested his mother had swallowed books and a candle. Melancholic, deeply affected by "the shadow of Summerhall." Well-loved by the smallfolk (wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen).

## Narrative Arc

Rhaegar read obsessively as a child, then at some point was motivated by something he read to become a warrior. Knighted at seventeen, he became a skilled fighter though never enthusiastic about combat. His closest friend was Ser Arthur Dayne; his squires were Myles Mooton and Richard Lonmouth; Jon Connington was a devoted friend.

At the Tourney at Harrenhal in 281 AC, Rhaegar defeated Ser Arthur Dayne, Ser Barristan Selmy, and others, winning the crown of love and beauty. He passed over his wife Elia Martell and laid the blue winter roses in Lyanna Stark's lap — the moment that "was all the ugliness in the world." His abduction of Lyanna triggered Robert's Rebellion.

Rhaegar married Elia Martell and fathered Rhaenys and Aegon. After the Battle of the Bells, Aerys finally sent Rhaegar to command the royal forces. At the ruby ford on the Trident, Robert killed Rhaegar with a blow from his warhammer that scattered the rubies from his breastplate. Robert says Rhaegar "won" despite dying; Ned thinks Rhaegar would not have frequented brothels. Barristan believes he would have been better than any of the three kings he served.

Rhaegar is frequently referenced throughout the series — by Daenerys, Ned, Robert, Cersei, Jaime, Jon Connington, and Barristan among others.
