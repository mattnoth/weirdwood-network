## Origins

### Early life

Prince Rhaegar was the firstborn son of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) and Queen [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen). He was born at [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall) in [259 AC](wiki:259_AC), on the same day as the [great tragedy there](wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafrhaegar_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) As a child he read obsessively, to the point that jests were made about his habits. He became a noted warrior later in life, although he did not initially seem inclined to martial habits. However, apparently by something he had read, Rhaegar became motivated to become a warrior.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-5)

At the age of seventeen, Rhaegar was [knighted](wiki:Knight),(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-6)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1) and from all reports grew into a skilled and capable fighter. The prince always distinguished himself well at [tourneys](wiki:Tourney), although he seldom entered the lists. Unlike warriors such as [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) or [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), Rhaegar was not enthusiastic about fighting.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-6)

Rhaegar's squires were [Myles Mooton](wiki:Myles_Mooton) and [Richard Lonmouth](wiki:Richard_Lonmouth), and after he knighted them they remained close companions. [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), whom he had squired with,(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) was a good friend to Rhaegar as well. Returning from a trip to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), Rhaegar once visited the [Connington](wiki:House_Connington) seat of [Griffin's Roost](wiki:Griffin's_Roost). His songs brought the castle's women to tears, while Lord [Armond Connington](wiki:Armond_Connington) sought [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen)'s support against rival [House Morrigen](wiki:House_Morrigen).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Rhaegar's closest and oldest friend, however, was Ser [Arthur Dayne](wiki:Arthur_Dayne),(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-4) the [Sword of the Morning](wiki:Sword_of_the_Morning), trusting him more than Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

Rhaegar often liked to visit the ruins of Summerhall with only his [harp](wiki:Harp) and when he returned he sang songs of such beauty they could reduce women to tears. Although Rhaegar was often dour, private and bookish, [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) noted at the [tournament in honor of Viserys's birth](wiki:Tournament_in_honor_of_Viserys's_birth) in [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) in [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) that the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) cheered for Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) twice as much as for King Aerys II, but only half as loudly for Tywin as for Rhaegar.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-7) Rhaegar fought well in the tourney besting a dozen skilled knights, among them Barristan Selmy, [Gerion](wiki:Gerion_Lannister), and [Tygett Lannister](wiki:Tygett_Lannister) but was defeated in the champion's tilt by his friend Arthur Dayne.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2) Aerys refused Tywin's offer at the tourney to betroth Cersei to Rhaegar.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-8) Rhaegar was also defeated by Barristan Selmy in the champion's tilt during a [tourney at Storm's End](wiki:Tourney_at_Storm's_End).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-7)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)

Rhaegar was born at a time when the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen) had declined, and once he came of age, there was no sister, or anyone else of their bloodline, available for him to marry.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-SSM_33-0)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) King Aerys sent his first cousin, Lord [Steffon Baratheon](wiki:Steffon_Baratheon), to seek a bride for Rhaegar.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-1) Despite [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) blood still being present in [Essos](wiki:Essos), Steffon could not find appropriate females of noble-enough birth for Rhaegar to wed.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-2)

After this in early [279 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Rhaegar was formally betrothed to the [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) princess, [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), the younger sister of [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne). They married the following year in [280 AC](wiki:280_AC), a lavish ceremony was held at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor). His father did not attend the [wedding](wiki:Marriage) as he was paranoid about an assassination attempt and nor did he permit the young Prince [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) to attend. Rhaegar and his father's relationship was straining at this point, and he and his new bride took up residence on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) instead of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Rhaegar and Elia had their first child, a girl named [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)),(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) in [280 AC](wiki:280_AC). When the babe was presented at court, Rhaegar's mother, Queen Rhaella, embraced her grandchild warmly while King Aerys remarked: "she smells Dornish". The relationship between Aerys and Rhaegar became more and more estranged.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-3)

Elia and Rhaegar had a son they named [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)). Elia, due to her delicate health, was bed-ridden for half a year after giving birth to Rhaenys and nearly died giving birth to Aegon, after which the [maesters](wiki:Maesters) told Rhaegar she would be unable to have any more children.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)

Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), whom Rhaegar corresponded with via [raven messages](wiki:Raven), remembers that Rhaegar believed his child Aegon to be [the prince that was promised](wiki:The_prince_that_was_promised).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)

When Lord Tywin resigned his position as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and left court, the new focus of King Aerys's mistrust and paranoia was his own son and heir, Prince Rhaegar. At court, there was growing tension between factions loyal to the king and to the prince. Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) dispatched a letter to the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), writing that tensions and division at court strongly resembled those before the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons). Pycelle was fearful a civil war would break out unless some accord could be reached that would satisfy both factions.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

### Tourney at Harrenhal

In [280 AC](wiki:280_AC),(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-36) Lord [Walter Whent](wiki:Walter_Whent) announced a tourney would be held at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) to rival any previous tournament. It is believed by some that the tourney was secretly arranged and financed by Prince Rhaegar, as a pretext, so Rhaegar could meet up with the great lords of the realm to discuss arranging a [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) and the removal of his father. The tournament was announced by Walter shortly after his brother, Ser [Oswell](wiki:Oswell_Whent) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), visited his older brother. When Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) alerted [Aerys II](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) to this possibility, the king decided to attend the tourney.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1)

During the great [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal), Rhaegar seemed unstoppable and defeated even Ser [Arthur Dayne](wiki:Arthur_Dayne). Taking the [winter rose](wiki:Blue_winter_rose) crown for the [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty), he revealed his interest in [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) by passing over his wife, Princess [Elia of Dorne](wiki:Elia_Martell), and setting it in Lyanna's lap. [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) later recalled that moment as "when all the smiles died".(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-8) The next year, Rhaegar seemingly kidnapped Lyanna, for reasons unknown. This act ultimately triggered [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) and the downfall of the [Targaryen dynasty](wiki:House_Targaryen).

### Robert's Rebellion

Some believe that Rhaegar spent the beginning of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), also known as the War of the Usurper, with [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) at the [tower of joy](wiki:Tower_of_joy) in the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). King Aerys sent Ser [Gerold Hightower](wiki:Gerold_Hightower), the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), to retrieve Rhaegar. Leaving Ser Gerold, Ser [Arthur Dayne](wiki:Arthur_Dayne), and Ser [Oswell Whent](wiki:Oswell_Whent) at the tower,(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafrhaegar_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-1) Rhaegar returned to the [crownlands](wiki:Crownlands) and took command of the Targaryen army after the defeat of his friend [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) in the [battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells). Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) was left at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) to protect Rhaegar's father and family.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

Rhaegar met Lord [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) in combat at a ford during the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), where the pair had a legendary duel in the raging rivers of the [Trident](wiki:Trident). Rhaegar, despite wounding Robert, was struck down with a massive blow to the chest from [Robert's warhammer](wiki:Robert's_warhammer), which scattered the [rubies](wiki:Rubies) encrusted in Rhaegar's armor into the water. While Rhaegar lay dead in the stream, soldiers of both armies scrabbled in the water to search for the rubies. The location was named the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford) thereafter. Rhaegar died with Lyanna Stark's name on his lips.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiafrhaegar_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-2) According to a semi-canon source, Rhaegar's body was cremated, as is [traditional for fallen Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-38)

Rhaegar's wife, [Elia](wiki:Elia_Martell), and their children were murdered by Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) and Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch) during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing). Amory dragged the screaming girl, Princess [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)), from under her father's bed and stabbed her to death. Gregor, whom Rhaegar had knighted, killed the infant Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)) by dashing his head against the wall while his mother watched. He then raped and killed Elia while still covered in the blood and brains of her son.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Meanwhile, Jaime Lannister killed Rhaegar's father, King Aerys, to prevent the [wildfire plot](wiki:Wildfire_plot), earning himself the [Kingslayer](wiki:Kingslayer) sobriquet.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)

The circumstances of Rhaegar's taking of Lyanna, and of her reaction to it, remain unknown. The [Starks](wiki:House_Stark), [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark) in particular, saw it as an abduction, and Robert Baratheon imagined that Rhaegar was raping Lyanna while he kept her prisoner. However, the Targaryen tradition sees Rhaegar acting out of true love for Lyanna. Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), Lyanna's brother, and Lord [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed) found her at Rhaegar's tower of joy in Dorne.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiaflyanna_stark.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0) Lyanna died in the Red Mountains after Eddard's arrival and his [defeat of the three Kingsguard](wiki:Combat_at_the_tower_of_joy). She made her brother give a promise, the content of which remains unknown.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0)

What all sides seem to agree upon is that the meeting of Rhaegar and Lyanna was fateful as it led to immense bloodshed. This leads to imaginations of an alternate history, in which Rhaegar never became infatuated with Lyanna. [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) blames his sister [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) once for the downfall of the Targaryens, because she was not born earlier, as Rhaegar could then have married her and might have never become interested in Lyanna.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-9) Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) imagines that, had King Aerys accepted Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s proposal of marrying his daughter [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) to Rhaegar, the dragon prince might have never looked twice at Lyanna.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

## Appearances & Description

The beautiful(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-12) Rhaegar had [deep purple](wiki:Purple_eyes)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) or indigo colored eyes.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) He had long, elegant fingers,(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) and was taller than his younger brother, [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), would be in his adulthood.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Rhaegar's hair was similar(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) to the silver-blond of Viserys and their sister, [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) The hair of [Aurane Waters](wiki:Aurane_Waters) is reminiscent of Rhaegar's,(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) although the Bastard of Driftmark's is more silvery than gold.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Rhaegar owned night-black plate armor, with the three-headed dragon of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) decorated in [rubies](wiki:Rubies) on its breastplate,(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) and underneath the plate he wore [golden](wiki:Gold) ringmail.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) Rhaegar decorated his helm with gold, orange, and red silken streamers resembling flames.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3)

Rhaegar was an intelligent young man, who excelled at anything to which he put his mind,(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) and grew to be a great [knight](wiki:Knight) and a skilled musician.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) The latter, however, held his preference; men said Rhaegar loved his [silver](wiki:Silver)-stringed [harp](wiki:Harp) more than he loved his lance.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) has described Rhaegar as valiant, honorable, and noble(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) while [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) has called him determined, deliberate, dutiful, and single-minded.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1) The crown prince was said to have been uninterested in the play of other children as a boy, but bookish "to a fault". He learned to read at such an early age that people jested that his mother [Rhaella](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) had swallowed some books and a candle during her pregnancy.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2) Later in life, however, Rhaegar did acquire several trusted and loyal companions.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-3) Rhaegar was deeply affected by "the shadow of [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall)", because he was "[born in grief](wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall)" and was considered melancholic at times.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2) At the same time, Summerhall was also Rhaegar's favorite place.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-3)

The prince was well-loved by the people of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4) during his lifetime and is still fondly remembered to this day.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) While [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) despises Rhaegar and accuses him of having raped [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), the only thought of [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), Lyanna's brother, is he did not think Rhaegar frequented brothels.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) remembers the iron tones of Rhaegar's voice.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1) [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), who served three kings, thinks that Rhaegar would have been better than any of them.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) His sister, Daenerys, is frequently compared to Rhaegar as a compliment.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-4) People who had romantic feelings toward Rhaegar include [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-5) and [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-27) [Rhaegar Frey](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey) is presumably named after the Prince of Dragonstone.

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Discussing [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) with Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) vilifies Rhaegar for raping Ned's sister, [Lyanna](wiki:Lyanna_Stark).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0)

When the royal party moving south from [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) reaches the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford), [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) and her friend [Mycah](wiki:Mycah) decide to search the [Trident](wiki:Trident) for [rubies](wiki:Rubies) from Rhaegar's armor.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

During her fever dream, [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) sees her brother Rhaegar mounted on a stallion as black as his armor. Fire glimmers red through the narrow eye slit of his helm. When Daenerys lifts his polished black visor, the face within is her own.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)

When Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) discusses with his brother Ser [Kevan](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) and his son [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) the dire situation of the [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) forces after the defeats in the [battle in the Whispering Wood](wiki:Battle_in_the_Whispering_Wood) and the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), Tyrion jests that his father should take comfort from the fact that Prince Rhaegar is still dead.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

### A Clash of Kings

The green and bronze [Rhaegal](wiki:Rhaegal), one of three [dragons](wiki:Dragon) hatched by Daenerys, is named in honor of her brave brother who was killed on the green banks of the Trident.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)

Rhaegar appears in Daenerys's vision in the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying). He discusses with his wife, [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), what he will name his son ([Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar))) and his destiny. Rhaegar states that the boy is [the prince that was promised](wiki:The_prince_that_was_promised) and his is [the song of ice and fire](wiki:The_song_of_ice_and_fire). Dany also has a vision of Rhaegar's death at the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford) of the [Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) at the hands of [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3)

### A Storm of Swords

While aboard the ship *[Balerion](wiki:Balerion_(ship))*, [Arstan Whitebeard](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) tells Daenerys that Rhaegar was able and dutiful. While Rhaegar was bookish in his youth, he decided to become a warrior after reading about [something unknown](wiki:The_prince_that_was_promised) in his scrolls.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-5) Arstan later tells of the melancholy in Rhaegar's fondness of [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-10)

While resting his head on a [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) stump, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) has a fever dream of five of his old [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) brothers under King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen). Beside them, crowned in mist and grief with his long hair streaming behind him, rides Rhaegar, who burns with a cold light of changing colors. Rhaegar's shade accuses Jaime of not protecting his family during the [sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-1)

### A Feast for Crows

While standing vigil over the funeral bier of his father, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), Jaime recalls the day he said farewell to Rhaegar, in the yard of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). Rhaegar had wanted to call a council after [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) was suppressed, but the prince was instead killed at the [Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-2) Jaime also recollects that when Lord [Owen Merryweather](wiki:Owen_Merryweather) failed to contain the rebellion Rhaegar could not be found, causing King Aerys to turn to Rhaegar's friend, Lord [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), whom he raised to [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0)

Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) remembers wanting to marry Rhaegar after meeting him at the [tournament in honor of Viserys's birth](wiki:Tournament_in_honor_of_Viserys's_birth), but Aerys refused Tywin's suggestion of betrothal. Cersei, Robert's widow, never forgave Robert for killing Rhaegar at the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-9)

The [Elder Brother](wiki:Elder_Brother_(Quiet_Isle)) of the [Quiet Isle](wiki:Quiet_Isle) informs [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) six rubies from the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident) have washed up nearby.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Prior to Daenerys marrying [Hizdahr zo Loraq](wiki:Hizdahr_zo_Loraq), Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) tells her that Rhaegar was very fond of [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell); Dany thinks the word *fond* speaks volumes.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) During her wedding procession she looks about for her paramour, [Daario Naharis](wiki:Daario_Naharis), hoping secretly that he will come and carry her off at swordpoint, as Rhaegar carried off his northern girl, [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0)

Barristan later regrets having rescued King [Aerys II](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) from [Duskendale](wiki:Defiance_of_Duskendale), as he thinks Rhaeger would have been a better king than [Jaehaerys II](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen), Aerys II, or [Robert I](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) Barristan also regrets not having defeated Rhaegar in the lists at the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal), as the victorious prince had proclaimed [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) the [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty).(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-2)

Traveling south on the [Rhoyne](wiki:Rhoyne), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) discovers the [sellsword](wiki:Sellsword) Griff is actually [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington), the exiled friend of Rhaegar and former [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to Aerys II. The youth called Young Griff is said to actually be Rhaegar's son, [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)), widely believed to have been murdered by Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing). The exiles claim that [Varys](wiki:Varys) had switched the infant Aegon with a [peasant baby](wiki:Pisswater_prince) and taken Aegon to safety with [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis), and that Gregor killed the peasant child instead.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0)(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1)

Jon discusses the issue of trust with Aegon, and then recalls that Rhaegar had been aware of Aerys's paranoia.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) After [landing in the stormlands](wiki:Landing_of_the_Golden_Company) with the [Golden Company](wiki:Golden_Company) to claim the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) for Aegon, Jon vows he will not fail Rhaegar's son as he failed his father. Jon thinks that Rhaegar's [eyes were a darker purple](wiki:Purple_eyes) than those of the boy.(wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)
