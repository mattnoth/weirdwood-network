## Origins

Lomas traveled the [known world](wiki:Known_world) and wrote two famous books, *[Wonders](wiki:Wonders)* and *[Wonders Made by Man](wiki:Wonders_Made_by_Man)*, in which he cataloged sixteen sights he considered wonders.(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

One of the wonders Lomas recorded are the [Valyrian roads](wiki:Valyrian_roads).(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) He also saw and described the [triple walls of Qarth](wiki:Triple_walls_of_Qarth).(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Lomas visited the colossal ruins of the [Great Pyramid of Ghis](wiki:Great_Pyramid_of_Ghis).(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)

Lomas interviewed descendants of the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar) living in the ruins of [Chroyane](wiki:Chroyane).(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_long_night.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_ten_thousand_ships.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) He visited [Norvos](wiki:Norvos) and named its [three bells](wiki:Three_bells) a wonder made by man. He also visited the large cavern system a hundred leagues northwest of Norvos, and listed it as one of the seven natural wonders.(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

Lomas marveled at the [Titan of Braavos](wiki:Titan_of_Braavos)(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and named the [Long Bridge](wiki:Long_Bridge) of [Volantis](wiki:Volantis) as one of the wonders made by man.(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_volantis.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) He also named the [Palace With a Thousand Rooms](wiki:Palace_With_a_Thousand_Rooms) in [Sarnath](wiki:Sarnath) among the man-made wonders.(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_grasslands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

Lomas visited the [Summer Isles](wiki:Summer_Isles), where he interviewed sages of the isles.(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_the_summer_isles.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

Lomas passed east of the massive [Bone Mountains](wiki:Bone_Mountains) but he did not do so by taking a ship in the [Shivering Sea](wiki:Shivering_Sea). Like many others, he either went through the mountain passes or by way of the [Jade Gates](wiki:Jade_Gates).(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafbeyond_the_free_cities:_east_of_ib.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) It is said that at the sight of the Bones, Lomas lost heart, believing he had reached the ends of the earth.(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

Lomas visited [Yi Ti](wiki:Yi_Ti), where he was awestruck by its wealth and rich culture.(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_yi_ti.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Lomas also apparently visited the successor cities of the [Patrimony of Hyrkoon](wiki:Patrimony_of_Hyrkoon)—[Bayasabhad](wiki:Bayasabhad), [Samyriana](wiki:Samyriana), and [Kayakayanaya](wiki:Kayakayanaya)—as he said that there are no fiercer fighters in the world than their warrior women.(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1)(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-14)

Lomas claimed that [Leng](wiki:Leng) is an island of "ten thousand [tigers](wiki:Tiger) and ten million [monkeys](wiki:Monkey)".(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_leng.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

In his *[The World of Ice & Fire](wiki:The_World_of_Ice_&_Fire_(Yandel))*, Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) is contradictory as to whether or not Lomas visited [Asshai](wiki:Asshai). In his chapter on the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands), Yandel writes that merchants in Asshai asked Lomas about the famous [gold](wiki:Gold) of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) In the book's section on Asshai and the [Shadow Lands](wiki:Shadow_Lands), however, Yandel writes that Lomas never reached the eastern city.(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_bones_and_beyond:_asshai-by-the-shadow.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

When [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), Ser [Rolly Duckfield](wiki:Rolly_Duckfield), and [Haldon Halfmaester](wiki:Haldon) speak of Lomas, Haldon reports the scribe to be long dead.(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6)

Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) recalls that the [Great Pyramid of Meereen](wiki:Great_Pyramid) was built to emulate the [Great Pyramid of Ghis](wiki:Great_Pyramid_of_Ghis).(wiki:Lomas_Longstrider.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
