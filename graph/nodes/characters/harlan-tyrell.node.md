## Identity

Harlan Tyrell was the steward of Highgarden whose forebears had served the Gardeners for centuries.

## Origins

### The Conquest

At the time of [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest) in [2 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest), Harlan Tyrell served as the [High Steward](wiki:High_Steward) to [Mern IX Gardener](wiki:Mern_IX_Gardener), [King of the Reach](wiki:King_of_the_Reach), an office his forebears had held for centuries.(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) After Mern died at the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire), King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) marched on [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), where Harlan yielded the castle without a fight, pledging his support to Aegon. For this, Aegon named him [Lord of Highgarden](wiki:Lord_of_Highgarden) and gave him dominion over the [Reach](wiki:Reach) as [Lord Paramount of the Mander](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Mander) and [Warden of the South](wiki:Warden_of_the_South),(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) despite the fact that the [Tyrells](wiki:House_Tyrell) could only claim descent from [Garth Greenhand](wiki:Garth_Greenhand) through the female line, while other houses, with older and more distinguished lineages, had closer blood ties to the now-extinct [House Gardener](wiki:House_Gardener).(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

Harlan's elevation to Lord of Highgarden caused a number of his bannermen to voice their resentment, who bitterly complained about having to serve an "upjumped steward". However, their complaints were of no avail. Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) speculates that Harlan had been chosen as the new Lord of Highgarden by Aegon I because the Tyrells had not taken up arms against the [Targaryens](wiki:House_Targaryen), whereas the [Oakhearts](wiki:House_Oakheart), the [Florents](wiki:House_Florent), the [Rowans](wiki:House_Rowan), the [Peakes](wiki:House_Peake), and the [Redwynes](wiki:House_Redwyne) had marched beside King Mern.(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

### First Dornish War

Harlan served the Targaryens loyally and proved able in his lordship of the Reach. During the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), he marched south in [4 AC](wiki:4_AC) beside King Aegon at the head of a massive army thirty thousand strong, led by two thousand mounted [knights](wiki:Knight) and three hundred [lords](wiki:Lord) and [bannermen](wiki:Bannermen). Lord Tyrell was heard to say that they had the strength to smash any Dornish army that tried to oppose them, even without Aegon and his [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Balerion](wiki:Balerion).(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) The king and Lord Tyrell warred against the [Dornish](wiki:Dornishmen) mountain lords in the [Prince's Pass](wiki:Prince's_Pass),(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) however the Dornishmen never gave proper battle, withdrawing upon sight of the invading host. Upon emerging from the Prince's Pass, Aegon divided his forces and marched against [Skyreach](wiki:Skyreach), while Harlan was sent south against Lord [Uthor Uller](wiki:Uthor_Uller) of the [Hellholt](wiki:Hellholt).(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

The Dornish sun proved unrelenting to the invaders as Lord Tyrell marched towards the Hellholt, with his men struggling to find water as every waterhole and oasis in the army's path had been poisoned. Horses began to die daily, followed by their riders; Harlan lost a quarter of his men and nearly all his horses to the Dornish sands. By the time the Tyrell army had arrived at the Hellholt, he founded it abandoned,(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) the Ullers having fled.(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

After the Targaryens had claimed [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), Harlan was placed in charge of putting down revolts, while Lord [Jon Rosby](wiki:Jon_Rosby) was given charge of Sunspear. The king had thought the Hellholt was well situated to deal with any revolts. However, the Tyrell men sickened from the sulphurous waters of the [Brimstone](wiki:Brimstone), while Lord [Vaith](wiki:House_Vaith) and Lord [Qorgyle](wiki:House_Qorgyle) had their spearmen cut down Tyrell foraging parties and patrols. In [5 AC](wiki:5_AC), upon learning of [the fall](wiki:Defenestration_of_Sunspear) of Sunspear and Lord Rosby's death at the hands of Princess [Meria Martell](wiki:Meria_Martell), Harlan gathered his remaining strength and announced his intentions to capture [Vaith](wiki:Vaith) and retake Sunspear. However, he vanished with his entire army in the sands of Dorne east of the Hellholt, never to be found again.(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

Harlan was succeeded in his titles by his young son [Theo](wiki:Theo_Tyrell), who was much reluctant to continue the war against Dorne following the fate that befell his father.(wiki:Harlan_Tyrell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reach:_house_tyrell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)
