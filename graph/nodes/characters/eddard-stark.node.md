## Identity

Lord Eddard Stark, called Ned, is the head of House Stark, Lord of Winterfell, and Warden of the North (track_b: Titles). He serves as Hand of the King to Robert I Baratheon and briefly claims the titles Protector of the Realm and Lord Regent (track_b: Titles). He is a major POV character in A Game of Thrones (wiki:Eddard_Stark).

## Origins

Born in 263 AC at Winterfell (track_b: Born), the second son of Lord Rickard Stark and Lady Lyarra Stark (track_b: Father, Mother). Fostered at the Eyrie by Lord Jon Arryn from the age of eight, where he befriended his fellow ward Robert Baratheon (wiki:Eddard_Stark). Died in 299 AC at the Great Sept of Baelor, King's Landing, aged 35–36 (track_b: Died).

## Allegiances

Eddard is `SWORN_TO` House Stark (track_b: Allegiance) and belongs to `CULTURE_OF` Northmen (track_b: Culture). He keeps faith with the old gods (wiki:Eddard_Stark).

## Appearances & Description

Eddard has a long face and long brown hair, with a closely-trimmed beard beginning to grey (wiki:Eddard_Stark). His dark grey eyes reflect his moods, turning soft as fog or hard as stone (wiki:Eddard_Stark). He is shorter and less handsome than his elder brother Brandon, according to Catelyn (wiki:Eddard_Stark). He wears furs and leathers when riding, and a surcoat of fine white velvet with a direwolf badge over the heart (wiki:Eddard_Stark). He wields Ice, a Valyrian steel greatsword, for ceremonial executions (wiki:Eddard_Stark).

## Narrative Arc

Eddard's elder brother Brandon and father Rickard are murdered by the Mad King Aerys II after Lyanna Stark's abduction by Prince Rhaegar Targaryen (wiki:Eddard_Stark). Now Lord of Winterfell, Eddard raises his banners and joins Robert's Rebellion, marrying Catelyn Tully — Brandon's former betrothed — to seal the alliance with House Tully (wiki:Eddard_Stark). After the rebel victory at the Trident, Eddard marches to King's Landing and finds it sacked by Tywin Lannister and Jaime sitting on the Iron Throne over Aerys's corpse — an image that permanently shapes his contempt for the Lannisters (wiki:Eddard_Stark). Eddard lifts the siege at Storm's End, then rides to the tower of joy in Dorne with six companions. Only he and Howland Reed survive the fight with three Kingsguard. Lyanna dies after extracting a promise from Eddard (wiki:Eddard_Stark).

Eddard returns to Winterfell with a bastard son, Jon Snow, and rules the North for fifteen quiet years (wiki:Eddard_Stark). In AGOT, after Jon Arryn's death, Robert asks Eddard to serve as Hand and betroth Sansa to Joffrey. At King's Landing, Eddard investigates Jon Arryn's death and discovers Cersei's children are Jaime's, not Robert's (agot-eddard-10, wiki:Eddard_Stark). After Robert's death, Eddard attempts to enforce Robert's will naming him Protector of the Realm, but Littlefinger betrays him and the gold cloaks turn on his men (agot-eddard-14). Eddard is imprisoned, and after a coerced confession on the steps of the Great Sept of Baelor, Joffrey orders his execution by Ser Ilyn Payne with Eddard's own sword, Ice (wiki:Eddard_Stark).
