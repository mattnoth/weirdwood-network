## Origins

### Youth

Eddard was born at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rawoiafeddard_stark.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) as the second son of Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark) and Lady [Lyarra Stark](wiki:Lyarra_Stark). He had one older brother, [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark), and two younger siblings, [Lyanna](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) and [Benjen](wiki:Benjen_Stark).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafappendix:_stark_lineage.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)

Eddard was [fostered](wiki:Fostering) by Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) from the age of eight.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) There, Eddard befriended his fellow ward, [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), while Lord Jon became like a second father to him.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) When he reached the age of sixteen, Eddard was a man grown, and divided his time between Winterfell and the Eyrie.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-28) During one of his visits to Winterfell, Eddard carried a wedding proposal from Robert to his father, Lord Rickard, who agreed to betroth his daughter Lyanna to Eddard's friend.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rawoiafeddard_stark.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) When Lyanna later expressed her belief that Robert would never keep to one bed, having heard about [Mya Stone](wiki:Mya_Stone), Robert's bastard daughter in the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn), Eddard recalled having seen the child, and was unable to deny her existence.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2) Lord [Rodrik Ryswell](wiki:Rodrik_Ryswell) hoped to wed his daughter [Barbrey](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin) to Brandon or Eddard.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

### Tourney at Harrenhal

In [281 AC](wiki:281_AC), at the age of eighteen, Eddard came down from the Eyrie to attend the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) Also present were Eddard's foster brother Robert, his foster father Jon, and his three siblings, Brandon, Lyanna, and [Benjen](wiki:Benjen_Stark).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) It was during this tourney that Eddard met [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed), one of his father's bannermen. Howland and Eddard remained friends for the rest of their lives. During the opening feast, Eddard saw [Ashara Dayne](wiki:Ashara_Dayne), a lady-in-waiting to Princess [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell). He danced with her later that night, but only after his older brother Brandon had spoken to her on his behalf, since he himself was too shy.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Eddard was present during the final jousts, where Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) passed over his own wife, Elia, to crown Lyanna the [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty). Eddard would remember this moment as "the moment when all smiles died" years later.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-2)

The following year, Eddard's sister Lyanna was abducted by Prince Rhaegar.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) Brandon rode to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) in anger, but he was arrested when he threatened the crown prince. Lord Rickard Stark was summoned to King's Landing to answer for the crimes of his son, and rode to the capital with two hundred men. When he demanded a [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat), both he and Brandon were executed at the command of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Racok55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) Aerys's next step was to demand that Jon Arryn send him the heads of his former wards, Eddard and Robert. Jon refused and instead called his banners.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)

### Robert's Rebellion

With his father and brother dead, Eddard was now the [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell). He wanted to raise his banners to join [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), but getting from the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) to the [north](wiki:North) was complicated by the loyalty to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) of [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown), the Vale's chief port.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) Eddard crossed the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) to the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers) and hired a fisherman to bring him to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) with a boat. They were caught by a storm on the [Bite](wiki:Bite) and the fisherman drowned. However, his daughter brought Eddard to the [Three Sisters](wiki:Three_Sisters). When he arrived at [Sisterton](wiki:Sisterton), [House Borrell](wiki:House_Borrell)'s maester urged [Lord Borrell](wiki:Lord_Borrell_(father_of_Godric)) to deliver Eddard's head to King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), but Lord Borrell, knowing that Jon Arryn and Robert Baratheon had [taken Gulltown](wiki:Taking_of_Gulltown) in the meantime, was unsure who would prevail in the conflict. Thus he let Ned leave for White Harbor, telling him to remain silent about his stay in Sisterton in case the rebellion failed.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1)

Eddard called his banners, and marched south to join Robert, arriving with Lord [Hoster Tully](wiki:Hoster_Tully) in time to turn the tide at the [Battle of the Bells](wiki:Battle_of_the_Bells).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Afterward, he went to [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) to marry Hoster's elder daughter, [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), who had been betrothed to his brother [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark) before his death.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2) Jon Arryn married Hoster's younger daughter, [Lysa](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), in the same ceremony. [The double wedding](wiki:Weddings_of_Catelyn_and_Lysa_Tully) bound [House Tully](wiki:House_Tully) to the rebels' cause.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

The rebels won their decisive victory in the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident), in which Robert killed Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) in single combat at the [ruby ford](wiki:Ruby_ford). Because Robert had been wounded by Rhaegar, it fell on Eddard to pursue the remnants of Aerys's armies to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

When Eddard arrived at the capital, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s force had already sacked the city. Eddard had developed contempt for [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) during the rebellion, as Tywin had remained neutral in an apparent attempt to join the winning side in the end. Eddard's contempt increased when he learned that Tywin had conquered the city by treachery and that his men had brutally killed Rhaegar's wife, [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), and children, [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)) and [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)). In addition, Lannister flags were flying over the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) when Eddard arrived and, as he rode into the throne room to claim the crown for Robert, he found King Aerys's body at the steps leading to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), on which the king's killer, Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), was sitting.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)

Eddard felt the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing) dishonored Robert's cause. He was disappointed that Robert, upon arriving in the city, did not share his moral outrage and in particular defended the murder of Rhaegar's children. He also ignored Eddard's counsel that Jaime should be made to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) for breaking his oath as a knight of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) by not protecting the king. Their disagreements created a rift between Eddard and Robert that not even Jon Arryn was able to breach.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)

Eddard left Robert in King's Landing and went to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), where he lifted the [siege of the castle](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End). Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) and his armies yielded to Eddard without a fight.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) Afterwards, Eddard traveled to the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) to liberate his sister [Lyanna](wiki:Lyanna_Stark), who apparently was held at the [tower of joy](wiki:Tower_of_joy). When Eddard arrived along with six companions, he found the tower guarded by three members of the Kingsguard, [Arthur Dayne](wiki:Arthur_Dayne), [Oswell Whent](wiki:Oswell_Whent), and Lord Commander [Gerold Hightower](wiki:Gerold_Hightower). The resulting [skirmish](wiki:Combat_at_the_tower_of_joy) saw the deaths of all but Eddard and one companion, [Howland Reed](wiki:Howland_Reed).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) Eddard is said to have killed Arthur, the [Sword of the Morning](wiki:Sword_of_the_Morning), in single combat.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_the_andals_arrive.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

Unfortunately, when Eddard found Lyanna, she was already dying.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) Before her death, she made Eddard promise her something;(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-1) what he promised her has not yet been revealed. The experience haunts Eddard and he recalls her *"promise me"* on multiple occasions.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-2)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3) However, the shared grief over Lyanna led to his reconciliation with his friend, now King Robert I.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-5)

When Eddard returned home to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), he brought with him his bastard son, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow). This strained the relationship with his wife [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), who had given birth to their son [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark) at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) during the war. Eddard refused to speak to her about Jon's mother. However, stories concerning her identity circulated nonetheless. When Catelyn heard rumors that the boy's mother was the [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) noblewoman [Ashara Dayne](wiki:Ashara_Dayne), she confronted Eddard about it, but he told her in a brusque manner that the child was his blood and that was all she needed to know. He subsequently silenced the rumors about Ashara.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3) [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) recalls hearing whispers that Jon's mother was common born.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0) Lord [Edric Dayne](wiki:Edric_Dayne) believes that, rather than his aunt Ashara, Jon Snow's mother was a woman called [Wylla](wiki:Wylla), who worked for [House Dayne](wiki:House_Dayne) and at one point served as Edric's wet-nurse.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0) When asked about the mother of his bastard son by King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Eddard also mentions the name Wylla.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-6) However, Lord [Godric Borrell](wiki:Godric_Borrell) claims that the daughter of the fisherman who sneaked Eddard away from the Fingers gave birth to Jon Snow.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-2)

Eddard buried the bones of his deceased kin in the [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell). Breaking the tradition of only Stark kings and lords having statues, Ned had them carved for Brandon and Lyanna next to that of Lord Rickard.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) [Barbrey Dustin](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin), now Lady of [Barrowton](wiki:Barrowton), resented that Eddard returned the horse but not the bones of her husband, Lord [Willam](wiki:Willam_Dustin), who had died fighting alongside Ned in Dorne.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1) Ned's younger brother, [Benjen](wiki:Benjen_Stark), joined the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rawoiafbenjen_stark.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

### Lord of Winterfell

As a second son, Eddard had never expected to become the [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell), and as such did not always feel equal to the task, determined it had all been meant for his elder brother Brandon.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-4) Lord Stark has taken little interest in affairs south of the [Neck](wiki:Neck).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-7) Eddard had a [small sept](wiki:Lady_Catelyn's_sept) built at Winterfell so that his new wife [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) could pray to [the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)

In [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), Eddard traveled south to help Robert suppress [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), and he participated in the decisive [battle at Pyke](wiki:Battle_at_Pyke). Greyjoy's Rebellion was the most recent time that Eddard saw Robert.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-2) After Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy)'s surrender, Eddard took Balon's only surviving son, [Theon](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy), to Winterfell as a ward and hostage.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-3) While Eddard tried to be like a father to Theon from time to time,(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) the boy still found Lord Stark to be cold.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1)

Eddard last saw Prince [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) when the boy was breastfeeding from his mother, Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

When Lord [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) tried to sell poachers he had caught on his lands into [slavery](wiki:Slavery) in [293 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Eddard traveled to [Bear Island](wiki:Bear_Island) to bring him to justice for his crime. Upon arrival, Ned found that Jorah and [Lynesse Hightower](wiki:Lynesse_Hightower) had fled into exile, however.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-8)

During his marriage to Catelyn, Eddard had five children with her: [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark), [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark), [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark), [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark), and [Rickon](wiki:Rickon_Stark).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) Eddard has taught Robb and [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), his bastard son, about command in battle.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)

Arya has visited [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor) twice with her father,(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) and Theon Greyjoy accompanied Lord Stark during a visit to Ser [Helman Tallhart](wiki:Helman_Tallhart) at [Torrhen's Square](wiki:Torrhen's_Square)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0) and several visits to the [Glovers](wiki:House_Glover) at [Deepwood Motte](wiki:Deepwood_Motte).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0) The [hill clans](wiki:Northern_mountain_clans) have always treated Lord Stark generously when he visited the [northern mountains](wiki:Northern_mountains),(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0) calling him "the Ned".(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0) Eddard hosted Lord [Rickard Karstark](wiki:Rickard_Karstark) and his daughter, [Alys](wiki:Alys_Karstark), when the girl was six.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) was part of [Lord Commander Qorgyle](wiki:Lord_Commander_Qorgyle)'s expedition to Winterfell to see Lord Stark.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) Eddard is wary of [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), Lord of the [Dreadfort](wiki:Dreadfort).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)

## Appearances & Description

> *See also: [Images of Eddard Stark](wiki:Category:Images_of_Eddard_Stark)*

Eddard has a long face(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and long brown hair.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) His closely-trimmed beard is beginning to grey, making him look older than his thirty-five years.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) His dark grey eyes(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) reflect his moods, turning soft as fog or hard as stone.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Eddard is shorter and less handsome than his older brother [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark) had been, according to [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark); however, she also states that Ned has a "good sweet heart beneath his solemn face".(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) He keeps faith with the [old gods](wiki:Old_gods).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) He is fiercely protective of his wife and children, whom he loves deeply.

Eddard is known for his unwavering sense of honor(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) and [justice](wiki:Laws_and_justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms) and his family finds him kind, although some consider his reserved personality a sign of coldness and disdain.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) He has a reputation for always telling the truth.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) Among his enemies, Eddard is seen as having cold, judgmental eyes(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) thought to reflect his frozen heart.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

While riding his [great warhorse](wiki:Horse) in the [north](wiki:North), Ned wears furs and leathers.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-3) Ned also wears silk(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) and linen(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) tunics and a belt of heavy silver links.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) His surcoat is fine white velvet with a [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf) badge over the heart.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Racok39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) Ned's cloaks are grey with white,(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) as well as black wool.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

Lord Stark wields sword(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) and dagger,(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) although he uses [Ice](wiki:Ice), a [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) [greatsword](wiki:Greatsword), for ceremonial purposes.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

Lord Eddard executes [Gared](wiki:Gared), a deserter of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) who claims to have been attacked by [Others](wiki:Others), at a [holdfast in the hills](wiki:Holdfast_in_the_hills). On the return to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), the party discovers a [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf) dead near a bridge, her throat pierced by a [stag](wiki:Stag) she had slain. Swayed by his bastard son, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), Eddard decides not to slay her newborn pups, and instead allow his children to raise them.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-5)

That same day, a [raven](wiki:Raven) arrives bearing word of the death of Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), who fostered Eddard as well as [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), and who had long served Robert as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-9) Soon Robert arrives with half the court, and the king insists that Eddard bring him to the [crypts](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell) so he can pay his respects to Eddard's late sister, [Lyanna](wiki:Lyanna_Stark).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-4) The Starks hold a [feast in honor of King Robert's visit to Winterfell](wiki:Feast_in_honor_of_King_Robert's_visit_to_Winterfell).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) Eddard grudgingly accepts Robert's offer to replace Lord Arryn as Hand, as well as a betrothal of Prince [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) to his daughter [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-5)

Another message arrives, this one from [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn), Jon's widow and the sister of Eddard's wife [Catelyn](wiki:Catelyn_Stark), sent to Winterfell in secret. Lysa warns the Starks that the [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) were involved in her husband's death.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-6) When Eddard's son Bran falls and slips into a coma, Eddard and the royal court remain at Winterfell for almost another fortnight,(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) though they eventually decide to leave.

During the journey south, Eddard and Robert are at odds whether or not to be concerned about the wedding of the exiled Princess [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) to Khal [Drogo](wiki:Drogo) of the [Dothraki](wiki:Dothraki).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-9) Eddard's youngest daughter [Arya](wiki:Arya_Stark) is involved in an incident in which crown prince [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) is injured by Arya's direwolf, [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-1) When Nymeria cannot be found, Queen [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) demands the death of Sansa's direwolf, [Lady](wiki:Lady_(direwolf)), and Eddard executes the wolf himself at [Darry](wiki:Darry). In order to prevent Queen Cersei from acquiring the pelt, he has her body sent north to be buried at Winterfell.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_55-0)

Upon arriving at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Eddard is summoned to a [small council](wiki:Small_council) meeting. Afterwards, Lord [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish) takes Eddard to one of his brothels, where he has been hiding Eddard's wife, Catelyn. She reveals how a [catspaw](wiki:Catspaw) tried to kill their son [Bran](wiki:Bran_Stark) with a [Valyrian steel dagger](wiki:Littlefinger's_blade). Petyr, known as Littlefinger, claims that the dagger used to be his, but was won in a bet by [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister). Littlefinger promises to aid Eddard.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-1) After receiving Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin)'s message that Bran is awake, Lord Stark and his daughters stay overnight in the godswood of the Red Keep.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)

Eddard is shocked to learn that the crown is heavily indebted due to Robert's extravagance, and Jon Arryn and the small council had been unable to restrain him. Robert decrees a great tournament is to be held in honor of Eddard's appointment as Hand of the King, despite Eddard's protests of the cost.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-2) The [Hand's tourney](wiki:Hand's_tourney), featuring a joust, melee, and archery contest, attracts knights and freeriders from all over the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-3)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) Ned and Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy), [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard), convince Robert not to participate in the melee.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)

Eddard discovers that his daughter Arya is in the possession of a sword, [Needle](wiki:Needle), but decides to have her trained in how to use it, instead of taking her sword away. He hires [Syrio Forel](wiki:Syrio_Forel), a former [First Sword of Braavos](wiki:First_Sword_of_Braavos).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-1) While Ned later wonders whether Syrio is a proper teacher for Arya, he allows the lessons to continue,(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) and is eventually willing to ask Syrio to continue his work at Winterfell.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-0)

Eddard investigates Jon Arryn's activities before his death, and he discovers that Jon spent a great deal of time with Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) and had been visiting several of Robert's bastard children in the city.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-2)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3) Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) provides the Hand with *[The Lineages and Histories of the Great Houses of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:The_Lineages_and_Histories_of_the_Great_Houses_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms)*, which Jon had been reading.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-3)

When news reaches King's Landing that Daenerys is pregnant, Robert holds a council meeting demanding she be put to death. Eddard and Barristan are the only ones to speak out against the move, but Robert, driven by his hatred of the Targaryens, insists on the assassination. Eddard, unwilling to cooperate, resigns his office in protest.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)

Before his planned departure from King's Landing to return to Winterfell, Eddard visits another of Robert's bastard children, a girl named [Barra](wiki:Barra), found by Lord Baelish. Returning from this meeting he is ambushed by Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), who wants revenge for the seizure of his brother Tyrion by Catelyn at the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn),(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-4) a fact Eddard had learned the day before from [Yoren](wiki:Yoren), a [wandering crow](wiki:Wandering_crow).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) Eight men(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-1) die from the ensuing melee, including [Jory Cassel](wiki:Jory_Cassel), [Heward](wiki:Heward), [Wyl](wiki:Wyl_(guard)), and [Tregar](wiki:Tregar), and Ned's leg is broken when his horse falls upon him.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-5) Pycelle treats Ned, who has a fever dream of the [tower of joy](wiki:Combat_at_the_tower_of_joy). While recovering, Robert visits his friend, pardoning him and returning him to office as Hand of the King.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-2)

Eddard sits on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) and hears petitions while Robert is out on a hunt. Ser [Raymun Darry](wiki:Raymun_Darry), Ser [Marq Piper](wiki:Marq_Piper), and Ser [Karyl Vance](wiki:Karyl_Vance) bring the news that several villages near the border of the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) have been ravaged by Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane). Eddard sends Lord [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion), [Thoros of Myr](wiki:Thoros_of_Myr), and a number of knights, and members of his [own household guard](wiki:House_Stark_guards) to bring the Mountain to justice for his crimes against the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)

While investigating Jon Arryn's interest in Robert's bastard children, Eddard discovers to his horror that Robert's three legitimate children are the product of incest between Queen Cersei and her brother, Jaime.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_59-1) Eddard decides to confront Cersei, giving her a chance to flee with her children while she still can.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) However, Cersei has orchestrated Robert's assassination. As Robert lays on his deathbed, Eddard rebuffs both the suggestion of Robert's youngest brother, Lord [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), that they seize control of the royal children, and Petyr Baelish's urgings to rule as [regent](wiki:Regent) while Cersei and Jaime's thirteen-year-old son, Prince Joffrey, accedes to the Iron Throne, instead planning to deliver it to the elder of Robert's younger brothers, Lord [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-3) Littlefinger promises Eddard the support of the [City Watch of King's Landing](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing). [Vayon Poole](wiki:Vayon_Poole) arranges for Sansa and Arya to return north on the *[Wind Witch](wiki:Wind_Witch)*,(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-1) and Ned orders [Tomard](wiki:Tomard) to deliver a letter to Stannis at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-4)

Not wanting to have her betrothal to Joffrey broken, Sansa informs Queen Cersei of her father's plan to leave King's Landing.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-0) When Ned confronts Cersei in the throne room, he is betrayed by Littlefinger. [Red cloaks](wiki:House_Lannister_guards) and [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt)'s gold cloaks kill Eddard's remaining men, and Littlefinger takes the Valyrian dagger from Lord Stark.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_63-0) Syrio allows Arya to escape Ser [Meryn Trant](wiki:Meryn_Trant),(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_64-0) but Sansa is captured by the Lannisters.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_65-0)

In response to [his father's imprisonment for treason](wiki:Arrest_of_Eddard_Stark) by Cersei, [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) calls the northern banners to Winterfell and marches south.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0) For his part in Ned's downfall, Janos Slynt is named [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal) by King Joffrey.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0)

Varys visits Eddard Stark in the dungeons, informing him that if he confesses to treason his life will be spared and he will be given the opportunity to join the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch). Eddard initially refuses but agrees to swallow his honor to save the life of his daughter Sansa, who is in Lannister custody.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-4) He is taken to the steps of the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) where, unbeknownst to him, Yoren, his daughter Arya, and the disguised Barristan are amongst the crowd.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0) Eddard makes a false public confession but the plan goes awry when King Joffrey declares that Eddard must be executed, to the evident shock of Cersei, Varys, and the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(fat_one)). Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne) beheads Eddard with his own greatsword, [Ice](wiki:Ice).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2) Joffrey has Eddard's head placed on a spike and forces Sansa to view it.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0)

Bran and [Rickon Stark](wiki:Rickon_Stark) dream of their father's death before a raven arrives at Winterfell.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-2) After the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), Robb is proclaimed [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) by northmen and rivermen at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0)

### A Clash of Kings

A stonemason creates a granite statue in Ned's image within the [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Racok4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0)

Eddard's execution at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) has caused a serious deterioration in relations between the crown and the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), as the High Septon claims that Stark's beheading there profaned the sept with blood.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Racok3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-1)

### A Storm of Swords

[Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark) would like to name a son Eddard if she were to wed [Willas Tyrell](wiki:Willas_Tyrell), but Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) instead arranges for her [to wed](wiki:Wedding_of_Tyrion_Lannister_and_Sansa_Stark) his son, [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_76-0) [Jeyne Westerling](wiki:Jeyne_Westerling), Robb's queen, would like to name her children Eddard and Brandon if they have twins.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0) Robb is slain in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), however.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_62-1)

Tywin has [Tobho Mott](wiki:Tobho_Mott) reforge Ned's [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) greatsword, [Ice](wiki:Ice),(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-0) into two smaller longswords which become known as [Widow's Wail](wiki:Widow's_Wail)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-0) and [Oathkeeper](wiki:Oathkeeper).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_80-0)

After the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall), [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) offers to name [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) as [Lord of Winterfell](wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell), so that a son of Eddard can help Stannis win the [north](wiki:North).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-0) Jon refuses the offer, however, and is instead chosen [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Recalling that [Janos Slynt](wiki:Janos_Slynt) had helped kill his father, Jon beheads Janos at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) for insubordination.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_83-0)

In [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), [Wylla Manderly](wiki:Wylla_Manderly) recalls Eddard's death when declaring her support for [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) instead of [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_84-0)

[Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark), while training to become a [greenseer](wiki:Greenseer), sees a vision of his father in the past through the eyes of the [heart tree](wiki:Heart_tree) in the [godswood of Winterfell](wiki:Godswood_of_Winterfell). Through the [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood), Bran hears his father's prayers that his wife will forgive him and that [Jon](wiki:Jon_Snow) and [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark) will grow together as brothers.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_85-0)

[Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) leads Lady [Barbrey Dustin](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin) into the [crypts of Winterfell](wiki:Crypts_of_Winterfell), where they see Eddard's statue. Barbrey blames Eddard for the death of her husband, Lord [Willam Dustin](wiki:Willam_Dustin). She tells Theon that if she ever discovers the location of Eddard's bones, she will never allow them to be buried in the crypts along with his ancestors, in revenge for Eddard's failure to return her husband's bones to her.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-2) Many other northern houses remember Eddard fondly, however, and are willing to march to war against [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) to rescue the Bolton-controlled "[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark)" (actually [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)), the daughter of "The Ned".(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-1)(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

During the [walk of atonement](wiki:Walk_of_atonement) by [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) through [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), the face of one of the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) reminds her of Eddard.(wiki:Eddard_Stark.cite_ref-Radwd65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_86-0)
