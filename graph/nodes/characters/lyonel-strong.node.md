## Appearances & Description

Lyonel was a large, burly man, with balding hair. He was a formidable man.(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
Lyonel was a man of great natural gifts.(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf731_4-0) As he was slow of speech and a taciturn man, he was often mistaken for an unintelligent brute. However, having studied at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), he was extremely literate and well learned. As a result of his time at the Citadel, Lyonel had an exhaustive knowledge of the [laws](wiki:Laws_and_justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms) of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)
Lyonel was a renowned warrior,(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rtrotd71.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) with a reputation as a formidable battler.(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

## Origins

As a youth, Lyonel studied at the [Citadel](wiki:Citadel), where he earned six links on his chain before deciding that the [maester](wiki:Maester)'s life was not for him.(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-5) Lyonel became the [Lord of Harrenhal](wiki:Lord_of_Harrenhal) during the later reign of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf731_4-1)
In late [105 AC](wiki:105_AC), Lord Lyonel was invited to court at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to serve as [master of laws](wiki:Master_of_laws) for King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), on account of his exhaustive knowledge of laws within the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). Lyonel brought his four children to court with him; his [maiden](wiki:Maiden) daughters became [handmaids](wiki:Handmaid) to Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), his elder son Ser [Harwin](wiki:Harwin_Strong) was made a captain in the [City Watch](wiki:City_Watch_of_King's_Landing), and his second son [Larys](wiki:Larys_Strong) joined the king's [confessors](wiki:Confessor).(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-6) Lyonel served ably in his office.(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf45.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
In [109 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), after Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower) overreached himself by pushing Viserys to name Otto's grandson Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) as his heir over Princess Rhaenyra, Viserys stripped him of his office as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and appointed Lyonel in his place, favoring Lyonel's stoic nature.(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-7)(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf45.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) In [111 AC](wiki:111_AC), when the allegation that Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) had taken Princess Rhaenyra's [maidenhead](wiki:Maiden) came before the [small council](wiki:Small_council), Lord Lyonel's counsel to Viserys was that Daemon should be executed for [treason](wiki:Treason). [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), however, reminded the council about the taint of [kinslaying](wiki:Kinslaying), and Daemon was exiled instead. As the council discussed a consort for Rhaenyra, Viserys confided to Lyonel that Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) was pushing for a marriage between their son Aegon and Rhaenyra to ensure her son would succeed to the throne. Ultimately, the council selected Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon) as Rhaenyra's husband. After the wedding, the couple moved to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) accompanied by Lyonel's daughters, as well as Harwin, Rhaenyra's [sworn shield](wiki:Sworn_shield).(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-8)
When the exiled Prince Daemon had twin daughters with his wife Lady [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon) in [116 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Daemon wrote to Viserys asking leave to present his daughters to court. While Lyonel and the council argued heatedly against it, Viserys consented and reconciled with his brother. Meanwhile, as Rhaenyra's sworn shield and constant companion, Harwin was rumored to be the father of the princess' three sons, for the boys lacked the [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) traits that Rhaenyra and Laenor shared.(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-9)
In [120 AC](wiki:120_AC), over mounting rumors of his grandsons' parentage, Viserys removed Ser Harwin as Princess Rhaenyra's sworn shield. Lyonel left the court to accompany Harwin on his return to [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal). Shortly after their arrival, a fire broke out in the tower they were sleeping in, and killed both Lyonel and Harwin, along with three retainers and a dozen servants. The fire was considered an accident, and blamed on the [curse of Harrenhal](wiki:Curse_of_Harrenhal). However, some records of the time suggested it was a murder, and listed various suspects. The court [fool](wiki:Fool) [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom)'s [Testimony](wiki:The_Testimony_of_Mushroom) claimed that Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) was the man behind the deaths, stating that the Sea Snake sought vengeance against the man who cuckolded his son. Septon Eustace's [writings](wiki:The_Reign_of_King_Viserys,_First_of_His_Name,_and_the_Dance_of_the_Dragons_That_Came_After) suggested it was Prince Daemon, seeking to eliminate a rival for Rhaenyra's affections. Others suspected Lyonel's younger son Larys, who stood to inherit Harrenhal with his father and elder brother dead, or King Viserys, who only sought to have Harwin killed, with Lyonel's death being an unfortunate accident.(wiki:Lyonel_Strong.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-10)
