## Origins

Prince Duncan was the first child and eldest son born to then-Prince [Aegon "Egg" Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen) and his wife [Betha Blackwood](wiki:Betha_Blackwood). He was presumably named for his father's dearest friend, Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), and so was nicknamed "Duncan the Small"(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3) (however, when asked about it, [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin) refused to answer).(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rssm1105.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) In [233 AC](wiki:233_AC), Duncan's grandfather King [Maekar](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) died in the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising), and through the decision of the [Great Council of 233 AC](wiki:Great_Council), Maekar's fourth son Aegon became king of Westeros. Duncan, Aegon's eldest son, became [Prince of Dragonstone](wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone) and heir to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### Jenny of Oldstones

In [237 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Prince Duncan was betrothed to a daughter of [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon) of [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), one of several advantageous betrothals arranged by his mother at that time. However, in [239 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), while traveling in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), Duncan encountered and fell in love with and married a "strange, lovely, and mysterious" [peasant](wiki:Peasant) woman known as [Jenny of Oldstones](wiki:Jenny_of_Oldstones).(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)

Despite the fact that King Aegon had married for love himself, and was a friend to the [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) and had practically grown up with them, he could not approve of the marriage of the heir to the throne to a commoner, and did all he could to have it undone. But Duncan stubbornly refused to put Jenny aside, even when the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon_V)), [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester), and [small council](wiki:Small_council) joined together to insist that he choose between the Iron Throne and his wife. Rather than give up Jenny, Duncan abdicated as Prince of Dragonstone, and gave up his claim to the throne in favor of his brother [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_II_Targaryen). After this, Duncan became known as "the Prince of Dragonflies".(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-10)

Unfortunately, even Duncan's abdication could not restore the peace. Duncan's spurned betrothed had been greatly grieved when he broke their betrothal. Her father, Lord [Lyonel Baratheon](wiki:Lyonel_Baratheon), was so enraged by the shame and dishonor brought to [House Baratheon](wiki:House_Baratheon) that he renounced his fealty to the Iron Throne and declared himself [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King).(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_stormlands:_house_baratheon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) A short, bloody rebellion followed, which only ended when Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) defeated Lord Lyonel in single combat. King Aegon also betrothed his youngest daughter [Rhaelle](wiki:Rhaelle_Targaryen), Duncan's sister, to [Lord Lyonel's heir](wiki:Ormund_Baratheon), and she was sent to Storm's End.(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4)

Eventually Jenny of Oldstones was accepted at court, where she was politely called "Lady Jenny".(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1)

### Later life

Prince Duncan defeated Ser [Barristan Selmy](wiki:Barristan_Selmy) in the tourney at [Blackhaven](wiki:Blackhaven) in [247 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), when Barristan was only a ten-year-old squire and participating in the tourney as a [mystery knight](wiki:Mystery_knight). Prince Duncan took pity on the small knight and jousted against him when no one else would, giving Barristan the nickname "*the Bold*".(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Radwd59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Years later, in [253 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Duncan was defeated by Barristan at the [winter tourney at King's Landing](wiki:Winter_tourney_at_King's_Landing).(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)

In [259 AC](wiki:259_AC), Prince Duncan died in the [tragedy at Summerhall](wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall) along with his father, King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), his namesake Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall), and several other members of the royal court.(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-13)(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_aegon_v_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Duncan_Targaryen.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
