## Identity

Aemon Targaryen (son of Viserys II) (b. 136 AC) — child of Viserys II Targaryen and Larra Rogare; sworn to House Targaryen; Prince. One of 3 Aemon Targaryens — see the Aemon Targaryen disambiguation entry.

## Origins

### Early life

Aemon was born to Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) and Lady [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare) on the eve of [Smith's Day](wiki:Smith's_Day) in [136 AC](wiki:136_AC). A [dragon's egg](wiki:Dragon's_egg) was placed in his cradle.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) All rejoiced at his birth, except his older brother [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), who was found by their mother hitting the newborn Aemon with Aemon's egg.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)

Aemon was inseparable from his sister [Naerys](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen) when they were young.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Stories speak of Aemon's doomed love for his sister.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Ragot51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Aemon and Naerys supposedly loved each other.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Ragot44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) According to the singers, both Aemon and Naerys cried the day Naerys married Aegon in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), although historians report that Naerys had only cried during the [bedding](wiki:Bedding), while Aemon had quarreled with Aegon at the [wedding](wiki:Marriage) feast.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5) Aemon grew to be so accomplished in the lists and with the sword that he was deemed worthy of carrying the [Valyrian steel](wiki:Valyrian_steel) sword [Dark Sister](wiki:Dark_Sister).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-6) Shortly after Naerys and Aegon's wedding,(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-11) Aemon joined the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), taking his vows at the age of seventeen during the reign of his uncle, King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-7)(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rawoiafaemon_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rasos12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) During his life, Aemon would serve under five kings: his uncle Aegon III, his cousins [Daeron I](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen) and [Baelor I](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen), his father [Viserys Aemon once fought Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) and claimed he never faced a finer swordsman.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

### Conquest of Dorne

Aemon took part in the [invasion of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne), led by his cousin and king, [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen). Aemon prevented an assassination attempt on Daeron by throwing himself in the path of a [poisoned](wiki:Poison) arrow meant for his king. Aemon survived the poison and was sent back to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to recover.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

Upon his recovery Aemon returned to [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) to protect his king and won great fame by defeating a Dornish champion. A rebellion in Dorne undid Daeron's conquest, however. Aemon was with Daeron when he met the [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) under a peace banner in [161 AC](wiki:161_AC), together with four other knights of the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard). Three of those Kingsguard knights died defending Daeron, one shamefully yielded, and Aemon was captured after slaying two of the betrayers.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) [Lord Wyl](wiki:Lord_Wyl_(conquest_of_Dorne)) of [Wyl](wiki:Wyl_(castle)) eventually put Aemon naked in a [crow cage](wiki:Crow_cage) placed above a pit filled with [vipers](wiki:Snake).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### Reign of Baelor

The new king, [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen), made peace with the [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne). On his return trip from [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear), Baelor tried to free his cousin Aemon. [House Wyl](wiki:House_Wyl) was told to release Aemon into Baelor's custody, but instead they gave Baelor the key to the cage and an invitation to use it. The Dragonknight, fearing for his cousin and new king's safety, begged Baelor to leave him, but Baelor refused and walked into the pit.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1)

Singers say the snakes bowed their heads and refused to bite Baelor due to protection from [the gods](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2) However, some [maesters](wiki:Maesters) believe Baelor was bitten six times before reaching Aemon's cage,(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) while [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) claim the king was bitten half a hundred times before using the key.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) The freed Aemon carried Baelor upon his back, while the Wyls wagered on how long they would last.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4)

The venom put Baelor in a coma, so Aemon carried the comatose king north. A [septon](wiki:Septon) in a village located on the [Boneway](wiki:Boneway) in the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) gave Aemon clothing and an ass to carry Baelor. The cousins arrived at [Blackhaven](wiki:Blackhaven), where the castle's maester treated Baelor. The king eventually awoke upon the journey to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End), but it was half a year more before he was fit to travel to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_baelor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-5)

### Reign of Viserys II

Aemon served during the brief reign of his father [Viserys II Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen), king from [171 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) to [172 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-8)

### Reign of Aegon IV

Aemon's elder brother, [Aegon IV](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), succeeded their father, Viserys II. During Aegon's reign, Aemon remained the [Lord Commander of the Kingsguard](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard),(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rawoiafaemon_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) a position he held until his death.

When Naerys was accused of adultery and treason by Ser [Morgil Hastwyck](wiki:Morgil_Hastwyck), Prince Aemon defended his sister's honor in [trial by combat](wiki:Trial_by_combat) and slew Morgil.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) This inspired many songs and furthered Aemon's renown, much to King Aegon's annoyance.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Rumors remained about Aemon being the actual father of Aegon's son and heir, Prince [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rawoiafaemon_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) This caused several people to look at one of Aegon's bastards, [Daemon Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), as the new heir to the throne. The truth of these rumors have never been proven or disproven. According to the writings of [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Kaeth](wiki:Kaeth) in *[Lives of Four Kings](wiki:Lives_of_Four_Kings)*, Aegon IV secretly started the rumors of Naerys's adultery and used Morgil to instigate this tale, though at the time Aegon denied this. These accusations coincidentally started when Aegon and his heir, Prince Daeron, were quarreling. Daeron opposed Aegon's plan for an unprovoked war on [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), and the prince being Aemon's son would give King Aegon a legitimate reason to set aside his heir in favor of his bastard, Daemon.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)

Queen [Naerys Targaryen](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen) was near death after the birth of [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)) and a stillborn son. There were no known rumors about the parentage of Princess Daenerys.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) Aegon's [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), [Lord Bracken](wiki:Lord_Bracken), hoped the king would marry his daughter [Barba](wiki:Barba_Bracken), the mother of Aegon's bastard [Aegor Rivers](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) if the queen died. Naerys recovered, however, and Prince Daeron and the Dragonknight forced Barba to leave with the infant Aegor for [Stone Hedge](wiki:Stone_Hedge).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-4) In contrast to Barba, Aegon's next mistress, [Melissa Blackwood](wiki:Melissa_Blackwood), was befriended by Aemon, Daeron, and Naerys.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-5)

Disguised as a [mystery knight](wiki:Mystery_knight), Prince Aemon once won a [tourney](wiki:Tourney) after his brother Aegon had forbidden him to take part, because Aegon wanted to crown his mistress of that time as the [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty). After winning as the "Knight of Tears", Aemon crowned Naerys as queen of love and beauty instead.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rasos24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

Despite Aegon's feelings of disrespect and hatred towards Aemon and Aegon's provocations, Aemon died honorably, defending his brother and king against the assassination attempt by two [brothers](wiki:Brothers_Toyne) of [House Toyne](wiki:House_Toyne) who sought revenge for the torture and execution of their brother, Ser [Terrence Toyne](wiki:Terrence_Toyne).(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rawoiafaegon_iv_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rawoiafaemon_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) Queen Naerys grieved Aemon's death and died in childbirth a year later.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-6)

## Appearances & Description

As an infant, Aemon was large, lusty and strong, and just as beautiful as his brother [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) was.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

Aemon possessed some piety, making him more alike to his younger sister [Naerys](wiki:Naerys_Targaryen) than to his older brother Aegon.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) He became a great jouster and swordsman later in life. It is said that Aemon considered [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) to have been the best swordsman he ever faced.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) He is considered by some to have been the noblest knight to have ever lived.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Aemon became known as the Dragonknight because the crest on his helm was a three-headed dragon of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) wrought in white gold.(wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_II).cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
