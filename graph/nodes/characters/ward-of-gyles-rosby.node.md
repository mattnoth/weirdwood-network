## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

The [ward](wiki:Ward) of Lord [Gyles Rosby](wiki:Gyles_Rosby) refuses Lady [Falyse Stokeworth](wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth) and her husband Ser [Balman Byrch](wiki:Balman_Byrch) when they seek shelter at [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) on their way to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). Falyse believes that the ward will try to claim Rosby when Gyles passes away, although she feels that Rosby should instead pass to herself and Balman.(wiki:Ward_of_Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

After [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) informs Cersei that Gyles has died, Cersei contrives to seize his [gold](wiki:Gold), lands, and castle. Pycelle begins to object, telling her again that Gyles had a ward, but she orders the hesitant Pycelle to tell the ward that Gyles’s dying wish was that all his lands and wealth go to Cersei's son, King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Ward_of_Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### A Dance with Dragons

The Rosby inheritance is brought up during the [small council](wiki:Small_council) meeting in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s throne room. According to Pycelle, six claims have been put forward, but the council, led by Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister), decides to settle the issue at a later date as they have more pressing matters to deal with.(wiki:Ward_of_Gyles_Rosby.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
