## Origins

Ser Galladon is remembered as a [perfect knight](wiki:True_knight) whose valor was so great the [Maiden](wiki:Maid_(the_Seven)) fell in love with him. She gave him an enchanted sword, the [Just Maid](wiki:Just_Maid), to demonstrate her love for him. No sword could check the sword's blows, and no shield could stop them.(wiki:Galladon_of_Morne.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) According to the legend, Galladon only unsheathed the sword three times, not once against a mortal man, for the fight would have been unfair.(wiki:Galladon_of_Morne.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Galladon is said to have slain a [dragon](wiki:Dragon) with the [Just Maid](wiki:Just_Maid).(wiki:Galladon_of_Morne.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

Galladon might have been a warrior from the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes) whom singers later immortalized as a [knight](wiki:Knight), as knighthood was introduced to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) by [Andals](wiki:Andals) who [arrived later](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals).(wiki:Galladon_of_Morne.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) In *[Kin of the Stag](wiki:Kin_of_the_Stag)*, Maester [Hubert](wiki:Hubert) suggests that Galladon was a historical figure from more recent times, noting that [Morne](wiki:Morne) was a royal seat of petty kings on the eastern coast of [Tarth](wiki:Tarth) until the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_King) made them submit. Hubert believes Morne's ruins were made by Andals and not the [First Men](wiki:First_Men).(wiki:Galladon_of_Morne.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)

[Galladon Tarth](wiki:Galladon_Tarth), the late son of Lord [Selwyn Tarth](wiki:Selwyn_Tarth),(wiki:Galladon_of_Morne.cite_ref-Raffc31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) was presumably named after the Perfect Knight.
