## Culture

Thralls of the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) may only be obtained by paying the "[iron price](wiki:Iron_price)" (capturing them by raiding) under the [Old Way](wiki:Old_Way). They are not [slaves](wiki:Slaves) since they may not be sold and they may not be obtained by paying the "gold price."(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Unlike the children of slaves, the children of thralls are free if they are born on the Iron Islands,(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) although some ironborn believe the children must be still dedicated to the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God).(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) The children of thralls cannot be taken from their parents until they are at least seven years old, the age when most ironborn begin an apprenticeship or join a crew.(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
Thralls are used for farming and mining, activities which the ironmen consider unsuitable for free men.(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Educated thralls are valued by the ironborn as stewards, tutors, and scribes, while craftsmen thralls are also highly regarded.(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) Some [ironborn priests](wiki:Priests_of_the_Drowned_God) are known to sacrifice thralls to the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God) by slitting their throats and giving their bodies to the sea.(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Radwd62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Some of the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) clans who dwell in the lands north of the [Wall](wiki:Wall) also practice thralldom.(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Racok43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
Thralls are forbidden in [Braavos](wiki:Braavos).(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
[Thrall's Bane](wiki:Thrall's_Bane) is an ironborn longship and part of the [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet).(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

## Origins

Thralls were commonly used by the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) who ruled [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) prior to the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals).(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) For instance, [Morgon Banefort](wiki:Morgon_Banefort), the [Hooded King](wiki:Hooded_King) of [Banefort](wiki:Banefort), used thralls in his twenty-year war against [Loreon I Lannister](wiki:Loreon_I_Lannister), [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock).(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) Some of the First Men of [the Vale](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) who did not submit to [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) after the [Battle of the Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_the_Seven_Stars) were reduced to thralls.(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
As time passed, thralldom south of the [Wall](wiki:Wall) came to be only practiced by the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) of the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands). After Lord [Lymond Hightower](wiki:Lymond_Hightower) of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) defended against [Theon III Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_III_Greyjoy), [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands), Lymond temporarily used thralldom to punish the captured ironborn through forced labor.(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
There were at least two thrall rebellions against [House Greyiron](wiki:House_Greyiron) when they were [Kings of the Iron Islands](wiki:Kings_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) King [Harmund III Hoare](wiki:Harmund_III_Hoare) was considering outlawing thralldom in the isles when the [Shrike](wiki:Shrike) began a successful revolt of the priests against his rule.(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Lord [Quellon Greyjoy](wiki:Quellon_Greyjoy) outlawed thralldom and freed the thralls of the isles, but this was reversed by his son and successor, [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy).(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
[House Humble](wiki:House_Humble) is held in low regard for descending from thralls and [salt wives](wiki:Salt_wife).(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

When he returns to [Pyke](wiki:Pyke), [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) is attended by thralls in service to [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy).(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) His father, Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), intends to conquer the [north](wiki:North) and make its people thralls and [salt wives](wiki:Salt_wives).(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Racok24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) During the [harrying of the Stony Shore](wiki:Harrying_of_the_Stony_Shore), some of the northern women are taken as thralls.(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Racok37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
[Thoren Smallwood](wiki:Thoren_Smallwood) states that [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder)'s great host of [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) contains mostly women, children, and thralls.(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Racok43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

[Qarl the Thrall](wiki:Qarl_the_Thrall) is a supporter of [Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) as [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) After the [taking of the Shields](wiki:Taking_of_the_Shields), Lord Captain [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) is disturbed that Euron intends for captives to be sold into [slavery](wiki:Slavery) instead of being taken as thralls or salt wives.(wiki:Thrall.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
