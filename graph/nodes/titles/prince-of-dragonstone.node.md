## Origins

### Targaryen Dynasty[ ]

After [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) [conquered](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest) the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms), he decided to construct a new capital city, [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), in the new town growing around his newly constructed seat, the [Aegonfort](wiki:Aegonfort), which was torn down in [35 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) in preparation for the construction of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). The seat of Dragonstone remained in possession of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen). Aegon I had two children: [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), born in [7 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and [Maegor](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), born in [12 AC](wiki:12_AC). Despite being Aegon I's eldest son and heir for many years, Aenys never held the title "Prince of Dragonstone".(wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone.cite_ref-1) Aenys was raised by Aegon I at King's Landing, and accompanied his father on royal progressions. Meanwhile Maegor was raised by his mother, Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen), at Dragonstone and in time became known as the "Prince of Dragonstone".(wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Maegor continued to hold the title during the earlier years of Aenys's own reign. It was only in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC), a year after Maegor had left the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) in exile, that Aenys established "Prince of Dragonstone" as a formal title by appointing it to his own heir, Prince [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)).(wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
Upon Aenys's own death, Maegor returned from exile and claimed the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). He had three wives already, but in [47 AC](wiki:47_AC), still lacking an heir, married three widowed women known to be fertile. One of these was his niece, Princess [Rhaena](wiki:Rhaena_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aenys_I)), who had borne twin girls to her late brother, Prince Aegon. Maegor named one of the girls, [Aerea](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen), as his heir until he had children of his own.(wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) However, she has not been confirmed to have been appointed the title "Princess of Dragonstone".
Not every Targaryen monarch held the title "Prince of Dragonstone" before coming to the throne, such as in times when there were several deaths in rapid succession so a younger son instantly succeeded to the throne (such as Aerys I and Aegon V).

### Baratheon Dynasty[ ]

After [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), the Targaryen dynasty was at an end. The title "Prince of Dragonstone" has not been used by the new royal house, [House Baratheon of King's Landing](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing). Instead, Dragonstone was granted to Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) as his personal seat. He now rules Dragonstone as the [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone), a title which had also been used by the Targaryens prior to [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).
However, two children of King Aerys II still remain. [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), who had been crowned on Dragonstone [prior to its fall](wiki:Fall_of_Dragonstone),(wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone.cite_ref-Rawoiaf_rhaella_targaryen.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) named his young sister [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), as his sole heir, the Princess of Dragonstone.(wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) After Viserys' death, Daenerys declared herself Queen. She has not yet named a new heir.

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

From her birth until the death of her brother, [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) uses the title Princess of Dragonstone, as heir of [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen), who is the claimant to the Iron Throne.(wiki:Prince_of_Dragonstone.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
