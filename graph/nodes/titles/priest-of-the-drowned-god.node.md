Priest of the Drowned God is a title from the AWOIAF wiki.

## Edges


## Culture

The priests worship the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God) and oppose his rival, the [Storm God](wiki:Storm_God). Followers of their religion are symbolically drowned by having salt water poured over their heads by priests or having their head placed in water.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) Some priests, such as [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), believe that true believers must actually be drowned and then revived with the [kiss of life](wiki:Kiss_of_life).(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Aeron's acolytes are known as [drowned men](wiki:Drowned_men).(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
Most priests wander the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) barefoot and poorly clad in seawater robes.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Theirs is an oral tradition, so most cannot read. [Lords](wiki:Lord) and [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) are obliged to give them shelter, and some priests only eat fish. Most only bathe in the sea.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Priests sometimes drink a mouthful of salt water to strength their faith.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Priests perform the ceremony when a man weds a [rock wife](wiki:Rock_wife) or a [salt wife](wiki:Salt_wife).(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Priests of old called [kingsmoots](wiki:Kingsmoot)(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) and carried burning brands.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) They are more supportive of the [Old Way](wiki:Old_Way) and paying the [iron price](wiki:Iron_price).(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_red_kraken.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) They also act as [prophets](wiki:Prophet).(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

## Origins

### Ancient History[ ]

While [maesters](wiki:Maesters) believe the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) descend from the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God)'s priests claim their people came from the sea.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Priests proudly tell stories of the [Grey King](wiki:Grey_King) and ancient reavers from the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes).(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) The greatest priest was [Galon Whitestaff](wiki:Galon_Whitestaff), who called for a kingsmoot to name [Urras Greyiron](wiki:Urras_Greyiron) as [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-2)
Priests denounced [Urrathon IV Goodbrother](wiki:Urrathon_IV_Goodbrother) for claiming the crown while [Torgon Greyiron](wiki:Torgon_Greyiron) was away.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) [Urron Greyiron](wiki:Urron_Greyiron) killed half a hundred priests and prophets at the kingsmoot in which he established [House Greyiron](wiki:House_Greyiron) as hereditary Kings of the Iron Islands.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-3) Priests ardently opposed [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) for wedding [Andals](wiki:Andals) and supporting the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), claiming the Hoares had "black blood".(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) King [Horgan Hoare](wiki:Horgan_Hoare) slaughtered priests after they attacked followers of the Seven.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) The [Shrike](wiki:Shrike) led a rebellion against King [Harmund III Hoare](wiki:Harmund_III_Hoare).(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3)

### Targaryen Era[ ]

During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), King [Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare) was killed in the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal). Twoscore priests crowned [Lodos](wiki:Lodos) at [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk), and the would-be king called for [krakens](wiki:Kraken) to destroy the ships of King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen). When none of the beasts appeared, a stone-laden Lodos walked into the sea with thousands of followers to see his supposed father, the Drowned God.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
Priests were angered by Lord [Vickon Greyjoy](wiki:Vickon_Greyjoy) allowing the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) to return to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) After Lord [Goren Greyjoy](wiki:Goren_Greyjoy) slew [Lodos the Twice-Drowned](wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned) in [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) allowed the septons to be expelled from the islands.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
Maester [Cerrick](wiki:Cerrick) studied the beliefs and customs of the priests.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### Baratheon Dynasty[ ]

When [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) first named himself [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands) at the [Grey King's Hall](wiki:Grey_King's_Hall), [Tarle the Thrice-Drowned](wiki:Tarle_the_Thrice-Drowned) placed a driftwood [crown](wiki:Crown) upon his head.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
Some time after [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy)'s ship went down in a storm, but he washed up ashore, safely and unharmed. Due to his near-death experience, he underwent a spiritual reawakening, and dedicated himself as a priest of the Drowned God.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

After [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy) calls for a [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk) after the death of his brother [Balon](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy), [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North),(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) his effort is supported by other priests throughout the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
Aeron Damphair attempts to rouse the islands against his brother, King [Euron Crow's Eye](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), after Euron's selection at [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk).(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Erik Ironmaker](wiki:Erik_Ironmaker) hunts down [drowned men](wiki:Drowned_men) and priests during the search for the vanished Damphair.(wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
