## Origins

Driftmark was settled by [House Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) prior to the arrival of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)).(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Ships from Driftmark allowed the Velaryons to control the middle of the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) prior to [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
Shortly after the death of her husband, King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) brought her children to [Castle Driftmark](wiki:Castle_Driftmark).(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) When King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) lay comatose after a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven), Alyssa proclaimed on Driftmark that her son, Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Aenys_I)), was the true king.(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) After the [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) were defeated by Maegor at the Great Fork of the [Blackwater](wiki:Blackwater_Rush), Alyssa and her Velaryon kin left Driftmark and submitted to Maegor in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-2)
Many merchants traded at Driftmark instead of King's Landing to avoid the taxes of Lord [Edwell Celtigar](wiki:Edwell_Celtigar), the [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) for King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) When Ser [Lucamore Strong](wiki:Lucamore_Strong) was disgraced from the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), his second wife and her children were sent to Driftmark to be fostered with Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)).(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
Because the ancestral castle Driftmark was damp and cramped, Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) constructed a new castle on the opposite side of the island to house the [Driftwood Throne](wiki:Driftwood_Throne): [High Tide](wiki:High_Tide), so-called because it was built on a tidal island only linked to Driftmark at low tide. As the island prospered from Lord Corlys's wealth, two large port towns grew on up the coasts of Driftmark: [Hull](wiki:Hull) beneath Castle Driftmark and [Spicetown](wiki:Spicetown) beneath High Tide.(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) When King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) brought his court to Driftmark for the funeral of Ser [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon) in [120 AC](wiki:120_AC), the number of [dragons](wiki:Dragon) gathered on the island led Septon [Eustace](wiki:Eustace_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)) to call it the new [Valyria](wiki:Valyria).(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
The dragon [Sheepstealer](wiki:Sheepstealer) was known for grabbing sheep from flocks in [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay) from Driftmark to as far as the [Wendwater](wiki:Wendwater).(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Lord Corlys, the Sea Snake, launched [black](wiki:Blacks) ships from Hull and Spicetown to block access to Blackwater Bay. High Tide and Spicetown were burned in the [Battle in the Gullet](wiki:Battle_in_the_Gullet), the latter so severely it was not rebuilt.(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) [Hugh Hammer](wiki:Hugh_Hammer) and [Ulf White](wiki:Ulf_White) were given small lands on Driftmark after the [fall of King's Landing](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing), but the two new knights felt they deserved greater rewards.(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) Driftmark became hostile to Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), the leader of the blacks, after she imprisoned Corlys.(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Since Lord Corlys had not yet reconciled with the Queen Dowager [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) on behalf of her son, King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen), the leader of the [greens](wiki:Greens), [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) prepared the [Velaryon fleet](wiki:Velaryon_fleet) at Driftmark to attack Aegon on Dragonstone. Conflict was avoided when Corlys was pardoned by Alicent.(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
Lord Corlys served as [a regent](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III) for King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen). After his death in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), Corlys's body was first sailed to Driftmark by [Marilda of Hull](wiki:Marilda_of_Hull) and Lord Alyn aboard [Mermaid's Kiss](wiki:Mermaid's_Kiss), then buried at sea near Dragonstone aboard [Sea Snake](wiki:Sea_Snake). [Daemion](wiki:Daemion_Velaryon) and [Daeron Velaryon](wiki:Daeron_Velaryon) reconciled with Alyn in return for lands on Driftmark.(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
Lady [Baela Targaryen](wiki:Baela_Targaryen) lived on Driftmark after wedding Alyn.(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) After learning that Alyn Oakenfist was sailing to the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea) to end [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) raiding, [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), desired to slay Alyn and then conquer Driftmark for himself. Dalton was slain by [Tess](wiki:Tess) at [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), however.(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rfab_under_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
In [138 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the bones of [Addam Velaryon](wiki:Addam_Velaryon), who had died fighting for the blacks at [Second Tumbleton](wiki:Second_Battle_of_Tumbleton) despite having been denounced by Rhaenyra, were sent from [Raventree Hall](wiki:Raventree_Hall) to Driftmark. For the epitaph on his older brother's tomb, Lord Alyn put only the word "LOYAL".(wiki:Driftmark.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
