## Origins

The [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) tasked Ser [Olyver Bracken](wiki:Olyver_Bracken) and Ser [Raymund Mallery](wiki:Raymund_Mallery) with restoring the crumbling Rimegate and [Sable Hall](wiki:Sable_Hall), respectively, but the two former [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) rebelled from the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) in [50 AC](wiki:50_AC) with the intention to make the castles their seats as [lords](wiki:Lord). Aided by [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), loyal brothers of the Night's Watch recaptured Rimegate and hanged Olyver's rebels. Lord [Walton Stark](wiki:Walton_Stark_(son_of_Brandon)) beheaded Olyver with [Ice](wiki:Ice).(wiki:Rimegate.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Lord Commander [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) intends to garrison the abandoned castles of Rimegate and [Hoarfrost Hill](wiki:Hoarfrost_Hill), and consults with [Lord Steward](wiki:Lord_Steward) [Bowen Marsh](wiki:Bowen_Marsh) and [First Builder](wiki:First_Builder) [Othell Yarwyck](wiki:Othell_Yarwyck) for their opinion of which [wildling](wiki:Free_folk) chieftains would be best suited to hold the castles. Both men strongly reject his suggestions, with Marsh urging they be hanged for their deeds and Yarwyck claiming it was a lethal choice between "bad" and "worse."(wiki:Rimegate.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
