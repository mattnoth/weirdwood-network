## Identity

King in the North is a royal title held by the ruling monarchs of the North — the vast region of Westeros north of the Neck — for thousands of years before Aegon's Conquest. The title originated after the Long Night and the founding of Winterfell, with House Stark of Winterfell becoming its primary and near-perpetual holders. In the age before the Targaryens, the Starks were called Kings of Winter (an older variant of the title) and held sway over the North for roughly eight thousand years since the Age of Heroes, beginning with legendary figures such as Brandon the Builder. The title's holders gradually subdued rival claimants, including the Red Kings of House Bolton to the east and the Barrow Kings to the south, consolidating the North under Stark rule. During the coming of the Andals, the Kings of Winter were the only kingdom in Westeros to successfully turn back Andal invasion, repelling them at Moat Cailin and the eastern shores. Notable individual kings include Theon Stark, who defeated the Andal warlord Argos Sevenstar at the Battle of the Weeping Water and then sailed to burn Andal villages in Andalos; Jon Stark, who drove raiders from the White Knife and built the Wolf's Den; and Torrhen Stark, the last independent King in the North, who surrendered his crown at the Trident to Aegon I Targaryen rather than face dragonflame, earning the epithet "the King Who Knelt." After Torrhen's submission, the Starks became Lords of Winterfell and the title fell dormant for nearly three centuries, with the Inn of the Kneeling Man marking the spot where Torrhen bent the knee.

The title was revived during the War of the Five Kings when, following Eddard Stark's execution by King Joffrey, the assembled northmen and river lords at Riverrun proclaimed Robb Stark King in the North and (separately) King of the Trident. Greatjon Umber was the first to declare Robb king, rousing the others with the argument that the North should rule itself again, as the Targaryen dragons were gone. Robb's smiths forged him a crown modeled on the ancient crowns of the Kings of Winter — a hammered bronze circlet incised with runes of the First Men, topped with nine iron spikes shaped like longswords. His reign was cut short at the Red Wedding, where he was slain by Roose Bolton at the Twins; his direwolf Grey Wind's head was sewn onto his body as a final insult to the King in the North. In the aftermath, House Bolton was named Warden of the North by the Iron Throne, effectively extinguishing the title again. Loyalist sentiment persisted among northern houses: Lyanna Mormont of Bear Island responded to Stannis Baratheon's demand for homage with the message "Bear Island knows no king but the King in the North, whose name is STARK."
