## Identity

King-Beyond-the-Wall is the informal title given to any wildling leader who manages to unite the fractious free folk clans and peoples north of the Wall under a single banner. The title carries no crown, scepter, or hereditary succession — it is earned through personal charisma, strength, and the ability to forge consensus among peoples who reject the concept of bending the knee entirely. Known holders across the ages include Bael the Bard (a legendary wildling bard who raided Winterfell and fathered a son on a Stark maiden before becoming king and dying at the Frozen Ford), Gendel and Gorne (brothers who led wildlings through ancient tunnels beneath the Wall into the North), Joramun (said to have blown the Horn of Winter to wake giants from the earth), Rodrik Flint, the Horned Lord, Raymun Redbeard (defeated by the Starks and Umbers at the Battle of Long Lake in 226 AC), and most recently Mance Rayder.

Mance Rayder — a former ranger of the Night's Watch who deserted — is the most prominent holder of the title in the series' present. He united the free folk in response to the Others and their wights emerging from the far north, seeking passage through the Wall not to conquer Westeros but to use it as a shield. Despite his numbers, he was defeated at the Battle of Castle Black and subsequently captured by Stannis Baratheon, whose red priestess Melisandre burns him as a sacrifice — though Mance is secretly swapped with the spearwife Rattleshirt under Melisandre's glamour at Jon Snow's instruction. The title's "king" designation causes concern that Mance's infant son may be considered to have royal blood suitable for sacrifice.
