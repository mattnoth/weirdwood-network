## Origins

Since the founding of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), the Sealords have opposed [slavery](wiki:Slavery) in all its forms and have fought many wars against slavers and their allies.(wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_braavos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

In [49 AC](wiki:49_AC) the Sealord sent a son to attend the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) of Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) and Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon).(wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Using the name "Alys Westhill", [Elissa Farman](wiki:Elissa_Farman) sailed to Braavos and sold three [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_eggs) to the Sealord in [54 AC](wiki:54_AC). Elissa used the funds to commission *[Sun Chaser](wiki:Sun_Chaser)*, which she used to explore the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea) two years later.(wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Septon [Barth](wiki:Barth), the [Hand](wiki:Hand) of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), came to Braavos to discuss the matter of the stolen dragon eggs. Barth threatened Braavos with [dragonfire](wiki:Dragonfire) should the eggs hatch; in return, the Sealord threatened to send the [Faceless Men](wiki:Faceless_Men) to assassinate the Targaryens. In the end, the Sealord denied having the eggs, but he agreed to forgive the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne)'s debts to the [Iron Bank of Braavos](wiki:Iron_Bank_of_Braavos).(wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

[Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon) was betrothed to the son of a Sealord, but the Sealord died before any marriage, and the son turned out to be a fool, wasting his family's money and power before ending up on [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark). Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), who wished to marry Laena, provoked the Braavosi into a challenge of single combat, and the fool died.(wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)(wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_viserys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

During the [Daughters' War](wiki:Daughters'_War), the Sealord allied with [Racallio Ryndoon](wiki:Racallio_Ryndoon) and the [Archon of Tyrosh](wiki:Archon_of_Tyrosh) to divide the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) jointly. The Sealord's prized dromond, *[Grand Defiance](wiki:Grand_Defiance)*, was sunk by [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) during [Lord Alyn's attack](wiki:Lord_Alyn's_attack). Lord [Manfryd Mooton](wiki:Manfryd_Mooton) negotiated with the Sealord in Braavos and prevented a war. Manfryd also returned a captured [elephant](wiki:Elephant) which had been meant for the [Sealord's menagerie](wiki:Sealord's_menagerie). The [fool](wiki:Fool) [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) greatly amused the Sealord, but Mushroom declined an offer to remain in Braavos.(wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

King [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen) planned to marry one of his sisters to the Sealord for an alliance to remove the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) pirates that were hindering trade with newly-conquered [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), but the marriage never occurred.(wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

[Bellegere Otherys](wiki:Bellegere_Otherys), the Black Pearl of Braavos, was the daughter of union between a princess from the [Summer Isles](wiki:Summer_Isles) and a Sealord's son.(wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos.cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Bmercy_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Cmercy.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

After [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), travelled to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) where [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) and [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) were being kept by Ser [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry). Oberyn and Willem signed [a secret pact](wiki:Secret_marriage_pact), in which Viserys was promised the hand of Doran's daughter, [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell), in return for [Dorne](wiki:Dorne)'s help in [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) reclaiming the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne). The pact was witnessed by the Sealord.(wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

[Syrio Forel](wiki:Syrio_Forel) once served as [First Sword](wiki:First_Sword) to a Sealord.(wiki:Sealord_of_Braavos.cite_ref-Ragot22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
