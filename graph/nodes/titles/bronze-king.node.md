## Origins

The Bronze Kings from [House Royce](wiki:House_Royce) of [Runestone](wiki:Runestone) had long waged war against [House Shett](wiki:House_Shett) of [Gulltown](wiki:Gulltown), who used the title [King of the True Men](wiki:King_of_the_True_Men). King [Osgood III Shett](wiki:Osgood_III_Shett) sought aid from [Andals](wiki:Andals) in his feud with [Yorwyck VI Royce](wiki:Yorwyck_VI_Royce), but Osgood was eventually betrayed by [House Grafton](wiki:House_Grafton).(wiki:Bronze_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) Yorwyck defeated Andals in battle several times, and his grandson, [Robar II Royce](wiki:Robar_II_Royce), led the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) resistance against the invading Andals after being proclaimed [High King of the Vale, the Fingers, and the Mountains of the Moon](wiki:High_King_of_the_Vale,_the_Fingers,_and_the_Mountains_of_the_Moon). Robar was defeated in the [Battle of the Seven Stars](wiki:Battle_of_the_Seven_Stars), however, and [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) subsequently ruled the [Vale of Arryn](wiki:Vale_of_Arryn) as [Kings of Mountain and Vale](wiki:Kings_of_Mountain_and_Vale).(wiki:Bronze_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)
Members of House Royce still wear ancestral [bronze](wiki:Bronze) armor engraved with runes, although there is no evidence that their armor is actually [magical](wiki:Magic).(wiki:Bronze_King.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
