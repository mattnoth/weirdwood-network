## Origins

Lord [Jon Rosby](wiki:Jon_Rosby) was named [Warden of the Sands](wiki:Warden_of_the_Sands) by King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) during the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), but Jon was killed in the [Defenestration of Sunspear](wiki:Defenestration_of_Sunspear).(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Rtwoiafdorne:_dorne_against_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) When word of Jon's death reached the [Hellholt](wiki:Hellholt), Lord [Harlan Tyrell](wiki:Harlan_Tyrell) set off towards [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) to punish Lord Rosby's murderers. However, somewhere east of the Hellholt amidst the red sands, Tyrell and his entire army disappeared. No man of them was ever seen again.(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
During the reign of King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), Lord Rosby was one of the few lords to remain loyal to Maegor near the end of his reign.(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) He along Lord [Jordan Towers](wiki:Jordan_Towers) were the last to see the king alive before Maegor's death on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) After the king was found dead, Lord Rosby committed suicide by drinking a cup of [hemlock](wiki:Poison). His young son received forgiveness from King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
A Lord Rosby died from the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) in the winter of [59 AC](wiki:59_AC).(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
During the reign of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), [Lord Rosby](wiki:Lord_Rosby) swore to defend the succession rights of Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen). In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) in which Rhaenyra's brother [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) challenged her rule by claiming Viserys's throne for himself, Lord Rosby was arrested by Aegon II's supporters. When given the choice between swearing his allegiance to Aegon II or dying, Lord Rosby pledged his support to Aegon II.(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) When Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) marched from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to attack [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) and ultimately [Rook's Rest](wiki:Rook's_Rest), he passed by Rosby and [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth) to command both lords to prove their loyalty by joining his attack.(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) When King's Landing [fell](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) to Queen Rhaenyra during [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), Lord Rosby attempted to switch his allegiance again. Queen Rhaenyra refused him, however, and ordered his tongue removed, after which he was executed. Following Lord Rosby's death, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) suggested marrying Rosby's young daughter to [Hugh Hammer](wiki:Hugh_Hammer), to grant Hammer the Rosby lands. Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), the [Hand of the Queen](wiki:Hand_of_the_Queen), argued instead in favor of Rosby's younger son. According to Lord Corlys, disinheriting the younger son in favor of the older daughter would call into question the rights of multiple other lords, who had elder sisters whose claims might suddenly be seen as superior. Unwilling to lose the support of such lords, according to [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun), Rhaenyra eventually named Rosby's son as the new Lord of Rosby.(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
When Rhaenyra later [fled](wiki:Flight_to_Dragonstone) the capital following the [riots](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing_(Dance_of_the_Dragons)), she sought refuge at Rosby but the elder sister of the new Lord of Rosby, whose claim had been put aside, barred her the gates of the castle at her approach.(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
Lord [Gyles Rosby](wiki:Gyles_Rosby) was among those offered as possible matches by [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), to his daughter, Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell).(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Gyles Rosby is a wealthy lord(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) frequently seen at the court of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), always coughing. He conspires with Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) to take Prince [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) out of the city and keep him safe at [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) disguised as a page. Ser [Jacelyn Bywater](wiki:Jacelyn_Bywater) lays a trap along the [Rosby road](wiki:Rosby_road), and Lord Gyles is captured, along with Tommen and his guard.(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Lord Gyles is among the gathered nobility to welcome the [Dornish](wiki:Dorne) party, headed by Prince [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), to King's Landing.(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
Gyles testifies at Tyrion's trial for the death of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) at his [wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding), stating that he had seen him filling Joffrey's [wedding chalice](wiki:Wedding_chalice).(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Rasos66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Lord Gyles is named [master of coin](wiki:Master_of_coin) (under the title of [lord treasurer](wiki:Lord_treasurer)) by Queen Regent Cersei.(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) Gyles attends the wedding of King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) and [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell).(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
Lord Gyles dies at King's Landing from his cough. He has no immediate heir, and though Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) raises that his [ward](wiki:Ward_of_Gyles_Rosby) has a claim, Cersei dismisses the ward as having no blood relation.(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

When the issue of succession at [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) is raised at the [small council](wiki:Small_council), [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) states there are six claimants. However, Ser [Kevan Lannister](wiki:Kevan_Lannister) tables the issue.(wiki:Lord_of_Rosby.cite_ref-Radwdepilogue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
