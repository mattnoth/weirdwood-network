## Origins

After the death of their royal spouse, a queen retains her title as queen and is known as the Dowager Queen or Queen Dowager. The Dowager Queen may still hold political power after her husband's death. Dowager Queen Visenya Targaryen advised her nephew King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen), though, paralyzed by indecision, he heeded little of it. She would go on to ensure her own son, Aenys' half-brother [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), succeeded as king and worked closely with Maegor to enforce his rule until her death.(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) In the immediate hours after her husband [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen)'s death, Dowager Queen [Alicent Hightower](wiki:Alicent_Hightower) was central figure in the scheme to crown her son [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) as king over their father's chosen heir, Aegon's elder sister [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
The Dowager Queen may also serve as Queen Regent in her son's minority. Dowager Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) would act as regent for her son [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) until he came of age.(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Queen Dowager [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) also served as Queen Regent(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) for both her sons, King [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon)(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) and King [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) The extent of this power can vary. In the case of Cersei, the willful personality of Joffrey(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) as well as the presence of her brother [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister)(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and father [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) curtailed her influence.(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) During Tommen's reign, without the influence of father and brother and with a far more obedient son, the Dowager Queen and Regent was able to govern as she wished.(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
In some cases, the queen may marry a surviving relative of the deceased king and thus not take on the title of Dowager Queen, as was the case of Queen [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell),(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) who wed first the self-proclaimed King [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon)(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and then his alleged nephews, King [Joffrey Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon)(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) and King [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Raffc12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
The widow of a [lord](wiki:Lord) may also be similarly styled as a Dowager Lady, as in the case of [Samantha Hightower](wiki:Samantha_Tarly), Dowager Lady of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Dowager_Queen.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
