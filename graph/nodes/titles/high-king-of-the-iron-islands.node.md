## Origins

### Origin[ ]

In antiquity each of the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) was ruled by two petty kings, a [rock king](wiki:Rock_king) who ruled the land and a [salt king](wiki:Salt_king) who led the island's ships, with both kings chosen by the island's captains in [kingsmoots](wiki:Kingsmoot).(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) [Galon Whitestaff](wiki:Galon_Whitestaff), a [prophet of the Drowned God](wiki:Prophet_of_the_Drowned_God), eventually called all of the salt and rock kings and the captains to [Nagga's Hill](wiki:Nagga's_Hill) on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk), where [Urras Greyiron](wiki:Urras_Greyiron) was chosen from among them as the first High King of the Iron Islands. Urras became the first monarch to rule all ironborn since the [Grey King](wiki:Grey_King).(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
After the death of Urras, his eldest son [Erich](wiki:Erich_I_Greyiron) claimed the kingship. An outraged Galon insisted that only a kingsmoot could choose the king, however, and the ironborn who gathered to Old Wyk condemned Erich to death. The would-be king was able to spare his life by breaking up Urras's crown and casting it into the sea, and [Regnar Drumm](wiki:Regnar_Drumm) was chosen as High King instead.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Whenever a high king died, his driftwood crown would thus be traditionally broken into the sea, and his successor, always chosen through a kingsmoot at the [Grey King's Hall](wiki:Grey_King's_Hall) on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk),(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) would then craft his own personalized crown from driftwood freshly washed up on the isle of his home.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

### Conquests and Losses[ ]

According to Archmaester [Haereg](wiki:Haereg)'s [History of the Ironborn](wiki:History_of_the_Ironborn), there were at least 111 high kings.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3) At least thirty-eight of them came from [House Greyiron](wiki:House_Greyiron), twice as many as any other house.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
While reavers of earlier generations had returned to their isles after raids, the united realm of the High Kings began to conquer distant lands.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4) The ironborn controlled the western shore of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) along the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea) during the reign of King [Qhored I Hoare](wiki:Qhored_I_Hoare), who boasted his writ ran "wherever men can smell salt water or hear the crash of waves".(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
Qhored was followed by weaker kings, however, and houses from the [green lands](wiki:Green_lands) such as the [Hightowers](wiki:House_Hightower), [Gardeners](wiki:House_Gardener), and [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) gained in strength.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-5) [Westermen](wiki:Westermen) recovered [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle) and [Kayce](wiki:Kayce) from ironborn control, and [Gerold Lannister](wiki:Gerold_Lannister), [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock), led the [Lannister fleet](wiki:Lannister_fleet) in a raid on the Iron Islands. The high kings eventually lost the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor), [Bear Island](wiki:Bear_Island), [Flint's Finger](wiki:Flint's_Finger), and much of the western shore over the following centuries.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

### Final Kingsmoots[ ]

When King [Urragon III Greyiron](wiki:Urragon_III_Greyiron) died during the [Age of Heroes](wiki:Age_of_Heroes), a kingsmoot was called by his family while the king's oldest son, [Torgon Greyiron](wiki:Torgon_Greyiron), was away raiding the [Mander](wiki:Mander). Urragon the Bald's younger sons were hoping that one of them would be elected, but the ironborn chose King [Urrathon IV Goodbrother](wiki:Urrathon_IV_Goodbrother) instead. The new king's first act was to order the executions of the sons of Urragon who were at the kingsmoot, and Urrathon became known as Badbrother during his two years of cruel rule.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
When Torgon returned to the Iron Islands, he declared the kingsmoot's decision unlawful as he had not been present to put forth his own claim. The priests, smallfolk, and captains agreed, and Urrathon was put down and hacked to pieces by his own captains.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) Although he was not chosen at a kingsmooot, Torgon the Latecomer succeeded Badbrother as king.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-6) Torgon had his son Urragon help him rule when he was old, and after Torgon died the son claimed the throne as King [Urragon IV Greyiron](wiki:Urragon_IV_Greyiron), again without having been chosen at a kingsmoot.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-7)
Urragon's own dying wish was for his great-nephew [Urron Greyiron](wiki:Urron_Greyiron) to succeed him as high king. The priests insisted upon a kingsmoot after Urragon passed, however, and hundreds of ironborn came to Old Wyk. Urron Redhand sent axemen to attack the attendees, with thirteen rock and salt kings and half a hundred priests killed.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-8) [House Greyiron](wiki:House_Greyiron) henceforth ruled as hereditary [Kings of the Iron Islands](wiki:Kings_of_the_Iron_Islands) unchosen by kingsmoot,(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-9) and the rock and salt kings were reduced to vassal [lords](wiki:Lord) or extinguished.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)

### Recent History[ ]

After the destruction of [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare), [Kings of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:Kings_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), in [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) fell into chaos and infighting. In opposition to [Qhorin Volmark](wiki:Qhorin_Volmark), who claimed to be [King of the Iron Islands](wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands), a holy man named [Lodos](wiki:Lodos) was given a driftwood crown by [priests of the Drowned God](wiki:Priests_of_the_Drowned_God) at [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk). Continuing the [Wars of Conquest](wiki:Wars_of_Conquest), King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) led an [invasion of the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands) and beheaded Qhorin. Lodos, meanwhile, walked into the sea with his followers after his claim that [krakens](wiki:Kraken) would destroy Aegon's fleet failed to occur.(wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
