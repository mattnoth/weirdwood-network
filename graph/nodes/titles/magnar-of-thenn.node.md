## Culture

[Magnar](wiki:Magnar) is a word in the [Old Tongue](wiki:Old_Tongue) translated as "[lord](wiki:Lord)" in the [Common Tongue](wiki:Common_Tongue).(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Rasos48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
The [Thenns](wiki:Thenns) appear to see [Styr](wiki:Styr) more as a [god](wiki:God) than a lord.(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Origins

[Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) had to defeat [Styr](wiki:Styr) three times before the Magnar agreed to support him as [King-beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-beyond-the-Wall).(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Styr](wiki:Styr), Magnar of Thenn, resents having to share authority with [Jarl](wiki:Jarl) when they are sent by [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) to cross the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) Jarl falls to his death while attempting to climb the Wall.(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Rasos30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Styr leads the [attack on Castle Black](wiki:Attack_on_Castle_Black) but is slain when the castle's wooden stair and several tons of ice collapse on him and his men.(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
Styr's son [Sigorn](wiki:Sigorn), the new Magnar of Thenn,(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Raffcappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) is held captive by [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) after the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall).(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Word spreads at Castle Black that Stannis intends to grant lands and castles to wildling leaders like [Rattleshirt](wiki:Rattleshirt) and the Magnar of Thenn.(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Stannis considers placing wildlings led by Sigorn as his van when he departs Castle Black, but [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), advises him against the idea.(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
[Iron Emmett](wiki:Iron_Emmett) recommends that Jon grant one of the ruined castles of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) to the Thenns.(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
[Alys Karstark](wiki:Alys_Karstark) flees [Karhold](wiki:Karhold) to avoid being forced to marry her kin [Cregan](wiki:Cregan_Karstark).(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Radwd44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Lord Commander Snow arranges for her to marry Sigorn. During [their wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Sigorn_and_Alys_Karstark), Sigorn dons arms for the newly-created [House Thenn](wiki:House_Thenn). Alys believes that Karhold will open its gates to her and Sigorn's two hundred Thenns.(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

## Quotes

The Magnar claimed to be the last of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), and ruled with an iron hand. His little land of [Thenn](wiki:Thenn_(valley)) was a high mountain valley hidden amongst the northernmost peaks of the [Frostfangs](wiki:Frostfangs), surrounded by [cave dwellers](wiki:Cave_dwellers), [Hornfoot](wiki:Hornfoot) men, [giants](wiki:Giants), and the [cannibal clans of the ice rivers](wiki:Ice-river_clans). [Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte) said the [Thenns](wiki:Thenns) were savage fighters, and that their Magnar was a [god](wiki:God) to them. Jon could believe that. Unlike [Jarl](wiki:Jarl) and [Harma](wiki:Harma) and [Rattleshirt](wiki:Rattleshirt), [Styr](wiki:Styr) commanded absolute obedience from his men, and that discipline was no doubt part of why [Mance](wiki:Mance_Rayder) had chosen him to go over the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Rasos26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
[The Magnar](wiki:Styr) is a ruler, not a raider.(wiki:Magnar_of_Thenn.cite_ref-Rasos30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)
Emmett: You might do well to bestow a castle on the Magnar.
Jon: I might if I could trust him. [Sigorn](wiki:Sigorn) blames me for his father's death, I fear. Worse, he was bred and trained to give orders, not to take them. Do not confuse the [Thenns](wiki:Thenns) with [free folk](wiki:Free_folk). [Magnar](wiki:Magnar) means [lord](wiki:Lord) in the [Old Tongue](wiki:Old_Tongue), I am told, but [Styr](wiki:Styr) was closer to a god to his people, and his son is cut from the same skin. I do not require men to kneel, but they do need to obey.
