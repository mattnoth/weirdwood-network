## Edges


## Origins

In [7 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Prince [Aenys Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) was born to King [Aegon I](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and Queen [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen). Aenys was weak and sickly at birth, and cried all the time. He rejected wet nurses, and would only nurse at his mother's breast.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)
In [47 AC](wiki:47_AC), King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) married his [Black Brides](wiki:Black_Brides), three women of proven fertility whose husbands he had killed. [Elinor Costayne](wiki:Elinor_Costayne)'s sons were sent away from her after her wedding to Maegor; her eldest was fostered at the [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie), her second son went to [Highgarden](wiki:Highgarden), and her youngest was given to a wet nurse, as Maegor was annoyed by Elinor nursing him.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-3)
According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), Queen [Alyssa Velaryon](wiki:Alyssa_Velaryon) did not breastfeed any of her children, who were thus all fed by wet nurses.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
It was said that Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) had learned to read before she was weaned, and the court [fool](wiki:Fool) would make jokes that she had spilled milk on [Valyrian](wiki:Valyria) scrolls while drinking from her wet nurse.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
Queen Alysanne and King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen)'s daughter, Princess [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Jaehaerys_I)) was talking and running before the age of one and a half, and her wet nurse described her as being in a great hurry.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) When the six-year-old princess was stricken with the [Shivers](wiki:Shivers) in [60 AC](wiki:60_AC), a wet nurse was summoned even though Daenerys had long been weaned, because some believed mother's milk could cure the disease.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1)
Prince [Baelon](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I))'s wet nurses complained that the baby sucked harder than any other they had encountered.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Princess [Saera](wiki:Saera_Targaryen) was a difficult child, and refused to be weaned until past the age of four. She wanted her mother's milk, and would scream and throw fits when Queen Alysanne had to dismiss another wet nurse. Prince [Valerion](wiki:Valerion_Targaryen) was a small and sickly baby who never thrived, despite the attempts of half a dozen wet nurses.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2)
In [77 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Prince Baelon and his wife Princess [Alyssa Targaryen](wiki:Alyssa_Targaryen) welcomed their first son, [Viserys](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen). He was described as a plump and pleasant baby, who drank his wet nurse dry.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)
In [82 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Princess [Daella Targaryen](wiki:Daella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Jaehaerys_I)) gave birth to her daughter, [Aemma Arryn](wiki:Aemma_Arryn), and though she wished to nurse her child, [childbed fever](wiki:Disease) had dried up her milk, and a wet nurse had to be sent for.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4)
In [114 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) ordered his son [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Viserys_I)) and his grandson [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) to be nursed by the same wet nurse, because he hoped to prevent enmity between them by raising them as [milk brothers](wiki:Milk_brother). Unfortunately, the plan was unsuccessful.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)
In [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Prince [Aemond Targaryen](wiki:Aemond_Targaryen) fell in love with [Alys Rivers](wiki:Alys_Rivers), a mysterious woman and reported [woods witch](wiki:Woods_witch) who was also a wet nurse at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Though reputable historians say Alys was the [bastard](wiki:Bastard) daughter of Lord [Lyonel Strong](wiki:Lyonel_Strong), the fool [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom)'s [Testimony](wiki:The_Testimony_of_Mushroom) claims that Alys was far older than she looked and had nursed Lyonel's sons, and perhaps even Lyonel himself.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) By [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), Alys was known as the "witch queen" of Harrenhal.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)
In [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), trials were held for those involved in the conspiracy surrounding the end of the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring) and the [secret siege](wiki:Secret_siege) of [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast). Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_II_Targaryen) attended every day, often accompanied by his wife [Larra Rogare](wiki:Larra_Rogare) and their son [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen), as well as the baby's wet nurse.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)
[Old Nan](wiki:Old_Nan) first came to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) to nurse Lord [Willam Stark](wiki:Willam_Stark)'s son [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(son_of_Willam)), whose [mother](wiki:Lyanne_Glover) had died in childbirth. Unfortunately, Brandon died of a [summer](wiki:Summer_(season)) [chill](wiki:Disease) when he was three, but Nan continued to live at Winterfell, nursing many children over the years and telling them stories. Even after Nan grew too old to serve as a wet nurse, she continued to take care of the Stark children and tell them her tales.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)
In [255 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) and his wife, Lady [Jeyne Marbrand](wiki:Jeyne_Marbrand), welcomed their fifth child, [Gerion](wiki:Gerion_Lannister). He was healthy, but his mother died within a month of his birth. Though Tytos was greatly saddened by the death of his wife, he soon became enamored of Gerion's wet nurse, and made her [his first mistress](wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_first_mistress). Tytos often retreated to her comforts rather than deal with ruling the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands). By [267 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), the wet nurse had been replaced by [Tytos's second mistress](wiki:Tytos_Lannister's_second_mistress).(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf761_33-0)
In [273 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Lady [Joanna Lannister](wiki:Joanna_Lannister) died in childbirth, and her son [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) was given to the care of a wet nurse.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) Tyrion's older sister, [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), wanted to show off the [dwarf](wiki:Dwarf) baby to [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock)'s guests, [Elia](wiki:Elia_Martell) and [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell). The wet nurse tried to send them away, but Cersei threatened her, calling her a "milk cow" who [didn't need a tongue](wiki:Tongue_mutilation).(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)
When the infant Prince [Jaehaerys Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aerys_II)) died in [274 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), his father, King [Aerys II](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), angrily blamed the baby's wet nurse, and had her beheaded. Aerys later blamed [his mistress](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen's_last_mistress) and her family, before taking the child's death as a sign from the [gods](wiki:Gods) that he should be true to his wife alone.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3)
The birth of Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) in [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) only seemed to make King Aerys II more fearful and obsessive. Though the baby was healthy, the king was terrified that he would die, and had the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard) watch him day and night. Even Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) was forbidden to be alone with her son, and when her milk dried up, Aerys insisted that his own [food taster](wiki:Food_taster) drink from the breasts of the prince's wet nurse, to make sure she had not smeared [poison](wiki:Poison) on her nipples.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-4)
In [283 AC](wiki:283_AC), after the end of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark)'s new bride [Catelyn Tully](wiki:Catelyn_Stark) traveled to [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) from [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun) with their first baby, [Robb](wiki:Robb_Stark). When Catelyn arrived, she found that her husband's [bastard](wiki:Bastard), [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), and his wet nurse were already living there, which greatly upset Catelyn.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Ragot6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
In [284 AC](wiki:284_AC) on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), Queen Rhaella Targaryen gave birth to her daughter [Daenerys](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), and died in childbirth. Shortly afterwards, King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon)'s brother [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) plannned to [assault Dragonstone](wiki:Assault_on_Dragonstone). As the Targaryen fleet had been destroyed by the storm during which Daenerys had been born, the garrison at Dragonstone planned to sell her and her brother Viserys to Robert. However, before they could enact this plan, Ser [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry) and several other loyal retainers rescued the children and smuggled them into exile, along with Daenerys's wet nurse, sailing to the [Free City](wiki:Free_City) of [Braavos](wiki:Braavos).(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

On the road to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), King [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_Baratheon)'s men are surprised to see [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark)'s [direwolf](wiki:Direwolf) [Lady](wiki:Lady) in their camp, and [Sandor Clegane](wiki:Sandor_Clegane) jokes that [the Starks](wiki:House_Stark) use direwolves as wet nurses.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

The infant Lady [Ermesande Hayford](wiki:Ermesande_Hayford) attends the [tourney](wiki:Tourney_on_King_Joffrey's_name_day) on King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon)'s [name day](wiki:Name_day), seated on her wet nurse's lap. Sansa Stark has heard gossip that Ermesande may soon be married to one of [Queen Regent](wiki:Queen_Regent) [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister)'s cousins, so that [the Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) may claim her lands.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Racok2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)
After [Tyrek Lannister](wiki:Tyrek_Lannister) marries Ermesande, he is mockingly nicknamed "Wet Nurse" by his fellow [squires](wiki:Squire).(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

In the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), [Edric Dayne](wiki:Edric_Dayne) tells [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) that her half brother [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) is his [milk brother](wiki:Milk_brother).(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-40) Edric explains that his mother did not have enough milk for him when he was born, so he had to be nursed by [Wylla](wiki:Wylla), a wet nurse at [Starfall](wiki:Starfall). When Arya asks who Wylla is, Edric replies that she is Jon's mother.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)
Sansa Stark is introduced to [Grisel](wiki:Grisel), [Petyr Baelish](wiki:Petyr_Baelish)'s former wet nurse and now the keeper of [his tower](wiki:Drearfort).(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rasos68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-0)
[Gilly](wiki:Gilly) arrives at [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) with [her infant son](wiki:Monster), and becomes a wet nurse for [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder)'s [newborn son](wiki:Aemon_Steelsong) because his mother [Dalla](wiki:Dalla) has died in childbirth.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

At Castle Black, [Lord Commander](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) Jon Snow explains to [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) that he is sending Gilly and her son away from the [Wall](wiki:Wall), and that they will need to find another wet nurse for his milk brother. Sam recommends goat milk in the meanwhile, as he has heard it's better for a baby than cow's milk. Before Gilly leaves for [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) with Sam and [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), she makes Jon promise to find the baby a good woman to be his wet nurse, so that he will grow up big and strong.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) In actuality, Jon has forced Gilly to secretly leave behind her son and take Mance's son, in order to protect him from [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre). For much of the journey to Oldtown, Gilly is overcome with grief at being separated from her child,(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Raffc15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Raffc26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) but eventually comes to love Dalla's child as much as she loves her own.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)
[Asha Greyjoy](wiki:Asha_Greyjoy) voyages from [Deepwood Motte](wiki:Deepwood_Motte) to [Harlaw](wiki:Harlaw) with her hostages, Lady [Sybelle Glover](wiki:Sybelle_Glover) and her children, [Gawen](wiki:Gawen_Glover) and the infant [Erena](wiki:Erena_Glover). Sybelle's milk dries up during the crossing, and they stop at the [Stony Shore](wiki:Stony_Shore) to search for a wet nurse for Erena; instead they find a goat. When Asha arrives at [Ten Towers](wiki:Ten_Towers), she asks her uncle, Lord [Rodrik Harlaw](wiki:Rodrik_Harlaw), if there is a nursing mother in the village, as Erena is not thriving.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
In [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) travels with a few trusted companions, including her milk brother [Garin](wiki:Garin_(orphan)). Arianne introduces Garin to Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), saying his mother was her wet nurse.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)
The late Lady [Lysa Arryn](wiki:Lysa_Arryn) used to breastfeed her son, young Lord [Robert](wiki:Robert_Arryn), who is prone to seizures.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Ragot34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) When the eight-year-old Robert has another seizure, Maester [Colemon](wiki:Colemon) describes the practice to the boy's stepfather, Petyr Baelish. Petyr mockingly asks if they should find Robert a wet nurse, and wean him when he marries, so he can move from his nurse's nipples to his wife's. Instead, Petyr suggests they use [sweetsleep](wiki:Sweetsleep) to calm Robert, and the maester hesitantly agrees.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Raffc23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-0)
[Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) travels to [Hayford](wiki:Hayford) and is introduced to Lady Ermesande, but when she starts to cry, the toddler is taken away to bed by her wet nurse.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

When [Daario Naharis](wiki:Daario_Naharis) insults Prince [Quentyn Martell](wiki:Quentyn_Martell), his friend [Gerris Drinkwater](wiki:Gerris_Drinkwater) defends him, and Daario mockingly calls Gerris Quentyn's wet nurse.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_49-0)
Lords [Torghen Flint](wiki:Torghen_Flint) and [Brandon Norrey](wiki:Brandon_Norrey) arrive at Castle Black, each bringing a wet nurse. The Norrey nurse is forty years old, and has extremely large breasts. The Flint nurse is fourteen years old and flat-chested, but has plenty of milk. With his two wet nurses, [Gilly's son](wiki:Monster) thrives.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_50-0) After Princess [Shireen Baratheon](wiki:Shireen_Baratheon) arrives at Castle Black, [Val](wiki:Val) insists that neither the baby, who she calls "little monster", nor his nurses should stay in the same tower as the girl, a survivor of [greyscale](wiki:Greyscale).(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-0) [Leathers](wiki:Leathers) thinks [Toregg](wiki:Toregg) has taken a liking to one of Monster's wet nurses, but Jon thinks Toregg prefers Val.(wiki:Wet_nurse.cite_ref-Radwd69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0)
