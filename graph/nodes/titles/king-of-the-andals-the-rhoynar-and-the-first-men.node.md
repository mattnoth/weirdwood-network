## Origins

Originally crowned as [King of All Westeros and Shield of His People](wiki:King_of_All_Westeros,_Shield_of_His_People) by his sister-wife [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen), [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) abandoned these titles when he was crowned by the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon's_Conquest)) at the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept) of [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) as "King of the [Andals](wiki:Andals), the [Rhoynar](wiki:Rhoynar), and the [First Men](wiki:First_Men), [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms), and [Protector of the Realm](wiki:Protector_of_the_Realm)". This title indicated that Aegon claimed authority over even the [Dornish](wiki:Dornishmen), despite their successful resistance to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) in [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest)(wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War).(wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

At the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) crowned his wife, [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), as Queen of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men, in opposition to Rhaenyra's half-brother, [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen).(wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men.cite_ref-Rfab13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) The civil war ended with Rhaenyra and Aegon II both dead and Rhaenyra's son, [Aegon III](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), sitting the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf47.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) dies and is succeeded by his son [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon),(wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) whose biological father is actually Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister).

### A Clash of Kings

The [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) is disputed by Joffrey and his uncles, [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) and [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings).(wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men.cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Storm of Swords

Joffrey is murdered at his [royal wedding](wiki:Purple_Wedding)(wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) and is succeeded by his younger brother, [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### A Feast for Crows

[Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) plots to crown Tommen's elder sister, Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), as Queen of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men,(wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) but her plot is foiled by her father [Doran](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne).(wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

The Queen of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), claims to be Queen of the Andals and the Rhoynar and the First Men.(wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

In [Volon Therys](wiki:Volon_Therys), [Jon Connington](wiki:Jon_Connington) introduces [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(Young_Griff)) as Aegon, the Sixth of His Name, King of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men.(wiki:King_of_the_Andals,_the_Rhoynar,_and_the_First_Men.cite_ref-Radwd24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
