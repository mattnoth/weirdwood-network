## Origins

### Targaryen Era[ ]

At the start of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen)'s [Wars of Conquest](wiki:Wars_of_Conquest), [Aegon the Conqueror](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) secured the mouth of the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush) with [Aegon's Landing](wiki:Aegon's_Landing). The newly-crowned king gave the title of [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships) to his loyal supporter, Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon), granting him command over the new royal fleet. Daemon and the Targaryen fleet were defeated by [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) in a [battle in the waters off Gulltown](wiki:Battle_in_the_waters_off_Gulltown), however.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) In [2 AC](wiki:2_AC) Aegon I was supported by a vast fleet when he subdued the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_greyjoys_of_pyke.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
Following the Targaryen conquest, [House Velaryon](wiki:House_Velaryon) often provided [the bulk](wiki:Velaryon_fleet) of the royal fleet and the House's lords almost always served as the masters of ships and lords admiral.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) During the reign of King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), another [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)) turned against the king in support of [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) in [48 AC](wiki:48_AC).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) sided with [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) against [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) at the start of the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), giving Rhaenyra naval control of [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay). However, this control was eventually broken with the [Battle of the Gullet](wiki:Battle_of_the_Gullet) in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rtpatq.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
During the [conquest of Dorne](wiki:Conquest_of_Dorne), Lord [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon) led the royal fleet in breaking the [Planky Town](wiki:Planky_Town) and sailing up the [Greenblood](wiki:Greenblood) in support of [Daeron I Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) In [174 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) King [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) expanded the royal fleet in preparation for another invasion of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne), but it scattered en route.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iv.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
Lord [Dagon Greyjoy](wiki:Dagon_Greyjoy) led the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) in raiding the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea) early in the reign of King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen). Rather than sending the royal fleet to the west coast of Westeros to deal with the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn), Lord [Bloodraven](wiki:Brynden_Rivers) kept the king's ships at Blackwater Bay to protect against [Bittersteel](wiki:Aegor_Rivers) and the [Blackfyre Pretenders](wiki:Blackfyre_Pretenders), who were across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea) in [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rtss.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) desired the largest fleet in the world in order to conquer [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), but, like most of his schemes, nothing came of it.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aerys_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
Royal fleets have sometimes been sent to subdue pirates from the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_the_quarrelsome_daughters:_myr.2C_lys.2C_and_tyrosh.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

### Baratheon Era[ ]

Lords [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn) and [Robert Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) [captured Gulltown](wiki:Taking_of_Gulltown) at the start of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). Robert then evaded the royal fleet of [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) during his voyage to [Storm's End](wiki:Storm's_End).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiafrobert.27s_rebellion.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Although the Targaryen dynasty fell with the death of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen) during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing), the pregnant Queen [Rhaella Targaryen](wiki:Rhaella_Targaryen) and Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) were protected at [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
Robert, the new king after the Sack, tasked his younger brother [Stannis](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) with constructing a new fleet to challenge Dragonstone.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Racokprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) While Rhaella was giving birth to [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), a fierce storm destroyed the Targyaren fleet at anchor, with winds ripping [gargoyles](wiki:Gargoyle) from the walls of Dragonstone onto the ships below. Ser [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry) eventually fled with Viserys and the infant Daenerys into exile in the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-1) Stannis, commanding from [Fury](wiki:Fury), led Robert's newly-built fleet in the [assault on Dragonstone](wiki:Assault_on_Dragonstone).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
During [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion), the royal fleet of [House Baratheon of King's Landing](wiki:House_Baratheon_of_King's_Landing) was joined by the [Redwyne fleet](wiki:Redwyne_fleet) of the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor) and other ships from the [Reach](wiki:Reach). The [Iron Fleet](wiki:Iron_Fleet) of [House Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy) was then defeated by Stannis in a [sea battle off Fair Isle](wiki:Sea_battle_off_Fair_Isle).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Lord [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon), the [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships) for King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), sails to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) after Robert decides to offer the position of [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) to Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Ragot20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Stannis is reported to be building new ships at Dragonstone.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

The royal fleet is divided during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), with many captains supporting Stannis at Dragonstone and others remaining loyal to his nephew, King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing). When Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) sails from the capital aboard [Seaswift](wiki:Seaswift), her escort includes [King Robert's Hammer](wiki:King_Robert's_Hammer), [Lionstar](wiki:Lionstar), and [Lady Lyanna](wiki:Lady_Lyanna).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
Stannis gains the support of many [storm lords](wiki:Stormlands) after the death of his younger brother, [Renly Baratheon](wiki:Renly_Baratheon), and the [capture of Storm's End](wiki:Siege_of_Storm's_End_(299)).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Racok42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) The Baratheon fleet sails from Storm's End north around [Massey's Hook](wiki:Massey's_Hook) to [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)
Lord Captain [Imry Florent](wiki:Imry_Florent) commands Stannis's fleet during the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater). The fleet—which also include the lords of the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea), [Myrish](wiki:Myr) sellsails, and [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan)'s pirates—is divided into ten lines of twenty ships, and vary from war galleys to sailing ships, carracks, and cogs. In contrast, Joffrey's King's Landing fleet is only a quarter the size.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2)
At the start of the battle, the fleets of the rival kings meet in the [Blackwater Rush](wiki:Blackwater_Rush). The defense of King's Landing, designed by [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), traps most of Stannis's fleet with a great chain blocking retreat to Blackwater Bay. [Wildfire](wiki:Wildfire) destroys most ships in the river, regardless of allegiance.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Racok58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3)
Following the battle, naval officers in Joffrey's service who survived are honored by the Iron Throne.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Traffic in the Blackwater Rush is greatly reduced by wreckage from the royal fleets.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rasos16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
The once-great fleet of Stannis has been reduced to the [Lysene](wiki:Lys) ships of [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Most ships which survived the wildfire inferno either surrendered to Joffrey's forces or were scuttled by their captains after sailing upriver.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rasos10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) Stannis sails the remnants of his forces from Dragonstone to [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea), after which they ride to [protect the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall) from [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) and his wildlings.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

There are less than a dozen warships left in King's Landing. Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) approves the building of [ten great dromonds](wiki:Cersei's_dromonds) under the command of Grand Admiral [Aurane Waters](wiki:Aurane_Waters).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) With the royal fleet greatly weakened, the [Redwyne fleet](wiki:Redwyne_fleet) is instead used during the [siege of Dragonstone](wiki:Siege_of_Dragonstone).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Raffc16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)
Lord Waters absconds from King's Landing with the new dromonds after the arrest of Cersei by the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven). Ser [Harys Swyft](wiki:Harys_Swyft) worries that Aurane intends to join Stannis, while Grand Maester [Pycelle](wiki:Pycelle) believes the grand admiral is sailing for the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Raffc43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Stannis tasks [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan) with bringing [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) from Eastwatch to [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor). When the twenty-nine ships are scattered by storms, however, Salladhor abandons Stannis, leaving the king without a fleet.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Radwd9.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)
[Lionstar](wiki:Lionstar) transports [Jared](wiki:Jared_Frey), [Rhaegar](wiki:Rhaegar_Frey), and [Symond](wiki:Symond_Frey) of [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey) to White Harbor as envoys of the new king in King's Landing, [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon).(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)

### The Winds of Winter[ ]

A pirate king calling himself the [Lord of the Waters](wiki:Lord_of_the_Waters) controls [Torturer's Deep](wiki:Torturer's_Deep) with three-decked warships.(wiki:Royal_fleet.cite_ref-34)
