## Culture

The Marcher lords have proudly defended the realms of the [Storm Kings](wiki:Storm_Kings) and [Kings of the Reach](wiki:Kings_of_the_Reach) against incursions from [Dorne](wiki:Dorne).(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Many ballads and tales praise the valor and skill at arms of the Marcher lords and their longbowmen.(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
[House Caron](wiki:House_Caron) uses the title [Lord of the Marches](wiki:Lord_of_the_Marches), although they do not hold dominion over the other marcher houses of the stormlands. The Carons and the [Swanns](wiki:House_Swann) both claim to be the oldest of the marcher lords.(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_stormlands:_the_men_of_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2)

## Origins

In [10 AC](wiki:10_AC) during the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), Lord [Fowler](wiki:House_Fowler) surprised the unsuspecting marcher lords by quickly raiding the [Reach](wiki:Reach) and capturing [Nightsong](wiki:Nightsong). Ser [Addam Hightower](wiki:Addam_Hightower) responded by marching from [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) to retake Nightsong, but while he was gone Ser [Joffrey Dayne](wiki:Joffrey_Dayne) burned the environs of Oldtown. The Fowlers had meanwhile burned Nightsong and fled into the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) with captive [Carons](wiki:House_Caron). Addam returned to Oldtown to confront Joffrey, but the [Daynes](wiki:House_Dayne) had by then also fled back to the mountains of [Dorne](wiki:Dorne).(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
After ascending the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne) in [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen) visited the marcher lords in their castles during his journey to Oldtown. When [Dornishmen](wiki:Dornishmen) began raiding the Marches in the [Second Dornish War](wiki:Second_Dornish_War), [Orys Baratheon](wiki:Orys_Baratheon), [Lord Paramount of the Stormlands](wiki:Lord_Paramount_of_the_Stormlands), responded by smashing Lord [Walter Wyl](wiki:Walter_Wyl) at [Stonehelm](wiki:Stonehelm). Nightsong resisted a siege by the [Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Aenys_I)), and the bandit leader's host was then destroyed by Lady [Ellyn Caron](wiki:Ellyn_Caron), Lord [Harmon Dondarrion](wiki:Harmon_Dondarrion), and Lord [Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly_(lord)) in the [Vulture Hunt](wiki:Vulture_Hunt).(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
Lords [Dondarrion](wiki:House_Dondarrion), [Selmy](wiki:House_Selmy), and [Tarly](wiki:House_Tarly) represented the Dornish Marches at the [Golden Wedding](wiki:Golden_Wedding) in [49 AC](wiki:49_AC).(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Rfabthe_year_of_the_three_brides_-_49_ac.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) visited [Horn Hill](wiki:Horn_Hill), Nightsong, and [Blackhaven](wiki:Blackhaven) in [54 AC](wiki:54_AC) after the elevation of [Alfyn](wiki:Alfyn) to [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon).(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Rfabbirth.2C_death.2C_and_betrayal_under_king_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) In [61 AC](wiki:61_AC) during the [Third Dornish War](wiki:Third_Dornish_War), Lord [Simon Dondarrion](wiki:Simon_Dondarrion) led a small force of marcher knights into the western Red Mountains against a [second Vulture King](wiki:Vulture_King_(Jaehaerys_I)), and Jaehaerys and Lord [Rogar Baratheon](wiki:Rogar_Baratheon) pursued the outlaws from the east.(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
Marcher lords attended the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC) at [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Rfabheirs_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) was slain fighting a rebellious lord on the Dornish Marches(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) during the [Peake Uprising](wiki:Peake_Uprising).(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), arranges a betrothal between his niece, Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), and Prince [Trystane Martell](wiki:Trystane_Martell) during the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings). Trystane's father [Doran](wiki:Doran_Martell), [Prince of Dorne](wiki:Prince_of_Dorne), fortifies the high passes of the [Red Mountains](wiki:Red_Mountains) to prevent the Marcher lords from aiding King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
Ser [Philip Foote](wiki:Philip_Foote) kills the last [Lord of Nightsong](wiki:Lord_of_Nightsong), Lord [Bryce Caron](wiki:Bryce_Caron), in single combat during the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater). He is awarded the lands, rights and incomes of [House Caron](wiki:House_Caron) as a reward by the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), founding [House Foote of Nightsong](wiki:House_Foote_of_Nightsong).(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Angry at the death of her father, [Oberyn Martell](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), [Obara Sand](wiki:Obara_Sand) wants to lead an army from the [Prince's Pass](wiki:Prince's_Pass) against the marcher lords and then [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown).(wiki:Marcher_lords.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
