## Origins

The [Eyrie](wiki:Eyrie) had been the seat of the kings of [House Arryn](wiki:House_Arryn) since its construction. During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), Queen Regent [Sharra Arryn](wiki:Sharra_Arryn) and her son, King [Ronnel Arryn](wiki:Ronnel_Arryn_(king)), took refuge in the Eyrie, believing themselves to be safe. However, after Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) flew to the Eyrie's inner courtyard upon her [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar), Sharra peacefully submitted to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) and the new [Lord of the Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms), [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)

The Lords of the Eyrie have been loyal to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) throughout most of its Dynasty. Lord Rodrik Arryn was married to Princess [Daella Targaryen](wiki:Daella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Jaehaerys_I)), while Lady [Jeyne Arryn](wiki:Jeyne_Arryn) supported the claim of her cousin, [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), and later served as one of the seven regents during the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III).(wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)

The Arryns sided with House Targaryen during the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion).(wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) During the reign of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), however, the loyalty of the Arryn's shifted. When Aerys II Targaryen demanded the heads of Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn)'s two former wards, Lord Jon called his banners instead, signalling the start of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion),(wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) which ended the Targaryen Dynasty. Following the coronation of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), Jon served as his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_vale:_house_arryn.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
