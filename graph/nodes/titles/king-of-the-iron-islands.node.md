## Origins

### Origin[ ]

In antiquity each of the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) was ruled by two petty kings, a [rock king](wiki:Rock_king) who ruled the land and a [salt king](wiki:Salt_king) who led that island's ships, with each chosen by the island's captains through a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) When [Galon Whitestaff](wiki:Galon_Whitestaff) called all of the minor kings and captains to a kingsmoot at [Nagga's Hill](wiki:Nagga's_Hill) on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk), [Urras Greyiron](wiki:Urras_Greyiron) was chosen to rule all of the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) as the first [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
After centuries of elected rulers, the kingsmoot tradition ended thousands of years ago after the controversial successions of several High Kings. [Urrathon IV Goodbrother](wiki:Urrathon_IV_Goodbrother) was chosen to succeed King [Urragon III Greyiron](wiki:Urragon_III_Greyiron) while Urragon's eldest son [Torgon](wiki:Torgon_Greyiron) was away raiding. When Torgon the Latecomer returned to the isles, the cruel Urrathon was overthrown and Torgon became High King without having been chosen in a kingsmoot. King Torgon was succeeded by his own son and advisor, [Urragon IV Greyiron](wiki:Urragon_IV_Greyiron), who was also chosen without a kingsmoot. [Priests of the Drowned God](wiki:Priests_of_the_Drowned_God) then insisted that a kingsmoot be held to choose Urragon's successor. [Urras Greyiron](wiki:Urras_Greyiron), Urragon's great-nephew and preferred successor, let loose his axemen during the meeting, with numerous petty kings and priests slaughtered at Old Wyk.(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_driftwood_crowns.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

### House Greyiron[ ]

Rather than as [High King of the Iron Islands](wiki:High_King_of_the_Iron_Islands), [Urras Redhand](wiki:Urras_Greyiron) styled himself simply King of the Iron Islands and made the throne hereditary within [House Greyiron](wiki:House_Greyiron). The [choosing](wiki:Choosing) of [salt](wiki:Salt_king) and [rock kings](wiki:Rock_king) in local [kingsmoots](wiki:Kingsmoot) ended, with the petty kings reduced to vassal [lords](wiki:Lord). Rather than the personalized crown of driftwood worn by each High King, the hereditary kings donned a crown of black [iron](wiki:Iron).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
The Greyirons ruled the Iron Islands for a thousand years,(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-date_8-0) during which they faced six rebellions from rival ironborn and two uprisings by [thralls](wiki:Thrall). The ironmen also lost control of their remaining conquests from the [green lands](wiki:Green_lands), including the [Shield Islands](wiki:Shield_Islands).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
The Greyirons eventually fell during the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to the islands.(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Houses [Drumm](wiki:House_Drumm), [Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare), [Greyjoy](wiki:House_Greyjoy), and [Orkwood](wiki:House_Orkwood), supported by [Andal](wiki:Andal) warriors, all helped to overthrow the last Greyiron king, [Rognar II](wiki:Rognar_II_Greyiron).

### House Hoare[ ]

The ironborn are said to have played the [finger dance](wiki:Finger_dance) to choose their next king, with [Harras Hoare](wiki:Harras_Hoare) emerging as the victor. Archmaester [Haereg](wiki:Haereg), however, believes that Harras Stump-hand, who had married an Andal maiden, became king with the support of her family.(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_iron_kings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3)
Kings from House Hoare allowed [septons](wiki:Septon) and [septas](wiki:Septa) to preach the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) and build [septs](wiki:Sept) in the Iron Islands, which outraged [priests of the Drowned God](wiki:Priests_of_the_Drowned_God). Hoare kings also encouraged the trading of [iron](wiki:Iron) ore for resources lacking in the archipelago, which angered proponents of the [iron price](wiki:Iron_price).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
A priest known as the [Shrike](wiki:Shrike) led an uprising against King [Harmund III Hoare](wiki:Harmund_III_Hoare), and the fallen king's younger brother, King [Hagon](wiki:Hagon_Hoare), expelled the Faith from the islands. When the brothers' mother, Queen [Lelia Lannister](wiki:Lelia_Lannister), was mutilated and sent back to [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), her nephew, the furious [King of the Rock](wiki:King_of_the_Rock), called the banners of the [westerlands](wiki:Westerlands) against the ironmen. Ser [Aubrey Crakehall](wiki:Aubrey_Crakehall), leader of the westermen, eventually defeated Hagon and conquered [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk) after seven years of war. Instead of returning to the green lands, Aubrey declared himself king and remained in the isles. Lacking the support of [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock) and opposed by his would-be subjects, Aubrey was eventually drowned by the Shrike.(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)
Centuries later, ironborn power began to be restored as daring traders ventured to Lannisport, [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), and the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities). Deterred from raiding the powerful kingdoms of the green lands, ironborn went reaving instead in the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), the [Basilisk Isles](wiki:Basilisk_Isles), and the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_black_blood.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)

### Recent History[ ]

King [Harwyn Hoare](wiki:Harwyn_Hoare) extended ironborn rule over the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) by taking the [Trident](wiki:Trident) from a [Storm King](wiki:Storm_King), [Arrec Durrandon](wiki:Arrec_Durrandon).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) Rather than as Kings of the Iron Islands, the last three Hoares ruled as [Kings of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:Kings_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers) until the deaths of King [Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare) and his family in the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal) during [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
The destruction of the Hoares led to chaos in the Iron Islands. [Qhorin Volmark](wiki:Qhorin_Volmark) of [Harlaw](wiki:Harlaw) claimed the kingship through his blood ties to House Hoare, [priests of the Drowned God](wiki:Priests_of_the_Drowned_God) crowned [Lodos](wiki:Lodos) on [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk), and other claimants rose on [Great Wyk](wiki:Great_Wyk), [Orkmont](wiki:Orkmont), and [Pyke](wiki:Pyke_(island)). King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) killed Qhorin and defeated the other claimants during his [invasion of the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands), and Lodos walked into the sea with his followers. Aegon allowed the ironborn to choose [Vickon Greyjoy](wiki:Vickon_Greyjoy) as his new [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rfabreign_of_the_dragon_-_the_wars_of_king_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), [Lodos the Twice-Drowned](wiki:Lodos_the_Twice-Drowned) rebelled against King [Aenys I Targaryen](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen). Lord [Goren Greyjoy](wiki:Goren_Greyjoy) defeated Lodos's followers and sent the head of the would-be priest-king to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) as a gift to Aenys.(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
In [289 AC](wiki:289_AC), Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) rebelled against King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) and declared himself King of the Iron Islands.(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_iron_islands:_the_old_way_and_the_new.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) Rather than an iron crown, [Tarle the Thrice-Drowned](wiki:Tarle_the_Thrice-Drowned) placed a driftwood crown on Balon's head at [Nagga's hill](wiki:Nagga's_hill).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) [Greyjoy's Rebellion](wiki:Greyjoy's_Rebellion) was defeated by Robert, however, and the ironborn were again made subjects of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), Lord [Balon Greyjoy](wiki:Balon_Greyjoy) again crowns himself King of the Iron Islands.(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Racok46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) Sending his [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) reavers to attack the [north](wiki:North), he then declares himself [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

King Balon is reported to have fallen to his death at [Pyke](wiki:Pyke) during a storm.(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) His exiled younger brother, [Euron Crow's Eye](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy), returns to the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands) and claims the [Seastone Chair](wiki:Seastone_Chair).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Rasos45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Balon's youngest surviving brother, [Aeron Greyjoy](wiki:Aeron_Greyjoy), opposes Euron's actions and calls for a [kingsmoot](wiki:Kingsmoot) to select Balon's successor.(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Against Aeron's intentions, Euron is chosen by the lords and captains who gather at [Nagga's hill](wiki:Nagga's_hill) for the [kingsmoot on Old Wyk](wiki:Kingsmoot_on_Old_Wyk). Euron intends to conquer all of [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) with the [dragons](wiki:Dragon) of [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Although most of the ironborn have withdrawn from the north, Euron continues to style himself [King of the Isles and the North](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_North).(wiki:King_of_the_Iron_Islands.cite_ref-Radwdappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)
