## Culture

Begging brothers often wear robes of undyed wool belted with hempen ropes.(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) While [septons](wiki:Septon) wear a crystal about their neck on a leather thong, begging brothers instead have a bowl.(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Origins

Begging brothers spread word of the liberation of the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands) from [House Durrandon](wiki:House_Durrandon) after the [battle at Fairmarket](wiki:Battle_at_Fairmarket), but [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare) then claimed the region for themselves.(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
The [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) were similar to begging brothers, but they wielded axes instead of bowls.(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) The Poor Fellows and their knightly counterparts, the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons), were outlawed by King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) during the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising).(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
When a begging brother preached against [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre) residing at [Fair Isle](wiki:Fair_Isle), Lord [Marq Farman](wiki:Marq_Farman) had him imprisoned in the dungeon of [Faircastle](wiki:Faircastle), where the preacher eventually died.(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Humble begging brothers opposed [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen)'s practice of [polygamy](wiki:Polygamy),(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1) so the [Seven Speakers](wiki:Seven_Speakers) espoused the [Doctrine of Exceptionalism](wiki:Doctrine_of_Exceptionalism) to change their minds.(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), the [Shepherd](wiki:Shepherd) was a begging brother who preached against Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

At [Cobbler's Square](wiki:Cobbler's_Square) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) sees a begging brother prophesizing about the [red comet](wiki:Red_comet).(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) When one prophet preaches that Ser Jaime [Lannister](wiki:Lannister) had murdered the rightful king, [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) has [Vylarr](wiki:Vylarr) take six prophets to the dungeons.(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys) disguises himself as a begging brother when coming to see Tyrion at [Shae's manse](wiki:Shae's_manse).(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Racok44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

During her search for [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) passes groups of begging brothers on the [Rosby road](wiki:Rosby_road), with some having joined the [sparrows](wiki:Sparrows).(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
Brothers with begging bowls pray at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor) while Ser Jaime holds a vigil for his father, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:Begging_brother.cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
