## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows

During the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), [septs](wiki:Sept) are despoiled and men and women sworn to the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) are killed, mutilated, and injured. While traveling to [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale), [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) encounters self-proclaimed sparrows and [begging brothers](wiki:Begging_brothers) who are marching to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with the bones of martyrs, hoping to lay them before King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) and plead for protection.(wiki:Sparrows.cite_ref-Raffc4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Sparrows.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)

Ser [Harys Swyft](wiki:Harys_Swyft) warns the [small council](wiki:Small_council) that there are already two thousand sparrows in the capital, with the number rising each day. These faithful are especially furious about the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Sparrows.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) observes two sparrows preaching to a crowd of hundreds of [smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) at [Cobbler's Square](wiki:Cobbler's_Square).(wiki:Sparrows.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Septon [Ollidor](wiki:Ollidor), a candidate to become the new [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon), is disgraced when sparrows drag him from a brothel.(wiki:Sparrows.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Sparrows instead carry one of their leaders, a small septon, into the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor).(wiki:Sparrows.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) The fool [Moon Boy](wiki:Moon_Boy) japes that this new High Septon is the "[High Sparrow](wiki:High_Sparrow)".(wiki:Sparrows.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) observes hundreds of sparrows in the plaza and gardens of the Great Sept, and bones and skulls are piled about the statue of King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Sparrows.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) Cersei, at the behest of the High Sparrow, overturns King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen)'s [law](wiki:King_Maegor's_laws) that banned holy men from taking up arms in return for forgiving debt. Sparrows join the reborn [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows), one of the two branches of the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant), en masse.(wiki:Sparrows.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3)(wiki:Sparrows.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

Jaime observes sparrows and [Poor Fellows](wiki:Poor_Fellows) at [Darry](wiki:Darry), the new seat of his cousin, Lord [Lancel Lannister](wiki:Lancel_Lannister). Lancel decides to join the [Warrior's Sons](wiki:Warrior's_Sons).(wiki:Sparrows.cite_ref-Raffc30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)

Some orphans are brought to the [crossroads inn](wiki:Crossroads_inn) by sparrows so they can be protected by the [brotherhood without banners](wiki:Brotherhood_without_banners).(wiki:Sparrows.cite_ref-Raffc37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
