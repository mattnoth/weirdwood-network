## Origins

King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) visited Tarbeck Hall in [55 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Tarbeck_Hall.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
Although of ancient linage, [House Tarbeck](wiki:House_Tarbeck) was impoverished by the time of [Gerold Lannister](wiki:Gerold_Lannister), Lord of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock). After the marriage of Lord [Walderan Tarbeck](wiki:Walderan_Tarbeck) to [Ellyn Reyne](wiki:Ellyn_Reyne), however, the Tarbecks' fortunes began to improve. Ellyn used [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) gold to rebuild the castle's curtain wall, strengthen its towers, and decorate its keep in splendor.(wiki:Tarbeck_Hall.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
According to a semi-canon source,(wiki:Tarbeck_Hall.cite_ref-sample_5-0) Lord [Denys Marbrand](wiki:Denys_Marbrand) was sent by Lord [Tytos Lannister](wiki:Tytos_Lannister) ca. [255 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) to deal with the truculent Walderan, whose claiming of land angered King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen). [Roger Reyne](wiki:Roger_Reyne), [Lord of Castamere](wiki:Lord_of_Castamere) and Walderan's ally, slew Denys before he reached Tarbeck Hall, however, and Ser [Reynard Reyne](wiki:Reynard_Reyne) convinced Tytos to make peace.
During the [Reyne-Tarbeck rebellion](wiki:Reyne-Tarbeck_rebellion) against House Lannister, Walderan and most of his kin were killed in the field by Ser [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the heir of Lord Tytos. Rather than starve out Tarbeck Hall, Tywin used siege engines to destroy its keep. Tywin smiled(wiki:Tarbeck_Hall.cite_ref-Raffc33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) when it collapsed on Ellyn and her son, [Tion the Red](wiki:Tion_Tarbeck), and he ordered the castle put to the torch. Tywin then defeated Roger's army from [Castamere](wiki:Castamere) when it arrived at the ruins of Tarbeck Hall. The ruins have remained vacant since the rebellion.(wiki:Tarbeck_Hall.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_house_lannister_under_the_dragons.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
