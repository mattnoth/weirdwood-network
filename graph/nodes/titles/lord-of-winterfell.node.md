## Origins

Prior to [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), the Lords of [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) styled themselves as [Kings in the North](wiki:Kings_in_the_North).(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) Members of [House Stark](wiki:House_Stark) are interred in the [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell), with statues only being made for the lords and kings by tradition.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) An iron longsword placed on each statue is said to keep [vengeful spirits](wiki:Ghosts) in the crypt.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

King [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark) peacefully yielded to King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) during the Conquest. After Torrhen gave up his crown, Aegon named him Lord of Winterfell and [Warden of the North](wiki:Warden_of_the_North).(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

Relations between Houses [Stark](wiki:House_Stark) and [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) were troublesome during the first century of the Targaryen dynasty. At the behest of Queen [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen), Lord Torrhen married his daughter to Lord [Ronnel Arryn](wiki:Ronnel_Arryn_(king)), though after much protest. His sons even spoke of rebelling.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) and Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) forced Lord [Alaric Stark](wiki:Alaric_Stark) to donate land to the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch), which was renamed the [New Gift](wiki:New_Gift), leaving the Starks bitter.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rfabjaehaerys_and_alysanne_-_their_triumphs_and_tragedies.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) According to Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), this might have been the reason why Lord [Ellard Stark](wiki:Ellard_Stark) supported the claim of [Laenor Velaryon](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon) over the claim of Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) during the [Great Council of 101 AC](wiki:Great_Council_of_101_AC).(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark) agreed to support [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) with the [Pact of Ice and Fire](wiki:Pact_of_Ice_and_Fire).(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_lords_of_winterfell.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Cregan delayed giving aid, however,(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) and by the time he led his army south King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) had been poisoned and Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon) had already send out ravens suing for peace, preventing Cregan from punishing the lords who had supported Aegon II. While the court awaited the replies, Cregan held sway and arrested all those involved in Aegon II's death, cowing the young Prince Aegon the Younger, soon-to-be crowned King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), into naming him [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King). Cregan served only a single day in that office, a time known as the [Hour of the Wolf](wiki:Hour_of_the_Wolf), and held trials for twenty-one out of twenty-two of his prisoners. The only one spared a trial was Lord Corlys; Aegon pardoned him and restored his titles to him, and Cregan agreed to allow it when [Aly Blackwood](wiki:Aly_Blackwood) offered him her hand in marriage. Cregan returned north the next day.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

In the past century, several of the Lords of Winterfell died in battle. Lord [Barthogan Stark](wiki:Barthogan_Stark) died in the [Skagosi](wiki:Skagos) rebellion during the reign of [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen),(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_north:_the_stoneborn_of_skagos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) and Lord [Beron Stark](wiki:Beron_Stark)(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) was injured after facing the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) of [Dagon Greyjoy](wiki:Dagon_Greyjoy), [Lord of the Iron Islands](wiki:Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands), early in the reign of [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen). As he slowly succumbed to his wounds, his wife and several recent Stark widows struggled over the succession, as there were multiple potential heirs.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-boskone1_11-0)(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-boskone2_12-0) Lord [Willam Stark](wiki:Willam_Stark) was killed by [Raymun Redbeard](wiki:Raymun_Redbeard), the [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall), in [226 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_wall_and_beyond:_the_wildlings.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

In [282 AC](wiki:282_AC), Lord [Rickard Stark](wiki:Rickard_Stark) was killed alongside his heir, [Brandon](wiki:Brandon_Stark), by King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), which sparked the outbreak of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), in which Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) was one of the leaders.

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones

After the death of [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), [Lord of the Eyrie](wiki:Lord_of_the_Eyrie), King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) names Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) as his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) After Robert dies, Eddard is arrested for treason(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) and later executed.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark) now the Lord of Winterfell, marches south with an army.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) After the [Battle of the Camps](wiki:Battle_of_the_Camps), Robb is declared [King in the North](wiki:King_in_the_North) by [northmen](wiki:Northmen) and [rivermen](wiki:Rivermen) gathered at [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun).(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Ragot71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Storm of Swords

[Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), [Lord of Casterly Rock](wiki:Lord_of_Casterly_Rock), marries his son [Tyrion](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) to Robb's sister, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), so they can use Sansa's claim on Winterfell to claim the north when Tyrion fathers a son on her.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

King Robb is murdered in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding).(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) offers to legitimize Eddard's bastard son, [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), and name him Lord of Winterfell.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) Stannis plans to marry [Val](wiki:Val) to his new Lord of Winterfell.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos76.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1) Jon considers the offer, but realizes he cannot forsake his father's [gods](wiki:Old_gods), which is one of the conditions of becoming Lord of Winterfell. Jon is elected as [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch) not long after.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Rasos79.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

### A Dance with Dragons

Stannis offers once more to make Jon the Lord of Winterfell, but Jon refuses, insisting that Winterfell belongs to his half sister, Sansa. Stannis considers granting Winterfell to [Arnolf Karstark](wiki:Arnolf_Karstark) instead.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)

[Ramsay Bolton](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) marries "[Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark)" (actually [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)),(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) through which he becomes the Lord of Winterfell,(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) although he has already used the title prior to the wedding.(wiki:Lord_of_Winterfell.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
