## Origins

At the outbreak of the [Faith Militant uprising](wiki:Faith_Militant_uprising) in [41 AC](wiki:41_AC), the Grand Captain of the Warrior's Sons was Ser [Damon Morrigen](wiki:Damon_Morrigen), leader of the [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) chapter of the order. He famously agreed to a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven) with the newly proclaimed King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen). Only Maegor survived the trial, although he was badly injured.(wiki:Grand_Captain.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0)
Subsequently, in [43 AC](wiki:43_AC) the Warrior's Sons chose a new Grand Captain to replace the deceased Ser Damon: Ser [Joffrey Doggett](wiki:Joffrey_Doggett), leader of the [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport) chapter of the Warrior's Sons was elected. Ser Joffrey commanded the surviving Warrior's Sons throughout the uprising and remained a major thorn in Maegor's side.(wiki:Grand_Captain.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
After Maegor's death, his successor [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) brought peace by negotiating with the [faith](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) to disband the [Faith Militant](wiki:Faith_Militant) - by extension ending the office of Grand Captain. Ser Joffrey had survived the war, but Jaehaerys then personally reconciled with him by appointing Ser Joffrey to his new [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard).(wiki:Grand_Captain.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
