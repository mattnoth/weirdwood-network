## Origins

Lord [Daemon Velaryon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon) of [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) was named the first [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships) by King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) at the start of [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest). He died during the war in the [battle in the waters off Gulltown](wiki:Battle_in_the_waters_off_Gulltown).(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabaegon.27s_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) Daemon's son [Aethan](wiki:Aethan_Velaryon) succeeded him as Lord of the Tides, and eventually also became master of ships to King Aegon I.(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) Aethan's son [Daemon](wiki:Daemon_Velaryon_(son_of_Aethan)) succeeded his father to both positions, serving under King [Maegor I](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen) and [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_maegor_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
By [90 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), Daemon's grandson, [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), had become the Lord of the Tides. That year, he married Princess [Rhaenys Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aemon)), the daughter of Prince [Aemon Targaryen](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)), the heir to the Iron Throne, and the granddaughter of King Jaehaerys I.(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabthe_long_reign_-_jaehaerys_and_alysanne:_policy.2C_progeny.2C_and_pain.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Like his ancestors, Corlys served as admiral of the royal fleet. In [92 AC](wiki:92_AC), when King Jaehaerys named Prince [Baelon Targaryen](wiki:Baelon_Targaryen_(son_of_Jaehaerys_I)) as his new heir following Aemon's death instead of Aemon's daughter Rhaenys, Corlys furiously gave up his admiralty. After Baelon died in [101 AC](wiki:101_AC), it was rumored that Corlys was massing ships and men on [Driftmark](wiki:Driftmark) to defend the rights of his son [Laenor](wiki:Laenor_Velaryon). Corlys used his wealth and status to gain support for Laenor during the [Great Council](wiki:Great_Council) of [101 AC](wiki:101_AC), but to no avail.(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabthe_heirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
Corlys's son Laenor married Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) in [114 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), and during their marriage Rhaenyra gave birth to three boys, although it was whispered that the children had been fathered by Ser [Harwin Strong](wiki:Harwin_Strong), and not Laenor Velaryon.(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabthe_heirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1) When Lord Corlys fell ill in [126 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-12) the succession of the Driftmark was called into question, as both his son and [daughter](wiki:Laena_Velaryon) had predeceased him. Law decreed that his eldest grandson, [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon), was his heir, but as Jacaerys already stood heir to the Iron Throne through his mother, Laenor's widow Rhaenyra urged Corlys to name her second son by Laenor, [Lucerys](wiki:Lucerys_Velaryon), as his heir instead. However, the eldest of Corlys's six cousins, Ser [Vaemond Velaryon](wiki:Vaemond_Velaryon), protested to this, insisting that Rhaenyra's three Velaryon sons had not been fathered by Laenor and were thus [bastards](wiki:Bastard). Instead, he believed he should be named Corlys's heir. Rhaenyra's new husband, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen), seized Ser Vaemond, beheaded him, and fed his body to the [dragon](wiki:Dragon) [Syrax](wiki:Syrax). Ser Vaemond's younger cousins, widow and sons then traveled to [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), where they laid their claim before King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen). After listening to their words, King Viserys had all their tongues removed, as per his earlier decree.(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rtrp.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabthe_heirs_of_the_dragon_-_a_question_of_succession.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)
During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) Lord Corlys fought for Rhaenyra Targaryen, who he served as [Hand of the Queen](wiki:Hand_of_the_Queen). He petitioned to Rhaenyra to have [Addam of Hull](wiki:Addam_of_Hull), supposedly the bastard son of Laenor, but according to [Mushroom](wiki:Mushroom) Corlys's own bastard son, legitimized.(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Corlys was eventually imprisoned by Rhaenyra,(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) and left behind in the dungeon when Rhaenyra fled the capital six months after her arrival. He was eventually released by [Trystane Truefyre](wiki:Trystane_Truefyre), who took over the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep).(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_overthrown.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) Corlys joined the side of King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) when the latter's forces took control of the capital again, and served Aegon II as [master of ships](wiki:Master_of_ships).(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1) Corlys was involved in the murder of Aegon II, and was arrested and tried for it by Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark), and sentenced to die. He was eventually pardoned by King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen),(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabthe_aftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) and served Aegon as a [regent](wiki:Regent) until his death in [132 AC](wiki:132_AC).(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0) Corlys was succeeded by his legitimized bastard grandson, [Alyn Velaryon](wiki:Alyn_Velaryon),(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1) who became a famous captain.(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_voyage_of_alyn_oakenfist.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Monford Velaryon](wiki:Monford_Velaryon), Lord of the Tides, is among the supporters of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Monterys Velaryon](wiki:Monterys_Velaryon) becomes the new Lord of the Tides after the death of his father, Monford, in the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater).(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)

### The Winds of Winter[ ]

A new pirate king stylizing himself the [Lord of the Waters](wiki:Lord_of_the_Waters), possibly the Velaryon bastard [Aurane Waters](wiki:Aurane_Waters), is reported to control [Torturer's Deep](wiki:Torturer's_Deep).(wiki:Lord_of_the_Tides.cite_ref-23)
