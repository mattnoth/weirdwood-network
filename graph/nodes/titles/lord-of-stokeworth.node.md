## Origins

Lord [Alyn Stokeworth](wiki:Alyn_Stokeworth) was named [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) by King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) in [34 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest). He was assigned the task over overseeing the construction of the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep) in [35 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) In [37 AC](wiki:37_AC), Lord Alyn was slain in battle by a bandit [outlaw](wiki:Outlaw) known as [Harren the Red](wiki:Harren_the_Red).(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rtsotd.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_targaryen_kings:_aenys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
During the reign of King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen), Lord Stokeworth was one of the few lords to remain loyal to Maegor near the end of his reign.(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_sons_of_the_dragon.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) After Maegor's death, he fled back to his castle.(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) He later traveled to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone), to speak with Maegor's successor, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen).(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfaba_surfeit_of_rulers.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
During the reign of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), [Lord Stokeworth](wiki:Lord_Stokeworth) swore to defend the succession rights of Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen). In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) in which Rhaenyra's brother [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) challenged her rule by claiming Viserys's throne for himself, Lord Stokeworth was arrested by Aegon II's supporters. When given the choice between swearing his allegiance to Aegon II or dying, Lord Stokeworth pledged his support to Aegon II.(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) When Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole) marched from [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to attack [Duskendale](wiki:Duskendale) and ultimately [Rook's Rest](wiki:Rook's_Rest), he passed by [Rosby](wiki:Rosby) and [Stokeworth](wiki:Stokeworth) to command both lords to prove their loyalty by joining his attack.(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_red_dragon_and_the_gold.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-1) When King's Landing [fell](wiki:Fall_of_King's_Landing) to Queen Rhaenyra during [130 AC](wiki:130_AC), Lord Stokeworth attempted to switch his allegiance again. Queen Rhaenyra refused him, however, and ordered his tongue removed, after which he was executed. Following Lord Stokeworth's death, Prince [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) suggested marrying Stokeworth's young daughter to [Ulf White](wiki:Ulf_White), to grant White the Stokeworth lands. Lord [Corlys Velaryon](wiki:Corlys_Velaryon), the [Hand of the Queen](wiki:Hand_of_the_Queen), argued instead in favor of Stokeworth's younger son. According to Lord Corlys, disinheriting the younger son in favor of the older daughters would call into question the rights of multiple other lords, who had elder sisters whose claims might suddenly be seen as superior. Unwilling to lose the support of such lords, according to Grand Maester [Munkun](wiki:Munkun), Rhaenyra eventually named Stokeworth's son as the new Lord of Stokeworth.(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_rhaenyra_triumphant.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
When Aegon II's forces retook King's Landing, Lord [Borros Baratheon](wiki:Borros_Baratheon) marched on Stokeworth, the young Lord of Stokeworth, through his steward and castellan, was brought to King's Landing in chain to swear obeisance before Aegon II, and was only allowed to leave after agreeing to pay a heavy ransom and provide suitable hostages to the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_short.2C_sad_reign_of_aegon_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Lady [Tanda Stokeworth](wiki:Tanda_Stokeworth), the reigning Lady of Stokeworth, resides at [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) with her two daughters, [Falyse](wiki:Falyse_Stokeworth) and [Lollys](wiki:Lollys_Stokeworth).(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Lady [Tanda Stokeworth](wiki:Tanda_Stokeworth) is present with her daughters when [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) departs for [Dorne](wiki:Dorne). During the [riot](wiki:Riot_of_King's_Landing) that breaks out as they return to the castle, her daughter [Lollys](wiki:Lollys_Stokeworth) is knocked off her saddle, and is raped by half a hundred men,(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) resulting in a pregnancy.(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Tanda and her daughters reside in [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) during the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater).(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) In the days after the battle, they attend the ceremony where King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) is betrothed to Lady [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell).(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

Lady Tanda offers Lollys to Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) as a possible wife for [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister).(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) She gives a pair of supple riding boots to King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) as a wedding present.(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) She flees from the Red Keep when Joffrey chokes to death at [his wedding feast](wiki:Purple_Wedding).(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rasos59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Rasos61.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Lady Tanda's hip is shattered from a fall off her horse. After a failed attempt to murder Ser [Bronn](wiki:Bronn) of the Blackwater, Lollys's husband, Tanda's heir Falyse is forced to leave the castle while her husband, Ser [Balman Byrch](wiki:Balman_Byrch), is killed.(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Raffc32.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) When Lady Tanda dies of a chill, Lollys succeeds Tanda as Lady of Stokeworth, with Bronn as her lord.(wiki:Lord_of_Stokeworth.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
