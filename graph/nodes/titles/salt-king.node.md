## Identity

The Salt King was one of two traditional petty-kingship titles of the Iron Islands, the other being the Rock King. According to the oldest surviving records at the Citadel of Oldtown, each of the Iron Islands was governed by two kings: a rock king who ruled the island on land, and a salt king who held command at sea. These kings were chosen through a kingsmoot, called by a priest of the Drowned God whenever a king died.

The dual salt-and-rock kingship system persisted for much of the Iron Islands' ancient history. The prophet Galon Whitestaff summoned the salt and rock kings to Old Wyk for a kingsmoot to choose a high king over all ironborn; the salt king of Orkmont, Urras Greyiron (called Ironfoot), was chosen as the first High King of the Iron Islands. The system was eventually abolished by Urron Greyiron, who descended on Nagga's Hill and slaughtered the assembled thirteen salt and rock kings along with half a hundred priests. Urron made the high kingship hereditary, wearing an iron crown rather than the traditional driftwood, and reduced other ironborn kings to vassal lords.
