## Origins

### Maegor and Jaehaerys[ ]

In the wake of King [Maegor I Targaryen](wiki:Maegor_I_Targaryen)'s death and the arrival of Prince [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen) to the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep), the King's Justice was arrested and put in the [black cells](wiki:Black_cells).(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Rfabprince_into_king_-_the_ascension_of_jaehaerys_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) After reaching the [age of majority](wiki:Age_of_majority) and claiming his rule for himself, King Jaehaerys I replaced the King's Justice, among other lesser offices.(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Rfaba_time_of_testing_-_the_realm_remade.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### The Dance and Regency[ ]

During the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), after Ser [Otto Hightower](wiki:Otto_Hightower) was dismissed as [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) and replaced with Ser [Criston Cole](wiki:Criston_Cole), all the captive [blacks](wiki:Blacks)— who had once sworn to defend the rights of Princess [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and refused to bend the knee of [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen)—were dragged one by one before the King's Justice at the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep)'s yard. Each man was given one final chance to swear fealty to Aegon. The King's Justice beheaded Lords [Hayford](wiki:House_Hayford), [Merryweather](wiki:House_Merryweather), [Harte](wiki:House_Harte), [Buckler](wiki:House_Buckler), and [Caswell](wiki:House_Caswell), as well as Lady [Fell](wiki:House_Fell), eight [landed knights](wiki:Landed_knight), and twoscore servants and retainers.(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Rfabthe_dying_of_the_dragons_-_the_blacks_and_the_greens.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
When [Orwyle](wiki:Orwyle), the former [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester), was captured in early [132 AC](wiki:132_AC) during the [regency of Aegon III](wiki:Regency_of_Aegon_III), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King), Ser [Tyland Lannister](wiki:Tyland_Lannister), repeatedly postponed his execution, pointing out that the office of King's Justice remained empty since the end of the Dance.(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_the_hooded_hand.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) Tyland's successor, Lord [Unwin Peake](wiki:Unwin_Peake), appointed Ser [Victor Risley](wiki:Victor_Risley), the [Knight of Risley Glade](wiki:Knight_of_Risley_Glade), as the King's Justice in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC).(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)
On the [Feast Day of Our Father Above](wiki:Feast_Day_of_Our_Father_Above) in [133 AC](wiki:133_AC), the gaols and dungeons of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) were emptied, and each prisoner was publicly executed by the King's Justice, with the aid of [Tessario](wiki:Tessario) and his [Fingers](wiki:Fingers_(guards)). Among those executed by the King's Justice were a "[Shepherd Reborn](wiki:False_Shepherd)", a [Pentoshi](wiki:Pentos) merchantman accused and found guilty of bringing the [Winter Fever](wiki:Winter_Fever) from [Sisterton](wiki:Sisterton) to King's Landing, and Orwyle.(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Rfabunder_the_regents_-_war_and_peace_and_cattle_shows.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)
Victor was arrested in [135 AC](wiki:135_AC), following the confession of Lord [George Graceford](wiki:George_Graceford), which implicated him in the attempt to overthrow power in the [secret siege](wiki:Secret_siege) amid the [Lysene Spring](wiki:Lysene_Spring). In [136 AC](wiki:136_AC), Victor was condemned to die, but demanded a [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle), in which he was killed by Ser [Gareth Long](wiki:Gareth_Long).(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### Recent History[ ]

Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne) was appointed as the King's Justice at the [wedding of Robert I Baratheon and Cersei Lannister](wiki:Wedding_of_Robert_I_Baratheon_and_Cersei_Lannister). His appointment was a gift from King [Robert](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) to Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), the father of his new bride, a sinecure to compensate Ilyn for the tongue he lost while in service to [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) during the reign of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen).(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark), the [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) for [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), sends Lord [Beric Dondarrion](wiki:Beric_Dondarrion) to hunt down Ser [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane). According to Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys), Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne), the King's Justice, looked displeased at not having been chosen.(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Ragot43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) orders Ilyn to execute Eddard at the [Great Sept of Baelor](wiki:Great_Sept_of_Baelor).(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

During the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater), Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) has Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne) remain with her in the Queen's Ballroom of [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast) in the Red Keep,(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Racok57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) in order to kill Cersei and her captive, [Sansa Stark](wiki:Sansa_Stark), should the Red Keep fall to [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon).(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) When [Osfryd Kettleblack](wiki:Osfryd_Kettleblack) informs Cersei that a groom and two maidservants where caught trying to flee the Red Keep with three of the king's horses, she has the King's Justice execute them and place their heads on pikes outside the stables.(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Racok60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1)
In the throne room following the battle, Joffrey orders Ilyn to behead a defiant [Florent](wiki:House_Florent) bastard who refuses to renounce [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) and the [Lord of Light](wiki:Lord_of_Light).(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) demands the services of Ser [Ilyn Payne](wiki:Ilyn_Payne) from Queen Regent [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) as his condition for leading the expedition to [Riverrun](wiki:Riverrun);(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4) Cersei acquiesces as she blames the King's Justice for the the escape of [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) from the dungeons.(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
In [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), Cersei Lannister suggests that she will have to appoint a new King's Justice due to Ser Ilyn's absence, in order to execute [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell), whom Cersei hopes to charge with treason.(wiki:King's_Justice_(title).cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
