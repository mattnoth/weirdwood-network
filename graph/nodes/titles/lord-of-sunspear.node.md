## Origins

After he ascended the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne), King [Daeron II Targaryen](wiki:Daeron_II_Targaryen) began negotiations with Prince [Maron Martell](wiki:Maron_Martell) of [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear). After two years, Maron agreed to be betrothed to Daeron's sister, Princess [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)). They were [wed](wiki:Wedding_of_Maron_Martell_and_Daenerys_Targaryen) in [187 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), after which Maron officially knelt for Daeron, bringing [Dorne](wiki:Dorne) under the rule of the Iron Throne.(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-2)(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
The concessions which Maron had won during his negotiations with Daeron and the increasing Dornish influence at court caused Daeron's other lords to grow dissatisfied. These complaints, among others, were reasons causing several lords to oppose Daeron II's rule during the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion).(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_daeron_ii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) Dorne sided with Daeron during the rebellion.
At the outbreak of [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion), Prince [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell) was slow to lend aid to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), as he was furious at the mistreatment of his sister, [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell). He eventually sent ten thousand men to fight for the Irone Throne at the [Battle of the Trident](wiki:Battle_of_the_Trident).(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Rasos37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) The battle was lost, and a fortnight later Princess Elia and her two young children were brutally murdered by two [Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister) knights, [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane) and [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch), during the [Sack of King's Landing](wiki:Sack_of_King's_Landing).(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Ragot3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) Although he prevented his brother [Oberyn](wiki:Oberyn_Martell) from starting a new rebellion against their [new monarch](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon),(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Rasos38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) in secret they have worked since then on bringing an end to the reign of King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), who had condoned the murders, and destroying [House Lannister](wiki:House_Lannister), whom they held responsible.(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Doran's brother traveled to [Braavos](wiki:Braavos) to sign a [secret marriage pact](wiki:Secret_marriage_pact) in his name, exchanging support to Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) for a marriage between him and Doran's [heir](wiki:Arianne_Martell).(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Radwd43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
Doran eventually became afflicted with gout, and in [298 AC](wiki:298_AC) moved from [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) to the [Water Gardens](wiki:Water_Gardens). Two years later, Doran's gout has become far worse, and he is no longer able to walk, but instead is usually confined to a wheelchair.(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Prince [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell), the Lord of Sunspear, is ordered by King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon) to present himself to court to declare his loyalty.(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Ragot57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

Prince [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell) accepts the offer of [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister), the acting [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King) in [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister)'s stead, of a betrothal between Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon) and Doran's youngest son, [Trystane](wiki:Trystane_Martell), justice for his murdered sister [Elia](wiki:Elia_Martell) and her children, and a seat on the king's [small council](wiki:Small_council), as well as some castles on the [Dornish Marches](wiki:Dornish_Marches), in return for his loyalty to the Iron Throne and support of Dorne in the war.(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Racok25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0) As part of the deal, he calls his banners.(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Prince [Doran Martell](wiki:Doran_Martell) refuses every suggestion of going to war to avenge his brother [Oberyn](wiki:Oberyn_Martell), who has died in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) while fighting as [someone's](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) champion in a [trial by battle](wiki:Trial_by_battle), as he does not consider his death to have been murder by law.(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) He arrests seven of Oberyn's eight [bastard](wiki:Bastard) daughters to prevent them from plotting against him, or being used in the plots of others,(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-2) and later arrests his own daughter and heir, [Arianne](wiki:Arianne_Martell), after foiling her plot to crown Princess [Myrcella Baratheon](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), with which she hoped to claim Doran's seat.(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
After having had her confided to solitude for several weeks, Doran reveals that he [had been working](wiki:Secret_marriage_pact) to restore [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) and rob Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) of all he held dear before killing him ever since he learned about [his sister's](wiki:Elia_Martell) death during [Robert's Rebellion](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion). With [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) dead, Doran now wants to ally House Martell with Queen [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), and has sent his son [Quentyn](wiki:Quentyn_Martell) to [Essos](wiki:Essos) to escort her to [Westeros](wiki:Westeros).(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Raffc13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Raffc21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Doran orders his lords and ladies to stall Ser [Balon Swann](wiki:Balon_Swann), who is traveling to [Sunspear](wiki:Sunspear) to present Doran with the head of [Gregor Clegane](wiki:Gregor_Clegane), [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell)'s killer.(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Raffc40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-2) After Balon has arrived, Doran asks [Obara](wiki:Obara_Sand), [Nymeria](wiki:Nymeria_Sand), and [Tyene Sand](wiki:Tyene_Sand) to take the place of their late father and assist him in his plans.(wiki:Lord_of_Sunspear.cite_ref-Radwd38.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
