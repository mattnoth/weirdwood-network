## Identity

The children of the forest are the diminutive, non-human indigenous inhabitants of Westeros who predate the arrival of the First Men. The First Men named them "children of the forest," though their own name for themselves translates as "those who sing the song of the earth." They are described as small beings, dark and beautiful, with large eyes and a deep connection to the natural world and the weirwood trees whose carved faces they may see through. They possessed greenseers and wood dancers, and wielded magic in their wars against the First Men.

When the First Men arrived on Westeros with bronze swords and horses, they began felling weirwood groves, provoking generational warfare. Though the children fought back with magic and greensight, the First Men were larger and technologically superior, and the wars went against the children until both sides agreed to the Pact on the Isle of Faces: the First Men received the open lands and coastlines while the children kept dominion of the deep woods. Four thousand years of peace followed, during which the First Men adopted the old gods of the forest, and the children taught them to use ravens to communicate. During the Long Night, children and First Men allied against the Others — the last hero is said to have sought out the children for their aid, and northern legends credit them with helping build the Wall. The coming of the Andals brought fresh destruction: the Andals burned weirwoods and slew children wherever they found them, considering them abominations. The children retreated to the far north and ultimately to the cave of the three-eyed crow beyond the Wall, where at least one of them, Leaf, still dwells as of *A Dance with Dragons*. A TWOW revelation suggests the children themselves created the first Other by pressing a dragonglass blade into a First Man's heart.

## Appearances & Description

The children who dwell in the cave beyond the Wall are "small compared to men, as a wolf is smaller than a direwolf." They have nut-brown skin dappled like a deer's with paler spots, and large ears "that could hear things that no man could hear." Their eyes are "big too, great golden cat's eyes that could see down passages where a boy's eyes saw only blackness." Their hands have only three fingers and a thumb, "with sharp black claws instead of nails." They sing in True Tongue — voices "as pure as winter air." (Bran POV, adwd-bran-03 — `sources/chapters/adwd/adwd-bran-03.md:93`)
