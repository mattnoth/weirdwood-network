## Culture

Tales in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) claim that large [dragons](wiki:Dragon) could pluck krakens from the sea.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

## Origins

The Seastone Chair, carved into the shape of a kraken, was said to have been found by the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) when they first came to [Old Wyk](wiki:Old_Wyk). [Archmaester](wiki:Archmaester) [Haereg](wiki:Haereg) argued that the chair was made by the first inhabitants of the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands), who came from an unknown land west of the Sunset Sea.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf75.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-2)
King [Loron Greyjoy](wiki:Loron_Greyjoy) was called "the Old Kraken". He led an invasion of the western shores of the [north](wiki:North), taking [Bear Island](wiki:Bear_Island) and [Cape Kraken](wiki:Cape_Kraken) in the process. After the Old Kraken died, King [Rodrik Stark](wiki:Rodrik_Stark) reclaimed Bear Island, while his sons and grandsons battled for Cape Kraken.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf711_20-0)
After King [Aegon I Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen)'s [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal) in [2 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest) resulted in the death of King [Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare) and the destruction of [House Hoare](wiki:House_Hoare), claimants to the kingship of the Iron Islands battled for power for more than a year. It was said that the waters between the islands were so choked with corpses, that hundreds of krakens appeared, attracted by the blood.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
In [2 AC](wiki:2_AC), King Aegon I Targaryen [invaded the Iron Islands](wiki:Invasion_of_the_Iron_Islands) to put down several rebellious would-be kings. [Priest](wiki:Priest_of_the_Drowned_God)-King [Lodos](wiki:Lodos) called upon the krakens of the deep to drag down Aegon's warships. When that failed to happen, Lodos filled his robes with stones, and walked into the sea to "take counsel" with his claimed father, the [Drowned God](wiki:Drowned_God). Thousands followed Lodos. Their corpses would wash up on the shores for years to come, except for Lodos's own body.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf754_22-0)
In [56 AC](wiki:56_AC), when planning to travel west of Westeros to discover new lands, [Alys Westhill](wiki:Alys_Westhill) chose to avoid the northern Sunset Sea and its many dangers, including monstrous krakens, and decided on a more southerly route for the journey of her [Sun Chaser](wiki:Sun_Chaser).(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)
Ser [Eustace Hightower](wiki:Eustace_Hightower) and his brother, Ser [Norman](wiki:Norman_Hightower), accompanied Alys Westhill's journey west, on their ships [Lady Meredith](wiki:Lady_Meredith) and [Autumn Moon](wiki:Autumn_Moon). When Ser Eustace returned to [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown) in [59 AC](wiki:59_AC), he related that in the midst of a great storm that struck Autumn Moon's mast with lightning, one of his crewmen screamed that he saw kraken arms rising from the sea. Ser Eustace then saw a monstrous wave sink Autumn Moon, but the crew of the Lady Meredith claimed it was a kraken that had pulled the ship beneath the sea. Afterwards, Ser Eustace's crew demanded to return to Westeros, afraid of encountering further krakens.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
In [128 AC](wiki:128_AC), while fighting in the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones) as a [sellsail](wiki:Sellsail), the fifteen-year-old [Dalton Greyjoy](wiki:Dalton_Greyjoy) saw his uncle killed and avenged his death. Because Dalton emerged from the fight entirely drenched in blood from a dozen wounds, men began calling him "the Red Kraken". Later that year, Dalton returned to the Iron Islands to claim the Seastone Chair after hearing of his father's death.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf755_23-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

While traveling on the [Balerion](wiki:Balerion_(ship)) in the [Summer Sea](wiki:Summer_Sea) from [Qarth](wiki:Qarth) to [Pentos](wiki:Pentos), Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont) convinces Queen [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) to instead sail for [Astapor](wiki:Astapor), buy an army of [Unsullied](wiki:Unsullied), and continue to Pentos overland. When she points out that there are dangers on such a long march, he tells her that there are dangers at sea as well, such as krakens that could pull their ship under.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2)
During the [small council](wiki:Small_council) meeting in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing) to discuss the progress of the [War of the Five Kings](wiki:War_of_the_Five_Kings), [Varys](wiki:Varys) tells the attendees that a kraken has been seen off the [Fingers](wiki:Fingers). The kraken reportedly attacked an [Ibbenese](wiki:Ibbenese) [whaler](wiki:Whale) [ship](wiki:Ship) and pulled it under.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)
While traveling on a ferry across the [Trident](wiki:Trident) during a storm, [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) sees an uprooted tree whose roots and limbs reach for them like the arms of a kraken.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rasos47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
Ser [Axell Florent](wiki:Axell_Florent) suggests that King [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) sack [Claw Isle](wiki:Claw_Isle) for its treasure, but [Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) convinces him otherwise.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) Later, Stannis's sellsail [Salladhor Saan](wiki:Salladhor_Saan) chides Davos for not letting him acquire [House Celtigar](wiki:House_Celtigar)'s treasure, reportedly including a [magic](wiki:Magic) [horn](wiki:Horn) that can summon krakens from the deep.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Quaithe](wiki:Quaithe) gives Daenerys Targaryen a [prophetic](wiki:Prophecy) warning about the dangers coming to her, including a [kraken](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy).(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)
At the [Merman's Court](wiki:Merman's_Court) in [House Manderly](wiki:House_Manderly)'s [New Castle](wiki:New_Castle) in [White Harbor](wiki:White_Harbor), Davos Seaworth sees a painting of a kraken and a grey [leviathan](wiki:Leviathan) locked in battle.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-1)

### The Winds of Winter[ ]

Lady [Valena Toland](wiki:Valena_Toland) tells Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) that [sellswords](wiki:Golden_Company) have invaded the [Stepstones](wiki:Stepstones), and that there are tales of krakens off the [Broken Arm](wiki:Broken_Arm), pulling under crippled galleys. Valena says their [maester](wiki:Maester) claims that the blood draws them to the surface. She adds that there are bodies which remain in the water, while a few have washed up on [Ghost Hill](wiki:Ghost_Hill)'s shores.(wiki:Kraken.cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Barianne_i_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Carianne_i.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
