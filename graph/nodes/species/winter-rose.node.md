## Origins

According to legend, [Bael the Bard](wiki:Bael_the_Bard) climbed the [Wall](wiki:Wall), took the [kingsroad](wiki:Kingsroad), and entered [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) as a singer under the name Sygerrik of [Skagos](wiki:Skagos). There he sang until midnight for Lord [Brandon Stark](wiki:Brandon_Stark_(the_daughterless)). Impressed by Bael's skills as a singer, Brandon asked him what he wanted as a reward. Bael answered by requesting the most beautiful flower blooming in Winterfell's gardens. As the blue winter roses were just blooming, Brandon agreed to offer him one. The following morning the single virgin daughter of the lord had disappeared. In her bed was the rose Bael had received from her father.(wiki:Winter_rose.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
[Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) loved the scent of blue winter roses.(wiki:Winter_rose.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) During the [tourney at Harrenhal](wiki:Tourney_at_Harrenhal), Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen) named Lyanna the [queen of love and beauty](wiki:Queen_of_love_and_beauty) instead of his wife, [Elia Martell](wiki:Elia_Martell), giving Lyanna a garland of blue roses.(wiki:Winter_rose.cite_ref-Ragot58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)(wiki:Winter_rose.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf_the_fall_of_the_dragons:_the_year_of_the_false_spring.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Eddard Stark recalls that when his sister [Lyanna](wiki:Lyanna) died, rose petals spilled from her palm, now dead and black.(wiki:Winter_rose.cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) In a fever dream about the [combat at the tower of joy](wiki:Combat_at_the_tower_of_joy), Eddard sees blue rose petals blowing across the sky.(wiki:Winter_rose.cite_ref-Ragot39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) In a later dream, the statue of [Lyanna Stark](wiki:Lyanna_Stark) in the [crypt of Winterfell](wiki:Crypt_of_Winterfell) has a garland of pale blue roses.(wiki:Winter_rose.cite_ref-Ragot47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

While in the [House of the Undying](wiki:House_of_the_Undying) in [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) has a vision of a sweet-scented blue flower growing from a chink in a wall of ice.(wiki:Winter_rose.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)
The [wildling](wiki:Wildling) [Ygritte](wiki:Ygritte) tells [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) of [Bael the Bard](wiki:Bael_the_Bard), a legendary [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall), a story she calls "the song o' the winter rose."(wiki:Winter_rose.cite_ref-Racok51.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
After [capturing Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell), [Theon Greyjoy](wiki:Theon_Greyjoy) dreams of the feast that Eddard held for King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon) at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell), except the feasters are all dead. Although he never met her, Theon thinks his vision of a slim, sad girl wearing a crown of pale blue roses can only be Lyanna.(wiki:Winter_rose.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
