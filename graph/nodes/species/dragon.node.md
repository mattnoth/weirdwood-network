## Appearances & Description

Dragons are scaled,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) reptilian creatures. They have four limbs: two wings, which are used as forelegs like [bats](wiki:Bat) and pterosaurs,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-6) and two rear legs.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rssm2267.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-8) [George R. R. Martin](wiki:George_R._R._Martin)'s dragons thereby differ from the traditional depiction of dragons as six-limbed creatures. Martin came to his decision because he was bothered by the fact that no [animal](wiki:Animal) in nature has ever evolved in such a way (i.e. four legs and two wings).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rssm2267.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) Nonetheless, some [A Song of Ice and Fire](wiki:A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire) artwork shows them with four legs and a detached pair of wings.
Dragons have strong jaws,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) sharp teeth(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0) and claws,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) leathery necks(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) and tails,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1) with horns on their heads.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) As young hatchlings, they are around the size of a [cat](wiki:Cat).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3) If it has enough food and freedom, a dragon never stops width of twenty feet within a year and a half.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-1) Dragons which are locked up, e.g., the [Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen) dragons housed in the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit) in [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), do not grow as large as dragons left free to fly as they arrived there, was smaller than [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) and [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor),(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) and Vermithor was considered ## Appearances & Description

It is said that dragons are fire made flesh.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Racok27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-2) Great heat emanates from dragons' bodies, to the point that they steam during cold nights.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-4) When a dragon is wounded, their blood is known to smoke.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-3)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) Smoke often comes from a dragon's mouth and nostrils,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) and [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame) is expelled from a dragon's gullet and out of its mouth.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-2)
Dragons are carnivore creatures, eating meat as well as fish, and breathing [fire](wiki:Dragonflame) to cook their [food](wiki:Food) before consuming it.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Racok12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-5)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-4) They are also known to exhibit behavior of opportunistic [cannibalism](wiki:Cannibalism).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-5)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-1)
A dragon's scales are largely, but not entirely, impervious to flame, and serve as protection for the more vulnerable flesh and musculature beneath. As a dragon ages, its scales thicken and grow harder, affording even more protection,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-3) leaving dragons nearly immune to fire,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rssm2267.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-23) although they can still be wounded by [dragonflame](wiki:Dragonflame) (e.g., [Moondancer](wiki:Moondancer)'s eyes being blinded by [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre)'s fire during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons)). Similarly, as dragons age, the fire they breathe becomes hotter and fiercer. Where a hatchling's flame can set straw aflame, the flames of older dragons, like [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) and [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) in the fullness of their power, could melt [steel](wiki:Steel) and stone.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-4)
Dragons are believed to be intrinsically tied to [magic](wiki:Magic) in the world. Since the death of the [last dragon](wiki:Last_dragon) in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), it was said that [summers](wiki:Summer_(season)) grew shorter and the [winters](wiki:Winter) colder and crueler,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) while magical spells became less powerful.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Racok49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

### Life and death[ ]

The lifespan of dragons is many times that of a human, though their maximum natural lifespan is difficult to determine: as beasts of war, most of the known examples died in battle.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-3) Balerion, the largest and oldest Targaryen dragon, was already alive when House Targaryen moved from [Valyria](wiki:Valyria) to [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) in [114 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) and died of old age(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-28) in [94 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) during the reign of King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen). Balerion was at least two hundred and seven years old when he died,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rasos8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-4)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) but as the only known example of a dragon who died of old age, it is unclear if this should be considered typical. It is possible that the wild dragon [Cannibal](wiki:Cannibal_(dragon)), whose date and cause of death are unknown, was about two hundred and fifty years old during the reign of [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), if there is truth to the claim that he was living on [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) even before the Targaryens arrived there.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-6)
Dragons are not invulnerable. The eyes are where a dragon is most vulnerable, and the brain behind them.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0) A scorpion's [iron](wiki:Iron) bolt through the eye killed [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes),(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf796_33-0) and three arrows through the eye [mercy killed](wiki:Gift_of_mercy) a greatly wounded [Tessarion](wiki:Tessarion).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-5) Unlike in stories, a dragon's underbelly scales are just as strong as those along their back and flanks.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1) Young dragons, with their softer scales, are more vulnerable than older ones; on [his rider](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen)'s first flight, [Stormcloud](wiki:Stormcloud) was hit by a scorpion bolt through his neck and shot by dozens of arrows into his belly, and died within the hour.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-7) [Vermax](wiki:Vermax)'s belly may have been torn by a grapnel, though the dragon definitely died when it became tangled in a sinking ship.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-8) Tales also speak of killing dragons with a spear down the gullet, but this is known to be impossible.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-2) Still, five dragons were killed in the [storming of the Dragonpit](wiki:Storming_of_the_Dragonpit), some with axes, spears, and swords, some crushed under stone or by strangulation.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-6)
Though it is rare for a man to be a [dragonslayer](wiki:Dragonslayer), dragons have often been known to wound and kill other dragons, both in Westeros and [Valyria](wiki:Valyria).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) Many dragons killed each other during the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons) ([129](wiki:129_AC)–[131 AC](wiki:131_AC)), using their teeth and claws. [Meleys](wiki:Meleys) half tore off [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre)'s wing,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-9) and Sunfyre was also wounded by [Moondancer](wiki:Moondancer), though not without blinding the other dragon's eyes with his flame.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-7) In a [three-way fight](wiki:Second_Battle_of_Tumbleton) between dragons, [Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor) ripped [Seasmoke](wiki:Seasmoke)'s head off, before he died from the wounds given to him by that dragon and by Tessarion.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-8) Earlier, Balerion crushed [Quicksilver](wiki:Quicksilver)'s neck with his teeth while tearing off her wing with his claws.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) When Balerion returned from a possible journey to Valyria with Princess [Aerea Targaryen](wiki:Aerea_Targaryen), the dragon had half-healed scars and bloody wounds from an unknown cause, including a nearly nine foot rent down his side.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-1)

### Reproduction[ ]

Historians, like [Septon](wiki:Septon) [Barth](wiki:Barth), [Grand Maester](wiki:Grand_Maester) [Munkun](wiki:Munkun), and [Maester](wiki:Maester) [Thomax](wiki:Thomax), disagree on the mating habits of dragons.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Dragons lay large, scaled [eggs](wiki:Dragon_egg) to reproduce.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) According to Archmaester [Gyldayn](wiki:Gyldayn), and agreed upon by Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel), the ability of a dragon to lay eggs is proof that said dragon is female. When a dragon is never observed to have laid eggs, this is taken as proof that it must have been male.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf716_37-0) However, according to Barth and Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), dragons have no fixed gender, but are “now one and now the other, as changeable as flame”.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1) Barth believed that dragons can change sex at need,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf716_37-1)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-2) but Maester [Anson](wiki:Anson) believes this to be erroneous, and states in his [Truth](wiki:Truth_(book)) that Barth's statement is simply a misunderstood esoteric metaphor.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf716_37-2)
Dragons believed to be female, e.g., [Syrax](wiki:Syrax),(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf45.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1) [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre),(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-1) [Tessarion](wiki:Tessarion),(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-2) and [Meleys](wiki:Meleys),(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-1) are referred to as a she-dragon or sometimes a queen.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf46.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)
It is implied that the bonds of a [dragonrider](wiki:Dragonrider) and their dragon may affect the dragon's mating habits. King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen)'s dragon Vermithor often coiled with his Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen)'s [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing), even five decades after their riders' deaths.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-9) [Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) and his wife [Laena Velaryon](wiki:Laena_Velaryon) often visited Dragonstone on their dragons [Caraxes](wiki:Caraxes) and Vhagar to fly with [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen) and [Syrax](wiki:Syrax), and during that period Syrax produced several clutches of eggs.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-3)

[Vermithor](wiki:Vermithor) and [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing) hatched.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-3) During the rule of King [Jaehaerys I ### Dragonriders[ ]

Dragons are intelligent creatures(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rssm2267.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-3) which can be trained to serve as battle mounts and understand vocal commands. They are said to be capricious in nature,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-1) and have to be trained to keep them from laying waste to everything around them.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_52-0) However, dragons will not allow everyone to mount them. In [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), the noble-born dragonriding families, the [dragonlord](wiki:Dragonlord) families, frequently [married brother to sister](wiki:Incest) to keep the bloodline pure,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf42.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_53-0) and it is generally believed that Valyrian heritage plays a part. During the civil war known as the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), Prince [Jacaerys Velaryon](wiki:Jacaerys_Velaryon) called upon Targaryen [bastards](wiki:Bastard) and their descendants, the so-called [dragonseeds](wiki:Dragonseeds), to try and mount a dragon. Regardless, men without Valyrian descent made their attempts as well.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-11) Having Valyrian blood (no matter how directly) is not a guarantee that bonding with a dragon will be successful.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-4)
Once a dragon has bonded with a rider, that dragon will not allow anyone else to mount it alone while its rider lives, no matter how familiar said person might be to the dragon.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-10) But when the person bonded to the dragon is on the dragon's back, they may take a passenger.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-2) When the rider of a dragon dies, that dragon can bond with a new rider.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-2) No rider has ever ridden a different dragon while their current dragon was alive.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_54-0) However, when Prince [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen)'s dragon [Balerion](wiki:Balerion) died, according to Martin, "[Viserys] did not take a second dragon",(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-55) leaving the possibility that a rider might bond with a new dragon after his/her first dragon has died. Indeed, Queen [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen), following the death of her dragon [Syrax](wiki:Syrax), insisted on finding more dragon eggs, as she "must have another dragon",(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-11) as did King [Aegon II Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_II_Targaryen) after the death of his dragon [Sunfyre](wiki:Sunfyre).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_56-0) Dragons who have had a rider once before are easier to bond with than wild dragons.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-12)
A dragon might to be able to sense when its current rider is in distress or has died. This is implied in the case of the dragon [Dreamfyre](wiki:Dreamfyre), who, despite being locked and chained in the [Dragonpit](wiki:Dragonpit), is said to have sensed when her rider [Helaena Targaryen](wiki:Helaena_Targaryen) committed suicide in [Maegor's Holdfast](wiki:Maegor's_Holdfast), at the other side of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-12) The sense of connection between dragon and rider may also be mutual — when [Drogon](wiki:Drogon) was impaled by a spear, he and his bonded owner, [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen), both screamed at the same moment, even though she had not yet ridden him.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-4)
Dragonriders usually ride their dragons using special saddles. Chains attached to the saddle are fastened to the rider's belt, for security.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-3)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-13) However, a rider could leave their chains dangling, if they wish.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-4) A dragonrider may also ride without a saddle, their legs around the dragon's back or neck.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-5)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-14)

## Origins

### Ancient Dragons[ ]

In [Barth](wiki:Barth)'s [Unnatural History](wiki:Unnatural_History), the [septon](wiki:Septon) considered several legends regarding the origins of dragons. The [Valyrians](wiki:Valyrians) claimed that dragons sprang forth from the [Fourteen Flames](wiki:Fourteen_Flames), a ring of volcanoes on the [Valyrian peninsula](wiki:Valyrian_peninsula).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf25.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-0) In [Qarth](wiki:Qarth), tales state dragons came from a second [moon](wiki:Moon) in the sky, which was scalded by the [sun](wiki:Sun) and cracked like an egg, and a million dragons poured forth. Dragons are also said to have originated from the [Shadow Lands](wiki:Shadow_Lands) beyond [Asshai](wiki:Asshai) and the islands of the [Jade Sea](wiki:Jade_Sea).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Ragot23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) Ancient tales from Asshai claim that an unnamed people first tamed these dragons, brought them to [Valyria](wiki:Valyria), and taught the Valyrians their arts before departing from the annals of history.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf25.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-1) Barth put forth the theory that dragons were created by Valyrian [bloodmages](wiki:Bloodmages) using [wyverns](wiki:Wyvern), which are flying reptiles that do not breathe flame.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf834_59-0) Dragons may also be related to [firewyrms](wiki:Firewyrm), which are wingless reptilian creatures that do breathe flame, that may have lived in the Fourteen Flames before the dragons arrived there.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Raffc22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_60-0)
Dragons populated most of the [known world](wiki:Known_world) in ages past,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rssm1062.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_61-0) and ancient dragon bones have been found as far north as [Ib](wiki:Ib) and as south as the jungles of [Sothoryos](wiki:Sothoryos).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf25.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-2) The yellow emperor [Chai Duq](wiki:Chai_Duq) of [Yi Ti](wiki:Yi_Ti) is said to have kept a dragon at his court after marrying a noblewoman from Valyria.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf841_62-0)
In [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), Ser [Galladon of Morne](wiki:Galladon_of_Morne) is said to have slain a dragon with the [Just Maid](wiki:Just_Maid),(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-1) [Crackbones](wiki:Crackbones) allegedly twisted a dragon's neck into a knot,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_51-2) and [Serwyn of the Mirror Shield](wiki:Serwyn_of_the_Mirror_Shield) slew the dragon [Urrax](wiki:Urrax).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-2) [Davos the Dragonslayer](wiki:Davos_the_Dragonslayer) is a hero beloved in the [Reach](wiki:Reach),(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf772_50-1) and dragons are also said to have once roosted on [Battle Isle](wiki:Battle_Isle) until the first [Hightower](wiki:House_Hightower) put an end to them.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf774_63-0) [Smallfolk](wiki:Smallfolk) claim that the hot springs below [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) are warmed by the breath of a subterranean dragon.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf716_37-3)
Outside of Westeros, dragons, [demons](wiki:Demons), and other creatures are said to inhabit the cliff caves above the [Ash](wiki:Ash_(river)) north of [Asshai](wiki:Asshai).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf844_64-0) [Sea dragons](wiki:Sea_dragon) in the [Sunset Sea](wiki:Sunset_Sea) and [ice dragons](wiki:Ice_dragon) in the [Shivering Sea](wiki:Shivering_Sea) are recalled in legends and folklore, but scholars have no proof of their existence, or how closely related they are to dragons.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf751_65-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf836_66-0) [Nagga](wiki:Nagga) is said to have been the first [sea dragon](wiki:Sea_dragon).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0)

### Valyrian Freehold[ ]

The [Valyrians](wiki:Valyrians) were originally a civilization sheltered in the [Fourteen Flames](wiki:Fourteen_Flames), a ring of volcanoes on the [Valyrian peninsula](wiki:Valyrian_peninsula).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf25.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-3) They mastered the art of raising dragons, and used them as weapons of war to carve out a massive empire, the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold). Valyrians told tales of themselves that claimed they were descended from dragons, and were kin to the ones they now controlled.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf25.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-4) The Valyrians are said to have used binding [spells](wiki:Spells) and [sorcerous](wiki:Sorcery) [dragon horns](wiki:Dragon_horn) to control their dragons.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Raffc19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-1)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_57-1)
The Valyrians used dragons to conquer the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis) in the [Ghiscari wars](wiki:Ghiscari_wars),(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf25.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_58-5) and hundreds of dragons were employed by Valyria during the [Rhoynish Wars](wiki:Rhoynish_Wars).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf28.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0)
On the day of the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria) in [102 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest), the Fourteen Flames and every hill for five hundred miles exploded, filling the air with ash and smoke and fire, which killed even the dragons in the sky.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_69-0) The [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlords) and dragons gathered in Valyria were all killed. A few dragonlords in [Lys](wiki:Lys) and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) survived, but they were soon killed in the political chaos after the Doom, along with their dragons. In [Qohor](wiki:Qohor), the dragonlord [Aurion](wiki:Aurion) declared himself [Emperor of Valyria](wiki:Emperor_of_Valyria) and led a host of thirty thousand men toward Valyria, but he, his great dragon, and his followers were not seen again. The time of dragons in Essos was over.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf29.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-0)

### The Targaryen Dragons[ ]

After the Doom and the chaos afterward, the only dragons known to have survived were on the island of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf29.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-1) They belonged to [House Targaryen](wiki:House_Targaryen), who had brought five dragons from Valyria when Lord [Aenar Targaryen](wiki:Aenar_Targaryen) and his family emigrated twelve years before, in [114 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest). Four of these dragons eventually died on Dragonstone, leaving only [Balerion](wiki:Balerion). However, the other dragons left eggs behind, and from these hatched [Vhagar](wiki:Vhagar) and [Meraxes](wiki:Meraxes).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-2)
Balerion was eventually claimed by Lord [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen), some time before he married his sisters [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) and [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf31.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1) Aegon became involved in the wars in the [Disputed Lands](wiki:Disputed_Lands) when [Pentos](wiki:Pentos) and [Tyrosh](wiki:Tyrosh) approached him, inviting him to join a grand alliance against [Volantis](wiki:Volantis). Aegon flew Balerion to meet the [Prince of Pentos](wiki:Prince_of_Pentos) and the [magisters](wiki:Magister) of the [Free City](wiki:Free_City), then flew to [Lys](wiki:Lys) in time to set ablaze a [Volantene](wiki:Volantene) fleet trying to take the city.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf29.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_70-2)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Radwd14.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1)
As the [Century of Blood](wiki:Century_of_Blood) came to an end, Aegon's interest in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) grew. His sister-wife Visenya had claimed Vhagar, and his other sister-wife Rhaenys rode Meraxes. In [2 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest) – [1 AC](wiki:1_AC), Aegon and his sisters used Balerion, Vhagar, and Meraxes in their [Wars of Conquest](wiki:Wars_of_Conquest) against the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms). Dragons were instrumental in the [burning of Harrenhal](wiki:Burning_of_Harrenhal) and the [Field of Fire](wiki:Field_of_Fire), and the threat of dragons led to the submissions of [Torrhen Stark](wiki:Torrhen_Stark) and [Sharra Arryn](wiki:Sharra_Arryn).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-3) Dragons were also used in the [First Dornish War](wiki:First_Dornish_War), which caused the death of Rhaenys and Meraxes at the [Hellholt](wiki:Hellholt) in [10 AC](wiki:10_AC).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf41.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_71-0)
Many dragons were hatched after [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest). During Aegon I's reign, "half a dozen" hatchlings were born on Dragonstone. One of the first was [Quicksilver](wiki:Quicksilver), hatched in [7 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), who bonded with Aegon I's elder son and heir, [Aenys](wiki:Aenys_I_Targaryen). Another two dragons hatched during the first year of Aenys's reign, in [38 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-4) Dragon fought dragon for the first time since the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria) in the [Battle Beneath the Gods Eye](wiki:Battle_Beneath_the_Gods_Eye), resulting in the death of Quicksilver in [43 AC](wiki:43_AC).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf43.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_72-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-5)
Over the course of one hundred and fifty years, the Targaryens rode their dragons as a symbol of their power, and as a means of transport. ### Extinction and Attempts at Revival[ ]

By the end of the reign of King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen), twenty dragons were alive. The majority of these dragons died during the civil war called the [Dance of the Dragons](wiki:Dance_of_the_Dragons), which began in [129 AC](wiki:129_AC) and lasted until mid [131 AC](wiki:131_AC).(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf45.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-2) By the end of the Dance in [131 AC](wiki:131_AC), only four dragons remained alive: the wild [Cannibal](wiki:Cannibal_(dragon)), never ridden; [Sheepstealer](wiki:Sheepstealer), hiding in the [Mountains of the Moon](wiki:Mountains_of_the_Moon) with [its rider](wiki:Nettles); [Silverwing](wiki:Silverwing), who had gone feral after the deaths of [her rider](wiki:Ulf_White) and [her mate](wiki:Vermithor); and the young dragon [Morning](wiki:Morning), who had hatched during the war.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf45.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-3) In [132 AC](wiki:132_AC), a messenger from [Alys Rivers](wiki:Alys_Rivers), [witch](wiki:Witch) queen of [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal), claimed to have seen a dragon within the castle, though the truth of the matter is uncertain.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_77-0)
There were still many dragon eggs left after the war, and at least a few hatched. Terrifyingly, one hatched a blind and wingless [wyrm](wiki:Wyrm) that attacked [its cradlemate](wiki:Laena_Velaryon_(daughter_of_Alyn)) and was killed.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rfab23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_41-1) At least two Dragonstone hatchlings were misshapen, and grew no bigger than [mastiffs](wiki:Dog) before they died.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-5) The [last dragon](wiki:Last_dragon) was a green female, small and stunted, with withered wings.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1) She died young in [153 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), during the reign of King [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen), thereafter known as the Dragonbane.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Ragot22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_78-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf47.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-0) She laid a clutch of five eggs,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-1) which never hatched.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3)
Archmaester [Marwyn](wiki:Marwyn) holds that the order of [maesters](wiki:Maesters), with their secret goal to suppress [magic](wiki:Magic), was responsible for the extinction of dragons.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Raffc45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_80-0) According to Ser [Arlan of Pennytree](wiki:Arlan_of_Pennytree), the summers became shorter after the last dragon died, and the winters longer and crueler.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-4)
After their extinction, the only remnants of dragons were their [bones](wiki:Dragonbone),(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-6) their skulls,(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-7) and their eggs, which eventually turned to stone over time.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_48-2)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf49.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf417.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-1) The eggs were highly valued for their beauty and exotic nature.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Ragot46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_47-1) The Targaryens had a collection of [nineteen dragon skulls](wiki:Skulls_of_the_Targaryen_dragons) of various sizes and ages, that they displayed on the walls of the [throne room](wiki:Iron_Throne) in the [Red Keep](wiki:Red_Keep). The oldest was thousands of years old.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-8)
Many futile attempts were made to revive dragons. [Aegon III Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_III_Targaryen) had nine mages cross the narrow sea to use their magics in attempt to hatch remaining dragon eggs.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-0)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf47.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_79-1) The pious King [Baelor I Targaryen](wiki:Baelor_I_Targaryen) attempted to hatch his egg by praying over it.(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_82-1)(wiki:Dragon.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf49.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_81-1) [Aegon IV Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_IV_Targaryen) ordered [pyromancers](wiki:Pyromancer) to build seven [mechanical "dragons"](wiki:Dragons_(mechanical)) that shot jets of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire). As a dragon ages, its scales thicken and grow harder, affording even more protection, even as its flames burn hotter and fiercer (where the flames of a hatchling can set straw aflame, the flames of Balerion or Vhagar in the fullness of their power could and did melt steel and stone). When two dragons meet in mortal combat, therefore, they will oft employ weapons other than their flame: claws black as iron, long as swords, and sharp as razors, jaws so powerful they can crunch through even a knight's steel plate, tails like whips whose lashing blows have been known to smash wagons to splinters, break the spine of heavy destriers, and send men flying fifty feet in the air.
