## Origins

### Dawn Age[ ]

Weirwoods once grew throughout [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), although they never took to the thinner soil of the [Iron Islands](wiki:Iron_Islands).(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_seven_kingdoms:_the_iron_islands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) The [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) are said to have carved faces in weirwoods during the [Dawn Age](wiki:Dawn_Age), before the coming of the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) from across the [narrow sea](wiki:Narrow_sea). There are still weirwood circles from the children in the high places of [Sea Dragon Point](wiki:Sea_Dragon_Point).(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Radwd26.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)

### First men[ ]

The [First Men](wiki:First_Men) arrived in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) by crossing the [Arm of Dorne](wiki:Arm_of_Dorne), and began to farm the land and build [ringforts](wiki:Ringfort) and villages, cutting down forest to do so, including the carved weirwoods.(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) This caused the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) to attack them, leading to [war](wiki:War_of_the_First_Men_and_the_children_of_the_forest).(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-1) The First Men feared that the children's [greenseers](wiki:Greenseers) could look through the eyes carved into the trees, and so continued to cut down many weirwood trees and groves with their [bronze](wiki:Bronze) axes.(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_dawn_age.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) During the conflict, the First Men made wooden and stone forts, in one case going so far as to build a timber castle entirely from weirwood, called the [Bower](wiki:Greenfield).(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_seven_kingdoms:_the_westerlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)
Eventually, the two sides fought one another to a standstill. They formed the [Pact](wiki:Pact) at the [Isle of Faces](wiki:Isle_of_Faces), in which the children would leave the land to the First Men, except for the deep forests, while the First Men would no longer cut down the weirwoods. In commemoration of this, all the weirwoods on the Isle were carved with faces so that the [gods](wiki:Old_gods) could witness the Pact, and the [green men](wiki:Green_men) were formed to protect the isle and tend to its weirwoods.(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_coming_of_the_first_men.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-2) After the First Men took up the faith of the old gods they created [godswoods](wiki:Godswood) within their castle walls and villages where a single weirwood, known as a [heart tree](wiki:Heart_tree), was planted so the gods could be worshiped.

### Andals[ ]

With the [coming of the Andals](wiki:Coming_of_the_Andals) to Westeros came the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven), which viewed the old gods as little more than [demons](wiki:Demons).(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_seven_kingdoms:_the_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0) Some First Men allied with children of the forest, such as the [Weirwood Alliance](wiki:Weirwood_Alliance) in the [stormlands](wiki:Stormlands),(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_seven_kingdoms:_the_stormlands:_andals_in_the_stormlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0) and the defenders of the sacred grove of [High Heart](wiki:High_Heart) against [Erreg the Kinslayer](wiki:Erreg_the_Kinslayer), who left only the stumps of the thirty-one ancient weirwood trees that had crowned this holy site.(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_seven_kingdoms:_the_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-1) Despite this, the [Andals](wiki:Andals) eventually conquered most of Westeros except the [north](wiki:North), cutting down or burning the weirwoods as they went, and hacking out their carved faces.(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_seven_kingdoms:_the_riverlands.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-2) Once the fighting died down though, the Andal kings sought to consolidate their victories and prevent further bloodshed on the basis of religion, and so kept the weirwoods in their castles' godswoods.(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_arrival_of_the_andals.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-1)
In most of Westeros, the wild weirwoods had been cut or burned down by around [700 BC](wiki:Years_before_Aegon's_Conquest),(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Ragot2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) except for on the [Isle of Faces](wiki:Isle_of_Faces) where the children and the First Men agreed to the peace. As the Andals never conquered the north, the worship of the old gods remains strong there. Weirwoods still grow in the forests of the north, and are prevalent [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall).

She buries [Dick Crabb](wiki:Dick_Crabb) beneath the tree after he is killed by [Shagwell](wiki:Shagwell).(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Raffc20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Varamyr Sixskins](wiki:Varamyr_Sixskins), a [skinchanger](wiki:Skinchanger) not known to be a greenseer, can see through the eyes of a carved weirwood briefly as he is dying.(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Radwdprologue.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0)
The [queen's men](wiki:Queen's_men) burn "[Mance Rayder](wiki:Rattleshirt)" in a wooden cage made from various trees, including weirwoods. The queen's men give weirwood branches to [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) who submit to [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) so the wood can feed the pyre, symbolizing their acceptance of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor).(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)
Bran travels to the [cave of the three-eyed crow](wiki:Cave_of_the_three-eyed_crow) beneath a great weirwood north of the [Wall](wiki:Wall). He observes weirwood roots in the cave.(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Radwd13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) [Snowylocks](wiki:Snowylocks) feeds Bran a paste of weirwood seeds from a carved weirwood bowl.(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2)
Lord [Tytos Blackwood](wiki:Tytos_Blackwood) desires to bury the bones of his son [Lucas](wiki:Lucas_Blackwood), killed in the [Red Wedding](wiki:Red_Wedding), beneath the great dead heart tree of [Raventree Hall](wiki:Raventree_Hall).(wiki:Weirwood.cite_ref-Radwd48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-1)
