## Culture

The harpy is said to have a woman's torso, the wings of a [bat](wiki:Bat) instead of arms, the legs of an eagle, and a scorpion's curled and venomous tail.(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) The harpy was the symbol of the [Old Empire of Ghis](wiki:Old_Empire_of_Ghis) and is as synonymous with the [Ghiscari](wiki:Ghiscari) as the [dragon](wiki:Dragon) is with the [Valyrian Freehold](wiki:Valyrian_Freehold). [Harpy's gold](wiki:Harpy's_gold) is a plant found at [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay).(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)
The harpy symbol still endures in [Slaver's Bay](wiki:Slaver's_Bay), and many plazas and gates in the [Slaver Cities](wiki:Slaver_Cities) are dominated by huge harpy statues of bronze or clay.(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1) Harpies also decorate the poles of Ghiscari silken pavilions.(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Radwd47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) A thunderbolt rested in the claws of the harpy of [Old Ghis](wiki:Old_Ghis).(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) The harpy of [Astapor](wiki:Astapor) has a heavy chain dangling from her talons, with an open manacle at either end.(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) The harpy of [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) has talons grasping a whip and an iron collar.(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Rasos42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) A harpy decorates the top of the [Great Pyramid](wiki:Great_Pyramid) in [Meereen](wiki:Meereen),(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Rasos57.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) and the throne within is carved in the shape of a harpy.(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

After the [siege of Meereen](wiki:Siege_of_Meereen), [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen) has the harpy torn down from atop the [Great Pyramid](wiki:Great_Pyramid).(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Rasos71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

The so-called [Sons of the Harpy](wiki:Sons_of_the_Harpy) oppose Daenerys's rule in [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) and use the symbol of the harpy as their calling card.(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) [Skahaz mo Kandaq](wiki:Skahaz_mo_Kandaq) believes the assassins are led by the [Harpy](wiki:Harpy), a secret general.(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Radwd30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)
During one of Daenerys's audiences in the [Great Pyramid](wiki:Great_Pyramid), a sculptor proposes to replace the head of the great bronze harpy in the [Plaza of Purification](wiki:Plaza_of_Purification) with one cast in Daenerys's image. The queen denies him with as much courtesy as she can muster.(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Radwd2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) [Drogon](wiki:Drogon) takes to basking in the sun at the apex of the Great Pyramid where the harpy of Meereen once stood. When attempting to capture the [dragon](wiki:Dragon), Daenerys's men fail three times to take him at the apex.(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Radwd11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
One of the [six sisters](wiki:Six_sisters), the trebuchets of [Yunkai](wiki:Yunkai) used at the [second siege of Meereen](wiki:Second_siege_of_Meereen), is called the Harpy's Daughter.(wiki:Harpies.cite_ref-Radwd47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)
