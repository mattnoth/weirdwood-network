## Culture

Sables are hunted for their fur. Ser [Waymar Royce](wiki:Waymar_Royce) has a long sable cloak,(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) and Ser [Jaremy Rykker](wiki:Jaremy_Rykker) has a heavy cloak richly trimmed with sable.(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Racok13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow) also has a sable cloak.(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Racok66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) Wisdom [Hallyne](wiki:Hallyne) has striped black-and-scarlet robes trimmed with sable.(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Racok20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) [Arnolf Karstark](wiki:Arnolf_Karstark)'s grey wool cloak is trimmed with black sable.(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Radwd12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) Sable trims the collar of Ser [Denys Mallister](wiki:Denys_Mallister) and accents the sleeves of his black velvet doublet.(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Rasos78.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
[Sable Hall](wiki:Sable_Hall) is one of the abandoned castles of the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) along the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)

## Origins

While drinking [wine](wiki:Wine) in the barracks of [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), [Gared](wiki:Gared) joked that Ser [Waymar Royce](wiki:Waymar_Royce) personally killed the sables for his fine cloak.(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

Ser [Waymar Royce](wiki:Waymar_Royce)'s splendid sable cloak is slashed when the young [ranger](wiki:Ranger) is attacked by [Others](wiki:Others) in the [haunted forest](wiki:Haunted_forest).(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Ragotprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

[Thoren Smallwood](wiki:Thoren_Smallwood) claims Ser [Jaremy Rykker](wiki:Jaremy_Rykker)'s sable cloak after his death.(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Racok13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
Lord [Mace Tyrell](wiki:Mace_Tyrell) and two of his sons, Ser [Garlan](wiki:Garlan_Tyrell) and Ser [Loras](wiki:Loras_Tyrell), wear green velvet trimmed with sable while in the [throne room](wiki:Throne_room) after the [Battle of the Blackwater](wiki:Battle_of_the_Blackwater).(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Racok65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

King [Tommen I Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon) wears cloth-of-gold beneath a sable mantle while attending the funeral of his grandfather, Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister).(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Raffc7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
[Euron Greyjoy](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy) claims a sable cloak from Lord [Baelor Blacktyde](wiki:Baelor_Blacktyde) after having him executed.(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Raffc29.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Lady [Walda Bolton](wiki:Walda_Frey) wears a sable hood when Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton) returns to the [north](wiki:North),(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Radwd20.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) and Lady [Barbrey Dustin](wiki:Barbrey_Dustin) wraps herself in sable at [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell).(wiki:Sable.cite_ref-Radwd41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)
