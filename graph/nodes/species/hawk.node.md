## Culture

Known for their speed(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) and eyesight,(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Racok52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0) captive hawks and [falcons](wiki:Falcon) can be used for [hunting](wiki:Hunting), a popular activity for noblemen(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Racok11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0)(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Radwd37.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0) and noblewomen.(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Racok19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Radwd6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rfab7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) [Willas Tyrell](wiki:Willas_Tyrell) is said to have the finest hawks in the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms),(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rasos19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) and [House Celtigar](wiki:House_Celtigar) is believed have magnificent hawks at [Claw Isle](wiki:Claw_Isle).(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) Merchants can sell hawks at markets.(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
[Maesters](wiki:Maesters) use [ravens](wiki:Raven) to send messages as they are better able than doves or pigeons to defend themselves from hawk attacks,(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Ragot60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) but many ravens are still brought down by the birds of prey.(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Ragot24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0)(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1)
The arms of [House Fowler](wiki:House_Fowler) depict a hooded hawk, and Lord [Franklyn Fowler](wiki:Franklyn_Fowler) is called the Old Hawk.(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Raffc41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) Four hawks' heads are included in the arms of [House Terrick](wiki:House_Terrick).
[Sphinxes](wiki:Sphinx) are said to have the wings of a hawk.(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Raffc0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
[Skinchangers](wiki:Skinchanger) who have entered the minds of hawks can lose their connection to the earth and instead only want to fly.(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Radwd0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
[Cragorn](wiki:Cragorn) has a great hawk [tattooed](wiki:Tattoo) on his chest.(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Radwd63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

## Origins

There are reports that [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) and his sister [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) hawked at the [Arbor](wiki:Arbor) with Lord [Redwyne](wiki:House_Redwyne) prior to [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest).(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rfab1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) After he became king, many lords of the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms) tried to impress Aegon I with feasting, balls, [hunting](wiki:Hunting), and hawking during his royal progresses.(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rfab3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
[Saera Targaryen](wiki:Saera_Targaryen) was able to persuade her father, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), to give her a variety of [animals](wiki:Animal), including a hawk. The princess often hunted and hawked with her friends, [Perianne Moore](wiki:Perianne_Moore) and [Alys Turnberry](wiki:Alys_Turnberry).(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rfab11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)
[Daemon Targaryen](wiki:Daemon_Targaryen) hawked with his niece, [Rhaenyra Targaryen](wiki:Rhaenyra_Targaryen).(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) Daemon's wife, Lady [Rhea Royce](wiki:Rhea_Royce), died after falling from her horse while hawking.(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rfab12.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)
Prior to the [Maiden's Day Ball](wiki:Maiden's_Day_Ball), [Lucinda Penrose](wiki:Lucinda_Penrose) was attacked by outlaws while hawking along [Blackwater Bay](wiki:Blackwater_Bay), and her bird was killed.(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rfab21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
Lord [Willam Stackspear](wiki:Willam_Stackspear) loved to hawk.(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)
"The Hawk" was the name for one of [three rebels](wiki:The_Rat,_the_Hawk,_and_the_Pig) during the reign of King [Aegon V Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen).(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf415.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
Focusing his attention on the sky, Lord [Luthor Tyrell](wiki:Luthor_Tyrell) rode off a cliff to his death while hawking.(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Rasos6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0)
Young [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) cared for a goshawk he named [Proudwing](wiki:Proudwing), but the bird was never the equal of [Thunderclap](wiki:Thunderclap), the gyrfalcon of his elder brother, [Robert](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon).(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) After becoming king, Robert I prefers to hunt and hawk and leave the tedium of governing to Lord [Jon Arryn](wiki:Jon_Arryn), his [Hand of the King](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Raffc17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0) Robert spent much of his time hawking during a visit to [Greenstone](wiki:Greenstone).(wiki:Hawk.cite_ref-Raffc25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
