## Appearances & Description

Mammoths are large, elephant-like animals, with great tusks. They are large enough to be ridden by [giants](wiki:Giants), or to carry tall wooden towers with people inside upon their backs.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-0) They leave huge footprints,(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-1) and although they have an awesome strength, their huge size makes them an easy target in battle.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-0)
Mammoths are shaggy,(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-0) wooly beasts,(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-2) and their colors are known to include white and grey.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-3)

## Origins

The [wood dancers](wiki:Wood_dancer) of the [children of the forest](wiki:Children_of_the_forest) are said to have called upon mammoths to fight on their behalf, but the [First Men](wiki:First_Men) proved too powerful.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf22.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-0)
In [58 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), when Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen) visited [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) feasted her on mammoth meat, washed down with [mead](wiki:Mead) and [stout](wiki:Beer).(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rfab10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0)
In [129 AC](wiki:129_AC), shortly before his death, King [Viserys I Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_I_Targaryen) told his grandchildren, [Jaehaerys](wiki:Jaehaerys_Targaryen_(son_of_Aegon_II)) and [Jaehaera](wiki:Jaehaera_Targaryen), a tale about their great-great-grandfather, King [Jaehaerys I Targaryen](wiki:Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen), and great-great-grandmother, Queen [Alysanne Targaryen](wiki:Alysanne_Targaryen), battling [giants](wiki:Giant), mammoths, and [wildlings](wiki:Wildlings) [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall).(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf45.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)
Hairy mammoths are said to roam the cold wastes beyond the [Port of Ibben](wiki:Port_of_Ibben)(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0) on [Ib](wiki:Ib), and the island's plains and hills.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf837_8-0) They also roam the woods north [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall).(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Ragot21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0)
While there used to be many mammoths in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros), now there only are a few hundred left.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Radwd34.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)

## Narrative Arc

### A Game of Thrones[ ]

[Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), [Lord Commander of the Night's Watch](wiki:Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch), tells [Tyrion Lannister](wiki:Tyrion_Lannister) that mammoths still live in the woods north of the [Wall](wiki:Wall).(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Ragot13.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) He later tells [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) that [Cotter Pyke](wiki:Cotter_Pyke) has written of mammoths heading south and east, towards the sea.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Ragot48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0)

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

A [wildling](wiki:Wildling) taken captive by [Qhorin Halfhand](wiki:Qhorin_Halfhand) swears that the free folk have [wargs](wiki:Warg) and mammoths.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Racok43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) As a [skinchanger](wiki:Skinchanger), Jon later enters his direwolf, [Ghost](wiki:Ghost), and sees [giants](wiki:Giant) riding mammoths.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Racok53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0) When Jon later encounters [Rattleshirt](wiki:Rattleshirt) and his mount, he sees that both man and mount are armored in bones, and recognizes mammoth bones among them.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Racok68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

After having joined the host of the [King-beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-beyond-the-Wall), [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder), Jon sees giants and their mammoths. The stink coming off them is choking, but Jon is unable to tell if the smell comes from the giants, or their mammoths.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-4)
When the host of Mance arrives at the [Wall](wiki:Wall), Jon identifies the sound of a mammoth and thinks there are a hundred.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-1) While fighting the wildlings during the night, the defenders of [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black) can hear mammoths trumpet. While one mammoth lies dying at the bottom of the Wall, the defenders can see another mammoth run through the woods, afire, trampling men and trees in its agony.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-2) At dawn, the burned carcass is seen covered by crows.
The wildlings continue the fight by having the giants bring up a ram. Mammoths are used to center the wildling line, a hundred or more, with giants upon their backs. The black brothers shoot arrows at the giants, and several mammoths are injured in the process. One of the injured beasts crashes into another, spilling giants over the ground, while another wounded mammoth goes berserk, smashing wildlings with his trunk and crushing archers underfoot.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_3-3) When the line of wildlings, giants and mammoths reaches the gate, the Night's Watch pushes an oil barrel down, which is set aflame. The remaining mammoths flee in terror, and the center breaks. During the next night, the wildlings skin one of the dead mammoths, and use its hide to cover the roof of the wooden turtle which covers a new ram.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
When the southron knights of [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon) attack the wildling camp, the mammoths begin to wander west. They manage to shatter the center [column](wiki:Column) of knights,(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0) and when the wildling hosts starts to break, only the giants upon their mammoths are able to hold.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos70.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-1) Eventually, however, the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall) is lost,(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0) and a few giants and mammoths are taken captive.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) The others flee.
At [Castle Black](wiki:Castle_Black), [Three-Finger Hobb](wiki:Three-Finger_Hobb) promises the brothers roast haunch of mammoth, hoping to gain a few more votes that way.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Rasos75.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

[Samwell Tarly](wiki:Samwell_Tarly) tells Jon Snow, the new Lord Commander, about [Others](wiki:Others) riding dead mammoths.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Stannis and [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) urge the wildling captives to join them by embracing the [Lord of Light](wiki:Lord_of_Light). Out of the forty captives that refuse, four are giants. One of them refuses to leave his mammoth behind, and the other giants refuse to leave their fellow giant.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1) The captives are allowed to go, and the giants with their mammoth are the last to leave. Two of them ride the mammoth, while the other two walk.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2)
[Tormund](wiki:Tormund)'s host contains more than eighty mammoths. As they are too big to pass the gate of Castle Black, they go the long way around, passing the Wall at [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea).(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Radwd53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) His people are required to give up their wealth while passing the gate, and among the items they give up is a toy mammoth made of actual mammoth hair.(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)
Tyrion takes to using the term "mammoth" to refer to huge, fat men, including [Illyrio Mopatis](wiki:Illyrio_Mopatis)(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Radwd1.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0) and [Yezzan zo Qaggaz](wiki:Yezzan_zo_Qaggaz).(wiki:Mammoth.cite_ref-Radwd66.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
