## Origins

After actions taken by [Malthar Xaq](wiki:Malthar_Xaq) and other princes of the Summer Isles began a new era of exploration and trade for their people, a fashion developed amongst the [dragonlords](wiki:Dragonlord) of [Valyria](wiki:Valyria) for apes from the islands.(wiki:Ape.cite_ref-Rtwoiaf831_1-2)
After the death of Queen [Visenya Targaryen](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen)'s [fool](wiki:Fool), [Lord Monkeyface](wiki:Lord_Monkeyface), she acquired an ape and dressed it in his clothing.(wiki:Ape.cite_ref-Rfab3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) She was wont to say that this "new one" was cleverer than the previous one.(wiki:Ape.cite_ref-Rfab3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-1)
According to Ser [Axell Florent](wiki:Axell_Florent), there once was a Targaryen princeling who kept an ape as a pet. This prince reportedly liked to dress the creature in his dead son's clothes and pretend he was a child and from time to time would even propose marriages for him, although the lords so honored always declined politely.(wiki:Ape.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Ape.cite_ref-8)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

When [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) first looks upon [Rorge](wiki:Rorge), he reminds her of a drawing she had once seen in a book of an ape from the Summer Isles.(wiki:Ape.cite_ref-Racok5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

When [Victarion Greyjoy](wiki:Victarion_Greyjoy) looks upon the crew of [his brother](wiki:Euron_Greyjoy)'s galley, the [Silence](wiki:Silence), he notes that some of the men are as squat and hairy as the apes of Sothoryos.(wiki:Ape.cite_ref-Raffc18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

[Davos Seaworth](wiki:Davos_Seaworth) recalls that during a feast at [Eastwatch-by-the-Sea](wiki:Eastwatch-by-the-Sea), Ser [Axell Florent](wiki:Axell_Florent) had entertained the table with the tale of a [Targaryen](wiki:Targaryen) princeling who had kept an ape as a pet. His story was intended as a slight to Davos, who some at the table viewed as akin to an ape dressed in silk and velvet in his role as [the king](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s [Hand](wiki:Hand_of_the_King).(wiki:Ape.cite_ref-Radwd15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1)
One of the [Brazen Beasts](wiki:Brazen_Beasts) assigned to guard the iron doors outside the pit where [Viserion](wiki:Viserion) and [Rhaegal](wiki:Rhaegal) are chained within the [Great Pyramid](wiki:Great_Pyramid) of [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) wears a mask with the likeness of an ape.(wiki:Ape.cite_ref-Radwd55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) [Gerris Drinkwater](wiki:Gerris_Drinkwater) wears a similar mask when he and others attempt to steal one of the [dragons](wiki:Dragon).(wiki:Ape.cite_ref-Radwd68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
