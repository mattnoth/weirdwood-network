## Culture

The sigils of [House Mormont](wiki:House_Mormont) and [House Brune](wiki:House_Brune_of_Brownhollow) depict a black bear and a bear paw, respectively.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-12) Some [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) also depict a bear claw on their shields.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos55.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)
Due to their sigil, some Mormonts have earned a bear-based nickname:
Lord [Jeor Mormont](wiki:Jeor_Mormont), the Old Bear(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Ragot19.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)
[Alysane Mormont](wiki:Alysane_Mormont), the She-Bear(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Radwd35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_15-0)
Ser [Jorah Mormont](wiki:Jorah_Mormont), called "my bear" by [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen)(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)
Daenerys Targaryen also refers to the late [Willem Darry](wiki:Willem_Darry) as a "sweet old bear", though that was unrelated to his sigil.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Racok48.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0)
Dancing bears are used as entertainment in [Westeros](wiki:Westeros) and the [Free Cities](wiki:Free_Cities).(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_iii.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0)(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-0) In Norvos, there is a festival that features bears dancing down the [Sinner's Steps](wiki:Sinner's_Steps) once a year.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Raffc2.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0)(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_free_cities:_norvos.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2)
Bear-baiting is another form of entertainment in Westeros.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Racok50.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0)
"[The Bear and the Maiden Fair](wiki:The_Bear_and_the_Maiden_Fair)" is a popular Westerosi song.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Racok21.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) There is also a silly song about a bear that drank too much.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-1)
Free folk make tents of sewn hides with the fur still on, including the hides of snow bears.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) The gate of [Craster's Keep](wiki:Craster's_Keep) has a bear's skull atop one of its gateposts.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Racok23.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos33.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0)
[Bear-paws](wiki:Bear-paws) is the name for snowshoes used by the members of the [northern mountain clans](wiki:Northern_mountain_clans)(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Radwd42.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) and by the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk).(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Radwd58.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0)

## Origins

Tales of the [Long Night](wiki:Long_Night) speak of the [Others](wiki:Others) riding the corpses of dead animals, including bears.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Raffc5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Radwd7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)
In antiquity, bears made their lairs within [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock).(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_westerlands:_casterly_rock.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)
[Harren Hoare](wiki:Harren_Hoare), [King of the Isles and the Rivers](wiki:King_of_the_Isles_and_the_Rivers), built [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal) with a lavish bear pit, ten yards across and five yards deep, walled in stone, floored with sand, and encircled by six tiers of [marble](wiki:Marble) benches.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-1)
To the unaccustomed eyes of the inhabitants of [King's Landing](wiki:King's_Landing), the [northmen](wiki:North) in their furs led by Lord [Cregan Stark](wiki:Cregan_Stark), looked like an army of armored bears.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rfabaftermath_-_the_hour_of_the_wolf.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)
[Sandoq the Shadow](wiki:Sandoq_the_Shadow) was a veteran [pit fighter](wiki:Pit_fighter) in [Meereen](wiki:Meereen), where he fought against men and animals, including bears.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rfabthe_lysene_spring_and_the_end_of_regency.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_20-2)

## Narrative Arc

### A Clash of Kings[ ]

The [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions), while foraging in the [riverlands](wiki:Riverlands), acquire a black bear and bring it to [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal).(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0) After the [fall of Harrenhal](wiki:Fall_of_Harrenhal) to Lord [Roose Bolton](wiki:Roose_Bolton), the castle's castellan, Ser [Amory Lorch](wiki:Amory_Lorch), is thrown naked into the bear pit(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Racok47.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-1) and torn apart.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0)

### A Storm of Swords[ ]

[Tormund Giantsbane](wiki:Tormund_Giantsbane) tells [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow) the reason for his title "Husband to Bears", claiming he drunkenly lay with a female bear while thinking it was a woman in furs, tearing her pelt off while she bit off half his penis. He claims that the next spring the [free folk](wiki:Free_folk) told stories of seeing a bald bear with odd-looking cubs.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0)
Jon encounters [Varamyr Sixskins](wiki:Varamyr_Sixskins), a wildling [skinchanger](wiki:Skinchanger) who rides a [snow bear](wiki:Snow_bear).(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-1)
A dead bear [wight](wiki:Wight) is among the wights and [Others](wiki:Others) that [attack](wiki:Fight_at_the_Fist) the [Night's Watch](wiki:Night's_Watch) at the [Fist of the First Men](wiki:Fist_of_the_First_Men). It is pale and rotting, with its fur and skin sloughed off and its front right leg burned to bone. [Thoren Smallwood](wiki:Thoren_Smallwood) nearly beheads the bear with his sword, but then the bear takes his head off.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos18.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0)
After Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) and Roose Bolton leave Harrenhal, [Vargo Hoat](wiki:Vargo_Hoat) throws [Brienne of Tarth](wiki:Brienne_of_Tarth) into the bear pit to amuse his [Brave Companions](wiki:Brave_Companions). While she makes a fight of it against the bear, they only give her a blunted [tourney](wiki:Tourney) sword to defend herself. Jaime, returning to the castle with his [Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton) escort, led by [Steelshanks Walton](wiki:Steelshanks_Walton), arrives in time to discover this. Jaime jumps into the bear pit to protect Brienne, and Walton has his crossbowmen kill the bear to protect his charge.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-2)
A dancing bear performs at the [wedding](wiki:Purple_wedding) of King [Joffrey I Baratheon](wiki:Joffrey_I_Baratheon) and Queen [Margaery Tyrell](wiki:Margaery_Tyrell).(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)
During the [battle beneath the Wall](wiki:Battle_beneath_the_Wall), Varamyr loses control over his beasts while in agony from the death of [Orell](wiki:Orell)'s [eagle](wiki:Eagle).(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Rasos73.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_37-0) The snow bear turns her claws on those around her, ripping apart four men before falling to a spear. She would have slain Varamyr had he come within her reach.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Radwdprologue.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_38-0)

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Returning to Harrenhal, Jaime Lannister sees the carcass of the bear in its pit, half-buried in the sand, only bones and ragged fur remaining. The story of Brienne fighting the bear has spread, though one version of the tale claims she was fighting naked.(wiki:Bear.cite_ref-Raffc27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0)
