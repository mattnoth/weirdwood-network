Dreams and prophecies is a magical concept/practice from the AWOIAF wiki.

## Origins

### Ancient prophecy[ ]

According to prophecy as written down in ancient books of [Asshai](wiki:Asshai)(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) from over five thousand years ago, the legendary hero [Azor Ahai](wiki:Azor_Ahai) is to be reborn again as a champion sent by [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor).(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0) The prophecy claims that Azor Ahai shall be born again amidst smoke and salt,(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Radwd22.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_8-0) to wake [dragons](wiki:Dragon) out of stone.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-0) This will occur after a long summer when an evil, cold darkness descends upon the world(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-1) and the red star bleeds.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-1) It is said that wielding [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer) once again, Azor Ahai will stand against the [Others](wiki:Others),(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-2) and deliver the world from darkness.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Radwd27.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_10-0)
The [red priestess](wiki:Red_priest) [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) uses the name Azor Ahai and the term "[the prince that was promised](wiki:The_prince_that_was_promised)"(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-0) interchangeably, although she tends to use the name Azor Ahai far more often.

### Century of Blood[ ]

[Daenys Targaryen](wiki:Daenys_Targaryen), also called "Daenys the Dreamer", was the daughter of [Aenar Targaryen](wiki:Aenar_Targaryen), [Lord of Dragonstone](wiki:Lord_of_Dragonstone). Daenys is reputed to have had a gift of prophecy; She wrote the book [Signs and Portents](wiki:Signs_and_Portents) in which she detailed her visions.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_12-0) Aenar and his family survived the [Doom of Valyria](wiki:Doom_of_Valyria) because they sold all of their holdings and belongings twelve years before the Doom and left Valyria, moving instead to the [island of Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone_(island)) where they claimed the [castle by the same name](wiki:Dragonstone). It is believed by scholars that Aenar's decision to relocate his family was based on one of Daenys's visions, in which she predicted the Doom.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rtwoiafancient_history:_the_doom_of_valyria.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_13-0)(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_reign_of_the_dragons:_the_conquest.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_14-0)

### Aegon's Conquest[ ]

At some point, Lord [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_I_Targaryen) of [Dragonstone](wiki:Dragonstone) had a [dragon dream](wiki:Dragon_dream) that contributed to his decision to conquer the [Seven Kingdoms](wiki:Seven_Kingdoms).(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-15)
During [Aegon's Conquest](wiki:Aegon's_Conquest), the [High Septon](wiki:High_Septon_(Aegon's_Conquest)) locked himself within the [Starry Sept](wiki:Starry_Sept) in [Oldtown](wiki:Oldtown), praying for seven days and nights and taking only bread and water for nourishment. When he emerged from the sept seven days later, the High Septon announced that the [Faith of the Seven](wiki:Faith_of_the_Seven) would not oppose Aegon and his sisters, [Visenya](wiki:Visenya_Targaryen) and [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen). The High Septon stated that the [Crone](wiki:Crone_(the_Seven)) had show him that, if Oldtown were to take up arms against the Targaryens, the city would burn and be destroyed. After hearing this prophecy, [Manfred Hightower](wiki:Manfred_Hightower_(Aegon's_Conquest)), [Lord of the Hightower](wiki:Lord_of_the_Hightower), refused to march out with his strength, and even opened Oldtown's gates to Aegon at Aegon's approach. The city was subsequently spared.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_16-0)

### After Aegon's Conquest[ ]

King [Maekar I Targaryen](wiki:Maekar_I_Targaryen) had six children with his wife, Lady [Dyanna Dayne](wiki:Dyanna_Dayne): [Daeron](wiki:Daeron_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), [Aerion](wiki:Aerion_Targaryen), [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)), [Daella](wiki:Daella_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Maekar_I)), [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), and [Rhae](wiki:Rhae_Targaryen). Of these six, at least one son was known to have had prophetic dreams,(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-0) although all four brothers might have had the ability.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-0)
Prince Daeron claimed to consistently dream of events that indeed came to pass. In [209 AC](wiki:209_AC), he confronted Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) at the [tourney at Ashford Meadow](wiki:Tourney_at_Ashford_Meadow) before he was to battle Duncan with his father and brother in a [trial of seven](wiki:Trial_of_seven). Daeron told Duncan,
"I dreamed of you and a dead [dragon](wiki:Dragon), you see. A great beast, huge, with wings so large they could cover this meadow. It had fallen on top of you, but you were alive and the dragon was dead."(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-1)
Daeron, considering the possibility that this dream was a prediction of his own death, feared Duncan for it and promised to stay on the ground if struck down during the trial of seven.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-2) While Daeron, Aerion, and Maekar survived the trial, Daeron's dream came true when Maekar's brother, Prince [Baelor Breakspear](wiki:Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)), who had fought on Duncan's side, removed his helm following the battle and revealed parts of his skull missing due to a blow given to him during the battle. As in Daeron's dream ("[the dragon] had fallen on top of you"), Baelor (the dragon) towered over Duncan and fell down, with the hedge knight catching him.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-3)
According to Daeron's brother Aemon, all four brothers dreamt of dragons one time or another.
"The last dragon died before you were born," said Sam. "How could you remember them?""I see them in my dreams, Sam. I see a red star bleeding in the sky. I still remember red. I see their shadows on the snow, hear the crack of leathern wings, feel their hot breath. My brothers dreamed of dragons too, and the dreams killed them, every one."(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-1)
Although Daeron died from a pox caught from a [whore](wiki:Prostitute),(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok6.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_19-0) his dreams have been implied(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-20) to have influenced his life-style(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rthk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_17-4) (e.g., [alcoholism](wiki:Alcoholism)). His brother Aerion died after drinking a cup of [wildfire](wiki:Wildfire), believing that doing so would transform him into a dragon,(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_maekar_i.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_21-0) while the youngest sibling Aegon died in [a fire](wiki:Tragedy_at_Summerhall) at [Summerhall](wiki:Summerhall) which Maester [Yandel](wiki:Yandel) has linked to Aegon's dream of having dragons fly over Westeros once again.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rtwoiafthe_targaryen_kings:_aegon_v.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_22-0)
Although Aegon's dreams of dragons might not have been prophetic, Daeron at least is known to have dreamed that one day the dragons, who at the time had been extinct for almost sixty years, would someday return.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-1) King [Aerys I Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_I_Targaryen) read a prophecy stating the same thing.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-2) Although Daeron did not live to see it, nearly a hundred and fifty years after the death of the [last dragon](wiki:Last_dragon), three [dragon eggs](wiki:Dragon_eggs) were hatched in the [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea) by [Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen).(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Ragot72.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_23-0) Aemon's believe that dreaming of dragons is what killed his brothers offer the possibility that Aerion, Aegon, or both, might have had a prophetic dream.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_18-2)
[Daemon II Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_II_Blackfyre), the third-born son of [Daemon I Blackfyre](wiki:Daemon_I_Blackfyre), had prophetic dreams that he considered to be completely reliable. He predicted the deaths of his two older brothers, [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Blackfyre) and [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Blackfyre), in his dreams, although they did not believe him. He further dreamt about the future membership of Ser [Duncan the Tall](wiki:Duncan_the_Tall) in the [Kingsguard](wiki:Kingsguard), and the hatching of a [dragon's egg](wiki:Dragon's_egg) at a white castle that he believed to be [House Butterwell](wiki:House_Butterwell)'s [Whitewalls](wiki:Whitewalls).(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-3)
Although Daemon was correct about his brothers, who both died during the [First Blackfyre Rebellion](wiki:First_Blackfyre_Rebellion) at the [Redgrass Field](wiki:Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field), and about Duncan becoming a [knight](wiki:Knight) of the Kingsguard later in life, his interpretation of the hatching of a dragon's egg at Whitewalls was false. Daemon had been convinced that his dream meant that an actual egg would hatch, and thus expected to soon have a real dragon. Lord [Brynden Rivers](wiki:Brynden_Rivers), however, offered a different interpretation, stating that the dream referred to Prince [Aegon Targaryen](wiki:Aegon_V_Targaryen), who had accompanied Ser Duncan the Tall to Whitewalls disguised as the squire Egg and who had taken out a ring with his father's sigil on it when troubles began, thereby announcing his identity.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rtmk.7B.7B.7B2.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_4-4)
In [276 AC](wiki:Years_after_Aegon's_Conquest), during the [tourney for King Aerys II](wiki:Tourney_for_King_Aerys_II) at [Lannisport](wiki:Lannisport), the [maegi](wiki:Maegi) known as [Maggy the Frog](wiki:Maggy_the_Frog) prophesied the futures of [Cersei Lannister](wiki:Cersei_Lannister) and [Melara Hetherspoon](wiki:Melara_Hetherspoon). Both girls were allowed to ask three questions, with Cersei going first. In response to Cersei's first question ("When will I wed the prince"), Maggy replied that she would never be wed to the prince, but instead be wed to "the king". Cersei believed this to mean that she would wed Prince [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen), the heir of King [Aerys II Targaryen](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), after Rhaegar would succeed his father on the [Iron Throne](wiki:Iron_Throne).(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-0) Maggy's first prophecy came true when Cersei [was wed](wiki:Wedding_of_Robert_I_Baratheon_and_Cersei_Lannister) in [284 AC](wiki:284_AC) to King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), who had won the throne during [a war](wiki:Robert's_Rebellion) in which both Aerys and Rhaegar had been killed.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc24.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_25-0)
Cersei's second question was a confirmation ("I will be queen, though?"), which Maggy confirmed before warning her that she would one day be replaced by a younger, more beautiful queen who would "cast you down and take all that you hold dear".(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-1)
Cersei's last question was whether she and the king would have children, to which Maggy replied that the king would have sixteen children, while Cersei would have three.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-2) Maggy's prediction on Cersei's children has thus far proven correct. Cersei has given birth to three children—[Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon), [Myrcella](wiki:Myrcella_Baratheon), and [Tommen Baratheon](wiki:Tommen_Baratheon)—during her marriage (none of them fathered by King Robert).(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Ragotappendix.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_26-0) The one time Cersei became pregnant by Robert, she had her brother [Jaime](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) find a woman to cleanse her, refusing to give birth to Robert's child.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_27-0) With regards to Cersei's children, Maggy further prophesied that "gold shall be their crowns and gold their shrouds" and claimed that "when your tears have drowned you, the [valonqar](wiki:Valonqar) shall wrap his hands about your pale white throat and choke the life from you".(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-3) Maggy's prediction on Robert's children is still unclear. Robert is known to have fathered many [bastards](wiki:Bastard) throughout the realm, although only seven have thus far been identified in [A Song of Ice and Fire](wiki:A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire); Lord [Varys](wiki:Varys), the [master of whisperers](wiki:Master_of_whisperers), claims to have knowledge of eight.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_28-0) Regardless, it is generally believed by the fandom that Maggy's prediction is indeed correct, and that Robert has had, in total, sixteen children.
When Melara asked whether she would someday marry Cersei's brother Jaime, Maggy prophecized that she would wed no one, going as far as to say that "Your death is here tonight, little one. Can you smell her breath? She is very close.".(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-4) According to Cersei, this prophecy of Maggy's quickly came true. She tells [Taena of Myr](wiki:Taena_of_Myr) that Melara drowned in a well.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_24-5) It has been implied that Cersei herself was responsible for Melara's death by pushing her into the well and leaving her there to drown.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc39.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_29-0)

## Narrative Arc

### Bran Stark[ ]

While in a coma after he has been thrown from the [First Keep](wiki:First_Keep) by Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister), [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) begins to experience a prescient dream. It depicts current events including Lady [Catelyn Stark](wiki:Catelyn_Stark)'s journey to King's Landing by ship with Ser [Rodrik Cassel](wiki:Rodrik_Cassel), Lord [Eddard Stark](wiki:Eddard_Stark) pleading with King [Robert I Baratheon](wiki:Robert_I_Baratheon), and [Sansa](wiki:Sansa_Stark) and [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) reacting to the deaths of the direwolf [Lady](wiki:Lady_(direwolf)) and the boy [Mycah](wiki:Mycah). It also includes more prophetic elements, including the "gathering storm" resulting from Catelyn's visit to the south and shadows around Sansa and Arya (one as dark as ash, with the terrible face of a hound, one armored like the sun, golden and beautiful, and one a giant in armor made of stone with only darkness and thick black blood in his visor looming over them).(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Ragot17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-0)
Bran's dream ends with a view of the [Wall](wiki:Wall), his [bastard](wiki:Bastard) brother [Jon Snow](wiki:Jon_Snow), the lands [beyond the Wall](wiki:Beyond_the_Wall), and the [three-eyed crow](wiki:Three-eyed_crow) telling Bran that he now knows why he has to survive his coma.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Ragot17.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_30-1)

### Daenerys Targaryen[ ]

[Daenerys Targaryen](wiki:Daenerys_Targaryen)'s first known experience with a prophetic dream happens a few days before [her wedding](wiki:Wedding_of_Drogo_and_Daenerys_Targaryen) to Khal [Drogo](wiki:Drogo). The dream starts off with [Viserys Targaryen](wiki:Viserys_Targaryen) hitting her, but quickly turns prophetic as she witnesses the hatching of a [dragon](wiki:Dragon).
Her thighs were slick with blood. She closed her eyes and whimpered. As if in answer, there was a hideous ripping sound and the crackling of some great fire. When she looked again, Viserys was gone, great columns of flame rose all around, and in the midst of them was the dragon.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Ragot11.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_31-0)
Later, when Daenerys is pregnant with [her child](wiki:Rhaego) from Drogo, the crones of the [dosh khaleen](wiki:Dosh_khaleen) predict her to be the eventual mother of the [stallion who mounts the world](wiki:Stallion_who_mounts_the_world).(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Ragot46.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_32-0)
After the stillbirth of her child and the practice of [bloodmagic](wiki:Bloodmagic) by [Mirri Maz Duur](wiki:Mirri_Maz_Duur) on [Drogo](wiki:Drogo) resulting in an apallic state, Daenerys asks Mirri when Drogo will he as he was. Mirri's reply is considered a prophecy:
When the sun rises in the west and sets in the east. When the seas go dry and mountains blow in the wind like leaves. When your womb quickens again, and you bear a living child. Then he will return, and not before.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Ragot68.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_33-0)
[Quaithe](wiki:Quaithe) has made prophecies to Daenerys on two separate occasions. The first is an instruction on where Daenerys should go:
To go north, you must go south. To reach the west, you must go east. To go forward you must go back, and to touch the light you must pass beneath the shadow.
Daenerys interprets this to mean that Quaithe wants her to go to [Asshai](wiki:Asshai). She asks Quaithe what she can find in Asshai that cannot be found in Qarth, and Quaithe replies "truth".(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok40.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_34-0) A year later, after Daenerys has departed [Meereen](wiki:Meereen) on [Drogon](wiki:Drogon)'s back,(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Radwd52.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_35-0) Daenerys considers staying at [the hill](wiki:Dragonstone_(hill)) in the [Dothraki sea](wiki:Dothraki_sea) on which the [dragon](wiki:Dragon) has made his lair. Recalling Quaithe's words ("to go forward you must go back"), which provides her with the resolve to walk back towards Meereen.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Radwd71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-0) After Daenerys becomes sick and begins to hallucinate, she envisions Quaithe repeating part of her instructions, and adds another: "Remember who you are, Daenerys. The dragons know. Do you?"(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Radwd71.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_36-1)
The second is a warning of dangers Daenerys is to face in the near future.

### Jojen Reed[ ]

[Jojen Reed](wiki:Jojen_Reed) has the [greensight](wiki:Greensight), the ability to have prophetic dreams. Although his sister [Meera](wiki:Meera_Reed) believes that the dreams “sometimes” come true, Jojen claims they always do.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-2) The green dreams can take strange shapes,(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-0) making them difficult to interpret.
Jojen has described several of his green dreams. At [Winterfell](wiki:Winterfell) he tells [Bran Stark](wiki:Bran_Stark) that he had a green dream in which Bran was sitting at supper, with Maester [Luwin](wiki:Luwin) bringing the food instead of a servant. While Bran received the king's cut of the roast, the tastiest piece there was, in the dream Bran was able to enjoy his food as much as [Big Walder](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Jammos)) and [Little Walder Frey](wiki:Walder_Frey_(son_of_Merrett)) were, despite the fact that they had been served meat that was "old and grey and dead".(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-3) Later, Bran learns from Luwin of [Robb Stark](wiki:Robb_Stark)'s victory over Ser [Stafford Lannister](wiki:Stafford_Lannister) in the [Battle of Oxcross](wiki:Battle_of_Oxcross), and the death of Ser [Stevron Frey](wiki:Stevron_Frey). While Bran is happy for the victory, he knows that only Lord [Tywin Lannister](wiki:Tywin_Lannister) matters. In contrast, neither Big Walder nor Little Walder seem bothered by their uncle Stevron's death, instead being more concerned about their improved positions in the line of succession for [House Frey](wiki:House_Frey).(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-1)
One of Jojen's dreams predicts Theon Greyjoy's [capture of Winterfell](wiki:Capture_of_Winterfell), in which the [ironborn](wiki:Ironborn) are depicted as waves of the sea, while the slain of Winterfell are depicted as drowned men.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-2)
In another one of Jojen's green dreams, he sees "[Reek](wiki:Reek)" skinning the faces of Bran and Rickon, who laid dead at his feet.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_39-3) After a fashion, this dream later comes true as well, when "Reek" (actually [Ramsay Snow](wiki:Ramsay_Snow)) advises Theon Greyjoy to kill two [miller's sons](wiki:Miller's_sons) from the [Acorn Water](wiki:Acorn_Water), who are around the same age as the missing Bran and Rickon, and presents their corpses as the bodies of the two Stark children. Ramsay later skins the two bodies so the ruse will not be discovered.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok56.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_40-0)
Jojen further claims to know the date of his death.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok28.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-4)

### Red priests[ ]

The [red priests](wiki:Red_priests), priests of [R'hllor](wiki:R'hllor), are trained to see visions in the flames of their [nightfires](wiki:Nightfire). These visions can be about the past, the future, or things happening far away from the location of the priest.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-1) It is not always easy to see visions in the flames, however,(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos36.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_43-0) and the priests do not always succeed.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Racok10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-3)(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos43.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_42-2)(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Radwd3.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_44-0)
It takes years of training to see the shapes beyond the flames, and even longer to learn to distinguish visions about the past from visions about a certain future and a possible future.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-0) Even after such long training, it remains difficult,(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_45-1) and even though the visions are never wrong,(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos25.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-2) priests might still err in their interpretation;(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos54.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_11-1)(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Radwd49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_9-1) many priests have been brought down by such incorrect interpretations.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_46-0)

### Jaime Lannister[ ]

Ser [Jaime Lannister](wiki:Jaime_Lannister) sleeps with his head on a [weirwood](wiki:Weirwood) stump after leaving [Harrenhal](wiki:Harrenhal). He dreams he is in the deepness of [Casterly Rock](wiki:Casterly_Rock). He is naked and alone, but has both his hands again. A dozen tall dark figures in cowled robes, who are hiding their faces, hold spears, and refuse to answer Jaime when he demands to know who they are. Instead, with their spears, they force him to descent into the deepness. In his dream, Jaime knows that his doom, which he describes as "something dark and terrible", awaits him below.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-0) Jaime eventually reaches a watery cavern deep below Casterly Rock. When he wonders out loud where he is, the voices of all [Lannisters](wiki:House_Lannister) answer that it is his "place". Jaime sees his father [Tywin](wiki:Tywin_Lannister), his sister [Cersei](wiki:Cersei_Lannister), and his eldest son, [Joffrey](wiki:Joffrey_Baratheon). Cersei is holding a torch, which is the only source of light in the cavern. Jaime pleads for her not to leave him alone, and requests a sword when they do turn to leave. His father replies that he has given him a sword, and Jaime sees it under the water. When he holds the sword, it catches fire.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-1)
[Brienne Tarth](wiki:Brienne_Tarth) appears from the darkness, her hands bound in chains. She is also naked, and asks Jaime to undo her chains. When she asks, a sword appears, which subsequently catches fire as well. From afar, Jaime hears Cersei say that when the flames on the swords go out, they will die, after which she leaves. Brienne questions what lives in the darkness, offering the suggestions of a [bear](wiki:Bear), a [cave lion](wiki:Cave_lion), and [direwolves](wiki:Direwolves), which Jaime all rejects. They ponder on what to do next, as shadows appear. Jaime recognizes [Oswell Whent](wiki:Oswell_Whent), [Jon Darry](wiki:Jon_Darry), [Lewyn Martell](wiki:Lewyn_Martell), [Gerold Hightower](wiki:Gerold_Hightower), [Arthur Dayne](wiki:Arthur_Dayne) and [Rhaegar Targaryen](wiki:Rhaegar_Targaryen). While Brienne repeatedly states how she swore to keep Jaime safe, the shadow of Arthur tells her that they all swore an oath, and the shadows blame Jaime for the deaths of [Aerys](wiki:Aerys_II_Targaryen), [Elia](wiki:Elia_Martell), [Rhaenys](wiki:Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)), and [Aegon](wiki:Aegon_Targaryen_(son_of_Rhaegar)). That is when the fire of Jaime´s sword starts going out.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rasos44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_66-2)
Jaime has another dream after [Riverrun](wiki:Siege_of_Riverrun) has been handed over to his aunt, [Genna Lannister](wiki:Genna_Lannister), and uncle, [Emmon Frey](wiki:Emmon_Frey). He dreams of his mother, Lady [Joanna Lannister](wiki:Joanna_Lannister), although at first he does not recognize her. Joanna asks him if he will forget Tywin as well, and tells her son that Tywin hated being laughed at most of all. Joanna asks Jaime who he is, and tells him that they all dreams of things they cannot have. She tells him "Tywin dreamed that his son would be a great knight, that his daughter would be a queen. He dreamed they would be so strong and brave and beautiful that no one would ever laugh at them." When Jaime insists that he is a knight and Cersei a queen, a tear rolls down Joanna's cheek. She pulls her hood over her head and walks away, while Jaime calls after her.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Raffc44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_67-0)

### Teora Toland[ ]

[Teora Toland](wiki:Teora_Toland), the younger daughter of Lady [Nymella Toland](wiki:Nymella_Toland), appears to have prophetic dreams. While present at the welcome feast for Princess [Arianne Martell](wiki:Arianne_Martell) and her companions, she states that she has dreamed of [dragons](wiki:Dragon) dancing in her dreams, and people dying wherever they danced.(wiki:Dreams_and_prophecies.cite_ref-Rtwow.5B.5Barianne_i_.28the_winds_of_winter.29.7Carianne.5D.5D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_68-0)
