## Narrative Arc

### A Feast for Crows[ ]

Because [Stannis Baratheon](wiki:Stannis_Baratheon)'s sword (which has a large ruby in its hilt)(wiki:Glamor.cite_ref-Racok31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_5-0) radiates no heat, only light, the dying Maester [Aemon](wiki:Aemon_Targaryen_(son_of_Maekar_I)) dismisses it as a false [Lightbringer](wiki:Lightbringer), its glow nothing more than a glamor.(wiki:Glamor.cite_ref-Raffc35.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_6-0)

### A Dance with Dragons[ ]

In [Braavos](wiki:Braavos), the [kindly man](wiki:Kindly_man) explains to [Arya Stark](wiki:Arya_Stark) that the magic of the [Faceless Men](wiki:Faceless_Men) is different from glamors, as their magic actually alters the object's appearance.(wiki:Glamor.cite_ref-Radwd64.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_2-1)
To spare [Mance Rayder](wiki:Mance_Rayder) from being executed by Stannis, [Melisandre](wiki:Melisandre) glamors the [King-Beyond-the-Wall](wiki:King-Beyond-the-Wall). Mance wears a ruby in a fetter and bone armor to appear as the [Lord of Bones](wiki:Lord_of_Bones), who is likewise glamored into appearing as Mance. Rattleshirt is thus burned in a cage, while Mance is sent on a mission to rescue "Arya" (actually [Jeyne Poole](wiki:Jeyne_Poole)) from [House Bolton](wiki:House_Bolton).(wiki:Glamor.cite_ref-Radwd10.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_7-0)(wiki:Glamor.cite_ref-Radwd31.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D.7B.7B.7B4.7D.7D.7D_1-5)
