## Identity

The Red Comet is a celestial phenomenon — a bright red comet — that appears in the sky at the end of A Game of Thrones and during A Clash of Kings. Its appearance is widely observed across Westeros and Essos and is interpreted in radically different ways by different characters and factions, making it a significant narrative symbol.

In the R'hllor prophetic tradition, the red comet is identified with the "bleeding star" of prophecy — the celestial sign heralding the rebirth of Azor Ahai. Melisandre cites it when proclaiming Stannis Baratheon as Azor Ahai reborn: "When the red star bleeds and the darkness gathers, Azor Ahai shall be born again amidst smoke and salt to wake dragons out of stone." Dalla, a lady at Dragonstone, relays that Melisandre told Queen Selyse Florent it was "dragonsbreath." Other characters interpret it differently: some see it as a herald of Daenerys Targaryen's dragons, some as a sign of war, and Salladhor Saan references it sardonically when discussing broken promises of gold. Maester Aemon later comes to believe the comet is connected to Daenerys and the prophecy of the prince that was promised.

The comet functions in the narrative as an omen whose ambiguity allows it to be claimed by multiple prophetic frameworks simultaneously. Its presence in the sky during the opening of the War of the Five Kings ties it to the general chaos and transformation sweeping Westeros and Essos.
