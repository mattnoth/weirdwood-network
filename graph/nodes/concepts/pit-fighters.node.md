## Identity

Pit fighters are professional combatants in the fighting pits of Meereen and the slave cities of Slaver's Bay, trained to fight for public entertainment. The fighting pits are deeply embedded in the culture and economy of Old Ghis, and pit fighters occupy a recognized social status — famous fighters like Khrazz, Barsena Blackhair, Camarron of the Count, Goghor the Giant, the Spotted Cat, Fearless Ithoke, and Belaquo Bonebreaker were prominent figures in Meereen before Daenerys Targaryen closed the pits after her conquest.

When Daenerys took Meereen and freed its slaves, she closed the fighting pits. Hizdahr zo Loraq — a Meereenese nobleman who had purchased the pits at a reduced price — petitioned Daenerys multiple times to reopen them. For his seventh petition, Hizdahr brought seven former pit fighters with him to personally beg the queen. Faced with her people's wishes, Daenerys eventually reopened the pits as a condition of her marriage to Hizdahr.

After Daenerys's disappearance on Drogon and Hizdahr's arrest, the pit fighters played an unexpected political and military role. Ser Barristan Selmy insisted on including several pit fighters — Camarron of the Count, Belaquo Bonebreaker, Goghor the Giant, and the Spotted Cat — on the ruling council of Meereen over Skahaz mo Kandaq's objections. During the second siege of Meereen, hundreds of pit fighters gathered to fight on Meereen's behalf, with Camarron, Goghor, and the Spotted Cat leading them in battle. Camarron himself proposed using ships to bring three hundred pit fighters around the Yunkish rear via the Skahazadhan river. That they fought despite historically favoring Hizdahr over Daenerys was noted by Barristan as a sign that freedom meant something to them.
