## Identity

The Queen of Love and Beauty is a ceremonial honor bestowed at tournaments in Westeros. When a knight wins a joust or tournament, he has the right to crown a woman of his choosing as the Queen of Love and Beauty — a symbolic gesture of admiration, typically by placing a crown of flowers on the chosen woman's head. The honor carries no formal power but carries significant social and romantic meaning, and choosing a woman other than one's own wife or betrothed can cause scandal.

The most famous and consequential bestowal in the narrative occurred at the Tourney at Harrenhal in 281 AC. After Prince Rhaegar Targaryen won the final joust, all expected him to crown his wife, Princess Elia Martell, as Queen of Love and Beauty. Instead, he rode past Elia and placed the crown on Lyanna Stark of Winterfell. Eddard Stark would recall this moment as "the moment when all smiles died" — an act widely interpreted as a public declaration of Rhaegar's feelings for Lyanna, contributing to the chain of events that led to Robert's Rebellion. Ser Barristan Selmy also reflects that had he won the tourney at Harrenhal, he would have crowned Ashara Dayne as Queen of Love and Beauty. At the Ashford Tournament, Lord Ashford's daughter held the title as the reigning Queen of Love and Beauty whom competitors jousted to defend.
