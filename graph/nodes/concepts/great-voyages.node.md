## Identity

The Great Voyages is a term used to describe the celebrated long-distance seafaring expeditions of the Velaryon admirals, particularly Corlys Velaryon (the Sea Snake) and his eventual successor Alyn Velaryon (Oakenfist). The voyages represent extraordinary feats of exploration far beyond the ordinary routes of Westerosi trade and navigation.

Corlys Velaryon made nine great voyages aboard his ship the Sea Snake. His first took him beyond the Jade Gates of Qarth to Yi Ti and Leng, bringing back silks, spices, and jade that doubled House Velaryon's wealth and eventually made Corlys the richest man in the Seven Kingdoms. His second voyage took him to Asshai, where he reportedly "lost his love and half his crew" and claimed to have seen Elissa Farman's Sun Chaser in harbor. His third voyage saw him become the first Westerosi to navigate the Thousand Islands in the Shivering Sea and visit Nefer in N'ghai and Mossovy.

Alyn Velaryon made six great voyages on six different ships, which he called "my ladies," in contrast to Corlys who made all his voyages on the Sea Snake. Alyn's first great voyage began as a mission against Dalton Greyjoy in the westerlands, dispatched by the Hand Unwin Peake who hoped Alyn would fail. Alyn's voyages were the subject of multiple written works: Ser Russell Stillman, who squired for and was knighted by Alyn, wrote a biography called Hard as Oak, and Maester Bendamure wrote Six Times to Sea: Being an Account of the Great Voyages of Alyn Oakenfist, considered the most complete and authoritative account.
