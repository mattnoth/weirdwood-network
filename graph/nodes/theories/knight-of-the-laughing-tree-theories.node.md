## Claim

At the Tourney at Harrenhal (281 AC), an anonymous mystery knight bearing a shield
painted with a laughing weirwood face — the "Knight of the Laughing Tree" — avenged
the crannogman Howland Reed after squires assaulted and mocked him, then vanished
rather than be unmasked, defying King Aerys and Prince Rhaegar's attempts to identify
him. The theory holds that the knight was **Lyanna Stark in disguise**, and that the
incident — which drew Rhaegar himself into the search for the mystery knight's
identity — is where her secret relationship with Rhaegar plausibly began, feeding
into (but distinct from) the `r-plus-l-equals-j` theory of Jon Snow's parentage.

## Evidence For

**Lyanna's established motive.** The entire incident begins with Lyanna ("the
she-wolf") personally rescuing Howland Reed from the squires who are beating him:

> "That's my father's man you're kicking," howled the she-wolf.
— sources/chapters/asos/asos-bran-02.md:183 (edge T10, node lyanna-stark)

This gives Lyanna — of the five named suspects Meera's story implicitly raises
(Howland himself, Brandon, Ned, Benjen, Lyanna) — the clearest personal stake in
avenging Howland's honor.

**The incident itself.** Meera's account of the mystery knight's feats is the entire
textual basis for the theory (and for its rival candidates):

> the Knight of the Laughing Tree spoke in a booming voice through his helm, saying,
> "Teach your squires honor, that shall be ransom enough."
— sources/chapters/asos/asos-bran-02.md:225 (edge T11, node howland-reed)

The knight is also described as "short of stature," in "ill-fitting armor made up of
bits and pieces" — physical details the theory reads as consistent with a
fifteen-year-old girl improvising armor and a horse rather than with any of Lyanna's
grown brothers.

**Rhaegar's direct involvement in the search.** Crucially, it is Rhaegar himself whom
Aerys sends to unmask the knight:

> The king was wroth, and even sent his son the dragon prince to seek the man, but
> all they ever found was his painted shield, hanging abandoned in a tree.
— sources/chapters/asos/asos-bran-02.md:229 (edge T12, node rhaegar-targaryen)

The theory reads this as the likely point of first contact between Rhaegar and
Lyanna: if Rhaegar identified her and chose to keep her secret, it would explain both
his later, otherwise-unexplained crowning of her as queen of love and beauty at the
same tourney, and the beginning of their relationship.

**A textual bridge to the Harrenhal crowning.** When Bran, hearing the story,
suggests the mystery knight should have "name[d] the wolf maid the queen of love and
beauty," Meera's reply confirms this already happened — as a matter of established
fact within the story world, not Bran's wish:

> "She was," said Meera, "but that's a sadder story."
— sources/chapters/asos/asos-bran-02.md:233 (edge T13, node tourney-at-harrenhal)

This directly ties the Knight of the Laughing Tree episode to Rhaegar's crowning of
Lyanna later in the same tourney (see `r-plus-l-equals-j` edge T4/T5), which the
"sadder story" line frames as consequential rather than incidental.

**Ned's suspicious silence.** When Meera finishes the tale, Jojen is surprised Bran
has never heard it:

> "Are you certain you never heard this tale before, Bran?" asked Jojen. "Your lord
> father never told it to you?"
— sources/chapters/asos/asos-bran-02.md:235 (edge T14, node eddard-stark)

This is suggestive rather than conclusive — it is read by some sub-theories as a hint
that Ned has closer personal knowledge of the knight's identity than he has ever let
on, whether or not Ned himself is a candidate for the knight.

## Evidence Against

Within the story itself, Bran concludes the knight was probably Howland — the plain,
textually "obvious" reading. (The community counter-argument to that reading is
video-sourced, not textual — see Ungrounded material.) No book passage confirms or
denies any candidate's identity outright; the mystery is deliberately left open in
the text as published.

Physical/characterization arguments raised by the source video against the other
named candidates (Howland himself, Brandon, Ned, Benjen) were not independently
grounded to verbatim quotes in this pass and are not represented as edges — see
Ungrounded material below.

## Ungrounded material (outside the corpus)

- **community** — Counter-argument to the plain Howland reading: ASOIAF mysteries
  typically present an appealing obvious answer that later proves incomplete or
  wrong (paralleling how Jon himself was long believed to be Ned's bastard). Genre
  reasoning from the source video, not a grounded textual beat.
- **book-interpretive** — Detailed case-by-case elimination of the other four named
  candidates (Howland via the plain textual reading; Brandon as "tall" and already
  jousting openly under his own name; Ned as "solemn"/distaste for tourney pageantry;
  Benjen as too young/unskilled) rests on characterization drawn from scattered
  passages across multiple books rather than any single citable quote, and was not
  grounded in this pass.
- **community** — A rival "crazier" theory holds that Bran Stark, using future
  greensight, warged into young Howland Reed at the moment of his lakeside prayer and
  used old-gods magic to win the jousts himself — paralleling the show's Season 6
  "hold the door" sequence. The source video explicitly judges this theory "kinda
  ridiculous" despite noting Bran's own in-text wish, after hearing the tale, that "a
  day would be enough" to be a knight (asos-bran-02.md:237) — a real quote, but one
  that supports only Bran's wistfulness, not the warging theory itself.
- **community** — The claim that Rhaegar and Lyanna secretly married on the Isle of
  Faces (near Harrenhal), later witnessed by Bran in a vision, is pure fan
  speculation with no textual anchor; it belongs to the broader `r-plus-l-equals-j`
  marriage question, not to this theory's core claim.
- **unknown / TWOW** — A reference to an unreleased *Winds of Winter* preview chapter
  (Elia Sand, nicknamed "Lady Lance," as precedent that a fifteen-year-old girl could
  plausibly joust) falls outside the published 5-book corpus and cannot be grounded
  against `sources/chapters/`.

## Status Notes

adjudicated the mystery knight's identity on screen. This theory has **no show
confirmation** — status is `open`, not `show-confirmed` (contrast `r-plus-l-equals-j`,
which the show did resolve).

despite some thematic resonance, while acknowledging the mystery is never resolved on
