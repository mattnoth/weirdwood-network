History[ ]

House Rogare was a rich and powerful banking dynasty whose bloodlines could be traced back to Valyria before the Doom.

The Rogare family from the Free City of Lys was once closely tied to the Seven Kingdoms of Westeros. Lady Larra Rogare was the wife of Prince Viserys Targaryen and her uncle Drazenko was the consort of Aliandra Martell, Princess of Dorne.

For some years after the Targaryen civil war known as the Dance of the Dragons, the Rogare Bank rivaled the Iron Bank of Braavos in size and the family became heavily involved in Westerosi court intrigues during the Lysene Spring. 

Larra's father, Lysandro the Magnificent, proclaimed himself First Magister for Life, but the Rogares began to weaken when he and Drazenko died with a day of each other in 135 AC. Lysandro's heir, the ambitious Lysaro Rogare, was a spendthrift who angered rival magisters: Lysaro's embezzlement caused the Rogare Bank to fail, and he was scourged to death at the Temple of Trade the same year. Lysaro's siblings were not executed, however, and Moredo Rogare, carrier of the Valyrian steel longsword Truth, once led a campaign against Lys. It is implied that Moredo was somewhat successful in his campaign against Lys, because in 138 AC he was able to put the bones of his dead brother, that had been left chained to a pillar at the Temple of Trade, into the family crypt.

Although the Rogares at the Red Keep were apparently guilty of some crimes, their rivals in King's Landing blamed them for many more. In 135 AC Lord Thaddeus Rowan was tortured in an attempt to find evidence against the Rogares, and Ser Marston Waters, the Hand of the King to Aegon III Targaryen, sent men to capture Larra after arresting her brothers. Aegon and Viserys refused to surrender Larra, however, and the case against the Rogares collapsed. Larra left the court at King's Landing after she grew homesick and returned to Lys in 139 AC.
