History[ ]

House Rykker received Duskendale following the Defiance of Duskendale and the fall of House Darklyn in 277 AC.

During the feast that King Aerys II Targaryen threw when Cersei Lannister first came to court, Lord Owen Merryweather talked about raising taxes on wine, prompting Lord Rykker to joke about Lord Tywin Lannister shitting gold. Tywin stared at the lord until Rykker gave up and walked away.

During Robert's Rebellion, Ser Jaremy Rykker fought for Aerys, surrendering to House Lannister forces during the Sack of King's Landing in 283 AC. He was given the choice of execution or the Wall by Tywin Lannister, and chose the Night's Watch.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Ser Jaremy Rykker commands the rangers in Benjen Stark's absence, but he is killed by a wight in Castle Black.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Ser Helman Tallhart and Robett Glover march on Duskendale. They are met by Lord Randyll Tarly and are defeated in the battle at Duskendale. The retreating northmen are then routed by Ser Gregor Clegane.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Lord Renfred Rykker is with Lord Tarly at Maidenpool. He leaves Ser Rufus Leek as castellan of the Dun Fort to look after his wife and his children, while he is away.

House Rykker at the end of the third century[ ]

The known Rykkers during the timespan of the events described in A Song of Ice and Fire are:

Lord Renfred Rykker, Lord of Duskendale.

Lady Rykker, his wife.

An unnamed number of children.

With unspecified familiar relationship to the main branch of the house there is also:

Ser {Jaremy Rykker}, a ranger of the Night's Watch. Slain by a wight.

Household[ ]

Ser Rufus Leek, one-legged greybeard castellan of the Dun Fort.

A bald maester with a freckled scalp and a stiff red mustache.

Historical Members[ ]

Lord Rykker, one of King Aerys II Targaryen's lickspittles who once made fun of Lord Tywin Lannister at a feast.
