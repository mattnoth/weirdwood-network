Traits[ ]

Members of House Belmore tend to have red hair.

History[ ]

The Belmores are of First Man origin. During the coming of the Andals to the Vale, they allied with Robar II Royce against the invading Andals. Robar's forces were defeated in the Battle of the Seven Stars, however, and the Belmores submitted to Artys I Arryn, the new King of Mountain and Vale.

Recent Events[ ]

A Storm of Swords[ ]

The Belmores support Lord Yohn Royce in pressuring Lady Lysa Arryn to enter the War of the Five Kings.

Ser Marwyn Belmore is Lysa's captain of the guards at the Eyrie, and he is mocked by Marillion, who names him Ser Ding-Dong. After the wedding of Petyr Baelish and Lysa, Petyr replaces Marwyn with Ser Lothor Brune as the captain of the guards.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Ser Marwyn is now the captain of the guards at the Gates of the Moon. He goes to the Eyrie with Lord Nestor and Ser Albar Royce to question Petyr and Alayne Stone about Lysa's death.

Lord Benedar Belmore is one of the six Lords Declarant. The first to arrive at the Gates of the Moon, Benedar sends word that he is blocking food supplies to the Eyrie. Petyr does not consider Lord Belmore as a threat, since he is corrupt and can be bought.

After the parley at the Eyrie, Lord Belmore befriends Petyr and attends the wedding of Lord Lyonel Corbray in Gulltown.

House Belmore at the end of the third century[ ]

The known Belmores during the timespan of the events described in A Song of Ice and Fire are:

Lord Benedar Belmore, Lord of Strongsong. A fat grey-bearded man.

With unspecified familiar relationship with the lord of the house there is also:

Ser Marwyn Belmore, a knight. Captain of the guards at the Eyrie, then at the Gates of the Moon.

{Belmore}, the wife of Ser Ronnel Arryn and the mother of Ser Elbert Arryn.
