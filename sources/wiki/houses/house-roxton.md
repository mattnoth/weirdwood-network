## Summary

House Roxton of the Ring is a noble house from the Ring in the Reach. Possibly a house of landed knights, they blazon their arms with a saltire of interlocked golden rings upon a sky blue field, according to semi-canon sources. Their words are not known. They possess a Valyrian steel sword called Orphan-Maker.

History[ ]

The Roxtons trace their ancestry back to Andal adventurers given lands and wives by the Three Sage Kings of House Gardener during the coming of the Andals to the Reach.

Ser Lorence Roxton was a victor in the War for the White Cloaks.

Ser Jon Roxton wielded the ancient Valyrian sword Orphan-Maker during the Dance of the Dragons. After killing Hugh Hammer, Bold Jon was slain at the Second Battle of Tumbleton.

House Roxton's seat and coat of arms  may be a reference to The Lord of the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien.

Notes[ ]

↑ Jon Roxton, head of the house at the time of the Dance of the Dragons, is always referred as a knight in Fire & Blood, except once when he claimed the bedchamber and wife of the late Lord Footly. Previously, Lorence Roxton was said to be the only victor of the War for the White Cloaks who came from a "lordly house", but it is possible the term "lordly" is used in that context to indicate noble houses in general.
