## Full Text

House Costayne of Three Towers is a noble house from Three Towers in the Reach, sworn to the Hightowers. Their castle is located on the southern coast of the Whispering Sound.

The Costaynes blazon their shield with quartered: a silver chalice on black, a black rose on yellow. Their words do not appear in the text.

History[ ]

Lady Elinor Costayne, the wife of Ser Theo Bolling, was forced to marry King Maegor I Targaryen seven days after Theo's execution in 47 AC.

Lord Owen Costayne of Three Towers declared for Princess Rhaenyra Targaryen during the Dance of the Dragons, going against his Hightower overlords in 129 AC. He was mortally wounded by Ser Jon Roxton's Valyrian steel black blade, Orphan-Maker, during the Battle of the Honeywine in 130 AC.

Along with Lords Beesbury and Redwyne, Lord Costayne accompanied the High Septon to King's Landing for the coronation of King Aegon III Targaryen and his wedding to Queen Jaehaera Targaryen in 131 AC. After the death of Queen Jaehaera in 133 AC, a Costayne girl was among the names suggested by Lady Samantha Tarly as possible brides for the king. Ser Leo Costayne invaded the Iron Islands after the death of Lord Dalton Greyjoy, but was slain at Great Wyk in 134 AC.

Lord Costayne fought for the Blackfyres during the First Blackfyre Rebellion in 196 AC. The Costayne force that participated in the Battle of the Redgrass Field fought on the left of Daemon Blackfyre's host. Ser Tommen Costayne participated in the tourney at Ashford Meadow in 209 AC. The son of the Blackfyre-supporting Lord Costayne participated in Lord Ambrose Butterwell's wedding festivities. This Lord Costayne was married to the daughter of Lord Ambrose by his first wife, and he considered himself better born than Lord Shawney. Lord Costayne was defeated in the wedding tourney at Whitewalls by Ser Glendon Flowers. After the Second Blackfyre Rebellion was put down in 212 AC, a man in service to Lord Vyrwel claimed to have been one of Lord Brynden Rivers's spies. He was promptly killed by a man in service to Lord Costayne.

Long Tom Costayne served in the Kingsguard for sixty years.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

House Costayne is a reference by George R. R. Martin to the author Thomas B. Costain. The family's arms reference two of Costain's novels, The Black Rose and The Silver Chalice, that Martin recommended to read.
