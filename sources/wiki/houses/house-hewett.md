## Full Text

House Hewett of Oakenshield rules over Oakenshield, one of the Shield Islands of the Reach.

Lord Hewett's castle, with thick walls and studded oaken gates evoking the ancient arms of the house, sits above the harbor of Lord Hewett's Town, which is a relatively big port.

They blazon their arms with an oaken escutcheon studded with iron, a field bendy of undulating blue and white. Their words are not mentioned in the books.

History[ ]

The Shield Islands are a considerable sea strength and were the first line of defense during the days when the ironborn pirates tried to sail up the Mander to sack the wealth of the Reach.

Recent Events[ ]

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Ser Glendon Hewett goes to Castle Black with Janos Slynt and Alliser Thorne, and he drags Jon Snow from his bed to accuse him of betrayal.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

King Euron Greyjoy and his captains celebrate the taking of the Shields by ironborn in Lord Hewett's castle. Lord Humfrey Hewett is muzzled and tied on his chair during the feast, his wife and daughters forced to serve the unwanted guests. His bastard daughter Falia Flowers becomes Euron's lover and suggests the idea to make them serve naked. At least one of Lord Hewett's daughters is raped during the feast, by Left-Hand Lucas Codd. The island of Oakenshield is given by Euron to Nute the Barber.

The tidings that reach King's Landing afterwards report Lord Hewett's death.

House Hewett at the end of the third century[ ]

The known Hewetts during the timespan of the events described in A Song of Ice and Fire are:

Lord {Humfrey Hewett}, Lord of Oakenshield. Reported dead after the capture of Oakenshield.

Lady Hewett, his wife. She is handsome though somewhat stout.

Sons.

Seven ladies aged from ten to twenty-five, Lord Hewett's daughters and good-daughters.

Falia Flowers, his bastard daughter.

Without specified connection to the main branch, there is also:

Ser Glendon Hewett, a member of the Night's Watch, serving at Eastwatch-by-the-Sea.
