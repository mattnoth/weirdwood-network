Appearance[ ]

Bolton guardsmen usually wear fur cloaks over chainmail, while their sergeants wear pale pink cloaks decorated with red teardrops.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Arya Stark kills a Bolton guard at Harrenhal and whispers valar morghulis - by Tim Tsang ©

After learning that Lord Roose Bolton plans to leave her with Vargo Hoat when he departs Harrenhal, Arya Stark kills a tall, thin Bolton guard at one of the castle's postern gates when she escapes with Gendry and Hot Pie.

According to Lady Donella Hornwood, Roose's bastard, Ramsay Snow, is massing men at the Dreadfort. Ramsay later brings six hundred men, whom he calls his father's garrison, to Winterfell, where they betray the host of northmen assembled by Ser Rodrik Cassel.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

When Lord Bolton hosts Ser Jaime Lannister and Brienne of Tarth at Harrenhal, the castle's Hall of a Hundred Hearths is guarded by a dozen spearmen. Roose tasks Steelshanks Walton with escorting Jaime to King's Landing. "Arya Stark" is later escorted by Walton and his men from King's Landing to Lord Bolton at the Twins.

Sandor Clegane brings Arya Stark to the Twins during the wedding of Lord Edmure Tully to Roslin Frey. Outside the castles, they are stopped by a Bolton sergeant who is more wary than Ser Donnel Haigh.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

The Bastard's Boys are men-at-arms who follow Ramsay Bolton and guard his new wife, "Arya", actually Jeyne Poole.

Walton commands Roose's guards while in Barrowton and Winterfell. Bolton spearmen are forced to intervene when Freys clash with Manderlys.
