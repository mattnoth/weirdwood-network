Pre-production[ ]

House of the Dragon was officially renewed for a second season on the 26th August 2022, five days after the pilot of the series aired.

On the 31 August 2022, Miguel Sapochnik announced he will not return as co-showrunner and director for season 2. He will remain an executive producer for the duration of the series, however. Ryan Condal will serve as the show's sole showrunner and continue to work closely with George R. R. Martin. Veteran Game of Thrones director, Alan Taylor, has been hired by HBO to be a director and executive producer for season 2.

Executive producer Jocelyn Diaz will not return for season 2. Sara Hess and Vince Gerardis have been confirmed to continue on as executive producers, however. Melissa Bernstein, Kevin de la Noy, and Loni Peristere have also been added as executive producers.

Production[ ]

Season 2 started filming on the April 11, 2023 and ended on September 29, 2023. The production was not affected by the WGA's strike as the final scripts were finished before the strike began, and notes from both HBO and George R. R. Martin were already addressed in earlier drafts.

Promotion[ ]

On 2 December 2023, HBO released the first official teaser for season 2. On 21 March 2024, HBO released two trailers focusing on the two factions of the Dance of the Dragons, the blacks and the greens. The premiere date of the season was revealed at the end of those trailers. On 14 May 2024, HBO released the final official trailer.

Title sequence[ ]

The opening credits for this season features the weaving of a tapestry depicting the history of the Targaryen family from before the Doom to the events from the season 1 finale. As the season progress new scenes are added to the tapestry; the funeral procession of Prince Jaehaerys Targaryen, the hanging of the Red Keep ratcatchers, the aftermath of the Battle at Rook's Rest, and a lign dividing the Targaryen dragons.
