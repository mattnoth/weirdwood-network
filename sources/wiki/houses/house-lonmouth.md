History[ ]

Ser Joffrey Lonmouth, a close friend of Ser Laenor Velaryon, was mortally wounded by Ser Criston Cole at the wedding tourney of Laenor and Princess Rhaenyra Targaryen. Laenor named his son, Prince Joffrey Velaryon, after his late friend.

Ser Richard Lonmouth was a former squire and close personal friend of Prince Rhaegar Targaryen. At a feast at the tourney at Harrenhal, Lord Robert Baratheon outdrank a member of House Lonmouth, most likely Richard.

House Lonmouth at the end of the third century[ ]

The known Lonmouths during the timespan of the events described in A Song of Ice and Fire are:

Ser Richard Lonmouth, called the "Knight of Skulls and Kisses", a companion and former squire of Prince Rhaegar Targaryen.

Historical Members[ ]

Ser Joffrey Lonmouth, called the "Knight of Kisses", a favorite of Ser Laenor Velaryon.
