About[ ]

The play is likely based on recent events that have taken place in Westeros. It includes two kings, the fat one and the boy. Izembaro plays the fat one, which is not a large part, but has a fine speech as he lies dying, and a splendid fight with a demonic boar before that. The play opens in a lichyard. There is a wedding scene. There is a rape scene in the second act.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

In Braavos, the mummers of the Gate perform The Bloody Hand in honor of Harys Swyft, an envoy from the Seven Kingdoms.
