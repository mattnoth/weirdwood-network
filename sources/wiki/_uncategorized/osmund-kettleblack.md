Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Osmund Kettleblack

Osmund stands six feet and six inches, most of it muscle. He has a hook nose, bushy eyebrows, and a spade-shaped beard described as brown or black. Osmund has broad shoulders but narrow buttocks. His chest is covered with black hair. Osmund thinks that he and Osfryd are stronger than their younger brother Osney. Like his two brothers, Osmund is illiterate.

Osmund and his brothers are amiable, being popular with both knights and servants, and amorous with serving women. Tyrion Lannister considers all three brothers to be deceitful and unsavory, but none as dangerous as Bronn. Petyr Baelish also considers the brothers to be treacherous. Ser Jaime Lannister thinks of Osmund as a dolt and less skilled than Ser Loras Tyrell.

History[ ]

Osmund's father, Oswell Kettleblack, has been in the service of Lord Petyr Baelish for a long time. Osmund and his brothers have known Petyr since they were children.

Ser Osmund served in the Stepstones and the Disputed Lands with the Gallant Men fighting for Lys and then Tyrosh. He claims to have been knighted on a battlefield by Ser Robert Stone, who is now dead.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

In order to match her brother Tyrion's personal power base of sellswords, Queen Cersei Lannister calls on the High Septon at the Great Sept of Baelor as cover for secret meetings with the Kettleblack brothers, who are supposed to hire other sellswords for her. Unbeknownst to Cersei, however, the Kettlebacks are already being paid by Bronn, Tyrion's captain. Ser Osmund sells Cersei's secrets and provides Bronn with information about Cersei's moves.

After Ser Boros Blount is stripped of his white cloak for cowardice on the Rosby road, Cersei names Osmund to the Kingsguard. Tyrion thinks the appointment gives him another agent close to King Joffrey I Baratheon.

Osmund and his brothers become great favorites in the Red Keep, getting along with both servants and highborns with their easygoing manner and their humor. It is rumored that they get best along with the serving wenches, although Osmund's position in the Kingsguard implies celibacy. Osmund eventually takes the position next to Joffrey that used to be reserved for Sandor Clegane, and Sansa Stark hears servants saying Osmund is as strong as the Hound, but younger and faster. However, Sansa doubts this, as she has never heard of the Kettleblacks before Osmund was named to the Kingsguard. 

Osmund and Ser Meryn Trant guard King Joffrey during the Battle of the Blackwater. When Joffrey goes off to catapult the Antler Men into the Blackwater Rush, Tyrion warns Osmund to keep the king safe and in the city. When Osney tells Cersei that the Mud Gate has come under attack by a ram, however, she issues an order for Osmund to bring the king from the city back to the Red Keep. When Osney points out Tyrion's diametrically opposite orders, Cersei threatens that she will send the Kettleblacks into the middle of the battle if they do not do as told. The flight of the king from the battlefield provokes some members of the City Watch to mutiny. Osmund later conveys the news to Osney that the defenders have been saved by the combined forces of the Lannisters and the Tyrells.

After the battle, Osmund and the other remaining members of the Kingsguard spend hours knighting more than six hundred men. While later standing guard, he leers at Sansa Stark when she visits the Red Keep's godswood. While Tyrion is recuperating from wounds suffered at the Blackwater, he regrets having trusted Osmund.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

  

Osmund by David Sourwine © Fantasy Flight Games

Osmund's brothers, Osney and Osfryd, receive their knighthoods after the Battle of the Blackwater. Cersei has the Kettleblacks retrieve Prince Tommen Baratheon from Rosby. Varys informs Tyrion that Cersei has been flirtatious toward Osmund.

Ser Osmund is one of the Kingsguard assigned to bring Sansa to the royal sept for her marriage to Tyrion Lannister. While Meryn's mere look promises violence, Osmund speaks to Sansa kindly and convinces her to come along quietly. During the feast, Osmund laughs when Tyrion mocks King Joffrey.

When Joffrey chokes at his own wedding feast, Osmund rips open the king's collar in an attempt to save the boy king.

Osmund stands guard outside the royal sept while Cersei is inside with Joffrey's corpse. He does not recognize Ser Jaime Lannister when Cersei's disheveled brother arrives, and Osmund is forced to apologize to the Lord Commander of the Kingsguard after disrespecting him.

Osmund is called as a witness for the prosecution during the trial of Tyrion for the death of King Joffrey, but his testimony is made up, infuriating Tyrion. 

Osmund is amiable towards Jaime when the Kingsguard meet in White Sword Tower. Osmund jokingly accuses the new High Septon of Joffrey's murder, and he laughs when Boros is appointed the food taster for King Tommen I Baratheon. Jaime is skeptical of Osmund's claimed history and his knighthood. Osmund brings Ser Gregor Clegane his shield when the Mountain duels Prince Oberyn Martell in Tyrion's trial by combat.

Lord Petyr Baelish reveals to Sansa that he had Oswell send his sons to King's Landing when Bronn was hiring sellswords for Tyrion, and thus the Kettleblack brothers have been secretly in his employ all along, unbeknownst to Cersei and Tyrion. However, Petyr believes the brothers are too treacherous to have been trusted with his scheme to kill Joffrey, and that Osmund has become especially unreliable since he was named to the Kingsguard.

When Jaime helps Tyrion escape from the black cells, Tyrion learns from Jaime that his first wife, Tysha, had not been a whore as claimed by their father, Lord Tywin Lannister, but was as she had said: an innocent, orphaned crofter's daughter. Furious, Tyrion tells Jaime that Cersei has been having sex with Osmund, Ser Lancel Lannister, and probably Moon Boy. Tyrion murders Tywin, the Hand of the King, before disappearing from the Red Keep.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Ser Osmund and Ser Boros Blount bring the news to Cersei that Tywin is dead. Cersei has Osmund escort her to the Tower of the Hand, where she finds that Tyrion has also killed Shae in Tywin's bed. Queen Cersei orders Ser Osmund and his brothers to secretly dispose of Shae's corpse. Cersei tells them that what they do with the corpse is of no concern, as long as nobody knows she was ever there. Cersei also orders Ser Boros Blount and the Kettleblacks to kill two sleeping gaolers, which upsets Jaime.

Osmund guards one of the doors of the Small Hall for the feast after King Tommen weds Margaery Tyrell. Afterward, Cersei has Osmund guard her while she has the Tower of the Hand destroyed with wildfire.

In order to ruin Margaery, Cersei plots for the young queen to be found in bed with Osney by Osmund. Osney is unable to seduce Margaery, however.

Tyrion's claim that Cersei has been sleeping with Osmund bothers Jaime. Cersei wants Osmund to command the Kingsguard when Jaime departs for the siege of Riverrun, but the Lord Commander insists that Ser Loras Tyrell have command instead. Jaime considers gelding Osmund and sending him to the Wall.

Osmund is among Cersei's escort when she visits the Great Sept of Baelor to visit the High Sparrow.

Osmund supports Cersei when she interrogates Grand Maester Pycelle, and the Queen Regent has Osmund stay out of sight for when his brother Osney's accusation of adultery against Margaery is made public. However, Cersei and Osney both end up imprisoned by the Faith of the Seven as well.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Tyrion's claim about Cersei and Osmund continues to bother Jaime.

Cersei falsely confesses to bedding all three of the Kettleblack brothers. As soon as Ser Kevan Lannister, the Lord Regent, hears this information, he has Osfryd and Osmund thrown into the dungeon of the Red Keep. Kevan plans to send them to the Wall if they plead guilty. If they plead innocent, they will face Ser Robert Strong in trial by combat.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

Osmund's name is twice misspelled as "Oswald" in A Storm of Swords. In A Dance with Dragons, Penny refers to Osmund's father Oswell as Oswald, though in this case it is intentional.
