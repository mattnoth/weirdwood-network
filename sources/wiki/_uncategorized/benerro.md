Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Benerro

Benerro is a tall and thin man with a gaunt face and a lipless mouth. His skin is white as milk. Red flame tattoos cover his cheeks, chin, and shaven head. His high voice carries well.

The High Priest preaches from a red stone pillar which connects to a lofty terrace of the Temple of the Lord of Light by slender stone bridge. Benerro can make flames leap from his fingers, and can trace fire in the air to form Valyrian glyphs. He also claims to be able to see the future in his flames.

Moqorro considers Benerro to be a righteous slave. The ruling triarchs of Volantis live in great fear of Benerro. 

History[ ]

Benerro was bought by the red temple while enslaved as a child.

Recent Events[ ]

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

At the Temple of the Lord of Light in Volantis, Benerro preaches that Daenerys Targaryen is Azor Ahai reborn, and he encourages many to support her. He says that Volantis will burn if the triarchs take up arms against Daenerys, and that they have "turned away from the light". Thousands of slaves and freedmen crowd the temple plaza to hear Benerro preach every night. 

In Selhorys, outside a red temple, Tyrion Lannister and Haldon overhear a red priest relating Benerro's word of Daenerys to a throng of people, mostly slaves. Haldon thinks they may be able to use Benerro's preachings to advance the cause of Prince Aegon Targaryen, and goes to find more information from Qavo Nogarys. The customs officer tells them that Benerro's "rantings" have instigated fighting between the followers of R'hllor and those who worship other gods. He says that Benerro and the other red priests should be quiet, or there will be retaliation. 

Benerro's preaching unnerves the triarchs, because four out of every five inhabitants of Volantis are slaves, and they fear an uprising. However, the triarchs are powerless to stop him, because half of the city guard, the tiger cloaks, worship R'hllor. Triarch Malaquo attempts to hire the Golden Company to kill Benerro and purge the temple of his followers, but the sellswords refuse.  

Not long after the arrival of Tyrion and Ser Jorah Mormont in Volantis, they make their way to the Long Bridge, and pass the red temple as Benerro is preaching. The crowd is packed solid, and they watch the priest address his followers. When Benerro's voice rises to a crescendo, Tyrion recognizes Doom and Darkness among the Valyrian glyphs of flame which leap from his fingers. Benerro points at the Black Wall and preaches that Daenerys is in danger, and he riles up the crowd. The mood of the crowd unnerves Tyrion, reminding him of the day of the riot of King's Landing. He thinks it is a very bad idea for Aegon to try to ally with Benerro, and hopes that Jon Connington has better sense than Haldon.

Benerro secretly sends his right hand, Moqorro, as an emissary to Daenerys in Meereen. Moqorro and soldiers of the Fiery Hand travel on the ship Selaesori Qhoran, even though the ship's destination is Qarth, because Benerro sees in the flames that the ship will never reach that city. The trading cog is eventually ruined by a storm in the Gulf of Grief, and Moqorro is lost overboard. Moqorro is rescued from the sea by a longship of Victarion Greyjoy's Iron Fleet that is traveling to Meereen.
