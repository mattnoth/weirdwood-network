## Full Text

Fleet from Volantis, by Tomasz Jedruszek © Fantasy Flight Games

The Volantene fleet is the fleet of the Free City of Volantis. While Volantis is powerful at sea, the city's greatest might is on land.

Three triarchs are elected annually to command the fleets and armies of Volantis. Often green in color, Volantene galleys are rowed by slaves.

History[ ]

At the start of the Century of Blood, Volantene fleets and armies gained control of Lys, Myr, and the southern reaches of the Rhoyne. The imperialist tigers overreached by attacking Tyrosh, however, and Volantis was pushed back by a coalition of Free Cities and Westerosi. Aegon Targaryen, Lord of Dragonstone, burned a Volantene fleet preparing to recapture Lys with the dragonflame of Balerion the Black Dread, and fire galleys from Qohor and Norvos destroyed much of a Volantene fleet at Dagger Lake. A Volantene fleet also attempted to reclaim the Valyrian peninsula, but it vanished in the Smoking Sea.

Triarch Marqelo Tagaros dispatched a squadron of ships toward Valyria to search for the golden fleet of Tommen II Lannister, King of the Rock, but the Volantenes returned having found no trace of the missing westermen.

The triarchs of Volantis have sent fleets to clear out pirate dens from the Stepstones and corsairs from the Basilisk Isles.

Recent Events[ ]

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Amid the Targaryen campaign in Slaver's Bay, envoys from Yunkai travel to Volantis to hire swords against Daenerys Targaryen, Queen of Meereen. Tyrion Lannister learns that Volantis is preparing for war against the dragon queen. Prince Quentyn Martell sees the harbor of Volantis crowded with warships, trading ships, and foreign vessels.

When the Golden Company nears Volantis, Lysono Maar sneaks into the city and sees teeming with galleys, cogs, and carracks. Lord Jon Connington believes the triarchs of Volantis would be willing to help transport the sellswords to Westeros to remove them from their city's vicinity. A great Volantene fleet stops at Lys while transporting the mercenaries to the Seven Kingdoms, but the landing of the Golden Company in the stormlands is disrupted by storms in the narrow sea.

Lord Captain Victarion Greyjoy leads the Iron Fleet toward Slaver's Bay to find Daenerys. When the ironborn briefly stop in Volantis for food and water, Victarion sees numerous Volantene galleys taking on provisions. The Volantenes have at least three hundred ships and possibly up to five hundred, including large dromonds of war. Victarion wants his smaller fleet to take Daenerys from Meereen before the Volantenes reach Slaver's Bay. 

Daenerys disappears from Daznak's Pit on the back of Drogon. According to Skahaz mo Kandaq, Daenerys's new husband, King Hizdahr zo Loraq, is conspiring with the Wise Masters of Yunkai. The plotters want to kill Rhaegal and Viserion before the Volantene fleet arrives, and they would then open the gates of Meereen to the Volantene soldiers.
