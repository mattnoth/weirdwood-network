## Full Text

Styr is the Magnar of Thenn and leader of the Thenns, a tribe of free folk.

In the television adaptation Game of Thrones, Styr is portrayed by Yuri Kolokolnikov.

Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Styr

Styr is a tall, lean man. He is clean shaven and bald, with grey eyes and a straight nose. Styr has lost both of his ears. 

The Magnar of Thenn rules with an iron fist and commands absolute loyalty from his men. He is seen more as a god than a man by the Thenns. Coming from a valley in the Frostfangs, Styr has never seen the Wall. He speaks the Old Tongue as well as the Common Tongue.

Styr wears bronze scale armor and a helm. He wields a weirwood spear with an ornate bronze head and slings a two-handed greatsword across his back in a leather sheath. The Magnar also carries a long knife.

History[ ]

Mance Rayder had to defeat Styr three times before the Magnar agreed to support him as King-beyond-the-Wall.

Recent Events[ ]

A Storm of Swords[ ]

When Rattleshirt brings Jon Snow to Mance Rayder, Jon thinks Styr is Mance, mistakenly calling him "Your Grace". Styr is wary of Jon, who claims to have deserted the Night's Watch.

Mance tasks Styr, together with Jarl, to scale the Wall and take Castle Black from the rear, and the King-Beyond-the-Wall has Jon accompany them. Styr has a hundred Thenns, five times as many raiders as Jarl. Jarl and Styr share the command, although Styr, who commands significantly more men than Jarl, resents this and often acts as the sole commander. Styr distrusts Ygritte's support for Jon. In a cave near Greyguard, Jarl and Styr interrogate Jon about patrols of the Night's Watch.

Jarl falls to his death while attempting to scale the Wall. Styr leads the surviving wildlings in descending at Greyguard. Styr orders Jon to kill a captive northman at Queenscrown, but Jon refuses and escapes the wildlings. Jon is able to warn the Night's Watch of the coming attack.

Styr leads his men in an attack on Castle Black. During the fighting, Jon sees the Magnar climbing the barricade constructed by the defenders. The Night's Watch trap Styr and his Thenns between fires on the steps, however. Styr is last seen as a portion of the Wall collapses on the lower third of the stair.

Stannis Baratheon informs Jon that the new Magnar of Thenn was taken captive during the battle beneath the Wall.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Styr's son, Sigorn, is the new Magnar of Thenn.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Jon, now the new Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, fears that Sigorn blames him for his father's death, and he recollects that Styr put Mole's Town to the torch. Jon arranges the wedding of Sigorn and Alys Karstark.
