## Full Text

Queen Rhaenys striking Grand Defiance, by Douglas Wheatley ©

The Velaryon fleet is a fleet in the service of House Velaryon of Driftmark. The Velaryons once had the most powerful fleet of the Seven Kingdoms and formed the bulk of the royal fleet.

Due to their tradition of seafarers and close ties to House Targaryen, the Velaryons were favored by the royal House and trusted with the maritime matters of the realm. So many Lords of the Tides served on the small council as master of ships throughout the first century of Targaryen rule, that the office was widely seen as almost hereditary.

History[ ]

Aegon the Conqueror[ ]

During the Conquest, the ships of House Velaryon carried Aegon the Conqueror’s soldiers across Blackwater Bay to Aegon's landing at the mouth of the Blackwater Rush. Lord Daemon Velaryon was made master of ships by Aegon, the first of many Velaryons to held that office. He led the fleet against the force of the Vale of Arryn and their Braavosi allies, but died in the battle in the waters off Gulltown.

The Velaryon fleet, led by Lord Aethan Velaryon, was used to ferry men to the Stepstones in two campaigns against the pirate king Sargoso Saan in 29 AC and 30 AC.

Jaehaerys I Targaryen[ ]

When his nephew, Prince Jaehaerys Targaryen, put forth his claim to the Iron Throne in 48 AC, Lord Daemon Velaryon, the admiral of the royal fleet, was the first of the great lords to forsake King Maegor I Targaryen, taking the royal fleet with him, and many other lords followed his example.

During Jaehaerys' minority, several lords visited him at Dragonstone, to the displeasure of the Hand of the King, Lord Rogar Baratheon. Rogar asked if Daemon could create a blockade around the island with the Velaryon fleet, in order to prevent these "lords lickspittle" to curry favor with the young king. Daemon refused, however.

With the death of the High Septon in 54 AC, King Jaehaerys feared the new High Septon would not be as supportive of his politics as his predecessor had been. While discussing the matter with his small council, Lord Daemon proposed to sent the Velaryon fleet to Oldtown as a show of force and insure the Most Devout would elect an High Septon which aligned with the king's views.

The Sea Snake[ ]

  

Corlys Velaryon by Eve Ventrue ©

Before inheriting the lordship of his House from his grandfather, Ser Corlys Velaryon made nine Great Voyages in the East aboard the ship that gave him his nickname, the Sea Snake. On his last voyage, Corlys bought twenty ships at Qarth and loaded them with valuable goods. Despite losing six ships at sea during the return trip, Corlys had augmented the size of his fleet and returned in Westeros with vast amount of wealth.

On the ninth day of the third moon of 92 AC, Lord Corlys set sail from Driftmark with his fleet for Tarth, to aid Prince Aemon Targaryen in fighting the Myrish exiles who had invaded the island. During the campaign, Aemon died, leading King Jaehaerys I Targaryen to appoint a new heir to the throne. He chose his second son, Prince Baelon, over Corlys' wife and daughter of Aemon, Princess Rhaenys. Furious over this decision, Corlys gave up his admiralty and his place on the king's small council and returned to Driftmark with his wife. Ever so passionate about ships, Lord Corlys spent the following years on Driftmark building many merchantmen and trading galleys thanks to the wealth he had amassed in the East. The shipping activity on the island increased, especially in the thriving towns of Hull and Spicetown, allowing the Velaryons to become for a time the richest family of the Seven Kingdoms.

After the death of Prince Baelon in 101 AC, the issue of succession arose anew. The nobility of Westeros assembled in a Great Council at Harrenhal to choose a new heir for the Old King. Rumors spread that Lord Corlys was readying a fleet to defend the rights of his son, Laenor.

In 106 AC, the Velaryon fleet assisted Prince Daemon Targaryen in his campaign in the Stepstones against Admiral Craghas Drahar of the Triarchy. Lord Corlys hoped to establish a Westerosi presence in the region in order to get rid of the toll that the Triarchy charges its merchant ships. Three years later, the Velaryon fleet was firmly established in the waters of the Stepstones and ensured the circulation of ships there. Fighting resumed quickly enough, however, against a new enemy: Racallio Ryndoon.

In 113 AC, King Viserys I Targaryen decided to marry his heir, Princess Rhaenyra Targaryen, to Laenor Velaryon. By this match the king hoped to regain the friendship of the Velaryons and their powerful fleet.

Dance of the Dragons[ ]

In 129 AC, King Viserys I Targaryen died and the civil war known as the Dance of the Dragons broke out. Lord Corlys Velaryon was the main supporter of Princess Rhaenyra Targaryen. With the control of the Velaryon fleet, Rhaenyra had superiority at sea against Aegon II Targaryen and the greens. At the end of the first black council, a strategy was defined: lacking the strength to take King's Landing by storm, it was decided the Velaryon fleet would be used to block the Gullet and prevent maritime traffic throughout Blackwater Bay, and provoke Aegon II to launch a reckless assault against the Velaryon fleet. Lord Corlys would command the fleet while his wife, Princess Rhaenys Targaryen, would fly overhead with her dragon, Meleys, to prevent any attack by another fleet or by a dragon.

The Velaryon blockade worked for the blacks, as merchants in King's Landing complained daily of it to Aegon II. His Hand, Ser Otto Hightower, started negotiations with Dalton Greyjoy, Lord of the Iron Islands]], and with the Triarchy, whose fleets combined with the royal fleet in the capital could rival the naval power of the Velaryons.

Towards the end of 129, the two youngest sons of Rhaenyra, Princes Aegon and Viserys, sailed aboard the Gay Abandon to Pentos where the Prince of the city agreed to foster them during the war. Lord Corlys sent seven of his ships to escort them. On the way, however, Lord Corlys's ships were sunk or taken when they came across the ninety warships fleet of the Triarchy led by Sharako Lohar of Lys. Aegon managed to flee on Stormcloud, but Viserys was captured and given up for dead.

On the fifth day of the new year, 130 AC, the rest of the Velaryon fleet was surprised by the Triarchy fleet north and south of Dragonstone. The Velaryon fleet won the terrible Battle of the Gullet but lost a third of its strength in the conflict.

In 130, Rhaenyra took the capital and ascended the Iron Throne. During her half-year rule over King's Landing, she had Corlys arrested for treason, after he warned his heir, Ser Addam Velaryon, to flee the city with his dragon, Seasmoke. This event cost her the support of the Velaryon fleet. With Corlys imprisoned in King's Landing and Addam dead at the Second Battle of Tumbleton, the command of the Velaryon fleet passed on Addam's brother, Ser Alyn Velaryon.

After Aegon II took Dragonstone and executed Rhaenyra, he sent his terms of peace to Alyn, promising pardon for him and his House in exchange of his allegiance to the king. Alyn refused to comply, however, and was even in mind of attacking Dragonstone. The stalemate was resolved when Lord Corlys swore allegiance to the cause of Aegon II before the Iron Throne. Alyn accepted his grandfather's choice, and escorted his new king with the Velaryon fleet from Dragonstone to King's Landing. Aegon II was found dead of poison in the aftermath of the Battle of the Kingsroad.

Regency of Aegon III[ ]

  

Alyn Velaryon by Chillyravenart ©

At the beginning of the regency of Aegon III in 131 AC, the Hand of King, Ser Tyland Lannister, launched the construction of fifty new galleys of war for the royal fleet. He justified this decision by saying that it would revive the activity of the shipyards and that these ships would help defend the city against the Triarchy. Many guessed that Tyland's true purpose was to lessen the Crown's dependence on the Velaryon fleet, however.

In 132 AC, Ser Alyn Velaryon became Lord of the Tides and the undisputed commander of the Velaryon fleet after the death of Lord Corlys Velaryon and the recognition of his rule by his distant cousins, Daemion and Daeron Velaryon.

In 133 AC, the Velaryon fleet was to join the modest royal fleet commanded by Ser Gedmund Peake, by order of the new Hand of King, Lord Unwin Peake. Together, they were ordered to go to the Stepstones and fight the pirate Racallio Ryndoon, already struggling with the Free Cities during the Daughters' War. Alyn amassed sixty galleys of war, thirty longships and more than a hundred cogs and great cogs, commanding the Velaryon fleet from his Queen Rhaenys. On their way, they stopped at Tarth, where Gedmund and Alyn argued about the strategy to adopt when they learned that Racallio had allied with the Sealord of Braavos and the Archon of Tyrosh. Gedmund wished to wait further instructions from the Hand, while Alyn insisted on attacking them immediately. The impatient Alyn decided to attack alone with his fleet, sinking many Braavosi ships and capturing eight galleys and eleven cogs which he added to his fleet. He took ninety-nine prisoners and seized food, drinks, weapons, money, and even an elephant. Alyn lost only three ships, including the True Heart of his cousin, Daeron.

As a result of Lord Alyn's attack against the Braavosi, a war between the Seven Kingdoms and Braavos threatened to break out. Lord Peake, however, sent the Velaryon fleet to the other side of the continent, in the Sunset Sea, to subdue Lord Dalton Greyjoy and his ironborn. Dalton had been assassinated by Tess by the time Alyn arrived in the westerlands, however.

In 135 AC, the Velaryon fleet was solicited by the Hand of the King, Lord Thaddeus Rowan, to transport an army to theVale of Arryn, where a succession crisis had begun after the death of Lady Jeyne Arryn the previous year. The sellsails hired by Isembard Arryn were easily beaten by the Velaryon fleet at Gulltown.

In the middle of 136 AC, Alyn sailed to Volantis by way of Pentos, Tyrosh, and Lys, with a large fleet of merchantmen he had amassed along with a dozen of his war galleys to guard them. Alyn intended to restore the fortune of his House, partly lost during the Dance of the Dragons and the bankruptcy of the Rogare Bank.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Lord Monford Velaryon answers Stannis Baratheon's summons when he crowns himself king and calls his banners. Monford's contribution to Stannis's royal fleet includes the large ships Pride of Driftmark, Bold Laughter, Harridan, and Seahorse.

Monford burns with his ship at the Battle of the Blackwater. Before sinking, the Pride of Driftmark manages to take down two ships of the royal fleet.
