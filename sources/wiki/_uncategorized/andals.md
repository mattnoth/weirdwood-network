Culture[ ]

Religion[ ]

The Andals followed the Faith of the Seven, believing that the Seven once walked the hills of Andalos in human form. Displaying a seven-pointed star, they brought their religion across the narrow sea to Westeros. They chiseled axes and stars in the Vale, although this practice declined as they expanded beyond the Vale.

Social hierarchy[ ]

  

The Andals brought knightly culture to Westeros, by Tomasz Jedruszek © Fantasy Flight Games

The Andals were led by war chiefs, warlords, and petty kings. The Faith of the Seven opposed incest and slavery. Regarding inheritance, Andal tradition holds that the rights of a trueborn son come before those of a daughter.

Knighthood came to Westeros with the Andals. Trial of seven was practiced in Andalos.

Military[ ]

The Andals are said to have learned ironworking from the Rhoynar. Andals used iron weapons and plate against the native tribes of what would become Andalos, and their steel swords and iron ringmail also surpassed the bronze equipment of the First Men of Westeros. Andals were skilled craftsmen, especially their blacksmiths and stonemasons. 

Ancient Andal warriors painted and carved seven-pointed stars on their flesh. The Andals brought the warrior tradition of the knight to Westeros. 

Unlike the First Men, the Andals were fearless seafarers who used longships.

Like the First Men, the early Andals built square towers and keeps, with round fortifications coming later. Andals built walled towns and stout stone-and-timber castles to protect their coasts. Andals also fortified some of their septs.

History[ ]

Andals in Essos[ ]

  

Hugor of the Hill, from Game of Thrones Blu-ray

The Andals were a tall and fair-haired people said to have originated in the Axe of northern Essos, although some say they came from south of the Silver Sea. They migrated and established Andalos in northwestern Essos, stretching south as far as the Flatlands and the Velvet Hills and engaging in conflict with the tribes that inhabited those lands, such as the so-called "hairy men". Legend tells that they were visited by the incarnations of the Seven, who crowned their first king, Hugor of the Hill. They learned the art of working iron from the Rhoynar who lived along the Rhoyne.

Septons and septas teach that the Seven had promised Hugor and his descendants great kingdoms in a foreign land, and this is why the Andals eventually left Essos for Westeros. Maesters, however, generally believe that the Andals fled because of the expanding Valyrian Freehold. The Valyrians established Myr in place of a walled Andal town, and many of the Valyrian colonists of Pentos intermarried with local Andals. Archmaester Perestan suggests that Norvos was originally founded by Andals. Before the Scouring of Lorath by Valyrian dragonlords, the Lorathi isles were ruled by Qarlon the Great, who desired to be King of All Andals.

Migration to Westeros[ ]

Main article: Coming of the Andals

It is unknown when the Andals sailed west across the narrow sea from Andalos; some sources date the coming of the Andals to Westeros to six thousand years ago. According to the True History, it occurred four thousand years ago, and some maesters instead claim it was only two thousand years ago.

Andals first landed in the Fingers and attacked the First Men living in the Vale. They burned weirwood groves, hacked down heart trees, and slaughtered the children of the forest. Everywhere they proclaimed the triumph of the Seven over the old gods.

A hill in the riverlands, now known to the Westerosi as High Heart, was sacred to the children of the forest. There, the Andal king Erreg the Kinslayer cut down the children’s grove of thirty-one weirwoods. It is said that the First Men killed half of the children of the forest with bronze blades, and the Andals finished the job with iron. 

After several hundred years of war, Andal warlords and adventurers conquered or married into the southern kingdoms of the First Men and created their own. They eventually conquered even the Iron Islands, though the Faith of the Seven was unable to take root in the archipelago. Only the Kings of Winter resisted the Andals successfully, stopping them at Moat Cailin and preventing them from conquering the north. After fending off an Andal invasion led by Argos Sevenstar in the Battle of the Weeping Water, King Theon Stark responded by raiding the coast of Andalos.

Due to migration and invasion, little is left of the original Andal culture in Andalos in Essos.

Andal Dominance[ ]

  

An Andal about to behead one of the First Men, from Game of Thrones Blu-ray

Despite their victory in Westeros, the Andals were outnumbered by the native First Men. The early Andal kings thus often tolerated worship of the old gods in godswoods to prevent religious turmoil, although the Faith of the Seven came to dominate southern Westeros over time. To solidify their right to rule, Andal kings often married the wives and daughters of defeated First Men kings. The Andals adopted the First Men's practice of the first night. According to George R. R. Martin, hardly any Westerosi are pure Andal or First Man after thousands of years of interbreeding. 

Runes were used by First Men in antiquity, but not by the Andals. Histories of the Dawn Age and the Age of Heroes were written by septons and maesters after the Andals came to Westeros, sometimes thousands of years after the times described.

According to legend, at some point in time at the Nightfort, the Rat Cook served an Andal king, possibly Tywell II Lannister or Oswell I Arryn, a pie made of bacon and, unknown to the king, the king's son. King Sherrit is said to have cursed Andals while at the Nightfort.

The wars lasted hundreds of years, but in the end the six southron kingdoms all fell before them. Only here, where the King in the North threw back every army that tried to cross the Neck, did the rule of the First Men endure. The First Men only left us runes on rocks, so everything we think we know about the Age of Heroes and the Dawn Age and the Long Night comes from accounts set down by septons thousands of years later. There are archmaesters at the Citadel who question all of it.
