Culture[ ]

Ygritte wears a rabbitskin vest.

The Windblown mockingly refer to Faezhar zo Faez as "the Rabbit".

History[ ]

During the Moon of the Three Kings, Trystane Truefyre granted the poor the right to take rabbits, hares, and deer from the kingswood.

After the First Blackfyre Rebellion, King Daeron II Targaryen granted the right to hunt red deer, boar, and rabbits in Wat's Wood to Wyman Webber, Lord of Coldmoat, and his descendants.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Arya Stark hunts down a rabbit by Mel Rubi

Arya Stark, disguised as "Arry" while part of Yoren's band of recruits for the Night's Watch, kills a rabbit as they travel north from King's Landing. Yoren stews the rabbit and gives a leg to Arya, who shares it with Gendry. Rorge calls mocks her as "Lumpyface Lumpyhead Rabbitkiller".

Benfred Tallhart raises a company of young lancers during the War of the Five Kings. After Leobald Tallhart calls them young rabbits, they dub themselves the Wild Hares and tie rabbitskins to the ends of their lances.

Gilly plans to breed rabbits in a rabbit hutch at Craster's Keep, but Ghost, the direwolf of Jon Snow, gets into the rabbits. Ghost eats one of the rabbits, and Jon cooks the other.

Rabbits are turned on spits and basted with honey in the kitchen of Harrenhal.

Three rabbits are flushed from the underbrush when Theon Greyjoy's hunting party searches for the missing Bran and Rickon Stark in the wolfswood.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Sharna serves rabbits to Arya and the brotherhood without banners at the Inn of the Kneeling Man.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Patchface is given a motley cloak of beaver pelts, sheepskins, and rabbit fur while at Eastwatch-by-the-Sea.
