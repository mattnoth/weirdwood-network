Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

During the riots in King's Landing, Lollys Stokeworth is knocked from her saddle as the royal party returns to the Red Keep. The gold cloaks later find her wandering naked on Sowbelly Row after having been raped by "half a hundred" men behind a tanner's shop. Lollys, traumatized by the event, becomes afraid to leave her chambers.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Lady Tanda Stokeworth hires Symon Silver Tongue to sing for her pregnant daughter; his music calms Lollys when her baby begins to kick.

Tyrion Lannister is arrested for the murder of King Joffrey I Baratheon at his wedding to Margaery Tyrell. In his tower cell, Tyrion summons his sellsword, Ser Bronn of the Blackwater, in case he needs him as a champion in a potential trial by combat. Bronn comes, but after some time and only reluctantly, as he is to marry Lollys in two days. Tyrion realizes that Queen Regent Cersei Lannister has arranged this marriage to take away his ally, and tries to argue Bronn out of the marriage. He says that Lollys is lackwitted and is pregnant with another man's bastard child, and is not even the heir to Castle Stokeworth. Bronn replies he doesn't want wits, that soon after Lollys gives birth she'll be pregnant with his child, and that her sister Falyse has no children and might die before her mother. As Tyrion cannot match Cersei's offer of a highborn wife and a castle, versus fighting Ser Gregor Clegane to the death, he says farewell to Bronn.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

During the funeral of Lord Tywin Lannister, who was murdered by Tyrion, Lollys is in labor, so she and her mother remain at Stokeworth. Falyse tells Queen Cersei that Lady Tanda asked if the baby is a boy, if they could name him after Tywin, but Cersei forbids it, indignant at the insult to her father.

When Lollys delivers a healthy son, Bronn names him after Tyrion. Ser Jaime Lannister finds this amusing, but Cersei is displeased by the sellsword's insolence.

Cersei tells Lady Falyse and her husband, Ser Balman Byrch, that the name of Lollys's son may mean that Bronn is hiding knowledge of Tyrion's whereabouts (though she later thinks that she does not truly believe this), and conspires with them to kill Bronn. However, the plan backfires, and Bronn kills Balman and exiles Falyse from Stokeworth. When Falyse flees to Cersei in terror for her life, Cersei gives her to Qyburn for his experiments in the black cells, to make her disappear and cover up the conspiracy.

After the death of Lady Tanda from a chill brought on by a broken hip, Lollys becomes the Lady of Stokeworth, with her husband Bronn calling himself Lord Stokeworth. Falyse also dies soon afterwards from Qyburn's experiments.

Notes[ ]

↑ When Lollys Stokeworth's bastard son was born in A Feast for Crows, readers expected him to be surnamed "Waters", as is standard in the Crownlands. However, the appendix of A Dance with Dragons revealed that he was actually surnamed "Tanner". Note that George R.R. Martin has stated that bastard surnames are only custom, and that "the highborn parent can bestow the usual name, a new one of his/her own devising, or none at all." It is likely that the black-humored Bronn named the child as an allusion to Lolly's rape behind a tanner's shop.
