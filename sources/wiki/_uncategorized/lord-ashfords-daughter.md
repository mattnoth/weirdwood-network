Appearance[ ]

Lord Ashford's daughter was a short girl with yellow hair and a round pink face.

History[ ]

In 209 AC, Lord Ashford hosted the tourney at Ashford Meadow to celebrate his daughter's thirteenth name day. Five champions wore a wisp of orange silk as her favor and defended her position as the reigning Queen of Love and Beauty; these included her brothers Ser Androw and Ser Robert Ashford, Lord Leo Tyrell, Ser Humfrey Hardyng, and Prince Valarr Targaryen.

Lord Ashford and his daughter watched the tourney from a viewing stand alongside princes of House Targaryen. During the trial of seven, mud landed on Lord Ashford and the fair maid when Ser Duncan the Tall shook the defeated Prince Aerion Targaryen before the viewing stand.
