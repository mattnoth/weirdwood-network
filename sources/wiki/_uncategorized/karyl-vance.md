Appearance and Character[ ]

Karyl would be handsome except for his winestain birthmark which discolors one side of his face and half his neck. He has sad eyes and a melancholy look. The knight has a calm demeanor, but his birthmark can darken when he is angered.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Ser Karyl accompanies his father, Lord Vance, to the pass below the Golden Tooth when hostilities threaten to break out.

Ser Marq Piper, Ser Raymun Darry, and Karyl are sent to King's Landing to receive King Robert I Baratheon's permission to hunt down the Lannister men who have been raiding the riverlands. Lord Eddard Stark, Hand of the King, agrees and sends Lord Beric Dondarrion with a party to kill Ser Gregor Clegane.

Karyl becomes Lord of Wayfarer's Rest after the death of his father in the battle in the hills below the Golden Tooth. Karyl and Marq raid the westerlands across the Red Fork of the Trident.

The new Lord Karyl is in the list of nobles King Joffrey I Baratheon declares must do him homage or be named traitors to the realm. Karyl and Marq instead bring the young Lyman Darry, Lord of Darry, to Riverrun, where Robb Stark is declared King in the North by northmen and rivermen.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Lord Karyl departs Riverrun to defend his lands with permission from his liege lord, Ser Edmure Tully. Karyl commands the defense of fords upstream from Riverrun in the Battle of the Fords.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Lord Karyl is among the seven nobles who launch the funeral boat of Lord Hoster Tully at Riverrun.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Lord Karyl bent the knee after the Red Wedding. He is one of the reluctant river lords who join with House Lannister in the siege of Riverrun, and he participates in Ser Jaime Lannister's war council. Once Riverrun surrenders, Karyl advises Jaime to resolve the siege of Raventree.
