## Summary

Espionage or spying involves a spy ring made up of individuals obtaining information considered secret or confidential without the permission of the holder of the information in order to benefit from that information, especially when playing the game of thrones.  The chief spy is called a spymaster.   In Westeros the master of whisperers is the king's spymaster.  Spymasters and their spies are sometimes called "spiders".

Known spies[ ]

Varys - the master of whisperers, or spymaster, for the Iron Throne of the Seven Kingdoms.

Ser Jorah Mormont - spied on Daenerys Targaryen for Varys, informing on her since she wed Khal Drogo, until she left Qarth

Jon Snow – ordered by Qhorin Halfhand to yield if they were caught by wildlings in order to act as a spy.

Lancel  Lannister - blackmailed by Tyrion Lannister into spying on Cersei for him.

People spying on Black Walder Frey – planted by Edwyn Frey

People spying on Edwyn Frey – planted by Black Walder Frey

House Blackwood spies in House Bracken

House Bracken spies in House Blackwood

Senelle - Taena of Myr reveals that Cersei Lannister’s maid is spying on her for Margaery Tyrell.

Little birds – part of Varys's vast spy ring

Little mice – part of young Varys's vast spy ring in Pentos

Theon Greyjoy – ordered to spy on Roose Bolton by his son Ramsay Snow, however Roose is aware of this

The Bastard's Boys - tasked by Roose Bolton with spying on Ramsay Snow on behalf of the Lord of the Dreadfort.

Osmund, Osney, and Osfryd Kettleblack - provided Bronn with information about Cersei Lannister's moves. In turn their loyalties actually lie with Petyr Baelish.

Several of Wyman Manderly's servants and two knights bribed by Symond Frey to spy on the Lord of White Harbor

Moon Boy - a spy for Varys according to Ser Dontos Hollard

Lysono Maar - spymaster for the Golden Company.

Symond Frey – House Frey spymaster

Mysaria - Prince Daemon Targaryen's spymaster
