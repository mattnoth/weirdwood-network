Culture[ ]

Braavos[ ]

Braavos is renowned worldwide for its cultured courtesans who enjoy high status. Every courtesan has her own barge and servants to work them. They are showered with gifts from goldsmiths, and craftsmen beg for their custom. Nobility and rich merchants pay the courtesans large amounts of money to appear alongside them at events, and bravos are known to kill each other in their names. The courtesans of Braavos are all free women, with the most famous taking poetic names.

The beauty of famed courtesans has inspired many songs, and there are marble and bronze statues of some Braavosi courtesans.

Some families have produced many generations of courtesans, such as the descendants of Bellegere Otherys.

Lys[ ]

The courtesans from the pleasure gardens of Lys are famed for their beauty, but they are all slaves, unlike the free women of Braavos. The Lyseni breed their slaves to develop beautiful courtesans and bedslaves.

History[ ]

  

Tyanna of the Tower, by Laura Avellino ©

Prior to becoming involved with Maegor Targaryen, Tyanna of the Tower was a tavern dancer who became a courtesan in Pentos.

Courtesans across the narrow sea were among the informants whom Grand Master Benifer utilized during his search for the dragon eggs taken by Elissa Farman.

Known as the Black Swan, Johanna Swann was a courtesan who rose to become ruler of Lys in all but name. The Triarchy fell into infighting after the murder of Sharako Lohar by a rival for the affections of the Black Swan.

Part of Lord Manfryd Mooton's mission to the Sealord of Braavos, Lord Follard chose to remain in Braavos because of a courtesan instead of returning to Westeros.

Lysandro Rogare is said to have contemplated having Viserys Targaryen work as a courtesan at the Perfumed Garden before the boy revealed his true identity.

Recent Events[ ]

A Feast for Crows[ ]

The kindly man offers to send Arya Stark from the House of Black and White to become a courtesan in Braavos, but the girl refuses and chooses to remain with the Faceless Men.

Brusco makes it plain to Cat of the Canals (a disguised Arya) that while selling her oysters, clams, and cockles she is never to speak to a courtesan unless spoken to first. Cat tells crew from the Brazen Monkey that she sold three cockles to Bellegere Otherys, the Black Pearl, who called to Cat as she was stepping off her barge. As she pushes her barrow along the canals, Cat sometimes glimpses courtesans floating by on their way to evenings with some lovers.

Dareon deserts from the Night's Watch while in Braavos, preferring instead to write songs for the city's courtesans.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Arya learns that pretty young girls at the Satin Palace dream of being courtesans.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

  

Bellegere Otherys, the Black Pearl, by Rae Lavergne ©

Famous courtesans attend a performance of The Bloody Hand at the Gate. Ser Harys Swyft is accompanied by the Black Pearl, and three members of Otharys are each accompanied by a courtesan as well.
