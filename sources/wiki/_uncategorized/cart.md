Culture[ ]

The Free Folk from beyond the Wall use wayns, sleds, and carts to transport their goods.

Riding in a cart is seen as a weakness by the Dothraki, who prefer horse riding. In their culture, carts are reserved for cripples, eunuchs, pregnant women just before the time of giving birth, the very old or very young people.

Hathays are a type of cart used in Volantis. They are pulled by dwarf elephants, and are more ornate than Westerosi oxcarts.

History[ ]

After the battle at Rook's Rest, the head of Meleys was brought back to King's Landing by the forces of King Aegon II Targaryen and drawn on a cart through the city, aweing the smallfolk into silence.

In 132 AC, Lord Benjicot Blackwood's carts were attacked by outlaws while he was on his way to Winterfell for the wedding of his aunt, Lady Alysanne Blackwood, to Cregan Stark the Lord of Winterfell.

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Viserys Targaryen, the "Cart King" - by Kim Sokol © FFG

After Khaleesi Daenerys Targaryen punishes her brother, Prince Viserys Targaryen, by making him walk back to the khalasar, he is greatly shamed in the eyes of the Dothraki, as they feel a man who does not ride is no man at all, and this earns him the mocking nickname Khal Rhae Mhar, the Sorefoot King. Khal Drogo later offers Viserys a place in a cart and he accepts, in his ignorance believing it the khal's way of apologizing to him for the wrong Daenerys did to him. Unknown to Viserys, this is a greater insult than walking on foot, and he gains a new nickname, Khal Rhaggat, the Cart King.
