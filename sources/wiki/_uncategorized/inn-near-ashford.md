Layout[ ]

Found beside a stream, the tall building was daub-and-timber with a common room and stables. The innkeep was a stout, short whey-faced woman who had two children, a boy who wanted to be a soldier and a girl interested in knights.

Ashford was a day's ride away, accessible by bearing north when a road forked at a burned mill.

History[ ]

After burying Ser Arlan of Pennytree, Dunk encountered Egg bathing in the stream by the inn and assumed he was a stableboy, not realizing the bald youth was actually Prince Aegon Targaryen. Dunk also found a drunken lordling in the common room, where Dunk was served lamb, duck, and ale by the innkeep. Dunk refused Egg's offer to to become his squire, choosing to continue alone to the tourney at Ashford Meadow. Egg later followed in the cart of a man bringing lambs to Ashford Castle.

The drunken lordling was actually Aegon's older brother, Prince Daeron Targaryen, and the siblings had departed Summerhall together for Ashford Meadow. Daeron did not want to participate in the tourney, so he shaved Aegon's head and hid at the inn. Aegon, however, wanted to be someone's squire and attend the tourney. Daeron, still drunk, was later found at the inn by their father, Prince Maekar Targaryen, to whom he claimed that Aegon had been abducted on the road by a huge robber knight.

Daeron's lies and Dunk's confrontation with another princely brother, Aerion Targaryen, led to a trial of seven being held at Ashford.
