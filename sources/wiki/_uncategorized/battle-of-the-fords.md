## Full Text

The Battle of the Fords is a series of engagements fought between the forces of House Tully and House Lannister during the War of the Five Kings. The fighting takes place on the fords of the Red Fork of the Trident.

Prelude[ ]

When Robb Stark, the new King in the North, marches west from Riverrun, he leaves his uncle, Ser Edmure Tully, to hold Riverrun and guard his rear. Edmure commands the rivermen, as his father, Lord Hoster Tully, is ill. Robb's northmen and Freys bypass the Golden Tooth using a goat track found by Grey Wind, Robb's direwolf. Near Lannisport, Robb surprises the newly-raised host of Stafford Lannister, who neglected to post enough sentries, in the Battle of Oxcross. The northmen capture Ashmark and pay the westerlands back in kind for the devastation inflicted on the riverlands.

Expecting Stannis Baratheon to be preoccupied for months with the siege of Storm's End, Lord Tywin Lannister risks leaving Harrenhal to stop Robb's raiding of his homeland. In response, Edmure orders Ser Helman Tallhart to leave the Twins and aid Lord Roose Bolton, who is at the crossroads, with recovering Harrenhal. Edmure thinks that once Robb returns from the west, Tywin can be trapped between Riverrun and Harrenhal and then defeated. Edmure's plan is supported by Lords Tytos Blackwood and Jonos Bracken, fierce rivals who usually disagree.

In order to return to the westerlands, Tywin must cross the Red Fork of the Trident. Edmure calls his banners, approximately eight thousand foot and three thousand horse, to prevent Tywin, who has near twice as many, from crossing the river. While returning to Riverrun, Catelyn Stark spots the tents of Lord Blackwood and Ser Marq Piper, as well as Darrys and Paeges. Edmure allows smallfolk to seek refuge in Riverun.

The Battle[ ]

Ser Edmure Tully organizes his forces so that every river crossing north and south of Riverrun is defended by a strong force. Lord Jason Mallister commands the defense of four fords, including the closest to the castle, while Lord Karyl Vance commands those upstream. The west bank of the Red Fork is higher than the east and wooded, so the defenders use those trees to hide archers and place scorpions as support. Caltrops and iron spikes are spread in the Red Fork. Edmure keeps his best knights as a reserve, to ride where they were needed most. Ser Perwyn Frey and Martyn Rivers lead Freys during the battle, and Ser Desmond Grell commands the remaining garrison of Riverrun. When the castle is informed that Storm's End has fallen to Stannis Baratheon, Maester Vyman sends a raven to Ashemark so that Robb Stark, who is en route to the Crag, can be informed.

Initially, Lord Tywin Lannister probes the rivermen's defenses, attempting to find an unguarded ford. House Mallister repels a group from House Brax during daytime and another group of westermen at night. In the morning, a rider informs Catelyn Stark that Ser Flement Brax was defeated six leagues to the south by Mallister archers and scorpions. House Vance also fends off an attack.

Three days later, Tywin attempts to force a crossing in a dozen places, but all are defeated by the rivermen. Lord Leo Lefford drowns, Ser Robert Brax is slain, Ser Lyle Crakehall is taken captive, and Ser Addam Marbrand is forced to retreat three times. The most heated fighting is at Stone Mill, where Edmure faces Ser Gregor Clegane. Although the Mountain's men manage to gain the west bank, they suffer heavy losses. Edmure turns back Gregor's men with his reserve, with the Mountain wounded by a dozen arrows. Unable to cross the Red Fork, Tywin is forced to retreat. The westermen army is last seen marching south-east.

Aftermath[ ]

Riverrun simultaneously celebrates Ser Edmure Tully's victory in the Battle of the Fords and Robb Stark's storming of the Crag. The King in the North is wounded at the Crag, however, and treated by Jeyne Westerling. After being told that his brothers, Bran and Rickon, have been killed, the grieving Robb sleeps with Jeyne, and then marries her to preserve her honor. Since Robb has broken his betrothal to a daughter of Lord Walder Frey, the Freys at the Crag and Riverrun angrily depart. The Freys at Harrenhal are also outraged.

After Robb returns to Riverrun, he and Ser Brynden Tully criticize Edmure for having confronted Tywin. They explain that Brynden's scouts had found an advantageous position on the Goldroad. Robb had hoped to draw Tywin into the westerlands and then either defeat Tywin on the Goldroad or lead the westermen on a chase along the Sunset Sea's coast. Robb explains Stannis Baratheon would have been able to conquer King's Landing. Edmure's defense of the fords delayed Tywin long enough for riders from Bitterbridge to inform him of happenings in the east, however. With Lord Petyr Baelish having negotiated an alliance between House Tyrell and House Baratheon of King's Landing, Tywin turned back east, rendezvousing with Lords Mace Tyrell, Mathis Rowan, and Randyll Tarly. The combined army then defeated Stannis in the Battle of the Blackwater.

To make amends, Edmure offers to lead Robb's van in the next battle. After the passing of Lord Hoster Tully, Edmure, now Lord of Riverrun, is convinced by Robb, Brynden, and Catelyn that he should wed one of Walder's daughters, so that the Freys will again support Robb.

Notes[ ]

↑ Houses Banefort, Bettley, Falwell, Ferren, Foote, Kenning, Lydden, Myat, Payne, Peckledon, Prester, Sarsfield, House Serrett, Stackspear, and Swyft are not directly mentioned at this battle. They are all with Tywin Lannister at Harrenhal or part of his host earlier in the War of the Five Kings, however, and likely participate in the Battle of the Fords as Tywin tries to cross the Red Fork.

↑ Houses Hamell, Moreland, Turnberry, and Yarwyck are not directly mentioned at this battle. Their banners are seen among the escort which brings Tywin's corpse from King's Landing back to Casterly Rock, however, which suggests these Houses may have been with Tywin at the earlier Battle of the Fords.

↑ House Plumm is not directly mentioned at this battle. In A Storm of Swords, however, the brothers Plumm are sent by Tywin to find Jaime following his release, which takes before this battle. This suggests that Plumms are part of Tywin's host, and men of the House may have taken part in the Battle of the Fords.
