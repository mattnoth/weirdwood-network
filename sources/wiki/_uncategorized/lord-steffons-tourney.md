History[ ]

Ser Barristan Selmy recounts to Daenerys Targaryen that her brother, Prince Rhaegar once rode brilliantly in a tourney at Storm's End, defeating Lord Steffon Baratheon, Lord Jason Mallister, Prince Oberyn Martell, a mystery knight who turned out to be Simon Toyne, and, after breaking twelve lances, Ser Arthur Dayne. However, Rhaegar was defeated by a knight of the Kingsguard in the champion's tilt.

Gerold Hightower, Lord Commander of the Kingsguard, wrote in Barristan's White Book entry that Lord Steffon's tourney took place at Storm's End, in which Barristan was named champion after unhorsing Lord Robert Baratheon, Prince Oberyn, Lord Leyton Hightower, Lord Jon Connington, Lord Jason, and Prince Rhaegar Targaryen.

Contradictions[ ]

Based on semi-canon sources, the tourney at Storm's End mentioned by Barristan and Lord Steffon's tourney are the same event, with the unnamed knight who defeated Rhaegar in the first account being actually Barristan, who didn't want to bring attention to himself. However, there are discrepancies between Barristan's recollection and Gerold Hightower's report. While Barristan tells Daenerys that Oberyn Martell and Jason Mallister were defeated by Rhaegar, the White Book states that they were defeated by Barristan himself. Furthermore, Barristan says that Rhaegar defeated Simon Toyne, but the White Book entry lists the tourneys at Oldtown and Storm's End after the vanquishing of the Kingswood Brotherhood, during which Barristan killed Simon. In addition to that, the White Book entry calls the event "Lord Steffon's tourney" and at the same time names Robert Baratheon "lord", implying Steffon was dead at the time of the tourney, whereas Barristan recounts that Steffon took part in the hastilude.

According to George R. R. Martin, the White Book is correct, while Barristan Selmy misremembered several details of the hastilude, conflating circumstances that happened at two or three different tourneys. With respect to some potential inconsistencies in the White Book's entry, Gerold Hightower may have listed Barristan's tourneys and the fight in the Kingswood not in a chronological order, which means that Simon Toyne may have actually competed against Rhaegar at Storm's End. Moreover, it is possible that the tourney was held after Steffon's death, in 278 AC or early 279 AC, and that the new lord Robert named it after the late lord as a homage to his own father. That would be consistent with Robert taking part in the joust, since in 278 AC he came of age, the time after which most knights receive their knighthoods, as well as with Toyne's death in 281 AC.

The Joust[ ]

As reported by Gerold Hightower in the White Book:

  Ser Barristan Selmy defeated Lord Robert Baratheon

  Ser Barristan Selmy defeated Prince Oberyn Martell

  Ser Barristan Selmy defeated Lord Leyton Hightower

  Ser Barristan Selmy defeated Lord Jon Connington

  Ser Barristan Selmy defeated Lord Jason Mallister

  Ser Barristan Selmy defeated Prince Rhaegar Targaryen

According to Barristan Selmy, Rhaegar Targaryen defeated Lord Steffon Baratheon, Lord Jason Mallister, Prince Oberyn Martell, Simon Toyne, disguised as a mystery knight, and Ser Arthur Dayne. However, Barristan's account is unreliable, since he confused events that happened at different tourneys.
