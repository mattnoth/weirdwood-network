Appearance and Character[ ]

The handsome Harrold has sandy hair, deep blue eyes, and dimples when he smiles. According to a semi-canon source, he has an aquiline nose and good teeth, and is tall like a lance, clean-limbed, and well muscled. It is said that Harrold has the look of Jon Arryn in his youth. 

Harry can be gallant and beguiling, but also cold and dismissive. Robert Arryn dislikes his cousin and is resentful of his status, believing that Harrold is just waiting for him to die so that he can take the Eyrie.

According to a semi-canon source, Harrold's personal heraldry that is displayed on his shield is quartered: the arms of House Hardyng and House Waynwood are displayed in the first and third quarters, respectively, and in the second and fourth quarters is the moon-and-falcon sigil of House Arryn. He is proud of his lineage.

History[ ]

  

Harrold, by Chillyravenart ©

Harrold is the son and only child of a landed knight of House Hardyng sworn to House Waynwood, and the youngest daughter of Alys Arryn and Ser Elys Waynwood. He was taken in as the ward of Lady Anya Waynwood at Ironoaks, where he was raised.

Shortly after Robert's Rebellion, the infant Harrold became the heir presumptive of his great-uncle Jon Arryn, Lord of the Eyrie. Jon had lost three heirs in succession: first, his nephew, Ser Elbert Arryn, was executed on orders of King Aerys II Targaryen in 282 AC; second, his nephew by marriage, Ser Denys Arryn, died at the Battle of the Bells the following year; and third, Denys's only son, who died shortly after his father. This left Harrold Hardyng as the last male descendant of Jon's father, Lord Jasper Arryn.

In 292 AC, Robert Arryn was born to Jon, becoming his heir. Robert became Lord of the Eyrie on Jon's death in 298 AC, and Harrold was confirmed as Robert's heir.

Despite his youth, Harrold has already fathered a bastard daughter, Alys Stone, on a lowborn servant named Cissy. He has another bastard on the way by a different woman, Saffron, a spice merchant's daughter from Gulltown. Lady Waynwood declined Lord Nestor Royce's offer to betroth Harry to Myranda Royce.

Recent Events[ ]

A Feast for Crows[ ]

During Petyr Baelish's meeting with the Lords Declarant at the Eyrie, Petyr suggests that Harrold should become his ward and a companion to young Lord Robert Arryn, but Lady Anya Waynwood rejects the offer. Lord Yohn Royce states that Robert should become his ward at Runestone, and befriend Harry there, but Petyr rejects that in turn.

Sansa Stark, who is masquerading as Petyr's bastard daughter, "Alayne Stone", learns from Myranda Royce that Harrold has fathered at least one baseborn child. Myranda informs Alayne that Harry won a small melee for squires held at Runestone, after which he was knighted by Bronze Yohn. Petyr later informs Alayne that Harrold is expected to father another child on a different woman.

Petyr, the Lord Protector of the Vale of Arryn, has arranged a betrothal between his "daughter" Alayne and Harrold. Lady Anya agreed to the betrothal in exchange for Petyr forgiving the Waynwood debt that he had acquired, but she insisted that Harry must agree to the marriage. Petyr tells Sansa his scheme: that once Harry succeeds Robert as Lord of the Eyrie, she will reveal her true identity and marry Harry, so that he and the armies of the Vale will reclaim Winterfell in her name.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

Harrold rides with the Waynwoods to the Gates of the Moon to attend the tourney for the Brotherhood of Winged Knights. There, he is met by Myranda Royce and Alayne Stone, his betrothed. When Alayne informs Harrold that he is to stay at Falcon Tower, he responds rudely, and insults her status as a bastard. An embarrassed Alayne runs off and encounters Ser Lothor Brune, who gives Harrold the moniker of "Harry the Arse". Petyr advises Alayne to give Harry a second chance at the feast, but to deny him her favor for the jousting.

Later, during the feast, after Alayne has danced with several eligible knights, Harry asks her forgiveness for his rudeness and asks her to dance. Alayne agrees, and while they dance, she inquires about Harrold's bastards. Harry responds honestly, explaining his relationships with Cissy and Saffron. Harrold seems to warm up to Alayne, bursting into a laugh upon hearing some of her clever japes. Alayne refuses Harry's request to wear her favor in the upcoming tourney, falsely claiming that she has already promised it to another.

Family[ ]

Harrold is the great-nephew of Lord Jon Arryn. Jon had a younger sister, Alys, who married the uncle of Lady Anya Waynwood, Ser Elys Waynwood. (Thus making Harrold and Anya first cousins once removed.) Alys and Elys had nine children together, a son named Jasper and eight daughters. The youngest of their daughters married a landed knight of House Hardyng sworn to the Waynwoods, and together they had Harrold.
