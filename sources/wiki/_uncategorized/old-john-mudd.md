## Full Text

Not to be confused with Young John Mudd.

Old John Mudd was a sellsword in service to the Golden Company. He was the father of Young John Mudd.

History[ ]

Old John's surname is of a house that had once loomed large in the histories of the Seven Kingdoms. House Mudd ruled as Kings of the Rivers and the Hills thousands of years ago until their line was extinguished with the death of King Tristifer V Mudd at the hands of the Andals. He may or may not have been of House Mudd, as in the free companies a man can call himself whatever he chooses, and not all were genuine. Besides his son, Young John Mudd, Old John may have been related to a serjeant in the company, Lorimas Mudd.
