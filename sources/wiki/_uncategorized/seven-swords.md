## Full Text

The Seven Swords is the largest inn in Duskendale. It is a four story structure that towers over the other buildings. Named after the seven members of House Darklyn who served in the Kingsguard, its sign has seven wooden swords painted white. It has a common room. Among some of the fare served at the Seven Swords is bread, hot crab stew, and wine. 

Recent Events[ ]

A Feast for Crows[ ]

As the captain's sister is repainting her oaken shield, Brienne of Tarth decides to stay overnight in the Seven Swords. She is given a room on the second floor. A woman with a liver colored birthmark on her face helps her with her bath. Brienne asks her about the Darklyns, and the woman tells Brienne that the lordly Darklyns are all gone. 

After her bath, Brienne walks to the Dun Fort to speak to the lord. She returns to the Seven Swords's common room for dinner, finding it crowded, but a dwarf brother lets her have his seat. She tells the brother of her quest to find her sister and a fool, and he tells her that he overheard a man called Nimble Dick in Maidenpool at the Stinking Goose bragging that he had "fooled a fool" seeking passage for three across the narrow Sea. By the time the common room empties, most of the talk turns to the recent death of Lord Tywin Lannister and who will rule until Tommen comes of age. When the innkeep spits into the fire at the mention of the oathbreaker Jaime Lannister ruling, Brienne gets up and leaves.  

The next day, Brienne is drinking a breakfast of milk and honey mixed with three raw eggs in the inn's common room when the captain's sister arrives and delivers the repainted shield. Afterwards, Brienne departs the Seven Swords and heads to Maidenpool in search of Nimble Dick.
