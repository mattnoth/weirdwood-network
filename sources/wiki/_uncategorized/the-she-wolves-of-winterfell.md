Plot[ ]

The novella is set in Winterfell. According to Martin, it will feature Ser Duncan the Tall and his squire Egg, who will meet the "She-Wolves": a group of formidable Stark wives, widows, mothers and grandmothers. According to fans' con reports, four of these "she-wolves" may be widows of previous Lords of Winterfell, and one the current Lady Stark whose husband Beron Stark is dying from a wound taken while fighting Ironborn. There may be a House Stark succession crisis, a struggle for power among the Stark ladies, possibly on behalf of their children, of which there were at least ten at the time. Additionally, Martin mentioned this story in connection with the fact that there has never been a ruling Lady of Winterfell.

The She-Wolves[ ]

The identity of the five "She-Wolves" has not been revealed yet. However, with the Stark lineage given in The World of Ice and Fire, their names may be deduced:

 Lady Lorra Royce, the soon to be widow of Lord Beron Stark.

 Lady Myriame Manderly, widow of Lord Rodwell Stark and Beron's sister-in-law.

 Lady Alys Karstark, widow of Lord Brandon Stark and Beron's mother.

 Lady Robyn Ryswell, widow of Lord Jonnel Stark and Beron's aunt by marriage.

 Lady Lynara Stark, widow of Lord Cregan Stark and Beron's grandmother.

Moreover, four Stark women — Arsa, Serena, and her daughters Arrana and Argelle — might be engaged to various degrees in the power struggle following Beron's injury, even though they did not marry a Lord of Winterfell. Also, Beron's son (and future Lord) Willam Stark's wives — Lyanne Glover and Melantha Blackwood — may be potentially involved if Willam married one or both of them before his father's death.
