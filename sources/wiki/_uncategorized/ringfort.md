Layout[ ]

Ringforts are rough-hewn, unmortared walls of circular construction; the ancient ringfort atop Seal Rock at White Harbor, for instance, is a circle of weathered stones. The ringwall at the Fist of the First Men crowns the top of the steep stony hill.

History[ ]

As the First Men spread throughout Westeros building farms and villages, they ruled from ringforts from the Land of Always Winter to the Summer Sea. The Fist of the First Men is said to have been a ringfort built during the Dawn Age.

Some believe that Winterfell was once a complex of linked ringforts, though little evidence remains. Old ruins of the First Men are found in the hills and bogs of Sea Dragon Point.

House Casterly built a ringfort atop Casterly Rock.

The Bloody Gate was once a rough-hewn, unmortared wall like the First Men's ringforts.

Instead of ringforts, the First Men eventually began building stout motte-and-bailey forts and then stone castles. Many stout stone-and-timber castles were built during the coming of the Andals to Westeros.

The older ringforts have been abandoned and left in ruins. The ringfort at Seal Rock, for instance, has been abandoned for hundreds of years.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

During the great ranging, the Night's Watch fortify the ringfort at the Fist of the First Men.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

House Manderly fortifies the ruins of the ringfort atop Seal Rock, placing scorpions and spitfires behind the standing stones and garrisoning it with crossbowmen.
