Appearance and Character[ ]

Ser Humfrey was a young bold knight. In the lists, he wore yellow and black stripes clothing with three beehives depicted on his shield.

History[ ]

Ser Humfrey participated in the tourney at Ashford Meadow in 209 AC where he jousted against Ser Humfrey Hardyng. They broke twelve lances in what was considered the best fought battle of the day, which the smallfolk soon began calling "the Battle of Humfrey". Humfrey Hardyng won the struggle and remained in the competition as a champion.

The two Humfreys joined the side of Ser Duncan the Tall in the trial of seven. Humfrey Beesbury was slain by Ser Donnel of Duskendale of the Kingsguard in the first pass.

Three years after the events at Ashford Meadow, Duncan remained troubled by what happened there and the deaths of both Humfreys and Prince Baelor Targaryen.
