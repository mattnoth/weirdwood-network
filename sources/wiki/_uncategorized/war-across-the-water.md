History[ ]

The war was the result of the Rape of the Three Sisters two thousand years ago, when northmen occupied the previously-independent islands known as the Three Sisters. In response, the pirate kings of the Sistermen bent the knee to King Mathos II Arryn in exchange for the Vale of Arryn's assistance in driving out the northmen.

Mathos sailed for Sisterton with a hundred warships. Although he never returned from war, his sons and descendants continued the fight against the northmen for a thousand years.

Control of the Three Sisters changed more than twelve times. Northmen landed on the Fingers three times; King Theon Stark once conquered the islands and landed an army on the Fingers, though it is unknown if this was part of the Worthless War or during a different time. A northern castle, the Wolf's Den at the mouth of the White Knife, was besieged by King Osgood Arryn and burned by his son, Oswin the Talon. The Starks attacked Gulltown in response and, unable to overcome its walls, burned hundreds of ships in the city's harbor.

The war eventually ended when House Stark simply lost interest in controlling the Three Sisters.

Aftermath[ ]

The war is described in Archmaester Perestan's A Consideration of History.

Rule by the triumphant Arryns over the Three Sisters has remained tenuous. Some Sistermen still resent northmen for the invasion.
