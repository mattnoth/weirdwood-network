## Summary

A tokar is a long, loose, shapeless sheet, worn as a garment in Ghiscari cities by the slaver class, both men and women. In Astapor, only the freeborn are allowed to wear tokars. The tokar is a sign of the wearer's wealth and power.

Description[ ]

The tokar must be wound around the hips and under an arm and over a shoulder to keep it on. It is wrapped this way to carefully display the dangling fringes, which are usually adorned with some decoration to signify the wearer's status. If wound too loose, the tokar might unravel and fall off. If it is wound too tight, it might tangle and trip the wearer. Even if wound properly, the wearer must hold the tokar in place with their left hand. Walking requires small steps and great balance to prevent tripping and falling on the trailing fringes. A person should not ride a horse while wearing a tokar.

The tokar may be worn over an undertunic, which may be made of linen.

Recent Events[ ]

A Storm of Swords[ ]

  

Daenerys Targaryen confronts the tokar-wearing Good Masters of Astapor. Art by Joshua Cairos © Fantasy Flight Games

During the fall of Astapor, Daenerys Targaryen orders her Unsullied to slay every man wearing a tokar or holding a whip. Many Good Masters trip over their garments while trying to flee.

The Wise Master Grazdan mo Eraz attempts to bribe Daenerys to prevent her from attacking Yunkai to free its slaves, but she refuses him. After Grazdan insults her, she says "Dracarys", and Drogon sets his tokar ablaze. When Grazdan complains that he was offered safe conduct, Daenerys tells him she will buy him a new tokar after Yunkai delivers its slaves to her.

After her conquest of Meereen, Daenerys has the Great Masters and their women gathered in the plaza before the Great Pyramid, stripped of their jewelry and tokars.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

In Meereen, Queen Daenerys wants to ban the tokar, but her advisors convince her otherwise. The Green Grace, Galazza Galare, explains that Daenerys must wear the attire of Old Ghis if she wants to be accepted as their ruler. Ben Plumm refers to the tokar as "floppy ears" that a "king o' the rabbits" must wear, and Daenerys adopts that term for her tokars, which she dislikes wearing. 

The snappish Viserion burns the tokar of Reznak mo Reznak.

Daenerys dresses in a tokar to hold court in the throne room of the Great Pyramid. One of the petitioners is a Great Master's son dressed in a frayed tokar, who wants his father's former household slaves executed for killing his family. Daenerys has to deny him, as she had declared a blanket pardon for all crimes committed during the sack, and will not punish slaves for rising up against their masters. The boy attempts to attack her, but fails when he trips on his own tokar. Daenerys forgives him and sends him away, and tells him to treasure the tokar that saved his life.

When Hizdahr zo Loraq comes to Daenerys for his seventh petition to re-open the fighting pits of Meereen, he has shaved his beard and cut his hair, and he wears a simple robe instead of a tokar.

A dying Astapori rider arrives in Meereen atop a pale mare, warning that Astapor is burning. Grey Worm informs Daenerys that under the man's tokar he was as thin as a skeleton and burning with fever. 

After Daenerys agrees to marry Hizdahr per the advice of Galazza Galare, Reznak informs her of the wedding garb for a bride according to Ghiscari tradition, a tokar of white silk, with dark red veils. The tokar is fringed with baby pearls, which symbolize fertility. Daenerys wears the traditional tokar during her wedding to Hizdahr, though she envies her Dothraki handmaids in their sandsilk trousers and painted vests.

At the ceremony for the re-opening of the fighting pits, after the pit fighter Barsena Blackhair is killed by a boar, Daenerys can no longer stand to wear her tokar and removes it, moments before Drogon arrives in Daznak's Pit.

Tyrion Lannister recalls that when he was performing in Daznak's Pit, all he saw of Daenerys was a slender girl with silvery hair, who was wrapped in a tokar and whose face was veiled.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

The tokar is likely inspired by the real-world toga, the distinctive garment of the aristocratic class of Ancient Rome, which was difficult to put on and challenging to wear correctly. The toga, a long length of woven fabric, typically white wool, was wrapped around the body and draped over the left shoulder; as no pins or ties were allowed to be used, the left arm had to hold the toga in place. The wearer had to walk carefully, in a dignified manner, to avoid disarrangement of the toga's folds. The toga was forbidden to be worn by non-citizens, foreigners, freedmen, and slaves. The tokar may also be inspired by the sari, a women's garment of the Indian subcontinent, that is similarly a long length of woven fabric wrapped about the body, but typically made in more opulent textiles and colors.
