Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

After Ser Rodrik Cassel captures "Reek", his party stops at the mill for a while before continuing to Winterfell. The miller's wife sells them hay for their horses, and Ser Rodrik speaks with her sons.

When Theon Greyjoy leads ironborn raiders to capture Winterfell, Bran and Rickon Stark become his prisoners and hostages. '"Reek", freed from Winterfell's dungeons, swears himself to Theon.

Bran and Rickon apparently escape Winterfell. Theon gathers a search party, including Reek, Maester Luwin, and the kennelmaster Farlen and his dogs, but fails to track them down in the wolfswood. However, Reek tells Theon he has a feeling that the Stark boys are hiding at the mill, and secretly presents Theon with the boys' fine clothing he had brought along in a bag. Theon sends the rest of the search party back to Winterfell, except for Reek and three ironborn, Aggar, Gelmarr, and Gynir. 

At the mill, Reek kills the miller's two sons, and when their mother begs Theon for mercy, Gelmarr cuts her down with one blow of his axe. The dead children are decapitated and their heads skinned and dipped in tar by Reek, and Theon dresses their bodies in the Stark children's finery. Theon returns to Winterfell and presents the heads and headless bodies as Bran and Rickon. Theon mounts their heads on the castle walls, and burns the bodies and clothing to bones and ashes. 

Later, Theon has a nightmare that he is having sex with the miller's wife again, but she has teeth below, and she both tears out his throat and bites off his manhood. Theon also dreams of the feast for King Robert I Baratheon's arrival at Winterfell, but soon realizes he is dining with the dead, including the miller's wife.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Having returned to Winterfell for Ramsay Bolton's wedding, a regretful Theon feels that the castle is full of ghosts for him. He thinks of the old ghosts from the crypts and the younger ones that he had made himself, including the miller's wife from Acorn Water and her two young sons.

In the godswood of Winterfell, Theon sees Bran's face in the weirwood and momentarily thinks he is being haunted by his ghost, before recalling that they had not actually killed Bran and Rickon, but instead only miller's sons. He speaks aloud that he had to have two heads or he would have been mocked, and is overheard by Holly and Rowan. Rowan accuses Theon of being a kinslayer, and Theon denies any relationship to Bran and Rickon while thinking to himself that the two children he killed were just some miller's sons. He thinks that he had known the miller's wife for years, and remembers that she had big heavy breasts, a sweet mouth, and a merry laugh, pleasures that he can never again experience.

Theories[ ]

It has been speculated that Theon fathered the miller's wife's sons, or at least the youngest one, as he bedded the miller's wife several times. However, this conjecture is disputed: Theon never considers the possibility that the children are his, which may imply that he had sex with the woman after they were already born; a proof of this may be Theon remembering "stretch marks on her belly", that could have come from childbirth.
