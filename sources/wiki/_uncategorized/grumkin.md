Folklore[ ]

Grumkins are implied to be of short stature or otherwise of very small size and may also steal and replace children.

Grumkins are associated with granting wishes, either by crafting magical objects which make wishes come true, or by directly giving people a number of wishes, of which the third wish is the last and must be used carefully. Grumkins can apparently enchant objects like swords.

Adults no longer believe in grumkins and consider them imaginary beings, comparable to legendary animals and supernatural entities or the monsters that children's wet nurses warn them against.

History[ ]

Old Nan has told stories about grumkins to the children of Lord Eddard Stark. Since Arya Stark looks more like her half brother Jon Snow than her trueborn siblings, her sister Sansa once asked their mother, Lady Catelyn Stark, if the real Arya had been taken by grumkins.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Tyrion Lannister jokes about grumkins and snarks to Jon Snow.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

The small council of King Tommen I Baratheon mocks the mission of the Night's Watch, with Qyburn saying they defend against grumkins and snarks.
