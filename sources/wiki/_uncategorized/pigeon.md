Culture[ ]

Pigeons are known for their large eggs.

Pigeons and doves can be cooked into pies. Skewered pigeon can also be served. Pigeon can be included in bowls of brown in Flea Bottom.

Great pies for wedding feasts can be filled with doves and songbirds, with the birds flying away after the pie is cut.

History[ ]

Since doves and pigeons can carry messages, the peaceful King Baelor I Targaryen attempted to have the maesters of the Citadel replace their messenger ravens with doves; the debacle is included in Archmaester Walgrave's Black Wings, Swift Words. Baelor also claimed to have seen a child speaking with doves that answered him in the voice of men and women. Baelor believd the birds spoke with the voices of the Seven, so the king convinced the Most Devout to choose the child as the new High Septon.

Pigeons and doves were part of the immense pie at the wedding at Whitewalls.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Arya Stark, living on the streets of King's Landing after escaping the arrest of Eddard Stark, learns to knock down pigeons with a stick. She eats some raw and sells others to shops in Flea Bottom.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Skewers of pigeon and capon are served during the harvest feast at Winterfell.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

A hundred doves are baked into a great pie for the wedding of King Joffrey I Baratheon and Margaery Tyrell. The royal couple cut the pie with a greatsword, allowing the birds to escape during the wedding feast. Cooked and spiced pigeon pies are served during the celebration.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Paezhar zo Myraq is mocked as the "Little Pigeon" by the Windblown.

The Iron Fleet captures a Myrish cog named Dove in Slaver's Bay.
