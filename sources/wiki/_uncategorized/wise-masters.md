Culture[ ]

Famed for training bed slaves, the Wise Masters are extremely rich.

Maester Yandel considers the Wise Masters to be corrupt.

Recent Events[ ]

A Storm of Swords[ ]

In reaction to the fall of Astapor to Daenerys Targaryen, the Wise Masters hire the Stormcrows and the Second Sons to defend Yunkai. One of the slavers, Grazdan mo Eraz, is sent to offer Daenerys fifty thousand golden marks to leave Slaver's Bay and head to Westeros, but Daenerys refuses. Victorious in the ensuing battle near Yunkai, Daenerys forces  the Yunkai'i free their slaves but does not conquer the city.

Daenerys is successful in her siege of Meereen. Cleon the Great, who has taken control of Astapor, sends Ghael as an envoy to Daenaerys. Ghael informs her that Yunkai is raising new soldiers, hiring more sellswords, and sending envoys to other slave cities. The envoy offers a pact between Meereen and Astapor to defeat the Wise Masters.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

The Wise Masters returned to slaving after Daenerys departed for Meereen.

The Wise Masters and their sellsword companies are successful in their siege of Astapor. The Windblown, under contract from Yunkai, give mocking nicknames to each of the topmost Wise Masters in order to identify them.

The Yunkai'i march north for the second siege of Meereen and establish their slave pens and auction block just south of the Skahazadhan. Yurkhaz zo Yunzak leads the Yunkish army, but after his death the Wise Masters rotate supreme command among themselves.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

By the time hostilities resume between Meereen and Yunkai, the mocking nicknames given by the Windblown to the Wise Masters have spread to the Second Sons. Ser Barristan Selmy sees two trebuchets defend by two dozen Wise Masters, each with their own detachment of slave soldiers.
