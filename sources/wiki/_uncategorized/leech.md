Culture[ ]

Medicinal leeches can be used by maesters to thin the blood and remove "bad blood". Maesters learn how to leech fevers at the Citadel. Healers at the House of the Red Hands in Braavos also use leeches in their treatments.

Roose Bolton, Lord of the Dreadfort, is convinced that regular leechings improve a person's health, and as such frequently has himself leeched, which has led to some calling him the Leech Lord, though never to his face.

History[ ]

Queen Alysanne Targaryen attended a lecture about leechcraft at the Citadel during her visit to Oldtown in 54 AC.

Grand Maester Mellos treated Viserys I Targaryen with leeches after the king cut himself on the Iron Throne and lost two fingers. Grand Maester Orwyle appeared to have more success using potions and tinctures, but Viserys's improvement was short-lived.

Chett grew up in Hag's Mire as the son of a leechman who sold leeches to maesters.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Lord Roose Bolton has himself leeched at Harrenhal, which frightens his squire, Elmar Frey. However, the leechings do not scare Roose's cupbearer "Nan", the incognito Arya Stark, and he has the girl remove leeches from his chest, arms, and legs.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Qyburn leeches the bad blood from Ser Jaime Lannister's eye as part of his treatment at Harrenhal.

Maester Pylos leeches the bad blood from Ser Davos Seaworth after the Onion Knight is returned to Dragonstone from Blackwater Bay. Pylos also leeches Edric Storm, a bastard son of the late King Robert I Baratheon, when the boy becomes sick with a chill. Melisandre, a red priestess of Asshai, tells Stannis Baratheon she can wake a stone dragon with Edric's king's blood. Stannis refuses to allow Melisandre to sacrifice the boy, however, and the king instead throws leeches filled with Edric's blood into flames while stating the names of the "three false kings:" Balon Greyjoy, Robb Stark, and Joffrey Baratheon. Balon falls to his death at Pyke, Robb is killed in the Red Wedding, and Joffrey chokes to death at his wedding feast. Davos has Edric smuggled from Dragonstone to save him from Melisandre.

Ser Gregor Clegane is poisoned by Prince Oberyn Martell during their trial by combat. Grand Maester Pycelle uses leeches on the Mountain in an attempt to heal him, but all of the leeches die.

Maester Colemon uses leeches to treat the shaking sickness of Lord Robert Arryn, but the boy dislikes the treatment.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

According to Aurane Waters, Ser Loras Tyrell has lost so much blood from the siege of Dragonstone that maesters dare not leech him.
