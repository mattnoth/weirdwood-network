## Full Text

Martyn Lannister is a squire from House Lannister. He is the third son of Ser Kevan Lannister and Lady Dorna Swyft and twin to Willem.

In the television adaptation Game of Thrones, Martyn is portrayed by Dean-Charles Chapman.

History[ ]

Lord Gawen Westerling once proposed that his daughter Jeyne wed Martyn or his brother, Willem Lannister, but the twins' father, Ser Kevan Lannister, refused because of House Westerling's connection with House Spicer.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Martyn is a part of the army gathered by Ser Stafford Lannister at Oxcross. He is among those captured when Robb Stark, King in the North, and his army take the Lannister force by surprise in the Battle of Oxcross.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Ser Kevan Lannister ponders having one of his twins, Willem and Martyn, or their cousin, Tion Frey, marry Sansa Stark, but all three boys are held captive at Riverrun.

After the murders of Willem and Tion at the hands of Lord Rickard Karstark, Robb fears for Martyn's survival. Guilt-ridden over Rickard's actions, Robb triples the guard around Martyn and arranges for him to be exchanged for Robett Glover.

In exchange for the freeing of Robett at Duskendale, Robb has Ser Rolph Spicer deliver Martyn to Lannister forces at the Golden Tooth.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

While speaking about Lord Lancel Lannister's decision to leave his wife, Amerei Frey, and Castle Darry in order to swear service as part of the newly reformed Faith Militant, Genna Lannister proposes to Ser Jaime Lannister the idea of Martyn, Lancel's younger brother, marrying Amerei to secure Darry for House Lannister.
