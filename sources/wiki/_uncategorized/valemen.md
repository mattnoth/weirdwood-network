Ethnicity[ ]

The Vale and the Mountains of the Moon were settled in antiquity by the First Men, who established numerous petty kingdoms in the region over many centuries. The Vale was where the Andals landed when they began their invasion of Westeros. Utilizing armament of iron, the Andals defeated the bronze-wielding First Men. Ruling House Arryn descends from one of the oldest and purest lines of Andal nobility. Some of the First Men submitted to the conquerors and accepted the Faith of the Seven, while others fled into the mountains and became the mountain clans considered savage by lowlanders.

Some Sistermen of the Three Sisters have webbing between their hands and feet, which they call the mark.

Religion[ ]

  

A message for the Bloody Gate, by Stephen Najarian ©

The Faith of the Seven, brought by the Andals, is the predominant religion among lowland Valemen. Carvings of seven-pointed stars and double-bladed axes, left by the Andal conquerors, can be found in the Fingers, the Mountains of the Moon, and at the Giant's Lance. House Arryn fostered learning in septries and septs. The mountain clansmen, however, do not follow the Seven.

Customs[ ]

The rich soil of the Vale allows the Valemen to grow fine wheat, barley, corn, pumpkins, and fruits. 

Some consider the knights of the Vale to be the finest of the Seven Kingdoms. The Winged Knight is a legendary figure in the Vale.

Sister's stew is popular in the Three Sisters. Some Sistermen use false lights to cause unsuspecting ships to wreck.

Unlike the feudal lowlanders, the clansmen who live in the Mountains of the Moon have a more egalitarian but also more violent society.

Bastards of noble origin raised in the Vale can be given the surname Stone.
