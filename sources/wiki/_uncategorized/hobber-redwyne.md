Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Hobber Redwyne
 

Hobber is homely, with orange hair and a square, freckled face.

Hobber despises Samwell Tarly, the son of Lord Randyll Tarly. "Ser Slobber", a mocking nickname given to Hobber by Sansa Stark and Jeyne Poole, becomes his address by the entire court. Queen Cersei Lannister is unable to tell the the twins apart and does not consider them to be particularly intelligent. The twins often argue over which one will be the next Lord of the Arbor.

Hobber has a chestnut mare and a black stallion caparisoned in burgundy and blue.

History[ ]

When Samwell Tarly was sent to the Arbor with intent to become Paxter's page and cupbearer, Hobber and Horas despised him on sight. They found a new method to humiliate him in the practice yard every day. One day, Hobber dressed a kitchen girl in his armor and allowed her to beat Sam with a wooden sword until he cried. When she revealed herself, all the squires and pages and stableboys laughed.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Ser Hobber and his brother, Ser Horas, take part in the Hand's tourney in King's Landing. Sansa Stark and Jeyne Poole mock Hobber and his brother, Ser Horas, as Ser Slobber and Ser Horror, respectively, and giggle whenever they see them.

The twins remain in the capital after the death of King Robert I Baratheon, and ignore Sansa Stark when she attends King Joffrey I Baratheon's first court session in the throne room.

Arya Stark, hiding in Flea Bottom, is nearly run down by the twins and their guardsmen as they race to the Great Sept of Baelor. The twins are present for the execution of Lord Eddard Stark at the Great Sept.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

The Iron Throne keeps the Redwyne twins as hostages for the good behavior of their father, Lord Paxter Redwyne, during the War of the Five Kings. Unlike other lords from the Reach, Paxter refrains from joining his forces to that of Renly Baratheon, the uncle and rival of King Joffrey.

During the tourney on King Joffrey's name day, Hobber faces Ser Meryn Trant of the Kingsguard, and they both break their first lances but remain seated. However, on the second round, Ser Meryn's lance strikes Hobber in the chest and unhorses him. Joffrey disapproves of Hobber's performance, while Horas rushes out to help his dazed brother from the field.

Hobber and Horas attempt to sneak out of King's Landing by bribing a guard and sailing away on Moonrunner, but the plot is discovered by Lord Varys. Tyrion Lannister, the Hand of the King, has the guard seized and sent to the Night's Watch, and the twins are placed under closer supervision.

The twins are in attendance when Tyrion holds court in the throne room, and they ignore Sansa Stark after the Battle of Oxcross.

After Renly's death, Hobber is allowed to accompany Lord Petyr Baelish on his mission to recruit House Tyrell at Bitterbridge while Horas remains in captivity at the Red Keep. This show of good faith allows Paxter to ride with the Tyrell host to defend the capital during the Battle of the Blackwater. Paxter and his sons are among those honored in the throne room after the battle.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Sansa notices one of the Redwyne twins sparring with Ser Tallad the Tall in an inner yard of the Red Keep.

Lord Tywin Lannister, now Joffrey's Hand, considers having his daughter, the widowed Queen Cersei Lannister, wed one of the Redwyne twins.

Ser Hobber attends the wedding of Margaery Tyrell and King Joffrey I Baratheon at the Great Sept, but the boy king is poisoned during the wedding feast. Hobber is called as a witness for the prosecution in the Tyrion's trial for Joffrey's death, and relates that he saw Tyrion fill the wedding chalice.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Hobber, Horas, and their father Paxter attend Tywin's funeral at the Great Sept.

According to Taena Merryweather, the twins are both in love with Queen Margaery, now the wife of King Tommen I Baratheon, forgetting their arguments over who will be the next Lord of the Arbor and instead wanting to join the Kingsguard just to be near her. Hobber and Horas visit with Margaery often, with Hobber bringing fruit and flowers and Horas taking up the lute. During one of Margaery's riding expeditions, the brothers escort Meredyth Crane and Janna Fossoway. On another occasion, Queen Regent Cersei Lannister sees Ser Osmund Kettleblack sparring in the yard with one of the brothers.

As part of her plan to remove Margaery, Cersei has the Blue Bard include the Redwyne twins among those alleged to have slept with the younger queen. So as not to lose the support of Redwyne fleet, Cersei intends for the brothers to be falsely accused. Arrest warrants are signed for Hobber and Horas, and they are given comfortable rooms in a tower.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

The Redwyne twins are declared innocent.
