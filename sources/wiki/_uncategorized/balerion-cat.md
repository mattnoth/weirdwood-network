## Full Text

For others with the same name, see Balerion (disambiguation).

Balerion was a kitten belonging to Princess Rhaenys Targaryen, who liked to pretend he was the great dragon, Balerion the Black Dread. After Rhaenys was killed in the Sack of King's Landing in 283 AC, it is unknown what became of her kitten.

Many readers believe that Balerion grew up to be the vicious black tomcat with a torn ear that still lives in the Red Keep; this has been somewhat confirmed by George R. R. Martin.

Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Balerion

The cat believed to be Balerion is an old black male cat, with one chewed and torn ear that leads him to appear one-eared. He is filthy and foul-tempered, and has been known to attack ravens in the rookery of the Red Keep.

History[ ]

The black tomcat in the Red Keep once stole a roast quail served to Lord Tywin Lannister at a dinner with King Robert I Baratheon. He also once clawed Prince Joffrey Baratheon's hand.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Arya Stark chases the old black tomcat. Art by Sir-Heartsalot ©

‎Arya Stark is assigned by Syrio Forel to catch every cat in the Red Keep, and she succeeds with all, except for one mean one-eared black tomcat. She chases him halfway across the castle before she finally catches him, and kisses his forehead while he tries to claw her. Prince Tommen Baratheon, his sister Princess Myrcella, and their septa observe this, and believe that Arya is a strange peasant boy who does not belong in that part of the castle. The septa commands red cloaks to grab Arya, but she escapes.

Lord Varys speaks to Lord Eddard Stark when he is imprisoned in the black cells of the Red Keep, and tells him about Princess Rhaenys Targaryen and her kitten.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Sansa Stark, visiting the godswood of the Red Keep late at night, encounters the one-eared black tom. He brushes against her leg, then spits at her and runs away.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Tommen, now king, is gifted three black kittens by his wife, Queen Margaery Tyrell. The boy king's mother, Queen Regent Cersei Lannister, wishes they were not black, as she feels Princess Rhaenys's black kitten brought her bad luck.

Arya Stark, as the Braavos street urchin and fishmonger Cat of the Canals, encounters many cats, one of which reminds her of the one-eared cat she had chased in the Red Keep.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

King Tommen Baratheon tells his uncle, Lord Regent Kevan Lannister, that the "bad cat" had been outside his window the previous night, but his kitten Ser Pounce had hissed at him and he ran off. Cersei explains about the old black tom, who once attacked her son Joffrey.
