Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Ser Morton is one of Lady Lysa Arryn's suitors after the death of Lord Jon Arryn.

Lady Anya Waynwood is present in the High Hall of the Eyrie, surrounded by her sons, when Tyrion Lannister demands a trial by battle to prove his innocence of the claims against him. Morton attempts to convince Catelyn Stark that Ser Vardis Egen, a knight, will make short work of Bronn, a mere sellsword, during the duel. However Bronn unexpectedly wins and slays Ser Vardis.

Quotes[ ]

Eon: Ser Vardis is a doughty fighter. He will make short work of the sellsword.

Catelyn: Will he, my lord? I wonder.

Morton: Women understand little of these things. Ser Vardis is a knight, sweet lady. This other fellow, well, his sort are all cowards at heart. Useful enough in a battle, with thousands of their fellows around them, but stand them up alone and the manhood leaks right out of them.

—Eon Hunter, Catelyn Stark, and Morton

Notes[ ]

↑ According to the Alayne sample chapter from The Winds of Winter, Roland is the grandson of Lady Anya Waynwood and the nephew of Ser Wallace Waynwood, Anya's third son. Unless one of Anya's daughters married a Waynwood, this only leaves Anya's two oldest sons as Roland's progenitor. Since the second oldest son, Ser Donnel Waynwood, was born in either 278 or 277 AC (see Donnel Waynwood), and Roland was born in or between 274 AC and 281 AC (see Roland Waynwood), it would be impossible for Roland to have been Donnel's son, leaving only Morton.
