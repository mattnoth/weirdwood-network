Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Jeyne Westerling

Jeyne is a pretty girl. She has curly chestnut hair, a heart-shaped face, a shy smile, and doe-like soft brown eyes. Jeyne is slender and willowy, with small, apple-sized breasts. Catelyn Stark thinks that though Jeyne is slender, she has good hips, and should have no trouble bearing children.

Although she is shy, Jeyne is polite and caring. Kevan Lannister thought she was a sweet child, and Jaime Lannister thinks that as an adolescent, Jeyne is more awkward than graceful.

History[ ]

House Westerling is an old house who descend from the First Men. Despite their ancient heritage and nobility, the family is not rich, as their mines have failed. Possibly to better his finances, Lord Gawen Westerling wed Sybell Spicer, the daughter of an ennobled family, House Spicer, who descend from a spice merchant and his wife, a maegi of Essos who provided potions to the people of Lannisport. Gawen once offered his daughter Jeyne to Ser Kevan Lannister as a match for one of his sons, Willem or Martyn, but Kevan rejected the offer because of the "doubtful blood" of the Spicers.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Robb Stark, the Young Wolf, agrees to marry a daughter of Lord Walder Frey as part of the alliance between Houses Stark and Frey at the start of the War of the Five Kings. Jeyne's father, Lord Gawen Westerling, is captured by Robb's army during the battle in the Whispering Wood. Robb becomes King in the North.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Jeyne nursing Robb Stark, by Lauren Cannon © Fantasy Flight Games

Gawen is held as a hostage at Seagard.

During Robb Stark's westerlands campaign, his army captures the Crag, the ancestral keep of House Westerling. Robb is wounded by an arrow in the battle, and Jeyne's mother, Sybell, and uncle, Ser Rolph Spicer, encourage the girl to nurse him. While recuperating at the Crag, Robb receives a message that his younger brothers, Bran and Rickon, have been killed by his former friend, Theon Greyjoy, who captured Winterfell. Jeyne comforts Robb in his grief, and they sleep together. To protect her honor after taking her maidenhead, Robb marries her the next day, but the marriage ends Robb's alliance with Lord Walder. The outraged Freys take their forces at the Crag and Riverrun back to the Twins. Black Walder Frey threatens to kill Jeyne so that a widower Robb could then marry a Frey, but Jeyne begs her husband to be merciful, and Black Walder is spared.

While at Harrenhal, Robb's incognito sister, Arya, learns from Elmar Frey that his House has been dishonored.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

  

Robb Stark introduces Jeyne to his mother, Catelyn Stark. By Elenyaart ©

When Robb Stark returns to Riverrun from his campaign in the westerlands, he introduces his mother, Catelyn Stark, to the Westerlings and Spicers, and to his new wife, Jeyne. Catelyn learns how Robb and Jeyne met and married, and that this is the reason the Frey alliance has been broken. Though she approves of Jeyne personally, Catelyn is distraught at the news; however, because Robb has forgiven her for her own actions done in grief for the deaths of Bran and Rickon, freeing the hostage Jaime Lannister, she has no choice but to forgive him in turn. She welcomes Jeyne and her family to Riverrun. Nevertheless, Catelyn worries that Robb no longer keeps Grey Wind with him because he makes Jeyne anxious, though Robb explains it is only because the direwolf killed a knight at the Crag whom Jeyne had known for years.

At a meeting of the small council in the Red Keep in King's Landing, Lord Tywin Lannister informs his son Tyrion of the marriage of Robb and Jeyne. Tyrion is astonished that Robb would reject the Freys for the much smaller support of the Westerlings. Tyrion also wonders why the Westerlings do not fear Tywin's wrath, citing the example of the destruction of the Reynes and Tarbecks, and why Tywin does not seem to be enraged, but instead pleased at the news. Tywin only tells him that the Westerlings are very aware of Castamere, and that Tyrion must soon marry Sansa Stark, Robb's sister and current heir, hostage in the Red Keep.

Jeyne and Robb are devoted to each other and frequently try to conceive an heir, which both Catelyn and Sybell agree is important. Sybell gives her daughter herbal possets to drink every morning, claiming it will help make her fertile. Jeyne tells Catelyn that she thinks she will be pregnant very soon. Jeyne also tells her that she tries hard to be a good wife for Robb, although she is worried about the pressure he is under as king. On Catelyn's advice, Jeyne tries to be patient and understanding. Robb, for his part, is able to smile and be temporarily without worries when he is in her company. 

  

Jeyne watches Robb Stark depart, by David Auden Nash © Fantasy Flight Games

After negotiations, Walder Frey agrees to ally again with the Starks in return for the marriage of Robb's uncle, Lord Edmure Tully, to Roslin Frey, and a personal apology from Robb.  When the Stark host leaves for the Twins, Robb chooses to keep Jeyne and most of her family at Riverrun, under the protection of Ser Brynden Tully, to avoid extra tension with the Freys. However, Jeyne is extremely reluctant to be separated from her husband. Robb and Jeyne say farewell three times, once in the godswood at Riverrun, once at the portcullis of the castle, and the third time an hour north of the Tumblestone, when Jeyne catches up with the army on a lathered horse. Though Robb is touched by her desire to accompany him, he sends Jeyne back to Riverrun with a dozen men to guard her.

Outside of the wedding of Edmure and Roslin at the Twins, Arya is puzzled when she hears a feasting northman cheer the Young Wolf and Queen Jeyne. Within the Twins, Houses Frey and Bolton betray and kill Robb, beginning the Red Wedding. Ser Kevan Lannister later instructs King Tommen I Baratheon to pardon the Westerlings and grant the title of Lord of Castamere to Rolph.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Although most of the river lords submit to the Iron Throne, Riverrun remains under the control of Brynden Tully, who refuses to surrender the castle or the Westerlings to Lannister or Frey forces. Jeyne goes into mourning for Robb, to the chagrin of her mother, who had been in secret negotiations with Lord Tywin Lannister. Jeyne refuses to give up the crown that Robb had given her, and when Sybell forcefully takes it away, Jeyne fights her mother, receiving a scratch on her forehead that eventually scabs over. 

The siege of Riverrun is settled peacefully by Ser Jaime Lannister in an agreement with the captive Edmure Tully. When Jaime interviews the Westerlings and asks if Jeyne is pregnant with Robb's child, Jeyne tries to flee, sobbing, while Sybell explains that she is not, as she had made sure of that per Lord Tywin's orders. Unbeknownst to Jeyne, the daily potions given to her by Sybell actually prevented pregnancy. Jaime agrees with Sybell that per her negotiations with Tywin Lannister, Jeyne and her sister, Eleyna, are to marry lords or heirs, but that Jeyne must remain unmarried for two years, to avoid tales that her dead husband Robb had given her a child and thus an heir to the north. Jaime tasks Ser Forley Prester with escorting Edmure and the Westerlings to the westerlands. Jaime tells Forley to have archers kill Jeyne if there is any attempt at escape. Before Jeyne leaves, Jaime notices that she has ripped her finely-made clothes in an act of mourning for Robb and rebellion against her mother.

Family[ ]

Jeyne has two brothers, Ser Raynald and Rollam, and a younger sister, Eleyna. Her elder brother Raynald was present at the Red Wedding, at which he freed Grey Wind and tried to escape the Freys by jumping in the Green Fork. However, he is presumed dead. Her younger brother Rollam served as Robb's squire.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

Due to the original discrepancy in the descriptions of Jeyne from Catelyn Stark in A Storm of Swords and Jaime Lannister A Feast for Crows, readers once speculated that the Jeyne presented to Jaime was an imposter, perhaps her sister Eleyna, perhaps to protect the fact that the real Jeyne was pregnant; however, since George R. R. Martin has confirmed that discrepancy was an error and current editions of A Feast for Crows have corrected it, the theory is baseless.

Martin has teased the fact that Jeyne would make an appearance in the prologue chapter of The Winds of Winter.
