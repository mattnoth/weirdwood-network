Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Prince Bran Stark, the heir of his elder brother Robb, is the Stark in Winterfell while Robb, the King in the North, campaigns in the south amid the War of the Five Kings.

Theon Greyjoy takes Bran and Rickon Stark prisoner with the capture of Winterfell and sits the throne within the castle's Great Hall. His sister Asha later mocks him for his inability to properly hold Winterfell, calling him Prince of Fools.

Prior to the battle at Winterfell, the outnumbered Theon tells Ser Rodrik Cassel's army of northmen that all who swear fealty to his father, Balon Greyjoy, as their king and to Theon as Prince of Winterfell will be confirmed in their rights. However, Theon is captured by Ramsay Snow during the sack of Winterfell.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

After noticing other people at Queenscrown, Bran tells himself not to be afraid, as he was the Prince of Winterfell. 

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

  

Theon Greyjoy, Ramsay Bolton, and "Reek", by NotanerArt ©

Having been tortured by House Bolton and the now-legitimized Ramsay, Theon emerges from the Dreadfort as "Reek". While Theon had been arrogant and cocksure during his brief time as Prince of Winterfell, "Reek" is traumatized and ruined.

Theon travels under a peace banner to present Ramsay Bolton's terms of rendition during the siege of Moat Cailin. Since Theon is the son of the late Balon Greyjoy, King of the Isles and the Rivers, Ramsay instructs Theon to act as a prince when speaking with the ironborn garrison guarding the three towers. However, Ramsay reminds Theon that he is still truly "Reek" at heart.

Later in the godswood of Winterfell, Theon is chosen to ceremoniously present "Arya Stark" (actually Jeyne Poole) in marriage to Ramsay, since Theon was the longtime ward of Eddard Stark, Lord of Winterfell, and it is believed that Arya's trueborn brothers are dead. Ramsay mocks Theon as Prince of Winterfell before his bedding with Jeyne, and a guard later mocks Theon as the Prince of Stink.
