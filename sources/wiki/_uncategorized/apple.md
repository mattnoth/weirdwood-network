Culture[ ]

Apples can be eaten by themselves or be prepared in cuisine, including baked apples, dried apples, apple crisps, applecakes, apple tarts, stewed apples, applesauce, buns, oatbread, and in salads. Apples are sometimes included in bowls of brown.

Cider is a popular alcoholic beverage made from fermented apples. Wine can be made from green apples.

Apples are included in the sigils of House Fossoway of Cider Hall and House Fossoway of New Barrel. Ser Derrick Fossoway, one of the Ninepenny Kings, was known as the Bad Apple.

History[ ]

Foss the Archer, a legendary son of Garth Greenhand, is remembered for shooting apples off the heads of maids he fancied. Rowan Gold-Tree, one of Garth's legendary daughters, is said to grown a tree from an apple wrapped in her golden hair.

According to Septon Barth, Lady Florence Fossoway's bookkeeping skills came from "counting apples since she learned to walk".

Queen Daenaera Velaryon was the target of an assassination attempt when the crusts of her apple tarts were poisoned.

Ser Arlan of Pennytree often gave apples to Chestnut. When Ser Duncan the Tall sold Sweetfoot to Henly, he gave a few [[currency|coppers] back so that the horse could have some oats and an apple.

Lord Randyll Tarly forbade his son Samwell from eating apples while at the Arbor after Lord Paxter Redwyne compared the youth to a pig with an apple in its mouth.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Lord Petyr Baelish eats an apple and tosses it into the Blackwater Rush while waiting for Lord Eddard Stark. Eddard learns from Jon Arryn's former stableboy that Lord Arryn always brought carrots and apples for his horses. Eddard sees farmers selling apples at the market near the River Gate.

Baked apples fragrant with cinnamon are served during the Hand's tourney.

Jon Snow has applecakes and blood sausage for breakfast at Castle Black. Jon takes a small withered brown apple when he attempts to desert from the Night's Watch.

Before killing the stallion which will be used for Khal Drogo's funeral pyre, Aggo feeds it a withered apple.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Lothor Brune eating an apple, by Alexandre Dainche © Fantasy Flight Games

Queen Regent Cersei Lannister recalls that the boar which had killed King Robert I Baratheon was served with mushrooms and apples at his funeral feast.

Yoren has his young recruits gather apples from orchards.

Apples are among the goods brought by the Myraham to Lordsport.

Baked apples are served at the harvest feast in Winterfell, and apple crisps are served at Bitterbridge.

Remaining behind to protect his sworn brothers from wildlings in the Skirling Pass, Squire Dalbridge asks for his garron to be given an apple once it is home.

Lothor Brune becomes known as Lothor Apple-Eater after capturing Ser Jon from House Fossoway of New Barrel and killing Ser Bryan and Ser Edwyd from House Fossoway of Cider Hall during the Battle of the Blackwater.

Meera Reed finds a big sack of apples in the kitchens of Winterfell.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Traveling in the riverlands, Arya Stark, Gendry, and Hot Pie pass through a burned village in which a dozen men hang from apple trees. Arya later throws a crabapple at Gendry.

Bran Stark and his companions see a dozen apple trees among the inn at Queenscrown.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

  

Alleras holding an apple, by elalendi ©

At the Quill and Tankard, Alleras uses apples thrown by Mollander for target practice.

Brienne Tarth sees a grove of apple trees at the Quiet Isle.

Ser Addam Marbrand's scouts find that some of Ser Daven Lannister's foragers have been hanged underneath a crabapple tree near Riverrun, with a crabapple shoved into each victim's mouth.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Lord Davos Seaworth speaks with an apple seller at Fishfoot Yard in White Harbor.

Apples are among the rations given by the Night's Watch to free folk at Mole's Town.

The ironborn of Asha Greyjoy drink Galbart Glover's apple wine.
