Varieties[ ]

Common alcoholic drinks include beer, fermented milk, hippocras, liquors, mulled spirits, rum, mead, cider, and wine. They are often served to the public in beer halls, brothels, inns, taverns, and winesinks. Lords often have beverages available in their halls and keeps as well.

Alcoholism[ ]

Some characters in A Song of Ice and Fire suffer from alcoholism.

Alcoholics[ ]

  

King Robert I Baratheon in his cups, by Joshua Cairós © Fantasy Flight Games

King Robert I Baratheon, considered a drunkard by Sansa Stark, Cersei Lannister, Jaime Lannister, and Barristan Selmy.

Ser Bartimus, who is usually drunk, according to Ser Davos Seaworth.

Ser Boros Blount, said to be half-drunk half the time according to Ser Osmund Kettleblack

Septon Cellador of Castle Black, considered a drunkard by Jon Snow, and Melisandre. He is seldom sober.

The Drunken Daughter, a prostitute in Braavos.

Dagon Greyjoy, also known as "Dagon the Drunkard".

Merrett Frey, who has the reputation of being the biggest drinker at the Twins.

Ser Ryman Frey, who is considered to be drunk more often than not.

Hammer, considered a drunk by Kem.

Maester Harmune, at Eastwatch-by-the-Sea, who is drunk more often than not.

Ser Dontos Hollard, considered a drunkard by Sansa Stark.

Tyrion Lannister, considered a drunk by Jon Connington. Tyrion is eventually forced into recuperation.

Merrel and Morgan, guards left at Castle Black to protect Melisandre.

Septon Moon, renowned as a lecherous drunkard.

Rymolf Stormdrunk, considered a drunk by Asha Greyjoy.

Ser Victor Risley, considered a sot by Mushroom.

Racallio Ryndoon, remembered as a glutton and a drunkard.

Ser Willam Stafford, remembered as the Drunken Knight.

Prince Daeron Targaryen, remembered by history as "Daeron the Drunken"

Princess Saera Targaryen, who attended prayers while drunk.

Todric, considered a drunkard by Theon Greyjoy.

Lord Bertrand Tyrell, known to have been a drunkard.

Lord Harmond Umber, remembered as the Drunken Giant

Lord Jon Umber, considered a frequent drinker by Catelyn Stark.

Ser Ulf White, remembered as a drunkard.

Morghaz zo Zherzyn, mocked as the "Drunken Conqueror" for his alcoholism.

Alcoholic tendencies[ ]

  

Robert I Baratheon and Cersei Lannister, by David Sourwine © Fantasy Flight Games

Dareon, who oft becomes drunk on firewine.

Cersei Lannister, who, following her rise to power, always appears to have a flagon of wine nearby, according to her brother Jaime.

Mollander, a novice at the Citadel, who begins to drink heavily after receiving news of his father's death in the Battle of the Blackwater.

Reformed alcoholics[ ]

Aeron Greyjoy, who drank a lot in his youth, but changed following a near-death experience.

Thoros of Myr, who always had a taste for drinking and was a drinking companion to King Robert I Baratheon, before experiencing a spiritual awakening.

The queen was in her cups, Jaime realized. Of late, Cersei always seemed to have a flagon of wine to hand, she who had once scorned Robert Baratheon for his drinking.
