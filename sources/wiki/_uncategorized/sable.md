Culture[ ]

Sables are hunted for their fur. Ser Waymar Royce has a long sable cloak, and Ser Jaremy Rykker has a heavy cloak richly trimmed with sable. Ramsay Snow also has a sable cloak. Wisdom Hallyne has striped black-and-scarlet robes trimmed with sable. Arnolf Karstark's grey wool cloak is trimmed with black sable. Sable trims the collar of Ser Denys Mallister and accents the sleeves of his black velvet doublet.

Sable Hall is one of the abandoned castles of the Night's Watch along the Wall.

History[ ]

While drinking wine in the barracks of Castle Black, Gared joked that Ser Waymar Royce personally killed the sables for his fine cloak.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Waymar Royce facing Others while wearing a sable cloak, by Jon Neimeister © Fantasy Flight Games

Ser Waymar Royce's splendid sable cloak is slashed when the young ranger is attacked by Others in the haunted forest.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Thoren Smallwood claims Ser Jaremy Rykker's sable cloak after his death.

Lord Mace Tyrell and two of his sons, Ser Garlan and Ser Loras, wear green velvet trimmed with sable while in the throne room after the Battle of the Blackwater.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

King Tommen I Baratheon wears cloth-of-gold beneath a sable mantle while attending the funeral of his grandfather, Lord Tywin Lannister.

Euron Greyjoy claims a sable cloak from Lord Baelor Blacktyde after having him executed.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Lady Walda Bolton wears a sable hood when Lord Roose Bolton returns to the north, and Lady Barbrey Dustin wraps herself in sable at Winterfell.
