Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Kojja Mo

Kojja is slender, and is taller than Samwell Tarly. She has smooth skin, as black as polished jet. She has extremely white teeth.

Kojja captains the Cinnamon Wind's red archers, and is an expert marksman with her double-curved goldenheart bow. She is able to speak at least the Summer and Common Tongues.

Recent Events[ ]

A Feast for Crows[ ]

  

Kojja Mo sings to baby Aemon Steelsong while Gilly relaxes. Illustrated by Amuelia ©

Kojja serves as the translator for the crew of the Cinnamon Wind while Samwell Tarly and Maester Aemon and Gilly travel aboard the ship. While Gilly is initially afraid of Kojja's appearance due to her sheltered upbringing, she soon discovers she is a good person, and trusts her with her baby. Kojja loves to bounce him upon her knee and sing to him in the Summer Tongue.

Kojja shoots a dozen pirates who try to board the Cinnamon Wind near the Stepstones on their journey to Oldtown. At Maester Aemon's funeral aboard the ship, Kojja translates Sam's eulogy for the rest of the crew.

After the funeral, the Summer Islanders drink rum and have sex to honor the dead. Kojja would have gone with Xhondo Dhoru, but instead she takes care of the baby, to give Gilly and Sam a chance to be intimate. The next day, Sam avoids Gilly over the shame of breaking his Night's Watch vows, until Xhondo drags him to Kojja. She confronts Sam on the matter, and threatens to put him off the ship unless he goes to Gilly. Kojja tells Sam that he honored the dead and the gods with their lovemaking, and that Gilly is aware she cannot marry him, but only wants his companionship for a time.

As the Cinnamon Wind travels through the Redwyne Straits, Kojja Mo has her archers shoot at an ironborn longship that approaches the ship, and it veers away. When they approach Oldtown, the captain of the Huntress boards the ship with six knights and asks to inspect the holds. Quhuru Mo confers with his daughter before allowing them to do so. When Sam leaves the ship at Oldtown, Kojja tells him that they will stay in port as long as necessary to sell their cargo and restock, but that Gilly can stay aboard as long as she likes.
