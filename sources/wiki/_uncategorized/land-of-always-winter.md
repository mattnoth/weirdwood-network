## Full Text

The Land of Always Winter, by Rene Aigner ©

The Land of Always Winter, also called the Lands of Always Winter, is the northernmost known part of Westeros, far beyond the Wall. The Frostfangs and several large lakes separate them from the haunted forest. 

The Land of Always Winter is permanently locked in winter and perpetually frozen. Mostly unexplored, it is near the Thenn valley. It is believed that the Others come from this region.

History[ ]

According to Maester Yandel, the children of the forest and giants lived in the Land of Always Winter during the Dawn Age. In the Age of Heroes following the Pact, Westeros was settled by petty kingdoms of the First Men from the Land of Always Winter to the Summer Sea. According to legend, the Others came from the Land of Always Winter during the Long Night.

A Game of Thrones[ ]

While comatose, Bran Stark has a vision in which he looks past the Wall far to the north. After passing the enormous haunted forest and then dead plains without vegetation, he becomes frightened after looking deep into the heart of winter.

And he looked past the Wall, past endless forests cloaked in snow, past the frozen shore and the great blue-white rivers of ice and the dead plains where nothing grew or lived, to the curtain of light at the end of the world, and then beyond that curtain. He looked deep into the heart of winter, and then he cried out, afraid, and the heat of his tears burned on his cheeks.
