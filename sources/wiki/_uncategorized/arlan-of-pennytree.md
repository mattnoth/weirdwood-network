"Arlan" redirects here. For others with the same name, see Arlan (disambiguation).

Arlan of Pennytree was a hedge knight and the master of Dunk, the later Ser Duncan the Tall.

Arlan's coat-of-arms was a winged chalice, silver on brown. It is unknown if he had any connection with House Hersy, whose arms also feature a winged chalice.

In the television adaptation A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms, Arlan is portrayed by Danny Webb.

Appearance and Character[ ]

Arlan was a small, slim man. Dunk thought the hedge knight was closer to sixty than fifty when he died.

Arlan was a kindly man, generous with giving praise. He enjoyed watching sunsets. The knight spoke highly of Baelor Targaryen, Prince of Dragonstone, and Leo Tyrell, Lord of Highgarden. Arlan affectionately claimed that Dunk was "thick as a castle wall and slow as an aurochs."

Arlan's weaponry included a longsword, an eight-foot ash war lance, and a dagger. His armor included a chain-mail hauberk, a dented iron halfhelm, and greaves and gorget. His battered oak shield depicted his winged chalice sigil.

At the time of his death Arlan owned a chipped garnet and three horses, which Dunk then inherited:

Thunder, Arlan's warhorse ridden only in tourney and battle

Chestnut, a swaybacked stot ridden by Dunk

Sweetfoot, a female palfrey

History[ ]

Arlan was born in the village of Pennytree in the riverlands. When Arlan was a little boy, his grandfather took him to King's Landing, where he saw the last dragon. The dragon died a year later.

In 193 AC, Ser Arlan unhorsed Lord Stokeworth and the Bastard of Harrenhal in a melee at King's Landing. Years prior to that, he had unhorsed Ser Damon Lannister in a tourney at Lannisport. In 200 AC, Arlan was defeated by Prince Baelor Targaryen, Prince of Dragonstone, in a tourney at Storm's End. Arlan later said he broke seven lances against Baelor, while Baelor remembered four lances. Arlan chose never to joust again after that prestigious showing.

Arlan's nephew, Roger of Pennytree, was his squire. During the First Blackfyre Rebellion, Arlan and Roger fought beneath Lord Hayford's banners, taking part in the Battle of the Redgrass Field, during which Roger was killed. Arlan was next to Lord Hayford when the lord was slain by Lord Gormon Peake.

At some later point Arlan met Dunk in the slums of Flea Bottom in King's Landing, and took him for his squire. The two of them remained together for years. Arlan and Ser Bennis of the Brown Shield were among knights hired by a Dornish merchant to escort him from Lannisport to the Prince's Pass. Arlan and Dunk also served at Highgarden for a time, and they also marched with a few peasant levies. Arlan once helped hang an outlaw who had been stealing rings. Arlan also served a minor lord in the stormlands in a regional dispute.

Three years before the tourney at Ashford Meadow, Arlan and Dunk served Lord Dondarrion in the battle with the Vulture King. Also three years before the Ashford tourney, Arlan spent a half year in service to Lord Florent. Once, a drunken Arlan promised to take Dunk to a brothel, but the knight did not remember the promise once sober.

Arlan died of a chill on the road leading to Ashford Meadow in 209 AC. Dunk buried Arlan's body on the western slope of a hill, facing the sunset. Dunk continued on to the tourney. At Ashford he claimed to Plummer that Arlan had knighted him as Ser Duncan the Tall before his passing, although there were no witnesses to the ceremony. Two years later, Duncan also claimed to Lady Rohanne Webber that he had been knighted by Arlan.

Family[ ]

Arlan had a sister whose name is not mentioned in the books. Her son, Roger, served Arlan as a squire until he was killed in the Battle of the Redgrass Field.
