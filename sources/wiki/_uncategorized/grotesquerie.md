Culture[ ]

Smallfolk often quietly kill dwarf infants, or sell them to a slaver's grotesquerie.

Purported "centaur skeletons" occasionally turn up in grotesqueries.

One of the triarchs of Volantis has a grotesquerie.

Yezzan's grotequerie[ ]

Yezzan zo Qaggaz, a Wise Master of Yunkai and an extremely wealthy slave trader, is obsessed with grotesques. He often purchases slaves with physical deformities to add to his "collection". Yezzan's grotequerie includes a boy with hairy, deformed legs resembling a goat's legs, a bearded woman, a feeble-minded two-headed girl from Mantarys, and a hermaphrodite named Sweets, who also sleeps with him. Yezzan used to own a giant, who he would make copulate with slave women so he could watch. However, the giant died, and Yezzan would pay much gold to acquire a new one. The slaves in Yezzan's collection wear gilded iron collars, with his name incised in Valyrian glyphs; a pair of tiny bells are affixed below the ears, so the wearer produces a tinkling sound with every step.

Recent events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

When talking to Jon Snow, Tyrion Lannister reflects on his dwarfism, and states that had he been born a peasant, he might have been sold to some slaver's grotesquerie.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

When meeting Tyrion Lannister during the tourney on King Joffrey's nameday, Sansa Stark muses that Tyrion walks like something out of a grotesquerie.

After Queen Cersei tells Tyrion she has found his mistress, Tyrion tells himself he must be hard and unmoving to pass this test, or else he had better seek out a grotesquerie.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

  

Tyrion Lannister, Sweets, and Penny in Yezzan's grotequerie. Art by mylestoyne ©

When Haldon challenges Qavo Nogarys to a game of cyvasse with Tyrion, Nogarys asks if Tyrion is for sale, as one of the Triarchs of Volantis is seeking to purchase a cyvasse-playing dwarf for his grotesquerie.

During the Windblown's march to Meereen, Dick Straw tells Quentyn Martell some of the gossip about Yezzan zo Qaggaz and his passion for grotesques.

After Tyrion, Penny, and Jorah Mormont are captured by slavers, they are bought by Yezzan outside of Meereen. The dwarfs Tyrion and Penny are added to Yezzan's grotesquerie, and are made to perform before his guests.

Yezzan becomes deathly ill from the pale mare epidemic, and Tyrion, Penny, and Jorah escape to the camp of the Second Sons during the chaos. Afterwards, when Penny listlessly says they were better off with Yezzan, Tyrion lies to her about the rest of Yezzan's "treasures", saying that all of them fell victim to the pale mare, though he does not know their actual fate.

Behind the scenes[ ]
