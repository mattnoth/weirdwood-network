Usage[ ]

Drums are used to assemble an army into formation, to give a command, and to help set a marching pace. Oarmasters on ships use drums to set a rhythm for oarsmen to follow.

Culture[ ]

A round tower that has a longer diameter length than height is called a "drum tower" due to its shape ressembling the instrument. Drum towers can be found throughout Westeros such as the Red Keep, Storm's End, the First Keep of Winterfell, or the Dun Fort. 

Recent events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Drums are used at the wedding of Daenerys Targaryen and Khal Drogo.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Lord Balon Greyjoy hosts his captains in a feast in the Great Keep of Pyke where music is played by fiddles, skins and drums.

Tyrion Lannister compares the three Kettleblack brothers to hollow drums.

When Ser Edmure Tully returns to Riverrun after the Battle of the Fords, the castle is celebrating. Rymund the Rhymer plays his harp, accompanied by a pair of drummers and a youth with a set of reed pipes.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Fiddlers, flutists and drummers performe "Flowers of Spring" at the wedding of Lord Edmure Tully and Roslin Frey.

The gallery of the throne room is packed with musicians such as drummers during the wedding of King Joffrey I Baratheon and Margaery Tyrell.

Drums are used by the wildlings during the Battle of Castle Black.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Drums play in a slow and measured tone for the honor procession of Lord Tywin Lannister's casket.
