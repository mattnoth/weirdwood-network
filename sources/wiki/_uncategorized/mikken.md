## Full Text

Mikken is the blacksmith at Winterfell.

In the television adaptation Game of Thrones, Mikken is portrayed by Boyd Rankin.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Mikken crafts for Jon Snow the sword Needle, which Jon gifts to his half-sister Arya Stark.

While Arya is in King's Landing, her father, Lord Eddard Stark, discovers Needle and recognizes Mikken's maker's mark.

Rickon Stark is upset, and his direwolf Shaggydog bites Mikken, tearing a chunk of flesh from his thigh.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Boyd Rankin as Mikken in the HBO adaptation

In order for Bran Stark to get around his room, Mikken installs metal bars along the walls so Bran can pull himself around. 

Jojen Reed foresees the death of Mikken in his green dreams, seeing him drowned when the sea flows over Winterfell's walls, but Mikken laughs it off when Bran tells him. 

When Theon Greyjoy captures Winterfell, Mikken angrily refuses to bend the knee, and is beaten by Stygg, an ironborn raider in Theon's service. After Mikken insults Theon and the ironborn, Stygg drives his spear through Mikken's neck.

Theon has a nightmare of eating with the dead at Winterfell, including Mikken.

When Bran and his companions leave Winterfell after its sack, Osha carries the sword from Eddard's tomb, one of the last swords to bear Mikken's mark.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Theon, who has been captured and tortured by Ramsay Snow, wanders through the destroyed structures of Winterfell, including the ruins of Mikken's forge.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

Theon Greyjoy and Jeyne Poole escape Winterfell, and are discovered by Mors Umber. Crowfood asks Jeyne questions about the castle to confirm her assumed identity of "Arya Stark", such as the names of servants, including the cook and the smith. Jeyne has a little trouble recalling Mikken's name, frustrating Theon, but is soon able to answer these questions to Mors's satisfaction.
