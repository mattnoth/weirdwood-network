Geography[ ]

Aegon's High Hill, the largest of King's Landing, is found in the southeast of the city. Atop the hill lies the Red Keep, the great fortress that dominates the capital. The hill and its castle overlook Blackwater Bay, and they command approaches to the city and the Blackwater Rush.

The bluffs of Aegon's High Hill are steep and rocky, spotted with lichen and gnarled thorny trees. Discrete niches cut into one of the bluffs allow a dangerous descent to the river below.

One of the four straight streets which starts at the unnamed great square in the center of the city, presumably the King's Way, leads to the Red Keep. This road is joined by the Hook, which curves down Aegon's High Hill to the Muddy Way. The River Row extends east from Fishmonger's Square to the hill.

Shadowblack Lane is a twisty route from the Red Keep to the foot of Aegon's High Hill. The Lands of Ice and Fire places Eel Alley along the lower slopes of Aegon's High Hill, but A Game of Thrones states the old inn on Eel Alley is halfway up Visenya's Hill.

History[ ]

When Lord Aegon Targaryen sailed from Dragonstone at the start of the Conquest, he chose the mouth of the Blackwater Rush as the site of his first base of operations. The Aegonfort, a crude wood-and-earth fortifications, was built on the tall hill that would later be given his name.

The city of King's Landing developed haphazardly around the Aegonfort, which itself came to spread halfway down Aegon's High Hill. In 35 AC, the king had the Aegonfort torn down to begin the construction of the Red Keep as a more durable and fitting seat for House Targaryen.

Maegor I Targaryen ordered secret passages and tunnels throughout the depths of the hill to connect with the Red Keep.

Princess Viserra Targaryen died from a horseracing accident in an alley at the foot of Aegon's High Hill.

At the end of the Dance of the Dragons, thousands climbed Aegon's High Hill to hear Prince Aegon the Younger proclaim an end to the fighting.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Tyrion Lannister sees the red comet in the skies above the Red Keep atop Aegon's High Hill.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Cersei Lannister keeps her focus on Aegon's High Hill during her walk of atonement through the streets of King's Landing. The smallfolk of Flea Bottom crowd the lower slopes of the hill during her journey.
