Description[ ]

The book barely mentions the reign of Viserys II Targaryen, who ruled after Baelor and before Aegon. Nevertheless, some consider Kaeth's writings too kind to Viserys.

In his book, Kaeth argues that King Daeron I made a mistake when he considered marrying one of his own sisters to the Sealord of Braavos. Daeron planned to do this in order to make an alliance between the Seven Kingdoms and Braavos, aiming to remove the pirates from the Stepstones that were hindering the trade with the newly conquered Dorne. Braavos, however, was at war with Lys and Pentos, who lend the Dornish aid because of Daeron's plans.

There are only four copies remaining in Westeros that are written in Kaeth's hand. The Citadel's copy has illuminations that are wondrous to behold. However, it is implied that common copies are also available, since the book is popular among the nobility.

Recent Events[ ]

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Tyrion Lannister gives a copy of Kaeth's work to King Joffrey I Baratheon as a wedding gift in 300 AC. The book, written by Kaeth himself, is huge, bound in leather, and gorgeously illuminated. Shortly after receiving the gift, Joffrey carves the book up with another gift, his Valyrian steel sword Widow's Wail, which leaves only three copies remaining written by Kaeth himself. Several of the guests are shocked by the wasteful and callous destruction of the book, and Tyrion is infuriated.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

The book is probably inspired by The Lives of the Twelve Caesars, a biographical work on Julius Caesar and the first eleven Roman emperors written by the historian Suetonius. George R.R. Martin has frequently talked about his love for Suetonius's works, and also the historian was a partial inspiration for Mushroom.
