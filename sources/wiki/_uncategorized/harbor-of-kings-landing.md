Layout[ ]

The harbor of King's Landing is found on the northern shore of the Blackwater Rush. The fish market is west of the harbor and the River Gate is to its north. The waterfront has a hundred quays, which are made of stone.

Ramshackle bait shacks, pot-shops, warehouses, merchants' stalls, alehouses, and whorehouses have been raised between the quays and the city walls.

History[ ]

As the new city of King's Landing grew during the reign of King Aegon I Targaryen, wharves and storehouses rose along the banks of the Blackwater Rush. Construction of city walls took place between 20–26 AC. 

Jaehaerys I Targaryen replaced the harbormaster of King's Landing after becoming king. Lord Edwell Celtigar, master of coin, tripled port fees.

The Shivers reached Blackwater Bay in 59 AC, with the disease believed to be carried to ports by rats.

  

Aegon III and Viserys at the docks, by Wouter Florusse

During the Dance of the Dragons, Ser Perkin the Flea led his gutter knights in looting wharves and warehouses and any ships not out to sea amid rioting in the city.

Winter Fever reached King's Landing through its port in 133 AC, with the disease first afflicting sailors, ferrymen, fishermongers, dockers, stevedores, and whores.

Prince Viserys Targaryen returned to King's Landing aboard Lady Baela, meeting his brother, King Aegon III Targaryen, at the docks. Traffic at the docks boomed during the Lysene Spring, especially with ships from Tyrosh, Myr, Pentos, Braavos, and Lys. Tessario the Tiger was arrested at a dockside tavern near the River Gate while seeking passage to the Port of Ibben.

King Aerys II Targaryen doubled the port fees at King's Landing against the objections of his Hand, Lord Tywin Lannister, though the king reversed his decision after being approached by a delegation of small lords and rich merchants.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

The River Gate, by Franz Miklis © Fantasy Flight Games

Catelyn Stark sees the busy port when she arrives at King's Landing aboard Storm Dancer.

Lord Eddard Stark sends Vayon Poole to the docks to find a ship for transport back to the north. Sansa and Arya Stark are to travel on the Wind Witch, but the ship's departure is halted because of Eddard's arrest. When Arya visits the harbor, she sees fewer ships than she remembered.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Princess Myrcella Baratheon sails from King's Landing to Braavos on Seaswift.

Tyrion Lannister, the acting Hand of the King, orders Bronn to have everything between the city walls and the water's edge burned in preparation for the Battle of the Blackwater.

During the battle, Ser Davos Seaworth sees that the Lannisters burned the riverfront, sank hulks to block access to the stone quays, and retreated to the Mud Gate. Tyrion sees the desolation of the fishmarket and the wharves, and he leads a charge down the riverfront and among the quays.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Tyrion and Bronn visit the riverside in the battle's aftermath, and Tyrion thinks the cost of rebuilding will be ruinous. Tyrion reports to his father, Lord Tywin Lannister, that the Blackwater will need to be dredged of ruined ships before the port can be reopened. Three-quarters of the quays will need to be repaired, with some potentially needing to be replaced.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Queen Regent Cersei Lannister approves the construction of new dromonds on the Blackwater.
