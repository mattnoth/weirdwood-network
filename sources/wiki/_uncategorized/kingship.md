Traditions[ ]

In Westeros, kings traditionally inherit the title through male-preference primogeniture, although the Iron Islands once had the elective kingsmoot. Dorne started using the title "Prince of Dorne" after Lord Mors Martell married Queen Nymeria, and the new rulers chose to abandon the Andal customs in favor of those of the Rhoynar. The Seven Kingdoms are now ruled by the King of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men who rules from the Iron Throne.

The city of Meereen on Slaver's Bay once had a king. Instead of kings, the Free Cities have rulers chosen through democratic and oligarchic systems, such as the triarchs of Volantis, the Prince of Pentos, the Archon of Tyrosh, and the Sealord of Braavos.

A high king is a "king of kings," who claims seniority over other kings. Notable kingdoms with such a system include the Iron Islands, Dorne, the Reach, and Sarnor, though the High King of Sarnor was typically more of a figurehead than a true ruler.

The feminine form of king is a queen. Most commonly, it refers to the wife of a king. It may also refer to women who rule or make claim to rule in their own right such as Storm Queen Argella Durrandon, Queen Marla Sunderland of the Three Sisters, and the Targaryen Queens Rhaenyra and Daenerys Targaryen. The husband of a reigning queen may bear the title prince consort or be dubbed her king and consort.

History[ ]

Westeros was ruled by a multitude of kings during the Age of the Hundred Kingdoms.

House Targaryen ruled the Seven Kingdoms as Kings of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men after Aegon's Conquest. House Baratheon succeeded the Targaryens through Robert's Rebellion.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Robert I Baratheon by Joshua Cairós © Fantasy Flight Games

Robert I Baratheon, King of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men, is mortally wounded by a boar, and he is succeeded by his son, King Joffrey I Baratheon. Renly Baratheon, Lord of Storm's End is crowned king at Highgarden in opposition to his nephew. Following the Battle of the Camps, Robb Stark is declared King in the North at Riverrun.

The exiled Viserys Targaryen dies at Vaes Dothrak, and his younger sister, Daenerys Targaryen, acknowledges herself as queen and the last of House Targaryen.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Rejecting the claims of Joffrey and Renly, Stannis Baratheon, Lord of Dragonstone, declares himself king. Renly is assassinated near Storm's End. Balon Greyjoy, Lord of the Iron Islands, secedes from the Seven Kingdoms and declares himself King of the Isles and the North.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Amid the War of the Five Kings, Balon falls to his death at Pyke and Robb is killed in the Red Wedding. Joffrey dies at his wedding feast and is succeeded by his younger brother, Tommen I Baratheon.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Since Euron Greyjoy claimed the Seastone Chair after Balon's death, Aeron Greyjoy calls for a kingsmoot on Old Wyk to decide succession in the Iron Islands. Despite the efforts of Aeron, Victarion, and Asha Greyjoy, Euron is chosen king after announcing he intends to conquer all of Westeros.

A new corsair king has risen in the Basilisk Isles.

Princess Arianne Martell fails in an attempt to crown Tommen's older sister, Princess Myrcella Baratheon, according to Dornish customs.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

A new pirate king has set up on Torturer's Deep.
