A goodwife is a woman of higher social standing who is not of the nobility. The term can also be used as a form of respect for smallfolk women.

History[ ]

Goodwives, fishermen, and fieldhands cheered loudly when King Aegon I Targaryen was crowned at the Aegonfort during Aegon's Conquest.

The first fool to serve at the Red Keep was the Goodwife, a fat man who dressed as a woman.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Amabel and Harra are fierce old goodwives who supervise servants at Harrenhal. After the fall of Harrenhal, Goodwife Harra is executed by House Bolton for having told Lady Shella Whent's household to serve House Lannister.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Maerie and Nolla are goodwives in the riverlands who are killed by Lannisters during the War of the Five Kings.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

During her search for Sansa Stark, Brienne of Tarth speaks with a goodwife at Rosby. Outside of Duskendale, Brienne addresses a woman on a turnip cart as goodwife.
