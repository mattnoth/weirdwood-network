## Summary

Description[ ]

See also: Images of Littlefinger's blade

Although the dagger is plain, it is finely made of Valyrian steel and has a smooth black dragonbone hilt. Its edge is extremely sharp. 

History[ ]

It is unknown where Lord Petyr Baelish originally acquired the dagger, or if it had any significance before he owned it. During the tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day in 298 AC, Ser Jaime Lannister faced Ser Loras Tyrell in the lists as the final match. Petyr bet on Jaime to win the joust, wagering his dagger, but lost it to King Robert I Baratheon, who had bet on Loras. Tyrion Lannister bet on the match as well, but he had supported his brother Jaime and also lost his wager.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Summer rescues Catelyn from the catspaw, by Chris Dien © Fantasy Flight Games

A catspaw sets a fire in the Library Tower of Winterfell as a distraction and then tries to assassinate the comatose Bran Stark in the sickroom. Bran's mother, Catelyn, fends off the assassin and the boy's unnamed direwolf kills the catspaw. The blade cuts Catelyn's fingers almost to the bone. Ser Rodrik Cassel discovers the dagger has a blade of Valyrian steel and a dragonbone hilt, which is far too fine a weapon for a common man like the catspaw. At a confidential meeting with Catelyn, Robb Stark, Theon Greyjoy, and Maester Luwin, Rodrik informs Catelyn, who then tells the others that her sister Lysa Arryn had sent a message accusing House Lannister of the death of her husband, Lord Jon Arryn. Catelyn reminds them that Jaime Lannister did not go hunting the day Bran fell from the First Keep, and that she does not believe that Bran truly fell, but that he was pushed after he must have overheard something.

Seeking further proof of the Lannisters' involvement in the attacks on Bran, Catelyn and Ser Rodrik travel to King's Landing with the blood-stained knife to inform Lord Eddard Stark, the Hand of the King to Robert I Baratheon. Rodrik first discusses the dagger with Ser Aron Santagar, the master-at-arms of the Red Keep, through which Lord Varys realizes Catelyn is in the capital. Varys informs Lord Petyr Baelish, who has Catelyn summoned to them. Varys nicks his finger with the knife, and Petyr reveals that he had once owned the blade. Petyr states he lost it to Tyrion Lannister during the tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day, however, when Ser Loras Tyrell defeated Tyrion's brother, Ser Jaime Lannister, in the lists. When Petyr and Catelyn discuss the dagger with Eddard, Petyr tells them that the dagger alone is not enough to prove House Lannister was behind the assassination attempt, and advises Eddard to get rid of the dagger. Eddard keeps the blade when his wife departs the capital.

Tyrion, who is called the Imp, is taken prisoner by Catelyn at the crossroads inn and brought to the Eyrie, where he wins a trial by combat. During his journeys, Tyrion insists to Catelyn and Bronn that he never owned the dagger, and that he would always bet on Jaime during a tourney, never against him.

When King Robert returns mortally wounded from his hunt in the kingswood, Eddard sheaths the Valyrian dagger at his waist. Petyr suggests to Eddard that they should gather support to seize Robert's children as Robert lays dying, to give them control over the kingdom. Eddard reminds Petyr of the men the Lannisters have killed, and shows him the dagger used in the attempt on Bran's life.

After the death of King Robert, his widow, Queen Cersei Lannister, gains the support of Petyr and Janos Slynt and his gold cloaks. When Eddard confronts Cersei in the throne room and is taken captive, Petyr takes the dagger Eddard wears at his hip and shoves it under his chin.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Petyr Baelish, by Christopher Bradley © Fantasy Flight Games

Tyrion Lannister, the acting Hand of the King for Joffrey I Baratheon, desires to confront Petyr Baelish about the dagger. At some point, Petyr waits for Tyrion in his chambers at the Tower of the Hand, where he offers the dagger to Tyrion as a gift. Tyrion declines, and realizes that Petyr is aware of the fact that Tyrion knows of his lie to House Stark.

When Petyr berates Tyrion about being lied to and tells him to leave him out of his deceptions in the future, Tyrion thinks he will only if it is mutual, noticing Littlefinger bearing the dagger sheathed at his hip.

In Riverrun's dungeon, Ser Jaime Lannister admits to having flung Bran Stark from the First Keep of Winterfell but denies Catelyn's accusation he later hired the catspaw to kill Bran. Jaime also insists that Tyrion would never have bet against him during Joffrey's tourney, contradicting Petyr's claim that Tyrion won the dagger from Petyr by betting on Ser Loras Tyrell instead of Jaime. Jaime recalls the dagger changing hands, however, as a drunken King Robert I Baratheon had displayed it at the tourney's feast.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

While discussing the new swords (Widow's Wail and Oathkeeper) made by Tobho Mott from Eddard Stark's Valyrian sword, Ice, Lord Tywin Lannister tells his son Tyrion that King Robert had a hundred daggers in his armory, but that the only knife the king ever used was a hunting knife he received in his youth from Lord Jon Arryn.

After receiving Widow's Wail from Tywin as a wedding gift, King Joffrey I claims that he is "no stranger to Valyrian steel". Tyrion later concludes that Joffrey was behind the assassination attempt on Bran Stark, deducing that the then-prince had taken the plain-looking dagger, which Robert had forgotten after winning it from Petyr at the tourney, from the king's baggage train and given it to a lackey at Winterfell.

After Joffrey's death at his royal wedding, Petyr Baelish uses the dagger to cut fruit, while talking to Sansa Stark about truth and lies and hidden plots.

Cersei and Jaime discuss the assassination attempt on Bran. Jaime briefly asks Cersei if she was involved, but she tells him not to be absurd and reminds him that they both hoped Bran would die after his fall. She says that even Robert hoped so, that in private with her and the children, he drunkenly complained that people are too weak to give the gift of mercy to crippled children. Jaime concludes that Joffrey tried to have Bran killed as a misguided attempt to impress Robert. When Jaime frees Tyrion from the Red Keep's dungeon, the brothers agree that Joffrey had been responsible.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

George R.R. Martin stated that A Storm of Swords would resolve the question of who was behind the assassination attempt on Bran Stark.

House of the Dragon alleges that the dagger once belonged to King Aegon I Targaryen, after being brought by Aenar Targaryen from Valyria, and that its blade, when heated by fire, displays Valyrian glyphs that read, "From my blood will come the Prince That Was Promised, and his will be the Song of Ice and Fire". Further, in Game of Thrones, the dagger was used to kill the leader of the White Walkers, and thus destroy all of them. However, the canonicity of these pieces of information is unconfirmed and highly unlikely.
