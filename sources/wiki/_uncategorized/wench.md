Pia by TheThreeHares ©

Wenches are serving women of common origin. They are usually young women, though there are some older wenches.

The term can also refer to young women regardless of their profession or upbringing, sometimes in a derogatory sense.

Culture[ ]

Some highborn ladies look down upon wenches because of their lower birth. The term can have a connotation of promiscuity. 

The Salty Wench is a longship captained by Quenton Greyjoy. Warrior Wench is a longship of the Iron Fleet.

History[ ]

The author of A Caution for Young Girls claimed to have spent part of her life as a serving wench in Myr.

Viserra Targaryen was taken by her friends to see inns along Eel Alley and River Row where serving wenches danced on tables.

Munkun alleged that Alys Rivers was a serving wench who dabbled in potions and spells.

King Aegon IV Targaryen legitimized all of his bastards on his deathbed, even those he had fathered on tavern wenches.

Cotter Pyke is the son of a tavern wench.

Cersei Lannister once dressed as a simple serving wench to meet with her brother, Ser Jaime Lannister, at an old inn on Eel Alley.

King Robert I Baratheon fathered Gendry on a tavern wench. He is also alleged to have fathered twins on a serving wench at Casterly Rock; when Queen Cersei found out, she had the babes killed and the mother sold to a slaver.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Kyra by Kep Trefler ©

Tyrion Lannister catches Grand Maester Pycelle abed with a serving wench.

To better protect Shae, Tyrion suggests having his concubine serve at the Red Keep. Shae objects to becoming a kitchen wench, however, and Varys instead arranges for her to became a maidservant to Lollys Stokeworth.

The Kettleblack brothers get on well with the serving wenches at the Red Keep.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Ser Jaime Lannister denigrates Brienne of Tarth by repeatedly referring to her as a wench. While traveling through lands of House Bracken, Ser Cleos Frey points out tavern wenches hanged for having slept with westermen during the War of the Five Kings.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

All of the serving wenches at the Quill and Tankard dote on Alleras.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Lord Wyman Manderly reveals to Lord Davos Seaworth that Ramsay Bolton hunts wenches in the woods near the Dreadfort, with only a few women managing to escape.
