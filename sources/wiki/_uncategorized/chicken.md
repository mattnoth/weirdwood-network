Culture[ ]

Male and female chickens are known as roosters and hens, respectively. Chickens are commonly used as poultry. Capons are roosters which have been castrated and raised for meat.

A blue bantam rooster is depicted in the arms of House Swyft, and a yellow rooster's head is included in the arms of House Herston.

A cockatrice has the head of a rooster.

History[ ]

Since Sunfyre, the dragon of King Aegon II Targaryen, healed awkwardly after the battle at Rook's Rest, Mushroom compared the beast to "a great golden fire-breathing chicken, hopping and fluttering from hill to hill".

Hens wandered the yard at Standfast. Ser Duncan the Tall sometimes had his squire, Aegon Targaryen, chase chickens to improve his speed.

While those seated at the high table at the wedding tourney at Whitewalls enjoyed a peacock roasted in its plumage, lowerborn guests were served capon stuffed with onions, herbs, mushrooms, and roasted chestnuts.

Tyrion Lannister and Tysha ate two whole chickens and part of a third at an inn while waiting for Jaime Lannister to return from Casterly Rock.

Samwell Tarly grew to hate blood and wept at even the slaughter of chickens.

Every year Roose Bolton sent piglets, chickens, and a bag of stars to the mother of Ramsay Snow to keep the boy's parentage a secret.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Jon Snow sneaks half a honey chicken to Ghost during the feast in honor of King Robert's visit to Winterfell.

Sansa Stark is served roast chicken while confined in Maegor's Holdfast.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Koss catches two chickens for Yoren's recruits at the Gods Eye town.

Bran Stark has cheese, capons, and brown oatbread while meeting with Ser Rodrik Cassel, Maester Luwin, and Lord Wyman Manderly in the solar of the Great Keep of Winterfell.

Capons stuffed with onions and mushrooms are served at Bitterbridge.

Hundreds of smallfolk bring cows, sheep, and chickens with them as they find refuge within Riverrun.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Cages of chickens are kept by the free folk host of Mance Rayder, King-Beyond-the-Wall. When Jon Snow is brought to Mance's tent, Dalla is cooking a brace of hens over a brazier; Tormund enjoys eating a chicken on a skewer.

Lysa Arryn often sings a song about a chicken who dressed as a fox to her son, Lord Robert Arryn.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

  

The arms of the Knight of the Red Chicken

Ser Creighton Longbough claims to have fought an epic fight with the Knight of the Red Chicken at the Battle of the Blackwater.

Capons stuffed with leeks and mushrooms are served when Ser Jaime Lannister visits Darry.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Lord Davos Seaworth sees chickens underfoot at Fishfoot Yard in White Harbor.

Theon Greyjoy has to fight the Bastard's girls for half a chicken.

Capons glazed with honey are served when Ser Balon Swann visits Sunspear.

Jon Snow sees Ser Axell Florent eating capons at Castle Black.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

Capons are served at the Gates of the Moon.

Telling Shagga and Timett how to pillage is like telling a rooster how to crow, but I should prefer to keep them with me.

—Tyrion Lannister to Tywin Lannister

Varys: Sweet Petyr, surely you do not mean to suggest that these puissant lords and noble knights could be bought like so many chickens in the market.

Petyr: Have you been to our markets of late, Lord Varys? You'd find it easier to buy a lord than a chicken, I daresay. Of course, lords cluck prouder than chickens, and take it ill if you offer them coin like a tradesman, but they are seldom adverse to taking gifts ... honors, lands, castles ...

—Varys and Petyr Baelish

A crisp roast chicken would set the world aright.
