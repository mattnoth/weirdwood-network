History[ ]

During the reign of Aenys I Targaryen, Savage Sam Tarly was among the marcher lords who participated in the Vulture Hunt.

In 49 AC, Lord Tarly was present at King's Landing for the wedding of Alyssa Velaryon and Rogar Baratheon.

Lord Alan Tarly declared for Princess Rhaenyra Targaryen and the blacks during the Dance of the Dragons, fighting and being taken captive in the battle on the Honeywine.

During Robert's Rebellion, Lord Randyll Tarly commanded the vanguard of the Reach at the Battle of Ashford, where he killed Lord Cafferen and forced Lord Robert Baratheon to retreat. Randyll's liege, Lord Mace Tyrell, took credit for the victory, however.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Lord Randyll is among the lords that join their forces to Renly Baratheon's beneath Bitterbridge. After Renly's death at Storm's End, Lord Randyll rides back to Bitterbridge where he captures Renly's stores and puts many men to death, mainly Florent men-at-arms, lest they go over to Stannis along with their lords. Randyll joins his liege lord, Lord Mace Tyrell, and the Tyrell-Lannister alliance at the Battle of the Blackwater, where he commands the center of the host.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Lord Randyll leaves the capital and march north towards Duskendale where he defeats the northern army led by Ser Helman Tallhart and Robett Glover. He then captures Maidenpool.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

The timid and cowardly Lord of Maidenpool, William Mooton, does not dare confront Lord Randyll, who rules the town in all but name. Lord Randyll arranges a betrothal for his son and heir, Dickon Tarly, to William's eldest daughter and heir, Eleanor Mooton.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Randyll returns to King's Landing upon receiving news of the arrest of Margaery Tyrell. He receives custody of her and her cousins after swearing a holy oath to return them for trial. As part of Lord Regent Kevan Lannister's efforts to assuage the men of the Reach, Lord Tarly is named justiciar on the small council of King Tommen I Baratheon.
