History[ ]

In the legend of the Rat Cook, dark red Dornish wine was part of the recipe for the pie in which the son of an Andal king was cooked.

After the Second Battle of Tumbleton, Ser Hobert Hightower gave casks of Dornish red and Arbor gold to Ser Ulf White. Having poisoned the sweeter golden wine, Hobert planned to drink the sour red. A suspicious Ulf insisted that Hobert share the gold, however, and both men died from the poison. 

Prince Baelor Targaryen ordered Prince Aegon Targaryen to serve sweet Dornish red to Ser Duncan the Tall during the tourney at Ashford Meadow.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

A wineseller in the Western Market of Vaes Dothrak offers a sweet Dornish red to Daenerys Targaryen. She decides to accept the summerwine, but the wineseller instead offers an Arbor red which he has secretly poisoned upon learning her identity.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Tyrion Lannister offers his dinner guess Janos Slynt a fine red wine. He asks if it's an Arbor red, but Tyrion tells him it's Dornish.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Queen Regent Cersei Lannister and her uncle, Ser Kevan Lannister, share a flagon of Dornish red during supper. Ser Jaime Lannister, Lord Commander of the Kingsguard, drinks Dornish red in White Sword Tower.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Tyrion Lannister finds sour reds from Dorne in the cellar of Illyrio's manse in Pentos. Tyrion drinks Dornish reds while traveling with Illyrio Mopatis toward the Rhoyne.
