Layout[ ]

The Great Keep contains the bedchambers for members of House Stark. The building is heated by water from hot springs below the castle, and Lady Catelyn Stark's bedchambers are the hottest rooms in the Great Keep. The towers of the Great Keep have winding stairs. The bulding's cellar has narrow windows.

Eddard Stark's solar contains an oak chair for the Lord of Winterfell and a long plank-and-trestle table.

There is a view of the yard from the covered bridge which connects the Great Keep and the armory. A dimly-lit gallery leads from the Great Hall to the Great Keep.

The gatehouse of Harrenhal is as large as Winterfell's Great Keep.

History[ ]

Theon Greyjoy once earned himself a thrashing by accidentally knocking Old Nan to the ground while carelessly leaping down the Great Keep's spiral stairs. Theon and Robb Stark have also often sparred on the stairs with wooden swords.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Bran Stark in his sickbed, by Victor Garcia © Fantasy Flight Games

After visiting the comatose Bran Stark in the castle's sickroom, Jon Snow speaks with Robb Stark in the yard and then goes to the Keep to say goodbye to Arya Stark.

Lady Catelyn Stark protects Bran when he is attacked in his sickbed by an assassin, and the attacker is killed by the boy's unnamed direwolf. The hysterical Catelyn is then brought to the Great Keep to be treated in her own chambers.

Hodor transports the crippled Bran to and from his chambers after he wakes from his coma.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Mikken hammers a row of iron bars into the wall of Bran's chambers so the boy can pull himself around.

Northmen come to Winterfell to celebrate the harvest feast. Bran, Maester Luwin, and Ser Rodrik Cassel meet with bannermen in the solar of Bran's late father, Lord Eddard Stark.

Werlag breaks down the door to Bran's chamber during the capture of Winterfell by ironborn, and Theon Greyjoy takes the boy into custody. Calling himself Prince of Winterfell, Theon claims Eddard's bedchamber.

After the sack of Winterfell, Bran sees the glow of a fire in the cellar beneath the Great Keep.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Lord Roose Bolton holds the wedding of his son Ramsay and "Arya Stark" (actually Jeyne Poole) in the godswood of Winterfell. After the guests feast in the Great Hall, Roose meets with bannermen in the Great Keep's solar. Ramsay keeps Jeyne confined to his bedchamber, a room lightly damaged by the sack and adorned with new furnishings from Barrowton. Men from the Dreadfort guard the main doors of the Great Keep, and the Bastard's Boys guard Ramsay's chamber. "Arya's" serving girls include Maisie and Jez.

Because several men have been found dead in Winterfell, the captive Theon is interrogated in the solar by Roose, Lady Barbrey Dustin, Roger Ryswell, and Ser Aenys Frey.

The Freys and the Manderlys fight after Little Walder Frey is found murdered, so Roose orders the two factions to advance on Stannis Baratheon in the wolfswood. Theon and Abel's washerwomen use this as an opportunity to sneak "Arya" from Ramsay's chambers in the Great Keep.
