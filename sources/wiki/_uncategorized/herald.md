## Summary

A herald is a position held in royal courts and noble households throughout the known world. Heralds are in charge of proclaming certain solemn publications or important messages. They beat the floor with their staffs or blow trumpets to quiet crowds, before making announcements. Heralds also announce the names of participants during tourneys.

Culture[ ]

The Prince of Pentos travels around the city in an ivory and gold palanquin, with three heralds going before him, carrying the golden scales of trade, the iron sword of war, and the silver scourge of justice.

History[ ]

In 50 AC, when King Jaehaerys I Targaryen reached the age of majority and claimed his rule for himself, he replaced the royal herald, among other lesser offices.

In 131 AC, the royal herald of Aegon II Targaryen was among those arrested by Lord Cregan Stark for the king's murder. He chose to join the Night's Watch to avoid execution.

In 133 AC, the Maiden's Day Ball was held to find a new queen consort for King Aegon III Targaryen. More than one thousand maidens from throughout Westeros, and even Essos, crowded into the Red Keep on the day of the ball. The royal herald announced the name and lineage of each noblewoman who presented themselves to the young king, but lost his voice in the process and had to be replaced. Aegon chose Lady Daenaera Velaryon, who was announced by the second herald, who also was somewhat hoarse by that point.

In 209 AC, at the tourney at Ashford Meadow, heralds boomed out the name of each knight that challenged the champions of the fair maid.

In 212 AC, during the wedding tourney at Whitewalls, the Butterwell herald was a portly man. He openly mocked Ser Glendon Flowers as the "Knight of the Pussywillows" and "Ser Glendon the Bastard", to the amusement of the crowd.

In 246 AC or 247 AC, at the tourney at Blackhaven, the ten-year-old Barristan Selmy entered the lists as a mystery knight. The herald laughed when he saw young Barristan in his ill-fitting armor.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

A herald announces the arrival of King Joffrey I Baratheon and his mother, Dowager Queen Cersei Lannister, in Joffrey's first court session as king. The herald announces Sansa Stark before she approaches the Iron Throne to ask for mercy for her father.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

A herald presents the participants of the tourney on King Joffrey's name day before they joust.

Lord Wyman Manderly brings a long tail of retainers with him to the harvest feast of Winterfell, including heralds.

Tyrion Lannister gives a signal to the herald to end the court session. During the bread riots in King's Landing, Tyrion commands three Kingsguard to escort heralds who will command the people to return to their homes.

During the award ceremony after the Battle of the Blackwater, heralds cry the name and deeds of the heroes of the battle as each one enters the throne room.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Heralds call out the names and titles of the lords and ladies making their entrance to the throne room during the wedding feast of King Joffrey I Baratheon and Margaery Tyrell. Heralds later summon the singers to entertain the guests.

A herald announces the witnesses heard at Tyrion's trial.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

After Cersei announces to the court that Queen Margaery is no maiden, the royal herald tries to quell the noise by beating on the floor with his staff.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

The Manderly herald slams his staff on the plank floor before announcing Ser Davos Seaworth to the Merman's Court.

A shavepate herald, wearing a shirt of polished copper disks and carrying a drum, cries to let the royal procession make its way to Daznak's Pit.

Missandei takes on the role of herald for Queen Daenerys Targaryen, but King Hizdahr zo Loraq later replaces her.
