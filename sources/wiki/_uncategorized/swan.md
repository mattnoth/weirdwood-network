Culture[ ]

Swans are known for their beauty and serenity.

Battling swans are found in the arms of House Swann of Stonehelm. Ser Balon Swann has wide white wings ornamenting his greathelm, and black and white swans fight on his shield. Balon also clasps his cloak with a silver brooch depicting two fighting swans, one ivory and the other onyx.

Westerosi refer to the ships of the Summer Isles as swan ships because of their billowing white sails, their figureheads, and their tall, curved prows.

History[ ]

Legends from Pentos claim that seven swan maidens once preyed on travelers in the Velvet Hills.

Lady Johanna Swann, known as the Black Swan, was a courtesan who rose to become the ruler of Lys in all but name.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Supper of Cersei and Tyrion Lannister, by JayDeewis ©

Arya Stark sees black swans swimming in the Gods Eye.

Cersei and Tyrion Lannister have roast swan stuffed with mushrooms and oysters during a meal at the Red Keep.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Anguy reveals that he spent much of his winnings from the Hand's tourney on the prostitutes Dancy, Jayde, and Alayaya, enjoying roast swan and wine from the Arbor with the girls.

Slivers of swan poached in a sauce of saffron and peaches are served at the wedding of King Joffrey I Baratheon and Margaery Tyrell.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Tyrion refuses black swan when it is served to him at Illyrio's manse in Pentos.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

Swans in almond milk are served at the Gates of the Moon.
