Character[ ]

Ravella is kind and welcoming, but also has a temper and a sharp tongue, as well as a scornful look towards those that have displeased her.

History[ ]

Ravella had a son who died when he was seven. According to Anguy, Ravella and Tom of Sevenstreams were once lovers. According to Tom, Ravella enjoyed his playing of the song "Let Me Drink Your Beauty".

Recent Events[ ]

A Storm of Swords[ ]

At the start of the War of the Five Kings, Lady Smallwood sends her daughter, Carellen, to a motherhouse in Oldtown, where Ravella's great-aunt is a septa.

Beric Dondarrion and Thoros of Myr visit Acorn Hall with a dozen of the brotherhood without banners, herding a flock of sheep. In return for Lady Smallwood's hospitality, Thoros gives her three sheep.

Northmen wearing the heraldry of House Karstark come to Acorn Hall's gates to see if the Smallwoods are harboring the escaped Jaime Lannister. Lady Ravella claims to them she had made love to Jaime until he was too exhausted to move, and when the Karstark men call her a liar, she has them sent off by having her men shoot arrows at them.

When Arya Stark and the brotherhood without banners come to Acorn Hall, Lady Ravella welcomes the outlaws, but becomes angry that they brought a young girl through a warzone. After she learns that Arya is highborn, Lady Smallwood insists that she take a bath and be dressed as a proper lady, giving her one of her daughter's dresses, green with embroidered acorns. Lady Smallwood gives the brotherhood information concerning the whereabouts of Lord Beric Dondarrion. She also relays the news that Jaime Lannister had escaped Riverrun, allegedly set free by Catelyn Stark, which upsets Arya to hear. After Arya tears her dress while scuffling with Gendry, Lady Smallwood has her washed again and put in another of Carellen's dresses, lilac with baby pearls. However, the dress is too delicate to ride in, so when Arya and the brotherhood leave Acorn Hall the next morning, Lady Smallwood gives her boy's clothing to wear, which had belonged to her late son.

When Arya worries that her brother, King Robb Stark, will not pay the brotherhood a ransom for her, Beric Dondarrion tells her if that is so, he will send her to the protection of Lady Smallwood.

When Sandor Clegane and Arya approach the Twins for the wedding of Edmure Tully and Roslin Frey, Arya recognizes Lord Smallwoood's pavilion by its acorn sigil and is reminded of Lady Smallwood.

In a village in the foothills of the Mountains of the Moon, some of the women try to make Arya wear a dress and do needlework, but as they are not Lady Smallwood, she refuses them.
