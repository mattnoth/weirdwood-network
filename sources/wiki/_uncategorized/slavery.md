Essos[ ]

Ghis and Slaver's Bay[ ]

  

A Wise Master of Yunkai being carried by slaves, by Tiziano Baracchi ©

For centuries the three great Slaver Cities of Slaver's Bay have been the linchpins of the slave trade, the place where Dothraki khals and the corsairs of the Basilisk Isles sell their captives and the rest of the world comes to buy from the Ghiscari.

The Good Masters of Astapor train the eunuch slave soldiers known as Unsullied. The Unsullied learn the way of the three spears.

Instead of warriors, bed slaves and boy whores are trained by the Wise Masters of Yunkai. A bed slave in Yunkai learns the way of the seven sighs and the sixteen seats of pleasure.

The Great Masters of Meereen train slaves to compete in fighting pits.

South of Slaver's Bay, New Ghis is a Ghiscari city which depends on slaves. Tolos, located west of the Slaver Cities, formed ties with the Ghiscari after the Doom of Valyria and is also dependent on slaves.

Free Cities[ ]

Well-spoken and gently-born slaves are prized and become tutors, scribes, bed slaves, healers, and priests in the Free Cities. A Slave of R'hllor is a man or woman who has been purchased to serve the red temples of R'hllor. Some escaped slaves join free companies.

Pentos participated in the slave trade for most of its history. Slavery is now forbidden by law, however, as part of the terms imposed by the Braavosi on Pentos a hundred years ago. In a city where wealth equals power, many rich magisters, such as Illyrio Mopatis, flout such laws and "free bond servants" who are slaves in all but name, are common on their estates, made to wear bronze collars and even get branded.

Lys has a high slave population, with bondsmen outnumbering freeborn three to one. Slaves are used for labor too harsh for Lysene hands, trained to act as guards and soldiers, and even posed for various performances. Lys is well known for its pleasure houses, training slaves in the arts of love and selling them as concubines and bed-slaves. The Lyseni breed slaves to develop every more beautiful courtesans and bedslaves.

In Volantis, due to the relative proximity to Slaver's Bay, there is a thriving slave market. There are five slaves for every free man. Slaves are always tattooed on the face, which prevents escaping on the constant flow of ships. Such tattoos are often designed to reveal the slave's role. A fool might be patterned in tattooed motley from neck to scalp. Slaves and freedmen are only allowed within the Black Walls with the permission of the Old Blood. The tiger cloaks are the slave soldiers of Volantis.

Qohor's city guard is comprised solely of Unsullied eunuch slave soldiers. However, much like in Pentos, slaves can be bought by powerful men who flout the laws.

Myr is a hub of trade in slaves, and bondsmen outnumber freeborn three to one.

Like nearby Lys and Myr, Tyrosh participates in the slave trade. Tyroshi slavers are known to be especially aggressive, even sailing north beyond the Wall in search of wildlings to enslave. Bondsmen outnumber freeborn three to one in the city.

Norvos permits slavery, with the bearded priests keeping a holy guard of slave soldiers.

Braavos is an exception among the Free Cities. Descended from slaves who escaped Valyria, the Braavosi do not permit slavery and they do not trade with Slaver's Bay.

The islands of Lorath became a refuge for freedmen and escaped slaves when worship of Boash was common in the colony.

Dothraki[ ]

The Dothraki make slaves by enslaving conquered peoples and selling them, often to the Ghiscari who train them. Dothraki keep slaves from many lands, notably in Vaes Dothrak, which was built by slaves, where they serve the dosh khaleen. Dothraki also sell their own kind, and when khalasars battle the defeated Dothraki are enslaved. Many Ibbenese women captured by Dothraki have ended up in the fighting pits of the Slaver Cities.

Basilisk Isles[ ]

Corsairs and pirates from the Basilisk Isles carry off captives, such as the Naathi, keeping them in holding pens on Talon and the Isle of Tears before selling them to the Slaver Cities or to Lys.

Qarth[ ]

The city of Qarth participates in the slave trade.

Further East[ ]

The Jogos Nhai enslave their enemies, who have included the Hyrkoon. The YiTish collect gold, goods, and slaves as tribute from bands of Jogos Nhai.

The Isle of Whips in the Jade Sea is a way station for slavers from half a dozen lands.

Slaves are used to carry palanquins in Asshai.

Westeros[ ]

The pirate kings on the Three Sisters took slaves.

Slavery is now abhorred and strictly forbidden on the Westerosi mainland. Both the old gods and the Faith of the Seven hold slavery to be an abomination. There have been no slaves in the Seven Kingdoms for thousands of years. 

Aenar Targaryen brought slaves to Dragonstone when his family moved from Valyria in anticipation of the Doom.

The ironborn of the Iron Islands keep thralls and salt wives. Neither are slaves in the literal sense, but are subject to forced labor and low status, and are not far removed from enslavement. The children of a thrall are born free, however, though they may have to be given to the Drowned God.

The illegal practice of slavery does take place in Westeros sometimes. Ser Jorah Mormont once sold a few poachers to a Tyroshi slaver and had to flee to the Free Cities when his crime was discovered by Lord Eddard Stark, who condemned Jorah to death for his crime. It is rumored that Cersei Lannister had the mother of King Robert I Baratheon's bastard twins sold to a passing slaver, presumably because she could not bear such an affront to her pride that close to home at Casterly Rock.

According to some such as Tyrion Lannister, the smallfolk of Westeros do not live too differently from slaves.

Some northmen believe that the free folk are slavers.

History[ ]

Old Ghis[ ]

  

Old Ghis practiced slavery, from Game of Thrones Blu-ray

Old Ghis practiced slavery. The Old Empire of Ghis developed into a major power in the east, dominating much of Essos before it eventually fell to Valyria. 

Old Valyria[ ]

  

A dragonlord oversees the sale of conquered peoples in Slaver's Bay, from Game of Thrones Blu-ray

At the beginning of their rise to power, the Valyrians watched the Ghiscari grow rich and powerful off the backs of conquered peoples. After the fall of Ghis, the self-styled freehold wanted its turn, seeking to emulate the success that slavery had brought to Ghis. The Valyrian Freehold made extensive use of thousands of slaves from a hundred different nations from throughout Essos. 

In the mines underneath the Fourteen Flames, "only the worst" slaves toiled, burned, and died to find gold and silver. Slave revolts were common in the mines, but the Valyrians were strong in sorcery and able to put them down. When there was war, the Valyrians took thousands of slaves, and when there was peace they bred them.

It is said the first Faceless Man existed in Valyria. There he brought death to the slaves who lived horrid lives toiling in the heat of the Fourteen Flames, praying for an end. He came to realize that the many gods they prayed to were one god, and that he was an instrument of the gods.

Many maesters believe the migration of Andals from Andalos to Westeros was to avoid Valyrian slavery.

Summer Isles[ ]

Slavers from Valyria, Old Ghis, and the Basilisk Isles raided the Summer Isles, and native princes sold rivals to the slavers during the Years of Shame. Chatana Qo eventually led the Summer Islanders to victory over the foreigners in the Slavers' Wars.

Gogossos[ ]

The city of Gogossos on the Isle of Tears outlived the Doom of Valyria and waxed rich and powerful during the Century of Blood. Some called it the "Tenth Free City", thanks to slavery and sorcery. Its slave markets became as notorious as those of the cities of Slaver's Bay. However, seventy seven years after the Doom a terrible plague, the Red Death, emerged from the slave pens of the city and swept across the Isle of Tears and then the rest of the Basilisk Isles.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

When talking to Jon Snow, Tyrion Lannister reflects that had he been born a peasant, he might have been sold to some slaver's grotesquerie on account of his dwarfism.

After Khal Drogo's defeat of Khal Ogo and the Lamb Men, Ser Jorah Mormont advises him to make for Meereen. The city had a plague the previous year so the brothels are paying double for healthy young girls and triple for boys under ten. Jorah tells Daenerys Targaryen that if enough enslaved children survive the journey the gold earned from their sale will buy them all the ships they need to sail for Westeros. Before her dragons are born, Daenerys frees the slaves of what is left to her of Drogo's khalasar.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Before giving her khalasar the order to enter the red waste, Daenerys contemplates heading downriver with her ragged band to the ports of Meereen, Yunkai, and Astapor. Rakharo warns her that Khal Pono's khalasar had ridden that way, however, driving thousands of captives before them to sell in the flesh marts of Slaver's Bay.

In the House of the Undying, Daenerys sees a vision of ten thousand slaves lifting bloodstained hands as she races by on her silver mare, riding like the wind.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

  

Mhysa, by Josu Hernaiz ©

Daenerys frees slaves at Astapor, Yunkai, and Meereen. She fills the ranks of her army with freedmen, people who are completely loyal to her and who have no aspirations beyond protecting their newly-obtained freedom.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Euron Greyjoy mentions that the price of slaves is rising since the fall of Astapor and Meereen. After the taking of the Shields, Euron plans to sell his captives as slaves in Lys and Volantis which will give the Iron Fleet sufficient gold to buy provisions. Victarion Greyjoy is disturbed by this, as it is not the Old Way.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

According to Qavo Nogarys, a customs officer Selhorys, the actions of Daenerys have smashed the slave trade which Volantis and Qarth rely on, making the dragon queen enemies behind the Black Wall and beyond.

As part of the peace between the Wise Masters and Daenerys Targaryen, Queen of Meereen, slavery is prohibited within the walls of Meereen but Yunkai is free to trade in slaves unmolested. The Yunkish lords besieging the city set up a slave market within sight of its walls.

Tyrion Lannister, Penny, and Ser Jorah Mormont are enslaved and become the property of Yezzan zo Qaggaz.

Slavers take some of the wildlings at Hardhome.

Ask yourself, if all men must grub in the dirt for food, how shall any man lift his eyes to contemplate the stars?
