Layout[ ]

The castle consists of ten towers all shaped differently, with wallwalks, covered bridges, kitchens, and dungeons.

The hall of Ten Towers contains the high seat of House Harlaw, with two giant scythes of beaten silver hanging crossed above the seat.

The Book Tower, the fattest of the castle's towers, is octagonal and made of large block stones. Lord Rodrik Harlaw does not allow food or drink in his library. The yard below contains a well.

The Widow's Tower is so called due to the presence of Rodrik's sister, Lady Gwynesse Harlaw.

A village lies near Ten Towers. There is good fishing off the Long Stone Quay.

History[ ]

The newest castle in the Iron Islands, Ten Towers was built by Lord Theomore Harlaw, who blamed the deaths of three infant sons on the dampness of ancient Harlaw Hall, which often flooded. The new castle was built at a better site, but the mercurial Theomore constructed ten towers different in appearance.

While growing up Asha Greyjoy felt more at home at Ten Towers than at the Greyjoy seat of Pyke. She enjoyed racing amongst Ten Towers' walks and bridges and reading in Lord Rodrik Harlaw's library. When the Faith of the Seven was tolerated in the Iron Islands, Rodrik kept septons at the castle to maintain his library.

Rodrik's older sister, Lady Gwynesse Harlaw, came back to Ten Towers after the death of her husband in the sea battle off Fair Isle during Greyjoy's Rebellion in 289 AC, saying she would remain until her grief had passed. Her residence is now known as the Widow's Tower, and the still-grieving Gwynesse believes that the castle should belong to her instead of the younger Rodrik.

Alannys Harlaw, the wife of Balon Greyjoy, Lord of the Iron Islands, moved to Ten Towers to live with her sister Gwynesse, as it is less raw on Harlaw than on the isle of Pyke.

Recent Events[ ]

A Feast for Crows[ ]

  

Lord Rodrik Harlaw and his library, by Nacho Molina © Fantasy Flight Games

Rival claimants arise in the Iron Islands after the death of Balon Greyjoy, King of the Isles and the North. Lord Rodrik Harlaw announces that Balon intended to be succeeded by Asha Greyjoy, Balon's daughter and Rodrik's niece, and Lord Harlaw calls her supporters to Ten Towers. Aeron Greyjoy calls for a kingsmoot to be held to choose Balon's successor.

Lord Baelor Blacktyde briefly visits Rodrik at Ten Towers before continuing on to Old Wyk. Potential supporters of Asha Greyjoy who assemble at Ten Towers include Botleys, Myres, Stonetrees, and Volmarks. Harlaw cousins include Boremund, Hotho Humpback, Ser Harras, and Sigfryd Silverhair.

Upon arriving at Ten Towers, Asha orders the steward Three-Tooth to have the captive Lady Sybelle Glover and her children, Gawen and Erena, comfortably quartered in one of the towers instead of the dungeons, and to have warm food served to the crew of Black Wind. Asha delays seeing her mother Alannys in the Widow's Tower until the next morning, and instead meets her uncle, Lord Rodrik, within the Book Tower. Worried that Asha will lose to her uncles Euron or Victarion at the kingsmoot, Rodrik offers to name her heir to Ten Towers if she remains on Harlaw, though Harras would remain heir to House Harlaw itself. Asha refuses and wants the Seastone Chair instead.

Euron ends up chosen by the ironborn who assemble at Nagga's hill for the kingsmoot on Old Wyk.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

When Euron Crow's Eye is chosen, Asha flees from Old Wyk back to Ten Towers on Harlaw to bid farewell to her mother. While taking on provisions, she learns that Euron has had her wed to Erik Ironmaker using a seal as her proxy. Asha leaves Gawen and Erena Glover at Ten Towers in the care of Alannys and her aunt, Gwynesse Harlaw, but continues on to Deepwood Motte in the wolfswood with the captive Lady Glover.
