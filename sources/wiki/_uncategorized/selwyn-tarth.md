Appearance and Character[ ]

Selwyn is said to be a good man. He has large hands.

History[ ]

Selwyn's daughters Alysanne and Arianne died in infancy. His son Galladon drowned when he was eight years old, leaving Brienne as his only living child. 

After the death of his wife, Selwyn took a new mistress every year. One mistress came from across the narrow sea; she had a slim waist, purple eyes, and a lovely voice.

Lord Tarth had a septa, Roelle, who educated Brienne. He also allowed his master-at-arms, Ser Goodwin, to train Brienne at arms. He kept a singer every year of Brienne's childhood, which led her to memorize all the songs.

Selwyn betrothed Brienne to the second son of Lord Bryen Caron, but the boy died of a chill when he was twelve. Selwyn then tried to wed Brienne to Ser Ronnet Connington, but the knight rejected her for her appearance. He last tried an older man for her, Ser Humfrey Wagstaff, but Brienne broke her betrothal to Wagstaff by defeating him in combat. After that, Lord Selwyn stopped trying to arrange a marriage for Brienne.

Selwyn hosted Renly Baratheon at Evenfall Hall during the Lord Paramount of the Stormlands's coming of age progress. Some time later, Selywn sent Brienne to serve Renly at Storm's End.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

When Ser Davos Seaworth is sent to ask after the allegiance of the stormlords, Lord Selwyn only agrees to a midnight meeting in a grove. The Evenstar informs Davos of his refusal to join Stannis Baratheon.

Selwyn instead supports Stannis's brother, Renly Baratheon. Selwyn's daughter, Brienne of Tarth, becomes one of Renly's Rainbow Guard. During the siege of Storm's End, Renly claims he is supported by Tarth arrows.

At Riverrun, Catelyn Stark and Brienne discuss Selwyn.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Brienne and Ser Jaime Lannister are captured by the Brave Companions, and Jaime protects Brienne from rape by claiming that Selwyn will ransom her with sapphires, drawing on Tarth's epithet of "the Sapphire Isle". After they are brought to Harrenhal, Qyburn sends a raven to Tarth demanding a ransom. Lord Selwyn responds by offering three hundred gold dragons for Brienne's safe return. Vargo Hoat rejects the offer, however, believing Selwyn is trying to cheat him by withholding sapphires.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Lord Randyll Tarly ridicules Brienne and advises her to return to her father at Tarth. The Elder Brother of the Quiet Isle also recommends that Brienne give up her quest and return home, saying that her father would rather have a living daughter than her broken shield. Brienne tearfully explains her family's history and her life since Tarth, and that she must find Sansa Stark or die trying.

After being captured by the brotherhood without banners, Brienne claims that Selwyn will ransom her squire, Podrick Payne, with sapphires, silver, or whatever they want.
