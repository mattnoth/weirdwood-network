Shade of the evening,, shade-of-the-evening, or evening shade is a thick, blue liquid consumed by the warlocks of Qarth. It is called "the wine of warlocks", but it is made from the inky blue leaves that grow on the black-barked trees found around the House of the Undying.

Characteristics[ ]

Shade of the evening is deep blue and viscous, flowing like honey. It has an unappetizing smell and initial taste, having been compared to ink, spoiled meat, and rotten flesh, but upon swallowing tastes like all things its imbiber has ever tasted, and more.

The liquid is used heavily by the warlocks of Qarth, whose lips have been turned blue by its extended use. The Undying Ones have drank so much of it not only their lips have turned blue, but their entire bodies as well.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Pyat Pree with shade of the evening, by Carlo Bocchio © Fantasy Flight Games

Xaro Xhoan Daxos tells Daenerys Targaryen that the warlocks of Qarth drink shade-of-the-evening until their lips turn blue.

Pyat Pree has a dwarf servitor give Daenerys a glass of shade to prepare her for her entrance into the House of the Undying.

While strolling the port of Qarth, Daenerys sees for sale a potent magical elixir made of virgin's milk and shade of the evening.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Euron Greyjoy, the new King of the Isles and the North, gives his brother Victarion a cup of shade of the evening, claiming to have taken possession of a cask from a captured galleas out of Qarth which was carrying warlocks. Victarion tries a small amount and promptly spits it out, referring to it as foul.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

Euron forces his captive brother Aeron Greyjoy to drink the liquid. Aeron has several nightmarish visions afterwards.
