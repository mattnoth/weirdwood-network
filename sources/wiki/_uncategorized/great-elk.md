Biology[ ]

Great elks can reach heights of ten feet at the shoulder. Their antlers may span nearly the same width. The adult males emit sounds similar to those of trumpets.

Geography[ ]

The great elk inhabits northernmost forests in both Westeros and northwestern Essos. In Westeros they are found in the Haunted Forest in the lands beyond the Wall, while in Essos they dwell in the Forest of Qohor.

Recent events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Daenerys Targaryen sees great elks during her journey through the Forest of Qohor.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Coldhands rides atop a great elk when he saves Samwell Tarly and Gilly from wights. Both Sam and Gilly ride the elk during their journey to the Nightfort.

Seconds before reuniting with Ghost, Jon Snow hungers for the flesh of a great elk, which makes him realize his direwolf is nearby.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

After failing to inhabit the body of Thistle, the spirit of Varamyr enters a portion of the Haunted forest and senses a great elk trumpeting. This unsettles the children clinging to his back.

Astride his great elk, Coldhands leads the party of Bran Stark, Jojen Reed, Meera Reed and Hodor to the cave of the three-eyed crow.

Behind the scenes[ ]

The great elk of the A Song of Ice and Fire novels appears to be based on the Megaloceros giganteus, also known as Irish elk, giant deer or Irish deer, a extinct genus of deer.
