Appearance[ ]

Beth has curly hair.

History[ ]

Ser Rodrik Cassel was married three times, and his wives gave birth to several daughters, including Beth's mother. However, he was widowed, and Beth was his only surviving child. Beth was raised at Winterfell with the Stark children. Though closer in age to Arya, she tends to hover around Sansa's more glamorous retinue.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Beth (bottom) doing needlework with Sansa and Arya Stark, and Jeyne Poole. By Beelphazoar ©

Beth is present during Septa Mordane's session of needlework with Princess Myrcella Baratheon at Winterfell. She participates in the conversation between Sansa Stark, Jeyne Poole, and Arya Stark about Prince Joffrey Baratheon. Beth dreamily says that Joffrey will marry Sansa and she will be queen, but as the betrothal has not yet been announced, Sansa gently tells her not to make up stories.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

When Lady Donella Hornwood comes to Winterfell, Bran Stark asks Ser Rodrik Cassel why he cannot marry her to solve the problems of her security, as he knows Rodrik thinks Lady Donella is pretty, and Beth would have a mother. However, Rodrik explains to Bran that he's only a knight and also is too old, and that after his death Donella would have the same issues, and Beth's prospects could also be endangered.

During the closing banquet of the harvest feast, Beth is seated at the main table beside her father, Ser Rodrik. After the dinner, she is invited to dance by Lord Wyman Manderly and Cley Cerwyn.

When Winterfell is captured by ironborn raiders led by Theon Greyjoy, Beth becomes one of the prisoners. Theon has the people of Winterfell brought to the Great Hall so they can hear Bran Stark yield the castle, and Bran sees Beth crying with fear.

  

Beth, atop the walls of Winterfell, by theghostofpyke ©

When Ser Rodrik tries to take back Winterfell, Theon uses Beth as a hostage, displaying her to her father standing atop the walls of the castle with a noose around her neck. Theon says she will hang if Rodrik attacks, or if his army still stands outside Winterfell by sunset, followed by more hanged hostages the following sunrise and sunset. Rodrik offers himself in Beth's place, but Theon knows the threat to her is more valuable than having Rodrik as a hostage. Rodrik is anguished, but still determined to attack. However, Ramsay Snow's treasonous attack on Rodrik and his men prevents any harm coming to Beth from Theon. 

After Winterfell's sack by the men of House Bolton, the orphaned Beth is taken to the Dreadfort.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

The sack of Winterfell is blamed on Theon's ironborn, who allegedly killed many of Winterfell's people. Lady Catelyn Stark is heartsick at the thought of the deaths of so many, including Beth, but she is told that Ramsay Snow took the women and children to the safety of the Dreadfort.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Beth remains a captive at the Dreadfort.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

In Braavos, Arya Stark, blinded by the Faceless Men, goes by the name "Blind Beth", possibly because she knew Beth Cassel in Winterfell.
