History[ ]

Dunk enjoyed duck at an inn near Ashford Meadow.

Ser Rolly Duckfield chose his surname because he was in a field with ducks nearby when he was knighted by Griff, earning him the nickname "Duck".

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Honey duck is served at Drogo's manse in Pentos.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Savory duck is served at the harvest feast in Winterfell.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Anguy brings down a duck which Lem later gives to Sharna at the Inn of the Kneeling Man.

While hawking along Blackwater Bay, Sansa Stark's merlin brings down three ducks.

Meera Reed uses her net to catch a duck in a marsh within the Gift.

Missandei serves duck eggs to Daenerys Targaryen in Meereen.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

The ironborn have stuffed ducks at Lord Hewett's castle after the taking of the Shields.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Ser Jorah Mormont shares a roast duck with Tyrion Lannister at the Merchant's House in Volantis.

Three duck's eggs fried in drippings are served to Jon Snow at Castle Black.

Ser Jaime Lannister finds the namesake tree of Pennytree between a duck pond and a blacksmith's forge.

Lacquered ducks are served at the Great Pyramid in Meereen.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

Arianne Martell is served honeyed duck by House Toland during her visit to Ghost Hill.

Ducks are served at the Gates of the Moon.

Quotes[ ]

Alysanne: A hundred naked men would frighten her.

Jaehaerys: A hundred naked ducks would frighten her.

—Alysanne Targaryen and Jaehaerys I Targaryen

Daenerys: What if I decide you're only worthy to be my fool? Or perhaps my cook?

Barristan: I would be honored, Your Grace. I can bake apples and boil beef as well as any man, and I've roasted many a duck over a campfire. I hope you like them greasy, with charred skin and bloody bones.

—Daenerys Targaryen and Barristan Selmy

Haldon: Tell our little friend how you came by your name, why don't you?

Rolly: A knight needs more than just the one name, and, well, we were in a field when he dubbed me, and I looked up and saw these ducks, so ... don't laugh, now.

—Haldon and Rolly Duckfield
