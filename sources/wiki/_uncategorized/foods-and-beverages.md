Themes[ ]

See also: Themes in A Song of Ice and Fire

George R.R. Martin has more than 160 vivid descriptions of food, that do not just lend color and flavor to the fictional world, but almost appear as a supporting character. The meals signal everything from a character's disposition to plot developments, but also foretell the last profitable harvest before the coming winter. Inedible-sounding food is eaten at the Red Wedding in A Storm of Swords, preparing readers for the nauseating circumstances to come.

A Feast of Ice and Fire is a cookbook by fans inspired by the story's food.
