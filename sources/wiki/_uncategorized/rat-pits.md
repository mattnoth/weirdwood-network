Description[ ]

Rat pits serve as an unsavory form of recreation, where rats in a pit are made to fight each other for the entertainment of the audience. Often dogs are set on the rats, and compete to see who can kill the most rats first. Bull-baiting is also sometimes held in rat pits, as are dogfights and bear-baiting. Sometimes the competitors may be humans fighting each other, allegedly even children with filed teeth.

Spectators pay to watch the fights, and also take bets on the participants. The attendees are usually the smallfolk inhabitants of the cities, although the highborn have been known to frequent the rat pits as well.

Rat pits are relatively small and illicit games, held in alleys or basements. They do not compare to the large and official fighting pits in Slaver's Bay, which apart from combat between human gladiators, also regularly feature fights between large animals such as lions, tigers, bears, etc, or fights between gladiators and these large animals.

History[ ]

The rat pits of Flea Bottom date back to the founding of King's Landing.

During Princess Viserra Targaryen's "one last night of laughter" before her marriage, she visited the rat pits with her companions, among other unsavory locations in King's Landing, before her death in a horse racing accident that night.

Prince Daemon Targaryen spent time with his friends and his gold cloaks in the seedy places of King's Landing, which earned him such nicknames as the "Prince of the City" and "Lord Flea Bottom". Daemon knew the rat pits well.

If the Testimony of Mushroom can be believed, when Ser Criston Cole of the Kingsguard searched for Prince Aegon Targaryen to inform him of the death of his father, King Viserys I Targaryen, he eventually found the prince drunk and naked in a Flea Bottom rat pit, watching two children with filed teeth fight each other for his entertainment while another young girl fellated him. Grand Maester Munkun's account corroborates that Aegon was "at his revels", but does not specify the details.

During the Dance of the Dragons, the riot of King's Landing started in the rat pits, among other seedy places in Flea Bottom.

Just like her father, Daemon, before her, Princess Baela Targaryen had a wild side to her and enjoyed spending time in the low places of King's Landing. She was known to wager coin and sometimes clothing in the rat pits of Flea Bottom.

According to a semi-canon source, Rorge owned a pot shop or tavern in Flea Bottom that also served as a rat pit where he would stage rat fights, dog fights, and bear cub fights for his profit. At some point, he found the orphan child Biter, and raised him ferally to compete in these fights. The adult Biter has teeth filed into points, and following Mushroom's account of the Flea Bottom rat pits in a previous era, this was likely done when he was a child competitor in the pits.
