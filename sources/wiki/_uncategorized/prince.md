Essos[ ]

The Rhoynar civilization was formed by independent city-states, each of which was ruled by a Prince or Princess.

Among the Free Cities, the Prince of Pentos, as well as the three Princes of Lorath, are the figurehead rulers of their respective cities. True power lies in the Magisters.

Westeros[ ]

In Westeros, the title is usually conferred to the children of kings. Prior to the War of the Usurper, the heir to the King of the Andals, the Rhoynar and the First Men held the title "Prince of Dragonstone",  and one his other sons held the title "Prince of Summerhall". The rest of the children of the monarch were styled Prince or Princess. Ever since the deposition of the Targaryens during Robert's Rebellion, the title "Prince of Dragonstone" has not been used, as the castle became the fief of the Lord of Dragonstone; the children of the monarch are referred to only as Prince or Princess.

In Dorne, the ruler of is styled Prince of Dorne, following the tradition of the Rhoynar immigrants, imposed by Nymeria during the unification of the region under House Martell. The offspring of the Prince are also styled "Prince" or "Princess", as we can see with Oberyn Martell and the children of Prince Doran Martell.

Summer Islands[ ]

Some of the rulers of the Summer Islands, like Jalabhar Xho, style themselves "Princes."
