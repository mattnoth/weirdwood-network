Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Jenny of Oldstones

Jenny is said to have been a strange and lovely girl who wore flowers in her hair.

Local villagers considered the wild Jenny a half-mad peasant, with some calling her a witch. She became popular with smallfolk throughout the Seven Kingdoms, however.

History[ ]

  

Jenny of Oldstones, dancing with the ghosts of Duncan Targaryen and Betha Blackwood - by Naomimakesart ©

Jenny claimed descent from long-vanished kings of the First Men. The ruined castle of Oldstones was once the seat of House Mudd.

Duncan Targaryen, the Prince of Dragonstone, met Jenny while traveling the riverlands in 239 AC. The prince loved Jenny so much he married her against the wishes of his father, King Aegon V Targaryen, breaking his betrothal to the daughter of Lyonel Baratheon, Lord of Storm's End. King Aegon tried to have the marriage undone, but Duncan refused to give Jenny up, ultimately relinquishing his rights to the Iron Throne for her, while the outraged Lyonel led a short-lived rebellion. Duncan became known as the Prince of Dragonflies, while his younger brother Jaehaerys became Prince of Dragonstone and the new heir.

Jenny was titled "Lady" by courtesy, and was eventually accepted at court. The smallfolk throughout the Seven Kingdoms cherished her, and she and Duncan were a favorite subject of singers for many years. Jenny was friends with a dwarf woman, a woods witch, whom she believed to be one of the children of the forest. The woman, whom Jenny brought to court, prophesied that the prince that was promised would be born from the line of Prince Aerys and Princess Rhaella Targaryen. When Prince Jaehaerys heard the prophecy, he arranged a marriage between his two children.

King Aegon and Prince Duncan were killed in the tragedy at Summerhall in 259 AC. It is uncertain if Jenny died in the fire or survived; according to a song, Jenny danced with her ghosts in the hall of kings, though this may be speaking of her life at Oldstones.

When Catelyn Tully was twelve, she camped at Oldstones while traveling to Seagard with her father, Lord Hoster Tully. Catelyn and Petyr Baelish, her father's ward, pretended to be Jenny and Duncan, with Catelyn winding flowers in her hair.

Recent Events[ ]

A Storm of Swords[ ]

The ghost of High Heart requests that Tom of Sevenstreams sing Jenny's song.

While at Oldstones, Robb Stark recalls a song about Jenny.

Tom sings about Jenny while waiting for Merrett Frey to arrive at Oldstones.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Among other sad songs, Marillion sings of Jenny and Prince Duncan Targaryen while imprisoned at the Eyrie.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

In Meereen, Ser Barristan Selmy recalls how all three sons of Aegon V Targaryen had married for love, including Duncan and Jenny, and that this resulted in tragedy and death.
