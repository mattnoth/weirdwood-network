History[ ]

He ruled Dorne when the Young Dragon, King Daeron I Targaryen, started his conquest of Dorne in 157 AC. The following year, he and forty of the most powerful Dornish lords bent their knees in the Submission of Sunspear.

After several long years of war, Daeron's brother and successor, King Baelor I Targaryen, walked barefoot from King's Landing to Sunspear in 161 AC to forge a peace between the Iron Throne and Dorne. The Prince accepted Baelor's proposal of peace, and betrothed his daughter and heir, Myriah, to Baelor's cousin, Prince Daeron Targaryen.

After the terms were arranged, he offered Baelor a galley to take him back to King's Landing, but Baelor insisted to walk. The Prince made arrangements to insure Baelor's safely on his way back and commanded Lord Wyl to released Baelor's cousin, Prince Aemon Targaryen.

By 174 AC, his son, Maron, had succeeded him as Prince of Dorne and Lord of Sunspear.
