Character[ ]

Lord Costayne was younger than Lord Shawney, but considered himself better born.

History[ ]

During the First Blackfyre Rebellion, Lord Costayne's lordly father fought for Daemon I Blackfyre in the Battle of the Redgrass Field.

In 212 AC, Lord Costayne came with his retinue to Whitewalls to attend the wedding of his father-in-law, Lord Ambrose Butterwell, and his second wife, the daughter of Lord Frey. On his way, Lord Costayne had to cross the Gods Eye via a ferry. He quarreled with Lord Shawney on the matter of which lord should cross first; Shawney argued that as the older of the two he should go first, whereas Costayne believed that as his House was nobler then Shawney's, he had the precedence.

During the wedding feast, Lord Costayne was seated on the dais. The next day, he competed in the wedding tourney, but lost to Ser Glendon Flowers. He sent his squire to ransom the sorrel he lost but Glendon decided to keep the horse. The angry lord told Glendon he had no right to use Fireball's coat of arms. When Glendon was falsely accused of stealing a dragon egg, Lord Costayne advocated for Glendon to die.

The fate of Lord Costayne following the arrival at Whitewalls of the Hand of the King, Brynden Rivers, and the suppression of the Second Blackfyre Rebellion is unknown.
