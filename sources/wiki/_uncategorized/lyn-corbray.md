Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Lyn Corbray

Lyn is a slender, handsome man with shoulder-length brown hair, a hard mouth, and restless eyes. Vain, reckless and hot-tempered, he has a wicked smile. Lyn is often venomous and mocking. The landless knight does not care for his elder brother, Lord Lyonel Corbray.

Ser Lyn wields Lady Forlorn and also carries a dagger. Coming from impoverished House Corbray, he is always short of money.

Kevan Lannister, Nestor Royce, and House Arryn guards all consider Lyn to be dangerous, with Kevan also considering him proud. Lord Robert Arryn dislikes Lyn. It is said that Lyn is "notoriously" uninterested in women. According to Petyr Baelish, Lyn likes "boys".

History[ ]

Ser Lyn won his knighthood during Robert's Rebellion. At the start of the war, he fought for House Targaryen alongside Lord Marq Grafton against Lord Jon Arryn at the gates of Gulltown. After being pardoned, Lyn joined Jon against the Targaryens.

Lyn's father was badly wounded during the Battle of the Trident. As Lyonel Corbray led their father back to the rear, Lyn took up the family's Valyrian steel longsword, Lady Forlorn, led a charge on the Dornish lines, and cut down the previously-wounded Prince Lewyn Martell of the Kingsguard.

Honoring Lyn's achievements on the battlefield, his father bequeathed him Lady Forlorn upon his death. Lyn's elder brother, Lyonel inherited Heart's Home and the rest of the family estate and titles, but still resented the loss of the family blade.

Lyn has slain many men in duels.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Lyn Corbray by Alexandre Dainche © Fantasy Flight Games

Ser Lyn is one of Lady Lysa Arryn's suitors after the death of Lord Jon Arryn. His squire is Mychel Redfort.

Lyn attends the trial of Tyrion Lannister at the Eyrie. He offers himself as champion of the Vale when Tyrion demands trial by combat, but is rebuffed by Lysa. Lyn suggests beheading Tyrion and sending his head to his brother, Ser Jaime Lannister, but is again refused.

Following Bronn's victory over Ser Vardis Egen in the trial, Lyn escorts Tyrion and Bronn to the Bloody Gate, where he commands them never to return to the Vale.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Lyn remains a suitor of Lysa.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Ser Kevan Lannister prefers having Lord Petyr Baelish ruling the Eyrie than any of Lysa's suitors, including Lyn.

Having married Lysa and been named Lord Protector of the Vale, Petyr travels to meet with the Corbrays.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Ser Lyn accompanies the Lords Declarant to their parley with Petyr and young Lord Robert Arryn at the Eyrie. Lord Nestor Royce claims that Lyn rarely left the side of Lysa for nearly half a year. During the parley, Ser Lyn grows tired of the conversation and threatens Petyr with his sword, Lady Forlorn, violating the laws of hospitality. Ser Lothor Brune moves to intercept, but it is the intervention of the other Lords Declarant that causes Lyn to put up his sword. Lyn leaves the parley shortly thereafter.

Later, Petyr admits to Sansa Stark that Lyn is in his employ, and it is his task to feign hatred for Petyr and to join every conspiracy against him and inform on them. Petyr states that Lyn's price is gold and boys.

With Mychel Redfort now a knight, Lyn's new squire is a lad considered a lout by Myranda Royce.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

Prior to the tourney for the Brotherhood of Winged Knights at the Gates of the Moon, Ser Lyn knocks down Ser Owen during a practice match. The buxom Myranda japes that Lyn would not be interested in her because of her gender.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

While it is mentioned that Lyn likes "boys", the age of these boys is unclear. As such, it is unclear if this is meant to imply that he is a pedophile. When asked about this, Elio Garcia agreed that the currently available information is too little to tell one way or the other, and agreed that "[Lyn being a pedophile is] an assumption without proof, so at least should be qualified".
