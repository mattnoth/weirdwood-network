Practitioners[ ]

In addition to common healers and midwives, there are a number of different orders practicing medicine, such as the maesters of the Citadel in Westeros, the House of the Red Hands in Braavos, the godswives of Lhazar, the Blue Graces of Slaver's Bay, and the Moonsingers of the Jogos Nhai. Septas and septons can also serve as healers. Among the Dothraki, barren herbwomen deal in potions and spells, and enslaved eunuchs wield knife, needle, and fire.

Commoners usually do not have access to a maester's services, like those who are rich or members of noble houses do, and have to rely on traveling barbers, local healers, midwives, hedge wizards, woods witches, and their own knowledge of herbs.

Treatment[ ]

  

Maester Ballabar with milk of the poppy. Art by Mauro Dal Bo © Fantasy Flight Games

Wounds are often cleaned with boiling wine, or treated with Myrish fire or firemilk, both of which burn on contact. Vinegar is also used to disinfect wounds as well as surgical instruments. Maesters learn to stitch wounds closed, and can use poultices containing mustard seeds, nettles, and bread mold to prevent infection. Maggots may be used if a wound is becoming gangrenous, as the maggots eat out the rotten parts. Leeches are also used to drain bad blood that builds up in wounds. Fractured limbs are immobilized using splints and plaster until the bones have knit; deep wounds may also be encased in plaster until the flesh grows back together. 

Pain can be dulled with potions containing opiates such as milk of the poppy or dreamwine, or by chewing willow bark or sourleaf. Moon tea is used as contraceptive, and to deal with unwanted pregnancies. 

The kiss of life is used by priests of the Drowned God to ritually revive drowned men, but at least one maester, Cerrick, has used it to revive someone from a more typical drowning. 

Poisons[ ]

Main article: Poison

Many poisons are used throughout the Seven Kingdoms and in Essos. Although the maesters of the Citadel study the histories and qualities of various poisons in addition to their healing arts, their use seems to be more widespread in the Free Cities, and the assassin guilds like the Faceless Men and the Sorrowful Men. Sweetsleep is a poison that can also be used as a medicinal treatment.

Diseases and Illness[ ]

  

Shireen Baratheon suffers from greyscale. Art by Sara Biddle © Fantasy Flight Games

Minor chills (colds), fevers, fluxes, and poxes are rarely deadly or even seriously debilitating, but can kill. The bloody flux is quite serious and often deadly. Whores often carry unpleasant poxes and venereal diseases. The continent of Sothoryos is home to many fatal diseases.

A number of diseases can be contracted primarily during childhood. Redspots is common, but it never kills anyone under the age of ten, and once contracted the victim is immune to it for the rest of their life. However, adults who did not contract it in childhood remain at risk of infection. Greyscale is rarely fatal to children, but nearly always fatal to adults. It makes the skin grey and hard as stone, and often leaves its survivors disfigured.

It was believed that Valyrians, such as members of House Targaryen, were immune to common illnesses, per the Doctrine of Exceptionalism. However, events have proved otherwise.
