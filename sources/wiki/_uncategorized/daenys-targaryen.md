History[ ]

  

Daenys and her family departing from Valyria, by Jota Saraiva

When Daenys was still a maiden, she had a powerful prophetic dream, showing the destruction of Valyria by fire. In 114 BC, her father, Lord Aenar Targaryen, heeded her dream and sold his holdings in the Valyrian Freehold and moved his family and all of their belongings to Dragonstone, an island in the narrow sea off the eastern coast of Westeros and the westernmost outpost of the Freehold. With them, they took five dragons, including Balerion. Though the Targaryens' retreat to faraway Dragonstone was perceived as an act of cowardice and surrender by rival dragonlords, many of whom were more powerful than they at the time, when the Doom of Valyria came twelve years later, Daenys' prophecy proved true, and House Targaryen was the only family of dragonriders which survived.

Daenys was married to her brother Gaemon, who followed their father as Lord of Dragonstone. Their children were Aegon and Elaena Targaryen. Daenys and Gaemon also had at least one other younger daughter, who would marry a petty lord.

Recent Events[ ]

A Feast for Crows[ ]

By the time of the War of the Five Kings, it is believed that visions written down by Daenys before the Doom of Valyria were in a book called Signs and Portents, that is now lost. Archmaester Marwyn claims he found three pages from it in his Book of Lost Books. After his kinsman Hotho Harlaw brought him a copy of the Book of Lost Books from Oldtown, Lord Rodrik Harlaw recounted Marwyn's claims about Daenys' alleged prophetic writings to his niece, Asha Greyjoy.

House of the Dragon[ ]

A prop history book used for the television series House of the Dragon contains text stating that "Daenys had claimed Balerion – at the time no bigger than a horse – the night before her fateful dream [of the Doom]", and the first person to whom she spoke about the prophetic vision was her mother, named Elaena Targaryen. The canon status of this information within the books is currently unknown.
