## Full Text

Oswell Whent was a knight from House Whent and a member of King Aerys II Targaryen's Kingsguard.

In the television adaptation Game of Thrones, Oswell is merged with Gerold Hightower, who is portrayed by Eddie Eyre.

Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Oswell Whent

Oswell wore a distinctive helmet emblazoned with a black bat with its wings spread, symbolizing House Whent. He was known and remembered for his dark humor.

History[ ]

Lord Walter Whent of Harrenhal, Oswell's older brother, announced the tourney at Harrenhal shortly after a visit from Oswell. It was proposed by Varys and believed by some that Prince Rhaegar Targaryen was secretly sponsoring the tourney in order to gather lords together to deal with the matter of his mad father, King Aerys II Targaryen, and used Oswell as a go-between to Lord Whent. Oswell was present at the tourney when Ser Jaime Lannister became a member of the Kingsguard.

According to a semi-canon source, Oswell, along with his other sworn brother Ser Arthur Dayne, aided Rhaegar in the kidnapping of Lyanna Stark near Harrenhal as the year of the false spring was ending. Robert's Rebellion began shortly after.

At the end of the rebellion, Lord Eddard Stark encountered Ser Oswell and two other knights of the Kingsguard, Arthur and Ser Gerold Hightower, at the tower of joy in Dorne. Eddard told them that King's Landing had already fallen and that King Aerys II Targaryen was dead. In the ensuing conflict, all three Kingsguard were killed. Eddard had been with six northern companions, but he and Howland Reed were the only survivors. Stones from the tower were used to build Oswell's cairn upon a nearby ridge.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Eddard Stark, fevered in the black cells of the Red Keep, dreams of the combat at the tower of joy, where he and his companions encountered and battled Oswell and two other knights of the Kingsguard, Ser Gerold Hightower and Ser Arthur Dayne.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

With his head on a weirwood stump, Jaime Lannister dreams he is below Casterly Rock, and is confronted with the shades of Prince Rhaegar Targaryen, and his fellow Kingsguard, Oswell, Arthur, Gerold, Jon Darry, and Lewyn Martell. They admonish Jaime for his murder of King Aerys II Targaryen, and for his failure to protect Rhaegar's wife Elia Martell, and children Rhaenys and Aegon.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Ser Barristan Selmy recalls that Lord Walter Whent announced the tourney at Harrenhal shortly after a visit from his brother, Oswell.
