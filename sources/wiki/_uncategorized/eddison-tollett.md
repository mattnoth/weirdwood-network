Eddison Tollett, better known as Dolorous Edd, is a squire from House Tollett and a steward of the Night's Watch.

In the television adaptation Game of Thrones, Eddison is portrayed by Ben Crompton.

Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Eddison Tollett

Eddison is a thin man, dour and grey-haired. He has a long face, like a mule's. He has a dry and pessimistic sense of humor. He is well-liked by his brothers in the Night's Watch.

History[ ]

Dolorous Edd appears to have been born to a lesser branch of House Tollett, as he speaks of being raised in a crude house such as the poorer of the smallfolk might have. Per George R. R. Martin, he was recruited to the Night's Watch by Yoren, who told him women could not resist a man in uniform, leaving out the Night's Watch vow of celibacy.

Edd once lost track of his white horse while it was snowing.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Dolorous Edd, by Amok © Fantasy Flight Games

Edd is part of the great ranging beyond the Wall. Jeor Mormont, Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, trusts his horse to Edd, and he also has Edd retrieve his axe to give as a gift to Craster. When Jon Snow joins Qhorin Halfhand's mission into the Frostfangs, Qhorin reminds Jeor that Edd can serve the Lord Commander as his steward.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Edd survives when wights attack the Fist of the First Men, helping Samwell Tarly retreat. They are among the survivors to make it back to Craster's Keep.

Once at Craster's Keep, Edd is one of the few men that takes Sam seriously when he tells the men he slew an Other with his dragonglass dagger. Edd, along with Dywen, makes Sam and Grenn tell Lord Commander Mormont the tale. After the death of the Lord Commander during the mutiny at Craster's Keep, Edd flees with Grenn, Giant and other loyalists back to the Wall for fear of being killed by the mutineers.

When the Weeper starts his assault near the Shadow Tower, Bowen Marsh assembles the garrison of Castle Black, along with Edd, to aid Ser Denys Mallister. During the battle beneath the Wall, the defenders name a straw sentinel after Edd.

  

Edd and other brothers of the Night's Watch - Grenn, Jon Snow, Pyp, Satin, and Samwell Tarly - by chillyravenart ©

Against his wishes, Edd receives a few votes during the election of the new Lord Commander. Edd later puts forth Jon Snow's name instead, and Jon is chosen by the black brothers.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

When Samwell Tarly leaves Castle Black, Edd says farewell to him.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Edd carries on his duties as Lord Commander Snow's steward. He is one of the men brought along as muscle to assist Jon in escorting Janos Slynt out of Castle Black to carry out his duties as garrison commander of Greyguard. When Janos refuses to obey the order, Edd and Iron Emmett escort Janos outside for his execution, and Edd fetches Jon a block per his request.

While riding to Mole's Town, Jon jokes that the solemn face carved by wildlings on an ash heart tree resembles Edd. 

Eventually, Lord Commander Snow assigns Emmett to Long Barrow, where Jon sent wildling spearwives, with Edd as his steward and second in command. Satin replaces Edd as the Lord Commander's steward.

Edd briefly returns to Castle Black with six wagons so that more spearwives can be transported to Long Barrow.
