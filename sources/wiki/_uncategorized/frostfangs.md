Geography[ ]

  

The Frostfangs by reneaigner©

The Frostfangs lie north of the Wall, just northwest of the Shadow Tower, separating the Lands of Always Winter to their west and the haunted forest to their east. Its southern peaks and foothills transition into the northern mountains, which are part of the north within the Seven Kingdoms. One of the largest mountains in the Frostfangs is called Forktop by the Night's Watch. 

The Frostfangs are a cruel and inhospitable wilderness of stone and ice, blue-grey in color with snow-capped peaks. However, the mountains conceal a diverse series of wonders. Icy waterfalls plunge over sheer stone cliffs, great lakes reflect snowy peaks, there are ravines so deep and black they seem to go all the way to hell, and bridges of natural stone span distances with only the sky to either side. 

The foothills of the Frostfangs have numerous winding valleys. The easiest way into the mountains is to follow the Milkwater to its source, but this path is visible from the Fist of the First Men. Other paths into the Frostfangs include the Giant's Stair and the Skirling Pass, if it is clear.

Hidden valleys sustaining small numbers of people exist in the Frostfangs. The Thenns live in one such valley in the northern Frostfangs. Wildflowers, coldsnaps, frostfires, and piper's grass grow in the mountain meadows.

History[ ]

Some free folk are said to worship dark gods beneath the Frostfangs.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Jon Snow and Ghost, by Guillem Pongiluppi ©

When Benjen Stark goes missing while ranging beyond the Wall, Ser Jaremy Rykker leads two sweeps from Castle Black and Qhorin Halfhand ventures forth from the Shadow Tower. They are unable to find the First Ranger, with the marks he left on trees stopping in the stony highlands in the northwest.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Ygritte by Mariana Vieira © Fantasy Flight Games

Craster informs Jeor Mormont, Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, that Mance Rayder, King-Beyond-the-Wall, is gathering the bulk of the free folk into the Frostfangs. Jeor does not expect the wildlings to remain in the inhospitable mountains for long.

Qhorin's rangers defeat Alfyn Crowkiller's wildlings, although a few flee toward the Frostfangs. A captured wildling informs Qhorin that Mance is searching for something to help bring down the Wall.

The Night's Watch send three scouting missions to search for Mance: one up the Giant's Stair under Jarman Buckwell, one to probe the Milkwater under Thoren Smallwood, and one into the Skirling Pass under Qhorin Halfhand.

In the Skirling Pass, Jon Snow and Stonesnake are sent ahead to dispatch wildling lookouts. They kill Orell and another man, but Jon releases Ygritte instead of executing her. Qhorin decides they should turn back after the rangers are discovered by the eagle of Orell, who was a skinchanger, and the Halfhand leaves Dalbridge to cover their retreat.

The eagle follows the black brothers as they head back. Qhorin sends Ebben ahead on horseback and Stonesnake afoot up the mountainside, but Ebben is slain by Rattleshirt's band of wildlings. Recognizing that they will be overtaken, the Halfhand commands Jon to yield to the wildlings and do whatever is asked of him in order to gain their trust and learn what they are searching for. When they are apprehended by Rattleshirt, Ygritte, and other free folk, Jon yields as he was instructed and kills Qhorin.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Jon Snow is brought before Mance Rayder by Rattleshirt. The free folk host departs the Frostfangs via the Milkwater.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Mance is said to have found the Horn of Joramun in a glacier among the Frostfangs, but Tormund insists Mance did not find the true horn.

Ser Denys Mallister writes that his rangers have spotted great camps of wildlings in the mountains beyond the Shadow Tower.
