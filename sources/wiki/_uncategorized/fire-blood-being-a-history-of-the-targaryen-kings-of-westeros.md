History[ ]

  

Gyldayn's three chief sources for the Dance of the Dragons: Eustace, Mushroom, and Munkun © Chase Stone in The Rise of the Dragon

Only the first volume of Gyldayn's work is known to have been completed, covering the reigns of the Targaryen kings from Aegon I to the regency of Aegon III Targaryen. The book incorporates the contents of several of the archmaester's earlier works with only minor amendments, such as The Princess and the Queen, The Rogue Prince, and The Sons of the Dragon.

In the original texts, after completing the first chapter, covering Aegon's Conquest, Archmaester Gyldayn sent a copy to Archmaester Gerold, perhaps for his commentary and approval. However, this manuscript was lost until Maester Yandel discovered it along with other fragments written by Gyldayn among Gerold's papers. Yandel included this chapter into his work The World of Ice & Fire, in which he wrote that he hoped the rest of Gyldayn's masterwork would one day be discovered. Yandel's  usage of Gyldayn's text was done with the latter's blessing.

Gyldayn drew upon many sources for his work. These included official court records and the writings of various maesters, especially the Grand Maesters, who kept written accounts with varying degrees of diligence. Another key source was the writings of Septon Barth, the long-time Hand of the King to Jaehaerys I Targaryen.

In covering the Dance of the Dragons, Gyldayn most heavily referenced three main sources, The Reign of King Viserys, First of His Name, and the Dance of the Dragons That Came After by Septon Eustace, The Dance of the Dragons, A True Telling by Grand Maester Munkun, and The Testimony of Mushroom as dictated by the court jester Mushroom. Both Mushroom and Eustace had close ties to the Targaryens and were well-placed to know much of their goings-on, though Gyldayn noted their accounts were often contradictory and filled with salacious rumors and accusations. Munkun, though not an eye-witness himself, drew heavily upon the confessions of the former Grand Maester Orwyle, who was, as well as interviews and other sources. Gyldayn considered Munkun's history " by far the most detailed and authoritative" and thus referenced him frequently.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

George R. R. Martin, the real life author of the fictional texts, decided to let Gyldayn live into Robert I Baratheon's reign, so his history book could cover the entire Targaryen dynasty. Hence in the latest prints, Gyldayn shared his uncompleted work with Archmaester Perestan, who then informed Yandel. Gyldayn was persuaded by Yandel to permit the use of passages from Gyldayn's work in Yandel's book.
