Culture[ ]

Frogs are included in the arms of House Marsh, and a toad appears in the arms of House Vypren.

Frogs are hunted by crannogmen in the Neck, and Meera Reed wields a slender three-pronged frog spear. Freys claim that eating frogs causes crannogmen to have green teeth and moss growing under their arms. Some rivermen believe that crannogmen have webbed hands and feet like frogs.

The Toad Stone, an ancient idol carved from greasy black stone in the shape of a a gigantic toad, can be found on the the Isle of Toads.

History[ ]

Meria Martell, Princess of Dorne, was called the "the Yellow Toad of Dorne" by Argilac Durrandon, the Storm King.

Lord Toad was a fool at Casterly Rock during the rule of Lord Gerold Lannister.

Maggy the Frog was a reputed sorceress at Lannisport.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Ser Alliser Thorne gives the nickname "Toad" to Todder.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Meera and Jojen Reed bring gifts of fish, frog, and fowl to Bran Stark for the harvest feast at Winterfell.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Meera hunts frogs while Bran and his companions travel through the wolfswood north of Winterfell, but Bran grows tired of eating them.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Arya Stark, disguised as Cat of the Canals, overhears sailors in Braavos speaking about rains of toads.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Prince Quentyn Martell takes the name Frog while disguised with the Windblown.

As part of the plot devised by Skahaz mo Kandaq and Ser Barristan Selmy to overthrow King Hizdahr zo Loraq, some of the Brazen Beasts who are part of the plan put on masks in the likeness of toads.
