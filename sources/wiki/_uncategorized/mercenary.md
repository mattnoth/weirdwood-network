Types[ ]

Sellsword[ ]

See also: Images of mercenaries

  

Hired Swords by Yann Tisseron © Fantasy Flight Games

A sellsword is a soldier who hires out his services to the highest bidder. Most sellswords are professionals with some experience in fighting. Sellswords are more common in Essos than in Westeros, as Westerosi lords are more likely to raise feudal levies. The mercantile cities of Lys, Myr, and Tyrosh are protected by hired sellswords. Some fighting men or exiles who run out of money become sellswords, such as Ser Jorah Mormont. Many sellswords keep their worldly possessions close at hand, such as expensive weaponry, armor, and silks.

Sellswords are often viewed negatively by others, with some nobles considering them unchivalrous and pitiless. Sellswords are not allowed within the Black Walls of eastern Volantis. Sellswords have a reputation for being willing to betray anyone for enough gold. Sellswords, pirates, and slavers inhabit the Basilisk Isles.

Free Companies[ ]

  

Mercenary Contract by Yulia Startsev © Fantasy Flight Games

Many sellswords are organized into free companies. Some are acclaimed for their loyalty and discipline, such as the Golden Company, while others are nothing but rabble joined together in search of loot, like the Brave Companions. Some captains are alleged to instigate wars during peacetime, and some companies attempt to conquer the Disputed Lands when not under contract. Men of the free companies name themselves as they choose, so some exiles from Westeros might claim the name of extinct Houses.

Freerider[ ]

"Freerider" is a broad term, sometimes used to denote a mounted sellsword, but more often referring to other mounted fighters who are not part of a lord's retinue or feudal levy. Most do not collect wages, instead fighting for plunder or a hope to be taken into a lord or knight's service. Freeriders are mostly used as scouts, outriders, foragers, and light cavalry.

Some freeriders are similar to hedge knights, only lacking knighthood. Others are green, untrained recruits and farm boys who have nowhere else to go. While some refugees flee to a larger settlement when war sweeps over villages, others become freeriders for a better chance of being fed and thereby avoid becoming outlaws or broken men.

Sellsail[ ]

A sellsail is a hired sailor who engages in naval battles for pay, including seaborne mercenaries and pirates. Most fleets are based in the Stepstones. Sellsails such as Salladhor Saan may work as pirates or smugglers when not employed.

Many ironborn, including Harwyn Hoare and Dalton Greyjoy, have worked as sellsails, as have Summer Islanders.

History[ ]

  

Island Mercenaries by Ian Kirkpatrick © Fantasy Flight Games

Sellswords helped the Golden Empire of Yi Ti conquer Leng centuries ago.

During the coming of the Andals to Westeros, some Andals were hired as mercenaries by First Men to fight their rivals, only to betray their patrons. Andals and sellswords helped to bring down House Greyiron in the Iron Islands. Lord Torrence Teague used wealth raided from the westerlands to hire sellswords from Essos, allowing him to eventually become King of the Trident.

Many free companies were formed during the Century of Blood, the aftermath of the Doom of Valyria. The Free Cities often hired the companies for their endless wars in the Disputed Lands and the Stepstones. Likewise, most sellsail fleets were formed during the wars of Lys, Myr, and Tyrosh.

The Bright Banners and the Second Sons fled instead of facing the khalasar of Khal Temmo, but the Three Thousand of Qohor held off the Dothraki.

Prince Daemon Targaryen hired sellswords for his war for the Stepstones. During the Dance of the Dragons, Ser Perkin the Flea granted knighthood to any sellswords who supported Trystane Truefyre, and mercenaries flocked to support Hugh Hammer. The collapse of the Triarchy greatly increased demand for mercenaries in Essos, causing surplus northmen after the Hour of the Wolf and other Westerosi to found new free companies.

The Golden Company was founded by Ser Aegor Rivers and other Westerosi exiles in the aftermath of the First Blackfyre Rebellion. Ser Lucas Inchfield considered Ser Duncan the Tall and Ser Bennis of the Brown Shield to have been sellswords when they served Ser Eustace Osgrey as sworn swords.

The peace between Braavos and Pentos prohibits the Pentoshi from hiring sellswords or free companies. House Baelish descends from a Braavosi sellsword.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

King Robert I Baratheon ponders abdicating the Iron Throne and fighting as a sellsword. Hiring an army of sellswords is not as expensive as hiring a Faceless Man to kill a merchant.

The sellswords Bronn and Chiggen help Catelyn Stark arrest Tyrion Lannister in hope of reward, and Bronn defends Tyrion in his trial by combat in hope of gold from Casterly Rock.

Sellswords and freeriders flock to the service of Lord Tywin Lannister at the Rock as tensions rise in the Seven Kingdoms. Similar men join the host of Robb Stark.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Stannis Baratheon hires sellswords and sellsails from Lys and Myr in preparation for the War of the Five Kings.

Ser Stafford Lannister raises a new host from sellswords, freeriders, and boys from Lannisport. The host is destroyed by Robb in the Battle of Oxcross, however.

Tywin employs the Brave Companions as reavers and foragers in the riverlands. Vargo Hoat betrays the Lannisters, however, and his Bloody Mummers aid in the fall of Harrenhal.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

The Mummers abandon the wounded Vargo almost to a man, allowing Ser Gregor Clegane to easily take Harrenhal.

Following the fall of Astapor to Daenerys Targaryen, Yunkai hires the Second Sons and the Stormcrows. Daario Naharis brings the Stormcrows to Daenerys's side, however, and they are victorious in the battle near Yunkai. Ben Plumm leads the defeated Second Sons who join Daenerys after the battle.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

  

Exiled Westerosi sometimes become mercenaries, screencap from Game of Thrones Bluray

The Golden Company breaks its contract with Myr, the first time it has ever done so.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

After the fall of Meereen to Daenerys, Yunkai hires several free companies to defeat her. Doran Martell, Prince of Dorne, sends his son, Prince Quentyn, to Essos to ally with and wed Daenerys. Quentyn and his companions, Ser Gerris Drinkwater and Ser Archibald Yronwood, join the Windblown during their journey.

Ben leads the Seconds Sons from Daenerys's service over to the Yunkai'i. Tyrion and Ser Jorah Mormont join the company during the second siege of Meereen. After Daenerys disappears from Meereen, Quentyn allies with the Tattered Prince in a failed scheme to claim dragons.

Supporting Aegon Targaryen, Jon Connington and Harry Strickland lead the landing of the Golden Company in the stormlands. There are reports of sellswords landing throughout southern Westeros.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

Stannis orders Ser Justin Massey to sail to Braavos and use the Iron Bank's gold to hire a reputable company.
