## Full Text

Due to editing issues caused by the length of the calculations, the list is divided into four pages. Use the navigation box, or navigate here to page 1, page 2 or page 4. You are currently on page 3.

In the main series and novellas, George R.R. Martin on most occasions does not state specifically in which year a character has been born or has died. This page lists the calculations to determine such years, years of birth mostly, but also years of death at times, as specific as is possible, for all characters for all this is possible.
This information then can be used in articles and serves to give a more complete image of the world of A Song of Ice and Fire.

The following guidelines are used for the calculations:

A character, both male and female, will be at least 12 years old at the birth of his or her first born child, for the following reason: a girl who has had her first flowering (i.e. first menstruation) is considered to be fit for both marriage and consummation of the marriage. Most highborn girls have their first flowering at the age of twelve or thirteen, indicating twelve as the youngest a girl can be for getting pregnant, and, as such, give birth. For boys, the same minimum age is used. Furthermore, we are given the precedence of the age of twelve for both males and females: the daughter of Unwin Peake died in childbirth at the age of twelve, while Viserys II Targaryen impregnated his wife at the age of twelve.

Siblings are assumed to be at least one year apart in age, taking into consideration that a woman who has just given birth will not immediately become pregnant again. This is only when discussing full-blood siblings. For siblings with the same father, but with a different mother, no such recovery period is required.

Where historic works are concerned which have been written by maesters, or sources recited from works written by maesters, it can be assumed that the age of a character stated in a year is the age that character would be turning that year. For example, Maegor I Targaryen was born in 12 AC. In historic works, his age in the year 25 AC will be stated as thirteen (the age he would be turning that year), without the author taking into account whether or not the described event took place before or after Maegor's nameday. This goes for historical works (i.e. The Sons of the Dragon, The Rogue Prince, The Princess and the Queen, Fire & Blood, and The World of Ice & Fire), not for POV-information, like A Game of Thrones and its sequels, or The Hedge Knight, and other tales of Dunk & Egg. Do note that on some occasions, this rule might not apply.

The following minimum ages for pages, squires, and knights are used, based on an overview of known ages:

Boys are considered to be at least six years old when becoming a page.

Boys are considered to be at least eight years old when becoming a squire.

Men are considered to be at least fifteen years old when becoming a knight.

A mother will be no older than ~52 years old at the earliest possibility for the birth of her youngest child, for the following reason: the Westerosi consider the age of forty-two to be "past [a woman's] childbearing years", and forty-four to be "well past her childbearing years", although women of forty-four have been known to give birth to a child. In real-life, the end of a woman's fertile cycle (menopause) occurs at an age of 49-52 on average, giving ~52 as a maximum age for giving birth to a child.

The characters are listed alphabetically, using full names. Maesters (like Aemon), will also be listed alphabetically under their birth names, unless those are not known.

Names that belong to several characters (for example, the name Walder Frey) will be listed alphabetically, but will have their nickname or relations listed in parentheses, so they can be distinguished from one another.

For calculations on placings of events, see Calculations of Events.

Kaeth[ ]

Birth[ ]

Kaeth was eighty years old when he was raised to the office of Grand Maester. This occurred in either 256 AC or 257 AC (due to his death, less than a year later, having occurred in either 257 AC or 258 AC, see below under "Death"). If Kaeth was eighty years old in either 256 AC or 257 AC, that gives him the following options:

79 turning 80 in 256 AC, placing his birth in 176 AC

80 turning 81 in 256 AC, placing his birth in 175 AC

79 turning 80 in 257 AC, placing his birth in 177 AC

80 turning 81 in 257 AC, placing his birth in 176 AC

No further specifications can be given. Kaeth was thus born in either 175 AC, 176 AC or 177 AC.

Death[ ]

Kaeth died within a year of being raised to the office of Grand Maester, after which he was replaced. His replacement, Ellendor, also died within a year of being raised to the office, which cause another replacement, Merion, to be send to King's Landing, who died on the journey over there. That was when Pycelle was raised, in 259 AC.

With Pycelle having started in 259 AC, that means that Merion died in 259 AC. Merion died on the way to King's Landing, which means he was named Grand Maester either in 259 AC or in 258 AC (it is unknown how long the journey takes exactly, or in which part of the year this all occurred).This means that Ellendor died in either 258 AC or 259 AC. With Kaeth dying a little less than a year before Ellendor, places Kaeth's death in either 257 AC or 258 AC.

Kiera of Tyrosh[ ]

Birth[ ]

Kiera of Tyrosh's only living child, Princess Vaella Targaryen, was born in 222 AC. Following rule #5, Kiera would be no older than ~52 at the birth of her youngest child, meaning that the earliest possible year of birth for Kiera is ~170 AC.

Prior to Vaella, Kiera also had stillborn sons with her first husband, Prince Valarr Targaryen. The hunchback septon who accuses Bloodraven of killing them by summoning a shadow mentions multiple sons but only a single shadow, suggesting their sons may have been twins. As Valarr's sons are not mentioned as an impediment to his uncle King Aerys I Targaryen taking the throne, they were born prior to Valarr's death in 209 AC (see the Valarr Targaryen). Kiera would have been at least twelve years old at the birth of her sons, meaning that her latest possible year of birth is 197 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Kiera was thus born between ~170 AC and 197 AC.

Death[ ]

Kiera's youngest child, Vaella, was born in 222 AC, meaning 222 is the earliest possible year of her death.

No further specifications can be given. Jena thus died in 222 AC or after.

Laena Penrose[ ]

Birth[ ]

As Laena's mother Elaena was born in 150 AC (see Elaena Targaryen), she would have reached the end of her child-bearing years near 200 AC, when she turned fifty. Laena was the second of the four children of Ronnel and Elaena, indicating that she could not have been born any later than 198 AC, when Elaena was fourty-eight years old.

No further specifications can be given. Laena was thus born in or between 185 AC and ~198 AC.

Lonnel Snow[ ]

Birth[ ]

Lonnel Snow was the bastard son of Brandon Stark. Brandon was born in no earlier than 140 AC and died no later than 212 AC (see Brandon Stark (son of Cregan)). Brandon would have been at least twelve years old at the moment of Lonnel's birth. Therefore, Lonnel could not have been born before 152 AC.

Brandon's relationship with Lonnel's mother precedes his marriage to Alys Karstark, so Lonnel cannot have been born earlier than his younger half-sibling Rodwell Stark, however they could have been born in the same year. Rodwell was born no later than 195 AC (see Rodwell Stark), so Lonnel was born in 195 AC the latest.

No further specifications can be given. Lonnel was thus born in or between 152 AC and 195 AC.

Loras Tyrell[ ]

Birth[ ]

Loras Tyrell is described as being sixteen years old during the Hand's tourney in 298 AC,  and later also when Eddard Stark sends men after Gregor Clegane. Catelyn Tully, upon seeing Loras in 299 AC, thinks he cannot have been more than two years older than Robb, who was, at that moment, fifteen, while also thinking how Loras could not yet have reached his second nameday wen Robert Baratheon won his crown in 283 AC. When Tyrion is speaking with Ser Loras after the Battle of the Blackwater, but before Margaery's and Joffrey's wedding, thus in 299 AC, Loras is mentioned to be seventeen years old.
This could give the following options:

15 turning 16 in 298 AC, 16 turning 17 in 299 AC, and 17 turning 18 in 300 AC, placing his birth year in 282 AC

16 turning 17 in 298 AC, 17 turning 18 in 299 AC, and 18 turning 19 in 300 AC, placing his birth year in 281 AC

On the day of Margaery's wedding to Tommen, in the year 300 AC, when Margaery herself is noted to be sixteen years old, Cersei notes that Loras is one year older than her. With Margaery still being sixteen years old, that would make Loras seventeen years old in 300 AC, meaning that Loras was born in 282 AC, as the other option would not be possible, compared to Cersei's quote.

In addition, Jaime Lannister mentions that Loras is seventeen years old in 300 AC, confirming that Loras was seventeen in both 299 AC and 300 AC, placing his birth in 282 AC.

Loras was thus born in 282 AC.

Lysa Tully[ ]

Birth[ ]

Lysa Tully was two years younger than her sister Catelyn. At one point, Catelyn describes a memory, during which she states that she herself was no older than Sansa (who was twelve at that time), while Lysa was younger than Arya (who was nine, soon to turn ten). Sansa and Arya are said to be two years apart in age. However, looking at the moment during the year their namedays take place, it is clear that Arya's nameday falls early in the year, while Sansa's nameday falls at the end of the year. So even though they are born three calendar years apart, they actually only differ two years and a few months in age, which is then rounded to two years when people are talking about it.

Looking at Catelyn's memory, she states there that she and Lysa are further apart in age than Sansa and Arya, yet she and Lysa still qualify as being two years apart in age. So Catelyn and Lysa differ nearly two and a half years in age (since anything more than two and a half years would be rounded up to three years, generally speaking). As Catelyn was born no earlier than 264 AC (see Catelyn Tully), Lysa was born no earlier than 266 AC.

Catelyn Tully was born no later than 265 AC (see CatelynTully). If Catelyn's birth fell in the first half of the year 265 AC, Lysa's birth, being between two and two and a half years later, would fall in 267 AC. If Catelyn's birth fell in the last few months of 265 AC, however, Lysa's birth could possibly fall in the first few months of 268 AC.

During Catelyn's earlier mentioned memory, she described Petyr Baelish as "even younger still" compared to Lysa. As Petyr Baelish was born in 268 AC (see Petyr Baelish), this should indicate that, if Lysa had indeed been born in 268 AC, Petyr would have been born much later that year.

No further specifications can be given. Lysa Tully was thus born in either 266 AC, 267 AC, or 268 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Perianne was thus born in or between 239 AC and 249 AC.

Perwyn Frey[ ]

Birth[ ]

Perwyn Frey's elder sister Tyta was born in 269 AC or 270 AC (see Tyta Frey). As Perwyn and Tyta have different mothers (Bethany Rosby and Alyssa Blackwood respectively), it is possible they were born in the same year. Thus, 269 AC is the first possible year of birth for Perwyn.

Perwyn's younger brother Olyvar was born in 281 AC (see Olyvar Frey). Between Perwyn and Olyvar are two brothers, meaning that Perwyn's latest possible birth year is 278 AC, three years before Olyvar.

No further specifications can be given. Perwyn was thus born in or between 269 AC and 278 AC.

Petyr Baelish[ ]

Birth[ ]

When Petyr Baelish was "scarcely fifteen", Brandon Stark was twenty years old. It is known that Brandon Stark died at the age of twenty in 282 AC, and that he was born in 282 AC. That means that Brandon would have been twenty years old in both 282 AC (19 turning 20), and in 283 AC (20 turning 21). As Brandon died in 282 AC, this shows that the duel took place in 282 AC.

Petyr Baelish was described as "still shy of 30" in 298 AC. This shows he was still 29 years old in 298 AC, though it is possible he turned 30 later that year, leaving the options:

28 turning 29 in 298 AC, placing his birth in 269 AC

29 turning 30 in 298 AC, placing his birth in 268 AC

"Still shy of 30" could suggest Petyr was even younger than 29 in 298 AC, which would place his birth more recently in the timeline.

Petyr's age in 282 AC is described as "scarcely fifteen"; However, several interpretations are possible. If Petyr was indeed fifteen years old in 282 AC, he would have been born in either 267 AC (14 turning 15 in 282 AC) or 266 AC (15 turning 16). As both options would have made Petyr thirty or older when meeting with Catelyn in 298 AC, it would seem that this interpretation of "scarcely fifteen" is not the correct usage.

However, "scarcely" can also be defined to mean "barely; hardly; not quite", indicating that the description of "scarcely fifteen" suggests that Petyr had not yet been fifteen years old at the time of the duel, but fourteen. This would give the following option:

13 turning 14 in 282 AC, placing his birth in 268 AC

As previously established, Petyr cannot have been 14 turning 15 in 282 AC, as it would place his birth before 268 AC. 

Petyr Baelish was thus born in 268 AC.

Note: Should Petyr have been younger than twenty-nine during 298 AC, his nameday would have occurred later (269, 270 etc.). As seen above, it would not be possible for Petyr to be either fourteen or fifteen during the time Brandon was twenty if this was the case, since it would lead to Petyr being turning 14 after Brandon had already died.

Petyr Frey[ ]

Birth[ ]

According to Edmure Tully, Petyr Frey is "a year or two" older than Robb Stark, who was born in 283 AC (see  Robb Stark). Being one year older than Robb Stark would place Petyr's birth in 282 AC, being two years older would place his birth in 281 AC. 

Petyr has a daughter, who was born in 294 AC (see  Perra Frey). With the minimum age of twelve at the birth of a firstborn child, this would place Petyr's birth in 282 AC or before.

However, Petyr married his wife Mylenda Caron at the age of ten, when she was three times his age. Mylenda is also mentioned to be twice as old as her husband in 300 AC. This information clarifies matters, because now we can use two linear equations to solve the variables:  

p = Petyr's age at wedding
m = Mylenda's age at wedding
x = years since wedding

m = 3p 
m + x = 2 (p + x)

Solving:
m + x = 2p + 2x
m = 2p + x
3p = 2p + x
p = x

if p = 10
then x = 10

Therefore Petyr was 10 then, 20 now; Mylenda was 30 then, 40 now.

Petyr being twenty in 300 AC and ten in 290 AC gives him the following options:

19 turning 20 in 300 AC, placing his birth in 280 AC

20 turning 21 in 300 AC, placing his birth in 279 AC

As Robb Stark was born in 283 AC, this eliminates Petyr's option of having been born in 279 AC, as four years is too much to be an estimate of "a year or two older".

Petyr was thus born in 280 AC.

Quentyn Martell[ ]

Birth[ ]

Quentyn Martell mentions he is eighteen years old during 300 AC. This leaves the options:

17 turning 18 in 300 AC, placing his birth in 282 AC

18 turning 19 in 300 AC, placing his birth in 281 AC

Quentyn received his knighthood at the age of eighteen, prior to leaving Dorne. Quentyn receiving his knighthood cannot have happened in 300 AC. Quentyn refused his uncle Oberyn to knight him, accepting knighthood from Lord Anders Yronwood instead. Since Oberyn Martell travelled away from Dorne in late 299 AC and did not return alive, Quentyn's refusal of Oberyn occurred in 299 AC. Secondly Lord Anders cannot have knighted Quentyn in 300 AC, as he went with his army to the Prince's Pass in 299 AC, while Quentyn remained at Yronwood, demonstrating that Anders would have knighted Quentyn in 299 AC, prior to departing Yronwood. Thus, as Quentyn was knighted in 299 AC, and was 18 upon receiving his knighthood, he would have been eighteen years old in both 299 AC as well as 300 AC, thereby demonstrating that Quentyn was thus 17 turning 18 in 299 AC and 18 turning 19 in 300 AC, placing his birth in 281 AC.

Quentyn Martell was thus born in 281 AC.

Birth[ ]

Qyle Martell[ ]

Birth[ ]

Qyle Martell was the younger brother of Aliandra Martell, who was born in 115 AC (see Aliandra Martell). Since Aliandra and Qyle were not twins, there is at least one year in between their births. Thus 116 AC is the earliest possible year of birth for Qyle.

In 132 AC Qyle was angered by the attention his sister Aliandra lavished upon Lord Alyn Velaryon.

No further specifications can be given. Qyle was thus born in or between 116 AC and 132 AC.

Note: In 132 AC, the regents of Aegon III suggested "a younger brother of the Princess of Dorne" as a potential husband for Lady Baela Targaryen. If Qyle is this younger brother, it would imply he was born closer to 116 AC than to 132 AC.
