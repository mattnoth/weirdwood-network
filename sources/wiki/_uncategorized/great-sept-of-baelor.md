## Summary

The Great Sept of Baelor, also called the Great Sept, Baelor's Sept, and Beloved Baelor's Sept, is the center of religious worship for the Faith of the Seven and the seat of the High Septon. The sept is located in King's Landing, the capital of the Seven Kingdoms. The building is named after the septon-king, Baelor I Targaryen. The Great Sept is rivaled only by the Starry Sept of Oldtown and the castle sept of Highgarden.

Layout[ ]

See also: Images of the Great Sept of Baelor

The Great Sept of Baelor is located at the top of Visenya's Hill in King's Landing. The plaza of white marble which surrounds the sept features a statue of King Baelor the Blessed standing tall and serene upon a plinth, his face a study of benevolence. Large gardens, capable of holding hundreds of people, surround the sept, and marble steps lead up to the building.

The Great Sept is an impressive marble structure with a great dome and seven slender crystal towers, the bells of which can be rung in mourning or to gather crowds. The lofty dome is made of leaded glass, gold, and crystal. Before the doors leading into the building is a raised marble pulpit from which a septon can address a gathering crowd.

Past the towering outer doors is the Hall of Lamps, with its suspended globes of colored leaded glass. The sept's floor is polished marble.

Past the entrance hall, through inner doors, is the cavernous sept proper, with seven broad aisles meeting beneath the dome. The interior contains benches for worshippers and a stepped marble bier upon which bodies can be placed for funerals. The building has high windows, and light is reflected into rainbows by hanging crystals and the glass dome. Incense sweetens the air.

The sept has towering stone statues of the Seven. Scented candles can be placed by worshippers at each altar, with most placed before those of the Mother and the Maiden but only a few before the Stranger. The Warrior stands on an altar of red marble. Different altars are used for different ceremonies, depending on their purpose. For example, marriages are conducted at the altar between two towering gilded statues of the Father and the Mother. There are several sets of doors leading into the sept proper; septons use the Father's Doors, septas the Mother's Doors, and silent sisters the Stranger's Steps. 

The Great Sept has seven transepts, various crypts and chapels, and a rainbow pool. The sept has tombs where kings are laid to rest, and its vaults hold costly vestments, rings, crystal crowns, and other treasures of the Faith. Below ground is a seven-sided audience chamber with the faces of the Seven carved into bare stone walls, with their eyes made of onyx, malachite, yellow moonstone, and jade. Cells of varying sizes for penitents are found in the towers, which have rough stone steps. Individuals accused by the Faith can also be held in chambers below the sept.

Thrice yearly septons from Baelor's Sept create heraldic drawings and illuminations for the White Book of the Kingsguard.

History[ ]

  

Bell tower in the plaza outside the Great Sept, from Game of Thrones Blu-ray

During the reign of King Aegon I Targaryen, a sept for the smallfolk of King's Landing was made in a sunken cog in the Blackwater Rush, while the High Septon in Oldtown sponsored a grander sept atop Visenya's Hill. The Sept of Remembrance was then built on the Hill of Rhaenys to honor the late Queen Rhaenys Targaryen. King Maegor I Targaryen destroyed the Sept of Remembrance during the Faith Militant uprising, however, and replaced it with the Dragonpit.

Inspired by a vision, Baelor I Targaryen ordered the construction of a great sept atop Visenya's Hill; the fate of the grand sept atop the hill from Aegon the Conqueror's reign is unknown. The Great Sept was completed years after the death of Baelor the Blessed, and it replaced the Starry Sept of Oldtown as the center of the Faith in the Seven Kingdoms.

When Dorne join the Seven Kingdoms during the reign of Daeron II Targaryen with the wedding of Maron Martell and Daenerys Targaryen, the king rode with Maron, Prince of Dorne, from the Red Keep to the Great Sept. The two men placed a wreath before the statue of Baelor the Blessed.

Ser Tywin Lannister, the Hand of the King to Aerys II Targaryen, wed his cousin, Joanna Lannister, within the Great Sept in 263 AC. Aerys made a walk of atonement through King's Landing to the Great Sept after the death of his infant son, Prince Jaehaerys, in 274 AC. Aerys's heir, Prince Rhaegar Targaryen, and Princess Elia Martell had a lavish wedding within the Great Sept in 280 AC. The wedding of King Robert I Baratheon and Queen Cersei Lannister was held in the sept after Robert's Rebellion.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Ser Ilyn Payne executes Lord Eddard Stark, by Mike S Miller

With the influx of people in King's Landing before the Hand's tourney, a woman's head is found floating in the rainbow pool of the Great Sept.

The sept's bells ring dolorously for a day and a night after the death of King Robert I Baratheon.

Lord Eddard Stark is taken to the steps of the Great Sept of Baelor where, unbeknownst to him, his daughter Arya Stark and Yoren are amongst the crowd. Eddard makes a false public confession, but the plan goes awry when the new king, Joffrey I Baratheon, declares that Eddard must be executed, to the evident shock of Queen Cersei Lannister, Varys, and the High Septon. Ser Ilyn Payne beheads Eddard on the steps with his own greatsword, Ice. Yoren prevents Arya from seeing her father die. Ser Barristan Selmy, in disguise after being dismissed from the Kingsguard by Joffrey, is also amongst the crowd. After Eddard's death, Barristan enters the sept and thanks the Seven that his white cloak had been stripped by Joffrey.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

The High Septon is outraged at Lord Stark's execution, claiming it profaned the Great Sept with blood. Queen Regent Cersei Lannister often meets with the High Septon at the sept, but after these brief visits she dons a plain cloak and secretly meets with the Kettleblack brothers.

Wisdom Hallyne informs Tyrion Lannister that two hundred jars of wildfire were discovered in a storeroom below the sept in 298 AC. To turn public opinion against Stannis Baratheon, now a supporter of R'hllor, Tyrion spreads the word that Stannis intends to burn the Great Sept.

Thousands jam the Great Sept of Baelor prior to the Battle of the Blackwater. The sept's bells joyously thunder after Stannis's attack on the city is defeated. More than six hundred new knights stand vigil in the Great Sept before walking barefoot to the Red Keep.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

The royal wedding of Joffrey and Margaery Tyrell takes place at midday on the first day of the year 300 AC in the Great Sept of Baelor. After Joffrey dies during his wedding feast, his body is entombed in the Great Sept.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

  

Cersei Lannister and the High Sparrow, by Melissa Findley © Fantasy Flight Games

Ser Jaime Lannister stands vigil for his late father, Lord Tywin, whose body rests under the Great Sept's lofty dome of glass and gold and crystal, upon a stepped marble bier. Tywin's corpse stinks so badly no one is able to enter the sept for days afterward.

Instead of a lavish ceremony in the Great Sept, King Tommen I Baratheon weds Joffrey's widow, Margaery, in the castle sept of the Red Keep.

Sparrows gather in the Great Sept's plaza and beneath its doors, piling up bones and skulls beneath the statue of Baelor the Blessed. After the High Septon dies, the Most Devout come close to selecting Septon Luceon as the successor. Sparrows enter the Great Sept, however, and force the elevation of the so-called High Sparrow. Queen Regent Cersei Lannister meets with the High Sparrow within the sept and agrees to allow the reestablishment of the Faith Militant.

The Faith of the Seven confines Margaery in a cell atop one of the Great Sept's towers, although a crowd gathers outside the sept in protest. Cersei is pleased at her rival's arrest, but the High Sparrow also has the queen regent imprisoned in a tower cell.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

  

Cersei Lannister disrobes on the steps of the Great Sept, by  Magali Villeneuve © Fantasy Flight Games

The High Sparrow uses the audience chamber as his sanctum.

Tyrion Lannister thinks that the Temple of the Lord of Light in Volantis is three times the size of the Great Sept of Baelor.

Poor Fellows surround the Great Sept, beneath which are imprisoned Osney Kettleblack and the Blue Bard. Cersei visits the High Sparrow in his sanctum and confesses to some of her accused crimes. After being moved to a larger tower cell, she meets with her uncle, Ser Kevan Lannister. Smallfolk and Poor Fellows crowd the plaza during Cersei's walk of atonement from the Great Sept to the Red Keep.
