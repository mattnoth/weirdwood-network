History[ ]

In 19 AC, King Aegon I Targaryen decreed that new city walls with seven gates, each with a massive gatehouse and defensive towers, be built to protect King's Landing. Grand Maester Gawen and Ser Osmund Strong, Hand of the King, headed the construction, which took place between 20–26 AC.

During the reign of Maegor I Targaryen, the king had his own wife and queen, Alys Harroway, tortured to death  and executed. Following her death, Alys's body was cut into seven parts and mounted on spikes above each of the seven gates of the city, one of which was above the Old Gate.

During the Dance of the Dragons, Lord Staunton's head was taken to the capital after the battle at Rook's Rest and mounted above the Old Gate. The following year, a massive riot broke out in the city. The captains of the Old Gate and Dragon Gate managed to sally out and restore some semblance of order to the streets north and east of Rhaenys's Hill. 

In the regency of Aegon III, the King's Hand, Tyland Lannister commanded the city gates to be strengthened so they could better resist attacks from within the city walls.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

After the arrest of Eddard Stark, his daughter Arya finds that the Dragon Gate, the Lion Gate, and the Old Gate are closed and barred.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Varys reports that the Antler Men have armed several hundred followers and plan to have the Old Gate seized during the upcoming Battle of the Blackwater. 

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Ser Jaime Lannister is sent by his sister, Queen Regent Cersei Lannister, to inspect each of the city gates.
