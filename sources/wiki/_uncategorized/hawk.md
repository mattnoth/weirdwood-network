Culture[ ]

  

Cragorn's hawk tattoo, by Yoann Boissonnet © Fantasy Flight Games

Known for their speed and eyesight, captive hawks and falcons can be used for hunting, a popular activity for noblemen and noblewomen. Willas Tyrell is said to have the finest hawks in the Seven Kingdoms, and House Celtigar is believed have magnificent hawks at Claw Isle. Merchants can sell hawks at markets.

Maesters use ravens to send messages as they are better able than doves or pigeons to defend themselves from hawk attacks, but many ravens are still brought down by the birds of prey.

The arms of House Fowler depict a hooded hawk, and Lord Franklyn Fowler is called the Old Hawk. Four hawks' heads are included in the arms of House Terrick.

Sphinxes are said to have the wings of a hawk.

Skinchangers who have entered the minds of hawks can lose their connection to the earth and instead only want to fly.

Cragorn has a great hawk tattooed on his chest.

History[ ]

There are reports that Aegon Targaryen and his sister Visenya hawked at the Arbor with Lord Redwyne prior to Aegon's Conquest. After he became king, many lords of the Seven Kingdoms tried to impress Aegon I with feasting, balls, hunting, and hawking during his royal progresses.

Saera Targaryen was able to persuade her father, King Jaehaerys I Targaryen, to give her a variety of animals, including a hawk. The princess often hunted and hawked with her friends, Perianne Moore and Alys Turnberry.

Daemon Targaryen hawked with his niece, Rhaenyra Targaryen. Daemon's wife, Lady Rhea Royce, died after falling from her horse while hawking.

Prior to the Maiden's Day Ball, Lucinda Penrose was attacked by outlaws while hawking along Blackwater Bay, and her bird was killed.

Lord Willam Stackspear loved to hawk.

"The Hawk" was the name for one of three rebels during the reign of King Aegon V Targaryen.

Focusing his attention on the sky, Lord Luthor Tyrell rode off a cliff to his death while hawking.

Young Stannis Baratheon cared for a goshawk he named Proudwing, but the bird was never the equal of Thunderclap, the gyrfalcon of his elder brother, Robert. After becoming king, Robert I prefers to hunt and hawk and leave the tedium of governing to Lord Jon Arryn, his Hand of the King. Robert spent much of his time hawking during a visit to Greenstone.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Daenerys Targaryen watches a hawk circling above the Dothraki sea in search of prey.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Patrek Mallister and Theon Greyjoy share a fondness for hawking.

Arya Stark observes a hawk lazily flying over the Gods Eye river.

Renly Baratheon keeps a pair of red-tailed hunting hawks in his pavilion at Bitterbridge.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Sansa Stark goes hawking in the kingswood with Margaery Tyrell.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Arianne Martell spies a hawk when her party approaches the Greenblood.

Ser Osmund Kettleblack informs Queen Regent Cersei Lannister that Queen Margaery often goes hawking with Janna Fossoway and Meredyth Crane.

While bringing the Mountain's skull to Sunspear, Ser Balon Swann is delayed by House Wyl with eight days of hunting and hawking.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Some of the Brazen Beasts wear hawk masks in Meereen.
