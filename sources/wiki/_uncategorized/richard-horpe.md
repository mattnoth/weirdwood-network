Appearance and Character[ ]

Ser Richard is lean, dark-haired, and hard-eyed. His ravaged face is marred by pockmarks and old scars; according to a semi-canon source this is because of the pox.

Richard enjoys battle and killing. He is one of the queen's men and is high in the councils of Stannis Baratheon, who considers the knight to be ambitious.

Richard's quilted doublet and faded surcoat display the three death's-head moths of House Horpe. He armors himself in plate and mail.

History[ ]

Richard wanted to join the Kingsguard when he was a squire, but King Robert I Baratheon chose another knight after Queen Cersei Lannister objected to him.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

When the Battle of the Blackwater turns against Stannis Baratheon, Richard and Ser Justin Massey convince Stannis to retreat back to Dragonstone.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

During the battle beneath the Wall, the wildling Dormund is killed by a knight with moths on his shield, possibly Richard.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

One of the queen's men, Richard is part of Stannis's court at Castle Black.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

  

Richard Horpe by Cindy Kinnard©

Still among Stannis's knights and sworn swords, Richard sits high in his councils. Richard and Ser Justin Massey are sent south from Castle Black, their departure noted by Dywen. Jon Snow, the new Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, deduces the so-called wrong-way rangers are acting as envoys for Stannis.

Stannis believes that Justin and Rich both aspire to be named Lord of Winterfell for their service, but the king plans to give the title to Arnolf Karstark. During Stannis's war council, Richard advocates for attacking the Dreadfort. Jon dissuades Stannis, however, and the king instead marches to court the northern mountain clans.

Stannis is victorious in the fight by Deepwood Motte, and Richard serves as his second-in-command during the march on Winterfell. Richard incorrectly believes the snowstorm which slows them down will break soon. He later concludes that the weakest in Stannis's host are beyond saving. The army stops at a crofters' village in the wolfswood, so Richard tasks his men with fishing in two nearby lakes.

When Melisandre prophesies a girl coming to the Wall, Jon Snow guesses she is Arya Stark and fears that Stannis would want to marry her to Richard, Justin, or Ser Godry Farring. The girl turns out to be Alys Karstark, however, and Jon arranges her marriage to Sigorn of the Thenns.

Richard is present when four Peasebury men are burned at the village for cannibalism. With Stannis's men slowed by the blizzard, Richard insists they must press on to attack Winterfell. He questions Justin's courage and dedication to R'hllor.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

Richard is present, along with Ser Godry Farring and Clayton Suggs, when Stannis interrogates Maester Tybald about his loyalties. Richard is then put in charge of disarming Arnolf Karstark's sleeping men, while Stannis breaks his fast with Arnolf, his son Arthor, and grandsons. Richard later reports that most of the Karstarks yielded, although a few were killed after resisting.

Richard and other queen's men want Stannis to sacrifice Theon Greyjoy to the Lord of Light, although Stannis is skeptical of Richard's sincerity.
