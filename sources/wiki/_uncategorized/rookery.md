## Full Text

Ravens at Oldtown, by Carolina Eade © Fantasy Flight Games

A rookery is a breeding ground for corvids such as crows, ravens, and rooks, as well as for marine mammals such as seals, narwhals, walruses, and sea lions.

In Westeros, maesters of the Citadel keep their trained ravens in a castle rookery, so ravenry is occasionally used as the terminology instead.

Maesters[ ]

Maesters of castles in the Seven Kingdoms usually tend to their own flock of ravens. When a master needs to send a message to another castle, he will fetch a raven from his rookery that was trained to fly to that castle. A maester's chambers are usually located near a castle's rookery.

History[ ]

Grand Maester Benifer was in his rookery when Princess Aerea Targaryen and Balerion returned to King's Landing in 56 AC.

Princess Viserra Targaryen liked to manipulate the boys who courted her, playing them off one against the other. To win her favor for a joust, she would send them on foolish quests, such as setting free all the ravens in the Red Keep rookery.

During the fall of Dragonstone, Ser Marston Waters seized the rookery to prevent Grand Maester Gerardys from sending ravens.

The cat Balerion has been known to attack ravens in the Red Keep rookery.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Maester Aemon feeds the ravens in the rookery at Castle Black, by Audrey Hotte © Fantasy Flight Games

During the sack of Winterfell, the maester's turret and the bridge connecting the Bell Tower to the rookery are destroyed. The maester's ravens are loosed, and eat the flesh of the slain, such as Poxy Tym.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Cleaning the rookery is among Chett's duties at Castle Black.

Sansa Stark builds a replica of Winterfell out of snow at the Eyrie. Petyr Baelish helps her make the bridge in between the rookery and the Bell Tower.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

After the death of Lady Lysa Arryn, ravens come and go from the Eyrie's rookery.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

During the taking of Griffin's Roost by the Golden Company, the castle's maester is thrown from the rookery. Later, Haldon Halfmaester is commanded by Lord Jon Connington to take charge of the rookery in his stead.
