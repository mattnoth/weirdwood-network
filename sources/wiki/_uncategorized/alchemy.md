Disciplines[ ]

The Alchemists' Guild of King's Landing in Westeros claim that in the past they were able to transmute metals, along with other powers. In recent centuries their power has waned, and they focus on creating the incendiary substance known as wildfire, which burns much hotter than pitch and cannot be extinguished by water. These pyromancers claim that wildfire's power is magical in nature, though the exact ingredients are a closely guarded secret.

The alchemists of Lys are experts are creating poisons, such as the strangler.

Alchemists, including practitioners of black alchemy, reside in Asshai.

History[ ]

  

Guildhall of the Alchemists, by Jonny Klein © Fantasy Flight Games

During the Age of Heroes, Prince Peremore the Twisted, the second son of King Uthor of the High Tower, invited numerous scholars, including wise men, teachers, priests, healers, singers, wizards, sorcerers, and alchemists to Oldtown. After Peremore's death, his brother, King Urrigon Hightower, granted land alongside the Honeywine to "Peremore's pets", who developed the tract into the Citadel of the maesters.

King Aenys I Targaryen dabbled in alchemy, astronomy, and astrology.

Queen Tyanna of the Tower, wife of Maegor I Targaryen, was said to practice alchemy and sorcery.

Archmaester Vaegon, born to House Targaryen, was devoted to alchemy, astronomy, mathematics, and other arcane arts.

King Aerys II Targaryen included the Alchemists' Guild of King's Landing in his wildfire plot.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

The Alchemists' Guild is commissioned to provide ten thousand jars of wildfire for the defense of King's Landing during the War of the Five Kings.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

In Oldtown, the novice Pate meets an alchemist who claims to be able to change iron into gold.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Melisandre considers alchemists and pyromancers to be practitioners of feeble tricks.
