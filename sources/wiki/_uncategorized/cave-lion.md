Distribution[ ]

Cave lions once inhabited Westeros. 

History[ ]

The greenseers of the children of the forest are said to have called upon cave lions to fight on their behalf against the encroaching First Men. 

Recent events[ ]

Jaime Lannister dreams of being trapped beneath Casterly Rock alongside Brienne Tarth. During it, she asks if a cave lion or other large wildlife is kept there, but Jaime tells her there is only doom.

Quotes[ ]

The greenseers employed their arts, and tales say that they could call the beasts of marsh, forest, and air to fight on
their behalf: direwolves and monstrous snowbears, cave lions and eagles, mammoths and serpents, and more. But the First Men proved too powerful. 

—Yandel in The World of Ice & Fire

Brienne: A cave lion? Direwolves? Some bear? Tell me, Jaime. What lives here? What lives in the darkness?

Jaime: Doom. Only doom.

—Brienne Tarth to Jaime Lannister in his dream

Behind the scenes[ ]

The cave lion of the A Song of Ice and Fire novels appears to be based on one of the species cave lion: Panthera spelaea, Panthera atrox or Panthera fossilis.
