Geography[ ]

Greywater Watch is roughly located in an eastern headwater of the Green Fork in the swamps of the Neck, southwest of Moat Cailin. It is a castle built upon a crannog, one of the man-made floating islands of the swamps, and it does not stay in the same place, making it impossible for ravens or enemies to find. According to Meera Reed, there is no maester there, nor any knights or masters-at-arms.

History[ ]

Ironborn and Andal warriors as well as Freys have attempted to conquer Greywater Watch, but none have been able to find it. Many of the invaders rode into bogs and sank because of their armor.

Lord Eddard Stark has sent letters to his friend, Lord Howland Reed, at Greywater over the years, but Bran Stark cannot recall any crannogmen visiting Winterfell in response.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Robb Stark sends word to Lord Howland Reed at Greywater Watch to have the crannogmen defend the Neck if Lord Tywin Lannister marches up the causeway.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

At Greywater, Jojen Reed dreams of black waves of salt water overcoming Winterfell. The youth also has a green dream of a winged wolf, so Howland sends his children to Winterfell to aid Bran Stark.

Jojen and his older sister, Meera Reed, attend the harvest feast at Winterfell and pledge the faith of Greywater.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

Greywater Watch may be a reference to George R. R. Martin's novelette Men of Greywater Station, as well as to Diana Wynn Jones's novel Howl's Moving Castle.
