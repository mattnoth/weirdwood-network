Layout[ ]

Half the size of Lord Hewett's Town, Lordsport is located near a wooded point with pine-clad bluffs. Lordsport provides safe anchor for those who wish to reach the castle of Pyke, seat of House Greyjoy. Otter Gimpknee's inn is located near the landing.

The town is known for its forges and metalworkers, who produce excellent swords, axes, ringmail, and plate. Sigrin is a prominent shipwright in Lordsport.

The seat of House Botley is a small square stone keep atop a hill overlooking the town; the banners of the Botleys droop from the castle's squat corner towers.

History[ ]

During the reign of House Hoare as Kings of the Iron Islands, ships from Lordsport began to trade at Lannisport, Oldtown, and the Free Cities.

Corlys Velaryon sailed to Oldtown, Lannisport, and Lordsport when he was a young man.

During Greyjoy's Rebellion, the timber-and-wattle castle of House Botley and the village of Lordsport were razed by the forces of King Robert I Baratheon before the battle at Pyke. After the war, Lordsport was rebuilt by its smallfolk, who used the stones from the burned remnants and cut new thatch for their roofs. Otter Gimpknee's inn at the harbor doubled in size. The sept was not rebuilt, however, as the ironmen prefer the Drowned God over the Faith of the Seven, the religion of the victorious Robert. Lord Sawane Botley rebuilt his stronghold in stone.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Returning to the Iron Islands for the first time since Greyjoy's Rebellion, Theon Greyjoy sails on the Myraham from Seagard to Lordsport. Theon, the heir of Balon Greyjoy, Lord of the Iron Islands, is displeased that the merchants of Lordsport do not recognize him. Aeron Damphair escorts his nephew from Lordsport to Pyke.

Lord Balon musters ironborn longships to Lordsport in preparation for his invasion of the north during the War of the Five Kings. Balon prevents merchant vessels like the Myraham from leaving the harbor and informing the green lands of the gathering of ironborn ships.

Theon acquires a horse, Smiler, from Sawane Botley, Lord of Lordsport, and takes on the mute Wex Pyke, the bastard of Sawane's half-brother Sargon, as his squire. Theon sees his newly-built longship, later named Sea Bitch, on the strand of Lordsport. Asha Greyjoy, calling herself "Esgred" and claiming to be the daughter of Ambrode and wife of Sigrin, escorts her unknowing brother from the town to Pyke, where she reveals her true identity.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

The captain of the Myraham reports that Euron Greyjoy sailed into Lordsport's harbor the day after the death of his brother Balon, King of the Isles and the North. Euron claimed the Seastone Chair at Pyke and drowned Lord Botley in a cask of seawater when he objected.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

It is reported that Euron had Sawane killed because he recognized Balon's heir to be Theon, not Euron. Ralf of Lordsport comes from the town, whose new lord is Germund Botley. Asha, a claimant to the Seastone Chair, intends to restore Lordsport to Sawane's son and heir, her childhood friend Tristifer Botley.
