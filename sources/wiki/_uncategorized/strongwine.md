History[ ]

When construction ended on the Red Keep in 45 AC, King Maegor I Targaryen sent wagonloads of strongwine and sweetmeats to the castle's builders and workmen. After three days of celebration, Maegor the Cruel had his knights kill the workers so that the secrets of the Red Keep would never be revealed.

Prince Aegon the Elder was fond of swilling ale and strongwine. As King Aegon II, he began to drown his fears in strongwine during the Dance of the Dragons. 

After the Battle of the Honeywine, Lord Ormund Hightower feasted Prince Daeron Targaryen on aurochs and strongwine, and knighted him as Ser Daeron the Daring.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

King Robert I Baratheon, drunk from multiple skins of wine, is mortally wounded while hunting a boar in the kingswood.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Queen Regent Cersei Lannister informs her brother Tyrion that Robert's squire, Lancel Lannister, had secretly given strongwine to the king during his hunt. Lancel later defends himself to Tyrion, telling his cousin that he had been given the strongwine by the queen.

Pears poached in strongwine are served in the harvest feast at Winterfell.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Ser Jaime Lannister avoids milk of the poppy and instead gulps strongwine while Qyburn treats the stump of his right arm.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Doran Martell, Prince of Dorne, enjoys a cup of sweet, heavy strongwine at the Water Gardens.

Cersei drinks a cup of strongwine after meeting with Qyburn.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Tyrion drinks strongwine from the Arbor while staying at Illyrio's manse in Pentos.

Dornish strongwine is served at the feast welcoming Ser Balon Swann to Sunspear.

Quotes[ ]

Cersei: He did that himself. All we did was help. When Lancel saw that Robert was going after boar, he gave him strongwine. His favorite sour red, but fortified, three times as potent as he was used to. The great stinking fool loved it. He could have stopped swilling it down anytime he cared to, but no, he drained one skin and told Lancel to fetch another. The boar did the rest.
