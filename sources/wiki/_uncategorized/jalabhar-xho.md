Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Jalabhar Xho

Jalabhar has the dark skin and extravagant feather cape typical of Summer Islanders. The feathers of his own cape are green and scarlet. Jalabhar speaks both the Common Tongue and his native Summer Tongue. 

Queen Cersei Lannister considers Jalabhar to be little more than a highborn beggar, and Prince Quentyn Martell thinks that Jalabhar is seen as an amusing curiosity by the court of King Robert I Baratheon.

History[ ]

Jalabhar Xho is the exiled Prince of the Red Flower Vale, likely exiled for losing a highly ritualized war as per the tradition of the Summer Isles. Following his exile, Jalabhar came to King's Landing in the Seven Kingdoms to request gold and swords from King Robert I Baratheon to help him regain the Red Flower Vale. Robert always entertained the notion of conquering the Summer Isles, but always put off Jalabhar's request for help. However, Robert never firmly declined him, telling him "Next year" instead of "No".

While Jalabhar continues to occasionally petition the Iron Throne to win back his seat, he has become a noted courtier at King's Landing. Having no other place to seek refuge, the exiled prince is one of the most colorful figures at court, alongside Thoros of Myr.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Jalabhar Xho, by Roman Papsuev ©

Jalabhar attends the Hand's tourney in King's Landing. Jeyne Poole admits to Sansa Stark that she is frightened by his looks. In the final round of the archery competition, Jalabhar and Ser Balon Swann get outshot by the commoner Anguy at a hundred paces.

After the death of King Robert I Baratheon, Jalabhar is among the score of nobles who attend King Joffrey I Baratheon's first court session in the Red Keep.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Jalabhar attends the tourney on King Joffrey's name day in King's Landing, one of the few nobles to do so as the War of the Five Kings has broken out. 

The prince is part of the procession that sees Princess Myrcella Baratheon off to Dorne. While returning to the Red Keep, the group is attacked by smallfolk as a riot breaks out in King's Landing. After the group narrowly returns to safety, Jalabhar believes he saw Ser Preston Greenfield of the Kingsguard ride back to futilely aid the High Septon, though he was not certain.

Following the defeat of Stannis Baratheon at the Battle of the Blackwater, Jalabhar dresses splendidly in feathers for the following court session.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Arya Stark remembers Jalabhar as the most colorful figure at court, alongside the red priest Thoros of Myr.

Jalabhar attends the wedding of Sansa Stark and Tyrion Lannister. After the ceremony, Jalabhar dances with Meredyth Crane, looking gorgeous in his feathered finery. He also dances with Sansa, telling her something in the Summer Tongue that she does not understand. 

As part of Lord Tywin Lannister's orders, Jalabhar is part of the group that accompanies Tyrion to meet the delegation from Dorne, ensuring the Prince of Dorne is greeted by more notables aside from Tyrion. Instead of Doran Martell, however, the the delegation is led by Oberyn Martell.

Jalabhar is a guest at the wedding of King Joffrey and Queen Margaery Tyrell, gifting Joffrey a great bow of golden wood and a quiver of arrows fletched with green and scarlet feathers. After the ceremony, Jalabhar is among the nobles enjoying the unseasonable heat in the courtyard, where Sansa asks him about wedding customs in the Summer Isles.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

  

Jalabhar Xho and Margaery Tyrell, with her cousins Megga, Alla, and Elinor. By Amuelia ©

Jalabhar visits Queen Margaery often, teaching her and her handmaids the Summer Tongue, and attaches himself to her entourage. He petitions the Iron Throne for aid in retaking his seat, the Red Flower Vale, but is put off by Queen Regent Cersei Lannister.

The Blue Bard is tortured and forced to list those he claims to have been Queen Margaery's secret lovers, with Jalabhar among them. Cersei has an arrest warrant signed for Jalabhar as part of her plans to remove Margaery, as well as getting rid of the prince, whom she deems to be an annoying beggar.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Jalabhar is held with the rest of Margaery's falsely-accused lovers in a dungeon, under the charge of Qyburn.
