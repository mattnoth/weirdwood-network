Range[ ]

  

Roaming wolfpack, by Sebastian Rodriguez © Fantasy Flight Games

Wolves can be found in the forests of Ib, the Forest of Qohor, and the forests of Westeros, such as the wolfswood (named for the great many wolves one can hear howling in the night), the White Wood, and on Bear Island. Wolves live in the red waste, and red wolves can also be found in Essos.

Behavior[ ]

Wolves live and hunt together in groups called packs. Due to the size and strength difference, a direwolf can establish dominance over a pack of common wolves. Wolf fur can be red, brown, or grey.

A warg is a skinchanger that specializes in wolves or dogs. One can form a magical bond with common wolves as well as direwolves.

Wolves often eat carrion.

Culture[ ]

  

The sigil of House Cassel depicts ten white wolves' heads.

The sigil of House Cassel depicts ten white wolf heads. Northmen can be called wolves because of the direwolf in the sigil of House Stark, and the Wolf Pack was founded by northmen after the Dance of the Dragons. "Wolves in the Hills" is a popular song in the Northern mountains. The Wolf's Den was raised by King Jon Stark at the mouth of the White Knife. Starks who show a certain wildness have been described as having the "wolf blood". Due to their sigil, several historical Starks received wolf-based nicknames:

King Rickard Stark, the Laughing Wolf

King Theon Stark, the Hungry Wolf

Lord Cregan Stark, the Wolf of the North and the Wolf of Winterfell

Rodrik Stark, the Wandering Wolf

In Westeros, the blackest part of night is referred to as the hour of the wolf.

Wolfsbane is a poison.

Ironborn raiders have been called the "wolves of the sea" by men of the westerlands and riverlands.

History[ ]

Pre-Conquest[ ]

In antiquity, wolves made their lairs within the Rock.

According to songs and legend, the children of the forest controlled armies of wolves and sent them against Andal invaders.

King Jon Stark raised the Wolf's Den to defend the mouth of the White Knife from raiders from the sea.

The War of the Wolves was a conflict between a King of Winter from House Stark and Gaven Greywolf.

Gorm the Wolf was a chieftain of the free folk who ruled Hardhome.

Targaryen Era[ ]

  

Varamyr, by Elena María Vacas ©

The wife of Lord Alaric Stark killed two wolves with her axe in her youth and used their skins to sew a cloak.

Ser Corlys Velaryon captained the Ice Wolf when he sailed the Shivering Sea.

During the Dance of the Dragons, Lord Roderick Dustin led a host of two thousand northmen who called themselves the Winter Wolves. At the end of the war, Lord Cregan Stark took control of King's Landing during a period remembered as the Hour of the Wolf. In the aftermath of the conflict, a sellsword company consisting exclusively of northmen, called the Wolf Pack, was founded by Hallis Hornwood and Timotty Snow.

Sandoq the Shadow was a veteran pit fighter in Meereen, who fought against men and animals, including wolves.

Varamyr, a skinchanger, was able to gain control of wolves. His master Haggon, a warg, died and was going to have a second life in his wolf Greyskin, but Varamyr resentfully forced him out and took Greyskin for himself. Varamyr later bonded with three wolves, One Eye, the head of the pack, Sly, the female, and Stalker, the "tail".

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

When Robb Stark rides to war, his guards insist upon calling him the Young Wolf. Robb is proclaimed King in the North after the Battle of the Camps.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

The direwolf Nymeria takes control over a pack of common wolves. Lord William Mooton and his sons track the pack to their lair, but the Mootons are forced to flee.

After the fall of Harrenhal to Lord Roose Bolton and the Brave Companions, Septon Utt's men are attacked by bold wolves. Roose is irritated by the howling of the wolf pack, so goes hunting and manages to kill nine wolves. He orders his men to sew the skins of the adults into a blanket for his bed, and to make gloves with the softer fur of the pups.

Rymund the Rhymer writes a song called "Wolf in the Night" about Robb's victory at the Battle of Oxcross.

Jojen Reed tells Bran Stark he had a green dream of a winged wolf, which he claims is Bran.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Jojen and his sister Meera tell Bran about the tourney at Harrenhal and the Knight of the Laughing Tree. The Reeds refer to a wild wolf, a quiet wolf, a she-wolf, and a wolf pup.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Nymeria's pack has grown to a great pack of hundreds of wolves who prowls the Trident. Some are still afraid of men, but others have become more aggressive under the leadership of the female direwolf and have killed horses and men.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Varamyr's wolves One Eye, Stalker, and Sly chase down and kill a party of wildlings for food. Varamyr considers which of his animals he will enter into when he dies, and thinks the younger Stalker might suit him, but prefers One Eye. Varamyr dies, and enters One Eye for his second life. 

Bran Stark's direwolf Summer encounters Varamyr's wolf pack eating the remains of the dead Night's Watch deserters. Summer sees that One Eye is not afraid of him even though he is twice the wolf's size. He realizes that One Eye has a warg within him, and they fight. After defeating One Eye and making him submit, Summer makes Stalker and Sly submit as well, and takes over leadership of the pack.

Near the cave of the last greenseer, Summer and his wolf pack find a wight snow bear, and tear it to pieces and devour it, even though the rotten meat still moves while they eat it.
