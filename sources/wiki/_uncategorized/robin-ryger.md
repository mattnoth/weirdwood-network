Appearance and Character[ ]

Robin is a stocky man with a bald head, bushy grey eyebrows, and brawny arms. Like Lord Hoster Tully, Ser Desmond Grell, and Utherydes Wayn, he is an old man.

Robin was known as a tenacious fighter in his prime.

Robin wears mail under his white surcoat, which is decorated with a weeping willow embroidered in pale green. He fastens his cloak with a silver trout.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Having served years as Knight of the Gate, Ser Brynden Tully speaks with Robin when he returns to Riverrun.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Ser Robin brings Ser Cleos Frey to the Great Hall of Riverrun to hear Robb Stark's terms for his release and for peace between the King in the North and King Joffrey I Baratheon. Robb orders Robin to make sure that Cleos has a good meal and clean clothing before the envoy departs for King's Landing.

Robin is introduced to Brienne Tarth when she and Catelyn return from Storm's End. Robin asks about the manner of Renly Baratheon's death.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Robin is sent by Ser Desmond Grell to re-capture Ser Jaime Lannister after Catelyn Stark sets him free, chasing after the Kingslayer in a river galley. After Robin refuses Jaime's offer of single combat, Brienne of Tarth, Jaime's protector, sinks Robin's ship with a rock in the Red Fork. Robin and his men walk back to Riverrun footweary and drenched.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

After the surrender of Riverrun, Robin and Ser Desmond Grell ask to join the Night's Watch. Both are to be escorted to Maidenpool by a dozen of the Mountain's men under the command of Raff the Sweetling.

Robin: Say what you will, Kingslayer, but say it quickly.

Jaime: I know a better way to settle this—single combat. You and I.

Robin: I was not born this morning, Lannister.

Jaime: No, but you're like to die this afternoon. I'll fight you in chains. What could you fear?

—Robin and Jaime Lannister
