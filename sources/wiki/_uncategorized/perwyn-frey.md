Character[ ]

According to Ser Daven Lannister, Perwyn is a decent man.

History[ ]

Ser Perwyn rode in the tourney on Prince Joffrey's name day in 297 AC.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Ser Perwyn attends the Hand's tourney in King's Landing with five other members of House Frey.

During Catelyn Stark's parley with Lord Walder Frey within the Twins, Perwyn is left outside in the company of Robb Stark to ensure his mother's safety.

Five Freys serve in Robb's personal guard during the battle in the Whispering Wood.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Perwyn is a member of the party that escorts Catelyn as she travels to treat with Renly Baratheon at Bitterbridge and Stannis Baratheon at Storm's End. When they return to Riverrun after Renly's death, Perwyn is informed of Robb's victories in the westerlands by his half brother, Martyn Rivers. Perwyn jests when he sees hanged plotters.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

While under house arrest for freeing Ser Jaime Lannister, Catelyn watches Perwyn and Martyn, who fought in the Battle of the Fords, unexpectedly leave Riverrun. Catelyn later learns the Freys left because of Robb's marriage to Jeyne Westerling instead of one of Walder's daughters.

Catelyn is surprised that Perwyn is not present at the Twins for the wedding of his sister, Roslin Frey, to Catelyn's brother, Lord Edmure Tully. Lothar Frey explains that Perwyn is away and delayed by the flooding of the Trident. As the Red Wedding begins, Catelyn thinks of the absences of Perwyn, Olyvar, and Alesander Frey, as well as Roslin's crying, as warnings of House Frey's betrayal.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Perwyn is present at the siege of Riverrun, where he tells Ser Daven Lannister that his pregnant sister Roslin is in love with Edmure and is praying for a girl. Unlike most of the Freys at the siege, Daven approves of Perwyn.
