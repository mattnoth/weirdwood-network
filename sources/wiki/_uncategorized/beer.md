Varieties[ ]

  

The inn at the crossroads offers a popular beer, by Marc Simonetti © Fantasy Flight Games

Mugs of strong black beer and autumn beer are served at Winterfell, and a brewhouse near the Wolf's Den in White Harbor is famed for its thick stout sometimes exported to Braavos and the Port of Ibben. Sweet beer is among the beverages served at the Red Keep. The inn at the crossroads offers thick brown ale, and nut-brown ale is served at Riverrun and Ashford. Brown beer is also available at Braavos and Sisterton. Barley beer is an offering in the riverlands.

Brown ale and yellow small beer is available to the Night's Watch. Beyond the Wall, Craster drinks thin yellow beer. Dothraki drink pepper beer, while pale ales are available in Pentos. Fruit beers and bittersweet black ales are offered in Volantis. Beer halls offering strong black beer line the shores of the Noyne in Norvos. Sour ale is sometimes consumed by travelers.

Beer is often stored in casks and kegs, and smaller quantities can be transported in skins. Beer is often served in cups, flagons, horns, mugs, and tankards. Some say the alehouse at Sherrer serves the finest ale south of the Neck, and fine brown ale is also brewed at an inn by the Gods Eye. The Night's Watch sometimes store beer in the icy storerooms of the Wall. Some castles, such as Harrenhal the Nightfort, and Riverrun, have brewhouses. Septries such as the Quiet Isle often have brewhouses.

History[ ]

The big-bellied Ser Willam Stafford was called "the Keg o' Ale" by smallfolk.

Queen Alysanne Targaryen praised a strong northern ale when she visited White Harbor in 58 AC. Lothor Burley served stout and mead when the queen arrived at Castle Black.

After the fall of King's Landing to Rhaenyra Targaryen amid the Dance of the Dragons, Lord Bartimos Celtigar doubled taxes on wine and ale to raise funds for the queen. Tom Tangletongue gave ale to the crew of Nessaria while gathering information about Sunfyre. During the Moon of the Three Kings, Gaemon Palehair decreed that the poor should receive bread and beer during famine. Tom Tanglebeard was drowned in a cask of ale when King Aegon II Targaryen was assassinated. Lord Torrhen Manderly preferred thick northern ale over the beer served at King's Landing.

"A Cask of Ale" is a favorite song of King Robert I Baratheon.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

A knight drinking a mug of ale, by Tomasz Jedruszek © Fantasy Flight Games

During the Hand's tourney, King Robert I Baratheon drinks strong black beer from a polished horn. 

Jeor Mormont, Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, believes that drinking lemon with his beer keeps his teeth strong.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Chilled autumn ale is served during the harvest feast at Winterfell.

Shagga dislikes the weaker ale served in King's Landing.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Sybell Spicer makes a posset of herbs, milk, and ale for her daughter, Jeyne Westerling.

Hobb cooks mutton in a broth of ale and onions at Castle Black.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Lord Wyman Manderly supplies black stout and yellow beer for the wedding of Ramsay Bolton to "Arya Stark" at Winterfell.

During the siege of Moat Cailin, the ironborn garrison consume ale because water in the nearby Neck is unsafe to drink.
