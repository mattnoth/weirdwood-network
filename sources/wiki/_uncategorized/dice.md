Culture[ ]

Dicing is a regular pastime among common people and nobles alike. It is often accompanied with drinking and gambling.

Expressions such as "rolling the dice", "tossing the dice" and "throwing the dice" designate someone making a risky attempt to do or achieve something.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

During his stay at the Wall, Tyrion Lannister spends his evening drinking and dicing with the high officers of the Night's Watch.

Tyrion wins a shadowskin cloak playing dice against the singer Marillion.

Arya Stark sees two guardsmen dicing while a third walked rounds around the Wind Witch preventing her to board the ship to escape King's Landing.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Wex Pyke is a good dice player and wins good money to the astonishment of Theon Greyjoy.

Members of Rattleshirt's band play dice against each other to win the gear of the dead Qhorin Halfhand.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Lord Randyll Tarly, who is dispensing justice in Maidenpool in place of Lord William Mooton, hears a sailor and one of Lord Mooton's bowman after a dice game turned sour. He judges the bowman guilty of cheating using a loaded dice. He condemns him to lose a little finger and to be stabbed in the palm of his hand with a nail.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Sellswords of the Windblown love to play dice against Archibald Yronwood as he is a poor player betting fearlessly but without great results.

According to Big Walder, his late cousin Little Walder owed silver coins to a White Harbor knight from a game of dice and implies Manderly men have killed him for it.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

Young John Mudd is a good dice player.

Euron Greyjoy lets his men trow dice over which ships will bind holy men to their prows before the battle against the Redwyne fleet.
