Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Valarr Targaryen

Valarr looked much like his father Baelor, though he was shorter, slimmer, and handsomer. The Young Prince had brown hair, with a streak of silver-gold running through it. He had blue eyes. Unlike his father who was renowned as one of the most powerful warriors in the realm, Valarr's jousting skills were modest at best.

Valarr wore black armor and carried a black lance during tourneys. His horse was also black, as well as its trappings. Atop his black helm was the three-headed dragon of House Targaryen, enameled in a rich red. Valarr's pavilion of black silk had a line of pointed scarlet pennons hanging from its roof.

After his death, Valarr was remembered as being bold, brave, and full of promise.

History[ ]

  

Valarr by Jaenarra ©

Prince Valarr was born the eldest son of Prince Baelor Targaryen, and his wife, Lady Jena Dondarrion. He had one younger brother, Prince Matarys. At some point, Valarr was married to Kiera of Tyrosh. They had sons, but all were stillborn.

Ashford Tourney[ ]

In 209 AC, Valarr was one of the original champions for Lord Ashford's daughter at the start of the tourney at Ashford Meadow. However, his status as a Targaryen prince, grandson of King Daeron II Targaryen, and future heir to the Iron Throne, prevented him from facing dangerous opponents. He won at least nine jousts against "undistinguished foes", including Ser Abelar Hightower and Lord Gawen Swann, and remained a champion after the first day's jousting.

During Ser Duncan the Tall's trial of seven, Valarr's father, Prince Baelor Breakspear, took part as the seventh knight on Dunk's side. However, as Baelor had not expected to fight in the tourney, he had not brought any armor to Ashford with him; therefore he borrowed Valarr's armor. Thus when Baelor first appeared on the field, Dunk briefly believed Valarr was coming to champion him.

Baelor died in the trial, and Valarr stood vigil by his body at the funeral. When Dunk tried to offer his sympathies, Valarr dismissed him.

Death and legacy[ ]

Valarr died in 209 AC from the Great Spring Sickness, as did his younger brother Matarys, and their grandfather, King Daeron II Targaryen. He was likely a teenager at the time of his death.

In 211 AC, Duncan had a nightmare where the dead Valarr berated him with the same words he had used at Baelor's funeral.

In 211 AC, Duncan and Prince Aegon Targaryen heard a hunchbacked septon in Stoney Sept preach that the Hand of the King, Brynden Rivers, had killed Valarr's sons in their mother's womb, and suggest that Brynden was responsible for Valarr's death. The septon was later beheaded for speaking treason.

Recent events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

While explaining the lineage of Maester Aemon to Jon Snow, Lord Commander Jeor Mormont says that Prince Baelor's two sons soon followed their father to the grave in the Great Spring Sickness.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Discussing both their fathers' objections to them becoming maesters, Maester Aemon, Valarr's cousin, tells Samwell Tarly that his grandfather sent him to the Citadel because too many dragons were as dangerous as too few. He mentions that King Daeron had four sons, three of whom had sons of their own. As the first-born son of King Daeron's eldest child, Prince Baelor, Valarr was one of the sons of the king's sons alluded to by Aemon.
