## Full Text

Due to editing issues caused by the length of the calculations, the list is divided into four pages. Use the navigation box, or navigate here to page 1, page 2 or page 3. You are currently on page 4.

In the main series and novellas, George R.R. Martin on most occasions does not state specifically in which year a character has been born or has died. This page lists the calculations to determine such years, years of birth mostly, but also years of death at times, as specific as is possible, for all characters for all this is possible.
This information then can be used in articles and serves to give a more complete image of the world of A Song of Ice and Fire.

The following guidelines are used for the calculations:

A character, both male and female, will be at least 12 years old at the birth of his or her first born child, for the following reason: a girl who has had her first flowering (i.e. first menstruation) is considered to be fit for both marriage and consummation of the marriage. Most highborn girls have their first flowering at the age of twelve or thirteen, indicating twelve as the youngest a girl can be for getting pregnant, and, as such, give birth. For boys, the same minimum age is used. Furthermore, we are given the precedence of the age of twelve for both males and females: the daughter of Unwin Peake died in childbirth at the age of twelve, while Viserys II Targaryen impregnated his wife at the age of twelve.

Siblings are assumed to be at least one year apart in age, taking into consideration that a woman who has just given birth will not immediately become pregnant again. This is only when discussing full-blood siblings. For siblings with the same father, but with a different mother, no such recovery period is required.

Where historic works are concerned which have been written by maesters, or sources recited from works written by maesters, it can be assumed that the age of a character stated in a year is the age that character would be turning that year. For example, Maegor I Targaryen was born in 12 AC. In historic works, his age in the year 25 AC will be stated as thirteen (the age he would be turning that year), without the author taking into account whether or not the described event took place before or after Maegor's nameday. This goes for historical works (i.e. The Sons of the Dragon, The Rogue Prince, The Princess and the Queen, Fire & Blood, and The World of Ice & Fire), not for POV-information, like A Game of Thrones and its sequels, or The Hedge Knight, and other tales of Dunk & Egg. Do note that on some occasions, this rule might not apply.

The following minimum ages for pages, squires, and knights are used, based on an overview of known ages:

Boys are considered to be at least six years old when becoming a page.

Boys are considered to be at least eight years old when becoming a squire.

Men are considered to be at least fifteen years old when becoming a knight.

A mother will be no older than ~52 years old at the earliest possibility for the birth of her youngest child, for the following reason: the Westerosi consider the age of forty-two to be "past [a woman's] childbearing years", and forty-four to be "well past her childbearing years", although women of forty-four have been known to give birth to a child. In real-life, the end of a woman's fertile cycle (menopause) occurs at an age of 49-52 on average, giving ~52 as a maximum age for giving birth to a child.

The characters are listed alphabetically, using full names. Maesters (like Aemon), will also be listed alphabetically under their birth names, unless those are not known.

Names that belong to several characters (for example, the name Walder Frey) will be listed alphabetically, but will have their nickname or relations listed in parentheses, so they can be distinguished from one another.

For calculations on placings of events, see Calculations of Events.

Ramsay Bolton[ ]

Birth[ ]

Ramsay Bolton was born when Rickard Stark was still Lord of Winterfell. This places Ramsay's birth in 282 AC the latest. No further specifications can be given.

Rast[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rast is two years older than Jon Snow, who was born in 283 AC (see Jon Snow). Rast was thus born in 281 AC.

Raya Stark[ ]

Birth[ ]

Raya Stark would have been at least one year younger than her older sister, Alys. Alys had been born no earlier than 134 AC (see Alys Stark), meaning that Raya could not have been born any earlier than 135 AC.

Raya would have been at least one year older than her younger sister, Myriah Stark. As the latest possible year of birth for Myriah is 165 AC (see Myriah Stark), Raya would have been born no later than 164 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Raya was thus born in or between 135 AC and 164 AC.

Raymont Baratheon[ ]

Birth[ ]

Raymont Baratheon was a younger son of Lord Orys Baratheon and his wife Lady Argella Durrandon. Raymont's parents married at some time after the battle known as the Last Storm (during Aegon's Conquest). As the Conquest began in 2 BC, Orys's first son couldn't be born any earlier than 2 BC. Since there was at least one year between the birth of Raymont and his brother, Raymont couldn't be born any earlier than 1 BC.

Raymont was a member of the Kingsguard in 41 AC. At the age of fifteen, Jaime Lannister was the youngest to join the Kingsguard ever, indicating Raymont was at least sixteen years old when he joined the Kingsguard. As 41 AC is the year of the first mention of Raymont being a Kingsguard knight, he was at least sixteen years old in 41 AC, though possibly older. This means that Raymont Baratheon was born at the latest in 25 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Raymont was thus born in or between 1 BC and 25 AC.

Death[ ]

Raymont Baratheon was a member of the Kingsguard by 41 AC. He was not among the seven Kingsguard knights of Maegor I Targaryen in 48 AC. As the Kingsguard serve for life and Raymont is not one of Maegor's Seven, this implies he had died by 48 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Raymont thus died in or between 41 AC and 48 AC.

Raymund Frey[ ]

Birth[ ]

Raymund Frey is the youngest child of Amarei Crakehall. Amarei died before Catelyn Tully was born. Catelyn Tully was born in either 264 AC or 265 AC (see Catelyn Tully), making 265 AC the last possible moment for Raymund to have been born.

Raymund's older brother Merrett was born in 262 AC (see Merrett Frey). After Merrett came Geremy and Raymund. Geremy and Raymund have not been mentioned to have been twins, indicating they were born at least one year apart. With Merret's birth in 262 AC, and with at least one year between Merrett and Geremy, and with at least one year between Geremy and Raymund, Merrett and Raymund will be at least two years apart in age, meaning that Raymund cannot have been born before 264 AC.

No further specification can be given. Raymund Frey was thus born in either 264 AC or 265 AC.

Raynald Westerling[ ]

Birth[ ]

Raynald Westerling is the older brother of Jeyne Westerling, who was born in 283 AC (see Jeyne Westerling). This means that one year earlier, 282 AC, is the last possible year of birth for Raynald. 

No further specifications can be given. Raynald was thus born in or before 282 AC.

Red Lamb[ ]

Birth[ ]

The Red Lamb is part of a group of young former slaves in training with Ser Barristan Selmy. According to Barristan, his lads are between the age of eight and twenty in 300 AC.
Being twenty in 300 AC gives the following options:

19 turning 20 in 300 AC, placing their birth in 280 AC

20 turning 21 in 300 AC, placing their birth in 279 AC

Barristan knights the Red Lamb, thus he is at least fifteen in 300 AC.
Being fifteen in 300 AC gives the following options:

14 turning 15 in 300 AC, placing his birth in 285 AC

15 turning 16 in 300 AC, placing his birth in 284 AC

No further specifications can be given. The Red Lamb was thus born in or between 279 AC and 285 AC.

Renly Baratheon[ ]

Birth[ ]

Renly Baratheon is estimated by Sansa Stark to have been "near twenty" years old in 298 AC, and is known to have been twenty-one years old in 299 AC. Being twenty-one years old in 299 AC gives the following options:

20 turning 21 in 299 AC, placing his birth in 278 AC

21 turning 22 in 299 AC, placing his birth in 277 AC

According to a semi-canon source, Renly was "a boy of six at the time of Robert's Rebellion". Robert's Rebellion took place in the latter part of 282 AC and a large part of 283 AC. Having been six years old during the war, would give Renly the following options:

5 turning 6 in 282 AC, placing his birth in 276 AC

6 turning 7 in 282 AC, placing his birth in 275 AC

5 turning 6 in 283 AC, placing his birth in 277 AC

6 turning 7 in 283 AC, placing his birth in 276 AC

The only overlapping year between the two sets of possibilities is 277 AC, meaning that Renly was twenty-one turning twenty-two in 299 AC, and five turning six in 283 AC, and that he was born in 277 AC.

 Note: Eddard Stark mentions that Renly had been a boy of eight when Robert won the throne. Renly having been eight years old in 283 AC would give the following options of birth:

7 turning 8 in 283 AC, placing his birth in 275 AC

8 turning 9 in 283 AC, placing his birth in 274 AC

Eddard's recollection does not align with the several statements of Renly's age as given in 299 AC, where Maester Cressen, Catelyn Tully, and Brienne of Tarth all give Renly's age as twenty-one (with birth options of 277 AC and 278 AC). Considering the closer relationsip of both Cressen and Brienne to Renly, and the fact that their statements are repeated on several occassions, Eddard's recollection of Renly's age at the time of the Rebellion's end appears to be incorrect.

Renly Norcross[ ]

Birth[ ]

Renly Norcross is two years old in 299 AC. He is three years old in 300 AC. That gives Renly the following options:

1 turning 2 in 299 AC, and 2 turning 3 in 300 AC, placing his birth in 297 AC

2 turning 3 in 299 AC, and 3 turning 4 in 300 AC, placing his birth in 296 AC

No further specifications can be given. Renly was thus born in either 296 AC or 297 AC.

Rhae Targaryen[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rhae Targaryen was younger than her brother Aegon, who was born in 200 AC (see Aegon V Targaryen). That means that Rhae was born no earlier than one year later, in 201 AC.

Rhae's mother had died by the time of the Tourney at Ashford in 209 AC. So technically, 209 AC is Rhae's last possible moment of birth.

No further specifications can be given. Rhae was thus born in or between 201 AC and 209 AC.

 Note: That neither Maekar, nor Daeron, Aerion or Aegon are still in deep mourning over their mother, would indicate that Dyanna Dayne had been dead for at least a while. A second indication is that Rhae was old enough, at some point in or before 209 AC, to put a love potion in the drink of her brother Aegon, suggesting she was born closer to 201 AC than to 209 AC.

Rhaegar Frey[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rhaegar Frey would have been at least twelve years old when his eldest child, Robert, was born. Robert was born in 286 AC (see Robert Frey (son of Rhaegar)), meaning Rhaegar was born the latest twelve years prior to 286 AC, in 274 AC.

Rhaegar was the second born son of Aenys Frey. Aenys was born no earlier than 237 AC (see Aenys Frey), meaning that Rhaegar cannot have been born earlier than thirteen years later, in 250 AC.

However, according to Davos Seaworth, Rhaegar is thirty years old at the maximum, or close to thirty years old. Being 30 years old in 300 AC (with thus as a maximum being 30 turning 31 in 300 AC) would place Rhaegars birth in 269 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Rhaegar was thus born in or between 269 AC and 274 AC.

Rhaegel Targaryen[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rhaegel Targaryen was the thirdborn son of Daeron II. He was born at least two years after his eldest brother Baelor, who was born in 170 AC (see Baelor Targaryen (son of Daeron II)). This would make the year 172 AC the first possible birth year of Rhaegel.

However, Rhaegel's aunt, Daenerys, was born in 172 AC (see Daenerys Targaryen (daughter of Aegon IV)). As Daenerys was born before Rhaegel's older brother Aerys, Aerys could thus have been born in 172 AC the earliest (later in the year than Daenerys), meaning that Rhaegel’s first possible year of birth is a year later, in 173 AC.

Rhaegel was born at least one year before his brother Maekar. Maekar's last possible year of birth was 179 AC (see Maekar I Targaryen), meaning that Rhaegel was born no later than 178 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Rhaegel was thus born in or between 173 AC and 178 AC.

Rhaella Targaryen[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rhaella Targaryen's firstborn child Rhaegar was born in 259 AC. She would have been at least twelve years old at his birth, meaning that she was born no later than 247 AC.

Rhaella is known to have been younger than her brother Aerys. Aerys was eighteen years old when he ascended the throne in 262 AC. That would mean that Rhaella was seventeen years old, or younger, in 262 AC, placing her birth in 245 AC or later.

No further specification can be given. Rhaella was thus born in either 245 AC, 246 AC or 247 AC.

Death[ ]

Rhaella Targaryen died when giving birth to Daenerys Targaryen. Daenerys was born in 284 AC (see Daenerys Targaryen).

Rhaella thus died in 284 AC.

Rhaelle Targaryen[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rhaelle Targaryen was the youngest child of Aegon V. Rhaelle's first elder sibling, Prince Daeron, was born in 228 AC (see Daeron Targaryen (son of Aegon V)). This means that Rhaelle was born in 229 AC the earliest.

Rhaelle's firstborn (and possibly only) son was born in 246 AC. Rhaelle would have been at least twelve years old at his birth, placing her birth no later than 234 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Rhaelle was thus born in or between 229 AC and 234 AC.

Death[ ]

Rhaelle Targaryen gave birth to Steffon Baratheon in 246 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Rhaelle thus died in 246 AC or after.

Rhaena Targaryen (daughter of Aegon III)[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rhaena Targaryen was two years younger than her sister Daena, who was born in 145 AC (see Daena Targaryen). This places Rhaena's birth in 147 AC.

Death[ ]

Rhaena Targaryen's claim to the Iron Throne was passed over after the death of King Baelor I in 171 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Rhaena thus died in 171 AC or after.

Note: Rhaena's younger sister Elaena, who lived to be at least seventy and died no earlier than 220 AC, (See the Elaena Targaryen calculation) is said to have outlived all her siblings. Additionally, their sister Daena is noted as living a much shorter life than Elaena, while Rhaena is not, suggesting that Rhaena most likely died at some point between the deaths of her sisters.

Rhaenys Targaryen[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rhaenys Targaryen has been mentioned to have been “a year or two” younger than her brother Aegon,  who has been mentioned to have been born in 27 BC. This means that Rhaenys was born in either 26 BC or 25 BC.

Rickard Redwyne[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rickard Redwyne was knighted in 55 AC. Rickard having been knighted in 55 AC means that he was at least fifteen years old by then. This places his birth no later than 40 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Rickard was thus born in or before 40 AC.

Rickard Stark[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rickard Stark's eldest son Brandon was born in 262 AC. Rickard would have been at least twelve years old when Brandon was born, meaning that Rickard cannot have been born later than 250 AC.

Grand Maester Pycelle tells Rickard's son Ned that King Maekar's summer took place before Rickard was born. King Maekar ruled from 221 AC until 233 AC, and this summer lasted seven years during his reign. The winter that followed this summer began in 231 AC, though it is unknown how long the autumn had lasted. The autumn is mentioned to have been short, by Pycelle, however, suggesting one, perhaps one and a half years, compared to other autumns.
Thus, the summer ended in 230/231 AC. This shows that Rickard Stark, Ned's father, was born no earlier than 230 AC. However, since it is stated that the summer had been long forgotten, it suggests that at the time of Rickards birth, the winter following Maekar's summer had already begun, or there had been several other  years that had passed as well, meaning that Rickard most likely had been born no earlier than several years after 230 AC, though no specificity can be given on how many years after 230 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Rickard Stark was thus born in or between 230 AC and 250 AC.

Rickard Wylde[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rickard Wylde has been mentioned to have been four years old in 299 AC and in 300 AC. Being four years old in both 299 AC and 300 AC means he was 3 turning 4 in 299 AC, and 4 turning 5 in 300 AC, placing his birth in 295 AC.

Rickard was thus born in 295 AC.

Rickon Stark[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rickon Stark was three years old when the direwolves are found and during the welcoming feast at Winterfell. Both events occur in 298 AC, giving the following options:

2 turning 3 in 298 AC, 3 turning 4 in 299 AC, 4 turning 5 in 300 AC, placing his birth in 295 AC

3 turning 4 in 298 AC, 4 turning 5 in 299 AC, 5 turning 6 in 300 AC, placing his birth in 294 AC

Rickon was “near four” when Robb left for war, and he was four when the news of Ned's death reached Winterfell. Rickon was still four when Theon Greyjoy attacked Winterfell. After the Sack of Winterfell Rickon is still four. In 300 AC, Rickon is still four years old. This shows that the only correct option for Rickon is 3 turning 4 in 299 AC, placing his birth in 295 AC.

Rickon was thus born in 295 AC.

Rickon Stark (son of Benjen)[ ]

Birth[ ]

Rickon Stark's son Cregan was born in 108 AC (See Cregan Stark). As Rickon would have been at least twelve years old at the birth of his son, Rickon had thus been born no later than 96 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Rickon was thus born in or before 96 AC.

Sansa Stark[ ]

Birth[ ]

Sansa Stark is eleven years old in 298 AC, and two years older than her sister Arya, who was born in 289 AC (see Arya Stark). Sansa is twelve in 299 AC, and tells Tyrion Lannister near the end of the year that she'll turn thirteen “in a moons turn”, indicating that maximally a month from that moment on, she'll have turned thirteen. Since this observation is made so close to the beginning of 300 AC, the possibility exists that 300 AC is less than a month away, which gives rise to the possibility that Sansa turns thirteen 300 AC.

The minimum amount of time that passes from the moment of Sansa's statement until the first day of 300 AC can be determined:

From Sansa's wedding until arrival of the news at Riverrun = 4 day minimum (see Note #1)

From arrival of the news of Sansa's wedding, until Hoster Tully's funeral = 1 day

From funeral of Hoster Tully until the departude from Riverrun = 1 day (minimum) 

A minimum of 19 days traveling from Riverrun to the Twins

From Green Fork to Twins = 1 day minimum

From arrival at the Twins until the Red Wedding = 0 days

From news of Red Wedding until King's Landing = 4 day minimum (see Note #1)

It can be concluded that a minimum of thirty days days pass between Sansa's wedding and Joffrey's wedding to Margaery on the first day of the 300th year After the Conquest, and thus that Sansa turns thirteen in 299 AC, placing her birth in 286 AC.

It should be noted that, when the news of the Red Wedding reaches King's Landing, Joffrey Baratheon believes there is still time for a raven to reach the Twins, and for a rider to return from the Twins to King's Landing with Robb Stark's head, before Joffrey's wedding on the first day of 300 AC. This suggests there are still several weeks (about a fortnight) between the news reaching King's Landing, and the beginning of 300 AC, thereby confirming Sansa’s nameday taking place in late 299 AC.

Note #1: The minimum of four days travel for the ravens from King's Landing to the Twins and back, is a distance guessed, compared to the distance between Oxcross and King's Landing, and the fact a raven needs 6 days to cross that distance.

I remember the first time my father took me to court, Robert had to hold my hand. I could not have been older than four, which would have made him five or six.

This quote shows that while Stannis was four, Robert could be both five and six. This means that after Stannis turned four, Robert remained five for a while, before turning six. It is unknown, however, when in the year the namedays fall. There are two scenarios:

Robert turns 6 the same year Stannis has turned 4, placing Robert's nameday after Stannis' nameday in the same year

Robert turns 6 the year after Stannis has turned 4, placing Robert's nameday before Stannis' nameday in the year.

The first option gives an age difference of two years, the second option gives an age difference of one year.

Stannis was thus born in 264 AC.

18 turning 19 in 282 AC, placing his birth in 263 AC

19 turning 20 in 282 AC, placing his birth in 262 AC

19 turning 20 in 283 AC, placing his birth in 263 AC

Both the timing of the mentioning of Stannis's age (the first month or the first half of the second month of 299 AC), and Stannis's age during the Siege for all available options, compared with Davos's quote, suggests that Stannis has yet to turn 35 in the year 299 AC, which would place his birth in 264 AC. Death[ ]

During the Tourney of the Hand in 298 AC, Sansa Stark mentions that she met Ser Raymar Royce and his father when they visited Winterfell, on their way to the Wall. The timeframe Sansa mentions was two years ago. During the prologue of A Game of Thrones, Ser Waymar has been on the Wall for almost half a year. The time in between Sansa remembering having met the Royces (during the Tourney of the Hand), and one of Ser Warmar's fellow rangers getting decapitated (during A Game of Thrones, Bran I), seems to have been at least seven months, since King Robert visited Winterfell for at least one month, and the journey from Winterfell to King's Landing, and thus also from King's Landing to Winterfell, took about 2 months each. Robert cannot have left for Winterfell until two weeks after Joffrey's 12th nameday (since he didn't leave until Jon Arryn had died, and Jon Arryn died a fortnight after Joffrey's nameday), which is known to have taken place in 298 AC (see the Joffrey Baratheon calculation), although most likely  more time passed in between Jon Arryn's death and Robert leaving for Winterfell, seeing as how Cersei and her children were not in King's Landing when Jon Arryn died (they were making a tour to Casterly Rock), but they were present in the royal party. Naturally, some weeks passed in between arriving at King's Landing and the Tourney of the Hand, which are guessed at the lower bar of one month.

Thus Sansa Stark remembers a while into the second half of 298 AC that she met the Royces 2 years prior, placing their visit to Winterfell into the second half of 296 AC. With Waymar dying after spending half a year on the Wall, Waymar's death thus occured in 297 AC.

Birth[ ]

Willas is said to be twice the age of Sansa Stark. Sansa, at that moment, is twelve. Since Garlan was twenty-two years old at that time, Willas would have been twenty-three years old (one year older than Garlan) or older. Being exactly twice Sansa's age would make him twenty-four years old, and keeping in mind that Sansa turns thirteen in roughly “a moons turn”, he could have been as old as twenty-six as well. Willas is considered a bit too old for Sansa by his grandmother, suggesting the age difference is reaching its limit. This means that Willas could not have been born any earlier than 273 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Willas was thus born in or between 273 AC and 276 AC.
