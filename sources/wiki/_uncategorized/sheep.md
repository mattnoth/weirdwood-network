Culture[ ]

  

The arms of House Rambton

Shepherds herd their sheep with sheepdogs.

Wool is frequently used in clothing, especially in Westeros. Wool is an important part of the northern economy, and White Harbor has a warehouse full of sheepskins. Free folk use sheepskin for jerkins, helms, and cloaks. Sheepskin has been worn by northmen and lambskin by crannogmen. Sheepskin can be used for maps and scrolls.

Lamb and mutton are commonly eaten throughout the known world.

The arms of House Stokeworth depict a white lamb. Lambswold is found in the riverlands, and the Sheepshead Hills are located in the north. House Umber graze their sheep in the high meadows of the northern mountains in summer. Dothraki refer to the peaceful Lhazareen as Lamb Men.

A ram is included in the arms of House Rambton. Ramsford and Ramsgate are settlements in the riverlands and the north, respectively, and Ramshead is a ship of the royal fleet.

Ironborn use thralls to tend their goats and sheep. Harrag Sharp is known as Harrag Sheepstealer.

Oznak zo Pahl styles his hair into two great curling ram's horns.

A battering ram is a siege engine used against gates and fortifications. Ships are often equipped with rams as well; Great Kraken, for instance, has a grey iron ram in the shape of a kraken.

History[ ]

  

Nettles delivers a sheep to Sheepstealer, by Ashley Hunter Rice ©

Legend claims that Corlos discovered gold in Casterly Rock after tracking a lion which had been hunting sheep.

During the First Dornish War, the vanguard of King Aegon I Targaryen was barred in the high passes of the Red Mountains by a wall of rotting sheep carcasses.

Sheepstealer was a dragon during the Dance of the Dragons with a taste for mutton. Nettles left out a freshly-killed sheep for the dragon every morning, causing Sheepstealer to grow accustomed to her presence and eventually allow her to mount him. When Nettles and Prince Daemon Targaryen departed each other, the girl slew a black ram and fed the animal to Sheepstealer before flying away from Maidenpool.

Rohanne Webber, Lady of Coldmoat, had five hundred head of black-nosed sheep.

Ser Goodwin had Brienne Tarth slaughter lambs and suckling pigs in an effort to toughen the girl.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Viserys Targaryen alleges that Dothraki men sleep with sheep; the same is also claimed of the Lhazareen by the Dothraki Quaro. The khalasar of Khal Ogo kill thousands of sheep during their attack on a Lhazarene town. To treat Drogo's wound, Mirri Maz Duur creates a poultice of firepod and sting-me-not and binds it in a lambskin. The khal removes the poultice, however, and the wound festers.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Gilly reveals to Jon Snow that Craster has been giving sheep to the gods.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

  

Drogon hunts down a sheep, by Regis Moulun © Fantasy Flight Games

Herds of sheep are part of the free folk host gathered by Mance Rayder.

Thoros of Myr gives three sheep to Lady Ravella Smallwood when the brotherhood without banners stop by Acorn Hall. The Mad Huntsman brings a flock of sheep to Stoney Sept.

Kella minds the sheep at the tower of House Baelish.

Lord Walder Frey mocks his son Merrett as Merrett Muttonhead.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Marwyn refers to the other archmaesters of the Citadel as grey sheep.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

While Reznak mo Reznak claims that they are sheep bones, Daenerys Targaryen recognizes that they are the bones of a child.

Arya Stark has a dream of Nymeria's pack of wolves attacking a flock of sheep.

Ser Jorah Mormont claims a greathelm sporting ram's horns, one of which is broken, from the armament of the Second Sons.

Rams are one of the animals depicted in the masks of the Brazen Beasts. The Red Lamb is a former slave in Meereen training to become a knight with Ser Barristan Selmy. After Rhaegal and Viserion escape from the Great Pyramid, a thousand sheep are herded into Daznak's Pit for the dragons to feed upon.
