Appearance[ ]

Considered a beautiful people, Naathi have round flat faces, dusky skin, and amber eyes which can resemble molten gold.

The Naathi appear to be immune to butterfly fever, a local disease which makes its victims violently spasm and die.

Culture[ ]

The Naathi are called the Peaceful People due to their belief in extreme pacifism. They make music rather than war and kill nothing, not even animals, eating only fruit and never flesh. They are widely sought after by slavers who consider them to make the best slaves.

The Naath believe in one god, the Lord of Harmony, who has existed and will exist for all time. He is the source of creation.

Naathi revere butterflies as messengers of the Lord of Harmony, charged with protection of the island and its people. These legends come from the butterfly fever that has laid waste to conquerors throughout history.

Economy[ ]

The Naathi were once involved in long-distance trade networks stretching from Essos to Westeros. Given that Naath's environment is well-suited for insect life, the island used to be a major exporter of silk from silkworm caterpillars. The Naathi's shimmering silks could be found in markets throughout the Seven Kingdoms and the Free Cities. The Naathi also exported fine handicrafts and delicate spiced wine.

Slaver raids intensified significantly after the Doom of Valyria, however, and the Naathi have largely abandoned their coasts to take refuge in the hills and forests of the interior. As a result the export trade has largely collapsed in the past four hundred years, and Naathi silks are less and less frequently seen in the known world's major cities.

History[ ]

During the height of the Old Empire of Ghis, one of their ships briefly landed in the Summer Isles, alerting their inhabitants that lands and peoples existed beyond their own waters. Summer Islander explorers then visited Naath to the east.

Ghiscari, Valyrians, Volantenes, and corsairs from the Basilisk Isles have raided or tried to settle the island. The Naathi claim that none of these invaders maintained a permanent presence on the island for more than a single year, however, as they all fell victim to butterfly fever.

Naathi were among the founders of Braavos.

The Naathi welcomed Princess Nymeria and her Rhoynar when they fled the Valyrians on ten thousand ships, but the refugees abandoned Naath when scores died from an unknown illness, presumably the butterfly fever.

Slavers and corsairs learned to limit their time on the island to avoid catching butterfly fever, instead capturing the Peaceful People in brief nighttime raids. The captives were then sold in Valyria and Slaver's Bay. The slaver attacks worsened during the Century of Blood, since Valyria no longer controlled the seas. Unwilling to break their vows of total pacifism, even to defend their homes and persons, the Naathi fled from their coasts to the hills and forests. As a result the silk exports which once flowed from Naath have dwindled away.

Recent Events[ ]

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Daenerys Targaryen takes into her service the scribe Missandei, who is originally from Naath.
