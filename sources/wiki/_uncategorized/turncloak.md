A turncloak is the term used for a treacherous, disloyal person who has betrayed those who put their faith in them, those who previously considered the turncloak loyal and worthy of trust. The term comes from turning your cloak from the heraldry of your original loyalties to that of your new master.

The betrayal is often an act of double-crossing, suddenly changing alliances and going over to the enemy, et al. To name someone a turncloak is to name them the worst kind of betrayer and traitor. 

A person may turn their cloak to benefit themselves, or for more personal reasons to change their loyalty. Sellswords have a reputation for being willing to betray anyone for enough gold.

Once a person is considered a turncloak, they can become a pariah and no one will put their trust in them or ever respect them, not even the people who greatly benefited from their betrayal.

Accused turncloaks[ ]

Luthor Largent - Commander of the City Watch of King's Landing, accused of turning cloak from King Aegon II Targaryen to Queen Rhaenyra Targaryen, but he said the gold cloaks had always been loyal to their former commander Daemon Targaryen.

Jon Snow - Qhorin Halfhand ordered Jon to yield to the wildlings and act as a spy. Some men of the Night's Watch, such as Janos Slynt, think Jon went over willingly and name Jon a turncloak.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

At the siege of Storm's End, King Renly Baratheon is killed by a shadow assassin. Afterwards, the majority of the host that had accompanied him to Storm's End swears their allegiance to his brother, King Stannis Baratheon. When Stannis confronts Ser Cortnay Penrose, castellan of Storm's End, Cortnay refuses to yield the castle and accuses the host of being turncloaks. This angers the king's men, and Lord Bryce Caron denies that any man there is a turncloak. However, Davos Seaworth feels that Cortnay is not entirely wrong.
