Customs[ ]

Plunder[ ]

  

House Orkwood raider, by Tommy Arnold © Fantasy Flight Games

In the Old Way, only women were allowed to buy ornaments (e.g. jewelry), whereas warriors were only allowed to wear the jewelry if they had paid the iron price for it, i.e. if they had acquired it from the corpses of enemies they had slain himself. 

Labor[ ]

Under the Old Way, farming and mining are derided as unsuitable for free men, but fishing is respected.

Military[ ]

Under the Old Way, the ironborn lived by the axe and the sword. Under the Old Way, the usage of catapults and siege engines, with the goal of starving a castle out, was considered to be without glory.

Execution[ ]

The Old Way dictated that a captive would have to be executed by giving him to the Drowned God, thus drowning him in salt water.

Thralldom[ ]

  

A thrall, by Emile Denis © Fantasy Flight Games

The Old Way allowed the ironborn to capture men and women on their raids, bringing them back to the Iron Islands. These captive thralls were put to work on the isles, serving as farmers and miners. Captured women served as scullions, cooks, seamstresses, weavers, and midwives, and the prettier and younger women became serving girls, whores, household drudges, or wives to other thralls.

While thralls are made to perform labor on the Iron Islands for those by whom they have been captured, they are not considered to be slaves. Taking slaves is not considered to be the Old Way.

Salt wives[ ]

The fairest, strongest, and most nubile captured women became salt wives, marrying ironmen in a ceremony preformed by a priest of the Drowned God. A man can join himself to multiple salt wives, the number of which speaks to his power, wealth, and virility. The children born from such a marriage are not considered to be bastards, but can inherit should their father have no surviving sons by his true, freeborn rock wife from the Iron Islands.

Medicine[ ]

Healing a person's wounds the Old Way meant healing the wound with fire and seawater.

History[ ]

  

Nighttime Raid, by Lake Hurwitz © Fantasy Flight Games

Under the Old Way, many a kingsmoot was held to elect the High King of the Iron Islands.

During his Conquest, Aegon the Conqueror pacified the ironborn and outlawed the custom, at least within the Seven Kingdoms of Westeros, as the ironmen were allowed to prey on the shipping and settlements of the Free Cities and faraway places. Those that continue to act in such a manner are said to be keeping to the Old Way.

Stealing women was made a crime throughout the Seven Kingdoms by Aegon, and reavers were forbidden to prey upon the king's domains, although these prohibitions have only been sporadically enforced by Aegon's successors. Thralls are still present on the Iron Islands today. Regardless, following the acquisition of primacy on the Iron Islands, the lords of House Greyjoy did not allow reaving close to home.

Dagon Greyjoy attempted to restore the Old Way to the islands, but he was eventually defeated by House Targaryen. Balon Greyjoy tried the same with Greyjoy's Rebellion, but he was subdued by King Robert I Baratheon.
