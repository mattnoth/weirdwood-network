## Full Text

A tourney at Old Oak was held in the Reach in 73 AC during the reign of Jaehaerys I Targaryen.

History[ ]

The tourney was held at Old Oak by Lord Oakheart, who was celebrating the birth of a young son with seven days of jousting. A mystery knight known as the Silver Fool took part in the tourney, accounting himself well before falling to Ser Rickard Redwyne. Rickard helped the mystery knight to his feet and unmasked him, revealing the Silver Fool to be none other than Prince Baelon Targaryen. Rickard bade Baelon to kneel, and knighted the prince on the spot. A feast was held after the conclusion of the jousting to make the tourney's end.

The Joust[ ]

  Prince Baelon Targaryen defeated both Fossoway twins

  Prince Baelon Targaryen defeated Ser Alyn Ashford

  Prince Baelon Targaryen defeated Ser Denys Oakheart

  Ser Rickard Redwyne defeats and unmasks Prince Baelon Targaryen
