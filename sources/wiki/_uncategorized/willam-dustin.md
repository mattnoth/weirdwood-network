Character[ ]

According to his widow, Barbrey Dustin, Willam was full of pride.

Lord Dustin rode a great red stallion.

History[ ]

Young Lord Willam had been married to Barbrey Ryswell for less than half a year before the outbreak of Robert's Rebellion. Barbrey urged him to send one of his kin in his place, but Willam insisted on leading the levies from Barrowton himself. Barbrey gave her husband a fine red stallion, the pride of the herds of her father, Lord Rodrik Ryswell. Willam vowed he would return to her mounted on the steed.

Willam was present at the wedding of his liege, Lord Eddard Stark, to Lady Catelyn Tully at Riverrun. During the bedding custom, Willam praised Catelyn's breasts to Eddard.

Willam was one of six companions who accompanied Eddard to the tower of joy in the Red Mountains of Dorne, where Lord Stark sought his sister, Lyanna Stark. They were opposed by three Kingsguard, however, and only Eddard and Lord Howland Reed survived the ensuing battle. Eddard buried the fallen in cairns raised from the tower's stones. Lord Stark returned the horse to Willam's widow, Barbrey, when he returned to the north, and told her that Willam had died an honorable death.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

While under the influence of the milk of the poppy, taken because of his broken leg, Lord Eddard Stark has a fever dream of Willam and his companions at the combat at the tower of joy.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

In the crypts of Winterfell, Barbrey Dustin, the widow of Willam and the Lady of Barrowton, reveals to Theon Greyjoy that she still resents that Eddard brought Willam's red stallion back from Dorne but not his bones. She mentions that Eddard did bring home his sister Lyanna's bones, but Barbrey promises that Eddard's own bones (being transported north from Riverrun) will never be laid to rest in the crypts beside hers, as she intends to feed them to her dogs.
