Ethnicity[ ]

  

Brienne of Tarth, by Quickreaver © Fantasy Flight Games

The stormlands were settled in antiquity by the First Men who drove out the children of the forest. The Storm Kings from House Durrandon of Storm's End were the most powerful of the stormlanders. The coming of the Andals led to wars between the First Men and the invading Andals, with the two peoples eventually coming together after neither could conclusively defeat the other.

Culture[ ]

Religion[ ]

The First Men of antiquity followed the old gods, but the Faith of the Seven gained ascendancy during the reign of King Ormund III Durrandon.

Customs[ ]

  

Anguy by Lucas Soriana © Fantasy Flight Games

The stormlanders have a strong martial tradition, with the marcher lords being especially known for their ferocity. The Dornish Marches are well known for their famous bowmen and ballads, and the stormlands are also praised for their seamen and sailors.

Many stormlanders live in moss-covered cottages among the region's rainy woods and green hills. The men of the rainwood have castles which appear to have been grown from the earth.

Stormlanders, especially the marchers, can have fierce rivalries with Dornishmen to their south.

Noble bastards raised in the stormlands can be given the surname Storm.
