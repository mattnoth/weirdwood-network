## Summary

The Battle of the Trident was the battle that ultimately determined the outcome of Robert's Rebellion. The battle was fought between rebel and royal troops at the Trident, across what would thereafter be known as the ruby ford. It ended in a victory for the rebels.

Background[ ]

After Lord Jon Connington's defeat in the Battle of the Bells, King Aerys II Targaryen at last realized that the rebellion was a major threat to House Targaryen, perhaps the greatest threat to the dynasty since Daemon Blackfyre. Lord Robert Baratheon left Stoney Sept for Riverrun, where Lords Jon Arryn and Eddard Stark married the daughters of Lord Hoster Tully in a double wedding. Around the time of the Trident, Robert announced his claim for the Iron Throne and led the combined Arryn, Baratheon, Stark, and Tully host of rebels.

King Aerys's heir, the crown prince Rhaegar Targaryen, returned from Dorne to take command of the royalist army. Aerys gracelessly reminded the Martells that Princess Elia was in his power and dispatched her uncle, Prince Lewyn Martell of the Kingsguard, to assume command of ten thousand Dornishmen marching up the kingsroad, and joined them to Rhaegar's royalist army en route to the Trident. At the same time, Ser Barristan Selmy and Ser Jonothor Darry, also of the Kingsguard, were sent to rally the scattered forces of Lord Connington. Additionally, Rhaegar persuaded his father to ask for help from his old Hand of the King, Lord Tywin Lannister.

Led by the loyalist lords Mace Tyrell and Paxter Redwyne, the Redwyne fleet and most levies from the Reach were at the siege of Storm's End. Some of Mace's men joined Rhaegar's host, however, according to a semi-canon source.

Opposing armies[ ]

Rebel Army[ ]

The army of the rebels consisted of: 

Forces from the north, including Houses House Bolton, Karstark, Manderly, Mormont, Stark, and Umber.

Forces from the Vale of Arryn, including Houses Arryn and Corbray, though a few houses had remained loyal to the Targaryens earlier in the war, like the Graftons.

Forces from the riverlands, although the riverlords were deeply divided during the war. House Tully and many subject lords, including House Mallister, took the side of the rebels, while other houses, such as Houses Darry, Goodbrook, Mooton, and Ryger, some with personal ties to the Targaryens, took up the royal cause. House Frey, the largest of the houses sworn to House Tully, was slow to gather their levies.

Some of the stormlands. Though Robert Baratheon was the Lord of Storm's End and a central figure of the rebellion, most of the stormlords were trapped in the south, and badly bloodied by Ashford and the siege of Storm's End. As such, their contribution north of the stormlands appears to have been limited.

Royal Army[ ]

The army of the House Targaryen royalists consisted of: 

Ten thousand Dornishmen in service to House Martell.

Survivors of the Battle of the Bells. These may have included loyal riverlords, stormlords, and Valemen.

Houses of the crownlands, including Houses Boggs, Brune, and Crabb from Crackclaw Point.

A sizable host from the Reach.

The Targaryen host had nearly forty thousand men, with at most a tenth of them knights. The rest were archers, freeriders, and foot soldiers consisting of spearmen and pikemen. The royalists were more numerous, but the rebels were more battle hardened.

Battle[ ]

  

The Battle of the Trident, by Abe Papakhian ©

The battle took place at the chief crossing of the Trident, near the crossroads of the river road and the kingsroad, when Rhaegar Targaryen tried to cross the river. Robert Baratheon used his booming voice to command during the fighting.

Dornishmen under the command of Prince Lewyn Martell threatened Robert's left flank. When Lord Corbray fell wounded, Ser Lyn Corbray picked up his father's Valyrian steel sword, Lady Forlorn, and led a charge which broke the Dornishmen. During the charge Lyn killed the already-injured Lewyn.

Elsewhere during the battle, Ser Barristan Selmy killed a dozen friends of Robert and Eddard Stark, and Lord Jason Mallister slew three of Rhaegar's bannermen.

Though many great lords and knights either lost their lives or made names for themselves in the fighting, the battle was decided when Robert and Rhaegar met in single combat on horseback in the stream. Robert eventually crushed Rhaegar's chest with a blow of his warhammer, which had been made by Donal Noye. The blow was so hard that the rubies adorning Rhaegar's armor were shattered free to litter the river, giving the ruby ford its name. Leaderless, the royal army broke and fled back to King's Landing.

Wounded by arrow, spear, and sword, Barristan was close to death when he was brought before Robert. Although he was counseled to kill him by Lord Roose Bolton, Robert spared Barristan's life and sent his own maester to tend to his adversary's wounds. Lord Wyman Manderly granted the Wolf's Den to Ser Bartimus as a reward for saving his life during the battle. The sons of Mors Umber were killed at the Trident, and Lord Grandison died a year later from wounds sustained in the fighting.

The son of Lord Goodbrook made peace with Lord Hoster Tully and Robert after the battle. Lord Walder Frey arrived after the fighting was over, so Hoster has since called him "the Late Lord Frey" and questioned his bannerman's loyalty.

Aftermath[ ]

  

The Battle of the Trident as depicted by Justin Sweet in The World of Ice & Fire.

The battle was the decisive engagement of the war. When King's Landing learned of Prince Rhaegar's death, King Aerys II Targaryen sent the pregnant Queen Rhaella with Prince Viserys to Dragonstone. Aerys kept Elia Martell and her children at the Red Keep as a threat against Doran Martell, Prince of Dorne, however, since the Mad King thought he had been betrayed by Doran's uncle, Prince Lewyn, at the Trident.

The way to King's Landing was now open for the rebels, with the only significant loyalist force left invested in the siege of Storm's End. The rebels also learned that Lord Tywin Lannister was heading toward King's Landing. Lord Robert Baratheon had been wounded during the battle, so Lord Eddard Stark led Robert's vanguard down the kingsroad towards the capital, racing to reach it before the Lannisters. Tywin arrived first, however, and Grand Maester Pycelle convinced the Mad King to open the city gates, resulting in the Sack of King's Landing.

Robert succeeded Aerys on the Iron Throne, with Barristan included among those pardoned by the new king. Lord Mace Tyrell yielded to Lord Stark after the Trident, ending the siege of Storm's End, and Eddard continued south to the tower of joy. Queen Cersei Lannister, Robert's new wife, came to believe that the wrong man died at the Trident.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

The exiled Daenerys and Viserys Targaryen, by aprilis420 ©

Recalling the stories told to her by her brother Viserys, Daenerys Targaryen sometimes imagines how their late brother Rhaegar died for the woman he loved. Viserys desires to avenge Rhaegar's death by killing King Robert I Baratheon.

Unhappy as king, Robert admits to his childhood friend, Lord Eddard Stark, that he sometimes regrets the outcome of the Trident and that he was unable to save Eddard's sister, Lyanna, during the war.

Arya Stark and Mycah leave the inn at the crossroads to look for any of Rhaegar's rubies remaining in the ruby ford.

A feverish Eddard has a fever dream of the tower of joy in which he tells Ser Arthur Dayne, Ser Oswell Whent, and Ser Gerold Hightower that he had unsuccessfully looked for the three Kingsguard on the Trident.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Ser Edmure Tully thinks of Rhaegar's fate while planning the Battle of the Fords against Lord Tywin Lannister.

While in the House of the Undying in Qarth, Daenerys has a vision of a dying prince sinking to his knees in water and murmuring a woman's name as he dies.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Daenerys dreams she is Rhaegar at the Trident. She flies atop a dragon and melts the ice-armored host of the Usurper with dragonfire.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Brienne of Tarth meets the Elder Brother of the Quiet Isle, who says he "died" in the Battle of the Trident and was reborn on the island.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Lord Jon Connington regrets his failure to slay Robert in the Battle of the Bells, as it would have prevented Rhaegar's death in the Battle of the Trident. Jon supports Rhaegar's presumed son, Aegon Targaryen, in his campaign for the Iron Throne.

Ser Bartimus drunkenly boasts to Lord Davos Seaworth about having saved the life of Lord Wyman Manderly at the Trident.
