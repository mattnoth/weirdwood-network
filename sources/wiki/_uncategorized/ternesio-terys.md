Ternesio Terys is the captain of the Titan's Daughter. He is the father of Yorko Terys and Denyo Terys.

In the television adaptation Game of Thrones, Ternesio is portrayed by Gary Oliver.

Appearance and Character[ ]

Ternesio keeps his grey hair cut short and neat. He has a square, windburnt face, that he keeps shaven. Ternesio is stout, and wears a long purple wool captain's coat.

Ternesio speaks the Common Tongue well, in addition to the Braavosi tongue. He has a deep voice. Ternesio often jokes with his crew, but when he frowns, they run.

Recent Events[ ]

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Ternesio Terys meets Arya Stark at Saltpans, where the Titan's Daughter is docked. Arya asks for passage to the Night's Watch castle Eastwatch-by-the-Sea, but Ternesio refuses her, as she has little money, and the north is too dangerous. However, when Arya shows him the iron coin that was given to her by Jaqen H'ghar, and says the phrase "Valar morghulis" as Jaqen had taught her, Ternesio replies, "Valar dohaeris", and offers her a cabin.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Ternesio captains the Titan's Daughter from Saltpans to Braavos. When the ship arrives at Braavos, he tells Arya that she has no need to wait for the customs check, and that he will lower a boat for his son Yorko to row her ashore. Arya wishes she could stay aboard the ship, but she can tell by the captain's anxiousness that he wants to be rid of her. Before Arya leaves, Ternesio asks her to remember his name, and the service he did for her.
