History[ ]

After the death of their royal spouse, a queen retains her title as queen and is known as the Dowager Queen or Queen Dowager. The Dowager Queen may still hold political power after her husband's death. Dowager Queen Visenya Targaryen advised her nephew King Aenys I Targaryen, though, paralyzed by indecision, he heeded little of it. She would go on to ensure her own son, Aenys' half-brother Maegor I Targaryen, succeeded as king and worked closely with Maegor to enforce his rule until her death. In the immediate hours after her husband Viserys I Targaryen's death, Dowager Queen Alicent Hightower was central figure in the scheme to crown her son Aegon II Targaryen as king over their father's chosen heir, Aegon's elder sister Rhaenyra Targaryen. 

The Dowager Queen may also serve as Queen Regent in her son's minority. Dowager Queen Alyssa Velaryon would act as regent for her son Jaehaerys I Targaryen until he came of age. Queen Dowager Cersei Lannister also served as Queen Regent for both her sons, King Joffrey Baratheon and King Tommen Baratheon. The extent of this power can vary. In the case of Cersei, the willful personality of Joffrey as well as the presence of her brother Tyrion Lannister and father Tywin Lannister as Hand of the King curtailed her influence. During Tommen's reign, without the influence of father and brother and with a far more obedient son, the Dowager Queen and Regent was able to govern as she wished.

In some cases, the queen may marry a surviving relative of the deceased king and thus not take on the title of Dowager Queen, as was the case of Queen Margaery Tyrell, who wed first the self-proclaimed King Renly Baratheon and then his alleged nephews, King Joffrey Baratheon and King Tommen Baratheon. 

The widow of a lord may also be similarly styled as a Dowager Lady, as in the case of Samantha Hightower, Dowager Lady of Oldtown.

Known Dowager Queens[ ]

Visenya Targaryen, widow of King Aegon I Targaryen

Alyssa Velaryon, widow of King Aenys Targaryen

Rhaena Targaryen, widow of King Maegor I Targaryen

Elinor Costayne, widow of King Maegor I Targaryen

Alicent Hightower, widow of King Viserys I Targaryen

Rhaella Targaryen, widow of King Aerys II Targaryen

Cersei Lannister, widow of King Robert Baratheon

Lelia Lannister, widow of King of the Iron Islands Harmund II Hoare

Notes[ ]

↑ Rhaella is not explicitly referred to as Dowager Queen in the text. However, A Game of Thrones establishes that Aerys II predeceased her in Daenerys's first chapter, making her a Dowager Queen, as the title refers to widows of deceased kings.
