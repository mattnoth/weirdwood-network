## Summary

A smuggler is a naval tradesman who carries out illegal trade, often dealing stolen goods, or evading customs, tax officers, or naval blockades. Some smugglers also engage in piracy or slavery.

Customs[ ]

Smugglers often employ black sails and muffled oars. They must be familiar with the tides, and many navigate using stars.

There is a smugglers' cove near the ruined castle of the Whispers in Crackclaw Point.

The Three Sisters have long been a haven for smugglers, and the Belly of the Whale is a smugglers' den in Sisterton.

History[ ]

  

Davos Seaworth, by Chris Dien © Fantasy Flight Games

The Night's Watch fleet at Eastwatch-by-the-Sea arrests and executes smugglers who sell weaponry to the free folk.

When King Aerys II Targaryen sat the Iron Throne, smugglers would be killed if caught by Ser Tristimun and the sea watch.

During Robert's Rebellion, almost a year into the siege of Storm's End, the notorious smuggler Davos evaded the Redwyne fleet's blockade of Shipbreaker Bay to smuggle onions and salted fish into the castle, knowing the starving garrison would pay him the best price. The food allowed Stannis Baratheon's men to hold on until Lord Eddard Stark arrived to break the siege. As a reward for his service, Stannis knighted Davos, giving him choice lands on Cape Wrath and allowing him to choose Seaworth as the name of his new house. However, as payment for his past crimes, Davos lost the first joint from each finger of his left hand. Davos found the ruling just, and agreed to the terms if Stannis would swing the blade himself.

Recent Events[ ]

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

While incarcerated in the Wolf's Den, Lord Davos Seaworth speaks about his past life as a smuggler with Therry. Lord Wyman Manderly enlists Davos to find Rickon Stark because of his smuggling skills.
