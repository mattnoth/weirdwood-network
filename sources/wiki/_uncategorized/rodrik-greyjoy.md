Character[ ]

According to Theon Greyjoy, Rodrik had a tendency for "drunken cuffs".

History[ ]

According to Asha Greyjoy, Theon lived in awe and fear of their elder brothers, Rodrik and Maron. Rodrik was good friends with Harras Harlaw in their youth. 

Balon Greyjoy, Lord of the Iron Islands, rose in rebellion against Robert I Baratheon, thinking the new king did not have great support amongst the nobility of the Seven Kingdoms and that Balon would be allowed to go his own way. Rodrik led longships in the storming of Seagard, causing the bronze bell of the Booming Tower at Seagard to be rung for the first time in three hundred years. Jason Mallister, Lord of Seagard, killed Rodrik beneath the castle walls and threw the ironmen back into the sea. Maron was later killed in the battle at Pyke.

Balon blames Eddard Stark, Lord of Winterfell, for the deaths of Rodrik and Maron during Greyjoy's Rebellion, though neither was actually slain by Eddard. Alannys Harlaw, Balon's grieving wife, eventually began to roam the halls of Pyke in search of her lost sons, even walking the rope bridge to the Sea Tower while barefoot; a maester often had to draw splinters from her heels.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Theon Greyjoy has little love for his eldest brother, telling Patrek Mallister he holds no enmity towards Patrek's father, Lord Jason, or the Mallisters for slaying Rodrik during Greyjoy's Rebellion. 

After the capture of Winterfell and the supposed deaths of Bran and Rickon Stark, two of Lord Eddard Stark's sons, Theon claims he has laid to rest the ghosts of his own brothers, Rodrik and Maron.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Alannys Harlaw continues to deeply grieve the loss of her eldest sons.

The Winds of Winter[ ]

Asha Greyjoy informs Stannis Baratheon that the deaths of Rodrik and Maron had shattered their mother, Alannys.
