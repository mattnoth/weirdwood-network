Culture[ ]

Cheese is often included with meals, such as breakfast and supper. Wedges can be cut from wheels of cheese. Cheese can be veined with wine. Mold can grow on cheese. Peppers stuffed with cheese is a food served in Dorne. Bread and cheese can be offered as part of the guest right custom.

Lackwits can be said to have cheese in their heads. Tywin Lannister, Lord of Casterly Rock, looks down upon the merchant princes of the Free Cities, considering them cheese lords and cheese kings.

History[ ]

  

The cheesemonger Illyrio Mopatis, by Jake Murray © Fantasy Flight Games

Cheesemakers prospered in the Seven Kingdoms during the reign of King Jaehaerys I Targaryen.

Recent Events[ ]

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Lark the Sisterman refers to Small Paul as "Cheese-for-wits".

On the morning of King Joffrey I Baratheon's marriage to Margaery Tyrell, the royal court has for breakfast a Dornish dish of onions, cheese, fiery peppers, and chopped eggs. Cheese-and-onion pies are later served during the wedding feast.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Tyrion Lannister thinks of Illyrio Mopatis, a cheesemonger and magister of Pentos, as the "lord of cheese". Tyrion is served foul-smelling cheeses while staying at Illyrio's manse.

While staying in the cave of the three-eyed crow, Bran Stark and his companions have cheese and milk from the goats living in the caverns.
