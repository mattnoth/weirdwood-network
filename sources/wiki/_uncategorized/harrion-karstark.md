Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Harrion Karstark

Harrion is a fierce young man with a beard. He wears a black cloak patterned with white suns.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Harrion accompanies his father, Lord Rickard, and his two brothers, Eddard and Torrhen, to Winterfell when Robb Stark calls the banners in response to the arrest of Eddard Stark in King's Landing. Harrion bows respectfully when welcomed by Bran Stark.

When the northern host reaches the Twins and splits, Harrion is placed under the command of Lord Roose Bolton and marches down the kingsroad. He is captured during the battle on the Green Fork by a hedge knight.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Tyrion Lannister, acting Hand of the King, offers to trade Harrion and Ser Wylis Manderly for Willem Lannister in an exchange of prisoners.

Lord Tywin Lannister holds captives from the battle on the Green Fork at the Tower of Dread in Harrenhal. Arya Stark observes Harrion walking the battlements alone.

After Tywin departs Harrenhal, the castle is taken by Lord Roose Bolton, a bannermen of Robb Stark, King in the North.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Roose sends Harrion, Robett Glover, Ser Helman Tallhart, and other northerners to take Duskendale. They are defeated by Lord Randyll Tarly in the battle at Duskendale, however, and the survivors are attacked by Ser Gregor Clegane while retreating to the kingsroad.

King Robb executes Lord Rickard Karstark for treason after he murders the captive Willem Lannister and Tion Frey at Riverrun. Robb fears this will make him an enemy of Harrion, Rickard's heir. Robb is betrayed by the Freys and Boltons in the Red Wedding.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Harrion is held captive at Maidenpool.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Harrion remains a captive of the Lannisters at Maidenpool.

Arnolf Karstark declares for Stannis Baratheon, hoping that when the Iron Throne learns of this they will execute Harrion, thus making Harrion's sister Alys the heir to Karhold. Arnolf's plan is to wed his son Cregan to Alys and thereby claim Karhold. Arnolf's declaration for Stannis is also a plot orchestrated alongside Roose Bolton; the plan is to turn against Stannis once his forces reach Winterfell.

Arnolf's plot to claim Karhold is ruined when Jon Snow, the new Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, arranges a marriage between Alys and Sigorn, the new Magnar of Thenn. Jon has Cregan imprisoned at Castle Black and has Clydas send a raven to Deepwood Motte in an attempt to warn Stannis of Arnolf's treachery. Cregan, who believes Harrion to be dead, is outraged at Alys marrying a wildling.

He belonged to Lord Tywin, but the fierce, bearded young man who liked to walk the battlements alone in a black cloak patterned with white suns had been taken by some hedge knight who meant to get rich off him. Brynden: Lord Karstark's heir was at Harrenhal as well. The eldest son, the one the Lannisters took captive on the Green Fork.

Robb: Harrion. His name is Harrion. A king had best know the names of his enemies, don't you think?

Brynden: You know that for a certainty? That this will make young Karstark your enemy?

Robb: What else would he be? I am about to kill his father, he's not like to thank me.

Brynden: He might. There are sons who hate their fathers, and in a stroke you will make him Lord of Karhold.

Robb: Even if Harrion were that sort, he could never openly forgive his father's killer. His own men would turn on him. These are northmen, Uncle. The north remembers.

—Brynden Tully and Robb Stark

—Roose Bolton to Jaime Lannister
