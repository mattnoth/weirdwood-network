Layout[ ]

Hardin's Tower has a broken battlement, from which stone has spilled into the yard below, and a lean.

History[ ]

According to Jon Snow, Hardin's Tower has been in disrepair for a hundred years.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Jon Snow's cells are located in Hardin's Tower when he begins training with the Night's Watch. After swearing his vows and becoming a steward, he moves to the Lord Commander's Tower to aid Jeor Mormont. After that tower is burned, however, Jon returns to his old cells in Hardin's Tower.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Jon sees old snow laying in drifts against Hardin's Tower when he returns to Castle Black after being beyond the Wall.

Big Boil attacks the door of Hardin's Tower with his axe during the attack on Castle Black, but Mully fires an arrow into his leg and forces the wildling to retreat.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Jon, now Lord Commander of the Night's Watch after Jeor's death, moves into Donal Noye's rooms behind the armory.

Hardin's Tower becomes the residence of nineteen wildling women and girls who accept Jon's call. Some of the men of the Night's Watch attempt to take advantage of the women there; although a dozen spearwives turn them away, the men hostilely refer to the building as "Harlot's Tower". Jon has his own men guard the tower, and the giant Wun Weg Wun Dar Wun sleeps in its entryway. Jon has the top floor of Hardin's Tower prepared for Val.

Wun Wun kills Ser Patrek of King's Mountain, who was smitten with Val, outside Hardin's Tower. When Jon comes to investigate the commotion, he is betrayed in the mutiny at Castle Black.
