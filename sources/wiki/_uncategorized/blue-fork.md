The riverlands and the location of the Blue Fork

The Blue Fork is one of the three main rivers of the Trident in the riverlands, the other two being the Green Fork and the Red Fork. The Blue Fork is named for the purity of its waters.

Course[ ]

The Blue Fork has its source in pure springs and marshy terrain south of Seagard and the Twins. In order to get around the Blue Fork when it swells its banks, one must travel through Sevenstreams and Hag's Mire.

The Blue Fork flows to the southeast past Oldstones, Fairmarket, and Ramsford, and it meets the Green Fork north of Lord Harroway's Town. The Trident then flows into the Bay of Crabs.

History[ ]

  

Arrec Durrandon watches the battle at Fairmarket, by Marc Simonetti ©

King Tristifer IV Mudd, the Hammer of Justice, ruled from a castle, now called Oldstones, which overlooked the Blue Fork.

According to a semi-canon source, the blue in the sigil of House Strong referred to the Blue Fork.

Harwyn Hoare, King of the Iron Islands, began his conquest of the riverlands by landing one hundred longships south of Seagard and marching them overland to the Blue Fork. His ironborn then used rivers to conquer the riverlands from the Storm King, Arrec Durrandon, with their greatest victory being the battle at Fairmarket.

The wooden bridge across the Blue Fork at Fairmarket is newer and larger than a bridge near Oldstones.

Ser Pate of the Blue Fork takes his title from the river.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

After departing the Twins, the army of Robb Stark sweeps around the headwaters of the Blue Fork and travels to the Whispering Wood. Robb's host is joined by small lords, hedge knights, and masterless men-at-arms.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Robb's army travels north from Riverrun toward the Twins for the wedding of Lord Edmure Tully to Roslin Frey. The Blue Fork is flooding its banks, however, with rising waters washing out the bridges at Fairmarket and Oldstones. When Galbart Glover and two others try to cross at Ramsford, two horses and one man are drowned and Galbart clings to a rock until being rescued. Robb's host thus rides around the Blue Fork, passing the ruins of Oldstones, rills and brooks near Sevenstreams, and the bogs of Hag's Mire. 

Following the Red Wedding, Merrett Frey rides to Oldstones to ransom his kinsman Petyr, but both Freys are killed by the brotherhood without banners.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Lucias Vypren and Black Walder Frey hunt the outlaws of the brotherhood along the Blue Fork.
