Route[ ]

The Goldroad begins at the Lion Gate on the western wall of King's Landing in the crownlands. The Map of the South from A Dance with Dragons, the "Westeros" map from The Lands of Ice and Fire, and the "Riverlands" map from The World of Ice & Fire depict the Goldroad running west and crossing the river which connects the Gods Eye and the Blackwater Rush, and southeast of Stoney Sept the Goldroad then crosses south of the Blackwater itself.

In contrast, "The Known World" map from The Lands of Ice and Fire, depicts the Goldroad crossing south of the Blackwater soon after leaving King's Landing (thereby avoiding the Gods Eye river altogether) and then remaining south of the Blackwater during its route west. "The West" map from The Lands of Ice and Fire, however, presents the road as crossing the Blackwater near King's Landing, returning north of the Blackwater after bypassing the Gods Eye river, and then crossing south of the Blackwater a final time before continuing its westward journey.

The Goldroad next runs through open plains in the northern Reach.

The castle of Deep Den guards the Goldroad as it passes through the southeastern hills of the westerlands. The road ends at Lannisport and Casterly Rock, seat of House Lannister.

History[ ]

The goldroad was made during the reign of King Jaehaerys I Targaryen, who was aided by his master of coin, Lord Martyn Tyrell. Its route passes near the Field of Fire.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

After Lord Eddard Stark is wounded in the streets of King's Landing, Ser Jaime Lannister flees the capital and takes the Goldroad to Casterly Rock.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Ser Brynden Tully tells his nephew, Ser Edmure Tully, that Brynden and Robb Stark, King in the North, had wanted to fortify a position on the gold road against Lord Tywin Lannister. Edmure's defeat of Tywin in the Battle of the Fords nullified their plan, however.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Ser Kevan Lannister takes the Goldroad from Casterly Rock to King's Landing to become the regent for King Tommen I Baratheon.
