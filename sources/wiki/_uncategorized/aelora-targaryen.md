History[ ]

Aelora was married to her twin brother, Prince Aelor Targaryen. When their father, Prince Rhaegel Targaryen, died in 215 AC, Aelor was named Prince of Dragonstone and heir to the Iron Throne by their uncle, King Aerys I Targaryen. As Rhaegel was often disregarded on account of his mental fragility, both he and his children were sometimes forgotten as heirs to Aerys who came ahead of Aelora's uncle, Prince Maekar Targaryen. Those more knowledgeable of the true line of succession were quick to point out that Rhaegel, Aelor, and Aelora all preceded Maekar.

During the reign of Aerys I, his Hand of the King, Lord Brynden Rivers, was the subject of much mistrust. Bloodraven's detractors worried that nobody in House Targaryen was fit to stand up to him This anti-Bloodraven sentiment ran so deep that some even suggested Rhaegel and his children might be killed by his magic. Even Prince Maekar was not free of such suspicions, though his youngest son, Aegon Targaryen, was quick to protest that Maekar loved his brother and would never harm him or his children.

In 217 AC, Aelora accidentally caused Aelor's death in a "grotesque mishap", which left her mad with grief. Following Aelor's death, King Aerys recognized Aelora as his new heir to the throne. Some time later, three men known as the Rat, the Hawk, and the Pig attacked Aelora at a masked ball, after which she committed suicide.
