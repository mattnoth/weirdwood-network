History[ ]

  

Lord Edmyn Tully, from Game of Thrones Blu-ray

During Aegon's Conquest, House Tully of Riverrun were the first of the rivermen support Aegon I Targaryen against Harren Hoare, King of the Isles and the Rivers. After Harren's death in the burning of Harrenhal, Aegon the Conqueror rewarded Lord Edmyn Tully by granting him the riverlands and naming him Lord Paramount of the Trident.

Although the Tullys have long been powerful river lords, the domains of Riverrun yield smaller armies than those of the Blackwoods, Brackens, Freys, and Vances. The Tully lineage was less proud than that of the Mallisters, and they were not as wealthy as as the Mootons. Riverrun is a strong castle, but it is significantly smaller than ruined Harrenhal. The Tullys have had difficulty at times in managing the quarrelsome river lords.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

With Hoster Tully, Lord Paramount of the Trident, ailing at Riverrun, his heir, Ser Edmure Tully, supports Robb Stark, the newly-crowned King in the North and King of the Trident.

After the assassination of King Renly Baratheon at Storm's End, Lord Petyr Baelish and Tyrion Lannister, the acting Hand of the King, plot to form an alliance with House Tyrell to win over any lords from the Reach or the stormlands who did not immediately declare for King Stannis Baratheon after Renly's death. Petyr volunteers to travel to Bitterbridge to broker the deal, and then in Highgarden he is successful in forging a Lannister-Tyrell alliance through the engagement of King Joffrey I Baratheon and Margaery Tyrell, the daughter of Mace Tyrell, Lord Paramount of the Mander.

Petyr returns to King's Landing with Tyrell forces alongside those of Lord Tywin Lannister. The allied armies are able to take Stannis's forces in the rear in the last hours of the Battle of the Blackwater, with Petyr providing the idea to dress Ser Garlan Tyrell in the late King Renly's armor during the battle so as to frighten Stannis's men-at-arms.

After the battle, Petyr is rewarded with the title Lord Paramount of the Trident and is made the new liege lord over the riverlands according to the Iron Throne. Petyr is also made Lord of Harrenhal, although the castle is still held by Lord Roose Bolton in Robb's name. Though Petyr has not set foot in Harrenhal, the title gives him nominal control over the war-torn riverlands and elevates his rank considerably.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

  

Lord Hoster Tully's funeral in the Red Fork, by Jauma ©

Hoster Tully passes away at Riverrun and his successor, Lord Edmure Tully, is attainted by the Iron Throne after being captured at the Red Wedding.

In addition to becoming Lord Paramount of the Trident, Petyr Baelish becomes Lord Protector of the Vale after wedding Lady Lysa Arryn.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Having been named Lord of Riverrun by the Iron Throne, Ser Emmon Frey assumes that he is now the liege of the rivermen, including even his own father, Lord Walder Frey. At the siege of Riverrun, however, Ser Jaime Lannister explains to Emmon that Lord Petyr Baelish of Harrenhal is Lord Paramount.
