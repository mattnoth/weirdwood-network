Culture[ ]

House Caswell includes a centaur in their sigil. Orbert Caswell, Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, was known as the Black Centaur.

History[ ]

A realm of centaurs supposedly existed in the eastern grasslands of central Essos during the Dawn Age. Archmaester Hagedorn has put forth the theory that these "centaurs" were no more than mounted warriors, as perceived by neighboring tribes who had not yet learned to tame and ride horses. His views have become widely accepted at the Citadel, despite the purported "centaur skeletons" that turn up in grotesqueries from time to time.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Daenerys Targaryen thinks that her khas and her handmaid Irri ride as fluid as centaurs.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Lady Barbrey Dustin compares the late Brandon and Lyanna Stark to centaurs because of their fondness for riding horses.

A yellow centaur was painted on his shield, pulling on a longbow. The same centaur adorned the white silk trappings of his horse, and gleamed atop his helm in yellow gold. A man who has a centaur for his sigil should ride better than that.
