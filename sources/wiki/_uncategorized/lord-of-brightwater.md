History[ ]

Lord Florent hired Ser Arlan of Pennytree and his squire, Duncan, in his service for half of the year 206 AC.

Lord Alester Florent was born at Brightwater Keep. He was once unhorsed in a tourney by Ser Denys Mallister.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Lord Alester follows House Tyrell in supporting Lord Renly Baratheon as king at the start of the War of the Five Kings. After Renly's death, Alester rallies his banners and is the first of those who swore to Renly to go over to Stannis Baratheon's cause. Lord Alester quickly converts to the worship of R'hllor, just like his niece and Stannis's wife, Selyse Florent, did before him. He is named Stannis's Hand mostly due to his connection through his niece.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

For their support of Stannis, the Florents are stripped of Brightwater Keep by the Iron Throne. Lord Alester tries to make peace with House Lannister by having Stannis give up his claim to the Iron Throne in exchange for him being named Lord of Storm's End and Dragonstone, with Florent vowing to do the same in exchange for Brightwater Keep and all his lands. For this treason, he is imprisoned in Dragonstone's dungeons, placed in the same cell as Ser Davos Seaworth. When Ser Davos is retrieved from the dungeons, Alester's brother, Ser Axell Florent, denounces him as a traitor and takes the torch from his cell.

The Iron Throne raises Ser Garlan Tyrell, the second son of Lord Mace Tyrell, to Lord of Brightwater Keep as a reward for the Battle of Blackwater.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Lord Alester is reported to have been sacrificed by Melisandre to R'hllor as a traitor. His son Alekyne succeeds him as the attained Lord of Brightwater Keep, though he has fled to Oldtown.

Garlan is preparing men to press his claim on Brightwater Keep, which is held by Ser Colin Florent, when news of the ironborn's taking of the Shields reaches him. He plans on gathering men to retake the islands instead of marching on Brightwater Keep.
