Appearance[ ]

The woman had yellow hair.

History[ ]

The woman worked in an alehouse and would bring home drunks from the establishment. She had a black-haired bastard named Gendry with King Robert I Baratheon, though she never revealed to the boy the identity of his father. She sometimes sang to Gendry but died when the boy was little.

Lord Jon Arryn asked Gendry about his mother during the Hand of the King's investigation into the parentage of Robert's various children.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Lord Eddard Stark, the new Hand of the King, learns from Gendry that Lord Jon Arryn had asked about the apprentice's mother.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Gendry has become a blacksmith at the crossroads inn during the War of the Five Kings. Since the youth resembles the late King Robert I Baratheon and the late Lord Renly Baratheon, Brienne of Tarth asks him about his parents.

Gendry: He asked me questions is all, m'lord.

Eddard: What sort of questions?

Gendry: How was I, and was I well treated, and if I liked the work, and stuff about my mother. Who she was and what she looked like and all.

Gendry: She died when I was little. She had yellow hair, and sometimes she used to sing to me, I remember. She worked in an alehouse.
