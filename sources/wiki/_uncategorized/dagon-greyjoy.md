History[ ]

By 211 AC, Lord Dagon and his ironborn were raiding along the Sunset Sea and the western coast of Westeros. They burned Little Dosk, carried off half the wealth of Fair Isle and a hundred women, and raided as far south as the Arbor. The ironmen also also attacked the north, so Lord Beron Stark gathered swords in an attempt to drive them off. Dagon and his forces retreated to the sea and Pyke whenever his adversaries retaliated on land, so Lord Tybolt Lannister began building ships for an attack on the Iron Islands.

Erik Ironmaker sailed with Dagon. The Lord of the Iron Islands frustrated the Starks and the Lannisters for some time, but the Last Reaver was eventually defeated by House Targaryen.

Lord Dagon and Dagon's Feast, longships of the Iron Fleet, are named after Dagon.

Recent Events[ ]

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Euron Greyjoy, King of the Isles and the North, sends the ironborn to attack the Reach for the first time since Dagon's era.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Asha Greyjoy's cousin, Dagon the Drunkard, was named after Lord Dagon.

Victarion Greyjoy recalls his ancestor Dagon while sailing with the Iron Fleet to Meereen.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

Dagon's name may be a reference to the sea god Dagon from H.P. Lovecraft's Cthulhu Mythos (which in turn is inspired by the Levantine deity Dagon, who was erroneously associated with fish in 19th and 20th century scholarship).
