## Full Text

Several printings of the collected graphic novel adaptation of The Hedge Knight include a roll of arms of the attendees of the tourney at Ashford Meadow among the supplements after the story. The roll differs between the editions of the trade paperback.

A number of the listed characters and/or their arms may be considered to be semi-canon, as they are not named or described in the original novella, or are only seen via their House sigils by Ser Duncan the Tall. The details given for several characters in the roll of arms have been confirmed by later inclusion in The World of Ice & Fire and other supplementary sources.

Additionally, the creators of the graphic novel adaptation appear as knights in some versions of the roll.

Roll of Arms[ ]

The first edition of the trade paperback (Devil's Due Publishing, 2004) has four pages of arms, along with an introduction page. The second edition (Marvel Comics, 2006), reduced the roll of arms to only two pages, the first and last, although some important figures from the central pages were moved to the last page. The Jet City edition (2013) reprints the Marvel two-page version of the roll.

Variant arms[ ]

The following characters are depicted in the roll of arms with variant shields which are not officially described in the novellas, but are heraldically differenced from the standard sigils of their original Houses.

 Ser Robert Ashford, a white border is shown around the Ashford arms

 Robert Blackwood, two white trees on red

 Roland Blackwood, three bare white trees on red

 Roger Blackwood, four black ravens on red

 Ser Tybolt Lannister, a gold border is shown around the Lannister arms

 Ser Howard Pyle, grey roundels surround the grey iron greathelm

 Ser Daeron Targaryen, the Targaryen dragon is shown in reverse, facing right

 Ser Valarr Targaryen, a red border is shown around the Targaryen arms

Notes[ ]

↑     The Blackwoods are the only characters not titled "Ser" in the roll of arms, as they would not be knights due to the House Blackwood tradition of worshiping the old gods. As the Ashford Tourney was reserved for knights only, according to George R.R. Martin (cited by Elio Garcia) allowances were made for the Blackwoods because of their high birth.

↑      It appears likely that Tommen Costayne, Alyn Garner, Howard Pyle, and Corwyn Rogers are named and their arms designed in tribute to the works of authors Thomas B. Costain, Alan Garner, Howard Pyle, and Roger Zelazny. Compare House Deddings, House Jordayne, and House Willum.

↑ Franklyn Frey was also a competitor at the later wedding tourney at Whitewalls.

↑ The first edition's roll of arms has Walder of Woodmere's sigil as a blue plate on white, similar to the arms of House Poole. However, in later editions his sigil is a grey castle on blue beneath a red bend sinister (same as the arms of Walder Rivers), suggesting that Walder of Woodmere was a bastard of House Frey.

↑    This is also differenced from the comic's main story, where the character is depicted using their family's standard sigil.

↑    The use of a portion of the motifs of the sigil of House Blackwood, and the numbers of each depicted on the shield, likely indicates that Robert, Roland, and Roger Blackwood are second, third, and fourth sons of their lines, similar to the personal shield devices of Ser Garlan Tyrell and Ser Loras Tyrell, which display two and three of the golden rose of House Tyrell, respectively. The differing use of weirwood trees and ravens may indicate that Roger Blackwood is from a separate collateral line than the others.
