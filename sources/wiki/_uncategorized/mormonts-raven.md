Appearance and Character[ ]

See also: Images of Mormont's raven

The raven of Jeor Mormont has shrewd and beady black eyes and big black wings. Jon Snow describes it as an "old raven", though its exact age remains unknown. It has been described as huge and scruffy. It seems to be rather large for its species.

The raven can speak, often repeating one or two words. Typically it is something recently said by people nearby, but it is often uncannily appropriate.

This raven is particularly fond of fruit and corn, eating kernels out of Jeor's hand; the Lord Commander keeps corn in his pocket. Maester Aemon considers this a rather rare trait, since most ravens prefer meat. Jon considers it bold, fearless, and clever.

The raven flies to Jeor when he whistles, and sometimes walks across the Lord Commander's shoulders. The bird quorks when Jeor strokes his head with his thumb.

History[ ]

The raven has served as the companion of Jeor Mormont for "long years".

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Jeor Mormont with his raven, by Amok ©

At Castle Black, Alliser Thorne orders recent recruit Jon Snow to report to Jeor Mormont, Lord Commander of the Night's Watch. Jon first notices the raven perched on the arm of Jeor. The Lord Commander gives Jon a letter that has arrived from Winterfell for him. The message was written by Robb Stark, informing Jon that Bran Stark has recovered from a coma. Though the boy is crippled, Jon is happy that his brother is going to live. The raven flies to the shoulder of Jeor, crying "Live! Live!"

During a meal, Alliser and Tyrion Lannister insult each other. Bystanders laugh, while the raven caws loudly from above a window, repeating the last word of Tyrion: Duel! Tyrion finds the bird annoying and privately considers throttling it. He decides against it, not wanting to insult Jeor.

Later, Jon is forced to fight the wight of Othor. Jon improvises and uses Jeor's oil lamp to start a fire. The raven starts cawing Burn, seemingly on its own initiative. When Jeor rewards Jon with Longclaw, the raven repeats many words of their conversation. Jeor thinks poorly of the boiled mutton prepared by Three-Finger Hobb, and the raven refuses to touch grey haunch.

After Jon unsuccessfully attempts to desert the Night's Watch to avenge his late father, Lord Eddard Stark, he brings Jeor's breakfast to the Lord Commander the next morning. The raven pecks at an egg and is fed some of Jeor's ham. Jeor asks whether Jon finds the war of Robb Stark to be more important than their own war. The raven flaps its wings and starts repeating War. Jon finds the words to be song-like. Jeor suggests that Eddard himself sent Jon to the Wall, though nobody truly knows why. The raven repeats the question: Why?

A Clash of Kings[ ]

The raven is present with Jeor when Jon meets with the Lord Commander in his solar.

Lord Commander Mormont brings his raven with him while commanding the great ranging, and it continues to repeat various words of Jeor's conversations. It appears to be grumpy when soaked at Craster's Keep. The raven screams when Qhorin Halfhand reports that Mance Rayder is searching for something in the Frostfangs.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Chett and others on the ranging plan to mutiny and murder Jeor. Small Paul is convinced to assist once he is told he can have Mormont's raven afterwards.

The raven survives the fight at the Fist and accompanies his master to Craster's Keep. Jeor is mortally wounded during the mutiny which takes place at Craster's, and the raven is present when Jeor gives his dying wish for his son Jorah to take the black. When Craster's wives urge Samwell Tarly to quickly leave with Gilly to escape Craster's sons, the raven repeats their instructions.

During a pause in the battle beneath the Wall, Jon Snow is sent to meet with Mance Rayder. As Jon travels to Mance's tent, he sees a raven eating the brain of a slain giant. The bird screams Snow at Jon and flies away.

In the aftermath of Mance's capture by Stannis Baratheon, elections for the position of Lord Commander of the Night's Watch are held at Castle Black in the cellar beneath the armory. No candidate has enough votes to secure election, and a new vote is taken. A large kettle, used as the ballot box, is brought to the hall's table by Samwell and Clydas. When Clydas takes off the lid of the kettle, a raven bursts out from inside. As it flies around the cellar three times, Samwell says he recognizes the bird, proclaiming it to be Mormont's raven. The raven lands on the table, next to the place where Jon is seated. Repeating the word snow, it spreads its wings and flies to Jon, perching on his shoulder. Ser Alliser Thorne, believing that Samwell is trying to fool them, is certain that this is one of several ravens which Samwell has trained to say snow, while Mormont's raven had a richer vocabulary. The raven looks at Jon and asks for corn. It soon changes its tune, repeating a new word: Kettle? This convinces the men that this is indeed Mormont's raven, and helps decide the election in favor of Jon. Samwell insists that he had nothing to do with the raven's appearance, claiming that it even scared him.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

  

Jon Snow with Ghost and the raven, by Michael Komarck ©

The raven dwells with Jon Snow, the new Lord Commander,in his quarters. When Samwell offers kernels of corn to the bird, it aggressively pecks at his hand.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

The raven wakes Jon up in the morning asking for corn. According to Jon, the raven ate Jeor's face after he died.

When Jon goes to visit Mole's Town, the raven awaits him on a chestnut tree carved with a face. The bird flaps down when Jon whistles.

Jon calls the raven a thief after it makes off with his breakfast bacon. The bird is present when Jon meets with Tycho Nestoris.

Jon has a dream in which he defends the Wall from wildlings and wights, and he wakes to find himself in bed with Mormont's raven pecking at his chest and calling him Snow. As Jon dresses, the raven keeps some distance from him and mutters the familiar words Corn, King, and Snow. When the raven twice says Jon Snow, Jon finds this peculiar, as he cannot remember any previous instance of the raven using his full name.

Mormont's raven seems agitated at the same time as Ghost, Jon's direwolf. The raven attends Jon's meeting with Tormund Giantsbane, during which Tormund jokes with Jon about receiving the bird as a gift. After speaking in the Shieldhall, Jon is stabbed in the mutiny at Castle Black.
