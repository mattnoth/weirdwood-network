Geography[ ]

Ulthos lies south of Asshai and the Shadow Lands of eastern Essos, with the Saffron Straits separating the landmasses. Several isles, including Ulos, are situated in these straits. Sothoryos is west of Ulthos across the Jade Sea.

Little is known about Ulthos, with some considering it a continent like Westeros and others reporting it to be a large island. According to cartographer Jonathan Roberts, illustrator of The Lands of Ice and Fire, Ulthos has a purple-black jungle. An unnamed river with at least one tributary runs through the jungle of northwestern Ulthos.

History[ ]

Merchants have dreamed of reaching Ulthos and Asshai by sailing west from Westeros. Elissa Farman believed there were other lands and seas located between Westeros and the far eastern shores of Ulthos and Essos.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

According to George R. R. Martin, the ambiguity over whether Ulthos is a separate continent or a big island off Essos highlights how people from Westeros do not know much about the Further East. Ulthos might be a reference to Ultima Thule, a name used on ancient and medieval maps to signify barely-known lands at the edge of charts. Ultima Thule is Latin for "Farthest Thule", and Ulthos is the farthest land that is known to people of Westeros.
