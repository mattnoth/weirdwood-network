Folkore[ ]

Snarks are supposed to live beyond the Wall with grumkins. In derision of the apparent futility of the Wall and the mission of the Night's Watch, the snarks are often referenced as an improbable danger that is hiding in the haunted forest. They are known to be elusive and some may have beards.

Adults no longer believe in snarks and consider them imaginary beings, comparable to legendary animals and supernatural entities or the monsters which children's wet nurses warn them against.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Tyrion Lannister jokes about grumkins and snarks to Jon Snow.

Jeor Mormont, Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, describes his sister Maege, Lady of Bear Island, as a hoary old snark to Jon.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Lord Varys disbelieves Ser Alliser Thorne's story of wights, thinking them as non-existent as grumkins and snarks.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

The small council of King Tommen I Baratheon mocks the mission of the Night's Watch, with Qyburn saying they defend against grumkins and snarks.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

Snarks may be a homage by George R. R. Martin to The Hunting of the Snark by Lewis Carroll.
