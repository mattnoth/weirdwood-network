## Full Text

"Lickspittle" in Westeros refers to people who behave obsequiously to those in power. They are also refer to as "sheep" or "cronies".

Lickspittles flater and agree with people they seek favors from. They usually give self-interest and bad counsel to their supperiors instead of honest ones.

History[ ]

The Faith Militant mockingly dubbed the High Septon "the High Lickspittle" for he never spoke out against Maegor the Cruel's actions towards the pious during their uprising.

Maegor's fourteen-year-old nephew, Prince Jaehaerys, succeeded him as King Jaehaerys I Targaryen. Various lords visited Jaehaerys on Dragonstone during his minority to plead forgiveness for having supported Maegor and to curry favor with the young king. This angered Jaehaerys's Hand, Lord Rogar Baratheon, who refer to them as "lords lickspittle". Those lords were:

 

Lord Albin Massey of Stonedance

Lord Staunton of Rook's Rest

Lord Darklyn of Duskendale

Lord Bar Emmon of Sharp Point

Lord Mooton of Maidenpool

Lord Stokeworth of Stokeworth

Young Lord Rosby of Rosby

Lord Harte

Lord Rollingford

When the question of inheritance for King Aegon II Targaryen was raised by his council of regents, lickspittles showed interest to Aegon's sisters and presumed heirs, Princesses Baela and Rhaena Targaryen.

King Aegon IV Targaryen is regarded as one of the worst kings in the history of Westeros, a corrupt and weak ruler. He  filled his court with men who had the ability to amuse and flatter him. It took more than a year for his successor, King Daeron II Targaryen, to clean house in court and in the City Watch, and repair the damages his father's misrule did.

During the final years of the reign of King Aerys II Targaryen, the royal court divided itself into two factions; one supporting Aerys and the other supporting the crown prince, Rhaegar Targaryen. Whereas people generally supported Rhaegar out of friendship and his charisma, Aerys's supporters mainly did it for their own interests. Among Aerys's supporters, whom Maester Yandel dubs "lickspittle lords", were:

 

Lord Qarlton Chelsted

Lord Lucerys Velaryon of Driftmark

Lord Symond Staunton of Rook's Rest

Lord Owen Merryweather of Longtable

Lord Rykker of Duskendale

Varys

Wisdom Rossart

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Stannis Baratheon values the counsel of his onion knight, Ser Davos Seaworth, as Davos always tells him the truth unlike his own bannermen.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Cersei Lannister considers Paxter Redwyne, Lord of the Arbor, and Mathis Rowan, Lord of Goldengrove, to be Mace Tyrell's lickspittles.

Harys Swyft, the Knight of Cornfield, Ser Emmon Frey, and Grand Maester Pycelle are Tywin Lannister's lickspittles.
