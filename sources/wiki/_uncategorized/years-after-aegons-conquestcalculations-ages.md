## Full Text

Due to editing issues caused by the length of the calculations, the list is divided into four pages. Use the navigation box, or navigate here to page 2, page 3 or page 4. You are currently on page 1.

In the main series and novellas, George R.R. Martin on most occasions does not state specifically in which year a character has been born or has died. This page lists the calculations to determine such years, years of birth mostly, but also years of death at times, as specific as is possible, for all characters for all this is possible.
This information then can be used in articles and serves to give a more complete image of the world of A Song of Ice and Fire.

The following guidelines are used for the calculations:

A character, both male and female, will be at least 12 years old at the birth of his or her first born child, for the following reason: a girl who has had her first flowering (i.e. first menstruation) is considered to be fit for both marriage and consummation of the marriage. Most highborn girls have their first flowering at the age of twelve or thirteen, indicating twelve as the youngest a girl can be for getting pregnant, and, as such, give birth. For boys, the same minimum age is used. Furthermore, we are given the precedence of the age of twelve for both males and females: the daughter of Unwin Peake died in childbirth at the age of twelve, while Viserys II Targaryen impregnated his wife at the age of twelve.

Siblings are assumed to be at least one year apart in age, taking into consideration that a woman who has just given birth will not immediately become pregnant again. This is only when discussing full-blood siblings. For siblings with the same father, but with a different mother, no such recovery period is required.

Where historic works are concerned which have been written by maesters, or sources recited from works written by maesters, it can be assumed that the age of a character stated in a year is the age that character would be turning that year. For example, Maegor I Targaryen was born in 12 AC. In historic works, his age in the year 25 AC will be stated as thirteen (the age he would be turning that year), without the author taking into account whether or not the described event took place before or after Maegor's nameday. This goes for historical works (i.e. The Sons of the Dragon, The Rogue Prince, The Princess and the Queen, Fire & Blood, and The World of Ice & Fire), not for POV-information, like A Game of Thrones and its sequels, or The Hedge Knight, and other tales of Dunk & Egg. Do note that on some occasions, this rule might not apply.

The following minimum ages for pages, squires, and knights are used, based on an overview of known ages:

Boys are considered to be at least six years old when becoming a page.

Boys are considered to be at least eight years old when becoming a squire.

Men are considered to be at least fifteen years old when becoming a knight.

A mother will be no older than ~52 years old at the earliest possible birth year of her youngest child, for the following reason: the Westerosi consider the age of forty-two to be "past [a woman's] childbearing years", and forty-four to be "well past her childbearing years", although women of forty-four have been known to give birth to a child. In real-life, the end of a woman's fertile cycle (menopause) occurs at an age of 49-52 on average, giving ~52 as a maximum age for giving birth to a child.

The characters are listed alphabetically, using full names. Maesters (like Aemon), will also be listed alphabetically under their birth names, unless those are not known.

Names that belong to several characters (for example, the name Walder Frey) will be listed alphabetically, but will have their nickname or relations listed in parentheses in accordance with their page name, so they can be distinguished from one another.

For calculations on placings of events, see Calculations of Events.

Addam Hightower[ ]

Birth[ ]

Addam Hightower was the father of Manfred Hightower, who was born at the latest in 10 BC (see Manfred Hightower (son of Addam)). Addam would have been at least twelve years old at the moment of Manfred's birth, meaning that 22 BC is the latest possible year of birth for Addam.

No further specifications can be given. Addam was thus born in or before 22 BC.

Death[ ]

Addam Hightower succeeded his father, Manfred Hightower, as Lord of the Hightower when Manfred died. Manfred died "soon after" Oldtown had been attacked in 10 AC. It is unknown how much time passed between the attack and Manfred's death. As 10 AC is the first possible moment for Manfred's death, Addam is known to have lived at least until 10 AC as well, placing his death no earlier than 10 AC.

Eventually, Addam was himself succeeded by his son, also named Manfred. Manfred already ruled Oldtown by 23 AC, demonstrating that Addam died no later than 23 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Addam thus died in or between 10 AC and 23 AC.

Addam Osgrey[ ]

Birth[ ]

Addam Osgrey was twelve years old in 196 AC. That gives him the following options:

11 turning 12 in 196 AC, placing his birth in 184 AC

12 turning 13 in 196 AC, placing his birth in 183 AC

No further specifications can be given. Addam was thus born in either 183 AC or 184 AC.

Aegon Blackfyre[ ]

Birth[ ]

Aegon Blackfyre was twelve years old when he died in 196 AC.
Being twelve years old in 196 AC gives the following options:

11 turning 12 in 196 AC, placing his birth in 184 AC

12 turning 13 in 196 AC, placing his birth in 183 AC

Aegon's father, Daemon Blackfyre, married Aegon's mother, Rohanne of Tyrosh, in 184 AC. That means that Aegon could not have been conceived earlier than 184 AC. 

This eliminates 183 AC as a possible birth year for Aegon, leaving only 184 AC.

Aegon Frey (son of Aenys)[ ]

Birth[ ]

Aegon Frey is at least one year older than his younger brother Rhaegar. Rhaegar was born no later than 274 AC (see Rhaegar Frey), meaning that Aegon was born no later than one year before, in 273 AC.

Aegon was the firstborn child of his father, Aenys Frey. Aenys was born no earlier than 238 AC (see Aenys Frey), meaning that Aegon was born no earlier than twelve years after 238 AC, placing his first possible moment of birth in 250 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Aegon was thus born in or between 250 AC and 273 AC.

Aegon Frey (son of Stevron)[ ]

Birth[ ]

Aegon Frey appears to be fifty years old in 299 AC. That would place his birth roughly in 249 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Aegon was thus born roughly in 249 AC.

Aegon V Targaryen[ ]

Birth[ ]

Aegon Targaryen was judged to be eight or nine in 209 AC. Aegon being eight or nine in 209 AC would give the following options:

7 turning 8 in 209 AC, placing his birth in 201 AC

8 turning 9 in 209 AC, placing his birth in 200 AC

9 turning 10 in 209 AC, placing his birth in 199 AC

Aegon was known to have been ten years old in 211 AC and eleven years old in 212 AC.
This leaves the following options:

9 turning 10 in 211 AC, and 10 turning 11 in 212 AC, placing his birth in 201 AC

10 turning 11 in 211 AC, and 11 turning 12 in 212 AC, placing his birth in 200 AC

In addition, Aegon is stated to have been twenty years old in 220 AC when he got married. As the last source of his age concerns a historical work, following rule #3, Aegon was born in 200 AC.

Aegon was thus born in 200 AC.

Aegon Targaryen (son of Gaemon)[ ]

Birth[ ]

Aegon Targaryen's eldest child, Maegon Targaryen, was born no later than 68 BC (see Maegon Targaryen). Aegon would have been at least twelve years old at the birth, he would have been born no later than 80 BC.

When Aegon's grandfather, Aenar Targaryen, fled Valyria for Dragonstone in 114 BC, he took with him all his kin, including his children. Aegon was not yet born at the time, therefore he would have been born no earlier than 114 BC.

No further specifications can be given. Aegon was thus born in or between 114 BC and 80 BC.

Aegon Targaryen (son of Rhaegar)[ ]

Birth[ ]

The World of Ice & Fire states that on the last day of 281 AC it began to snow in King's Landing, and that it took only a fortnight for the Blackwater to freeze. That caused King Aerys II to command the pyromancers "to drive the winter off with their magics", which led to the pyromancers burning their wildfire along the walls of the Red Keep for a month. It is then stated that Rhaegar Targaryen was not at King's Landing at that moment, nor was he at Dragonstone "with Princess Elia and their young son, Aegon", as he had taken to the road "with the coming of the new year". When exactly Rhaegar left is unclear. As such, it is known that Aegon Targaryen had been born by early 282 AC.

Elia Martell married Prince Rhaegar in 280 AC, and their first child, Rhaenys, was born later that same year. This means that Elia and Rhaegar were married within the first three months of the year, and Rhaenys was thus born within the last three months of the year. After Rhaenys' birth, Elia went on bedrest for half a year. With Rhaenys's birth within the last three months of the year, Elia's bedrest would have lasted until any point in either the fourth, fifth or sixth month of 281 AC. (Note that Elia's bedrest might not have lasted precisely six months.) Aegon will have been conceived after Elia's bedrest had ended, and with a nine month pregnancy, he will thus have been born no earlier than very late in the year 281 AC, and no later than very early 282 AC. Per the visions in the House of the Undying, Rhaegar was with Elia when he named Aegon. Rhaegar left Elia and Aegon in early 282 AC, thereby confirming that Aegon cannot have been born later than very early 282 AC.

When asked, The World of Ice & Fire co-author Elio Garcia stated that, in regards to the year of Aegon's birth, "George, too, is unclear on this point."

No further specifications can be given. Aegon was thus born in either late 281 AC or very early 282 AC.

Death[ ]

Aegon Targaryen died during the Sack of King's Landing. The Sack of King's Landing took place in 283 AC (see Robert's Rebellion/War of the Usurper).

Aegon thus died in 283 AC.

Aegor Rivers[ ]

Birth[ ]

Aegor Rivers is known to have been twenty-four years old at the Battle of the Redgrass Field in 196 AC. This would give Aegor the following options:

23 turning 24 in 196 AC, placing his birth in 172 AC

24 turning 25 in 196 AC, placing his birth in 171 AC

Aegor's mother, Barba Bracken, gave birth to Aegor after Aegon had ascended to the throne. Aegon ascended to the throne in 172 AC, ruling out 171 AC as a possible year of birth.

In addition, Aegor was born a fortnight before Princess Daenerys, who was born in 172 AC, and after Aegon IV had become king in 172 AC.

Aegor was thus born in 172 AC.

Death[ ]

Aegor Rivers was sixty-nine years old when he died. As Aegor had been born in 172 AC, following rule #3, this means that Aegor died in 241 AC.

Aelor Targaryen[ ]

Birth[ ]

Aelor Targaryen and his twin sister Aelora were the eldest children of Prince Rhaegel Targaryen, who was born in 173 AC at the earliest (see Rhaegel Targaryen). As Rhaegel will have been at least twelve years old at the birth of his twins, this places Aelor's birth no earlier than 185 AC. However, in 211 AC, Aelor and his twin are mentioned as Rhaegel's children, with the description of them still being "children", indicating they were still young at the time, and thus that Aelor had not yet reached manhood, therefore had not yet turned sixteen. As it is possible Aelor would turn sixteen later than year, this places his birth no earlier than 195 AC.

Aelor is known to have been born by 209 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Aelor was thus born in or between 195 AC and 209 AC.

Note: On first meeting Aerion Targaryen, who is about his own age, Duncan the Tall judges that he might be one of the sons of Baelor, Maekar, or Rhaegel Targaryen based on his age. Dunk was born in 193 AC (See the Duncan the Tall calculation), suggesting Aelor was likely born closer to 195 AC than 209 AC.

Aelora Targaryen[ ]

Birth[ ]

Aelora Targaryen and her twin brother Aelor were the eldest children of Prince Rhaegel Targaryen, who was born in 173 AC at the earliest (see Rhaegel Targaryen). As Rhaegel will have been at least twelve years old at the birth of his twins, this places Aelora's birth no earlier than 185 AC. However, in 211 AC, Aelora and her twin are mentioned as Rhaegel's children, with the description of them still being "children", indicating they were still young at the time, and thus that Aelora had not yet reached the age of majority, therefore had not yet turned sixteen. As it is possible Aelora would turn sixteen later than year, this places her birth no earlier than 195 AC.

Aelora's twin brother Aelor is known to have been born by 209 AC, meaning she too was born by that point as well.

No further specifications can be given. Aelora was thus born in or between 195 AC and 209 AC.

Note: On first meeting Aerion Targaryen, who is about his own age, Duncan the Tall judges that he might be one of the sons of Baelor, Maekar, or Rhaegel Targaryen based on his age. Dunk was born in 193 AC (See the Duncan the Tall calculation), suggesting Aelor was likely born closer to 195 AC than 209 AC. As Aelor's twin, the same would apply to Aelora.

Death[ ]

Aelora Targaryen outlived her brother-husband, Prince Aelor Targaryen, who died in 217 AC. However, she did not outlived her uncle, King Aerys I Targaryen, who died in 221 AC as Aerys's last heir before his death was his brother, Prince Maekar Targaryen.

No further specifications can be given. Aelora thus died in or between 217 AC and 221 AC.

Aelyx Targaryen[ ]

Birth[ ]

Aelyx Targaryen would have been at least one year older than his younger brother, Baelon Targaryen. As the latest possible year of birth for Baelon is 54 BC (see Baelon Targaryen (son of Aerys)), Aelyx would have been born no later than 55 BC.

Aelyx Targaryen was the first child of Lord Aerys Targaryen, who was born at the earliest in 101 BC (see Aerys Targaryen (son of Aegon)). Aerys would have been at least twelve years old at the moment of Aelyx's birth, meaning that 89 BC is the earliest possible year of birth for Aelyx.

No further specifications can be given. Aelyx was thus born in or between 89 BC and 55 BC.

Aemon Targaryen (son of Viserys II)[ ]

Cassana Estermont[ ]

Birth[ ]

Following rule #5, Cassana was no older than ~52 years old at the birth of her youngest child, Renly Baratheon, who was born in 277 AC (see Renly Baratheon). Therefore Cassana would have been born no earlier than ~225 AC.

Note: Cassana married Lord Steffon Baratheon, who was born in 246 AC. As one of the most prominent lords of Westeros, Steffon probably married a woman close to his age. Therefore Cassana birth year is likely closer to 250 AC than 225 AC.

Death[ ]

Cassana Estermont accompanied Steffon Baratheon when he went across the Narrow Sea on the orders of King Aerys II in 278 AC. They died on their way back. After their death, Tywin Lannister brought his daughter Cersei to court, at the age of 12. As Cersei was born in 266 AC, she would have been turning twelve in 278 AC, and would have been twelve turning thirteen in 279 AC.

Since Steffon and Cassana left on their mission in 278 AC, this means that Cassana died either in 278 AC or 279 AC.

Steffon and Cassana's mission was to find a bride for Prince Rhaegar Targaryen, in which they failed. Rhaegar was eventually betrothed to Elia Martell in early 279 AC, indicating that Steffon and Cassana's death could not have occurred later in that year.

No further specifications can be given. Cassana thus died in either 278 AC or early 279 AC.

Catelyn Tully[ ]

Birth[ ]

Catelyn Tully is two years older than her sister, Lysa Tully. At one point, Catelyn described a memory, during which she herself was no older than Sansa (who was 12 at that time), Lysa younger than Arya (who was 9, soon to turn 10) and Petyr Baelish even younger still. This indicates that Petyr was younger than Lysa by at least one year (otherwise, it would have stated that Petyr was the same age as Lysa had been at that point in time). 

Sansa and Arya are two years apart in age. However, looking at the moment during the year their namedays take place, it is clear that Arya's nameday falls early in the year, while Sansa's nameday falls at the end of the year. So even though they were born three calendar years apart, they actually only differ two years and a few months in age, which is then rounded to two years when people are talking about it.

Looking at Catelyn's memory, she states there that she and Lysa are further apart in age than Sansa and Arya, yet she and Lysa still qualify as being two years apart in age. So Catelyn and Lysa differ nearly two and a half years in age. Petyr was "younger still" during the memory, meaning he was at least one year younger than Lysa (otherwise they would have had the 'same age'). So between Catelyn and Petyr seems to be at least three years in age.

Petyr was born in 268 AC (see Petyr Baelish), and if Catelyn is at least three years older, that would place her birth no later than in 265 AC.

Amarei Crakehall died before Catelyn Tully was born. Amarei's son Merrett was born in 262 AC (See Merrett Frey). Amarei had two younger sons after Merrett, meaning that Amarei could not have died before 264 AC. As Catelyn could only have been born after Amarei's death, Catelyn cannot have been born before 264 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Catelyn Tully was thus born in either 264 AC or 265 AC.

Death[ ]

Catelyn dies during the Red Wedding, the news of which arrives at King's Landing before Joffrey's wedding, which will take place on the first day of 300 AC. The Red Wedding has thus occurred in 299 AC, meaning that Catelyn died in 299 AC.

Birth[ ]

Dyanna Dayne married Prince Maekar Targaryen and they had six children together. The eldest of those was Daeron Targaryen, who was born in 190 or 191 AC (see Daeron Targaryen (son of Maekar I)). Dyanna would have been at least twelve years old at the moment of Daeron's birth, meaning that 179 AC is the latest possible year of birth for Dyanna.

Following rule #5, Dyanna was no older than ~52 years old at the birth of her youngest child, Rhae Targaryen, who was born in or between 201 and 209 AC (see Rhae Targaryen). Therefore Dyanna would have been born no earlier than ~149 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Dyanna was thus born in or between ~149 AC and 179 AC.

Note: Maekar was born the fourth son of King Daeron II Targaryen in or between 174 and 179 AC (see Maekar I Targaryen). As a prince of the blood, Maekar probably married a woman close to his age. Therefore Dyanna birth year is likely closer to 179 AC than 149 AC.

Death[ ]

Dyanna Dayne is mentioned to have died by the time the Ashford Tourney takes place in 209 AC. This makes it possible that Dyanna had died earlier that same year, making 209 AC the last possible year of her death.

Dyanna's youngest child, Rhae, was born no earlier than 201 AC (see Rhae Targaryen). This means that Dyanna lived at least long enough to give birth to Rhae, making her first possible year of death 201 AC.

No further specifications can be given. Dyanna thus died in or between 201 AC and 209 AC.
