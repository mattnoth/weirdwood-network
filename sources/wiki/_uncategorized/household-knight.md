Duties[ ]

Knights in a household protect their master and can respond to emergencies.

Household knights and their families can associate with those of their lord. Some Kings of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men have used household knights and men-at-arms to protect members of the royal family instead of the Kingsguard.

Household knights can include sons of a noble house who have not yet come into their inheritance, such as Ser Horas and Ser Hobber Redwyne, the heirs of Lord Paxter Redwyne.

History[ ]

During the coming of the Andals to the Kingdom of the Reach, the Three Sage Kings took Andals into their service as household knights, such as Ser Alester Tyrell.

Ser Walton Towers was rewarded with Harrenhal by King Maegor I Targaryen after winning a melee between twenty-three of Maegor's knights in the streets of Harroway.

A daring wildling girl was fostered by one of the household knights of Lord Theomore Manderly.

Ser Laenor Velaryon was slain by one of his favorites, the household knight Ser Qarl Correy.

A score of knights in the household of Queen Rhaenyra Targaryen volunteered during the Sowing of the Seeds. According to Mushroom, the crippled King Aegon II Targaryen would watch his household knights couple with servants and ladies of his court.

The second mistress of Lord Tytos Lannister outraged his heir, Tywin Lannister, by giving orders to household knights. During the Reyne-Tarbeck revolt, Ser Tywin crushed Lord Walderan Tarbeck and his household knights with a much larger force.

Aerys II Targaryen took as mistress the pretty young daughter of one of his household knights. Following the death of his son Jaehaerys, Aerys accused his mistress of poisoning the child and had her and her family tortured to death.

Ser Colin Florent wed his daughter, Delena Florent, to Ser Hosman Norcross, one of his household knights, after the girl was deflowered by King Robert I Baratheon.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Queen Cersei Lannister has a dozen knights and a hundred men-at-arms under her command in King's Landing.

A Storm of Storms[ ]

A dozen household knights escort Lady Lysa Arryn to the Fingers.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Now Queen Regent, Cersei demeans her uncle, Ser Kevan Lannister, for having been a household knight in service to her late father, Lord Tywin Lannister, but Kevan explains he is still powerful because of his own incomes, accumulated wealth and base of support.

Ser Balman Byrch considers four knights taken into the household of Ser Bronn of the Blackwater to be no more than upjumped sellswords.

Ser Hyle Hunt is judged by the brotherhood without banners for being a household knight in service of Randyll Tarly.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Asha Greyjoy thinks that Ser Justin Massey, who has lost access to his ancestral lands through the War of the Five Kings, hopes to wed her and thereby improve his station, otherwise he will be no more than a household knight in the service of Stannis Baratheon.
