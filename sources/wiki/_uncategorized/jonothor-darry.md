Ser Jaime Lannister remembers Jonothor as an earnest man.

History[ ]

Jonothor was present at the tourney at Harrenhal in 281 AC.

After the Battle of the Bells during Robert's Rebellion in 283 AC, Jonothor and Ser Barristan Selmy of the Kingsguard were sent to rally the remnants of the forces commanded by Lord Jon Connington, the Hand of the King.

On the night that King Aerys II Targaryen burned his next Hand, Lord Qarlton Chelsted, Jon was standing watch outside the royal apartments with Ser Jaime Lannister when Aerys began raping his sister-wife, Queen Rhaella Targaryen. When Jaime stated they should protect her as well, Jon allowed that they should, but not from him.

Jonothor was one of Prince Rhaegar Targaryen's three Kingsguard who rode with him from the Red Keep to lead the royal army at the Battle of the Trident. Before departing, Jaime pleaded with Rhaegar to command Jon to remain at the Red Keep and guard Aerys in his stead. In response, Jon snapped at his younger sworn brother and reminded him of his duty.

Jonothor was slain at the Trident, cut down in the midst of the conflict.
