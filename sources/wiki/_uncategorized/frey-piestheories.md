## Summary

The Frey Pies Theory suggests that Wyman Manderly orders the killing of three Freys (Rhaegar, Symond, and Jared) who disappear on the way from White Harbor to Barrowton. According to the theory, Lord Manderly then has the men baked into the three huge pork pies he brings for the wedding of Ramsay Bolton and Arya Stark at Winterfell. The pies are described as "three great wedding pies, as wide across as wagon wheels, their flaky crusts stuffed to bursting with carrots, onions, turnips, parsnips, mushrooms, and chunks of seasoned pork swimming in a savory brown gravy."

Supporting Evidence[ ]

Lord Wyman Manderly tells Davos Seaworth that he does not believe the lies the Freys have been saying about the Red Wedding, and he knows his son Wendel was murdered by them in violation of guest right. He says that he had to appease the three Freys at White Harbor while his son Wylis was held hostage by the Lannisters, but now his son is home.

Wyman tells Davos he must travel to Winterfell for a wedding, along with the Freys. Wyman plans to give the three Freys guest gifts, a tradition that marks a guest's departure and the end of the obligations of a host to follow guest right.

Rhaegar, Symond, and Jared Frey, riding on the fine horses that Wyman has gifted them, disappear while on the road from White Harbor to Barrowton. According to Roose Bolton, Wyman insists the Freys traveled ahead of his column out of impatience over Wyman's slow pace, and he is distraught at their disappearance.

Ramsay Bolton searches for them, along with his squires Big Walder and Little Walder Frey, but they cannot be found. Big Walder tells Theon Greyjoy he never expected to find his cousins. He believes that Wyman had them killed, saying that's what he would do if he were Wyman. 

  

Lord Wyman Manderly and his three pies, by Jake Murray © Fantasy Flight Games

Lord Wyman presents three huge pork pies at the wedding in Winterfell, three matching the three missing Freys. Wyman serves portions to Roose, Walda Bolton, Hosteen Frey, and Aenys Frey, urging them to eat and praising the flavor. He serves them with great (drunken) exuberance. Wyman himself eats two portions from each pie.

As he leaves the wedding feast, Lord Wyman drunkenly tells the bard Abel to sing about the Rat Cook, a legendary Night's Watch cook who killed an Andal king's son and baked him into a pie he then served to his father. (Shortly after Bran Stark dreamed Robb Stark's death at the Red Wedding, he reflected on the legend of the Rat Cook: he was transformed into a giant white rat by the gods, not because of his deeds, but specifically because he dishonored guest right.) A song about that subject would typically be considered too gruesome for a wedding.

Hosteen and Aenys Frey later confront Lord Wyman about their missing relatives. In their argument, Hosteen says he will serve Wyman "all the bloody bits you can stomach", and Wyman laughs.
