## Full Text

Mechanical "dragons" were siege engines built by the Alchemists' Guild under orders of King Aegon IV Targaryen, to replace the extinct dragons of House Targaryen.

History[ ]

Like many other Targaryen kings after the Dance of the Dragons, Aegon was obsessed with regaining dragons. However, instead of trying to resurrect the dead dragons of his ancestors, Aegon turned to the pyromancers and commanded them to "build me dragons". The Guild built seven wood-and-iron monstrous engines, fitted with pumps that shot jets of wildfire. Though the "dragons" might have been useful in a siege, instead Aegon ordered these devices dragged to the Boneway to initiate an invasion of Dorne in 174 AC. The king's plan lacked any tactical sense, as the Boneway was too steep for the lumbering constructs. The man-made dragons did not even get that far, due to instability of wildfire and the difficulty of moving the massive siege engines. All seven were consumed by fire in the kingswood; hundreds of men operating them burned alive inside them and a quarter of the kingswood went up in flames. Aegon, after this humiliation, never spoke of conquering Dorne again.
