Culture[ ]

Mice are known for their timidity and are considered vermin.

A large white mouse with fierce red eyes is included in the arms of Ser Shadrich, who calls himself the Mad Mouse.

Cats which hunt mice are known as mousers.

Mousedown Mill is a location in the riverlands.

History[ ]

In some stories about House Lannister's acquisition of Casterly Rock, Lann the Clever filled a cleft in the castle with mice, rats, and other vermin to chase out House Casterly.

Marilda of Hull was known as Mouse when she was young because she was "small, quick, and always underfoot." Later in life, Marilda acquired a trading cog which she named Mouse. Mushroom referred to Marilda's palatial manse at Hull as the Mouse House. The tomb at Hull of Ser Addam Velaryon, one of Marilda's sons, has carvings of a mouse and the seahorse of House Velaryon.

When they worked together in Pentos, Varys and Illyrio Mopatis referred to Varys's network of informants as mice.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

One of the gifts which Daenerys Targaryen receives at her wedding is a gown made from the skin of a thousand mice, which Magister Illyrio Mopatis considers to be a handsome gift.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Serving incognito as a servant at Harrenhal, Arya Stark thinks of herself as a mouse while trying not to bring attention to herself.

Aggo purchases a skewer of honey-roasted mice at the port of Qarth.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Samwell Tarly finds a mouse in the library of Castle Black.

Queen Regent Cersei Lannister hears soft sounds in the walls of the Red Keep and tells herself that they are mice. King Tommen I Baratheon informs his mother that Ser Pounce caught a mouse but had it stolen away by Lady Whiskers. Dorcas makes a toy mouse with scraps of fur and ties it on a long string at the end of an old fishing pole so that Tommen can play with his three kittens.

Cossomo the Conjurer uses mice in his sleight of hand tricks.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Victarion Greyjoy thinks poorly of Maester Kerwin, considering him a mouse.
