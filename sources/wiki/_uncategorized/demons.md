Legends[ ]

Maegi are said by the Dothraki to lay with demons.

Legends of the Iron Islands describe Ygg as a demon tree who fed on human flesh.

It is said demons haunt the remaining lands of the Valyrian peninsula, the Smoking Sea,, the ruins of Hardhome, the island of Leng, the vicinity of Stygai, and the caves and cliffs of the Mountains of the Morn. The forests of Mossovy are said to be a land of demon hunters.

The Five Forts are said to have been erected by the Pearl Emperor of the Great Empire of the Dawn to keep the Lion of Night and his demons away from the realms of men.

According to reports, it is possible to fornicate with demons in Asshai. Demons, dragons, and other creatures are said to inhabit the cliff caves above the Ash.

The demon mother of ice giants is said to dance eternally through the night in the northern waters of the Shivering Sea.

Culture[ ]

The demon road connects Volantis and Meereen. Demon's dance is a poison known to maesters. Sea Demon is one of the ships of Stannis Baratheon. Wildfire is also called the jade demon.

Demons are depicted on some of the linked coins on the necklace of Vargo Hoat. Demons are among the gargoyles and grotesques which decorate Dragonstone. Demons are among the painted images on the shields of some free folk warriors. The helm of Ser Tommard Heddle depicted a scaled and slavering demon.

Slavers will brand a slave's cheek with a demon's mask to mark them as a dangerous and disobedient slave.

The bearded priests of Norvos consider the Black Goat of Qohor to be a demon.

"Demon" is also used as a term to describe some great fighters, such as "the demon of the Trident", King Robert I Baratheon, or the Kingsguard from House Darry known as the Demon of Darry. Archmaester Mancaster wrote Sea Demons: A History of the Children of the Drowned God of the Isles about the ironborn.

History[ ]

Ghiscari referred to Walano as the Demon Isle after first coming into contact with the dark-skinned inhabitants of the Summer Isles.

During their coming to the riverlands, conquering Andals considered the children of the forest and their weirwood trees to be little more than demons. Tales of the Warrior's Sons claim they were demonhunters.

Priests of the Drowned God denounced Othgar Hoare, King of the Iron Islands, as Othgar Demonlover, possibly because of House Hoare's support of the Faith.

Dragonglass and demonic black blood are said to have rained down from red clouds during the Doom of Valyria.

Alys Rivers was feared to be a witch who lay with demons.

The Shepherd claimed that dragons were demons summoned from the seven hells by Valyrian sorcerers.

Sam Stoops' wife believed the Lord of the Seven Hells would send a demon to collect the child whenever Rohanne Webber, Lady of Coldmoat, gave birth.

Serving girls at Casterly Rock believed that Maggy the Frog could summon demons.

Recent Events[ ]

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Dywen of the Night's Watch tells Jon Snow that the wildling Craster deals with slavers and demons.

Salloreon offers to craft for Tyrion Lannister a helm decorated as a demon's head with tall golden horns, though Tyrion declines. A begging brother in King's Landing refers to Tyrion, a dwarf, as a "little monkey demon". When revealing to Tyrion the story of his mutilation, Varys wonders if the voice heard from the flames in which his sexual organs were burned was a god, a demon, or a trick.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

When Daenerys Targaryen sees the elaborate hairstyles of Astapori guards, she compares them to a troupe of demons from the seventh hell.

Stannis Baratheon describes the Others as "demons made of snow and ice and cold".

A Feast for Crows[ ]

Alarmed by the War of the Five Kings and Stannis's support of R'hllor, sparrows in King's Landing warn of doom and demon worship.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

Leona Woolfield refers to R'hllor as a fire demon, though the queen's men in Stannis's company believe the Drowned God and weirwoods to be demons. Wulfe One-Ear considers the black-skinned Moqorro to be a demon priest, while the red priest considers the Drowned God to be a demon in thrall to the Other.

Slavers brand Ser Jorah Mormont's face with a demon's mask to mark him as dangerous and disobedient.
