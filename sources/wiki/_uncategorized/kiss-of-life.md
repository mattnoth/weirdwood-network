Method[ ]

Followers of the Drowned God are ritually drowned and then revived by priests. Many ironborn are symbolically drowned as infants by being briefly dipped into a tub of seawater. Older traditions have devotees literally drowned, however. Priests and their acolytes, the drowned men, then work the arms and pump the chest of the devotee, and repeatedly breathe air into their lungs until the devotee revives. Priests are occasionally unable to revive the drowned man, with the exception of Aeron Greyjoy, who has never lost a man.

Some priests claim to have been revived multiple times, such as Lodos the Twice-Drowned and Tarle the Thrice-Drowned.

Adversaries of the ironborn can be drowned as sacrifices to the Drowned God, but are not revived.

History[ ]

Priests of the Drowned God spoke out against Horgan Hoare, King of the Iron Islands, after Horgan allowed a sept to be built on Old Wyk. Worshipers of the Drowned God destroyed the sept and dragged followers of the Faith of the Seven into the water to be drowned, so they could regain their faith. In response, Horgan Priestkiller began slaughtering priests.

In 211 AC, Ser Duncan the Tall drowned while fighting a trial by combat in a river. Maester Cerrick, who was ironborn and had studied the practices of the priests of the Drowned God, was able to revive him using the kiss of life.
