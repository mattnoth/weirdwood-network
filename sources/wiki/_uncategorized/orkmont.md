Economy[ ]

  

Orkmont by Franz Miklis © Fantasy Flight Games

Rugged and mountainous, Orkmont is rich in ore, including iron, lead, and tin. It was once covered in forest but the timber was used extensively for shipbuilding. Orkmont is larger and richer than Pyke.

Houses[ ]

Orkmont contains the seats of House Orkwood and House Tawney. The island also has a branch of House Goodbrother. The extinct houses Greyiron and Hoare, both of whom once ruled the Iron Islands as iron kings, came from Orkmont.

History[ ]

Urras Greyiron, the salt king of Orkmont, was crowned the first High King of the Iron Islands in a kingsmoot on Old Wyk. Longships from Orkmont attacked the western shores of the north during the reign of King Harrag Hoare.

Urron Greyiron, a salt king from Orkmont, slaughtered his rivals at a kingsmoot on Old Wyk, becoming the first hereditary King of the Iron Islands. House Orkwood of Orkmont helped to overthrow House Greyiron during the coming of the Andals to the Iron Islands. During the rule of the iron kings of House Hoare, merchants and traders from Orkmont were known to call at Lannisport, Oldtown, and the Free Cities.

Following the death of Harren Hoare, King of the Isles and the Rivers, in the burning of Harrenhal, an ironman of Orkmont declared himself king but was killed in the succession squabble.

During Greyjoy's Rebellion, the forces of King Robert I Baratheon conquered Orkmont.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

  

Raiders of Orkmont by Jake Murray © Fantasy Flight Games

Lord Alyn Orkwood of Orkmont supports Euron Greyjoy with twenty longships. Besides the Orkwoods, Goodbrothers and Tawneys also travel from Orkmont to the kingsmoot on Old Wyk.

Behind the Scenes[ ]

The name of Orkmont may be inspired by Orkney, an archipelago in the Northern Isles of Scotland.
