Appearance and Character[ ]

Brandon is wrinkled and slight of build, but sly-eyed and spry. He resembles an old fox clad in fur and iron. The bearded Brandon is half a head taller than Torghen Flint.

The Norrey believes in the old gods and distrusts free folk.

Recent Events[ ]

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

House Norrey gives aid to King Stannis Baratheon when the king marches to the northern mountains and then defeats Asha Greyjoy in the fight by Deepwood Motte. The Norrey is too old to go on campaign, but his son, Brandon the Younger, joins Stannis's march on Winterfell.

Brandon and twelve fighting men visit Castle Black for the wedding of Alys Karstark and Sigorn, the new Magnar of Thenn. The Norrey also brings a wet nurse. Brandon is skeptical of the idea of Lord Commander Jon Snow to settle free folk south of the Wall.
