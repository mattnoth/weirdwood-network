Properties[ ]

  

The shop of Tobho Mott, by Logan Feliciano © Fantasy Flight Games

Steel is forged by blacksmiths. Cheap steel can be brittle, while castle-forged steel is considered higher quality. Polished steel can reflect light, but steel will rust if not cared for, especially if it is cheap. Using spells, the smiths of Qohor are said to make items superior even to the castle-forged steel of the Seven Kingdoms. 

Steel can appear blue, silver-blue, grey, and black, and some artisans decorate steel with paint or enamel. The House Targaryen guards of old wore black steel, for instance, and the knights of the Kingsguard wear white steel plate. Steel can also be jeweled, silvered, gilded, and filigreed.

Valyrian steel is lighter than common steel, and Valyrian-made blades are harder and sharper.

Steel gives greater protection than lesser armor, but the wearer is in greater risk of drowning while wearing the heavier equipment.

The blades of the Others are so cold that they shatter a steel blade on impact. Wildfire can seep into steel, which then goes up in flames. The goldenheart bows of the Summer Isles can penetrate plate.

The scales of a full-grown dragon are harder than steel. The fire of such a dragon is hot enough to melt steel and stone. Dragons can also bite through steel plate.

Armament[ ]

Weapons made of steel include axes, caltrops, crossbow bolts, daggers, lance points, maces, morningstars, pike points, scythes, spearheads, swords, and the tips of whips. Masters-at-arms can provide blunted weapons for training instead of live steel. Steel attire includes boots, caps, codpieces, gauntlets, gorgets, greaves, hauberks, helms, ringmail, plate, rondels, shinguards, and vambraces. Wooden shields can be banded with steel, and small shields can be made of the alloy. Horseshoes can also be made of steel.

Princess Rhaenys Targaryen wore a steel and copper armor which flashed in the sun.

Ser Brynden Tully owns grey ringmail under blackened steel, with a black trout adorning the helm's crest.

Lord Tywin Lannister's plate armor is made of steel and is enameled in deep crimson and highlighted with gold, and its rondels are golden sunbursts. Although Ser Jaime Lannister's armor appears to be made of gold, it is actually gilded steel. Steel lions crest the helms of House Lannister guards.

In the field Lord Randyll Tarly wears mail and boiled leather with a breastplate of grey steel.

Brienne of Tarth's dented steel armor is of a brilliant deep blue cobalt.

Sandor Clegane wears a steel helm shaped like a hound's head, while Vargo Hoat's black steel helm resembles the head of a goat. Ser Jorah Mormont owns a flat-topped greathelm of dark grey steel. Gendry made a steel helm shaped like a bull's head. Bronn owns a round steel halfhelm with a noseguard.

Instead of good steel, Thoros of Myr uses cheap steel for his swords, as wildfire ruins the blade.

Culture[ ]

  

The steel link in a maester's chain, by Anna Christenson © Fantasy Flight Games

Worthy warriors can be considered true steel, in comparison to lesser men.

Steel is one of the metals used by maesters for their chain links.

The Street of Steel in King's Landing contains numerous shops and forges.

When priests of the Drowned God pray for a believer, they ask that the follower be blessed with salt, stone, and steel. "Steel Rain" is an old reaving song sung by ironborn.

The Steel Road is one of the three mountain passes through the Bone Mountains in Essos.

It is forbidden to bare steel in Vaes Dothrak.

Free folk raiders steal iron and steel weapons from the north, as they lack the skill to forge steel themselves. The Vale mountain clans also wield scavenged steel of inferior quality.

Several individuals have aliases based on the metal:

Steelshanks Walton, captain of the guards for Roose Bolton, Lord of the Dreadfort.

Ser Garth Hightower, called "Greysteel", the second son of Leyton Hightower, Lord of the Hightower.

Steelskin, a pit fighter in Meereen.

Ser Florian Greysteel, the personal champion of Manfryd Mooton, Lord of Maidenpool, during the Dance of the Dragons.

Ser Aegor Rivers, called "Bittersteel", a Great Bastard and the founder of the Golden Company. The battle cry of his sellswords became "Beneath the gold, the bitter steel".

Steely Pate, a blacksmith living during the reign of King Daeron II Targaryen.

History[ ]

  

Jousting steed, by Tomasz Jedruszek © Fantasy Flight Games

The bronze weapons of the First Men were stronger than the stone and obsidian weapons of the children of the forest, but both peoples were no match for the iron and steel of the Andals.

The Valyrian Freehold made a metal of unparalleled quality, Valyrian steel.

King Aegon I Targaryen used the dragonflame of Balerion to forge the Iron Throne out of the swords and daggers of his enemies.

Ser Byron Swann meant to kill a dragon during the Dance of the Dragons with a spear and a shield of silvered steel, thus mimicking the legend of Serwyn of the Mirror Shield who slew the dragon Urrax.

Hugh Hammer was said to be so strong he could twist steel bars into torcs with his bare hands.

Prince Daemon Targaryen used a steel-tipped whip to control his dragon Caraxes.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

In return for their service, Tyrion Lannister promises to arm several Vale mountain clans with steel weapons and armor.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

  

Tyrion Lannister's chain, by Davis Engel © Fantasy Flight Games

In preparation for his defense of King's Landing during the War of the Five Kings, Tyrion has the blacksmiths of the city forge a chain of immense steel links long enough to cross the Blackwater Rush. During the Battle of the Blackwater, the royal fleets of King Joffrey I Baratheon and his rival, Stannis Baratheon, are blocked by the chain and largely destroyed by wildfire.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Arya Stark learns that the clansmen of the Vale of Arryn have become bolder after being armed with steel.

A Feast for Crows[ ]

When looking in the Night's Watch's archives, Samwell Tarly reads about dragonsteel, which he and Jon Snow think might have been Valyrian steel.

Since the babe was born during the battle beneath the Wall, Gilly gives the name "Aemon Steelsong" to the son of Mance Rayder and his late wife, Dalla.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

The company steel of the Second Sons is poorly maintained.
