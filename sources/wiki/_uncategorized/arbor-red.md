History[ ]

King Aegon II Targaryen died when his Arbor red was poisoned, likely at the behest of Lord Larys Strong.

Rich Arbor reds were served during the wedding tourney at Whitewalls.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

Arbor reds are among the wines offered to Daenerys Targaryen by a wineseller in the Western Market of Vaes Dothrak. When her identity is revealed, the wineseller tries to give her a dry Arbor red which he has secretly poisoned. Ser Jorah Mormont, aware of King Robert I Baratheon's attempts to kill Daenerys, interrupts the assassination attempt, however.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Tyrion Lannister uses a powder to poison the Arbor red of his sister, Queen Regent Cersei Lannister.

The captains of Quicksilver and Greyhound offer Arbor red to Daenerys when she meets with them to discuss possible transport from Qarth.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

  

The death of King Joffrey I Baratheon, by Magali Villeneuve © Fantasy Flight Games

During the wedding feast of King Joffrey I Baratheon and Margaery Tyrell, a cupbearer pours a flagon of dark Arbor red into the king's wedding chalice, and a drunken Joffrey later pours his chalice atop his uncle, Tyrion. After ordering Tyrion to refill the chalice, Joffrey chokes to death while eating pigeon pie and drinking wine. During Tyrion's trial for murder, he is accused by Grand Maester Pycelle of having poisoned the boy with the strangler.

A Dance with Dragons[ ]

King Hizdahr zo Loraq sends Draqaz and Miklaz to bring him flagons of Arbor gold and red.

Quotes[ ]

I have a dry red from the Arbor, crisp and delectable.

—Wineseller to Daenerys Targaryen.
