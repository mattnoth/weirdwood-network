## Full Text

Vaes Dothrak

Essos west of the Bone Mountains and the location of Vaes Dothrak

Vaes Dothrak is the only city of the Dothraki people. It is located in the northeast of the vast Dothraki sea, beneath the Mother of Mountains and near a lake, the Womb of the World. Vaes Dothrak has no walls.

The city is ruled and inhabited by slaves and the dosh khaleen, the wives of all the khals who have come before. It is forbidden to wear a blade or shed a free man's blood within the confines of the city. The name Vaes Dothrak means City of Riders in the Dothraki language.

Layout[ ]

See also: Images of Vaes Dothrak

The heart of the Dothraki sea and Dothraki culture, Vaes Dothrak is one of the largest cities of the known world, due to its expansive layout, and also one of the smallest, due to its low population. The city is large enough to hold every khalasar, were they all to return at once.

Vaes Dothrak has broad, windswept streets paved in grass and mud. There are carved stone pavilions, manses of woven grass, wooden towers, stepped pyramids faced with marble, and roofless log halls. Some of these palaces are protected by horny hedges instead of walls. All of the buildings were built by slaves brought from the lands the Dothraki have plundered.

The godsway runs from the Horse Gate of Vaes Dothrak to the Mother of Mountains. It is lined with monuments and holy symbols from a hundred different religions on both sides, showing the vast range and power of Dothraki conquests. Along the way it passes through the Western and Eastern Markets, the lake named the Womb of the World, and a pit where important ceremonies are done in Vaes Dothrak.

Hall[ ]

Located near the Eastern Market, the cavernous wooden feasting hall has walls rising forty feet high. It has a silk roof which can be raised or lowered. Inside the hall are three firepits. Those high in honor sit at a high bench, and those low in honor must sit at the back of the long hall. Surrounding the palace are horse yards fenced with high hedges, firepits, and hundreds of grass-covered round earthen houses.

Markets[ ]

The Western Market is a grand bazaar used by traders from the Free Cities. It is a great square of beaten earth surrounded by mud-baked brick, animal pens and whitewashed drinking halls. Hummocks rise from the earth and beneath the square lie large storerooms. The interior of the square is made up of stalls and crookback aisles shaded by woven grass. Products offered in the Western Market include Lysene perfumes, Myrish lace, fine wools, Pentoshi dyes, goldwork from Lannisport, ornately-patterned breastplates, and helms in the shapes of fanciful beasts. Food and ingredients in the market includes garlic, hot pepper, and sausages.

In the Eastern Market, traders from Yi Ti, Asshai, and the Shadow Lands come to trade. Animals in the market include manticores in silver cages, grey elephants, and striped white and black horses (zorses) of the Jogos Nhai. Food sold in the Eastern Market includes tree eggs, locust pie, and green noodles.

Traders are free to cross the Dothraki sea unmolested to Vaes Dothrak as long as they keep the peace, do not profane either the Mother of Mountains or the Womb of the World, and give the traditional gifts of salt, silver, and seed to the dosh khaleen. The Steel Road and the Stone Road originate in Vaes Dothrak and head east through the Bones. Other routes head west to the ruins of Vaes Leqse and Yalli Qamayi.

History[ ]

Scholars believe the ancestors of the Dothraki came from the lands beyond the Bone Mountains in the Further East of Essos, and the Dothraki themselves believe that their race was born in Vaes Dothrak. 

The disparate buildings of Vaes Dothrak were built by slaves in the fashion of their original lands. The monuments of the godsway were brought to Vaes Dothrak after being plundered from distant lands.

The exiled Ser Jorah Mormont has traveled as far east as Vaes Dothrak.

Recent Events[ ]

A Game of Thrones[ ]

  

Daenerys Targaryen and Jorah Mormont in the markets, by Marc Simonetti ©

Magister Illyrio Mopatis of Pentos is said to have friends in Vaes Dothrak.

After wedding Khal Drogo outside Pentos, Daenerys Targaryen travels with his khalasar to Vaes Dothrak, where she is presented to the dosh khaleen. Daenerys ceremonially eats a horse heart and washes in the Womb of the World. When a drunken Viserys Targaryen threatens his sister with an unsheathed sword in the long hall and demands the crown he was promised by Drogo, the khal has him killed by having molten gold poured on his head.

Daenerys often spends her mornings in the Eastern Market, sampling its foods and observing spellsingers and Shadow Men and exotic travelers from Asshai, Qarth, Yi Ti, Bayasabhad, Shamyriana, and Kayakayanaya.

Byan Votyris leads a caravan of four hundred horses to Vaes Dothrak from Pentos by way of Norvos and Qohor, so Ser Jorah Mormont suggests that Daenerys see the new wares in the Western Market. Daenerys exchanges one of her silver medallions for a feathered cloak from the Summer Isles. She also acquires a dozen flasks of scented oils, and she buys a fertility charm for Doreah. A birdseller teaches a parrot to say Daenerys's name, but she refuses to take the bird. A wineseller attempts to poison the pregnant Daenerys, but he is stopped by her khas and Jorah. The an assassination attempt leads Drogo to announce he will lead his khalasar against the Seven Kingdoms.

Drogo's khalasar departs Vaes Dothrak for Lhazar. Daenerys loses her child Rhaego, and Drogo's khalasar disbands after the khal dies. Daenerys burns Drogo's body on a pyre and hatches dragon eggs in the flames.

During his coma, Bran Stark has a vision of Vaes Dothrak beneath the Mother of Mountains.

A Clash of Kings[ ]

Daenerys thinks that other khals would attempt to slay her meager khalasar and drag her back to Vaes Dothrak to join the dosh khaleen. Her khalasar travels through the red waste toward Qarth.

A Storm of Swords[ ]

Following the siege of Meereen, the envoy Ghael informs Daenerys that Yunkai has dispatched riders to Vaes Dothrak to bring a khalasar against her.
