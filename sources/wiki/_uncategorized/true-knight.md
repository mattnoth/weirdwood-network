Culture[ ]

There are Westerosi songs and stories, particularly those depicting honorable knights and chivalry, that are very popular amongst all strata of society. In particular, Sansa Stark is captivated by the concept of the true knight.

A true knight must fully embrace the oath of knighthood:

In the name of the Warrior I charge you to be brave. In the name of the Father I charge you to be just. In the name of the Mother I charge you to defend the young and innocent. In the name of the Maid I charge you to protect all women.

Chivalric code[ ]

  

Ser Arthur Dayne, by Henning Ludvigsen © Fantasy Flight Games

True knights are supposed to:

Be brave and just.

Defend the young and innocent, especially women who can not defend themselves.

Be cleanly as well as godly.

Act with military courtesy: in combat when nobles and knights are taken prisoner, their lives are spared and are often held for ransom.
