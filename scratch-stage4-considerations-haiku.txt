⏺ Exactly the right frame. This is the broader pattern: anywhere the prompt asks the model to do a mental transform, a smaller/cheaper model can fumble it. Locking means removing those decision points.                                 
           
  Two categories of lock:

  1. Eliminate the decision — [LINK] substitution; vocab lock; pre-validating type contracts in the candidate generator. The model doesn't even see the question.                                                                         
  2. Mechanically validate the answer — schema validator; type-contract checker; suspicious-edges flagger. The model can be wrong, but the wrong rows get blocked or flagged.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
  The first is always cheaper at runtime; the second catches what the first can't (e.g., the semantic wrongness of saying "KNOWS" when the prose says nothing about knowing).
